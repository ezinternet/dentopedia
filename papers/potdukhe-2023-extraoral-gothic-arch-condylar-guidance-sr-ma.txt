Title: Evaluation of accuracy between extraoral Gothic arch tracing and various other methods assessing horizontal condylar guidance angle in completely edentulous patients: A systematic review and meta-analysis

Authors: Potdukhe et al.
Year: 2023
Journal: Journal of Indian Prosthodontic Society
DOI: 10.4103/jips.jips_216_23 (https://doi.org/10.4103/jips.jips_216_23)
PMID: 37861609
PMCID: PMC10705011
Source: Full text retrieved from PubMed Central (PMC).

=====================================================================
ABSTRACT
=====================================================================

Aim:
The aim of this systematic review and meta-analysis was to determine the accuracy of different methods of measuring horizontal condylar guidance (HCG) angle in comparison with extraoral Gothic arch tracing for completely edentulous patients.

Settings and Design:
This was a systematic review and meta-analysis following the Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses guidelines.

Materials and Methods:
Two reviewers searched the EBSCOhost, Cochrane Library, and PubMed/MEDLINE databases and the Google Scholar search engine for studies, randomized controlled trials, cross-sectional studies, and quasi-experimental studies published from January 2005 to December 2022 on various other methods of determining HCG angle in completely edentulous patients compared with extraoral Gothic arch tracing method.

Statistical Analysis Used:
Meta-analysis was conducted from the reported quantitative data.

Results:
A total of 513 articles were obtained from different electronic databases, of which 22 studies were included for qualitative synthesis and 20 studies were included for meta-analysis. For the right side, a statistically significant difference was observed for panoramic radiograph (<0.05, pooled mean difference = 5.08 [2.17, 7]) and cephalogram (<0.05, pooled mean difference = 10.65 [8.81, 12.49]), whereas no statistically significant difference was observed for cone-beam computed tomography (CBCT) (=0.41, pooled mean difference = 4.39 [-6.10, 14.87]) and protrusive interocclusal wax record (=0.92, pooled mean difference = -0.45 [-9.62, 8.72]) as compared with extraoral Gothic arch tracing method. For the left side, a statistically significant difference was observed for panoramic radiograph (<0.05, pooled mean difference = 5.07 [1.95, 8.18]) and cephalogram (<0.05, pooled mean difference = 10.24 [8.65, 11.83]), whereas no statistically significant difference was observed for CBCT (=0.31, pooled mean difference = 4.05 [-3.74, 11.84]) and protrusive interocclusal wax record (=0.72, pooled mean difference = -1.21 [-7.86, 5.43]) as compared with extraoral Gothic arch tracing method.

Conclusion:
The cephalogram and panoramic radiograph obtained higher HCG angles in completely edentulous patients than extraoral Gothic arch tracing.

INTRODUCTION

Complete edentulism due to local, systemic, and physiological factors results in a continuous resorption process leading to loss of vertical dimension, loss of functional efficiency, and disturbance in stomatognathic structures. Harmonious coordination between the condylar path, mandibular movement, and occlusal morphology of teeth determines the longevity of a complete denture prosthesis. The mandibular guidance generated by the condyle and articular disc traversing the contour of the glenoid fossa represents the condylar guidance. Condylar guidance, the determinant of the occlusion obtained directly from the patient, is the most accurate reference point. Inaccurate transfer of condylar guidance values on the articulator causes occlusal interferences during mandibular movements. Horizontal condylar guidance (HCG) is obtained from the protrusive movements, and lateral condylar guidance is obtained from lateral movements.

Extraoral tracers or intraoral tracers are widely used for Gothic arch tracing or arrowhead tracing to obtain HCG angle through set protrusive interocclusal record (IOR) on semi-adjustable articulators. It is one of the oldest methods and provides a reproducible mandibular border movement in horizontal plane and so has been used as a comparison group. However, few studies reported higher HCG values obtained from Gothic arch tracing and stated that it aids to develop precise occlusion without any interference, whereas few studies reported lesser accuracy due to lack of neuromuscular coordination, instability of denture bases, and higher complexity with Gothic arch tracing. Few studies reported that interocclusal recording medium can affect the accuracy due to differences in composition, properties, compressibility, hardness, dimensional stability, accuracy, fine reproduction of details, and elastic recovery.

Radiographic methods such as panoramic radiographs, cephalogram, and cone-beam computed tomography (CBCT) have been used for determining HCG angle. The condylar guidance angle was determined by the intersection of two reference lines, first Frankfort horizontal plane (orbitale to porion) and second from the most superior point on an articular eminence to the most inferior point on an articular tubercle determining the mean condylar path inclination. These bony reference points are identified and traced on two-dimensional and three-dimensional radiographs to measure HCG angle. However, differences were obtained between cephalogram, CBCT, and panoramic radiograph due to variations in resolution, contrast ratio, method of calculation, and clarity.

Digital methods such as electronic facebow, digital tracers, kinesiography, and axiography use ultrasonic sensors to measure HCG angle in three dimensions displayed on the computer. Luke carried out a systematic review on radiographic techniques and protrusive IOR and reported the occurrence of some clinical variability among both methods. No clear evidence has been reported on which method is accurate to determine the HCG angle in completely edentulous patients.

Therefore, the aim of this systematic review and meta-analysis was to determine the accuracy between extraoral Gothic arch tracing and other different methods used for measuring HCG angle in completely edentulous patients. This will give clinicians a choice of simplified, accurate, and convenient methods to determine the HCG angle from the patient for obtaining balanced occlusion without occlusal interferences for prosthetic rehabilitation in completely edentulous patients. The null hypothesis was that no statistically significant difference will be observed in the accuracy of measuring HCG angle between extraoral Gothic arch tracing and various other methods that are used in completely edentulous patients.

MATERIALS AND METHODS

Protocol registration:
According to the PRISMA 2020 guidelines, this systematic review and meta-analysis was conducted and registered at the Prospective Register of Systematic Reviews (PROSPERO) database under the code CRD42022283974.

Review question (PICOS):
"Is there a difference in the accuracy of other different methods as compared with extraoral Gothic arch tracing used for measuring the HCG angle in completely edentulous patients?"
- Population: completely edentulous patients with healthy ridges above 35 years of age.
- Intervention: studies determining HCG angle using panoramic radiograph, cephalogram, CBCT, CT scan, digital methods, intraoral tracing, and interocclusal wax record in completely edentulous patients.
- Comparison: studies determining HCG angle using extraoral Gothic arch tracing through set protrusive IOR in completely edentulous patients.
- Outcome: HCG angle in completely edentulous patients.
- Study design: cross-sectional studies, randomized controlled trials, and quasi-experimental studies.

Inclusion/exclusion criteria:
Cross-sectional studies, RCTs, and quasi-experimental studies evaluating accuracy of other methods vs extraoral Gothic arch tracing for measuring HCG angle in completely edentulous patients. Full-text studies published in English between January 2005 and December 2022 included. Human studies included. Case reports, case series, reviews, questionnaires, surveys, and non-English studies before 2005 excluded.

Search strategy:
Databases: EBSCOhost, Cochrane Library, PubMed/MEDLINE, Google Scholar. Cohen kappa score of 0.90 indicates perfect agreement between two authors.

Risk of bias:
Newcastle-Ottawa tool (modified case-control scale) used for quality assessment. Eight items from three domains (Selection, Comparability, Outcome). Graded poor/fair/high.

Meta-analysis:
Statistical analysis using Review Manager (RevMan) Version 5.4 (Cochrane Collaboration, 2020). Forest plot obtained using mean, standard deviation, and total at 95% confidence interval (CI), with p<0.05 as statistically significant. I-squared test used to measure heterogeneity. If I-squared >50%, random effect model applied; if <50%, fixed effect model applied. Funnel plot used to detect publication bias.

RESULTS

Literature search:
A total of 513 articles were obtained, of which 22 were duplicates. 491 abstracts were screened and 422 not relevant excluded. 69 articles eligible for full-text assessment. After screening, 47 articles excluded (31 inappropriate study groups, 12 data on dentulous patients, 2 intraoral tracing technique, 2 lateral condylar guidance). For qualitative synthesis, 22 studies included. For meta-analysis, 20 studies included.

Characteristics of included studies:
325 participants, average age 45-60 years. 2 studies in Egypt, 1 in Nepal, 19 in India. 21 studies used Hanau Wide Vue semi-adjustable articulator; one each used Hanau H2, Girrbach Dental, and Bio Art articulators. 15 studies measured HCG using panoramic radiograph; 6 using CBCT; 3 using cephalogram; 6 using IOR; 1 using axiograph; 1 using functiograph and intraoral tracer.

Included studies (selected condylar guidance values, GT = Gothic arch tracer):
- Verma 2022 (India, n=20): GT 27.75/27.25; Cephalogram 37.40/37.40; OPG 35.05/33.35; CBCT 33.95/33.85. Cephalogram > OPG > CBCT > tracer.
- EL-Ghiaty 2022 (Egypt, n=12): GT 36.14/30.71; IOR 26.57/21.63; CBCT 26.57/18.74. Wax protrusive record more practical.
- Sharma 2022 (India, n=15): GT 22.33/25.67; CBCT 44.60/44.47; IOR 35.87/36.33. CBCT highest.
- Kumar 2022 (India, n=30): GT 26.67/27; CBCT panoramic 29.33/29.52; CBCT cephalogram 31.24/31.63. CBCT viable alternative.
- Abdeen 2022 (Egypt, n=12): Protrusive wax record more practical.
- Ambata 2021 (India, n=50): GT 23.46/24.02; OPG 30.90/31.66; Cephalogram 34.36. Lateral cephalogram alternative.
- Shetty 2021 (India, n=20): GT 33/27.75; OPG 36.50/32.50; Axiograph 25.65/25.55. Axiograph alternative.
- Keerthi 2020 (India, n=22): GT 17.04/19.18; OPG 22.12/23.17. OPG higher.
- Mittal 2020 (India, n=15): GT 35/30.07; OPG 33.27/30.93. OPG reliable.
- Jerath 2019 (India, n=15): GT 31.31/31.10; IOR 26.80/27.8; CBCT 33.09/33.81. CBCT highest > tracer > wax.
- Bhandari 2018 (Nepal, n=25): GT 21.32/21.08; OPG 24/24.48. OPG higher.
- Amin 2018 (India, n=30): GT 24.20; OPG 29.10. OPG higher.
- Khalikar 2017 (India, n=10): GT 29.5/31.03; OPG 25.8/26. OPG/protrusive reliable.
- Kumari 2016 (India, n=10): GT 25.70/25.40; OPG 37.10/38. OPG not reliable.
- Patil 2015 (India, n=10): GT 18.50/20; OPG 17/17.30. Gothic arch similar to radiographic.
- Acharya 2015 (India, n=20): GT 26.50/26.75; OPG 30.35/29.50. OPG higher.
- Godavarthi 2015 (India, n=20): GT 38.62/38.05; OPG 36.6/34.17. IOR higher than OPG.
- Shah 2014 (India, n=24): GT 30.42/32.38; OPG 38.54/41.88. OPG higher, no correlation.
- Shetty 2013 (India, n=15): GT 12.73/13.47; OPG 37.13/35.13; IOR 24/21.67. OPG higher.
- Shah 2013 (India, n=20): GT 35.45/37.35; OPG 37.9/38.9. OPG higher.
- Thakur 2012 (India, n=28): GT 22.93/23.93; IOR 21.64/21.28. Gothic arch higher than wax.
- Nandini 2005 (India, n=10): Cephalometrics, Chandra tracer, intraoral tracer, functiograph, checkbite. Articulator > cephalometric.

Risk of bias: 20 studies graded good, 2 (Shetty 2013, Nandini 2005) graded fair.

Meta-analysis (20 studies): Comparators pooled vs extraoral Gothic arch tracing — 15 studies panoramic radiograph, 2 cephalogram, 5 CBCT, 4 interocclusal wax record.

RIGHT SIDE HCG ANGLE:
- Panoramic radiograph: I-squared = 89% (random effect). Statistically significant (p<0.05), pooled MD = 5.08 [2.17, 7], 95% CI. OPG higher than Gothic arch tracing.
- Cephalogram: I-squared = 0% (fixed effect). Statistically significant (p<0.05), pooled MD = 10.65 [8.81, 12.49], 95% CI. Cephalogram higher than Gothic arch tracing.
- CBCT: I-squared = 99% (random effect). NOT significant (p=0.41), pooled MD = 4.39 [-6.10, 14.87], 95% CI.
- Protrusive interocclusal wax record: I-squared = 98% (random effect). NOT significant (p=0.92), pooled MD = -0.45 [-9.62, 8.72], 95% CI.

LEFT SIDE HCG ANGLE:
- Panoramic radiograph: I-squared = 90% (random effect). Statistically significant (p<0.05), pooled MD = 5.07 [1.95, 8.18], 95% CI. OPG higher than Gothic arch tracing.
- Cephalogram: I-squared = 0% (fixed effect). Statistically significant (p<0.001), pooled MD = 10.24 [8.65, 11.83], 95% CI. Cephalogram higher than Gothic arch tracing.
- CBCT: I-squared = 98% (random effect). NOT significant (p=0.31), pooled MD = 4.05 [-3.74, 11.84], 95% CI.
- Protrusive interocclusal wax record: I-squared = 95% (random effect). NOT significant (p=0.72), pooled MD = -1.21 [-7.86, 5.43], 95% CI.

DISCUSSION

The null hypothesis was accepted for CBCT and interocclusal wax record (no significant difference vs Gothic arch tracing, both sides). The null hypothesis was rejected for cephalogram and panoramic radiograph (both gave significantly higher values than Gothic arch tracing).

Variability attributed to: complex arrangement of hight extraoral tracer, compressibility of underlying tissues, stability of temporary record bases, patient precision in mandibular movements, and differences in interocclusal recording medium properties. Radiographic errors due to improper bony landmark identification, 2D cephalometric image, manual tracing, radiographic distortion, head position, magnification and projection errors.

Clinical recommendation: For measuring HCG angle in completely edentulous patients, the clinician can opt for cephalogram and panoramic radiograph as alternative, convenient, accurate methods to record, measure, and transfer the HCG angle for programming the semi-adjustable articulator to obtain balanced occlusion.

Limitations: only analytical cross-sectional studies and English-only studies included; different comparison groups across studies yielded smaller numbers for cephalogram, CBCT, and wax record. Future trials comparing only two methods recommended.

CONCLUSION
1. Higher HCG angles were obtained from cephalogram and panoramic radiograph compared with extraoral Gothic arch tracing, with statistically significant difference for both right and left HCG angles.
2. Based on mean difference and number of studies, the panoramic radiograph is the best method to obtain higher and more accurate HCG angle in completely edentulous patients as compared with extraoral Gothic arch tracing.
