Title: Vertical Ridge Augmentation Using Collagen Membrane and Tenting Screws in the Esthetic Zone: A Case Series.
Authors: Chacón G, Saleh MHA, Decker A, Kan JYK, Wang HL
Journal: International Journal of Periodontics & Restorative Dentistry
Year: 2024
Volume: 44(1):81-89
DOI: 10.11607/prd.6373
PMID: 37471156

ABSTRACT

Background:
Successful rehabilitation of severely atrophic, short-span edentulous ridges in esthetic regions can seldom be done without some form of vertical ridge augmentation (VRA). The best available evidence shows that guided bone regeneration procedures may present a very predictable option with reduced potential for complications compared to alternative options.

Aim:
This case series presents a novel technique to achieve predictable VRA with a low complication rate using tenting screws and cross-linked resorbable membranes.

Methods:
- N = 10 patients (5 men, 5 women)
- Location: severe vertical defects in the ESTHETIC ZONE
- Material: cross-linked resorbable collagen membrane + tenting screws (titanium osteosynthesis screws used to tent/support the membrane without collapsing)
- No titanium mesh — cross-linked collagen IS the barrier, tenting screws provide the space maintenance

Results:
- Mean healing time: 9.3 months
- Mean defect resolution: 80%
- Mean VBG: 6.2 ± 1.61 mm
- Only 1 case with reduced defect resolution (50%), but still achieved 6 mm bone gain
- Complication rate: LOW (not quantified in abstract but implied minimal)

KEY INNOVATION — Tenting Screw Concept:
Tenting screws = titanium mini-screws placed into bone BEFORE membrane, creating a mechanical framework that holds the cross-linked collagen membrane in a tented position over the defect without collapsing. This partially replaces the space-maintenance function of Ti mesh.
- The screws are placed in a pattern matching the planned augmentation shape
- Cross-linked collagen covers the framework
- Bone graft placed under the tented membrane

WHY CROSS-LINKED COLLAGEN (not native collagen):
- Native collagen resorbs in 4–6 weeks (too fast for large VRA)
- Cross-linked collagen (ribose or glutaraldehyde) maintains barrier function 6–9+ months
- Resorbable = no 2nd surgery for removal
- BUT: slower degradation → more exposure risk than native collagen (Wessing SR+MA: 30% higher)

WHY NOT TI MESH:
- Authors: tenting screws + XL collagen = simpler, avoids Ti mesh exposure and 2nd-stage mesh removal
- Avoids Ti mesh neurological injury risk during manipulation
- Resorbable membrane = lower total procedural burden

RESULTS COMPARISON:
- 6.2 mm VBG is COMPETITIVE with Ti mesh (Sabri 2024 SR+MA: 3.36 mm; Cucchi 2017: 4.2 mm)
- However n=10, esthetic zone only, single operator — small case series

CONTEXT:
Authors are Wang HL group (University of Michigan) — prolific in VRA research.
