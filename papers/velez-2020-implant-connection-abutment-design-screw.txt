Influence of Implant Connection, Abutment Design and Screw Insertion Torque on Implant-Abutment Misfit
Vélez J, Peláez J, López-Suárez C, Agustín-Panadero R, Tobar C, Suárez MJ.
J Clin Med. 2020;9(8):2365. doi:10.3390/jcm9082365. PMID 32722131; PMCID PMC7463581.
Source: PubMed Central full text (https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7463581/)

ABSTRACT
Background: An accurate fit at the implant-abutment interface is an important factor to avoid biological and mechanical complications. The aim of this study was to evaluate the marginal misfit at the implant-abutment interface on external and Morse taper connection, with straight and angulated abutments under different insertion torque loads. Materials and Methods: A total of 120 implants were used, 60 with external connection (EC) and 60 with Morse taper connection (IC). Straight (SA) (n=60) and angulated abutments (AA) (n=60) were randomly screwed to each connection at different torque levels (n=10 each): 10, 20 and 30 Ncm. All specimens were subjected to thermal and cyclic loading and the misfit was measured by scanning electron microscopy. Data were analyzed with one-way ANOVA, t-test and Kruskal-Wallis test. Results: Significant differences (<0.001) were found between connections and abutments regardless of the torque applied. Morse taper connections with straight and angulated abutments showed the lowest misfit values (0.6 µm). Misfit values decreased as torque increased. Conclusions: The misfit was affected by the type of connection. The type of abutment did not influence the fit in the Morse taper connection. The higher the tightening torque applied the increase in the fit of the implant-abutment interface.

1. INTRODUCTION (key points)
Internal connections provide better esthetics, joint strength, microbial seal, long-term stability and crestal bone levels vs external connection. Morse taper connection is a conical internal connection that creates friction/cold-welding, a larger interface surface area, good seal, less microleakage, superior joint stability and less marginal bone loss. The prosthetic abutment is fixed to the implant through a screw; the gap between implant and abutment may cause bacterial leakage and biomechanical complications (screw loosening, rotation/fracture, reduced preload). Degree of filtration depends on connection geometry, fit precision, rotational freedom, applied torque, micromovements and abutment material. Limited data exist on the influence of torque level on implant-abutment fit, and few studies compare straight vs angulated abutments.

2. MATERIALS AND METHODS
Two implant designs (Mozo-Grau SA, Valladolid, Spain): external hexagon connection (EC, n=60) and internal connection with 11° Morse taper + double internal hexagon + platform switching (IC, n=60); same dimensions. Two abutment types per connection: straight hexagonal abutment (SA, n=60) and 15° angulated abutment (AA, n=60); 2 mm cuff height; titanium grade V (Ti-6Al-4V). 120 methacrylate specimens; implants placed at 35-45 Ncm per manufacturer. Abutments tightened at 10, 20, or 30 Ncm with a dynamometric torque wrench; retightened after 10 min. Four groups (n=30) by connection×abutment, each split into 3 torque subgroups (n=10). Thermocycling 1200 cycles 5-55°C; 250,000 mechanical cycles, 200 N vertical load (2.5 mm vertical, 0.7 mm lateral, 60 mm/s, 2 Hz) in Fusayama-Meyer artificial saliva; specimens embedded 3 mm above resin per ISO 14801:2007. Misfit measured by SEM at 1000× (gold-coated), 6 equidistant points (A-F, mesial+distal), 10 measurements/point = 60/specimen, by two researchers. Stats: ANOVA, Tamhane T2, t-test, Kruskal-Wallis + Bonferroni; α=0.05; power analysis required ≥7/group for 80% power.

3. RESULTS
All groups showed misfit values below 3 µm regardless of connection, abutment or torque.
- Connection: IC (Morse taper) presented significantly lower marginal misfit than EC (p=0.001), regardless of abutment/torque. IC-SA showed lowest misfit values. Morse taper SA and AA both ~0.6 µm.
- Abutment: straight abutments (SA) had better marginal fit than angulated (AA) (p=0.004) overall (1.3 vs 2.1 µm). For IC, no difference between SA and AA.
- Torque (10/20/30 Ncm, pooled): significant differences (p=0.001). 30 Ncm best fit vs 10 and 20 Ncm. Differences 10 vs 20 (p=0.001) and 10 vs 30 (p=0.001); NO significant difference between 20 and 30 Ncm (p=0.10).
- ECSA group: torque did not affect fit. ECAA: difference between 10 and 30 Ncm (p=0.008). ICSA (p=0.001) and ICAA (p=0.001): differences 10 vs 20 and 10 vs 30. Lowest misfit values with 30 Ncm in all groups. Best Morse taper fit at 30 Ncm (manufacturer recommended torque), values close to 0 µm.

4. DISCUSSION (key points)
The best fit values were obtained for the Morse taper connection at 30 Ncm torque load in both abutments, which is the manufacturer's recommended torque, with values close to 0 µm — clinically reinforcing the importance of following manufacturer instructions. However, no differences were observed between 20 and 30 Ncm. Under-tightening (below recommended) → high risk of screw loosening, screw fracture/deformation under low preload, less interface contact → less effective bacterial seal and more microleakage. Over-tightening (above recommended) → screw exceeds yield strength, loses mechanical characteristics, decreased preload, possible screw fracture. Morse taper has positive geometric locking (friction/wedging) protecting the abutment screw from excessive functional load. Titanium grade V (higher strength, lower frictional coefficient, higher preload) may have contributed to low misfit. Limitations: only one implant system; microleakage not assessed; no standardized method to measure interface fit.

5. CONCLUSIONS
Within limitations: (1) Morse taper (internal conical) connection had better marginal adjustment than external connection regardless of torque or abutment type; (2) straight abutments showed less misfit than angulated abutments in both connections; (3) misfit is directly proportional to applied torque — lower misfit at higher insertion torque (best at 30 Ncm, the manufacturer-recommended value for this system).
