Theoretical and methodological approaches in designing, developing, and delivering interventions for oral health behaviour change.

Kitsaras G, Asimakopoulou K, Henshaw M, Borrelli B.
Community Dentistry and Oral Epidemiology. 2023 Feb;51(1):91-102.
DOI: 10.1111/cdoe.12817
PMID: 36749671

ABSTRACT

Oral health behaviour change interventions are gaining momentum globally; after lagging other disciplines, the field is developing fast. Theories from medicine/healthcare are now being applied to oral health behaviour change with varying results. Despite the importance of using theories, the variety/variation of theories creates dilemmas and potential hazards. Some theories like COM-B (Capability, Opportunity, Motivation–Behaviour) and frameworks like the Behaviour Change Wheel represent opportunities for oral-health behaviour change with careful consideration vital. Different methodological approaches to intervention development are used, with many opportunities. CO-DESIGNING and co-developing interventions with intended users/stakeholders from the start is an important component of successful, effective interventions — one oral-health interventions must consistently implement. Technology-based approaches are a major delivery vehicle. With multiple options for designing/developing/delivering interventions, careful selection of appropriate, user-inclusive, adaptable approaches is essential; the field should reflect on lessons from other disciplines while maximizing available theories, frameworks, methodologies, techniques.
