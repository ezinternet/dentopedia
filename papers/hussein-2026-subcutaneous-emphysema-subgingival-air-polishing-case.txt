Subcutaneous Emphysema Following Subgingival Polishing Using an Air Polishing System With Subgingival Nozzle: A Case Report

Hussein et al. International Journal of Dental Hygiene. 2026. DOI: 10.1111/idh.70083. PMID: 42071315.

ABSTRACT (abstract-only — full text not retrieved)

Subcutaneous facial emphysema (SFE) is a rare but potentially serious complication of dental procedures involving pressurised air; historically air-driven handpieces, more recently air-polishing systems. Reports involving subgingival air-polishing nozzles in periodontal pockets are limited. Case: SFE after air-polishing in a deep periodontal pocket — glycine-based air-powder abrasion used in a 5 mm deep pocket for biofilm eradication; periorbital and right-cheek swelling arose. Hospitalisation for surveillance; antibiotics given to control microbial spread. Discharged stable after 3 days, no further complications. Message: SFE may occur during subgingival air polishing even in supportive periodontal therapy and at sites without BOP or suppuration; safety should not be generalised across devices/protocols; careful case selection, strict device-specific instructions, early recognition of swelling/crepitus essential.
