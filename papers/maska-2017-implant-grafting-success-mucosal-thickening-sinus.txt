Dental implants and grafting success remain high despite large variations in maxillary sinus mucosal thickening

[Full text retrieved from PMC5243237 - content summarized from PMC retrieval]
Background: Although mucosal thickening is the most common radiographic finding regarding sinus pathology, knowledge regarding its clinical significance on outcomes of dental implants and grafting is limited.
Methods: Retrospective CBCT analysis of 29 cases, University of Michigan 2004-2014. Mucosal thickening measured at multiple sites using sagittal views.
Results: 100% implant survival rate. 93.1% had mucosal thickening; 65.5% severe (>5mm). Periodontal disease history was the only significant factor (p=0.004). No association with gender, age, smoking, ridge height.
Conclusions: Physiologic mucosal thickening in varied ranges is not associated with implant or grafting failure.
