Title: Clinical characteristics of osteonecrosis of the jaw related to denosumab: a 5-year retrospective cohort study
Authors: Keuk-Je Cho, Ming-Xu Jin, So-Young Choi, Tae-Geon Kwon
Journal: J Korean Assoc Oral Maxillofac Surg (JKAOMS)
Year: 2026
Volume/Issue: 52(1):18-26
DOI: 10.5125/jkaoms.2026.52.1.18
PMID: 41755379
Published: 2026-02-28
Institution: Kyungpook National University Dental Hospital (KNUDH), Daegu, Korea
IRB: KNUDH-2024-11-04-00
Source URL: https://www.jkaoms.org/journal/view.html?doi=10.5125%2Fjkaoms.2026.52.1.18

ABSTRACT:
Objectives: This study aimed to investigate the clinical characteristics, treatment outcomes, and factors influencing treatment success in patients with denosumab (Dmab)-related osteonecrosis of the jaw (DRONJ). 
Patients and Methods: This retrospective cohort study included patients diagnosed with DRONJ and treated between August 2019 and August 2024. Patients divided into three groups: Group 1 (low-dose Dmab, Prolia 60mg/6mo), Group 2 (BP→low-dose Dmab transition), Group 3 (high-dose Dmab, Xgeva 120mg/4wk). Treatment success = complete + partial healing.
Results: 178 DRONJ patients. Most DRONJ occurred in osteoporosis patients. Prior BP use resulted in earlier DRONJ onset (16.0 vs 23.2 months after Dmab start, P<0.001) but did not affect severity or outcomes. Overall treatment success: 81.5% (G1: 85.0%; G2: 82.8%; G3: 53.8%, P=0.027). Dmab dosage was the only significant predictor of success (OR 5.13; 95%CI 1.19-22.14; P=0.028).
Conclusion: High-dose Dmab demonstrated significantly poorer prognosis and more frequent recurrence than low-dose Dmab or BP-to-Dmab transition.

DEMOGRAPHICS:
- n=178 (193 eligible, 15 excluded)
- Group 1 (Prolia 60mg): n=60, 33.7%
- Group 2 (BP→Prolia): n=105, 59.0%
- Group 3 (Xgeva 120mg): n=13, 7.3%
- Mean age: G1 77.4±6.7yr; G2 76.6±7.2yr; G3 63.5±8.9yr (P<0.001)
- Female: 86.0%
- Osteoporosis: 92.7%

DMAB DURATION BEFORE MRONJ:
- Group 1: 23.2±15.3 months
- Group 2: 16.0±11.3 months (P<0.001 vs G1)
- Group 3: 27.9±18.8 months
- Prior BP duration (Group 2): 56.7±46.4 months before Dmab switch

PRECIPITATING FACTORS:
- Tooth extraction: 45.5% (most common)
- Periodontal disease: 14.0%
- Peri-implantitis: 12.4%
- Denture-related trauma: 9.6%
- Implant surgery: 6.2%
- Apical lesions: 2.2%
- Idiopathic: 9.0%

STAGING (2022 AAOMS):
- Stage 1: 18.0% (n=31)
- Stage 2: 60.7% (n=108)
- Stage 3: 21.3% (n=38)

LOCATION:
- Mandible: 60.1% (posterior mandible 49.5%)
- Maxilla: 34.8%
- Both jaws: 5.1%
- Single-site: 88.7%, Multi-site: 11.3%

TREATMENT MODALITY:
- Conservative: 18.0% (n=32)
- Surgical (sequestrectomy/curettage): 81.6% (n=146)

OUTCOMES:
- Complete healing: 138/178 = 77.5%
- Partial healing: 7 = 3.9%
- No healing: 10 = 5.6%
- Recurrence: 23 = 12.9%
- Overall treatment success (complete+partial): 81.5%
  - Group 1 (Prolia): 85.0%
  - Group 2 (BP→Prolia): 82.8%
  - Group 3 (Xgeva): 53.8% (P=0.027)
- Surgical vs conservative complete healing: 78.8% vs 71.9% (P=0.657, not significant)
- Recurrence by group: G1 11.7%, G2 12.4%, G3 23.1%

LOGISTIC REGRESSION (predictors of treatment success):
- Dmab dosage: significant (OR 5.13; 95%CI 1.19-22.14; P=0.028)
- Age, treatment duration, lesion location, MRONJ stage, treatment modality: NOT significant
