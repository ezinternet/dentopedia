[ABSTRACT-ONLY — paywalled, no PMC OA] Baneshi M, O'Malley L, El-Angbawi A, Thiruvenkatachari B. Effectiveness of clear orthodontic aligners in correcting malocclusions: a systematic review and meta-analysis. J Evid Based Dent Pract 2024;25(1):102081. DOI 10.1016/j.jebdp.2024.102081.

SR+MA of RCTs only (RoB 2). 600 records -> 21 RCTs, 970 participants, all comparing clear aligners vs fixed appliances. 3 high RoB, 14 unclear, 1 low. Primary outcome (treatment accuracy: ABO objective grading, Little Irregularity Index, PAR): no significant difference between CA and FA. Secondary outcomes (meta-analyzed): plaque index MD -0.76 (95% CI -1.14, -0.38); gingival index MD -0.61 (-0.78, -0.44); bleeding index MD -0.71 (-0.92, -0.49) all favoring CA. QoL at 6 months better for CA (MD -4.37; -6.93, -1.80). CA also better for chairside time, pain, root resorption. Overall evidence quality low (periodontal) to moderate (QoL). Both work well for simple non-extraction malocclusions; more high-quality studies needed for complex cases.
