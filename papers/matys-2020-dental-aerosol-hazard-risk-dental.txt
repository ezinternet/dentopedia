Dental Aerosol as a Hazard Risk for Dental Workers
Matys J, Grzech-Leśniak K.
Materials (Basel). 2020 Nov 12;13(22):5109. doi:10.3390/ma13225109
PMID: 33198307  PMCID: PMC7697028
Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7697028/
(Full text retrieved from PubMed Central via PubMed MCP.)

== ABSTRACT ==
Standard dental procedures, when using a water coolant and rotary instruments, generate aerosols with a significantly higher number of various dangerous pathogens (viruses, bacteria, and fungi). Reducing aerosols to a minimum is mandatory, especially during COVID-19. The study aimed to evaluate the amount of aerosol generated during standard dental procedures such as caries removal (using dental bur on a high- and low-speed handpiece and Er:YAG laser), ultrasonic scaling, and tooth polishing (using silicone rubber on low-speed handpiece) combined with various suction systems. Airborne aerosols (0.3–10.0 μm) were measured using the PC200 laser particle counter (Trotec GmbH) at three sites: manikin, operator, and assistant mouth. Suction systems: saliva ejector, high volume evacuator (HVE), saliva ejector + extraoral vacuum, HVE + extraoral vacuum, Zircevacuator (Mr. Thirsty One-Step®), and two customized HVEs (white and black). Caries removal with a high-speed handpiece and saliva ejector generated the highest amount of spray particles at each site. The aerosol at the manikin mouth was highest during caries removal with the low- and high-speed handpiece. The new HVE (black) and the Zircevacuator showed the lowest increase in aerosol level during high-speed caries removal. The Er:YAG laser produced the lowest aerosol amount at the manikin level. Ultrasonic scaling caused a minimal aerosol rise. The Er:YAG laser and the new wider HVEs significantly improved suction efficiency, improving biological safety in the dental office during COVID-19.

== FULL TEXT (key sections) ==

INTRODUCTION
Dental professionals are among the workers most exposed to coronavirus family infection (incl. SARS-CoV-2). Primary transmission: direct mucous membrane (eyes/nose/oral cavity) contact with contagious respiratory bioaerosol. Dental aerosol with the most considerable concentration is visible when using all rotary instruments (high- and low-speed handpieces), air abrasion, or ultrasonic scalers. Sources of airborne contamination: saliva, dental instrumentation, respiratory sources, operative site. Aerosol = liquid/solid particles ≤50 μm suspended in air; splatter = water droplets >50 μm. Particles 0.5–10 μm can be inhaled and held in terminal bronchioles/alveoli. Three forms of dental-office aerosol: (1) contagious respiratory aerosol; (2) pure water-air mist from rotary instruments/scalers/piezo/air-abrasion; (3) mixture of diluted aerosol spray with respiratory bioaerosol. Lasers (esp. Er:YAG, 2940 nm, high water absorption) used for caries removal, endodontic irrigation, periodontal/implant, debonding. Aim: evaluate aerosol generation during caries removal, scaling, and polishing using different suction systems; assess effect of Er:YAG laser and external vacuum on aerosol reduction.

MATERIALS AND METHODS
Three-dimensional manikin with natural tooth (FDI #35). Aerosol measured at three sites: (1) next to open mouth (manikin, sensor at 2 cm), (2) dental operator mouth (40 cm), (3) dental assistant mouth (40 cm). PC200 laser particle counter, six particle sizes (0.3, 0.5, 1.0, 2.5, 5.0, 10.0 µm) summed to total. Each site measured six times; sensor turned on 60 s after treatment start. Office air standardized (AC off, windows closed) to a stable 28,000–30,000 particle baseline; air purifier (NV1050, Novaerus, 800 m³/h) used to reset (~5 min for 20 m³ office). Suction systems: saliva ejector EM15, HVE EM19 EVO, each ± extraoral vacuum (MAcURAY PRO), Zirc evacuator Mr. Thirsty One-step, customized white HVE, customized black HVE. Procedures conducted WITHOUT rubber dam. Statistics: ANOVA + post-hoc Tukey, Statistica; p<0.05 significant.

RESULTS
3.1 High-speed handpiece: aerosol at manikin mouth significantly higher than operator/assistant during caries removal with saliva ejector, saliva ejector+extraoral, HVE alone, HVE+extraoral (p<0.001). New black HVE and Zircevacuator showed lowest increase, with no significant difference between sites; both new HVEs + Zircevacuator significantly reduced aerosol vs conventional suction (p<0.001).
3.2 Low-speed handpiece: caries removal with low-speed bur + saliva ejector and classic HVE showed highest aerosol at manikin mouth and significant decrease at operator/assistant (p<0.001). HVE decreased aerosol ~4× (manikin) and ~3× (operator) vs saliva ejector. Caries removal with a low-speed handpiece produced significantly LOWER aerosol particles than a high-speed turbine.
3.3 Er:YAG laser: with classic HVE generated significantly less aerosol than high-speed handpiece (all suction combos) and low-speed handpiece during caries removal — manikin 28.6 ± 1.3, operator 29.2 ± 0.8, assistant 29.0 ± 1.1 (p<0.001).
3.4 Tooth polishing (silicone rubber, low-speed contra-angle): generated the HIGHEST aerosol particle number vs other procedures. Manikin aerosol significantly higher than operator/assistant at 1000 and 10,000 RPM (p<0.001). Increasing RPM from 1000→10,000 with HVE significantly reduced manikin aerosol vs saliva ejector (p<0.001).
3.5 Ultrasonic scaling: produced lower aerosol than caries removal (rotary handpieces) and low-speed polishing with saliva ejector/HVE (p<0.001). HVE significantly decreased aerosol at manikin (p=0.005) and operator (p=0.003) vs saliva ejector.

DISCUSSION
Working high- and low-speed handpieces pose a special risk, increasing transmission of pathogens in a bioaerosol/water-spray mixture. Caries removal with a high-speed handpiece + saliva ejector generated the highest spray particles at each site. New black HVE and Zirc evacuator showed lowest increase during high-speed caries removal. Er:YAG produced lowest aerosol. Ultrasonic scaling minimal rise. Aerosols <50 µm remain airborne for a prolonged time; more particles → higher infection risk. Saliva ejectors poorly remove aerosolized particles vs HVEs (wider tips, up to ~90% removal, but must be close to tooth). Customized wider evacuators removed 2× and 8× more aerosols vs HVE and saliva ejector respectively during high-speed caries removal. Extraoral evacuators added ~33% reduction. Zirc evacuator 88% reduction (32.1 ± 2.0) vs HVE (60.5 ± 3.0). Conducted without rubber dam (rubber dam limits water-air spray contact with mucosa but not always feasible). Ultrasonic scaling lower aerosol potential than rotary caries removal. Er:YAG explosion effect limited range → lower aerosol; disadvantage is device cost; wider HVEs are an inexpensive efficient alternative.

CONCLUSIONS
The most intensive dental aerosol occurs with a high-speed handpiece or ultrasonic scaler. Aerosol amount during caries removal, polishing, and scaling is strictly connected with the method/device and significantly with the suction device. HVEs remove a significant amount of aerosol; wider customized HVEs are most efficient. Er:YAG laser caries removal is characterized by low aerosol generation. Saliva ejector alone is not recommended during silicone-rubber polishing, ultrasonic scaling, or conventional caries treatment.
