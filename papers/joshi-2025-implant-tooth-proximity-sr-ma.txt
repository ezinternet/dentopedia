Joshi NV, Rao P, Talreja P, Joshi M, Shetty AR, Sachdev SS, Khan S.
The Clinical Impact of Dental Implant Placement in Close Proximity to Natural Teeth: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis.
Cureus. 2025 Dec 12;17(12):e99077. doi:10.7759/cureus.99077. PMID:41531572 PMCID:PMC12791176

[FULL TEXT — PMC12791176]

OBJECTIVE
Evaluate clinical/radiographic outcomes when implants are placed <1.5 mm from adjacent natural teeth (or direct root contact).

METHODS
- PROSPERO ID: CRD42024499790
- Databases: PubMed, Cochrane, Scopus, Web of Science, ScienceDirect, Google Scholar, Semantic Scholar
- Search: inception to October 2025
- Included: human observational studies, case-control, cohort, case series documenting proximity-related outcomes
- Excluded: animal, in vitro, narrative reviews, no distance data
- n=11 studies (8 observational + 3 case series); 1,727 participants; >2,000 implants
- Follow-up: 6–75 months
- Quality: NOS (7–8/9 for all observational) + JBI for case series (8–10/10)
- Meta-analysis: random-effects model (DerSimonian-Laird)

KEY RESULTS

1. Pulpal/periapical pathology:
   - Kucukkurt 2020: 20.5% of teeth touching implant required RCT; 2.3% extracted; implant survival 96.4%
   - Hamdoon: 3 teeth developed periapical lesions after drill contact → RCT saved all
   - Tasharoie: <1 mm proximity → NO pulpal loss of vitality
   - Yi (Korea): <1% root injury rate; no extraction needed

2. Marginal bone/peri-implant:
   - Ng 2018: <1 mm → increased BOP and interproximal bone loss (BL implants)
   - Kucukkurt: peri-implantitis bone loss in several sites

3. Caries/structural:
   - Smith: 69 sites with approximal/cervical/root caries (especially posterior)
   - Tagger-Green: secondary caries most common complication; also tooth cracks and mobility

4. Implant survival: >95% in all studies

QUANTITATIVE SYNTHESIS
- Pooled proportion: 0.58 (95% CI 0.55–0.62) for any proximity-related clinical or radiographic alteration
- Pooled effect size: 0.11 (95% CI 0.03–0.22) — low but measurable risk
- Heterogeneity: I²=95% (substantial)

GRADE certainty:
- Pulpal/bone outcomes: MODERATE
- Caries/structural: LOW
- Implant survival: HIGH

CONCLUSIONS
- <1.5 mm implant-to-tooth distance may cause localized biological damage (pulpal, periapical, bone loss, caries)
- Does NOT significantly compromise implant longevity (survival >95%)
- Severity depends more on direction/depth of drill than distance alone
- CBCT-guided osteotomy and ≥1.5 mm safety margin are essential

CLINICAL RELEVANCE FOR SPACING DECISIONS
- Minimum safety distance = 1.5 mm (implant surface to root surface)
- In practice: root-to-implant center = 1.5 mm + implant radius → e.g., Ø4.0 mm implant = center must be ≥3.5 mm from root
- Violations → most commonly pulpal damage and secondary caries (not implant failure)
