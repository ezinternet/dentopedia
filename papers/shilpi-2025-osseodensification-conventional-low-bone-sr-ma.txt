Comparing the Efficacy of Osseodensification Over Conventional Drilling Technique on Implant Stability and Bone Density in Low Bone Density Sites: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis. J Evid Based Dent Pract. 2025 Jun;25(2):102132. doi: 10.1016/j.jebdp.2025.102132. Epub 2025 Mar 4. PMID: 40335187.

[Abstract only — full text not retrieved; paywalled journal]

ABSTRACT: To assess the efficacy of osseodensification (OD) over conventional drilling technique (CD) on implant stability and bone density in low bone density sites. An electronic and manual search were conducted to analyze the effect of OD over CD technique on implant stability and bone density in human-based randomized controlled trials (RCTs) and nonrandomized controlled trials (NRCTs). The risk of bias was assessed using (RoB 2.0) and (ROBINS-I) tools for RCTs and NRCTs respectively. The meta-analysis was applied with RevMan 5.4, using the random-fixed effects model. Heterogeneity was assessed by a Q test and quantified with I² statistics. Our literature search identified 1,454 publications, of which only 6 met all the inclusion criteria. The meta-analysis showed that ISQ was greater in OD than in the CD group without being statistically significant both immediately (SMD 2.13 [95%CI = -0.08, 4.35], P = .06) and at follow-up (SMD 1.81 [95%CI = -0.41, 4.03], P = .11). The difference in bone density in OD vs CD was statistically significant immediately after placement (SMD 2.14 [95%CI = 0.68, 3.59], P = .004) and nonsignificant at 3–7 months (SMD 1.54 [95%CI = -0.34, 3.43], P = .11). The findings show that OD reveals greater implant stability and improved bone density in areas with low bone density compared to CD. However, more clinical studies are needed.
