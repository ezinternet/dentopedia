Virtual bone-implant contact surface effect on implant stability in fresh extraction and healed sites: a pilot randomised clinical trial
Askin Ekinci S, Fettahzade S, Gocmen G, Bayram F, Ozkan Y
Scientific Reports 16(1), 2026. DOI: 10.1038/s41598-026-48697-8 | PMID: 41974820 | PMCID: PMC13230908

ABSTRACT
This study aimed to assess the effect of bone-implant contact surface area (BIC) on implant stability at fresh extraction sites without grafting, at simultaneous grafting, and at healed implant sites using a novel digital workflow that combines preoperative CBCT segmentation with postoperative intraoral scanning. A randomized controlled trial was conducted in 30 patients requiring dental implants in the upper premolar or molar regions. Group I received immediate implant placement with grafts, Group II received immediate implant placement without grafts, and Group III followed the standard implant placement protocol for healed edentulous regions. All implants placed by same surgeon, same implant system, same surgical protocol. BIC and virtual non-contact surfaces (BINC) calculated using digital registration. Final analysis included 28 patients (10 males, 18 females). Group III showed better primary stability and insertion torque than immediate placement groups (P=0.014 and P=0.007). No statistically significant difference between Group I and Group II in insertion torque or ISQ. Intraoperative ISQ and ISQ-60 days strongly correlated with BIC only in Group II (r=0.62, P=0.03 and r=0.58, P=0.05). Group I and Group II similar primary stability; Group III greater; BIC meaningful indicator of early mechanical stability in Group II.

(Full text retrieved via PubMed Central. Key extracted numbers:
- n=30 enrolled, 28 final (Group I immediate+graft, Group II immediate no graft, Group III healed). Upper premolar/molar, single missing tooth, intact socket walls, ≥4mm apical bone.
- Implants: aggressive-thread tapered screw-type Ti, SLA surface (DXL GmbH). Freehand, no surgical guide. Drill extended 2-3mm apical for primary stability. Insertion 40 Ncm 20 rpm, then manual torque ratchet.
- BIC/BINC digital workflow: preop CBCT segmented (Slicer 5.4.0), 3D STL overlaid on postop intraoral scan (3Shape Trios) with scanbody + implant STL; surface areas measured in Meshmixer; ROI math yields BIC and BINC. ISQ by Osstell RFA, ≥3 repeats, highest value.
- ISQ measured intraop, 60d (healing cap placement, ~2mo), 90d (before loading, ~3mo).
- Sample size: G-Power, alpha 0.05, effect size 0.82, power 95% → 27, expanded to 30.
- Results table: Insertion torque (Ncm): GI 32.0±4.2, GII 36.3±7.1, GIII 41.1±6.8. Intraop ISQ: GI 65.0±7.6, GII 62.0±10.0, GIII 71.6±6.2. BIC (mm2): GI 206.2±30.3, GII 206.8±48.2, GIII 302.2±64.5. BINC (mm2): GI 101.0±24.6, GII 106.6±22.9, GIII NA.
- BIC GIII > GI (P=0.003) and > GII (P=0.002); no GI-GII diff. Insertion torque GIII > GI (P=0.007). Intraop ISQ GIII > GII (P=0.014).
- Correlations BIC vs: insertion torque strong in GII (r=0.66, P=0.02), NS in GI/GIII. Intraop ISQ in GII (r=0.62, P=0.03), NS GI. ISQ-60 in GII (r=0.58, P=0.05), NS GI/GIII. ISQ-90 NS in all subgroups; pooled all implants BIC-ISQ90 moderate r=0.44, P=0.01.
- Discussion notes: Jun et al — graft-related ISQ gain greater when baseline ISQ ≤65. Ko et al — BIC affects ISQ in immediate+graft. Bony gaps <2mm may not heal spontaneously. Time increased stability across all groups regardless of graft.
- Limitations: short follow-up (60/90d = early healing, osseointegration incomplete); single posterior maxilla missing tooth; virtual measurement not a histology substitute; bone mineral density not directly evaluated; pilot, small per-group n.
- Conclusion: immediate-with-graft and immediate-without-graft similar primary stability; healed sites significantly greater. BIC meaningful indicator of early mechanical stability in immediate WITHOUT grafting (GII); in immediate WITH grafting, grafting does not contribute to early mechanical stability and stability influenced by factors beyond native bone contact. Novel digital workflow (CBCT segmentation + postop IOS, no surgical guide) for non-invasive BIC estimation.)
