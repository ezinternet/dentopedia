Predictive mathematical modeling of biomechanical behavior in all-on-4 implants design: effects of distal implant and occlusal load angulation using RSM based on FEA

Authors: Murat F, Sevinç Gül SN, Şensoy AT
Journal: Frontiers in Bioengineering and Biotechnology
Year: 2025
DOI: 10.3389/fbioe.2025.1644776
PMID: 40901251
PMCID: PMC12399523

ABSTRACT:
This study presents a predictive biomechanical modeling approach for the All-on-4 treatment concept, focusing on implant angulation and occlusal load direction. FEA integrated with RSM (Response Surface Methodology): 15 simulation models via Central Composite Design. Distal implant angulations: 15°, 30°, 45°. Occlusal load directions: 45°, 67.5°, 90° (both sagittal AP and frontal BL planes). Maximum von Mises stress in cortical bone = response variable.

KEY FINDINGS:
- Frontal load angle (BL direction) = most significant effect (29.83%, p=0.003)
- Implant angulation = 2nd most significant (26.96%, p=0.005)
- Sagittal load angle = NOT significant linearly (p=0.681)
- Significant interaction: sagittal × frontal load angles (p=0.012)
- R² = 93.39%; adjusted R² = 81.49% (strong model fit)

STRESS RESULTS:
- Minimum cortical stress: 95.75 MPa → Experiment 1 (15° implant, 45° sagittal, 45° frontal)
- Maximum cortical stress: 265.72 MPa → Experiment 3 (45° implant, 45° sagittal, 90° frontal)
- Cortical bone consistently highest stress vs trabecular bone, prosthesis, bar
- Strain concentrations at implant neck in Experiments 2 and 15: 3,654–3,940 µɛ (approaching pathological remodeling threshold)
- Posterior implant (Implant 1) peak stress: 397.04 MPa; peak strain: 3,940 µɛ

MECHANISTIC INSIGHTS:
- Reducing implant tilt generally decreases peri-implant cortical stress
- Frontal (buccolingual) oblique loading is more detrimental than sagittal oblique loading
- 45° sagittal load direction → beneficial (reduces bending moment from occlusal loading)
- 90° frontal load + high implant angle = worst-case stress scenario
- No universally optimal angle — outcome depends on interaction of implant tilt × load direction
- Cortical and trabecular bone behave differently: cortical absorbs most stress; trabecular buffers

FEA MODEL:
- 3D edentulous mandible, Nobel Active 4.3 × 13 mm implants
- All-on-4: 2 anterior (axial, canine region) + 2 posterior (15°/30°/45° distal tilt)
- Cantilever: 5 mm in all models (held constant)
- Load: 200 N static, applied to distal molar crown
- Cortical bone: 2 mm uniform thickness; trabecular core
- Materials: homogeneous, isotropic, linear elastic
- ANSYS 2024R2; ~1.1 million nodes; skewness <0.29

CLINICAL TAKE:
- Minimize distal implant tilt (15° optimal within 15–45° range)
- Control occlusal load direction: reduce lateral (BL) forces via cusp design, occlusal scheme
- Optimize cusp inclination and load direction together with implant positioning
- Further clinical validation needed before definitive angle recommendation
