What is health economics?

Listl S, Grytten JI, Birch S.
Community Dent Health. 2019 Nov 28;36(4):262-274.
doi: 10.1922/CDH_4581Listl13
PMID: 31664797

ABSTRACT (abstract-only — full text not retrieved)

Describes the principles of economics and their application to the promotion, protection and restoration of oral health in populations and the planning, management and delivery of oral health care. After illustrating the economic determinants of oral health, the DEMAND for oral health care is discussed with particular reference to ASYMMETRIC INFORMATION between patient and provider. The reasons for MARKET FAILURE in (oral) health care and implications for efficiency and equity are explained. Describes how economic evaluation contributes to policies aimed at maximising oral health gains where resources are constrained. The BEHAVIOURAL aspects of patients' demand for and dental professionals' provision of oral health services are discussed. Finally, outlines methods for planning the dental workforce in ways that reflect system goals.
