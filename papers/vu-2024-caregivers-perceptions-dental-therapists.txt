Caregivers' Perceptions of Dental Therapists.
Vu VV, McDonough R, Chandwani N, Discepolo K.
J Dent Child (Chic). 2024;91(2):90-98.
PMID: 39123337

ABSTRACT (abstract-only — full text not retrieved):

To investigate caregivers' understanding of midlevel providers and whether
there is theoretical acceptance of midlevel dental therapist (DT) integration
into the dental workforce. Validated DT-integration surveys were disseminated
to caregivers of pediatric medical patients in an urban teaching hospital;
subjects reported previous care by a midlevel medical provider and were
questioned on acceptance of a midlevel dental provider (DT). 202 questionnaires
evaluated: 71.2% recalled previous midlevel (ML) medical care; 28.7% were
unaware (UML). MLs were significantly in agreement with DTs performing primary
tooth restorations (p<0.001), permanent tooth restorations (p=0.001), primary
tooth extractions (p=0.022), and emergency treatment (p<0.001). Both UML and ML
significantly agreed procedures should be completed under the supervision of a
dentist (p<0.001). Conclusion: caregivers of pediatric medical patients are
receptive to DTs and largely agree with them performing dental procedures, but
dissemination of information about DTs is needed to increase public awareness of
their education, scope of practice, and workforce integration.
