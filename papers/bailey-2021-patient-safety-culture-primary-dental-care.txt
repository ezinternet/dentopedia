Developing a Patient Safety Culture in Primary Dental Care.

Bailey E, Dungarwalla M.
Prim Dent J. 2021 Mar;10(1):89-95.
doi: 10.1177/2050168420980990
PMID: 33722142

ABSTRACT

Patient safety should be at the heart of any healthcare service; systems, teams, individuals and environments must work in tandem for safety/quality. Research into patient safety in dentistry is still early; most originates from secondary care and academia, yet ~95% of dental care is provided in primary care. This paper provides an overview of the evidence base for patient safety in dentistry and discusses: human factors; best practice; the SECOND VICTIM concept; potential for over-regulation; and creating a patient safety culture. Through these concepts it aims to give the reader tools to develop a patient safety culture in practice.
