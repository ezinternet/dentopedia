Teamwork and patient safety in dynamic domains of healthcare: a review of the literature.

Manser T.
Acta Anaesthesiologica Scandinavica. 2009 Feb;53(2):143-51.
PMID: 19032571
DOI: 10.1111/j.1399-6576.2008.01717.x

ABSTRACT (abstract-only — full text not retrieved)

Reviews research on teamwork in highly dynamic domains of healthcare (operating rooms, intensive care, emergency medicine, trauma/resuscitation) focusing on quality and safety of patient care. Evidence from three areas supports the teamwork–patient-safety relationship:

(1) Studies of factors contributing to critical incidents and adverse events show that teamwork plays an important role both in the causation and the prevention of such events.

(2) Research on providers' perceptions shows that staff perceptions of teamwork and attitudes toward safety-relevant team behavior relate to quality and safety of patient care, and that perceptions of teamwork and leadership style associate with staff well-being, which may in turn impact the ability to provide safe patient care.

(3) Observational studies of teamwork behaviors related to high clinical performance identify patterns of communication, coordination, and leadership that support effective teamwork.

A challenge for future research is to develop and validate team-performance assessment instruments and sound theoretical models that integrate findings from the three areas, in order to improve team training and to design clinical work systems supporting effective teamwork and safe patient care.
