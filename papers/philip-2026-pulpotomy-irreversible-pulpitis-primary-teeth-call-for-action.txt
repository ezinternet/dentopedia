Title: Pulpotomy for treating primary teeth with irreversible pulpitis: a call for action. Journal: Eur Arch Paediatr Dent 2026. (Narrative review.)
Aim: assimilate evidence for pulpotomy as a treatment option for vital primary teeth clinically diagnosed with irreversible pulpitis.
Methods: structured search MEDLINE/Embase/WoS/Cochrane; clinical studies + systematic reviews of pulpotomy outcomes in primary teeth with irreversible pulpitis.
Results: 7 clinical studies + 2 meta-analyses included. Across clinical studies, clinical success 95-100% and radiographic success 90-100% at 6-month to 1-year follow-up. The meta-analyses concluded pulpotomy may be a viable alternative to conventional pulpectomy for irreversible pulpitis in primary teeth. New diagnostic considerations and procedural guidelines proposed.
Conclusion: accumulating histological + clinical evidence supports pulpotomy as a successful, minimally invasive option for primary teeth with clinical symptoms of irreversible pulpitis; calls for revision of international guidelines to reduce unnecessary invasive interventions.
