Couso-Queiruga E, Ramseier CA, Chappuis V, Janner SFMJ, Buser D, Brägger U, Salvi GE.
Impact of Marginal Misfit in Implant-Supported Fixed Dental Prostheses on Peri-Implant Bone Levels: A Retrospective Quantitative Analysis.
Clin Oral Implants Res. 2025 Oct;37(1):69-78. doi:10.1111/clr.70053. PMID:41046331 PMCID:PMC12767562

[FULL TEXT — PMC12767562]

OBJECTIVE
Evaluate the impact of marginal fit of implant-supported prostheses (ISP) on peri-implant bone levels. Determine a clinically relevant threshold for radiographic vertical misfit gap at ISP. Identify risk factors.

METHODS
- Design: Retrospective, single-center (University of Bern), STROBE guidelines
- Implants placed 1997–2001; standardized periapical radiographs at 10yr post-loading
- n=301 patients, 505 implants; mean follow-up 10.6 ± 0.7 years
- All soft-tissue level implants (Straumann) — 3.3/4.1/4.8 mm diameter; 6–14 mm length
- Outcome: DIB (distance between implant shoulder and most coronal crestal bone)
- Gap categorization: GapCAT (present/absent), GapDIM (0 mm / >0–<0.1 mm / ≥0.1 mm)
- Analysis: multivariable linear mixed-effects model

RESULTS
Radiographic findings:
- Mean DIB: 3.3 ± 0.7 mm overall
- GapCAT present: 249 implants (49.3%)
- Implants with GapDIM ≥ 0.1 mm: 159

DIB by gap category:
- No gap: 3.22 ± 0.8 mm
- Gap present: 3.43 ± 0.6 mm (P=0.001)
- Gap >0–<0.1 mm: 3.36 ± 0.5 mm (P=0.001)
- Gap ≥ 0.1 mm: 3.45 ± 0.7 mm (P=0.001)
- Each 0.1 mm increase in gap → +0.08 mm DIB increase (P=0.03)

Multivariable model significant predictors of increased DIB:
- Smoking: estimate +0.36 mm (P=0.0019)
- History of periodontitis: estimate +0.16 mm (P=0.001)
- GapDIM: estimate +0.04 mm (P=0.28) → NOT significant in multivariate after confounders

Protective factor:
- Transcrestal sinus floor elevation: estimate −0.54 mm (P<0.001)

NOT significant: neck height, implant length, bone grafting, diabetes, GapDIM (in multivariate)

CONCLUSIONS
1. ISPs without radiographic gaps exhibit lower DIB than those with gaps (3.22 vs 3.43 mm, P=0.001)
2. Gaps ≥ 0.1 mm are associated with significantly greater bone loss
3. Each 0.1 mm gap increment → +0.08 mm DIB increase
4. In multivariate model, GapDIM itself did not remain significant — confounders (smoking, periodontitis) dominate
5. Smoking and periodontitis history are the strongest independent risk factors for bone loss

CLINICAL INTERPRETATION
The gap at the ISP interface (misfit) affects bone stability. The clinical threshold is ≥0.1 mm: below this, the clinical impact is minimal. Above this, bone loss is measurably higher.
However: misfit likely acts through a BIOLOGICAL pathway (biofilm reservoir at the gap → inflammation → bone loss) rather than purely biomechanical stress — consistent with the finding that GapDIM lost significance when biological risk factors were controlled.
