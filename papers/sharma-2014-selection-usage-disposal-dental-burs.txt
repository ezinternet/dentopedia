== ABSTRACT (abstract-only; full text not retrieved) ==
Purpose: Cross-sectional questionnaire study to evaluate local practitioners' knowledge of dental bur selection, usage, sterilization, and disposal in restorative procedures.
Methods: 20-question proforma (4 answers each) in 3 categories — selection, usage/sterilization, disposal — mailed to practitioners with telephone follow-up. Results expressed as percentages.
Results: 131/150 responded (87%). Most preferred DIAMOND burs (75%) over TUNGSTEN CARBIDE (15%). Most dentists used burs until worn out (85%); 33% agreed cutting efficiency decreases with use. Sterilization once daily (35%) or per patient (35%). Nearly all (100%) discarded worn burs into the dustbin.
Conclusion: Diamond burs predominated the choice for cutting efficiency irrespective of grit size; burs were reused until worn out, and minimal coolant was used during preparation (operators believed coolant did not affect cutting efficiency).
Keywords: Cross-sectional study; Cutting efficiency; Diamond burs; Tungsten carbide burs.
