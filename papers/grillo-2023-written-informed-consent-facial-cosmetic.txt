Title: The importance of written informed consent in facial cosmetic surgery litigation.
Authors: Grillo R, Brozoski MA, Naclério-Homem MDG
Journal: Journal of Cranio-Maxillofacial Surgery 2023; 51(7-8):403-406
DOI: 10.1016/j.jcms.2023.08.007
PMID: 37591743

Abstract (abstract-only — full text not retrieved):

Aim: discuss litigation related to facial cosmetic surgery focusing on written consent. A 10-year online search for facial cosmetic surgery litigation in Brazil (2012-2022) was performed. 992 litigations were identified; 122 met inclusion criteria. The number of legal disputes has been increasing over the years. Female patients were more prone to litigation. Financial damage ranged from $75 to $500,000 (mean $59,536). Many lawsuits with appropriate written consent had a favorable outcome for the defendant. The primary specialty involved was plastic surgery (67%), followed by maxillofacial surgery (9%). Written consent significantly reduces both litigation losses and financial damage.
