Patient satisfaction surveys in dental school clinics: a review and comparison.

Ebn Ahmady A, Pakkhesal M, Zafarmand AH, Lando HA.
J Dent Educ. 2015 Apr;79(4):388-93.
PMID: 25838009
(DOI not indexed in PubMed)

ABSTRACT

Health care is becoming more patient-centered; patients' experiences and
satisfaction are taken more seriously. Patient satisfaction influences
treatment cooperation, and better cooperation leads to healthier patients
over the long term — this applies in the dental school clinic setting as
well. Dental school clinics' administrators and clinicians should know the
dimensions of patient satisfaction in order to provide the highest-quality
care.

Aim: to review studies (published 1980 through March 2014) in order to
identify the dimensions used to measure patient satisfaction with services
in dental school clinics.

Methods: PubMed was used as the search source; identified dimensions were
categorized. Through several search stages, 41 of 730 articles were
selected; after critical appraisal, nine articles were retained.

Results: Five dimensions of patient satisfaction were identified:
QUALITY, INTERACTION, ACCESS, ENVIRONMENT, and COST.

Conclusion: Determining these dimensions can help dental institutions
provide the highest-quality care.
