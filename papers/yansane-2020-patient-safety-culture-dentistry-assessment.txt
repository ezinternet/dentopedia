Assessing the Patient Safety Culture in Dentistry.

Yansane A, Lee JH, Hebballi N, Obadan-Udoh E, White J, Walji M, Easterday C, Rindal B, Worley D, Kalenderian E.
JDR Clin Trans Res. 2020;5(4):399-408.
PMID: 31923373
DOI: 10.1177/2380084419897614

ABSTRACT (abstract-only — full text not retrieved)

Medical errors are among the leading causes of death in the US, and patients can be harmed during the course of care. A robust culture of safety is therefore imperative. The primary objective of this study was to evaluate the patient safety culture among providers at 4 US dental institutions, comparing the results with those of their medical counterparts (2016).

This was a cross-sectional study using the Medical Office Survey on Patient Safety Culture (MOSPSC), modified for dentistry, administered at 4 US dental institutions in 2016. All dental team members were invited to participate. Of 1,615 invited, 656 providers responded (40.6% response rate).

Medical institutions outperformed the dental institutions on 9 of 10 safety-culture dimensions, 6 of 6 overall quality items, and 8 of 9 patient-safety/quality issues. The surveyed dental institutions had their strongest average percentage-positive scores in ORGANIZATIONAL LEARNING (85%) and TEAMWORK (79%).

Conclusion: Safety culture has progressed over time, but heterogeneity remains within academic dental, private (nonacademic), and medical clinics. Administering the survey in dental clinics is a key measure to identify areas for improvement.
