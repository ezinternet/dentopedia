Lee J, Park JS, Feng B, Wang KN.
The utilisation of teledentistry in Australia: A systematic review and meta-analysis.
Journal of Dentistry. 2024;149:105265.
DOI: 10.1016/j.jdent.2024.105265
PMID: 39059707

ABSTRACT (abstract-only — full text not retrieved)

Teledentistry is the use of information-based technologies to deliver healthcare
remotely — used in regional/rural/remote regions and during COVID-19. This
systematic review and meta-analysis aimed to determine teledentistry utilisation
in Australia. The authors searched PubMed, Google Scholar, EMBASE, and Web of
Science to June 2024; the JBI Critical Appraisal Tools were used for quality/bias
assessment.

Eighteen articles met inclusion: six diagnostic tests, six cross-sectional studies,
four economic evaluations, one qualitative study, one expert opinion.

Teledentistry was accurate for screening caries (average sensitivity = 69.7%,
average specificity = 97.4%); a non-significant negative correlation between
specificity and sensitivity (r = 0.432). Opinions were MIXED from clinicians but
POSITIVE from patients. Teledentistry may lead to savings for patients and
healthcare providers.

Conclusion: teledentistry increases healthcare access especially in
regional/rural/remote areas and is an effective caries-screening tool; clinician
opinions are mixed, with implementation barriers identified.
