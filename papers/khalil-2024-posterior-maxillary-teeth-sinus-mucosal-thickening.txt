The Impact of Posterior Maxillary Teeth on Maxillary Sinus: Insights From Cone-Beam Computed Tomography Analysis

Authors: Khalil et al. (Pharos University, Egypt)
Journal: Cureus 2025
DOI: 10.7759/cureus.76578
PMCID: PMC11774551
Published: 2024/2025

Abstract:
Objective: Evaluate the relationship between maxillary sinus mucosal thickening (MT) and three dental factors: tooth location (premolars/molars), periodontal status, and root proximity to sinus floor (CBCT).

Methods:
- Retrospective cross-sectional study, 124 patients (248 maxillary sinuses, 992 teeth)
- CBCT using Morita 3D units, voxel 0.2mm
- Mucosal thickness graded: Grade 1=0-2mm (normal), Grade 2=2-10mm (moderate), Grade 3=>10mm (severe)
- Root tip proximity (RPMT) classified Types 0-4 (Sharan classification)
- Periodontal bone loss: Type 1 <25%, Type 2 25-50%, Type 3 >50%
- Multivariable logistic regression

Results:
- MT detected in 90 (72.6%) patients; 42 (33.9%) bilateral
- Grade 1 most prevalent (72.5%), Grade 2 (19.2%), Grade 3 (8.4%)
- 46% of teeth had root proximity to sinus floor (Types 1-4)
- 80.2% mild-moderate periodontal bone loss; 82.1% no periapical lesion

Logistic regression (AOR):
- Tooth location: 1st molar AOR=3.97; 2nd molar AOR=2.30; 2nd premolar AOR=1.57 (vs 1st premolar)
- RPMT: Type 2 AOR=2.27; Type 1 AOR=2.24 (vs Type 0)
- Periodontal bone loss: Severe AOR=2.75; Mild-moderate AOR=1.68 (vs non-periodontitis)
- Periapical lesion AOR=32.7; No periapical lesion (vs extracted) AOR=19.8
- Gender: not significant

Conclusions: Second molars, severe periodontal bone loss, and periapical lesions associated with increased sinus MT risk. Interdisciplinary approach (dentists, periodontists, ENT) recommended. Pre-treatment CBCT screening for high-risk cases.

CBCT classification:
- RPMT Type 0: no contact between root apex and sinus cortical borders
- Type 1: root apex in contact with sinus cortical borders, inferiorly curving floor
- Type 2: root apex laterally adjacent to sinus cavity, apex outside sinus
- Type 3: root apex protruding into sinus with inferiorly curving floor
- Type 4: sinus floor enclosing part or all of the root apex
