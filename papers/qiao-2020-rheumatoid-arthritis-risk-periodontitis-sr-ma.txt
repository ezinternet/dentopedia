Rheumatoid arthritis risk in periodontitis patients: A systematic review and meta-analysis.

Authors: Qiao Y, Wang Z, Li Y, Han Y, Zhou Y, Cao X
Journal: Joint Bone Spine 2020;87(6):556-564
PMID 32593704 | DOI 10.1016/j.jbspin.2020.04.024

NOTE: abstract-only — full text not retrieved. Content below is the PubMed abstract.

OBJECTIVE: To evaluate whether periodontitis represents a risk factor for rheumatoid arthritis (RA), given limited/inconsistent prior evidence.

METHODS: PubMed, Cochrane Library, Embase, Web of Science, Wanfang searched for studies comparing periodontitis patients with controls. Pooled odds ratio (OR) with 95% CI for the association between periodontitis and RA.

RESULTS: Thirteen studies, 706,611 periodontitis patients and 349,983 controls. Pooled OR of RA risk between periodontitis and controls = 1.69 (95% CI 1.31-2.17; P<0.0001) — periodontitis patients had 69% greater RA risk. By disease type: incident RA OR 1.70 (95% CI 0.75-3.85, P<0.001); mixed RA OR 1.61 (1.26-2.06; P<0.001). By disease duration: >5 y OR 2.88 (0.66-12.62, P=0.018); <5 y OR 2.59 (0.83-8.11, P<0.001); mixed OR 1.53 (1.05-2.22, P<0.001).

CONCLUSION: Increased risk of RA in patients with periodontitis vs healthy controls. Association is higher for incident RA, and for RA with disease duration >5 years. (Supports the periodontitis–RA bidirectional link / shared inflammatory + citrullination pathway.)
