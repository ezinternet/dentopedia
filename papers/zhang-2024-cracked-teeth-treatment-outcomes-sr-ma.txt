Title: The treatment outcomes of cracked teeth: A systematic review and meta-analysis.
Journal: J Dent 2024;142:104843.

Aim: analyze clinical treatment outcomes of cracked teeth (CT) retaining vital dental pulp (CT-VDP) or undergoing root canal treatment (CT-RCT).
Methods: Medline, Embase, PubMed, Cochrane. Studies of tooth survival rate (TSR), pulp survival rate (PSR), success rate (SR) with >=1-yr follow-up. Risk of bias: Newcastle-Ottawa.
Results: 27 studies qualitative, 26 in MA. SR of monitoring without restorative treatment 80% at 3 yr. CT-VDP: TSR 92.8-97.8% (1-6 yr), PSR 85.6-90.4% (1-3 yr), SR 80.6-89.9% (1-3 yr). CT-RCT: TSR 90.5-91.1% (1-2 yr), SR 83.0-91.2% (1-4 yr). Direct restorations without cuspal coverage for CT-VDP increased risk: pulpal complications RR=3.2 (95% CI 1.51-6.82, p=0.002), extraction RR=8.1 (1.05-62.5, p=0.045) vs full-crown. CT-RCT without full-crown had 11.3-fold higher extraction risk than with full-crown (p<0.001).
Conclusion: monitoring without restoration may be an option for asymptomatic CT. Direct restorations without cuspal coverage significantly increase pulpal complication and extraction risk in CT-VDP. Full-crown restorations strongly recommended for symptomatic CT and CT-RCT.
