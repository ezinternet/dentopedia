Title: Partially demineralized allogeneic cancellous bone plug versus deproteinized bovine bone mineral for guided bone regeneration: a pilot randomized controlled clinical trial
Authors: Siqi Rao, Jing Zhou, Wei Niu, Chenlu Dong, Shijie Pan, Wei Sun
Journal: Clinical Oral Investigations 2026;30(6)
DOI: 10.1007/s00784-026-06914-8
PMID: 42126621 | PMCID: PMC13171694
Source: PubMed Central full text

=== ABSTRACT ===

Objectives
To compare a partially demineralized allogeneic cancellous bone plug (ACBP) with deproteinized bovine bone mineral (DBBM) for guided bone regeneration (GBR), focusing on CBCT-based dimensional outcomes and histomorphometric tissue composition at implant placement.

Materials and methods
In this prospective randomized controlled clinical trial, 25 adults requiring GBR for two common indications—posterior maxillary sinus floor elevation or mandibular posterior extraction socket preservation—were randomized to ACBP (n=12) or DBBM (n=13), with collagen membrane coverage in both groups. Radiographic endpoints were indication-specific and were reported separately for sinus and socket sites. CBCT was obtained at baseline and 6 months to assess augmented height (sinus sites) and ridge dimensions at standardized levels (socket sites). At 6 months (implant placement), a 3-mm trephine core was harvested for H&E staining and histomorphometric quantification of vital bone (VB, %) and residual graft material (RG, %). Between-group comparisons used independent-samples t tests (two-sided P<0.05).

Results
All participants completed the 6-month follow-up. VB did not differ significantly between ACBP and DBBM (38.84 ± 8.37% vs. 32.18 ± 11.04%, P=0.105), whereas RG was significantly lower with ACBP (11.18 ± 5.48% vs. 19.80 ± 8.23%, P=0.006). CBCT outcomes were comparable between groups, with no significant differences in sinus height gain (7.82 ± 1.52 mm vs. 8.21 ± 4.30 mm, P=0.852) or socket-related ridge dimensional changes (all P>0.05).

Conclusion
Within a 6-month healing interval, ACBP showed CBCT-based dimensional outcomes comparable to DBBM and a lower residual graft fraction with a similar vital bone fraction at implant placement, suggesting that the observed difference was more related to graft turnover than to greater bone formation.

Clinical Relevance
At routine implant timing (~6 months), use of ACBP was associated with a lower residual graft fraction at re-entry while maintaining short-term CBCT-based dimensional stability.

=== FULL TEXT ===

Introduction

Insufficient alveolar bone volume remains a key limitation in implant dentistry. Bone loss associated with periodontitis, trauma, or long-term edentulism can compromise primary implant stability, restrict prosthetically driven implant positioning, and reduce the long-term structural support required for predictable outcomes. Guided bone regeneration (GBR) has therefore become a mainstream approach for localized augmentation. By using a barrier membrane to exclude soft-tissue ingrowth and to maintain a secluded space, GBR promotes vascular ingrowth and bone regeneration within a protected compartment. Importantly, the clinical performance of GBR is strongly influenced by the grafting material placed beneath the membrane, which must provide space maintenance while integrating into the host remodeling environment.

From a clinical standpoint, graft materials can be viewed along a continuum between volume stability and biological turnover. A graft that remodels too rapidly may lose contour before sufficient mineralized tissue forms, whereas a graft that remodels too slowly may leave a substantial proportion of residual particles during early to mid-term healing, potentially delaying replacement by vital bone and raising questions regarding long-term tissue dynamics. Although autogenous bone is considered the reference standard due to its osteogenic/osteoinductive/osteoconductive properties, donor-site morbidity and limited availability restrict its routine use. Consequently, allogeneic and xenogeneic substitutes are widely used in contemporary GBR procedures.

Among xenogeneic substitutes, deproteinized bovine bone mineral (DBBM) is among the most widely used and best documented grafting biomaterials in implant-related augmentation procedures. It has been extensively applied in GBR-related indications such as sinus floor elevation and ridge preservation, with generally favorable clinical outcomes and histologic evidence of osteoconduction. From a dimensional perspective, evidence synthesized from randomized clinical trials suggests that xenografts tend to exhibit limited volumetric shrinkage compared with faster-remodeling grafts, supporting their practical role in space maintenance—an essential prerequisite for predictable GBR. Nevertheless, the clinical advantages of DBBM are closely linked to its slow remodeling profile, which may also translate into a substantial proportion of residual graft particles at the time of implant placement and beyond. Histologic human data indicate that osseointegrated DBBM particles can persist long-term and may show little tendency toward substitution over time—findings that likely contribute to the radiographically documented stability of augmented contours, yet also highlight a "stability versus turnover" trade-off. Long-term observations after sinus augmentation likewise report the presence of newly formed bone interspersed with residual DBBM particles even many years postoperatively, underscoring the slow degradation kinetics of DBBM in vivo.

In parallel, allogeneic bone substitutes, particularly those undergoing controlled demineralization, have been proposed as an alternative approach aiming to balance scaffold stability with biological turnover. Demineralization may expose matrix-bound osteoinductive proteins (e.g., BMPs), potentially enhancing osteogenic signaling while preserving a trabecular framework for osteoconduction; however, osteoinductive activity can vary with donor and processing parameters, and demineralization protocols may influence growth-factor availability. In this context, a commercially available partially demineralized allogeneic cancellous bone plug (ACBP) has been proposed as an alternative grafting approach, offering a plug-shaped configuration that may facilitate handling and provide intrinsic shape stability in clinical defects. However, direct head-to-head clinical evidence comparing ACBP with DBBM, particularly using combined three-dimensional imaging for dimensional stability and histomorphometry for tissue composition, remains limited, warranting controlled clinical evaluation.

Therefore, the present randomized controlled clinical trial was designed to clarify whether ACBP can provide space maintenance comparable to the clinical benchmark DBBM while exhibiting a different remodeling pattern within a clinically relevant healing interval. We compared the two materials in GBR with respect to newly formed vital bone, residual graft fraction at implant placement as an indicator of remodeling behavior, and maintenance of the augmented contour.

Materials and methods

Study design and participants
This single-center, prospective randomized controlled clinical trial was conducted at the Affiliated Stomatological Hospital of Hunan University of Chinese Medicine, Changsha, China, and enrolled adults aged 18 to 65 years who required guided bone regeneration before implant placement. Participants were recruited between November 2023 and November 2024. Primary and secondary outcomes were assessed 6 months after augmentation. Eligible indications included mandibular posterior extraction sockets with a horizontal defect dimension greater than 5 mm presenting as three- or four-wall defects, and posterior maxillary sites with residual subantral bone height less than 5 mm requiring lateral window sinus floor elevation. Participants were required to maintain good oral hygiene and comply with follow-up. Both sinus floor elevation and mandibular posterior extraction socket preservation were included as common GBR-related clinical scenarios managed under a standardized membrane-based protocol. Major exclusions included uncontrolled systemic diseases, disorders of bone metabolism, immunodeficiency, local active infection, heavy smoking defined as more than 10 cigarettes per day, pregnancy or lactation, and inability to cooperate due to psychiatric conditions.

The study was approved by the Ethics Committee of the Affiliated Stomatological Hospital of Hunan University of Chinese Medicine, approval number (2023) [Research] No. 87. All participants provided written informed consent prior to enrollment. The trial was not prospectively registered prior to participant enrollment. This study was reported in accordance with the CONSORT guidelines.

Randomization and blinding
Participants were allocated to the experimental or control group using a random number table. An independent staff member generated the allocation sequence. To ensure allocation concealment, the allocation sequence was kept by a surgical assistant who was not involved in enrollment, and group assignment was revealed only after enrollment, immediately before graft placement. A total of 25 patients were enrolled and randomized, with 12 assigned to the experimental group and 13 to the control group. Sites included both sinus floor elevation and extraction socket preservation indications.

Blinding of the surgeon was not feasible due to the distinguishable appearance and handling characteristics of the biomaterials. Outcome assessment was performed under blinded conditions. The CBCT assessor and the histomorphometric examiner were unaware of group allocation throughout the measurement process. Statistical analyses were conducted by an investigator who was blinded to group allocation and did not have access to the code key until all analyses were completed.

Interventions and surgical procedures
The control intervention was deproteinized bovine bone mineral (DBBM; Bio-Oss, Geistlich Pharma AG, Wolhusen, Switzerland). The experimental intervention was a partially demineralized allogeneic cancellous bone plug (ACBP; allogeneic bone graft, 9 × 9 × 12 mm, model SDBM-GK-P/S/H; Hubei Osteolink Biomaterials Co., Ltd., Wuhan, China). The material is a commercially available medical device approved for clinical use in China. The ACBP was soaked in sterile physiological saline for 10 min before placement according to the manufacturer's instructions. All sites were covered with an absorbable collagen membrane (Heal All, 2 × 2.5 cm; Yantai Zhenghai Bio-Tech Co., Ltd., Yantai, China).

All procedures were performed by the same senior implantologist. Patients received preoperative antibiotics and rinsed with 0.12% chlorhexidine. Under local anesthesia (compound articaine hydrochloride injection, 1.7 mL), a crestal incision was made, a full-thickness mucoperiosteal flap was elevated, and the defect was debrided. The allocated graft material was then placed, covered with a collagen membrane, and the flap was closed with tension-free suturing using 3-0 braided polyester sutures. For posterior maxillary sites, the graft material was placed in the subantral compartment created by lateral window sinus floor elevation, whereas for mandibular posterior extraction sites, it was placed within the socket defect for ridge preservation. At 6 months, cone-beam computed tomography was performed, followed by implant placement. Prior to implant placement, a 3-mm trephine was used to harvest a cylindrical bone core from the center of the augmented site for histological analysis.

Outcome measures
The primary outcome was the histomorphometric residual graft material fraction (%) at implant placement, assessed on trephine core biopsies harvested from the augmented sites. Secondary outcomes included the vital bone fraction (%) and CBCT-based dimensional changes (sinus height gain; ridge height and ridge width changes at W8/W10/W12). Radiographic endpoints were indication-specific and were reported separately for sinus floor elevation and extraction socket preservation.

Radiographic assessment
CBCT scans were obtained using a KaVo 3D eXam i CBCT system with the following acquisition settings: field of view 130 mm, voxel size 0.3 mm, 120 kVp, 5 mA, and 8.9 s exposure time. DICOM data were imported into Mimics 21 for three-dimensional reconstruction, registration, and fitting to ensure consistency of measurement locations between time points. For the sinus floor elevation subgroup, the preoperative and 6-month datasets were registered to identify the same measurement location. The vertical distance from the highest point of the original sinus floor to the apex of the postoperative bony contour was measured and recorded as the augmented bone height. For all extraction socket preservation sites in both treatment groups, the mandibular canal served as a stable anatomical reference. Ridge height was defined as the vertical distance from the superior wall of the mandibular canal to the highest point of the alveolar crest. Ridge width was measured on three coronal planes 8 mm, 10 mm, and 12 mm above the superior wall of the mandibular canal as the buccolingual dimension at each level.

Histological and histomorphometric assessment
At implant placement, cylindrical bone cores were harvested using a 3-mm trephine from the center of the augmented site. Specimens were fixed in 4% paraformaldehyde and processed for routine histology including decalcification, paraffin embedding, sectioning, and hematoxylin and eosin staining. For each biopsy, two non-consecutive sections (20 μm thickness) were analyzed. Histomorphometric quantification was performed using ImageJ. Three randomly selected fields within the regions of interest were evaluated for each specimen. Newly formed vital bone (VB) was identified as mineralized bone matrix containing osteocyte lacunae and showing continuity with regenerated trabecular bone and marrow spaces. Residual graft material (RG) was identified as acellular graft remnants with morphology distinct from host bone, typically characterized by sharp borders and the absence of osteocyte lacunae within the graft matrix. The area fractions of VB and RG were expressed as percentages of the analyzed area. All histomorphometric measurements were conducted by an examiner blinded to group allocation.

Statistical analysis
Statistical analyses were performed with SPSS version 25.0. Continuous variables are presented as mean ± standard deviation. Normality was assessed using the Shapiro–Wilk test. Between-group comparisons were performed using independent-samples t tests or Welch's t test, as appropriate. Statistical significance was set at a two-sided P value < 0.05. Indication-specific subgroup analyses were exploratory. For the sinus floor elevation subgroup, an exploratory ANCOVA was performed with 6-month augmented bone height as the dependent variable, treatment group as the fixed factor, and baseline residual subantral bone height as a covariate. No missing outcome data occurred; therefore, no imputation was performed.

Results

Participant flow and analyzed sites
A total of 25 participants were enrolled and randomized to receive ACBP (n=12) or DBBM (n=13). The treated sites included maxillary sinus floor elevation and mandibular posterior extraction socket preservation. In the ACBP group, 7 sites underwent socket preservation and 5 sites underwent sinus floor elevation; in the DBBM group, 8 sites underwent socket preservation and 5 sites underwent sinus floor elevation. All randomized participants completed the planned 6-month follow-up and were included in the radiographic and histological analyses. No intervention-related adverse events or unintended events were observed in either group during the study period.

Histological and histomorphometric outcomes
At implant placement (6 months after augmentation), bone core biopsies were harvested from the center of the augmented sites and subjected to histological and histomorphometric evaluation to quantify newly formed vital bone and residual graft material. The percentage of newly formed vital bone was 38.84 ± 8.37% in the ACBP group and 32.18 ± 11.04% in the DBBM group (P=0.105). In contrast, the percentage of residual graft material was 11.18 ± 5.48% in the ACBP group and 19.80 ± 8.23% in the DBBM group (P=0.006).

Radiographic outcomes
Radiographic endpoints were indication-specific and are reported separately for the sinus floor elevation and extraction socket preservation subgroups.

Sinus floor elevation subgroup
In the sinus floor elevation subgroup, the baseline residual subantral bone height was 2.82 ± 0.63 mm in the ACBP group and 1.47 ± 0.87 mm in the DBBM group. At 6 months, the augmented bone height increased to 10.64 ± 1.98 mm and 9.68 ± 4.00 mm, respectively. The mean height gain over the 6-month interval was 7.82 ± 1.52 mm in the ACBP group and 8.21 ± 4.30 mm in the DBBM group (P=0.852). Baseline residual subantral bone height differed between groups and should be considered when interpreting between-group comparisons of height gain. In an exploratory ANCOVA adjusting for baseline residual subantral bone height, the between-group comparison of 6-month augmented bone height yielded an adjusted P value of 0.801.

Extraction socket preservation subgroup
For the extraction socket preservation subgroup, ridge dimensions were evaluated on CBCT at baseline and 6 months. Ridge height was 16.91 ± 2.91 mm at baseline and 16.51 ± 3.02 mm at 6 months in the ACBP group, and 15.29 ± 1.42 mm at baseline and 15.81 ± 1.38 mm at 6 months in the DBBM group. The change in ridge height over 6 months was −0.30 ± 0.22 mm in the ACBP group and 0.51 ± 1.24 mm in the DBBM group (P=0.079).

Ridge width was assessed at three predefined levels (8, 10, and 12 mm). At 8 mm, the change was −0.21 ± 0.23 mm in the ACBP group and −0.35 ± 0.37 mm in the DBBM group (P=0.437); at 10 mm, −0.61 ± 0.72 mm and −0.63 ± 0.68 mm (P=0.957); and at 12 mm, −0.77 ± 0.90 mm and −0.74 ± 0.96 mm (P=0.945).

Discussion

GBR is widely used for localized alveolar augmentation before implant placement, and graft selection remains clinically important because materials differ in remodeling behavior and contour maintenance. Although DBBM is well documented clinically, its slow turnover and persistent graft particles during early healing have sustained interest in partially demineralized allogeneic alternatives, for which direct head-to-head clinical comparisons with DBBM remain limited.

In this context, the present trial addresses this evidence gap and suggests that ACBP provides radiographic dimensional outcomes broadly comparable to DBBM, while being associated with less residual graft material at implant placement and a similar proportion of newly formed vital bone.

A key issue in interpreting graft performance in guided bone regeneration is the remodeling behavior of the augmented compartment, as different biomaterials may follow distinct turnover trajectories even when the final contour appears clinically acceptable. Prior clinical and histologic studies have consistently characterized DBBM as a slowly resorbing xenogeneic mineral that can persist as particulate remnants during early healing. This property may favor space maintenance but may also contribute to a higher residual graft fraction at implant placement. By contrast, partially demineralized allogeneic cancellous constructs have been proposed to remodel more dynamically. In the present trial, the ACBP group showed a lower residual graft fraction than the DBBM group, whereas the proportion of newly formed vital bone was similar at 6 months. These findings suggest that, within the observed healing window, the main distinction between the materials relates more to graft turnover and residual material burden than to the overall proportion of newly formed vital bone.

From a clinical perspective, preservation of the augmented contour and ridge dimensions during healing is a central consideration in GBR, as dimensional collapse can complicate implant placement and compromise prosthetic planning. Previous clinical studies and systematic reviews suggest that DBBM provides reliable space maintenance, while different graft types may show broadly comparable radiographic dimensional changes during early healing when membrane coverage and wound stability are maintained. In line with this, our CBCT assessment showed comparable radiographic dimensional outcomes between ACBP and DBBM over 6 months in both subgroups. A plausible explanation is that early dimensional stability depends largely on mechanical stability of the grafted compartment, membrane function, graft immobilization, and flap management, whereas material-related differences may be expressed more clearly in remodeling patterns than in gross contour changes at this stage. Under these conditions, contour maintenance may depend more on the integrity of the space-maintaining system than on intrinsic differences between graft materials, particularly at 6 months. Clinically, these observations support a balanced approach to material selection, in which dimensional stability during healing is considered together with the remodeling profile observed at re-entry.

Radiographic contour maintenance and histologic tissue composition are complementary but distinct endpoints in GBR. During intermediate healing, such as ridge preservation or sinus augmentation, these two processes do not necessarily evolve in parallel. Human biopsy-based studies and systematic reviews suggest that different graft categories may yield similar proportions of newly formed vital bone at comparable healing time points, whereas residual biomaterial may differ markedly, reflecting distinct remodeling kinetics rather than osteogenic potential per se. As a clinical benchmark, DBBM is characterized by slow resorption and persistence of particulate remnants during early healing, a feature that may support space maintenance but is also associated with a higher residual graft fraction at re-entry. Against this background, the present findings extend existing evidence by showing that ACBP achieved radiographic dimensional outcomes broadly comparable to DBBM within a 6-month healing window, while being associated with less residual graft material and a similar proportion of newly formed vital bone at implant placement. This distinction between external contour stability and internal tissue composition suggests that similar clinical dimensions may mask material-specific turnover behavior during intermediate healing. Although these principles apply to both ridge preservation and sinus floor elevation, the two procedures differ in anatomical context and measurement framework; accordingly, radiographic outcomes were analyzed separately by indication, and the sinus subgroup findings should be interpreted with appropriate caution given the small sample size and baseline imbalance.

While these findings provide a more nuanced understanding of how radiographic and histologic endpoints relate during intermediate GBR healing, several limitations should be acknowledged. First, this single-center study included a relatively limited sample size, and no a priori sample-size calculation was performed; therefore, small-to-moderate between-group differences may not have been detectable. In addition, the inclusion of two clinical indications further reduced the statistical power for indication-specific comparisons. Second, the follow-up period was restricted to 6 months, capturing an intermediate phase of remodeling but not addressing longer-term dimensional stability, implant survival, or peri-implant tissue outcomes. Third, histomorphometric evaluation relied on localized trephine cores obtained at implant placement, which may not fully reflect spatial heterogeneity across the entire augmented compartment, especially in anatomically complex sites. Future multicenter trials with larger, stratified cohorts, extended follow-up, and standardized perioperative data collection are warranted.

Conclusion

In conclusion, this pilot randomized controlled clinical trial showed that both ACBP and DBBM supported bone regeneration over a 6-month healing interval in sinus floor elevation and extraction socket preservation. Histomorphometry indicated similar proportions of newly formed vital bone between groups, while the residual graft fraction was lower in the ACBP group. CBCT-based dimensional outcomes were comparable between materials.
