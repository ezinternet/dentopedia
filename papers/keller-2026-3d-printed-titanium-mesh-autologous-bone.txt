Title: Bone Augmentation Using 3D-Printed Individualized Titanium Mesh and Pure Autologous Bone Graft: A Retrospective Study.
Authors: Keller P, Sterba M, Abitbol S, Bochet Q, Behr M
Journal: The International Journal of Oral & Maxillofacial Implants
Year: 2026 (epub 2026-02-10)
Volume: ahead of print, pages 1-23
DOI: 10.11607/jomi.11587
PMID: 41665971

ABSTRACT

Background:
After tooth loss, alveolar bone resorption is irreversible and often results in insufficient bone volume for implant placement. GBR with 3D-printed individualized titanium meshes has been increasingly employed. Various mesh designs (macro-porous, micro-porous) and different surgical techniques have been proposed. Although reliable, complications such as mesh exposure and limited bone gain still occur.

Aim:
To evaluate the reliability of a GBR technique using micro-perforated grade-2 titanium meshes held by two vestibular screws and pure autologous bone — clinical outcomes and bone gain.

Methods:
Retrospective study, 16 patients at a single dental center.
Meshes digitally designed and manufactured using Direct Metal Laser Sintering (DMLS) in grade-2 titanium (micro-perforated design).
Two-step protocol:
  Step 1 — mesh placement + pure autologous bone graft (no xenograft)
  Healing period ≥ 4 months
  Step 2 — mesh removal + implant placement (with secondary slight GBR)

Outcome measures: Planned vs. real bone augmentation (pre- and post-surgical CBCT). Three operators measured 102 total measurements. Statistical: linear mixed model.

Key distinction: PURE autologous bone only (no xenograft mix); MICRO-perforated Ti grade-2 mesh; held by only 2 vestibular screws.

Results:
- All 16 meshes successfully implanted — NO COMPLICATIONS, NO MESH EXPOSURE
- All patients showed sufficient bone after 122–160 days for safe implant placement
- Planned vs. real bone augmentation: NO significant difference (statistical linear mixed model, p>0.05)
- Targeted augmentation successfully achieved in all cases
- Short-term clinical reliability: HIGH
- Predictability: HIGH — CAD-planned dimensions matched clinical outcomes

Key Finding: Zero mesh exposure in 16 consecutive patients. Authors attribute this to:
  1. Micro-perforation design (reduces tissue tension by improving tissue compliance)
  2. Pure autologous bone (faster, more complete integration)
  3. Two-screw vestibular fixation (simplicity, reduced bulk)
  4. Minimal mesh bulk (grade-2 Ti, smaller cross-section)

Conclusion:
These results suggest a high level of short-term clinical reliability and predictability of GBR using 3D-printed individualized micro-perforated grade-2 titanium meshes with pure autologous bone. The technique achieves targeted augmentation with zero short-term exposure events.

Limitation: Short-term data only; small n=16; retrospective design; no control group.
