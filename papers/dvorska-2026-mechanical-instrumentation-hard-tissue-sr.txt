The Effect of Mechanical Instrumentation on Dental Hard Tissue In Vitro: A Systematic Review
Dvorska et al. International Journal of Dental Hygiene. 2026.
DOI: 10.1111/idh.70107 ; PMID: 42244102

ABSTRACT (abstract-only — full text not retrieved)

Aim: Analyse effects of hand instruments, ultrasonic scalers and air-polishing devices on dental hard tissues, focusing on material loss and surface roughness. Searched MEDLINE, EMBASE, Cochrane Library. Included studies on human cementum and dentine using ≥6 human tooth specimens; excluded powders other than glycine or erythritol and combined treatments.

Results: Most studies reported higher substance loss with hand curettes vs ultrasonic scalers.
Root substance loss:
- Ultrasonic scalers: 0.28–14.73 µm per stroke (mean 4.89) and 0.33–8.20 µm per second (mean 2.24)
- Hand instruments: 0.86–20.60 µm per stroke (mean 6.0)
- Air polishing with low-abrasive powders: 0.12–23.40 µm (mean 8.65)
Surface roughness mixed (air polishing did not increase roughness in one study; others reported increases and decreases).

Conclusion: Substance loss per second highest with air-polishing devices vs ultrasonic; substance loss per stroke greatest with hand instruments vs ultrasonic scalers; heterogeneity limits comparison.
