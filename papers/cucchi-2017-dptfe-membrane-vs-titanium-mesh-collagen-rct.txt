Title: Evaluation of complication rates and vertical bone gain after guided bone regeneration with non-resorbable membranes versus titanium meshes and resorbable membranes. A randomized clinical trial.
Authors: Cucchi A, Vignudelli E, Napolitano A, Marchetti C, Corinaldesi G
Journal: Clinical Implant Dentistry and Related Research
Year: 2017
DOI: 10.1111/cid.12520
PMID: 28745035
PMCID: PMC5655714

[FULL TEXT — PMC open access]

BACKGROUND / AIM
Pilot, parallel-group, double-blind RCT comparing two GBR approaches for atrophic posterior mandible:
- Group A (n=20): d-PTFE titanium-reinforced membrane (Cytoplast Ti-250XL; Osteogenics Biomedical) — non-resorbable, space-maintaining on its own
- Group B (n=20): Ti mesh (Trinon Titanium GmbH) + cross-linked collagen membrane (Osseoguard, Zimmer Biomet) — Ti mesh for space maintenance + XL collagen as GBR barrier cover

KEY CLINICAL QUESTION: Is Ti mesh + cross-linked collagen equivalent to d-PTFE Ti-reinforced membrane for VRA in the posterior mandible?

METHODS

Study design: Pilot, parallel, double-blind RCT; Bologna, Italy; Ethics 30/2013/O/Disp
N=40 patients, 108 implants placed simultaneously with GBR (one-stage approach)
Follow-up: T0 (surgery) → T1 (reopening, 9 months) → T2 (loading, ~3 months after T1)

Inclusion:
- Partial edentulism, atrophic posterior mandible
- Vertical + horizontal bone resorption requiring 3D bone regeneration
- Vertical peri-implant bone defect ≥ 2 mm (after implant placement in ideal prosthetic position)

Exclusion: residual bone height <5 mm, smokers >10 cig/day, systemic disease, immunosuppression, bisphosphonates

Implants: BT SAFE tapered, double-variable thread, double-acid-etched (DAE) surface (Biotec srl)
Graft: 50:50 autogenous bone (bone scraper, external oblique ridge) + bone allograft (EnCore, Osteogenics)
Decortication: yes (cortical perforations)
Fixation: mini-screws (lingual then buccal)
Group B only: cross-linked collagen membrane (Osseoguard) placed OVER Ti mesh per GBR principles
Suture: Cytoplast PTFE; primary tension-free closure required

VBG measurement: UNC-15 probe at 4 sites (mesial, distal, buccal, lingual) around each implant
- IBD (initial bone defect, T0) = distance from implant top to first bone-implant contact
- FBD (final bone defect, T1) = same measurement after healing
- VBG = IBD - FBD

Complication classification (Fontana et al. 2011):
Surgical: Class A (vascular) / Class B (neurological) / Class C (flap)
Healing: Class I (no exposure) to Class IV (abscess without exposure)
+ Major vs Minor: based on impact on newly formed bone

RESULTS

PRIMARY IMPLANT STABILITY
- IT: 80.0 Ncm (A) vs 79.0 Ncm (B) — NS
- RFA (ISQ): 87.0 (A) vs 84.5 (B) — NS
- All 108 implants: 97.2% showed IT >35 Ncm and ISQ >60
- At T1: all implants osseointegrated; ISQ 71.0 (A) vs 66.5 (B) — NS

SURGICAL COMPLICATIONS
- Group A (d-PTFE): 5.0% (1/20) — 1 temporary mental nerve paresthesia
- Group B (Ti mesh + XL collagen): 15.8% (3/19) — 3 temporary paresthesias
- p = 0.34 (NS) — but d-PTFE numerically better
- All neurological complications spontaneously recovered within 1 month
- KEY REASON: Ti mesh required more surgical time and manipulation for adaptation/seating

HEALING COMPLICATIONS
- Group A: 15.0% (3/20)
  - 0-1mo: abscess without exposure (Class IV, Major)
  - 1-3mo: early membrane exposure + infection (Class III, Major) → complete failure
  - 3-6mo: late membrane exposure without infection (Class II, Minor) → no bone impact
  - Major: 10.0% (2/20)
- Group B: 21.1% (4/19)
  - 0-1mo: abscess without exposure (Class IV, Major) + early exposure + infection (Class III, Major) → failure
  - 1-3mo: late exposure + infection (Class III, Major) → Ti mesh removed, implants retained
  - 3-6mo: late exposure without infection (Class II, Minor)
  - Major: 15.8% (3/19)
- p = 0.69 (NS) — no significant difference in overall or major/minor rates

VERTICAL BONE GAIN (VBG)
| Site     | Group A (d-PTFE)  | Group B (Ti mesh + XL collagen) |
|----------|-------------------|--------------------------------|
| Mesial   | 3.6 ± 1.2 mm      | 3.3 ± 1.0 mm                   |
| Lingual  | 4.2 ± 1.2 mm      | 3.8 ± 0.8 mm                   |
| Distal   | 4.1 ± 1.0 mm      | 4.0 ± 1.0 mm                   |
| Buccal   | 5.0 ± 1.0 mm      | 5.1 ± 1.4 mm                   |
| MEAN     | 4.2 ± 1.0 mm      | 4.1 ± 1.0 mm                   |

- p = 0.58 (NS) — virtually identical VBG

- Supra-implant bone overgrowth: 63.0% (A) vs 61.4% (B) — NS
- Partial bone regeneration with residual defect: 25.9% (A) vs 22.7% (B) — NS
- Both groups: residual defects always at buccal sites

DISCUSSION INSIGHTS

Ti mesh handling:
"d-PTFE membranes were shaped, adapted, and stabilized more readily and quickly than Ti mesh devices. Additionally, during reopening surgery, Ti mesh required more time and more effort than d-PTFE membranes. Thus, d-PTFE showed better manageability and ease of use and required less dexterity and fewer manual skills."

Exposure and bone loss relationship for Ti mesh:
"In the case of Ti mesh exposure, the lack of bone volume was significantly positively correlated with the area of mesh exposed, with a 16.3% deficit in bone volume for every cm² of mesh exposed."

Abscess (without exposure):
"Abscess formation in both groups occurred during the first postoperative month, probably due to bacterial contamination of the barrier device during membrane handling or bacterial contamination of the graft."

Late exposure:
"Late exposure of the barrier devices did not influence bone regeneration in either group." → consistent with Abu-Mostafa SR (4-week threshold)

One major difference in Group B: when Ti mesh was removed due to late infection exposure → IMPLANTS RETAINED IN SITU (GBR revision possible without losing implants).

CONCLUSIONS

Both d-PTFE Ti-reinforced membranes and Ti mesh + cross-linked collagen membranes produced:
- Similar healing complication rates (15.0% vs 21.1%, NS)
- Similar VBG (4.2 vs 4.1 mm, NS)
- Similar implant stability and osseointegration

Difference: d-PTFE membranes showed fewer surgical complications (5% vs 15.8%, NS but lower) and easier handling. Ti mesh required more surgical dexterity, longer handling time, and caused more neurological injuries during manipulation.

IMPLICATION: d-PTFE Ti-reinforced membrane is an effective alternative to Ti mesh + cross-linked collagen, with potentially simpler surgery. Cross-linked collagen used here as a COVER over Ti mesh (not standalone for VRA).
