TITLE: Evaluation of bacterial spectrum of orofacial infections and their antibiotic susceptibility
AUTHORS: Nagendra S Chunduri, Krishnaveni Madasu, Venkateswara R Goteki, Tanveer Karpe, Haranadha Reddy
JOURNAL: Annals of Maxillofacial Surgery 2012;2(1):46-50
RETRIEVED: 2026-06-17 via PubMed MCP (get_full_text_article)

=== ABSTRACT ===
Objective: assess common microorganisms causing orofacial infections and their antimicrobial susceptibility (India). Methods: 68 patients, aseptic sampling, culture + susceptibility, descriptive stats. Results: 64 aerobic + 87 anaerobic strains; predominant Streptococcus viridans (64%), Prevotella (43%), Peptostreptococcus (26%), Porphyromonas (7%), Fusobacterium (14%). Isolates highly sensitive to amoxicillin-clavulanate and amoxicillin alone, clindamycin, levofloxacin; more resistance to erythromycin. Conclusion: amoxicillin retains powerful activity; amox-clav and clindamycin useful alternatives for severe orofacial infections; erythromycin of questionable benefit.

=== KEY DATA ===
- n=68 (37 M / 31 F), mean age 32y. 151 isolates (~2.2/patient). Submandibular space most common (34%), then buccal (28%).
- Aerobic Gram+ cocci 34%; anaerobic Gram- bacilli 40% (predominant).
- Susceptibility: Gram+ cocci -> amox-clav 95%, amoxicillin 90%, levofloxacin 83%; Gram- rods -> amox-clav 97% (erythromycin 62%); anaerobic GPC & GNR -> amox-clav 100%, clindamycin 100%.
- Amoxicillin 90% (G+ cocci), 79% (G- rods). amox-clav broadens spectrum to beta-lactamase producers -> "most effective option" for dentoalveolar infection.
- Erythromycin: questionable benefit. Levofloxacin active but high MIC -> limited benefit.
- Single geographic location, pilot; multicentric confirmation needed.
