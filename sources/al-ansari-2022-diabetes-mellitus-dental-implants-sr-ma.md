## One-line Summary
SR+MA (89 publications, 5510 diabetic vs 62,780 non-diabetic implants): OR 1.777 (p<0.001) higher failure in DM; T1DM > T2DM risk (OR 4.477); MBL difference +0.776mm; maxilla worse than mandible.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Materials 15(9):3227 (April 2022)
- DOI: 10.3390/ma15093227
- Design: Systematic review + meta-analysis + meta-regression; 89 publications

## 2. Key Results
- Overall OR for implant failure (DM vs non-DM): **1.777** (p<0.001) → 77.7% higher failure risk
- Type 1 DM vs Type 2: OR 4.477 (p=0.032) — T1DM significantly higher risk
- MBL mean difference: **0.776 mm** more in DM patients (p=0.027)
- MBL difference increases with follow-up time: +0.032mm/month (p<0.001)
- OR decreases slightly with follow-up time: −0.007/month (p=0.048)
- Maxilla: failure difference significant; mandible: not significant

## 3. Meta-Regression Findings
- Longer follow-up → MBL gap widens between DM and non-DM
- Longer follow-up → failure OR gap slightly narrows (surviving implants in DM are well-osseointegrated)

## 4. Limitations
- High heterogeneity (I²) expected given different DM definitions, follow-up periods
- Many studies do not stratify by glycemic control level
- Retrospective/prospective mix; variable confounding
