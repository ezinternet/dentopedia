## One-line Summary
SR (9 studies/guidelines, 2000–2025): epinephrine 1:100,000–1:200,000 only safe vasoconstrictor in pregnancy; levonordefrin lacks safety data; felypressin contraindicated (oxytocic properties → uterine contractions).

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Journal of Clinical Medicine 14(13):4773 (July 6, 2025)
- DOI: 10.3390/jcm14134773
- Design: Systematic review (PRISMA 2020); PubMed, Jan 2000–May 2025; 95 screened → 9 included

## 2. Vasoconstrictor Comparison in Pregnancy

| Agent | Safety in Pregnancy | Evidence | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Epinephrine** 1:100,000–1:200,000 | Generally safe at low doses | Moderate | ✅ Use; limit 2 carpules |
| **Levonordefrin** | Insufficient safety data; potential uterine vasoconstriction | Weak | ⚠️ Avoid |
| **Felypressin** | Oxytocic properties → uterine contractions | Moderate (contraindication) | ❌ Contraindicated |

## 3. Epinephrine in Pregnancy
- At dental doses (1:100,000 or 1:200,000) → minimal systemic absorption
- Endogenous epinephrine release from maternal anxiety/pain >> exogenous dental dose
- Key: **well-administered, profound anesthesia** → minimal epinephrine needed, minimal maternal stress
- Avoid intravascular injection; aspirate before injecting
- 2nd trimester preferred for elective procedures

## 4. Clinical Protocol Recommendations
- Use lowest effective concentration (1:200,000 preferred over 1:100,000)
- Limit to 2 cartridges maximum
- Avoid plain (vasoconstrictor-free) LA for prolonged procedures (insufficient duration → repeat injections → more total drug)
- Consult obstetrician for complex or lengthy procedures
- Emergency dental care: any trimester; elective: 2nd trimester

## 5. Limitations
- Only 9 studies/guidelines met criteria — very limited evidence base
- No RCTs on vasoconstrictors in pregnant dental patients (ethical constraints)
- Observational/guideline-based conclusions only
