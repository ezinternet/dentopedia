## Why Ingested

Adds a head-to-head water-flosser-vs-dental-floss SR focused specifically on the *plaque-reduction* endpoint, complementing [[interdental-cleaning/mancinelli-lyle-2024-water-flosser-vs-interdental-brush-rct]] (water flosser vs interdental brush) and providing the general-adult counterpoint to the orthodontic-only [[interdental-cleaning/yiamwattana-2025-oral-irrigator-vs-floss-orthodontic-sr-ma]], which found no oral-irrigator superiority. The two SRs disagree, so holding both is needed to characterize the tension in the device-comparison literature.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (PRISMA/PROSPERO-registered, 7 RCTs, n≈155 vs 156 adults; qualitative synthesis, NO meta-analysis due to I²=97% heterogeneity) finding the majority of included trials (4/7) favored water flossers over dental floss for plaque reduction — especially at inaccessible interproximal areas — while 3/7 found no between-group difference; GRADE certainty "moderate."

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Comparing the effectiveness of water flosser and dental floss in plaque reduction among adults: A systematic review
- **Authors**: Mohapatra S, Rajpurohit L, Mohandas R, Patil S
- **Journal**: Journal of Indian Society of Periodontology, 2024; 27(6): 559–567
- **DOI**: 10.4103/jisp.jisp_597_22 · PMID 38434511 · PMCID PMC10906797
- **Type**: Systematic review (qualitative synthesis; meta-analysis precluded by heterogeneity)
- **Registration**: PROSPERO (registered 2022-11-25); conducted per PRISMA 2020

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR to directly compare dental floss (the "gold standard" interdental aid) vs water flosser specifically on the **plaque-reduction** outcome in adults (prior SRs — Kotsakis, Ng & Lim, Sälzer — focused on gingival/bleeding endpoints and generally found little plaque-score effect).
- Synthesizes 7 RCTs and finds the **majority favor water flossers** over floss for plaque reduction, with a recurrent finding that water flossers reach **inaccessible interproximal/posterior areas** that floss cannot.
- Provides a clinical recommendation framing water flossers as an effective alternative for patients with **limited manual dexterity, orthodontic appliances, or dental prostheses**.
- Transparently reports that high clinical + statistical heterogeneity (I²=97%, p<0.00001) made meta-analysis inappropriate, so the headline is qualitative, not pooled.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Question (PICO)**: P = adults (>18 y) with dental plaque; I = water flosser; C = dental floss; O = plaque reduction.
- **Databases**: PubMed, Scopus, Cochrane, ScienceDirect, Lilac, Google Scholar + hand search; window **January 1, 2002 – October 31, 2022**.
- **Inclusion**: RCTs in healthy adults >18 y comparing floss vs water flosser with plaque-score reduction as the primary outcome; English-language. **Exclusion**: other interdental comparators; plaque as secondary outcome.
- **Selection**: two independent reviewers (SM, LR) screened titles/abstracts; third reviewer (RM) arbitrated. 2103 records → 2098 de-duplicated titles → 45 title-eligible → 10 full-text → **7 included**.
- **Quality**: JBI critical-appraisal checklist for RCTs (13 items); GRADE for certainty of evidence; heterogeneity via I² and Chi-square (RevMan 5.4.1).
- **Included designs**: all RCTs — 4 parallel, 3 split-mouth; 6 single-blind, 1 blinding-unclear; sample sizes 34–83; ages 18–50 y. Interdental aid was an adjunct to manual toothbrushing in 6/7 studies.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **All 7 trials** showed significant pre-/post-cleaning plaque reduction for *both* devices.
- **4/7 trials** showed water flosser significantly superior to floss (Goyal 2013, Akram 2015, Sarlati 2016, Batool 2021). Representative whole-mouth/approximal numbers (Goyal 2013): floss 57.7% whole-mouth / 63.4% approximal vs water flosser **74.4% whole-mouth / 81.6% approximal**.
- **3/7 trials** found no significant between-group difference (Sasikumar 2016, Abdellatif 2021, Sawan 2022) — though Sawan still found the water flosser better at the **distal molar** surface.
- **Inaccessible interproximal areas**: water flosser repeatedly outperformed floss at posterior/proximal surfaces hard for floss to reach.
- **GRADE**: certainty rated **moderate**; imprecision flagged "serious" because baseline plaque scores differed between treatment/control groups. Pooled (reported but not relied upon): **SMD 2.47 (95% CI 0.75 to 4.18)** higher with water flosser across 6 studies (155 vs 156 patients) — but I²=97%, so meta-analysis was deemed unsuitable.
- Best methodological quality: Akram 2015 (11/13 JBI "Yes"); lowest: Sasikumar 2016 (9/13).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **No valid meta-analysis** — I²=97% heterogeneity (study design split-mouth vs parallel, duration immediate vs 4–6 wk, differing baselines, adjunct-vs-sole intervention) makes the pooled SMD unreliable.
- Small total sample (max single-study n=83); only 7 studies; all from a narrow set of countries (India ×2, Saudi Arabia ×2, Pakistan, Iran, Iraq).
- Baseline plaque scores were not equalized (no oral prophylaxis at start), inflating imprecision.
- Heterogeneous devices (WaterPik vs Aquajet) and floss types (waxed/unwaxed/unspecified); flossing duration mostly unreported; **patient compliance not measured** in any study; Hawthorne effect possible.
- **Cost was overlooked** in every included trial — water flossers are expensive, need water/power, and are hard to use away from home.
- Future work: adequately powered RCTs with standardized design, longer follow-up, and compliance assessment.

## 6. Related Work

- Contrasts with prior SRs (Kotsakis; Ng & Lim; Sälzer) that found water flossers reduce gingival inflammation/bleeding but with minimal measurable plaque-score change — the authors attribute the divergence to different included studies and to 2D plaque indices missing reductions in plaque *thickness*.
- Within this wiki, sits alongside [[interdental-cleaning/mancinelli-lyle-2024-water-flosser-vs-interdental-brush-rct]] (device-vs-device RCT) and stands in tension with [[interdental-cleaning/yiamwattana-2025-oral-irrigator-vs-floss-orthodontic-sr-ma]] (orthodontic SR+MA, no irrigator superiority), and with adjunctive-therapy evidence in [[interdental-cleaning/liu-2025-water-flossing-adjunct-nspt-periodontitis-rct]] and [[interdental-cleaning/tyler-2023-waterpik-manual-toothbrush-orthodontic-rct]].

## 7. Glossary

- **Water flosser / oral irrigator / water jet**: power-driven device delivering a pulsating, pressure-controlled (50–90 psi) water stream to disrupt and flush interdental/subgingival plaque; introduced 1962.
- **Dental floss ("gold standard")**: string interdental aid; ADA states it can remove up to ~80% of interdental plaque, but only ~10–30% of adults floss regularly.
- **RMNPI (Rastogi Modified Navy Plaque Index)**: plaque index scoring facial, lingual, proximal, and marginal tooth sections separately — favored here for resolving interproximal plaque.
- **JBI checklist**: Joanna Briggs Institute critical-appraisal tool for RCTs (13 items).
- **GRADE**: Grading of Recommendations, Assessment, Development and Evaluations — rates certainty as very low/low/moderate/high across risk of bias, inconsistency, indirectness, imprecision, publication bias.
- **I² / heterogeneity**: statistic quantifying between-study inconsistency; I²=97% here indicates extreme heterogeneity, precluding meta-analysis.
- **SMD (standardized mean difference)**: effect-size metric; pooled estimate 2.47 (95% CI 0.75–4.18) reported but not relied upon due to heterogeneity.
- **Hawthorne effect**: behavior change because participants know they are being observed, potentially inflating measured plaque reduction.
