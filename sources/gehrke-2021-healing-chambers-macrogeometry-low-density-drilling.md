## Why Ingested

저밀도골에서 일차안정성을 결정하는 변수로 osteotomy 술식뿐 아니라 implant macrogeometry(healing chamber)의 기여를 분리 평가하기 위해 인제스트. [[implants/el-kholey-2019-drilling-technique-low-density-bone-sr]] 가 지적한 "undersized로도 type IV 골 일차안정성이 충분치 않을 수 있음"을 in-vitro polyurethane 모델에서 정량 확인하며, macrogeometry가 보완 변수임을 제시.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro polyurethane-block study (120 implants, 2 macrogeometries × 3 osteotomy techniques) showing undersized osteotomy raises insertion torque/stability/pullout only in PCF-20 bone (not PCF-10), while healing-chamber macrogeometry retains more bone on the surface after pullout.

## 한줄요약

In-vitro 폴리우레탄 블록 실험(임플란트 120개, 2 macrogeometry × 3 osteotomy) — undersized osteotomy는 PCF-20 골에서만 토크·안정성·pullout을 유의하게 높이고 PCF-10(최저밀도)에서는 한계가 있으며, healing chamber 설계가 pullout 후 표면 잔류골을 더 많이 확보.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Isolates implant macrogeometry (healing chambers) as a variable independent of drilling technique in low-density bone.
- Stratifies by two surrogate bone densities (PCF-10, PCF-20 polyurethane blocks).
- Combines insertion torque, RFA stability, pullout resistance, and residual bone weight in one design.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- In-vitro experimental study; 120 implants, 6 groups (2 macrogeometries × 3 drilling techniques), n=20/group.
- Polyurethane blocks PCF-10 and PCF-20 (type IV–like low density).
- Outcomes: insertion torque (IT), initial stability, pullout resistance, residual bone weight on implant.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- PCF-10 blocks: initial stability low across all groups regardless of technique.
- PCF-20 blocks: undersized osteotomy significantly increased all measured values.
- Healing-chamber macrogeometry: more bone retained on the surface after pullout test.
- Conclusion: even undersized osteotomy may not guarantee adequate stability in type IV bone; macrogeometry (healing chambers) contributes additional bone-surface engagement.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- In-vitro polyurethane surrogate — no biological healing, no osseointegration.
- Static mechanical surrogates of bone density; not patient bone.
- No clinical survival endpoint.

## 6. Related Work
- Healing-chamber macrogeometry literature (lower IT/RFA similar to conventional in prior studies).
- Undersized/condensation osteotomy literature for low-density bone.

## 7. Glossary
- PCF: pounds per cubic foot — polyurethane block density surrogate for bone quality.
- Healing chamber: void spaces in implant macrogeometry promoting bone apposition.
- Pullout resistance: axial force to extract the implant.
