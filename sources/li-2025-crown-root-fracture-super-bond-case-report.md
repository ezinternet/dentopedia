## Why Ingested

Super-Bond(4-META/MMA-TBB)의 치관-치근 파절편 재접착(crown-root fragment reattachment) 적응증에 대한 장기(55개월) 임상 근거 — 위키에 4-META/MMA-TBB의 in-vitro 접착력 자료는 있어도 실제 장기 임상 reattachment anchor가 없었다. [[resin-bonding/kodaira-2022-resin-bonding-titanium-adhesive-monomers-review]]가 4-META/MMA-TBB(Super-Bond C&B)를 열순환 내구성 최우수 luting agent로 보고한 in-vitro 결론을, 본 case report가 혈액·습기 오염 환경의 실제 구강에서 55개월 무탈착·치주건강 유지로 임상 확장한다. fiber-post-to-dentin 접착의 [[resin-bonding/dhawan-2026-bonding-total-self-etch-fiberpost]]와도 짝을 이룬다(본 증례는 #1.4 fiber post로 fragment 보강).

## One-line Summary

Case report (n=1, 55-month follow-up): a complicated oblique crown-root fracture of the maxillary left central incisor was treated by immediate fragment reattachment with the Super-Bond C&B (4-META/MMA-TBB) adhesive and a #1.4 fiber post under a M-MIST labial flap, achieving stable restoration, PD <3 mm with negative BoP, no root/bone resorption, and complete periapical cyst healing at 55 months.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Long-follow-up clinical anchor (55 months) for crown-root fragment reattachment** — most reattachment evidence is short-term; this case documents 55-month survival with maintained periodontal/periapical health, supporting reattachment as a durable conservative alternative to crown lengthening, orthodontic extrusion, or extraction + implant.
2. **Super-Bond (4-META/MMA-TBB) as the moisture-tolerant adhesive of choice** — the case argues the technique succeeds precisely because Super-Bond's tributylborane (TBB) catalyst polymerizes *better* when exposed to moisture/air, unlike conventional/hydrophilic adhesives whose bond is degraded by blood/saliva contamination at the subgingival fracture line.
3. **M-MIST flap + fiber-post-reinforced reattachment protocol** — combines a small full-thickness labial mucoperiosteal flap (lingual papilla preserved) for direct visualization and moisture control with a #1.4 glass fiber post to mechanically reinforce the fragment-to-root bond.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Patient/tooth**: 54-year-old female, maxillary left central incisor (tooth 21), oblique crown-root fracture palatal→labial extending ~3 mm from gingival margin; mesial fracture end ~1 mm subgingival (below alveolar crest). Tooth had prior root canal treatment + apical cyst removal 1 year earlier, no crown protection; discolored. Presented within 1 h, fragment stored in water; fragment had completely fallen out.

**Why not the alternatives**: immediate implant ruled out (apical cyst resection → insufficient apical bone → no primary stability); crown lengthening risked gingival margin discrepancy on a central incisor; orthodontic extrusion too time-consuming. Patient strongly wanted to preserve the natural tooth.

**Reattachment protocol**:
1. Fragment disinfected with 2% chlorhexidine, stored in 0.9% saline.
2. Lingual post space prepared with diamond burs + Peeso reamers for straight-line passive seating of a #1.4 fiber post.
3. M-MIST design: small full-thickness *labial* mucoperiosteal flap elevated to expose root fracture line; lingual flap left unreflected to preserve interdental papilla.
4. Moisture control: two palatal saliva ejectors + ferric-sulfate hemostatic gel (ViscoStat Clear) for a dry field.
5. Adhesion: fragment etched with 37% phosphoric acid (Scotchbond Universal Etchant); root surface treated with Super-Bond C&B dentin conditioner; rinse/dry.
6. Bonding: fragment, #1.4 fiber post (Matchpost RTD), and root surface coated with Super-Bond C&B (4-META/MMA-TBB) cement; fragment visually aligned and reattached, fiber post reinforcing the bond.
7. Excess cement removed, root planing, flap sutured (4-0 Dafilon nonabsorbable).
8. At 12 months (periodontal health confirmed): definitive all-ceramic crown, cemented with RelyX Ultimate resin cement.

**Follow-up**: 1 week, 1 month, then quarterly to 12 months; thereafter 6-monthly clinical + radiographic to 55 months.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **12 months**: proper fragment adaptation, good periodontal health, no root or bone resorption — gated the definitive crown.
- **55 months**: all-ceramic crown margin well-sealed; periodontal tissues healthy; gingival color/texture satisfactory; **probing depth <3 mm** labial and palatal; **bleeding on probing negative**; radiograph shows **no alveolar bone or root resorption** and **complete healing of the periapical cystic area**. No microleakage, debonding, or periodontal complications across the period. Patient satisfied with esthetics and function.
- **Biological-width rationale**: with only ~1 mm subgingival fracture extension and good plaque control, biological width can re-establish itself; a cited SR confirms favorable outcomes even when biological width is violated.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Single case (n=1)**, lowest evidence tier; no comparator, no generalizable effect size.
- Authors state the method **may not suit teeth with severely damaged biological width**.
- Success is conditional on a **prompt, intact, well-fitting fragment**, minimal subgingival extension, adequate residual root length/stability, and rigorous moisture/contaminant isolation.
- A lifelong biannual clinical+radiographic follow-up protocol was instituted; longer multi-case series needed to establish reattachment + Super-Bond durability statistically.

## 7. Glossary

- **Crown-root fracture**: fracture involving enamel, dentin, and cementum, extending below the gingival margin (often violating biological width); ~5% of dental injuries.
- **Super-Bond C&B**: a self-curing 4-META/MMA-TBB resin adhesive/luting cement (Nissin/Sun Medical); the tri-n-butylborane (TBB) initiator makes polymerization *enhanced* by moisture/air, giving high bond durability and moisture tolerance.
- **4-META/MMA-TBB**: 4-methacryloxyethyl trimellitate anhydride / methyl methacrylate / tri-n-butylborane — the carboxylate-anhydride functional-monomer + initiator system underlying Super-Bond's hybrid-layer formation.
- **M-MIST (modified minimally invasive surgical technique)**: minimal flap design (here, small labial full-thickness flap with lingual papilla preserved) shown to reduce probing depth and gain clinical attachment with minimal recession.
- **Biological width**: the dento-gingival junction dimension (junctional epithelium + connective tissue attachment) that must be respected/re-established for periodontal health around restorations.
- **Fragment reattachment**: bonding the original fractured tooth fragment back to the tooth — conservative, less invasive alternative defined by IADT 2020 guidelines for crown-root fractures.
