## One-line Summary
Systematic review on prevalence and risk factors of iatrogenic mandibular fractures (intra- and postoperative) following lower third molar extraction, with incidence ranging 0.0033%–0.075%.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical and Experimental Dentistry 2022;14(1):e85-94
- **DOI**: 10.4317/jced.58390
- **Study type**: Systematic review
- **Institution**: University of Barcelona / Teknon Medical Center, Barcelona, Spain

## 2. Key Contributions
- Reports prevalence range for mandibular fractures after lower third molar (3MI) extraction: 0.0034%–0.075% (intraoperative + postoperative combined)
- Identifies postoperative fractures as more common than intraoperative
- Characterizes the typical fracture patient and tooth position profiles
- Emphasizes that preoperative planning can predict and reduce fracture risk

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Electronic databases: Cochrane, PubMed (MEDLINE), Scopus
- Key search terms: "Molar, Third"; "Mandibular Fractures"; "Molar Third, Removal"; "Molar Third, Complications"; "Dental Extractions, Complications"
- Inclusion: articles with ≥10 patients, published in English in last 10 years
- Exclusion: non-human studies, case reports
- Received 07/03/2021, accepted 16/04/2021

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Incidence**: Overall 0.0034%–0.075%; postoperative fractures 0.0042%–0.0046%; intraoperative 0.0033%–0.0036%
- **Patient profile**: Predominantly male, aged 40–60 years
- **Primary cause of postoperative fractures**: Premature chewing force
- **Tooth position risk factors**: Deeply impacted, Pell & Gregory Class II/III and B/C, Winter's mesioangular classification, left mandibular side
- **Fracture site**: Mandibular angle most common (40%)
- **Timing**: Most postoperative fractures occur within first 4 weeks after surgery
- **Weak mandibular areas**: Angle > condyle > symphysis > body > coronoid process
- Inadequate surgical technique (excessive force, incorrect odontosection) identified as intraoperative cause

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Limited number of qualifying studies (≥10 patients, English, last 10 years)
- Heterogeneity in reporting methods and classification systems across studies
- Retrospective nature of most included studies
- Need for standardized fracture reporting in oral surgery registries

## 6. Related Work
- Pires et al. 2017 (late mandibular fracture SR, 124 cases) — analysis of postoperative timing and treatment
- Bodner et al. 2011 (189 cases analysis)
- Ethunandan et al. series on incidence ranges

## 7. Glossary
- **3MI**: Lower third molar (tercer molar inferior)
- **Pell & Gregory classification**: Classifies lower third molar impaction by relationship to ramus (Class I/II/III) and depth (A/B/C)
- **Winter's classification**: Classifies angulation: mesioangular, distoangular, vertical, horizontal
- **Mandibular angle fracture**: Fracture at gonial angle, most common site (40%) due to bone anatomy and presence of impacted molar
- **Iatrogenic fracture**: Fracture caused by surgical procedure
