## Why Ingested

Most recent (2026) and largest human-only SR+MA in the osseodensification (OD) primary-stability evidence base (555 patients, 685 implants) — confirms OD raises both insertion torque and ISQ over standard osteotomy, updating the pooled estimates feeding [[overviews/osseodensification-clinical-applications]]. Reinforces the human ISQ advantage reported by [[implants/gaspar-2022-implant-stability-osseodensification-conventional-sr-ma]].

## One-line Summary
SR+MA (555 patients, 685 implants, in vivo human trials only) — OD raises insertion torque (45.75 ± 6.55 N·cm vs 38.00 ± 7.01 N·cm, P<0.001) and ISQ (MD 3.24, 95% CI 0.72–5.95, P=0.024) over standard osteotomy.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (in vivo human trials only)
- **Journal**: International Journal of Oral & Maxillofacial Surgery, 2026 (in press, YIJOM-5540)
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.ijom.2026.02.008
- **Sample**: 555 patients (59.2% female where sex was reported); 685 implants total — 343 OD, 342 standard osteotomy
- **Primary Question**: Does OD drilling improve insertion torque (IT) and implant stability quotient (ISQ) compared with conventional subtractive osteotomy in healthy adults receiving single or multiple implants in partially/fully edentulous segments?
- **Search**: English-language articles, last 10 years up to September 11, 2025; risk of bias assessed via RoB 2, Newcastle–Ottawa, and GRADE.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Pooled mean IT: OD 45.75 ± 6.55 N·cm vs standard 38.00 ± 7.01 N·cm; P < 0.001.
- Pooled ISQ mean difference (random effects): 3.24 (95% CI 0.72–5.95), P = 0.024.
- Targeted scope: excludes Summers osteotome and magnetic-mallet "densification" studies, reviewing only counterclockwise OD vs subtractive drills — a methodological tightening over earlier SRs (Gaspar 2021, Yu 2022, Gaikwad 2022, Lima Monteiro 2024, Kalra 2025).
- Reports successful IT range from the underlying literature as 25–60 N·cm (Berardini 2016).
- Longitudinal ISQ data were insufficient to test trends over time — primary stability advantage demonstrated only at insertion.
- Open access (CC BY-NC-ND 4.0).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
The authors restricted inclusion to *in vivo human clinical trials* (single- or multi-implant placement in partially or fully edentulous segments) comparing counterclockwise OD drills against conventional subtractive osteotomy in healthy adults. They explicitly excluded studies using Summers osteotomes, magnetic mallets, and other adjunctive densifying techniques that prior SRs had bundled in.

Databases were searched up to 11 September 2025. PRISMA 2020 was followed. Quality assessment used RoB 2 (RCTs), Newcastle–Ottawa (non-randomized), and GRADE for the overall body of evidence. Outcomes were IT (N·cm) and ISQ at the time of placement. ISQ was synthesized with a random-effects model.

By tightening inclusion to OD-only studies, this SR addresses the prior literature's frequent conflation of densification methodologies. The narrower scope explains why effect sizes appear smaller than in Yu 2022 (which lumped OD with osteotome and under-drilling).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- IT: OD 45.75 ± 6.55 N·cm vs standard 38.00 ± 7.01 N·cm — difference ~7.75 N·cm, P < 0.001.
- ISQ: random-effects mean difference 3.24, 95% CI 0.72–5.95, P = 0.024. Lower CI bound is close to 0; effect is statistically significant but the magnitude is small (<5 ISQ units).
- 685 implants total (343 OD / 342 standard).
- Longitudinal ISQ pooling was not possible due to heterogeneity / insufficient data.
- I² and study-level effect sizes not extracted from the available text but stated to be presented in the full paper.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- ISQ effect size (MD 3.24, CI 0.72–5.95) is statistically significant but clinically modest — ISQ within ±5 units is within the normal measurement noise band; whether this translates to fewer implant failures is not established by this SR.
- No longitudinal ISQ pooling — cannot say if the early-stability advantage is maintained during the stability-dip period (weeks 2–4).
- Heterogeneity (I²) values not retrievable from the available text.
- Some included studies likely have industry ties (the OD literature is concentrated around the Versah/Coelho/Huwais ecosystem) — author-level conflict-of-interest filtering across the included trials is not described in the abstract.
- The SR cannot answer downstream outcomes that matter clinically: implant survival, MBL, soft-tissue stability, time to functional loading.
- Search restricted to English-language articles; non-English RCTs may have been missed (Yu 2022 by contrast included Mandarin sources).

## 7. Glossary
- **Insertion torque (IT)**: Peak rotational torque (N·cm) required to seat an implant; surrogate for primary mechanical stability.
- **Implant stability quotient (ISQ)**: Resonance-frequency-derived stability metric (0–100, higher = stiffer interface); Osstell device.
- **Random-effects model**: Meta-analysis model that assumes heterogeneity between studies and yields wider, more conservative CIs than fixed-effects.
- **RoB 2 / Newcastle–Ottawa / GRADE**: Standard tools for assessing within-study and across-study evidence quality.
- **Misch bone classification**: D1–D4 quality scale used to stratify recipient bone; D3/D4 is "soft" / low-density.
