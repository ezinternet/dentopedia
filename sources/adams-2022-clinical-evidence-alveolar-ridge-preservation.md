## One-line Summary
Narrative review + 2 case reports of late xenograft failure (5–10 years post-ARP) published in BDJ — challenges the routine use of ARP by highlighting that statistical dimensional preservation does not consistently translate to patient-centred clinical benefit, and documents under-reported chronic graft complications.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: British Dental Journal 2022;233(6):471–476 (Sept 23 2022)
- **Author**: Single-author UK restorative dentist (private practice)
- **Type**: Narrative review with embedded case reports
- **Audience**: General/restorative dentists in NHS/private UK context

## 2. Key Contributions
- Skeptical counterweight to ARP-positive SR/MA literature — surfaces clinical experience that **xenograft can fail late (5–13 years) with fibrous encapsulation, chronic inflammation, and peri-implantitis-like presentation**.
- Documents 2 illustrated cases:
  - Case 1: Xenograft ARP → implant at 11 → 12 mo draining sinus, non-integrated graft debrided.
  - Case 2: ARP ≥10 yrs prior → 21 peri-implantitis presentation → non-integrated graft discovered, debrided.
- Highlights global BSM market ($663M in 2020, projected 11.4% CAGR 2021–2028) and notes that 29% of all bone-graft use is now ARP — commercial pressure context.
- Re-frames Carmagnola 2003 histology (7-mo Bio-Oss core biopsies: lamellar bone 26±23.7%, residual graft 21±20%, contact 40±37%) as evidence of variable integration.

## 3. Methodology
- Narrative literature review (not systematic) of recent SRs and case series.
- Two case reports from author's private practice with serial radiographs and intraoperative photos.
- Histology of debrided material in one case: "inflamed mucosa and granulation tissue; calcified material of unclear nature."

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Notes that despite statistically significant dimensional preservation, systematic reviews fail to demonstrate clear patient-centred clinical benefit (e.g., implant survival, aesthetic outcome at 5+ years).
- Late xenograft failure presents as: peri-implantitis with non-integrated graft, fibrous encapsulation, chronic infection.
- Rodriguez & Nowzari 2019 case series cited: long-term xenograft complications up to 13 years.
- Recommends: limit ARP to specific indications (Type 2/3 socket per Elian, >50% buccal plate loss per Jung), do not use ARP universally.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Single-author narrative review — selection bias toward skeptical conclusions.
- 2 case reports cannot estimate failure rates.
- Histology of Case 2 inconclusive ("calcified material of unclear nature").
- No prospective cohort data on long-term graft fate after ARP.

## 7. Glossary
- **BSM**: Bone Substitute Material.
- **ARP**: Alveolar Ridge Preservation.
- **Type 2 socket (Elian)**: Facial soft tissue present, buccal plate partially missing.
- **Type 3 socket (Elian)**: Both facial soft tissue and buccal plate markedly reduced.
- **Fibrous encapsulation**: Connective tissue formation around graft particles instead of bony integration.
