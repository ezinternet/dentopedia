## Why Ingested

Introduces the endocrown as the conservative, post-free alternative to traditional (post-)core-crown for endodontically treated teeth with severe coronal damage. This SR provides the laboratory (in-vitro) evidence base that drives clinical adoption, directly extending [[wiki/post-and-core/]] knowledge on when to choose endocrown over a post-retained restoration.

## One-line Summary

SR of 31 in-vitro studies: endocrowns show similar or superior survival, more favorable failure patterns, and lower stresses than (post-)core-crown restorations for anterior and posterior endodontically treated teeth.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of Esthetic and Restorative Dentistry, 36(2):303-323
- **Publication date**: 2023-08-12
- **Study type**: Systematic Review (in-vitro studies only)
- **Included studies**: 31 in-vitro studies (9 molar, 14 premolar, 8 anterior)
- **Search databases**: MEDLINE/PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides the first comprehensive SR comparing endocrown vs (post-)core-crown biomechanics across all tooth types
- Demonstrates endocrown superiority in failure mode: more reparable fracture patterns
- Covers stress distribution in restorative and luting material, not just fracture load
- Encompasses anterior teeth — previously under-studied for endocrown use

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Systematic search across three major databases
- Inclusion: in-vitro biomechanical studies comparing endocrown vs traditional (post-)core-crown
- Outcomes: survival rate under fatigue and monotonic loading; failure mode; stress in restorative and luting materials (FEA and experimental)
- 31 studies stratified by tooth type: 9 molar, 14 premolar, 8 anterior
- abstract-only — full text not retrieved

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Survival/fracture load**: majority of studies — endocrown ≥ (post-)core-crown, regardless of tooth type
- **Failure mode**: endocrown failures more favorable (reparable/adhesive) vs (post-)core-crown (often catastrophic root fracture)
- **Stress in restorative material**: endocrown produced lower stresses for molars and premolars
- **Stress in luting material**: endocrown produced lower stresses for premolars
- Conclusion consistent across anterior and posterior teeth

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- All 31 studies are in-vitro — no clinical RCT data included
- In-vitro conditions do not fully replicate oral environment (fatigue, moisture, aging)
- Heterogeneity in test protocols limits quantitative meta-analysis
- Need for long-term clinical studies to validate laboratory findings

## 6. Related Work

- Post-and-core: fiber post vs cast post survival, ferrule effect, endocrown alternative
- Endocrown indications in clinical practice — post-free option for severely damaged teeth

## 7. Glossary

- **Endocrown**: Single-piece ceramic restoration anchored into pulp chamber macroretention; no intracanal post required
- **Core-crown**: Crown restoration supported by a separately built core (composite/amalgam/cast metal) with or without intracanal post
- **Monotonic load**: Single increasing load to failure (fracture resistance test)
- **Fatigue loading**: Cyclic loading simulating masticatory cycles
- **FEA**: Finite element analysis — computational stress distribution modeling
