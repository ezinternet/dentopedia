## Why Ingested

사용자가 CAD/CAM 치과 기공(dental laboratory) 관련 논문을 요청하여 인제스트. 기존 [[wiki/complete-denture/jafarpour-2024-cadcam-versus-traditional-complete-dentures]]는 CAD/CAM 총의치가 기공·총비용에서 유의하게 저렴하다고 보고했으나, 본 SR+MA는 비용-효율성(cost-efficiency) 메타분석 방법론으로 재검증한 결과 유의한 차이가 없음을 보여 상반된 결론을 제공한다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (5 studies, 4 retrospective + 1 prospective, n=184 patients, J Prosthodont 2025) found no statistically significant difference between digital (CAD/CAM) and conventional removable complete denture workflows in laboratory costs (MD -239.77 USD, p=0.106), clinical costs (MD 74.39 USD, p=0.451), total costs (MD -357.76 USD, p=0.258), or number of sessions (MD -1.47, p=0.351); operator experience significantly affected clinical costs and session count.

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (5편, 후향적 4 + 전향적 1, n=184명, J Prosthodont 2025): 디지털(CAD/CAM) 대 전통(conventional) 총의치(완전의치) 제작 워크플로우 간 기공비(laboratory cost, MD -239.77달러, p=0.106), 임상비(clinical cost, MD 74.39달러, p=0.451), 총비용(total cost, MD -357.76달러, p=0.258), 내원횟수(sessions, MD -1.47, p=0.351) 모두 통계적으로 유의한 차이 없음; 술자 숙련도(operator experience)는 임상비와 내원횟수에 유의한 영향을 미침.

- **Title**: Cost-efficiency of digital versus conventional workflow for removable complete dentures: A systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Authors**: Muehlemann E, Pachiou A, Sáenz-Ravello G, Jung RE, Strauss FJ, Liguori MG
- **Journal**: Journal of Prosthodontics, 2025;35(3):243-251
- **DOI**: 10.1111/jopr.70074
- **PROSPERO registration**: CRD42024607329
- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (SR+MA) of economic/cost outcomes

## 2. Key Contributions

- First quantitative meta-analysis (not merely a cost-analysis descriptive review) comparing laboratory, clinical, and total costs plus number of treatment sessions between digital (CAD/CAM) and conventional removable complete denture (RCD) workflows.
- Applied a rigorous economic-evaluation data-harmonization method (Bagepally et al. framework): adjusting each study's original currency to 2025 values via country-specific inflation indices, then standardizing to 2025 international USD using Purchasing Power Parity (PPP).
- Performed subgroup analysis by operator experience (dental students vs. prosthodontic specialists), revealing that experience — not workflow type per se — was the dominant driver of clinical cost and session-count variability.
- Directly contrasts with an earlier systematic review (cost-analysis only, no patient-outcome integration) that had reported lower laboratory and total costs for digital workflows.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Conducted per Cochrane systematic review guidelines and reported per PRISMA 2020.
- Databases: Medline (PubMed), Embase, Web of Science, Scopus, searched to February 10, 2025; plus reference lists, OpenGrey gray literature, and clinicaltrials.gov.
- PICO: edentulous patients receiving RCDs (P); digitally designed/manufactured RCDs (I); conventionally designed/manufactured RCDs (C); cost-efficiency (O).
- Inclusion: RCTs, non-controlled prospective studies, observational studies, case series (English only) comparing digital vs. conventional RCD fabrication, or reporting conventional RCD outcomes alone.
- Exclusion: case reports, in vitro studies, reviews, editorials, conference abstracts, and implant-supported/attachment-retained (including double-crown) overdentures.
- Two independent reviewers (A.P., M.G.L.) screened title/abstract then full text via Rayyan; disagreements resolved by two co-authors; Cohen's kappa used for inter-reviewer agreement (kappa=0.93 at full-text stage).
- Risk of bias: ROBINS-I V2 tool for non-randomized studies.
- Statistics: random-effects model with Hartung-Knapp adjustment; heterogeneity via Q-test and I²; τ² estimated via REML/Q-profile methods; subgroup analysis (students vs. specialists) via Chi² test; "meta" package in R v4.4.2; patient as unit of analysis; WebPlotDigitizer v5.2 used to extract graphed data where needed.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Search yielded 540 records → 407 after deduplication → 8 full-text screened → 5 studies included (2019–2025; 4 retrospective, 1 prospective; 184 patients, 202 RCDs).
- Conventional RCDs: compression molding in all studies. Digital RCDs: milling (3 studies) or milling/3D printing (2 studies).
- **Laboratory costs**: pooled MD = -239.7 (2025 USD; 95% CI -714.4 to 234.8), t=-1.6, not significant; I²=77.7%, Q-test p=0.004 (substantial heterogeneity). No significant subgroup difference by operator experience (Chi²=0.6, n.s.).
- **Clinical costs**: pooled MD = 74.4 (95% CI -120.0 to 268.7), t=0.7, not significant; I²=97.8%, Q-test p<0.0001. Significant subgroup difference by experience (Chi²=260.9, p<0.0001) — clinical costs varied considerably with operator skill level.
- **Total costs**: pooled MD = -357.7 (95% CI -1340.1 to 624.6), t=-1.2, not significant; I²=73.8%, Q-test p=0.02. No significant subgroup difference (Chi²=1.6, n.s.).
- **Number of sessions**: pooled MD = -1.5 (95% CI -6.7 to 3.8), t=-1.2, not significant; I²=95.1%, Q-test p<0.0001. Significant subgroup difference by experience (Chi²=14.9, p=0.0001) — experienced clinicians trended toward fewer sessions with digital workflows.
- Risk of bias: 4/5 studies moderate risk, 1/5 high risk (ROBINS-I V2).
- No included study reported quantitative OHRQoL or patient satisfaction data usable in meta-analysis.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Substantial heterogeneity across studies in operator experience, patient demographics, fabrication protocols, and economic-evaluation methodology.
- No quantitative patient-centered effectiveness data (OHRQoL, satisfaction) available — results reflect efficiency/resource-utilization, not patient-reported treatment effectiveness.
- Initial capital investment in digital equipment (milling machines, scanners, software licenses) was not captured in any included study's cost accounting, likely underestimating true digital-workflow cost.
- Clinical costs were largely time-based estimates rather than actual patient billing, so findings represent resource utilization rather than direct patient expense.
- Cross-country economic contexts (Switzerland, US, Japan, Italy) differ in willingness-to-pay thresholds, limiting direct generalizability of pooled cost estimates.
- Only 5 studies met inclusion criteria — moderate-to-high risk of bias in 5/5; call for more standardized, prospective, multi-center economic-evaluation studies with consistent cost-reporting methods and inclusion of equipment amortization.

## 7. Glossary

- **RCD (Removable Complete Denture)**: 총의치/완전의치 — a full removable prosthesis replacing all teeth in an edentulous arch.
- **CAD-CAM**: Computer-Aided Design / Computer-Aided Manufacturing digital workflow, typically combining intraoral scanning with milling or 3D printing.
- **Cost-efficiency**: an economic-evaluation framework relating treatment cost to clinical outcome/effectiveness, distinct from simple cost analysis.
- **PPP (Purchasing Power Parity)**: currency standardization index used to make cross-country costs comparable.
- **ROBINS-I V2**: Risk Of Bias In Non-randomized Studies of Interventions, version 2 — a bias-assessment tool for non-RCT designs.
- **Hartung-Knapp adjustment**: a correction to random-effects meta-analysis confidence intervals that improves accuracy with few studies.
- **MD (Mean Difference)**: pooled effect-size metric expressing the average numeric difference (cost, sessions) between two groups.
