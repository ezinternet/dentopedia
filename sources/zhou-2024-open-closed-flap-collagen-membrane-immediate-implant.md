## Why Ingested

Part of a recent GBR / immediate-implant wound-closure collection. This two-cohort study tests whether open healing of an exposed collagen membrane can replace flap-release/CTG complete closure at immediate-implant GBR sites — directly extending the flap-vs-flapless debate in [[immediate-implant/pitman-2023-immediate-implant-flap-flapless-sr-ma]] and the open-vs-closed healing question in [[immediate-implant/bouffleur-2026-open-vs-closed-healing-immediate-implant-rct]].

## One-line Summary

Two retrospective cohorts (118 patients; 58 complete closure vs 60 open healing) of immediate implant + simultaneous GBR with Bio-Gide: open healing of the exposed collagen membrane gave bone/soft-tissue outcomes equivalent to flap-released complete closure when initial crestal wound width was ≤6 mm, with less pain/swelling.

## 1. Document Information

- Type: Retrospective comparison of two cohorts (CC vs OH)
- Journal: Journal of Dentistry 2024; vol 141, article 104735 (February 2024)
- DOI: 10.1016/j.jdent.2023.104735
- Setting: Fudan University Zhongshan Hospital, Shanghai, China; surgeries 1999–2021.
- Funding: National Natural Science Foundation of China (81870793); Pujiang Talents (21PJD010).

## 2. Key Contributions

- First assessment of the **open-healing (exposed collagen membrane)** technique applied to immediate implant placement with simultaneous GBR (previously used mainly for socket preservation).
- Provides a practical wound-width threshold (≤6 mm initial crestal exposure) below which open healing performs comparably to complete closure.
- Suggests that the additional surgery for complete closure (periosteal-release flap, CTG, or rotated palatal flap) can often be avoided.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 118 patients receiving Bio-Gide collagen membrane coverage for immediate implant + GBR; 20 anterior and 98 posterior teeth.
- **Complete closure (CC) group**, n=58: gingival flaps released to fully cover the membrane (protocol used before 2020).
- **Open healing (OH) group**, n=60: no flap release; membrane left exposed (protocol since January 2020).
- All received amoxicillin + analgesics for 7 days.
- Crestal open wound width measured post-op (W0) and at 1, 2, and 16 weeks (W16).
- Bone mass change assessed by CBCT after placement and at W16.
- For 16 OH-group patients, gingival/bone tissue over the cover screw harvested and analyzed (staining) at W16.
- No statistically significant baseline differences (age, gender, implant site, brand) between groups (P > 0.05).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **No wound dehiscence** occurred in the CC group from W0 to W16.
- Vertical and horizontal bone dimension changes **not significantly different** between OH and CC.
- OH open wound width narrowed: mean 5.8 (±1.3) mm at W0 → 4.7 (±1.3) at W1 → 3.3 (±1.2) at W2 → 0.1 (±0.4) mm at W16.
- OH soft tissue completely healed at W16 when initial wound width was **≤6 mm**.
- For initial widths **≥7 mm**, cover screws were exposed in 5/16 patients at W16, but this did not affect final restorations.
- Histology (staining): keratinized mucosa and new bone formation above the implant in the OH group.
- Conclusion: open healing achieves healing outcomes similar to complete closure; releasing flaps or using tissue grafts is unnecessary, with less postoperative pain and swelling.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Retrospective, single-center, sequential-protocol design (CC before 2020, OH after) — temporal confounding (surgeon experience, materials) possible.
- Short outcome horizon (16 weeks) for the primary healing endpoints.
- Histology limited to 16 OH patients; no formal pain/swelling effect sizes in the abstract.

## 7. Glossary

- **OH** — Open healing (exposed collagen membrane, no flap release).
- **CC** — Complete closure (flap released for full membrane coverage).
- **GBR** — Guided bone regeneration.
- **CTG** — Connective tissue graft.
- **RPF** — Rotated palatal flap.
- **W0/W16** — Post-op week 0 / week 16 time points.
