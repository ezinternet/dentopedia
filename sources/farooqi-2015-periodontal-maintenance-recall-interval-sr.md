## One-line Summary
치주유지치료(Periodontal Maintenance, PM) 주기 SR (JEBDP 2015): 8편 코호트, RCT 없음; 3–6개월 단기 주기가 치아 보존 유리; 특정 주기를 모든 환자에게 권고할 근거 부족.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Evidence-Based Dental Practice 2015
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.jebdp.2015.10.001
- **Study type**: Systematic review
- **Search**: MEDLINE, EMBASE, PubMed; until April 2014; 1,095 abstracts screened

## 2. Key Contributions
- First systematic review specifically focused on optimal recall interval for periodontal maintenance (PM)
- Only 8 cohort studies met inclusion criteria; zero RCTs found — highlights major evidence gap
- All 8 studies assessed PM compliance with recommended 3–6 month interval as primary focus
- Shorter PM intervals (3–6 months) associated with more tooth retention
- In 2 studies reporting mean recall intervals by group: significant tooth loss differences when interval neared 12 months
- Conclusion: evidence for prescribing a specific PM interval is weak; further RCTs or large electronic database evaluations needed

## 3. Methodology
- **Databases**: MEDLINE, EMBASE, PubMed; search until April 2014
- **Screened**: 1,095 abstracts/titles
- **Quality assessment**: CASP (Critical Appraisal Skills Programme)
- **Inclusion**: Cohort studies with PM compliance as variable; reported tooth retention or clinical attachment outcomes
- **Exclusion**: Studies without PM recall intervals; RCTs not found

## 4. Key Results
- Compliant patients (RC, 3–6 months): better tooth retention vs erratic/non-compliant
- Non-compliant patients: progressively more tooth loss as interval extended toward 12 months
- No RCT evidence exists to define an optimal PM interval for all patient risk levels

## 5. Limitations
- No RCTs; all studies observational (cohort)
- PM interval varied across studies; no standardization
- Cannot determine ideal interval for different caries/periodontitis risk categories
- Author contact attempted for missing data; limited response
