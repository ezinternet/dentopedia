## Why Ingested

Tests whether adding resorbable synthetic biomaterials (α-TCP or biphasic calcium phosphate, BBCP) to DBBM overcomes DBBM's slow resorption problem while maintaining volume. Addresses the hybrid-grafting strategy for bone augmentation — relevant to understanding DBBM's limitations and how synthetic adjuncts affect the resorption/bone-formation balance. Extends [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/fischer-2022-bone-envelope-arp-dbbm-sr-ma]] by probing the material-science side of why DBBM may underperform as a sole graft.

## One-line Summary

Rat critical-size defect study (n=30, 5 groups, 4 weeks): α-TCP or BBCP mixed 1:1 with DBBM showed similar new bone formation to negative control, paradoxically, while DBBM alone showed significantly less new bone than negative control; synthetic additives resorbed without volume change.

- **Year**: 2022
- **Journal**: International Journal of Molecular Sciences (IJMS)
- **DOI**: 10.3390/ijms231810516
- **Study type**: Animal (preclinical in vivo — rat critical-size calvarial defect model)
- **Institution**: University Hospital, University of Bern, Switzerland; Nippon Dental University, Japan; University of Bern Periodontology

## 2. Key Contributions

- Demonstrated that adding resorbable synthetic materials (α-TCP or BBCP) to DBBM at 1:1 ratio rescues new bone formation that is paradoxically suppressed by DBBM alone.
- DBBM alone showed significantly lower new bone area than negative control (empty defect) at 4 weeks (p = 0.0252).
- All synthetic additive groups (α-TCP+DBBM, BBCP 3%HA+DBBM, BBCP 6%HA+DBBM) showed bone formation comparable to empty defect control.
- Synthetic materials resorbed (decreased material density) without significant volume change — confirming resorption at molecular level without macroscopic collapse.
- HA coating degree (3% vs 6%) did not significantly affect resorption or bone formation over 4 weeks.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Animal model**: 15 Wistar rats (8–10 weeks), bilateral cranial critical-size defects (2 defects/rat = 30 total defects)
- **Treatment groups** (n=6 defects each):
  1. Negative control (NC): empty defect
  2. DBBM alone (Bio-Oss)
  3. α-TCP + DBBM (1:1 by volume)
  4. BBCP 3%HA/97%α-TCP + DBBM (1:1)
  5. BBCP 6%HA/94%α-TCP + DBBM (1:1)
- **Healing period**: 4 weeks post-surgery
- **Assessment methods**:
  - Micro-CT: mineralized tissue volume/total volume (MV/TV), mineral density (MD), horizontal defect closure (HDC)
  - Histology + histomorphometry: new bone area, residual material area, tissue types
- **Outcome**: 1 defect excluded due to dura mater damage; 29 defects analyzed

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Group | New Bone Area | Mineral Density vs DBBM | Key finding |
|---|---|---|---|
| NC (empty) | Reference | — | Baseline natural healing |
| DBBM alone | Significantly lower than NC (p=0.0252) | Reference | Paradoxical suppression |
| α-TCP + DBBM | Similar to NC | Higher (p=0.0319) | Rescued bone formation |
| BBCP 3%HA + DBBM | Similar to NC | Higher (p=0.0050) | Rescued bone formation |
| BBCP 6%HA + DBBM | Similar to NC | Higher (p=0.0191) | Rescued bone formation |

- All biomaterial groups showed greater MV/TV than NC, confirming osteoconductive scaffold function
- Material density decreased in all synthetic additive groups (resorption occurring) while volume unchanged at 4 weeks
- No significant difference between 3% and 6% HA-coated BBCP on bone formation or resorption
- Lateral defect borders showed more bone ingrowth than central areas (consistent with centripetal healing pattern)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Very short observation period (4 weeks); DBBM's osteoconductive benefit likely emerges at longer time points (12–24 weeks)
- Rat calvarial model: flat, membranous bone with different healing biology than alveolar bone
- 1:1 mixing ratio not clinically optimized — different ratios may yield better results
- No membrane used; clinical GBR scenarios would include membrane
- Micro-CT cannot reliably distinguish newly formed bone from residual DBBM (similar mineral density)
- Small sample size (n=6/group)

## 6. Related Work

- DBBM's well-established long-term volume maintenance (14–16 citations in paper): the slow resorption that makes it valuable for ridge preservation may paradoxically limit early bone formation
- BBCP (biphasic calcium phosphate) osteoconductive/biodegradable properties: α-TCP core degrades, HA coating provides scaffold
- Fujioka-Kobayashi et al. (earlier work): established BBCP biocompatibility in critical-size rabbit defects
- Chakar et al. (2014): BCP vs DBBM with platelet lysate — BCP showed greater osteoconductivity and resorption than DBBM (aligns with this study's finding)

## 7. Glossary

- **DBBM**: 탈단백 우골 — deproteinized bovine bone mineral; xenograft slowly resorbed by osteoclasts
- **α-TCP (alpha-tricalcium phosphate)**: α-삼인산칼슘 — resorbable synthetic calcium phosphate
- **BBCP (Bi-layered Biphasic Calcium Phosphate)**: 이층형 이상성 인산칼슘 — α-TCP core coated with nanocrystalline HA
- **HA (Hydroxyapatite)**: 수산화인회석 — crystalline calcium phosphate similar to bone mineral
- **Critical-size defect**: 임계크기 결손 — bone defect that does not heal spontaneously without intervention
- **MV/TV (Mineralized Volume/Total Volume)**: 광화 부피 비율 — micro-CT metric for tissue mineralization density
- **HDC (Horizontal Defect Closure)**: 수평 결손 폐쇄 — percentage of defect width filled by new bone on sagittal plane
- **Osteoconduction**: 골전도 — ability of a scaffold to guide new bone growth along its surface
