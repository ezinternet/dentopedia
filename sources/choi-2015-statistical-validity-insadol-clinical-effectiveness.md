## One-line Summary
Korea University + Mayo Clinic critical methodological appraisal of the 5 (4 analyzable) Korean clinical trials of Insadol (옥수수 불검화 정량추출물 = titrated unsaponifiable Zea mays L. fraction) used by the manufacturer to claim periodontal efficacy — concludes that none of the trials provide compelling evidence due to flawed randomization, missing rationale for sample size, inappropriate statistical tests, unclear blinding, and inconsistent follow-up periods.

## 2. Key Contributions
- **First independent methodological audit of the Korean Insadol clinical evidence base**, written by a Korean prosthodontics academic + a Mayo Clinic clinician — i.e., genuinely external review.
- Identifies that Insadol (옥수수 불검화 정량추출물 = titrated unsaponifiable Zea mays L. fraction) had **66.8% market share** of Korean periodontal-treatment drug market in 2011, despite its evidence base resting on 5 small clinical trials performed between 1982 and 1994 — and **no further clinical trial since 1994** (a 20-year gap at time of writing).
- Tabulates 9 methodological variables (publication year, age range, sample size, follow-up period, co-intervention with periodontal therapy, randomization performed, randomization success-check, blinded measurement, type of statistical test) across the 4 analyzable trials (1982, 1988, 1991, 1994) — the 5th was Polish-language with abstract only and excluded.
- Concludes that **none of the trials demonstrate compelling clinical effectiveness** of Insadol for periodontal disease, either as monotherapy or as adjunct to standard periodontal treatment.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: Critical appraisal of published clinical trials listed by the Insadol manufacturer (www.insadol.co.kr accessed 1 March 2014)
- **Source of trial list**: manufacturer website listed 5 clinical trials as proof of clinical effectiveness; 4 were retrievable in Korean or English (1982, 1988, 1991, 1994); 1 trial was in Polish with abstract only and excluded
- **Extracted variables** (9):
  1. Year of publication
  2. Subject age range
  3. Sample size
  4. Follow-up period
  5. Co-administration with contemporary periodontal treatment
  6. Whether randomization was performed
  7. Whether randomization balance was checked, and outcome
  8. Whether blinded outcome measurement was performed
  9. Type of statistical test used
- **No quantitative meta-analysis**; method is narrative critical appraisal

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Critical findings per trial**:

| Year | Sample / Arms | Follow-up | Co-treatment | Randomization | Blinding | Stat test | Key methodological flaw |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1982 | Test 20, Control 10 (placebo only) | 3 mo | None | Performed but **failed** — test arm had more severe periodontitis than control | Not stated | not stated/inadequate | Imbalance at baseline invalidates between-group comparison |
| 1988 | 4 arms × 10 (Insadol mono, Insadol + scaling, OHI + scaling, placebo) | 1 mo | Variable | Performed, balance not checked | Not stated | F-statistic (ANOVA) used, but n = 10 per arm means normality unverified; Kruskal-Wallis would be more appropriate; no multiple-comparison correction | Type I error inflation, no rationale for which co-treatment is responsible |
| 1991 | 87 patients, 2,056 tooth surfaces, 3 surfaces/tooth × 6 arms | 1 mo | Multiple combinations (OHI ± scaling ± flap surgery) | Unclear | Unclear paired vs unpaired t-test | t-test (correct one not specified) | Wrong unit of analysis (surface vs patient); randomization to combinations unclear; can't decide which adjunct combo is best |
| 1994 | Periodontal flap surgery patients, test + control | 2 mo | Periodontal flap surgery | Performed, balance not checked | Not stated | Student t-test (should be paired); no direct between-group comparison of changes | Within-group only; cannot infer Insadol benefit |

**Aggregate findings**:
- Age ranges across trials: 20–66 years (no consistent target population)
- Sample-size rationale: **none of the trials state any rationale**
- Follow-up period (1–4 months) chosen without pathophysiological rationale for periodontal latent period
- Randomization performed in all 4 trials, but **3 of 4 trials never checked balance after randomization**, and 1 of 4 trials explicitly failed
- Blinding **unclear in 2 of 4** trials
- Inappropriate statistical test in **3 of 4** trials
- **No clinical trial of Insadol has been performed since 1994** (a 20-year gap at time of writing)
- Conclusion: the four available trials do **not** demonstrate compelling evidence of therapeutic effectiveness of Insadol for periodontal disease, with or without contemporary periodontal treatment

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- The paper itself is a methodological critique — its conclusion is "evidence is insufficient," not "drug is ineffective" (absence of evidence ≠ evidence of absence)
- 1 trial (Polish-language) excluded for unretrievable full text — possible inclusion bias against the manufacturer
- Quantitative re-analysis (e.g., recomputing p-values, recalculating effect sizes from raw data) was not attempted — only methodological labeling
- Authors call for a properly designed RCT of Insadol that includes:
  - Pre-specified sample-size calculation
  - Properly stratified randomization with balance check
  - Blinded outcome assessment
  - Appropriate statistical tests (paired/repeated-measures for within-subject comparisons; rank-based tests for small samples)
  - Direct between-group comparison of clinical periodontal endpoints
  - Inclusion of the elderly population (up to ~82 years, current Korean life expectancy)

## 7. Glossary
- 인사돌 (Insadol): Korean OTC periodontal drug, active ingredient = 옥수수 불검화 정량추출물 = titrated unsaponifiable Zea mays L. fraction
- 옥수수 불검화 정량추출물 (titrated unsaponifiable Zea mays L. fraction): the active ingredient — sterols/triterpenoids/other lipid-soluble compounds from corn that resist saponification (i.e., the non-soap-forming lipid fraction)
- 치주질환치료제 (periodontal drug): Korean regulatory category encompassing OTC adjunct supplements
- Randomization check: post-randomization comparison of baseline characteristics between arms to confirm balance
- Statistical validity: appropriateness of the statistical analysis to the data, design, and research question
- Blinded measurement: outcome assessment done without knowledge of group assignment (single blind, often), to prevent ascertainment bias
- ANOVA / F-statistic: parametric test for differences among ≥3 group means; assumes normality and homoscedasticity
- Kruskal-Wallis test: non-parametric counterpart of one-way ANOVA, appropriate for small or non-normal samples
- Paired vs unpaired t-test: paired = same subjects measured twice (pre/post); unpaired = independent groups. Using unpaired when paired is required loses statistical power and is technically wrong
- Confidence interval: range that quantifies precision of an estimate; relevant for re-evaluating non-significant findings
- Confounding variable: a variable that influences both treatment assignment and outcome, biasing the apparent treatment effect
- CRIS / KCT registration: Clinical Research Information Service of Korea — Korean trial registry
