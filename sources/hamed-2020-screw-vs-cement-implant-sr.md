## One-line Summary
Systematic review (12 studies): no significant overall difference in survival between screw and cement-retained implant reconstructions; cement = more biological complications; screw = more mechanical complications.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Systematic review
- **Journal**: Clinical, Cosmetic and Investigational Dentistry 12:9-16 (2020)
- **DOI**: 10.2147/CCIDE.S231070
- **Studies included**: 12

## 2. Key Contributions
- Synthesizes complication patterns across both retention types
- Identifies divergent complication profiles as the key clinical differentiator
- No overall survival difference — choice should be driven by patient/site risk factors

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: Systematic review (PRISMA)
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane, Scopus, Web of Science
- **Included**: 12 studies comparing screw vs cement-retained implant-supported reconstructions
- **Outcomes**: Survival, biological complications, mechanical complications, marginal bone loss

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Overall survival**: No statistically significant difference between SR and CR
- **Biological complications** (peri-implant mucositis, peri-implantitis, cement remnants): **higher in cement-retained**
- **Mechanical complications** (screw loosening, screw fracture, crown fracture): **higher in screw-retained**
- **Marginal bone loss**: Trend toward lower MBL in screw-retained (not always significant)
- **Retrievability**: Screw-retained significantly superior for re-intervention

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Heterogeneity across included studies
- Variable follow-up periods
- Differences in implant systems, abutment types, and cement types not controlled

## 6. Related Work
- Khurshid 2025: prospective data aligns with SR findings
- Tomar 2025: SR+MA for zirconia crowns specifically — early MBL advantage for screw-retained

## 7. Glossary
- **Biological complications**: Peri-implant tissue disease, cement remnants causing inflammation
- **Mechanical complications**: Prosthetic hardware failure — screw loosening, fractures
- **Retrievability**: Ease of removing the crown for maintenance or re-intervention
