## One-line Summary
SR of 16 papers covering 2176 coronectomies: IAN injury 0.59%, lingual nerve injury 0.22%, infection 3.95%, root extraction 5.28% — coronectomy is a low-risk, feasible alternative to third molar extraction.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Healthcare 2021, 9, 750
- **DOI**: 10.3390/healthcare9060750
- **PROSPERO**: CRD42020135485

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive SR on coronectomy feasibility and complication rates
- 16 papers, 2176 coronectomies analyzed
- Confirms coronectomy as a low-risk alternative when IAN proximity is a concern
- Only 5 studies judged methodologically appropriate (quality assessment)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Systematic review; PROSPERO-registered
- Inclusion: RCTs, CCTs, prospective/retrospective studies with/without control group
- Outcomes extracted: IAN injury, lingual nerve (LN) injury, infection, dry socket, root migration, extraction of remaining root, reintervention, pain

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
| Complication | Rate |
|---|---|
| IAN injury | 0.59% |
| Lingual nerve injury | 0.22% |
| Infection | 3.95% |
| Dry socket | 1.12% |
| Root extraction (reintervention) | 5.28% |
| Any reintervention | 1.13% |
| Pain | 22.04% (most reported complication) |

- IAN injury rate of 0.59% far lower than complete extraction (0.5–8%)
- Root migration occurs over time but rarely requires re-extraction

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Only 5 of 16 included studies were methodologically appropriate
- Heterogeneous study designs and follow-up periods
- No meta-analysis; descriptive SR only

## 6. Related Work
- Complements cervera-espert-2016 (SR+MA showing root migrates ~2mm/2yr)
- Relates to sarikov-2014 (IAN injury risk factors for third molar extraction)
- Relates to li-2022 (IAC position as risk factor for IAN injury)

## 7. Glossary
- **Coronectomy / Intentional Partial Odontectomy**: removal of crown only, leaving roots in socket
- **IAN**: Inferior Alveolar Nerve
- **LN**: Lingual Nerve
- **IAC**: Inferior Alveolar Canal
