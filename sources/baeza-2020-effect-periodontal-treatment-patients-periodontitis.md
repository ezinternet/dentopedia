## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 9 RCTs: scaling and root planing in type 2 diabetics with periodontitis significantly reduced HbA1c (mean difference 0.56%, 0.36-0.75) and CRP (DM 1.89, 1.70-2.08), both p<0.01 with I²=0% heterogeneity.

## 한줄요약

9개 RCT 체계적문헌고찰·메타분석: 제2형 당뇨 + 치주염 환자에서 치석제거·치근활택술(SRP)이 당화혈색소(HbA1c)를 0.56%(0.36-0.75) 감소시키고 C-반응성단백(CRP)을 1.89(1.70-2.08) 낮춤(모두 p<0.01, 이질성 I²=0%).

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis (PRISMA-compliant, Cochrane standards)
- **Journal**: Journal of Applied Oral Science, 2020;28:e20190248
- **DOI**: 10.1590/1678-7757-2019-0248
- **Authors/affiliation**: Baeza, Morales, Cisterna, Cavalla, Jara, Isamitt, Pino, Gamonal — Universidad de Chile, Facultad de Odontología, Santiago, Chile
- **Clinical question (PICO)**: In patients with periodontitis and type 2 diabetes (T2D), does conventional periodontal treatment vs. no periodontal treatment improve metabolic control (HbA1c) and reduce systemic inflammation (CRP)?

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies the effect of scaling and root planing (SRP) on **both** metabolic control (HbA1c) and systemic inflammation (CRP) in T2D patients — earlier SRs largely limited their analysis to HbA1c alone.
- Adds CRP as a co-primary outcome, framed as a bridge to cardiovascular risk reduction given CRP's role as an independent predictor of cardiovascular disease.
- Demonstrates a statistically significant, clinically meaningful HbA1c reduction (~0.56%) with no detectable between-study heterogeneity.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases**: MEDLINE via PubMed and Cochrane CENTRAL, from oldest records to 10 July 2018.
- **Search terms**: "periodontitis" OR "periodontal" AND (therapy OR treatment) AND (diabetes OR metabolic control OR glycemic control OR glycated hemoglobin OR HbA1c OR systemic inflammation OR C-reactive protein OR hsCRP).
- **Eligible design**: Randomized controlled trials only.
- **Participants**: Diagnosed T2D + periodontitis; ≥3 months post-intervention follow-up; HbA1c and CRP measured at baseline and end of follow-up.
- **Intervention**: Conventional periodontal treatment — oral hygiene instruction + SRP (with or without flap surgery); comparator = no periodontal treatment (passive option).
- **Outcomes**: Primary — HbA1c and CRP; Secondary — adverse events related to periodontal treatment.
- **Quality assessment**: Cochrane Collaboration risk-of-bias tool; two independent reviewers (MB, AM), Cohen's kappa threshold 0.61, disagreements resolved by consensus.
- **Synthesis**: Random-effects meta-analysis; effect estimated as weighted average of difference in means (DM) between groups; heterogeneity by Q-statistic (χ² and I²); significance p<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **9 RCTs included**.
- **HbA1c**: SRP effective in reducing HbA1c — DM = 0.56 (95% CI 0.36–0.75); p<0.01.
- **CRP**: SRP effective in reducing CRP — DM = 1.89 (95% CI 1.70–2.08); p<0.01.
- **Heterogeneity**: None detected (I² = 0%, p>0.05) for the pooled analyses.
- **Conclusion**: SRP has a positive impact on metabolic control and reduces systemic inflammation in patients with T2D and periodontitis.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Search limited to two databases (PubMed/MEDLINE and Cochrane CENTRAL) and English-language records only — possible language/publication bias.
- Variability in SRP protocols (with/without flap surgery) and in diabetes management across included RCTs.
- Follow-up generally short (≥3 months); long-term durability of HbA1c/CRP reductions and translation into hard cardiovascular endpoints not established.
- Surprisingly low (0%) heterogeneity despite clinical diversity warrants cautious interpretation.

## 6. Related Work

- Prior systematic reviews evaluating periodontal therapy and glycemic control restricted to HbA1c endpoints (e.g., Cochrane/Engebretson-type reviews).
- Epidemiologic evidence of bidirectional T2D–periodontitis association (T2D roughly doubles risk of periodontitis).
- Literature on CRP as an inflammatory marker and independent cardiovascular risk predictor, and on AGE/RAGE-driven exaggerated inflammatory response in diabetes.

## 7. Glossary

- **SRP** — Scaling and Root Planing: mechanical removal of subgingival biofilm/calculus.
- **HbA1c** — Glycated hemoglobin; marker of long-term glycemic control (poor control defined as >7%).
- **CRP** — C-reactive protein; acute-phase reactant, sensitive systemic inflammation marker and independent cardiovascular risk predictor.
- **T2D** — Type 2 diabetes mellitus.
- **DM (in results)** — Difference in Means (weighted between-group effect estimate).
- **AGEs** — Advanced Glycation End-products; drive monocytic inflammatory response in diabetes.
- **I²** — Statistic quantifying between-study heterogeneity (0% = none).
