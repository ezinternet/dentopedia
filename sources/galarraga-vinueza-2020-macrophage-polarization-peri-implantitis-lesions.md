## One-line Summary

Immunohistochemical study of granulation tissue biopsies from 20 human peri-implantitis lesions: macrophages occupy ~14% of the inflammatory connective tissue, with M1 (CD80) significantly exceeding M2 (CD206) at advanced sites; CD68 and M1 expression correlate with probing depth.

## 한줄요약

사람 임플란트주위염 병소 20개의 육아조직 생검 면역조직화학 연구: 대식세포가 염증성 결합조직의 약 14%를 차지하고, 진행 병소에서 M1(CD80)이 M2(CD206)보다 유의하게 우세; CD68·M1 발현이 탐침깊이와 상관.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Clinical Oral Investigations 2021;25(4):2335-2344 (e-pub 2020)
- **Design**: Cross-sectional immunohistochemical characterization of human peri-implantitis granulation tissue
- **Setting**: Department of Oral Surgery and Implantology, Goethe University Frankfurt (recruitment May-Nov 2019; ethics 92/19)
- **Sample**: 20 patients / 20 implants with peri-implantitis indicated for surgery

## 2. Key Contributions

- First study to characterize and correlate macrophage M1/M2 polarization with **disease severity** at peri-implantitis sites.
- Focuses on **intrabony-defect granulation tissue** (not supracrestal soft tissue), where macrophages drive granulation-tissue formation and disease progression.
- Establishes CD68 (macrophage), CD80 (M1), CD206 (M2) histomorphometry with CD68 used as an internal control for M1/M2 attribution.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Diagnosis**: BOP ± suppuration + PD ≥6 mm + radiographic MBL ≥3 mm. Severity by MBL/implant length: slight <25%, moderate 25-50%, advanced >50%.
- **Inclusion**: ≥1 screw-type titanium implant with peri-implantitis; plaque index <1; non-smokers; no untreated periodontitis; no autoimmune/inflammatory disease; controlled diabetes (HbA1c ≤7).
- **Tissue**: all granulation tissue circumferentially harvested from the intrabony defect with plastic curettes; 4% formalin; 4 μm serial sections.
- **IHC**: CD68 (1:200), CD80 (1:200), CD206 (1:300); EnVision detection; Mayer's hematoxylin. Positive-cell area expressed as % of infiltrated connective tissue (ICT).
- **Statistics**: paired t-test (M1 vs M2 within severity), one-way ANOVA + Tukey (among groups), linear regression vs PD and BOP%. n=6 per severity group. α=0.05. Proof-of-principle sample; no a priori power calculation.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Sample**: 12 F / 8 M; 35% history of periodontitis; mean implant function 9.15 ± 6 y.
- **Biopsy**: mean surface 7.61 ± 1.4 mm²; ICT 2.5 ± 1.9 mm² (33% of biopsy).
- **Macrophage load**: CD68 = 14.36% (95% CI 11.4-17.2) of ICT — higher than prior reports (Berglundh 5.2%, Carcuac & Berglundh 11%), attributed to severity and intrabony-defect focus.
- **Polarization**: M1 (CD80) 7.07% (95% CI 5.9-9.4); M2 (CD206) 5.22% (95% CI 3.8-6.6); overall M1 vs M2 ns. **Mean M1/M2 ratio 1.56.**
- **Severity**: M1 significantly > M2 at **advanced** cases (p=0.01); slight/moderate ns; no M1/M2 difference among the three groups.
- **Correlations**: CD68% ↔ PD (Coef 3.18, R=0.77, p=2.7E-07); M1% ↔ PD (Coef 1.65, R=0.57, p=0.0001). No association of M2% or M1/M2 with PD/BOP.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- No healthy peri-implant tissue comparator (ethical) — severity groups served as internal control.
- Peri-implant soft-tissue (supracrestal) component not analyzed; total ICT not fully captured.
- Marker cross-reactivity (CD68/CD80/CD206 can stain fibroblasts, dendritic cells, endothelial subsets, B cells); M1/M2 marker selection remains unstandardized.
- Small proof-of-principle sample; future work on immune-modulating agents to shift M1→M2 for resolution/repair.

## 7. Glossary

- **Granulation tissue**: inflamed, vascular, macrophage- and fibroblast-rich connective tissue from the peri-implant intrabony defect.
- **M1 macrophage (CD80)**: classically activated, pro-inflammatory, tissue-destructive, osteolytic phenotype.
- **M2 macrophage (CD206)**: alternatively activated, anti-inflammatory, tissue-repair/angiogenic phenotype.
- **CD68**: pan-macrophage marker (used here as attribution control).
- **ICT (infiltrated connective tissue)**: the inflamed connective-tissue area quantified histomorphometrically.
- **M1/M2 ratio**: index of inflammatory (destructive) vs reparative macrophage balance.
