## Why Ingested

Extends [[occlusion/velasquez-2022-occlusal-analysis-natural-dentition-sr]] (T-Scan as the dominant quantitative occlusal-analysis tool) into the post-orthodontic finishing context: does a CR-guided occlusal adjustment one month after debonding measurably improve T-Scan-recorded force distribution? This RCT supplies a negative/trend-only answer (no significant difference at 1 or 6 months, n=30) — a real-world ceiling on what occlusal adjustment achieves when baseline static intercuspation is already acceptable. Also pairs with [[occlusion/didier-2026-occlusal-contacts-ios-vs-carbon-paper-concordance]] on the digital-vs-conventional occlusal recording theme.

## One-line Summary

Interventional RCT (n=30, Class I extraction cases) — CR-guided occlusal adjustment one month post-debonding produced NO statistically significant difference vs no-adjustment control in T-Scan III occlusal contact intensity or number at 1 or 6 months, only a non-significant trend toward better posterior force distribution.

## 한줄요약

중재 RCT (n=30, Class I 발치 케이스) — 디본딩 1개월 후 중심위(CR) 기반 교합조정을 시행해도 비조정 대조군 대비 T-Scan III 교합접촉 강도·개수에서 1·6개월 시점 모두 통계적 유의차 없었고, 후방 힘 분포 개선의 비유의 경향만 관찰됨.

## 2. Key Contributions

- One of the few RCTs to test whether **occlusal adjustment after orthodontic debonding** measurably changes objectively recorded occlusion (T-Scan III), rather than only static alignment indices (Little's, PAR).
- Quantifies the effect at three timepoints (T0 debonding, T1 one month, T2 six months) for two outcomes — occlusal contact **intensity** and **number**, split anterior/posterior.
- Bottom line: **no statistically significant benefit** of adjustment; only a non-significant trend toward improved posterior force distribution. Provides an evidence ceiling against over-treating an already-acceptable finished occlusion.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **n=30** (15 intervention, 15 control), both sexes, ages 17–28 (mean 20.20±3.20). 17 F / 13 M. Groups balanced for age (P=0.624) and sex (P=0.713).
- **Inclusion**: Class I dental malocclusion, crowding >8 mm, fixed orthodontics with extraction of all four first premolars, MBT 0.022 prescription.
- **Exclusion**: systemic disease, poor OH, gingivitis/periodontitis, deep caries to CEJ, bone-metabolism drugs, parafunction, TMJ disorder history.
- **Intervention**: at T1 (one month post-debond), intervention group received CR-guided occlusal adjustment; control received none. Both got upper/lower 1.5 mm PETG Essix retainers (full-time 4 months then nights); no fixed retainers (to avoid confounding contact analysis).
- **Occlusal adjustment**: muscle deprogramming with cotton rolls; CR via **Dawson bimanual manipulation**; premature contacts marked with 8-µm Arti-Fol. "Heavy contact" = thick continuous film mark AND T-Scan force **>20% of total occlusal load**; enamel reduction 50–100 µm with egg-shaped 833F bur; eccentric interferences removed to achieve **canine guidance**; fluoride applied.
- **T-Scan III recording**: Tekscan 85-µm/2,500-cell sensor; Frankfort plane parallel, mandible to CR via physiological swallowing; 3 closures with 3-min breaks, mean used; single trained operator. Recorded in centric relation at T0/T1/T2.
- **Stats**: SPSS v25; Shapiro-Wilk (all normal); independent/paired t-test, Mann-Whitney, Chi-square, Wilcoxon; α=0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **No significant difference** between intervention and control for any comparison (all P > 0.05).
- **Contact intensity** (intervention vs control, P): T1–T0 anterior −1.43±5.43 vs 0.60±5.78 (P=0.329), posterior 1.10±6.10 vs −0.60±5.78 (P=0.440); T2–T0 anterior −2.17±6.34 vs 0.23±4.15 (P=0.230), posterior 2.17±6.34 vs −0.23±4.15 (P=0.230); T2–T1 anterior P=0.815, posterior P=0.659.
- **Contact number**: T1–T0 anterior P=0.164, posterior P=0.243; T2–T0 anterior P=0.806, posterior Z=−0.127 P=0.902; T2–T1 anterior Z=−1.226 P=0.250, posterior Z=−1.097 P=0.285.
- Interpretation: adjustment **immediately intensified posterior contacts** but continued tooth settling over six months erased the difference; only a **non-significant trend** of improved posterior force balance favoring the intervention.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample (pilot-derived n=15/group) — underpowered for subtle differences; authors flag possible inaccurate variance estimation.
- Short follow-up (6 months); recommend ≥12 months for long-term stability.
- Narrow subgroup (Class I, 4-premolar extraction, MBT) limits generalizability.
- T-Scan measures **relative**, not absolute, force (mitigated by high intra-session reliability and the focus on temporal change).
- No patient-reported outcomes, no formal canine-guidance/group-function classification at baseline.

## 6. Related Work

- **Bonadio et al.** — no difference in occlusal stability/relapse (Little's, PAR) between occlusal-adjustment and no-adjustment groups at 5 years (77 Class I non-extraction patients). Concordant negative result.
- **Fathalla et al.** — no significant change in bite-force distribution pre/post orthodontic premolar-extraction treatment, despite improved posterior balance.
- **Koval et al.** (case report) — T-Scan-guided adjustment resolved a 30% right-molar overload, shortened closing cycle.
- **Mahmoud Helal et al.** — T-Scan III: orthodontically treated (non-extraction) patients showed heavier centric contacts and more premature balancing-side contacts vs untreated.

## 7. Glossary

- **T-Scan III**: digital occlusal analysis system (Tekscan); pressure-sensitive Mylar sensor recording relative force intensity, contact number/location, and timing (occlusion/disclusion time) in 2D/3D.
- **Centric relation (CR)**: condyle-seated reference jaw position; here achieved by Dawson bimanual manipulation.
- **MIP**: maximum intercuspation position.
- **Essix retainer**: removable thermoplastic (PETG) vacuum-formed retainer.
- **Dawson approach**: bimanual chin-point manipulation technique to seat the mandible in CR.
- **Canine guidance**: mutually protected occlusion scheme where the canine disoccludes posterior teeth during lateral excursion.
