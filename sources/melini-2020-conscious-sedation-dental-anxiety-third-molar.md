## Why Ingested

Adult counterpart to the pediatric/behavioral-management literature already in the wiki — where [[behavioral-dentistry/dental-anxiety/cernega-2022-efficacy-hypnosis-dental-anxiety-phobia]] and the non-pharmacological pediatric pages cover behavioral routes, this SR documents the **pharmacological conscious-sedation** route for adult dental anxiety in a reproducible surgical model (third molar extraction). ## One-line Summary

Systematic review (BMC Oral Health, 17 RCTs, n=1788) of conscious sedation for dental anxiety during third molar extraction: narrative synthesis only (heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis); 6 studies showed some anxiety improvement, but findings overall inconclusive and conflicting due to non-standardized outcome measures.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Conscious sedation for the management of dental anxiety in third molar extraction surgery: a systematic review
- **Authors**: Melini M, Forni A, Cavallin F, Parotto M, Zanette G
- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health 2020; 20(1):155
- **DOI**: 10.1186/s12903-020-01136-0
- **PMID**: 32466796 ; **PMCID**: PMC7254703
- **Type**: Systematic review of RCTs (narrative synthesis; PRISMA-guided)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic review focused specifically on **conscious sedation for dental anxiety** in the reproducible model of third molar extraction surgery.
- Aggregates 17 RCTs (n=1788) spanning 11+ sedative agents and multiple anxiety scales/timing.
- Documents the field's core methodological problem: **anxiety is usually a secondary, non-standardized outcome**, making pooling impossible and conclusions unreliable.
- Proposes a concrete standardization agenda for future trials (drug selection, comparison sequence, validated outcome set, assessment timing).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: SR of parallel + crossover RCTs; PRISMA-reported; protocol not registered.
- **Search**: MEDLINE/PubMed, EMBASE, SCOPUS, Cochrane CENTRAL; Jan 1978 – Mar 2019; no language restriction; PubMed strategy "third molar" AND "conscious sedation"; reference hand-search.
- **Population**: adults (≥16y) undergoing third molar extraction.
- **Intervention/comparator**: any conscious sedation (IV / inhalation / oral / combination) vs. any sedation or no sedation.
- **Outcome**: patient-reported anxiety OR stress-hormone alteration only (observer-assessed anxiety excluded).
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane Handbook — 7 RCT domains + 5 additional crossover-specific domains.
- **Synthesis**: narrative only (heterogeneity in drugs, anxiety measures, and assessment timing precluded meta-analysis).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Yield**: 125 non-duplicate records → 50 full text → **17 RCTs** included (7 crossover, 10 parallel); n=1788 (range 12–997).
- **Agents studied**: midazolam, dexmedetomidine, nitrous oxide, clonidine, remifentanil, zaleplon, triazolam, chlordemethyldiazepam, propofol, fentanyl, methohexital.
- **Patient-reported anxiety** (14 studies measured it): 6 studies showed some significant between-arm difference (triazolam>placebo; midazolam>no-intervention; midazolam 2 mg>1 mg; propofol>midazolam; fentanyl+midazolam(+methohexital) combinations lowest; midazolam>diazepam by VAS but not STAI). 9 studies unclear/underpowered or null (e.g. midazolam vs. clonidine; remifentanil vs. placebo — no difference).
- **Stress hormones** (3 studies): lower cortisol with midazolam vs. placebo; significant perioperative renin variation with remifentanil vs. placebo (ACTH/aldosterone unchanged); midazolam vs. NO/O2 unclear.
- **Risk of bias**: allocation concealment unclear in all; detection bias unclear in 13/17; 4 studies high attrition risk; 4 high "other bias" from incorrect crossover analysis.
- **Bottom line**: pharmacological conscious sedation did **not consistently** demonstrate anxiety-management advantage; findings inconclusive and conflicting.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Unclear study quality in several domains; several crossover trials analyzed incorrectly (period effect not removed).
- Heterogeneity in drugs, scales, and assessment timing prevented meta-analysis.
- Single procedure (third molar extraction) limits generalizability.
- **Future work**: standardize study design — critical drug selection (favor proven anxiolytic + low risk + ease + satisfaction); ordered comparison sequence; placebo (not no-intervention) arm; agreed validated outcomes (VAS/DAS + objective stress hormones; avoid observer assessment); assessment at baseline, pre-sedation, and post-sedation-but-pre-anesthetic (postoperative timing biased by spontaneous anxiety reduction); add intraoperative physiology including Analgesia/Nociception Index (ANI). Notes behavioral/CBT interventions may give longer-lasting fear reduction than sedation, and weak evidence for opioid anxiolysis.

## 7. Glossary

- **Conscious sedation**: drug-induced depression of consciousness during which the patient responds purposefully to verbal command, maintaining airway and spontaneous ventilation.
- **VAS / DAS / STAI / ISAR**: visual analogue scale / Corah Dental Anxiety Scale / State-Trait Anxiety Inventory / Interval Scale of Anxiety Report — patient-reported anxiety instruments.
- **Crossover RCT**: each subject receives both interventions in sequence, acting as own control; requires sequence randomization and period-effect handling.
- **ANI (Analgesia/Nociception Index)**: heart-rate-variability-derived index of relative parasympathetic tone, proposed as an intraoperative objective measure.
- **Narrative synthesis**: qualitative summary used when quantitative pooling (meta-analysis) is precluded by heterogeneity.
