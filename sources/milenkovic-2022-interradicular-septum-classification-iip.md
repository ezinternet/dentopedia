## One-line Summary
CBCT-based proposal of classification criteria for inter-radicular septum shape in maxillary molars, with explicit linkage to prosthetic-driven IIP planning.

## 1. Document Information
- **Title:** Criteria for the Classification of the Interradicular Septum Shape in Maxillary Molars with Clinical Importance for Prosthetic-Driven Immediate Implant Placement
- **Authors:** Jovana Milenkovic, Milica Vasiljevic, Nemanja Jovicic, Dragan Milovanovic, Dragica Selakovic, Gvozden Rosic
- **Year:** 2022
- **DOI:** 10.3390/diagnostics12061432
- **Study type:** cross-sectional

## 2. Key Contributions
Translates anatomical IRS variations into a clinical taxonomy that supports prosthetic-driven placement decisions — bridge between Smith-Tarnow and modern digital workflow.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Retrospective CBCT review. IRS shape categorization and association with optimal implant trajectory derived from prosthetic plan.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
Defines shape categories and quantifies their prevalence; identifies categories most amenable to predictable IIP.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
Anatomy-only; classification not validated against outcome data within this paper.

## 7. Glossary
- **IIP:** Immediate Implant Placement — placement at the time of tooth extraction.
- **IIPP:** Immediate Implant Placement and Provisionalization — IIP combined with same-visit provisional restoration.
- **SRP:** Sagittal Root Position — Kan I/II/III/IV classification of root-to-bone relationship.
- **CBCT:** Cone-Beam Computed Tomography.
- **PES/WES:** Pink/White Esthetic Score — esthetic outcome indices for anterior implants.
- **EMBL:** Early Marginal Bone Loss.
