## Why Ingested

Super-Bond(4-META/MMA-TBB계 레진시멘트)의 적응증을 현대 CAD-CAM 보철 기질로 확장하는 근거. PEEK는 화학적으로 불활성이라 접착이 어렵고, 표면처리(알루미나 샌드블라스팅 + 비열 플라즈마)가 결합강도·내구성에 미치는 영향을 SBS로 직접 평가한 in-vitro 연구. Super-Bond가 두 MMA계 luting system 중 하나(SB)로 직접 시험됨. 어떤 기능성 모노머/접착 화학이 어떤 기질에 작동하는가라는 질문에서, 4-META/MMA-TBB 화학과 표면처리의 상보성을 보여줘 [[resin-bonding/delgado-2025-functional-monomers-dental-adhesive-technology]] (functional-monomer 기술 리뷰)와 [[resin-bonding/kodaira-2022-resin-bonding-titanium-adhesive-monomers-review]] (MMA/adhesive-monomer → substrate 형제 페이지)를 임플란트·금속 외 substrate로 확장한다.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro SBS study (CAD-CAM PEEK, 244 specimens, n=10/group) showing alumina airborne-particle abrasion + non-thermal plasma treatment, combined with MMA-based luting systems (Beautylink BL and Super-Bond SB), gives superior or comparable shear bond strength and markedly improved post-thermocycling (20,000×) durability vs single treatments; AB drives durability for SB, plasma adds wettability/durability especially for BL.

## 한줄요약

In-vitro 전단결합강도(SBS) 연구(CAD-CAM PEEK 244개, 군당 n=10): 알루미나 샌드블라스팅(AB) 후 비열 플라즈마(PT) 병용 + MMA계 레진시멘트(Beautylink·Super-Bond)가 단일 처리보다 우월하거나 동등한 결합강도와 2만회 열순환 후 향상된 내구성을 보임 — Super-Bond는 AB가 내구성의 주역, Beautylink는 플라즈마가 젖음성·내구성에 기여.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Confirms Super-Bond (SB) as an MMA-based luting system tested on PEEK.** Two commercial MMA-based luting systems were evaluated: **Super-Bond (SB)** — a self-cure MMA-based resin cement used with an MMA-containing primer — and **Beautylink (BL)** — a dual-cure composite-based resin cement used with an MMA-containing primer. Both rely on MMA-containing primers for PEEK adhesion.
2. **Combined surface treatment (AB + PT) is best/equivalent and most durable.** Sequential alumina airborne-particle abrasion (mechanical) followed by air-atmosphere plasma treatment (chemical) gives superior or comparable SBS to single treatments and markedly improves post-thermocycling durability.
3. **Mechanistic split between the two systems.** For BL, plasma adds wettability and durability (UT fully debonded after aging). For SB, airborne-particle abrasion (AB) is the dominant durability driver; plasma alone (PT) did not maintain bonding after aging.
4. **Practical clinical recommendation**: both procedures are simple, fast, device-available, and safer than acid etching; plasma should be performed chairside immediately before luting because plasma-induced surface energy decays over time.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Specimens**: Commercial CAD-CAM PEEK blocks → 2 mm plates (diamond wheel saw), polished to #600, ultrasonically cleaned. Total **244 specimens**, randomized to **4 groups (n = 61/group)**: UT (untreated), PT (plasma-treated), AB (alumina airborne-particle abrasion / sandblasting), AB + PT (abrasion then plasma).
- **Plasma**: performed in an air atmosphere (non-thermal); intended to introduce polar groups (–C=O, –COOH, –OH).
- **Surface characterization**: SEM–EDX (15 kV), stylus profilometry (Ra, 10 mm trace, n=10), contact angle (static sessile drop, primers of each luting system, n=10).
- **SBS test**: Teflon tube (ID 5 mm), primer + luting agent; BL light-cured, SB self-cured; both stored 25 °C/1 h then 37 °C distilled water 24 h (**initial**). **Aged** group: 20,000 thermocycles 5–55 °C, 60 s dwell. Universal testing machine, crosshead 1.0 mm/min, n=10/group. Failure modes by optical microscopy ×30.
- **Statistics**: R 4.5.2; one-way ANOVA + Tukey for roughness/contact angle; two-way ANOVA (treatment × aging) + simple main effects + Tukey for SBS; p < 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

> **NOTE on numbers**: per-group SBS values in MPa are presented in the article's figures/tables, which were NOT captured in the JATS full-text. The qualitative/statistical findings below are quoted verbatim from the text; absolute MPa values require the PDF figures.

**Surface roughness (Ra)**:
- PT did not significantly change roughness vs UT.
- AB significantly increased roughness; AB + PT also significantly higher than UT, **no significant difference vs AB**. → plasma does not affect roughness of abraded PEEK.

**Wettability (contact angle, primer droplet)**:
- **BL primer**: PT significantly reduced contact angle vs untreated; AB also reduced it; **AB + PT gave a significantly lower contact angle than either PT or AB alone**.
- **SB primer**: contact angle **could not be measured in any group** — too low / immediate spreading, no stable droplet.

**Shear bond strength — BL system** (two-way ANOVA: treatment p < 0.001, ηp² = 0.400; aging p = 0.003, ηp² = 0.128; interaction p = 0.022, ηp² = 0.114):
- **Initial**: no significant differences among treatment groups.
- **Aged**: significant differences; **AB + PT significantly higher than PT and AB**; **UT showed complete debonding** after thermocycling.

**Shear bond strength — SB (Super-Bond) system** (two-way ANOVA: treatment p < 0.001, ηp² = 0.595; aging p < 0.001, ηp² = 0.466; interaction p = 0.012, ηp² = 0.139):
- **Initial**: AB + PT significantly higher than UT.
- **Aged**: **AB and AB + PT significantly higher than UT**; **PT did not maintain performance comparable to AB**. → AB is the dominant durability driver for SB.

**Failure modes**: predominantly adhesive (PEEK–cement interface) in all groups. Mixed failures only in a few AB + PT specimens (1 BL initial, 1 SB initial, 2 BL aged) — interfacial strength locally approaching cohesive strength.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- SBS test produces non-uniform interfacial stress (tensile/peeling components) not fully reflecting clinical loading; microtensile or complex loading suggested.
- Aging limited to thermocycling; **no cyclic mechanical fatigue** loading.
- SEM–EDX qualitative only; quantitative surface chemistry not characterized.
- Residual alumina particles remained on PEEK (not removable by air blowing or ultrasonication); their net effect on adhesion not clarified.
- SB primer wettability unmeasurable → wettability-role interpretation for SB must be cautious.

## 6. Related Work

- MMA-containing primers (Signum PEEK Bond, Visio.link) enhance PEEK–veneer bonding.
- MMA-based luting systems (Super-Bond, Block HC Cem) with MMA primers outperform composite-based resin cements on PEEK.
- Sequential surface treatments (sulfonic/sulfuric acid etching + plasma, piranha + abrasion) previously shown to improve PEEK bonding; this study extends evidence to durability with combined AB + plasma + MMA luting systems.

## 7. Glossary

- **PEEK** — poly(ether ether ketone); chemically inert high-performance thermoplastic used for CAD-CAM crowns, bridges, posts, frameworks.
- **MMA** — methyl methacrylate; reactive monomer base of Super-Bond (4-META/MMA-TBB chemistry) and MMA-containing primers/cements.
- **Super-Bond (SB)** — self-cure MMA-based resin cement (4-META/MMA-TBB system) used with an MMA-containing primer; one of the two luting systems tested.
- **Beautylink (BL)** — dual-cure composite-based resin cement with MMA-containing primer.
- **AB (airborne-particle abrasion)** — alumina sandblasting; produces micromechanical roughening/interlocking.
- **PT (plasma treatment)** — air-atmosphere non-thermal plasma; raises surface energy, adds polar groups (–C=O, –COOH, –OH).
- **SBS** — shear bond strength (MPa).
- **Thermocycling** — 20,000 cycles 5–55 °C aging protocol for durability.
- **ηp²** — partial eta squared (effect size).
