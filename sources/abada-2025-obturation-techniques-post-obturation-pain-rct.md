## Why Ingested

Seeds the wiki's first Ceraseal/bioceramic-sealer (calcium-silicate sealer) cluster with a head-to-head clinical RCT of CeraSeal vs AH Plus across three obturation techniques. Extends [[endodontics/song-2022-sealer-based-obturation-epoxy-calcium-silicate-rct]] (which found no postoperative-pain or extrusion difference between calcium-silicate and epoxy-resin sealers) by reporting, in 150 single-visit molar cases, that AH Plus produced significantly higher pain than CeraSeal overall and specifically under continuous-wave compaction. Provides a clinical-pain/extrusion anchor for the single-cone CeraSeal evidence in [[endodontics/spinelli-2024-three-year-single-cone-ceraseal-cohort]] and the apical-extrusion ranges in [[endodontics/zamparini-2023-premixed-calcium-silicate-carrier-based-2year]].

## One-line Summary

RCT (n=150 mandibular first molars, single-visit, asymptomatic irreversible pulpitis) comparing CeraSeal vs AH Plus across cold lateral / continuous-wave / single-cone obturation: pain was low-to-moderate (VAS 0–1.4) in all groups; obturation technique alone did not affect pain (p=0.124) but AH Plus gave significantly higher pain than CeraSeal overall (p<0.001) and under continuous-wave compaction (p<0.001); sealer-extrusion incidence did not differ between groups (p=0.499) but extrusion of either sealer raised pain (p<0.001).

## 한줄요약

RCT(하악 제1대구치 150개, 단일내원, 무증상 비가역적 치수염): CeraSeal vs AH Plus를 측방가압·연속파가압·단일콘 충전으로 비교. 모든 군에서 통증 낮음(VAS 0–1.4). 충전법 자체는 통증 차이 없음(p=0.124)이나 AH Plus가 CeraSeal보다 통증 유의하게 높음(전체 p<0.001, 연속파가압에서 p<0.001). 실러 일출 빈도는 군간 차이 없음(p=0.499)이나 실러가 일출되면 통증 증가(p<0.001).

## 2. Key Contributions

- First wiki paper to clinically compare **CeraSeal** (META BIOMED, premixed calcium-silicate / bioceramic sealer) against **AH Plus** (epoxy-resin sealer) for post-obturation pain and sealer extrusion in a single-visit RCT.
- Crosses **sealer type** with **obturation technique** (cold lateral compaction, continuous-wave compaction, single-cone) in a 5-arm design.
- Isolates that sealer chemistry (AH Plus > CeraSeal) drives the pain difference, not obturation technique per se, with the AH Plus penalty concentrated under heated (continuous-wave) compaction.
- Reports that sealer extrusion — though not different in incidence between groups — independently raises postoperative pain.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: prospective RCT, 5 parallel arms, n=30 each (total 150). Power analysis: minimum 27/group (G*Power, alpha 0.05, power 80%).
- **Patients**: ASA I–II, aged 18–60, mandibular first molar with **asymptomatic irreversible pulpitis**, three separate canals, no periapical pathosis (PAI ≤ 2), periodontally healthy. Exclusions included recent analgesics/antibiotics (7 days), necrotic/symptomatic pulp, immature root, cracks.
- **Arms**: (1) CLC-CS, (2) CWC-CS, (3) SC-CS [all CeraSeal]; (4) CLC-AH, (5) CWC-AH [AH Plus]. Single-cone done only with CeraSeal (no SC-AH arm).
- **Treatment**: single endodontist; 4% articaine IANB; ProTaper Next (mesial X2 25/.06, distal X3 30/.06); 5.25% NaOCl + 17% EDTA + saline final irrigation; AH Plus canals dried, CeraSeal canals kept slightly wet. CWC via System B (200 °C) + Obtura III (160 °C).
- **Outcomes**: post-obturation pain on VAS (0–10) at 6/12/24/48/72 h with SMS reminders; sealer extrusion (present/absent beyond radiographic apex) on standardized periapical radiographs by two blinded calibrated endodontists (Cohen kappa > 0.8); cases with >2 mm extrusion excluded.
- **Stats**: Kruskal-Wallis + Dunn (intergroup pain), Friedman + Nemenyi (intragroup), chi-square (extrusion), FDR correction, R 4.4.2.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Pain ranged 0–1.4 (no pain to mild)** across all groups; no emergency visits.
- **Intergroup pain**: NS at 6 h (p=0.068), 24 h (p=0.171), 72 h (p=0.068). Significant at **12 h** (p=0.018; CWC-CS lowest, CWC-AH highest) and **48 h** (p=0.015; CLC-CS lowest, CWC-AH highest).
- **Intragroup**: pain peaked at 6 h then fell significantly in every group (p<0.001); decline began after 6 h for CWC-CS, after 12 h for SC-CS/CLC-AH/CWC-AH, after 24 h for CLC-CS; all ~0 by 72 h.
- **Obturation technique (pooled)**: not associated with pain (CLC 0.56, CWC 0.65, SC 0.54; p=0.124).
- **Sealer type (pooled)**: AH Plus 0.69 vs CeraSeal 0.53; **p<0.001** (AH Plus higher).
- **Interaction**: with CeraSeal, technique did not matter (p=0.890); with AH Plus, **CWC gave more pain than CLC** (p=0.010). Under CWC, **AH Plus > CeraSeal** (p<0.001).
- **Sealer extrusion**: intergroup NS (p=0.499); highest SC-CS 60%, lowest CLC-CS 40%, CWC groups 50%. By technique p=0.186 (CLC 40%, CWC 50%, SC 60%); by sealer p=0.548 (CeraSeal 50%, AH Plus 45%).
- **Extrusion → pain**: presence of extrusion raised pain regardless of sealer (p<0.001) and within each sealer (CeraSeal p=0.004, AH Plus p=0.030).
- **Gender**: females reported higher pain than males (0.75 vs 0.36; p<0.001).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Short 72-h follow-up; no long-term pain or treatment-success outcomes.
- No single-cone AH Plus arm (asymmetric design — SC tested only with CeraSeal).
- Single operator and single center; pain thresholds, canal anatomy not fully controlled.
- Extrusion scored only as present/absent (volume not quantified); authors suggest 3D imaging for volumetric extrusion in future work.

## 6. Related Work

- Song et al. (CeraSeal/ADseal/EndoSeal TCS vs AH Plus, sealer-based obturation): no extrusion or postoperative-pain difference — contrasted here by the AH Plus pain penalty.
- Zamparini et al. SR/MA: premixed bioceramic apical extrusion 11.8–59.8% vs controls 11.8–33.3%.
- Seron et al. SR/MA: bioceramic showed less extrusion than AH Plus (contrast with this study's near-equal incidence).
- Fonseca et al.: bioceramic more extrusion than resin (contrast); Khandelwal et al.: BioRoot RCS less pain than AH Plus (concordant); Ricucci et al.: extruded bioceramic without foreign-body/inflammatory histology.

## 7. Glossary

- **CeraSeal (CS)** — premixed calcium-silicate / bioceramic root canal sealer (META BIOMED, Korea); resin-free, high pH, hydrophilic (canal kept slightly wet).
- **AH Plus (AH)** — epoxy-resin-based root canal sealer (Dentsply); releases bisphenol A diglycidyl ether, ~7-h setting, canal dried before use.
- **CLC** — cold lateral compaction (finger spreader + accessory cones).
- **CWC** — continuous wave compaction (heated down-pack + backfill; System B + Obtura).
- **SC** — single-cone obturation (master cone only; recommended with bioceramic sealers).
- **Sealer extrusion** — radiopaque sealer detected beyond the radiographic apex.
- **Asymptomatic irreversible pulpitis** — inflamed pulp without spontaneous pain; delayed-positive thermal/electric response.
- **VAS** — visual analog scale, 0–10 pain self-report.
