## Why Ingested

잔존골 높이(Residual Bone Height, RBH)가 극도로 낮은 경우(0.4–1.5 mm)에도 골밀도화(Osseodensification, OD) 경치조골 접근이 측방창(Lateral Window)을 대체할 수 있는지 검증하는 사례. 전통적으로 측방창 전용 영역으로 여겨지던 RBH ≤1.5 mm까지 경치조골 적응증을 확장하는 근거로, 술식 선택의 경계 사례를 채운다. See [[overviews/sinus-lift-technique-selection]].

## One-line Summary
3-patient case series proposing an OD-based crestal sinus window technique for very low RBH (0.4–1.5 mm), reporting 10.3–13.6 mm vertical bone gain comparable to lateral window with no membrane perforations.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Case report / case series (n = 3 patients)
- **Journal**: Journal of Oral Implantology 2021;47(1):45
- **DOI**: 10.1563/aaid-joi-D-19-00292 (manuscript text references aaid-joi-D-19-00288 in footer — minor inconsistency)
- **Sample**: 3 healthy non-smoking patients with RBH 0.4–1.5 mm (single hyperpneumatized molar site; large posterior sextant with/without septum)
- **Primary Question**: Can osseodensification be used to create a transcrestal "window" that achieves lateral-window-equivalent vertical bone gain in extremely atrophic posterior maxilla?

## 2. Key Contributions
- Proposes the "osseodensified crestal sinus window" as a novel category between transcrestal and lateral approaches.
- Extends transcrestal indications to RBH ≤ 1.5 mm — historically a lateral-window-only zone.
- Reports vertical sinus bone height gain of **10.3 to 13.6 mm** in three patients — within lateral-window magnitude (literature typical crestal gain 3–4 mm).
- 0 membrane perforations across 3 cases.
- Describes a stepwise Densah bur protocol: enter to 4.0 mm with Ø 4.0 bur, then advance with Ø 5.3 bur in CCW mode at 1100 rpm with copious irrigation, in 1-mm increments to a max of 3 mm past the sinus floor.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Three consecutively presented patients selected for their difficult sinus-lift indications: large posterior sextant edentulism (with and without septa) and a single hyperpneumatized maxillary molar site. Pre-op CBCT planning. Surgical sequence:
1. Full-thickness flap.
2. Crestal osteotomy at the center of the alveolar crest using Densah Ø 4.0 to engage the sinus floor.
3. Densah Ø 5.3 in OD (CCW) mode at 1100 rpm with copious irrigation, advanced past the sinus floor in 1-mm increments to a maximum of 3 mm past floor.
4. Bone graft material introduced through the densified crestal window.
5. Implant placement (timing protocol detailed in case-specific descriptions).

The author argues that the OD bur's densifying mode burnishes a thickened cortical "window" on the sinus floor that resists membrane perforation and accommodates larger graft volumes than conventional transcrestal techniques.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- 3/3 healing uneventful, no Schneiderian membrane perforations, no complications.
- Vertical sinus bone height gain: **10.3 to 13.6 mm** (range across 3 cases).
- For comparison, the cited literature (Zitmann & Scharer, Woo & Le, Pal et al.) reports typical mean crestal-approach gain of only 3–4 mm.
- Specific implant survival, post-op CBCT volumetric data, follow-up duration: not extractable from the available front/back text.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- **n = 3** — case series, lowest evidence tier; cannot estimate complication rate or generalize.
- Single operator, single private practice (Kirkland, Quebec).
- No prospective comparator (no concurrent lateral-window control).
- Follow-up duration and long-term implant survival not extractable from available text.
- Membrane perforation reported as 0/3 — but with n=3, a 95% CI on the perforation rate is very wide.
- Author declares no conflict of interest; however the technique relies on a proprietary device (Densah/Versah).
- The claim that vertical gain is "comparable to lateral window" is supported by within-patient observation, not by a head-to-head trial.

## 7. Glossary
- **RBH (residual bone height)**: vertical bone available below the sinus floor in the edentulous posterior maxilla.
- **Schneiderian membrane**: thin epithelial lining of the maxillary sinus; perforation is the primary complication of sinus elevation.
- **Crestal / transcrestal approach**: sinus elevation via osteotomy through the alveolar crest (vs lateral antrostomy from the buccal wall).
- **Lateral window**: classic Tatum/Caldwell-Luc style buccal antrostomy for sinus floor elevation.
- **Densah CCW mode**: counter-clockwise (densifying) Densah-bur rotation.
- **Septum**: bony partition within the sinus (Underwood's septa) complicating lateral window design.
