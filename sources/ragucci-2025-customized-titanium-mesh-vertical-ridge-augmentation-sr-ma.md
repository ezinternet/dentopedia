## Why Ingested

맞춤형(CAD/CAM) 티타늄메쉬가 기성 메쉬 대비 수직/수평 골증대량·합병증에서 실제 우위가 있는지 SR+MA로 정량화. 기존 [[implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/chiapasco-2021-customized-cadcam-titanium-meshes-gbr]]와 [[implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/tay-2022-healing-complications-vertical-gbr-sr-ma]]의 합병증 논의를 직접 비교 메타분석으로 확장.

## One-line Summary
SR+MA (22 studies: 3 RCT, 6 prospective, 12 retrospective, 1 cohort; 608 patients, 1,318 implants) comparing customized CAD/CAM titanium mesh (CTM) vs conventional titanium mesh (TM) for ridge augmentation; comparable vertical bone gain (5.14 vs 6.24 mm, NS), significantly greater horizontal gain with CTM (6.38 vs 3.85 mm, P=0.004), and a non-significant trend to less exposure with CTM (20.3% vs 30.9%).

## 한줄요약
맞춤형 CAD/CAM 티타늄메쉬(CTM) vs 기성 메쉬(TM)를 비교한 SR+MA(22편, 608명·임플란트 1,318개). 수직골증대량은 유사(5.14 vs 6.24 mm, NS), 수평골증대는 CTM이 유의하게 우수(6.38 vs 3.85 mm, P=0.004), 막노출은 CTM이 낮은 경향(20.3% vs 30.9%, NS).

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: International Journal of Implant Dentistry (Int J Implant Dent) 2025;11:55
- DOI: 10.1186/s40729-025-00643-5
- Institution: Universitat Internacional de Catalunya (UIC), Barcelona
- Design: Systematic review + meta-analysis; search of 4 databases (PubMed, Cochrane Central, Web of Science, Google Scholar) up to Jan 2025

## 2. Key Contributions
- First meta-analysis directly pooling CTM vs TM outcomes for ridge augmentation.
- Separates vertical vs horizontal bone-gain effects, isolating where customization actually helps.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 22 studies included (3 RCT, 6 prospective, 12 retrospective, 1 cohort); 608 patients, 1,318 implants.
- Outcomes: vertical bone gain, horizontal bone gain, membrane/mesh exposure, complications.
- Random-effects meta-analysis with between-group comparison.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Vertical bone gain: TM 6.24 mm vs CTM 5.14 mm — NS (P=0.628).
- Horizontal bone gain: CTM 6.38 mm vs TM 3.85 mm — significant favoring CTM (P=0.004).
- Mesh exposure: TM 30.9% vs CTM 20.3% — NS (P=0.721).
- Other complications (e.g. infection): more frequent in TM, not significant.
- Conclusion: CTM gives comparable bone gain, superior horizontal gain, and a trend to fewer complications.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Predominantly retrospective evidence (12/22); heterogeneity in defect type, graft, membrane coverage.
- Exposure/complication differences underpowered (NS despite clinically meaningful gaps).
- Cost/manufacturing-time of CTM not weighed against marginal clinical gain.

## 6. Related Work

- Cucchi 2024 (PTFE vs Ti-mesh; peri-implant bone loss SR+MA).

## 7. Glossary
- CTM: customized (CAD/CAM or 3D-printed) titanium mesh.
- TM: conventional (intraoperatively adapted) titanium mesh.
- Vertical/horizontal bone gain: ridge dimension increase along respective axis.
- Mesh exposure: dehiscence of soft tissue over the mesh, a primary complication mode.
