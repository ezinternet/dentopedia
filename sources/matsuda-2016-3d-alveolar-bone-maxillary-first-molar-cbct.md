## Why Ingested

Maxillary first molar (MFM) immediate implant placement (IIP) hinges on the residual bone available beyond the root apices and between the roots — but most IIP guidance is anatomically vague. This CBCT study of 150 healthy MFMs quantifies the exact dimensions (buccal/palatal plate thickness, sinus floor-to-apex/furcation distances, inter-radicular socket width) and converts them into a 3-tier favorable/intermediate/contraindicated decision framework. It supplies the anatomical substrate behind palatal-root-socket IIP and inter-radicular septum engagement strategies. Reinforces [[immediate-implant/deporter-2021-cbct-immediate-maxillary-molar-implant]] (CBCT residual-bone assessment for maxillary molar IIP), extends [[immediate-implant/milenkovic-2022-interradicular-septum-classification-iip]] (septum-anchored stability with per-root dimensions), and underpins the palatal-root anchorage rationale in [[immediate-implant/liu-2024-maxillary-immediate-molar-palatal-root-socket]].

## One-line Summary

Retrospective CBCT cross-sectional study (95 patients, 150 healthy maxillary first molars): mean buccal/palatal plate 1.58/1.34 mm, sinus floor-to-furcation 6.51 mm, palatal root apex intrudes a mean −2.2 mm into the sinus (deeper than MB/DB roots); ~32% of MFMs judged contraindicated for immediate implant placement on residual-bone grounds.

## 한줄요약

후향적 CBCT 단면연구 (환자 95명, 건강한 상악 제1대구치 150개): 평균 협측/구개측 골판 두께 1.58/1.34 mm, 상악동저-치근분지부 거리 6.51 mm, 구개측 치근단은 평균 −2.2 mm로 상악동 안으로 가장 깊이 함입(MB/DB 치근보다 깊음); MFM의 약 32%가 잔존골 기준 즉시식립 금기로 판정됨.

- **DOI**: 10.1097/ID.0000000000000430
- **Study type**: Retrospective cross-sectional CBCT morphometric study
- **Sample**: 150 maxillary first permanent molars from 95 patients (32 male, 63 female), aged 18–76 (mean 37.2 ± 14.9 y), treated 2011–2014 at Whittier Oral Surgery
- **Device**: Picasso Duo CBCT (VA Tech, South Korea); single examiner, implant-planning software

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Per-root and palatal-root sinus relations** in 3D — palatal root apex is the deepest intruder into the maxillary sinus (mean −2.2 mm, 95% CI −2.7 to −1.7), significantly deeper than MB (−0.36 mm) and DB (0.32 mm) apices.
2. **Quantified buccal vs palatal plate thickness** at 2 mm from crest: buccal 1.58 mm (thicker, favorable for IIP gap healing) vs palatal 1.34 mm; 25% of palatal sites but only 8% of buccal sites are <1 mm.
3. **Socket geometry**: mesio-distal 7.3 mm and bucco-palatal 10.5 mm at crest — a wide socket that makes primary stability inherently challenging.
4. A **3-tier clinical decision framework** (favorable / intermediate-needs-sinus-augmentation / contraindicated) keyed to sinus floor-to-apex, sinus floor-to-furcation, and inter-root distances.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Inclusion: ≥18 y, ≥1 MFM present with ≥2 occluding posterior teeth per quadrant, no sinus pathology, no periodontal bone loss/infection/severe resorption/trauma/periapical lesion, no prior GBR/GTR, no metal-restoration artifacts.

CBCT reorientation: coronal long axis perpendicular to occlusal plane; sagittal buccal–palatal crest line parallel to horizontal; axial bucco-palatal slicing in 3 planes — **Mid slice** (mid-tooth), **MB-P slice** (mesio-buccal to palatal root), **DB-P slice** (disto-buccal to palatal root).

Measurements per tooth:
1. Buccal and palatal bone-wall width at 2 mm from crest.
2. Sinus floor (SF) → furcation distance (Mid slice).
3. SF → apex of MB root (MB-P slice), DB root (DB-P slice), palatal root (Mid slice).
4. Inter-root apex distance: MB→palatal (MB-P slice), DB→palatal (DB-P slice).
5. Mesio-distal and bucco-palatal socket size at crest.

Analysis: SPSS 22, descriptive stats (mean, SD, 95% CI), histograms. Negative SF-to-apex values denote root intrusion into the sinus.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Parameter | Mean (SD) / value |
|---|---|
| Buccal plate thickness (2 mm from crest) | 1.58 (0.6) mm |
| Palatal plate thickness (2 mm from crest) | 1.34 (0.54) mm |
| SF → furcation distance | 6.51 (2.94) mm, range 0–16 mm |
| SF → MB root apex | −0.36 mm (95% CI −0.91 to 0.19) |
| SF → DB root apex | 0.32 mm (95% CI −0.27 to 0.9) |
| SF → palatal root apex | −2.2 mm (95% CI −2.7 to −1.7) — deepest intrusion |
| Mesio-distal socket size at crest | 7.3 (0.84) mm |
| Bucco-palatal socket size at crest | 10.5 (0.90) mm |

- **Per-root significance**: SF-to-apex significantly differs for MB vs palatal and DB vs palatal (palatal deeper), but NOT MB vs DB.
- **Thin-plate prevalence**: buccal <1 mm in 8% of sites; palatal <1 mm in 25% of sites. 92% of buccal sites ≥1 mm, ~20% ≥2 mm.
- **Engaging apical bone**: 46% of MFMs had >5 mm of engaging apical bone (favorable for primary stability).
- **Background incidence (cited)**: root intrusion into sinus rises posteriorly — first premolars 1.5%, second premolars 14.8%, first molars 40.5%, second molars 44.7%.

**Author decision framework for MFM IIP:**
- **Favorable**: SF-to-apex >5 mm OR SF-to-furcation >5 mm, AND inter-root distance >5 mm → adequate bone for primary stability (center-of-socket implant, jumping gap grafted).
- **Intermediate**: SF-to-apex 2–4 mm OR SF-to-furcation 2–4 mm, with inter-root ≥5 mm → width adequate but height marginal; technically challenging, may need simultaneous sinus augmentation (perforation risk).
- **Contraindicated (~32% of sample)**: SF-to-apex <2 mm and inter-root (bucco-lingual) <5 mm, OR SF-to-furcation <2 mm → insufficient residual bone for primary stability; stage the case.

Stability prerequisites stated: ~5 mm residual apical bone height and, for a 4 mm-diameter implant, ~5 mm bone width at the inter-radicular crest. Pluemsakunthai animal data cited: widest (3 mm) jumping gap had best bone fill.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Retrospective, single-center, single-examiner (no reported inter-/intra-examiner reliability).
- **Healthy MFMs only** — periodontally/periapically diseased molars (the typical extraction candidate) were excluded, so dimensions may not transfer to real IIP cases.
- Cross-sectional anatomy, not outcomes — the favorable/contraindicated thresholds are anatomy-derived and clinical-experience-based, not validated against implant survival.
- Single CBCT device/protocol; thresholds (5 mm apical, 5 mm width) asserted from clinical experience rather than measured.
- Age effect on sinus pneumatization noted but not modeled within the sample.

## 6. Related Work

- Tian 2016 (ref 4) — proximity of maxillary posterior teeth to sinus via CBCT.
- Vera et al (ref 34) — buccal/palatal plate thickness of maxillary anterior/premolar teeth (anterior 0.83 mm, premolar 1.13 mm); plate thickens anterior→posterior.
- Pluemsakunthai et al (ref 36) — animal gap-distance bone-healing study (3 mm gap best fill).
- Prior CBCT root-to-sinus work showed only 39% of roots projecting onto the sinus on panoramic films actually protrude on CT (panoramic over-projects ~2.1×).

## 7. Glossary

- **MFM** — Maxillary First Molar.
- **SF** — Sinus Floor (maxillary sinus floor).
- **IIP** — Immediate Implant Placement (into a fresh extraction socket).
- **MB / DB root** — Mesio-Buccal / Disto-Buccal root of the MFM.
- **Inter-radicular bone/septum** — bone between the three roots, the primary anchorage zone for molar IIP.
- **Jumping gap** — void between implant surface and socket walls, grafted with biomaterial.
- **Engaging apical bone** — residual bone apical to the socket that the implant tip engages for primary stability.
