## One-line Summary
Systematic review of 6 studies comparing PRF vs. PRP after mandibular third molar extraction; both improve healing, but PRF shows advantages in soft tissue healing and bone density in 4/6 studies, with easier preparation and fully autologous nature.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: The Journal of Contemporary Dental Practice 2024; DOI 10.5005/jp-journals-10024-3727
- **Published**: December 20, 2024 (received August 4, 2024; accepted September 24, 2024)
- **Study type**: Systematic review
- **Institution**: Kalinga Institute of Dental Sciences, KIIT University (lead), multiple Indian institutions

## 2. Key Contributions
- Direct comparison of PRF vs. PRP (not just PRF vs. control) in mandibular third molar extraction context
- PICO design: P = mandibular third molar impaction; I = PRF; C = PRP; O = pain, swelling, trismus, soft tissue healing, bone regeneration
- Concludes PRF advantage over PRP: easier preparation and fully autologous (no additives needed)
- NHLBI quality assessment tool applied to all included studies

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Databases: Scopus, PubMed, Google Scholar, Cochrane Central Register of Controlled Trials
- Search period: January 2014 – June 2024
- PRISMA guidelines followed
- PICO criteria strictly applied
- NHLBI (National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute) quality assessment tool used for bias evaluation
- Inclusion: studies on mandibular third molar impacted tooth extraction with PRF and/or PRP intervention

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Studies included**: 6 articles
  - 1 split-mouth prospective clinical study
  - 3 split-mouth comparative studies
  - 2 unilateral randomized comparative prospective studies
- **4/6 studies**: Significant improvement in soft tissue wound healing and increase in bone density in PRF site vs. PRP
- **2/6 studies**: No significant difference between PRF and PRP for pain, trismus, swelling, bone formation
- All 6 studies scored predominantly "good" on NHLBI quality assessment
- Both PRF and PRP positively influence healing after mandibular third molar extraction
- PRF advantage: ease of preparation (centrifugation without additives) and complete autologous nature

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small number of qualifying studies (n=6)
- Heterogeneity in PRF/PRP preparation protocols across studies
- Variation in outcome measurement timepoints
- Mandibular third molars have denser cortical bone → higher postoperative complication risk; need larger RCTs
- Future studies should standardize platelet concentrate protocols

## 6. Related Work
- Al-Maawi et al. 2021 (PRF in extraction socket healing SR) — broader extraction socket context
- Literature on bone regeneration techniques using graft materials and barrier membranes
- Pharmacological/non-pharmacological methods to reduce healing time after lower wisdom tooth extraction

## 7. Glossary
- **PRF (Platelet-Rich Fibrin)**: Second-generation platelet concentrate; centrifuged without additives; fibrin matrix with growth factors; completely autologous
- **PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma)**: First-generation platelet concentrate; requires thrombin and calcium chloride additives; anticoagulants needed
- **NHLBI quality assessment tool**: National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute tool for evaluating study quality
- **Split-mouth design**: Each patient serves as own control; one side receives intervention, contralateral side receives control
- **Pericoronitis**: Inflammation around partially erupted tooth crown, common indication for third molar extraction
