## One-line Summary

Narrative clinical article (Gomez-Meda et al., 2021) introducing the Esthetic Biological Contour (EBC) concept, a zone-based framework for designing the emergence profile of implant-supported restorations in the esthetic zone.

## 한줄요약

## 2. Key Contributions

- Introduced the **Esthetic Biological Contour (EBC) concept**: a structured, zone-based approach to emergence profile design for implant-supported restorations.
- Differentiated the emergence profile into distinct anatomical zones (sub-gingival, gingival margin, supragingival), each with specific design parameters and biologic rationale.
- Integrated 3D implant position, implant design features (collar, connection), and peri-implant soft tissue thickness into a single decision-making framework.
- Provided design guidelines applicable to both interim (provisional) and definitive implant restorations in the esthetic zone.
- Emphasized that emergence profile contour must account for the biologic width around implants (≈3–4 mm for bone-level implants) and the thickness of the peri-implant mucosa.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Type**: Narrative review and concept proposal based on literature synthesis.
- **Key references incorporated**:
  - Su et al. (2010): Critical contour vs. subcritical contour distinction (J Periodontol).
  - Steigmann et al. (2014): Emergence profile based on 3D implant position.
  - González-Martín et al. (2020): Contour management guidelines for immediate and delayed cases.
  - Esquivel et al. (2021): Impact of 3D implant position on emergence profile design.
  - Linkevicius studies: Soft tissue thickness and crestal bone stability.
  - Cochran & Hermann: Biologic width around titanium implants.
- **Framework zones defined**:
  - **Zone 1 (Implant–Abutment Interface / Sub-crestal zone)**: governed by implant platform position, connection design, and biologic width requirements.
  - **Zone 2 (Subcritical contour / Connective tissue zone)**: concave to neutral contour; supports connective tissue attachment; critical for crestal bone stability.
  - **Zone 3 (Critical contour / Junctional epithelium zone)**: slight convexity near the gingival margin; position of the mucosal margin.
  - **Zone 4 (Supragingival / Esthetic zone)**: convexity matching the natural crown emergence; most visible esthetic component.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **No original data**: concept paper; all quantitative claims derive from cited studies.
- Soft tissue thickness ≥2 mm at the buccal aspect associated with better crestal bone stability (Linkevicius data).
- Abutment height ≥3 mm required to establish biologic width without bone resorption (Galindo-Moreno 2014).
- Concave subcritical contour (Zone 2) shown to support connective tissue and reduce inflammation vs. convex contours (Su 2010; Rompen 2007).
- Proper Zone 3 (critical contour) design determines gingival margin position — convexity pushes gingiva apically; concavity elevates it.
- Implant 3D position (apico-coronal, bucco-lingual, mesio-distal) directly determines feasibility of each EBC zone design — palatally/apically placed implants allow more generous convexity without compromising biologic width.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- No prospective clinical trial or controlled study; entire concept derived from narrative literature synthesis.
- Zone boundaries are conceptual; exact angular/linear thresholds for each zone not quantified.
- Applicability in posterior sites, bone-level vs. tissue-level implant designs, and different connection types not fully addressed.
- EBC Concept 2.0 (Esquivel, Gomez-Meda, Strauss, Blatz — follow-up article) extends the framework with more detailed zone management.

## 7. Glossary

- **EBC (Esthetic Biological Contour)**: A zone-based conceptual framework integrating biologic and esthetic requirements for implant emergence profile design.
- **Subcritical contour**: The portion of the emergence profile apical to the gingival margin; ideally concave to neutral, supporting connective tissue attachment.
- **Critical contour**: The profile portion at and just coronal to the gingival margin crest; determines the position of the mucosal margin.
- **Biologic width (peri-implant)**: The combined dimension of the junctional epithelium and connective tissue zone around an implant — approximately 3–4 mm for bone-level implants.
- **Zone 1–4**: EBC's four spatial zones of the implant emergence profile from the implant platform to the supragingival crown contour.
