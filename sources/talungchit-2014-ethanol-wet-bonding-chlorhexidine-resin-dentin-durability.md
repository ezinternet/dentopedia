## Why Ingested

Directly tests two of the durability strategies catalogued in [[wiki/resin-bonding/liu-2011-limitations-bonding-dentin-bond-degradation-review]] — ethanol-wet bonding (better hydrophobic-monomer infiltration) and chlorhexidine (MMP inhibition / collagen preservation) — providing in-vitro mechanistic evidence (micro-Raman monomer quantification + TEM nanoleakage) that the two together yield a more durable resin-dentin bond. Reinforces the review's thesis that bond degradation is hydrolysis of resin-sparse, water-rich hybrid layer plus collagenolysis.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro micro-Raman + μTBS + TEM bench study (Talungchit et al. 2014, J Adhes Dent; abstract-only retrieved) showing ethanol-wet bonding raises bis-GMA/TEG-DMA molar concentration and μTBS across the hybrid layer immediately and after aging, and that adding chlorhexidine further preserves collagen integrity and limits nanoleakage at 1 year.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides **direct quantitative evidence** (via micro-Raman spectroscopy) that ethanol-wet bonding (EW) achieves higher bis-GMA and TEG-DMA molar concentrations within the hybrid layer than conventional water-wet bonding (WW) — i.e., superior infiltration of hydrophobic monomers into demineralized dentin.
- Couples molecular monomer quantification with mechanical (μTBS) and morphological (TEM nanoleakage, collagen fibril diameter, interfibrillar width, hybrid layer thickness) outcomes, immediately and after aging.
- Demonstrates that **chlorhexidine (CHX)** layered onto EW further preserves collagen integrity and limits nanoleakage after 1-year storage, supporting combined EW + CHX as a more durable bonding strategy.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Three-step etch-and-rinse adhesive (resin: 70% bis-GMA / 28.75% TEG-DMA) applied to coronal dentin under either a clinically relevant **ethanol-wet bonding (EW)** protocol or **conventional water-wet bonding (WW)**.
- **Micro-Raman spectroscopy**: bis-GMA and TEG-DMA molar concentrations measured at various positions across the resin/dentin interface for EW and WW; measurements repeated at the same positions after **7-month storage** in phosphate buffer solution containing 0.1% sodium azide.
- **μTBS and hybrid-layer morphology (TEM)** compared for bonding groups with and without chlorhexidine application, **immediately and after 1-year storage**, assessing nanoleakage, collagen fibril diameter, collagen interfibrillar width, and hybrid layer thickness.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- EW specimens showed **significantly higher monomer molar concentrations and μTBS throughout the hybrid layer**, both immediately and after storage — direct evidence of superior hydrophobic-monomer infiltration vs WW.
- Microscopically, EW **maintained interfibrillar width and hybrid layer thickness**, preserving space for resin infiltration and retention.
- **Chlorhexidine application further preserved collagen integrity and limited the degree of nanoleakage in EW after 1-year storage.**
- Conclusion: EW enhances infiltration of hydrophobic monomers into demineralized dentin; a more durable resin-dentin bond may be achieved by combining a clinically relevant EW protocol with chlorhexidine.
- (Exact numeric values for monomer concentration, μTBS in MPa, and nanoleakage scores are not given in the abstract; full text not retrieved.)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro bench study — no clinical (in-mouth) endpoint; durability inferred from storage-aging models (7-month buffer, 1-year storage), not intraoral function.
- Abstract-only: precise effect sizes and statistical details beyond "significant" are unavailable.
- Single adhesive formulation; generalizability to other etch-and-rinse and universal adhesives not established.
- Future work: clinical trials of the combined EW + CHX protocol and longer-term durability confirmation.

## 7. Glossary

- **Ethanol-wet bonding (EW)**: bonding technique replacing residual water in demineralized dentin with ethanol, expanding collagen interfibrillar spaces and improving infiltration of hydrophobic resin monomers.
- **Water-wet bonding (WW)**: conventional wet-bonding technique leaving the demineralized dentin water-saturated before adhesive application.
- **Hybrid layer**: the resin-infiltrated demineralized collagen zone at the resin/dentin interface.
- **μTBS (microtensile bond strength)**: tensile bond strength measured on small (~1 mm²) resin-dentin beams.
- **Nanoleakage**: nanometer-scale penetration of tracer into incompletely resin-infiltrated regions of the hybrid layer; a marker of degradation.
- **Chlorhexidine (CHX)**: a broad-spectrum antimicrobial that also inhibits matrix metalloproteinases (MMPs), slowing collagenolytic degradation of the hybrid layer.
- **bis-GMA / TEG-DMA**: hydrophobic dimethacrylate resin monomers; bisphenol A-glycidyl methacrylate and triethylene glycol dimethacrylate.
