## Why Ingested

This narrative review synthesizes 55 clinical studies on laminate veneer (LV) survival rates and complications, covering key material choices (feldspathic, LRG, LDS), preparation design, dentin exposure, bonding protocols, and bruxism effects — directly complementing and contextualizing the SR+MA evidence in [[wiki/veneers/klein-2025-ceramic-laminate-veneer-survival-complications-sr-ma]].

## One-line Summary

Narrative review (55 clinical studies, 2000–2023) of laminate veneer clinical performance: silica-based ceramics (feldspathic, LRG, LDS) achieve 72–100% survival across follow-up periods, with dentin exposure, bruxism, and adhesive protocol quality as the key modifiable outcome determinants.

## 한줄요약

내러티브 리뷰 (55개 임상연구, 2000–2023): 라미네이트 비니어 (Laminate Veneer, LV)의 생존율은 실리카 기반 세라믹(장석도재, 류사이트강화글라스세라믹 (Leucite-Reinforced Glass Ceramic, LRG), 리튬디실리케이트 (Lithium Disilicate Ceramic, LDS))에서 72–100%이며, 상아질 노출·이갈이·접착 프로토콜이 핵심 수정 가능 예후 인자.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of Prosthodontic Research (J Prosthodont Res)
- **Volume/Issue**: 68(3): 368–379
- **Published**: Available online 12 January 2024; accepted 7 December 2023
- **Study type**: Narrative review
- **Database search**: MEDLINE/PubMed, Web of Science, Cochrane Library, Google Scholar; 2000 to April 2023
- **Inclusion**: Clinical trials with ≥3 year observation; excluded case reports, case series, reviews, abstracts, in vitro studies, observational studies
- **Total studies included**: 55 articles

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Comprehensive material comparison**: Documents survival rates by material (feldspathic 64–100%, LRG 76–100%, LDS 87–100%) across short (<5y), mid (5–10y), and long-term (>10y) follow-up
2. **Dentin exposure**: Quantifies the negative impact of >50% dentin exposure on bonding durability; immediate dentin sealing (IDS) shown to mitigate this risk (95.5% vs 81.8% without IDS at 11y per Gresnigt)
3. **Bruxism management**: Occlusal devices significantly improve LV survival in bruxism patients (89.1% with splint vs 63.9% without splint at 8y)
4. **Surface treatment standard**: HF etching + silane priming established as gold standard for silica-based ceramics; controversy remains on the role of additional adhesive application
5. **No zirconia LV data**: No studies meeting ≥3y clinical criteria for zirconia LVs were identified — highlights evidence gap
6. **Preparation design vs survival**: Incisal overlap (palatal chamfer/butt joint) vs non-overlap (window/feather edge) outcomes discussed; no-prep variants show competitive survival in feldspathic cases

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search strategy**: Keywords "laminate" OR "veneer" OR "porcelain" OR "feldspathic" OR "lithium disilicate" OR "composite resin" OR "zirconia" OR "survival" OR "success"
- **Reviewer process**: 5 independent researchers evaluated titles/abstracts; disagreements resolved by discussion
- **Data extraction**: All reviewers participated; manual search supplemented electronic databases
- **Study design**: Narrative review — no meta-analysis or PRISMA flow; does not quantitatively pool survival rates
- **Key subgroup analyses**: By observation period, material type, preparation design (prep/min-prep/no-prep), dentin exposure level, tooth vitality (vital/nonvital), location (maxillary/mandibular, anterior/premolar), and bruxism status

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Survival by Observation Period
- **≤5 years**: 95.1–100%
- **≤10 years**: 82.5–100%
- **>10 years**: 72.6–95.0%
- Layton: survival at <6y significantly higher than 5–11y or 10–16y; Rinke: annual intervention rate increased from 0.6 to 1.78 between 3y and 10y

### Survival by Material
| Material | Survival range | n (articles) |
|---|---|---|
| Feldspathic porcelain | 64–100% | 21 |
| LRG (e.g. IPS Empress) | 76–100% | 16 |
| LDS (e.g. IPS e.max) | 87–100% | 10 |
| Indirect composite resin | 75–87% at 10y | 2 |

### Failure Modes by Time
- **<1 year**: debonding, fracture
- **1–5 years**: fracture, chipping, endodontic complications
- **>5 years**: caries, marginal disintegration, retention loss, shade mismatch, gingival recession

### Bruxism
- With occlusal device: 89.1% survival at 8y
- Without occlusal device: 63.9% survival at 8y (Faus-Matoses 2020, n=364)

### Immediate Dentin Sealing (IDS) Effect (Gresnigt, 11y)
- With IDS: 96.4% survival
- Without IDS: 81.8% survival

### Vitality of Abutment
- Conflicting evidence: Beier showed nonvital teeth have significantly higher failure risk; other studies found no significant difference
- Full-coverage restoration considered more appropriate for nonvital teeth

### Location
- Maxillary vs mandibular: one study showed significantly higher survival for maxillary; Rinke found no significant difference
- Premolars may have higher failure rate due to narrower counter-occlusal layer and higher stress

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative review design — no quantitative pooling; susceptible to selection bias and heterogeneous reporting
- High heterogeneity in preparation design, materials, surface treatment, luting agent protocols across included studies
- Most studies excluded nonvital teeth — limits generalizability
- Insufficient long-term (>10y) data for LDS and newer materials
- No clinical trial data (≥3y) for zirconia or resin-matrix hybrid LVs
- Insufficient data on resin-matrix hybrid materials and composite indirect LVs for long-term clinical recommendations
- Adhesive application to LV intaglio: contradictory findings between clinical and laboratory evidence — further research needed

## 7. Glossary

- **LV**: Laminate veneer — thin ceramic or composite restoration bonded to the enamel surface
- **LRG**: Leucite-reinforced glass ceramic (e.g. IPS Empress)
- **LDS**: Lithium disilicate ceramic (e.g. IPS e.max CAD/Press)
- **IDS**: Immediate dentin sealing — application of dentin adhesive immediately after preparation before temporization
- **HF**: Hydrofluoric acid etching — creates microporosities in silica-based ceramics for micromechanical interlocking
- **MDP**: 10-methacryloyloxydecyl dihydrogen phosphate — functional phosphate monomer for zirconia bonding
- **Debonding**: Loss of adhesive bond between the LV and abutment tooth
- **Window preparation**: Incisal non-overlap preparation design
- **Butt joint preparation**: Incisal overlap preparation design ending at the incisal edge
- **Palatal chamfer**: Incisal overlap design extending onto the palatal surface
