## Why Ingested

Cross-disciplinary validation of the osseodensification (OD) mechanism — the first application outside dentistry (spinal hardware fixation in a sheep model), showing OD raises pullout strength, BIC and BAFO. Strengthens the mechanistic generalizability claim in [[overviews/osseodensification-clinical-applications]] by demonstrating the compaction-autografting effect holds in non-dental bone.

## One-line Summary
Orthopaedic/spinal sheep model (12 sheep, cervical vertebrae C2/C3/C4) — OD raised pullout strength (p<0.01), BIC (p<0.01) and BAFO (p=0.024) vs regular drilling over 0/3/6-week time points.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Animal experimental (in vivo, biomechanical + histomorphometric), orthopaedic/spinal context
- **Journal**: Journal of the Mechanical Behavior of Biomedical Materials 2017;69:275–281
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.jmbbm.2016.12.020 (also reported as 10.1016/j.jmbbm.2017.01.020 in NIH manuscript metadata — DOI mismatch noted)
- **Sample**: 12 sheep (~65 kg avg), fixation devices 4 × 10 mm placed in C2, C3, C4 vertebral bodies; left = regular drilling, right = OD; C2/C4 = t=0, C3 = t=3 and t=6 weeks
- **Primary Question**: Does osseodensification drilling improve pullout strength and bone integration of spinal surgical hardware vs regular drilling?

## 2. Key Contributions
- **Translates OD outside dentistry** — first published application of Densah/OD principle to spinal hardware fixation.
- Pullout strength significantly higher for OD vs regular drilling (collapsed over time, **p<0.01**; Wilcoxon at C2/C4 p=0.031; C3 collapsed at 3+6 weeks p=0.027).
- BIC% significantly higher for OD vs R (**p<0.01**).
- BAFO% significantly higher for OD vs R (**p=0.024**).
- Histology shows **residual bone chips** in OD samples acting as nucleating sites for new bone — same mechanism Witek 2019 reports in dental implants.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
12 sheep (~65 kg). Each animal received 4 mm × 10 mm fixation devices in C2, C3, and C4 vertebral bodies. Within each animal, left-side devices were placed with regular drilling (R) and right-side devices with osseodensification drilling (OD) — paired within-subject design. C2 and C4 yielded the t=0 (immediate sacrifice) data point; C3 yielded t=3 weeks and t=6 weeks data. Outcomes: pullout strength (biomechanical), %BIC and %BAFO (histomorphometry). Statistical analysis used Wilcoxon signed-rank test for paired pullout comparisons.

A noted temporal pattern: BAFO **decreased** between 0 and 3 weeks, then **reversed** between 3 and 6 weeks, returning roughly to t=0 levels. Authors attribute the dip to initial remodeling resorption followed by woven-bone regain — a classic implant-healing pattern.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Pullout strength collapsed over time: OD > R, **p < 0.01**.
- Wilcoxon paired at C2 and C4 (t=0): each p = 0.031.
- C3 pullout at 3 and 6 weeks collapsed: p = 0.027.
- %BIC: OD > R, **p < 0.01**.
- %BAFO: OD > R, **p = 0.024**.
- BAFO time pattern: 0→3 weeks decrease, 3→6 weeks recovery to ≈ t=0.
- Histology: bone chips in OD samples (white arrows in Fig. 9), remodeling sites in both groups.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- **Orthopaedic / cervical spine context**, not dental implantation — generalizability to dental osteotomy is a separate inferential step.
- Bone composition and loading environment of cervical vertebrae differ from edentulous human alveolar ridge.
- 6 weeks maximum follow-up — no long-term fixation, fatigue, or loaded data.
- 12 sheep — moderate power; pairing within-subject mitigates between-animal variance.
- Same NYU/Coelho group (with Witek 2019, Inchingolo 2021 connections) — independence of evidence chain is constrained.
- DOI inconsistency in the manuscript metadata (16/j.jmbbm.2016.12.020 in citation vs 2017.01.020 in HHS public access stamp).

## 7. Glossary
- **Pullout strength**: biomechanical force required to extract a screw/fixation device from bone — primary anchorage metric in spinal hardware research.
- **Wilcoxon signed-rank test**: nonparametric paired test, appropriate for matched within-subject comparisons with small n.
- **Cervical vertebra C2/C3/C4**: upper cervical spine vertebral bodies used here as a paired anatomical site.
- **Remodeling**: bone-resorption/formation turnover that follows implant placement, typically with an early resorptive dip in bone density.
