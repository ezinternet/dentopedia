## One-line Summary
Beagle-dog histologic study (7 dogs, mandibular premolar fresh sockets, semi-submerged SLA implants): osseointegration that established at 4 weeks (woven bone filling the implant–socket gap and contacting the SLA surface) was subsequently lost at the buccal aspect as the thin buccal bone wall underwent marked surface resorption, with the buccal crest migrating >2 mm apical to the marginal SLA border by 12 weeks.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Clinical Oral Implants Research 2006;17(6):615–624
- **Pages**: 615–624 (10 pages)
- **Institution**: State University of Maringá (Brazil); Sahlgrenska Academy / University of Gothenburg (Sweden)
- **Study type**: In-vivo experimental animal study (beagle dog model, histologic ground sections)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Demonstrated that **osseointegration is not static** — bone-to-implant contact achieved in the early phase of socket healing can be **partially lost** as the buccal bundle bone is resorbed.
- Provided histologic evidence that the **buccal bone wall around immediate implants undergoes vertical and surface resorption**, with the marginal buccal crest dropping >2 mm apical to the implant shoulder by 12 weeks.
- Established the dog mandibular premolar model as a reproducible system for studying immediate-implant ridge changes — a model widely cited and replicated since.
- Provided the histologic basis for clinical recommendations to **place immediate implants palatally and to graft the buccal gap** when treating thin-walled sockets.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 7 beagle dogs; mandibular third (P3) and fourth (P4) premolars used as experimental teeth bilaterally.
- Buccal and lingual full-thickness flaps elevated; distal roots removed; implants installed in fresh extraction sockets; semi-submerged healing.
- In 5 dogs: right mandible operated first, then left mandible 2 months later; all sacrificed 1 month after the second (left) installation → giving paired ~4-week and ~12-week healing time points within each dog.
- In 2 dogs: bilateral installation in a single session; biopsies immediately after implant placement (baseline reference).
- Specimens processed by the Donath cutting–grinding technique for non-decalcified ground sectioning and stained for histologic and morphometric analysis.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **4 weeks**: the marginal gap between implant and socket walls was filled with **woven bone in direct contact with the SLA surface**.
- **Buccal and lingual cortical walls** both showed marked **surface resorption** during this interval.
- **Buccal bone wall height** decreased — the thin buccal plate (largely bundle bone) lost vertical dimension.
- **12 weeks**: the buccal crest was located **>2 mm apical** of the marginal border of the SLA surface, exposing previously osseointegrated implant surface to soft tissue.
- The lingual wall, which is thicker and less dependent on bundle bone, showed less vertical loss.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Animal model (beagle dog) — anatomy and healing kinetics differ from humans; absolute resorption magnitudes may not translate directly.
- Small sample (n = 7 dogs), short follow-up (max 12 weeks).
- Sites were healthy non-infected sockets — does not address immediate placement in infected or compromised sockets.
- No buccal gap graft or barrier membrane tested — does not directly answer whether bone grafting prevents the observed resorption.
- Single implant system / surface (SLA) — does not isolate the role of surface design.

## 7. Glossary
- **Tissue modeling**: shape change of a tissue by net resorption or formation at surfaces; here referring to the postextraction alveolar process.
- **Bundle bone**: bone that anchors periodontal ligament fibers; lines the inner socket wall and **disappears after extraction**, driving buccal plate loss.
- **SLA (sandblasted, large-grit, acid-etched)**: a roughened titanium implant surface that promotes early osseointegration.
- **Osseointegration**: direct structural and functional connection between living bone and an implant surface (Brånemark definition).
- **Semi-submerged healing**: implant healing protocol in which the cover screw or healing abutment protrudes through the mucosa during the integration phase.
