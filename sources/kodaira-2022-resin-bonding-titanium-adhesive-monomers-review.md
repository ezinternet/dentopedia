## Why Ingested

Extends the MDP chemical-bonding story from tooth/zirconia substrates to **titanium**. The sibling page [[wiki/resin-bonding/nagaoka-2017-mdp-zirconia-chemical-interaction-nmr]] establishes (by NMR) that 10-MDP forms a stable phosphate–zirconium chemical bond; this 2022 review surveys the parallel evidence that the same family of functional monomers (MDP, MEPS, 6-MHPA, 4-META) chemically bonds resin luting agents to titanium/titanium alloy — closing the substrate gap and reinforcing the "functional-monomer surface treatment" theme across the resin-bonding category. Timely because cast titanium restorations were newly added to Japan's national health insurance (NHI) system as palladium/gold prices rose, expanding clinical demand for reliable resin-to-titanium luting.

## One-line Summary

Narrative literature review (abstract-only) concluding that adhesive functional monomers (MDP, MEPS, 6-MHPA, 4-META) in primers/luting agents bond resin to titanium and titanium alloy as effectively as silanization plus tribochemical silica coating.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Surveys the literature on **titanium bonding systems**, focused specifically on the role of the **adhesive (functional) monomer** in the luting agent.
- Concludes that adhesive monomers are **effective for bonding to titanium**, achieving results **equal to** the established procedures of **silanization + tribochemical silica coating**.
- Identifies four functional monomers that promote resin–titanium bonding: **MDP** (10-methacryloyloxydecyl dihydrogen phosphate), **MEPS** (methacryloyloxyalkyl thiophosphate derivative), **6-MHPA** (6-methacryloxyhexyl phosphonoacetate), and **4-META** (4-methacryloyloxyethyl trimellitate anhydride).
- Clinical motivation: expanding the application of **resin-bonded fixed prostheses** made of titanium/titanium alloys, prompted by cast titanium restorations entering the Japanese NHI system after palladium/gold price increases.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Narrative literature review (not a systematic review / meta-analysis); no pooled statistics, search-protocol, or risk-of-bias appraisal reported in the abstract.
- Scope: titanium and titanium-alloy substrates; comparison axis is functional-monomer chemistry vs. silanization + tribochemical silica coating surface treatment.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

(abstract-only — quantitative bond-strength values not available in the abstract)

- Adhesive functional monomers are effective for bonding resin luting materials to titanium.
- Functional-monomer bonding performance is reported as **equal to** silanization + tribochemical silica coating.
- A primer or luting agent containing **MDP, MEPS, 6-MHPA, or 4-META** promotes the bonding characteristics between titanium and the resin material.

## 7. Glossary

- **MDP** — 10-methacryloyloxydecyl dihydrogen phosphate; phosphate functional monomer forming stable chemical bonds to metal oxides (zirconia, titanium).
- **MEPS** — methacryloyloxyalkyl thiophosphate derivative; thiophosphate functional monomer.
- **6-MHPA** — 6-methacryloxyhexyl phosphonoacetate; phosphonate/carboxylate functional monomer.
- **4-META** — 4-methacryloyloxyethyl trimellitate anhydride; carboxylate (anhydride) functional monomer.
- **Tribochemical silica coating** — air-abrasion with silica-modified alumina to deposit a silica layer enabling silane coupling on otherwise non-silica substrates.
- **Silanization** — application of a silane coupling agent to bridge silica surfaces and methacrylate resin.
- **NHI (Japanese national health insurance)** — public reimbursement system; inclusion of cast titanium restorations drove the clinical interest motivating this review.
