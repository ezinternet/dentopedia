## One-line Summary
Korean-language clinician special-feature narrative review summarizing the conservative-first treatment ladder for TMJ osteoarthritis: self-care, NSAIDs, occlusal splint, arthrocentesis (+HA/steroid), surgery as last resort.

## 1. Document Information

- Author: Ji Rak Kim (김지락), Dept. of Oral Medicine, Kyungpook National University
- Type: narrative review (임상가를 위한 특집 / clinician special feature)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Conservative-first treatment algorithm for TMJ OA in Korean clinical context
- Practical arthrocentesis technique detail (entry point, anesthesia choice, two-needle lavage)
- Drug-selection guidance (NSAID first-line; celecoxib if GI risk; muscle relaxant if myalgia)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative review; no systematic search or quantitative synthesis.
- Treatment goals framed as: pain reduction, halting condylar bone destruction, restoring joint function.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Start of care**: reassurance + self-management (avoid parafunction), the first-line intervention.
- **Pharmacology**: NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen, aceclofenac) most frequent — suppress IL-6/TNF-α; pain relief typically within ~1 week. Celecoxib (selective COX-2) for patients with GI disturbance history. Muscle relaxants when masticatory muscle tension/myalgia present. Cautions initial overdose side effects.
- **Occlusal splint**: effective; hard > soft (more durable, stable, retentive); full-arch covering with accurate opposing contacts preferred; upper vs lower appliance efficacy equivalent (upper more stable for severe bruxism/masseter hypertrophy; lower more esthetic/comfortable for long daytime wear); a retrospective study reported splint induced favorable condylar bone remodeling.
- **Arthrocentesis**: anesthesia by intra-articular infiltration or auriculotemporal nerve block — warns the temporal branch of the facial nerve may be co-anesthetized (eyelid won't close); deep needle insertion advised and patient must be pre-informed. Initial entry ~10 mm anterior + 2 mm inferior to the tragus along tragus–lateral canthus line (anatomy/opening-dependent — don't fixate on numbers). Two-needle bidirectional lavage more effective. Inject HA for lubrication, steroid for anti-inflammation/analgesia.
- **Surgery**: last resort (autograft/alloplastic joint replacement) for severe irreparable function loss + intractable pain; incomplete recovery, uncertain long-term prognosis, possible second surgery.
- Cytokine-based / disease-modifying OA drugs: research ongoing, not yet clinically applicable.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative review; no graded evidence or effect sizes.
- Korean-clinic context; device fabrication conventions may not generalize.
- Disease-modifying pharmacotherapy not yet available.
