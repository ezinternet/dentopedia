## One-line Summary
SR+MA of clinical endodontic irrigation and dressing protocols for apical periodontitis: single-visit treatment achieves higher healing rates (RR 1.10) than multi-visit with Ca(OH)2; NaOCl concentration and CHX vs NaOCl are not significant for 7-day pain.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: International Endodontic Journal 56(S3):422–435, 2023
- **DOI**: 10.1111/iej.13777
- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (clinical RCTs)
- **Special issue**: IEJ Supplement 3

## 2. Key Contributions
- Single-visit RCT superiority vs. multi-visit + Ca(OH)2 dressing: **RR 1.10 (95% CI 1.03–1.19)** for radiographic healing of apical periodontitis
- No significant difference between high vs. low NaOCl concentrations for healing or pain
- No significant difference between CHX and NaOCl for 7-day postoperative pain
- Ca(OH)2 as intracanal dressing: no additional healing benefit over single-visit in pooled RCTs

## 3. Methodology
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane, Embase (date range per protocol)
- **Inclusion**: RCTs in patients with radiographically confirmed apical periodontitis; reporting healing or pain outcomes; ≥12 months follow-up for healing outcomes
- **Primary outcomes**: Radiographic healing, postoperative pain
- **Statistical**: Random-effects meta-analysis; relative risk (RR) for healing, mean difference for pain scores

## 4. Key Results

### Healing (radiographic)
| Comparison | RR | 95% CI | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Single-visit vs. multi-visit + Ca(OH)2 | 1.10 | 1.03–1.19 | Single-visit superior |
| High vs. low NaOCl concentration | NS | — | No difference |

### Postoperative pain (7-day)
| Comparison | Result |
|---|---|
| CHX vs. NaOCl | NS (no significant difference) |
| High vs. low NaOCl concentration | NS |

## 5. Limitations
- Heterogeneity in study protocols (NaOCl concentrations, activation methods, follow-up intervals)
- "Single-visit" protocols varied considerably across included RCTs
- Radiographic healing criteria varied (periapical index, CBCT, PA radiograph)
- Limited data on CBCT-confirmed healing; most based on 2D periapical radiographs
- No data on patient-reported outcomes beyond pain (e.g., QoL, missed work)

## 7. Glossary
- **Apical periodontitis (AP)**: Inflammatory response at root apex due to bacterial biofilm; diagnosed radiographically as periapical radiolucency
- **Ca(OH)2**: Calcium hydroxide intracanal dressing; raises pH, bactericidal; used between visits in multi-visit protocols
- **RR (Relative Risk)**: Ratio of healing probability; RR 1.10 = 10% higher healing rate in single-visit group
- **Single-visit treatment**: Complete shaping, irrigation, and obturation in one appointment
