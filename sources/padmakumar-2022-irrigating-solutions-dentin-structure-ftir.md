## One-line Summary
In vitro FTIR + SEM study (n=48): silver citrate and ozonated olive oil cause less chemical alteration of root dentin than NaOCl 5.25%; silver citrate removes smear layer on SEM; all solutions lower microhardness vs. control.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Functional Biomaterials 13(4):197, 2022
- **DOI**: 10.3390/jfb13040197
- **Study type**: In vitro; n=48 extracted single-rooted human teeth

## 2. Key Contributions
- First FTIR analysis comparing silver citrate and ozonated olive oil (alternative/novel irrigants) vs. NaOCl 5.25%
- **Silver citrate**: Smear layer removal on SEM comparable to NaOCl; less FTIR dentin chemical alteration
- **Ozonated olive oil**: Minimal smear layer removal; least chemical alteration overall
- **NaOCl 5.25%**: Effective smear layer removal at coronal/middle; greatest FTIR dentin chemical change (collagen denaturation signal)
- All irrigants reduced microhardness compared to distilled water control; NaOCl had greatest reduction
- EDTA + NaOCl combination: best overall smear layer removal (SEM score) but more chemical alteration than alternatives

## 3. Methodology
- **n=48**: Human extracted single-rooted premolars; standardized preparation to ISO #40
- **Groups** (n=8 each):
  1. Distilled water (control)
  2. NaOCl 5.25%
  3. NaOCl 2.5%
  4. EDTA 17%
  5. NaOCl 5.25% + EDTA 17%
  6. Silver citrate 0.12%
  7. Ozonated olive oil
  8. CHX 2%
- **Outcomes**:
  - FTIR spectroscopy: chemical bond analysis (mineral/organic ratio, collagen integrity)
  - SEM: smear layer scoring (1–5 scale by canal third)
  - Vickers microhardness

## 4. Key Results

### Smear layer removal (SEM score, lower = less smear)
| Irrigant | Coronal | Middle | Apical |
|---|---|---|---|
| NaOCl 5.25% + EDTA | Best | Best | Best |
| NaOCl 5.25% | Good | Good | Moderate |
| Silver citrate | Good | Moderate | Moderate |
| CHX 2% | Poor | Poor | Poor |
| Ozonated olive oil | Minimal | Minimal | Minimal |

### FTIR dentin alteration (collagen integrity)
- NaOCl 5.25% > NaOCl 2.5% > EDTA > Silver citrate > Ozonated olive oil
- Lower collagen/mineral ratio with NaOCl = collagen denaturation
- Silver citrate and ozonated olive oil: FTIR near-baseline (minimal collagen disruption)

### Microhardness
- All irrigants: reduced vs. distilled water control (p < .05)
- NaOCl 5.25% + EDTA: greatest microhardness reduction

## 5. Limitations
- In vitro only; does not reflect clinical irrigation dynamics (agitation, temperature, flow)
- Silver citrate and ozonated olive oil not validated for clinical root canal use
- Antibacterial efficacy of novel irrigants not assessed in this study
- n=8 per group is small

## 7. Glossary
- **FTIR (Fourier Transform Infrared Spectroscopy)**: Technique measuring chemical bond vibration; identifies changes in mineral (phosphate peak 1035 cm⁻¹) and organic (amide I/II peaks 1640/1540 cm⁻¹) dentin components
- **Silver citrate**: Silver salt of citric acid; antimicrobial; proposed alternative to CHX; not yet in routine clinical use
- **Ozonated olive oil**: Olive oil saturated with ozone gas; antimicrobial/antifungal; low tissue toxicity; not standard irrigation protocol
- **Vickers microhardness**: Indentation hardness test; measures dentin resistance to permanent deformation; decreased by chelating agents that remove Ca²⁺
