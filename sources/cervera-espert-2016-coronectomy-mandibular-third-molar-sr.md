## One-line Summary
SR+MA (12 articles): coronectomy causes significantly less IAN sensory loss and fewer dry sockets than complete extraction; root migrates average 2 mm within 2 years; no difference in pain or infection.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Med Oral Patol Oral Cir Bucal. 2016 Jul 1;21(4):e505-13
- **DOI**: 10.4317/medoral.21074
- **Institution**: University of Valencia, Spain

## 2. Key Contributions
- SR+MA of coronectomy for impacted mandibular third molars in contact with IAC
- 12 articles included (PubMed + Cochrane; publication up to 2014; ≥10 cases; ≥6 months follow-up)
- Significantly less IAN sensory loss with coronectomy
- Root migration average 2 mm/2 years — rarely requires reintervention

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- SR+MA; PubMed + Cochrane; 12 eligible articles
- Inclusion: coronectomy of mandibular M3 near IAC; minimum 10 cases; minimum 6-month follow-up
- Outcomes: IAN sensory loss, dry socket, pain, infection, root migration, reintervention rate

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
| Outcome | Coronectomy vs Complete Extraction |
|---|---|
| IAN sensory loss | Significantly less (coronectomy superior) |
| Dry socket | Significantly less (coronectomy superior) |
| Pain | No statistically significant difference |
| Infection | No statistically significant difference |
| Root migration | Average 2 mm/2 years |

- Root fragment migrates coronally after coronectomy but rarely requires re-extraction
- Coronectomy strongly indicated when M3 is in direct contact with IAN

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Studies up to 2014 (pre-CBCT widespread adoption era)
- Heterogeneity among included studies
- Longer-term root migration data limited

## 6. Related Work
- Povoa-2021: SR covering 2176 coronectomies (IAN 0.59%)
- Sarikov-2014: IAN injury risk factors review
- Li-2022: IAC lingual position SR+MA

## 7. Glossary
- **M3**: Third Molar (mandibular)
- **IAC**: Inferior Alveolar Canal
- **IAN**: Inferior Alveolar Nerve
- **Coronectomy**: Crown removal only, roots left in socket
