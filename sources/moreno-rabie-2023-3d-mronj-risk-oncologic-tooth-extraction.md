## Why Ingested

기존 MRONJ 페이지들([[drug/jung-2022-denosumab-mronj-osteoporosis-5year]], [[drug/lee-2023-bisphosphonates-dental-procedures-bronj]], [[drug/baghalipour-2025-mronj-prevention-management-review]])은 임상·약물 위험인자 중심이고 **발치 전 영상기반 위험예측**은 다루지 않는다. 본 후향적 case–control(Moreno Rabie 2023)은 발치 전 CBCT가 임상적 골노출(MRONJ) 발생 전 단계의 골변화(골막반응·격리골)를 보여줄 수 있는지 검증해, "발치해도 되는가"의 영상 의사결정 근거를 보강한다. 동시 인제스트되는 비스포스포네이트 영상 페이지([[drug/koth-2017-clinical-radiographic-haematological-bisphosphonate-therapy]])와 짝을 이룬다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Three-dimensional clinical assessment for MRONJ risk in oncologic patients following tooth extractions
- **Authors**: Catalina Moreno Rabie, Rocharles Cavalcante Fontenele, Nicolly Oliveira Santos, Fernanda Nogueira Reis, Tim Van den Wyngaert, Reinhilde Jacobs (Leuven / Campinas / Antwerp / Karolinska)
- **Journal**: Dentomaxillofacial Radiology 2023;52(8):20230238
- **DOI**: 10.1259/dmfr.20230238
- **Study type**: Retrospective, longitudinal case–control study (single center, UZ/KU Leuven; STROBE-reported)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First study to use **pre-operative CBCT** (rather than panoramic) to identify clinical and local radiographic predictors of MRONJ in **oncologic** patients on anti-resorptive drugs (ARDs) undergoing tooth extraction.
- Proposes that **periosteal reaction and sequestrum formation** seen on CBCT *before* clinical bone exposure may represent **pre-clinical (latent) MRONJ lesions**.
- Documents **drug-type–specific** radiographic signatures: bisphosphonate use → significantly more osteosclerosis; denosumab → no early radiographic signal (risk of late detection).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Cohort**: 525 ARD-treated patients with CBCT (2010–2020) screened → **study group n = 47 oncologic patients (87 extractions)** meeting inclusion (oncologic-dose ARD; extraction within 1 yr of CBCT; documented socket follow-up). Excluded prior head/neck radiation, pre-existing MRONJ at site, poor image quality.
- **Control group**: n = 50 (106 extractions), matched for age, gender, extracted tooth; no ARD history.
- **ARDs**: zoledronic acid 4 mg, denosumab 120 mg, pamidronate 90 mg, alendronate, ibandronate. Bisphosphonate-only n = 21; denosumab-only n = 24; both n = 2.
- **Imaging**: 3D Accuitomo 170 or Newtom VGi evo CBCT; voxel 80–300 µm; assessed in IMPAX. Three blinded, calibrated oral/maxillofacial radiologists scored each extraction site; outcome = mode (≥2 agree); calibration on 21 external CBCTs.
- **Parameters scored per site**: alveolar bone loss (horizontal/angular), furcation, lamina dura (normal/thickened), PDL space (normal/widened), endodontic treatment quality, periapical lesion (size + cortical involvement), root remnant, trabecular pattern (osteosclerosis / osteolysis / **periosteal reaction** / **sequestrum**), and mandibular cortical width (MCW).
- **Stats**: χ²/Fisher's exact (categorical), Mann–Whitney U (ordinal); Fleiss' κ (interobserver), Cohen's κ (intraobserver, 48 sites re-read). p ≤ 0.05 significant.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **MRONJ incidence**: 15/47 patients (32%); 33/87 sites (38%). Worst stage: stage 1 in 63.6%, stage 2 in 30.3%, stage 3 in 6.1% of MRONJ sites.
- **Cancers**: breast 40.4%, multiple myeloma 23.4%, prostate 21.3%, renal/lung/stomach/pancreatic minority. Cancer type NOT associated with MRONJ (p = 0.642).
- **Study vs control radiographic differences** (ARD group significantly more): thickened lamina dura, widened PDL space, osteosclerosis, osteolysis, sequestrum formation. **Sequestrum formation occurred exclusively in the study group.**
- **Clinical risk factors for MRONJ (within study group)**:
  - **Multiple simultaneous extractions** (MRONJ+ mean 3 teeth vs MRONJ− 1 tooth, p = 0.006).
  - **Smoking** (current smokers 100% developed MRONJ, p = 0.004).
  - **Shorter drug holiday** (MRONJ+ 7.8 mo vs MRONJ− 27.5 mo, p = 0.021). Denosumab subgroup: MRONJ+ 1.9 mo vs MRONJ− 11 mo, p = 0.041; bisphosphonate subgroup p = 0.094 (NS).
  - **Younger age** (MRONJ+ mean 64 vs MRONJ− 71 yr, p = 0.025).
- **Radiographic predictors of bone exposure**: periosteal reaction (localized + extensive) → borderline higher risk (p = 0.051); all periosteal-reaction and sequestrum sites also had caries/periodontitis/periapical pathology with infectious/symptomatic teeth.
- **ARD-type comparison (BP n=42 vs Dmab n=42 extractions)**: NS for lamina dura, PDL, osteolysis, periosteal reaction, sequestrum. **Bisphosphonate → significantly more osteosclerosis** (localized 12% vs 9%, extensive 74% vs 45%, p = 0.003). Denosumab showed no distinguishing early sign.
- **Mucosal healing time**: control 2.4 wk vs study group 14.2 wk (p < 0.001); MRONJ+ sites 42.8 wk.
- **Reliability**: substantial interobserver agreement overall κ = 0.69 (range 0.41 PDL/osteolysis to 0.95 endodontic treatment); intraobserver κ = 0.87.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Retrospective, single-center; modest sample (47 oncologic patients); heterogeneous ARD regimens, doses, and CBCT protocols (voxel 80–300 µm, mixed devices).
- No histopathological confirmation of the proposed pre-clinical lesions (periosteal reaction, sequestrum).
- Periosteal-reaction association only borderline (p = 0.051); confounded by concurrent dental infection at all such sites.
- No multivariable regression / adjusted risk estimates reported; associations are univariable.
- Findings limited to oncologic (high-dose) population — not generalizable to osteoporosis-dose ARD.

## 6. Related Work

- Builds on prior panoramic-radiograph MRONJ predictor studies (Moreno-Rabié et al., Gaeta-Araujo et al.) by moving to 3D CBCT.
- AAOMS MRONJ staging (Ruggiero/AAOMS position paper) used to define outcome.
- Pichardo et al. — BRONJ vs DRONJ radiographic differences (BRONJ more sequestrum/cortical osteolysis), contextualized here at pre-clinical stage.

## 7. Glossary

- **MRONJ** — Medication-Related Osteonecrosis of the Jaw: exposed/probeable bone in maxillofacial region persisting >8 weeks in ARD-treated patients, no jaw radiation/metastasis.
- **ARD** — Anti-Resorptive Drug (bisphosphonates, denosumab).
- **BRONJ / DRONJ** — bisphosphonate- / denosumab-related ONJ.
- **Drug holiday** — interruption of ARD therapy before tooth extraction.
- **Sequestrum** — fragment of necrotic bone separated from healthy bone.
- **Periosteal reaction** — new bone formation by the periosteum in response to insult.
- **MCW** — Mandibular Cortical Width.
- **CBCT** — Cone-Beam Computed Tomography.
- **L-PRF** — Leukocyte- and Platelet-Rich Fibrin.
