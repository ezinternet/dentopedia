## Why Ingested

Combined human+animal SR+MA strengthening the osseodensification (OD) primary-stability evidence base — quantifies the human ISQ gain (median 8.57 at baseline, attenuating to MD 4.49 at secondary timepoint) and large animal histomorphometric effects (IT/BIC/BAFO), feeding the [[overviews/osseodensification-clinical-applications]] synthesis. Reinforces and updates the earlier pooled estimates of [[implants/inchingolo-2021-osseodensification-implant-site-osteotomy-sr-ma]].

## One-line Summary
SR+MA (Quintessence Int 2024;55:212–223) of 11 studies (8 databases through August 2021) — OD raised human ISQ by median 8.57 vs conventional drilling at baseline (no significant difference at secondary timepoint, MD 4.49) and yielded large effect sizes in animal studies (insertion torque MD 46.79; BIC MD 2.17; BAFO MD 2.11); certainty of evidence low to moderate.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (combined human + animal)
- **Journal**: Quintessence International 2024;55(3):212–223
- **DOI**: 10.3290/j.qi.b4656937; PubMed 37975644
- **Authors**: Fabiana Lima Monteiro, Cláudia L. Moreira, Vanessa Galego Arias Pecorari, Cássio Cardona Orth, Julio Cesar Joly, Daiane Peruzzo
- **Search**: 8 databases through August 2021
- **Sample**: 11 studies (subset analysed for human ISQ; subset for animal histomorphometry)
- **Quality**: Risk of bias — human studies: 3 moderate, 1 low; animal studies: 4 moderate, 2 low, 1 high. Certainty of evidence: low to moderate.
- **Primary Question**: Does OD drilling, compared with conventional drilling, increase human ISQ and IT? Does it increase animal ISQ, BIC, and BAFO?

## 2. Key Contributions
- Broadest OD SR+MA published to date (8 databases, 11 studies), pooling human and animal evidence in parallel arms.
- **Human studies — primary stability**: OD produced higher ISQ values with a summarised median difference of **8.57** versus conventional drilling. (Clinically substantial — comparable to ISQ "very stable" vs "moderate" categories.)
- **Human studies — secondary stability**: summarised median difference of **4.49** in favour of OD — *not* statistically significant at this timepoint.
- **Animal studies — insertion torque**: mean difference (MD) of **46.79** Ncm favouring OD vs conventional — very large effect size.
- **Animal studies — BIC**: MD = 2.17 favouring OD.
- **Animal studies — BAFO**: MD = 2.11 favouring OD.
- High heterogeneity (I² not in extracted text, but the authors flag it) across studies.
- Risk of bias: moderate in most studies (3 of 4 human studies moderate, 1 low; 4 of 7 animal studies moderate, 2 low, 1 high).
- Certainty of evidence ranged from low to moderate via GRADE-style assessment.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
A PRISMA-style SR+MA searching eight databases up to August 2021. Eligibility: human and animal studies comparing OD with conventional drilling for endosseous implants. Two parallel meta-analytic arms were structured:

**Human arm** — outcomes: ISQ via RFA at baseline (primary stability) and at follow-up (secondary stability); insertion torque (IT). Effect-size metric: summarised median difference.

**Animal arm** — outcomes: insertion torque, BIC, BAFO. The counterclockwise OD configuration was compared with conventional drilling. Effect-size metric: mean difference.

Of the 447 publications initially screened, 11 were included. Risk-of-bias appraisal: 4 human studies (3 moderate, 1 low); 7 animal studies (4 moderate, 2 low, 1 high). Certainty of evidence rated low to moderate, reflecting heterogeneity and the small individual study sizes.

The very large animal IT effect (MD 46.79) reflects the magnitude of OD vs conventional torque differences in low-density bone preclinical models — consistent with Huwais 2017 bench numbers — but the high heterogeneity warns against treating this as a precise estimate.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **n studies**: 11 (out of 447 screened)
- **Human ISQ baseline (primary stability)**: summarised median difference = **8.57** favouring OD
- **Human ISQ follow-up (secondary stability)**: summarised median difference = **4.49** favouring OD — NOT statistically significant
- **Animal insertion torque**: MD = **46.79** (very large effect size, high heterogeneity)
- **Animal BIC**: MD = **2.17** favouring OD
- **Animal BAFO**: MD = **2.11** favouring OD
- **Risk of bias** — human studies: 3 moderate, 1 low; animal studies: 4 moderate, 2 low, 1 high
- **Certainty of evidence**: low to moderate
- Specific 95% CI and I² heterogeneity statistics not extractable from the available text.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Combined human and animal evidence, but only 4 human studies — the strongest claim (OD primary stability) rests on a thin clinical base.
- High heterogeneity flagged by authors; certainty of evidence low-to-moderate.
- "Summarised median difference" is an unconventional pooled effect metric; readers should refer to the forest plots in the full PDF.
- Risk of bias: most studies moderate; 1 animal study high risk.
- Secondary stability ISQ difference (MD 4.49) was not significant — by 4–6 months, OD and conventional implants converge on stability, suggesting biology equalises the early mechanical advantage.
- Search ends August 2021 — newer RCTs not captured.
- Authors call for further RCTs.

## 7. Glossary
- **OD (Osseodensification)**: Counterclockwise compaction drilling with tapered multi-fluted bur.
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: 1–100 RFA-derived lateral stability index.
- **IT (Insertion Torque)**: Peak rotational resistance during implant placement (Ncm).
- **BIC**: Histomorphometric % of implant surface in direct bone contact.
- **BAFO**: Bone area fraction occupancy.
- **MD (Mean Difference)**: Difference of group means in original units.
- **Summarised median difference**: Pooled median-of-differences metric used here for human ISQ — less standard than WMD/SMD.
- **GRADE**: System for rating certainty of evidence (high / moderate / low / very low).
