## Why Ingested

Adds a post-PCI (percutaneous coronary intervention) clinical dataset directly on point with the perioperative-antiplatelet bleeding question — 100 patients within 1 year of coronary stenting, extracted on uninterrupted single (aspirin) vs dual (aspirin + clopidogrel) antiplatelet therapy. Reinforces the continue-antiplatelet recommendation in [[drug/anticoagulants/shah-2022-perioperative-antiplatelet-therapy-sr-ma]] by supplying a high-risk recent-stent population where interruption fear is greatest yet all bleeding was locally controllable. (abstract-only — full text not retrieved)

## One-line Summary

Prospective study (n=100, recent-PCI patients) — uninterrupted single vs dual antiplatelet therapy showed no significant difference in post-extraction bleeding (complete hemostasis 78% vs 64%, p=0.08); all persistent/delayed bleeding controlled locally.

## 한줄요약

전향적 연구 (n=100, 최근 PCI 환자) — 단일 vs 이중 항혈소판요법을 중단하지 않은 채 발치 시 출혈에 유의차 없음 (완전지혈 78% vs 64%, p=0.08); 모든 지속·지연 출혈은 국소 처치로 조절됨.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides direct clinical evidence in a **recent-PCI (within 1 year of percutaneous stent insertion)** population — the subgroup where clinicians are most tempted to interrupt antiplatelet therapy and where stent thrombosis risk on interruption is highest.
- Compares **single antiplatelet therapy (SAPT)** — aspirin 100 mg — against **dual antiplatelet therapy (DAPT)** — aspirin 100 mg + clopidogrel 75 mg — for post-extraction bleeding, both arms uninterrupted.
- Concludes that simple or complicated extraction of multiple teeth can be performed safely within the first post-PCI year **without interruption** of antiplatelet therapy.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Prospective, two-group comparative study.
- **Population**: 100 patients with history of percutaneous coronary stent insertion in the past year, requiring tooth extraction.
- **Groups**: Monotherapy group (n=50, aspirin 100 mg) vs dual-therapy group (n=50, aspirin 100 mg + clopidogrel 75 mg). Antiplatelet therapy uninterrupted in both arms.
- **Outcome categories**: "Complete hemostasis," "persistent bleeding," "delayed bleeding."
- **Covariates analyzed**: Sex, age, underlying diseases, number of teeth and roots extracted, type of procedure (simple vs complicated/surgical).
- **Statistics**: Between-group comparison of bleeding status against demographic/procedural variables.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Complete hemostasis**: 39/50 (78%) monotherapy vs 32/50 (64%) dual therapy.
- **Persistent bleeding**: 11/50 (22%) monotherapy vs 14/50 (28%) dual therapy.
- **Delayed bleeding**: 0/50 monotherapy vs 4/50 (8%) dual therapy.
- **Between-group difference in bleeding status: not statistically significant (p = 0.08).**
- No significant association of bleeding with sex, age, underlying diseases, number of teeth/roots extracted, or procedure type (all p > 0.05).
- **All persistent and delayed bleeding episodes were easily controlled by local hemostatic measures.**

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only retrieval — exact local hemostatic protocol, follow-up window for "delayed" bleeding, and definitions are not captured here.
- Single-center, modest sample (n=100); p=0.08 trend toward more bleeding with DAPT may reflect underpowering rather than true equivalence.
- No interrupted/bridged comparator arm — study addresses the safety of NOT interrupting, not a head-to-head against interruption.
- Observational (not randomized) allocation between SAPT and DAPT.

## 7. Glossary

- **PCI (Percutaneous Coronary Intervention)**: Catheter-based coronary revascularization, typically with stent placement.
- **SAPT (Single Antiplatelet Therapy)**: One agent, here aspirin 100 mg.
- **DAPT (Dual Antiplatelet Therapy)**: Two agents, here aspirin + clopidogrel; standard after stenting to prevent stent thrombosis.
- **Exodontia**: Tooth extraction.
- **Persistent bleeding**: Bleeding continuing immediately after the procedure beyond expected.
- **Delayed bleeding**: Re-bleeding after initial hemostasis was achieved.
