## 2. Key Contributions

1. Comprehensive integration of dentin structure, permeability, and pain transmission theories in a single 2026 clinical reference.
2. Detailed mechanistic coverage including hydrodynamic theory (Brännström), direct innervation theory, neuroplasticity/sensitization (TRP receptors, CGRP, substance P), and odontoblast mechanosensory hypothesis.
3. Systematic diagnostic framework: history → controlled stimulation → pain assessment → exclusion of pulpal/periodontal/restorative/structural pathologies.
4. Tiered management algorithm: patient education → home desensitizing agents → in-office occlusive/laser/iontophoresis → restorative/surgical intervention → endodontic therapy (last resort).
5. Review of emerging biomimetic and nanotechnology strategies: nano-hydroxyapatite, self-assembling peptides (P11-4, SAPM), calcium-phosphate delivery systems (CPP-ACP, bioactive glass, NovaMin, functionalized tricalcium phosphate), mesoporous silica nanoparticles, and magnetic bioceramic nanobots ("CalBots", 387 ± 55 nm).
6. Triple-C recall framework (Continue / Change / Cease) for long-term maintenance.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Electronic literature search of major biomedical databases and relevant dental journals, supplemented by hand-searching of reference lists.
- **Selection criteria**: Clinical relevance, methodological quality, applicability to diagnosis and management of dentin hypersensitivity.
- **Perspective**: Restorative dentistry and endodontic focus; integrates basic science with clinical practice.
- **Limitations acknowledged**: Variable prevalence data (1%–98%) reflecting methodological heterogeneity across surveys; no single "gold standard" therapy; most emerging biomimetic approaches lack robust long-term clinical trial evidence.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Epidemiology
- Prevalence: 1%–98% across studies; peaks in 3rd–4th decade; higher in women; most affected teeth: canines > first premolars > incisors; buccal cervical surfaces most commonly affected.

### Mechanistic Theories
- **수역학설 (Hydrodynamic Theory)**: Fluid displacement in dentinal tubules activates mechanosensitive A-delta nerve fibers; open tubule density doubled and diameter increased at hypersensitive sites; basis for tubule-occluding therapies.
- **직접 신경지배설 (Direct Innervation Theory)**: Myelinated Aδ fibers may approach DEJ; C fibers involved in dull persistent pain; complements hydrodynamic explanation.
- **신경가소성 (Neuroplasticity)**: Peripheral sensitization via inflammatory mediators (substance P, CGRP, leukotriene B4); upregulation of Nav channels and TRP receptors (TRPV1 highlighted); explains persistent symptoms despite tubule occlusion.
- **상아모세포 기계감각 (Odontoblast Mechanosensory)**: Odontoblasts express TRP family members, voltage-gated Na channels, mechanosensitive K/Ca channels; may act as primary sensory transducers.

### Diagnostic Process
- Diagnosis of exclusion; differential includes: caries, cracked tooth syndrome, reversible/irreversible pulpitis, defective restorations, postoperative sensitivity.
- Controlled clinical stimulation tests: thermal, air-blast (evaporative), tactile (probe), osmotic (hypertonic sucrose).

### Home Management
- Desensitizing toothpastes: tubule-occluding (stannous fluoride, strontium salts, CPP, NovaMin/arginine) + nerve-modulating (potassium nitrate).
- RDA of dentifrice should remain below 250.
- Potassium nitrate, stannous fluoride, NovaMin (calcium sodium phosphosilicate), arginine — superior clinical results.
- Mouth rinses (potassium nitrate, aluminum lactate, arginine) comparable to toothpastes; improvement within 4–6 weeks.

### In-Office Management
- **Fluoride varnishes** (5% NaF, e.g., Duraphat): forms fluorapatite, partial tubule sealing.
- **Oxalate salts**: calcium oxalate crystals within tubules; 1.5%–3% potassium oxalate strips.
- **Glutaraldehyde + HEMA (e.g., Gluma)**: protein coagulation, multilayer protein plugs — immediate and long-lasting relief.
- **Lasers**: Nd:YAG (1064 nm) and Er,Cr:YSGG (2780 nm) — superior immediate reduction; diode (685–980 nm) — more gradual. Combined laser + topical agents: superior short-term, not confirmed long-term.
- **Iontophoresis**: Low-intensity current for fluoride/potassium penetration; limited contemporary use.

### Restorative / Surgical
- Resin composites, RMGIC for significant hard-tissue loss.
- Connective tissue grafts / coronally advanced flaps for gingival recession-associated hypersensitivity.
- Endodontic therapy: last resort only.

### Emerging Technologies
- **Nano-hydroxyapatite (nHAp)**: biomimetic mineral deposition in/over tubules; encouraging dentifrice results.
- **Self-assembling peptides (SAP/SAPM)**: fibrillar matrix scaffold → hydroxyapatite nucleation → intrafibrillar remineralization (biomimetic-guided mineral regrowth); limited long-term clinical trials.
- **Mesoporous silica nanoparticles** (CCMS, Ca-DMSN-L, P-DMSN-S): controlled Ca/PO4 ion release.
- **CalBots**: magnetic calcium silicate nanobots, 387 ± 55 nm; penetrate ~500 µm into tubules; currently experimental.
- **Potassium-doped bioactive glass**: dual mechanism — tubule occlusion (Ca/PO4) + neural desensitization (K⁺).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative (not systematic) review — selection bias possible; no formal quality assessment of included studies.
- No meta-analysis; no quantitative synthesis of effect sizes.
- Biomimetic and nano-based approaches: mostly in vitro/early in vivo evidence; standardized protocols and long-term RCTs still needed.
- Emerging delivery systems (CalBots): limited by cost, accessibility, inconsistent clinical outcomes.

## 7. Glossary

- **수역학설 (Hydrodynamic Theory)**: Brännström's theory — external stimuli displace dentinal tubule fluid → mechanosensitive nerve activation.
- **상아질 세관 (Dentinal Tubules)**: Microscopic channels from pulp to DEJ containing odontoblastic processes and fluid.
- **도말층 (Smear Layer)**: Amorphous layer of ground dentin fragments overlying exposed tubule orifices; when disrupted, increases tubule patency and hypersensitivity.
- **상아모세포 (Odontoblast)**: Dentin-forming cell lining the pulp chamber; may act as mechanosensor via TRP ion channels.
- **NovaMin (칼슘나트륨인산규산염)**: Calcium sodium phosphosilicate bioactive glass releasing Ca²⁺ and PO₄³⁻ ions for tubule occlusion and remineralization.
- **TRP 수용체 (Transient Receptor Potential receptors)**: Ion channels expressed on sensory neurons and odontoblasts; upregulated in peripheral sensitization.
- **CGRP (칼시토닌 유전자 관련 펩타이드, Calcitonin Gene-Related Peptide)**: Neuropeptide released by sensitized pulpal nerves; mediates neurogenic inflammation.
- **Triple-C 유지 체계 (Triple-C Recall Framework)**: Continue / Change / Cease — pragmatic post-treatment maintenance decision algorithm.
- **CalBots**: Magnetic calcium silicate bioceramic nanoparticles (~387 nm) guided by external magnetic field to penetrate dentinal tubules.
