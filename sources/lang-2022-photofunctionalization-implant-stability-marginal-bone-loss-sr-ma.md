## Why Ingested

This is the first photofunctionalization-SPECIFIC clinical SR+MA on implant stability and marginal bone loss in the wiki. It complements [[implants/surface/plasma/pesce-2020-photo-plasma-activation-titanium-sr-ma]] (which pooled photo- and plasma-activation together) by isolating UV photofunctionalization alone, and it gives meta-analytic backing to the single-cohort early-stability signal in [[implants/isq/seol-2017-uv-implant-resonance-frequency-early-loading]] and the 7-year prospective clinical data in [[implants/surface/hirota-2020-uv-photofunctionalization-dental-implant-7year]].

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (7 clinical studies, 5 in meta-analysis; 430 patients / 734 implants): UV photofunctionalization significantly increased the osseointegration speed index (OSI; MD = 2.13, p = 0.007) and improved implant stability at 2 months (MD = 3.48, p = 0.04), but pooled effects on stability at other time points and on marginal bone loss were non-significant — high heterogeneity, low study count and high risk of bias temper the conclusion.

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (임상연구 7편, 메타분석 5편; 환자 430명 / 임플란트 734개): UV 광기능화는 골유착 속도지수(OSI, MD=2.13, p=0.007)와 식립 2개월째 임플란트 안정성(MD=3.48, p=0.04)을 유의하게 높였으나, 다른 시점 안정성과 변연골소실(MBL)에 대한 통합 효과는 비유의 — 이질성·연구 수·비뚤림 위험으로 결론은 제한적.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First evidence-based synthesis of the **clinical** (not animal) effects of UV photofunctionalization on dental implant outcomes.
- Quantifies two endpoints separately: implant stability (ISQ via resonance frequency analysis + one removal-torque study) and marginal bone loss (MBL).
- Introduces and pools the **osseointegration speed index (OSI)** = [(ISQ at loading) − (ISQ at placement)] / healing time in months — a rate metric that normalizes across studies with different healing periods.
- Identifies that the strongest, most consistent clinical signal of photofunctionalization is the *rate* of establishing stability (OSI), not absolute stability or MBL.
- Catalogues the main heterogeneity drivers: bone quality, implant characteristics, and non-standardized UV parameters (wavelength 250–382 nm, processing time 10–20 min, varied devices).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Focused question (PICOS)**: In adults (≥18 y) receiving dental implants, does photofunctionalization (test) vs non-photofunctionalization (control) improve implant stability and reduce MBL?
- **Eligible designs**: RCTs, CCTs, cohort and case-control studies. Excluded: animal/in vitro, case reports/series, reviews, cross-sectional.
- **Primary outcomes**: implant stability + rate of stability development (OSI). **Secondary outcome**: MBL (mesial and distal).
- **Screening**: 1788 records from databases → 292 duplicates removed → 1484 excluded on title/abstract → 12 included; +185 from manual search (3 to full-text). After full-text of 15 articles, 8 excluded → **7 studies in qualitative synthesis, 5 in meta-analysis**.
- **Statistics**: Review Manager 5.4; mean differences (MD) with 95% CI; significance at p = 0.05 (two-tailed z). Heterogeneity via Q-test and I². Fixed-effects if I² < 50%, otherwise random-effects.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Included studies (7 total; 5 meta-analyzed)
- Designs: 5 RCTs (2 split-mouth), 1 CCT, 1 case-control.
- Publication years: 1 in 2016, 2 in 2020, 4 in 2021.
- Countries: Japan, Lithuania, Pakistan, Korea, India.
- **Population: 430 patients, 734 implants.**
- All used delayed loading; reported healing time 3–8 months (4 studies). One study used immediate implants.
- UV parameters varied: wavelengths 250–382 nm (382 nm UVA, 260 nm UVC, 257.3 nm reported); devices TheraBeam Affinity (λ ≈ 360 nm + 250 nm), TheraBeam SuperOsseo (≈0.05 mW/cm² at 360 nm; ≈2 mW/cm² at 250 nm), and others; processing time 10–20 min in 6 studies.

### Risk of bias
- RCTs (RoB 2.0, n=5): 1 (20%) "some concerns", 3 (60%) "high risk" (main driver: selection of reported result; 2 with baseline-difference concerns). All RCTs low risk for deviations, missing data, outcome measurement.
- Case-control and CCT (ROBINS-I): both **serious risk of bias**.

### Meta-analysis — Implant stability (ISQ, MD; test − control)
- **Immediately after placement** (2 studies, 3 outcomes): NS — MD = −5.73, 95% CI −18.47 to 7.01, p = 0.38, I² = 86%.
- **2 weeks** (Heo & Shah; 52 PF vs 53 non-PF implants): NS — MD = 0.24, 95% CI −0.68 to 1.16, p = 0.62, I² = 0%.
- **4 weeks**: NS — MD = 0.97, 95% CI −0.88 to 2.82, p = 0.31, I² = 69%.
- **2 months**: **SIGNIFICANT** in favor of photofunctionalization — MD = 3.48, 95% CI −0.23 to 6.73*, p = 0.04, I² = 88% (significant heterogeneity, p = 0.004). *(CI as reported in the paper.)*
- **4 months**: NS — MD = 6.22, 95% CI −4.19 to 16.63, p = 0.24, I² = 99%.

### Meta-analysis — OSI (rate of stability development; 2 studies)
- **SIGNIFICANT increase with photofunctionalization** — MD = 2.13, 95% CI 0.57 to 3.68, p = 0.007, I² = 81%.

### Meta-analysis — Marginal bone loss (MBL, MD; test − control; 4 studies reported MBL, 2 pooled)
- **2 months — mesial**: NS — MD = −0.05, 95% CI −0.40 to 0.29, p = 0.76, I² = 89%.
- **2 months — distal**: NS — MD = −0.22, 95% CI −0.64 to 0.19, p = 0.29, I² = 89%.
- **6 months — mesial**: NS — MD = −0.27, 95% CI −0.67 to 0.13, p = 0.18, I² = 93%.
- **6 months — distal**: NS — MD = −0.48, 95% CI −1.13 to 0.17, p = 0.14, I² = 97%.
- Direction of point estimates favors photofunctionalization (lower MBL) at all four, but none significant; Choi and Sandhu (excluded from pooling for incompatible MBL timepoints/missing SDs) both reported lower MBL with PF.

### Bottom line
Photofunctionalization significantly improves the **rate** of establishing implant stability (OSI) and absolute stability at the **2-month** time point; effects on stability at other times and on MBL are not statistically significant. Qualitatively, benefit appears greatest in poor-quality bone and in complex/staged cases.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Few pooled studies (only 5 meta-analyzed; OSI and several stability/MBL pools rest on just 2 studies each).
- High heterogeneity across most pools (I² up to 99%), driven by differences in bone quality, implant characteristics, and non-standardized UV parameters (wavelength, processing time, device).
- High/serious risk of bias in most included studies (selection of reported result; confounding; missing data).
- Three studies did not report ISQ ± SD; one did not report MBL ± SD — authors could not obtain additional data, forcing exclusion from pooling.
- Future work: larger, long-term RCTs with rigorous design controlling for bone quality and implant factors; head-to-head RCTs comparing UV wavelengths (e.g., UVC vs UVA) and processing times to identify optimal photofunctionalization parameters.

## 7. Glossary

- **Photofunctionalization (UV treatment, 광기능화)**: UV irradiation of titanium/TiO₂ surfaces to remove hydrocarbon contamination, restore superhydrophilicity and positive surface charge, and improve protein/cell adhesion — reverses biological "aging" of titanium.
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient, 임플란트 안정성 지수)**: resonance frequency analysis (RFA) measure of implant mechanical stability (0–100).
- **OSI (Osseointegration Speed Index, 골유착 속도지수)**: [(ISQ at loading) − (ISQ at placement)] / healing time (months); rate of stability development.
- **MBL (Marginal Bone Loss, 변연골소실)**: radiographic peri-implant crestal bone loss; success threshold ≤1.5 mm first year, ≤0.2 mm/year thereafter.
- **MD (Mean Difference)**: pooled effect size for continuous outcomes (test − control).
- **I² / Q-test**: statistical heterogeneity metrics across studies.
- **RoB 2.0 / ROBINS-I**: Cochrane risk-of-bias tools for RCTs / non-randomized studies.
