## One-line Summary
Prospective study (70 implants, 16 patients, 7yr): UV-PF (15 min, pre-placement) → regular/complex cases 100% success; cancer-resection cases 22.2%; ISQ +21.9 in complex cases; BIC ~60% (aged Ti) vs ~90% (fresh Ti).

## 1. Document Information
J Clin Med 2020;9:2733. doi:10.3390/jcm9092733. Yokohama City University + UCLA. Single-center prospective.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Longest follow-up (7 years) for UV-photofunctionalized implants
- Demonstrates UV-PF achieves 100% success in regular AND anatomically complex (severely atrophied) cases
- Cancer-resection bone: significantly lower success (22.2%) — UV-PF cannot overcome pathophysiological compromise
- ISQ increase of 21.9 points in complex cases confirms accelerated secondary osseointegration

## 3. Methodology
16 patients, 70 implants (4 submerged). UV irradiation 15 min prior to placement. Groups: regular (n=30), complex (n=21, severely atrophied requiring GBR/sinus), cancer (n=15, resection/radiation sites). ISQ at placement and 2nd-stage surgery.

## 4. Key Results
| Group | n | 7-yr success | ISQ change |
|-------|---|-------------|------------|
| Regular | 30 | 100% | +3.2 |
| Complex (atrophied) | 21 | 100% | +21.9 |
| Cancer resection | 15 | 22.2% | −3.5 |
- Multivariate: bone quality, location, cancer resection = significant failure predictors

## 5. Limitations
Single center; small n; no randomized control (no non-UV group); cancer group heterogeneous (radiation ± resection).

## 6. Related Work
Park 2025 (comprehensive UV-PF review), Minamikawa 2016 (aging mechanism), Seol 2017 (ISQ case reports).

## 7. Glossary
Complex cases: severely atrophied jaws requiring GBR or sinus augmentation; UV-PF: 15-min UV irradiation removing hydrocarbon from Ti surface.
