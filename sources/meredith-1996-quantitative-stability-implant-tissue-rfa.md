This is the foundational origin paper that introduced **Resonance Frequency Analysis (RFA)** as a non-invasive method to quantify implant stability — the entire ISQ field traces back to it. ## One-line Summary

Landmark in-vitro + in-vivo method paper that first introduced resonance frequency analysis (RFA) for quantifying implant-tissue interface stability; a small transducer's resonance frequency correlated strongly (r=0.94, p<0.01) with exposed fixture height and rose with surrounding-material stiffness, founding what later became the ISQ scale.

## 한줄요약

공명주파수분석 (Resonance Frequency Analysis, RFA)을 임플란트-조직 계면 안정성 정량화에 처음 도입한 기념비적 in-vitro·in-vivo 방법 논문 — 소형 트랜스듀서의 공명주파수가 노출된 고정체 높이와 강한 상관(r=0.94, p<0.01)을 보였고 주변 재료의 강성(stiffness)이 증가하면 함께 상승했으며, 이것이 훗날 임플란트 안정성 지수 (Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ) 척도의 기원이 되었다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Quantitative determination of the stability of the implant-tissue interface using resonance frequency analysis
- **Authors**: N. Meredith (Bristol Dental Hospital, Department of Oral and Dental Science), D. Alleyne, P. Cawley
- **Journal**: Clinical Oral Implants Research, Volume 7, Issue 3, Pages 261–267
- **Year / Date**: September 1996
- **DOI**: 10.1034/j.1600-0501.1996.070308.x
- **Study type**: In-vitro experimental (transducer/aluminium-block + PMMA polymerisation model) with in-vivo confirmation
- **Source extracted**: Wiley Online Library abstract page (full methods/results tables not present in the available PDF)

## 2. Key Contributions

- **Introduces RFA to implant dentistry** — proposes resonance frequency analysis as a non-invasive, quantitative alternative to the crude clinical stability tests of the era (percussion with a blunt instrument, non-standardised radiographs).
- **Establishes the physical principle** — the resonance frequency of a small transducer attached to an implant fixture tracks the stability/stiffness of the implant-tissue interface.
- **Demonstrates sensitivity to exposed fixture height** — a strong correlation (r=0.94, p<0.01) between measured resonance frequency and the height of fixture exposed above its embedding medium, i.e. RFA detects effective implant length / level of marginal support.
- **Models the healing process** — embedding implants in self-curing PMMA and measuring resonance frequency during polymerisation reproduces the rising interfacial stiffness of bone maturation, with frequency increasing as stiffness increased.
- **In-vivo validation** — resonance frequency measurements on implants in vivo correlated well with the in-vitro findings, supporting clinical applicability.
- This work is the conceptual and methodological seed for the later **Implant Stability Quotient (ISQ)** scale and commercial RFA devices.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Three linked experiments (per abstract):

1. **Exposed-height model (in-vitro).** Implant fixtures were embedded at different heights in an aluminium block; a small transducer was attached and its resonance frequency measured. Resonance frequency was related to the height of fixture exposed.
2. **Stiffness/healing model (in-vitro).** Implants were embedded in self-curing polymethylmethacrylate (PMMA); resonance frequency was measured at intervals during polymerisation, simulating the increasing stiffness of bone surrounding an implant during healing.
3. **In-vivo measurement.** Resonance frequency measurements were made on implants in vivo and compared against the in-vitro results.

The transducer-on-fixture resonance frequency is the single measured output across all three; the design isolates two stability determinants — effective exposed implant length (height) and surrounding-material stiffness (maturation).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Resonance frequency vs exposed fixture height**: strong correlation, **r = 0.94, p < 0.01** (in-vitro aluminium-block model).
- **PMMA polymerisation model**: a significant increase in resonance frequency was observed, related to the increase in surrounding-material stiffness during curing.
- **In-vivo**: measurements correlated well with the in-vitro findings.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Primarily a proof-of-concept method paper — small/early experimental scope; clinical thresholds (e.g. loading cut-offs) are not established here.
- In-vitro models (aluminium block, PMMA) approximate but do not equal living bone behaviour.
- The raw resonance frequency (kHz) reported here is not yet the normalised 1–100 ISQ scale; that abstraction came in later work.
- Available PDF is the abstract page only — detailed methods, full results, and discussion are not extractable.

## 7. Glossary

- **Resonance Frequency Analysis (RFA, 공명주파수분석)**: non-invasive technique measuring the resonance frequency of a transducer attached to an implant; the frequency reflects interface stability/stiffness.
- **Implant Stability Quotient (ISQ, 임플란트 안정성 지수)**: the normalised 1–100 scale later derived from RFA resonance frequency (not yet defined in this 1996 paper).
- **PMMA (Polymethylmethacrylate, 폴리메틸메타크릴레이트)**: self-curing acrylic used here to model the stiffening of bone during healing.
- **Transducer**: small vibrating sensor attached to the fixture, excited to find the implant-transducer resonance frequency.
- **Exposed fixture height**: length of implant fixture above its embedding medium — a proxy for effective implant length / level of marginal support.
