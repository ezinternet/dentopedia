## Why Ingested

This paper supplies the atomic-level mechanistic basis for why 10-MDP-containing primers/cements bond durably to zirconia — the chemistry that downstream clinical comparisons (cleaning protocols, primer head-to-heads) take for granted. It establishes, via ¹H/³¹P MAS NMR + 2D HETCOR, that 10-MDP binds zirconia through both ionic interaction (deprotonated P-O⁻ ↔ partial-positive Zr) AND hydrogen bonding (P=O···Zr-OH / P-OH···Zr-OH), rejecting the prior "ionic-only" hypothesis. It is the zirconia counterpart of the MDP-on-tooth-substrate nanolayering work and underpins [[wiki/resin-bonding/yoshihara-2011-nanolayering-mdp-enamel-dentin]]: both establish that 10-MDP's bonding effectiveness rests on a specific, NMR/TEM-verified chemical interaction with the substrate, not merely on micromechanical retention.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro NMR mechanistic study (¹H/³¹P MAS NMR + 2D ¹H→³¹P HETCOR) showing 10-MDP binds zirconia via both ionic (deprotonated P-O⁻ ↔ partial-positive Zr) and hydrogen bonding (P=O / non-deprotonated P-OH ↔ Zr-OH), with concentration-dependent shear bond strength; the "ionic-only" hypothesis was rejected.

## 한줄요약

## 2. Key Contributions

- First study to resolve the chemical interaction of 10-MDP with zirconia at the atomic level using solid-state ¹H and ³¹P MAS NMR plus 2D ¹H→³¹P HETCOR NMR — methods that, unlike TOF-SIMS, FTIR, or XPS used in prior work, give direct structural evidence of the *type* of chemical bond.
- Demonstrated that 10-MDP–zirconia bonding is NOT solely ionic (the tested hypothesis was rejected): it involves both ionic bonding (deprotonated P-O⁻ ↔ partially positive Zr) and hydrogen bonding (P=O oxo group and one non-deprotonated P-OH ↔ Zr-OH and/or neighboring phosphate groups).
- Showed a clear concentration dependency of shear bond strength on applied 10-MDP, with a measurable bonding threshold (no effect at ≤1 ppb; significant gain at higher concentrations).
- Proposed three interaction models (hydrogen bonding via P=O···Zr-OH; ionic bonding via P-O⁻↔Zr; and combined ionic + hydrogen bonding) consistent with the deconvoluted ³¹P resonances.
- Showed that only ONE P-OH group of 10-MDP interacts with zirconia (consistent with 10-MDP's two pKa values, 2.2 and 7.0, and overall near-neutral primer pH), while the other OH can hydrogen-bond to a neighboring 10-MDP molecule — the first report of inter-monomer hydrogen bonding between adsorbed 10-MDP phosphate groups.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **SBS specimens:** Sintered Y-TZP plates (10×10×3 mm) polished with 15-μm diamond (no sandblast on the surface of interest, to isolate the chemical effect of 10-MDP), washed (distilled water + acetone, ultrasonic), then UV-cleaned with a Xe₂ excimer lamp (λ=172 nm). Each plate (n=10/group) dipped 1 h in acetone solutions of 10-MDP ranging 0.1 ppb → 1 wt%; controls received no 10-MDP. Excess monomer washed off (acetone ×2), nitrogen-dried. Sandblasted zirconia bars luted with Clearfil Esthetic Cement (Kuraray Noritake), cured 1 min (G-Light Prima II Plus); ~25-μm cement film; stored 24 h/37 °C in water.
- **SBS test:** Instron 5565, crosshead 0.5 mm/min; failure mode classified (cohesive/adhesive/mixed) under light microscope (40×). Statistics: one-way ANOVA + Tukey, α=0.05.
- **NMR specimens:** 2 g zirconia nanopowder (3 mol% yttria-stabilized, grain ≤100 nm, surface area 10–25 m²/g) mixed with 50 mL of 25 wt% 10-MDP/acetone (ultrasonic 20 min, then 40 min static); coated particles separated by centrifugation and thoroughly acetone-washed to remove unbound monomer. Untreated zirconia powder as comparison.
- **NMR methodology:** Agilent DD2 500 MHz; 3.2-mm zirconia rotor, MAS at 15 kHz. ³¹P direct-polarization MAS at 202.3 MHz (pulse 2.8 μs/π/2, recycle 90 s, ~790 scans, ref. NH₄H₂PO₄ 1.0 ppm vs 85% H₃PO₄); ¹H MAS at 499.8 MHz (pulse 1.15 μs/π/4, recycle 120 s, ~4 scans, ref. adamantane 1.91 ppm vs TMS). 2D ¹H→³¹P HETCOR via cross-polarization (contact 3 ms, recycle 5 s, 96 scans/increment, 64 t₁ slices, 15 kHz MAS). Each analysis run twice for repeatability.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **SBS concentration dependency:** No difference between control (no 10-MDP) and 0.1-ppb or 1-ppb 10-MDP; higher concentrations gave significantly higher SBS with clear concentration dependency. Minimum effective threshold ≈ above 1 ppb (i.e., ~1 ppb and below = no benefit).
- **Failure mode:** 100% adhesive failure for 10-MDP ≤1 ppm; at ≥10 ppm only 50% adhesive, the other 50% mixed (cohesive+adhesive) — indicating stronger interfacial bonding at higher 10-MDP.
- **¹H MAS NMR:** Broad resonance at 5.0 ppm = Zr-OH + adsorbed surface water; six sharp resonances (1.4, 1.6, 1.9, 4.1, 5.5, 6.0 ppm) = methacryloyloxy groups of 10-MDP; broad ~6 ppm peak = mixed OH (surface hydrated layer + P-OH of 10-MDP).
- **2D ¹H→³¹P HETCOR:** Slices at −2.8 and −7.1 ppm (³¹P) show ¹H resonances for P-OH (8–11 ppm), Zr-OH (6.8 ppm), and CH₂-O-P of methacryloyloxy (4.0 ppm) — placing P-OH in close proximity to surface Zr-OH (adsorption via H-bonding and/or ionic interaction).
- **³¹P deconvolution (adsorbed 10-MDP):** four peaks at −13.7, −7.7, −2.6, 0.3 ppm.
  - 0.3 ppm (10.0%) = physisorbed / hydrogen-bonded 10-MDP monomer, NO P-OH deprotonation.
  - −13.7 ppm (21.4%) = physisorbed 10-MDP dimer, NO deprotonation.
  - −7.7 ppm (48.4%) and −2.6 ppm (20.2%) = chemisorbed 10-MDP monomer (the strong peaks; total ~68.6% chemisorbed monomer).
- **Free-monomer reference (³¹P in acetone):** sharp peaks at 0.93 ppm (monomer) and −13.7 ppm (dimer).
- **Mechanism:** P=O oxo group and one non-deprotonated P-OH engage in hydrogen bonding with zirconia or neighboring phosphates; the other P-OH can deprotonate (P-O⁻) and ionically bond to partial-positive Zr. The two distinct shifts (−7.7, −2.6 ppm) reflect two different P-OH chemical states. → 10-MDP–zirconia bonding is ionic **and** hydrogen bonding; ionic-only hypothesis rejected.
- 10-MDP pKa₁ = 2.2, pKa₂ = 7.0; near-neutral overall primer pH means typically only one P-OH interacts with zirconia, the other with a neighboring 10-MDP.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro mechanistic study only — no thermocycling/aging endpoint and no long-term clinical durability data in this study (durability inferred from cited literature).
- NMR used zirconia *nanopowder* (high surface area) with a high 10-MDP concentration (25 wt%) to maximize signal; SBS used clinically relevant polished plates — the two arms are not on identical surfaces, so direct quantitative transfer of bonding fractions to clinical surfaces is indirect.
- High 10-MDP loading promotes inter-monomer (10-MDP–10-MDP) hydrogen bonding/dimerization, which may not contribute to substrate bonding; authors note 10-MDP concentration should be optimized in future work.
- Three interaction models proposed but the precise stoichiometry/coordination (single- vs double-coordinate, cf. Xie's simulation) at clinical surfaces remains to be confirmed.

## 7. Glossary

- **10-MDP** — 10-methacryloyloxy-decyl-dihydrogen-phosphate; an acidic functional monomer with a polymerizable methacrylate, a 10-carbon hydrophobic spacer, and a dihydrogen-phosphate group that bonds to metal oxides and hydroxyapatite.
- **Y-TZP** — yttria-stabilized tetragonal zirconia polycrystal; the high-toughness dental zirconia.
- **MAS NMR** — magic-angle-spinning nuclear magnetic resonance; solid-state NMR that narrows lines by spinning the sample at 54.74° to the magnetic field.
- **HETCOR** — heteronuclear correlation NMR; 2D experiment correlating two nuclei (here ¹H and ³¹P) through-space, revealing which protons are near which phosphorus.
- **Chemisorption vs physisorption** — chemical (bond-forming) adsorption vs weak (van der Waals / physical) adsorption.
- **Ionic bonding (here)** — electrostatic interaction between deprotonated phosphate (P-O⁻) and partially positive Zr at the oxide surface.
- **Hydrogen bonding (here)** — P=O···Zr-OH or P-OH···Zr-OH (and inter-monomer P-OH···O=P) interactions.
- **pKa** — acid dissociation constant; 10-MDP has two (2.2 and 7.0) for its two phosphate protons.
- **SBS** — shear bond strength.
- **ppb / ppm** — parts per billion / parts per million (concentration).
