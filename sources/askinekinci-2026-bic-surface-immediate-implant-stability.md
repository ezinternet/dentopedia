## Why Ingested

기존 [[immediate-implant/witoonkitvanich-2025-immediate-implant-stability-maxilla-mandible]]가 즉시식립 ISQ 안정성을 부위(상악/하악)로 비교했다면, 본 RCT는 동일 macro-design 임플란트에서 *fresh extraction (graft/no-graft) vs healed* 부위 간 primary stability와 가상 BIC의 상관을 직접 측정한다. 즉시식립의 잔존 gap에 graft를 채우는 것이 초기 기계적 안정성에 기여하는지, 그리고 native bone contact(BIC)가 ISQ를 얼마나 예측하는지에 대한 근거를 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

Pilot RCT (n=28; 3 groups) showing immediate placement with vs without grafting yields similar primary stability, healed sites are significantly higher (ISQ/torque P=0.014/0.007), and virtual BIC correlates with early ISQ only in the no-graft immediate group (r=0.62/0.58).

## 한줄요약

파일럿 무작위대조시험 (Randomized Controlled Trial, RCT, n=28, 3군): 즉시식립 시 골이식 유무는 초기 안정성에 차이가 없고 (치유부 골 부위가 유의하게 높음, P=0.014/0.007), 가상 골-임플란트 접촉면적 (virtual Bone-Implant Contact surface, BIC)은 비이식 즉시식립군에서만 초기 임플란트 안정성 지수 (Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ)와 상관 (r=0.62/0.58).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Proposes a **non-invasive digital workflow to estimate virtual bone-implant contact surface area (BIC)** under clinical conditions: preoperative CBCT segmentation (Slicer 5.4.0) overlaid on postoperative intraoral scan (3Shape Trios) + scanbody + implant STL, surface areas computed in Meshmixer — *no surgical guide required*, sidestepping CBCT metal-artifact distortion around the placed implant.
- Directly compares primary stability across three clinically distinct conditions with identical implant macro-design/surface: immediate + graft (Group I), immediate no graft (Group II), healed site (Group III).
- Shows grafting the residual peri-implant gap does **not** improve early mechanical stability of immediate implants vs no graft.
- Demonstrates that virtual BIC predicts early ISQ/torque **only when no graft is present** — i.e., BIC reflects native apical bone engagement; graft placement decouples ISQ from native BIC.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Population: ≥18 y, single missing upper premolar/molar requiring extraction (Groups I/II) or healed edentulous site ≥6 mo (Group III); ≥4 mm apical bone; intact socket walls; good hygiene; favorable occlusion. Excluded: systemic contraindications, untreated periodontitis, acute infection, heavy smokers.
- Allocation: sequentially numbered opaque sealed envelopes; group revealed after extraction (Groups I/II); analysts blinded.
- Intervention: freehand placement, identical manufacturer drilling sequence, drill extended 2–3 mm into apical bone for primary stability; aggressive-thread tapered screw-type Ti implant with SLA surface (DXL GmbH). Insertion 40 Ncm / 20 rpm then manual torque ratchet. Group I gap filled with bovine xenograft (Cerabone 0.5–1.0 mm) + absorbable collagen membrane; Group II gap left ungrafted; Group III standard protocol.
- Outcomes: BIC, virtual non-contact surface (BINC), insertion torque (manual ratchet at final position), ISQ (Osstell RFA, ≥3 repeats, highest value). ISQ at intraop, 60 d (healing-cap placement), 90 d (before loading). Intraoral scan with scanbodies at 3 mo.
- Statistics: Shapiro–Wilk normality; unpaired t / Mann–Whitney (2-group); one-way ANOVA / Friedman (3-group) with Dunn post hoc; Pearson/Spearman correlation (0.50–1.00 = strong). Sample size G-Power (alpha 0.05, effect size 0.82, power 95%) → 27, expanded to 30.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Final n=28 (2 lost to follow-up, one each Groups I/II). No complications.
- Insertion torque (Ncm): GI 32.0±4.2, GII 36.3±7.1, GIII 41.1±6.8 — GIII > GI **P=0.007**; GI vs GII NS.
- Intraoperative ISQ: GI 65.0±7.6, GII 62.0±10.0, GIII 71.6±6.2 — GIII > GII **P=0.014**; GI vs GII NS.
- BIC (mm²): GI 206.2±30.3, GII 206.8±48.2, GIII 302.2±64.5 — GIII > GI (P=0.003) and > GII (P=0.002); GI vs GII NS. BINC: GI 101.0±24.6, GII 106.6±22.9, GIII NA.
- BIC correlations (Group II only significant): insertion torque r=0.66 (P=0.02); intraoperative ISQ r=0.62 (P=0.03); **ISQ-60 r=0.58 (P=0.05)**. NS in Groups I and III for all.
- ISQ-90 NS within every subgroup; pooled across all implants BIC–ISQ90 moderate r=0.44 (P=0.01).
- Stability increased over time in all groups (secondary stability / osseointegration), independent of grafting.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Pilot, small per-group n (~9–10) → subgroup correlations underpowered; ISQ-90 subgroup signals likely diluted.
- Short follow-up: 60/90 d are early healing — osseointegration incomplete; slow-resorbing xenograft may show higher late ISQ not captured here.
- Narrow scenario: single missing posterior maxilla tooth, intact walls; bone density (a key osseointegration determinant) not directly measured.
- Virtual BIC is a geometric estimate, **not** a substitute for histologic bone-to-implant contact; graft material in the gap is not reflected in the CBCT-derived BIC by design.
- Future: longer follow-up; external validation of the digital BIC workflow.

## 6. Related Work

- Ko et al. — BIC significantly affected ISQ in immediate placement with grafting.
- Jun et al. — graft placement increased ISQ, with greater gain when baseline ISQ ≤65.
- Prior literature: bony gaps <2 mm may not heal spontaneously; CBCT–intraoral-scan superimposition for implant position determination.

## 7. Glossary

- **BIC (virtual Bone-Implant Contact surface)**: geometrically estimated area (mm²) of implant surface in contact with native alveolar bone, derived here from CBCT + IOS registration (not histologic BIC).
- **BINC (virtual Bone-Implant Non-Contact surface)**: implant outer-surface area not in contact with native bone (peri-implant gap region).
- **IIS (Implant Inner Surface)**: inner threaded surface of the implant STL, excluded from contact analysis.
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: 1–100 RFA-derived stability scale (Osstell).
- **Insertion torque**: rotational resistance (Ncm) at final seating, a primary-stability surrogate.
