## One-line Summary
Network meta-analysis of 16 RCTs (2158 patients) comparing antibiotic regimens for prevention of dry socket and SSI after lower third molar extraction; antibiotics reduce both complications but NNT is high (25 for dry socket, 18 for SSI), arguing against routine use.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: International Journal of Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery 2024;53:57–67
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.ijom.2023.08.001
- **Accepted**: August 2, 2023; Available online: August 21, 2023
- **Study type**: Systematic review with pairwise and network meta-analysis
- **Institution**: School of Medicine and Health Sciences, Universitat de Barcelona / IDIBELL

## 2. Key Contributions
- First network meta-analysis comparing multiple antibiotic regimens (amoxicillin ± clavulanic acid, metronidazole, azithromycin, clindamycin) for dry socket and SSI prevention
- Establishes NNT values: 25 for dry socket, 18 for SSI — both unfavorably high
- Concludes routine antibiotic prophylaxis is NOT justified for healthy patients undergoing lower third molar extraction
- Addresses both direct (pairwise) and indirect (network) comparisons

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Systematic review of randomized clinical trials
- 16 articles included; 2158 patients; 2428 lower third molars
- Antibiotics analyzed: amoxicillin (with and without clavulanic acid), metronidazole, azithromycin, clindamycin
- Pairwise meta-analysis for direct comparisons
- Network meta-analysis for indirect comparisons (treatments not directly compared in RCTs)
- Primary outcomes: dry socket (alveolar osteitis), surgical site infection (SSI)
- Secondary outcome: antibiotic-related adverse events

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Dry socket**: Pooled results favor antibiotic use; NNT = 25
- **Surgical site infection**: Pooled results favor antibiotic use; NNT = 18
- **Background incidence**: SSI 1–13% in all third molar extractions; dry socket ~0.5–5% of all extractions
- **Most common antibiotic**: Amoxicillin alone or with clavulanic acid
- **Conclusion**: Despite statistical significance, high NNT values mean clinicians should evaluate individual risk before prescribing
- Antibiotic-related adverse events: allergic reactions, GI effects (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea), microbiota disruption, resistance risk, rare hematological complications

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- 16 RCTs only; limited data on some antibiotic comparisons in network
- Heterogeneity in antibiotic dosing, timing, and duration across trials
- No studies compare multiple antibiotic regimens head-to-head for both outcomes
- Individual patient risk stratification needed — not addressed by current evidence

## 6. Related Work
- 2016 systematic review and meta-analysis showing unfavorable cost-benefit ratio for indiscriminate antibiotic use
- Bailey et al. 2020 Cochrane review on surgical techniques for mandibular wisdom teeth
- Growing antimicrobial stewardship movement discouraging routine prophylaxis in low-risk patients

## 7. Glossary
- **NNT (Number Needed to Treat)**: Number of patients who must receive treatment for one to benefit; NNT=25 for dry socket means 25 patients treated to prevent 1 dry socket
- **Dry socket (alveolar osteitis)**: Painful postoperative complication; premature blood clot loss; incidence ~0.5–5%
- **SSI (Surgical Site Infection)**: Postoperative wound infection; incidence 1–13% after third molar extraction
- **Network meta-analysis**: Statistical method allowing comparison of multiple treatments including those not directly compared in RCTs
- **Amoxicillin-clavulanic acid**: Beta-lactam antibiotic with beta-lactamase inhibitor; most commonly used for third molar prophylaxis
- **Antimicrobial stewardship**: Practice of optimizing antibiotic use to reduce resistance and adverse effects
