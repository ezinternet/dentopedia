## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Frontiers in Dental Medicine 2023, 4:1281337
- **DOI**: 10.3389/fdmed.2023.1281337
- **Published**: 14 November 2023
- **Study type**: Original research (prospective clinical; non-randomized controlled; microbiology sub-study of parent RCT)
- **Institution**: KU Leuven & University Hospitals Leuven (Belgium); Ghent University

## 2. Key Contributions
- Quantifies bacterial load reduction at 4 time points during REP disinfection protocol (2 sessions)
- Identifies specific bacterial species in necrotic immature permanent teeth
- Evaluates whether LPRF (leukocyte and platelet-rich fibrin) as scaffold affects bacterial clearance and clinical/radiographic outcomes
- Key finding: LPRF is NOT recommended in REPs — associated with retreatment and persistent apical periodontitis

## 3. Methodology
- **Design**: Non-randomized controlled clinical study; microbiology sub-study of parent cohort (n=29 total; n=14 with microbiology samples)
- **Sampling**: 4 microbiological samples (S1–S4) at two treatment sessions:
  - S1: before first irrigation (1.5% NaOCl + saline)
  - S2: after first irrigation
  - S3: before second-session rinse (17% EDTA)
  - S4: after EDTA rinse
- **Detection**: qPCR with species-specific primers
- **Outcomes**: bacterial load (total and species); clinical/radiographic success at 3-year recall

## 4. Key Results
| Species | Prevalence |
|---|---|
| Fusobacterium nucleatum | 100% (14/14) |
| Treponema denticola | 100% (14/14) |
| Enterococcus faecalis | 100% (14/14) |
| Prevotella intermedia | 42.9% (6/14) |
| Tannerella forsythia | 42.9% (6/14) |
| Parvimonas micra | 28.6% (4/14) |
| Actinomyces naeslundii | 14.3% (2/14) |
| Filifactor alocis / P. endodontalis / P. gingivalis | 7.1% each (1/14) |

- Total bacterial load significantly reduced after Session 1 (S1→S2); maintained through Session 2
- LPRF group: 2/14 required retreatment (early post-treatment flare-up); 2 others showed persistent apical periodontitis at 3-year recall
- Control group: all cases clinically asymptomatic; periapices healed radiographically at last recall
- Conclusion: standard disinfection (1.5% NaOCl + 17% EDTA) effective; LPRF scaffold should NOT be used in REPs

## 5. Limitations
- Small sample (n=14 with microbiology); no statistical correlation between species and outcomes achievable
- Non-randomized design; allocation bias possible
- Single center; species panel fixed by available qPCR primers

## 7. Glossary
- **REP**: Regenerative Endodontic Procedure
- **LPRF (Leukocyte and Platelet-Rich Fibrin)**: Autologous scaffold made from centrifuged patient blood; contains growth factors; used in REPs as alternative to blood clot — this study finds it harmful
- **qPCR (Quantitative PCR)**: Species-specific quantification of bacterial DNA; more sensitive than culture
- **E. faecalis**: Resistant gram-positive; common REP root canal pathogen; significant because it resists standard irrigation
