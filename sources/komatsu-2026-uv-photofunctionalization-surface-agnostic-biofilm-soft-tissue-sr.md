## Why Ingested

This is the newest (2026) and broadest UV photofunctionalization systematic review, reframing the technology as a **surface-agnostic / topography-preserving** strategy for peri-implant health — a conceptual upgrade over the existing narrative-review and single-interface evidence we hold. It extends the bacterial/biofilm evidence in [[implants/surface/dorigatti-de-avila-2015-uv-photofunctionalization-bacterial-biofilm]], reinforces and elevates (narrative → SR) the tri-interface review [[implants/surface/park-2025-uv-photofunctionalization-osseointegration-soft-tissue]], and operationalizes the aging/topography framing of [[implants/surface/ogawa-2025-3d-theory-osseointegration-material-topography]]. It is a candidate anchor for [[overviews/plasma-surface-treatment-dental-implants]]. Same group as our Ogawa/Park UCLA pages.

## One-line Summary
Systematic review with qualitative synthesis (34 studies: 9 bacterial/biofilm + 13 soft-tissue + 13 clinical) framing UV photofunctionalization as a surface-agnostic, topography-preserving "positive-sum" reactivation technology — most consistently reducing early bacterial attachment/biofilm and enhancing fibroblast/epithelial sealing on Ti (and emerging on zirconia), with the strongest clinical signal being accelerated implant stability development.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Reframes UV photofunctionalization (UV-PF) as a **surface-agnostic physicochemical reactivation technology**: it restores high surface energy and favorable surface chemistry *without altering the underlying surface architecture* (topography-preserving).
- **"Positive-sum" / interface-first** thesis: conventional surface design forces trade-offs (micro-roughness boosts osteoconductivity but raises plaque-retention risk; smooth transmucosal surfaces discourage bacteria but with uncertain soft-tissue integration). UV-PF sidesteps the trade-off by improving interfacial biology independent of topography.
- First UV-PF SR to jointly evaluate three peri-implant-health domains — bacterial/biofilm, soft-tissue sealing, human clinical — across both **titanium and zirconia**.
- Clinical translation framing: enables **zone-specific topography selection** (smooth transmucosal + rough endosteal) while maximizing soft-tissue affinity and suppressing early colonization.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Design: **systematic review with qualitative (narrative) synthesis** (confidence: `sr`). No meta-analysis (heterogeneity precluded pooling).
- Search/screening/full-text evaluation yielded **34 articles**: **9 bacterial/biofilm**, **13 soft-tissue** (1 overlapping with the bacterial set), and **13 clinical** studies.
- Materials covered: titanium and zirconia surfaces.
- Synthesis attended explicitly to **protocol heterogeneity** dimensions: UV wavelength band, exposure duration, device configuration, and material/surface type.
- Conceptual frame: the biologic **"race to the surface"** among osteogenic cells, peri-implant soft-tissue cells, and bacterial pathogens; UV-PF removes storage-acquired hydrocarbons ("biological aging") to convert surfaces to a high-energy, superhydrophilic state chairside.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
*(abstract-level; no pooled effect estimates reported in abstract.)*

| Outcome domain | Studies | Synthesis signal |
|---|---|---|
| Bacterial / biofilm | 9 | UV-PF **most consistently reduced early bacterial attachment and/or early biofilm accumulation** across several Ti topographies → early anti-adhesive, biofilm-suppressive phenotype. |
| Soft-tissue sealing | 13 (1 shared) | Generally **enhanced fibroblast/epithelial attachment, spreading, and sealing-relevant functional behaviors** on both Ti and zirconia; optimal underlying topography for soft-tissue integration **remains unresolved**. |
| Human clinical | 13 | Most consistent signal = **accelerated and enhanced implant stability development**; selected studies suggested favorable peri-implant soft-tissue parameters and/or crestal bone maintenance, but outcomes were **variable**. |

- **Surface-agnostic / positive-sum concept**: evidence strongest for **titanium**; zirconia support is **emerging**, primarily from soft-tissue and interface-focused models.
- The clinical evidence base most strongly supports **accelerated osseointegration/stability development** rather than the harder peri-implant-health endpoints.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- **Abstract-only ingest** — full text not retrieved; granular per-study data, risk-of-bias, and quantitative ranges unavailable here.
- Clinical outcomes **variable**, limited by heterogeneity in UV protocols, surface systems, endpoints, and follow-up duration.
- **No meta-analysis** (qualitative synthesis only).
- Optimal topography for soft-tissue integration unresolved; zirconia clinical evidence sparse.
- Future need: longitudinal studies with **standardized peri-implant health, microbiologic, and mucosal inflammatory endpoints**.

## 7. Glossary
- **UV photofunctionalization (UV-PF)**: chairside UV treatment that removes storage-acquired hydrocarbons ("biological aging") and converts the implant surface to a high-energy, superhydrophilic state without changing topography.
- **Surface-agnostic / topography-preserving**: enhancement acting on surface chemistry/energy independent of (and without altering) the surface architecture.
- **Positive-sum (interface-first) strategy**: improving multiple peri-implant objectives (bone, soft tissue, anti-biofilm) simultaneously rather than trading one against another.
- **Race to the surface**: competition among osteogenic cells, soft-tissue cells, and bacteria for early dominance at the implant-tissue interface.
- **Biological aging**: time-dependent hydrocarbon accumulation and loss of surface energy on stored titanium.
- **Zone-specific topography**: clinically selecting smooth transmucosal vs rough endosteal surfaces, with UV-PF maximizing biology in each zone.
