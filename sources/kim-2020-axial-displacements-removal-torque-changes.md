## Why Ingested

User request: papers on the implant abutment-screw **settling effect**. This in-vitro study directly quantifies settling (axial displacement) and post-loading removal torque across 5 connection geometries, mechanistically extending [[prosthetic-materials/velez-2020-implant-connection-abutment-design-screw]] (insertion-torque vs misfit) and [[prosthetic-materials/ren-2024-morse-taper-abutment-subsidence-locking-force]] (Morse-taper subsidence), and complementing the connection-design comparison in [[implants/seong-2016-external-internal-implant-connection-system]]. It supplies the "settling effect" mechanism that underlies screw-loosening risk discussed in [[prosthetic-materials/khurshid-2025-screw-vs-cement-crown-complications]].

## One-line Summary

In-vitro static-loading study (5 implant-abutment connection designs, n=10 each, 700 N vertical load) showing settling (axial displacement) increases with tapered interfaces (external butt-joint lowest 0.8 µm, internal 8° Morse-taper octagon highest 25.6 µm), and that two-piece abutments lose removal torque (preload) from settling while one-piece abutments gain it via friction.

## 한줄요약

In-vitro 정적하중 연구(5종 임플란트-지대주 연결, 군당 n=10, 700 N 수직하중): settling(축방향 변위)은 테이퍼 계면일수록 커지고(외부 버트조인트 0.8 µm 최소 → 내부 8° Morse-taper 옥타곤 25.6 µm 최대), 2-piece 지대주는 settling으로 제거토크(preload)가 감소하나 1-piece 지대주는 마찰로 오히려 증가.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies the **settling effect as axial displacement (µm)** under a single clinically realistic 700 N static vertical load (max posterior bite force), isolating static loading from cyclic loading.
- Directly couples settling magnitude to **post-loading removal torque (preload retention)** across 5 distinct macro-geometries from one manufacturer (Osstem), controlling for fabrication source.
- Demonstrates a **geometry gradient**: flat external butt-joint settles least; tapered internal connections settle more; steeper 8° Morse-taper (octagon) settles more than 11° (hexagon).
- Shows an **abutment-type divergence**: two-piece (tension/screw-joint) abutments LOSE removal torque after settling; one-piece (friction-retained) abutments GAIN removal torque — opposite directions from the same settling phenomenon.
- Provides a clinical caution that settling-driven preload loss in molar regions can cause **occlusal lowering** and screw loosening after delivery.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: In-vitro, 5 groups × n=10 = 50 implant-abutment assemblies, Osstem systems.
  - **Ext**: external butt-joint + cemented two-piece abutment
  - **Int-H2**: internal hexagon + transfer (two-piece) abutment
  - **Int-H1**: internal hexagon + rigid (one-piece) abutment
  - **Int-O2**: internal octagon (8° Morse taper) + Comocta (two-piece) abutment
  - **Int-O1**: internal octagon (8° Morse taper) + solid (one-piece) abutment
- **Tightening protocol**: 30 Ncm applied twice at 10-min interval; initial removal torque measured 10 min after second tightening; re-secured to 30 Ncm.
- **Loading**: 700 N vertical static load (Instron 8841 universal testing machine, 1 mm/min), corresponding to maximum posterior biting force.
- **Settling measurement**: change in total implant-abutment assembly length before vs after loading, electronic digital micrometer (1 µm resolution).
- **Outcome**: post-loading removal torque vs initial removal torque (digital torque gauge MGT12E).
- **Statistics**: one-way ANOVA + Tukey HSD across groups; paired t-test for initial vs post-loading removal torque within each group; α = 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Settling values (axial displacement) after 700 N (mean ± SD):**
- Ext: 0.8 ± 0.45 µm
- Int-H1: 10.2 ± 0.84 µm
- Int-H2: 11.2 ± 0.84 µm
- Int-O2: 19.2 ± 4.21 µm
- Int-O1: 25.6 ± 2.97 µm
- Order: **Ext < Int-H1, Int-H2 < Int-O2 < Int-O1** (α = 0.05)

**Post-loading removal torque (mean ± SD), initial torque 30 Ncm:**
- Int-O1: 39.64 ± 4.28 Ncm (↑ increased vs initial)
- Int-H1: 36.38 ± 6.25 Ncm (↑ increased)
- Ext: 22.78 ± 0.40 Ncm (↓ small significant decrease)
- Int-H2: 11.62 ± 0.56 Ncm (↓ decreased)
- Int-O2: 1.14 ± 0.40 Ncm (↓ near-total loss; screw nearly came loose)
- Order: **Int-O2 < Int-H2 < Ext < Int-H1, Int-O1**

**Mechanistic split**: one-piece internal abutments (Int-H1, Int-O1) → removal torque ↑ (friction-dominant retention); two-piece internal abutments (Int-H2, Int-O2) → removal torque ↓ (tension/screw-joint, preload lost to settling). The steeper 8° Morse-taper octagon settles most.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Static loading only (single 700 N), not cyclic fatigue — though authors note pattern paralleled their prior cyclic study.
- Single manufacturer (Osstem); generalizability to other connection systems limited.
- Crown simulated with a friction-seated metal cap (no cement); in the original internal-octagon design the crown margin seated on the fixture (no vertical stop space), possibly **overestimating** settling values in those groups.
- Did not use the direct elongated-screw method (Haack et al.) to measure preload change directly; removal torque is an indirect proxy.
- Small n per group; no clinical correlation.

## 7. Glossary

- **Settling effect (embedment relaxation)**: micro-flattening of contacting metal asperities at the screw/abutment/implant interface after loading, shortening the assembly and reducing screw preload.
- **Preload**: the clamping tension stored in the abutment screw after tightening; lost preload → screw loosening risk.
- **Removal (reverse) torque**: torque needed to loosen the screw; used here as an indirect index of retained preload.
- **One-piece (solid/rigid) abutment**: abutment + screw as one body — retention is friction-dominant.
- **Two-piece abutment**: separate abutment seated by a discrete screw — retention is tension (screw-joint) dominant.
- **Morse taper**: conical internal connection; steeper cone angle (8° here vs 11°) → more frictional/wedge locking and greater axial settling.
