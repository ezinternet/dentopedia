## Why Ingested

The wiki's cracked-tooth restoration evidence ([[wiki/cracked-tooth/zhang-2024-cracked-teeth-treatment-outcomes-sr-ma]], [[wiki/cracked-tooth/liu-2025-restorative-design-materials-stress-cracked-teeth-fea]]) leans toward cuspal-coverage/full-crown protection of post-endodontic cracked teeth, but lacks a head-to-head RCT of full crown vs. a more conservative porcelain inlay. This prospective RCT (Wang 2025, n=106) directly compares the two restorations on clinical success, pain, gingival health, and masticatory function, adding a primary-trial data point to the treatment-outcomes sibling page.

## One-line Summary

Prospective RCT (n=106, post-RCT cracked teeth): porcelain inlay outperformed full crown on success rate, day-1/3/7 VAS pain, 6-month gingival indices, masticatory efficiency, and bite force (all P<0.05). (Abstract-only; Chinese-language study.)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides a direct, randomized comparison of **full crown vs. porcelain inlay** for cracked teeth after root canal treatment — a comparison underrepresented in the wiki's existing cracked-tooth evidence.
- Reports the **conservative porcelain inlay** as clinically superior to the full crown across success, pain, gingival, and functional endpoints, challenging the default assumption that maximal cuspal coverage (full crown) is the optimal post-endodontic restoration for all cracked teeth.
- Adds quantitative masticatory-function endpoints (chewing efficiency %, bite force in lbs) tracked over 6 months.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Prospective study, described as randomized by random-number-table allocation.
- **Population**: 106 patients with cracked teeth who underwent root canal treatment, December 2020–December 2023.
- **Groups**: Experimental group (porcelain inlay) n=53; Control group (full crown) n=53.
- **Endpoints**:
  - Clinical effect ("good rate") and total incidence of adverse reactions.
  - Pain by VAS at baseline and post-treatment day 1, 3, 7.
  - Gingival condition: plaque index (PLI), gingival index (GI), gingival bleeding index (BI), periodontal pocket depth (PD) at baseline and 6-month follow-up.
  - Masticatory ability: chewing efficiency and bite force at baseline and 1, 3, 6 months.
- **Analysis**: SPSS 25.0; significance at P<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Success / adverse reactions**: Experimental (porcelain inlay) had a higher good rate and lower total incidence of adverse reactions vs. control (full crown) (P<0.05).
- **VAS pain (day 1 / 3 / 7)**: Experimental 4.43±0.68 / 3.15±0.63 / 2.04±0.51 vs. Control 4.86±0.57 / 3.81±0.67 / 2.86±0.52 — experimental significantly lower at all timepoints, both declining (P<0.05).
- **Gingival indices at 6 months**: GI, PLI, BI, PD all significantly lower in experimental than control (P<0.05).
- **Masticatory efficiency (1 / 3 / 6 mo)**: Experimental 70.25±5.57% / 85.36±6.74% / 92.57±6.35% vs. Control 62.35±5.44% / 73.57±6.32% / 84.26±6.22% (P<0.05).
- **Bite force (1 / 3 / 6 mo, lbs)**: Experimental 112.52±9.67 / 130.52±11.58 / 143.54±13.40 vs. Control 98.53±9.47 / 113.25±10.54 / 125.36±12.46 (P<0.05).
- **Conclusion**: Porcelain inlay is clinically superior to full crown for cracked teeth after root canal treatment — better chewing ability, better gingival condition, and lower adverse-reaction incidence.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Abstract-only**: Full text not retrieved; methods detail (crack classification, inclusion/exclusion, tooth type distribution, blinding, allocation concealment, material specifics, longer-term survival) cannot be verified from the abstract.
- **Randomization**: Described as random-number-table allocation but allocation concealment and blinding are not documented in the abstract; risk of bias unknown.
- **Single-center, single-language (Chinese) study**; generalizability and selection of cases (which cracked teeth received an inlay vs. crown beyond randomization) unclear.
- **Follow-up** is relatively short (6 months for gingival/functional endpoints); cracked-tooth restorations require multi-year survival data, absent here.
- **Surprising direction**: superiority of the more conservative restoration warrants confirmation, since broader cracked-tooth literature emphasizes cuspal coverage; case-mix (extent of crack) may drive the result.

## 7. Glossary

- **Cracked tooth (after RCT)**: a tooth with an incomplete fracture managed with root canal treatment, then definitively restored.
- **Full crown (total crown)**: complete cuspal-coverage restoration encasing the clinical crown.
- **Porcelain inlay**: an intracoronal ceramic restoration replacing lost tooth structure without full cuspal coverage (more conservative than a crown).
- **VAS**: visual analogue scale for pain (0–10).
- **PLI / GI / BI / PD**: plaque index / gingival index / gingival bleeding index / periodontal pocket depth — gingival/periodontal status measures.
- **Masticatory efficiency**: percentage measure of chewing performance.
- **Bite force**: occlusal force, here reported in pounds (lbs).
