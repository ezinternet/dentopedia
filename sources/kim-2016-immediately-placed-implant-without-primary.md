## One-line Summary
Case report and literature review of successfully osseointegrated implants placed immediately in extraction sockets despite lack of primary stability.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Clinical case report with literature review
- **Institution**: Inje University Sanggye Paik Hospital, Department of Oral & Maxillofacial Surgery
- **Journal**: Journal of Dental Implant Research
- **Volume**: 35, No. 1
- **Year**: 2016
- **Pages**: 17-21
- **Clinical Scenario**: Immediate implant placement where mechanical stability difficult to achieve

## 2. Key Contributions
- Challenges paradigm that primary stability absolutely required for successful osseointegration
- Documents successful healing in immediate placement without rigid primary stability
- Discusses biological factors that can compensate for mechanical deficiency
- Provides clinical management strategy for compromised extraction socket situations
- Expands treatment options when optimal primary stability cannot be achieved

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Case Presentation**: Single or multiple clinical cases
- **Clinical Variables**:
  - Extraction socket characteristics (bone volume, cortical plate integrity)
  - Implant design features (diameter, length, thread pitch)
  - Insertion torque values
  - Stability assessment methods
- **Follow-up Evaluation**:
  - Clinical healing progression
  - Radiographic osseointegration evidence
  - Functional outcomes
  - Implant survival confirmation
- **Literature Review**: Supporting evidence from immediate implant research

## 4. Key Results and Findings
- Implants placed immediately after extraction achieve osseointegration despite low primary stability
- Healing timeline may extend compared to conventional delayed placement
- Bone remodeling and resorption patterns documented
- Clinical success achieved through modified loading protocols (delayed loading)
- Radiographic evidence confirms bone apposition and implant integration

## 5. Clinical Implications
- Immediate placement can be attempted even when primary stability suboptimal
- Modified treatment approach required: delayed loading protocol essential
- Careful case selection critical (avoid severe compromising factors)
- Surgical technique modifications may enhance soft tissue adaptation
- Shorter treatment timeline benefits despite extended implant healing phase
- Patient satisfaction remains high due to esthetic and functional outcomes

## 6. Limitations and Future Work
- Small sample size in single case report
- Long-term follow-up outcomes require extended observation
- Biological response variables not fully controlled in clinical setting
- Need for prospective studies comparing immediate placement with/without primary stability
- Definition and measurement of "primary stability" varies—inconsistent terminology

## 7. Glossary
- **Immediate implant placement (즉시 식립)**: Implant insertion at time of tooth extraction
- **Primary stability (초기고정)**: Mechanical stability from implant-bone friction at insertion
- **Extraction socket (발치와)**: Alveolar bone defect following tooth removal
- **Osseointegration (골유착)**: Direct bone-implant contact formation
- **Delayed loading**: Implant loading deferred until biological integration mature
