## Why Ingested

[[veneers/ali-2023-conventional-minimally-invasive-veneers-sr]]가 최소삭제 vs 전통적 준비의 *임상 결과*를 비교하지만, "얼마나·어디를 삭제할지"를 결정하는 구체적 의사결정 규칙은 위키에 부재했다. 본 case report(Coachman 2014)는 Gürel의 APT 철학을 한 단계 정량화한 "Do the Math"(P = LT − EV) 기법을 제시하여, 목표 색조 변화에 필요한 정확한 삭제량·위치를 수식으로 산출하는 근거를 추가한다.

## One-line Summary

Case report introducing the "Do the Math" technique — an additive diagnostic wax-up transferred intraorally via an aesthetic pre-evaluative temporary (APT), combined with a mathematical formula (P = LT − EV) computed per tooth region to indicate the exact amount and location of reduction needed to achieve the desired final color and shape, minimizing over- or under-preparation.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Frames veneer preparation as three "generations": (1) **depth-guide generation** (preset-depth diamond burs on the tooth — non-individualized, often over-aggressive), (2) **silicone-index generation** (diagnostic wax-up + silicone indexes guiding freehand prep with repeated re-evaluation), and (3) **mock-up / APT generation** (the authors' recommended method — prep performed *through* the aesthetic pre-evaluative temporary).
- Introduces the **"Do the Math"** technique: a mathematical layer on top of the third-generation APT method that computes preparation amount per tooth region from color and volume goals.
- Defines the core formula **P = LT − EV**, where EV = extra volume added by the mock-up, LT = laminate veneer thickness required for the desired initial→final color, and P = the amount of tooth preparation.
- Establishes the principle that **the more volume added on the mock-up, the more conservative (often zero) the preparation** — directly tying additive diagnostic waxing to enamel preservation.
- Provides color-driven thickness guidance: a 1–2 shade-tone change is achievable with a thin ~0.3 mm laminate; ≥3 tone changes require more invasive preparation; significantly dark teeth require intrasulcular margin extension.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Workflow**: Digital Smile Design (DSD) photographic/video analysis of tooth–smile–face relationship → Visagism-guided personalized anatomy → diagnostic additive wax-up → silicone indexes → bis-acrylic APT (Luxatemp, DMG) placed intraorally as a "test drive" evaluated by patient and team → adjustments for esthetics/phonetics/occlusion → impression of approved mock-up as reference.
- **Preparation through the APT**: predetermined-depth diamond burs (single-ringed burs of 0.3, 0.5, 0.7 mm) used to cut grooves *on the mock-up surface*, not directly on enamel — combining first-generation depth-bur precision with a precise additive guide.
- **Sequence**: incisal grooves first (1–1.5 mm reduction to allow incisal stratification) → buccal grooves (depth set by required color change) → union of grooves through APT → APT removed → final cervical/interproximal finishing lines (retraction cords placed under microscope).
- **Per-region calculation**: P = LT − EV applied independently at cervical third, middle third, incisal third, and interproximal area.
- **Case**: middle-aged man complaining of tooth color, size, and overly open incisal embrasures; esthetic crown lengthening performed; final restorations were ultrathin lithium disilicate (IPS e.max, Ivoclar) veneers (~0.3 mm buccal thickness).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Per-region preparation quantities for the presented case (A2 → A1 color change), from Table 1:

| Region | Extra volume EV (mm) | Laminate thickness LT (mm) | Preparation P (mm) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cervical third | 0.1–0.3 | 0.3 | 0–0.2 |
| Middle third | 0.4 | 0.3 | 0 |
| Incisal third | 0.6 | 0.5 | 0 |
| Interproximal area | 0.1–0.3 | 0.5 | 0.2–0.4 |
| Incisal edge | 1 | 1–1.5 | 0–0.5 |

- Because EV met or exceeded LT in the middle and incisal thirds, **enamel of the middle and incisal thirds remained untouched** (P = 0).
- Buccal reduction for the small A2→A1 change was only 0.2–0.5 mm; the final ultrathin e.max veneers were ~0.3 mm buccal thickness.
- The mathematical approach prevents both over-preparation (cutting too much to avoid a color miss) and under-preparation (not achieving target color).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single illustrative case report — no comparative or survival data; lowest tier of clinical evidence (case-report).
- Additive mock-ups are **not effective for malformed, malpositioned, or crowded teeth** that must be prosthetically reoriented; in such cases orthodontics may minimize prep, or an initial preparation is needed for proper mock-up fit.
- **Significantly dark teeth** still require intrasulcular margin extension so the color difference between substrate and restoration is hidden; diastema closure likewise needs interproximal intrasulcular extension.
- Incisal preparation design remains controversial in the literature (incisal coverage vs not); the authors note volume reestablishment often repositions the incisal edge so no extra reduction occurs.
- Ceramic strength must be weighed: feldspathic ceramics have lower flexural strength but greater stratification versatility; lithium disilicate has higher strength and can be machined/pressed/layered.

## 7. Glossary

- **APT (Aesthetic Pre-evaluative Temporary)**: bis-acrylic mock-up built from the diagnostic wax-up, transferred intraorally; serves as a test-drive and as the physical guide through which the tooth is prepared.
- **Do the Math**: this paper's technique — applying the P = LT − EV calculation per tooth region to determine exact preparation amount/location for the target color and shape.
- **EV (Extra Volume)**: additional tooth volume added by the mock-up relative to the original tooth contour.
- **LT (Laminate Thickness)**: ceramic veneer thickness required to convert the substrate color to the desired final color.
- **P (Preparation)**: amount of tooth structure to remove = max(0, LT − EV); when EV ≥ LT the value clamps to 0 (no reduction).
- **Three generations of preparation**: depth-guide (1st), silicone-index (2nd), mock-up/APT (3rd).
- **DSD (Digital Smile Design)**: photographic/video facial-dental analysis and treatment-planning protocol.
- **Visagism**: framework relating dental composition to facial type and patient personality for personalized esthetics.
