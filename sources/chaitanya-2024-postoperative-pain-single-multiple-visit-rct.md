## One-line Summary

Prospective RCT (n=100): single-visit vs multiple-visit RCT showed no significant difference in post-operative pain (VAS at 24/48/72 h); single-visit group trended toward higher complete-healing rates at 6 and 12 months.

## 한줄요약

전향적 RCT (n=100): 단일내원 vs 다회내원 근관치료가 술후 통증(VAS 24/48/72시간)에서 유의차 없었고, 단일내원군이 6·12개월 완전치유율에서 더 높은 경향을 보임 — 단일내원이 통증·치유 면에서 동등하거나 유리한 대안임을 시사.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Head-to-head RCT (n=100) using post-operative pain (VAS at 24/48/72 h) as primary outcome, with 6- and 12-month radiographic healing as secondary outcome.
- Confirms no significant difference in post-operative pain between single- and multiple-visit RCT at any time point.
- Reports a (non-significant) trend toward better complete healing in the single-visit arm.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Prospective RCT conducted 2021–2022 at a tertiary care facility; IRB-approved.
- **Sample**: 100 patients, ages 18–65, requiring RCT for irreversible pulpitis / apical periodontitis; randomized 1:1 (single-visit n=50, multiple-visit n=50) by computer-generated randomization.
- **Single-visit**: RCT completed in one session.
- **Multiple-visit**: Two or more appointments per usual practice.
- **Pain outcome**: Visual Analogue Scale (VAS) at 24, 48, 72 h; recorded by blinded assessor.
- **Healing outcome**: Periapical radiographs at 6 and 12 months, assessed by two independent radiologists with allocation concealed.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Demographics comparable (single-visit mean age 42.5±8.3 y; multiple-visit 43.2±7.9 y; balanced sex ratio).

| Time (h) | Single-visit VAS (Mean±SD) | Multiple-visit VAS (Mean±SD) |
|---|---|---|
| 24 | 3.2±1.1 | 3.5±1.2 |
| 48 | 2.8±1.0 | 3.0±1.1 |
| 72 | 2.5±0.9 | 2.7±1.0 |

- No significant difference in post-operative pain between groups at 24/48/72 h.
- Single-visit group showed higher rates of complete healing at 6 and 12 months (authors interpret as possibly due to immediate obturation reducing inter-appointment recontamination); the difference is described qualitatively, not as a significant statistical effect.
- Conclusion: single-visit RCT provides comparable pain relief with potentially better healing — a feasible, effective option.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- 12-month follow-up only; long-term success not assessed.
- Healing outcome reported narratively without explicit per-group success counts or P values in the extracted text.
- Single-center tertiary care setting; case selection may favor straightforward cases.
- Authors note severe infections / anatomical complexity may not be ideal single-visit candidates.

## 6. Related Work

- Pak JG, White SN 2011 (J Endod) — SR of pain prevalence/severity before, during, after RCT.

## 7. Glossary

- **VAS (Visual Analogue Scale)**: 0–10 self-reported pain intensity scale.
- **Single-visit RCT**: Cleaning, shaping, and obturation in one appointment.
- **Multiple-visit RCT**: Treatment over ≥2 appointments, root canal system left dressed/sealed between visits.
- **Apical periodontitis / irreversible pulpitis**: Primary pulpal/periapical indications for root canal therapy.
