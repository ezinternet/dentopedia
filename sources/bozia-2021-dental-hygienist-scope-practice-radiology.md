## Why Ingested

[[practice-management/ha-2023-dentist-issues-constitutional-court-decisions]] maps how Korean constitutional jurisprudence draws the boundaries of the dental scope of practice; this paper supplies the international comparator — a worldwide survey of how 26 countries legally delegate (or withhold) oral radiography from the dental hygienist (DH). It is a concrete case study of task redistribution / scope-of-practice regulation, the same legal axis the Korean review examines, and shows how widely a single delegated task (taking/indicating/diagnosing radiographs, owning equipment) varies by jurisdiction — even within a single country by state/province.

## One-line Summary
Worldwide cross-sectional survey (27-item online questionnaire, 26/31 IFDH/EDHF member countries, 84% response): 78% of countries legally let dental hygienists take intraoral radiographs but 42% require a dentist referral first, 46% bar DHs from radiographic diagnosis, and only 27% let DHs independently own radiographic equipment — scope of practice varies by country and sometimes by state/province.

## 한줄요약
전 세계 단면조사 (27문항 온라인 설문, IFDH/EDHF 회원 31개국 중 26개국 응답, 응답률 84%): 78%의 국가에서 치과위생사(Dental Hygienist, DH)가 구내 방사선촬영을 합법적으로 시행하나 42%는 치과의사 의뢰가 선행돼야 하고, 46%는 DH의 방사선 진단을 금지하며, 27%만 DH 단독 장비 소유를 허용 — 업무범위(scope of practice)는 국가별·때로 주/도별로 상이.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First worldwide assessment of the **legal position** (not attitudes) of the dental hygienist (DH) specifically in **oral radiology** — a task traditionally inside the dentist's scope.
- Quantifies cross-national variation in four delegated competencies: taking radiographs, indicating (referral), diagnosing from radiographs, and owning equipment.
- Frames the finding as a **task-redistribution / scope-of-practice** problem and argues for a worldwide common framework to ease DH mobility.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Part of the "Worldwide Dental Hygienists Extended Scope of Practice" project.
- **Target group**: all national DH associations that were members of the International Federation of Dental Hygienists (IFDH) or European Dental Hygienists Federation (EDHF) in 2018 (31 countries).
- **Instrument**: 27-item online questionnaire (Google Forms), scoping + pilot, reviewed by 5 DHs, peer-reviewed by IFDH/EDHF/NVM. Mixed open- and closed-ended items covering education, independent practice, task delegation, and legal status in radiology, cariology, anaesthesia.
- **Procedure**: emailed Nov 2018, 4-week window + reminders, closed Dec 2018. Post-collection validation email to responding associations (79% validation response). Descriptive analysis only.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Response**: 26/31 countries (84%); non-responders = Germany, Korea, Nepal, New Zealand, UAE.
- **Recognition / autonomy**: DH officially recognised in all but Austria; 58% allow independent practice without dentist supervision; 54% allow direct access.
- **Taking radiographs**: 78% (abstract) / 77% (body) of countries legally allow DHs to take intraoral radiographs; NOT allowed in Austria, Czech Republic, Italy, Japan, Russia, Slovakia.
- **Referral**: 42% require a dentist referral/indication first (body: of those who can take a film, 45% need no referral, 30% require one).
- **Diagnosis**: 46% of countries bar the DH from formulating a radiographic diagnosis (abstract); body reports 50% may make a diagnosis.
- **Equipment ownership**: only 27% allow the DH to independently own radiographic equipment.
- **Full task set** (own + indicate + take + diagnose): 5 countries (19%).
- **Extraoral / CBCT**: panoramic barred in Sweden & Israel; only panoramic allowed in Poland; CBCT permitted to DHs in Australia, South Africa, Spain, Switzerland, US.
- **Within-country variation**: US (50 states), Canada (provinces), Switzerland, Australia are multi-jurisdictional — scope differs internally; data reported as the common minimum.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Legal status may differ from daily practice (illegal provision of care); some countries have no DH radiography regulation at all.
- Large Asian/South American populations not represented (not affiliated with the international DH association → excluded); in some countries the DH profession does not exist.
- Minor internal inconsistency between abstract figures (78% take films; 46% may not diagnose) and body figures (77%; 50% may diagnose).
- Authors recommend aligning legal positions and minimum competencies worldwide and building a common framework; further research in non-included countries.

## 6. Related Work
- Johnson's 21-nation comparative study (1987–2006) on international DH profiles — DH-as-auxiliary → collegial collaborative model.
- Dutch task-redistribution legislation granting selected bachelor-level DHs independence in primary caries, local anaesthesia, and intra-oral radiology indication.
- EDHF common European curriculum (Domain III, patient-centred care) listing oral radiology as a competence area.
- Dental therapist (DT) profession (US, NZ, Australia, Canada, UK) as a complicating scope-of-practice comparator.

## 7. Glossary
- **DH (Dental Hygienist / 치과위생사)**: oral-health professional whose legal scope of practice varies by country.
- **Scope of practice / 업무범위**: the set of clinical tasks a profession is legally permitted to perform.
- **Task redistribution / 업무 재분배**: policy shifting tasks (here, intra-oral radiology) from dentist to DH under defined criteria.
- **IFDH / EDHF**: International Federation of Dental Hygienists / European Dental Hygienists Federation — the membership frames that defined the survey population.
- **Indication (referral) / 의뢰**: whether a dentist must first authorise a radiograph before the DH takes it.
- **CBCT (Cone Beam Computed Tomography / 콘빔 전산화단층촬영)**: 3D extraoral imaging; permitted to DHs in only a few countries.
- **DT (Dental Therapist / 치과치료사)**: a separate auxiliary profession with its own scope, distinct from the DH.
