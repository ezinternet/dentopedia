## One-line Summary
SR of 6 in vitro studies on 3D-printed crown bond strength: photopolymer-printed crowns show bond strength comparable to milled materials when properly pretreated, with airborne-particle abrasion (alumina) generally recommended.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Dentistry 144 (2024) 104908
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.jdent.2024.104908
- **Institution**: University of Otago (Sir John Walsh Research Institute), New Zealand
- **Search**: Ovid MEDLINE, Scopus, Web of Science, Google Scholar (through January 2023)
- **Included**: 6 in vitro studies (5 photopolymerized resin, 1 zirconia 3D-printed)

## 2. Key Contributions
- First SR specifically addressing additively manufactured (AM) crown bond strength
- Confirms that AM crowns can achieve bond strength comparable to milled (subtractive) crowns when surface treatment is adequate
- Identifies airborne-particle abrasion (Al₂O₃) as the most consistently effective pretreatment
- Highlights research gap — only 6 studies, all in vitro; no clinical data yet
- Modified CONSORT-based risk of bias: all included studies had low risk of bias

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: SR following PRISMA-P
- **PICO(S)**: P=AM crown specimens; I=cementation protocols/post-processing; C=untreated or milled crowns; O=bond strength; S=quantitative in vitro
- **Inclusion**: English, full-text, peer-reviewed, in vitro, bond strength testing (tensile, micro-tensile, micro-shear, shear, pull-off)
- **Interventions analyzed**: Surface pretreatments, airborne abrasion pressure, cement type, crown taper, artificial aging

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Bond strength of photopolymer AM crowns is high and comparable to milled equivalents
- No definitive superior cement type identified across studies
- Airborne-particle abrasion with Al₂O₃ generally recommended for surface preparation
- 3/6 studies directly compared to milled materials — bond strengths were similar
- The single zirconia 3D-printing study showed acceptable bond strength after appropriate pretreatment

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Only 6 studies, all in vitro — no clinical translation
- Heterogeneity in test methods (tensile vs shear vs micro-tensile)
- Specific to crown geometries; long-span bridge data absent
- Material formulations evolving rapidly; review may be outdated quickly
- Clinical validation studies for permanent restorations urgently needed

## 6. Related Work
- Complements Lim 2023 (milled vs 3D-printed inlay accuracy)
- Adjacent to El-Shazly 2026 (3D-printed hybrid plasma bonding)
- Bond strength methodology aligns with Hardan 2021 (universal adhesive dentin SR)

## 7. Glossary
- **Additive Manufacturing (AM, 적층제조)**: 3D printing — layer-by-layer construction; SLA, DLP for dental
- **SLA (Stereolithography, 광경화조형)**: UV laser cures photopolymer resin layer by layer
- **DLP (Digital Light Processing, 디지털광원처리)**: Projector cures entire layer at once
- **Subtractive manufacturing**: Milling from a block (zirconia, lithium disilicate, PMMA)
- **Photopolymer resin**: Liquid resin + photoinitiators; cured by UV/visible light
- **Airborne-particle abrasion (샌드블라스팅)**: Al₂O₃ particles propelled to roughen/clean surface
