## One-line Summary

In-vitro MTT + qRT-PCR study (HGF-1 fibroblasts, 25–100% eluates, days 1/3/7) finding both premixed bioceramic sealers cause concentration- and time-dependent cytotoxicity and IL-6/IL-8/TNF-α upregulation, with NeoSEALER Flo slightly more cytotoxic/pro-inflammatory than CeraSeal, while AH Plus (epoxy-resin control) produced the highest cytokine levels overall.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Direct head-to-head comparison of two premixed calcium-silicate bioceramic sealers — **CeraSeal** (MetaBiomed, South Korea) and **NeoSEALER Flo** (Avalon BioMed/Nusmile, USA) — against the AH Plus epoxy-resin gold-standard, on human gingival fibroblasts (HGFs), the first responders to apical sealer extrusion/leakage.
- Couples a viability readout (MTT) with a mechanistic inflammatory readout (qRT-PCR of IL-6, IL-8, TNF-α at both gene-expression and cytokine-protein level).
- Quantifies a biocompatibility ranking: **CeraSeal > NeoSEALER Flo** for HGF safety; both bioceramics less cytotoxic and less pro-inflammatory than AH Plus at the protein level.
- Rejects the null hypothesis (a statistically significant difference exists among the tested sealers), with clinical guidance favoring CeraSeal where minimal tissue irritation is critical (apical periodontitis, compromised periapical tissue, high sealer-extrusion risk).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: in-vitro experimental; three groups — Group A = AH Plus (control), Group N = NeoSEALER Flo, Group C = CeraSeal.
- **Cells**: human gingival fibroblast line HGF-1 (ATCC CRL-2014, via VACsera ATCC); DMEM + 10% FBS + 1% pen-strep, 37 °C / 5% CO₂; plated 1×10⁴ cells/well in 96-well plates.
- **Eluate prep**: Teflon disks 5 mm × 2 mm, 10 disks per group, UV-sterilized 30 min, immersed in medium; eluates incubated 24 h at 37 °C, filtered (0.22 μm). Eluates tested at **25%, 50%, 75%, 100%** (v/v).
- **Exposure times**: 1, 3, 7 days.
- **Cytotoxicity**: MTT (20 µL 5 mg/mL, 4 h; formazan in DMSO; OD 570 nm; ELX-800 reader) → cell-viability %.
- **Inflammation**: qRT-PCR (TRIzol RNA, SYBR green, GAPDH housekeeping, 2^-ΔΔCt) for IL-6, IL-8, TNF-α; cytokine protein levels (pg/mL) also reported.
- **Sample size**: PASS 15, one-sample t-test, α 0.05, power 80% → 60 RCS discs (10/subgroup).
- **Stats**: SPSS v25.0; Shapiro-Wilk; one-way ANOVA + Tukey; Kruskal-Wallis for non-parametric; p<0.05. Experiments in triplicate.
- **Ethics**: Sinai University Faculty of Oral & Dental Medicine IRB (Kantara, Egypt), approval SU.REC.2024 (12).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Cytotoxicity (concentration):** Concentration-dependent for both bioceramics (p<0.05). At 100% eluate, NeoSEALER Flo reduced HGF viability by **45%**, CeraSeal by **35%** vs control.

**Cell viability % by day (mean ± SD):**

| Day | NeoSEALER Flo | CeraSeal | AH Plus | Between-group p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 88.78 ± 3.84 | 93.32 ± 2.88 | 87.95 ± 5.53 | 0.317 (NS) |
| 3 | 78.02 ± 1.89 | 82.74 ± 2.56 | 77.81 ± 4.49 | 0.18 (NS) |
| 7 | 71.89 ± 3.44 | 75.43 ± 2.14 | 69.80 ± 1.97 | 0.094; CeraSeal > AH Plus p=0.028 |

Between-material differences mostly non-significant (p>0.05); CeraSeal had significantly higher viability than AH Plus on **day 7** (p=0.028). Within each material, viability dropped significantly across time.

**Pro-inflammatory cytokines at 100% (pg/mL):** NeoSEALER Flo — IL-6 150±12, IL-8 120±10, TNF-α 90±6; CeraSeal — IL-6 130±10, IL-8 100±8, TNF-α 80±5; **AH Plus highest** — IL-6 190±20, IL-8 140±8, TNF-α 100±4. Cytokine output rose with concentration across all groups (p<0.05).

**Gene expression at 100% (fold change):** NeoSEALER Flo — IL-6 3.5±0.5, IL-8 2.8±0.3, TNF-α 2.2±0.2; CeraSeal — IL-6 3.0±0.4, IL-8 2.5±0.3, TNF-α 1.9±0.2; AH Plus — IL-6 4.2±0.8, IL-8 3.2±0.7, TNF-α 2.7±0.6. AH Plus vs both bioceramics significant (p=0.013, 0.006); the two bioceramics did not differ significantly (p>0.05). CeraSeal showed the least expression.

**Bottom line:** biocompatibility ranking CeraSeal > NeoSEALER Flo, both safer than AH Plus on protein-level cytokines; null hypothesis rejected.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro only; cannot reproduce the complex in-vivo periodontal/periapical environment.
- Single cell type (HGFs); no osteoblasts or immune cells.
- Authors call for longitudinal designs, in-vivo animal models, diverse cell populations, and testing additional bioceramic formulations.

## 7. Glossary

- **HGF (human gingival fibroblast)**: predominant periodontal cell, first responder to sealer extrusion/leakage; used as the biocompatibility test cell here (line HGF-1, ATCC CRL-2014).
- **Bioceramic / calcium-silicate sealer**: premixed root-canal sealer based on tri-/dicalcium silicate that sets on moisture and is marketed for bioactivity (hydroxyapatite formation).
- **MTT assay**: tetrazolium reduction assay quantifying mitochondrial activity as a cell-viability proxy.
- **qRT-PCR (2^-ΔΔCt)**: relative gene-expression quantification normalized to GAPDH.
- **IL-6 / IL-8 / TNF-α**: pro-inflammatory cytokines — immune regulation / neutrophil chemotaxis / acute inflammatory orchestration, respectively.
- **Eluate**: extract leached from the set sealer into culture medium, tested at graded dilutions.
