## Why Ingested

Answers the most fundamental decision in the field — "do we even need a post?" — with clinical survival data, the necessary counterweight to the material-choice papers [[post-and-core/tsintsadze-2022-survival-rates-glass-fiber-metal-posts]]. Frames when a post adds value (it improves survival here), against the minimally-invasive no-post / endocrown alternative in [[post-and-core/mously-2024-anterior-endocrowns-alternative-core-crown]].

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (17 clinical studies in quantitative synthesis, 7,278 patients / 7,330 ETT): post placement was associated with a statistically significant improvement in survival of endodontically treated teeth vs no post (P<0.001).

## 한줄요약

SR+MA(정량분석 임상연구 17편, 환자 7,278명·근관치료치아 7,330개): 포스트 식립이 무포스트 대비 근관치료치아 생존율을 유의하게 향상시켰음(P<0.001).

## 1. Document Information

Systematic review and meta-analysis, *Journal of Oral Science* (2024;66(4):207-214). Abstract-only ingest. PubMed/Scopus/Web of Science search (no time restriction, English). 57 studies qualitative; 17 clinical (11 prospective, 6 retrospective) in quantitative analysis. Mean age 45.46 ± 12.1 yr.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Large pooled cohort (7,330 ETT) directly testing post vs no-post on clinical survival.
- Found a statistically significant survival advantage for post placement (P<0.001).
- Addresses the controversial "post necessity" question with clinical (not just in-vitro) evidence.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Four-component search (ETT, fixed prosthesis, post restoration, survival rate); 57 studies qualitative → 17 clinical for meta-analysis.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Statistically significant difference in survival rate between ETT with vs without posts, favoring posts (P<0.001).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

Heterogeneous study designs (prospective + retrospective); no time restriction may include older protocols. Does NOT stratify by remaining coronal structure — the modern view is that post benefit is conditional on substance loss, which a pooled estimate can mask. Abstract-only ingest. Contrast: in-vitro/biomechanical reviews (e.g. mously-2024) often find no post benefit when a ferrule is present.

## 6. Related Work

Counterpart to the minimally-invasive endocrown / no-post option in [[post-and-core/mously-2024-anterior-endocrowns-alternative-core-crown]]; the survival benefit must be read alongside the ferrule data in [[post-and-core/al-dabbagh-2024-circumferential-ferrule-fiber-posts-survival]].

## 7. Glossary

- **Post (dowel)** — intraradicular retainer placed to retain a core foundation in a structurally compromised ETT.
- **Core** — coronal foundation (composite/cast) built over/around the post to support the final crown.
- **No-post (direct/adhesive core)** — restoration relying on adhesive bonding and remaining dentin without an intracanal post.
