## One-line Summary

Abstract-only RCT (80 carious primary molars): vs the conventional technique, the Hall technique for preformed metal crowns required less than half the chair time (7.1 vs 17.4 min, p<0.001), caused significantly less child pain (p=0.03), and earned significantly better parental acceptability (ease, duration, satisfaction, all p<0.001).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies the process/patient-experience advantages of the Hall technique over the conventional PMC technique.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Primary-care, two-arm parallel RCT, 80 participants, single pediatric dentist. Outcomes: procedural time; child pain (Wong-Baker FACES); behaviour (Frankl); parental perceptions/acceptability (5-pt Likert). (Abstract-level only.)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Procedural time: CT 17.4±2.9 vs HT 7.1±2.8 min (p<0.001).
- Child pain: significantly higher in CT (χ=12.27; p=0.03).
- Behaviour: comparable (positive CT 65%, HT 85%).
- Parental comfort perception NS (p=0.20), but HT significantly better for procedural ease, duration, satisfaction, and desire to repeat (all p<0.001).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Process/perception outcomes only (no long-term restoration survival here — see companion outcome RCTs); single operator; abstract-only.

## 7. Glossary

- **Hall technique (HT)**: SSC cemented over caries without anaesthesia/prep/caries removal.
- **Conventional technique (CT)**: SSC after local anaesthesia, tooth prep, and caries removal.
- **Wong-Baker FACES / Frankl**: child pain / behaviour scales.
