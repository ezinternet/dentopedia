## Why Ingested

Anchors the "abandon CR" pole of the long-running centric-relation terminology debate, extending the occlusion-controversy hub at [[occlusion/fukushima-2016-controversy-with-respect-occlusion]]. Provides the provocative thesis that the rebuttals (Fornai 2022, Goldstein 2022) and the consensus paper (Kattadiyil 2021) are responding to, so the wiki holds both poles of the argument rather than only the establishment view.

## One-line Summary

Narrative review (~70 years of CR literature) concluding the term "centric relation" is semantically, conceptually and practically flawed and should be abandoned; in healthy dentate patients, maximum intercuspation determines the TMJ relationship and is biologically acceptable.

## 한줄요약

약 70년간의 CR 문헌을 검토한 서술적 리뷰로, "중심위(centric relation, CR)"라는 용어가 의미론적·개념적·실용적으로 결함이 있어 폐기되어야 한다고 결론지음 — 건강한 유치악 환자에서는 최대교두감합(maximum intercuspation, MIP)이 턱관절 관계를 결정하며 생물학적으로 수용 가능하다고 주장.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Argues, from a ~70-year literature survey, that the definition of CR has been repeatedly altered (rearmost/retruded → anterosuperior condylar positions), producing persistent confusion and disagreement among both clinicians and academics.
- Frames the changing definitions as a driver of "unnecessary diagnostic and therapeutic procedures" (e.g., CR registration, condylar-position assessment, occlusal therapy aimed at relocating the mandible).
- Advances the most provocative position in the debate: the term CR is flawed on semantic, conceptual and practical grounds and should be **abandoned** entirely.
- Proposes an individualised replacement concept — every person has a unique TMJ relationship not capturable by any single term; in healthy dentate patients this is determined by maximum intercuspation (MIP) and is biologically acceptable.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PubMed search using the terms 'centric relation', 'masticatory muscles', 'maxillomandibular relationship' and 'condylar position'.
- Relevant literature spanning the past ~70 years to the present was scrutinised (narrative/critical appraisal, not a systematic review — no reported PRISMA flow, inclusion criteria, or quantitative synthesis; *abstract-only, detail not extractable*).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- The review reports a "problematic pattern of changing definitions and clinical disagreements" with significant downstream impact on practice.
- No quantitative outcomes (this is a conceptual/terminology argument, not an empirical study). *Numerical detail not available — abstract-only.*

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative review → selection/confirmation bias risk; the authors' prior position on evidence-based occlusion is well known.
- Conclusion ("abandon CR") is a strong normative claim that conflicts with prosthodontic-record practice and is directly contested by subsequent rebuttals.
- Abstract-only ingest: argumentation steps, cited evidence, and any proposed alternative terminology beyond "MIP in healthy dentate patients" are not captured here.

## 7. Glossary

- **Centric relation (CR)**: A maxillomandibular relationship of the mandible to the maxilla, classically defined (with much variation over time) as a condylar position independent of tooth contact — historically "retruded," later "anterosuperior" against the articular eminences.
- **Maximum intercuspation (MIP / MICP)**: The complete intercuspal occlusion of opposing teeth independent of condylar position; the tooth-determined bite position.
- **Condylar position**: The spatial position of the mandibular condyle within the glenoid fossa, central to gnathological CR concepts.
