## One-line Summary
Systematic review of 14 in vitro studies: QMix superior to NaOCl, EDTA, CHX, MTAD, and Cetrimide against E. faecalis; superior to NaOCl only when contact >10 min or volume >3 mL.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Restorative Dentistry & Endodontics 45(2):e23, 2020
- **DOI**: 10.5395/rde.2020.45.e23
- **Study type**: Systematic review (14 studies; no meta-analysis due to heterogeneity)

## 2. Key Contributions
- QMix consistently superior or equivalent to all tested irrigants against *E. faecalis* across all 14 included studies
- vs. NaOCl: QMix superior **only when NaOCl contact time >10 min or volume >3 mL** was not used; at high contact/volume, NaOCl comparable
- vs. EDTA, CHX, MTAD, Cetrimide: QMix consistently superior
- QMix's dual antibacterial mechanism: CHX component provides immediate bactericidal effect; EDTA component chelates Ca²⁺ from bacterial cell wall
- All included studies are in vitro; no clinical RCT data available

## 3. Methodology
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane, Google Scholar (inception–2019)
- **Inclusion**: In vitro studies; *E. faecalis* as test organism; QMix as test irrigant; comparison with ≥1 other irrigant
- **Exclusion**: Animal/clinical studies; no comparison group
- **Quality assessment**: Modified Newcastle-Ottawa Scale adapted for in vitro studies
- **14 studies included**; no meta-analysis (outcome measurements too heterogeneous)

## 4. Key Results

### QMix vs. comparators (direction of effect)
| Comparator | QMix result | Condition |
|---|---|---|
| NaOCl (any concentration) | Superior | Short contact (<10 min) or small volume (<3 mL) |
| NaOCl (high contact/volume) | Comparable | >10 min contact OR >3 mL volume |
| EDTA (any) | QMix superior | All conditions |
| CHX (0.2–2%) | QMix superior | All conditions |
| MTAD | QMix superior | All conditions |
| Cetrimide | QMix superior | All conditions |

### Proposed mechanism
- CHX in QMix: disrupts bacterial cell membrane → bactericidal
- EDTA in QMix: chelates Ca²⁺ → destabilizes Gram-positive cell wall
- Synergistic effect > individual components

## 5. Limitations
- Exclusively in vitro; biofilm models vary widely (planktonic vs. biofilm)
- QMix concentration standardized (marketed concentration), but contact time/volume varied
- E. faecalis model does not fully represent polymicrobial endodontic microbiota
- No clinical healing outcome data

## 7. Glossary
- **QMix**: Final rinse irrigant (Dentsply Sirona); composition: 2% CHX + EDTA + detergent (Brij-35); used as single-step final rinse, no separate EDTA + CHX steps needed
- **E. faecalis**: Enterococcus faecalis; Gram-positive facultative anaerobe; most prevalent organism in failed root canal treatment; resistant to Ca(OH)2, NaOCl at low concentrations
- **MTAD**: Mixture of tetracycline isomer, acid (citric acid), and detergent (Tween 80); irrigant developed as single-step final rinse
- **Cetrimide**: Cationic detergent with antibacterial properties; used as irrigant/disinfectant in some protocols
