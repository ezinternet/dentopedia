## Why Ingested

기존 [[implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/chiapasco-2021-customized-cadcam-titanium-meshes-gbr]]가 맞춤형 CAD/CAM 티타늄 메시의 GBR 효능을 다뤘는데, 본 RCT는 그 맞춤형 메시 개념을 새 재료(맞춤형 PEEK 메시)로 확장하고 prebent Ti mesh와 직접 비교한다. 또한 [[implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/cucchi-2024-ptfe-mesh-ti-mesh-vertical-ridge-rct]]의 mesh head-to-head RCT 흐름에 PEEK라는 새로운 비교 arm을 더해, 메시 재료 선택지의 volumetric 근거를 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

RCT (14 patients / 28 maxillary sites) comparing prebent titanium mesh + 60:40 autograft:xenograft vs customized PEEK mesh + 70:30 graft for predominantly horizontal ridge augmentation: no significant difference in linear horizontal (3.02 vs 2.42 mm, p=0.065) or vertical gain (1.12 vs 1.09 mm), but PEEK gained significantly more absolute bone volume (499 vs 371 mm³, p=0.004) while also losing more graft volume (175 vs 128 mm³, p=0.040); proportional resorption was near-identical (~34–35%); exposure was numerically lower with PEEK (7.1% vs 14.3%, NS); 100% implant placement in both.

## 한줄요약

RCT (환자 14명 / 상악 28부위): prebent 티타늄 메시(자가골:이종골 60:40) vs 맞춤형 PEEK 메시(70:30)를 주로 수평 골증대에 비교 — 수평(3.02 vs 2.42 mm, p=0.065)·수직(1.12 vs 1.09 mm) 선형 증대는 차이 없으나, PEEK가 절대 골량 증가(499 vs 371 mm³, p=0.004)와 이식재 흡수량(175 vs 128 mm³, p=0.040) 모두 유의하게 컸고, 비율 흡수는 거의 동일(~34–35%); 메시 노출은 PEEK 7.1% vs Ti 14.3%(유의차 없음), 양 군 모두 임플란트 식립 100%.

## 1. Document Information

- Type: Randomized clinical trial (RCT), single-center, two parallel arms
- Setting: Faculty of Dentistry, Suez Canal University, Egypt
- Population: 14 adults with partially edentulous maxillary ridges, predominantly horizontal defects (horizontal width 2–5 mm at 2 mm below crest), sometimes with minor vertical component
- Randomization: patient-level (n=14, 7 per group); measurement at site level (28 sites, 14 per group); SNOSE allocation concealment; mixed-effects model accounts for site-within-patient clustering
- Blinding: surgeon/patient not blinded (distinct mesh properties); single examiner (B.A.A.); CBCT anonymized; ICC > 0.90 (intra-examiner)
- Follow-up: CBCT preop, immediate postop, and 6 months (before mesh removal); implants placed at 6 months
- Funding/registration: prospective registration June 25, 2025; recruitment Jul 2025–Jan 2026

## 2. Key Contributions

- One of few RCTs adding **3D volumetric CBCT evaluation** (gained bone volume mm³, graft loss volume mm³) to the conventional linear (horizontal/vertical mm) assessment of mesh-based GBR.
- Direct comparison of **prebent CAD/CAM titanium mesh** vs **customized milled PEEK mesh** for predominantly horizontal maxillary augmentation.
- Explicitly frames itself as a **two-protocol comparison (Mesh A + Graft A vs Mesh B + Graft B)**, NOT an isolated material comparison, because graft composition deliberately differed (60:40 Ti vs 70:30 PEEK).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Control (titanium):** STL model 3D-printed from virtually augmented CBCT; readymade Ti mesh prebent over model preoperatively; intraoperatively graft = 60:40 autogenous (mandibular ramus, ACM bur) : xenograft; fixed with 3–4 microscrews.
- **Study (PEEK):** patient-customized 0.5–0.7 mm perforated PEEK mesh (medical-grade Juvora blocks, 5-axis milled) covering buccal/crestal/palatal; graft = 70:30 autogenous : xenograft (higher autograft to compensate lower PEEK porosity/permeability); 3–4 microscrews.
- Both arms: full-thickness flap, recipient-site decortication, periosteal releasing incision for tension-free closure, Prolene 4-0 sutures; AMX/CLV 875/125 mg prophylaxis, 0.12% CHX rinse.
- **CBCT analysis:** Scanora3D (133 μm voxel); OnDemand 3D (Cybermed, Korea); semi-automatic threshold segmentation; voxel-based registration on stable landmarks; bone gain = preop→immediate-postop volume difference; graft resorption = immediate-postop→6-month difference.
- **Statistics:** linear mixed-effects model (treatment = fixed, patient = random intercept), Fisher exact for categorical, Shapiro-Wilk normality, α=0.05, R 4.4.1.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Titanium (control) | PEEK (study) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Horizontal bone gain (mm) | 3.02 ± 0.68 | 2.42 ± 0.38 | 0.065 (NS) |
| Vertical bone gain (mm) | 1.12 ± 0.10 | 1.09 ± 0.25 | 0.738 (NS) |
| Gained bone volume (mm³) | 370.82 ± 51.69 | 499.47 ± 80.46 | 0.004 (sig.) |
| Graft loss volume (mm³) | 127.73 ± 35.55 | 174.83 ± 40.78 | 0.040 (sig.) |
| Graft loss (% of baseline) | ~34% | ~35% | ~equal |
| Mesh exposure | 2/7 (14.3%) | 1/7 (7.1%) | NS |
| Exposure timing | 4–5 months | 3 months | — |
| Severe bleeding | 1 (14.3%) | 0 | NS |
| Implant placement success | 7/7 (100%) | 7/7 (100%) | — |

- All postop edema resolved within 1 week; no donor-site infection; no neurosensory disturbance in either group.
- Exposures (both groups) managed conservatively (saline/CHX irrigation, OH); did not compromise regeneration; no PEEK fracture at 0.5–0.7 mm thickness.
- Null hypothesis (no difference in gain/volume/stability) **partially rejected**: linear gain similar, but absolute volume and absolute graft loss differed — though proportional resorption did not.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Confounded by design:** graft ratio differed (60:40 vs 70:30), so volumetric differences cannot be attributed to mesh material alone → interpret as treatment-protocol comparison only.
- Small sample (n=14 patients), underpowered for secondary/complication outcomes → no equivalence claim.
- Short 6-month follow-up; no implant survival or crestal-bone-stability long-term data.
- Single-center; surgeon not blinded (performance bias); no histology / pseudoperiosteum assessment.
- No standardized baseline defect dimensions or planned-augmentation volume → regeneration rate (RR) could not be computed; baseline comparability uncertain.
- No cost/fabrication-time/availability analysis.

## 6. Related Work

- Mounir et al. 2019 — first prebent Ti vs customized PEEK mesh RCT (basis for sample-size calc); used 2 mm PEEK thickness; found no difference in graft loss.
- Zhu et al. 2024 — quantitative PEEK mesh assessment; Gouda et al. 2023 — histomorphometry.
- De Santis et al. 2021, 2024 — patient-specific digitally designed Ti mesh for vertical/horizontal/combined defects.
- Di Spirito et al. — Ti mesh exposure 20–66%.

## 7. Glossary

- **GBR** — Guided Bone Regeneration.
- **PEEK** — poly-ether-ether-ketone; radiolucent, low-tissue-irritation polymer barrier alternative to titanium.
- **Prebent Ti mesh** — titanium mesh adapted/bent preoperatively over a 3D-printed virtually augmented STL model.
- **ACM bur** — auto chip maker bur for harvesting particulate autogenous bone (mandibular ramus donor).
- **Gained bone volume** — preop→immediate-postop CBCT volume difference (mm³).
- **Graft loss / resorption volume** — immediate-postop→6-month CBCT volume difference (mm³).
- **Regeneration rate (RR)** — newly formed bone volume ÷ planned augmentation volume (not computable here).
- **SNOSE** — sequentially numbered opaque sealed envelopes (allocation concealment).
