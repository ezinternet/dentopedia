Part of a TMD physical-therapy ingest batch reinforcing the wiki's physiotherapy cluster. This is the foundational 2015 systematic review of RCTs isolating manual therapy (MT) effects on TMD pain and mouth opening, and it directly reinforces [[wiki/tmj/gonzalez-sanchez-2023-tmj-physiotherapy-treatment-sr]] by providing the earlier, technique-stratified GRADE evidence base (myofascial release/massage on masticatory muscles, upper-cervical thrust/mobilisation) that the later physiotherapy syntheses build on.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review of 8 RCTs (7 of high PEDro quality) showing manual therapy improves pain, maximum mouth opening (MMO) and pressure pain threshold (PPT) in TMD — myofascial release/massage on masticatory muscles and upper-cervical spine thrust/mobilisation are more effective than control (low–high GRADE), as effective as botulinum toxin (moderate), while thoracic manipulation is not effective.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic review to synthesise the **isolated** effect of manual therapy (MT) on **maximum mouth opening (MMO)** and **pain** in subjects with signs and symptoms of TMD, restricted to **randomised controlled trials**.
- Stratifies the evidence **by MT technique** rather than treating "manual therapy" as one block, producing technique-specific GRADE recommendations.
- Applies **PEDro scale** for risk of bias and **GRADE** for overall quality of the body of evidence, and computes treatment effect size for **pain, MMO and pressure pain threshold (PPT)**.
- Establishes that effectiveness is **technique-dependent**: masticatory-muscle myofascial release/massage and upper-cervical manipulation/mobilisation are supported, whereas thoracic manipulation is not.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases searched**: MEDLINE, Cochrane, Web of Science, SciELO, EMBASE.
- **Inclusion**: RCTs applying MT for TMD compared to other intervention, no intervention, or placebo.
- **Data extraction**: Two authors independently extracted data.
- **Risk of bias**: PEDro scale (per-trial methodological quality rating).
- **Evidence synthesis**: GRADE (Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation) to grade overall body-of-evidence quality (Data S1 details the GRADE criteria; Data S2 lists reasons for excluding full-text-screened studies).
- **Outcome metrics**: treatment effect size calculated for pain, MMO, and PPT (Hedges/Cohen effect-size references cited).
- **Included trials**: **8 RCTs**, **7 of high methodological quality**.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

From the abstract's technique-stratified GRADE synthesis:

| MT technique | Comparator | Direction / effectiveness | GRADE evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Myofascial release + massage on masticatory muscles | Control | More effective | Low to moderate |
| Myofascial release + massage on masticatory muscles | Botulinum toxin injection | As effective (equivalent) | Moderate |
| Upper cervical spine thrust manipulation or mobilisation | Control | More effective | Low to high |
| Thoracic manipulation | Control | Not effective | — |
| MT technique protocols (overall) | — | Effective | Moderate to high |

- Overall conclusion: **widely varying evidence** that MT improves pain, MMO and PPT in TMD **depending on the technique**.
- **Methodological heterogeneity** across trial protocols frequently lowered the quality (GRADE) of evidence.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only **8 RCTs** met inclusion; small and heterogeneous evidence base.
- **No quantitative pooling / meta-analysis** — effect sizes computed per trial, not pooled, due to methodological heterogeneity of protocols.
- Heterogeneity in MT protocols and outcome measurement degraded GRADE quality.
- Authors call for **standardised evaluations and better study designs** (e.g. validated sham/placebo MT procedures, intention-to-treat analysis) to strengthen clinical relevance.

## 6. Related Work

- McNeely 2006; Medlicott & Harris 2006 — earlier systematic reviews of physical therapy / exercise / MT for TMD.
- Guarda-Nardini 2012 — botulinum toxin vs fascial manipulation for jaw myofascial pain (basis for the MT-vs-BoNT equivalence finding).
- Kalamir 2012 — intraoral myofascial therapy RCT for chronic myogenous TMD.
- La Touche 2009/2013; Oliveira-Campelo 2010 — upper-cervical MT effects on TMD pain and PPT.
- Packer 2014 — upper-thoracic manipulation on PPT in women with TMD (basis for the "thoracic not effective" finding).
- Gomes 2014 — massage vs occlusal splint on mandibular ROM.

## 7. Glossary

- **TMD** — Temporomandibular disorder.
- **MT** — Manual therapy (myofascial release, massage, joint mobilisation/manipulation).
- **MMO** — Maximum mouth opening (range-of-motion outcome).
- **PPT** — Pressure pain threshold (quantitative sensory measure of mechanical pain sensitivity).
- **PEDro scale** — Physiotherapy Evidence Database scale; rates methodological quality / risk of bias of RCTs.
- **GRADE** — Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation; rates overall quality of a body of evidence.
- **Thrust manipulation** — High-velocity low-amplitude (HVLA) joint manipulation, here applied to the upper cervical spine.
