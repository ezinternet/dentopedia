## One-line Summary
BMJ Rapid Recommendations clinical practice guideline (2023) for chronic TMD-associated pain; recommends conservative non-pharmacological treatments first, with conditional recommendations for splints, exercise, and BTX in refractory cases.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: BMJ 2023;383:e076227
- **Published**: 15 December 2023
- **DOI**: 10.1136/bmj-2023-076227
- **Study type**: Clinical practice guideline (consensus/Rapid Recommendations)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Evidence-based guideline using GRADE methodology for chronic TMD pain management
- Covers full spectrum: education/self-care, physiotherapy, occlusal splints, pharmacotherapy, and interventional approaches
- Integrates patient values and preferences into recommendation strength
- Provides decision aids for shared decision-making
- Explicitly addresses irreversible vs. reversible treatment sequencing

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- BMJ Rapid Recommendations framework: systematic evidence review + guideline panel
- Panel: multidisciplinary (TMD specialists, pain medicine, physiotherapy, patient representatives)
- GRADE Evidence-to-Decision (EtD) framework
- Each recommendation rated: strong vs. conditional; for vs. against
- Linked systematic reviews commissioned to support guideline

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks (Recommendations)
- **Strong recommendation**: Conservative, reversible treatments as first line (education, self-care, jaw rest)
- **Conditional for**: Stabilization splints (교합안정장치) as adjunct for myofascial TMD
- **Conditional for**: Structured physiotherapy and exercise therapy
- **Conditional for**: BTX (보툴리눔독소) injection for refractory myofascial pain unresponsive to other treatments
- **Strong against**: Irreversible occlusal modifications (grinding, crowns) for TMD
- **Conditional against**: Routine arthrocentesis as first-line
- Pharmacotherapy: short-term NSAIDs, muscle relaxants, or tricyclic antidepressants (amitriptyline) for chronic cases

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Underlying evidence base is predominantly low certainty (GRADE)
- Recommendations rely on extrapolation from chronic pain literature in some areas
- Limited RCT evidence for many specific interventions
- Guideline covers chronic pain; acute TMD management not addressed

## 7. Glossary
- **Rapid Recommendations**: BMJ process for producing timely guidelines linked to new high-quality evidence
- **GRADE EtD**: Evidence-to-Decision framework linking evidence certainty to recommendation strength
- **Conservative-first approach**: Reversible, non-invasive treatments prioritized before invasive/irreversible options
- **Stabilization splint** (교합안정장치): Removable full-arch occlusal appliance; first-line reversible occlusal intervention
- **Amitriptyline**: Tricyclic antidepressant with analgesic properties at low doses; used in chronic myofascial pain
- **Strong vs. conditional recommendation**: GRADE designation; strong = applies to most patients, conditional = shared decision-making required
