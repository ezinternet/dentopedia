## Why Ingested

기존 food-impaction 페이지들([[food-impaction/kim-2025-factors-influencing-proximal-contact-loss]], [[food-impaction/liang-2020-prevalence-associated-factors-retrospective]], [[food-impaction/fathi-2022-prevalence-proximal-contact-loss-umbrella]])은 임플란트 보철 인접 근심접촉상실(Proximal Contact Loss, PCL)의 유병률(34–66%)·위험인자를 정량화했지만 모두 **반응적(reactive) 관리**(재제작·재접착·접촉면 회복)에 그쳤다. 본 retrospective(Liu 2025)는 처음으로 **예방적(proactive) 교합조정 프로토콜(MM-DD)**을 최장 21.5년 추적으로 검증해, PCL을 46.7%→2.7%로 낮추는 직접 임상 근거를 제공한다. 따라서 위 페이지들의 "PCL은 흔하고 위험인자가 많다"는 결론에 **예방 전략 분기**를 더해 확장한다.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective single-practice study (124 single-tooth posterior implants, 100 patients, 2–21.5 yr): the MM-DD occlusal adjustment — selectively relieving the mesial occlusal surface of the mesial adjacent tooth and the distal occlusal surface of the distal adjacent tooth at crown delivery to permit physiologic mesial drift — cut open-contact incidence from 46.7% (conventional control, n=49) to 2.7% (MM-DD, n=75; p<0.001), with Kaplan–Meier contact survival 99.1%/97.5%/96.2% at 5/10/15 yr.

## 한줄요약

후향 단일 의원 연구(124 단일 구치부 임플란트, 100명, 2–21.5년): crown 장착 시 근심인접치의 근심교합면·원심인접치의 원심교합면을 선택적으로 삭제해 생리적 mesial drift를 허용하는 MM-DD 교합조정으로 open contact를 46.7%(전통적 대조군)→2.7%로 격감(p<0.001), 접촉 생존율 5/10/15년 99.1/97.5/96.2%.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First long-term (up to 21.5 yr) clinical validation of a **preventive** occlusal protocol for open proximal contacts adjacent to posterior implants, vs the field's prior reactive strategies.
- Defines the **MM-DD technique**: at crown delivery, selectively relieve the **mesial occlusal surface of the mesial adjacent tooth** and the **distal occlusal surface of the distal adjacent tooth**, creating occlusal relief zones that accommodate physiologic mesial drift while keeping firm central-fossa contact on the implant crown and only light axial contact on adjacent teeth.
- Quantifies a large effect: open-contact incidence 2.7% (MM-DD) vs 46.7% (conventional control), p<0.001.
- Provides Kaplan–Meier proximal-contact survival curves and time-to-open-contact data for both arms.
- Links open contacts to downstream harm (food impaction 86.7% vs 3.2%; greater proximal bone loss and probing depth) and reframes occlusal planning as a maintenance lever.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Retrospective chart/radiograph review, private practice, July 2000 – January 2022; single operator (CLL). IRB Kaohsiung Medical University Hospital.
- **Sample**: 100 patients (52 F/48 M), 124 single-unit posterior implant crowns (28 maxillary premolars, 32 maxillary molars, 18 mandibular premolars, 46 mandibular molars). 124 mesial + 110 distal contact surfaces. Age 24–84 (mean 54.2 ± 12.1).
- **Groups**: MM-DD group n=75 (crowns delivered with MM-DD adjustment, 40–50 μm articulating paper); conventional control n=49 (historical, 2000–2005, conventional adjustment without targeted relief).
- **Implants**: 3 manufacturers, moderately rough, 3.5–6.0 mm Ø, 8.5–13 mm length; healed-ridge / delayed / immediate placement; screw- or cement-retained (RMGIC); PFM, zirconia, or lithium disilicate.
- **Outcome assessment**: proximal contact integrity by floss + 21 μm articulating paper (tight / normal / open by floss resistance + visual gap). Patient-reported food impaction, discomfort VAS 0–10, 5-point Likert satisfaction. Annual standardized periapical radiographs for crestal/proximal bone. Periodontal probing, BoP, recession, adjacent-tooth mobility.
- **Follow-up**: 2 wk, 3 mo, 6 mo, then annual.
- **Statistics**: Kaplan–Meier (contact survival), chi-square/Fisher, t-test/Mann–Whitney, multivariate regression for open-contact risk factors. α=0.05; post-hoc power >80% to detect ≥20% difference.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Open contacts at final evaluation**: 2/75 (2.7%) MM-DD vs 23/49 (46.7%) control (p<0.001). By location 1.9% maxillary, 2.8% mandibular posterior.
- **Mean time to open contact**: MM-DD 8.3 ± 3.4 yr (range 4.2–16.8); control 3.6 ± 1.8 yr (range 0.9–8.1).
- **Kaplan–Meier contact survival**: MM-DD 99.1% (5 yr) / 97.5% (10 yr) / 96.2% (15 yr); control 66.7% (5 yr) / 53.3% (10 yr).
- **>10-yr subgroup**: open contacts 2.9% MM-DD vs 60.0% control (p<0.001).
- **Symptoms (open vs closed contact)**: food impaction 86.7% vs 3.2% (p<0.001); discomfort VAS 4.7 ± 2.0 vs 0.8 ± 0.5 (p<0.001); satisfaction Likert 2.5 ± 0.7 vs 4.7 ± 0.4 (p<0.001).
- **Group symptoms (MM-DD vs control)**: food impaction VAS 0.8 ± 0.6 vs 4.7 ± 1.1; satisfaction 4.7 vs 2.5 (p<0.001).
- **Bone/probing**: open contact → +0.83 ± 0.31 mm proximal bone loss (p<0.001), +1.1 ± 0.4 mm probing depth at adjacent teeth (p<0.001). MM-DD marginal bone change <0.3 mm at 5 yr vs 0.9 mm control (p<0.05). No implant failures in either group.
- **Subgroups**: younger (24–40 yr) lower open-contact rate 1.8% vs >60 yr 2.9% (p=0.042); second molars 3.4% > first molars 2.6% > premolars 2.0% (p=0.021); no difference by sex or restorative material.
- **Salvage for the 2.7% that failed**: proximal recontouring, MM-DD re-adjustment (more aggressive), crown replacement with enhanced contours, or watch-and-wait for mild asymptomatic cases.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single private practice, single experienced clinician → limited generalizability; retrospective design (selection/recall bias); non-randomized historical control.
- Individual variation in tooth morphology, arch form, bone density, occlusal force, parafunction not fully controlled.
- Restricted to single-tooth posterior implants — multi-unit / anterior applicability unknown.
- Conventional articulating-paper marking only; no digital occlusal analysis (T-Scan) or FEA to quantify force redistribution.
- Authors call for prospective multi-center RCTs vs conventional adjustment with digital occlusal measurement.

## 6. Related Work

- Reported open-contact incidence 34–66% in posterior implants (Koori, Byun); mandibular more susceptible.
- Reactive strategies critiqued: Greenstein & Cavallaro (static proximal-contact reinforcement), Wat et al. (proximal morphology modification) — neither addresses occlusal force dynamics.
- Downstream-harm framing consistent with Pang et al. and Monje et al. (food impaction, peri-implant inflammation, reduced QoL).

## 7. Glossary

- **Open (proximal) contact**: loss of interproximal contact between an implant crown and adjacent natural tooth; floss passes without resistance, often with visible gap and food impaction.
- **Mesial drift**: physiologic mesial migration of PDL-supported natural teeth under functional load; absent in ankylosed osseointegrated implants, producing the implant–dentition mismatch that opens contacts.
- **MM-DD (mesial–mesial/distal–distal) occlusal adjustment**: at crown delivery, relieve the mesial occlusal surface of the mesial adjacent tooth and the distal occlusal surface of the distal adjacent tooth, creating relief zones that let adjacent teeth drift mesially toward the implant crown and keep the contact closed.
- **PCL (Proximal Contact Loss)**: synonym used elsewhere in this wiki for open proximal contact.
