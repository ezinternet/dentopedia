## Why Ingested

This is the origin/cornerstone RCT of the Watanabe Toothpick method (T. Watanabe, the method's eponym, is a coauthor) and the single direct head-to-head trial showing the Toothpick method removes more proximal plaque than the Bass method. It supplies the empirical primary-study evidence that the systematic review [[periodontics/rajwani-2020-manual-toothbrushing-techniques-plaque-gingivitis-sr]] cites, and anchors the clinical-outcome line carried forward by [[periodontics/lee-2020-watanabe-toothpick-method-srp-diabetic-periodontitis-rct]].

## One-line Summary

RCT (n=20 dental students, split-mouth, modified Quigley-Hein index): the Toothpick toothbrushing method removed significantly more proximal-surface plaque than the Bass method.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Cornerstone primary RCT of the Watanabe **Toothpick method** of toothbrushing, coauthored by its eponym T. Watanabe.
- First direct head-to-head comparison of the Toothpick method against the **Bass method** for supragingival plaque removal.
- Demonstrates the Toothpick method's key advantage is at the **proximal (interproximal) tooth surfaces** — the sites both methods historically struggle with and where the Toothpick technique's brush-tip insertion is intended to work.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Subjects**: 20 male university dental students.
- **Pre-experiment washout**: all subjects abstained from all oral hygiene procedures for 48 h before each experiment.
- **Design**: two experiments.
  - **Exp. I (professional brushing)**: a dentist brushed each subject's mouth — 2 quadrants with the Toothpick method, the remaining 2 quadrants with the Bass method (split-mouth). Total brushing time 2 min per mouth.
  - **Exp. II (self-brushing)**: subjects brushed their own teeth after receiving toothbrushing instruction.
- **Outcome measure**: dental plaque scored by a **blind examiner** using a **modification of the Quigley and Hein plaque index** at **6 sites around each tooth**.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- The Toothpick method removed **significantly more plaque from proximal tooth surfaces** than the Bass method.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small homogeneous sample (n=20, all male dental students) — limited external validity to general patients.
- Short-term, plaque-only outcome; no gingivitis, bleeding, or longitudinal endpoints in this study.
- Abstract-only ingestion: numeric effect sizes and statistical detail unavailable from the source text.

## 7. Glossary

- **Toothpick method (Watanabe method)**: toothbrushing technique inserting the brush tip into interproximal/embrasure spaces in a toothpick-like motion to clean proximal surfaces and gingival margins.
- **Bass method**: standard intrasulcular toothbrushing technique with bristles angled ~45° toward the gingival sulcus.
- **Quigley-Hein plaque index (modified, Turesky)**: scores supragingival plaque on a 0–5 scale; this study used 6 sites per tooth.
- **Split-mouth design**: each subject receives both interventions in different quadrants, serving as their own control.
