## Why Ingested

기존 [[wiki/caries/konukman-turker-2026-hall-vs-modified-hall-primary-molars-rct]]는 Hall 기법 내부 변형(conventional Hall vs modified Hall)을 비교했다. 본 Mohamed 2026 RCT는 한 단계 위 질문 — 전통적 삭제 기법(Conventional Technique, CT) vs Hall 기법(Hall Technique, HT) — 을 12개월 비열등성 설계로 직접 비교해, 삭제 없는 Hall 접근의 임상 성공·생존이 전통 기법과 동등함을 보강한다. 또한 HT 직후 발생하는 교합 수직고경(Occlusal Vertical Dimension, OVD) 상승이 1개월 내 자연 회복됨을 정량화해 임상 우려에 답한다. (abstract-only)

## One-line Summary

12-month non-inferiority RCT (n=80 carious primary molars, 73 returned): preformed metal crowns by conventional vs Hall technique both achieved 100% "Successful" clinical outcomes and 100% survival; HT-induced OVD increase (1.18±0.68 mm) reverted to baseline within 1 month (P=0.32).

## 한줄요약

12개월 비열등성 RCT (유구치 80개, 73개 추적): 기성 금속관(Preformed Metal Crown, PMC)을 전통 삭제 기법(CT)과 Hall 기법(HT)으로 수복한 두 군 모두 임상 성공 100%·생존 100%였고, HT 직후 늘어난 교합 수직고경(OVD, 1.18±0.68 mm)은 1개월 내 기저치로 회복되어 차이 없었다(P=0.32).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Head-to-head 12-month RCT directly comparing the **conventional technique (CT)** (caries removal + tooth preparation) against the **Hall technique (HT)** (no caries removal, no preparation, no local anesthesia) for placing preformed metal crowns (PMCs) on carious primary molars.
- Demonstrates **clinical non-inferiority** of the minimally invasive HT: identical "Successful" outcomes and 100% restoration survival in both arms at 12 months.
- Quantifies and resolves a common clinical concern about HT — the **transient occlusal vertical dimension (OVD) increase** immediately after seating (1.18±0.68 mm) that self-corrects within one month.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Non-inferiority, primary care-based, two-arm parallel-group RCT.
- **Sample**: 80 carious primary molars indicated for PMC restoration, randomly allocated to CT and HT groups.
- **Intervention**: Standardized procedural techniques in both arms; PMCs placed by CT (conventional preparation) vs HT (sealing-in without preparation).
- **Follow-up**: 12 months; 73 of 80 participants returned for follow-up assessment.
- **Outcomes**: Clinical outcome graded as Successful / Minor failure / Major failure; restoration survival (functional presence); OVD measurement (baseline, immediately post-HT, and 1 month) for the HT arm.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Recall**: 73/80 participants returned at 12 months.
- **Clinical outcome**: All PMC-restored teeth in BOTH CT and HT groups graded "Successful" — no Major or Minor failures in either arm.
- **Survival**: 100% in both groups — all PMC-restored primary molars functionally present at 12 months.
- **OVD (HT arm)**: average increase immediately after placement = **1.18 ± 0.68 mm**; reverted to pre-treatment values within 1 month, with **no significant difference** between baseline and 1-month OVD (**P = 0.32**).
- **Bottom line**: Clinical success and survival were similar for CT and HT; the non-invasive HT is a viable alternative to the traditional CT.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Abstract-only**: full text not retrieved; group-level n per arm at follow-up, randomization/blinding detail, caries severity stratification, and exact CIs are unavailable.
- Short 12-month horizon; PMC durability differences (if any) may emerge only over the full primary-molar exfoliation period.
- 100%/100% outcomes in both arms mean the trial detected no separation — non-inferiority is supported, but a longer follow-up and larger sample are needed to detect rare late failures.
- OVD analysis reported only for the HT arm.

## 7. Glossary

- **PMC (Preformed Metal Crown)**: prefabricated stainless-steel crown used to restore carious primary molars (a.k.a. stainless steel crown, SSC).
- **CT (Conventional Technique)**: traditional PMC placement involving caries removal and tooth preparation under local anesthesia.
- **HT (Hall Technique)**: minimally invasive PMC placement that seals caries under the crown without caries removal, tooth preparation, or local anesthesia.
- **OVD (Occlusal Vertical Dimension)**: vertical distance between maxillary and mandibular arches at occlusion; transiently increased after HT crown seating.
- **Non-inferiority trial**: design testing whether a new treatment is not unacceptably worse than the standard, within a predefined margin.
