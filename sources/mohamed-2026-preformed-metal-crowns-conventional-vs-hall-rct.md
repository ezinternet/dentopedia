## Why Ingested

기존 [[wiki/caries/konukman-turker-2026-hall-vs-modified-hall-primary-molars-rct]]는 Hall 기법 내부 변형(conventional Hall vs modified Hall)을 비교했다. 본 Mohamed 2026 RCT는 한 단계 위 질문 — 전통적 삭제 기법(Conventional Technique, CT) vs Hall 기법(Hall Technique, HT) — 을 12개월 비열등성 설계로 직접 비교해, 삭제 없는 Hall 접근의 임상 성공·생존이 전통 기법과 동등함을 보강한다. 또한 HT 직후 발생하는 교합 수직고경(Occlusal Vertical Dimension, OVD) 상승이 1개월 내 자연 회복됨을 정량화해 임상 우려에 답한다.

## One-line Summary

12-month non-inferiority RCT (n=80 carious primary molars, 73 returned at 12 months): preformed metal crowns by conventional vs Hall technique both achieved 100% "Successful" clinical outcomes and 100% restoration survival; HT-induced OVD increase (mean 1.18±0.68 mm) reverted to baseline within 1 month (P=0.32).

## 한줄요약

12개월 비열등성 RCT (유구치 80개, 73개 추적): 기성 금속관(Preformed Metal Crown, PMC)을 전통 삭제 기법(CT)과 Hall 기법(HT)으로 수복한 두 군 모두 임상 성공 100%·생존 100%였고, HT 직후 늘어난 교합 수직고경(OVD, 1.18±0.68 mm)은 1개월 내 기저치로 회복되어 차이 없었다(P=0.32).

## 2. Key Contributions

- First primary care-based, open-label, two-arm parallel-group **non-inferiority RCT** directly comparing the **Conventional Technique (CT)** (caries removal, tooth preparation, local anesthesia) against the **Hall Technique (HT)** (no caries removal, no tooth preparation, no local anesthesia) for placing PMCs on carious primary molars at 12-month follow-up.
- Demonstrates **clinical non-inferiority** of the minimally invasive HT: identical "Successful" outcomes (100%) and 100% restoration survival in both arms — no Major or Minor failures in either group.
- Quantifies and resolves the primary clinical concern about HT — the **transient OVD increase** immediately after seating (mean 1.18±0.68 mm) spontaneously self-corrects within one month, returning to pre-treatment baseline (P=0.32).
- Part 2 of a two-part RCT; Part 1 (Mohamed et al. Eur Arch Paediatr Dent 2025) showed HT was significantly superior to CT on procedural time, child pain perception, and parental acceptability. Part 2 (this paper) confirms equivalent 12-month clinical outcomes.
- Contributes to accumulating evidence supporting adoption of HT as a **routine rather than radical** treatment option in paediatric dentistry.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

### Study Design

- **Design**: Non-inferiority, primary care-based, open-label, two-arm parallel-group RCT.
- **Setting**: Paediatric Dental Clinics, Primary Health Care Corporation (PHCC), State of Qatar.
- **Non-inferiority margin**: 15% (based on 94% assumed success rate for both groups from prior RCT by Elamin et al. 2019).
- **Sample size**: Minimum 64 participants required (power 80%, α 0.05); increased to **80** to account for attrition.
- **Randomization**: Permuted block randomization; sequence generated off-site by NP; allocation concealed by sequentially numbered opaque sealed envelopes (SNOSE).
- **Blinding**: Open-label; blinding of participants not possible after allocation. Single operator (HM) for all procedures.

### Participants

- **Ages**: 3–9 years; healthy ASA I or II; cooperative (Frankl Behaviour Rating 3 or 4).
- **Inclusion**: Vital primary molars with active occluso-proximal dentinal caries lesion; asymptomatic or reversible pulpitis; no irreversible pulpitis/infection/swelling; no inter-radicular/periapical pathosis; pre-operative radiograph with ≥1 mm sound dentine band between lesion and pulp.
- **One tooth per child**: 40 per arm (n=40 CT, n=40 HT).
- **Teeth**: Majority second primary molars in both groups; maxillary and mandibular molars included; no significant baseline differences between groups (P>0.05 for age, gender, tooth or jaw type).

### Interventions

**CT group**: UK National Clinical Guidelines in Paediatric Dentistry protocol — local anesthesia, complete caries excavation, occlusal and proximal tooth preparation; 3M stainless steel crowns (3M ESPE, MN, USA) pre-crimped; cemented with low-viscosity GIC (Fuji Triage, GC Corp., Tokyo, Japan).

**HT group**: Hall technique user's manual protocol — no local anesthesia, no caries removal, no tooth preparation; appropriately-sized 3M ESPE PMC cemented with low-viscosity GIC (Fuji Triage) directly over carious molar; child bit down firmly on cotton roll until cement set.

### Outcome Measures

- **Clinical outcome** (1, 6, 9, 12 months): Successful / Minor failure / Major failure (criteria from Innes et al. 2011):
  - Successful: satisfactory PMC, no pulpal signs/symptoms, or PMC-restored tooth exfoliated without failures
  - Major failure: irreversible pulpitis/dental abscess requiring pulp therapy or extraction; restoration loss with unrestorable tooth; new caries at PMC margins; PMC perforation
  - Minor failure: reversible pulpitis manageable without pulp therapy; restoration loss with restorable tooth; PMC-associated ectopic eruption/impaction of first permanent molar
- **Restoration survival**: Functional presence of PMC-restored primary molar at 12 months without major failure.
- **OVD resolution** (HT arm only): Measured with UNC CP15 millimeter probe from lower canine gingival margin to upper canine tip (ipsilateral; contralateral if ipsilateral missing) at baseline, immediately post-PMC, and at 1-month follow-up.

### Statistical Analysis

- Normality: Kolmogorov-Smirnov test.
- Clinical outcomes: McNemar's two-tailed exact probability test (matched pairs).
- Survival: Kaplan-Meier analysis with log-rank (Mantel-Cox) model.
- OVD: Multilevel linear regression (95% CI); variables including age, gender, tooth type (1st/2nd primary molar), jaw (maxillary/mandibular).
- Reliability: Intra-examiner Cohen's kappa.
- Significance: P<0.05; SPSS v24.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Retention

- 73 of 80 (91.3%) returned at 12-month follow-up.
- CT: 36 of 40 returned (90%); HT: 37 of 40 returned (92.5%).
- No significant difference in dropout between groups (P=0.28).

### Clinical Outcomes at 12 Months

| Outcome | CT (n=36) | HT (n=37) | Total (n=73) | P-value |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Successful | 36 (100%) | 37 (100%) | 73 (100%) | 1.0 |
| Minor failure | 0 (0%) | 0 (0%) | 0 (0%) | — |
| Major failure | 0 (0%) | 0 (0%) | 0 (0%) | — |
| Dropout | 4 (10%) | 3 (7.5%) | 7 (8.8%) | 0.28 |

- No Major or Minor failures in either arm.
- Intra-examiner Cohen's kappa = 1.0 for clinical outcome reliability.

### Restoration Survival at 12 Months

- CT: 36/36 (100%) — all crowns functionally present.
- HT: 37/37 (100%) — all crowns functionally present.
- Kaplan-Meier survival curves: flat for both groups.

### OVD Resolution (HT Arm, n=40)

- **Baseline OVD**: mean 3.45 ± 0.85 mm
- **Immediately post-PMC placement**: mean 4.63 ± 1.0 mm
- **Mean OVD increase**: 1.18 ± 0.68 mm (slightly higher for second primary molars vs first, not statistically significant)
- **1-month follow-up OVD**: mean 3.5 ± 0.91 mm
- **Baseline vs 1-month OVD**: no significant difference (P = 0.32)
- Multilevel linear regression: no association between OVD changes and age, gender, tooth type, or jaw type.
- Intra-examiner Cohen's kappa for OVD measurements: 0.95–0.97.

### Contextual Benchmarks from Literature

- Literature 12-month overall success: CT 91.3–100%, HT 88.6–100%.
- Literature 24-month overall success: CT 85.2–86.5%, HT 86.3–88.1%.
- Literature 12-month survival: CT 95.7–100%, HT 96.9–100%.
- Literature 24-month survival: CT 91.4–92.6%, HT 91.7–95.9%.
- OVD re-equilibration after HT reported to resolve within 2–4 weeks in multiple prior studies.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **No blinding** of trial participants or the single treating operator after allocation; potential operator bias.
- **Single operator (HM)** performed all procedures — limits external generalizability across operators.
- **No periodontal health evaluation** or radiographic outcome assessment included.
- **12-month follow-up** may be insufficient to detect rare late failures; PMC durability across full primary molar exfoliation period (typically 5–8 years) requires longer studies.
- **100% success in both arms** resulted in low statistical power for comparative outcome analyses; the trial is powered as non-inferiority, not superiority.
- **OVD analysis only in HT arm** — no comparative OVD data for the CT arm.
- **Single setting** (PHCC Qatar) limits generalizability to other healthcare contexts.

## 7. Glossary

- **PMC (Preformed Metal Crown, 기성 금속관)**: Prefabricated stainless-steel crown used to restore carious primary molars; also called stainless steel crown (SSC).
- **CT (Conventional Technique, 전통 기법)**: Traditional PMC placement involving local anesthesia, complete caries removal, and occlusal/proximal tooth preparation before crown cementation.
- **HT (Hall Technique, Hall 기법)**: Minimally invasive PMC placement that seals caries under the crown without caries removal, tooth preparation, or local anesthesia; introduced by Dr. Norna Hall in the 1980s.
- **OVD (Occlusal Vertical Dimension, 교합 수직고경)**: Vertical distance between maxillary and mandibular arches at occlusion; transiently increased after HT crown seating due to no occlusal tooth reduction.
- **Non-inferiority trial (비열등성 시험)**: RCT design testing whether the new treatment is not unacceptably worse than the standard within a predefined margin (here: 15%).
- **SNOSE (Sequentially Numbered Opaque Sealed Envelopes)**: Allocation concealment method; envelopes numbered sequentially and opened in order at enrollment.
- **Frankl Behaviour Rating (Frankl 행동 등급)**: 4-point scale assessing child cooperation in dental settings; Ratings 3–4 (positive/definitively positive) required for study entry.
- **GIC (Glass Ionomer Cement, 글래스아이오노머 시멘트)**: Luting cement used for PMC cementation in both arms; Fuji Triage (low-viscosity) used here.
