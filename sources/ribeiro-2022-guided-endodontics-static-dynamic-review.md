## One-line Summary
Literature review (63 studies): static navigation (SN) and dynamic navigation (DN) for guided endodontics both offer accurate, minimally invasive access to calcified/complex canals — each has distinct advantages for different clinical scenarios.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Personalized Medicine 2022, 12, 1516
- **DOI**: 10.3390/jpm12091516
- **Institution**: University of Coimbra, Portugal

## 2. Key Contributions
- First comprehensive comparison of static vs dynamic navigation techniques in guided endodontics
- 63 studies included covering SN and DN for root canal access
- Identifies specific clinical indications for each technique
- Guided endodontics particularly valuable for pulp canal obliteration (PCO) cases

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Literature review; PubMed, Scopus, Cochrane Library; 2011–2021; English, Spanish, Portuguese
- Initial: 649 articles (449 PubMed + 168 Scopus + 32 Cochrane); 63 included after full-text analysis
- Outcomes: access accuracy, tooth structure preservation, iatrogenic complication rate

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
**Static Navigation (SN):**
- Uses CBCT-derived 3D-printed guide template
- High precision for pre-planned access; suitable for experienced clinicians
- Less flexible intraoperatively

**Dynamic Navigation (DN):**
- Real-time CBCT-based navigation (like GPS for drills)
- More flexible; better for unexpected anatomy changes
- Higher cost and steeper learning curve

**Key findings:**
- Both SN and DN: highly accurate for PCO and complex canal anatomy
- Guided endodontics > conventional in: less iatrogenic damage, greater tooth structure preservation
- Less experienced operators benefit more from guided techniques
- No cost-benefit or time studies published

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- No RCTs comparing SN vs DN head-to-head
- No cost-benefit or time-efficiency studies
- Rapidly evolving field; older studies may not reflect current systems

## 7. Glossary
- **PCO**: Pulp Canal Obliteration
- **SN**: Static Navigation (3D-printed guide)
- **DN**: Dynamic Navigation (real-time tracking)
- **MIA**: Minimally Invasive Access cavity
- **CBCT**: Cone-Beam Computed Tomography
