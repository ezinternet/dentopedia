## Why Ingested

Complete-denture fabrication is increasingly digital, yet the relative clinical merits of additive (3D printing) versus subtractive (milling) manufacturing remain debated. This SR+MA directly fills a gap in [[complete-denture/alhajj-2017-occlusal-vertical-dimension-complete-denture-review]] which focuses on conventional prosthodontic benchmarks (OVD, retention) without addressing the digital-fabrication comparison; Khorasani 2024 quantifies the retention and satisfaction difference between the two digital methods.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (4 RCTs/clinical studies, n=patients) finds no statistically significant difference in denture retention (SMD=0.165, p=0.343) or patient satisfaction (SMD=−0.595, p=0.236) between 3D-printed and milled complete dentures, though high heterogeneity limits conclusions.

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (4편)에서 3D 프린팅과 밀링으로 제작된 총의치 간 유지력(SMD=0.165, p=0.343) 및 환자 만족도(SMD=−0.595, p=0.236) 차이 없음 — 단, 높은 이질성으로 해석에 주의 필요.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First SR+MA directly comparing retention and patient satisfaction between additive manufacturing (AM/3D printing) and subtractive manufacturing (SM/milling) for complete dentures.
2. Pooled analysis reveals no statistically significant difference in retention (SMD=0.165 N, 95% CI: −0.176 to 0.506, p=0.343, I²=58.72%).
3. Pooled analysis reveals no statistically significant difference in patient satisfaction (SMD=−0.595, 95% CI: −1.579 to 0.389, p=0.236, I²=85.94%).
4. High heterogeneity (I²=58–86%) and very small evidence base (4 eligible studies) preclude definitive conclusions.
5. Risk of bias assessed with Cochrane RoB 2 (RCTs) and ROBINS-I tools; publication bias via Begg's funnel plot and Egger test.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **PICO**: Population=edentulous patients with digitally fabricated complete dentures; Intervention=AM (3D printing); Comparison=SM (milling); Outcomes=retention and patient satisfaction
- **Search strategy**: 6 databases + Google Scholar; English-language only; no date restriction; up to August 2024
- **Screening**: 2 calibrated reviewers independently screened titles/abstracts then full texts; conflicts resolved by consensus or third reviewer
- **Data extraction**: general study details, study design (arch, impression material, scanner, software), AM specifics (print material, technology, postprocessing, build angle, layer thickness), SM specifics (milled material, device settings), sample size, outcome measurements
- **Statistical analysis**: random effects model (DerSimonian-Laird); standardized mean difference (SMD); heterogeneity via Cochran's Q, I², Tau²; publication bias via Begg's funnel plot + Egger test; sensitivity analyses
- **Initial records**: 1,098; after deduplication and screening → 4 eligible studies

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Retention
- Pooled SMD = 0.165 N (95% CI: −0.176 to 0.506)
- P-value = 0.343 (not significant)
- I² = 58.72% (moderate–high heterogeneity)
- Direction: AM slightly higher mean retention numerically, but not significant

### Patient Satisfaction
- Pooled SMD = −0.595 (95% CI: −1.579 to 0.389)
- P-value = 0.236 (not significant)
- I² = 85.94% (very high heterogeneity)
- Direction: SM showed numerically higher patient satisfaction, but not significant

### Risk of Bias
- Assessed with Cochrane RoB 2 (for RCTs) and ROBINS-I (for non-randomized studies)
- Publication bias: Begg's funnel plot and Egger test performed; results interpreted cautiously given n=4 studies

### Conventional vs Digital Context
- Conventional PMMA limitations: microbial colonization risk, resin shrinkage, dimensional changes, 6+ visits required
- SM (milling) advantages: improved surface properties (Candida adherence, roughness, wettability), better mechanical properties, fewer visits
- AM (3D printing) advantages: reduced material waste, greater customization, fewer visits; disadvantages: volumetric shrinkage, distortion during polymerization/postprocessing, lower satisfaction vs conventional in some reports

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

1. **Very small evidence base**: only 4 studies met inclusion criteria; results are preliminary
2. **High heterogeneity**: I²=58–86% in both outcomes; diverse measurement methods, materials, and protocols contribute
3. **English-language restriction**: non-English publications may be relevant
4. **Heterogeneous AM technologies**: different 3D printing materials (resins), technologies (SLA, DLP, FDM), build angles, and layer thicknesses pooled together
5. **Short follow-up**: most included studies had limited longitudinal follow-up
6. **No implant-retained or partial dentures**: results apply only to conventional complete dentures
7. **Future need**: larger, standardized RCTs with longer follow-up; standardized outcome measurement tools for retention and satisfaction

## 6. Related Work

- Studies comparing AM vs SM digital dentures (retention, satisfaction, dimensional accuracy): the 4 included studies cover clinical parameters
- Previous SRs on digital complete dentures have addressed accuracy and fit but not retention/satisfaction specifically in AM vs SM comparison
- Conventional complete denture literature: multiple visits, OVD establishment, retention principles

## 7. Glossary

- **AM (Additive Manufacturing)**: 3D printing/rapid prototyping — builds objects layer by layer from digital files; includes SLA (stereolithography), DLP (digital light processing), FDM (fused deposition modeling)
- **SM (Subtractive Manufacturing)**: milling/CAD-CAM — carves denture from pre-fabricated PMMA pucks
- **PMMA**: Polymethyl methacrylate — conventional and digital denture base material
- **SMD (Standardized Mean Difference)**: meta-analytic effect size measure used when outcomes are on different scales
- **I²**: measure of statistical heterogeneity; >75% = very high, 50–75% = moderate
- **RoB 2**: Cochrane Risk of Bias tool version 2 (for randomized trials)
- **ROBINS-I**: Risk of Bias in Non-randomized Studies of Interventions tool
- **PROSPERO**: International Prospective Register of Systematic Reviews
- **Retention**: the resistance of a denture to displacement along its path of insertion; key functional parameter
