## One-line Summary
Case report (n=1, 29-y female): a ~25 mm cyst-like periradicular lesion involving two maxillary molars healed completely by 9 months after single-visit non-surgical orthograde root canal therapy (no intracanal medicament), with sinus tract resolution in 2 weeks.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Demonstrates that a large (~25 mm) cyst-like periradicular lesion, most likely an infected radicular cyst of endodontic origin, can heal completely without endodontic surgery.
- Reinforces that correct diagnosis plus effective orthograde root canal treatment of the infected canal system is sufficient for healing of large lesions.
- Shows single-visit RCT without intracanal medicament achieving full radiographic and clinical resolution.
- Highlights the contributory role of prompt (within 2 weeks) permanent coronal restoration to a successful endodontic outcome.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Patient:** 29-year-old healthy female; chief complaint of mild pain in the right maxillary sinus area; no significant medical history.
- **Findings:** Right maxillary first molar non-vital (negative electric pulp test and cavity test), not previously root-treated, non-carious; adjacent second molar had poor prior endodontic treatment with incomplete obturation. Minor firm vestibular swelling, buccal sinus tract with purulent exudate; teeth non-tender, non-mobile.
- **Imaging:** Gutta-percha cone used to trace sinus tract on periapical x-ray; panoramic radiograph revealed a well-circumscribed radiolucency ~25 mm in diameter extending from distal of second premolar to distal of second maxillary molar; first molar showed profound root resorption.
- **Treatment:** Single-visit RCT of both molars. Access cavity, rubber dam; no exudate from canals. Instrumentation with Flexo-File #15–40 (step-back technique) with copious sterile normal saline irrigation. Working length by radiographs. Obturation with gutta-percha and Roth's 801 sealer by lateral condensation. Permanent amalgam restoration at 2 weeks.
- **Recall:** 1 day, 2 weeks, 9 months, 12 months.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Signs/symptoms including the sinus tract resolved within 2 weeks.
- At 9-month and 1-year recalls: no signs/symptoms; panoramic and periapical radiographs showed complete bony regression of the lesion (increased density, trabecular regeneration).
- No sensitivity to percussion or palpation at follow-up.
- Cited context: ~74% of 42 endodontically treated teeth showed bony healing of large periradicular lesions in one study; 2008 Cochrane review found no significant difference between single- vs multiple-visit RCT radiologic success.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Single case (n=1); no histologic confirmation — radicular cyst diagnosis was clinical/radiographic (nonvital teeth, >1.6 mm diameter, radiopaque border).
- Healing assessed with conventional 2D radiography; authors note complete healing is difficult to confirm with conventional radiographic techniques.
- Per Calişkan, prognosis for large periradicular lesions may be lower; outcomes case-specific (young, healthy patient favors healing).

## 7. Glossary
- **Periradicular/periapical lesion:** Inflammatory bony radiolucency around the root apex from pulpal infection.
- **Radicular cyst:** Inflammatory odontogenic cyst arising from epithelial rests at the apex of a non-vital tooth.
- **Orthograde (non-surgical) RCT:** Root canal treatment performed through the crown, without apical surgery.
- **Sinus tract:** Drainage channel from a periapical abscess to a mucosal/cutaneous surface.
- **Lateral condensation:** Obturation technique compacting gutta-percha laterally with accessory cones.
- **Step-back technique:** Canal shaping with progressively larger files at progressively shorter lengths.
