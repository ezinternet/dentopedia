## One-line Summary
Narrative review (Periodontology 2000): synthesizes TSFE foundational principles, treatment planning, surgical execution, postoperative management, emerging technologies. Provides **standardized TSFE outcome-reporting checklist** for clinicians and researchers.

## 1. Document Information
University of Michigan group (Stuhr, Saleh, Testori, Wang, Decker). Companion to Decker 2024 scoping review. Open-access.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive synthesis of TSFE evolution since 1980s introduction
- Examines osteotome-mediated bone condensation and controlled sinus floor fracture as primary stability mechanisms
- Addresses treatment planning, surgical execution, postoperative management
- Reviews emerging technologies and materials
- **Proposes standardized clinical and outcome-reporting checklist** for TSFE

## 3. Methodology / Scope
- Narrative review
- TSFE indication: RBH ≥4 mm + bone width ≥5 mm (subject to modification with new tech)
- Synthesis of long-term stability factors

## 4. Key Points
- TSFE = minimally invasive alternative to lateral window
- Osteotome condensation increases peri-implant bone density → enhanced primary stability
- Long-term stability depends on:
  - Patient selection (RBH, anatomy, systemic health)
  - Surgical execution (membrane management, depth control)
  - Postoperative management
  - Graft material choice (or no graft)
- Proposed standardized outcome reporting will improve research consistency

## 5. Limitations
- Narrative review, not SR+MA
- Subjective synthesis
- Checklist requires validation
