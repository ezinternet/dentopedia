## One-line Summary
Comprehensive review of non-surgical and surgical treatment approaches for chronic periodontitis with evidence-based outcomes.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Literature review/educational article
- **Year**: 2009 (Korean Journal of Aesthetic Dentistry)
- **Subject**: Therapeutic management of chronic periodontitis
- **Scope**: Comparative analysis of treatment modalities

## 2. Key Contributions
- Establishes hierarchy of periodontal disease treatment: non-surgical as primary therapy, surgical as adjunctive
- Documents evidence for non-surgical periodontal therapy efficacy
- Defines indications for surgical intervention when non-surgical fails
- Reviews mechanisms by which both approaches achieve periodontal health
- Provides clinical decision-making framework for treatment selection

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Evidence Base**: Published literature on periodontitis treatment outcomes
- **Non-surgical Treatment Components**:
  - Mechanical debridement (scaling and root planing with hand or ultrasonic instruments)
  - Biofilm control and patient oral hygiene
  - Subgingival antimicrobial therapy (adjunctive)
- **Surgical Treatment Options**:
  - Periodontal regeneration (grafting, membrane techniques)
  - Periodontal access flaps for deep pocket elimination
  - Guided tissue regeneration (GTR)
  - Osseous surgery
- **Outcome Measures**: Probing depth reduction, clinical attachment level gain, bleeding reduction, bacterial composition changes

## 4. Key Results and Findings
- Non-surgical therapy alone achieves:
  - Probing depth reduction
  - Clinical attachment level improvement
  - Gingival inflammation reduction
  - Shift toward healthier microbial flora
- Axelsson and Lindhe studies: Long-term improvements maintained with non-surgical approach
- Badersten et al.: Similar outcomes with and without surgery for many cases
- Surgical therapy reserved for:
  - Residual pockets >4mm after non-surgical therapy
  - Need for periodontal regeneration
  - Access limitations requiring surgical visibility
  - Esthetic requirements

## 5. Clinical Implications
- Non-surgical therapy is primary treatment for chronic periodontitis
- Mandatory re-evaluation phase critical—assess response before surgical intervention
- Patient motivation and compliance essential for sustained results
- Supportive periodontal therapy (SPT) maintains treatment outcomes long-term
- Surgical intervention rates decreased with evidence-based non-surgical approach
- Combined approach (non-surgical + surgical) most effective for advanced disease

## 6. Limitations and Future Work
- Outcomes vary based on patient compliance
- Long-term maintenance requires ongoing professional care and patient self-care
- Response to therapy influenced by systemic factors (smoking, diabetes, etc.)
- Need for standardized outcome reporting across studies
- Clinical trial data for newer antimicrobial agents

## 7. Glossary
- **Chronic periodontitis (만성 치주염)**: Bacterial infection of periodontal tissues with slow progression and attachment loss
- **Non-surgical therapy (비외과적 치료)**: Mechanical and antimicrobial treatment without surgical flaps
- **Scaling and root planing (치석제거 및 치근활택술)**: Mechanical removal of subgingival biofilm and calculus
- **Probing pocket depth (치주낭 깊이)**: Measurement from gingival margin to base of pocket
- **Clinical attachment level (임상부착수준)**: Measurement of periodontal support loss
- **Supportive periodontal therapy (주기적 치주치료)**: Ongoing professional care to maintain treatment outcomes
