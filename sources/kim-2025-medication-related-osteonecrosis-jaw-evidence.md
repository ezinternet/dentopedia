## One-line Summary

Consensus statement by 5 Korean societies providing Korean-adapted evidence-based guidelines for MRONJ prevention, diagnosis, and management — including drug-class-specific prophylactic drug holiday recommendations and advocacy for early surgical intervention across all MRONJ stages.

## 한줄요약

한국 5개 학회 공동 2025 MRONJ 포지션 스테이트먼트 — 약제별 예방적 drug holiday 기준(oral BP 2개월, IV zoledronate 6-12개월, denosumab 수술 3-4개월 전), 전 병기 적극 수술 권고, CTX 무용론 재확인.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: J Korean Assoc Oral Maxillofac Surg (JKAOMS) 2025;51(6):333-353
- **Published**: 2025-12-31
- **Societies**: Korean Society for Bone and Mineral Research, Korean Association of Oral and Maxillofacial Surgeons, Korean Association of Maxillofacial Plastic and Reconstructive Surgeons, Korean Society of Osteoporosis, Korean Endocrine Society
- **Method**: Systematic literature review + 3 rounds of formal Delphi-type surveys
- **Funding/COI**: Not reported in abstract

## 2. Key Contributions

- **Korean drug holiday specificity**: Provides concrete timings tailored to each drug class (not just "may consider" as in AAOMS 2022), including Korean nationwide cohort data showing IV zoledronate risk doesn't appreciably decline until 6 months, significant reduction only ≥12 months
- **Denosumab timing**: Explicitly recommends scheduling invasive dental procedure 3-4 months after last Dmab dose (bone turnover declining, before rebound fracture risk window at 6-9 months) — clearer than AAOMS 2022
- **SERM bridge strategy**: Recommends interim raloxifene for high-fracture-risk patients requiring extended Dmab holiday — novel vs AAOMS 2022
- **Stage 1 surgery advocacy**: Among 92 Stage 1 lesions managed conservatively, 91.3% still had exposed bone and many progressed — supports surgical intervention even at Stage 1
- **Diagnostic 8-week addendum**: Allows MRONJ diagnosis without the 8-week wait criterion if necrotic bone is confirmed clinically or radiographically

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Multidisciplinary consensus — systematic literature review + 3-round formal expert survey (Delphi-type)
- **Population**: Korean clinical context — aging population, Korean insurance/drug formulary environment
- **Evidence synthesis**: International evidence (AAOMS, JBMR, MASCC) + Korean-specific cohort data (600,000 BP patient nationwide cohort; Korean IV BP timing cohort)
- **Scope**: Prevention, diagnosis (criteria + staging), epidemiology, pathogenesis, risk factors, drug holidays (prophylactic + therapeutic), treatment modalities, sequential therapy strategies

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Epidemiology (Korean data)**:
- Korean nationwide cohort (600,000 BP patients): 254 MRONJ cases = 0.04% prevalence
- Oral BPs: 0.10% prevalence, 36.6/100,000 person-years incidence
- Denosumab (osteoporosis): 0-0.18% prevalence; FREEDOM-extension 10yr 0.3% (5.2/10,000 patient-years)
- Cancer/bone metastases (zoledronate): 0.7-4.0%; Xgeva: 0.8-2.0%; real-world 5-12.6%
- BP→Dmab switch: significantly higher risk than monotherapy

**Drug holiday recommendations (Table 3)**:
| Drug | Prophylactic holiday | Post-op resumption |
|---|---|---|
| Oral BP (low risk/short use) | None | 6-8 weeks |
| Oral BP (prolonged/high risk factors) | 2 months before | 6-8 weeks |
| IV Zoledronate (elective) | 6-12 months before | 6-8 weeks |
| Denosumab | 3-4 months after last dose | 6-8 weeks |

**Drug holiday evidence**: SR 2022 RR 0.73 (95% CI 0.51-1.06) — not statistically significant. Korean IV BP cohort: ibandronate risk declines ~90 days; zoledronate not until 6 months, significant ≥12 months.

**Prevention**: Pre-treatment dental evaluation reduces MRONJ by 77.3% in cancer patients (meta-analysis cited).

**Conservative vs surgical (Stage 1)**: 91.3% of conservatively managed Stage 1 cases still had exposed bone; many progressed.

**Therapeutic drug holiday**: Meta-analysis (13 studies) — complete cure 85% with holiday vs 56.4% without. Korean retrospective (54 MRONJ patients): longer holiday (median 9 months) → better surgical prognosis.

**CTX**: NOT recommended for MRONJ risk assessment — large-scale studies and meta-analyses show it is not a reliable predictor.

**Teriparatide**: Strongest adjunctive evidence — RCT: 6-month teriparatide (20 µg/day) + surgery promoted MRONJ resolution significantly vs surgery alone.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Consensus-based; drug holiday recommendations rest on relatively sparse RCT data — the underlying SR (RR 0.73, not significant) does not definitively prove benefit
- Korean-specific: applicability may differ in non-Asian populations
- No direct RCT evidence for SERM bridging strategy; proposed based on mechanism
- Drug holiday duration for denosumab in oncology patients is extrapolated; high fracture risk makes prolonged holiday dangerous

## 6. Related Work

- Ruggiero 2022 (AAOMS) — primary international guideline this statement extends to Korean context
- JBMR/MASCC 2022 position statements — compared and integrated
- FREEDOM-extension trial — Dmab 10-year incidence data cited
- Korean nationwide BP cohort — IV BP timing data backbone of zoledronate recommendation
- Hwang 2023 Korean retrospective (54 MRONJ patients) — drug holiday prognosis data

## 7. Glossary

- **MRONJ**: Medication-Related Osteonecrosis of the Jaw
- **BMA**: Bone-Modifying Agent (includes BPs and denosumab)
- **Stage 0**: Non-specific symptoms without exposed bone (retained in Korean staging)
- **Drug holiday (prophylactic)**: Pausing antiresorptive before invasive dental procedure
- **Drug holiday (therapeutic)**: Pausing antiresorptive after MRONJ diagnosis to facilitate healing
- **SERM**: Selective Estrogen Receptor Modulator (e.g., raloxifene) — proposed interim agent during denosumab holiday to buffer rebound fracture risk
- **Rebound fracture risk window**: 6-9 months after last Dmab dose — VF risk spikes if holiday extends beyond this
