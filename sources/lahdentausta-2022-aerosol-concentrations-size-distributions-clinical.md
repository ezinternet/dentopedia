## Why Ingested

사용자 요청: 하이스피드(에어터빈)·로우스피드 치과 엔진(핸드피스) 관련 논문을 PubMed 풀텍스트로 인제스트. 신규 `dental-handpiece` 카테고리의 첫 임상 정량 근거 — 84명 253건 실제 진료에서 에어터빈 vs 하이스피드 vs 로우스피드 핸드피스의 에어로졸 생성량을 입자크기별로 직접 측정한 드문 real-clinical 데이터. 같은 카테고리의 manikin 실험 [[dental-handpiece/matys-2020-dental-aerosol-hazard-risk-dental]]를 실제 환자 데이터로 보강·대비한다.

## One-line Summary

Clinical observational study (84 patients, 253 procedures, optical particle sizer) finding air turbine handpieces generated the highest <1 μm and total aerosol concentrations, while high- and low-speed handpieces and ultrasonic scalers elevated aerosol vs oral examination — supporting substitution of air turbine with high-speed handpiece in poor epidemic conditions.

## 한줄요약

실제 진료 관찰연구(환자 84명·253건, 광학입자측정기) — 에어터빈 핸드피스가 <1 μm 미세·총 에어로졸을 가장 많이 생성했고 하이스피드·로우스피드 핸드피스와 초음파 스케일러도 구강검진 대비 에어로졸을 유의하게 높였으며, 감염 위험 상황에서 에어터빈을 하이스피드 핸드피스로 대체할 것을 시사.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Real-clinical (not bench/manikin) quantification of aerosol generation across 8 procedure types on 84 actual patients with routine high-volume suction in use.
- Direct head-to-head of air turbine vs high-speed vs low-speed handpiece by particle size class (<1 μm, 1–5 μm, >5 μm, total) and by particle mass.
- Air turbine = dominant generator of sub-micron (<1 μm) aerosol; low-speed handpiece preferentially raises coarse (>5 μm) particle/mass.
- Quantified fallow time/half-life: median half-life ~21.7 min; estimated air-turbine fallow time ~98.5 min — far longer than routine room turnover.
- Provides evidence base for the pandemic recommendation to substitute air turbine with electric high-speed handpiece when rubber dam is impossible.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Design: prospective clinical observational aerosol measurement study.
- Population: 84 patients (54 F / 30 M; mean age 53.3 ± 15.9 y); 253 procedures; 3 dentists; 6 operating rooms (ACR 2.76–6.19 /h).
- Instrument: Optical Particle Sizer TSI Model 3330 (0.3–10 μm, 16 channels), ~50 cm above patient head; 1-min background before each procedure.
- Procedure classes (n): oral exam (52), air turbine (8), high-speed (6), low-speed (30), ultrasonic scaling (31), hand-instrument periodontal (60), endodontic (12), intraoral radiograph (24), local anesthesia (31). Min 6 per class.
- Outcomes: particle number + mass concentrations in <1 μm / 1–5 μm / >5 μm / total; half-life and emission-rate modeling (Indoor Aerosol Model).
- Statistics: non-parametric Mann-Whitney / Kruskal-Wallis, SPSS v27; medians/IQR (skewed data); outliers excluded from maxima.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Air turbine: significantly elevated <1 μm median (p=0.013) and maximum (p=0.016) number concentrations and <1 μm particle mass (p=0.046, p=0.006) vs background; total particle mass increase NS.
- Low-speed: elevated >5 μm median (p=0.023) and maximum (p=0.013) number, >5 μm mass (p=0.021), maximum total particle mass (p=0.022).
- High-speed: no significant elevation vs its own background, BUT significant vs oral examination.
- Ultrasonic scaler: slight >5 μm maximum number/mass elevation.
- Significant concentration peaks: air turbine 7/8; low-speed 2/30; high-speed 3/6; root canal 6/12.
- Emission rate: air turbine ~5.2×10⁸ #/min; root canal ~1.5×10⁸ #/min.
- Half-life (22 drilling ops, turbine+low-speed) median 21.7 min (8.5–47.9); air-turbine fallow time est. ~98.5 min.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Did not assay whether aerosols contained viruses/microbes — no infectivity estimate.
- Six rooms with differing ventilation/volume (mitigated by per-procedure background subtraction + room-size in emission model).
- High background aerosol (1970s building, non-surgical ventilation) may underestimate small changes.
- Single device; size range capped at 10 μm (no coarse >10–100 μm data); small n for high-speed (6) and air turbine (8) limits power for those subgroups.

## 6. Related Work

- Bench/manikin handpiece-aerosol studies (Matys & Grzech-Leśniak 2020) — complementary controlled comparison.
- High-volume evacuation efficacy literature (≥300 l/min reduces spray mist).
- Dental-unit-waterline waterborne-infection reports (legionellosis, pneumonia).

## 7. Glossary

- AGP: aerosol-generating procedure.
- OPS: optical particle sizer.
- Air turbine handpiece: air-driven high-speed (~250,000–400,000 rpm) handpiece; here the highest sub-micron aerosol generator.
- High-speed (electric) handpiece: electric-micromotor-driven high-speed handpiece; proposed lower-aerosol substitute for air turbine.
- Low-speed handpiece: micromotor contra-angle for polishing/caries/endo; here raises coarse (>5 μm) particles.
- Fallow time: interval needed for aerosol to clear before the next patient.
- ACR/ACH: air change rate per hour.
