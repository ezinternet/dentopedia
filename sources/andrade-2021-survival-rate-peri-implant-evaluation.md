## Why Ingested

USER EMPHASIS — diabetes & dental treatment. Extends the diabetes-implant evidence base ([[drug/systemic-disease/al-ansari-2022-diabetes-mellitus-dental-implants-sr-ma]]) specifically to the **immediate-loading (IL)** protocol, which prior diabetes-implant SR/MAs largely treated under conventional loading. Adds the answer to "is immediate loading safe in DM2?" — a question general diabetes-implant pages do not resolve.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (7 studies qualitative / 5 quantitative, ~2020) finds immediately loaded dental implants in type 2 diabetics show no survival difference vs nondiabetics (RR 1.00, 95% CI 0.96–1.04), even with poor glycemic control (RR 1.08), and no MBL difference vs conventional loading.

- **Title**: Survival rate and peri-implant evaluation of immediately loaded dental implants in individuals with type 2 diabetes mellitus: a systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Authors**: Andrade CAS, Paz JLC, de Melo GS, Mahrouseh N, Januário AL, Capeletti LR
- **Journal**: Clinical Oral Investigations 2021;26(2):1797–1810
- **DOI**: 10.1007/s00784-021-04154-6
- **PMID**: 34586502 | **PMCID**: PMC8479496
- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (PRISMA, PROSPERO CRD42021223736)
- **Risk of bias tool**: Joanna Briggs Institute (JBI) Critical Appraisal Tools

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First SR+MA dedicated to immediate loading (IL) of dental implants in DM2** — prior diabetes-implant reviews focused on conventional loading.
2. Three pooled analyses: (a) IL survival in DM2 vs nondiabetic; (b) IL survival in **uncontrolled** DM2 vs nondiabetic; (c) MBL of IL vs conventional loading (CL) in DM2 at 12 months.
3. Bottom line: DM2 — even uncontrolled — does **not** appear to be a risk factor for IL survival when surgical/prosthetic protocols and oral hygiene are maintained.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search** (04 Jan 2021): MEDLINE/PubMed, Cochrane, BVS, Web of Science, Scopus, LIVIVO + gray literature (ProQuest, OpenGray, Google Scholar, hand search).
- **Screening**: 3566 records → 2210 after de-dup → 49 full-text → **7 qualitative, 5 quantitative**. Two blinded reviewers; Cohen's κ = 0.87.
- **Eligibility**: human studies (clinical trials, cohort, case-control, cross-sectional, case series) reporting survival/success or peri-implant measures (BOP, PD, MBL) of IL in DM2. Excluded reviews, animal/in-vitro, case reports, non-DM2-specified, merged-data studies.
- **Included studies**: Portugal, Lebanon, Spain, Saudi Arabia, Egypt, Romania; published 2008–2020; follow-up mostly 6–24 mo (one 5-yr, one 12-yr); n = 4–108 patients; 16–352 implants; prostheses single/partial/overdenture/All-on-Four.
- **Statistics**: RevMan 5.3; RR for survival (count), MD for MBL (continuous); 95% CI; I² for heterogeneity (>50% high, <25% low).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **IL survival, DM2 vs nondiabetic**: RR = **1.00**, 95% CI 0.96–1.04; p = 0.91; **I² = 0%** (no difference; studies ≥24 mo follow-up).
- **IL survival, uncontrolled DM2 vs nondiabetic**: RR = **1.08**, 95% CI 0.87–1.33; p = 0.48; **I² = 70%** (no significant difference; high heterogeneity).
- **MBL, IL vs CL in DM2 at 12 mo**: MD = **−0.08 mm**, 95% CI −0.25 to 0.08; p = 0.33; **I² = 83%** (no difference; only 2 studies).
- Survival 100% in 5/7 studies; 86.3–100% in the other two (lower = uncontrolled DM2 group). Success rate reported in 4 studies, all 100%.
- All 5 quantitative studies rated **low risk of bias** (JBI); weakest items were confounding control and statistical appropriateness.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Very few primary studies on IL in DM2; **max 3 studies in any meta-analysis**.
- High heterogeneity in the uncontrolled-DM2 (I² 70%) and MBL (I² 83%) analyses; e.g., Tawil included only 1 uncontrolled patient; Aguilar-Salvatierra found 86.5% survival in uncontrolled group; a third study's uncontrolled patients improved HbA1c during follow-up (confounding the "uncontrolled" label).
- Mostly ≤24-month follow-up — limits long-term/late peri-implantitis inference (though early IL failures cluster in the first months, so 24 mo is adequate for osseointegration failure).
- Mixed designs (observational vs clinical trials); heterogeneous success-rate definitions. Authors call for homogeneous primary studies.

## 6. Related Work

- Reinforces / extends [[drug/systemic-disease/al-ansari-2022-diabetes-mellitus-dental-implants-sr-ma]] — applies the diabetes-implant question to the immediate-loading protocol.
- Contrasts a 2020 nondiabetic SR+MA showing reduced crestal bone loss with conventional vs immediate loading — not reproduced here within DM2.
- Cites divergent prior SRs: one (2019) reports higher peri-implant disease risk in DM2; another (2016) reports similar success to healthy patients.

## 7. Glossary

- **IL** — immediate loading: prosthetic element placed within 72 h of implant surgery.
- **CL** — conventional (delayed) loading.
- **DM2** — type 2 diabetes mellitus.
- **MBL / BOP / PD** — marginal bone loss / bleeding on probing / probing depth.
- **AGEs** — advanced glycation end products; accumulate in hyperglycemia, impairing bone remodeling and vascularization.
- **HbA1c** — glycated hemoglobin; glycemic-control marker.
- **RR / MD / I²** — risk ratio / mean difference / heterogeneity index.
