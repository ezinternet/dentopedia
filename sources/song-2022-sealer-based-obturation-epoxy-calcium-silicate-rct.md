## Why Ingested

Seeds the wiki's first CeraSeal / bioceramic calcium-silicate-sealer cluster with a head-to-head RCT (CeraSeal and EndoSeal TCS vs AH Plus and ADseal) reporting void, sealer-extrusion, and postoperative-pain outcomes. Anchors the clinical (in-vivo) end of the bioceramic-sealer evidence ladder against in-vitro siblings, complementing [[endodontics/spinelli-2024-three-year-single-cone-ceraseal-cohort]] (3-year single-cone CeraSeal survival) and [[endodontics/abada-2025-obturation-techniques-post-obturation-pain-rct]] (obturation technique vs post-obturation pain). Source: PubMed / PMC, DOI [10.3390/ma15155146](https://doi.org/10.3390/ma15155146).

## One-line Summary

RCT (80 enrolled, 71 analyzed teeth, 4 sealer arms n=20: AH Plus, ADseal, CeraSeal, EndoSeal TCS) found no significant difference between calcium-silicate-based and epoxy-resin-based sealers in void, sealer extrusion, or 3-month postoperative pain — filling-quality variation tracked product properties, not sealer class.

## 한줄요약

RCT(등록 80개, 분석 71개 치아, 4개 실러군 각 n=20: AH Plus·ADseal·CeraSeal·EndoSeal TCS): 칼슘실리케이트 실러와 에폭시레진 실러 사이에 기포·실러 압출·3개월 술후통증의 유의한 차이가 없었고, 충전 품질 차이는 실러 종류가 아니라 제품 특성에 따랐다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First clinical head-to-head RCT in this wiki comparing two **calcium-silicate-based sealers (CSBS)** — **CeraSeal** (Meta Biomed) and **EndoSeal TCS** (Maruchi) — against two **epoxy-resin-based sealers (ERBS)** — **AH Plus** (Dentsply Sirona, the gold standard) and **ADseal** (Meta Biomed).
- All four sealers delivered with a **sealer-based obturation** (single-cone) technique, isolating the sealer as the variable.
- Reports clinically meaningful endpoints (radiographic voids, sealer extrusion, VAS postoperative pain, percussion/palpation, periapical radiolucency regression) rather than in-vitro physicochemical proxies.
- Finds filling-quality differences track individual product properties, **not** the ERBS-vs-CSBS class division.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Prospective single-center RCT; G*Power sample-size (α 0.05, power 0.8, ω 0.4, Df 3 → 69; enrollment goal 83 assuming 20% dropout). 80 patients/80 teeth randomized 1:1:1:1 (n=20/arm). Not blinded (handling properties differ).
- **Inclusion**: asymptomatic irreversible pulpitis and/or pulp necrosis ± chronic apical periodontitis. **Exclusion**: mobility, crack, internal/external resorption, calcification, acute periapical periodontitis/abscess, severe systemic disease, intentional endo, C-shaped canals.
- **Treatment**: two-visit, single endodontist (>20 yr). Visit 1 — OneFlare coronal flaring, PathFile #1 glide path, ProTaper Next instrumentation (apical X2–X5), 2.5% NaOCl + passive ultrasonic irrigation (PUI), Calcipex II intracanal Ca(OH)₂, Caviton temp. Visit 2 — NaOCl + EDTA PUI, sealer-based obturation: CSBS via premixed syringe + master GP cone; ERBS via master cone; pumping motion; coronal GP severed and compacted at orifice.
- **Evaluation**: paralleling-technique postoperative radiographs (voids below orifice via two shifted radiographs; extrusion per canal and per tooth); recall at 1 week, 1 month, 3 months with VAS pain, percussion, palpation, and radiographic apical-radiolucency assessment.
- **Statistics**: Pearson chi-square / Fisher exact, α 0.05, SPSS v15.0.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Recall**: 72/80 received allocated intervention, 71 analyzed (98.6% recall; 1 dropout).
- **Voids (per canal)**: EndoSeal TCS 60% and AH Plus 56.3% higher than ADseal 31.6% and CeraSeal 29.4% — **not significant (p > 0.05)**. Voids more common in molars (50%, 23/46) than premolars (38.5%, 5/13) or anteriors (30.7%, 4/13) → anatomy-driven.
- **Sealer extrusion (per tooth / per canal)**: AH Plus 56.3% / 33.3% and CeraSeal 58.8% / 29.7% higher than EndoSeal TCS 35% / 17.8% and ADseal 21.1% / 12.2% — **not significant (p > 0.05)**.
- **Postoperative pain (VAS)**: all groups 0–0.06 at 1 week; unchanged except AH Plus, which rose from 0.06 (1 wk) to 0.13 (1 mo and 3 mo). One AD case had a positive percussion response; no other positive percussion/palpation at any interval.
- **3-month outcome**: all cases maintained except one ADseal case (recurring sinus tract at 1 month from a cemental tear on the mesial root). Periapical radiolucency regression: ADseal 3/7, EndoSeal TCS 4/10, AH Plus 2/6, CeraSeal 2/8. Pooled by class: ERBS 38.5% (5/13) vs CSBS 33.3% (6/18) — similar.
- **Bottom line**: no significant CSBS-vs-ERBS difference in void, extrusion, or short-term pain/healing.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Pilot scale**: small per-arm n (20) and 4 arms limit power; non-significant differences may reflect underpowering rather than true equivalence.
- **Short follow-up (3 months)**: too brief to capture true periapical healing or the authors' flagged concern about **hydraulic resorption of calcium-silicate sealers**; long-term studies required.
- **Not blinded** (operator-aware of sealer due to differing handling).
- Single operator / single center limits generalizability.

## 7. Glossary

- **CSBS (Calcium-Silicate-Based Sealer)**: bioceramic root canal sealer (e.g., CeraSeal, EndoSeal TCS) that sets via hydration of calcium silicates, biocompatible and resin-free.
- **ERBS (Epoxy-Resin-Based Sealer)**: epoxy-resin sealer (e.g., AH Plus, ADseal); long-standing gold standard for dimensional stability/resorption resistance.
- **CeraSeal**: premixed CSBS (Meta Biomed) of calcium silicates + zirconium oxide.
- **EndoSeal TCS**: pozzolan/MTA-based premixed CSBS (Maruchi), syringe-delivered.
- **Sealer-based obturation**: single-cone technique where the sealer (not compacted GP) provides the primary seal.
- **Void**: radiographic gap within the filling acting as a potential bacterial reservoir / microleakage site.
- **Sealer extrusion**: sealer pushed beyond the apical foramen into periapical tissue.
- **VAS (Visual Analog Scale)**: 0–10 patient-reported pain measure.
- **PUI (Passive Ultrasonic Irrigation)**: ultrasonic activation of irrigant to improve canal cleaning.
