## Why Ingested

Seeds the wiki's first **retreatment/retrievability** evidence for the Ceraseal/bioceramic-sealer cluster: prior Ceraseal pages cover clinical outcome and biocompatibility, but none address what happens when a Ceraseal/AH Plus Bioceramic single-cone fill must be *removed*. This in-vitro micro-CT study is the retrieval counterpart to the clinical [[endodontics/spinelli-2024-three-year-single-cone-ceraseal-cohort]] cohort and complements the wettability of [[endodontics/kunam-2024-wettability-ahplus-ceraseal-guttaflow]] (which mechanistically explains why these CSBSs come out more easily than epoxy AH Plus).

## One-line Summary

In-vitro micro-CT study (36 mandibular premolars, n=12/group) showing AH Plus Bioceramic and Ceraseal single-cone fills are more retrievable than epoxy-resin AH Plus during retreatment (94.8% / 92.5% vs 87.1% removed after WaveOne Gold + XP-endo Finisher).

## 한줄요약

In-vitro 마이크로-CT 연구 (하악소구치 36개, 군당 12개): AH Plus Bioceramic·Ceraseal 단일콘 충전이 에폭시 레진 AH Plus보다 재근관치료 시 제거가 잘 됨 (WaveOne Gold + XP-endo Finisher 후 제거율 94.8%·92.5% vs 87.1%).

## 2. Key Contributions

- First micro-CT retrievability comparison of two calcium silicate-based sealers (**AH Plus Bioceramic [AHB]** and **Ceraseal [CER]**) against the epoxy-resin reference **AH Plus Jet (AHJ)** in a single-cone protocol.
- Quantifies that both CSBSs leave **less residual filling material** than AH Plus after retreatment, rebutting the concern that biomineralizing CSBSs are hard to remove.
- Demonstrates that **supplementary XP-endo Finisher (XPF)** instrumentation meaningfully increases removal, especially in the apical region.
- SEM/EDS evidence that **Ceraseal forms more mineral deposits** in dentinal tubules than AHB (consistent with CER containing both di- and tricalcium silicate vs AHB's tricalcium silicate only).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Specimens**: 36 single-oval-canal human mandibular premolars, crowns reduced to 20 mm; prepared with WaveOne Gold (WOG) to apical #35 (#25/7% primary then #35/6% medium); irrigation 3% NaOCl, final 17% EDTA + saline.
- **Groups (n=12)**: sealer-based (single-cone) obturation with WOG medium gutta-percha cone + one sealer: **AHJ** (epoxy resin, auto-mix syringe), **AHB**, or **CER**. Stored 3 weeks at 36.5 C / 95% humidity to set.
- **Retreatment (two phases)**: (1) WOG primary + medium files to WL, apical patency #15 K-file; (2) **XPF** 1 min at 1000 rpm, 7-8 mm stroke to 2 mm short of WL, NaOCl warmed to 36 C for austenite phase transformation.
- **Imaging**: SkyScan 1273 micro-CT (110 kV, 136 uA, 25 um) at three time points (after fill, after WOG, after XPF); CTAn 3D volumetry, scans overlapped/aligned to the initial fill. % removed volume computed for total length, middle-cervical, and apical (4 mm) regions.
- **Surface analysis**: FE-SEM (x1500/x3000) + EDS on one minimal-residual specimen per group.
- **Statistics**: one-way ANOVA + Tukey (volumes), Kruskal-Wallis + Bonferroni (% removed after WOG and by XPF), three-way ANOVA (sealer x region x instrument). Alpha 5%.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Metric (total canal) | AHJ (AH Plus) | AHB | CER (Ceraseal) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Initial filling volume (mm3) | 10.56 ± 2.95 | 10.79 ± 2.73 | 11.26 ± 2.47 (NS) |
| % removed after WOG | 76.0 ± 7.1 | 84.4 ± 7.2 | 81.6 ± 7.7 |
| % removed after WOG + XPF | 87.1 ± 4.4 | 94.8 ± 2.1 | 92.5 ± 3.1 |
| Residual volume after XPF (mm3) | 1.35 | 0.55 | 0.82 |

- After WOG alone: **no significant difference** in residual volume between groups overall; AHB > AHJ only in the middle-cervical region for % removed.
- After WOG + XPF: **AHB and CER removed significantly more** than AHJ in apical, middle-cervical, and total (p < 0.05). AHJ retained the greatest residue; AHJ residual > AHB in apical region.
- **Apical patency** verified throughout (#10 K-file during prep; #15 K-file confirming retreatment completion).
- Three-way ANOVA: sealer, root region, and instrument each significant; only 'root region × instrument' interaction significant.
- **SEM/EDS**: AHJ plugged tubule orifices; AHB few globular particles; **CER numerous mineral deposits** in tubules. AHB/CER higher Ca:P ratio; AHJ higher P:Ca. Nitrogen + fluoride in AHJ and CER particles, absent in AHB; zirconium/silica not detected.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Retreatment performed only **3 weeks** after obturation — clinically the interval is longer, likely producing a stronger dentin-sealer mineralized bond and lower retrievability.
- Canal **not enlarged** beyond the original preparation size during retreatment (larger apical size is generally recommended to maximize XPF action).
- Canals **dried with paper points** before filling (favors AH Plus bond; CSBS prefers a slightly moist canal), possibly skewing relative bond conditions.
- In-vitro single-oval-canal premolar model; no clinical/microbiological outcome.

## 7. Glossary

- **CSBS** — Calcium silicate-based sealer (bioceramic root canal sealer; e.g., Ceraseal, AH Plus Bioceramic).
- **Ceraseal (CER)** — premixed single-syringe CSBS (Metabiomed, South Korea): tricalcium + dicalcium silicate + tricalcium aluminate, 45-50% zirconium oxide.
- **AH Plus Bioceramic (AHB)** — CSBS (Dentsply Sirona) using tricalcium silicate only, dimethyl sulfoxide vehicle, 50-70% zirconium oxide.
- **AH Plus Jet (AHJ)** — epoxy resin-based reference sealer; bonds covalently to dentin collagen.
- **Single-cone / sealer-based obturation** — gutta-percha cone matched to final preparation size, relying on sealer to fill the remainder.
- **WaveOne Gold (WOG)** — reciprocating NiTi file system used for preparation and retreatment.
- **XP-endo Finisher (XPF)** — flexible MaxWire NiTi finishing file (#25, zero taper) that expands at body temperature for supplementary cleaning/retrieval.
- **Retrievability** — ease of removing set filling material during endodontic retreatment.
- **Micro-CT** — micro-computed tomography; non-destructive 3D volumetry of residual filling.
