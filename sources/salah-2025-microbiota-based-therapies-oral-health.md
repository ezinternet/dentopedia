## Why Ingested

구강 미생물총 기반 치료(프로바이오틱스, 포스트바이오틱스, 포식세균, 박테리오파지, 구강 미생물 이식)를 망라한 최신 narrative review로, 기존 [[wiki/oral-microbiology/index]] 페이지들이 개별 병원체(red complex, F. nucleatum 등) 수준에서 다루지 못한 치료적 개입 전반을 한 곳에 정리한다. 특히 Bdellovibrio bacteriovorus를 이용한 선택적 포식 및 박테리오파지 정밀 치료가 oral-microbiology 범주에서 독립적으로 다루어지지 않아 공백 채우기 목적으로 인제스트.

## One-line Summary

Narrative review (2025) synthesizing the oral microbiome's role in caries, periodontitis, halitosis, and candidiasis, and cataloguing emerging microbiota-based therapies — probiotics (S. salivarius K12/M18, Lactobacillus spp.), postbiotics, predatory bacteria (Bdellovibrio bacteriovorus), bacteriophages, and oral microbiota transplantation.

## 한줄요약

2025년 narrative review: 구강 미생물 불균형(우식·치주염·구취·칸디다증)의 기전을 정리하고, 프로바이오틱스·포스트바이오틱스·포식세균(Bdellovibrio)·박테리오파지·구강 미생물 이식 등 신흥 미생물 기반 치료를 체계적으로 개관.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Comprehensive mapping of oral dysbiosis across four disease categories: dental caries, periodontal disease, halitosis, and oral candidiasis.
2. Detailed mechanistic account of the red complex (P. gingivalis / T. forsythia / T. denticola) virulence: gingipains, C5a receptor cleavage, neutrophil evasion, dentilisin-mediated epithelial disruption.
3. Synthesis of clinical evidence (last 5 years) for probiotics in gingivitis/periodontitis management — S. salivarius K12 and M18, Lactobacillus reuteri, L. rhamnosus.
4. Introduction of postbiotics (inanimate bacteria / metabolites) as a safety-stability advantage over live probiotics.
5. Mechanistic review of predatory bacteria (Bdellovibrio bacteriovorus / BALOs) — selective Gram-negative targeting, periplasmic invasion, self-limiting replication.
6. Bacteriophage catalogue targeting S. mutans (SMHBZ8, ɸAPCM01), Enterococcus faecalis (PEf771, HEf13), Fusobacterium nucleatum (FNU1), and S. gordonii (ΦSG005).
7. Concept description of oral microbiota transplantation (OMT): canine periodontitis models, pediatric oncology mucositis prevention, intra-oral halitosis rat model.
8. Critical appraisal of limitations: microbiome resilience, inter-individual variability, donor screening, standardization deficits.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Narrative (non-systematic) literature review; no PRISMA flow; no formal search strategy reported.
- Covers: compositional ecology (16S rRNA, metagenomics), metabolomics (SCFAs, VSCs, polyamines, indoles), and clinical trial summaries.
- Three-stage caries dysbiosis model (Takahashi & Nyvad): dynamic stable → acidogenic → aciduric.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Oral microbiome composition
- >1100 taxonomic groups in the Human Oral Microbiome Database.
- Dominant phyla: Firmicutes, Proteobacteria, Actinobacteria, Bacteroidetes, Fusobacteria, Spirochaetes, TM7.
- Fungi constitute only 0.004% of oral microorganisms.

### Caries dysbiosis
- S. mutans: key virulence factors — acid tolerance, lactic acid production, sucrose→glucan synthesis (glucosyltransferase B), "rotund" biofilm architecture → severe localized demineralization.
- Three-stage transition: health (non-mutans streptococci + Actinomyces) → acidogenic → aciduric (mutans streptococci + lactobacilli dominant).

### Periodontal red complex
- P. gingivalis keystone pathogen: gingipains cleave C3/C5/LL-37; LPS weak TLR4 agonism → blunted IL-8 → permissive environment for secondary colonizers.
- T. denticola dentilisin → disrupts epithelial tight junctions.
- Metabolites (propionate, succinate, homoserine) → RANKL upregulation, osteoclastogenesis, neutrophil impairment.

### Probiotics (clinical evidence)
- S. salivarius M18 & K12 (Babina et al.): reduced gingival and plaque indices in gingivitis.
- Probiotic mouthwash ≈ chlorhexidine for plaque/bleeding in chronic gingivitis.
- Probiotic adjunct to SRP: reduced bleeding on probing, IL-1β, IL-8; increased IL-10, salivary IgA.
- L. acidophilus CCFM8724: reduced S. mutans and Lactobacillus in children with caries.
- S. salivarius 7746 (from caries-free individuals): antimicrobial activity vs oral pathogens.

### Postbiotics
- Heat-killed L. plantarum, L. paracasei, L. rhamnosus lozenges: elevated Streptococcus salivarius, enhanced salivary IgA, reduced plaque.
- Heat-inactivated L. paracasei ET-22: reduced halitosis-related bacteria (T. denticola, F. nucleatum, P. gingivalis) and VSC production.

### Predatory bacteria (BALOs)
- B. bacteriovorus: invades Gram-negative periplasm → cell lysis; selective for Gram-negatives, spares Gram-positives and eukaryotic cells.
- Self-limiting: ceases replication when prey is depleted → resistance risk lower than antibiotics.
- Targets: P. gingivalis, T. forsythia in vitro biofilms; limited by anaerobic oral environment.

### Bacteriophages
- SMHBZ8 / ɸAPCM01: lytic against S. mutans type c; SMHBZ8 reduces caries in murine model.
- PEf771 (against E. faecalis): burst size 78; superior to 10 antibiotics in root canal models.
- HEf13 (E. faecalis): burst size 352; effective in ex vivo dentin model.
- FNU1: lyses F. nucleatum, disrupts biofilm → periodontitis therapeutic candidate.
- ΦSG005: infects S. gordonii; encodes AcrIIA5-like Cas9 inhibitor.

### Oral microbiota transplantation (OMT)
- Canine model: natural periodontitis treated by OMT.
- Pediatric oncology (Goloshchapov et al.): maternal saliva OMT reduced chemo-induced mucositis severity; no adverse effects.
- Halitosis rat model (Huang & Cheng): healthy OMT donor → reduced malodor bacteria and breath scores.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative review without systematic search or quality appraisal of primary studies.
- Microbiome resilience: restored pathogens can re-emerge post-therapy.
- BALOs restricted to Gram-negatives; cannot address Gram-positive pathogens.
- Bacteriophage host range is narrow; phage resistance can emerge.
- OMT: not yet applied in human periodontal clinical trials; donor screening and safety unresolved.
- Postbiotics/probiotics lack manufacturing standardization; dosing and strain-specific effects unclear.
- Long-term safety data for all novel modalities is limited.

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| BALOs | Bdellovibrio and like organisms — predatory bacteria |
| VSC | Volatile sulfur compounds (H₂S, methyl mercaptan, dimethyl sulfide) — halitosis mediators |
| OMT | Oral microbiota transplantation |
| Keystone pathogen | Low-abundance pathogen (e.g., P. gingivalis) that disproportionately reshapes community ecology |
| Postbiotic | Preparation of inanimate bacteria and/or their components conferring host health benefit (ISAPP 2019) |
| Gingipains | Cysteine proteases of P. gingivalis (RgpA, RgpB, Kgp) — cleave host proteins and immune mediators |
| SCFAs | Short-chain fatty acids (acetate, propionate, butyrate) — dual role: mucosal homeostasis vs periodontitis-associated cytotoxicity |
| ALS3 | Agglutinin-like sequence 3 — key Candida adhesin mediating epithelial attachment |
| MOI | Multiplicity of infection — ratio of phage to bacterial cells |
