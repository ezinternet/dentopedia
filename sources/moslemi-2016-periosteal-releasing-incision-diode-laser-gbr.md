## Why Ingested

Part of the flap-design / tension-free primary closure collection. This case series tests a diode-laser periosteal releasing incision (PRI) as a lower-morbidity alternative to scalpel PRI for achieving tension-free GBR closure, providing the device-level execution of the "periosteal releasing incision" advancement category framed in [[suture-wound-closure/plonka-2017-flap-designs-flap-advancement-implant-therapy]]. It complements the closure techniques in [[suture-wound-closure/attia-2023-coronally-advanced-lingual-flap-titanium-mesh]] and [[suture-wound-closure/lee-2018-flap-management-alveolar-ridge-preservation]].

## One-line Summary

Case series (17 patients, anterior-maxilla lateral ridge augmentation) showing a 940-nm diode-laser periosteal releasing incision achieved tension-free primary closure in all cases with minimal bleeding, mild pain (VAS 20.6 ± 12.1 mm at day 1), no hematoma/intense swelling, and 100% implant success at 6 months post-loading.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First report (per authors) on clinical use of laser for periosteal releasing incision / flap advancement during bone augmentation.
- Demonstrates that diode-laser PRI achieves tension-free primary closure while substantially limiting the intraoperative bleeding and postoperative morbidity (hematoma, swelling, pain) associated with scalpel PRI.
- Confirms no negative impact on implant survival at 6 months post-loading.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 17 patients (8 male, 9 female, age 31–65) requiring lateral ridge augmentation in the anterior maxilla; one defect treated per patient by GBR; healthy nonsmokers.
- Flap: full-thickness mid-crestal incision in keratinized gingiva, two buccal vertical releasing incisions (mesial + distal), full-thickness flap elevated to anterior nasal spine.
- Laser: 940-nm GaAlAs diode (ezLase, Biolase), 400-µm disposable tip, contact mode, 2 W pulse mode (pulse interval 1 ms, pulse length 1 ms), aimed nearly parallel to buccal bone to avoid bone irradiation; carbonized layer wiped with saline gauze.
- Grafting: tenting screws for space, demineralized freeze-dried bone allograft (Cenobone) + resorbable membrane (Cenoderm, 2 mm); internal mattress sutures for passive closure, continuous mattress at margins.
- Postop: amoxicillin 500 mg TID × 7 d, NSAID (ibuprofen/Gelofen 400 mg), 0.2% chlorhexidine BID × 14 d.
- Outcomes: swelling (0–3 scale) and pain (VAS 0–100 mm) assessed daily for 6 days; NSAID capsules counted; implant success (Albrektsson criteria) 6 months after loading; implants Implantium (Dentium, Korea).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Minimal bleeding during laser PRI; tension-free primary closure obtained in all cases; uneventful healing; no wound dehiscence or infection.
- No patient experienced intense (grade 3) swelling; maximum swelling on day 3 (1.47 ± 0.50), subsided by day 6.
- Pain: peak on day 1, mean VAS 20.59 ± 12.10 mm (classified as mild); resolved by day 6 (no pain at time of surgery due to immediate NSAID).
- NSAID use: mean 1.53 ± 1.04 capsules on day 1; none needed after day 4.
- All implants successful and functional; none failed at 6 months post-loading.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-arm case series (no scalpel-PRI control group) — comparisons to scalpel are referenced from literature, not measured head-to-head here.
- Small sample (n=17), single site type (anterior maxilla lateral augmentation), short follow-up (6 months post-loading).
- Outcomes are early soft-tissue/morbidity and implant survival; quantitative bone-gain not the primary endpoint.

## 6. Related Work

- Periosteal releasing incision as the most common method for tension-free GBR closure (background).
- Laser oral surgery advantages: hemostasis, decontamination, reduced postoperative pain.

## 7. Glossary

- PRI: Periosteal Releasing Incision — incision/scoring of the periosteum to mobilize a full-thickness flap for tension-free closure.
- GBR: Guided Bone Regeneration.
- VAS: Visual Analogue Scale (0–100 mm) for pain.
- DFDBA: Demineralized Freeze-Dried Bone Allograft.
- NSAID: Non-Steroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drug.
- Diode laser (940 nm): GaAlAs soft-tissue laser used here in contact mode to cut periosteum.
