## Why Ingested

Clear aligner therapy is widely assumed to either protect against or provoke bruxism, but the wiki had no page settling the question — existing aligner/muscle pages cover masticatory EMG and TMJ but not bruxism per se. This first-ever SR on the aligner–bruxism interaction directly extends [[orthodontics/clear-aligner/lekaviciute-2024-clear-aligner-masticatory-muscle-emg-sr-ma]] (masseter EMG activity) and complements [[orthodontics/clear-aligner/montasser-2026-tmj-changes-clear-aligner-scoping-review]] (TMJ changes) by adding the parafunction/bruxism dimension.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (11 studies, n=818, 72.8% female; no meta-analysis possible) finding clear aligners have a predominantly **neutral** effect on bruxism — often reducing tonic contractions (clenching) but with inconsistent/transient effects on phasic activity (grinding) and no change in overall sleep-bruxism index; GRADE certainty moderate (RCTs) to very low (non-randomised).

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰 (11편, n=818, 여성 72.8%, 메타분석 불가): 투명교정장치 (Clear Aligner)는 이갈이 (Bruxism)에 대체로 **중립적** — 긴장성 수축 (tonic/clenching)은 종종 감소시키나 위상성 수축 (phasic/grinding)과 수면이갈이지수 (SB index)에는 일관된 영향 없음. 근거수준은 RCT 중등도, 비무작위 매우 낮음.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First systematic, critical synthesis** of the clear-aligner–bruxism interaction, framing aligners explicitly as neutral / protective / risk.
2. Pooled 11 studies (818 patients) spanning sleep bruxism (SB), awake bruxism (AB) and mixed/self-reported phenotypes, stratified by diagnostic method (instrumental EMG vs ecological momentary assessment vs questionnaire).
3. Established that the dominant signal is **neutrality**, with a recurring nuance: aligners tend to lower **tonic** contractions (clenching) while phasic (grinding) effects are inconsistent and frequently transient (first month → functional adaptation, not therapy).
4. Provided a structured a priori neutral/protective/risk classification table and a GRADE certainty appraisal (moderate RCTs / very low non-randomised).
5. Flagged that "risk" signals came only from cross-sectional self-report without baseline data (cannot establish temporality → hypothesis-generating only).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases**: EMBASE, PubMed/Medline, Scopus, Web of Science + grey literature (Google Scholar, OpenGrey), through 15 April 2025; reference-list hand-searching.
- **PECOS**: P = children/adults under orthodontic treatment; E = clear aligners; C = fixed orthodontics or no comparison; O = presence/modification of SB or AB (instrumental, clinical, or self-reported); S = RCTs, observational (case-control, cross-sectional, cohort), case series >10.
- **Exclusions**: no aligner use, animal studies, reviews/abstracts/letters/case reports <10, full-text not retrieved.
- **Diagnostic tiers**: portable EMG±ECG (Bruxoff) → definite SB; smartphone ecological momentary assessment (e.g., BruxApp) → definite AB; questionnaires (Oral Behaviour Checklist, OBC) → possible/probable bruxism.
- **Risk of bias**: Joanna Briggs Institute (JBI) Critical Appraisal Tools (high ≤49% / unclear 50-69% / low ≥70% "yes").
- **Synthesis**: qualitative only — heterogeneity in materials, appliance protocol (active vs passive/retainer), follow-up (8 days–12 months) and EMG scoring prevented meta-analysis. GRADE SoF via GRADEpro.
- **Selection counts**: 857 records → 408 de-duplicated → 14 full-text → 11 included.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Sample**: 11 studies, 818 patients; 72.8% female. 5 RCTs + 6 non-randomised (2 cohort, 2 cross-sectional, 1 case-control, 1 quasi-experimental). Mostly Italy (7).
- **Sleep bruxism**: no aligner study changed the overall **SB index**. Tonic contractions reduced in Bargellini 2017 (1, 3 mo) and Bargellini 2024 (6, 12 mo); Castroflorio 2018 RCT showed **increased phasic** contractions (transient); Manfredini 2018 passive retainers neutral; Pittar 2023 reduced contraction **amplitude** (not frequency/duration).
- **Awake bruxism**: neutral — Colonna 2024 (EMA, no AB difference across no-aligner/passive/active) and Pereira 2021 RCT (AB ~53.5% aligner vs 51.3% fixed, no difference at 6 mo). Liu 2017 RCT: ↓ OBC scores at 3-6 mo but ↑ temporalis/SCM EMG (recruitment shift, not AB frequency change).
- **Mixed/self-reported**: conflicting. Heleiwa-Ferioli 2024 reported ↓ clenching/grinding, ↓ muscle pain, ↓ TMJ pain (no headache change). Saccomanno 2021 found **higher** self-reported bruxism in aligner vs metal-brace users; Saccomanno 2022 ~50.3% reported clenching/bruxism during therapy (24% TMJ pain, 26.9% headache) — both lacked baseline data.
- **Risk of bias**: 1 moderate (case-control), 10 high.
- **GRADE**: moderate (RCTs), very low (non-randomised).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Marked methodological heterogeneity (diagnostic method, active vs passive appliance, follow-up 8 d–12 mo, populations) → meta-analysis infeasible.
- 10/11 studies high risk of bias; RCTs small and short with incomplete blinding/allocation reporting.
- Few studies operationalised the consensus possible/probable/definite SB/AB framework, conflating diagnostic certainty levels.
- Cross-sectional/self-report designs cannot establish temporality; no baseline → "risk" findings are descriptive, not causal.
- The proposed gradual-adaptation wear protocol (start 2-3 h/day, ramp over 1-2 weeks for high-hypervigilance patients) is **expert reasoning**, not derived from included data.
- Future: harmonised diagnostic criteria, standardised EMG protocols, adequately powered RCTs with long-term follow-up.

## 7. Glossary

- **SB (Sleep Bruxism)**: rhythmic (phasic) or non-rhythmic (tonic) masticatory muscle activity during sleep.
- **AB (Awake Bruxism)**: repetitive/sustained tooth contact, bracing or thrusting during wakefulness.
- **Tonic contraction**: sustained clenching-type muscle activity (occlusal load).
- **Phasic contraction**: rhythmic grinding-type muscle activity.
- **SB index**: sleep-bruxism episodes per sleep hour (portable EMG/ECG, e.g., Bruxoff).
- **EMA (Ecological Momentary Assessment)**: real-time smartphone-based self-report of AB behaviours (e.g., BruxApp).
- **OBC (Oral Behaviour Checklist)**: validated 21-item DC/TMD Axis II questionnaire of parafunctional behaviours.
- **JBI**: Joanna Briggs Institute critical appraisal tools.
- **GRADE**: Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation (certainty of evidence).
