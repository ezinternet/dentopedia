## Why Ingested

This SR+MA OPENS the oral-mucositis sub-topic in `oral-medicine`, which previously held oral mucosal disease pages on aphthous, lichen planus, and pemphigoid but no chemo/radiotherapy-induced oral mucositis (OM) page. It extends the oral-ulcerative/erosive disease coverage of [[oral-medicine/coppola-2023-herpetic-gingivostomatitis-sr]] and [[oral-medicine/dean-2022-oral-chronic-gvhd-review]] (the latter shares the HSCT pediatric-oncology context) into the cancer-treatment-induced OM space, where honey emerges as the only meta-analytically supported agent and LLLT shows no benefit.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (34 RCTs identified, 5 meta-analyzed) of OM prevention/treatment in pediatric cancer patients: topical honey shortened hospital stay in severe OM (MD=-4.33d, p=0.002); LLLT showed no benefit for OM grade >=II.

- **Search**: MEDLINE + Scopus, January 2000 – March 2023
- **Population**: Children undergoing chemotherapy/radiotherapy or hematopoietic stem cell transplantation (HSCT)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First focused SR+MA we hold on chemo/radiotherapy- and HSCT-induced oral mucositis (OM) specifically in the **pediatric** population.
- Quantifies the burden: chemotherapy-induced OM incidence in children can reach **91.5%**, with major quality-of-life impact.
- 34 RCTs met inclusion criteria across diverse interventions/agents; 5 RCTs were pooled (subsets investigating LLLT or honey).
- Identifies **honey** as the most promising agent (only intervention with a statistically significant pooled benefit) and finds **LLLT** without demonstrable benefit in this pediatric pooled analysis.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Systematic search of MEDLINE and Scopus (Jan 2000–Mar 2023) for RCTs on OM management in pediatric cancer patients.
- 34 RCTs identified meeting inclusion criteria.
- Meta-analysis restricted to 5 RCTs that investigated either Low Level Laser Therapy (LLLT) or honey.
- Outcomes pooled: mean duration of hospital stay (honey); risk of OM grade >=II (LLLT, prevention and therapeutic application).
- Effect measures: mean difference (MD) for hospital stay; risk ratio (RR) for OM grade >=II.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Honey (topical, 2 RCTs)**: shortened mean duration of hospital stay in children with severe OM — **MD = -4.33 days, p = 0.002** (significant benefit).
- **LLLT — prevention/treatment of OM grade >=II**: **RR = 0.99, p = 0.99** (no effect).
- **LLLT — therapeutic application, lower risk of OM grade >=II**: **RR = 0.48, p = 0.58** (point estimate favorable but not significant).
- Conclusion: honey is a promising candidate for treating OM in pediatric patients; LLLT not supported by the pooled pediatric data; further high-quality RCTs needed.
- (Abstract-only — individual agent-by-agent narrative results from the 34 RCTs beyond honey/LLLT were in the full text not retrieved here.)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 5 of 34 RCTs were meta-analyzable (only LLLT and honey had poolable, comparable outcomes); the honey pooled estimate rests on just 2 RCTs.
- Heterogeneous OM grading scales, agents, and underlying malignancies/regimens across included RCTs limit pooling.
- Authors explicitly call for further high-quality RCTs.
- This wiki record is abstract-level; full risk-of-bias appraisal and the full agent inventory were in the unretrieved PMC body.

## 6. Related Work

- Pediatric/HSCT oral complications context: oral chronic graft-versus-host disease.
- Oral ulcerative/erosive mucosal disease management (aphthous, herpetic gingivostomatitis) as adjacent OM differentials and symptom-management literature.

## 7. Glossary

- **OM (Oral Mucositis)** — inflammatory/ulcerative injury of the oral mucosa caused by chemotherapy/radiotherapy or conditioning regimens.
- **HSCT** — Hematopoietic Stem Cell Transplantation.
- **LLLT** — Low Level Laser Therapy (photobiomodulation).
- **OM grade >=II** — clinically significant mucositis (erythema/ulceration with functional impact) per common OM grading scales.
- **MD / RR** — mean difference / risk ratio (pooled meta-analytic effect measures).
