## One-line Summary
Retrospective case series (19 patients, 22 implants in fresh maxillary molar sockets) combining immediate implant placement with simultaneous transcrestal (Summers/BAOSFE) sinus floor elevation and early loading: 100% survival and 95.45% success at mean 33.12 ± 16.98 months follow-up; mean crestal bone resorption only −0.054 ± 0.56 mm.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Advanced Periodontology & Implant Dentistry 2018;10(2):68–76.
- **Pages**: 68–76 (9 pages).
- **Institution**: Department of Periodontics, Implant Research Center, Tehran Medical Sciences, Islamic Azad University, Tehran, Iran.
- **Study type**: Retrospective case series (clinical follow-up of consecutive cases).

## 2. Key Contributions
- Reports long-term (up to 5 years; mean 33.12 months) clinical outcomes of a combined protocol: immediate implant placement into fresh maxillary molar sockets, simultaneous transcrestal sinus elevation (Summers' bone-added osteotome sinus floor elevation, BAOSFE), and early loading.
- Demonstrates that even in posterior maxilla with limited interradicular bone (5–6 mm prerequisite), this combination protocol can achieve survival comparable to conventional staged approaches.
- Provides crestal bone-level data showing negligible peri-implant bone loss when soft tissue sealing (coronally advanced flap or connective tissue pedicle graft) is added.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 19 patients (12 F / 7 M, mean age 56.28 y), 22 implants (Xive Dentsply or Axiom Antogyr) placed in fresh sockets of extracted maxillary molars.
- Atraumatic extraction by piezosurgery and tooth sectioning; socket debridement.
- Drilling into interradicular septum stopped 1 mm short of sinus floor; Summers' osteotome BAOSFE; Valsalva maneuver to verify membrane integrity.
- Grafting with Cerabon or Tutodent (bovine bone substitute) into root sockets; sites covered with acellular dermal allograft (SureDerm) or collagen membrane (Tutopach).
- Non-submerged healing; soft tissue sealing via coronally advanced flap or palatal connective tissue pedicle graft.
- Early loading after 6–8 weeks of healing once Periotest confirmed osseointegration.
- Outcomes: survival, Buser success criteria, first bone-implant contact (FBIC) measured on periapical radiographs (X-Pod calibrated).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Mean follow-up**: 33.12 ± 16.98 months (range up to 5 years).
- **Survival rate**: 100% (no implant lost in function after early healing).
- **Success rate (Buser)**: 95.45%.
- **Crestal bone loss (mean)**: −0.054 ± 0.56 mm (range −0.86 to +0.90 mm).
- **Mesial bone loss**: −0.02 ± 0.55 mm; distal −0.09 ± 0.59 mm (P = 0.232, no significant mesial-distal difference).
- **45.5%** of surfaces showed bone loss, 22.7% no change, 31.8% coronal gain.
- One peri-implantitis case at 15 mo (residual cement / marginal gap), treated with GBR and resolved.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Retrospective single-arm design — no concurrent control of delayed or staged protocol.
- Small sample (n=22 implants), single center, multiple operators not detailed.
- Prerequisite of ≥5–6 mm interradicular bone limits generalizability to severely resorbed septa.
- Follow-up heterogeneous (12–62 mo).

## 7. Glossary
- **BAOSFE (Bone-Added Osteotome Sinus Floor Elevation)**: Summers' transcrestal technique using osteotomes to up-fracture sinus floor with bone graft.
- **Schneiderian membrane**: bilaminar mucoperiosteal membrane lining the maxillary sinus.
- **FBIC**: First Bone-Implant Contact — most coronal bone-implant contact point on radiograph.
- **Buser success criteria**: absence of mobility, pain, peri-implant infection, and radiolucency.
