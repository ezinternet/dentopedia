## Why Ingested

[[resin/meereis-2018-polymerization-shrinkage-stress-resin-sr-ma]]가 "레진 기질(matrix) 개질이 수축응력 감소에 최대 기여"라고 결론지었는데, 본 in-vitro 재료개발 연구는 그 전략의 구체적 사례(polyurethane dimethacrylate oligomer PU-MA + glass flake filler)로 volume shrinkage 1.35% 달성 + 노화 후 기계적 안정성을 보강한다. matrix-modification 가설의 실증 데이터. abstract-only.

## One-line Summary
In-vitro material-development study showing a Bis-GMA/TEGDMA composite modified with 20 wt% polyurethane dimethacrylate (PU-MA) oligomer and 75 wt% glass-flake/borosilicate fillers (PU-PG-75%) achieves low volumetric shrinkage (1.35%) with mechanical properties that remain relatively stable after 60-day water immersion + 5000 thermocycles.

## 한줄요약
In-vitro 재료개발: Bis-GMA/TEGDMA에 20 wt% 폴리우레탄 디메타크릴레이트(PU-MA) 올리고머 + 75 wt% 글래스 플레이크 충전 시(PU-PG-75%) 부피수축 1.35%로 낮추고, 60일 침수 + 5000회 열순환 노화 후에도 기계적 물성이 상용품보다 안정적.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Journal of Dentistry, 2024;151:105426
- e-pub 2024-10-24
- Affiliation: School and Hospital of Stomatology, Jilin University, China
- Base resin: Bis-GMA/TEGDMA; modifier: PU-MA; fillers: glass flakes + Si-Al-borosilicate glass (65-75 wt%)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Demonstrates PU-MA oligomer (20 wt%) as a matrix-modification route to low volumetric shrinkage (1.35% at PU-PG-75%)
- Maps the trade-off: higher filler content → lower shrinkage but higher water sorption/solubility and reduced depth of cure
- Shows the experimental PU-PG-75% retains flexural/hardness properties better than commercial controls after aging (60 d water + 5000 thermocycles)
- Uses laser-triangulation real-time shrinkage kinetics

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Hand-fabricated composites: Bis-GMA/TEGDMA + PU-MA + 65-75 wt% mixed glass-flake/borosilicate fillers
- Shrinkage kinetics by laser-triangulation set-up
- Mechanical-physical: three-point bending (flexural strength, modulus) + micro Vickers hardness, before and after aging (60-day water immersion + 5000 thermocycles)
- One-way ANOVA/LSD; independent T-test (pre/post aging); two-way ANOVA (composition × aging); α=0.05

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- PU-PG-75%: volume shrinkage 1.35% (lowest)
- ↑ filler content → ↑ water sorption & solubility, ↓ depth of cure (P<0.05)
- After aging: commercial products' flexural strength, elastic modulus, Vickers hardness dropped markedly; PU-PG-75wt% relatively stable
- Conclusion: 20 wt% PU-MA effectively decreases shrinkage; aging significantly reduces flexural properties of experimental materials

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Experimental, hand-fabricated (not commercial); bench-only; no cavity/clinical data
- Higher filler reduces depth of cure and raises sorption/solubility — clinical curing depth concern
- Abstract-only ingest; full numbers not extracted

## 6. Related Work
- Concrete instance of the resin-matrix-modification strategy validated by Meereis 2018
- Parallel material-development angle to He 2026 (thiol-ene low-shrinkage)

## 7. Glossary
- **PU-MA**: polyurethane dimethacrylate oligomer. **Glass flake filler**: plate-shaped glass reinforcement. **Depth of cure**: depth to which adequate conversion is achieved. **Thermocycling**: thermal fatigue aging (here 5000 cycles).
