## Why Ingested

Part of a focused collection on flap design and peri-implant soft-tissue management. This prospective case series compares the microsurgical roll-in-envelope (RIE) flap against the holding-suture (HS) technique for second-stage implant soft-tissue handling, extending the flap-advancement/closure theme of [[suture-wound-closure/lee-2018-flap-management-alveolar-ridge-preservation]] toward the soft-tissue-phenotype (thickness) outcome rather than mesh/membrane coverage. It contrasts with the major-advancement closure techniques in its siblings [[suture-wound-closure/attia-2023-coronally-advanced-lingual-flap-titanium-mesh]] and [[suture-wound-closure/wang-2024-poncho-technique-titanium-mesh-bone-regeneration]].

## One-line Summary

Prospective case series (10 posterior implants, 10 patients, randomly assigned by flap design) reporting that the microsurgical roll-in-envelope (RIE) flap yielded a ~three-fold greater peri-implant mucosal thickness than the holding-suture (HS) technique with less pain/discomfort and no dropouts.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Describes and clinically evaluates the microsurgical roll-in-envelope (RIE) flap for managing peri-implant soft tissue.
- Directly compares RIE to the commonly used holding-suture (HS) flap technique on peri-implant mucosal thickness.
- Positions RIE as a soft-tissue-phenotype-modification alternative that, when sufficient supporting tissue is present, may reduce pain/discomfort compared with connective tissue grafts.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 10 posterior dental implants in 10 healthy individuals.
- Randomly assigned treatment to each of the two groups by flap design (RIE vs HS).
- Main outcome: mucosal thickness, measured at the time of surgery and at 6 and 12 weeks.
- Microsurgical (operating-microscope) approach for the RIE arm.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Healing was uneventful at all sites with no patient drop-outs.
- The comparison revealed a three-fold reduction in mucosal thickness in the HS group compared to the RIE group (i.e., RIE preserved/created ~3× greater mucosal thickness).
- Conclusion: In the presence of sufficient peri-implant supporting tissues and when indicated, the RIE flap yields superior outcomes, reducing pain/discomfort compared with connective tissue grafts.
- Exact mucosal-thickness values, standard deviations, and p-values are not reported in the ingested abstract-only text.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Very small sample (n=10), case-series design, short follow-up (12 weeks).
- Operator- and microscope-dependent technique; generalizability limited.
- Indication restricted to sites with sufficient existing peri-implant supporting tissue.
- Long-term phenotype stability and esthetic outcomes not reported in the available text.

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary

- RIE: Roll-In-Envelope flap — minimally invasive microsurgical flap that rolls de-epithelialized connective tissue into an envelope to thicken peri-implant mucosa.
- HS: Holding-Suture technique — flap stabilized/positioned with a holding suture for soft-tissue management.
- Peri-implant phenotype: composite of mucosal thickness, keratinized tissue width, and supracrestal tissue around an implant.
- CTG: Connective Tissue Graft — autogenous soft-tissue graft used as the comparator standard for thickening.
