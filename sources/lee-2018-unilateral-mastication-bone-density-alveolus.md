## One-line Summary
Cross-sectional CBCT study in 25 unilateral mastication adults showing chewing-side alveolar bone density is significantly higher than non-chewing side, especially in posterior regions.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Asia-Pacific Journal of Multimedia Services Convergent with Art, Humanities, and Sociology, Vol.8, No.8, August 2018, pp.11-18
- **DOI**: 10.21742/AJMAHS.2018.08.21
- **Institution**: Department of Orthodontics, Chonnam National University, Gwangju, Korea
- **Language**: Korean/English bilingual

## 2. Key Contributions
- Quantified alveolar bone density (Hounsfield units) differences between chewing and non-chewing sides using CBCT
- Found statistically significant higher bone density on chewing side at canine, premolar, molar regions (not incisors)
- Posterior regions showed greatest asymmetry; mandibular molar showed largest difference

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: Cross-sectional study
- **Sample**: 25 adults (6 male, 19 female; mean age 24y 5mo) with self-reported unilateral mastication habit
- **Exclusion**: <18 years, craniofacial anomaly, TMD, missing teeth, severe crowding
- **Imaging**: CBCT (Alphard Vega; 80kV, 5mA, 0.39 mm voxel, 200×179 mm FOV)
- **Measurement sites**: Interradicular bone at incisor, canine, premolar, molar regions; 5 mm apical to alveolar crest; Hounsfield units via EzImplant software
- **Statistics**: Paired t-test; method error by Dahlberg formula (range 4.8–11.3 HU)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
| Region | Chewing HU (max) | Opposite HU (max) | Significant? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Maxillary incisor | 848 ± 99 | 843 ± 109 | No |
| Maxillary canine | 724 ± 144 | 654 ± 170 | Yes (p<0.05) |
| Maxillary premolar | 557 ± 138 | 481 ± 148 | Yes |
| Maxillary molar | 503 ± 119 | 413 ± 117 | Yes |
| Mandibular molar | 513 ± 152 | 409 ± 149 | Yes (largest diff) |

- Bone density decreases anterior → posterior (regardless of chewing side)
- Incisor region: no significant side difference (smaller occlusal forces on incisors)
- Greatest chewing/opposite difference: mandibular molar region

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small sample (n=25); predominantly female
- Self-reported chewing preference (subjective)
- CBCT HU values not standardized across machines (intra-subject comparison valid but not cross-study)
- Did not measure actual occlusal force
- Cross-sectional design; cannot establish causality

## 7. Glossary
- **Hounsfield unit (HU)**: CT radiodensity unit; bone typically 400–1900 HU
- **Unilateral mastication**: Habitual chewing predominantly on one side
- **Interradicular bone**: Bone between roots of adjacent teeth
- **CBCT**: Cone-beam computed tomography; low-dose 3D imaging
