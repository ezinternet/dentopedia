## Why Ingested

치주-임플란트 소프트 티슈 관리에서 각화점막 증대(Keratinized Mucosa Augmentation, KMA) 수술의 적응증과 술식 선택 기준이 임상적으로 자주 혼선되어 왔다. 이 컨센서스 문서는 second-stage surgery 시점에 한정해 각 술식(ARF, FGG, SFGG, 소프트티슈 대체재)별 적응증·결정 트리를 명시적으로 제공함으로써, 기존의 개괄적 소프트티슈 지침([[implants/sanz-2022-keratinized-mucosa-around-implants-consensus]])을 second-stage 시점 임상 워크플로우로 구체화한다.

## One-line Summary

Chinese multi-center expert consensus (29 authors, IJOS 2025) providing a technique-selection decision tree for peri-implant keratinized mucosa augmentation at second-stage surgery based on implant site, residual KM width, mucosa thickness, and patient factors.

## 한줄요약

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Standardized decision tree**: First consensus document focused exclusively on PIKM augmentation at second-stage surgery, integrating site (maxillary anterior/posterior, mandibular), residual KM width, mucosa thickness, and patient preference into a single clinical pathway.
2. **Technique characterization table**: Comparative advantages/disadvantages for ARF, FGG, SFGG, and soft tissue substitutes (ADM, XCM) summarized in one structured table.
3. **Quantitative thresholds**: Minimum 4 mm total KM (≥2 mm buccal + ≥2 mm lingual/palatal); KM ≤2 mm associated with elevated peri-implantitis, biofilm, MBL, mucosal recession.
4. **Site-specific mandibular decision algorithm**: Residual KM ≥3–4 mm → ARF feasible; 2–3 mm → SFGG or FGG; <2 mm → FGG mandatory before second-stage.
5. **Timing rationale**: Articulates why second-stage is the optimal window (osseointegration confirmed, stable soft tissue, integrates into existing surgery, reduces future interventions).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Format**: Narrative/expert consensus. Not a systematic review or meta-analysis.
- **Evidence basis**: Review of published RCTs, cohort studies, and prior consensus statements; supplemented by pooled expert opinion from 29 specialists across 20 Chinese institutions.
- **Surgical techniques covered**:
  - **ARF (Apically Repositioned Flap)**: partial-thickness flap displaces existing KM apically; exposed periosteum heals by secondary intention forming new KM. Average gain ~1.15 mm at 12 months; shrinkage 40–70%. No second surgical site; good color match. Posterior maxilla gold standard.
  - **FGG (Free Gingival Graft)**: autogenous palatal epithelialized graft. Gold standard for KM width/thickness gain; long-term stable. Mean KM gain ~4.1 mm. Donor site morbidity; color mismatch.
  - **SFGG (Strip FGG)**: narrow strip variant (~2–3 mm) combined with soft tissue substitutes; reduces donor site harvest area; better color match than full FGG; technique-sensitive.
  - **Soft Tissue Substitutes**:
    - *ADM (Acellular Dermal Matrix)*: allograft scaffold; KM gain 2.59 mm; contraction 56–72% vs FGG 12–16%; delayed healing +5–20 days.
    - *XCM (Xenogeneic Collagen Matrix)*: porcine bilayer collagen; shrinkage 34–51%; KM gain ~1.8 mm vs FGG 4.1 mm. Shorter surgical time, less morbidity.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Decision tree (second-stage surgery)**:

| Implant site | Residual KM | Recommended technique |
|---|---|---|
| Maxillary anterior (esthetic zone) | Insufficient | SFGG + soft tissue substitute (first choice); FGG if aesthetics not priority |
| Maxillary posterior | Insufficient | ARF (palatal KM abundant) |
| Mandible (any) | ≥3–4 mm | ARF, SFGG, or FGG |
| Mandible | 2–3 mm | SFGG or FGG (patient preference, aesthetics, technique sensitivity determine) |
| Mandible | <2 mm | FGG before second-stage surgery |

**Technique performance summary**:
- ARF: +1.15 mm KM at 12 months; shrinkage 40–70%; no donor site
- FGG: ~4.1 mm KM gain; 12–16% contraction; donor site pain
- ADM: 2.59 mm KM gain; 56–72% contraction; delayed healing
- XCM: 1.8 mm KM gain; 34–51% contraction; better patient comfort

**Mucosa thickness**: Thin biotype → prefer FGG or MARF (add connective tissue component to increase thickness); ARF or XCM alone insufficient for thin phenotype.

**Mandibular posterior caveat**: Muscle tension + lingual anatomy → FGG strongly preferred; at least 2 mm KM must remain on lingual side after any ARF incision.

**Aesthetic zone (maxillary anterior)**: FGG creates "patchy" color mismatch → SFGG + XCM preferred for esthetic cases.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Consensus based primarily on expert opinion; long-term RCT evidence for several techniques (especially PADM, PRF membranes, silk fibroin) remains insufficient.
- No inclusion of patient-reported outcomes (PROs/PROMs) in the decision framework.
- Soft tissue substitute effectiveness shows higher contraction rates and lower augmentation compared to FGG — more head-to-head RCTs needed.
- Alternative novel scaffolds (porcine acellular dermal matrix PADM, PRF membranes, silk fibroin) require more clinical validation before routine recommendation.
- Economic factors addressed qualitatively only; cost-effectiveness analyses are lacking.
- Future research needed on: optimal suturing techniques, timing variations, and patient-specific biological factors.

## 7. Glossary

- **PIKM**: Peri-implant keratinized mucosa — stratified squamous epithelium between mucogingival junction (MGJ) and mucosal margin around implants
- **ARF**: Apically Repositioned Flap — splits existing KM and displaces it apically to expose periosteum that heals forming new KM
- **MARF**: Modified ARF — coronal portion preserved to reduce recession; wider KM band repositioned
- **FGG**: Free Gingival Graft — autogenous epithelialized graft harvested from hard palate
- **SFGG**: Strip Free Gingival Graft — narrow (~2–3 mm) strip variant; paired with soft tissue substitutes
- **ADM**: Acellular Dermal Matrix — human-derived allograft scaffold
- **XCM**: Xenogeneic Collagen Matrix — porcine bilayer resorbable collagen membrane
- **PADM**: Porcine Acellular Dermal Matrix — xenogeneic ADM alternative
- **MGJ**: Mucogingival Junction — border between attached/keratinized and alveolar mucosa
- **KM**: Keratinized Mucosa (general term; PIKM when around implants)
- **Second-stage surgery**: Implant uncovery procedure (abutment connection) performed after osseointegration is confirmed
