## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional online survey of 537 German dentists (from 1070 participants, ~0.5% of the profession): prophylactic antibacterial-drug (ABD) use is generally restrained but highly inconsistent — a positive trend only in endocarditis-risk and immunosuppression/organ-transplant groups, a negative trend to negative consensus in diabetes and joint-replacement groups; amoxicillin is the overwhelming first-line and clindamycin the second-line drug across all four groups.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic survey of German dentists' **prophylactic** ABD prescribing across four distinct medical risk groups (endocarditis risk, joint replacement, diabetes, immunosuppression/organ transplant) in a single instrument.
- Quantifies the **gap between German-language guidelines and everyday practice** per condition and per dental procedure, using an explicit consensus/trend classification (>80% = consensus; 50–80% = trend; else inconclusive).
- Confirms internationally observed patterns (Italy, Switzerland, Saudi Arabia): wide variation, tendency to over-administer for procedures lacking guidelines, amoxicillin first-line, clindamycin second-line.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Online-only survey (SoSci platform), open 10.2023–03.2024, distributed via state dental associations (Saarland, Berlin, Baden-Württemberg, Lower Saxony, Mecklenburg-Western Pomerania, Westphalia-Lippe, Thuringia).
- **537 fully completed questionnaires** from 1070 participants (incomplete excluded). ~0.5% of Germany's ~101,344 dentists.
- Systemic ABD only. Four sections × (condition subtypes + dental-procedure types). Frequency scale: never (0%) / rarely (<25%) / occasionally (25–50%) / frequently (50–75%) / very frequently (>75%) / always (100%) / not specified.
- Most- and second-most-used ABD asked per section (choices: amoxicillin, clindamycin, penicillin V, doxycycline, metronidazole, ampicillin, vancomycin, azithromycin, clarithromycin, not specified).
- Also: self-rated confidence (yes/no) and year of state examination.
- **Classification**: ≥80% "always+very often" (or "never+rarely") = positive (or negative) consensus; 50–80% = trend; otherwise inconclusive.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Group-level pattern** — positive trends only in endocarditis-risk and immunosuppression/organ-transplant; negative trend → negative consensus in diabetes and joint replacement. The only **positive consensus** in the whole survey: prosthetic heart valve (72% always, 10% very often).

- **Endocarditis prophylaxis** (Table 1): positive consensus only for prosthetic valve; positive trend for survived bacterial endocarditis (68/10) and heart transplant (69/5). Inconclusive for cyanotic defects, hypertrophic cardiomyopathy, mitral valve prolapse, acquired valvular dysfunction, pulmonary shunts. By procedure: positive trend for extraction (51/9), implantation (46/6), periodontal (43/8), bone procedures (48/10); negative leaning for mucosal/gingival injury, local anesthetic injection (Never 62%), PA probing; consensus NOT to treat for suture removal (Never 76%). Largely matches DGZMK/DGK; deviations only for gingival manipulation and local-anesthetic injection.
- **Joint replacement** (Table 3): no positive consensus/trend at all. Negative consensus (>80% never/rarely) for oral-mucosa inflammation, mucosal/gingival injury, local anesthetic (Never 81%), suture removal (Never 88%), PA probing. Negative trend for >2-yr post-op, hip replacement, extraction, periodontal, trauma. **Discrepancy** with German Society for Endoprosthetics (AE), which recommends a single preop 2 g amoxicillin for invasive procedures.
- **Diabetes** (Table 4): most negative-consensus questions of any group. Negative consensus against ABD in type 1 and type 2 DM, oral-mucosa inflammation, mucosal/gingival injury, local anesthetic (Never 84%), PA probing, suture removal. Negative trend for poorly-controlled DM, extraction, periodontal, trauma. Only German guideline (DGZMK + German Diabetes Society) recommends ABD at **implantation** — survey's ambiguous approach deviates.
- **Immunosuppression / organ transplant** (Table 5): highest compliance after endocarditis. Positive trend for immunosuppressive disease (42/14), immunosuppressive meds (37/16), post-transplant (51/12), and for bone procedures (43/11), extraction (52/12), implantation (46/6). DGZMK S2k guideline recommends ABD before dentoalveolar procedures (single 2 g amoxicillin; 2nd choice 0.6 g clindamycin); not followed for periodontal procedures or trauma.
- **Drug choice**: amoxicillin first-line, clindamycin second-line in **all four groups** — consistent with guidelines (clindamycin reserved for penicillin allergy).
- **Confidence**: ~75% feel confident using prophylactic ABD despite heterogeneous behavior (authors note an "anecdotal-evidence trap").

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only ~0.5% of German dentists; voluntary, anonymous, unmonitored → multiple-response, self-selection (issue-aware dentists), and social-desirability bias possible.
- **Self-reported behavior, not actual prescription records.** ABD choices capped at 10 options.
- Not all state associations participated. Authors call for RCTs on prophylactic ABD, and for better dissemination of guidelines.

## 7. Glossary

- **ABD** — antibacterial drug(s); the paper's term for systemic antibiotics used prophylactically.
- **DGZMK** — German Society of Dentistry and Oral Medicine.
- **DGK** — German Society for Cardiology and Cardiovascular Research.
- **AE** — German Society for Endoprosthetics.
- **AHA** — American Heart Association (2007 endocarditis-prophylaxis guideline).
- **S2k guideline** — German consensus-based clinical guideline (structured expert consensus, no systematic evidence grading).
- **Consensus vs trend** — ≥80% agreement = consensus; 50–80% = trend (study's own thresholds).
