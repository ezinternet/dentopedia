## One-line Summary
Cureus 2025: Narrative review (2015-2025 literature): future of implants — zirconia/Ti-Zr alloys, AI/robotics/3D printing, bioactive surfaces, smart implants; clinical adoption limited by cost, learning curves, inconsistent long-term data.

## 1. Key Results
- Literature 2015–2025 (PubMed, Scopus, Google Scholar)
- Materials: titanium → zirconia, Ti-Zr alloys, scaffold-based designs for enhanced integration
- Digital technologies: AI, robotics, 3D printing → improved surgical precision and planning
- Bioactive surfaces + regenerative biomolecules → better healing outcomes
- Smart implants with sensors: real-time monitoring potential
- Limitations: high cost, steep learning curves, inconsistent long-term data, regulatory gaps

## 2. Clinical Implications
- Zirconia implants and Ti-Zr alloys emerging as viable titanium alternatives
- AI/robotic placement shows promise but remains niche in mainstream practice
- Minimally invasive techniques and smart diagnostics = future direction
- Patient-centered factors (comfort, cost, accessibility) must guide technology adoption
