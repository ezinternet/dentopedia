## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive review of factors influencing IOS accuracy: device technology, scanning strategy, arch size, tooth preparation design, environmental factors, operator experience
- Analyzes structured light, confocal microscopy, active wavefront sampling, and photogrammetry technologies
- Larger arches (full arch vs quadrant) show greater cumulative error — key clinical implication for full-arch implant cases
- Powder application: reduces some error but alters surface texture; modern powder-free IOS generally comparable
- Temperature and moisture can affect scan accuracy for some systems

## 3. Methodology
- **Database**: PubMed; keyword search
- **Type**: Narrative review; no systematic search protocol or PRISMA
- **Factors evaluated**: Scanner technology, scanning strategy, arch size, tooth presence/type, operator factors, environmental conditions

## 4. Key Results
- **Full arch vs quadrant**: Full arch scanning accumulates greater error (critical for implant full-arch cases)
- **Technology**: Structured light and confocal-based systems perform comparably in most settings; no single superior technology
- **Operator experience**: Significant factor; consistent scanning strategy reduces error
- **Edentulous arches**: Reduced anatomical landmarks → greater IOS error vs dentate arches

## 5. Limitations
- Narrative review; no meta-analysis or systematic quality assessment
- Rapid technology evolution makes some findings outdated quickly
- Heterogeneous accuracy metrics across studies (trueness vs precision)
