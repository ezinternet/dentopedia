## Why Ingested

레진 복합재 라미네이트 비니어의 생존율에 대한 체계적 SR+MA가 부재하여 세라믹 비니어와의 비교 근거를 보완하기 위해 인제스트. [[veneers/klein-2025-ceramic-laminate-veneer-survival-complications-sr-ma]]에서 다루는 세라믹 비니어 생존율과 직접 대비되는 레진 계열 근거를 제공한다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 7 studies (3 RCTs + 4 cohorts): resin composite laminate veneers showed 88% overall pooled survival rate (mean follow-up 24–97 months), with direct veneers outperforming indirect (91% vs 84%).

## 한줄요약

7편 연구(RCT 3 + 코호트 4) SR+MA: 레진 복합재 라미네이트 비니어 생존율 88%(추적관찰 24–97개월), 직접법(91%)이 간접법(84%)보다 우수.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of Evidence-Based Dental Practice
- **Volume/Issue**: Vol. 23, Issue 4, December 2023, 101911
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.jebdp.2023.101911
- **Registration**: PROSPERO CRD42022336857
- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA specifically focusing on resin composite (not ceramic) laminate veneers
- Identified 7 eligible studies from 827 initially screened
- Reported pooled survival rates separately for direct vs indirect approaches
- Catalogued mechanical and aesthetic complications: surface roughness, color mismatch, marginal discoloration most frequent
- Highlighted critical gap: published evidence comparing direct vs indirect resin composite is scarce

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases**: MEDLINE (PubMed), Scopus, Cochrane Central Register; hand search of reference lists
- **Time range**: 1998 to May 2022
- **Inclusion**: RCTs and cohort studies, minimum 2-year follow-up, permanent dentition
- **Studies included**: 7 total (3 RCTs, 4 cohort studies)
- **Quality assessment**: 3 published scales for quality and risk of bias
- **Statistical method**: Meta-analysis with pooled survival rates; heterogeneity by I² statistic
- **Heterogeneity (RCTs)**: I² = 50.5% (P = .108)
- **Inter-rater reliability**: Cohen's κ = 0.81 at title/abstract screening stage

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Overall pooled survival rate**: 88% (95% CI: 81%–94%), mean follow-up 24–97 months
- **Direct laminate veneer survival**: 91%
- **Indirect laminate veneer survival**: 84%
- **Most common complications**: surface roughness > color mismatch > marginal discoloration
- **Complication characterization**: most complications clinically acceptable with or without reintervention
- **Note on evidence**: studies on resin composite veneers dating to 1990s, yet comparative direct-vs-indirect evidence remains sparse

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 7 studies included despite broad search; indirect resin composite comparisons especially underrepresented
- Moderate heterogeneity in RCT subset (I² 50.5%) limits pooling precision
- Short to medium mean follow-up (24–97 months); long-term ≥10-year data largely absent
- Authors recommend high-quality RCTs with standardized outcome reporting for indirect resin composite veneers

## 6. Related Work

- Gresnigt et al. 2019 (J Dent): RCT indirect resin composite vs ceramic veneers, up to 10-year findings
- Mesjering et al. 1998 (J Dent): 2.5-year clinical trial on three veneer types
- Gresnigt et al. 2012 (J Dent): RCT direct laminate veneers with two micro-hybrid composites
- Crins et al. 2021 (Dent Mater): RCT direct and indirect composites in severe tooth wear patients
- Mazzetti et al. 2022 (Dent Mater): 10-year practice-based evaluation ceramic and direct composite veneers

## 7. Glossary

- **Laminate veneer**: thin shell restoration bonded to the facial surface of teeth for esthetic correction
- **Direct veneer**: fabricated chairside directly onto the tooth using composite resin placement and sculpting
- **Indirect veneer**: fabricated on a dental model/cast in the laboratory, then bonded to the prepared tooth
- **I²**: heterogeneity statistic; values 25/50/75% reflect low/moderate/high heterogeneity
- **PRISMA**: Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses — reporting guideline
- **PROSPERO**: International prospective register of systematic reviews
