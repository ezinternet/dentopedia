## Why Ingested

A methodological caution flag for how we read TMD arthrocentesis meta-analyses. This Letter to the Editor (Fuentes-Barría 2026, *Jpn Dent Sci Rev*) argues that the Valenzuela-Fuenzalida et al. arthrocentesis SR+MA pools fundamentally non-comparable comparators (splints, physiotherapy, PRP/HA injections, arthroscopy, arthrocentesis variants) under extreme heterogeneity (I² 85–98%), so its pooled estimates lack a clinical referent. It `refines` (qualifies) our existing arthrocentesis SR+MA pages by warning that their high-I² pooled effects may reflect between-study dispersion rather than a real treatment effect — directly relevant to how confidently we read [[tmj/ijoms-2023-arthrocentesis-conservative-tmd-sr-ma]] (+1.12 mm MMO, high heterogeneity) and [[tmj/zhang-2024-arthrocentesis-injectable-agent-tmd-sr]]. Ingested as a critical-appraisal lens, not a new clinical result. According to PubMed; [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jdsr.2026.04.004).

## One-line Summary
Letter to the Editor critiquing a TMD arthrocentesis SR+MA: pooling mechanistically distinct comparators under I²=85–98% heterogeneity (plus SMD on a mm scale and mixed diagnoses) leaves the pooled estimates clinically uninterpretable.

## 2. Key Contributions

A structured critique that the targeted arthrocentesis meta-analysis is statistically valid in form but clinically uninterpretable in substance, because:
1. It pools mechanistically distinct comparators into one summary estimate (conflates separate clinical questions).
2. It reports extreme between-study heterogeneity (I² 85–98%) without adequate subgroup explanation.
3. It uses standardized mean difference (SMD) for outcomes already on a common, clinically meaningful scale (millimeters).
4. It mixes heterogeneous diagnostic entities (disc displacement, osteoarthritis, arthralgia, other joint conditions) into pooled estimates.

The broader contribution is a transferable appraisal lesson: a random-effects model does not legitimize pooling when the underlying clinical/statistical heterogeneity is extreme.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

No new data or analysis — this is an opinion/commentary letter. The author appraises the target meta-analysis against standard meta-analytic principles: requirement of a shared underlying effect for a pooled estimate to be interpretable, the meaning of I² thresholds, the case for pre-specified subgroup analysis to explain heterogeneity, and the rationale for choosing mean difference vs SMD by outcome scale.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Core claims (qualitative; the letter cites figures from the target paper):
- **Comparator pooling**: occlusal splints, physiotherapy, intra-articular injections (PRP and hyaluronic acid), arthroscopic surgery, and multiple arthrocentesis variants were combined — "beyond acceptable clinical diversity," so the pooled effect "lacks a clear clinical referent."
- **Heterogeneity**: I² 85–98% across most outcomes. At this magnitude, statistically significant (especially large) effects are "at high risk of being driven by between-study dispersion rather than reflecting a consistent treatment effect," and random-effects modeling does not rescue interpretability.
- **Subgroups**: no sufficiently robust or pre-specified subgroup analyses to explain variability → principal heterogeneity source left unaddressed.
- **Outcome metric**: SMD applied to outcomes measured in mm (e.g., maximum mouth opening / maximum interincisal opening) is hard to justify — reduces clinical interpretability and can artificially inflate/deflate effect sizes via dependence on within-study variance. Even analyzed separately, MMO and MIO are not necessarily comparable across settings.
- **Population mixing**: heterogeneous diagnoses (disc displacement, OA, arthralgia, other) pooled into overall estimates obscure applicability to specific patient groups.
- **Bottom line**: pooled estimates may reflect statistical aggregation of non-comparable data, not clinically meaningful summaries.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-author letter, no quantitative reanalysis; arguments are conceptual, not empirically re-tested.
- Critiques one specific meta-analysis; does not propose a fully reworked analysis.
- Recommended path forward: clinically coherent comparisons, rigorous handling of heterogeneity, and outcome standardization to support valid, actionable conclusions.

## 7. Glossary

- **Heterogeneity (I²)**: proportion of total variation across studies due to between-study differences rather than chance; ~85–98% denotes extreme inconsistency.
- **Random-effects model**: meta-analytic model assuming true effects vary across studies; accommodates but does not justify extreme heterogeneity.
- **SMD (Standardized Mean Difference)**: effect size scaled by pooled SD; used when outcomes are on different scales — questionable when the outcome already has a common interpretable unit (mm).
- **MMO / MIO**: Maximum Mouth Opening / Maximum Interincisal Opening — common mandibular-function endpoints in TMD trials.
- **Clinical referent**: the specific, interpretable clinical question/comparison a pooled estimate is meant to answer.
