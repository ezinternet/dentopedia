## One-line Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis (11 studies: 9 RCTs + 2 CCTs) of flap management strategies in alveolar ridge preservation: no significant differences in bone width or height between flapped and flapless techniques, but **flapped ridge preservation produced a statistically significant shrinkage of keratinized gingiva width (WMD -3.21 mm; 95% CI -4.10 to -2.33; p<0.00001)** vs flapless — supporting flapless ARP for keratinized tissue preservation.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: International Journal of Oral & Maxillofacial Implants, 2018; pages 613–621A
- **DOI**: 10.11607/jomi.6368
- **PubMed ID**: 29763499
- **Article type**: Systematic review with meta-analysis
- **Search**: MEDLINE, EMBASE, Cochrane Central Register (CENTRAL) through February 2017
- **Inclusion**: RCTs, CCTs, and prospective cohort studies with ≥10 subjects evaluating flap-management variations in ridge preservation

## 2. Key Contributions
- First meta-analysis to isolate **flap design (flapped vs flapless vs free gingival graft seal)** as the variable in ridge preservation, while controlling for graft material as a co-factor.
- Confirms that **bone dimensional preservation is similar** between flapped and flapless approaches.
- Quantifies the **soft-tissue penalty of flap elevation**: ~3.2 mm keratinized gingiva width loss vs flapless.
- Reframes the flap decision around soft-tissue preservation rather than bone preservation.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Electronic searches: MEDLINE (PubMed), EMBASE, Cochrane CENTRAL up to February 2017; supplemented by hand-searching.
- Eligible designs: RCTs, controlled clinical trials (CCTs), prospective cohort studies with n ≥ 10.
- Outcomes pooled by random-effects model: bone width change, bone height change, keratinized gingiva width change.
- Risk-of-bias assessment with Cochrane and Newcastle-Ottawa scales as appropriate.
- 11 studies (9 RCTs + 2 CCTs) included for qualitative synthesis; subsets pooled per outcome.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Bone width change** (flapped vs flapless): no statistically significant difference.
- **Bone height change** (flapped vs flapless): no statistically significant difference.
- **Bone width change** (free gingival graft seal vs flapless): no statistically significant difference.
- **Keratinized gingiva (KG) width change**: flapped ARP showed significantly greater shrinkage vs flapless — weighted mean difference **−3.21 mm** (95% CI −4.10 to −2.33; p<0.00001; heterogeneity I²≈51%; χ² p=0.15).
- Interpretation: flapless approaches preserve bone equivalently to flapped, with substantial KG width advantage.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small number of pooled studies per outcome; heterogeneous graft materials (DBBM, FDBA, β-TCP) confound flap-effect.
- Search cut-off February 2017 — newer flapless RCTs and tunnel-flap techniques not captured.
- KG measurement methods varied (clinical caliper vs standardised photographs).
- Long-term (>6 month) KG outcomes under-reported.

## 7. Glossary
- **Alveolar Ridge Preservation (ARP)**: socket grafting ± membrane after extraction to limit ridge resorption.
- **Flapless ARP**: extraction and graft placement without mucoperiosteal flap elevation; primary closure achieved via socket seal or open healing.
- **Flapped ARP**: mucoperiosteal flap elevated, graft placed, often with primary closure pulling KG coronally.
- **Free Gingival Graft (FGG)**: epithelialised palatal graft used to seal socket entrance over the graft.
- **Keratinized Gingiva (KG) Width**: bucco-coronal dimension of attached keratinized mucosa measured from MGJ to free gingival margin.
- **WMD**: weighted mean difference in random-effects meta-analysis.
