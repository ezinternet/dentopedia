## Why Ingested

Two parallel examiner-blind RCTs confirming a new anhydrous 0.454% stannous fluoride (SnF₂) toothpaste beats a standard fluoride toothpaste for dentin hypersensitivity (DH) on both clinician-assessed (Schiff, Yeaple tactile) and patient-reported (LMS pain, DHEQ-48 QoL) outcomes — moving the wiki's toothpaste evidence from formulation-mapping to head-to-head efficacy. Extends [[wiki/dentin-hypersensitivity/martins-2022-desensitizing-toothpastes-formulations-scoping-review]], which maps SnF₂ among desensitizing-toothpaste active ingredients but does not adjudicate comparative efficacy.

## One-line Summary

Two examiner-blind parallel RCTs (Study 1 n=104, Study 2 n=113; 18–65 y; healthy adults with DH): twice-daily anhydrous 0.454% SnF₂ toothpaste (Test) gave significantly greater Schiff reduction, tactile-threshold increase, and LMS pain improvement than standard fluoride toothpaste (Reference) at Day 28 and 56 (all p<0.05); Study 1 also superior DHEQ-48 QoL at Day 56.

## 한줄요약

검사자 맹검 평행 RCT 2건(Study 1 n=104, Study 2 n=113; 18–65세 DH 성인): 무수 0.454% 불화주석(SnF₂) 치약(Test)을 하루 2회 사용 시 표준 불소 치약(Reference)보다 Day 28·56에서 Schiff 감소·촉각역치 증가·LMS 통증 개선이 유의하게 컸고(모두 p<0.05), Study 1은 Day 56 DHEQ-48 삶의 질도 우월.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Head-to-head superiority RCTs (×2 independent sites) of a **new** anhydrous, glycerine-based 0.454% SnF₂ toothpaste (reformulated for stain removal / organoleptic benefit) vs a standard fluoride toothpaste for DH.
- Combines **clinician-assessed** (Schiff evaporative score, Yeaple tactile threshold) and **patient-reported** outcomes (validated Labeled Magnitude Scales for pain; DHEQ-48 for oral-health-related quality of life) in one design.
- Demonstrates, for the first time, that the anhydrous SnF₂ formulation produces a **superior** LMS pain reduction versus a Reference treatment, with Schiff–LMS alignment linking the clinical indicator to perceived pain.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Two single-center, 56-day, randomized, controlled, parallel, examiner-blind trials; all products overwrapped with opaque labels. Study 1 = Salus Research (Fort Wayne, IN; ClinicalTrials.gov ID 300103). Study 2 = All Sum Research Center (Melbourne, FL; ID 300104). Veritas IRB-approved.
- **Participants**: healthy adults 18–65 y; ≥20 natural teeth; self-reported DH 6 months–10 years; ≥2 non-adjacent sensitive teeth (incisors/canines/premolars) in different quadrants with tactile threshold ≤20 g AND Schiff ≥2 at screening and baseline; erosion/abrasion/recession (EAR), MGI 0, no mobility.
- **Acclimatization**: 14–28 days with regular fluoride toothpaste (Colgate Cavity Protection, 1000 ppm F) + soft brush.
- **Intervention**: Test = 0.454% w/w SnF₂ (1100 ppm F ion); Reference = Crest Cavity Protection (1100 ppm F as NaF). Brush ≥1 min twice daily ×56 days. Test group additionally instructed to brush sensitive test teeth first ("focused brushing," per US label); Reference whole-mouth only.
- **Assessments**: Tactile = Yeaple constant-pressure probe (start 10 g, +10 g steps; threshold = pain on two consecutive applications; higher = lower sensitivity; max 80 g at Days 28/56). Evaporative = 1-s air blast 1 cm from dentin, Schiff 0–3. LMS = four 100-mm scales (intensity, duration, tolerability, description). DHEQ-48 = Section 1 (everyday impact) + Section 2 (restrictions, adaptation, social impact, emotional impact, identity).
- **Stats**: mITT; primary/secondary = change-from-baseline Schiff and tactile threshold at Days 28/56, Test vs Reference; MMRM (unstructured covariance, Kenward-Roger), LSM, 95% CI, two-sided p at 5%; SAS 9.4+. 50/arm gives ≥90% power to detect a 0.4-unit Schiff difference (SD 0.6).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Enrolled**: Study 1 N=103 (Test 52 / Reference 51; mean age ~49.9 y, predominantly female). Study 2 N=112 (Test 56 / Reference 56; mean age Test 43.2 / Reference 39.7 y). (104 / 113 randomized per abstract.)
- **Schiff** (Test vs Reference, all p<0.0001):
  - Study 1: Day 28 Test −1.75 vs Reference −0.10 (diff −1.65); Day 56 Test −2.25 vs −0.31 (diff −1.94).
  - Study 2: Day 28 Test −0.79 vs −0.17 (diff −0.62); Day 56 Test −1.40 vs −0.33 (diff −1.07).
- **Tactile threshold (g)** (all p<0.0001):
  - Study 1: Day 28 Test +36.59 vs Reference +1.23 (diff +35.37); Day 56 Test +60.07 vs +2.70 (diff +57.37).
  - Study 2: Day 28 Test +16.75 vs +2.45 (diff +14.31); Day 56 Test +41.13 vs +4.41 (diff +36.72).
- **LMS** (all 4 domains, both studies, both timepoints, Test vs Reference p<0.05): e.g. Intensity Day 56 — Study 1 Test −41.36 vs Reference −8.18 (diff −33.22); Study 2 Test −26.66 vs −5.99 (diff −20.67).
- **DHEQ-48**: Study 1 — Test superior to Reference at Day 56 across Section 1, total score, all domains, effect on life, global oral health (total-score diff −24.49, p=0.0002); no between-group difference at Day 28. Study 2 — within-Test improvements, but NO significant between-treatment difference at Day 28 or 56 (Reference also improved → attributed to placebo effect).
- **ADA criterion** (≥20% between-treatment difference) met by Day 28, increasing to Day 56.
- **Safety**: well tolerated, no serious adverse events. Study 1: 9 treatment-related TEAEs (8 Test in 4 participants, 1 Reference); one Test participant with 3 severe TEAEs withdrew; rest mild, all resolved. Study 2: no treatment-related TEAEs.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Asymmetric brushing instructions**: Test group used focused brushing of sensitive areas (per US label), Reference whole-mouth only — a per-protocol design choice that mirrors real-world label use but is not a pure formulation-only comparison.
- **DHEQ-48 placebo sensitivity**: Study 2 showed no between-group QoL difference (Reference also improved), attributed to placebo/Hawthorne effects in a multi-visit short trial; between-treatment QoL discrimination may be limited. Stratified/larger sampling suggested.
- Single-center per study; both US populations.

## 7. Glossary

- **DH (Dentin Hypersensitivity)** — short, sharp transient dental pain from exposed dentin (enamel loss / gingival recession), via the hydrodynamic theory.
- **SnF₂ (Stannous Fluoride)** — tubule-occluding active; stannous ion precipitates insoluble deposits onto dentin, blocking tubules. Anhydrous glycerine-based formulation stabilizes the reactive ion.
- **Schiff sensitivity score** — examiner-rated 0–3 response to a 1-s evaporative (air) stimulus.
- **Yeaple (tactile) threshold** — constant-pressure probe force (g) at which pain is reported; higher = lower sensitivity.
- **LMS (Labeled Magnitude Scale)** — validated 100-mm patient-pain scale (intensity, duration, tolerability, description).
- **DHEQ-48 (Dentine Hypersensitivity Experience Questionnaire)** — condition-specific oral-health-related quality-of-life instrument.
- **mITT (Modified Intent-to-Treat)** — randomized participants with ≥1 dose and ≥1 post-baseline assessment.
- **MMRM (Mixed Model with Repeated Measures)** — longitudinal analysis used for within/between-group change-from-baseline estimates.
