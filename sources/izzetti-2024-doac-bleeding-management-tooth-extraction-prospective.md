## Why Ingested

기존 [[drug/anticoagulants/moldovan-2023-anticoagulant-oral-surgery-bleeding-sr]] (SR) 와 [[drug/anticoagulants/inchingolo-2024-anticoagulation-therapy-dental-practice-sr]] (SR) 가 제시한 "발치 시 직접경구항응고제(Direct Oral Anticoagulant, DOAC)는 중단 불필요, 국소지혈로 충분"이라는 결론을, 49명 전향코호트의 시간순(intra/peri/post-op) 출혈 동역학으로 보강한다. 특히 DOAC 중단 여부가 출혈률에 영향을 주지 않았다는 1차 데이터가 위 SR들의 권고를 뒷받침한다.

## One-line Summary

Prospective cohort (n=49 DOAC patients, 136 extractions): ~50% had intra-/early peri-op bleeding peaking day 1 post-op, but all episodes were mild and controlled with local hemostasis; DOAC suspension did NOT reduce post-operative bleeding, while >3 teeth, flap elevation, and osteotomy raised bleeding rates.

## 한줄요약

전향코호트(DOAC 49명·발치 136개): 약 50%에서 술중·초기 술후 출혈(1일째 최고)이 있었으나 모두 경미하고 국소지혈(거즈압박·트라넥삼산)로 조절됐고, DOAC 중단은 술후 출혈을 줄이지 못함. 3개 초과 발치·판막거상·골삭제가 출혈률을 높임.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Time-resolved bleeding dynamics in DOAC patients after extraction — intra-operative, peri-operative (every 20 min to 80 min), and post-operative (daily to day 7) — rather than a single binary outcome.
2. Direct comparison of DOAC suspension vs maintenance on the same protocol: suspension did NOT reduce post-operative bleeding (only a non-significant peri-op trend).
3. Procedural risk factors (>3 teeth, flap elevation, osteotomy, posterior/complex extractions) quantified as the main drivers of bleeding, above choice to suspend.
4. Drug-level signal: rivaroxaban and apixaban associated with more frequent bleeding episodes; rivaroxaban dominated delayed (day 4–7) bleeding.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Population**: consecutive adults (>18 y) on DOACs requiring ≥1 extraction, 2021–2023. Excluded: antiplatelet/VKA therapy, coagulopathies. Pre-op CBC, PT, PTT, INR.
- **n = 49** completing (21 F, mean age 72.2 ± 8.3 y), 136 extractions. Drugs: rivaroxaban 17, apixaban 16, edoxaban 8, dabigatran 8. Mean DOAC duration 38 mo.
- **Suspension**: 33/49 (67%) interrupted pre-op (mean suspension 28.48 ± 13.04 h); all refrained ≥24 h after surgery. Indications: AF 55.1%, DVT 24.5%, ischemic cardiomyopathy 18.3%, PE 16.3%.
- **Surgery**: standardized minimally invasive; mepivacaine 2% + epi 1:100,000; collagen sponge + interrupted absorbable sutures; **no additional hemostatic measure placed prophylactically**. Stratified by teeth count (≤3 vs >3), flap, osteotomy.
- **Bleeding definitions**: intra-op = obscured field; peri-op = oozing/active bleeding within 80 min uncontrolled by local measures/gauze (rescue: tranexamic-acid gauze → oxidized cellulose + sutures → diathermocoagulation); post-op = bleeding >12 h forming hematoma needing intervention/hospitalization.
- **Stats**: Kruskal–Wallis, Mann–Whitney, Wilcoxon, chi-squared/Fisher. Sample size ≥28 (alpha 0.05, power 0.9), enrolled 50.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Intra-operative bleeding**: 27/49 (55.1%). >3 extractions bled in 69.2% vs 50% for ≤3 (p<0.001). No suspended-vs-maintained difference.
- **Peri-operative bleeding**: 28/49 (57.1%) at 20 min → 38.8% at 40 min → 28.6% at 60 min → 16.3% at 80 min. Apixaban vs rivaroxaban differed at 20 min. More frequent posterior vs anterior (81% vs 64%) and complex vs simple (26% vs 13%). No suspended-vs-maintained difference at any timepoint.
- **Post-operative bleeding**: peaked day 1 — 23/49 (46.9%); day 2 22.4%; day 3 8.2%; days 4–7 only rivaroxaban. Rivaroxaban vs dabigatran significant at T1 (p=0.016) and T2 (p=0.023). Non-significant trend: early post-op bleeding (T1–T2) in non-discontinuers vs delayed bleeding (T3–T7) in those who suspended.
- **All bleeding episodes were mild and manageable with gauze compression** (± tranexamic acid); none required reversal agents.
- **Drivers**: >3 teeth, flap elevation, osteotomy all significantly raised bleeding (p<0.05); rivaroxaban + apixaban highest bleeding risk.
- **Context**: bleeding ~50% here is higher than Yagyuu (10.4%), Hiroshi (1.65% dabigatran / 3.41% rivaroxaban), Patel (13%, 6% needing extra local measures) — likely because every oozing event was captured at fine 20-min intervals.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small (n=49), unbalanced across the four DOACs; no within-drug suspension stratification.
- Heterogeneous suspension protocols (selection bias); timing relative to last dose not standardized.
- No DOAC activity assays (anti-Xa) to relate drug level to bleeding.
- Surgical-procedure variability limits generalizability. Larger balanced studies needed for protocol-grade guidance.

## 6. Related Work

- Moldovan 2023 SR and Inchingolo 2024 SR — synthesized evidence supporting non-interruption of DOACs for routine extractions with local hemostasis; this cohort gives time-resolved primary data consistent with them.
- Brennan et al. (cited): comparable bleeding across DOACs (apixaban 39%, rivaroxaban 37%, dabigatran 27%); rivaroxaban bleeders had ~2x pre-extraction levels.
- van Diermen / EHRA protocols (cited): extract ≥1–3 h after dose or at trough (~12 h).

## 7. Glossary

- **DOAC** — Direct Oral Anticoagulant (dabigatran = direct thrombin inhibitor; apixaban/rivaroxaban/edoxaban = factor Xa inhibitors).
- **Peri-operative bleeding (here)** — bleeding within 80 min of surgery, monitored every 20 min, uncontrolled by routine local measures.
- **Post-operative bleeding (here)** — bleeding >12 h forming a hematoma requiring intervention/hospitalization.
- **Idarucizumab / andexanet alfa** — specific reversal agents for dabigatran / factor Xa inhibitors.
- **Local hemostatic measures** — gauze compression, tranexamic acid mouthwash/gauze, oxidized cellulose, collagen sponge, additional sutures, diathermocoagulation.
