## Why Ingested

Short implant marginal bone behavior and crown-to-implant (C:I) ratio effects are a recurring clinical question when planning posterior atrophic cases. This 2009 Korean retrospective provides early radiographic evidence comparing short vs. standard implants at 1 year and evaluates C:I ratio influence — foundational data that contextualizes newer SRs such as [[wiki/implants/anitua-2022-short-vs-longer-implants-single-crown]] and [[wiki/implants/arbildo-vega-2025-short-vs-standard-implants-edentulous-umbrella-review]].

## One-line Summary

Retrospective radiographic analysis of 40 single short implants (<10 mm) at Yonsei University found marginal bone loss comparable to standard implants at 1 year, with crown-to-implant ratio showing no statistically significant effect on bone loss.

## 한줄요약

연세대학교 단일 단근 임플란트 40개의 후향적 방사선 분석 결과, 1년 추적 시 변연골 소실이 표준 임플란트와 유사하였으며, 치관-임플란트 비율(C:I ratio)은 골 소실에 유의한 영향을 미치지 않았다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Implantology (Korean journal), Vol. 13, No. 3, 2009
- **Institution**: Department of Prosthodontics, Kangnam Severance Hospital, College of Dentistry, Yonsei University, Seoul, Korea
- **Study type**: Retrospective clinical study
- **Sample**: 40 single short implant cases drawn from 389 short implant cases installed at Yongdong Severance Dental Hospital from 2003
- **Implant system**: Brånemark system (machined surface, hexed/headed, short fixtures ≤10 mm)
- **Follow-up**: 1 year post-loading
- **Software**: UTHSCSA ImageTool for marginal bone measurement; SAS for Windows v8 for statistics

## 2. Key Contributions

1. One of the earlier Korean-language retrospective studies specifically comparing marginal bone level (MBL) changes between short (<10 mm) and standard (≥10 mm) single implants at 1 year.
2. Evaluated crown-to-implant (C:I) ratio effects on marginal bone loss — a clinically relevant parameter when short implants produce inherently high C:I ratios.
3. Provides reference data for the Brånemark machined-surface short implant, an older implant design, offering historical context for modern roughened-surface short implant literature.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Patient selection**: 40 single short implant cases selected from 389 short implant cases; compared against standard implant controls.
- **Measurement**: Marginal bone loss measured on periapical radiographs using UTHSCSA ImageTool calibrated to 0.1 mm increments; measured mesial and distal to the implant.
- **C:I ratio measurement**: Crown length (from occlusal surface to bone crest) divided by implant length (intrabony portion); grouped into C:I ≤1 vs. C:I >1 sub-groups.
- **Statistics**: SAS for Windows v8; group comparisons with significance threshold p<0.05.
- **Positive values** = bone loss; negative values = bone apposition.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Parameter | Short implant | Standard implant | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Marginal bone loss (mm) | mean (SD reported) | mean (SD reported) | NS |
| Crown-to-implant ratio | mean (SD) | mean (SD) | — |

- **Marginal bone loss**: Short implants showed marginal bone loss values not statistically different from standard implants at 1-year follow-up (p > 0.05).
- **C:I ratio subgroup analysis**: Neither the C:I ≤1 group nor C:I >1 group showed statistically significant differences in marginal bone loss within short implants, nor compared to standard implants.
- **Bone apposition**: Some cases showed bone apposition (negative values), seen in both groups.
- **Splinting effect**: Authors note that short implants in this series were single-unit restorations, so splinting effects could not be evaluated.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Short follow-up (1 year)**: Marginal bone remodeling continues beyond year 1; long-term data needed.
- **Small sample**: 40 short implant cases; limited statistical power for subgroup analyses.
- **Machined surface**: Brånemark machined-surface fixtures used — lower BIC than modern roughened surfaces, limiting generalizability to contemporary implants.
- **Retrospective design**: Selection bias possible; no randomization.
- **Radiographic measurement only**: No clinical parameters (probing depth, bleeding on probing) reported.
- **No functional loading data**: Occlusal force, parafunctional habits not controlled.
- Authors recommend longer follow-up and larger prospective studies.

## 6. Related Work

- Tawil & Younan (2003): 12–92-month clinical evaluation of short machined-surface implants — survival >95%.
- Rokni et al. (2005): C:I ratio assessment with short sintered porous-surfaced implants — comparable findings.
- Tawil et al. (2006): Influence of prosthetic parameters (including C:I ratio) on survival/complications of short implants.
- das Neves et al. (2006): Longitudinal analysis of short implants; review of survival data.
- ten Bruggenkate et al. (1998): 6 mm non-submerged implants multicenter RCT (1–7 years).

## 7. Glossary

- **Short implant**: Fixture length <10 mm (per this study's definition; modern consensus varies 6–8 mm threshold)
- **Standard implant**: Fixture length ≥10 mm
- **Marginal bone loss (MBL)**: Radiographic crestal bone loss measured from implant shoulder to first bone-implant contact
- **Crown-to-implant ratio (C:I ratio)**: Crown height (from occlusal table to bone crest) ÷ implant intrabony length; >1 means crown longer than implant
- **UTHSCSA ImageTool**: Free radiographic measurement software (University of Texas Health Science Center San Antonio)
- **Bone apposition**: Net bone gain (negative value in this study's convention)
- **BIC (Bone-to-Implant Contact)**: Percentage of implant surface in direct contact with bone
