## Why Ingested

Extends the etiologic picture of persistent apical periodontitis ([[wiki/endodontics/diagnosis/nair-2006-causes-persistent-apical-periodontitis-review]], [[wiki/endodontics/diagnosis/karamifar-2020-endodontic-periapical-lesion-an-overview]]) from "which microbes persist" to "which microbial *virulence factors* (LPS/endotoxin, LTA) drive the lesion size and symptoms," quantifying both Gram-negative and Gram-positive byproducts in retreatment cases and linking them to clinical signs and coronal restoration quality.

## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional study (n=40 retreatment teeth with apical periodontitis): LPS (mean 5.1 EU/mL) and LTA (mean 537.5 pg/mL) detected in all canals; higher LPS correlated with larger periapical radiolucency and presence of clinical signs/symptoms, and defective coronal restoration increased virulence-factor load.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies BOTH Gram-negative (LPS/endotoxin) and Gram-positive (LTA) virulence factors in the SAME secondary/persistent endodontic infections — most prior work isolated one.
- Links endotoxin load to a hard radiographic outcome (periapical radiolucency area, measured in ImageJ) rather than only to bacterial counts.
- Identifies coronal restoration quality as a direct modifier of intracanal virulence-factor burden.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 40 patients (19–69 y) referred for retreatment; all teeth previously root-filled with radiographic apical periodontitis and defective filling and/or coronal restoration and/or persistent symptoms.
- Strict contamination control: gamma-sterilised instruments (Co-60), rubber dam, H2O2/NaOCl crown disinfection inactivated with sodium thiosulphate, swab culture + PCR sterility check (positive cases excluded), LPS-free (LAL) water.
- **LPS** quantified by Limulus amebocyte lysate (LAL) assay (EU/mL); **LTA** quantified by ELISA (pg/mL).
- Periapical radiolucency area measured as mean of 3 ImageJ readings.
- Exclusions: antibiotics within 3 months, ASA-III, non-isolatable teeth, absent coronal restoration, periodontal pockets ≥3 mm.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- LPS and LTA detected in 100% of samples.
- Mean LPS 5.1 EU/mL (±2.83); mean LTA 537.53 pg/mL (±134.14).
- Higher LPS associated with larger periapical bone-resorption area and with presence of clinical signs/symptoms.
- Coronal restoration quality directly influenced LPS/LTA load in secondary-infection canals.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Cross-sectional, single-centre, n=40 — associative not causal.
- No microbial identity correlation reported in this extract (virulence factors quantified, species not the focus).
- Periapical area is 2D radiographic (not CBCT volumetric).

## 7. Glossary

- **LPS (lipopolysaccharide / endotoxin)**: outer-membrane component of Gram-negative bacteria; pro-inflammatory, bone-resorptive.
- **LTA (lipoteichoic acid)**: Gram-positive cell-wall component (e.g. E. faecalis); promotes biofilm adhesion and cytokine release.
- **LAL assay**: Limulus amebocyte lysate test for endotoxin quantification.
- **Secondary/persistent endodontic infection**: infection in previously root-treated teeth, driving retreatment need.
