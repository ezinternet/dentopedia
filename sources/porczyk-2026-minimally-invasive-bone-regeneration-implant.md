## One-line Summary

Narrative review of three minimally invasive bone regeneration strategies for implants — Bone Core Technique, SPAL, and IDR — framed within an indication-driven clinical decision-making pathway; evidence strength is uneven across techniques and conventional augmentation remains indispensable in advanced defects.

## 한줄요약

임플란트 골재생의 최소침습 3가지 술식(골코어 기법, SPAL, IDR)을 결손 형태 기반 임상 결정 프레임워크로 정리한 서술 고찰; 술식별 근거 수준은 불균등하고 광범위 결손에서는 conventional 증대술이 여전히 필수.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Indication-driven decision framework**: Proposes a clinical algorithm (Figure 1) integrating defect morphology, implant timing, and biologic priority to select among Bone Core Technique, SPAL, IDR, and conventional augmentation.
2. **Three minimally invasive strategies reviewed**:
   - **Bone Core Technique**: autogenous bone cylinder harvested from the implant osteotomy itself; avoids second donor site; strongest dedicated evidence (prospective study, n=186, ≥5y follow-up).
   - **SPAL (Sub-Periosteal Peri-implant Augmented Layer)**: subperiosteal pouch creation to preserve periosteal vascular envelope; corrects buccal peri-implant dehiscence with limited flap elevation; emerging histologic evidence but limited comparative data.
   - **IDR (Immediate Dentoalveolar Restoration)**: extraction + immediate implant + simultaneous hard/soft tissue reconstruction in esthetic zone; high technique sensitivity; evidence largely from broader immediate implant literature.
3. **Biologic framework**: Each technique prioritizes a distinct biologic compartment — osteogenic potential (Bone Core), periosteal vascularity (SPAL), or socket architecture/contour (IDR).
4. **Critical appraisal**: Minimal invasiveness ≠ universal superiority; case selection and operator experience are the defining success factors.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Structured non-systematic literature search; no formal PRISMA, risk-of-bias assessment, or meta-analysis.
- Priority to clinical studies, prospective/retrospective cohorts, case series, technique descriptions, and biologically oriented conceptual papers.
- Foundational landmark papers retained regardless of publication date.
- Represents structured critical appraisal, not evidence-based clinical guideline.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Bone Core Technique
- Prospective study: n=186 consecutively treated patients, ≥5 years follow-up, favorable hard-tissue outcomes in localized peri-implant defects.
- Indication: localized peri-implant dehiscence-type defects amenable to limited autogenous augmentation without second donor site.
- Limitation: restricted graft volume; not applicable for large horizontal/vertical deficiencies.

### SPAL
- Limited to retrospective observations, case reports, and one histologic case report.
- Supports biologic plausibility of subperiosteal augmentation with periosteal preservation.
- Needs stronger validation for standardized clinical use.

### IDR
- Dominated by case reports and technique-driven descriptions.
- High-risk esthetic zone; combines extraction, implant, bone graft (e.g., maxillary tuberosity autograft), and provisionalization in one stage.
- Much cited evidence actually derives from broader immediate implant literature, not IDR-specific studies.
- Requires primary stability, prosthetic control, and advanced surgical expertise.

### Comparative Appraisal
- Evidence hierarchy: Bone Core > SPAL > IDR for protocol-specific data.
- Conventional GBR (e.g., resorbable membranes + DBBM) remains the reference standard for extensive defects.
- Direct patient-reported outcome comparisons (pain, swelling, recovery time) are scarce across all three techniques.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- No systematic methods or meta-analysis; cannot quantify treatment effects.
- Heterogeneous evidence base; most available studies are technique-driven and expert-center-derived.
- Soft-tissue outcomes (mucosal thickness, volumetric stability, PES) inconsistently reported.
- Future research priorities: standardized outcome measures, comparative prospective RCTs, longer follow-up, learning-curve documentation.

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Bone Core Technique | Minimally invasive augmentation using an autogenous bone cylinder harvested from the implant osteotomy itself |
| SPAL (Sub-Periosteal Peri-implant Augmented Layer) | Technique placing graft material into a subperiosteal pouch to preserve periosteal vascularity while correcting buccal dehiscence |
| IDR (Immediate Dentoalveolar Restoration) | One-stage protocol combining extraction, immediate implant placement, and simultaneous hard/soft tissue reconstruction in the esthetic zone |
| Periosteal preservation | Surgical principle of maintaining intact periosteal vascular supply to support wound healing and contour stability |
| Indication-driven decision-making | Clinical framework selecting technique based on defect morphology, biologic priority, and operator capability rather than universal protocol preference |
| Buccal dehiscence | Peri-implant buccal bone defect where the implant surface is exposed through an opening in the buccal plate |
