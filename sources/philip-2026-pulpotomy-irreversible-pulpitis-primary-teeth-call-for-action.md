## One-line Summary

Narrative review with structured search (7 clinical studies + 2 meta-analyses): pulpotomy for primary teeth clinically diagnosed with irreversible pulpitis achieved 95–100% clinical and 90–100% radiographic success at 6–12 months using calcium silicate cements, supporting it as a minimally invasive alternative to pulpectomy and calling for international guideline revision.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Histological reappraisal**: Contemporary immunocytochemical studies show primary and permanent tooth pulps have similar vascularity and immune-cell distribution; the traditional "primary pulp is more vulnerable" dogma is outdated. Dhillon et al. (2023) found clinical diagnosis of irreversible pulpitis matched histological irreversible pulpal inflammation in less than half of primary molars evaluated.
2. **New diagnostic nomenclature** (Table 2): Proposes four primary-tooth pulpitis stages — Initial pulpitis, Mild pulpitis, Moderate pulpitis, Severe pulpitis — with corresponding histological characteristics and treatment options, adapting the Wolters et al. (2017) permanent-tooth framework to primary dentition.
3. **Clinical evidence synthesis**: 7 studies reviewed (3 RCTs, 2 prospective NRCTs, 1 retrospective cohort, 1 prospective single-arm) plus 2 meta-analyses (Lin et al. 2024; Chawla et al. 2026). Calcium silicate cements (MTA, Biodentine, iRoot BP Plus, CEM, BCP) with PMC restoration yielded clinical success 95–100%, radiographic success 90–100% at 6–12 months.
4. **Proposed procedural protocol**: Detailed stepwise protocol covering pre-operative clinical/radiographic assessment, rubber dam isolation, carious tissue excavation, pulp chamber deroofing and vitality assessment (uniformly reddish-pink vascular tissue = vital), coronal pulp amputation, NaOCl haemostasis (0.5–5%) within 10 minutes, calcium silicate cement (2–3 mm) + PMC or zirconia crown.
5. **Call for action**: Explicitly requests revision of international guidelines (AAPD, EAPD) to incorporate minimally invasive pulpotomy as a recognized treatment option alongside pulpectomy for irreversible pulpitis in primary teeth.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Literature search**: MEDLINE, Embase, Web of Science, Cochrane Library, searched up to January 2026. Boolean operators combining: 'pulpotomy', 'coronal pulpotomy', 'pulpectomy', 'extraction', 'irreversible pulpitis', 'primary teeth', 'primary molars', 'deciduous teeth', 'deciduous molars'. Reference lists manually screened.

**Inclusion criteria**: (1) pulpotomies in primary teeth with clinical signs/symptoms of irreversible pulpitis; (2) RCTs, non-randomised clinical trials, prospective/retrospective cohort studies, case–control studies; (3) minimum 6-month post-treatment follow-up; (4) English language. Data extracted: country/year, study design, sample size, patient age, tooth type, haemostasis agent/time, pulpotomy medicament, final restoration type, follow-up periods, clinical/radiographic outcomes.

**Design note**: The paper describes this as a "narrative review" with "a structured and transparent literature search designed to strengthen the methodological clarity of this narrative review, rather than to constitute a formal systematic review" — no risk-of-bias assessment was performed.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Clinical success rates (Table 4)**:
- Philip et al. 2025 (MTA): 100% at 6 months
- Sabbagh et al. 2024 (CEM): 98.6% at 6 months; 97.1% at ≥12 months
- Al-Nassar et al. 2023 (MTA): 95% at 6 & ≥12 months; (BCP): 100% at 6 & ≥12 months
- Hu et al. 2023 (iRoot BP Plus): 98.9% at 18 months
- Eshghi et al. 2022 (MTA): 96.15%; (BD): 100% at follow-up
- Al-Awwad et al. 2021 (MTA): 100%; (FC): 75%; (PRF): 91.7% at 6 months
- Memarpour et al. 2016 (CEM): 97.8% at 6 months; 92.8% at ≥12 months

**Radiographic success rates**:
- Philip et al. 2025 (MTA): 93.9% at 6 months
- Sabbagh et al. 2024 (CEM): 98.6% at 6 months; 96.4% at ≥12 months
- Al-Nassar et al. 2023 (MTA/BCP): 95–100% at 6 & ≥12 months
- Hu et al. 2023: 95.5% at 18 months
- Memarpour et al. 2016 (CEM): 97.8% at 6 months; 90.4% at ≥12 months

**Meta-analyses**:
- Lin et al. 2024 (5 studies, 266 primary teeth, children 3–9 years): pulpotomy is a prospective alternative to conventional pulpectomy for irreversible pulpitis.
- Chawla et al. 2026: pulpotomy is effective, minimally invasive alternative to pulpectomy (clinical and radiographic success comparison).

**Key case selection criteria for pulpotomy (intra-operative gate)**: vital pulp confirmed visually (reddish-pink tissue); haemostasis achieved within 10 minutes with NaOCl; no clinical swelling/sinus tract; no periapical radiolucency; no pathological root resorption; early-stage furcal radiolucency acceptable (not extensive).

**Diagnostic revision**: Traditional haemostasis cut-off of 5 minutes reconsidered; up to 10 minutes acceptable for primary teeth. Formocresol inferior to MTA (FC 75% vs MTA 100% clinical success, Al-Awwad 2021).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative synthesis without formal risk-of-bias assessment; not a systematic review.
- Most studies are NRCTs; limited high-quality RCT evidence; small sample sizes.
- Short follow-up (6–18 months); long-term data beyond 2 years lacking.
- Clinical diagnosis of irreversible pulpitis inaccurate (Dhillon et al. 2023: <50% histological concordance); objective pulpal biomarkers still absent.
- Haemostasis time as a surrogate for pulpal inflammation status is questioned.
- Authors acknowledge more high-quality comparative research is needed to determine long-term success and refine protocols.
- The proposed new diagnostic nomenclature for primary tooth pulpitis (Table 2) requires prospective validation before widespread clinical use.

## 7. Glossary

- **Irreversible pulpitis (primary tooth)**: clinical diagnosis (based on spontaneous/lingering pain, percussion sensitivity) traditionally requiring pulpectomy or extraction; proposed here as a spectrum rather than a binary state.
- **Pulpotomy**: removal of coronal pulp tissue, retaining vital radicular pulp sealed with a bioactive medicament; less invasive than pulpectomy.
- **Calcium silicate cements (CSCs)**: bioactive, hydrophilic, dimensionally stable materials (MTA, Biodentine, iRoot BP Plus, CEM, BCP) with biocompatible, immunomodulatory, and osteogenic properties used as pulpotomy medicaments.
- **PMC (preformed metal crown)**: preferred post-pulpotomy final restoration; preformed zirconia crowns offered as an aesthetic alternative.
- **Haemostasis time**: time to achieve bleeding control at radicular pulp amputation site; used as surrogate for inflammation depth; 10 minutes proposed as upper limit for primary teeth.
- **NaOCl (sodium hypochlorite)**: preferred haemostatic agent (0.5–5%) over saline (lacks disinfection) and ferric sulphate (masks true pulp status).
- **Furcal radiolucency**: primary molar equivalent of periapical radiolucency in permanent teeth; early-stage indicates inflammatory product release through pulp chamber floor, not necessarily necrosis.
