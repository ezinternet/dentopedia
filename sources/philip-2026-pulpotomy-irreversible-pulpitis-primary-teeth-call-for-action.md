## One-line Summary

Abstract-only narrative review (7 clinical studies + 2 meta-analyses): pulpotomy for primary teeth clinically diagnosed with irreversible pulpitis achieved 95–100% clinical and 90–100% radiographic success at 6–12 months, supporting it as a minimally invasive alternative to pulpectomy and calling for guideline revision.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Synthesizes histologic + clinical evidence that radicular pulp is often treatable by pulpotomy even when clinically labeled irreversible pulpitis.
- Proposes revised diagnostic considerations and procedural guidelines; calls for international guideline revision.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Structured search MEDLINE/Embase/WoS/Cochrane; clinical studies + systematic reviews of pulpotomy outcomes in primary teeth with irreversible pulpitis. (Abstract-level only.)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- 7 clinical studies + 2 meta-analyses synthesized.
- Clinical success 95–100%, radiographic success 90–100% at 6-month–1-year follow-up.
- Meta-analyses: pulpotomy a viable alternative to pulpectomy for irreversible pulpitis in primary teeth.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative synthesis (not a fresh meta-analysis); abstract-only; case selection (true pulp status) remains the key uncertainty.

## 7. Glossary

- **Irreversible pulpitis (primary tooth)**: clinical diagnosis traditionally treated by pulpectomy/extraction.
- **Pulpotomy**: coronal pulp removal, retaining vital radicular pulp.
