## One-line Summary
ITI Consensus Conference SR+MA (COIR 2023 Suppl 26, n=3118 publications screened / 68 included, mean 37.2 implants per study, mean follow-up 2.8 y): 상악 심미부위 single tooth 즉시식립 + 즉시부하 (Type 1A) selection criteria — 엄격한 환자·부위 선정 시 high survival; facial gap >2 mm (+3.1 PP, p=0.04) + 근관 감염 부재 (+2.6 PP, p=0.004) 유의한 우호 인자.

## 2. Key Contributions
1. **ITI Consensus reference** for Type 1A (immediate placement + immediate loading) selection criteria in maxillary esthetic zone single tooth replacement.
2. Quantifies the impact of selection criteria (facial gap dimension, endodontic infection, study era pre/post 2012).
3. Updates and clarifies the long-standing ITI implant timing classification.

## 3. Methodology
- MEDLINE + Embase + Cochrane search
- 3,118 publications screened → 68 included
- Mean 37.2 implants/study; mean follow-up 2.8 years
- Univariate risk group comparison; study time-correcting MA (bias-corrected, study size-weighed bootstrap tests)
- Type 1A definition (per Gallucci et al. 2018 ITI consensus): immediate implant placement on same day as extraction + immediate restoration/loading on same day or within 1 week.

## 4. Key Results
[근거강함]
| Factor | Effect on survival | p-value |
|---|---|---|
| Studies before vs since 2012 | Pre-2012 lower by 1.9 PP (95% CI -0.3, -4.0) | 0.02 |
| Facial gap >2 mm vs ≤2 mm | Higher by 3.1 PP (95% CI 0.2, 5.3) | 0.04 |
| Endodontic infection absent | Higher by 2.6 PP (95% CI 0.9, 5.1) | 0.004 |

Conclusion: Type 1A demonstrates high survival in studies reporting **strict patient and site selection**. Further research needed on esthetic and functional success.

## 5. Limitations
- 68 included studies have highly selective inclusion/exclusion criteria — survival rates reflect best-case selection, not real-world cases
- Mean follow-up 2.8 y — limited long-term data
- Heterogeneity in Type 1A definition and outcome reporting
- Esthetic and functional success outcomes inadequately reported across literature
