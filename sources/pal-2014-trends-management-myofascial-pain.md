## One-line Summary

Narrative/management review (orofacial maxillofacial perspective) placing the spray-and-stretch technique within a two-phase treatment algorithm for masticatory myofascial pain — alongside occlusal splints, massage, ultrasound, TENS, LLLT, biofeedback, trigger-point injection (saline/LA/botulinum toxin), muscle relaxants (tizanidine), and tricyclic antidepressants.

## 한줄요약

악안면 영역 근막통 관리 리뷰 — 분사신장(spray-and-stretch)을 교합 스플린트·물리치료(마사지·초음파·TENS·LLLT·바이오피드백)·트리거포인트 주사(생리식염수·국소마취제·보툴리눔)·근이완제(tizanidine)·삼환계 항우울제를 포함한 2단계 치료 알고리즘 안에 위치시킴.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Locates **masticatory myofascial pain** explicitly within the TMD classification (Bell/Okeson) and separates it from myofascial pain dysfunction syndrome (Laskin 1969) and fibromyalgia.
- Comprehensive **treatment menu** for orofacial myofascial pain organized into a two-phase algorithm, with spray-and-stretch as one of the physical-therapy/trigger-point modalities.
- Maps **facial trigger-point referral patterns** (teeth, alveolar ridge, tongue, palate, temporal/frontal, TMJ, ear, pharynx, occiput).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Literature search (MEDLINE, PubMed, Google Scholar; keywords "myofascial", "trigger points", "myospasms", "myofascial pain dysfunction syndrome", "facial myalgia", "taut bands") plus textbooks. No formal inclusion/quality criteria → narrative review.
- Organized by diagnostic process (palpation gold standard; jaw-tracking/sonography/vibratography/sEMG and RDC/TMD as limited adjuncts) and by treatment modality.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

(Review — no pooled effect estimates.) Modality-level claims:
- **Physical therapy**: deep stroking massage; ultrasound (high-power favored over conventional for acute pain, Majlesi & Unalan 2004); TENS (high-frequency/high-intensity reduces myofascial pain, Graff-Radford 1989); LLLT (analgesia via microcirculation/endorphin/gate mechanisms); biofeedback (EMG frontalis/masseter); chiropractic ischemic compression; **stretch-and-spray technique**.
- **Occlusal splint therapy** for neuromuscular harmony.
- **Trigger-point injection** (saline, local anesthetic, botulinum toxin A); evidence modest; ultrasound-guided more effective.
- **Pharmacology**: tizanidine (first-line muscle relaxant), topical thiocolchicoside, tricyclic antidepressants (amitriptyline ≤75 mg/day cardio-safe) for sleep + central sensitization.
- Goals stated: reduce pain, **restore normal mouth opening**, improve QoL.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative, not systematic → selection bias, no quality grading or pooled estimates; many cited modalities ("ultrasound", "spray-and-stretch") flagged in the text as low-evidence/empirical.
- Older (2014) evidence base; predates current TMD axis-II / pain-science emphasis.
- No head-to-head ranking of modalities for masticatory muscle specifically.

## 7. Glossary

- **Spray-and-stretch (분사신장)**: vapocoolant applied over muscle/referral zone to reflexively lower tone, permitting passive stretch and trigger-point inactivation.
- **MTrP (Myofascial Trigger Point)**: hyperirritable spot in a taut band; active (jump sign, referred pain) vs latent.
- **RDC/TMD**: Research Diagnostic Criteria for Temporomandibular Disorders (Dworkin & LeResche 1992).
- **LLLT (Low-Level Laser Therapy)**: photobiomodulation analgesic modality.
