## One-line Summary

Narrative overview (HKU) of dentist–patient communication — covers why it matters (efficiency, less litigation, less anxiety, more adherence/satisfaction), barriers (time, rapport, oral-health illiteracy, language, dentist skill/perception), strategies (patient-centered approach, jargon-free verbal + non-verbal skills), and training (didactic + role-play + interviewing + assessment; MI, open-ended questions, affirmations, reflective listening, summaries).

## 2. Key Contributions
- Single-source synthesis of the four pillars: importance, challenges, strategies, training of dentist–patient communication.
- Operationalizes patient-centered care = understanding patient's illness + shared decision-making + pacing to the patient.
- Specifies training components for dental education, with MI named as a core skill.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative review structured around importance → challenges → strategies → training.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Benefits: accurate information transfer, dentist efficiency/confidence, lower occupational stress, fewer complaints/litigation, reduced dental anxiety, more trust, better adherence and satisfaction.
- Barriers: time constraints, rapport difficulty, patient oral-health illiteracy, dentist communication skill/perception, language.
- Strategies: patient-centered premise; simple jargon-free language; supportive body posture/gestures (non-verbal).
- Training: structured pedagogy — didactic instruction, role-play, patient interviewing, ongoing assessment; key skills = MI, open-ended questioning, affirmations, reflective listening, summaries.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative (non-systematic) — no quality appraisal or pooled data.
- Descriptive guidance rather than comparative evidence on which strategies/training work best.

## 7. Glossary
- **Patient-centered approach**: understanding the patient's illness, sharing decisions, and matching the patient's pace.
- **Reflective listening**: restating/clarifying the patient's meaning to confirm understanding.
- **Oral-health illiteracy**: limited capacity to obtain/understand basic oral-health information.
