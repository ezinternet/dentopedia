## Why Ingested

총의치 수명(총의치 장수, longevity)에 대한 근거 기반 데이터가 부족한 상황에서, 이 SR+MA는 42개 연구를 풀링하여 상악/하악별 가중평균 수명과 실패율 추이를 정량화한 유일한 메타분석이다. [[wiki/complete-denture/sanjeevan-2021-efficiency-simplified-versus-traditional-denture]]에서 총의치 제작 효율을 다루지만, 완성된 보철물이 얼마나 오래 기능하는지에 대한 근거는 없어 임상적 수명 상담(교체 시기 안내)에 직접 활용 가능한 수치를 보완한다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 42 studies found complete dentures have a weighted mean longevity of 10.1 ± 4.0 years overall, with maxillary dentures lasting longer (10.3 yr) than mandibular (8.6 yr), and failure proportions rising from 5% at ≤2 years to 41% at ≥10 years.

## 한줄요약

42개 연구의 SR+MA 결과, 총의치 가중평균 수명은 10.1 ± 4.0년이며, 상악의치(10.3년)가 하악의치(8.6년)보다 길고, 실패율은 2년 이하 5%에서 10년 이상 41%로 증가한다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: The Journal of Prosthetic Dentistry
- **Volume/Issue**: Volume 125, Issue 4, pp. 611–619
- **Published**: April 2021 (online: 2020)
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.prosdent.2020.02.023
- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (PRISMA-compliant)
- **Setting**: Multi-center (predominantly university dental clinics worldwide)

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First dedicated SR+MA** quantifying complete denture longevity — previous literature reported single-center series without pooled estimates.
2. **Arch-specific weighted means**: maxillary 10.3 ± 3.8 yr, mandibular 8.6 ± 2.6 yr, combined (both arches) 10.8 ± 4.7 yr, overall 10.1 ± 4.0 yr.
3. **Failure-proportion pooling by follow-up interval**: defines time-dependent failure rates using random-effects models, giving clinicians benchmarks for patient counseling on denture replacement timing.
4. **Risk of bias**: 24 studies low, 18 very low — overall high methodological quality of included evidence.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases**: MEDLINE, CINAHL (EBSCO), Dentistry and Oral Sciences Database, Cochrane Library
- **Initial yield**: 21,607 unique abstracts → 273 met inclusion criteria → 42 full-text studies included
- **Inclusion criteria**: Studies reporting longevity/survival of complete dentures in edentulous adults; sufficient quantitative data on denture in-service time
- **Quality assessment**: AXIS tool (cross-sectional study quality instrument)
- **Statistical approach**:
  - Weighted means and weighted standard deviations (Bland & Kerry method)
  - Pooled failure proportions via random-effects meta-analysis (DerSimonian & Laird)
  - Heterogeneity assessed with I² statistic
- **Longevity definition**: Duration from denture insertion to prosthesis failure (replacement, repair beyond function, or patient-reported non-use)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Denture type | Weighted mean longevity (yr) | SD (yr) |
|---|---|---|
| Maxillary only | 10.3 | 3.8 |
| Mandibular only | 8.6 | 2.6 |
| Both arches combined | 10.8 | 4.7 |
| Overall (all studies) | 10.1 | 4.0 |

**Pooled failure proportions by follow-up duration:**

| Follow-up period | Pooled failure proportion | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|
| ≤2 years | 0.05 | 0.00–0.10 |
| 5–6 years | 0.12 | 0.08–0.16 |
| ≥10 years | 0.41 | 0.28–0.53 |

- Failure rate increases progressively with denture age
- Maxillary dentures consistently outlast mandibular dentures (attributed to greater residual ridge resorption in mandible, reduced retention/stability)
- Studies predominantly from university/academic settings — may overestimate longevity vs. private practice fabrication

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

1. **University bias**: Nearly all included studies sourced from academic dental clinics; results may not generalize to community or private-practice dentures
2. **Heterogeneous "failure" definitions**: Studies used varied criteria (patient-reported, clinician-assessed, replacement events) — inconsistency limits precision
3. **Lack of patient-level variables**: Data on patient age, systemic conditions, parafunctional habits, and denture care were insufficient for subgroup analysis
4. **Historical data**: Studies span decades; fabrication techniques and materials have evolved, potentially inflating heterogeneity
5. **No digital/CAD-CAM data**: No milled or 3D-printed complete dentures included — longevity of digital dentures remains unaddressed
6. **Future directions**: Standardization of longevity outcome definitions; studies from community settings; inclusion of digital fabrication methods; patient-level predictor analysis

## 6. Related Work

- Carlsson & Omar (2010) — narrative review on future of complete dentures; cited as context for edentulism trends
- Sanjeevan et al. (2021) — SR+MA on efficiency of simplified vs. conventional fabrication technique (different outcome — process efficiency, not longevity)
- Bergman & Carlsson (1985) — landmark long-term clinical study of complete denture wearers (26-year single-center series); key primary study in this MA

## 7. Glossary

- **Longevity (총의치 수명)**: Duration a denture remains serviceable from insertion to failure/replacement
- **Weighted mean (가중평균)**: Mean calculated by weighting each study by sample size
- **DerSimonian and Laird method (DerSimonian-Laird 방법)**: Standard random-effects meta-analysis estimator for heterogeneous studies
- **AXIS tool**: Critical appraisal checklist for cross-sectional studies (Downes et al., 2016)
- **Failure proportion (실패율)**: Proportion of dentures failing within a specified follow-up period
- **Edentulism (무치악)**: Complete tooth loss requiring prosthetic rehabilitation
