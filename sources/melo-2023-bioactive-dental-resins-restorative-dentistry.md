## Why Ingested
Bioactive resins represent a frontier strategy against secondary caries — the leading cause of resin restoration failure — directly complementing caries-diet research covered in [[caries/moynihan-2016-sugars-dental-caries-threshold-intake]] and biofilm pathogenesis described in oral-microbiology cluster papers such as [[oral-microbiology/koo-2013-exopolysaccharides-matrix-biofilm-virulence]].

## One-line Summary
Narrative critical review synthesizing strategies for incorporating antimicrobial, remineralizing, and antidegradation bioactivity into resin composites and adhesives to prevent secondary caries, identifying key material vulnerabilities limiting clinical translation.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Defines "bioactivity" in restorative dentistry per 2022 FDI Policy Statement: anticaries approaches involving biofilm modulation, mineral-loss prevention, and polymeric resistance.
- Surveys three agent classes: (1) metallic/metal-oxide nanostructures (ZnO, TiO2, Ag, Au/Ag/Pd); (2) polymeric antimicrobials (QAMs, QPEI nanoparticles, MDPB — the only commercially used antimicrobial monomer); (3) mineralizing agents (bioactive glasses, calcium phosphate systems such as NACP).
- Presents clinical failure-rate data: annual failure rate of high-caries-risk patients ~4.6% vs. ~1.6% in low-risk patients over 10 years (Opdam et al. 2014 meta-analysis); 29-year practice study cumulative survival 91.7% at 6 y, 81.6% at 12 y, 71.4% at 19 y (annual failure rate 1.92%).
- Identifies quantum dots (1–10 nm; ZnO, TiO2, Ta2O5) functionalized with ionic liquids as a strategy to overcome nanoparticle agglomeration.
- Highlights DMAHDM and DMADDM as QAMs with robust antibiofilm activity against plaque-derived microcosm biofilms (P < 0.05 vs. controls, including reduction of Fusobacterium nucleatum, A. actinomycetemcomitans, P. gingivalis, Prevotella intermedia).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Design: narrative critical review (non-systematic); published as "Critical Reviews in Oral Biology & Medicine" section of J Dent Res.
- Population/scope: resin composites, adhesives, and luting agents used in direct restorative dentistry.
- Structure: (a) clinical failure context → (b) bioactive agent classes → (c) formulation challenges → (d) clinical translation prerequisites → (e) future directions.
- No systematic search protocol or PRISMA flow stated; authors are active bench researchers with primary data cited from their own group.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Secondary caries is the leading cause of resin restoration failure (majority of systematic survival studies).
- Annual failure rate high-caries-risk: 4.6% vs. low-risk: 1.6% over 10 y (Opdam et al. 2014).
- ZnO: most tested metal oxide; biocompatible and radiopaque; inhibits MMP catalytic activity; but requires high concentration for mature biofilm reduction, compromising flexural strength and degree of conversion.
- Ag nanoparticles: strong S. mutans activity; disadvantage — color change and impaired photoactivation.
- Au/Pt: chelate Zn2+ in MMP-8/-9 active sites (anti-MMP benefit).
- MDPB (12-methacryloyloxydodecyl pyridinium bromide): sole commercially available antimicrobial monomer.
- DMAHDM (5%) + NACP (20%) composite: statistically significant (P < 0.05) reduction of total CFU and key periopathogens vs. control in plaque-derived microcosm biofilm model.
- Main formulation vulnerabilities: particle agglomeration, color alteration, susceptibility to hydrolysis, loss of physicochemical core properties.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Author-stated: in vitro remineralization models poorly replicate the dynamic oral environment (saliva–bacteria interactions, pH cycling); long-term bond strength effects require further investigation.
- Additionally identified: narrative design introduces selection bias; most data from the authors' own lab; no head-to-head clinical RCTs comparing bioactive formulations; regulatory pathway for bioactive agents not discussed.
- Future: multifunctional designs combining antimicrobial + remineralizing + antidegradation (e.g., QAM + NACP + MMP inhibitor); smarter controlled-release nanocapsule/microsphere systems.

## 7. Glossary
- QAM: quaternary ammonium monomer — positively charged contact-kill antimicrobial incorporated into resin matrix.
- QPEI: quaternary ammonium polyethyleneimine — crosslinked nanoparticle with multiple quaternary amine groups.
- MDPB: 12-methacryloyloxydodecyl pyridinium bromide — only commercially used antimicrobial monomer (Clearfil Protect Bond).
- DMAHDM: dimethylaminohexadecyl methacrylate — QAM with C16 chain showing strong antibiofilm activity.
- DMADDM: dimethylaminododecyl methacrylate — QAM with C12 chain.
- NACP: nanosized amorphous calcium phosphate — remineralizing filler releasing Ca2+ and PO43−.
- MMP: matrix metalloproteinase — host enzyme that degrades collagen in the hybrid layer.
- Ta2O5: tantalum pentoxide — metal oxide used as quantum dot in dental adhesive research.
- SC: secondary caries — caries developing at the margins of existing restorations.
