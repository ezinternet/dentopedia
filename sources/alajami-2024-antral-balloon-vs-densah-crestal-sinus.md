## One-line Summary
RCT (crestal sinus lift + simultaneous implant, 12-month): both successful; Densah/OD gave higher implant primary stability (p=0.004) and bone density; antral balloon gave more immediate vertical bone gain but significant resorption by 6 months.

## 1. Document Information
- BMC Oral Health 2024;24:916. doi 10.1186/s12903-024-04609-8. Randomized clinical trial.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Head-to-head of antral membrane balloon vs Densah bur for crestal sinus lift with simultaneous placement, with 12-month clinical/radiographic outcomes.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- RCT, posterior maxilla, crestal (transcrestal) sinus lift + simultaneous implant. Groups: antral membrane balloon vs Densah (OD) bur. Outcomes: implant success, primary/secondary stability, vertical bone height, bone density over 12 months.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- All implants successful at 12 months.
- Primary stability: significantly favored Densah (p = 0.004); no difference at 6 months (p = 0.07).
- Vertical bone height: balloon higher immediately post-op (p < 0.0001) but significant reduction by 6 months (p < 0.0001).
- Bone density: Densah significantly higher (p ≤ 0.05).
- Conclusion: both viable; balloon = better immediate vertical gain (partly resorbing); Densah = higher primary stability and density.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Single-trial; n and graft details to verify in full text; 12-month follow-up (no long-term survival); balloon vertical gain partially transient.

## 7. Glossary
- OD: osseodensification (Densah burs). Antral membrane balloon: balloon-assisted transcrestal sinus floor elevation. RBH: residual bone height.
