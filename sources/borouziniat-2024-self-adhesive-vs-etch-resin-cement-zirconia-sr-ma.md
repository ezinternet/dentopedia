## Why Ingested

Cement-class selection for zirconia is a recurring clinical question, and the wiki already holds [[dental-materials/zirconia/kwon-2026-self-adhesive-resin-cement-zirconia]] (self-adhesive resin cement performance on zirconia) and [[resin/mahrous-2020-mdp-caf-self-adhesive-resin-cement-microshear]] (MDP/CAF priming with self-adhesive cement). This 2024 SR+MA (26 studies) directly compares self-adhesive vs self-etch vs total-etch resin cement classes against zirconia, supplying the head-to-head class ranking those single-system pages lack — reinforcing that 10-MDP-bearing self-adhesive/self-etch cements outperform total-etch and conventional cements.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review + meta-analysis (26 studies, 4 comparison groups) finding no significant difference in bond strength of self-adhesive vs self-etch resin cement to zirconia (immediate or delayed), while total-etch resin cement bonds significantly LOWER than self-adhesive (p=.00); both self-adhesive and self-etch are suitable for zirconia per manufacturer instructions.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰 + 메타분석(26편, 4개 비교군) — 지르코니아에 대한 자가접착(self-adhesive) vs 자가부식(self-etch) 레진시멘트의 결합강도는 즉시·지연 모두 유의차 없음. 전부식(total-etch)은 자가접착보다 유의하게 낮음(p=.00). 자가접착·자가부식 모두 제조사 지침대로 사용 시 지르코니아에 적합.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Direct head-to-head meta-analytic comparison of three resin cement classes — self-adhesive, self-etch, total-etch — bonded to yttria-stabilized tetragonal zirconia polycrystal (Y-TZP), a comparison previously fragmented across single-system studies.
- Establishes that self-adhesive and self-etch resin cements are statistically equivalent in bond strength to zirconia, both immediately and after aging.
- Demonstrates total-etch resin cement is significantly inferior, attributing this to the absence of phosphate (10-MDP) functional monomer that chemically bonds to zirconia oxides.
- Confirms (descriptive analysis) that self-adhesive resin cement bonds significantly stronger than resin-modified glass ionomer (RMGI), glass ionomer (GI), and zinc phosphate cements (p<0.001).
- Clinical bottom line: a 10-MDP-containing self-adhesive or self-etch resin cement, applied per manufacturer instructions (commonly with airborne-particle/sandblast surface treatment), is the recommended class for zirconia luting.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Guidelines**: PRISMA; PICOS-structured search.
- **Databases**: PubMed, Scopus, Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews, ISI/Web of Science core collection (plus Biosis, Current Contents, Medline, SciELO, etc.), searched up to November 1, 2021, no language/time restriction.
- **Inclusion**: in-vitro studies comparing bond strength of self-adhesive vs self-etch or total-etch resin cement to zirconia; shear or tensile test; appropriate statistics (sample size, mean, SD reported); cement applied per manufacturer instructions. No relevant clinical studies were found.
- **Risk of bias**: 6 methodological criteria (teeth randomization, caries/restoration-free teeth, materials per specification, same operator for adhesive procedures, sample-size calculation, blinded testing-machine operator); 1–2 items = high, 3–4 = medium, 5–6 = low.
- **Grouping**: four groups by cement type × storage time — (1) immediate self-etch vs self-adhesive, (2) delayed self-etch vs self-adhesive (>48 h storage), (3) immediate total-etch vs self-adhesive, (4) delayed total-etch vs self-adhesive (>48 h storage).
- **Statistics**: random-effects model (high heterogeneity, I² >90% in self-etch comparisons); Cochran Q for heterogeneity; Comprehensive Meta-Analysis software v2. Conventional cements (GI, RMGI, zinc phosphate) analyzed descriptively.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Search yield**: 858 records (422 ISI, 145 PubMed, 164 Scopus, 114 Embase, 13 Cochrane) → 376 after de-duplication → 109 after title/abstract → 43 full-text → **26 studies selected** (17 excluded for not following manufacturer instructions or incomplete statistics; all in English).
- **Risk of bias** (of 33 articles assessed): 1 low, 16 medium, 16 high.
- **Group 1 — immediate, self-etch vs self-adhesive**: 15 articles / 32 categories; I²=93.50; **no significant difference (p=0.055)**.
- **Group 2 — delayed, self-etch vs self-adhesive**: 14 articles / 29 categories; I²=92.68; **no significant difference (p=0.143)**.
- **Group 3 — immediate, total-etch vs self-adhesive**: 9 articles / 19 categories; total-etch **significantly LOWER (p=0.000)**.
- **Group 4 — delayed, total-etch vs self-adhesive**: 9 articles / 16 categories; total-etch **significantly LOWER (p=0.000)**.
- **Conventional cements (descriptive)**: self-adhesive resin cement bond strength significantly higher than RMGI, GI, and zinc phosphate (p<0.001).
- **Mechanistic note**: most studies used RelyX Unicem (self-adhesive, contains 10-MDP); 10-MDP chemically bonds zirconia oxides, forming nanolayers resistant to thermocycling/hydrolytic degradation; total-etch cements lack phosphate monomer. Da Silva reported self-adhesive bond 2–3× higher than total-etch RelyX ARC at 24 h, with self-adhesive retaining ~2× advantage at 6 months despite overall decline.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- All included studies are in vitro; no clinical trials met inclusion criteria — extrapolation to clinical retention/survival is indirect.
- Very high statistical heterogeneity (I² >90% in self-etch comparisons) limits the precision of pooled estimates; the self-etch vs self-adhesive equivalence rests on non-significant results in highly heterogeneous data.
- Predominance of medium/high risk-of-bias studies (only 1 of 33 low risk); sample-size calculation and operator blinding rarely reported.
- Three bonding interfaces (cement–zirconia, dentin–cement–zirconia, composite–cement–zirconia) pooled together; the weaker cement–dentin interface can confound assessment of true cement–zirconia bond.
- Heterogeneous surface treatments, cement brands, aging protocols, and test methods (SBS/MSBS/MTBS/push-out) across studies.

## 7. Glossary

- **Y-TZP**: Yttria-stabilized tetragonal zirconia polycrystal — the common non-etchable, chemically inert dental ceramic.
- **10-MDP**: 10-methacryloyloxy-decyl-dihydrogen-phosphate — phosphate ester functional monomer that chemically bonds to zirconia/metal oxides.
- **Self-adhesive resin cement**: dual-cure resin cement requiring no separate etch/primer; bonds chemically (often via 10-MDP) (e.g., RelyX Unicem, G-Cem, Maxcem).
- **Self-etch resin cement**: resin cement used with a self-etching primer; combines micromechanical + chemical bonding (e.g., Multilink, Panavia F2).
- **Total-etch resin cement**: resin cement requiring separate phosphoric-acid etch step; typically lacks phosphate monomer for zirconia (e.g., RelyX ARC, Variolink II).
- **SBS / MSBS / MTBS**: shear / microshear / microtensile bond strength tests.
- **TSC**: tribochemical silica coating — surface treatment depositing silica to improve bonding when paired with 10-MDP primer.
- **RMGI / GI**: resin-modified glass ionomer / glass ionomer cement.
