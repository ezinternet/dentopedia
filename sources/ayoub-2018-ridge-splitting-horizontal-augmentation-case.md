## 2. Key Contributions
- Modified ridge splitting with piezosurgery: precise, controlled cortical osteotomy
- Sticky bone (albumin allograft + PRF): combined biologic graft technique
- Simultaneous implant placement at time of ridge expansion
- Split-mouth design: contralateral control comparison
- Avoids donor-site morbidity of extraoral/intraoral onlay grafts

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Split-mouth case report. Atrophic maxillary ridge with insufficient horizontal width. Modified technique: piezosurgery for cortical splitting, osteotome expansion, sticky bone (albumin-coated allograft + PRF) graft, simultaneous implant placement. Clinical and radiographic assessment at follow-up.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Successful horizontal ridge augmentation achieved
- Simultaneous implant placement without complications
- Piezosurgery: controlled and predictable bone cuts
- Sticky bone maintained volume and promoted healing
- No donor-site morbidity; reduced overall surgical burden

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
Case-level evidence; limited generalizability. No control group. Single operator/center.

## 7. Glossary
- **One-piece implant**: Implant with integrated abutment (no separate abutment screw)
- **Sticky bone**: Autologous fibrin + allograft mixture with PRF
- **PRF**: Platelet-rich fibrin — autologous growth factor membrane
- **Piezosurgery**: Ultrasonic bone cutting with selective hard-tissue cutting
