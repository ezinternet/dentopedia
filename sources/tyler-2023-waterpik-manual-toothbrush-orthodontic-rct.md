## Why Ingested

A brand-named device (Waterpik) RCT added to manual toothbrushing in fixed-appliance orthodontic patients, with a 56-week follow-up that found **no benefit** for any oral-hygiene index — a long-follow-up counterweight to the short-term marketing claim that a Waterpik with an orthodontic tip improves plaque control. It pairs directly with [[wiki/interdental-cleaning/yiamwattana-2025-oral-irrigator-vs-floss-orthodontic-sr-ma]] (oral irrigator NOT superior to floss in fixed-appliance orthodontics): both point to no demonstrated superiority for water-flossing in this population, and Tyler is one of the few trials testing the irrigator as an *adjunct to* (not a replacement for) a manual toothbrush over a long course of treatment.

## One-line Summary

Single-centre two-arm parallel single-blind RCT (n=40, ages 10-20, upper+lower fixed appliances, 56-week follow-up): adding a Waterpik to a manual toothbrush produced NO statistically significant difference in plaque, gingival, or interdental-bleeding indices vs manual toothbrush alone — trial stopped at interim analysis, no evidence of benefit.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First RCT to test a Waterpik (with orthodontic tip) **as an adjunct to a manual toothbrush** over a clinically realistic follow-up (56 weeks) in fixed-appliance orthodontic patients — most prior evidence was 4-week.
2. Found **no benefit** for plaque (OMPI), gingival (GI), or interdental-bleeding (IBI) indices, directly challenging the single prior short-term positive trial (Sharma, 28 days).
3. Identified why earlier positive results may not generalise: the prior trial enrolled only patients with poor baseline hygiene (Quigley-Hein >=3.0), a short follow-up captured a likely **novelty effect**, and recruited patients already in braces.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: single-centre, single-operator, two-arm, parallel-group, stratified, single-blind RCT, 1:1 allocation. Orthodontic dept, York Hospital, UK (NHS, non-fee-paying).
- **Participants**: 40 fit/well patients aged 10-20, full upper+lower arch fixed appliances (pre-adjusted edgewise MBT brackets, Transbond XT bonding), normal periodontal health, brushing >=2x/day, not already using a Waterpik.
- **Arms**: Control = manual toothbrush (MTB) + standardised OHE + provided toothbrush/toothpaste(1450 ppm F)/mouthwash. Intervention = same + Waterpik Water Flosser WP-560 with orthodontic tip, once nightly ~1 min.
- **Outcomes**: plaque (Orthodontic Modification of Plaque Index, OMPI; Plaque Index at baseline), gingival (Gingival Index, GI), interdental bleeding (Interdental Bleeding Index, IBI), trauma, adherence (diaries), satisfaction (questionnaire). Recorded at baseline (T0), 8 (T1), 32 (T2), 56 (T3) weeks.
- **Randomisation**: stratified block (gender, age <15 vs >=15, baseline IBI <=20% vs >20%), 8 blocks, sealed opaque envelopes; switched to coin toss after participant 33 (COVID-19). Operator (DT) blinded to allocation.
- **Analysis**: intention-to-treat; generalised linear mixed model (handles non-normal + missing data); ICC for OMPI repeatability.
- **Sample size**: powered on OMPI (alpha 0.05, effect size 0.5, SD 0.3, power 0.9) → min 7/group; recruited 20/group (40 total).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Interim analysis at trial stop**: 40 recruited, 85% data collected; n at 8/32/56 wk = 40/32/19. Trial stopped because sample size reached, dropouts fewer than expected, and continuing during COVID-19 was deemed unethical.
- **Baseline (Intervention vs Control)**: age 14.9 vs 14.8 yr; %female 50 vs 65; PI 0.95 vs 0.94; GI 0.77 vs 0.74; IBI 27.1% vs 34.7%.
- **Mixed-model differences (treatment − control)**:
  - **OMPI (plaque)**: 0.199 (p=0.88, 95% CI −0.24 to 0.27) — NS
  - **GI (gingival)**: −0.008 (p=0.94, 95% CI −0.22 to 0.20) — NS
  - **IBI (interdental bleeding)**: 5.6% (p=0.563, 95% CI −13.22 to 24.42) — NS
- **No statistically significant difference for any variable.**
- ICC for OMPI = 0.911 (95% CI 0.700-0.977), good repeatability. No trauma in either group at any visit; no harms.
- **Conclusion**: no evidence to support adding a Waterpik to a manual toothbrush for oral hygiene in fixed-appliance orthodontic patients.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Selection bias toward good baseline hygiene**: patients with poor hygiene are typically excluded from orthodontic treatment, so a floor effect may obscure any benefit (mean baseline plaque 0.92/3, vs Sharma's 3.73/5 in the positive trial).
- **Imbalanced gender** (control 65% female vs 50% intervention); females have lower plaque/more oral-health knowledge → could mask a Waterpik effect (authors judge differences small).
- **Early stop / reduced power** at later timepoints (n=19 at 56 wk); COVID-19 disrupted recruitment and randomisation method.
- **OMPI subjectivity** and Hawthorne effect (appointment-day cleaning) apply to both arms.
- **Adherence uninterpretable** (<25% diaries returned); satisfaction questionnaire unvalidated.
- **Generalisability limited** to NHS secondary-care, non-fee-paying patients (lower compliance, more complex malocclusions) — caution extending to primary-care or self-paying patients.

## 6. Related Work

- Sharma et al. — only prior RCT with Waterpik orthodontic tip; positive plaque result over 28 days but enrolled poor-hygiene patients and used a different index (Turesky-modified Quigley-Hein). Short follow-up captures novelty effect.
- [[wiki/interdental-cleaning/yiamwattana-2025-oral-irrigator-vs-floss-orthodontic-sr-ma]] — SR+MA: oral irrigator not superior to dental floss in fixed-appliance orthodontics (both NS, extreme heterogeneity).
- [[wiki/interdental-cleaning/thomassen-2025-airfloss-essential-oils-vs-floss-rct]] — air-floss vs floss RCT, both equivalent → device choice driven by adherence, not efficacy.

## 7. Glossary

- **OMPI** — Orthodontic Modification of Plaque Index (0-3); adapts the Plaque Index for teeth with brackets in situ.
- **PI** — Plaque Index (Silness-Loe), 0-3; used at baseline before appliance placement.
- **GI** — Gingival Index (Loe-Silness), 0-3.
- **IBI** — Interdental Bleeding Index, 0-100%; bleeding after depressing the interdental papilla with a wooden stick.
- **MTB** — manual toothbrush.
- **GLMM** — generalised linear mixed model; handles non-normal, repeated-measures, and missing data.
- **Hawthorne effect** — behaviour change (better cleaning) caused by awareness of being observed / having an appointment.
- **Novelty effect** — transient improvement from the newness of a device that fades with continued use.
