## One-line Summary
Scoping review (PRISMA-ScR, 37 articles from 633 records) of 25-year transcrestal sinus floor elevation (TSFE) literature. Identifies 4 treatment modalities (osteotome + 3 alternatives). Reports radiographic bone levels, implant failure >5 yr, ISQ at placement, complications across techniques.

## 1. Document Information
University of Michigan group (Decker, Stuhr, Testori, Wang). Decker and Stuhr share first authorship. Published in CID, open-access.

## 2. Key Contributions
- 25-year systematic landscape of TSFE evolution
- Four treatment modalities identified — osteotome as control, plus 3 alternatives
- Primary outcomes: radiographic bone levels + implant failure >5 years
- Secondary outcomes: ISQ at placement + complications
- Minimum 12-month healing in included studies

## 3. Methodology
- PRISMA-ScR scoping review
- 633 records → 574 screened → 37 included
- Included designs: RCT, non-RCT controlled, prospective cohort, case series
- Comparison: 4 modalities vs control (traditional osteotome technique)

- 4 modalities identified for TSFE

## 5. Limitations
- Scoping review (not full SR+MA with statistical pooling)
- Heterogeneous designs included
- Outcome reporting inconsistency across primary studies
