## Why Ingested

기존 [[drug/analgesics/smith-2017-nsaids-postoperative-endodontic-pain]]와 [[drug/analgesics/samani-2025-nsaids-endodontic-postoperative-network]]는 NSAID 전반의 근관치료 후 통증 효과를 다루지만, 개별 약제 케토롤락 (Ketorolac) 단독의 비교효과 SR/MA는 부재했다. 본 SR+MA (Ping 2026, 7편 RCT)는 케토롤락 vs 다른 진통제의 시간대별 (6/12/24 h) 우열을 정량화해 endodontic 통증 약제 선택 근거를 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 7 RCTs (5 meta-analyzed): ketorolac significantly outperforms comparator analgesics (dexamethasone, ibuprofen, tapentadol, etodolac, prednisolone) for post-endodontic pain at 6 h (SMD -0.82) and reduces rescue-medication need (RR 0.50), but the advantage disappears by 12 and 24 h.

## 한줄요약

7편 RCT (5편 메타분석) SR+MA: 케토롤락 (Ketorolac)은 근관치료 후 통증에서 6시간 시점 다른 진통제보다 유의하게 우수하고 (SMD -0.82) 추가 진통제 필요를 줄이지만 (RR 0.50), 12·24시간에는 차이가 사라진다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA isolating **ketorolac specifically** (rather than NSAIDs as a class) for post-endodontic pain, across all routes (oral, local injection, intranasal).
- Quantifies a **time-resolved efficacy window**: ketorolac is superior at 6 h but converges with comparators by 12–24 h, mechanistically tied to its ~6 h plasma half-life.
- Confirms a **rescue-medication benefit** (RR 0.50): fewer patients need supplementary analgesia after ketorolac.
- Documents intranasal ketorolac (Sprix) as the only nasally-delivered NSAID and a noninvasive option for endodontic pain.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases**: PubMed, Web of Science, ScienceDirect, Cochrane Library, Embase, Scopus, SinoMed, CNKI, Webvpn, Wanfang Data. Search deadline ~August 2025.
- **Framework**: PICOS inclusion/exclusion. RCTs only.
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane Handbook 5.1.0 (7 domains), two independent reviewers + third for consensus.
- **Statistics** (RevMan 5.3): continuous data via inverse-variance random-effects model, **SMD** as effect measure; dichotomous data via **RR**, fixed-effect, Mantel-Haenszel. Heterogeneity by I² and Cochran Q.
- **Yield**: 386 records → 290 after dedup → 13 after screen → **7 studies qualitative**, **5 quantitative** (2 lacked extractable data).
- Included studies published 1994–2024; heterogeneous in pain scales (0-10 / 0-100 / 0-170 VAS, Heft-Parker, VRS) and administration (5 pre-op, 1 intracanal, 1 post-op; 3 oral, 3 local injection, 1 intranasal).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Studies | Effect | 95% CI | p | I² |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pain at **6 h** | 3 (Akhlaghi, Praveen, Sethi) | SMD = **-0.82** | -1.18 to -0.46 | <0.00001 | 47% |
| Pain at 12 h | 2 (Praveen, Sethi) | SMD = -0.29 (NS) | -1.36 to 0.78 | 0.59 | 92% |
| Pain at 24 h | 3 (Akhlaghi, Praveen, Sethi) | SMD = -0.25 (NS) | -1.07 to 0.56 | 0.54 | 90% |
| **Additional medication** needed | 3 (Akhlaghi, Penniston & Hargreaves, Rogers) | RR = **0.50** | 0.35–0.73 | 0.0002 | 0% |

- **Qualitative**: 5/7 studies favored ketorolac; 1 (Rogers) found no difference vs ibuprofen/dexamethasone; 1 (Praveen) found prednisolone more sustained.
- **Comparators beaten** at 6 h: dexamethasone, ibuprofen, tapentadol, etodolac, prednisolone.
- **Pharmacokinetic rationale**: ketorolac IM/IV onset ~10 min, peak 75–150 min; intranasal peak ~45 min; oral onset 30–60 min, peak 1.5–4 h — faster than comparators (dexamethasone ~60 min, etodolac ~80 min, ibuprofen 1.5–3 h, prednisolone ~2 h, tapentadol delayed). ~6 h half-life explains loss of advantage by 12 h.
- **Risk of bias**: 3 low risk (Akhlaghi, Praveen, Sekhar); allocation concealment unclear in 4; 1 high risk for blinding (Rogers, different administration methods).
- **Side effects** (only Sethi reported): mild headache, dizziness, sweating, injection-site pain.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Few RCTs included; varied comparison groups; some studies could not provide raw data.
- **High heterogeneity** at 12 h (I²=92%) and 24 h (I²=90%) — pooled NS estimates are unstable.
- Small sample sizes precluded subgroup analysis by dosing frequency or route.
- Authors call for large-sample, multicenter RCTs with standardized pain assessment.

## 7. Glossary

- **SMD** (Standardized Mean Difference): effect size pooling outcomes measured on different scales.
- **RR** (Risk Ratio): ratio of event probability (here, needing rescue analgesia) between groups.
- **Heft-Parker VAS**: a 0–170 mm visual analog scale anchored with verbal descriptors, common in endodontic pain trials.
- **Symptomatic irreversible pulpitis**: pulpal inflammation with lingering/spontaneous pain requiring pulp removal; the dominant indication in these RCTs.
- **Intranasal ketorolac (Sprix)**: only NSAID approved for nasal delivery; noninvasive route with ~45 min time-to-peak.
