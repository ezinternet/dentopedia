## One-line Summary
Systematic review of 38 clinical trials comparing MBL around bone-level (BL) and tissue-level (TL) implants, finding high heterogeneity across studies and no definitive winner — both designs show acceptable MBL under appropriate conditions.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: J Korean Assoc Oral Maxillofac Surg 2021;47:153-174
- **Type**: Systematic review (PRISMA)
- **Search**: PubMed, Google Scholar, Scopus, Web of Science; English; up to September 2020
- **Studies included**: 38 clinical trials
  - Table 1: 18 studies — BL implants only
  - Table 2: 10 studies — TL implants only
  - Table 3: 10 studies — direct BL vs TL comparison

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive comparison of BL vs TL designs regarding MBL and bone-related outcomes
- Highlights role of implant–abutment connection (AFC) design on bacterial microleakage and MBL
- Platform-switching: documented reduction in MBL vs conventional connection
- SLActive surface: accelerates osseointegration, bone apposition
- Morse taper connection: minimal bacterial leakage among AFC types
- Conclusion: vast methodological heterogeneity prevents definitive BL vs TL comparison

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Inclusion**: Clinical trials reporting BL change (MBL, BIC, bone fill) after BL or TL implant placement
- **Exclusion**: In vivo animal studies, soft tissue/esthetic focus only, biomechanical/stability focus, no bone-specific data
- **Outcome measures**: MBL (mm), BIC (mm), bone fill (mm²), new bone height (mm)
- **Measurement methods**: periapical radiograph (majority), CBCT, panoramic, CT
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane criteria; largely unclear risk in most studies

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Bone-Level (BL) Implants — Selected Studies
- **Filippi 2013** (908 SLActive implants, 1–3 yr): BL change >1 mm in <5% of implants; SVR 98.5%
- **Wu 2018** (114 implants, 5 yr): MBL 1st year ~1.2 mm (majority of total loss); MBL yr 1–5: ~0.78 mm additional; no difference submerged vs non-submerged
- **Ghazal 2019** (47 TiZr SLActive): BL change at 12 mo: narrow 3.3mm = −0.27 mm vs standard 4.1mm = −0.48 mm
- **Buser 2009** (20 GBR + SLActive, maxilla ant): 12 mo mean bone loss 0.18 mm; 15/20 implants <0.25 mm loss
- **Vanlıoğlu 2014** (55 platform-switched, 4 yr): MBL 0.12 mm final; 7 implants showed bone gain
- **Lago 2017** (67 platform-switched): Baseline→1 yr: 0.06 mm; 1→5 yr: 0.23 mm; total 5 yr: 0.28 mm

### Platform-Switching Effect
- Conventional design: 0.7–2.5 mm bone loss at 6 months loading
- Platform-switched: 0.12–0.29 mm bone loss at 1 year
- One study: no bone loss at 2 years with platform switching

### Abutment-Fixture Connection (AFC) Types and MBL
| Connection | Bacterial Leakage | MBL Risk |
|---|---|---|
| External hexagonal | High | Higher |
| Internal hexagonal | Moderate | Moderate |
| Conical | Low | Lower |
| Morse taper | Minimal | Lowest |
| Platform-switched | N/A (design modifier) | Significantly reduced |

### SLActive Surface
- Accelerates osseointegration via blood clot stabilization and hydrophilic surface
- Used in 7 of 38 studies; consistently associated with low MBL
- Bone apposition documented in BL SLActive implants

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Vast heterogeneity in study design, follow-up, implant systems, measurement methods
- Risk of bias largely "unclear" — randomization/allocation poorly reported
- Direct BL vs TL comparison limited to 10 studies
- No meta-analysis performed due to heterogeneity
- Need for standardized long-term RCTs comparing BL vs TL head-to-head

## 7. Glossary
- **BL (Bone-Level) implant**: Implant platform placed at crestal bone level; more flexible abutment options; preferred in esthetic zones
- **TL (Tissue-Level) implant**: Implant platform positioned at soft tissue level; eliminates crestal microgap; may show metallic shadow through thin tissue
- **MBL (Marginal Bone Loss)**: Radiographic peri-implant bone loss — key success indicator
- **AFC (Abutment-Fixture Connection)**: Junction between implant body and abutment — source of microgap and bacterial leakage
- **Platform-switching**: Using a smaller-diameter abutment than the implant platform; shifts stress inward; reduces crestal bone loss
- **Morse taper**: Conical friction-fit connection; superior seal against bacterial leakage
- **SLActive**: Sandblasted large-grit acid-etched hydrophilic surface (Straumann); enhances early osseointegration
- **BIC (Bone-Implant Contact)**: Histological measure of direct bone-to-implant interface percentage
