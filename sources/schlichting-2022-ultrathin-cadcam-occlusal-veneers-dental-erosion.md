## Why Ingested

치아 침식증(dental erosion)에 의한 후방 교합면 마모의 보존적 수복을 위한 CAD-CAM 교합면 비니어(occlusal veneer)의 중기(최대 3년) RCT 임상 성적 데이터가 위키에 부족하였음. 이 논문은 세라믹(e.max CAD) vs 복합레진(Lava Ultimate) 초박형 교합면 비니어를 무작위 비교하여 생존율·합병증을 보고한 핵심 전향적 근거임. [[wiki/veneers/klein-2025-ceramic-laminate-veneer-survival-complications-sr-ma]]에서 다루지 않은 교합면(occlusal) 비니어와 복합레진 CAD-CAM 계열을 보완한다.

## One-line Summary

Prospective RCT (n=11 participants, 60 restorations) comparing CAD-CAM ceramic (e.max CAD) vs composite resin (Lava Ultimate) ultrathin occlusal veneers for severe dental erosion over up to 3 years: 100% vs 84.7% Kaplan-Meier survival with no statistically significant difference (P=.124), but significantly greater surface degradation in the composite resin group (P=.003).

## 한줄요약

전향적 무작위대조시험(Randomized Controlled Trial, RCT) (n=11명, 60개 수복물): 중증 치아 침식증(dental erosion)에 CAD-CAM 세라믹 교합면 비니어(occlusal veneer, e.max CAD) vs 복합레진(composite resin, Lava Ultimate)을 최대 3년 추적 — 카플란-마이어(Kaplan-Meier) 생존율 100% vs 84.7% (P=.124, 유의하지 않음), 레진군에서 표면 조도(surface roughness) 유의하게 높음 (P=.003).

- **Setting**: University clinic setting; 11 participants (mean age 30.4 years)

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First medium-term RCT** directly comparing CAD-CAM ceramic (lithium disilicate, e.max CAD) vs CAD-CAM composite resin (Lava Ultimate) ultrathin occlusal veneers in patients with severe dental erosion.
2. **No complete failures** in either group over up to 3 years; all failures were partial (chipping, restorable) in the composite resin group only.
3. **Survival rates**: 100% ceramic vs 84.7% composite resin — clinically relevant gap that was not statistically significant at this sample size.
4. **Surface degradation**: Composite resin veneers showed significantly greater surface roughness increase compared to ceramic, indicating clinical aging concern.
5. Validates the ultrathin CAD-CAM occlusal veneer approach (0.5–1 mm) as a clinically acceptable conservative treatment for severely eroded posterior teeth.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

### Participants and Restorations
- 11 participants with severe dental erosion, mean age 30.4 years
- 60 total restorations: 24 ceramic (e.max CAD) + 36 composite resin (Lava Ultimate)
- Material assignment was randomized per tooth

### Preparation and Bonding Protocol
- **Preparation**: Trial-restoration driven; minimal to no preparation, occlusal surface only
- **Immediate dentin sealing (IDS)**: OptiBond FL applied at preparation; considered a fundamental step (Magne 2005)
- **Ceramic surface treatment**: Hydrofluoric acid etching + silane coupling agent
- **Composite resin surface treatment**: Airborne-particle abrasion + silane coupling agent
- **Cementation**: Preheated composite resin (Filtek Z100) adhesive luting for all restorations
- **Tooth preparation surface**: Airborne-particle abrasion + phosphoric acid etching

### Outcome Assessment
- Modified United States Public Health Service (USPHS) criteria
- Evaluations: baseline + annually for up to 3 years
- **Primary outcome**: Time to failure (scores 4 or 5 = partial or complete failure)
- **Survival analysis**: Kaplan-Meier method

### Statistical Analysis
- Kaplan-Meier survival curves with log-rank test for survival differences
- Wilcoxon/Mann-Whitney for secondary outcomes (surface roughness scores)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | e.max CAD (ceramic) | Lava Ultimate (composite resin) | P-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Total restorations | 24 | 36 | — |
| Failures (complete) | 0 | 0 | — |
| Partial failures (chipping) | 0 | 5 (scored 4) | — |
| Kaplan-Meier survival (3y) | 100% | 84.7% (SE 0.065%) | .124 (NS) |
| Surface roughness degradation | Minimal | Significant | .003 |

- **No complete losses** in either group
- All 5 failures in composite resin group were chipping events scored 4 (partial failure, restorable)
- Surface roughness was significantly worse for composite resin — indicative of material surface aging in oral environment

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

1. **Small sample size** (n=11, 60 restorations): underpowered to detect the observed ~15% survival difference between groups (P=.124 not significant)
2. **Short-to-medium follow-up** (up to 3 years): longer-term data needed to determine if the survival gap widens
3. **Young participant cohort** (mean 30.4 years): severe erosion at young age may not represent typical clinical population
4. **Single-operator, single-center**: limits generalizability
5. **Composite resin chipping mechanism** not fully characterized — future work should investigate fatigue loading patterns specific to composite resin occlusal veneers
6. Longer follow-up RCTs with larger samples are needed to establish definitive superiority of ceramic

## 7. Glossary

- **Ultrathin occlusal veneer**: Adhesively bonded indirect restoration covering only the occlusal surface, ≤1 mm thickness (minimal or no preparation)
- **e.max CAD**: IPS e.max CAD (Ivoclar Vivadent) — lithium disilicate glass-ceramic block for CAD-CAM milling
- **Lava Ultimate**: 3M ESPE — resin nanoceramic (RNC) CAD-CAM block; composite resin-based
- **CAD-CAM**: Computer-Aided Design / Computer-Aided Manufacturing
- **IDS (Immediate Dentin Sealing)**: Application of adhesive to freshly cut dentin immediately after preparation (before temporization), before definitive restoration insertion; improves bond strength
- **USPHS criteria**: United States Public Health Service clinical evaluation criteria; scores 1–5, with 4 = partial failure (restorable), 5 = complete failure (replacement needed)
- **Kaplan-Meier**: Statistical method for estimating survival (time-to-event) from censored data
- **Biocorrosion / dental erosion**: Chemical dissolution of tooth structure by non-bacterial acids (dietary or gastric); also called erosive tooth wear (ETW)
- **OptiBond FL**: Kerr — total-etch, three-step adhesive system used for IDS in this study
- **Filtek Z100**: 3M ESPE — preheated composite resin used as luting agent
