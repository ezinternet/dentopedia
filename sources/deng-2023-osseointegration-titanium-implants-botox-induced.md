## One-line Summary
Rat femoral implant study (Virginia Commonwealth U.) — botox-induced muscle paralysis compromises trabecular and cortical bone (BV/TV ↓, porosity ↑) and reduces BIC for smooth (PT) titanium implants; biomimetic SLAnano surfaces + local semaphorin 3A delivery via Cu-free click hydrogel rescue osseointegration.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Biomimetics 2023;8(1):93
- **DOI**: 10.3390/biomimetics8010093
- **Published**: 25 February 2023
- **Lead institution**: VCU Department of Biomedical Engineering (Boyan/Schwartz lab)
- **Design**: In vivo controlled animal experimental study; male 12-wk-old Sprague-Dawley rats
- **Model**: Botox injection into rat hindlimb to mimic mechanically unloaded bone (analogue of disuse/neuromuscular pathology)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Establishes that botulinum toxin A (botox)-induced muscle paralysis compromises both trabecular and cortical bone phenotype at the distal metaphysis, mid-shaft, and proximal femur.
- Demonstrates that smooth (PT) Ti implants have markedly impaired osseointegration in botox-treated rats, whereas multiscale biomimetic SLAnano (grit-blasted, acid-etched, aged) surfaces retain superior BIC under muscle paralysis.
- Shows that local semaphorin 3A (sema3A) delivered in a Cu-free click hydrogel rescues bone formation specifically at sites with biomimetic-surface implants, while sema3A injection alone or applied to smooth implants is ineffective.
- Provides mechanical evidence: removal torque max load / torsional stiffness / yield point reduced by botox for rough Ti implants regardless of sema3A; smooth Ti unaffected because already poor.
- Translational relevance: muscle paralysis (clinical analogue: long-term bed rest, spinal cord injury, neuromuscular pathology — and by extension, repeated masticatory-muscle BTX injections) compromises osseointegration of smooth-surface implants.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Custom titanium implants from grade-4 Ti rods, 2.5 mm diameter × 3.5 mm length × 0.8 mm pitch (Straumann custom).
- Two implant surfaces studied (both hydrophobic):
  - PT: machined pre-treatment, smooth.
  - SLAnano: SLA microroughness + aged nanoscale features (modSLA aged ≥1 month → SLAnano).
- Botox model: intramuscular injection 3 weeks prior to implant insertion → mechanically unloaded femur.
- Implants inserted into distal end of right femurs; harvest 28 days post-insertion.
- Six groups: control+PT, control+SLAnano, BTX+PT, BTX+PT+Sema3A, BTX+SLAnano, BTX+SLAnano+Sema3A.
- Sema3A delivery: copper-free click hydrogel (PEG-dithiol + DBCO-maleimide / PEG-N3, RAFT polymerization), in situ crosslinking, ~14-day degradation; delivered into cortical defect before insertion and above implant after insertion.
- Outcomes:
  - MicroCT: trabecular BV/TV, total porosity, trabecular thickness/number; cortical BV/TV, porosity, thickness; BIC (total, bone marrow space, cortical).
  - Mechanical: 3-point bending (max load, stiffness, toughness) and removal torque (max load, torsional stiffness, yield point).
- Statistics: One-way ANOVA + Tukey post-hoc among groups; two-way ANOVA for surface × botox interactions; Wilcoxon paired between contralateral legs.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Whole-bone botox effect (distal metaphysis):**
  - Botox reduced trabecular BV/TV, increased total porosity, reduced trabecular thickness and number.
  - Botox reduced cortical BV/TV, increased cortical porosity, reduced cortical thickness.
  - Sema3A injection alone did NOT rescue trabecular phenotype in healthy or botox rats; cortical phenotype likewise unaffected at distal metaphysis.
- **Local sema3A burst release effect at trochanter/mid-diaphysis:**
  - Sema3A restored total porosity to normal levels in botox-treated rats at trochanter site.
  - No effect of sema3A on cortical thickness at trochanter or mid-diaphysis.
- **Osseointegration (microCT-quantified BIC at 28 days):**
  - SLAnano implants in botox-compromised rats: improved BIC over BTX+PT.
  - Sema3A delivered via hydrogel: enhanced BIC further in BTX+SLAnano+Sema3A group.
  - Sema3A had no rescuing effect on BTX+PT implants.
- **Mechanical (removal torque):**
  - Botox reduced max load / torsional stiffness / yield point by 56% for rough (SLAnano) titanium implants regardless of sema3A.
  - PT (smooth) implants: no botox vs. healthy difference (already poor baseline).
  - Sema3A did not affect mechanical properties despite increasing BIC.
- 3-point bending of whole femurs showed analogous botox-driven decreases.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Rat model; translation to human bone (especially mandible/maxilla) requires caution.
- Bone site studied was femur, not jaw — direct extrapolation to dental implants in BTX-injected masseter/temporalis areas requires further work.
- Sema3A delivery formulation novel; clinical hydrogel/sema3A combos not yet available.
- 28-day implant integration window — long-term remodeling outcome not assessed.
- Authors discuss disuse models broadly (bed rest, SCI, space flight, neurectomy) as comparable mechanically unloaded conditions.

## 6. Related Work
- Authors' prior work using same botox-unloading rat model with modSLA implants — same direction of effect.
- De la Torre Canales 2024 umbrella review — clinically catalogs mandibular bone changes after repeated BoNT-A injection in M-TMD patients.
- Diabetic and ovariectomized rat models showing biomimetic Ti improves osseointegration in compromised bone.

## 7. Glossary
- **BIC** (골-임플란트 접촉률, Bone-to-Implant Contact): Percentage of implant surface in direct contact with bone, primary osseointegration outcome.
- **BV/TV** (골부피분율, Bone Volume / Total Volume): MicroCT-derived bone fraction.
- **PT** (Pre-Treatment): Machined smooth titanium surface.
- **SLA** (Sand-blasted Large-grit Acid-etched): Microroughened Ti surface.
- **modSLA / SLAnano**: SLA + nanoscale features; modSLA is hydrophilic (saline-stored), SLAnano is the hydrophobic aged form used here.
- **Sema3A** (세마포린 3A, Semaphorin 3A): Originally axon guidance molecule; osteoprotective — promotes osteoblast differentiation, inhibits osteoclast resorption.
- **Botox-induced muscle paralysis model**: Intramuscular BoNT-A causes flaccid paralysis → mechanical unloading of adjacent bone.
- **Cu-free click hydrogel**: PEG-based hydrogel formed via copper-free strain-promoted azide-alkyne cycloaddition; biocompatible biologic delivery vehicle.
- **Removal torque test**: Mechanical assay measuring force required to unscrew an integrated implant — surrogate for integrated-bone mechanical strength.
