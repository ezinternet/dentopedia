## Why Ingested

상악동 거상술(sinus floor elevation) 후 임플란트가 상악동 내로 돌출되어 발생한 상악동염(implant-related sinusitis) 관리 경로를 다룬 한국 증례보고. 기존 [[sinus-lift/lateral/craig-2024-ent-oral-surgeon-collaboration-sinus-elevation]]이 ENT-구강악안면외과 협진 원칙을 다루지만 합병증 처치 절차의 구체적 수술 기법(FESS + Caldwell-Luc 병용)이 부재하여 본 증례로 보완.

## One-line Summary

Case report (n=1): combined FESS + intraoral (Caldwell-Luc) approach resolved implant-related maxillary sinusitis caused by a 5 mm sinus-intruding implant with ostium obstruction; 10-month follow-up showed full sinus recovery.

## 한줄요약

증례보고 (n=1): 골돌출형 임플란트(5 mm 돌출)로 유발된 상악동 자연공 폐쇄성 상악동염에서 기능적 내시경 부비동 수술(FESS) + 구내접근법 병용 시 10개월 추적에서 완전 회복.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of the Korean Association of Oral and Maxillofacial Surgeons (JKAOMS)
- **Study type**: Case report
- **Institution**: Department of Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery, School of Dentistry, Keimyung University Dongsan Medical Center, Daegu, Korea
- **Publication date**: April 2014

## 2. Key Contributions

- Describes a dual-approach protocol (FESS + intraoral Caldwell-Luc) for implant-related maxillary sinusitis when ostium obstruction is present
- Establishes the clinical threshold: implant protrusion >4 mm into the sinus can cause sinusitis/rhinosinusitis
- Demonstrates that FESS alone is insufficient when an intraoral route is needed (oro-antral fistula closure, loose implant removal)
- Highlights the necessity of otolaryngologist–oral surgeon cooperative team approach under general anesthesia

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Single case report, retrospective narrative
- **Patient**: 39-year-old female referred 3 days after #16 implant placement using osteotome sinus elevation at a local clinic
- **Imaging**: Panoramic radiograph, coronal CT, Water's view X-ray, transverse dental CT
- **Medical management first**: 4-week course of penicillin/clindamycin, NSAIDs, expectorants, antihistamines — no improvement
- **Surgical protocol**:
  1. FESS (by otolaryngologist): middle turbinate excision → uncinate process removal → natural ostium enlargement → sinus irrigation, mucosal preservation
  2. Intraoral approach (by OMFS): implant removal + primary closure of oro-antral fistula
- **Postoperative**: polyvinyl acetal nasal packing for 1 week
- **Follow-up**: 1 week (radiopacity reduced, mucosal thickening persisted) and 10 months (full recovery confirmed on CT)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- CT confirmed 5 mm implant protrusion into the right maxillary sinus with a displaced bone fragment from the sinus floor
- Ethmoidal sinus co-involvement (opacification) confirmed on CT — indicating extension beyond the maxillary sinus
- Water's view showed haziness in the right maxillary sinus
- At 1 week post-op: radiopaque phase markedly decreased; mucosal thickening still present
- At 10 months: full sinus recovery confirmed on transverse dental CT
- No postoperative complications reported

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single case; no controlled comparison between FESS-only, intraoral-only, or combined approaches
- Authors note further studies are needed to evaluate FESS efficacy for recalcitrant implant-related sinusitis and recurrence prevention
- Implant removal was indicated here due to loss of primary stability + 5 mm exposure; the approach for stable implants with shorter protrusion (<4 mm) is not addressed

## 6. Related Work

- FESS preferred over Caldwell-Luc for shorter hospital stay and lower morbidity in maxillary sinusitis (cited in Introduction)
- Implant exposure >4 mm from sinus floor associated with sinusitis/rhinosinusitis (literature cited)
- Maxillary sinus floor lift can cause postoperative sinusitis via temporal obstruction of the ostio-meatal unit (OMU) through elevation of the Schneiderian membrane

## 7. Glossary

- **기능적 내시경 부비동 수술 (Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery, FESS)**: Endoscopic surgery that widens the ostiomeatal unit to facilitate paranasal sinus drainage while preserving normal anatomical structures
- **상악동 자연공 (natural ostium)**: The natural opening of the maxillary sinus into the nasal cavity, located in the ostiomeatal unit
- **골돌출형 임플란트 (sinus-intruding implant)**: An implant fixture that has penetrated through the sinus floor into the sinus cavity
- **Caldwell-Luc approach**: Intraoral surgical approach to the maxillary sinus via a sublabial incision through the anterior sinus wall
- **Ostiomeatal unit (OMU)**: The functional drainage pathway of the maxillary sinus; obstruction leads to sinusitis
- **구강-상악동 누공 (oro-antral fistula)**: Abnormal communication between the oral cavity and the maxillary sinus
