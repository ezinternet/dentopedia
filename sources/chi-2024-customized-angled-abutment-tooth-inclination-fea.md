## One-line Summary

FEA + in vitro (4 customized angled abutments 20–33° based on CBCT incisor inclination vs standard 15°): customized higher-angle abutments → higher stress under axial loading; standard 15° → higher stress under oblique loading; load direction determines which abutment design wins.

## 한줄요약

FEA+실험 (CBCT 절치 경사도 기반 맞춤형 20–33° 지대주 4종 vs 표준 15°): 맞춤형 고각도 지대주 → 축방향 하중에서 골 응력 증가; 표준 15° → 사선 하중에서 응력 증가; 하중 방향이 최적 지대주를 결정한다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: FEA + in vitro strain gauge experiment
- **Journal**: International Journal of Oral & Maxillofacial Implants
- **Year**: 2024
- **DOI**: [10.11607/jomi.10877](https://doi.org/10.11607/jomi.10877)
- **Sample**: 4 customized abutment models (21.9°, 24.15°, 20.22°, 33°) from CBCT of patients aged 20–65
- **Control**: Commercial 15° angled abutment
- **Context**: Immediate implant placement, anterior maxilla; load 100 N axial + 50 N oblique

## 2. Key Contributions

- First study designing customized abutments based on actual CBCT-measured incisor inclination across age groups
- Showed that "matching tooth inclination with abutment" does NOT universally reduce bone stress
- Clarified the load-direction dependency: axial load favors low-angle abutments; oblique load favors high-angle abutments
- Provides biomechanical rationale for careful abutment angle selection based on expected occlusal loading

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- CBCT measurement of maxillary incisor inclination across age groups (20–23, 24–39, 40–65 years)
- Average inclinations: 21.9°, 24.15°, 20.22°, 33° (age group averages and overall)
- 3D maxillary bone models from patient CBCT; FEA in standard software
- In vitro: strain gauge experiment (21.9° customized vs 15° standard); 100 N axial, 50 N oblique

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Under **axial loading**:
- Customized higher-angle abutments (21.9–33°) → ELEVATED stress on implant and bone vs 15° control
- More angulation = more bending moment = more stress at crestal bone level

Under **oblique loading**:
- Standard 15° abutments → HIGHER stress on implant and bone vs customized
- Customized abutments better aligned with tooth/force axis → lower oblique stress

In vitro (strain gauge):
- No statistically significant difference in bone strain between 21.9° customized and 15° standard under axial loading
- Standard 15° → greater bone strain under oblique loading

**Clinical implication**: customized abutments reduce stress under oblique (real-world) masticatory loads, but increase it under pure axial loads. Because real occlusion involves both, careful occlusal design is critical.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only extraction (full text paywalled)
- Static loading only (no dynamic/fatigue analysis)
- Small sample of CBCT patients; angle ranges may not represent full clinical range
- No in vivo / long-term clinical data

## 6. Related Work

- Erdoğdu 2024: abutment angle → fatigue performance; angle ↑ = stress ↑ = fatigue ↓ (multiunit context)
- Murat 2025: occlusal load direction dominates implant angle effect on cortical stress

## 7. Glossary

- **Angled abutment**: connects to axially-placed implant, redirects crown emergence axis
- **CBCT incisor inclination**: actual tooth angulation measured from CT — basis for customized abutment design
- **Axial loading**: force along implant long axis (pure vertical)
- **Oblique loading**: force at angle to implant axis — more representative of real chewing forces
