## Why Ingested

Cold atmospheric plasma (CAP) is emerging as a disinfection modality in endodontics, but its efficacy relative to established intracanal medicaments (Ca(OH)₂, TAP) against the most resistant endodontic pathogen — E. faecalis — has rarely been benchmarked head-to-head in a well-controlled in vitro model. This paper provides that direct comparison and reinforces the overview framework being built in [[endodontics/irrigation/ardila-2026-intracanal-medicaments-enterococcus-faecalis-sr]], which identified antibiotic pastes as superior to Ca(OH)₂ against E. faecalis.

## One-line Summary

In vitro study (n=63 teeth, 21-day E. faecalis biofilm): triple antibiotic paste completely eliminated bacteria (greatest CFU reduction); helium/oxygen cold atmospheric plasma significantly reduced biofilm but less so than TAP; calcium hydroxide showed no significant reduction versus positive control.

## 한줄요약

체외 연구(63개 발치치, 21일 E. faecalis 바이오필름): 삼중항생제 페이스트(TAP)가 완전한 균 제거(최대 CFU 감소)를 달성했고, 헬륨/산소 냉대기압 플라즈마는 유의하게 감소시켰으나 TAP보다 열세였으며, 수산화칼슘은 대조군 대비 유의한 차이가 없었다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First direct three-way head-to-head comparison of CAP (helium/oxygen jet, 10 min), Ca(OH)₂ (12 days), and TAP 10 mg/mL (12 days) against a mature 21-day E. faecalis biofilm in human root canals.
2. Demonstrates that cold atmospheric plasma has a statistically significant antibacterial effect on E. faecalis biofilm, establishing its biological plausibility as an endodontic disinfectant.
3. Confirms that Ca(OH)₂ — the most widely used interappointment medication — has negligible effect against established E. faecalis biofilm, aligning with systematic review evidence.
4. SEM imaging corroborates CFU-based findings, showing disrupted biofilm architecture in CAP and TAP groups.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Sample preparation**:
- 63 single-rooted human teeth; decoronated at CEJ, length standardized to 12–14 mm
- Canals prepared to ProTaper F3 (WL: k-file #15 confirmed patency)
- Irrigation: 5.25% NaOCl + 17% EDTA + saline final rinse; apical ends sealed with composite

**Biofilm formation**:
- E. faecalis ATCC 29212; 0.5 McFarland standard (1.5 × 10⁸ CFU/mL)
- 50 µL inoculum per tooth; 37°C incubation for **21 days** (BHI changed every 2 days) to produce mature biofilm penetrating dentinal tubules

**Groups (n=10 each)**:
- Group 1: Positive control for Ca(OH)₂/TAP — methylcellulose gel placed 12 days
- Group 2: Ca(OH)₂ paste, 12 days
- Group 3: TAP 10 mg/mL (metronidazole + ciprofloxacin + minocycline), 12 days
- Group 4: Helium/oxygen CAP jet, **10 minutes**
- Group 5: Positive control for plasma (untreated saline)
- Group 6: Negative control (no bacterial inoculation)

**Outcome measure**:
- F4 ProTaper file used to collect biofilm; serial dilution plating on BHI agar
- % CFU reduction = log(CFU/mL group) / log(CFU/mL same-group control)
- SEM imaging of 3 representative specimens (CAP-treated, positive control, negative control)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Group | CFU Reduction vs Control | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| TAP (10 mg/mL, 12d) | **Complete elimination** | p < 0.05 vs control |
| CAP (He/O₂, 10 min) | Significant reduction | p < 0.05 vs control |
| Ca(OH)₂ (12d) | Negligible | No significant difference vs control |

- **TAP > CAP > Ca(OH)₂** in antibacterial efficacy hierarchy
- SEM: biofilm structure disrupted in CAP group; intact in Ca(OH)₂ group
- CAP mechanism: reactive oxygen/nitrogen species (RONS) interact with bacterial membranes; He/O₂ mix generates plasma plume at near-room temperature (~37°C) without thermal tissue damage

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **In vitro** model — cannot replicate in vivo complexity (apical anatomy, periapical tissue, salivary contamination)
- CAP exposure was only 10 minutes vs 12 days for medicaments — not time-equivalent comparison; clinical delivery of CAP into root canal depth remains a technical challenge
- Single CAP device/gas mix (He/O₂); results may not generalize to other plasma jets (Ar, N₂ mixes)
- TAP at 10 mg/mL chosen for cytotoxicity-mitigated regenerative protocols — higher concentrations might further widen the gap
- Minocycline discoloration risk with TAP not discussed
- No assessment of dentinal tubule penetration depth for CAP

## 6. Related Work

- Ca(OH)₂ limitation against E. faecalis: multiple SRs show Ca(OH)₂ alone insufficient for deep biofilm (consistent with this study's finding)
- TAP efficacy: well-established in regenerative endodontic literature; cytotoxicity trade-off at higher concentrations (Evans et al. 2014 cited)
- CAP in dentistry: Fridman et al., Laroussi et al. — antimicrobial RONS mechanism
- This paper is cited in the ardila-2026 SR as a primary study contributing to the antibiotic-paste-superiority signal

## 7. Glossary

- **CAP (Cold Atmospheric Plasma)**: partially ionized gas at near-ambient temperature; generates RONS with antimicrobial/biofilm-disrupting properties
- **TAP (Triple Antibiotic Paste)**: metronidazole + ciprofloxacin + minocycline; standard in regenerative endodontics
- **CFU**: colony-forming units — quantitative measure of viable bacteria
- **RONS**: reactive oxygen and nitrogen species — primary antimicrobial agents in CAP
- **McFarland standard**: turbidity reference for bacterial suspension concentration
