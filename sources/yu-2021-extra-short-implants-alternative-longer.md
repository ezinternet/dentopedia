## One-line Summary
Meta-analysis of 21 RCTs (2576 implants, 1387 patients) showing extra-short implants (≤6 mm) have equivalent survival to longer implants (≥8 mm) at 1–3 years and lower MBL and biological complications; at 5 years, longer implants show better survival unless bone augmentation was used.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Scientific Reports (2021) 11:8152 (Nature Publishing Group)
- **Type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (PRISMA, AMSTAR-2; PROSPERO CRD42020155342)
- **PICO**: Extra-short implants ≤6 mm (I) vs. longer implants ≥8 mm with or without bone augmentation (C) in edentulous jaws (P); survival rate, MBL, biological and prosthetic complication rates (O)
- **Only RCTs**; ≥12 months follow-up required
- **Sample**: 21 RCT series, 31 articles, 2576 implants (1243 extra-short + 1333 longer), 1387 patients

## 2. Key Contributions
- Extra-short implants equivalent to longer implants in survival at 1 and 3 years
- At 5 years: longer implants superior in native bone — but **NOT** when bone augmentation was performed
- Extra-short implants show significantly less MBL (from implant placement) and far fewer biological complications
- No difference in prosthesis complications
- Smoking, loading method: no significant effect on survival in meta-regression

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Search**: PubMed, Web of Science, EMBASE, Cochrane (until Nov 2020)
- **Extra-short**: ≤6 mm; **Longer**: ≥8 mm (7 mm implants excluded)
- **Statistics**: Stata 15; WMD for MBL; RR for binary outcomes; random-effects when I² > 50%
- **Subgroups**: Maxilla vs. mandible; with vs. without bone augmentation; loading method; smoker inclusion
- **Risk of bias**: ROB 2 (5 high risk, 7 some concerns, 9 low risk); GRADE: low to moderate quality evidence

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Implant Survival
- 1 year: RR = 1.002 (CI 0.981–1.024), p = 0.856 — **no difference** (moderate quality)
- 3 years: RR = 0.996 (CI 0.968–1.025), p = 0.772 — **no difference** (moderate quality)
- 5 years (overall): RR = 0.970 (CI 0.944–0.997), **p < 0.05** — longer implants BETTER
  - 5 years with BA (reconstructed bone): RR = 0.977 (CI 0.945–1.010), p = 0.171 — **no difference**
  - 5 years without BA (native bone): RR = 0.955 (CI 0.912–0.999), p < 0.05 — longer implants better

### Marginal Bone Loss
- From implant placement: WMD = **−0.22 mm** (CI −0.277 to −0.164), p < 0.01 — favors extra-short (low quality)
- From prosthesis restoration: WMD = 0.016 mm (CI −0.036 to 0.068), p = 0.555 — **no difference** (moderate quality)

### Biological Complications
- RR = **0.321** (CI 0.243–0.422), p < 0.01 — strongly favors extra-short (moderate quality)
- Extra-short implants have ~68% lower biological complication rate

### Prosthesis Complications
- RR = 1.308 (CI 0.893–1.915), p = 0.168 — **no difference** (moderate quality)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Short follow-up for most studies (1–3 years predominant)
- Heterogeneity in implant systems, surfaces, loading protocols
- Geographic concentration (Italy-heavy primary studies)
- Need long-term (≥5 year) multicenter RCTs
- Extra-short definition (≤6 mm) vs. short (7–9 mm) distinction not always clear in clinical practice

## 7. Glossary
- **Extra-short implant**: ≤6 mm intrabony length (stricter than "short implant" which can mean ≤10 mm)
- **WMD (Weighted Mean Difference)**: Pooled continuous outcome measure accounting for study size
- **RR (Risk Ratio)**: RR < 1 = lower event rate in extra-short group; RR > 1 = higher rate
- **Biological complications**: Peri-implant mucositis, peri-implantitis, bone loss around implant
- **Prosthesis complications**: Screw loosening, crown fracture, decementation, etc.
- **Loading method**: Immediate (same day), early (< 3 months), conventional (≥ 3 months)
