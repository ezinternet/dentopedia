## One-line Summary
SR+MA comparing exercise therapy to occlusal splints for painful TMD; found exercise therapy equivalent or superior for pain relief and MMO improvement, suggesting it as a viable non-appliance alternative.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Annals of Palliative Medicine 2021;10(6):6122–6132
- **DOI**: 10.21037/apm-21-451
- **Affiliation**: Shanghai Ninth People's Hospital
- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis

## 2. Key Contributions
- Head-to-head comparison of exercise therapy vs. occlusal splint therapy (교합안정장치) for painful TMD
- Quantitative pooling of pain (VAS) and MMO outcomes via meta-analysis
- Supports exercise therapy as effective and cost-efficient alternative to splint fabrication
- Highlights patient adherence advantages of exercise-based protocols

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Databases: PubMed, Cochrane, EMBASE, Web of Science
- Inclusion: RCTs with head-to-head or comparative arms (exercise vs. splint)
- Primary outcomes: pain (VAS/NRS), MMO
- Secondary: patient satisfaction, recurrence
- Random-effects meta-analysis; heterogeneity assessed via I²
- GRADE assessment for certainty of evidence

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Exercise therapy showed no significant inferiority to occlusal splints for pain reduction (pooled MD near zero, p>0.05)
- MMO improvement: exercise therapy numerically superior in several trials (pooled mean difference favoring exercise)
- Patient adherence higher with exercise protocols in most studies
- Overall certainty of evidence: moderate
- No serious adverse events reported with either intervention

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Limited number of eligible RCTs
- Short-to-medium follow-up (≤6 months in most trials)
- Exercise protocols varied considerably across studies (jaw stretching, Rocabado, Graded Motor Imagery)
- Long-term comparative data (>12 months) lacking

## 7. Glossary
- **Exercise therapy**: Structured jaw physiotherapy including range-of-motion exercises, muscle stretching, postural training
- **Painful TMD**: TMD subtype presenting with myofascial pain and/or arthralgia as primary complaint
- **VAS** (Visual Analogue Scale): 0–100 mm pain self-report scale
- **MMO** (하악 최대개구량): Maximum mouth opening in millimeters
- **MD**: Mean difference (effect size metric in continuous outcome meta-analysis)
