## Why Ingested

Extends the elastodontic/myofunctional evidence base along the **transverse / posterior-crossbite axis** — the weakest documented EA indication. Existing sibling pages on transverse correction ([[wiki/orthodontics/myofunctional/ortu-2025-removable-devices-palatal-expansion-rct]]) and the broader interception reviews ([[wiki/orthodontics/myofunctional/ronsivalle-2023-elastodontic-appliances-malocclusion-interception-children-review]], co-authored by Ronsivalle who also co-authored this paper) flag that EA efficacy in functional posterior cross-bite (FPXB) and mild maxillary contraction was previously unproven. This is the **first study** to quantify palatal/maxillary-arch changes after EA treatment of FPXB, using 3D digital-model superimposition (surface-to-surface matching + deviation analysis), and so reinforces the transverse-correction claim with objective imaging evidence.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective cohort (n=39; TG=25, CG=14, mean age 7y) — AMCOP elastodontic activator corrected functional posterior cross-bite and increased palatal symmetry (surface-matching 81.1%→92.3%) plus intermolar/intercanine widths over 12 months, versus no change in untreated controls.

## 한줄요약

후향적 코호트 (n=39; 치료군 25, 대조군 14, 평균 7세) — AMCOP 탄성교정장치(Elastodontic Appliance, EA)가 기능성 후방 교차교합(Functional Posterior Cross-Bite, FPXB)을 교정하고 12개월간 구개 대칭성(표면 일치율 81.1%→92.3%)과 대구치/견치 폭경을 증가시켰으며, 미치료 대조군은 변화 없었다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First study** in the literature to investigate the effect of elastodontic appliances (EAs) on functional posterior cross-bite (FPXB) and mild maxillary contraction in growing children — a previously unproven EA indication.
2. Applies a **3D digital-model imaging workflow** (intra-oral scan superimposition + mirrored-model surface-to-surface matching + Euclidean deviation analysis) to quantify palatal morphology and symmetry objectively, rather than only linear arch widths.
3. Demonstrates that the asymmetry in FPXB is **not purely dental** but involves the dentoalveolar process (skeletal-level component), localized to the lower palate at the alveolar process, sparing the basal bone.
4. Shows that an untreated control group (delayed treatment due to COVID-19 restrictions) provides an ethically acceptable "heritage" comparator to separate treatment effects from growth.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Retrospective controlled cohort. Subjects seeking orthodontic treatment at the Department of Orthodontics, University of Catania, Sept 2017–Dec 2020. IRB-approved, Helsinki-compliant, parental informed consent.
- **Sample**: n = 39 total, mean age 7 ± 0.8 y.
  - **Treatment group (TG)**: n = 25, mean age 7.3 ± 0.9 y — received **AMCOP Integral/Basic activator** (Ortho Protec, BA, Italy), an elastomeric preformed device favoring expansion of both arches with oral flanges isolating perioral muscles and guiding tongue posture at the palatine spot.
  - **Control group (CG)**: n = 14, mean age 6.8 ± 0.7 y — postponed treatment by one year due to COVID-19 social/health restrictions.
- **Inclusion**: (1) intra-oral scan at T0 (pre-tx) and T1 (after appliance removal); (2) posterior cross-bite of ≥2 maxillary posterior teeth; (3) mandibular shift toward cross-bite ≥2 mm in centric occlusion (i.e. functional = FPXB); (4) class I or edge-to-edge molar relationship.
- **Exclusion**: missing teeth, anterior cross-bite, TMD, prior orthodontic treatment, caries, mobile deciduous posterior teeth, craniofacial deformities.
- **Wear protocol**: appliance worn at night + 2 h/day during active correction; patients instructed to bite the device with lips in contact; after FPXB correction, retention = 2 h/day. Slight occlusal grinding of maxillary deciduous canines as needed to remove interferences.
- **Imaging timing**: intra-oral scans + bite registration at T0 and at T1 = 12 months (Carestream 3600).
- **Measurements** (Ortho Analyzer, 3Shape):
  - Linear: intermolar width (IMW, permanent first molars) and intercanine width (ICW, deciduous canines), using the dento-gingival junction center as landmark; emi-lateral widths (eIMW, eICW) compared cross-bite vs non-cross-bite side.
  - Median palatal plane (MPP) traced from a point adjacent to the 2nd ruga and a 2nd point 1 cm distal along the palatal raphe.
- **3D surface-to-surface matching** (Geomagic Control X 2017): a gingival-plane–defined palate model was mirrored across MPP for both T0 (T0m) and T1 (T1m); original and mirrored models superimposed (preliminary MPP registration + best-fit alignment); deviation analysis computed mean/max Euclidean surface distance across 100% of surface points, with color-coded maps at 0.5 mm tolerance (green = match). The **% of distances within tolerance** = degree of palatal symmetry.
- **Reliability**: same operator (15 y experience, A.L.G.) repeated at 4 weeks (intra-operator); a second operator (V.R.) performed inter-operator. ICC: linear 0.901–0.936; 3D 0.887–0.931 (all excellent).
- **Statistics**: Shapiro–Wilk + Levene (normal → parametric). Paired/unpaired Student's t-tests for inter-timing, inter-group, and cross-bite-vs-non-cross-bite comparisons; chi-square for categorical. Significance p < 0.05. Sample-size calc: 13/group for a 1.52 mm eIMW difference at 80% power. SPSS v24.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Arch widths (IMW, ICW)**: statistically significant increase T0→T1 in TG (p < 0.05); slight non-significant reduction in CG (p > 0.05).
- **Baseline asymmetry**: at T0, eICW and eIMW significantly narrower on the cross-bite side vs non-cross-bite side in both groups (~1 mm for eICW, ~1.5 mm for eIMW) — mild maxillary-arch asymmetry.
- **Asymmetry reduction**: at T1, the eICW/eIMW mean differences between sides were significantly reduced vs T0 in the TG (p < 0.05) but unchanged in CG (p > 0.05) — expansion was greater on the cross-bite side, reducing asymmetry even vs CG (p < 0.05).
- **Palatal surface symmetry (% agreement, original vs mirrored model)**:
  - T0: TG 81.12% ± 3.03, CG 79.36% ± 2.95 (mild morphological asymmetry, both groups).
  - T1: **TG 92.32% ± 4.05** (significant increase, p < 0.05); CG 77.47% ± 3.14 (slight non-significant reduction, p > 0.05).
- **Deviation map**: palatal vault predominantly green (matched); intense blue on one alveolar-process side and red on the other — asymmetry confined to the lower palate at the alveolar process level, basal bone spared.
- **Cross-bite correction**: ALL TG subjects achieved complete FPXB correction with coincident upper/lower midlines in both centric occlusion and centric relation. CG showed NO spontaneous correction (FPXB is not self-correcting).
- **Reliability**: intra- and inter-operator ICC 0.887–0.936 (excellent).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Small sample**, particularly the limited control group (n=14) — described as a "heritage" untreated sample acceptable given ethical constraints on recruiting untreated growing controls.
- **Retrospective design** in both groups — confounders could not be controlled a priori, though inclusion/exclusion produced a homogeneous sample for age and dental/skeletal maturation.
- **No long-term follow-up** — stability of palatal/transverse improvements beyond 12 months unknown.
- Authors call for **randomized studies** and **comparative trials** against other FPXB appliances (skeletal vs dentoalveolar expanders).

## 7. Glossary

- **EA (Elastodontic Appliance)**: removable silicone-elastomer interceptive device generating light biological elastic forces; derived from the Occlus-O-Guide / Eruption Guidance Appliance (EGA).
- **AMCOP**: brand of elastodontic activator (Ortho Protec); Integral/Basic version favors bi-arch expansion.
- **FPXB (Functional Posterior Cross-Bite)**: posterior cross-bite with a functional mandibular shift (≥2 mm) toward the cross-bite side caused by mild bilateral maxillary constriction and occlusal interference.
- **IMW / ICW**: intermolar width (permanent 1st molars) / intercanine width (deciduous canines).
- **eIMW / eICW**: emi-lateral (hemi-arch) intermolar/intercanine width, measured per side to assess asymmetry.
- **MPP (Median Palatal Plane)**: reference plane traced along the median palatal raphe for mirroring/registration.
- **Surface-to-surface matching / deviation analysis**: 3D method computing Euclidean distance between an original palate model and its mirror to quantify symmetry (% within tolerance).
- **T0 / T1**: pre-treatment / post-treatment (12-month) time points.
- **ICC (Intraclass Correlation Coefficient)**: reliability statistic.
