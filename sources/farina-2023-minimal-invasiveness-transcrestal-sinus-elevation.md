## One-line Summary
Systematic review (19 articles, 15 studies) on what makes transcrestal sinus floor elevation (tSFE) less invasive: powered instruments with stop devices, screwable osteotomes, standardized drill sequences with trephine cores, and immediate implant placement at molar extraction sites with ≥4 mm interradicular septum all reduce invasiveness while preserving clinical effectiveness.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Periodontology 2000, vol. 91, issue 1, pp. 145–166
- **Published**: 01 August 2022 (issue 2023)
- **Study type**: Systematic review of controlled clinical trials
- **Affiliation**: Research Centre for the Study of Periodontal Diseases, University of Ferrara, Italy

## 2. Key Contributions
- First systematic literature review that explicitly frames the **invasiveness construct** of transcrestal sinus floor elevation (tSFE), encompassing morbidity, chair time, autologous tissue harvest, reconstructive devices, and cost.
- Shows that powered instruments outperform manual osteotomes + hand mallet on intraoperative and patient-reported morbidity, without sacrificing clinical effectiveness.
- Endorses **standardized drill sequences** (including a trephine bur to harvest a bone core) for chair-time efficiency and histomorphometric output.
- Defines a **clinical decision rule** at molar sockets: if interradicular septum ≥ 4 mm in height, immediate tSFE + implant placement is a valid one-step option.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Literature search of controlled clinical trials evaluating tSFE invasiveness or its determinants.
- Nineteen articles representing 15 unique studies included.
- Invasiveness operationalized as: (a) intra-/post-surgery morbidity, (b) treatment time, (c) need for autologous harvest and reconstructive devices, (d) costs, (e) elimination of preoperative invasive diagnostics.
- Subdomain analyses: instrumentation (manual osteotomes vs powered drills vs ultrasonic), pressure-control devices (screwable osteotomes, stop devices), grafting strategy (graft vs no graft), and immediate vs staged implant placement.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Postoperative pain after tSFE**: < 12 / 100 on VAS — comparable to implant placement in native bone.
- **Network meta-analysis cited (Al-Moraissi et al)**: tSFE superior to lateral SFE at residual bone height (RBH) **4–8 mm**.
- **Farina trial (RBH 3–6 mm)**: tSFE outperformed lateral SFE on chair time, intra- and postoperative complications, patient-reported outcomes, rescue analgesic dose, OHRQoL, and cost.
- **Standardized drill + trephine sequence**: reduced chair time, high patient tolerability, allows bone-core histomorphometry.
- **Immediate placement** at molar extraction sites: feasible when interradicular septum ≥ 4 mm.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Heterogeneity in tSFE protocols (instrumentation, graft, implant geometry) limits formal meta-analysis pooling.
- Most studies are short- to medium-term; long-term marginal bone level and implant survival data are sparse for newer powered protocols.
- Patient-reported outcome instruments are inconsistent across studies.
- Cost analyses are jurisdiction-specific.

## 7. Glossary
- **tSFE (transcrestal sinus floor elevation)**: Sinus floor augmentation through the implant osteotomy itself, without a lateral window.
- **RBH (residual bone height)**: Vertical bone between alveolar crest and sinus floor.
- **Osteotome (Summers technique)**: Tapered mallet-driven instrument originally used to in-fracture the sinus floor.
- **Screwable osteotome**: Screw-driven osteotome variant that replaces mallet impact with controlled torque to reduce pressure spikes.
- **Stop device**: Drill-mounted depth-limiter that prevents over-penetration past the sinus floor.
- **Trephine bur**: Hollow cylindrical bur that harvests a cylindrical bone core (usable for histomorphometry and as autograft).
- **Interradicular septum**: Bony partition between roots of a multi-rooted tooth, used as a stable platform for immediate implant placement after extraction.
