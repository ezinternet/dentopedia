## Why Ingested

2014년 기초 SR+MA로 경사 임플란트 변연골 소실 vs 수직 임플란트 비교 최초 정량분석. [[implants/implants-clinical-decision-ladder]] 의 tilted implant 근거 baseline 제공.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (19 studies, n=670 patients, 1,494 axial + 1,338 tilted implants, ≥1yr): crestal bone loss around tilted vs axial implants NOT significantly different (P=.09 at 1yr, P=.30 at ≥3yr).

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (19편, n=670명, 1,494 수직+1,338 경사 임플란트, ≥1년): 경사 임플란트의 변연골 소실은 수직 임플란트와 유의한 차이 없음 (1년 P=.09, 3년↑ P=.30) — 단기·중기에 예측 가능한 기법.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: European Journal of Oral Implantology (Suppl 2)
- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis
- **Search**: Electronic + hand search through January 2014
- **Included studies**: 19 articles (14 prospective + 5 retrospective)

## 2. Key Contributions
- First meta-analysis on marginal bone loss: axial vs tilted implants
- Establishes tilted implant as predictable technique with excellent short-to-mid-term prognosis
- Identifies maxillary implants at higher risk of failure than mandibular

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Inclusion criteria independent of study design
- Data on marginal bone loss and survival extracted
- Effect of implant tilting, location, prosthesis type, loading mode, study design analyzed
- Statistical: meta-analysis comparing crestal bone level change

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Total**: 670 patients, 716 prostheses (415 maxilla, 301 mandible)
- **Implants**: 1,494 axial + 1,338 tilted
- **Crestal bone loss at 1yr**: axial 0.43–1.13 mm; tilted 0.34–1.14 mm → NS (P=.09)
- **Crestal bone loss at ≥3yr**: trend toward lower loss in axial, NS (P=.30)
- **Failures (within 1yr)**: 46 implants (18 axial, 28 tilted) in 38 patients; most in maxilla
- **Survival at ≥12mo**: maxilla 97.4% vs mandible 99.6% (P significant)
- **Prosthesis failure**: none reported
- Location, loading mode, restoration type, study design → no significant effect on MBL

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Long-term data scarce (2014)
- Need for randomized trials
- Predominantly observational studies

## 6. Related Work
- Del Fabbro et al. 2022: updated SR+MA with longer follow-up showing axial < tilted MBL
- Lin & Eckert 2018: SR confirming no survival difference

## 7. Glossary
- **Crestal bone level change**: marginal bone loss around implant collar (mm)
- **Tilted implant**: distal inclination of posterior implant in edentulous jaw
