## Why Ingested
근관치료된 구치의 수복 선택(직접 복합레진 vs 간접 수복)에 대한 최상위 근거를 보강하기 위해 ingest. 기존 [[inlay/angeletaki-2016-direct-vs-indirect-inlay-onlay-sr-ma]]와 [[inlay/bresser-2023-intracoronal-restorations-longevity-sr-ma]]의 direct-vs-indirect 비교를 endodontically treated posterior teeth라는 특정 시나리오로 한정·확장하며, 적응증 교란(indication confounding) 때문에 관찰연구의 우열 판정이 무효라는 점을 명시적으로 강조한다.

## One-line Summary
SR+MA (22 studies: 2 RCTs, 3 prospective, 17 retrospective) on endodontically treated posterior teeth: over the short term (2.5–3 y), low-quality evidence shows no difference in tooth survival or restorative success between direct composite resin and indirect restorations (pooled RCT OR for tooth retention 2.06 [0.08–53.52], P=.66), but observational data are confounded because direct restorations were used on teeth with more remaining tissue or worse prognosis.

## 한줄요약
SR+MA (22편: RCT 2, 전향 3, 후향 17). 근관치료된 구치를 직접 복합레진 vs 간접 수복으로 비교 — 단기(2.5~3년)에는 치아 생존율·수복 성공률에 차이 없음(RCT 통합 OR 2.06 [0.08–53.52], P=.66), 단 관찰연구는 직접수복이 잔존치질이 많거나 예후 불량한 치아에 적용된 적응증 교란(confounding)이 심해 근거 수준이 낮음.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Focuses specifically on **endodontically treated POSTERIOR teeth** and segregates direct composite resin from other direct materials (amalgam/GI), unlike earlier reviews that clustered them.
- PICO: in endodontically treated teeth (P), what is the difference in clinical performance (O) between indirect restorations (I) and direct composite resin (C)?
- Demonstrates that the dominant problem in this literature is **indication confounding**: direct restorations are preferentially placed on teeth with more remaining coronal tissue (≥1 marginal ridge) OR on teeth with an unfavorable prognosis — making naive direct-vs-indirect survival comparisons in observational studies invalid.
- Provides a **target-RCT framework** (ROBINS-I) to grade observational studies and a GRADE appraisal yielding low-quality evidence.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Databases**: MEDLINE/PubMed, CENTRAL, EMBASE searched 6 Dec 2020. Records: MEDLINE 564, EMBASE 701, CENTRAL 10 (1275 total; 938 after de-duplication).
- **Inclusion**: RCTs, prospective and retrospective clinical trials published since 2000 comparing direct composite resin AND indirect restorations; English/German/Dutch. Excluded: case reports, in vitro, surveys, anterior-only, pre-2000.
- **Screening**: 2 independent reviewers (M.C.F.M.d.K., M.M.G.), third reviewer (M.S.C.) for disagreements; hand search added 12. 41 full texts → 22 included.
- **Risk of bias**: ROB2 for RCTs; ROBINS-I for prospective/retrospective. Confounding by restorative indication scored from low to critical. GRADE for body of evidence.
- **Meta-analysis**: Review Manager v5.4.1; Mantel–Haenszel random-effects odds ratio (OR) with 95% CI; heterogeneity via I². Pooling restricted to studies with clearly described posterior composite resin and indirect restorations (α=.05).
- **Outcomes**: tooth retention/survival and restorative success.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **22 studies included**: 2 RCTs, 3 prospective, 17 retrospective. Teeth per study 57 to 1,462,936 (median 220). Follow-up: short term (2–5 y) in 13 studies, medium term (7.3–11 y) in 9.
- Data sources: 5 insurance/national databases, 14 university settings, 3 private practices.
- Most indirect restorations were complete crowns; only 2 studies included partial restorations (24 gold partial crowns, 69 inlays). Posts used in 13 studies (12%–100%, median 46%).
- **Short-term tooth retention (RCTs pooled)**: OR = 2.06 (95% CI 0.08–53.52), P=.66 → no difference (only Skupien 2016 estimable; Mannocci 2002 not estimable). Quality: **low**.
- **Short-term restorative success (RCTs pooled)**: OR = 2.31 (95% CI 0.59–9.08), P=.29 → no significant difference; all reported restorative failures were repairable.
- **Observational meta-analysis not possible** — heterogeneous data, frequent inability to isolate posterior composite resin outcomes.
- Risk of bias: 9 observational studies "critical" (confounding on prognosis vs target RCT), 11 "serious".
- Signal of confounding: in some studies direct composite resin showed more reinterventions/fractures (e.g., Jirathanyanatt 2019 HR 3.9 [1.8–8.7]; Stenhagen 2020 more reinterventions) — but these direct restorations were placed on teeth with uncertain prognosis.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Overall evidence quality **low** (GRADE); only 2 RCTs, both short-term (≤3 y).
- Pervasive **confounding by indication** — direct vs indirect not randomly assigned; coronal tissue amount and prognosis differ systematically between groups.
- Heterogeneous outcome definitions; many studies could not isolate posterior composite resin from other direct materials (amalgam, GI).
- No valid medium/long-term comparison possible.
- **Call for future RCTs** controlling for amount of remaining coronal tooth tissue and other baseline characteristics before concluding superiority of either approach.

## 6. Related Work
- Earlier SRs comparing direct vs indirect restorations on endodontically treated teeth clustered amalgam with composite resin, limiting validity.
- Related to the broader inlay/onlay direct-vs-indirect literature (Angeletaki 2016 SR+MA; Bresser 2023 intracoronal restorations longevity SR+MA).
- Endodontically treated posterior teeth are reported more prone to restoration/tooth loss, motivating the focused question.

## 7. Glossary
- **ETT**: Endodontically treated tooth.
- **Direct composite resin restoration**: chairside resin filling, single appointment, minimal tooth removal, lower cost.
- **Indirect restoration**: lab/CAD-CAM-fabricated crown, inlay, onlay, or endocrown — more rigid material, greater anatomic control.
- **Confounding by indication**: systematic bias where treatment choice correlates with baseline prognosis/tissue, distorting outcome comparison.
- **ROB2 / ROBINS-I**: Cochrane risk-of-bias tools for RCTs and non-randomized studies, respectively.
- **GRADE**: Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation — rates certainty of evidence.
- **OR (odds ratio)**: ratio of odds of an event between groups; CI crossing 1 = non-significant.
- **Marginal ridge**: proximal coronal wall; remaining marginal ridges proxy preserved tooth structure.
