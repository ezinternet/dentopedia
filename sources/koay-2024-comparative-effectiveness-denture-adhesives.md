## Why Ingested

기존 [[wiki/complete-denture/shu-2021-systematic-review-meta-analysis-denture-adhesives]]는 의치접착제가 유지력·저작력을 향상시킨다는 것을 보여주었지만, 크림·파우더·스트립·쿠션 등 접착제 유형 간 직접 비교 근거가 없었다. 본 NMA(Koay 2024)는 네트워크 메타분석으로 유형별 효능 순위를 제공해 임상 선택에 직접 활용 가능한 근거를 추가함.

## One-line Summary

Network meta-analysis of 17 RCTs comparing denture adhesive types (cream, powder, strip, cushion) for complete dentures — cream and powder adhesives most effective for mandibular retention (Kapur's index) and bite force (moderate certainty evidence).

## 한줄요약

## 2. Key Contributions

- First network meta-analysis (NMA) comparing types of denture adhesives directly against each other using SUCRA rankings
- Provides head-to-head comparative efficacy of cream, powder, strip, and cushion adhesives
- Uses GRADE approach to assess certainty of evidence across outcomes
- Identifies cream adhesives as most effective for bite force (both incisal and premolar/molar regions)
- Identifies powder adhesives as most effective for mandibular retention (Kapur's index)
- Demonstrates that no adhesive type achieves significance for all measured outcomes

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Study Design**: Systematic review with network meta-analysis (NMA)

**PICO Framework**:
- Population: Complete denture wearers
- Intervention: Denture adhesives (cream, powder, strip, cushion)
- Comparator: Placebo, no adhesive, or other adhesive types
- Outcomes: Bite force, denture retention (Kapur's index, dislodgments), masticatory performance, OHRQoL

**Search Strategy**:
- Databases: Scopus, PubMed, Cochrane CENTRAL
- From inception to July 2023
- PROSPERO registration: CRD42023451045

**Inclusion Criteria**:
- Randomized controlled trials
- Complete denture wearers
- Denture adhesive interventions with measurable outcomes

**Quantitative Analysis**:
- Network meta-analysis framework
- Surface Under the Cumulative Ranking (SUCRA) system for ranking adhesive types
- GRADE approach for certainty of evidence assessment

**Studies Included**: 17 articles in quantitative analysis

**Adhesive Types Compared**:
1. Cream adhesives (e.g., Fixodent, Protefix, Corega)
2. Powder adhesives
3. Strip adhesives
4. Cushion adhesives

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Cream Adhesives — Bite Force**:
- Incisal region: RR = 7.63 (95% CI: 3.34, 11.91), P < .05
- Premolar/molar region: RR = 33.66 (95% CI: 15.15, 52.16), P < .05

**Cream Adhesives — Retention**:
- Reduced denture dislodgments: RR = -5.35 (95% CI: -9.93, -0.77), P = 0.022
- Kapur's index — maxillary denture: RR = 0.80 (95% CI: 0.18, 1.42), P = 0.012
- Kapur's index — mandibular denture: RR = 1.27 (95% CI: 0.67, 1.87), P < .05

**Powder Adhesives — Bite Force**:
- Premolar/molar region: RR = 36.00 (95% CI: 16.20, 55.79), P < .05

**Powder Adhesives — Retention**:
- Kapur's index — mandibular denture: RR = 1.54 (95% CI: 0.58, 2.49), P = 0.002

**SUCRA Rankings** (higher = better):
- Bite force (incisal): Cream adhesives ranked highest
- Bite force (premolar/molar): Powder adhesives ranked highest by point estimate, cream competitive
- Mandibular retention (Kapur's): Powder > Cream

**Key Negative Finding**:
- Strip and cushion adhesives did not achieve statistical significance for any primary outcome
- No adhesive type was significant for masticatory performance or OHRQoL outcomes

**Certainty of Evidence**: Moderate (GRADE) for the significant findings (cream and powder improving mandibular retention and premolar/molar bite force)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small number of RCTs per adhesive type comparison (network sparsity)
- Heterogeneity in outcome measurement tools and timing across studies
- Limited long-term data (most studies are short-term crossover trials)
- Most studies do not adequately report on adverse effects
- Lack of head-to-head RCTs comparing cream vs powder directly
- Under-representation of strip and cushion adhesive studies
- Future research needed: long-term RCTs, standardized outcome measurement, patient-centered outcomes (OHRQoL)

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary

- **Denture Adhesive (DA)**: Material applied to the tissue-bearing surface of a denture to improve retention and stability via adhesion/cohesion mechanisms
- **Kapur's Index**: Clinical scoring system (0–3) assessing denture retention through standardized displacement tests
- **SUCRA**: Surface Under the Cumulative Ranking curve — probability-based ranking in NMA; higher value = higher likelihood of being the best intervention
- **NMA (Network Meta-Analysis)**: Meta-analytic method allowing indirect comparisons of interventions not directly compared in head-to-head trials
- **GRADE**: Grading of Recommendations, Assessment, Development, and Evaluations — framework for rating certainty of evidence (high/moderate/low/very low)
- **Bite Force / Occlusal Force**: Measured in Newtons (N); assessed at incisal or premolar/molar regions using force transducers
- **Denture Dislodgment**: Number of times denture is displaced from basal seat during standardized functional tests
