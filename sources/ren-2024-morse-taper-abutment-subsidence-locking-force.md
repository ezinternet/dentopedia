## Why Ingested

"어버트먼트 침하(sinking-down)"는 순수 Morse taper(Bicon형) 마찰결합 임플란트의 임상 특유 현상으로, 시멘트·스크류 보철과 달리 별도 고정 스크류 없이 마찰력만으로 결합되어 시간이 지나며 기구·교합력에 의해 어버트먼트가 추가로 가라앉아 후기 교합관계가 바뀐다. 기존 [[implants/seong-2016-external-internal-implant-connection-system]]는 외부/내부 연결 생역학을 다루지만 마찰결합(taper-lock) 특유의 침하 정량 데이터가 없었고, [[prosthetic-materials/ziada-2025-abutment-material-stress-distribution-fea]]는 어버트먼트 응력만 FEA로 본다. 본 벤치 실험(Ren 2024)은 침하량(subsidence)과 결합력(locking force)을 잠금깊이·잠금기둥경·외벽두께·하중으로 정량화해 "왜 ≥5회 가압 시팅과 지연 최종 교합조정이 필요한가"의 근거를 제공한다.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro bench study on Bicon-type pure Morse taper (1.5° taper) implant-abutment specimens (≥10 per group) showing abutment subsidence rises with seating load (r=0.645) but falls most strongly with deeper locking depth (r=−0.807) and larger locking-post diameter and thicker implant outer wall, while pull-apart locking force rises with load (r=0.963), depth (r=0.607), and post diameter (r=0.372) — supporting ≥5 seating loadings and delayed final occlusal adjustment to prevent late occlusal change from continued sinking.

## 한줄요약

순수 Morse taper(Bicon형, 1.5°) 임플란트-어버트먼트 벤치 실험(그룹당 ≥10): 어버트먼트 침하량은 시팅 하중↑에 따라 증가(r=0.645)하나 잠금깊이↑(r=−0.807, 최강)·잠금기둥경↑·외벽두께↑에 따라 감소했고, 분리 결합력은 하중(r=0.963)·잠금깊이(r=0.607)·기둥경(r=0.372)에 따라 증가 — 후기 교합변화를 막으려면 시팅 시 ≥5회 가압과 최종 교합조정 지연을 권고.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies, for a **pure Morse taper (taper-lock, no retention screw)** connection, the two outcomes clinicians care about: how firmly the abutment locks (pull-apart "locking force") and how far it sinks ("abutment subsidence" / 下沉量), as functions of four design/loading variables.
- Identifies **locking depth as the dominant lever on subsidence** (r=−0.807): deeper engagement = less sinking.
- Shows **seating load is the dominant lever on locking force** (r=0.963) and itself increases subsidence (r=0.645) — i.e., the same force that locks the abutment also seats it deeper.
- Demonstrates **implant outer-wall thickness** is uncoupled from locking force but inversely related to subsidence (thicker wall → less sinking via reduced elastic deformation).
- Translates bench data into a seating protocol: **load the abutment ≥5 times at seating**, use a provisional with only preliminary occlusal adjustment, and defer final restoration/occlusal adjustment until after a settling-in period.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Design: in-vitro mechanical (bench) experiment on custom-machined specimens modeled on the **Bicon** pure Morse taper implant-abutment design.
- Fixed parameter: locking taper uniformly **1.5°**.
- Independent variables (factorial):
  - Locking depth: **1.0, 2.0, 3.0 mm**
  - Locking-post (column) diameter: **2.5, 3.0, 3.5 mm**
  - Implant outer-wall thickness: **0.15, 0.30 mm**
  - Testing-machine loading force: **200, 300, 400 N**
- ≥10 specimens per group.
- Loading protocol: universal testing machine, identical for all — **finger pressure + specified load × 5 times**.
- Subsidence measurement: total implant-abutment height measured (1) before finger pressure, (2) after finger pressure, (3) after 5× machine loading → Δheight = amount of abutment sinking.
- Locking force: implant and abutment pulled apart on the universal testing machine; **separation (subluxation) force** recorded.
- Analysis: correlation coefficients between each variable and the two outcomes.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Locking force (pull-apart) — increases with:**
- Loading force: **r=0.963** (strongest)
- Locking depth: **r=0.607**
- Locking-post diameter: **r=0.372**
- Implant outer-wall thickness: **no significant correlation**.

**Abutment subsidence (下沉量) —**
- Increases with loading force: **r=0.645**
- Decreases with locking depth: **r=−0.807** (strongest determinant)
- Decreases with locking-post diameter: **r=−0.280**
- Decreases with thicker implant outer wall (inverse relationship; no exact r reported).

**Net design reading:** deeper locking depth and larger post diameter simultaneously raise locking force *and* cut subsidence — favorable on both axes; raising seating load raises locking force (good) but also raises subsidence (must be managed clinically by repeated loading + delayed final occlusion).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Abstract-only ingest** — methods/numbers taken from the structured English abstract; the full Chinese body (exact subsidence in µm, statistics, figures) was not available via PMC.
- Pure in-vitro bench model; no saliva, cyclic mastication fatigue, or long-term in-vivo settling — extrapolation to clinical occlusal drift is indirect.
- Custom Bicon-mimicking specimens, not a marketed system; absolute values may not transfer to commercial taper-lock implants.
- Correlation coefficients reported without confidence intervals or full ANOVA in the abstract.
- No comparison to screwed (internal hex/conical screw-retained) connections within the same protocol.

## 6. Related Work

- Bicon / pure Morse taper (taper-lock) implant-abutment literature on cold welding and seating.
- Settling-effect studies on **screwed** connections (external/internal hex, octagon, conical) where subsidence drives preload loss / screw loosening — a distinct mechanism from taper-lock cold-weld subsidence.
- FEA of abutment stress distribution as a complementary biomechanical lens.

## 7. Glossary

- **Pure Morse taper / taper-lock connection**: implant-abutment joint held by friction (cold welding) between matching conical surfaces with no retention screw (e.g., Bicon). Distinct from screw-retained conical (Morse) connections.
- **Abutment subsidence (基台下沉 / sinking-down)**: progressive vertical seating of the abutment deeper into the implant under load; in taper-lock systems this continues after initial placement and can lower the occlusal contact over time.
- **Locking force / subluxation force (锁结力)**: the axial tensile force needed to pull the abutment out of the implant; the empirical measure of joint retention.
- **Locking depth (锁结深度)**: axial length of the conical engagement between abutment post and implant bore.
- **Locking post / column diameter (锁结柱直径)**: diameter of the conical engaging portion of the abutment.
