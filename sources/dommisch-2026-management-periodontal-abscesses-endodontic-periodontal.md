## Why Ingested

2018 분류체계(Caton/Herrera) 이후 급성 치주병소(치주농양·EPL)의 *관리 근거*를 처음으로 체계화한 EFP-계열 SR로, 진단 프레임([[periodontics/caton-2018-classification-scheme-periodontal-periimplant-diseases]])과 stage IV 치료 가이드라인([[periodontics/herrera-2022-treatment-stage-iv-periodontitis-efp]])을 임상 응급 관리 측면에서 보강·확장한다.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (no meta-analysis; 7 PICOS questions, ~21 included studies) of periodontal abscess and endodontic-periodontal lesion (EPL) management: combined drainage + periodontal therapy reduces PD 2–3 mm and BOP to 30–44% in abscesses (recurrence 13–23% at 15 mo), while EPL interventions yield PD reductions of 2.5–9.3 mm and tooth-loss 0–25% over 3–120 mo, on limited, heterogeneous, low-to-high-bias evidence.

## 한줄요약

치주농양과 치수-치주 복합병소(EPL) 관리에 대한 체계적 문헌고찰(메타분석 불가, 7개 PICOS, 약 21편): 농양은 배농 후 치주치료 병용으로 PD 2–3mm 감소·BOP 30–44%로 호전(15개월 재발 13–23%), EPL은 PD 2.5–9.3mm 감소·치아상실 0–25%(3–120개월)였으나 근거가 제한적·이질적이라 강한 결론은 불가.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Systematic review (PRISMA-conformant; meta-analysis not feasible due to heterogeneity)
- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Periodontology, 2026 (Open Access, CC-BY)
- **DOI**: 10.1111/jcpe.70135
- **Registration**: PROSPERO CRD420251078257 (registered 21 June 2025)
- **Affiliations**: Charité Berlin (DE); Queen Mary University of London / University of Parma; Complutense University of Madrid (ETEP group, D. Herrera); UCLouvain Brussels
- **Aim**: Evaluate the management of periodontal abscesses and endodontic-periodontal lesions (EPLs), framed around the 2018 Classification (Caton et al. 2018; Herrera et al. 2018).

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic synthesis of the *management* (not just diagnosis/classification) of acute periodontal lesions — periodontal abscesses and EPLs — since the 2018 World Workshop classification.
- Structures the clinical problem into 7 focused PICOS questions: 2 for periodontal abscesses (acute FQ1, post-acute FQ2) and 5 for EPLs (with root damage FQ3–4; without root damage FQ5–7, including a pulpal-inflammation/pain question FQ6).
- Defines "root damage" operationally: root perforation, external root resorption, cracks/fractures (severe root damage) — a key axis distinguishing EPL subgroups and prognosis.
- Provides quantitative outcome ranges for clinicians: PD, BOP, suppuration, ovoid elevation resolution, abscess recurrence, and tooth loss/survival.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases**: PubMed, Embase (via Ovid), plus a third electronic database and manual/hand searches for intervention studies.
- **Study designs included**: any intervention study (RCTs, prospective and retrospective cohorts/case series).
- **Outcomes**: pocket depth (PD), bleeding on probing (BOP), suppuration on probing (SOP), ovoid elevation, tooth loss/survival, abscess recurrence, pain reduction (for EPL pulpal inflammation).
- **Screening yield**: periodontal abscesses — 987 articles identified, 17 to full text; EPLs — 545 identified, 44 to full text.
- **Included studies**: periodontal abscesses — 3 RCTs + 2 prospective case series (5 total); EPLs — 9 RCTs, 6 prospective, 2 retrospective, 1 case series (18 total).
- Risk of bias ranged from low to high; meta-analysis not feasible due to heterogeneity of designs and small sample sizes.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Periodontal abscesses** (combined approaches: drainage + periodontal therapy):
  - PD reductions of 2–3 mm.
  - BOP reduced from 100% to 30%–44%.
  - Ovoid elevation reduced from 92%–100% to 0%–11%.
  - Abscess recurrence 13.3%–23% within 15 months follow-up.
- **EPLs** (endodontic and/or periodontal, non-surgical or surgical, alone or combined):
  - PD reductions ranged from 2.5 to 9.3 mm.
  - Tooth loss rate 0% to 25% over 3–120 months follow-up.
- Epidemiology cited: periodontal abscess is one of the most frequent dental emergencies; EPL prevalence ~4.9% at patient level / 0.4% at tooth level (Ruetters et al. 2022).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Meta-analysis impossible — heterogeneous study designs, small sample sizes, varied follow-up (3–120 months).
- Evidence graded as limited and weak for abscesses; for EPLs, conclusions are precluded by heterogeneity.
- Risk of bias spanned low to high across included studies.
- Optimal *sequencing* of endodontic vs periodontal therapy in EPLs remains unclear (Schmidt et al. 2014).
- Future need: standardized outcome sets and adequately powered RCTs for acute periodontal lesion management.

## 6. Related Work

- 2018 World Workshop Classification (Caton et al. 2018; Herrera et al. 2018) — defines periodontal abscess and EPL case definitions used here.

## 7. Glossary

- **Periodontal abscess**: acute localized infection with pus accumulation in the gingival wall of a periodontal pocket; primary signs are ovoid elevation along the root surface and BOP.
- **EPL (endodontic-periodontal lesion)**: pathological communication between pulp and periodontal tissues; primary signs are deep pockets reaching/near the apex with radiographic bone loss and negative/altered pulp vitality response.
- **Root damage**: root perforation, external root resorption, or cracks/fracture (severe root damage).
- **Ovoid elevation**: dome-shaped swelling of periodontal tissues, a primary case-definition sign of periodontal abscess.
- **BOP / PD / SOP**: bleeding on probing / probing depth / suppuration on probing — standard periodontal endpoints.
- **PICOS**: Population, Intervention, Comparison, Outcome, Study design — the focused-question framework.
