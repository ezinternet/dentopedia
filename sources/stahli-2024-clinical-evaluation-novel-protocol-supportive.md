## Why Ingested
유지치주치료(Supportive Periodontal Care, SPC)에서 subgingival debridement 방법(에어폴리싱 기반 GBM vs 전통 SRP) 직접 비교 RCT로, 1년 BoP·PPD·치근이개부·환자보고결과(PROM)에서 두 modality가 임상적으로 동등함을 보여 SPC 효과를 다룬 [[periodontics/manresa-2018-supportive-periodontal-therapy-cochrane-sr]]를 modality 선택 측면에서 보강·확장한다. 또한 recall interval을 다룬 [[periodontics/farooqi-2015-periodontal-maintenance-recall-interval-sr]]와 함께 SPC 프로토콜 의사결정의 근거로 활용 가능.

## One-line Summary
RCT (n=41 SPC patients, 1-year) comparing guided biofilm management (erythritol air-polishing + ultrasonic, no hand instruments) vs conventional scaling and root planing during supportive periodontal care: SRP gave a statistically significant BoP reduction (14.7%→7.9%, p=0.004) while GBM did not (12.2%→9.0%, p=0.191), but final BoP, PPD, recession, treatment time and overall satisfaction were comparable; GBM had fewer furcation-involved teeth (p=0.015) and SRP scored slightly better on pain (p=0.035).

## 한줄요약
RCT (유지치주치료 환자 41명, 1년): 가이드 바이오필름 관리(에리스리톨 에어폴리싱+초음파, 수기기구 미사용) vs 전통적 스케일링·치근활택술(SRP) 비교 — SRP만 BoP가 유의하게 감소(14.7%→7.9%, p=0.004)했고 GBM은 유의차 없음(12.2%→9.0%, p=0.191)이었으나 최종 BoP·PPD·치은퇴축·치료시간·전체 만족도는 비슷; GBM은 치근이개부 병변 치아 수가 더 적었고(p=0.015) SRP는 통증 점수가 약간 더 좋았다(p=0.035).

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Human randomized controlled clinical trial (two parallel groups), 1-year results of a planned 3-year study.
- **Journal**: Journal of Periodontology, 2024;95:1025–1034.
- **Setting**: Department of Periodontology, University of Bern, Switzerland.
- **Registration / ethics**: NCT05799261; ethical approval ID 2019-00046 (Canton of Bern); CONSORT-reported; Helsinki Declaration (1975, rev. 2013).
- **Funding**: Geistlich-Stucki-Stiftung; ADIC foundation; Clinical Research Foundation; EMS AG.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Head-to-head RCT of two subgingival debridement modalities during supportive periodontal care (SPC) in well-maintained periodontitis patients.
- Test = "guided biofilm management" (GBM): erythritol air-polishing (14 µm) with biofilm disclosure + slim ultrasonic tip for calculus, no hand curettes, no rubber-cup polishing. The authors deliberately use "management" instead of the commercial "guided biofilm therapy (GBT)" term.
- Control = conventional scaling and root planing (SRP): hand curettes + ultrasonic scalers + rubber-cup/paste polishing.
- Includes patient-reported outcome measures (PROMs: pain, satisfaction via 100 mm VAS) and treatment-time effectiveness, addressing a gap in recent literature on well-maintained SPC patients.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Patients**: 59 SPC patients enrolled (Jan–May 2022); periodontitis stage I–IV, grade A/B; residual PPD < 6 mm; active treatment completed ≥3 years prior. 41 analyzed at 1 year (18 dropouts; 5 due to PPD > 5 mm deterioration, 2 GBM / 3 SRP).
- **Randomization**: block randomization by an uninvolved biostatistician, sealed envelopes, balanced for smoking, sex, and BoP. Allocation: 21 GBM / 20 SRP.
- **Recall**: two visits per year (every 6 months).
- **Blinding**: outcome assessors (dentists) masked; operators and patients could not be blinded to modality.
- **Primary outcome**: change in % bleeding on probing (BoP) at 1 year. Secondary: PPD, recession, furcation involvement (classified ≤3 mm or >3 mm), PROMs (pain, satisfaction), treatment time.
- **Calibration**: inter/intra-operator calibration every 3 months; kappa improved from 0.79 to 0.94.
- **Sample size**: 11/group to detect 5% BoP difference (SD 2.5%, power 0.9, α 0.05); inflated to 20/group for dropouts.
- **Statistics**: Wilcoxon signed-rank (within-group), Mann–Whitney U (between-group), Pearson χ²/Fisher exact for categorical; α = 0.05 two-sided.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **BoP (primary)**: GBM 12.2%→9.0% (p=0.191, NS); SRP 14.7%→7.9% (p=0.04, significant). No significant between-group difference in change; final values low and similar.
- **PPD**: GBM 2.42→2.39 mm; SRP 2.44→2.43 mm; no within- or between-group significance.
- **Sites with PPD 4–5 mm + BoP**: no differences over time or between groups.
- **Recession**: comparable, no significant differences over time or between groups.
- **Furcation**: furcation-involved multirooted teeth (≤3 mm and >3 mm, not through-and-through) significantly fewer in GBM than SRP at 12 months — 3.43 (GBM) vs 6.65 (SRP), p=0.015; initial (≤3 mm) furcation reduction in GBM trended (p=0.055).
- **PROMs**: pain VAS 15 mm (GBM) vs 7.6 mm (SRP), favoring SRP (p=0.035); satisfaction 84.8 mm (GBM) vs 97.3 mm (SRP), NS (p=0.068).
- **Treatment time**: GBM 42.4 min vs SRP 39.6 min, NS (p=0.118).
- **Safety**: no unintended side effects/harms observed.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small sample (41 analyzed) with a high dropout (18/59), limiting power for secondary outcomes.
- Well-maintained, highly compliant cohort with low baseline BoP/PI (<20%) — floor effect limits generalizability to less-maintained populations.
- Only 1-year results of a planned 3-year trial; longer follow-up pending.
- Operators/patients not blinded to modality.
- The statistically significant SRP-only BoP reduction is plausibly driven by a slightly (NS) higher SRP baseline rather than true superiority.

## 6. Related Work
- Aligns with four recent systematic reviews (refs 30–33) and short-term RCTs (2003–2022) showing comparable BoP outcomes for air-polishing vs SRP over ≤6–12 months.
- Builds on erythritol subgingival air-polishing literature (refs 18–21) and EFP SPC/maintenance frameworks.

## 7. Glossary
- **SPC (Supportive Periodontal Care)**: maintenance phase after active periodontal therapy; regular professional biofilm removal to sustain periodontal stability.
- **GBM (Guided Biofilm Management)**: protocol of biofilm disclosure + erythritol air-polishing + ultrasonic calculus removal, no hand instruments (author term for commercial "GBT").
- **SRP (Scaling and Root Planing)**: mechanical debridement with hand curettes + ultrasonic scalers + polishing.
- **BoP (Bleeding on Probing)**: % of bleeding sites; primary inflammation/stability marker.
- **PPD (Probing Pocket Depth)**: distance from gingival margin to pocket base.
- **Erythritol air-polishing**: sugar-alcohol abrasive powder (14 µm) for gentle subgingival biofilm removal.
- **PROM**: patient-reported outcome measure (here pain and satisfaction via 100 mm VAS).
