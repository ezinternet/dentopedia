## One-line Summary
In vitro acrylic-cube model comparing Osstell (RFA/ISQ) and Periotest (PTV) for sensitivity and reliability across simulated hard/soft interfaces, horizontal bone-loss levels, and a hardening interface; Osstell was more reliable overall and the only system that tracked interface stiffening over time.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Soft (polyvinyl siloxane) interface dropped ISQ to 53.95 ┬▒ 0.24, just below the 55-ISQ failure-risk threshold reported by Sennerby, and was statistically distinct from hard interface (P < 0.001).
- Both systems detected the four levels of horizontally exposed fixture (0 / 2 / 4 / 6 mm) with strong correlations: PTV r = +0.967 (positive ŌĆö higher exposure, more mobility), ISQ r = ŌłÆ0.946 (negative ŌĆö higher exposure, lower stiffness).

- Osstell uniquely tracked progressive stiffening of the autopolymerizing resin interface over time (r = 0.986 between ISQ and stiffness change); Periotest was less sensitive to this stiffness modulation.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Forty-nine implant fixtures were embedded centrally in acrylic cubes across three experimental parts. Part I (n = 14): seven fixtures in direct acrylic contact ("hard" interface) and seven in polyvinyl siloxane impression material ("soft" interface ŌĆö simulating fibrous encapsulation). Part II (n = 28): four sets of seven fixtures each with 0, 2, 4, or 6 mm of fixture body horizontally exposed above the cube surface, simulating progressive crestal bone loss. Part III (n = 7): fixtures placed in contact with a thin mix of autopolymerizing acrylic resin that progressively hardened over 28 minutes, simulating an interface that stiffens with time (analogue of bone maturation/secondary stability).

Each fixture was measured with both Osstell (resonance frequency analysis via Smartpeg transducer, ISQ scale 1ŌĆō100) and Periotest (electronically dampened percussion, PTV scale roughly ŌłÆ8 to +50). Means and standard deviations were calculated within groups. Correlations between exposure level and each output, and between resin hardening time and each output, were computed with Pearson r. Comparisons used analysis of variance / parametric testing with P < 0.001 as the significance threshold for the principal between-group comparisons.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Hard vs soft interface**: ISQ 70.0 ┬▒ 0.0 (hard) vs 53.95 ┬▒ 0.24 (soft); PTV ŌłÆ3.52 ┬▒ 0.39 (hard) vs significantly higher (soft); both differences P < 0.001.
- **Progressive horizontal exposure (0 ŌåÆ 6 mm)**:
  - ISQ: 70.43 ŌåÆ 64.14 ŌåÆ 57.47 ŌåÆ 52.14 (monotonic decline, r = ŌłÆ0.946)
  - PTV: ŌłÆ3.52 ŌåÆ ŌłÆ1.14 ŌåÆ +1.28 ŌåÆ +4.33 (monotonic rise, r = +0.967)
- **Hardening interface (Part III, 1 ŌåÆ 28 min)**: ISQ rose monotonically from ~35ŌĆō40 to ~75 with r = 0.986 between ISQ and stiffness; Periotest reportedly less sensitive.
- **Reliability (SD by exposure level, Osstell vs Periotest, mm 0/2/4/6)**:
  - Osstell SD: 0.0, 0.24, 0.40, 0.32 (slight loss of reliability as stability decreased)
  - Periotest SD: 0.39, 0.0, 0.0, 0.14 (gained reliability as stability decreased)
- Both systems sensitive; Osstell more reliable overall and the only system to track resin stiffening over time.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- In vitro acrylic-cube model is a crude analogue of trabecular bone; polyvinyl siloxane and autopolymerizing resin are not biologically representative interfaces.
- Single fixture geometry tested (one implant brand/design); results may not generalise to wider/narrower or differently threaded designs.
- Small group sizes (n = 7 per condition) ŌĆö adequate for the very low within-group variance observed but underpowered for subtler effects.
- Periotest reliability paradox (improving SD with worsening stability) is hardware-dependent and may reflect saturation of the dampening sensor rather than a genuine biological property.
- No in vivo correlation ŌĆö clinicians should not extrapolate the ISQ-stiffness curve linearly to bone maturation.

## 7. Glossary
- **RFA (Resonance Frequency Analysis)**: Measurement of an implant's lateral stiffness via a Smartpeg transducer excited by magnetic pulses; output as ISQ.
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: 1ŌĆō100 scale derived from RFA; higher = stiffer interface. Clinical danger zone ISQ < 55.
- **PTV (Periotest Value)**: Electronic dampening percussion output; range roughly ŌłÆ8 to +50; lower (more negative) = stiffer interface. Inversely related to ISQ.
- **Smartpeg**: Magnetic transducer screwed into the implant for RFA measurement.
- **Polyvinyl siloxane (PVS)**: Elastomeric impression material used here as a soft-tissue interface analogue.
