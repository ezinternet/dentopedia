## Why Ingested

The Dahl concept (relative axial tooth movement to create restorative space for localized tooth wear) is a long-standing occlusal-management technique that, until now, lacked a synthesized evidence base. This is the **first SR+MA** on fixed Dahl restorations, quantifying failure/complication rates and — critically for occlusion — the proportion of patients who re-establish posterior occlusal contacts. It extends the wiki's occlusal-contact / occlusal-scheme material (see [[occlusion/velasquez-2022-occlusal-analysis-natural-dentition-sr]] on natural occlusal-contact distribution) and applies an occlusal-loading rationale that complements [[occlusion/fan-2018-occlusal-trauma-and-excessive-occlusal]] (controlled occlusal forces vs. traumatic overload). Ingested now to give the occlusion category a high-evidence anchor on managing localized anterior tooth wear.

## One-line Summary

First SR+MA (11 studies; 8 low / 3 moderate RoB) on fixed Dahl restorations for localized tooth wear: indirect restorations had much lower annual failure rates (0–0.7%) than direct (0–8.7%, P<.05), and complete posterior occlusal re-establishment occurred in a pooled 86.9% of patients (low–moderate GRADE certainty).

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (with meta-regression and Poisson regression)
- **Journal**: The Journal of Prosthetic Dentistry, 2026; 135(6):e21–e33
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.prosdent.2025.12.028
- **PMID**: 41500883
- **Source basis**: PubMed abstract only — full text not retrieved (no PMC record). Quantitative detail below is limited to what the abstract reports.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA evaluating fixed Dahl restorations as a treatment for localized tooth wear, filling an acknowledged evidence gap.
- Direct vs. indirect material comparison via Poisson regression: indirect annual failure rates significantly lower than direct.
- Meta-analysis + meta-regression of **occlusal re-establishment** (the defining mechanism of the Dahl concept — relative axial movement allowing previously separated posterior teeth to re-contact), with age tested as a moderator.
- GRADE certainty rating applied to pooled outcomes.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases**: PubMed, Embase, Cochrane CENTRAL, Web of Science, Scopus, and ClinicalTrials.gov, searched up to June 2025.
- **Eligible designs**: RCTs, cohort studies, and case series.
- **Risk of bias**: revised Cochrane RoB tool (RCTs), Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (cohorts), Joanna Briggs Institute critical appraisal (case series).
- **Certainty**: GRADE.
- **Statistics**: Poisson regression comparing direct vs. indirect material clinical results (α=.05); meta-analysis and meta-regression for occlusal re-establishment.
- **Included studies**: 11 total — 8 low risk, 3 moderate risk of bias.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Direct materials**: annual failure rate (AFR) 0%–8.7%; annual complication rate (ACR) 5.2%–30.2%; follow-up 1.3–7 years.
- **Indirect materials**: AFR 0–0.7%; ACR 5.6%–7.2%; follow-up 1.7–2.2 years.
- Indirect AFR significantly lower than direct (P<.05).
- Most failures/complications were restoration fracture, wear, and loss.
- **Occlusal re-establishment** (reported 1–25.4 months): pooled proportions — no occlusal contact 0.6%, partial 10.0%, complete 86.9%; not significantly influenced by patient age.
- **GRADE**: low to moderate certainty for pooled proportions.
- **Bottom line**: fixed Dahl restorations are a suitable option for localized tooth wear; high patient satisfaction, but wide therapy-time range and a high propensity for failures/complications.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Low-to-moderate GRADE certainty; included designs span RCTs to case series (heterogeneous evidence base).
- Indirect-material follow-up much shorter (1.7–2.2 y) than direct (up to 7 y), so the direct-vs-indirect AFR contrast is confounded by exposure time — caution in concluding indirect superiority over the long term.
- Wide range of "time of therapy" and high complication propensity flagged by the authors.
- This summary is built from the abstract only; effect sizes, CIs, and per-study data were not retrievable.

## 7. Glossary

- **Dahl concept**: A space-gaining technique for localized (usually anterior) tooth wear in which a restoration or appliance is placed in supra-occlusion on the worn teeth; over weeks–months, relative axial movement (intrusion of the contacting teeth + over-eruption of the separated posterior teeth) re-establishes full posterior occlusal contact without removing sound tissue elsewhere.
- **Occlusal re-establishment**: Return of occlusal contacts on the posterior (initially separated) teeth after Dahl-induced relative axial movement; graded here as none / partial / complete.
- **AFR / ACR**: Annual failure rate / annual complication rate — incidence of restoration failures or complications normalized per year of follow-up.
- **GRADE**: Grading of Recommendations, Assessment, Development, and Evaluation — system for rating certainty of evidence.
- **Poisson regression**: Regression model for count/rate outcomes (here, failure/complication rates) used to compare direct vs. indirect materials.
