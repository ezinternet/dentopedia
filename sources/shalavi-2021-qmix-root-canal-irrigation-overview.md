## One-line Summary
Narrative overview of QMix root canal irrigant: CHX + EDTA + detergent combination used as single-step final rinse; simultaneous smear layer removal + antibacterial without separate chelating/disinfecting steps.

## 2. Key Contributions
- QMix composition: **2% CHX + EDTA + Brij-35 (surfactant/detergent)**; manufactured by Dentsply Sirona
- Designed as single final-rinse irrigant replacing the separate EDTA → CHX two-step protocol
- Documented properties:
  - Smear layer removal (EDTA component; equivalent to 17% EDTA)
  - Antibacterial action (CHX component; effective against E. faecalis and polymicrobial flora)
  - Biofilm disruption (Brij-35 surfactant lowers surface tension)
- Does not produce the NaOCl–CHX precipitate (orange/brown precipite = para-chloroaniline) if separated by water rinse
- Lower cytotoxicity than high-concentration NaOCl in most in vitro models
- Should NOT be mixed directly with NaOCl in the canal (CHX–NaOCl precipitate risk)

## 4. Key Results Summary
| Property | QMix Performance |
|---|---|
| Smear layer removal | Equivalent to EDTA 17% |
| Antibacterial (E. faecalis) | Superior to EDTA, NaOCl (short contact), CHX alone |
| Cytotoxicity | Lower than 5.25% NaOCl; comparable to 1–2.5% NaOCl |
| Biofilm disruption | Brij-35 aids penetration into tubules |
| Dentin erosion | Less than 17% EDTA at standard contact time |

### Recommended clinical protocol
1. Shaping with NaOCl as primary irrigant throughout
2. Final rinse: 2 mL distilled water flush (removes NaOCl residue)
3. Final rinse: 2 mL QMix for 1 minute (contact time)
4. Final distilled water flush
5. Dry and obturate

## 7. Glossary
- **QMix**: Final-rinse irrigant combining 2% CHX + EDTA + Brij-35 detergent; marketed as simultaneous chelation + disinfection
- **Brij-35**: Non-ionic surfactant; reduces surface tension of irrigant to improve dentinal tubule penetration
- **Para-chloroaniline (PCA)**: Toxic precipitate formed when CHX and NaOCl mix directly; orange/brown discoloration; QMix requires water rinse between NaOCl and QMix application
- **EDTA component**: Chelates Ca²⁺ from hydroxyapatite → removes inorganic smear layer component
