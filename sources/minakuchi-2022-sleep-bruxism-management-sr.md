## One-line Summary
Systematic review from Okayama University on sleep bruxism (수면이갈이) management in adults; occlusal splints and biofeedback devices show most evidence; pharmacological and BTX options supported by limited data.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Japanese Dental Science Review 2022
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.jdsr.2021.12.001
- **Affiliation**: Okayama University
- **Study type**: Systematic review

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive synthesis of management strategies for sleep bruxism (수면이갈이) in adults
- Covers: occlusal splints, biofeedback, pharmacotherapy, BTX, cognitive behavioral therapy
- Evaluates outcomes: bruxism event frequency (PSG-based), muscle activity (EMG), tooth wear, TMD symptoms
- Integrates ICSD-3 and AASM diagnostic criteria for sleep bruxism
- Provides evidence hierarchy for clinical decision-making

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Search: PubMed, Embase, Cochrane
- Inclusion: RCTs and clinical trials on sleep bruxism management in adults; polysomnography (PSG) or EMG-validated bruxism diagnosis preferred
- Primary outcomes: bruxism episode frequency, masseter/temporalis EMG amplitude
- Secondary: pain, tooth wear, patient-reported outcomes, sleep quality
- Narrative synthesis with evidence grading

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Occlusal splints** (교합안정장치): Moderate evidence for muscle activity reduction; no evidence for reducing bruxism events (do not cure bruxism, only protect teeth)
- **Biofeedback devices**: Most evidence for reducing bruxism episodes in the short term; compliance challenge
- **Pharmacotherapy**: Clonazepam, clonidine — some evidence but limited by side effects; not first-line
- **BTX injection** (보툴리눔독소): Effective for masseter muscle volume and pain reduction; equivocal for bruxism event frequency
- **CBT and relaxation**: Low evidence but favorable safety profile
- No treatment shown to permanently eliminate sleep bruxism
- Splints are currently the most widely used intervention despite limited evidence of bruxism cessation

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Heterogeneous diagnostic criteria (many studies used EMG without PSG confirmation)
- Short follow-up in most RCTs (≤3 months)
- Lack of standardized outcome measures for bruxism severity
- Need for PSG-validated long-term RCTs

## 7. Glossary
- **Sleep bruxism** (수면이갈이): Sleep-related movement disorder characterized by rhythmic masticatory muscle activity and teeth grinding/clenching during sleep
- **PSG (Polysomnography)**: Gold-standard sleep monitoring; used for definitive sleep bruxism diagnosis
- **RMMA** (Rhythmic Masticatory Muscle Activity): Characteristic EMG pattern defining sleep bruxism episodes
- **EMG** (근전도): Electromyography; measures muscle electrical activity; used as surrogate for bruxism monitoring
- **Biofeedback device**: Sensor-based device that provides auditory/vibration feedback to interrupt bruxism events during sleep
- **Clonazepam**: Benzodiazepine with evidence for short-term sleep bruxism reduction; limited by dependency risk
