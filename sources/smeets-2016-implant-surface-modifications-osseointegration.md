## One-line Summary
Comprehensive narrative review of dental implant surface modifications (SLA, acid-etching, hydrophilic SLActive, DCD, laser, protein coatings, growth factors) and their effects on osseointegration in healthy and compromised bone.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: BioMed Research International 2016; Article ID 6285620
- **Study type**: Narrative review

## 2. Key Contributions
- Systematic classification of established vs experimental surface modifications
- Reviews SLA and SLActive as clinical gold standards
- Presents emerging technologies: Discrete Crystalline Deposition (DCD), laser ablation, protein/drug/growth factor coatings
- Addresses surface needs for compromised bone (diabetes, osteoporosis, bisphosphonates, radiotherapy)

## 3. Surface Types Reviewed
1. **Sandblasting + acid-etching (SLA)**: micro-roughness 1–2 µm; gold standard
2. **SLActive (hydrophilic SLA)**: chemically active with nitrogen; faster early BIC
3. **DCD (Discrete Crystalline Deposition)**: bioresorbable calcium phosphate particles
4. **Laser ablation**: micro/nanotopography control
5. **Protein/peptide coatings**: fibronectin, RGD sequences
6. **Growth factor coatings**: BMP-2, rhBMP-7, PDGF
