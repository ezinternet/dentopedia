## One-line Summary
Umbrella review of 18 high-quality SRs (Karolinska/Egas Moniz) showing BoNT-A is superior to placebo but not to standard treatments for myogenous TMD (M-TMD) pain, with notable adverse events on muscle and bone — recommended only as a last-line option.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Drugs 2024, 84(7):779-809
- **DOI**: 10.1007/s40265-024-02048-x
- **Lead institution**: Karolinska Institutet (Division of Oral Rehabilitation)
- **Registration**: PROSPERO CRD42023468160
- **Methodology**: Aromataris umbrella-review methodology; only high-quality SRs included
- **Study type**: Umbrella review of systematic reviews

## 2. Key Contributions
- First umbrella review explicitly restricted to high-quality SRs of BoNT-A for M-TMD (myogenous TMD), summarizing the entire SR body since database inception through Dec 2023.
- Disentangles "BoNT-A vs. placebo" (positive) from "BoNT-A vs. standard treatment" (no superiority) — a distinction often blurred in primary RCTs.
- Catalogs adverse events on muscle (atrophy, fatty infiltration, weakness) and bony tissue (mandibular bone-volume loss, condylar/alveolar changes).
- Provides explicit clinical recommendation: BoNT-A should be a last-line option for M-TMD given its serious AE profile.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Databases: MEDLINE, EMBASE, CINAHL, Cochrane CENTRAL, Web of Science, Epistemonikos, ClinicalTrials.gov, ICTRP (inception → 6 Dec 2023).
- PICOTS: P=adults with mastication myalgia (M-TMD); I=intramuscular BoNT-A any dose; C=no Tx / placebo / non-invasive / minimally invasive / invasive comparators; O=pain (VAS/NRS), MMO, adverse events; T=short (≤3 mo), intermediate (3–5 mo), long (≥6 mo); S=SRs only.
- Quality appraisal: umbrella-review-methodology working-group critical appraisal checklist; only high-quality SRs retained.
- Languages: English, Spanish, Portuguese, Greek, Scandinavian.
- Excluded: editorials, narrative reviews, single RCTs, animal/in-vitro, observational, case-series.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **18 high-quality SRs included** out of larger initial pool.
- BoNT-A vs. placebo: significantly reduces pain intensity.
- BoNT-A vs. standard treatments (splints, physiotherapy, pharmacotherapy, dry needling): no superiority for pain.
- BoNT-A vs. placebo or standard: no superiority for mandibular range of motion (MMO).
- Adverse events: higher risk for muscle adverse events (atrophy, fatty replacement, weakness, bite-force reduction) and bony tissue changes (mandibular bone volume loss, alveolar and condylar bone changes) compared with controls.
- Overall evidence quality: moderate for efficacy; clinically meaningful safety signal.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Heterogeneity across underlying SRs: BoNT-A brand, dose, dilution, injection sites, follow-up varied widely.
- Few SRs evaluated long-term (≥6-month) outcomes systematically.
- No standardized M-TMD pain or AE outcome set across SRs.
- Need for high-quality head-to-head RCTs of BoNT-A vs. established M-TMD therapies with long follow-up.

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary
- **BoNT-A** (보툴리눔독소 A형, Botulinum Toxin Type A): Neurotoxin from *Clostridium botulinum*; presynaptic acetylcholine blocker producing flaccid muscle paralysis; also has antinociceptive effects (CGRP, substance P, glutamate suppression).
- **M-TMD** (저작근성 측두하악장애, Myogenous TMD): TMD subtype with masticatory-muscle origin pain; ~45% of TMD diagnoses.
- **Umbrella review**: SR of systematic reviews; highest tier of evidence synthesis.
- **MMO** (최대개구량, Maximum Mouth Opening): Inter-incisal distance at maximal voluntary jaw opening.
- **AE** (이상사례, Adverse Event): Any untoward medical occurrence in a treatment recipient.
- **AMSTAR-2 / Aromataris checklist**: Methodological-quality appraisal tools for SRs.
