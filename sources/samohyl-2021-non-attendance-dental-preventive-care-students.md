## Why Ingested

Extends [[practice-management/alkhurayji-2024-factors-patient-failure-attend-dental]] from a clinic no-show framing to a population-level *avoidance* framing: identifies the family/SES and parental-behavior predictors that determine whether adolescents and young adults attend preventive care at all. The standout finding — that a parent's own dental-care avoidance independently predicts the child's avoidance (AOR ~3.4-4.2) — gives clinics a concrete recall-targeting lever (engage the parent, not just the patient).

## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional school-based survey (Slovakia, n=515 students aged 15-22 + 681 parents): male gender, older age, incomplete family, low household income (<EUR 900), home stress, and — most strongly — a parent's own avoidance of preventive care independently predicted non-attendance at dental preventive care; not visiting pediatric care raised the odds of skipping dental care (AOR 5.14, 95% CI 2.40-10.99).

## 한줄요약

슬로바키아 고등학생 대상 단면 설문(학생 515명, 부모 681명): 남성, 고연령, 결손가정, 저소득(<900유로), 가정 스트레스, 그리고 무엇보다 부모 자신의 예방진료 기피가 청소년의 치과 예방진료 비참여를 독립적으로 예측했다(소아과 진료 미수진 시 AOR 5.14, 95% CI 2.40-10.99).

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Factors Associated with Non-Attendance at Dental Preventive Care in Slovak High School Students
- **Authors**: Samohyl M, Babjakova J, Vondrova D, Jurkovicova J, Stofko J, Kollar B, Hirosova K, Filova A, Argalasova L
- **Journal**: International Journal of Environmental Research and Public Health, 2021;18(3):1295
- **DOI**: 10.3390/ijerph18031295
- **PMID**: 33535551 ; **PMCID**: PMC7908624
- **Design**: Cross-sectional, school-based survey (The Youth and Parents Risk Factor Behavior Survey / YABS), Bratislava, Slovakia
- **Sample**: 515 students (15-22 y, 37.9% male) + parents (390 mothers, 291 fathers); study group = 57 non-attenders, control = 458 attenders

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies, in a parent-linked adolescent sample, that **parental avoidance of preventive care is an independent predictor** of the child's dental non-attendance — mother AOR 4.19 (95% CI 1.50-11.71), father AOR 3.41 (95% CI 1.24-9.32).
- Shows skipping **pediatric** preventive care co-travels with skipping **dental** care (AOR 5.14; 95% CI 2.40-10.99) — non-attendance is a general health-behavior pattern, not tooth-specific.
- Frames non-attendance as a family/SES phenomenon: incomplete (divorced/single) family, low household income (<EUR 900), and home stress cluster in avoiders.
- Policy implication: extend free twice-yearly preventive check-ups to adolescents/young adults over 18, who lose the under-18 insurance entitlement and show the highest treatment need (69% in the 19+ group).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Two standardized questionnaires (student + parent), derived from CDC's BRFSS and YRBSS instruments; Slovak version pilot-validated on 20 respondents.
- 2384 questionnaires distributed (798 students, 1586 parents); response rates 64.0% / 66.1%.
- Outcome: self-reported non-attendance at dental preventive care in the previous year (study group n=57 vs control n=458).
- Analysis: t-test (age), chi-square / Fisher exact (categorical), and multiple logistic regression mutually adjusted for age, gender, family completeness, and parental dental preventive care, reporting AOR with 95% CI; significance p < 0.05; missing data treated as random and excluded.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Sex**: males avoided more than females (54.4% vs 45.6%, p < 0.01); male gender significant in multivariate model.
- **Age**: older students (18-19 y: 21.8%; 19+ y: 20.0%) avoided more in bivariate analysis; not significant after adjustment.
- **Family/SES (bivariate)**: incomplete family 56.1%; home stress 17.5%; divorced mothers and maternal household income < EUR 900 associated with higher avoidance.
- **Cross-care pattern**: >34.5% attended neither dental nor pediatric preventive care; AOR for the joint pattern 5.14 (95% CI 2.40-10.99).
- **Parental behavior (multivariate, independent predictors)**: maternal non-attendance AOR 4.19 (1.50-11.71, p < 0.01); single/divorced/widowed father AOR 3.73 (1.21-11.54, p < 0.05); paternal non-attendance AOR 3.41 (1.24-9.32, p < 0.05).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Cross-sectional, hypothesis-generating design — no causal inference.
- Small study (non-attender) group, n=57.
- Sample representative only of high school students in Bratislava; out-of-school adolescents excluded.
- Self-reported questionnaire data; no objective dental status/experience.
- Missing data from non-response and incomplete families (judged random).

## 6. Related Work

- Builds on the BRFSS/YRBSS surveillance tradition and the Slovak HBSC oral-health-related-behaviours findings (female gender, lower age, higher family affluence, higher parental education/employment associated with attendance).
- Echoes American (male gender, lack of insurance) and Polish (female gender, large-city residence) studies on irregular dental visits.

## 7. Glossary

- **Preventive (dental) check-up**: scheduled examination intended to detect disease early; in Slovakia free twice-yearly for under-18s.
- **AOR (adjusted odds ratio)**: odds ratio from multivariate logistic regression mutually adjusted for covariates.
- **YABS / Youth and Parents Risk Factor Behavior Survey**: ongoing Slovak cross-sectional school-based survey of students and their parents, derived from CDC BRFSS/YRBSS.
- **Incomplete family**: divorced, single-parent, or otherwise non-intact family structure.
