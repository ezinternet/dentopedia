## Why Ingested

Adolescent orthodontic adherence (oral hygiene, appointment attendance, instruction recall) is the behavioral substrate that behaviour-change counseling such as motivational interviewing aims to move. This SR maps which intervention *types* actually shift adherence in 12–18 y patients, complementing the early-childhood MI evidence in [[behavioral-dentistry/motivational-interviewing/jahanshahi-2022-motivational-interviewing-prevent-early-childhood]] and the behaviour-change-mechanism framing of MI in general practice ([[behavioral-dentistry/motivational-interviewing/kay-2016-motivational-interviewing-general-dental-practice]]). ## One-line Summary

Systematic review (3 databases, 1966–Mar 2012) of RCTs on adherence-improving interventions in orthodontic patients aged 12–18; 381 articles screened → 4 RCTs, each testing a different method (rewards/awards, Hawthorne effect, written information, plaque-microbiology demonstration); all except the reward/award intervention improved adherence, but evidence is insufficient to identify a single best method.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic review focused specifically on interventions to improve **adherence/compliance in adolescent orthodontic patients**, an age group where non-adherence (poor oral hygiene, missed appointments) commonly compromises treatment.
- Defined adherence broadly across four outcome domains: recall of information given by the orthodontic team, attendance at appointments, self-reported oral hygiene behavior, and clinical oral-hygiene indexes.
- Catalogued the intervention types tested in RCTs and their direction of effect, exposing how thin the high-quality evidence base actually is (only 4 RCTs).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Electronic searches of three databases plus a hand search.
- All review authors screened titles/abstracts; 381 articles identified, 21 met inclusion at screening, full texts retrieved.
- Final inclusion: **4 RCTs**, each testing a distinct intervention method:
  1. A system of **rewards or awards**
  2. The **Hawthorne effect** (behavior change from being observed/attended to)
  3. **Written information**
  4. **Demonstration of the microbiology of plaque**
- Heterogeneity of interventions and outcomes precluded meta-analysis; synthesis is narrative.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **3 of 4 interventions improved adherence**: the Hawthorne effect, written information, and plaque-microbiology demonstration were each associated with improved adherence.
- The **reward/award** intervention was the exception — *not* associated with improved adherence.
- No single method could be recommended over the others on the available evidence.
- Implied take-home: the common thread among the effective interventions is **clinician time and attention spent illustrating why adherence matters**, rather than extrinsic incentives.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 4 RCTs, each a single intervention type — no replication, no head-to-head, no meta-analysis possible.
- Outcome heterogeneity (self-report vs clinical index vs attendance) limits comparability.
- Search ends March 2012; later trials not captured.
- Authors call for future studies to (a) use **multiple adherence-assessment methods** (self-report, behavioral observation/recording, change in clinical indexes) in combination, and (b) test **different intervention types** for comparative effectiveness.

## 7. Glossary

- **Adherence / compliance**: the extent to which a patient follows agreed recommendations (here: oral hygiene, attendance, following instructions).
- **Hawthorne effect**: change in behavior arising from awareness of being observed or attended to, rather than from the specific intervention content.
- **Clinical oral-hygiene index**: objective scored measure of plaque/gingival status (e.g., plaque index) used as a behavioral proxy.
