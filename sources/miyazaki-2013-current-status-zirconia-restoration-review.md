## One-line Summary
J Prosthodont Res 2013: narrative review — Y-TZP/Ce-TZP properties, CAD/CAM workflow, zirconia-veneer bond (chipping main complication), zirconia-resin bond (MDP primer required), surface finish + enamel wear, clinical outcomes.

## 1. Key Results
- Y-TZP: best mechanical properties among dental ceramics; Ce-TZP/Al2O3: highest fracture toughness
- Main clinical complication: veneering porcelain chipping (not addressed by chemical bond)
- Zirconia-resin bond: MDP primer is key; sandblasting increases roughness but aging degrades bond
- Surface finish: polished > glazed for antagonist enamel wear
- Marginal adaptation: clinically acceptable for FDPs
- Monolithic zirconia introduced as solution to chipping problem
