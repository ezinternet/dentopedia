## One-line Summary
Case report (26F, #16, RBH 3.5 mm): endoscopically-controlled flapless transcrestal sinus elevation + PRF + simultaneous 12 mm implant. ISQ 72.6 at surgery; crown delivered at 12 weeks; demonstrates extending transcrestal indication to RBH <4 mm.

## 1. Document Information
Single case report in Medicine (Baltimore) plus brief literature review. Patient: 26-year-old female, healthy, missing #16, RBH 3.5 mm.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Extends transcrestal indication to **RBH <4 mm** (traditionally lateral-only territory)
- Endoscope provides direct visualization → reduces undetected membrane perforation
- PRF as sole grafting material (no xenograft)
- Simultaneous implant placement with high primary stability (ISQ 72.6)
- Loading at 12 weeks demonstrates feasibility of accelerated protocol

## 3. Methodology
- Endoscopic flapless transcrestal approach
- Two-step membrane elevation
- PRF clots condensed into elevated space
- 12 mm implant placed simultaneously
- ISQ measured in 5 directions (occlusal, buccal, lingual, mesial, distal) — **mean ISQ = 72.6** at surgery
- Follow-up ISQ at 8 and 12 weeks
- Full ceramic crown delivered at 12 weeks
- CBCT at 6 months post-loading

## 4. Key Results
- High primary stability despite RBH 3.5 mm (favorable for endoscope-controlled technique + PRF compaction effect + implant macro-design)
- No membrane perforation (endoscope verification)
- 6-month CBCT: satisfactory bone formation around implant

## 5. Limitations
- **n=1 case report**
- Endoscope equipment requirement limits widespread adoption
- Short follow-up
- No control group

## 7. Glossary
- **TSFE**: Transcrestal sinus floor elevation
- **PRF**: Platelet-rich fibrin
- **RBH**: Residual bone height
- **ISQ**: Implant Stability Quotient
- **CBCT**: Cone-beam computed tomography
