## One-line Summary

In vitro study (n = teeth with 3-week E. faecalis biofilm) demonstrating complete biofilm eradication within 12 minutes of AC argon/oxygen cold plasma treatment with no significant dentin microhardness or roughness change.

## 한줄요약

In vitro 연구: AC 아르곤/산소 냉각 플라즈마 12분 처치로 3주 성숙 E. faecalis 바이오필름 완전 사멸, 상아질 미세경도·표면 조도 유의한 변화 없음.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First demonstration that **AC argon/oxygen (Ar/O₂) atmospheric pressure cold plasma** completely eradicates a fully matured (3-week) E. faecalis biofilm in extracted root canals within **12 minutes**.
2. Shows superior antimicrobial durability compared to Ca(OH)₂, 2% chlorhexidine gel, and Ca(OH)₂/chlorhexidine combination (all tested for 1 week).
3. Establishes **mechanical safety**: no significant change in Vickers microhardness or 3D surface roughness of root canal dentin after plasma treatment — critical for clinical translation.
4. Identifies **reactive oxygen species (ROS)** as the primary bactericidal mechanism.
5. SEM and CLSM dual-confirmation of complete biofilm destruction at 12 min endpoint.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

### Biofilm Model
- 3-week-old E. faecalis biofilm grown inside extracted human teeth (single root canals)
- Model mimics chronic, mature clinical infection — harder to disinfect than planktonic or young biofilm

### Plasma Device
- AC (alternating current) Ar/O₂ cold plasma jet
- Atmospheric pressure (no vacuum chamber needed)
- Low temperature — safe for tissue contact

### Treatment Groups
| Group | Agent | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Cold plasma | AC Ar/O₂ plasma jet | 3, 6, 9, 12 min |
| Control 1 | Ca(OH)₂ | 1 week |
| Control 2 | 2% CHX gel | 1 week |
| Control 3 | Ca(OH)₂ + CHX | 1 week |
| Negative control | Saline | — |

### Assessment Methods
- **CFU count**: Colony-forming units after serial dilution plating → quantitative viability
- **SEM**: Morphologic changes of biofilm after plasma
- **CLSM (confocal laser scanning microscopy)**: Live/dead staining to confirm viability
- **Vickers Hardness Tester**: Microhardness before and after plasma
- **3D Profile Measurement Laser Microscope**: Surface roughness (Ra) before and after plasma

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Endpoint | Finding |
|---|---|
| Complete sterilization | 12 min cold plasma → 0 detectable CFU |
| Partial reduction | 3–9 min → progressive log reduction |
| Ca(OH)₂ (1 week) | Significant residual viable bacteria |
| 2% CHX (1 week) | Better than Ca(OH)₂ but incomplete |
| Ca(OH)₂ + CHX (1 week) | Incomplete; less effective than 12-min plasma |
| Microhardness change | Not significant (p > 0.05) |
| Surface roughness change | Not significant (p > 0.05) |
| SEM/CLSM at 12 min | Confirmed complete biofilm disruption |

**Key mechanistic finding**: Plasma generates ROS (•OH, O₃, H₂O₂, O•, etc.) that oxidize bacterial cell membranes and disrupt DNA — mechanism distinct from ionic/pH-based mechanisms of Ca(OH)₂ and CHX.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In vitro only — no in vivo or ex vivo clinical validation
- Plasma jet geometry may not penetrate tortuous or lateral canals effectively
- Treatment time of 12 min may be clinically impractical without fiber optic/flexible plasma delivery
- Does not test smear layer removal or penetration into dentinal tubules specifically
- ROS quantification not directly measured inside canal (inferred from prior plasma physics literature)
- Single bacterial species (E. faecalis) — polymicrobial biofilm not tested

## 6. Related Work

- Du et al. 2012 (J Endod): earlier nonequilibrium plasma against E. faecalis biofilm in vitro — this study extends with AC Ar/O₂ and adds safety data
- Pan et al. 2013 (J Endod): cold plasma therapy of tooth root canal E. faecalis — predecessor from same research group
- Wang et al. 2011 (Plasma Med): DC nonthermal plasma microjet for root canal infection — same group, DC variant
- Jiang et al. 2012 (ISRN Dent): cold plasma jet against E. faecalis biofilms in vitro — parallel work

| Term | Definition |
