## One-line Summary
SR+MA (29 studies, Iranian population): MB2 prevalence 60% in maxillary first molars, 33% in second molars — detection rates highest with microscope (78%/61%), lowest with periapical radiograph (15%/4%); I²=97% in all subgroups.

- **Journal**: PLOS One 20(7): e0327006, July 11, 2025

## 2. Key Contributions
- First SR+MA on MB2 prevalence specifically in Iranian population
- 29 studies included; multiple databases including Persian-language sources (Magiran, SID, IranMedex)
- Detection method subanalysis: microscope highest, periapical X-ray lowest
- Highlights method-dependent variability in detection

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- PRISMA SR+MA; PROSPERO-registered
- Databases: PubMed, Scopus, Magiran, SID, IranMedex, Google Scholar; through May 31, 2024
- Inclusion: cross-sectional studies; Iranian population; maxillary molars; MB2 prevalence data
- Quality assessment: JBI Critical Appraisal Tool for prevalence studies
- STATA18; inverse variance method; random-effects; 95% CI

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
| Tooth | MB2 Prevalence | 95% CI | I² |
|---|---|---|---|
| Maxillary 1st molar | 60% | 53–67% | 97.14% |
| Maxillary 2nd molar | 33% | 25–42% | 97.42% |

**By detection method (maxillary 1st molar):**
| Method | Prevalence |
|---|---|
| Microscope | 78% |
| CBCT | ~63% (implied) |
| Periapical radiograph | 15% |

**By detection method (maxillary 2nd molar):**
| Method | Prevalence |
|---|---|
| Microscope | 61% |
| Periapical radiograph | 4% |

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Extremely high heterogeneity (I²=97% across all groups)
- Population-specific; may not extrapolate to non-Iranian populations
- Method variability is the dominant driver of heterogeneity

## 6. Related Work
- Wu-2025: Han population SR+MA (PMFM 63.7%, PMSM 23.3%)
- Comparison note: Iranian PMFM 60% vs Han PMFM 63.7% — consistent across East/South Asian populations
