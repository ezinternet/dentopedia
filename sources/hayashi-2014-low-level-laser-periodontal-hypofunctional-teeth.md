## Why Ingested

교합 자극의 상실(occlusal hypofunction)이 치주조직·치조골에 미치는 영향을 직접 보여주는 동물 실험으로, "occlusion → alveolar bone" synthesis의 기초 근거. 기존 [[occlusion/fan-2018-occlusal-trauma-and-excessive-occlusal]]는 교합 *과부하*(occlusal trauma)를 다루지만, 본 논문은 반대 극단인 교합 *저하/무자극*이 PDL stricture와 치조골 BMD 감소를 유발함을 보여 교합-치조골 축의 양 끝을 보완한다. 또한 [[occlusion/leone-2026-occlusal-overload-and-periodontitis-integrating]]의 교합-치주조직 상호작용 논의에 hypofunction 측 데이터를 추가한다.

## One-line Summary

Rat experimental study (n=24, 4 groups): occlusal hypofunction reduced PDL thickness and alveolar bone BMD; low-level laser (LLL, 904–910 nm) irradiation restored both to control levels and raised bFGF/VEGF-positive cell counts.

## 한줄요약

랫드 실험(n=24, 4군): 교합 무자극(hypofunction)은 치주인대(PDL) 두께와 치조골 골밀도(BMD)를 감소시켰고, 저출력 레이저(LLL, 904–910 nm) 조사가 이를 대조군 수준으로 회복시키며 bFGF·VEGF 양성세포를 증가시켰다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Confirms that occlusal hypofunction (loss of occlusal stimuli) during the growth period weakens periodontal tissue — measurable PDL stricture/thinning and reduced alveolar-bone BMD.
2. Demonstrates that low-level laser (LLL) irradiation to hypofunctional molars restores PDL thickness and alveolar-bone BMD to levels statistically indistinguishable from normal-occlusion controls.
3. Provides a candidate molecular mechanism: LLL raises bFGF- and VEGF-immunopositive cell numbers in the PDL (fibroblasts, cementum, alveolar bone-lining cells) of hypofunctional teeth back to control levels.
4. Clinical implication for orthodontics: because hypofunctional teeth (e.g., open-bite, high-canine cases) are prone to greater root resorption during tooth movement, LLL pre-conditioning may help recover periodontal structure before orthodontic movement.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Animals**: 24 male Wistar rats, 5 weeks old, randomized into hypofunctional and control groups.
- **Hypofunction model**: An anterior metal cap (maxillary incisors) + anterior bite plate (mandibular incisors), bonded with composite resin, raised the bite and eliminated occlusal force at the molars for 2 weeks. Hypofunction confirmed by soft X-ray at 5 and 7 weeks.
- **Four groups**: control, control+LLL, hypofunctional, hypofunctional+LLL.
- **Laser**: Low-level diode laser (Lumix2 HEPL, Fisioline), wavelength 904–910 nm, peak power 45 W, pulse repetition ≤30 kHz, 200 ns pulse, 8.0 mm probe. Dose 48.6 J, 30 kHz, applied to maxillary first molar through the gingival sulcus, 3 minutes every other day for 2 weeks.
- **Imaging**: Micro-CT (SkyScan1176) at 5 and 7 weeks — PDL thickness (buccal side of distal palatal root, maxillary first molar) and BMD of maxillary alveolar bone (palatal side, 500 mm³).
- **Histology/IHC**: H-E staining; immunohistochemistry for bFGF (rabbit polyclonal) and VEGF (chicken polyclonal); positive PDL cells counted in a 300×400 mm rectangular area (middle one-third buccal aspect of distal palatal root), blinded, 3 sections × 5 samples per group.
- **Statistics**: Repeated one-way ANOVA + Tukey-Kramer test, significance p<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Body weight**: No significant difference among groups (normal growth).
- **PDL thickness (7 wk)**: Significantly smaller in the hypofunctional group than all others. No significant difference among control, control+LLL, and hypofunctional+LLL. (No significant differences at 5 wk.)
- **Alveolar bone BMD (7 wk)**: Significantly lower in hypofunctional than control. Higher in hypofunctional+LLL than hypofunctional, with no significant difference between control and hypofunctional+LLL. (No significant differences at 5 wk.)
- **Histology (H-E)**: Hypofunctional group showed PDL stricture and thinner PDL; hypofunctional+LLL PDL resembled controls.
- **bFGF & VEGF IHC**: Significantly fewer positive cells (fibroblasts, cementum, alveolar bone-lining cells) in hypofunctional vs other groups (P<0.01); hypofunctional+LLL significantly higher than hypofunctional and similar to control (P<0.01).
- **Conclusion**: Occlusal hypofunction during growth weakens periodontal tissue (PDL stricture, lower alveolar crest BMD); LLL irradiation restores a near-normal periodontal condition, mediated at least in part by enhanced bFGF/VEGF expression and associated angiogenesis/bone remodeling.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Animal (rat) growth-period model — direct extrapolation to adult human occlusion is limited.
- Small sample (n=24; 5 samples/group for IHC) and short timeframe (2-week appliance + 2-week LLL).
- Mechanism is correlative (bFGF/VEGF cell counts), not causally dissected; no functional knockdown.
- Single laser dose/wavelength tested; no dose-response curve (LLL is known to be biphasic).
- Long-term durability of recovery, and whether LLL reduces root resorption during subsequent orthodontic movement, were proposed but not tested.

## 7. Glossary

- **Occlusal hypofunction**: State of teeth deprived of occlusal stimuli (e.g., open bite, infraocclusion); leads to disuse atrophy of periodontal tissue.
- **PDL (periodontal ligament)**: Fibrous connective tissue anchoring the tooth to alveolar bone; thins/stricts under hypofunction.
- **BMD (bone mineral density)**: Quantitative measure of bone mineralization; reduced in hypofunctional alveolar bone.
- **LLL / LLLT (low-level laser / therapy)**: Low-power (here 904–910 nm diode) photobiomodulation, claimed to stimulate bone formation, angiogenesis, and fibroblast/osteoblast proliferation.
- **bFGF (basic fibroblast growth factor)**: Angiogenic mitogen; drives mesenchymal differentiation, angiogenesis, ECM formation.
- **VEGF (vascular endothelial growth factor)**: Primary angiogenic mediator; increases vascular permeability; involved in bone remodeling.
