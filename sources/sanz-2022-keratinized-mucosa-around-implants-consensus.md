## Why Ingested

임플란트 주위 각화점막 (Keratinized Peri-Implant Mucosa, KPIM)의 임상적 중요성에 대한 공식 consensus report로, DGI/SEPA/Osteology 워크숍 Group 1의 34개 합의 성명서와 임상 권고안을 담고 있다. 기존 [[implants/thoma-2021-soft-tissue-management-implants-eao-consensus]]이 soft tissue management 전반을 다루는 반면, 본 보고서는 KM < 2 mm threshold와 외과적 증대술(자가이식 vs 대체재)에 특화된 합의 기반 권고를 제공하여 임상 결정 근거를 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

DGI/SEPA/Osteology 2022 consensus report (34 statements, 2 SRs): keratinized peri-implant mucosa (KPIM) < 2 mm is associated with increased peri-implantitis, plaque, mucosal recession and MBL; FGG is standard of care for KPIM augmentation, with xenogeneic substitutes as a comparable alternative to CTG.

## 한줄요약

DGI/SEPA/Osteology 2022 합의 보고서 (34개 성명·2편 SR 기반): KPIM < 2 mm는 임플란트 주위염·치태·점막퇴축·변연골소실 증가와 연관되며, 자유치은이식(FGG)이 표준술식이고 이종이식(xenograft)은 결합조직이식(CTG)과 유사한 대안으로 인정된다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **34 consensus statements** covering: measurement of KPIM, threshold values, associations with peri-implant disease, surgical augmentation techniques, and future research directions.
2. **8 clinical practice implications** and **13 future research implications** formally approved by all workshop participants.
3. **KT < 2 mm threshold** established as the operative clinical cutoff: associated with higher biofilm accumulation, BOP/inflammation, mucosal recession, and marginal bone loss (MBL).
4. **FGG as standard of care** confirmed for KPIM augmentation; xenogeneic substitutes shown to be non-inferior to CTG with lower post-operative morbidity.
5. Clarifies that KM width beyond 2 mm (up to 11 mm) provides no additional reduction in mucosal inflammation — the threshold effect is at 2 mm.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Evidence base**: Two systematic reviews commissioned for the workshop:
- **SR #1** (Ramanauskaite et al., 2022): Influence of KT width on peri-implant tissue health — PECO design (KT < 2 mm vs ≥ 2 mm). 22 articles (21 studies: 15 cross-sectional, 5 longitudinal, 1 pre-post case series). Risk of bias: high to low.
- **SR #2** (Montero et al., 2022): Efficacy of soft-tissue substitutes vs autogenous grafts for KPIM augmentation — PICOS design. 11 articles (10 investigations: 5 RCTs, 1 CCT, 3 prospective case series, 1 retrospective case series).

**Consensus process**: Workshop group discussion → plenary session approval → formal publication as supplement to Clin Oral Implants Res.

**Search period**: SR #1 through September 2020 + updated search October 2020–January 31, 2022. SR #2 through corresponding period.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### KPIM and Peri-Implant Disease (SR #1)

| Outcome | KT < 2 mm | KT ≥ 2 mm |
|---|---|---|
| Peri-implant mucositis prevalence | 20.8%–46.6% | 20.5%–53% |
| Peri-implantitis prevalence | 10.5%–44% | 5.1%–17% |
| Plaque scores | Significantly higher | Reference |
| Mucosal recession (cross-sectional) | Significantly more | Reference |
| Marginal bone loss | Significantly more | Reference |
| Probing depth | No significant difference | Reference |
| Brushing discomfort/pain | Higher (n=4 studies) | Lower |
| OHRQoL | No significant difference | Reference |

- WMD for BOP, mBI, PI, MBL, and MR all significantly favored KT ≥ 2 mm.
- Evidence on peri-implant mucositis incidence (not prevalence) is inconclusive due to methodological heterogeneity.
- Lingual KT band appears equally important but is underreported in the literature.

### KPIM Augmentation Techniques (SR #2)

| Comparison | WMD / WME | CI | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Autogenous grafts vs all substitutes (KM gain) | WMD = 0.9 mm more with autogenous | [0.3; 1.4] | p = .001 |
| Autogenous grafts vs xenografts only | WMD = 0.8 mm (NS) | [0.0; 1.6] | p = .062 |
| All substitutes: KM width increase | WME = 3.0 mm | [2.2; 3.7] | p < .001 |
| Xenografts only: KM width increase | WME = 3.5 mm | [2.4; 4.5] | p < .001 |
| Shrinkage (autogenous vs substitutes) | WMD = −3.7% (NS) | [−10.1; 2.7] | p = .256 |
| Post-operative pain | Significantly lower with substitutes | — | — |
| Surgical time reduction (substitutes) | WMD = 18.5 min less | [10.3; 26.8] | p ≤ .001 |

- FGG augmentation range: 1.5–6.5 mm KPIM gain in comparative studies.
- CTG gain: 2.3–2.6 mm.
- Graft shrinkage (surface area): 42.4%–60% from baseline to 12 months.
- Allogenic substitutes showed significantly higher shrinkage than autogenous grafts (mean difference −19.5%; p = .009).

### Clinical Practice Implications (selected)

1. Assess KPIM width routinely with a mm-scaled probe at buccal and lingual aspects.
2. Reassess KPIM at each follow-up appointment (changes expected over time).
3. When KPIM < 2 mm is associated with pathological changes, surgical augmentation is indicated.
4. KPIM augmentation should aim for non-mobile (attached) mucosa, not just width.
5. In shallow vestibule situations, deepening the vestibule should be combined with autogenous grafting.
6. Xenogeneic substitutes are a valid alternative when donor-site morbidity is a concern.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- High heterogeneity among included studies (different KT threshold definitions, case definitions for mucositis/peri-implantitis).
- No data on incidence (only prevalence) of peri-implant mucositis/peri-implantitis relative to KT presence/absence.
- SR #2 included studies with overall unclear to high risk of bias.
- Limited data on lingual KT measurements.
- No evidence on impact of KT on OHRQoL.
- No comparative data on attached (non-mobile) mucosa attainment between graft types.

**Future research priorities:**
- Standardize primary/secondary outcome measures globally.
- Define precise KT threshold levels for peri-implant tissue stability.
- Evaluate mucosal mobility (attached vs non-attached) as a distinct clinical parameter.
- Apply imaging technologies (e.g., ultrasound, OCT) to assess KPIM volume longitudinally.
- Report lingual KT values separately.
- Investigate effects of implant location, vestibular depth, and suprastructure design on KPIM.

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| KPIM / PIKM | Keratinized peri-implant mucosa — keratinized soft tissue surrounding dental implants |
| PIM | Peri-implant mucosa — total soft tissue seal around implants |
| KT | Keratinized tissue — tissue with a keratinized outer epithelium (includes attached and non-attached) |
| FGG | Free gingival graft — autogenous graft harvested from palate; standard of care for KPIM augmentation |
| CTG | Connective tissue graft — autogenous subepithelial graft; used for thickening and/or width gain |
| Xenogeneic substitute | Soft-tissue substitute of animal origin (e.g., collagen matrix); alternative to autogenous grafts |
| Allogenic substitute | Soft-tissue substitute of human cadaveric origin |
| WMD | Weighted mean difference — meta-analytic statistic for continuous outcomes across studies |
| WME | Weighted mean effect — meta-analytic estimate of treatment effect magnitude |
| BOP | Bleeding on probing — marker of peri-implant mucosal inflammation |
| MBL | Marginal bone loss — radiographic measure of crestal bone changes around implants |
| MR | Mucosal recession — apical migration of the peri-implant mucosal margin |
| PROMs | Patient-reported outcome measures |
| Mucosal junction | Junction between keratinized and non-keratinized alveolar mucosa; used as distal reference for KT measurement |
| Apically positioned flap | Surgical technique to reposition keratinized tissue apically, creating a new zone of KT |
