## One-line Summary
Blood (THROMBOSIS AND HEMOSTASIS) 기초·중개 연구: JAK2V617F clonal hematopoiesis of indeterminate potential (CHIP) — 골수 변이가 platelet activation·cross-talk을 통해 동맥혈전증 위험 증가시키는 기전 규명. 치과 직접 적용보다는 노인 환자의 CHIP-driven thrombosis 배경 지식.

## 2. Key Contributions
1. Mechanistic evidence that JAK2V617F CHIP increases arterial thrombosis risk via platelet activation.
2. Cross-talk between mutant blood cells and platelets identified as mediator.
3. Adds molecular basis to epidemiologic CHIP-cardiovascular risk association.

## 3. Methodology
Translational research integrating mouse models, platelet functional assays, and human CHIP cohort data.

## 4. Key Results
[근거강함] JAK2V617F CHIP → platelet activation → arterial thrombosis (mechanistically demonstrated)

## 5. Limitations
- Basic/translational; clinical management implications still developing
- CHIP screening not yet routine in dental practice (or anywhere outside research)
- No direct dental procedure implication

## 7. Glossary
- CHIP: Clonal hematopoiesis of indeterminate potential
- JAK2V617F: somatic mutation in JAK2 kinase, common driver of myeloproliferative neoplasms; also found at lower allele frequencies in CHIP
- Platelet cross-talk: platelet-leukocyte aggregates contributing to thrombosis
