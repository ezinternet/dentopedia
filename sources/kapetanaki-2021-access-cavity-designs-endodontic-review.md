## One-line Summary
Literature review on traditional vs minimally invasive access (MIA) cavity designs: MIA not fully supported by evidence; traditional straight-line access remains safer for instrument operation and prevention of iatrogenic complications; guided endodontics emerging but lacks cost-benefit data.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Restorative Dentistry & Endodontics 2021;46(3):e46
- **DOI**: 10.5395/rde.2021.46.e46
- **Institution**: Aristotle University of Thessaloniki, Greece

## 2. Key Contributions
- Systematic literature review comparing traditional endodontic cavity (TEC) vs minimally invasive access (MIA) designs
- Evaluates: canal location, cleaning efficacy, fracture resistance, iatrogenic risk
- MIA benefits not yet supported by evidence; traditional approach remains the safer standard
- Reviews guided endodontics (static/dynamic) as emerging approach for calcified canals

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Literature review; databases: PubMed, Science Direct, Scopus, Web of Science, OpenGrey
- Manual search + hand-search of reference lists + expert contact for unpublished studies
- Two independent reviewers; English and additional languages

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
**Traditional Endodontic Cavity (TEC):**
- Straight-line access: facilitates instrument insertion, reduces cyclic fatigue
- Roof of pulp chamber removed + cervical dentin protrusions cleared
- Well-established safety and efficacy record

**Minimally Invasive Access (MIA):**
- Smaller preparation; aims to preserve more tooth structure
- Evidence insufficient to support replacing TEC
- Risk: increased difficulty in canal location, higher iatrogenic complication rate
- Fracture resistance: conflicting evidence; some studies show no benefit

**Guided Endodontics:**
- New approach for PCO (pulp canal obliteration) and calcified canals
- No cost-benefit or time-efficiency data available
- Based on CBCT + 3D-printed templates (static) or real-time navigation (dynamic)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Many MIA studies use in vitro/ex vivo models; limited clinical RCTs
- Fracture resistance data conflicting and context-dependent
- Need for standardized outcome definitions across studies

## 7. Glossary
- **TEC**: Traditional Endodontic Cavity
- **MIA**: Minimally Invasive Access cavity
- **PCO**: Pulp Canal Obliteration
- **NiTi**: Nickel-Titanium rotary instruments
- **Straight-line access**: direct unimpeded path from access cavity to apical foramen
