## One-line Summary
Perspective article reviewing the evolution of implant placement timing (ITI Type 1–4), current evidence-based criteria for each protocol, and future directions toward AI-guided, biologically personalized timing decisions.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Periodontal Research 2026; 61:101–106
- **Type**: Perspective article (part of the JPR 60th anniversary "Past/Present/Future" series)
- **Authors**: University of Zurich, Clinic of Reconstructive Dentistry
- **Scope**: Clinical overview of implant placement timing classification, evidence, gray zones, and future trends

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive synthesis of ITI Type 1–4 timing classification with survival rates and clinical indications
- Identifies "gray zones" — borderline cases not covered by rigid timing categories
- Articulates future shift: from time-based protocols → biologically guided, patient-specific timing
- Introduces "as early as safely possible" as the emerging guiding principle
- Highlights AI + CBCT + bioactive materials as the next transformative forces

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative perspective (not systematic review)
- Synthesizes ITI consensus statements, systematic reviews, and clinical studies
- Organized historically: Past (delayed) → Present (ITI Type 1–4) → Future (personalized)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### ITI Timing Classification

| Type | Timing | Survival Rate | Key Advantages | Key Risks |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Type 1** | Immediate (day of extraction) | 95–98% | Fewer surgeries, immediate temporization | Recession risk (thin phenotype), technique-sensitive |
| **Type 2** | 4–8 weeks (soft tissue healed) | 96–99% | Better soft tissue management, easier GBR | Longer treatment, grafting usually needed |
| **Type 3** | 12–16 weeks (partial bone fill) | 97–99% | Stable substrate, lower infection risk | Ridge resorption may require augmentation |
| **Type 4** | >6 months (complete healing) | >95% | Optimal bone/soft tissue, stable ridge | Longest treatment time, maximum ridge loss |

### Type 1 (Immediate) — Prerequisites
- No acute infection or active periodontal pathology
- Intact socket walls (all 4 walls present)
- Primary stability: **IT ≥ 35 Ncm**
- Controlled gap grafting (implant–socket gap)
- Risk: midfacial recession higher in thin periodontal phenotype + high smile line

### Type 2 — Best For
- Minor infection or limited bone defects where Type 1 is contraindicated
- Thin buccal plate cases: mucosal healing reduces bacterial contamination
- Tension-free primary closure facilitates GBR membrane management

### Type 3 & 4 — Best For
- Type 3: suboptimal sockets, thin buccal bone, prior periapical pathology, minor augmentation needed
- Type 4: extensive bone loss, prior infection, sites requiring major augmentation, low esthetic demand

### Gray Zones and Clinical Dilemmas
- Thin buccal plate < 1 mm: not automatic contraindication if primary stability achievable + regeneration planned
- Endodontically treated teeth with residual periapical pathology: borderline for Type 1
- Thin gingival phenotype + high smile line: biologically successful but esthetic risk → Type 2 often safer
- Combined risk factors (smoking + thin phenotype + history of periodontitis): combined impact poorly quantifiable

## 5. Future Directions
- **AI + CBCT**: machine-learning models analyzing bone volume, density, socket morphology + clinical variables → risk stratification and outcome prediction for implant stability and MBL
- **Bioactive materials**: growth factors, bioactive scaffolds, cell-modulating biomaterials → accelerated osteogenesis may reduce biological limitations of early placement
- **Soft tissue phenotype modification**: connective tissue grafts, volume-stable collagen matrices → reduce midfacial recession risk
- **Paradigm shift**: healing time as a *modifiable* variable, not a fixed biological constraint
- **Future principle**: "when for this patient, in this socket, under these systemic and local conditions" — not "wait 3–6 months"

## 7. Glossary
- **Type 1**: Immediate placement — at time of extraction
- **Type 2**: Early placement — 4–8 weeks, after soft tissue healing
- **Type 3**: Early placement — 12–16 weeks, after partial bone fill
- **Type 4**: Late/delayed placement — >6 months, after complete bone remodeling
- **Thin periodontal phenotype**: Thin gingival tissue + thin buccal bone; higher recession risk
- **GBR (Guided Bone Regeneration)**: Membrane-based bone augmentation technique
- **Midfacial recession**: Gingival recession on the buccal/facial surface of an anterior implant — major esthetic complication
- **Primary stability**: IT ≥ 35 Ncm threshold for immediate loading/placement
