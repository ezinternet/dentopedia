## Why Ingested

The most current (2025) narrative review consolidating the clinical, microbiological, and salivary-chemistry evidence for bacterial black stain — directly updates and extends [[caries/zyla-2015-black-stain-dental-caries-review]] with a decade of newer mechanistic data (iron-sulfide precipitation pathway, salivary ion profiling, alternative treatment evidence). Core reference needed for any black-stain overview or caries-protective-flora synthesis.

## One-line Summary

2025 narrative review (Eur J Oral Sci) of dental black stain: Actinomyces-dominant biofilm + altered salivary iron/pH drives ferric-sulfide deposition; prevalence 2.4–20%; inverse caries association confirmed; standard treatment is ultrasonic scaling with emerging alternatives (PDT, coconut oil, probiotics).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Most up-to-date (2025) synthesis of dental black stain etiology, microbiology, salivary chemistry, and treatment
- Proposes unified mechanism: oral bacteria (especially Actinomyces spp.) metabolize sulfur compounds → H₂S + salivary iron → insoluble ferric sulfide/iron sulfide deposits form the characteristic black pigment
- Confirms prevalence range 2.4–20%, affecting both genders equally across all ages
- Identifies salivary differences in black stain individuals: elevated iron, sodium, copper, calcium/phosphate; higher pH; lower salivary flow
- Reviews treatment hierarchy: ultrasonic scaling (standard), with PDT, virgin coconut oil, peroxide therapy, lactoferrin, and probiotics as alternatives
- Emphasizes repeated professional cleaning as primary intervention; cautions against enamel damage from aggressive therapies

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Narrative review design; no systematic search protocol or PRISMA
- Sources: contemporary literature on black stain microbiology, salivary biochemistry, epidemiology, and clinical management
- Scope: bacterial-origin black stain only (distinguished from extrinsic staining from tobacco, food, chlorhexidine, etc.)
- Covers clinical presentation (dark lines/incomplete coalescence of dark spots at cervical third), prevalence, microflora, salivary parameters, and treatment options

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Parameter | Finding |
|---|---|
| Prevalence | 2.4–20% (both genders equally) |
| Dominant microorganism | Actinomyces spp. (most abundant in black stain plaque across studies) |
| Caries association | Inverse: black stain associated with lower caries incidence |
| Salivary iron | Elevated in black stain individuals |
| Salivary pH | Higher in black stain individuals |
| Salivary flow | Lower in black stain individuals |
| Other elevated ions | Sodium, copper, calcium/phosphate |
| Pigment chemistry | Iron ions + H₂S (bacterial metabolite) → ferric sulfide/iron sulfide |
| Standard treatment | Repeated ultrasonic professional cleaning |
| Alternative treatments | PDT, virgin coconut oil, peroxide therapy, lactoferrin, oral probiotics |

- Caries-protective effect hypothesized from: elevated pH, high calcium/phosphate (remineralization potential), and antimicrobial properties of Actinomyces-dominant biofilm
- No single treatment shown superior to ultrasonic scaling in evidence quality

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative (not systematic) review: no formal risk-of-bias assessment or heterogeneity quantification
- Underlying mechanistic pathway (iron sulfide precipitation) remains inferential — no in vitro confirmation model cited
- Most cited epidemiological studies have heterogeneous diagnostic criteria for black stain
- Limited RCT evidence for alternative treatments (PDT, probiotics, coconut oil)
- Salivary composition findings are largely cross-sectional; directionality unproven

## 7. Glossary

- **Black stain**: External tooth discoloration forming dark lines or spots at the cervical third; bacterial-origin subtype distinct from tobacco/food staining
- **Actinomyces**: Gram-positive, filamentous oral bacterium; dominant species in black stain biofilm
- **Ferric sulfide / Iron sulfide (FeS)**: Insoluble black pigment formed by reaction of Fe³⁺ ions with H₂S from bacterial sulfur metabolism
- **H₂S**: Hydrogen sulfide; metabolic byproduct of anaerobic bacterial sulfur reduction
- **PDT**: Photodynamic therapy — light-activated antimicrobial treatment
- **Lactoferrin**: Iron-binding glycoprotein with antimicrobial properties; proposed as alternative treatment
- **Ultrasonic scaling**: Standard mechanical removal of black stain deposits using cavitation energy
