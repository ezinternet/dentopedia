## One-line Summary
Comparative prospective study evaluating a variable platform-switched subcrestal angle-correction implant in ITRT (immediate tooth replacement therapy), aimed at avoiding socket perforation under uniaxial implant placement.

## 1. Document Information
- **Title:** Immediate Tooth Replacement Therapy in Postextraction Sockets: A Comparative Prospective Study on the Effect of Variable Platform-Switched Subcrestal Angle Correction Implants

- **Year:** 2020

- **Study type:** prospective

## 2. Key Contributions
Validates angle-correction implant geometry as a tool to reconcile prosthetic-driven positioning with bony-housing constraints in postextraction sockets.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Comparative prospective design. ITRT cases with the angle-correction implant vs reference. Clinical and radiographic outcomes through ~12 months.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
Favorable soft-tissue stability and esthetic outcomes with the test implant. Reduced incidence of unfavorable socket perforation events.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
Industry-aligned implant; not a true RCT; medium-term follow-up.

## 7. Glossary
- **IIP:** Immediate Implant Placement — placement at the time of tooth extraction.
- **IIPP:** Immediate Implant Placement and Provisionalization — IIP combined with same-visit provisional restoration.
- **SRP:** Sagittal Root Position — Kan I/II/III/IV classification of root-to-bone relationship.
- **CBCT:** Cone-Beam Computed Tomography.
- **PES/WES:** Pink/White Esthetic Score — esthetic outcome indices for anterior implants.
- **EMBL:** Early Marginal Bone Loss.
