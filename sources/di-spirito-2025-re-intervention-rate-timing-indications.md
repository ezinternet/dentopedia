## Why Ingested

[[wiki/oral-surgery/cervera-espert-2016-coronectomy-mandibular-third-molar-sr]] established coronectomy as a nerve-sparing alternative but left the *re-intervention* burden (how often, when, why retained roots need a second surgery) underspecified. This 2025 umbrella review (SR of SRs) is the first synthesis to quantify re-intervention rate (4.45%), timing (mean 10.4 months), and indication breakdown — directly extending the older anchor's safety/efficacy story into the long-term follow-up dimension.

## One-line Summary

Umbrella review (6 systematic reviews, 5896 subjects, 7913 successful coronectomies): overall re-intervention rate 4.45%, mostly within ~10 months, driven by root exposure (16.76%) and migration (12.20%), with IAN injury rare (0.76%).

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Systematic review of systematic reviews (umbrella review), PRISMA 2020, PROSPERO CRD4202455688
- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Medicine, 2025;14:3877
- **Quality appraisal**: AMSTAR 2; overlap assessed via Corrected Cover Area (CCA)
- **Databases**: Scopus, MEDLINE/PubMed, BioMed Central, Web of Science, Cochrane, PROSPERO (to 30 Dec 2024)
- **Inclusion**: subjects undergoing coronectomy, follow-up ≥6 months

## 2. Key Contributions

- First umbrella-level estimate of re-intervention burden after mandibular third-molar coronectomy.
- Separates re-intervention *rate* from re-intervention *indications* (root exposure, infection, pain) and from root *migration* (a sequela, not automatically a re-intervention trigger).
- Confirms IAN injury remains rare after coronectomy even on pooled SR-of-SR data.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 83 records retrieved → 33 duplicates removed → 50 screened → 6 systematic reviews included.
- Pooled denominators: 5896 subjects, 7913 *successful* coronectomies (i.e. excluding cases converted to immediate full extraction).
- Narrative synthesis with descriptive statistics (Microsoft Excel); subgroup characterization by patient/tooth/coronectomy variables.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Overall re-intervention rate: 4.45%.**
- **Timing**: 6 months to 10 years; **mean 10.4 months** (most re-interventions early).
- **Indications for re-intervention**: root exposure 16.76% (primary), infection 4.55%, pain 2.84%.
- **Root migration: 12.20%** (common, typically stabilizes within ~1 year).
- **IAN injury: 0.76%** (rare).
- Background comparators cited: coronectomy infection 2.49%, alveolar osteitis 1.22%, post-op pain 9.55%.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative (no meta-analysis); heterogeneous primary-study definitions of "re-intervention" and "migration."
- Overlap among the 6 included SRs (shared primary studies) inflates apparent sample.
- Calls for standardized imaging protocols and refined, consensus re-intervention criteria.

## 7. Glossary

- **Coronectomy (partial odontectomy)**: removal of the crown with intentional retention of roots to avoid IAN trauma.
- **Re-intervention**: secondary surgery on a previously coronectomized site (root retrieval), distinct from initial intra-op conversion failure.
- **Root migration**: coronal movement of retained roots post-op, usually self-limiting within ~1 year.
- **AMSTAR 2 / CCA**: methodological-quality and overlap-assessment tools for umbrella reviews.
