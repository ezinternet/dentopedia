## One-line Summary
In-vitro study (MC3T3-E1): C/Ti ratio dose-dependently suppresses osteoblast attachment and spreading; ALP and calcium mineralization reduced >40% at C/Ti=1.0; first direct evidence that hydrocarbon contamination is a primary cause of suboptimal BIC.

## 1. Document Information
J Dent Res 2014;93(7):698-703. doi:10.1177/0022034514536578. Tokyo Medical and Dental University + UCLA.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First study directly testing dose-response of hydrocarbon on osteoblast activity
- Quantifies C/Ti ratio effect on cell attachment, spreading, ALP, mineralization
- Provides mechanistic explanation for BIC plateau at 45-65% (Ogawa 2025 context)
- Implies hydrocarbon removal (UV-PF) as rational countermeasure

## 3. Methodology
cpTi grade-2 disks, mirror-polished. Carbon concentration regulated by machine oil varnish + acetone washing (C/Ti = 0.3, 0.7, 1.0). MC3T3-E1 osteogenic cells. Parameters: attachment, spreading, ALP, calcium deposition (day 20).

## 4. Key Results
| C/Ti ratio | Cell attachment | ALP | Calcium mineralization |
|-----------|----------------|-----|----------------------|
| 0.3 | Baseline | Baseline | Baseline |
| 0.7 | ↓ | ↓ | ↓ |
| 1.0 | Significantly ↓ | >40% reduction | >40% reduction |
- Cell spreading concentration-dependently suppressed
- Commercial implants: 17.9–76.5% carbon detected across 34 different implant surfaces (Morra et al. 2003 cited)

## 5. Limitations
In vitro; mirror-polished surface only; MC3T3-E1 cell line not primary osteoblasts; mechanism of carbon-cell interaction not elucidated.

## 6. Related Work
Minamikawa 2016 (long-term C accumulation), Ogawa 2025 (D3 = carbon/aging), Park 2025 (UV-PF removes hydrocarbon).

## 7. Glossary
C/Ti ratio: carbon-to-titanium atomic ratio by XPS; MC3T3-E1: pre-osteoblastic mouse cell line; ALP: alkaline phosphatase.
