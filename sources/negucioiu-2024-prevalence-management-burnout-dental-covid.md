## Why Ingested

Adds the post-COVID temporal arc and the management/modifiable-factor angle (workplace relationships) to the burnout evidence base, updating the currency of [[overviews/dentist-burnout-mental-health-overview]].

## One-line Summary
SR (no MA) — first post-pandemic dentist burnout review covering the full pre/during/post-COVID arc; confirms high EE/DP/reduced-PA across all phases, with pandemic-era stressors amplifying impact and workplace relationships/dynamics emerging as the leading modifiable factor.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Healthcare (MDPI), 12:2366, Nov 26 2024
- DOI: 10.3390/healthcare12232366
- Type: Systematic review (PRISMA), no meta-analysis
- PROSPERO: CRD42024586616
- Search period: January 2014 → April 2024 (covers pre/during/post-COVID arc)
- Affiliation: Iuliu Hațieganu Medicine and Pharmacy University, Cluj-Napoca, Romania (Prosthetic Dentistry)
- Quality: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale

## 2. Key Contributions
- First SR explicitly covering the **full pandemic arc** (before / during / after COVID-19) for dental burnout
- Includes both **prevalence** and **management/intervention** evidence
- Identifies **workplace dynamics and professional relationships** as the highest-leverage modifiable factor
- Demonstrates differential effects by educational level and age
- Calls for occupational health strategies (vs. individual coping interventions)

## 3. Methodology
- Databases: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science (Jan 2014 → Apr 2024)
- Eligibility: prevalence of burnout, interventions to reduce burnout, impact of burnout on dental practices
- Quality: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (observational studies)
- PRISMA-compliant
- Narrative synthesis (no pooled effect estimates)

## 4. Key Results
- High levels of all 3 MBI components (EE, DP, reduced PA) confirmed across pre/during/post-COVID phases
- Significant variation by:
  - Workplace factors
  - Professional relationships
  - Pandemic-related stresses
- Differential effects by:
  - Educational level
  - Age
- Workplace dynamics and professional relationships identified as central modifiable lever
- Calls for targeted public health initiatives and occupational health strategies

## 5. Limitations
- Narrative synthesis only (no MA) — no pooled effect sizes
- MDPI Healthcare journal — note publisher (rigor varies, though this paper is PROSPERO-registered)
- Heterogeneous study designs included
- "Post-COVID" period only partially observable (search end April 2024)

## 6. Related Work
- Moro 2022 (MBI-only SR+MA — primarily pre-COVID)
- Long 2023 (SR+MA through Oct 2021 — partial COVID)
- Plessas 2022 (intervention SR — non-pandemic-specific)

## 7. Glossary
- **NOS (Newcastle-Ottawa Scale)**: quality assessment tool for observational studies
- **PROSPERO**: international prospective register for systematic reviews
- **MDPI**: Multidisciplinary Digital Publishing Institute
- **Occupational health strategies**: workplace-level interventions targeting systemic stressors
