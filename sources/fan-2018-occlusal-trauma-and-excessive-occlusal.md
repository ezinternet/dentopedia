## One-line Summary
2017 World Workshop narrative review establishing case definitions for occlusal trauma and excessive occlusal forces, concluding their role in initiating/progressing periodontitis remains controversial and that primary occlusal trauma does not cause attachment loss in healthy periodontium.

## 한줄요약
2017 World Workshop narrative review — 교합외상·과도교합력의 case definition을 정립; 치주염 발생·진행에서의 역할은 여전히 논쟁적이며, 건강한 치주에서 primary occlusal trauma는 부착소실을 유발하지 않음.

## 1. Document Information
- J Periodontol 2018;89(Suppl 1):S214–S222 (jointly published in J Clin Periodontol). DOI 10.1002/JPER.16-0581.
- 2017 World Workshop on Classification of Periodontal and Peri-Implant Diseases and Conditions.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Provides consensus case definitions for occlusal trauma, traumatic occlusal force, and related terms.
- Reviews effects on periodontium: periodontitis initiation/progression, abfraction, gingival recession.
- Clarifies primary vs secondary occlusal trauma and diagnostic considerations.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative review commissioned for the 2017 World Workshop; synthesis of human and animal evidence with consensus case definitions.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Role of occlusal trauma in initiation/progression of periodontitis remains controversial.
- Primary occlusal trauma (excessive force on normal periodontium) does not cause irreversible attachment loss.
- No conclusive evidence that occlusal trauma causes gingival recession or non-carious cervical lesions/abfraction.
- Provides diagnostic signs (e.g., increasing mobility, PDL widening, fremitus) without a single definitive test.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Human evidence largely associative; causation unresolved.
- Animal-model extrapolation limits.

## 6. Related Work
- Glickman vs Waerhaug historical debate on co-destruction.
- 2017 World Workshop companion papers (periodontitis, peri-implant).

## 7. Glossary
- Primary occlusal trauma: injury from excessive force on a normal periodontium.
- Secondary occlusal trauma: injury from normal/excessive force on a reduced (periodontitis-weakened) periodontium.
- Fremitus: palpable tooth vibration during occlusal contact.
