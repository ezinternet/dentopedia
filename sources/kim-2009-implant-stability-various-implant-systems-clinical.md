## Why Ingested

ISQ 변화 패턴이 임플란트 시스템(macrodesign)보다 골질(bone quality)에 의해 지배된다는 임상 근거가 필요했는데, 이 prospective study (Kim 2009)는 세 가지 상용 시스템(Astra/Camlog/Replace)을 24주간 추적해 "ISQ 시간 변화는 시스템이 아니라 bone type에 따라 갈린다"는 직접 증거를 제공한다. 매크로디자인·골질이 ISQ에 미치는 영향을 다룬 [[implants/isq/stoilov-2023-macrodesign-length-diameter-bone-quality-isq]]를 임상 시계열 데이터로 보강하고, design(cylindrical vs tapered) 비교에 초점을 둔 [[implants/isq/nandini-2022-cylindrical-vs-tapered-implant-isq]]와 대비된다(여기서는 design 간 차이가 골질 효과에 가려짐).

## One-line Summary

Prospective clinical study (28 patients, 45 implants, 3 systems — Astra Osseospeed/Camlog/Replace, single-stage, no loading) tracking ISQ over 24 weeks: ISQ change patterns over time differed significantly by bone type (not by implant system); stability rose sharply weeks 0–6 (largest gain in Type 4 bone) then plateaued through 6 months; no implants failed.

## 한줄요약

전향적 임상연구(환자 28명, 임플란트 45개, 3개 시스템 — Astra/Camlog/Replace, 단일 단계, 무하중)에서 24주간 임플란트 안정성 지수 (Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ)를 추적한 결과, ISQ의 시간적 변화 패턴은 임플란트 시스템이 아니라 골질 유형 (bone type)에 따라 유의하게 갈렸으며, 안정성은 0~6주에 급증(4형 골에서 증가폭 최대)한 뒤 6개월까지 정체(plateau)했고, 실패한 임플란트는 없었다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Bone quality, not implant system, governs the ISQ time course.** Across three commercially distinct macrodesigns, a two-factor mixed-model ANOVA showed a significant time × bone-type interaction in every system (Group A P=.0022, C P=.017, R P=.0018), i.e. the *shape* of the ISQ trajectory was determined by bone type.
2. **Quantified the baseline ISQ gradient by bone type**: immediately after placement, mean ISQ was >70 in Type 1, 65–70 in Type 2 & 3, and 48–50 in Type 4 (ordering Type 4 < Type 2&3 < Type 1).
3. **Characterized the early-healing "stability dip/recovery" inversely by bone type**: the *percent* ISQ gain from week 0→6 was largest in the softest bone (Type 4 ≈ 48–56% across systems) and smallest in dense Type 1 bone (≈0.5–7%), then a plateau weeks 6→24.
4. **Anatomical location (maxilla vs mandible) did not significantly affect ISQ change** by the two-sample t-test in any of the three groups (only Group R showed a mixed-model location effect, P=.0238, not confirmed by t-test).
5. Demonstrated that magnetic RFA (Osstell) gives reproducible, noninvasive, painless ISQ readings clinically across systems.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Patients**: 28 total (25 male, 3 female; mean age 58.6 ± 9.23), recruited Mar–Sep 2004 wanting implant placement. (Abstract states "25 patients"; Methods text states 28 — the source carries this internal discrepancy; implant n=45 is consistent.)
- **Inclusion**: adults ≥18; adequate oral hygiene (mean modified sulcus bleeding index ≤1, modified plaque index ≤1); sufficient bone volume; edentulous site ≥6 months post-extraction with healthy adjacent teeth; negative pregnancy test in fertile women.
- **Exclusion**: smokers >10 cigarettes/day; alcohol/drug addiction history (past 5 yr); severe clenching/bruxism; SBE risk; uncontrolled hypertension or diabetes; malignant tumor.
- **Implants**: 45 implants, 3 systems / groups:
  - **Group A** = Osseospeed (Astra Tech, Sweden)
  - **Group C** = Camlog (Biotechnologies AG, Switzerland)
  - **Group R** = Replace (Nobel Biocare, Sweden)
  - Distribution: Maxilla n=13 (A 4 / C 6 / R 3), Mandible n=32 (A 11 / C 11 / R 10).
- **Surgery**: one operator, **single-stage (one-stage) technique** per each manufacturer's protocol; placed with manual torque wrench; healing abutment connected and sutured. **No immediate or early loading** in any implant.
- **Bone quality**: Lekholm & Zarb Type 1/2/3/4, judged by drilling resistance. For analysis, **Types 2 & 3 were pooled** (intermediate bone hard to differentiate reliably). Distribution: Type 1 n=11, Type 2&3 n=25, Type 4 n=9.
- **ISQ measurement**: Osstell magnetic RFA; system-specific SmartPeg torqued to 4–5 N·cm; probe held 2–3 mm from the magnet; measured in **two directions (buccolingual + mesiodistal)**, three reps each. ISQ recorded at **0 (placement), 3, 6, 8, 10, 12, 24 weeks** post-op.
- **Statistics**: two-factor mixed-model ANOVA (time × type, time × location); contrast comparisons between time points; ANOVA + Tukey multi-comparison across bone types (P=.05); two-sample t-test for maxilla vs mandible.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **No failures / no complications**: 45/45 implants survived to 24 weeks; readings highly reproducible.
- **Baseline ISQ by bone type** (immediately post-placement): Type 1 >70; Type 2&3 65–70; Type 4 48–50 (statistically distinct, P<.05 in all groups).
- **Time × bone-type interaction significant** in all systems: Group A P=.0022, Group C P=.017, Group R P=.0018.
- **% ISQ increase, week 0 → 6** (sharp early rise):
  - Group A: Type 1 +4.78%, Type 2&3 +8.73%, Type 4 +56.47%
  - Group C: Type 1 +0.52%, Type 2&3 +6.38%, Type 4 +49.11%
  - Group R: Type 1 +7.37%, Type 2&3 +11.87%, Type 4 +47.81%
  - → Magnitude of early gain ordered **Type 1 < Type 2&3 < Type 4** (softest bone gains most).
- **Weeks 6 → 24 (six months): plateau** (slow increase). Significance of the slow phase varied: e.g. Group A Type 1 not significant (P=.052), Types 2&3/4 significant (P=.044, .0326); Group C Types 1 & 2&3 significant (P=.0356, P<.0001) but Type 4 not (P=.3715); Group R Type 1 not significant (P=.6411), Type 4 significant (P=.0005, .0462).
- **Location**: no significant maxilla-vs-mandible difference in ISQ change by two-sample t-test in any group (Group R had a mixed-model location effect P=.0238, attributed to small/uneven maxilla sample — 5 of 13 maxilla cases were Type 4 bone).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Small, uneven samples** per cell (e.g. some bone-type × system cells n=2–4); no power analysis; under-powered subgroup comparisons (notably location).
- **No control group, no randomization, single operator** — descriptive prospective design, not an RCT.
- **Could not analyze implant length** effect (acknowledged as an important ISQ factor) due to insufficient length-stratified samples.
- Bone type assigned subjectively by drilling resistance (Types 2 & 3 had to be merged).
- Patient-count discrepancy in the text (25 vs 28).
- **No functional loading / no long-term (>6 mo) prosthetic outcomes**; ISQ used as a surrogate for osseointegration, not clinical success endpoints.
- Authors call for study of mechanotransduction during early loading across systems and its effect on RFA readings.

## 6. Related Work

- **Meredith et al. (1994–1997)**: introduced/validated resonance frequency analysis (RFA) for implant stability; correlation of RFA with bone–implant contact. Foundational basis cited throughout.
- **Cochran et al. (2002)**: reduced healing times on SLA implants; source of the week 6–6 month plateau concept.
- **Roberts (rabbit bone studies)**: woven-bone formation and lamellar deposition timeline (week 0–6 rise, remodeling weeks 6–18, maturity ~54 weeks) — mechanistic explanation for why soft bone (Type 4) gains ISQ fastest (cancellous healing faster than cortical lamellar remodeling).
- **Friberg, Sennerby, Lekholm**: cutting torque vs RFA comparisons; mandible-shows-higher-stability literature that this study did *not* reproduce.
- **Nedir et al. (2004)**: predicting osseointegration from primary stability.

## 7. Glossary

- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: 1–100 scale from resonance frequency analysis; higher = stiffer bone–implant interface (greater stability).
- **RFA (Resonance Frequency Analysis)**: noninvasive method measuring the resonance of an implant–transducer system; here the magnetic Osstell device + SmartPeg.
- **Primary stability**: mechanical fixation at placement (press-fit of implant against bone); determined by bone stiffness, implant geometry/length, contact area.
- **Secondary stability**: biological stability gained over time via bone formation/remodeling at the interface (osseointegration).
- **Plateau effect**: slow ISQ change after the rapid week 0–6 rise, as woven bone matures into load-bearing lamellar bone.
- **Lekholm & Zarb bone quality**: Type 1 (dense cortical) → Type 4 (thin cortex, sparse low-density trabeculae).
- **SmartPeg**: system-specific magnetic transducer screwed into the implant for Osstell RFA.
- **Single-stage (one-stage) technique**: implant placed with a healing abutment transmucosally, no second uncovering surgery.
