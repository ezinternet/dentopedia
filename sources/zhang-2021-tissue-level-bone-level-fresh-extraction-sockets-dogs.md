## Why Ingested

즉시식립 시 임플란트 경부 디자인(조직레벨 vs 골레벨)이 경조직·연조직 리모델링에 미치는 영향을 직접 비교한 조직형태계측 동물실험 근거가 필요했음. 특히 [[wiki/implants/atieh-2025-tissue-vs-bone-level-peri-implantitis-sr-ma]]에서 제시한 조직레벨 vs 골레벨 임플란트의 주위염 위험도 차이를 생물학적 폭경(biologic width) 관점에서 보완하는 자료로 활용.

## One-line Summary

Dog histomorphometric study (n=6 dogs, 24 implants) comparing Straumann SP (tissue-level) vs BL (bone-level) implants in fresh extraction sockets: no significant difference in vertical bone resorption between designs, but bone-level implants showed significantly wider lingual biologic width when placed 1 mm subcrestal (3.16 vs 2.43 mm, P=0.009).

## 한줄요약

개(6마리, 24개 임플란트) 즉시식립 조직형태계측 연구: 조직레벨(SP)과 골레벨(BL) 임플란트 간 수직골흡수는 유의차 없으나, 1 mm 하방 식립 시 골레벨 임플란트의 설측 생물학적 폭경이 유의하게 더 넓음(3.16 vs 2.43 mm, P=0.009).

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Demonstrates that tissue-level implants with divergent smooth collar (SP) and bone-level implants with platform switching (BL) produce equivalent vertical bone resorption in fresh extraction sockets at 6 months, regardless of insertion depth (crestal or 1 mm subcrestal).
2. Identifies a significant difference in lingual biologic width: BL implants establish wider biologic width (3.16 mm) than SP implants (2.43 mm) when placed 1 mm subcrestal (P=0.009).
3. Confirms that buccal bone resorption consistently exceeds lingual resorption for both implant types, consistent with prior animal studies on immediate implant placement.
4. Shows that 1 mm subcrestal placement does not increase bone loss compared to crestal placement for either implant design.
5. Documents thin buccal bone plate in dog extraction sockets (0.53–0.71 mm at crest level), highlighting the clinical vulnerability of the buccal wall.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Animal model**
- 6 adult male Labrador dogs, mean age 1 year, weight 21–22 kg
- Bilateral mandibular premolars extracted; each root extracted individually after crown sectioning
- Buccal bone wall width measured with vernier caliper at 4 depths: 0, −1, −3, −5 mm from crest
- 24 implants total; 4 implants per dog (2 SP + 2 BL), randomized across 4 premolar sockets per side

**Implants**
- Straumann Standard Plus (SP): tissue-level, SLA surface, 4.8 mm neck diameter, 1.8 mm machined turned collar, 3.3 mm body diameter × 10 mm length
- Straumann Bone Level (BL): bone-level, SLA surface, platform switching with 0.265 mm horizontal offset, micro-thread neck, 3.3 mm × 10 mm
- Both types placed at 2 insertion depths: 0 mm (flush) and −1 mm (1 mm subcrestal) relative to buccal alveolar crest
- Healing abutments placed; non-submerged healing protocol; no prosthetic loading during 6-month observation

**Histological preparation**
- Fixed in 10% formalin; dehydrated in ascending alcohol; embedded in glycol-methacrylate resin
- Sectioned buccal-lingual with Leica SP1600 Saw Microtome; ground to ~70 µm final thickness
- Stained with Van Gieson method; imaged with Olympus BX51 + DP71 camera
- Measurements via Image-Pro Plus 6.0

**Histomorphometric landmarks and measurements**
- PM: peri-implant free mucosal margin
- IS: implant shoulder
- BIC: most coronal bone-to-implant contact point
- aBE: most apical border of the barrier (junctional) epithelium
- Epithelial length: PM–aBE
- Connective tissue length: aBE–BIC
- Biologic width (BW): PM–aBE + aBE–BIC
- Vertical bone resorption: IS–BIC distance minus pre-designed insertion depth (SP: additional 1.8 mm machined neck deducted)

**Statistics**
- SPSS v19; Levene test for homogeneity; two-sample t-test (homogeneous) or Wilcoxon sign-rank test (heterogeneous); α=0.05; n=6 per subgroup

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Buccal bone wall thickness at extraction**

| Depth from crest | Range across sites |
|---|---|
| 0 mm (crest tip) | 0.53–0.71 mm |
| −1 mm | 0.77–0.93 mm |
| −3 mm | 1.20–1.29 mm |
| −5 mm | 1.50–1.58 mm |

No significant difference between sites (P>0.05).

**Vertical bone resorption: SP vs BL (Table 3)**

| Side | Depth | SP (mm) | BL (mm) | P |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Buccal | 0 mm | 3.15 ± 0.52 | 2.70 ± 0.52 | 0.165 |
| Buccal | −1 mm | 2.55 ± 0.41 | 2.99 ± 0.21 | 0.091 |
| Lingual | 0 mm | 1.68 ± 0.38 | 1.68 ± 0.37 | 1.000 |
| Lingual | −1 mm | 1.47 ± 0.18 | 1.56 ± 0.19 | 0.419 |

No significant differences for any comparison (P>0.05).

**Biologic width: SP vs BL (Table 3)**

| Side | Depth | SP (mm) | BL (mm) | P |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Buccal | 0 mm | 3.34 ± 0.66 | 3.44 ± 1.11 | 0.853 |
| Buccal | −1 mm | 3.35 ± 0.42 | 3.57 ± 0.57 | 0.464 |
| Lingual | 0 mm | 2.43 ± 0.25 | 3.00 ± 0.58 | 0.052 |
| Lingual | −1 mm | 2.43 ± 0.42 | 3.16 ± 0.36 | **0.009*** |

Sole significant finding: wider lingual BW in BL implants at −1 mm subcrestal.

**Effect of insertion depth within each implant type**
- SP implants: no difference in bone resorption or BW between 0 mm and −1 mm
- BL implants: no difference in bone resorption or BW between 0 mm and −1 mm
- Both types: buccal bone resorption consistently and significantly greater than lingual (P≤0.010)

**BIC position (histological)**
- SP: most coronal BIC below the machined-SLA junction (border of turned collar)
- BL: most coronal BIC at or below implant-abutment junction (platform edge)
- Scattered BIC zones between threads observed at coronal portion of BL implants — interpreted as secondary bone contact after partial loss of primary BIC at thin buccal wall

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample size: n=6 dogs, n=6 per subgroup (adequate for pilot-level evidence, insufficient for definitive conclusions)
- Only two neck designs compared; no grafting material in gap between implant and socket wall (no-graft protocol)
- 6-month unloaded healing only — no functional loading; longer follow-up with occlusal contact needed
- Dog mandibular premolar anatomy may not fully represent human alveolar socket geometry or bone density
- Platform switching offset in BL implants (0.265 mm) is relatively small; results may differ with larger offsets (e.g., 0.4–0.65 mm as in Bressan 2014)
- BIC data not reported as a percentage (BIC%) — biologic width components reported but not bone resorption relative to implant length

## 6. Related Work

- Bressan et al. 2014: dog study with platform switching implants (0.4/0.65 mm offset); found greater bone preservation than tissue-level — differs from present null result; authors attribute difference to larger offset and thicker buccal bone
- Saito et al. 2018: prospective clinical study, Superline implants, platform mismatch 0.33/0.58 mm; similar no-preservation finding when mismatch is small
- Araujo & Lindhe 2005/2006: seminal dog studies on dimensional ridge changes after tooth extraction and immediate implant placement — provided reference framework for buccal > lingual bone loss pattern
- Grassi et al. (cited): RCT showing no-graft immediate implant achieves comparable buccal bone changes to grafted sites
- Souza et al. (cited): transmucosal component design influences biologic width establishment

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Tissue-level implant (조직레벨 임플란트) | Implant with machined/polished transmucosal collar extending above bone crest; soft tissue interface at implant body |
| Bone-level implant (골레벨 임플란트) | Implant placed at or below alveolar crest; tissue interface at separately attached abutment |
| Platform switching (플랫폼 스위칭) | Mismatch between wider implant platform and narrower abutment diameter; designed to shift IAJ inward and reduce crestal bone stress |
| Biologic width (생물학적 폭경, BW) | Combined height of junctional epithelium (PM–aBE) and supracrestal connective tissue (aBE–BIC) around the implant |
| BIC (골-임플란트 접촉, Bone-to-Implant Contact) | Most coronal point of direct bone contact with implant surface, used to define crestal bone level |
| IS-BIC (수직골흡수) | Distance from implant shoulder to most coronal BIC, corrected for insertion depth; equals vertical crestal bone resorption |
| Van Gieson stain | Histological stain distinguishing collagen (red) from muscle/cytoplasm (yellow); used to visualize bone-connective tissue interfaces |
| SLA surface (모래분사 대립 산부식면) | Sand-blasted large-grit acid-etched titanium surface; standard rough surface for osseointegration |
| Non-submerged healing (비매몰 치유) | Healing protocol where healing abutment is exposed to oral cavity from day of surgery; no second-stage surgery needed |
| aBE (접합상피 최하방 경계) | Most apical border of the barrier/junctional epithelium; landmark separating epithelial from connective tissue attachment |
