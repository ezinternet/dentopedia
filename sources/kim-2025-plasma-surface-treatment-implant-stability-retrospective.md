## One-line Summary

Retrospective cohort (73 plasma-treated implants in 47 patients, single-arm): chairside non-thermal plasma immediately before placement was associated with progressive ISQ increase (78.97±5.52 → 83.74±4.36 at week 8, p<0.001) with no stability dip; greatest gains in low-initial-stability, mandibular, shorter, and higher-insertion-torque implants.

## 한줄요약

후향 코호트(환자 47명, 플라즈마 처리 임플란트 73개, 단일군): 식립 직전 chairside 비열 플라즈마 후 ISQ가 78.97±5.52→83.74±4.36(8주, p<0.001)로 점진 상승하고 stability dip이 없었다. 초기 안정성 낮을수록·하악·짧은 fixture·높은 식입토크에서 이득 최대.

## 1. Document Information

- Journal: Materials 2025, 18(19):4568
- DOI: 10.3390/ma18194568
- Study type: retrospective cohort (human), single-arm (no control)
- Device: ACTILINK Reborn; non-thermal atmospheric pressure plasma

## 2. Key Contributions

- Real-world human cohort showing ISQ trajectory and absence of stability dip after plasma.
- Subgroup gradient identifying who benefits most.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 73 plasma-treated implants in 47 patients (Jun 2023–Oct 2024). NTAP immediately before placement.
- ISQ at baseline, weekly for 4 weeks, and week 8. Subgroups: initial ISQ, jaw, length/diameter, insertion torque.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- No stability dip; uneventful healing.
- Mean ISQ 78.97±5.52 (placement) → 83.74±4.36 (week 8), p<0.001.
- Greatest relative gains in lower-initial-stability implants; very stable implants changed little.
- Mandibular sites, shorter fixtures, higher insertion torque → greater gains.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- No control group; retrospective; short-term ISQ only.
- "No dip" cannot be attributed to plasma without a control — requires RCT confirmation.
