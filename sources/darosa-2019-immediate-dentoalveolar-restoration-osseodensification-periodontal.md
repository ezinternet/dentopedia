## One-line Summary
Two-case clinical report combining Immediate Dentoalveolar Restoration (IDR) — autogenous tuberosity bone block — with osseodensification osteotomy to achieve immediate implant primary stability in periodontally compromised extraction sites.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Case report / preliminary clinical communication (n = 2 cases)
- **Journal**: International Journal of Periodontics & Restorative Dentistry, 2019;39(4):527-534
- **DOI**: 10.11607/prd.3883 (PMID 31226191)
- **Sample**: 2 clinical cases, severe alveolar bone loss after periodontally compromised extractions
- **Primary Question**: Can combining the IDR technique (autogenous tuberosity block grafting at extraction) with osseodensification osteotomy preparation improve immediate implant primary stability in periodontally compromised sockets?

## 2. Key Contributions
- Reports a workflow that pairs IDR (autogenous bone-block reconstruction of the buccal plate) with osseodensification site preparation for immediate implant placement.
- Documents improved primary stability (measured as higher insertion torque, exact numbers not given in extractable abstract) in two periodontally compromised cases.
- Outcomes assessed by clinical exam, photography, radiography, and CBCT — soft- and hard-tissue positioning evaluated at follow-up.
- Proposes osseodensification as a force-multiplier for IDR by densifying the residual host bone/graft interface during osteotomy.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
This is a non-controlled clinical communication describing two cases of severely periodontally compromised teeth managed with: (1) atraumatic extraction; (2) Immediate Dentoalveolar Restoration (IDR) — autogenous bone block harvested from the maxillary tuberosity and shaped to reconstruct the buccal plate/socket walls; (3) osseodensification osteotomy preparation using Densah burs in counterclockwise mode; (4) immediate implant placement with provisional. Outcomes captured: insertion torque, clinical soft-tissue contour, periapical/CBCT bone-fill imaging. No quantitative comparison group, no formal statistics.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
Numerical detail not extractable from available text — abstract only (publisher paywall). Authors qualitatively report that adding osseodensification raised insertion torque sufficient to support immediate provisionalization in both cases. Soft- and hard-tissue stability described as maintained at follow-up.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- n = 2; case-report level evidence, no comparator, no blinded outcome assessor.
- No quantitative ISQ/Ncm/MBL data extractable from available text.
- Selection of "compromised" sites is operator-defined; reproducibility across operators unknown.
- Long-term peri-implant tissue stability and survival not characterized.
- Publisher paywall limits independent verification — full PDF text unavailable for this summary.

## 7. Glossary
- **IDR (Immediate Dentoalveolar Restoration)**: technique developed by da Rosa et al. using autogenous bone block from the maxillary tuberosity to reconstruct the buccal plate at the moment of extraction, enabling immediate implant placement in sites with buccal-plate dehiscence.
- **Osseodensification (OD)**: bone-preserving osteotomy preparation using Densah burs in counterclockwise mode to compact and plastically expand alveolar bone.
- **Periodontally compromised site**: extraction socket with significant bone loss secondary to chronic periodontitis (typically with buccal dehiscence and reduced trabecular volume).
