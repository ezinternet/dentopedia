## Why Ingested
Locates the response problem at the policy layer: national rules can actively undermine learning from complaints. Explains the organisational source of the 'complaint handler's bind' ([[complaint-management/pone.0325185]]) and frames what clinic-level policy should avoid.

## One-line Summary
NHS Trust case study (20 staff interviews + documents) showing national complaint policies create muddled routes, validity-scrutiny, futile data collection, and adverse incentives that undermine improvement.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal of the Royal Society of Medicine 2022;115(10):390-398.
- NIHR Imperial Patient Safety TRC + LSE. Large acute multi-site NHS Trust.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Examines how national policies shape *local* complaint practice -- an under-studied link.
- Identifies four adverse areas: muddled routes for formal complaints, investigations structured to scrutinise complaint 'validity', futile data-collection systems, and adverse incentives/workarounds.
- Shows policy-as-intended diverges from policy-as-practised.
- Argues complaint systems are structured to defend rather than to learn.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Qualitative case study at a large acute multi-site NHS Trust: semi-structured interviews with 20 clinical, managerial, complaints, and patient-advocacy staff plus documentary analysis of complaint policies and infrastructure.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
Four practice areas where national policy had adverse local consequences: (1) muddled routes for raising formal complaints; (2) investigative procedures structured to scrutinise the 'validity' of complaints rather than learn; (3) futile data-collection systems; (4) adverse incentives and workarounds. Together these undermine an improvement-focused approach.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
Single NHS Trust; English-policy-specific. Staff perspectives (n=20), not patient outcomes. Qualitative/interpretive.

## 6. Related Work
Policy-level companion to Van Dael (2022, JHSRP) on complaint-safety linkage and to Gillespie & Reader (2025) on the handler's bind.

## 7. Glossary
- **Policy-as-intended vs policy-as-practised**: gap between written policy and frontline enactment.
- **Validity scrutiny**: investigative framing that asks whether a complaint is legitimate rather than what can be learned.
