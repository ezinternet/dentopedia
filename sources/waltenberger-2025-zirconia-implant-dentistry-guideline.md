## One-line Summary
German S3 clinical guideline (DGI/DGZMK) on all-ceramic implant-supported restorations recommending lithium disilicate and all zirconia generations for single crowns (~96–97% 3-year survival), restricting FDPs and full-arch cases to 3Y-TZP zirconia, and cautioning against polymer-infiltrated ceramics and highly translucent zirconia in load-bearing indications.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First DGI-led S3 guideline specifically addressing all-ceramic materials for implant prosthodontics
- Addresses three central clinical questions:
  1. Long-term performance of all-ceramic implant restorations
  2. Material selection by indication
  3. Design features improving long-term success
- Explicitly warns against polymer-infiltrated ceramics for implant use
- Flags translucent (high-Y zirconia) generations as lacking long-term implant validation
- Emphasizes importance of bonding protocols and interdisciplinary collaboration

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Evidence synthesis**: Systematic literature review by authors; predefined selection and extraction criteria
- **Evidence rating**: GRADE approach
- **Three clinical sections**:
  - Part A: Implant-supported single crowns
  - Part B: Short-span fixed dental prostheses (3–4 units)
  - Part C: Full-arch restorations
- **Consensus process**: Strong consensus reached for every recommendation
- **Recommendation strength**:
  - A ⇑⇑ ("shall") — strong recommendation
  - B ⇑ ("should") — moderate recommendation
  - 0 ⇔ ("can") — weak/optional recommendation

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
**Single crowns (Part A):**
- Lithium disilicate, silicate ceramics, all zirconia generations: ~96–97% 3-year survival — recommended
- Polymer-infiltrated ceramics: inferior performance — NOT recommended for implant crowns

**Short-span FDPs (Part B):**
- Only 3Y-TZP zirconia supported by clinical evidence for 3–4 unit spans
- Micro-veneering and monolithic designs reduce chipping risks

**Full-arch restorations (Part C):**
- Only 3Y-TZP zirconia has adequate evidence
- Patient education essential due to limited evidence for newer materials
- Interdisciplinary collaboration required

**Design principles:**
- Bonding protocols must be adhered to
- Monolithic or micro-veneered designs preferred over full-veneered to reduce chipping
- Translucent (high-Y) zirconia has increased translucency but reduced strength — indication-specific limitations apply; long-term implant data lacking

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Evidence for full-arch all-ceramic restorations remains limited
- Long-term clinical performance of newer zirconia generations (4Y, 5Y-TZP) on implants not yet validated
- Guideline focused on German clinical practice context; applicability to other healthcare systems may vary

## 7. Glossary
- **S3 guideline**: Highest level of German clinical guideline (evidence-based + formal consensus process)
- **DGI**: Deutsche Gesellschaft für Implantologie — German Association of Oral Implantology
- **DGZMK**: Deutsche Gesellschaft für Zahn-, Mund- und Kieferheilkunde — German Society of Dentistry and Oral Medicine
- **AWMF**: Arbeitsgemeinschaft der Wissenschaftlichen Medizinischen Fachgesellschaften — regulatory body for German medical guidelines
- **AGREE II**: Appraisal of Guidelines for Research and Evaluation instrument
- **GRADE**: Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation
- **3Y-TZP**: 3 mol% yttria-stabilized tetragonal zirconia polycrystal — classic high-strength zirconia
- **Polymer-infiltrated ceramic**: Hybrid material (e.g., VITA Enamic); not suitable for implant crowns per this guideline
- **Micro-veneering**: Thin ceramic veneer layer on monolithic zirconia framework to improve esthetics with reduced chipping risk
