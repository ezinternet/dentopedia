## Why Ingested

ISQ 시계열 데이터(2개월~24개월)와 MBL 상관관계를 동시에 추적한 2년 전향적 코호트가 부재했음. 본 연구(Fu et al. 2023, n=156 임플란트)는 primary ISQ→secondary ISQ 회복 궤적과 MBL에 대한 bone density·IT의 상대적 영향력을 정량화하여 [[wiki/overviews/implants-isq-stability-ladder]]의 ISQ 해석 프레임워크를 보강하고, [[wiki/overviews/isq-loading-threshold]]의 임계값 근거를 임상 코호트 관점에서 검증하기 위해 인제스트.

## One-line Summary

2-year prospective pilot study (n=90 patients, 156 implants, Kaohsiung) tracking ISQ from surgery through 24 months and MBL at 7 time points, finding bone density and insertion torque — not primary ISQ — are the dominant predictors of marginal bone loss.

## 한줄요약

90명·156개 임플란트 2년 전향적 파일럿 연구(고슝): ISQ 추적(수술~24개월) 및 7시점 MBL 측정에서 변연골 소실의 주 예측인자는 primary ISQ가 아닌 골밀도·삽입 토크였음.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Provides a 2-year ISQ time course (primary through 24 months) for 156 internal-hex implants (MIS Seven), measured with Osstell Mentor at 10 time points.
2. Demonstrates that implant diameter (not length) has high positive correlation with primary ISQ (r=0.772) and IT (r=0.684).
3. Establishes bone density (Misch D1–D4) as the strongest single predictor of IT (r=0.836) and a moderate predictor of primary ISQ (r=0.529).
4. Shows that bone density and IT are the dominant predictors of MBL (r=0.826 and r=0.794 respectively), while primary ISQ shows no significant effect on MBL.
5. Finds a moderate negative correlation between secondary ISQ and MBL (r=−0.581), consistent with the ISQ-tracks-bone-health relationship.
6. Reports 100% 2-year implant survival rate in the non-bone-augmentation cohort.
7. Documents that secondary ISQ stabilizes after 6 months and bone density differences among groups converge by 9 months post-op.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Design**: Single-center prospective pilot study, Department of Dentistry, Kaohsiung Medical University Hospital.

**Participants**: 90 patients (42 male, 48 female; mean age 55.8±11.4 years, range 28–76 years); 156 internal-hex implants (MIS Seven) placed to support single-unit fixed crowns. Exclusion criteria included need for bone augmentation, active infection, uncontrolled periodontitis, severe bruxism, pregnancy.

**Implant parameters**:
- Lengths: 10, 11.5, 13 mm
- Diameters: 3.3, 3.75, 4.2, 5.0 mm
- Distribution: maxillary anterior 19.2%, maxillary posterior 26.9%, mandibular anterior 18%, mandibular posterior 35.9%

**Bone density assessment**: Preoperative CBCT (Implametric/NNT viewer; Misch D1–D4 classification), supplemented by intraoperative tactile feedback (Lekholm and Zarb classification). Bone density distribution: D1 6.4%, D2 32.7%, D3 42.9%, D4 18%.

**ISQ measurement**: Osstell Mentor with Smartpeg transducers. Four-direction measurement (buccal, lingual/palatal, mesial, distal); mean recorded. Time points: surgery (primary), 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 9, 12, 18, 24 months post-op.

**IT measurement**: Torque wrench (MIS) at final insertion; implant platform placed at buccal crest level.

**MBL evaluation**: Digital periapical radiographs with customized positioning jig (paralleling technique) at baseline (immediate post-op), 3, 6, 9, 12, 18, 24 months. Magnification calibrated to implant length. Average of mesial and distal bone level from implant platform.

**Crown delivery**: 4–5 months post-implant placement (delayed loading). Screw-retained or cement-retained crowns with lingual/palatal notches for ISQ access.

**Statistics**: SPSS 22.0; t-test, one-way ANOVA, Pearson and Spearman correlations; significance at P≤0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Insertion Torque**:
- Mean IT: 33.75±6.82 Ncm (range 20–50 Ncm)
- Bone density effect: D1=47.25±6.92, D2=40.81±7.63, D3=30.79±4.61, D4=23.16±8.20 Ncm (P=0.014)
- Diameter effect: 3.3mm=29.51, 3.75mm=31.62, 4.2mm=34.63, 5mm=40.17 Ncm (P=0.032)
- Length: no significant effect (P=0.575)
- Age and gender: no significant effect

**Primary ISQ**:
- Mean primary ISQ: 65.06±6.28 (range 53–85)
- Bone density: D1=78.47±6.71, D2=68.49±8.03, D3=63.95±7.26, D4=56.68±5.87 (P=0.027)
- Diameter: 3.3mm=60.18, 3.75mm=63.20, 4.2mm=65.14, 5mm=73.77 (P=0.029)
- Length: no significant effect (P=0.186)

**Secondary ISQ Time Course**:
- At 2 months: overall ~66–67 ISQ (slightly above or near primary); bone density and diameter differences remain
- Fluctuations noted 2–6 months
- Stabilizes after 6 months (no significant changes 6→24 months)
- At 24 months: overall ~74–75 ISQ; all subgroups converge; diameter differences narrow to within 4 ISQ by 9 months

**Correlations**:
- IT / bone density: r=0.836 (high positive)
- Primary ISQ / implant diameter: r=0.772 (high positive)
- IT / implant diameter: r=0.684 (moderate positive)
- IT / primary ISQ: r=0.602 (moderate positive)
- Primary ISQ / bone density: r=0.529 (moderate positive)
- Primary ISQ / implant length: r=0.438 (weak)
- IT / implant length: r=0.217 (very weak)

**Marginal Bone Loss**:
- Significant change between 3 and 6 months (tiny, not clinically significant per authors)
- No significant MBL differences after 12 months
- Bone density / MBL: r=0.826 (high positive — denser bone → more MBL initially, likely reflecting higher IT-related compression)
- IT / MBL: r=0.794 (high positive)
- Secondary ISQ / MBL: r=−0.581 (moderate negative)
- Age, gender, implant length, diameter, primary ISQ: no significant effect on MBL

**Survival**: 100% at 24 months (no mobility, no fixture/screw fracture).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Pilot study with relatively small sample (90 patients, 156 implants); single implant system (MIS Seven internal hex).
- Single center; single operator — limits generalizability.
- Bone density by CBCT and tactile feel; no quantitative HU-cutoff reported per Misch tier.
- No bone augmentation cases — results may not apply to regenerated bone sites.
- MBL radiographic measurement (periapical x-ray); 2D cannot capture all 3D bone changes.
- Crowns delivered at 4–5 months (delayed protocol); no early or immediate loading subgroups for comparison.
- Authors call for larger samples, wider implant size range, and longer follow-up.
- Correlation design does not allow causal inference (e.g., whether high IT truly causes MBL or both share bone-density etiology).

## 6. Related Work

- Meredith (1998): original RFA proposal for implant stability assessment
- Sennerby & Roos (1998): surgical determinants of clinical success
- Atsumi et al. (2007): methods used to assess implant stability (IT + RFA comparison)
- Tözüm et al.: negative correlation between MBL and ISQ within 6 months; compensated by remodeling at 6–12 months
- Mumcu: age, gender, cantilevers influenced MBL; implant length/diameter did not
- Noaman & Bede: implant diameter favorable for stability; length had no effect (consistent with this study)
- Han et al.: neither diameter nor surface modifications affected ISQ (contrasting)
- Sim & Lang: ISQ influenced by bone structure and implant length (partial conflict)

## 7. Glossary

- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: RFA output value 1–100; higher = greater stability
- **IT (Insertion Torque)**: Rotational resistance at final seating in Ncm; reflects bone compression
- **RFA (Resonance Frequency Analysis)**: Non-invasive vibration-based method for measuring implant stiffness in bone
- **MBL (Marginal Bone Loss)**: Radiographic distance from implant platform to first bone-to-implant contact; indicator of peri-implant bone health
- **Primary stability**: Mechanical interlocking at implant placement; dominated by bone density and implant diameter
- **Secondary stability**: Biological stability from osseointegration and bone remodeling; emerges over weeks–months
- **Misch D1–D4**: Bone density classification by CT/tactile feel: D1=dense cortical, D2=thick cortical+dense trabecular, D3=thin cortical+loose trabecular, D4=low-density trabecular
- **Smartpeg**: Osstell-specific magnetic transducer screwed onto implant for RFA measurement
- **Osstell Mentor**: Wireless RFA device (Integration Diagnostics AB, Gothenburg) for ISQ measurement
- **IAJ (Implant-Abutment Junction)**: Connection design at the implant-abutment interface; affects crestal bone behavior
