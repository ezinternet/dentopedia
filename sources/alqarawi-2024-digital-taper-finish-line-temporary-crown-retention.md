## One-line Summary
Saudi Dent J 2024;36:1477-1482 (Saudi Arabia) — 2×2×2 in vitro factorial: 10° vs 20° TOC × chamfer vs shoulder × CAD/CAM milled vs 3D printed provisional molar crowns; 3D-printed retain higher (~6.6-7.65 MPa) than milled (~2.35-3.52 MPa); best combo = 3D printed + 20° TOC + shoulder finish line.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: The Saudi Dental Journal 2024;36:1477-1482

## 2. Key Contributions
- 3-factor interaction (TOC × finish line × manufacturing tech) in provisional retention
- 3D printing shows higher retention than milling in this configuration — counterintuitive given typical bulk strength comparisons
- Validates 20° TOC + shoulder for long-term provisional retention

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 4 mandibular first molar prep configurations (2 TOC × 2 finish line)
- 2 manufacturing methods: CAD/CAM milled vs 3D printed
- 8 groups × n=10 = 80 specimens
- Cement (consistent protocol)
- Thermocycling: 5000 cycles at 5°C/55°C, 30 s dwell
- Pull-off force → tensile strength (MPa) via universal testing machine
- Statistics: ANOVA

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Group | Manufacturing | TOC | Finish | Mean tensile (MPa) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Milled | 10° | Chamfer | 2.35 |
| 2 | Milled | 10° | Shoulder | 3.52 |
| 3 | Milled | 20° | Chamfer | 3.21 |
| 4 | Milled | 20° | Shoulder | 2.45 |
| 5 | 3D printed | 10° | Chamfer | 6.6 |
| 6 | 3D printed | 10° | Shoulder | 6.91 |
| 7 | 3D printed | 20° | Chamfer | 7.65 |
| 8 | 3D printed | 20° | Shoulder | 7.45 |

- 3D printed > milled across all configurations (roughly 2-3× higher tensile)
- Best retention: 3D printed + 20° TOC + chamfer (7.65 MPa)
- Best for extended periods: 3D printed + 20° TOC + shoulder (per abstract)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- In vitro; thermocycled but no real masticatory loading
- Provisional crowns only (not definitive)
- Single cement type
- Material-property differences (printed resin vs milled PMMA) not isolated
