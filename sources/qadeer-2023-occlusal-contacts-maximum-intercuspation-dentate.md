## Why Ingested

Establishes the baseline question of what a "normal" number of occlusal contacts at maximum intercuspation (MICP) actually is, and shows the count is driven mainly by the detection method — directly reinforcing [[occlusion/didier-2026-occlusal-contacts-ios-vs-carbon-paper-concordance]], which demonstrates that contact detection differs by indicator (IOS vs carbon paper). Anchors the MICP-contact theme for the CR/CO/MICP batch alongside [[occlusion/kattadiyil-2021-relationship-centric-occlusion-maximal-intercuspal]] and [[occlusion/velasquez-2022-occlusal-analysis-natural-dentition-sr]].

## One-line Summary

Systematised review of 18 studies over four decades (1980s–2022) finding the number of occlusal contacts at MICP in dentate adults ranges widely from 11 to 70, with variation driven mainly by the occlusal indicator/technique and population differences.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies the literature-wide range of occlusal contacts at MICP in dentate adults: **11 to 70**.
- Demonstrates there is **no single "normal" contact count** — the figure is method-dependent.
- Catalogues the occlusal indicators used across four decades of studies and frames their heterogeneity as the primary source of between-study variation.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Systematised review of literature spanning the 1980s–2022.
- Electronic searches in **three databases**: PubMed, Livivo, and Ovid, using a combination of search terms.
- Screening flow: **651 records via PubMed + 37 via Livivo/Ovid** → after removing duplicates/irrelevant records, **168 screened** → **61 full-text articles** → **18 included** (those reporting occlusal contacts as a quantifiable value).
- Occlusal indicators across included studies: articulating paper, wax sheets, shimstock foil, impression material, gnathodynamometers, and digital occlusal analysis systems.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Number of occlusal contacts at MICP ranged widely: 11–70.**
- Variation attributed to: (1) type and technique of occlusal indicator used, (2) diversity among sampled populations, and (3) differing data-collection procedures.
- Clinical implication: occlusal-contact counts are not directly comparable across studies/methods; the detection method must be standardised before a count is interpreted.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only ingest — full text not retrieved; numeric per-study breakdown and per-indicator subgroup figures not available here.
- "Systematised" (not full PRISMA systematic) review; no meta-analytic pooling of the 11–70 range.
- Wide heterogeneity in indicators/populations precludes a defensible single reference value; standardised, method-specific norms are needed.

## 7. Glossary

- **MICP (maximum intercuspation)**: the occlusal position of complete intercuspation of opposing teeth, independent of condylar position; also called maximal intercuspal position.
- **Occlusal indicator**: a recording medium/device used to register tooth contacts (articulating paper, shimstock foil, wax, impression material, gnathodynamometer, digital occlusal analysis system).
- **Shimstock foil**: thin (~8–12 µm) metal foil used to detect presence/absence of a tooth contact by hold/pull-through.
- **Gnathodynamometer**: device measuring occlusal force/bite force at contact points.
- **Systematised review**: a review using systematic-style search/screening methods but not meeting full systematic-review (e.g., PRISMA) requirements.
