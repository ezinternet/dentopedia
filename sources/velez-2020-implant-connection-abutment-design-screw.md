## Why Ingested

User asked what abutment-screw tightening torque to use for a Morse-taper ("Astra-type") conical-connection implant. The wiki had [[prosthetic-materials/ren-2024-morse-taper-abutment-subsidence-locking-force]] (screwless pure Morse taper, Bicon) but no page on the **screw-retained** internal-conical (Morse taper) joint and its dependence on insertion-torque magnitude. This in-vitro study directly compares 10/20/30 Ncm abutment-screw torque on a screwed 11° Morse-taper system. NB: the tested system is Mozo-Grau, not Astra Tech — no Astra-specific abutment-torque paper exists in PubMed; Astra Tech's own abutment-screw torque is 20–25 Ncm (lower than this system's 30 Ncm), so the value is system-specific, not universal.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro study (120 implants, 60 external-hex + 60 11° Morse-taper, screwed at 10/20/30 Ncm, thermo-/cyclic-loaded) showing implant-abutment marginal misfit is lowest with the Morse-taper connection and decreases as insertion torque rises, best at the manufacturer-recommended 30 Ncm (≈0 µm), with no significant gain over 20 Ncm.

## 한줄요약

In-vitro 연구(임플란트 120개: 외측육각 60 + 11° Morse taper 60, 10/20/30 Ncm 체결, 열·기계 하중): 임플란트-어버트먼트 변연 부적합은 Morse taper에서 가장 낮고 체결 토크가 높을수록 감소하여 제조사 권장 30 Ncm에서 최소(≈0 µm)였으나 20 Ncm 대비 유의차는 없었음.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Isolates the effect of **abutment-screw insertion torque magnitude** (10/20/30 Ncm) on implant-abutment marginal misfit, holding implant system constant — limited prior data on this axis.
- Confirms internal **Morse-taper** (11°) connection yields lower marginal misfit (≈0.6 µm) than external hexagon, across abutments and torques.
- Adds the angulated- vs straight-abutment comparison: straight fits better overall, but for the Morse taper connection abutment angulation did **not** significantly affect fit.
- Shows misfit is inversely related to torque, with the floor reached by ~20–30 Ncm (no significant 20→30 gain) for this system.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Design: in-vitro bench study, n=120 implants. 2 connections × 2 abutments × 3 torque levels; n=10 per cell; ≥7/group needed for 80% power.
- Connections: external hexagon (EC) vs internal 11° Morse-taper + double internal hex + platform-switching (IC), same dimensions.
- Abutments: straight hexagonal (SA) vs 15° angulated (AA), 2 mm cuff, titanium grade V (Ti-6Al-4V).
- Torque: abutment screws tightened at 10, 20 or 30 Ncm with a calibrated dynamometric wrench; retightened after 10 min.
- Aging: 1200 thermocycles (5–55 °C) + 250,000 mechanical cycles (200 N vertical, 2.5 mm vertical / 0.7 mm lateral, 2 Hz) in Fusayama-Meyer artificial saliva; 3 mm simulated bone resorption per ISO 14801:2007.
- Outcome: marginal misfit by SEM at 1000× (gold-coated, sectioned specimens), 60 measurements/specimen by two examiners. Stats: ANOVA, Tamhane T2, t-test, Kruskal-Wallis + Bonferroni; α=0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- All groups < 3 µm misfit. Morse-taper (IC) < external (EC), p=0.001; IC-SA lowest; IC SA and AA ≈ 0.6 µm.
- Straight vs angulated overall: 1.3 vs 2.1 µm (p=0.004); for IC, no SA-vs-AA difference.
- Torque (pooled): 30 Ncm best fit; significant 10 vs 20 (p=0.001) and 10 vs 30 (p=0.001); **20 vs 30 Ncm not significant (p=0.10)**.
- Per-group: ECSA fit torque-insensitive; ICSA and ICAA improved significantly from 10→20 and 10→30 Ncm; lowest misfit at 30 Ncm (manufacturer-recommended) in all groups, ≈0 µm for Morse taper.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single implant system (Mozo-Grau) — not generalizable to Astra Tech or other geometries; manufacturer-recommended torque (30 Ncm here) is system-specific.
- In-vitro only; microleakage not measured (inferred low from low misfit).
- No universally standardized misfit-measurement method, hampering cross-study comparison.
- Static SEM after aging; does not capture in-vivo preload decay or repeated clinical (un)screwing.

## 6. Related Work

- ISO 14801:2007 fatigue-testing standard for endosseous dental implants.
- Prior misfit/microleakage studies on Morse-taper vs external/internal connections (cited within); reports of 2.3–5.6 µm Morse-taper misfit vs ≈1.25 µm here.
- Preload/torque literature: under-torque → screw loosening/microleakage; over-torque → yield, preload loss, screw fracture.

## 7. Glossary

- **Morse taper / internal conical connection**: tapered friction (cold-weld) implant-abutment joint; here 11° with a retention screw (screw-retained taper) — distinct from screwless "pure" taper-lock (Bicon).
- **Marginal misfit**: vertical/horizontal gap at the implant-abutment interface (µm).
- **Insertion (tightening) torque**: torque applied to the abutment screw; generates screw elongation → preload clamping the joint.
- **Platform switching**: abutment narrower than implant platform; shifts the microgap inward.
- **Ti grade V (Ti-6Al-4V)**: higher-strength titanium alloy, lower frictional coefficient, higher preload than grade IV.
