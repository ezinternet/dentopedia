## One-line Summary
Overview of 14 SRs on BoNT-A for bruxism (UFSC Brazilian Centre for Evidence-Based Research) — most SRs report reduced pain, bruxism events, and bite force, but all but one score "critically low" on AMSTAR-2, so no definitive conclusion can be drawn.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Toxins 2025, 17(5):249
- **DOI**: 10.3390/toxins17050249
- **Published**: 16 May 2025
- **Lead institution**: Federal University of Santa Catarina (UFSC) — Brazilian Centre for Evidence-Based Research (COBE)
- **Methodology**: Overview of systematic reviews (with/without meta-analysis); AMSTAR-2 appraisal; corrected covered area (CCA) for overlap
- **Study type**: Overview of SRs (narrative review of SRs)

## 2. Key Contributions
- First overview of systematic reviews specifically focused on bruxism (rather than the broader TMD spectrum).
- Distinguishes BoNT-A effects on (a) pain, (b) bruxism event frequency, (c) maximum bite force, (d) functional movements.
- Documents methodological-quality crisis: 13 of 14 SRs rated "critically low" on AMSTAR-2, 1 rated "low" (none "moderate" or "high").
- Quantifies high overlap among primary studies across SRs using CCA (10.11%).
- Explicit warning against extrapolating SR conclusions to clinical decision-making for bruxism.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 7 databases plus grey literature, last search 16 June 2024.
- Two-phase independent screening (titles/abstracts → full text).
- AMSTAR-2 for SR methodological quality.
- Corrected covered area (CCA) for primary-study overlap among SRs.
- Outcomes assessed: pain, bruxism events, maximum bite force, functional movements.
- Inclusion: SRs (with or without MA) of BoNT-A vs. placebo / no Tx / other interventions for adult bruxism.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **14 SRs included** (from 461 references + 196 grey-literature/reference-list sources; 344 after dedup; 30 to full-text).
- **AMSTAR-2 confidence**: 13/14 "critically low"; 1/14 "low" (none ≥ "moderate").
- **Overlap**: CCA = 10.11% — rated "high" overlap (i.e., same primary RCTs repeatedly pooled).
- Outcome direction in included SRs:
  - Pain reduction with BoNT-A: 10/14 SRs report effectiveness.
  - Bruxism event frequency reduction: 7/14 SRs.
  - Maximum bite force reduction: 5/14 SRs (which can be effect or AE depending on framing).
  - Functional movements: no SR effectively evaluated.
- Only 1 SR attempted GRADE certainty assessment, and that attempt was deemed unsuccessful.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Conflation of bruxism with TMD across primary studies and reviews; diagnostic criteria heterogeneous.
- No standardized BoNT-A dose, dilution, or injection sites in primary literature.
- Need for high-quality RCTs with consensus diagnostic criteria and standardized outcomes.
- Calls for AMSTAR-2-compliant future SRs and proper certainty grading (GRADE).

## 6. Related Work
- De la Torre Canales 2024 (Drugs umbrella review) — same approach but for M-TMD, similar methodological reservations.
- Saini 2024 (PLoS ONE SR+MA) — null primary findings dovetail with this overview's caution.
- Multiple primary RCTs included as underlying studies (Guarda-Nardini, Jadhao, Ayala, etc.).

## 7. Glossary
- **BoNT-A** (보툴리눔독소 A형, Botulinum Toxin Type A).
- **Bruxism** (이갈이/이악물기, awake/sleep): Repetitive masticatory-muscle activity (clenching/grinding/bracing); SB = sleep bruxism, AB = awake bruxism.
- **AMSTAR-2**: Appraisal tool with 16 critical/non-critical items for SR methodological quality.
- **CCA** (Corrected Covered Area): Metric (%) quantifying overlap of primary studies across SRs in an overview.
- **GRADE**: Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation framework.
- **Overview**: Review of systematic reviews (one level below umbrella review in some taxonomies).
