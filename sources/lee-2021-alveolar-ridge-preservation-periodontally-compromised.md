## Why Ingested

Most alveolar ridge preservation (ARP) evidence comes from intact, non-infected sockets, so the question of whether ARP helps in *periodontally compromised* (infected) sockets — exactly the teeth most often extracted in practice — is undersettled. This large retrospective cohort (418 sites) directly addresses ARP feasibility/benefit in infected sockets, qualifying the patient-selection nuance in [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/mardas-2023-alveolar-ridge-preservation-overtreatment]] (ARP is not always over-treatment when the socket is compromised) and complementing failure-predictor work in [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/kang-2025-implant-failure-predictors-ridge-preservation]].

## One-line Summary

Retrospective cohort (418 periodontally compromised extraction sites, 287 patients): ARP significantly reduced implant-placement infeasibility (0.8% vs 4.7% no-ARP) and reduced the need/severity of bone augmentation at implant placement.

## 한줄요약

후향연구 (치주적으로 손상된 발치와 418부위, 287명): ARP를 시행하면 임플란트 식립 불가율이 0.8%로 무ARP군(4.7%)보다 유의하게 낮아지고, 식립 시 골이식 필요성·범위도 줄어들었다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Retrospective study of alveolar ridge preservation compared with no alveolar ridge preservation in periodontally compromised extraction sockets
- **Authors**: Jungwon Lee, Junseob Yun, Jung-Ju Kim, Ki-Tae Koo, Yang-Jo Seol, Yong-Moo Lee (Seoul National University)
- **Journal**: International Journal of Implant Dentistry 2021;7:23
- **DOI**: 10.1186/s40729-021-00305-2
- **Study type**: Retrospective cohort (chart review, STROBE-reported)
- **Setting**: Seoul National University Dental Hospital (SNUDH), extractions 2011–2015, implant placement 2011–2018

## 2. Key Contributions

- One of the few large cohorts evaluating ARP specifically in **periodontally/endodontically compromised (infected) sockets**, where most clinical extractions actually occur.
- Defines a primary outcome rarely measured directly: **feasibility of implant placement** at the planned site (binary yes/no), rather than only dimensional bone change.
- Shows ARP not only enables placement but **attenuates the severity of bone augmentation** (horizontal, vertical, total) needed at the time of implant surgery.
- Uses multivariable logistic regression to isolate the ARP effect from confounders (sex, location, etc.).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Single-center retrospective chart review following STROBE; IRB-approved (IRB073/06-18); waiver of consent for anonymous chart review.
- **Population**: Teeth extracted due to chronic periodontal pathology or combined endodontic–periodontal lesions.
  - *Periodontal indication*: mobility >2°, deepest probing depth >8 mm, apically narrowing marginal bone loss.
  - *Combined endo–perio*: radiographic periapical lesion >4 mm diameter + negative electric pulp test + the periodontal characteristics above.
- **Groups**: ARP defined as bone graft procedure performed immediately after extraction. ARP group used a resorbable membrane without flap elevation (secondary-intention healing); patients self-selected after being offered ARP. Graft materials included deproteinized bovine bone mineral (Bio-Oss) and others.
- **Outcomes**:
  - Primary: feasibility of implant placement (yes/no).
  - Secondary: horizontal bone augmentation, vertical bone augmentation, sinus floor elevation, total bone augmentation at implant placement, and implant failure before loading (implant removed before loading = failure).
- **Analysis**: Multivariable logistic regression. Sinus floor elevation analyzed only for maxillary posterior sites. P < 0.05 significant.
- **Screening flow**: 10,060 extraction patients screened → 574 eligible sites (277 no-ARP, 297 ARP) → final 418 sites that received implants (171 no-ARP, 247 ARP) in 287 patients.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Implant-placement infeasibility**: ARP **0.8%** vs no-ARP **4.7%** — significantly lower with ARP.
- **Horizontal and vertical bone augmentation**: significantly influenced by **location** and by **no ARP** (i.e., absence of ARP increased augmentation need).
- **Total bone augmentation**: significantly influenced by **sex, location, and no ARP**.
- **Safety (from authors' prior linked cohort)**: ARP in compromised sockets with thorough debridement + systemic antibiotics was safe — 2.7% infection rate, 0.7% reinfection requiring biomaterial removal.
- **Conclusion**: ARP in periodontally compromised sites may improve implant-placement feasibility and attenuate the severity of subsequent bone augmentation.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Retrospective, single-center, self-selected** groups → selection/indication bias (patients who agreed to ARP may differ systematically).
- **No CBCT at extraction** → the configuration/severity of pre-extraction bone destruction could not be characterized or matched between groups, a major potential confounder.
- Sites that never received an implant (e.g., restored elsewhere) were excluded, introducing attrition bias.
- Outcomes stop at implant placement / before loading — no long-term survival, marginal bone loss, or esthetic data.
- No randomization; causal inference limited. RCTs in infected sockets would strengthen the evidence.

## 6. Related Work

- Self-cites the authors' prior safety cohort on ARP in periodontally compromised sockets (infection/reinfection rates) as the source of the ARP group.
- Builds on classic post-extraction dimensional-change literature (Araujo, Tan, Schropp) that established ridge resorption ARP aims to mitigate.
- Relevant to ARP efficacy / over-treatment debate (Mardas 2023) and implant-failure-predictor work after ridge preservation (Kang 2025).

## 7. Glossary

- **ARP (Alveolar Ridge Preservation)**: Immediate guided bone regeneration following tooth extraction to minimize ridge resorption; here defined as bone graft immediately post-extraction.
- **Periodontally compromised socket**: Extraction site from a tooth lost to chronic periodontal or combined endo–perio disease (infected socket).
- **Implant-placement feasibility**: Whether an implant could be placed at the planned site without prohibitive bone deficiency.
- **Secondary-intention healing**: Open healing of the grafted socket under a resorbable membrane without flap closure.
- **Bone augmentation (horizontal/vertical/total)**: Additional grafting required at the time of implant surgery to compensate for residual bone deficiency.
