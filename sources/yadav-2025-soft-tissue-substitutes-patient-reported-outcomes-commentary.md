## One-line Summary

EBD commentary appraising a SR+MA (29 studies, 19 RCTs) finding that soft-tissue substitutes significantly reduce postoperative pain, analgesic consumption, and surgery time vs autogenous grafts in peri-implant soft tissue augmentation, with equivalent patient satisfaction and esthetics.

- **Appraised SR**: A systematic review and meta-analysis evaluating PROMs in peri-implant soft tissue augmentation, comparing soft-tissue substitutes vs autogenous grafts; databases searched up to November 22, 2021

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Synthesizes PROM data from 29 clinical studies (19 RCTs, 7 non-randomized studies, 3 case series) via 11 subgroup meta-analyses using random-effects models.
2. Quantifies significantly reduced **postoperative pain** (VAS) with substitutes vs SCTG for both mucosal thickness gain and keratinized tissue gain outcomes.
3. Demonstrates significantly lower **analgesic consumption** (~1.56 fewer tablets) and shorter **surgery time** (~10.9 fewer minutes) with substitutes.
4. Confirms that **patient satisfaction, esthetic perception, and quality of life** are statistically equivalent between substitutes and autogenous grafts.
5. Provides a critical methodological take: notes the SR's AMSTAR 2 appraisal, GRADE evidence certainty, and use of prediction intervals — key EBD quality markers.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Article type**: EBD commentary — this is a secondary appraisal document, not a primary study. The commentary summarizes and critically evaluates the methodology and results of an underlying SR+MA.

**Appraised SR design**:
- Databases: PubMed, Embase, CENTRAL, Web of Science, Epistemonikos + grey literature
- Search cutoff: November 22, 2021
- PICO: peri-implant soft tissue augmentation; intervention = soft tissue substitutes; comparator = autogenous grafts (primarily SCTG); outcomes = PROMs
- Study eligibility: RCTs, non-randomized studies, case series meeting PICO framework
- Data synthesis: Weighted mean difference (WMD) with 95% CI; 11 subgroup meta-analyses; random-effects models
- Quality tools referenced: AMSTAR 2 (SR quality), GRADE (evidence certainty), Cochrane Handbook methodology, prediction intervals (IntHout et al. 2016)

**PROMs evaluated**:
- Pain scores (VAS 0–100 and 0–10)
- Analgesic/painkiller consumption (number of tablets)
- Patient satisfaction (VAS)
- Aesthetic perception
- Surgery duration (minutes)
- Quality of life

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Comparison | n studies | WMD | 95% CI | P |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pain (0–100 VAS), mucosal thickness gain | Substitute vs SCTG | 4 | −14.91 units | −23.40 to −6.42 | 0.0006 |
| Pain (0–10 VAS), mucosal thickness gain | Substitute vs SCTG | 4 | −1.62 units | −3.23 to −0.01 | 0.05 |
| Pain (0–100 VAS), keratinized tissue gain | Substitute vs SCTG | 2 | −21.43 units | −30.28 to −12.58 | <0.0001 |
| Pain (0–10 VAS), keratinized tissue gain | Substitute vs SCTG | 4 | −1.65 units | −2.64 to −0.66 | 0.001 |
| Analgesic consumption | Substitute vs autograft | 6 | −1.56 tablets | −1.91 to −1.22 | <0.00001 |
| Surgery time | Substitute vs autograft | 5 | −10.9 min | −17.19 to −4.60 | <0.0001 |
| Patient satisfaction | Substitute vs autograft | — | NS | — | NS |
| Aesthetic perception | Substitute vs autograft | — | NS | — | NS |
| Quality of life | Substitute vs autograft | — | NS | — | NS |

All statistically significant differences favor soft-tissue substitutes (less pain, less analgesic use, shorter surgery). Non-significant outcomes indicate equivalence (not inferiority) for patient satisfaction, esthetics, and QoL.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

**Inherent to commentary format**: This is an EBD commentary (secondary appraisal), not a primary study or a new meta-analysis. It does not provide independent data and is limited to critically summarizing the appraised SR. Commentary authors cannot alter the included studies or SR methodology.

**Appraised SR limitations** (as noted in the commentary):
- SR search cutoff was November 2021 — evidence published since then is not captured.
- Only 2–6 studies per subgroup meta-analysis — small subgroup sizes reduce precision and generalizability.
- Heterogeneity in substitute materials (xenograft collagen matrices, allografts, synthetic matrices) is likely pooled, masking product-specific differences.
- Case series (n=3) included alongside RCTs, introducing variable evidence quality within the pool.
- Long-term PROM data beyond early postoperative period are sparse; satisfaction/esthetics at ≥12 months may differ.

**Future directions**: Head-to-head trials comparing specific substitute materials; longer PROM follow-up (≥12 months); standardized PROM measurement tools across studies.

## 7. Glossary

- **PROMs (Patient-Reported Outcome Measures)**: Outcomes reported directly by the patient without clinician interpretation — e.g., pain VAS, satisfaction, QoL.
- **SCTG (Subepithelial Connective Tissue Graft)**: Autogenous palatal connective tissue graft; gold standard comparator for soft tissue augmentation.
- **Soft tissue substitute**: Allogenic, xenogenic, or synthetic graft material replacing donor-site harvest — e.g., collagen matrix (CMX), acellular dermal matrix.
- **WMD (Weighted Mean Difference)**: Meta-analytic effect size for continuous outcomes, weighting each study by its inverse variance.
- **VAS (Visual Analog Scale)**: 0–10 or 0–100 patient self-reported pain/satisfaction scale.
- **AMSTAR 2**: Appraisal of Multiple Systematic Reviews, version 2 — quality assessment tool for SRs including RCTs and non-RCTs.
- **GRADE**: Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation — framework for evidence certainty (high/moderate/low/very low).
- **Random-effects model**: Meta-analytic model assuming true effect sizes vary across studies; appropriate when between-study heterogeneity is expected.
- **EBD Commentary**: Evidence-Based Dentistry journal format — structured critical appraisal of a recently published systematic review for a clinical audience.
