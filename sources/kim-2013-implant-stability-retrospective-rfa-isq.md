## Why Ingested

기존 [[wiki/implants/isq/]] 카테고리에 직경·위치별 ISQ 기저값 데이터가 부족한 상태였음. 본 후향적 연구(Kim 2013)는 국내 Osstem US II plus 임플란트 90개를 대상으로 식립 직후 및 인상채득 시점의 ISQ를 직경(4 mm vs 5 mm)·길이(10/11.5/13 mm)·위치(상악 vs 하악)별로 분석하여, 직경과 위치가 ISQ에 미치는 영향을 실증한다. 이는 [[wiki/implants/isq/]] 내 하중 시기 결정 근거 페이지들의 "diameter effect" 항목을 보강하는 데 활용된다.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective RFA study (n=90 implants, 72 patients) showing wider diameter (5 mm > 4 mm) and mandibular placement yield significantly higher ISQ, while implant length (10–13 mm) does not significantly affect stability.

## 한줄요약

후향적 RFA 연구(90개 임플란트, 72명): 직경 5 mm가 4 mm보다 ISQ 유의하게 높고, 하악이 상악보다 ISQ 유의하게 높으나, 길이(10–13 mm)는 ISQ에 유의한 영향 없음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: 대한치과보철학회지 (Journal of Korean Academy of Prosthodontics), Vol. 51, No. 4, 2013, pp. 269–275
- **DOI**: 10.4047/jkap.2013.51.4.269
- **Study type**: Retrospective cohort / chart review
- **Institution**: Department of Prosthodontics, School of Dentistry, Chosun University, Gwangju, Korea
- **Implant system**: Osstem US II plus (external hex, RBM surface, straight body), ø4 mm and ø5 mm, lengths 10/11.5/13 mm
- **Device**: Osstell Mentor (Smartpeg) for RFA measurement

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Confirms that implant **diameter** significantly affects ISQ: ø5 mm implants show higher ISQ than ø4 mm at both surgery and impression taking (P<.05).
2. Demonstrates **jaw position** effect: mandibular implants show significantly higher ISQ than maxillary implants (P<.05), consistent with the cortical bone density difference.
3. Finds **implant length** (10, 11.5, 13 mm) does NOT significantly affect ISQ values (P>.05) — stability plateau at this length range.
4. ISQ values at impression taking are **consistently higher** than at surgery across all subgroups, confirming progressive osseointegration between placement and loading time.
5. Provides normative ISQ reference data for a Korean clinic using a domestic implant system under a single experienced operator, reducing inter-operator bias.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Study design**: Retrospective chart review at a single private practice (대전 뿌리깊은 치과의원), 2007–2011.

**Sample**: 72 patients (41 male, 31 female; mean age 45.3 years, range 22–65), 90 Osstem US II plus implants placed in anterior and posterior regions, maxilla and mandible. Six subgroups (n=15 each): ø4 mm × 10/11.5/13 mm; ø5 mm × 10/11.5/13 mm.

**Exclusion criteria**: Uncontrolled diabetes or systemic disease, heavy smokers (≥20 cigarettes/day), poor oral hygiene, periodontal disease, parafunction (bruxism), augmentation procedures, immediate implants, fully edentulous patients.

**RFA measurement**:
- Smartpeg (matched to implant type) connected to fixture
- Osstell Mentor measured buccal and palatal/lingual directions, 2 readings each → mean ISQ
- Measured at T1 (implant placement) and T2 (impression taking)

**Loading timing**: Based on T1 ISQ; most implants showed ISQ ≥65, enabling early loading. Mandible: ~8 weeks; Maxilla: ~12 weeks.

**Statistics**: SPSS 21.0; Independent t-test (gender comparison); One-way ANOVA + Tukey HSD (length, diameter, position comparisons); Kolmogorov-Smirnov normality check + paired t-test (T1 vs T2 within groups). α=0.05 throughout.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Variable | Finding | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| Gender | No difference in ISQ (M: 77.80/81.12; F: 79.72/82.33 at T1/T2) | P>.05 |
| Length (ø4 mm) | 10 mm: 74.33→77.43; 11.5 mm: 76.03→79.33; 13 mm: 77.47→80.07 | P>.05 (NS) |
| Length (ø5 mm) | 10 mm: 79.27→82.33; 11.5 mm: 81.07→84.97; 13 mm: 83.90→85.90 | P>.05 (NS) |
| Diameter (10 mm) | ø4 mm: 74.33→77.43; ø5 mm: 79.26→82.33 | P<.05 (sig.) |
| Position | Maxilla: 75.82→79.25 (n=36); Mandible: 80.58→83.29 (n=54) | P<.05 (sig.) |
| T1 vs T2 | All groups: ISQ higher at impression than at surgery | P<.05 |

**Key threshold reference**: Most implants showed ISQ ≥65 at placement, used as criterion for early loading (1–2 months earlier than conventional protocol).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-center retrospective design; single implant brand and surface treatment (RBM).
- Small per-group n (15 per subgroup) limits power for subgroup comparisons.
- No long-term follow-up; only two-point ISQ measurement (T1 and T2) — the stability dip phenomenon (ISQ decrease followed by recovery) cannot be evaluated.
- No bone quality/density grading (e.g., Misch D1–D4 or HU from CBCT), limiting regression analysis of confounders.
- Length range 10–13 mm; shorter implants (<10 mm) not included — cannot generalize to short implants.
- Loading time was guided by ISQ ≥65 but exact loading protocol varied per case.

## 6. Related Work

- Meredith (1998): original RFA method for implant stability measurement.
- Bischof et al.: position (jaw) and bone quality drive ISQ; length and diameter do not — contrasted by this study's diameter finding.
- Han et al.: diameter not significant for ISQ — contradicts this study.
- Kim et al. (2009): diameter affects ISQ change rate at 3 months, length does not — consistent with this study.
- Langer et al.; Polizzi et al.: larger diameter → higher ISQ — consistent with this study.

## 7. Glossary

- **RFA (Resonance Frequency Analysis, 공진주파수분석법)**: Non-invasive method using resonance frequency to measure implant-bone interface stiffness; expressed as ISQ.
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient, 임플란트 안정성 지수)**: Scale 1–100 reflecting stiffness of implant-bone construct; higher = more stable.
- **일차안정성 (Primary stability)**: Mechanical fixation immediately post-placement by cortical bone contact; before biological osseointegration.
- **이차안정성 (Secondary stability)**: Biological stability gained through woven and lamellar bone formation during osseointegration.
- **Smartpeg**: Disposable transducer peg screwed to implant for Osstell measurements.
- **Osstell Mentor**: Handheld RFA device using magnetic resonance principle.
- **RBM (Resorbable Blast Media) surface**: Surface treatment on Osstem US II plus implants using resorbable particles to create micro-roughness.
- **Early loading (조기 하중)**: Loading 1–2 months earlier than conventional protocol, enabled when ISQ ≥65 at surgery.
