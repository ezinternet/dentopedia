## One-line Summary
Meta-analysis of 64 RCTs finds glass ionomer cement (GIC) restorations are superior to amalgam and equivalent to resin-composite in preventing secondary caries, in both primary and permanent dentition.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Dental Materials 39 (2023) e1–e17
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.dental.2023.10.008
- **PROSPERO**: CRD42022380959
- **Institutions**: University of Hong Kong; UT Health Science Center at Houston
- **Search**: PubMed, Web of Science, Cochrane, Scopus (last search 3 Jan 2023)
- **Included studies**: 64 RCTs (8,310 GIC restorations vs 5,857 amalgam/composite), 1–10 year follow-up

## 2. Key Contributions
- First contemporary meta-analysis quantifying GIC's preventive effect on secondary caries vs amalgam and composite, stratified by dentition
- Strong evidence that GIC outperforms amalgam in both primary (RR 0.55) and permanent (RR 0.20) teeth — challenging the legacy assumption that amalgam's antimicrobial metals confer caries resistance
- Confirms equivalence of GIC vs composite for secondary caries — supports GIC as a viable choice when fluoride release/chemical bonding is desired
- No significant difference between conventional GIC and resin-modified GIC for secondary caries prevention
- Aligns with WHO 2021 listing of GIC as essential medicine

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: SR + meta-analysis, PRISMA-compliant
- **PICOS**: P=all ages; I=GIC (cGIC or RMGIC) restorations; C=amalgam or resin-composite; O=secondary caries incidence; S=RCTs
- **Exclusion**: in vitro, animal, reviews, case reports, retrospective/observational, non-restorative GIC use (sealant/liner/cement), studies lacking secondary caries incidence
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane Handbook 5.4.1 (6 domains)
- **Analysis**: RevMan 5.4, fixed-effects, RR with 95% CI, I² heterogeneity, p<0.05 significant
- **Follow-up stratification**: short (1y), medium (2–4y), long (≥5y)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
| Comparison | Dentition | n studies | RR | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| GIC vs amalgam | Primary | — | 0.55 | 0.41–0.72 |
| GIC vs amalgam | Permanent | — | 0.20 | 0.11–0.38 |
| GIC vs composite | Primary | — | 0.92 | 0.77–1.10 |
| GIC vs composite | Permanent | — | 0.77 | 0.39–1.51 |
| cGIC vs RMGIC | Primary | — | 1.12 | 0.67–1.87 |
| cGIC vs RMGIC | Permanent | — | 1.63 | 0.34–7.84 |

- Meta-analysis split: 21 studies (4,807 restorations) primary; 38 studies (4,885 restorations) permanent
- 5 studies excluded from meta-analysis due to non-standard outcome criteria (Danish PDHS, radiograph-only, FDI)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- High heterogeneity across studies (operators, isolation protocols, material brands, caries diagnostic criteria)
- Follow-up periods variable (1–10 years); long-term comparative data still limited
- Risk-of-bias issues in older RCTs (blinding, allocation concealment)
- Patient caries risk (high-caries-risk populations) may modify the effect; meta-analysis did not stratify by caries risk
- Confounding from operator skill, isolation, and occlusal load not controlled

## 6. Related Work
- Builds on prior meta-analyses by Mickenautsch and Yengopal showing GIC anti-cariogenic effect; this update incorporates contemporary RMGIC products and revised CONSORT-era RCTs
- Aligns with Worthington 2021 Cochrane review on composite vs amalgam (broader composite vs amalgam comparison)

## 7. Glossary
- **Secondary caries (recurrent caries)**: Caries lesion adjacent to existing restoration margin; responsible for ~60% of restoration failure
- **GIC (Glass Ionomer Cement, 글래스아이오노머시멘트)**: Restorative material releasing fluoride; chemical bond to hydroxyapatite via polyalkenoate anions
- **RMGIC (Resin-Modified Glass Ionomer Cement)**: GIC with HEMA/resin monomers + photoinitiators; light-curable
- **RR (Risk Ratio)**: Ratio of event probabilities between groups
- **PROSPERO**: International prospective register of systematic reviews
