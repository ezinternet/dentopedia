## One-line Summary
즉시식립(Immediate Implant, Type-1) 시 골·연조직 이식 병용 효과 SR+MA (J Clin Med 2024): 임플란트 안정성 지수(Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ), 골소실, 치은후퇴, 핑크 심미지수(Pink Esthetic Score, PES) 평가.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Medicine 2024, 13, 821
- **DOI**: 10.3390/jcm13030821
- **Published**: 31 January 2024
- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis

## 2. Key Contributions
- Evaluated efficacy of hard and/or soft tissue grafts combined with type-1 (immediate) implant placement on healing and outcomes
- PICO strategy: patients requiring type-1 implants; tissue grafts (hard/soft/combined) vs no graft; multiple outcomes
- **Primary outcomes**: implant survival rate, pocket depth, marginal peri-implant recession, bone loss, volumetric bone change, interproximal bone level, papilla migration, radiographic evaluation
- **Secondary outcomes**: Pink Esthetic Score (PES), ISQ, Visual Analogue Score (VAS), biological complications (fistula, pain, mucositis, peri-implantitis)

## 3. Methodology
- **Design**: SR+MA following PICO
- **Setting**: Universidade Católica Portuguesa, Viseu, Portugal
- **Inclusion**: Clinical studies on immediate (type-1) implant placement with tissue grafts; reporting at least one defined outcome
- **Meta-analysis**: Conducted for outcomes with sufficient data (ISQ, bone loss, recession, PES)

## 4. Key Results
- Tissue grafts (hard or soft) associated with:
  - Improved ISQ values (greater primary stability) vs non-grafted controls
  - Reduced marginal peri-implant recession
  - Better volumetric bone preservation
  - Improved PES (esthetic outcomes)
- Bone loss and biological complications: variable across studies; grafting generally favorable but heterogeneity limits conclusions
- Combined hard + soft grafts showed strongest benefits for esthetic outcomes (PES)

## 5. Limitations
- High heterogeneity in graft material type (xenograft, allograft, autograft, connective tissue, collagen matrix)
- Short-term follow-up in many included studies
- Varying protocols for gap filling, flap management, and provisional restoration
- Selection bias: immediate implant cases may represent favorable anatomy
