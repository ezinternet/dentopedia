## Why Ingested

Fills a 5-year currency gap against the older epidemiology anchor [[wiki/implants/derks-2015-peri-implant-health-disease-epidemiology]]: a 57-study SR+MA (search window through Dec 2021) that re-estimates peri-implantitis prevalence and quantifies how case definition, probing-depth use, and function time move the number. It refines, not overturns, the older anchor by showing the estimate is still highly definition-dependent.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 57 observational/experimental studies: peri-implantitis prevalence is 19.53% (95% CI 12.87–26.19) at the patient level and 12.53% (95% CI 11.67–13.39) at the implant level, with the rate inflated when probing depth is used as a diagnostic criterion.

## 한줄요약

57편 관찰·실험 연구 SR+MA: 임플란트주위염 유병률은 환자 단위 19.53% (95% CI 12.87–26.19), 임플란트 단위 12.53%이며, 탐침 깊이(probing depth)를 진단 기준에 포함하면 유병률이 더 높게 나옴.

## 1. Document Information

- Journal: BMC Oral Health (2022) 22:449
- Design: Systematic review + meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD42022313472), PRISMA-guided
- Search window: 1 Dec 2005 – 31 Dec 2021; MEDLINE/PubMed, Web of Science, Science Direct, Cochrane
- 57 articles included (18 cross-sectional, 18 longitudinal, 1 case–control, 17 cohort, 3 RCTs)
- Inclusion: ≥10 implant subjects, ≥5-year function time, peer-reviewed English
- Risk of bias: Cochrane tool; high-risk papers rejected

## 2. Key Contributions

- Pooled patient-level prevalence 19.53% (95% CI 12.87–26.19); implant-level 12.53% (95% CI 11.67–13.39).
- Meta-analysis of the largest definition subgroup (Group 2: BOP + PD ≥6 mm + BL ≥2 mm, 31 articles): random-effects patient-level prevalence 20.0% (95% CI 16.6–23.7); fixed-effects 19.6% (95% CI 18.4–20.8); quality-effects 19.2% (95% CI 15.2–23.6).
- Demonstrates that including probing depth as a criterion raises prevalence (patient-level 24.69% with PD vs 17.56% without; implant-level 15.21% vs 11.99%), though differences were not statistically significant (p = 0.27 / p = 0.31).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Studies grouped into four definition buckets: Group 1 (BOP + PD ≥6 mm + bone loss ≥3 mm), Group 2 (BOP + PD ≥6 mm + BL ≥2 mm), Group 3 (progressive bone loss), Group 4 (other). Meta-analysis run on Group 2 via MetaXL. Heterogeneity by I²; publication bias by Egger regression (p ≥ 0.1) + Doi plot; sensitivity analyses by leave-one-out. Time-variable subanalysis split studies by function 5–9 y vs longer.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Patient-level 19.53%; implant-level 12.53%.
- Function time made no significant difference: patient-level 17.1% (5–9 y) vs 18.63% (longer), p = 0.82; implant-level 10.98% vs 9.76%, p = 0.8.
- Probing-depth use inflated prevalence but not significantly.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

High residual heterogeneity persists even after restricting to a single clinical case definition; convenience sampling and selection bias common in primary studies; authors stress the unmet need to identify diagnostic markers for accurate disease classification.

## 6. Related Work

Builds on the 2017 World Workshop classification (BOP/suppuration + PD ≥6 mm + bone loss ≥3 mm) and prior prevalence estimates (14.38–24.27%). Reinforces the older epidemiology anchor (Derks & Tomasi 2015).

## 7. Glossary

- **Peri-implantitis**: destructive polymicrobial inflammatory lesion causing progressive peri-implant bone loss.
- **Patient-level prevalence**: % of patients with ≥1 affected implant.
- **Implant-level prevalence**: % of implants affected.
- **BOP**: bleeding on probing. **PD**: probing depth.
