## Why Ingested

기존 [[wiki/implants/versah-protocols/rittipakorn-2025-clockwise-osseodensification-primary-stability-cadaveric]]는 저밀도골에서 1차 안정성을 끌어올리는 *수술 기법*(시계방향 골밀도화 드릴링) 축을 다룬다. 본 in vitro study는 같은 저밀도골 문제를 *임플란트 매크로지오메트리*(나사 디자인·테이퍼·self-cutting) 축에서 보완 — 1차 안정성 결정인자의 다른 한 축을 채워 ISQ/IT threshold 논의를 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

In vitro polyurethane-block study (36 implants, 3 macro-geometries × bone types 3 and 4): implant geometry and bone density were the key determinants of primary stability — triple-thread Zimmer TSV gave the highest insertion/removal torque, while double-thread deeper-thread Dentium Superline gave the highest ISQ, in both low-density bone types.

## 한줄요약

폴리우레탄 블록 in vitro 연구(임플란트 36개, 매크로지오메트리 3종 × 골질 3·4형): 1차 안정성은 임플란트 형태와 골밀도가 핵심 결정인자였고, triple-thread Zimmer TSV가 삽입/제거 토크 최고, 깊은 double-thread Dentium Superline이 ISQ 최고였다(두 저밀도골 모두).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Directly compares three commercial implant macro-geometries (Straumann BLT, Zimmer TSV, Dentium Superline) under controlled drilling in standardized low-density synthetic bone (types 3 and 4), isolating thread/body design as the variable.
- Quantifies primary stability with three complementary metrics: ISQ (RFA), insertion torque (IT), and removal torque (RT).
- Shows that the "best" implant depends on the metric: triple-thread/small-pitch geometry maximizes torque, whereas double-thread/deep-thread geometry maximizes ISQ.
- Confirms a strong positive correlation between bone density and every stability metric across all three designs.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Implants**: 36 total, 12 per group. All tapered body, length 10 mm, diameter 4.0–4.1 mm. Each manufacturer-recommended for low-density bone / immediate loading.
  - **Straumann BLT (SBLT)**: single thread, reverse-buttress shape, pitch 0.8 mm, depth 0.3 mm, apical Ø 1.8 mm, self-cutting, no micro-thread.
  - **Dentium Superline (DSL)**: double thread, reverse-buttress shape, pitch 1.8 mm, depth 0.40 mm, apical Ø 3.8 mm, self-cutting (half-apical blade), no micro-thread.
  - **Zimmer TSV (ZTSV)**: triple thread, V-shape, pitch 0.6 mm, depth 0.36 mm, apical Ø 3.5 mm, micro-thread, not self-cutting.
- **Synthetic bone**: rigid polyurethane blocks (SYNBONE, Malaysia), 5×5×5 cm, per ASTM F-1839-08. 20 PCF (0.35 g/cm³) = bone type 3; 15 PCF (0.24 g/cm³) = bone type 4.
- **Procedure**: controlled drilling/testing machine; implants placed per manufacturer protocol by one clinician. IT (max clockwise force causing bone stripping) and RT measured on a Hung Ta testing machine. ISQ via RFA with MultiPeg (Osseo 100+, NSK), measured twice (buccal + mesial) and averaged.
- **Stats**: sample size from Soror (effect size f=0.57, α=0.05, power 80% → 33, +10% → 36; G*Power 3.1). IT and RT by one-way ANOVA; ISQ by Kruskal-Wallis with post hoc; SPSS 22; significance p<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Null hypothesis (no difference across geometries) was **rejected**; all metrics differed significantly (p<0.001).

**Bone type 3 (20 PCF), mean ± SD:**
| Metric | SBLT | ZTSV | DSL |
|---|---|---|---|
| IT (Ncm) | 26.50 ± 2.36 | **30.21 ± 1.38** | 22.90 ± 1.60 |
| RT (Ncm) | 21.43 ± 1.59 | **23.25 ± 2.30** | 14.23 ± 2.33 |
| ISQ | 61.00 ± 0.52 | 60.88 ± 0.38 | **63.29 ± 0.54** |

**Bone type 4 (15 PCF), mean ± SD:**
| Metric | SBLT | ZTSV | DSL |
|---|---|---|---|
| IT (Ncm) | 10.93 ± 0.34 | **18.07 ± 1.71** | 10.37 ± 1.15 |
| RT (Ncm) | 8.86 ± 1.02 | **14.48 ± 1.81** | 6.34 ± 0.74 |
| ISQ | 57.33 ± 1.05 | 55.70 ± 0.58 | **58.46 ± 0.78** |

- **Ordering**: IT and RT → ZTSV > SBLT > DSL. ISQ → DSL > SBLT > ZTSV (both bone types).
- **Non-significant post-hoc pairs**: IT SBLT vs DSL (type 4, p=0.337); RT SBLT vs ZTSV (type 3, p=0.126); ISQ SBLT vs ZTSV (type 3, p=0.270). All other pairs significant.
- **Density correlation (R, stability vs bone density)**: IT 0.943–0.959, RT 0.830–0.960, ISQ 0.842–0.968 — strong positive across all designs (higher density → higher stability).
- **Mechanistic interpretation**: ZTSV high torque attributed to triple thread + small pitch (larger contact area, mechanical interlocking, higher insertion resistance). DSL high ISQ attributed to deep (0.40 mm) double thread increasing functional surface area / bone contact. DSL low RT linked to half-apical self-cutting blade. Optimal IT in low-density bone cited as ~30–35 Ncm.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Polyurethane does not replicate biological bone (no remodeling, osteocytes, vessels, mineralized matrix); homogeneous and isotropic.
- Clinical variables not captured: cortical thickness, anatomical variation, surgical technique, surgeon experience, patient systemic factors (e.g., osteoporosis).
- Only **primary** stability assessed; secondary stability and cellular/osseointegration responses not evaluated.
- Clinical implications (immediate/early loading) are exploratory; authors stress findings cannot be generalized until clinical trials validate them.

## 6. Related Work

- Tapered > cylindrical primary stability in low-density bone (Supachaiyakit; in vitro/clinical literature).
- Deeper threads → higher ISQ / primary stability (Reinaldo, Cucinelli, Menini).
- Smaller pitch / larger surface area → higher IT (Yamaguchi, Soror, Benalcazar-Jalkh).
- Self-cutting half-apical blade → lower RT (Tumedei); self-cutting designs recommended for osteoporotic/type-4 bone (Falco).
- V-shaped vs square thread biomechanics and FEA stress distribution; Barbosa RCT (V-shaped better than square); Olmedo-Gaya (under-preparation → higher IT/ISQ in posterior maxilla).

## 7. Glossary

- **Primary stability**: mechanical engagement between implant and bone walls at placement (vs secondary stability from healing/new bone).
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: 0–100 RFA-derived stiffness index of implant–bone interface.
- **IT / RT (Insertion / Removal Torque)**: peak clockwise force to seat the implant / force to unscrew it (Ncm).
- **Macro-geometry**: implant body shape and thread design (taper, pitch, depth, number of threads, thread profile, self-cutting features).
- **PCF**: pounds per cubic foot — density rating of polyurethane test blocks (20 PCF ≈ type 3, 15 PCF ≈ type 4).
- **Bone types 3/4**: progressively less dense (more cancellous) bone, typical of posterior maxilla.
