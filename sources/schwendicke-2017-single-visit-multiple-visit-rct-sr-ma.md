## Why Ingested

근관치료 1회법 vs 다회법 비교에서 장기 합병증, 술후 통증, flare-up 위험도를 동시에 분석한 SR+MA+TSA(시험순차분석)가 필요했음. 특히 [[endodontics/irrigation/ordinola-zapata-2022-intracanal-medicaments-status-directions]]에서 근관내 medicament(수산화칼슘) 사용 근거를 보완하는 임상 outcome 데이터로 활용되며, 이 SR이 해당 논쟁의 핵심 기반 문헌임.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA+TSA of 29 RCTs (4,341 patients): single-visit RCT did not increase long-term complications or postoperative pain vs multiple-visit, but significantly raised flare-up risk (RR 2.13; 95% CI 1.16–3.89), though all evidence was weak-to-moderate and firm TSA conclusions were not reached.

## 한줄요약

29개 RCT(4,341명) SR+MA+TSA: 1회법과 다회법 간 장기 합병증·술후 통증 차이 없음; 단, 1회법의 flare-up 위험 유의하게 높음(RR 2.13)—전반적 근거 수준 낮음-보통, TSA 기준 확정 결론 미도달.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First SR+MA to apply **Trial Sequential Analysis (TSA)** to the single-visit vs multiple-visit RCT evidence base — separates spuriously crossed significance thresholds from true firm evidence.
2. Demonstrates that **long-term complications** (≥1 year: pain, infection, periapical lesion change) are equivalent between strategies (RR 1.00; 95% CI 0.75–1.35; weak evidence; n=1,257 teeth, 10 trials).
3. **Postoperative pain** is also equivalent (RR 0.99; 95% CI 0.76–1.30; moderate evidence; n=3,008 teeth, 20 trials).
4. **Flare-up** is significantly higher with single-visit (RR 2.13; 95% CI 1.16–3.89; very weak evidence; n=1,110 teeth, 8 trials) — but a single high-incidence study drives the estimate; excluding it renders the finding non-significant (RR 1.85; 0.89–3.86).
5. Meta-regression shows preoperative pulp status (vital/non-vital), pain, periapical lesion, instrumentation type, and intracanal medication do **not** significantly moderate any outcome.
6. GRADE: long-term complications = weak; pain = moderate; flare-up = very weak.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search**: PubMed/MEDLINE, EMBASE, Cochrane Central + opengrey.eu; search date March 10, 2016; no date or language restrictions.
- **Eligibility**: RCTs or controlled trials without selection bias; permanent teeth with closed apices; must report ≥1 year complications and/or short-term pain and/or flare-up.
- **Exclusions**: primary teeth, internal resorption, open apices.
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane RoB tool; 23 of 29 trials had high risk of bias; only 6 low risk.
- **Statistical model**: Random-effects (DerSimonian-Laird); continuity correction +1 for zero events; RR + 95% CI.
- **TSA**: Diversity-adjusted required information size (DARIS) calculated for each outcome; conventional and TSA monitoring boundaries plotted.
- **Publication bias**: Funnel plots + Egger's test; trim-and-fill for flare-up.
- **Subgroup/meta-regression**: vitality, pain, periapical lesion, medication, instrumentation type.

### Outcome definitions
| Outcome | Definition |
|---|---|
| Long-term complications | Pain, infection/swelling/sinus track, periapical lesion change ≥1 year |
| Short-term pain | Any pain <1 year (continuous or binary; extracted at shortest post-treatment timepoint after obturation) |
| Flare-up | Acute exacerbation <1 year leading to further intervention (reopening, I&D, retreatment) |

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Studies (n) | Teeth | RR (95% CI) | I² | GRADE |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Long-term complications | 10 | 1,257 | 1.00 (0.75–1.35) | Low | Weak |
| Postoperative pain | 20 | 3,008 | 0.99 (0.76–1.30) | Low | Moderate |
| Flare-up | 8 | 1,110 | 2.13 (1.16–3.89) | Low | Very weak |

- TSA: DARIS not reached for any outcome; Z-curve did not cross firm superiority, inferiority, or futility boundaries.
- Sensitivity (flare-up, excluding highest-incidence study): RR 1.85 (0.89–3.86) — non-significant.
- Publication bias suspected for flare-up (funnel plot asymmetry); Egger's test p=0.26 (non-significant).
- Calcium hydroxide intracanal medication vs none: no significant moderating effect on any outcome.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

1. **High risk of bias in 23/29 trials** — sequence generation, allocation concealment, assessor blinding commonly not reported; limits GRADE ratings.
2. **Flare-up driven by one study** (20% single-visit rate vs ~5–10% in others); removing it eliminates significance — very weak foundation.
3. **Composite long-term outcome** mixes radiographic, clinical, and symptomatic components with different retreatment thresholds for clinicians vs patients.
4. **Pain outcome heterogeneity** introduced by pooling different scales, timepoints, and definitions.
5. **TSA underpowered**: DARIS was not reached for any outcome — firm conclusions (benefit, harm, or futility) require more trials.
6. **External validity**: most trials from specialist/academic settings; primary care performance may differ.
7. **Future trials recommended**: higher internal validity, longer follow-up, primary care settings, preoperative condition stratification.

## 6. Related Work

- Wong et al. (previous SR, 2014) — pooled short-term and long-term outcomes without separation; outdated.
- Figini et al. (SR, 2008) — similar conclusions; no TSA.
- Sathorn et al. (SR, 2005/2008) — focused on pain only.
- Ordinola-Zapata et al. 2022 — intracanal medicaments review; complements the medication-effect null finding here.

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Flare-up (근관치료 후 급성 악화) | Acute exacerbation of pulpal/periapical pathosis after treatment initiation, typically severe pain/swelling requiring further intervention |
| TSA (Trial Sequential Analysis, 시험순차분석) | Cumulative meta-analysis method applying monitoring boundaries to control type I/II errors from repeated testing as trials accumulate |
| DARIS (Diversity-Adjusted Required Information Size) | Sample size needed to reliably detect the expected treatment effect in a meta-analysis, accounting for between-study heterogeneity |
| GRADE | Evidence grading framework: very weak / weak / moderate / strong |
| RR (Risk Ratio, 위험비) | Ratio of complication risk in single-visit group vs multiple-visit group |
| Intracanal medication (근관내 약제) | Temporary antimicrobial dressing (typically calcium hydroxide) placed between visits in multiple-visit RCT |
