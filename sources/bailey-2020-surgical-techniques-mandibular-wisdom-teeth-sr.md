## One-line Summary
Cochrane systematic review comparing surgical techniques for mandibular wisdom tooth removal, including flap design, lingual nerve protection, and bur vs. chisel techniques, across multiple RCT outcomes (alveolar osteitis, infection, nerve injury, pain, swelling, trismus).

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews 2020, Issue 7, Art. No.: CD004345
- **DOI**: 10.1002/14651858.CD004345.pub3
- **Study type**: Cochrane systematic review (3rd edition)
- **Institution**: Cochrane Collaboration

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive comparison of multiple surgical technique variables for mandibular wisdom tooth extraction
- Outcomes assessed: alveolar osteitis (dry socket), wound infection, permanent altered sensation (nerve injury), wound dehiscence, pain, swelling, trismus, maximum mouth opening
- Covers: flap type, lingual nerve protection, bur vs. chisel for bone removal, tooth sectioning techniques

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Cochrane methodology with systematic database searches
- Included only randomized controlled trials (RCTs)
- Comparisons structured by technique variable:
  - Comparison 1: Surgical flap type (A versus B)
  - Comparison 2: Lingual nerve protection versus no protection
  - Additional comparisons: bone removal technique, tooth sectioning, wound closure
- Outcomes at 7 days (alveolar osteitis, wound infection) and longer term (permanent nerve changes >6 months)
- Risk of bias assessment for all included trials

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Multiple flap design comparisons evaluated for alveolar osteitis, wound infection, wound dehiscence, and pain at 24 hours, swelling and trismus at 1 week
- Lingual nerve protection: assessed for permanent altered tongue sensation up to 6 months
- Results presented as forest plots and summary of findings tables across comparisons
- Alveolar osteitis (dry socket) used as primary infection outcome at 7 days
- Permanent altered tongue sensation measured at >6 months for nerve injury assessment

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- High heterogeneity across RCTs in surgical technique definitions and outcome measurement
- Many comparisons have limited number of studies
- Low certainty of evidence for most comparisons due to risk of bias in included trials
- Need for well-designed RCTs with standardized outcome reporting

## 7. Glossary
- **Alveolar osteitis (dry socket)**: Painful postoperative complication from premature blood clot loss; primary infectious outcome measure
- **Lingual nerve protection**: Use of a lingual retractor to reduce risk of lingual nerve injury during bone removal
- **Flap design**: Surgical incision pattern to access impacted tooth (triangular, envelope, modified envelope, etc.)
- **Permanent altered sensation**: Nerve injury lasting >6 months; includes lingual nerve and inferior alveolar nerve
- **Trismus**: Restricted mouth opening post-surgery
