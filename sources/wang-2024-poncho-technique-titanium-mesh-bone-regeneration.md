## Why Ingested

Part of the flap-design / tension-free primary closure collection, specifically the titanium-mesh soft-tissue management subgroup. The modified "Poncho" technique (MPT) is a major-advancement flap for closure over customized Ti-mesh, the direct conceptual sibling of the coronally advanced lingual flap RCT [[suture-wound-closure/attia-2023-coronally-advanced-lingual-flap-titanium-mesh]] and an instantiation of the "major advancement (≥7 mm)" stratum framed in [[suture-wound-closure/plonka-2017-flap-designs-flap-advancement-implant-therapy]]. It also complements the periosteal-release approach in [[suture-wound-closure/moslemi-2016-periosteal-releasing-incision-diode-laser-gbr]].

## One-line Summary

Case series (5 patients, 23 surgical sites, severe atrophic ridges with customized titanium mesh) showing the modified "Poncho" technique (MPT) achieved uneventful soft-tissue healing at 95.7% of sites (1/23 partial dehiscence) with Landry's healing index rising from 3 ± 0.47 to 4.69 ± 1.06 over 30 days.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Introduces the modified "Poncho" technique (MPT), a flap design for tension-free passive primary closure when major flap elevation is required over a digitally customized titanium mesh in bone regeneration.
- Provides a step-by-step protocol for major flap advancement and a double-suture closure strategy.
- Reports early-stage (3, 7, 14, 30 day) healing quality using Landry's healing index (LHI).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Cohort: 5 patients undergoing customized bone regeneration (CBR) with customized titanium mesh; 23 surgical sites analyzed.
- Inclusion: atrophic ridges (horizontal, vertical, or mixed defects), residual bone <6 mm height and <4 mm width, needing staged implant-supported rehabilitation; good general/oral health.
- Exclusion: non-customized-mesh augmentation, uncontrolled diabetes (HbA1c ≥7.5%), heavy smokers (>10 cig/d), alcohol/chronic drug abuse, immunosuppression, bisphosphonate history, recent local radiation, uncontrolled local infection.
- MPT steps: (1) buccal initial superficial horizontal incision beyond MGJ; (2) buccal vertical + palatal extension (45° vertical toward adjacent teeth, sulcus incision buccal-mesial to palatal-mesial); (3) strategic buccal vertical-releasing incisions (hokey/hock-rod shape preserving papilla; ≥2 teeth beyond target in partial edentulism, ≥15 mm in full edentulism); (4) oblique incision through muscular-submucosal layer ≥10 mm then periosteum, blunt dissection separating deep submucosal layer (~10–20 mm); (5) palatal full-thickness mucoperiosteal flap elevation; (6) GBR with bone graft + customized mesh + resorbable membrane; (7) double-suture closure (first line: submucosal intermediate layer to innermost palatal flap, horizontal mattress 4-0 polyglactin; second line: mucosal flap to beveled palatal flap, interrupted 5-0 polyglactin), with cross-linked hyaluronic acid (xHyA) referenced.
- Outcomes: complications (flap dehiscence, mesh exposure, infection, necrosis) and Landry's healing index at 3, 7, 14, 30 days postop.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- 95.7% of surgical sites experienced uneventful soft-tissue healing during the observation period.
- Only 1 of 23 sites exhibited partial wound dehiscence at postsurgical days 14 and 30, with mild inflammation.
- Landry's healing index increased from 3 ± 0.47 to a final value of 4.69 ± 1.06, indicating substantial healing improvement over time.
- Conclusion: MPT shows promise for passive, predictable primary wound closure beneath a digitally customized titanium mesh, with high successful-healing rate and minimal early complications.
- Context: documented Ti-mesh exposure complication rates of 15.2–23.9% in the literature provide the comparator baseline.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Very small patient cohort (n=5; 23 sites), single-arm case series, no control group.
- Short observation window (30 days) focused on early soft-tissue healing; no bone-gain, implant-survival, or long-term outcomes reported.
- Technique-sensitive, expertise-dependent; generalizability limited.

## 6. Related Work

- Prior "poncho" technique experience by the same team on patient-specific titanium mesh GBR (referenced as antecedent).
- Conventional advancement methods (vertical releasing incisions, periosteal scoring, double-flap incision) cited as limited in severe atrophy.

## 7. Glossary

- MPT: Modified "Poncho" Technique — flap design for major advancement and tension-free closure over customized Ti-mesh.
- CBR: Customized Bone Regeneration.
- GBR: Guided Bone Regeneration.
- LHI: Landry's Healing Index — clinical wound-healing score.
- xHyA: cross-linked Hyaluronic Acid.
- MGJ: Mucogingival Junction.
- Customized/patient-specific titanium mesh: digitally designed rigid GBR barrier.
