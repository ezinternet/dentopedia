## One-line Summary
SR+MA (14 CBCT studies, 35,929 teeth, Han population): MB2 prevalence 63.7% in maxillary first molars (PMFM), 23.3% in second molars (PMSM); males have significantly higher odds (OR 1.53–1.79); high heterogeneity (I²>95%).

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Dental Sciences 21 (2026) 333–340 (published online 2025)
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.jds.2025.09.008
- **Institution**: Tri-Service General Hospital / National Defense Medical University, Taipei, Taiwan

## 2. Key Contributions
- First SR+MA specifically focused on Han Chinese population CBCT-based MB2 prevalence
- 14 studies, 15,639 participants, 35,929 teeth
- Sex-stratified analysis: males significantly higher MB2 odds in both molar types
- No significant left-right side difference

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- PRISMA-compliant SR+MA
- Databases: PubMed, MEDLINE, Scopus, ClinicalKey; through 17 August 2025
- Inclusion: CBCT-based MB2 prevalence studies in permanent maxillary molars; Han population
- Random-effects model for pooling; heterogeneity by I²

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Very high heterogeneity (I²>95%) despite high study quality
- Population-specific: applicable to Han Chinese; caution extrapolating to other ethnicities
- Authors recommend CBCT imaging for complex endodontic cases

## 6. Related Work
- Hasheminia-2025: Iranian population SR+MA (PMFM 60%, PMSM 33%)
- Comparison: Han PMFM 63.7% vs Iranian 60% — relatively consistent across Asian populations

- **PMSM**: Permanent Maxillary Second Molar
- **CBCT**: Cone-Beam Computed Tomography
- **Han population**: Largest ethnic group in China/Taiwan
