## One-line Summary
Cross-sectional CBCT study in a Pakistani population evaluating SRP (Kan classification) and labial bone thickness (LBT) at three apicocoronal levels in maxillary canine-to-canine teeth.

## 1. Document Information

## 2. Key Contributions
Population-specific prevalence data for SRP and LBT, important because non-Caucasian distributions are under-reported in implant decision-making literature.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Cross-sectional retrospective CBCT review. SRP classified per Kan (Class I/II/III/IV). LBT measured at P1 (crest), P2 (2 mm), P3 (4 mm) perpendicular to tooth long axis.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
Class I = 81.7% (n=196), Class III only 0.4%. Mean LBT 0.5–0.9 mm. No significant association between tooth type and SRP class (p=0.510).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
Single-center; younger-skewed sample limits generalizability to elderly bone changes.

## 7. Glossary
- **IIP:** Immediate Implant Placement — placement at the time of tooth extraction.
- **IIPP:** Immediate Implant Placement and Provisionalization — IIP combined with same-visit provisional restoration.
- **SRP:** Sagittal Root Position — Kan I/II/III/IV classification of root-to-bone relationship.
- **CBCT:** Cone-Beam Computed Tomography.
- **PES/WES:** Pink/White Esthetic Score — esthetic outcome indices for anterior implants.
- **EMBL:** Early Marginal Bone Loss.
