## Why Ingested

This is the seminal articulation of the APT (Aesthetic Pre-evaluative Temporary) / mock-up-guided minimally invasive porcelain laminate veneer (PLV) preparation philosophy — prepare *through* the mock-up so reduction is referenced to the final restorative contour, not the existing (often worn/dark) tooth, thereby preserving enamel. It is the conceptual ancestor of the thickness/preparation criteria synthesized in [[veneers/ali-2023-conventional-minimally-invasive-veneers-sr]] and the no-prep vs conventional debate in [[veneers/chandode-2026-no-preparation-conventional-veneers-clinical-considerations]].

## One-line Summary

Clinical technique / narrative paper establishing the Aesthetic Pre-evaluative Temporary (APT) workflow — mock-up → wax-up → APT → silicone index — that lets the clinician prepare porcelain laminate veneer teeth *through* the approved provisional, referencing reduction to the final restorative contour to maximize enamel preservation and patient/laboratory communication.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Articulates the **"minimal tooth preparation by design"** principle: minimal reduction is achieved not by free-handing on the existing tooth but by *designing* the final outcome first (mock-up/wax-up) and preparing relative to it.
- Establishes the **APT (Aesthetic Pre-evaluative Temporary)** as both an evaluation tool (esthetics, function, phonetics) and a **preparation guide** — the clinician prepares *through* the APT, which carries the exact final contours (incisal edge position, facial volume).
- Defines the communication chain: **composite mock-up → wax model → APT → silicone index**, with the same silicone index passed to the ceramist so the laboratory reproduces the patient-approved contours.
- Frames esthetics as an objective subject requiring structured communication among dentist, patient, and ceramist, and emphasizes case selection + functional evaluation before any reduction.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Not an empirical study — a single-author clinical technique exposition (with at least one illustrative case). The described workflow:

1. **Analyzing the smile** — three-dimensional assessment of the existing smile to visualize the final outcome.
2. **Functional evaluation** — assess whether the teeth can bear the proposed restorations (the snippet uses a retained deciduous canine #63 in the #11 position as an example: if it can withstand lateral occlusal forces, canine-guided occlusion is planned; if doubtful, splinting is considered).
3. **Treatment planning** — a composite mock-up helps clinician and patient visualize and share the esthetic endpoint, since intraoral exam alone cannot.
4. **Mock-up** — a freehand-carved composite (need not be as precise as a lab wax-up) conveys general tooth length, facial bulkiness, and effects on lip, phonetics, and occlusion; can be poured into a cast as a guide for the technician.
5. **Tooth preparation through APT** — because the APT resembles the exact final contours, the clinician prepares the tooth structure *through* the APT, treating even complex cases as if the teeth were already in their final aligned position.
6. **Laboratory procedures** — the ceramist must use the **same silicone index** that created the patient-approved APT; in the illustrated case a pressable porcelain with a layering technique was used.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

No numeric outcomes are reported in the available abstract + snippets. Qualitative claims:

- The mock-up → wax-up → APT → silicone-index sequence provides the **best esthetic, phonetic, and functional outcome** and improves communication with patient and laboratory.
- **Permanent diagnostic provisionals** can further strengthen this communication.
- Patients can evaluate esthetics/function/phonetics **themselves and with family/friends** before committing to the definitive restorations.
- Although PLVs are among the most conservative treatment options, **rules must be followed** (careful case selection, treatment planning, communication) to realize that conservatism.

## 7. Glossary

- **PLV (Porcelain Laminate Veneer)**: thin bonded ceramic facial restoration for anterior esthetics.
- **APT (Aesthetic Pre-evaluative Temporary)**: a provisional reproducing the planned final contours, used to evaluate esthetics/function/phonetics AND as a preparation guide (prepare *through* the APT).
- **Mock-up**: freehand composite (or transferred wax-up) preview of the final outcome placed intraorally without reduction.
- **Silicone index**: a putty matrix capturing the approved mock-up/APT contours, shared with the laboratory to reproduce them.
- **Preparation through the mock-up/APT**: reducing the tooth referenced to the final restorative volume rather than the existing tooth surface, minimizing unnecessary enamel removal.
