## Why Ingested

Yonsei University preclinical data on BMP-2 timing in damaged sockets — addresses a practical protocol question (immediate vs delayed biologics application) not covered by existing SR papers on ridge preservation; Korean institution with direct clinical relevance for biologics-augmented ARP protocols. Links to [[wiki/bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation]] for context on ARP grafting strategies.

## One-line Summary

Beagle dog RCT comparing immediate vs 2-week delayed BMP-2 injection into damaged extraction sockets found significantly greater new bone area with immediate application (10.8 vs 6.3 mm², p=0.043).

## 한줄요약

개 실험에서 손상된 발치와에 BMP-2를 즉시 적용한 군이 2주 지연 적용군보다 신생골 면적이 유의하게 컸음 (10.8 vs 6.3 mm², p=0.043).

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Clinical Oral Investigations, 25(1):275-282
- **Publication date**: 2020-05-26
- **Study type**: Animal in vivo (beagle dog, bilateral split-mouth design)
- **Affiliation**: Department of Periodontology, Research Institute for Periodontal Regeneration, Yonsei University College of Dentistry, Seoul, Korea

## 2. Key Contributions

- First direct head-to-head comparison of timing of BMP-2 application (immediate vs 2-week delay) in a surgically damaged socket model
- Demonstrates that delaying BMP-2 injection does not confer an advantage; immediate application produced superior new bone formation
- Provides preclinical evidence supporting immediate BMP-2 application in alveolar ridge preservation protocols

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Model**: Bilateral buccal bone defects (4 mm wide × 9 mm high) created at distal roots of mandibular fourth premolars in 5 beagle dogs
- **Control**: Collagenated biphasic calcium phosphate (CBCP) soaked in BMP-2 applied immediately at surgery
- **Test**: CBCP soaked in saline placed at surgery; BMP-2 solution injected 2 weeks later
- **Sacrifice**: 2 weeks post-grafting (4 weeks post-extraction)
- **Outcomes**: Clinical (swelling, inflammation), radiographic (ridge width), histomorphometric (new bone area mm²)
- **Note**: Abstract-only — full text not retrieved

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- New bone area: control (immediate BMP-2) 10.8 ± 7.0 mm² vs test (delayed BMP-2) 6.3 ± 3.1 mm² (p=0.043)
- Swelling and inflammatory reactions predominantly observed in control group at 2 weeks — suggests initial BMP-2 provokes greater early inflammation
- Ridge width: no significant difference between groups
- Conclusion: delayed BMP-2 application showed no advantage over immediate application

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample size (n=5 dogs, 10 sites)
- Short observation period (2 weeks post-grafting) — long-term remodeling not assessed
- Animal model may not translate directly to human clinical outcomes
- Single BMP-2 concentration used; dose-response not evaluated
- Abstract-only data — specific BMP-2 concentration and carrier preparation details unavailable

## 7. Glossary

- **BMP-2 (Bone Morphogenetic Protein-2)**: 골형성단백질-2, a growth factor promoting osteoblast differentiation and bone formation
- **CBCP (Collagenated Biphasic Calcium Phosphate)**: 콜라겐 함유 이상 인산칼슘, a bone graft substitute combining HA and β-TCP with collagen
- **Damaged extraction socket**: 손상된 발치와, socket with surgically created buccal bone defect simulating clinical extraction trauma
- **Alveolar ridge preservation (ARP)**: 치조제보존술, procedure to minimize dimensional changes after tooth extraction
