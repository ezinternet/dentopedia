## Why Ingested

임플란트-지대주 연결부(Implant-Abutment Connection, IAC)의 마이크로갭(microgap) 거동은 생물학적·기계적 합병증의 핵심 원인으로 논의되나, 원추각(cone angle)에 따른 차이를 고해상도 방사광(synchrotron) 영상으로 직접 비교한 연구는 드물다. 이 논문은 기존 [[prosthetic-materials/implant-connection]] 관련 지식을 보강하며, 소원추각(<12°) friction-fit 연결부와 대원추각(~45°) butt-joint 연결부의 변위 모드 차이를 0.1 μm 정밀도로 정량화한다.

## One-line Summary

In vitro synchrotron microtomography study (n=4 systems, static 30–200 N loading) found pre-existing microgaps (0.15–9 μm) in all IAC designs that expanded up to 40.5 μm under load; small-angle conical friction-fit connections (<12°) caused inward abutment displacement deforming the outer implant wall, while the large-angle butt-joint (45°) distributed force differently without implant-wall deformation.

## 한줄요약

방사광 마이크로단층촬영으로 4종 임플란트 시스템(3종 소원추각 friction-fit, 1종 대원추각 butt-joint) 정하중 시험 시 모든 연결부에서 하중 전 마이크로갭(0.15–9 μm)이 존재했고 하중 증가 시 최대 40.5 μm까지 증가했으며, 소원추각 연결부는 지대주 내측 변위로 임플란트 외벽 변형을 유발한 반면 대원추각 butt-joint는 다른 힘 분산 양상을 보였다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First comparative SRX (synchrotron X-ray) study assessing both conical friction-fit and large-angle butt-joint IAC in parallel, at 0.1 μm resolution.
2. Demonstrated pre-existing microgaps in ALL tested systems even before loading (range 0.15–9 μm).
3. Identified two distinct modes of abutment displacement: inward (conical <12°) vs. non-deforming (butt-joint ~45°).
4. Showed that small-angle conical connections deform the outer implant wall, which may stress peri-implant bone.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Systems tested** (2 specimens each):
  - Medentika Microcone (MA): conical, RI 3.5×11 mm
  - Medentis ICX-Templant (MS): conical, 4.1×12.5 mm
  - NobelActive Internal RP (NO): conical, 4.3×11.5 mm
  - BEGO Semados (BE): angulated butt-joint (~45°), 4.1×11.5 mm
- **Cone angles**: MA, MS, NO all <12°; BE ~45°
- **Loading protocol** (ISO 14801:2008):
  - 30 N at 90°, 100 N at 90°, 200 N at 30° (off-axis)
- **Imaging**: Synchrotron phase-contrast radioscopy (PCR) + microtomography at ESRF, Grenoble; accuracy 0.1 μm
- **Tightening torque**: MA/MS/BE 30 Ncm, NO 35 Ncm
- **Embedding**: methylmethacrylate in 15 mm brass cylinder

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Parameter | Finding |
|---|---|
| Pre-load microgap | 0.15–9 μm (all systems) |
| Max microgap under load | 40.5 μm at 100 N / 90° off-axis |
| Conical <12° displacement | Inward axial → outer implant wall deformation |
| Butt-joint 45° displacement | Force distributed differently; no outer wall deformation observed |
| Load effect | Microgap increases with both force magnitude and angulation |

- Conical connections (friction-fit): abutment settles deeper into implant body under load, causing outward bulging of implant shoulder wall.
- BEGO Semados butt-joint: force distribution diverges — less implant-wall deformation but different stress pathway.
- No system was microgap-free prior to loading.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample size (n=2 per system); results are exploratory.
- Static loading only — cyclic fatigue behavior not assessed.
- No biological outcome correlation (only mechanical in vitro).
- Different implant dimensions across systems complicate direct comparison.
- Authors call for cyclic loading SRX studies and FEA modeling to predict bone stress implications.

## 6. Related Work

- Previous SRX studies (refs 11, 12, 34, 35) showed microgap existence in conical IAC; this study extends to butt-joint comparison.
- Microleakage/bacterial colonization of IAC linked to peri-implant mucositis (43%) and peri-implantitis (22%) (ref 21).
- Titanium wear particles in peri-implant tissue discussed as inflammatory mediators (refs 23–26).

## 7. Glossary

- **IAC (Implant-Abutment Connection, 임플란트-지대주 연결부)**: interface zone between implant body and abutment
- **Microgap (마이크로갭)**: sub-millimeter gap at IAC interface, measured in μm
- **Friction-fit / press-fit (마찰-끼워맞춤)**: conical interference fit relying on friction for stability
- **Butt-joint connection (맞댐연결부)**: horizontal/wide-angle mating zone without interference fit
- **SRX (Synchrotron X-ray, 방사광 X선)**: high-brightness, quasi-parallel X-ray enabling sub-μm imaging
- **Phase-contrast radioscopy (위상차 방사선투시)**: imaging technique using Fresnel propagation for enhanced crack/void detection
- **Abutment displacement (지대주 변위)**: movement of abutment relative to implant body under load
