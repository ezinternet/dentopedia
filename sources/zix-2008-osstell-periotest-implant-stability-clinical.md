## One-line Summary
Clinical trial (213 one-stage implants in 65 patients): direct comparison of resonance frequency analysis (Osstell, ISQ) and damping capacity assessment (Periotest); ISQ mean 57.66 ± 8.19, Periotest mean -5.08 ± 2.02; correlation between techniques moderate to good (Pearson -0.64); intraclass correlation higher for ISQ (0.99) than Periotest (0.88); ISQ more precise and less variable than Periotest.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: International Journal of Oral & Maxillofacial Implants 2008;23:525–530
- **Pages**: 525–530 (6 pages)
- **Institution**: Department of Prosthodontics, University of Berne, Switzerland
- **Study type**: In-vivo clinical trial (cross-sectional methodological study)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Quantitative head-to-head comparison of the two non-invasive implant stability techniques used clinically: **Osstell (RFA / ISQ)** vs **Periotest (damping capacity assessment, DCA)**.
- Establishes that ISQ has **higher measurement precision** (ICC 0.99) than Periotest (ICC 0.88).
- Identifies **implant diameter** (not length) as a significant determinant of both measurements.
- Provides reference distributions of ISQ and Periotest values in stable, loaded and unloaded one-stage implants.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 213 clinically stable, loaded and unloaded one-stage implants in 65 patients (Berne, Switzerland).
- Each implant measured in triplicate by **resonance frequency analysis (Osstell)** and **Periotest (damping capacity assessment)**.
- Statistics: descriptive; Pearson and Spearman correlation; intraclass correlation coefficient; SPSS 11.0.2.
- Sub-analyses: correlation of measurement with implant length and diameter.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Mean ISQ**: 57.66 ± 8.19.
- **Mean Periotest value (PTV)**: -5.08 ± 2.02 (negative range = stable implant).
- **Pearson correlation ISQ vs PTV**: -0.64 (moderate-good, expected inverse relationship).
- **Spearman correlation**: -0.65.
- **Single-measure ICC**: ISQ 0.99, Periotest 0.88 — ISQ much more precise.
- **Distribution shape**: Periotest values diverge from Gaussian more than ISQs.
- **Implant length**: NS for both techniques.
- **Implant diameter**: significant correlation with both techniques (p < .005).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Cross-sectional; no longitudinal follow-up of stability change.
- Selected stable implants — does not measure techniques' sensitivity to failing implants.
- Older Osstell generation (mentor / earlier); modern Osstell devices may differ slightly.

## 7. Glossary
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: 1–100 scale derived from RFA resonance frequency.
- **PTV (Periotest Value)**: scale −8 to +50; lower = more stable; tooth normative ~ −3 to +1.
- **DCA (Damping Capacity Assessment)**: percussion-based stability measurement (Periotest).
- **ICC**: intraclass correlation coefficient — measurement precision/reproducibility metric.
