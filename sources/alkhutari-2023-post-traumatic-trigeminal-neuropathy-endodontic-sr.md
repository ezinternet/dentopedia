## Why Ingested

삼차신경통 치과 감시 인제스트 — 근관치료(RCT)가 유발하는 외상성 삼차신경병증(painful post-traumatic trigeminal neuropathy, PTTN), 즉 술자가 직접 야기하는 의인성(iatrogenic) 신경손상을 다룬다. 발치·임플란트와 함께 PTTN의 주요 원인 중 하나가 근관치료(전체 삼차신경손상의 6-8%)이며, 지속성 치아치조통(persistent dentoalveolar pain, PDAP)·비전형 치통(atypical odontalgia, AO)으로 오진되어 불필요한 재치료가 반복되는 임상 함정을 정량화한다. 형제 삼차신경통 페이지 [[oral-medicine/khan-2023-trigeminal-neuralgia-therapeutic-approach-sr]]를 보강하고, 구강안면통증·신경병증 진단을 다루는 [[oral-medicine/jaaskelainen-2018-burning-mouth-syndrome-neuropathic-pain]] 및 [[oral-medicine/kouri-2024-small-fiber-neuropathy-burning-mouth-syndrome]]와 신경병증성 통증 기전을 공유한다.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review of 10 studies (n = 8 to 1,331) finding that endodontic therapy accounts for 6-8% of post-traumatic trigeminal neuropathy (PTTN) cases, with non-odontogenic neuropathic pain emerging 3-48 months after root canal treatment, predominantly in women in their mid-40s, frequently misdiagnosed as atypical odontalgia/persistent dentoalveolar pain.

## 한줄요약

근관치료가 외상성 삼차신경병증(PTTN)의 6-8%를 차지하며, 비치성 신경병증성 통증이 근관치료 후 3-48개월에 발생하고 주로 40대 중반 여성에 호발하며 비전형 치통(AO)으로 오진되기 쉽다는 점을 보인 10개 연구 체계적 문헌고찰.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies endodontic therapy / root canal treatment (RCT) as a discrete iatrogenic cause of painful post-traumatic trigeminal neuropathy (PTTN): **6-8% of all trigeminal nerve injuries** across the largest series.
- Establishes the **onset window**: PTTN appears **3-48 months** after endodontic treatment.
- Consolidates the **demographic signature**: female predominance (65-85% of PDAP cases), **mid-40s** modal age.
- Clarifies the **misdiagnosis trap** — PTTN is repeatedly mislabeled atypical odontalgia (AO) / persistent dentoalveolar pain (PDAP), driving unnecessary repeat procedures.
- Compiles the diagnostic toolkit (clinical exam, diagnostic LA/anesthetic test, CBCT, VAS, S-LANSS, OHIP-14, QST) and endorses Graff-Radford & Solberg (1992) criteria.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Databases: PubMed, Web of Science, Cochrane CENTRAL; English only; published 2009-2020; human subjects.
- Search terms: painful PTTN, PDAP, AO, neuropathic orofacial pain, atypical facial pain, phantom tooth pain, persistent idiopathic facial pain.
- Five independent reviewers; third reviewer adjudicated disagreements.
- 10 articles included: 2 retrospective clinical trials, 4 retrospective studies, 1 retrospective observational, 1 retrospective+prospective cohort, 1 prospective cohort, 1 prospective longitudinal cohort.
- Sample sizes 8 to 1,331; mean age 40 to 55.4 years.
- IRB Riyadh Elm University (FRP/2020/278/293 (REU)). PRISMA flow used.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Endodontic share of trigeminal nerve injury** (Van der Cruyssen 2020, n=1,331): third molar 519 (48%) > dental implants 146 (13%) = non-third-molar extraction 136 (13%) > local anesthesia 125 (12%) > **endodontic treatment 86 (8%)** > trauma 74 (6-7%); mean 112 days to diagnosis.
- **Renton & Yilmaz** — iatrogenic inferior alveolar nerve injury (IANI) causes: TM surgery 60%, LA 19%, implants 18%, **endodontics 8%**.
- **Klazen 2018** (Leuven, 8,845 patients, 53 iatrogenic injuries): endodontics-related **6%**; 68% female; avg age ~42 years.
- **Prevalence of persistent non-odontogenic pain after RCT**: Klasser 2011 = **3.1%**; Pigg = **3.4%**; Ram = AO in **3-6%** of endodontic patients; Jacobs = 5.7% phantom pain (women:men 9:1).
- **Onset interval**: 3-48 months; non-odontogenic pain after ≥6 months post-RCT = **34% (95% CI 1.4-5.5)**.
- **Arch**: conflicting — Oshima (87.5% maxillary), List (56% upper vs 45% lower), Nixdorf (53% maxillary, 89% posterior) vs Klasser (higher in mandible, no sex preference); review concludes no clear maxilla/mandible variation overall.
- **Mechanistic causes after failed endodontics**: over-instrumentation/perforation communicating with external root surface, internal/external resorption defects, root fracture, lower molar apices close to inferior dental canal/mental foramen, dens invaginatus, periapical lesions, cysts.
- **Predictive factors** (Philpott 2018): post-RCT, female gender, history of chronic pain, teeth responsive to pulp test, preoperative pain, preexisting tooth crack, diameter of preoperative radiolucency; 20% showed persistent pain.
- **Pain mechanism**: impaired conditioned pain modulation (CPM); prolonged cold-pain after-sensation; 96% of PPTTN cases (Benoliel 2012) had trigeminal sensory abnormalities (allodynia, hyper/hypoalgesia).
- **Management for pain**: amitriptyline 50-75 mg/day and/or carbamazepine 400-600 mg/day + vitamin B complex.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- High-quality research lacking; only retrospective + small prospective cohorts; no meta-analysis (heterogeneous terminology and outcome reporting → SR without MA).
- Terminology inconsistency (AO vs PDAP vs PTTN vs phantom tooth pain) across studies hampers pooling.
- Arch predilection results conflict; sample sizes range enormously (8 to 1,331), limiting comparability.
- No formal incidence denominator for endodontic-specific PTTN in most studies.

## 7. Glossary

- **PTTN** — painful post-traumatic trigeminal neuropathy; trauma-induced trigeminal nerve lesion with sensory dysfunction.
- **PDAP** — persistent dentoalveolar pain; idiopathic chronic neuropathic pain in teeth/jaws.
- **AO** — atypical odontalgia; idiopathic continuous toothache without local/referred cause >4 months.
- **RCT** — root canal therapy / treatment (endodontic).
- **IANI** — iatrogenic inferior alveolar nerve injury.
- **CPM** — conditioned pain modulation; descending endogenous pain-inhibitory mechanism.
- **PPTTN** — peripheral painful traumatic trigeminal neuropathy.
- **S-LANSS** — Self-administered Leeds Assessment of Neuropathic Symptoms and Signs.
