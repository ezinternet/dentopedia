## Why Ingested

Human-only systematic review that complements the more animal-heavy / umbrella-level vitamin D literature already in the wiki: it quantifies *human* implant-loss rates stratified by vitamin D status (3.9–11.4%, peaking at 11.1% in severe deficiency), which is exactly the patient-facing number missing from broader syntheses. Reinforces the deficiency-as-risk-factor signal in [[implants/vitamin-d/miron-2025-vitamin-d-deficiency-early-implant-failure]], refines the inconclusive-causality verdict of [[implants/vitamin-d/tallon-2024-vitamin-d-osseointegration-umbrella-review]] with study-level human numbers, and anchors to [[implants/fan-2024-smoking-early-implant-failure-sr-ma]] because the single largest study here (Mangano) found smoking + periodontal disease compounded vitamin D deficiency into a ~4× early-loss risk.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (PRISMA) of 7 human studies (2008–2021; 1462 participants, 4450 implants); implant-loss rate 3.9–11.4% (avg 8.37%), highest (11.1%) in severe vitamin D deficiency compounded by smoking/periodontitis — but overall no significant association between vitamin D level and osseointegration; advocates pre-surgical vitamin D screening.

## 한줄요약

사람 대상 7편(2008–2021; 참가자 1462명·임플란트 4450개)을 묶은 PRISMA 체계적 고찰. 임플란트 소실률(implant loss rate)은 3.9–11.4%(평균 8.37%)로, 중증 비타민 D 결핍 + 흡연·치주염이 겹친 군에서 최고 11.1%. 다만 비타민 D 수치와 골유착(osseointegration) 사이에 유의한 연관성은 없었고, 술전 비타민 D 선별검사를 권고.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First/among-few **human-only** systematic syntheses of vitamin D and dental implant osseointegration: 7 studies, 1462 participants, 4450 implants.
- Quantifies a **human implant-loss range of 3.9–11.4%** (overall average 8.37%) stratified by vitamin D status.
- Highlights the **Mangano et al. dose-response signal**: 3.9% overall loss rising to 11.1% in severe deficiency, ~4× higher early-loss prevalence when low vitamin D coincides with smoking and periodontal disease.
- Surfaces a **genetic dimension**: VDR TaqI polymorphism not associated with loss (Alvim-Pereira), but rs3782905 allele G significantly associated with poor osseointegration (Pereira).
- Bottom line is deliberately **cautious/inconclusive**: despite the deficiency signal, the review's formal conclusion is *no significant association* between vitamin D level and osseointegration in humans — contrasting with more consistent positive animal findings.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane Library, Web of Science; scope to December 2023.
- **Inclusion**: human studies measuring serum vitamin D and correlating with implant success; clear methodology; peer-reviewed English.
- **Exclusion**: non-human (in vitro/animal), no specific vitamin D–osseointegration focus, insufficient outcome data, non-peer-reviewed sources.
- **Screening**: 677 initial → 195 screened (106 duplicates removed) → 7 included (two reviewers, third resolving disagreements).
- **Quality**: Newcastle–Ottawa Scale (cohorts) + Cochrane risk-of-bias tool (RCT); rated low/medium/high.
- **Designs of included 7**: 2 case–control, 3 case reports, 1 case series, 1 retrospective cohort, 1 RCT (Kwiatek, the only high-quality study).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Study | Country | Key finding |
|---|---|---|
| Alvim-Pereira (2008) | Brazil | 9.8% loss; **no** association VDR TaqI polymorphism ↔ loss |
| Pereira | Brazil | 11.4% loss; rs3782905 allele G ↔ poor osseointegration |
| Flanagan | USA | Successful implant under vitamin D3 supplementation (low serum vit D not prohibitive if calcium managed) |
| Bryce | UK | Successful implant despite severe deficiency (age 29) |
| Fretwurst | Germany | 2/9 implants failed under deficiency; replaced successfully after supplementation (vit D rose to 46 μg/L) |
| Mangano (n=885) | Italy | **3.9% overall → 11.1% in severe deficiency**; ~4× early-loss with low vit D + smoking + periodontitis |
| Kwiatek (2021, RCT) | Poland | Significantly higher bone density after 12 wk supplementation (23.9 → 33.1 ng/mL) |

- Total: 1462 participants, 4450 implants; **average loss 8.37%**.
- Anatomical note: anterior maxilla osseointegration ~90% vs posterior maxilla ~70% (cited).
- Smoking alone can raise implant failure by >140% (cited context for Mangano compounding).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- High **heterogeneity** in design, vitamin D assessment, and osseointegration evaluation — limits comparability.
- Mostly **observational + small/case-level** studies (only 1 RCT) → cannot infer causality; possible publication and English-language bias.
- Inconsistent reporting of vitamin D levels and **no standardized supplementation protocol** (dose, timing, target level) across studies.
- Authors call for **prospective intervention trials** with uniform methodology to clarify the relationship and guide pre-surgical vitamin D management.

## 7. Glossary

- **Osseointegration**: direct structural/functional bone-to-implant contact without interposing soft tissue; basis of implant stability.
- **Implant loss rate**: proportion of placed implants lost over follow-up.
- **VDR (Vitamin D Receptor) polymorphism**: genetic variants (e.g., TaqI, rs3782905) potentially modulating vitamin D's effect on bone.
- **BIC (Bone-to-Implant Contact)**: histological measure of bone apposition on the implant surface.
- **Severe vitamin D deficiency**: very low serum 25-OH vitamin D; the stratum with the highest (11.1%) loss in Mangano et al.
