## Why Ingested

기존 벌크필 임상 근거 페이지([[resin/silva-2023-bulk-fill-vs-conventional-resins-umbrella]], [[resin/boaro-2019-bulk-fill-composites-clinical-properties-sr-ma]])는 주로 변연적합·생존율 중심이고, 벌크필의 **마모저항·경도** 기전 데이터는 부족했다. 본 in-vitro 연구(Osiewicz 2022)는 벌크필이 응력부담 부위에서 단독으로는 부적합하나 일반 컴포지트 커버층 아래에서는 충분히 단단하다는 기전적 근거를 제공해 임상 페이지를 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro two/three-body ACTA wear study of 4 bulk-fill composites vs 1 nanohybrid (Grandio): uncovered bulk-fills wore 59-118 µm/200k cycles vs 31 µm for the nanohybrid and had much lower Vickers hardness, but a 0.5-1.0 mm conventional cover layer wore the same as the nanohybrid alone (bulk-fill substrate stiff enough to act as a monoblock).

## 한줄요약

In-vitro ACTA 마모시험 — 벌크필 레진 4종 vs 나노하이브리드(Grandio) 비교: 커버 없는 벌크필은 59-118 µm/200k cycle로 나노하이브리드(31 µm)보다 마모가 크고 비커스 경도도 낮았으나, 0.5-1.0 mm 일반 컴포지트로 덮으면 나노하이브리드 단독과 동일한 마모를 보여 벌크필이 단단한 받침(monoblock) 역할을 함. 단독 응력 부담 부위에는 부적합.

- **Journal**: Dental Materials 38 (2022) 549–553

- **Study type**: In-vitro laboratory study (two-/three-body wear + microhardness)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies intrinsic wear rate and Vickers hardness of four commercial bulk-fill composites against a highly filled nanohybrid control under a standardized ACTA wear protocol (ISO/TS 14569).
- Tests a layered restoration concept: whether a low-modulus bulk-fill substrate beneath a conventional hybrid cover layer compromises the cover layer's wear resistance.
- Demonstrates that bulk-fill substrates are stiff enough to support a conventional top layer (acts as a monoblock), so the layered system wears like the conventional composite alone.
- Establishes correlations between filler content (wt%, vol%), hardness, and wear rate, with vol% filler being the strongest predictor.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Materials**: 4 bulk-fill composites — SDR Smart Dentin Replacement (SDR), X-tra base (XBA), Filtek Bulk Fill (FUP), Dual-Curing Bulk Composite (FBFL) — vs conventional nanohybrid Grandio (GDO) as control.
- **Wear test**: ACTA wear machine, three-body, ISO/TS 14569. Specimen wheel (Ø48 mm) vs stainless-steel antagonist wheel (Ø19 mm), 15% circumferential speed difference, 15 N spring force, third-body slurry of rice + millet seed shells (pH 7). One run = 200,000 cycles (~55.5 h) at 1 Hz. Wear measured by profilometry (10 tracings/wheel, n=20), loss in µm.
- **Three configurations**: bulk-fill covered with (1) 0.5 mm GDO, (2) 1.0 mm GDO, (3) no cover (pure bulk-fill).
- **Time points**: after 3 days and after 1 month (stored in water at room temperature).
- **Microhardness**: Vickers, n=5 specimens, 5 indentations each, 20 N load, 20 s dwell.
- **Statistics**: two-way ANOVA (material × configuration); one-way ANOVA + Tukey post hoc (P<0.05 wear; α=0.05 hardness); independent t-tests across time intervals; linear correlations (wt%, vol%, hardness vs wear).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Covered configurations (0.5 / 1.0 mm GDO cover)**: wear ≈ 29.3–32.8 µm/200k rev at 3 days — not clinically different from GDO alone (any significant differences were within ~2 µm).
- **Uncovered bulk-fills (config 3) at 3 days**: 59.3–118.4 µm/200k rev, all significantly higher than GDO (31.4 µm). Highest wear = SDR (118.4 µm), lowest among bulk-fills = FUP (59.3 µm); XBA 60.5, FBFL 69.7 µm.
- **At 1 month**: wear rates lower than at 3 days for all (e.g. SDR 96.3, GDO No-Cover 22.4, FUP 49.0, FBFL 59.3 µm); all 3-day rates significantly higher than 1-month (run-in / bedding-in effect).
- **Vickers hardness**: GDO 124 > XBA 70 > FUP 62 > SDR 45 > FBFL 40. Conventional nanohybrid highest; FBFL lowest.
- **Correlations (wear at day 3)**: wt% filler r=0.645 (strong); Vickers hardness r=0.758 (strong); vol% filler r=0.844 (very strong, best predictor).
- SDR's wear (~120 µm) approaches that of temporary filling materials (e.g. Protemp 4 ~100 µm), suggesting it is unsuitable for stress-bearing restorations.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro ACTA simulation; no direct intraoral validation — clinical wear involves saliva, enamel antagonists, and complex masticatory dynamics not fully reproduced.
- Single conventional control (Grandio) and one representative material per bulk-fill class.
- Two time points only (3 days, 1 month); long-term wear behavior not assessed.
- Stainless-steel antagonist rather than enamel; absolute µm values are device-/protocol-specific.
- Approximal/proximal wear and water-uptake degradation discussed but not directly measured.

## 6. Related Work

- Three-body wear benchmarks: ~20 µm/200k cycles for ceramics, enamel, posterior composites; 30–60 µm for most conventional hybrids; ~100 µm for temporary/GIC materials.
- Prior ACTA work by the same group linking filler wt% to wear rate across restorative composites.
- Bulk-fill literature on depth of cure, polymerization shrinkage stress, and mechanical resistance (fracture toughness/abrasion) showing no consistent improvement over conventional composites.

## 7. Glossary

- **Bulk-fill composite**: resin composite formulated (larger fillers, fewer pigments, modified monomers, higher translucency) to be placed/cured in thick (up to 4-5 mm) single increments.
- **ACTA wear machine**: three-body wear apparatus (ISO/TS 14569) using two rotating wheels and a food-mimicking slurry.
- **Two-/three-body wear**: material loss from direct contact (two-body) and from interposed abrasive medium (three-body).
- **Vickers hardness (VH)**: surface hardness via diamond pyramidal indenter under defined load.
- **E-modulus (modulus of elasticity)**: material stiffness; higher modulus → less deformation under load → lower fatigue wear.
- **Monoblock behavior**: layered system in which a sufficiently stiff substrate lets a top layer distribute stress as if it were a single homogeneous block.
- **Surface fatigue wear**: subsurface stress-driven crack initiation and propagation leading to surface damage under cyclic loading.
