## Why Ingested

10-MDP는 in-vitro에서 hydroxyapatite와 안정적인 chemical bond (MDP-Ca salt, nanolayering)를 형성한다는 근거가 강하지만, 이 분자적 이점이 **self-etch dentin 접착의 실제 임상 성과**로 이어지는지는 별개의 질문이다. 본 SR+MA (de Oliveira 2021, 11 RCTs)는 NCCL(주로 dentin substrate) 수복에서 10-MDP 함유 vs 비함유 self-etch 접착제를 직접 비교해 임상 결과(retention 등)에 차이가 없음을 보여, in-vitro bond-strength 우위가 임상으로 자동 전이되지 않는다는 경고를 제공한다. Sibling SR+MA [[wiki/resin-bonding/fehrenbach-2021-10-mdp-bonding-performance-sr-ma]] (bond-strength 관점)와 짝을 이뤄 "분자 vs 임상" gap을 메운다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 11 RCTs (NCCL restorations) found no significant difference in clinical performance between self-etch adhesives with vs without the 10-MDP functional monomer (p=0.05; I²=0–53%; moderate-to-high GRADE certainty).

## 한줄요약

11개 RCT를 메타분석한 결과, NCCL 수복에서 10-MDP 함유 self-etch 접착제와 비함유 접착제 사이에 임상 성과 차이가 없었다 (p=0.05, I²=0–53%, GRADE 중등도–높음).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Pools the **clinical** (not in-vitro) evidence on whether the 10-MDP functional monomer in self-etch adhesives improves restoration outcomes in NCCL.
- Restricts inclusion to RCTs and applies the Cochrane risk-of-bias tool + GRADE, yielding a high-quality clinical answer rather than a bench-strength inference.
- Provides a clean negative result: presence of 10-MDP did **not** influence clinical performance, tempering the assumption that 10-MDP's favorable chemistry translates into clinical superiority.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Question**: Do self-etch adhesives **with** 10-MDP outperform those **without** it, clinically, in NCCL restorations?
- **Databases searched**: MEDLINE (via PubMed), Cochrane Library, Scopus, Web of Science, OpenGrey, Clinical Trials, Current Controlled Trials, EU Clinical Trials Register; plus hand-search of included-study references.
- **Inclusion**: Randomized controlled clinical trials comparing self-etch adhesive systems with vs without the 10-MDP functional monomer for NCCL restorations.
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane Collaboration tool.
- **Certainty of evidence**: GRADE.
- **Synthesis**: Heterogeneity assessed via I²; data grouped and pooled in quantitative meta-analysis.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Records after duplicate removal**: 4208 manuscripts retrieved.
- **Studies included (qualitative + quantitative)**: 11 RCTs (all 11 carried into the meta-analysis).
- **Risk of bias**: 9 studies low risk, 2 unclear.
- **GRADE certainty**: moderate-to-high.
- **Primary finding**: No statistical difference in clinical performance between "with vs without 10-MDP" adhesives for **all** evaluated criteria (p=0.05).
- **Heterogeneity**: I² ranged from 0% to 53%.
- **Conclusion**: Presence of the 10-MDP functional monomer did **not** influence clinical performance of NCCL restorations.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Abstract-only ingest** — full text not retrieved; per-criterion effect estimates (retention rate, marginal discoloration, marginal adaptation, secondary caries) and confidence intervals are not available here.
- Population is **NCCL** specifically (a sclerotic-dentin-heavy, low-stress substrate); results may not generalize to caries-affected dentin, coronal dentin, or load-bearing restorations.
- Heterogeneity up to 53% indicates between-study variability (adhesive brands, MDP concentration/purity, operator, follow-up length) not fully resolved.
- "With/without 10-MDP" is a coarse contrast — formulations differ in more than the single monomer, so a null result does not isolate 10-MDP's specific contribution.

## 7. Glossary

- **10-MDP (10-methacryloyloxydecyl dihydrogen phosphate)**: Functional acidic monomer; its phosphate group chemically bonds to calcium in hydroxyapatite, forming stable MDP-Ca salts and nanolayering.
- **Self-etch adhesive**: Adhesive system that conditions and primes dentin/enamel simultaneously without a separate phosphoric-acid etch-and-rinse step.
- **NCCL (noncarious cervical lesion)**: Cervical hard-tissue loss not caused by caries (abrasion/erosion/abfraction); a standard clinical model for adhesive retention trials.
- **Functional monomer**: Monomer carrying a chemically reactive group (e.g., phosphate, carboxyl) enabling chemical interaction with tooth substrate.
- **GRADE**: System for rating the certainty of a body of evidence.
- **I² (heterogeneity)**: Proportion of variability across studies due to between-study differences rather than chance.
