## Why Ingested

Reinforces the medico-legal value of documentation seen in [[complaint-management/park-2016-analysis-of-malpractice-claims-associated]]: this Brazilian 10-year litigation analysis quantifies that appropriate written informed consent shifts facial-cosmetic-surgery lawsuits toward favorable defendant outcomes and reduces financial damage — direct evidence that consent quality is a defensible complaint/malpractice control, relevant to dental cosmetic/esthetic procedures.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective 10-year (2012-2022) analysis of Brazilian facial cosmetic surgery litigation (992 identified, 122 included) found written informed consent significantly reduces both litigation losses and mean financial damage ($59,536).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies that appropriate written informed consent is associated with favorable defendant outcomes in facial cosmetic surgery litigation.
- Documents a rising trend of legal disputes over the 10-year period.
- Identifies female patients as more prone to litigation and reports the specialty distribution (plastic surgery 67%, maxillofacial surgery 9%).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Online search of facial cosmetic surgery litigation in Brazil over a 10-year window (2012-2022).
- 992 litigations identified; 122 met inclusion criteria and were analyzed.
- (abstract-only — full text not retrieved; inclusion/exclusion criteria and statistical model not extractable.)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- 992 litigations identified → 122 included.
- Number of legal disputes increasing over the years.
- Female patients more prone to litigation.
- Financial damage range: $75 to $500,000 (mean $59,536).
- Many lawsuits with appropriate written consent had a favorable outcome for the defendant.
- Primary specialty: plastic surgery (67%), then maxillofacial surgery (9%).
- Written consent significantly reduces both litigation losses and financial damage.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only — full text not retrieved; effect sizes, CIs, and adjustment for confounders are unavailable.
- Single-country (Brazil) legal context limits direct generalization to other jurisdictions.
- Retrospective, litigation-record-based design; selection and reporting biases possible.

## 7. Glossary

- Written informed consent: a documented, signed disclosure of procedure risks/benefits/alternatives obtained before treatment, serving as a medico-legal defense.
- Litigation loss: an outcome unfavorable to the defendant (clinician) in a legal dispute.
- Defendant: in this context, the clinician/surgeon being sued.
