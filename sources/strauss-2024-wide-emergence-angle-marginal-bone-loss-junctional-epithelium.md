## Why Ingested

기존 [[implants/soft-tissue/sanz-martin-2022-buccal-soft-tissue-dehiscence-implants-sr-ma]]와 [[implants/soft-tissue/tavelli-2025-soft-tissue-dehiscences-implant-sites-ao-aap]]는 buccal 연조직 열개·퇴축의 임상 결과를 정리하지만, prosthetic emergence angle이 supracrestal soft-tissue seal과 MBL에 미치는 인과를 조직학적으로 보여주는 근거가 부족했다. 본 6마리 개 preclinical RCT (Strauss 2024)는 20°/40°/60°/80° 4단계 emergence angle을 직접 비교해, ≥60° 광각이 junctional epithelium 연속성을 파괴하고 MBL을 4배 증가시킴을 micro-CT·조직학·synchrotron으로 입증 — convex profile이 mucosal recession을 더 많이 유발한다는 임상 관찰의 조직학적 기전 설명.

## One-line Summary

Preclinical RCT (6 mongrel dogs, 48 implant sites, 4 emergence-angle groups 20/40/60/80°, 24 wk): wider restorative emergence angle progressively increased marginal bone loss (24-wk MBL ~4× higher at 80° vs 20°) and ≥60° angles impaired junctional-epithelium continuity, supporting keeping the angle <40°.

## 한줄요약

전임상 RCT (개 6마리, 임플란트 48부위, emergence angle 20/40/60/80° 4군, 24주): 보철 emergence angle이 넓을수록 변연골소실이 단계적으로 증가했고(80°군 24주 MBL이 20°군의 약 4배), 60° 이상 광각은 접합상피(junctional epithelium) 연속성을 손상시켜 — 각도를 40° 미만으로 유지할 것을 뒷받침.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First longitudinal preclinical study to isolate the effect of restorative **emergence (restorative) angle** on both peri-implant **hard tissue (MBL)** and **supracrestal soft tissue**, including the previously under-studied **buccal aspect**, using four controlled angles (20°, 40°, 60°, 80°).
- Demonstrates a **dose-dependent** relationship: the wider the angle, the greater the MBL — overcoming the confounding inherent in prior cross-sectional/cohort data that produced the arbitrary 30°/40° thresholds.
- Provides **histological** evidence that wide angles (≥60°) disrupt junctional-epithelium integrity and disorganize supracrestal connective tissue — a candidate mechanism linking suboptimal emergence profile to peri-implant disease and mucosal recession.
- Adds **synchrotron imaging** (Pohang Light Source-II) alongside micro-CT and undecalcified histology, reducing metal artefacts for buccal bone-loss depiction.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Model**: 6 male mongrel dogs (12-17 kg); ARRIVE/3Rs; IACUC-approved (Yonsei, IRB 2022-0174).
- **Design**: mandibular P1-P4 + M1 extracted; 3-month healing; 4 bone-level implants per hemimandible (Conelog screw-line, 7 mm × 3.8 mm, ≥15 Ncm). Each implant randomly allocated (per hemimandible) to a customized abutment of one of four emergence angles (20°/40°/60°/80°), placed immediately.
- **Follow-up**: 24 weeks. Periapical radiographs at abutment insertion + 6/9/16/24 wk (MBL = implant shoulder to first BIC; inter-thread pitch 0.7 mm calibration; ImageJ; ICC 0.99). Micro-CT (SkyScan 1072, 35 μm) fBIC at mesial/distal/buccal. Clinical BOP + plaque (PCR) at 24 wk. Undecalcified histology (48 sites; PMMA; Toluidine blue) for fBIC and descriptive buccal soft-tissue + distance from thickest inflammatory infiltrate to fBIC. Synchrotron 3D on 24-wk samples.
- **Statistics**: GEE adjusted for baseline soft-tissue thickness, Bonferroni; Kruskal-Wallis + Dunn for histomorphometry; α=0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- All implants osseointegrated; no losses/complications over 24 wk.
- **MBL (radiographic)**: increased over time in all groups (p<0.001); significant angle effect (p<0.001) and time×angle interaction (p<0.001), 80° greatest. Adjusting for baseline soft-tissue thickness did not change findings (thickness NS, p=0.915).
- **24-wk mean MBL**: 20° = 0.07 ± 0.20 mm; 40° = 0.11 ± 0.27 mm; **60° = 0.23 ± 0.31 mm (>2×)**; **80° = 0.38 ± 0.27 mm (~4× the 20° group)**.
- **Micro-CT fBIC (mesial/distal)**: 0.10 / 0.13 / 0.20 / 0.40 mm for 20/40/60/80°; inter-group p<0.0001. Buccal fBIC rose with angle numerically but NS (p=0.637).
- **Plaque** rose with angle: 66.6% / 66.6% / 91.7% / 100%. BOP: 14% / 25% / 31% / 25% (60° highest; 80° possibly underestimated due to inability to probe under wide angle).
- **Histology**: 20° and 40° → continuous, intact junctional epithelium + organized connective tissue. **60° and 80° → disorganized connective tissue, more inflammatory infiltrate, partial/absent junctional epithelium**, with biofilm against abutment. Distance from inflammatory infiltrate to buccal fBIC **decreased** with wider angle (1.69 / 1.67 / 1.09 / 1.00 mm; 20° vs 80° trend p=0.065) — consistent with Waerhaug's ~1 mm plaque "radius of action".
- **Conclusion**: keep restorative angle as narrow as clinically possible (**<40°**).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Preclinical dog model; no blinding; small sample/power; short (24-wk) follow-up; **no implant loading** with conventional crowns/zirconia. Per-hemimandible randomization caused slight imbalance (more 40° abutments at narrower P2 ridge → 40° outliers). Junctional-epithelium impairment could partly be a histological artefact (tissue compression/tearing under wide angle). Statistical significance ≠ established clinical MCID.
- Future: test whether placing the wide angle 1-2 mm more coronal (tissue-level implant) avoids the complications by moving the plaque front >1 mm from crestal bone.

## 6. Related Work

- Cross-sectional/cohort data proposing 30°/40° thresholds: Katafuchi, Schwarz, Yi, Strauss. Rungtanakiat (BOP vs angle). de Tapia RCT (cleansability/BOP recurrence). Siegenthaler RCT (convex vs concave profile → recession). Waerhaug plaque radius-of-action hypothesis.

## 7. Glossary

- **Emergence/restorative angle**: angle between the average tangent of the transmucosal/transitional contour and the implant long axis.
- **MBL (marginal bone loss)**: corono-apical resorption measured from implant shoulder to first bone-to-implant contact.
- **fBIC (first bone-to-implant contact)**: most coronal bone-implant contact point (radiographic/micro-CT/histologic).
- **Junctional epithelium**: epithelial seal forming the "cuff-like" supracrestal soft-tissue barrier around the transmucosal segment.
- **GEE (generalized estimating equations)**: regression handling intra-subject correlation of repeated measures.
