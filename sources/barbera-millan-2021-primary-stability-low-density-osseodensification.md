## Why Ingested

Adds a controlled in-vitro head-to-head of Osseodensification (OD) against conventional **under-drilling (UD)** — not just hole-drilling — in low-density (type D4) bone, reinforcing [[wiki/implants/versah-protocols/mercier-2022-osseodensification-primary-stability-cadavers]] and [[wiki/implants/versah-protocols/moghaddas-2025-osseodensification-standard-drilling-isq-itv]] that OD raises insertion torque and ISQ. The large n (55/group) and the explicit UD comparator strengthen the evidence that OD's primary-stability benefit is real even versus the established under-sizing trick used in soft bone.

## One-line Summary

In vitro pig-tibia study (Lekholm-Zarb type IV bone, n=55 implants/group): Osseodensification produced significantly higher insertion torque (21.7 vs 8.9 Ncm) and ISQ (69.8 vs 65.2) than conventional under-drilling.

## 한줄요약

저밀도 돼지 경골(Lekholm-Zarb type IV) in vitro 연구(군당 임플란트 55개): 골밀도화(OD)가 기존 언더드릴링(UD)보다 식립 토크(21.7 vs 8.9 Ncm)와 ISQ(69.8 vs 65.2) 모두 유의하게 높았다.

- **Journal**: Medicina Oral Patología Oral y Cirugía Bucal 2021;26(3):e361–7
- **DOI**: 10.4317/medoral.24231
- **Study type**: In vitro experimental (porcine tibia bench model)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Direct in-vitro comparison of **Osseodensification (OD, Densah burs)** vs **conventional under-drilling (UD)** — the established soft-bone technique — rather than vs plain hole-drilling, in low-density bone.
- Large sample (110 implants, 55/group) with randomization of bone sections to reduce bias.
- Quantifies both biomechanical primary-stability parameters (insertion torque + RFA/ISQ) and models their association by sigmoid regression.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Implants**: 110 Klockner Vega internal-connection bone-level implants, 4 mm diameter × 10 mm length.
- **Bone model**: 30 coronal sections of frozen fresh pig tibia (mechanical properties resembling Lekholm-Zarb type D4 low-density human maxilla); cortical-bone-free coronal trabecular bone to isolate primary-stability effect.
- **Groups (randomized via envelopes, 55 each)**: Group 1 = under-drilling (UD, control); Group 2 = osseodensification (OD, Densah burs run counterclockwise at 1200 rpm with abundant irrigation). 3 implants per section.
- **Outcomes**: Insertion torque recorded with calibrated Implantmed motor (5 Ncm increments; >50 Ncm finished by ratchet and recorded as ">50 Ncm"); ISQ measured at 4 sites (mesial/distal/buccal/lingual) with Penguin RFA, mean used.
- **Statistics**: R; Student t-test for ISQ and torque between groups; sigmoid regression for torque–ISQ association.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Insertion torque**: UD 8.87 ± 6.17 Ncm vs OD 21.72 ± 17.14 Ncm (p=0.000).
- **ISQ (RFA)**: UD 65.16 ± 7.45 vs OD 69.75 ± 6.79 (p=0.001).
- OD significantly improved primary stability on both parameters.
- Sigmoid regression: ISQ rose with torque up to ~75.6 — a plateau beyond which more torque did not raise ISQ. Torque–ISQ fit was better in OD (pseudo r²=0.420) than UD (pseudo r²=0.272).
- Authors note all implants (UD or OD) reached values compatible with immediate loading per the literature.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In vitro only — pig tibia is a proxy for human type IV maxilla; no osseointegration, healing, or clinical follow-up.
- Single implant system / diameter / length; cortical bone removed, so results don't capture the cortical contribution to stability seen clinically.
- Bone sections inherently non-homogeneous (mitigated by n and randomization).
- OD ISQ values could not be benchmarked against prior literature (novel technique at the time).
- Authors call for clinical studies to confirm and to support OD use in low-density bone.

## 6. Related Work

- Huwais et al. — OD vs hole-drilling in porcine tibia: higher insertion torque with OD (corroborates).
- Santamaria-Arrieta et al. (2016) — corticotomized veal-rib trabecular model: similar torque/ISQ.
- Moon et al. (2010), Rastelli et al. (2014) — HD/UD/over-drilling porcine ribs without cortex: UD ISQ matches this study.
- Chávarri-Prado et al. (2020), Karl & Grobecker-Karl (2018) — polyurethane-block HD/UD/expansion baselines.

## 7. Glossary

- **Osseodensification (OD, 골밀도화)**: Non-subtractive drilling that compacts (densifies) trabecular bone apically and laterally using specialized burs (Densah) rotated counterclockwise with irrigation, increasing bone-to-implant contact and primary stability.
- **Under-drilling (UD, 언더드릴링)**: Preparing an osteotomy diameter notably smaller than the implant to raise mechanical engagement and primary stability in soft bone.
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient, 임플란트 안정성 지수)**: 1–100 RFA-derived measure of implant stability from the implant's natural vibration frequency in bone.
- **RFA (Resonance Frequency Analysis, 공명주파수분석)**: Method underlying ISQ; allows longitudinal stability monitoring, unlike insertion torque.
- **Type IV / D4 bone**: Lekholm-Zarb low-density bone — thin or absent cortex over sparse trabeculae, typical of posterior maxilla.
