## One-line Summary
하치조신경관(Inferior Alveolar Canal, IAC) 위치와 신경 손상 연관성 SR+MA (Healthcare 2022): 11편 포함; 설측(Lingual) 위치가 협측(Buccal) 대비 신경 손상 위험 유의하게 높음.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Healthcare 2022, 10, 1782
- **DOI**: 10.3390/healthcare10091782
- **Published**: 16 September 2022
- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis

## 2. Key Contributions
- Compared IAN injury rates between buccal vs lingual position of inferior alveolar canal (IAC) relative to lower third molar (LM3)
- Searched PubMed, Cochrane CENTRAL, Web of Science, Journals@Ovid (2009–2021); 1,063 articles screened; 11 studies included
- Meta-analysis (Review Manager, Cochrane): lingual IAC position significantly associated with higher IAN injury rate
- Provides quantitative basis for pre-surgical risk assessment using CBCT/OPG to determine canal position

## 3. Methodology
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane CENTRAL, Web of Science, Journals@Ovid
- **Publication period**: 2009–2021
- **Inclusion**: Studies comparing IAN injury rates by IAC position (buccal vs lingual to LM3)
- **Outcome**: IAN injury rate (paresthesia, anesthesia, neurosensory disturbance)
- **Meta-analysis**: Review Manager (Cochrane Collaboration)

## 4. Key Results
| IAC Position | IAN Injury Risk |
|---|---|
| Lingual (tongue-side) | Significantly higher — highest risk group |
| Buccal (cheek-side) | Lower risk |
| Overall meta-analysis | Lingual position: significantly increased IAN injury rate |

## 5. Limitations
- Publication year restriction (2009–2021) may miss relevant older literature
- Heterogeneity in IAN injury definition and assessment methods
- Some studies used OPG only (vs CBCT), limiting position accuracy
- All included studies observational; no RCTs
