## One-line Summary
Systematic review (J Clin Med 2025, 29 articles): comparing 5 implant bed preparation methods (classical drilling, laser, piezoelectric, osteotomy/osteotomes, osseodensification) on histological and histomorphometric outcomes — classical drilling causes more microcracks, uneven margins, osteocyte damage, and thermal injury; alternatives yield higher %BIC and less damage; classical drilling can still match outcomes if rotation/cooling/load are optimal.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Compares all five major preparation methods in one synthesis.
- Histology + histomorphometry as primary outcomes (not just temperature).
- Notes that classical drilling outcomes are protocol-dependent: with correct rotation, cooling, load, equivalence is achievable.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- PRISMA SR; March 2025 search.
- 5 method families: drilling, laser, piezoelectric surgery, osteotomy/osteotomes, osseodensification.
- Outcomes: microcracks, bone margin morphology, osteocyte vitality, thermal injury, %BIC, healing.
- 29 articles included; heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Classical drilling: more microcracks, uneven margins, osteocyte damage, thermal injury, lower %BIC than alternatives.
- Alternative methods (laser, piezo, osteotomes, osseodensification): fewer microcracks, less inflammation, lower thermal risk, denser/more regular bone, higher %BIC.
- Classical drilling can match alternatives when rotation, cooling, and load are optimal.
- Uniform methodology and clear parameter standards are absent across the field.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis.
- Outcomes pooled across heterogeneous animal/in vitro models.
- Long-term clinical data limited.
- Future: standardized parameter studies for each method.

## 7. Glossary
- **Osseodensification**: counter-rotation densifying-bur technique (Huwais, Densah bur).
- **Osteotomy (osteotomes)**: Summer's osteotome bone condensation.
- **%BIC**: percent bone-to-implant contact (histomorphometric).
- **Microcracks**: subsurface bone fissures induced by mechanical drilling.
