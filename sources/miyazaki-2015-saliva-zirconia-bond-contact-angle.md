## One-line Summary
Dental Materials Journal 2015;34(1):91-97: saliva contamination of zirconia reduces resin cement bond strength; contact angle measurement correlates with bondability; cleaning with NaOH-ZrO2 agent (Ivoclean) most effective.

## 1. Document Information
Journal: Dental Materials Journal 2015;34(1):91-97
Authors: Masashi Miyazaki et al., Nihon University
DOI: 10.4012/dmj.2014-066

## 2. Key Contributions
- Saliva contamination significantly lowers contact angle → reduced wettability → lower bond strength
- NaOH/ZrO2-based cleaning agent (Ivoclean) restores surface energy better than phosphoric acid or acetone
- Contact angle measurement serves as predictor of zirconia bondability after cleaning

## 3. Methodology
- Zirconia discs air-abraded with 50 µm Al2O3
- Contaminated with artificial saliva; cleaned with various agents (water, phosphoric acid, acetone, Ivoclean)
- Contact angle measured by goniometry; bond strength by shear test after 24h + 10,000 thermocycles

## 4. Key Results
- Ivoclean cleaning: lowest contact angle (~10°), highest bond strength
- Phosphoric acid: intermediate cleaning effect
- Water alone: inadequate decontamination
- Bond strength correlated with surface free energy restoration

## 5. Limitations
- In vitro only; no aging beyond 10,000 cycles
- Single zirconia brand tested
