## Why Ingested

기존 보유 임플란트 주위염(Peri-implantitis) 치료 논문에서 미노사이클린(Minocycline) 국소투여 RCT 없음; 다기관 설계(두 한국 치과대학병원)로 신뢰도 높음. 전신 항생제 노출 최소화 전략으로서 국소약물전달(Local Drug Delivery, LDD)의 임상 근거를 보강. [[wiki/implants/peri-implantitis]]

## One-line Summary

Multicenter single-blind RCT (n=46) showing adjunctive subgingival 2% minocycline ointment reduces probing depth and BOP at 12 weeks versus debridement alone in peri-implantitis, with transient microbiologic benefits at 8 weeks but insufficient follow-up for radiographic conclusions.

## 한줄요약

다기관 단일맹검 RCT(n=46): 임플란트 주위염 비외과적 치료 시 2% 미노사이클린 연고 보조요법이 12주 시점에 탐침깊이(Probing Depth, PD)와 탐침출혈(Bleeding on Probing, BOP)을 유의하게 개선하나, 방사선학적 골안정성 결론을 도출하기엔 추적 기간 부족.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First multicenter Korean RCT evaluating locally delivered 2% minocycline HCl (ointment) as adjunct to subgingival mechanical debridement for peri-implantitis
- Multidimensional outcomes: clinical (PD, BOP), radiographic (MBL), microbiologic (peri-implant pathogens), and patient-reported (discomfort, OHRQoL)
- Demonstrates short-term PD and BOP benefit without systemic antibiotic exposure
- Provides evidence for local drug delivery (LDD) strategy limiting antimicrobial resistance risk

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

abstract-only — full text not retrieved

- **Design**: Single-blind (examiner-blind), randomized, placebo-controlled, multicenter RCT
- **Centers**: Two Korean dental university hospitals (Jeonbuk National University + Wonkwang University)
- **Randomization**: 48 participants randomized; 46 analyzed per-protocol (2 withdrawals post-randomization in test group)
- **Groups**:
  - Test (n=22): subgingival debridement + 2% minocycline HCl ointment
  - Control (n=24): subgingival debridement + placebo ointment
- **Intervention timing**: Baseline, weeks 1, 2, and 3
- **Follow-up**: 12 weeks
- **Outcome measures**: PD (probing pocket depth), BOP (bleeding on probing), MBL (marginal bone level, radiographic), microbiologic counts of peri-implant pathogens, patient discomfort, OHRQoL

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

abstract-only — full text not retrieved

- **PD**: Test group showed significantly lower baseline-adjusted probing pocket depth at 12 weeks vs. control
- **BOP**: Less frequent bleeding on probing in test group at 12 weeks
- **Microbiologic**: Lower levels of selected peri-implant pathogens in test group at 8 weeks (p < 0.05); between-group differences less consistent at 12 weeks
- **Radiographic**: Results should be interpreted cautiously — 12-week period too short for structural stability conclusions
- **Swelling (early)**: Lower in test group at 3 weeks (p < 0.05)
- **OHRQoL**: No significant between-group differences in improvement
- **Clinical relevance**: Short-term clinical benefit; limits systemic antibiotic exposure

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

abstract-only — full text not retrieved

- Small sample size (n=46 per-protocol)
- Short follow-up (12 weeks): insufficient for radiographic MBL stability conclusions or definitive disease resolution
- Single-blind design (not double-blind)
- Microbiologic benefits at 8 weeks not sustained consistently to 12 weeks — long-term microbiologic durability unknown
- No data beyond 12 weeks; longer-term RCTs needed

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary

- **Peri-implantitis (임플란트 주위염)**: Inflammatory condition affecting soft and hard tissues around dental implants; characterized by BOP, PD increase, and progressive MBL
- **Minocycline (미노사이클린)**: Tetracycline-class broad-spectrum antibiotic; 2% ointment formulation used for subgingival local delivery
- **BOP (Bleeding on Probing, 탐침출혈)**: Clinical indicator of peri-implant/periodontal inflammation
- **PD (Probing Depth, 탐침깊이)**: Depth of peri-implant sulcus/pocket measured with periodontal probe
- **MBL (Marginal Bone Level, 변연골 수준)**: Radiographic measurement of crestal bone around implant
- **Local Drug Delivery (LDD, 국소약물전달)**: Placement of antimicrobial agent directly into peri-implant pocket; limits systemic exposure
- **OHRQoL (Oral Health-Related Quality of Life, 구강건강관련 삶의 질)**: Patient-reported outcome measure
