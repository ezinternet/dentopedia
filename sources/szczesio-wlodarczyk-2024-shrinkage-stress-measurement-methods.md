## Why Ingested

[[resin/meereis-2018-polymerization-shrinkage-stress-resin-sr-ma]]와 [[resin/parra-gatica-2023-flowable-bulk-fill-composites-sr]]가 보고하는 "수축응력 2-3 MPa" 같은 절대값을 해석할 때, 측정 방법(photoelastic vs contraction-force)에 따라 같은 재료의 수축응력 값이 수배 차이 난다는 methodological caveat가 위키에 없었다. 본 연구는 동일 10종 레진을 두 방법으로 측정해 값의 비교 가능성/한계를 보강한다 — 다른 논문 간 stress 수치를 함부로 비교하면 안 된다는 근거. abstract-only.

## One-line Summary
In-vitro methodological comparison (10 commercial composites) showing photoelastic shrinkage-stress values (6.4-13.4 MPa) are several-fold higher than contraction-force values (1.2-4.8 MPa) for the same materials, yet the two methods correlate strongly (r=0.8).

## 한줄요약
In-vitro 방법론 비교(상용 레진 10종): 동일 재료라도 photoelastic 법(6.4-13.4 MPa)이 contraction-force 법(1.2-4.8 MPa)보다 수배 높은 수축응력 값을 주지만, 두 방법은 강하게 상관(r=0.8) — 논문 간 절대값 비교 시 측정법 확인 필수.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Demonstrates that absolute shrinkage-stress numbers are method-dependent: photoelastic >> contraction-force for identical composites
- Establishes a strong linear correlation (r=0.8) between the two methods — rankings are largely preserved even if magnitudes are not
- Provides a caution for cross-study comparison: 2-3 MPa from one paper is not directly comparable to 10+ MPa from another using a different rig
- Computes slope of linear trendline and C-factor for each specimen

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Two methods on the same 10 commercial composites: photoelastic method and contraction-forces measurement
- Slope of linear trendline + C-factor calculated per specimen
- Comparison of value ranges and inter-method correlation

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Photoelastic stress range: 6.4-13.4 MPa
- Contraction-force stress range: 1.2-4.8 MPa
- Inter-method correlation: r = 0.8 (strong, positive, linear)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Abstract-only; per-material values, C-factor details, and statistics not extracted
- No tooth/cavity model; bench rigs only
- Does not establish which method better predicts clinical microleakage/cuspal deflection

## 6. Related Work
- Complements composition-strategy SR+MA (Meereis 2018) and flowable BF property SR (Parra-Gatica 2023) by addressing measurement validity

## 7. Glossary
- **Photoelastic method**: stress inferred from birefringence in a photoelastic medium around the curing composite. **Contraction-force method**: stress measured directly as force/area between bonded rigid surfaces (e.g., Bioman-type). **C-factor**: ratio of bonded to unbonded surface area.
