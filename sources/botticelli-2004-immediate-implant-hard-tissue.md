## One-line Summary
J Clin Periodontol 2004;31(10):820-828: 18 humans, 21 immediate implants, no membrane/filler, 4-month re-entry — buccal bone resorbed ~56%, lingual/palatal ~30%; gap fills completely; no prosthetic loading at 4 months.

## 1. Key Results
- 18 subjects, 21 immediate implants (premolar/molar sites); no membrane, no grafting
- Buccal bone height resorbed 56% (from 3.6mm gap to residual ~1.8mm expected resorption)
- Lingual/palatal: ~30% height reduction
- Marginal gap fills in with woven bone and connective tissue by 4 months
- Implant contact with bone at re-entry: histologically confirmed osseointegration
- Conclusion: significant buccal bone loss occurs even with proper immediate implant placement

## 2. Methodology
- Prospective human in-vivo study
- Sites: maxillary/mandibular premolars and molars
- Measurements at implant placement and surgical re-entry at 4 months
- No gap management (no membrane, no bone graft)
- Variables: buccal/lingual/mesial/distal bone height, gap width

## 3. Clinical Implications
- Immediate implant does NOT preserve ridge dimensions
- Buccal plate resorbs substantially — esthetic risk in anterior sites
- Supports need for contour augmentation at time of placement
- Foundational reference establishing the "buccal wall resorption" paradigm
