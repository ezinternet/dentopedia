## Why Ingested

Quantifies the absolute amount of external apical root resorption (EARR) attributable to clear aligners using pre/post CBCT — the safety endpoint that comparative aligner-vs-fixed reviews only addressed relatively. Reinforces [[wiki/orthodontics/clear-aligner/baneshi-2024-clear-aligners-malocclusion-sr-ma]], whose RCT efficacy synthesis noted a root-resorption trend favoring clear aligners, by supplying the magnitude (−0.56 mm overall) and the worst-affected tooth (maxillary central incisor).

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (9 CBCT studies) — clear aligners cause minimal but measurable EARR: overall incisor root length −0.56 mm and upper-incisor root volume −13.34 mm³, greatest at maxillary central incisors (−0.74 mm).

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Root resorption in clear aligner treatment detected by CBCT: a Systematic review and Meta-analysis
- **Authors**: Butsabul P, Kanpittaya P, Nantanee R
- **Journal**: International Dental Journal 2024;74(6):1326–1336
- **Year**: 2024 (publication date 2024-05-01)
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.identj.2024.04.008 · PMID 38692962 · PMCID PMC11551579
- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD42022372249; PRISMA; Cochrane Handbook)
- **Risk-of-bias tools**: Cochrane RoB (RCT) + ROBINS-I (non-RCT)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA to quantify the **absolute** amount of EARR from clear aligners using **pre- vs post-treatment** CBCT values — prior reviews only compared aligners against fixed appliances (relative, confounded by differing indications/duration/mechanism).
- Pooled overall incisor root-length loss: **MD = −0.56 mm** (95% CI −0.73, −0.38; P < .00001) — slightly above Gandhi et al.'s prior 0.44 mm estimate.
- Identifies the **maxillary central incisor** as the worst-affected tooth (−0.74 mm), confirmed by 3D volume data.
- Adds a 3D root-volume endpoint: upper-incisor volume loss **MD = −13.34 mm³** (95% CI −16.57, −10.10; P < .00001).
- Argues CBCT is more reliable than 2D panoramic/periapical for EARR quantification (2D over/underestimates; RCR-based panoramic data too heterogeneous to pool).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases**: PubMed, SCOPUS, EBSCO, Cochrane Library, LILACS (to May 2023) + manual/reference-list search; no language/date restriction.
- **PICOS**: Population = permanent dentition needing ortho; Intervention = comprehensive clear aligner treatment; Comparison = pre vs post; Outcome = amount of root resorption; Design = RCT, prospective, retrospective. Excluded: syndromes, pathology, periodontitis, prior root resorption history.
- **Screening**: 435 retrieved → 355 after de-duplication → 34 after title/abstract → **16 qualitative** → **9 quantitative** (meta-analysis). Two independent reviewers (PB/PK), third arbiter (RN); EndNote 20.4.1.
- **Included pool**: 16 studies (1 RCT, 1 prospective, 14 retrospective); publication 2013–2023; **638 patients, 6524 teeth**; treatment duration 15–30 months. Breakdown: 7 pre/post aligner-only, 3 aligner ± adjunct (HFV, LIPUS), 6 aligner vs fixed (PEA/self-ligating).
- **Outcomes pooled**: root length change (mm) and root volume loss (mm³), CBCT only. 4 panoramic studies (relative root-crown ratio, %) could NOT be pooled — heterogeneous outcome metric.
- **Statistics**: MD with 95% CI; I² > 40% = heterogeneity → random-effects; subgroup + sensitivity (removing Chen et al.) + funnel-plot publication-bias analysis. RevMan 5.4.
- **Risk of bias**: RCT = moderate (blinding of participants/examiner high risk due to obvious radiographic-method differences; small sample after dropout). ROBINS-I: 11 moderate, 4 high (outcome measurement + retrospective design; >half lacked sample-size calculation).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Overall incisor root length**: MD = −0.56 mm (95% CI −0.73, −0.38; P < .00001). Funnel plot symmetrical (small publication bias).
- **Maxillary central incisor** (7 studies): MD = −0.74 mm (95% CI −0.98, −0.49; P < .01) — greatest resorption. Sensitivity test (remove Chen et al.): −0.64 mm, still > lateral incisors.
- **Maxillary lateral incisor** (6 studies): MD = −0.56 mm (95% CI −0.92, −0.21; P = .002), I² = 33%.
- **Mandibular central incisor** (3 studies): MD = −0.47 mm (95% CI −0.78, −0.17; P = .002) — significant.
- **Mandibular lateral incisor** (3 studies): MD = −0.50 mm (95% CI −1.24, −0.21; P = .16) — NOT significant, I² = 80% (very high heterogeneity).
- **Maxillary canine** (3 studies): MD = −0.76 mm (95% CI −1.76, −0.24; P = .14) — NS, I² = 70%.
- **Mandibular canine** (3 studies): MD = −0.51 mm (95% CI −1.26, −0.24; P = .18) — NS, I² = 62%.
- **Root volume — overall incisors** (3 studies): MD = −13.34 mm³ (95% CI −16.57, −10.10; P < .00001).
  - Maxillary central incisor: −19.91 mm³ (95% CI −25.94, −13.88; P < .01).
  - Maxillary lateral incisor: −12.06 mm³ (95% CI −18.67, −5.45; P < .01).
- **2D / qualitative findings**: EARR incidence 41.81–100% (all patients had ≥1 resorbed tooth); most mild (<10%); severe (>20%) in only ~3.69% (Gay) and ~6.31% (Krieger) of teeth, mostly maxillary incisors. 2D ~0.2 mm higher than CBCT (overestimation).
- **Risk factors**: age and sex generally not significant (Aman: women < men, P < .4); treatment duration controversial (3 studies NS; Yi r = 0.3651, P < .0205); extraction increased risk (Liu); severe crowding > mild (Aman); vertical movement (intrusion/extrusion) positively correlated; root apex approximation to palatal cortical plate strongly correlated with severity.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Scarcity of high-quality RCTs — pool dominated by retrospective non-RCTs (risk of bias).
- Only anterior teeth (central/lateral incisors, canines) had poolable data; total-dentition EARR untested.
- 4 panoramic studies excluded from quantitative synthesis (incompatible RCR metric, heterogeneity).
- High heterogeneity in canine and mandibular-lateral-incisor subgroups (I² 62–80%).
- Volume measured as absolute (mm³), biasing larger teeth toward more apparent loss (Liu: relative % loss put lateral > central).
- Future needs: studies in extraction cases, severe crowding, large tooth movement, and whole-dentition EARR.

## 7. Glossary

- **EARR (External Apical Root Resorption)**: loss of apical root tissue, the resorption pattern typical of orthodontic tooth movement; mostly irreversible.
- **CBCT (Cone-Beam Computed Tomography)**: 3D imaging giving more accurate/reliable root-length and root-volume measurement than 2D radiography.
- **RCR (Root-Crown Ratio)**: relative root-length metric used in 2D panoramic studies; scale-stable but not poolable here.
- **ROBINS-I**: Risk Of Bias In Non-randomised Studies of Interventions tool.
- **MD (Mean Difference)**: pooled effect size for continuous outcomes (here, mm of root length or mm³ of volume).
- **I²**: heterogeneity statistic; > 40% triggered random-effects modeling.
- **LIPUS / HFV**: low-intensity pulsed ultrasound / high-frequency vibration — adjunct devices tested alongside aligners.
