## Why Ingested

에센셜 오일 가글 (Essential Oil Mouthwash, EOMW)이 구강 미생물군집 (Oral Microbiome)의 구성에 미치는 영향을 정량적으로 분석한 RCT로, 기존 16S 상대 빈도 연구보다 훨씬 정밀한 "spike-in 절대 정량" 방법론을 처음 적용한 연구다. 이상증식 (Dysbiosis) → 건강한 구성으로의 전환 메커니즘 증거를 제시하여 [[oral-microbiology/lamont-2018-oral-microbiota-dynamic-communities-host]]가 정립한 구강 미생물 생태학 역학 모델을 직접 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

Parallel-group RCT (n=153 gingivitis subjects, 6 weeks); alcohol-containing EO mouthrinses (LCM and ACPM) produced ≥37% gingivitis reduction and shifted dysbiotic plaque microbiome toward healthy composition using a novel spike-in quantitative metagenomics method.

## 한줄요약

병렬군 무작위대조시험 (Randomized Controlled Trial, RCT) (n=153, 6주); 알코올 함유 에센셜 오일 가글 (Essential Oil Mouthwash, EOMW) (LCM·ACPM)이 치은염 (Gingivitis) ≥37% 감소 및 이상증식 (Dysbiosis) 치태 (Plaque) 미생물군집을 건강 상태로 전환함을 spike-in 절대 정량 메타게놈 (Metagenome) 분석으로 최초 입증.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First application of spike-in quantitative 절대 정량 메타게놈 시퀀싱 (Quantitative Shotgun Metagenomic Sequencing) to an oral care product RCT — overcomes the compositional bias of relative abundance methods (16S rRNA)
- Demonstrated that LCM and ACPM use for 4–6 weeks shifts dysbiotic gingivitis microbiome toward the composition of naturally healthy subjects (beta-diversity convergence)
- Identified a "microbiome reset" mechanism: EO mouthrinses do not selectively kill pathogens but broadly reduce all bacteria, allowing early-colonizing health-associated commensals to reestablish
- Validated the spike-in quantitative approach against colony-counting and 16S rRNA qPCR, confirming concordance within expected ranges
- Alcohol-free EO prototype (AFPM) showed reduced microbiological efficacy (no significant TML reduction beyond week 1) despite comparable gingivitis clinical improvement, suggesting additional non-plaque mechanisms for gingivitis reduction

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Design**: Randomized, controlled, examiner-blind, single-center, parallel-group clinical trial (Oct–Dec 2019, Salus Research Inc., Fort Wayne IN)

**Groups** (4 arms, moderate gingivitis subjects; n=32 healthy reference cohort for comparison only):
1. **LCM** (LISTERINE Cool Mint Antiseptic): 0.092% eucalyptol, 0.042% menthol, 0.060% methyl salicylate, 0.064% thymol, 21.6% alcohol (n=31 completers)
2. **ACPM** (alcohol-containing prototype): Same EO formula + new sensorial flavor ingredient, 21.6% alcohol (n=29 completers)
3. **AFPM** (alcohol-free prototype): Same EO formula + new sensorial flavor, no alcohol (n=30 completers)
4. **HA** (hydroalcohol negative control): 5% hydroalcohol, no EOs (n=31 completers)

**Treatment protocol**: Brushing (twice daily) + 30 s rinse with 20 mL of assigned mouthrinse (twice daily) for 6 weeks. Fluoridated toothpaste + standard soft toothbrush provided to all subjects.

**Inclusion criteria (gingivitis subjects)**: ≥18 years, ≥20 natural teeth including 4 molars; whole-mouth mean MGI ≥1.95, TPI ≥1.95, EBI ≥10% bleeding sites, ≤3 sites with PPD >5 mm.

**Clinical endpoints**: Primary = mean Modified Gingival Index (MGI) and Turesky Plaque Index (TPI) at week 6; secondary = same at week 4, Expanded Bleeding Index (EBI) at weeks 4 and 6.

**Microbiome collection**: Supragingival plaque from 4 teeth (upper right first molar, upper right lateral incisor, lower left second molar, lower left lateral incisor) at weeks 1, 4, 6; pooled per subject; stored at -80°C.

**Novel quantitative microbiome method**:
- Shallow shotgun metagenomic sequencing (SMS) at CosmosID Inc.
- Samples spiked with **ZymoBIOMICS Spike-in Control II** (Zymo Research) prior to DNA extraction
- Linear regression of spike-in control input DNA against output relative abundance → conversion to absolute **calculated microbial units (CMUs)**
- 856 unique taxa identified at species level; 213 classified as human oral bacteria; further subdivided into commensals, gingivitis-associated, periodontitis-associated, malodor-associated, caries-associated
- Alpha diversity: Shannon-Weaver index, observed richness; Beta diversity: weighted UniFrac + PCoA + PERMANOVA

**Sample size**: 30/group required (effect size 0.75, α=0.05, 80% power); 32/group recruited (5% dropout allowance); 128 gingivitis + 32 healthy enrolled (157 total); 4 dropouts.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Clinical efficacy at week 6 vs. HA control**:
- MGI reduction: LCM −44.4% (p<0.001), ACPM −37.4% (p<0.001), AFPM −40.6% (p<0.001)
- TPI (plaque) reduction: LCM −26.2% (p<0.001), ACPM −27.3% (p<0.001), AFPM −14.0% (p<0.001)
- EBI (bleeding) reduction: LCM −38.0% (p<0.001), ACPM −36.3% (p<0.001), AFPM −40.0% (p<0.001)

**Total microbial load (TML) — longitudinal**:
- LCM and ACPM: significant TML reductions at weeks 1, 4, and 6 vs. baseline
- AFPM: significant reduction only at week 1
- HA control: no significant TML changes vs. baseline

**Alpha diversity**:
- Shannon-Weaver index: LCM and ACPM significantly reduced after 4 and 6 weeks; AFPM and HA not significantly changed
- Observed species richness: LCM and ACPM significantly decreased after 6 weeks

**Microbiome category-specific reductions (weeks 1, 4, 6 vs. HA)**:
- Commensals: significantly reduced by LCM and ACPM (not AFPM)
- Gingivitis-associated species: significantly reduced by LCM and ACPM
- Malodor/halitosis-associated species: significantly reduced by LCM and ACPM
- Acidogenic (caries-associated) species: no significant differences between groups (by design — caries-risk subjects were excluded)

**Return from dysbiosis**:
- Beta-diversity (weighted UniFrac PCoA): LCM and ACPM subjects' microbiome converged toward healthy reference cohort composition after 4 weeks; AFPM and HA subjects remained clustered with gingivitis profiles
- This is described as the first demonstration of LCM twice-daily use reverting a gingivitis-associated microbiome to a healthier state

**Prominent species significantly elevated in gingivitis vs. healthy at baseline** (absolute quantification, logCMU):
- Fusobacterium nucleatum: healthy 2.968 vs. gingivitis 4.472 (Δ+1.504, p=0.001)
- Candidatus Saccharibacteria TM7x: 3.929 vs. 5.055 (Δ+1.126, p=0.002)
- Prevotella veroralis: 1.876 vs. 3.339 (Δ+1.463, p=0.002)

**Methodology validation**: SMS spike-in CMU values aligned well with colony counting for total anaerobes, Streptococci, and Actinomyces (within published ranges); 16S qPCR showed values ~1 order of magnitude higher due to non-specific amplification of aerobic/transient extraoral species.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Industry sponsorship (Johnson & Johnson)**: Multiple authors are J&J employees; the study was designed and funded by the manufacturer of LISTERINE. This creates substantial bias risk — results favor the commercial product, and the "prototype" comparators are investigational J&J products. Findings should be interpreted cautiously pending independent replication.
- **Single center, short duration**: 6-week trial at one site in Indiana; long-term microbiome effects and reversibility upon cessation are unknown.
- **Demographic homogeneity**: 89.5% Caucasian, 100% non-smokers; generalizability to diverse populations, smokers, or periodontitis patients is limited.
- **No analysis of host inflammatory response**: The study measured microbial outcomes but not cytokines, immune cell infiltration, or other host-response markers that would provide mechanistic insight into disease modification.
- **AFPM paradox**: The alcohol-free prototype showed comparable gingivitis reduction (−40.6%) to alcohol-containing mouthrinses despite lower microbiological efficacy — the non-plaque mechanism for this effect is uncharacterized.
- **Relative vs. absolute**: Despite the methodological advance, CMU values depend on spike-in efficiency assumptions; independent validation against flow cytometry or other gold-standard absolute counting methods was not performed.
- **Microbiome categorization subjectivity**: Classification of 213 oral species into health/disease categories was based on literature review; some species appear in multiple categories (overlap acknowledged in Additional File 1).

## 7. Glossary

- **에센셜 오일 가글 (Essential Oil Mouthwash, EOMW)**: Mouthrinse formulated with a combination of essential oils (thymol, eucalyptol, menthol, methyl salicylate) providing broad-spectrum bactericidal action. LISTERINE and its prototypes are the prototype EOMW products studied here.
- **이상증식 (Dysbiosis)**: Disruption of the stable ecological equilibrium of the oral microbiome, characterized by a shift toward increased abundance of pathogenic bacteria relative to health-associated commensals.
- **얕은 샷건 메타게놈 시퀀싱 (Shallow Shotgun Metagenomic Sequencing, SMS)**: DNA sequencing method that reads all DNA in a sample (host + microbial), providing species-level taxonomic and functional profiles; more specific than 16S rRNA amplicon sequencing.
- **Spike-in 절대 정량 (Spike-in Quantification)**: Method where known amounts of reference DNA from non-human bacteria are added to samples before processing; enables conversion of relative sequencing reads into absolute bacterial cell numbers (CMUs) via linear regression.
- **계산 미생물 단위 (Calculated Microbial Units, CMU)**: Output unit of the spike-in quantification method; represents the estimated absolute number of bacterial cells of a given species per sample, expressed as log10 values in this study.
- **수정 치은 지수 (Modified Gingival Index, MGI)**: Clinical index scoring gingival inflammation on a 0–4 scale (0=normal, 4=severe) used as the primary efficacy endpoint.
- **베타 다양성 (Beta Diversity, β-diversity)**: Measure of difference in microbial community composition between samples; assessed here by weighted UniFrac distance + PCoA ordination to visualize whether gingivitis microbiomes converged toward healthy reference composition.
