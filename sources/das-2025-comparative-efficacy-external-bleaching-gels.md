## Why Ingested

Provides a comparative summary of CP vs HP bleaching gel efficacy and sensitivity (ΔE and ΔSGU outcomes) needed to contextualize the concentration NMA findings — extends the evidence base in [[tooth-whitening/terra-2025-at-home-bleaching-concentration-tooth-sensitivity]] by including a head-to-head agent comparison with GRADE quality-of-evidence rating.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (PROSPERO CRD42023483434) comparing HP vs CP external bleaching gels found carbamide peroxide superior by ΔE but equivalent by ΔSGU; both have moderate-quality evidence for sensitivity outcomes; higher-concentration CP showed less sensitivity than higher-concentration HP.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Compared HP-based vs CP-based external bleaching gels for whitening performance and longevity
- Found CP gels superior on ΔE (spectrophotometric color change) though equivalent on ΔSGU (shade guide units)
- Graded quality of evidence as moderate for ΔE*, sensitivity risk, and sensitivity intensity across all agent types
- Noted that higher-concentration CP gels produce less tooth sensitivity than higher-concentration HP gels — consistent with slower peroxide release mechanism of CP
- Covers whitening longevity as a secondary outcome (an area not well-addressed in previous SRs)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search sources**: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, Cochrane Library (July 2024; updated August 1, 2024)
- **Reviewers**: Two independent reviewers
- **Primary outcomes**: Changes in tooth color (ΔE by spectrophotometer/colorimeter; ΔSGU by shade guide)
- **Subgroup analysis**: CP vs HP gel subgroups; various concentrations
- **Heterogeneity**: Cochran Q test + I-statistic
- **GRADE**: Applied to quality-of-evidence assessment

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Comparison | ΔE Result | ΔSGU Result |
|---|---|---|
| CP vs HP | CP superior | Equivalent |
| High-concentration CP vs high-concentration HP | — | CP less sensitivity |
| Quality of evidence | Moderate | Moderate |

- Higher-concentration CP bleaching gels produced less tooth sensitivity than higher-concentration HP — attributed to slower, more sustained peroxide release from CP (which decomposes first to urea + HP)
- Whitening longevity data available but not quantitatively pooled (SR level)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only available; full methodology and forest plots not accessible
- No formal meta-analysis (SR only), limiting effect size pooling
- Moderate (not high) quality of evidence limits clinical guideline translation
- Longevity data insufficiently powered to draw firm conclusions

## 7. Glossary

- **ΔE (Delta E)**: Spectrophotometric color difference; ΔE > 3.3 considered visible to naked eye; ΔE > 1.0 clinically perceptible
- **ΔSGU (Delta Shade Guide Units)**: Change in shade guide steps; more clinically intuitive but less precise than ΔE
- **CP (Carbamide Peroxide)**: Bleaching agent that decomposes into urea + HP; slower release, lower peak HP, less sensitivity
- **HP (Hydrogen Peroxide)**: Direct oxidant; faster-acting, higher peak peroxide, more sensitivity at same concentration
- **PROSPERO**: International prospective register of systematic reviews
