## Why Ingested

상악동 점막비후가 임플란트 및 골이식 성공률에 미치는 임상적 영향을 CBCT로 직접 평가한 후향적 연구. "생리적 점막비후는 임플란트 실패와 무관하다"는 핵심 임상 결론을 제공. 새로운 "Mucosal Thickening Index" 제안. [[sinus-lift/lateral/akbari-2022-maxillary-sinus-mucosal-thickening-ridge-height]]와 함께 점막비후 임상의사결정의 근거.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective CBCT study (n=29, University of Michigan, mean follow-up 3.3y) showed 100% implant survival despite 93.1% prevalence of mucosal thickening; only periodontal disease history was significantly associated with thickening severity.

## 한줄요약

CBCT 후향적 연구(n=29, 추적 3.3년): 93.1%에서 점막비후 존재에도 임플란트 생존율 100%, 치주질환 과거력만이 비후 정도와 유의한 연관인자.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Retrospective cohort study
- **Journal**: International Journal of Implant Dentistry (2017)
- **DOI**: [10.1186/s40729-017-0064-8](https://doi.org/10.1186/s40729-017-0064-8)
- **Sample**: 29 CBCT scans, University of Michigan School of Dentistry, 2004-2014; mean follow-up 3.3±2.2y

## 2. Key Contributions

- First study proposing a 4-tier "Mucosal Thickening Index": no (≤1mm), minimal (1-2mm), moderate (2-5mm), severe (>5mm)
- Demonstrated 100% implant survival with physiologic mucosal thickening (up to 1/3 sinus volume)
- Identified periodontal disease as the only independent predictor of mucosal thickening
- Proposed clinical threshold: mucosal thickening not contraindicated for SFE if no sinusitis/infection signs

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Retrospective CBCT analysis (i-CAT scanner)
- Inclusion: partial edentulism, ≥18y, received dental implants after sinus grafting, clinical + radiographic follow-up
- Exclusion: unclear CBCT, no follow-up, periapical pathosis, implants outside sinus area
- Measurements: sagittal views at ¼, ½, ¾ of AP sinus width; maximum thickening point also recorded
- Standardized orientation: zygoma horizontal (axial), hard palate horizontal (coronal/sagittal)
- Linear mixed models for associations; intra-examiner reproducibility 99%
- Variables tested: age, gender, smoking, respiratory/cardiovascular/diabetes history, periodontal disease, endodontic treatment, edentulism, ridge height

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Mucosal Thickening Index distribution:**
| Category | Threshold | Prevalence |
|---|---|---|
| No thickening | ≤1mm | 6.9% |
| Minimal | 1-2mm | 6.9% |
| Moderate | 2-5mm | 20.7% |
| Severe | >5mm | 65.5% |

- **Implant survival rate**: 100% (all patients, including those with severe thickening)
- **Mean maximum thickening**: 8.34±5.70mm (range 1.55-22.81mm)
- **Middle section average**: 4.87±5.10mm; anterior: 4.63±4.95mm; posterior: 3.46±3.17mm
- **Only significant factor**: periodontal disease history (p=0.004) — periodontal disease 3-5× more likely to have mucosal thickening
- **Not significant**: gender (p=0.054), smoking (p=0.541), endodontic treatment (p=0.379), ridge height (p=0.889)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample size (n=29) due to strict inclusion criteria
- Primarily Caucasian North American cohort — ethnic/geographic generalizability limited
- No sinusitis patients included — cannot comment on pathologic thickening outcomes
- CBCT measurement standardization challenges with anatomical variation

## 7. Glossary

- **Mucosal Thickening Index**: 4-tier classification proposed by this study (no/minimal/moderate/severe)
- **Sinus floor elevation (SFE)**: surgical procedure to augment bone height in posterior maxilla by lifting Schneiderian membrane
- **Physiologic mucosal thickening**: thickening without radiographic signs of infection; clinically not a contraindication per this study
