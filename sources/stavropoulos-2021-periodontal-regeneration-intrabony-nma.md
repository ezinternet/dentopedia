## One-line Summary
SR and NMA of 30 RCTs (3–20 years post-op) showing periodontal regenerative/reconstructive therapy in intrabony defects produces significantly better residual PD and CAL gain than open flap debridement, with combination approaches (GTR+grafting, EMD+grafting) appearing most efficacious.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Periodontology, 2021; 48(3):410–430
- **DOI**: 10.1111/jcpe.13409
- **First published**: 01 December 2020 (online)
- **Access**: Open Access
- **Study type**: Systematic review with Bayesian random-effects network meta-analysis (NMA)

## 2. Key Contributions
- First NMA providing a hierarchy of periodontal regenerative/reconstructive procedures for medium- to long-term outcomes (3–20 years)
- Demonstrates OFD is the least efficacious treatment; all regenerative approaches outperform it
- Combination therapies (GTR + grafting, EMD + grafting) appear superior to monotherapies
- Tooth loss after regenerative treatment is rare (0.4%) vs OFD (2.8%)
- Provides evidence-based framework for selecting treatment modality in intrabony defects

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: Systematic review with Bayesian random-effects network meta-analysis (NMA)
- **Focused question**: "In periodontitis patients with intrabony defects, what are the medium- and long-term benefits of periodontal regenerative/reconstructive procedures compared with OFD?"
- **Included studies**: 30 RCTs (overall review); 21 RCTs in NMA
- **Follow-up**: ≥3 years post-operatively; range 3–20 years
- **Treatments compared**: Grafting, GTR, EMD, combinations thereof, blood-derived growth factor constructs, OFD only
- **Outcomes**: 
  - Clinical: Residual PD, CAL gain, tooth loss
  - Radiographic: Residual defect depth (RDD), bone gain (radiographic bone level, RBL)
- **Statistics**: Descriptive statistics + Bayesian random-effects NMA where data permitted

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
**PD (Probing Depth) outcomes from NMA (21 RCTs):**
- OFD was least efficacious
- Regenerative treatments significantly shallower residual PD in 4/8 comparisons
- Range of mean differences: −2.37 to −0.60 mm (favoring regeneration)

**CAL gain outcomes from NMA:**
- Regenerative treatments larger CAL gain in 6/8 comparisons
- Range of mean differences: 1.26 to 2.66 mm (favoring regeneration)

**Tooth loss:**
| Treatment | Tooth loss rate |
|---|---|
| Regenerative/reconstructive | 0.4% |
| OFD | 2.8% |

**Combination approaches (GTR+grafting, EMD+grafting):**
- Appeared most efficacious overall, but clear hierarchy could not be established due to limited evidence

**Radiographic outcomes:**
- Sparse data; no relevant comparisons possible for NMA

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Evidence overall weak; clear hierarchy among regenerative approaches could not be established
- Sparse radiographic data limiting NMA on bone outcomes
- Heterogeneity in defect morphology, patient populations, and follow-up durations
- Tooth loss evidence sparse, preventing definitive conclusions
- Head-to-head RCTs comparing combination vs monotherapy modalities needed
- Need for standardized reporting of long-term outcomes

## 7. Glossary
- **OFD (Open Flap Debridement)**: Conventional surgical access for root debridement without regenerative materials
- **GTR (Guided Tissue Regeneration)**: Use of barrier membranes to exclude non-osteogenic cells and promote periodontal regeneration
- **EMD (Enamel Matrix Derivative)**: Protein extract (primarily amelogenin) promoting periodontal regeneration; commercial product: Emdogain
- **Grafting**: Bone graft or bone substitute materials placed in defect to support regeneration
- **Intrabony defect**: Periodontal bone defect extending apically beyond the alveolar crest into the alveolar bone
- **PD (Probing Depth)**: Distance from gingival margin to base of pocket
- **CAL (Clinical Attachment Level)**: Distance from CEJ to base of pocket; primary outcome for regeneration
- **NMA (Network Meta-Analysis)**: Simultaneous comparison of multiple treatments in a single analysis using direct and indirect evidence
- **RDD (Residual Defect Depth)**: Radiographic measurement of remaining bone defect post-treatment
- **RBL (Radiographic Bone Level)**: Amount of bone gained radiographically post-treatment
