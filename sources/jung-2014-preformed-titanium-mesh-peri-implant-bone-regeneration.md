## Why Ingested

Early Korean evidence for a *customized, preformed* (ready-to-use, no cut/bend) titanium mesh as a peri-implant GBR barrier — the historical anchor that the later CAD/CAM custom-mesh GBR literature builds on. It supplies the foundational rationale (preformed round/blunt edges → less mucosal irritation → lower exposure; macro-pore space maintenance; FDBA+autograft histology) that the modern reviews systematize. Reinforces [[wiki/implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/chiapasco-2021-customized-cadcam-titanium-meshes-gbr]] and provides primary-study grounding for [[wiki/implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/ragucci-2025-customized-titanium-mesh-vertical-ridge-augmentation-sr-ma]].

## One-line Summary

Prospective case series (10 patients / 12 implants) using a customized 3D preformed titanium mesh (SMARTbuilder; Osstem) over a 1:1 intraoral autograft + FDBA mixture for peri-implant dehiscence/fenestration defects: 4-month core biopsy showed ~80% vital bone, mesh exposure in 3/10 (30%), and all implants functional with stable marginal bone at 12 months.

## 1. Document Information

- Title: Preliminary evaluation of a three-dimensional, customized, and preformed titanium mesh in peri-implant alveolar bone regeneration
- Authors: Gyu-Un Jung, Jae-Yun Jeon, Kyung-Gyun Hwang, Chang-Joo Park
- Journal: Journal of the Korean Association of Oral and Maxillofacial Surgeons 2014;40(4):181-187
- DOI: 10.5125/jkaoms.2014.40.4.181
- Study type: Prospective preliminary clinical case series (n=10 patients, 12 implants)
- Setting: Hanyang University Hospital, Seoul, Korea (IRB HYH IRB 2012-06-014); enrollment Aug 2012–Jun 2014

## 2. Key Contributions

- Reports clinical + histologic outcomes of a **customized, three-dimensional, preformed titanium mesh** (SMARTbuilder; Osstem) used as a ready-to-use GBR barrier for peri-implant defects occurring immediately at implant placement.
- Combines the mesh with a **1:1 volume mixture of intraoral autologous bone (mandibular ramus / adjacent site) and freeze-dried bone allograft (FDBA)** — a graft/barrier combination the authors note is rarely reported with histologic evidence.
- Provides **histomorphometric evidence** (4-month core biopsy) of new bone formation with FDBA particles incorporated into vital bone.
- Argues that the preformed, round/blunt-edged design (no intraoperative cutting/bending) shortens surgery and lowers mesh-exposure risk — a precursor argument to later CAD/CAM customized titanium mesh GBR.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Patients**: 10 partially edentulous patients (5 M / 5 F, aged 17–87), all with fenestration or dehiscence around the implant fixture requiring simultaneous GBR.
- **Implants**: 12 implants (TS III CA; Osstem) placed under local anesthesia via trapezoidal full-thickness flap with two vertical releasing incisions; threads partially exposed in all cases.
- **Mesh**: Customized 3D preformed titanium mesh (SMARTbuilder; Osstem, Busan, Korea), selected by defect size/configuration. Three types — Type I (1-wall augmentation), Type II (2-wall), Type III (3-wall). A "height" (anchor) connected to the implant fixture; mesh placed over it and immobilized by **cover cap** (staged approach) or **healing abutment** (transmucosal GBR). Height, cover cap, healing abutment purpose-designed for the mesh.
- **Graft**: Autologous bone chips harvested with Autobone Collector (Osstem) mixed 1:1 by volume with FDBA (Sure-Oss; HansBiomed) placed on the defect, covered by mesh; tension-free primary closure.
- **Postoperative**: antibiotics + analgesics tid × 10 days, 0.12% chlorhexidine rinse; sutures out at 2 weeks; review every 4 weeks.
- **Re-entry**: at ~4 months, minimal-flap re-entry to remove mesh and examine regenerated bone; **core biopsy** via 2.2 mm internal-diameter trephine drill; healing abutment + sutures placed.
- **Histology**: specimen fixed in 10% buffered formalin, MMA-embedded, diamond-saw sectioned to ~40 μm, H&E stained; histomorphometry by light microscopy (vital bone, residual graft, soft tissue grossly measured).
- **Follow-up**: osseointegration and peri-implant tissue checked 2 months after re-entry; complications + success rate tracked for 12 months after definitive prosthesis.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Bone regeneration**: satisfactory bone with limited fibrous tissue beneath the mesh. At re-entry a thin connective tissue layer ("pseudoperiosteum") was observed but not debrided; beneath it FDBA particles were embedded/incorporated in regenerated bone.
- **Histomorphometry (core from patient #2)**: allograft particles well-incorporated and surrounded by newly formed vital bone and connective tissue. Reported as ~**80% vital bone**, ~**5% fibrous marrow tissue**, ~**15% remaining allograft / inflammatory cell infiltrate** (text describes ~80% vital bone surrounding allograft, 5% fibrous marrow, 15% remaining; abstract phrases the 15% as remaining allograft).
- **Complications**: postsurgical complications in **3/10 patients (30%)** — titanium mesh exposure in 2 patients, small flap dehiscence over the cover cap in 1; distribution one maxilla, two mandible. Exposed meshes/cover caps irrigated with 0.12% chlorhexidine until fibrous tissue formed; no infection or suppuration. Some graft resorption at exposed sites only.
- **Implant outcome**: all implants functional without significant complications. Radiographs at mesh insertion, removal, and 12 months after definitive prosthesis showed **stable marginal bone** in all implants.
- **Context**: authors' 30% exposure compares with Corinaldesi 14.8% (mandible) and Louis 52% (43–55%, maxilla+mandible); even exposed cases achieved acceptable regeneration and implant survival.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Preliminary**, small (n=10) single-arm case series with no control/comparator and short follow-up (12 months post-prosthesis).
- Histomorphometry reported essentially from a single representative core (patient #2); no per-patient quantitative bone-fill or dimensional (mm) bone-gain measurements, no formal volumetric/CBCT analysis.
- Mesh exposure remained a notable problem (30%), the chief liability of titanium mesh GBR.
- Mixed indication (dehiscence/fenestration defects, not vertical ridge augmentation per se); generalizability to true vertical augmentation is not established.
- Future work: controlled comparisons and longer follow-up; the customized/preformed concept later extended via CAD/CAM (DMLS) meshes.

## 6. Related Work

- Boyne 1969 — titanium mesh first proposed for alveolar ridge reconstruction.
- Rakhmatia et al. 2013 — review of titanium mesh / barrier membranes for GBR (space maintenance, macro-pore biology).
- Corinaldesi et al. 2007, 2009; Louis et al. 2008 — titanium mesh + particulate bone augmentation outcomes / exposure rates.
- Ciocca et al. 2011 — CAD/CAM (DMLS) customized titanium mesh for prosthetically guided bone regeneration.
- Proussaefs & Lozada 2006 — histologic-histomorphometric mesh augmentation (~36% new bone at 8 months, autograft+bovine 1:1).
- Gutta et al. 2009 — barrier membrane pore size and bone regeneration.

## 7. Glossary

- **GBR (Guided Bone Regeneration)**: barrier-membrane technique separating grafted defect from soft tissue to allow bone formation.
- **Preformed titanium mesh**: factory-shaped, ready-to-use mesh with round/blunt edges, requiring no intraoperative cutting/bending; SMARTbuilder (Osstem) types I–III for 1-/2-/3-wall augmentation.
- **FDBA (Freeze-Dried Bone Allograft)**: mineralized allogeneic bone graft (here Sure-Oss; HansBiomed) used 1:1 with autologous bone.
- **Dehiscence / Fenestration defect**: vertical exposure of the implant body at the crest (dehiscence) or a window of exposure surrounded by bone (fenestration).
- **Pseudoperiosteum**: thin connective tissue layer forming under a titanium mesh, left undebrided at re-entry.
- **Macro-pore**: large mesh pore permitting blood flow / nutrient diffusion and cell migration, prioritizing space maintenance over strict cell occlusiveness.
