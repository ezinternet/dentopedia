## Why Ingested

치실 (Flossing)과 구강양치액 (Mouthrinse)의 효과를 플라크 지수(Plaque Index) 같은 임상 지표가 아닌 샷건 메타게놈 시퀀싱(Shotgun Metagenomic Sequencing)을 통한 절대적 세균 정량으로 비교한 최초의 대규모 RCT로, 기존 [[interdental-cleaning/thomassen-2025-airfloss-essential-oils-vs-floss-rct]]에서 임상 지표로만 확인된 에센셜 오일 가글의 우월성을 미생물군집 수준에서 기전적으로 검증한다.

## One-line Summary

12-week parallel RCT (n=256 gingivitis subjects, 5 arms) using quantitative shotgun metagenomics found that brushing+flossing (BF) did not reduce supragingival plaque microbiota vs. brushing alone (B), whereas essential-oil mouthrinses (BA, BZ, BFZ) significantly reduced diversity, species richness, and total bacterial abundance; subgingival synergy was seen only with combined flossing+rinsing (BFZ).

## 한줄요약

12주 평행군 무작위대조시험(Randomized Controlled Trial, RCT) (치은염 256명, 5군)에서 샷건 메타게놈 시퀀싱(Shotgun Metagenomic Sequencing)을 이용한 결과, 치솔질+치실(BF)군은 치솔질 단독(B)군 대비 치은연상 플라크(Supragingival Plaque) 미생물군집(Microbiome)의 유의한 변화가 없었던 반면, 에센셜오일(Essential Oil, EO) 가글(BA·BZ·BFZ)군은 다양성(Diversity)·세균총 풍부도·총 세균량을 유의하게 감소시켰으며, 치은연하(Subgingival) 상승효과(Synergy)는 치실+가글 병용(BFZ)군에서만 관찰되었다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First trial to use **quantitative absolute-abundance metagenomics** (spiked-DNA standard + shotgun sequencing = calculated microbial units, CMU) rather than relative-abundance 16S profiling to measure plaque microbiome response to oral hygiene products
- Demonstrated that **flossing alone adds no microbiome-level plaque control** supragingival vs. brushing alone (Shannon-Weaver, species richness, total CMU: all ns)
- Showed **EO mouthrinses reduce diversity, species richness, and total bacterial abundance** significantly at both 4 weeks and 12 weeks (BA > BZ/BFZ)
- Identified **subgingival synergy** of flossing + rinsing (BFZ) against total bacteria, *P. gingivalis*, and *F. nucleatum*, not seen with either alone
- Proposed three-step mechanistic model: (1) rapid 30-s bactericidal kill; (2) attenuated re-seeding enriched with commensals; (3) repeated use prevents biofilm maturation → sustained eubiosis
- Classified 574 species from supragingival plaque into clinical categories: commensal (158), gingivitis (67), malodor (30), acidogenic (21), unknown (109), transient (228)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Design**: Examiner-blind, controlled, randomized, single-center, parallel-group RCT (April–July 2022)

**Participants**: 300 adults enrolled; 288 analyzed (30 periodontally healthy reference group + 258 randomized gingivitis subjects). Gingivitis subjects randomized into 5 arms:
- **B** (n=53): brushing only
- **BF** (n=50): brushing + Reach® Unflavored Waxed Dental Floss once daily
- **BA** (n=51): brushing + Listerine® Cool Mint® Antiseptic (alcohol EO) twice daily × 30 s
- **BZ** (n=52): brushing + Listerine® Cool Mint® Zero Alcohol (alcohol-free EO) twice daily × 30 s
- **BFZ** (n=52): brushing + flossing + Listerine® Cool Mint® Zero Alcohol

All subjects used Colgate Cavity Protection toothpaste + standard soft toothbrush, brushing 2×/day × 1 min.

**Compliance**: Virtual daily supervision (video call on weekdays); diary + product weighing at each visit.

**Primary endpoints**: Clinical gingivitis (MGI) and plaque (TPI, EBI) scores (reported separately).

**Secondary endpoints (this paper)**:
- Supragingival plaque microbiome by shotgun metagenomic sequencing (baseline, 4 wk, 12 wk)
- Subgingival plaque viable bacteria by vPCR (week 12 only)

**Microbiome method**: ZymoBIOMICS Spike-in Control II added to samples → DNA extraction → Nextera XT library prep → Illumina HiSeq sequencing (3–4M paired-end 2×150 bp reads per sample) → CosmosID analysis → species-level CMU quantification via standard calibration curves.

**vPCR method**: PMAxx™ dye photoactivation to exclude dead-cell DNA; SYBR GREEN qPCR targeting 16S rRNA (total bacteria), *P. gingivalis*, *F. nucleatum*, *Fusobacterium periodonticum*.

**Alpha diversity**: Shannon-Weaver Diversity Index + observed species richness (vegan package)

**Beta diversity**: Weighted UniFrac PCoA (phyloseq package; PERMANOVA significance testing)

**Statistics**: Mixed effects model for repeated measures (MMRM) with baseline covariate; pairwise tests at α=0.05, two-sided.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Healthy vs. gingivitis (baseline)**:
- No significant difference in α-diversity (Shannon-Weaver p=0.336; richness p=0.147) or β-diversity
- Gingivitis subjects had 44% higher total bacteria abundance (log10 Δ=0.25, p=0.012)
- 36 species significantly more abundant in gingivitis cohort (Leptotrichia spp., Fusobacterium nucleatum, Prevotella spp., etc.)

**BF vs. B (flossing effect on supragingival plaque)**:
- Shannon-Weaver diversity: **ns** at 4 wk and 12 wk
- Observed species richness: **ns**
- Total bacteria abundance: **ns**
- β-diversity (weighted UniFrac): **ns**
- Commensal, gingivitis, malodor, acidogenic species groups: **ns**
- Only 11 commensal species increased marginally in BF vs. B
- TPI (plaque index): ns at 4 wk (p=0.696) and 12 wk (p=0.164)

**BA, BZ, BFZ vs. B and BF (mouthrinse effects)**:
- All three rinsing groups had **significant reductions** in Shannon-Weaver diversity, species richness, and total bacteria vs. B and BF at 4 wk and 12 wk
- **BA vs. BF** reductions (4 wk / 12 wk): commensal 82.0% / 75.4%; gingivitis 93.6% / 91.3%; malodor 88.5% / 85.2%
- **BZ vs. BF** reductions (4 wk / 12 wk): commensal 58.2% / 46.6%; gingivitis 85.8% / 80.2%; malodor 68.5% / (ns at 12 wk)
- **BFZ vs. BF** reductions: commensal 52.6% at 4 wk; gingivitis 84.5% / 75.9%; malodor 60.7% at 4 wk
- BA showed greater reductions than BZ/BFZ overall; BZ and BFZ showed similar reductions
- No significant effect against acidogenic bacteria in any group

**Subgingival plaque vPCR (week 12)**:
- BF: no effect on total bacteria; selective control of *F. nucleatum* (p<0.05)
- BA, BZ alone: no significant effect on total bacteria or indicator species
- **BFZ (synergy)**: significant reductions in total bacteria, *P. gingivalis*, and *F. nucleatum* vs. B

**Clinical scores (reported separately)**: BF and all rinsing groups significantly reduced bleeding (EBI) and gingival inflammation (MGI) at 4 wk and 12 wk. BFZ showed the largest reduction in EBI/MGI, consistent with subgingival antimicrobial synergy.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Industry sponsorship (major COI)**: Funded by Johnson & Johnson; randomization generated by J&J Biostatistics; four of seven authors are J&J employees/consultants. Results favor J&J's Listerine products.
- Single-center study (Fort Wayne, Indiana) — generalizability limited
- Predominantly female (78.6%) and Caucasian (88.2%) population
- Plaque specimens pooled from 4 selected teeth only — may not represent whole-mouth microbiome
- Subgingival vPCR performed at week 12 only — no longitudinal subgingival data
- Subjects collected 8–18 h after last intervention — short-term post-use effects; not steady-state habitual use
- Subjects excluded for active caries → acidogenic species underrepresented
- No immunological/cytokine endpoints
- Authors note need for future studies on microbiome effects at tongue, cheek, oropharynx, and saliva

## 7. Glossary

- **CMU (Calculated Microbial Units)**: Absolute-abundance bacterial quantification derived by spiked-DNA standard curves; enables cross-sample comparison of total bacterial biomass
- **vPCR (Viability PCR)**: PCR preceded by PMAxx™ photoactivatable dye that intercalates and permanently binds dead-cell DNA, blocking its amplification — quantifies only live bacteria
- **Shannon-Weaver Diversity Index**: Alpha-diversity metric capturing both species richness and evenness; higher value = more diverse community
- **EO mouthrinse (Essential Oil Mouthrinse)**: Listerine-type formulation containing thymol, eucalyptol, menthol, and methyl salicylate; bactericidal within 30 s of contact with plaque biofilm
- **Eubiosis (유비오시스)**: State of balanced, health-associated oral microbial community; counterpart to dysbiosis
- **PERMANOVA**: Permutational Multivariate Analysis of Variance; non-parametric test for beta-diversity differences between groups using distance matrices
