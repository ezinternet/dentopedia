## One-line Summary

12-month RCT (n=36) comparing triangular vs round neck design in immediately placed anterior maxillary implants found no significant differences in MBL, probing depth, buccal bone width, or soft tissue thickness at 12 months; 88.9% survival in both groups.

## 한줄요약

상악 전치부 즉시식립 임플란트에서 삼각형 넥 vs 원형 넥을 비교한 12개월 RCT(n=36): MBL, 탐침깊이, 협측골폭, 연조직 두께 모두 그룹 간 유의한 차이 없음(생존율 양 군 88.9%).

## 2. Key Contributions

- First RCT directly comparing triangular neck (TN) vs round neck (RN) implant geometry in immediate placement with provisionalization protocol
- Provides 12-month radiographic (MBL via CBCT) and clinical (PD, BOP, STT) outcomes for a novel neck design
- Null result: no superiority demonstrated for TN over RN; study explicitly underpowered for equivalence testing

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Participants**: 36 patients, randomly assigned to TN (test) or RN (control)
- **Protocol**: Immediate implant placement + immediate provisionalization, anterior maxilla
- **Intraoperative measures**: Insertion torque (IT), ISQ, crestal width (CW), soft tissue thickness (STT) at 3 mm and 5 mm
- **Imaging**: CBCT for buccal bone width (BBW) at day of surgery and at 4 months (definitive crown placement)
- **Postoperative follow-up**: 4 months and 12 months — MBL, PD, BOP, CW, STT

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample (n=36; 18 per group) — underpowered for equivalence testing; the study's own caveat is that estimates are compatible with small between-group differences
- Single-center design (University of Catalonia, Barcelona)
- 12-month follow-up only; longer-term data needed to detect diverging bone remodeling trajectories
- Abstract does not report absolute MBL values or CI widths

## 7. Glossary

- **TN (Triangular Neck)**: Innovative implant neck with triangular cross-section geometry (test group)
- **RN (Round Neck)**: Conventional implant neck with circular cross-section (control group)
- **BBW (Buccal Bone Width)**: Horizontal thickness of the buccal/facial bone plate, assessed via CBCT
- **MBL (Marginal Bone Loss)**: Radiographic change in crestal bone level from baseline
- **STT (Soft Tissue Thickness)**: Mucosal/gingival thickness at 3 mm and 5 mm from gingival margin
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: Resonance frequency analysis-based measure of implant stability (scale 1–100)
- **BOP (Bleeding on Probing)**: Indicator of peri-implant inflammation
- **PD (Pocket Depth)**: Probing depth around implant
- **CW (Crestal Width)**: Width of the alveolar crest
