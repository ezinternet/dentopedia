## Why Ingested

기존 [[drug/anticoagulants/moldovan-2023-anticoagulant-oral-surgery-bleeding-sr]]과 [[drug/anticoagulants/inchingolo-2024-anticoagulation-therapy-dental-practice-sr]]은 발치·구강수술에서 DOAC을 중단하지 말라(continuation)고 권고하지만, 그 근거는 대부분 치과 단독 소규모 데이터다. 본 SR+MA (Xiang 2026)는 dental procedure를 포함한 minimal bleeding-risk surgery 전반(24 studies, n=8663)에서 continuous vs interrupted DOAC을 직접 비교하고, 특히 RCT data만 분리했을 때 두 전략 간 출혈·혈전 차이가 모두 사라짐을 보여 "continue가 우월"이라는 관찰연구 신호가 selection bias일 수 있음을 드러낸다. 치과 발치는 명시적으로 minimal bleeding-risk 카테고리(extractions/restorations/prosthetics/endodontics/cleanings/fillings)에 포함되어 직접적 임상 관련성이 있다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (24 studies, n=8663; 4 dental) — continuous DOAC reduced major bleeding (OR 0.57, 95% CI 0.37-0.87) and thrombotic events (OR 0.54, 0.33-0.91) overall, but ALL differences vanished in high-quality RCT data (very low/low GRADE certainty); both continue and interrupt are acceptable for minimal bleeding-risk procedures incl. minor dental.

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (24편, n=8663; 치과 4편) — 전체로는 continuous DOAC이 대출혈(OR 0.57)·혈전(OR 0.54)을 줄였으나 고품질 RCT만 보면 모든 차이가 사라짐(selection bias 의심, GRADE very low/low); 발치 등 minimal-risk 시술에서 continue·interrupt 둘 다 허용 가능.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (PRISMA 2020; PROSPERO CRD42024621729)
- **Journal**: Research and Practice in Thrombosis and Haemostasis 2026;10(3):103419
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.rpth.2026.103419 | PMID 42027304 | PMCID PMC13099467
- **Source**: PubMed Central full text (PMC13099467)
- **Search**: Ovid Medline, CENTRAL, EMBASE, CINAHL, trial registries — inception to Sep 11, 2025
- **Included**: 24 studies, n=8663 patients (8 retrospective, 8 prospective, 8 RCTs)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Largest synthesis to date (11 more trials than prior reviews) of **continuous vs interrupted DOAC** for **minimal bleeding-risk procedures**, with minor dental procedures (extractions, restorations, prosthetics, endodontics, cleanings, fillings) explicitly within scope.
- Pre-specified subgroup analyses by **study design** (RCT/prospective/retrospective), **procedure type** (ablation/pacemaker/dental), and **interruption timing** (morning of / 24h / 48h / 72h).
- Demonstrates that the apparent benefit of continuation is **driven by observational data** and disappears under randomized, blinded conditions — a selection-bias caution that directly informs how to read the (continuation-favoring) dental anticoagulation literature.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Populations**: AF (n=8448 of 8663) and VTE patients on DOACs; apixaban (n=2991), rivaroxaban (n=2479), dabigatran (n=2042), edoxaban (n=786).
- **Procedures**: ablation (13 studies), pacemaker insertion (4), dental (4); also coronary angiography/radial, dermatologic, cataract eligible.
- **Comparison**: continuous (DOAC continued morning of procedure) vs interrupted (≥1 dose held; classified day-of / 24h / 48h / 72h).
- **Outcomes**: primary efficacy = composite arterial+venous thromboembolism; primary safety = bleeding (major + minor, ISTH major-bleed definition in 9 studies); secondary = mortality, total complications, length of stay.
- **Analysis**: DerSimonian-Laird random-effects, OR + risk difference; I² heterogeneity; ICEMAN for subgroup credibility.
- **Quality**: Cochrane RoB 2.0 (RCTs), ROBINS-I v2 (nonrandomized), GRADE.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Pooled bleeding effect (continuous vs interrupted):**
- Overall bleeding: 2.4% vs 3.2%; **OR 0.79; 95% CI 0.62-1.01; I²=0%** (NS).
- **Major bleeding: 1.0% vs 2.2%; OR 0.57; 95% CI 0.37-0.87; I²=0%** — significant, favoring continuous.
- Minor bleeding: 4.6% vs 4.8%; **OR 0.93; 95% CI 0.69-1.24; I²=0%** (NS).

**Thrombotic events** (19 studies): 5.7% vs 6.3%; **OR 0.54; 95% CI 0.33-0.87 (abstract reports 0.33-0.91); I²=52-60%** — favors continuous overall; NS at 30 days (OR 0.57, 0.28-1.16).

**The critical caveat — RCT subgroup (8 RCTs):** thrombotic OR 0.53 (0.17-1.64) NS; **major bleeding OR 0.82 (0.42-1.61) NS**; minor bleeding OR 1.02 (0.71-1.45) NS — **all differences disappear**. Significant design-subgroup difference for total bleeding: RCT OR 0.96 (0.70-1.32) vs nonrandomized OR 0.56 (0.38-0.81), p=.03.

**Dental subgroup** (underpowered, very wide CIs): major bleeding OR 1.02 (0.08-12.70); minor bleeding OR 1.44 (0.11-18.47) — no signal either way.

**Secondary**: mortality OR 0.33 (0.08-1.41) NS; total complications OR 1.05 (0.71-1.54) NS; post hoc stroke/TIA OR 0.73 (0.26-2.04) NS.

**Risk of bias / certainty**: 15/16 nonrandomized at high RoB (confounding); 4/8 RCTs high RoB, 2 low. GRADE very low (thrombotic, all bleeding) to low (major/minor bleeding, complications, mortality).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Heavy reliance on observational data with selection bias (low-risk patients preferentially continued; comorbid patients interrupted — Sheikh et al).
- Moderate heterogeneity in thrombotic and overall bleeding outcomes; DOACs not separated despite differing half-lives; race/ethnicity rarely reported.
- Dental subgroup too small to draw procedure-specific conclusions.
- Calls for more blinded RCTs separating arterial/venous thromboembolism and major/minor bleeding, and evaluating silent cerebral events.

## 6. Related Work

- Consistent with smaller reviews (Mendoza — cardiac device; Basu-Ray — catheter ablation; Ha — AF ablation) finding continuous DOAC noninferior to interrupted.

## 7. Glossary

- **DOAC**: Direct oral anticoagulant (FXa inhibitors apixaban/rivaroxaban/edoxaban; direct thrombin inhibitor dabigatran).
- **Continuous regimen**: DOAC taken the morning of the procedure (not held).
- **Interrupted regimen**: ≥1 DOAC dose held (day-of, 24h, 48h, or 72h before).
- **Minimal bleeding-risk procedure**: per ACCP/ISTH — incl. minor dental (extractions, restorations, prosthetics, endodontics, cleanings, fillings), cardiac device implantation, ablation, cataract, minor dermatologic.
- **ISTH major bleeding**: International Society on Thrombosis and Haemostasis standardized definition.
- **ROBINS-I v2 / RoB 2.0 / GRADE**: nonrandomized RoB tool / Cochrane RCT RoB tool / certainty-of-evidence framework.
