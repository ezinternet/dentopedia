## Why Ingested

The GentleWave® (GW) multisonic system is promoted for conservative (small apical prep) root canal disinfection at premium cost; this 12-study SR tests whether it is actually superior to cheaper alternatives and finds its advantage **inconsistent** — directly reinforcing [[wiki/endodontics/irrigation/boutsioukis-2022-irrigants-irrigation-methods-status-directions]]'s thesis that no activation method is proven to improve outcomes over the NaOCl+EDTA standard. It pairs with [[wiki/endodontics/irrigation/varadan-2025-multisonic-ultrasonic-irrigant-biofilm-sr]] (multisonic ≈ ultrasonic on biofilm) as the larger, broader-outcome GW review.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (12 in-vitro studies, QUIN tool, narrative synthesis) of GentleWave vs conventional/advanced irrigation: GW gave significantly higher LPS/LTA reduction than PUI and generally better biofilm/smear removal, but superiority was inconsistent across studies (some favored optimized PUI; Er:YAG comparable for debris), with cost/heterogeneity limiting clinical interpretation.

## 한줄요약

GentleWave를 기존·고급 세정법과 비교한 in vitro 12편 체계적 문헌고찰(QUIN 도구, 서술적 종합): GW는 PUI보다 LPS/LTA 감소가 유의하게 컸고 biofilm·smear 제거도 대체로 우수했지만, 우월성은 연구마다 일관되지 않았고(최적화된 PUI가 동등·우수한 경우, Er:YAG는 debris 제거 동등), 비용·이질성 때문에 임상적 결론은 제한적이다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Systematic review (PRISMA), narrative synthesis (no meta-analysis)
- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health (2026) 26:296
- **Authors**: Sıla Nur Usta, Eda Doğuş, Mustafa Gündoğar — University of Health Sciences (Ankara) & Medipol University (Istanbul), Turkey
- **Databases**: PubMed (MEDLINE), Web of Science (all), Scopus, ScienceDirect, Cochrane Library (searched 9 July 2025)
- **Registration**: OSF (DOI 10.17605/OSF.IO/AZN3T)
- **Included studies**: 12 in-vitro studies
- **Risk of bias**: QUIN Tool (in-vitro dentistry)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Broadest GentleWave disinfection SR to date (12 studies), spanning microbiological outcomes (biofilm, bacterial count, LPS, LTA) **and** smear/debris removal.
- Frames the question economically: is the high-cost GW justified over accessible alternatives, especially in **conservative (small apical prep)** canals it is marketed for?
- Conclusion: GW is effective in conservative preps, but its **superiority over conventional/advanced systems is inconsistent** across studies.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **PICOS**: P = extracted human teeth (mature apices) / ex-vivo models; I = GentleWave® system; C = other conventional/advanced activation (PUI, XP-Endo Finisher, laser-assisted); O = biofilm removal, microbial reduction (CFU, qPCR), LPS/LTA, smear/debris removal; S = in-vitro.
- Substantial heterogeneity in designs/interventions/outcomes → **narrative synthesis** rather than meta-analysis.
- Rationale: conservative shaping (smaller apical prep) reduces instrumented wall percentage and irrigant penetration, so advanced activation is invoked to compensate — GW is specifically marketed for this scenario.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **LPS/LTA**: GW gave **significantly higher** reductions in lipopolysaccharide and lipoteichoic acid than PUI.
- **Biofilm + debris/smear**: majority of studies showed enhanced removal with GW.
- **But inconsistent**: some studies found **similar or superior** disinfection for PUI, particularly when PUI was paired with adequate canal preparation and optimized protocols.
- **Er:YAG lasers**: achieved **debris removal comparable** to GW.
- Net: GW effective in conservative-prep context, but no consistent across-the-board superiority.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- All in-vitro; no clinical (healing) outcomes.
- High heterogeneity in study design, intervention protocols, and outcomes → narrative-only, no pooled effect.
- Methodological limitations of included studies + **cost/accessibility** of GW limit definitive clinical interpretation.
- Calls for standardized in-vitro protocols and clinical trials to establish whether GW gives clinically meaningful, cost-effective advantage.

## 7. Glossary

- **GentleWave® (GW)**: Multisonic system (Sonendo) using broad-spectrum acoustic energy + continuous fluid delivery + negative apical pressure to generate hydrodynamic cavitation and acoustic streaming; marketed for conservative (small) apical preparations.
- **PUI (passive ultrasonic irrigation)**: Acoustic-streaming activation via an oscillating ultrasonic file.
- **LPS / LTA**: Lipopolysaccharide (Gram-negative) and lipoteichoic acid (Gram-positive) endotoxins — molecular markers of microbial burden beyond viable counts.
- **SNI**: Syringe-needle irrigation (conventional, no activation).
- **QUIN Tool**: Quality assessment instrument for in-vitro studies in dentistry.
- **Conservative preparation**: Smaller apical prep size that preserves dentin but reduces instrumented wall percentage and irrigant penetration.
