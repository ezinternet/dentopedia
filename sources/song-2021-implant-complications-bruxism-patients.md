## One-line Summary
Editor's Opinion (2-page narrative commentary) from Jeju National University arguing that bruxism is associated with late implant complications (fracture of fixture/screw/prosthesis, severe MBL, decementation), and recommending screening and adjunctive interventions (occlusal splint, botulinum toxin injection) before implant installation in bruxers.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Provides a Korean OMFS journal-level position framing that:
  - Bruxism is positively associated with **late** implant complications (osseointegration is not the failure mode; occlusal overload is)
  - Listed late complications: fixture fracture, screw loosening, screw fracture, fracture of prosthesis (ceramic/porcelain), severe marginal bone loss (MBL), decementation
  - Clinicians should perform pre-implant bruxism screening (clinical + instrumental)
  - Occlusal splint and/or botulinum toxin injection into masticatory muscles are recommended adjunctive treatments before implant installation
- Frames the correlation as **still controversial but pragmatically clinically relevant**.

- Article type: Editor's Opinion — short narrative commentary, not a systematic review
- Reference base: 9 cited papers (Lavigne 2008 J Oral Rehab; Lobbezoo 2008, 2018; Yu & Kim 2020 fixture fractures; Albrektsson 2012 EAO consensus; Manfredini 2014 SR; Chitumalla 2018 5-year retrospective; Tosun 2003 PSG study; Fernández-Núñez 2019 BoNT SR)
- Structure:
  - I. Introduction (definition of bruxism, parafunctional habit, association with implant complications)
  - II. Implant Complications Related to Bruxism (early vs late failures; late failures driven by occlusal overload)
  - III. Assessment of Bruxism — non-instrumental (clinical inspection, self-report) vs instrumental (EMG, polysomnography PSG)
  - IV. Treatments of Bruxism — occlusal splint (non-invasive, distributes force), botulinum toxin (reduces muscle strength)
  - V. Editor's Opinion — recommendation for pre-implant screening + adjunctive intervention

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Distinguishes early (osseointegration failure) vs late (occlusal overload) implant failures; bruxism → late
- Lists late complications associated with bruxism: implant fracture, screw loosening, screw fracture, prosthesis fracture (ceramic/porcelain), severe marginal bone loss, decementation
- Diagnostic hierarchy:
  - Non-instrumental: masticatory muscle hypertrophy, linea alba on cheek, tongue/lip indentation, severe muscle/TMJ pain, extreme attrition, prosthesis complications, self-report questionnaires (limited validity)
  - Instrumental (most accurate): EMG (awake bruxism), polysomnography (PSG, gold standard for sleep bruxism)
- Treatment hierarchy:
  - Occlusal splint — first-line, non-invasive, distributes occlusal force
  - Botulinum toxin injection into masticatory muscles — reduces parafunctional force
- Practical recommendation: history taking (prior repetitive prosthesis failure, nocturnal bruxism) + clinical inspection BEFORE implant surgery; if positive, explain risk to patient and recommend splint and/or BoNT

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Editor's Opinion format = narrative commentary, not a systematic review; no formal evidence appraisal
- "Controversial" correlation acknowledged — author cites SR (Manfredini 2014) but does not quantify effect size
- 9 references only; not exhaustive
- Single-author, single-page commentary — practical guidance rather than rigorous evidence synthesis
- No specific clinical thresholds (e.g., MBL cutoff, splint frequency, BoNT dose) provided

## 7. Glossary
- Bruxism: parafunctional masticatory muscle activity (clenching/grinding/thrusting) during sleep or wakefulness
- Sleep bruxism (SB) vs awake bruxism (AB)
- Linea alba: white horizontal occlusal-plane line on buccal mucosa from chronic cheek-biting
- Attrition: mechanical occlusal tooth wear (vs erosion = chemical wear from acid)
- EMG: electromyography (records masticatory muscle activity)
- PSG: polysomnography — gold standard for sleep bruxism diagnosis
- Occlusal splint: hard or soft acrylic appliance covering teeth to redistribute occlusal force
- BoNT: botulinum toxin (type A is the dental form, e.g., Botox, Dysport, Meditoxin)
- MBL: marginal bone loss around implant
- Late implant failure: failure after osseointegration is established, typically due to occlusal/biological overload (cf. early failure = osseointegration not achieved)
- Decementation: cement-retained prosthesis loosening from abutment
