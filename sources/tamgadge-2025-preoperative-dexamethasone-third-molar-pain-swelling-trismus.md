## Why Ingested

[[drug/analgesics/satpathi-2024-corticosteroids-dentistry-review]]가 치과 코르티코스테로이드 사용을 narrative하게 개괄하는 데 비해, 이 split-mouth RCT는 단일 술전 IM dexamethasone 4 mg가 매복 하악 제3대구치 발치 후 통증·부종·개구제한을 실제 수치로 줄이는지 placebo와 직접 대조해 보강한다. 술전 corticosteroid를 third-molar swelling/trismus 관리 근거로 사용.

## One-line Summary

Split-mouth single-blind placebo-controlled trial (n=60 bilateral impacted mandibular third molars): a single preoperative 4 mg IM dexamethasone significantly lowered postoperative VAS pain (day 2: 1.2 vs 2.3; day 7: 0.4 vs 1.6, both p<0.001), improved mouth opening (3.5 vs 2.7 cm, p<0.001), and reduced facial swelling by day 7 (2.1 vs 2.8 cm, p=0.04), with no adverse reactions.

## 한줄요약

split-mouth 단일맹검 위약대조 시험 (n=60, 양측 매복 하악 사랑니): 술전 dexamethasone 4 mg 근육주사 1회가 위약 대비 술후 통증(2일 1.2 vs 2.3, 7일 0.4 vs 1.6, p<0.001), 개구량(3.5 vs 2.7 cm, p<0.001)을 유의하게 개선하고 7일째 부종도 줄였으며(2.1 vs 2.8 cm, p=0.04) 이상반응은 없었다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Direct intra-patient (split-mouth) comparison of a single preoperative 4 mg IM dexamethasone vs sterile-water placebo for third molar surgery sequelae, controlling for inter-individual variability.
- Confirms benefit across all three classic sequelae — pain, trismus (mouth opening), and swelling — with the same single low-dose preoperative protocol.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 60 patients aged 18–30 years, all requiring bilateral surgical extraction of impacted mandibular third molars.
- Right side = test (4 mg dexamethasone IM preoperatively); left side = control (1 cc sterile water placebo). Single-blind.
- The two extractions separated by ≥3 weeks (washout).
- Exclusions: medical comorbidities, pregnancy, symptomatic third molars.
- Outcomes measured preoperatively and on postoperative days 2 and 7: pain (VAS), mouth opening (interincisal, Vernier caliper), facial swelling (fixed anatomical landmark distances).
- Impaction distribution: mesioangular 41.66%, vertical 30%, horizontal 21.66%, distoangular 6.66%.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Pain (VAS)**: Day 2 — test 1.2±0.5 vs control 2.3±0.7 (p<0.001); Day 7 — test 0.4±0.2 vs control 1.6±0.6 (p<0.001).
- **Mouth opening**: test 3.5±0.3 cm vs control 2.7±0.4 cm (p<0.001) — significantly better at both days.
- **Swelling**: test 2.1±0.3 cm vs control 2.8±0.5 cm (p=0.04) significant at day 7; no significant difference at day 2.
- No adverse reactions reported in either arm.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Moderate sample size; single center.
- Single-blind only (patient blinded; operator/assessor blinding not stated as double).
- Subjective pain measurement (VAS); authors suggest objective inflammatory biomarkers in future work.
- No standardized dosing protocol established; large-scale trials called for.

## 6. Related Work

- O'Hare SR+MA — dexamethasone efficacy for third molar pain/trismus/swelling.
- Lima — oral dexamethasone superior to diclofenac for postoperative discomfort.
- Grossi — submucosal dexamethasone reduces postoperative pain and edema.
- Neupert — prolonged analgesic effect from extended half-life.
- Mazhar — pre-emptive oral ketorolac vs local tramadol (alternative analgesic strategies).

## 7. Glossary

- **VAS**: Visual Analog Scale (0–10 pain).
- **Trismus**: restricted mouth opening; here measured as interincisal distance.
- **Split-mouth design**: each patient serves as their own control (one side test, contralateral side control).
- **Dexamethasone**: long-acting glucocorticoid with potent anti-inflammatory and minimal mineralocorticoid effect.
