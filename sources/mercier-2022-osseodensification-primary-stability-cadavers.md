## One-line Summary

Cadaver study (21 mandibles, 58 implants) showing OD (Densah burs) significantly increased insertion torque (34.9 vs 23.6 Ncm, p=0.036) and peri-implant bone density (p=0.026) vs standard drilling.

## 2. Key Contributions
- OD significantly higher IT vs SD in cadaveric mandibular bone (p=0.036): OD 34.9┬▒19.1 Ncm vs SD 23.6┬▒9.8 Ncm
- Moderate positive IT-ISQ correlation in OD (╧Б=0.527) тАФ supports IT as stability predictor with OD
- CBCT-confirmed significant peri-implant bone density increase with OD (p=0.026)
- Notable: OD IT values (34.9 Ncm) very close to Rittipakorn-2025 OD IT (34.0 Ncm) тАФ cross-cadaver replication

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Specimens:** 21 formalin-fixed human cadaver mandibles
- **Osteotomies:** 29 OD (Densah profile drills, Versah) + 29 SD (Axiom drills, Anthogyr) = 58 total; implants placed in all
- **Additional:** 16 osteotomies without implant placement for bone tissue analysis
- **Implants:** Axiom system (Anthogyr)
- **Measurements:** Insertion torque (IT, Ncm) + ISQ; CBCT for peri-drilling bone density
- **Statistics:** Mann-Whitney U (IT/ISQ comparison); Spearman ╧Б (IT-ISQ correlation); ╬▒=0.05

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- IT: OD 34.9┬▒19.1 Ncm vs SD 23.6┬▒9.8 Ncm (p=0.036) тАФ significant
- IT-ISQ correlation: OD ╧Б=0.527 (moderate positive); SD not reported as significant
- Bone density: significant increase with OD vs SD (p=0.026) on CBCT

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Cadaveric formalin-fixed tissue тАФ no biological healing or bone remodeling
- Abstract-only; methodological details (ISQ values, implant geometry) not fully accessible
- Need: in vivo clinical confirmation of IT and bone density findings

## 7. Glossary
- **OD:** Osseodensification with Densah burs (Versah) тАФ counterclockwise compaction mode
- **SD:** Standard/conventional drilling (cutting direction, bone removed)
- **IT:** Insertion Torque (Ncm) тАФ surrogate for primary stability
- **ISQ:** Implant Stability Quotient via RFA
