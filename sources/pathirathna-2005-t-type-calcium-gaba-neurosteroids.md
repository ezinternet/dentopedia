## One-line Summary

In vivo (rat thermal nociception) + in vitro electrophysiology study showing that locally injected 5α-reduced neurosteroids produce potent, dose-dependent, enantioselective peripheral analgesia primarily via blockade of T-type Ca²⁺ channels, with GABA-A potentiation enhancing (but not independently driving) the effect.

## 한줄요약

동물(rat) in vivo 열통각 + in vitro 전기생리 실험 — 국소 주사한 5α-환원 신경스테로이드가 주로 T-type Ca²⁺ 통로 차단으로 강력한 말초 진통을 내고, GABA-A 강화는 단독으로는 진통을 못 내지만 T-type 차단 진통을 증폭한다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First demonstration that 5α-reduced neuroactive steroids exert potent **peripheral** (not just central) analgesia.
- Dissects the dual molecular mechanism using mechanism-selective synthetic analogues:
  - Compounds with T-type activity but no GABA-A activity (ECN) → still analgesic.
  - Compounds with GABA-A activity but no T-type activity (CDNC24) → NOT analgesic alone.
  - Combining sub-threshold GABAergic CDNC24 with ECN → greatly enhanced, bicuculline-sensitive analgesia.
- Conclusion: T-type Ca²⁺ channel blockade is the primary peripheral analgesic mechanism; GABA-A channels do not carry baseline pain transmission but can amplify T-type–mediated anti-nociception.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- In vivo: adult rats, radiant-heat thermal nociception (paw withdrawal latency) after local injection of neurosteroids — alphaxalone (ALPX), allopregnanolone (3α5αP), ACN, and mechanism-selective analogues ECN and CDNC24.
- Pharmacologic antagonism: bicuculline (GABA-A antagonist) co-injection to test GABA-A contribution.
- In vitro: voltage-clamp recording of T-type Ca²⁺ currents and GABA-A–mediated currents.
- Enantioselectivity tested to confirm specific receptor/channel mediation.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- ALPX, 3α5αP, ACN: potent, dose-dependent, enantioselective anti-nociception in vivo + modulation of both T-type Ca²⁺ and GABA-A currents in vitro.
- ALPX analgesia only **incompletely** antagonized by bicuculline → GABA-A is not the sole mechanism.
- ECN (T-type only) analgesic but with smaller threshold increase than ALPX/3α5αP/ACN.
- CDNC24 (GABA-A only) not analgesic; but ineffective-dose CDNC24 + ECN → markedly enhanced, bicuculline-sensitive analgesia.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Animal (rat) model, thermal nociception only — no chronic/neuropathic or orofacial endpoint.
- Local-injection paradigm; systemic pharmacokinetics/translation not addressed.
- 2005 vintage; mechanistic, not clinical.

## 7. Glossary

- T-type Ca²⁺ channel (low-voltage activated; CaV3.x, esp. CaV3.2): low-threshold calcium channel that boosts neuronal excitability; its blockade is anti-nociceptive.
- Neurosteroid / neuroactive steroid: endogenous or synthetic steroid that rapidly modulates ion channels (T-type Ca²⁺ block, GABA-A potentiation); e.g. allopregnanolone (3α5αP).
- Enantioselective: effect dependent on the specific 3D mirror-image form, indicating a defined molecular target rather than nonspecific membrane action.
- Bicuculline: competitive GABA-A receptor antagonist used to probe GABA-A involvement.
