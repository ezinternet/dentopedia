## One-line Summary

SR+MA (12 RCTs + 1 non-RCT) of patient-reported outcomes in sinus floor elevation finding graftless and transcrestal approaches associated with less postoperative pain/discomfort than grafted lateral SFE, with overall evidence sparse.

## 한줄요약

## 2. Key Contributions
- First focused PROM synthesis across SFE approaches (lateral vs transcrestal vs graftless).
- Primary outcome: postoperative pain (VAS, first 7 days).
- Ranks patient-experience burden: graftless/transcrestal < grafted/lateral.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- SR+MA; PubMed, Scopus, Cochrane CENTRAL to 2025-01-31.
- Randomized + non-randomized comparative studies; ≥1 group with maxillary SFE, ≥5 patients/group, PROMs assessed.
- 104 identified → 56 screened → 12 RCTs + 1 non-RCT included.
- Pairwise meta-analysis on proportion reporting pain at days 1, 3, 7. Primary outcome VAS pain; secondary other PROMs/satisfaction.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Risk of bias: 10 moderate, 1 RCT high; non-RCT moderate.
- Meta-analysis feasible only for proportion reporting pain (days 1/3/7).
- Graftless approach: less pain at days 3 and 7 vs SFE with graft.
- Transcrestal SFE: less postoperative discomfort than lateral SFE.
- Conclusion: reduced discomfort/inflammation with graftless and transcrestal approaches; paucity of PROM evidence overall.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Abstract-only ingest (publisher preprint page); quantitative pooled estimates not captured. [미검증]
- Meta-analysis limited to pain proportions; other PROMs narrative only.
- Sparse evidence base; heterogeneous PROM instruments.

## 7. Glossary
- PROM: patient-reported outcome measure. SFE: sinus floor elevation.
- VAS: visual analog scale (pain).
