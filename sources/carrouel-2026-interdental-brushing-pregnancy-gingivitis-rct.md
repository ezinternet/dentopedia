## Why Ingested

This RCT secondary analysis (n=323) quantifies calibrated **interdental brush (IDB)** use plus individualised instruction as a primary-prevention strategy for pregnancy-associated gingivitis — a high-value interdental-cleaning efficacy result (BOP 56%→12%, −79.9%; IDB the strongest independent predictor of bleeding resolution, OR=3.14). It extends [[wiki/interdental-cleaning/el-haddad-2026-toothpick-use-interdental-papilla-loss-cross-sectional]] (interdental-cleaning behaviour and outcomes) with hard RCT efficacy data in a special-risk population, and complements plaque/gingivitis device-efficacy evidence in [[wiki/periodontics/luo-2026-electric-toothbrush-technologies-plaque-gingivitis-nma]].

## One-line Summary

Multicentre single-blind RCT (secondary analysis, n=323 nulliparous women at preeclampsia risk, intact periodontium, enrolled at 12 wk gestation): daily calibrated interdental brushes + instruction vs routine antenatal care cut BOP from 56% to 12% (−79.9%, p<0.001) by 8 months — most of it in the first week — while controls stayed flat; IDB use was the strongest independent predictor of bleeding resolution (OR=3.14, 95% CI 2.01–4.90).

## 한줄요약

다기관 단일맹검 RCT(2차 분석, n=323, 자간전증 고위험·임신 12주 등록 초임부): 매일 보정형 치간칫솔(IDB)+개별 지도군이 일상 산전관리 대조군 대비 8개월째 탐침 시 출혈(BOP)을 56%→12%로 −79.9% 감소시켰고(p<0.001) 대부분 첫 주에 일어났으며, 치간칫솔 사용이 출혈 소실의 가장 강력한 독립 예측인자였다(OR=3.14, 95% CI 2.01–4.90).

## 2. Key Contributions

- First RCT to evaluate calibrated interdental brushing across the **entire gestational period** (12 wk → 8 months) in women at high preeclampsia risk with an intact periodontium.
- Demonstrates that pregnancy-associated gingival bleeding is **reversible by mechanical interdental biofilm disruption**, with most of the effect occurring within the first week.
- Quantifies interdental brushing as the **strongest independent predictor** of bleeding resolution (OR=3.14, 95% CI 2.01–4.90), robust to a priori covariate adjustment.
- Reframes interdental hygiene as an integrable **primary-prevention** component of antenatal oral care.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Multi-centre, single-blind, two-parallel-arm (1:1) RCT, six antenatal clinics in Dakar, Senegal, Mar 2022 – Jul 2023. Outcome assessors and statisticians masked; participants could not be blinded.
- **Population**: 323 nulliparous, sub-Saharan African women, 18–40 y, enrolled at 12 wk gestation, ≥20 natural teeth, intact periodontium (periodontitis excluded); preeclampsia risk defined by nulliparity + sub-Saharan African origin. Active n=162, control n=161.
- **Intervention**: Personalised kit of calibrated interdental brushes (Curaprox CPS, Curaden; ISO 16409:2016) sized to interdental space, daily evening use, demonstrations + standardised compliance booklet. Adherence 87.0%.
- **Control**: Routine oral hygiene (toothbrushing + toothpaste; no interdental devices or mouthrinses).
- **Recalls**: T1 (3 mo +1 wk), T2 (4 mo), T3 (5 mo), T4 (8 mo). BOP, CAL, PD, GI, PI full-mouth, 6 sites/tooth, Williams probe at 20 g.
- **Primary gingival outcome**: BOP (% bleeding sites) at T4 (ITT). Secondary: longitudinal BOP change. Mixed linear models + logistic regression. R 4.4.0; significance two-sided p<0.05. Per-protocol and PI-stratified sensitivity analyses.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Primary (ITT)**: Active BOP 56% → 12% at 8 months, total −79.9% (−92.8% to −65.6%, p<0.001); control stable (+5.3%, p=0.685). Between-group mean difference in change −0.43 (95% CI −0.49 to −0.36); MLM treatment × time interaction p<0.001.
- **Time course**: Most reduction in first week — T1 −56.8% (p<0.001); then T2 −28.7%, T3 −23.1%, T4 −18.2%. Between-group difference significant from T1 onward.
- **Severe baseline subgroup** (BOP > Q3, >0.81; active n=40): −84.3% reduction (p<0.05); univariate OR=14.8 (95% CI 4.9–44.7) but unstable in penalised model.
- **Predictor**: Interdental brushing strongest independent predictor of bleeding resolution at T4 — OR=3.14 (95% CI 2.01–4.90) per abstract; multivariable model OR=3.84 (95% CI 2.01–7.32, p<0.001).
- **Robustness**: Per-protocol mean difference −0.42 (−0.50 to −0.33), MLM p<0.001, consistent with ITT. Effect significant across all baseline-PI strata (p<0.001), no PI × treatment interaction (all p>0.09) — addresses the baseline PI imbalance (control PI higher, p<0.001). Adjusted attrition lower in intervention arm (20.8% vs 34.1%, p=0.015). No adverse effects.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Trial **powered for preeclampsia**; BOP was a prespecified secondary outcome (longitudinal/subgroup analyses exploratory, unadjusted for multiplicity).
- ~40% attrition at T4 (balanced; ITT + per-protocol concordant).
- **No microbiological or immunological/biomarker data** — mechanism (pathogen load, cytokine modulation) plausible but unproven in pregnancy.
- BOP is a **surrogate of inflammation**, not tissue destruction or long-term periodontal risk.
- In this cohort, interdental brushing did **not** significantly reduce composite obstetric outcomes (preeclampsia, preterm birth, low birth weight, SGA — reported separately).
- Single Dakar population (high internal validity, limited generalisability; excludes women with periodontal disease); follow-up limited to gestation (postpartum unknown).
- Industry funding (Curaden AG); one author reports personal fees from Curaden.

## 7. Glossary

- **BOP (bleeding on probing)** — % of sites bleeding on gentle probing; here the primary gingival outcome and surrogate marker of gingival inflammation.
- **Calibrated interdental brush (IDB)** — interdental brush sized to the embrasure per ISO 16409:2016; shown to outperform floss on interdental plaque/BOP.
- **Pregnancy-associated gingivitis** — plaque-induced gingivitis modified by systemic (hormonal) factors per 2018 EFP/AAP classification; up to ~70% prevalence.
- **ITT (intention-to-treat)** — analysis by randomised assignment regardless of adherence; the prespecified primary analysis population here.
- **Preeclampsia** — pregnancy hypertensive disorder; the trial's primary endpoint (this is a secondary gingival analysis).
- **EFP/AAP 2018 classification** — gingivitis extent: healthy (<10% BOP), localised (10–30%), generalised (>30%).
