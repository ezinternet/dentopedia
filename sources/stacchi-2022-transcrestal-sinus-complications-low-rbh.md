## Why Ingested
기존 [[overviews/sinus-lift-technique-selection]] Section 3(천공 위험·관리)는 격벽·측벽 두께만 천공 위험인자로 다룬다. 이 논문은 **bucco-palatal sinus width >12mm**를 천공·조기실패 양쪽의 독립 위험인자로 추가하는 핵심 근거다. 또한 [[sinus-lift/transcrestal/guo-2020-non-grafted-platelet-concentrations-grafted-transcrestal-sinus]] 비이식 TSFE 안전성 논의를 RBH≤5mm 저잔존골 극단 케이스로 확장한다.

## One-line Summary
Retrospective multicenter study (n=430 implants, RBH ≤5 mm, transcrestal sinus floor elevation): early implant failure 2.8%; bucco-palatal sinus width >12 mm (OR 8.5) and membrane perforation (OR 4.21) are the only independent predictors of early failure.

## 한줄요약
후향적 다기관 연구(n=430, RBH≤5mm 경치조골 거상): 조기 임플란트 실패 2.8%, 협구개 상악동 폭 >12mm(OR 8.5)와 막천공(OR 4.21)이 조기 실패의 유일한 독립 위험인자.

- Journal: Clinical Oral Implants Research 2022;33(6):783–791
- DOI: 10.1111/clr.13959; Received 19 Aug 2021, Accepted 2 May 2022
- 7 clinical centers (Italy, multicenter retrospective chart review)
- Open access (CC BY-NC-ND). No specific funding declared.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First study to identify **bucco-palatal sinus width (SW) >12 mm** as a clinical risk factor for both membrane perforation and early implant failure in transcrestal sinus lift.
- Quantifies perforation rate by sinus width: narrow (≤12 mm) 1.1% vs wide (>12 mm) 16.1%.
- Confirms membrane perforation as independent predictor of early failure even with transcrestal (less invasive) approach.
- Large single-implant cohort restricted to the difficult RBH ≤5 mm scenario.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Design: retrospective multicenter chart review, anonymized records, 7 centers.
- Population: 430 patients, single-implant transcrestal sinus floor elevation, RBH ≤5 mm.
- Predictors tested: patient (age, sex, smoking, perio history), sinus (BH, bucco-palatal width at 10 mm height, floor inclination, Underwood septa), surgical (technique = rotary/osteotome/ultrasonic, implant surface, simultaneous vs staged, membrane perforation).
- SW measured on CBCT when available; narrow ≤12 mm, wide >12 mm. Outcome at 1 year of loading.
- Statistics: univariate + multivariate stepwise logistic regression; χ² for complications.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Survival: 418/430 implants in function at 1 yr; early implant failure 12 cases (2.8%).
- Multivariate predictors of early failure: Sinus Width wide OR 8.50 (95% CI 1.02–70.42, p=.047); Membrane perforation OR 4.21 (95% CI 1.10–16.05, p=.035). No other variable significant (RBH itself NS, OR 1.21).
- Membrane perforation 7.2% overall; univariate predictor = bucco-palatal width only (OR 15.5, 95% CI 3.59–56.59, p=.000). Narrow 1.1% vs wide 16.1%.
- Other adverse events: acute sinusitis 0.9%, implant displacement into sinus 0.7%, oro-antral fistula 0.2%, BPPV 0.5% (osteotome cases).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Author-stated: retrospective design, CBCT not available for all patients (SW measured only in subset), periapical radiographs not standardized, low event numerosity prevented analysis of post-op complications.
- Additional: heterogeneous surgical techniques/operators across 7 centers; residual confounding; 1-yr follow-up only (early failure focus, no long-term).

## 6. Related Work
- Pommer 2009 (in vitro membrane detachment force ∝ width) — biological basis for SW finding.
- Diaz-Olivares 2021, Al-Moraissi 2018 (perforation meta-analyses).
- Stacchi 2018 (defined narrow/wide SW threshold).

## 7. Glossary
- RBH/BH: residual bone height between alveolar crest and sinus floor.
- SW (bucco-palatal sinus width): buccal-to-palatal distance measured at 10 mm height including the crest; narrow ≤12 mm, wide >12 mm.
- TSFE: transcrestal (crestal) sinus floor elevation.
- BPPV: benign paroxysmal positional vertigo (osteotome malleting complication).
- Underwood septa: bony sinus septa.
