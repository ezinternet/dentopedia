## Why Ingested

Extends the airway/OSA axis of myofunctional elastodontic therapy: where [[wiki/orthodontics/myofunctional/inchingolo-2022-amcop-elastodontic-protocol-interception-review]] describes the AMCOP elastodontic interceptive protocol qualitatively, this retrospective cephalometric study quantifies the *magnitude* of pharyngeal airway change AMCOP produces versus mainstream functional appliances (Twin Block, RPE) in the same Class II growing cohort. It anchors a key clinical caveat — elastodontic airway gains are real but the smallest of the three (small-to-moderate effect sizes), and all gains are 2D-morphological, not functional.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective lateral-cephalometric comparison (n=50 growing Class II children, 6–12 y) of pharyngeal airway change after Twin Block vs RPE vs AMCOP elastodontic therapy: all three significantly enlarged SPAS/MAS/PAS, but Twin Block produced the largest gains (SPAS +2.1 mm, large effect d=1.35) and AMCOP the smallest (SPAS +0.85 mm, small-moderate d=0.55).

## 한줄요약

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First within-cohort head-to-head of three mechanistically distinct growing-patient appliances (Twin Block = true sagittal mandibular advancement; RPE = transverse maxillary expansion; AMCOP = elastodontic neuromuscular re-education) on the same pharyngeal airway parameters (SPAS, MAS, PAS).
2. Quantifies a dose-response intuition: the appliance with the greatest mandibular advancement (Twin Block, ΔSNB +1.7°, ΔCo–Gn +3.3 mm) also produced the greatest linear airway enlargement, while non-sagittal appliances produced smaller linear changes.
3. Provides effect-size–stratified airway estimates for AMCOP specifically (small-to-moderate, d=0.46–0.55), giving the elastodontic literature a comparative airway benchmark it previously lacked.
4. Frames all results as **morphological, not functional** — explicitly cautions that 2D linear airway gains do not equal airflow/AHI improvement, and positions the study as hypothesis-generating for future CBCT + polysomnography work.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Sample**: 50 consecutive pediatric patients (27 F, 23 M; mean age 9.2 ± 1.8 y; range 6–12 y) from institutional archive. Class II skeletal malocclusion (ANB > 4°), good general health, no systemic/respiratory conditions. Excluded: craniofacial syndromes, poor radiographs, airway-affecting conditions, prior orthognathic/fixed-appliance treatment.
- **Groups** (no matching; baseline-comparable in age, sex, ANB/SNB, duration, p>0.05):
  - **Group A — Twin Block (TB)**, n=18: upper/lower acrylic plates, construction bite 5–7 mm advancement + 2–4 mm vertical; worn 20–22 h/day; active 10–12 mo (mean 11.0 ± 0.8 mo).
  - **Group B — RPE (Hyrax)**, n=16: banded to upper premolars/molars; 1/4-turn per day until midline diastema; passive retainer ≥6 mo; mean duration 9.0 ± 0.8 mo.
  - **Group C — AMCOP SC elastodontic**, n=16: medical-grade silicone; worn overnight 8–10 h + 1–2 h daytime; mean 11.0 ± 0.8 mo.
- **Imaging**: Digital lateral teleradiographs (T0 pre / T1 post), natural head position, maximum intercuspation, lips relaxed, gentle nasal breathing. Single operator, standardized exposure.
- **Tracing**: Delta Dent v2.2.1; single trained examiner, **not blinded** (acknowledged bias). Intra-examiner reliability: ICC 0.86–0.94 skeletal, 0.83–0.91 airway (20 cephalograms retraced at 2 wk).
- **Airway parameters**: SPAS (superior posterior airway space, retropalatal), MAS (middle airway space, oropharyngeal at tongue body), PAS (posterior/inferior airway space, retroglossal at tongue base).
- **Statistics**: SPSS 27. Paired t-tests for intra-group T0→T1; one-way ANOVA + Tukey post-hoc for inter-group; α<0.05. Cohen's d effect sizes for all airway pre–post changes.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Skeletal** (TB greatest): TB ΔSNB +1.7° (p<0.001), ΔANB −1.5° (p<0.001), ΔCo–Gn +3.3 mm. RPE and AMCOP modest but significant (p<0.05).

**Dentoalveolar** (Table 3):
- Overjet: TB −3.6 mm (6.7→3.1, p<0.001); RPE −1.6 mm (p=0.012); AMCOP −1.7 mm (p=0.009).
- Overbite: TB −1.4 mm (p=0.014); RPE −0.6 mm (p=0.093, NS); AMCOP −0.8 mm (p=0.081, NS).
- IMPA: TB +2.9° (p=0.019); RPE +1.5° (p=0.046); AMCOP +1.5° (p=0.052, NS).

**Pharyngeal airway** (Δ mm [95% CI], Cohen's d):
- **Twin Block**: SPAS +2.14 [1.92; 2.36] d=1.35; MAS +1.96 [1.81; 2.12] d=1.24; PAS +1.42 [1.27; 1.57] d=1.11 (all p<0.001; large effects).
- **RPE**: SPAS +1.41 [1.15; 1.67] d=0.84; MAS +0.89 [0.67; 1.10] d=0.76; PAS +0.75 [0.47; 1.04] d=0.59 (moderate-to-large).
- **AMCOP**: SPAS +0.85 [0.60; 1.10] d=0.55; MAS +0.91 [0.73; 1.09] d=0.48; PAS +0.58 [0.36; 0.81] d=0.46 (small-to-moderate; all CIs above zero).

**Intergroup (ANOVA + Tukey)**: Significant intergroup differences for SNB, ANB, Co–Gn, and all airway parameters (p<0.05). TB > both RPE and AMCOP for SNB and SPAS/MAS/PAS (p<0.01). RPE vs AMCOP not significant (p>0.05).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Retrospective, single-center → selection bias; no objective compliance verification (TB and AMCOP especially).
- **No untreated control group** → cannot separate treatment effect from normal growth.
- **2D lateral cephalometry only** → linear sagittal projection; no airway volume, cross-sectional area, or asymmetry. Authors flag CBCT as the desirable upgrade but deemed unjustified radiation in asymptomatic children.
- **Morphometric, not functional** → no AHI, oxygen saturation, polysomnography, or airflow data; linear gains do NOT confirm reduced sleep-disordered breathing.
- Single non-blinded examiner; no long-term follow-up (stability unknown).
- Future: prospective longitudinal designs with 3D imaging, objective compliance monitoring, and functional respiratory/sleep outcomes.

## 7. Glossary

- **AMCOP**: elastodontic interceptive appliance (Protection Smile, Italy); soft medical-grade silicone applying light elastic forces for neuromuscular balance, tongue posture, dentoalveolar guidance.
- **Twin Block (TB)**: bite-block functional appliance posturing mandible forward for true sagittal Class II correction.
- **RPE**: Rapid Palatal Expander (Hyrax); transverse maxillary expansion via midpalatal suture.
- **SPAS / MAS / PAS**: superior posterior / middle / inferior-posterior airway spaces — linear pharyngeal widths at retropalatal, oropharyngeal, and retroglossal levels.
- **OSA / AHI**: obstructive sleep apnea / apnea–hypopnea index (pediatric abnormal ≥1 event/h).
- **SNB / ANB / Co–Gn**: mandibular sagittal position / maxillo-mandibular discrepancy / total mandibular length (condylion–gnathion).
- **Cohen's d**: standardized effect size (≈0.2 small, 0.5 moderate, 0.8 large).
