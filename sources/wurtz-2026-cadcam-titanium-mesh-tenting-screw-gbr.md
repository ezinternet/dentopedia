## One-line Summary

Retrospective study (n=40, 5-year follow-up) comparing CAD/CAM customized titanium mesh vs tenting screws with resorbable collagen membrane for horizontal/vertical GBR: equivalent bone gain, significantly shorter operative time with Ti mesh (62.4 vs 72.7 min), 100% implant survival.

## 한줄요약

맞춤형 CAD/CAM 티타늄 메시 vs 텐팅 스크루+흡수성 콜라겐막 후향적 비교(n=40, 5년): 골증대량 동등, Ti 메시군 수술 시간 유의하게 짧음(62.4 vs 72.7분), 임플란트 생존율 100%.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Retrospective observational study (STROBE-compliant)
- **Journal**: Clinical Implant Dentistry and Related Research, 2026;28(2):e70139
- **Ethics**: CERA approval no. 2025/101; treatments performed in 2020, records retrieved 2025
- **Registration**: Not an RCT; retrospective allocation

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Equivalent bone gain**: CAD/CAM Ti mesh and tenting-screw + collagen membrane produced statistically identical vertical and horizontal bone gain at 6 months (p>0.05 all comparisons)
2. **Significantly shorter operative time**: Ti mesh group 62.4 min (range 60–65) vs tent-pole 72.7 min (range 60–85), p<0.05 — attributed to passive fit eliminating intraoperative trimming/bending
3. **100% implant survival** at 5 years: 60 implants in 40 augmented sites, no failures; stable marginal bone levels
4. **Pseudo-periosteum**: Class 2 in 5 tent-pole vs 2 Ti-mesh sites (NS, p=0.13); benign finding
5. Confirms PASS principle: space maintenance (not barrier material) is the key GBR determinant

## 3. Methodology

- **Design**: Retrospective, 2 groups (n=20 each), ethics approved
- **Group A (Tent-pole)**: DBBM + 50% autologous bone (ramus scraper), covered with resorbable collagen membrane stabilized by tenting screws; primary closure
- **Group B (CAD/CAM Ti mesh)**: Same graft mixture, covered with patient-specific CAD/CAM Ti mesh + resorbable collagen membrane overlay; primary closure
- **Follow-up**: CBCT at T0 and T1 (6 months); peri-implant marginal bone levels at prosthetic loading and 5 years
- **Statistics**: Independent t-test, ANCOVA (adjusted for age), Fisher's exact test; post-hoc power analysis (detectable Cohen's d=0.91)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Bone gain at 6 months (T1):**

| Site | Tent-pole height | Ti-mesh height | Tent-pole width | Ti-mesh width |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Maxilla | 8.70±2.60 mm | 8.93±2.54 mm | 4.70±1.45 mm | 5.27±1.54 mm |
| Mandible | 9.35±2.32 mm | 9.25±2.26 mm | 4.90±1.34 mm | 5.30±1.42 mm |

All intergroup comparisons: p>0.05.

**Marginal bone loss at 5 years:**
- Maxilla: 1.80±0.38 mm (tent-pole 1.82±0.36 mm vs Ti-mesh 1.78±0.40 mm)
- Mandible: 1.35±0.27 mm; posterior > anterior
- No significant intergroup difference

**Operative time**: 72.7 min (tent-pole) vs 62.4 min (Ti-mesh), p<0.05

**Complications**: No major complications (infection, graft failure) in either group

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Retrospective design; allocation not randomized → selection bias possible
- Small sample (n=20/group); post-hoc power detects only large effects (d=0.91)
- No volumetric 3D analysis; only linear bone gain measured
- 5-year follow-up limited to marginal bone levels, not ridge volumetric stability
- Needs prospective RCTs with larger cohorts for definitive comparison

## 7. Glossary

- **Tent-pole technique**: Tenting screws placed under resorbable membrane to maintain space without mesh rigidity
- **CAD/CAM Ti mesh**: Patient-specific customized titanium mesh designed from preoperative CBCT; passive fit
- **Pseudo-periosteum**: Fibrous connective tissue layer forming beneath rigid barriers; Class 0–3 classification
- **DBBM**: Deproteinized bovine bone mineral (xenograft particulate)
