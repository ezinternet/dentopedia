## One-line Summary
Retrospective histomorphometric analysis (n=24, 4 mo) of post-extraction socket preservation with Bio-Oss + calcium sulphate vs Bio-Oss alone: combined group showed significantly more vital bone (62.5% vs 31.25%) and less acellular bone (5% vs 32.91%).

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Medicine 2025, 14, 3 (received Nov 2024, published 24 Dec 2024).
- **Study type**: Retrospective evaluation (2023) of a 2008–2009 pilot prospective protocol — same surgeon, randomized assignment to test/control at time of original surgery, histomorphometry at 4 months.
- **Setting**: University of Verona, Dentistry and Maxillo-Facial Surgery Clinic.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Demonstrates that combining a fast-resorbing carrier (calcium sulphate) with slow-resorbing DBBM (Bio-Oss) yields more vital bone and less acellular bone at 4 months than DBBM alone — a meaningful protocol modification.
- Provides 4-month histomorphometric anchor (vital bone, acellular bone, residual graft, fibrous tissue) for socket preservation studies.
- All sites covered with collagen membrane (Bio-Gide) and primary closure — represents a "best-practice" protocol baseline.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Retrospective evaluation of patients treated at Verona 2008–2009 for hopeless mono-/pluri-radicular tooth extraction with implant-prosthetic rehabilitation plan.
- Test group (n=12 sites): Bio-Oss Spongiosa 0.25–1 mm (Geistlich) + CalMatrix calcium sulphate binder (Lifecore Biomedical) in 50:50 proportion.
- Control group (n=12 sites): Bio-Oss alone (saline-moistened).
- Both groups: Bio-Gide collagen membrane coverage, primary closure via full-thickness mucoperiosteal flap.
- Atraumatic extraction; bone curettage; same operator.
- Biopsy at 4 months via trephine during implant osteotomy; histomorphometry: vital bone (VB), acellular bone (AB), residual graft particles (RP), fibrous tissue (FT).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Vital bone**: Test 62.5% vs Control 31.25% (p < 0.05).
- **Acellular bone**: Test 5% vs Control 32.91% (p < 0.05).
- Both differences statistically significant.
- Authors interpret as calcium sulphate accelerating early vital-bone formation while reducing inert/acellular fraction.
- Ridge dimensional changes maintained in both groups (per radiographic eval).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Retrospective design (2023 evaluation of 2008–2009 surgery records) — though originally randomized.
- Small n (24 samples total).
- Single timepoint (4 months); long-term implant outcomes not reported in this paper.
- Single operator/center — generalization limited.

## 7. Glossary
- **DBBM (Deproteinized Bovine Bone Mineral)**: Anorganic bovine bone, slow-resorbing osteoconductive scaffold.
- **Calcium sulphate (CalMatrix)**: Fast-resorbing biocompatible binder; acts as a temporary cell-friendly matrix that disappears in weeks.
- **Vital bone (VB)**: Histologic bone tissue containing viable osteocytes — index of active osteogenesis.
- **Acellular bone (AB)**: Mineralized tissue lacking viable cells — typically residual/encapsulated.
