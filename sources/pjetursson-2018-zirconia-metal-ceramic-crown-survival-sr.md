## Why Ingested

Key SR comparing zirconia-ceramic vs metal-ceramic crown survival and complication rates on implants — foundational clinical evidence for single crown material selection, with direct relevance to prosthetic material choice in daily practice. [[dental-materials/warreth-2020-all-ceramic-restorations-narrative-review]]

## One-line Summary

SR of 35 studies (n=5,275 crowns): zirconia-ceramic implant SCs show comparable 5-year survival to metal-ceramic (97.6% vs 98.3%) but significantly more material fractures (2.1% vs 0.2%) and fewer aesthetic complications.

## 한줄요약

임플란트 지지 단일 지르코니아 크라운 SR (35편, n=5,275): 5년 생존율은 금속도재관(98.3%)과 유사(97.6%)하나 재료 파절이 유의하게 많고(2.1% vs 0.2%) 심미적 합병증은 적음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Clinical Oral Implants Research, 2018; 29(Suppl. 16):199–214
- **Study type**: Systematic review (with meta-analysis for 5-year proportions using Poisson regression)
- **Registration**: PROSPERO CRD42017079002
- **Search**: PubMed up to November 2016; 5,263 titles → 35 included studies
- **Inclusion**: Human studies, ≥10 patients, ≥3 years follow-up, implant-supported SCs only

## 2. Key Contributions

- First comprehensive SR directly comparing veneered/monolithic zirconia vs metal-ceramic implant-supported SCs
- Meta-analysis using robust Poisson regression for 5-year survival and complication proportions
- Notes that no monolithic zirconia studies met the ≥3 year inclusion criteria at time of writing (pre-2016)
- Prepared for ITI Consensus Conference 2018

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PICO-defined focused question
- Electronic MEDLINE search + manual reference screening
- Dual independent reviewer selection; disagreements resolved by discussion
- Poisson regression models for summary 5-year estimates with 95% CI
- Separated data into SCs (this review) vs FDPs (companion Sailer 2018 review)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Survival rates (5-year)**
- Metal-ceramic SCs: 98.3% (95% CI: 96.8–99.1; n=4,363)
- Zirconia-ceramic SCs: 97.6% (95% CI: 94.3–99.0; n=912)
- Difference not statistically significant

**Complication-free rate (5-year)**
- Metal-ceramic: 86.7% (95% CI: 80.7–91.0; n=1,300)
- Zirconia-ceramic: 83.8% (95% CI: 61.6–93.8; n=76)

**Technical complications**
- Chipping of veneering ceramic: 2.9% (MC) vs 2.8% (ZC) — similar
- Material fracture (crown loss): 2.1% (ZC) vs 0.2% (MC) — p=0.001, significantly more in zirconia

**Aesthetic complications**
- Zirconia-ceramic SCs had fewer aesthetic complications than metal-ceramics
- Biological outcomes: similar between groups

**Monolithic zirconia**: No studies fulfilled the ≥3-year inclusion criteria — excluded from analysis

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample for zirconia group (n=76 for complication analysis vs n=1,300 for MC)
- Wide CI for zirconia complication-free rate reflects limited data
- No monolithic zirconia data could be included (insufficient follow-up in literature at time of search)
- Heterogeneity among included studies not fully modeled

## 6. Related Work

- Companion review: Sailer et al. 2018 (FDPs, same dataset/conference)
- Prior SR: Jung et al. 2012 — 5-year SC survival 96.3% (all materials combined)
- Sailer et al. 2009 — zirconia abutments comparable to metal abutments

## 7. Glossary

- **SC**: Single crown
- **FDP**: Fixed dental prosthesis
- **Veneered zirconia**: Zirconia framework + feldspathic veneering ceramic
- **Monolithic zirconia**: Full-contour zirconia, no veneering layer
- **Chipping**: Fracture/delamination of veneering ceramic only (not full crown failure)
- **Material fracture**: Crown fracture requiring replacement (prosthetic failure)
