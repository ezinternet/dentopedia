## Why Ingested

임플란트 수술 전 CBCT에서 상악동 병변의 유병률을 체계적으로 분류한 cross-sectional 연구. 상악동 점막비후가 임플란트 후보 환자의 31.4%에서 발견되며 가장 흔한 병변임을 확인. [[sinus-lift/pseudocyst/kucukkurt-2025-maxillary-sinus-pathologies-cbct-prevalence]]의 대규모 연구와 짝을 이룸.

## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional CBCT study (n=140 implant candidates) found 63.5% had maxillary sinus pathologies; mucosal thickening (31.4%) was most prevalent, followed by retention cysts (17.1%), with no significant association with age, sex, or dentition status.

## 한줄요약

임플란트 후보 환자 140명 CBCT 분석: 63.5%에서 상악동 병변, 점막비후(31.4%)가 가장 흔하고 연령·성별·치아 상태와 유의한 연관 없음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Descriptive / cross-sectional study
- **Journal**: Journal of Advanced Periodontology & Implant Dentistry (2021)
- **DOI**: [10.34172/japid.2021.001](https://doi.org/10.34172/japid.2021.001)
- **Sample**: 140 CBCT images, implant candidates, northern Iran; mean age 46y (range 18-81)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Population-based prevalence of sinus pathology in implant candidates (Iranian cohort)
- Clear threshold definition: mucosal thickening >5mm
- Five-category classification system
- No significant demographic predictors identified

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 6-month consecutive CBCT review from 2 oral radiology clinics (summer/autumn)
- Inclusion: implant candidates ≥18y with full maxillary sinus visibility
- Exclusion: prior sinus lift or implant placement, trauma
- CBCT: NewTom 3G, FOV=9", kVp=120, mA=150
- Single examiner (oral and maxillofacial radiologist) — all three planes reviewed
- Categories: mucosal thickening >5mm, retention cyst, partial/complete opacification, polypoidal thickening, healthy
- Chi-square test; significance p<0.05

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Finding | Frequency |
|---|---|
| Mucosal thickening (>5mm) | 31.4% (88/280 sinuses) |
| Retention cyst | 17.1% (48/280) |
| Partial/complete opacification | 9.3% (26/280) |
| Polypoidal mucosal thickening | 5.7% (16/280) |
| Healthy | 36.5% (102/280) |

- Overall pathology rate: 63.5%
- Higher frequency in males (65.5%) vs females (63.5%) — not significant
- Higher in <46y vs ≥46y — not significant
- Higher in partial edentulism vs complete edentulism or full dentition — not significant (p=0.061)
- Mucosal thickening thought to reflect odontogenic irritation, periapical lesions, and periodontal disease

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single examiner (reduces inter-examiner variability but limits calibration)
- No correlation with clinical symptoms or systemic history
- Seasonal collection (summer/autumn) may influence retention cyst prevalence
- Threshold >5mm for mucosal thickening (vs >2mm or >3mm in other studies) — may undercount mild thickening

## 7. Glossary

- **Mucosal thickening**: membrane thickness >5mm (this study); other studies use >2mm or >3mm
- **Polypoidal mucosal thickening**: localized sessile or pedunculated mucosal protrusion
- **Mucus retention cyst**: dome-shaped radiopaque cyst from blocked seromucous gland ducts
