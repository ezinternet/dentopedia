## Why Ingested
Provides the landmark mechanistic description of EPS-microcolony 3D architecture and pH microenvironments in cariogenic biofilms; directly extends [[oral-microbiology/bowen-2011-streptococcus-mutans-glucosyltransferases]] and is a primary reference for [[oral-microbiology/karygianni-2020-biofilm-matrixome-extracellular-components]].

## One-line Summary
Review of experimental evidence establishing the S. mutans-derived EPS matrix as the principal cariogenic biofilm virulence determinant: Gtf-mediated glucan forms a 3D scaffold creating compartmentalised pH microenvironments (pH 4.5–5.5) at the microcolony/enamel interface that resist neutralisation for >2 hours.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Establishes EPS matrix (not simply acid production) as the primary determinant of cariogenic biofilm virulence.
- Uses fluorescent probe incorporated during EPS synthesis to map 3D spatio-temporal EPS development in intact mixed-species biofilms by confocal microscopy — first such technique for intact oral biofilm.
- Documents three-phase EPS matrix assembly model: (i) surface EPS primer → bacterial clustering; (ii) continuous in-situ EPS expansion → EPS-enmeshed cell core; (iii) core → 3D microcolony scaffold with bridging between microcolonies.
- Maps compartmentalised pH microenvironments (pH 4.5–5.5) within EPS-microcolony complexes using fluorescent pH indicators; low pH persists >2 hours despite neutral-pH buffer exposure.
- Demonstrates deletion of gtfB and gtfC eliminates acidic microenvironments and reduces caries virulence in rodent model.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Design: review with supporting novel experimental data (mixed-species biofilm imaging).
- In vitro model: mixed-species biofilms (S. mutans, S. oralis, A. naeslundii) grown on saliva-coated hydroxyapatite (sHA) with 1% sucrose for 43, 67, 91, and 115 hours.
- Techniques: fluorescent probe-labelled EPS visualisation by confocal laser scanning microscopy; 3D rendering; two-photon excitation microscopy for pH mapping; fluorescent pH indicator incorporated into 3D matrix.
- Quantitative proteomics (MudPIT) for GtfB/GtfC protein abundance at early biofilm phase.
- In vivo virulence: rodent caries model with gtfB/gtfC deletion mutants.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- EPS matrix core is assembled primarily by GtfB and GtfC; gtfB and gtfC deletion eliminates EPS-microcolony complexes.
- Low-pH environments (pH 4.5–5.5) detected at interior of EPS-microcolony complexes and at microcolony/sHA interface even when bathed in neutral pH (7.0) buffer.
- >2 hours of neutral pH buffer required before neutralisation of acidic microenvironments in intact biofilm.
- GtfB and GtfC protein abundance enhanced at early biofilm phase by MudPIT quantitative proteomics.
- gtfB and gtfC expression highly induced in mixed-species biofilms vs. single-species S. mutans biofilms — interspecies signalling via autoinducers.
- Glucanohydrolase (dextranase/mutanase) treatment of biofilm dismantles matrix, eliminates acidic microenvironments, and prevents microcolony formation.
- GtfD forms soluble glucan as primer for GtfB; GtfD soluble product also serves as local sugar reservoir via degradation by dextranase/fructanase.
- Deletion of gtfB and/or gtfC associated with reduced virulence in rodent caries model (Yamashita et al., 1993, referenced).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Diffusion limitation mechanisms for charged ions (saliva buffering) within EPS matrix not fully elucidated.
- Relative contributions of insoluble EPS vs. intracellular polysaccharides to local acid reservoir not fully quantified.
- Authors call for further investigation of spatial-temporal EPS synthesis and acidic niche development as potential therapeutic targets.

## 7. Glossary
- EPS-microcolony complex: structured 3D cluster of bacterial cells enmeshed in and surrounded by exopolysaccharide matrix.
- MudPIT: Multidimensional Protein Identification Technology — high-throughput quantitative proteomics using liquid chromatography-mass spectrometry.
- pH microenvironment: localised region of low pH (4.5–5.5) within the biofilm, sheltered by diffusion-limiting EPS matrix from bulk saliva neutralisation.
- gtfB/gtfC: genes encoding S. mutans glucosyltransferases B and C; deletion eliminates insoluble glucan matrix and abolishes cariogenic virulence.
- Autoinducer: inter-bacterial signalling molecule driving upregulation of gtfB/gtfC in mixed-species biofilms compared to monospecies.
