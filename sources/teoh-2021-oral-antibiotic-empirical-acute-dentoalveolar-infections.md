## Why Ingested

[[wiki/drug/lockhart-2019-antibiotic-pulpal-periapical-pain-swelling]](치수·치근단 통증 비처방)에서 한 걸음 더 — 이미 **급성 치성감염(농양)**이 생겼을 때 어떤 경구 항생제를 쓸지, 그리고 **항생제가 정말 필요한지**를 묻는다. 항생제 스튜어드십 클러스터([[wiki/drug/mendez-millan-2024-antibiotic-overprescription-apical-periodontitis-sr-ma]])를 "감염 발생 후" 시나리오로 확장.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (2,994 screened → 8 included): across many regimens (amoxicillin, amox/clav, cefalexin, clindamycin, metronidazole, etc.) for acute dentoalveolar infections, virtually all showed clinical success, and one study found no antibiotics gave the same outcome as broad-spectrum — concluding broad-spectrum first-line empirical antibiotics are unnecessary, narrow-spectrum is equally effective in healthy patients, and dental treatment (drainage / source removal) is the primary determinant of success.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(2,994편 선별 → 8편 포함): 급성 치성감염(농양)에 쓰인 여러 항생제 요법(amoxicillin, amox/clav, cefalexin, clindamycin, metronidazole 등) 거의 모두 임상 성공, 한 연구에서는 **무항생제 = 광범위 항생제와 동일 결과**. 결론: 광범위 항생제 1차 경험적 처방 불필요, 건강한 환자엔 좁은 스펙트럼도 동등하게 효과적이며, **감염원 제거(배농·근관치료·발치)가 성공의 1차 요인**.

- **Journal**: Antibiotics (MDPI) 2021, 10(3), 240
- **DOI**: 10.3390/antibiotics10030240; published 28 Feb 2021; open access (CC BY)
- **Institution**: Melbourne Dental School, University of Melbourne; National Centre for Antimicrobial Stewardship, Australia
- **Type**: Systematic review (no meta-analysis)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Maps the heterogeneity of empirical oral antibiotic regimens used for acute dentoalveolar infections and shows they nearly all "work" — undermining the case for broad-spectrum first-line.
- Highlights a study where **no antibiotics matched broad-spectrum** outcomes when drainage was achieved.
- Reframes the primary therapeutic lever as **dental treatment (drainage/source control)**, with antibiotics as adjunct only for spread/systemic involvement.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Three-database systematic search; two independent reviewers screened + quality-assessed; extracted regimens and clinical outcomes.
- 2,994 studies identified → 8 included.
- Antibiotics used: amoxicillin, amoxicillin/clavulanic acid, cefalexin, clindamycin, erythromycin, metronidazole, moxifloxacin, ornidazole, phenoxymethylpenicillin. Regimens varied in dose/frequency/duration.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Vast majority of regimens → clinical success regardless of spectrum.
- One study: patients receiving **no antibiotics had the same outcome** as broad-spectrum recipients.
- Ideal choice/regimen/spectrum **unclear** — but broad-spectrum first-line is unnecessary.
- Narrow-spectrum agents appear as effective in otherwise healthy individuals.
- Effectiveness of **dental treatment (source control)** is the primary success factor; the role of antibiotics is questioned in primary space odontogenic infections **if drainage can be established**.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 8 heterogeneous studies; no meta-analysis possible; varied regimens/outcomes.
- No microbial sampling in routine community practice → empirical basis only.
- Does not resolve immunocompromised / spreading-infection scenarios.

## 7. Glossary

- **Acute dentoalveolar infection / abscess**: pus-forming infection from necrotic pulp / periodontal / pericoronal tissues, with periapical connective-tissue inflammation.
- **Empirical therapy**: antibiotic chosen without microbial sampling (routine in community dental practice).
- **Source control / drainage**: removing the cause (RCT, extraction, incision & drainage) — the primary treatment.
- **Narrow- vs broad-spectrum**: e.g. phenoxymethylpenicillin/amoxicillin (narrower) vs amox-clav/moxifloxacin (broader); review favours narrow first-line.
