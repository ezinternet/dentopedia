## Why Ingested

기존 occlusion 카테고리에는 [[occlusion/di-fiore-2022-periimplant-bone-loss-overload-occlusal-analysis]] (492→7편 SR, ROBINS-I)가 교합 과부하–임플란트주위 골소실 관계를 보수적으로 다뤘다. 본 Mojaver 2025 SR(80편)은 동일 PICO를 훨씬 넓은 근거 풀로 재검토하며 골소실 정량범위(0.65–3.0 mm)와 임플란트주위염 발생률(20–50%) 수치를 제공하고, 기계적 과부하–biofilm 염증의 dual-pathway 모델을 제시해 di-fiore 페이지를 보강·확장한다. 또한 [[occlusion/fan-2018-occlusal-trauma-and-excessive-occlusal]]의 교합외상 개념을 임플란트 맥락으로 contextualize.

## One-line Summary
Narrative SR (160→80 studies, no meta-analysis) reporting that occlusal overload/trauma associates with marginal bone loss (0.65–3.0 mm) and peri-implantitis incidence (20–50%), driven by a mechanical-overload × biofilm-inflammation dual pathway.

## 한줄요약
서술적 SR(160→80편, 메타분석 없음) — 교합 과부하·외상이 임플란트 변연골소실(0.65–3.0 mm) 및 임플란트주위염 발생률(20–50%)과 연관되며, 기계적 과부하 × biofilm 염증의 이중 경로가 핵심 동인.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Synthesizes 80 studies (clinical, experimental, FEA, prior SR) on occlusal overload/trauma and peri-implant health under a single PICO.
- Quantifies two outcome bands: occlusal-factor marginal bone loss ~0.65–1.20 mm (Table 1); traumatic-force bone level change 1.0–3.0 mm with peri-implantitis 20–50% (Table 2).
- Proposes a **dual-pathway model**: mechanical overload synergizes with bacterial biofilm-induced inflammation to accelerate peri-implant tissue breakdown (overload alone vs overload + inflammation behave differently).
- Reaffirms biomechanical rationale: implants lack a periodontal ligament, so no force modulation / mechanoreceptor feedback → inherently vulnerable to overload.
- Supports occlusal-scheme optimization: canine guidance and lingualized occlusion associated with less crestal bone loss than group function / monoplane.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **PICO**: In adult patients (≥18y) with dental implants, does occlusal trauma / increased loading vs normal occlusal forces increase risk of peri-implantitis or other peri-implant pathologies?
- **Databases**: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, Cochrane Library. (NOTE: abstract says Jan 2000–Oct 2023; the inclusion-criteria text says Jan 2010–Jan 2025 — an internal inconsistency in the paper.)
- **Designs included**: RCT, prospective/retrospective cohort, case-control, longitudinal, SR/MA, plus experimental and FEA studies; English only.
- **RoB**: Cochrane RoB (RCTs), Newcastle-Ottawa (cohort/case-control), ROBVIS RoB 2.0 visualizer; overall low–moderate.
- **Synthesis**: narrative only; explicitly **no meta-analysis**. Egger's test mentioned but "not included."

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- 160 references screened → **80 included**.
- Table 1 (occlusal-factor MBL): ~0.65 mm (lingualized occlusion, Kumari 6y) to 1.50 mm (overload case reports). Canine guidance < group function (Bahaa 0.85 mm). Early/immediate-loading studies clustered 0.70–0.90 mm. Rismanchian: no significant difference early vs standard loading.
- Table 2 (traumatic forces): bone level change 1.0–3.0 mm; peri-implantitis 20–50%. Naert SR 1.5–3.0 mm / 30% (marked loss in **inflamed** sites); Garaicoa-Pazmiño SR 1.5 mm / 32% (overload = risk factor for breakdown); Xu (rats) 50% with periodontal disease; Mattheos (15 dogs) loss of osseointegration **without** marginal bone loss; Koller: cemented > screw-retained MBL.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Heterogeneous designs and inconsistent definitions of "occlusal overload" prevent pooling — hence narrative-only synthesis (no MA → causal inference weak; associations, not proof).
- Most clinical studies short follow-up (≤3y); long-term outcomes unclear.
- Surrogate radiographic bone-loss measures rather than direct biomechanical assessment.
- Probable publication bias toward positive associations (effect sizes may be overstated).
- Internal date-range inconsistency and reliance on many small experimental/FEA and case-report entries weaken the evidence weight despite the large count (n=80).
- Future: standardized overload definitions, longer longitudinal studies, integrated biomechanical-biological methods.

## 7. Glossary
- **Occlusal overload**: nonphysiological occlusal force exceeding the implant–bone interface tolerance.
- **Marginal bone loss (MBL)**: radiographic crestal bone reduction around an implant.
- **Peri-implantitis**: inflammatory disease with progressive peri-implant bone loss after initial healing.
- **Dual-pathway model**: combined contribution of mechanical overload and biofilm-induced inflammation to peri-implant breakdown.
- **Canine guidance vs group function**: lateral-excursion occlusal schemes; canine guidance concentrates less localized stress.
