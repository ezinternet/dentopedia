## One-line Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis of 13 clinical studies showing that etch-and-rinse mode for universal adhesives provides superior retention, marginal adaptation, and marginal staining compared to self-etch mode.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Annals of Palliative Medicine, Vol 10, No 5, May 2021, pp. 5462–5473
- **DOI**: 10.21037/apm-21-890
- **Institution**: Department of Stomatology, Zhongshan Hospital, Xiamen University, Xiamen, China
- **Search period**: January 2000 – March 2020
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane Library, Embase

## 2. Key Contributions
- First meta-analysis specifically comparing etch-and-rinse vs self-etch application modes of universal adhesives using clinical (not in vitro) outcomes
- Demonstrates clinically meaningful superiority of etch-and-rinse mode for retention, marginal adaptation, and marginal staining
- Confirms no significant difference in secondary caries or postoperative sensitivity between modes
- Provides evidence-based guidance for clinician adhesive strategy selection

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: Systematic review with meta-analysis (PRISMA)
- **Databases searched**: PubMed, Cochrane Library, Embase (January 2000 – March 2020)
- **Initial records**: 2,516 non-replicated records identified and filtered
- **Included studies**: 13 randomized clinical trials (RCTs)
- **Software**: RevMan 5.3.5 (Cochrane, London, UK)
- **Outcome measures**: Retention rate, marginal adaptation, marginal staining, secondary caries, postoperative sensitivity
- **Statistical measure**: Odds ratio (OR) with 95% confidence intervals

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
| Outcome | OR (etch-and-rinse vs self-etch) | 95% CI | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Retention rate (favoring E&R) | 0.35 | 0.18–0.71 | 0.003 |
| Marginal adaptation (favoring E&R) | 0.49 | 0.36–0.67 | <0.001 |
| Marginal staining (favoring E&R) | 0.49 | 0.36–0.66 | <0.001 |
| Secondary caries | No significant difference | — | — |
| Postoperative sensitivity | No significant difference | — | — |

- Etch-and-rinse mode showed significantly better enamel bond strength versus self-etch (consistent with prior in vitro meta-analyses by Rosa et al., Cuevas-Suárez et al., Elkaffas et al.)
- Very low incidence rate of secondary caries in both groups

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Heterogeneity across included studies (different adhesive products, substrates, clinical contexts)
- Most studies focused on Class V NCCL restorations; generalizability to other cavity types limited
- Long-term (>5-year) clinical data remains sparse
- Publication bias not fully excluded
- Need for standardized clinical outcome reporting across trials

## 6. Related Work
- Rosa et al. — meta-analysis on enamel/dentin bond strength of universal adhesives
- Cuevas-Suárez et al. — confirmed ER advantage for enamel bond strength
- Elkaffas et al. — similar findings for bond strength
- Kaczor et al. — meta-analysis on nanoleakage of universal adhesives
- Diniz et al. — in vitro study showing ER advantage for enamel bond strength
- Stape et al. — effect of etching method and pH on fatigue strength of universal adhesives

## 7. Glossary
- **Universal adhesive**: 8th-generation adhesive that can be used in etch-and-rinse, self-etch, or selective-etch modes
- **Etch-and-rinse (E&R)**: Application mode with prior phosphoric acid etching and rinsing before adhesive placement
- **Self-etch (SE)**: Application mode where acidic adhesive demineralizes and infiltrates simultaneously without separate etching step
- **Marginal adaptation**: Degree to which restoration margin is flush with the tooth surface
- **Marginal staining**: Discoloration at the restoration-tooth interface
- **OR (Odds Ratio)**: Statistical measure comparing outcome frequency between two groups; OR < 1 here indicates fewer failures in etch-and-rinse group
