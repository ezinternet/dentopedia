## Why Ingested

TMD 물리치료 분사신장(spray-and-stretch / vapocoolant) 논문 감시 요청. 위키의 TMD 물리치료 근거는 운동요법·도수치료 SR 중심([[tmj/gonzalez-sanchez-2023-tmj-physiotherapy-treatment-sr]], [[tmj/zhu-2024-exercise-therapy-tmj-derangement-sr]])이고 근막통(myofascial) 트리거포인트 비활성화의 고전 기법인 Travell-Simons 분사신장 자체를 직접 측정한 1차 임상연구가 없었다. 본 연구(Koole 2019, J Prosthet Dent)는 구강안면통증 환자 60명 + 무증상 대조 61명에서 vapocoolant 분사가 최대개구량(MMO)을 즉시 증가시키는지를 정량화해, [[tmj/chan-2022-myogenous-tmd-diagnosis-treatment-review]]가 권고하는 근막통 처치의 임상 근거를 보강한다. Source는 PMC 전문 미제공이라 PubMed 초록 기반(abstract-only).

## One-line Summary

Clinical study (n=121: 60 orofacial-pain patients + 61 asymptomatic controls) showing that a vapocoolant spray-and-stretch maneuver to both cheeks immediately increased maximal mouth opening (MMO), with a larger relative gain in pain patients than controls (P<.001) and larger in women than men — abstract-only, full text not retrieved.

## 한줄요약

구강안면통증 환자 60명 + 무증상 대조 61명 임상연구 — 양 볼에 vapocoolant 분사 후 최대개구량(MMO)이 즉시 증가했고, 통증 환자에서 대조군보다(P<.001), 여성에서 남성보다 증가폭이 컸다 (PubMed 초록 기반, 전문 미확보).

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Spray and stretch technique and its effects on mouth opening
- **Authors**: Paul Koole, Adriaan J. J. Zonnenberg, Ronald Koole
- **Journal**: The Journal of Prosthetic Dentistry 2019;123(3):455–460
- **DOI**: [10.1016/j.prosdent.2019.05.032](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.prosdent.2019.05.032)
- **PMID**: 31326152
- **Study type**: Prospective interventional clinical study (two-group: orofacial-pain vs control); single immediate-effect measurement, no randomization/no sham arm
- **Source basis**: PubMed abstract (PMC full text not available — `full_text: false`)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies the **immediate effect of vapocoolant spray** on maximal mouth opening (MMO) in a real orofacial-pain population — most prior spray-and-stretch evidence is on trunk/limb/neck myofascial pain, not the masticatory system.
- Frames spray as a way to achieve the stretch component of the classic **spray-and-stretch** technique **without painful passive stretching** — a comfort/compliance argument for TMD myofascial management.
- Direct head-to-head of **symptomatic (n=60) vs asymptomatic (n=61)** subjects, isolating how baseline pain modifies the response.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Groups**: 61 controls (33 M / 28 F) from a general dental office; 60 orofacial-pain patients (30 M / 30 F) drawn from >750 oral-surgery patients. Pain verified by palpation of TMJs and masticatory, neck, and shoulder muscles.
- **Procedure**: Baseline MMO measured twice with a TMJ equilateral-triangle gauge (interincisal distance) → vapocoolant sprayed twice on both cheeks from mandibular angle to temple → MMO re-measured twice.
- **Analysis**: 2-way ANOVA for group effects with sex correction; covariance model to test age effect (α=.05).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **MMO increased** after spraying in most participants.
- **Group effect**: relative (%) and absolute MMO gain both significant (P<.001), larger in **orofacial-pain patients** than controls.
- **Sex**: women gained more than men for baseline (P=.008); the gain trend by sex did not reach significance (P=.090 for relative gain, P=.074 overall).
- **Age**: an effect on baseline appeared by group (P=.032) but was not significant in the covariance model (P=.73).
- **Clinical message**: spray can prevent the discomfort of passive stretching while still improving opening.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Abstract-only ingest** — methods/results detail limited to the PubMed abstract; effect-size magnitudes (mm) and SDs not captured.
- **Immediate effect only** — no follow-up; durability of MMO gain and symptom/pain outcomes (NRS, function) not reported here.
- **No sham/comparator arm** and no randomization → cannot separate the vapocoolant cooling effect from suggestion/repeat-measurement/warm-up; "spray alone vs spray-and-passive-stretch vs sham" remains untested.
- Population is heterogeneous "orofacial pain" rather than DC/TMD-classified myofascial vs arthrogenous subgroups.

## 7. Glossary

- **Spray-and-stretch (분사신장)**: Travell & Simons myofascial technique — a vapocoolant (e.g., ethyl chloride / fluoromethane) is swept over the muscle/referral zone to produce a brief cutaneous cold stimulus that reflexively reduces muscle tone, allowing the muscle to be passively stretched and the trigger point inactivated.
- **Vapocoolant**: rapidly evaporating topical coolant spray producing transient skin cooling.
- **MMO (Maximal Mouth Opening, 최대개구량)**: maximum interincisal distance, a standard masticatory range-of-motion metric.
- **Orofacial pain / myofascial pain (근막통)**: regional muscle pain with hyperirritable trigger points referring pain in a characteristic pattern.
