## Why Ingested

기존 [[implants/soft-tissue/jensen-2023-iti-consensus-bone-dimensions-soft-tissue]]와 [[implants/soft-tissue/sanz-2022-keratinized-mucosa-around-implants-consensus]]는 임플란트 주위 연조직 봉쇄 (Soft Tissue Seal)·생물학적 폭경 (Biologic Width)을 다루지만 보철 출현각 (Emergence Angle, EA)이 그 형성에 미치는 영향을 조직학적으로 입증한 자료가 없었다. 본 비글견 전임상 연구 (Lee 2025)는 좁은 EA(30°) vs 넓은 EA(60°) split-mouth 비교로 넓은 EA가 결합조직 부착을 손상시키고 변연골 개조 (Bone Remodeling)를 촉진함을 조직학·방사선학으로 처음 증명해, 보철 윤곽이 연조직 봉쇄에 미치는 기전 근거를 보강한다.

## One-line Summary
Beagle dog split-mouth preclinical study (n=5 dogs, 30 implants, 30° narrow vs 60° wide emergence angle, splinted prostheses, 6-month function): wide emergence angle produced greater crestal bone remodeling (24-wk e.g. T-splint 1.4 vs 0.57 mm), larger infiltrated connective tissue, shorter connective tissue attachment (~0.7 vs ~1.1 mm), and reduced peri-implant epithelial collagen — failing to form an adequate soft tissue seal.

## 한줄요약
비글견 split-mouth 전임상 연구 (n=5, 임플란트 30개): 넓은 출현각 (Emergence Angle 60°)이 좁은 각도 (30°)보다 변연골 개조를 더 많이 일으키고 결합조직 부착을 짧게(~0.7 vs ~1.1 mm) 만들어 안정적 연조직 봉쇄 형성을 방해했다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: The Effect of Restorative Emergence Angle on Soft and Hard Tissue Around Splinted Implants: A Preclinical Study
- **Authors**: Seung-Ju Lee, Yuseung Yi, Frank Schwarz, Jungwon Lee, Ki-Tae Koo (Seoul National University; Goethe-University Frankfurt)
- **Journal**: Clinical Oral Implants Research 2025;36(12):1603-1613
- **DOI**: 10.1111/clr.70035 (PMID 40859745, PMCID PMC12669426)
- **Study type**: Preclinical animal (beagle dog), split-mouth, randomized abutment assignment

## 2. Key Contributions

- First animal study to test the effect of the **restorative prosthetic emergence angle** (not just abutment geometry) on peri-implant soft and hard tissues, using actual splinted prostheses.
- Direct histological evidence that a **wide emergence angle (60°)** compromises connective tissue attachment and the soft tissue seal vs a **narrow angle (30°)**.
- Quantified biologic-width failure under wide EA: connective tissue attachment shrank to ~0.7 mm (vs ~1.1 mm narrow), junctional epithelium lengthened to ~2.7 mm (vs ~2.1 mm), and collagen fiber fraction in peri-implant epithelium fell.
- Showed the **middle/splinted positions** in the wide-EA group bore the highest frequency of high (>0.5 mm) bone remodeling.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: split-mouth, 5 beagle dogs, 30 implants (6 per dog). Bilateral P2-P3-P4 extracted; 2-mo healing; 3 implants placed equicrestally per side (3.1 × 7 mm, NR line Dentium), ≥3 mm inter-implant distance, non-submerged.
- **Intervention**: at 3 mo, custom titanium grade V abutments with **narrow (30°)** or **wide (60°)** emergence angle randomly assigned per hemi-mandible; 3 splinted resin crowns cemented (FujiCEM 2); 30 Ncm; out of occlusion. 6 months of function.
- **Position stratification**: T-end (mesial/distal end of terminal implant), T-splint (splinted zone of terminal implant), M-splint (splinted zone of middle implant).
- **Outcomes**: primary = histologic IS-to-bone-crest distance; secondary = radiographic crestal bone remodeling (0/4/12/24 wk, ImageJ), histomorphometry (PM, aJE, IS, BC, fBIC landmarks; ICT area), and circularly polarized light collagen-fiber area fraction (Picrosirius red, OE/OCT/PISE/PIE/PICT regions).
- **Statistics**: animal as unit (n=5), nonparametric — Friedman + Dunn (within group across positions), Wilcoxon signed-rank (between angles), Cochran's Q + McNemar/Bonferroni (high vs low remodeling).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Radiographic bone remodeling at 24 wk (WE vs NE)**: T-end 0.90 vs 0.12 mm; T-splint 1.4 vs 0.57 mm; M-splint 1.03 vs 0.61 mm. WE greater at every site.
- **High (>0.5 mm) remodeling distribution** across 6 groups significant (Cochran Q=14.487, p=0.013) but no significant pairwise post hoc; all WE splinted zones (T-splint, M-splint) classed as high remodeling.
- **Histomorphometry**: IS-BC distance larger in WE at all sites (e.g. T-splint 1.43 vs 1.01 mm). ICT area significantly larger in WE at T-splint (2.76 vs 1.34 mm²) and M-splint (3.79 vs 1.56 mm²). Connective tissue attachment (aJE-fBIC) significantly longer in NE at T-end (1.10 vs 0.55 mm) and T-splint (1.23 vs 0.83 mm).
- **Biologic width**: NE epithelial ~2.1 mm / CT ~1.1 mm (adequate); WE epithelial ~2.7 mm / CT ~0.7 mm (inadequate).
- **Collagen (circularly polarized light)**: PIE collagen fraction lower in WE (significant at M-splint, 29.17% vs 42.66%). OE collagen declined toward middle in WE (T-end 85.46% > T-splint 54.13% > M-splint 33.77%).
- **Histology**: NE thin junctional epithelium + firm CT attachment; WE plaque in the gap, JE down-growth to threads, extensive infiltrated CT.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Analysis limited to mesial/distal interproximal regions (no buccal/lingual).
- Only emergence angle studied (emergence profile not assessed).
- No non-splinting comparison group.
- No clinical parameters (probing depth, BOP, plaque scores).
- Small sample (n=5) — splinting-configuration effects only trends, not powered.

## 6. Related Work

- Yi et al., Katafuchi et al. — clinical cross-sectional links between >30° emergence angle and marginal bone loss / peri-implantitis; Strauss et al. proposed 40° threshold.
- Souza et al. — prior animal study (15° vs 45° healing abutments) showing more remodeling at wider angle via apical biologic-width shift.
- 2017 World Workshop (Araujo & Lindhe) — reference biologic-width dimensions (~2 mm epithelial, 1-2 mm CT).

## 7. Glossary

- **Emergence angle (EA)**: angle between the implant platform and the prosthesis margin; here narrow=30°, wide=60°.
- **Biologic width / soft tissue seal**: combined junctional epithelial + connective tissue attachment forming the peri-implant barrier.
- **ICT**: infiltrated (inflammatory) connective tissue area.
- **fBIC**: first bone-to-implant contact. **aJE**: apical extension of junctional epithelium. **IS**: implant shoulder. **BC**: bone crest. **PM**: peri-implant mucosa margin.
- **T-end / T-splint / M-splint**: terminal-end, terminal-splinted-zone, and middle-splinted-zone positions of the three-implant splint.
