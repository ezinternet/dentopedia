## Why Ingested

The existing [[wiki/implants/zhou-2021-placement-loading-protocols-single-implants-sr]] and [[wiki/implants/benic-2014-loading-protocols-single-implant-crowns-sr-ma]] focus on single-unit implant crowns; this SR+MA (Aiquel 2021, Clin Oral Implants Res) specifically addresses **multiple-unit fixed dental prostheses (FDPs)** supported by ≥2 implants — a distinct clinical context where cantilever forces, splinted biomechanics, and patient selection criteria differ. It is the companion SR to the ITI S5 Consensus on multiple-unit FDPs and provides the only meta-analysis on DPIL vs. DPDL survival for this prosthetic category.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (14 studies, 21 cohort groups, ≥3-yr follow-up) found that in multiple-unit implant-supported FDPs, all placement/loading combinations except IPIL achieved >90% implant survival, and a meta-analysis of 3 RCTs showed no significant survival difference between delayed placement with immediate vs. delayed loading (p = .227).

## 한줄요약

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First SR+MA specifically restricted to implant-supported **multiple-unit** fixed dental prostheses (≥2 implants per FDP) — distinguishing this from the large body of single-crown literature.
2. Systematically mapped all 9 possible placement/loading protocol combinations (IPIL, IPEL, IPDL, EPIL, EPEL, EPDL, DPIL, DPEL, DPDL) onto the available evidence; found that only 5 of 9 combinations were represented in the literature.
3. Performed the only quantitative meta-analysis (3 RCTs) comparing DPIL vs. DPDL for survival rate in multiple-unit FDPs.
4. Constructed four GRADE Summary of Findings tables (DPIL vs. DPDL; IPIL vs. DPDL; DPEL vs. DPDL; IPEL vs. DPDL).
5. Highlighted that biological complication (peri-implantitis, mucositis) data are critically underreported in multiple-unit FDP studies.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**PICOS framework**
- Population: Partially edentulous patients with FPDs on ≥2 implants
- Intervention: Immediate or early placement/loading vs. delayed protocols
- Comparison: Any two of the 9 protocol combinations
- Outcomes (primary): Implant survival; biological complications (peri-implantitis, mucositis)
- Outcomes (secondary): MBL change (mm), probing depth, soft-tissue level, BOP, keratinized tissue width, plaque index
- Study types: RCTs, prospective/retrospective cohort studies, case series with identifiable protocols

**Inclusion criteria (key)**
- ≥10 patients per study arm; ≥3 years of follow-up
- FPDs supported by ≥2 root-form implants; diameter 3–6 mm
- Adequate separate reporting of placement and loading timing
- Permanently screw-retained or cemented FPDs only

**Exclusion criteria (key)**
- Full-arch dentures or removable superstructures
- Irradiated bone, alveolar clefts, medications compromising bone metabolism
- Inability to distinguish single-unit crowns from multiple-unit FDPs

**Screening**: 7,002 titles identified → 360 full texts screened → 14 studies included (21 cohort groups)

**Risk of bias tools**: Cochrane RoB 2.0 for RCTs; Newcastle-Ottawa Scale for cohorts; Joanna Briggs Institute checklist for case series

**Statistical approach**: Random-effects meta-analysis for survival rate (DPIL vs. DPDL only — only this comparison had ≥3 eligible RCTs). MBL pooling attempted but precluded by heterogeneity. GRADE certainty assessed for all four comparisons.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Protocol distribution across included studies (21 cohort groups)

| Protocol | Cohort groups | Implants |
|---|---|---|
| DPIL (delayed placement + immediate loading) | 11 | 502 |
| DPDL (delayed placement + delayed loading) | 7 | 476 |
| DPEL (delayed placement + early loading) | 1 | 107 |
| IPIL (immediate placement + immediate loading) | 1 | 20 |
| IPEL (immediate placement + early loading) | 1 | 67 |

No included studies covered IPDL, EPIL, EPEL, or EPDL.

### Implant survival rates by protocol

| Protocol | Survival rate | Follow-up (mean) | Notable studies |
|---|---|---|---|
| DPIL | 97.2% (weighted cumulative) | 60.1 ± 37.8 months | 5 RCTs, 3 prospective cohorts, 2 case series |
| DPDL | 98.1% (weighted) | Variable (3–15 yr) | 3 RCTs, 1 prospective cohort, 1 retrospective, 1 case series |
| DPEL | 100% | 55 months | 1 cohort (Oxby 2015) |
| IPIL | 90% | 47.6 months | 1 cohort (Siebers 2010) — only protocol below 90% |
| IPEL | 100% | 55 months | 1 cohort (Oxby 2015) |

### Meta-analysis (DPIL vs. DPDL) — 3 RCTs

- **Survival rate**: No significant difference (p = .227); DPIL (74 patients / 188 implants) vs. DPDL (72 patients / 182 implants)
- **GRADE certainty**: VERY LOW for all outcomes across all four GRADE comparisons

### MBL changes (mean radiographic bone loss from loading to follow-up)

| Protocol | Mean MBL change | Implants analyzed |
|---|---|---|
| DPIL | 0.71 ± 0.66 mm | 378 |
| DPDL | 1.68 ± 0.97 mm | reference |
| DPEL | 0.28 ± 0.88 mm | 107 |
| IPIL | 1.57 ± 0.91 mm | 298 (between-study comparison) |
| IPEL | 0.99 ± 1.35 mm | 67 (between-study comparison) |

Note: MBL comparisons are between-study (not pooled meta-analysis); heterogeneity precluded pooling.

### Biological complications

- DPIL: 2.6% rate (peri-implantitis); DPDL: 3.7% rate
- Mucositis: not reported in any included study
- Peri-implant probing depths: DPIL 2.83 ± 0.92 mm; DPDL 3.12 ± 1.08 mm

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

1. **Sparse evidence**: Only 5 of 9 protocol combinations are represented; early placement (EP) protocols are virtually absent from the multiple-unit FDP literature.
2. **Extreme heterogeneity**: Study designs, patient populations, implant systems, and outcome reporting are so heterogeneous that only one head-to-head meta-analysis (DPIL vs. DPDL) was feasible.
3. **GRADE certainty is VERY LOW** for all four comparisons — no conclusion on MBL or biological complications is possible at a level above speculation.
4. **Biological complication underreporting**: Mucositis was not reported in any study despite being a primary outcome; peri-implantitis definition varied widely.
5. **Confounders**: Most studies did not adequately control for smoking, periodontitis history, bone quality, implant surface, or concurrent bone grafting.
6. **Protocol definition ambiguity**: Several studies used non-standard timing language (e.g., ">3 months" without upper bound), requiring reviewer judgment in classification.
7. **Lack of immediate/early placement studies**: The EP-based combinations (EPIL, EPEL, EPDL) are absent from the multiple-unit FDP evidence base.
8. **Future need**: Large, adequately powered, standardized RCTs reporting DPIL vs. DPDL with biological outcomes (MBL, mucositis, peri-implantitis using 2017 World Workshop definitions) for multiple-unit FDPs.

## 6. Related Work

- Gallucci et al. 2018 (Clin Oral Implants Res 29:S16:106–134): Prior SR on placement/loading protocols in partially edentulous patients, finding survival >97% across all protocols — this study restricts to multiple-unit FDPs and attempts meta-analysis
- Benic et al. 2014 (Clin Oral Implants Res): SR+MA on loading protocols for single-unit implant crowns
- Zhou et al. 2021 (Clin Oral Implants Res): SR on placement/loading protocols for single implants
- Schrott et al. 2014 (IJOMI 29:S239–255): Prior SR on loading protocols for extended edentulous sites

## 7. Glossary

- **DPIL**: Delayed placement (>6 months post-extraction) + immediate loading (prosthesis within 1 week of implant placement)
- **DPDL**: Delayed placement + delayed loading (prosthesis >2 months after implant placement)
- **DPEL**: Delayed placement + early loading (prosthesis 1 week–2 months after implant placement)
- **IPIL**: Immediate placement (same day as extraction) + immediate loading
- **IPEL**: Immediate placement + early loading
- **MBL**: Marginal bone level — radiographic measurement of crestal bone relative to implant shoulder
- **FDP**: Fixed dental prosthesis — includes fixed partial dentures (bridges) supported by ≥2 implants
- **GRADE**: Grading of Recommendations, Assessment, Development and Evaluations — framework for assessing certainty of evidence
- **BOP**: Bleeding on probing — clinical indicator of peri-implant inflammation
- **PROSPERO**: International prospective register of systematic reviews
