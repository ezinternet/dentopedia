## Why Ingested

치핑된 도재 보수 시 HF 산처리와 표면 거칠기 처리(다이아몬드 디스크)의 효과, 그리고 접착 방법(유니버설 접착제 vs 별도 실란+접착제)의 역할을 비교한 연구. [[resin-bonding/unknown-2009-effect-of-silane-treatment-timing]]과 함께 실란 처리 방법이 표면처리보다 결합강도에 더 큰 영향을 미친다는 일관된 근거를 제공한다.

## One-line Summary

In vitro study (n=40, 4 groups): for feldspathic porcelain chipping repair, bonding method (separate silane+adhesive > universal adhesive) matters more than surface treatment (HF etching ≈ diamond roughening); universal adhesive groups showed high debonding rates during thermal cycling.

## 한줄요약

인 비트로 연구 (n=40, 4그룹): 장석질 도재 치핑 수리 시 표면처리 방법(HF 산처리 ≈ 다이아몬드 거칠기)보다 접착 방법(별도 실란+접착제 > 유니버설 접착제)이 결합강도에 더 중요하며, 유니버설 접착제 그룹은 열순환 중 높은 탈락률을 보였다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: European Journal of Oral Sciences, 2025; 133:e70014
- **DOI**: 10.1111/eos.70014
- **Study type**: In vitro experimental
- **Institution**: Nordic Institute of Dental Materials (NIOM), Oslo; University of Oslo, Norway
- **Funding**: NIOM; Institute of Clinical Dentistry, University of Oslo

## 2. Key Contributions

- Demonstrated that separate silane primer + adhesive (BISCO Intraoral Repair Kit) significantly outperforms universal adhesive (Scotchbond Universal, 3M) for porcelain repair regardless of surface treatment
- Showed HF acid etching and diamond disc roughening produce equivalent bond strength when the same bonding method is used
- Documented high failure rates during thermal cycling with universal adhesive (7/10 in R-UA, 5/10 in E-UA debonded = 0 MPa bond strength)
- Attributed universal adhesive inferiority to silane monomer instability at suboptimal pH within all-in-one formulations

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Material**: Feldspar porcelain (Initial Zr-FS, GC Europe), 5×5×2 mm specimens embedded in acrylic resin
- **Groups** (n=10 each):
  - R-UA: Roughening (P220 diamond disc) + Scotchbond Universal adhesive (3M)
  - R-SA: Roughening + silane primer + adhesive (BISCO Intraoral Repair Kit)
  - E-UA: HF etching (9.5%, 90 s) + Scotchbond Universal
  - E-SA: HF etching (9.5%, 90 s) + silane primer + adhesive (BISCO)
- **Composite resin**: Filtek Supreme XTE A3 (3M), built up in bonding mold clamp per ISO 29022:2013 (Ø 2.38 mm)
- **Aging**: 37°C distilled water 24h + 5,000 thermal cycles (5–55°C)
- **Test**: Shear bond strength (1 mm/min); fracture mode by light microscopy
- **Statistics**: Shapiro-Wilks normality; Wilcoxon rank-sum test (non-normal data)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- R-UA: 7/10 specimens debonded during thermal cycling (SBS = 0 MPa); very low mean SBS
- E-UA: 5/10 specimens debonded during thermal cycling (SBS = 0 MPa); very low mean SBS
- R-SA vs E-SA: No significant difference (p>0.05) → surface treatment type does not matter when using separate silane+adhesive
- R-UA vs E-UA: No significant difference (p>0.05) → same result with universal adhesive regardless of surface treatment
- R-SA / E-SA significantly higher bond strength than R-UA / E-UA (p<0.05)
- Fracture mode: debonded specimens = adhesive fractures; surviving specimens = mixed/cohesive fractures

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only one type of feldspathic porcelain and composite resin tested
- Universal adhesive tested: only Scotchbond Universal — other universal adhesives may differ in silane content/stability
- n=10 per group; limited statistical power for subgroup analyses
- Intraoral conditions (contamination, humidity) not simulated beyond thermal cycling

## 6. Related Work

- Cites evidence that universal adhesives have unstable silane due to low pH hydrolysis
- BISCO Intraoral Repair Kit (silane primer TMSPM in acetone) used as gold standard repair protocol
- Scotchbond Universal composition: BADGE-DMA, HEMA, DDM, PAMA, DMAEMA, MEK, silane-treated silica, ethanol, water

## 7. Glossary

- **HF**: Hydrofluoric acid (9.5% used here — higher concentration than typical lab 4.9–5%)
- **R-UA / E-UA**: Roughening or Etching + Universal Adhesive
- **R-SA / E-SA**: Roughening or Etching + Separate silane + Adhesive
- **TMSPM**: 3-(Trimethoxysilyl)propyl methacrylate — silane in BISCO primer
- **Chipping**: Cohesive/adhesive fracture of veneering porcelain in bilayered restorations
- **Universal adhesive**: Single-bottle adhesive combining silane, MDP, and other monomers
