## Why Ingested

임플란트 body shape(tapered vs cylinder)·thread depth가 하중기 골 응력에 미치는 영향을 FEA로 본 연구로, [[implants/leblebicioglu-kurtulus-2022-fea-implant-design-bone-density-stress]] 의 design×bone density 응력 결과와 짝을 이루는 FEA 클러스터 구성. tapered body가 응력 peak가 높다는 상반된 신호를 제공해 macrogeometry 선택의 trade-off를 보강.

## One-line Summary

Finite element analysis of the mandible showing crestal cortical bone around the implant neck bears the highest stress, and tapered implant bodies produce higher peak von Mises stress than cylindrical bodies across all bone types.

## 한줄요약

하악 FEA — 임플란트 경부 주위 crestal cortical bone에 최대 응력 집중, tapered body가 cylinder body보다 모든 골종에서 peak von Mises 응력이 높음.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Compares tapered vs cylindrical body and thread depth on mandibular stress.
- Localizes peak stress to crestal cortical bone at the implant neck.
- Flags a tapered-body stress penalty — a counterpoint to taper's primary-stability benefit.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 3D finite element analysis; mandible (anterior and posterior regions).
- Variables: implant body design (tapered vs cylinder), thread depth.
- Outcome: stress distribution, maximum equivalent von Mises stress.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Highest stresses at crestal cortical bone around the implant neck (all models).
- Tapered body > cylindrical body in peak von Mises stress, across all bone types.
- Thread-depth changes modulate the stress pattern.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- FEA assumptions (homogeneous/isotropic, static load); no biological response.
- Mandible-specific; two body shapes.
- No fatigue/cyclic loading.

## 6. Related Work
- Thread-geometry/stress FEA literature; tapered-vs-cylindrical ISQ studies.

## 7. Glossary
- von Mises stress: scalar yield-prediction stress measure.
- Crestal cortical bone: dense bone at the alveolar crest around the implant neck.
