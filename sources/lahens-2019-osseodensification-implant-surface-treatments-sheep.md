## One-line Summary
Sheep ilium study (12 animals, 72 implants — 36 acid-etched, 36 machined) showing OD CCW/CW drilling raises insertion torque and BIC vs regular drilling (BIC P=0.024 CW, P=0.006 CCW), with CCW-OD eliminating the surface-treatment effect on BIC/BAFO.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Animal in vivo study (ovine ilium model)
- **Journal**: Journal of Biomedical Materials Research Part B: Applied Biomaterials, 107(3):615-623, 2019 (online 06 Aug 2018)
- **DOI**: 10.1002/jbm.b.34154
- **Sample**: 12 sheep, 6 implants per animal placed bilaterally in ilium (72 implants total: 36 acid-etched, 36 as-machined; 3 implants of each surface per animal × 3 drilling techniques)
- **Primary Question**: Does OD drilling differentially affect osseointegration of machined vs acid-etched implants in low-density bone (ovine ilium)?

## 2. Key Contributions
- Three-arm drilling factor: regular subtractive (R), OD clockwise (CW), OD counterclockwise (CCW).
- Insertion torque significantly higher with both OD-CCW and OD-CW vs R (P < 0.001).
- BIC% significantly higher with CW (P = 0.024) and CCW (P = 0.006) vs R.
- For CCW-OD, no statistical difference in BIC or BAFO between acid-etched and machined surfaces at either 3-wk or 12-wk time point — i.e., OD "rescues" the machined surface.
- At 3 weeks, preosteoblasts and resorbing/forming bone visible at the interface; at 12 weeks, new bone formation extends into the trabecular region in all groups.
- Important conclusion: in low-density bone, OD drilling provides higher stability and no osseointegration impairment regardless of surface treatment.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Twelve adult sheep received 6 implants each, placed bilaterally in the iliac crest — a recognized low-density-bone model. Within each animal, three implants were acid-etched and three were as-machined; one of each surface was placed via each of three techniques:
1. Regular subtractive (R): 2.0 mm pilot → 3.2 mm → 3.8 mm twist drills.
2. OD clockwise (CW): Densah Bur 2.0 mm pilot → 2.8 mm → 3.8 mm multifluted tapered.
3. OD counterclockwise (CCW): same Densah burs run in reverse.

Outcomes: insertion torque (peri-operative), histologic and histomorphometric analysis at 3 and 12 weeks (BIC%, BAFO%, qualitative cellular characterization).

The factorial design (3 drilling × 2 surfaces × 2 time points) is unusual in OD literature, which usually tests drilling alone. By varying surface, the authors directly test whether OD's benefit depends on surface treatment — and find it does not.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Insertion torque: OD-CCW > OD-CW > R; CCW/CW vs R both P < 0.001.
- BIC%: CW vs R P = 0.024; CCW vs R P = 0.006.
- CCW-OD acid-etched vs CCW-OD machined: no statistical difference in BIC or BAFO at either time point.
- 3-week histology: resorbing bone + bone-forming precursors (preosteoblasts) at the interface — consistent with active early remodeling.
- 12-week histology: new bone formation extending into trabecular region across all groups.
- Exact effect sizes (mean BIC%, BAFO%) and CIs are not retrievable from the available abstract text.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Ovine ilium model — well-established low-density-bone analog, but not directly equivalent to atrophic maxillary posterior (D3/D4) clinical scenarios.
- The "OD rescues machined surface" finding is biologically interesting but counterintuitive — independent replication needed, especially given that machined implants have largely been retired clinically (replaced by SLA, SLActive, etc.).
- Author group overlap with prior OD studies (Neiva, Bonfante, Jimbo, Coelho et al.) — independent groups should replicate.
- 3 and 12 weeks are early time points; no late remodeling or removal-torque data.
- Exact P-values for insertion torque are not given as numeric effect estimates.

## 7. Glossary
- **Acid-etched surface**: Implant surface treated with strong acid to create micro-roughness; precursor to SLA.
- **Machined surface (as-machined)**: Bare titanium surface from CNC machining without further treatment; historically associated with slower osseointegration.
- **Ilium (iliac crest)**: Pelvic bone with low-density trabecular structure; standard ovine model for D3/D4 bone in humans.
- **OD-CW / OD-CCW**: Densah burs run clockwise (cutting, similar to conventional drilling but with bur geometry that still compacts) vs counterclockwise (intended densification mode).
- **Preosteoblast**: Bone-precursor cell visible early in osteointegration histology.
