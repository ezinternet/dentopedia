## One-line Summary

Korean single-center retrospective cohort (NHIS Ilsan Hospital, 2015–2021; n=293 patients, 414 teeth) — continuing DOAC therapy during tooth extraction did not significantly increase post-extraction bleeding vs discontinuing (12/246 = 4.9% continued vs 0/47 discontinued, P=0.122).

## 한줄요약

한국 단일기관 후향 코호트(국민건강보험 일산병원, 환자 293명·치아 414개) — 발치 시 직접경구항응고제(DOAC) 유지군과 중단군 간 발치 후 출혈에 유의한 차이가 없어(유지군 4.9% vs 중단군 0%, P=0.122), DOAC 중단 없이 발치 가능함을 시사.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First Korean cohort quantifying post-extraction bleeding risk on DOACs (dabigatran, rivaroxaban, apixaban, edoxaban), with explicit comparison of a continue-DOAC group vs a discontinue-DOAC group.
- Provides real-world (NHIS Ilsan Hospital) evidence that continuing DOAC therapy during extraction does not raise bleeding incidence, addressing a domestic evidence gap on short-term DOAC discontinuation.
- Per-drug bleeding incidence breakdown and analysis by extraction technique (simple vs surgical/osteotomy) and arch (maxillary vs mandibular).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Setting/period**: NHIS Ilsan Hospital, dental extractions 2015–2021, patients with accurate DOAC prescription records.
- **N**: 293 patients (133 M / 160 F), 414 teeth extracted (231 maxillary, 183 mandibular).
- **Groups**: 246 patients extracted while continuing DOAC vs 47 patients extracted after discontinuing DOAC (discontinuation only on prescriber recommendation after consultation).
- **DOACs**: dabigatran (Pradaxa, direct thrombin/IIa inhibitor), rivaroxaban (Xarelto), apixaban (Eliquis), edoxaban (Lixiana) — last three are factor Xa inhibitors.
- **Exclusions**: extraction before DOAC start, no extraction needed, inadequate drug/dose data, pregnancy, age <19, mandibular radiation/maxillofacial tumor surgery history, osteonecrosis/osteomyelitis of jaws.
- **Bleeding definition**: active bleeding or blood seepage judged by an oral surgeon, controlled by suturing, direct pressure, or biting. PT/INR not routinely measured; recent INR used if available from other departments.
- **Hemostasis/care**: pre-extraction antibiotic prophylaxis (cefadroxil 1,000 mg / clindamycin 300 mg / amoxicillin 1,000 mg); 2% lidocaine w/ 1:80,000 epinephrine; 3-0 braided silk sutures; gauze pressure; absorbable gelatin sponge for persistent bleeding. No socket grafting.
- **Stats**: chi-square test comparing the two groups; per-drug bleeding frequency.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Overall bleeding**: 12/293 patients (the abstract states 6.9%; body text computes 4.1% of 293 and 4.9% of the 246 continued; 12/414 = 2.9% of sites). Bleeding occurred at 12 extraction sites.
- **Continue vs discontinue**: 12/246 (4.9%) in continued-DOAC group vs 0/47 in discontinued group — no significant difference (**P=0.122**).
- **INR**: 1.15±0.50 (continued) vs 1.13±0.55 (discontinued), P=0.866; no correlation between INR and bleeding (DOACs poorly reflected by INR).
- **Age**: 77.4±8.9 (continued) vs 74.5±10.6 (discontinued) years, P=0.087.
- **Per drug** (continued group): dabigatran 3/75, rivaroxaban 3/53, apixaban 7/216, edoxaban 0 — differences not significant.
- **By technique**: 5/87 osteotomy/surgical extractions vs 7/327 simple extractions bled.
- **By arch**: 4 maxillary vs 8 mandibular bleeding sites (mandible > maxilla pattern, consistent with warfarin literature).
- **Conclusion**: continuing DOAC therapy during extraction does not increase post-extraction bleeding tendency; local hemostatic management is key.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-center, retrospective; unequal group sizes (246 vs 47) with no events in the small discontinued group, limiting the P=0.122 comparison.
- Inconsistent denominators/percentages between abstract (6.9%) and body (4.1%/4.9%); per-DOAC subgroups small (esp. edoxaban) and underpowered.
- No standardized PT/INR or anti-Xa monitoring; bleeding severity not graded (e.g., HAS-BLED, grade scale).
- Authors call for larger multicenter studies across the full DOAC range.

## 6. Related Work

- Müller et al. — delayed bleeding higher in DOAC/phenprocoumon vs no-anticoagulant.
- Hua et al. (meta-analysis) — DOAC bleeding risk lower than VKAs.
- Hiroshi/Yoshikawa/Yagyuu et al. — DOAC vs warfarin post-extraction bleeding comparable.
- Berton et al. (prospective) — single tooth extraction without discontinuing DOAC appropriate.
- Ockerman et al. (RCT) — 10% tranexamic acid (TXA) mouthwash reduces delayed bleeding.
- Iwata et al. — most post-extraction bleeding grade 1, controllable by gauze/pressure.

## 7. Glossary

- **DOAC (Direct Oral Anticoagulant)**: anticoagulants directly inhibiting a coagulation factor — dabigatran (thrombin/IIa), rivaroxaban/apixaban/edoxaban (factor Xa).
- **VKA (Vitamin K Antagonist)**: e.g., warfarin; indirectly inhibits multiple vitamin K-dependent factors; monitored by INR.
- **INR (International Normalized Ratio)**: standardized PT measure; reliable for warfarin, NOT a reliable monitor for DOACs.
- **Anti-factor Xa assay / thrombin time / ecarin clotting time**: specific DOAC activity tests, used selectively (e.g., emergencies), not for routine monitoring.
- **TXA (Tranexamic Acid)**: antifibrinolytic; 10% mouthwash studied for reducing delayed post-extraction bleeding.
- **HAS-BLED**: bleeding-risk score used in anticoagulated patients.
