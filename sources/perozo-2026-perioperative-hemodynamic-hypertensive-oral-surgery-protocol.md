## Why Ingested

Wiki had vasoconstrictor-in-LA risk only for pregnancy ([[drug/systemic-disease/uritu-2025-vasoconstrictor-dental-anesthesia-pregnancy-sr]]); the controlled-hypertensive perioperative pathway (BP-based risk tiers, epinephrine cap, NSAID-avoidance, chairside emergency drugs) was undocumented. This pilot cohort (n=39) supplies effect sizes — hemorrhage 66.7% vs 9.5%, baseline-HTN eta-squared=0.33 — and a packaged 3-phase chairside protocol, extending the medically-compromised-patient management thread.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective analysis of prospectively collected observational data (n=39: 21 normotensive vs 18 treated hypertensive) showing baseline hypertensive status is the dominant perioperative hemodynamic determinant (eta-squared=0.33) and a 7-fold higher hemorrhagic-complication rate (66.7% vs 9.5%, p=0.008) during oral surgery; introduces the unvalidated 3-phase Perozo perioperative BP-management protocol.

## 한줄요약

전향적으로 수집된 관찰자료의 후향분석(n=39: 정상혈압 21 vs 치료중 고혈압 18)으로, 기저 고혈압 상태가 구강수술 중 혈역학 변동의 최대 결정인자(eta제곱=0.33)이고 출혈합병증이 7배 높았음(66.7% vs 9.5%, p=0.008)을 보였으며, 미검증 3단계 Perozo 수술전후 혈압관리 프로토콜을 제안한다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Retrospective secondary analysis of prospectively collected, de-identified observational clinical data (pilot cohort); also proposes a protocol. Not a clinical trial (no research-assigned interventions; ICMJE non-trial).
- **Journal**: Cureus 2026;18(4):e107163.
- **Setting**: Two public hospitals, Maracaibo, Venezuela (Hospital Universitario de Maracaibo, Hospital Central de Maracaibo). IRB-classified non-human-subjects (de-identified data).
- **DOI**: [10.7759/cureus.107163](https://doi.org/10.7759/cureus.107163) · PMID 42147593 · PMCID PMC13179726.
- **Source**: PubMed / PMC full text.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Quantifies baseline hypertensive status as the **primary** perioperative hemodynamic determinant during oral surgery (eta-squared = 0.33), above anesthetic, timing, and anxiety.
2. Documents a large excess hemorrhagic-complication risk in controlled hypertensives (66.7% vs 9.5%; Cramer's V = 0.55; p = 0.008).
3. Packages findings + AHA-ADA / WHO-ISH principles into the **Perozo protocol** — a structured 3-phase, 20-step chairside perioperative BP-management framework.
4. Differential anxiety physiology: anxiety drives **SBP** rise in hypertensives but **HR** rise in normotensives (challenging the unidimensional-stressor view).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Population**: 39 adults; normotensive n=21, treated/controlled primary hypertensive n=18. Excluded: uncontrolled HTN (SBP ≥180 / DBP ≥110 at baseline), acute CV event within 3 months, pregnancy, anesthetic contraindications.
- **Hemodynamic monitoring**: automated digital sphygmomanometers; SBP, DBP, MAP, HR at four time points — (1) preoperative baseline, (2) during LA administration, (3) active intraoperative phase, (4) immediate postoperative (still in chair).
- **Anxiety**: State-Trait Anxiety Inventory (STAI), licensed; thresholds low ≤37, moderate 38–44, high ≥45.
- **Anesthetics (observed, not randomized)**: 2% lidocaine + 1:100,000 epinephrine, or 3% plain mepivacaine; surgeon-selected per cardiovascular risk per AHA-ADA.
- **Timing**: morning (08:00–12:00) vs afternoon (13:00–17:00).
- **Statistics**: repeated-measures ANOVA, Pearson Chi-squared (Cramer's V), Pearson correlation; α = 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Variable | Normotensive (n=21) | Hypertensive (n=18) | Test | Stat | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Preop SBP (mmHg) | 128 ± 9 | 159 ± 12 | RM-ANOVA | F=8.41 | 0.006 |
| Intraop SBP (mmHg) | 136 ± 10 | 170 ± 15 | RM-ANOVA | F=9.02 | 0.004 |
| Intraop DBP (mmHg) | 82 ± 7 | 97 ± 9 | RM-ANOVA | F=6.75 | 0.013 |
| Peak HR (bpm) | 86 ± 8 | 85 ± 9 | RM-ANOVA | F=0.14 | 0.71 (NS) |
| Hemorrhagic complications | 9.5% | 66.7% | Chi-squared | 7.02 | 0.008 |
| Anxiety vs SBP change | — | — | Pearson r | r=0.58 | 0.01 |

- **Determinant**: baseline BP status eta-squared = 0.33 (dominant).
- **Anesthetic**: lidocaine+epinephrine > plain mepivacaine for BP/HR rise, mainly in hypertensives; well tolerated in normotensives.
- **Circadian timing**: morning peak SBP HTN >250 vs NT 196 mmHg (+34%); afternoon HTN 187 vs NT 168 mmHg (+11%). Larger morning→afternoon fall in hypertensives.
- **Anxiety gradient** — HTN (ΔSBP): low 1.5±2.0 → moderate 14.7±2.5 → high 24.9±2.8 mmHg. NT (ΔHR): low 1.0±0.6 → moderate 6.5±1.0 → high 9.6±1.5 bpm.

**Perozo protocol (3 phases):**
- **Phase I — Preop risk stratification**: Low (SBP<140, DBP<90) → routine; Moderate (140–159 / 90–99) → minor procedures, reduce anxiety, monitor BP; High (160–179 / 100–109) → short procedures, strict BP monitoring; Very high (≥180 / ≥110) → defer + urgent medical consult.
- **Phase II — Intraop / emergency meds**: captopril 25 mg SL (hypertensive crisis); nifedipine bitten capsule (acute surge); nitroglycerin SL (coronary symptoms); clonidine (refractory HTN); epinephrine capped at 0.04 mg (2 cartridges of 1:100,000) in controlled hypertensives.
- **Phase III — Postop**: vital-sign monitoring in recovery; **avoid NSAIDs**; antibiotics if indicated; written discharge instructions; warning-sign education; structured follow-up.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small pilot (n=39) — limited power, no causal inference, no robust multivariable modeling.
- Single-country, two-center Venezuelan public-hospital design — limited generalizability (US has more resistant HTN, polypharmacy, metabolic comorbidity).
- Retrospective-on-prospective design → unmeasured confounders (antihypertensive class, HTN duration, target-organ damage, baseline trait anxiety).
- **Protocol is unvalidated / hypothesis-generating** — multicenter prospective validation required before clinical adoption.

## 6. Related Work

- Muzyka & Glick — elevated perioperative CV risk in hypertensive dental patients.
- Bader et al. — systematic review of acute hemodynamic responses to vasoconstrictor-containing LA.
- AHA-ADA two-cartridge epinephrine limit; WHO-ISH BP thresholds.

## 7. Glossary

- **AH / HTN**: arterial hypertension. **NT**: normotensive.
- **SBP / DBP / MAP / HR**: systolic / diastolic blood pressure, mean arterial pressure, heart rate.
- **STAI**: State-Trait Anxiety Inventory.
- **eta-squared (η²)**: ANOVA effect size (proportion of variance explained).
- **Cramer's V**: effect size for chi-squared association.
- **AHA-ADA / WHO-ISH**: American Heart Association–American Dental Association / World Health Organization–International Society of Hypertension guideline bodies.
