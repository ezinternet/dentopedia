## Why Ingested

Earliest direct histomorphometric comparison of DBBM vs BCP (60%HA/40%β-TCP) with autologous platelet lysate (APL) in standardized rabbit femoral critical-size defects. Provides the pre-PRF-era baseline showing that BCP is significantly more osteoconductive and resorbable than DBBM, while platelet lysate adds no measurable benefit to either. Relevant context for evaluating growth factor adjuncts in bone grafting and understanding the BCP/DBBM performance divergence referenced in [[bone-regeneration/fujioka-kobayashi-2022-synthetic-biomaterials-dbbm-augmentation]].

## One-line Summary

Rabbit femoral critical-size defect RCT (n=12, 6 weeks): BCP produced 70% more new bone and 60% less residual material than DBBM; adding autologous platelet lysate (APL) to either material provided no additional benefit.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Bone Formation with Deproteinized Bovine Bone Mineral or Biphasic Calcium Phosphate in the Presence of Autologous Platelet Lysate: Comparative Investigation in Rabbit
- **Authors**: Carole Chakar, Nada Naaman, Emmanuel Soffer, Nicolas Cohen, Nada El Osta, Hervé Petite, Fani Anagnostou
- **Year**: 2014
- **Journal**: International Journal of Biomaterials
- **DOI**: 10.1155/2014/367265
- **Study type**: Animal (randomized, rabbit femoral condyle critical-size defect model)
- **Institution**: Saint-Joseph University Beirut; Université Paris 7 Diderot / UMRCNRS 7052; Pitié-Salpêtrière Hospital, AP-HP, Paris, France

## 2. Key Contributions

- Established that BCP (60% HA / 40% β-TCP) is significantly more osteoconductive than DBBM in standardized critical-size defects at 6 weeks.
- New bone area in BCP defects was **70% greater** (p<0.001) than in DBBM defects.
- Residual material area in BCP defects was **60% lower** (p<0.001) than in DBBM defects — confirming BCP's faster resorption.
- Autologous platelet lysate (APL), prepared by freeze-thaw cycles, added NO additional bone formation benefit when combined with either DBBM or BCP.
- Provided early evidence that the DBBM vs BCP performance gap is intrinsic to material properties, not growth factor context.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Animals**: 12 male New Zealand rabbits (4 months, mean 3.5 kg)
- **Design**: 2-group randomized (DBBM vs BCP), each group n=6; split-femur (bilateral defects per animal)
  - Group 1 (n=6): DBBM vs DBBM + APL (bilateral femoral condyle)
  - Group 2 (n=6): BCP vs BCP + APL (bilateral femoral condyle)
- **Defect**: Circular critical-size defect in femoral condyle (standardized size)
- **Graft materials**:
  - DBBM: Bio-Oss (Geistlich), 250–1000 µm particles, 75–80% porosity
  - BCP: BoneCeramic (Straumann), 500–1000 µm particles, 60%HA/40%β-TCP, 90% porosity
- **APL preparation**: Rabbit blood → platelet-rich plasma → platelet pellet → freeze-thaw cycles (5×) → centrifuge → platelet lysate at ~10⁶ platelets/µL
- **Healing period**: 6 weeks
- **Assessment**: Non-demineralized histology + histomorphometry (new bone %, residual material %)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Group | New Bone Area | Residual Material Area |
|---|---|---|
| DBBM alone | Reference (lower) | Reference (higher) |
| DBBM + APL | Similar to DBBM alone | Similar to DBBM alone |
| BCP alone | +70% vs DBBM (p<0.001) | −60% vs DBBM (p<0.001) |
| BCP + APL | Similar to BCP alone | Similar to BCP alone |

- New bone was observed **around particles** of all tested materials, confirming osteoconductive scaffold properties for both
- APL in vitro: enhanced proliferation of foetal rat calvaria cells by 100–200% at tested concentrations — biological activity confirmed; in vivo effect not translated
- The failure of APL to show in vivo benefit may reflect inadequate growth factor concentration at the defect site, rapid diffusion, or material-platelet interaction masking effects

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 6-week healing time; DBBM's scaffold function may reach parity at longer time points
- Critical-size femoral condyle defect: not an oral/dental site; different vascularity and regeneration biology
- APL biological activity confirmed in vitro but not calibrated for in vivo adequacy
- No control (empty defect) group included to calibrate spontaneous healing baseline
- Small sample size (n=6/group, 12 animals total)

## 6. Related Work

- Fujioka-Kobayashi et al. (2022): α-TCP/BBCP + DBBM hybrid also showed BCP-based synthetics outperform DBBM alone in new bone formation — consistent with this study's BCP vs DBBM finding
- PRP/PRF clinical evidence in GBR and sinus lift: inconsistent results align with this study's APL finding of no additional benefit
- DBBM slow-resorption paradox: the same slow resorption that maintains ridge volume may limit early osteoconduction (theme across multiple studies)
- BCP (60/40) vs DBBM clinical studies: conflicting results noted by paper's authors — this study provides standardized defect data to help resolve the conflict

## 7. Glossary

- **BCP (Biphasic Calcium Phosphate)**: 이상성 인산칼슘 — synthetic bone substitute; 60%HA/40%β-TCP ratio provides osteoconductive scaffold with controllable resorption
- **β-TCP (beta-tricalcium phosphate)**: β-삼인산칼슘 — resorbable component of BCP; faster resorption than HA
- **DBBM**: 탈단백 우골 — deproteinized bovine bone mineral; slowly resorbed xenograft
- **APL (Autologous Platelet Lysate)**: 자가혈소판용해물 — platelet concentrate prepared by freeze-thaw; releases growth factors (PDGF, TGF-β, IGF)
- **Critical-size defect**: 임계크기 결손 — bone defect too large to heal spontaneously
- **Histomorphometry**: 조직형태계측 — quantitative measurement of tissue components in histological sections
- **PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma)**: 혈소판풍부혈장 — platelet concentrate; different from platelet lysate (no freeze-thaw activation)
- **Osteoconduction**: 골전도 — passive scaffolding allowing bone ingrowth along material surface (no induction or recruitment capacity)
