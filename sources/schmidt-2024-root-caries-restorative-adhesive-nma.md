## One-line Summary
NMA of 9 RCTs (1,263 root caries lesions, 473 patients) finds no statistically significant winner among restorative strategies for root caries; weak trend favoring 2-3-step etch-and-rinse + composite or RMGIC over conventional GIC at 24 months. CINeMA confidence: low.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First NMA of root caries restoration approaches comparing all combinations of adhesives (1-SE, 2-SE, 2-ER, 3-ER) × restorative materials (CC, GIC, RMGIC)
- Demonstrates the evidence vacuum: clinically meaningful hierarchy cannot be established from current literature
- Identifies critical research need: standardized, well-designed RCTs for root caries restoration
- Includes special populations: nursing home residents (1 trial) and head-and-neck radiotherapy patients (2 trials)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: SR + network meta-analysis (NMA)
- **Inclusion**: RCTs with ≥2 restorative strategies for cavitated root caries lesions in adults
- **Primary outcome**: Survival rate of restorations at 6/12/24 months
- **Statistics**: Random-effects NMA stratified by follow-up; I-statistics for variance ratio
- **Material/adhesive combinations**: 1-SE, 2-3ER, with CC, GIC, RMGIC
- **Confidence**: CINeMA assessment of NMA

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- 547 studies screened → 9 eligible for NMA
- Population: 473 patients, 1,263 root caries lesions
- 24-month survival (NMA point estimates):
  - 2-3ER/CC vs GIC: OR 2.65 (95% CI 1.45–4.84) — tendency to favor composite
  - RMGIC vs GIC: OR 2.05 (95% CI 1.17–3.61)
- Caveat: findings not statistically significant after NMA confidence adjustment; CINeMA confidence low

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Only 9 trials eligible — sparse network
- High clinical heterogeneity (patient populations: healthy / nursing home / post-radiotherapy)
- Survival rate outcomes vary in definition across studies
- Working only with the ScienceDirect export PDF (abstract + intro); full Methods/Discussion not in PDF — need to retrieve full text for deeper extraction
- Need standardized RCTs with consistent outcome reporting, follow-up beyond 24 months, and stratification by patient risk

## 7. Glossary
- **Root caries lesion (치근우식)**: Caries on exposed root surface (cementum/dentin); growing in aging populations with gingival recession
- **NMA (Network Meta-Analysis, 네트워크 메타분석)**: Compares multiple interventions simultaneously via direct and indirect comparisons
- **CINeMA**: Confidence In Network Meta-Analysis — GRADE-derived framework for NMA confidence
- **CC**: Conventional Composite (resin composite)
- **GIC / RMGIC**: Glass Ionomer Cement / Resin-Modified GIC
- **1-SE / 2-SE / 2-ER / 3-ER**: 1- or 2-step Self-Etch / 2- or 3-step Etch-and-Rinse adhesive
- **RoB-2**: Cochrane Risk of Bias tool v2
