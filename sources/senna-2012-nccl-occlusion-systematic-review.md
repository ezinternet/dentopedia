## One-line Summary
Systematic review (3 prospective + 25 cross-sectional studies) concluding that extreme heterogeneity in design and diagnostic criteria, plus substantial bias, masks any conclusion about occlusion's role in NCCL aetiology (no meta-analysis possible).

## 2. Key Contributions
- First SR specifically on the NCCL-occlusion association from clinical studies.
- Documents that heterogeneity in classification/diagnostic criteria and high bias (non-blinded examiners) prevent any firm conclusion.
- Meta-analysis not possible due to design heterogeneity.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- SR of clinical studies (MEDLINE, 286 retrieved → 28 included: 3 prospective, 25 cross-sectional). No meta-analysis. No GRADE reported in abstract.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Wide reported NCCL prevalence range reflecting non-standardized criteria.
- Evaluation bias (non-blinded examiners) and heterogeneous independent variables identified as masking factors.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Abstract-only ingest; primary-study heterogeneity precludes pooling. Calls for standardized diagnostic criteria and blinded prospective designs.

## 6. Related Work
- Silva 2013 (parallel SR, same period), Duangthip 2017 (abfraction SR), Grippo 2012.

## 7. Glossary
- NCCL: noncarious cervical lesion. Cross-sectional study: prevalence/association snapshot, no temporality.
