## One-line Summary
J Clin Periodontol 2025;52:1584-1594: 4-arm RCT (n=22/group), posterior sites, CBCT 6mo — TM+XG and MFGG+XG: superior vertical preservation; all groups: significant horizontal reduction; USH: 67% sinus elevation, 71% short implants; MFGG = more pain.

## 1. Key Results
- 4-arm RCT; posterior region only (not anterior); n=22 per group
- Groups: MFGG, MFGG+XG, TM+XG (titanium membrane + xenograft), USH (unassisted socket healing)
- CBCT immediately post-op and at 6 months
- Vertical: TM+XG and MFGG+XG significantly better than USH and MFGG alone (p<0.05)
- Horizontal: all groups showed significant reduction (p<0.05) — no group spared
- USH consequences: 67% sinus floor elevation rate (maxillary), 71% required short implants (<8.5mm)
- Pain: MFGG groups significantly more postoperative pain vs USH (p<0.05)
- UNICAMP (University of Campinas) / University at Buffalo

## 2. Clinical Implications
- Posterior ridge resorption: bone grafting reduces need for additional surgery
- Titanium membrane + xenograft = gold standard for vertical preservation in posterior
- MFGG alone (no graft): no better than USH for vertical, but more morbidity
- Horizontal loss is inevitable even with ARP — clinicians must counsel patients accordingly
- USH in posterior maxilla: high rate of sinus problems and need for short implants
