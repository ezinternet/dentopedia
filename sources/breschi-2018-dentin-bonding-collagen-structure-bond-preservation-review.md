Narrative review (abstract-only) framing resin-dentin durability: the hybrid layer degrades via endogenous MMP/cysteine-cathepsin collagenolysis and resin leaching — both enabled by residual unbound water — and is preserved by removing that water and silencing the enzymes.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Frames decades of effort toward durable resin-dentin bonds around three pillars: (1) understanding the mechanisms of hybrid layer (HL) degradation, (2) manufacturing improved adhesive systems, and (3) developing strategies for HL preservation.
- Identifies **two main HL-degradation mechanisms**: enzymatic degradation of collagen fibrils, and leaching of resin from the HL.
- Pinpoints **residual unbound water** trapped between denuded collagen fibrils at the bottom of the HL as the common enabler of both mechanisms.
- Names the **endogenous dentinal enzymes** responsible: matrix metalloproteinases (MMPs) and cysteine cathepsins, activated to degrade the denuded collagen matrix.
- Organizes **preservation strategies** into two conceptual families: removal of unbound water from the inter-fibrillar gaps, and silencing of endogenous enzymatic activity.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

*Abstract-only — quantitative benchmarks not retrieved.* The qualitative results reported:

- HL degradation proceeds through **two mechanisms**: (1) enzymatic collagen-fibril degradation, (2) resin leaching from the HL.
- Both are **enabled by residual unbound water** between denuded collagen fibrils trapped at the bottom of the HL.
- **Endogenous MMPs and cysteine cathepsins** are activated and degrade the denuded collagen matrix.
- **Preservation strategies** entail (a) removal of unbound water from inter-fibrillar gaps and (b) silencing endogenous enzymatic activity by various modes.
- The authors conclude that, despite remaining hurdles, **impressive progress** has been achieved, with the large research volume reflecting the field's importance and the push toward greater quality and longevity of resin-dentin bonds.

## 7. Glossary

- **Hybrid layer (HL)**: the resin-infiltrated demineralized dentin collagen zone that forms the resin-dentin bond.
- **Denuded collagen fibrils**: collagen left demineralized but not fully resin-infiltrated, exposed to water and enzymes.
- **Unbound water**: residual water trapped between collagen fibrils that enables both enzymatic degradation and resin leaching.
- **MMPs (matrix metalloproteinases)**: endogenous dentinal collagenolytic enzymes activated within the HL.
- **Cysteine cathepsins**: endogenous proteolytic enzymes that, with MMPs, degrade the denuded collagen matrix.
- **Resin leaching**: loss of polymer from the HL over time, a non-enzymatic degradation mechanism.
- **Enzyme silencing**: HL-preservation strategy of inhibiting endogenous MMP/cathepsin activity (e.g., MMP inhibitors).
