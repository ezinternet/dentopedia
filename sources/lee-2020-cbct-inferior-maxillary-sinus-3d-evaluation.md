## One-line Summary
Retrospective 3-D CBCT analysis of 60 healthy Korean adults (30 M / 30 F, 20s, no missing teeth, no asymmetry, no systemic disease) using Mimics 22.0 to evaluate the inferior part of the maxillary sinus relative to the nasal cavity floor (NCF). Inferior sinus dimensions (height NCF-F, width CW/SW, volume V) are significantly larger in males than females; bizygomatic breadth correlates with sinus dimensions.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Scientific Reports 2020;10:21012
- **Institution**: Dan-Kook University Dental College, Cheonan, South Korea (Department of Oral Anatomy; Department of Dentomaxillofacial Radiology)
- **Study type**: Retrospective cross-sectional CBCT-based anatomical study with 3-D reconstruction
- **Ethics**: Dankook University Dental Hospital IRB DUDH IRB 2015-12-022 (waiver of consent for retrospective imaging)

## 2. Key Contributions
- First study to fully **3-D visualize and quantify** the inferior part of the maxillary sinus (below NCF reference plane) using Mimics segmentation rather than 2-D CBCT slices.
- Establishes sex-based reference values for inferior sinus height, coronal width, sagittal width, and volume in young healthy Korean adults.
- Demonstrates that **bizygomatic breadth (facial width)** correlates with inferior sinus dimensions — clinically useful proxy for sinus size when CBCT unavailable.
- Adds palatal-nasal recess (PNR) angle measurements.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 60 young adult subjects (orthodontic patients, malocclusion class I/II/III, in their 20s).
- Sample size calculated by G-Power 3.1.
- CBCT: Alphard 3030, 0.39 mm slice, 512×512 matrix.
- 3-D reconstruction in Mimics 22.0 (Materialise).
- NCF (nasal cavity floor) used as reference plane separating "inferior sinus" from upper sinus.
- Parameters measured:
  - **NCF-F**: height from sinus floor to NCF
  - **CW**: coronal-view width of inferior sinus
  - **SW**: sagittal-view width of inferior sinus
  - **V**: volume between sinus floor and NCF
  - **PNR angle**: palatal-nasal recess angle
  - **Bizygomatic breadth** as facial width covariate
- Statistics: Mann-Whitney U test, linear regression (SPSS 23.0), α=0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **NCF-F (male)**: left 15.06 mm / right 13.30 mm.
- **NCF-F (female)**: left 10.59 mm / right 11.19 mm.
- **CW (male)**: ~24.5 mm; (female) ~20.5 mm.
- **SW (male)**: ~32.5 mm; (female) ~26.1 mm.
- **V (male) > V (female)** significantly (both sides).
- All inferior sinus dimensions significantly larger in males (p<0.05).
- Bizygomatic breadth ≥127 mm group showed larger sinus dimensions; linear regression confirmed correlation.
- 3-D measurements gave more accurate volumes than 2-D cross-sections (which underestimate).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small sample (n=60); young adults only (20s).
- Single ethnic group (Korean).
- Orthodontic patients with no missing teeth — does not capture edentulous/pneumatized post-extraction sinuses.
- Cross-sectional; no longitudinal pneumatization data.
- Authors call for larger multi-ethnic 3-D studies.

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary
- **NCF (Nasal Cavity Floor)**: horizontal reference plane through the nasal cavity floor used to define "inferior" sinus volume.
- **PNR (Palatal-Nasal Recess)**: angle between palatal bone and nasal wall at the medial sinus floor.
- **Bizygomatic breadth**: maximum linear distance between zygions; proxy for facial width.
- **Mimics**: Materialise medical image segmentation/3-D reconstruction software.
- **CW/SW**: coronal-view width / sagittal-view width of the inferior sinus compartment.
