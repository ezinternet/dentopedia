## Why Ingested

Existing zirconia/LiDi crown survival pages [[dental-materials/zirconia/leitao-2022-monolithic-zirconia-single-crown-survival-sr]] and [[dental-materials/zirconia/aswal-2023-cadcam-zirconia-lithium-disilicate-crowns-sr-ma]] report pooled survival but do not isolate the occlusal-overload risk modifier of sleep bruxism with an objective EMG diagnosis. This 3-year RCT (Bömicke 2026) directly tests whether SB degrades survival/success of monolithic LiDi vs Zir molar crowns, contextualizing the occlusal-overload literature ([[occlusion/fan-2018-occlusal-trauma-and-excessive-occlusal]]) for material selection in bruxers.

## One-line Summary

3-year RCT (n=109, 4 groups by EMG-confirmed sleep bruxism × randomized monolithic LiDi vs zirconia molar crown, no occlusal guards): sleep bruxism showed no detectable impact on technical complications, survival, or success of either material.

## 한줄요약

3년 RCT (환자 109명, EMG로 확인한 수면 이갈이 유무 × 무작위 배정한 모놀리식 리튬디실리케이트 vs 지르코니아 대구치 단일 크라운, 교합안정장치 미사용): 수면 이갈이는 두 재료 모두에서 기술적 합병증·생존율·성공률에 유의한 영향을 주지 않았다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First RCT to test whether definitively diagnosed (EMG-confirmed) sleep bruxism affects monolithic all-ceramic molar single-crown outcomes, using a three-pronged SB diagnosis (self-report + clinical examination + portable BruxOff EMG/ECG device).
- Direct 2-material comparison (LiDi vs Zir), stratified by SB status → four groups over 3 years, with protective occlusal guards deliberately withheld to isolate bruxism effect.
- Zero technical complications (no ceramic fractures, no chipping) in any of the four groups confirms high fracture resistance of monolithic LiDi and Zir molar crowns even in unmitigated bruxers.
- Demonstrates that prior studies excluding bruxers likely do not misrepresent the materials' clinical performance, at least for a 3-year horizon.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Prospective, 2-arm, single-center RCT (parallel 1:1 randomization). Block randomization stratified by SB status; allocation concealed in sequentially numbered sealed envelopes. Participants blinded to crown material.
- **Screening period**: 2015–2019; treatments performed 2015–2020 at Heidelberg University Prosthodontics.
- **Participants**: Adults ≥18 years requiring a single molar crown (first or second molar); natural antagonists required; full periodontal stability and adequate endodontic status required. Exclusions: known allergy, acute neuropsychiatric disorders, hemorrhagic diathesis, cardiac pacemaker, pregnancy/breastfeeding, planned relocation, poor compliance, inadequate oral hygiene.
- **SB classification** (American Academy of Sleep Medicine criteria, three-pronged — all three must agree):
  1. Self-report: structured questionnaire (Paesani et al.) + structured interview (Raphael et al.)
  2. Clinical signs: abnormal tooth wear, cheek/tongue impressions, or masseter hypertrophy
  3. Portable EMG/ECG (BruxOff, AllDer, Italy): 5 nights ≥5 h/night; SB confirmed if >2 episodes/h in ≥1 night
- **Groups (3-year analysis)**: LiDi-SB (n=27), LiDi-no SB (n=21), Zir-SB (n=21), Zir-no SB (n=24); 109 enrolled, 16 excluded before 3-year analysis.
- **SB severity breakdown**: In LiDi-SB — moderate 14 (51.9%), heavy 13 (48.1%); in Zir-SB — moderate 7 (33.3%), heavy 14 (66.7%).
- **Crown materials**:
  - LiDi: IPS e.max Press (Ivoclar Vivadent) wax patterns milled (Zirkonzahn M1/M5) and pressed (Programat EP 5000); stained and glaze fired.
  - Zir: Prettau Zirconia (Zirkonzahn), 3 mol% Y-TZP, milled from pre-sintered blocks, colored, sintered at 1600°C (Zirkonofen 700).
  - All crowns: monolithic, designed digitally (Zirkonzahn.Modellier), fabricated by 3 dental technicians; high-gloss polish verified by independent examiner before cementation.
- **Preparation**: ≥1.2 mm occlusal and ≥1.0 mm axial reduction; rounded edges, chamfer finish line, 6–12° total occlusal convergence. Abutments built up with dual-polymerizing composite resin (Rebilda DC white, VOCO).
- **Crown thickness** (verified pre-insertion): mean occlusal 1.3–1.4 mm across groups; mean axial 1.2 mm.
- **Cementation**: self-adhesive dual-polymerizing resin cement (3M RelyX Unicem Aplicap). LiDi intaglio surfaces etched with 5% HF for 20 s + silanized (Monobond Plus, Ivoclar Vivadent); Zir surfaces air-abraded with 50 µm alumina at 0.1 MPa.
- **Protective guards**: NOT permitted throughout trial (design intention to isolate bruxism effect).
- **Follow-up**: 1 week, 6 months, 1, 2, and 3 years. At each visit: technical + biological complications assessed; at 6 months and 1/2/3 years also: probing depths (6 sites/tooth), mobility, vitality.
- **Primary outcome**: technical complications (ceramic fracture/chipping). Secondary: survival rate, success rate.
- **Sample size**: 2-stage design. Stage 1 = interim analysis at 1 year; no technical complications → no further recruitment needed per predefined protocol. Convenience samples continued as-is: LiDi n=53, Zir n=50 at enrollment.
- **Statistics**: Fisher's exact test, α=0.05; 95% CIs by Newcombe Wilson-based method (R v4.4). Survival = restoration still in situ, no replacement required. Success = restoration in situ with no complication affecting restoration and/or abutment tooth.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Technical complications**: None occurred in any group at any time point. Technical complication rates therefore not analyzed (zero-event).

**Complications overall**: 12 biologic or other complications recorded in 10 restorations across all groups.
- Most frequent: recurrent periodontitis (PPD >4 mm or PPD 4 mm + BOP): LiDi-SB 7.4%, LiDi-no SB 4.8%, Zir-SB 4.8%, Zir-no SB 0%.
- Symptomatic irreversible pulpitis → RCT: LiDi-SB 3.7%, LiDi-no SB 4.8%, Zir-SB 4.8%, Zir-no SB 0%.
- Vertical root fracture (→ extraction or hemisection, classified as failure): LiDi-SB 1 tooth (3.7%), Zir-SB 1 tooth (4.8%), no-SB groups 0%. Both fractures occurred in bruxism groups (1 vital, 1 endodontically treated).
- Loss of proximal contact: LiDi-SB 3.7%, LiDi-no SB 4.8%, Zir 0%.
- Loss of adjacent tooth (crown removed for FPD, classified as failure): Zir-no SB 4.2%.
- 3 crowns total classified as failures.

**Survival rates at 3 years**:

| Material | SB | no-SB | 95% CI of difference | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| LiDi | 96.3% (26/27) | 100% (21/21) | −0.1828 to 0.1481 | >0.999 |
| Zir | 95.2% (20/21) | 95.8% (23/24) | −0.2190 to 0.1940 | >0.999 |

**Success rates at 3 years**:

| Material | SB | no-SB | 95% CI of difference | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| LiDi | 81.5% (22/27) | 95.2% (20/21) | −0.3590 to 0.1450 | 0.211 |
| Zir | 85.7% (18/21) | 95.8% (23/24) | −0.3390 to 0.1530 | 0.326 |

**Annual data (1-year)**:
- LiDi-SB 100% / LiDi-no SB 100% survival; Zir-SB 95.7% / Zir-no SB 96.3% survival.

**Baseline group imbalances** (despite randomization): Zir-SB had 71.4% men vs ~33% in other groups; no-SB groups were older (Zir-no SB mean 62.0 years vs Zir-SB 49.1); Zir-no SB had more second molars (41.7%).

**Conclusion (authors)**: Within 3 years, sleep bruxism had no detectable influence on technical complications or survival/success of monolithic LiDi or Zir molar single crowns. Null hypothesis retained.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Small sample size**: 2-stage design was predefined; since 1-year interim showed negligible differences, further recruitment was not feasible monocentrically. Groups of ~21–27 are underpowered for rare fatigue failures.
- **3-year horizon**: fatigue-driven ceramic failures and low-event-rate long-term complications (e.g., vertical root fracture) are likely underdetected at 3 years.
- **Baseline group imbalances**: Zir-SB overrepresented men (higher bite forces) and LiDi-SB overrepresented women; no-SB groups older (lower bite forces). These cofounding factors may partially mask or mitigate between-group differences.
- **Single occlusal guard exclusion**: findings primarily apply to bruxers without splint therapy. May not generalize to clinical settings where guards are standard.
- **SB assessed only at baseline**: bruxism fluctuates; long-term SB exposure may differ from baseline status. Follow-up did not reassess SB.
- **Detection bias risk**: treating dentists performed 41% of their own recall examinations. No transillumination, dye, or high-magnification devices for early fatigue crack detection.
- **Radiographic follow-up**: not standardized at predefined intervals; only when clinically indicated — may undercount biological complications.
- **Strict survival definition**: one crown counted as failure solely because adjacent tooth was lost (crown itself intact). This slightly underestimates material-specific performance.
- **Absolute crown thickness safety margin**: at 1.3–1.4 mm occlusal (exceeding minimum recommendations), results may not extend to more aggressive minimal-thickness designs.
- **Future work needed**: larger multicenter RCTs with longer follow-up; reassessment of SB status during follow-up; extension to multi-unit FDPs, implant-supported crowns, and anterior restorations.

## 6. Related Work

- Monolithic zirconia single-crown SR+MA (Leitão 2022; Mazza et al. 2022): pooled fracture rates 0.18% (Zir) and 0.19% (LiDi) at ~1–2 years; consistent with zero fractures here.
- Large-scale dental lab survey (Sulaiman et al. 2020, n=188,695 crowns): LiDi 0.96% and Zir 0.54% fracture within 7.5 years; confirms real-world low fracture baseline.
- 1-year interim results of this same RCT (Schmitter et al. 2022, J Clin Med): 100% survival all LiDi groups; 95.7–96.3% Zir; no technical complications.
- Bruxism–restoration failure scoping review (Al-Talib et al. 2025, J Dent) and SR+MA (de Souza Melo et al. 2018, J Prosthet Dent): association still unclear due to inconsistent bruxism definitions; this RCT provides the clearest current evidence.
- BruxOff validity study (Ohlmann et al. 2022, J Oral Rehabil): self-reports and clinical signs have limited accuracy compared to home-EMG — supports the 3-pronged diagnostic approach used here.

## 7. Glossary

- **Sleep bruxism (SB)**: masticatory muscle activity during sleep characterized by rhythmic (phasic) or non-rhythmic (tonic) tooth clenching/grinding; distinct from awake bruxism. "Definite" SB requires instrumental EMG confirmation.
- **BruxOff**: portable single-channel ambulatory EMG/ECG device for objective SB detection over multiple nights; >2 episodes/h = positive.
- **Monolithic crown**: single homogeneous ceramic block crown with no veneering porcelain overlay, eliminating chipping risk at the veneering–core interface.
- **Lithium disilicate (LiDi)**: press-ceramic (IPS e.max Press); moderate flexural strength (~400 MPa); favorable esthetics; requires HF etching + silane for adhesive cementation.
- **Zirconia (Zir)**: 3 mol% yttria-stabilized tetragonal zirconia polycrystal (Prettau, Y-TZP); high flexural strength (~900–1200 MPa); must be air-abraded for cementation with self-adhesive cement.
- **Survival vs success**: survival = restoration still in situ with no replacement required; success = survival plus no complication of any kind affecting the restoration or abutment tooth.
- **Recurrent periodontitis**: here defined operationally as PPD >4 mm, or PPD 4 mm + bleeding on probing at a follow-up visit.
- **Newcombe Wilson-based method**: method for computing 95% CI of difference in proportions for small samples.
