## Why Ingested

Existing zirconia/LiDi crown survival pages [[dental-materials/zirconia/leitao-2022-monolithic-zirconia-single-crown-survival-sr]] and [[dental-materials/zirconia/aswal-2023-cadcam-zirconia-lithium-disilicate-crowns-sr-ma]] report pooled survival but do not isolate the occlusal-overload risk modifier of sleep bruxism with an objective EMG diagnosis. This 3-year RCT (Bömicke 2026) directly tests whether SB degrades survival/success of monolithic LiDi vs Zir molar crowns, contextualizing the occlusal-overload literature ([[occlusion/fan-2018-occlusal-trauma-and-excessive-occlusal]]) for material selection in bruxers. Abstract-only (no PMC full text).

## One-line Summary

3-year RCT (n=109, 4 groups by EMG-confirmed sleep bruxism × randomized monolithic LiDi vs zirconia molar crown, no occlusal guards): sleep bruxism showed no detectable impact on technical complications, survival, or success of either material.

## 한줄요약

3년 RCT (환자 109명, EMG로 확인한 수면 이갈이 유무 × 무작위 배정한 모놀리식 리튬디실리케이트 vs 지르코니아 대구치 단일 크라운, 교합안정장치 미사용): 수면 이갈이는 두 재료 모두에서 기술적 합병증·생존율·성공률에 유의한 영향을 주지 않았다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides RCT-level, prospectively gathered evidence on whether sleep bruxism (SB) — a presumed occlusal-overload driver — actually degrades the clinical performance of monolithic all-ceramic molar single crowns.
- Uses an **objective SB diagnosis** combining a structured questionnaire, clinical examination, and a portable EMG device (BruxOff), rather than self-report alone.
- Directly compares two leading monolithic materials (lithium disilicate vs zirconia) under the same SB stratification.
- Deliberately withholds protective occlusal guards, isolating the unmitigated effect of bruxism on the restoration.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Prospective clinical RCT; crown material randomly allocated, SB status assessed and used as a stratifying factor → four groups.
- **Population**: 109 patients each requiring a single molar crown.
- **Groups**: LiDi-SB (n=27), LiDi-no SB (n=21), Zir-SB (n=21), Zir-no SB (n=24) — monolithic crowns.
- **SB assessment**: structured questionnaire + clinical examination + portable EMG (BruxOff).
- **Intervention constraint**: protective occlusal guards (night guards) NOT permitted.
- **Follow-up**: 1 week, 6 months, 1 year, 2 years, 3 years after cementation.
- **Outcomes**: technical complications, survival rate, success rate.
- **Statistics**: Fisher's exact test, α = 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Technical complications**: none occurred in any group.
- **Survival** (LiDi): SB 96.3% vs no-SB 100% (95% CI of difference −0.1828 to 0.1481; p > 0.999).
- **Survival** (Zir): SB 95.2% vs no-SB 95.8% (95% CI of difference −0.2190 to 0.1940; p > 0.999).
- **Success** (LiDi): SB 81.5% vs no-SB 95.2% (95% CI of difference −0.3590 to 0.1450; p = 0.211).
- **Success** (Zir): SB 85.7% vs no-SB 95.8% (95% CI of difference −0.3390 to 0.1530; p = 0.326).
- **Conclusion**: over 3 years, SB had no statistically detectable impact on complications, survival, or success for either monolithic LiDi or Zir molar crowns.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Numerically lower success in SB groups (e.g., LiDi 81.5% vs 95.2%) suggests a possible trend that the study may be underpowered to detect; wide confidence intervals reflect small per-group sample sizes.
- 3-year horizon is relatively short for fatigue-driven ceramic failures and for low-event-rate molar crowns.
- Single-tooth molar crowns only; results may not extend to multi-unit FDPs, anterior restorations, or implant-supported crowns where overload risk differs.
- SB severity gradation and dynamic occlusal load were not (per abstract) the analysis axis — only SB presence/absence.
- Abstract-only ingestion: detailed methodology (preparation, cementation protocol, success-criteria definition, dropout handling) not captured here.

## 7. Glossary

- **Sleep bruxism (SB)**: involuntary masticatory-muscle activity (clenching/grinding) during sleep; a candidate occlusal-overload driver for restoration failure.
- **Monolithic crown**: single homogeneous ceramic block crown (no veneering porcelain), reducing chipping risk.
- **Lithium disilicate (LiDi)**: glass-ceramic with high translucency and moderate flexural strength.
- **Zirconia (Zir)**: yttria-stabilized tetragonal zirconia polycrystal restorative ceramic with high flexural strength.
- **BruxOff**: portable single-channel EMG/ECG device for objective ambulatory sleep-bruxism detection.
- **Survival vs success**: survival = restoration still in situ/functional; success = survival plus absence of any (even reparable) complication or quality deficiency.
