## Why Ingested

이 endotoxin/세포벽 성분(LTA/LPS) 배치의 한 축으로, 기존 [[endodontics/ordinola-zapata-2022-intracanal-medicaments-status-directions]]가 정리한 calcium hydroxide의 *E. faecalis* 한계를 보완할 신규 intracanal medicament 후보(postbiotic LTA)를 제시한다. 다른 배치 논문들이 LTA를 제거 대상 virulence factor로 다루는 것과 대비되게, 본 in-vitro는 LTA(probiotic 유래)를 치료제로 역전시킨다는 점에서 contrast 가치가 있다.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro human dentinal cylinder model (n=28): lipoteichoic acid purified from *Lacticaseibacillus rhamnosus* GG (Lgg.LTA, 50 μg/ml) inhibited 2-week *E. faecalis* intracanal biofilm as effectively as calcium hydroxide, while sterile water and NMP vehicle had little effect.

## 한줄요약

발치한 사람 치아 상아질 실린더 모델(n=28) in-vitro 연구: 프로바이오틱 *Lacticaseibacillus rhamnosus* GG에서 정제한 지질테이코산(Lgg.LTA, 50 μg/ml)이 2주간 형성된 *E. faecalis* 근관 내 바이오필름을 수산화칼슘과 동등하게 억제했고, 증류수·NMP 부형제는 효과가 거의 없었다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Lipoteichoic Acid from *Lacticaseibacillus rhamnosus* GG as a Novel Intracanal Medicament Targeting *Enterococcus faecalis* Biofilm Formation
- **Authors**: Ji-Young Yoon, Somin Park, Dongwook Lee, Ok-Jin Park, WooCheol Lee, Seung Hyun Han (Seoul National University)
- **Journal**: Journal of Microbiology 2024;62:897–905
- **DOI**: 10.1007/s12275-024-00165-6
- **Study type**: In-vitro (ex-vivo human dentinal cylinder biofilm model)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First evaluation of *Lactobacillaceae*-derived LTA as an intracanal medicament in a human tooth root canal model (prior LTA anti-biofilm work was plate/abiotic-surface only).
- Identifies Lgg.LTA as the most effective among three *Lactobacillaceae* LTAs (vs *L. casei*, *L. acidophilus*) against *E. faecalis* biofilm in preliminary crystal-violet screening.
- Positions a postbiotic (inert microbial component) as a safer alternative to live probiotics (avoids bacteremia risk) and to calcium hydroxide (avoids apical-extrusion tissue irritation).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 28 dentinal cylinders (4 mm length, 1.5 mm canal) from single-rooted extracted human teeth; smear layer removed with 5.25% NaOCl + 17% EDTA, NaOCl neutralized with sodium thiosulfate, autoclaved.
- *E. faecalis* KCTC 3206 inoculated at 1×10⁸ CFU/ml, biofilm matured 2 weeks (fresh BHI every 3 days).
- Four intracanal treatment groups (n=6 each): sterile distilled water (SDW), 20% NMP (vehicle control, at MBC), calcium hydroxide (CleaniCal, 30% CH), Lgg.LTA 50 μg/ml; 24 h at 37 °C.
- Outcomes: scanning electron microscopy (SEM), confocal laser scanning microscopy (CLSM) live/dead, and culture CFU enumeration of residual *E. faecalis* including intratubular bacteria.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Lgg.LTA inhibited *E. faecalis* intracanal biofilm formation **as much as calcium hydroxide**; SDW and NMP vehicle had little effect.
- Lgg.LTA reduced both live and dead bacteria within dentinal tubules — suggesting reduced re-infection potential.
- Among the three LTAs, Lgg.LTA was the most potent anti-biofilm agent in preliminary screening.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro single-species (*E. faecalis*) monobiofilm; no polymicrobial or host-tissue context.
- Short 24 h medicament contact; no clinical apical periodontitis healing outcome.
- Optimal concentration, vehicle for clinical delivery, and substantivity not established.

## 6. Related Work

- Builds on plate-level *Lactobacillaceae* LTA anti-biofilm work (Jung 2019, Kim 2020) by moving into a human root canal model.
- Contrasts with the conventional view of LTA as a virulence factor / disinfection target ([[endodontics/irrigation/alquria-2023-disinfection-protocols-bacteria-lta-lps]]).

## 7. Glossary

- **LTA (lipoteichoic acid)**: amphiphilic surface polymer of Gram-positive bacterial cell walls; TLR-2 ligand. Here used therapeutically (probiotic-derived) rather than as a virulence factor.
- **Postbiotic**: inert (non-viable) microorganisms and/or their components with bioactivity.
- **NMP (N-methyl-2-pyrrolidone)**: solvent used as vehicle control.
- **Lgg**: *Lacticaseibacillus rhamnosus* GG, a probiotic strain.
