## Why Ingested

KM 증대술의 *치료 효과*(증대 후 결과)를 정량화한 SR+MA로, 2026-06-14 batch b의 KM 클러스터([[keratinized-mucosa-peri-implant-health-overview]])에서 "증대술 축"의 효과 크기를 보강한다. 위험인자 SR([[mahardawi-2023-lack-keratinized-mucosa-peri-implantitis-sr-ma]], [[ravida-2022-keratinized-mucosa-width-peri-implant-disease-sr-ma]])이 "KM 부족 → 나쁨"을 보였다면, 본 논문은 FGG/CTG 증대가 무엇을 얼마나 회복하는지(FGG KM +2.6 mm, CTG MR −2 mm)를 수치로 제시해 [[sanz-2022-keratinized-mucosa-around-implants-consensus]] · [[stefanini-2023-soft-tissue-augmentation-peri-implant-sr]]의 술식 권고를 정량 근거로 뒷받침한다.

## One-line Summary
SR+MA (11 clinical studies, 290 participants): around functioning implants, FGG gained a weighted mean 2.6 mm of keratinized mucosa with reduced mucosal inflammation and no crestal bone change up to 4 years, while CTG reduced peri-implant mucosal recession by a weighted mean 2 mm.

## 한줄요약
SR+MA(임상 11편, 290명): 기능 중인 임플란트 주위에서 유리치은이식(FGG)은 각화점막을 가중평균 2.6 mm 증가시키고 점막 염증을 줄였으며 4년까지 치조정골 변화가 없었고, 결합조직이식(CTG)은 점막 퇴축(MR)을 가중평균 2 mm 감소시켰다.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Focuses specifically on **functioning (already-restored) dental implants**, not implants at placement/second stage.
- Quantifies the *therapeutic* effect of autogenous soft-tissue grafts: FGG for keratinized mucosa (KM) width gain; CTG for peri-implant mucosal recession (MR).
- Weighted mean KM gain after FGG = **2.6 mm**, with significant reduction of mucosal inflammation and **no change in crestal bone levels up to 4 years**.
- Weighted mean MR reduction after CTG = **2 mm**.
- Reinforces that **lack of KM is associated with greater peri-implant mucosal inflammation**.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Systematic search of electronic databases for autogenous soft-tissue grafts used to create/augment KM around functioning dental implants.
- Two investigators independently screened; **573 publications retrieved → 11 clinical studies included (290 participants)**.
- Outcomes pooled by graft type: FGG → KM width gain + inflammation; CTG → mucosal recession.
- Meta-analysis reported as weighted mean changes.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **FGG**: weighted mean KM width gain 2.6 mm; significant reduction in mucosal inflammation; crestal bone levels stable up to 4 years.
- **CTG**: weighted mean mucosal recession (MR) reduction 2 mm.
- Lack of KM → greater mucosal inflammation around functioning implants.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small evidence base (11 studies, 290 participants); heterogeneous designs.
- Autogenous grafts only — soft-tissue substitutes (xenogeneic/allogeneic matrices) not covered (contrast with [[sanz-2022-keratinized-mucosa-around-implants-consensus]], which finds xenograft non-inferior for KM gain).
- Follow-up generally ≤4 years; longer-term stability uncertain.
- Pooled weighted means without full heterogeneity/GRADE reporting in the abstract.

## 7. Glossary
- **KM (keratinized mucosa)**: band of keratinized tissue around an implant; "adequate" generally defined as ≥2 mm width.
- **FGG (free gingival graft)**: autogenous epithelialized palatal graft used primarily to increase KM width.
- **CTG (connective tissue graft)**: subepithelial autogenous graft used primarily to increase soft-tissue thickness and treat/decrease mucosal recession.
- **MR (mucosal recession)**: apical migration of the peri-implant mucosal margin.
