## One-line Summary
STROBE-compliant retrospective cohort comparing short (≤6.5 mm) vs longer (≥7.5 mm) implants supporting single-crown restorations in posterior premolar/molar regions. Same surgeon, same prosthetic concept (transepithelial abutment + screw-retained crown).

## 2. Key Contributions
Single-operator/single-protocol design isolates implant length as the variable. Provides medium-term evidence that short implants are non-inferior for single-crown indications in posterior regions.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Retrospective cohort following STROBE. 88 short + 88 long implants. Outcomes: survival, marginal bone level changes, prosthetic complications.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
No statistical differences. Median follow-up 31 (short) vs 35 (long) months. Prosthetic complications: screw loosening 2 vs 5, ceramic chipping 1 vs 0. MBL ≥2 mm in 1/88 vs 3/88 (p=0.312).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
Retrospective; non-randomized; single surgeon; medium follow-up. Inclusion bias possible.
