## One-line Summary
Retrospective longitudinal cohort (103 patients with maxillary molar sinus floor elevation: crestal vs lateral approach, followed 3 years with serial orthopantomographs) showed lateral approach produced 12.00 mm grafted bone height vs 8.07 mm for crestal, but post-graft resorption was nearly identical (~0.95–0.98 mm total over 3 years, mostly in year 1) — both approaches give predictable long-term bone maintenance, with crestal vs lateral selected by available initial bone, not by long-term stability concerns.

- **Journal**: Medicina 2023;59(6):1132
- **Pages**: 1–10 (10 pages, open access MDPI)

- **Study type**: Retrospective longitudinal radiographic cohort

## 2. Key Contributions
- Direct head-to-head **3-year radiographic** comparison of crestal vs lateral sinus floor elevation in a Korean cohort.
- Quantifies absolute grafted bone gain by technique (lateral 12 mm vs crestal 8 mm) and the *resorption pattern over time*.
- Confirms that **first-year resorption** dominates both techniques; thereafter the height plateaus.
- Provides clinical justification for choosing **crestal** when residual bone height (RBH) is sufficient, and **lateral** when RBH is insufficient — without compromising long-term graft maintenance.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 103 patients undergoing maxillary molar implant placement with sinus elevation 2017–2020 at Kyungpook National University.
- Groups by approach (clinician's preoperative decision based on RBH):
  - **Crestal approach** (Summers osteotome / similar) — when RBH typically ≥4–5 mm.
  - **Lateral approach** (Tatum window) — when RBH typically <4 mm.
- Outcome: **bone height (mm)** measured on serial orthopantomographs at:
  - Immediately post-op
  - 1 year post-implant
  - 2 years
  - 3 years
- Magnification correction applied to panoramic measurements.
- Statistics: between-group comparison + within-group time-series.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Total bone gain post-op (mean)**:
  - Crestal: 8.07 mm
  - Lateral: 12.00 mm
- **Total bone height loss over 3 years**:
  - Crestal: 0.98 mm
  - Lateral: 0.95 mm
- **Year-1** accounts for the largest portion of resorption in both approaches.
- **Years 2–3** changes are minimal — graft height plateaus.
- Implication: technique choice does not materially affect long-term bone maintenance, only the initial graft volume achievable.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Retrospective; assignment not randomized (RBH-driven selection bias).
- 2D panoramic measurements — known magnification distortion vs CBCT 3D ground truth.
- No histologic outcome (vital bone formation %).
- Grafting material(s) not analyzed by type — heterogeneity in graft.
- No survival rate of implants reported in abstract.
- Single center, Korean population.

## 7. Glossary
- **Crestal approach**: minimally invasive transalveolar approach via the implant osteotomy (Summers/osteotome).
- **Lateral approach (Tatum window)**: trapdoor osteotomy through the lateral antral wall for direct access to the SM and graft placement.
- **RBH (Residual Bone Height)**: vertical bone between alveolar crest and sinus floor before surgery.
- **Grafted bone height**: post-op vertical bone from crest to most apical augmented limit.
- **Orthopantomograph (OPG)**: panoramic radiograph used for serial follow-up; magnification ~25%.
- **Resorption**: post-graft volume loss, predominantly during the first year of healing.
