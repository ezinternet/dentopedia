## Why Ingested

미각상실·침분비저하의 병인 스펙트럼(바이러스성/자가면역성/약물성/노화성)에 "방사선치료 유발" 축을 추가하기 위해 검색. 기존 [[oral-medicine/tsuchiya-2023-covid-19-oral-sequelae-gustatory-saliva]]가 바이러스 수용체 발현 기전으로 미각·타액 동시장애를 설명했다면, 본 전향적 코호트는 두경부암 방사선치료에서 실제 선량-반응 관계로 "미각이상이 미뢰 선량보다 타액선 선량에 더 강하게 상관"함을 정량 검증해, 방사선 유발 병인의 실제 기전(타액선 손상 매개 vs 미뢰 직접손상)에 대한 근거를 제공한다.

## One-line Summary

Prospective observational cohort (31 HNSCC patients undergoing IMRT, CiTAS/EORTC questionnaires at baseline, 3 weeks, 3 months) finding acute dysgeusia correlates significantly with mean dose to submandibular and parotid glands (p=0.05 each) but NOT with dose to the taste-bud-bearing tongue mucosa itself, suggesting radiation-induced taste alteration is substantially mediated through salivary gland damage rather than direct taste bud injury alone.

## 한줄요약

전향적 관찰 코호트(두경부편평세포암 IMRT 환자 31명, CiTAS/EORTC 설문 기저·3주·3개월 추적) — 급성 미각이상(dysgeusia)은 미뢰(taste bud) 선량이 아니라 악하선·이하선 평균선량과 유의하게 상관(각 p=0.05)함을 발견 — 방사선 유발 미각장애가 미뢰 직접손상보다 타액선 손상을 매개로 발생함을 시사.

## 1. Document Information

- Journal: La Radiologia Medica, 2023;128(12):1571–1579
- Publication date: 2023-08-29
- Design: prospective observational cohort study, single center (Radiation Oncology Unit, University of Florence, Italy)
- Recruitment: September 2019–November 2020, all patients gave written informed consent

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides one of the few prospective dosimetric analyses specifically isolating taste-bud-bearing tongue mucosa as a distinct organ at risk (OAR), contoured separately from the whole oral cavity or entire tongue, allowing direct dose-response testing against patient-reported dysgeusia.
- Counterintuitive key finding: dose to taste buds did NOT correlate with dysgeusia, but did correlate with dry mouth and pain mouth; dysgeusia instead correlated with salivary gland (submandibular/parotid) dose — challenging the classical taste-bud-atrophy model of radiation-induced taste loss.
- Consistent with Stieb et al.'s independent finding that salivary gland dosimetry (not taste-bud dose) predicts late taste impairment, reinforcing a salivary-gland-mediated mechanism for radiation-induced dysgeusia.
- Documents the temporal trajectory of taste alteration: CiTAS subdomain scores roughly double from baseline to 3-week follow-up, then partially recover by 3 months (though global health status recovers more fully than mouth-dryness/saliva-thickness scores).
- Synthesizes prior dose-threshold literature (60 Gy for oral cavity, 40–50 Gy thresholds from other cohorts, 20 Gy anterior-tongue threshold) into a coherent discussion of anatomic sub-volume sensitivity.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: prospective observational cohort, hypothesis-generating (p=0.05 threshold explicitly framed as such).
- **Population**: 31 analyzed HNSCC patients (71% oropharyngeal, 60% HPV-positive among those), treated with IMRT (tomotheropy, 3-dose-level SIB 69.9/59.4/52.8 Gy in 33 fractions); 77.4% received concurrent cisplatin.
- **Contouring**: taste-bud volume contoured as an OAR (axial elaboration of midsagittal contour per latest guidelines) separately from anterior third, medium third, and base of tongue; major salivary glands contoured per Brouwer et al.
- **Outcome measures**: CiTAS (Chemotherapy-Induced Taste Alteration Scale, 18-item, 4 subdomains via Likert 1–5), EORTC QLQ-C30, EORTC QLQ-H&N43, assessed at baseline (T0), 3 weeks post-RT (T1), and 3 months post-RT (T2).
- **Statistics**: univariable logistic Cox regression (taste impairment vs. dosimetric/clinical variables); ordinal logistic regression (CiTAS vs. symptom classes); SPSS v24.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Mean doses: anterior tongue third 23.85 Gy, medium third 35.50 Gy, base tongue 47.67 Gy; taste buds 32.72 Gy (median); parotid glands 25/23 Gy (R/L); submandibular glands 47.8/39.4 Gy (R/L).
- **Dysgeusia** correlated with submandibular gland mean dose (95% CI 0–0.02, p=0.05) and parotid gland mean dose (95% CI 0–0.02, p=0.05) — NOT with taste-bud dose.
- **Dry mouth** correlated with mean dose to anterior tongue third (p=0.04), medium third (p=0.04), taste buds (p=0.03), and submandibular glands (p=0.02).
- **Pain mouth** correlated with mean dose to taste buds (p=0.04), submandibular glands (p=0.03), base tongue (p=0.02).
- CiTAS subdomain scores worsened substantially from baseline to T1 (e.g., general taste alterations 1.17→2.59) then partially recovered by T2 (1.83) — consistent with the known 4–12 month recovery window for radiation-induced taste dysfunction reported in prior literature.
- Global health status nearly fully recovered by T2 (69.35 baseline → 62.37 at T1 → 71.39 at T2), while mouth dryness/saliva thickness scores remained markedly elevated at T2 (11.29 baseline → 49.46 at T1 → 46.67 at T2) — dissociating taste/global-QoL recovery from persistent salivary dysfunction.
- Contextualizes prior dose-threshold findings: ~60 Gy to entire oral cavity associated with near-universal taste loss; other cohorts report 40–50 Gy oral-cavity thresholds; anterior-tongue doses as low as 20 Gy can cause temporary taste loss (Yamashita).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Author-acknowledged: did not evaluate concurrent chemotherapy's independent impact on dysgeusia (a cited comparator study found less frequent severe taste alteration with chemotherapy, non-significant).
- Small sample size (31 patients); no stratification by smoking status, alcohol consumption, or HPV status, all of which may affect baseline taste function.
- Only acute taste impairment assessed (baseline, 3 weeks, 3 months) — no longer-term (6–12 month) follow-up captured in this analysis.
- Authors suggest other dosimetric parameters (D95, D5) might better predict taste impairment than mean dose alone; propose future integration of dosiomics/radiomics and machine learning with salivary gland and gustatory OAR morphological data for improved acute/late dysgeusia prediction.

## 6. Related Work

- Cites Stieb et al. (independent confirmation that salivary gland dosimetry, not taste-bud dose, predicts late taste impairment).
- Cites Martini et al. 2019 (31 HN patients, progressive dysgeusia peaking at week 7, partial post-RT recovery heterogeneous by CiTAS dimension).
- Cites historical taste-bud-atrophy mechanistic literature: Mossman (bovine taste-cell membrane integrity decline with radiation dose), Just (papilla epithelial changes without taste bud structural change), Yamashita (taste bud atrophy, not nerve damage, as primary mechanism), Sandow (6-month recovery indicating taste-cell-level rather than nerve-level radiation action).
- Cites dose-threshold literature: Sapir et al. (oral cavity/tongue dose correlating with severe dysgeusia in 73 oropharyngeal patients), Chen et al. (glossectomy as dominant long-term factor; <50 Gy vs. ≥50 Gy oral cavity dose thresholds), Chen 2022 (87 Taiwanese patients, >40 Gy oral cavity dose threshold), Kamprad et al. (entire-tongue irradiation and major gustatory defects).

## 7. Glossary

- **CiTAS**: Chemotherapy-Induced Taste Alteration Scale, an 18-item self-administered patient-reported outcome measure covering 4 gustatory dimensions (intensity, discomfort, phantogeusia/parageusia, general alterations).
- **OAR (organ at risk)**: a normal-tissue structure whose radiation dose must be limited/monitored during radiotherapy planning.
- **IMRT**: intensity-modulated radiotherapy.
- **SIB**: simultaneous integrated boost, an IMRT technique delivering multiple dose levels concurrently.
- **Dmean/Dmin/Dmax**: mean/minimum/maximum radiation dose delivered to a contoured structure.
