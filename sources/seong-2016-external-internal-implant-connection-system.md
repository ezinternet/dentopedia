## Why Ingested

임플란트 픽스처-어버트먼트 연결부(fixture-abutment connection) 설계의 외부/내부 연결 차이는 임상 프로토콜, 합병증 발생률, 골 응력 분산에 직접 영향을 미친다. 한국 임상 맥락에서 이 내용을 정리한 KDA 교육 리뷰로, [[implants/ayoub-2024-abutment-bending-guide-one-piece-implant]]의 단일-구조 어버트먼트 굽힘 특성 논의와 연계하여 외부/내부 연결 생역학을 이해하는 배경 문서로 활용.

## One-line Summary

Narrative review (Korean Dental Association, 2016) comparing external hexagon vs. internal connection implant systems across biomechanics, microgap, clinical procedures, and prosthetic applications.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Systematic comparison of external hexagon (EH) and internal connection (IC) implant-abutment interface designs
- Review of biomechanical stress distribution differences between connection types
- Analysis of microgap behavior, sinking-down phenomenon, and anti-rotation features
- Clinical guidance for choosing connection type based on prosthetic scenario (single unit, multiple unit, screw- vs cement-retained)
- Discussion of impression techniques (transfer vs pick-up) appropriate for each connection type
- Review of abutment options: customized/casting abutments, platform switching considerations

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**External Connection (External Hexagon):**
- Historical origin: Brånemark system; external hexagonal anti-rotation feature
- Abutment sits on top of the implant shoulder; more susceptible to lateral loading stress transfer to crestal bone
- Microgap at the abutment-implant interface is larger and exposed to the oral environment
- Abutment screw loosening risk is higher, especially under lateral forces
- Easier for multiple-unit prostheses (transfer impression technique) and laboratory procedures
- Compatible with a wide range of prosthetic components and casting abutments

**Internal Connection (Internal Cone/Morse Taper):**
- Internal connection places the abutment-implant interface below the crestal bone level
- Morse taper (conical internal connection) achieves a cold-weld effect — higher screw-joint stability than spline-type internal connections
- Sinking-down phenomenon: abutment seats slightly deeper under load; generally a positive feature (tighter fit)
- Microgap is repositioned internally, reducing bacterial microleakage exposure at the bone crest
- Better stress distribution to bone — less crestal bone loss potential
- Non-hex (rotation-free) engagement in some Morse taper systems requires precise restoration design
- Internal connection implants 4–5 mm diameter have engagement depth ~3 mm (vs ~1.7 mm for external hex)
- Allows platform switching (prosthetic platform smaller than implant diameter), which shifts the microgap medially away from the crestal bone

**Prosthetic Considerations:**
- Screw-retained prostheses reduce the risk of residual cement complications vs cement-retained
- Gold screw with screw abutment: recommended for retrievability; reduces cast post applications
- Porcelain chipping risk is similar between types but more manageable with proper screw-retained design
- Impression: transfer-type impressions commonly used for multiple-unit external connection; pick-up type preferred for internal connections in some scenarios
- Light-body impression material preferred for detailed abutment margin capture

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative review without systematic search protocol or meta-analytic pooling
- No original clinical data; entirely synthesis of existing literature
- Some Korean-language body content is inaccessible due to PDF encoding (Korean text rendering)
- 2016 publication date predates more recent conical connection RCT data
- No head-to-head clinical outcome data (survival, MBL) presented — biological performance not directly compared in original clinical trials

## 7. Glossary

- **External hexagon (외부 육각형, EH)**: Anti-rotation feature projecting above the implant platform; original Brånemark design
- **Internal connection (내부 연결, IC)**: Abutment engages inside the implant body; includes Morse taper (conical) and spline-type variants
- **Morse taper (모스 테이퍼)**: Conical interference-fit internal connection; produces cold-weld–like stability
- **Microgap (마이크로갭)**: Microscopic gap at fixture-abutment interface; site of bacterial colonization
- **Sinking-down (싱킹-다운)**: Slight apical seating of abutment under load in internal connection; enhances joint seal
- **Platform switching (플랫폼 스위칭)**: Using a prosthetic abutment narrower than the implant platform diameter; shifts microgap medially
- **Anti-rotation (회전방지)**: Geometric feature (hexagon, spline, groove) preventing abutment rotation on the fixture
- **Screw-joint stability (나사 접합 안정성)**: Resistance of the abutment screw to loosening under functional load
