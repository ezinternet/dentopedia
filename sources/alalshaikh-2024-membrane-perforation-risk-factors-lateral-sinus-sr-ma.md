## Why Ingested

This comprehensive risk-factor SR+MA quantifies and ranks the predictors of Schneiderian membrane perforation in lateral-window maxillary sinus elevation, reinforcing and extending [[wiki/sinus-lift/lateral/nemati-2023-membrane-perforation-risk-lateral-sinus]]. It provides effect sizes (smoking 1.6×, septa 3×, thin membrane <1.5 mm) that strengthen the membrane-thickness / septa / smoking risk triad already noted in our perforation pages ([[wiki/sinus-lift/lateral/diaz-olivares-2021-schneiderian-membrane-perforation-sinus-lift]]), giving a quantitative anchor for preoperative CBCT risk stratification.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of (mostly retrospective) lateral-window sinus elevation studies identifying the most prominent Schneiderian membrane perforation risk factors as thin membrane (<1.5 mm), sinus septa (3× risk), and smoking (1.6× risk; ~1 perforation per 7 smokers).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Systematically scrutinizes and ranks the risk factors associated with Schneiderian membrane perforation specifically during **lateral window** preparation for maxillary sinus elevation.
- Provides quantitative effect estimates for the three most prominent factors: membrane thickness, presence of septa, and smoking.
- Distinguishes factors with robust association from those without measurable effect (window size, number of missing teeth).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Search across multiple electronic databases; critical evaluation of studies meeting inclusion criteria.
- Systematic review followed by meta-analysis of identified risk factors.
- The included evidence base is predominantly **retrospective**, which the authors flag as the main limitation of the evidence pool.
- (abstract-only — exact databases, search dates, number of pooled studies, and pooled statistics beyond those reported below were not retrieved.)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Thin membranes (<1.5 mm)** are at significant risk of perforation versus thicker membranes.
- **Smoking** increased perforation likelihood by **1.6 times** — approximately **one perforation in every seven smokers** treated.
- **Presence of septa** increased perforation risk **3 times** versus absence of septa.
- **Piezosurgery vs rotary instruments**: marginal significance (**P = .05**) favoring reduced perforation risk with piezosurgery.
- **Lateral wall thickness** and **residual ridge height**: significant factors, but on **limited evidence (3 studies)**.
- **Number of missing teeth** and **window size**: did **NOT** affect perforation likelihood.
- **Most prominent factors overall**: membrane thickness, septa, and smoking.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Most included studies are **retrospective**, limiting causal inference.
- Lateral wall thickness and residual ridge height conclusions rest on only **3 studies**.
- Piezosurgery benefit is only marginally significant (P = .05).
- Abstract-only ingest: full numeric heterogeneity, confidence intervals, and risk-of-bias details not retrieved.

## 7. Glossary

- **Schneiderian membrane**: the mucoperiosteal lining of the maxillary sinus; its integrity is the key intra-operative endpoint in sinus elevation.
- **Lateral window technique**: lateral antrostomy approach for maxillary sinus floor elevation.
- **Sinus septa**: bony partitions within the maxillary sinus that complicate window osteotomy and membrane elevation.
- **Piezosurgery**: ultrasonic bone-cutting that selectively cuts mineralized tissue, theoretically sparing soft membrane.
- **Residual ridge height (RRH)**: vertical bone remaining below the sinus floor.
