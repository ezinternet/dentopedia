## One-line Summary
JPIS 2025;55(6):425-435: Narrative review of maxillary sinus hypoplasia (MSH) — reduced sinus volume variants including sinus atelectasis, inferior meatus pneumatization, and ethmomaxillary sinus; all require CBCT diagnosis and alter sinus lift surgical planning.

## 1. Key Results
- MSH = reduction in sinus volume (opposite of pneumatization)
- Sinus floor positioned more cranially than nasal floor
- 3 clinically relevant variants for implant therapy:
  1. Chronic maxillary sinus atelectasis (ostium blockage → implant infection risk)
  2. Inferior meatus pneumatization → lateral nasal expansion → nasal floor elevation needed
  3. Ethmomaxillary sinus → septum-like structure → two bone windows required for LSA
- Diagnosis requires 3D CBCT imaging
- MSH can significantly impact sinus floor elevation surgery

## 2. Clinical Implications
- Pre-surgical CBCT essential to identify MSH variants before sinus lift
- Each MSH subtype requires modified surgical approach
- Clinicians must recognize MSH early to avoid complications
