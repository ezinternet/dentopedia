## Why Ingested

Existing clear-aligner FEA/biomechanics coverage was anchored on [[orthodontics/clear-aligner/nakornnoi-2024-aligner-trimline-biomechanics-tooth-movement-sr]] (single design variable — trimline extension). This 2025 SR is the first to systematically map the *whole* CA biomechanics literature simulated by FEA (29 studies) across all three movement directions and every auxiliary class (attachments, power ridges, divots, intermaxillary elastics, membrane thickness/composition). It extends the trimline-focused review into a full FEA biomechanics atlas and supplies the mechanistic "uneven crown-vs-root stress → torque → side effect → auxiliary counterbalance" framework that underlies aligner attachment and biomechanics pages.

## One-line Summary

PROSPERO-registered systematic review of 29 FEA studies of clear-aligner biomechanics: all CA side effects (roller-coaster effect, anchorage loss, tipping, rotation) stem from uneven crown-vs-root stress distribution, and auxiliaries (attachments, power ridges, divots, intermaxillary elastics, membrane thickness) are designed to rebalance stress and convert tipping into translation; CA exerts up to 8-11× PDL pressure on anchorage teeth vs fixed appliances during extraction-space closure.

## 한줄요약

29편 FEA 연구를 모은 PROSPERO 등록 체계적 문헌고찰: 투명교정장치 (Clear Aligner, CA)의 모든 부작용(롤러코스터 효과, 고정원 손실, 경사이동, 회전)은 치관-치근 간 불균등 응력 분포에서 비롯되며, 보조장치(어태치먼트, 파워릿지, 디봇, 악간고무, 멤브레인 두께)는 응력을 재균형화해 경사이동을 평행이동으로 바꾸는 역할을 한다; 발치공간 폐쇄 시 CA는 고정성 장치 대비 치주인대에 최대 8-11배 압력을 가한다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic review to map the **entire FEA-simulated clear-aligner biomechanics literature** (29 studies) organized by the three orthodontic movement directions (mesiodistal, buccolingual, occlusal/vertical).
- Establishes a unifying mechanistic thesis: CA delivers force only to crowns via shape-memory membrane → inherent **unbalanced crown-vs-root stress** → torque → all characteristic side effects.
- Catalogs each auxiliary class (attachments by shape/position, power ridges, divots, intermaxillary/intramaxillary elastic systems, membrane thickness and soft/hard layering) against the specific side effect it offsets.
- Documents the **roller-coaster effect (RCE)** during extraction-space closing and the anchorage strategies (direct/moderate/indirect strong anchorage) that mitigate it.
- Quantifies the periodontal cost: CA imposes up to **8-11× PDL pressure** on anchorage teeth vs fixed appliances during extraction space closing, with maximum PDL stress proportional to CA thickness.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **PICO**: P = malocclusion patients; I = CA orthodontic treatment; C = different CA/auxiliary designs; O = FEA biomechanics (Von Mises stress, maximum principal stress, displacement).
- **Databases**: Web of Science + PubMed; keywords "clear aligner" AND "finite element" (+ synonyms bracketless/invisible/removable/clear/Invisalign).
- **Screening**: 186 records → 35 duplicates removed → exclusions (2 reviews, 2 non-English, 102 irrelevant) → 45 full-text → 13 excluded (no FEA data) + 3 excluded (FEA-vs-experimental only) → **29 included**.
- **Two independent reviewers** (H.C., X.H.); disagreements by discussion; verification by L.Y., D.L.
- **Risk of bias**: graded with SBU (Swedish Council on Technology Assessment) + CRD (Centre for Reviews and Dissemination) system — all 29 articles graded "B" (moderate reliability).
- FEA software in included studies: **ABAQUS (13/29), Ansys (13/29)**.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Movement distribution**: 18 mesiodistal (9 extraction closure, 6 non-extraction space opening, 3 scattered diastema), 13 buccolingual (8 torque/bodily, 5 rotation), 8 occlusal (4 extrusion, 4 intrusion).
- **Data**: 27/29 report tooth displacement, 24/29 report stress (20/24 of PDL); 20/29 model PDL, all with linear elastic model.
- **Membrane thickness**: 0.75 mm CA induces less incisal tipping and more even PDL stress than 0.5 mm; max PDL stress proportional to thickness; multi-layer CA with >50% soft layer reduces lingual inclination of anteriors and mesial tipping of posteriors. Seo 2021: 0.75 vs 0.5 mm → 6% / 0.03% higher principal PDL stress for lingual inclination / axial rotation.
- **Power ridge**: height adjusts Crot location (root apex = controlled tipping; further = bodily retraction) without bowing or Von Mises change; optimal height relative to CA thickness.
- **Rotation**: posterior rotation activation should not exceed 1.2°; under 1.2°, efficiency higher with attachment on single target tooth than on target + proximal teeth.
- **Extrusion**: horizontal rectangular attachment = highest incisal-edge stress concentration (best efficiency); rectangular palatal attachment more effective than buccal; mandibular incisor extrusion more efficient than maxillary.
- **PDL**: CA imposes up to 8-11× PDL pressure on anchorage teeth vs FA in extraction closure; but CA gives lower periodontitis/gingivitis risk, lower gingival/plaque index, probing depth, sulcus bleeding than FA.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **No meta-analysis possible** — no unified standard FEA protocols, analytical parameters, or data presentation forms across studies.
- Only **initial instantaneous effects** modeled; no long-term biomechanical analysis.
- No biological events modeled (e.g., crown/root length); a recent study shows crown length closely relates to CA FEA outcomes — flagged as future direction.
- All PDL models linear elastic, though non-linear is reported more accurate/reliable.
- Heatmap reporting inconsistencies: missing scale bars/units, low resolution.
- Material fatigue / thermal aging (Kuntz et al., 80 samples, 5 brands — Angel® showed significant property reduction) under-evaluated.

## 7. Glossary

- **CA** — Clear Aligner (투명교정장치)
- **FA** — Fixed Appliance (고정성 교정장치)
- **FEA / FEM** — Finite Element Analysis / Method (유한요소해석)
- **RCE** — Roller-Coaster Effect: lingual/distal tipping + extrusion of anteriors and mesial tipping + intrusion of posteriors during extraction-space closure
- **PDL** — Periodontal Ligament (치주인대)
- **PETG / TPU** — Poly-ethylene terephthalate glycol / Thermoplastic polyurethane (aligner membrane polymers)
- **Cres / Crot** — Center of resistance / Center of rotation
- **Power ridge** — labial-surface ridge that generates buccolingual root torque
- **Divot** — labial surface indentation generating contact load (alternative to attachment)
- **AMS** — Advanced Mandibular Spring
- **Von Mises stress / Maximum principal stress** — equivalent and principal stress measures used in FEA outputs
