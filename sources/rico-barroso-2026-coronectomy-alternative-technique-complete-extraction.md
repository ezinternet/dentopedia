## Why Ingested

[[wiki/oral-surgery/cervera-espert-2016-coronectomy-mandibular-third-molar-sr]] is now a decade old; this 2026 systematic review (16 primary studies, 2551 patients) refreshes the head-to-head coronectomy-vs-extraction nerve-injury comparison with newer cohorts (incl. Hamad 2024, n=422). It extends the anchor by giving current group-stratified IAN/LN injury and failure-conversion numbers for clinical counseling.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (16 studies, 2551 patients, 2280 coronectomies vs 841 extractions): IAN injury 0.56% (coronectomy) vs 6.53% (extraction); 6.22% of coronectomies failed/converted; root migration the leading coronectomy-specific complication.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Systematic review (PRISMA), JBI Critical Appraisal for prevalence studies
- **Note on label**: PDF header states "Publication Types: Review"; methods describe a PRISMA systematic review with formal study selection (211→16) and risk-of-bias appraisal, so it is best classified as a systematic review (without meta-analysis) rather than a narrative review.
- **Journal**: Med Oral Patol Oral Cir Bucal 2026;31(1):e19–30
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane (search Oct 2024); 2005–2024, English/Spanish, ≥10 coronectomy cases, ≥6-month follow-up

## 2. Key Contributions

- Updated group-stratified nerve-injury comparison: coronectomy dramatically lowers IAN injury vs extraction.
- Quantifies coronectomy failure (intra-op conversion to extraction) at 6.22%, mostly from root mobilization.
- Documents that postoperative pain is similar between groups; root migration is the dominant coronectomy-specific issue.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 211 records → 39 after filters → 16 included (10 prospective cohorts, 3 RCTs, 2 case-control, 1 retrospective cohort).
- 2551 patients; 2280 coronectomies, 841 complete extractions.
- Follow-up 6–144 months; JBI prevalence appraisal (most studies 100% "Yes", very low risk; one medium-risk).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **IAN injury**: coronectomy **0.56%** vs extraction **6.53%**; permanent IAN injury **0.13%** vs **1.18%**.
- **Lingual nerve injury**: extraction 0.12%, coronectomy 0.26% (infrequent, transient in both).
- **Postoperative pain** (most common in both): extraction 15.69% (132/841), coronectomy 14.21% (324/2280); mean duration 3.40 vs 2.61 days; mean VAS 1.18 vs 1.31.
- **Coronectomy failure/conversion: 6.22%** (144/2280), mostly intra-op root mobilization.
- **Root migration**: leading coronectomy-specific complication, mainly first 6 months.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only PubMed + Cochrane searched; no meta-analytic pooling.
- Mixed primary designs (mostly observational); migration definitions vary.
- Long-term retained-root fate beyond migration period underreported.

## 7. Glossary

- **MTM**: mandibular third molar.
- **IAN / LN**: inferior alveolar nerve / lingual nerve.
- **Coronectomy failure**: intra-op root mobilization forcing conversion to complete extraction.
- **JBI appraisal**: Joanna Briggs Institute critical-appraisal tool for prevalence studies.
