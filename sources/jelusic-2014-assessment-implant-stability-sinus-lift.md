## One-line Summary
Prospective clinical study (44 test + 48 control patients, 170 Astra Tech implants): ISQ values **equal between augmented and non-augmented posterior maxilla** at 4 months post-placement, regardless of grafting material (β-TCP vs 60% HA + 40% β-TCP).

## 1. Document Information
Original research, University of Zagreb. Bilingual Croatian/English. Astra Tech OsseoSpeed implants used in all groups.

## 2. Key Contributions
- ISQ at 4 months post-placement **statistically equivalent** between sinus-augmented and non-augmented posterior maxilla (p=0.789)
- Choice of grafting material (pure β-TCP vs HA+β-TCP) does not affect ISQ outcome (p=0.697 and p=0.402 vs control)
- Implants in augmented sites can be prosthetically loaded as predictably as those in native bone

## 3. Methodology
- **Test group**: 44 healthy patients indicated for sinus lift
  - 46 implants placed after pure β-TCP augmentation (Bioresorb, Sybron Implant Solutions)
  - 39 implants placed after 60% HA + 40% β-TCP augmentation
- **Control group**: 48 patients, 85 implants in non-augmented lateral maxilla
- 6-month sinus healing → implant placement
- CBCT before placement to exclude graft dislocation
- RFA (Osstell ISQ) measurements at 4 months after implant placement

## 4. Key Results
- No implant loss in any group
- No statistically significant ISQ difference between augmented vs non-augmented (p=0.789)
- No difference between specific graft material vs corresponding non-augmented (p=0.697 / p=0.402)

## 5. Limitations
- Only one time point (4 months post-placement) — no trajectory data
- Specific to Astra Tech OsseoSpeed surface
- Two-stage protocol (delayed implant placement)
- Croatian single-center study
