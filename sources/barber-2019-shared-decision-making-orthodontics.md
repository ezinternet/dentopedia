## 2. Key Contributions

- Frames shared decision-making (SDM) as making decisions **'with' rather than 'about'** patients, applied to the orthodontic setting.
- Outlines the **key stages** of the SDM process as a structure clinicians can follow.
- Identifies the **main barriers** to SDM adoption in orthodontics.
- Identifies **evidence gaps** that must be filled before SDM can be fully integrated into orthodontic practice.

## 7. Glossary

- **Shared decision-making (SDM):** collaborative process where clinician and patient make a healthcare decision together, integrating the best evidence with the patient's values and preferences — deciding 'with' rather than 'about' the patient.
- **Decision aid:** tool (leaflet, web app, etc.) presenting treatment options and their outcomes/trade-offs to support a patient in deliberating choices.
- **Patient preferences:** the patient's values, priorities, and acceptable trade-offs that should shape preference-sensitive treatment choices.
- **Preference-sensitive decision:** a decision with more than one reasonable option whose "best" choice depends on the patient's values (e.g., extraction vs. non-extraction orthodontics).
