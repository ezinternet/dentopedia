## Why Ingested

Implant macrogeometry (tapered vs cylindrical) is routinely cited as a primary-stability lever, but whether it actually changes the early-healing stability trajectory — versus surgical technique — is contested. This Evidence-Based Dentistry critical summary (Mourão & Dos Santos Pereira 2025) appraises a parallel-group RCT (Charoenniwassakul et al., J Clin Periodontol 2025) tracking MIT/ISQ/IST over 6 weeks, providing an implant-design early-healing-stability sibling to [[wiki/implants/isq/monje-2025-implant-design-stability-early-healing-rct]]. The appraisal's bottom line — surgical technique outweighs macrogeometry, and osteotomy non-standardization confounds design attribution — is a direct caution for any threshold/loading-decision page.

## One-line Summary

EBD critical summary (Mourão & Dos Santos Pereira 2025) appraising a 2-arm RCT of tapered vs cylindrical implants over 6 weeks of early healing: tapered gave higher insertion torque (MIT) but cylindrical gave higher ISQ/IST at 6 weeks; surgical technique mattered more than macrogeometry, and unstandardized osteotomy confounds the design comparison.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides an independent critical appraisal of an RCT directly comparing **tapered vs cylindrical** implant macrogeometry on early-healing stability.
- Highlights a clinically important dissociation: **mechanical primary stability (insertion torque)** does not track with **resonance-frequency / percussion secondary stability** between designs.
- Foregrounds the methodological caveat that **osteotomy preparation was not standardized** between groups, so observed differences cannot be attributed to implant design alone.
- Concludes that **surgical technique had a greater impact on primary stability than implant macrogeometry** — repositioning macrogeometry as a secondary lever in good-quality healed bone.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Appraised RCT design (as summarized in the commentary):
- **Design**: randomized clinical trial, two parallel groups (tapered vs cylindrical implants).
- **Follow-up**: six weeks of healing.
- **Stability outcomes**: Maximum Insertion Torque (MIT); Implant Stability Quotient via resonance frequency analysis (ISQ); Implant Stability Test via percussion analysis (IST).
- **Population**: healthy adults with posterior edentulous areas healed ≥2 months, no need for bone grafting.
- **Exclusions**: smokers; uncontrolled systemic disease; conditions affecting bone metabolism; maxillomandibular pathologies. (Favors experimental control but limits applicability to complex cases.)
- **Analysis**: repeated-measures ANOVA; multiple imputation for missing follow-up data; correlations evaluated between under-preparation, torque, and secondary stability; mechanical stability interpreted separately from biological stability to characterize the healing transition.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Tapered implants demonstrated **significantly higher MIT** (maximum insertion torque).
- Cylindrical implants presented **higher ISQ and IST** values at the end of six weeks.
- **Both** implant types exhibited the physiological pattern of **transient stability reduction** (the early-healing stability dip).
- The **lack of standardization in osteotomy preparation** between groups prevents differences from being attributed exclusively to implant design.
- Both designs achieved **adequate stability for early loading** in healed bone of good quality.
- **Surgical technique had a greater impact on primary stability than implant macrogeometry.**

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only ingest — full commentary text not retrieved; numeric effect sizes and CIs unavailable.
- The appraised RCT did not standardize osteotomy preparation between arms, confounding design attribution.
- Strict inclusion (good-quality healed bone, non-smokers, systemically healthy) limits generalizability.
- Findings explicitly **do not apply** to immediate loading, regenerated bone, or low-density bone sites.
- Future work should standardize drilling protocol to isolate macrogeometry, and extend to immediate-loading / poor-bone scenarios.

## 7. Glossary

- **MIT (Maximum Insertion Torque)**: peak torque recorded during implant placement; a mechanical proxy for primary stability.
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: 1–100 stability scale from resonance frequency analysis (RFA).
- **IST (Implant Stability Test)**: stability value derived from percussion analysis.
- **Macrogeometry**: gross implant shape (tapered vs cylindrical body, thread design).
- **Primary stability**: mechanical anchorage at placement; **Secondary stability**: biological anchorage from osseointegration during healing.
- **Stability dip**: transient drop in total stability during early healing as primary stability decreases before secondary stability rises.
- **Under-preparation**: osteotomy drilled narrower than implant to raise insertion torque/primary stability.
