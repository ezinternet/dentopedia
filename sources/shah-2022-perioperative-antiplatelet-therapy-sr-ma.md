## One-line Summary
ACCP 가이드라인 근거 SR+MA: 장기 항혈소판제 (APT) 복용 환자 비심장 수술 — 짧은 vs 긴 중단 기간 출혈 차이 NS (low COE); aspirin 지속 시 위약 대비 주요 출혈 증가 (RR 1.31, high COE) + 주요 혈전증 감소 (RR 0.74, high COE).

## 1. Document Information
- Authors: Shah S et al.; senior Murad MH
- Type: Systematic review + meta-analysis (supports ACCP guideline development)
- Lit search: inception to July 2020

## 2. Key Contributions
1. SR-level evidence base for ACCP perioperative APT guideline.
2. Quantifies aspirin continuation trade-off: bleeding +31% vs thromboembolism -26%.
3. Demonstrates lack of significant difference between short vs long APT interruption windows (low certainty of evidence, COE).

## 3. Methodology
- Databases: MEDLINE, EMBASE, Scopus, Cochrane
- Search through July 16, 2020
- Outcomes: major bleeding, major thromboembolism
- Population: long-term APT, elective surgery
- Statistics: meta-analysis where possible

## 4. Key Results
[근거강함]
| Comparison | Outcome | Effect | COE |
|---|---|---|---|
| Shorter vs longer APT interruption | Major bleeding | NS | low |
| Aspirin continued vs placebo | Major bleeding | RR 1.31 (1.15-1.50) | high |
| Aspirin continued vs placebo | Major thromboembolism | RR 0.74 | (favors continuation) |

## 5. Limitations
- Most evidence in non-dental/non-cardiac surgery; extrapolation to dental extraction requires care
- Few RCTs comparing specific APT interruption windows
- DOAC era data limited at the time of cutoff (2020)
