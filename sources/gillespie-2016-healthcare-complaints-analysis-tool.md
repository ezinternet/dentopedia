## Why Ingested
HCAT is the working instrument of the classification axis -- it turns the [[complaint-management/bmjqs-2013-002437]] taxonomy into a reliable coding tool with severity, stage-of-care, and harm. For a dental clinic this is the template for a structured complaint log rather than free-text notes.

## One-line Summary
Development and reliability study creating HCAT: 7 problem categories x 4 severity levels, plus stage-of-care and harm coding, validated on UK national complaint samples.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Introduces the novel idea that complaints should be coded by *severity*, not just presence/absence.
- Defines 7 conceptually distinct problem categories: quality, safety, environment, institutional processes, listening, communication, respect & patient rights.
- Adds orthogonal coding of stage of care and degree of harm.
- Provides a reliability-tested, transferable instrument suitable for organisational learning and trend monitoring.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Taxonomy from the 2014 systematic review refined through three iterations of discriminant content validity testing with educated lay raters (n=58, 58, 55). Explicit coding procedures added for 7 categories (4 severity levels each), stage of care, and harm. Iteratively refined by coding UK national complaint samples (n=25, 80, 137, 839). Final reliability test: 14 lay raters coded a referent sample of 125 complaints.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
The 7 HCAT problem categories were confirmed conceptually distinct. Raters identified on average 1.94 problems (SD=0.26) per complaint letter. Reliability and accuracy reached acceptable levels for service monitoring across the three domains (clinical, management, relationships).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
Validated with educated lay raters on UK letters; transferability to other systems/languages required separate testing (later done in Denmark). Severity coding adds rater burden. Letters as a data source under-capture complaints made verbally or not escalated.

## 6. Related Work
Operationalises Reader, Gillespie & Roberts (2014). Subsequently validated by Bogh et al (2019) on Danish compensation claims and applied in safety-linkage and policy studies by the same LSE/Imperial group.

## 7. Glossary
- **HCAT**: Healthcare Complaints Analysis Tool.
- **Severity**: 4-level rating of how serious a coded problem is within a category.
- **Discriminant content validity**: testing whether raters reliably assign items to the intended category and not others.
