## Why Ingested

This RCT (Lee 2020, Seoul National University) tests the **Watanabe method (toothpick method)** of professional toothbrushing as a once-weekly adjunct to SRP in T2DM chronic periodontitis — a clinical application of the toothpick brushing technique to a high-risk systemic population, where the mechanical-stimulation/wound-healing effect (not just plaque removal) is the rationale. It extends [[periodontics/baeza-2020-effect-periodontal-treatment-patients-periodontitis]] (SRP lowers HbA1c in T2DM) by adding the toothpick-method arm, and complements [[drug/enteghad-2024-diabetes-mellitus-periodontal-periimplant-disease-review]] on the bidirectional diabetes–periodontitis link.

## One-line Summary

Double-blind RCT (n=60 T2DM chronic-periodontitis patients, 3 arms, 12 wk): both SRP and SRP + Watanabe-method toothpick toothbrushing (SRPAT, once weekly x5) improved HbA1c and periodontal status, but only SRPAT significantly reduced serum endotoxin (-0.78 EU/mL, p=0.003) and produced significantly greater BOP reduction (-16.5% vs SRP -7.3%).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Directly tests whether **adding professional toothbrushing by the Watanabe / toothpick method** to standard SRP confers extra benefit in a systemically high-risk group (T2DM chronic periodontitis).
- The rationale for the toothpick method is not plaque removal alone but **mechanical stimulation that mimics the repair phase of wound healing** — prior histology shows it raises gingival basement-cell proliferation ~2.5x vs a scaler and activates (procollagen-positive, PCNA-positive) fibroblasts and junctional epithelium.
- Uses **systemic** inflammatory readouts (serum endotoxin, serum IL-1β) and HbA1c rather than only local gingival-crevicular-fluid markers.
- Finds the toothpick-method adjunct (SRPAT) uniquely lowered serum endotoxin and gave superior bleeding control — relevant to diabetic patients' infection/bleeding susceptibility.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: double-blind, parallel-group RCT; community-health-centre maintenance patients (Gwangjin-gu Public Health Centre, Seoul). June 2013–June 2014.
- **Participants**: 60 T2DM patients analysed (20/group; 15 dropped out of an initially larger pool due to age and long intervention period). Estimated 72 needed (α 0.05, power 0.80, effect size 0.40).
- **Inclusion**: PD >5 mm + attachment loss in ≥2 quadrants; BOP at those sites; ≥20 remaining teeth; HbA1c ≥6.5%; non-smoker. **Exclusion**: alcohol/drug abuse, chronic liver disease, BMI ≥40.
- **Randomization**: by rolling a dice into 3 arms.
- **Arms**:
  - **CTR** (control): diabetes medical screening only; no periodontal treatment (treated after study).
  - **SRP**: supragingival scaling at visit 1, root planing 2 weeks later, recall/additional treatment at 12 wk.
  - **SRPAT**: SRP **plus** additional toothbrushing by the **Watanabe method using a 2-row toothbrush**, performed by a trained dentist **once a week from visit 1 through visit 5** (≥5 weekly professional toothbrushing sessions); subgingival calculus removed at visit 2.
- **All groups** received identical baseline oral-health education + toothbrush instruction to remove behavioural bias.
- **Outcomes**: primary — HbA1c, serum endotoxin (LAL/Pyrotell-T turbidimetric), serum IL-1β (high-sensitivity ELISA). Periodontal — PD (UNC-15 probe, 6 sites/tooth at 6 index teeth), calculus index (CI), BOP. Follow-up 12 weeks. Intra-examiner kappa 0.976.
- **Stats**: repeated-measures ANOVA; paired t-test / Wilcoxon for within-group; Tukey / Mann-Whitney for between-group; Bonferroni-Holm adjustment in figures.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Baseline**: no significant inter-group difference in any variable (all p>0.05).

**HbA1c (baseline → 3 mo, change, within-group p)**: SRP 6.64 → 6.47 (−0.17, p=0.009); SRPAT 6.68 → 6.43 (−0.25, p=0.001); CTR 6.76 → 6.98 (**+0.22**, p=0.001). Between-group p=0.000. Both treatment arms lowered HbA1c; control worsened.

**Serum endotoxin (EU/mL)**: SRP 1.48 → 1.08 (−0.39, p=0.287, NS within-group); **SRPAT 1.70 → 0.92 (−0.78, p=0.003)**; CTR 1.49 → 2.35 (**+0.91**, p=0.004). Between-group p=0.000. **Only SRPAT achieved a significant within-group endotoxin decrease.**

**Serum IL-1β**: no significant change within or between any group (between-group p=0.404).

**BOP (%)**: SRP 18.93 → 11.66 (−7.27, p=0.013); **SRPAT 22.25 → 5.73 (−16.52, p<0.001)**; CTR 21.29 → 22.66 (+1.37, p=0.524, NS). SRPAT BOP reduction significantly greater than SRP (p<0.025).

**PD**: SRP −0.08 (p=0.043); SRPAT −0.09 (p=0.039); CTR +0.18 (p=0.001). **CI**: SRP −1.90; SRPAT −1.55; CTR +0.50 (NS).

**Correlations**: baseline endotoxin strongly correlated with PD (rho 0.809, p=0.000). Changes in endotoxin and HbA1c correlated with changes in PD/CI/BOP.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Preliminary report; short 12-week follow-up (chosen for participant safety in diabetics).
- Older population (high-age public-health-centre attendees) with complex comorbidity → potential bias; 15 dropouts.
- **No "professional toothbrushing only" arm matched on visit number** — SRPAT had more visits than SRP, so the extra benefit cannot be fully separated from visit frequency (authors cite ethical impossibility of a toothbrushing-only group, as no usual care equivalent exists).
- All participants (incl. controls) were health-conscious regular attendees → behavioural bias.
- Modest HbA1c effect (~0.2–0.25%), consistent with SR estimate (~0.4%).
- Future: longer follow-up, standardized equal visit numbers, larger samples.

## 7. Glossary

- **Watanabe method (toothpick method / toothpick brushing)**: a manual toothbrushing technique using a 2-row toothbrush in which bristles are inserted interproximally like a toothpick; produces mechanical stimulation of the gingiva that promotes fibroblast and junctional-epithelium proliferation (wound-healing-like effect) in addition to plaque removal.
- **SRPAT**: scaling and root planing with additional toothbrushing (the Watanabe-method adjunct arm).
- **HbA1c**: glycated haemoglobin; reflects average glycaemia over ~2–3 months.
- **Serum endotoxin (LPS)**: lipopolysaccharide from Gram-negative bacteria; systemic marker linked to metabolic endotoxaemia; measured by LAL (Limulus amebocyte lysate) turbidimetric assay.
- **BOP**: bleeding on probing.
- **CI**: calculus index (oral hygiene index criteria).
- **PD**: periodontal pocket depth.
