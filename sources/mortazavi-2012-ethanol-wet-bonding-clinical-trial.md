## One-line Summary
RCT (36 NCCLs, 12 patients, 12 months): ethanol wet bonding, OptiBond FL (etch-and-rinse), Clearfil SE Bond — all 100% retention at 6mo; ethanol wet group 91.67% at 12mo; no significant difference between groups (p=0.358).

## 1. Document Information
Isfahan University of Medical Sciences, Iran. Dental Research Journal 2012. 1-year RCT on noncarious cervical lesions comparing ethanol wet bonding vs conventional adhesives.

## 2. Key Contributions
- One of few clinical RCTs on ethanol wet bonding technique (EWB)
- EWB technique: substitutes water in water-saturated dentin with ethanol → more hydrophobic bonding zone → theoretically better durability
- At 12 months: EWB retention slightly lower (91.67%) but difference NS (p=0.358)
- All groups: no marginal defects, discoloration, or caries at 12 months
- Postoperative sensitivity: zero in all groups

## 3. Methodology
- 36 noncarious cervical lesions (NCCLs), 12 patients
- 3 groups: (1) OptiBond FL (etch-and-rinse), (2) Clearfil SE Bond + enamel etch, (3) EWB with OptiBond FL adhesive component
- Composite resin restoration; evaluation at 24h, 6, 9, 12 months
- USPHS criteria: marginal discoloration, marginal defect, retention, caries, postoperative sensitivity
- Tooth vitality assessed

## 4. Key Results
| Group | 6mo retention | 12mo retention |
|---|---|---|
| OptiBond FL (E&R) | 100% | 100% |
| Clearfil SE Bond | 100% | 100% |
| Ethanol wet bonding | 100% | 91.67% |

- No significant difference between groups at 12 months (p=0.358)
- All teeth vital throughout study

## 5. Limitations
- Small sample (n=36 lesions, 12 patients)
- 12-month follow-up only — durability at 2–5yr unknown
- EWB technique variability (clinician dependent)
- Single-center, Iran

## 6. Related Work
- Tay & Pashley 2003 — theoretical basis: hydrophilic adhesives degrade; EWB addresses this
- Daneshkazemi 2013 — in vitro: Clearfil SE Bond µTBS degrades with cycling
- Oza 2022 — 2-year RCT on universal adhesive modes in NCCLs

## 7. Glossary
- **EWB**: Ethanol wet bonding — dentin progressively dehydrated with ethanol → hydrophobic
- **NCCL**: Noncarious cervical lesion
- **USPHS**: United States Public Health Service criteria (clinical evaluation standard)
- **OptiBond FL**: 3-step etch-and-rinse adhesive (Kerr); considered gold standard E&R
- **Clearfil SE Bond**: 2-step self-etch adhesive (Kuraray)
