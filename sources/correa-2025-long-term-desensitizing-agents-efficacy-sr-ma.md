## Why Ingested

지각과민(DH) **치료법의 장기(≥6개월) 효능을 작용기전별로 순위화**한 SR+MA — 기전·진단을 다룬 종설([[dentin-hypersensitivity/kaur-2026-dentin-hypersensitivity-enigma-revisited]], [[dentin-hypersensitivity/li-2026-physical-chemical-strategies-dentin-hypersensitivity]])에 빠져 있던 "어느 치료가 오래 가는가"의 정량 근거를 보완. 레이저 단독 SR-MA([[dentin-hypersensitivity/faraoni-2023-laser-therapy-dentin-hypersensitivity-sr-ma]])를 전(全) 제제 비교로 확장.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (22 studies / 15 meta-analyzed; RCTs, ≥6-month follow-up, 1991–2024): for long-term dentin-hypersensitivity relief, glutaraldehyde and low-level laser therapy achieved the largest effect sizes, whereas adhesive systems and calcium-phosphate–based agents showed no statistically significant long-term benefit (abstract-only).

## 한줄요약

SR+MA(22편/메타 15편, RCT·최소 6개월 추적, 1991–2024): 지각과민 장기 완화에서 **글루타르알데히드(Gluma 계열)와 저출력 레이저(LLLT)가 최대 효과크기**, 반면 **접착시스템·칼슘인산염계는 통계적으로 유의한 장기 효과 없음** (초록 기반).

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (PRISMA; PROSPERO CRD42020210693)
- **Journal**: Journal of Dentistry 2025;163:106186 · **PMID** 41139001 · **DOI** 10.1016/j.jdent.2025.106186
- **Artifact**: pubmed-text, **abstract-only** (no PMC OA full text; figures/forest-plot detail beyond abstract not captured)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA focused specifically on the **long-term (≥6 month)** efficacy of DH desensitizing agents, grouped by **mechanism of action** (tubular occlusion, neural effect, or both).
- Ranks agents by effect size: **glutaraldehyde** and **low-level laser therapy (LLLT)** are the long-term winners.
- Flags **adhesive systems** and **calcium-phosphate–based agents** (e.g., CPP-ACP) as lacking statistically significant long-term benefit — a caution against assuming short-term relief persists.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Databases: PubMed/MEDLINE, Cochrane CENTRAL, Embase, Scopus, Web of Science, Lilacs/BBO; last search January 2025.
- Inclusion: adults with DH, **RCTs**, treatment with desensitizing agents, **minimum 6-month follow-up**. Studies 1991–2024.
- 22 studies in the SR; **15 in the meta-analysis**.
- Outcome: pain intensity from **evaporative, cold, or tactile** stimuli; pooled as **bias-corrected standardized mean change (SMC)** with 95% CI.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Several desensitizing agents produced significant long-term pain reductions.
- **Largest effect sizes: glutaraldehyde and LLLT.**
- **Adhesive systems and calcium-phosphate–based agents: NOT statistically significant** at long-term follow-up.
- Clinical message: prefer glutaraldehyde or LLLT when durable relief is the goal; select adhesives/CaP cautiously given limited long-term evidence.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only ingest — exact SMC values, per-agent CIs, heterogeneity (I²), and risk-of-bias detail not captured beyond the abstract.
- Long-term RCTs (≥6 mo) for DH are relatively few; agent classes are unevenly represented.
- Does not rank by onset/short-term relief (cf. kinetic trials) — "long-term winner" ≠ "fastest onset."

## 7. Glossary

- **Tubular occlusion (세관 폐쇄)**: blocking open dentinal tubules to stop fluid movement (hydrodynamic theory) — e.g., glutaraldehyde-protein precipitation, mineralizing agents.
- **Neural mechanism (신경 차단)**: reducing nerve excitability (e.g., potassium salts).
- **Glutaraldehyde (글루타르알데히드)**: protein-coagulating agent (Gluma-type) that occludes tubules.
- **LLLT (저출력 레이저)**: low-level laser therapy; analgesic/biomodulatory desensitization.
- **SMC (표준화 평균 변화량)**: standardized mean change, a within-subject effect-size metric.
