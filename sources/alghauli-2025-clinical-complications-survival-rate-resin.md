## Why Ingested

기존 [[wiki/inlay/bustamante-2020-ceramic-hybrid-composite-onlays-sr-ma]] (2020)는 온레이 위주로 세라믹·하이브리드·복합레진을 비교했지만 RMC 크라운, 라미네이트 베니어, 교합면 베니어까지 포괄한 종합 SR+MA는 없었다. 본 Alghauli 2025 SR+MA(21편, 2024년 10월 검색)는 레진 매트릭스 세라믹(PICN·RNC) 전체 수복물 유형의 합병증·생존율을 처음으로 망라하여 재료 선택 권고의 근거를 확장한다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (21 studies, 2024 search) of resin matrix ceramic (PICN and RNC) restorations: RMC crowns showed higher complications than conventional ceramics; veneers and intracoronal restorations were comparable to lithium disilicate; overall outcomes depend on restoration size and cavity design.

## 한줄요약

레진 매트릭스 세라믹(Resin Matrix Ceramic, RMC) 수복물 SR+MA (21편): RMC 크라운은 세라믹 대비 합병증 높음; 베니어·인트라코로날 수복물은 리튬 디실리케이트(Lithium Disilicate, LD)와 유사; 생존율은 수복물 크기·형태에 의존.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of Esthetic and Restorative Dentistry, Volume 37, Issue 7, July 2025, pp. 1721–1739
- **First published**: 04 April 2025
- **DOI**: 10.1111/jerd.13471
- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis (SR+MA)
- **Search date**: October 2024
- **Databases**: PubMed, Scopus, Cochrane
- **Materials studied**: Polymer Infiltrated Ceramic Network (PICN) — e.g., Vita Enamic, Lava Ultimate — and Resin Nano Ceramic (RNC) — e.g., Ceram.x

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA to evaluate RMC restorations comprehensively across ALL restoration types: full/partial crowns, laminate veneers, occlusal veneers, inlays, onlays, and implant-supported restorations
- Stratified analysis by restoration type reveals that RMC material performance is strongly dependent on restoration geometry (size and cavity design)
- RMC crowns (full and partial coverage): higher complication rates vs conventional ceramic restorations
- RMC veneers (laminate and occlusal): comparable outcomes to lithium disilicate — favorable niche
- RMC intracoronal restorations (inlays/onlays): similar to lithium disilicate inlays/onlays; better than direct composite
- Provides clinical guidance distinguishing RMC "good indications" (veneers, intracoronal) from "poor indications" (crowns)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PRISMA 2020 framework
- **Inclusion criteria**: Clinical studies (RCTs, prospective, retrospective) evaluating complications of PICN and RNC restorations
- **Risk of bias tools**: Cochrane RoB 2 (for RCTs), ROBINS-I (for non-randomized studies)
- **Meta-analysis**: Review Manager (RevMan); inverse variance method; fixed effects model; results as risk ratios (RR) with 95% CI; α ≤ 0.05
- **Studies included**: 21 studies
- **Study types included**: RCTs and prospective/retrospective clinical studies
- **Restoration types covered**: Full crowns, partial crowns (onlays/endocrowns), laminate veneers, occlusal veneers, inlays, implant-supported crowns

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Crowns (full and partial coverage)
- RMC full and partial crowns exhibited **higher rates of clinical complications** compared to conventional ceramic restorations (lithium disilicate)
- This finding was consistent across the included RCTs and prospective studies
- Poorer success and survival rates associated with full and partial coverage crown designs

### Veneers (laminate and occlusal)
- Laminate veneers: **comparable outcomes** between RMC and lithium disilicate materials
- Occlusal veneers: **comparable outcomes** between RMC and lithium disilicate materials
- RMC veneers demonstrated favorable clinical performance — a valid indication

### Intracoronal Restorations (inlays/onlays)
- RMC intracoronal restorations showed clinical complications **similar to lithium disilicate** inlays and onlays
- RMC intracoronal restorations showed **lower complications** compared to direct composite restorations
- Preferred over composite for indirect intracoronal restorations

### Implant-Supported Restorations
- Some included studies evaluated PICN/RNC on implant abutments
- Comparable peri-implant bone levels and periodontal parameters reported vs lithium disilicate in at least one RCT (Mahmoud 2024)
- Adhesive bonding to zirconia abutments showed some failures (Schepke 2016, 2018)

### Overall Conclusions
- Overall success and survival rates of RMC: **dependent on restoration size and cavity design**
- Poorer outcomes in full/partial crown format; favorable in veneers and intracoronal restorations
- 21 studies included — limitation: many included studies have short to medium follow-up; long-term RCT data lacking

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Heterogeneity in included studies (material brands, follow-up duration, operator skill, tooth location)
- Most included studies have short to medium follow-up (1–5 years); lack of long-term ≥10 year data
- Operator variability (experience, bonding protocol) significantly influences outcomes — not always controlled
- Absence of standardized complication classification across studies complicates pooling
- Recommendation: long-term randomized clinical trials to provide further evidence for RMC indications

## 6. Related Work

- Bustamante-Hernández 2020 (SR+MA, IJERPH): onlays only; ceramic > hybrid > composite; overall 94.2% survival — predecessor narrower in scope
- Al-Haj Husain 2020 (SR+MA, JCM): hybrid CAD-CAM partial/full-coverage restorations — overlapping population
- Fathy 2022 (SR, COI): RMC partial coverage restorations only — narrower scope
- Spitznagel et al. (2018, 2020): PICN single-crown prospective studies (3–5 year) — included in this SR+MA
- Hassan 2024 (RCT): lithium disilicate vs RNC nanoceramic onlays — included in this SR+MA
- Morimoto 2016 (SR+MA): inlay/onlay/overlay survival — predecessor for benchmark comparison
- Klein 2025 (veneers SR+MA, JERD): feldspathic/leucite/lithium disilicate veneers — complements the veneer subsection

## 7. Glossary

- **RMC** — Resin Matrix Ceramic; umbrella term for hybrid ceramic materials with an interpenetrating polymer-ceramic network or ceramic particles in resin matrix
- **PICN** — Polymer Infiltrated Ceramic Network; dual-network (ceramic network + polymer network) — commercial examples: Vita Enamic, Lava Ultimate (considered PICN-type)
- **RNC** — Resin Nano Ceramic; ceramic nanoparticles in resin matrix — e.g., Ceram.x, LavaTM Ultimate (also classified here), Cerasmart
- **RoB 2** — Cochrane Risk of Bias 2 tool for randomized trials
- **ROBINS-I** — Risk of Bias In Non-randomized Studies of Interventions
- **RevMan** — Review Manager software by Cochrane for meta-analysis
- **RR** — Risk Ratio (relative risk), used as effect measure in this meta-analysis
- **Fixed effects model** — Statistical model assuming single true effect size; appropriate when studies are homogeneous
