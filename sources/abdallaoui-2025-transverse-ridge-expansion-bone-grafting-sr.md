## Why Ingested

Answers the recurring clinical question "graft or not when expanding a narrow ridge?" with a width-based threshold (≥3 mm → expansion alone is predictable; <3 mm → graft for stability/volume preservation). The wiki had individual split-crest case/cohort papers ([[wiki/bone-regeneration/ayoub-2018-ridge-splitting-horizontal-augmentation-case]], [[wiki/bone-regeneration/simion-1992-jawbone-enlargement-split-crest-gtr]], [[wiki/bone-regeneration/enislidis-2006-staged-ridge-splitting-implant-mandible]]) but no synthesis weighing the grafting decision across studies. This 15-study SR (which itself includes Simion 1992 and Enislidis 2006) reinforces those primary pages and gives the decision rule.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (PRISMA, 15 clinical studies — RCTs/cohorts/case series, n 20-120 each, baseline ridge 2.5-4.5 mm) of transverse ridge expansion (TRE) with vs without bone grafting: both achieve predictable widening 3.2-6.1 mm with implant survival 92-100%; grafting adds only a modest, clinically non-significant gain — TRE alone is reliable for ridges ≥3 mm, graft for <3 mm or long-term volume preservation.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(PRISMA, 임상연구 15편 — RCT/코호트/증례군, 기준 치조제 폭 2.5-4.5 mm): 횡적 치조제 확장술(Transverse Ridge Expansion, TRE)을 골이식 유무로 비교 — 양쪽 모두 3.2-6.1 mm 폭 증가와 임플란트 생존율 92-100% 달성, 골이식의 추가 이득은 통계적·임상적으로 미미. ≥3 mm 치조제는 확장술 단독으로 신뢰 가능, <3 mm 또는 장기 부피 보존이 필요할 때만 선택적으로 골이식.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Synthesizes 15 clinical studies (2000-2024) directly comparing TRE with vs without adjunctive bone grafting — a decision that had been clinician-preference-driven rather than evidence-anchored.
- Establishes a width threshold for the grafting decision: ridges ≥3 mm respond predictably to expansion alone; grafting is reserved for <3 mm or long-term volume preservation.
- Quantifies the grafting "benefit" as modest and clinically non-significant: grafted gain 4.5-6.1 mm vs non-grafted 3.2-5.0 mm, with identical implant survival.
- Recasts grafting from a routine adjunct to a selective, case-dependent strategy.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Databases: PubMed, Embase, Cochrane Library. Keywords: ("ridge expansion" OR "split-crest" OR "alveolar expansion") AND ("dental implant" OR "implant placement") AND ("bone graft" OR "bone regeneration"). Human, English, Jan 2000 - Dec 2024.
- PICO: horizontally deficient ridges needing implants; TRE + graft vs TRE without graft; outcomes = horizontal bone gain, implant stability, survival, marginal bone loss, complications.
- Inclusion: RCTs, prospective/retrospective cohorts, case series ≥10 patients, ≥6-month follow-up. Exclusion: animal/in vitro, case reports <10, narrative reviews, opinion, no outcome data.
- Selection: two reviewers, Cohen's kappa = 0.86.
- Identified 356 records → 48 full-text → 15 included.
- Risk of bias: RoB-2 (RCTs) and ROBINS-I (non-randomized).
- Note: the paper internally double-counts study designs (Results section: 5 RCTs / 6 prospective cohorts / 4 case series; Risk-of-bias section: 2 RCTs / 6 cohorts / 7 case series). Treat the design tally as approximate.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Horizontal bone gain: 3.2-6.1 mm overall. Grafted 4.5-6.1 mm; non-grafted 3.2-5.0 mm. Difference modest, not clinically significant.
- Primary stability (insertion torque/ISQ): trend toward higher in grafted groups; not statistically significant in most studies.
- Implant survival: 92-100%, no significant difference grafted vs non-grafted. Success (MBL <1.5 mm, no mobility, no infection) comparable.
- Complications: buccal plate fractures 4-10%; soft tissue dehiscence 3-8%; occasional autogenous graft resorption; non-grafted minor horizontal relapse <1 mm (grafted ridges more volume-stable). Overall rates similar.
- Biomaterials: xenografts (Bio-Oss) and alloplasts more dimensionally stable than autograft (autograft prone to partial resorption).
- Technique: piezosurgery consistently lowered complications and improved expansion control vs rotary burs/chisels.
- Site: TRE more effective/predictable in maxilla than mandible (lower cortical density, higher elasticity → fewer plate fractures).
- Threshold: ridges ≥3 mm → expansion alone predictable; <3 mm or need for long-term volume preservation → graft.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Methodological quality moderate-to-low; considerable heterogeneity in surgical technique, grafting materials, follow-up — precluded meta-analysis (qualitative synthesis only).
- Mostly small samples, short-to-mid-term follow-up. Many case series (moderate-to-high risk of bias); few RCTs.
- Internal inconsistency in study-design tally (see Methodology note).
- Future: standardized RCTs with uniform outcomes and long-term follow-up; grafted-vs-non-grafted comparisons stratified by ridge-width category to define thresholds precisely; integration of piezosurgery, digital planning, biologic adjuncts (PRGF, i-PRF).

## 7. Glossary

- TRE (Transverse Ridge Expansion / 횡적 치조제 확장술): controlled separation of buccal and lingual/palatal cortical plates to widen a narrow ridge for implant placement; synonymous with ridge splitting / split-crest in this context.
- Split-crest / Ridge split (치조정 분할): the crestal-incision variant of TRE; greenstick-fracture buccal plate hinged at the base.
- PRGF (Plasma Rich in Growth Factors / 성장인자 풍부 혈장): autologous platelet concentrate used as a biologic adjunct.
- i-PRF (injectable Platelet-Rich Fibrin / 주사형 혈소판 풍부 피브린): liquid PRF formulation for regenerative adjunct use.
- Piezosurgery (압전 수술): ultrasonic bone-cutting instrumentation associated with fewer plate fractures and better expansion control.
- DBBM / Bio-Oss (이종골이식재): deproteinized bovine bone mineral xenograft, more dimensionally stable than autograft here.
- ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient / 임플란트 안정성 지수): RFA-derived primary-stability metric.
