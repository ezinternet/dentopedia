## Why Ingested

Part of a focused collection on vertical ridge augmentation (VRA). This SR/MA is the first to take peri-implant bone loss (PBL) as the *primary* outcome across all VRA techniques, providing the synthesis backbone for the head-to-head Ti-mesh/PTFE RCT in [[implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/cucchi-2024-ptfe-mesh-ti-mesh-vertical-ridge-rct]] and the complication-focused MA in [[implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/tay-2022-healing-complications-vertical-gbr-sr-ma]].

## One-line Summary

SR/MA of 42 studies (11 RCTs) on VRA techniques: pooled peri-implant bone loss (PBL) 1.38 mm (95% CI 1.10–1.66) at mean 41.0 ± 27.8 months; PBL varied by technique — SBB 0.66 mm, GBR 1.06 mm, Onlay 1.31 mm, Inlay 1.72 mm, osteodistraction 1.81 mm.

## 1. Document Information

- Study type: Systematic review + meta-analysis (3 pairwise meta-analyses), PRISMA-compliant, PROSPERO-registered.
- Journal: Clinical Implant Dentistry and Related Research, 2024;26:15–65 (received 23 Mar 2023, accepted 12 Sep 2023, published in 2024 vol 26 issue 1).
- DOI: 10.1111/cid.13282
- Note: assigned stem year 2023 corrected to 2024 to match the journal publication (vol 26, 2024).

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR/MA to use peri-implant bone loss (PBL) after ≥12 months functional loading as the *primary* outcome across VRA techniques (prior reviews used VBG/complications as primary).
- Pools PBL by technique: GBR, Inlay, Onlay, osteodistraction, and split bone block (SBB).
- Reports secondary outcomes: vertical bone gain, healing/surgical complication rates, implant survival and success.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Search for all studies on VRA with PBL measurement and ≥12-month follow-up; PRISMA + PROSPERO.
- 42 studies included; 11 were RCTs. RCTs available only for GBR, onlay, and inlay techniques.
- Weighted mean estimate (WME) computed for PBL overall and per technique; weighting by technique contribution.
- Three pairwise meta-analyses to fully evaluate outcomes.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Overall PBL (WME): 1.38 mm (95% CI 1.10–1.66) at mean follow-up 41.0 ± 27.8 months.
- Technique weight contribution: GBR 32.9%, Inlay 30.6%, Onlay 25.0%, osteodistraction 7.6%, SBB 3.9%.
- Per-technique WME (95% CI) for PBL:
  - GBR: 1.06 mm (0.87–1.26)
  - Inlay: 1.72 mm (1.00–2.43)
  - Onlay: 1.31 mm (0.87–1.75)
  - Osteodistraction: 1.81 mm (CI as reported in text; lowest weight)
  - Split bone block (SBB): 0.66 mm (0.55–0.77)
- Conclusion: PBL may be influenced by the type of VRA intervention, but RCTs evaluating long-term peri-implant bone level behaviour are needed across all techniques.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- RCTs available only for GBR, onlay, inlay — osteodistraction and SBB rest on weaker designs and small weights.
- Wide CIs for inlay and osteodistraction; heterogeneity in follow-up (mean 41 ± 27.8 months) and implant/membrane brands.
- Authors call for dedicated RCTs on long-term peri-implant bone levels for all VRA techniques.

## 6. Related Work

- VRA: vertical ridge augmentation
- PBL: peri-implant bone loss
- VBG: vertical bone gain
- WME: weighted mean estimate
- GBR / SBB / Inlay / Onlay / osteodistraction: VRA technique categories
- SBB: split bone block technique
