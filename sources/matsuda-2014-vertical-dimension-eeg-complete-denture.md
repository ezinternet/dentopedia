## Why Ingested

총의치 수직고경(Occlusal Vertical Dimension, OVD) 설정 오류가 단순 기계적 문제를 넘어 중추신경계·심리 상태에 영향을 준다는 객관적(EEG) 근거로, [[complete-denture/alhajj-2017-occlusal-vertical-dimension-complete-denture-review]]·[[complete-denture/fayad-2025-vertical-dimension-determination-prosthodontics-review]]가 임상 기법으로 강조하는 "정확한 VDO 결정"의 생리학적 당위성을 보강한다. 동물에서 OVD 변화가 뇌 스트레스 반응을 일으킨다는 [[tmj/wang-2024-tmj-ovd-elevation-occlusal-loss-rats]]의 사람 대응 근거이기도 하다.

## One-line Summary

Clinical EEG study (n=21 complete denture wearers, duplicate dentures at −3 mm and +5 mm VDO): an immediate +5 mm increase in vertical dimension raised the EEG negative-mood indicator after gum chewing, and a −3 mm decrease reduced occlusal force — showing immediate VDO error perturbs both psychological state and bite force.

## 한줄요약

총의치 착용자 21명에서 수직고경(Vertical Dimension of Occlusion, VDO)을 −3 mm/+5 mm로 바꾼 복제 의치로 뇌파(Electroencephalogram, EEG)를 측정: +5 mm 증가 시 껌 저작 후 부정적 심리지표가 유의하게 상승하고, −3 mm 감소 시 교합력이 유의하게 감소 — 즉각적 VDO 오류가 심리 상태와 교합력을 모두 교란함.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: The influence of vertical dimension of occlusion changes on the electroencephalograms of complete denture wearers
- **Authors**: Risa Matsuda, Yoshikazu Yoneyama, Masakazu Morokuma, Chikahiro Ohkubo (Division of Prosthodontics, Tsurumi University)
- **Journal**: Journal of Prosthodontic Research, 2014;58(2):121–126
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.jpor.2014.01.003
- **Type**: Original clinical study (in vivo, within-subject)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First objective (EEG) evidence on how acute VDO change affects sensory perception and brain activity in complete denture wearers.
- Links prosthodontic VDO error to a measurable central-nervous/psychological response, not just mechanical/esthetic outcome.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Subjects**: 21 regular complete-denture wearers (12 M / 9 F, mean age 76.6 y) at Tsurumi University Dental Hospital.
- **Intervention**: from each subject's original denture, two duplicate dentures were fabricated with altered VDO — one **−3 mm** (decreased) and one **+5 mm** (increased).
- **Measures**: EEG activity and occlusal force recorded before and after gum chewing with each denture. From EEG, a "negative indicator" score (psychological condition) and a stable-neuronal-activity index (D α) were derived.
- **Statistics**: Wilcoxon signed-rank test, α = 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **+5 mm denture**: significant increase in the negative (mood) indicator score after gum chewing (p < 0.05).
- **D α (stable neuronal activity)**: no significant change before/after chewing with any denture (p > 0.05).
- **Occlusal force**: significantly decreased between the original denture and the **−3 mm** denture (p < 0.05).
- **Conclusion**: immediate VDO changes of a complete denture influence psychological condition (increased VDO) and occlusal force (decreased VDO).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample (n = 21), elderly cohort, immediate (acute) exposure only — no adaptation/long-term follow-up.
- EEG-derived "negative indicator" is an indirect psychological proxy; mechanism (trigeminal → CNS) inferred, not directly mapped.
- Two fixed offsets (−3 / +5 mm) rather than a dose-response gradient.

## 6. Related Work

- OVD determination methods that aim to avoid such errors: Alhajj 2017, Fayad 2025, Goyal 2026 (this batch).
- Animal evidence of OVD change → CNS stress: Wang 2024 (TMJ OVD elevation in rats).
- Rest-position basis of VDO: Sheppard 1975.

## 7. Glossary

- **VDO / OVD (Vertical Dimension of Occlusion)**: lower-face height when posterior teeth (or denture teeth) are in occlusion.
- **EEG (Electroencephalogram)**: scalp recording of cortical electrical activity; here used to index arousal/psychological state.
- **D α**: study-defined index of stable neuronal (alpha-band) activity.
- **Negative indicator score**: EEG-derived proxy for negative psychological condition.
