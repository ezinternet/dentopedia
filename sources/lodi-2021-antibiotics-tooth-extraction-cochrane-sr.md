## One-line Summary
Cochrane systematic review (3rd update, CD003811.pub3) evaluating antibiotic prophylaxis to prevent post-extraction complications; outcomes tracked at day 6–7 include infectious complications, pain, fever, swelling, trismus, dry socket, and adverse events.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews 2021, Issue 2, Art. No.: CD003811
- **DOI**: 10.1002/14651858.CD003811.pub3
- **Type**: Cochrane systematic review (3rd update of original review)
- **Institution**: Università degli Studi di Milano and collaborators, Italy

## 2. Key Contributions
- Provides the highest level of evidence (Cochrane SR) on antibiotic prophylaxis specifically for tooth extraction complications in otherwise healthy patients
- Evaluates multiple outcomes at day 6–7: post-surgical infectious complications, pain (dichotomous and VAS score), fever, swelling, trismus, dry socket, and adverse events
- Synthesized data from multiple randomized trials comparing antibiotic versus placebo around the time of tooth extraction
- Addresses both prophylactic and therapeutic aspects in the peri-extraction period
- Informs the ongoing debate over routine antibiotic prescribing for dental extractions in healthy patients and the public health imperative of antibiotic stewardship

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Search strategy**: Cochrane Oral Health Group Trials Register, CENTRAL, MEDLINE, EMBASE; bibliographic databases searched with no language restrictions
- **Study design**: Randomized controlled trials comparing antibiotic versus placebo or no treatment in patients undergoing tooth extraction
- **Population**: Adult patients (immunocompetent) undergoing tooth extraction (routine or surgical)
- **Intervention**: Any antibiotic regimen (type, dose, duration not restricted) given perioperatively
- **Comparator**: Placebo or no antibiotic
- **Outcomes measured at day 6–7**:
  - Postsurgical infectious complications (primary)
  - Pain (dichotomous)
  - Pain score (VAS 0–10)
  - Fever
  - Swelling
  - Trismus
  - Dry socket (alveolar osteitis)
  - Adverse events
- **Data analyses**: Meta-analyses labeled 1.1 through 1.8; risk ratio (RR) and mean difference (MD) with 95% confidence intervals

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Infectious complications**: Antibiotics reduce post-extraction infection risk; meta-analysis 1.1 (Analysis 1.1, day 6–7)
- **Pain**: Antibiotics reduce pain (dichotomous; Analysis 1.2) and pain score (VAS, Analysis 1.3) at day 7
- **Fever**: Antibiotic effect on fever assessed (Analysis 1.4)
- **Swelling**: Antibiotic effect on swelling at day 7 (Analysis 1.5)
- **Trismus**: Antibiotic effect on trismus at day 7 (Analysis 1.6)
- **Dry socket**: Antibiotics reduce dry socket rate (Analysis 1.7, day 6–7) — clinically relevant as dry socket is the most common post-extraction complication in third-molar surgery
- **Adverse events**: Antibiotic-related adverse events (principally gastrointestinal) documented (Analysis 1.8); number needed to harm must be weighed against benefit
- Overall conclusion consistent with limited benefit of routine antibiotic prophylaxis for healthy patients and concerns about antibiotic resistance; guidelines generally recommend restriction to high-risk cases

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Heterogeneity in antibiotic type, dosing, timing, and patient populations across included trials
- Most trials conducted before modern antibiotic stewardship policies; generalizability to current practice may be limited
- Limited data on immunocompromised patients; review focused on generally healthy individuals
- Adverse events reporting inconsistent across trials
- Evidence certainty variable across outcomes due to risk of bias in primary studies

## 6. Related Work
- Vidovic-Juras 2024: antibiotic prophylaxis for dental procedures — updated indications; routine not recommended
- Wilson 2021 (AHA): IE prophylaxis restricted to high-risk cardiac conditions
- Oral Surgery category papers: coronectomy, IAN management (lodi focuses on extraction complications broadly)

## 7. Glossary
- **Dry socket (alveolar osteitis)**: Painful post-extraction complication characterized by loss of the blood clot from the socket; particularly common after mandibular third-molar extractions
- **Trismus**: Difficulty opening the mouth following extraction; due to inflammation/muscle spasm
- **VAS**: Visual Analogue Scale; 0–10 or 0–100 mm scale for pain measurement
- **NNT/NNH**: Number needed to treat / number needed to harm; key metrics for weighing antibiotic benefit vs. adverse event risk
- **Antibiotic stewardship**: Policy of limiting antibiotic use to indicated cases to reduce resistance development
- **Cochrane pub3**: Third update of the original Cochrane review CD003811; reflects updated literature search and re-analysis
