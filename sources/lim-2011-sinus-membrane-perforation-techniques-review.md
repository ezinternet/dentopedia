## Why Ingested

상악동 거상술 합병증 중 가장 흔한 상악동막 천공(perforation)의 처치 방법을 체계적으로 정리한 한국어 종설로, [[wiki/sinus-lift/lateral]] 범주의 임상적 처치 결정 트리를 보완하기 위해 수집. 천공 크기별(5 mm 미만 / 5~10 mm / 10 mm 이상) 처치 프로토콜과 임플란트 성공률 데이터를 직접 제공한다.

## One-line Summary

Korean narrative review (PubMed 1998–2009, 7 studies, n>436 implants) summarizing repair techniques for maxillary sinus membrane perforations by size, with implant success rates ranging 69.6–98.9%.

## 한줄요약

한국 종설(PubMed 1998~2009, 7편, 임플란트 436개 이상): 상악동막 천공 크기별 처치법(교원질막, 층판골, 협부지방대, 봉합술)과 임플란트 성공률(69.6~98.9%) 정리.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Size-stratified repair protocol: small (<5 mm) vs medium (5–10 mm) vs large (>10 mm) vs very large (>15 mm) perforations each carry distinct management recommendations.
2. Synthesizes implant success rates by perforation management approach from 7 selected studies (n>436 implants): 69.6%–98.9%.
3. Reviews Fugazzoto & Vlassis Class I–V perforation classification (by location and severity).
4. Highlights piezoelectric surgery as a technique to reduce perforation incidence.
5. Identifies anatomic risk factors: thin membrane (28%), septa (22%), adhesions (17%), prior surgery (17%), scar (11%), cyst (5%).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search**: PubMed, 1990–2010
- **Keywords**: sinus lift, sinus augmentation, sinus floor elevation, sinus graft, sinus perforation, repair of sinus perforation, repair of sinus membrane
- **Inclusion criteria**: clinical studies of lateral-window sinus lift in humans; perforation repair performed; implant success rate documented by repair technique
- **Final selection**: 7 articles meeting criteria
- **Data extracted**: author, year, perforation size, repair method, implant system, bone graft material, number of patients, number of implants, implant success rate

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Perforation incidence & classification
- Incidence: 10–60% across studies
- Fugazzoto & Vlassis classification: Class I (near osteotomy margins, mesiodistal) → Class V (perforation within pre-existing tear); Class V hardest to manage, lowest success

### Size-stratified repair recommendations (from reviewed studies)

| Perforation size | Primary repair technique | Implant success range |
|---|---|---|
| Very small (< about 2 mm) | No repair needed; membrane folds during elevation | Normal |
| Small (< 5 mm) | Resorbable collagen membrane (Bio-Gide, Bio-Mend) ± suture (6-0 Vicryl) | 94.4%–98.9% |
| Medium (5–10 mm) | Collagen membrane + lamellar bone (from osteotomy) | 91.9% |
| Large (> 10 mm) | Lamellar bone ± buccal fat pad; block bone graft | 74.1% |
| Very large (> 15 mm) | Slow-resorbing collagen membrane (Bio-Mend, 1–2 sheets, pin-fixed) ± PRP; microsurgical suture (8-0) ± fibrin glue; collagen membrane pouch technique | ~100% (small case series) |

### Study-level implant success rates (Table 1 data)
- Proussaefs 2004 (>2 mm, collagen membrane): 69.6% (n=21)
- Shlomi 2004 (>5 mm, demineralized lamellar bone sheet): 89.7% (n=68; patient count = series N)
- Hernández-Alfaro 2006 (<5 mm): 97.1%; (5–10 mm): 91.9%; (>10 mm): 74.1%
- Ardekian 2006 (<10 mm, collagen membrane): 94.4% (n=35 patients)
- Becker 2008 (<5 mm): 98.9% (n=93 implants); >5 mm + suture
- Pikos 2008 (>15 mm, slow-resorbing membrane + PRP): 100% (n=10 implants; case series)
- Kim 2008 (15 mm, buccal fat pad): 100% (n=2 implants; case report)

### Risk factors for perforation
- Residual bone height ≤3 mm → 85% perforation rate (Ardekian); 6 mm → 25%
- Ardekian: no significant difference in implant success between groups despite higher perforation rate at lower RBH

### Fibrin glue & tissue adhesives
- Fibrin glue (fibrinogen → fibrin net) reported as adjunct for large perforations
- Cyanoacrylate adhesive tested in rabbit model (Choi et al.)

### Prevention strategies
- Piezosurgery reduces perforation risk
- Round (rather than sharp) osteotomy window corners
- Pre-op CT for membrane thickness, septa location, residual bone height
- Pre-op screening for seasonal allergy, allergic rhinitis, mucosal thickening

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative review of only 7 studies; no systematic meta-analysis or risk-of-bias assessment
- Heterogeneous study designs (case reports through prospective series)
- Very large perforations represented only by small case series (n=1–2); success rates unreliable
- No direct head-to-head RCT of repair techniques
- Publication limited to Korean journal with Korean-language body; limited international visibility

## 6. Related Work

- Fugazzoto & Vlassis perforation classification (Class I–V) — referenced but not authored here
- Hernández-Alfaro 2006: largest comparative dataset across size categories
- Pikos 2008: very large (>15 mm) perforation management
- Kim 2008: buccal fat pad for 15 mm perforation (case report)
- Choi et al.: fibrin glue and cyanoacrylate animal study

## 7. Glossary

- **Schneiderian membrane** (슈나이더막): pseudostratified ciliated columnar epithelium lining the maxillary sinus
- **Sinus membrane perforation** (상악동막 천공): tear in the Schneiderian membrane during sinus lift — most common complication
- **Lateral window approach** (측방접근법): standard sinus lift via lateral bony window
- **Resorbable collagen membrane** (흡수성 교원질막): e.g., Bio-Gide, Bio-Mend — primary repair material for small/medium perforations
- **Lamellar bone** (층판골): thin cortical bone plates often harvested from the osteotomy window itself, used as scaffold for medium–large perforations
- **Buccal fat pad** (협부지방대): pedicled fat graft from buccal space used for large perforations
- **Fibrin glue** (피브린 글루): fibrinogen-based adhesive forming fibrin net; adjunct for perforation sealing
- **Piezosurgery** (피에조수술): ultrasonic bone surgery; reduces membrane perforation risk versus rotary instruments
- **RBH** (Residual Bone Height, 잔존치조골 높이): key determinant of perforation risk
- **PRP** (Platelet-Rich Plasma, 혈소판풍부혈장): growth factor concentrate sometimes added to large-perforation repair
