## Why Ingested

Adds an Iraqi (Middle Eastern) subpopulation data point to the C-shaped canal prevalence map, complementing the Korean cross-sectional micro-CT morphology data in [[endodontics/anatomy/song-2008-cross-sectional-morphology-and-minimum-canal]] and extending coverage beyond the East-Asian populations that dominate the literature. It uniquely reports both mandibular AND maxillary second molar C-shaped/fused-root configurations in the same cohort, reinforcing the female-predominance and bilateral-symmetry signals seen across the sibling prevalence pages ([[endodontics/anatomy/shemesh-2017-cshaped-canals-cbct-mandibular-molars]], [[endodontics/anatomy/shekarian-2023-cshaped-canals-three-rooted-mandibular-iranian]]).

## One-line Summary

CBCT cross-sectional study (368 mandibular + 369 maxillary second molars, Iraqi subpopulation): C-shaped canal prevalence 17.4% in mandibular vs 7.9% in maxillary second molars; significantly higher in females (23% vs 10.4%, p=0.001); C2 most common mandibular type (56.3%), MB-DB fusion most common maxillary type (65.5%).

## 한줄요약

CBCT 횡단 연구(이라크 인구, 하악 제2대구치 368개·상악 제2대구치 369개): C-shaped 근관 유병률은 하악 17.4% vs 상악 7.9%, 여성에서 유의하게 높음(23% vs 10.4%, p=0.001); 하악은 C2형(56.3%), 상악은 MB-DB 융합(65.5%)이 가장 흔함.

## 1. Document Information

- Type: Retrospective cross-sectional CBCT study
- Population: Iraqi subpopulation (private B&R Dental Center, Sulaimani, Kurdistan Region/Iraq; Feb 2018–May 2020)
- Sample: 368 mandibular second molars (164 male / 204 female, mean age 28.33y) and 369 maxillary second molars (151 male / 218 female, mean age 32.86y)
- Imaging: GALILEOS Sirona comfort PLUS; 0.25/0.125 mm isotropic voxel, 15 cm FOV, 98 kVp, 3–5 mA, 14 s
- Journal: Scanning, Volume 2022, Article ID 4886993

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides mandibular AND maxillary second molar C-shaped/fused-root prevalence in the same Iraqi cohort.
- Confirms strong female predominance of mandibular C-shaped canals (23% vs 10.4%, p=0.001).
- Documents laterality pattern: mandibular C-shaped canals predominantly bilateral; maxillary fused roots predominantly unilateral.
- Classifies maxillary fused-root configuration by Jo et al. 9-type scheme.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Classification: Fan et al. modified Melton technique for mandibular C-shaped canals (C1–C5); Jo et al. 9-type root-fusion scheme for maxillary molars.
  - C1: uninterrupted "C", no separation; C2: semicolon, α or β ≥ 60°; C3: 2–3 separate canals, both angles < 60°; C4: single round/oval canal; C5: no lumen.
- C-shaped canal recorded when pulp chamber floor was visible on axial plane.
- Two observers (endodontist + radiologist); interexaminer reliability kappa = 0.87 (fully reliable).
- Statistics: chi-square and Fisher's exact tests; significance p ≤ 0.05; SPSS v25.
- Variables: gender, age (<30, 30–39, 40–49, ≥50), tooth position (right/left), unilateral/bilateral.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Mandibular second molars: 64/368 (17.4%) C-shaped.
  - Female 47 (23%) vs Male 17 (10.4%), p=0.001 (significant).
  - No significant difference by age (p=0.734) or side (p=0.747).
  - Configuration: C2 56.3% (36), C3 23.4% (15), C1 15.6% (10), C4 4.7% (3); no significant difference by age/gender/position.
- Maxillary second molars: 7.9% C-shaped (fused root).
  - Type I subtype C (MB-DB fusion) most common = 65.5%.
  - No significant difference by fused-root type, age, or position.
- Laterality: mandibular C-shaped mostly bilateral (both genders); maxillary mostly unilateral (both genders).
- Mandibular > maxillary second molar prevalence (17.4% vs 7.9%).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single private-center retrospective sample; may not represent the entire Iraqi population.
- Relatively young mean ages may not capture age-related calcification effects.
- CBCT voxel resolution lower than micro-CT gold standard; fine isthmuses/fins may be missed.
- No clinical/treatment-outcome correlation.

## 7. Glossary

- C-shaped canal: root canal whose axial cross-section resembles the letter "C", often with webs/fins/isthmuses connecting canals.
- Fan classification (C1–C5): CBCT axial classification of C-shaped canal orifice/cross-section.
- Radix / fused root: morphological root variations; here maxillary fused-root types classified per Jo et al.
- Hertwig's epithelial root sheath: developmental structure whose failure to fuse buccally/lingually is the proposed etiology of C-shaped canals.
- CBCT: cone-beam computed tomography, 3D non-invasive imaging used to assess root canal morphology.
