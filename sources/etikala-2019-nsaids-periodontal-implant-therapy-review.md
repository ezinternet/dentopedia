## One-line Summary
서술적 고찰: NSAIDs가 치주 상처 치유에 상충된 결과; 선택적 COX-2 억제제는 골형성 억제 → 임플란트 골유착 저해 가능성; 임상 근거 부족으로 결론 유보 (Compend Contin Educ Dent 2019).

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Compendium of Continuing Education in Dentistry 2019;40(2):e1–e9
- PMID: 30767546
- Design: Narrative review of literature
- Databases: PubMed (MEDLINE)
- Included: 9 clinical studies (periodontal), 4 animal studies + 2 clinical studies (implant)

## 2. Key Contributions
- NSAIDs (most commonly prescribed for dental postoperative pain) have uncertain effects on wound healing
- Periodontal healing: conflicting results — some NSAIDs may support wound healing via inflammation modulation; others inhibit
- Implant osseointegration: selective COX-2 inhibitors specifically implicated in bone formation inhibition around orthopedic implants
- Overall conclusion: NSAIDs negatively affect osseointegration of titanium implants (within limitations)
- Quality of human implant clinical evidence: POOR; conflicting results from animal models
- Practical implication: dental surgeons must be aware of potential NSAID effects on osseous healing

## 3. Methodology
Narrative literature review. PubMed MEDLINE search. Dual scope: (1) periodontal wound healing — 9 clinical studies; (2) dental implant osseointegration — 4 animal + 2 human clinical studies. No systematic search protocol or quality appraisal reported.

## 4. Key Results — Summary Table
| Context | Drug Type | Effect on Healing |
|---|---|---|
| Periodontal wound healing | NSAIDs (general) | Conflicting — no clear conclusion |
| Implant osseointegration | COX-2 selective inhibitors | Inhibit bone formation (animal data) |
| Implant osseointegration | NSAIDs (human clinical) | Negative effect but low evidence quality |

## 5. Limitations
- Narrative (not systematic) review — subject to selection bias
- Very few human implant studies (n=2) — most evidence from animals
- No quantitative synthesis
- Does not distinguish short-term perioperative NSAIDs from chronic use
