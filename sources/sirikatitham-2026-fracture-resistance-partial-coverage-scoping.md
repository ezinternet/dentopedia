## Why Ingested

Extends [[wiki/inlay/prott-2025-partial-coverage-restorations-posterior-scoping]] from a different angle: Prott (2025) scoped clinical *survival* of posterior PCRs by material, whereas this scoping review maps in-vitro *fracture resistance and fracture patterns* by preparation design (overlay / MOD overlay / MOD onlay), occlusal thickness, and ceramic type (LDS / ZLS / PICN / RNC). It also synthesizes the single-study fracture findings that the wiki already holds in isolation — [[wiki/inlay/hofsteenge-2023-preparation-design-fracture-strength-disilicate-inlay]] and [[wiki/inlay/griffis-2022-tooth-cusp-preservation-lithium-disilicate-onlay-fatigue]] — into a load-threshold map, giving a chairside answer to "does an MOD box weaken my onlay, and by how much, in a premolar vs a molar?"

## One-line Summary

Scoping review (34 in-vitro studies; 9 premolar, 25 molar) of fracture resistance of non-endodontically-treated posterior teeth restored with ceramic partial coverage restorations (PCRs); MOD overlays lowered molar fracture loads vs anatomic overlays, increasing occlusal thickness generally helped, proximal boxes worsened fracture severity, and all designs still exceeded maximum bite forces.

## 한줄요약

비-근관치료 구치를 세라믹 부분피개수복물(PCR)로 수복했을 때의 파절저항을 다룬 스코핑 리뷰(34편: 소구치 9·대구치 25) — MOD 박스는 대구치 anatomic overlay 대비 파절하중을 낮췄고, 교합 두께 증가는 대체로 유리, proximal box는 파절 양상을 악화시켰으나 모든 디자인의 파절하중은 최대 교합력을 상회했다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First literature review to organize posterior PCR **fracture resistance + fracture pattern** simultaneously across three independent factors: restoration type (overlay / MOD overlay / MOD onlay), preparation design (occlusal thickness, anatomic-only, proximal box, peripheral shelf, IDS, deep margin elevation), and ceramic type (LDS, ZLS, PICN, RNC).
2. Produces a **load-threshold map** separating premolars (350-1,954 N) from molars (880-4,995 N), each cross-tabulated against material and occlusal thickness.
3. Resolves a clinically debated question: an internal **MOD box lowers molar overlay load capacity** (and worsens fracture severity), but the effect is **less significant in premolars** — tooth-type-dependent guidance.
4. Bottom-line reassurance: even the weakest designs exceed maximum bite forces (premolar 392 N, molar 602 N), so design choice should weigh seating stability and adhesive support, not just fracture numbers.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Scoping review, protocol registered at OSF; PRISMA flow; last search update June 2025.
- **Databases**: PubMed, Web of Science, ScienceDirect. Two compound search strings (onlay/overlay/occlusal veneer/partial coverage × fracture resistance × preparation × lithium OR hybrid ceramic).
- **Screening**: 193 identified → 113 after de-duplication → 61 after title/abstract → 24 included + 10 from cross-reference = **34 final** (9 premolar, 25 molar). Model-teeth (non-human-substrate) studies excluded.
- **Definitions fixed by the review**: onlay = covers ≥1 cusp; overlay = all-cusp + supragingival margin (occlusal veneer counts as overlay); MOD overlay = overlay + internal MOD preparation. Lithium-silicate = LDS + ZLS; hybrid = PICN + RNC.
- **Quality**: RoBDEMAT 9-domain tool. Outcome = vertical (and where reported oblique) load-to-fracture (N) plus fracture pattern (mild crack/chipping vs severe mixed/unfavorable).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Risk of bias**: 8/34 low, 26/34 medium, 0 high. Four domains (standardization, testing procedure, statistics, outcome reporting) were "yes" in all studies.

**Premolars (350-1,954 N overall)**
- LDS overlay 0.5/0.6 mm: 471-1,781 N; 1 mm: 866-1,495 N; 1.5 mm: 1,005 N (aging).
- LDS-MOD overlay: 0.5 mm 833-997 N; 1 mm 1,087-1,108 N; 2 mm 963-1,361 N.
- LDS-MOD onlay: 0.5 mm 1,192 N; 2 mm 837-1,182 N.
- 2-mm LDS onlay (no aging) 682-938 N; 2-mm ZLS 624-756 N (oblique).
- **MOD box effect minimal/positive in premolars**: Gierthmuehlen found MOD overlay ≥ overlay (1,129>1,005 at 1.5 mm; 1,087>866 at 1 mm).
- Modifiers ↑resistance: IDS, wider proximal box (load but more fracture), preheated resin composite luting (1,954/1,505 N) > dual-cure (1,495/1,158 N) > flowable (≈900-1,068 N).

**Molars (880-4,995 N overall)**
- LDS overlay scales with thickness/design: 0.5 mm 880-3,391 N; 1 mm 1,406-3,390 N (anatomic+others up to 3,216 N); 1.5 mm anatomic up to **4,995 N**; 2/2.4 mm 2,869-4,766 N.
- **LDS-MOD overlay 1,295-1,326 N** and **ZLS-MOD overlay 2,066-2,189 N** — both LOWER than their non-MOD overlay counterparts (LDS 1,406-4,995 N; ZLS 2,177-2,738 N). This is the headline molar finding.
- Hybrids: PICN 0.5-1.5 mm 1,839-3,540 N; RNC 0.5-2.4 mm 1,024-3,620 N. LDS > hybrid at thick occlusal, hybrid ≥ LDS at thin (0.5-1 mm) — superiority **controversial**.
- Modifiers: anatomic-reduction-only > proximal box > peripheral shelf (Ferraris LDS 3,216 > 2,462 > 2,350 N); shoulder-shelf effect controversial; IDS, dual-cure cement, enamel phosphoric-acid+self-etch all ↑resistance; deep margin elevation neutral on load (1,986 ≈ 1,776 N) but ↑fracture severity.

**Fracture pattern**
- Premolars: 2 proximal boxes → 75-100% severe mixed fractures (worst); thicker occlusal and wider boxes both ↑severity.
- Molars: LDS-MOD overlays → more severe mixed fractures (up to 100%) than LDS anatomic overlays; RNC-MOD onlays showed 0% mild fractures; proximal-box effect on pattern NOT seen in molars (contrast with premolars).

**Clinical anchor**: max bite force 392 N (premolar) / 602 N (molar) — every PCR design exceeded it.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- All-in-vitro; no clinical/RCT outcomes synthesized (the authors cite a separate 24-180 mo MA showing LDS 98% ≈ hybrid onlay 99% survival).
- Heterogeneous test setups (aging vs no aging, vertical vs oblique load, varied substrates) make cross-study numbers indicative, not pooled — no meta-analysis.
- Scarce ZLS and PICN data for premolars.
- Authors call for: RCTs comparing preparation designs; testing modern fully-crystallized LDS/ZLS; oblique-horizontal (debonding) force testing; shallow conservative retentive vs anatomic non-retentive comparisons.

## 7. Glossary

- **PCR (Partial Coverage Restoration)**: indirect restoration covering part of the tooth (inlay/onlay/overlay) — minimally invasive alternative to a full crown.
- **Overlay**: all-cusp coverage with a supragingival margin (occlusal veneer = overlay).
- **Onlay**: PCR covering at least one cusp.
- **MOD overlay/onlay**: overlay/onlay with an internal mesio-occluso-distal box (retentive feature).
- **LDS**: lithium disilicate glass ceramic (e.g., IPS e.max); biaxial flexural strength ~609 MPa.
- **ZLS**: zirconia-reinforced lithium silicate.
- **PICN**: polymer-infiltrated ceramic network hybrid (e.g., Vita Enamic); ~193 MPa.
- **RNC**: resin-nanoceramic hybrid (e.g., Lava Ultimate / Cerasmart); ~300 MPa.
- **IDS (Immediate Dentin Sealing)**: sealing freshly cut dentin at preparation to improve bond and adaptation.
- **Peripheral shelf**: circumferential axial-wall step (e.g., buccal full veneering) that increases mechanical wrapping.
- **RoBDEMAT**: Risk of Bias tool for in-vitro DEntal MATerial research (9 domains).
- **Maximum bite force benchmark**: premolar 392 N, molar 602 N.
