## Why Ingested

Existing KM augmentation literature ([[wiki/overviews/keratinized-mucosa-peri-implant-health-overview]]) and consensus documents focus on suturing as the default FGG fixation method, but suturing fails in cases with heavy muscle pull or a compromised/unstable recipient bed. This technique report introduces titanium pins — a repurposed GBR fixation tool — as an alternative FGG/ARF fixation method during vestibuloplasty, offering a concrete procedural option not captured in the overview or the existing consensus pages.

## One-line Summary

Technique report (n = 3) describing the use of titanium pins — traditionally used in GBR — to fixate free gingival grafts (FGGs) and apically repositioned flaps (ARFs) during vestibuloplasty, yielding successful KM augmentation with minimal shrinkage in cases where conventional suturing was impractical.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Novel tool repurposing**: Adapts titanium membrane fixation pins (a standard GBR hardware item) to stabilize soft tissue grafts (FGG) and mucoperiosteal flaps (ARF) during vestibuloplasty — no hardware development required.
2. **Addresses suturing failure mode**: Conventional suturing of FGGs during vestibuloplasty is undermined by heavy muscle pull and an unstable/periosteum-absent recipient bed; titanium pins mechanically override these limitations.
3. **Graft physiology rationale**: Intimate graft-to-periosteum contact secured by pins promotes capillary ingrowth, proper revascularization, and reduced graft shrinkage, counteracting the ischemia that causes partial graft failure.
4. **Patient-morbidity reduction claim**: Authors state the technique shortens procedure time and reduces patient morbidity compared to multi-suture approaches, though this is not quantified in the available abstract.
5. **Applicability**: Used in cases of insufficient keratinized mucosa (KM) width around both implant restorations and natural teeth, presenting discomfort during oral hygiene and peri-implant/periodontal inflammation.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Design**: Prospective technique report / case series  
**Sample**: 3 patients with insufficient KM width  
**Clinical presentation**: All three patients showed discomfort during oral hygiene and inflammation around implant restorations and/or natural teeth attributable to inadequate KM  

**Surgical protocol (extracted from abstract)**:
- Vestibuloplasty with either FGG or ARF at the surgical site
- Titanium pins (the type used to fixate titanium mesh/membrane in GBR procedures) used instead of or in addition to sutures for primary graft fixation
- Pins ensure intimate graft-periosteum contact across the entire recipient bed surface, including zones where suture purchase is limited by muscle anatomy or absence of stable tissue margins
- Postoperative follow-up performed (duration not specified in abstract)

**Outcome measures (reported qualitatively)**:
- Graft integration (success/failure)
- KM width gain
- Vestibular depth gain
- Graft shrinkage
- Complications

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

**Intrinsic study limitations**:
- Very small sample (n = 3); no statistical analysis possible
- No control group (no parallel suture-only arm)
- Qualitative outcomes only in the available abstract — no mm measurements or shrinkage percentages extractable from this source
- Follow-up duration unstated in abstract; long-term KM stability unknown

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| 각화점막 (Keratinized Mucosa, KM) | Stratified squamous keratinized epithelium lining the gingiva/peri-implant mucosa; provides mechanical protection and facilitates oral hygiene |
| 유리치은이식편 (Free Gingival Graft, FGG) | Autogenous soft tissue graft harvested from the palate; includes epithelium and lamina propria; used to augment KM width |
| 근단이동판막 (Apically Repositioned Flap, ARF) | Mucoperiosteal flap reflected and sutured apically to expose the existing keratinized band and deepen the vestibule |
| 전정성형술 (Vestibuloplasty) | Surgical procedure to deepen the vestibular sulcus and/or increase the apico-coronal dimension of attached/keratinized tissue |
| 티타늄 핀 (Titanium Pins) | Small-diameter titanium fixation screws/pins designed to tack membranes or meshes to bone in GBR; repurposed here for soft tissue graft stabilization |
| 재혈관화 (Revascularization) | Ingrowth of blood vessels from the recipient bed into the graft; critical for graft survival in the first 48–72 h post-surgery |
| 이식편 수축 (Graft Shrinkage) | Postoperative dimensional reduction of the graft; minimized by intimate graft-periosteum contact |
