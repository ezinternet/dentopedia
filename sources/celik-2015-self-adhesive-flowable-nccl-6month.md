## One-line Summary
Split-mouth RCT (abstract only, 80 NCCLs) showing a self-adhesive flowable composite failed badly at 6 months (33% success, 27/40 lost to retention) vs 100% for a nanohybrid with 3-step etch-and-rinse.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Demonstrates a self-adhesive flowable composite (Fusio Liquid Dentin) is clinically unacceptable for NCCLs at 6 months.
- Strong contrast with a conventional 3-step etch-and-rinse (Optibond FL) + nanohybrid (G-aenial) at 100% success.
- Empirical support for the principle that a dedicated adhesive/etching step is critical to NCCL retention.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Randomized, controlled, single-center, split-mouth; 80 NCCLs; SAFC (Fusio Liquid Dentin) vs E&R 3-step (Optibond FL) + G-aenial. FDI criteria at 1 week and 6 months. Wilcoxon Signed Rank, Mann-Whitney U (alpha=0.05).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- SAFC success 33% (27/40 unacceptable due to retention failure) vs E&R/nanoC 100% at 6 months.
- Significant group difference also for color and translucency.
