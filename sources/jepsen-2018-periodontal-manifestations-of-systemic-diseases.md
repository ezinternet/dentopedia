## 1. Document Information
- J Periodontol 2018;89(Suppl 1):S237–S248 (jointly in J Clin Periodontol). DOI 10.1002/JPER.17-0733.
- Consensus report of workgroup 3, 2017 World Workshop.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Updates the 1999 classification for systemic-disease periodontal manifestations and developmental/acquired conditions.
- Integrates four underlying reviews: systemic diseases; mucogingival conditions; traumatic occlusal forces; dental prostheses/tooth-related factors.
- Provides case definitions and diagnostic considerations for these categories.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Consensus report based on four commissioned systematic/narrative reviews; expert working-group agreement.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Systemic diseases/conditions can affect periodontitis course or the periodontal attachment apparatus.
- Gingival recessions are highly prevalent and linked to hypersensitivity, root caries, NCCLs, and esthetic impairment.
- Occlusal forces can injure teeth and the attachment apparatus (links to the occlusal-trauma review).
- Developmental/acquired tooth- and prosthesis-related factors may predispose to periodontal disease.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Consensus synthesis; individual evidence strength varies by condition.
- Many associations are observational.

## 6. Related Work
- Fan 2018 (traumatic occlusal forces — one of the four informing reviews).
- 1999 Armitage classification (superseded framework).

## 7. Glossary
- Mucogingival condition: deviation from normal gingival/alveolar mucosa dimensions/relations (e.g., recession).
- Developmental/acquired condition: tooth/prosthesis-related factor predisposing to periodontal disease.
