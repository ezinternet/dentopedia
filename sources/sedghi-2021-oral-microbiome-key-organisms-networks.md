## Why Ingested
Provides the ecological and omics-level framework for understanding oral microbial community assembly, succession, and dysbiosis that contextualises matrix and species-specific papers such as [[oral-microbiology/jakubovics-2021-dental-plaque-biofilm-matrix]] and [[oral-microbiology/bloch-2024-oral-streptococci-modulators-health-disease]].

## One-line Summary
Narrative review covering the composition, assembly, and dysbiotic shifts of the oral microbiome — from pioneer colonisers and multi-omics research techniques to the polymicrobial networks driving caries, periodontitis, and systemic disease.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Synthesises the successional colonisation model of the oral microbiome: pioneer streptococci (S. mitis, S. sanguinis, S. gordonii, S. salivarius) → later anaerobes via bridging organisms.
- Reviews culture-independent research methods (16S rRNA, shotgun metagenomics, metatranscriptomics, metabolomics, single-cell sequencing) and their specific applications and limitations in oral microbiome research.
- Describes how diet (Westernised/Industrial Revolution dietary shift) and oral hygiene profoundly shape microbiome composition, with acid-tolerant and acidogenic organisms expanding under high-sugar conditions.
- Documents the polymicrobial pathogenesis model for caries and periodontitis: disease attributed to microbial network dysbiosis, not single pathogens.
- Reviews oral microbiota contributions to systemic disease (cardiovascular disease, hypertension) via ectopic dissemination of oral pathobionts.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Design: narrative (non-systematic) review.
- Scope: community assembly from neonatal to adult life; research technique assessment; disease-specific microbial ecology; systemic connections; modulation strategies (probiotics, arginine supplementation).
- No clinical population or intervention design; narrative synthesis of published experimental, epidemiological, and omics literature.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Over 250 species from the oral cavity have been isolated and characterised in culture.
- Pioneer colonisers (S. mitis, S. sanguinis, S. gordonii, S. salivarius) bind tongue and cheek cells prior to tooth eruption.
- Illumina MiSeq (short-read), PacBio and Oxford Nanopore (long-read) represent the three dominant next-generation sequencing platforms.
- Oral bacteria detected in small intestine, lungs, heart, placenta, and brain — suggesting oral microbiome as a reservoir for systemic pathobionts.
- Oral probiotics targeting alkalinity or anti-S. mutans activity and arginine supplementation are emerging microbiome modulation strategies.
- Germ-free mouse models confirm causal role of oral microbiota in caries and periodontal bone loss.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Mechanistic data on oral-to-systemic disease pathways remain sparse; most associations are epidemiological.
- In vitro biofilm models cannot fully recapitulate oral cavity complexity (salivary flow, species succession, inflammatory substrates).
- Authors advocate for longitudinal, integrated multi-omics approaches combining sequencing, metabolomics, and clinical data.
- Challenges in separating highly complex clinical sample mixtures while simultaneously visualising diverse molecules.

## 6. Related Work
- Builds on Human Oral Microbiome Database (HOMD) infrastructure for species cataloguing.
- Contextualises key pathogen roles reviewed by Hajishengallis & Lamont (keystone pathogen and polymicrobial synergy models).
- Complements systemic disease linkage evidence from cardiovascular and metabolic disease microbiome literature.

## 7. Glossary
- Shotgun metagenomics: random fragmentation and adapter ligation to sequence all microbial DNA in a sample unbiasedly, enabling pan-genomic analysis.
- Metatranscriptomics: sequencing of mRNA from environmental samples to capture transcriptionally active microbes.
- CRISPR-Cas9 depletion: depletion of abundant human DNA sequences to enhance bacterial signal in low-burden samples.
- Pathobiont: commensal organism that becomes pathogenic under dysbiotic or immunocompromised conditions.
- Pioneer coloniser: first bacterial species to adhere to a new oral surface, setting conditions for successional colonisation.
