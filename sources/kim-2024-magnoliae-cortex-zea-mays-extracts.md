## One-line Summary
Two-month canine RCT (n=10 beagles, 5 per arm) demonstrating that oral combined Magnoliae cortex 40 mg + Zea mays L. extract 20 mg in ligature-induced periodontitis improves GI, PPD, CAL, and BoP versus vehicle and significantly suppresses tissue TNF-α expression; in vivo confirmation of the in-vitro Magnoliae+Zea mays anti-inflammatory effect.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First in vivo (canine model) demonstration of safety, periodontal clinical efficacy, and immune-modulating activity (CD4+/CD8+ T cells, TNF-α tissue expression) of the Magnoliae cortex (소엽 후박) + Zea mays L. (옥수수 불검화 추출물) combination at lower dose (40 + 20 mg/day) proportional to body weight, over 2 months.
- Establishes molecular signature: significantly reduced tissue TNF-α expression in MZ group vs control; trends for IL-1β, IL-6, IL-17, IL-8 reduction.
- Provides safety data: CBC and serum chemistry remained within reference ranges throughout 8-week dosing (only a single transient ↓K⁺ in 1 dog pre-induction).
- Links in vitro mechanism (Kim 2018, JPIS) to in vivo clinical effect in a periodontitis model that resembles human disease.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Subjects: 10 beagle dogs, ligature-induced periodontitis model
- Randomization: 5 per arm
  - MZ group: oral capsule containing Magnoliae cortex extract 40 mg + Zea mays L. extract 20 mg, 1× daily, 2 months
  - Control: empty gelatin capsule, same regimen
- Time points: 0, 1, 4, 8 weeks post-medication (PM)
- Periodontal indices: Plaque Index (PI), Gingival Index (GI), Periodontal Pocket Depth (PPD), Clinical Attachment Loss (CAL), Bleeding on Probing (BoP)
- Systemic safety: serial CBC (WBC/RBC/Hb/Hct/Plt/Neut/Lymph/Mono/Eo/Baso) and serum chemistry (albumin, ALP, ALT, amylase, AST, total bilirubin, BUN, Ca, Cr, glucose, total protein, Na, K, Cl)
- Immunology: PBMC flow cytometry for CD4+ and CD8+ T cell percentage at −4, 0, 1, 3, 5, 8 weeks
- Tissue analysis: inflammatory cytokines (IL-1β, IL-6, IL-17, TNF-α) and chemokine (IL-8) expression in gingival tissue, comparing MZ vs control

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- All dogs maintained normal general health, body weight, body temperature.
- CBC and serum chemistry within reference ranges throughout dosing.
- Clinical periodontal indices (mean ± SE):
  - GI (MZ vs control): baseline 2.43/2.43 → 4 wk 1.64* vs 1.84, 8 wk 1.61* vs 1.84 (*p < 0.05 between groups)
  - PPD (MZ vs control): baseline 2.13/2.11 → 8 wk **1.86 vs 2.13** (significant between-group at 8 wk PM)
  - CAL (MZ vs control): baseline 2.40/2.38 → 8 wk **1.39 vs 1.82** (significant)
  - BoP (MZ vs control): baseline 0.81/0.81 → 4 wk 0.63 vs 0.78 (p < 0.05 between groups at 4 wk PM)
  - PI: no significant between-group difference at any time point
- Immune: CD4+ higher and CD8+ lower in MZ group; significantly differed only at 8 wk PM
- Tissue cytokines: TNF-α expression significantly lower in MZ group; IL-1β, IL-6, IL-17, IL-8 trended lower

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Very small sample (n = 5 per arm)
- Animal model (beagles) with ligature-induced periodontitis — not naturally occurring disease
- 8-week dosing — short relative to clinical periodontal therapy timescales
- Blinding of clinical index assessor not explicitly described
- Mechanism inferred from cytokine expression; no direct anti-microbial assessment in this trial (unlike Kim 2018 in vitro work)
- Effects of mechanical plaque removal (e.g., professional scaling) not co-administered, which differs from human clinical practice where these drugs are adjunct to SRP
- Industry/Inje University context — replication by independent group not yet established

## 7. Glossary
- Magnoliae cortex: bark of Magnolia officinalis stems and roots; active phenolics honokiol & magnolol
- Zea mays L. extract: corn-derived extract; in periodontal context refers to titrated unsaponifiable fraction (active ingredient of Insadol)
- Honokiol/magnolol: bioactive phenolic compounds from Magnoliae cortex with documented TNF-α, NF-κB, MAPK inhibition
- PI/GI/PPD/CAL/BoP: standard clinical periodontal indices (plaque/gingival/pocket depth/clinical attachment loss/bleeding on probing)
- CBC: complete blood count
- PBMC: peripheral blood mononuclear cell
- CD4+/CD8+: T helper / T cytotoxic cell subsets
- Ligature-induced periodontitis: experimental dog model where a suture tied around the cervical margin promotes plaque retention and accelerates periodontitis
- PM: post-medication (timepoint nomenclature in the paper)
