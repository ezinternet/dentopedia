## One-line Summary

Systematic review + meta-analysis (≥5-year follow-up) finding short implants in the posterior region have survival similar to long implants in the mandible but possibly lower in the maxilla, with fewer biological complications overall.

## 한줄요약

≥5년 추적 SR+MA — 구치부 short implant는 하악에서 long과 생존율 유사하나 상악에서는 더 낮을 수 있고, 전반적으로 생물학적 합병증은 적음 (부위 의존성).

## 2. Key Contributions
- Stratifies the short-vs-long comparison by jaw (maxilla vs mandible) at ≥5 years.
- Flags a possible maxillary survival disadvantage for short implants — nuance most pooled analyses miss.
- Confirms lower biological-complication rate with short implants.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- SR+MA; minimum 5-year observation; short vs longer implants in the posterior alveolar region.
- Subgroup by arch (maxilla, mandible).
- Outcomes: survival rate, biological complications.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Mandible: short-implant survival similar to long implants.
- Maxilla: short-implant survival may be lower than long implants.
- Biological complications: lower with short implants.
- Conclusions to be interpreted with caution (limited participant/implant numbers).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Limited number of participants and implants.
- Arch-specific signal needs confirmation in adequately powered trials.
- Heterogeneity in implant systems and surfaces.

## 6. Related Work
- Posterior short-implant survival literature; Esposito, Felice trials.

## 7. Glossary
- Posterior alveolar region: posterior maxilla/mandible.
- Biological complications: peri-implant soft/hard tissue complications.
