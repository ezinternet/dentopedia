## Why Ingested

RCT (Kim 2025, n=60) that uses the **toothpick method (TPM)** of professional toothbrushing as the mechanical delivery vehicle for a natural Cibotium barometz (CB) extract to treat peri-implant mucositis — the first study to combine TPM with a natural anti-inflammatory agent against PIM-related bacteria, extending the toothpick method into peri-implant maintenance. Reinforces [[implants/peri-implantitis/jepsen-2015-primary-prevention-periimplantitis-managing-mucositis]] (mucositis management = primary prevention of peri-implantitis, plaque removal central) and offers a natural-agent counterpoint to the chlorhexidine/probiotic adjuncts reviewed in [[implants/peri-implantitis/mauriello-2026-peri-implant-mucositis-adjunctive-narrative-review]].

## One-line Summary

Double-blind RCT (n=60: CB-TPM 21 / chlorhexidine-TPM 20 / saline-TPM 19), all delivered by the toothpick method — only CB-TPM reduced salivary occult blood (Cohen's d = 1.148) and uniquely produced large-effect (d >= 0.8) reductions in P. micra, T. forsythia, and P. intermedia, positioning natural CB extract + TPM as a side-effect-free non-surgical option for peri-implant mucositis.

이중맹검 RCT(n=60: CB-TPM 21 / 클로르헥시딘-TPM 20 / 생리식염수-TPM 19) — 세 군 모두 토스픽 방법(TPM)으로 전달했을 때, CB-TPM만 타액 잠혈을 감소(Cohen's d=1.148)시켰고 P. micra·T. forsythia·P. intermedia에서 단독으로 큰 효과(d>=0.8) 감소를 보여, 천연 CB 추출물+TPM이 부작용 없는 비수술적 임플란트주위 점막염 옵션임을 제시.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Effects of oral hygiene management containing Cibotium Barometz J. Smith extract on peri-implant mucositis: a randomized clinical trial
- **Authors**: Yu-Rin Kim, Seoul-Hee Nam (Korea)
- **Journal**: BMC Complementary Medicine and Therapies, 2025
- **PMID**: 40329262 | **PMCID**: PMC12054295 | **DOI**: 10.1186/s12906-025-04900-3
- **Trial registration**: ICTRP KCT0008626 (17/07/2023, retrospectively registered)
- **Study type**: Randomized, double-blind, controlled clinical trial (3 parallel arms)
- **Setting**: M Dental Clinic, Busan, South Korea; CB extract prepared at Kangwon National University. Jan–Jul 2023.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First clinical trial to test the **toothpick method (TPM)** as the delivery vehicle for a **natural Cibotium barometz (CB) extract** against peri-implant mucositis (PIM).
- Head-to-head comparison of three solutions, all applied by the SAME mechanical method (TPM): CB extract (10 mg/mL) vs 0.12% chlorhexidine vs saline.
- Demonstrates a side-effect-free natural agent matching or exceeding chlorhexidine on inflammatory and microbiological endpoints — relevant because chlorhexidine carries staining, taste change, mucosal epitheliolysis, and rare hypersensitivity/parotitis risks.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Randomized, double-blind, controlled trial. Computer-generated randomization; double-blinding of subjects and researchers.
- **Sample size**: G*Power 3.1 (α=0.05, power=0.95, effect size=0.5) → 54 required. 72 recruited → 67 eligible → 60 analyzed after excluding device/measurement errors.
- **Three arms** (all TPM-delivered):
  - **CBG** (experimental): CB extract 10 mg/mL — n = 21
  - **CG** (positive control): 0.12% chlorhexidine — n = 20
  - **SG** (negative control): saline — n = 19
- **Intervention**: Toothpick method = expert-performed Watanabe toothbrushing with a **double-row medium-bristle toothbrush**. Solution applied to the brush.
- **Inclusion**: ≥20 y, ≥1 oral implant, PIM diagnosed within past year (soft-tissue inflammation without bone loss, per Papapanou et al.).
- **Exclusion**: dentin caries, xerostomia, smoking, chronic disease, current antibiotics, severe periodontitis (PD>4 mm), recent periodontal surgery, bleeding disorders. Scaling done 1 week before baseline to equalize plaque/calculus.
- **Outcomes** (before vs after TPM):
  - Salivary occult blood, leukocytes, proteins, total (Sill-Ha ST-4910 saliva analyzer).
  - 12 PIM-related bacteria by real-time PCR (paper-point subgingival sampling at implant).
  - PSR, BOP, satisfaction.
- **Statistics**: ANOVA + chi-square (between-group), paired t-test (pre/post), Cohen's d (effect size: 0.2/0.5/0.8), eta-squared (0.01/0.06/0.14), Duncan post-hoc.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Baseline homogeneity**: No significant between-group differences in demographics, oral health status, BOP (SG 6.00 / CG 5.50 / CBG 5.90, p=0.967), implant count, or baseline bacteria.

**Satisfaction**: CBG 4.43/5 vs CG 1.40 vs SG 1.58.

**Saliva (Cohen's d, pre→post):**
- Occult blood: **SG increased** (53.63→77.26, d=-2.212, p<0.001); **CG no change** (d=-0.017, p=0.942); **CBG decreased** (55.19→37.19, d=1.148, p<0.001). Between-group after: η²=0.459, p<0.001.
- Leukocytes: SG NS (d=0.382); CG d=1.248; CBG d=1.462 (both p<0.001).
- Proteins: SG NS; CG d=0.599 (p=0.015); **CBG d=1.965** (54.43→9.05, p<0.001).
- Total: SG increased (NS); CG d=0.640 (p=0.010); **CBG d=2.281** (p<0.001).
- Only CBG reached large effect (d≥0.8) for occult blood, proteins, and total.

**Bacteria (12 species, Cohen's d):**
- **SG significantly INCREASED 5/12** (P. micra, E. nodatum, T. forsythia, C. rectus, S. aureus).
- **CG significantly increased 3/12** (P. micra, E. nodatum, T. denticola).
- **Large effect (d≥0.8) ONLY in CBG**: *Parvimonas micra* (d=1.088), *Tannerella forsythia* (d=0.631 — strong CBG-only decrease; SG increased), *Prevotella intermedia* (d=0.912). The paper highlights three species significant only in CBG.
- **Large effect in BOTH CBG and CG**: *Fusobacterium nucleatum* (CBG d=2.007 / CG d=1.832), *Staphylococcus aureus* (CBG d=1.122 / CG d=0.907). (*Prevotella nigrescens* CBG d=4.055 / CG d=0.563.)
- *Eikenella corrodens* and *A. actinomycetemcomitans* decreased in all three groups.

**Bottom line**: CB-applied TPM reduced inflammatory bleeding (occult blood) where chlorhexidine and saline did not, and uniquely suppressed several red-complex/PIM bacteria — without chlorhexidine's side effects.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single clinic, small arms (~20 each), short single-session pre/post design — no long-term follow-up.
- Some bacteria still rose after TPM in SG/CG (and saline as negative control inherently lacks antibacterial action), attributed to implant surface topography, peri-implant immunology, and uneven bacterial deposition.
- Authors call for long-term and large-scale RCTs plus in-vitro mechanistic verification, and study of other natural ingredients.

## 7. Glossary

- **TPM (Toothpick Method)**: Expert-performed Watanabe toothbrushing with a double-row medium-bristle toothbrush; mechanical plaque-removal technique used here as the delivery vehicle for each solution.
- **PIM (Peri-implant mucositis)**: Reversible soft-tissue inflammation around an implant without bone loss; precursor to peri-implantitis.
- **CB (Cibotium barometz)**: Traditional Asian-medicine fern rhizome extract with antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, hemostatic, and osteoclast-inhibiting bioactivity; contains flavonoids, tannins, terpenoids.
- **Salivary occult blood**: Early salivary biomarker of periodontal/peri-implant inflammation (Sill-Ha analyzer).
- **Cohen's d**: Effect size (0.2 small / 0.5 medium / 0.8 large).
