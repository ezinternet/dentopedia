## Why Ingested

Plateau root form (PRF) 임플란트 주위 피질골의 나노인덴테이션 (Nanoindentation) 기계적 성질이 기능 연수에 따라 어떻게 변화하는지에 대한 인간 회수 연구(Human Retrieval Study)로, 기능 부하 기간에 따른 골 성숙 및 기계적 특성 변화의 근거를 제공한다. 이 논문은 [[wiki/implants/gil-2015-progressive-plateau-root-form-osseointegration-retrieval]]의 조직형태계측(Histomorphometry) 데이터와 상보적인 기계적 데이터를 제공하며, PRF 임플란트 장기 골-임플란트 반응 메커니즘 이해를 위해 인제스트함.

## One-line Summary

Human retrieval study (n=30 implants, 0.3–24 years) showing cortical bone elastic modulus and hardness around plateau root form implants increase significantly during the first 5 years of function (r=0.84 and r=0.78 respectively) and stabilize thereafter, measured by nanoindentation.

## 한줄요약

인간 회수 임플란트 연구(n=30, 0.3–24년 기능)에서 플래토 루트폼 임플란트 주위 피질골의 탄성계수(Elastic Modulus)와 경도(Hardness)가 기능 후 5년까지 유의하게 증가하고(r=0.84, r=0.78) 이후 안정화됨을 나노인덴테이션으로 확인.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First study to quantify nanoindentation-level mechanical properties (elastic modulus, hardness) of cortical bone surrounding plateau root form (PRF) implants in human retrievals spanning a wide time range (0.3 to ~24 years).
2. Demonstrated a significant positive correlation between time in vivo and both elastic modulus (E, r=0.84) and hardness (H, r=0.78) of peri-implant cortical bone.
3. Identified a critical threshold at approximately 5 years: E and H values were significantly higher after 5 years in vivo (p<0.001), with stable mechanical properties thereafter.
4. Found no significant effect of implant arch location (maxilla vs. mandible) or positioning on peri-implant bone mechanical properties.
5. Found no significant long-term effect of implant surface treatment on bone biomechanical response (E: p≥0.09; H: p≥0.3).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Study design**: Observational human retrieval study

**Specimens**: 30 plateau root form implants-in-bone retrieved from patients across different in-vivo times (0.3 to ~24 years) due to retreatment needs. All showed no clinical failure at retrieval.

**Specimen preparation**:
- Dehydration and embedding in methacrylate-based resin
- Sectioned along bucco-lingual long axis
- Fixed to acrylic plates
- Non-decalcified processing to slides ~50 μm thickness

**Nanoindentation protocol**:
- Testing performed under wet conditions
- Bone areas within first three plateaus tested (plateau contact zones)
- 120 indentations per implant total (n=120/implant)
- Maximum load: 300 μN
- Loading rate: 60 μN/s
- Holding time: 10 s; Unloading time: 2 s
- Oliver–Pharr method used to compute E (GPa) and H (GPa)

**Statistical analysis**: Pearson correlation coefficients for time-dependent changes; group comparisons for arch location and surface treatment effects (p-value threshold p<0.05).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Parameter | Correlation with time in vivo | Significance after 5 years |
|---|---|---|
| Elastic Modulus (E, GPa) | r = 0.84 | p < 0.001 (higher) |
| Hardness (H, GPa) | r = 0.78 | p < 0.001 (higher) |

**Key quantitative findings**:
- Both E and H increased as time in vivo elapsed across the full 0.3–24 year range
- Significant increase in E and H observed after 5 years in vivo (p<0.001)
- Mechanical properties plateau and stabilize after the ~5-year mark
- Arch (maxilla vs mandible) had no significant effect on E or H
- Implant positioning had no significant effect on E or H
- Implant surface treatment had no significant long-term effect on bone mechanical properties (E: p≥0.09; H: p≥0.3)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- PDF is a Wiley HTML print with limited full-text body; quantitative mean E and H values by time group not recoverable from available text
- Retrospective observational design: implants retrieved due to retreatment needs (not failures), so selection bias possible
- Sample size moderate (n=30 implants)
- Nanoindentation at ~50 μm slide level measures local lamellar/interlamellar bone, which may not fully represent bulk bone behavior
- Testing limited to first three plateau zones; bone properties deeper from the interface not characterized
- No data on opposing arch or occlusal load history per patient

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Plateau root form implant (PRF) | Implant design with horizontal grooves/plateaus along the body, rather than a standard threaded design; osseointegration occurs across the plateau contact surfaces |
| Elastic modulus (E, GPa) | Measure of bone stiffness (resistance to elastic deformation); higher E = stiffer bone |
| Hardness (H, GPa) | Measure of resistance to permanent deformation under load |
| Nanoindentation | Technique using a nanoscale indenter tip to measure local mechanical properties of materials at sub-micron scale; Oliver–Pharr method computes E and H from load-displacement curves |
| Oliver–Pharr method | Standard algorithm for extracting E and H from nanoindentation load-displacement data |
| Retrieval study (인간 회수 연구) | Study using implants/devices retrieved from human patients; provides direct in-vivo evidence unavailable from animal models |
| Non-decalcified processing | Histological preparation that preserves mineral content, allowing mechanical testing of native calcified bone |
| BIC (Bone-to-Implant Contact) | Percentage of implant surface in direct contact with bone; histomorphometric metric complementary to nanoindentation |
| Cortical bone (피질골) | Dense outer shell of bone; the tissue measured in this nanoindentation study |
| Lamellar bone (층판골) | Mature, organized bone tissue with characteristic lamellar structure; replaces woven bone during remodeling |
