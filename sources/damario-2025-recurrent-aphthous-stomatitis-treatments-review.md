## Why Ingested

[[oral-medicine/manfredini-2021-recurrent-aphthous-stomatitis-treatment]] already covers RAS management, but this 2025 review adds a focused 10-year (2013–2023) RCT-only synthesis (45 trials) that emphasizes the rising evidence for non-pharmacological options (LLLT, omega-3, natural mucoadhesives) over corticosteroids — reinforcing and extending the treatment-ladder picture. It also complements [[oral-medicine/gasmi-2021-oral-aphthous-pathophysiology-treatment]]'s pathophysiology-anchored treatment account with comparative pain-outcome data per modality.

## One-line Summary

Narrative/literature review of 45 RCTs (2013–2023) on RAS treatment; non-pharmacological therapies (LLLT, omega-3, natural mucoadhesive gels) gave the best risk–benefit ratio, often matching or exceeding corticosteroids, with LLLT offering the fastest pain relief.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Synthesizes 45 RCTs from a 10-year window (2013–2023) on recurrent aphthous stomatitis (RAS) treatment, with **pain management** as the primary comparable outcome.
- Argues that **non-pharmacological therapies offer the best risk–benefit ratio**, frequently matching or surpassing cortisone-based treatment with fewer adverse effects.
- Identifies **low-level laser therapy (LLLT)** as the single most promising modality, mainly for its immediate (day-1) pain relief and absence of significant adverse effects.
- Builds a practical, RAS-form-specific treatment ladder distinguishing short-term (minor/infrequent lesions) from long-term (recurrent) management.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PRISMA-structured literature review; protocol registered on PROSPERO (CRD42023382368).
- Inclusion: RCTs in human patients, 2013–2023 (up to Jan 2023), any language, with a comparator (placebo or other active substance). Databases: PubMed, EMBASE, CINHAL.
- Exclusion: RAS as a secondary effect of a primary disease (Crohn's, Behçet's); polytherapy confounding healing; treatment as adjuvant to complex multi-agent regimens.
- Screening flow: 123 records → 28 duplicates removed → 95 screened → 47 excluded (off-topic) → 3 excluded (criteria) → **45 studies** included.
- Risk of bias: two independent assessors, Cochrane RevMan v5.4. Low risk for selection/performance/attrition; detection and reporting bias often unclear. Results stratified single-blind vs double-blind.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Treatment categories and representative pain (VAS) outcomes:

**Topical**
- Acemannan (Aloe vera, Bhalang): pain score 7.5 vs control 6.5 vs corticosteroid 8.3 — corticosteroid best, Aloe vera a viable alternative.
- Myrrh + Aloe vera mucoadhesive gels (Mansour): Aloe vera 76.6% complete healing by day 6; myrrh 76.7% near-painless by day 6 (myrrh > Aloe vera).
- Curcumin (Bakhshi): 1% nanomicelle vs 2% gel, day-4 VAS 2.58 vs 3.75 (favoring nanomicelle); both reached 0 by day 7. Curcumin vs triamcinolone (Deshmukh): no significant difference.
- Silver nitrate / propolis / rhubarb / walnut (n=125): silver nitrate fastest symptom resolution (mean 1.16 days).
- Triester glycerol oxide gel (Ofluoglu): outperformed 0.1% triamcinolone on pain and lesion size (days 2–6), aided by mucosal adhesion.

**Systemic supplements**
- Omega-3 (Hadian, Nosratzehi, El Khouli): three 6-month RCTs, all significantly better than placebo with no side effects; El Khouli test-group VAS fell to 1.48 by 6 months vs placebo ~6.6. Reduced RAS recurrence.
- Probiotics (Pedersen): improved healing, pain, episode frequency vs placebo.
- Levamisole ± low-dose prednisolone (Xue n=350; Sharda n=50): reduced ulcer number, size, pain, frequency.

**Laser**
- CO2 single session (Prasad/Pai): VAS 8.48 → 0.68 immediately post-treatment (p<0.001).
- 660 nm diode (Soliman): day-6 efficacy index 100 in all cases.
- Diode vs pharmacological (Huo): day-1 VAS 2.04 vs 4.85, favoring laser; converged near zero by day 7.
- Diode in children with MiRAS (Bardellini, n=60): significant from day 1 (VAS 1 vs 3, p=0.0001).
- 809 nm LLLT: VAS 84.7 → 31.5 by day 2 vs placebo near-flat.
- Er,Cr:YSGG (Yilmaz): significant, most notable immediately post-treatment (p<0.01).

Double-blind studies provided the more robust evidence; LLLT, omega-3, and curcumin gels showed the strongest, most consistent effects.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Heterogeneity across studies (varying sample sizes, age ranges) → between-study risk of bias.
- Sparse data on systemic treatments and on several recent local treatments.
- Single-blind trials susceptible to observer bias on subjective pain/healing outcomes.
- Many compounds are not yet in validated, commercially suitable oral formulations; future formulation development expected to expand options.

## 7. Glossary

- **RAS**: Recurrent aphthous stomatitis — recurring painful oral mucosal ulcers; ~20% prevalence.
- **MiRAS**: Mucosal inflammatory / minor recurrent aphthous stomatitis variant used in several trials.
- **LLLT**: Low-level laser therapy — non-thermal photobiomodulation for pain relief and healing.
- **VAS**: Visual analog scale for pain.
- **TGO**: Triester glycerol oxide — mucoadhesive gel alternative to topical corticosteroid.
- **Acemannan**: Polysaccharide extract of Aloe vera.
