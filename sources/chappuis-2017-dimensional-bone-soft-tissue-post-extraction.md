## One-line Summary
Periodontol 2000 2017;73:73-83: narrative review synthesizing animal + human evidence on post-extraction bone/soft tissue changes — bundle bone resorbs entirely; facial wall <1mm in 90% anterior maxilla; socket grafting partially counteracts resorption; 2mm facial wall needed to maintain dimensions.

## 1. Key Results
- Bundle bone (tooth-dependent): 0.2-0.4mm thick; completely resorbs after extraction
- Facial bone wall <1mm in 90% of anterior maxilla cases; <0.5mm in 66%
- Post-extraction facial vertical bone loss: ~2.2mm (beagle dog, premolar sites)
- Immediate implant does NOT prevent socket remodeling; BIC apical margin 2.6mm on buccal
- Socket grafting partially counteracts marginal ridge contraction
- 2mm facial bone wall threshold required to maintain dimensions after immediate implant
- Human data: ridge width loss up to 50% in first year; 2/3 within first 3 months
- Systematic review: 2.6-4.5mm width loss; 0.4-3.9mm height loss in healed sockets

## 2. Modulating Factors
- Facial bone wall thickness (primary predictor)
- Tooth angulation
- Flap elevation (increases resorption)
- Socket grafting (partially protective)
- Immediate implant (does NOT substitute for PDL-derived bone)
