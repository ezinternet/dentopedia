## One-line Summary
Case report (1 patient, 70-year-old male, non-restorable maxillary molar; CBCT incidentally revealed antral pseudocyst): combined **platelet-rich fibrin (PRF) + resorbable collagen membrane** technique used to repair a Schneiderian membrane perforation created during pseudocyst removal at the lateral-window sinus lift; staged healing produced a thickened, fibrotic but intact membrane allowing successful subsequent implant placement.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Dentistry Journal (MDPI) 2023;11(3):84
- **Pages**: 10 pages, open access (CC BY)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Describes a **stepwise protocol** for managing a Schneiderian perforation when it occurs in conjunction with removal of an **antral pseudocyst** — a less commonly reported clinical scenario.
- Documents the use of **PRF combined with collagen membrane** (rather than collagen alone) for perforation repair, leveraging PRF growth factors (TGF-β, VEGF, PDGF) to promote membrane healing.
- Achieves **fibrotic membrane repair** that, on re-entry, was thickened but intact — supporting successful sinus elevation and implant placement.
- Argues for a **staged approach** when sinus pathology (pseudocyst) is encountered: biopsy / removal first, repair, healing interval, then sinus lift and implant.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Patient: 70-year-old healthy Caucasian male, presenting for implant replacement of a non-restorable maxillary molar.
- **Preoperative CBCT** revealed insufficient vertical height (<5 mm) → lateral-window sinus lift planned; CBCT incidentally identified a sinus floor lesion later confirmed histologically as an **antral pseudocyst**.
- **First surgery**: lateral window osteotomy; pseudocyst removed → resulting Schneiderian membrane perforation. **PRF membranes** (prepared via centrifugation of autologous blood, no anticoagulant) layered over the perforation and stabilized with a **resorbable collagen membrane**.
- Healing interval allowed; clinical and radiographic follow-up.
- **Second surgery**: re-entry showed thickened but intact, fibrotic membrane; standard sinus elevation with bone graft completed; implant placed.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Histology of biopsy specimen during initial entry confirmed **antral pseudocyst** (pseudostratified ciliated columnar epithelium overlying connective tissue, no malignancy).
- The PRF + collagen repair produced a **clinically intact, thickened (fibrotic) Schneiderian membrane** at re-entry.
- Sinus floor augmentation completed without further perforation; implant placement successful.
- Authors note the technique **shortened the overall treatment timeline** compared with abandoning the side or doing a fully staged approach with bone grafting then later sinus lift.
- Background literature cited: Schneiderian perforation incidence up to ~60% in lateral approaches; piezoelectric instrumentation reduces perforation rates; perforations >10 mm conventionally require more complex repair.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- n = 1 — case report; no comparator, no quantitative outcome metrics, no long-term implant survival data shown beyond the placement event.
- Selection bias (single favorable case); no information on failures of the technique.
- The relative contribution of PRF vs collagen membrane cannot be separated from a single case.
- Patient was healthy and non-smoker — generalizability to medically compromised patients unknown.

## 7. Glossary
- **Schneiderian membrane**: the bilaminar mucoperiosteal lining of the maxillary sinus, key target of preservation during sinus elevation.
- **Antral pseudocyst**: a benign sinus-floor lesion formed by accumulation of inflammatory exudate beneath the sinus mucosa; often incidental on CBCT and asymptomatic.
- **PRF (Platelet-Rich Fibrin)**: second-generation autologous platelet concentrate, prepared by centrifuging blood without anticoagulant; releases growth factors (TGF-β, VEGF, PDGF) over time.
- **Lateral window approach**: sinus floor elevation via an osteotomy in the lateral antral wall, used when residual ridge height is <5 mm.
- **Resorbable collagen membrane**: barrier of bovine/porcine collagen used to seal a perforation and contain the bone graft.
- **Piezoelectric surgery**: ultrasonic bone cutting that selectively spares soft tissue, reducing membrane perforation rates.
