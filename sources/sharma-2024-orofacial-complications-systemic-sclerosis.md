## Why Ingested

전신경화증(Systemic Sclerosis, SSc) 환자의 구강 합병증(소구증·구강건조증·치주염·하악골 흡수)을 의학적 고려 대상 환자 관리 맥락에서 정리하기 위해 인제스트. SSc 환자의 ~14%가 동반하는 이차성 쇼그렌증후군의 구강 건조 기전과 연결되어 [[drug/systemic-disease/shiboski-2016-acr-eular-sjogren-classification-criteria]]를 보강하고, 면역억제 환자 임플란트 의사결정 관점에서 [[drug/systemic-disease/shahi-2026-implant-outcomes-diabetes-mellitus-sr]]와 대비된다.

## One-line Summary

Narrative review (J Dent Res, 3-author) of orofacial complications in systemic sclerosis: microstomia (52–80%), xerostomia (sicca 70%; secondary Sjögren ≤14%), more severe periodontitis (stage III/IV in 90% dcSSc vs 60% lcSSc), condylar resorption (10–50%), trigeminal neuralgia, dysgeusia; MMP-9/CXCL-4 in GCF track early disease; dental implants can succeed but need vasculopathy/bone-quality risk assessment.

## 한줄요약

전신경화증의 구강안면 합병증을 정리한 서술적 종설(J Dent Res, 3저자): 소구증(52–80%), 구강건조증(sicca 70%·이차 쇼그렌 ≤14%), 더 심한 치주염(dcSSc III/IV기 90% vs lcSSc 60%), 하악과두 흡수(10–50%), 삼차신경통, 미각이상; 치은열구액 MMP-9·CXCL-4가 조기 질환과 상관; 임플란트는 혈관병증·골질 평가가 전제되면 성공 가능.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Consolidates the understudied **orofacial burden of systemic sclerosis (SSc / scleroderma)**, an autoimmune fibrosing connective tissue disease, into a single dental-facing review. Orofacial manifestations affect **up to 80%** of SSc patients.
- Frames SSc oral disease mechanistically: vascular injury → hypoxia (HIF-1α) and fibrogenic chemokines (TGF-β, ET-1, IL-1α) → myofibroblast differentiation and collagen-rich ECM remodeling.
- Highlights **microstomia** (reduced mouth opening), not skin thickness score, as the orofacial complication most correlated with oral outcomes — proposing it as a future clinical-trial end point.
- Surfaces candidate periodontal biomarkers (**MMP-9, CXCL-4** in gingival crevicular fluid, GCF) that track early-onset (<2 y) disease, whereas IL-6 does not correlate with periodontal parameters.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Narrative (non-systematic) review; no pooled data, search strategy, or quality appraisal reported.
- Organized by SSc classification (limited cutaneous lcSSc / diffuse cutaneous dcSSc / sine scleroderma — prevalence 64% / 28% / 8%) and then by anatomical orofacial site, followed by a molecular-mechanism synthesis (interleukins, CXCL-4, MMP-9).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Epidemiology**: female:male 4:1; highest incidence 47/100,000 (Canadian First Nations); dcSSc pulmonary fibrosis prevalence ~30%, 10-y mortality ~40%.
- **Microstomia**: 52–80% of patients; 2.5× higher Mouth Handicap in SSc Scale (MHISS) in dcSSc vs lcSSc; impairs mastication/deglutition → malnutrition, impairs hygiene, limits dental treatment.
- **Salivary**: sicca syndrome 70%; secondary Sjögren's syndrome ≤14% (associated with lcSSc); xerostomia from gland fibrosis → caries/erosion/periodontitis risk (compounded by gastroesophageal reflux).
- **Periodontitis**: worse than controls (attachment loss, recession, plaque/gingival indices); advanced (stage III/IV) in 90% dcSSc vs 60% lcSSc; PDL widening in >1/3 of patients. Notably gingiva does NOT develop the skin's fibrotic phenotype (scar-less gingival healing).
- **TMJ/bone**: condylar resorption 10–50% prevalence; secondary apertognathia (anterior open bite) reported; resorption concentrated at muscle-attachment sites (mandibular angle, condylar head, coronoid, zygomatic arch).
- **Neuropathy**: trigeminal neuralgia more prevalent than general population and may precede SSc diagnosis.
- **Biomarkers**: GCF MMP-9 and CXCL-4 elevated, correlate with periodontal parameters and early disease; IL-6 elevated but no correlation with periodontal clinical parameters.
- **Implants**: survival reported unaffected by SSc; success possible with preoperative risk assessment, but may be contraindicated with severe vasculopathy.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative review of a literature the authors describe as "scant" and low-quality; many cited findings come from small samples (e.g., 38 lcSSc patients; preliminary dcSSc biofilm studies).
- lcSSc vs dcSSc differences in TMJ involvement and microbiota largely uninvestigated; dysgeusia etiology unstudied.
- Authors call for: oral end points (microstomia) in SSc trials, validation of MMP-9/CXCL-4 as predictive biomarkers, and larger oral-microbiome studies across the oral–gut axis.

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary

- **SSc / scleroderma**: 전신경화증 (Systemic Sclerosis) — autoimmune fibrosing connective tissue disease.
- **lcSSc / dcSSc**: limited vs diffuse cutaneous SSc.
- **Microstomia**: 소구증 — reduced mouth opening from perioral skin fibrosis.
- **Sicca syndrome**: dry mouth/eyes from glandular fibrosis (here, non-lymphocytic).
- **MHISS**: Mouth Handicap in Systemic Sclerosis Scale.
- **GCF**: 치은열구액 (Gingival Crevicular Fluid).
- **CXCL-4**: Chemokine (C-X-C motif) ligand 4 — antiangiogenic profibrotic chemokine, SSc severity biomarker.
- **MMP-9**: Matrix Metalloproteinase-9 (gelatinase/type IV collagenase).
- **Apertognathia**: anterior open bite.
