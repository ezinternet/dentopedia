## Why Ingested

Part of a focused collection on vertical ridge augmentation, contributing the soft-tissue mechanism: this randomized split-mouth dog study shows that pre-augmentation soft-tissue expansion prevents flap dehiscence and significantly improves new bone formation. It mechanistically explains why mesh/graft exposure is so costly in [[implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/chiapasco-2021-customized-cadcam-titanium-meshes-gbr]] and supports the flap-management emphasis of [[implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/merli-2025-tacos-technique-alveolar-ridge-titanium-mesh]].

## One-line Summary

Randomized split-mouth animal study (10 beagle dogs, supra-alveolar critical-size mandibular defects): pre-augmentation soft-tissue expansion (STE) before alloplastic-scaffold (BCP/PEG) vertical GBR eliminated flap dehiscence (0/STE vs 8 control, p=0.002), preserved flap microcirculation, and significantly increased new bone (median NB 3.81 vs 1.06 mm², p=0.023).

## 한줄요약

무작위 분할구 동물 연구 (비글견 10마리, 하악 임계크기 상치조 결손): 동종이형 스캐폴드(BCP/PEG) 수직 골유도재생 전 연조직 확장(STE)을 시행하면 피판 열개가 사라지고(STE 0건 vs 대조군 8건, p=0.002), 피판 미세순환이 보존되며, 신생골이 유의하게 증가(신생골 중앙값 3.81 vs 1.06 mm², p=0.023).

## 1. Document Information

- Journal: Clinical Oral Implants Research, Vol. 28, 2017, pp. 640–647
- DOI 10.1111/clr.12848; accepted 20 March 2016; open access (CC BY-NC-ND)
- Affiliations: Witten/Herdecke University (Periodontology; Oral Biology & Materials Science); Rotes-Kreuz-Krankenhaus Kassel (Maxillofacial Surgery); study performed in Hungary
- Study type: randomized, split-mouth controlled **animal** experiment (beagle dogs); histomorphometry + immunohistochemistry.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First report of vertical augmentation using GBR with **alloplastic scaffolds alone** after **soft-tissue expansion (STE)** preconditioning (prior STE evidence used autogenous grafts).
- Links soft-tissue quality/microcirculation to bone-regeneration outcome: better-preconditioned flaps → no dehiscence → significantly more new bone.
- Uses a supra-alveolar critical-size defect model designed for rigorous testing of extra-skeletal (above-contour) vertical regeneration.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 10 male beagle dogs (mean age 8.1 mo, ~12.2 kg).
- Premolars extracted, bone reduced to create bilateral supra-alveolar critical-size mandibular defects.
- 6 weeks later, self-filling tissue expanders (0.7 ml; Osmed) placed submucosally in randomly allocated **test** mandibular sites for a 5-week expansion; contralateral side = **control** (split-mouth).
- Vertical augmentation: recipient bone perforated; defect grafted with **biphasic calcium phosphate (BCP) + blood + resorbable polyethylene glycol (PEG) membrane** (Straumann BCP/PEG). Scaffold volume (ScVol) measured.
- Test-site wound closure achieved without additional flap advancement; control sites required periosteal/submucosal release for passive closure.
- Flap microcirculation by laser Doppler flowmetry (baseline, 2 min, post-surgery, day 3). Primary outcome = soft-tissue dehiscence (yes/no) at 2 weeks.
- Histomorphometry + immunohistochemistry (osteocalcin, transglutaminase-II) at 8 weeks. Stats: Mann–Whitney U, Fisher exact, Wilcoxon, Spearman, chi-squared; α=0.05; analyses blinded.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Three expanders lost before augmentation. Scaffold volumes similar between groups (test median 141 mm³ vs control 130 mm³, p=0.529).
- **Soft-tissue healing**: uneventful in all STE sites vs **8 dehiscences in control sites (p=0.002)**.
- **Microcirculation**: perfusion dropped significantly after surgery in controls (p<0.001) but remained stable in STE sites (p=0.627); post-augmentation microcirculation significantly better in STE (p=0.012); controls still significantly below baseline at day 3 (p=0.005).
- **Histomorphometry (medians)**: total augmented area (TA) test 9.49 vs control 5.26 mm²; **new bone (NB) test 3.81 vs control 1.06 mm² (p=0.023)**; connective tissue (CT) test 19.80% vs control 42.27% of TA (p=0.009, favoring test). Two of ten control sites showed no bone formation at all.
- **Correlation**: TA strongly correlated with NB in test sites (r=0.794, p=0.006) but not in controls (r=0.614, n.s.).
- **Immunohistochemistry**: transglutaminase-II reactivity (maturating bone matrix marker) significantly stronger in test (p=0.036); osteocalcin similar between groups.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Animal (beagle) model; short 8-week healing; small n (10 dogs, split-mouth).
- Microcirculation/soft-tissue detail partly reported in a companion paper (Kaner et al. 2015).
- Alloplastic-scaffold-only design; translation to human vertical augmentation and to titanium-mesh GBR not directly tested here.

## 6. Related Work

- Prior STE-with-autogenous-graft work (Kaner & Friedmann 2011; Abrahamsson 2012; Mertens 2013; review Asa'ad 2015).
- Critical-size supra-alveolar defect model (Wikesjö 2006); BCP/PEG osteoconductivity (Friedmann 2009/2015; Thoma 2012); dehiscence rates up to 50% in vertical augmentation (Jensen & Terheyden 2009).

## 7. Glossary

- **STE (Soft Tissue Expansion)**: implanting a tissue expander to grow a surplus of soft tissue/vascularity before reconstructive surgery, easing tension-free closure.
- **Critical-size / supra-alveolar defect**: a defect above the residual bone contour that will not heal spontaneously — used to test extra-skeletal (vertical) regeneration.
- **BCP/PEG**: biphasic calcium phosphate granules + polyethylene glycol resorbable membrane (alloplastic scaffold).
- **Laser Doppler flowmetry (LDF)**: optical measurement of flap microcirculation/perfusion.
- **Transglutaminase-II (TG-II)**: marker of maturating, calcifying bone matrix.
- **Flap dehiscence**: wound-margin opening exposing graft/scaffold — the primary complication studied.
