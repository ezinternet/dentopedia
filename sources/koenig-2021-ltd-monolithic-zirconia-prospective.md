## Why Ingested

First prospective clinical study with ex vivo monitoring documenting intraoral low-temperature degradation (LTD) in second-generation 3Y-TZP monolithic zirconia prostheses — establishes that LTD occurs in vivo after 6 months and progresses over 2 years, a safety concern not previously confirmed in real intraoral conditions. [[dental-materials/warreth-2020-all-ceramic-restorations-narrative-review]]

## One-line Summary

Prospective study (n=101 posterior elements, 2-year ex vivo monitoring): LTD confirmed in 3Y-TZP monolithic zirconia prostheses from 6 months onward; grain pull-out in occlusal areas; glazing does not protect; clinical impact remains unknown.

## 한줄요약

전향적 임상연구 (n=101, 2년 추적): 2세대 3Y-TZP 모놀리식 지르코니아에서 6개월 후 LTD 발생 확인, 교합면 그레인 탈락 관찰, 유약(glaze)은 보호 효과 없음; 임상적 영향 미확인.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Dental Materials, July 2021
- **Study type**: Prospective clinical study with ex vivo monitoring
- **Registration**: ClinicalTrials.gov NCT02150226; Ethics Committee CHU Liège approved
- **Follow-up timepoints**: Baseline, 6 months, 1 year, 2 years (5-year protocol; this report = 2-year results)
- **Ethics**: Written patient consent; Strobe guidelines complied

## 2. Key Contributions

- First prospective study with ex vivo Raman spectroscopy and SEM monitoring of intraoral LTD in functioning dental prostheses
- Confirms LTD occurs in second-generation 3Y-TZP (translucent zirconia) in vivo — not only in vitro autoclave conditions
- Documents LTD onset at 6 months, progressive and non-uniform over 2 years
- Identifies grain pull-out in occlusal areas as distinct from classical LTD nucleation-growth process
- Shows glazing offers no protection against LTD
- Monoclinic phase quantification underestimates LTD in areas with grain pull-out (stress artifact)
- Historically contextualizes against "Prozyr® failures" in orthopedic zirconia (600–800 fractures)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Design**: Prospective clinical study with ex vivo monitoring
**Sample**: 101 posterior tooth elements (molars + premolars)
**Material**: Second-generation 3Y-TZP monolithic prostheses (translucent zirconia, milled by CAD/CAM)

**Monitoring protocol**:
- Timepoints: baseline, 6 months, 1 year, 2 years
- Technique: Raman spectroscopy (n=2,640 monoclinic phase measurement points per timepoint) + SEM
- Areas analyzed per element: occlusal, axial, glazed, unglazed (1–2 mm surface)
- 6 areas on molars, 4 areas on premolars

**Advanced analyses**: Raman depth mapping + high-resolution SEM on selected samples

**LTD indicator**: Tetragonal-to-monoclinic (t-m) phase transformation, quantified by monoclinic phase fraction (Vm)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **LTD onset**: 6 months after intraoral placement in 3Y-TZP
- **Progression**: Non-uniform, with localized clusters of transformed grains; continued over 2 years
- **Axial areas**: Classical nucleation-growth LTD pattern, progressing surface-inward (tens of microns depth)
- **Occlusal areas**: Tribological (masticatory) stress generates grain pull-out from monoclinic clusters — different mechanism; monoclinic quantification underestimates true aging extent
- **Glazed areas**: Glaze does NOT protect against LTD
- **Clinical significance**: LTD impact on mechanical failure risk unknown; no prosthetic failures attributed to LTD reported at 2 years
- **Context**: ISO 13356-2015 states monoclinic phase must not exceed 25% for biomedical Y-TZP after autoclave aging; in vivo conditions differ from autoclave

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Access to only ScienceDirect abstract/highlights section for this source (paywall) — details from highlights and introduction section
- 2-year results only; full 5-year protocol ongoing (see Koenig 2024)
- Clinical impact of LTD not yet established — no failure endpoint correlation
- Autoclave in vitro aging conditions differ from in vivo (pH variability, ionic saliva, mechanical stress not captured)
- Grain pull-out mechanism complicates standard Raman quantification

## 7. Glossary

- **LTD**: Low-temperature degradation — slow tetragonal-to-monoclinic phase transformation of 3Y-TZP in aqueous environments at body temperature
- **3Y-TZP**: 3 mol% yttria-doped tetragonal zirconia polycrystal — standard dental zirconia; susceptible to LTD
- **t-m transformation**: Tetragonal-to-monoclinic crystal phase transformation; drives LTD
- **Raman spectroscopy**: Vibrational spectroscopy technique used to quantify monoclinic phase fraction
- **Grain pull-out**: Physical ejection of monoclinic-transformed grains due to tribological (friction/wear) stress
- **Vm (monoclinic fraction)**: Percentage of monoclinic phase; indicator of LTD severity
- **Prozyr® failures**: Landmark orthopedic zirconia failures (2000s) attributed to LTD
