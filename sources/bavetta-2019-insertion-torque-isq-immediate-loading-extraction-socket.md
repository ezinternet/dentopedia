## One-line Summary
Retrospective study of 41 tapered implants showing fresh extraction socket implants start with significantly lower ISQ (57.6 vs 72.9) than healed-bone implants; ITV ≥35 Ncm better predictor than ISQ for immediate loading decisions.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: BioMed Research International 2019, Article ID 9720419
- DOI: 10.1155/2019/9720419
- Design: Retrospective, 41 tapered implants, immediate loading, posterior maxilla/mandible

## 2. Key Contributions
- Fresh socket implants have significantly lower initial ISQ than healed-bone implants
- ITV (insertion torque value) is a better criterion than ISQ alone for fresh socket immediate loading
- Both groups converge in stability by 4 months

## 3. Methodology
- 41 tapered implants: fresh sockets (n=22) vs healed bone (n=19)
- Osstell ISQ measured at placement (t0) and 4 months (t1)
- ITV measured with torque wrench
- 100% immediate loading (provisional crown at 24–72h)

## 4. Key Results
- t0 ISQ fresh socket: 57.55 ± 1.93; healed: 72.86 ± 5.25 (p<0.001)
- t1 ISQ fresh socket: 68.68; healed: 74.54 (p=NS at 4 months)
- ITV ≥35 Ncm was the loading criterion applied for fresh sockets
- 100% survival rate at follow-up
- All implants loaded immediately regardless of ISQ (ITV was primary decision tool)

## 5. Limitations
- Retrospective design
- Small sample (41 implants)
- No control (non-immediate loading) group
- ITV not uniformly reported with SD

## 6. Related Work
- Andreatta 2020: simplified loading protocol with IT>40 Ncm criterion
- Andersson 2019: ISQ <70 at placement → higher failure risk
