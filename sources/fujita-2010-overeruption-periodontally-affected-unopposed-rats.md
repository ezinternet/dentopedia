## Why Ingested

Supplies a key modifier for the [[wiki/overviews/unopposed-tooth-overeruption-overview]] thesis that overeruption is a periodontal-ligament/alveolar-bone phenomenon (so whether a tooth is endodontically treated vs. vital is irrelevant — the active tissue is the periodontium, not the pulp). This controlled rat micro-CT experiment dissociates the two drivers — loss of antagonist and periodontitis — and shows periodontitis *amplifies* the supraposition of an unopposed tooth, with the largest effect on the lingual alveolar bone level. It reinforces [[wiki/occlusion/wang-2022-unopposed-molar-overeruption-implant-cbct]] (clinical CBCT magnitude of unopposed molar overeruption) by giving the mechanistic/animal-model counterpart for why compromised periodontium accelerates extrusion.

## One-line Summary

Animal study (60 adult male rats, 4 groups, 4 wk, micro-CT) showing loss of antagonist causes unopposed-molar overeruption that becomes significantly *larger* in the presence of experimentally induced periodontitis, with the lingual alveolar bone level lowered most.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Experimentally separates the two drivers of overeruption — **loss of antagonist** and **periodontitis** — in a controlled 2×2 design (with/without unopposed status × with/without induced periodontitis).
- Demonstrates that periodontitis does **not** by itself extrude an opposed (antagonist-present) molar, but **amplifies** overeruption once the antagonist is removed.
- Identifies the **lingual alveolar bone level** of unopposed periodontally affected molars as the site of greatest bone loss, locating the periodontal change that accompanies the enhanced extrusion.
- Provides the animal/mechanistic counterpart to clinical observations that compromised periodontal support accelerates supraeruption of unopposed teeth.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Animals**: 60 adult male rats, divided equally into **four groups** (n = 15 each).
- **Periodontitis induction**: in two of the four groups, periodontitis was induced by a **silk thread (ligature) placed around the cervix of the right mandibular molar**.
- **Unopposed-tooth simulation**: in two groups (one with and one without induced periodontitis), the crowns of the **right maxillary molars were reduced occlusally by grinding** to remove the antagonist and simulate unopposed teeth.
- **Group structure** (2×2): opposed/healthy; opposed/periodontitis; unopposed/healthy; unopposed/periodontitis.
- **Duration**: 4 weeks, after which animals were killed.
- **Imaging/measurement**: **micro-computed tomography (micro-CT)** to measure the **vertical position of molars** and the **buccal and lingual alveolar bone levels**.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Opposed molars (antagonist present)**: no significant difference in overeruption between periodontitis and healthy groups — periodontitis alone did not cause extrusion.
- However, the **alveolar bone level of opposed molars with periodontitis was lower** than that of healthy molars (confirming the ligature induced real periodontal breakdown).
- **Healthy unopposed molars**: extruded (overerupted) compared with molars that had an antagonist; their alveolar bone level was **not** influenced by the overeruption itself.
- **Unopposed molars with periodontitis**: exhibited **significantly larger extrusion** than healthy unopposed molars.
- The **lingual alveolar bone level** of unopposed periodontally affected molars was **lower than that of all other groups** (healthy and periodontally affected).
- **Conclusion**: Loss of antagonist causes overeruption of the unopposed tooth, which becomes **more prominent in the presence of periodontitis**.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Rat model — alveolar/periodontal biology and continuously erupting rodent dentition limit direct transfer to human overeruption magnitudes.
- Short observation window (4 weeks).
- Ligature-induced periodontitis is an acute experimental model, not equivalent to chronic human periodontitis.
- Quantitative magnitudes (μm of extrusion, mm of bone level change) and statistical values are not recoverable from the retrieved landing-page text (abstract-level detail available).

## 7. Glossary

- **Overeruption / supraposition / supraeruption**: continued vertical eruption (extrusion) of a tooth that has lost its opposing (antagonist) tooth.
- **Unopposed tooth**: a tooth with no occluding antagonist in the opposing arch.
- **Ligature-induced periodontitis**: experimental periodontitis created by tying a silk thread around the tooth cervix to accumulate plaque and provoke periodontal breakdown.
- **Alveolar bone level**: the height of the supporting bone crest around a tooth; lowering indicates bone loss/attachment loss.
- **Micro-CT (micro-computed tomography)**: high-resolution 3D X-ray imaging used here to measure tooth vertical position and bone levels.
- **Antagonist**: the opposing tooth that occludes against a given tooth.
