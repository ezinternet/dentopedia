## Why Ingested

[[endodontics/irrigation/fortea-2024-chelating-agents-endodontic-treatment-sr]]가 7% maleic acid를 포함했으나, 본 scoping review가 MA 단독으로 apical smear layer 제거·항균·접착 이점을 심화. EDTA 한계(특히 apical third) 대안 근거.

## One-line Summary
Scoping review (PRISMA-ScR) concluding 7% maleic acid is a potent root-canal irrigant with consistent superiority in apical smear-layer removal and adhesion/regeneration advantages, best at short (1–3 min) conditioning to limit erosion.

## 한줄요약
Scoping review: 7% 말레산(maleic acid)은 apical smear layer 제거에서 일관된 우월성과 접착·재생 이점을 가진 강력 세정제이며, 침식 최소화 위해 1–3분 단시간 적용이 최적.

## 1. Document Information
- BMC Oral Health 2026;26:76 (article 12903-025-07361-9). Manipal. PRISMA-ScR + Arksey & O'Malley framework.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Consolidates maleic-acid evidence: smear-layer removal, antimicrobial efficacy, dentin effects, material interactions.
- Recommends short conditioning times (1–3 min) to balance efficacy vs erosion.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Scoping review (PRISMA-ScR 2018; Arksey & O'Malley); maps role of MA across endodontic outcomes.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- 7% MA superior to EDTA for apical smear-layer removal; advantages in adhesion and (vs EDTA) calcium-hydroxide elimination/regeneration.
- Demineralizing effect requires controlled, short application.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Scoping (heterogeneous evidence); long-term clinical data limited; standardized concentration/time needed.

## 6. Related Work
- MA deep-dive complementing the broader chelating-agents SR.

## 7. Glossary
- Maleic acid (MA): mild low-pH organic acid chelator/conditioner.
- Apical third: region where EDTA smear-layer removal is least effective.
