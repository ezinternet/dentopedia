## Why Ingested

바늘없는/컴퓨터 제어 압력 침윤마취기(STA, Single Tooth Anesthesia System) 관련 질의에 대응하기 위해 인제스트. 기존 [[local-anesthesia/ramanathan-2023-efficacy-reliability-single-tooth-anesthesia]]가 외과적(매복 제3대구치 발치) 적응증에서 WANDSTA를 IANB와 비교한 반면, 본 RCT는 통상적 수복치료(class I/II restoration) 상황에서 STA를 전통적 침윤마취와 직접 비교해 STA의 근거 기반을 비외과적 일상 진료 영역으로 확장한다.

## One-line Summary

RCT (n=80) comparing computer-controlled Single Tooth Anesthesia (STA) system versus traditional local infiltration for maxillary posterior restorative treatment: no significant difference in perceived pain (p=0.59) or systolic BP (p=0.09) during the anesthetic injection itself, but STA patients reported significantly less pain during the restorative procedure (p<0.001) and had significantly better treatment experience and future preference (p=0.04), despite significantly higher heart rate throughout (already elevated at baseline).

## 한줄요약

RCT(n=80): 상악 구치부 수복치료에서 컴퓨터 제어 단일치아마취(Single Tooth Anesthesia, STA) 시스템과 전통적 침윤마취를 비교한 결과, 주사 시 통증(p=0.59)과 수축기혈압(p=0.09)은 유의차가 없었으나, 수복治료 중 STA군의 통증이 유의하게 낮았고(p<0.001) 치료 경험 만족도와 향후 재선호도도 STA가 유의하게 높았음(p=0.04). 다만 STA군의 심박수는 기저치부터 이미 높았고 전 구간에서 유의하게 상승.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Comparison of perceived pain and patients' satisfaction with traditional local anesthesia and single tooth anesthesia: A randomized clinical trial
- **Authors**: Al-Obaida MI, Haider M, Hashim R, AlGheriri W, Celur SL, Al-Saleh SA, Al-Madi EM
- **Journal**: World Journal of Clinical Cases, 2019 Oct 6;7(19):2986-2994
- **DOI**: 10.12998/wjcc.v7.i19.2986
- **PMID**: 31624746 / **PMCID**: PMC6795719
- **Study type**: Parallel-arm randomized controlled trial (RCT), n=80
- **Setting**: Princess Nourah bint Abdulrahman University Dental Clinics, Riyadh, Saudi Arabia; IRB No. H-01-R-059

## 2. Key Contributions

- Direct RCT comparison of the STA System (Milestone Scientific; computer-controlled intra-ligamentary local anesthetic delivery, dynamic pressure sensing) against traditional infiltration for a **non-surgical, everyday restorative** indication (class I/II restorations on maxillary premolars/molars) — a gap the authors explicitly note is under-studied relative to nerve-block/surgical STA comparisons.
- Multi-timepoint measurement (before/during/after injection, and during the procedure itself) of perceived pain (Wong-Baker FACES), heart rate, and systolic blood pressure, rather than a single injection-pain endpoint.
- Captures a dissociation: STA offers **no injection-pain advantage** but a **procedural-comfort and satisfaction advantage** — clarifying which part of the STA value proposition actually holds up under a parallel-arm (non-split-mouth) design.
- Notes methodological critique of prior favorable STA literature (undisclosed nerve-block vs infiltration comparator, topical anesthetic confounds, split-mouth carryover effects) and explains why this trial deliberately used a parallel-arm design instead.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Parallel-arm (not split-mouth) RCT; computer-generated randomization into STA group (n=40) vs traditional infiltration group (n=40).
- **Inclusion**: Adults >18y, ASA ≤2, needing class I/II restorations on maxillary premolars/molars, normal periodontium, no periapical pathology (confirmed radiographically).
- **Exclusion**: ASA >2, psychiatric illness/allergy history, lidocaine sensitivity, analgesic use in prior 12h, history of unpleasant dental treatment experience.
- **Blinding**: Patients not informed of group assignment; STA unit always powered on with audible feedback disabled in both groups to mask device identity.
- **STA protocol**: 2% lidocaine + 1:100,000 epinephrine, 1.8 mL (multi-rooted) or 0.9 mL (single-rooted), STA-intraligamentary injection mode, speed 0.005 mL/s, 30-gauge extra-short needle, inserted mesiobuccal/distobuccal at 45° (molars) or single mesial/distal point (premolars).
- **Control**: 2% lidocaine + 1:100,000 epinephrine via standard infiltration technique.
- **Outcome measures**: Wong-Baker FACES pain scale (before/during/after injection, and during procedure — English + Arabic versions); heart rate and blood pressure at same timepoints; objective anesthetic efficiency via electric pulp testing every 10 min up to 60 min; post-procedure validated questionnaire on treatment experience, anxiety, numbness, and future-method preference (1–10 scale).
- **Statistics**: Paired t-test for within-group pre/post comparisons; unpaired t-test for between-group comparisons (pain, HR, SBP, satisfaction).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Injection pain: no significant difference between STA and infiltration before (p=0.72), during (p=0.59), or after (p=0.19) anesthesia.
- Procedure pain: STA significantly lower than infiltration during the restorative procedure (p=0.008 between-group; within-group before-vs-during-procedure drop p<0.001 for STA vs p=0.18 NS for infiltration).
- Heart rate: STA significantly higher than infiltration at all timepoints (before p<0.001, during p=0.03, after p<0.001), but baseline group difference existed pre-injection and absolute values stayed within normal range; number of patients showing HR increase during anesthesia was similar between groups (22/40 STA vs 25/40 infiltration), suggesting comparable anxiety rather than a drug/device-specific cardiovascular effect.
- Systolic BP: no significant between-group difference at any timepoint (p=0.39, 0.09, 0.11).
- Post-procedure satisfaction: STA had significantly better treatment experience (p=0.04) and significantly higher preference for the same method in future (p=0.04); anxiety levels (p=0.2) and achieved numbness (p=0.08) not significantly different.
- Anesthetic efficiency (pulp testing): infiltration group had more patients responsive early (3 at 10 min, 1 at 20 min) vs STA (2 at 10 min), suggesting STA had a longer/more reliable duration of action during the procedure window.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Restricted to maxillary posterior restorative procedures only; generalizability to mandibular teeth, surgical procedures, or other restoration classes not established.
- Parallel-arm (not split-mouth) design trades within-patient control for reduced carryover bias — a considered trade-off, not an oversight, but it does increase between-subject variance.
- Baseline heart-rate imbalance between groups (present even before anesthesia) complicates interpretation of the "STA raises heart rate" finding — likely reflects randomization variance/anxiety rather than a true device effect.
- Authors explicitly call for further studies on STA onset/duration kinetics and broader out-patient procedure applicability.

## 6. Related Work

- Discussed against Campanella et al, Garret-Bernardin et al, and Grace et al (split-mouth designs, mixed nerve-block/infiltration comparators) — this trial's parallel-arm, single-technique-comparator design was chosen specifically to address confounds in that prior literature.
- Complements [[local-anesthesia/ramanathan-2023-efficacy-reliability-single-tooth-anesthesia]], which evaluates STA (WANDSTA) against IANB for surgical third-molar removal — together the two papers span STA's evidence base across non-surgical restorative and surgical extraction indications.

## 7. Glossary

- **STA (Single Tooth Anesthesia) System**: Milestone Scientific computer-controlled local anesthetic delivery system (CCLAD) providing controlled flow-rate intra-ligamentary injection with visual/audible feedback and periodontal-ligament-tissue detection.
- **CCLAD**: Computer-Controlled Local Anesthetic Delivery System — device class introduced 1997, using motor-driven plunger control (vs manual syringe pressure) to standardize injection rate.
- **Wong-Baker FACES pain scale**: Six-face pictorial self-report pain scale used across age groups, including where numeric scales are harder to use.
- **SBP**: Systolic blood pressure.
- **Intra-ligamentary injection**: Anesthetic delivery into the periodontal ligament space, producing localized single-tooth anesthesia with reduced soft-tissue/facial numbness compared to nerve blocks or wide-field infiltration.
