## Why Ingested

The interdental-cleaning collection has efficacy and head-to-head device RCTs but no documented infection-control hazard signal. This case report adds the safety counterweight: a water flosser filled with nonsterile tap water harbored *Legionella pneumophila* serogroup 1 (Lp1) biofilm and was the genomically confirmed source of nosocomial Legionnaires' disease in an immunocompromised patient. It refines the favorable safety read of [[interdental-cleaning/mancinelli-lyle-2024-water-flosser-vs-interdental-brush-rct]] by flagging a low-incidence but serious device-reservoir risk that benign-population RCTs cannot surface, and it belongs in [[overviews/interdental-cleaning-devices-synthesis]] as the device's adverse-safety anchor.

## One-line Summary

Case report (n=1, France 2026): a 48-yo myeloma/auto-HSCT patient developed fatal-risk nosocomial Legionnaires' disease whose Lp1 isolate had an *identical* whole genome (sequence type 42) to Lp1 cultured from his home water flosser (300 CFU/L) while all hospital water sources tested negative — first literature evidence that nonsterile-water water flossers can aerosolize *Legionella* biofilm and infect immunocompromised patients.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Case of *Legionella pneumophila* Serogroup 1 Infection Linked to Water Flosser, France
- **Authors**: Slekovec C, Biguenet A, Jeanvoine A, Hocquet D, Bertrand X
- **Journal**: Emerging Infectious Diseases, 2026 Feb; 32(2)
- **DOI**: 10.3201/eid3202.251386 · **PMID**: 41714876 · **PMCID**: PMC12928229
- **Type**: Single case report with molecular source attribution (whole-genome sequencing) and environmental sampling.
- **Setting**: Hematology unit, Besançon University Hospital, France, August 2024.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First reported transmission of Legionnaires' disease via a water flosser** (oral irrigator), establishing a previously unrecognized extra-hospital source of nosocomial *Legionella* infection.
2. **Genomic source attribution**: the patient's Lp1 isolate and the Lp1 isolate from his personal water flosser had *identical* whole genomes (sequence type 42), while every hospital water source (filtered/unfiltered sink and shower, toilet, ward network) tested negative — a clean molecular link rather than circumstantial association.
3. **Mechanistic framing of the device as a hazard**: nonsterile tap water + lack of reservoir cleaning → biofilm in tubing; pressurized oral jet → inhalable aerosol → risk concentrated in immunocompromised hosts.
4. **Infection-control message for healthcare settings**: patient-owned devices brought from home are not captured by admission inventories and bypass hospital *Legionella* controls.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Clinical case**: 48-yo man with IgG-Kappa myeloma admitted for peripheral autologous hematopoietic stem cell transplantation (auto-HSCT); fever and *Legionella*-positive blood culture on day 10; respiratory deterioration day 16–17; MICU transfer with positive BinaxNOW urinary antigen (Lp1), PCR-positive bronchoalveolar lavage, and culture-positive endotracheal aspirate.
- **Environmental sampling** (NF T 90-431 standard): 500 mL water in sterile vials with 20 mg sodium thiosulfate, inoculated on GVPC plates, incubated 36 °C for 8–11 days, identified by MALDI-TOF MS. Sources sampled: bathroom sink and shower (with and without anti-*Legionella* Filt'Ray 2G filter), toilet bowl, and the patient's water flosser (tank filled with sterile water, device run, 100 mL collected from the jet).
- **Molecular linkage**: whole-genome sequencing of the two Lp1 isolates (patient + water flosser) via Oxford Nanopore; assembly with Flye; sequence type determined with legsta.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Hospital sources negative**: mixed sink/shower water (filtered and unfiltered) and toilet bowl showed no *Legionella*; all 10 routine 2024 ward-network tests were negative.
- **Water flosser positive**: Lp1 *and* *Legionella* serogroup 2–14 recovered at **300 CFU/L** from the device jet; reservoir showed fouling consistent with nonsterile-water use and absent cleaning.
- **Genomic identity**: patient and water-flosser Lp1 isolates had **identical genomes, sequence type 42** (a known but non-major clone in France and elsewhere) → common origin, most likely the patient's home water network.
- **Clinical course**: acute respiratory distress syndrome, mechanical ventilation, dialysis, cardiorespiratory arrest (no sequelae), multi-organ failure, extremity necrosis, sacral ulcer; **62-day total hospitalization including 47 days in MICU**; patient survived. No further hospital cases after the incident.
- **Hospital-acquired Legionnaires' mortality is ~3× community-acquired** (cited 8.6% vs 28.6%), underscoring the stakes in immunocompromised inpatients.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **n=1**: single case; no cleaning/use history was obtainable (patient could not be interviewed), so frequency of device use during hospitalization and water type are unknown.
- **Low incidence**: no other water-flosser *Legionella* cases exist in the literature, indicating a low (but possibly underrecognized) risk; routine devices are not screened, so unrecognized cases cannot be excluded.
- **Generalizability**: the hazard is concentrated in immunocompromised/hospitalized hosts; the report does not establish risk for healthy users.
- **Future direction**: awareness among manufacturers and users; controlled use of patient-owned aerosol-generating devices in healthcare settings; capture of such devices in admission inventories.

## 6. Related Work

- Prior studies have shown bacterial colonization of water flossers and transmission via contaminated jets, including colonization by major caries-associated pathogens that is difficult to prevent — this report extends that body of work to a confirmed *Legionella* infection.

## 7. Glossary

- **Water flosser / oral irrigator**: device delivering a pressurized water jet for interdental cleaning; reservoir is typically filled with tap water.
- **Legionnaires' disease**: severe pneumonia from inhaling aerosols contaminated with *Legionella* spp.; high mortality in hospitalized/immunocompromised patients.
- **Lp1 (Legionella pneumophila serogroup 1)**: the serogroup most associated with human disease.
- **Auto-HSCT**: autologous hematopoietic stem cell transplantation — a profoundly immunocompromising procedure.
- **CFU/L**: colony-forming units per liter, a bacterial concentration measure.
- **Sequence type (ST)**: whole-genome/MLST-derived strain identifier; ST42 here linked patient and device isolates.
- **Biofilm**: surface-attached microbial community (here, within device tubing) that resists clearance and seeds the water jet.
