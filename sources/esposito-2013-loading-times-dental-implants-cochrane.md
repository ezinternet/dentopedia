## Why Ingested

임플란트 로딩 타이밍(즉시/조기/통상 부하)의 임상적 차이를 근거중심으로 정리하기 위해 인제스트. 기존 [[wiki/implants/benic-2014-loading-protocols-single-implant-crowns-sr-ma]]가 단일 임플란트 크라운에 집중하는 반면, 본 Cochrane SR(pub5)은 전악 범위를 포괄하며 부하 시기 전반을 다루는 최고 수준의 근거 문서임.

## One-line Summary

Cochrane SR+MA (26 RCTs, n=1217, 2120 implants): no clinically significant difference in implant/prosthesis failure or marginal bone loss between immediate, early, and conventional loading at 1 year; insertion torque ≥35 Ncm recommended for immediate/early loading.

## 한줄요약

코크란 체계적 문헌고찰(26개 RCT, 1217명, 2120개 임플란트): 즉시·조기·통상 부하 간 임플란트/보철 실패율 및 변연골 소실에서 임상적으로 유의한 차이 없음; 즉시/조기 부하 전제조건으로 삽입 토크 ≥35 Ncm 권고.

- **Affiliation**: Cochrane Oral Health Group, University of Manchester, UK

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Highest-level evidence synthesis** on implant loading timing — 5th update of a landmark Cochrane review covering RCTs up to June 2012
2. **Three loading definitions standardized**: Immediate (within 1 week), Early (1 week–2 months), Conventional (after 2 months)
3. **No statistically significant difference** in prosthesis failure or implant failure for immediate vs conventional loading (RR 1.90; 95% CI 0.67–5.34 for prosthesis; RR 1.50; 95% CI 0.60–3.77 for implant)
4. **Marginal bone level**: a small but statistically significant bone level change favoring immediate loading (MD -0.10 mm; 95% CI -0.20 to -0.01; P=0.03; 9 trials) — but judged clinically insignificant
5. **Overall failure rate low**: mean implant failure rate of 2.5% across all 26 trials
6. **Key prerequisite**: insertion torque ≥35 Ncm for immediate/early loading success
7. **Evidence quality**: very low (GRADE) due to high/unclear risk of bias in most primary studies and evidence of reporting bias

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Study design**: Cochrane systematic review with meta-analysis
- **Search date**: June 8, 2012
- **Databases**: Cochrane Oral Health Group Trials Register, CENTRAL, MEDLINE (1946–2012), EMBASE (1980–2012); no language or date restrictions
- **Eligibility**: All RCTs of root-form osseointegrated dental implants, follow-up 4 months to 1 year, comparing same implant type under immediate/early/conventional loading, occlusal vs non-occlusal loading, or progressive loading
- **Outcomes**: Prosthesis failure, implant failure, radiographic marginal bone level changes
- **Analysis**: Fixed-effect models; mean differences (MD) for continuous outcomes; risk ratios (RR) for dichotomous outcomes; 95% confidence intervals; GRADE quality assessment
- **Included studies**: 26 of 45 identified RCTs; total 1217 participants, 2120 implants
- **Risk of bias**: 2 trials low risk, 12 high risk, 12 unclear

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Immediate vs Conventional Loading (15 RCTs)
- **Prosthesis failure**: RR 1.90 (95% CI 0.67–5.34); 8 trials — no significant difference
- **Implant failure**: RR 1.50 (95% CI 0.60–3.77); 10 trials — no significant difference
- **Marginal bone loss**: MD -0.10 mm (95% CI -0.20 to -0.01; P=0.03); 9 trials — statistically significant but clinically negligible; I² = 44%

### Early vs Conventional Loading (3 RCTs)
- Insufficient evidence for prosthesis failure, implant failure, or bone loss differences

### Immediate vs Early Loading (6 RCTs)
- Insufficient evidence for any outcome difference

### Occlusal vs Non-occlusal Loading (2 RCTs)
- Insufficient evidence for any outcome difference

### Progressive Loading
- No trials identified

### Summary of Findings (GRADE: very low quality for all comparisons)
- Low-risk population assumed: ~10/1000 prosthesis failures with conventional loading → ~19/1000 with immediate
- Mean overall implant failure rate: 2.5% across all 26 trials
- Insertion torque ≥35 Ncm identified as prerequisite for immediate/early loading

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Majority of included trials at high or unclear risk of bias
- Evidence of reporting bias
- Very short follow-up (minimum 4 months to 1 year) — long-term effects unknown
- Heterogeneity in implant systems, loading protocols, and patient populations
- No trials evaluating progressive loading
- Authors recommend more well-designed RCTs reported per CONSORT guidelines and registered with trial registries
- Evidence quality: GRADE very low for all main comparisons

## 7. Glossary

- **Immediate loading**: Prosthesis attached within 1 week of implant placement
- **Early loading**: Prosthesis attached between 1 week and 2 months post-placement
- **Conventional loading**: Prosthesis attached after 2 months post-placement (traditional 3–8 month healing period)
- **Occlusal loading**: Prosthesis placed in contact with opposing teeth
- **Non-occlusal loading**: Prosthesis not in contact with opposing teeth during healing
- **Insertion torque**: Rotational resistance force at implant placement; ≥35 Ncm considered prerequisite for immediate loading
- **RR (Risk Ratio)**: Relative risk comparing failure rates between loading groups
- **MD (Mean Difference)**: Average difference in marginal bone level change (mm)
- **GRADE**: Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation — evidence quality framework
- **Osseointegration**: Structural and functional connection between living bone and implant surface
