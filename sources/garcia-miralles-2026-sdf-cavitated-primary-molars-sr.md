## Why Ingested

기존 [[wiki/caries/zheng-2025-silver-diamine-fluoride-vs-varnish-caries-prevention-rct]]는 SDF의 우식 *예방* 효과를 다루지만, 이미 와동이 형성된 상아질 우식(cavitated dentinal lesion)을 *전통적 수복*과 직접 비교한 무작위 근거는 정리되지 않았다. 본 SR (Garcia-Miralles 2026)은 유구치 cavitated dentinal caries에서 silver diamine fluoride (SDF) vs 전통 수복을 head-to-head로 비교한 RCT만 합성해, SDF의 최소침습 적응증을 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review of RCTs (2 eligible trials, no quantitative pooling) finding that silver diamine fluoride (SDF) may yield favourable short-term (≤12 mo) caries-arrest and treatment-failure outcomes for cavitated dentinal lesions in primary molars versus conventional restorative treatment — but randomised evidence is limited.

## 한줄요약

RCT 체계적 문헌고찰(적격 시험 2편, 정량 통합 불가): 유구치의 와동성 상아질 우식에서 silver diamine fluoride (SDF, 은 다이아민 불화물)가 전통적 수복 대비 12개월 이내 단기 우식정지·실패율에서 유리할 수 있으나 무작위 근거는 제한적.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First focused systematic review restricting inclusion to **RCTs that directly compare SDF with conventional restorative treatments** for **cavitated dentinal caries in primary molars** (narrower question than the broad SDF literature, which mixes non-cavitated lesions and preventive comparators).
- Pre-specified clinical outcomes: **caries arrest (primary)** and **treatment failure/need for retreatment (secondary)** at ≤12 months, with effect estimates planned as **risk ratios (RR) with 95% CI**.
- Transparent reporting of the evidence gap: only **2 trials** (Zhi et al. 2012; CariedAway trial 2025) met eligibility; heterogeneity in outcome definitions and study design precluded **quantitative pooling**.
- Both included trials reported effect estimates consistently **in favour of SDF** for caries arrest and retreatment reduction, albeit with low-to-moderate certainty (GRADE).
- Provides specific RR values from each trial: Zhi 2012 — RR 1.57 (95% CI 1.13–2.17) for caries arrest, SDF vs GIC; CariedAway — RR 0.85 (95% CI 0.78–0.94) for treatment failure, SDF vs ART.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Protocol**: PRISMA 2020 compliant; PROSPERO pre-registration; PICO framework; no deviations from registered protocol.
- **Search databases**: MEDLINE (PubMed), Embase (OVID), Cochrane CENTRAL, Scopus — final search October 2025; no language, date, or geographic restrictions; supplemented by manual reference-list screening.
- **Eligibility (PICO)**:
  - **Population**: children aged ≤12 years with cavitated dentinal caries in primary molars (recognised clinical and/or radiographic criteria).
  - **Intervention**: SDF application (any concentration, frequency, or follow-up duration).
  - **Comparator**: conventional restorative treatments — ART, GIC restorations, composite resin, stainless steel crowns, Hall technique.
  - **Primary outcome**: caries arrest at ≤12 months.
  - **Secondary outcome**: treatment failure/need for retreatment within the same follow-up.
  - **Study design**: RCTs or controlled clinical trials only; observational studies, case reports, and narrative reviews excluded.
- **Study selection**: 433 records initially identified (427 electronic + 6 citation), 391 duplicates removed, 42 screened by title/abstract, 48 full-text assessed, 46 excluded, **2 RCTs included**.
- **Data extraction**: piloted extraction form; two reviewers independently; discrepancies resolved by consensus or third reviewer; lesion-level and participant-level data extracted and analysed separately.
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane Risk of Bias 2 (RoB 2) instrument; five domains assessed; two independent reviewers.
- **Statistical analysis**: R v4.3.1 (meta, metafor packages); random-effects model (Hartung–Knapp–Sidik–Jonkman method) planned if pooling feasible; cluster-RCT adjusted with ICC-based design effects; continuity correction 0.5 for zero cells; heterogeneity via I², τ², and 95% prediction intervals; pre-planned subgroup analyses (SDF concentration, application frequency, comparator type); publication bias via funnel plots/Egger's test only if ≥10 studies available.
- **Synthesis**: quantitative meta-analysis **not performed** due to heterogeneity; qualitative narrative synthesis and GRADE assessment.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Study inclusion**
- 433 records screened → **2 RCTs included**: Zhi et al. 2012 (China) and CariedAway trial (USA, 2025).

**Included studies**

| Study | Design | Setting | Population | Intervention vs Comparator | Outcome | Follow-up |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Zhi et al. 2012 | Individual RCT | Kindergartens, China | Preschool 3–4 yr; cavitated dentinal caries in primary teeth | 38% SDF annual or semi-annual vs high-fluoride GIC | Caries arrest (lesion level) | 12, 18, 24 mo; 12-mo extracted |
| CariedAway Trial 2025 | Cluster-RCT | School-based programmes, USA | Children 5–13 yr; ICDAS 5–6 cavitated primary molars | 38% SDF vs ART | Treatment failure/retreatment (participant level) | Up to 48 mo; 12-mo extracted |

**Risk of bias (RoB 2)**
- Zhi et al. 2012: **low risk** overall (minor concern: measurement of outcomes domain).
- CariedAway 2025: **some concerns** overall (cluster design, baseline imbalances, measurement of outcomes; remaining domains low risk).
- No study rated at high risk of bias.

**Primary outcome — caries arrest (≤12 months)**
- Zhi et al.: 38% SDF (annual and semi-annual) vs high-fluoride GIC → **RR = 1.57 (95% CI 1.13–2.17; p < 0.05)** significantly more arrested lesions with SDF. No clinically relevant difference between annual and semi-annual application schedules.
- GRADE certainty: **low** (⬤⬤◯◯) — limited sample size, single RCT for this outcome.

**Secondary outcome — treatment failure / retreatment (≤12 months)**
- CariedAway: SDF vs ART → treatment failure in **45.5% (SDF) vs 53.3% (ART)**: **RR = 0.85 (95% CI 0.78–0.94)**, statistically significant reduction in retreatment needs favouring SDF.
- GRADE certainty: **moderate** (⬤⬤⬤◯) — single cluster-RCT (n=1,668 participants); some concerns on design.

**Overall**
- Both trials consistently favoured SDF.
- Quantitative pooling not performed (heterogeneous outcome definitions and designs).
- GRADE certainty ranges from **low** (caries arrest) to **moderate** (retreatment).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Very small evidence base**: only 2 eligible RCTs → limited statistical power and generalisability.
- **No meta-analysis**: heterogeneity in outcome definitions (lesion-level vs participant-level) and study designs (individually randomised vs cluster-RCT) prevented pooling.
- **Short follow-up**: ≤12 months; durability of caries arrest and long-term restorative/retreatment needs unknown.
- **Cultural/setting specificity**: Zhi et al. (China) and CariedAway (USA school-based programmes) — may not generalise to other populations and healthcare systems.
- **Protocol heterogeneity**: application frequency (annual vs semi-annual) explored only within Zhi et al.; concentration comparisons (12% vs 38%) not evaluable across trials.
- **Missing evidence**: no data on aesthetic outcomes (black staining acceptability), patient/parent perspectives, or SDF combined with other minimally invasive strategies (e.g., SDF before GIC or Hall crown).
- **Future research needs**: standardised diagnostic criteria for cavitated dentinal caries; follow-up >2 years; diverse populations; caregiver perspectives; combination strategies (SDF + GIC or Hall crown).

## 7. Glossary

- **Silver diamine fluoride (SDF)**: topical antimicrobial/remineralising agent (typically 38%) that arrests dentinal caries via Ag⁺ antibacterial action (protein denaturation, membrane disruption) + fluoride-driven fluorapatite formation; stains arrested lesions black.
- **Cavitated dentinal lesion (와동성 상아질 우식)**: a caries lesion with frank cavitation extending into dentin (ICDAS 5–6), beyond the non-cavitated enamel/white-spot stage.
- **Caries arrest (우식 정지)**: clinical halting of an active lesion (hardening/darkening) without surgical removal.
- **ART (atraumatic restorative treatment, 비외상적 수복치료)**: minimal cavity preparation using hand instruments followed by GIC restoration; requires no electricity or rotary instruments.
- **GIC (glass ionomer cement, 글래스아이오노머 시멘트)**: fluoride-releasing restorative material; high-fluoride GIC used as comparator in Zhi et al.
- **Hall technique**: stainless steel crown placed without cavity preparation over carious primary molars.
- **Risk ratio (RR, 위험비)**: ratio of event probability in the intervention group to that in the comparator; RR > 1 = more events in intervention (favourable for caries arrest); RR < 1 = fewer events in intervention (favourable for failure/retreatment reduction).
- **RoB 2**: Cochrane Risk of Bias 2 tool for randomised trials; five domains: randomisation, deviations from intended interventions, missing outcome data, outcome measurement, selective reporting.
- **GRADE**: framework rating certainty of evidence as high/moderate/low/very low; low certainty = further research likely to change confidence in the estimate.
- **ICDAS**: International Caries Detection and Assessment System; scores 5–6 indicate extensive dentinal cavitation.
- **CariedAway trial**: US cluster-RCT (Ruff et al. 2023/2025) evaluating SDF vs ART in school-based programmes; one of the two included trials.
