## Why Ingested

기존 [[wiki/caries/zheng-2025-silver-diamine-fluoride-vs-varnish-caries-prevention-rct]]는 SDF의 우식 *예방* 효과를 다루지만, 이미 와동이 형성된 상아질 우식(cavitated dentinal lesion)을 *전통적 수복*과 직접 비교한 무작위 근거는 정리되지 않았다. 본 SR (Garcia-Miralles 2026)은 유구치 cavitated dentinal caries에서 silver diamine fluoride (SDF) vs 전통 수복을 head-to-head로 비교한 RCT만 합성해, SDF의 최소침습 적응증을 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review of RCTs (2 eligible trials, no pooling) finding that silver diamine fluoride (SDF) may yield favourable short-term (≤12 mo) caries-arrest and treatment-failure outcomes for cavitated dentinal lesions in primary molars versus conventional restorative treatment — but randomised evidence is limited.

RCT 체계적 문헌고찰(적격 시험 2편, 정량 통합 불가): 유구치의 와동성 상아질 우식에서 silver diamine fluoride (SDF, 은 다이아민 불화물)가 전통적 수복 대비 12개월 이내 단기 우식정지·실패율에서 유리할 수 있으나 무작위 근거는 제한적.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First focused systematic review restricting inclusion to **RCTs that directly compare SDF with conventional restorative treatment** for **cavitated dentinal caries in primary molars** (a narrower, more clinically decisive question than the broad SDF-vs-anything literature).
- Pre-specified clinical outcomes: **caries arrest** and **treatment failure / need for retreatment at ≤12 months**, with effect estimates as **risk ratios (RR) with 95% CI**.
- Honest reporting of the evidence gap: only **2 trials** met eligibility, and **heterogeneity in outcome definitions and study design precluded quantitative pooling** — so the review frames SDF as a promising minimally invasive option rather than a proven equivalent.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Systematic review of RCTs following standard SR methodology (qualitative synthesis; meta-analysis not feasible).
- **Databases searched**: MEDLINE (PubMed), Embase, Cochrane CENTRAL, and Scopus, **up to October 2025**, supplemented by manual screening of reference lists.
- **Eligibility (PICO)**:
  - **Population**: children with cavitated dentinal lesions in primary molars.
  - **Intervention**: silver diamine fluoride (SDF).
  - **Comparator**: conventional restorative treatments.
  - **Study design**: randomised controlled trials only.
- **Outcomes extracted**: caries arrest; treatment failure or need for retreatment at ≤12 months. Effect estimates reported as **risk ratios with 95% CI**.
- **Synthesis**: **2 trials** met eligibility; due to heterogeneity in outcome definitions and study design, **quantitative pooling was not feasible** (narrative synthesis).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Included trials**: only **2 RCTs** met the eligibility criteria.
- **Pooling**: **not performed** — heterogeneity in outcome definitions and study design made quantitative meta-analysis infeasible.
- **Direction of effect**: available randomised evidence is limited but **suggests SDF may provide favourable short-term (≤12 month) outcomes** for cavitated dentinal caries in primary molars compared with conventional restorative treatment.
- **Effect measure used**: risk ratios with 95% confidence intervals (specific point estimates not given in the abstract).
- **Bottom line**: SDF may offer a **minimally invasive option** for managing cavitated caries in primary molars; its **simplicity and low cost** make it particularly relevant in **young children and community-based settings**.
- (Abstract-only: per-trial sample sizes, individual RR values, and CIs are not reported in the retrieved text.)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Very small evidence base**: only 2 eligible RCTs — conclusions are tentative.
- **No meta-analysis**: heterogeneity in outcome definitions and study design prevented quantitative pooling, limiting the strength of synthesis.
- **Short follow-up**: outcomes assessed at ≤12 months only; durability of caries arrest and longer-term restorative/retreatment needs unknown.
- **Authors' call**: further well-designed RCTs with **longer follow-up** are needed before SDF can be positioned as equivalent to conventional restoration.

## 7. Glossary

- **Silver diamine fluoride (SDF)**: topical antimicrobial/remineralising agent (typically 38%) that arrests dentinal caries; minimally invasive, low cost, stains the arrested lesion black.
- **Cavitated dentinal lesion**: a caries lesion with frank cavitation extending into dentin (beyond non-cavitated enamel/white-spot stage).
- **Caries arrest**: clinical halting of an active lesion (hardening/darkening) without surgical removal.
- **Risk ratio (RR)**: ratio of the probability of an outcome in the intervention group to that in the comparator group; RR with 95% CI was the planned effect measure.
- **Conventional restorative treatment**: traditional caries removal and placement of a restoration (e.g., glass-ionomer or composite restoration).
- **Primary molar**: deciduous (baby) molar tooth.
