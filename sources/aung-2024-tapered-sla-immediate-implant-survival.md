## Why Ingested

A site-specific (positional) survival breakdown for immediately-placed tapered SLA bone-level implants — it sharpens the pooled SR+MA picture of [[immediate-implant/patel-2023-immediate-vs-delayed-implant-survival-sr-ma]] by showing *where* immediate placement fails (maxillary anterior/molar, all sinus-related) vs where it is reliable (mandible 100%, premolar 100%), and complements the tapered-implant primary-stability focus of [[immediate-implant/sierra-rebolledo-2021-undersized-drilling-immediate-tapered-implants-maxilla]].

## One-line Summary

Retrospective single-surgeon SNU study of 49 immediately placed tapered SLA internal-submerged bone-level implants (34 patients, 5–14 yr follow-up): cumulative 5-yr survival 93.88%, with 100% in the mandible and both premolar regions, lowest in maxillary anterior (85.38%); all 3 failures were maxillary sinus–related.

- **DOI**: [10.1186/s40902-024-00428-7](https://doi.org/10.1186/s40902-024-00428-7) · PMID 38727979 · PMCID PMC11087388
- **Study type**: Retrospective clinical/radiographic cohort, single surgeon, single center (SNUDH)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Reports 5-yr (mean 7.43 yr, range 5–14 yr) survival of 49 immediately placed **tapered, sand-blasted, acid-etched (SLA) internal submerged bone-level implants** (Luna®, Shinhung) in 34 patients.
- Frames outcomes as a **positional rationale**: survival differs by jaw region, and the authors translate this into site-by-site immediate-placement recommendations.
- Documents that **all failures clustered in the maxilla and were sinus-related**, reinforcing maxillary-sinus pathology as the dominant risk for immediate maxillary implants.
- Shows **progressive marginal bone gain** (not loss) around immediate implants through 5 years on SLA surfaces.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 2009–2018, one surgeon (S.M.K), Seoul National University Dental Hospital; IRB S-D20200007.
- **Inclusion**: Luna® tapered SLA internal submerged implant, immediate placement at any arch region, ≥5 yr panoramic follow-up.
- **Surgical**: flapless atraumatic extraction preferred; multi-rooted teeth sectioned to preserve interradicular septum/buccal plate; anterior fixtures placed slightly palatal with ≥1.5 mm buccal wall; posterior fixtures in mid-septum; immediate implants seated deeper than healed-site implants; bone grafting only when gap warranted (91.8% had **no** additional graft). Two-stage (re-entry 3–6 mo, prosthesis 2–3 mo later).
- **Survival**: ICOI Pisa Consensus health scale; survival = radiographic bone loss 2 mm–half implant body without mobility.
- **MBL**: panoramic radiographs at T0/T1(3 mo)/T2,T3(3 mo & 1 yr post-loading)/T4(5 yr); mesial+distal, triplicate; PiView-Star PACS; distortion-corrected.
- **Stats**: SPSS 26; Shapiro–Wilk; independent-sample test; one-way ANOVA for survival by location/diameter/length (p<0.05).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Distribution**: 49 implants / 34 patients — 23 mandible, 26 maxilla; 13 anterior, 12 premolar, 24 (48.98%) molar. Mean age 65.91 yr (40–86). Most common: 4 mm diameter (40.82%), 8.5 mm length (53.36%).
- **Cumulative 5-yr survival: 93.88%.**
  - Mandible anterior/premolar/molar: **100%**.
  - Maxilla premolar: **100%**.
  - Maxillary molar: 95.83%.
  - **Maxillary anterior: 85.38% (lowest).**
- **By diameter**: 3.5 & 5 mm = 100%; 4 mm = 96.2%; 4.5 mm = 84.61% (NS).
- **By length**: 10 & 11.5 mm = 100%; 7 mm = 87.5%; 8.5 mm = 93.75% (NS).
- **MBL**: significant marginal bone **gain** from 3 mo (mesial −0.68 ± 1.61 mm), progressive significant gain mesial+distal through 5 yr vs baseline (p<0.05); consecutive-appointment changes NS.
- **Failures**: 3/49 (6.12%) in 2 patients, **all maxillary sinus–related** — one #16 (HTN/renal transplant, sinusitis → loss of osseointegration at 5-mo re-entry), and #13/#23 in a denture patient with bilateral sinus pneumatization (peri-implantitis, pre-loading failure). All replaced after sinus lift/graft and functional at final.
- **Comorbidity**: 61.74% metabolic disease, 8.82% bone disease — no clear association with failure (failures driven by sinus anatomy/pathology).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Retrospective; single surgeon, single center → limited generalizability.
- No internal comparison arm against delayed placement.
- Small n (49 implants) → between-group survival differences (diameter, length) not statistically significant.
- Authors call for extended follow-up, larger samples, and comparative designs vs delayed placement.

## 6. Related Work

- Patel 2023 SR+MA — pooled immediate vs delayed survival (this paper adds positional granularity).
- Sierra-Rebolledo 2021 — undersized drilling for primary stability of immediate tapered implants in maxilla.
- Sodnom-Ish (same group) — 105 delayed tissue-level tapered SLA implants, 98.1% survival.
- Mustakim et al. — maxillary-molar immediate-placement socket/ABH classification used here.

## 7. Glossary

- **SLA**: sand-blasted, large-grit, acid-etched titanium surface (micro-rough, osteoconductive).
- **Bone-level implant**: implant–abutment junction at crestal bone (vs tissue-level above crest).
- **Internal submerged / two-stage**: implant covered by mucosa during healing, re-entered later.
- **Positional rationale**: site-specific immediate-placement decision logic by jaw region.
- **ABH**: alveolar bone height (Mustakim maxillary-molar classification).
- **MBL**: marginal bone loss (here, net gain observed).
- **ICOI Pisa Consensus**: implant health scale defining success/survival/failure.
