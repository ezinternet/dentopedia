## Why Ingested

각화점막 (Keratinized Mucosa, KM) 폭과 주위염 위험의 관계에 대한 기존 증거는 혼재되어 있었으며, [[wiki/implants/peri-implantitis/roccuzzo-2025-keratinized-mucosa-peri-implant-20year-mandible]]가 장기 관찰 단일기관 데이터를 제공하는 반면, 이 SR+MA는 교란변수를 통제한 16편의 횡단 연구 풀링 결과로 KM 부족이 독립적 위험인자임을 양적으로 확정하는 근거를 제공한다. 또한 [[wiki/implants/galarraga-vinueza-2025-peri-implant-disease-risk-factors]]의 위험인자 포괄 검토를 KM 특이적 OR 수치로 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 22 studies (n=4,044 patients, 13,265 implants) found that lack of keratinized mucosa is an independent risk factor for peri-implantitis (OR=2.78, 95% CI 2.07–3.74), consistent across multiple subgroup analyses adjusting for case definition, prosthesis type, and maintenance scheme.

## 한줄요약

22편 포함 SR+MA(환자 4,044명, 임플란트 13,265개)에서 각화점막 부족이 주위염의 독립적 위험인자(OR=2.78, 95% CI 2.07–3.74)임을 교란변수 통제 하위군 분석을 통해 일관되게 확인하였다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First SR+MA focused specifically on **peri-implantitis** (not broader peri-implant disease) as the outcome in relation to KM width.
2. Quantified risk across five distinct meta-analytic models, each controlling a different confounding dimension (case definition, prosthesis type, maintenance regime, multivariate adjustment).
3. Confirmed OR >2.0 for lack of KM across all subgroups — including the confounder-adjusted analysis (OR=3.68) — elevating KM adequacy from controversial to clinically actionable.
4. Demonstrated that heterogeneity drops to I²=0% when studies share a common peri-implantitis case definition (MBL ≥2 mm), supporting the call for standardized definitions in future research.
5. Graded overall certainty of evidence as **"moderate"** per the GRADE system, acknowledging cross-sectional study dominance.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**PICO**
- P: Partially or fully edentulous patients receiving dental implants
- I: Lack of adequate keratinized mucosa at the implant site
- C: Adequate keratinized mucosa present
- O: Occurrence of peri-implantitis

**Inclusion criteria**
- Human studies (cross-sectional, cohort, case-control; prospective or retrospective)
- ≥100 implants available for analysis
- ≥1 year postoperative follow-up
- Clear case definition of peri-implantitis

**Search and screening**
- 1,248 initial records → 968 after deduplication → 117 full-text assessed → 22 included in qualitative synthesis; 16 cross-sectional studies meta-analyzed
- Inter-examiner agreement: κ=0.80

**KM threshold definitions across included studies**
- <2 mm vs ≥2 mm: 13 studies
- <1 mm vs ≥1 mm: 2 studies
- Presence/absence only: 5 studies
- One study reported mean KM width (no cutoff)

**Statistical approach**
- Random-effects model (DerSimonian-Laird) when I²>0; fixed-effects when I²=0
- Sensitivity analysis: omitting one study at a time (OR range 2.53–2.96, no material change)
- Publication bias: funnel plot asymmetry assessed; slight asymmetry noted at small-effect end, not confirmed as true bias

**Quality scoring**: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale adapted for cross-sectional studies; scores 5–8/9

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Cross-sectional design dominance → only prevalence, not incidence, assessed
- Wide heterogeneity in case definitions of peri-implantitis across included studies
- Insufficient longitudinal data to perform cohort meta-analysis
- Site-specific confounders (implant location, bone augmentation history, implant surface type) not quantifiable from available data
- Optimal minimum KM width not established; arbitrary thresholds used across studies
- Single study for removable prosthesis (overdenture) — cannot generalize

**Future research needs**: Longitudinal RCTs or prospective cohorts with standardized peri-implantitis case definitions (ideally 2017 World Workshop criteria), reporting KM width at baseline and during follow-up, with multivariate confounder adjustment.

## 6. Related Work

- Wennström & Derks: concluded data on KM significance still scarce (pre-2023 review)
- Meta-analysis (cited as ref 30): KM <2 mm as risk factor still considered "low" evidence
- Kim et al. (cited as ref 48): inflammation increases with insufficient KM
- World Workshop 2017 (Berglundh): evidence on KM width and peri-implantitis insufficient at time of consensus

## 7. Glossary

- **KM (Keratinized Mucosa, 각화점막)**: Band of attached, keratinized soft tissue surrounding implants; measured in mm as KM width (KMW)
- **KMW (Keratinized Mucosa Width, 각화점막 폭)**: Typically dichotomized at 2 mm; <2 mm classified as "inadequate" in most studies
- **Peri-implantitis (주위염)**: Inflammatory condition around implant with progressive marginal bone loss (MBL) beyond initial remodeling, plus signs of mucosal inflammation (BoP, suppuration)
- **MBL (Marginal Bone Loss, 변연골소실)**: Radiographic bone loss at implant margins; threshold for peri-implantitis varied 0.5–3 mm across included studies
- **BoP (Bleeding on Probing, 탐침출혈)**: Universally required sign across all included case definitions
- **OR (Odds Ratio, 승산비)**: Ratio of odds of peri-implantitis in KM-inadequate vs KM-adequate groups
- **I² (Heterogeneity statistic, 이질성 지수)**: Percentage of variation between studies not due to sampling error; I²=52% (overall), 0% (homogeneous subgroups)
- **GRADE**: Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation — evidence certainty rated "moderate" in this review
- **PROSPERO**: International register of systematic reviews
- **PRISMA**: Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-analyses
