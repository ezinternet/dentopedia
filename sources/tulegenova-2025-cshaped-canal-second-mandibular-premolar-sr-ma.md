Extends our premolar C-shaped/morphology coverage with a pooled prevalence anchor. [[wiki/endodontics/anatomy/pertek-hatipoglu-2025-radicular-grooves-cshaped-taurodontism-premolars]] characterizes C-shaped/groove morphology and taurodontism in premolars at the descriptive level; this SR+MA supplies the missing population-level *how-common-is-it* number (pooled C-shaped canal prevalence in mandibular second premolars ≈1%) plus the geographic-variation signal, turning the morphology page into a prevalence-aware sibling.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (5 databases, random-effects) of C-shaped canal prevalence in adult mandibular second premolars: pooled 1.31% participant-level (95% CI 0.68–2.53) and 0.96% tooth-level (95% CI 0.46–2.00), high heterogeneity (I²=90–93%), highest in South America (5.83%), lowest in Australia (0.42%). Abstract-only.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides a pooled, population-level prevalence estimate for C-shaped canal anatomy specifically in the **mandibular second premolar** (a tooth less classically associated with C-shaped canals than the mandibular second molar).
- Reports two estimands side-by-side — **participant-level** (1.31%) and **tooth-level** (0.96%) — clarifying that prevalence depends on the denominator.
- Quantifies **geographic variation**, identifying South America as the highest-prevalence region and Australia as the lowest, supporting region-specific diagnostic vigilance.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PRISMA-guided systematic review.
- Databases searched (5): PubMed, Web of Science, ScienceDirect, Scopus, Google Scholar.
- Eligible designs: cross-sectional, prospective, and observational studies on C-shaped canal prevalence in **adult** mandibular second premolars.
- Random-effects model (RStudio) to pool prevalence and 95% CIs.
- Subgroup analysis by geographic region.
- Abstract does not report the exact number of included studies, study-selection counts, or risk-of-bias details (full text not retrieved).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Participant-level pooled prevalence**: 1.31% (95% CI 0.68–2.53; I²=90%; P<0.01).
- **Tooth-level pooled prevalence**: 0.96% (95% CI 0.46–2.00; I²=93%; P<0.01).
- **Heterogeneity**: substantial across both analyses (I²=90–93%).
- **Geographic — highest**: South America 5.83% (95% CI 0.57–40.04; I²=95%; P<0.01) — note the very wide CI.
- **Geographic — lowest**: Australia 0.42% (95% CI 0.31–0.93).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Abstract-only ingest** — full text (study counts, PRISMA flow, risk of bias, complete subgroup table) not available; numbers above are limited to those reported in the abstract.
- Very high heterogeneity (I²=90–95%) means the pooled point estimates should be read with caution; the South America subgroup CI (0.57–40.04) is so wide as to be near-uninformative (likely few/small studies).
- Imaging-modality variation (CBCT vs sectioning vs radiography) across primary studies is a plausible heterogeneity driver but is not resolved in the abstract.
- Region-specific and modality-stratified primary data would tighten estimates.

## 7. Glossary

- **C-shaped canal**: a root canal configuration where canals are connected by a fin/web forming a C in cross-section, named for its 180°-arc orifice; complicates instrumentation, irrigation, and obturation.
- **Pooled prevalence**: meta-analytic combined estimate of how common a finding is across studies.
- **Random-effects model**: meta-analysis model assuming true effect varies across studies; appropriate under high heterogeneity.
- **I² (heterogeneity)**: percentage of total variability across studies due to between-study heterogeneity rather than chance; >75% is high.
- **Participant-level vs tooth-level**: prevalence denominator is either people examined or individual teeth examined; the same dataset yields different rates.
