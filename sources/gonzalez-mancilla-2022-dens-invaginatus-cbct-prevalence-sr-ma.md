## Why Ingested

치아형태학 감시 인제스트 — dens invaginatus(치내치, dens in dente) CBCT 유병률. 상악 측절치 호발이며 invagination이 근관계를 복잡하게 만들어 근관치료 난이도와 예후에 직결되는 발달 이상. 기존 CBCT-기반 prevalence 페이지 [[endodontics/anatomy/betancourt-2016-mb2-prevalence-location-cbct]] (MB2 유병률·위치)와 같은 "CBCT가 2D 방사선보다 해부 변이를 더 많이 검출한다"는 주제를 dens invaginatus로 확장·보강한다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (4 retrospective studies, 2,009 CBCTs) — pooled CBCT-assessed prevalence of dens invaginatus is 9.0% (95% CI 7.2–10.8%), roughly double conventional-radiograph estimates (0.25–7.7%); maxillary lateral incisor most affected and Oehlers Type I most common.

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (후향연구 4편, CBCT 2,009건) — CBCT로 측정한 치내치(dens invaginatus) 통합 유병률 9.0% (95% CI 7.2–10.8%)로 2D 방사선 추정치(0.25–7.7%)의 약 2배. 상악 측절치 최다 호발, Oehlers Type I 최다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- **First SR+MA** of CBCT-assessed dens invaginatus prevalence (authors state none existed previously).
- **Pooled prevalence 9.0%** (95% CI 7.2–10.8%; p<0.001) across 2,009 CBCT images — 179 dens invaginatus cases. Random-effects model.
- Demonstrates the **CBCT vs 2D gap**: 9% (CBCT) vs the historically reported 0.25–7.7% from conventional radiographs; within Capar's head-to-head study CBCT detected 10.7% vs 3% on panoramic.
- Confirms **maxillary lateral incisor** as the most affected tooth (then maxillary central incisor) and **Oehlers Type I** as the most common form.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PRISMA-guided search of PubMed + SCOPUS up to 21 Oct 2021, no language/year limit. Terms: (dens invaginatus OR dens in dente) AND (prevalence OR frequency) AND (cone OR CBCT OR tomography).
- Inclusion: adults, dens invaginatus diagnosed **by CBCT**. Exclusion: conventional-radiography diagnosis, no prevalence, case reports.
- 7 articles screened → **4 included** (all retrospective). From Capar's comparative study only the CBCT arm was pooled.
- Statistics: OpenMeta Analyst; Tau² and Higgins I²; random-effects. Heterogeneity bands low <25 / slight 25–50 / moderate 50–75 / high >75%.
- Quality: Oxford evidence scale (all 3b, retrospective → moderate); Hoy Risk of Bias Tool for prevalence studies — 80% parameters reported = low overall risk of bias.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Pooled prevalence: 0.090 (9.0%), 95% CI 0.072–0.108 (7.2–10.8%), p<0.001.** 179/2,009.
- **Heterogeneity: I² = 43.73% (slight); Tau² = 0.00** (df 5.332, p=0.149).
- Individual studies (patient-level prevalence):
  - **Chen** — 1,004 patients (Chinese/Beijing), 85 cases = **8.47%**; higher in males (10.75% vs 6.68%).
  - **Alkadi** — 505 patients (Saudi), 2,790 maxillary anterior teeth, 37 cases = **7.3%**; no gender difference.
  - **Mabrouk** — 200 patients (Tunisian), 4,945 anterior teeth, 25 cases = **12.5%**; men higher (NS).
  - **Capar** — 300 patients (Turkish), CBCT 32 cases = **10.7%** vs panoramic 9 cases = **3%**; no gender difference.
- Most-affected tooth across all studies: **maxillary lateral incisor** > maxillary central incisor.
- Most common Oehlers type: **Type I** (per Oehlers' own historical frequency, 79%).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only **4 included studies** (small evidence base); all retrospective (Oxford 3b).
- Convenience/regional samples (Chen = Beijing; Alkadi) may limit generalizability.
- Note: abstract cites the "Robins-I tool" but Methods/Results describe the Hoy prevalence-RoB tool — minor internal inconsistency.
- Authors call for large-scale, multi-centre studies.

## 7. Glossary

- **Dens invaginatus (dens in dente)**: developmental anomaly — enamel/dentin invaginates from the outer surface toward the pulp before calcification.
- **Oehlers classification**: radiographic grading of invagination depth (Type I–III/IIIa/IIIb).
- **CBCT**: cone beam computed tomography — 3D imaging overcoming 2D distortion/overlap.
- **CoCoPop**: Condition–Context–Population mnemonic for prevalence review questions.
- **Voxel**: smallest 3D volume element; smaller voxel = higher CBCT resolution.
