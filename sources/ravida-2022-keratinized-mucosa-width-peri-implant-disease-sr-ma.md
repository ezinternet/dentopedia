## Why Ingested

각화점막폭(Keratinized Mucosa Width, KMW)이 <2 mm vs ≥2 mm일 때 임플란트 주위 질환 위험도 차이가 임상적으로 유의미한지에 대한 근거가 기존 리뷰마다 엇갈렸다. 이 SR+MA+TSA는 개입 연구(RCT·비RCT·전향적 코호트)만 포함하고 TSA로 표본 검정력을 직접 평가한 가장 엄격한 형태의 연구로, [[wiki/implants/peri-implantitis/mahardawi-2023-lack-keratinized-mucosa-peri-implantitis-sr-ma]]와 비교 시 결론이 공통적으로 "현재 근거 불충분"을 가리키므로 양쪽 참조가 필수적이다.

## One-line Summary

SR + meta-analysis + TSA (9 studies, 685 implants; RCT/prospective interventional designs only): KMW <2 mm vs ≥2 mm showed significantly higher plaque index (MD 0.37, p=0.002) but no significant difference in MBL, PD, or soft-tissue recession, with TSA confirming all findings except mPI are underpowered and inconclusive (GRADE: very low to low).

## 한줄요약

SR+MA+TSA (9연구, 685임플란트; 개입 연구 한정): 각화점막폭 <2 mm군은 플라크 지수(MD 0.37, p=0.002)만 유의하게 높았고, 변연골소실·탐침 깊이·연조직 퇴축에서는 유의차 없었으며, TSA로 plaque index 외 전 결과가 검정력 부족·결론 불확정으로 확인됨(GRADE: very low~low).

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **TSA as the distinguishing method**: First SR+MA to apply TSA to the KMW question, directly testing whether current evidence is statistically conclusive beyond type I/II error adjustment.
2. **Plaque index is the only conclusive finding**: TSA confirmed adequate power (430 implants > RIS 310) for mPI — ≥2 mm KMW yielded significantly lower plaque index (MD 0.37 [0.16, 0.58], p=0.002) — but all other outcomes remain underpowered and inconclusive.
3. **MBL: statistically significant but TSA-overturned**: Conventional meta-analysis showed MD = 0.17 mm MBL favoring ≥2 mm KMW (p=0.03), but TSA showed this is underpowered (n=257 < RIS 424), reclassifying it as an inconclusive trend.
4. **Design improvement over prior reviews**: Restricts to interventional designs (RCT + cohort with comparison arm), avoiding the confound that <2 mm KMW may be a *consequence* rather than *cause* of peri-implant disease.
5. **GRADE assessment**: Low quality for PD, mPI, MBL; very low quality for soft-tissue recession and PROMs.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Protocol**: PRISMA guidelines; PROSPERO-registered (CRD42021233756)
- **PECO**: Population = systemically healthy adults; Exposure = <2 mm KMW at implant placement; Comparison = ≥2 mm KMW; Outcomes = PD, mPI, mGI, MBL, REC, CAL, implant survival, peri-implantitis incidence, brushing discomfort (PROMs)
- **Search**: PubMed/MEDLINE, Scopus, Web of Science, Grey Literature; manual search of 12 journals; no language/date restriction
- **Screening kappa**: κ=0.85 (title/abstract), κ=0.87 (full-text)
- **Risk of bias tools**: ROBINS-I (non-RCTs); Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (prospective cohorts)
- **Statistical model**: Fixed-effect (I²<50%) or random-effect (I²>50%); inverse-variance weighting; pooled MD with 95% CI; significance p<0.05
- **TSA**: Computed Required Information Size (RIS) per outcome; z-curve plotted against conventional threshold and trial sequential boundary to assess conclusiveness
- **GRADE**: Applied per outcome to rate certainty of evidence

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Probing Depth (PD)
- 3 studies, 430 implants (265 KMW≥2, 165 KMW<2)
- Pooled MD = 0.03 mm (95% CI: −0.08, 0.15); p=0.55; I²=35%
- **TSA**: z-curve does not cross conventional or sequential boundary → **inconclusive**; RIS = 2171 implants (actual n=430 → severely underpowered)

### Marginal Bone Loss (MBL)
- 2 studies, 257 implants (103 KMW<2, 154 KMW≥2)
- Pooled MD = 0.17 mm (95% CI: 0.01, 0.32); p=0.03; I²=0% — nominally significant
- **TSA**: z-curve crosses conventional threshold only, NOT the sequential boundary → **inconclusive after TSA**; RIS = 424 implants (actual n=257 → underpowered)
- GRADE: Low quality

### Soft-tissue Recession (REC)
- 2 studies, 219 implants (52 KMW≥2, 167 KMW<2)
- Pooled MD = 0.35 mm (95% CI: −0.45, 1.15); p=0.39; I²=92%
- **TSA**: Inconclusive; RIS = 2525 implants (actual n=430 → very underpowered)
- GRADE: Very low quality

### Mean Plaque Index (mPI)
- 3 studies, 430 implants (265 KMW≥2, 165 KMW<2)
- Pooled MD = 0.37 (95% CI: 0.16, 0.58); p=0.002; I²=84%
- **TSA**: z-curve crosses BOTH conventional and sequential boundary → **conclusive finding**; RIS = 310 implants (actual n=430 → sufficient power)
- GRADE: Low quality (due to serious inconsistency and indirectness)

### Peri-implantitis incidence / Implant survival / CAL / GI
- Insufficient comparable studies for quantitative synthesis

### Brushing Discomfort (PROMs — 1 study, 4 yr)
- KMW<2 mm: mean VAS 12.28 ± 17.59; 51.4% reported some discomfort
- KMW≥2 mm: mean VAS 4.25 ± 8.39; majority reported no discomfort
- GRADE: Very low quality

### Summary by TSA verdict

| Outcome | Conventional p | TSA verdict | Power |
|---|---|---|---|
| PD | NS (0.55) | Inconclusive | RIS 2171; severely underpowered |
| MBL | Sig (0.03) | **Inconclusive** (TSA reversal) | RIS 424; underpowered |
| REC | NS (0.39) | Inconclusive | RIS 2525; very underpowered |
| mPI | Sig (<0.001) | **Conclusive** | RIS 310; powered |

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Heterogeneity**: High I² for mPI (84%) and REC (92%) limits pooled estimate interpretation
- **Study design imbalance**: Only 1 RCT; most are prospective cohorts without randomization
- **Confounding**: Baseline KMW defined at implant placement, but may change post-surgically; prosthetic type, oral hygiene instruction, and maintenance protocol vary
- **Missing data**: One author did not respond to data request; one responded partially
- **Short follow-up**: Most studies 1–5 years; long-term peri-implantitis risk may differ
- **Generalizability**: Varied implant brands, arch positions (mostly mandibular), mixed edentulism types
- **Future need**: Larger-sample RCTs with ≥5-year follow-up, standardized KMW measurement protocol, and peri-implantitis incidence as primary endpoint are required to achieve RIS thresholds for MBL and PD

## 6. Related Work

- Ravidà et al. builds on the EAO 3rd Consensus (2012) and 6th Consensus Report which both noted inconclusive evidence for KMW benefit in maintained populations
- Contrasts with observational/cross-sectional reviews that reported KMW as a risk indicator — this study argues causality cannot be established from cross-sectional designs

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| KMW (각화점막폭, Keratinized Mucosa Width) | Apico-coronal height of keratinized peri-implant soft tissue from mucosal margin to mucogingival junction |
| TSA (시험순차분석, Trial Sequential Analysis) | Meta-analytic technique adjusting for type I/II errors; computes RIS and plots z-curve vs sequential monitoring boundary |
| RIS (필요정보크기, Required Information Size) | Minimum total sample needed for a meta-analysis to achieve target power and significance |
| MD (평균차, Mean Difference) | Weighted mean difference between KMW≥2 and <2 mm groups for continuous outcomes |
| GRADE | Grading of Recommendations, Assessment, Development and Evaluation — framework rating certainty of evidence |
| ROBINS-I | Risk Of Bias In Non-randomized Studies of Interventions tool |
| mPI (평균 플라크 지수, Mean Plaque Index) | Composite plaque accumulation score used as a surrogate for oral hygiene efficiency |
| MBL (변연골소실, Marginal Bone Loss) | Radiographic measure of crestal bone level change from baseline |
| PROMs | Patient-reported outcome measures; here assessed via VAS brushing discomfort score |
| peri-implantitis (임플란트 주위염) | Inflammatory condition with bone loss around osseointegrated implants |
| mucositis (임플란트 주위 점막염) | Reversible peri-implant soft-tissue inflammation without radiographic bone loss |
