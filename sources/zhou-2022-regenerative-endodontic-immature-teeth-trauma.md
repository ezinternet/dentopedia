## 2. Key Contributions
- Dental trauma affects 7.4–58% of children aged 8–11; IPT have thin dentinal walls → fracture risk if root development halted
- Traditional RCT/apexification: removes necrotic pulp but cannot revitalize; walls remain thin; no further root development
- REPs defined: "biologically-based procedures aimed to replace damaged structures including dentin and root structures, as well as cells of the pulp-dentin complex"
- Historical trace: REP origins to Hermann (1952) vital pulp amputation with Calxyl
- Major approaches compared: direct pulp capping, partial pulpotomy, apexification (Ca(OH)₂ vs MTA), apexogenesis, pulp revascularization/REP
- Goal distinction: apexification = root-end closure only; REP = resumed root development + continued dentin deposition → thicker walls

## 3. Methodology
- Narrative review of case reports and clinical studies
- Compares different REP approaches in case reports by their advantages and limitations
- Focus: immature permanent teeth (IPT) with pulp necrosis following dental trauma

## 4. Key Results
| Approach | Outcome Goal | Key Limitation |
|---|---|---|
| Ca(OH)₂ apexification | Apical barrier formation | Long treatment; increased root fracture risk |
| MTA apexification | Faster apical closure | No further root development; thin walls remain |
| Partial pulpotomy (MTA/BiodentineⓇ) | Preserves vital pulp base; continued development | Requires some vital pulp tissue present |
| Pulp revascularization/REP | Root elongation + wall thickening | Unpredictable; dependent on stem cell survival |
| Apexogenesis | Continued root development when pulp partial vital | Limited to partial necrosis cases |

- REP success depends on: adequate disinfection, scaffold (blood clot/PRP/PRF), biocompatible coronal barrier (MTA/Biodentine)
- IPT with open apex: large vascularized canal space → good candidate for REP if blood clot can be induced
- Mineralization pattern post-REP: often cementum/bone-like tissue rather than true pulp-dentin complex

## 5. Limitations
- Narrative review; evidence primarily from case reports and small series — low evidence level
- Heterogeneity in REP protocols across cases; no standardized outcome metrics
- "Regenerated" tissue histologically ≠ original pulp-dentin complex in most cases

## 7. Glossary
- **REP (Regenerative Endodontic Procedure)**: Biologically-based procedure replacing pulp-dentin complex structures; includes revascularization and pulp regeneration approaches
- **IPT (Immature Permanent Tooth)**: Tooth with incomplete root development; thin dentinal walls; open apex
- **Apexification**: Procedure to induce apical barrier formation (Ca(OH)₂ or MTA) without root development
- **Apexogenesis**: Preservation of vital pulp to allow continued root development
- **Biodentine**: Calcium silicate-based material used as pulp capping/coronal barrier in REPs
