## Why Ingested

ParaPost 제품(Coltène/Whaledent 티타늄 기성 포스트)의 생체역학을 직접 다룬 photoelastic 연구. 기존 [[post-and-core/wang-2019-fiber-posts-vs-metal-posts-severely-damaged]]와 [[post-and-core/van-landuyt-2025-rct-glass-fiber-versus-metal-posts]]가 임상 생존율로 fiber > metal을 보인 근거를, 이 논문은 응력 분포 메커니즘(강성 금속 포스트가 dentin으로 응력을 전달) 수준에서 뒷받침한다.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro photoelastic bench study (PSM-5 blocks, 3 groups × 3): rigid ParaPost (metal) concentrates high stress apically under vertical load and cervically under oblique load, whereas fiber and cast posts distribute stress evenly — but the 1.25 mm fiber post fractured below 90 N oblique load.

## 한줄요약

광탄성 인비트로 연구 (PSM-5 블록, 3군×3): 강성 금속 ParaPost는 수직하중 시 근단부·경사하중 시 치경부에 응력이 집중되는 반면 fiber·cast post는 응력이 고르게 분포함 — 단, 1.25 mm fiber post는 90 N 경사하중 전에 파절됨.

## 1. Document Information

- Journal: Iranian Endodontic Journal, 2010
- First author affiliation: Department of Prosthodontics, Shahid Beheshti University of Medical Sciences, Tehran, Iran
- Study type: in-vitro photoelastic stress analysis
- PMID 23130023 / PMC3471570; no DOI issued

## 2. Key Contributions

- Directly compares three structurally different posts (glass fiber, ParaPost titanium/metal, Ni-Cr cast) with identical 1.25 mm width and parallel-sided design in one photoelastic model.
- Confirms mechanistically the clinical concern that rigid metal (ParaPost) posts transfer/concentrate stress rather than distribute it.
- Quantifies stress as fringe order (FO): ParaPost apical FO=4 (vertical), cervical FO=5 (oblique) vs FO=2+ even distribution for fiber/cast.
- Documents that a narrow (1.25 mm) glass fiber post fractures before reaching 90 N oblique load.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Nine PSM-5 epoxy photoelastic blocks (10×45×45 mm; modulus similar to dentin), each with a drilled 9 mm × 0.8 mm canal.
- 3 groups (n=3): FiberLux fiber post (Coltène/Whaledent), ParaPost (Coltène/Whaledent), custom Ni-Cr cast post — all 1.25 mm, parallel-sided, flat-ended.
- Cemented with Panavia F (Kuraray); 4 mm supracanal length retained.
- Polariscope imaging under no load, 135 N vertical, and 90 N oblique (26° to long axis). Fringe orders read at coronal/middle/apical thirds; ΔFO ≥ 1 deemed significant. Non-anatomic model (no root/PDL simulation).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Vertical 135 N: ParaPost apical FO=4 (coronal/middle FO=2+); Fiber FO=2+ even; Cast FO=2+ even.
- Oblique 90 N: ParaPost cervical FO=5 (middle FO=1, apical FO=2); Cast cervical FO=3+; Fiber post fractured before 90 N.
- All specimens stress-free before loading (adequate cementation, no residual stress).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Non-anatomic, qualitative photoelastic model; n=3/group; no PDL, root morphology, or dynamic/fatigue loading.
- Only one fiber post product and one width (1.25 mm) tested; results on fiber fracture are width-specific.
- Static single loads (135 N / 90 N) rather than cyclic mastication.

## 6. Related Work

- Standlee et al. 1972 (first photoelastic endodontic post study); Cohen et al. (Flexi Post/Flange/ParaPost/Access Post); Kishen et al. (photoelastic–fractographic correlation with ParaPost); Mattison 1982/1983 (cast gold posts); Teixeira et al. (fiber post oblique fracture 45–72 N).

## 7. Glossary

- **Fringe order (FO)**: photoelastic measure of stress magnitude; higher FO = higher stress concentration.
- **ParaPost**: parallel-sided serrated/vented titanium prefabricated post system (Coltène/Whaledent).
- **PSM-5**: epoxy photoelastic material with dentin-like elastic modulus.
- **Oblique loading**: off-axis load simulating lateral masticatory forces.
