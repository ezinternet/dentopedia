## Why Ingested

This SR+MA provides the key quantitative pooled estimates for SCTG benefit (MBML, FSTT, MBL) during IIPP — critical numbers missing from the narrative summaries in [[wiki/immediate-implant/type-1a-immediate-placement-immediate-loading-decision]] and [[wiki/immediate-implant/immediate-implant-soft-tissue-gap-grafting-material-choice]]. The WMD figures (MBML 0.54 mm, FSTT 0.79 mm, MBL 0.13 mm) are benchmarks for deciding when SCTG is mandatory at the time of immediate implant placement.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 7 studies (n=272) shows SCTG at IIPP significantly reduces mid-buccal mucosal recession (WMD 0.54 mm), increases facial soft tissue thickness (WMD 0.79 mm), and reduces marginal bone loss (WMD 0.13 mm) at 12 months versus no graft.

## 한줄요약

즉시임플란트+즉시임시치아(IIPP) 시 결합조직이식(SCTG) 동시 시행은 12개월 시점에서 협측점막퇴축 0.54 mm 감소, 연조직두께 0.79 mm 증가, 변연골소실 0.13 mm 감소 효과를 보인 SR+MA (7개 연구, n=272).

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First SR+MA to pool three key soft-tissue/bone outcomes (MBML, FSTT, MBL) for SCTG vs no SCTG at IIPP with meta-analysis.
2. Demonstrates statistically significant benefit of SCTG for all three outcomes with low heterogeneity (I² = 0–16%).
3. Pooled WMD for MBL shows SCTG also protects marginal bone, not only soft tissue — a finding less intuitive than the mucosal-level benefit.
4. Identifies short follow-up (≤12 months in all included studies) and measurement heterogeneity as the primary evidence gap.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**PICOS clinical question**
- P: Systemically healthy patients undergoing single immediate implant + immediate provisionalization in the esthetic zone
- I: Simultaneous soft tissue augmentation with sub-epithelial connective tissue graft (SCTG)
- C: IIPP without simultaneous SCTG
- O: Mid-buccal mucosal level (MBML) [primary]; facial soft tissue thickness (FSTT) and marginal bone loss (MBL) [secondary]
- S: Prospective/retrospective clinical studies/case series (≥10 cases), ≥12-month follow-up

**Study selection**
- 861 articles identified → 683 after deduplication → 83 full-text → 7 included (published 2007–2021, n=272 patients)
- All flapless approach, non-traumatic extraction, bone-level implants, buccal gap left from socket wall

**Included studies**

**Soft tissue augmentation techniques**: 5 studies used palatal SCTG; 1 used maxillary tuberosity SCTG; 2 studies included a sub-group treated with xenogeneic collagen matrix. Placement was supraperiosteal (3 studies) or subperiosteal (3 studies).

**Gap management**: Autogenous bone, autogenous+xenograft, xenograft alone, xenograft+collagen membrane, or allograft+xenograft mixture used to fill buccal gap across studies.

**Quality assessment**: RCTs assessed with Cochrane RoB 2 — three RCTs high risk of bias (poor randomization reporting), one RCT medium risk. Observational studies assessed with Newcastle-Ottawa Scale — all three medium quality (7–8 stars).

**Statistical methods**: Random-effects models; weighted mean difference (WMD) with 95% CI; heterogeneity by Cochran Q and I²; threshold I² < 50% acceptable; R version 4.0.2.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Mid-buccal mucosal level (MBML)** — 5 studies
- WMD 0.54 mm (95% CI 0.33–0.75; p<0.001) in favor of SCTG
- I² = 16% (low heterogeneity)
- Interpretation: SCTG group had 0.54 mm less recession at 12 months

**Facial soft tissue thickness (FSTT)** — 4 studies
- WMD 0.79 mm (95% CI 0.37–1.22; p<0.001) in favor of SCTG
- Mean difference from individual studies: +1.04 mm gain in SCTG groups (Frizzera, Runcharrassaeng, Migliorati, De Angelis)
- Interpretation: SCTG produces significantly thicker buccal soft tissue

**Marginal bone loss (MBL)** — 4 studies
- WMD 0.13 mm (95% CI 0.07–0.18; p<0.001) more bone loss in control group
- I² = 0% (no heterogeneity)
- Interpretation: SCTG protects marginal bone in addition to soft tissue

**Implant survival**: 100% in all studies except Zuiderveld (one failure each in SCTG and control arm)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

1. **Short follow-up**: All included studies ≤12 months; long-term benefit of SCTG beyond this time point remains unclear.
2. **Heterogeneous measurement methods**: Standardized photographs, direct clinical measurements, dental cast measurements — prevents direct comparison.
3. **Risk of bias**: 3 of 4 RCTs at high risk (randomization reporting); observational studies at medium quality.
4. **Unreported confounders**: Soft tissue phenotype not reported in 4 of 7 studies; keratinized mucosa width, papilla volume not assessed.
5. **No publication bias analysis**: Small number of studies precluded funnel plot assessment.
6. **Collagen matrix evidence**: Xenogeneic collagen matrix as SCTG alternative still has insufficient evidence — mentioned as future research priority.

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary

- **IIPP**: Immediate implant placement and provisionalization — implant + provisional crown on the day of extraction
- **SCTG**: Sub-epithelial connective tissue graft — autogenous soft tissue graft from palate/tuberosity placed at time of implant
- **MBML**: Mid-buccal mucosal level — the marginal position of the buccal mucosa relative to a reference point; change = recession
- **FSTT**: Facial soft tissue thickness — horizontal width of buccal soft tissue profile
- **MBL**: Marginal bone loss — radiographic change in crestal bone level around implant
- **WMD**: Weighted mean difference — pooled metric from random-effects meta-analysis
- **Flapless approach**: Implant surgery without raising a mucoperiosteal flap
- **Buccal gap**: Space between the implant surface and the buccal socket wall; filled with graft material in IIPP protocols
- **I²**: Higgins and Thompson heterogeneity statistic; <50% considered acceptable
