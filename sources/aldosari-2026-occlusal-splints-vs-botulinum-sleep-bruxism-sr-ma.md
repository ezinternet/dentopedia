## One-line Summary

SR+MA (36 studies, adults with sleep bruxism) found no significant difference between occlusal splints and BTX-A in pain reduction at intermediate follow-up; BTX-A showed faster short-term relief; occlusal splints preferred when mechanical protection is the primary goal; moderate certainty of evidence. Abstract-only.

## 한줄요약

성인 수면 브럭시즘 SR+MA(36개 연구)에서 교합안정장치(OS)와 BTX-A는 중간 추적 시점 통증 감소에 유의한 차이가 없었으며, BTX-A는 단기 통증 완화가 더 빠름; 기계적 보호가 목표일 때 OS가 선호; 증거 확실성은 중등도. 초록만 수집.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Most comprehensive head-to-head comparison of OS vs BTX-A for adult sleep bruxism in the literature (n=36 studies)
- Meta-analysis result: **no statistically significant difference** in pain at intermediate follow-up — clinically important equivalence finding
- Identifies differential advantages: BTX-A faster short-term onset; OS provides irreplaceable mechanical protection
- Moderate certainty of evidence for pain outcomes; lower certainty for other outcomes (muscle activity, bite force, QoL)
- Provides practical clinical algorithm: choose based on primary treatment goal (pain relief vs mechanical protection)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Database search**: PubMed, Embase, Scopus, Web of Science, ScienceDirect, Google Scholar (1998 – February 2025)
- **Total included**: 36 studies (qualitative synthesis); subset for meta-analysis (pain outcomes)
- **Population**: Adults with sleep bruxism (SB)
- **Comparators**: Occlusal splint (OS) vs botulinum toxin type A (BTX-A)
- **Outcomes**: Pain-related clinical sequelae (primary), jaw muscle activity, bite force, mandibular mobility, adverse events
- **Evidence certainty**: GRADE or similar (moderate for pain; lower for others)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Pain at intermediate follow-up (meta-analysis)**: No significant difference between OS and BTX-A
- **Short-term relief**: BTX-A faster in some studies
- **BTX-A effects**: Temporary reduction in jaw muscle activity and bite force (confirmed)
- **OS role**: Primarily mechanical protection; function maintenance
- **Certainty of evidence**: Moderate for pain; lower for other reported outcomes
- **Adverse events**: Not detailed in abstract; implied manageable per broader context

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract only — GRADE specifics, heterogeneity statistics (I²), individual study risk of bias not available
- "Sleep bruxism" definition variability across 36 included studies
- Intermediate follow-up pooled — different follow-up periods may dilute temporal differences
- Future: standardized SB diagnosis (polysomnography); long-term (>1yr) head-to-head RCT; OS type subgroup (hard vs soft)

## 7. Glossary

- **OS** (Occlusal Splint, 교합안정장치): removable acrylic device covering dental occlusal surfaces; primary function is mechanical tooth protection and muscle relaxation
- **BTX-A** (Botulinum Toxin Type A, 보툴리눔 독소 A형): neurotoxin causing muscle paralysis; used for bruxism via masseter/temporalis injection
- **Sleep bruxism** (수면 브럭시즘): repetitive jaw-muscle activity during sleep characterized by clenching/grinding
- **Intermediate follow-up** (중간 추적): follow-up period between short-term (<3 months) and long-term (>1 year)
- **GRADE**: Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation; evidence certainty framework
