## Why Ingested

ParaPost vs FRC post 파절저항을 다룬 in-vitro 연구로, 방금 인제스트한 [[post-and-core/izadi-2020-core-materials-fracture-resistance-parapost]]와 결론(FRC > ParaPost, core 재료는 무관)이 동일하게 재현됨. 다만 설계·수치가 Izadi를 매우 근접하게 답습하여 near-duplicate 주의가 필요 — 두 페이지를 상호 링크해 가시화한다.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro fracture-resistance study (90 mandibular premolars, 9 groups × 10): pooled FRC-post groups significantly higher fracture load than ParaPost groups (380.1 vs 262.8 N, P<0.05); core material type had no effect — findings that closely replicate Izadi et al. 2020.

## 한줄요약

인비트로 파절저항 연구 (하악 소구치 90개, 9군×10): FRC post군 통합 파절하중이 ParaPost군보다 유의하게 높음 (380.1 vs 262.8 N, P<0.05); core 재료 종류는 무관 — Izadi 등 2020을 매우 근접하게 재현한 결과.

## 1. Document Information

- Journal: Journal of Pharmacy & Bioallied Sciences, 2022
- Institutions: Indian dental colleges + Qassim University (Saudi Arabia)
- Study type: in-vitro fracture resistance (universal testing machine)
- PMID 36110633 / PMC9469336 / DOI 10.4103/jpbs.jpbs_789_21
- Funding: Nil. COI: none declared.
- **Near-duplicate caution**: design and group means track Izadi et al. 2020 (Dent Res J, PMC7688039) very closely; this paper cites Izadi directly.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Independent (India-based) replication of the FRC-post-vs-ParaPost fracture-resistance comparison.
- Confirms FRC > ParaPost pooled fracture load with a smaller sample (n=90).
- Reconfirms that core material (Photo Core / LuxaCore / Core Max II) does not drive fracture resistance.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 90 extracted mandibular first premolars, randomized to 9 groups (n=10): I FRC post; II–V FRC + Photo Core / LuxaCore / composite core / Core Max II; VI ParaPost; VII–IX ParaPost + LuxaCore / Photo Core / Core Max II.
- Root canal treatment; silicone PDL simulation; acrylic mounting; post space with Peeso reamers; posts cemented with Panavia F2; core build-up; crowns seated.
- Load at 45° to long axis, crosshead 1 mm/min to fracture. SPSS 21, Student's t + one-way ANOVA (P<0.05).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Group means (N): I 452±61.5; II 412.6±42.4; III 362.4±40.8; IV 430.4±112.4; V 384.2±90.6; VI 288.1±81.5; VII 240.6±74.2; VIII 276.4±68.1; IX 246.5±50.2.
- Pooled FRC (II–V) 380.1±72.1 vs pooled ParaPost (VI–IX) 262.8±70.6; t=8.2, P=0.01.
- FRC posts higher fracture resistance than ParaPosts; core material type not a determinant.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample; in-vitro static loading, no thermocycling/fatigue; tooth-selection variability (author-stated).
- Design and numeric results closely mirror Izadi et al. 2020 — limited independent evidential weight.

## 6. Related Work

- Izadi et al. 2020 (near-identical protocol and values); Makade et al. (ParaPost highest fracture resistance vs control).

## 7. Glossary

- **FRC post**: fiber-reinforced composite post; modulus near dentin.
- **ParaPost**: parallel rigid metal prefabricated post (Coltène/Whaledent).
- **Core build-up material**: LuxaCore (dual-cure), Photo Core (light-cure), Core Max II (powder/liquid resin).
- **Fracture resistance**: peak load (N) at specimen fracture.
