## One-line Summary

Community comparative intervention (n=31 three-time vs n=40 single toothpick-method instruction, 1-year follow-up): repeated instruction significantly reduced gingival redness/swelling and gave higher CPITN improvement, while single instruction did not change gingival inflammation at 1 year — repeated instruction outperformed single.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Tests **instruction frequency** (3 monthly sessions vs 1 single session) of the **Watanabe Toothpick Method** in a real community periodontal health program.
- Shows a **dose effect**: only the repeated-instruction course resolved gingival inflammation (redness/swelling) at 1 year.
- Links the clinical effect to **behavior**: repeated-instruction subjects brushed more actively and were more satisfied with the program (self-report questionnaire).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Setting:** residents of a small town; community periodontal health program.
- **Two non-randomized courses:**
  - Three-time instruction: n=31, mean age 48.7 ± 9.2 yr.
  - Single instruction: n=40, mean age 55.2 ± 8.3 yr.
- **First visit (both groups):** dental health education class; self-observation of dental plaque under a **phase-contrast microscope**; baseline recording of teeth and periodontal tissue; instruction to brush by the **Toothpick Method**.
- **Outcomes recorded:** percentage of sites with gingival redness and swelling; percentage of sites with bleeding on probing (BOP); CPITN code; plus a 1-year self-report questionnaire (brushing behavior, satisfaction).
- **Follow-up:** 1 year (gingival inflammation also assessed at 1 and 2 months in the three-time group per abstract).
- *Abstract-only — full methodology (sampling, statistics, exact site counts) not retrieved.*

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Gingival redness/swelling:** significantly decreased at 1 month, 2 months, and 1 year in the **three-time** group; **no change** at 1 year in the **single** group.
- **Bleeding on probing (BOP):** significantly decreased in **both** groups.
- **CPITN improvement ratio:** higher in the three-time group than the single group.
- **Questionnaire:** three-time group brushed more actively and were more satisfied than the single group.
- **Conclusion:** the three-time (repeated) instruction course was more effective than the single instruction course.
- *Numeric effect sizes / p-values beyond "significant" are not given in the truncated abstract.*

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Non-randomized** allocation; groups differ in mean age (single group older, 55.2 vs 48.7 yr) — confounding by age/cohort possible.
- **Small samples** (31 vs 40); single community.
- **Self-report** behavior/satisfaction outcomes are subject to bias.
- **Abstract-only here** — full text (Japanese) not retrieved; exact statistics, baseline comparability, and dropout unknown.
- BOP improved in both groups, so part of the benefit may be attributable to the shared baseline education + phase-contrast plaque demonstration, not the extra sessions alone.

## 7. Glossary

- **Toothpick Method (Watanabe method):** a manual toothbrushing technique in which a soft brush is inserted from the buccal/lingual into the interdental space in a toothpick-like motion to clean the interproximal area and stimulate the gingiva.
- **CPITN:** Community Periodontal Index of Treatment Needs — population-level periodontal screening code (0–4).
- **BOP:** bleeding on probing — a sign of gingival inflammation.
- **Phase-contrast microscope:** used here to let subjects directly observe their own dental plaque microflora as a motivational/educational tool.
