## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 20 in-vivo RCTs (≥2263 teeth, >1090 patients) finding laser caries excavation gives restoration survival and pulp vitality equivalent to burs, significantly reduces need for anesthesia and pain, but takes significantly longer — all at low/very-low evidence level.

## 한줄요약

생체 RCT 20편(치아 ≥2263개, 환자 1090+명) SR+MA — 레이저 우식 제거는 수복물 생존·치수생활력이 bur와 동등하고 마취 필요·통증을 유의하게 줄이지만 시간이 유의하게 더 걸린다(근거수준 낮음~매우낮음).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Most up-to-date quantitative synthesis (prior MAs ≤2016/2017) of laser vs bur for caries excavation across 5 patient/technique outcomes.
- Establishes clinical equivalence for restoration survival and pulp vitality (hard endpoints), not just surrogate measures.
- Quantifies the patient-comfort case for lasers: significantly less anesthesia and pain.
- Quantifies the cost: significantly slower excavation (the core indication trade-off).
- Grades all evidence low/very-low — tempers enthusiasm.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Design: systematic review + meta-analysis of in-vivo controlled clinical trials (RCT/CCT/cohort; split-mouth/parallel/crossover).
- PICOS: P = patients of any age with initial caries; I = laser caries excavation; C = conventional bur excavation; O = restoration survival, excavation speed, pulp sensitivity/vitality, anesthesia need, pain.
- 589 records → 315 dedup → 44 full-text → 20 included. ≥2263 teeth, >1090 patients, age 3–72.
- Lasers: mostly Er:YAG (2940 nm); 2 Er,Cr:YSGG (2780 nm); 1 CO₂ (10600 nm); 1 Nd:YAG (1064 nm). 17 split-mouth, 2 parallel, 1 unspecified.
- RoB 2.0: 4 low, 9 unclear, 7 high risk; GRADE low–very low. Sensitivity analyses excluding high-RoB / split-mouth studies → only minor changes.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Restoration survival: RR 1.51 (95% CI 0.87–2.64), NS — low evidence (12 studies).
- Pulp vitality: RR 1.46 (95% CI 0.33–6.46), NS — low evidence (7 studies).
- Need of anesthesia: RR 0.29 (95% CI 0.11–0.75), significantly FEWER with laser — very low evidence (7 studies).
- Pain sensation: RR 0.35 (95% CI 0.22–0.54), significantly LESS with laser — very low evidence (8 studies).
- Excavation speed: MD 2.23 (95% CI 1.89–2.57), significantly MORE time with laser — low evidence (12 studies).
- Median follow-up 12 months (range 1 day–48 months).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Overall low/very-low GRADE certainty; 7/20 studies high risk of bias.
- Heterogeneous lasers/parameters, lesion types, follow-up (1 day–48 mo), selective vs non-selective excavation.
- Pain/anesthesia are subjective (Likert/VAS); reviewers not blinded to journal/authors.
- Future: standardized higher-quality RCTs; longer survival data; optimal laser parameters.

## 7. Glossary

- Er:YAG (2940 nm): water-absorbing erbium laser; preferred for enamel/dentin and selective caries (high water in carious dentin).
- Er,Cr:YSGG (2780 nm): mineral(phosphate/OH)-absorbing erbium-family laser.
- Selective caries excavation: leaving affected dentin, removing only infected dentin.
- RR / MD: risk ratio / mean difference.
- GRADE: certainty-of-evidence grading (here low/very low).
