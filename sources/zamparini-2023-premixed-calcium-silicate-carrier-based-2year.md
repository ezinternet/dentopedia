## Why Ingested

Seeds the wiki's first Ceraseal / premixed calcium-silicate bioceramic-sealer cluster with a clinical (not bench) anchor: the first prospective study to pair Ceraseal with a *warm carrier-based* technique and report 2-year healing/survival. Pairs with the single-cone cohort in [[endodontics/spinelli-2024-three-year-single-cone-ceraseal-cohort]] (same Bologna group, cold technique) to bracket Ceraseal clinical evidence across both warm and cold obturation, and contrasts with the epoxy-vs-calcium-silicate obturation RCT [[endodontics/song-2022-sealer-based-obturation-epoxy-calcium-silicate-rct]].

## One-line Summary

Prospective cohort (Bologna master program), 89 root canal treatments at 24 mo: Ceraseal premixed calcium-silicate sealer with warm carrier-based (Thermafil) obturation matched AH Plus on healing (91.1% vs 88.6%, NS) and survival (97.8% overall), with lower apical extrusion (13.3% vs 25%) and uniquely showed radiographic disappearance of 3/6 extrusions by 24 months.

## 2. Key Contributions

- **First clinical study** to test a premixed calcium-silicate (CaSi) bioceramic sealer (**Ceraseal**, MetaBiomed) with a *warm carrier-based* gutta-percha technique (Thermafil) — prior CaSi-sealer clinical data were limited to cold techniques and 12-month follow-up.
- Demonstrated **non-inferiority to AH Plus** (epoxy-resin control) on 24-month healing and survival.
- Documented a CaSi-specific phenomenon: **radiographic disappearance (resorption) of apically extruded Ceraseal** — 3 of 6 extrusions undetectable at 24 months, whereas all AH Plus extrusions stayed stable.
- Showed Ceraseal's **lower apical extrusion rate** (13.3% vs 25%) and tied this to its lower radiopacity (~6.5 mmAl vs AH Plus 10.0-11.8 mmAl) and physicochemical profile.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Prospective cohort, University of Bologna Endodontic Clinical Section, May 2019-March 2022. Ethics OUTENDOPROSP / CE 20079; STROBE-reported.
- **Sample**: 85 patients / 94 root canal treatments enrolled; assigned to **Ceraseal-TF (n=47)** or **AH Plus-TF (n=47)** by operator training. Power calc: 40/group (alpha 0.05, power 80%), inflated to 47/group for drop-out. End-line: 82 patients / 89 RCTs (drop-out 3.6%).
- **Operator allocation**: 12 post-graduate Master in Endodontics operators randomly assigned (Feb-Apr 2019) to be trained on *one* technique only (Ceraseal-TF or AH Plus-TF), to create homogeneous trained operator groups — note: randomization is of operators, not teeth, so this is a cohort, not an RCT.
- **Obturation**: Both arms used Thermafil warm carrier-based technique with Thermaprep oven; sealer applied with K-file #20 to WL-3 mm, pre-heated carrier to WL-0.5 mm.
- **Ceraseal composition** (as stated): tricalcium silicate 20-30%, dicalcium silicate 1-10%, tricalcium aluminate 1-10%, zirconium dioxide (radiopacifier) 45-50%, thickening agents.
- **Outcome assessment**: Periapical radiographs at baseline, post-fill, T6, T12, T24. PAI scored single-blind by 2 non-treating evaluators (weighted kappa 0.90). Sealer extrusion measured in ImageJ, binned 0 / 0.1-1.0 / 1.1-3.0 / >3 mm.
- **Definitions**: Success = PAI 1-2 + no symptoms; healed = PAI <3 at 24 mo. Survival = success + retained teeth.
- **Statistics**: Chi-squared / Fisher exact; multilevel mixed-effect logistic regression (person-level fixed effects age/gender, tooth-level random effects clinical parameters, robust SE for patient clustering).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome (24 mo) | Ceraseal-TF | AH Plus-TF | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Healed (PAI 1-2) | 91.1% | 88.6% | 0.624 (Pearson χ²); Fisher 0.714 — NS |
| Cumulative survival (both groups) | 97.8% (2/89 lost) | — | — |
| Teeth lost | 1 (horizontal root fracture) | 1 (horizontal root fracture) | — |
| Apical sealer extrusion | 6/44 = 13.3% | 11/44 = 25% | 0.040 (baseline distribution) |
| Extrusion resorbed by 24 mo | 3 of 6 (other 3 stable) | 0 of 11 (all stable) | — |

- Overall extrusion across both arms: 17/89 = 19.0%.
- **No new periapical lesions or re-exacerbations** during follow-up; no pain/fistula/swelling.
- **Predictors of non-healing** (multilevel regression, p<0.05): preoperative initial PAI >3, and presence of a previous (secondary) endodontic treatment. Filling technique/sealer was *not* a predictor.
- Sealer extrusion did **not** affect healing outcome.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Short follow-up (24 months)** — ESE guidelines recommend >=4 years to declare healing.
- **Cohort, not RCT** — operators (not teeth) randomized; allocation by operator training.
- Primary and secondary root canal treatments **not analyzed separately** (potential survival bias).
- Lower Ceraseal radiopacity may **under-detect** small extrusions radiographically, partly confounding the extrusion-rate comparison.
- Authors call for **long-term studies** to confirm root-canal seal stability and whether intracanal sealer resorption also occurs.

## 7. Glossary

- **CaSi / calcium silicate cement** — hydraulic bioceramic material (tricalcium/dicalcium silicate) that sets in wet environments and releases calcium ions.
- **Ceraseal** — premixed CaSi-based bioceramic root-canal sealer (MetaBiomed, Republic of Korea) used in this study.
- **Warm carrier-based technique (Thermafil / TF)** — obturation using a pre-heated gutta-percha-coated carrier inserted to working length.
- **PAI (Periapical Index)** — 5-point radiographic score of periapical status; PAI 1-2 = healed, >=3 = lesion.
- **Apical extrusion** — sealer pushed beyond the apex into periapical tissue.
- **Radiopacity (mmAl)** — radiographic density expressed as equivalent mm of aluminium.
- **AH Plus** — epoxy-resin-based sealer used as control; chemically inert and dimensionally stable once set.
