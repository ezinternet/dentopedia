## Why Ingested

티타늄 메쉬 VABA의 노출 합병증 스펙트럼(7.69–66.66%)과 노출 시점(조기 vs 지연)에 따른 결과 차이를 문헌화하기 위해 수집. [[wiki/implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/tay-2022-healing-complications-vertical-gbr-sr-ma]]의 합병증 분류 데이터를 시간축 관점에서 보완한다.

## One-line Summary

SR (8 trials, 2011–2021): VABA with Ti mesh yields 2.56–4.78 mm vertical gain; exposure rate 7.69–66.66%; early exposure (<4 weeks) → inadequate regeneration; late exposure → minimal bone loss.

## 한줄요약

SR (8편, 2011–2021): 티타늄 메쉬 수직골증대 = 2.56–4.78 mm; 노출률 7.69–66.66%; 조기 노출(4주 이내) → 골재생 불충분; 지연 노출 → 경미한 골흡수 또는 영향 없음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Study type**: Systematic review
- **Journal**: Journal of Contemporary Dental Practice
- **Year**: 2022 (published 2022-12-01)
- **DOI**: 10.5005/jp-journals-10024-3444
- **PMID**: 37125527
- **Search period**: 2011–2021
- **Studies included**: 8 (3 RCT, 2 prospective, 3 retrospective) out of 574 retrieved

## 2. Key Contributions

- Classifies TM use into three modalities: direct adaptation / pre-bent on 3D model / 3D-printed
- Documents critical threshold: early exposure ≤4 weeks → inadequate bone regeneration
- Demonstrates that late exposure → minimal bone impact (only slight resorption)
- Shows wide range of exposure rates (7.69–66.66%) suggesting technique-dependence
- First SR to include all three TM modalities in comparison

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Databases: PubMed, SCOPUS, Science Direct, Cochrane Library
- Risk of bias: CASP checklist
- Inclusion: studies on VABA by GBR with TM, 2011–2021
- Three Ti mesh modalities compared:
  1. Adapted directly on bone (conventional)
  2. Pre-bent on 3D models preoperatively
  3. 3D-printed custom mesh

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Bone Gain:**
- 2 RCTs: 20.8% bone gain (relative measure)
- Other studies: 2.56–4.78 mm mean vertical gain

**Mesh Exposure:**
- All studies reported exposure
- Range: 7.69% to 66.66%
- Early exposure (<4 weeks) → inadequate bone regeneration
- Late exposure → no effect or slight bone resorption

**Early Removal:**
- 2 studies, 1 case each: 2.4–11.1% required early removal

**Infection:**
- 3 studies, 1 case each: 5%, 11.1%, 25%

**Overall reliability:** All TM types showed exposure but TM demonstrated reliability and efficacy for VABA.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 8 studies met inclusion criteria (high heterogeneity)
- Wide range of exposure rates suggests technique/operator dependence
- Cannot distinguish modality-specific outcomes (conventional vs CAD/CAM vs 3D-printed) statistically
- Meticulous follow-up required for exposure detection and management

## 7. Glossary

- **VABA**: vertical alveolar bone augmentation
- **CASP**: Critical Appraisal Skills Programme
- **Early exposure**: mesh exposure within 4 postoperative weeks (critical threshold)
- **Late exposure**: exposure after 4 weeks (generally manageable without implant plan compromise)
