## 1. Document Information
- Authors: K.D. Rainsford
- Type: Comprehensive narrative review (highly cited, 571+ citations)
- Journal: Inflammopharmacology 2009;17:275-342

## 2. Key Contributions
1. Single most-comprehensive ibuprofen review covering pharmacology + clinical + safety.
2. Defines safety profile at OTC (800-1,200mg/day) vs prescription (1,800-2,400mg/day) doses.
3. Establishes mechanism (COX-1/COX-2 derived prostanoid reduction).

## 3. Methodology
Comprehensive narrative review of preclinical, clinical, and safety literature on ibuprofen through ~2009.

## 4. Key Results
[합의수준]
- OTC dose (800-1,200mg/day): safety comparable to paracetamol
- Prescription dose (1,800-2,400mg/day): used for chronic rheumatic indications
- Mechanism: COX-1/COX-2 inhibition → prostanoid reduction → anti-inflammatory + analgesic + antipyretic
- Safety profile well-characterized; GI risk dose-dependent

## 5. Limitations
- 2009 review; post-publication ibuprofen safety updates (cardiovascular, kidney) since
- Narrative not systematic
