## One-line Summary
Multicenter retrospective (21 patients / 28 implants) showing osseodensification produces measurable alveolar ridge expansion, with greater coronal expansion in narrower starting ridges (2.83 ± 0.66 mm in 3–4 mm ridges) and 92.8% implant survival.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Multicenter retrospective clinical study
- **Journal**: Implant Dentistry 2019;28(4):349-355
- **DOI**: 10.1097/ID.0000000000000898
- **Sample**: 21 patients, 28 implants across 3 starting-width groups (3–4 mm n=9, 5–6 mm n=12, 7–8 mm n=7)
- **Primary Question**: How much alveolar ridge expansion does osseodensification produce, and is it predictable across different starting ridge widths?

## 2. Key Contributions
- Quantifies coronal ridge expansion by starting width: 2.83 ± 0.66 mm (3–4 mm group), 1.50 ± 0.97 mm (5–6 mm group), 1.14 ± 0.89 mm (7–8 mm group); group difference p < 0.05.
- Reports mean insertion torque 61.2 ± 13.9 Ncm and mean ISQ 77 ± 3.74 at implant placement after OD ridge expansion.
- 26/28 implants integrated → 92.8% survival rate (2 failures).
- Greatest expansion in narrowest ridges, confirming the OD plastic-deformation / spring-back mechanism is most pronounced where there is adequate trabecular bone to compact.
- Recommends against undersizing osteotomies with OD in ridge expansion cases — instead oversize the osteotomy to prevent over-strain.
- Cautions against OD in type I cortical ridges or thin-base resorbed ridges due to microfracture risk.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Retrospective chart review across multiple private and academic centers. Patients treated with implant placement via osseodensification were identified; alveolar ridge width was measured at the crest and 10 mm apical to the crest before and after OD instrumentation. Patients were stratified into 3 groups by initial coronal ridge width (3–4, 5–6, 7–8 mm). Insertion torque (Ncm) and ISQ values were recorded at implant placement. Implants were followed to integration outcome; survival rate reported. Comparison of expansion between groups by ANOVA-style contrast (p-value reported as < 0.05 for between-group difference at the crest).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Coronal expansion: 2.83 ± 0.66 mm (group 1, 3–4 mm), 1.50 ± 0.97 mm (group 2, 5–6 mm), 1.14 ± 0.89 mm (group 3, 7–8 mm), between-group p < 0.05.
- Mean insertion torque overall: 61.2 ± 13.9 Ncm.
- Mean ISQ overall: 77 ± 3.74.
- Implant survival: 26/28 = 92.8% (2 failures during integration).
- Authors note 16 cases with serial 6-week ISQ data showed stable ISQ — interpreted as compacted autograft providing a near-seamless primary-to-secondary stability transition (Reference 17).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Retrospective, multicenter, no comparator group, no blinding.
- Small sample (n = 21 patients / 28 implants), unbalanced subgroups.
- Apical (10 mm below crest) expansion values not foregrounded in extractable text; only coronal expansion compared statistically.
- No standardized CBCT measurement protocol described in extractable text — heterogeneous measurement risk.
- 92.8% survival is lower than typical implant series (~95–98%); two failures in a small sample materially affect the rate.
- Authors are inventors / licensors of the OD technique — potential intellectual conflict.

## 7. Glossary
- **Osseodensification (OD)**: bone-preserving osteotomy using Densah burs in counterclockwise rotation; compacts and plastically deforms trabecular bone outward.
- **Compaction autografting**: native bone particulate displaced laterally and apically into trabecular spaces by the bur, providing autograft scaffold for new bone.
- **Spring-back effect**: elastic recoil of plastically deformed bone against the implant after seating, increasing immediate BIC.
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: resonance frequency analysis index, 1–100.
- **Plastic deformation**: permanent, non-elastic shape change of bone under sustained strain.
