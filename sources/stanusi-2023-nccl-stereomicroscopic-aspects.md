## Why Ingested
NCCL 형태(wedge vs saucer)와 병인(교합과부하 vs 칫솔질) 연결을 stereomicroscope로 검증. [[nccl/goodacre-2022-nccl-morphology-etiology-restoration-guidelines]]의 형태 분류와 깊이 진행 데이터를 영상학적으로 보강.

## One-line Summary
Stereomicroscopic study of 27 extracted teeth with NCCLs showing 59% wedge-shaped (scratches/furrows/cracks, linked to occlusal+brushing) vs 37% saucer-shaped (smooth walls), with 67% under 500 µm depth.

## 한줄요약
발치 NCCL 27개 stereomicroscope 연구 — wedge형 59%(긁힘·균열, 교합+칫솔질 연관)·saucer형 37%(매끈한 벽), 깊이의 67%가 500µm 미만.

## 1. Document Information
- Diagnostics (MDPI) 2023;13:2590. DOI 10.3390/diagnostics13152590. Open access (CC BY). Univ of Medicine and Pharmacy of Craiova, Romania.
- Continuation of a prior OCT study on 112 teeth / 56 patients.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First reported use of stereomicroscopy (up to 75x) for NCCL morphology/early diagnosis.
- Links wedge-shaped lesions (scratches, furrows, cracks) to occlusal overload + tooth brushing; saucer-shaped to smooth-wall biocorrosion/abrasion.
- Quantifies depth distribution, supporting early-lesion detection rationale.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 27 teeth (13 incisors, 14 premolars) with NCCLs from prior 112-tooth cohort, examined on NIKON SMZ 745T stereomicroscope at 10.05x-75x, depth measured with NIS-A-AMEAS software. Ex vivo, descriptive.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Shape: 16 wedge (59%), 10 saucer (37%), 1 mixed/irregular (4%).
- Depth: 18 (67%) <500 µm; 3 (11%) 500-1000 µm; 2 (7%) 1000-1500 µm; 4 (15%) >1500 µm.
- Arch: 11 maxillary (40.7%), 16 mandibular (59.3%).
- Cited prevalence context: global average ~46.7%, increases with age.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small ex vivo sample from a single center; etiology inferred from morphology (not causally established); no clinical follow-up.

## 6. Related Work
- Prior OCT cohort (ref 29), Goodacre 2022 (morphologic classification), OCT/micro-CT/SEM NCCL imaging literature.

## 7. Glossary
- Stereomicroscope: low-power 3D-capable optical microscope; here used at up to 75x.
- Wedge-shaped / saucer-shaped: principal NCCL morphologic categories.
