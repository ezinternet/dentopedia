## One-line Summary

Cochrane SR+MA (23 RCTs, ~3,206 randomised / 2,583 analysed, mostly healthy impacted-third-molar patients): prophylactic antibiotics may reduce postsurgical infection by ~66% (RR 0.34, NNT 19) and dry socket by ~34% (RR 0.66, NNT 46) — both low-certainty; no clear effect on pain/fever; timing (pre/post/both) not important; given antimicrobial resistance, prescribe individually, not routinely.

## 한줄요약

코크란 SR+MA (RCT 23편, 무작위 ~3,206명/분석 2,583명, 대부분 건강한 매복 사랑니 환자): 예방적 항생제가 술후 감염을 약 66%(RR 0.34, NNT 19)·건성 발치와 (Dry Socket)를 약 34%(RR 0.66, NNT 46) 감소시킬 수 있음(둘 다 낮은 확실성); 통증·발열엔 불확실; 투여 시점(술전/술후/양쪽) 차이 불명확; 항생제 내성 (Antimicrobial Resistance, AMR) 때문에 일상적 처방이 아닌 개별화 결정 권고.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews, 2021, Issue 2, Art. No.: CD003811 (pub3)
- **DOI**: 10.1002/14651858.CD003811.pub3
- **Institution**: Cochrane Oral Health; University of Milan and collaborators (Italy)
- **Type**: Cochrane intervention SR with meta-analysis

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies prophylactic-antibiotic benefit for two distinct extraction complications (infection and dry socket) with NNTs.
- Establishes that benefit is demonstrated almost exclusively in **healthy patients having impacted third molar surgery** — not routine extractions, not immunocompromised patients.
- Frames the antimicrobial-resistance trade-off: modest absolute benefit (NNT 19 for infection) must be weighed against population-level resistance and individual adverse effects.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- RCTs of antibiotics vs placebo given when no infection present, to prevent post-extraction complications; any age.
- 23 trials, ~3,206 randomised (2,583 analysed). Risk of bias: 16 high, 4 unclear, 3 low.
- Outcomes: postsurgical infectious complications (day 6–7), dry socket, pain (dichotomous & VAS), fever, adverse effects; subgroup by antibiotic timing.
- 21/23 trials = healthy patients, impacted third molars, often by oral surgeons; none immunocompromised.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Effect | n / studies | Certainty | NNT |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Postsurgical infection | RR 0.34 (0.19–0.64) | 1,728 / 12 | Low | 19 (15–34) |
| Dry socket | RR 0.66 (0.45–0.97) | 1,882 / 13 | Low | 46 (29–62) |
| Pain (dichotomous) | RR 0.59 (0.31–1.12), NS | 675 / 3 | Very low | — |
| Pain (VAS, MD) | −0.26 (−0.59–0.07), NS | 422 / 4 | Very low | — |
| Fever | RR 0.66 (0.24–1.79), NS | 475 / 4 | Very low | — |
| Adverse effects (mild, transient) | RR 1.46 (0.81–2.64), NS | 1,277 / 8 | Very low | — |

- No clear evidence that timing (preoperative / postoperative / both) matters.
- Results may not generalise beyond healthy third-molar surgery patients.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Most evidence at high risk of bias; certainty low to very low.
- Population narrow (healthy, impacted third molars); no data on immunocompromised or systemically-ill patients, or routine carious/periodontal extractions.
- Absolute benefits modest; AMR and adverse-effect considerations limit routine use.

## 7. Glossary

- **Prophylactic antibiotic**: antibiotic given before infection occurs to prevent it (vs therapeutic, given to treat).
- **Postsurgical infectious complication**: wound infection (swelling, pus, fever) after extraction, typically assessed day 6–7.
- **NNT**: number needed to treat to prevent one event; 19 = treat 19 healthy third-molar patients to prevent one infection.
- **AMR (Antimicrobial Resistance)**: population-level loss of antibiotic efficacy from overuse; central to the prescribing argument.
- **Impacted third molar**: wisdom tooth requiring surgical extraction; the population in 21/23 trials.
