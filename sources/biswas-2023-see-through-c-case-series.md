## Why Ingested

A clinical worked-example of how the prevalence/morphology data is operationalized chairside — two mandibular second molar C-shaped cases (C1 and C2 orifice types) managed under the dental operating microscope with ultrasonic irrigation and thermoplasticized obturation. Applies the classification and management principles catalogued in [[wiki/endodontics/anatomy/fernandes-2014-cshaped-root-canal-configuration-review]] and the cross-sectional morphology in [[wiki/endodontics/anatomy/song-2008-cross-sectional-morphology-and-minimum-canal]] to real cases, complementing the population-level CBCT prevalence in [[wiki/endodontics/anatomy/shemesh-2017-cshaped-canals-cbct-mandibular-molars]] and [[wiki/endodontics/anatomy/abdalrahman-2022-cshaped-canal-second-molars-iraqi-cbct]].

## One-line Summary

Case series (n=2) of mandibular right second molar C-shaped canals (Melton type I/C1 and type II/C2) managed under operating microscope with ultrasonic irrigation, MTA apical plug (case 1), and thermoplasticized gutta-percha; asymptomatic at 1-year recall.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Demonstrates **microscope-guided ("see-through") management** of two distinct C-shaped orifice morphologies in mandibular second molars, emphasizing "if you can see it, you can probably do it."
- Case 1: C1-shaped orifice (Melton type I) extending distal → mesiolingual with one common apical exit; complicated by mid-treatment apical-constriction breakage, salvaged with an **orthograde MTA apical plug** (apical 5 mm) followed by Thermafil obturation; metal-ceramic crown.
- Case 2: C2-shaped orifice (Melton type II) — distal-to-mesiobuccal canal plus a separate mesiolingual canal; biomechanical prep with ProFile series + circumferential filing, ultrasonic activation, thermoplasticized GP, composite restoration.
- Both cases used **5.25% NaOCl + 17% EDTA** alternately with **ultrasonic file activation** to debride the inaccessible fins of the C-shaped system.
- Reinforces ultrasonic irrigation + thermoplasticized obturation as the practical answer to the cleaning/sealing difficulty inherent to C-shaped anatomy.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Two-case clinical case series. Both teeth diagnosed via IOPA radiograph (single conical root) and confirmed clinically under a dental operating microscope (Möller Wedel, ×2–×24) after access. Working length confirmed radiographically. Cleaning/shaping by ProFile rotary + circumferential hand filing + ultrasonic activation. Obturation by Thermafil thermoplasticized GP; case 1 received an MTA (ProRoot, Dentsply) apical barrier. Outcome by clinical and radiographic 1-year recall.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Case 1** (45 M): C1 orifice, single common apical exit; MTA apical plug after constriction breakage; asymptomatic at 1 month and at 1-year recall with no periapical lesion and no PDL widening.
- **Case 2** (32 F): C2 orifice (semicolon), separate mesiolingual canal; asymptomatic at 1 month; 1-year recall asymptomatic, no periapical lesion, no PDL widening (patient later lost to follow-up after migration).
- Both cases classified per **Melton with Fan's modification** (C1–C5).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- n=2; no control, short (1-year) follow-up; case 2 partly lost to follow-up.
- Diagnosis relied on IOPA + microscope without preoperative CBCT, which would more definitively map the canal course apically.
- Case-level evidence: illustrative of technique, not generalizable prevalence or outcome data.

## 6. Related Work

- Cooke & Cox (1979) — original C-shaped description, cited.
- Melton et al. with Fan's modification — classification used (C1–C5).

## 7. Glossary

- **C1 / C2 orifice (Melton/Fan)**: C1 = continuous uninterrupted C; C2 = semicolon (discontinuity, one angle >60°).
- **Apical plug (MTA)**: orthograde mineral trioxide aggregate barrier placed in the apical few mm when the natural constriction is lost/perforated.
- **Thermoplasticized obturation (Thermafil)**: carrier-based heated gutta-percha for 3-D fill of irregular canal space.
- **Ultrasonic irrigation/activation**: ultrasonically energized file/tip to drive irrigant into fins and isthmuses of the C-shaped system.
- **Circumferential filing**: brushing/filing motion against all walls to clean the broad ribbon-shaped canal.
