## Why Ingested

[[wiki/behavioral-dentistry/communication-relationship/ho-2025-overview-dentist-patient-communication-quality]]가 환자중심 진료의 한 축으로 "shared decision-making(SDM)"을 들지만 정의·실행법은 얕음. 본 primer(ISDM 공동제작, J Gen Intern Med 2025)는 임상의 관점에서 SDM이 무엇이고 흔한 오해·장벽을 어떻게 다루는지를 정리 — 임플란트·심미 등 선택지가 갈리는 치과 의사결정에 직접 적용. 의료 일반 근거지만 우리 클리닉 동의·상담 프로토콜의 골격.

## One-line Summary

Co-produced primer (25 collaborators, 13 countries; International Society for Shared Decision Making) describing shared decision-making from the clinician's perspective — clarifies what SDM is, addresses common clinician concerns/barriers, and positions decision support (incl. patient decision aids) for routine practice.

## 한줄요약

공동제작 primer(13개국 25인, 국제SDM학회) — 임상의 관점에서 공유의사결정(SDM)을 정의·해설. 흔한 우려·장벽을 다루고, 환자 의사결정 보조도구(decision aids)를 일상 진료에 어떻게 쓸지 제시. 의료 일반이나 선택지가 갈리는 치과 결정에 적용 가능.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Journal of General Internal Medicine 2025 (page 3889+)
- DOI: 10.1007/s11606-025-09707-z
- Study type: Co-produced primer / consensus-style guidance (not a trial or SR)
- Production: collaboration with clinicians, patient representatives, health-services researchers; ISDM members co-produced and revised through asynchronous editing

## 2. Key Contributions
- Reframes SDM in clinician-facing terms, directly targeting the "supportive in principle but hard to do" gap.
- Names and addresses the concrete concerns/barriers clinicians raise about SDM.
- Locates patient decision aids and decision support within the SDM workflow.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Purposive sample of 25 people from 13 countries (ISDM); preliminary statement substantially revised through structured asynchronous collaboration to reflect the group's comments.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Produces a clinician-oriented description of SDM (what it is / is not) and practical responses to common objections.
- Emphasizes that SDM is feasible in routine care and supported by decision aids; relevance argued for everyday clinical encounters.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Primer/consensus, not empirical — no effect estimates; general-medicine framing, not dentistry-specific.
- Reflects a SDM-advocate community (purposive ISDM sample) → orientation bias toward SDM adoption.

## 7. Glossary
- **Shared decision-making (SDM)**: clinician and patient jointly choose among options using best evidence and the patient's values/preferences.
- **Patient decision aid (PDA)**: tool presenting options/outcomes/probabilities to support SDM.
- **ISDM**: International Society for Shared Decision Making.
