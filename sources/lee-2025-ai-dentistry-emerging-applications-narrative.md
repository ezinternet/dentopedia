## One-line Summary
J Dent 2025;155:105648 — narrative review (120 articles, 4 reviewers) of AI applications across dental education, patient care, and practice management; emphasises adaptive learning, simulation/VR training, multi-specialty diagnostic/clinical decision support, and operational workflow integration.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Journal of Dentistry 2025;155:105648
- Published: April 2025
- Type: Narrative review (no formal evidence-based quality assessment)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Three-pillar framework for AI in dentistry: education, patient care, practice management
- Highlights adaptive learning and simulation/VR for dental training as emerging educational use cases
- Discusses AI-enabled administrative workflow optimisation

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Topic-divided narrative review (4 reviewers); electronic searches across multiple databases
- 120 articles included; English-language restriction; no PRISMA, no formal quality grading
- Scope keywords: AI, technologies, healthcare, education, dentistry, restorative, prosthodontics, periodontics, endodontics, oral surgery, oral pathology, oral medicine, implant dentistry, dental education, patient care, practice management

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Education: adaptive learning systems, simulation, VR, administrative efficiency
- Patient care: improved diagnosis, disease prediction, prognosis classification, clinical decision-making, treatment execution across specialties
- Practice management: AI-driven operational tools, claim processing, scheduling, communication

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Data privacy as primary ethical concern
- Need for large datasets for model training (institutional data sharing barrier)
- Ethical considerations require ongoing clinical, regulatory, and educational engagement

## 6. Related Work
- Aligned with Schwendicke and other contemporary dental AI curriculum/review series
