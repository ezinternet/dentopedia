## Why Ingested
Formalizes the PSD model first sketched in [[oral-microbiology/hajishengallis-2012-psd-model-periodontal-disease]] into a generalizable framework for mucosal inflammatory diseases, adding molecular detail on interspecies signaling (FimA/Mfa1–SspA/B, tyrosine kinase/phosphatase Ptk1/Ltp1/CdhR), epithelial-cell invasion synergy, and the "driver–passenger" pathobiont concept — and broadens reach to link with [[oral-microbiology/lamont-2018-oral-microbiota-dynamic-communities-host]].

## One-line Summary
Narrative review elaborating the PSD model to explain how keystone pathogens (P. gingivalis), accessory pathogens (S. gordonii, A. actinomycetemcomitans), and pathobionts cooperate through interspecies signaling, complement evasion, and chemokine paralysis to drive chronic polymicrobial inflammatory disease.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Synthesizes PSD model roles: keystone pathogens, accessory pathogens, pathobionts, and inflammophilic organisms within a unified narrative.
- Details Ptk1/Ltp1/CdhR tyrosine (de)phosphorylation cascade governing P. gingivalis–S. gordonii community development.
- Introduces the "Black Queen Hypothesis" analogy: keystone organisms maintain costly community-essential functions (like complement evasion).
- Extends driver–passenger model (from colorectal cancer) to periodontal disease — P. gingivalis as driver, pathobionts (Filifactor alocis, Peptostreptococcus stomatis, Prevotella spp., Megasphaera, Selenomonas, Desulfobulbus) as passengers.
- Documents cooperative GEC invasion: P. gingivalis + F. nucleatum invade in higher numbers than either alone; P. gingivalis + F. alocis synergistic infection with distinct host transcriptional response.
- Shows T. denticola suppresses F. nucleatum-driven IL-8 in GECs by blocking lysosomal fusion and reducing intracellular ROS.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative review synthesizing mouse infection models, in-vitro co-culture systems, human metatranscriptomic/metagenomic studies, and molecular mechanistic data.
- Mouse model: dual infection P. gingivalis + S. gordonii → more alveolar bone loss than either alone.
- Metatranscriptomic data cited: majority of virulence factors upregulated in periodontitis derived from underappreciated species (not traditional red complex).
- A. actinomycetemcomitans spatial model: positioned >4 µm from S. gordonii (optimal cross-feeding, sub-toxic H2O2).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- P. gingivalis + S. gordonii co-infection: more alveolar bone loss in mice than either alone; S. gordonii classified as accessory pathogen.
- Ptk1/Ltp1/CdhR signaling: pABA from S. gordonii activates Ptk1 in P. gingivalis → protein tyrosine phosphorylation promotes FimA/Mfa1 adhesin expression and community formation; over time Ltp1 dephosphorylates Ptk1, lifting CdhR inhibition → constrains further community growth.
- InpA (Prevotella intermedia cysteine protease) cooperates with HmuY (P. gingivalis haemophore) for haem acquisition from haemoglobin.
- P. gingivalis + T. denticola: symbiotic biofilm growth in vitro; mutual metabolic cross-feeding; co-culture increases virulence factors (dentilisin, RgpA, Kgp, HagA); more bone loss upon co-infection.
- A. actinomycetemcomitans: positioned >4 µm from S. gordonii; KatA (catalase) and DspB (dispersin B) for H2O2 management; both required for cross-feeding.
- Complement: GCF contains complement components at up to 80% of serum concentration; P. gingivalis, T. forsythia, P. intermedia proteases evade complement-mediated killing.
- Metatranscriptomics: majority of virulence factors upregulated in periodontitis microbiome derived from underappreciated species, not red complex.
- P. gingivalis + F. nucleatum: co-invasion of GECs in higher numbers than either alone.
- CXCL8 suppression by SerB (S536-p65 dephosphorylation) extends to CXCL10, CXCL9, CXCL11 via IRF-1/Stat1 → reduces Th1 chemokines; promotes Th17 via IL-6/IL-23.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Causal inference in humans limited to cross-sectional microbiome data; temporal dynamics of driver–passenger transition undefined.
- Black Queen Hypothesis as analogy — not formally tested for periodontal community.
- Therapeutic targets (C5aR, interspecies signaling, specific complement proteases) require clinical trial validation.

## 7. Glossary
- PSD model: Polymicrobial Synergy and Dysbiosis.
- Pathobiont: commensal-turned-pathogen in dysbiotic context.
- Inflammophilic organism: organism that thrives on nutrients from inflammatory tissue breakdown.
- Ptk1: P. gingivalis tyrosine kinase; phosphorylates adhesin regulators.
- Ltp1: P. gingivalis tyrosine phosphatase; dephosphorylates Ptk1, constraining community.
- CdhR: transcription factor in P. gingivalis; negative regulator of mfa1.
- InpA: Prevotella intermedia interpain A — cysteine protease for haem acquisition.
- KatA: A. actinomycetemcomitans catalase for H2O2 detoxification.
- DspB: dispersin B — releases A. actinomycetemcomitans from biofilm to avoid H2O2.
- Black Queen Hypothesis: evolutionary framework in which costly functions are retained by a subset of community members for communal benefit.
