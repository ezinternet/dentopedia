## One-line Summary

In vitro nano-DMA + micro-permeability study (5 groups, 1-year SBF aging): rinse-out AB-type proanthocyanidin (PAC-DESIGNER) primers raised the complex modulus of the hybrid layer and underlying dentin and reduced interfacial micropermeability vs unprimed control, with trimeric (DP3) PACs outperforming tetrameric (DP4) — a layer-specific mechanism for durable resin-dentin bonding.

## 한줄요약

in vitro nano-DMA + 미세투과도 연구 (5군, 1년 SBF 노화): rinse-out AB형 프로안토시아니딘(PAC-DESIGNER) 프라이머가 무처치 대조군 대비 혼성층·하부 상아질의 복합탄성계수를 높이고 계면 미세투과도를 낮췄으며, 삼량체(DP3)가 사량체(DP4)보다 우수 — 내구성 상아질 접착의 층별 기전을 제시.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Resolves the **layer-specific** action of proanthocyanidin (PAC) biomodulation across the three resin-dentin interface components — adhesive (AD), hybrid layer (HL), underlying dentin (UD) — rather than a bulk bond-strength number.
- Uses the **DESIGNER** standardization concept (Depletion and Enrichment of Select Ingredients Generating Normalized Extract Resources) to produce reproducible, scalable PAC mixtures from plant waste (Pinus massoniana, Cinnamomum verum).
- Compares **trimeric (DP3)** vs **tetrameric (DP4)** and **interflavan-linkage (IFL) configuration** (ABA vs ABB) effects over 1-year aging.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 25 sound human molars; mid-coronal dentin, standardized smear layer.
- 5 groups (n=5): unprimed control, CV-AB, PM-AB (trimeric), CV-ABB, PM-ABA (tetrameric).
- Etch-and-rinse: **35% glycolic acid** (mild α-hydroxyl acid, shallower demineralization) → rinse-out neutral PAC primer (6.5% w/v, HEPES pH~7, 1 min) → model methacrylate adhesive → composite.
- Aging in simulated body fluid (pH 7.4, 37°C) to **24 h / 6 months / 1 year**.
- **Nano-DMA** (TI980 triboindenter, Berkovich, 50 Hz, hydrated): complex modulus E* and damping capacity (tan δ) at AD, HL, UD (10 μm below HL).
- **Micro-permeability**: rhodamine-B fluorescence emission intensity at HL and UD.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Hybrid layer**: all PAC groups higher E* vs control (p<0.05), sustained at 6 months; at 1 year CV-AB & PM-AB highest; **PM-AB stable over the full year** while others declined after 6 months.
- **Underlying dentin**: all PAC > control at 1 year; **PM-AB highest**; trimeric > tetrameric. Demonstrates PAC diffusion into mineralized dentin.
- **Adhesive layer**: no difference vs control at immediate/6 months; at 1 year CV-ABB & PM-AB higher.
- **Damping capacity**: native viscoelastic behavior preserved (UD stayed within native range 0.02–0.3).
- **Micro-permeability**: all PAC groups LESS rhodamine infiltration at HL and UD (p<0.001); control's UD micropermeability rose after 1 year.
- **Trimeric (DP3) > tetrameric (DP4)**; terminal monomer matters (PM-AB catechin > CV-AB epicatechin for HL); IFL config: CV-ABB (2 B-type) favors AD, PM-ABA (A-type) favors HL durability.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In vitro only; surrogate nano-mechanical/permeability endpoints (bond strength reported in a companion prior study).
- Model adhesive (not a commercial product); single etchant (glycolic acid).
- Translation to clinical rinse-out primers and combined functional-monomer systems remains to be tested.

## 7. Glossary

- **PAC**: proanthocyanidin — plant polyphenol collagen cross-linker.
- **DESIGNER**: Depletion and Enrichment of Select Ingredients Generating Normalized Extract Resources (standardized botanical mixtures).
- **DP**: degree of polymerization (DP3 trimeric, DP4 tetrameric).
- **IFL**: interflavan linkage (A-type rigid double bond / B-type flexible single bond).
- **nano-DMA**: nano-dynamic mechanical analysis; **E***: complex modulus; **tan δ**: damping capacity.
- **HL / UD / AD**: hybrid layer / underlying dentin / adhesive layer.
