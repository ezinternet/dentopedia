## One-line Summary
Narrative review proposing that residual ridge resorption (RRR) is a **pathological**, not merely physiological, process driven by oral-barrier-tissue lymphocyte-mediated osteoclast activation on the external alveolar bone surface — Rag2-/- mouse data show that T/B lymphocyte deficiency abolishes external-surface osteoclastogenesis after extraction while intra-socket remodeling remains normal.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Prosthodontic Research 2023;67(1):12–22 (online Feb 2022)
- **Lead institution**: Department of Molecular and Regenerative Prosthodontics, Tohoku University Graduate School of Dentistry (Sendai, Japan)
- **Type**: Narrative review with original Rag2-/- mouse data integrated
- **Funding**: JSPS KAKENHI

## 2. Key Contributions
- Reframes RRR from "passive disuse atrophy after edentulism" to a **disease entity** with identifiable cellular/molecular pathway.
- Proposes the **"oral-barrier osteoclast"** concept: a distinct osteoclast population on the external bone surface (vs intra-socket remodeling osteoclasts), requiring oral mucosal lymphocyte signaling.
- Rag2-/- mouse data: genetically lymphocyte-deficient mice show normal intra-socket osteoclastogenesis but **absence of external-surface osteoclasts** → RRR mechanism dissociable from physiologic remodeling.
- Repopulation with human CD3+ T cells in Rag2-/- restores external-surface osteoclasts → confirms T-cell sufficiency.
- Therapeutic implication: targeting external-surface osteoclasts (anti-lymphocyte signaling, RANKL blockade locally) could selectively block RRR without disrupting socket healing.

## 3. Methodology (review + supporting original data)
- Literature synthesis on RRR clinical characterization (cephalographs, panoramic, CBCT, digital impression).
- Original mouse data:
  - Tooth extraction in WT vs Rag2-/- mice (lacks functional T and B cells).
  - Histology + TRAP staining for osteoclasts.
  - Adoptive transfer: hCD3+ T cells intraperitoneally into Rag2-/-.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Clinical: width reduction 32% (3 mo) and 29–63% (6 mo); height reduction 15% (3 mo), 11–22% (6 mo) — cites Tan 2012.
- Mechanism: WT mice → osteoclasts both inside socket and on external alveolar surface; Rag2-/- mice → osteoclasts only inside socket, none on external surface.
- hCD3+ T cell repopulation → external-surface osteoclasts return.
- RANKL/OPG ratio and DAMPs/PAMPs/TLR signaling implicated as mediators.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Mouse model — direct translation to human RRR pathology unverified.
- Clinical interventions targeting oral barrier osteoclasts (e.g., local denosumab) untested for ARP context.
- Does not address surgical/prosthetic ARP techniques — purely mechanistic.

## 7. Glossary
- **RRR (Residual Ridge Resorption)**: Long-term, progressive reduction of edentulous alveolar bone after tooth loss.
- **Oral-barrier osteoclast**: Osteoclast on the external (mucosa-facing) surface of alveolar bone, proposed as the cellular driver of RRR.
- **Rag2-/- mice**: Lacks functional V(D)J recombination → no mature T or B lymphocytes.
- **RANKL / OPG**: Receptor Activator of Nuclear factor Kappa-B Ligand / Osteoprotegerin — central osteoclastogenesis regulator pair.
- **DAMPs / PAMPs / TLRs**: Damage-/Pathogen-Associated Molecular Patterns / Toll-Like Receptors — innate immune sensing pathway.
