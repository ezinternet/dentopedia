## Why Ingested

골밀도화 시계방향 osteotomy의 cadaveric 1차 안정성 연구 [[wiki/implants/versah-protocols/rittipakorn-2025-clockwise-osseodensification-primary-stability-cadaveric]]는 undersized drilling을 1차 안정성을 높이는 대안 술식으로 함께 논의한다. 이 RCT는 그 대안 술식(undersized drilling)을 즉시식립 상악 전치부에서 직접 검증해, "축경 드릴링이 IT는 올리지만 RFA 안정성은 개선하지 않는다"는 임상 근거를 제공한다 — cadaveric/벤치 모델의 IT↑ 소견을 RCT에서 한정/반박하는 자료.

## One-line Summary

RCT (n=30 immediate tapered implants, anterior maxilla; 16 conventional vs 14 undersized drilling): undersized drilling did NOT significantly improve primary stability — IT slightly higher in UD (41.36 vs 38.44 Ncm, p=0.654) but RFA/ISQ consistently higher in conventional group at all timepoints, no significant between-group ISQ difference over 12 weeks.

## 한줄요약

RCT (즉시식립 tapered 임플란트 30개, 상악 전치부; 통상 16 vs 축경 14): 축경(undersized) 드릴링은 1차 안정성을 유의하게 개선하지 못함 — 삽입 토크는 축경군이 약간 높았으나(41.36 vs 38.44 Ncm, p=0.654) RFA/ISQ는 오히려 통상군이 모든 시점에서 더 높았고 12주간 군간 ISQ 차이는 유의하지 않음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Effect of undersized drilling on the stability of immediate tapered implants in the anterior maxillary sector. A randomized clinical trial
- **Authors**: Sierra-Rebolledo A, Tariba-Forero D, Rios-Calvo M, Gay-Escoda C
- **Journal**: Med Oral Patol Oral Cir Bucal. 2021;16(2):e187–94
- **DOI**: 10.4317/medoral.24107
- **PMID**: 33395401 · **PMCID**: PMC7980290
- **Study type**: Randomized, comparative, parallel-group clinical trial (RCT)
- **Setting**: Master of Dentistry and Dental Implantology clinic, Santa Maria University, Caracas, Venezuela (ClinicalTrials.gov registered; Bioethics Ref. CBB-FO-USM31032015)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Probably the **first RCT** to assess the effect of undersized drilling on the primary stability of **immediate** implants (prior evidence was bench/animal/cadaver and a small 3-implant tomographic case series).
- Directly compares conventional vs undersized drilling sequence for immediate tapered implants in the esthetically demanding anterior maxilla, tracking stability longitudinally (insertion, 6 weeks, 12 weeks).
- Finds that the IT increase classically attributed to undersized drilling does NOT translate into higher RFA-measured stability — challenging IT as a stability surrogate and questioning the clinical necessity of undersizing in immediate maxillary placement.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Randomized parallel-group RCT, 1:1 allocation by coin toss (external observer) immediately before drilling.
- **Sample size**: Powered for minimum ISQ effect of 7 (SD 5), 5% type I / 10% type II error, 15% dropout → ≥28 subjects (14/group). 30 enrolled.
- **Patients**: Adults >18 y needing single implant + extraction of a maxillary anterior tooth; excluded ischemic heart disease, uncontrolled diabetes, coagulation disorders, head/neck radiotherapy, IV bisphosphonates, uncontrolled periodontal disease. CBCT-confirmed ≥5 mm bone from apex to nasal fossa/sinus cortex, no buccal/palatal crest vertical defect >4 mm.
- **Implants**: 30 tapered, conical-connection grade-23 Ti-6Al-4V ELI, dual acid-etched + sandblasted, 3.75 mm × 13 mm (C1, MIS Implants Technologies).
- **Groups (drilling sequence)**:
  - **Conventional (CD)**: marking 1.9 mm @1500 rpm → pilot 2.4 mm/13 mm @800 rpm → twist 3.0 mm @400 rpm (complete sequence).
  - **Undersized (UD)**: marking 1.9 mm @1500 rpm → pilot 2.4 mm/13 mm @400 rpm; **3.0 mm twist drill omitted** (incomplete sequence → narrower bed).
- **Insertion**: 20:1 handpiece at 20 rpm / 10 Ncm, final seating by ratchet to 4 mm below gingival margin. Minimally traumatic extraction, 4% articaine + 1:100,000 epinephrine, two calibrated operators.
- **Outcome measures**: maximum insertion torque (IT) via MIS MT-RI040/MT-RT070 meters; implant stability by RFA (Osstell ISQ, Smartpeg #49) at insertion (RFA1), 6 wk (RFA2), 12 wk (RFA3).
- **Statistics**: SPSS 20; Kolmogorov-Smirnov normality; Student t-test for IT; repeated-measures ANOVA for ISQ across timepoints; p<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Cohort**: 30 patients (19 F / 11 M), mean age 48.87 ± 14.84 y; 16 CD (53.3%) vs 14 UD (46.7%). No adverse events or implant failures over 12 weeks.
- **Insertion torque**: overall median 35 Ncm, mean 39.80 ± 7.14 Ncm (range 17–75). UD 41.36 ± 18.86 Ncm vs CD 38.44 ± 15.99 Ncm — UD higher but **NOT significant (p=0.654)**.
- **ISQ**: overall range 50.0–79.5. At insertion mean 64.83 ± 6.63. **CD always higher than UD** at all three timepoints (e.g., insertion CD 65.50 ± 6.03 vs UD 64.07 ± 7.41). No significant between-group difference at any timepoint.
- **Longitudinal**: repeated-measures ANOVA — progressive, similar ISQ rise in both groups, no significant between-group difference. Overall mean ISQ at 12 weeks 68.10 ± 6.21, still lower in UD than CD.
- **Loading note**: mean ISQ never reached the ISQ-70 immediate-loading threshold during follow-up → authors recommend occlusal loading of anterior maxillary immediate implants only after 12 weeks.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small single-center sample (n=30), short 12-week follow-up (osseointegration period only; no long-term survival/MBL/esthetic outcomes).
- IT data show large SDs (e.g., UD 41.36 ± 18.86), reducing power to detect IT differences; bone-density stratification not reported as a covariate although authors note stability may depend more on bone density than drilling protocol.
- No histology/BIC (clinical RFA only); no esthetic (PES/recession) endpoints despite anterior-maxilla esthetic rationale.
- Single implant system and one undersizing protocol (omit final twist drill) — does not test graded undersizing degrees.

## 6. Related Work

- Friberg (1999) — first proposed drilling-sequence modification to raise primary stability in poor-density bone.
- Toia et al. — 108 implants in healed bone; IT difference favoring undersized drilling under low density but not statistically significant.
- Coelho / Norton — high IT may compromise early stability via bone compression, warming, and remodeling.
- Kim — RFA dip in first ~3 weeks then recovery from week 4–6 in mature bone (explains the stability trajectory).
- [[wiki/implants/versah-protocols/rittipakorn-2025-clockwise-osseodensification-primary-stability-cadaveric]] — cadaveric osseodensification study discussing undersized drilling as an alternative primary-stability technique.

## 7. Glossary

- **Undersized drilling (UD)**: incomplete osteotomy sequence (final-diameter drill omitted) leaving a narrower bone bed than the implant, increasing bone compression and insertion torque.
- **IT (insertion torque)**: peak rotational force (Ncm) at implant seating; ≥35 Ncm a common primary-stability target.
- **RFA / ISQ**: resonance frequency analysis; Implant Stability Quotient (Osstell), 0–100; ISQ ≥65 considered good, ≥70 often used as immediate-loading threshold.
- **BIC**: bone-to-implant contact (histologic osseointegration measure).
- **Primary (mechanical) stability**: stability at placement from bone friction/compression, distinct from secondary (biologic) stability from osseointegration.
