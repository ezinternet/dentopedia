## One-line Summary
J Prosthet Dent 2015;113(3):175-184 (Otago, NZ) — systematic review of 1006 → 23 studies on clinical tooth preparation geometry; recommended TOC values have shifted from unachievable 2-5° taper (1960s) to a realistic 10-22° range; in-mouth preparation gives higher TOC than out-of-mouth; calls for standardized cross-section-based measurement.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Journal of Prosthetic Dentistry 2015;113(3):175-184
- Type: Systematic review (no meta-analysis)
- Search: Scopus, PubMed, ScienceDirect to 5 Dec 2013

## 2. Key Contributions
- Documents historical drift of recommended TOC from 2-5° → 10-22°
- Aggregates 20 TOC studies, 4 margin design, 4 margin angle, 3 abutment height
- Identifies methodological heterogeneity as primary barrier to comparison
- Calls for standardized cross-section measurement protocol

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Database search Scopus/PubMed/ScienceDirect + hand search
- 1006 initial → 983 excluded → 23 included
- Extracted: TOC, margin design, margin angle, abutment height, measuring method

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- 20/23 studies reported TOC; wide methodological variation
- Historical TOC recommendation evolution:
  - 1960s era: 2-5° (unachievable)
  - Current: 10-22° (realistic, clinically achievable)
- TOC is the dominant preparation parameter
- In-mouth preparations consistently exceed recommended values
- Out-of-mouth (e.g. on extracted teeth, typodont) closer to recommendation

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- No meta-analysis (methodological heterogeneity)
- Limited margin design / abutment height data (only 3-4 studies each)
- Recommends standardized measurement using cross sections + standardized reporting
