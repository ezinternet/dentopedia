## Why Ingested

기존 [[wiki/implants/isq/al-jetaily-2010-osstell-periotest-implant-stability-in-vitro]]은 Osstell과 Periotest를 비교했으나, 새로운 DCA 기기인 Anycheck(IST)와 RFA(Osstell Beacon+)의 직접 비교 데이터가 부족했음. 본 연구는 두 기기의 골밀도·임플란트 길이별 상관관계를 체계적으로 정리해 ISQ-IST 등가성 판단 근거를 제공.

## One-line Summary

In vitro study (n=60 implants, polyurethane bone blocks) comparing Osstell Beacon+ (ISQ/RFA) vs Anycheck (IST/DCA): both devices showed similar trends across bone densities and lengths, with Anycheck simpler to use regardless of operator expertise.

## 한줄요약

인공 골블록(n=60) 체외 연구에서 Osstell Beacon+(ISQ/공명주파수분석)과 Anycheck(IST/감쇠용량분석)는 골밀도·임플란트 길이 변화에 따른 유사한 경향을 보였으며, Anycheck가 측정자 숙련도에 무관하게 사용이 간편했다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First direct comparison of Osstell Beacon+ (RFA) and Anycheck (DCA/IST) under controlled polyurethane bone conditions isolating length and density variables
- Demonstrates that both devices capture the same stability trends — validating Anycheck as an alternative to Osstell
- Highlights that Anycheck (contact method) is less operator-sensitive than Osstell (contactless SmartPeg attachment), reducing inter-examiner variability
- Identifies a density-dependent length effect: longer implants benefit primary stability in low-density bone but not in medium-density bone beyond 10 mm

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Materials**:
- 60 implants: IS-III Active (Neobiotech), 4.0 mm diameter; lengths 7.3 / 10 / 13 mm (10 per length per group)
- Group I: 15 PCF polyurethane (0.24 g/cm³, low-density cancellous)
- Group II: 30 PCF polyurethane (0.48 g/cm³, medium-density cancellous)
- No cortical bone layer (to isolate pure density/length effects)

**Devices**:
- RFA: Osstell Beacon+ (ISQ scale 1–100); SmartPeg type 5 at 4–6 Ncm; 4-direction measurements × 5 replicates
- DCA: Anycheck (IST scale 1–99, Neobiotech); healing abutment at 10 Ncm; 2-direction × 5 replicates; 10° jig for standardized angle

**Observers**: expert (5-year experienced dental hygienist) vs non-expert (naive)

**Statistics**: paired t-test, one-way ANOVA with Scheffe/Duncan post hoc, simple linear regression (ISQ/IST vs bone density and vs implant length), Pearson R between devices

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Effect of bone density**:
- Both ISQ and IST were significantly higher in Group II (30 PCF) vs Group I (15 PCF) across all lengths (P <0.001)
- Correlation (R) between BMD and ISQ: expert R = 0.999–1.000; non-expert R = 0.891–0.978 depending on length
- Correlation (R) between BMD and IST: expert R = 0.988–0.999; non-expert R = 0.961–0.980

**Representative ISQ values**:
- 7.3 mm Group I (low): expert ~60.5, non-expert ~58.5
- 7.3 mm Group II (medium): expert ~75.3, non-expert ~73.0
- 13 mm Group I: expert ~69.3, non-expert ~64.1
- 13 mm Group II: expert ~77.4, non-expert ~71.4

**Effect of implant length**:
- Group I (low density): significant positive correlation between length and stability (ISQ R² = 0.956 expert, 0.573 non-expert; IST R² = 0.885 expert)
- Group II (medium density): no significant difference or decrease beyond 10 mm (ISQ R² = 0.900 expert, 0.066 non-expert)

**Device comparison**:
- ISQ and IST showed similar directional trends with bone density and length
- Osstell ISQ showed greater inter-examiner variability (contactless, SmartPeg torque-sensitive)
- Anycheck IST showed less inter-examiner difference due to direct contact measurement

**Key clinical conclusion**: At medium density, a 10 mm implant achieves adequate primary stability; longer implants do not add meaningful benefit. At low density, longer implants significantly compensate for poor bone quality.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Artificial bone cannot fully replicate in vivo viscoelastic properties; no cortical bone layer (limits extrapolation to clinical scenarios with cortical bone)
- Very difficult/impossible to place implants in 10 PCF bone (0.16 g/cm³) — D4 extreme excluded
- Anycheck requires near-vertical measurement; clinical positions may introduce angular error
- IST color scale (red <60, orange 60–64, green ≥65) calibrated differently from ISQ thresholds (<60 low, 60–69 moderate, ≥70 high)
- Future: in vivo validation; inter-operator reliability study with varying clinical experience

## 6. Related Work

- Al-Jetaily & Al-Dosari (2010): compared Osstell vs Periotest in vitro — this study extends to Anycheck (DCA)
- Kastel et al. (2019): SmartPeg torque variability affects ISQ reliability — consistent with Osstell inter-examiner variability here
- Chatvaratthana et al. (2017): ISQ correlated with cortical bone thickness — this study deliberately excluded cortical bone to isolate density/length
- Turkyilmaz & McGlumphy (2008): ISQ increased with bone density — confirmed here in controlled model

## 7. Glossary

- **ISQ** (Implant Stability Quotient, 임플란트 안정성 지수): RFA output, scale 1–100; <60 low, 60–69 moderate, ≥70 high
- **IST** (Implant Stability Test, 임플란트 안정성 검사): DCA output from Anycheck, scale 1–99; red <60, orange 60–64, green ≥65
- **RFA** (Resonance Frequency Analysis, 공명주파수분석): non-invasive stability measurement via sinusoidal excitation of SmartPeg
- **DCA** (Damping Capacity Analysis, 감쇠용량분석): stability measurement via timed rod contact with implant/abutment
- **PCF** (pounds per cubic foot): polyurethane bone block density unit; 15 PCF ≈ low-density cancellous (D3–D4), 30 PCF ≈ medium-density
- **BMD** (Bone Mineral Density, 골밀도): here described by PCF of polyurethane model
