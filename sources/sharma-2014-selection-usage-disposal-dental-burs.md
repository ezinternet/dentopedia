## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional questionnaire survey (131/150 practitioners) showing diamond burs are preferred (75%) over tungsten carbide (15%), most burs reused until worn out (85%), with variable sterilization and universal dustbin disposal — abstract-only ingest.

## 한줄요약

단면 설문조사(개원의 131/150) — 다이아몬드 bur 선호(75%) > 텅스텐카바이드(15%), 대부분 마모될 때까지 재사용(85%), 멸균 빈도·냉각수 사용 가변, 폐기는 전원 일반쓰레기 — 초록만 인제스트(PMC 본문 비어 있음).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies real-world clinician bur preference: diamond (75%) >> tungsten carbide (15%).
- Documents reuse-until-worn behavior (85%) and awareness that cutting efficiency drops with use (only 33% agreed).
- Captures sterilization frequency variability (daily 35% / per-patient 35%) and universal non-segregated disposal (100% dustbin).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 20-question proforma (4 options each) mailed to 150 local practitioners; telephone follow-up.
- 131 responses (87% response rate); results as percentages.
- Domains: bur selection, usage/sterilization, disposal.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Bur preference: diamond 75%, tungsten carbide 15%.
- Reuse until worn out: 85%; agreed efficiency decreases with use: 33%.
- Sterilization: once daily 35%, every patient 35%.
- Disposal: dustbin 100%.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Low-evidence cross-sectional survey; single locality; self-reported behavior (recall/social-desirability bias).
- Abstract-only ingest (PMC body unavailable) — no methodological detail beyond abstract.
- No clinical outcome; describes attitudes/practice, not cutting-efficiency measurement.

## 7. Glossary

- Diamond bur: abrasive (grinding) rotary instrument; grit sizes; preferred for enamel/ceramic.
- Tungsten carbide bur: bladed (cutting) rotary instrument; efficient on dentin/amalgam, smoother margins.
- Grit size: diamond particle coarseness (coarse → fine/extra-fine).
- Cutting efficiency: material removal rate per applied load/time; declines with bur wear.
