## One-line Summary
SR + network meta-analysis (informing the ADA clinical practice guideline) ranking nonrestorative caries treatments — silver diamine fluoride (SDF) and sealant + fluoride combinations rank among the most effective for arresting/reversing lesions.

## 한줄요약
체계적문헌고찰+네트워크 메타분석(ADA 임상진료지침 근거): 비복원 우식치료를 순위화 — 은나노다이아민불소(SDF)와 실란트+불소 조합이 병변 정지·역전에 가장 효과적인 축에 속함.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal of Dental Research 2019;98(1):14–26. ADA Council on Scientific Affairs expert panel. Informs Slayton 2018 JADA guideline.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First comprehensive NMA across multiple nonrestorative caries interventions and all relevant surfaces.
- Enables direct + indirect ranking of fluoride- and nonfluoride-based interventions.
- Evidence base for the ADA nonrestorative caries clinical practice guideline.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- SR + Bayesian/frequentist NMA of parallel and split-mouth RCTs (any follow-up). MEDLINE, Embase, Cochrane CENTRAL.
- Outcomes: (1) arrest/reversal of noncavitated and cavitated lesions on primary and permanent teeth; (2) adverse events.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Multiple agents effective for arresting/reversing lesions; SDF (e.g., 38%) and sealant + fluoride-varnish combinations rank highly across surfaces.
- Effectiveness varies by lesion type (noncavitated vs cavitated), dentition, and surface (coronal vs root).
- Adverse events minor (e.g., SDF staining).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Heterogeneous protocols/concentrations/frequencies; variable follow-up; sparse networks for some comparisons; staining acceptability not captured in efficacy ranking.

## 6. Related Work
- Chestnutt 2017 FS vs FV RCT; Cochrane reviews on sealants and varnish; ADA guideline (Slayton 2018).

## 7. Glossary
- NMA: network meta-analysis. SDF: silver diamine fluoride. Nonrestorative/micro-invasive: lesion-level management preserving tooth structure.
