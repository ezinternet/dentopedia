## Why Ingested

하악 임플란트 지지 피개의치(Mandibular Implant-Supported Overdenture, MISO) 어태치먼트 선택에 대한 SR+MA로, 기존 [[complete-denture/prasad-2024-attachment-systems-mandibular-implant]]보다 연구 기간(2010–2020)이 겹치는 독립적 SR+MA. 두 리뷰가 서로 다른 포함 기준과 메타분석 결과를 보고하므로 비교 근거로 활용.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 6 RCTs (2010–2020) comparing ball, bar, magnet, locator, and telescopic attachments for mandibular 2-implant overdentures — ball < locator in prosthodontic complications (RR 0.55, p=0.03); bar ≈ telescopic in MBL and probing depth; bar = best retention, telescopic = best patient satisfaction.

## 한줄요약

하악 2임플란트 피개의치 어태치먼트 비교 SR+MA (6 RCT): 볼 어태치먼트가 로케이터보다 보철합병증 적음 (RR 0.55, p=0.03); 바≈텔레스코픽 (MBL·탐침깊이 유의차 없음); 최고 유지력=바, 최고 환자만족=텔레스코픽.

- **Institution**: Department of Prosthodontics, College of Dental Sciences and Research Centre, Ahmedabad, Gujarat, India

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides a PRISMA-compliant SR+MA specifically covering RCTs from 2010–2020, bridging the gap after Kim et al. 2012 systematic review
- Quantitatively compares ball vs. locator attachment for prosthodontic maintenance (meta-analysis, single RCT, n=48)
- Quantitatively compares milled bar vs. telescopic attachment for probing depth and marginal bone loss (meta-analysis, single RCT, n=45)
- Qualitative synthesis of retention and patient satisfaction across 4 attachment types: ball, bar, magnet, telescopic
- Clinical decision framework based on inter-arch space, bone quality, patient economics, and dexterity

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Design**: PRISMA-compliant SR+MA

**PICOTS**:
- P: Completely edentulous mandibular arch patients
- I: Late implant placement, delayed loading, ≥2 interforaminal implants
- C: Ball, bar, magnet (locator), telescopic attachments
- O: Prosthodontic maintenance/complications, retention, peri-implant tissue changes, patient satisfaction
- T: 2010–2020, minimum 1-year follow-up
- S: RCTs and crossover clinical trials

**Search databases**: PubMed/MEDLINE, Cochrane Central (CENTRAL), ScienceDirect; plus manual journal hand-search

**Screening**: 235 studies identified → 122 full-text assessed → 6 final included

**Risk of bias**: Cochrane Collaboration's tool (all 6 studies = low risk)

**Statistical tool**: RevMan 5.4 (Cochrane)
- Dichotomous data: Risk Ratio (RR) with 95% CI
- Continuous data: Mean Difference (MD) with 95% CI

**Meta-analysis performed for**: prosthodontic complications (ball vs. locator) and peri-implant tissue changes (bar vs. telescopic)

**Systematic review only (insufficient data for MA)**: retention and patient satisfaction

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Prosthodontic Complications (Ball vs. Locator)
- Ball attachment: significantly fewer complications than locator
- RR = 0.55, 95% CI, P = 0.03 (n=48 patients, 24 events)
- Single RCT; heterogeneity not applicable

### Peri-implant Probing Depth (Bar vs. Telescopic)
- No statistically significant difference
- RR = 0.20, 95% CI, P = 0.74 (n=45, single RCT)
- Milled bar showed 0.2 mm less probing depth

### Marginal Bone Loss (Bar vs. Telescopic)
- No statistically significant difference
- MD = 0.35, 95% CI, P = 0.10 (n=45, single RCT)

### Retention (qualitative)
- Bar attachment: highest retention
- Ball/stud: good retention, simple
- Telescopic: good retention with favorable tissue response
- Magnet: lowest retention, most maintenance needed

### Patient Satisfaction (qualitative)
- Telescopic: best patient satisfaction + least peri-implant mucosal changes
- Bar: high satisfaction but technically demanding and costly
- Ball: good satisfaction, preferred for limited inter-arch space
- Magnet: lower satisfaction due to corrosion and demagnetization

### Clinical Decision Summary
| Scenario | Recommended attachment |
|---|---|
| Limited inter-arch space | Ball/stud |
| Maximum retention needed | Bar |
| Best peri-implant tissue health | Telescopic |
| Cost/simplicity | Ball/stud |
| Patient satisfaction priority | Telescopic |

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 6 RCTs met inclusion criteria (2010–2020); evidence base remains thin
- Meta-analysis performed on single RCTs per comparison — heterogeneity not calculable
- Masticatory effectiveness and cost-effectiveness not quantitatively assessed
- Retention and patient satisfaction outcomes lacked data for meta-analysis
- All included studies used two-implant protocols; generalizability to 4-implant overdentures limited
- Different follow-up durations (1–5 years) across studies limit long-term conclusions
- Future multi-center RCTs with standardized outcome measures needed

## 6. Related Work

- Kim et al. 2012: Prior SR comparing attachment systems (up to August 2010); present review extends that search to 2020
- McGill Consensus (2002): 2-implant overdenture as first choice for edentulous mandible
- Prasad et al. 2024: Independent SR+MA on same topic with different inclusion criteria

## 7. Glossary

- **Overdenture attachment system (피개의치 어태치먼트)**: retentive coupling between implant and removable denture; matrix (female) in denture, patrix (male) on implant
- **Ball/stud attachment (볼 어태치먼트)**: spherical abutment retained by elastic O-ring; simple, low cost, allows some movement
- **Bar attachment (바 어태치먼트)**: connecting bar between implants with clip retention; highest retention and splinting effect
- **Magnet/locator attachment (자석·로케이터 어태치먼트)**: magnetic retention or LOCATOR proprietary design; easy insertion but susceptible to demagnetization/wear
- **Telescopic attachment (텔레스코픽 어태치먼트)**: double crown (inner coping + outer crown) system; superior tissue response and patient satisfaction
- **Prosthodontic maintenance (보철유지관리)**: any technical intervention required after prosthesis delivery (activation, replacement of matrix, relining)
- **Marginal bone loss (변연골소실, MBL)**: vertical bone resorption around implant neck; key peri-implant tissue metric
- **Probing depth (탐침깊이, PD)**: depth of peri-implant sulcus on probing; indicator of peri-implant tissue health
- **RevMan**: Review Manager software by Cochrane for meta-analysis
- **PICOTS**: Population, Intervention, Comparison, Outcome, Time, Study design framework
