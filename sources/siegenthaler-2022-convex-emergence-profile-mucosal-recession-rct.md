## Why Ingested

The emergence-profile shape (convex vs concave) of an implant provisional/final restoration is one of the most directly actionable "how to shape the abutment/restoration" decisions, yet it had little RCT evidence. This 3-arm RCT (convex vs concave vs healing-abutment-only control) supplies the key prospective evidence that a **concave** emergence profile preserves the mid-facial mucosal margin while a **convex** profile drives recession — refining the broader healing-abutment customization literature and reinforcing the buccal-profile RCT sibling [[wiki/implants/wang-2022-buccal-emergence-profile-peri-implant-rct]]. It also serves as a primary input refining [[wiki/implants/ruhstorfer-2024-customized-vs-conventional-healing-abutments-sr]].

## One-line Summary

3-arm RCT (n=47 implants, anterior; convex vs concave emergence vs healing-abutment control): at 12 months, mid-facial mucosal recession occurred in 64.3% of convex, 14.3% of concave, and 31.4% of control sites; convex carried ~13× higher odds of recession vs concave (OR 12.69, 95% CI 1.89–88.48, p=.01).

## 한줄요약

3군 RCT (임플란트 47개, 전치부; 볼록 vs 오목 emergence vs 치유지대주 대조군): 12개월에 협측 점막 퇴축 빈도가 볼록 64.3%, 오목 14.3%, 대조군 31.4% — 볼록이 오목 대비 퇴축 위험 약 13배 (OR 12.69, 95% CI 1.89–88.48, p=.01). 오목 형태가 점막 변연을 안정시킨다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First RCT to directly compare three transmucosal conditioning modalities — **convex** emergence provisional, **concave** emergence provisional, and **healing-abutment-only** control (no provisional) — for their effect on mid-facial mucosal margin stability in prosthetically ideal-position anterior implants.
2. Demonstrates that emergence-profile **shape**, not merely the presence of a provisional, governs recession risk: convex contour → high recession (64.3% at 12m), concave contour → low recession (14.3%).
3. Quantifies the effect as an adjusted odds ratio: convex vs concave OR 13.38 at 6m and 12.69 at 12m (both significant); control vs concave not significant.
4. Challenges the routine need for an implant provisional: PES/WES aesthetics did not differ between groups, while provisionals added ~2.5× more appointments and ~869 CHF (>50% of restoration cost).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Sample**: 47 patients (20 F / 27 M; mean age 60.3 y, range 25.5–81.2), each receiving 1 implant (OsseoSpeed EV, Astra Tech / Dentsply Sirona) in the anterior maxilla (40) or mandible (7) — incisors, canines, or premolars. Implants placed in prosthetically driven position (3–4 mm below prospective crown margin), screw-retained, by one faculty.
- **Sample-size basis**: Fisher exact test, α=5%, power 80%, assumed 56% difference in recession frequency → 13/group needed; 47 enrolled accounting for 20% dropout.
- **Randomization**: After abutment connection and before final impression, sealed-envelope allocation (computer-generated list) to CONVEX, CONCAVE, or CONTROL.
- **Test groups (CONVEX, CONCAVE)**: undercontoured screw-retained provisional crown on a temporary Ti abutment; the undercontour was filled stepwise with flowable composite to a convex or concave contour, shaped ≈1–2 mm above the abutment neck. Soft-tissue contour reassessed at 1 week; up to 3 conditioning appointments allowed. Final: monolithic zirconia crown cemented to individualized zirconia abutment (Atlantis) with the **same** emergence profile as the provisional, torqued 25 Ncm.
- **Control group**: standard Ti healing abutment (HealDesign EV / Healing Uni EV), diameter 5–6.5 mm, height 3.5–6.5 mm, at faculty discretion; no provisional.
- **Follow-up**: baseline (7–10 d after crown insertion), 6m and 12m after loading, all assessed by a blinded, calibrated examiner.
- **Primary outcome**: mucosal margin level — presence/absence of recession (dichotomized), from change in clinical crown length vs crown-insertion baseline.
- **Secondary outcomes**: PES/WES aesthetics, keratinized tissue width/height, buccal soft-tissue thickness (endo file ISO 15 at 1 mm apical to margin), papilla height, linear/profilometric contour changes (STL superimposition; ROI at 1 mm = ROI-1/TW1 and 3 mm = ROI-3/TW3 below margin), time/cost (CHF), marginal bone level (MBL on standardized radiographs, ImageJ).
- **Statistics**: multivariable logistic regression for recession presence (adjusted for soft-tissue thickness and KT width; CONCAVE as reference); GEE linear models with Wald χ² for repeated measures; Bonferroni post hoc; α=5%; SPSS v27.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Attrition**: after final impression, 2 lost (1 implant failed, 1 patient died) → 45 at baseline; 41 at 6m (4 Covid-related no-shows, rescheduled); 44 at 12m (1 left country). Implant and restoration survival between crown insertion and 12m = **100%**.
- **Recession frequency at 6m**: CONVEX 53.8% · CONCAVE 7.7% · CONTROL 21.4%.
- **Recession frequency at 12m**: CONVEX **64.3%** · CONCAVE **14.3%** · CONTROL 31.4%.
- **Mean recession magnitude at 12m**: CONVEX 0.72 ± 0.60 mm (median 0.50) · CONCAVE 1.00 ± 0.00 mm (median 1.00) · CONTROL 0.90 ± 0.65 mm (median 0.50). (At 6m: CONVEX 0.83 ± 0.40, CONCAVE 1.00 ± 0.00, CONTROL 0.83 ± 0.28 mm.)
- **Adjusted odds of recession (reference = CONCAVE)**:
  - 6m: CONVEX OR **13.38** (95% CI 1.29–138.51, p=.030*); CONTROL OR 3.30 (0.29–37.74, p=.335, NS).
  - 12m: CONVEX OR **12.69** (95% CI 1.89–88.48, p=.010*); CONTROL OR 2.43 (0.37–15.63, p=.350, NS).
  - With CONTROL as reference: CONVEX tended more prone (OR 5.2, p=.05).
- **Aesthetics (PES/WES)**: no between-group difference at any time (PES p=.735; WES p=.842); PES tended to improve over time in all groups (p=.061), magnitude similar (p=.552).
- **Soft-tissue thickness**: decreased in all groups over time (p=.003), magnitude similar between groups (p=.130/.421). CONVEX trended thinner at baseline (3.20 mm vs CONCAVE 4.04, CONTROL 4.56 mm; inter-group NS) — a plausible mechanism for its higher recession risk.
- **Peri-implant tissue width (TW1/TW3) & profilometric contour (ROI-1/ROI-3)**: significant reduction over time in all groups, magnitude similar between groups (ROI-3 between-group trend p=.067).
- **Marginal bone level**: similar between groups (p=.599); MBL increased (bone gain) over time (p<.001), magnitude similar.
- **Time/cost**: CONVEX mean 2.6 visits / 884 CHF; CONCAVE 2.4 visits / 854 CHF; CONTROL 0 provisional visits / 0 CHF. Provisional added ~869 CHF (>50% of the ~1500 CHF total restoration cost). Mean modification visits 1.6 (convex), 1.4 (concave).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Recession assessed by periodontal probe without a customized measuring stent; magnitude (~1 mm) is near the probe's error range → random error possible.
- Convex/concave contour was defined qualitatively — no numeric threshold, range, or angle was measured.
- Buccal plate thickness was not assessed (patients already had implants), a potential confounder; randomization is expected to balance it.
- The study cannot represent all clinical scenarios — when soft-tissue overgrowth must be deliberately reduced for aesthetics, a convex profile is intentionally indicated.
- 12-month follow-up only; longer-term margin behavior of concave profiles not yet established here.

## 6. Related Work

- Rompen et al. — pilot reporting ~13% recession with concave emergence (consistent with this study's 14.3%).
- Seyssens et al. — 10-year prospective on immediate single implants suggesting convex profile increases mid-facial recession risk (concordant).
- Furze et al. (1- and 3-year) — found provisionals improved PES, contrasting with this study's null aesthetic effect (attributed to broader inclusion incl. premolars and wide age range).
- Jemt — earlier study finding negligible added benefit of provisionals on soft-tissue volume at 2 years (concordant with this study's questioning of routine provisionals).
- Koutouzis & Ali — concave profile permits more room for soft-tissue ingrowth (mechanistic rationale).

## 7. Glossary

- **Emergence profile**: the transition zone (transmucosal contour) of the restoration from implant shoulder/platform to the mucosal margin.
- **Convex profile**: outwardly bulging transmucosal contour; in this trial associated with higher recession risk (and intentionally used to push down overgrown tissue).
- **Concave profile**: inwardly curved transmucosal contour; permits soft-tissue ingrowth, associated with mucosal-margin stability.
- **Mid-facial mucosal recession**: apical shift of the buccal mucosal margin relative to crown-insertion baseline (primary outcome).
- **PES / WES**: pink/white aesthetic score indices (0–10) for peri-implant soft tissue and crown aesthetics.
- **TW1 / TW3 (ROI-1 / ROI-3)**: peri-implant tissue width / profilometric contour measured 1 mm and 3 mm below the mid-facial mucosal margin.
- **MBL**: marginal bone level (radiographic implant shoulder-to-crest distance).
- **GEE**: generalized estimating equations — regression for correlated repeated measures.
