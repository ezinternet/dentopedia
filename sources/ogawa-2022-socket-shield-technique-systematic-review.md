## One-line Summary
J Prosthodont Res 2022;66(1):12-18: SR of 20 studies, 288 patients, socket shield technique with immediate implant — 9.5% complication rate; 90.5% survival without complications; follow-up 3-60 months; long-term evidence insufficient.

## 1. Key Results
- PubMed, Google Scholar, ScienceDirect; January 2000–December 2018
- 20 studies: 1 RCT, 2 cohort, 14 case reports, 3 retrospective case series
- 288 patients; follow-up 3–60 months
- Complications/adverse effects: 26/274 cases = 9.5%
- Survival without complications: 90.5%
- 12/20 studies rated good quality
- Complication types: failed osseointegration, shield mobility + infection, shield exposure, shield migration, apical root resorption
- Cases >12 months: mostly good esthetic appearance

## 2. Clinical Implications
- Socket shield technique (SST) shows feasibility with low complication rate
- Evidence base: predominantly case reports — low level
- Long-term predictability cannot be confirmed with available evidence
- Japan Prosthodontic Society-affiliated review
