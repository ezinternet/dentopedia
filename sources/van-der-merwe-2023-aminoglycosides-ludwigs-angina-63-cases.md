## One-line Summary
Retrospective analysis of 63 Ludwig's angina cases at University of Pretoria demonstrating that empirical aminoglycoside use is not well-supported by bacterial culture data, with predominantly streptococcal (aminoglycoside-resistant) organisms identified.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Stomatology, Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery 2023
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.jormas.2023.101409
- **Type**: Retrospective study
- **Year**: 2023

## 2. Key Contributions
- Retrospective microbiological analysis of 63 consecutive Ludwig's angina cases
- Challenges empirical aminoglycoside use by demonstrating that culture results show predominantly Gram-positive (streptococcal) organisms for which aminoglycosides have poor activity
- Provides data from a South African tertiary referral center serving a largely immunocompromised and diabetic population
- Guides evidence-based antibiotic stewardship for deep neck space infections

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Retrospective chart and culture review, University of Pretoria / Steve Biko Academic Hospital
- 63 consecutive Ludwig's angina cases over study period
- Data collected: demographics, comorbidities, source of infection, microbiology (pus cultures, blood cultures), antibiotic regimens, surgical management, outcomes
- Culture results analyzed for organism type and antimicrobial sensitivity patterns
- Statistical analysis correlating organism type with empirical antibiotic choice (aminoglycosides)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Source**: Odontogenic origin predominant (mandibular molars most common source)
- **Organisms**: Predominantly Gram-positive streptococci (aminoglycoside-intrinsically resistant); mixed flora with anaerobes also common; Gram-negative organisms (aminoglycoside-sensitive) found in subset (~minority)
- **Aminoglycoside coverage gap**: Most organisms isolated are intrinsically resistant to aminoglycosides (streptococci) or require high synergistic dosing
- **Comorbidities**: High prevalence of diabetes mellitus and HIV in the cohort (South African context)
- **Treatment**: Surgical drainage (I&D) + IV antibiotics; airway management critical
- **Outcomes**: Data on mortality, ICU stay, tracheostomy rates presented
- **Conclusion**: Empirical aminoglycoside use not justified by culture data; β-lactam + metronidazole or penicillin/ampicillin-sulbactam recommended empirically

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Single-center, retrospective design
- Culture results may not represent true infecting flora (contamination, prior antibiotics)
- Population may not be generalizable to low-risk populations without HIV/DM comorbidities
- Larger prospective multicenter studies needed

## 6. Related Work
- Related to deep neck space infection literature (Gargava 2022)
- Connects to antibiotic stewardship in head and neck infections
- Relevant to MRONJ and systemic disease management in dental patients

## 7. Glossary
- **Ludwig's Angina**: Rapidly spreading bilateral cellulitis of submandibular, submental, and sublingual spaces; odontogenic origin in most cases; life-threatening airway emergency
- **Aminoglycosides**: Class of antibiotics (gentamicin, tobramycin, amikacin) active against aerobic Gram-negative bacilli; intrinsically poor activity against streptococci and anaerobes
- **Empirical Antibiotic Therapy**: Antibiotic treatment initiated before culture results are available, based on expected flora
- **I&D**: Incision and Drainage; primary surgical management of Ludwig's angina
- **Antibiotic Stewardship**: Program to optimize antibiotic use and minimize resistance
- **Streptococci**: Gram-positive cocci; most common organisms in odontogenic infections; naturally resistant to aminoglycosides
