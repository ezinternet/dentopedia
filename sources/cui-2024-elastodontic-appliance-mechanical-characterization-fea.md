## Why Ingested

This paper supplies the **material/biomechanics substrate** for the EF-Line/elastodontic family: it explains *how* silicone elastomer elastodontic appliances (EAs) deliver light, biocompatible force (viscoelastic stress-softening / Mullins effect) and *how* that force-delivery degrades with intraoral and artificial salivary aging. Where the sibling scoping review [[wiki/orthodontics/myofunctional/ureni-2024-elastodontic-devices-sagittal-malocclusion-scoping-review]] catalogs *clinical* sagittal-malocclusion outcomes, this study grounds the force-mechanism and the 6–9-month replacement window in measured mechanical/structural data (compression set, peak stress, FTIR/XRD/SEM), and flags an alkaline-saliva (periodontitis) degradation risk that has direct protocol implications.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro mechanical + structural bench study (46 EAs artificial-aged at pH 6/7/8 + 5 clinically retrieved EAs, 2–12 mo) showing elastodontic appliances are PDMS + silica-filler viscoelastic elastomers whose stress-softening (Mullins) ability and peak stress decline and compression set rises with aging — driven by side-chain (–CH₃) scission + Si–O–Si main-chain crosslinking, accelerated under alkaline (OH⁻) saliva, with structural integrity still acceptable up to ~6 months of clinical service.

## 한줄요약

체외 기계·구조 분석 연구(인공노화 EA 46개 pH 6/7/8 + 임상 회수 EA 5개 2~12개월): 엘라스토돈틱 장치(Elastodontic Appliance, EA)는 PDMS + 실리카 필러 점탄성 엘라스토머이며, 노화될수록 응력연화(Mullins 효과)와 최대응력은 감소하고 압축영구변형(Compression Set, CS)은 증가한다. 기전은 측쇄(–CH₃) 절단 + Si–O–Si 주쇄 가교이며 알칼리(OH⁻) 타액에서 가속되고, 구조적 무결성은 임상 사용 약 6개월까지는 허용 가능하다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Identifies the polymer chemistry of elastodontic appliances as **polydimethylsiloxane (PDMS) + silica-based filler particles** via FTIR/XRD — establishing EAs as two-part highly viscoelastic liquid silicone rubber (LSR) devices.
2. Characterizes the **force-delivery mechanism** quantitatively: viscoelastic stress-softening (Mullins effect), residual strain, and compression set under cyclic compression — these are *why* EAs exert light, biocompatible orthodontic force when the patient bites the occlusal plate.
3. First study to analyze the **EA aging/degradation mechanism under defined salivary pH** (6, 7, 8) rather than a neutral environment only — directly relevant to caries-prone (acidic) and periodontitis (alkaline, up to pH 8) patients.
4. Combines **artificial aging** (n=46, controlled pH/time) with **clinically retrieved appliances** (n=5, 2/3/6/9/12 months) so in-vitro mechanics are anchored to real intraoral wear.
5. Establishes a clinically actionable degradation timeline and the alkaline-saliva risk → recommends **surface modification / filler modification** for periodontitis patients and proposes a ~6-month structurally-acceptable service window.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Samples**
- 46 standard-shape EAs for artificial aging: groups A (1 mo), B (2 mo), C (3 mo), each n=15, plus 1 REF (unaged).
- Each timepoint subdivided by artificial-saliva pH 6, 7, 8 (labeled X6/X7/X8; e.g., C8 = 3 mo, pH 8).
- 5 clinically retrieved EAs worn 2, 3, 6, 9, 12 months (5 patients, salivary pH 6.6–7.4), retrieved due to rupture or design re-submission.

**Artificial aging protocol**
- Artificial saliva (CMC, Leagene), pH pre-adjusted to 6/7/8, 37 °C water bath, beakers foil-sealed (light excluded), saliva volume = 3× sample volume (full submersion), fluid changed every 3 days.
- Accelerated-aging equivalence: **3 months of 24-h artificial aging ≈ 6 months of clinical service** (12 h/day wear).
- Clinical retrieved specimens: ~7 mm × 3 mm × 2 mm pieces cut from buccal flange (BF) and occlusal plate (OP), 2 each (=4 specimens/EA).

**Compression tests** (universal testing machine E43.104 MTS, 23 °C, crosshead ~5 mm/min, pre-force 1 N, standard dental-model fixture, 5 repeats):
- **T4** — 4 cycles, increasing strain amplitude 0.5 / 1 / 1.5 / 2 mm; load from 1 N pre-force to amplitude then unload at same speed. → Mullins index (MI).
- **T10** — 10 cycles at fixed 1.5 mm amplitude. → compression set (CS) + peak stress (PS, measured at 1.0 mm to avoid elastic limit).

**Derived parameters**
- **Mullins index (MI)** = ratio of stress in loading n to loading n+1 at max strain of loading n (stress-softening / energy-absorption proxy).
- **Compression set (CS)** = ratio of instant residual strain to strain amplitude (measured instantly after unloading; inversely proportional to resilience).
- **Peak stress (PS)** at 1.0 mm in T10.

**Structural characterization**
- SEM-EDS (JSM-7001F, 15.0 kV; gold-sputtered 120 s) — surface morphology + elemental mapping of C, O, Ca, Si.
- XRD (SmartLab, CuKα, 40 kV / 200 mA, 5–100° 2θ, 0.02° step, 5°/min) on BF samples.
- ATR-FTIR (Thermo Nicolet iS50, 400–4000 cm⁻¹) for organic functional groups.

**Statistics**: SPSS 26. Two-way ANOVA (peak stress); three-way ANOVA + LSD post-hoc (MI, CS). Significance p < 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Material identity**
- FTIR REF peaks: Si–O–Si at 1100–1000 cm⁻¹, Si–CH₃ at 1257 cm⁻¹, Si–(CH₃) at 792 cm⁻¹; C–H stretches of –CH₃ bend / –CH₂ at 2958 and 2917 cm⁻¹ → **PDMS**.
- XRD: amorphous diffractogram; broad peak 2θ = 10–15° (crosslinked PDMS), broad peak ~2θ = 22° (SiO₂ filler) → silicon-based material.

**Mechanical (mean ± SD, selected from Table 3)**
- REF: MI(1–2) 1.021 ± 0.006, MI(2–3) 1.048 ± 0.007; CS1 0.335, CS2 0.346, CS10 0.406; peak stress **14.818 ± 1.627**.
- MI trend: REF has largest MI except A8/B8; **MI decreases with prolonged aging time**. Alkaline saliva (A8/B8/C8) showed the largest MI (p < 0.01 at 1 & 2 mo, p < 0.05 at 3 mo); neutral vs acidic not significant (p = 0.696 at 1 mo, p = 0.938 at 2 mo).
- Peak stress: decreased after 1 month and with immersion time (p < 0.01); **neutral (pH 7) saliva retained highest stress potential** (p < 0.01). Peak stress fell from ~14.8 (REF) toward ~9.8–10.5 by 3 months (e.g., C6 9.878, C7 10.416, C8 9.752).
- Compression set: lower CS when aged in artificial saliva than REF (p < 0.01); CS rose with pH at 1 mo (p < 0.01); non-monotonic at 2 mo (B8 ↑, B7 ↓, p < 0.01); acidic vs neutral not significant at 3 mo (p = 0.380); CS increased with cycles (p < 0.01). Instant CS values ranged ~0.3–0.45.
- **Aging time predominates**: no significant cycle-number × pH interaction (MI p = 0.32, CS p = 0.75, peak stress p = 0.29).
- A "slant drop" in T4 cycle 3/4 and a "shoulder" around 3 N in T10 = signs of **filler–polymer interface destruction**, more prominent in aged samples.

**Structural / morphological (SEM-EDS)**
- REF: homogeneous smooth BF (parallel creases ~3 μm spacing) and OP (white filler particles, shallow pores); even elemental dispersion, no agglomeration.
- In-vitro 3 mo: C6/C7 only minor voids/pores; **C8 (alkaline) most severe** — surface delamination + outer-layer film cracking (filler debonding).
- Silicone filler particle diameter **3–5 μm** (larger when packed).
- Intraoral aging timeline:
  - 2–3 mo: little morphology change early; voids + outer-layer film detachment appear by 3 mo.
  - 6 mo: BF scale-like structure, microcracks ~50 μm long, filler exfoliation; OP ordered parallel ridges ~70 μm gaps — **still acceptable, no daily-use impact**.
  - 9 mo: OP ridge gaps narrowed to 20–40 μm; dental calculus (<200 μm) attached between ridges; complete surface debonding (C/O agglomeration at ridge margins).
  - 12 mo: visible OP fractures, cross-section voids/microfractures (interior less severe than surface); larger calculus plaques 50–300 μm with high Ca agglomeration; **no longer suitable for intraoral service**.

**FTIR/XRD aging chemistry**
- No new chemical bands / no oxygen-containing groups → **molecular composition unchanged** by aging.
- With aging: Si–O–Si main-chain absorption **increases** (more crosslinking / higher polymerization); –CH₃ and Si–(CH₃) absorption **decreases** (side-chain scission). Alkaline-aged sample had lowest –CH₃ (~2960 cm⁻¹) peak height.
- XRD aged samples match REF amorphous pattern (intraoral aging doesn't disrupt matrix), but additional sharp crystalline peaks (except 2-mo) match **Fukalite** (ICDD 01-075-5080 / 01-075-2979, Ca-silicate-carbonate-hydroxide) → calcium silicate carbonate hydroxide forms on surface (detached filler = calcification nucleus for calculus).

**Mechanism synthesis**
- Side-chain (–CH₃) scission + Si–O–Si main-chain crosslinking → denser polymer network → reduced inter-chain mobility → **lower Mullins effect, higher compression set, reduced viscoelasticity/stress-softening** (material stiffens).
- H⁺ and OH⁻ both catalyze Si–O–Si crosslinking; OH⁻ (alkaline) reacts with silicone to form silanol salts, breaking side-chain methyl groups → poor surface/inner adhesion → interfacial defects (worst degradation). Water plasticizing effect also reduces stress-softening.
- Intraoral samples degraded **more severely** than in-vitro → compression loading + bacterial adhesion outweigh salivary immersion alone.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only **one EA brand** tested — results may not generalize to other LSR formulations/shapes.
- Clinical impact of measured mechanical change on actual tooth-movement / OMT efficiency **not yet demonstrated** (needs clinical trials).
- CS measured *instantly* after unloading (no full strain-recovery wait), so reported CS (0.3–0.45) is higher than literature values that allow recovery.
- Future: surface modification (increase abrasion resistance, preserve stable surface), nanoscale particulate fillers to improve viscoelasticity against salivary aging; 3D-scanning/3D-printing for customized EA fabrication.

## 6. Related Work

- Klammt's Elastic Open Activator (EOA) — origin of elastodontic concept.
- Prior 5-cycle EA compression test reporting 0.95–1.75% plastic deformation (not in simulated oral environment).
- Clear-aligner (thermoformed) viscoelastic aging studies — surface molecular structure stable for weeks, but elastic modulus drops + stress relaxation accelerates with use.
- Silicone-rubber medical-material aging studies in sweat/disinfectant/sebum; silicone composite insulator pH-sensitivity literature.

## 7. Glossary

- **Elastodontic appliance (EA)** — removable silicone-elastomer myofunctional device delivering light force via biting on occlusal plates; e.g., EF-Line family.
- **Oral myofunctional therapy (OMT)** — re-education of perioral muscles via removable appliances; EA worn during sleep + ≥2 h awake (~12 h/day).
- **PDMS** — polydimethylsiloxane, the silicone polymer matrix of EAs.
- **LSR** — liquid silicone rubber, highly elastic modern EA ingredient.
- **Mullins effect / Mullins index (MI)** — stress-softening of a filled elastomer on repeated loading; MI quantifies energy-absorption ability (lower MI = stiffer/less absorbing).
- **Compression set (CS)** — residual deformation after unloading; inverse of resilience (higher CS = worse permanent deformation).
- **Viscoelasticity** — time-dependent stress–strain behavior (stress relaxation) that lets EAs exert slight, biocompatible forces.
- **BF / OP** — buccal flange / occlusal plate (the two sampled appliance regions).
- **Fukalite** — Ca-silicate-carbonate-hydroxide crystalline phase forming on aged EA surface (calculus precursor).
