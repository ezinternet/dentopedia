## Why Ingested

치은퇴축(Gingival Recession, GR) RT1 결손의 근면피복(root coverage)에서 자가혈소판농축물(Autologous Platelet Concentrates, APC — PRF/PRP/CGF 등)이 결합조직이식(Connective Tissue Graft, CTG)의 구개부 채취 morbidity를 회피하는 대안으로 얼마나 유효한지를 정량화한, Periodontology 2000의 systematic review + network meta-analysis(NMA, 109 RCT · 4712 결손 · 3188명). 자매 PRF 근거인 [[wiki/periodontics/sherif-2025-iprf-vitamin-c-nonsurgical-periodontitis]](비외과 치주에서의 iPRF)와 [[wiki/oral-surgery/al-maawi-2021-prf-extraction-socket-healing-sr]](발치와 PRF)의 PRF 적응증 지도를 근면피복 영역으로 확장한다. 또한 치주성형 술식 선택 프레임인 [[wiki/periodontics/mucogingival-surgery-apf-fgg-ctg]]에 "PRF는 언제 CTG를 대체하나(baseline KTW 존재 시)"라는 정량 근거를 더한다.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review + network meta-analysis (109 RCTs, 4712 RT1 recessions, 3188 patients; Periodontology 2000, 2024): among APCs added to a coronally advanced flap (CAF), only CAF+PRF significantly increased mean root coverage over CAF alone (+6.12%, 95% CI 1.83–10.42, p=0.02) — PRP and CGF did not — and the PRF benefit grew with more baseline keratinized tissue width (KTW), while no APC improved KTW or gingival thickness (GT).

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰 + 네트워크 메타분석(109 RCT · RT1 치은퇴축 4712 · 환자 3188명; Periodontology 2000, 2024): 치관변위판막(CAF)에 더한 자가혈소판농축물 중 CAF+PRF만 CAF 단독 대비 평균 근면피복을 유의하게 높였고(+6.12%, 95% CI 1.83–10.42, p=0.02), PRP·CGF는 무의미; PRF 효과는 baseline 각화조직폭(KTW)이 클수록 커졌으나 어떤 APC도 KTW·치은두께(GT)는 개선하지 못함.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Periodontology 2000, 2024
- **Design**: Systematic review + network meta-analysis (NMA) of randomized controlled trials; PROSPERO-registered, PRISMA-NMA reporting, Cochrane methodology
- **Search**: MEDLINE/PubMed, EMBASE, CENTRAL, LILACS, Web of Science, Scopus + grey literature + hand search; last search 2023-01-30; no language/date restriction
- **Population/PICOS**: RT1 (Cairo type 1, no interproximal attachment loss) gingival recession; APC (PRF/PRP/CGF/L-PRF/T-PRF/PCG) or flap alone; comparator = CAF alone (control); outcomes mRC, KTW gain, GT gain; RCTs ≥10 patients/arm, ≥6-month follow-up
- **Included**: 109 RCTs; 4712 treated recessions in 3188 patients entered the NMA
- **Risk of bias**: RoB-2 on 111 assessments — 34 (30.6%) low, 32 (28.8%) some concerns, 45 (40.5%) high; commonest issues = deviations from intended interventions (D2) and missing outcome data (D4), largely unblinded outcome assessment

## 2. Key Contributions

- First **network meta-analysis** ranking APC preparation methods (PRF vs PRP vs CGF vs CAF alone) for RT1 root coverage on a common comparator (flap alone), enabling indirect comparisons never made head-to-head in RCTs.
- Establishes **PRF as the only APC that significantly augments mean root coverage** of a coronally advanced flap; PRP and CGF show no significant benefit.
- Identifies **baseline keratinized tissue width (KTW) as a positive effect modifier** of the PRF benefit — the more KTW at baseline, the greater the PRF root-coverage gain.
- Shows **APCs do not increase KTW or gingival thickness (GT)** — a predictable KTW/GT gain remains a property of autogenous grafts (CTG), positioning APCs as an adjunct in KTW-adequate sites, ideally with a scaffold/matrix for GT.
- Provides a decision framework: CTG preferred where KTW is deficient / recession is deep; PRF (± matrix) reasonable where an adequate KTW band already exists, to reduce palatal-harvest morbidity.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Model**: mixed-model NMA with CAF alone as reference; separate models for mRC, KTW gain, GT gain. Baseline Rec/GT/KT and single- vs multiple-site approach entered as covariates; arms weighted by treated sample size; multivariate handling of multi-arm-study correlation; heterogeneity variance from random-effects distribution; linearity checked (no non-linearity).
- **Analyzed network**: only CAF, CAF+PRF, CAF+PRP, CAF+CGF had ≥3 independent trials sharing a common arm (92 studies) → comparable; CAF+T-PRF and CAF+PCG had single arms and were **not** statistically compared.
- **Data**: of 109 RCTs — 105 reported KTW, 108 Rec, 46 GT. APC arms: PRF 32, CGF 4, L-PRF 3, PRP 3, PCG 1, T-PRF 1; flap-alone arms 99. Software: R 4.2.1 (lme4, lmerTest, dplyr, tidyr; igraph/ggplot2 for plots).
- **Screening**: dual independent (SB, LT), arbitration by HLW/HS; kappa 0.93 (title/abstract) and 0.97 (full-text).
- **Centrifugation protocols tabulated** (Table 2): PRF membranes commonly ~2700g/12 min or 3000g/10 min; CGF multi-step 2400–3000g; PRP two-spin 1300g then 2000g/10 min; many PRF protocols "not reported" (NR) — a reporting-standardization gap.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Mean root coverage (mRC), NMA vs CAF alone:**
- **CAF+PRF: +6.12%** (95% CI 1.83 to 10.42, p=0.02) — only significant arm.
- CAF+CGF: +7.47% (95% CI −3.88 to 18.82, p=0.33) — NS.
- CAF+PRP: −0.61% (95% CI −14.43 to 13.21, p=0.92) — NS.
- **Baseline KTW × CAF+PRF interaction: +4.98** (95% CI 0.85 to 9.12, p=0.03) — PRF benefit rises with more baseline KTW.
- Time × mRC: −0.146 (95% CI −0.21 to −0.08, p<0.01) — modest recession relapse over time.

**Keratinized tissue width (KTW) gain, NMA vs CAF alone (all NS):**
- CAF+PRP −0.66 mm (95% CI −5.31 to 3.98, p=0.77); CAF+PRF −0.10 mm (−0.87 to 0.67, p=0.79); CAF+CGF +0.84 mm (−1.24 to 2.93, p=0.42). Time +0.003 mm (p=0.03) — slight KTW increase across all treatments.

**Gingival thickness (GT) gain, NMA vs CAF alone (all NS):**
- CAF+PRP −0.31 mm (p=0.461); CAF+PRF +0.04 mm (p=0.78); CAF+CGF +0.25 mm (p=0.53). Time −0.004 mm (p<0.001) — slight GT reduction over time.

**Head-to-head CTG vs PRF (descriptive, Table 3):** CTG generally matched or exceeded PRF on mRC (e.g., Uraz 2015 CTG 96.5% vs PRF 75.3%; Joshi 2020 CTG 93.3% vs PRF 70.6%), though some trials were near-equivalent (Tunali 2015 77.4% vs 76.6%; Uzun 2018 93.2% vs 93.3%). Authors emphasize CTG (viable transplanted cells) still yields higher mRC on the average defect.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- APCs vs matrices/scaffolds and vs other biologics (rhPDGF-BB, EMD) **could not be assessed** — too few trials; these comparisons are the key open question.
- 40.5% of assessed studies at **high risk of bias** (mostly unblinded outcome assessment, missing data); many centrifugation protocols "not reported."
- Analysis limited to **RT1** defects and mostly **PRF-alone-vs-CAF**; combinations (PRF + CTG, PRF + collagen matrix) largely untested.
- Heterogeneous PRF sub-types pooled; single-arm APCs (T-PRF, PCG) uncompared.
- **Future**: adequately powered RCTs comparing APCs with graft substitutes/matrices and other growth factors; standardized blood-preparation reporting; cost-effectiveness.

## 6. Related Work

- Sherif 2025 (iPRF + vitamin C, non-surgical periodontitis) — sibling PRF-efficacy evidence in a different periodontal application.

## 7. Glossary

- **APC (Autologous Platelet Concentrate)**: blood-derived product from venipuncture + centrifugation; includes PRP, PRGF, PRF, L-PRF, A-PRF, T-PRF, CGF.
- **PRF (Platelet-Rich Fibrin)**: second-generation, anticoagulant-free fibrin scaffold with sustained growth-factor release (PDGF, TGF-β1, IGF, VEGF).
- **PRP / PRGF**: first-generation platelet concentrates requiring anticoagulant/activator; weaker, shorter-lived fibrin network.
- **CGF (Concentrated Growth Factor)**: PRF variant made by multi-speed centrifugation.
- **CAF (Coronally Advanced Flap)**: flap coronally repositioned to cover the exposed root; the control/positive comparator here.
- **CTG (Connective Tissue Graft)**: autogenous palatal graft, root-coverage gold standard for mRC, but with harvest morbidity.
- **mRC (mean root coverage)**: mean % of the exposed root re-covered.
- **KTW (keratinized tissue width)** / **GT (gingival thickness)**: soft-tissue quality metrics tied to long-term stability.
- **RT1 (Cairo Recession Type 1)**: recession with no interproximal attachment loss.
- **NMA (network meta-analysis)**: combines direct + indirect comparisons across a treatment network.
