## One-line Summary

Systematic review (8 papers; 5 RCTs) of motivational interviewing within general dental practice — all 5 RCTs showed MI-inclusive interventions positively affected oral health and health behaviour; MI proposed as a high-value addition to the dental team skill set.

## 한줄요약

치과 일반진료에 한정한 MI 체계적 문헌고찰(8편, RCT 5편) — RCT 5편 전부에서 MI 포함 개입이 구강건강·건강행동에 긍정적 효과. MI를 치과 팀 스킬셋에 추가할 가치가 있다는 영국발 근거.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: British Dental Journal 2016;221(12):785–791
- DOI: 10.1038/sj.bdj.2016.952
- Study type: Systematic review (subset of a larger oral-health-promotion SR), 8 included papers, 5 RCTs
- Authorship: Elizabeth Kay (Peninsula Dental School) — established UK dental public-health author
- Setting: General dental practice (one-to-one chairside), papers since 1994; 20 online resources searched (Ovid Medline, Embase incl.)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Narrows the broad MI evidence base specifically to the general-dental-practice context, so GDPs can decide whether to train in MI.
- Frames MI conceptually as autonomy support / person-centred conversation — the clinician abandons the "I have the solution / fix the wrong thing" stance.
- Concludes MI is a worthwhile, trainable addition to the dental team skill set for chairside oral-health promotion.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Subset analysis of a larger SR on whether oral-health promotion in dental practice is effective.
- Inclusion: papers applying the behavioural/psychological theories underpinning MI to oral health promotion; published since 1994.
- 8 papers met quality criteria; 7 judged externally valid; 5 were RCTs.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- All 5 RCTs: MI-inclusive interventions had a positive effect on oral health and/or health behaviour.
- MI grounded in autonomy support reduces patient defensiveness and facilitates change in a one-to-one setting.
- Recommendation: MI training for dental personnel (dentists and team) is a useful skill addition.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small evidence base (8 papers); heterogeneous outcomes; no head-to-head of training methods.
- Narrative synthesis (no meta-analysis); advocate framing.
- Does not quantify pooled effect size — qualitative "positive effect" only.

## 7. Glossary
- **Motivational interviewing (MI)**: collaborative, person-centred guiding style to elicit and strengthen a patient's own motivation to change.
- **Autonomy support**: clinician stance acknowledging the patient's choices are entirely their own.
- **GDP**: General Dental Practitioner.
