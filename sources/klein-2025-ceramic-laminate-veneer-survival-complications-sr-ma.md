## Why Ingested

Morimoto 2016 SR+MA (장석계/글래스세라믹만 포함)의 후속 근거로, 리튬 디실리케이트(LDS)와 지르코니아를 포함한 최신 4가지 세라믹 소재별 생존율 및 합병증 비율을 비교함. [[veneers/morimoto-2016-feldspathic-glass-ceramic-veneer-survival-sr-ma]] 대비 더 넓은 소재 범위와 단기/중기/장기 시점별 분류를 제공하여 임상적 소재 선택에 직접적인 근거를 제시.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 29 studies (7,753 veneers, 986 patients): LDS shows highest survival (96.81%) and lowest complication rates at 10.4 years; feldspathic and LRGC comparable in survival but higher complication rates; zirconia 100% at 2.6 years (insufficient long-term data).

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (29편, 7,753개 비니어): LDS 생존율 96.81%로 최고, 장석계·LRGC와 생존율은 유사하나 합병증 훨씬 낮음; 지르코니아 2.6년 100% 생존(장기 데이터 부족).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Comprehensive SR+MA covering four ceramic material types: feldspathic, leucite-reinforced glass-ceramic (LRGC), lithium disilicate (LDS), and zirconia
- First systematic review to stratify survival and complication rates at three time points: short-term (1–3 years), mid-term (4–6 years), and long-term (≥7 years)
- Distinguished three complication categories: technical, esthetic, and biological
- Found LDS had lowest long-term complication rates despite comparable survival to other materials
- Zirconia showed 100% survival but only short-term data available (mean 2.6 years)
- Identified no statistically significant difference in survival rates between feldspathic, LRGC, and LDS by metaregression

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search databases**: MEDLINE (via PubMed), Cochrane Central Register of Controlled Trials (via Cochrane Library), Scopus, WHO International Clinical Trials Registry Platform, ClinicalTrials.gov
- **Search period**: Inception to February 19, 2024
- **Search strategy**: Developed by information scientist (MIM) based on text analysis of 18 known clinical studies
- **Reference screening**: Hand-searched reference lists of included studies and recent reviews
- **Deduplication**: Deduklick algorithm via Rayyan software
- **Initial titles**: 12,592 + 14 from hand-search
- **After deduplication**: 7,913 titles
- **Full-text screened**: 98 articles
- **Studies included**: 29 studies (41 study arms)
  - 2 RCTs, 13 prospective cohorts, 14 retrospective clinical investigations
- **Sample**: 7,753 anterior ceramic laminate veneers in 986 patients (age range 17–87 years)
- **Follow-up range**: 1.0–20.7 years; studies published 1994–2024
- **Countries**: Belgium, Brazil, China, Germany, Italy, Iran, Netherlands, Poland, Spain, Switzerland, Turkey, USA
- **Risk of bias tools**: Cochrane RoB 2.0 for RCTs; MINOR (Methodological index for non-randomized studies) for observational studies
- **Statistical analysis**: Random-effects model (DerSimonian and Laird); arcsine transformation for proportion data; JASP software (v0.19.0); Egger's test for funnel plot asymmetry (if ≥10 studies per meta-analysis)
- **PICO**:
  - P = patients needing ceramic laminate veneers on permanent anterior teeth/premolars
  - I = ceramic laminate veneers ≥1 year in function
  - C = not considered (prognosis-focused)
  - O = survival + technical/esthetic/biological complications
- **Preparation designs recorded**: No-prep, window/feather, butt joint, palatal chamfer (5 designs total across studies)
- **Fabrication methods**: Conventional (majority), digital workflow (5 study arms), conventional-digital hybrid (1 arm)
- **Cementation**: Light-curing or dual-curing composite cements

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Pooled Survival Rates (at 10.4 years pooled observation)

| Material | Pooled Survival Rate | CI | Studies |
|---|---|---|---|
| Feldspathic | 96.13% | — | Multiple |
| LRGC | 93.70% | — | Multiple |
| LDS | 96.81% | — | Multiple |
| Zirconia | 100% | — | 1 study (2.6 yr only) |

- No statistically significant difference between feldspathic, LRGC, and LDS survival rates (metaregression p > 0.05)

### Complication Rates at 10.4 Years

| Material | Technical | Esthetic | Biological |
|---|---|---|---|
| Feldspathic | 41.48% | 19.64% | 6.51% |
| LRGC | 29.87% | 17.89% | 4.4% |
| LDS | 6.1% | 1.9% | 0.45% |
| Zirconia | 0% | 0% | 0% (at 2.6 yr) |

- LDS dramatically outperforms other materials in all complication categories
- Technical complications: cracks, chipping, bulk fracture, debonding, marginal gaps
- Esthetic complications: marginal discoloration (slight to severe)
- Biological complications: tooth fracture, secondary caries, endodontic complications, tooth loss
- Digital vs conventional workflow comparison was not feasible due to insufficient studies

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Most included studies were retrospective (14/29), introducing selection and recall bias
- Only 2 RCTs available in the field; both met inclusion criteria with 3 eligible study arms
- Zirconia data limited to one study at 2.6 years — no long-term evidence available
- Digital workflow studies too few for separate analysis
- Heterogeneity was present in many subgroup analyses (high I² in some groups)
- Arcsine transformation necessary for extreme proportions but limits clinical interpretability
- No long-term RCT data available for any ceramic veneer material
- Future need: more RCTs with standardized outcomes, longer follow-up, digital workflow studies, zirconia long-term data

## 7. Glossary

- **Feldspathic porcelain**: Traditional glassy-matrix ceramic; highest esthetics, lowest strength; requires platinum foil or refractory die fabrication
- **LRGC (Leucite-Reinforced Glass-Ceramic)**: Glass-ceramic with leucite crystals; e.g., IPS Empress; moderate strength, excellent esthetics
- **LDS (Lithium Disilicate)**: Glass-ceramic with lithium disilicate crystals; e.g., IPS e.max Press/CAD; best balance of esthetics and strength among glass-ceramics
- **Zirconia**: Polycrystalline ceramic (no vitreous phase); highest strength; bonding requires air-particle abrasion + primer (APC concept); newer generations have improved translucency
- **RMC (Resin-Matrix Ceramic)**: Ceramic-reinforced polymer composites (e.g., Cerasmart, Vita Enamic); excluded from this review
- **Technical complication**: Material-level failure — cracks, chipping, bulk fracture, debonding, marginal gaps
- **Esthetic complication**: Marginal discoloration (slight/superficial to severe with penetration)
- **Biological complication**: Tooth-level events — fracture, secondary caries, endodontic treatment, tooth loss
- **APC concept**: Air-particle abrasion + Primer + Composite cement — recommended bonding protocol for zirconia
- **MINOR**: Methodological Index for Non-Randomized Studies — risk of bias tool for observational studies
- **Arcsine transformation**: Statistical transformation for proportion data to normalize variance at extremes (0% or 100%)
- **Random-effects model**: Statistical model assuming true effect size varies across studies; accounts for between-study heterogeneity
