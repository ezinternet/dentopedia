## Why Ingested

근관치료된 대구치(Endodontically-Treated Teeth, ETT) 수복 시 최소침습 준비(occlusal veneer/overlay) vs. 전통적 adhesive crown 간 피로 후 파절강도 차이를 직접 비교한 인비트로 연구. 기존 [[inlay/griffis-2022-tooth-cusp-preservation-lithium-disilicate-onlay-fatigue]]가 활성 치아의 리튬 디실리케이트 파절강도를 다루었으나, ETT에서 준비 형태(preparation design)와 재료(LS vs PICN) 두 변수를 교차 비교한 근거가 부재하여 ingest.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro study (n=84 ETT molars, 6 groups) comparing post-fatigue fracture resistance of lithium disilicate (LS) vs polymer-infiltrated ceramic network (PICN) restorations across three preparation designs (occlusal veneer 1.2 mm, overlay 1.6 mm, adhesive crown 2 mm): overlay was a valid alternative to adhesive crown; LS significantly outperformed PICN on static fracture load (p=0.0002).

## 한줄요약

인비트로 연구(n=84 근관치료 대구치, 6군): 리튬 디실리케이트 (Lithium Disilicate, LS)와 고분자 침윤 세라믹 네트워크 (Polymer-Infiltrated Ceramic Network, PICN) 3가지 수복 형태(교합면 비니어·오버레이·접착 크라운) 피로 후 파절강도 비교 — 오버레이가 접착 크라운의 유효 대안이며, LS가 PICN보다 정적 파절강도 유의하게 높음(p=0.0002).

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Polymers (MDPI), 2022, 14, 5084
- **DOI**: 10.3390/polym14235084
- **Published**: November 23, 2022
- **Institution**: Department of Surgical Sciences, C.I.R. Dental School, University of Turin, Italy; IRCCS San Raffaele Hospital/Vita Salute University, Milan, Italy
- **Study Type**: In-vitro experimental (chewing simulation + static fracture)
- **Study Design**: 2×3 factorial (2 materials × 3 preparation designs), n=14 per group

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First direct 2×3 comparison of LS vs PICN across three preparation depths in ETT context
2. Demonstrates overlay (1.6 mm occlusal) is a statistically valid alternative to adhesive crown (2.0 mm) in ETT — no significant difference in fracture resistance
3. Confirms occlusal veneers (1.2 mm) inferior to adhesive crown (p=0.042) in ETT with high residual structure loss — caution warranted for ETT
4. LS significantly superior to PICN on static fracture resistance (p=0.0002), though PICN showed zero catastrophic failures during cyclic loading
5. Characterizes failure modes: non-catastrophic fractures more frequent with minimally invasive preparations

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Specimen preparation**:
- 84 human maxillary molars, mature apices, extracted for periodontal compromise (donors age 41–67, mean 53±13 years)
- All specimens endodontically treated (ProTaper Next X1-X2; NaOCl/EDTA irrigation)
- Standardized MOD cavities prepared; residual buccal/oral cusp wall thickness 1.5±0.2 mm
- Core build-up with bulk-fill composite (Grandioso X-Tra, Voco)

**Preparation designs (n=28 each, split into 2 material subgroups of n=14)**:
- Occlusal veneer: 1.2 mm occlusal reduction + interproximal boxes
- Overlay: 1.6 mm occlusal reduction + 1.5 mm shoulder at mid-crown
- Adhesive crown: 2.0 mm occlusal reduction + chamfer margin 1 mm above CEJ

**CAD/CAM materials**:
- LS: E-max CAD (Ivoclar Vivadent) — lithium disilicate glass ceramic, SiO₂ 60–65%
- PICN: Vita Enamic (Vita) — feldspar ceramic 86 wt% + polymer 14 wt% (UDMA/TEGDMA)

**Luting**: Futurabond U (universal adhesive) + Bifix QM (dual-cure cement); HF etching (5% LS 20s, PICN 60s)

**Fatigue test**: CS-4.4 chewing simulator, 50 N, 1,500,000 cycles, 1 Hz, distilled water, steatite balls (6 mm), silicon PDL simulation layer

**Static fracture test**: Universal testing machine (Instron), 6 mm steel sphere, 1 mm/min crosshead speed, load applied parallel to long axis

**SEM analysis**: Gold-sputter-coated replicas at 500× magnification, pre- and post-fatigue

**Statistics**: Shapiro-Wilk normality test, two-way ANOVA, Tukey post-hoc (α=0.05, STATA v12.0)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Fracture resistance (mean ± SD, Newtons)**:

| Preparation | PICN | LS |
|---|---|---|
| Occlusal veneer | 1806.6 ± 270.1 | 2029.5 ± 295.3 |
| Adhesive crown | 1555.4 ± 393.9 | 1859.2 ± 232.0 |
| Overlay | 1726.9 ± 301.1 | 2059.5 ± 308.0 |

- **Preparation design** significantly affected fracture resistance (p=0.0429)
- **CAD/CAM material** significantly affected fracture resistance (p=0.0002)
- **Interaction** between preparation and material: not significant (p=0.8218)
- Occlusal veneer < Adhesive crown (p=0.042); Occlusal veneer vs Overlay: NS (p=0.095); Overlay vs Adhesive crown: NS
- LS > PICN across all preparations (p=0.002)

**Events during cyclic loading**:
- Occlusal veneer: 1 chipping (LS), 3 chippings (PICN) — no debonding
- Overlay: 0 events (LS), 2 chippings (PICN)
- Adhesive crown: 1 debonding + 1 core fracture (LS), 2 debondings (PICN)

**Failure modes (non-catastrophic/catastrophic)**:
- LS occlusal veneer: 75% non-catastrophic, 25% catastrophic
- PICN occlusal veneer: 71.4% non-catastrophic, 28.6% catastrophic
- LS overlay: 71% non-catastrophic, 28% catastrophic
- PICN overlay: 65.5% non-catastrophic, 34.5% catastrophic
- LS adhesive crown: 50% non-catastrophic, 50% catastrophic
- PICN adhesive crown: 72.7% non-catastrophic, 27.3% catastrophic

**Clinical conclusion**: In ETT with high structural loss, overlay is a valid minimally-invasive alternative to adhesive full crown; occlusal veneers should be avoided. LS preferred for static load resistance; PICN favorable under physiological cyclic loads.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro study — no clinical correlation, artificial chewing simulation may not capture all oral environment variables
- Only maxillary molars — results may not generalize to mandibular molars or anterior teeth
- 50 N fatigue force (physiological); clinical bruxism forces not simulated
- Artificial aging limited to mechanical cycling (no thermal cycling, no chemical degradation)
- Relatively short observation analog (~5 years simulation); long-term in-vivo data needed
- No bonded full metal or zirconia crown comparator
- Sample from single institution (Turin); variation in operator skill not assessed for the preparation standardization

## 6. Related Work

- Frankenberger et al.: minimally invasive preparations less successful in ETT rehabilitation
- Skouridou et al.: similar fracture strength between full crown and overlays
- Vagropoulou et al. (SR+MA in vivo): comparable survival rate (<90%) for full crowns vs overlays at 5 years
- Facenda et al. (review): PICN adequate under physiological load (≤200 N); LS preferred at higher loads
- Homai et al.: zero PICN crown survival vs 70% LS survival after fatigue (contradictory — different methodology)
- Swain et al.: better PICN outcomes vs LS after mechanical cycling (crowns on composite dies)
- Dioguardi et al. (SR+MA): higher failure risk for ETT vs vital teeth with indirect bonded restorations

## 7. Glossary

- **ETT (Endodontically-Treated Tooth)**: 근관치료된 치아 — tooth after root canal therapy, compromised by structural loss and altered dentin properties
- **LS (Lithium Disilicate)**: 리튬 디실리케이트 — reinforced glass-ceramic (E-max CAD, Ivoclar Vivadent)
- **PICN (Polymer-Infiltrated Ceramic Network)**: 고분자 침윤 세라믹 네트워크 — hybrid material combining ceramic matrix (86 wt%) and polymer (14 wt%) (Vita Enamic)
- **Occlusal veneer**: 교합면 비니어 — minimal preparation (1.2 mm) covering only occlusal surface
- **Overlay**: 오버레이 — intermediate preparation (1.6 mm) covering cusps with proximal shoulder
- **Adhesive crown**: 접착 크라운 — full-coverage preparation (2.0 mm) with chamfer margin near CEJ
- **MOD cavity**: 근심-교합-원심 와동 — mesio-occlusal-distal cavity preparation
- **CEJ (Cemento-Enamel Junction)**: 법랑-백악 경계
- **Chewing simulator**: 저작 시뮬레이터 — device applying cyclic loads to mimic oral function
- **Non-catastrophic fracture**: 비재난성 파절 — fracture above CEJ, potentially repairable
- **Catastrophic fracture**: 재난성 파절 — fracture below CEJ, non-repairable
