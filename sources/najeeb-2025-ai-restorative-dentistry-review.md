## One-line Summary
BMC Oral Health 2025;25:592 — PRISMA-style literature review (63 studies, 2020–Jan 2025) of AI in restorative dentistry: caries detection (up to 95% accuracy), endodontics, dental restorations, tooth surface loss, shade determination, regenerative dentistry; flags data privacy, algorithmic bias, AI training programs in dental education.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: BMC Oral Health 2025;25:592
- DOI: 10.1186/s12903-025-05989-1
- Type: PRISMA-flow literature review (no meta-analysis)
- Window: 2020 to January 2025

## 2. Key Contributions
- Restorative-focused AI synthesis (caries, endo, restorations, tooth surface loss, shade, regenerative)
- Highlights specific accuracy benchmarks (e.g., 95% caries detection)
- Connects AI deployment to 3D printing for personalized restorations as emerging integration target

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Systematic search; English-language; 2020–Jan 2025
- 63 peer-reviewed studies included; PRISMA flow diagram (identification, screening, eligibility, inclusion)
- Inclusion criteria: AI applications in restorative dentistry, publication timeframe
- Focus: accuracy metrics and clinical relevance

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Caries detection: up to 95% accuracy
- Treatment planning: efficiency gains, reduced patient chair time
- Domains covered: caries detection, pedodontics, dental restorations, endodontics, tooth surface loss, tooth shade determination, regenerative dentistry
- Predictive analytics for personalised treatments highlighted

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Data privacy concerns
- Algorithmic bias
- Interpretability of AI decision-making (black-box problem)
- Need for standardised AI training programs in dental education
- Future direction: AI + 3D printing for personalised restorations; AI training programs for dental professionals

## 6. Related Work
- Cross-references contemporary 2023–2025 AI-in-dentistry literature
- Echoes Mallineni 2024, Lee 2025 cross-discipline framings but narrower restorative focus
