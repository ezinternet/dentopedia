## Why Ingested

상악 전치부 즉시식립 계획 시 절치관(Incisive Canal, IC) 해부학적 변이가 식립 위치에 미치는 영향을 정량화한 CBCT 연구. 기존 [[wiki/immediate-implant/panda-2018-cbct-incisive-canal-anterior-maxillary]]와 함께 IC 변이의 인종·성별 차이를 보완하며, 상악 전치부 즉시식립 시 IC 침범 리스크 평가 근거로 활용.

## One-line Summary

CBCT study (n=90 Mongoloids) quantifying maxillary incisive canal/foramen dimensions — IC mean length 16.33 mm, width 3.85 mm — and demonstrating gender and age effects on anterior maxillary bone thickness relevant to immediate anterior implant placement.

## 한줄요약

CBCT 횡단면 연구(n=90 몽골로이드)에서 상악 절치관 평균 길이 16.33mm, 폭 3.85mm를 정량화하고, 남성이 더 큰 IC 및 전방 골두께를 가지며 연령 증가 시 전방 골두께가 감소함을 확인.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Cross-sectional CBCT morphometric study
- **Journal**: PLOS ONE
- **Published**: February 13, 2015
- **DOI**: 10.1371/journal.pone.0117251
- **Sample**: 90 Mongoloid subjects (46 males, 44 females; Chinese and Malay), ages 15–75 years
- **Equipment**: i-CAT CBCT (0.3 mm voxel; Simplant v13 analysis software)
- **Institution**: Faculty of Dentistry, University of Malaya, Malaysia

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Provides ethnic-specific (Mongoloid/Asian) normative CBCT data for IC/IF dimensions — distinct from European/Caucasian norms commonly cited.
2. Demonstrates significant gender dimorphism: males have larger IC, larger IF, and thicker anterior maxillary bone.
3. Documents age-related anterior maxillary bone thinning (even in fully dentate subjects), increasing IC encroachment risk for older patients regardless of canal dimensions.
4. Characterizes canal shape (funnel, broader superiorly), course (predominantly slanted-curved with 1 channel at mid-portion), relevant to implant angulation planning.
5. Reports anterior maxillary bone thickness mean of 7.63 mm — a safety margin benchmark for palatally-angulated immediate implants.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Study design**: Retrospective CBCT image analysis (ethics-approved; IRB DF DP 1302/0013[P]).

**Image selection**: 100 consecutive CBCT scans; 6 excluded (pathology, poor quality); 4 edentulous excluded; n=90 analyzed.

**Measurements (sagittal + axial MPR)**:
- Incisive foramen (IF): labiopalatal (LP) and mesiodistal (MD) diameter
- Nasal foramen (NF): LP diameter
- IC: length (nasal to palatal opening) and mid-canal width
- Anterior maxillary bone thickness: mean of 3 levels (nasal spine, anteroinferior cortical, midpoint)
- IF location: distance from anteroinferior cortical plate
- Canal channels (axial): number of canaliculi at mid-portion
- IC course: straight vs curved; direction: vertical vs slanted (>10° from vertical)

**Statistics**: Independent t-test / Mann-Whitney U (gender); ANOVA / Kruskal-Wallis (age groups); chi-square (categorical). ICC 0.97, κ 0.92 (intra-rater reliability).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Parameter | Mean (SD) | Gender difference |
|---|---|---|
| IF labiopalatal diameter | 2.80 mm | M > F (p < 0.05) |
| IF mesiodistal diameter | 3.49 mm | M > F (p < 0.05) |
| NF labiopalatal diameter | 6.06 mm | NS |
| IC length | 16.33 mm | NS |
| IC width (mid-canal) | 3.85 mm | M > F (p < 0.05) |
| Anterior maxillary bone thickness | 7.63 mm | M > F (p < 0.05) |

- **Age effect**: Anterior maxillary bone thickness decreases with age (p < 0.05), but IC dimensions are age-stable.
- **Canal shape**: Majority funnel-shaped (broader superior opening at NF).
- **Canal direction**: Majority slanted (>10° from vertical).
- **Canal course**: Predominantly curved.
- **Channels at mid-portion**: Mostly single channel; some bifurcated.
- **Compared to non-Asian literature**: This population has a longer, more slanted-curved IC, narrower IF, suggesting higher lateral implant encroachment risk with palatal angulation.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-center, single-ethnicity (Mongoloid: Chinese + Malay) — generalizability to other populations limited.
- Retrospective convenience sample; indication for original CBCT not standardized to implant planning.
- No direct measurement of implant-to-IC proximity or clinical outcome correlation.
- 4 edentulous cases excluded — ridge resorption effects on IC/bone thickness not captured.
- Future: prospective studies correlating IC dimensions with post-immediate-implant osseointegration outcomes.

## 6. Related Work

- Mraiwa et al: anterior maxillary bone thickness measurement methodology (3-level averaging) — methodology adopted here.
- Song et al: IC direction/course classification criteria — adopted here.
- Liang et al: IF location measurement methodology — adopted here.
- Artzi et al: novel surgical cortico-cancellous block graft technique to bypass IC during implant placement.
- Two prior studies cited (refs 14, 15): females and younger patients at higher IC-proximity risk — partially contradicted here (age effect on bone, not canal dimensions).

## 7. Glossary

- **IC (Incisive Canal / 절치관)**: Bony channel connecting nasal floor to oral cavity; transmits nasopalatine nerve and artery.
- **IF (Incisive Foramen / 절치공)**: Inferior (palatal) opening of the IC, under incisive papilla.
- **NF (Nasal Foramen / 비강공)**: Superior (nasal floor) opening(s) of the IC; often bifurcated.
- **Nasopalatine nerve (비구개신경)**: Branch of V2; supplies anterior palatal mucosa; transected without lasting deficit.
- **Anterior maxillary bone thickness (전방 상악골 두께)**: Labial bone anterior to IC; critical safety margin for palatal implant angulation.
- **Mongoloid**: East/Southeast Asian ethnic classification used in this study's demographic grouping.
- **Funnel-shaped canal**: IC wider at superior (nasal) end, narrowing toward palatal IF.
