## One-line Summary

Systematic review (17 clinical trials, 39 AO treatments, 2010–2020): only 53.8% of treatments met the pain-control benchmark (VAS ≤4 by day 2, or ≥85% pain-free by day 7); successful options group into basic (intra-alveolar irrigation) and specific (Alveogyl, Neocones, SaliCept Patch, low-level laser, PRF); heterogeneity prevents ranking.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(임상시험 17편, 건성 발치와 (Alveolar Osteitis, AO) 치료 39개, 2010–2020): 통증조절 기준(2일째 VAS ≤4 또는 7일째 ≥85% 무통)을 충족한 치료는 53.8%뿐. 성공 옵션은 기본(발치와 내 세척)과 특정(Alveogyl, Neocones, SaliCept Patch, 저출력 레이저 (LLLT), 혈소판농축섬유소 (Platelet-Rich Fibrin, PRF))으로 분류; 이질성으로 순위화 불가.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Med Oral Patol Oral Cir Bucal. 2021 Nov 1;26(6):e691-702 (Oral Surgery section)
- **DOI**: 10.4317/medoral.24256 (Article No. 24256)
- **Institution**: Oral Medicine Dept, Facultad de Odontología, Universidad Nacional de Córdoba, Argentina
- **Type**: Systematic review (PRISMA), no meta-analysis (heterogeneity)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR focused specifically on **treatment** (pain control) of established AO, complementing prevention-focused reviews.
- Proposed an explicit, clinically-anchored success benchmark: VAS ≤4 by day 2 OR ≥85% pain-free by day 7.
- Classified the field into **basic** (intra-alveolar irrigation) vs **specific** procedures, framed by availability/advantages rather than a single winner.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Structured electronic + hand search of PubMed, Scopus, Cochrane Library, OpenGrey, Google Scholar, Jan 2010–Jul 2020; PRISMA.
- Inclusion: original English/Spanish clinical trials reporting VAS (0–10) pain control or % pain relief.
- 17 trials included; 39 distinct treatments analysed (RCT + non-RCT). Most populations were mandibular posterior / third molar.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **53.8%** of the 39 treatments met the proposed pain-control parameters.
- Pain-control successes grouped as: **basic** = intra-alveolar irrigation; **specific** = Alveogyl®, Neocones®, SaliCept Patch®, low-level laser, PRF.
- Representative trial signals (heterogeneous, not pooled):
  - PRF: faster/lower postop pain vs irrigation-only in several RCTs (Kamal 2020, Yüce 2019); but King 2018 found no difference vs Alveogyl.
  - Alveogyl vs ZOE: mixed — Supe 2018 favoured Alveogyl; Chaurasia 2017 favoured ZOE.
  - Low-level laser (diode/Er,Cr:YSGG): Rani 2015 — laser better than Alveogyl, diode best; Eshghpour 2015 — Alvogyl better early, red laser better late.
  - Hyaluronic acid + aminocaproic acid after curettage faster than Alveogyl (Dubovina 2016); Neocones better over time than Alveogyl/ZOE (Faizel 2014).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Treatments multiple, heterogeneous, hard to compare; no meta-analysis or ranking possible.
- Many single-trial interventions; variable populations and outcome timing.
- Old Alvogyl formulation issue applies (cf. Daly 2022).

## 7. Glossary

- **Alveolar osteitis (AO) / dry socket**: painful post-extraction complication; here studied as a condition to *treat*, not prevent.
- **Pain-control benchmark (this SR)**: VAS ≤4 by day 2, or ≥85% of patients pain-free by day 7.
- **Alveogyl® / Neocones® / SaliCept Patch®**: proprietary intrasocket medicated dressings.
- **PRF (Platelet-Rich Fibrin)**: autologous fibrin scaffold placed in the socket.
- **LLLT (Low-Level Laser Therapy)**: photobiomodulation for pain/healing.
