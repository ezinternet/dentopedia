## Why Ingested

임플란트 기능 부하 기간에 따른 골-임플란트 접촉률 (Bone-to-Implant Contact, BIC) 변화를 인간 회수체(retrieval)로 직접 검증한 연구가 필요했음. [[wiki/implants/romanos-2003-bone-implant-interface-loading-conditions-monkey]] (원숭이 동물 실험)에서 기능 부하 증가에 따라 BIC와 주위골 밀도가 향상됨을 보였으나, 이를 장기간 인간 임상 데이터로 확장하는 근거가 부재했음. 본 93개 Bicon 회수체 연구는 120일~18년 부하 범위에서 BIC/BAFO 모두 시간에 따라 증가함을 통계적으로 입증한다.

## One-line Summary

Human retrieval study of 93 plateau root form implants (Bicon, 120 days–~18 years in function) showing that BIC and BAFO increase progressively over functional loading time, independently of implant design and clinical variables.

## 한줄요약

인간 회수 임플란트 93개(Bicon, 기능 부하 120일~약 18년)를 조직형태계측으로 분석하여 BIC와 BAFO가 임플란트 디자인·임상 변수와 무관하게 기능 부하 시간에 따라 점진적으로 증가함을 입증한 후향적 회수 연구.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First large-scale human retrieval database (n=93, single manufacturer) to statistically model BIC and BAFO as a function of functional loading time across a wide range (120 days to ~18 years).
2. Demonstrated that osseointegration around plateau root form implants is **progressive** — not plateauing after initial healing but continuing to improve under long-term loading.
3. Showed that the loading-time effect on BIC/BAFO is **independent** of implant design variables (surface type, diameter, length) and clinical variables (jaw location, anterior/posterior region).
4. Provided histomorphometric evidence supporting the biomechanical adaptation hypothesis: bone remodels toward higher contact and occupancy under sustained functional load.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Implant type**: Plateau root form implants (Bicon LLC) — multiple surface types and dimensions included.
- **n**: 93 retrieved implants collected over ~15 years from human patients requiring prosthetic retreatment.
- **Loading range**: 120 days to approximately 18 years of functional loading.
- **Retrieval indication**: Prosthetic retreatment (not implant failure); implants were clinically osseointegrated at time of retrieval.
- **Histologic preparation**: Undecalcified sections using the Donath and Breuner (1982) Sage-Schliff sawing and grinding technique — standard protocol preserving implant-bone interface integrity.
- **Outcome measures**:
  - **BIC** (Bone-to-Implant Contact): percentage of implant surface in direct contact with bone.
  - **BAFO** (Bone Area Fraction Occupancy): percentage of available peri-implant area occupied by mineralized bone.
- **Statistical model**: BIC and BAFO analyzed as a function of multiple independent variables: surface type, diameter, length, jaw (maxilla vs mandible), region (anterior vs posterior), and functional loading time. Multivariable approach to isolate the time effect from confounders.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Both **BIC and BAFO increased over functional loading time** (hypothesis confirmed).
- The time-dependent increase was statistically significant and **independent of**:
  - Implant surface type
  - Implant diameter
  - Implant length
  - Jaw (maxilla vs mandible)
  - Region (anterior vs posterior)
- Loading range of 120 days to ~18 years provides the widest human retrieval window for plateau root form implants published at this time.
- Results are consistent with prior smaller retrieval studies (Coelho et al. 2009, 2010) and the Baldassarri et al. (2012) mechanical properties dataset from the same Bicon cohort.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **PDF depth**: Full body text, tables, and figures not available from the extracted PDF (Wiley HTML print only — abstract + references). Numerical BIC/BAFO values and regression coefficients could not be extracted.
- **Single manufacturer**: All implants are Bicon plateau root form; generalizability to screw-type implants not established by this study.
- **Retrieval bias**: Implants retrieved for prosthetic retreatment reasons represent surviving (i.e., osseointegrated) implants — failed implants are not represented. This overestimates population-level BIC/BAFO.
- **Retrospective design**: No randomization or controlled follow-up intervals; confounding by patient-level variables (bone quality, systemic health, occlusal forces) is possible despite multivariable adjustment.
- **Histomorphometry only**: Mechanical properties not measured here (see Baldassarri et al. 2012 for paired mechanical data from same cohort).

## 6. Related Work

- **Baldassarri et al. 2012** (J Biomed Mater Res B): Mechanical properties (elastic modulus, hardness) of human bone surrounding Bicon plateau root form implants retrieved after 0.3–24 years; paired dataset confirming bone maturation over time from the same cohort.
- **Coelho et al. 2009** (J Biomed Mater Res B): 30 Bicon plateau root form implants retrieved after 8–13 years; precursor histomorphometric analysis.
- **Coelho et al. 2010** (J Long Term Eff Med Implants): Plasma-sprayed hydroxyapatite-coated Bicon implants retrieved after 2 months to 13 years.
- **Romanos et al. 2003** (J Periodontol): Bone-implant interface changes under different loading conditions in the Macaca fascicularis monkey — animal-level evidence for loading-driven osseointegration progression.
- **Piattelli et al. 1998** (J Periodontol): 230 retrieved implants; early cross-sectional human retrieval reference.

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| BIC (Bone-to-Implant Contact, 골-임플란트 접촉률) | Percentage of implant surface length (or area) in direct apposition with mineralized bone on histologic sections |
| BAFO (Bone Area Fraction Occupancy, 골면적분율) | Percentage of the defined peri-implant area (threads/valleys) occupied by mineralized bone tissue |
| Plateau root form implant | Implant design with horizontal ledge (plateau) geometry rather than a standard V-thread screw profile; characteristic of Bicon implants |
| Sage-Schliff technique | Donath & Breuner (1982) undecalcified sawing-and-grinding histologic preparation method allowing intact implant-bone interface evaluation |
| Retrieval study (회수 연구) | Study design in which previously implanted devices are removed from humans and analyzed histologically/mechanically; provides direct human evidence not obtainable from imaging alone |
| Osseointegration (골유착) | Direct structural and functional connection between living bone and the implant surface, as defined by Branemark; assessed here by BIC |
| Histomorphometry (조직형태계측) | Quantitative measurement of tissue structure from histologic sections |
