## Why Ingested

구강노쇠 (Oral Frailty) 연구에서 신체노쇠 (Physical Frailty)의 대표 지표인 악력 (Grip Strength)이 사망·심혈관 결과의 독립 예측인자임을 대규모 코호트로 확립한 기초 근거로 ingested. [[geriatric-dentistry/dibello-2022-oral-frailty-indicators-adverse-health-outcomes]]에서 "신체노쇠(OR 1.67)" 연관 기술의 하부 근거가 바로 이 Leong 2015 계열의 악력 데이터이며, [[overviews/oral-frailty-geriatric-synthesis]]의 신체노쇠-구강노쇠 연결 논리를 물리적 증거로 뒷받침한다. 악력은 EWGSOP·Asian Working Group for Sarcopenia(AWGS) 기준의 핵심 측정값으로, 노년치의학 클리닉에서 간편하게 적용 가능한 신체노쇠 스크리닝 도구다.

## One-line Summary

Large prospective cohort (PURE, n=139,691, 17 countries, median 4.0-year follow-up) shows grip strength is inversely associated with all-cause mortality (HR 1.16 per 5 kg reduction), cardiovascular mortality (HR 1.17), MI (HR 1.07), and stroke (HR 1.09), and is a stronger predictor of mortality than systolic blood pressure.

## 한줄요약

대규모 전향적 코호트 (PURE, n=139,691, 17개국, 추적 중앙값 4.0년): 악력 (Grip Strength) 5 kg 감소당 전체사망 위험비 (Hazard Ratio, HR) 1.16, 심혈관사망 HR 1.17로 수축기혈압보다 강한 예측인자.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Largest multi-country prospective study (17 nations, low/middle/high income) linking grip strength to cardiovascular and all-cause mortality.
- Grip strength outperforms systolic blood pressure as a mortality predictor — a clinically actionable comparison for risk stratification.
- Risk association consistent across country-income strata (except cancer and respiratory hospital admission).
- Provides empirical basis for grip strength as a simple, inexpensive, universal physical-frailty screening tool.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: Large prospective cohort (the PURE study)
- **Enrollment**: January 2003 – December 2009; 142,861 enrolled; 139,691 with known vital status included
- **Countries**: 17 countries across low-, middle-, and high-income strata
- **Follow-up**: Median 4.0 years (IQR 2.9–5.1)
- **Exposure**: Grip strength measured by Jamar dynamometer
- **Outcomes**: All-cause mortality, cardiovascular mortality, non-cardiovascular mortality, MI, stroke, diabetes, cancer, pneumonia hospitalization, COPD hospitalization, fall injury, fracture
- **Analysis**: Cox proportional hazards with adjustment for age, sex, education, physical activity, diet, BMI, smoking, alcohol; competing-risk sub-distribution model (Fine & Gray)
- **n**: 139,691 participants; 3,379 (2%) deaths during follow-up

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | HR per 5 kg reduction in grip strength | 95% CI | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| All-cause mortality | 1.16 | 1.13–1.20 | <0.0001 |
| Cardiovascular mortality | 1.17 | 1.11–1.24 | <0.0001 |
| Non-cardiovascular mortality | 1.17 | 1.12–1.21 | <0.0001 |
| Myocardial infarction | 1.07 | 1.02–1.11 | 0.002 |
| Stroke | 1.09 | 1.05–1.15 | <0.0001 |

- Grip strength was a **stronger predictor** of all-cause and cardiovascular mortality than systolic blood pressure.
- No significant association with incident diabetes, pneumonia/COPD hospitalization, fall injury, or fracture.
- In high-income countries only: cancer risk inversely associated with grip strength (HR 0.916, 95% CI 0.880–0.953); not replicated in middle/low-income countries.
- Associations between grip strength and outcomes (except cancer and respiratory hospitalization) were similar across country-income strata.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Median follow-up of only 4.0 years — longer observation needed to capture full mortality curves and incident chronic disease.
- Abstract-only data available; precise methodology details (e.g., grip strength measurement protocol, covariate handling) inferred from reference set and prior PURE publications.
- Mechanistic pathway from grip strength to cardiovascular outcomes not established by this observational design.
- Further research needed to identify determinants of muscular strength and test whether improving strength reduces mortality and cardiovascular events.
- Full text paywalled — possible additional sub-analyses not captured here.

## 6. Related Work
- Dibello 2022: SR linking oral frailty indicators (including physical frailty) to 6 adverse outcomes in older adults — grip strength is the physical-frailty surrogate underlying "physical frailty OR 1.67" in that review.
- Zhu 2024 SR+MA: oral frailty associated with physical frailty (OR 1.67); grip strength is the standard physical-frailty measurement instrument underpinning that pooled estimate.
- Li 2024 SR+MA: prevalence of oral frailty in older adults — physical frailty comorbidity context.

## 7. Glossary
- **악력 (Grip Strength)**: 악력계(dynamometer)로 측정하는 손의 최대 쥐는 힘; 전신 근육량·근력의 대리지표로 신체노쇠(Physical Frailty) 스크리닝에 사용.
- **PURE**: Prospective Urban Rural Epidemiology — 17개국 저·중·고소득 국가를 포함한 맥마스터대학 주도의 대규모 전향적 코호트.
- **HR (Hazard Ratio, 위험비)**: Cox 비례위험모형에서 노출군과 비노출군 간 사건 발생 위험의 비율.
- **신체노쇠 (Physical Frailty)**: 근감소, 피로, 체중 감소, 보행속도 저하, 악력 감소를 포함하는 노년 취약 증후군; 구강노쇠(Oral Frailty)와 양방향 연관.
- **Jamar dynamometer**: 손 악력 측정 표준 기구; 클리닉 및 역학 연구 모두에서 사용.
