## One-line Summary
Comprehensive review: MRONJ 4-level prevention framework (primary→quaternary); primary prevention (pre-medication dental clearance) = most effective; secondary/tertiary for at-risk and established cases; quaternary addresses overmedicalisation.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Oral Health and Preventive Dentistry 23:403–417 (2025)
- DOI: 10.3290/j.ohpd.c_2169
- Design: Comprehensive narrative review; Google Scholar + PubMed

## 2. MRONJ — Causative Medications
- **Antiresorptives**: bisphosphonates (oral/IV), denosumab (RANK-L inhibitor)
- **Antiangiogenics**: bevacizumab, sunitinib
- **mTOR inhibitors**: everolimus, temsirolimus
- IV > oral route; oncologic dosing > osteoporosis dosing

## 3. Four-Level Prevention Framework

### Primary Prevention (MOST EFFECTIVE)
- Before starting antiresorptive/antiangiogenic medication:
  - Complete dental exam + panoramic radiograph
  - Extract hopeless teeth (socket healed before first drug dose, ideally ≥3 weeks)
  - Treat active caries, periodontal disease
  - Optimize oral hygiene instructions
  - Remove poorly fitting dentures

### Secondary Prevention (During medication therapy)
- Avoid elective invasive procedures where possible
- If surgery necessary: drug holiday (oral BP: 2 months before + after), specialist consult
- IV BP / denosumab: extreme caution — no consensus on drug holiday efficacy
- Non-surgical options first: endodontic therapy vs extraction, orthodontic vs surgical
- Antiseptic mouthwash (CHX 0.12%–0.2%) for any procedure

### Tertiary Prevention (Established MRONJ)
- Conservative: CHX rinse, antibiotics (amoxicillin/metronidazole), pain management
- Surgical debridement for Stage 2–3 resistant to conservative treatment
- HBO (hyperbaric oxygen) therapy: adjunct for refractory cases
- Sequestrectomy + primary closure for Stage 3
- Goal: symptomatic relief + prevent further deterioration

### Quaternary Prevention
- Reduce MRONJ incidence by addressing root causes (osteoporosis prevention: Ca/Vit D, exercise)
- Limit unnecessary antiresorptive prescriptions
- More research on alternative medications with lower MRONJ risk

## 4. Dental Specialist Role by Specialty
- **Oral Surgery**: extractions (minimize trauma; atraumatic technique)
- **Periodontics**: non-surgical preference; antibiotic coverage
- **Implantology**: implant placement contraindicated in active MRONJ; extreme caution in at-risk patients
- **Endodontics**: preferred over extraction when possible

## 5. Limitations
- Narrative review; no quantitative synthesis
- Drug holiday evidence remains debated (especially IV BP and denosumab)
