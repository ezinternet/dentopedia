## One-line Summary
Prospective clinical study (n=15) of BTX-A injected on the Yonsei triangle for gummy smile: significant decrease in gingival exposure of ~3.5 ± 1 mm at 2 weeks, peak effect maintained through month 3, full reversal back to baseline by months 5-6; 67% "very satisfied", 27% satisfied at 1 month.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Confirms BTX-A as a competent, risk-free, minimally invasive nonsurgical therapy for excessive gingival display
- Applies Yonsei triangle injection technique (Hwang et al.) in clinical practice
- Provides 6-month longitudinal follow-up with monthly measurements demonstrating reversibility
- Stratifies injection technique by gummy smile subtype (anterior, posterior, mixed)
- Quantifies patient satisfaction using a structured questionnaire at 1 month

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Population: 15 patients (10F, 5M), aged 18-40, gingival exposure >2 mm, no prior Botox, no neuromuscular disease
- Distribution: anterior gummy smile 53%, posterior 7%, mixed 40%
- Product: Botox (onabotulinumtoxinA, Allergan) 100 U vial diluted in 2.5 mL 0.9% saline → 4 U per 0.1 mL
- Injection sites (Yonsei triangle): Point 1 (lateral ala of nose), Point 2 (midpoint of nasolabial fold between ala and commissure), Point 3 (maxillary point at 1/4 distance between ala and tragus); 1 cm radius circles overlapping at the Yonsei point center
- Dose: 4 U per side at Yonsei point + 2 U at philtrum of upper lip (for anterior and mixed gummy smile); for posterior gummy smile, injection 1 cm lateral to Yonsei triangle (targeting zygomaticus major)
- Technique: direct perpendicular insertion with 30G insulin syringe, 1 mL; EMLA + ice for analgesia
- Evaluation: standardized photographs (4 ft, Canon 1300D) at day 3, 7, 15, then monthly through month 6; gingival display measured from gingival crest to lower border of upper lip on maximal smile
- Statistics: paired Student's t-test, SPSS v20

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
Mean gingival display on maximum smile (mm ± SD):

|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-op | 7.07 ± 1.28 | — | — |
| Day 3 | 6.20 ± 1.32 | 0.87 | <0.001 |
| Day 7 | 5.07 ± 0.96 | 2.00 | <0.001 |
| Day 15 | 3.40 ± 1.06 | 3.67 | <0.001 |
| Month 1 | 3.07 ± 1.03 | 4.00 | <0.001 |
| Month 2 | 3.07 ± 1.03 | 4.00 | <0.001 |
| Month 3 | 3.47 ± 1.46 | 3.60 | <0.001 |
| Month 4 | 5.00 ± 1.07 | 2.07 | <0.001 |
| Month 5 | 6.67 ± 1.29 | 0.40 | 0.028 |
| Month 6 | 6.93 ± 1.28 | 0.13 | 0.164 (NS) |

- Peak effect at months 1-2 (4 mm reduction)
- Effect lasts ~4 months; reversal essentially complete by month 6
- Patient satisfaction at 1 month: 67% very satisfied, 27% satisfied, 6% disappointed
- All patients willing to repeat treatment
- No adverse events reported

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small sample (n=15), single arm, no comparator
- Only one posterior gummy smile patient (7%) — protocol for posterior subtype underpowered
- Direct (palpation-guided) injection without EMG localization
- No randomization, no blinding; subjective photographic measurement
- Single center; Indian cohort

## 7. Glossary
- BTX-A: botulinum toxin type A
- Yonsei point/triangle: anatomic landmark at confluence of LLSAN, LLS, zygomaticus minor (1 cm lateral to ala, 3 cm from oral commissure)
- LLSAN: levator labii superioris alaeque nasi
- LLS: levator labii superioris
- Excessive gingival display: >2-3 mm gingiva exposed at full smile
