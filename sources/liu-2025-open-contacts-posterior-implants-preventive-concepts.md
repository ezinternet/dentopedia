## Why Ingested

This narrative review is the concepts/companion piece to the proximal-contact-loss (PCL) evidence already in the wiki — it synthesizes etiology, incidence, and consequences and then introduces the preventive **MM-DD occlusal adjustment** concept that the wiki's quantitative PCL pages do not cover. It reinforces and contextualizes [[food-impaction/kim-2025-factors-influencing-proximal-contact-loss]] (younger age, single-unit, mandibular, time>12mo risk factors) and the prevalence syntheses [[food-impaction/fathi-2022-prevalence-proximal-contact-loss-umbrella]] and [[food-impaction/sheba-2023-interproximal-contact-loss-systematic-meta]], adding a proactive *management* angle (occlusal-vector adjustment) to a literature otherwise dominated by prevalence/risk description.

## One-line Summary

Narrative review (56 studies + 148-case 25-yr private-practice retrospective) of open proximal contacts beside posterior implants; introduces the MM-DD occlusal-adjustment concept (remove mesial-of-mesial / distal-of-distal occlusal contacts to steer adjacent teeth toward the implant), reporting open-contact incidence 11.5% vs 34-66% conventional.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Synthesizes the multifactorial etiology of open proximal contacts adjacent to posterior implants: biomechanical disparity (ankylosed implant vs PDL-mediated tooth mobility), prosthetic contour, surgical positioning, occlusal force distribution, mesial drift.
2. Tabulates incidence (34-66%; SR range 17-66%) and risk factors: mandibular arch, single-unit restoration, time since restoration (esp. >12 mo), younger age, occlusal scheme, opposing-dentition type.
3. Documents consequences: food impaction (up to 86.7%; Wang et al. 78.6% where PCL present), gingival inflammation, proximal bone loss, reduced satisfaction.
4. Reviews five preventive/corrective domains (prosthetic design, surgical placement, occlusal management, corrective, biomechanical) and their limitations.
5. Introduces the **MM-DD occlusal adjustment technique** and reports preliminary 148-case retrospective + matched control data.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Literature search**: PubMed, Scopus, Google Scholar; Jan 2000-Mar 2024. After dedup 183 articles → 92 full-text → 56 included (PRISMA-style).
- **Retrospective clinical observations**: 25-yr private practice (1999-2024), 148 single-unit posterior implants (92 mandibular, 56 maxillary), mean age 51.6 ± 10.9 yr, ≥12-mo follow-up. Screw-retained n=87 / cement-retained n=61; zirconia n=82, PFM n=39, lithium disilicate n=27. Implants 3.5-6.0 mm × 8.5-13 mm, healed sites.
- **MM-DD technique**: remove occlusal contact from the *mesial occlusal surface of the mesial adjacent tooth* and the *distal occlusal surface of the distal adjacent tooth*, creating space for physiologic migration of adjacent teeth TOWARD the implant crown (rather than away), preserving contact. Occlusion checked with 42 µm articulating paper in MIP + excursions; implant crown set to primary contact at central fossa, secondary contact >1 mm inside marginal ridge.
- **Outcomes**: contact integrity (floss + 21-50 µm shim stock), standardized periapical marginal bone levels, VAS (0-10) for food impaction / discomfort / satisfaction.
- **Ethics**: IRB Kaohsiung Medical University Hospital KMUHIRB-E(I)-20250203.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **MM-DD group open-contact incidence**: 11.5% (17/148) vs historical 34-66%.
- **Matched conventional control (n=102)**: open contact 38.2% (39/102); food impaction in 76.9% of those cases.
- **Food impaction**: MM-DD 18.4% vs control higher; discomfort VAS 1.9 ± 1.4 (MM-DD) vs 4.2 ± 2.3 (control), p<0.01.
- **Subgroup (retrospective cohort)**: mandibular 15.2% vs maxillary 7.1%; single-unit 13.9% vs splinted 4.8%; <50 yr 16.7% vs older 9.3%; risk rises after 12 mo.
- **Open-contact cases vs intact**: VAS food impaction 4.9 ± 1.8 vs 1.5 ± 1.1; discomfort 4.2 ± 2.3 vs 1.6 ± 1.4 (p<0.01).
- **Material note**: zirconia restorations (higher fracture resistance, dimensional stability) may preserve contact better than metal-ceramic; resin-based materials more wear-prone.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- MM-DD evidence is **retrospective, single-operator, no RCT**; potential selection/measurement bias. Historical 34-66% comparator is not a concurrent control for the headline figure.
- Heterogeneous populations; patient-specific factors (occlusal scheme, parafunction, maintenance compliance) uncontrolled.
- Authors call for prospective RCTs with standardized outcomes, calibrated examiners, long follow-up, and FEA/biomechanical modeling. A 25-yr companion retrospective study is referenced.

## 7. Glossary

- **Open (proximal) contact / proximal contact loss (PCL)**: loss of the interproximal contact between an implant restoration and an adjacent natural tooth, opening a gap.
- **Mesial drift**: physiologic tendency of natural teeth to migrate mesially; greater in the mandible.
- **MM-DD technique**: mesial-mesial / distal-distal occlusal adjustment — selective removal of occlusal contacts to redirect adjacent-tooth migration toward the implant.
- **Ankylosis (implant)**: osseointegrated implant is rigidly fixed in bone, lacking PDL-mediated mobility, unlike natural teeth.
- **Shim stock**: thin (here 21-50 µm) metal foil used to check contact tightness.
