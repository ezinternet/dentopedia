## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Etiologic equation as a teaching device**: "Normal function + stimuli (events) > physiological tolerance → TMD symptoms." Both sides of the inequality matter; lowering tolerance (gender, genetics, malocclusion, systemic factors) can produce TMD even with normal function alone.

2. **Bruxism reframed**: ~70% of bruxism is CNS-mediated and not permanently resolvable; goal is damage control, not elimination. Only 20–30% attributable to occlusal interference + emotional stress.

3. **End-feel as a bedside differential**: Muscle disorder = soft end-feel (pain but slight further opening with passive stretch); joint disorder = hard end-feel. Lateral excursion to the contralateral side painful in joint disorders.

4. **Deviation vs deflection on opening path**:
   - Deviation (도중편향) = path deviates then returns to midline → may indicate disc displacement with reduction (DDwR).
   - Deflection (종점편향) = path deviates progressively, doesn't return → suggests disc displacement without reduction (DDwoR) or intra-articular adhesion.

5. **Diagnostic anesthetic blockade as both diagnostic and therapeutic**: 2% lidocaine *without* vasoconstrictor for trigger point injection (masseter/trapezius ~½ ampule; temporalis ~⅓ ampule); fan-shaped needling disrupts the trigger band.

6. **Bilateral manipulation loading test** (figure 4): thumbs on chin pushing down, four fingers under mandibular angle pushing up — pain on loading suggests intracapsular origin.

7. **Treatment philosophy**: definitive treatment (modify cause) vs supportive therapy (modify symptoms only). Examples — orthognathic surgery for asymmetry-driven TMD, posterior prosthetic restoration to achieve bilateral mastication.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Narrative clinical review structured as:
- I. Introduction (epidemiology — 75% have ≥1 sign, 33% ≥1 symptom; TMD ranked as 4th major oral disease)
- II. Diagnosis: etiology framework, then 7 exam steps (history, ROM, end-feel, opening path, palpation including referred pain vs trigger point, joint loading, diagnostic anesthesia, adjunctive imaging)
- III. Treatment:
  - 1. Masticatory muscle disorders — 4 subtypes (protective cocontraction, local muscle soreness, myofascial pain with trigger points, chronic centrally mediated myalgia)
  - 2. TMJ joint disorders — disc displacement, retrodiscitis, osteoarthritis; stabilizing splint as default
  - 3. Occlusal appliance therapy — hard resin, maxillary preferred; canine-guided occlusion; 2–7 day adjustments
  - 4. Arthrocentesis and lavage — two-needle superior joint space irrigation with saline; option to inject steroid or sodium hyaluronate; landmark = mid-tragocanthal line

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

[claude해석] This is a narrative review with no quantitative outcomes of its own. Cited epidemiology: 75% of population has ≥1 sign, 33% ≥1 symptom of TMD; myofascial pain accounts for ~50% of muscle-origin pain.

[합의수준] Treatment recommendations align with reversible-first hierarchy widely endorsed in TMD guidelines: medication → physical therapy → splint → arthrocentesis → arthroscopy/open surgery.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-author narrative perspective; no systematic literature review.
- No outcome data; relies on cited references and clinical experience.
- 2012 publication — predates the 2014 DC/TMD criteria revision and recent BMJ 2023 chronic TMD guideline; some terminology (e.g., "stabilizing appliance") and treatment positioning has since shifted (Singh 2024 Cochrane: strong evidence against irreversible occlusal interventions; Zhu 2020 BTX-A for myofascial pain).

## 6. Related Work

- Valesan 2021 SR+MA — TMD prevalence ~31%, painful TMD 11–13%.
- Zhang 2020 RCT-SR — occlusal splint vs no treatment / vs placebo splint.
- Singh 2024 Cochrane SR — occlusal interventions: low evidence overall, strong against irreversible work.
- IJOMS 2023 SR+MA — arthrocentesis MMO improvement +1.12 mm.
- Zhang 2024 SR — arthrocentesis + HA most evidence; PRP emerging.

## 7. Glossary

- **TMD (Temporomandibular Disorder, 측두하악장애)**: cluster of disorders involving preauricular/masticatory muscle pain, mandibular ROM limitation, and joint noise.
- **Protective cocontraction (보호성 상호수축)**: CNS-mediated muscle splinting after sudden structural change or pain; usually self-limited.
- **Trigger point (발통점)**: hyperirritable spot within a taut muscle band that produces referred pain when palpated.
- **Disc displacement with reduction (정복성 관절원판변위, DDwR)**: disc returns to normal position during opening, producing clicking.
- **Disc displacement without reduction (비정복성 관절원판변위, DDwoR)**: disc remains displaced, producing limited opening (closed lock).
- **Arthrocentesis and lavage (관절 천자 및 세정술)**: two-needle saline irrigation of the superior joint space, ± intra-articular steroid or sodium hyaluronate.
- **Bilateral manipulation (양수 수조작법)**: clinical loading maneuver to differentiate intracapsular from extracapsular pain.
