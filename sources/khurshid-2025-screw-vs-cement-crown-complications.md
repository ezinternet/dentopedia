## One-line Summary
Prospective study (100 patients, 12 months) comparing screw-retained vs cement-retained single implant crowns; screw loosening higher in screw-retained (18% vs 4%), marginal gap and cement washout higher in cement-retained.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Prospective clinical study
- **Journal**: Journal of Pharmaceutical and Bioallied Sciences 17:S3316 (2025)
- **DOI**: 10.4103/jpbs.jpbs_1359_25
- **Patients**: 100 (50 screw-retained, 50 cement-retained)
- **Follow-up**: 12 months

## 2. Key Contributions
- Direct head-to-head comparison of complication rates at 12 months
- Quantifies specific complication types with significance testing
- Supports differentiated decision-making based on complication risk profile

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: Prospective parallel-group study
- **n**: 100 patients; 50 SR (screw-retained), 50 CR (cement-retained) single implant crowns
- **Inclusion**: Single-tooth implant restoration, posterior region
- **Follow-up**: 12 months
- **Outcomes**: Screw loosening, marginal gap, cement washout, peri-implantitis, crown fracture

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Complication | Screw-Retained | Cement-Retained | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Screw loosening | 18% | 4% | 0.032 |
| Marginal gap | 8% | 20% | <0.05 |
| Cement washout | 2% | 14% | <0.05 |
| Peri-implantitis | 4% | 10% | 0.146 (NS) |
| Crown fracture | NS | NS | NS |

- Screw-retained: higher mechanical complications (screw loosening)
- Cement-retained: higher biological/marginal complications (cement washout, marginal gap)
- Peri-implantitis trend favored screw-retained but did not reach significance at 12 months

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Short follow-up (12 months) — longer data needed for peri-implantitis significance
- Single center, not blinded
- No stratification by operator experience or implant system

## 6. Related Work
- Hamed 2020 (SR): same directional finding — cement = biological; screw = mechanical
- Tomar 2025 (SR+MA): screw superior at 6 months MBL; long-term comparable

## 7. Glossary
- **Screw-retained (SR)**: Crown retained by access screw; retrievable; no cement
- **Cement-retained (CR)**: Crown cemented onto abutment; less retrievable; risk of cement excess
- **Marginal gap**: Space between crown margin and implant abutment interface
- **Cement washout**: Dissolution or displacement of cement from under the crown
