## Why Ingested

[[cracked-tooth/raj-2025-cracked-tooth-syndrome-diagnostic-dilemma]] frames the diagnostic uncertainty of cracked tooth syndrome qualitatively; this ex vivo STARD/QUADAS-2 study supplies the actual head-to-head diagnostic-accuracy numbers (sensitivity/specificity/PPV against a micro-CT gold standard) for the four visual tools clinicians reach for — quantifying exactly how unreliable transillumination, microscopy, macrophotography, and DIAGNOcam are. It also extends [[cracked-tooth/li-2021-cracked-tooth-syndrome-etiology-review]] by showing that 20% of teeth extracted on crack suspicion had no dentine-extending crack, reinforcing the overdiagnosis/overtreatment concern.

## One-line Summary

Ex vivo diagnostic-accuracy study (n=30 extracted teeth, micro-CT gold standard) comparing macrophotography, surgical microscope, fiber-optic transillumination and DIAGNOcam: transillumination most accurate (65.3%) and most sensitive (68.8%) but with poor specificity (53.6%), while high-mag microscope and macrophotography gave the highest specificity (92.9%) and PPV (~96-97%); no visual tool can definitively diagnose or locate cracks.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First STARD/QUADAS-2-guided head-to-head** diagnostic-accuracy comparison of four common visual crack-detection tools against a **micro-CT gold standard** — a reference far more accurate than the ground sections used in prior work.
2. Quantifies the **transillumination false-positive problem**: highest accuracy/sensitivity but specificity only 53.6% (would falsely flag ~47 of 100 crack-free teeth) → overtreatment risk.
3. Shows **high-magnification microscopy (21.25x) and macrophotography** give the highest specificity (92.9%) and PPV (96.7% / 96.4%) and perform robustly across crack-prevalence scenarios.
4. Demonstrates **clinical experience matters** — experienced dentists 64.6% vs inexperienced 56.8% accuracy (chi-square p=0.011).
5. Shows **no tool localizes cracks well** (~50%; only 11.1% spread between best and worst).
6. Notes **20% of teeth extracted on crack suspicion had no dentine-extending crack**, underscoring overdiagnosis.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Sample**: 30 teeth extracted due to suspected cracks; stored in formalin then physiological saline, kept hydrated; retention 3-9 months post-extraction.
- **Crack definition**: discontinuity in coronal tooth structure extending into dentin (enamel-confined cracks ignored). True positive = crack crossing the DEJ on micro-CT.
- **Gold standard**: micro-CT (Skyscan 1272, Bruker; 0.007 mm pixel, 100 kV, 100 uA, 0.11 mm Cu filter), assessed by a blinded endodontist.
- **Index tests** (13 images/tooth): 1 macrophotography, 2 DIAGNOcam (plain + transillumination; fluorescence hidden), 4 microscope (5.1x / 12.5x / 21.25x via CJ-Optik Flexion turret), 6 fiber-optic transillumination angles (Microlux 2, AdDent). Macrophotography/transillumination shot with Nikon D850 + 105 macro + 20 mm extension tube.
- **Setup**: tooth in silicone putty → endodontic sextant → phantom head on dental chair (simulating clinical positioning).
- **Examiners**: 4 (two senior >20 yr; two new graduates <5 yr), reviewing randomized PowerPoint images over two Zoom sessions 2 weeks apart with 10% overlap for intra-rater reliability.
- **Stats** (Jamovi): accuracy, sensitivity, specificity, PPV, NPV; Cohen's & Fleiss' kappa; chi-square for experience; PPV/NPV modeled across 0-100% prevalence.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Prevalence in sample**: 80% (24/30) had cracks crossing the DEJ; 20% (6/30) did not despite crack-suspicion extraction.

**Diagnostic accuracy (% , location as %):**

| Index test | Accuracy | Sensitivity | Specificity | PPV | NPV | Location |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Macrophotography | 60.6 | 51.9 | **92.9** | 96.4 | 34.2 | 51.0 |
| Microscope 5.1x (low) | 52.2 | 48.0 | 64.7 | 80.3 | 29.3 | 43.4 |
| Microscope 12.5x (med) | 55.1 | 50.0 | 79.2 | 91.8 | 25.3 | 44.4 |
| Microscope 21.25x (high) | 62.5 | 54.6 | **92.9** | **96.7** | 34.7 | 49.9 |
| DIAGNOcam | 64.4 | 65.4 | 60.7 | 86.1 | 32.1 | 51.5 |
| Transillumination | **65.3** | **68.8** | 53.6 | 83.5 | 33.3 | **54.6** |

**Reliability**: intra-rater kappa moderate-to-substantial (0.481-0.650; agreement 74-84%); inter-rater fair (Fleiss' kappa all four = 0.275; senior pair 0.306, new pair 0.252).

**Experience**: Examiner 4 (new grad) highest at 70.4%; Examiner 3 (new grad) lowest at 43.2%. Experienced mean 64.6% vs inexperienced 56.8% (chi-square 6.54, p=0.011; log OR 0.428, p=0.013).

**Prevalence modeling**: high-mag microscope and macrophotography perform well across both high- and low-prevalence; low-mag microscope worst. At 0 prevalence specificity=accuracy; at 100% prevalence sensitivity=accuracy.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Ex vivo / small sample** (n=30; only 6 crack-negative teeth) — wide confidence intervals (especially NPV and specificity).
- Images reviewed off a slideshow rather than live clinical examination; symptoms deliberately excluded (clinical appearance only).
- Authors call for studies on **combinations of diagnostic tools** and development of a clinically relevant diagnostic strategy; a separate study will examine the 6 crack-negative extracted teeth.
- Note that DIAGNOcam/transillumination have fixed light intensity — manufacturer adjustability suggested.

## 7. Glossary

- **STARD**: Standards for Reporting of Diagnostic Accuracy Studies.
- **QUADAS-2**: Quality Assessment of Diagnostic Accuracy Studies tool (risk-of-bias).
- **Micro-CT**: micro-computed tomography; 3D high-resolution imaging used here as the gold standard.
- **DEJ**: dentino-enamel junction; crack must cross it to count as a true positive.
- **Transillumination**: passing light through a tooth so a crack disrupts light transmission.
- **DIAGNOcam**: KaVo near-infrared (850 nm) dual-source transillumination device, primarily a caries tool.
- **PPV / NPV**: positive / negative predictive value (probability a positive/negative result is true; prevalence-dependent).
- **Sensitivity / Specificity**: proportion of true cracks correctly flagged / true non-cracks correctly cleared.
- **Craze lines**: superficial enamel cracks not extending into dentin (clinically insignificant; cause transillumination false positives).
