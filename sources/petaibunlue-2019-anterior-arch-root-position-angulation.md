## One-line Summary
CBCT cross-sectional study evaluating how anterior arch form combined with Kan-class SRP relates to root-to-bone angulation in the maxillary esthetic zone.

## 1. Document Information
- **Title:** Influence of the anterior arch shape and root position on root angulation in the maxillary esthetic area

- **Study type:** cross-sectional

## 2. Key Contributions
Demonstrates that SRP alone is insufficient — combining arch form gives a more predictive model of root-to-bone angulation, refining IIP planning.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Retrospective CBCT review of 98 anterior arches. SRP and AA arch classification; mid-sagittal root vs bone angulation measured.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
SRP carries a larger effect than arch form alone, but combined model (SRP × arch form) outperforms either variable individually (p<0.05).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
Cross-sectional; single population; arch classification still operator-judged.

## 7. Glossary
- **IIP:** Immediate Implant Placement — placement at the time of tooth extraction.
- **IIPP:** Immediate Implant Placement and Provisionalization — IIP combined with same-visit provisional restoration.
- **SRP:** Sagittal Root Position — Kan I/II/III/IV classification of root-to-bone relationship.
- **CBCT:** Cone-Beam Computed Tomography.
- **PES/WES:** Pink/White Esthetic Score — esthetic outcome indices for anterior implants.
- **EMBL:** Early Marginal Bone Loss.
