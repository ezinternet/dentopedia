## One-line Summary
Contemp Clin Dent 2015;6(3):409 (Narayana Dental College, India) — in vitro study comparing 12° vs 20° total occlusal convergence (TOC) on retention of Ni-Cr single crowns and 3/6/9-unit FPDs across canine, premolar, molar dies; 12° retains better for canine (101.7N vs 59.7N) and molar (100.8N vs 81.2N), NS for premolar (P > 0.05); retention scales with unit number except 3 vs 6 units at 20°.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Contemporary Clinical Dentistry 2015;6(3):409
- Type: In vitro experimental
- Affiliation: Narayana Dental College and Hospital, Nellore, India

## 2. Key Contributions
- Quantifies convergence × tooth type × unit count interaction in FPD retention
- Provides per-tooth dislodgement force tables
- Highlights canine and molar as TOC-sensitive vs premolar as TOC-insensitive

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Standardized milled dies at 12° and 20° convergence simulating canine, premolar, molar
- n = 55 per convergence group (single + multi-unit FPDs)
- Cast Ni-Cr alloy crowns/FPDs cemented with zinc phosphate, tensile dislodgement via Instron
- Statistics: Mann-Whitney test

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Tooth | 12° (Mean ± SD, N) | 20° (Mean ± SD, N) | P |
|---|---|---|---|
| Canine | 101.7 ± 7.5 | 59.7 ± 4.8 | <0.01 ** |
| Premolar | 111.3 ± 9.9 | 93.0 ± 13.6 | 0.12 NS |
| Molar | 100.8 ± 2.6 | 81.2 ± 2.6 | <0.01 ** |

- Retention increases with FPD unit count (3 → 6 → 9) significantly EXCEPT 3 vs 6 units in 20° group (P > 0.05)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- In vitro only; zinc phosphate cement (not contemporary adhesive)
- Ni-Cr cast crowns, not modern monolithic ceramics
- No fatigue/thermocycling

## 6. Related Work
- Shillingburg textbook recommendation 6° taper baseline cited
- Clinically achievable 14.3° mean per prior surveys cited
