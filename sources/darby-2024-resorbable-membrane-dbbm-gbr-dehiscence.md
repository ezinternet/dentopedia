## Why Ingested

Direct comparison of resorbable collagen membrane + DBBM vs DBBM alone in standardized 5×3 mm buccal dehiscence defects at early implant placement — quantifies the membrane's additive value for new bone percentage in anterior maxilla. Provides unique translational evidence using the maxillary second incisor canine model, which more closely resembles clinical anterior implant sites than the commonly used posterior mandible model. Extends [[bone-regeneration/wessing-2018-gbr-collagen-membranes-particulate-graft-sr-ma]] with site-specific histological data.

## One-line Summary

Animal study (9 dogs, split-mouth): resorbable collagen membrane over DBBM in buccal dehiscence defects tended to retain more bone substitute (%BS, p=0.052) but did not increase new bone formation compared to DBBM alone.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: The effect of a barrier membrane on the incorporation of deproteinized bovine bone mineral (DBBM) in experimental defects at the time of early implant placement. A preclinical study
- **Authors**: Rebecca Ellis, Stephen Chen, Fernando Mª Muñoz Guzón, Ivan Darby
- **Year**: 2024
- **Journal**: Clinical Oral Investigations
- **DOI**: 10.1007/s00784-024-05748-6
- **Study type**: Animal study (randomized experimental, split-mouth, dog model)
- **Institution**: Melbourne Dental School, University of Melbourne; Faculty of Veterinary Medicine, Lugo, Spain

## 2. Key Contributions

- Utilized the novel maxillary second incisor (I2) canine model — unique for its resemblance to human anterior single-tooth implant sites with adjacent natural teeth.
- Split-mouth design: test side (DBBM + resorbable collagen membrane) vs control (DBBM alone) in 5×3 mm standardized buccal dehiscence defects.
- Primary finding: membrane use had **no significant effect** on new bone formation (B, %B) or first bone-to-implant contact (fBIC) at 6 weeks.
- Membrane tended to retain more bone substitute (%BS, p=0.052) and mineralized tissue (%MT, p=0.052) at central sections.
- Lateral sections showed significantly more bone ingrowth than central sections (B, %B, MT), confirming bone regeneration proceeds primarily from lateral walls.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Model**: 9 healthy adult female Mongrel Hound dogs, mean age 16.5 months
- **Design**: Randomized split-mouth; maxillary second incisor (I2) bilateral sites
- **Timeline**:
  - Week 0: Extraction of I2 bilaterally
  - Week 6: Implant placement + creation of 5×3 mm standardized buccal dehiscence defect
  - Week 12 (total): Euthanasia + histomorphometric analysis
- **Implant**: Early (type 2) implant placement after 6-week healing
- **Treatment groups**: Test = DBBM + resorbable collagen membrane (Bio-Gide equivalent); Control = DBBM alone (no membrane)
- **Outcome measures**:
  - First bone-to-implant contact (fBIC)
  - Buccal bone thickness at 1 mm increments from implant shoulder
  - Regenerated area (RA)
  - New bone (B, %B), bone substitute (BS, %BS), mineralized tissue (MT, %MT)
  - Analysis at central and lateral histological sections
- **Statistics**: SigmaPlot; split-mouth paired analysis

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Test (membrane+DBBM) | Control (DBBM alone) | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| fBIC | Similar | Similar | NS |
| Buccal bone thickness | Similar | Similar | NS |
| RA (regenerated area) | Similar | Similar | NS |
| B (new bone area) | Similar | Similar | NS |
| %B (new bone %) | Similar | Similar | NS |
| %BS (bone substitute %) | Higher (central) | Lower (central) | p=0.052 |
| %MT (mineralized tissue %) | Higher (central) | Lower (central) | p=0.052 |

- **Lateral vs central sections**: Significantly more B, %B, and MT at lateral sections than central sections (p<0.05) — demonstrates that bone ingrowth comes primarily from lateral walls of the defect, not the bone substitute-filled centre
- **Membrane effect**: Protective of bone substitute material volume, but does not accelerate or increase new bone formation at 6 weeks

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Short observation period (6 weeks after augmentation); longer healing may show membrane benefit
- Only one animal model and one defect geometry (5×3 mm); other sizes may yield different results
- No non-grafted control (DBBM vs membrane vs no graft triangle not fully tested)
- Heterogeneous membrane integration rates not measured
- Translational gap: dogs heal faster and have different bone density/vascularity from humans

## 6. Related Work

- Wessing et al. (2018): SR+MA showing membranes improve GBR outcomes — this study's DBBM-specific findings refine that general conclusion by showing no new-bone benefit in 6-week dehiscence defects
- Circumferential peri-implant defect studies (porcine, rodent models): more bone regeneration at centre when membranes used — contrasting this study's central-section findings
- Mandibular dehiscence studies in dogs: some showed greater regenerated area with membrane, some did not — this study adds anterior maxilla data

## 7. Glossary

- **GBR (Guided Bone Regeneration)**: 골유도재생술 — technique using barrier membrane to exclude non-osteogenic cells and permit bone ingrowth
- **DBBM (Deproteinized Bovine Bone Mineral)**: 탈단백 우골 — slowly resorbing xenograft; Bio-Oss
- **Dehiscence defect**: 열개 결손 — bony defect where implant surface is exposed on buccal aspect, lacking bone cover
- **fBIC (First Bone-to-Implant Contact)**: 최초 골-임플란트 접촉점 — most coronal point where bone contacts the implant surface
- **Split-mouth design**: 분구강 설계 — each animal serves as its own control (one side = test, contralateral = control)
- **Maxillary I2 model**: 상악 측절치 모델 — novel canine model mimicking human anterior single-tooth implant site
- **RA (Regenerated Area)**: 재생 면적 — total area within the defect outline at histology
- **MT (Mineralized Tissue)**: 광화조직 — includes both new bone and residual bone substitute
