## One-line Summary
JPIS 2026;56(1):31-38: Cross-sectional (n=142 implants, 101 patients, >10yr): PCL rate 53.7%; mesial 65.6% > distal 28.3%; molar position + poor periodontal status of adjacent tooth = significant risk factors.

## 1. Key Results
- n=142 implants, 188 adjacent teeth, 101 patients; all >10 years function (Pusan National University)
- Overall PCL rate 53.7% — more than half of long-term implants
- Mesial PCL 65.6% >> distal PCL 28.3%
- Significant risk factors (P<0.05): molar region placement; adjacent tooth plaque, mobility, BOP, pocket depth ≥4mm
- No implant-related factors significantly associated after multivariable analysis

## 2. Clinical Implications
- PCL is common in long-term implants — must be monitored at maintenance visits
- Periodontal condition of adjacent teeth is a key modifiable risk factor → periodontal treatment paramount
- Molar implants are higher risk for PCL — requires vigilant follow-up
- Mesial contacts more prone to loss than distal — relevant for prosthetic design
