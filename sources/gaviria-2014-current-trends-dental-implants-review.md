## One-line Summary

Narrative review (2014) tracing dental implant history from ancient civilizations to modern titanium endosseous implants, covering macro/micro design parameters, surface modification strategies (HA coating, sandblasting, acid-etching, anodization), and emerging trends (drug delivery, growth factors, biomimetic surfaces).

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Concise historical timeline from ancient (600 AD Mayan mandible fragment) through Brånemark's 1957 osseointegration discovery to modern era
2. Systematic classification of implant types: subperiosteal, blade, ramus frame, and endosseous (focus)
3. Organized framework of design parameters: macro features (threads, body shape), implant-abutment connection (internal vs external hex), size/diameter
4. Comprehensive review of surface modification approaches: physical (sandblasting, grit-blasting), chemical (acid-etching, HF treatment, anodization), biochemical (HA coating, growth factor loading)
5. Emerging trends: drug delivery from implant surfaces, biomimetic coatings, surface functionalization with BMP/VEGF/RGD peptides

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Narrative (invited) review; no systematic search protocol or PRISMA
- Covers basic science, animal studies, and clinical evidence without formal quality grading
- Organized thematically: history → implant types → design requirements → surface modification → future directions

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Historical milestones
- 600 AD: Mayan mandible fragment with carved shell implants — first known dental implants
- 1948: Goldberg & Gershkoff — first successful subperiosteal implant
- 1957: Per-Ingvar Brånemark discovers osseointegration (bone grows in direct contact with Ti without rejection)
- 1965: First Ti dental implants placed in human patient; served >40 years
- 1982: FDA approval of Ti dental implants; Toronto Conference on Osseointegration
- 1992: Start of modern ceramic surface development

### Implant design parameters
- **Body shape**: tapered vs cylindrical; conical implants show higher primary stability but cylindrical show higher survival (canine study: 95.5% threaded vs 75.4% smooth)
- **Thread design**: pitch, depth, and helix angle affect bone-implant contact and stress distribution
- **Diameter**: standard (3.75–4.0 mm), narrow (<3.5 mm), wide (>4.5 mm); wider diameter increases bone contact area
- **Length**: affects primary stability; longer implants preferred in low-density bone (D3/D4)
- **Abutment connection**: external hex (original) → internal hex (greater stability, lower micromovement)

### Surface modification
- **Sandblasting/grit-blasting**: Ra 1–2 µm; increases surface area; improves mechanical interlocking
- **Acid-etching (SLA)**: HF or HCl/H2SO4 creates micropits; promotes osteoblast attachment; SLA surface is gold standard for rough surface
- **Anodization**: TiO2 oxide layer formation; can increase oxide thickness; improves corrosion resistance
- **HA coating**: plasma spray (25–50 µm); enhances early osseointegration but long-term stability concerns (coating delamination)
- **Growth factor delivery**: BMP-2, BMP-7, VEGF, FGF loaded onto implant surfaces; promote osteogenesis and angiogenesis
- **RGD peptides**: cell-adhesion sequence promotes osteoblast attachment
- **Antibacterial coatings**: Ag nanoparticles, chlorhexidine — emerging to reduce peri-implantitis risk

### Clinical benchmarks
- ~450,000 osseointegrated implants placed/year (2014 estimate); ~95% success rate for single-tooth replacement
- 69% of adults aged 35–44 have lost ≥1 permanent tooth; 26% of adults >74 have lost all teeth

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative review only — no meta-analysis, no pooled data, no quality assessment of included studies
- Many surface modification outcomes cited from animal/in vitro studies without robust clinical validation
- Future directions identified: antimicrobial implant surfaces, zirconia implants, CAD/CAM-guided surgery, drug-eluting implants with controlled release kinetics, biomimetic hierarchical surfaces

## 7. Glossary

- **골유착 (Osseointegration)**: direct structural and functional connection between living bone and implant surface under load — coined by Brånemark
- **골내 임플란트 (Endosseous implant)**: implant placed within bone (most common type today)
- **골막하 임플란트 (Subperiosteal implant)**: implant resting on bone surface under periosteum — mostly historical
- **SLA (Sandblasted Large-grit Acid-etched)**: surface treatment creating micro-rough texture; current clinical standard
- **HA (Hydroxyapatite, HA)**: calcium phosphate ceramic coating; chemically similar to bone mineral; enhances early osseointegration
- **BMP (Bone Morphogenetic Protein, BMP)**: growth factor family promoting osteogenesis; used in surface functionalization
- **RGD 펩타이드 (RGD peptide)**: Arg-Gly-Asp amino acid sequence; promotes osteoblast cell adhesion to implant surface
- **어버트먼트 (Abutment)**: component connecting implant body to prosthetic crown; internal/external hex connection
- **Ra (표면 거칠기, Surface Roughness Ra)**: arithmetic mean surface deviation; implant surfaces typically Ra 1–2 µm
