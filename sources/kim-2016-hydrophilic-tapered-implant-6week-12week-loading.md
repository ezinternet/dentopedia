## One-line Summary
RCT (30 patients, 41 maxillary implants): 6-week loading vs 12-week loading — same 97.56% success, same MBL; bone quality in 6-week group correlated with stability; 6-week loading acceptable if bone quality considered.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Journal of Advanced Prosthodontics 8(5):396-403 (2016)
- DOI: 10.4047/jap.2016.8.5.396
- Design: Prospective randomized clinical trial

## 2. Key Results
- Patients: 30, Implants: 41 (maxillary posterior)
- Group 1 (12 weeks, control): 11 patients, 15 implants
- Group 2 (6 weeks, early loading): 19 patients, 26 implants
- Survival: 100% (all survived)
- Success rate: **97.56%** (both groups)
- MBL: <1mm after 1 year in all but 1 implant (both groups equivalent)
- ISQ not correlated with: age, healing period, insertion torque, diameter, location
- **Only bone quality in Group 2 correlated with stability** (p<0.05)

## 3. Clinical Implication
- Hydrophilic tapered surface allows 6-week loading in maxillary posterior area
- BUT bone quality must be carefully assessed before 6-week loading
- Standard 12-week loading remains safe backup if bone quality questionable

## 4. Limitations
- Small sample (41 implants)
- Short observation (12 months post-restoration)
- Single center
