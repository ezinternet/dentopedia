## Why Ingested

Part of a focused collection on suture techniques and primary wound closure after impacted mandibular third molar surgery. This is the largest single-trial suture-vs-sutureless comparison in the cluster (n=101) and is the one that isolates a *trismus* benefit for the sutureless arm, sharpening the picture from [[suture-wound-closure/takadoum-2022-sutureless-socket-technique-third-molars]] (no difference on any outcome) and [[suture-wound-closure/kumar-2022-suture-versus-sutureless-third-molar-impactions]] (broad pain/swelling/trismus benefit).

## One-line Summary
Prospective RCT (n=101) of primary silk-suture closure vs suture-less closure after impacted mandibular third molar removal; pain, swelling, and lingual nerve function were comparable, but the suture-less group had significantly less trismus and fewer delayed-healing cases.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Large (n=101) prospective RCT directly comparing primary closure with silk sutures (Group A) against suture-less closure (Group B).
- Isolates a specific benefit of the suture-less technique — reduced trismus and fewer delayed-healing cases — while showing pain, swelling, and lingual nerve function are equivalent.
- Adds to the suture-vs-sutureless evidence base with lingual nerve sensation as an explicit secondary outcome.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: Prospective randomized clinical trial; simple (lottery-based) randomization to Group A (primary closure, silk sutures) or Group B (suture-less).
- **Setting**: Department of Oral & Maxillofacial Surgery, Goa Dental College & Hospital, India.
- **Inclusion**: Age 17–55, ASA I, asymptomatic impacted third molars of moderately difficult impaction (Pederson difficulty index 5–7).
- **Exclusion**: ASA II–IV; known/suspected anesthetic allergy; immunocompromised; medications affecting healing; pregnant/lactating; bleeding disorders.
- **Primary outcomes**: pain intensity, facial swelling, trismus.
- **Secondary outcomes**: lingual nerve sensation, postoperative complications.
- **Statistics**: non-parametric tests + repeated-measures ANOVA; significance P < 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Among 101 participants, both techniques showed **similar** outcomes for pain, swelling, and lingual nerve function.
- Suture-less closure → **significantly less trismus** and **fewer cases of delayed wound healing**.
- Conclusion: suture-less method may reduce trismus vs multiple sutures; pain, swelling, and lingual nerve function are comparable.

## 7. Glossary
- **Suture-less closure**: flap repositioned and held by cheek pressure without stitches; socket heals by secondary intention.
- **Trismus**: restricted mouth opening from masticatory muscle inflammation/spasm.
- **Pederson difficulty index**: scoring of third molar surgical difficulty (depth, angulation, ramus relationship).
- **ASA classification**: American Society of Anesthesiologists physical status grading.
- **Lingual nerve sensation**: tested as a marker of nerve injury risk.
