## One-line Summary
Systematic review of 12 osseodensification (OD) studies through Nov 2018 — average increase in insertion torque, BIC and BAF vs conventional drilling, but evidence is predominantly animal/histologic.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Systematic review (qualitative analysis, no meta-analysis)
- **Journal**: Journal of Oral Biology and Craniofacial Research, 10 (2020) 375–380
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.jobcr.2019.10.002
- **Sample**: 12 articles included from a database of 132 — 8 animal histologic studies, 2 human clinical, 1 case series, 1 case report
- **Primary Question**: What is the effect of osseodensification on primary implant stability in clinical and histologic evaluation?

## 2. Key Contributions
- First systematic review focused exclusively on osseodensification (Densah bur) technique developed by Huwais in 2015.
- 10/12 included studies measured insertion torque; 7/12 measured BIC; 6/12 measured BAF.
- Quality assessment via ARRIVE guidelines: 6/8 animal studies had a high score.
- Reports an **average increase** in insertion torque, BIC and BAF for OD vs conventional drilling — direction of effect, no pooled estimate.
- Honest stance: extrapolation to long-term clinical success "cannot be ascertained until further evidence becomes available."

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
PRISMA-compliant search of PubMed-Medline, Embase, and Google Scholar from 2010 to November 2018 with no language restriction. PICO framed around effect of OD on primary stability of implants placed in humans on clinical and histologic basis. Boolean keyword combinations included "osseointegration", "implant stability", "implant primary stability", "osseodensification", "densah", "bone density", "insertion torque", "removal torque". Two independent reviewers screened titles/abstracts and then full-text. No language restriction was applied.

The review is intentionally **qualitative** — given heterogeneity of designs (animal vs human, histologic vs torque-only), no meta-analysis was attempted. Quality of animal studies was assessed against the ARRIVE checklist. The Densah bur protocol is described mechanistically: 4+ lands with negative rake angle so edges are non-cutting; clockwise (cutting) mode reaches the desired depth, counterclockwise (densifying) mode burnishes a compacted autograft layer along the osteotomy wall and apex, intended to raise BIC and torque.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- 132 → 12 included studies after screening.
- 8 animal histologic, 2 human clinical, 1 case series, 1 case report.
- 10/12 insertion torque, 7/12 BIC, 6/12 BAF.
- Direction of effect: OD > conventional for torque, BIC, BAF.
- Pooled numerical effect sizes / confidence intervals not provided (review is qualitative — no meta-analysis).
- 6/8 animal studies rated "high score" on ARRIVE quality.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Body of evidence dominated by **animal histologic** studies; only 2 human clinical studies — generalizability to long-term clinical outcomes is weak.
- Qualitative synthesis only — no pooled effect sizes, no I², no heterogeneity quantification.
- ARRIVE was applied to animal studies but no comparable risk-of-bias instrument for human studies is detailed in the extracted text.
- Authors themselves note long-term clinical success cannot be inferred from current evidence.
- Industry/commercial bias of OD literature (Densah is a proprietary system) is not formally addressed in extracted text.

## 7. Glossary
- **Osseodensification (OD)**: site preparation using specially designed multi-flute burs (Densah) run counterclockwise to compact bone laterally and apically.
- **BIC (Bone-to-Implant Contact)**: histomorphometric % of implant surface in direct contact with bone.
- **BAF (Bone Area Fraction occupancy)**: % of bone occupying the area between implant threads.
- **ARRIVE guidelines**: reporting checklist for animal in-vivo experiments.
- **PRISMA**: reporting guidance for systematic reviews and meta-analyses.
