## One-line Summary
Foundational 12-month prospective clinical and radiographic study (Schropp et al. 2003; n=46 patients, single premolar/molar extractions) using study casts, linear radiographic analysis, and subtraction radiography — established that **major dimensional changes of the extraction site occur within the first 12 months after extraction**, with most resorption (~two-thirds of horizontal ridge loss) happening in the first three months.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: International Journal of Periodontics & Restorative Dentistry, 2003;23(4):313–323
- **PubMed ID (PMID)**: 12956475
- **Publication date**: August 2003
- **Study design**: Prospective clinical and radiographic cohort, 12-month follow-up
- **Affiliation**: Department of Periodontology and Oral Gerontology, Royal Dental College, Aarhus University, Denmark

## 2. Key Contributions
- Most-cited clinical reference establishing the **magnitude and time-course of post-extraction ridge resorption in humans**.
- Demonstrates ~50% reduction of buccolingual width and ~two-thirds of all resorption within the first three months.
- Combines three measurement modalities (study casts, linear radiographs, subtraction radiography) — methodological model for later post-extraction studies.
- Provides the comparator baseline against which alveolar ridge preservation (ARP) trials have been measured for two decades.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Prospective cohort of 46 patients requiring extraction of a single premolar or molar tooth (anterior premolar through second molar) without subsequent socket grafting.
- Measurements at extraction, 3, 6, and 12 months:
  - Study casts: bucco-lingual width and contour at fixed reference points.
  - Standardised periapical radiographs and panoramic radiographs.
  - Digital subtraction radiography for fine bone density change detection.
- No bone graft or socket-management intervention performed (unassisted healing).
- Analysis: paired comparisons over the 12-month follow-up.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Buccolingual ridge width**: reduced approximately 50% during the 12-month observation period (~12 mm baseline → ~5.9 mm at 12 months in original reporting).
- **Two-thirds of total horizontal resorption occurred within the first 3 months** after extraction.
- Vertical changes (mid-buccal): less pronounced than horizontal but still measurable.
- Subtraction radiography demonstrated active bone formation inside the socket peaking in the first months.
- Soft-tissue contour mirrored the underlying bony resorption with measurable buccal collapse.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Mixed tooth types (premolars and molars) reported together — site-specific differences blurred.
- Cast-based clinical measurement subject to mucosal thickness variation.
- No CBCT (not yet routine in 2003) — three-dimensional ridge mapping unavailable.
- Sample of 46 patients limits subgroup analysis (smoking, periodontal phenotype, gender).

## 6. Related Work
- Couso-Queiruga 2021 — SR/MA confirming Schropp's magnitude in modern pooled estimates.
- Van der Weijden 2009 — SR confirming pattern of post-extraction loss.
- Araújo & Lindhe 2005 — dog model linking buccal bundle bone resorption to ridge loss.
- Cardaropoli 2003 — histologic dynamics of bone formation in extraction sockets.
- Trombelli 2008 — modeling/remodeling of human extraction sockets.

## 7. Glossary
- **Unassisted socket healing**: spontaneous extraction socket repair without grafting or membrane.
- **Buccolingual ridge width**: horizontal alveolar dimension measured from buccal to lingual/palatal cortex.
- **Subtraction radiography**: digital image-subtraction technique comparing standardised radiographs to detect small density changes.
- **Bundle bone**: portion of buccal alveolar wall continuous with PDL; resorbs after extraction (described later in animal models).
