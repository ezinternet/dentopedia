## Why Ingested

Specifically addresses PRF socket preservation in heavy smokers (≥10 cigarettes/day) — smoking is a key modifier in extraction socket healing that most ARP trials explicitly exclude. Provides four-arm comparison (A-PRF, sticky bone/A-PRF, FDBA/CM, collagen plug alone) in a high-risk population. [[bone-regeneration/kollati-2019-cerabone-prf-socket-preservation-rct]]

## One-line Summary

Four-arm single-blind RCT (n=18 smokers, 40 sockets) comparing A-PRF, sticky bone+A-PRF, FDBA+collagen membrane, and resorbable collagen plug alone for soft-tissue healing after ARP in upper molars: A-PRF groups showed significantly better mesio-distal closure and healing index; collagen plug alone performed worst.

## 한줄요약

흡연자 대상 4군 RCT (n=18, 소켓 40개): A-PRF 및 A-PRF+sticky bone군에서 순수 콜라겐 플러그군 대비 유의하게 우수한 연조직 폐합 및 치유 지수, FDBA+CM군은 중간 수준 (추적 28일).

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Diagnostics (MDPI)
- **Volume/Issue**: 12(10), 2403
- **ClinicalTrials.gov**: NCT05161455
- **Study design**: Single-blind, parallel-group, 4-arm RCT
- **Sample**: 18 current heavy smokers (≥10 cigarettes/day) with 40 upper molar extraction sockets
- **Follow-up**: 28 days (soft-tissue healing focus; baseline, 10, 21, 28 days)
- **Setting**: Dental University Hospital, King Saud University, Riyadh, Saudi Arabia
- **Period**: June 2021 – April 2022
- **Ethics**: IRB E-21-5999 (KSUMC) and PR 0122 (CDRC); Helsinki Declaration

## 2. Key Contributions

- First (or among very few) RCT examining PRF specifically in heavy smokers for ARP
- Four-arm design allows direct comparison of PRF types vs conventional FDBA+membrane vs passive collagen plug
- Reports patient-reported experience measures (PREMs) alongside clinical healing indices
- Demonstrates that autologous PRF can partially compensate for smoking-induced healing impairment

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Inclusion**: Heavy smokers (≥10 cigs/day), ≥21 years, upper molar requiring extraction, 4-wall socket configuration.

**Exclusion**: Active periodontal disease, periapical lesion, ankylosis, pregnancy, systemic disease affecting healing (uncontrolled DM, osteoporosis, bisphosphonate use), inadequate oral hygiene.

**Randomization**: Socket-level simple randomization (sequence generated by one periodontist, patients blinded to allocation).

**Groups**:
- Group I (A-PRF): Advanced PRF alone — centrifugation at 200 g × 14 min
- Group II (A/S-PRF): "Sticky bone" — FDBA cortico-cancellous (250–1000 µm, Citagenix) mixed with autologous fibrin glue + A-PRF membrane cover
- Group III (FDBA/CM): FDBA (Citagenix) + crosslinked collagen membrane (Citagenix) — positive control
- Group IV (RCP): Resorbable collagen plug alone (Citagenix) — negative control

**Outcome measures**:
- *Primary*: Socket mesio-distal soft-tissue closure (periodontal probe at 10, 21, 28 days)
- *Secondary*: Healing pattern index (0–3 scale); patient-reported PREMs (pain, swelling, satisfaction)
- No radiographic bone assessment at 28 days (soft-tissue only primary endpoint)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | A-PRF | A/S-PRF | FDBA/CM | RCP (control) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mesio-distal closure (p) | significant (p=0.012) | significant (p=0.012) | intermediate | worst |
| Healing pattern index | p<0.0001 | p<0.0001 | intermediate | least favorable |
| PREMs (comfort/pain) | favorable | favorable | moderate | less favorable |

- Both PRF groups significantly superior to RCP on mesio-distal closure (p=0.012) and healing index (p<0.0001)
- A-PRF and A/S-PRF were not significantly different from each other — sticky bone adds cost without clear soft-tissue benefit in smokers at 28 days
- RCP (collagen plug alone) showed the least favorable soft-tissue outcomes among smokers

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Short 28-day follow-up — no bone volume or implant outcome data
- Small sample (n=18, 40 sockets) — underpowered for subgroup analysis
- Upper molars only — may not generalize to anterior or mandibular sites
- No never-smoker comparison arm — cannot quantify magnitude of smoking impairment
- Socket-level randomization within same patient may introduce bleeding/healing interactions
- No radiographic endpoints

## 6. Related Work

- Kollati 2019 shows PRF benefit in non-smokers with xenograft; this study extends PRF evidence to smokers
- Smoking impairs healing via vasoconstriction, fibroblast mutation, immunosuppression (nicotine, CO, HCN)
- Sticky bone / A/S-PRF technique growing in ARP; this RCT provides evidence in the most under-studied population (smokers)

## 7. Glossary

- **A-PRF (Advanced PRF)**: Low-speed centrifugation PRF (200 g × 14 min) yielding higher leukocyte and growth factor content than classical PRF
- **Sticky bone (A/S-PRF)**: FDBA mixed with autologous fibrin glue (from PRF processing) creating a cohesive moldable graft material
- **FDBA**: Freeze-dried bone allograft (cortical-cancellous blend)
- **RCP**: Resorbable collagen plug — used as passive wound coverage
- **PREM**: Patient-Reported Experience Measure
- **Mesio-distal closure**: Linear distance of soft-tissue closure across socket opening, measured with periodontal probe
