## Why Ingested

기존 visit-protocol 근거([[endodontics/visit-protocol/mergoni-2022-single-versus-multiple-visits-cochrane-sr]], [[endodontics/visit-protocol/schwendicke-2017-single-visit-multiple-visit-rct-sr-ma]])는 주로 1차 근관치료를 다루는데, 이 RCT는 근단병소를 가진 치아의 *재치료(retreatment)* 상황으로 visit 횟수 비교를 확장하면서도 "유의차 없음" 결론을 동일하게 도출해 그 결론을 보강(reinforce)한다.

## One-line Summary

RCT (n=100, single-rooted asymptomatic retreatment teeth with periapical lesions, 24-mo follow-up): single-visit (CHX final irrigation) vs two-visit (Ca(OH)₂ dressing) root canal retreatment showed no significant difference in healing (single-visit 88.6% healed vs two-visit 86.7%, p>.05); lesion size and pre-op filling length affected outcome.

## 한줄요약

RCT (n=100, 근단병소 동반 무증상 단근 재치료치, 24개월 추적): 단일내원(CHX 최종세정) vs 2회내원(Ca(OH)₂ 첩약) 근관 재치료가 치유율에서 유의차 없음(단일내원 88.6% 치유 vs 2회내원 86.7%, p>.05); 병소 크기와 술전 근충 길이가 결과에 영향.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Extends the single- vs multiple-visit question into the *retreatment* context (previously root-canal-treated teeth with periapical lesions) — a higher-difficulty scenario than primary RCT.
- Compares single-visit retreatment using 2% chlorhexidine (CHX) as the final irrigant against two-visit retreatment with a calcium hydroxide inter-appointment dressing.
- Confirms no significant healing difference at 24 months, and identifies lesion size and pre-operative root-filling length as significant outcome modifiers.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: RCT; 100 root-canal-treated, single-rooted, asymptomatic teeth with periapical lesions, retreated by one operator.
- **Allocation**: 50 randomized to single-visit, 50 to two-visit.
- **Both groups**: Removal of existing root filling; re-instrumentation by step-back technique with hand files; irrigants 2.5% NaOCl and 5% EDTA; obturation by lateral compaction.
- **Single-visit group**: 5 mL 2% chlorhexidine (CHX) as final irrigation, then immediate obturation.
- **Two-visit group**: Calcium hydroxide paste as inter-appointment dressing, obturated at second visit.
- **Follow-up**: 24 months; recall rate 89/100 (89%).
- **Outcome**: Success based on combined "healed" + "healing" cases. Chi-square / Fisher's exact test for group differences and outcome factors.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

At 24 months:

| Outcome | Single-visit | Two-visit |
|---|---|---|
| Healed | 39 (88.6%) | 39 (86.7%) |
| Healing | 3 (6.8%) | 2 (4.4%) |
| Failed | 2 (4.5%) | 4 (8.9%) |

- No statistically significant difference in success rates between groups (p>.05).
- Size of the periapical lesion and the pre-operative length of the root canal filling had a statistically significant impact on outcome (p<.05).
- Conclusion: single-visit and two-visit retreatment yield equivalent 24-month healing in asymptomatic teeth with periapical lesions.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Restricted to single-rooted, asymptomatic teeth — excludes symptomatic and multi-rooted retreatment cases.
- 24-month follow-up; longer-term recurrence not assessed.
- Single operator/center; outcome partly determined by lesion size and pre-op filling length (confounders, though analyzed).
- CHX vs Ca(OH)₂ comparison is confounded with visit number (the two arms differ in both factors).

## 7. Glossary

- **Root canal retreatment**: Re-do of a previously root-filled tooth (filling removal + re-instrumentation + re-obturation).
- **CHX (chlorhexidine)**: Antimicrobial final irrigant used here in the single-visit arm.
- **Ca(OH)₂ (calcium hydroxide)**: Inter-appointment intracanal dressing used in the two-visit arm.
- **Healed / healing / failed**: Standard radiographic/clinical outcome categories; success combines healed + healing.
- **Step-back technique / lateral compaction**: Hand-file canal shaping and gutta-percha obturation methods used.
