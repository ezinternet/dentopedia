## Why Ingested
Pretreatment strategy for the difficult eroded-dentin substrate; supports [[resin-bonding/sirirangsee-2025-papain-deproteinization-eroded-dentin]].

## One-line Summary
In-vitro: papain deproteinization improves and stabilizes self-etch adhesive µTBS to eroded dentin (notably UBQ), with Raman-confirmed collagen removal.

## 한줄요약
in-vitro — papain 탈단백이 침식상아질에서 self-etch µTBS 향상·안정화(UBQ 유의), Raman으로 collagen 제거 확인.

## 1. Document Information
Scientific Reports 2025; article s41598-025-92975-w. DOI 10.1038/s41598-025-92975-w. Open access. Chulalongkorn University; Tokyo Medical and Dental University.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Papain deproteinization overcomes collagen-rich barrier of eroded dentin.
- Raman amide/phosphate ratio links deproteinization to hybrid-layer improvement.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
In-vitro. Artificially eroded dentin ± papain; bonded with Clearfil SE Bond 2 (SEB) and Clearfil Universal Bond Quick (UBQ); µTBS at 24 h and after 10,000 thermocycles; Raman amide/phosphate; SEM. 3-way ANOVA + Tukey, t-tests (p<0.05).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
Papain: ↑initial µTBS (UBQ significant; SEB slight); stabilized µTBS post-thermocycling for both (p>0.05); ↓amide-to-phosphate ratio (p<0.05); thicker hybrid layer / improved interface.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
In-vitro; artificial erosion model; two adhesives; clinical translation needed.

## 6. Related Work
Demir 2024 (surface treatment & dentin bonding); eroded-dentin adhesion literature.

## 7. Glossary
- Eroded dentin: acid-demineralized, collagen-exposed dentin.
- Deproteinization: enzymatic removal of surface collagen/organic matrix.
- Hybrid layer: resin-infiltrated demineralized dentin zone.
