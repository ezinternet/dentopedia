## Why Ingested

레진접착 성공의 전제인 수분/오염 격리(러버댐 vs 코튼롤)가 실제 수복물 생존율에 미치는 영향을 RCT 메타분석으로 정량화한 최고 근거수준(Cochrane SR+MA) 자료로, 접착 시 격리 방법 선택의 임상 근거를 보강한다. [[resin-bonding/miao-2021-rubber-dam-isolation-restorative-treatment]] 페이지로 다른 접착/수복 결과 페이지들의 방법론적 전제(격리)를 연결한다.

## One-line Summary

Cochrane SR+MA (6 RCTs, 1342 participants, all high risk of bias): rubber dam isolation may increase survival of direct composite restorations of NCCLs at 6 months (OR 2.29, 95% CI 1.05–4.99; low-certainty), with very uncertain evidence at later time points.

## 한줄요약

Cochrane 체계적 문헌고찰+메타분석 (RCT 6편, 1342명, 모두 비뚤림 위험 높음): 러버댐 격리가 6개월 시점에 비우식성 치경부병소(NCCL) 직접 복합레진 수복물의 생존율을 높일 수 있으나(OR 2.29, 95% CI 1.05–4.99; 낮은 근거수준), 그 이후 시점의 근거는 매우 불확실함.

- **Citation**: Miao C, Yang X, Wong MCM, Zou J, Zhou X, Li C, Wang Y. *Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews* 2021, Issue 5, Art. No.: CD009858.
- **DOI**: 10.1002/14651858.CD009858.pub3
- **Editorial group**: Cochrane Oral Health Group.
- **Affiliations**: West China Hospital of Stomatology, Sichuan University, Chengdu, China; University of Hong Kong.
- **Publication status**: New search for studies; content updated, no change to conclusions; published Issue 5, 2021.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides the highest-evidence-tier (Cochrane SR+MA) synthesis comparing **rubber dam isolation** versus alternative moisture-control methods for direct and indirect restorative treatments.
- Quantifies the effect of isolation method on **restoration survival/failure**, a downstream outcome of moisture/contamination control central to adhesive dentistry.
- Updates the 2016 review with a new search (through 13 January 2021); conclusions unchanged.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Objective**: Assess effects of rubber dam isolation vs other isolation types for direct and indirect restorative treatments.
- **Search**: Cochrane Oral Health Trials Register, CENTRAL, MEDLINE, Embase, LILACS, SciELO, plus Chinese databases (CBM, VIP, CNKI) and trial registries; no language/date restriction; searched 13 January 2021.
- **Selection criteria**: Randomised controlled trials (including split-mouth) of >1 month duration comparing rubber dam vs alternative isolation methods in restorative treatment.
- **Data collection**: Two authors independently screened, extracted data, and assessed risk of bias; disagreements resolved by discussion; certainty assessed with GRADE.
- **Comparisons**: (1) Rubber dam vs cotton rolls (5 studies); (2) Rubber dam vs the Isolite system (1 study).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Included studies**: 6 RCTs conducted worldwide 2010–2015; 1342 participants total (233 lost to follow-up); mostly children. **All included studies at high risk of bias.**
- One of the five cotton-roll comparisons was excluded from analysis due to data inconsistencies; of the four remaining, three reported restoration survival (minimum 6-month follow-up).
- **6 months (NCCL direct composite, pooled 2 studies, n=192)**: rubber dam **may increase** survival — OR 2.29 (95% CI 1.05–4.99); low-certainty evidence.
- **12 months (1 study, n=30)**: little to no effect — OR 1.38 (95% CI 0.45–4.28); very low-certainty.
- **18 months (1 study, n=30)**: OR 1.00 (95% CI 0.45–2.25); very low-certainty.
- **24 months (proximal ART, primary molars, children; 1 study, n=559)**: rubber dam may reduce failure risk — HR 0.80 (95% CI 0.66–0.97); very low-certainty.
- **Adverse effects / direct cost**: none of the included studies reported these.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- All included trials at high risk of bias; most outcomes from single small studies; certainty mostly very low (only the 6-month NCCL outcome reaches low-certainty).
- Heterogeneity of restoration types (NCCL composite, proximal ART in primary molars) limits pooling.
- No data on adverse effects or treatment cost.
- Authors call for further high-quality RCTs with follow-up beyond 6 months, evaluating different restorative treatment types, adverse effects, and costs.

## 6. Related Work

- Builds on the adhesive-dentistry principle that moisture/saliva contamination impairs bonding of restorative materials.
- Complements isolation-method comparisons (cotton rolls, Isolite system) in operative dentistry.

## 7. Glossary

- **Rubber dam**: thin latex/non-latex sheet placed over the tooth to isolate it from saliva and the rest of the mouth.
- **Cotton roll isolation**: simple inexpensive moisture control using absorbent cotton rolls plus suction; requires frequent replacement.
- **Isolite system**: combined isolation/suction/illumination device.
- **NCCL**: non-carious cervical lesion.
- **ART**: atraumatic restorative treatment.
- **OR / HR**: odds ratio / hazard ratio.
- **GRADE**: framework for rating certainty of evidence.
