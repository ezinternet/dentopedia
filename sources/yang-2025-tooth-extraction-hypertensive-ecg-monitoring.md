## Why Ingested

기존 [[drug/systemic-disease/uritu-2025-vasoconstrictor-dental-anesthesia-pregnancy-sr]]가 심혈관계 부담 우려 환자에서 발치/마취의 안전성을 다루지만, 고혈압 환자의 발치 중 실시간 혈압·심전도 위험인자에 대한 정량 임계값은 없었다. 본 대규모 retrospective (n=2,059)는 술전 SBP ≥159 mmHg, 수술 시간, 발치 개수를 발치 중 심혈관 사건의 독립 예측인자로 제시해, medically compromised 환자의 발치 술전 평가·intraoperative 모니터링 의사결정 근거를 보강한다. [[drug/systemic-disease/colibasanu-2025-drug-drug-interactions-dental-patients-retrospective]]가 다루는 polypharmacy 고령 환자군과 인구학적으로 겹친다 (평균 74.6세, 70% 동반질환).

## One-line Summary

Single-center retrospective case-control study (n=2,059 extractions in 1,314 hypertensive patients under intraoperative ECG monitoring): 8.01% needed intraoperative antihypertensive drugs and 37.20% developed transient new-onset ECG abnormalities (all non-lethal); preoperative SBP ≥159 mmHg (AUC 0.91) and operative time (OR 3.62) predicted BP intervention, while age, female sex, cerebrovascular disease, number of teeth extracted, and operative time predicted ECG abnormalities.

## 한줄요약

고혈압 환자 발치 2,059건 후향 연구: 8.01%가 발치 중 혈압강하제 필요, 37.20%에서 일과성 신규 부정맥(모두 비치명적) 발생; 술전 SBP ≥159 mmHg(AUC 0.91)와 수술시간이 혈압 개입을, 고령·여성·뇌혈관질환·발치개수·수술시간이 신규 심전도 이상을 예측.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First study to derive a quantitative preoperative-SBP threshold** (≥159 mmHg, AUC 0.91, sens 84.24%/spec 88.75%) for predicting need for intraoperative antihypertensive intervention during tooth extraction in hypertensive patients — a gap noted because no extraction-specific risk tool existed (ACC/AHA classes dental procedures only as generically "low-risk").
2. **Large monitored cohort** (n=2,059 extraction visits, 1,314 unique hypertensive patients, mean age 74.6 y) with continuous intraoperative ECG + serial BP.
3. **Separates two distinct outcomes** with different risk-factor profiles: (a) intraoperative BP requiring drug intervention (driven by hemodynamic/procedural factors) vs (b) new-onset ECG abnormalities (driven by patient-related + procedural factors).
4. **Reassuring overall safety signal**: all arrhythmias non-lethal, only 2/2,059 procedures terminated, no life-threatening emergencies.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Source: ECG-monitoring clinic records, Peking University School of Stomatology, year 2022. From 3,312 records → 2,059 extraction visits (1,314 patients) after excluding non-hypertensive (1,003), non-extraction (235), incomplete monitoring (15).
- Each consultation treated as independent event (745 repeat visits among the 1,314 patients).
- BP/HR recorded at three reported timepoints (preoperative, start of operation, intraoperative maximum); BP every 5–10 min; continuous ECG (NIHON KOHDEN BSM-2351 C). Cardiologist co-managed.
- **Intervention criterion**: oral/sublingual antihypertensive when intraoperative SBP ≥180 mmHg, or SBP >170 mmHg on ≥2 measurements.
- **ECG abnormality**: new finding absent on preoperative ECG.
- Multivariate logistic regression (enter method) for both outcomes; ROC for continuous-variable cutoffs. SPSS v27, P<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Cohort**: 53.42% female, 46.58% male; mean age 74.62 ± 11.42 (range 27–99). 70.40% had ≥1 other systemic comorbidity (coronary heart disease 32.3%, diabetes 29.5%, cerebrovascular disease 22.5%, arrhythmia 16.5%). CCB monotherapy most common antihypertensive (25.3%). 45.9% single-tooth extractions; periodontitis ~37.8% of indications; most procedures 20–30 min (56.6%).

**Outcome 1 — Intraoperative BP intervention** (165/2,059 = 8.01%; 167 deemed high-risk, 2 terminated):
- 164/165 had intraoperative SBP peak >180 mmHg. Nitroglycerin used in 86.06% (0.3–0.5 mg); minority captopril/metoprolol/combinations.
- Independent predictors: preoperative SBP OR 1.138 (P<0.001); SBP at operation start OR 1.019 (P=0.028); HR at start OR 1.064 (P=0.004); estimated operative time OR 3.620 (P<0.001).
- ROC: **preoperative SBP AUC 0.91**; cutoff 158.5 mmHg → Youden 0.73; at **159 mmHg** sensitivity 84.24%, specificity 88.75%.
- NOT significant: age, sex, coronary heart disease, diabetes, cerebrovascular disease, arrhythmia, number of teeth, handpiece, surgical incision (P=0.068, trend), periodontitis.

**Outcome 2 — New-onset intraoperative ECG abnormalities** (766/2,059 = 37.20%, all non-lethal):
- Types: supraventricular premature atrial contraction 44.39%, ventricular premature contraction 42.30% (together 86.69%); sinus arrhythmia 5.61%; rare AV block (0.78%), SVT (0.91%).
- Independent predictors: age OR 1.024 (P<0.001); female sex OR 0.796 (P=0.019, higher risk for females); cerebrovascular disease OR 1.301 (P=0.020); SBP at start OR 1.011 (P=0.006); number of teeth extracted OR 1.182 (P<0.001); estimated operative time OR 1.236 (P=0.017).
- ROC: age AUC only 0.57; cutoff 79.5 y (Youden 0.10) — weak discrimination.
- NOT significant: coronary heart disease, diabetes, preoperative SBP/HR, handpiece, incision, periodontitis.

**Baseline hemodynamics**: Females higher SBP/HR, lower DBP (all P<0.001). Age ≥76 higher SBP, lower DBP, slower HR (arterial stiffness). Coronary heart disease patients had lower SBP/DBP/HR throughout.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-center retrospective → selection bias, no causality, limited generalizability.
- Did NOT capture: local anesthetic dose/type, time since last antihypertensive dose, postoperative complications (bleeding/infection).
- ECG-abnormality model has weak discrimination (age AUC 0.57); predictors statistically but not strongly predictive.
- Repeat-visit clustering (745 repeats) treated as independent events — possible within-patient correlation not adjusted.
- Authors call for prospective longitudinal multi-variable studies.

## 7. Glossary

- **SBP/DBP**: Systolic / diastolic blood pressure.
- **AUC**: Area under the ROC curve (discrimination; 0.5 = chance, 1.0 = perfect).
- **Youden index**: Sensitivity + specificity − 1; maximized to pick ROC cutoff.
- **PVC (ventricular premature contraction)**: Premature ventricular beat.
- **PAC (supraventricular premature atrial contraction)**: Premature atrial beat.
- **CCB / ARB / ACEI**: Calcium channel blocker / angiotensin receptor blocker / ACE inhibitor (antihypertensive classes).
- **Nitroglycerin**: Sublingual vasodilator used here for acute intraoperative BP control.
