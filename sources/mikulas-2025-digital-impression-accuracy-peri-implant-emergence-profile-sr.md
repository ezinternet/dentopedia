## Why Ingested

Implant crown 보철의 디지털 워크플로우에서 잇몸 형태(emergence profile, EP)를 어떻게 옮기느냐가 심미 성패를 가른다 — [[prosthetic-materials/tajti-2023-monolithic-zirconia-metal-ceramic-implant-crowns-sr-ma]]가 implant single crown의 재료·생존을 다뤘다면, 이 SR(Mikulás 2025)은 그 직전 단계인 연조직 EP를 IOS로 캡처할 때 direct vs indirect vs conventional 정확도를 비교해 디지털 임프레션 프로토콜 선택 근거를 제공한다.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (24 studies; 1 RCT, 2 cross-sectional, 4 cross-over, 12 case reports, 5 dental techniques) finding indirect EP scanning (extraoral scan of the provisional, superimposed) most accurately captures the peri-implant emergence profile, while direct scanning after provisional removal shows considerable soft-tissue collapse (~200–500 μm immediate, up to ~1 mm by 20 min).

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(24편; RCT 1·단면 2·교차 4·증례 12·술식 5)로, 임플란트 주위 잇몸형태(emergence profile, EP)를 디지털로 옮길 때 간접 스캐닝(임시보철 구외 스캔 중첩)이 가장 정확하고, 임시보철 제거 직후 직접 스캐닝은 연조직 붕괴(즉시 약 200~500 μm, 20분에 최대 약 1 mm)로 정확도가 떨어진다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic review to determine the best method for capturing the peri-implant **emergence profile (EP)** for implant-supported single crowns.
- Maps three workflow families: **conventional (CM)** elastomeric impression with customized open-tray coping; **direct** intraoral EP scan immediately after provisional (ISPR) removal; **indirect** extraoral scan of the ISPR superimposed onto the in-situ scan.
- Bottom line: **indirect EP scanning is the most promising digital method**; **direct scanning is less advised** because of rapid peri-implant soft-tissue collapse once the ISPR is removed.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PICO: accuracy (O) of direct/indirect digital impression methods (I1, I2) vs conventional impression (C) for capturing peri-implant EP in single implant-supported crowns (P).
- 4 databases (MEDLINE, CENTRAL, Embase, Web of Science) searched 7 Aug 2024; PRISMA 2020; Cochrane Handbook.
- Risk of bias: RoB 2 (Ling RCT), ROBINS-I (cross-sectional/cross-over), JBI checklist (case reports). Most studies moderate-to-high risk of bias.
- 24 studies: 5 dental techniques, 12 case reports, 1 pilot RCT (Ling), 2 cross-sectional (Joda; Galibourg — note Li also cross-sectional), 4 cross-over (incl. 2 pilots: da Silva Marques, Duran, Xiong, Li/Li grouping). Mostly maxillary esthetic zone, Straumann implants, phenotype not reported. Scanners: 3Shape TRIOS (most common), iTero, Carestream CS3600, Cerec Omnicam, Planmeca, 3M ESPE.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Direct scanning — soft-tissue collapse (vs conventional or indirect reference):**
- da Silva Marques: ~244 μm (CIIC prevented 243.89 μm change; below 1 mm threshold, n=6, NS).
- Xiong: direct 414.7 ± 116.0 μm vs conventional; IOS+ISPR (indirect) 230.6 ± 85.5 μm vs conventional; mucosa collapsed ~500 μm within 20 s of ISPR removal regardless of phenotype.
- Li: ~200 μm immediate up to ~1 mm at 20 min vs indirect; significant 0.08–0.18 mm immediately; papilla/zenith displacement up to 0.07 mm immediate / 0.27 mm at 20 min (clinically minimal).
- Galibourg: EP volume −5% (30 s), −10% (2 min), −14% (5 min) vs immediate direct scan.
- Joda: −5.5% (5 min), −21.7% (10 min).
- Duran: 0.51 mm (coronal) to 1.35 mm (midlevel) — larger because whole cross-sectional MD/BL distances measured (not comparable).
- Ling (RCT): definitive-crown EP volumetric difference 1.87 mm (direct) vs 0.75 mm (indirect) labially; 1.66 mm vs 0.65 mm palatally. PES direct 7.89 ± 1.52 vs indirect 8.09 ± 2.27.

**Indirect scanning:** Xiong — replicates EP as accurately as conventional method (ISPR as impression coping). Ling RCT — acceptable replication of provisional submucosal contour. Canullo — 3-year esthetic/bone outcomes similar to conventional (PES analog 8 / digital 9). Limitation: multiple scan files superimposed; scanner software can struggle with large/complex files.

**Conventional reference IOS soft-tissue trueness (cited):** Wei ~80 μm; Gan 130.5 ± 34.0 μm trueness / 55.3 ± 11.2 μm precision; Deferm 0.02 ± 0.07 mm trueness / 80 μm precision.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Limited well-designed studies; high heterogeneity in study type, phenotype, implant diameter/depth; moderate-to-high risk of bias → planned quantitative meta-analysis not interpretable (materials published in appendix).
- No agreed clinically acceptable threshold for EP discrepancy.
- ISPR-as-impression-coping (analog/semi-digital) impractical for everyday use (cast must be poured immediately to return the provisional).
- Future: homogeneous RCTs with larger samples comparing indirect scanning to the conventional gold standard.

## 6. Related Work

- Tajti et al. 2023 — monolithic zirconia vs metal-ceramic implant crowns (downstream restoration this EP-capture step feeds).
- Multilayered monolithic zirconia (Tanaka) may permit fully digital workflows without a physical veneering cast.

## 7. Glossary

- **Emergence profile (EP)**: subgingival-to-supragingival contour of the restoration shaping the peri-implant soft tissue.
- **ISPR**: implant-supported provisional restoration; conditions/supports the soft tissue.
- **Direct EP scan**: intraoral scan of the soft tissue immediately after ISPR removal.
- **Indirect EP scan**: extraoral scan of the removed ISPR, superimposed onto the in-situ scan to recover the submucosal EP (no time pressure from collapse).
- **CM**: conventional (elastomeric) impression method, the gold-standard comparator.
- **CIIC**: customized implant impression coping.
- **PES**: pink esthetic score.
