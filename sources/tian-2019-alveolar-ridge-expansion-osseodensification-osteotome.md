## One-line Summary
Porcine in vivo split-mouth study (n=12 implants, 4-week healing) reporting BIC 62.5% with osseodensification vs 31.4% with conventional osteotome (P=0.018) in atrophic mandibular ridges.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Animal in vivo study (porcine model)
- **Journal**: Journal of Craniofacial Surgery, 30(2):607-610, March/April 2019
- **DOI**: 10.1097/SCS.0000000000004956
- **Sample**: 12 endosteal implants (4 mm × 13 mm) in horizontally atrophic mandibular ridges of porcine models; OD n=6, conventional osteotome n=6
- **Primary Question**: Does osseodensification (OD) drilling outperform conventional osteotome site preparation for alveolar ridge expansion in terms of osseointegration (BIC) and bone area fraction (BAFO)?

## 2. Key Contributions
- BIC% was ~62.5% in OD vs ~31.4% in conventional osteotome group (P = 0.018).
- BAFO (bone area fraction occupancy) showed no significant difference between drilling techniques (P = 0.198).
- Provides first in vivo histologic comparison of OD vs Summers-style osteotome in the ridge-expansion context (previously OD evidence was mainly in low-density bone, not horizontally deficient ridges).
- Demonstrates that OD's biological advantage (compaction autografting + spring-back) is preserved even when the goal is lateral ridge expansion rather than density enhancement.
- Conducted in atrophic mandibular ridges 8 weeks after premolar extraction — model relevant to clinical ridge-expansion candidates.
- Funded by BRR Tech (manufacturer-linked); independent replication is warranted.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Twelve 4 mm × 13 mm endosteal implants were placed in porcine mandibles after bilateral premolar extractions had created horizontally atrophic ridges. Six implants were placed via OD drilling (counterclockwise-rotating Densah-style burs), and six via conventional Summers osteotome site preparation. Animals were sacrificed at 4 weeks.

Outcomes were histologic: undecalcified sections stained with Stevenel's Blue + Van Gieson's Picro Fuschin; quantitative measures were bone-to-implant contact percentage (BIC%) and bone area fraction occupancy (BAFO%), each reported as mean with 95% CI. Statistical analysis used an omnibus test followed by post-hoc pairwise comparison using a pooled standard-error estimate, α = 0.05.

The 4-week sacrifice time captures early osseointegration; longer time points and biomechanical (insertion torque, removal torque, ISQ) were not the focus of this paper as published.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- BIC% OD ≈ 62.5%, conventional ≈ 31.4%, P = 0.018 (significant favoring OD).
- BAFO%: no statistical difference, P = 0.198 (similar overall bone fill within peri-implant region for both techniques).
- Effective doubling of BIC with OD at 4 weeks suggests faster or denser bone apposition at the implant surface despite equivalent total peri-implant bone volume.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Very small sample (n=6 per arm) — high risk of Type II error for BAFO and low precision on the BIC point estimate; no 95% CI numbers are reported in the abstract.
- Single time-point (4 weeks) — cannot speak to long-term remodeling, MBL, or implant survival.
- Porcine ridge healing biology differs from human edentulous bone; translational caution required.
- Only histologic endpoint reported; missing insertion torque, ISQ, and pullout that would tie biology to clinical primary stability.
- Industry funding (BRR Tech) flagged in the disclosure; OD evidence base is heavily dominated by groups associated with the technology.

## 7. Glossary
- **Osseodensification (OD)**: Non-extraction osteotomy preparation using counterclockwise-rotating multifluted burs that plastically deform and compact trabecular bone outward.
- **Conventional osteotome**: Summers-type technique using hand-mallet–driven tapered osteotomes to compact bone laterally and apically.
- **BIC% (bone-to-implant contact)**: Linear fraction of implant perimeter in direct contact with mineralized bone on histologic sections.
- **BAFO% (bone area fraction occupancy)**: Areal fraction of peri-implant zone occupied by bone within the implant threads.
- **Atrophic ridge**: Alveolar ridge with insufficient buccolingual width to host a standard-diameter implant without expansion or grafting.
