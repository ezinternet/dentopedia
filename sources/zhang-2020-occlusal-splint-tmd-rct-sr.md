## One-line Summary
Systematic review of RCTs evaluating occlusal splint efficacy for TMD; found moderate evidence that stabilization splints reduce pain and improve function compared to no treatment, but evidence quality is limited.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Acta Odontologica Scandinavica (2020)
- **DOI**: 10.1080/00016357.2020.1759818
- **Affiliation**: Fujian Medical University
- **Study type**: Systematic review of randomized controlled trials

## 2. Key Contributions
- Synthesizes RCT evidence on occlusal splint efficacy for temporomandibular disorders (악관절증, TMD)
- Evaluates stabilization splints vs. control devices vs. no treatment
- Assesses outcomes: pain intensity, maximum mouth opening (하악 최대개구량, MMO), and quality of life
- Highlights lack of standardized outcome measures across trials

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Systematic search of MEDLINE, Embase, Cochrane Central
- Inclusion: RCTs examining occlusal splints (교합안정장치) for TMD
- Risk of bias assessment using Cochrane tool
- Narrative synthesis (meta-analysis not performed due to heterogeneity)
- Outcomes: pain (VAS/NRS), MMO, joint sounds, quality of life

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Stabilization splints showed statistically significant pain reduction compared to no treatment in most included trials
- Limited evidence for superiority over placebo/flat-plane splints
- MMO improvements were inconsistent across studies
- Quality of evidence: low to moderate (GRADE)
- High risk of bias in many included RCTs due to blinding limitations

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Heterogeneity in TMD diagnosis criteria (RDC/TMD vs. DC/TMD) limits pooling
- Short follow-up periods in most trials (typically ≤3 months)
- Blinding of patients is inherently difficult for splint interventions
- Future trials should use standardized DC/TMD diagnostic criteria

## 7. Glossary
- **TMD** (악관절증): Temporomandibular disorder; umbrella term for pain/dysfunction of the TMJ and masticatory muscles
- **Occlusal splint** (교합안정장치): Removable dental appliance worn over teeth to reduce masticatory muscle activity and joint loading
- **MMO** (하악 최대개구량, Maximum Mouth Opening): Primary functional outcome measure in TMD studies
- **RDC/TMD**: Research Diagnostic Criteria for Temporomandibular Disorders (older classification)
- **DC/TMD**: Diagnostic Criteria for Temporomandibular Disorders (updated 2014 classification)
- **VAS**: Visual Analogue Scale for pain measurement
