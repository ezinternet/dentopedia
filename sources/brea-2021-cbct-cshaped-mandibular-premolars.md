## Why Ingested

Extends the C-shaped canal morphology framework from molars (anchored by [[endodontics/anatomy/song-2008-cross-sectional-morphology-and-minimum-canal]]) to mandibular PREMOLARS — a less-studied but treatment-critical site — in a Venezuelan (Latin American) cohort. It complements the premolar-focused [[endodontics/anatomy/yu-2012-cbct-mandibular-premolars-root-canal-morphology]] and adds premolar-specific prevalence to the molar-dominated sibling prevalence pages.

## One-line Summary

CBCT cross-sectional study (380 mandibular first + 308 second premolars, 292 Venezuelan patients): overall 19.2% (132/688) had a C-shaped root canal system, with 83.3% of cases in first premolars vs 16.7% in second premolars; C3 most common in first premolars (59.1%); no significant gender difference; bifurcation mostly in the middle third (~50–53%).

## 한줄요약

CBCT 횡단 연구(베네수엘라 292명, 하악 제1소구치 380·제2소구치 308): 전체 19.2%(132/688)가 C-shaped 근관계, 그 중 83.3%가 제1소구치·16.7%가 제2소구치; 제1소구치는 C3형이 가장 흔함(59.1%); 성별 차이 무의미; 분지(bifurcation)는 주로 중간 1/3(~50–53%).

## 1. Document Information

- Type: Retrospective cross-sectional CBCT study
- Population: Venezuelan (Caracas), CBCT acquired Jan 2014–Dec 2017
- Sample: 380 mandibular first premolars + 308 second premolars = 688 teeth from 292 patients (175 female / 117 male)
- Imaging: Kodak 9000 3D, 60–90 kV, 2–15 mA, voxel 76 μm, slice 200 μm
- Journal: BMC Oral Health (2021) 21:236

## 2. Key Contributions

- Premolar-specific (not molar) C-shaped prevalence — fills a gap relative to molar-dominated literature.
- First-premolar predominance of C-shaped configuration (opposite to molar pattern where second molars dominate).
- Reports level of canal bifurcation and radicular groove location.
- High inter-/intra-observer agreement (Kappa 0.90–0.94; ICC 0.89–0.91).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Two endodontists evaluated images twice (1-week interval); radiologist arbitrated disagreements.
- Fan et al. classification (extended for premolars):
  - C1: continuous C; C2: semicolon/discontinuous C; C3: two separate round/oval/flat canals; C4: single canal (C4a round, C4b oval, C4c flat); C5: ≥3 separate canals; C6: no lumen/no intact canal.
- Recorded: tooth position, root/canal number, C-shaped configuration, bifurcation level (cervical/middle/apical), radicular groove location.
- Statistics: chi-square (α=0.05); SPSS 21.0.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Overall C-shaped root canal system: 19.2% (132/688).
  - Male 9.16% (63), Female 10.03% (69) — no significant gender difference.
  - Of C-shaped cases: 83.33% in first premolars, 16.66% in second premolars.
- First premolar Fan distribution: C1 3.63%, C2 9.09%, C3 59.09%, C4a 21.81%, C4b 1.8%.
- Second premolar Fan distribution: C1 13.63%, C2 18.18%, C3 13.63%, C4a 27.27%, C4b 9.09%.
- Canal bifurcation in the middle third: ~53.36% (first premolars), ~50.09% (second premolars).
- No statistical differences between C-shaped system and gender, or Fan configuration and gender (p<0.05 used as threshold).
- Conclusion: C-shaped anatomy relatively LOW in second premolars vs first premolars.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-center Venezuelan sample; limited generalizability.
- CBCT resolution below micro-CT; subtle isthmuses may be missed.
- No clinical outcome correlation.
- First-vs-second premolar imbalance in sample size.

## 6. Related Work

- Cooke & Cox 1979 (first C-shaped description).
- Fan et al. (cross-sectional root-shape classification, here extended to premolars).
- Prior premolar CBCT studies in China, India, Iran, USA, Finland.

## 7. Glossary

- C-shaped canal/root: root canal system whose cross-section forms a "C", with isthmuses connecting canals and radicular grooves.
- Radicular groove: longitudinal depression on the root surface (mesial/distal/buccal/lingual) producing C-shaped morphology.
- Bifurcation level: third of the root (cervical/middle/apical) at which a canal divides.
- Fan classification (C1–C6 here): cross-sectional canal-shape categories.
- ICC: intraclass correlation coefficient (reliability for counts of roots/canals).
