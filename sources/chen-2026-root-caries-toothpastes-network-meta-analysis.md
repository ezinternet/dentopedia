## Why Ingested

This SR + network meta-analysis (J Dent 2026) ranks toothpaste active agents — high-concentration sodium fluoride (5000 ppm), arginine+fluoride, and bioactive calcium/phosphate (CaSO4 + NH4H2PO4) systems — for arresting and reversing root caries, giving clinically actionable first-line non-invasive guidance for high-risk (often geriatric) patients. It extends [[caries/meyer-lueckel-2019-intervene-root-caries-process-sr-ma]], which already established 5000 ppm F toothpaste, CHX varnish, and SDF as superior lesion-arresting agents, by pooling newer bioactive and arginine formulations head-to-head against standard 1450 ppm NaF. ## One-line Summary

SR + network meta-analysis (Cochrane, 9 databases; 13 in vitro + 3 in situ + 12 clinical studies): high-concentration sodium fluoride toothpaste (5000 ppm) most effectively reduces root-caries lesion depth, hardens lesions, and prevents new caries — bioactive Ca/P and arginine+fluoride pastes are alternatives, while standard 1450 ppm NaF may be insufficient in high-risk patients.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic review with network meta-analysis to rank toothpaste active agents specifically for **management of root caries**, integrating laboratory (in vitro), in situ, and clinical evidence.
- Confirms a significant overall benefit of active toothpaste over placebo on root-caries lesion depth and mineral loss.
- Positions **high-concentration sodium fluoride (5000 ppm)** as the most effective single agent across lesion depth reduction, lesion hardening, and prevention of new lesions.
- Identifies **bioactive calcium/phosphate** and **arginine + fluoride** toothpastes as viable alternatives, particularly for patients who cannot tolerate or decline prescription high-fluoride pastes.
- Flags that **standard 1450 ppm NaF may be insufficient** to arrest active root caries in high-risk patients.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Conducted per Cochrane guidelines.
- Literature searches across **nine databases** (up to 31.01.2024).
- In vitro and in situ studies evaluated with a quality assessment tool; **Cochrane RoB 2.0** for clinical studies.
- Two reviewers independently performed study selection, data extraction, risk-of-bias appraisal, and **GRADE** certainty assessment.
- Eligible studies: **13 in vitro + 3 in situ + 12 clinical**.
- Mean differences (MD) computed for lesion depth, mineral loss, and root-caries increments; risk ratios (RR) for changes in lesion hardness and new root carious lesions; random-effects model.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Toothpaste vs placebo**: significant reduction in root-caries lesion depth (MD −55.75; 95% CI −90.70 to −20.80; p=0.002) and mineral loss (MD −1.18; 95% CI −1.85 to −0.51; p=0.0006).
- **High sodium fluoride (5000 ppm) or 1.5% arginine + 1450 ppm NaPOF vs standard 1450 ppm NaF** (hardening/reversing lesions): RR 1.60 (95% CI 0.92 to 2.79).
- **Bioactive toothpaste (1100 ppm NaF + 1% CaSO4 + 1.1% NH4H2PO4) vs standard toothpaste** (lesions becoming hard): RR 1.81 (95% CI 1.39 to 2.36).
- High-concentration sodium fluoride toothpaste was most effective at reducing lesion depth, improving hardness, and preventing new caries.
- **Clinical takeaway**: high-concentration sodium fluoride (5000 ppm, by prescription) can be first-line non-invasive therapy for arresting active root caries; OTC fluoride+arginine or bioactive (Ca/P) toothpastes are alternatives for patients who cannot tolerate or decline high-fluoride prescriptions.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only ingest — full text not retrieved (no PMC open access); detailed network geometry, GRADE certainty ratings per comparison, and heterogeneity statistics are not available here.
- The pivotal high-fluoride/arginine vs standard comparison (RR 1.60) had a 95% CI crossing 1 (0.92 to 2.79), indicating the hardening/reversal benefit was not statistically conclusive in that pooled estimate.
- Mixed evidence base (in vitro + in situ + clinical) of differing validity; clinical inference rests on 12 clinical studies.

## 7. Glossary

- **Root caries**: carious lesions on exposed root (cementum/dentin) surfaces, common with gingival recession in older/high-risk patients.
- **5000 ppm NaF toothpaste**: high-concentration prescription sodium fluoride dentifrice.
- **Arginine + fluoride**: arginine (e.g., 1.5%) combined with fluoride (NaPOF / NaF) to enhance remineralization and pH buffering.
- **Bioactive toothpaste (CaSO4 + NH4H2PO4)**: calcium- and phosphate-delivering system (here 1100 ppm NaF + 1% calcium sulfate + 1.1% ammonium dihydrogen phosphate).
- **NaPOF**: sodium monofluorophosphate.
- **MD / RR**: mean difference / risk ratio.
- **GRADE / RoB 2.0**: certainty-of-evidence framework / Cochrane risk-of-bias tool for randomized trials.
