## 2. Key Contributions
- Zr-IRFPD: 672±123N ≈ full-coverage Zr-FDP: 672±183N (no significant difference)
- ZrC-IRFPD (feldspathic coating): 638±59N — not significantly different from Zr groups
- VS-IRFPD (zirconia-reinforced lithium silicate): 391±136N — significantly lower (p<0.05)
- Failure mode: connector fracture in all groups
- Surface treatment (sandblasting vs coating) did not significantly affect fracture load in Zr

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
In vitro study (n=64 extracted premolars and molars) comparing fracture load of 3-unit ceramic FDPs: zirconia IRFPD with sandblasting (Zr-IRFPD), zirconia IRFPD with feldspathic coating (ZrC-IRFPD), zirconia reinforced lithium silicate IRFPD (VS-IRFPD), and full-coverage zirconia FDP (Zr-FDP/control). After 5000 thermocycles, load-to-failure tested. Fracture load: Zr-FPD 672±183N, Zr-IRFPD 672±123N, ZrC-IRFPD 638±59N (no significant difference, p>0.05). VS-IRFPD 391±136N (significantly lower, p<0.05). Failure predominantly at connector area.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
