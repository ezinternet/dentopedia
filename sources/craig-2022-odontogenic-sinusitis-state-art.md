## One-line Summary

Narrative review (ENT perspective) outlining the diagnosis and multidisciplinary management of odontogenic sinusitis (ODS), covering clinical suspicion criteria, microbiology, dental vs. ESS treatment sequencing, and 7 key clinical scenarios.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Prevalence context**: ODS accounts for 25%–40% of all chronic maxillary sinusitis and 45%–75% of unilateral maxillary sinus opacification on CT.
2. **Dual-confirmation diagnostic framework**: ODS requires (a) otolaryngologist confirmation of sinusitis via nasal endoscopy and (b) dental specialist confirmation of dental pathology — neither alone is sufficient.
3. **Clinical suspicion features**: unilateral maxillary sinus opacification on CT, overt dental pathology on CT, unilateral middle meatal purulence on endoscopy, subjective foul smell, alpha-hemolytic streptococci/anaerobes in sinus cultures, relative sparing of posterior ethmoid and sphenoid.
4. **Antibiotics are NOT curative** for ODS due to treatable dental pathology (multiple case series; multidisciplinary consensus confirmed).
5. **Overall resolution rate**: 90%–100% when both dental pathology AND sinusitis are addressed.
6. **Primary dental treatment success**: ~60% for extraction in AP-ODS; ~77% in the largest prospective series (Simuntis, n=96), but caveats apply (limited disease burden, no pretreatment endoscopy).
7. **ESS timing**: Primary ESS resolves symptoms significantly faster (7–12 days vs. 35–56 days for primary RCTx/extraction). In patients with significant symptom burden, primary ESS is a valid option.
8. **ESS extent for uncomplicated ODS**: maxillary antrostomy alone is an option regardless of CT sinusitis extent; opening all diseased sinuses also reasonable.
9. **Implant-related ODS**: primary ESS generally successful; implant removal not necessary unless peri-implantitis or mobility is present.
10. **OAF-related ODS**: 90%–100% success when ESS + OAF closure are performed concurrently or in close proximity.
11. **Complicated ODS** (extrasinus spread): ~7% of published ODS cases; orbital (69%), intracranial (19%), osseous (4%); only 50% of vision-loss cases recovered sight — worse than non-ODS complicated sinusitis.
12. **Radiologist miss rate**: 60%–70% of dental pathology is missed on CT — clinicians must actively suspect ODS.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Single-author narrative review based on systematic literature search of the ODS evidence base up to mid-2021.
- Synthesizes findings from original research, cohort studies, case series, and recent international consensus statements (Craig 2021 IFAR diagnosis; Craig 2020 IFAR management).
- Presents 7 clinical scenarios to operationalize diagnostic/therapeutic recommendations.
- References 55 citations spanning 1986–2021.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Evidence base is predominantly level 4–5 (90%–100% of ODS publications over 30 years); no RCTs exist.
- Heterogeneous study definitions (mucosal thickening ≠ ODS; variable endoscopy use).
- Primary RCTx efficacy for AP-ODS: only case reports/small series — success rate undeterminable.
- Adjunctive treatments (saline irrigation, decongestants) unstudied in ODS.
- ESS extent (maxillary antrostomy vs. full FESS) in uncomplicated ODS: no consensus, more data needed.
- Marginal periodontitis as ODS cause: very little published, no ODS treatment studies.
- Asymptomatic ODS natural history: unstudied.
- OAC/OAF risk when extracting in the context of active ODS: not specifically studied.

## 7. Glossary

- **ODS**: Odontogenic sinusitis (치과기원 부비동염) — bacterial maxillary sinusitis from infectious dental pathology or iatrogenic dental injury
- **AP**: Apical periodontitis (치근단 치주염) — endodontic disease from pulpal necrosis; most common ODS cause
- **OAC/OAF**: Oroantral communication/fistula (구강-상악동 교통/누공) — connection between oral cavity and maxillary sinus
- **ESS**: Endoscopic sinus surgery (내시경 부비동 수술)
- **FESS**: Functional ESS
- **RCTx**: Root canal therapy (근관치료)
- **Middle meatal purulence**: Pus visible in the middle meatus on nasal endoscopy — key confirmatory finding for ODS
- **Lund-Mackay score**: CT-based sinusitis severity scoring system
- **Balloon sinuplasty**: Balloon dilation of sinus ostia — NOT recommended for ODS due to severe edema/polyps
- **Pott's puffy tumor**: Frontal osteomyelitis with subperiosteal abscess — ~50% may have odontogenic cause
