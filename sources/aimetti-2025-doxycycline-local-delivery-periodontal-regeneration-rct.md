## Why Ingested

기존 [[periodontics/stavropoulos-2021-periodontal-regeneration-intrabony-nma]]는 EMD/xenograft 재생술의 평균 결과를 제시하지만, BoP+ 잔존 부위에서 국소 독시사이클린(DOX, doxycycline)을 사전 투여하는 BOOST 전처치가 실제로 결과를 개선하는지는 RCT 수준 근거가 없었다. 본 RCT (Aimetti 2025)는 그 공백을 직접 채우며, 국소항생제의 최적 사용 시점(수술 전 2주)과 용도(항염·항교원분해)를 명확히 한다.

## One-line Summary

Triple-blind RCT (n=60, stage III/IV periodontitis) showed that locally delivered doxycycline (BOOST approach) 2 weeks before MIST with EMD + xenograft significantly improved CAL gain (4.1 vs. 3.2 mm), composite outcome success (83.3% vs. 46.7%), and radiographic bone fill (3.5 vs. 2.7 mm) at 1 year.

## 한줄요약

3중맹검 무작위대조시험(n=60, 3·4기 치주염)에서 최소침습수술(MIST, Minimally Invasive Surgical Technique) 2주 전 국소 독시사이클린(DOX, doxycycline) BOOST 투여군이 대조군 대비 임상부착수준(CAL, Clinical Attachment Level) 획득 4.1 대 3.2 mm, 복합성공률(COM, Composite Outcome Measure) 83.3% 대 46.7%, 방사선학적 골충전 3.5 대 2.7 mm에서 유의하게 우수한 1년 결과를 보였다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First RCT to test the BOOST concept: locally delivered doxycycline (DOX) 2 weeks *prior* to periodontal regeneration surgery as a site-targeted immuno-inflammatory modulator.
2. Demonstrated that BOOST significantly reduced preoperative BoP (30% vs. 66.7%) and improved Early Wound Healing Index (EHI: 1.5 vs. 2.2, p=0.041) — mechanistic bridge from pre-surgical inflammation control to better healing.
3. At 1 year, BOOST achieved superior CAL gain (+0.9 mm advantage), PPD reduction (+0.8 mm), composite outcome success (83.3% vs. 46.7%, NNT=2.73), pocket closure (83.3% vs. 60.0%, NNT=4.29), and radiographic bone fill (+0.8 mm).
4. Multivariate regression confirmed treatment allocation (BOOST), defect morphology (number of bony walls), EHI, and baseline PPD as independent predictors of CAL gain.
5. Repositions local antimicrobials from step-2 adjunct (modest long-term benefit) to pre-surgical site preparation tool with host-modulatory rationale (anti-collagenolytic, anti-inflammatory via MMP/IL-1β/IL-6 suppression).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Design:** Triple-blind, randomized (permuted blocks 1:1), parallel-arm clinical trial. Patient, surgeon, and examiner all blinded.

**Population:** Stage III/IV periodontitis, FMPS/FMBS < 15%, non-smokers, age ≥ 18, one site with residual PPD ≥ 6 mm + BoP+ + radiographic intrabony defect ≥ 3 mm, no furcation involvement, no systemic disease affecting healing.

**Sample size:** n=60 (30 per group); powered to detect 0.5 mm CAL gain difference (α=0.05, 80% power, 20% drop-out adjustment).

**Intervention — Test (BOOST):** Subgingival debridement + single subgingival administration of doxycycline hyclate in biodegradable polylactic-polyglycolic acid gel (Ligosan, Kulzer) 2 weeks before surgery.
**Intervention — Control:** Subgingival debridement alone (DOX procedure simulated).

**Surgery (both groups):** MIST under magnification, EDTA root conditioning, EMD (Emdogain, Straumann) + bone xenograft (Bio-Oss, Geistlich), Gore-tex 6/0 suture. Performed by postgraduate residents under senior supervision (standardized technique).

**Follow-up:** Baseline (T0, 14 days before surgery), surgery day (T1), 2 weeks post-surgery (EHI), 12 months (T2).

**Outcomes:**
- Primary: CAL gain at 12 months
- Secondary: PPD reduction, REC change, BoP, PI, pocket closure, COM, radiographic IBD reduction, defect angle change

**Calibration:** Intra-class correlation: 0.92 for CAL, 0.95 for PPD (clinical); ICC 0.86 for radiographic measurements.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Parameter | BOOST | Control | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| BoP at surgery (T1) | 30.0% | 66.7% | < 0.001 |
| EHI at 2 weeks post-op | 1.5 ± 0.9 | 2.2 ± 1.4 | 0.041 |
| CAL gain (12 mo) | 4.1 ± 1.9 mm | 3.2 ± 2.0 mm | 0.019 |
| PPD reduction (12 mo) | 4.4 ± 1.8 mm | 3.6 ± 1.9 mm | 0.040 |
| REC increase (12 mo) | 0.2 ± 0.9 mm | 0.4 ± 0.7 mm | NS |
| Pocket closure | 83.3% (25/30) | 60.0% (18/30) | 0.045 |
| COM success | 83.3% (25/30) | 46.7% (14/30) | 0.006 |
| Radiographic IBD fill | 3.5 ± 1.2 mm | 2.7 ± 1.3 mm | 0.027 |
| Defect angle change | 9.7° ± 5.1° | 3.6° ± 4.5° | < 0.001 |

**NNT for COM:** 2.73 (extremely favorable — 1 additional success per 3 patients treated with BOOST)
**NNT for pocket closure:** 4.29

**Multivariate predictors of CAL gain:** Treatment (β=1.12, p<0.001), bony walls/defect morphology (β=0.98, p=0.003), EHI (β=0.93, p=0.008), baseline PPD (β=0.71, p<0.001).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

1. Multiple operators performing surgery introduce variability (though improves generalizability)
2. Standardized biomaterial protocol (EMD + xenograft) limits external validity to other regenerative approaches
3. No clinical measurement stent used
4. No patient-reported outcomes assessed
5. No cost-benefit analysis
6. Selection criteria (non-smokers, well-controlled plaque) limit applicability to challenging patient profiles
7. Future: molecular phenotyping may identify which sites most benefit from BOOST (personalized pre-surgical treatment decision)

## 7. Glossary

- **BOOST (BiO-Optimizing Site Targeted approach):** Concept of locally delivered doxycycline as a pre-surgical site modulator to reduce inflammation and improve regeneration outcomes
- **MIST (Minimally Invasive Surgical Technique):** Papilla-preserving flap approach for periodontal regeneration avoiding vertical releasing incisions
- **DOX:** Doxycycline hyclate in biodegradable polylactic-polyglycolic acid gel (Ligosan)
- **EHI (Early Wound Healing Index):** Scale assessing interproximal soft tissue healing 2 weeks post-surgery (1-2 = good; 3-5 = suboptimal)
- **COM (Composite Outcome Measure):** Dichotomous success criterion: PPD ≤ 4 mm AND CAL gain ≥ 3 mm at 1 year
- **IBD (Intrabony Defect Depth):** Distance from alveolar bone crest to bottom of defect on periapical radiograph
- **EMD (Enamel Matrix Derivative, 에나멜기질단백, EMP):** Emdogain; biologic scaffold promoting periodontal regeneration
- **MMP (Matrix Metalloproteinase, 기질금속단백분해효소):** Enzymes degrading extracellular matrix; inhibited by doxycycline via zinc-binding
- **NNT (Number Needed to Treat):** Number of patients treated to achieve one additional favorable outcome; NNT=2.73 for COM is clinically very favorable
