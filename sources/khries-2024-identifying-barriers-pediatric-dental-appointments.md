## Why Ingested

Extends appointment-attendance / non-attendance evidence from adults to the pediatric population — quantifying the parent-, patient-, and administrative-level barriers (forgetfulness, scheduling, staff miscommunication) that drive missed pediatric dental visits, directly informing scheduling and retention SOP. Reinforces the adult/general no-show and dental-attendance evidence in [[practice-management/jkda-2024-62-6-001]].

## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional questionnaire study (n=265 children <12 yr, Jordan): 44% missed-appointment rate despite 85% insured; non-attendance significantly linked to child age, gender, and public-transport use, with social/family commitments (15.5%), forgetfulness (11.2%), and staff miscommunication (10.3%) as leading barriers.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies the missed-appointment (non-attendance) rate in a Jordanian pediatric dental clinic at **44%** (117/265) despite **85% insurance coverage** — demonstrating that financial barriers alone do not explain non-attendance.
- Partitions non-attendance into a **four-domain taxonomy**: parent/caregiver, patient, administrative, and nonspecific reasons — directly mappable to scheduling/retention interventions.
- Identifies the demographic correlates of missed appointments (child age, gender, transportation mode) and, importantly, the **null** correlates (parental occupation and education).
- Fills a stated literature gap on pediatric dental absenteeism in Jordanian children.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Cross-sectional, descriptive and analytical.
- **Population**: Pediatric patients <12 yr with >3 prior appointments at the clinic; every third eligible follow-up patient selected. Exclusion: <3 prior appointments. Final n = 265 (age 2.5–12 yr, mean 6.85 ± 1.7).
- **Instrument**: Pre-tested, expert-validated structured questionnaire completed by parents/guardians via direct interview after informed consent; covered child details, family background, and non-attendance factors. Cronbach's alpha = 0.95.
- **Data window**: 3 months (Nov 2023 – Jan 2024; chosen to span school vacation).
- **Analysis**: SPSS v26. Descriptive statistics; chi-square test for associations between missed appointments and gender, parental occupation, education, and transport type. Significance P ≤ 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Missed-appointment rate**: 44% (117/265 missed; 148 did not).
- **Sample**: 57% male; 76% school-age; 99.6% Jordanian; 85% insured; 89% medically free. 92% lived with both parents; 79% used public transport; majority had below-high-school parental education.
- **Significant associations** (P < 0.05): child age (P = 0.0041), type of transportation (P = 0.002); gender reported significant in text (Table 3 chi-square P = 0.091).
- **Non-significant**: father/mother occupation (P = 0.561 / 0.134), father/mother education (P = 0.132 / 0.129).
- **Leading non-attendance factors** (Table 4):
  - Parent/caregiver: social & family commitments 15.5%, forgetfulness 11.2%, parent/caregiver illness 8.6%, work obligations 6%, dental issue resolved 4.3%.
  - Patient: patient illness 12.1%, school commitment 6.9%, fear of dental treatment 3.5%.
  - Administrative: staff miscommunication 10.3%, long wait time 5.2%, long gaps between follow-ups 5.2%.
  - Nonspecific: 11.2% (insurance-card renewal; treatment expense for uninsured child).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-region (Jordan) sample limits generalizability.
- Self-reported questionnaire data subject to response bias.
- Cross-sectional design — no causal inference, no longitudinal tracking of interventions.
- Authors recommend diverse multi-region/socioeconomic sampling and integrating objective dental-record data with survey responses.
- Proposed interventions: automated reminders, clear cancellation/no-show policy, reduced wait times, shortened follow-up gaps, school-linked/mobile programs, oral health literacy campaigns, anxiety-reduction technology.

## 7. Glossary

- **Missed appointment / non-attendance**: Patient fails to attend, cancel, or reschedule a booked appointment.
- **Missed-appointment rate**: Proportion of patients with ≥1 missed appointment (here 44%).
- **Cronbach's alpha**: Internal-consistency reliability coefficient (0.95 = excellent here).
- **Chi-square test**: Test of association between categorical variables (used for demographic correlates of non-attendance).
- **Cross-sectional study**: Observational design capturing data at a single time point.
