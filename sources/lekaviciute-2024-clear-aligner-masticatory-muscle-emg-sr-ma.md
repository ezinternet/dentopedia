## One-line Summary

SR+MA (6 studies, 108 adults, NOS low–moderate RoB; PROSPERO CRD42024522231): clear aligner treatment transiently raised masseter sEMG at maximal clench then it fell, with mixed temporalis/resting responses, but the pooled meta-analysis found a NON-significant difference in masseter and temporalis activity — evidence too thin for firm conclusions.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: The effect of clear aligner treatment on masticatory muscles (masseter, temporalis) activity in adults: a systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Authors**: Lekavičiūtė R, Paldauskaitė S, Stučinskaitė S, Trakinienė G
- **Journal**: European Journal of Orthodontics 2024;46(4)
- **Year**: 2024 (2024-08-01)
- **DOI**: 10.1093/ejo/cjae030 · **PMID**: 38943433 · **PMCID**: PMC11213980
- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (PRISMA 2020; PROSPERO CRD42024522231)
- **Evidence base**: 6 studies (2 prospective cohort, 3 observational longitudinal, 1 observational longitudinal case–control), 108 adults, mean age 22–35; risk of bias low–moderate by Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS). All 6 entered the meta-analysis.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA to **pool** masseter and temporalis surface EMG (sEMG) data across clear aligner treatment (CAT), where prior reviews stayed qualitative.
- Provides a quantitative bottom line: pooled random-effects effect sizes masseter 0.20 (P = .078) and temporalis 0.24 (P = .128); both 95% CIs contain 0 → NON-significant.
- Disaggregates by clenching task: maximal voluntary clenching (MVC), submaximal voluntary clenching (SMVC), and mandibular resting position (RP) — showing the muscle response is task-dependent and time-dependent.
- Heterogeneity low (masseter I² = 14.99%, Q P = .261; temporalis I² = 45.53%, Q P = .127); no small-study/publication bias (Egger P = .237 masseter, P = .949 temporalis); no moderating effect of mean age, study quality, or study design.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **PICO**: adults of any sex (P) undergoing CAT (I), masticatory muscle activity comparison (C), masseter/temporalis sEMG outcomes (O).
- **Search**: PubMed, Web of Science, SCOPUS, Cochrane Library, Google Scholar up to March 2024; English only; keywords ('clear aligners' OR 'orthodontic aligners') AND ('masticatory muscle' OR 'muscle of mastication' OR 'electromyography'). Aligner thickness 0.7–0.8 mm inclusion criterion.
- **Screening**: 2418 records → 1102 title/abstract → 163 → 18 full-text → 6 included (3 independent investigators, 4th as arbiter).
- **RoB**: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (selection, comparability, outcome) for 5 cohort + 1 case–control.
- **Synthesis**: random-effects model in RStudio; Tau², I², Q-statistic; forest plots, funnel plots, Egger's test, sensitivity (leave-one-out) and moderator/confounder analyses (mean age, study quality, study design).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Maximal voluntary clenching (MVC)** — masseter: significant INITIAL increase (P < .05) then non-significant subsequent decrease (Lou: peak at passive/first active aligner, then drop wk4; Paes-Souza: rise wk1–2 then gradual fall, ~20% net at 32 wk). Two studies (Nota, Liu) found no MVC difference. Temporalis (MVC) mixed: 2 studies significant increase (Paes-Souza ~70%; Liu at 3 mo), 1 no change (Nota).
- **Submaximal voluntary clenching (SMVC)** — only Paes-Souza measured; consistent SIGNIFICANT REDUCTION in masseter activity over time (RMS −20%, right masseter 108.6→88.7, left 87.8→68.1); temporalis instead rose ~90% (P = .000).
- **Mandibular resting position (RP)** — mixed: Nota significant masseter DECREASE after 1 mo (P < .05); Paes-Souza significant masseter INCREASE ~30% at 8 mo (P = .001); Liu no change. Temporalis at RP: 2 significant increases (Paes-Souza ~80%; Liu at 3 mo), 1 no change.
- **POC / Activity index** — Dellavia & Tepedino: significant changes in Percentage Overlapping Coefficient (POC) for masseter and temporalis; Torque Coefficient (TC) and Asymmetry index (Asym) generally unchanged. Dellavia: parameters returned to normal range 3 months after treatment ended.
- **CAT vs fixed appliances** (Dellavia only): both groups returned to normal range; significant POC change only in the fixed (FOA) group, not CAT.
- **POOLED meta-analysis**: masseter ES 0.20 (P = .078), temporalis ES 0.24 (P = .128) — both NON-significant (CIs include 0). Sensitivity: Lou influential for masseter, Liu influential for temporalis.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 6 small studies (n 10–26; total 108); single-arm designs dominate, only 1 study compared CAT vs FOA, only 1 measured SMVC, only 1 followed after treatment ended.
- sEMG can pick up cross-talk from adjacent muscles; heterogeneous monitoring periods; aligner systems/material mechanical properties not standardized across studies.
- Authors explicitly state existing literature is insufficient for concrete conclusions; larger, standardized longitudinal trials needed.

## 6. Related Work

- Prior orthodontic-treatment-on-muscle meta-analysis and the general sEMG occlusion literature (e.g. single 0.25 mm intercuspal interference altering elevator-muscle activity) frame the rationale.
- Comparators cited: fixed appliances/brackets, clear thermoplastic vs wrap-around retainers, Bimler functional device (neuromuscular technique reduced masseter/temporalis at rest).

## 7. Glossary

- **CAT** — clear aligner treatment.
- **sEMG / ssEMG** — (standardized) surface electromyography; measures bioelectrical muscle activity via skin electrodes.
- **MM / TA** — masseter muscle / anterior temporalis muscle.
- **MVC / SMVC / RP** — maximal voluntary clenching / submaximal voluntary clenching / mandibular resting position.
- **POC** — Percentage Overlapping Coefficient (symmetry of paired muscle activation).
- **TC** — Torque Coefficient; **AC** — Activity index; **Asym** — Asymmetry index.
- **RMS** — root mean square of the sEMG amplitude.
- **NOS** — Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (risk-of-bias tool for cohort/case–control studies).
- **I² / Tau² / Q-statistic** — heterogeneity metrics in meta-analysis.
