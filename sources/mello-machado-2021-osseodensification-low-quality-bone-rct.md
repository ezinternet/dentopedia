## One-line Summary
Double-blind RCT (16 patients, 55 implants) in D3/D4 bone — osseodensification produced higher insertion torque (39.0 ± 6.4 vs 32.0 ± 3.4 Ncm, p < 0.001) than standard undersized drilling but identical ISQ at placement and at 6 months, with equivalent survival.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Double-blind, randomized clinical trial (RCT)
- **Journal**: Applied Sciences 2021;11:640 (MDPI, open access)
- **DOI**: 10.3390/app11020640
- **Sample**: 16 patients with D3 or D4 bone (Misch classification); G1 osseodensification n = 29 implants, G2 standard undersized drilling n = 26 implants
- **Primary Question**: In low-quality bone, does osseodensification with a wider osteotomy (healing-chamber concept) maintain primary stability and 6-month survival vs the standard undersized drilling protocol?

## 2. Key Contributions
- First double-blind RCT directly contrasting OD (healing-chamber preparation) with standard undersized drilling in clinically diagnosed D3/D4 bone.
- Insertion torque significantly higher with OD: 39.0 ± 6.4 Ncm (G1) vs 32.0 ± 3.4 Ncm (G2), p < 0.001.
- ISQ at placement equivalent: 67.1 ± 3.2 (G1) vs 65.5 ± 2.7 (G2), p > 0.05.
- ISQ at 6 months equivalent: 74.0 ± 3.6 (G1) vs 73.3 ± 3.2 (G2), p > 0.05 — both groups gained significant ISQ from placement to 6 months (p < 0.001 within-group).
- Survival equivalent at 6 months (p > 0.05).
- Demonstrates the bone "healing chamber" can be created by OD (wider osteotomy) without sacrificing primary stability or 6-month osseointegration in low-quality bone.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Sixteen subjects with D3 or D4 bone density classified by Misch were enrolled and randomized to receive implants prepared either by OD (G1, n = 29 implants) using Densah burs in CCW with an osteotomy wider than the implant core diameter (healing-chamber concept), or by standard undersized drilling (G2, n = 26 implants) where the final drill diameter is narrower than the implant thread to maximize friction-fit. Double-blinded (patient and outcome assessor); the surgeon could not be blinded due to drill differences. Outcomes: insertion torque (IT, Ncm) and ISQ at placement, and ISQ + survival at 6 months. Statistical comparison between groups by independent t-tests; within-group time effect by paired test (significance p < 0.05).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Insertion torque: G1 39.0 ± 6.4 Ncm vs G2 32.0 ± 3.4 Ncm, p < 0.001 (OD higher).
- ISQ at placement: G1 67.1 ± 3.2 vs G2 65.5 ± 2.7, p > 0.05 (no difference).
- ISQ at 6 months: G1 74.0 ± 3.6 vs G2 73.3 ± 3.2, p > 0.05 (no difference).
- Within-group ISQ increase from placement → 6 months: significant in both groups, p < 0.001.
- Survival at 6 months: equivalent between groups, p > 0.05 (exact n for failures not extractable from available text).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Only 16 patients despite 55 implants — clustering within-patient inflates apparent precision; the relevant n for survival analysis is closer to 16.
- 6-month follow-up only; MBL and long-term survival unreported.
- Surgeon unblinded by necessity (drill type visible).
- "Healing chamber" gap not directly measured radiographically/histologically; inferred from drilling geometry.
- Insertion torque difference (~7 Ncm) is statistically significant but small in absolute clinical terms; ISQ — the analog more closely linked to osseointegration outcome — was identical.
- Living-document note: authors frame OD as enabling the healing-chamber concept "without any reduction in implant stability." The data support equivalence rather than superiority on ISQ/survival; the higher IT may even be a counter-intended finding given the healing-chamber rationale (a chamber implies a gap, which should mean lower IT). This tension is worth keeping in mind.

## 7. Glossary
- **D3 / D4 bone**: Misch's clinical bone-density classification; D3 = thin cortical with fine trabecular, D4 = no cortical, fine trabecular (low-density, typical posterior maxilla).
- **Healing chamber / healing gap**: intentional space between implant body and osteotomy wall filled by coagulum to favor de novo bone formation away from the implant surface.
- **Insertion torque (IT)**: torque (Ncm) measured at final implant seating; proxy for primary mechanical stability.
- **ISQ**: Implant Stability Quotient (RFA-derived, 1–100), proxy for stability over time.
- **Osseodensification (OD)**: Densah-bur counterclockwise osteotomy that compacts rather than extracts bone.
- **Undersized drilling**: standard protocol where the final drill is narrower than the implant thread diameter to obtain friction-fit and high IT.
