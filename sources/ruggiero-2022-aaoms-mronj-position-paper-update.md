## One-line Summary
AAOMS 2022 MRONJ position paper update: staging system unchanged (0–3), drug holiday remains controversial with no AAOMS consensus, romosozumab added to at-risk medications, bone turnover markers (CTX) no longer recommended for risk assessment.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery 80:920-943, 2022
- **Organization**: American Association of Oral and Maxillofacial Surgeons (AAOMS)
- **Previous versions**: 2007, 2009, 2014 position papers
- **Document type**: Consensus/Position paper

## 2. Key Contributions
- Updates MRONJ definition, staging, risk estimates, prevention algorithms, and treatment guidelines
- Adds romosozumab (anti-sclerostin monoclonal antibody) to MRONJ-associated medications
- Clarifies drug holiday controversy: no AAOMS consensus; working group evenly split
- Removes bone turnover markers (CTX) as useful clinical decision tool
- Provides specific denosumab holiday timing: surgery at 3-4 months post-last dose, restart 6-8 weeks post-surgery
- Highlights rebound vertebral fracture risk on denosumab discontinuation

## 3. MRONJ Case Definition (unchanged from 2014)
All three criteria required:
1. Current or previous antiresorptive therapy (alone or combined with immune modulators/antiangiogenics)
2. Exposed bone or bone probeable through fistula in maxillofacial region, persisting >8 weeks
3. No history of radiation therapy to jaws; no metastatic disease to jaws

## 4. Staging System
- **At-risk**: Asymptomatic, no necrotic bone, on antiresorptive therapy
- **Stage 0** (non-exposed bone variant): No clinical necrosis but nonspecific symptoms/radiographic findings (alveolar bone loss, sclerosis, thickened PDL)
- **Stage 1**: Exposed/necrotic bone or probeable fistula; asymptomatic, no infection
- **Stage 2**: Exposed/necrotic bone with infection/inflammation; symptomatic
- **Stage 3**: Stage 2 + one of: necrosis beyond alveolar bone, pathologic fracture, extraoral fistula, oral-antral/nasal communication, osteolysis to inferior border/sinus floor

## 5. Medications
- **Bisphosphonates**: Alendronate, risedronate, zoledronic acid, ibandronate (oral/IV)
- **Denosumab**: Prolia® (60 mg q6mo) for osteoporosis; Xgeva® (120 mg qmo) for malignant bone disease
- **Romosozumab** (new): Anti-sclerostin monoclonal antibody; increases bone formation + decreases resorption
- All three classes now considered MRONJ-associated

## 6. Drug Holiday Controversy
- No randomized evidence sufficient to support or refute drug holidays
- AAOMS working group evenly split; no consensus reached
- Key concern for denosumab: rebound vertebral fractures on discontinuation
- **Practical guidance if holiday chosen**:
  - Denosumab: surgery at 3–4 months after last dose (waning osteoclast inhibition); restart 6–8 weeks post-surgery
  - This minimizes holiday length while preserving bone healing window
- Bone turnover markers (CTX): no longer validated for clinical decision-making

## 7. Prevention Strategies
| Patient Group | Strategy |
|---|---|
| Pre-therapy, non-malignant | Educate on risk; optimize dental health concurrent with ARD |
| Pre-therapy, malignant | Optimize dental health BEFORE ARD if systemic conditions allow; extract non-restorable teeth |
| During ARD, non-malignant | No routine alteration; consider drug schedule, comorbidities, surgery extent |
| During ARD, malignant | Avoid dentoalveolar surgery if possible; root retention preferred; implants contraindicated |

- Regimented dental surveillance → 2.5× reduction in relative MRONJ risk (prostate cancer, prospective study)
- Periodic dental prophylaxis, caries control, non-operative endodontics essential throughout therapy

## 8. Causality / Differential Diagnosis
Common MRONJ mimics: alveolar osteitis, sinusitis, periodontitis, periapical pathology, chronic sclerosing osteomyelitis, fibro-osseous lesions, sarcoma, TMJ disorders, atypical neuralgias

## 9. Limitations
- Narrative/consensus process; evidence base for some recommendations remains weak
- Drug holiday section limited by absence of RCT data

## 10. Glossary
- **CTX**: C-terminal telopeptide of type I collagen (bone resorption marker, no longer used for MRONJ risk stratification per AAOMS 2022)
- **Romosozumab**: Sclerostin inhibitor (Evenity®); monthly SC injection; approved for osteoporosis
- **SRE**: Skeletal-Related Event (pathologic fracture, spinal cord compression, hypercalcemia)
