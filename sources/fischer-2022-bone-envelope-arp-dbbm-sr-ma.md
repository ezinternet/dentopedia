## Why Ingested

Quantifies the residual bone envelope (width/height) achievable after ARP with DBBM — directly answers the clinical question "can we place standard-diameter implants without additional augmentation?" after socket preservation. Provides probability figures (6–19%) for implant placement with ≥2 mm surrounding bone, which is crucial context for [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/scheyer-2016-alveolar-ridge-preservation-multicentre-rct]] and other ARP protocol pages.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 9 RCTs (177 ARP vs 130 SH implants): DBBM-based ARP gains 1.13 mm ridge width on average, but only 6–19% of cases achieve ≥2 mm circumferential bone to avoid all further augmentation.

## 한줄요약

9개 RCT 메타분석: DBBM 치조골보존술은 평균 1.13 mm 폭 유지 이점을 주나, 추가 골증대 없이 표준 직경 임플란트 식립 가능한 경우는 6–19%에 불과.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Bone envelope for implant placement after alveolar ridge preservation: a systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Authors**: Kai R. Fischer, Alex Solderer, Kristina Arlt, Christian Heumann, Chun Ching Liu, Patrick R. Schmidlin
- **Year**: 2022
- **Journal**: International Journal of Implant Dentistry
- **DOI**: 10.1186/s40729-022-00453-z
- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (SR+MA)
- **Institution**: University of Zurich, Center of Dental Medicine, Switzerland

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA to frame ARP outcomes as a **probability of implant placement without additional GBR**, rather than merely reporting dimensional changes.
- Calculated NNT (number needed to treat) for implant placement with 2 mm surrounding bone: 3.25 mm implant: 19%, 4.0 mm implant: 14%, 5.0 mm implant: 6%.
- Mean ridge width difference of **1.13 mm** in favour of ARP with DBBM (95% CI 0.28–1.98, I² = 68%, p < 0.01).
- Reframes the clinical message: while ARP reduces shrinkage, **4 out of 5 cases** still require no ARP or still need additional augmentation at implant placement.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases**: PubMed, Embase, CENTRAL (2013–August 2022)
- **PICO**: P = patients with tooth extraction; I = ARP with (collagenated) DBBM ± membrane; C = spontaneous healing (SH); O = probability of implant placement without additional GBR (2 mm bone housing)
- **Inclusion**: RCTs, ≥10 participants, test = DBBM ± membrane, control = no grafting
- **Exclusion**: Animal studies, non-RCT, non-DBBM grafts, no SH control
- **Studies included**: 9 RCTs, total 177 DBBM-group implants / 130 SH-group implants
- **Statistical approach**: Random-effects meta-analysis; mean differences with 95% CI; I² for heterogeneity; probability (risk difference, RD) calculations for each implant diameter
- **PRISMA** guidelines followed; initial search identified 2583 studies

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Implant diameter | Probability (no further augmentation) | RD (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3.25 mm | 19% | 0.19 (0.06–0.32) | <0.01 |
| 4.0 mm | 14% | 0.14 (0.05–0.23) | <0.01 |
| 5.0 mm | 6% | 0.06 (0.00–0.12) | 0.06 |

- **Ridge width gain**: Mean difference 1.13 mm (95% CI 0.28–1.98), I² = 68%
- **Heterogeneity** was substantial (I² = 68%), reflecting variation in DBBM formulations, membrane use, healing times, and measurement methods
- **Clinical bottom line**: ARP with DBBM is beneficial but insufficient to avoid additional augmentation in ~80–94% of cases depending on implant diameter

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- High heterogeneity (I² = 68%) limits meta-analytic precision
- Included studies varied in healing time, DBBM type (with/without collagen), membrane use, and measurement method
- No standardized protocol for determining "adequate bone housing" clinically
- Lack of data on vertical bone dimension and soft tissue outcomes
- Long-term implant survival after ARP not addressed

## 6. Related Work

- Schropp et al. (2003): Horizontal bone loss 5–7 mm within 12 months post-extraction (~50% original width) — the landmark resorption data motivating ARP
- Bundle bone physiology: Inevitable post-extraction resorption of the buccal wall due to loss of periodontal attachment function
- GBR outcomes at the time of implant placement when ARP insufficient: standard staged protocols remain necessary in most cases

## 7. Glossary

- **ARP (Alveolar Ridge Preservation)**: 치조골보존술 — socket grafting at extraction to limit post-extraction resorption
- **DBBM (Deproteinized Bovine Bone Mineral)**: 탈단백 우골 — xenograft bone substitute (e.g., Bio-Oss) composed of bovine bone with organic components removed
- **SH (Spontaneous Healing)**: 자연치유 — socket healing without grafting; control condition
- **NNT (Number Needed to Treat)**: 치료에 필요한 수 — number of patients needing treatment for one additional success
- **RD (Risk Difference)**: 위험차 — absolute difference in proportion of successes between treatment and control
- **Bone envelope**: 골외피 — the bony housing around a placed implant; requires ≥1–2 mm thickness circumferentially for osseointegration
- **I²**: 이질성 지수 — measure of statistical heterogeneity in meta-analysis; 68% = substantial
