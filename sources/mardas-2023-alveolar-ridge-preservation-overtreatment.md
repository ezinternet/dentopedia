## One-line Summary
Critical review (Periodontology 2000, open access) examining the four conventional objectives of **alveolar ridge preservation (ARP)** — limiting dimensional change, promoting bone formation, preserving soft tissue/contour, and enabling implant placement without additional augmentation — and concluding that ARP is clinically valid and safe but is not universally indicated; in selected scenarios (thick walls, posterior sites, no esthetic demand) unassisted socket healing yields comparable outcomes and ARP can constitute overtreatment.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Periodontology 2000 2023;93(1):289–308
- **Pages**: 289–308 (~20 pages; full review with figures and tables)
- **Type**: Open-access narrative/critical review by EFP-affiliated authors (Queen Mary Univ. of London; Defence Dental Services UK; Univ. of Zurich)
- **Study type**: Critical narrative review — synthesizes systematic reviews, meta-analyses, RCTs, and clinical/anatomical evidence

## 2. Key Contributions
- Frames the ARP debate as a question of **clinical scenarios** rather than a binary "yes/no" recommendation.
- Reviews evidence for each of the four canonical ARP objectives separately, identifying which are robustly supported and which are weak.
- Identifies the **anterior maxilla with a thin buccal wall (bundle bone, <1 mm)** as the strongest indication for ARP and the **posterior with intact thick walls** as the weakest.
- Surveys ARP **technique categories** — socket grafts (particulate graft alone or with biologics) vs **socket sealing** (barrier membrane GBR or soft-tissue grafts) — and the variable outcomes between them.
- Provides a decision framework for when **unassisted socket healing** is sufficient versus when ARP adds meaningful benefit over and above the cost, morbidity, and complexity for the patient.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Critical narrative review of postextraction healing biology and ARP evidence.
- Sections include: postextraction healing biology, dimensional change after extraction (with anatomic/tooth-type modifiers), four ARP objectives, technique categories (socket graft vs socket sealing), evidence per objective, discussion of overtreatment risk.
- Draws on prior SRs and meta-analyses for quantitative anchors (e.g., Tan 2012, Avila-Ortiz 2014, Couso-Queiruga 2021).
- No new primary data; no PRISMA workflow declared.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Postextraction resorption is significant regardless of management**, but greatest at the buccal aspect of the anterior maxilla due to bundle bone.
- **ARP reduces but does not eliminate** dimensional change; horizontal width loss is reduced by ~1.5–2 mm and vertical mid-buccal loss by ~1–1.5 mm versus unassisted healing in pooled SRs (consistent with Avila-Ortiz 2014, Couso-Queiruga 2021).
- **New bone formation within the socket** is not consistently increased by ARP; many graft materials show residual particles and may delay or reduce vital bone fill versus unassisted healing.
- **Soft-tissue/contour preservation** is improved by socket-sealing techniques (especially soft tissue graft over socket).
- **Implant placement without additional augmentation**: ARP increases the chance of straightforward implant placement only in cases where unassisted healing would have produced a deficient ridge; in posterior sites with thick walls the difference is small.
- **Overtreatment scenarios** identified: posterior sites with thick walls and adequate vertical bone, sites planned for immediate implant placement, sites where prosthetic plan already requires staged augmentation.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative review, no quantitative pooling — relies on prior SRs.
- Heterogeneity in ARP technique definitions limits direct comparison.
- Long-term (>5 year) data on implants placed into ARP-treated sites are limited.
- Cost-effectiveness analyses largely absent — true overtreatment judgment requires economic and morbidity data.
- Calls for studies stratified by tooth type, buccal wall thickness, and prosthetic plan.

## 7. Glossary
- **ARP (Alveolar Ridge Preservation)**: any procedure aiming to limit postextraction dimensional change and promote bone formation in the socket.
- **Socket graft**: ARP using particulate bone graft or substitute alone or with biologics; the socket is filled with graft material.
- **Socket sealing (SS)**: ARP using a barrier membrane (GBR) or soft-tissue graft to seal the socket entrance, with or without bone graft.
- **Bundle bone**: PDL-attachment bone lining the inner socket; vanishes after extraction and drives buccal collapse.
- **Unassisted socket healing**: extraction alone with primary blood clot, no graft or membrane.
- **Overtreatment**: a procedure whose added benefit does not justify added cost, morbidity, or complexity in a given clinical scenario.
