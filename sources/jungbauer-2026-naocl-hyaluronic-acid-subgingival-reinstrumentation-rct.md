## Why Ingested

기존 [[periodontics/khattri-2020-adjunctive-systemic-antimicrobials-non-surgical-treatment]]는 비외과적 치주치료에 전신 항균제를 보조 사용했을 때의 근거를 다루지만, 보조제별 권고가 불충분하다는 한계가 있다. 본 RCT (Jungbauer 2026)는 SPC(유지치료) 단계의 잔존 포켓에 대한 **재기구조작(subgingival re-instrumentation, SRI)** 에 국소 보조제(아미노산-차아염소산나트륨 AA-NaOCl + 가교 히알루론산 xHA, "clean and seal")를 더했을 때의 추가 임상·미생물 효과를 직접 측정해, 국소 보조제 근거를 보강한다. 깊은 포켓에서 효과가 더 크다는 임상 의사결정 신호를 제공.

## One-line Summary

Single-center RCT (n=42; 21/group) — adjunctive amino-acid NaOCl + cross-linked HA ("clean and seal") added to subgingival re-instrumentation (SRI) of residual pockets in SPC patients yielded an extra 0.50 mm PD reduction and 0.57 mm CAL gain at 6 months, with 88.1% vs 38.1% pocket closure and significant reduction of 5/8 periopathobionts; effect greatest in deeper pockets.

## 한줄요약

단일기관 RCT (환자 42명, 군당 21명) — 유지치료(SPC) 단계 잔존 포켓의 재기구조작(SRI)에 아미노산-차아염소산나트륨(AA-NaOCl) + 가교 히알루론산(xHA, "clean and seal")을 보조로 추가하니 6개월 시점에 PD가 0.50 mm 더 감소하고 CAL이 0.57 mm 더 회복, 포켓 폐쇄율 88.1% 대 38.1%, 8개 중 5개 치주병원균 유의 감소했고, 깊은 포켓일수록 효과가 컸다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Randomized controlled trial (single-center, prospective, 2 parallel arms, 1:1 block randomization)
- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health 2026;26(1)
- **DOI**: [10.1186/s12903-026-08694-9](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12903-026-08694-9)
- **PMID**: 42218449 · **PMCID**: PMC13262014
- **Trial registration**: German Clinical Trials Register DRKS00017415
- **Source**: PubMed Central full text (according to PubMed)
- **Setting**: Private dental practice, Germany; Feb 2022–Dec 2023

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First clinical trial (per authors) to evaluate the change of the **subgingival microbiota** after the "clean and seal" protocol (AA-NaOCl + xHA) applied specifically during **subgingival re-instrumentation (SRI)** in supportive periodontal care (SPC), not during initial therapy.
2. Demonstrates a measurable additional clinical benefit (ΔPD, ΔCAL, pocket closure) of the local adjunct over SRI alone in previously-treated residual pockets — a setting where adjunct evidence had been negative in a prior systematic review.
3. Shows the benefit is **PD-dependent** (greater in deeper pockets), supporting selective use rather than blanket application.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Participants**: periodontitis stage III/IV, grade B/C; post-active therapy; ≥16 teeth; ≥40 y; 2–8 residual sites (PD ≥5 mm, or PD ≥4 mm + BOP+); API ≤35%. Excluded: systemic-disease periodontitis, immunosuppression, recent antibiotics, pregnancy/lactation, cancer, >5 cig/day, allergy, special diets.
- **n**: 77 screened → 44 randomized (22/arm) → 1 dropout/arm → **42 analyzed (21 control / 21 test)**. 71% female both groups.
- **Per patient 2 study sites** = the two non-adjacent highest-PD pockets (more distal chosen if tied).
- **Both groups**: SPC session (oral hygiene re-instruction, supragingival erythritol air polishing, air-scaler) + SRI under LA with Gracey curettes + air scaler + erythritol air polishing, saline rinse.
- **Test group adds** ("clean and seal"): freshly mixed AA-NaOCl gel (Perisolv) instilled 60 s before instrumentation, at hand-curette→air-scaler transition, and after mechanical cleaning; saline rinse; then xHA gel (hyaDent BG) applied, **re-applied subgingivally after 1 week**.
- **Retreatment**: persisting pockets re-instrumented per allocated protocol at T1 (3 mo).
- **Timepoints**: T0 baseline, T1 = 3 mo, T2 = 6 mo. PD/CAL with UNC15 probe (intra-rater ICC 0.8).
- **Biomarkers**: IL-1β, MMP-8 by ELISA from GCF (Periopaper). **Microbiology**: 8 periopathobionts by qPCR (log10 counts/sample) from paper points. Lab analyst blinded.
- **Stats**: a priori power ≥80% for 1 mm ΔPD at T2; Friedman (intragroup), Mann–Whitney U + Bonferroni (intergroup), Pearson Chi-square (nominal); p<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Probing depth (PD, mm):**

| | Baseline | T1 (3 mo) | T2 (6 mo) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Control | 4.93 (±1.05) | 4.26 (±1.04) | 4.05 (±0.96) |
| Test | 4.69 (±0.87) | 3.60 (±0.73) | 3.31 (±0.72) |

- **Additional ΔPD (test vs control)**: +0.43 mm at T1 (test 1.10 [95% CI 0.87–1.32] vs control 0.67 [0.46–0.87]; p=0.007); **+0.50 mm at T2** (test 1.38 [1.11–1.65] vs control 0.88 [0.62–1.14]; p=0.008).
- **Additional ΔCAL at T2**: **+0.57 mm** (test 1.07 [0.70–1.44] vs control 0.50 [0.24–0.76]; p=0.017).
- **BOP+ reduction at study sites by T2**: 78.6% (test) vs 19.0% (control), intergroup p<0.001.
- **Pocket closure (PD ≤4 mm, BOP-) at 6 mo**: residual pockets decreased **88.1% (n=37, test) vs 38.1% (n=16, control)**, intergroup p<0.001.
- **Immunology**: no significant IL-1β or MMP-8 change at any timepoint, no intergroup difference.
- **Microbiology**: significant decrease of **5 of 8 periopathobionts in the test group only** (including A. actinomycetemcomitans, a negative predictive factor); control counts essentially stable.
- No adverse effects.

**Benchmark context (from discussion):** AA-NaOCl alone ~+0.21–0.31 mm ΔPD; HA alone ~+0.2 mm; sustained-release CHX/tetracycline fibres +0.6–0.7 mm; flapless EMD +0.79 mm. During **initial** therapy (SI) AA-NaOCl+xHA added 1.1 mm (4–6 mm pockets) / 2.1 mm (≥7 mm) — larger than the SRI effect here because SRI sites are previously-treated non-responders.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Treatment blinding impossible (NaOCl taste/smell); only lab analyst blinded.
- Single center, single experienced operator; limited n (21/group).
- Only the 2 highest-PD pockets per patient treated/included (balances sites and avoids overtreatment, but lowers number of analyzed sites and limits confounder assessment — initial PD, root number, furcation, defect angulation).
- Course of retreatment within systematic therapy not differentiated.
- Larger trials with longer follow-up needed.

## 7. Glossary

- **SRI** — Subgingival re-instrumentation: repeated subgingival debridement of residual pockets during supportive care.
- **SPC** — Supportive periodontal care (step 4 of EFP S3 guideline; periodontal maintenance).
- **AA-NaOCl** — Amino-acid–buffered sodium hypochlorite gel (Perisolv); biofilm/matrix-degrading antimicrobial.
- **xHA** — Cross-linked hyaluronic acid gel (hyaDent BG); blood-clot stabilization, wound healing.
- **"Clean and seal"** — Protocol pairing AA-NaOCl (bacterial clearance) + xHA (clot stabilization).
- **PD / CAL / BOP** — Probing depth / clinical attachment level / bleeding on probing.
- **Pocket closure** — PD ≤4 mm and BOP-negative.
