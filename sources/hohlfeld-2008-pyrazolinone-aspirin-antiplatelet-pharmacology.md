## One-line Summary
Human platelet in vitro study demonstrating that dipyrone (metamizole) and other pyrazolinones — via their active metabolite 4-methylaminoantipyrine (MAA) — block aspirin's COX-1 acetylation similarly to ibuprofen, through a hydrogen-bond interaction with serine-530.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Thrombosis and Haemostasis 2008;6:166–73
- **DOI**: 10.1111/j.1538-7836.2007.02786.x
- **Institution**: Institut für Pharmakologie und Klinische Pharmakologie, Heinrich-Heine-Universität Düsseldorf, Germany
- **Study type**: In vitro (human platelet-rich plasma); supplemented by computerized docking
- **Received**: 17 August 2007; Accepted: 4 October 2007

## 2. Key Contributions
- First study to demonstrate that dipyrone (INN: metamizol) interferes with the antiplatelet effect of aspirin
- Identified MAA (4-methylaminoantipyrine), the active metabolite of dipyrone, as the responsible agent
- Showed class effect: other pyrazolinone derivatives produce the same interference
- Computational docking confirmed MAA forms a strong hydrogen bond with serine-530 of COX-1, blocking aspirin acetylation — same mechanism as ibuprofen
- Challenged earlier hypothesis that dipyrone acts by a COX-independent mechanism and preferentially inhibits COX-2

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Platelet preparation**: Human platelet-rich plasma (PRP) from healthy volunteers
- **Aggregation assay**: Arachidonic acid (AA)- and collagen-induced platelet aggregation in PRP
- **Thromboxane measurement**: TXB2 formation in PRP
- **P-selectin expression**: Flow cytometry (marker of platelet activation/secretion)
- **Cell-free COX enzyme assay**: Luminol-enhanced luminescence of human platelet microsomes (quantification of COX-1 activity)
- **Computational docking**: Based on published crystal structure of COX-1; modeled binding of MAA, aspirin, and ibuprofen
- **Compounds tested**: Dipyrone metabolite MAA, additional pyrazolinone derivatives, ibuprofen (positive control), naproxen (positive control)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **MAA (dipyrone metabolite)**: Largely attenuated or completely abolished AA-induced platelet aggregation inhibition by aspirin
- **MAA effect on TXB2**: Preserved thromboxane synthesis that aspirin would otherwise suppress
- **MAA effect on P-selectin**: Prevented aspirin-induced reduction in platelet P-selectin expression (platelet activation marker)
- **Other pyrazolinones**: Similar results to MAA — all interfere with aspirin
- **Ibuprofen and naproxen**: Similar interference as positive controls (consistent with Catella-Lawson 2001)
- **COX enzyme activity**: MAA attenuated aspirin's effect on platelet microsomal COX activity, confirming competition at the enzyme level
- **Docking result**: MAA forms a **strong hydrogen bond with serine-530** of COX-1, thereby occupying the same binding site as aspirin and preventing acetylation
- Clinical implication: dipyrone (metamizole), widely used as an analgesic in Europe and parts of Asia, should be considered an aspirin antagonist in cardiovascular patients

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- In vitro model; plasma concentrations of MAA used were based on therapeutic doses but may not fully reflect in vivo pharmacokinetics
- Does not address whether the interaction occurs in vivo in humans at standard clinical doses
- Only active metabolite (MAA) tested; prodrug pharmacokinetics not directly evaluated
- No clinical outcome data (cardiovascular events)

## 6. Related Work
- Catella-Lawson 2001 (NEJM): established ibuprofen–aspirin interaction in humans; rofecoxib/diclofenac safe
- Renda 2006 (Clin Pharmacol Ther): confirmed in OA+IHD patients; celecoxib safe
- Yokoyama 2013: in vitro 9-NSAID screen; ibuprofen and mefenamic acid confirmed; diclofenac no effect

## 7. Glossary
- **Dipyrone (metamizole)**: Pyrazolinone analgesic/antipyretic prodrug; widely used outside the US; marketed as Novalgin, Analgin, Metamizol
- **MAA (4-methylaminoantipyrine)**: Primary active metabolite of dipyrone responsible for analgesia and, as shown here, COX-1 interaction
- **Pyrazolinone**: Chemical class including dipyrone, aminopyrine, antipyrine; distinct structural class from traditional NSAIDs
- **P-selectin**: Platelet alpha-granule membrane protein; surface expression indicates platelet activation
- **Serine-530**: The serine residue in COX-1 acetylated by aspirin (equivalent to serine-529 in some numbering schemes)
- **Hydrogen bond with Ser-530**: MAA's mechanism of occupying the COX-1 active site, preventing aspirin's covalent acetylation
