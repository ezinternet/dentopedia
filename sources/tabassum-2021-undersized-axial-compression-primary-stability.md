## Why Ingested

저밀도골 일차안정성 확보를 위한 osteotomy 변형 술식 근거를 보강하기 위해 인제스트. 기존 [[implants/yu-2022-primary-implant-stability-alternative-site-preparation-sr-ma]] 와 [[implants/slete-2018-histomorphometric-comparison-three-osteotomy-techniques]] 가 다룬 undersized/대체 osteotomy 효과를, 측방압축(undersized)에 더해 축방향압축(under-drilling depth)을 결합한 실험으로 확장한다.

## One-line Summary

Cadaver-goat experimental study (44 implants, 4 surgical approaches) showing that combining lateral (undersized) and axial (under-depth) bone compression significantly raises insertion torque and %BIC vs standard drilling, proposed as an alternative for low-density bone.

## 한줄요약

염소 사체 실험(임플란트 44개, 4개 술식)에서 측방압축(언더사이즈)과 축방향압축(식립 깊이 미달 드릴링)을 결합하면 표준 드릴링 대비 식립 토크와 골-임플란트 접촉률(BIC)이 유의하게 증가 — 저밀도골 대체 술식으로 제안.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Separates and then combines two distinct compression vectors: lateral (undersized diameter) and axial (drilling shorter than implant length).
- Quantifies effect on insertion torque, removal torque, %BIC, and micro-CT bone volume.
- Provides histologic/micro-CT evidence of where bone densification concentrates (inner zone adjacent to implant).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Experimental ex vivo study in 4 cadaver goat femoral condyles.
- 44 dental implants (Dyna Ø4.2 mm, length 10 mm), n=11 per approach.
- Approach-1: standard (10 mm depth, 4 mm diameter). Approach-2: under-depth (8 mm depth, 4 mm). Approach-3: full depth, undersized diameter. Approach-4: under-depth + undersized (8 mm depth, 3.6 mm).
- Outcomes: insertion torque (n=11), removal torque (n=7), %BIC (n=4), bone architecture by micro-CT + histology (n=4).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Insertion torque: approaches 2, 3, 4 significantly higher than approach 1 (P<0.05).
- %BIC: approaches 3 and 4 significantly higher than approaches 1 and 2 (P<0.05).
- %Bone volume: approach 2 significantly higher than approach 1 in the inner zone adjacent to the implant (P<0.05).
- Conclusion: lateral + axial compression improves primary stability; candidate alternative for low-density bone.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Ex vivo cadaver-goat model — no healing/osseointegration data; primary stability only, not secondary.
- Small histology/BIC subsamples (n=4); no clinical survival outcome.
- Authors explicitly call for in vivo confirmation.

## 6. Related Work
- Undersized osteotomy literature (Sennerby & Roos; Buchter et al.).
- Micromotion threshold (Meredith 1998) for fibrous encapsulation.

## 7. Glossary
- %BIC: bone-to-implant contact percentage.
- Axial compression: not drilling to full implant length, compacting bone apically.
- Lateral compression: undersized osteotomy diameter, compacting bone circumferentially.
