## Why Ingested

[[wiki/bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/bassir-2018-alveolar-ridge-preservation-meta-analysis]]는 효과량을 conventional + radiographic 측정 혼합으로 pooling했으나, 본 SR+MA (Pickert 2022)는 CBCT 측정만으로 한정해 측정-이질성을 제거하고 graft 재료군(xenogenic vs allogenic) 간 직접 비교를 제공한다 — anchor의 "어느 재료가 우월한가" 미해결 질문을 CBCT subgroup 근거로 보강.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (16 qualitative / 9 quantitative RCTs, CBCT-only) showing xenogenic graft materials produced significantly less vertical and horizontal ridge reduction than allogenic in alveolar ridge preservation, though no material fully prevented dimensional loss.

## 한줄요약

CBCT 측정만 모은 SR+MA(정성 16편·정량 9편)로, 치조제보존술에서 xenograft가 allograft보다 수직·수평 골 흡수를 유의하게 덜 일으켰으나(P<0.05) 어떤 재료도 치조제 치수 손실을 완전히 막지는 못했다.

## 1. Document Information

- Journal: Journal of Periodontal & Implant Science (JPIS), 2022 Feb;52(1):3-27
- DOI: 10.5051/jpis.2007100355
- Design: Systematic review and meta-analysis (PRISMA), RCTs only, CBCT-only measurement
- Search: MEDLINE + Cochrane CENTRAL, Nov 2019–Jan 2020
- Yield: 1,582 records → 16 studies qualitative, 9 studies quantitative

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA restricting included data to **CBCT radiographic measurements only**, eliminating the measurement heterogeneity (periapical/cephalometric/cast/probe) that confounds prior reviews.
- Subgroup quantitative comparison by graft origin: xenogenic vs allogenic (autogenic insufficient for pooling).
- Reports outcomes at standardized levels: vertical midbuccal & midpalatal/midlingual; horizontal width at 1, 3, 5 mm from initial crest.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PICO: healthy adults receiving ARP after atraumatic permanent tooth extraction; intervention = socket fill with autogenic/allogenic/xenogenic material (± membrane/soft-tissue graft); comparison = different graft materials; outcome = CBCT-measured dimensional change.
- Inclusion: RCTs, English, ≥1 CBCT-measured outcome, follow-up ≥3 months.
- Exclusion: editorials/reviews/case reports/series, no CBCT, third-molar-only sites, immediate-implant-for-ARP, <3-month follow-up.
- 3-stage independent dual screening with senior-reviewer arbitration.
- Effect measure: weighted mean difference (WMD) with standard error (SE).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Vertical midbuccal**: xenogenic WMD = −0.20 (SE 0.26) vs allogenic WMD = −0.90 (SE 0.22) — xenogenic less reduction (P<0.05).
- **Horizontal at 1 mm from crest**: xenogenic WMD = −1.32 (SE 0.07) vs allogenic WMD = −2.99 (SE 0.96).
- **Horizontal at 3 mm**: xenogenic WMD = −0.78 (SE 0.11) vs allogenic WMD = −1.63 (SE 0.40).
- Autogenic subgroup: insufficiently reported, no statistics performed.
- Background benchmark cited: ARP yields ~1.47 mm vertical and ~1.83 mm horizontal less reduction than unassisted healing.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small number of poolable studies (9), heterogeneity in membrane use, follow-up timing, and surgical approach.
- No autogenic quantitative arm.
- CBCT-only filter improves measurement consistency but reduces the evidence pool.
- No clinical/implant-survival endpoints; purely dimensional radiographic outcomes.

## 6. Related Work

- Builds on broader ARP meta-analyses (Bassir 2018, Avila-Ortiz 2019, Couso-Queiruga 2021) that pooled mixed measurement methods.
- Consistent with the consensus that ARP attenuates but does not abolish post-extraction atrophy.

## 7. Glossary

- **ARP** — Alveolar Ridge Preservation (치조제보존술)
- **CBCT** — Cone-Beam Computed Tomography (콘빔CT)
- **WMD** — Weighted Mean Difference (가중평균차)
- **Xenograft** — graft of animal (e.g., bovine) origin (이종골)
- **Allograft** — graft of human donor origin (동종골)
