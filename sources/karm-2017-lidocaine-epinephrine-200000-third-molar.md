## One-line Summary
Korean-language KDA Journal introduction of a multicenter, randomized, double-blind, crossover phase IV RCT (NCT02696369, n=65 enrolled / 51 analyzed) comparing 2% lidocaine + 1:80,000 vs 1:200,000 epinephrine for surgical extraction of bilateral impacted mandibular third molars — analgesic efficacy equivalent; hemodynamic safety (SBP, HR) significantly better with 1:200,000 epinephrine.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Demonstrates equipotency at lower epinephrine concentration**: Halving epinephrine from 1:80,000 to 1:200,000 in 2% lidocaine does not reduce analgesic efficacy for mandibular third molar surgery (VAS pain at extraction and 2/4/6 h post-op; onset, duration; intraoperative bleeding; operator/patient satisfaction; drug dosage — all non-significant).

2. **Quantifies hemodynamic advantage**: Changes in systolic blood pressure and heart rate after anesthetic administration significantly smaller with 1:200,000 (p ≤ 0.01).

3. **Practical recommendation**: For hemodynamically unstable patients (cardiovascular disease, hypertension) requiring third molar surgery, 2% lidocaine with 1:200,000 epinephrine is preferable to 1:80,000 — same anesthesia, lower cardiovascular load.

4. **Clarifies the role of the vasoconstrictor**: 1:200,000 epinephrine still provides the vasoconstriction needed for prolonged anesthesia and hemostasis without delivering the higher catecholamine load of 1:80,000.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Multicenter, randomized, double-blind, crossover (within-subject), phase IV trial.
- **Participants**: Healthy adults (ASA I–II, age ≥19) with bilateral impacted mandibular third molars confirmed on panoramic radiography; 65 enrolled (L80 n=34, L200 n=31), 51 analyzed (78.5% completion); 10 dropped to randomization errors, 4 ineligible.
- **Exclusion criteria**: lidocaine hypersensitivity, active infection/abscess at extraction site, bleeding disorder, hyperthyroidism, atherosclerosis, heart failure, seizure disorder, uncontrolled hypertension or diabetes, current vasoconstrictor/antidepressant/sedative/analgesic, anticoagulant/antiplatelet/steroid within 1 week, other concurrent surgery, pregnancy/lactation, prior oral surgery.
- **Intervention**: Each participant received 2% lidocaine + 1:80,000 epinephrine (L80) at one side and 2% lidocaine + 1:200,000 epinephrine (L200) at the contralateral side in two visits 1–4 weeks apart.
- **Primary outcome**: VAS pain immediately post-extraction.
- **Secondary outcomes**: onset and duration of analgesia, VAS at 2/4/6 h, time to first pain, intraoperative bleeding, operator and participant satisfaction, drug dose, adverse events, vital signs (BP/HR) for 70 min post-injection (q1 min for first 5 min, q3 min to 20 min, q5 min to 70 min).
- **Statistics**: Paired t-test / Wilcoxon signed rank / unpaired t-test as appropriate; α = 0.05; SPSS v21. Power calculation: α=0.025, β=0.1, power=90%, prior VAS difference 15 mm → 23/group needed; with 20% dropout, ≥29/group enrolled.
- **Ethics**: Helsinki Declaration; informed consent.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- All participants were healthy young adults (ASA I–II) — the population most needing the lower-epinephrine formulation (cardiovascular disease, uncontrolled HTN) was excluded by the eligibility criteria. The authors acknowledge the need for follow-up studies in cardiovascular patients but note the ethical complexity.
- Phase IV registration trial — primary mission was to confirm equivalence + safety, not to detect rare adverse events.
- Crossover design assumes no period effect; healing/sensitization between sides could bias second-visit measurements. Mitigated by 1–4 week washout but not eliminated.
- [claude해석] The KDA Journal article is a Korean-language summary; the original full data is in Karm MH et al. (J Dent Anesth Pain Med, 2017 — separate English publication).

## 7. Glossary

- **L80 / L200**: 2% lidocaine HCl with 1:80,000 / 1:200,000 epinephrine.
- **VAS (Visual Analogue Scale)**: 0–100 mm patient-reported pain score.
- **NCT (ClinicalTrials.gov registration number)**: NCT02696369.
- **ASA I/II**: American Society of Anesthesiologists physical status — healthy / mild systemic disease.
- **Crossover design**: each participant serves as own control, receiving both interventions in randomized sequence.
- **Hemodynamic instability**: clinically significant changes in BP/HR; high-risk groups include uncontrolled hypertension, ischemic heart disease, arrhythmia.
