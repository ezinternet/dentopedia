## Why Ingested

Extends the orthodontic water-flosser evidence base with a head-to-head split-mouth RCT pitting Super Floss against a Waterpik oral irrigator — a comparison the existing orthodontic RCT [[interdental-cleaning/tyler-2023-waterpik-manual-toothbrush-orthodontic-rct]] (Waterpik vs manual toothbrush) and SR+MA [[interdental-cleaning/yiamwattana-2025-oral-irrigator-vs-floss-orthodontic-sr-ma]] (oral irrigator vs floss) do not isolate at the single-device level with surface-by-surface granularity. Reinforces the broad equivalence finding while surfacing one site-specific water-flosser advantage (distal molar interproximal).

## One-line Summary

Single-blind split-mouth RCT (n=34 orthodontic patients) found Super Floss and a Waterpik water flosser equally effective at immediate plaque removal (both ~0.6→0.13 RMNPI, between-group p=0.951), with the water flosser marginally superior only at the hard-to-reach distal molar interproximal surface (MD −0.21, 95% CI −0.37 to −0.04, p=0.033).

## 2. Key Contributions

- First (per the authors) split-mouth RCT directly comparing **Super Floss** (Oral-B) against a **Waterpik cordless water flosser** for plaque removal in fixed-orthodontic patients, isolating the two interproximal aids on opposite sides of the same mouth.
- Demonstrates **equivalence in overall immediate plaque removal** (between-group mean-difference p=0.951) — both aids cut RMNPI plaque scores by roughly 4-fold in a single session.
- Identifies a **site-specific exception**: the water flosser outperformed Super Floss at the **distal interproximal surface of the molar** (a region poorly accessible to threaded floss behind brackets/wires), MD −0.21 (95% CI −0.37 to −0.04, p=0.033).
- Adds orthodontic-population evidence to the broader interdental-cleaning literature, where water-flosser-vs-floss comparisons are mostly in non-orthodontic patients.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: single-blind, randomized, controlled, parallel trial with **split-mouth** allocation — each patient received Super Floss on one randomly assigned side and the Waterpik water flosser on the other, so each subject served as their own control.
- **Participants**: young adults 18–35 y near the end of fixed orthodontic treatment; braces from right first molar to left first molar; pocket depth ≤3 mm; no flossing in the prior 24 h. **Exclusions**: systemic disease, craniofacial anomaly, periodontal problems, spacing/missing teeth in the examined arch, smokers. 62 screened → **34 enrolled** (equal M/F; mean age 23.7 ± 7.7 y).
- **Sample size**: GPower 3.1.9.4, effect size 1, α=5%, power 80% → minimum 34.
- **Outcome measure**: **Rustogi et al. modified Navy Plaque Index (RMNPI)**, scored 0 (absent) / 1 (present) per section; nine sections combined into marginal and proximal regions. Analysis focused on **proximal areas (A, D, C, F)**; sections G and H not recorded due to brackets. Canine, one premolar, and one molar evaluated per side.
- **Procedure (single visit)**: baseline RMNPI with WHO probe → standardized oral-hygiene instruction (modified Bass, soft-bristled brush, fluoridated toothpaste) → 2 min brushing + **2 min interproximal cleaning**. Waterpik tip at 90° to tooth at gingival margin; pressure 45–75 PSI (3.1–5.2 Bar), flow 8 oz/min (237 mL), 1200 pulses/min. Plaque re-measured **immediately after cleaning**.
- **Blinding/calibration**: two examiners, RMNPI calibration Cohen's kappa 0.8; examiners blinded to which floss type was used per side.
- **Statistics**: SPSS v22; normality (Kolmogorov–Smirnov, Shapiro–Wilk); paired t-tests before/after and between aids; p<0.05 significant.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Overall plaque reduction (both significant, p<0.001)**: Super Floss 0.56 ± 0.35 → 0.13 ± 0.26; Water flosser 0.61 ± 0.35 → 0.13 ± 0.28.
- **Between-group**: no significant difference in mean reduction (**p=0.951**); no baseline imbalance (preintervention p=0.379).
- **By tooth type**: both aids significantly reduced plaque (p<0.001) at canine, premolar, and molar. Mean differences trended higher for the water flosser at every tooth but were **not** statistically significant.
- **Surface-level exception**: comparable effect on mesial and distal interproximal surfaces of all teeth **EXCEPT** the **distal molar**, where the water flosser was significantly more effective — **MD −0.21, 95% CI −0.37 to −0.04, p=0.033**.
- **Split-mouth (side) analysis**: both interventions significantly reduced plaque on left and right sides with no significant between-group difference.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Immediate single-cleaning measurement only** — plaque scored right after one cleaning; no prolonged/longitudinal effect captured (an "instant-effect" study). **No gingival or bleeding-index outcome** was measured.
- Possible **Hawthorne effect** (patients aware of being observed clean better than usual).
- **Single-center, small sample** (n=34); authors note difficulty standardizing the two interventions.
- **Future work**: long-term effects, longitudinal bleeding index, larger multicenter sample, and water jet with ozonated water; further study of daily combined toothbrush + floss use.

## 6. Related Work

- **Orthodontic water-flosser RCT** — Tyler 2023 (Waterpik + manual toothbrush vs manual toothbrush alone). This paper instead isolates Waterpik vs Super Floss within the same mouth.
- **Orthodontic oral-irrigator SR+MA** — Yiamwattana 2025 (oral irrigator vs floss in orthodontic patients), into which this RCT's equivalence finding fits.
- **Non-orthodontic water-flosser-vs-floss** — Mohapatra 2024 (SR) and Mancinelli-Lyle 2024 (water flosser vs interdental brush RCT), broader device-comparison context.
- Cited supportive trials: Sharma et al. (water floss reduced bleeding on probing by 41.2% over 28 days; water > air floss); Goyal et al. (water flosser > super floss for gingivitis and bleeding).

## 7. Glossary

- **Super Floss (Oral-B)**: a pre-cut floss with three integrated segments — a **stiffened-end threader** (to position floss under orthodontic wires, bridges, or implants), a **spongy/fuzzy floss** middle segment (cleans wide gaps and around brackets), and a **regular floss** end (removes plaque under the gingival margin). Purpose-built for orthodontic appliances and fixed prostheses.
- **Water flosser / oral irrigator**: a device delivering a pulsating pressurized water jet to disrupt and flush interproximal/subgingival plaque (here a Waterpik Cordless Freedom).
- **RMNPI (Rustogi et al. modified Navy Plaque Index)**: site-based plaque index dividing each tooth surface into nine sections, scored present/absent, allowing separation of marginal vs proximal plaque.
- **Split-mouth design**: each patient receives both interventions, one per side, controlling for inter-patient variability.
- **Hawthorne effect**: behavior change (improved cleaning) caused by awareness of being observed in a study.
