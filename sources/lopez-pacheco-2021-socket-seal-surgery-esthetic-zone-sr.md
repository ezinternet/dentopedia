## One-line Summary
Int J Implant Dent 2021;7:13: SR+MA+TSA of 6 RCTs, 135 sockets esthetic zone — socket seal surgery (FGG, CM, CS, ADM, PTFEm); FGG without bone fill best height; CM vs FGG no clinical difference; TSA: insufficient data for definitive conclusions.

## 1. Key Results
- 6 RCTs; 135 sockets; esthetic zone; ≥3 months follow-up post-extraction
- Techniques: FGG (free gingival graft), CM (collagen matrix), CS (collagen sponge), ADM (acellular dermal matrix), PTFEm (PTFE membrane)
- FGG without bone fill: significant buccal (-1.42mm exp vs -0.01mm ctrl) and lingual bone height preservation
- CM vs FGG with bone filling: no clinical differences in dimensional bone changes
- CM vs CS: no differences in width or gingival thickness
- Meta-analysis CM vs FGG: trend for less horizontal reduction at −1, −3, −5mm in favor of FGG (NS)
- TSA: accrued data did not reach required information size → more evidence needed
- Cayetano Heredia Peruvian University

## 2. Clinical Implications
- Multiple socket seal surgery techniques are predictable for ridge preservation
- No single technique proven superior — FGG has best evidence for height but more morbidity
- Insufficient RCT data to make definitive recommendations; evidence base still growing
