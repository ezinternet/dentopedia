## One-line Summary

Review: the Gow-Gates mandibular nerve block (open-mouth, anesthetic deposited near the mandibular condyle neck close to the trigeminal nerve's exit from the foramen ovale) and the Akinosi-Vazirani closed-mouth block (anesthetic fills the pterygomandibular space) are reliable alternatives to standard IANB, particularly valuable when a patient has a history of IANB failure from anatomical variability or accessory innervation.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Describes the **Gow-Gates technique**: mouth open wide, anesthetic administered just anterior to the neck of the condyle, targeting the mandibular branch of the trigeminal nerve near its exit from the foramen ovale — i.e., blocking the nerve trunk higher/more proximally than a standard IANB.
- Describes the **Akinosi-Vazirani technique**: mouth closed, anesthetic deposited to fill the pterygomandibular space — useful when limited mouth opening precludes standard or Gow-Gates approaches.
- Explicitly frames both as **particularly advantageous for patients with a history of standard IANB failure due to anatomical variability or accessory innervation** — directly actionable for the clinical scenario in this cluster.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Narrative review of technique descriptions and prior clinical literature on the Gow-Gates and Akinosi-Vazirani blocks.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Both techniques are indicated for any mandibular-arch dental procedure.
- Both are framed as more reliable than standard IANB specifically in patients with anatomical variability or known accessory innervation.
- Having skill in both techniques increases a dentist's ability to consistently achieve successful mandibular anesthesia across patients.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative review — no pooled success-rate comparison (e.g., meta-analytic RR) between Gow-Gates/Akinosi-Vazirani and standard IANB is presented in the abstract.

## 7. Glossary

- **Gow-Gates technique**: an open-mouth mandibular nerve block targeting the trigeminal nerve near the condylar neck, close to its exit from the foramen ovale — blocks the nerve more proximally (and broadly, including accessory branches) than a standard IANB.
- **Akinosi-Vazirani technique**: a closed-mouth mandibular nerve block depositing anesthetic into the pterygomandibular space, useful for patients with limited mouth opening (e.g., trismus).
- **Foramen ovale**: skull base opening through which the mandibular division of the trigeminal nerve exits.
