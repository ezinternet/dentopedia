## One-line Summary
SR (12 studies, PRISMA): no statistically significant correlation between insertion torque and ISQ (r=0.366, p=0.079); IT and RFA measure independent aspects of primary stability — low certainty evidence.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Clinical Implant Dentistry and Related Research 2018;20(1):26–33
- DOI: 10.1111/cid.12565
- Published online: 01 December 2017
- Design: Systematic review (PRISMA)
- Databases: PubMed, Web of Science, SCOPUS, Cochrane Library, OVID, SciELO + manual search
- No language or year restrictions
- 12 studies included (from 2,017 identified)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Pooled Pearson r=0.366, p=0.079 — NOT statistically significant
- IT and RFA are independent, incomparable methods: each captures different biomechanical properties
- Certainty of evidence: LOW (downgraded for risk of bias and indirectness)
- Clinical recommendation: define ONE method per implant/patient; do not interchange or combine as equivalent measures
- Contrasts with Tisci 2026 (r=0.44, p<0.001 — moderate and significant) — likely reflects search date and included study differences

## 3. Methodology
PRISMA-compliant SR. Electronic databases: PubMed, Web of Science, SCOPUS, Cochrane, OVID, SciELO. Manual searches. No restrictions. Clinical trials reporting RFA (ISQ) and IT included. 3 independent researchers. 12 final studies. Pearson r pooled.

## 4. Key Results
- Pooled r: 0.366 (p=0.079, NS)
- Certainty: Low
- Conclusion: IT and RFA are independent measurement methods

## 5. Limitations
- Only 12 studies — smaller pool than later SRs
- Considerable methodological heterogeneity across included studies
- Search cutoff predates 2018 studies; Tisci 2026 (n=48 studies) supersedes

## 6. Related Work
- Tisci 2026 (larger SR+MA: r=0.44 significant — larger evidence base, same question)
- Konuklu 2026 (RCT: IT measured alongside ISQ trajectory at 5 protocols)
- Huang 2020 (17 ISQ influencing factors including bone quality and drilling technique)
