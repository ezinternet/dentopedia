## One-line Summary
Split-mouth in vivo study (30 paired implants): tapered implants achieved significantly higher ISQ, less postoperative pain, and better peri-implant health than cylindrical implants at all timepoints.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Cureus 14(10):e29675 (October 2022)
- DOI: 10.7759/cureus.29675
- Design: In vivo, split-mouth RCT, 30 patients (60 implants: 30 tapered vs 30 cylindrical)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Tapered > cylindrical ISQ at placement and through healing
- Lower postoperative pain (VAS) with tapered design
- Better peri-implant tissue health (BOP, probing depth) with tapered design

## 3. Methodology
- Split-mouth design: each patient received one tapered + one cylindrical implant (same site conditions)
- ISQ measured at placement (T0), 3 months (T1), 6 months (T2)
- Peri-implant parameters: bleeding on probing (BOP), probing depth, pain VAS
- Same diameter and length used within pairs

## 4. Key Results
- Tapered ISQ > cylindrical at all timepoints (p<0.05)
- Pain VAS significantly lower in tapered group
- BOP and probing depth better in tapered group
- No implant failures in either group (100% survival)

## 5. Limitations
- Single center, relatively small sample (30 pairs)
- Limited implant brand/system variety
- 6-month follow-up only
