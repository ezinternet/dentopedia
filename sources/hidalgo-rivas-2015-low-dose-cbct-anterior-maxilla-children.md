## One-line Summary

Phantom dosimetry + clinician IQ scoring: a paediatric anterior-maxilla CBCT low-dose protocol (DAP ≥127 mGy·cm², CNR ≥4.8) gives ~50% dose cut vs manufacturer.

## 한줄요약

## 2. Key Contributions

- Defines acceptable paediatric IQ threshold: DAP ≥127 mGy·cm² with PTFE contrast-to-noise ratio ≥4.8.
- Resulting low-dose protocol = up to 50% reduction vs manufacturer recommendation.
- Authors note it as the first study addressing dose optimization in paediatric clinical dental CBCT protocols.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Image-quality phantom to relate objective IQ to DAP across exposure settings; paediatric skull phantom in water bath scored by 8 clinicians on 9 IQ aspects (5-point scale) for impacted maxillary canine assessment.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Couples objective + subjective IQ to dose to justify a cautious 50%-reduced paediatric protocol for the anterior maxilla.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

Single CBCT machine; phantom (no in-vivo diagnostic accuracy); indication-specific (anterior maxilla/impacted canine).
