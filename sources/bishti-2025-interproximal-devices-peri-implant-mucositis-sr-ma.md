## Why Ingested

SR+MA (Bishti 2025, RWTH Aachen) on **self-performed interproximal cleaning** (dental floss vs interdental brush vs water flosser/oral irrigator ± CHX) **adjunctive to toothbrushing** around implant-supported restorations for managing peri-implant mucositis — the device-level home-care question that natural-tooth periodontal evidence has long answered but implant evidence had not. It complements [[implants/peri-implantitis/jepsen-2015-primary-prevention-periimplantitis-managing-mucositis]] (mucositis is the reversible precursor to peri-implantitis; primary prevention = patient-administered plaque control) by quantifying *which* interproximal aids actually move PI/BOP around implants, and pairs with the adjunctive-therapy framing of [[implants/peri-implantitis/mauriello-2026-peri-implant-mucositis-adjunctive-narrative-review]].

## One-line Summary

PRISMA SR+MA (792 records → 6 RCTs, only 2 meta-analysed for I²=68–98% heterogeneity): oral irrigators and interdental brushes showed higher but NOT significant improvement in peri-implant mucositis signs vs dental floss, and toothbrushing + water oral irrigator was NOT significantly better than toothbrushing alone for BOP/PI — all devices give some benefit but self-performed interproximal-hygiene evidence around implants remains limited.

## 한줄요약

PRISMA 체계적문헌고찰+메타분석(792편→6 RCT, 이질성 I²=68–98%로 2편만 메타분석): 구강세정기(water flosser)와 치간칫솔이 치실보다 임플란트 주위 점막염 개선이 더 컸으나 통계적으로 유의하지 않았고, 칫솔질+물 구강세정기가 칫솔질 단독 대비 BOP/PI에서 유의차 없음 — 모든 기구가 일부 효과는 있으나 임플란트 주위 자가 치간위생 근거는 여전히 제한적.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First SR+MA to restrict to RCTs/prospective trials of self-administered interproximal aids vs toothbrushing alone** around implants (prior reviews mixed device categories or pooled teeth + implants).
2. Maps the four home-care interproximal devices for implants: **dental floss, interproximal/interdental brush, oral irrigator with water (water flosser), oral irrigator with CHX**, against PI / GI / BOP / IL-6.
3. Bottom line: all devices give *some* benefit, but **no device proved superior** and **toothbrushing + water irrigator ≈ toothbrushing alone** in the pooled analysis — exposing how thin and heterogeneous the implant home-care evidence base is.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **PRISMA SR + Cochrane Handbook v6.2**; PubMed MEDLINE, Cochrane CENTRAL, Google Scholar gray literature, up to Feb 2024; no language/date restriction; manual reference screening.
- **PICO** — Population: implant-supported restorations with peri-implant mucositis; Intervention: homecare interproximal device; Comparison: manual/electric toothbrush alone; Outcome: PI, PD, BOP, interleukin.
- **Inclusion**: RCTs + prospective human studies, ≥10 patients, single/multi-unit implant restorations with mucositis. **Exclusion**: retrospective/case report/animal/in-vitro, removable implant restorations, peri-implantitis patients, teeth-and-implant-pooled data.
- **Quality**: Cochrane Collaboration tool (RCTs), ROBINS-I (non-randomized). **GRADE** for certainty.
- **Stats**: random-effects models, DerSimonian–Laird τ², I², 95% CI, forest plots, RevMan 5.4.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Flow**: 792 records (781 electronic + 11 manual) → 674 excluded on title → 118 abstracts → 24 full-text → **6 RCTs** extracted; only **2 eligible for meta-analysis** (heterogeneity).
- **Included RCTs** (2013–2024, all parallel-design): Almoharib (14 d), Bevilacqua (2 wk), Bunk (4/8/12 wk), Magnuson (30 d), Nwachukwu (3/6 mo), Tütüncüoğlu (2/4/12 wk). 248 participants, ages 23–89, single implant/patient. Four lacked a control → descriptive only.
- **Device counts**: water flosser n=4, interproximal brush n=4, dental floss n=4, oral irrigator + 0.06% CHX n=1.
- **Descriptive signals**: oral irrigators and interdental brushes → higher but **non-significant** improvement vs floss. Magnuson: water flosser 81.8% BOP reduction (2.45-fold, p=0.0018) vs floss 33.3% (NS). Bunk: water/CHX irrigation gave significantly fewer BOP-positive sites vs control at 12 wk (p=0.004); CHX vs water NS (p=0.16); CHX improved mucositis severity vs toothbrushing alone (p=0.001); water vs control severity drop NS (p=0.06).
- **Meta-analysis (2 studies, toothbrushing alone vs + water flosser)**:
  - **Modified PI**: no significant difference; mean difference 0.2 favoring control; I²=68%.
  - **BOP**: no significant difference; mean difference 0.24 favoring control; I²=98%.
- **Risk of bias**: every study ≥1 high/unclear domain; performance bias unclear in all (no blinding possible); ≥3 high attrition; all low selection bias.
- **GRADE**: modified PI = moderate; BOP = low (I²=98%). Short-term (≤3 mo) low; >6 mo moderate–high.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Limited evidence — some device categories represented by a single study; only 2 of 6 studies meta-analysable.
- Substantial heterogeneity (I² up to 98%); no standardized peri-implant mucositis definition across studies; mostly short follow-up; frequent absence of control groups; predominantly single crowns; little jaw-location stratification.
- Floss caution: 3 case reports implicate retained floss remnants in peri-implant sulcus → localized inflammation and marginal bone resorption when rough implant surfaces are exposed; IDB/irrigators safer there. Implants differ from teeth (contour, no interproximal caries) → floss suitability questioned.
- Future RCTs: larger samples, ≥6-month follow-up, toothbrushing-alone control, clear mucositis criteria per latest classification.

## 7. Glossary

- **Peri-implant mucositis**: reversible inflammation of peri-implant soft tissue (BOP, visual inflammation) without supporting bone loss.
- **Interproximal cleaning device**: home-care aid for the surface between adjacent restorations — dental floss, interdental/interproximal brush, oral irrigator/water flosser.
- **Oral irrigator / water flosser**: device delivering a pulsed water (or CHX) jet for interproximal cleaning.
- **PI / GI / BOP / IL-6**: plaque index / gingival index / bleeding on probing / interleukin-6 (proinflammatory cytokine).
- **I²**: statistic quantifying between-study heterogeneity (higher = more inconsistent).
