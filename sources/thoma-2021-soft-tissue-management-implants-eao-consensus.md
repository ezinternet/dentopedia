## Why Ingested

임플란트 연조직 증대(soft tissue augmentation)의 시점·재료(자가이식 vs 대체재)별 효과에 대한 EAO 합의문 — 즉시식립 심미부 권고의 상위 근거. 단일 RCT인 [[immediate-implant/fettouh-2024-soft-tissue-volume-midfacial-gingival-immediate-implant]]·[[immediate-implant/de-angelis-2021-immediate-implant-provisionalization-soft-tissue-retrospective]]와 SR인 [[immediate-implant/seyssens-2021-immediate-implant-connective-tissue-graft-sr-ma]]를 합의 권고 수준으로 묶는다.

## One-line Summary

6th EAO consensus report (group 2; based on 3 systematic reviews + 1 critical review) concluding that soft tissue augmentation at immediate or delayed implant placement reduces mid-buccal recession and (at immediate placement) increases mucosal thickness in esthetic sites, with autologous grafts showing a consistent but non-significant tendency to outperform substitutes, and insufficient evidence on optimal timing.

## 한줄요약

제6차 EAO 합의문(group 2; SR 3편 + critical review 1편 기반) — 심미부에서 즉시·지연 식립 시 연조직 증대가 중안면 점막 퇴축을 줄이고(즉시식립은 점막 두께도 증가), 자가이식이 대체재보다 일관되나 유의하지 않게 우세하며, 증대 시점에 대한 근거는 불충분하다.

## 1. Document Information

- Journal: Clinical Oral Implants Research 2021;32(Suppl. 21):174–180. Open access.
- 6th EAO Consensus Conference, Working Group 2; supported by the European Association for Osseointegration.
- Basis: 3 systematic reviews + 1 critical review prepared in advance.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Synthesizes the soft-tissue-augmentation evidence into EAO consensus statements and clinical recommendations.
- Separates effects by placement timing (immediate vs delayed) and graft type (autologous vs substitute).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Consensus process over commissioned systematic/critical reviews assessing clinical, radiographic, and patient-reported outcomes of soft-tissue augmentation at implants.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Immediate placement + STA: less mid-buccal recession (4 RCTs, 2 CCTs) and thicker mid-buccal mucosa (2 RCTs) vs no augmentation.
- Delayed placement + STA: less recession (1 RCT, 5 CCTs); no significant thickness increase (1 RCT, 3 CCTs).
- Autologous grafts vs soft-tissue substitutes: no significant difference, but a consistent tendency favoring autologous (less recession in 2 RCTs, thicker mucosa in 4 RCTs); studies possibly underpowered/heterogeneous.
- Preferable timing for augmentation: insufficient evidence.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Underpowered, heterogeneous primary studies; no medium-term data for soft-tissue substitutes.
- Future research: head-to-head autologous vs substitute with 2D/3D volumetric outcomes.

## 6. Related Work

- EAO/ITI soft-tissue augmentation systematic reviews; CTG and collagen-matrix trials at implants.

## 7. Glossary

- **STA**: soft tissue augmentation.
- **Soft tissue substitute**: xenogeneic collagen matrix or similar graft replacement.
- **Mid-buccal mucosa**: facial peri-implant soft tissue at the midfacial point, the key esthetic recession site.
