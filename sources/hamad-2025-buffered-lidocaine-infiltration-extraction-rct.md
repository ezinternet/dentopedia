## Why Ingested

Buffered-LA cluster의 다른 RCT는 모두 IANB(nerve block)를 다루는데, 본 RCT는 **maxillary supraperiosteal infiltration**이라는 다른 투여 경로에서 buffering 효과를 검증해 cluster의 외적 타당성을 넓힌다. 최대 표본(n=100)으로 injection pain·onset·duration을 모두 유의하게 개선함을 보여 [[wiki/drug/kalra-2024-buffered-lidocaine-articaine-extraction-rct]]의 onset 데이터와 [[wiki/drug/karunakar-2024-buffered-precooled-lidocaine-ianb-pulpitis]]의 duration 데이터를 infiltration 맥락에서 재확인하고, [[wiki/drug/hwang-2023-proposal-clinical-guidelines-local-anesthesia-dentistry]] 가이드라인에 buffered-infiltration 근거를 더한다.

## One-line Summary

Double-blind prospective RCT (n=100, 50/group, ASA I/II, 18–60y), maxillary posterior extraction with supraperiosteal infiltration: buffered 2% lidocaine/1:100,000 (pH 7.3) beat conventional (pH 4.2) on injection pain (VAS 2.8±0.7 vs 4.2±0.9), onset (2.3 vs 4.7 min) and duration (45.8±7.6 vs 36.4±8.2 min), all p<0.01; reinjection rate not different (4% vs 8%, p=0.678).

## 한줄요약

이중맹검 전향적 RCT (n=100, 군당 50명, ASA I/II, 18–60세), 상악 구치 발치 골막상 침윤마취: buffered 2% 리도카인/1:100,000(pH 7.3)이 conventional(pH 4.2) 대비 주사 통증(VAS 2.8±0.7 vs 4.2±0.9), 발현(2.3분 vs 4.7분), 지속(45.8±7.6분 vs 36.4±8.2분) 모두 우수(전부 p<0.01); 재주사율은 차이 없음(4% vs 8%, p=0.678).

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Infiltration-route evidence** (maxillary supraperiosteal), complementing the nerve-block-focused buffering literature — buffering benefit holds for infiltration, not just IANB.
2. **Largest sample in the buffered-LA cluster (n=100)** with G*Power-based sizing (effect size 0.8, power 90%).
3. **Verified pH with a calibrated meter** (Group A 4.2±0.1, Group B 7.3±0.1) — mechanistic confirmation that the alkalinization was actually achieved, not assumed.
4. Demonstrated buffering improves the full triad (less injection pain, faster onset, longer duration) in a clean chronic-periapical-lesion extraction model.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: randomized, prospective, double-blind RCT; hospital dental clinic, Sep 2024–Apr 2025.
- **n = 100** (50/group), age 18–60, ASA I/II, maxillary posterior teeth with chronic periapical lesions.
- **Groups**:
  - Group A (control): conventional 2% lidocaine + 1:100,000 adrenaline (pH 4.2±0.1).
  - Group B (experimental): buffered — 0.18 mL 8.4% NaHCO3 added to 1.8 mL 2% lidocaine/1:100,000 epi (1:10), prepared immediately before injection, final pH ~7.4 (measured 7.3±0.1).
- **Dose**: 1.8 mL per tooth, supraperiosteal infiltration, 27-G needle, injection speed 1 mL/min.
- **Blinding**: surgeon blinded to LA type; outcomes assessed by a second blinded investigator.
- **Outcomes**: injection pain (VAS 0–10), onset (first numbness on probing after needle withdrawal), duration (to first post-extraction analgesic), reinjection need.
- **Statistics**: independent t-test / Mann–Whitney U (continuous), Chi-square (categorical); p<0.05; SPSS.
- **Exclusions**: pregnancy/lactation, systemic infection, multiple-extraction need, interfering medications.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Baseline (Table 1): groups comparable in age (35.2 vs 34.8 y), gender, baseline pain (VAS 6.5 vs 6.7, p=0.53).

Outcomes (Table 2):

| Parameter | Group A (Conventional) | Group B (Buffered) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Injection pain (VAS) | 4.2 ± 0.9 | 2.8 ± 0.7 | < 0.01 |
| Onset (min) | 4.7 | 2.3 | < 0.01 |
| Duration (min) | 36.4 ± 8.2 | 45.8 ± 7.6 | < 0.01 |
| Reinjection required | 8% (4/50) | 4% (2/50) | 0.678 (NS) |

Effect sizes: ~33% lower injection-pain VAS, ~51% faster onset (−2.4 min), ~26% longer duration (+9.4 min) with buffering.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **No success-rate or intraoperative-pain-during-extraction endpoint** — efficacy inferred from reinjection rate (which was NS), not a direct success metric.
- **Single anesthetic, single route, single concentration** — only 2% lidocaine/1:100,000 infiltration; no articaine or block comparator.
- **Reinjection p=0.678** — the one efficacy-adjacent endpoint did not separate the groups, so the "better anesthetic efficacy" claim rests mainly on duration/onset, not on a hard success outcome.
- **ASA I/II healthy adults only** — no medically compromised or inflamed-pulp (SIP) population.
- A referenced pH-meter "Model XYZ, Brand" placeholder suggests incomplete methods reporting.
- Future: add direct success endpoint, compare against articaine, test medically compromised cohorts.

## 7. Glossary

- **Supraperiosteal (infiltration) anesthesia**: LA deposited above the periosteum near the apex; the standard route for maxillary teeth (thin cortical bone).
- **Buffered lidocaine**: 0.18 mL 8.4% NaHCO3 + 1.8 mL 2% lidocaine/epi (1:10), pH ~7.4.
- **Onset**: time from needle withdrawal to first numbness on probing.
- **Duration**: time from injection to first post-extraction analgesic requirement.
- **Reinjection**: supplementary LA needed for inadequate anesthesia — a surrogate for failure.
- **Chronic periapical lesion**: long-standing periradicular pathology; tissue not acutely inflamed like SIP.
