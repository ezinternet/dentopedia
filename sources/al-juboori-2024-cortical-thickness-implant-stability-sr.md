## One-line Summary

Systematic review concluding thicker cortical bone enhances both primary and secondary dental implant stability, reflected in higher insertion torque and implant stability quotient (ISQ) values.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰 — 더 두꺼운 cortical bone이 임플란트 일차·이차 안정성을 모두 높이며, 식립 토크·ISQ 값 상승으로 나타남.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Synthesizes cortical-thickness–stability evidence across primary and secondary stability.
- Links cortical thickness to both insertion torque and ISQ.
- Provides an implant-side counterpart to the miniscrew cortical-thickness literature.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Systematic review; electronic databases.
- Studies relating cortical bone thickness to primary/secondary implant stability (insertion torque, ISQ).
- Qualitative synthesis.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Thicker cortical bone → higher insertion torque and higher ISQ (better primary stability).
- Cortical thickness also associated with secondary (osseointegration-phase) stability.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Heterogeneous measurement of cortical thickness (CBCT vs histology).
- Confounding by overall bone density and implant macrogeometry.
- Few longitudinal secondary-stability data.

## 6. Related Work
- Cortical thickness–ISQ (Chatvaratthana 2017); cadaver BMD–stability (Nkenke 2003); miniscrew cortical thickness (Marquezan 2014).

## 7. Glossary
- Cortical bone thickness (CtTh): thickness of the dense outer bone layer.
- Primary vs secondary stability: mechanical (placement) vs biological (osseointegration).
