## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 13 clinical studies: ceramic laminate veneers show 89% overall cumulative survival at median 9 years; glass-ceramic outperforms feldspathic (94% vs 87%); fracture/chipping is the most frequent complication (4%).

## 한줄요약

## 2. Key Contributions

- Established pooled survival estimates for feldspathic porcelain and glass-ceramic laminate veneers at long-term follow-up (median 9 years)
- Demonstrated that ceramic material type (feldspathic vs glass-ceramic) does not significantly influence failure rate by metaregression
- Quantified individual complication rates: debonding, fracture/chipping, secondary caries, severe marginal discoloration, and endodontic problems
- Found no statistically significant influence of incisal coverage on failure rates (OR 1.25, 95% CI: 0.33–4.73)
- Concluded enamel/dentin preparation effect on failure could not be meta-analyzed due to heterogeneity

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search databases**: Cochrane and PubMed
- **Initial articles identified**: 899
- **Studies included**: 13 clinical trials
- **Follow-up period**: Median 9 years
- **Statistical methods**: Cochran Q test and I² statistic for heterogeneity assessment; metaregression for moderator analysis
- **Outcomes extracted**: Survival rates, debonding, fracture/chipping, secondary caries, endodontic problems, severe marginal discoloration, incisal coverage influence
- **Ceramic types analyzed**: Feldspathic porcelain and glass-ceramic (leucite-reinforced and lithium disilicate not separately analyzed as today's taxonomy)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Estimate | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|
| Overall cumulative survival | 89% | 84%–94% |
| Glass-ceramic survival | 94% | 87%–100% |
| Feldspathic survival | 87% | 82%–93% |
| Debonding rate | 2% | 1%–4% |
| Fracture/chipping rate | 4% | 3%–6% |
| Secondary caries rate | 1% | 0%–3% |
| Severe marginal discoloration | 2% | 1%–10% |
| Endodontic problems | 2% | 1%–3% |
| Incisal coverage OR | 1.25 | 0.33–4.73 |

- Metaregression: ceramic type and follow-up period had no significant influence on failure rate
- Fracture/chipping was the most frequent complication
- Incisal coverage odds ratio 1.25 was not statistically significant (CI crosses 1.0)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 13 studies included; heterogeneity limited pooled analysis of some subgroups
- Enamel vs dentin preparation influence could not be meta-analyzed due to insufficient data
- No separation between leucite-reinforced vs lithium disilicate glass-ceramics
- Wide CIs on some estimates reflect limited data
- Metaregression sample size was insufficient to draw definitive conclusions on moderators
- Authors called for more RCTs with standardized reporting of preparation designs

## 6. Related Work

- Preceded by narrative reviews and earlier meta-analyses (Kreulen et al. 1998) without full PRISMA methodology
- Companion paper by same group (Albanesi 2016) focused specifically on incisal coverage design
- Followed by Klein et al. 2025 which expanded to include leucite-reinforced, LDS, and zirconia with larger search

## 7. Glossary

- **Laminate veneer**: Thin ceramic restoration bonded to the facial surface of anterior teeth
- **Feldspathic porcelain**: Traditional sintered porcelain with glassy matrix; high esthetics but lower strength
- **Glass-ceramic**: Ceramic with crystalline phases (leucite or lithium disilicate) embedded in glassy matrix; higher strength than feldspathic
- **Cumulative survival rate**: Proportion of restorations remaining in service (not requiring replacement) over follow-up
- **Debonding**: Loss of adhesive bond between veneer and tooth, requiring re-cementation
- **Chipping**: Cohesive fracture limited to a portion of the ceramic veneer
- **Metaregression**: Regression analysis to identify variables explaining heterogeneity across studies
- **OR (Odds Ratio)**: Ratio of odds of failure in one group vs another
