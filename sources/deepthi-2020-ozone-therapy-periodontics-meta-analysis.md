## Why Ingested
치주염의 비외과적 치료(SRP)에 대한 부가요법 근거를 보강하기 위해 ingest. SRP 단독 가이드라인을 다룬 [[periodontics/smiley-2015-nonsurgical-periodontitis-srp-guideline]]와 대비되며, 오존 부가효과가 작고 이질성이 높아 부가요법의 한계를 보여주는 근거로 활용.

## One-line Summary
SR+MA of 7 RCTs (n=13-113 per study) finding ozone as an adjunct to scaling and root planing produced small, mostly non-significant improvements in PPD, CAL, PI, GI under high heterogeneity (I2=80.49%).

## 한줄요약
7개 RCT를 메타분석(연구당 n=13-113)한 결과, 오존 치료를 SRP(scaling and root planing)에 부가하면 PPD·CAL·PI·GI가 소폭 개선되나 대부분 통계적으로 유의하지 않고 이질성이 매우 높음(I2=80.49%).

## 2. Key Contributions
- First meta-analysis (per the authors) evaluating ozone therapy as an adjunct to mechanical scaling and root planing (SRP) for periodontitis in humans.
- Synthesizes clinical periodontal outcomes (PPD, CAL, PI, GI, BOP) comparing SRP + ozone vs SRP alone / placebo.
- Reviews ozone biology and rationale: powerful oxidant, antimicrobial without inducing resistance, host immune modulator, biocompatible (aqueous form) to fibroblasts, cementoblasts, and epithelial cells.
- Concludes ozone "can be used effectively as an adjunct" to SRP, but the quantitative effect sizes are small and largely non-significant.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Focused question: How effective is ozone therapy as an adjunct in treating periodontitis?
- PICO: Population = chronic and aggressive periodontitis adults; Intervention = ozone (gaseous/aqueous/ozonated oil gel) adjunct to SRP; Comparison = ozone group vs placebo/no-treatment; Outcomes = PPD, CAL, BOP, PI, GI.
- Databases: PubMed, Medline, Embase, Cochrane, Science Direct, Google Scholar, Sci-hub; hand-searching; period Jan 1, 2010 - Jul 30, 2018.
- Inclusion: peer-reviewed, English, human RCT, >6 weeks follow-up, >10 patients, reporting change in PPD/CAL/GI/PI/BOP. Exclusion: in vitro, animal, retrospective cross-sectional.
- Two independent reviewers; kappa for agreement.
- Screening: 123 identified → 109 excluded by title/abstract → 14 full-text → exclusions for missing patient data/comparison and review articles → 7 studies eligible for meta-analysis (abstract states 6 included; body states 7).
- Random-effects model; weighted mean difference (WMD); heterogeneity by Q and I2; funnel plots for publication bias.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- 7 included studies; participants 13-113 per study; ages 20-72; chronic or aggressive periodontitis; mostly RCTs at universities/oral health-care centers.
- PPD: WMD 0.03 (95% CI -0.44, 0.50); random-effects estimate 0.0286 (effectively no significant difference).
- PI: WMD -0.23 (95% CI -0.64, 0.19); estimate -0.226.
- GI: WMD -0.45 (95% CI -1.37, 0.46); estimate -0.454.
- CAL: WMD -0.26 (95% CI -0.89, 0.37); estimate -0.263.
- All four pooled estimates have confidence intervals crossing zero → not statistically significant.
- Heterogeneity high: Q = 10.241, I2 = 80.49%.
- Funnel plots symmetrical → no evident publication bias.
- Individual studies (e.g., Shoukheba & Ali 2014 ozonated olive oil gel in aggressive periodontitis; Sacco 2016 oxygen-ozone in 113 patients) reported improvements in clinical parameters maintained up to 3-6 months.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Very high statistical heterogeneity (I2 = 80.49%) — varied ozone forms (gaseous, aqueous, ozonated oil gel), delivery, dosing, and follow-up across studies.
- Small number of studies and small per-study sample sizes; some studies excluded for lack of individual patient data.
- Internal inconsistency in reported study count (abstract "six," body "seven").
- Pooled effects are small and statistically non-significant despite the favorable narrative conclusion.
- Heterogeneous outcome reporting limits robust pooling; standardized ozone protocols and larger RCTs are needed.

## 6. Related Work
- Smiley et al. 2015 — nonsurgical SRP clinical practice guideline (baseline mechanical therapy).
- Studies of other SRP adjuncts (local antimicrobials, chlorhexidine, lasers) form the broader adjunctive-therapy literature this paper sits within.

## 7. Glossary
- Ozone (O3): triatomic oxygen; powerful oxidant with antimicrobial, immune-modulating, and wound-healing properties; used gaseous or aqueous.
- SRP (Scaling and Root Planing): mechanical removal of subgingival biofilm/calculus; standard nonsurgical periodontal therapy.
- PPD (Probing Pocket Depth): depth from gingival margin to pocket base.
- CAL (Clinical Attachment Level/Loss): distance from CEJ to pocket base; key periodontitis severity metric.
- PI / GI (Plaque Index / Gingival Index): plaque accumulation and gingival inflammation scores.
- BOP (Bleeding on Probing): inflammation indicator.
- WMD (Weighted Mean Difference): pooled effect-size metric in meta-analysis.
- I2: proportion of total variation due to heterogeneity rather than chance.
