## Why Ingested

"바늘없는 압력 침윤마취기" 관련 조사 중 The Wand(컴퓨터 제어 정압 침윤마취 시스템) 관련 근거를 찾다가 확인. 이 RCT는 장비 자체의 임상효과가 아니라 "환자에게 장비 작동원리를 설명하는 것"이 불안·통증을 줄이는지를 검증한다는 점에서 [[behavioral-dentistry/dental-anxiety/appukuttan-2016-strategies-manage-dental-anxiety-phobia]]가 제시하는 "정보 제공 → 불안 감소" 전략의 반증 사례로서 가치가 있다.

## One-line Summary

Single-blinded RCT (n=68, Barcelona) among upper third molar extraction patients found that a detailed verbal explanation of The Wand computerized anesthesia system before injection did NOT significantly reduce dental anxiety (ISAR/MDAS/DFS/STAI-S) or perceived pain (VAS) vs no information; need of intraoperative re-anesthesia (42.6%) was unrelated to anxiety but significantly related to longer operative time (p=0.007).

## 한줄요약

단일맹검 RCT (n=68, 바르셀로나, 상악 제3대구치 발치): The Wand 컴퓨터 제어 마취 시스템 작동원리에 대한 상세 구두 설명이 불안(ISAR/MDAS/DFS/STAI-S)이나 통증(VAS)을 유의하게 줄이지 못함; 술중 재마취 필요율(42.6%)은 불안도와 무관했으나 수술시간 증가와는 유의한 관련(p=0.007).

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Influence of information concerning a computerized anesthesia system on dental anxiety: a randomized controlled clinical trial
- **Authors**: Rizzo-Lorenzo A, Sánchez-Torres A, Noguera-Mutlló C, Pérez-Beltrán I, Figueiredo R, Valmaseda-Castellón E (University of Barcelona Dental Hospital, Master of Oral Surgery and Orofacial Implantology)
- **Journal**: Medicina Oral, Patología Oral y Cirugía Bucal 2020;25(2):e217-e223
- **DOI**: 10.4317/medoral.23315
- **PMID**: 32040470 / **PMCID**: PMC7103442
- **Study type**: Single-blinded randomized controlled trial (RCT), n=68 (34/group)
- **Ethics**: University of Barcelona Dental Hospital ethical review board (Protocol 11/2016); CONSORT + Declaration of Helsinki compliant

## 2. Key Contributions

- First study (per authors) to directly test whether *pre-injection informational explanation* of a computerized anesthesia delivery system (The Wand) affects measured dental anxiety and perceived pain, as opposed to testing the device's intrinsic pain-reduction effect.
- Uses four validated anxiety instruments simultaneously (ISAR, MDAS, DFS, STAI-S/T) plus 4-point VAS pain scoring (buccal/palatal puncture and infiltration), a more rigorous multi-instrument anxiety capture than most single-scale dental-anxiety RCTs.
- Reports on need for intraoperative re-anesthesia as a secondary outcome and its correlates (operative time, radiological difficulty, anxiety level) — an outcome rarely tracked in computerized-anesthesia-device literature for adults.
- Null result on the information-anxiety hypothesis, contrasting with prior literature (Wang interview study, Heaton CARL desensitization RCT) that supported information/explanation reducing dental fear — flags that not all "more information" interventions transfer to reduced anxiety, especially with computerized/unfamiliar equipment (beeping sounds, unfamiliar pen-shaped handpiece) that could itself provoke novelty-related fear.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Single-blinded, two-arm parallel RCT. Randomization via computer-generated sequence (randomization.com).
- **Population**: 18–45y patients requiring upper third molar extraction (erupted/partially/fully impacted), asymptomatic, ASA I–II, no anesthetic allergy, single extraction only, surgery ≤60 min, no active infection, no concurrent systemic antibiotics/analgesics, normal hemodynamics. Dental professionals/students excluded (prior device knowledge).
- **Intervention (experimental group, n=34)**: Standardized verbal explanation of The Wand's mechanism (pen-shaped design, tissue-resistance-independent constant low-pressure flow, target anesthetized area) delivered before anesthesia.
- **Control group (n=34)**: No specific information given.
- **Anesthesia protocol (both groups)**: The Wand (Milestone Scientific) — supraperiosteal infiltrative technique, 1.6 mL buccal + 0.2 mL palatal, 25 mm 30G needle, 4% articaine + 1:100,000 epinephrine (Artinibsa/Inibsa). Administered by 3 blinded, calibrated researchers.
- **Outcome measurement**: Post-anesthesia, a separate investigator administered ISAR, MDAS, DFS, STAI-S questionnaires plus 4× 100mm VAS (buccal puncture, buccal infiltration, palatal puncture, palatal infiltration) simultaneously. STAI-T (trait anxiety) collected at a prior visit 1 (baseline).
- **Surgical technique**: Full-thickness flap, high-speed (40,000 rpm) handpiece + tungsten carbide bur with saline irrigation for bone removal as needed; 3/0 silk interrupted suture closure. Performed by Master's fellows of similar experience.
- **Sample size**: G*Power 3.1.9.2, α=0.05, power=0.9, effect size d=0.8 → 68 total (34/arm).
- **Statistics**: SPSS 22.0; Shapiro-Wilk normality test; non-parametric U-Mann Whitney (continuous) and Fisher exact (dichotomic MDAS/DFS cutoffs) for between-group comparisons; significance p<0.05.
- No topical anesthesia was used, deliberately, to avoid confounding VAS pain measurement.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Flow**: 85 assessed → 17 lost to follow-up (surgical visit cancellation) → 68 analyzed (34/group).
- **Demographics**: 47 women (69.1%), 21 men (30.9%), mean age 28.8±9.3y (range 18–67). 30 right (44.1%) / 38 left (55.9%) third molars. Mean operative time 19.2±11.7 min. Parant classification: 55 conventional extraction (type 1), 13 required ostectomy (type 2).
- **Primary outcome (anxiety)**: No statistically significant between-group differences on ISAR, MDAS, DFS, or STAI-S — detailed Wand explanation did not reduce measured anxiety. Overall sample anxiety scores were low; few highly anxious individuals were captured (limiting power to detect an effect in that subgroup).
- **Secondary outcome (pain)**: No significant between-group VAS differences at buccal or palatal puncture/infiltration.
- **Re-anesthesia**: 29/68 (42.6%) needed intraoperative re-anesthesia. Significantly associated with longer operative time — 15.5 min (SD 8.8) for no-re-anesthesia cases vs 24 min (SD 13.3) for re-anesthesia cases (p=0.007). NOT significantly associated with anxiety level on any questionnaire, nor with previous explanation (p=0.806), nor with radiological difficulty (distal space p=0.245; depth p=0.400; Winter's position p=0.235) or intervention type (Parant p=0.059).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small proportion of highly anxious individuals in the sample limited the study's power to detect an information effect specifically in that subgroup — the authors speculate more between-group difference might emerge with more extremely anxious patients.
- Inclusion of patients with prior third-molar-extraction experience could bias anxiety questionnaire responses (expectation effects).
- Single-blinded design (patient not blinded to group assignment by definition — they either received or did not receive an explanation); surgeon/assessor blinding maintained where feasible.
- Surgeries performed by postgraduate trainees ("Master fellows") of similar but limited experience — could inflate the re-anesthesia rate (42.6% is high relative to mean operative time) versus specialist-performed extractions; possible overestimation from trainees interpreting intraoperative discomfort/pressure sensations as insufficient anesthesia.
- Findings contradict prior literature (Wang interview-based suggestions; Heaton CARL desensitization RCT) supporting information/explanation as anxiety-reducing — authors note the amount and format of information is a controversial variable; excessive or poorly-timed information (e.g., visual "tell-show-do" exposure to instruments) can paradoxically increase anxiety, and the specific novelty of computerized-device information (unfamiliar sounds/beeps, pen-shaped handpiece) may not behave like general procedural information.
- No comparison arm using conventional syringe anesthesia — study isolates only the "explanation vs no explanation" variable within Wand-anesthetized patients, not Wand vs conventional syringe.
- Single-center, single-procedure-type (upper third molar extraction) design limits generalizability to other procedures/populations.

## 6. Related Work

- Shah et al. (cited in-text): compared perceived pain of palatal injections via The Wand vs traditional syringe — better results with The Wand (referenced but not separately ingested here).
- Wang et al. (cited in-text): interview study of MDAS-diagnosed anxious patients — patients reported more information about treatment indication/steps would reduce anxiety, but "tell-show-do" visual exposure to instruments would exacerbate it.
- Heaton et al. (cited in-text): RCT of a computerized systematic-desensitization program (CARL) vs an informative pamphlet for dental injection fear — CARL produced greater fear reduction than the leaflet alone.
- Patini et al. (cited in-text): pediatric RCT (ages 5–12, contralateral extractions) — conventional syringe needed 4× more intraoperative re-anesthesia than The Wand.

## 7. Glossary

- **The Wand**: Milestone Scientific computerized, pressure-regulated, footswitch-activated local anesthesia delivery system with a pen-shaped handpiece; delivers anesthetic at constant volume/pressure independent of tissue resistance.
- **ISAR**: Interval Scale of Anxiety Response — 90mm vertical VAS-based anxiety measure.
- **MDAS**: Modified Dental Anxiety Scale — 5-item scale (Corah's Dental Anxiety Scale modification), max 25, ≥19 = very anxious.
- **DFS**: Dental Fear Survey — 20-item scale, score 20 (no fear) to 100 (terrified); cutoff 63 for clinically significant dental anxiety.
- **STAI (STAI-T / STAI-S)**: State-Trait Anxiety Inventory — 40-item scale split into trait anxiety (STAI-T, stable disposition, measured at baseline) and state anxiety (STAI-S, transient emotional state, measured post-procedure).
- **VAS**: Visual Analog Scale, 100mm, used for perceived pain at 4 anesthesia timepoints.
- **Supraperiosteal infiltrative technique**: injection technique depositing anesthetic near the periosteum above the apex of the target tooth.
- **Parant classification**: classification of third molar extraction complexity/type (type 1 = conventional, type 2 = requires ostectomy).
- **Pell & Gregory / Winter classification**: radiological classifications of impacted third molar position/depth relative to ramus and adjacent tooth.
- **Re-anesthesia**: supplemental intraoperative anesthetic administration when the initial dose proves insufficient.
