## Why Ingested

Prepared as the companion systematic review to the 2021 SEPA/DGI/Osteology Foundation consensus workshop on peri-implant soft tissues, this SR provides the quantitative meta-analytic evidence base for choosing between soft tissue substitutes and autogenous grafts for peri-implant keratinized mucosa (KM) augmentation. It directly complements [[wiki/implants/sanz-2022-keratinized-mucosa-around-implants-consensus]], providing the primary data synthesis that the consensus statement translates into clinical recommendations, and extends [[wiki/implants/oh-2024-keratinized-mucosa-augmentation-functioning-implants-sr-ma]] by comparing substitute-vs-autograft efficacy rather than augmentation vs no augmentation.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review with meta-analysis (11 studies, 282 subjects) showing free gingival grafts augment peri-implant keratinized mucosa significantly more than soft tissue substitutes overall (WMD −0.9 mm), though xenogeneic collagen matrices are a viable alternative to CTG and achieve >2 mm gain with reduced surgical morbidity.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰 및 메타분석(11개 연구, 282명)에서 자가유리치은이식(FGG)이 연조직 대체재에 비해 임플란트 주위 각화점막 증가량이 유의하게 더 크나(WMD −0.9 mm), 이종 콜라겐 기질(Mucograft®)은 결합조직이식(CTG)과 유사한 결과로 수술 이환율 감소 대안이 될 수 있음.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First SR with meta-analysis** specifically comparing soft tissue substitutes vs autogenous grafts for peri-implant KM augmentation in RCTs/CCTs.
2. Quantifies the KM gain deficit for substitutes overall (WMD −0.9 mm vs autogenous; p = .001) but shows xenografts are not significantly inferior to autogenous when analyzed alone (WMD −0.8 mm; p = .062).
3. Demonstrates collagen matrix (CM) is not significantly different from CTG in KM gain (n = 1 study; WMD 0.0 mm; p = .948).
4. Provides the first pooled effectiveness estimate for substitutes alone: WME +3.0 mm KM gain (xenografts +3.5 mm, allografts +1.6 mm).
5. Shows surgical time savings of ~18.5 min with substitutes and significantly lower postoperative pain/analgesic use.
6. Confirms no significant difference in shrinkage rate between substitutes and autogenous grafts (WMD −3.7%; p = .256), except allografts shrink markedly more (~52%).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

### PICOS Questions

**PICOS #1 (efficacy, head-to-head)**: In implant patients, what is the efficacy of soft tissue substitutes vs autogenous grafts (FGG/CTG) for peri-implant KM augmentation in RCTs and CCTs with ≥6-month follow-up?

**PICOS #2 (effectiveness, all evidence)**: In implant patients, what is the effectiveness of soft tissue substitutes for peri-implant KM augmentation in RCTs, CCTs, cohort studies, or case series with ≥6-month follow-up and ≥10 patients?

### Included Studies

| Study | Design | Substitute | Control | n subjects | Follow-up |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Lorenzo et al. 2012 (Spain) | RCT parallel | Mucograft® (xenograft) | CTG | 22→22 | 6 mo |
| Basegmez et al. 2013 (Turkey) | RCT parallel | AlloDerm® (allograft) | FGG | 36→36 | 6 mo |
| Schmitt et al. 2016 (Germany) | CCT parallel | Mucograft® (xenograft) | FGG | 48→48 | up to 5 yr |
| Vellis et al. 2019 (USA) | RCT split-mouth | Mucograft® (xenograft) | FGG | 30→30 | 6 mo |
| Tarasenko et al. 2020 (Russia) | RCT parallel | Mucograft® (xenograft) | FGG | 40→40 | 6 mo |
| Solonko et al. 2022 (Spain; unpublished) | RCT parallel | Mucograft® (xenograft) | FGG | 54→46 | 6 mo |
| Park 2006 (Korea) | Case series | SureDerm® (allograft) | — | 10→10 | 6 mo |
| Maiorana et al. 2016/2018 (Italy/USA) | Case series | Mucograft® (xenograft) | — | 15→13 | 12 & 60 mo |
| Lim et al. 2018 (Korea) | Retrospective CS | Mucograft® (xenograft) | — | 11→11 | 12 mo |
| Papi & Pompa 2018 (Italy) | Case series | Mucoderm® (xenograft) | — | 12→12 | 12 mo |

**Pooled subjects**: 282; **Pooled implants**: ≥440; **Final analysis**: 265 subjects / ≥437 implants

**Surgical technique**: Apically positioned flap (APF) + substitute or autograft in all studies except Papi (bilaminar technique).

**Timing of augmentation**: Post-implant placement (8 studies), simultaneous (1 study), pre-implant placement (1 study).

### Risk of Bias

- All 5 included RCTs: some concerns or high risk of bias (RoB 2.0)
- CCT (Schmitt 2016): unclear overall risk (ROBINS-I)
- Case series (NOS): mean score 3.0/8 (range 2–4)
- No significant publication bias for PICOS #1 primary outcome (p = .560); significant potential small-study effects for PICOS #2 combined (p = .009)

### Statistical Methods

- Weighted mean difference (WMD) for head-to-head comparisons (PICOS #1)
- Weighted mean effect (WME) for pre-post changes (PICOS #2)
- DerSimonian–Laird random-effects model when significant heterogeneity
- Heterogeneity: I² statistic + Q test (chi-square)
- Software: STATA® + OpenMeta[Analyst]

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Primary Outcome: KM Width Gain

**PICOS #1 — Substitute vs Autogenous (RCTs + CCT):**

| Comparison | n studies | WMD (mm) | 95% CI | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| All substitutes vs autogenous | 6 | −0.9 | [−1.4; −0.3] | .001 |
| Substitutes vs FGG only | 5 | −1.0 | [−1.6; −0.5] | <.001 |
| Xenografts vs autogenous | 5 | −0.8 | [−1.6; 0.0] | .062 (NS) |
| Xenograft (Mucograft®) vs CTG | 1 | 0.0 | [−0.9; +0.9] | .948 (NS) |

Negative WMD = autogenous grafts yielded more KM gain.

**PICOS #2 — Effectiveness of substitutes alone (all study types):**

| Subgroup | n studies | WME (mm) | 95% CI | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| All | 9 | +3.0 | [2.3; 3.8] | <.001 |
| Xenografts | 7 | +3.5 | [2.5; 4.6] | <.001 |
| Allografts | 2 | +1.6 | [1.4; 1.7] | <.001 |
| Post-implant placement | 5 | +2.5 | [1.9; 3.1] | <.001 |
| 6-month follow-up | 5 | +2.2 | [1.6; 2.7] | <.001 |
| 12-month follow-up | 4 | +3.7 | [1.7; 5.7] | <.001 |

All subgroups achieved clinically meaningful >2 mm KM threshold (except point estimate borderline at 6 months).

### Secondary Outcomes

**Shrinkage (PICOS #1, dimensional change):**

| Comparison | n | WMD (%) | 95% CI | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| All substitutes vs autogenous | 5 | −3.7 | [−10.1; +2.7] | .256 (NS) |
| Xenograft vs FGG | 4 | −6.1 | [−13.3; +1.1] | .099 (NS) |
| Xenograft vs CTG | 1 | +6.3 | [+1.6; +11.0] | .009 |

Allografts (AlloDerm®) showed the highest individual shrinkage: ~52%. Xenograft (Mucograft®) WME shrinkage: −16.5% (1–6 months); −44.3% (immediate postop to 6 months).

**Surgical time**: Substitutes reduced operating time by ~18.5 min (based on 2 studies with usable data).

**Postoperative pain / analgesic use**: Significantly reduced with substitutes vs autogenous grafts.

**Esthetics**: Soft tissue substitutes yielded better blending, contour, and texture than FGG; comparable to CTG.

**Peri-implant clinical outcomes**: Significant improvement in plaque indices, bleeding on probing, and probing depth regardless of graft type. No significant difference in marginal bone level changes between groups.

**Complication rate**: No significant difference in graft loss or surgical complications between substitutes and autogenous grafts.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small number of included studies (11 total; only 5 RCTs, all with unclear or high RoB)
- High heterogeneity in most meta-analyses (I² often >90%); random-effects estimates require cautious interpretation
- Seven of 8 porcine collagen matrix studies used Mucograft® only — limited evidence for Mucoderm® and none for other xenografts
- KM measured by probe/caliper without iodine staining; keratinized attached mucosa (KAM) vs vestibular depth distinctions not consistently evaluated
- Only one study comparing substitute vs CTG (Lorenzo 2012); the non-inferiority conclusion rests on a single RCT
- Economic costs not evaluated in any included study
- No long-term (>5 year) RCTs available
- Controversy remains on whether KM quantity, KAM, or vestibular depth is the key peri-implant health determinant

**Future research needs**: Long-term RCTs (>5 years), standardized KAM measurement protocols, health economic analyses, evaluation of newer substitute products beyond Mucograft®.

## 6. Related Work

- **Lorenzo et al. 2012**: Single RCT comparing Mucograft® vs CTG around implants — foundational comparison study included in this SR.
- **Schmitt et al. 2016**: Long-term CCT (up to 5 years) — only study with extended follow-up, showing durable results with Mucograft®.
- **Thoma et al. 2018**: SR/MA on soft tissue augmentation effects on peri-implant health vs no treatment (positive effect on clinical outcomes regardless of augmentation type).
- **Tavelli et al. 2020**: SR/NMA on peri-implant soft tissue phenotype modification — confirmed FGG superiority for KM gain, consistent with Montero findings.
- **Moraschini et al. 2020**: SR/MA on xenogeneic collagen matrix around implants — complementary evidence base.

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| KM (Keratinized Mucosa) | Keratinized squamous epithelium in gingiva, hard palate, and edentulous ridges; measured in mm width |
| FGG (Free Gingival Graft) | Autogenous epithelialized graft harvested from palate; gold standard for KM augmentation |
| CTG (Connective Tissue Graft) | De-epithelialized autogenous graft; gold standard for volume augmentation, comparable to xenograft for KM |
| CM (Collagen Matrix) | Xenogeneic porcine collagen matrix (e.g., Mucograft®, Mucoderm®) used as substitute |
| ADM (Acellular Dermal Matrix) | Allogeneic human or animal dermal substitute (e.g., AlloDerm®, SureDerm®) |
| APF (Apically Positioned Flap) | Flap design repositioning keratinized tissue apically; combined with graft/substitute to create KM band |
| WMD | Weighted mean difference — between-group comparison metric in meta-analysis |
| WME | Weighted mean effect — pre-post within-group comparison metric |
| PICOS | Population, Intervention, Comparison, Outcome, Study design — framework for SR question formulation |
| Shrinkage | Dimensional contraction of graft material post-surgery (expressed as %) |
| Morbidity | Donor-site pain, bleeding, healing complications from autograft harvesting |
