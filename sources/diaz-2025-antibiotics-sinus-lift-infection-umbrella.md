## Why Ingested

상악동거상술(Sinus Floor Elevation, SFE) 후 항생제 사용에 대한 umbrella review로, 예방적 항생제가 고위험군(막 천공, 측방 접근, 전신질환)에서만 필요하며 단순 SFE에서는 루틴 사용이 불필요하다는 근거를 제공한다. [[sinus-lift/lateral/kozuma-2017-chronic-sinusitis-sinus-augmentation-infection]]의 감염 관리 패턴을 상위 수준 증거(umbrella review, 7개 SR/MA 종합)로 보강하고, [[drug/torof-2023-antibiotic-dental-implant-procedures-sr-ma]]의 임플란트 시술 전반 항생제 근거와 연계되는 상악동 특화 synthesis.

## One-line Summary

Umbrella review of 7 SR/MAs (>18,900 patients) finds postoperative infection rates of 0.3–11.6% after sinus floor elevation, with low-certainty evidence that antibiotic prophylaxis benefits only high-risk cases (membrane perforation, extensive lateral approach, systemic comorbidities); routine use is unnecessary in uncomplicated procedures.

## 한줄요약

7개 체계적 문헌고찰 종합 umbrella review: 상악동거상술(Sinus Floor Elevation, SFE) 후 감염률 0.3–11.6%, 항생제(Antibiotic, ATB) 예방 투여는 슈나이더막(Schneiderian Membrane, SM) 천공·광범위 측방 접근·전신질환 등 고위험 상황에서만 근거 있고, 단순 SFE에서는 루틴 투여 불필요하다는 낮은~매우 낮은 수준의 근거 확인.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First umbrella review** synthesizing all SR/MAs on antibiotic use specifically for SFE infection prevention and management, with AMSTAR 2, GROOVE, and GRADE appraisal
2. **Infection rates by technique**: lateral window 1.7–2.9%, transcrestal 0.3–1.0% — technique more important than ATB regimen
3. **Routine prophylaxis not supported** in uncomplicated SFE; evidence favors risk-based prescribing
4. **Slight overlap** among primary studies (CCA = 1.33%) confirms independent evidence contribution of included reviews
5. **Implant survival** consistently >90% regardless of ATB regimen (highest: 98.4% in Salgado-Peralvo et al. 2023)
6. **GRADE certainty**: low for infection prevention; very low for infection management; moderate for implant survival comparisons
7. Supports **antimicrobial stewardship** in oral implantology — targeted over routine prophylaxis

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Umbrella review per PRISMA 2020 + PRIOR 2022 guidelines; Cochrane guidance for overviews
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane Library, Scopus, Web of Science, EBSCO; manual Google Scholar search
- **Screening**: 268 records identified → 35 duplicates removed → 236 screened → 43 full-text evaluated → 7 included
- **Inter-rater agreement**: Fleiss kappa = 0.76 (substantial); 100% agreement on final inclusion
- **Quality appraisal**: AMSTAR 2 (2 high, 3 moderate, 1 low, 1 critically low)
- **Overlap**: GROOVE tool — citation matrix of 175 unique primary studies; CCA = 1.33% (slight)
- **Evidence certainty**: GRADE methodology
- **Synthesis**: Descriptive narrative (no de novo meta-analysis due to heterogeneity)
- **PICO**: P = patients undergoing SFE; I = systemic ATBs; C = no ATB/placebo/alternative regimens; O = infection incidence, implant survival, complications

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Infection rates

| Setting / Review | Infection rate |
|---|---|
| Tan et al. 2008 (transcrestal) | 0.8% (range 0–2.5%), n=2,830 |
| Pjetursson et al. 2008 (lateral) | 2.9% (range 0–7.4%), n=3,716/12,020 implants |
| Hsu et al. 2022 (lateral) | 1.74% |
| Hsu et al. 2022 (transcrestal) | 0.31% |
| Salgado-Peralvo et al. 2023 | 0–23.3% (individual studies) |
| Allevi et al. 2022 (sinusitis post-implant) | 36.6% developed sinusitis; 39.8% secondary to SFE |
| Schlund et al. 2022 | 0.5–11.6% |

### Implant survival

| Review | Survival rate | Follow-up |
|---|---|---|
| Tan et al. 2008 | 92.8% | 2.4 years |
| Pjetursson et al. 2008 | 90.1% | 2.8 years |
| Salgado-Peralvo et al. 2023 | 98.4% (pooled) | varied |
| Schiavo-Di Flaviano et al. 2024 | 97.0% (with SM perforation), 97.7% (without) | varied |
| Hsu et al. 2022 | 91.0% | max 132 months |

### ATB regimens most used

- **Prophylaxis**: Amoxicillin 2 g PO 1h pre-op, or Amoxicillin/Clavulanate 7–10 days post-op; Clindamycin 300–600 mg q6–8h for penicillin allergy
- **Treatment**: Amoxicillin/Clavulanate, Levofloxacin; combined with corticosteroids, decongestants, CHX rinses
- **Failure**: Switch to Moxifloxacin if no improvement in 7–10 days (resistance concern)

### GROOVE overlap analysis
- 175 unique primary studies across 7 SR/MAs
- CCA = 1.33% → slight overlap → reviews contribute largely independent evidence

### GRADE certainty
| Outcome | Certainty |
|---|---|
| ATB prophylaxis vs infection incidence | LOW |
| Management of sinus graft infection | VERY LOW |
| Implant survival with vs without ATB | MODERATE |
| Comparison of ATB regimens | LOW |

| Rating | Reviews |

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Wide heterogeneity in ATB type, dosage, timing, duration, and outcome definitions across included reviews
- Most underlying evidence from observational studies, not RCTs
- Inconsistent outcome definitions (infection: clinical vs radiographic vs patient-reported)
- No language restriction applied, but search focused on European and Latin American databases
- Quantitative pooling not possible due to heterogeneity
- Several included reviews had ATB outcomes as secondary findings

**Future research needs**:
- Well-designed RCTs stratified by patient risk (healthy vs comorbidities) and surgical approach
- Standardized outcome definitions for postoperative infection (clinical + radiographic)
- Comparative RCTs: short vs extended ATB courses; ATB vs placebo; systemic vs local delivery
- Microbiological/antimicrobial resistance profiling of post-SFE sinus flora
- Multicenter trials with standardized protocols and follow-up
- Patient-reported outcomes (pain, quality of life)

## 6. Related Work

- Tan et al. 2008 — transcrestal SFE SR (0.8% infection rate, 92.8% implant survival)
- Pjetursson et al. 2008 — lateral SFE SR (2.9% infection, 19.5% SM perforation)
- Salgado-Peralvo et al. 2023 — SR on ATB prophylaxis in SFE (no statistically significant difference in failure rates between ATB vs no ATB)
- Allevi et al. 2022 — SR on sinusitis following implant surgery (ESS + ATB: 94.7% resolution)
- Hsu et al. 2022 — SR on SFE complications by technique
- Schlund et al. 2022 — SR on sinus graft infections (critically low AMSTAR 2)
- Schiavo-Di Flaviano et al. 2024 — SR on SM perforation risk and implant outcomes
- 2022 SEI Consensus Report on preventive ATB therapy in implantology

## 7. Glossary

- **SFE**: Sinus Floor Elevation (= sinus lift, 상악동거상술)
- **SM**: Schneiderian Membrane (슈나이더막, = 상악동막)
- **ATB**: Antibiotic (항생제)
- **UR**: Umbrella review (우산형 고찰 — SR/MA들을 재종합)
- **AMSTAR 2**: A Measurement Tool to Assess Systematic Reviews 2 (SR 방법론적 질 평가 도구)
- **GROOVE**: Graphical Representation of Overlap for Overviews (중복 평가 도구)
- **CCA**: Corrected Covered Area (중복 지수 — 0% 완전 독립, 100% 완전 중복)
- **GRADE**: Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation (근거 확실성 평가 체계)
- **ESS**: Endoscopic Sinus Surgery (내시경적 부비동 수술)
- **OAF**: Oro-Antral Fistula (구강-상악동 누공)
- **CHX**: Chlorhexidine (클로르헥시딘)
- **Amoxicillin/Clavulanate**: Broad-spectrum beta-lactam/beta-lactamase inhibitor combination (아목시실린/클라불란산)
- **Stewardship**: Antimicrobial stewardship (항균제 관리 체계 — 적정 사용 촉진)
- **PRIOR 2022**: Preferred Reporting Items for Overviews of Reviews
