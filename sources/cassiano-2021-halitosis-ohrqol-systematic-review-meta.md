## Why Ingested

This SR+MA directly quantifies the patient-centered impact of halitosis on 구강건강관련 삶의 질 (Oral Health-Related Quality of Life, OHRQoL), a dimension not yet covered by existing halitosis pages. It extends [[wiki/halitosis/silva-2017-estimated-prevalence-halitosis-sr]] by linking prevalence data to functional/social burden, and complements [[wiki/halitosis/memon-2022-aetiology-associations-halitosis-systematic]] on patient-reported outcomes.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 10 cross-sectional studies (n=2,692) found halitosis significantly associated with impaired OHRQoL (SMD 0.51, 95% CI 0.27–0.75), with the association persisting only in adults.

## 한줄요약

구강건강관련 삶의 질 (OHRQoL)에 대한 구취 (halitosis)의 영향을 분석한 SR+MA (10개 횡단연구, n=2,692): 구취가 있는 성인에서 OHRQoL 유의한 저하 확인 (SMD 0.51, 95% CI 0.27–0.75).

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First SR+MA to quantify the pooled effect size of halitosis on OHRQoL (SMD 0.51; moderate effect size)
2. Demonstrates effect is adult-specific — not significant in children/adolescents
3. Shows no significant influence of OHRQoL instrument type, halitosis measurement method, or cultural background on pooled estimates
4. Identifies methodological quality (risk of bias) as a partial moderator explaining ~20% of between-study heterogeneity
5. Provides synthesis across diverse cultural contexts (Brazil, China, Switzerland, India, Jordan, Iran, Nigeria)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Search strategy**: PubMed/Medline, Web of Science, Scopus, EMBASE — through June 2021

**Inclusion criteria**:
- Observational studies
- Assessed halitosis in association with OHRQoL
- Any population, any OHRQoL instrument

**Screening/selection**:
- 13 studies included in the systematic review
- 10 studies eligible for meta-analysis (all cross-sectional)
- Total n = 2,692 individuals

**OHRQoL instruments used across included studies**:
- OHIP-14 (Oral Health Impact Profile-14)
- HALT (Halitosis Associated Life-Quality Test)
- OMQ-QoL (Oral Malodor Quality of Life)
- GOHAI (Geriatric Oral Health Assessment Index)
- Custom/translated instruments

**Halitosis assessment methods across studies**:
- Organoleptic scoring
- Volatile sulfur compound (VSC) measurement (Halimeter)
- Self-reported halitosis
- Combinations of the above

**Statistical analysis**:
- Pooled standardized mean difference (SMD) — random-effects model (DerSimonian & Laird)
- Subgroup analyses: age group (adults vs. children/adolescents), OHRQoL instrument, halitosis measurement method, cultural background
- Meta-regression: examined moderators of between-study heterogeneity
- Publication bias: Egger's test

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Result |
|---|---|
| Pooled SMD (all studies) | 0.51 (95% CI 0.27–0.75) |
| Effect interpretation | Moderate effect (Cohen's d conventions) |
| Statistical significance | Yes (p < 0.01) |
| Heterogeneity | Substantial (I² reported) |
| Adults subgroup SMD | Significant association maintained |
| Children/adolescents subgroup | Association NOT significant |
| OHRQoL instrument influence | No significant effect on pooled estimate |
| Halitosis measurement method influence | No significant effect on pooled estimate |
| Cultural background influence | No significant effect on pooled estimate |
| Risk of bias explanation of heterogeneity | ~20% of between-study variance |

**Publication bias**: Meta-regression and Egger's test indicated potential publication bias concern; high-risk-of-bias studies overestimated the association magnitude.

**Included study populations**:
- Adults from Brazil, China, Switzerland, India, Jordan, Iran, Nigeria, New Zealand
- Age range: adolescents to elderly; adult-specific analyses most robust

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- All 10 meta-analyzed studies were cross-sectional → cannot establish causality (directionality unclear: does halitosis cause OHRQoL impairment, or do patients with poor OHRQoL perceive more halitosis?)
- High heterogeneity across studies limits interpretation
- Publication bias risk — methodologically weaker studies overestimate effect
- Only 3 of 13 included studies included children/adolescents, limiting subgroup power
- Inconsistency in halitosis measurement methods across studies (organoleptic vs VSC vs self-report)
- Lack of longitudinal/interventional data: no RCT evidence on whether treating halitosis improves OHRQoL
- Future need: prospective/interventional studies to test whether halitosis treatment improves OHRQoL

## 7. Glossary

- **OHRQoL (Oral Health-Related Quality of Life, 구강건강관련 삶의 질)**: Patient-reported measure of how oral conditions affect functional, psychosocial, and social well-being
- **SMD (Standardized Mean Difference, 표준화 평균 차이)**: Effect size metric allowing comparison across studies using different scales; SMD ~0.2 = small, ~0.5 = moderate, ~0.8 = large
- **OHIP-14 (Oral Health Impact Profile-14)**: Most widely used OHRQoL questionnaire; 14 items across 7 domains
- **HALT (Halitosis Associated Life-Quality Test, 구취관련 삶의 질 검사)**: Halitosis-specific QoL instrument
- **VSC (Volatile Sulfur Compounds, 휘발성 황화합물)**: H₂S, CH₃SH — primary chemical mediators of halitosis; measured by Halimeter
- **Organoleptic scoring (관능 평가)**: Clinical assessment of breath odor by trained examiner (gold standard)
- **Meta-regression (메타회귀)**: Statistical technique to identify moderators of pooled effect size across studies
