## Why Ingested

[[wiki/oral-surgery/cervera-espert-2016-coronectomy-mandibular-third-molar-sr]] established the IAN-protective effect but predates modern meta-analytic rigor (Peto OR for rare events, GRADE, trial sequential analysis). This 2026 SR+MA reinforces the anchor with a conclusive effect estimate (Peto OR 0.23 for IAN injury, TSA-confirmed) and modern certainty grading, strengthening rather than overturning the foundational conclusion.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (8 studies — 3 RCTs + 5 cohorts, 1488 teeth): coronectomy cut IAN injury vs total extraction (Peto OR 0.23, 95% CI 0.13–0.39, p<0.0001; TSA-conclusive), with no difference in dry socket or infection and a low 1.2% root-retrieval re-operation rate.

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (8편 — RCT 3 + 코호트 5, 1488치): 치관절제가 발치 대비 하치조신경 손상 감소 (Peto OR 0.23, 95% CI 0.13–0.39, p<0.0001, TSA 확정), 건성치조염·감염은 차이 없음, 치근 회수 재수술률 1.2%로 낮음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis, PRISMA, PROSPERO CRD420261305351
- **Journal**: Cureus 2026;18(3):e105646 (published 22 Mar 2026)
- **Risk of bias**: RoB 2.0 (RCTs), ROBINS-I (non-randomized); GRADE certainty; trial sequential analysis (TSA)
- **Statistics**: random-effects REML with Hartung-Knapp-Sidik-Jonkman (HKSJ) adjustment; Peto OR for rare events; Freeman-Tukey transform for single-arm proportions

## 2. Key Contributions

- Methodologically the most rigorous coronectomy-vs-extraction meta-analysis to date (Peto OR for zero-cell rare events, HKSJ CIs, prediction intervals, TSA, GRADE).
- TSA confirms the IAN-injury-prevention evidence is *conclusive* (not merely significant).
- Quantifies that root migration is frequent but re-operation to retrieve roots is rare (pooled 1.2%).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Databases: PubMed/MEDLINE, Embase, Scopus, Cochrane (inception to present).
- PICOS: high-risk MTM (radiographic IAN proximity); coronectomy vs total extraction; RCTs + prospective cohorts.
- 8 studies included (3 RCTs, 5 cohorts), 1488 teeth.
- Outcomes pooled with random-effects models; NNT/NNH conversion for clinical translation.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **IAN injury**: Peto OR **0.23** (95% CI 0.13–0.39, p<0.0001) favoring coronectomy; **TSA-conclusive**.
- **Dry socket**: RR 0.68, p=0.22 (no significant difference).
- **Postoperative infection**: RR 0.87, p=0.71 (no significant difference).
- **Re-operation (root retrieval)**: pooled **1.2%** (95% CI 0.0–4.4%).
- Root migration frequent but rarely requires secondary surgery.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 8 studies; some single-arm proportional outcomes have wide CIs.
- Heterogeneity in surgical protocol, imaging modality, and follow-up; explored via subgroup/meta-regression.
- Long-term (>multi-year) retained-root outcomes still limited.

## 7. Glossary

- **Peto OR**: odds-ratio method suited to rare events / zero-cell studies.
- **TSA (trial sequential analysis)**: adjusts for repeated testing / required information size to judge whether evidence is conclusive.
- **HKSJ adjustment**: conservative CI correction for meta-analyses with few studies.
- **NNT / NNH**: number needed to treat / to harm.
- **GRADE**: certainty-of-evidence grading framework.
