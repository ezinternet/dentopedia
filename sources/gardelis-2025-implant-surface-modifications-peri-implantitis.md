## One-line Summary
Systematic review (17 studies, ≥3-year follow-up) — rough/modified implant surfaces carried higher peri-implantitis recurrence and implant loss after surgical treatment than smooth surfaces; reconstructive approaches did better, but certainty is low.

## 한줄요약
체계적문헌고찰(17편·추적 ≥3년): 수술적 임플란트주위염 치료 후 거친(modified) 표면이 매끈한(machined) 표면보다 재발·임플란트 상실 위험이 높음; 재건술식이 더 유리하나 근거확실성은 낮음.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Links implant surface roughness to long-term (≥3y) surgical peri-implantitis outcomes.
- Rough surfaces → higher recurrence and implant loss.
- Reconstructive (graft + membrane) approaches outperformed non-reconstructive.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- SR of clinical studies, minimum 3-year follow-up, surgical peri-implantitis treatment stratified by surface type (smooth vs rough).
- Outcomes: recurrence, survival, clinical/radiographic parameters, surgical approach (reconstructive vs non-reconstructive). 17 studies included.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Rough (modified) surfaces: higher peri-implantitis recurrence and implant loss vs smooth (machined/turned).
- Reconstructive approaches (bone grafts + membranes): more favorable outcomes than non-reconstructive.
- Certainty of evidence: LOW (heterogeneity, small samples, methodological limits).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- High heterogeneity, no meta-analysis, low certainty; surface classification crude (smooth vs rough). Need well-designed long-term trials.

## 6. Related Work
- Smeets 2016 surface modifications; Monje 2025/Soldini 2025 reconstructive RCTs.

## 7. Glossary
- Modified/rough surface: micro-roughened (e.g., SLA, TiUnite). Machined/turned: smooth surface.
