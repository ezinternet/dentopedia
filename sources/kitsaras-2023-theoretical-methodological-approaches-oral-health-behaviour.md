## Why Ingested

Situates motivational interviewing (MI) within the broader theory-and-method landscape of oral-health behaviour change. The wiki holds MI-specific efficacy/uptake papers — [[behavioral-dentistry/motivational-interviewing/kay-2016-motivational-interviewing-general-dental-practice]] and [[behavioral-dentistry/motivational-interviewing/bonetti-2018-general-health-promotion-dental-engage]] — but lacked a meta-level map showing that MI is *one technique within a wider design space* governed by overarching theories (COM-B) and frameworks (Behaviour Change Wheel). This narrative review provides that scaffold: how to *select* a theory, *develop* via co-design, and *deliver* (increasingly via technology). It frames where MI sits and warns that uncritical theory-borrowing from medicine is hazardous.

PubMed abstract-only (PMID 36749671). DOI: [10.1111/cdoe.12817](https://doi.org/10.1111/cdoe.12817).

## One-line Summary
Narrative review (Community Dent Oral Epidemiol 2023) mapping the theory/method/delivery landscape of oral-health behaviour change: overarching theories like COM-B and the Behaviour Change Wheel must be chosen with care, interventions should be co-designed with users/stakeholders from the start, and technology is now a major delivery vehicle — MI is one technique within this wider, theory-driven design space.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Frames oral-health behaviour change as a fast-developing field that has lagged other health disciplines but is now actively borrowing their theories.
- Names **COM-B** (Capability, Opportunity, Motivation → Behaviour) and the **Behaviour Change Wheel** as the leading overarching theory/framework opportunities for oral-health interventions.
- Argues that the **proliferation of theories creates dilemmas and hazards** — uncritical theory selection is a real risk, not a neutral default.
- Elevates **co-design / co-development with users and stakeholders from the outset** as a defining component of successful interventions that oral-health work must implement consistently.
- Identifies **technology-based approaches** as a major delivery vehicle for the field.
- Positions specific techniques (e.g. motivational interviewing) as elements selected within a larger design space — design, development, and delivery are distinct decisions.

## 3. Methodology
- Narrative review / expert synthesis (abstract-only — full text not retrieved); no systematic search protocol reported in the abstract.
- Organizes the field along three axes: **designing** (which theory/framework), **developing** (how — co-design with stakeholders), and **delivering** (through what channel — increasingly technology).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- No quantitative effect sizes (conceptual narrative review; abstract-only — full text not retrieved).
- Bottom line: with multiple options at each of the design/development/delivery stages, **careful, user-inclusive, adaptable selection** is essential; the field should learn lessons from other disciplines while maximizing the available theories, frameworks, methodologies, and techniques.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Conceptual, non-systematic; offers orientation rather than graded evidence.
- Cautions explicitly that the variety/variation of available theories creates dilemmas and potential hazards — borrowing without fit-checking can mislead.
- Abstract-only here: specific worked examples, technique inventory, and detailed recommendations in the full text are not captured.

## 7. Glossary
- **COM-B**: behaviour model holding that behaviour (B) arises from Capability, Opportunity, and Motivation; the hub of the Behaviour Change Wheel.
- **Behaviour Change Wheel (BCW)**: a framework built around COM-B that maps intervention functions and policy categories to behavioural diagnosis.
- **Co-design / co-development**: developing an intervention together with intended users and stakeholders from the start, rather than designing for them.
- **Motivational interviewing (MI)**: a patient-centred, directive counselling style for eliciting behaviour change (Miller & Rollnick); one technique selectable within the wider design space.
