## Why Ingested

Adds the first operational appointment-attendance / no-show evidence to the practice-management category, which previously held only regulatory ([[practice-management/ha-2023-dentist-issues-constitutional-court-decisions]]) and clinical-workflow ([[practice-management/nosrat-2025-endodontist-gp-nsrct-clinical-approaches-cohort]]) pages. Missed appointments are a core chair-time and resource-efficiency problem; this retrospective gives concrete who/when patterns (single, female, ≤35y, morning slots) that managers can act on, extending the category from "what the clinic may do" toward "how the clinic runs its schedule."

## One-line Summary

Retrospective record review of 1,364 missed dental appointments (Riyadh military medical city, Jan–May 2024): nonattendees were predominantly single (56.2%), female (60.7%), ≤35y (67.3%), with morning slots and new-patient/initial visits most missed; day-of-week and age vs. time/date showed no significant association.

## 한줄요약

사우디 리야드 군병원 치과센터의 4개월간 미내원(no-show) 1,364건 후향 분석 — 미내원자는 미혼(56.2%), 여성(60.7%), 35세 이하(67.3%)가 다수였고 오전·초진 예약에서 결석이 많았으며, 요일·연령과 시간/날짜 사이에는 유의한 연관이 없었다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Retrospective secondary data analysis (single-center record review)
- **Journal**: Cureus 2024;16(8):e67061
- **Setting**: Dental Center, Department of Oral and Dental Health, Prince Sultan Military Medical City, Riyadh, Saudi Arabia
- **Period**: 1 January 2024 – 1 May 2024 (4 months)
- **Sample**: 1,364 nonattended (missed) appointment records
- **Analysis software**: IBM SPSS v25.0
- **Ethics**: IRB-approved (Prince Sultan Military Medical City); de-identified data, written consent waived; Declaration of Helsinki

## 2. Key Contributions

- First retrospective secondary analysis of dental-appointment nonattendance among Riyadh City dental centers (per authors).
- Profiles *who* misses appointments (marital status, gender, age, health status, visit type) and *when* (time-of-day, day-of-week).
- Translates the descriptive pattern into two operational levers: offer **evening appointments** and **walk-in slots** to recover missed chair time.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Retrospective secondary analysis of existing appointment medical records (no patient contact).
- **Population**: All patients with scheduled appointments during the study window; included all ages/genders and those with prior nonattendance history. Excluded records that were undocumented or had incomplete information.
- **Data capture**: Standardized data sheet recording appointment date/time, demographics (age, gender, marital status), health status (fit/unfit), previous attendance history, and dental-service/visit type.
- **Independent variables**: demographics (age, gender, health status), prior attendance history, appointment time, service type.
- **Statistics**: correlation analysis, ANOVA, and chi-square tests; descriptive statistics for population characteristics; inferential statistics for associations.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Profile of nonattendees (N=1,364):**
- Marital status: Single **56.2%** (767), Married 43.3% (591), Divorced 0.1%, Widow 0.3%.
- Gender: Female **60.7%** (828), Male 39.3% (536).
- Health status: Fit 59.4% (810), Unfit 40.6% (554).
- Age: ≤25y **36.7%** (500), 26–35y **30.6%** (417), 36–45y 19.9%, 46–55y 9.1%, ≥56y only **3.8%** (52). → ≤35y = 67.3% of all no-shows.

**Timing:**
- Most nonattendance for **morning** slots (8:00 AM–3:00 PM); fewer 4:00–8:00 PM.
- Day-of-week totals: **Tuesday highest (331)**, Monday 274, Wednesday 273, Thursday 251, **Sunday lowest (234)**.
- New (initial) patients dominated misses: Sunday 86.0% new vs 14.0% follow-up; Monday 83.2% new.

**Association tests (all non-significant):**
- Age group vs appointment time: F = 0.224, **p = 0.925**.
- Age group vs appointment date: F = 0.840, **p = 0.500**.
- Day-of-week vs marital status p=0.919; gender p=0.908; health status p=0.971; age p=0.954; visit type p=0.137.
- Correlations: age vs date r = 0.029 (weak positive); age vs time r = −0.011 (weak negative).

**Bottom line:** clear *demographic* skew (single, female, young, new-patient, morning) but **no statistically significant** day/time-by-characteristic interactions in this sample.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-center, military-medical-city population in Riyadh → limited generalizability.
- Short window (4 months); authors recommend a **full-year** dataset.
- Limited variable set — no distance-to-clinic, insurance, reminder-system, or socioeconomic data (factors flagged by prior systematic reviews).
- Descriptive/associational only; no multivariable model, no effect sizes for intervention impact.
- No comparison group of attended appointments → cannot compute true no-show *rate* or relative risk.

## 6. Related Work

- Prior systematic review cited: young age, insurance coverage, and distance from clinic as key nonattendance factors; previous nonattendance predicts future nonattendance.
- Alkhuwaiter et al.: fear/anxiety contributing to missed first appointments (supported here by high new-patient miss rate).
- Bhatia et al.: multiple factors including appointment timing contributing to missed dental visits.
- A prior Saudi study emphasized retrospective data analysis for predicting nonattendance rates.

## 7. Glossary

- **Nonattendance / no-show**: a scheduled appointment the patient fails to attend without prior cancellation.
- **Visit type (New vs Follow-up)**: initial (first) appointment vs subsequent/review appointment.
- **Fit / Unfit (health status)**: recorded medical fitness — fit = healthy, unfit = has medical issues.
- **ANOVA**: analysis of variance, tests differences in means across groups.
- **Chi-square test**: tests association between categorical variables.
