## Why Ingested

[[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/feng-2024-autogenous-particulated-dentin-graft-arp]]가 autogenous dentin graft의 ARP 효과를 보였으나, gold-standard xenograft (DBBM)와의 직접 head-to-head RCT + 6개월 histology 근거가 부족했다. 본 RCT (Xu 2026, n=50)는 DDM vs DBBM을 CBCT 차원변화·histomorphometry로 직접 비교해, [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/kim-2024-collagenated-xenograft-arp-rct]] 같은 xenograft RCT에 autogenous-dentin 대안 근거를 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

RCT (n=50; DDM 25 vs DBBM 25): autogenous demineralized dentin matrix showed greater early (3-mo) horizontal resorption at mid-socket (0.77 vs 0.44 mm, p=0.006) but comparable 6-month dimensional stability and superior histological tissue integration (active remodeling, SATB2+ osteoblasts) vs DBBM.

## 한줄요약

RCT (n=50; DDM 25 vs DBBM 25): 자가 탈회상아질기질(DDM)은 3개월 시점 socket 중간부 수평흡수가 더 컸으나(0.77 vs 0.44 mm, p=0.006), 6개월 차원안정성은 DBBM과 동등했고 조직학적 통합·신생골 형성은 더 우수해 ARP 대안으로 유효.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First (per authors) high-level RCT directly comparing autogenous demineralized dentin matrix (DDM) against the gold-standard xenograft DBBM (Bio-Oss) for alveolar ridge preservation (ARP), pairing CBCT dimensional analysis with 6-month histomorphometry/immunohistochemistry.
2. Demonstrates that DDM has **distinct remodeling kinetics** — significantly faster early (3-month) horizontal contraction at the mid-socket — yet reaches **6-month dimensional stability statistically equivalent to DBBM** at all levels (horizontal coronal/middle/apical, vertical buccal/lingual).
3. Provides histological evidence that DDM undergoes active biodegradation (osteoclastic lacunae) and is intimately fused with mature new bone with strong osteogenic markers (Ki-67, SATB2), whereas DBBM remains a quiescent, non-resorbing osteoconductive scaffold.
4. Frames DDM as a biologically viable, autogenous, cost-effective "waste-to-resource" alternative that recycles the extracted tooth and avoids xenograft disease-transmission/immune-rejection risk.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Prospective, randomized, single-blind RCT. Computer-generated randomization; allocation concealment via sequentially numbered opaque sealed envelopes (SNOSE). Surgeon not blinded (distinct graft appearance); radiographic and histological assessors blinded.
- **Participants**: 50 patients requiring single-tooth extraction (Liaoning, Feb–Jun 2025). 21 male / 29 female, age 21–70 (mean 47.2 ± 13.6 y).
- **Groups**: Test = DDM (n=25); Control = DBBM (n=25).
- **Inclusion**: age 18–70, good health; single non-restorable premolar/molar (positions 4–7) with adjacent teeth; ≥3 mm residual apical bone height; sufficient tooth structure for DDM prep; no acute infection.
- **Exclusion**: uncontrolled systemic disease; heavy smoking (>10/day); bone-metabolism drugs (bisphosphonates, corticosteroids) within 6 mo; head/neck radiation; metabolic bone disease; severe untreated periodontitis; pregnancy/lactation.
- **DDM preparation**: extracted tooth mechanically cleaned (calculus/caries/PDL removed), processed with autogenous tooth bone graft system (TSBK-ATB-01, Beijing Topstissue) into dentin particles (400–800 µm), partially demineralized and sterilized, rinsed, densely packed into socket.
- **DBBM**: Bio-Oss packed into socket.
- **Both groups**: minimally invasive flapless extraction preserving plates + thorough curettage; graft covered with resorbable collagen membrane (Bio-Gide, Geistlich) and crisscross non-resorbable sutures.
- **Radiographic**: CBCT at T0 (post-op), T1 (3 mo), T2 (6 mo). Mimics 21.0 3D reconstruction; standardized reorientation on stable landmarks. Proportional H_ref protocol — horizontal width measured at fixed 25% (apical), 50% (middle), 75% (coronal) vertical coordinates so measurements occur at identical spatial coordinates across timepoints (avoids crestal-resorption measurement shift).
- **Clinical**: Landry healing index at 7 d, 3 mo, 6 mo.
- **Histology**: subset of 10 patients (5/group) bone core biopsy at 6 mo; HE, Ki-67, SATB2 immunohistochemistry.
- **Statistics**: SPSS v27; Shapiro-Wilk normality; t-test or Mann-Whitney U; Chi-square/Fisher; ICC for reliability (intra-examiner ICC >0.9). Two-tailed p<0.05. Sample size: ≥20/group for power 0.80, alpha 0.05; 50 enrolled with 20% dropout allowance.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Baseline**: no significant between-group differences (age, sex, jaw, tooth type). All 50 completed 3- and 6-month follow-up; **no dropouts**.
- **3-month dimensional change (DDM vs DBBM)**:
  - Horizontal middle (50%): **0.77 ± 0.59 vs 0.44 ± 0.45 mm — significant, p=0.006** (DDM greater loss).
  - Horizontal apical (25%): 0.55 ± 0.43 vs 0.36 ± 0.26 mm; NS (p=0.069).
  - Horizontal coronal (75%): 1.16 ± 0.84 vs 1.26 ± 1.39 mm; NS (p=0.684).
  - Vertical buccal: 1.50 ± 1.19 vs 1.06 ± 0.75 mm; NS (p=0.197).
  - Vertical lingual: 0.91 ± 0.82 vs 0.85 ± 0.67 mm; NS (p=0.846).
- **6-month dimensional change (DDM vs DBBM) — all NS**:
  - Vertical buccal: 2.12 ± 1.30 vs 1.72 ± 0.84 mm; p=0.205.
  - Vertical lingual: 1.47 ± 0.83 vs 1.21 ± 0.68 mm; p=0.238.
  - Horizontal coronal (75%): 2.10 ± 1.71 vs 1.95 ± 1.59 mm; p=0.628.
  - Horizontal middle (50%): 1.42 ± 1.10 vs 1.14 ± 0.74 mm; p=0.426.
  - Horizontal apical (25%): 0.85 ± 0.49 vs 0.73 ± 0.51 mm; p=0.118.
- **Soft tissue (Landry, 7 d)**: DDM 3.88 ± 0.33 vs DBBM 3.76 ± 0.52; NS (p=0.419). No severe infection or graft rejection in either group.
- **Re-entry (6 mo)**: both groups' sockets completely filled with regenerated hard tissue; width/height preserved; all implants placed with satisfactory primary stability, no complications.
- **Histology (6 mo)**:
  - **DDM**: residual particles undergoing active remodeling, intimately surrounded by mature new bone + highly vascularized connective tissue; dense Ki-67+ (active turnover/angiogenesis); strong SATB2 nuclear expression in osteoblasts; osteoclastic lacunae on particle surfaces (biodegradation → host bone).
  - **DBBM**: new bone primarily along DBBM particle surfaces; tissue quiescent (minimal Ki-67); SATB2+ osteoblasts lining new bone; no particle resorption (stable osteoconductive scaffold).
- **Radiographic integration**: at 6 mo DBBM particles remained discrete high-density radiopacities; DDM radiodensity merged with host bone (blurred boundaries) — interpreted as tissue integration / active remodeling.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Relatively small sample size (n=50; histology subset only 10).
- Short follow-up (6 months); long-term marginal bone stability under implant loading not assessed.
- Single-center, single-surgeon; single-blind only (surgeon unblinded).
- Vertical resorption >2 mm seen in some cases in both groups, possibly reflecting thin-buccal-bone phenotype predominance; bundle-bone resorption physiologically inevitable regardless of graft.
- Future: longer-term implant-loading outcomes, larger multicenter samples.

## 6. Related Work

- Schropp et al., Araujo et al., Tan et al. — post-extraction dimensional change kinetics (rapid remodeling 0–3 mo, continuing to 6 mo; horizontal loss 3.79–3.87 mm, vertical >1 mm at 6 mo).

- Chappuis et al., Timock et al. — CBCT standardized reorientation methodology.

## 7. Glossary

- **DDM (Demineralized Dentin Matrix)**: autogenous graft processed from the patient's own extracted tooth; partially demineralized dentin particles (here 400–800 µm) exposing type I collagen and growth factors (BMP, TGF-β, OPN).
- **DBBM (Deproteinized Bovine Bone Mineral)**: xenograft (e.g., Bio-Oss); osteoconductive, low-resorption "gold-standard" for volume stability in ARP.
- **ARP (Alveolar Ridge Preservation)**: grafting the extraction socket to minimize post-extraction ridge atrophy.
- **Creeping substitution**: gradual replacement of graft material by host bone via coupled resorption/new-bone formation.
- **SATB2**: nuclear transcription factor marker of osteoblastic differentiation.
- **Ki-67**: cell-proliferation marker (active turnover/angiogenesis).
- **Landry healing index**: 1 (very poor) to 5 (excellent) soft-tissue healing scale.
- **BIC (Bone-Implant Contact)**: proportion of implant surface in direct contact with bone.
