## One-line Summary
SR+MA of 130 studies (20 in MA) and 1972 perforated-and-repaired sinuses: pooled implant loss in repaired SMP sites **4% (95% CI 2–8%)**; no significant difference vs non-perforated sinuses; perforation size not associated with implant loss (meta-regression P=0.86). Resorbable collagen membrane is the most commonly reported repair material. Conclusion: properly repaired Schneiderian membrane perforations do not compromise implant survival.

- **Institution**: Universidade Federal de Alfenas (Brazil), São Paulo State University (Araraquara, Brazil)
- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis (PRISMA, random-effects)
- **Search**: PubMed, Web of Science, Scopus, Embase, Cochrane — up to Feb 2021

## 2. Key Contributions
- Largest synthesis to date on **sinus membrane perforation (SMP) repair outcomes** (1972 repaired sinuses).
- Quantifies pooled implant loss after SMP repair: **4%** — comparable to non-perforated baseline.
- Demonstrates **no association between perforation size and implant loss** (meta-regression P=0.86).
- Catalogs repair materials (collagen membrane most common) and complications (sinusitis most frequent).
- Supports clinical recommendation that proper repair preserves implant prognosis.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- PROSPERO-registered SR+MA; PRISMA guidelines.
- Electronic search: PubMed, Web of Science, Scopus, Embase, Cochrane Library through Feb 2021.
- Inclusion: studies reporting SMP encountered and submitted for repair during sinus elevation.
- Two independent reviewers; data extraction on repair material, perforation size, bone formation, complications, implant placement timing, implant loss.
- Random-effects model meta-analysis for proportion of implant loss.
- Meta-regression: perforation size vs implant loss.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **130 studies** included in qualitative synthesis; **20** in quantitative meta-analysis.
- **1972 sinuses** perforated and repaired.
- **Implant loss in repaired SMP sites**: pooled 4% (95% CI 2.0–8.0).
- **Implant loss: repaired SMP vs non-perforated**: no significant difference.
- **Implant loss vs perforation size**: not associated (meta-regression P=0.86).
- **Most common repair material**: resorbable collagen membrane.
- **Most common postoperative complication**: sinusitis.
- **Bone formation parameters**: majority of studies reported no difference between perforated-repaired and intact sinuses.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- High heterogeneity in repair protocols, materials, and outcome reporting.
- Many included studies are case series / retrospective with potential publication bias.
- Search closed February 2021 (some recent PRF / amnion-chorion studies excluded).
- Perforation size reporting inconsistent across studies — limits granular meta-regression.
- Need for standardized SMP size reporting and RCTs of repair materials.

## 7. Glossary
- **SMP (Sinus Membrane Perforation)**: any disruption of Schneiderian membrane continuity during sinus elevation.
- **Collagen membrane**: resorbable bovine/porcine collagen barrier; most common SMP repair material.
- **Meta-regression**: meta-analysis technique to assess whether a study-level covariate (e.g., perforation size) modifies the effect estimate.
- **PRISMA**: Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses.
- **PROSPERO**: international prospective register of systematic reviews.
