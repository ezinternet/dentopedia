## Why Ingested
Abfraction에 초점을 둔 임상 의사결정 review. [[nccl/grippo-2012-abfraction-abrasion-biocorrosion-nccl-20year]] schema를 임상 진단·치료 결정(특히 "수복하지 말고 monitoring")으로 번역. NCCL 보존적 관리 원칙의 근거.

## One-line Summary
Narrative review concluding abfraction theory is unproven, occlusal adjustment/restoration are not indicated to prevent progression, and asymptomatic lesions should be monitored >=6 months before any invasive procedure.

## 한줄요약
Abfraction 이론은 미입증이며 진행 예방 목적의 교합조정·수복은 적응증이 아니고, 무증상 병변은 침습적 처치 전 6개월 이상 monitoring하라고 결론지은 review.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Synthesizes evidence that occlusal loading shows only weak/inconsistent association with NCCLs; abfraction theory "yet to be proven."
- Reproduces Grippo pathodynamic schema and applies it to personalized risk-factor assessment.
- Explicit management stance: no restorative or occlusal-adjustment intervention to prevent further loss; restore only for form/function replacement, hypersensitivity relief, or esthetics.
- Recommends >=6-month monitoring of asymptomatic lesions; combined restorative-surgical approach when gingival recession coexists.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative review of etiology, diagnosis, and treatment literature; no systematic protocol or grading.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- OCT-based evidence cited: dentin demineralization promotes early NCCL formation while occlusal stress contributes to progression (multifactorial, not classic abfraction).
- Prevalence note: greater in incisors and premolars than canines/molars; mandibular premolars affected more often/severely than maxillary.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative; relies on heterogeneous primary studies. Abfraction mechanism still lacks confirmatory clinical data.

## 6. Related Work
- Grippo 2012 (schema source), finite-element/photoelastic stress studies, OCT NCCL studies.

## 7. Glossary
- Abfraction: "to break away" (Latin ab + fractio); cervical tooth flexure from occlusal stress causing microfractures.
- HSB: Hunter-Schreger band (low packing density cervically, proposed to facilitate abfraction).
