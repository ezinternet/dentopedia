## One-line Summary
Meta-analysis of 21 in vitro studies confirms IDS improves bond strength over DDS for indirect restorations; long-term benefit largest with 3-step etch-and-rinse adhesives or addition of a flowable resin layer.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Gels 2022, 8, 175 (MDPI)
- **DOI**: 10.3390/gels8030175
- **Search**: PubMed, Cochrane, ISI Web of Science, Scielo, Scopus, Embase (through Jan 2022)
- **Included**: 22 studies qualitative, 21 in meta-analysis (3,717 initial hits)
- **PICOS**: P=indirect restorations; I=IDS; C=DDS; O=bond strength; S=in vitro

## 2. Key Contributions
- Quantitative confirmation of IDS bond strength advantage (immediate and aged)
- Subgroup analysis by adhesive class:
  - 2-step EAR: NS for immediate, NS for aged
  - 3-step EAR: ↑ bond strength for both immediate and aged
  - 1-step SE: NS for immediate
  - 2-step SE: ↑ bond strength
  - Universal: ↑ bond strength
- Flowable resin + adhesive combination significantly enhances aged bond strength
- Supports 3-step EAR + flowable resin as the most reliable IDS protocol for long-term bond stability

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: SR + meta-analysis (PRISMA)
- **PICOS**: indirect restorations, IDS vs DDS, bond strength, in vitro
- **Adhesive subgroups**: 2-ER, 3-ER, 1-SE, 2-SE, universal adhesive
- **Aging conditions**: thermocycling, water storage
- **Analysis**: RevMan v5.4.1; standardized mean difference (SMD), 95% CI

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
**Immediate bond strength** (IDS vs DDS overall): IDS superior, p<0.001
**Aged bond strength** (IDS vs DDS overall): IDS superior, p=0.001

| Adhesive subgroup | Immediate BS | Aged BS |
|---|---|---|
| 2-step EAR | NS (p=0.07) | NS |
| 3-step EAR | IDS superior | IDS superior (p<0.001) |
| 1-step SE | NS (p=0.15) | NS |
| 2-step SE | IDS superior | IDS superior |
| Universal | IDS superior | IDS superior |
| Adhesive + flowable resin layer | IDS superior | IDS superior (p=0.01) |

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- All included studies in vitro — no clinical confirmation
- High heterogeneity across studies (substrate, aging protocols, test methods)
- Different brands of adhesives within subgroups
- Clinical RCTs comparing IDS vs DDS for indirect restorations are urgently needed
- Long-term (≥5y) clinical survival comparison still absent

## 6. Related Work
- Quantitative companion to Samartzi 2021 narrative IDS review
- Adjacent to Magne / Pashley original IDS conceptual work
- Critiqued by Reis 2024 (BRD myths #6) — clinical post-op sensitivity/longevity similar to DDS

## 7. Glossary
- **IDS**: Immediate Dentin Sealing
- **DDS**: Delayed Dentin Sealing
- **SMD (Standardized Mean Difference)**: Effect size for continuous outcomes across heterogeneous units
- **2-step EAR / 3-step EAR**: Two- or three-step Etch-and-Rinse adhesive
- **1-step / 2-step SE**: One- or two-step Self-Etch adhesive
- **Universal adhesive**: Multi-mode adhesive containing 10-MDP
- **Flowable resin**: Low-viscosity composite used as IDS overlay to fill irregularities
