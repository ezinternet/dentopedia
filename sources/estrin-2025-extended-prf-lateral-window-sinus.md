## One-line Summary
Multicenter case series (22 patients / 22 lateral window sinus lifts / 31 implants) using extended platelet-rich fibrin (e-PRF, heat-denatured albumin) as the sole barrier membrane covering the lateral window: 100% uneventful healing at 6 months, mean post-op sinus height 12.3 mm with mean endosinus bone gain 8.0 mm; suggests e-PRF as a 100% autologous, low-cost alternative to collagen membrane.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Clinical Implant Dentistry and Related Research 2025;27(1):e13427.
- **Type**: Original Article — multicenter case series.
- **Authors**: Authors from 3 clinical facilities; corresponding author R.J. Miron (holds IP on PRF).
- **Published online**: 13 December 2024; print 2025.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First case series using **extended PRF (e-PRF)** — produced by heating platelet-poor plasma to denature albumin, prolonging resorption from ~2 weeks (standard PRF) to 4–6 months — as the **sole barrier membrane** covering the lateral antrostomy window.
- Demonstrates that a 100% autologous membrane can replace collagen barriers in lateral window sinus lift while maintaining graft containment and avoiding soft tissue invagination.
- Provides quantitative endosinus bone-gain data at 6 months for a heterogeneous graft cohort (allograft / xenograft / autologous dentin "sticky bone").

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 22 patients (12 F / 10 M, mean age 58.8 y, range 32–78) from 3 clinical centers (June 2022 – December 2023).
- Standard lateral window technique using "sticky bone" with allograft, xenograft, or autologous dentin matrix.
- **e-PRF preparation**: PPP layer heated to denature albumin; resulting membrane used to cover the lateral window solely (no collagen).
- One case used e-PRF additionally to repair a sinus membrane perforation.
- Outcomes at 6-month CBCT: residual bone height (RBH), endosinus bone level change, implant survival at second-stage surgery.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Implants placed**: 31 across 22 lateral window lifts.
- **Survival at second-stage (~6 mo)**: 100% (no failures, no post-op complications).
- **Mean 6-month post-op sinus height**: 12.3 mm.
- **Mean endosinus bone gain**: 8.0 mm.
- No soft tissue invagination through lateral window; no graft displacement into buccal mucosa.
- e-PRF successfully sealed one intraoperative Schneiderian membrane perforation.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Case series — no comparator group (no head-to-head vs collagen membrane).
- Heterogeneous graft materials (allograft / xenograft / autologous dentin) confound the membrane-specific contribution.
- 6-month follow-up; long-term implant survival into grafted bone unknown.
- Conflict of interest: senior author holds IP on PRF and founded PRF R&D LLC.
- Larger longitudinal RCTs vs collagen membranes called for.

## 7. Glossary
- **e-PRF (extended platelet-rich fibrin)**: PRF membrane with heat-denatured albumin extending resorption to 4–6 mo.
- **PPP (platelet-poor plasma)**: supernatant layer above platelet/fibrin clot in centrifuged blood.
- **Sticky bone**: bone graft particles mixed with autologous fibrin matrix (i-PRF or PRF exudate) to improve handling.
- **RBH (Residual Bone Height)**: bone height under maxillary sinus floor prior to grafting.
- **Lateral window technique**: lateral antrostomy in maxillary sinus wall for graft access (Tatum/Boyne).
