## Why Ingested

The [[overviews/dental-appointment-no-show-overview]] flagged reminder-intervention RCT evidence as a gap — the overview catalogued no-show rates and reasons but lacked a controlled trial quantifying how much a reminder actually moves attendance. This paper supplies that: an SMS-reminder dental trial showing on-time attendance more than doubling (79.2% vs 35.5%). It also closes the loop on [[practice-management/bhatia-2018-pediatric-dental-appointments-no-show-rates-reasons]], where 48.3% of parents said they *want* a reminder the day before — Prasad 2012 demonstrates that acting on that preference works.

## One-line Summary

Controlled trial in 4 outpatient dental departments (India, n=206) — SMS appointment reminders raised on-time attendance to 79.2% vs 35.5% with no reminder.

## 한줄요약

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies the attendance effect of a low-cost SMS appointment reminder in a real dental-clinic setting.
- On-time attendance was **79.2% in the SMS test group vs 35.5% in the no-reminder control group** — a large, statistically significant difference.
- Frames SMS as a viable, inexpensive reminder channel relative to staff phone calls or mailed reminders, addressing the two most-cited nonattendance causes: forgetting and date/time confusion.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Patients attending the four departments over a 4-month period who provided a contact mobile number were enrolled.
- **n=206** total (male 124, female 82): test group **96** (male 57, female 39) received an SMS reminder; control group **110** (male 67, female 43) received no reminder.
- Outcome: rate of attendance "on time" at the scheduled appointment.
- (Abstract-only; randomization/allocation procedure, blinding, reminder timing/wording, and statistical test were not recoverable from the retrieved text.)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- On-time attendance: **test 79.2% vs control 35.5%** (reported as significantly higher in the test group).
- Effect direction is consistent with the broader reminder literature: an active reminder roughly doubles on-time attendance versus relying on patient memory.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Abstract-only ingest** — full text not retrieved; methodological detail (allocation, blinding, exact statistics, CI) is unavailable.
- Design appears to be a **quasi-experimental / non-randomized controlled trial** rather than a true RCT; group balance and allocation method are unstated, so selection/confounding cannot be excluded (e.g., test vs control assignment criteria unknown).
- Single-center, single-country (India), academic dental-school clinic — generalizability to private practice or other health systems is uncertain.
- No-show *cost* and patient-acceptability outcomes not reported.

## 7. Glossary

- **SMS (Short Message Service)**: text-message channel on mobile phones; here used to deliver appointment reminders.
- **Nonattendance / no-show**: failure to attend a scheduled appointment without prior cancellation.
- **On-time attendance**: arriving for the scheduled appointment at the appointed time (the trial's primary outcome).
- **ITS-CDSR**: ITS Centre for Dental Studies and Research, the study site.
