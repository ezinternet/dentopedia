As the first-ever umbrella review of teledentistry cost and oral-health evidence, it anchors the cost-of-care-model branch and pairs with the teledentistry SR-of-SRs being ingested concurrently.

## One-line Summary

First-ever umbrella review of teledentistry (10 systematic reviews, AMSTAR-2): teledentistry is widely *assumed* cost-saving and shows positive oral-health signals, but all included reviews were low/critically-low quality, so there is insufficient evidence for either long-term clinical effectiveness or true cost-effectiveness.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First umbrella review specifically synthesizing systematic reviews on teledentistry **costs and oral-health outcomes**.
- Applies the IADR e-oral health network **consensus definition** of teledentistry to classify interventions, rather than the (inconsistent) terms used by individual reviews — addressing the m-health / tele-oral medicine / telehealth terminology confusion.
- Quantifies the evidence gap: across 10 reviews, only **5 distinct primary studies** carried any form of economic evaluation, and none performed cost-benefit / cost-utility / incremental analysis.
- Documents that all 10 included reviews were **low or critically-low quality** on AMSTAR-2.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PRISMA-consistent; methodology per Aromataris et al. umbrella-review approach (analytical unit = the review, not primary studies).
- **Seven databases** searched inception → 24 Apr 2023 (PubMed/MEDLINE, EMBASE, CINAHL, Web of Science, Scopus, Cochrane CDSR, NHSEED & HTA) plus JBI, DARE, PROSPERO; manual cross-referencing + citation tracking; languages EN/NL/ES/DE.
- Eligibility: peer-reviewed systematic reviews of primary studies whose interventions met the e-oral health network teledentistry definition (synchronous or asynchronous provider↔patient/caregiver or provider↔provider communication separated by distance). Excluded: training-only telehealth, unidirectional comms (e.g. automated reminder texts), reviews not separately reporting teledentistry outcomes.
- Two independent reviewers (JS, AQ) screened in Rayyan; third reviewer (RM) for consensus. Quality appraised with **AMSTAR-2** (7 critical, 9 non-critical domains).
- **No meta-analysis** — heterogeneity of interventions, populations and outcomes precluded it; results synthesized narratively.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Searches yielded 880 records after deduplication → 171 full texts → **10 reviews included** (abstract cites 749 identified / 10 included).
- **Oral-health outcomes** (2 reviews): Ben-Omran 2021 found no significant GOHAI/OHRQoL difference vs control; Estai 2018 found teledentistry comparable to or better than non-telemedicine on DFS/periodontal indices/oral-hygiene scores. Both concluded evidence is not yet conclusive for long-term clinical effectiveness.
- **Costs** (10 reviews): all suggested teledentistry is cost-saving, but only 5 reviews reported costs as an outcome of interest, and the 10 reviews collectively drew on just **5 distinct primary economic-evaluation studies** (cost description, cost analysis, cost-minimization, time-effectiveness). Modality data (sync vs async) in only 2 reviews; asynchronous store-and-forward reported as least costly (Aquilanti 2020), real-time better on cost outcomes (Joshi 2020).
- **Quality**: every included review rated **critically low or low** on AMSTAR-2; weakest domains Q3, Q7, Q8, Q13, Q14; none reported primary-study funding (Q10).
- Benchmark: telemedicine had 33 economic evaluations identified 1990–2007 (Bergmo) — far more than teledentistry; consistent with Eze et al. telemedicine umbrella review (92% low/critically-low quality).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Findings limited by the **critically low quality** of the underlying reviews → high risk of bias; nine reviews excluded for missing key info (possible selection bias).
- Heterogeneity precluded subgroup/meta-analysis.
- Special-needs populations (disability, mental illness, institutionalized/older people) — high oral-disease burden but **no experimental teledentistry effectiveness study** exists for them.
- Authors call for high-quality experimental designs (RCTs, factorial, stepped-wedge, SMARTs, MRTs), economic evaluations adhering to Drummond standards, EQUATOR-network reporting, and **universal teledentistry terminology** (IADR e-oral health network).

## 7. Glossary

- **Teledentistry**: use of information/telecommunication technology to provide oral healthcare between a provider and patient/recipient or between providers separated by distance (IADR e-oral health network definition).
- **Umbrella review**: a systematic review of existing systematic reviews; analytical unit is the review, not primary studies.
- **AMSTAR-2**: validated tool to assess methodological quality of systematic reviews of randomized and non-randomized studies (7 critical + 9 non-critical domains).
- **Synchronous vs asynchronous (store-and-forward)**: real-time vs time-independent provider-patient communication.
- **Economic evaluation**: comparison of costs and outcomes of ≥2 alternatives (cost-benefit, cost-effectiveness, cost-utility, cost-minimization); partial evaluations = cost description / cost-consequence / cost analysis only.
