## One-line Summary
First SR focused on UK DCPs (hygienists, therapists, clinical dental technicians, orthodontic therapists) — only 12 studies exist, dominated by therapist/hygienist data; determinants map to the same macro/meso/micro framework as dentists, but overall evidence base is thin.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Primary Dental Journal, 11(2):40–50, June 2022
- DOI: 10.1177/20501684221101663
- PMID: 35658664
- Study type: Systematic review (no meta-analysis)
- Scope: UK only

## 2. Key Contributions
- Fills the gap left by dentist-only burnout literature — DCPs (the larger workforce numerically) had been understudied
- Confirms that the macro/meso/micro determinant framework (Salazar 2019) applies to DCPs too
- Identifies job satisfaction and professional career trajectory as primary explored factors
- Documents the workforce subgroups that are essentially absent from the literature: clinical dental technicians, orthodontic therapists

## 3. Methodology
- 7-database SR
- Inclusion: studies of UK dental hygienists, dental therapists, clinical dental technicians, or orthodontic therapists
- Mixed Methods Appraisal Tool (MMAT) for quality
- Qualitative synthesis (heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis)

## 4. Key Results
- 12 included studies
- Quality: 11 medium-to-high (4* or 5*), 1 low (2*)
- 10 studies focused on dental therapists and/or hygienists
- 1 study each on orthodontic therapists and clinical dental technicians
- Job satisfaction and professional careers = the primary determinants explored
- Determinants map to macro (healthcare system, regulation), meso (job spec, workplace), micro (personal, relationships)
- General lack of evidence on overall health & wellbeing per se

## 5. Limitations
- UK-only — generalizability limited
- Very small evidence base (n=12)
- Outcome heterogeneity prevents pooling
- Specific DCP subgroups (clinical dental technicians, orthodontic therapists) essentially unstudied

## 6. Related Work
- Plessas 2022 (intervention side, broader population)
- Salazar 2019 model of dental professional wellbeing determinants
- BDA workforce surveys

## 7. Glossary
- **DCP**: Dental Care Professional — non-dentist clinical roles (hygienist, therapist, technician, nurse)
- **Macro factors**: healthcare system, regulation
- **Meso factors**: job specification, workplace characteristics
- **Micro factors**: personal aspects, career stage, relationships
- **MMAT**: Mixed Methods Appraisal Tool
