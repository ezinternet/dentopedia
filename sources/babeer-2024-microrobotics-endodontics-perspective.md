## Why Ingested
Frontier perspective on magnetically controlled microrobots for root canal disinfection — a technology review extending [[endodontics/irrigation/boutsioukis-2022-irrigants-irrigation-methods-status-directions]] (which concluded current activation methods cannot predictably access anatomical intricacies) into nanotechnology-enabled multifunctional solutions. Complements [[endodontics/ordinola-zapata-2022-intracanal-medicaments-status-directions]] by targeting inaccessible biofilm that even optimal medicament strategies cannot reach.

## One-line Summary
Perspective narrative review describing three categories of magnetically controlled microrobots (aggregated iron oxide microswarms, 3D-molded soft helicoids ~700 μm × 1.5 mm, and helical silica nanorobots ~3 μm) that can disrupt biofilm, generate ROS, deliver antibiotics on-demand, and retrieve microbiome samples from root canal intricacies inaccessible to conventional methods.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Reviews the landscape of medical microrobotics and its direct application to endodontic disinfection challenges.
- Describes three endodontic microrobot platforms in detail: aggregated IONP microswarms, 3D-molded soft helicoids, and helical silica nanorobots.
- IONP microswarms: magnetically configurable; disrupt mixed-species biofilm; retrieve apical microbiome samples; catalytic peroxidase-like activity (H₂O₂ → free radicals at acidic pH); showed higher killing than conventional 3% NaOCl in preliminary experiments.
- 3D-molded helicoids: ~700 μm × 1.5 mm, hydrogel-embedded IONPs; rotating magnetic field drives screw-like shear; loaded with antibiotic microcapsules released by rapid oscillating field; CBCT-trackable position.
- Silica nanorobots: ~3 μm, glancing-angle-deposition fabrication; penetrate dentinal tubules to ~1000 μm depth (deeper than current adjunctive methods); retrieved by switching magnetic field; hyperthermia kills E. faecalis inside tubules.
- First integration of simultaneous biofilm disruption, antimicrobial killing, drug delivery, and diagnostic sampling in a single endodontic platform.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Design: narrative perspective review (non-systematic), HHS Public Access author manuscript.
- Scope: general microrobotics advances in medicine → magnetic microrobots for biofilm → three specific endodontic platforms → future outlook.
- Evidence level: in vitro and ex vivo proof-of-concept studies; no human/animal RCTs.
- Key limitation acknowledged by authors: pre-clinical stage; clinical translation, safety, and complete nanoparticle retrieval not yet established.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Aggregated IONP microswarms: in vitro — disrupted mixed-species biofilm on canal walls; retrieved microbial samples from apical region; eliminated biofilm within narrow isthmus (Hwang 2019 cited); preliminary killing activity vs 3% NaOCl (comparative data reported but specific % kill not quantified in available text).
- 3D helicoids (~700 μm × 1.5 mm): actuated to apical region in vitro and tracked via CBCT in ex vivo root canals; retrieved by reversing rotational field; antibiotic release on-demand via rapid oscillating field.
- Helical silica nanorobots (~3 μm): dentinal tubule penetration depth ~1000 μm — exceeds current adjunctive endodontic methods; hyperthermia (high oscillating magnetic field) kills E. faecalis inside tubules in vitro; partially retrieved by field switching (complete retrieval requires additional strategies).
- Microswarms and helicoids: both contain IONPs with intrinsic catalytic (peroxidase-like) activity generating ROS from H₂O₂ at acidic pH.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- All data are in vitro / ex vivo proof-of-concept; no in vivo or clinical trials.
- Complete retrieval of nanorobots from all dentinal tubule sections not yet demonstrated; elemental confirmation (ICP-OES, SEM-EDS) still pending for microswarms.
- Biocompatibility and systemic safety of IONPs in the endodontic context untested clinically.
- Microrobot size limits access to complex 3D anatomies beyond current test geometries.
- Future: in vivo feasibility, safety profiling, scale-up fabrication, integration with existing clinical imaging for real-time navigation, use in regenerative endodontics where mechanical instrumentation is avoided.

## 6. Related Work
- Boutsioukis & Arias-Moliz 2022 (IEJ): directly cited as establishing that existing activation methods lack predictable access to root canal intricacies and cannot improve long-term outcomes — the clinical gap that microrobotics aims to fill.
- Ordinola-Zapata 2022 (IEJ): intracanal medicaments review — microrobots could enhance delivery of antimicrobials to spaces where Ca(OH)₂ fails.
- Hwang et al. 2019, Dasgupta et al. 2022, Babeer et al. 2022: primary experimental papers on helicoids, silica nanorobots, and microswarms respectively.

## 7. Glossary
- IONP: iron oxide nanoparticle — catalytic, magnetically controllable building block.
- EPS: extracellular polymeric substance — biofilm matrix barrier.
- ROS: reactive oxygen species — generated by IONP peroxidase-like catalysis in H₂O₂.
- Glancing angle deposition (GLAD): nanofabrication technique producing helical silica nanostructures.
- ICP-OES: inductively coupled plasma – optical emission spectrometry (elemental analysis for nanoparticle retrieval confirmation).
- Hyperthermia: heat generation by IONPs under high oscillating magnetic fields — kills bacteria within dentinal tubules.
