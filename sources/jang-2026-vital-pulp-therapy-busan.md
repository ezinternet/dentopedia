## 3. Methodology and Architecture
This is a clinical expert lecture, not a primary research study.

**Concept of Vital Pulpectomy (바이탈 근관치료)**:
- Remove only potentially infected intracoronal/upper canal pulp tissue
- Preserve healthy apical pulp to stimulate tertiary dentin formation
- Working length: 2–3 mm short of apex (상아-시멘트 경계부)
- Canal enlargement: 2–3 sizes above initial file size, removed within 2–3 years

**Protocol Steps**:
1. **Access Opening**: Wide-field access, straight-line access, remove pulp stones, visualize all canal orifices
2. **Canal Enlargement**: Remove coronal pulp debris, enlarge 2–3 sizes; for calcified canals enlarge sufficiently; for mild calcification ultrasonic alone may suffice
3. **Irrigation**: NaOCl 5.25% + ultrasonic activation; 10-second bursts × 3 cycles before orifice exposure; 10-second bursts × 5–9 cycles after orifice exposure; EDTA replaced by NaOCl + ultrasonic (dissolves pulp tissue surface, minimizes wound, removes smear layer)
4. **Pressure Hemostasis**: Wet cotton pellet placed, left to expand; pressure hemostasis until bleeding stops; if bleeding persists from single canal → enlarge further and return
5. **Filling**: Endoseem placed via furcation canal first; apply wet cotton pellet × 3 minutes to set; ≥3 mm Endoseem barrier required between core and vital apical pulp; crown restoration strongly recommended for long-term success

**Key material**: Endoseem (엔도셈) — bioceramic barrier material (expands with moisture; barrier role between infected coronal zone and vital apical pulp)

**Working length norms**:
- Maxillary molars: crown access 2 mm, orifice depth 6 mm; WL ~10 mm (or 8 mm if significant occlusal wear)
- Mandibular molars: crown access 1.5 mm, orifice depth 6 mm; WL ~10 mm (or 8 mm if significant occlusal wear)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
(Clinical lecture — no original research data. References clinical experience and published guidelines.)

- Vital Pulpotomy: high success rate internationally recognized (USA, Europe, China)
- Vital Pulpectomy: particularly useful for cracked teeth; crowning recommended for long-term sealing
- Partial pulpotomy (부분치수절단술): higher rate of post-op sensitivity and discomfort vs. vital pulpotomy
- VPT followed by delayed crowning: rarely causes problems
- Furcation lesions: lining the chamber floor with Endoseem or Endoseal often resolves lesions (2-year follow-up cases shown)
- After VPT, adjacent teeth to implants (over-erupted, 2–3 months post-prosthesis sensitivity): VPT resolves symptoms effectively

## 7. Glossary
- **Vital Pulpectomy (바이탈 근관치료)**: Removal of coronal and upper-root pulp tissue while preserving healthy apical pulp; Endoseem acts as biological barrier
- **Vital Pulpotomy (바이탈 치수절단술)**: Removal of coronal pulp only; canal pulp fully preserved
- **Endoseem (엔도셈)**: Bioceramic material used as the coronal barrier in Vital Pulpectomy; must be ≥3 mm thick
- **Tertiary dentin (삼차상아질)**: Reparative/reactionary dentin formed by vital pulp in response to Endoseem barrier
- **Working length (작업장 길이)**: Distance from reference point to termination point (2–3 mm short of apex in Vital Pulpectomy)
- **Pressure hemostasis (압박지혈)**: Wet cotton pellet compression to achieve hemostasis before Endoseem placement
