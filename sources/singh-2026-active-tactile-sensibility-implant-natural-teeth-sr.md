## Why Ingested

기존 occlusion 카테고리는 임플란트 보철의 교합 접촉을 어떻게 설계하는지(가벼운 접촉, 시간경과 변화)를 다루지만, *왜* 임플란트가 더 가벼운 접촉을 요구하는지의 신경생리학적 근거 — 치주인대 기계수용기 소실과 골유착감각(osseoperception) — 은 페이지로 정리되지 않았다. 이 SR은 단일 임플란트 vs 자연치의 능동 촉각 감수성(ATS) 역치를 정량 비교(임플란트 10–100µm vs 자연치 <10–50µm)하여, [[occlusion/zhang-2022-two-occlusal-patterns-posterior-implant-crowns-prospective]]의 가벼운 임플란트 접촉 프로토콜과 [[occlusion/mao-2024-occlusal-changes-implant-supported-single-crowns]]의 시간경과 교합 변화 관찰에 기계수용적 근거를 제공한다.

## One-line Summary

PRISMA SR of 6 clinical studies (4 RCTs, 1 prospective, 1 cross-sectional): single-implant active tactile sensibility thresholds (10–100 µm) are consistently higher (coarser) than natural-tooth thresholds (<10–50 µm), but osseoperception improves over time with functional loading; immediate loading and occlusion against a natural antagonist accelerate sensory recovery — the physiological rationale for lighter implant occlusal contacts.

## 한줄요약

임상연구 6편(RCT 4·전향 1·단면 1)을 종합한 PRISMA 체계적 고찰: 단일 임플란트의 능동 촉각 감수성(ATS) 역치(10–100µm)는 자연치(<10–50µm)보다 일관되게 높지만(둔감), 기능적 부하와 함께 골유착감각이 시간경과로 개선된다. 즉시부하·자연치 대합이 감각 회복을 가속한다 — 임플란트 교합을 더 가볍게 설계해야 하는 생리학적 근거.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies the **active tactile sensibility (ATS) gap** between single implants (10–100 µm thresholds) and natural teeth (<10–50 µm), confirming implants are measurably coarser at occlusal force discrimination.
- Frames **osseoperception** as the mechanism partially restoring tactile feedback after loss of periodontal-ligament (PDL) mechanoreception, and shows it **improves longitudinally** with functional loading (neuroplasticity).
- Identifies **loading protocol** (immediate > delayed) and **antagonist type** (natural tooth > implant) as modifiable factors accelerating sensory rehabilitation.
- Provides the physiological rationale underpinning the clinical convention of **lighter occlusal contacts on implant restorations**.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PRISMA 2020-compliant systematic review; PROSPERO CRD420251104655.
- Databases: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science + manual search; literature up to **June 2025**.
- Inclusion: human studies reporting **quantifiable ATS thresholds** for a single implant opposing a natural tooth, compared contralaterally with natural tooth-to-tooth pairs, measured by **psychophysical testing** (e.g., foil/thickness detection).
- Data extracted: study design, implant variables, testing protocols, outcomes.
- **6 studies included**: 4 randomized controlled trials, 1 prospective cohort, 1 cross-sectional.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Threshold ranges**: implants 10–100 µm vs natural teeth <10–50 µm (implants consistently higher/coarser).
- **Loading**: immediate loading → earlier and greater tactile feedback than delayed loading.
- **Implant location** (anterior vs posterior): inconsistent effects across studies.
- **Antagonist**: implant-opposing-natural-tooth > implant-opposing-implant for tactile sensibility.
- **Demographics**: no consistent effect of age or sex.
- **Longitudinal**: progressive ATS improvement with continued functional loading.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 6 heterogeneous studies; no meta-analysis (threshold ranges only, not pooled estimates).
- Psychophysical testing methods (foil detection etc.) vary, limiting cross-study comparability.
- Abstract did not report risk-of-bias grading or GRADE certainty (full text not available here).
- Inconsistent location effects and lack of demographic signal may reflect underpowering.

## 7. Glossary

- **Active tactile sensibility (ATS)**: ability to detect a thin object (e.g., foil) actively bitten between opposing teeth; lower threshold = finer discrimination.
- **Osseoperception**: perception/sensory feedback mediated by osseointegrated implants in the absence of a PDL, thought to arise from periosteal, mucosal, muscular, and central mechanoreceptors.
- **Periodontal ligament (PDL) mechanoreception**: PDL-embedded Ruffini-like receptors that give natural teeth their fine occlusal force/contact discrimination.
- **Immediate loading**: functional loading of an implant at or shortly after placement, versus delayed (conventional healing period) loading.
- **Neuroplasticity**: central reorganization that, with functional loading, progressively improves implant-mediated tactile feedback.
