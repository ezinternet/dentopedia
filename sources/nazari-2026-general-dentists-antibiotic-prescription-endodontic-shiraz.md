## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional questionnaire (n=103 general dentists/residents, Shiraz, Iran): moderate-to-good AAE knowledge (70.9% knew first-line) but low-to-moderate performance — 69.9% inappropriately prescribed antibiotics for immunocompromised endodontic patients, 51.5% for localized acute apical abscess; residents outperformed GPs (p=0.019); 88.3% wanted more training.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First assessment of AAE-guideline adherence for endodontic antibiotic prescribing among general dentists in Shiraz, Iran (2024).
- Demonstrates a dissociation: theoretical knowledge moderate-to-good but real-case clinical performance only low-to-moderate, with NO significant correlation between the two.
- Quantifies indication-specific over-prescription (immunocompromised patients, localized abscess) and the non-guideline first-line combination habit (amoxicillin + metronidazole).
- Shows residents outperform general practitioners on performance (academic supervision effect), while gender, experience, and workplace show no association.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Validated questionnaire (CVI=0.89, Cronbach's α=0.82), built on AAE 2017 guidelines + literature, vetted by 3 board-certified endodontists; 20-dentist pilot.
- Simple random sampling, 140 invited, 103 completed (response rate 73.6%); inclusion = general dentists/residents with ≥2-5 yr endodontic experience in Shiraz; specialists/non-practicing excluded.
- Sections: demographics, self-assessment, knowledge of prescribing principles, and clinical case-based performance scenarios. Score 0-14 (1 point/correct); <half=weak, half=moderate, >half=good, all=excellent.
- Analysis: SPSS v22 then re-run v27; descriptive stats, Spearman, Kruskal-Wallis, chi-square, binary logistic regression; significance <0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Sample: 67.9% general dentists, 32.1% residents; 66% women; 73.8% had 2-5 yr experience; prescribing frequency: 44.7% <2×/week, 31% 2-4×/week, 24.3% never.
- Guideline awareness: 84.5% had NO awareness of current guidelines; only 15.5% familiar. Info sources: 51.4% guidelines/textbooks, 36% no specific reference.
- First-line (non-allergic): amoxicillin+metronidazole combination chosen by 70.9% (deviation); amoxicillin 35.9%, penicillin V 10.7% (the actual recommended first-line) chosen less often. Penicillin-allergic: clindamycin majority (50.5%); only 5.8% knew azithromycin, none moxifloxacin.
- Duration: 50.5% chose 7-10 days, 43.7% 3-7 days; few chose incorrect 10-20 days; none "until pain subsides."
- Indications (Yes% prescribe): irreversible pulpitis 1%, pulpal necrosis 9.7%, symptomatic apical periodontitis 7.8%, localized acute apical abscess 51.5% (over-prescription), diffuse facial swelling 70.9% (valid), abscess with fever/trismus 77.7% (valid), immunocompromised localized abscess 70.9% (valid indication per table), after all RCT 0%, pain 2-3 d post-RCT only 17.5% (AAE-recommended, under-recognized).
- Prophylaxis (adults, non-allergic): amoxicillin 2 g 1 h preop 90.3% (guideline-concordant); penicillin-allergic: clindamycin 66%.
- Performance cases: diffuse facial swelling — 41.7% prescribed antibiotics before RCT despite immediate treatment being feasible (non-compliant); irreversible pulpitis case — 84.5% correctly chose RCT without antibiotics; immunocompromised patient case — only 30.1% guideline-compliant, 69.9% prescribed antibiotics.
- Residents' performance significantly higher than GPs (p=0.019); no association with gender, experience, or workplace. 49.5% felt knowledge sufficient; 88.3% wanted further training.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Self-reported questionnaire → response/social-desirability bias likely inflates reported adherence.
- Single-city (Shiraz) sample, modest n (103); not necessarily generalizable.
- Authors call for mixed-method validation of actual prescribing and for case-based workshops + curriculum integration of antimicrobial stewardship.

## 6. Related Work

- Aligns with Sbricoli 2024 (Italy) and Nabavizadeh / Zahabiyoun (Iran) showing dental antibiotic knowledge needs enhancement.
- Reinforces AAE-stewardship evidence that antibiotics are unnecessary for uncomplicated dentoalveolar infection without systemic signs (Lockhart 2019).
- Echoes Cuevas-Gonzalez SR: beta-lactams/amoxicillin dominate prophylactic prescribing.

## 7. Glossary

- AAE: American Association of Endodontists.
- CVI: Content Validity Index. Cronbach's α: internal-consistency reliability coefficient.
- Antimicrobial stewardship: coordinated effort to optimize antibiotic use and curb resistance.
- Acute apical abscess: localized pus collection at the root apex; antibiotics indicated only with systemic involvement (fever, trismus, immunocompromise).
