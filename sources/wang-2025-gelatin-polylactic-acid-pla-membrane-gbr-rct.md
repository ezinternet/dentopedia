## One-line Summary
RCT (n=32 single-tooth gaps with alveolar bone defects) comparing a gelatin/polylactic-acid (GT/PLA) barrier membrane vs commercial Guidor® PLA membrane for GBR; 100% implant survival both arms, no significant difference in bone resorption, osteogenic efficacy, soft-tissue index, wound healing, exposure, or infection at 6 months — GT/PLA non-inferior.

## 한줄요약
젤라틴/폴리락트산(GT/PLA) 차폐막과 상용 Guidor® PLA막을 GBR에 비교한 RCT(n=32). 6개월에 임플란트 생존 100%, 골흡수·골생성효율·연조직지수·창상치유·막노출·감염 모두 군간 유의차 없음 → GT/PLA 비열등.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Medical Science Monitor (Med Sci Monit) 2025
- DOI: 10.12659/MSM.944713 (published 2025-01-05)
- Institution: Nanchang University / Jiangxi Provincial People's Hospital, China
- Design: Randomized clinical trial (envelope randomization, n=16 per arm)

## 2. Key Contributions
- First reported clinical comparison of a gelatin/PLA composite membrane against an established commercial bioresorbable PLA matrix (Guidor®) in human GBR.
- Demonstrates non-inferiority of a low-cost synthetic composite barrier across imaging and soft-tissue endpoints.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 32 patients, single missing tooth + alveolar bone defect needing GBR.
- Randomized (envelope) to GT/PLA (experimental, n=16) or Guidor® (control, n=16); both covered the bone substitute.
- CBCT immediately and at 6 months → bone resorption volume + osteogenic efficacy.
- Soft tissue index (STI), wound healing, membrane exposure, infection incidence recorded.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Implant survival 100% both groups.
- Mean bone resorption: 148.54 ± 107.42 mm³ (GT/PLA) vs 185.25 ± 85.31 mm³ (Guidor®), P=0.163 (NS).
- Osteogenic efficacy: 75% (GT/PLA) vs 56% (Guidor®), P=0.458 (NS).
- STI, wound healing, exposure, infection: no significant between-group difference (P>0.05).
- Conclusion: GT/PLA non-inferior with good efficacy and biocompatibility.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small n (16/arm), underpowered for the numerically favorable but NS osteogenic-efficacy difference.
- Single 6-month timepoint; no histology; CBCT-based surrogate outcomes.

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary
- GT/PLA: gelatin / polylactic acid composite barrier membrane.
- Guidor®: commercial bioresorbable PLA matrix barrier membrane (control).
- STI: soft tissue index.
- Osteogenic efficacy: proportion of defect achieving adequate new bone (study-defined).
