## One-line Summary

Narrative overview (2015) of titanium implant surface modification methods — additive (plasma spray, HA coating, calcium phosphate), subtractive (sandblasting, acid etching, anodization), and material-modification (laser, ion implantation) — cataloguing how macro/micro/nanotopography each influence osseointegration.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Systematic catalogue of titanium implant surface treatments organized into three major classes: additive, subtractive, and material-modification methods.
2. Multilevel roughness framework: macro (>10 µm), micro (1–10 µm), and nano (<1 µm) topography each drive different biological responses (mechanical interlocking vs. secondary osseointegration vs. protein adsorption/cell signaling).
3. Optimal roughness range of Sa 1–2 µm identified as balancing enhanced BIC against peri-implantitis risk at excessively rough surfaces.
4. Covers clinical indications for rough vs. smooth surfaces: rough surfaces for poor bone quality/short implants; smooth transmucosal collar for soft-tissue health.
5. Reviews emerging nanosurface technologies (anodization, discrete crystalline deposition, laser ablation at nano scale) and bioactive coatings (BMP-2, fibronectin, RGD peptides).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Narrative literature review drawing on published bench, animal, and clinical studies. No systematic search protocol or PRISMA methodology stated. Key cited authors include Wennerberg, Klokkevold, Cooper, Brånemark, Linder, Albrektsson. Coverage spans:

- **Macro features**: screw threads (V-thread, buttress, reverse buttress, spiral), press-fit cylinders, sintered beads.
- **Additive methods**: titanium plasma spray (TPS), hydroxyapatite (HA) coating, calcium phosphate coating, magnetron sputter coating.
- **Subtractive methods**: sandblasting/grit blasting (Al₂O₃, TiO₂, ZrO₂), acid etching (H₂SO₄/HCl, HF), combined SLA (sandblasted large-grit acid-etched).
- **Anodization (micro-arc oxidation)**: creates TiO₂ micropores with incorporated Ca/P; increases oxide layer thickness.
- **Other methods**: electron beam, laser treatment, ion implantation, dimpling, cold working.
- **Nanosurface**: discrete crystalline deposition (DCD), alkali treatment, hydrogen peroxide treatment, sol-gel, biochemical surface modifications (fibronectin, RGD peptides, growth factors).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Surface class | Key finding cited |
|---|---|
| Rough vs. smooth | Higher removal torque and BIC for rough (Wennerberg 1997, Wilke 1990, Johansson 1990) |
| Macro Sa > 10 µm | Risk of peri-implantitis ↑; optimal moderate roughness 1–2 µm |
| TPS | Ra ~7 µm; increases surface area; HA tends to delaminate |
| SLA | Most widely studied combined approach; Sa ~1.5–2 µm; high BIC |
| Anodization | Micropores 0.5–10 µm; Ca/P incorporation; Friatec/Nobel TiUnite |
| Nanosurface | Altered protein adsorption, fibronectin conformation → improved cell adhesion |
| HA coating | Promotes early bone apposition but long-term delamination concern |

- Transmucosal (collar) zone: smooth surface preferred to reduce plaque retention and support soft-tissue health.
- Short implants in deficient bone: rough surfaces show superior outcomes vs. smooth.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative review without systematic methodology; selection bias possible.
- Does not provide pooled quantitative estimates (no meta-analysis).
- Many cited studies are animal/in-vitro; direct clinical translation needs verification.
- Authors note future directions: nanosurface engineering, bioactive coatings delivering growth factors (BMP-2), and surface modifications that simultaneously manage both osseointegration and peri-implant soft tissue.

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Sa | Arithmetic mean height of the surface (3D roughness parameter) |
| Ra | 2D arithmetic mean roughness |
| BIC | Bone-to-Implant Contact ratio |
| TPS | Titanium Plasma Spray — thermal spray coating adding rough Ti layer |
| SLA | Sandblasted, Large-grit, Acid-etched — combined subtractive protocol |
| HA | Hydroxyapatite — calcium phosphate ceramic coating promoting bone apposition |
| Anodization | Electrochemical oxidation creating controlled TiO₂ microporous layer |
| DCD | Discrete Crystalline Deposition — nanotopography technique |
| RGD | Arginine-Glycine-Aspartate cell-adhesion peptide sequence |
| BMP-2 | Bone Morphogenetic Protein-2 — growth factor used in bioactive coatings |
| Macrotopography | Surface features > 10 µm (threads, pores) |
| Microtopography | Surface features 1–10 µm (grit blast craters, acid etch pits) |
| Nanotopography | Surface features < 1 µm (crystalline deposits, anodized nanopores) |
