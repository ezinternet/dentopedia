## One-line Summary
ICU 후향적 연구(n=1,157, 포르투갈): NSAIDs 사용이 급성신손상(AKI) 독립적 위험인자(OR 1.98); 중증 AKI(stage 3)는 2년 생존율 유의하게 악화(aHR 1.42) — 치과 임상과의 연관성: 수술 후 NSAID 처방 시 신기능 손상 환자 주의 (J Crit Care 2025).

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Journal of Critical Care 2025;90:155202
- DOI: 10.1016/j.jcrc.2025.155202
- Design: Retrospective cohort
- Setting: Hospital Vila Franca de Xira ICU (Portugal), January 2015–December 2023
- n = 1,157 patients (ICU stay >24h; excluded: pre-existing kidney dysfunction on admission)

## 2. Key Contributions
- NSAID use independently associated with AKI: OR 1.98
- NSAID use higher in surgical vs medical patients (62% vs 23%)
- Severe AKI (stage 3): 34% vs 26% in NSAID vs non-NSAID group (p=0.008)
- Severe AKI → significantly worse 2-year survival (aHR 1.42, 95% CI 1.09–1.86, p=0.011)
- Non-severe AKI: no significant 2-year survival impact
- Renal function recovery did not significantly improve long-term survival once severe AKI occurred

## 3. Methodology
Retrospective cohort, 9-year period. All ICU admissions >24h included. AKI tracked via serum creatinine and urine output. Exclusion: pre-existing chronic or acute kidney dysfunction. Short- and long-term outcomes through 2 years of follow-up. Logistic regression for AKI incidence; Cox regression for survival.

## 4. Key Results
| Outcome | NSAID group | Non-NSAID group | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| AKI rate | 67% | 51% | 0.009 |
| Severe AKI (stage 3) | 34% | 26% | 0.008 |
| 2-year survival (severe AKI) | aHR 1.42 (worse) | — | 0.011 |
| 2-year survival (renal recovery) | 80% | 78% | 0.636 (NS) |

Surgical patients: 62% non-scheduled + 61% scheduled received NSAIDs. Medical patients: 23%.

## 5. Limitations
- ICU population — very different from routine dental patients; limited direct generalizability
- Retrospective; confounding by indication (sicker patients may receive different analgesia)
- Specific NSAID types and doses not stratified
- Not designed to address dentistry-specific NSAID use

## 6. Dental Relevance
Routine dental patients are far from ICU severity, but this study quantifies the dose-related nephrotoxic risk of NSAIDs. High-risk dental patients (CKD, elderly, ACE inhibitor/diuretic users) warrant caution with NSAID prescribing. Preoperative renal function consideration recommended before extended NSAID courses post-implant or periodontal surgery.
