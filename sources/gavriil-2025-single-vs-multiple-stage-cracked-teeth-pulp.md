## One-line Summary

Abstract-only SR+MA (14 cohort studies, symptomatic vital cracked teeth): single- vs multiple-stage treatment did not differ for pulp survival/success, but single-stage with indirect restorations gave the best pulp success, onlays outperformed crowns, direct restorations were inferior for pulp success, and short interim duration (1–2 wk) beat long; 1-yr tooth survival 96.1–100%; very low certainty.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Compares restorative **staging** (single- vs multiple-stage) for pulp outcomes in symptomatic vital cracked teeth.
- Ranks restoration types by pulp success: indirect onlay > crown > direct.
- Identifies short interim duration (1–2 wk) as favorable in multiple-stage treatment.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

5-source + gray-literature search to Dec 3 2024; permanent symptomatic vital cracked teeth, ≥1-yr follow-up after complete SST or MST; Newcastle-Ottawa + Cochrane RoB2; single-arm random-effects MA; GRADE. (Abstract-level only.)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- 14 cohort studies.
- No significant SST vs MST difference for 1-/2-yr pulp survival and 1-yr pulp success (P>.05).
- SST + indirect > MST + indirect (P<.05) and > SST + direct (P<.001) for pulp success.
- Indirect onlays > crowns (P<.05); direct ≈ onlays for pulp survival but inferior for pulp success (P<.001).
- Short interim (1–2 wk) > long (2–3 mo) pulp success (P<.05).
- 1-yr tooth survival 96.1–100%. Certainty very low.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- All included evidence observational (cohort); very low certainty; substantial heterogeneity.
- Abstract-only: pooled effect estimates and CIs for each comparison not fully extractable.

## 7. Glossary

- **SST / MST**: single-stage vs multiple-stage (phased) restorative treatment.
- **Indirect onlay**: lab/CAD-CAM partial-coverage restoration; here best pulp success.
- **Pulp success vs survival**: asymptomatic vital pulp (success) vs any retained vitality (survival).
