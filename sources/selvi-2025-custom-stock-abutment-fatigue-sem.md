## Why Ingested

어버트먼트 나사 풀림(screw loosening)은 임플란트 보철에서 가장 흔한 기계적 합병증이며, CAD-CAM 맞춤 어버트먼트(custom abutment)와 기성 어버트먼트(stock abutment) 중 어느 쪽이 장기 하중에 더 안정적인지 근거가 부족했다. 본 연구는 200만 사이클 피로 시험 후 제거 토크값(Removal Torque Value, RTV)과 SEM 표면 분석을 통해 두 어버트먼트 유형의 기계적 동등성을 확인, [[prosthetic-materials/overview-implant-abutment-connection-stability]] 종합 페이지의 근거를 강화한다.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro study (n=44 conical-hex assemblies) showing CAD-CAM custom abutments had lower baseline removal torque than stock abutments but maintained equivalent preload stability after 2 million fatigue cycles, with greatest preload loss occurring before 1 million cycles.

## 한줄요약

체외 실험(n=44, 내부 원추형-헥스 연결)에서 CAD-CAM 맞춤 어버트먼트(custom abutment)는 기성 어버트먼트(stock abutment)보다 기저 제거 토크값(Removal Torque Value, RTV)이 낮았으나, 200만 사이클 피로 하중 후 예압(preload) 유지 능력은 동등하였으며 가장 큰 예압 소실은 50,000~100만 사이클 구간에서 발생하였다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Demonstrated that CAD-CAM custom abutments and stock abutments achieve equivalent preload maintenance after 2 million fatigue cycles, despite different baseline RTVs.
2. Identified the critical window for preload loss (50,000–1,000,000 cycles), recommending early abutment screw retightening.
3. SEM analysis revealed more extensive surface wear in stock abutments vs. localized adaptations in custom abutments, with thread integrity maintained in both groups.
4. Post-fatigue re-tightening restored RTVs in both groups, confirming reversibility of preload loss.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **n**: 44 internal conical-hex implant-abutment assemblies; divided into Stock Abutment (SA) and Custom Abutment (CA) groups (n=22 each)
- **Connection type**: Internal conical-hex connection
- **Custom abutments**: CAD-CAM milled
- **Fatigue protocol**: ISO-14801 standard cyclic loading
- **Cycle checkpoints**: Baseline → 50,000 → 500,000 (1×10⁶) → 1,500,000 (1.5×10⁶) → 2,000,000 (2×10⁶) cycles
- **Primary outcome**: Removal Torque Value (RTV) at each checkpoint and after post-fatigue re-tightening
- **Secondary outcome**: SEM surface morphology at baseline, 1×10⁶, and 2×10⁶ cycles

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Checkpoint | Finding |
|---|---|
| Baseline RTV | SA > CA (statistically significant) |
| Up to 1×10⁶ cycles | Progressive RTV decrease; CA significantly lower than SA |
| 1.5×10⁶ – 2×10⁶ cycles | Partial RTV recovery in both groups; no significant difference between SA and CA |
| Post-fatigue re-tightening | RTV restored in both groups |
| SEM – SA group | More extensive surface wear patterns |
| SEM – CA group | Localized surface adaptations; thread integrity maintained |

**Key clinical finding**: The greatest preload loss occurs between 50,000 and 1 million cycles — the early post-insertion period.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro study — does not replicate the full oral environment (saliva, thermal cycling, multi-directional forces)
- Single implant-abutment connection type (conical-hex); results may not generalize to other connection geometries
- Limited to titanium abutments; zirconia or hybrid abutments not included
- No assessment of marginal fit changes after fatigue
- Future research: in vivo clinical studies, other connection types, and zirconia abutment comparison

## 6. Related Work

- Klongbunjit et al. 2021 (IJID): Comparable RTV study up to 1 million cycles; customized Ti vs straight Ti vs hybrid zirconia
- Yi et al. 2019 (JAP): CAD/CAM vs prefabricated in Morse taper; axial displacement and RTV after cyclic loading
