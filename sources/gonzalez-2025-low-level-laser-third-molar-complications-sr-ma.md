## One-line Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis confirming that low-level diode laser therapy (LLLT) significantly reduces pain, swelling, and trismus following surgical third molar extraction compared to controls.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of the American Dental Association (JADA) 2025;156(3):211-224.E11
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.adaj.2024.09.005
- **Type**: Systematic Review and Meta-analysis
- **Year**: 2025

## 2. Key Contributions
- Provides pooled quantitative evidence on LLLT efficacy for three major third molar post-extraction complications: pain, swelling (edema), and trismus
- Evaluates diode laser specifically (most clinically used LLLT device type)
- Assesses optimal parameters: wavelength (typically 630–1000 nm), energy density, application timing
- Informs evidence-based decisions for adjunct laser therapy in oral surgery practice

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Systematic review following PRISMA guidelines
- Database search: PubMed, Embase, Cochrane, Scopus, Web of Science
- Included: RCTs comparing LLLT vs. placebo/sham laser or no treatment following mandibular third molar surgical extraction
- Outcomes: pain (VAS), facial swelling (facial measurements), mouth opening (trismus, mm)
- Meta-analysis using random-effects model; subgroup analyses by laser parameters, application timing, and anatomical site of application
- Risk of bias: Cochrane RoB 2.0 tool

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Pain**: LLLT significantly reduced postoperative pain at 24h, 48h, and 72h; statistically significant reduction in VAS scores (pooled SMD favoring LLLT)
- **Swelling**: Facial edema measurements significantly lower in LLLT groups at 48–72h and 7-day follow-up
- **Trismus**: Mouth opening was significantly better preserved/recovered in LLLT groups
- Effect size moderate to large for all three outcomes
- Diode wavelengths 800–940 nm appeared most effective based on subgroup analysis
- No adverse events reported with LLLT
- Benefits observed regardless of whether LLLT applied intraorally, extraorally, or both

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- High heterogeneity in laser parameters across studies (wavelength, power, energy density, sessions)
- Lack of standardized LLLT protocols makes direct comparison difficult
- Some included studies had small sample sizes
- Need for standardized LLLT protocols and multi-center RCTs

## 6. Related Work
- Complements PRF literature for third molar complication management
- Related to photobiomodulation therapy (PBMT) evidence in wound healing
- Informs the broader field of adjunctive physical therapies in oral surgery

## 7. Glossary
- **LLLT (Low-Level Laser Therapy)**: Application of low-power laser light to stimulate biological processes (photobiomodulation); does not generate heat; wavelengths 630–1000 nm typical
- **Diode Laser**: Semiconductor-based laser device; most common LLLT device type in dentistry; portable and cost-effective
- **Trismus**: Restricted mouth opening (<35 mm interincisal distance); common after third molar surgery due to muscle edema/spasm
- **Photobiomodulation (PBM)**: Mechanism by which LLLT affects cellular metabolism via cytochrome c oxidase activation, increasing ATP production and reducing inflammation
- **VAS**: Visual Analog Scale; 0–100 mm scale for pain measurement
- **SMD**: Standardized Mean Difference; effect size metric used in meta-analysis
