## One-line Summary

In vitro + in vivo (rat femur) study: a handheld piezoelectric-direct-discharge non-thermal plasma applied to alkali-treated titanate nanonetwork (TNS) titanium eliminated biofilm contamination (Staphylococcus aureus) without destroying the surface nanostructure/roughness, and improved rat BMSC osteogenic activity and in vivo bone formation — a combined decontamination + osseointegration strategy for peri-implantitis.

## 한줄요약

in vitro+in vivo(쥐 대퇴골) 연구: 휴대형 압전직접방전(PDD) 비열 플라즈마를 알칼리처리 티타네이트 나노네트워크(TNS) 티타늄에 적용 — 표면 나노구조·거칠기는 그대로 둔 채 바이오필름(Staphylococcus aureus)을 제거하고, 쥐 골수중간엽줄기세포(BMSC) 골형성 활성과 in vivo 골형성을 개선. 제염+골유착 동시 전략(임플란트주위염 적용).

## 1. Document Information

- Journal: International Journal of Molecular Sciences 2020, 21(10):3533
- DOI: 10.3390/ijms21103533
- Study type: in vitro (cell + antibacterial) + in vivo rat femur

## 2. Key Contributions

- Combines surface nanostructuring (TNS) with handheld plasma; achieves decontamination AND bone response on one surface.
- Plasma cleans biofilm without destroying the nanostructure — key for re-using/rescuing a nanotextured surface.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- TNS specimens treated with piezoelectric direct discharge (PDD) plasma.
- Decontamination tested vs Staphylococcus aureus.
- Rat BMSCs: attachment, ALP, ECM mineralization, osteogenic gene expression; in vivo rat femur for bone response.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Nanotopography and surface roughness not significantly changed by plasma.
- Cell and bone-formation activity improved by TNS + plasma.
- Plasma effectively eliminated biofilm contamination.
- Conclusion: promising for treating nanomaterials immediately before implantation and as a peri-implantitis strategy.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Combined intervention (nanostructure + plasma) makes plasma-specific contribution harder to isolate.
- Animal (rat) + in vitro; no human/clinical data.
