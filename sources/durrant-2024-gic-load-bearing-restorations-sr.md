## Why Ingested
RCT-level evidence on the long-contested load-bearing use of GIC; strengthens [[glass-ionomer/durrant-2024-gic-load-bearing-restorations-sr]] and the GIC overview's decision points.

## One-line Summary
SR of 12 RCTs concluding GIC is comparable to conventional materials for many parameters in Class I/II restorations.

## 한줄요약
RCT 12편 SR — 하중부 수복에서 GIC가 여러 임상 지표상 기존 재료와 비교 가능.

## 1. Document Information
Med Sci Monit 2024; 30: e943489. DOI 10.12659/MSM.943489. Received 2023-12-13; published 2024-02-14. CC BY-NC-ND. Corresponding: Mutahar (Portsmouth), Al Moaleem (Jazan).

## 2. Key Contributions
- RCT-only synthesis scoped to load-bearing (Class I/II) restorations.
- Concludes overall performance comparable to traditional materials.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
PRISMA-style SR. Databases: EBSCO, PubMed, Embase, Cochrane. Inclusion: RCTs only; 12 trials. USPHS-type clinical outcome parameters.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
GIC suitable/comparable: surface margin, esthetic anatomical form, retention, fracture, marginal adaptation, occlusal contour, wear, approximal form. No significant difference: postoperative sensitivity, recurrent caries, tooth integrity.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
Heterogeneous outcome reporting; GIC's known lower fracture toughness/wear resistance; need standardized long-term RCTs.

## 6. Related Work
Black's classification (Class I/II); Minamata/EU amalgam restrictions; resin composite as alternative.

## 7. Glossary
- Load-bearing restoration: Class I (occlusal) or Class II (proximal ≥2 surfaces).
- USPHS criteria: clinical evaluation parameters for restorations.
