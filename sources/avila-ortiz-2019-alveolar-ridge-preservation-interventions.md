## Why Ingested

The EFP/AO consensus SR+MA that supplies the 9-modality ARP taxonomy underpinning [[overviews/socket-preservation-arp-overview]], and the "ARP is effective" position that [[overviews/bone-regeneration-socket-biology-and-arp-critique]] weighs against the Cochrane downgrade. Pairs with [[atieh-2021-interventions-replacing-missing-teeth]] as the two anchor syntheses on ARP efficacy.

## One-line Summary
SR+MA of 22 RCTs (25 articles) categorizing ARP into 9 distinct treatment modalities — all reduce post-extraction dimensional loss vs spontaneous healing, but no head-to-head superiority of any single modality on patient-reported outcomes; published as the EFP/AO Workshop reference paper.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Periodontology 2019;46(Suppl 21):195–223 (EFP/AO Consensus Workshop)
- **Type**: Systematic review with random-effects meta-analyses
- **Corrigendum**: J Clin Periodontol 47(1):129 (Dec 2019)
- **Funding**: EFP/AO joint workshop support

## 2. Key Contributions
- Defines and tabulates **9 ARP treatment modalities** (BBP+SS, BBG/PC+SS, CPBP+SS, AG+SS, AP±SS, ABDP, cell therapy, etc.), providing a usable taxonomy.
- Restricted to RCTs and at least two studies per comparison → addresses earlier criticism of mixing study designs.
- Includes patient-reported outcome measures (PROMs) — though scarce reporting in primary studies limits MA.
- Anchors the **2019 EFP/AO consensus** position that ARP is effective.

## 3. Methodology
- Comprehensive database + grey literature + hand search.
- 3,003 records → 1,789 after de-duplication → 25 articles (22 RCTs) included.
- Two independent reviewers screened and extracted.
- Random-effects MA performed where ≥2 studies in the same modality reported the same outcome at 3–6 mo.
- 9 ARP modalities compared to spontaneous socket healing (control).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Most modalities significantly reduce horizontal and vertical dimensional loss vs control.
- Network of comparisons does **not** establish a single superior modality; xenograft/allograft + socket sealing are most evidence-rich.
- PROMs (pain, swelling, patient satisfaction) reported in too few trials for meaningful pooled analysis.
- Authors' conclusion: "ARP is an effective therapy to attenuate the dimensional reduction of the alveolar ridge that normally takes place after tooth extraction."

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Substantial between-study heterogeneity (different socket morphology, defect classifications, follow-up periods).
- Few trials specify defect type (intact vs dehisced buccal plate) — clinically critical variable.
- Short follow-up (3–6 mo) for most outcomes; long-term implant survival data scarce.
- Need for standardized core outcome set in future ARP RCTs.

## 7. Glossary
- **ARP**: Alveolar Ridge Preservation
- **SS**: Socket Sealing (membrane / soft tissue coverage)
- **BBP / BBG**: Bovine bone particles / granules (xenograft)
- **AG / AP**: Allograft / Alloplast
- **ABDP**: Autologous blood-derived products (PRF/PRP)
- **CTh**: Cell therapy
- **PROM**: Patient-reported outcome measure
