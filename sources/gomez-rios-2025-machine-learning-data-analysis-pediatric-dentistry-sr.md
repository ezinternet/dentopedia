## One-line Summary

Systematic review (PRISMA, QUADAS-2; 20 of 1945 screened articles) finding machine learning in paediatric dentistry is dominated by caries prediction (14/20 studies, using socio-demographic/behavioural/biological predictors) and that ML-derived cost analyses favour sealants + fluoride, while concluding that ML-specific methodological guidelines and quality scales are still lacking.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Maps the current landscape of machine learning (ML) applications for data analysis in paediatric dentistry across four databases (PubMed, Web of Science, Scopus, Lilacs).
- Quantifies the dominance of caries prediction as the principal ML use-case (14 of 20 included studies).
- Identifies the predictor classes feeding ML caries-prediction models: socio-demographic, behavioural, and biological.
- Surfaces ML-derived health-economic findings: children with early caries lesions incur higher insurer costs, and sealant + fluoride recipients show greater cost savings.
- Flags a methodological gap — the absence of ML-specific reporting guidelines and quality-assessment scales for this study type.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Design: systematic review adhering to the PRISMA statement.
- Search: PubMed, Web of Science, Scopus, and Lilacs databases.
- Screening yield: 20 articles meeting eligibility criteria out of 1945 initially screened.
- Quality appraisal: QUADAS-2 scale applied to included articles.
- Scope of included topics: caries prediction; impact of oral health on adolescents' quality of life (OHRQoL); predictors of the need for a second treatment under deep sedation; effectiveness of preventive dental services.
- (Abstract-only: no per-model algorithm types, sample sizes, or pooled performance metrics were reported in the retrieved text.)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- 1945 records screened → 20 articles included.
- 14 of 20 articles focused on caries prediction.
- Caries-prediction predictors clustered into three classes: socio-demographic, behavioural, biological.
- ML cost analysis: children with early caries lesions incur higher costs for insurers.
- ML cost analysis: children receiving sealants and fluoride demonstrated greater cost savings.
- Background context cited: dental caries affects 514 million children worldwide.
- Conclusion: ML algorithms can identify patterns in large datasets, enhancing approaches to paediatric oral diseases; integration into research and educational programs is recommended.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Authors note ML-specific methodological guidelines and quality scales are necessary for improved scientific evidence in this domain.
- Small evidence base (n = 20 studies) and heavy concentration on a single task (caries prediction) limit generalisability across paediatric subspecialties.
- This wiki record is abstract-only; no quantitative model performance (AUC, sensitivity/specificity) was available for extraction.

## 7. Glossary

- Machine learning (ML): subfield of AI in which algorithms learn patterns from data to make predictions without explicit rule-based programming.
- PRISMA: Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses — reporting standard for systematic reviews.
- QUADAS-2: Quality Assessment of Diagnostic Accuracy Studies, version 2 — risk-of-bias tool for diagnostic accuracy studies.
- OHRQoL: Oral Health-Related Quality of Life.
- Early childhood caries (ECC): caries lesions in primary teeth of young children.
