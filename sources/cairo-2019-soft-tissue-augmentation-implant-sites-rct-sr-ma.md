## Why Ingested

임플란트 주위 연조직 증대술의 RCT 근거를 정량화한 핵심 SR+MA로, autogenous CTG와 이종이식 콜라겐 매트릭스(XCM) 간 연조직 두께 증가량(STT WMD −0.30 mm, CTG 우위)을 처음 메타분석한 논문이다. 기존 [[wiki/overviews/keratinized-mucosa-peri-implant-health-overview]]의 치료 근거 섹션과 [[wiki/implants/montero-2022-soft-tissue-substitutes-vs-autogenous-keratinized-mucosa-sr]]의 선행 evidence로서 연결된다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 14 RCTs (475 patients, 538 implants) showing soft-tissue augmentation improves keratinized tissue height and thickness at implant sites, with connective tissue graft (CTG) superior to xenogeneic collagen matrix (XCM) for soft-tissue thickness gain (WMD −0.30 mm, 95% CI −0.43 to −0.17).

## 한줄요약

14개 RCT (475명, 538개 임플란트) 메타분석: 임플란트 주위 연조직 증대술은 각화조직 높이·두께를 향상시키며, 연결조직이식(CTG)이 이종콜라겐 매트릭스(XCM)보다 연조직 두께 증가에 유의하게 우수함(WMD −0.30 mm).

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First SR+MA restricted to **RCTs only** for soft-tissue augmentation around implants — eliminates selection bias present in earlier reviews that pooled observational and experimental designs.
2. Establishes that soft-tissue augmentation yields measurable gains in both **keratinized tissue height (KT)** and **soft-tissue thickness (STT)** versus no augmentation.
3. Quantifies CTG vs XCM comparison via meta-analysis: CTG superior for STT gain (WMD −0.30 mm, 95% CI −0.43 to −0.17; P < .00001).
4. Highlights that only 5 of 14 included RCTs were at low risk of bias — underscoring the fragility of the current evidence base.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Study design**: Systematic review + random-effects meta-analysis
- **Search**: Manual + electronic database searches (databases not specified in available abstract)
- **PRISMA** compliance reported (references 12–13 cite PRISMA statement)
- **Inclusion criteria**: Randomized controlled trials evaluating surgical soft-tissue augmentation at implant sites
- **Primary outcomes**: Change in keratinized tissue height (KT) and soft-tissue thickness (STT)
- **Statistical method**: Weighted mean differences (WMD) with 95% confidence intervals; random-effects model
- **Risk of bias**: Formal assessment — 9/14 studies rated as having unclear or high risk of bias; only 5/14 at low risk
- **Studies included**: 14 RCTs; 475 patients; 538 implants
- **Comparisons examined**:
  - Augmentation vs no augmentation (single-study level)
  - CTG vs XCM (meta-analysis)
  - Free gingival graft (FGG) vs vestibuloplasty; FGG vs acellular dermal matrix (ADM); L-PRF; volume-stable collagen matrix (VSCM) — cited as individual RCTs in references (single-study comparisons)

**Note on data depth**: The source PDF is a Wiley Online Library HTML print capturing only the abstract and reference list. No tables of individual study data, forest plots, or subgroup analyses are available from this PDF. Quantitative results are limited to what is stated in the abstract.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Comparison | Outcome | WMD (mm) | 95% CI | P-value |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| CTG vs XCM | STT gain (favors CTG) | −0.30 | −0.43 to −0.17 | < .00001 |

- All 14 included single studies reported higher STT and KT with augmentation vs no augmentation.
- Meta-analysis for KT (keratinized tissue height): results not quantified in abstract — described as favoring augmentation in single-study comparisons only.
- CTG superiority over XCM for STT gain is the only pooled meta-analytic estimate explicitly reported.
- Evidence certainty: low overall (9/14 RCTs at unclear/high risk of bias).

**Included RCT examples** (from reference list):
- Sanz et al. 2009 (mucograft prototype vs FGG for KT width)
- Lorenzo et al. 2012 (XCM vs no augmentation)
- Basegmez et al. 2012, 2013 (FGG vs vestibuloplasty; FGG vs ADM)
- Thoma et al. 2016 (VSCM vs CTG for STT)
- Cairo et al. 2017 (XCM vs CTG — same group)
- Rojo et al. 2018 (CTG from lateral palate vs tuberosity)
- Temmerman et al. 2018 (L-PRF for KT width)
- Puzio et al. 2018 (CTG vs XCM, ultrasound assessment)
- Zuiderlved et al. 2018 (soft-tissue grafting at single implant, esthetics)
- Vellis et al. 2019 (XCM vs FGG around functioning implants)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **PDF depth limit**: Only abstract + references available; full methodology, individual study forest plots, and subgroup analyses are not accessible from this source.
- **Risk of bias**: 9/14 studies at unclear or high risk of bias — pooled estimates should be interpreted cautiously.
- **Heterogeneity**: Extent of statistical heterogeneity (I²) not reported in abstract; likely present given procedural diversity.
- **Outcome heterogeneity**: KT and STT measured at variable follow-up timepoints across studies; long-term (>1 year) data sparse.
- **Limited meta-analysis scope**: Only CTG vs XCM comparison had sufficient homogeneity for pooling; most other comparisons remain at single-study level.
- **Authors' conclusion**: Further RCTs at low risk of bias are needed before definitive procedural recommendations can be made.

## 7. Glossary

- **CTG** (Connective Tissue Graft, 연결조직이식): Subepithelial connective tissue graft harvested from palate — gold standard autogenous soft-tissue graft.
- **XCM** (Xenogeneic Collagen Matrix, 이종콜라겐 매트릭스): Porcine-derived collagen matrix (e.g., Mucograft) used as CTG substitute.
- **KT** (Keratinized Tissue, 각화조직): Width/height of keratinized mucosa around implants; primary peri-implant soft-tissue dimension.
- **STT** (Soft-Tissue Thickness, 연조직 두께): Buccal mucosal thickness at implant site; second primary outcome.
- **WMD** (Weighted Mean Difference, 가중평균차): Meta-analytic pooled difference in continuous outcomes across studies.
- **FGG** (Free Gingival Graft, 유리치은이식): Autogenous graft including epithelium + connective tissue; used primarily for KT gain.
- **ADM** (Acellular Dermal Matrix, 무세포진피기질): Human-derived allograft soft-tissue substitute.
- **VSCM** (Volume-Stable Collagen Matrix): Three-dimensional collagen matrix designed for volumetric soft-tissue augmentation.
- **L-PRF** (Leukocyte- and Platelet-Rich Fibrin, 백혈구혈소판풍부피브린): Autologous platelet concentrate used for soft-tissue augmentation.
- **APF** (Apically Positioned Flap, 근단변위판막): Flap technique used in combination with grafts to increase keratinized mucosa zone.
