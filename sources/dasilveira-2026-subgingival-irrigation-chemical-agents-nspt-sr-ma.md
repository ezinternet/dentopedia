## Why Ingested

기존 [[periodontics/ramanauskaite-2020-antiseptics-adjuncts-scaling-root-planing]]가 항균제를 SRP 보조로 다룬 반면, 이 2026 SR+MA는 **약제(PVP-I/CHX/EO/OW/BA)를 치은연하 세척(subgingival irrigation)으로 전달**하는 좁은 시나리오만 16편 RCT로 모아, 어떤 약제·전달법(시린지 vs 초음파)·추적기간에서도 PPD·CAL·BOP에 추가 이득이 없음을 보여 "세척 보조"라는 흔한 임상 습관의 근거 결핍을 정량화한다. [[periodontics/khattri-2020-adjunctive-systemic-antimicrobials-non-surgical-treatment]]의 전신 항균제 논의와 대비되는 국소·세척 채널 근거.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 16 RCTs (712 patients): adjunctive subgingival irrigation with chemical agents (PVP-I, CHX, essential oils, ozonated water, boric acid) during NSPT gave NO additional benefit over water/saline in PPD reduction (MD 0.01 mm), CAL gain (MD 0.09 mm), or BOP; evidence low to very low.

## 한줄요약

16편 RCT(712명) SR+MA — NSPT에 약제(PVP-I·CHX·정유·오존수·붕산) 치은연하 세척을 더해도 물/식염수 대비 PPD(MD 0.01mm)·CAL(MD 0.09mm)·BOP에 추가 이득 없음(근거수준 낮음~매우낮음).

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (PRISMA; PROSPERO ID 1011516)
- **Journal**: Clinical Oral Investigations 2026;30(6)
- **Databases**: MEDLINE/PubMed, Web of Science, LILACS (up to March 2025); RoB 2.0; GRADE
- **PICOS**: Periodontitis patients (systemically healthy) → NSPT + chemical-agent subgingival irrigation (syringe or ultrasonic) vs NSPT alone / water / saline → PPD reduction (primary), CAL gain, BOP change; RCTs ≥3-month follow-up.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First update since Van der Sluijs 2016 (adds 8 RCTs, stricter eligibility): overturns that review's reported slight PVP-I CAL benefit — **not confirmed**.
- Tests the *delivery channel* specifically: subgingival irrigation (not gels, not mouthwash, not full-mouth disinfection, not slow-release chips).
- Subgroup-resolves by agent, irrigation method (syringe vs ultrasonic), follow-up time, and furcation involvement.
- Frames result within antimicrobial-stewardship: no benefit + CHX tolerance/resistance concern → routine adjunctive subgingival irrigation not supported.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 16 RCTs (1244 records screened); 13 parallel, 3 split-mouth; 712 patients (336 M, 376 F); age 26-72y; follow-up 3-12 months (7×3mo, 6×6mo, 3×12mo).
- 15 studies excluded smokers; 1 (Cosyn 2013) included smokers.
- Delivery: 13 ultrasonic-scaler irrigation, 3 syringe.
- Agents/concentrations: PVP-I 10%/1%/0.5%/0.1%/unreported; EOs (thymol/eucalyptol/methyl salicylate/menthol/ethanol); CHX 0.2% (4) and 0.12% (1); BA 0.75%; ozonated water.
- Random-effects MD meta-analyses for CAL, PPD, BOP; heterogeneity by Cochran Q and I²; subanalyses by agent, method, follow-up, furcation.
- Excluded: gel vehicles, full-mouth disinfection, additional mouthwash in test group, <3mo follow-up, extracts/phytotherapy, systemic antibiotics/corticosteroids within 3mo.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Overall pooled (13 studies), all non-significant, low quality:**
  - PPD change: MD **0.01 mm** (95% CI -0.11 to 0.14; P=0.82; I²=73%)
  - CAL gain: MD **0.09 mm** (95% CI 0.00 to 0.18; P=0.05; I²=0%)
  - BOP reduction: MD **-1.48** (95% CI -5.84 to 12.88; P=0.51; I²=0%)
- By agent: PVP-I, CHX, EO, BA — no additional benefit in any parameter.
- **Ozonated water** (single study): slight significant PPD reduction (~0.40 mm) — but original RCT found no significant effect; clinically marginal, interpret with caution.
- Follow-up: no difference in PPD/BOP at 3/6/12mo. CAL gain at **12 months** (non-furcation): small but significant MD **0.25 mm** (95% CI 0.09-0.40; P=0.002; I²=0%) — only 2 studies, one with unequal groups; caution.
- Irrigation method (syringe vs ultrasonic): no difference in PPD, CAL, BOP.
- Furcation studies (3): PPD MD 0.02, CAL MD -0.08, BOP MD 0.56 — all NS.
- Residual pockets/pocket closure: reduction in all groups, no between-group difference (Al-Saeed, Habashneh, Leonhardt, Ribeiro ×2, Rosling, Vitt).
- No substantial publication bias on funnel plots.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- 14/16 RCTs "some concerns" RoB (no prospective registration → Domain 5); 2 high RoB (Rosling 2001 Domain 2; Saglam 2013 Domain 3+5). GRADE low to very low.
- Outcomes mostly full-mouth means — dilute deep-pocket effects; no standardized residual-pocket definition (no MA possible).
- Adverse effects reported in only 2 studies; only 1 study reported a PRO (VAS, no difference); no validated PROMs.
- High PPD heterogeneity (I²=73%): varied concentrations, full-mouth vs deep-pocket reporting, non-standard irrigant volume/duration.
- Future: site/patient-level endpoints (pocket closure, PPD<4mm), standardized irrigant volume/time, longer follow-up, validated PROMs, novel agents/delivery.

## 6. Related Work

- Van der Sluijs et al. 2016 — prior SR (reported slight PVP-I CAL gain; not confirmed here).
- EFP S3 guideline (Sanz 2020) — chemical adjuncts/slow-release chips: no significant long-term effect, insufficient CAL/BOP/pocket-closure data.

## 7. Glossary

- **NSPT** — non-surgical periodontal therapy (subgingival instrumentation, EFP step 2).
- **CA** — chemical agents (antiseptics/irrigants).
- **PVP-I** — povidone-iodine; **CHX** — chlorhexidine; **EO** — essential oils; **OW** — ozonated water; **BA** — boric acid.
- **PPD** — probing pocket depth; **CAL** — clinical attachment level; **BOP** — bleeding on probing.
- **RoB 2.0** — Cochrane risk-of-bias tool v2; **GRADE** — certainty-of-evidence framework.
