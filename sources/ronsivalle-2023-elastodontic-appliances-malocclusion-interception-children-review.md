## Why Ingested

Anchor synthesis page for the EF-Line/elastodontic myofunctional-orthodontics batch: this is a systematic-search review (4 databases, PICO, Cohen kappa) that maps *which* malocclusions are amenable to elastodontic appliances (EAs) across six clinical domains. Reinforces and predates the sibling [[wiki/orthodontics/myofunctional/ureni-2024-elastodontic-devices-sagittal-malocclusion-scoping-review]] (a scoping review narrowed to sagittal discrepancies) — Ronsivalle 2023 supplies the broader indication map (sagittal + transversal + atypical swallowing + bad habits + two-phase + retention) into which the sagittal-focused scoping evidence fits.

## One-line Summary

Systematic-search hybrid review (4 databases, 2403→13 included studies, mostly case reports/series + retrospective; 1 prospective) concluding elastodontic appliances can intercept mild-to-moderate dentoskeletal malocclusion in mixed dentition (mild class III, class II with high overjet/overbite, functional posterior crossbite, anterior crowding, atypical swallowing) but with weak evidence — caution before substituting conventional functional appliances.

## 한줄요약

체계적 검색 기반 하이브리드 리뷰(4개 DB, 2403건→최종 13편, 대부분 증례보고/증례군·후향연구, 전향 1편)로 탄성교정장치(Elastodontic Appliance, EA)가 혼합치열기의 경도~중등도 치성-골격성 부정교합(경도 3급, 과도한 overjet/overbite의 2급, 기능성 구치부 반대교합, 전치부 총생, 비정형 연하)을 차단치료할 수 있으나 근거 수준이 낮아 기존 기능성 장치 대체에는 신중해야 한다고 결론.

- **Title**: Elastodontic Appliances for the Interception of Malocclusion in Children: A Systematic Narrative Hybrid Review
- **Authors**: Ronsivalle V, Nucci L, Bua N, Palazzo G, La Rosa S, Conforte C, Lo Giudice A
- **Journal**: Children (Basel) 2023;10(11):1821
- **DOI**: 10.3390/children10111821 | **PMID**: 38002912 | **PMCID**: PMC10670240
- **Study type**: Systematic narrative hybrid review (HR) — systematic search methodology (Web of Science, Embase, Scopus, PubMed; PICO eligibility; Cohen kappa inter-rater; EndNote X9) with narrative (qualitative, domain-based) synthesis. No meta-analysis.
- **Affiliation**: Department of Orthodontics, University of Catania (figures from its clinical database).
- **Research question**: "Which malocclusions and/or dental developmental discrepancies may be amenable to treatment with elastodontic devices?"

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First systematic-search overview defining the **indication map** for elastodontic appliances (EAs) in interceptive orthodontics, organized into six clinical domains: sagittal discrepancies, transversal discrepancies, atypical swallowing/bad habits/teeth malposition, two-phase orthodontics, and lack of teeth retention.
2. Establishes EAs as removable **silicone elastomer** appliances delivering light biological elastic forces, working via the neuromuscular system rather than direct active force ("elastodontic space" / neutral zone created by lip–tongue–cheek harmony) — fewer dentoalveolar compensations than fixed appliances.
3. Historical lineage: Kesling **positioner** (1945) → Soulet & Besombes **activator** (1950, France, "interceptive orthopedics") → Bergersen **Occlus-O-Guide / eruption guidance appliance (EGA)** (1975).
4. Documents treatment protocol (active phase 6–8 months, max 1 year; night + 1–2 h daytime use; retention phase 12–16 months, night-only) and design principles (vestibular/lingual flanges, four-plane orthopedic action: vertical, transverse, sagittal, torsional).
5. Flags weak evidence base and calls for long-term comparative research vs conventional functional devices.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Systematic narrative hybrid review following published HR methodology — systematic search/selection rigor + narrative data presentation.
- **Eligibility (PICO)**: P = growing human subjects; I = treatment with elastodontic devices; C = before/after outcomes or considerations with EAs; O = occlusal/radiographic dental and/or skeletal outcomes. Reviews excluded. No year or language limits.
- **Databases**: Web of Science, Embase, Scopus, PubMed (searched up to September 2023); reference-list hand-search; health-sciences librarian assisted strategy.
- **Selection/extraction**: EndNote X9; duplicates removed; two reviewers (V.R., S.L.R.) screened titles/abstracts then full texts; disagreements resolved by third author (G.P.); inter-rater agreement by Cohen kappa.
- **Synthesis**: Narrative, domain-based (no meta-analysis), supplemented by clinical iconographic examples.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Screening flow**: 2403 citations → 2168 records after duplicate removal → 2029 removed on title/abstract → 120 full texts retrieved → **13 studies included**.
- **Included study designs (13)**: 3 case reports, 3 case–control studies, 1 case series, 5 retrospective studies, 1 prospective study. *(Note: §4.5 Limitations states the number included is "only 16" — internal inconsistency in the paper; Results section gives the explicit 13-study breakdown.)*
- **Background efficacy figures cited**: ~15% of mixed-dentition disorders can be corrected and ~49% improved with interceptive orthodontic treatment.
- **Sagittal — mild class III** (2 studies, 1 retrospective + 1 case series): EAs produced significant skeletal change (ANB) beyond expected dentoalveolar change; skeletal change mainly from **decreased SNB** (mandibular postural change after anterior crossbite correction), with small non-significant SNA increase and significant vestibular inclination of upper incisors → corrects negative overjet.
- **Sagittal — class II** (prospective + case series): SNA unchanged pre/post at 1 year; **SNB significantly increased**; ANB reduced to physiological values; significant increase in lower anterior and total facial height and mandibular length; no effect on cranio-mandibular vertical relationship; minor counterclockwise mandibular growth-rotation tendency (2 studies). EAs effective for class II with high overjet.
- **Transversal — functional posterior crossbite** (1 prospective): inter-molar width (IMW) and inter-canine width (ICW) significantly increased; crossbite side gained more IMW/ICW than non-crossbite side, reducing the inter-side difference post-treatment.
- **Transversal — anterior crowding** (1 retrospective + 1 prospective + 1 case series + 2 case reports): EAs address early anterior crowding via anterior elastic channel promoting transverse dentoalveolar growth.
- **Atypical swallowing / bad habits** (retrospective + case–control): EAs prevent bilabial contact, reposition tongue, instruct correct swallowing; improve postural problems, bad habits, and breathing in class II oral-breathers.
- **Two-phase orthodontics** (case report): 13-yr-old, class I malocclusion / severe crowding both arches on skeletal class II base, overjet 4 mm, overbite 4.5 mm, deep curve of Spee, upper midline shifted right → after **4 months** active EA + myofunctional therapy, overbite reduced to edge-to-edge, anterior teeth aligned, mild mesial tipping of first molars; subsequent fixed therapy easier/faster.
- **Lack of teeth retention** (case report): EAs effective in dentinogenesis imperfecta thanks to minimum tooth anchorage (where bonded/banded appliances fail).
- **Airway/OSAS**: class II patients have smaller airway volumes; two studies report EAs improve deglutition, phonation, and upper-airway respiratory function vs untreated controls, with inferior hyoid shift — proposed for pediatric OSAS.
- **Pontilicus posticus (PP)**: incidental cephalometric finding reported in 12.6% of patients, more frequent in skeletal class II — should be evaluated during orthodontic/elastodontic treatment.
- **Tolerability**: prefabricated (cheaper than custom functional devices), no impression/construction bite needed, not caries-susceptible, no periodontal harm or root resorption; mild side effects (excess salivation, pain) that fade with use.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small number of included studies with weak designs (predominantly case reports/series and retrospective) → insufficient to draw firm conclusions.
- Internal inconsistency in reported study count (13 in Results vs "16" in Limitations).
- Narrative synthesis only — no quantitative pooling/meta-analysis; heterogeneous outcomes across distinct domains.
- Some data retrieved as secondary outcomes and not fully discussed.
- Authors call for long-term studies on effects, advantages/disadvantages of EAs vs conventional functional devices; CAD-CAM / 3D-printing customized EAs proposed as a future direction.
- Conclusion urges **caution** before treating EAs as an alternative to functional devices given absent solid evidence.

## 7. Glossary

- **Elastodontic appliance (EA)** — prefabricated removable silicone-elastomer myofunctional device delivering light elastic forces to intercept malocclusion in mixed dentition.
- **Interceptive orthodontics** — early treatment (mixed dentition) eliminating factors that prevent harmonious arch development.
- **ANB / SNA / SNB** — cephalometric sagittal angles (A-Nasion-B; Sella-Nasion-A; Sella-Nasion-B) defining maxillo-mandibular relationship.
- **IMW / ICW** — inter-molar width / inter-canine width (transverse arch dimensions).
- **OVJ / OVB** — overjet / overbite.
- **EGA (eruption guidance appliance) / Occlus-O-Guide** — Bergersen 1975 prefabricated elastomeric appliance combining positioner + functional appliance features.
- **Positioner** — Kesling 1945 elastomeric finishing/retention device, forerunner of EAs.
- **Hybrid review (HR)** — review combining systematic search rigor with narrative data synthesis.
- **Elastodontic space** — neutral zone created by lip–tongue–cheek muscular harmony within which tooth movement occurs.
- **Pontilicus posticus (PP)** — abnormal bony bridge of the atlas; incidental cephalometric finding (12.6%), more frequent in skeletal class II.
