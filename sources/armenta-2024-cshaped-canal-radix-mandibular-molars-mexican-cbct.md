## Why Ingested

Adds a Mexican (Latin American) population data point to the C-shaped canal prevalence map and uniquely co-reports radix entomolaris prevalence in the same mandibular-molar cohort, extending the morphology framework anchored by [[endodontics/anatomy/song-2008-cross-sectional-morphology-and-minimum-canal]]. Its female-predominance and C2-dominant findings reinforce the pattern seen in the sibling Iraqi/Iranian prevalence pages ([[endodontics/anatomy/shemesh-2017-cshaped-canals-cbct-mandibular-molars]], [[endodontics/anatomy/shekarian-2023-cshaped-canals-three-rooted-mandibular-iranian]]).

## One-line Summary

CBCT cross-sectional study (2173 mandibular molars — 1057 first + 1116 second — from 724 Mexican CBCT scans): C-shaped canal prevalence 0.2% in first vs 14.1% in second mandibular molars; female predominance (27.3% vs 13.3%, p=0.004, OR 1.78); C2 most common type (39.3%); radix entomolaris in 3.4% of first and 1.4% of second molars.

## 1. Document Information

- Type: Retrospective, observational, cross-sectional (transversal) CBCT study
- Population: Mexican (Durango City), patients treated 2018–2023, Juarez University
- Sample: 2173 mandibular molars (1057 first + 1116 second) from 724 CBCT scans (454 female / 270 male; mean age 44.15 ± 19.54y)
- Imaging: Newtom VGiEvo; RadiAnt DICOM viewer 2023.1
- Journal: Dentistry Journal (Dent. J.) 2024, 12, 212

## 2. Key Contributions

- First detailed Mexican-population C-shaped canal + radix prevalence with Fan classification breakdown.
- Co-reports radix entomolaris in the same cohort (3.4% first, 1.4% second molars).
- Confirms second > first mandibular molar prevalence gradient (14.1% vs 0.2%).
- Significant gender effect (OR 1.78) and side effect (left 3.7 highest at 14.8%).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Three specialists (endodontics, endoperiodontics, oral radiology) evaluated via RadiAnt software.
- Fan et al. C1–C5 classification:
  - C1: continuous C; C2: interrupted/semicolon, α & β < 60°; C3: 2–3 separate canals; C4: single round/oval canal; C5: no lumen.
- Radix entomolaris = supernumerary distolingual third root.
- FDI nomenclature. Inclusion: fully developed roots; exclusion: RCT-treated, impacted, developmental anomalies, open apices, calcified, third molars.
- Statistics: chi-square; IBM SPSS Statistics 25.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- C-shaped canals: 160 teeth total.
  - First mandibular molar: 0.2%; Second mandibular molar: 14.1%.
  - Highest: left second mandibular molar (3.7) 14.8%; right second (4.7) 13.5% (p < 0.001).
  - Gender: female 27.3% vs male 13.3% (p=0.004, OR 1.78).
  - Most common configuration: C2 (39.3%).
- Radix entomolaris: 52 teeth.
  - First mandibular molar 3.4%; second 1.4% (more frequent in first molars, p < 0.001).
- Global context cited: overall C-shaped prevalence 13.9%; China highest 44.0%; Mexico ~14.2%.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-city retrospective sample (Durango); regional rather than nationally representative.
- CBCT voxel resolution below micro-CT gold standard.
- No treatment-outcome correlation.
- Mixed ethnic background limits direct comparison with single-ethnicity cohorts.

## 7. Glossary

- C-shaped canal: root canal whose axial cross-section resembles "C", with connecting fins/isthmuses.
- Radix entomolaris (RE): supernumerary distolingual third root of a mandibular molar.
- Radix paramolaris (RP): supernumerary mesiobuccal third root.
- Fan classification (C1–C5): CBCT axial classification of C-shaped canal cross-section.
- FDI nomenclature: two-digit tooth-numbering system (e.g., 3.7 = lower left second molar).
- OR (odds ratio): here 1.78 for female vs male C-shaped canal odds.
