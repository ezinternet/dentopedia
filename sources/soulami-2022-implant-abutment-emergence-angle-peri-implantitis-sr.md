## Why Ingested

Adds the prosthetic-design risk axis (emergence angle/profile) to the peri-implantitis evidence base summarised in [[wiki/overviews/peri-implantitis-management-overview]], complementing prosthetic-design work such as [[wiki/implants/peri-implantitis/lin-2025-influence-of-prosthetic-designs]] with a focused SR on emergence angle (EA) >30° and convex emergence profile (EP).

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (3 studies, 168–349 implants): a larger implant-abutment emergence angle (>30°) and a convex emergence profile are associated with higher peri-implantitis prevalence / marginal bone loss vs EA <30°, but with only 3 eligible studies causality is unproven and findings must be read with caution.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(3편, 임플란트 168–349개): 큰 임플란트-지대주 출현각(>30°)과 볼록한 출현윤곽(convex EP)이 작은 각(<30°)보다 임플란트주위염 유병률·변연골소실과 연관. 단 3편뿐이라 인과성은 미확립, 신중 해석 필요.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Systematic review (no meta-analysis — heterogeneity precluded pooling)
- **Journal**: Clinical and Experimental Dental Research 2022;8:795–806
- **Institution**: ACTA, Amsterdam, Netherlands
- **DOI**: 10.1002/cre2.594 (open access)
- **Protocol**: ACTA ethics #2020168

## 2. Key Contributions

- First focused SR isolating implant-abutment **emergence angle (EA)** and **emergence profile (EP)** as peri-implantitis risk variables.
- Synthesises a consistent signal: EA >30° and convex EP → higher peri-implantitis/MBL, especially in bone-level implants.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PICO: P = patients with dental implants; I = large EA and/or convex EP; C = small EA and/or concave/straight EP; O = peri-implant disease.
- Databases: PubMed + Cochrane Library, inception–April 2022; 3 search strategies; hand-search of reference lists.
- Eligible designs: (R)CTs, cohort, cross-sectional, case-control in systemically healthy adults (≥18 y). Excluded narrative reviews, case reports, denture-supported restorations, non-EA abutment angles.
- Two reviewers (Soulami, van der Weijden), third (Slot) for disagreement. p<.05 significant. Meta-analysis only if homogeneous — not performed.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- 1116 unique titles → **3 included studies** (168–349 implants each).
- Two studies: mean peri-implantitis prevalence 16.7% and 24.8% (implant level); both showed significantly higher peri-implantitis in bone-level implants with EA >30° vs <30°.
- Third study: MBL tended to be smaller when EA ~20°–40°.
- One study: convex EP → significantly higher peri-implantitis vs concave/straight EP; another: convex EP higher prevalence when combined with EA >30°.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 3 eligible studies → no meta-analysis; associations "should be considered with caution."
- Observational designs → causality unresolved.
- EA/EP definitions and measurement varied across studies (heterogeneity).

## 7. Glossary

- **Emergence angle (EA)**: angle of the implant-abutment surface to the implant long axis.
- **Emergence profile (EP)**: contour of the restoration as it emerges from the implant platform (convex / concave / straight).
- **Bone-level implant**: implant platform placed at the crestal bone level.
