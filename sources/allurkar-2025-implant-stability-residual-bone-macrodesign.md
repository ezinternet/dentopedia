## One-line Summary
In vitro bovine rib (type-IV mimic): ISQ increases with residual bone height; at RBH 6–9 mm Straumann Tapered Effect macro-design shows highest ISQ vs SP/BL/BLT (p<0.01).

## 1. Document Information
Open-access in vitro study in Bioinformation. Aim: evaluate role of residual bone height (RBH) and implant macro-design on ISQ during placement in maxillary sinus floor elevation (SFE).

## 2. Key Contributions
- ISQ values **increase as residual bone height increases** (3 → 6 → 9 → 15 mm)
- Tapered Effect (TE) design yields highest ISQ at RBH 6 mm and 9 mm
- Standard Plus / Bone Level / Bone Level Tapered show lower ISQ at intermediate RBH

## 3. Methodology
- 160 fresh bovine rib specimens (type-IV bone mimic)
- 4 RBH categories: 3, 6, 9, 15 mm × 4 macro-designs (Straumann SP / TE / BL / BLT)
- Osstell Mentor RFA for ISQ
- Digital torque driver for maximum insertion torque (MIT)

## 4. Key Results
- ISQ ↑ with RBH ↑
- At RBH 6 mm and 9 mm: TE > SP, BL, BLT (p<0.01)
- Implant stability is jointly determined by RBH + macro-design in SFE

## 5. Limitations
- In vitro bovine rib model only
- No clinical follow-up — only placement ISQ
- 4 macro-designs from single manufacturer (Straumann)
