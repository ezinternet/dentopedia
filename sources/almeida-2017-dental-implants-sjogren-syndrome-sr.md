## Why Ingested

Sjögren-specific implant outcome evidence to anchor the autoimmune-dental implant batch. Where [[wiki/implants/esimekara-2022-dental-implants-autoimmune-diseases-sr]] pools many autoimmune diseases, this SR isolates Sjögren's syndrome (SS) — the autoimmune disease with the most direct oral/salivary impact (xerostomia, caries, periodontal disease) and thus the highest a priori implant-failure concern. It reinforces the autoimmune-SR conclusion that implants remain a viable option, while quantifying SS-specific survival, MBL, and biological-complication numbers.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (6 studies, 1997–2016; 357 implants in SS patients) finding high mean implant survival (93.7–94.6% over mean ~4 y), low marginal bone loss, low biological complications, and improved quality of life in all studies.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic review (per authors, as of 2017) to assess implant survival/success specifically in Sjögren's syndrome (SS) patients — a population with no prior synthesized evidence despite high dental rehabilitation need.
- Synthesizes 6 observational studies (1 case series, 1 prospective cohort, 4 retrospective cohorts), 357 implants in primary/secondary SS subjects.
- Reports a SS-pooled mean implant survival of 94.6 ± 5.6% over a mean 3.97 years (an alternate reported figure of 93.7% appears across the dataset depending on weighting); positions this against ~95.3–96.5% 10-year survival in healthy patients, suggesting possible higher early-failure risk in SS.
- Documents low MBL (good bone stability comparable to non-SS subjects) and predominantly low biological complications, with mucositis the most common.
- Confirms quality-of-life improvement (masticatory function, comfort, satisfaction) in all studies that assessed it.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Protocol**: PRISMA-P based, registered PROSPERO CRD42016053277; followed Cochrane Handbook, PRISMA, AMSTAR; PICOS framework.
- **Focused question**: What are the clinical outcomes of implants placed in patients with SS?
- **Databases**: PubMed/MEDLINE, Cochrane CENTRAL, Web of Science, EMBASE (to June 2017); grey literature (Grey Literature Report, OpenGrey); ClinicalTrials.gov; hand-search of journals + cross-referencing. No date or language restriction.
- **Population**: adults (≥18 y) with SS rehabilitated with dental implants.
- **Outcomes**: primary = implant survival; secondary = MBL, biological complications, quality of life.
- **Eligible designs**: prospective/retrospective cohort, case series, controlled clinical trial, RCT. Excluded: animal studies, in-vitro, case reports, reviews, untreated periodontal-disease participants, <18 y, other metabolic diseases.
- **Screening**: two independent reviewers; Cohen's kappa 0.79 and 0.85 (excellent agreement).
- **Risk of bias**: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS) for non-randomized studies; ≥6 stars = high quality.
- **Statistics**: descriptive only (mean survival and follow-up = sum of reported values / total events; Excel). No meta-analysis.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Search yield**: 115 (PubMed) + 3 (Cochrane) + 13 (Web of Science) + 20 (EMBASE) → 11 full texts → 5 excluded (3 case reports, 1 duplicate, 1 review) → **6 studies included** (1997–2016).
- **Design mix**: 1 case series (Payne 1997), 1 prospective cohort (Isidor 1999), 4 retrospective cohorts (Weinländer 2010, Öczakir 2015, Korfage 2016, Albrecht 2016).
- **Subjects**: range 3–205, mean 45.3; **357 implants** total in primary/secondary SS.
- **Pooled survival**: **94.6 ± 5.6%** over mean **3.97 years** follow-up. Per-study survival: Payne 88.4% (26/3 failed), Isidor 87% (54/7 failed), Weinländer 100% (21/0), Öczakir 100% (12/0), Korfage 97% (140/4 failed), Albrecht 95.2% (104/5 failed).
- **Surface signal**: the two studies with the most failures (Payne, Isidor) were the only two using machined-surface implants; early/late failure data not reported, so surface-effect conclusions not drawable.
- **MBL**: assessed in 3 studies (mean 4.2 y follow-up): Isidor 0.65 ± 0.07 mm; Korfage 0.89 ± 0.9 mm; Weinländer 3.1 ± 0.7 mm. Generally low/stable; no difference vs non-SS (Misch success threshold MBL <2 mm).
- **Biological complications**: peri-implant parameters in 2 studies — one found greater probing depth and bleeding in SS vs healthy; another found more mucositis (up to 94% of SS carriers) and peri-implantitis vs non-SS. No difference between primary vs secondary SS; no correlation with SS duration or immunosuppressive drug use.
- **Quality of life**: improved (mastication, comfort, satisfaction) in all 3 studies that assessed it.
- **Risk of bias (NOS)**: Korfage 8/9, Albrecht 7/9, Isidor 6/9, Weinländer 6/9 (high quality); Payne 5/9, Öczakir 5/9 (potential bias). No study reached max score; none followed a transparency guideline (e.g., STROBE).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- No RCTs; only 1 of 6 studies prospective; small body of observational evidence.
- Two studies (Payne, Öczakir) at high risk of bias (NOS <6) — interpret with caution.
- Confounders (smoking, medications/corticosteroids, osteoporosis) not adjusted; failure timing (early vs late) and primary-vs-secondary SS contribution to failures not reported.
- No descriptive meta-analysis weighting; descriptive pooling only.
- Future work: more (preferably prospective) long-term studies; microbiological/cytokine/biomolecular profiling of peri-implant disease in SS; clarify primary vs secondary SS influence.

## 7. Glossary

- **SS (Sjögren's syndrome)**: chronic autoimmune disease with focal mononuclear infiltration of salivary/lachrymal glands → xerostomia, increased caries/periodontal disease; primary (alone) vs secondary (with another autoimmune disease, e.g., RA, SLE); ~9:1 female predominance, age 40–60.
- **MBL (Marginal Bone Loss)**: radiographic peri-implant crestal bone loss; Misch success threshold <2 mm regardless of follow-up.
- **NOS (Newcastle-Ottawa Scale)**: quality/risk-of-bias tool for non-randomized studies (max 9 stars; ≥6 = high quality).
- **Mucositis**: peri-implant soft-tissue inflammation without bone loss; most common biological complication.
- **Peri-implantitis**: peri-implant inflammation with progressive bone loss.
- **PICOS**: Population, Intervention, Comparison, Outcome, Study design framework.
- **PROSPERO**: international prospective register of systematic reviews (this SR: CRD42016053277).
