## Why Ingested

교합면 비니어(Occlusal Veneer)의 재사용 가능성을 연구한 논문으로, 기존 wiki의 [[wiki/veneers/schlichting-2022-ultrathin-cadcam-occlusal-veneers-dental-erosion]]이 초박형 교합면 비니어의 임상 결과를 다루지만 레이저 탈접(Debonding) 프로토콜이나 재료별 취약성은 다루지 않음. 본 연구는 Er;Cr:YSGG 레이저를 이용한 리튬 디실리케이트·초고밀도 리튬 디실리케이트·지르코니아 비니어의 탈접 시간 및 손상 패턴을 in vitro로 비교하여, 재료 선택 시 레이저 debonding 내성이라는 새로운 임상 기준을 제공함.

## One-line Summary

In vitro study (n=24 extracted molars, 3 ceramic groups) comparing Er;Cr:YSGG laser debonding of 0.5 mm ultrathin CAD/CAM occlusal veneers: debonding time was equivalent across lithium disilicate, highly condensed lithium disilicate (LiSi), and translucent zirconia (~87 s, p=0.995), but 60% of LiSi samples were damaged (fractured or cracked) vs. 0% damage in Emax or zirconia.

## 한줄요약

초박형 교합면 비니어(Occlusal Veneer) in vitro 연구(n=24): Er;Cr:YSGG 레이저 탈접(Laser Debonding) 시간은 리튬 디실리케이트(Lithium Disilicate, LD), 초고밀도 리튬 디실리케이트(LiSi), 반투명 지르코니아(Translucent Zirconia, STML) 간 유의한 차이 없음(~87초, p=0.995), 그러나 LiSi 표본의 60%가 균열 또는 파절 손상을 보여 임상적 재사용 적합성에 의문 제기.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Demonstrates that Er;Cr:YSGG laser (6 W, 20 Hz, scanning method) can efficiently debond 0.5 mm ultrathin CAD/CAM occlusal veneers of three ceramic types in approximately 87 seconds, with no material-dependent difference in debonding time.
2. Reveals a critical material-specific vulnerability: highly condensed lithium disilicate (GC Initial LiSi — HDM technology with microsized crystals) suffers 60% damage rate (40% fracture, 20% cracks) during laser debonding, while standard lithium disilicate (IPS Emax CAD) and translucent zirconia (Katana STML) show zero damage.
3. Proposes that HDM (High-Density Micronization) crystal architecture of LiSi — smaller, uniformly dispersed crystals — is more susceptible to laser-induced crystal disintegration compared to the traditional larger-crystal random dispersion of conventional lithium disilicate.
4. Confirms that equal translucency parameters across materials (all matched to low translucency) explain equivalent laser transmission and thus equivalent debonding times.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Study design**: In vitro, one-way ANOVA, 3 groups × 8 samples (n=24 total)

**Materials tested**:
- Group A: IPS Emax CAD (conventional lithium disilicate)
- Group B: GC Initial LiSi (highly condensed lithium disilicate — HDM)
- Group C: Katana STML (super translucent multilayered zirconia)

**Specimen preparation**:
- 24 freshly extracted, caries-free upper first molars stored in saline
- Flat occlusal preparation exposing central dentin with peripheral enamel (10 mm MD diameter standard)
- Veneers designed with Exocad software, milled with 5-axis milling machine (350i PRO, imes-icore), 0.5 mm thickness
- Surface treatment: HF acid + silane for glass ceramics; Al2O3 sandblasting + MDP primer (Z Prime Plus, Bisco) for zirconia
- Cementation: dual-cure resin cement (Breeze, Pentron), 250 g loading device, selective etching (37% phosphoric acid)

**Laser protocol**:
- Er;Cr:YSGG (Biolase Waterlase iPlus 2.0), 2780 nm wavelength
- 6 W, 20 Hz, 80% water, 60% air, MGG6 Saffire tip (600 μm diameter)
- Non-contact scanning method, tip perpendicular at 2 mm distance, 15 s horizontal scanning movements

**Outcome measures**:
- Debonding time (seconds) from laser start until veneer detachment
- Stereomicroscopic inspection for cracks/fracture (Olympus SZ40)
- Mode of failure: adhesive / mixed / cohesive classification

**Statistics**: One-way ANOVA + Tukey post hoc; Shapiro-Wilk normality; Levene homogeneity; α = 0.05; R v4.3.2

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Debonding time (mean ± SD)**:
| Material | Mean (s) | 95% CI | SD |
|---|---|---|---|
| Emax CAD | 86.94 | 68.86–105.02 | 20.63 |
| LiSi Initial | 86.14 | 64.09–108.19 | 25.16 |
| Katana STML | 87.52 | 69.60–105.44 | 20.45 |
- p = 0.995 (no significant difference)

**Debonding status**:
- Emax CAD: 100% intact (8/8)
- Katana STML: 100% intact (8/8)
- LiSi Initial: 40% intact (3/8), 40% fractured (4/8), 20% cracked (2/8)
  → Only 40% of LiSi samples were undamaged after debonding

**Mode of failure**: No cohesive failures in any group. Mixed failure predominated in Emax (6/8) and LiSi (7/8); adhesive failure more frequent in zirconia (5/8).

**Clinical implication**: Er;Cr:YSGG laser safe for retrieving and potentially reusing Emax and zirconia veneers; LiSi requires further investigation before laser debonding can be recommended clinically.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample size (n=8 per group) — power calculated from prior study, but limits generalizability
- In vitro only — no thermal cycling, fatigue, or aging simulation; clinical debonding conditions will differ
- One cement type and shade used — different cements or shades may affect laser transmission and debonding time
- Single veneer thickness (0.5 mm) — results may not apply to thicker restorations
- Authors call for further investigation of LiSi debonding using varied laser parameters or different protocols
- No pulp temperature measurement — safety at 6 W in clinical setting should be confirmed

## 6. Related Work

- Alikhasi et al. 2019 (J Lasers Med Sci): No significant debonding time difference between feldspathic ceramic and lithium disilicate — consistent with present findings
- Rechmann et al. 2014 (Lasers Surg Med): Demonstrated differences in laser transmission between zirconia (medium opacity) vs. lithium disilicate (low translucency) — translucency matching explains why current study found no difference
- Eid et al. 2021 (Lasers Dent Sci): Used same Er;Cr:YSGG parameters; reported differences in debonding times — attributed to materials with different transmission values in that study
- Zhang et al. 2021 (Lasers Med Sci): At 5 W Er:YAG, perceptible color changes and microcracks in lithium disilicate — consistent with crystal disintegration hypothesis proposed here
- Karagoz-Yildirak & Gozneli 2020 (Lasers Med Sci): Evaluated rebonding strength after Er:YAG laser debonding — supports reuse potential
- Deeb et al. 2022 (J Prosthodont): Scoping review of erbium laser-assisted ceramic debonding — provides context for methodology

## 7. Glossary

- **Er;Cr:YSGG**: Erbium, chromium-doped yttrium scandium gallium garnet laser; 2780 nm wavelength; high affinity for water molecules
- **HDM (High-Density Micronization)**: GC's technology in LiSi blocks — microsized lithium disilicate crystals evenly dispersed in glassy matrix (vs. random large-crystal dispersion in conventional LD)
- **STML (Super Translucent Multilayered)**: Katana zirconia grade with translucency closest to lithium disilicate while retaining posterior mechanical properties
- **Scanning method**: Laser delivery by moving handpiece horizontally across surface rather than fixed-point; reduces heat conduction
- **APC concept**: Air-particle abrasion, zirconia primer (MDP), adhesive composite resin — bonding protocol for zirconia
- **MDP (10-methacryloxydecyl dihydrogen phosphate)**: Phosphate monomer primer for zirconia; chemical coupling
- **Thermal ablation**: Fastest laser-ceramic interaction mechanism; less heat generation than thermal softening
- **Debonding time**: Measured from laser activation until visible/audible veneer detachment
