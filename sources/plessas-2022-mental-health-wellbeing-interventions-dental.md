## One-line Summary
Systematic review of workplace interventions for mental health & wellbeing in the dental sector — paucity of evidence, especially for primary prevention; secondary/tertiary (psychoeducational, mindfulness) dominate.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Evidence-Based Dentistry / British Dental Journal (BDJ), 2022 Dec 7
- DOI: 10.1038/s41432-022-0831-0
- Study type: Systematic review (no meta-analysis), narrative synthesis
- Affiliation: Peninsula Dental School, University of Plymouth, UK
- Context: UK dentist mental wellness — workforce loss risk

## 2. Key Contributions
- Maps interventions across the 3-tier prevention framework (primary / secondary / tertiary) for dental workforce mental health
- Confirms a research gap at the **primary prevention** level (organisational and systemic interventions)
- Argues for adapting evidence-based workplace interventions from other healthcare professions (medicine, nursing) to the dental context
- Calls for early-career and undergraduate-stage embedding of mental wellbeing recognition

## 3. Methodology
- Standard SR methodology (PRISMA-style)
- Population: dental care professionals (dentists, DCPs)
- Intervention: any structured workplace mental-health intervention
- Outcomes: any wellbeing/burnout/stress/anxiety measure
- Quality assessment of included studies

## 4. Key Results
- Very few studies meet inclusion (research gap)
- Most interventions are **secondary prevention** (psychoeducational, mindfulness/CBT, stress management training)
- Almost no **primary prevention** at organisational/systems level (workload, regulation, autonomy)
- **Tertiary prevention** (return-to-work, treatment) understudied
- Stressors that drive burnout (regulation, NHS conditions, fear of litigation) are NOT addressed by current intervention literature

## 5. Limitations
- Small body of evidence
- Heterogeneous interventions and outcome measures preclude pooling
- Most studies short follow-up, no cost-effectiveness data

## 6. Related Work
- BDA mental health surveys (UK)
- Salazar et al. 2019 model of dentist mental health determinants
- General healthcare workforce wellbeing literature (medicine, nursing)

## 7. Glossary
- **Primary prevention**: interventions targeting upstream causes (workplace design, workload, regulation) before distress develops
- **Secondary prevention**: skill-building for individuals already exposed to stress (mindfulness, CBT, resilience training)
- **Tertiary prevention**: treatment & rehabilitation for those already symptomatic (burnout treatment, return-to-work)
- **DCP**: Dental Care Professional (hygienist, therapist, technician, nurse)
- **GDP**: General Dental Practitioner
