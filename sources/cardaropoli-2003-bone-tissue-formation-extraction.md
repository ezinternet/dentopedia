## One-line Summary
Animal experiment (9 mongrel dogs, mandibular 4th premolar distal-root extraction sockets sacrificed at 1/3/7/14/30/60/90/120/180 days) histologically defined the temporal sequence of socket healing: blood clot → provisional matrix (day 7) → woven bone (day 14) → mineralized bone peak at day 30 (88% of socket volume) → remodeling to mostly lamellar bone marrow at day 180 (mineralized bone 15%, BM 85%); landmark paper establishing the modern timeline of post-extraction socket healing.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Periodontology 2003;30(9):809–818

- **Institution**: Department of Periodontology, The Sahlgrenska Academy at Göteborg University, Sweden
- **Study type**: In-vivo animal experimental histology study

## 2. Key Contributions
- First detailed **9-time-point histomorphometric timeline** of extraction-socket healing in a controlled large-animal model.
- Defined three vertical compartments (**marginal**, **central**, **apical**) and tracked their independent healing kinetics.
- Established that **mineralized bone peaks at ~day 30** then is replaced by bone marrow during remodeling — explaining why ridge dimensions and density continue changing for months.
- Documented formation of a **"hard tissue bridge"** (cortical bone) that closes the socket coronally — a fundamental observation cited by virtually all subsequent socket-preservation and immediate-implant literature.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 9 mongrel dogs.
- Bilateral 4th mandibular premolars hemisected; distal root extracted, mesial root retained → standardized "experimental unit" socket.
- Sacrifice time points: 1, 3, 7, 14, 30, 60, 90, 120, 180 days.
- Biopsies demineralized in EDTA, dehydrated, paraffin embedded.
- Serial sections 7 µm thick in mesiodistal plane; 3 central sections per socket evaluated.
- Morphometric measurement of volume fractions for: blood clot, provisional matrix (PM), provisional connective tissue (PCT), woven bone, lamellar bone, bone marrow (BM), connective tissue.
- Separate measurement of marginal vs central vs apical compartments.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Day 1–3**: blood clot fills socket.
- **Day 7**: clot partly replaced by provisional connective tissue (PCT).
- **Day 14**: socket comprised of provisional matrix (PM) + **woven bone** appears.
- **Day 30**: mineralized bone occupies **88% of socket volume** — peak mineralization.
- **Day 60**: bone marrow ~75% of central compartment.
- **Day 180**: mineralized bone reduced to **15%**, BM increased to **85%** — net remodeling toward lamellar bone with marrow spaces.
- **Coronal closure**: hard tissue bridge of cortical bone forms over the socket aperture.
- Spatial gradient: apical compartment heals earlier than marginal.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Animal (dog) socket — healing is faster than human; absolute time points not directly transferable.
- No external dimensional measurement of ridge contour change (the well-known horizontal/vertical ridge loss was characterized in companion Araújo/Lindhe papers).
- No intervention arms (no graft, no membrane) — describes natural healing only.
- 9 animals — small n for between-animal variability.

## 7. Glossary
- **PCT (Provisional Connective Tissue)**: granulation-like tissue replacing the blood clot within the first week.
- **Provisional Matrix (PM)**: collagen-rich extracellular scaffold preceding mineralization.
- **Woven bone**: immature, randomly oriented collagen-fiber bone — first mineralized tissue.
- **Lamellar bone**: organized, mature bone replacing woven bone via remodeling.
- **Hard tissue bridge**: cortical lid of bone that seals the socket coronally during healing.
- **Histomorphometry**: quantitative tissue volume measurement on histological sections.
