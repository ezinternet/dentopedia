## 2. Key Contributions
- 3Y-TZP (1998): highest strength/toughness, opaque → core material
- 5Y-PSZ (2014+): higher translucency, cubic+tetragonal, lower strength
- Multilayer (M3Y, M5Y, 2015+): gradient translucency for esthetic zones
- 6Y/UHTZ (2016–2021): ultra-high translucent, mostly cubic, lowest strength
- Trade-off: yttria content ↑ → translucency ↑, flexural strength ↓

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Narrative review by Ban S (Aichi Gakuin University) summarizing the development history of dental zirconia from 1998 to 2021. Covers evolution from 3Y-HA (conventional TZP, 1998) through high-translucent 3Y-TZP, 5Y-PSZ, multilayer types (M3Y, M5Y, M6Y), to ultra-high translucent zirconia (UHTZ, 2021). Explains crystalline phases (monoclinic, tetragonal, cubic), stabilizers (yttria), stress-induced phase transformation toughening. Discusses trade-off between translucency and strength with increasing yttria content.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
