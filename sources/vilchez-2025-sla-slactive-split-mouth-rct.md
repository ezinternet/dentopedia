## Why Ingested

Surface chemistry comparisons (SLActive vs SLA) are frequently cited when discussing ISQ stability thresholds and early loading decisions. This RCT directly addresses whether the modified hydrophilic SLActive surface confers measurable ISQ or MBL advantages over conventional SLA in matched split-mouth pairs вҖ” a direct relevance to [[wiki/implants/isq/andersson-2019-rfa-factors-5year-neoss-survival]], which established that surface modifications influence early RFA trajectories. VГӯlchez 2025 provides Level I evidence that ISQ trajectories and MBL outcomes converge by 8 weeks and 12 months post-loading despite different surface chemistries.

## One-line Summary

Split-mouth RCT (n=68, 136 implants, 12 months): modified SLActive and conventional SLA Bone Level Tapered Roxolid implants showed no significant difference in ISQ or in the primary outcome (MBL change loadingвҶ’12 months, 0.04 vs 0.07 mm, p=0.658), with 100% survival after loading in both groups.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Level I RCT evidence that modified SLActive and SLA Bone Level Tapered Roxolid implants have equivalent primary outcome вҖ” MBL change from loading to 12 months вҖ” in matched split-mouth pairs.
2. ISQ values high and equivalent for both surfaces at placement (SLActive 73.0 Вұ 10.3 vs SLA 72.3 Вұ 9.8, p=0.636) and at 8 weeks (75.5 vs 75.9, p=0.805).
3. Confirms that greatest MBL change occurs during the healing phase (placementвҶ’loading), not post-loading, pointing to implant placement factors rather than surface chemistry as the dominant driver.
4. Subgroup signal: modified SLActive performed slightly better in maxillary implants and in soft bone (types III-IV) for MBL changes placementвҶ’12 months.
5. Rigorous split-mouth design eliminates inter-patient confounding; both surfaces placed in the same patient eliminates systemic and host biology differences.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Design**: Dual-center, split-mouth, parallel-arm RCT. One test implant (modified SLActive) and one control implant (SLA) per patient in separate quadrants. Randomization: computer-generated block randomization stratified by center and arch; opaque sealed envelopes opened at surgery. Blinding: independent examiners, patients blinded.

**Implants**: Bone Level Tapered Roxolid (Straumann AG). Lengths 8/10/12 mm; diameters 3.3/4.1 mm. Subcrestal placement (~1 mm below bone crest). Platform-switching abutments (2.5 mm height) placed at surgery (one abutmentвҖ“one time concept).

**Loading protocol**: Digital impression at 8 weeks; crown delivery at ~10 weeks if abutment torque вүҘ35 N/cm. Some patients delayed due to COVID-19.

**ISQ measurement**: Osstell Beacon; average of perpendicular and parallel measurements at placement and 8-week visit. Protocol amended after first 2 patients to reduce measurement frequency (from weekly to 2 timepoints) to protect osseointegration.

**MBL measurement**: AutoCAD 2023 on standardized periapical radiographs (Rinn system). Calibrated to implant thread distance (0.8 mm). Blinded expert measured mesial and distal, mean of 2 readings. Subcrestal bone above implant shoulder coded as zero.

**Statistical analysis**: Paired t-test (intergroup), mixed ANOVA with Bonferroni correction (repeated measures MBL), Wilcoxon (categorical). Non-inferiority margin for primary outcome: 0.10 mm.

**Sample size**: 51 pairs needed (92.4% power at 5% significance); 62 planned with 20% dropout; 61 completed analysis.

**Population**: 70 screened вҶ’ 68 enrolled (FMPS <20%, вүҘ3 months healed ridge, no grafting within 6 months, KMW вүҘ2 mm). Mean age 54.9 years; 35.7% treated periodontitis history; ~15% current smokers.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Two centers with different operators; no inter-center examiner calibration
- Implant site types (single-tooth gaps vs. short edentulous spans) not systematically recorded вҖ” affects biomechanical loading interpretation
- COVID-19 disruptions caused delayed loading in 14/68 patients
- ISQ protocol was amended mid-study (weekly measurements abandoned after 2 patients)
- OHIP-14 cannot be linked to specific implant surfaces; may be influenced by treatment duration
- Follow-up limited to 12 months post-loading вҖ” longer-term data needed
- Subgroup analysis (maxilla/bone type) underpowered; exploratory only

## 7. Glossary

- **SLA**: Sandblasted, Large-grit, Acid-etched вҖ” Straumann implant surface, moderately rough, hydrophobic
- **SLActive / modified SLActive**: SLA stored in aqueous saline solution (hydrophilic, chemically active); modified version adds 55В°C post-production holding step
- **ISQ**: Implant Stability Quotient вҖ” RFA-based metric (0вҖ“100 scale) measuring implant stability; recorded with Osstell Beacon device
- **MBL**: Marginal Bone Level вҖ” distance from implant shoulder to first bone-to-implant contact on periapical radiograph
- **BIC**: Bone-to-Implant Contact вҖ” histomorphometric measure of osseointegration
- **PPD**: Probing Pocket Depth
- **BOP**: Bleeding on Probing
- **KMW**: Keratinized Mucosa Width
- **WHI**: Wound Healing Index (Landry scale 1-5)
- **OHIP-14**: Oral Health Impact Profile (14-item short form) вҖ” OHRQoL patient-reported outcome
- **Split-mouth design**: Each patient serves as their own control; test and control implants placed in separate quadrants of the same patient
- **Bone Level Tapered Roxolid**: Straumann implant with Roxolid alloy (Ti-Zr) in tapered configuration
- **Platform switching**: Abutment diameter smaller than implant platform diameter вҖ” associated with MBL preservation
- **One abutment-one time concept**: Abutment placed at surgery and not removed вҖ” avoids repeated abutment disconnection cycles that can increase MBL
