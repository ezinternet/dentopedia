## 3. Medical Consultation Indications
Required before invasive/physiologically stressful dental treatment when:
- Any medical problem that could result in complications (MI, CVA history, CHF, DM, COPD, severe asthma, seizures, hepatitis, CKD, adrenal insufficiency)
- Drug reactions possible (LA, vasoconstrictors, N2O, antibiotics, NSAIDs/opioids)
- Immunosuppression → infection/wound healing risk (HIV, leukemia, corticosteroids, transplant)
- Patient requires hospitalization or specialist co-management

## 4. Key Points by Category

### Vasoconstrictors (Section 23) — most referenced
- Epinephrine 1:100,000 generally safe in controlled CVD patients (limit 2 cartridges = 0.036 mg epi)
- Absolute contraindications: uncontrolled hyperthyroidism, pheochromocytoma
- Relative contraindications: unstable angina, recent MI <6 months, uncontrolled arrhythmia, uncontrolled HTN

### Anticoagulants (Section 3)
- Continue warfarin for routine extractions if INR ≤3.5
- DOACs: generally continue; timing of last dose matters for surgical procedures
- Local hemostatic measures: tranexamic acid, oxidized cellulose, sutures

### Bisphosphonates / MRONJ (Section 6)
- Oral BP <3 yrs, no risk factors: proceed normally
- Oral BP >3 yrs or IV BP: refer/consult; drug holiday consideration
- Staging: Stage 0–3; invasive procedures in Stage 2–3 = contraindicated without specialist co-management

### Diabetes (Section 12)
- HbA1c ≥9% = poorly controlled; delay elective surgery
- Hypoglycemia risk: morning appointments; patient must eat; glucose check
- Metformin: lactic acidosis risk if iodine contrast (dental CBCT with contrast)

### Hypertension (Section 15)
- BP >180/110: defer all elective treatment
- BP 160–179/100–109: defer elective; emergency with caution
- BP <160/100: proceed normally
