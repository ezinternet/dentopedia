## Why Ingested

Seeds the EF-Line / elastodontic (myofunctional-orthodontics) evidence base in the new `orthodontics/myofunctional` subcategory. This is the first scoping review to evaluate elastodontic-device outcomes specifically by **cephalometric** measurement on the **sagittal plane** in mixed-dentition Class II patients, so it anchors the quantitative ceiling (ANB, SNB, OVJ effect ranges) against which the batch siblings are read. It reinforces [[wiki/orthodontics/myofunctional/ronsivalle-2023-elastodontic-appliances-malocclusion-interception-children-review]] (interception-in-children review) and pairs with the AMCOP-protocol and overjet/overbite-reduction siblings — all establish that elastodontic appliances beat no-treatment but underperform rigid functional appliances (twin-block).

## One-line Summary

Scoping review (PRISMA-ScR; 16 studies, n=345 treated, mean age 8.48 y) finding elastodontic appliances produce statistically significant sagittal correction in mixed-dentition Class II — SNB ↑ (13 studies, +0.61° to +2.82°), ANB ↓ (11–12 studies, −0.38° to −2.42°), OVJ ↓ (10 studies, −2.1 to −4.58 mm) — effective vs no-treatment but weaker than twin-block.

## 한줄요약

스코핑 리뷰(PRISMA-ScR; 16편, 치료군 345명, 평균 8.48세)로, 혼합치열기 2급 부정교합에서 탄성교정장치(elastodontic appliance)가 통계적으로 유의한 시상면 개선(SNB ↑ 13편 +0.61°~+2.82°, ANB ↓ 11~12편 −0.38°~−2.42°, OVJ ↓ 10편 −2.1~−4.58 mm)을 보였으나, 무치료 대비 효과적이되 트윈블록보다는 약함.

## 2. Key Contributions

- **First scoping review to assess elastodontic devices by cephalometric (teleradiographic) outcomes** on the sagittal plane in growing mixed-dentition subjects; secondary aim compares them to conventional functional appliances and to untreated controls.
- Consolidates **16 studies** (search 13 May 2024 across Scopus, PubMed, Embase, Web of Science) with quantitative effect ranges for the key sagittal cephalometric variables.
- Establishes the field consensus: **elastodontic appliances (EAs) > no treatment**, but **EAs < rigid functional appliances (twin-block)** for skeletal/dental/soft-tissue change — though EAs are simpler, less invasive, and cost-effective.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: PRISMA-ScR scoping review (not registered).
- **PICO**: Population = growing humans in mixed dentition; Intervention = elastodontic-device treatment; Comparison = pre/post vs other functional appliances or untreated; Outcome = dental and/or skeletal change via teleradiographic (cephalometric) data.
- **Research question**: "What is the dento-skeletal effect of elastodontic devices on the sagittal plane in mixed-dentition patients?"
- **Screening**: 1662 records → 726 after de-duplication (Zotero + manual) → 83 full-text → **16 included**. Two reviewers (AV, CSF) screened; third (RU) resolved disagreements.
- **Data extracted**: author/year, country, design, sample (sex/age), sample-size calculation, malocclusion type, device + wear instructions, control type, compliance, follow-up, cephalometric measurements.
- No publication-year or language restriction. **No methodological-quality appraisal was performed** (acknowledged limitation).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Corpus** (16 studies):
- Countries: Italy 8, China 3, Turkey 3, India 1, Egypt 1.
- Years: 2021 (6), 2022 (5), 2023 (3), 2004 (1).
- Design: **12 retrospective, 4 prospective**; 14 had a control group (Ciftci and Inchingolo had none).
- Sample: **345 treated patients** (142 M, 148 F, 55 unreported); mean age **8.48 y** (reported in 15 studies); sample-size calculation in 6 studies.
- Malocclusion: **15 studies Class II** (6 of these Class II Div 1; 2 also included Class I); **1 study Class III** (Ronsivalle).
- Control type: untreated in 6, another appliance in 6, both in 2.
- Follow-up: 6 months to ~3 years.

**Skeletal cephalometric outcomes (Class II):**
- **SNB ↑** significant in **13 studies** (range **+0.61° to +2.82°**); decreased in the 1 Class III study; 1 study did not analyze, 1 non-significant.
- **ANB ↓** (skeletal class improvement) in **12 studies** (Discussion cites 11 with significant decrease, range **−0.38° to −2.42°**, indicating mandibular advancement / forward repositioning); 2 studies non-significant.
- **SNA ↓** (maxillary protrusion reduction) significant in only **2 studies**.
- **Mandibular length ↑** documented in **8 studies** (low heterogeneity); not evaluated in 7; 1 non-significant.
- **Mandibular growth direction (SN-Go-Gn) ↓ divergence** in **3 studies**; non-significant in 7.
- **FMA (facial divergence)**: largely unchanged, no significant improvement in 6 studies; not mentioned in 10.

**Dentoalveolar outcomes:**
- **OVJ ↓** significant in **10 studies** (range **−2.1 mm to −4.58 mm**); 1 study reported OVJ *increase* in Class III subjects.
- **OVB ↓** significant in **4 studies**.

**Head-to-head (EA vs other functional appliances):**
- Coban and Madian: **no significant difference** between myobrace and twin-block.
- Johnson: **twin-block superior** (Ar-Gn, OVB, SN-Go-Gn) vs myobrace.
- Galluccio: greater OVJ/OVB reduction with **twin-block vs PMA Occlus-o-Guide**.
- Yang: greater SNB increase with myofunctional preformed appliance vs conventional early appliance.
- Zhang: **T4K** better than Hyrax (SNA, SNB, Go-Me).

**EA vs untreated controls (selected p-values):**
- Ciavarella: Co-Gn mandibular-size increase, PMA AMCOP vs control, p=0.0173 — but Patano (same device) found no Co-Me change (p=0.102).
- Galluccio: Ar-Pg ↑ (p=0.004), SNA ↓ (p=0.017) for PMA Occlus-o-Guide vs control.
- Johnson: Go-Me (p=0.005), Ar-Gn (p<0.001), SNB (p=0.017) significant for myobrace vs control.
- OVJ and OVB reductions significantly greater with EAs than controls in several studies.

**Bottom line**: EAs give significant ANB/SNB/OVJ improvement (mandibular advancement / forward repositioning) in mild-to-moderate sagittal Class II mixed dentition; results are variable and outcome-dependent on compliance (typical regimen 1–4 h/day plus overnight).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **No methodological-quality appraisal** of included studies.
- Material chemistry/physics and structural differences between elastodontic-appliance types not detailed.
- Class III evidence is a single study — insufficient to draw conclusions.
- **Predominantly retrospective** (12/16); heterogeneous control groups (some functional appliances, some untreated).
- Compliance poorly documented despite being a direct determinant of outcome.
- Calls for more **prospective, randomized** trials vs both untreated and established-functional-appliance controls.
- Advantages of EAs over traditional functional appliances remain unclear.

## 6. Related Work

- Ronsivalle et al. 2023 — elastodontic appliances for malocclusion interception in children (review); the only included Class III study is also by this group.
- Inchingolo (AMCOP) and Lanteri (eruption guide appliance / EGA) — AMCOP and customized-vs-preformed elastodontic protocols.
- Ortu et al. 2021 — elastodontic overjet/overbite reduction (batch sibling).
- Twin-block / Fränkel-2 / myobrace literature as the rigid-functional-appliance comparators.

## 7. Glossary

- **Elastodontic appliance (EA / elastomer)**: preformed or custom removable orthodontic device of biomedical silicone/elastic material; activated by patient muscle function (light biological elastic forces) to correct malocclusion in growing patients.
- **EF (Education Fonctionnelle)** / **EF-Line**: a commercial family of elastodontic/myofunctional appliances.
- **AMCOP**: Armonizzatori Multifunzionali Cranio-Occluso Posturali (a PMA elastodontic-device family).
- **EGA**: eruption guide appliance.
- **T4K / Trainer / myobrace**: prefabricated myofunctional appliances.
- **SNB**: Sella–Nasion–Point B angle (mandibular anteroposterior position).
- **SNA**: Sella–Nasion–Point A angle (maxillary anteroposterior position).
- **ANB**: Point A–Nasion–Point B angle (skeletal sagittal jaw relationship).
- **OVJ**: overjet · **OVB**: overbite.
- **SN-Go-Gn / FMA**: mandibular-plane divergence angles (vertical pattern).
- **PRISMA-ScR**: PRISMA extension for Scoping Reviews.
- **Twin-block (TB) / FR-2 (Fränkel-2)**: rigid removable functional appliances used as comparators.
