## One-line Summary

Retrospective cohort (n=728, single Beijing center) — perioperative hypertension occurred in 26.77% of implant + bone-augmentation surgeries; male (RR 1.74), age ≥60 (RR 2.35), and surgical time >60 min (RR 1.65) were risk factors, but their effect varied by augmentation approach (significant in GBR, attenuated in lateral-window sinus lift).

## 한줄요약

후향적 코호트(n=728, 단일 베이징 센터) — 임플란트 동기 골증대술의 26.77%에서 围术期 고혈압 발생; 남성(RR 1.74)·60세 이상(RR 2.35)·수술시간 60분 초과(RR 1.65)가 위험인자였으나, 그 효과는 골증대 술식에 따라 달라짐(GBR에서 유의, 상악동 외측창 거상술에서는 약화).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies the **incidence of perioperative hypertension (26.77%)** in outpatient implant placement performed simultaneously with bone augmentation — a procedure class long enough and stimulating enough to provoke intraoperative blood-pressure surges.
- Identifies three patient/procedure risk factors with relative risks: **male sex (RR 1.74), age ≥60 years (RR 2.35), surgical time >60 min (RR 1.65)**.
- Shows the bone-augmentation **approach modifies which factors matter**: the overall incidence does not differ across GBR vs. transalveolar vs. lateral-window sinus lift, but the active risk factors do.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Single-center retrospective cohort, Peking University School and Hospital of Stomatology, Sept 2021–Aug 2022.
- **Sample**: 728 patients undergoing dental implant placement with simultaneous bone augmentation, meeting inclusion/exclusion criteria.
- **Exposure factors analyzed**: gender, age, surgical time, surgical (bone-augmentation) approach — guided bone regeneration (GBR), transalveolar (transcrestal) maxillary sinus floor elevation, lateral-window maxillary sinus floor elevation.
- **Outcome**: perioperative hypertension, derived from blood-pressure variability (systolic/diastolic/mean arterial pressure variability) across the procedure.
- **Analysis**: correlation of perioperative hypertension with exposure factors; relative risks with 95% CI; subgroup analysis by augmentation approach.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Blood-pressure variability** (mean ± SD, max):
  - Systolic: 9.93% ± 6.63% (max 50.41%)
  - Diastolic: 12.45% ± 8.79% (max 68.75%)
  - Mean arterial pressure: 10.02% ± 6.61% (max 49.48%)
- **Overall perioperative hypertension incidence: 26.77%.**
- **Risk factors (whole cohort, P < 0.05):**
  | Factor | RR (95% CI) |
  |---|---|
  | Male sex | 1.74 (1.21–2.50) |
  | Age ≥60 years | 2.35 (1.54–3.58) |
  | Surgical time >60 min | 1.65 (1.15–2.38) |
- **By augmentation approach** (incidence not significantly different across the three, P > 0.05; but risk-factor profile differs):
  - **GBR**: risk factors = male, age ≥60 y, surgical time >60 min (all P < 0.05).
  - **Transalveolar (transcrestal) sinus lift**: risk factor = age ≥60 y only (P < 0.05).
  - **Lateral-window sinus lift**: male, age ≥60 y, surgical time >60 min were NOT risk factors (P > 0.05).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-center, retrospective design — selection and documentation bias; findings reflect one Chinese tertiary stomatology center's patient mix and protocols.
- **Abstract-only ingestion**: PMC full body text was not retrievable (article is in Chinese). Definitions of "perioperative hypertension" threshold, anesthesia/sedation protocol, baseline BP/comorbidity adjustment, and confounder handling are not captured here — confirm against the full Chinese text before clinical reliance.
- No reported adjustment for baseline antihypertensive medication, anxiety, or sedation depth; relative risks are crude/stratified rather than fully multivariable in the abstract.
- Approach-specific null findings (lateral-window) may reflect smaller subgroup sizes / power rather than true absence of effect.

## 7. Glossary

- **Perioperative hypertension (围术期高血压)**: clinically significant blood-pressure elevation occurring during the surgical period.
- **Blood-pressure variability**: percentage fluctuation of SBP/DBP/MAP across the procedure relative to baseline.
- **GBR (Guided Bone Regeneration)**: barrier-membrane bone augmentation for ridge defects.
- **Transalveolar (transcrestal) sinus floor elevation**: osteotome/internal approach to lift the sinus membrane.
- **Lateral-window sinus floor elevation**: lateral antrostomy (external) approach to sinus augmentation.
- **RR (Relative Risk)**: ratio of outcome probability in exposed vs. unexposed groups.
