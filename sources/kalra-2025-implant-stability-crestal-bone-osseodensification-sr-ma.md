## One-line Summary
SR+MA in J Prosthet Dent 2025;133(6):1462.e1–1462.e7 — 5 studies, 109 patients, 198 implants in low-density bone: OD significantly raised ISQ at both baseline and follow-up (P<.05), but crestal bone level (CBL) changes did NOT differ significantly between OD and conventional drilling (P>.05) at either timepoint.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: PRISMA systematic review + meta-analysis
- **Journal**: Journal of Prosthetic Dentistry 2025;133(6):1462.e1–1462.e7
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.prosdent.2024.07.011
- **Authors**: Janvi Kalra, Pankaj Dhawan, Neha Jain
- **Search**: SCOPUS, PubMed, Google Scholar + manual search; January 2013 – January 2024
- **Quality tool**: Joanna Briggs Institute (JBI) tools for risk-of-bias

- **Primary Question**: In low-density bone, how do OD osteotomy and conventional osteotomy compare on implant stability (ISQ) and crestal bone level (CBL)?

## 2. Key Contributions
- Most recent (2025) human-focused OD SR+MA in low-density bone — directly relevant to the soft-bone indication that OD claims to address.
- **Implant stability (ISQ)**: OD significantly higher than conventional at both baseline and follow-up (P < .05) — replicates Gaspar 2022 and Lima Monteiro 2024 findings.
- **Crestal bone level (CBL) — null finding**: no significant difference in CBL changes between OD and conventional drilling at baseline or follow-up (P > .05). This is the new contribution: OD's mechanical advantage in primary/secondary stability does *not* translate into measurable crestal bone protection.
- Authors conclude OD is advantageous for primary and secondary stability and for bone expansion in low-density bone, but NOT for preventing crestal bone loss.
- α = .05 throughout; forest plots generated.
- Five studies / 109 patients / 198 implants — modest evidence base.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
PRISMA-guided systematic review of SCOPUS, PubMed, and Google Scholar (plus manual searching) for articles published January 2013 – January 2024. Eligibility: studies comparing OD with conventional osteotomy in low-density bone, reporting ISQ and/or CBL. Risk of bias assessed with the Joanna Briggs Institute (JBI) tools. Forest plots generated; significance threshold α = .05.

Five studies met inclusion, yielding pooled data from 109 patients and 198 implants. The studies focused on low-density bone sites (e.g., posterior maxilla, type D3–D4 bone) where the OD claim is most clinically relevant.

Two outcomes were analysed:
1. **ISQ** (RFA-derived implant stability) at baseline and follow-up.
2. **CBL** (crestal bone level changes, typically measured radiographically) at baseline and follow-up.

The differentiating feature of this review is the explicit examination of CBL — a crucial long-term outcome that prior OD SR+MAs had not pooled.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **n**: 5 studies / 109 patients / 198 implants
- **ISQ at baseline**: significantly higher with OD (P < .05) — direction and significance consistent with Gaspar 2022 and Lima Monteiro 2024
- **ISQ at follow-up**: significantly higher with OD (P < .05)
- **CBL at baseline**: no significant difference (P > .05)
- **CBL at follow-up**: no significant difference (P > .05)
- **Bone expansion**: authors note OD also enables bone expansion in low-density bone (qualitative claim from included studies)
- Specific MD, 95% CI, and I² values not extractable from the front/back excerpt; full forest plots in the PDF body.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Modest sample size: 5 studies, 109 patients, 198 implants — limited statistical power for the CBL null finding (absence of evidence ≠ evidence of absence).
- Follow-up durations heterogeneous; long-term (≥ 5 year) CBL data sparse.
- Bone-density stratification within "low-density" not granular; D3 vs D4 effects pooled.
- Specific RoB scores per study, I² heterogeneity, and 95% CIs not extractable from the available text excerpt.
- The CBL null finding could be: (a) genuine — primary-stability gain doesn't translate to crestal bone protection; (b) underpowered — small n masking a real effect; (c) follow-up too short. Authors do not resolve this.
- Could not assess implant survival or complications as primary outcomes.

## 7. Glossary
- **CBL (Crestal Bone Level / Crestal Bone Loss)**: Radiographic measurement of vertical distance from implant platform to first bone-to-implant contact; standard long-term implant health metric.
- **ISQ**: Implant Stability Quotient (RFA-derived, 1–100).
- **JBI (Joanna Briggs Institute)**: Provider of structured RoB tools commonly used for cohort and quasi-experimental study appraisal.
- **Low-density bone**: Typically D3 or D4 in the Misch classification; the principal indication for OD.
- **OD (Osseodensification)**: Counterclockwise Densah bur compaction drilling.
- **Bone expansion**: Lateral widening of the alveolar ridge achievable with compaction drilling in narrow ridges.
