## Why Ingested

Part of a focused collection on suture techniques & primary wound closure (this batch = suture biomechanics / tissue adhesive / gingival graft suturing). This is the clinical RCT anchor of the batch — it tests whether suture technique (conventional vs modified sling) and graft technique (gingival unit transfer vs conventional graft) affect free gingival graft (FGG) shrinkage and keratinized-tissue gain in patients. It contextualizes the ex-vivo flap-stabilization findings of [[suture-wound-closure/pabst-2024-cyanoacrylate-tissue-adhesive-coronally-advanced-flap]] with a real-patient soft-tissue-graft outcome.

## One-line Summary

Three-month parallel-design RCT (60 enrolled, 52 completed; 4 groups of n=13): in free gingival graft surgery, graft technique mattered more than suture technique — gingival unit transfer (GUT) showed less graft shrinkage than conventional graft (CG) regardless of suture, while the modified sling suture (MSS) gave no advantage and GUT+conventional-suture had the largest keratinized-tissue-width gain.

## 한줄요약

3개월 평행설계 RCT (60명 등록, 52명 완료; 4군 각 n=13): 유리치은이식술(FGG)에서 봉합법보다 이식편 기법이 더 중요했음 — 치은단위이식(GUT)이 봉합법과 무관하게 통상이식(CG)보다 이식편 수축이 적었고, 변형 슬링봉합(MSS)은 이점이 없었으며 GUT+통상봉합이 각화조직폭 증가가 가장 컸음.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Tests a novel **modified sling suture (MSS)** — a sling-suture variant incorporating the lingual papilla, hypothesized to be less invasive and reduce graft shrinkage — against conventional suture (CS) in FGG.
- Factorial comparison crossing suture technique (MSS vs CS) with graft technique (gingival unit transfer GUT vs conventional graft CG).
- Finds that **graft technique (GUT) drives reduced shrinkage**, not suture technique — null hypothesis on MSS effectively retained.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Patients:** 60 systemically healthy patients with Cairo RT2/RT3 mandibular-anterior recessions, <1 mm attached gingiva, PI/GI <1; 52 completed (5 missed 1-month, 3 missed 3-month controls).
- **Four groups (n=13 each):** (a) GUT+MSS, (b) GUT+CS, (c) CG+MSS, (d) CG+CS.
- **Two-stage randomization by coin toss:** first suture technique (MSS/CS), then graft technique (GUT/CG).
- **Surgery:** recipient bed by horizontal incision along MGJ + two vertical releasing incisions, deepithelialized; donor graft (1-1.5 mm thick) harvested from maxillary premolar palate. GUT includes marginal + interdental gingiva (surface-to-surface contact for blood supply); CG is the conventional palatal graft. All surgery by one periodontist (S.S.).
- **Outcomes:** primary = dimensional change (Δ) of graft surface area (GSA, ImageJ photogrammetry, calibrated to 5 mm probe reference); secondary = keratinized tissue width (KTW), relative gingival recession height (rGRH), relative vestibule depth (rVD), measured via acrylic stent reference points + UNC 15 probe + digital caliper at baseline, 1 and 3 months. Graft shrinkage% = [(preop area - postop area)/preop area]×100.
- **Statistics:** SPSS 22; normality by Kolmogorov-Smirnov/Shapiro-Wilk; One-Way ANOVA + Tukey HSD or Kruskal-Wallis + Dunn; repeated-measures ANOVA + Bonferroni intra-group; Fisher-Freeman-Halton for qualitative; Spearman correlation. Power analysis: 13/group (diff in proportions 0.44 for KTW, 95% CI, 80% power).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **KTW gain:** increase at 1 and 3 months was significantly higher in **GUT+CS than GUT+MSS** (p<0.05); no significant KTW difference among other groups.
- **rGRH:** decreased over time in groups; intragroup change significant only in GUT+MSS (p<0.05); between-group differences not significant (p>0.05).
- **rVD:** no significant between-group differences (p>0.05).
- **Graft surface area (GSA) / shrinkage:** GSA decreased in all groups vs baseline (p<0.05). The **CG+MSS group had the greatest dimensional change and graft shrinkage** at all time points vs GUT+MSS and GUT+CS (p<0.05); no significant difference among the other groups.
- No significant correlation between graft dimensional change and baseline clinical measurements (p>0.05).
- **Conclusion:** keratinized tissue can be successfully obtained with both GUT and CG; **regardless of suture technique, GUT showed less graft shrinkage than CG.** The MSS did not deliver the hypothesized shrinkage benefit.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Short follow-up (3 months) — does not capture longer-term stability/remodeling.
- Single operator and single center; modest per-group n (13).
- Retrospective trial registration (after enrollment start).
- Reverse of MSS hypothesis: MSS did not reduce shrinkage; in CG it was associated with the greatest shrinkage.

## 7. Glossary

- **Free gingival graft (FGG):** autogenous epithelialized graft to augment keratinized tissue.
- **Gingival unit transfer (GUT):** FGG variant including marginal + interdental gingiva for surface-to-surface contact and better revascularization (Allen & Cohen).
- **Conventional graft (CG):** standard FGG from palatal donor without the marginal-gingiva unit.
- **Modified sling suture (MSS):** sling-suture variant incorporating the lingual papilla, hypothesized to be less invasive.
- **Conventional suture (CS):** standard suture comparator.
- **Graft surface area (GSA) / shrinkage:** ImageJ-measured graft area and % reduction from baseline.
- **Cairo RT2/RT3:** recession types with interproximal attachment loss (RT2 ≤ buccal, RT3 > buccal).
