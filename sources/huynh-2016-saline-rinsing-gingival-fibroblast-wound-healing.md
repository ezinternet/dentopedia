## Why Ingested

Saline rinsing is widely advised in post-surgical and periodontal wound care but lacked in-vitro mechanistic evidence. This paper fills that gap — providing the first controlled data on NaCl concentration–response in human gingival fibroblasts — and directly informs clinical recommendations on rinse concentration in [[wiki/periodontics/herrera-2014-acute-periodontal-lesions]] and post-periodontal-surgery wound care contexts.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro study (Chulalongkorn, 2016) showing short-term rinsing with 0.9–1.8% NaCl significantly promotes human gingival fibroblast migration and ECM gene expression, with 1.8% being optimal, while having no effect on oral keratinocytes; Cl⁻ ion identified as the active mediator.

## 한줄요약

In vitro 연구 (쭐라롱콘대, 2016): 단기간 0.9–1.8% NaCl 세척이 인간 치은 섬유아세포(hGF)의 이동 및 세포외기질(ECM) 유전자 발현을 유의하게 촉진 (1.8%가 최적)하며, 구강 각화세포(hNOK)에는 효과 없음; Cl⁻ 이온이 핵심 매개체로 확인됨.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First controlled NaCl dose–response data for hGFs**: Demonstrated a bell-shaped response — 0.9% and 1.8% NaCl enhance migration; 7.2% inhibits it; 3.6% intermediate stimulation in transwell but not scratch.
2. **ECM gene upregulation confirmed**: 1.8% NaCl produced >3-fold induction of type-I collagen (COL1) mRNA and significant upregulation of fibronectin (Fn) and focal adhesion kinase (FAK) mRNA.
3. **Cl⁻ ion identified as active mediator**: KCl replicated the NaCl effect; NaH₂PO₄ and KH₂PO₄ did not — implicating the chloride ion, not sodium or osmolality alone, as the functional driver.
4. **Cell-type specificity**: Effect is fibroblast-specific; human normal oral keratinocytes (hNOKs) were unaffected at all concentrations tested.
5. **Migration, not proliferation**: MTT assays confirmed NaCl rinsing does not alter hGF viability or proliferation — wound closure acceleration is purely migration-driven.
6. **Cytoskeletal reorganization**: Immunofluorescence showed increased FAK clustering and enhanced F-actin stress fibers after 1.8% NaCl, consistent with enhanced focal adhesion dynamics.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Cell models**:
- Primary hGFs: isolated from healthy third-molar extraction sites (n = 18–25 yr-old volunteers), passages 3–6
- hNOKs: commercial keratinocyte-serum-free medium cultures; used as negative-control cell type

**Rinsing protocol**: 2-minute rinse in NaCl-supplemented culture medium, 3×/day (every 5–6 h) for 48 h (scratch assay) or 24 h (transwell/MTT)

**NaCl concentrations tested**: 0, 0.9, 1.8, 3.6, 7.2% (w/v)

**Comparator salts** (equimolar to NaCl 0.121 M Na⁺ / 0.187 M Cl⁻):
- 2.9% KCl (chloride source without Na⁺)
- 10% NaH₂PO₄ (sodium source without Cl⁻)
- 9.7% KH₂PO₄ (neither Na⁺ nor Cl⁻)

**Assays**:
- Scratch (wound-healing) assay: 24-well plates, ImageJ area analysis at 0, 24, 48 h
- Transwell migration assay: 8-μm pore polycarbonate membrane, crystal-violet staining, cell count + area by ImageJ
- MTT viability/proliferation assay: 570 nm optical density
- Semi-quantitative RT-PCR: COL1, Fn, FAK, GAPDH; 3–4 independent experiments
- Immunofluorescence: anti-FAK (Alexa Fluor 488), rhodamine-phalloidin (F-actin), DAPI (nuclei); Axio Observer.Z1 fluorescence microscope

**Statistics**: One-way ANOVA with Dunnett T3 (unequal variances) or Tukey HSD post-hoc; independent-samples t-test for RT-PCR; α = 0.05; SPSS v.21

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Endpoint | Control (0% NaCl) | 0.9% NaCl | 1.8% NaCl | 3.6% NaCl | 7.2% NaCl |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| hGF scratch closure (48 h) | baseline | ↑* | ↑** (strongest) | NR | ↓** (inhibited) |
| hGF transwell migration | baseline | ↑* | ↑** | ↑* | NS |
| hGF proliferation (MTT) | baseline | NS | NS | NS | NS |
| hNOK scratch/migration | baseline | NS | NS | NS | NS |
| COL1 mRNA (vs. 0%) | 1× | — | >3× ↑* | — | — |
| Fn mRNA | 1× | — | slight ↑* | — | — |
| FAK mRNA | 1× | — | marked ↑* | — | — |

**Ion specificity (vs. 1.8% NaCl)**:
- KCl: replicates migration ↑ → Cl⁻ is the active ion
- NaH₂PO₄ / KH₂PO₄: no effect → Na⁺ and PO₄ not responsible

**FAK/F-actin**: FAK redistributed from diffuse cytoplasmic to peri-nuclear focal clusters; F-actin stress fibers markedly increased after 1.8% NaCl — consistent with enhanced focal adhesion formation and lamellipodia activity.

**Non-findings**: No significant changes in TGF-β, MMP1, MMP2 mRNA — saline does not appear to trigger major proteolytic remodeling or pro-fibrotic TGF-β signaling.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **In vitro only**: Monolayer cell culture cannot replicate the complexity of in vivo wound healing (vascularization, immune cells, 3D architecture, fluid dynamics of rinsing).
- **Short exposure window**: 48 h — longer-term effects (scar formation, tissue remodeling) not examined.
- **Primary cells variability**: Donor-to-donor heterogeneity in hGF behavior; small number of donors not stated precisely.
- **Osmolality not independently controlled**: The study attributes the effect to Cl⁻ ion but does not completely disentangle osmolarity contributions from ion specificity (hyperosmolarity per se could have partial effect).
- **No in vivo or clinical validation**: Authors note this as the logical next step.
- **Single rinse duration tested (2 min)**: Clinical rinses may vary from 30 s to several minutes.

## 7. Glossary

- **hGF (Human Gingival Fibroblast)**: Primary mesenchymal cells of the gingival connective tissue; key effectors of periodontal wound healing.
- **hNOK (Human Normal Oral Keratinocyte)**: Epithelial cells lining the oral mucosa; used here as a cell-type specificity control.
- **FAK (Focal Adhesion Kinase)**: Non-receptor tyrosine kinase that mediates focal adhesion assembly/disassembly, central to cell migration signaling.
- **F-actin**: Filamentous actin; cytoskeletal polymer forming stress fibers and lamellipodia essential for directional cell migration.
- **COL1 (Type-I Collagen)**: The principal structural protein of the periodontal ECM; its upregulation indicates active wound-healing matrix deposition.
- **Fibronectin (Fn)**: Extracellular matrix glycoprotein that mediates cell adhesion and migration via integrin binding.
- **Scratch assay**: In vitro wound-healing assay in which a linear gap is made in a confluent cell monolayer and closure rate is measured.
- **Transwell migration assay**: Quantitative chemotaxis/haptotaxis assay using a porous membrane; measures directed cell migration independent of proliferation.
- **MTT assay**: Colorimetric cell-viability/metabolic-activity assay based on formazan production.
- **Hyperosmolarity**: Osmolality exceeding 290 mOsmol/kg; 0.9% NaCl ≈ isotonic (308 mOsmol/kg), 1.8% ≈ mildly hypertonic.
