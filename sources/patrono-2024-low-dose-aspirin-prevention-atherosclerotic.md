## Why Ingested

[[wiki/drug/catella-lawson-2001-cyclooxygenase-inhibitors-aspirin-nejm]]는 NSAID-aspirin 약력학 상호작용을 다룬 2001년 근거인데, 저용량 aspirin의 메커니즘·효능·출혈 위험에 대한 30년치 종합이 필요했다. 본 2024 State-of-the-Art Review는 치과 발치·임플란트 수술 전 항혈소판 관리 의사결정에 적용(applies-to)할 최신 통합 근거를 제공한다 (저용량 aspirin 중단 여부 판단의 근거).

## One-line Summary

State-of-the-art narrative review of 30 years of low-dose aspirin (75–100 mg/day) evidence: irreversible platelet COX-1 acetylation suppresses thromboxane A2, reducing atherothrombosis (e.g., RR 0.52, NNT 10 in one secondary-prevention dataset) while increasing GI bleeding and lowering colorectal adenoma recurrence.

## 한줄요약

저용량 aspirin(75–100 mg/일)에 관한 30년 근거를 통합한 State-of-the-Art 종설로, 혈소판 COX-1의 비가역적 아세틸화가 thromboxane A2를 억제해 죽상혈전증을 줄이고(일부 2차예방 데이터 상대위험비 0.52, NNT 10) 위장관 출혈을 늘리며 대장 선종 재발을 낮춘다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Synthesises 30 years of antiplatelet evidence revisiting aspirin's role across the cardiovascular risk continuum.
- Reframes the efficacy-vs-safety shift, the rise of aspirin-free regimens after PCI, and P2Y12-inhibitor monotherapy comparisons.
- Consolidates the chemopreventive (colorectal cancer) evidence alongside the cardiovascular and bleeding profile.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Expert narrative review (no systematic search/PRISMA). Draws on IPD overviews/meta-analyses (Antiplatelet Trialists' Collaboration), RCTs, and pharmacology literature.
- Structured as mechanism → pharmacokinetics → pharmacodynamics → clinical efficacy and safety.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Mechanism: low-dose aspirin selectively acetylates Ser-529 of platelet COX-1, permanently blocking the COX channel → near-complete suppression of TXA2 biosynthesis with low daily dosing.
- Potency: ID50 to inhibit platelet COX-1 shifts from 26 mg (single dose) to ~3 mg (repeated daily dosing) — an eight-fold cumulative-dosing shift.
- Pharmacokinetics: ~50% oral bioavailability, 15–20 min half-life; COX-2 in nucleated cells resynthesised within hours, sparing endothelial/renal COX-2 at low doses.
- Efficacy (graphical-abstract dataset): aspirin 75 mg vs placebo RR=0.52, p=0.0001, NNT=10.
- Trade-offs: reduced atherothrombosis risk; increased GI bleeding from pre-existing mucosal lesions; decreased sporadic colorectal adenoma recurrence.
- Historical baseline: medium-dose (75–325 mg) aspirin not bettered by higher doses or other regimens for vascular-event prevention (Antiplatelet Trialists' Collaboration, 145 trials).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative review — no pooled effect estimate, susceptible to selection emphasis.
- Primary-prevention benefit narrower / more bleeding-sensitive than secondary prevention.
- Optimal perioperative continuation/interruption not the review's primary focus; clinical extrapolation needed.

## 7. Glossary

- **COX-1 / COX-2**: cyclooxygenase isozymes; low-dose aspirin selectively/irreversibly inhibits platelet COX-1.
- **TXA2 (Thromboxane A2)**: platelet agonist/vasoconstrictor; its suppression underlies aspirin's antithrombotic effect.
- **ID50**: dose inhibiting platelet COX-1 by 50% (26 mg single → ~3 mg repeated).
- **NNT**: number needed to treat.
- **RR**: rate ratio. **DAPT**: dual antiplatelet therapy. **PCI**: percutaneous coronary intervention.
- **P2Y12 inhibitors**: clopidogrel, prasugrel, ticagrelor (ADP-receptor antiplatelets).
