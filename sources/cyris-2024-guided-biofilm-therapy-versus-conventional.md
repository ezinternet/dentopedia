## Why Ingested

기존 [[periodontics/jo-2008-management-of-dental-biofilm-through]] 및 [[periodontics/smiley-2015-nonsurgical-periodontitis-srp-guideline]]의 SRP 중심 NSPT 근거에 더해, 저연마 분말 에어폴리싱 기반 GBT 프로토콜이 전통적 기구 조합과 임상적으로 동등한지를 직접 비교한 RCT가 필요했다. 본 연구(Cyris 2024)는 stage III/IV 환자에서 두 프로토콜의 포켓 폐쇄·PPD 감소가 동등하고 GBT가 시술시간만 단축됨을 보여, NSPT 기구 선택의 근거를 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

Split-mouth RCT (n=60 stage III/IV periodontitis patients, treated by supervised dental students): Guided Biofilm Therapy (erythritol air-polishing + ultrasonic) and conventional SRP (hand curettes/sonic + rotary polishing) gave equivalent PPD reduction and pocket closure in NSPT, with GBT significantly faster (30.3 vs 34.6 min/quadrant, p<0.001).

## 한줄요약

분악 RCT (60명, stage III/IV 치주염, 감독하 치대생이 시술): 비외과적 치주치료에서 가이드 바이오필름 치료(GBT, 에리스리톨 에어폴리싱+초음파)와 전통적 SRP(수기 큐렛/소닉+회전연마)의 PPD 감소·포켓 폐쇄 효과는 동등했고, GBT가 사분악당 시술시간만 유의하게 짧았다(30.3 vs 34.6분, p<0.001).

## 2. Key Contributions

- First RCT to compare Guided Biofilm Therapy (GBT) vs conventional instrumentation specifically in **non-surgical periodontal therapy (NSPT)** for stage III/IV periodontitis (most prior GBT evidence is from supportive periodontal therapy / gingivitis).
- Performed by **inexperienced practitioners** (3rd-clinical-semester dental students, n=100) under supervision, addressing the question of a learning curve / operator-experience dependence.
- Demonstrates clinical equivalence of the two protocols on the primary endpoint (pocket closure) while quantifying a treatment-time advantage for GBT.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Split-mouth RCT; contralateral quadrant pairs (I/III vs II/IV) randomly assigned to GBT (test) or conventional SRP (control) via random.org, allocation kept in sealed envelopes.
- **GBT protocol**: plaque staining → supragingival air-polishing with erythritol → ultrasonic scaler (PERIO SLIM PIEZON, EMS) on PPD ≥ 4 mm sites → supra/subgingival erythritol air-polishing (AIRFLOW Prophylaxis Master, EMS PLUS powder), no nozzle handpiece to avoid emphysema.
- **Conventional protocol**: plaque staining → sonic scaler (Proxeo W&H) + Gracey curettes (5/6, 7/8, 11/12, 13/14) on PPD ≥ 4 mm sites → rubber-cup rotary polishing with abrasive paste (RDA 170) + interdental devices (EVA tip).
- **Outcomes**: PPD, gingival recession (GR), CAL (calculated), BOP, plaque control record (O'Leary PCR), treatment time per quadrant, VAS pain. Recorded at 6 sites/tooth with UNC-PCP15 probe.
- **Timepoints**: T0 baseline, T1 reevaluation 5 ± 2 months (mean 4.47 months) after NSPT. No professional intervention between final instrumentation and T1.
- **Primary endpoint**: % of tooth surfaces achieving closed pocket (PPD ≤ 4 mm without BOP).
- **Examiners**: two calibrated, blinded examiners (C.S., C.G.); ± 1 mm acceptable deviation; statistician blinded.
- **Statistics**: Multilevel analysis (tooth-surface, tooth, patient level). Non-normal data → Wilcoxon (within-group), Mann–Whitney U (between-group), p<0.05. Power calc based on Geisinger 2007; 15 patients sufficient, fourfold sample (60) used → deliberate overpower.
- **Sample**: 171 screened → 77 selected → 17 dropouts → **60 analyzed** (stage III=36/IV=24; grade A/B/C = 1/31/28; 16 smokers; 3 diabetics; mean age 46.3 y).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **PPD reduction (all diseased surfaces)**: 57.0% GBT vs 58.7% control (p=0.067, NS). PDD-difference T1-T0 = -0.9 mm both groups.
- **Target endpoint (PPD ≤ 4 mm without BOP)**: 11.5% GBT vs 11.2% control (p=0.714, NS).
- **Pocket closure context**: at T1, PPD ≤ 4 mm achieved at 91.1% GBT / 90.9% control sites; PPD ≤ 5 mm at 95.1% / 95.0% — comparing favorably vs literature (Wennström 66–77%; Suvan SR mean 74%).
- **BOP at T1**: 15.9% GBT vs 14.3% control — small but statistically significant difference (p=0.037); however BOP *change* T0→T1 not significantly different (p=0.621).
- **Treatment time per quadrant**: 30.3 (28.3) min GBT vs 34.6 (24.5) min control — significantly shorter for GBT (p<0.001).
- **Pain (VAS)**: during treatment 2.4 GBT vs 2.5 control (NS); post-op ~0.1 both (NS).
- **CAL, GR, tooth mobility, tooth-type distribution**: no significant between-group differences at T0 or T1.
- **Bottom line**: Both protocols produced significant and clinically comparable improvement in all periodontal parameters; GBT's only consistent edge was shorter treatment time.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Short follow-up (mean ~4.5 months); no long-term stability or tooth-loss data.
- Treatment delivered by students over 3 semesters → variable operator skill and repeated cross-over of operators; supervised setting may not generalize to solo experienced clinicians.
- Split-mouth design risks carry-over of oral hygiene improvement across quadrants and possible contamination.
- Deliberate overpowering (n=60) flagged by authors as possible resource waste; primary endpoint difference was small and clinically marginal.
- Statistical significance of BOP at T1 (15.9% vs 14.3%) is of uncertain clinical relevance.
- No microbiological outcomes despite mechanistic discussion of erythritol/glycine effects on pathogens.

## 6. Related Work

- Suvan et al. — SR on subgingival instrumentation; combination of instruments yields best/most predictable results (mean pocket closure ~74%).
- Wennström et al. — full-mouth scaling, pocket closure 66–77% at 3–6 months.
- Geisinger et al. (2007) — basis for power calculation.
- EFP / international NSPT recommendations (Sanz/Herrera workflow) underpin the staged therapy framework.

## 7. Glossary

- **NSPT**: Non-surgical periodontal therapy.
- **GBT**: Guided Biofilm Therapy — EMS protocol combining erythritol air-polishing, ultrasonic scaling, and plaque-disclosing-guided workflow.
- **SRP**: Scaling and root planing.
- **PPD**: Pocket probing depth.
- **BOP**: Bleeding on probing.
- **CAL**: Clinical attachment level/loss.
- **GR**: Gingival recession.
- **PCR**: Plaque control record (O'Leary index).
- **RDA**: Relative dentin abrasion (polishing paste abrasiveness).
- **Closed pocket / pocket closure**: site reaching PPD ≤ 4 mm without BOP — surgical-therapy endpoint.
- **Split-mouth design**: each patient receives both treatments in contralateral quadrants, acting as own control.
