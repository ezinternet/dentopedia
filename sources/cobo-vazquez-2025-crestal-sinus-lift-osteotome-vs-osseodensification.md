## Why Ingested

경치조골 상악동거상술(Transcrestal Sinus Augmentation)에서 고전 osteotome(Summers)와 골밀도화(Osseodensification, OD) 중 어느 술식을 택할지 직접 비교한 근거가 필요. 본 PRISMA SR+MA(13 studies, 519 sites)는 OD가 동등한 골증대량으로 합병증을 약 5배 낮춤을 보여 술식 선택의 핵심 근거가 된다. See [[overviews/sinus-lift-technique-selection]].

## One-line Summary
PRISMA-guided SR+MA (13 studies, 519 sites; 10 osteotome / 3 osseodensification) comparing crestal sinus lift techniques — similar bone gain (OD 3.37 mm, OST 3.18 mm) and survival (94.1–100%) but markedly lower complication rate for OD (2.78%) vs OST (14.32%).

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (PRISMA, random-effects)
- **Journal**: International Journal of Implant Dentistry 2025;11:36
- **DOI**: 10.1186/s40729-025-00615-9
- **Authors**: Cobo-Vázquez et al., Complutense University of Madrid
- **Sample**: 13 studies / 519 treated sites (10 osteotome studies; 3 osseodensification studies); typical implant dimensions 4 × 10 mm
- **Primary Question**: How do the classic Summers osteotome (OST) and Huwais osseodensification (OD) crestal sinus lift techniques compare for bone gain, marginal bone loss, implant survival, follow-up time, and complications?

## 2. Key Contributions
- First PRISMA-guided SR+MA directly comparing the two crestal sinus lift schools — OST (Summers 1994, hand-malleted compaction) vs OD (Huwais & Meyer 2013, Densah counterclockwise).
- Residual subsinus bone height (RBH) at baseline: 5.94 mm OD vs 5.00 mm OST — note OD cohorts started with slightly more native bone, a baseline confounder.
- Bone gain was clinically equivalent between techniques: 3.37 mm OD vs 3.18 mm OST.
- Implant survival ranged 94.1–100% across both techniques — no clinically meaningful between-group difference detected.
- **Major finding — complications**: OST complication rate 14.32% vs OD 2.78% (≈ 5× lower with OD). Drives the authors' "safer and more predictable" conclusion despite the small OD evidence base.
- Searched MEDLINE/PubMed, SCOPUS, and CENTRAL; risk-of-bias appraisal via Newcastle–Ottawa (observational) and Cochrane RoB tool (RCTs); random-effects meta-analysis for survival, RBH, and bone gain.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
The review followed PRISMA 2020 guidelines and was conducted across three databases (MEDLINE/PubMed, SCOPUS, Cochrane CENTRAL) up to early 2025. Studies comparing or reporting on crestal sinus lift via osteotome (Summers technique) or osseodensification (Huwais Densah burs) with simultaneous implant placement were eligible. Quality appraisal used the Newcastle–Ottawa scale for observational studies and the Cochrane Collaboration tool for randomised trials. A random-effects meta-analysis was performed on three outcomes: implant survival rate, residual bone height, and bone gain.

Thirteen studies met inclusion (10 OST, 3 OD), totalling 519 treated sites. The most frequently used implant macrogeometry was 4 mm diameter × 10 mm length. Complication rates, follow-up windows, and marginal bone loss data were synthesised narratively.

The 10 : 3 imbalance between OST and OD studies is a structural feature of the OD literature in 2025 — the technique is younger and clinical RCTs are still accruing. The authors flag this asymmetry as a caveat.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Residual bone height (baseline)**: 5.94 mm OD vs 5.00 mm OST
- **Bone gain (post-procedure)**: 3.37 mm OD vs 3.18 mm OST — clinically similar
- **Implant dimensions (most frequent)**: 4 mm × 10 mm in both groups
- **Implant survival range**: 94.1–100% (both groups)
- **Complication rate**: OST 14.32% vs OD 2.78% — ≈ 5× lower with OD
- **Studies / sites**: 13 / 519 (10 OST, 3 OD)
- I² and pooled effect-size CI not extractable from front+back text available; readers should consult the full PDF for forest plots.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Only 3 OD studies vs 10 OST — OD effect estimates are based on a thin evidence base.
- Baseline RBH was higher in OD cohorts (5.94 vs 5.00 mm) — favourable baseline confounds the bone-gain and complication comparison.
- Heterogeneous grafting materials, simultaneous vs delayed implant placement, and follow-up windows across included studies.
- Newcastle–Ottawa quality was reported but specific scores per study are not visible in the extracted text.
- Operator experience and learning-curve effects not adjusted for.
- The 14.32% vs 2.78% complication-rate gap may partly reflect publication-era differences (OST studies are older, with less sophisticated membrane management).

## 7. Glossary
- **Crestal / transcrestal sinus lift**: Sinus floor elevation accessed through the implant osteotomy itself (crest), without lateral window osteotomy.
- **Osteotome (OST) technique**: Summers 1994 method using calibrated hand-driven osteotomes to fracture the sinus floor green-stick and elevate the membrane via crestal compaction; typically mallet-driven.
- **Osseodensification (OD) technique**: Huwais counterclockwise Densah-bur compaction; in sinus lift, the densifying bur both prepares the osteotomy and hydraulically elevates the membrane via the autografted bone column.
- **Residual bone height (RBH)**: Vertical sub-sinus native bone in mm available before sinus floor elevation; a key case-selection variable.
- **Bone gain**: Vertical increase in radiographic bone height post-procedure.
- **PRISMA**: Reporting standard for systematic reviews and meta-analyses.
