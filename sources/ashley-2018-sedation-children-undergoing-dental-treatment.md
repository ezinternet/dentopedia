## One-line Summary

Cochrane SR+MA (2nd update; 50 RCTs, 3704 children ≤16y): moderate-certainty evidence that oral midazolam 0.25–1 mg/kg improves cooperative behaviour vs placebo (SMD 1.96, 95% CI 1.59–2.33; 6 studies, 202 participants, I²=90%); evidence for 33 other sedatives too sparse/heterogeneous to pool, and 81% of trials at high risk of bias.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides the highest-evidence appraisal of conscious-sedation efficacy for behaviour management in paediatric dentistry.
- Establishes oral midazolam as the only sedative with poolable, moderate-certainty evidence of benefit over placebo.
- Documents the field's fragmentation: 34 different sedatives across 50 trials, widely varying dosages/routes/timing, mostly high risk of bias — a call for standardised, midazolam- or nitrous-oxide-comparator trials.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Inclusion: RCTs of conscious sedation comparing ≥2 drugs/techniques/placebo, administered by the dentist or dental team, in children up to 16 years. Cross-over trials excluded.
- Two authors independently extracted methods, participants, interventions, outcomes, results in duplicate; trial authors contacted when reporting was unclear; risk of bias assessed per Cochrane guidance.
- Studies grouped into placebo-controlled, dosage, and head-to-head comparisons. Primary outcome = behaviour; meta-analysis feasible only for oral midazolam vs placebo. GRADE certainty applied.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- 50 studies, 3704 participants. Risk of bias: 40 (81%) high, 9 (18%) unclear, 1 low.
- 34 different sedatives used, frequently combined with inhalational nitrous oxide.
- Oral midazolam vs placebo (primary outcome, behaviour): **SMD 1.96 (95% CI 1.59 to 2.33), P < 0.0001, I² = 90%; 6 studies; 202 participants** — moderate-certainty evidence favouring midazolam at doses 0.25–1 mg/kg.
- Adverse effects, where reported, were few and minor.
- Secondary outcomes: no conclusions possible due to inconsistent/inadequate reporting.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- 81% of included trials at high risk of bias; only one low-risk trial.
- The pooled midazolam estimate rests on 6 small, clinically heterogeneous studies (I²=90%) at high/unclear risk of bias — hence moderate, not high, certainty.
- Extreme heterogeneity in agents, doses, routes and timing precluded meta-analysis of all other sedatives and of secondary outcomes.
- Authors call for well-designed, well-reported RCTs evaluating new regimens against oral midazolam or inhaled nitrous oxide.
- This summary is abstract-only (PMC full text empty); per-drug/sub-group detail beyond the abstract was not retrieved.

## 7. Glossary

- Conscious sedation: drug-induced depression of consciousness during which the patient maintains protective reflexes and responds to verbal command; used to relieve anxiety and manage behaviour.
- Oral midazolam: short-acting benzodiazepine given by mouth (often in juice); the only sedative with poolable benefit in this review.
- SMD (standardized mean difference): effect-size metric for combining studies measuring behaviour on different scales; ~0.8 = large, 1.96 here = very large.
- Inhalational nitrous oxide: N₂O/O₂ inhalation sedation, frequently co-administered with the oral agents studied.
