## One-line Summary

Consensus position paper (78 studies, 6 databases, 2012–2022) endorsing combined eyewear+mask+face-shield PPE, high/low-volume evacuation + saliva ejector + rubber dam, and appropriate ventilation/filtration to reduce aerosol transmission during dental AGPs; mouthrinses cut bacterial contamination but show limited effect against SARS-CoV-2.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Joint CDHA/ADHA position statement summarizing the evidence for methods that reduce infection-transmission risk during aerosol-generating procedures (AGPs) in dentistry and dental hygiene, prompted by the COVID-19 outbreak.
- Synthesizes evidence across four control axes: personal protective equipment (PPE), aerosol-reduction devices (evacuation/suction/rubber dam), mouthrinses, and operatory ventilation/filtration.
- Frames practical, evidence-supported recommendations to improve provider and patient safety in oral-health settings.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Risk of transmission**: Evidence on infection transmission (including SARS-CoV-2) between dental hygienists and their patients is **limited**.
- **Mouthrinses**: Several are effective at reducing **bacterial** contamination in aerosols, but their effectiveness against **SARS-CoV-2** is limited.
- **PPE**: Combined use of **eyewear, masks, and face shields** is effective in preventing contamination of the facial and nasal region during AGPs.
- **Aerosol-reduction devices**: **High-volume evacuation** (with or without intraoral suction), **low-volume evacuation**, **saliva ejector**, and **rubber dam** (when appropriate) reduce aerosol transmission beyond the generation site.
- **Operatory air**: An appropriate combination of **ventilation and filtration** limits aerosol spread.
- (No pooled effect sizes, particle-count reductions, or quantitative benchmarks are reported in the abstract.)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Underlying literature on transmission risk between hygienists and patients — especially for SARS-CoV-2 — is sparse.
- Mouthrinse efficacy against SARS-CoV-2 remains unproven despite antibacterial benefit.
- Authors call for **more studies conducted in real oral-health clinical environments** to shape future practices and protocols and ensure safe clinical care.

## 7. Glossary

- **AGP (Aerosol-Generating Procedure)**: A dental/hygiene procedure that produces aerosols and splatter (e.g., ultrasonic scaling, high-speed handpiece use).
- **PPE (Personal Protective Equipment)**: Barriers such as eyewear, masks, and face shields protecting the provider's face and mucosa.
- **HVE (High-Volume Evacuation)**: Suction device removing large air/aerosol volume near the generation site.
- **LVE (Low-Volume Evacuation) / Saliva Ejector**: Lower-flow intraoral suction aids.
- **Rubber dam**: Isolation barrier that reduces salivary aerosolization when the procedure permits.
- **Ventilation and filtration**: Operatory air-management measures (air exchange, HEPA/portable filtration) limiting aerosol dispersion.
- **CDHA / ADHA**: Canadian Dental Hygienists Association / American Dental Hygienists' Association.
