## Why Ingested

악골 위치·교합 변화가 성장기 vs 성인 하악과두(mandibular condyle) 형태에 미치는 영향을 정량화한 in-vivo micro-CT rat study로, 이미 보유한 [[wiki/tmj/wang-2024-tmj-ovd-elevation-occlusal-loss-rats]] (수직고경 상승+교합 상실로 인한 성인 rat TMJ 변화)와 짝을 이뤄 "교합 부하 변화 → 과두 적응" 동물 근거를 보강한다. 또한 과두 적응의 연령 의존성(성장기 > 성인) 논점을 [[wiki/tmj/song-2023-understanding-tmj-osteoarthritis-pathophysiology]]의 기계적 부하-연골 항상성 맥락에 연결한다.

## One-line Summary

In-vivo micro-CT study of 56 Wistar rats (young + adult, unilateral maxillary molar extraction): reduced occlusal function did NOT hinder condylar process height or base width growth, but the extraction-side condyle of growing rats failed to gain cross-sectional surface (became thinner while keeping length); adult condyles were largely unaffected by the occlusal change.

## 한줄요약

56마리 Wistar rat(어린/성인, 편측 상악 대구치 발치) in-vivo micro-CT 연구: 교합 기능 감소는 하악과두의 높이·기저폭 성장은 막지 못했으나, 성장기 rat의 발치측 과두는 단면적이 증가하지 못해(길이는 유지하면서 얇아짐) 형태가 변했고, 성인 과두는 교합 변화에 거의 영향받지 않았다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First in-vivo longitudinal micro-CT study to examine condylar process morphology under **unilateral** masticatory function (unilateral maxillary molar extraction) in BOTH growing and adult rats, separating overloaded vs unopposed sides.
2. Dissociates three condylar parameters: process **height** and base **width** grow normally despite reduced occlusal function, but average **cross-sectional surface** is occlusion-sensitive in growing animals.
3. Demonstrates an **age-dependent adaptive window**: growing condyles change shape (thin out) under hypofunction; adult condyles are largely refractory to the occlusal change.
4. Finds that the **overloaded and unopposed sides were similarly affected** — a unilateral functional asymmetry (without mandibular lateral shift) did not produce a measurable left/right condylar asymmetry.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Animals**: 56 Wistar rats in two age groups — young (4 weeks) and adult (26 weeks). Each age group: 16 experimental + 12 controls. Followed 12 weeks; sacrificed at 16 and 38 weeks.
- **Intervention**: maxillary RIGHT molars extracted (under Ketamine 90 + Xylazine 10 mg/kg, Buprenorphine 0.1 mg/kg). All animals fed a **soft diet**, water ad libitum (controls also soft diet, to isolate extraction effect from surgical morbidity).
- **Side definitions** (experimental rats): right side = **unopposed/hypofunctional** (YU, AU); left side = **overloaded/hyperfunctional** (YO, AO). Groups: YC, YE, AC, AE.
- **Imaging**: in-vivo micro-CT (Quantum GX, PerkinElmer) at T0 (start), T1 (1 month), T2 (3 months). Scan: FOV 60 mm, 90 kV, 88 μA, high-resolution, 4 min; reconstruction voxel 30 μm, FOV 15.4 mm. DICOM exported, analyzed in OsiriX; reoriented to the condylar base (AB line through deepest posterior mandibular notches, per Denes et al.). Operator **blinded** (randomized numbers; teeth removed from scans).
- **Measurements**: (a) condylar base width, (b) condylar process height, (c) average condylar cross-sectional surface (mean of slice surfaces parallel to the base = indirect volume proxy). Outcome = T90 − T0 difference.
- **Statistics**: JASP; two-way ANOVA (independent vars Age × Group + interaction); Tukey post-hoc; Kruskal-Wallis for condyle height (unequal variances by Levene). Method error: Dahlberg random error, paired-t systematic error, Houston reliability on 30 repeats.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Method error**: no systematic error; reliability >96% all parameters; random errors 0.029 mm (height), 0.028 mm (width), 0.115 mm² (cross-section). No weight differences between groups. One YC and one AC rat died during anaesthesia.
- **Condylar process height**: NO control-vs-experimental difference. Strong age effect — young grew 1.36 mm vs adult 0.24 mm over 3 months (P < .001).
- **Condylar base width**: NO control-vs-experimental difference (Group P = .922). Age effect — young grew 1.85 mm vs adult 0.20 mm (P < .001).
- **Condylar cross-sectional surface**: significant Age×Group interaction (P = .002). Young control gained +0.25 mm² (P = .043); young experimental showed NO increase. YC change > YE by 0.19 mm² (P = .010); YC > all adult groups (0.28 mm², P < .001). Adult group: no significant change. Young overloaded (YO) vs young unopposed (YU): no difference.
- **Age effect**: adults had larger absolute condylar height, width and cross-section than young at baseline; young rats nearly reached adult-T0 values by T2.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Controls were also fed a **soft diet**, likely underestimating effects — a hard-diet control might have revealed larger differences (and possibly height/width effects).
- Bite force / actual masticatory loading was **not measured**; unilateral functional asymmetry was assumed from extraction.
- No condylar cartilage / histology; only bony morphology by micro-CT.
- Cross-sectional surface used as an indirect volume proxy rather than direct volumetric segmentation.
- Findings are in rats; extrapolation to human adult TMJ adaptation is limited.

## 6. Related Work

- Kiliaridis (soft vs hard diet, reduced condylar head growth); Denes et al. (bite-block / diet, similar cross-sectional measurement — the closest comparator).
- Nakano (occlusal-splint-induced mandibular lateral shift produced asymmetric mandibles — contrast: that involved displacement, this study only functional asymmetry).
- Enomoto, Abo (condylar height / neck width unaffected — concordant with the height/width null findings here).

## 7. Glossary

- **Condylar process**: the superior bony projection of the mandibular ramus forming the mandibular component of the TMJ.
- **Unopposed/hypofunctional side**: extraction side lacking occlusal contact (reduced load).
- **Overloaded/hyperfunctional side**: contralateral side assumed to receive higher functional force.
- **Cross-sectional surface (condylar)**: average area of slices parallel to the condylar base; indirect index of condylar volume/thickness.
- **micro-CT (in-vivo)**: longitudinal 3D micro-computed tomography allowing repeated imaging of the same live animal over time.
- **Dahlberg's formula**: standard method to quantify random measurement error from duplicate measurements.
