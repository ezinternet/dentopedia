## Why Ingested

기존 식편압입 카테고리는 근접접촉소실 (Proximal Contact Loss, PCL) 의 유병률·부위·위험인자를 정량화한 SR/MA·코호트 중심이었으나, **PCL이 인접 자연치의 인접면 우식 (interproximal caries) 으로 이어지는 생물학적 연쇄**를 명시적으로 다룬 페이지가 없었다. 본 narrative review (Alsaloum 2026) 는 PCL을 단순 보철 합병증이 아닌 우식 발생의 직접 위험인자로 재규정하고, 임플란트 함입 (infraposition) 의 성장·연령·성별 메커니즘까지 묶어 "PCL → 식편압입 → biofilm → 우식·골소실" 인과 경로를 보강한다. 또한 [[food-impaction/sheba-2023-interproximal-contact-loss-systematic-meta]] 의 정량 결과(mesial 44.2% vs distal 27.5%)와 일관된 mesial 우위를 narrative로 재확인.

## One-line Summary
Narrative review reframing proximal contact loss (PCL) between implant restorations and adjacent natural teeth as a direct biological risk factor for interproximal caries — synthesizing PCL prevalence (18–66%), consistent mesial > distal predominance, time-dependent progression (~9–10%/year; ~50% lost by 3–9 yr), the implant-infraposition (lifelong ~0.1–0.2 mm/yr eruption) growth mechanism by age/sex, and prevention via tight contacts + Essix retainers (~15–50% PCL reduction).

## 한줄요약
서술적 리뷰: 임플란트 보철-자연치 사이 근접접촉소실(PCL)을 인접면 우식의 직접 위험인자로 재규정 — PCL 유병률 18–66%, 일관된 mesial>distal, 시간의존 진행(연 ~9–10%, 3–9년 내 절반 소실), 평생 치아맹출(~0.1–0.2 mm/yr)에 의한 임플란트 함입 기전, Essix 리테이너로 PCL ~15–50% 감소.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Narrative review (PubMed article type: Review)
- **Journal**: Clinical, Cosmetic and Investigational Dentistry, 2026; vol 18 (pii 585610)
- **Publication date**: 2026-03-30
- **Source**: PubMed / PMC full text (PMC13049379). According to PubMed; DOI: https://doi.org/10.2147/CCIDE.S585610
- **First author**: Mohammed Alsaloum (King Saud Bin Abdulaziz University for Health Sciences, Riyadh, Saudi Arabia)
- **Aim**: Elucidate the frequency of interproximal caries on natural teeth adjacent to implants and implant infraocclusion across all age groups, and the factors driving PCL.

## 2. Key Contributions

- **Reframes PCL as a direct caries risk factor**, not merely a prosthetic/mechanical complication — the open embrasure causes food impaction + plaque retention → cariogenic biofilm → sustained acid production → demineralization of the adjacent tooth's proximal surface.
- Synthesizes **PCL prevalence**: 18% to >65% across studies; ~3–33.5%/year with a central tendency near ~10%/year.
- Documents **consistent mesial > distal predominance** across many cohorts (e.g., Koori 51.8% mesial vs 15.6% distal; Chao-Hua 27% vs 5%, 5.4-fold; Byun 2.1-fold, P=0.048; schematic figure 52% mesial vs 16% distal), driven by physiologic mesial drift of natural teeth against the ankylosed (fixed) implant.
- Links PCL to the **infraposition / growth mechanism**: lifelong slow vertical tooth eruption (~0.1–0.2 mm/yr in adults; 1.2–1.5 mm/yr during active growth) leaves the static implant in relative infraocclusion; >1 mm infraposition observed in a substantial fraction at 15–20 yr.
- **Prevention emphasis**: tight proximal contacts at delivery, regular follow-up + radiographs, and removable (Essix) retainers reducing PCL incidence (one study 30%→15%, a 50% reduction; another ~15% reduction primarily mesial).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Narrative (non-systematic) review; no PRISMA protocol, search strategy, or pooled estimates reported. Confidence label: `narrative-review`.
- Organized into two thematic pillars: (1) **Infra-position** — age/growth, gender, jaw-bone growth (maxillary transverse/sagittal/vertical), syndromic patients (ectodermal dysplasia/anodontia, acromegaly); (2) **Interproximal Contact Loss** — early presentation, patient/prosthesis factors, location-specific differences, time-in-function, prevention, and PCL → caries.
- Evidence synthesized from prospective, retrospective, cross-sectional studies plus prior SR/reviews (Bento, Varthis) and case reports; no formal risk-of-bias appraisal.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Prevalence**: PCL 18% to >65%; ~10%/year typical. Within 1 yr: 11% (4,325 implants), 12.5% (7-yr prospective), 24.3% (1-yr preliminary). Two prospective studies: 16.5%→25.7% (yr1→yr2); 3.3%→21.3% (>6-fold yr2).
- **Site**: Mesial >> distal repeatedly (Koori 51.8/15.6%; Chao-Hua 27/5%; Saber 42.1/14.5%; Byun 2.1×). Molar > premolar (42.6% molars, 22.2% 2nd PM, 10% 1st PM). Arch effect inconsistent (some mandible-higher: Shi 37.2% vs 9.1%; Koori; some maxilla-higher: Varthis 57.9% maxilla; many show no significant arch difference — Saber, Jui-Ying, Mehanna).
- **Timing**: PCL detectable as early as 3 months; ~9%/yr rise; Kaplan-Meier ~50% of contacts lost within 3–9 yr; progression up to 12 yr.
- **Patient factors**: Gender — no measurable effect (Bento 41% women vs 36% men; multiple studies NS). Age — steady rise in frequency/size (esp. mesial), though Saber found no age effect. Removable antagonist prostheses → lower contact-change rate (reduced force transmission).
- **Infraposition magnitude**: adult eruption ~0.1–0.2 mm/yr; implant placed at age 7 → ~10 mm more apical than neighbors after 9 yr (Ranly); children with single implants ~age 12 → 5–7 mm relative infraocclusion after 4 yr; maxillary incisors shift ~2.5 mm buccal + 6 mm down between ages 9–25.
- **Caries link**: food impaction associated with inflammatory peri-implant soft-tissue change in >50% of impacted cases; PCL framed as a direct caries risk factor for the adjacent natural tooth.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative review — no systematic search, no quantitative pooling, no risk-of-bias assessment; vulnerable to selective citation.
- The headline "interproximal caries frequency" is **not actually quantified** with a pooled caries-incidence figure; the caries connection is mechanistic/inferential rather than measured (no study reporting caries rate on teeth adjacent to PCL is cited with a numeric outcome).
- Several in-text figure/table references are unresolvable in the extracted text (figures not retrievable via PMC text endpoint).
- Syndromic-implant evidence (ectodermal dysplasia, acromegaly) is limited to case reports; authors note more clinical studies are needed.
- Growth/infraposition claims are largely extrapolated from orthodontic and single-case longitudinal data.

## 6. Related Work

- Sheba 2023 SR+MA (mesial 44.2% vs distal 27.5%, posterior only) — the quantitative anchor this review echoes narratively.

- Andersson, Bernard, Jemt, Schwartz-Arad, Thilander, Huanca/Jonasson/Kiliaridis — infraposition/eruption longitudinal evidence.

## 7. Glossary

- **PCL (Proximal/Interproximal Contact Loss)**: opening of the contact between an implant-supported restoration and an adjacent natural tooth.
- **Infraposition / Infraocclusion**: relative apical position of an ankylosed (non-erupting) implant crown as adjacent natural teeth continue to erupt; the occlusal plane discrepancy that develops over years.
- **Mesial drift**: physiologic forward (mesial) migration of natural teeth; the primary driver of mesial-predominant PCL against a fixed implant.
- **Essix retainer**: clear removable vacuum-formed retainer used to limit post-restoration tooth movement and reduce PCL.
- **Interproximal caries**: caries on the proximal surface of the natural tooth adjacent to the implant, initiated by food impaction + cariogenic biofilm in the open embrasure.
