## One-line Summary
PRISMA SR+MA of maxillary overdentures supported by 4 vs 6 splinted implants (15 of 1865 articles, 1–10 yr follow-up): no statistical difference in implant survival (p=0.34, 0.3), overdenture survival (p=0.74, 0.9), prosthodontic complications, or patient satisfaction — bar-supported overdenture on 4 splinted implants is a viable conservative option compared to 6.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Addresses a long-debated maxillary OD question: do we need 6 implants, or do 4 suffice?
- Combines random-effects meta-analysis with non-parametric Fisher's test for robustness.
- Provides a clear, conservative answer: **4 splinted implants are non-inferior to 6 for typical clinical outcomes**.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Databases: Cochrane Central, MEDLINE (PubMed), EMBASE; hand search 2000–2019.
- Inclusion: studies comparing 4 vs 6 implants supporting maxillary OD, ≥1-yr follow-up, ≥10 fully edentulous patients.
- 15 of 1865 articles included.
- RoB: Cochrane RoB 2 (RCTs) and Newcastle-Ottawa (non-randomized).
- Statistical: random-effects meta-analysis (risk ratios for survival; risk differences for prosthodontic outcomes) + non-parametric unpaired Fisher's test.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Implant survival 4 vs 6 splinted**: no significant difference (p=0.34 meta, 0.3 Fisher).
- **Overdenture survival**: no significant difference (p=0.74 meta, 0.9 Fisher).
- **Prosthodontic complications**: no significant between-group differences.
- **Patient satisfaction**: uniformly high across studies, independent of implant count.
- **Risk of bias**: randomized studies often high RoB; non-randomized = acceptable quality.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- High RoB in randomized studies limits strength of conclusion.
- Wide range of follow-up periods (1–10 yr) and prosthetic designs.
- Few studies investigated non-splinted maxillary OD with 4 implants — splinting (bar) is the assumed default.
- Need head-to-head RCTs of bar-splinted vs individual attachments in the maxilla.

## 6. Related Work
- McGill (2002) / York (2009) consensus statements — mandibular 2-implant OD as default, but no parallel statement for maxilla.
- Patel 2025, Sutariya 2021: mandibular OD attachment comparisons.
- Tayari 2024: implant-supported OD failure-risk SR (maxilla flagged as higher-risk arch).

## 7. Glossary
- **Splinted overdenture**: implants joined by a bar; load distributed across implants.
- **Risk ratio (RR)**: ratio of event probabilities between two groups.
- **Risk difference**: absolute difference in event probability between two groups.
