## Why Ingested

[[wiki/orthodontics/alharbi-2018-miniscrew-failure-rate-orthodontics-sr-ma]] gives a pooled failure rate but not a map of *how* TADs are actually used in practice (brand, diameter, length, loading, force, site). This 2022 scoping review extends the anchor by charting the descriptive landscape of TAD characteristics and success-failure rates across 103 studies, providing the practice-pattern context the pooled estimate lacks.

## One-line Summary

Scoping review (103 studies, 2010–2020): maps real-world TAD practice — most placed in the maxilla, inter-radicular between 2nd premolar and 1st molar, AbsoAnchor most-used brand, typical 1.6 mm × 8 mm self-drilled immediately-loaded screws (40–800 g); 47.6% of studies reported success rates ≥90%.

## 한줄요약

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Scoping review (PRISMA-ScR; JBI methodology). Per CLAUDE.md vocabulary, recorded as `sr`.
- **Journal**: Dentistry Journal 2022;10:78
- **Databases**: PubMed-MEDLINE, Scopus, LILACS, EMBASE; 2010–2020; English/Spanish/Portuguese
- **PCC question** (not registered in PROSPERO, per scoping-review convention): characteristics and success-failure rates of TADs in orthodontic treatment

## 2. Key Contributions

- Broad descriptive map of TAD characteristics and reported success-failure rates (no quality appraisal — scoping by design).
- Identifies dominant device parameters, placement sites, loading protocols, and force ranges in the literature.
- Highlights methodological-design variability and geographic concentration of publications.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Phase A (from prior umbrella review): 156 records → 83 full-text. Phase B (new search): 518 records → 462 de-duplicated → 37 full-text.
- Final inclusion: **103 articles**.
- Weighted-mean success rate computed via sample-size weighting formula; results as mean ± SD.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **103 studies included** (2010–2020).
- Most TADs placed in the **maxilla**, **inter-radicular between 2nd premolar and 1st molar**.
- Most-used brand: **AbsoAnchor** (Dentos, Daegu, Korea).
- Most common device/biomechanics: **diameter 1.6 mm, length 8 mm**, **self-drilled**, closed placement technique, **immediate loading**, forces **40–800 g**.
- **47.6% of studies reported success rates ≥90%.**
- Articles published across 47 journals; AJODO most frequent (n=26); top author Motoyoshi M. (n=4).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Scoping design → no risk-of-bias/quality appraisal and no pooled effect estimate.
- High methodological-design variability across primary studies.
- Geographic publication concentration; recommends more standardized study designs.

## 7. Glossary

- **TAD**: temporary anchorage device (orthodontic mini-implant/miniscrew/miniplate).
- **Scoping review**: maps the breadth of literature without quality appraisal or pooling.
- **Self-drilled**: screw inserted without a pre-drilled pilot hole.
- **Immediate loading**: force applied at/near placement rather than after a healing delay.
