## Why Ingested

[[inlay/prott-2025-partial-coverage-restorations-posterior-scoping]]가 부분 피개 수복(PCR)의 임상 생존율을 종합했지만 재료별 최소 두께·비파괴 하중 근거는 부족했다. 본 in-vitro 연구(Spitznagel 2025)는 composition-gradient multilayer zirconia를 0.5/1.0/1.5 mm 세 두께에서 PCR vs crown으로 직접 비교해, 초박형(0.5 mm)에서 PCR이 crown보다 우월한 failure load를 보임을 정량화한다 — PCR 설계의 두께-의존적 역학 우위를 보강하고, [[dental-materials/zirconia/cesar-2024-dental-zirconia-15years-material-processing]]의 yttria-gradient 멀티레이어 zirconia 논의를 임상 수복물 형상에 적용한다.

## One-line Summary
In-vitro fatigue + load-to-failure study (n=72, IPS e.max ZirCAD Prime Esthetic 4Y/5Y composition-gradient multilayer zirconia molar restorations): all survived 1.2M-cycle fatigue (97.2% crack-free); at 0.5 mm ultrathin, partial-coverage restorations far outperformed crowns (2047 vs 695 N, p<0.001), no difference at 1.0 mm, crowns superior at 1.5 mm (3503 vs 2777 N, p=0.005).

## 한줄요약
multilayer zirconia 어금니 수복물 in-vitro 피로·파절하중 연구(n=72) — 0.5 mm 초박형에서는 부분피개(PCR)가 크라운보다 파절하중 압도(2047 vs 695 N), 1.0 mm는 동등, 1.5 mm는 크라운 우위(3503 vs 2777 N). 크라운은 최소 1.0 mm 필요.

## 2. Key Contributions

- **First in-vitro study** evaluating *full-veneer* molar restorations made from composition-gradient multilayer zirconia (4Y-PSZ dentin / 5Y-PSZ incisal) across a thickness range comparing partial-coverage (PCR) vs full crown.
- Demonstrates a **crossed thickness × design interaction**: PCR is mechanically superior at ultrathin (0.5 mm), equal at 1.0 mm, inferior at 1.5 mm — reversing the conventional assumption that full crowns are always stronger.
- Provides minimum-thickness guidance: PCRs clinically viable at all tested thicknesses; **crowns need ≥1.0 mm** in molars.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: 2×3 factorial — restoration type (PCR vs crown) × ceramic thickness (occlusal/buccal 0.5/0.4, 1.0/0.6, 1.5/0.8 mm); 6 groups, n=12, n=72 total (G*Power: f=0.26, 80% power, α=0.05).
- **Material**: IPS e.max ZirCAD Prime Esthetic (Ivoclar Vivadent) — seamless gradient 4Y-PSZ (dentin) → 5Y-PSZ (incisal); restorations positioned within the transition layer, 3.3 mm from blank top.
- **Preparation**: maxillary first molar typodont; PCR = non-retentive "full-veneer" (occlusal+buccal+proximal, no box, 2 shallow 0.2 mm diagonal seating notches, chamfer cervical margin); crowns same dimensions without notches.
- **Substrate**: resin-composite dies (Filtek Z100, ~18 GPa ≈ dentin), epoxy-embedded to mimic bone.
- **Cementation**: 50 µm Al₂O₃ air-abrasion + Monobond Plus primer (MDP) + Adhese Universal + Variolink Esthetic DC dual-cure resin cement.
- **Fatigue**: chewing simulator, 1.2M cycles, 49 N, 1.6 Hz, thermocycling 5–55 °C (≈5 years clinical use), steatite sphere sliding 0.5 mm.
- **Single load to failure (SLF)**: Zwick universal machine, 3 mm steel ball, 1.5 mm/min; failure = crack/fracture or ≥20% Fmax drop.
- **Analysis**: ANOVA + Tukey post hoc + independent t-tests; fractography via polarized light microscopy + SEM.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Fatigue (1.2M cycles):** 100% survival both designs; only 2 cracked specimens — C-0.5 (crack @ 802,485 cycles) and PCR-0.5 (crack @ 551,960 cycles), both radial cracks from intaglio surface confined to ceramic. Overall success 97.22%.

**Mean failure load (N), single-load-to-failure:**

| Group | Thickness (occl/bucc mm) | Mean (N) | Median | SD |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| C-1.5 | 1.5/0.8 | 3503 | 3380 | 628 |
| PCR-1.5 | 1.5/0.8 | 2777 | 2915 | 509 |
| PCR-0.5 | 0.5/0.4 | 2047 | 2047 | 298 |
| PCR-1.0 | 1.0/0.6 | 2018 | 1974 | 346 |
| C-1.0 | 1.0/0.6 | 1957 | 1890 | 274 |
| C-0.5 | 0.5/0.4 | 695 | 594 | 224 |

**Statistics:** restoration type (p=0.02) and thickness (p<0.001) both significant; crossed interaction (p<0.001). Per-thickness: 0.5 mm → PCR ≫ crown (2047 vs 695 N, p<0.001); 1.0 mm → no difference (p=0.63); 1.5 mm → crown > PCR (3503 vs 2777 N, p=0.005). In PCRs, 0.5 mm = 1.0 mm (p=0.983).

**Clinical context:** all groups except C-0.5 exceeded normal posterior masticatory range (289–700 N); C-0.5 (~695 N) nears the upper physiological/parafunctional threshold (~900 N), flagging risk.

**Failure modes:** crowns 1.0/1.5 mm → Type IV severe longitudinal tooth/die fracture (more catastrophic, transmit stress to substrate); C-0.5 → mostly Type II cohesive ceramic fracture, intact die; PCRs all thicknesses → mostly Type III combined ceramic+substrate fracture.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro design; resin-composite dies instead of extracted human teeth.
- Single commercial material (IPS e.max ZirCAD Prime Esthetic) — limits generalizability to other gradient systems.
- No failure-load comparison vs lithium disilicate or other zirconia compositions in same protocol.
- No clinical data exist for monolithic zirconia PCRs; authors call for RCTs and FEA stress-distribution analysis.
- Manufacturer currently restricts indication to single crowns ≥1.0 mm — ultrathin PCR use is off-label/experimental.

## 6. Related Work

- Prott 2025 scoping review — survival of posterior PCRs (clinical).
- Occlusal-veneer fatigue studies of ZirCAD Prime (0.3–0.6 mm) and ZirCAD Prime Esthetic (0.5–1.5 mm); 5Y-PSZ-only veneers showed isolated failures (7.8%), 0.5 mm veneer success dropped to 50%.
- Full-contour crown studies showing failure load declines with rising yttria (3Y-TZP > 4Y-PSZ > 5Y-PSZ); yttria at occlusal surface is primary determinant of fracture resistance.

## 7. Glossary

- **PCR (Partial-Coverage Restoration)**: conservative restoration covering part of the crown (occlusal ± buccal/proximal) rather than the full circumference; here a non-retentive "full-veneer" design.
- **Composition-gradient multilayer zirconia**: a single block with a continuous yttria gradient — 4Y-PSZ (high strength, dentin zone) to 5Y-PSZ (high translucency, incisal zone) with a transition zone, no discrete interfaces.
- **4Y-PSZ / 5Y-PSZ**: 4 mol% / 5 mol% yttria partially-stabilized zirconia; higher yttria = more translucent, more cubic phase, lower strength/toughness and reduced transformation toughening.
- **Single-load-to-failure (SLF)**: monotonic compressive loading to fracture, reporting max load (N).
- **Transformation toughening**: stress-induced tetragonal→monoclinic phase change at crack tips producing compressive stresses that impede crack propagation; diminished at higher yttria content.
- **LTD (Low-Temperature Degradation)**: hydrothermal aging-driven t→m transformation degrading zirconia; 5Y-PSZ (cubic-rich) is resistant, 3Y/4Y are susceptible.
