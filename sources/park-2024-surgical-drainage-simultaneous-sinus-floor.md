## Why Ingested

만성 상악동염 (Chronic Maxillary Sinusitis, CMS) 환자에서 내시경 부비동 수술 (Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery, FESS) 없이 상악동거상술 (Maxillary Sinus Floor Augmentation, MSFA)을 동시에 시행할 수 있는지는 임상에서 중요한 결정 분기점. 이 증례 보고는 구강내 배액만으로 CMS를 해결하면서 동시에 MSFA + 임플란트 식립에 성공한 드문 사례를 제시하여, 기존 [[sinus-lift/lateral/craig-2024-ent-oral-surgeon-collaboration-sinus-elevation]]에서 다루는 ENT-구강외과 협진 필요성에 대한 보완적 시각을 제공한다.

## One-line Summary

Case report (n=2) demonstrating that intraoral surgical drainage with intentional Schneiderian membrane incision, simultaneous with lateral-window MSFA and implant placement, resolved chronic maxillary sinusitis without FESS, with favorable 1-year outcomes.

## 한줄요약

만성 상악동염 환자 2명에서 FESS 없이 측방창 접근 MSFA 도중 구강내 배액(의도적 막 절개)을 동시 시행하여 1년 추적에서 성공적인 임플란트 생존율과 상악동 병변 소실을 보고한 증례 보고.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Medicina (Kaunas), 2024;60(8):1256
- **Institution**: Department of Periodontology, Kyung Hee University Dental Hospital, Seoul, Republic of Korea
- **Study type**: Case report (n=2 patients)
- **Follow-up**: 1 year after prosthetic loading

## 2. Key Contributions

- First description of **intentional long-incision perforation** of the Schneiderian membrane (using a #15 blade) for thorough drainage, as opposed to small-needle puncture, performed simultaneously with MSFA
- Demonstrates resolution of complete unilateral maxillary sinus opacification (including ostium blockage) without FESS in 2 patients
- Provides a clinical rationale for intraoral sinus drainage as an alternative when FESS is refused
- Documents use of a Prichard elevator to prevent graft particle displacement through the perforation during xenograft placement

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Patient selection criteria:**
- Complete unilateral maxillary sinus opacification on CBCT
- Diagnosis of CMS confirmed by otolaryngologist
- Failed 2-week antibiotic course (amoxicillin/clavulanate 625 mg)
- FESS recommended but refused by patients
- CMS symptoms improved (mild) at time of surgery

**Surgical protocol (both cases):**
1. Full-thickness mucoperiosteal flap with vertical releasing incisions
2. Lateral bony window ~1.5 cm × 2.0 cm prepared with round bur
3. **Intentional horizontal incision** in upper third of exposed Schneiderian membrane (#15 blade)
4. Suction drainage of purulent exudate in all directions
5. Thorough saline irrigation until clear
6. Careful membrane detachment and elevation from sinus floor (distant from perforation)
7. Perforation naturally reduced in size and relocated buccally (tension relief, mucosal folding)
8. Xenograft placement (OsteonXeno, Genoss) with Prichard elevator covering perforation
9. Simultaneous SLA implant placement (Implantium, Dentium)
10. Primary flap closure without membrane at lateral window

**Case 1**: 59-year-old male smoker, rhinogenous CMS, right maxilla, 3 × Ø3.8 × 10 mm implants, nasal polyp at middle meatus blocking ostium
**Case 2**: 55-year-old male smoker, odontogenic CMS (right maxillary molar with advanced apical lesion), 4-tooth extraction + 3 implants simultaneously, peri-implant defects covered with collagen membrane, additional implant at second premolar during uncovering

**Postoperative medications**: Ciprofloxacin 500 mg + naproxen (Anaprox 275 mg) × 2 weeks; patients instructed not to blow nose

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Case 1 | Case 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Sinus opacification | Resolved (residual mild thickening on augmented floor) | Resolved (nasal polyps also disappeared) |
| Implant survival | 100% (3/3) at 1 year | 100% (4/4 including additional) at 1 year |
| Augmented bone height | Maintained | Maintained |
| Peri-implant bone loss | None observed | None observed |
| Postoperative complications | Transient nasal bleeding, facial swelling, hematoma (resolved) | Uneventful |
| Graft displacement | None | None |

- Nasal polyp in Case 1 showed reduction after drainage (indirect positive effect on ostial patency through membrane thickness reduction)
- Lateral window closed with regenerated hard tissue at uncovering (6 months)
- No clinical symptoms related to sinusitis at 1-year recall

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Very small sample (n=2) — cannot generalize; evidence base remains case-report level
- Short follow-up (1 year); long-term CMS recurrence risk unknown
- No endoscopic visualization of sinus cavity during drainage (limited visibility)
- No universally accepted criteria for MSFA in CMS; critical threshold of mucosal thickening not defined
- Patient selection critical: CMS with mild symptoms, infection limited to maxillary sinus only
- Requires considerable surgical experience — not for routine application

## 6. Related Work

- Modified/mini-Caldwell–Luc operation: more conservative than classic Caldwell–Luc; preserves sinus mucosa but still requires separate healing period before MSFA
- Drainage with 21-gauge aspiration needle during lateral MSFA (previous retrospective study by Park et al.): showed drainage > extraction in reducing mucosal thickness; 100% implant survival at 6-year follow-up
- One-bony-window technique for pseudocyst removal during MSFA (prior publication): large window + intentional perforation → favorable outcomes
- Unrepaired perforation non-inferior to repaired perforation (multiple retrospective studies)
- FESS + MSFA combined (combined interdisciplinary approach): requires hospitalization, GA, special instruments

## 7. Glossary

- **CMS (만성 상악동염, Chronic Maxillary Sinusitis)**: Rhinosinusitis persisting >12 weeks; unilateral involvement suggests fungal or odontogenic origin
- **MSFA (상악동거상술, Maxillary Sinus Floor Augmentation)**: Lateral-window bone grafting procedure to augment posterior maxillary bone height for implant placement
- **FESS (내시경 부비동 수술, Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery)**: Gold-standard endoscopic surgery to restore sinus clearance and ventilation
- **Schneiderian membrane (슈나이더막)**: Sinus mucoperiosteum lining the inner wall of the maxillary sinus
- **Ostium (자연공, Natural Ostium)**: Natural opening of the maxillary sinus into the nasal cavity; patency is essential for mucociliary clearance
- **MCC (점막섬모수송, Mucociliary Clearance)**: Ciliary movement of pseudostratified columnar epithelium removing mucus and debris toward the ostium
- **Osteomeatal complex (골메아틸복합체)**: Anatomic region in middle meatus through which the maxillary sinus drains; blockage leads to sinusitis
- **Prichard elevator**: Periodontal instrument repurposed here to cover the perforation during graft packing
- **SLA surface (모래분사-산처리 표면)**: Sandblasted large-grit acid-etched implant surface promoting osseointegration
- **Odontogenic sinusitis (치성 상악동염)**: CMS secondary to adjacent dental pathology (endodontic/periodontal disease, oroantral fistula, iatrogenic injury)
