## One-line Summary
SR (40 clinical studies + 16 aggregated publications): poorly controlled DM → ↑ peri-implantitis + long-term implant loss; controlled DM = similar success to healthy controls; perioperative antibiotics + CHX improve outcomes; no contraindication for controlled DM.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: International Journal of Implant Dentistry 8:1 (2022)
- DOI: 10.1186/s40729-021-00399-8
- Design: Systematic review (PRISMA), 40 clinical + 16 aggregated studies

## 2. Key Results
- Poorly controlled DM: ↑ peri-implantitis (especially early post-implantation), ↑ long-term implant loss
- Controlled DM: success rates comparable to healthy controls
- Prediabetes: possible negative effect on peri-implant disease, no effect on implant survival (limited data)
- Perioperative anti-infective therapy (antibiotics + CHX): recommended as standard → improves implant success

## 3. Methodology
- PICO: diabetic vs healthy patients with implants; complication rate endpoint
- 56 total publications included
- HbA1c threshold: ≥6.5% = DM; poorly controlled = HbA1c ≥9% (various study definitions)

## 4. Clinical Recommendations
- DM is NOT a contraindication for implants if controlled (HbA1c <8% recommended in most studies)
- Check HbA1c before treatment planning
- Perioperative antibiotics standard
- Chlorhexidine irrigation/mouthwash recommended
- Prolonged healing time expected; later loading protocols considered

## 5. Limitations
- Variable HbA1c thresholds across included studies
- Heterogeneous follow-up periods
- Prediabetes data very limited
