## Why Ingested

Part of a focused collection on vertical ridge augmentation (VRA). This RCT isolates the specific question of whether a perforated Ti-reinforced PTFE mesh needs an overlying collagen membrane — directly testing the device used in [[implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/cucchi-2024-ptfe-mesh-ti-mesh-vertical-ridge-rct]] and providing primary RCT data for the SR in [[implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/yousefi-koma-2025-titanium-mesh-collagen-membrane-vertical-ridge-sr]].

## One-line Summary

Non-inferiority RCT (n=30) of perforated Ti-reinforced d-PTFE mesh alone vs PTFE + collagen membrane (CM) for VRA: non-inferiority of PTFE-alone NOT demonstrated for absolute VBG (4.5 ± 2.1 vs 4.1 ± 2.7 mm), but outcomes were comparable (complication 6.7% both); PTFE+CM showed more type-1 pseudo-periosteum, suggesting CM helps prevent soft-tissue ingrowth.

## 한줄요약

비열등성 RCT (n=30): 천공형 Ti-보강 d-PTFE mesh 단독 vs PTFE+콜라겐막(CM) VRA 비교 — 절대 수직골증가(4.5±2.1 vs 4.1±2.7 mm)에서 PTFE 단독의 비열등성 입증 실패, 다만 합병증(양군 6.7%) 등은 유사. PTFE+CM에서 type-1 가골막 비율 높아 CM이 연조직 침입 방지에 도움 시사.

## 1. Document Information

- Study type: Parallel-group non-inferiority randomized controlled clinical trial (CONSORT, ID-COSM outcomes).
- Journal: Journal of Clinical Periodontology, 2025;52:575–588.
- DOI: 10.1111/jcpe.14129
- Registration: ClinicalTrials.gov NCT04843488; ethics 269/2019-SZTE (University of Szeged).
- Single operator (I.U.), Urban Regeneration Institute, Budapest; treatment April 2021–May 2023.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First RCT to test whether a perforated Ti-reinforced d-PTFE mesh requires an overlying collagen membrane (CM) in VRA.
- Primary outcome: absolute vertical bone gain (VBG); non-inferiority margin design (PTFE alone = test, PTFE+CM = control).
- Reports pseudo-periosteum type, bone density, regeneration rates, and complications.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 30 partially edentulous patients with vertical (>1 mm) + horizontal defects; randomized to PTFE+CM (control) or PTFE alone (test).
- Two surgical phases: VRA at T0; mesh removal + implant placement at 9 months (T1).
- Graft + perforated Ti-reinforced d-PTFE mesh; CM added over mesh in control group.
- CBCT-based assessment of VBG, effective regeneration rate, volumetric/horizontal bone gain, need for additional augmentation.
- Prophylactic amoxicillin 500 mg TID (or clindamycin 150 mg QID for penicillin allergy); full-thickness mucoperiosteal flaps with periosteal releasing incisions.
- Research hypothesis H1: PTFE alone non-inferior to PTFE+CM for absolute VBG.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Non-inferiority of PTFE alone vs PTFE+CM was NOT demonstrated for absolute VBG: 4.5 ± 2.1 mm (PTFE+CM) vs 4.1 ± 2.7 mm (PTFE alone).
- Relative VBG: 85.8% ± 10.6% (PTFE+CM) vs 79.2% ± 16.6% (PTFE alone).
- Effective regeneration rate: 72.3% ± 16.4% (PTFE+CM) vs 69.3% ± 17.9% (PTFE alone).
- Complication rate: 6.7% in both groups.
- Higher incidence of type-1 pseudo-periosteum in the PTFE+CM group.
- Conclusion: non-inferiority of PTFE alone for absolute VBG was not established; however, both techniques gave comparable VBG, complication rates, and bone density. The higher type-1 pseudo-periosteum and lacking bone volume in PTFE+CM suggests adding a CM may help prevent soft-tissue ingrowth.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample (n=30); non-inferiority not met but groups numerically comparable — possible underpowering for the chosen margin.
- Single highly experienced operator; generalizability limited.
- 9-month mesh-removal timepoint; longer-term peri-implant outcomes not the focus of this report.

## 6. Related Work

- Urban et al. 2021 (case series, 65 vertical defects with perforated PTFE + CM: 3% complications, 5.2 mm VBG, 89.2% complete fill); Alotaibi et al. 2023 (~16% GBR complication); Zhang et al. 2022 (Ti-reinforced d-PTFE superior MA); Tay et al. 2022.

## 7. Glossary

- VRA: vertical ridge augmentation
- d-PTFE: dense polytetrafluoroethylene (perforated, Ti-reinforced)
- CM: collagen membrane
- VBG: vertical bone gain; HBG: horizontal bone gain
- Pseudo-periosteum: soft-tissue layer forming under the barrier (type 1 = thin/favourable)
- ID-COSM: Implant Dentistry Core Outcome Set and Measurement
