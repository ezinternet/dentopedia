## Why Ingested

기존 위키의 IOS 정확도 페이지들([[wiki/digital-workflow/singh-2025-intraoral-scanners-accuracy-umbrella-review]], [[wiki/digital-workflow/alkadi-2023-intraoral-scanner-accuracy-factors]])은 스캐너·운용자·환자 요인 위주였고, **지대치 형성(tooth preparation) 자체가 어떻게 IOS 정확도를 좌우하는가**는 체계적으로 정리되어 있지 않았다. 본 SR(39편)은 finish line, taper(TOC), margin geometry, surface, interdental space 등 형성 요인별 정확도 영향을 정리해 그 공백을 메운다.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (39 in-vitro/in-vivo studies) finding that simpler preparation geometry, greater total occlusal convergence (TOC), anatomic occlusal reduction, supragingival/chamfer finish lines, and wider interdental space all improve intraoral scanner (IOS) accuracy for tooth-supported restorations; existing foundation restorations and apical finish-line locations reduce it.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(39편): 형성 형태가 단순할수록, 축면 경사(TOC)가 클수록, 교합면을 해부학적으로 삭제할수록, finish line이 치은연 위·chamfer 형태일수록, 인접치와의 간격이 넓을수록 구강스캐너(IOS) 정확도가 좋아지고, 기존 코어 수복물·치은연하 finish line은 정확도를 떨어뜨린다.

- **Title**: Tooth preparation-related factors that impact the accuracy of intraoral scanners for fabricating tooth-supported restorations: A systematic review
- **Authors**: Marta Revilla-León, Panagiotis Ntovas, Junying Li, Abdul Basir Barmak, John C. Kois, Miguel Gómez-Polo
- **Journal**: The Journal of Prosthetic Dentistry 2025;134(5):1555.e1-1555.e20 (November 2025)
- **Type**: Systematic review (no meta-analysis)
- **Databases**: PubMed/Medline, Scopus, Embase, Web of Science, Cochrane + manual search
- **Appraisal**: Joanna Briggs Institute critical appraisal (2 independent reviewers, third for consensus)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic synthesis dedicated to **tooth preparation-related factors** (rather than scanner/operator/patient factors) affecting IOS accuracy for tooth-supported (crown/inlay/onlay/FPD) restorations.
- Organizes 39 included articles into 5 preparation-related factor categories:
  1. Preparation geometry — complexity, total occlusal convergence (TOC), occlusal preparation design
  2. Restorations (foundation/core build-ups or adjacent restorations)
  3. Surface finishing
  4. Finish line location and design
  5. Interdental space

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Studies analyzing any tooth-preparation-related factor on IOS accuracy (trueness/precision) for tooth-supported restorations were included.
- Accuracy reported as trueness and precision (deviation in µm) where available.
- Heterogeneity in scanners, materials, and measurement protocols precluded meta-analysis; narrative synthesis by factor category.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Preparation geometry**: The simpler the geometry, the greater the TOC, and the more anatomic the occlusal reduction → the better scanability and IOS accuracy.
- **Foundation/existing restorations**: Existing restorations reduce IOS accuracy. Among composite resin foundation materials tested in crown preparations, accuracy discrepancy was ≤5 µm.
- **Surface finishing**: 19-µm trueness and 5-µm precision discrepancy measured among different finishing procedures.
- **Finish line location**: The more apical the finish line, the lower the IOS accuracy. Apical/width gingival displacement from displacement cords dramatically improved IOS accuracy, especially with intracrevicular finish lines.
- **Finish line design**: Chamfer finish line design had the best accuracy values.
- **Interdental space**: The greater the space between the preparation and adjacent teeth, the better the IOS accuracy.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Predominantly in-vitro studies; clinical generalizability limited.
- High heterogeneity → no meta-analysis.
- Limited data on interdental space precluded determining an optimal threshold for maximizing IOS accuracy.

## 6. Related Work

- Builds on the umbrella/SR body on IOS accuracy (operator, patient, scanner factors) by isolating the preparation variable.
- Companion to author's own guides on maximizing IOS accuracy (operator/patient factors) and parameters for tooth-supported restorations.

## 7. Glossary

- **IOS**: Intraoral scanner.
- **Trueness**: Closeness of measured surface to the true/reference geometry.
- **Precision**: Reproducibility between repeated scans.
- **TOC (Total Occlusal Convergence)**: Combined angle of opposing axial walls of a preparation.
- **Finish line**: Cervical margin of the preparation (chamfer, shoulder, etc.).
- **Foundation restoration**: Core build-up beneath a crown preparation.
