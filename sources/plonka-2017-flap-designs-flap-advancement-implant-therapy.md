## One-line Summary

Systematic review (PubMed + manual search, English articles Jan 1990–Sep 2015) categorizing GBR flap designs by required advancement — minor (<3 mm), moderate (3–6 mm), major (≥7 mm) — and the incision/release/suturing techniques that achieve passive primary closure under the PASS principle.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Categorizes flap designs by the amount of advancement required for primary passive closure during GBR, building on Greenstein et al. (2009) strata: minor (<3 mm), moderate (3–6 mm), major (≥7 mm).
- Reviews material types, incision design, reflection, releasing, and suturing techniques.
- Frames GBR success around the "PASS" principle: Primary wound closure, Angiogenesis, Stability, Space maintenance.
- Quantifies incremental advancement contributions: one vertical incision +1.1 mm, second vertical incision +1.9 mm, periosteal releasing incision +5.5 mm from baseline (per Park et al.).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Two reviewers (A.P., R.S.) searched PubMed and manually searched relevant journals (IJOMI, Clinical Oral Implants Research, Implant Dentistry, Journal of Periodontology, IJPRD).
- Search terms included "flap designs," "guided bone regeneration," "ridge augmentation," "socket augmentation."
- Inclusion: English-language articles January 1990 – September 2015.
- Flap designs grouped by ability to achieve flap extension per Greenstein et al. (2009).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Advancement strata and recommended techniques:
  - Minor (<3 mm): full-thickness reflection beyond the mucogingival junction (MGJ).
  - Moderate (3–6 mm): two vertical releasing incisions, plus periosteal scoring ~1 mm depth as needed.
  - Major (≥7 mm): deeper periosteal scoring 3–5 mm into submucosa; split-thickness flaps to extend coverage.
- Specific mild-advancement (1–3 mm) designs reviewed: esthetic buccal flap (immediate implants, esthetic zone, buccal-plate fenestration) and mucogingival pouch flap (buccal dehiscence/fenestration grafting without splitting the flap).
- Complication context: membrane exposure is the most common GBR complication; vertical GBR complication rate 0–45.5% vs horizontal 0–24%; membrane-exposure range 6.95–13.1%. Exposure can cut regeneration substantially (e.g., ePTFE exposure reduced regeneration from 96.6% to 46.5%; ~6× more bone gain in non-exposed vs exposed sites per Machtei meta-analysis).
- Immediate implant placement associated with higher wound opening (80%) vs delayed (17–32%).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative/qualitative synthesis without meta-analysis or formal risk-of-bias appraisal.
- Search window ends September 2015 (now dated; newer technique-specific evidence exists).
- Recommendations are largely technique-categorization and expert-derived rather than pooled effect estimates.

## 7. Glossary

- GBR: Guided Bone Regeneration.
- PASS: Primary closure, Angiogenesis, Stability, Space maintenance — the four properties for GBR success.
- MGJ: Mucogingival Junction.
- PRI: Periosteal Releasing Incision — scoring the periosteum to mobilize a full-thickness flap.
- Split-thickness flap: partial-thickness dissection leaving periosteum on bone, used to gain additional coverage.
- ePTFE: expanded polytetrafluoroethylene non-resorbable membrane.
