## One-line Summary

In vitro 4-arm comparison (n=40 extracted primary incisors) showed NTPP achieved the highest E. faecalis colony reduction (98.79%) vs CHX, diode laser, and propolis in primary root canals.

## 한줄요약

유치 전치 40개 in vitro 실험에서 비열 압력 플라스마(NTPP)가 E. faecalis 군집 감소율 98.79%로 CHX·다이오드 레이저·프로폴리스 중 최고 항균 효과를 보임.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health
- **Year**: 2025 (published online 2024)
- **Volume/Page**: 25:54
- **DOI**: 10.1186/s12903-024-05349-5
- **Study type**: In vitro experimental (4-group parallel comparison)
- **Institution**: Al Azhar University, Faculty of Dental Medicine for Girls, Cairo, Egypt; National Center for Radiation Research and Technology, Egyptian Atomic Energy Authority; Badr University in Cairo

## 2. Key Contributions

- First study to directly compare NTPP (non-thermal pressure plasma) with three conventional/alternative disinfection modalities (CHX, diode laser 810 nm, propolis) in primary anterior root canals
- Demonstrated NTPP superiority with 98.79% colony-forming unit (CFU) reduction of E. faecalis, a key endodontic pathogen resistant to standard chemo-mechanical treatment
- Established that gamma irradiation (25 kGy) is a reliable standardization method for ensuring true bacterial contamination baseline
- Highlighted NTPP's mechanism (reactive oxygen/nitrogen species, charged particles) as effective in the enclosed root canal space
- All tested methods achieved statistically significant reduction (p < 0.001 for CHX, diode laser, NTPP; p = 0.035 for propolis)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Study design**: 40 single-rooted primary anterior teeth extracted at Al Azhar University outpatient dental clinics. Written parental consent obtained. Ethics approval: REC-PD-24-03.

**Specimen preparation**:
- Ultrasonic cleaning + 1% NaOCl disinfection + thymol storage
- Access cavity prepared with round bur/tapered diamond bur
- Pulp extirpation (H-file), rotary preparation (Kido-S rotary files)
- Smear layer removal: 1% NaOCl (organic) + 17% EDTA (inorganic), 0.9% saline rinse
- Decoronation below CEJ → standardized 8 mm root length
- Apical foramen sealed with light-cured composite resin

**Sterilization**: Gamma irradiation (Cobalt-60, 1.774 kGy/h dose rate, total 25 kGy, Egyptian Atomic Energy Authority)

**Sterility verification**: Nutrient agar (gram+), MacConkey agar (gram−), Sabouraud dextrose agar (fungal) — all plates incubated 37°C × 24 h

**Bacterial inoculation**: E. faecalis suspension injected into canals

**Groups (n=10 each)**:
- Group I: Chlorhexidine (CHX) irrigation
- Group II: Diode laser (810 nm)
- Group III: Ethanolic extract of propolis (100% w/v, macerated in 96% ethanol)
- Group IV: NTPP (non-thermal pressure plasma jet)

**Outcome measure**: CFU count before vs. after treatment; log reduction values (mean ± SD)

**Statistical analysis**: One-way ANOVA, G*Power 3.1.9.4 (α=0.05, β=0.2, n=10/group)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Group | Log CFU reduction (mean ± SD) | % CFU reduction |
|---|---|---|
| CHX | significant (p<0.001) | — |
| Diode laser | significant (p<0.001) | — |
| Propolis | significant (p=0.035) | 81.99% (lowest) |
| **NTPP** | **4.06 ± 0.88** | **98.79% (highest)** |

- NTPP produced the highest log CFU reduction (4.06 ± 0.88) — statistically the best performer
- Propolis showed the least reduction (81.99%) with the weakest statistical significance (p=0.035)
- All four modalities reduced colony counts significantly
- NTPP mechanism: reactive species (ROS/RNS), UV photons, electric field, and charged particle bombardment acting synergistically on bacterial membranes

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In vitro only (extracted teeth); direct clinical extrapolation requires in vivo validation
- Small n=10 per group
- Single pathogen tested (E. faecalis); multispecies biofilm not assessed
- Propolis preparation method (100% w/v ethanolic extract) not standardized across the literature — concentration varies widely
- Plasma device parameters (jet type, exposure time, distance) not fully detailed — reproducibility unclear
- No cytotoxicity/biocompatibility assessment of NTPP on periapical tissues of primary teeth, whose apical foramina may communicate with permanent tooth follicle
- Clinical NTPP delivery into narrow pediatric root canals (especially curved canals) requires specialized catheter-tip adaptation

## 7. Glossary

- **NTPP** (Non-Thermal Pressure Plasma): plasma generated at atmospheric pressure without significant thermal energy transfer; fourth state of matter releasing reactive oxygen/nitrogen species (ROS/RNS) with antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and tissue-repair properties
- **E. faecalis**: gram-positive facultative anaerobe; principal persistent endodontic pathogen resistant to standard irrigation
- **CFU** (Colony-Forming Units): quantitative bacterial culture measure
- **CHX** (Chlorhexidine): broad-spectrum cationic biguanide irrigant; gold alternative to NaOCl for primary teeth (avoids follicle toxicity)
- **Diode laser (810 nm)**: bactericidal via photothermal effect penetrating >1 mm through dentin
- **Propolis**: natural bee-derived wax-like substance with broad biomedical antimicrobial/regenerative properties
- **Gamma irradiation (25 kGy)**: standard sterilization method for research specimens
