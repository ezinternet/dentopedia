## One-line Summary
KU Leuven µTBS study: 2-step SE Clearfil SE Bond 2 maintains bond strength to saliva-contaminated dentin without decontamination; 1-step SE Clearfil Universal Bond Quick requires either Katana Cleaner or CSE2 primer for full recovery.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: The Journal of Adhesive Dentistry 2025;27:65–74
- **DOI**: 10.3290/j.jad.c_1966
- **Institution**: KU Leuven (BIOMAT), Belgium — Van Meerbeek group
- **Design**: In vitro µTBS (micro-tensile bond strength) with linear mixed modeling

## 2. Key Contributions
- Quantifies a clinically common problem (saliva contamination during bonding) for two leading Kuraray adhesives
- Demonstrates 2-step SE Clearfil SE Bond 2 (CSE2) is robust to saliva contamination without separate decontamination — major clinical implication
- 1-step SE Clearfil Universal Bond Quick (CUBQ) requires decontamination (Katana Cleaner or CSE2 primer) for full recovery
- Supports Katana Cleaner (10-MDP–containing decontaminant) as effective intraoral cleaning agent for saliva-contaminated dentin

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: In vitro µTBS comparative
- **Adhesives**: Clearfil SE Bond 2 (2-step SE, "CSE2") vs Clearfil Universal Bond Quick (1-step SE, "CUBQ"); both Kuraray Noritake
- **Decontamination protocols**:
  - Katana Cleaner ("KC", Kuraray) — 10-MDP-containing decontaminant
  - CSE2 primer ("CSE2p") as decontamination
  - Negative control: saliva-contaminated, no decontamination ("saliva(–)")
  - Positive control: clean, no saliva ("clean(+)")
- **Substrate**: Bur-cut human dentin
- **Aging**: Immediate (1-week water storage) vs aged (50,000 thermocycles)
- **Statistics**: Linear mixed modeling (LMM) with REML estimation, α=0.05

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **CSE2 (2-step SE)**: outperformed CUBQ overall; satisfactory bond strength even WITHOUT separate decontamination
- **CUBQ (1-step SE)**: bond strength reduced by saliva contamination without decontamination; restored to acceptable levels by KC or CSE2p
- **Best decontamination protocol**: CSE2p, closely followed by KC
- **Clinical recommendation**:
  - With CSE2: no additional decontamination strictly required
  - With CUBQ: decontamination strongly recommended (KC or CSE2p)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- In vitro only — no clinical confirmation
- Two adhesives from one manufacturer (Kuraray); generalizability limited
- µTBS surrogate for clinical retention; clinical RCTs needed
- Single contamination scenario (saliva); blood/sulcular fluid not tested
- Bur-cut dentin only; carious or sclerotic dentin substrate behavior unknown

## 6. Related Work
- Aligns with prior Van Meerbeek work on adhesive technique sensitivity
- Complements Hardan 2021 (universal adhesive dentin bond strength SR/MA)
- Adjacent to Awad 2022 / Genc 2025 (zirconia decontamination with Katana Cleaner)
- Reaffirms differential robustness of 2-step over 1-step SE — consistent with classical Van Meerbeek classification

## 7. Glossary
- **10-MDP (10-Methacryloyloxydecyl Dihydrogen Phosphate)**: Functional monomer with strong chemical bond to hydroxyapatite, zirconia, metal oxides
- **µTBS (Micro-Tensile Bond Strength, 미세인장결합강도)**: Bond strength test on ~1mm² beam specimens
- **Saliva contamination**: Common during isolation failure; reduces bond by lowering surface energy + chemical interference
- **Katana Cleaner (KC)**: Kuraray 10-MDP-containing decontaminant; both extraoral and intraoral use
- **Clearfil SE Bond 2 (CSE2)**: Gold-standard 2-step self-etch adhesive
- **Clearfil Universal Bond Quick (CUBQ)**: 1-step universal adhesive from same manufacturer
- **LMM / REML**: Linear mixed modeling / restricted maximum likelihood — handles nested experimental designs
