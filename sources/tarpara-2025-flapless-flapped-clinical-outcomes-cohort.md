## Why Ingested

Part of the user's flapless-implant PubMed ingest. Paired with [[implants/surendra-2025-flapless-versus-flapped-crestal-bone]] (RCT), this non-randomized cohort gives the **opposite crestal-bone verdict** — flapless and flapped lost bone equally at 12 months — so the two pages together show how design and follow-up shape the answer. It also adds the pain / probing-depth dimension that the immediate-implant flapless syntheses ([[immediate-implant/pitman-2023-immediate-implant-flap-flapless-sr-ma]]) and ridge-preservation flap papers ([[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/araujo-2009-ridge-alterations-flap-vs-flapless]]) do not cover for healed-ridge placement.

## One-line Summary

Prospective non-randomized cohort (n=20, single posterior implants, 12-month post-load) finding flapless placement gave significantly less postoperative pain and lower probing depth at 6 months, but **no difference in crestal bone height** vs the flapped technique at any time point.

## 한줄요약

단일 구치부 임플란트 비무작위 코호트 (n=20, 부하 후 12개월)에서 무피판(flapless)이 술후 통증·6개월 탐침깊이는 유의하게 낮았으나, 치조정 골높이(CBH)는 피판군과 어느 시점에도 차이 없음.

## 1. Document Information

- Cureus, 2025 (published 2025-04-18).
- PMID 40390720; PMCID PMC12086267; DOI 10.7759/cureus.82547.
- Single-center prospective cohort, Maharaja Ganga Singh Dental College, Sri Ganganagar, Rajasthan; data Jan 2023–Jan 2025.
- Ethics MGSDC|SY/23/05; Declaration of Helsinki.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Separates **early** (pain, soft-tissue) benefits of flapless from **long-term hard-tissue** outcomes, showing the crestal advantage does not persist.
- Multi-domain outcome set: VAS pain, four-site probing depth, ImageJ-quantified crestal bone height over 12 months post functional loading.
- Pragmatic, real-world allocation by biotype/ridge/surgeon judgment rather than randomization — externally valid but bias-prone.
- Reinforces that postoperative pain and early probing depth, not bone level, are where flapless differs.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Design: prospective cohort, **non-randomized**; allocation by clinical judgment (thick biotype + adequate keratinized mucosa + favorable ridge → flapless; thin biotype / need for visualization → flap).
- Population: 20 adults (18–60 y), single posterior endosseous implant, ≥6 mm buccolingual width, ≥10 mm height, ≥2 mm keratinized mucosa; standard medical exclusions; non-smokers.
- Sample size: G*Power, 80% power, α=5%, effect size 0.85 → 10/group.
- Protocol: delayed placement (≥3 mo post-extraction), delayed loading; Adin implants; flapless via tissue punch (osteotomy 800–1000 rpm) with 2 mm healing collar vs full-thickness flap with cover screw + 3-0 silk sutures (removed day 7); amoxicillin-clavulanate prophylaxis.
- Outcomes: VAS pain days 3 and 7; probing depth at 6 and 12 mo (4 sites, plastic UNC-15); crestal bone height on standardized IOPA (Rinn XCP, ImageJ) at baseline / 6 / 12 mo post-load. Single blinded examiner, ICC 0.85.
- Statistics: Stata 18; t-tests for normal VAS/PD, Mann-Whitney U + Friedman for non-normal CBH; α=0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Postoperative pain (VAS): day 3 flapless 3.1±0.74 vs flap 5.7±0.95 (p=0.001); day 7 flapless 0.5±0.71 vs flap 2.4±1.17 (p=0.001).
- Probing depth at 6 months significantly lower for flapless at all 4 sites (e.g., MB 3.43±0.42 vs 3.96±0.57, p=0.030); at 12 months differences NOT significant (p>0.05).
- Crestal bone height: no significant intergroup difference at baseline, 6, or 12 months (all p>0.05); significant intragroup loss over time in both arms (Friedman p=0.001).
- Authors note the 6-month PD difference, though significant, stays within normal physiological range — limited clinical relevance.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample (n=20) and **non-randomized** allocation → selection bias; underpowered for subtle hard-tissue differences.
- 12-month follow-up; 2D radiography (no CBCT); VAS subjectivity; single center.
- Sex and arch location not stratified.

## 6. Related Work

- Cites Gao et al. systematic review (14 RCTs): flapless lower VAS at 24 h (attenuating after day 3) and lower PD, comparable bone-level changes — concordant with this cohort's crestal finding.
- Directly contradicts the RCT crestal advantage of [[implants/surendra-2025-flapless-versus-flapped-crestal-bone]].

## 7. Glossary

- **Crestal bone height (CBH):** distance from a fixed apical reference to the most coronal bone-implant contact; here measured mesial + distal on standardized IOPA via ImageJ.
- **Probing depth (PD):** sulcular depth around the implant restoration at four sites; surrogate for peri-implant soft-tissue health.
- **Non-randomized cohort:** groups formed by clinical/operator decision rather than random assignment — pragmatic but subject to confounding by indication.
