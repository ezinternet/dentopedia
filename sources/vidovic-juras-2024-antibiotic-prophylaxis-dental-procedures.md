## One-line Summary
Narrative review (2024): antibiotic prophylaxis indications significantly narrowed due to antibiotic resistance concerns; currently indicated for IE high-risk, immunocompromised, IV bisphosphonate/antiangiogenic, irradiated head/neck; prosthetic joints no longer routine.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Dentistry Journal 12(11):364 (November 2024)
- DOI: 10.3390/dj12110364
- Design: Narrative review

## 2. Current Indications for AP (Narrowed List)

| Indication | Current Recommendation |
|---|---|
| IE high-risk (4 AHA categories) | ✅ Required — Amoxicillin 2g PO |
| Prosthetic joints | ❌ Not routinely recommended (AAOS/ADA 2012) |
| Immunocompromised (HIV, leukemia, transplant, chemotherapy) | ✅ Case-by-case |
| IV bisphosphonate / antiangiogenic therapy | ✅ Recommended for surgical procedures |
| Head/neck irradiation (>50 Gy) | ✅ Recommended for surgical procedures |
| Healthy patients — routine extraction | ❌ Not indicated |
| Poorly controlled DM alone | ⚠️ Case-by-case (not standard) |

## 3. Antibiotic of Choice
- First line: **Amoxicillin** (β-lactam) — 2g PO 30–60 min before
- Penicillin allergy: Clindamycin 600mg OR azithromycin 500mg
- Cannot take PO: Ampicillin IV/IM

## 4. Antibiotic Resistance Context
- Overuse of prophylactic antibiotics major contributor to resistance
- No prophylaxis for low-risk patients (even with prosthetic joints, well-controlled DM, healthy elderly)
- Single pre-procedure dose; post-procedure antibiotics not indicated for prophylaxis

## 5. Limitations
- Narrative review; some institutional variation in protocols
- Rapidly evolving guidelines (especially for prosthetic joints and DM)
