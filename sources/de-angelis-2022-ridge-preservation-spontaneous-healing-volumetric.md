## Why Ingested

기존 [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/fischer-2022-bone-envelope-arp-dbbm-sr-ma]] (SR+MA, DBBM ARP의 dimensional benefit)와 [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/sanchez-perez-2025-dbbm-alveolar-ridge-preservation-histologic]] (조직학적 근거)가 제시한 ARP의 차원 보존 효과를, CBCT 중첩 기반 **volumetric** + linear + PES + 환자만족도로 직접 정량화한 단일치 retrospective 비교(45명). xenograft+resorbable membrane ARP가 spontaneous healing 대비 HW-1 수축을 절반 이하(0.86 vs 2.03 mm)로 줄이고 후속 grafting 필요를 감소시킴을 보강하는 임상 데이터로 활용.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective comparative study (n=45 single-tooth sites; 23 spontaneous healing vs 22 ARP with bovine xenograft + resorbable pericardium membrane) using 6-month CBCT volumetric superimposition: ARP roughly halved horizontal ridge loss at 1 mm (0.86 ± 0.49 vs 2.03 ± 0.54 mm, P < 0.001), reduced volumetric shrinkage (62.66 vs 106.41 mm³, P = 0.004), improved PES (11.6 vs 9.9, P = 0.007), and cut later additional grafting (9% vs 26%, NS P = 0.11), with equal pain and patient satisfaction and 100% 1-year implant survival in both groups.

## 한줄요약

후향적 비교연구(단일치 발치 45부위; 자연치유 23 vs 소뼈 xenograft+흡수성 심막막 ARP 22). 6개월 CBCT 중첩 체적분석에서 ARP가 1 mm 수평폭 수축을 절반 수준으로(0.86 vs 2.03 mm, P<0.001), 체적 수축도 감소(62.66 vs 106.41 mm³, P=0.004)시켰고, PES 향상(11.6 vs 9.9, P=0.007)·후속 추가 골이식 감소(9% vs 26%, NS P=0.11)를 보였으나 통증·환자만족도는 양군 동등, 양군 모두 1년 임플란트 생존율 100%.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Retrospective comparative clinical study
- **Journal**: International Journal of Implant Dentistry 2022;8:62
- **Setting**: Division of Oral Surgery and Implantology, Catholic University of the Sacred Heart, Rome, Italy (single center)
- **Funding/Conflicts**: Open Access (CC BY 4.0); not stated in extracted text
- **Sample**: 45 patients (21 F / 24 M; mean age 50.7 ± 12.61 y) each receiving a single-tooth extraction with planned implant-prosthetic rehabilitation (extraction reasons: caries 53%, fracture 36%, trauma 7%, external root resorption 4%)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Direct head-to-head of spontaneous socket healing (SH) vs ARP (xenograft + resorbable membrane) in single-tooth sites with **CBCT-based 3D volumetric superimposition** (T0 = 1 week pre-extraction, T1 = 6 months post-extraction), not just linear measurement.
- Quantifies both linear (HW at 1/3/5 mm; buccal & palatal vertical height) and volumetric ridge change, plus downstream esthetic (PES), patient-reported (pain, satisfaction), and clinical (need for additional grafting, implant survival) outcomes.
- Group allocation by patient choice (patients refusing ARP were treated with SH) — pragmatic but non-randomized (key limitation).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Groups**: SH (n=23) = socket left to heal by secondary intention after curettage/irrigation, margins stabilized with 4-0 PGA resorbable suture. ARP (n=22) = alveolus packed with cortico-spongious bovine granules (Re-Bone, UBGEN; particle 0.25–1 mm, low-temp processed to avoid ceramization), covered with resorbable bovine pericardium membrane (Shelter Fast, UBGEN) under buccal/lingual flap, compressive sutures.
- **Imaging**: CBCT 1 week before and 6 months after extraction; digital models of the two time points superimposed for volumetric analysis. Intraoral radiography at implant placement, prosthesis delivery, and 1-year.
- **Linear measures**: Horizontal width at 1, 3, 5 mm below the most coronal ridge aspect (HW-1/3/5); vertical buccal (BH) and palatal (PH) height; preoperative buccal bone thickness (BBT-1/3/5).
- **Baseline phenotype**: Facial soft tissue thickness (FSTT) probed 1 mm apical to mid-buccal margin (thick >1 mm vs thin ≤1 mm); preoperative buccal bone thickness on CBCT (thick vs thin).
- **Outcomes**: Primary = vertical & horizontal ridge change at 6 months. Secondary = soft-tissue volumetric change, patient-perceived pain, PES (esthetic, 1 year post-prosthesis), patient satisfaction (12 months).
- **Implant timing**: Second-stage implant surgery after the 6-month follow-up.
- **Postop**: 0.12% chlorhexidine BID ×14 d, amoxicillin 1 g q12h ×6 d, ketoprofen 50 mg q12h ×3–4 d.
- **Statistics**: Independent-samples and paired t-tests; STATA; α = 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Horizontal width loss (6 mo)** — ARP < SH at every level (P < 0.001):
  - HW-1: SH 2.03 ± 0.54 vs ARP 0.86 ± 0.49 mm
  - HW-3: SH 1.35 ± 0.50 vs ARP 0.55 ± 0.42 mm
  - HW-5: SH 0.94 ± 0.46 vs ARP 0.46 ± 0.37 mm
- **Volumetric change**: SH 106.41 ± 24.32 mm³ vs ARP 62.66 ± 17.50 mm³ (P = 0.004); significant reduction vs baseline within both groups (P < 0.001).
- **Vertical change (mm)**: SH buccal 0.9 ± 0.7 / palatal 0.75 ± 0.04; ARP buccal 0.31 ± 0.1 / palatal 0.25 ± 0.03 — intergroup differences significant (P < 0.05).
- **Modifiers (independent t-test)**: In both groups, thick preoperative FSTT and thick buccal bone → significantly less HW-1 loss. In SH, **flapless** (no flap) → less HW-1 loss (flap elevation → larger loss, P < 0.001). In ARP, occurrence of complications → worse HW-1 (P < 0.001).
- **PES (1 year)**: ARP 11.6 ± 2.2 vs SH 9.9 ± 1.8 (P = 0.007).
- **Patient satisfaction (12 mo)**: ARP 8 ± 1.2 vs SH 8.1 ± 1.1 (P = 0.79, NS). Pain: no significant difference (P = 0.89).
- **Additional grafting before/at implant placement**: ARP 9% (2/22, transcrestal sinus lift) vs SH 26% (6/23); P = 0.11 (NS, trend favoring ARP).
- **Complications**: 2 ARP patients (9%) delayed wound healing, no infection, no adjunctive therapy. No peri-implantitis. 1-year implant survival 100% in both groups.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Retrospective, non-randomized** — allocation by patient preference (those refusing ARP → SH) → selection/confounding bias.
- Single center, small sample (22–23 per arm); the additional-grafting difference (9% vs 26%) was not statistically significant (P = 0.11), likely underpowered.
- Mixed anterior/posterior sites (majority posterior, ~65–73%); ARP esthetic benefit (PES) most relevant to anterior, where the sample is small.
- Short follow-up (6 mo for ridge; 1 y for implant/PES); long-term peri-implant and esthetic stability unknown.
- FSTT measured by endodontic-file probing (operator-dependent), single xenograft/membrane brand — limits generalizability.

## 6. Related Work

- Tonetti et al. — ARP can prevent ~1.5–2.4 mm horizontal and ~0.8–2.5 mm vertical resorption (cited as benchmark).
- Araujo et al. (2013) — bone loss magnitude depends on buccal wall thickness, tooth position/angulation, surgical trauma, flap elevation (thin facial bone <1 mm in ~90% of anterior maxilla).
- Consistent with SR/MA evidence that ARP (xenograft/allograft + barrier) reduces dimensional ridge change vs spontaneous healing.

## 7. Glossary

- **ARP**: Alveolar Ridge Preservation — grafting/sealing of the fresh socket to limit post-extraction dimensional change.
- **SH**: Spontaneous Healing — socket left to heal by secondary intention without grafting.
- **HW-1/3/5**: Horizontal ridge width measured 1, 3, 5 mm below the most coronal ridge crest.
- **FSTT**: Facial Soft Tissue Thickness (thick >1 mm / thin ≤1 mm).
- **PES**: Pink Esthetic Score — esthetic assessment of peri-implant soft tissue (0–14).
- **CBCT**: Cone Beam Computed Tomography.
- **DBBM**: Deanorganic Bovine Bone Mineral (bovine xenograft class).
