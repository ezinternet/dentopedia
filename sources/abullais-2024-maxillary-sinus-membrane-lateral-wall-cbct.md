## Why Ingested

상악동 거상술 천공 방지 (Sinus Membrane Perforation Prevention) 지식 클러스터의 해부학적 기준치 보강용. 기존 [[sinus-lift/lateral/munakata-2021-sinus-membrane-thickness-edentulous-cbct]]는 무치악 코호트(일본)에서 SMT를 보고했고, [[sinus-lift/lateral/manor-2024-cbct-prognostic-factors-sinus-perforation]]은 천공 예측인자를 분석했으나 측벽 두께(Lateral Wall Thickness, LWT)와 얼굴 지수(Facial Index)의 관계를 다룬 연구가 부재. 본 연구(Abullais 2024, 사우디 코호트 n=75)는 얼굴형(안면 지수)이 MT·LWT에 영향을 주지 않음을 보여 [[wiki/overviews/sinus-lift-lateral-2026-synthesis]]의 Cluster 2 해부학 섹션에 "인구집단 기준치"로 추가 가능.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective CBCT study (n=75, 150 sinuses, Saudi Arabia) showing no correlation between facial index (euryprosopic/mesoprosopic/leptoprosopic) and maxillary sinus membrane thickness (MT) or lateral wall thickness (LWT); LWT was significantly greater in females (p<0.05), and premolar MT was significantly thicker than molar MT (p<0.001).

## 한줄요약

후향적 원뿔빔 전산화 단층 촬영 (Cone-Beam Computed Tomography, CBCT) 연구 (n=75, 150 상악동, 사우디아라비아): 안면지수 (Facial Index) 유형(euryprosopic/mesoprosopic/leptoprosopic)이 상악동 막 두께 (Membrane Thickness, MT) 및 측벽 두께 (Lateral Wall Thickness, LWT)와 상관없음; 여성이 남성보다 LWT 유의하게 두꺼움(p<0.05); 소구치 영역 MT가 대구치 영역보다 유의하게 두꺼움(p<0.001).

- **Journal**: PLoS ONE 19(3): e0298403
- **Year**: 2024 (Published March 25, 2024)
- **DOI**: 10.1371/journal.pone.0298403
- **Study type**: Retrospective radiographic image analysis (cross-sectional)
- **Sample**: 75 subjects (39 male, 36 female), 150 maxillary sinuses; mean age 32.62±14.22 years (range 20–60); King Khalid University, Abha, Saudi Arabia
- **Imaging**: Kavo 3D Pro CBCT; 89 kVp, 9.0 mA, 16 sec; Nov 2019–Dec 2020

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Established that facial index (euryprosopic, mesoprosopic, leptoprosopic) has **no significant correlation** with MT or LWT — surgeons cannot use facial morphotype as a surrogate predictor.
2. Quantified mean MT: premolar 1.46±0.49 mm vs molar 1.17±0.53 mm (p<0.001) — premolar zone carries thicker membrane, possibly relevant to window placement choice.
3. LWT was significantly greater in females vs males (premolar p=0.018; molar p=0.032), with no corresponding sex difference in MT.
4. Age group 40–49 years had the thinnest MT in the premolar region (p=0.021) — a nuance for middle-aged patients.
5. Supports the established message: CBCT is indispensable for site-specific pre-surgical planning regardless of gross facial morphology.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Study design**: Retrospective image analysis
- **IRB**: Approved (KKU IRB/KKUCOD/ETH/2019-20/074)
- **Imaging software**: Cliniview v11.10.1 (3DOnDemand, GE Healthcare)
- **Facial index formula**: (Morphological facial height N–Gn) / Bizygomatic width Zyr–Zyl × 100
- **Facial types** (Bannister/Williams classification): hypereuryprosopic (<79%), euryprosopic (80–84.9%), mesoprosopic (85–89.9%), leptoprosopic (90–94.9%), hyperleptoprosopic (>95%); in this Saudi sample only euryprosopic, mesoprosopic, leptoprosopic were represented.
- **Measurement points**: MT and LWT measured at 3 points, each 3 mm apart from the sinus floor, in premolar and molar regions (coronal CBCT sections)
- **Inter-observer**: Three examiners, measured twice with 10-day interval
- **Statistics**: Independent t-test, ANOVA, Pearson correlation; α=0.05

- MT at age 40–49 years was significantly thinner in premolar region (p=0.021); no significant age effect on LWT.
- Facial index distribution: ~44% leptoprosopic, ~35% mesoprosopic, ~21% euryprosopic; no hyper- types observed.
- Strong positive correlation between MT premolar and MT molar (r=0.534, p<0.001).
- LWT in molar region significantly correlated with side (right vs left, p=0.009).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-center, single-ethnicity (Saudi) sample: MT and LWT values may differ across populations; compare with Munakata 2021 (Japanese edentulous) and Lee 2020 (Korean young adults).
- Moderate sample size (n=75 subjects, 150 sinuses); retrospective design (no randomization).
- Exclusion of subjects with sinus pathology or heavy smokers may underestimate real-world variance.
- The risk of asymptomatic chronic sinusitis inflating MT not fully excluded.
- High-altitude population (Abha, KSA) may systematically differ from sea-level populations.
- Future: multicenter, multiethnic prospective designs; link MT/LWT values directly to intraoperative perforation events.

## 6. Related Work

- Munakata 2021: MT in edentulous Japanese cohort (mean 1.09±1.30 mm) — similar ranges, different ethnic/dentition context
- Lee 2020: 3D CBCT inferior sinus dimensions in Korean young adults; sex differences in sinus volume
- Manor 2024: CBCT predictors of perforation — floor width most significant factor, not MT alone
- Yang 2024 (SR+MA): LWT >2 mm and septa increase perforation OR
- Monje 2016: LWT ≥1.25 mm as threshold for reamer-drill approach

## 7. Glossary

- **MT (Membrane Thickness, 막 두께)**: Schneiderian (sinus mucosal) membrane thickness measured by CBCT
- **LWT (Lateral Wall Thickness, 측벽 두께)**: Bony lateral wall thickness measured by CBCT at the sinus augmentation window site
- **Facial Index (안면지수)**: Morphological facial height / bizygomatic width × 100; classifies facial form into euryprosopic (broad) to leptoprosopic (long)
- **Euryprosopic (광안형)**: Broad facial type (facial index 80–84.9%)
- **Mesoprosopic (중안형)**: Round/intermediate facial type (85–89.9%)
- **Leptoprosopic (협안형)**: Long/narrow facial type (90–94.9%)
- **Nasion (비근점)**: Midpoint of the nasofrontal suture
- **Gnathion (이부점)**: Lowest midline point on the mandible
- **Zygion (관골점)**: Most lateral point on the zygomatic arch
