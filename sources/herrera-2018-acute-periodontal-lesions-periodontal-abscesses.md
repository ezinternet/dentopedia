## One-line Summary
2017 World Workshop review proposing classification frameworks for three acute/combined periodontal conditions — periodontal abscesses (by etiology), necrotizing periodontal diseases (by host immune status), and endo-periodontal lesions (by fractures/perforations and presence of periodontitis).

## 한줄요약
2017 World Workshop review — 급성·복합 치주병변 3종의 분류틀 제안: 치주농양(병인별), 괴사성 치주질환(숙주 면역상태별), 치수치주복합병변(파절·천공 유무 및 치주염 동반 여부별).

## 1. Document Information
- J Periodontol 2018;89(Suppl 1):S85–S102 (jointly in J Clin Periodontol). DOI 10.1002/JPER.16-0642.
- 2017 World Workshop on Classification of Periodontal and Peri-Implant Diseases and Conditions.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Etiology-based classification of periodontal abscesses (PA), most often in pre-existing pockets.
- Links necrotizing periodontal diseases (NPD) to host immune response as a classification axis.
- Classifies endo-periodontal lesions (EPL) by fractures/perforations and presence/absence of periodontitis — directly prognosis/treatment relevant.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative review of literature on PA, NPD, and EPL for the 2017 World Workshop, building objective classification criteria.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- PA: classify by etiologic factor; most frequent type arises in pre-existing periodontal pockets.
- NPD: classification should incorporate host immune status (e.g., immunocompromise).
- EPL: classify by presence/absence of root fractures/perforations and of periodontitis — these drive prognosis and treatment.
- These lesions are low-frequency but clinically urgent (acute pain, rapid tissue destruction).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Evidence base is largely case series/observational for rare acute lesions.
- Classification consensus precedes high-quality outcome validation.

## 6. Related Work
- 2017 World Workshop companion consensus (Jepsen 2018 workgroup 3).
- Endo-periodontal lesion management literature.

## 7. Glossary
- Periodontal abscess (PA): localized purulent infection of the periodontal tissues.
- Necrotizing periodontal diseases (NPD): NUG/NUP — ulceration/necrosis tied to impaired host response.
- Endo-periodontal lesion (EPL): combined pulpal and periodontal pathology of one tooth.
