## Why Ingested

This 3-year prospective cohort seeds the wiki's first Ceraseal (Meta Biomed premixed calcium-silicate bioceramic sealer) clinical-outcome page and supplies the **longest clinical follow-up** available for Ceraseal + single-cone (prior evidence capped at 24 months). It extends [[endodontics/zamparini-2023-premixed-calcium-silicate-carrier-based-2year]] (same Bologna group, 24-month carrier-based premixed CaSi outcome) to 36 months and a single-cone protocol, and complements the in-vitro Ceraseal characterization papers ([[endodontics/kunam-2024-wettability-ahplus-ceraseal-guttaflow]], [[endodontics/maharti-2024-calcium-silicate-apatite-deposition-ph]]) with hard clinical PAI/survival endpoints. Source: PubMed/PMC ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.14744/eej.2024.75537)).

## One-line Summary

Prospective cohort (52 patients, 58 RCTs; Ceraseal + single-cone) reporting 92.7% survival and 92.1% per-protocol healing (PAI ≤2) at 36 months, with 100% healing in pulpitis and pulp-necrosis teeth.

## 2. Key Contributions

- **First long-term (36-month) clinical evidence** for the single-cone technique paired with **Ceraseal** premixed bioceramic sealer — prior Ceraseal clinical data was limited to ≤24 months.
- High clinical effectiveness: **survival 92.7%**, per-protocol **success/healing 92.1%** (PAI ≤2), intention-to-treat healing 79.3%.
- **100% healing of pulpitis and pulp-necrosis teeth** at 36 months (per protocol); retreatment (exacerbated periapical lesion) lower at 80%.
- Characterizes **sealer extrusion behaviour over time**: moderate extrusion (24%), with a subset showing complete radiographic resorption (3/14 extrusions, 21%).
- No iatrogenic complications and no post-operative symptoms despite treatment by supervised postgraduate operators.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Single-arm prospective cohort, University of Bologna endodontic department, May 2019–June 2023; STROBE-compliant; ethics OUTENDOPROSP CE 20079.
- **Sample**: 67 screened → 52 patients / 58 root canal treatments enrolled (14 incisors, 8 canines, 15 premolars, 21 molars). ASA 1–2, age 18–75.
- **Protocol**: Rubber dam isolation; Root ZX apex locator; glide path to #15.02; rotary NiTi (Mtwo/Rotate, VDW) for primary RCT; reciprocating NiTi (Reciproc Blue) for retreatment. Irrigation 5% NaOCl (5 mL), 10% EDTA if calcified, sterile water final rinse.
- **Obturation**: **Ceraseal** (MetaBiomed, Cheongju, South Korea) single-cone technique with gutta-percha (Mynol); sealer spread with K-file 3 mm short of WL.
- **Restoration**: post-endodontic composite (Clearfil SE BOND + G-Aenial) at 1 week; provisional/definitive crowns where structure insufficient.
- **Outcome**: Periapical Index (PAI) by two single-blind evaluators at baseline, 6, 12, 24, 36 months; sealer extrusion measured in mm (ImageJ). Linear + logistic regression with two-way clustering (teeth, patients); per-protocol and intention-to-treat (LOCF). Barnard CSM exact test for baseline associations.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Survival**: 38/41 = **92.7%** (after excluding 2 deaths and 15 unknown dropouts). 3 extractions for horizontal root fracture.
- **Mean PAI (ITT, n=58)**: 2.5 → 1.5 at 36 months (Δ −1.0, 95% CI −1.4 to −0.6).
- **Mean PAI (PP, n=38)**: 2.4 → 1.2 at 36 months (Δ −1.3, 95% CI −1.7 to −0.8).
- **Healed (PAI ≤2), ITT**: 41.4% → **79.3%** (OR 5.43, 95% CI 2.74–10.76).
- **Healed (PAI ≤2), PP**: 42.1% → **92.1%** (OR 16.04, 95% CI 4.79–53.74).
- **By diagnosis (PP)**: pulpitis 100%, pulp necrosis 100%, retreatment 80%. (ITT: pulpitis 100%, necrosis 71%, retreatment 65%.)
- **Sealer extrusion**: 14/58 (24%); 11 without resorption, 3 with complete radiographic resorption.
- **Significant associations with healing at 36 months** (ITT): pulpitis +46% (p=0.002), exacerbated periapical lesion −34% (p=0.034), no extrusion +33% (p=0.028), extrusion-without-resorption −39% (p=0.003), composite restoration +28% (p=0.045), crown −30% (p=0.036).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- No control group → no direct comparison to a traditional (epoxy-resin) sealer.
- Limited cohort (52 patients) and 36-month follow-up — below the **ESE-recommended ≥4 years** for conclusive healing determination.
- No CBCT (ALARA); 2D PAI only.
- High loss to follow-up (34.5%; 25.9% for unknown reasons).
- Authors call for long-term head-to-head trials of Ceraseal vs other bioceramic and traditional sealers, and biological-mechanism studies of periapical healing.

## 7. Glossary

- **Ceraseal**: Premixed (ready-to-use) calcium silicate-based bioceramic root canal sealer (Meta Biomed, South Korea); ~20–30% tricalcium silicate, ~45–50% zirconium dioxide radiopacifier; releases calcium ions and alkalises the environment.
- **Single-cone technique**: Cold obturation using one gutta-percha master cone matched to the prepared canal plus sealer; relies on a hydraulic/bioceramic sealer to fill irregularities.
- **PAI (Periapical Index)**: 5-point radiographic score of periapical status; PAI ≤2 = healed/healthy.
- **Sealer extrusion**: Apical egress of sealer beyond the radiographic apex; CaSi-based sealers may resorb radiographically over time.
- **ITT / PP**: Intention-to-treat (all cases, LOCF imputation) vs per-protocol (only 36-month completers).
- **ESE**: European Society of Endodontology — recommends ≥4-year follow-up for conclusive healing.
