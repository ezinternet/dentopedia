## One-line Summary

In-vitro SEM/EDX + pH study (15 premolars, 14-day PBS immersion) showing premixed Ceraseal deposits more interfacial apatite (23.65 vs 18.49 µm) and reaches higher pH (11.48 vs 9.19 at day 14) than AH Plus Bioceramic, indicating superior biomineralization.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Head-to-head in-vitro biomineralization comparison of two **premixed** calcium silicate-based sealers: **Ceraseal** (Meta Biomed Co., Cheongju, Korea) and **AH Plus Bioceramic** (Dentsply Sirona, USA).
- Quantifies interfacial-layer apatite deposition on root canal dentin (SEM/EDX + ImageJ) and tracks pH over 14 days in PBS.
- Links higher calcium silicate proportion (Ceraseal) to both greater apatite deposition and higher sustained alkalinity, vs AH Plus Bioceramic's reduced calcium silicate / increased zirconium dioxide formulation.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Apatite test**: 15 mandibular second premolars (single straight root/canal, closed apex, ~25 mm), ProTaper Gold crown-down to F3, irrigation 2.5% NaOCl + 17% EDTA (sonic Endoactivator), F3 gutta-percha. Three groups (n = 5 each): AH Plus Bioceramic, Ceraseal, dentin-only control. Set 24 h (37°C, 100% humidity), then 14-day PBS immersion (changed every 5 days). Roots cut 5 mm from apex, platinum-coated, SEM/EDX (Jeol). Apatite thickness = average of 50 perpendicular lines, ImageJ; inter-observer ICC.
- **pH test**: sealers in molds (2 mm × 5 mm), set 24 h, immersed in PBS; digital pH meter (Hanna) on days 0, 7, 14 (n = 3/group, triplicate).
- **Stats**: Mann–Whitney (apatite), one-way ANOVA + post hoc LSD (pH), p < 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Apatite deposition (median µm)**: Ceraseal **23.65** (21.93–25.78) > AH Plus Bioceramic **18.49** (18.02–19.43); significant (p < 0.05). ICC = 0.998.
- **Interfacial Ca/P ratio**: AH Plus Bioceramic 2.01, Ceraseal 4.01 — both above hydroxyapatite (1.67); dentin Ca/P 1.61 (AHPBC) and 1.71 (Ceraseal).
- **pH (mean)**: at 0 h all ~7.4. Day 7: Ceraseal 11.29, AH Plus Bioceramic 9.32, control 7.39. Day 14: Ceraseal **11.48**, AH Plus Bioceramic **9.19**, control 7.39. Significant group differences at days 7 and 14 (p < 0.05).
- **Pattern**: Ceraseal pH rose continuously through day 14; AH Plus Bioceramic peaked at day 7 then declined (within-group day-7→14 changes not statistically significant for either).
- **Conclusion**: Ceraseal showed superior biomineralization potential (more apatite, higher alkalinity).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Restricted 14-day observation period limits understanding of apatite morphology maturation.
- Analysis confined to SEM/EDX; the mineral phase was not identified (no XRD/FTIR).
- In-vitro only — in-vivo validation needed; small sample (n = 5/group apatite, n = 3/group pH).

## 6. Related Work

- Reyes-Carmona et al (2009): Portland cement petal-like crystals at day 14 in PBS.
- Gandolfi et al (2010): cloud-like apatite morphology at day 14, granular layer by 28–60 days.
- Prati & Gandolfi (2015): decreased silica, increased phosphorus by day 28.
- Zamparini et al (2022): Ceraseal higher alkalinity/calcium release than NeoSealer Flo and AH Plus Bioceramic (but reported pH decrease from 24 h — differs from this study).

## 7. Glossary

- **Biomineralization**: a bioactive material's ability to form an apatite-like layer when in contact with physiological fluid (pH rise + mineral ion release + apatite formation).
- **Interfacial layer / mineral infiltration zone**: apatite-rich region precipitated between root canal dentin and sealer.
- **Ca/P ratio**: calcium-to-phosphorus ratio; stoichiometric hydroxyapatite = 1.67.
- **PBS**: phosphate-buffered saline (~9–10 mM phosphate), body-like medium used to drive apatite precipitation.
- **Premixed calcium silicate sealer**: ready-to-use bioceramic sealer requiring no mixing (e.g., Ceraseal, AH Plus Bioceramic).
