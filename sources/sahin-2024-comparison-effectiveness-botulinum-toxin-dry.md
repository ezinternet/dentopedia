## One-line Summary
4-arm RCT (n=80, Turkey) finds BoNT-A, dry needling, pharmacological therapy, and manual therapy produce equivalent VAS / MMO / OHIP-14 outcomes through 12 weeks for bruxism-induced myalgia — null hypothesis supported.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: J Oral Facial Pain Headache 2024;38(4):101-110
- **DOI**: 10.22514/jofph.2024.043
- **Published**: 12 December 2024
- **Trial ID**: NCT06583551
- **Lead institution**: University of Health Sciences Hamidiye School of Dentistry, Istanbul
- **Design**: 4-armed parallel, open-label, prospective randomized clinical trial; CONSORT 2010-adherent

## 2. Key Contributions
- First RCT to directly compare four common bruxism-myalgia therapies head-to-head: BoNT-A vs. dry needling (DN) vs. pharmacological therapy (PT) vs. manual therapy (MT).
- Pre-specified null hypothesis: all four therapies similarly effective in early outcomes — supported by data.
- Standardized 80-patient cohort with bruxism diagnosed by Pintado (AB) and AASM (SB) clinical criteria.
- Three pre/post timepoints (2, 4, 12 weeks) with three outcome instruments (VAS, MMO, OHIP-14).
- Implies that less-invasive/cheaper modalities can be reasonable first choices over BoNT-A.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 80 adult bruxism patients (44F/36M), ASA I-II, moderate-to-severe masticatory muscle pain, no prior bruxism Tx, no TMJ disorder/pregnancy/neuromuscular disease.
- Randomized 1:1:1:1 to four groups (n=20 each): BoNT-A, DN, PT, MT — all delivered by the same maxillofacial surgeon.
- Sample size: G*Power, F-test ANOVA, power 0.9, alpha 0.05, effect size 0.4 → 16 per group + 20% buffer = 20/group.
- Outcomes:
  - VAS (0-10) for pain.
  - MMO (mm) via TheraBite ruler — maximum painless mouth opening.
  - OHIP-14 (Turkish OHIP-14-TR) for oral health-related QoL.
- Statistics: mixed-design repeated-measures two-way ANOVA; Tukey post-hoc.

- All four groups significantly reduced VAS pain over time within group, but no between-group differences at any timepoint.
- MMO did not change significantly in any group.
- OHIP-14 actually trended upward (worsened) in the BoNT-A group at 4 and 12 weeks (within-group p=0.020), unique among the four groups.
- Adverse events: mild, transient — bruising at injection sites (BoNT-A) and one DN patient with post-procedure itching; no patient excluded.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Open-label by necessity (different mechanical natures of the four interventions).
- Single center, single operator.
- 12-week follow-up — does not capture long-term BoNT-A muscle/bone adverse outcomes.
- Sample size powered for primary VAS comparison; secondary outcomes (e.g., OHIP-14 trend) underpowered.
- Diagnostic criteria mixed Pintado (AB) and AASM (SB) — bruxism phenotyping heterogeneity.

## 7. Glossary
- **BoNT-A** (보툴리눔독소 A형): see prior glossaries.
- **DN** (건성 침술/드라이 니들링, Dry Needling): Insertion of thin monofilament needle into myofascial trigger points without injectate.
- **PT** (약물요법, Pharmacological Therapy): Sedatives, anxiolytics, NSAIDs, antidepressants, muscle relaxants for bruxism.
- **MT** (도수치료, Manual Therapy): Hands-on physical therapy targeting jaw muscles and ROM.
- **MMO** (최대개구량, Maximum Mouth Opening): Here measured pain-free with TheraBite ruler.
- **OHIP-14** (구강건강영향척도-14, Oral Health Impact Profile-14): 14-item QoL instrument; higher = worse OHRQoL.
- **Pintado criteria**: Self-report items for awake bruxism diagnosis.
- **AASM**: American Academy of Sleep Medicine — clinical findings checklist for sleep bruxism.
