## One-line Summary
Survey of Korean dental school endodontic faculty showing 89% prefer electronic apex locators (EAL) over radiography for working length determination, with 78% favoring an apical limit of 0.5–1.0 mm short of the radiographic apex.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Cross-sectional survey (questionnaire)
- **Institution**: Department of Conservative Dentistry, Wonkwang University
- **Journal**: Korean Journal of Conservative Dentistry (보존과학회지), pp. 42–52
- **Year**: 2016
- **Response rate**: 90% of distributed questionnaires returned

## 2. Key Contributions
- Establishes the dominant preference for EAL over radiography among Korean endodontic educators
- Quantifies the preferred apical limit (0.5–1.0 mm short of radiographic apex) in a Korean academic context
- Documents the majority consensus on EAL reading interpretation (APEX mark − 0.5 mm)
- Provides data on clinical decision-making when EAL and radiographic measurements conflict

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: Questionnaire-based survey distributed to endodontic teachers across multiple Korean dental schools
- **Survey content**: Two domains — (1) preferred method of working length determination; (2) preferred apical limit for canal instrumentation and obturation
- **EAL-specific questions**: Which mark to use (APEX vs. 0.5/1.0 marks), how to handle EAL–radiograph discordance

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
| Question | Result |
|---|---|
| Preferred working length method | EAL: **89%**, radiograph: remainder |
| Preferred apical limit | 0.5–1.0 mm short of radiographic apex: **78%** |
| Preferred EAL reading | APEX mark − 0.5 mm: **41%** |
| When EAL vs. radiograph conflict | Chose EAL: **74%** |

- Majority consensus: EAL is primary; radiograph is confirmatory, not definitive.
- RootZX (J. Morita) cited as a reference EAL device; APEX mark interpreted as major foramen.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Survey limited to Korean dental school faculty; may not represent general practitioner preferences
- Does not include clinical outcome data linking working length choice to treatment success rates
- Self-reported preferences may not reflect actual clinical practice

## 7. Glossary
- **Working length (WL)**: Distance from a coronal reference point to the apical stop of canal preparation
- **Apical limit**: The most apical point to which instrumentation and obturation should extend
- **EAL (Electronic Apex Locator)**: Electronic device measuring impedance/frequency to locate the apical foramen
- **Radiographic apex**: Most apical point of the root as seen on a periapical radiograph
- **CDJ (Cemento-Dentinal Junction)**: Histological junction where dentin meets cementum; ideal apical stop ~0.5–1.0 mm from radiographic apex
- **APEX mark**: EAL reading corresponding to the major apical foramen on devices such as RootZX
