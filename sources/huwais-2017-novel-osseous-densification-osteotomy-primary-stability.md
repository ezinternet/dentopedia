## Why Ingested

Foundational paper that introduced the osseodensification (OD) concept and the Versah bur design — the historical anchor for the entire [[overviews/osseodensification-clinical-applications]] evidence base and the [[overviews/versah-protocol-selection-map]]. Establishes the bench mechanism (counterclockwise compaction layer, raised bone mineral density, tripled BIC) that all later clinical SR+MAs test.

## One-line Summary
Foundational Huwais & Meyer bench study (porcine tibial bone, 72 osteotomies, 3 techniques × 4.1/6.0 mm implants) introducing osseodensification (OD): counterclockwise multi-fluted bur compacts a peri-osteotomy layer of higher bone mineral density and triples BIC compared with standard drilling, without raising temperature or ISQ.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: In vitro bench / biomechanical study
- **Journal**: International Journal of Oral & Maxillofacial Implants 2017;32(1):27–36
- **DOI**: 10.11607/jomi.4817; PMID 27741329
- **Authors**: Salah Huwais (inventor), Eric G. Meyer
- **Sample**: 72 osteotomies in porcine tibial plateau bone samples; three preparation techniques; 4.1 mm and 6.0 mm diameter implants
- **Primary Question**: Does counterclockwise compaction drilling with a tapered multi-fluted bur (osseodensification) increase primary implant stability, peri-implant bone mineral density, and bone-to-implant contact relative to conventional cutting drilling?

## 2. Key Contributions
- **Introduces the osseodensification (OD) concept**: same tapered multi-fluted bur rotated counterclockwise (densifying) instead of clockwise (extracting), creating a layer of compacted, autografted bone along the osteotomy wall.
- Compared three preparation techniques head-to-head in matched porcine tibial bone: (1) standard drilling, (2) osseous extraction drilling with the new bur clockwise, (3) osseous densification with the same bur counterclockwise.
- **Significantly increased insertion and removal torques** with OD vs both controls — the primary mechanical claim.
- Documented the **spring-back / elastic recovery** mechanism: OD osteotomy diameters measured smaller than both extraction-drilling and standard-drilling osteotomies, despite the same bur — produces interference fit at implant insertion.
- **BIC tripled (~3× increase)** for implants prepared with OD vs standard drilling — the histomorphometric claim that underpins subsequent OD marketing.
- **No significant ISQ difference** among the three groups — an underappreciated nuance: OD raises insertion torque without raising RFA-measured lateral stiffness in this model.
- **No significant temperature difference** among groups — counterargument against the intuition that compaction would generate more heat.
- Imaging (SEM + micro-CT) documented a discrete layer of increased bone mineral density around the periphery of OD osteotomies.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Seventy-two osteotomies were prepared in porcine tibial plateau bone samples — a model chosen for its mixed cortical/cancellous architecture. Three preparation techniques were tested in matched samples: (1) **standard drilling** (conventional clockwise cutting drills), (2) **osseous extraction drilling** using the new tapered multi-fluted bur clockwise (control for bur geometry), (3) **osseous densification** using the same bur counterclockwise (the experimental technique). The same bur design isolates the effect of rotation direction from geometric confounders.

Outcome measures: (a) surgical process — temperature rise during preparation, drilling force, and drilling torque; (b) mechanical stability — implant insertion torque, removal torque, and ISQ at insertion of 4.1 mm and 6.0 mm diameter implants; (c) bone imaging — scanning electron microscopy of osteotomy walls, micro-CT measurement of peri-osteotomy bone mineral density, and histomorphology to quantify percentage bone-to-implant contact (BIC).

The use of porcine tibia and the bench setting permit direct mechanical measurement under controlled conditions but do not capture biological healing.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Insertion torque**: significantly higher with OD vs standard drilling and vs extraction drilling (specific Ncm values not in extracted text but commonly quoted ~49 vs ~25 Ncm in low-density bone in derivative literature).
- **Removal torque**: significantly higher with OD vs both controls.
- **ISQ**: no significant difference among the three groups.
- **Osteotomy diameter**: OD < extraction drilling < standard drilling (spring-back/elastic strain effect — same bur).
- **Peri-osteotomy bone mineral density**: discrete layer of increased BMD with OD, documented on SEM and micro-CT.
- **BIC**: ~3× higher with OD vs standard drilling.
- **Temperature**: no significant difference among groups.
- Exact numeric tables (means ± SD, p-values) are in the paywalled body and not extractable from the available abstract.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Porcine tibial bench model — no biological healing component; the BIC measurement is immediate post-insertion, not after osseointegration.
- Inventor-led study with strong conflict of interest (Huwais is patent holder).
- The dissociation between insertion torque (raised) and ISQ (unchanged) deserves clinical scrutiny — torque is an axial mechanical measurement; ISQ is a lateral resonance measurement; they probe different aspects of stability and the OD effect appears axial-dominant in this model.
- 3× BIC ratio is striking and has not been fully replicated in animal in-vivo models; subsequent SR+MAs (e.g. Gaikwad 2022) report more modest BIC effects.
- Bone type heterogeneity within porcine tibia is not fully characterised — D1–D4 stratification absent.
- Implant macrogeometry (brand, thread design) and irrigation protocol details not extractable from the available text.

## 7. Glossary
- **OD (Osseodensification)**: Counterclockwise drilling with tapered multi-fluted bur to compact bone along osteotomy walls rather than excavate it.
- **Densah bur**: Tapered multi-fluted bur (Versah LLC); the same tool operates as densifier (CCW) or extractor (CW).
- **Spring-back / elastic strain recovery**: Viscoelastic recoil of bone walls after bur removal, leaving the osteotomy diameter smaller than the bur — contributes to implant interference fit.
- **BIC (Bone-to-Implant Contact)**: Percentage of implant surface in direct histological contact with bone.
- **BMD (Bone Mineral Density)**: Mineralised bone mass per unit volume; measured here by micro-CT.
- **Insertion torque (IT)**: Peak rotational resistance during implant placement (Ncm).
- **Removal torque**: Peak rotational resistance to unscrewing the implant after insertion.
- **ISQ**: Resonance-frequency-derived lateral stability index (1–100).
