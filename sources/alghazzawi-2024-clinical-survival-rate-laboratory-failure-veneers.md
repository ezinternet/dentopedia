## Why Ingested

비니어의 실험실(in vitro) 파절·탈락 원인이 임상 생존율에 어떻게 반영되는지 연결짓는 서사적 리뷰가 부족하여 추가. 기존 [[wiki/veneers/klein-2025-ceramic-laminate-veneer-survival-complications-sr-ma]]에서 제시한 생존율 메타분석을 기계적 실패 메커니즘 관점에서 보완한다.

## One-line Summary

Narrative review (1999–2024) synthesizing in vitro failure mechanisms and clinical survival of dental veneers: >90% survival at 10+ years; minimal/no-prep glass-ceramic veneers show highest survival; fracture > debonding > color change as ranked failure modes.

## 한줄요약

서사적 문헌고찰(1999–2024): 치과 비니어(dental veneer)의 실험실 파절·탈락·색변화 원인과 임상 생존율(survival rate)을 연결 분석; 최소삭제(minimal/no-prep) 유리도재(glass-ceramic) 비니어가 10년 이상 >90% 생존율로 최우수.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Links in vitro failure mechanisms directly to clinical outcomes — a practical bridge rarely drawn in single-study papers.
2. Provides a systematic table of fracture factors (Table 1), debonding factors (Table 2), color-change factors (Table 3), and clinical survival rates across multiple studies (Table 4).
3. Establishes ranked failure hierarchy: fracture > debonding > color change.
4. Recommends specific clinical and lab communication protocols to reduce each failure mode.
5. Confirms no survival difference between maxillary and mandibular teeth, nor between males and females.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search strategy**: keyword-based PubMed/Medline + Scopus + Google Scholar + Science Direct; no PRISMA flow diagram reported (narrative, not SR/MA).
- **Inclusion**: articles 1999–2024 on tooth preparation, veneer aging, type, and resin cement type.
- **Exclusion**: marginal fit, microhardness, water sorption, polishability, occlusal veneers.
- **Data synthesis**: narrative — tables summarize failure causes (in vitro) and survival/success rates (in vivo), then clinical significance is drawn from the comparison.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Fracture (Primary Failure Mode)
- Veneer/cement thickness ratio: ceramic/composite ratio <3 → catastrophic fracture (Magne et al.).
- Higher modulus of elasticity of supporting substrate → greater failure load; dentin (18 GPa) vs enamel (70–80 GPa) is a key difference.
- Once adhesively luted, 0.6 mm lithium disilicate shows same fracture load as conventionally luted 1.5 mm lithium disilicate.
- Failure load of 0.3 mm lithium disilicate (2002 ± 427 N) comparable to 0.5 mm veneers.
- Palatal chamfer and complete coverage crowns provide highest fracture resistance.

### Debonding (Second Most Common Failure)
- Dentin exposure >50% is major debonding risk factor; immediate dentin sealing (IDS) is recommended.
- HF acid etching protocol: 20 s for glass ceramic, 60–90 s for feldspathic porcelain; silane 60 s after.
- Die spacer thickness must not exceed 1/3 of veneer thickness.
- Contamination (blood, saliva, handpiece oil, fluoride polishing paste) is a major debonding cause.

### Color Change
- Zirconia specimens showed greater color change with thermal aging than glass ceramics.
- UDMA-based resin cements with smaller filler, lower filler content → better long-term color stability.
- Light-cured polymerization preferred for thin veneers (<1 mm) — better long-term color.

### Clinical Survival Rates (Table 4 highlights)
| Study | Survival | Follow-up |
|---|---|---|
| De Angelis 2023 (no-prep) | 97.4% | Mean 43.1 mo |
| Aslan 2019 | 97.4% | 10 years |
| Demirekin 2022 (IPS e.max Press) | 99.7% | 10 years |
| Fotiadou 2021 (lithium disilicate) | 96.3% | 6.6 years |
| Rinke 2020 | 91.8% | 10 years |
| Mihali 2022 | 91.77% | 7 years |
| Morimoto 2016 (SR) | 89% overall; 94% glass-ceramic; 87% feldspathic | 9 years |
| Smielak 2022 | 100% (no-prep/min-prep) vs 9.67% (conventional) | — |
| Limet 2023 (RCT pool) | 88% (95% CI: 81–94%) | 24–97 months |

### Key Clinical Recommendations
- Fracture reduction: evaluate occlusion immediately post-cementation; use high-flexural-strength materials (glass-ceramic 450 MPa, zirconia 1200 MPa); select low-modulus resin cement; use thin, highly polished veneers.
- Debonding reduction: minimize preparation; use IDS when dentin exposure >50%; prevent contamination; proper HF etching + silane; use light-cure polymerization for thin (<1 mm) veneers.
- Color stability: select UDMA-based resin cements; prefer glass-ceramic over feldspathic; use light-cure polymerization.
- Night guard recommended for all veneer patients.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative review — no meta-analysis, no PRISMA, risk of selection bias.
- Occlusal veneers explicitly excluded (different loading scenario).
- Laboratory substrate (resin analogs) does not fully replicate clinical dentin variability.
- Long-term RCT data on ultrathin zirconia veneers still limited (Silva 2023: only 3 patients, 4.33 year mean follow-up).
- Calls for long-term in vivo studies using ultratranslucent zirconia veneers and newer glass ceramics.

## 6. Related Work

- Morimoto 2016 SR+MA: 89% overall 9-year survival; glass-ceramic 94%, feldspathic 87%.
- Klein 2025 SR+MA: broader SR of ceramic laminate veneer survival/complications.
- Magne et al.: ceramic/composite ratio threshold study (ratio <3 = catastrophic failure).
- De Angelis 2023: adhesive bonding turns 0.6 mm into equivalent of 1.5 mm conventional.

## 7. Glossary

- **IDS (Immediate Dentin Sealing)**: sealing exposed dentin immediately after preparation before temporization — prevents bacterial contamination and enhances bond strength.
- **UDMA**: urethane dimethacrylate — resin monomer with better color stability than BisGMA.
- **HF etching**: hydrofluoric acid etching of ceramic intaglio surface — creates micromechanical retention for silane-mediated bonding.
- **Feldspathic porcelain**: traditional dental porcelain; low flexural strength (60–70 MPa); highest translucency.
- **Glass-ceramic (lithium disilicate)**: IPS e.max; flexural strength 450 MPa; significantly stronger than feldspathic.
- **AFR (Annual Failure Rate)**: percentage of restorations failing per year.
- **Die spacer**: space between die and veneer interior accommodating resin cement thickness; must not exceed 1/3 veneer thickness.
