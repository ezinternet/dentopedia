## One-line Summary
EFP S3 guideline for stage IV periodontitis: interdisciplinary rehabilitation (orthodontics, splinting, prosthetics) following active periodontal therapy; based on 13 commissioned SRs.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Periodontology 2022; 49(Suppl. 24): 4–71
- **Study type**: Clinical practice guideline (S3 level, EFP)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Companion guideline to Sanz 2020 for stage IV (most severe) periodontitis
- Addresses functional sequelae: tooth flaring/drifting, bite collapse, tooth mobility ≥2, severe ridge defects
- Emphasises interdisciplinary approach: periodontics + orthodontics + prosthodontics
- 13 specifically commissioned SRs; GRADE methodology

## 3. Key Features of Stage IV Periodontitis
- Shared severity with stage III but with additional functional sequelae
- <20 remaining teeth (<10 opposing pairs); masticatory dysfunction
- Hazard ratio 3.73× higher risk of periodontal tooth loss vs stage I over 10–30 years

## 4. Key Recommendations
- Complete active periodontal therapy (per Sanz 2020 CPG) before rehabilitation
- Comprehensive diagnosis and re-evaluation before treatment planning
- **Orthodontic therapy**: tooth movement to correct pathological migration; supported by SR evidence
- **Tooth splinting**: semi-permanent splints for mobile teeth after periodontal stabilisation
- **Occlusal adjustment**: limited evidence; adjunct only
- **Prosthetic rehabilitation**: implant-supported FDPs or RPDs after periodontal stabilisation; ISQ/loading protocols apply
- **SPT**: high-intensity recall (≤3 months initially) mandatory for stage IV

## 5. Limitations
- Heterogeneous evidence quality for interdisciplinary interventions
- Long-term data on combined perio-prosthetic outcomes limited

## 6. Related Work
- Sanz 2020: mandatory prerequisite (stages I–III treatment)
- Stage IV patients needing implants → ISQ-based loading protocols apply
