## Why Ingested
CR-MIP 과두 변위(Condylar Displacement, CD)와 부정교합·두개안면 형태의 관계를 정리한 scoping review. [[tmj/]] 카테고리의 TMD 위험 요인 및 [[occlusion/]] 카테고리의 교합-TMJ 관계 근거 보강.

## One-line Summary
Scoping review (10 studies): hyperdivergent facial pattern is the primary skeletal risk factor for increased CR-MIP condylar displacement; condyles typically displace posteroinferiorly; evidence on other malocclusions remains inconclusive.

## 한줄요약
스코핑 리뷰 (10편): 과두발산형(hyperdivergent) 안면 골격이 CR-MIP 과두 변위의 주요 위험 요소; 과두는 주로 후하방 변위; 기타 부정교합과의 관계는 불확실.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Medicine 2023, 12, 689
- **Study type**: Scoping Review (PRISMA-ScR)
- **Database search**: PubMed, Cochrane, Web of Science, Scopus (up to Feb 2022)
- **Included studies**: 10 studies from 2321 identified

## 2. Key Contributions
- Hyperdivergent skeletal pattern = main risk factor for increased condylar displacement (CD)
- Condyles displace predominantly in posteroinferior direction in hyperdivergent patients
- Clinically significant CD threshold: ≥2 mm vertical/horizontal; ≥0.5 mm transverse
- CR-MIP discrepancy and malocclusion: no proven causal relationship with TMD
- Routine TMD screening before orthodontic treatment recommended

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- PRISMA-ScR checklist; JBI Critical Appraisal Tool
- 5 analysis categories: vertical skeletal pattern, sagittal, vertical malocclusion, horizontal malocclusion, transverse malocclusion
- Methods used in included studies: MPI/CPI, CBCT, articulator-mounted casts

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Hyperdivergent pattern**: consistently associated with increased CD (posteroinferior direction)
- **Skeletal Class II**: possibly higher TMJ disc displacement/degenerative disorder risk
- **Transverse malocclusion (crossbite)**: may be linked with transverse CD
- **Other malocclusions**: evidence insufficient or conflicting
- CD cannot diagnose TMD in isolation from clinical exam

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Only 10 studies included; methodological heterogeneity
- Most studies retrospective; CR recording methods vary
- Cannot establish causation between CD and TMD
- Need for prospective studies with standardized CD measurement

## 6. Related Work
- Okeson: CR = most musculoskeletally stable mandibular position
- Roth's functional orthodontics: CR as treatment reference
- No proven benefit of CR-based treatment for TMD prevention

## 7. Glossary
- **CR**: Centric relation — most anterosuperior condylar position
- **MIP**: Maximum intercuspal position — maximum tooth contact position
- **CD**: Condylar displacement — vector from CR to MIP
- **MPI/CPI**: Mandibular/Condylar position indicator
