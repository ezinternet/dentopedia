## Why Ingested

The [[wiki/overviews/unopposed-tooth-overeruption-overview]] explicitly names the **age effect** (younger individuals show larger supraposition of unopposed teeth) as a next-ingest gap — the existing pages establish that overeruption is variable but do not provide a controlled within-study young-vs-adult contrast. This rat micro-CT experiment is the primary animal source for that claim: it directly compares supraposition magnitude between 4-week (young/growing) and 26-week (adult) rats under identical occlusal-grinding conditions, and adds the dento-alveolar distinction (bone followed the tooth in young animals but not in adults). It reinforces the clinical observation in [[wiki/occlusion/livas-2016-fixed-retention-unopposed-molar-overeruption]] that overeruption potential is age-dependent.

## One-line Summary

Animal micro-CT study (40 Wistar rats): unopposed maxillary molars in young (4-wk) rats showed supraposition of 262 ± 135 µm vs only 125 ± 108 µm in adults (26-wk) after 4 weeks (P < 0.01), with alveolar bone following the tooth only in the young.

- **Title**: Supraposition of unopposed molars in young and adult rats
- **Authors**: Tadashi Fujita, Xavier Montet, Kazuo Tanne, Stavros Kiliaridis
- **Journal**: Archives of Oral Biology, Volume 54, Issue 1, January 2009, Pages 40–44
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.archoralbio.2008.07.010
- **Study type**: Controlled animal (rat) experimental study with micro-CT outcome
- **Source artifact**: ScienceDirect landing-page print (abstract + section-snippet level; full Results/Methods detail beyond the figures quoted is abstract-level only — not invented here)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic experimental test (in an animal model) of whether the overeruption (supraposition) potential of unopposed molars differs between young/growing and adult individuals — a question raised but not directly answered by prior human cross-sectional data (Craddock & Youngson; Kiliaridis et al.).
- Quantifies the age effect: young unopposed molars roughly **double** the supraposition of adult unopposed molars (262 vs 125 µm) over the same 4-week interval.
- Distinguishes **true tooth movement vs dento-alveolar growth**: in young animals the alveolar bone level was *higher* on the supraposed (unopposed) side, indicating bone followed the erupting tooth; in adults there was no such alveolar bone difference, implying the (smaller) adult supraposition is not accompanied by equivalent alveolar apposition.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Animals**: 40 male Wistar rats — 20 young (4-week-old) and 20 adult (26-week-old) — as experimental animals, plus 40 age-matched controls.
- **Intervention**: the right maxillary molar crowns were reduced occlusally by ~0.8 mm by grinding with a dental hand-piece under anaesthesia, removing the antagonist contact for those teeth.
- **Groups**: Young Unopposed (YU), Young Control (YC), Adult Unopposed (AU), Adult Control (AC).
- **Husbandry**: soft diet and water ad libitum during the experiment.
- **Duration**: 4 weeks, then sacrifice.
- **Outcome measurement**: micro-CT scanning; vertical tooth position (supraposition) measured as the height difference between the unopposed/experimental molar and the contralateral molar that retained its antagonist; alveolar bone level also assessed.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Supraposition (vertical tooth position), young**: 262 ± 135 µm higher than the contralateral molars with antagonists, vs −23 ± 63 µm in the young control group (P < 0.01).
- **Supraposition, adult**: 125 ± 108 µm — significantly less than the young group (P < 0.01).
- **Conclusion on age**: overeruption potential of unopposed molars is higher in young (growing) than in adult rats.
- **Alveolar bone**: in young animals the alveolar bone level was higher in the unopposed (supraposed) molars, while in adults no alveolar bone difference was found between unopposed and opposed molars.
- **Body weight**: no significant differences between unopposed and control groups in either young or adult animals (rules out diet/growth confounding by weight).
- (The 4 first right molars were still not in occlusion at the end of the 4-week period, confirming the antagonist contact had not been re-established.)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Animal (rat) model — continuously/rapidly growing dentition is not a direct analog of human adult tooth eruption; absolute µm values do not transfer to clinical mm thresholds.
- Short observation (4 weeks); long-term plateau or catch-up not assessed.
- Source available to this wiki is the ScienceDirect landing print, so detailed per-group SDs beyond those quoted and the exact bone-height metrics are at abstract/snippet level only.
- Mechanism (PDL remodeling vs continued eruption drive vs alveolar growth) inferred but not directly dissected.

## 6. Related Work

- Craddock & Youngson — 120 subjects, 83% of unopposed teeth overerupt (0.5–5.4 mm), 17% none.
- Kiliaridis et al. — molars losing antagonists after age 26 had lower prevalence of severe (≥2 mm) supraposition (14%) than the unknown-age-at-loss group (29%), suggesting younger loss → more overeruption.
- Longitudinal human data — adult unopposed molars overerupt ~0.80 ± 0.65 mm over ≥10 years.
- Fujita et al. 2010 (periodontally affected unopposed molars in adult rats) — companion line of work.

## 7. Glossary

- **Supraposition / overeruption**: vertical migration of a tooth beyond the occlusal plane when its antagonist contact is lost.
- **Unopposed molar**: a molar with no occluding antagonist tooth.
- **Dento-alveolar growth vs tooth movement**: whether the supporting alveolar bone migrates with the tooth (growth/apposition) or the tooth moves relative to stable bone.
- **Micro-CT**: micro-computed tomography, used here to quantify vertical tooth position and alveolar bone level.
- **Wistar rat**: a common outbred laboratory rat strain.
