## Why Ingested

General-healthcare (not dental-specific) evidence ingested to anchor the dental team-communication / workforce axis. It is the broadest single SR cataloguing *every* type of team-improvement intervention used in healthcare organizations — training, tools, organizational redesign, and combined programmes — so it provides the taxonomy and evidence-base ranking that the dental clinic can extrapolate from (a dental practice is a small healthcare team facing the same teamwork-and-handoff vulnerabilities). Reinforces [[overviews/dental-workforce-delegation-skill-mix-overview]] by supplying the upstream menu of how team effectiveness is actually improved (delegation/skill-mix needs functioning teams), and contextualizes SBAR/checklists as one branch within that menu.

## One-line Summary

SR (GRADE; 297 studies, 2008–Jul 2018, 7 databases) of healthcare team-effectiveness interventions: a 3+1 taxonomy (training [CRM/TeamSTEPPS/simulation], tools [SBAR/(de)briefing checklists/rounds/tech/monitoring], organizational redesign, combined programmes); training is most-studied and principle-based + simulation-based training offer the greatest chance of improving (mostly non-technical) team functioning, predominantly in acute hospital settings on low-to-moderate evidence.

## 한줄요약

SR(GRADE·297편·2008~2018·7개 DB): 의료팀 효과성 향상 중재를 3+1 분류(훈련[CRM/TeamSTEPPS/시뮬레이션]·도구[SBAR/체크리스트/라운드]·조직 재설계·복합 프로그램)로 정리. 훈련이 가장 많이 연구됐고, 원리기반 훈련(CRM·TeamSTEPPS)과 시뮬레이션 기반 훈련이 (주로 비기술적) 팀 기능 개선 가능성이 가장 높았으나 대부분 급성기 병원·낮은 근거수준.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Systematic review (GRADE-rated evidence base; no meta-analysis)
- **Journal**: Human Resources for Health 2020;18(1):2
- **Scope**: General healthcare organizations (NOT dental-specific). Ingested as a transferable anchor for the dental team-communication axis.
- **Search**: 7 databases (EMBASE, MEDLINE Ovid, Web of Science, Cochrane Library, PsycINFO, CINAHL EBSCO, Google Scholar), English peer-reviewed, 2008–July 2018.
- **Yield**: 6025 screened → 639 after title/abstract → 297 included (3 independent reviewers, PRISMA).
- **Predecessor**: updates the authors' own 2008 (publ. 2010) review.

## 2. Key Contributions

- A unified, decade-spanning taxonomy of healthcare team-effectiveness interventions across all settings/designs (most prior reviews were narrow — one intervention or one setting).
- Four main categories: **Training**, **Tools**, **Organizational (re)design**, **Programme** (combination).
- GRADE-rated evidence-base ranking identifying which interventions are best-supported (principle-based + simulation training; (de)briefing checklists).
- Maps four research gaps: non-hospital settings, long-term effects, contextual reporting, and under-used outcomes (e.g. professional well-being).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Four-area keyword search (team × healthcare × intervention × outcome), librarian-assisted, modeled on the 2008 review's strategy.
- Two reviewers screened independently; a third settled disagreements; "when in doubt, advance to next stage."
- GRADE scale to grade empirical evidence; downgraded one category for important inconsistencies.
- Categorization developed over three iterations on the 297 articles (50 → 50 → 197), peer-reviewed, with subcategories clustered/renamed (e.g. "programme-based" → "principle-based"; (de)briefing + checklists merged into "(de)briefing checklists").

The 3+1 taxonomy:
1. **Training** — principle-based (CRM, TeamSTEPPS), method-based (simulation-based training), general team training.
2. **Tools** — structuring (SBAR, (de)briefing checklists, rounds), facilitating (communication technology), triggering (monitoring/feedback, score cards/dashboards).
3. **Organizational (re)design** — restructuring pathways, schedules, roles/responsibilities, dedicated teams.
4. **Programme** — a bundle combining training + tools + redesign (e.g. CUSP, medical team training/MTT).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Most-studied**: Training; simulation-based training the single most-researched training type (69 studies, 16 of moderate-to-high evidence).
- **Setting**: predominantly acute hospital (ED, OR, ICU, surgical unit); primary care / nursing homes / long-term care under-represented.
- **Outcomes**: mostly **non-technical skills** (teamwork, communication, situational awareness, leadership, decision-making, task management); mostly subjective measures (SAQ, NOTECHS), few objective ones.
- **Evidence quality**: a bulk of studies low-level; TeamSTEPPS — half moderate-to-high; (de)briefing checklists — ~1/3 high/moderate; SBAR, rounds, triggering tools — (very) low.
- **Bottom line ranking**: **principle-based training (CRM, TeamSTEPPS) and simulation-based training offer the greatest opportunity** for improving team functioning; (de)briefing checklists also well-supported; combined programmes show promise.
- **Caveat (Whyte)**: briefings can paradoxically mask knowledge gaps, disrupt positive communication, reinforce professional divisions, create tension, perpetuate problematic culture.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Cannot claim exhaustive capture per subcategory (would need umbrella reviews).
- Categories limited to "team" literature (not integrated care / network medicine).
- Grey literature and non-English/non-empirical/non-peer-reviewed excluded → publication bias toward standardized interventions.
- Four research gaps: (1) non-hospital settings, (2) long-term/sustainability effects, (3) contextual reporting for replication, (4) under-used outcomes incl. professional well-being and "deadly combinations" between outcomes.

## 6. Related Work

- Hughes 2016 meta-analysis (team training ↔ teamwork/organizational performance); Murphy 2016 (simulation-based team training for resuscitation); O'Dea 2014 (CRM in acute care). These narrow prior reviews are subsumed under this broad update.
- Relevant to the dental wiki: SBAR handoff communication evidence; dental workforce delegation/skill-mix.

## 7. Glossary

- **CRM (Crew Resource Management)** — aviation-derived team training maximizing use of all available resources; targets non-technical skills and error prevention/trapping/mitigation.
- **TeamSTEPPS** — AHRQ/DoD team-training system ("CRM and more"); five domains: team structure, leadership, communication, situational monitoring, mutual support.
- **SBAR (Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation)** — structured communication/handoff technique (a "structuring" tool).
- **Non-technical skills** — cognitive/social skills (teamwork, communication, situational awareness, leadership, decision-making, task management); measured via SAQ, NOTECHS.
- **GRADE** — Grading of Recommendations, Assessment, Development, and Evaluation; scale used to rate evidence level.
- **Programme** — combination of training + tools + redesign (e.g. CUSP, MTT).
