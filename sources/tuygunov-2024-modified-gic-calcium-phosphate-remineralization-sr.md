## One-line Summary
SR of 5 in-vitro studies finding Ca/P-releasing modifications enhance GIC bioactivity, hydroxyapatite formation, pH, and cariostasis.

## 한줄요약
in-vitro 5편 SR — Ca/P 방출 개질이 GIC 생체활성·재광화·pH·항우식 향상.

## 1. Document Information
Dent Mater J 2024; 43(1): 1–10. DOI 10.4012/dmj.2023-132. Published 2024-01-25. Open access. University of Malaya (Biomaterials Technology Research Groups). PROSPERO-registered.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First focused SR on Ca/P-ion-releasing GIC modification (RoBDEMAT bias tool).
- Articulates the Ca/P-bioactivity vs fluoride-release trade-off.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
PRISMA SR. Databases: PubMed-MEDLINE, Scopus, Web of Science. 5 in-vitro studies. Bias: RoBDEMAT. Modifiers: CPP-ACP, nano-hydroxyapatite, Ca/phosphate compounds.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
Ca/P incorporation → ↑hydroxyapatite formation, ↑pH, ↑cariostatic effect; some formulations ↑mechanical properties; CPP-ACP may ↓fluoride release.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
In-vitro only; small study pool (n=5); heterogeneous modifiers/methods; clinical efficacy unproven — longitudinal studies needed.

## 6. Related Work
Skrtic et al. (Ca-phosphate remineralization); Mazzaoui/Al Zraikat (CPP-ACP in GIC); nano-hydroxyapatite GIC studies.

## 7. Glossary
- CPP-ACP: casein phosphopeptide–amorphous calcium phosphate.
- RoBDEMAT: risk-of-bias tool for dental-materials studies.
- Bioactivity: capacity to promote hydroxyapatite/mineral formation.
