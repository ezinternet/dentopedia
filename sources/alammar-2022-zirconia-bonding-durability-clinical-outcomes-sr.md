## Why Ingested

지르코니아 접착 내구성의 임상적 근거를 구축하기 위해 인제스트. 기존 [[resin-bonding/dimitriadi-2019-silane-reactivity-lithium-disilicate-bond-strength]]가 리튬 디실리케이트에 대한 실란 처리의 실험실적 근거를 제공하나, 임상 세팅에서 지르코니아-레진 결합의 장기 내구성에 대한 SR은 별도로 필요하다. 이 논문은 24편 임상 연구를 체계적으로 검토해 APA·TBS APA + MDP 프라이머 + MDP 기반 레진 시멘트의 임상 결합 프로토콜을 제시한다.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review of 24 clinical studies (up to Sept 2020) on zirconia restoration bonding to tooth/implant abutments: APA + MDP primer + MDP-based resin cement is the most durable protocol; surface pretreatment can sometimes be omitted without compromising certain zirconia restoration types.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(24편 임상연구): 지르코니아 수복물에서 APA/TBS APA 표면처리 + MDP 프라이머 + MDP 기반 레진 시멘트 조합이 가장 지속적인 임상 결합 결과를 보였으며, 일부 수복 유형에서는 표면처리 생략도 임상 결과에 영향을 미치지 않음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: The International Journal of Prosthodontics
- **Volume/Issue**: 04/2022, Pages 512–529
- **DOI**: 10.11607/ijp.6871
- **Published**: September 26, 2022
- **Study Type**: Systematic Review (without meta-analysis due to data heterogeneity)
- **Search Period**: English-language dental journals up to September 2020

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides a clinical-evidence synthesis (Part II) complementing laboratory data (Part I) on zirconia bonding durability
- Identifies APA (airborne-particle abrasion) and TBS APA (tribochemical silica coating) as the surface pretreatment methods with positive clinical bonding outcomes
- Establishes MDP (10-methacryloyloxydecyl dihydrogen phosphate) monomer-based primer + MDP or phosphoric ester monomer-based resin cement as the recommended durable bonding protocol
- Finds that skipping surface pretreatment did not affect clinical outcomes of certain zirconia restoration types on most tooth/implant abutment surfaces
- Notes that resin cement type is a less influential factor in clinical outcomes compared to surface pretreatment method and primer type
- Provides practical clinical bonding guidelines for different tooth and implant abutment surfaces

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search strategy**: Combination of free-text words and MeSH terms in dental journals
- **Inclusion criteria**: Clinical studies on zirconia restorations bonded to tooth or implant abutments, published up to September 2020, English language
- **Screening**: 2,856 studies identified; 24 studies finally included
- **Heterogeneity**: Sufficient heterogeneity in surface pretreatment methods, primers, resin cements, abutment types, zirconia restoration types and designs precluded meta-analysis
- **Outcomes evaluated**: Clinical bonding performance, survival/failure rates, adhesive protocol variables

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **24 studies included** covering a range of:
  - Surface pretreatment methods (APA, TBS APA, no treatment)
  - Primers (MDP monomer-based vs. others)
  - Resin cements (MDP-based, phosphoric ester-based, others)
  - Abutment types (different tooth surfaces, implant abutments)
  - Zirconia restoration designs

**Primary clinical conclusions:**
1. APA and TBS APA surface pretreatment yielded positive clinical outcomes for zirconia/resin bonding on all identified tooth surface types
2. Chemically activating APA or TBS APA surfaces with MDP monomer-based primer + MDP monomer or phosphoric ester monomer-based resin cement = durable bonding protocol for all tooth surface types
3. Skipping surface pretreatment did not affect clinical outcomes for certain zirconia restoration types on most tooth or implant abutment surfaces
4. Type of resin cement = less influential factor clinically

**Limitation**: The relatively limited number of studies (n=24) and marked heterogeneity of extracted data precluded meta-analysis.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 24 studies included — insufficient for meta-analysis
- High heterogeneity across surface pretreatment methods, primers, cements, abutment types, and restoration designs limits direct comparison
- Publisher webpage PDF — full-text detail limited to abstract/conclusions (full article behind paywall)
- Future well-designed RCTs with standardized protocols needed to allow quantitative synthesis

## 7. Glossary

- **APA**: Airborne-particle abrasion — mechanical surface pretreatment of zirconia using alumina particles
- **TBS APA**: Tribochemical silica coating — silica-modified airborne-particle abrasion (e.g., CoJet, Rocatec)
- **MDP**: 10-methacryloyloxydecyl dihydrogen phosphate — bifunctional phosphate monomer with high affinity for zirconia
- **Resin cement**: Dual-cure or self-cure luting cement with resin matrix used for bonding ceramic restorations
- **Zirconia**: Polycrystalline ceramic (Y-TZP or multilayer) — high strength but chemically inert surface requires pretreatment for reliable bonding
