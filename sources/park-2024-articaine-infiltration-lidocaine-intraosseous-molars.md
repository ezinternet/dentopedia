## Why Ingested

Korean (SNU Bundang Hospital) head-to-head data on anesthetizing single mandibular molars for caries treatment without IANB — directly relevant to our clinic's restorative/endodontic workflow. Extends the local-anesthesia evidence base in [[wiki/drug/hwang-2023-proposal-clinical-guidelines-local-anesthesia-dentistry]] (mandibular molar anesthesia is the hardest case the guideline addresses) and complements the agent/concentration discussion in [[wiki/drug/karm-2017-lidocaine-epinephrine-200000-third-molar]] by comparing a *technique* axis (articaine buccal infiltration vs lidocaine intraosseous) rather than a concentration axis.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective chart review (n=48, 24 ABI vs 24 LIO, SNU Bundang Hospital, ICDAS 5–6 mandibular molars): lidocaine intraosseous anesthesia via QuickSleeper5 achieved 100% success with no supplemental anesthesia vs 70.8% for 4% articaine buccal infiltration (P<0.001), with less injection pain (Heft-Parker VAS 17.3 vs 32.0, P<0.01), faster onset (91.7% numb at 1 min vs 33.3% at 5 min, P<0.001), and shorter duration (118.6 vs 231.3 min, P<0.001).

## 한줄요약

후향적 의무기록 분석 (n=48, ABI 24 vs LIO 24, 서울대분당병원, ICDAS 5–6 하악 대구치): QuickSleeper5 골내마취(LIO)는 추가 마취 없이 100% 성공 vs 4% 아티카인 협측 침윤(ABI) 70.8% (P<0.001), 주사 통증 더 적음(Heft-Parker VAS 17.3 vs 32.0, P<0.01), 발현 빠름(1분에 91.7% 마취 vs ABI는 5분에 33.3%, P<0.001), 지속 짧음(118.6 vs 231.3분, P<0.001).

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First direct comparison of ABI vs LIO** (rather than ABI vs lidocaine IANB) for single mandibular molar caries treatment — the existing literature only compared articaine infiltration against IANB.
2. **Quantifies the success-rate gap**: computer-controlled intraosseous lidocaine (QuickSleeper5) reached 100% primary success (no supplemental anesthesia needed) vs 70.8% for articaine buccal infiltration.
3. **Shows the technique, not the agent, drives speed**: LIO's direct bone-marrow deposition gave 1-min onset in 91.7% of cases, far faster than articaine infiltration despite articaine's higher lipid solubility.
4. **Characterizes the trade-off**: LIO is faster and more reliable but much shorter-acting (118.6 vs 231.3 min) — relevant when planning longer restorative/endodontic appointments.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Population**: 48 patients/teeth, mandibular 1st and 2nd molars with advanced caries (ICDAS 5–6, decay into inner 1/3 dentin), EPT-positive on two sessions, age >19, single-tooth treatment, no block anesthesia, no neurologic symptoms.
- **Groups (allocation by device availability, NOT randomized)**: when QuickSleeper5 available → LIO; otherwise → ABI.
- **ABI**: one cartridge (1.8 mL) 4% articaine + 1:100,000 epinephrine (Huons), 30-gauge needle, injected over 4 min at point bisecting mesial–distal root apices.
- **LIO**: one cartridge (1.8 mL) 2% lidocaine + 1:100,000 epinephrine (Huons), QuickSleeper5 with 30-gauge 16-mm Aiguilles needle into mesial/distal interdental alveolar bone over 4 min (slow controlled deposition, needle rotation ~15,000 rpm).
- **Outcomes**: success (no supplemental anesthesia; supplemental = periodontal 2% lidocaine 1:100,000 if EPT-positive after 9 min); pain during anesthesia (Heft-Parker VAS 0–170); onset (EPT 64-reading at 1/3/5/7/9 min); duration (subjective lip-numbness recall at follow-up); post-anesthesia lower-lip numbness; subjective heart-rate increase.
- **Statistics**: SPSS v27, chi-square/Fisher exact + independent t-tests, α=0.05. Groups homogeneous in age, sex, ICDAS, tooth position (all NS).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | ABI (4% articaine infiltration) | LIO (2% lidocaine intraosseous) | P |
|---|---|---|---|
| No supplemental anesthesia needed (success) | 17/24 (70.8%) | 24/24 (100%) | <0.01 (χ²=8.20, Fisher) |
| Pain during anesthesia (Heft-Parker VAS, M±SD) | 32.0 ± 18.6 | 17.3 ± 17.3 | <0.01 (t=2.9) |
| Onset at 1 min | 3/24 (12.5%) | 22/24 (91.7%) | <0.001 (χ²=33.3) |
| Onset failure (no numb) | 6/24 (25.0%) | 0 (0%) | (same test) |
| Duration of anesthesia (min, M±SD) | 231.3 ± 79.7 | 118.6 ± 52.2 | <0.001 (t=5.8) |
| Post-anesthesia lower-lip numbness | 24/24 (100%) | 20/24 (83.3%) | 0.109 (NS, Fisher) |
| Subjective heart-rate increase | 0 (0%) | 2/24 (8.33%) | (transient, returned to baseline) |

- The 7 ABI supplemental cases: 6 EPT-positive after 9 min + 1 discomfort despite EPT-negative.
- Notable: LIO still produced lower-lip numbness in 83.3% — anesthetic diffuses to the mental/inferior alveolar nerve even from intraosseous deposition.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Retrospective, non-randomized**: allocation by device availability (selection bias). Authors explicitly call for an RCT.
- **Small single-center sample** (n=48), Korean population only.
- **Duration measured by patient recall** of lip-numbness resolution at follow-up, not serial EPT — so "duration" is soft-tissue subjective, not pulpal.
- **Pulpal status not stratified** (caries-treated vital teeth, not specifically reversible vs irreversible pulpitis); future work should stratify by pulpal condition.
- **Heart-rate by self-report only**; ECG monitoring recommended (prior studies showed HR rise in 67% with fast LIO; slow 4-min deposition here likely blunted perception).
- **Invasiveness/cost of LIO device** (cortical bone drilling, QuickSleeper5 equipment) limits routine use; ABI remains the fallback when the device is unavailable.

## 6. Related Work

- Remmers 2008 (J Endod): intraosseous 2% lidocaine 87% success in irreversible pulpitis vs 60% IANB.
- Corbett 2008 / Kanaa 2006 / Robertson 2007 / Nydegger 2014: articaine buccal infiltration ~65% success for mandibular molars; articaine faster to negative EPT than lidocaine (4.2 vs 7.7 min).
- Replogle 1999 / Susi 2008: cardiovascular effects of intraosseous lidocaine 1:100,000 epi — HR rise greater with fast deposition.
- [[wiki/drug/karm-2017-lidocaine-epinephrine-200000-third-molar]] — concentration axis (1:80,000 vs 1:200,000) for mandibular surgery; pairs with this technique-axis study.
- [[wiki/drug/hwang-2023-proposal-clinical-guidelines-local-anesthesia-dentistry]] — Korean clinical-guideline framing for mandibular anesthesia difficulty.

## 7. Glossary

- **ABI**: Articaine buccal infiltration anesthesia.
- **LIO**: Lidocaine intraosseous anesthesia (here via computer-controlled QuickSleeper5).
- **IANB / LIANB**: Inferior alveolar nerve block (lidocaine IANB).
- **ICDAS 5–6**: International Caries Detection and Assessment System scores 5–6 (decay into inner 1/3 dentin, close to pulp).
- **Heft-Parker VAS**: 0–170 visual analogue scale for pain intensity.
- **EPT**: Electric pulp testing (used to confirm onset/anesthesia depth).
- **QuickSleeper5**: Computer-controlled intraosseous anesthesia device (DHT, Cholet, France).
