## One-line Summary

Scoping review (JBI method; 1,727 records → 16 articles) of dentist–patient relationship (DPR) determinants — factors grouped into 6 domains (dentist, patient, society/environment, clinical structure, clinical process, outcome); "communication" was the most frequent determinant (8×), then "trust" (6×), with patient-level "dental fear/anxiety" commonly used to measure DPR.

## 한줄요약

의사-환자 관계(DPR) 결정요인 scoping review(JBI, 1,727건 → 16편) — 요인을 6개 domain(치과의사·환자·사회환경·임상구조·임상과정·결과)으로 분류. 가장 빈번한 결정요인은 "커뮤니케이션"(8회), 다음 "신뢰"(6회), 환자 수준에서는 "치과 공포/불안"이 DPR 측정에 자주 사용.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: International Dental Journal 2024 (FDI World Dental Federation), open access CC BY-NC-ND
- DOI: 10.1016/j.identj.2024.02.015
- Affiliation: Seoul National University (Dental Education / Preventive & Social Dentistry)
- Study type: Scoping review (Joanna Briggs Institute guidance)
- Search: 6 major electronic databases, July 2023; participants = general adults + dentists

## 2. Key Contributions
- Maps the dispersed DPR literature into a 6-domain conceptual model rather than relying on normative/ethical assertions or extrapolation from generic medicine.
- Ranks determinant frequency: communication (8) > trust (6); identifies clinical-process domain as where most determinants sit.
- Flags that DPR research is overwhelmingly from the patient perspective (14/16) and lacks a validated DPR evaluation scale.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 1,727 records identified → 16 included (9 quantitative, 7 nonempirical).
- Determinants charted and classified against a conceptual DPR model with 6 domains.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- 6 domains: dentist, patient, society/environment, clinical structure, clinical process, outcome.
- Most determinants in clinical-process domain; "communication" most frequently counted (8), "trust" (6).
- Patient-level "dental fear/anxiety" frequently used as a DPR measure → ties relationship quality to anxiety.
- All but 2 articles took the patient perspective.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Scoping (not systematic) review — maps extent, does not appraise effect or quality.
- Small included set (16); heterogeneous designs; mostly patient-side.
- Calls for a comprehensive DPR framework and a validated evaluation scale.

## 7. Glossary
- **DPR**: Dentist–Patient Relationship.
- **Scoping review**: maps the extent, range, and nature of evidence on a topic (vs. SR which appraises and synthesizes effect).
- **Determinant**: factor influencing the construct (here, relationship quality).
