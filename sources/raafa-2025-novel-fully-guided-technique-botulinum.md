## One-line Summary
Prospective double-blinded RCT (n=20) showing that a CT-segmentation–based 3D-printed needle guide for BoNT-A injection into the lateral pterygoid muscle is **equally effective as EMG-guided injection** for TMJ disc displacement with reduction (DDWR), with significantly greater LPM-tenderness reduction at 3 and 6 months.

## 1. Document Information
- Study type: Prospective, double-blinded, randomized controlled trial
- Setting: Faculty of Dentistry, Alexandria University, Egypt
- Ethics: IRB No. 001056 — IORG 0008839; Ethics committee No. 0837-01/2024 (approved 2024-01-16)
- Registration: ClinicalTrials.gov NCT06633445 (retrospectively registered 2024-10-01)
- Reporting: CONSORT-compliant; Helsinki Declaration
- Indication: TMJ disc displacement with reduction (DDWR) per DC/TMD criteria + MRI confirmation

## 2. Key Contributions
1. Introduces a **muscle-segmentation–based 3D virtual augmented model** of the LPM (not just bone-based estimation) for needle planning — first such technique reported for BoNT-A LPM injection.
2. Direct head-to-head RCT vs the current standard (EMG guidance) for LPM injection — demonstrates **non-inferiority on most outcomes and superiority on LPM tenderness** at 3 and 6 months.
3. Provides a reproducible, cost-effective, low-experience-dependent workflow (CT + intraoral scan → Mimics segmentation → Blue Sky Plan needle planning → 3D-printed needle insertion guide).
4. Confirms BoNT-A LPM injection effectiveness for DDWR symptoms (clicking elimination 100% at 1 mo, sustained disc-position improvement, MIO recovery, TMJ/LPM tenderness reduction).
5. Reduces dependence on EMG operator skill and avoids out-of-target injection complications (dysphagia, nasal regurgitation, nasal tone) associated with EMG-guided technique.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Population: 20 patients with DDWR (DC/TMD + MRI), ages 20–45 y, Angle Class I; group I (study, fully guided): 9 F + 1 M, age 27.30 ± 5.36 y; group II (control, EMG): 10 F, age 23.10 ± 2.85 y.
- Exclusions: degenerative joint disease, neurological/musculoskeletal/bleeding disorders, pregnancy/lactation, prior TMJ surgery, Botox hypersensitivity, posterior edentulous saddles, anterior open bite, MRI contraindication.
- Sample size: 20 (10/group), based on MIO change effect size, α=0.05, power 80% (Rosner's method, G*Power 3.1.9.7), referencing prior LPM-BoNT studies.

### Group I (fully guided) workflow
1. Intraoral digital scan of maxillary teeth (CEREC Omnicam AC; STL).
2. CT scan with soft-tissue window (0.5 mm slice), patient biting on a bite block to prevent inter-arch contact (DICOM).
3. Mimics innovation suite (Materialise): segment skull bone, then segment right and left LPM in axial cuts with lasso tool, merge into 3D model (STL).
4. Blue Sky Plan v4.7: import DICOM + both STL files; place "customized implant" representing needle; design needle insertion guide with guide tube (1.5 mm hole diameter, 15–20 mm height, zero offset) from buccal of upper molars to center of LPM, confirming from all 3D views.
5. Guide 3D-printed in standard resin (Esun).
6. Injection: patient upright on bite block, 25 U BTX-A (Botox; Allergan) via 22-G cannula needle through the guide, negative aspiration before injection.

### Group II (EMG-guided) workflow
- 27-G monopolar cannulated needle electrode inserted intraorally above the upper molar, lateral to maxillary tuberosity.
- Patient manipulates mandible until loud signal from EMG device (Neuro-EMG-Micro; Neurosoft); aspirate and inject 25 U BTX-A.

### Post-op
Both groups: open/close several times post-injection, stay upright for 4 h to avoid BTX diffusion to pharyngeal muscles.

### Outcomes
- MIO (mm) — bilateral, baseline and 1, 3, 6 mo.
- TMJ clicking — present/absent, bilateral palpation at lateral pole anterior to tragus.
- LPM tenderness — intraoral palpation behind maxillary tuberosity; VAS 0–10.
- TMJ tenderness — palpation; VAS 0–10.
- Disc position — Kruita method on MRI at 3 mo: TD/TP ratio where T = lowest articular eminence edge, P = highest external auditory canal edge, D = intersection at posterior disc.

### Randomization & Blinding
- Computer-generated random list (Microsoft Excel); sealed envelopes opened by assistant 2 days pre-procedure (to allow guide fabrication).
- Outcome assessor and data analyst blinded (operator and patient could not be blinded due to the visible guide).
- Statistics: SPSS 20; Shapiro-Wilk → Student t / Mann-Whitney; RM-ANOVA + Bonferroni / Friedman + Dunn; categorical: Chi-square / Fisher; α=0.05.
- No dropouts.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Maximum Interincisal Opening (MIO)
- Group I: insignificant decrease at 1 mo (p=0.163); significant improvement at 3 and 6 mo (p<0.001*).
- Group II: significant decrease at 1 mo (p<0.001*); significant improvement at 3 and 6 mo (p<0.001*).
- No statistically significant inter-group difference (p=0.201, 0.122, 0.080 at 1, 3, 6 mo).

### Disc position (TD/TP, Kruita method, 3 mo)
- Group I: 0.40 ± 0.04 → 0.48 ± 0.04.
- Group II: 0.43 ± 0.03 → 0.51 ± 0.05.
- Both significantly improved within group; no inter-group difference (p=0.291).

### Clicking
- 100% disappearance in both groups at 1 mo.
- At 3 mo: 1/10 in group I and 2/10 in group II regained clicking; stable through 6 mo without further injection.

### LPM tenderness (VAS 0–10, median (IQR))
| | Pre-op | 1 mo | 3 mo | 6 mo |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Group I (test) | 10 (10–10) | 0 (0–0) | 0 (0–1) | 0 (0–2) |
| Group II (control) | 10 (8–10) | 1 (0–4) | 4 (2–5) | 4 (2–5) |
| p-value (inter-group) | 0.579 | 0.052 | **0.009\*** | **0.015\*** |
- Intra-group reduction significant in both (p<0.001).
- **Group I superior at 3 and 6 mo** for LPM tenderness reduction.

### TMJ tenderness
- Both groups: significant reduction at all follow-ups; no inter-group difference (p=1, 0.739, 0.739).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small sample (n=20) and single-center.
- Sex imbalance (group I had 1 male; group II all female).
- Group I patients tend to be older than group II (27.3 vs 23.1 y); no formal adjustment.
- CT radiation exposure is a real drawback vs EMG (no ionizing radiation); CBCT-only protocol with similar segmentation accuracy may be the next step.
- 6-month follow-up only — durability of disc reduction and symptom relief beyond 6 mo unknown.
- Operator-blinding impossible due to visible guide; potential performance bias.
- The 25 U fixed dose was used; dose-response not addressed.
- Guide fabrication adds 2-day pre-procedural lead time and per-patient cost (3D-printed resin); cost-effectiveness vs disposable EMG electrodes not formally analyzed.
- No assessment of intra-articular pressure, joint sounds via vibration analysis, or jaw kinematics.

## 7. Glossary
- **LPM** — Lateral Pterygoid Muscle (외측익돌근); deep masticatory muscle, two heads, inserts into condyle and TMJ articular disc.
- **DDWR** — Disc Displacement With Reduction (정복성 관절원판변위); anteriorly displaced disc that returns to position on opening.
- **DC/TMD** — Diagnostic Criteria for Temporomandibular Disorders; standardized TMD classification.
- **BTX-A** — Botulinum Toxin Type A (here: onabotulinumtoxinA, Botox; Allergan).
- **MIO** — Maximum Interincisal Opening (mm).
- **TD/TP** — Kruita method ratio for MRI disc-position assessment.
- **STL** — Stereolithography file (3D surface mesh).
- **DICOM** — Digital Imaging and Communications in Medicine (medical-imaging standard).
- **CAD/CAM** — Computer-Aided Design / Computer-Aided Manufacture.
- **Mimics / Blue Sky Plan** — Software for medical image segmentation and surgical planning.
- **EMG** — Electromyography; functional muscle-detection guidance for needle placement.
