## Why Ingested

노출 0% 사례를 보고한 소규모 후향 연구로서, 메쉬 설계(마이크로퍼포레이션 grade-2 Ti) + 순수 자가골 조합이 [[wiki/implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/sabri-2024-titanium-mesh-bone-augmentation-sr-ma]] 풀드 노출률(10.8%)과 상반되는 실제 zero-exposure 사례를 제공한다. 왜 노출이 생기지 않았는지에 대한 기술적 가설(micro-perforated design, 2-screw, pure autograft)을 문헌화.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective (n=16): 3D-printed micro-perforated grade-2 Ti mesh + pure autologous bone → 0% mesh exposure, targeted augmentation achieved in all cases (planned = real CBCT outcomes).

## 한줄요약

후향적 연구 (n=16): 3D 프린팅 마이크로퍼포레이션 grade-2 Ti 메쉬 + 순수 자가골 → 메쉬 노출 0%, 계획과 실제 CBCT 골증대량 일치.

## 1. Document Information

- **Study type**: Retrospective case series
- **Journal**: International Journal of Oral & Maxillofacial Implants
- **Year**: 2026 (epub 2026-02-10)
- **DOI**: 10.11607/jomi.11587
- **PMID**: 41665971
- **N**: 16 patients, 102 CBCT measurements (3 operators)
- **Healing period**: 122–160 days before implant placement

## 2. Key Contributions

- First series to report zero mesh exposure in 16 consecutive patients using micro-perforated DMLS Ti mesh
- Demonstrates planned vs real augmentation equivalence via CBCT subtraction (statistical: linear mixed model)
- Specific technique: pure autologous bone only (no xenograft); 2 vestibular screws; secondary slight GBR at re-entry
- Grade-2 Ti (softer, more biocompatible than grade-4 or grade-5)
- DMLS manufacturing (Direct Metal Laser Sintering) for individualized mesh

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Retrospective, single center
- Mesh design: CAD/CAM, micro-perforated, DMLS grade-2 titanium
- Fixation: 2 vestibular screws only
- Graft: PURE autologous bone (no xenograft)
- Two-step: mesh placement → ≥4 months healing → mesh removal + implant placement (+ secondary slight GBR)
- Outcome measurement: pre- and post-surgical CBCT; 3 operators × 102 measurements; linear mixed model
- Augmentation efficacy defined as cm³ gain / mm of planned augmentation

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Mesh exposure**: 0/16 (0%) — no complications
- **All patients**: sufficient bone for implant placement after 122–160 days
- **Planned vs real augmentation**: no significant difference (linear mixed model, p>0.05)
- Targeted augmentation achieved in 100% of cases

Hypothesized reasons for zero exposure:
1. Micro-perforated design → tissue compliance, reduced mucosal tension
2. Pure autologous bone → faster integration, less inflammatory response than xenograft
3. 2-screw vestibular fixation → minimal mesh bulk, lower profile
4. Grade-2 Ti → softer, higher biocompatibility vs grade-4/5

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small n=16, single center, retrospective
- No control group; no long-term implant outcomes
- Short-term data only (4–5 months to implant placement)
- Cannot fully attribute zero exposure to single factor — technique is composite

## 7. Glossary

- **DMLS**: Direct Metal Laser Sintering — additive manufacturing for metal
- **Grade-2 Ti**: commercially pure titanium, higher ductility/biocompatibility vs alloy grades
- **Micro-perforated**: fine perforations that allow tissue ingrowth while maintaining rigidity
- **Secondary slight GBR**: minor additional grafting performed at re-entry surgery
- **CBCT subtraction analysis**: volumetric comparison of pre- and post-op 3D bone models
