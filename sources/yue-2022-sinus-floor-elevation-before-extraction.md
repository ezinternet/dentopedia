## One-line Summary
First reported case (3-year follow-up) of **Modified Lateral Sinus Floor Elevation (MSFE) performed *before* extraction** of a periodontally hopeless maxillary molar (#16): residual bone height augmented from 1–2 mm to 12–14 mm; tooth retained 13 months pre-extraction; immediate implant placement at extraction; predominantly new bone histologically; <2 mm bone loss after 3 y of loading, no peri-implantitis.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Proposes and documents a **novel timing strategy**: lateral sinus floor elevation (LSFE) is performed *prior to* extraction of a periodontally hopeless tooth — the tooth then serves as a "tent" / spacer while the graft consolidates, allowing immediate implant placement at extraction.
- Shortens edentulous interval and preserves residual chewing function, addressing patient acceptance issues for staged LSFE.
- Reports histologic evidence of new bone formation and 3-year implant function in the augmented site.
- Includes a paired conventional (post-extraction) LSFE on contralateral tooth #26 for informal comparison.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 33-year-old female with aggressive periodontitis, hopeless #16 and #26 (Class II–III mobility, PD 6–10 mm), 1–2 mm residual bone height.
- Initial periodontal therapy (IPT) and frequent SPT until remittent status achieved.
- **MSFE on #16**: crevicular incision, full-thickness flap, 5 × 8 mm lateral antrostomy at apex of hopeless tooth (≥3–5 mm above sinus floor), sinus membrane elevated ≥10 mm, lined with Bio-Gide collagen membrane, grafted with 1.25 g Bio-Oss small particles, antrostomy covered with collagen membrane, Prolene 4-0 sutures.
- Tooth #16 preserved 13 months with monthly SPT to control biofilm and maintain pulp vitality.
- Atraumatic extraction → biopsy via 2-mm trephine → immediate implant placement (4.8 × 10 mm SLA Straumann, torque 35 N·cm, 1 mm below buccal plate); gap <2 mm, no additional graft; healing abutment + closure.
- Loading after 4 months healing; cement-retained zirconia crown.
- Contralateral #26: conventional post-extraction LSFE (Bio-Oss + Bio-Gide) by same surgeon, implant placed 7 months later.
- Antibiotic prophylaxis: amoxicillin 500 mg pre-op + 3 × daily × 7 d.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Bone height augmentation**: 1–2 mm → 12–14 mm before extraction.
- **Pulp vitality**: preserved throughout 13-month pre-extraction interval.
- **Tooth retention pre-extraction**: 13 months partial function.
- **Implant insertion torque**: 35 N·cm.
- **Histology at extraction site**: predominance of new bone.
- **3-year post-loading**: <2 mm bone height resorption; no peri-implantitis; no mobility; PD 2–4 mm.
- **Comparator (#26 conventional LSFE)**: same outcome qualitatively but with extended edentulous interval (>7 mo before implant).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Single case — generalizability undefined; no controlled study yet.
- Indication restricted to teeth with controllable periodontal disease (remittent status) and intact sinus floor anatomy.
- Risk of infection propagation from periodontally compromised tooth into the graft not directly assessed.
- Long-term peri-implant bone stability beyond 3 y still to be confirmed.
- Authors call for controlled studies and clear indications/safety criteria.

## 6. Related Work
- Tatum 1986 — lateral window method (basis for MSFE).
- Boyne & James 1980 — original maxillary sinus floor grafting.
- Pjetursson & Lang 2014 — review of sinus elevation techniques.
- Estrin 2025 — alternative lateral window barrier strategies (e-PRF).
- Ayubian Markazi 2018; Liu 2019 — combined immediate implant + transcrestal lift comparators.

## 7. Glossary
- **MSFE (Modified Sinus Floor Elevation)**: LSFE performed before extraction of a periodontally hopeless tooth.
- **LSFE (Lateral Sinus Floor Elevation)**: classical lateral window approach to maxillary sinus floor.
- **IPT / SPT**: Initial Periodontal Therapy / Supportive Periodontal Therapy.
- **PD**: Probing Depth.
- **Bio-Oss / Bio-Gide**: deproteinized bovine bone mineral / porcine collagen membrane (Geistlich).
