## Why Ingested

총의치 수직고경(Occlusal Vertical Dimension, OVD) 결정의 고전적 토대 — "하악 안정위(rest position) + 자유공극(freeway space)" 방법이 왜 불안정한지를 두부계측(cephalometric)으로 보여준 1975년 원전으로, [[complete-denture/alhajj-2017-occlusal-vertical-dimension-complete-denture-review]]·[[complete-denture/fayad-2025-vertical-dimension-determination-prosthodontics-review]]가 "단일 방법으로 안정위에만 의존하지 말라"고 권고하는 근거의 역사적 출발점이다.

## One-line Summary

Classic cephalometric study (n=50 complete denture wearers) measuring mandibular rest-position vertical dimension with facial lead markers vs skeletal landmarks, edentulous vs with-denture: facial soft tissue masks true mandibular movement, rest position is variable and shifts on denture insertion, and the edentulous rest position is therefore not a reliable basis for determining OVD.

## 한줄요약

총의치 착용자 50명의 하악 안정위 수직고경을 두부계측 방사선상에서 안면 표지(lead marker)와 골격 표지로 측정(무치악 vs 의치 장착): 안면 연조직이 실제 하악 움직임을 가려 안정위가 변동적이고 의치 장착 시 이동하므로, 무치악 안정위는 OVD 결정의 신뢰할 기준이 못 됨.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Vertical dimension measurements
- **Authors**: Irving M. Sheppard, Stephen M. Sheppard
- **Journal**: The Journal of Prosthetic Dentistry, 1975;34(3):269–277
- **DOI**: 10.1016/0022-3913(75)90103-1
- **Type**: Clinical cephalometric study (in vivo)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantified, on cephalometric radiographs, how unreliable the edentulous mandibular rest position is as a reference for OVD.
- Showed facial (soft-tissue) measurements mask skeletal mandibular position — a caution against purely facial VD methods.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Subjects**: 50 complete denture wearers.
- **Method**: cephalometric radiographs with lead markers attached to the face and skeletal landmarks; vertical dimension of mandibular rest position measured both facially (between markers) and skeletally (between landmarks).
- **Comparison**: radiographs taken **without dentures (edentulous)** vs **with dentures (edentoprosthetic)** in the mouth.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

The findings support seven statements:
1. Facial structures tended to **mask** the positional changes of the mandible.
2. The **edentulous** mandibular rest position varied within the time frame of the exam (not constant).
3. The range of skeletal rest-position measurements was **greater with greater denture age/experience**.
4. The **edentoprosthetic** rest position was more frequently constant when measured **skeletally** than facially, and more constant than the edentulous rest position.
5. On denture insertion, the edentulous rest position **increased** for most subjects (decreased for the remainder).
6. Subjects whose rest position **decreased (up-shifted)** on insertion had **older dentures** and more years of wearing experience.
7. The edentulous mandibular rest position is **not particularly suitable** for determining VDO, and even less so when facial measurements are used.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- 1975 cephalometric (2-D radiographic) methodology; manual landmark identification.
- Rest position is itself a variable physiologic target; single-session imaging.
- Descriptive (no modern inferential modeling); establishes caution rather than an alternative protocol.

## 7. Glossary

- **VDO / OVD (Vertical Dimension of Occlusion)**: lower-face height with teeth in occlusion.
- **Rest position (rest VD)**: mandibular postural position with muscles at minimal tonus; OVD is classically derived as rest VD minus freeway space.
- **Freeway space (interocclusal rest space)**: gap between teeth at rest, ~2–4 mm.
- **Edentulous vs edentoprosthetic**: without vs with dentures in the mouth.
- **Cephalometric radiograph**: standardized lateral skull radiograph for landmark measurement.
