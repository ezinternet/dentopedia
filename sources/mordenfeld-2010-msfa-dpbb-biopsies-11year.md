## One-line Summary
Human biopsies harvested 11 years after MSFA with 80% DPBB + 20% autogenous bone (n=11 patients): 44.7% lamellar bone, 38% marrow, 17.3% DPBB; DPBB-bone contact 61.5%; particle size unchanged vs 6-month and pristine — DPBB does not resorb, integrates as a permanent osteoconductive scaffold.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Clinical Oral Implants Research 2010;21(9):961–970 (e-pub 3 Aug 2010).
- **Study type**: Prospective long-term histomorphometric analysis (re-entry biopsy 11 years after grafting).
- **Setting**: Swedish multi-center (Gävle Public Dental Health, Albrektsson group).

## 2. Key Contributions
- Provides the **longest histomorphometric data** (mean 11.5 years) on DPBB particles in human maxillary sinus grafts.
- Demonstrates DPBB particles **do not resorb significantly** over 11 years — particle length and area equal to 6-month samples and to pristine (un-implanted) particles.
- Quantifies tissue composition at 11 years: 44.7% lamellar bone / 38% marrow / 17.3% DPBB, with 61.5% DPBB-to-bone contact.
- Settles the long-standing debate on DPBB resorption: clinically meaningful resorption does not occur — DPBB acts as a permanent space-maintaining osteoconductive scaffold.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 20 patients (14 women, 6 men; mean 62 yr) with severe posterior maxillary atrophy underwent MSFA in 30 sinuses (subantral bone <5 mm) with 80% DPBB + 20% autogenous bone.
- 11 patients volunteered for re-entry biopsy at mean 11.5 years (range presumably ~11 yr).
- Biopsies non-decalcified, plastic-embedded; histomorphometric measurements: total bone area %, total DPBB area %, marrow space area %, DPBB-bone contact (% of particle surface length), particle length, particle area.
- Comparator: 6-month biopsies from same patients (n=9 paired samples) + pristine particles from manufacturer.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Lamellar bone**: 44.7 ± 16.9%
- **Marrow space**: 38 ± 16.9%
- **DPBB area**: 17.3 ± 13.2%
- **DPBB-bone contact**: 61.5 ± 34% (of particle surface length)
- **Particle length & area at 11 yr**: NS difference vs 6-month same-patient samples or vs pristine particles
- → DPBB particles **well integrated in lamellar bone** with no measurable resorption over 11 years.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- 11/20 patient subset (55%) — selection bias possible (volunteers).
- Standard deviation for DPBB-bone contact wide (±34%) — site-level heterogeneity.
- Histomorphometry only — no implant-level clinical data in this paper (see Mordenfeld 2014 companion).
- 80:20 mix only — pure DPBB long-term data not addressed here.

## 7. Glossary
- **DPBB (Deproteinized Bovine Bone)**: Anorganic bovine bone graft; Bio-Oss is prototypical product.
- **MSFA (Maxillary Sinus Floor Augmentation)**: Lateral window or transcrestal elevation of sinus floor with graft.
- **Lamellar bone**: Mature, organized bone tissue with concentric Haversian systems.
- **Particle surface contact**: Fraction of graft particle perimeter directly apposed to bone (vs marrow/soft tissue).
- **Non-decalcified plastic embedding**: Sectioning bone without acid demineralization, preserving mineralized phase — required for accurate residual particle quantification.
