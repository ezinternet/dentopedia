## One-line Summary

Retrospective CBCT study (680 scans, 1,340 mandibular sides): retromolar canal (RMC) found in 16.12% of sides, carrying accessory nerve fibers alongside blood vessels — a documented anatomical cause of mandibular block anesthesia failure and a structure to spare or additionally anesthetize during retromolar surgery.

## 한줄요약

후향적 CBCT 연구(680건, 하악 1,340측): 후구치관(retromolar canal, RMC)이 16.12%에서 관찰되었고 혈관과 함께 부가신경섬유를 포함 — 하악블록마취 실패의 문서화된 해부학적 원인이며, 후구치부 수술 시 보존하거나 추가 국소마취를 고려해야 할 구조.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies the **prevalence (16.12% of mandibular sides)** and **morphology (type A1 vertical 39.82%, type C horizontal 6.02% least common)** of the retromolar canal (RMC) using CBCT — the largest such series cited in this cluster.
- Confirms the RMC contains **accessory nerve fibers** in addition to blood vessels, giving it direct relevance to anesthesia failure and to surgical injury risk (hemorrhage, dysesthesia) in the retromolar region.
- Provides quantitative anatomical benchmarks (diameter, height, distance to second molar) useful for preoperative CBCT planning.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Retrospective analysis of 680 CBCT scans (1,340 mandibular sides).
- RMC identified, classified by course type (A1 vertical, others including horizontal type C), and measured (diameter, height, distance to second molar).
- Statistical testing for demographic and CBCT-resolution effects on RMC frequency.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **216/1,340 mandibular sides (16.12%)** had an identifiable RMC — roughly 1 in 6 retromolar areas.
- Type A1 (vertical course): 39.82% (most common); Type C (horizontal course): 6.02% (least common).
- Mean RMC diameter: 1.03 mm (SD 0.27); mean height: 10.19 mm (SD 2.64); mean distance to second molar: 15.10 mm (SD 2.83).
- No significant effect of demographic factors or CBCT spatial resolution on RMC frequency.
- Clinical recommendation: spare the RMC during retromolar surgery, or consider additional locoregional anesthesia in the retromolar region when one is present.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Retrospective, single-modality (CBCT) imaging study — no direct histological confirmation of nerve fiber content within each identified canal (based on established anatomical literature + imaging correlation).
- Abstract-only ingest — full classification-type breakdown (percentages for all RMC types, not just the extremes) not retrieved.

## 7. Glossary

- **Retromolar canal (RMC)**: an anatomical variant branching from the mandibular canal toward the retromolar region, carrying blood vessels and accessory nerve fibers.
- **CBCT (cone beam computed tomography)**: 3D dental imaging modality used here to identify and measure the RMC preoperatively.
