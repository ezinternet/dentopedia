## Why Ingested

나사 풀림(abutment-screw loosening)은 임플란트 보철 합병증 중 가장 흔한 것 중 하나이며, 재조임(retightening) 타이밍이 전조임 소실(preload loss)에 미치는 영향에 대한 체계적 데이터가 부족하다. 본 논문은 내부 vs 외부 육각 연결부(internal vs external hexagon)에서 재조임 시점(2·5·10분)과 전조임 유지량을 직접 비교하여 [[wiki/prosthetic-materials/sun-2026-abutment-contamination-internal-hex-preload]]가 다루는 오염 변수와 함께 나사 조임 프로토콜의 기계적 근거를 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

In vitro study (n=80 implants, internal vs external hexagon) showed retightening at 2 minutes after initial torque (35 Ncm) significantly reduced preload loss compared to 5- or 10-minute intervals or no retightening.

## 한줄요약

체외 연구(내·외부 육각 임플란트 각 40개): 초기 조임(35 Ncm) 후 2분 시점 재조임이 5분·10분·비재조임군 대비 전조임 소실을 유의하게 감소시켰다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of Oral Implantology, Vol. XLVI, No. 1, 2020
- **DOI**: 10.1563/aaid-joi-D-18-00138
- **Institution**: Department of Medical, Oral and Biotechnological Sciences, University of Chieti–Pescara, Chieti, Italy
- **Study type**: In vitro experimental study
- **Sample size**: 80 implants total — 40 internal hexagon (IG) + 40 external hexagon (EG), each subdivided into 4 subgroups of 10

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Established that retightening at **2 minutes** post-initial tightening produces significantly less preload loss than at 5 or 10 minutes for both connection types.
2. Demonstrated that **internal hexagon** connections maintain higher residual torque (lower preload loss) than external hexagon connections across all time groups.
3. Provided quantitative torque-loss data as a function of retightening delay, filling a gap not addressed by prior in vitro studies (Dixon 1995, Bakaeen 2002, Siamons, Tzenakis).
4. Proposed that a standardized 2-minute retightening protocol should be evaluated in randomized clinical trials.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Implants**: WAY Milan (Geass S.r.l., Udine, Italy) — 3.8 × 15 mm internal hexagon (IG) and external hexagon (EG) titanium implants with titanium abutment screws (WAPM0014184)
- **Embedding**: Acrylic resin (Orthojet) in PVC cylinders (26 mm diameter, 20 mm height) to simulate a rigid bone-analog environment
- **Torque application**: Digital torque meter (HTG2-5 N, Imada, Japan), decimal precision (0.1 Ncm), square hand wrench; initial torque 35 Ncm per manufacturer recommendation
- **Subgroups**:
  - IG0 / EG0: control (no retightening)
  - IG2 / EG2: retightening at 2 minutes
  - IG5 / EG5: retightening at 5 minutes
  - IG10 / EG10: retightening at 10 minutes
- **Detorque measurement**: All implants detorqued at 30 minutes post-initial tightening
- **Blinding/bias control**: Single operator, same morning session over 3 consecutive days
- **Outlier removal**: Chauvenet's and Pierce's Criteria — 5 outliers removed from 80 data points
- **Statistics**: Shapiro-Wilk normality → Student t-test (each retightening group vs control) + one-way ANOVA; P < 0.05

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Subgroup | Retightening time | Preload loss (mean ± SD) | vs control significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| IG0 | None | Highest loss | — (reference) |
| IG2 | 2 min | Lowest loss in IG | P < 0.05 |
| IG5 | 5 min | Intermediate | P < 0.05 |
| IG10 | 10 min | Higher than IG2/IG5 | P < 0.05 |
| EG0 | None | Highest loss in EG | — (reference) |
| EG2 | 2 min | Lowest loss in EG | P < 0.05 |
| EG5 | 5 min | Intermediate | P < 0.05 |
| EG10 | 10 min | Higher than EG2/EG5 | P < 0.05 |

- **Internal hexagon** consistently showed lower absolute preload loss than external hexagon at matched time points.
- **2-minute retightening** was the single most effective interval across both connection designs.
- Settling effect literature range: 2–15% of initial preload lost (confirmed consistent with this study).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **In vitro only**: Acrylic resin ≠ living bone; no cyclic occlusal loading applied during the 30-minute observation window.
- **Static load**: Does not simulate dynamic/fatigue loading that occurs clinically.
- **Single torque value**: Only 35 Ncm tested; manufacturers may recommend different values for different systems.
- **Single implant brand**: Geass S.r.l. WAY Milan system — generalizability to other systems unconfirmed.
- **Short observation window**: 30-minute total; long-term relaxation not captured.
- **Authors' recommendation**: RCTs needed to validate 2-minute retightening as a standard clinical protocol.

## 6. Related Work

- Dixon et al (1995): Proposed retightening at 10 minutes — first in vitro protocol for settling effect reduction.
- Bakaeen et al (2002): Supported 10-minute retightening in vitro.
- Siamons et al: Also supported 10-minute interval.
- Tzenakis et al: Suggested retightening at 5 minutes (half preload lost immediately, rest over 5 minutes).
- This study contradicts the 10-minute recommendation and refines the optimal window to 2 minutes.

## 7. Glossary

- **Preload**: Tensile force generated within an abutment screw when tightening torque is applied; proportional to tightening torque and responsible for clamping force.
- **Settling effect (embedding relaxation)**: Flattening of microscopic surface irregularities at the abutment-screw/implant interface under load, causing a reduction in preload immediately after tightening. Responsible for 2–15% preload loss.
- **Detorque**: Removal torque — the torque required to loosen the abutment screw; used here as a proxy measurement for residual preload at 30 minutes.
- **Internal hexagon**: Implant-abutment connection where the hexagonal anti-rotation feature is located inside the implant body; generally offers more resistance to micromovement than external hexagon.
- **External hexagon**: Classic Brånemark-derived design with hexagonal protrusion on the implant platform.
- **Ncm**: Newton-centimeter, unit of torque.
