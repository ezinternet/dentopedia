## One-line Summary
Narrative review covering prosthodontic-specific modifications for 9 systemic disease groups: arthritis, CVD, endocrine, hematologic/oncologic, neurologic, bone disorders, pulmonary, liver, kidney disease.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: International Journal of Dentistry 2022, Article ID 7510578
- DOI: 10.1155/2022/7510578
- Design: Narrative review

## 2. Key Contributions
- Prosthodontics-specific (not general dentistry) management modifications per disease
- Covers rarely-discussed conditions: CKD, cirrhosis, COPD in the prosthetic context
- Drug interaction alerts for prosthodontic drugs (methacrylate, LA)

## 3. Disease-Specific Prosthodontic Considerations

### Arthritis (OA / RA)
- OA/TMJ: short appointments, special impression trays, reversible procedures in active phase
- RA: fixed over removable (manual dexterity ↓); prosthetic joints → prophylactic antibiotics for implant placement
- Methotrexate/DMARDs → delayed healing; consult rheumatologist

### Cardiovascular Disease
- All CVD: short appointments, morning preferred (midday actually), profound LA
- Anticoagulants: do NOT stop for impressions/try-ins; adjust for surgical phases
- Post-MI: delay definitive prosthetics ≥6 months (active phase); emergency only in first month

### Endocrine Disorders
- DM: poorly controlled → delayed healing, infection risk; HbA1c check pre-implant
- Hypothyroidism: CNS depressant sensitivity ↑; hyperthyroidism: arrhythmia risk with epinephrine
- Adrenal insufficiency (steroid users): stress protocol (corticosteroid supplementation)

### Hematologic / Oncologic
- Radiation therapy head/neck → xerostomia, mucositis, OSTEORADIONECROSIS risk
- ORN: no implants in irradiated bone without HBO therapy evaluation
- Leukemia/lymphoma: avoid elective in active chemotherapy phase; WBC/platelet check

### Neurologic
- Parkinson's: tremor → difficult impressions; fixed prosthetics preferred
- Stroke: anticoagulants/antiplatelets must be continued; LA with epinephrine: caution if BP uncontrolled
- Seizure disorders: avoid triggers; remove removable prosthetics pre-procedure

### Bone Disorders (Bisphosphonates / BRONJ)
- Oral bisphosphonate >3 years → BRONJ risk for implants and surgical extractions
- Recommend drug holiday (2 months before/after) in high-risk; IV bisphosphonate = contraindication

### Pulmonary (COPD / Asthma)
- Semi-supine position (not supine); rubber dam with care
- Aspirin/NSAIDs may trigger aspirin-exacerbated asthma; avoid or substitute

### Liver Disease (Cirrhosis)
- Impaired drug metabolism; LA dose reduction; avoid acetaminophen
- Coagulation factors ↓ → check INR; PT >1.5× normal → consult

### Chronic Kidney Disease
- Uremia → platelet dysfunction; epinephrine caution in hypertensive CKD
- Avoid nephrotoxic drugs (NSAIDs, aminoglycosides); dose adjust for renally-cleared drugs

## 4. Limitations
- Narrative review; variable evidence quality per disease category
- Prosthodontic focus excludes endodontic/surgical specifics
