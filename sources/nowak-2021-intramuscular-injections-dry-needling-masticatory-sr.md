## Why Ingested

Part of a TMD physical-therapy ingest batch. The spray-and-stretch / manual-therapy pages cover non-penetrating myofascial techniques; this SR maps the *needling* arm of myofascial pain management — dry needling plus intramuscular injections (botulinum toxin, local anesthetics, PRP, collagen) WITHIN masticatory muscles — so it complements those pages and supplies the technique-level (muscle, dose, navigation, sessions) detail that the BTX meta-analyses ([[wiki/tmj/saini-2024-botulinum-toxin-tmd-sr-ma]], [[wiki/tmj/zhu-2020-btx-painful-tmd-sr-ma]]) report only as pooled effects. It reinforces [[wiki/tmj/chan-2022-myogenous-tmd-diagnosis-treatment-review]] (myogenous TMD management) by detailing the invasive-needling options that review lists, and pairs with the dry-needling-vs-spray RCT [[wiki/tmj/ustun-2024-dry-needling-cold-spray-stretching-rct]].

## One-line Summary

PICOS/PRISMA systematic review of 28 clinical trials (≥evidence level 1–3) cataloguing intramuscular injection and dry-needling techniques in masticatory muscles for myofascial pain — masseter (65%) and temporalis (27%) dominate, BTX is the most-studied substance, palpation is the main navigation method, and pterygoid (lateral 8%, medial <1%) needling remains scarcely studied and unstandardized.

- **DOI**: 10.3390/ijerph18189552
- **Published**: 10 September 2021 (open access, CC BY)
- **Registration**: PROSPERO ID 272124
- **Type**: Systematic review of clinical trials (qualitative/technique mapping; no own pooled meta-analysis)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic review focused specifically on the **technical execution** of needling (which muscle, which substance, navigation method, dose, number of sessions) rather than on pooled efficacy — explicitly complementary to Al-Moraissi et al.'s 2020 network meta-analysis of efficacy.
- 28 included articles, broken down per target muscle into four tables (masseter, temporalis, lateral pterygoid, medial pterygoid). Because some papers needled multiple muscles/substances, these were counted as separate "trials" → distribution of interventions.
- Quantifies the field: masseter is the dominant target (65% of interventions; 711 patient cases), temporalis 27% (294 cases), lateral pterygoid 8% (90 cases), medial pterygoid <1% (5 cases).
- Maps substance use: botulinum toxin (BTX) most studied; for masseter, 17 BTX trials vs 7 local anesthetic, 4 deep dry needling, 2 PRP, 1 superficial dry needling, 1 collagen. Temporalis: all BTX except one lidocaine. Pterygoids: only BTX, deep dry needling, percutaneous needle electrolysis.
- Maps navigation: palpation 72.5%, EMG 11.8%, not-specified 9.8%, EPI® electrotherapy 3.9%, MRI 2.0%.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Framework**: PICOS eligibility + PRISMA reporting. Protocol registered (PROSPERO 272124).
- **Databases**: PubMed (MEDLINE/PMC) and BASE (Bielefeld Academic Search Engine), searched 18 April 2021. 367 records → 28 included after de-duplication (Rayyan QCRI), blind title/abstract screening (2 reviewers, Cohen's κ = 0.98) and blind full-text assessment (3 reviewers, total convergence).
- **Population**: adult patients with myofascial orofacial pain (RDC/TMD, DC/TMD or ICOP). Excluded: animals; fibromyalgia, neuralgia, oromandibular dystonia, cerebral palsy, masseter hypertrophy; experimentally induced pain.
- **Intervention**: intramuscular injections into and/or dry needling of masticatory muscles. Excluded: injections into non-masticatory muscles, nerve blockades, acupuncture, multi-muscle punctures.
- **Comparison**: any or none (control group not required).
- **Outcome**: primary = effective penetration of the target muscle; secondary = any positive treatment effect (pain decrease, increased maximum mouth opening, increased pressure pain threshold, MTP deactivation).
- **Study design**: primary studies of evidence level 1–3 (RCTs, prospective cohorts, retrospective case-control); English; published 2011 or later.
- **Important limitation by design**: the review deliberately did NOT assess scientific quality, risk of bias, or conflict of interest of the included studies — it evaluated similarities and divergences of technique, not effectiveness.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Included studies**: 28; 24/28 (86%) published 2016 or later.
- **Intervention counts (by muscle)**: 32 masseter "trials", 13 temporalis, 5 lateral pterygoid, 1 medial pterygoid.
- **Patient cases**: masseter 711, temporalis 294, lateral pterygoid 90, medial pterygoid 5. Group sizes ranged 5–61 (largest = BTX into masseter).
- **Substances (masseter)**: 17 BTX, 7 local anesthetic (lidocaine/mepivacaine/morphine sulfate), 4 deep dry needling (DDN), 2 PRP, 1 superficial dry needling (SDN), 1 collagen.
- **Dosing (masseter BTX)**: 20–150 IU, with 30–50 IU dominant. Temporalis BTX lower: 10–100 IU, dominant 20–25 IU. Lateral pterygoid BTX: 10 and 20 IU. Local anesthetic: 0.2–0.5 mL per puncture (Nitecka-Buchta used 2 mL per muscle).
- **Injection sites**: 51% of trials set a specific number of sites per muscle (masseter 2–5, temporalis 2–3); 37% targeted MTPs directly; 12% unspecified. Two-to-three punctures dominated; single puncture rare; Guarda-Nardini used a "chessboard" multi-puncture BTX pattern.
- **Navigation aids** beyond palpation: EMG (Ernberg audio-amplified; Patel monopolar electrode needle — also reached LPM; Sipahi-Calis reflex-guided); EPI®/percutaneous needle electrolysis (Lopez-Martos — earlier improvement than DDN, no adverse effects); MRI infrared navigation (Brainlab, Pons — high accuracy/safety for LPM upper head, but complex/costly, only 4/6 improved). Out-of-eligibility approaches noted: CT-derived custom intraoral guides (Oliveira, Yoshida) and ultrasound strain/shear-wave elastography (Sanabria, Olchowy).
- **Dry needling technique notes**: Hong's "fast in, fast out" with local twitch response (LTR) is classic, but Hakim/Perreault suggest LTR is not necessary and repeated LTR-requiring needling may impair reinnervation; Ozden found superficial DN out-performed deep DN for pain reduction (n=40).
- **BTX safety signal**: cited evidence of dose-related bone loss in masticatory complex (Raphael 2014 osteopenia pilot; De la Torre Canales 2020 dose-dependent coronoid/condylar volume loss — low dose safe), though Raphael 2020 (n=35) found no clinically significant bone change — monitoring advised at high doses.
- **Novel substances**: PRP (growth-factor-driven muscle regeneration; Al-Moraissi NMA ranked PRP most effective for short-term pain) and collagen (Nitecka-Buchta RCT: collagen superior to lidocaine) flagged as promising but low-quality evidence needing larger trials.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- By design, **no quality/risk-of-bias appraisal and no pooled meta-analysis** — pure technique cataloguing, so it cannot rank efficacy.
- High heterogeneity and lack of standardization across trials make objective comparison difficult; the authors call for a standardized needling protocol.
- Lateral and medial pterygoid needling is severely under-studied (5 and 1 papers) — a priority gap, especially given diagnostic/therapeutic value of LPM injection for TMJ internal derangement.
- Non-BTX substances (PRP, collagen) need high-quality, larger, longer-term trials.
- No comparison exists between MTP-targeted vs anatomically-standardized injection placement.

## 7. Glossary

- **MTP** — myofascial trigger point: hyperirritable spot in a taut band of skeletal muscle, painful on compression/stretch/overload, with referred pain.
- **Wet needling** — injection of a substance (BTX, local anesthetic, PRP, collagen) into the muscle.
- **Dry needling (DN)** — insertion of a solid filiform needle without injecting fluid. **DDN** = deep dry needling; **SDN** = superficial dry needling.
- **LTR** — local twitch response: involuntary contraction confirming needle placement in an MTP.
- **BTX** — botulinum toxin (Botox); **LA** — local anesthesia; **PRP** — platelet-rich plasma.
- **PNE** — percutaneous needle electrolysis (galvanic current via needle, EPI® equipment).
- **EMG** — electromyography (functional navigation); **EPI®** — electrotherapy equipment for PNE.
- **LPM / MPM** — lateral / medial pterygoid muscle.
- **PICOS** — Population, Intervention, Comparison, Outcome, Study design framework.
