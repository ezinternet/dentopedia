## One-line Summary
Med Sci Monit 2025;31:e946676 (Zhejiang/Hangzhou, China) — narrative review of AI (ML, ANN, deep learning) across endodontics, periodontics, implantology, orthodontics, prosthodontics, and oral/maxillofacial surgery; covers diagnosis, treatment planning, execution, follow-up; flags data annotation, fine-grained feature limits, bias, legal/privacy risks.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Medical Science Monitor 2025;31:e946676
- Published: 2025-04-08 (received 2024-09-25, accepted 2025-02-11)
- Affiliations: Zhejiang Chinese Medical University; Hangzhou Dianzi University; Zhejiang Provincial People's Hospital
- Type: Narrative review (no PRISMA, no quality grading)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Maps AI applications across the major dental specialties
- Distinguishes ML, ANN, deep learning subdomains and their dental use cases
- Synthesises both opportunities and emerging legal/ethical challenges in oral healthcare AI

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative literature review; no systematic search protocol disclosed
- Scope: imaging diagnosis, treatment planning, treatment execution, follow-up, prognosis
- Specialties covered: endodontic disease, periodontal disease, implantology, orthodontics, prosthodontics, oral/maxillofacial surgery

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- AI deployment areas: prevention, early screening, accurate diagnosis, treatment plan design assistance, treatment execution, follow-up monitoring, prognosis assessment
- Imaging-based deep learning dominates current implementations (radiograph caries detection, periapical/periodontal lesion detection, implant identification, cephalometric landmarks)
- Reports ANN and CNN architectures as most common for dental image analysis

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Inaccurate data annotation across institutions
- Limited capability for fine-grained feature expression
- Lack of universally applicable models (institutional dataset bias)
- Potential bias in learning algorithms
- Legal risks: medical malpractice attribution, data privacy breaches
- Future direction: improve generalisability and clinical validation pathways

## 6. Related Work
- Foundational AI history: McCarthy (1956 Dartmouth conference)
- LeCun, Bengio, Hinton (2015) deep learning Nature paper cited as theoretical anchor
