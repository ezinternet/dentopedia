## One-line Summary

In vivo miniature pig study (16 SLA implants in 4 pigs, frontal bone, 8-week healing; cold argon plasma applied chairside 240 s before insertion): plasma group showed higher bone-to-implant contact (90.4% vs 86.5%) and interthread bone density (72.5% vs 63.4%), with peri-implant bone density similar (60.5% vs 61.1%); authors describe the increases as slight and fluorescence labeling did not indicate significantly faster/stronger mineralization.

## 한줄요약

미니돼지 in vivo(SLA 임플란트 16개, 4마리, 전두골, 8주; 식립 전 cold argon plasma 240초): 플라즈마군이 골-임플란트 접촉률(90.4% vs 86.5%)·나사산간 골밀도(72.5% vs 63.4%)는 더 높았으나, 임플란트주위 골밀도는 유사(60.5% vs 61.1%). 저자는 증가폭을 "약간 높음"으로 기술하고, 형광표지상 광물화가 유의하게 빠르거나 강하지는 않았다.

## 1. Document Information

- Journal: Journal of Cranio-Maxillofacial Surgery 2019, 47(3):484–490
- DOI: 10.1016/j.jcms.2018.12.011 (open access)
- Study type: in vivo animal (miniature pig), single 8-week timepoint
- Source caveat: abstract/discussion-level (saved publisher page)

## 2. Key Contributions

- One of the first in vivo tests of pre-insertion CAP conditioning of SLA dental implants.
- Confirms CAP does not alter surface morphology (SEM) while modestly raising osseointegration metrics.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 16 SLA (sand-blasted, acid-etched) implants in frontal bone of 4 miniature pigs.
- Test implants conditioned chairside with cold atmospheric (argon) plasma for 240 s (60 s/quadrant) before insertion.
- Sequential fluorescence labeling; after 8 weeks, radiological/histological/histomorphometric evaluation.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Surface morphology unchanged by CAP (SEM).
- BIC: 90.4% (plasma) vs 86.5% (control).
- Interthread bone density: 72.5% (plasma) vs 63.4% (control).
- Peri-implant bone density: 60.5% (plasma) vs 61.1% (control) — essentially equal.
- Fluorescence: chronological, homogeneous mineralization; no indication of significantly faster/stronger bone adherence.
- No adverse peri-implant effects.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small (16 implants/4 pigs), single timepoint (8 wk), animal model.
- Effect sizes modest; authors call for prospective clinical trials.
- Source is abstract/discussion-level (no full statistics).
