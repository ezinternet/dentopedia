## Why Ingested

즉시식립 시 jumping gap에 채우는 그래프트 재료에 **콜라겐을 병용**하는 것이 경조직·심미 결과를 추가로 개선하는지에 대한 정량 근거. 기존 [[immediate-implant/sanz-2017-bone-graft-gap-immediate-implant-rct]](gap grafting)과 [[immediate-implant/zaki-2021-bone-substitute-materials-immediate-implant-sr-ma]](골대체재 SR+MA)를 콜라겐 변수로 확장하며, [[immediate-implant/elsheikh-2023-prf-xenograft-alloplastic-immediate-implant]]의 gap-filling 재료 비교와 짝을 이룬다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 7 RCTs (205 patients, 279 implants) finding that adding collagen to bone-grafting material in the immediate-implant jumping gap significantly improves buccal bone thickness (MD −0.43 mm, 95% CI −0.72 to −0.41) and esthetic score (MD −1.23, 95% CI −1.90 to −0.55), with no difference in implant failure or soft-tissue thickness.

## 한줄요약

7개 RCT(205명·279 임플란트) SR+MA — 즉시식립 jumping gap에 골이식재 + 콜라겐을 병용하면 협측 골두께(MD −0.43 mm)와 심미 점수(MD −1.23)가 유의하게 좋아지고, 임플란트 실패율·연조직 두께에는 차이가 없었다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA isolating the **collagen** variable (collagen-infused vs plain bone graft vs no graft) in immediate implant placement.
- Quantifies a buccal-bone-thickness and esthetic benefit specifically attributable to collagen incorporation in the gap graft.
- Confirms no penalty in implant failure rate or soft-tissue thickness from adding collagen.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Design: systematic review + meta-analysis of **RCTs only**.
- Databases: PubMed, Embase, Cochrane CENTRAL + hand search.
- Risk of bias: Cochrane ROB 2.0 (2 low, 3 moderate, 2 high).
- Comparisons (two subgroups): collagen+bone graft vs bone graft alone; collagen+bone graft vs no graft.
- Outcomes: implant failure rate, buccal bone resorption/thickness, soft-tissue thickness, esthetic scores.
- Both fixed- and random-effects models used.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Included: 7 RCTs, 205 patients, 279 implants (from 580 screened).
- Implant failure: no significant difference (95% CI 0.17 to 11.84).
- Soft-tissue thickness: no significant difference.
- Collagen + bone graft vs comparators → **buccal bone thickness MD −0.43 mm (95% CI −0.72 to −0.41)** favoring collagen.
- **Esthetic outcome MD −1.23 (95% CI −1.90 to −0.55)** favoring collagen.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small evidence base (7 RCTs, low patient numbers); 2 of 7 at high ROB.
- Heterogeneity in collagen products and grafting materials.
- Sign conventions of pooled MDs are confusingly reported in the abstract; direction interpreted as favoring collagen for buccal bone/esthetics.

## 7. Glossary

- **Jumping gap**: the void between the implant surface and the buccal bone plate in an immediate implant socket.
- **Bundle bone**: the tooth-dependent inner bundle of the buccal plate that resorbs after extraction.
