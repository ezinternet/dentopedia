## One-line Summary
Narrative review of smear layer removal efficacy of root canal irrigants: MTAD fastest overall; QMix ≈ EDTA; maleic acid ≈ EDTA; HEBP (etidronate) weakest; NaOCl alone inadequate.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Contemporary Dental Practice 20(3):395–402, 2019
- **PMID**: 31204334
- **Study type**: Narrative review

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive comparison of smear layer-removing irrigants: EDTA, MTAD, QMix, maleic acid, citric acid, HEBP, NaOCl, CHX
- **MTAD**: superior smear layer removal speed vs. EDTA; complete removal in shorter contact time
- **QMix**: equivalent to EDTA for smear layer removal; advantage of combined antibacterial (CHX) action
- **Maleic acid (7%)**: comparable to EDTA 17%; lower surface tension may aid penetration
- **HEBP (etidronate)**: weakest chelating action; not recommended as sole smear layer remover
- **NaOCl alone**: cannot remove smear layer inorganic component; EDTA/chelator always required
- **CHX**: no chelating action; smear layer remains; antibacterial only

## 3. Methodology
- Narrative review; no systematic search protocol or PRISMA
- Sources: PubMed literature up to ~2018
- Authors: Experienced endodontists; included in vitro, in vivo, and clinical data where available
- No quantitative pooling; qualitative summary by irrigant type

## 4. Key Results by Irrigant

### Chelating agents (smear layer removal)
| Irrigant | Efficacy | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| EDTA 17% | Standard reference | Requires 1–3 min contact; removes inorganic smear |
| MTAD | Superior to EDTA | Faster; also antibacterial (tetracycline + acid + detergent) |
| QMix | ≈ EDTA | Adds CHX antibacterial; single-step |
| Maleic acid 7% | ≈ EDTA | Lower surface tension; comparable removal |
| Citric acid 10% | ≈ EDTA | Less studied; adequate chelation |
| HEBP (etidronate) | Inferior | Weakest chelation; needs high concentration/contact |

### Non-chelating agents
| Irrigant | Smear layer | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| NaOCl (any %) | None | Dissolves organic component only; cannot remove inorganic |
| CHX (any %) | None | No chelating action; purely antibacterial |

### Recommended protocols (from review)
- NaOCl throughout shaping → final rinse with EDTA 17% (1 min) or QMix or MTAD → distilled water rinse (for EDTA to avoid precipitate with NaOCl if sequenced incorrectly)
- NaOCl + EDTA alternating sequence: removes both organic and inorganic smear layer components

## 5. Limitations
- Narrative review; no systematic search; selection bias possible
- In vitro results dominate; clinical relevance unverified for most comparators
- HEBP data sparse; requires larger confirmatory studies
- MTAD tetracycline staining risk not addressed clinically

## 7. Glossary
- **Smear layer**: Amorphous debris layer created by instrumentation; covers dentinal tubule orifices; inorganic (hydroxyapatite) + organic (collagen, bacteria, pulp remnants) components
- **HEBP (etidronate)**: Bisphosphonate chelator; weaker than EDTA; proposed as NaOCl-compatible (no precipitate), but inferior smear removal
- **MTAD**: Mixture of tetracycline isomer (doxycycline) + citric acid + Tween 80; removes smear layer + antibacterial
- **Maleic acid**: Organic acid chelator; 7% solution ≈ EDTA 17% smear removal; proposed as less cytotoxic alternative
