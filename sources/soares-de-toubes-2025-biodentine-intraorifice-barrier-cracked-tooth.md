## Why Ingested

[[cracked-tooth/zhang-2024-cracked-teeth-treatment-outcomes-sr-ma]] establishes that cracks with root extension and periodontal pocket involvement carry the worst survival prognosis, but does not specify a material/technique to manage the radicular crack segment once the canal is treated. This case report documents a concrete intraorifice-barrier protocol (Biodentine placed 2 mm beyond the apical-most point of the crack) for exactly that worst-case scenario — a root-extended cracked molar with an endo-periodontal lesion — adding a procedural data point to the otherwise outcome-focused literature ([[cracked-tooth/gavriil-2025-single-vs-multiple-stage-cracked-teeth-pulp]], [[cracked-tooth/raj-2025-cracked-tooth-syndrome-diagnostic-dilemma]]). Single case, so hypothesis-generating only — no survival inference.

## One-line Summary

Case report (n=1): root-extended cracked mandibular molar (#36, radix entomolaris, endo-periodontal lesion, pulp necrosis) treated with root canal therapy + Biodentine intraorifice barrier placed 2 mm apical to the crack; complete periradicular and alveolar bone healing with retained function at 48-month CBCT follow-up.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Documents the Biodentine **intraorifice barrier** technique (following the de Toubes protocol) applied to a root-extended cracked tooth complicated by an endo-periodontal lesion — the highest-risk cracked-tooth presentation.
- The barrier was placed inside the canal: obturation material in the affected mesial canals was removed to **2 mm beyond the apical-most point of the crack**, and Biodentine was condensed into that space to seal/reinforce the radicular crack segment.
- Reports complete periradicular tissue healing and **alveolar bone repair on 48-month CBCT**, with resolution of the >5 mm mesial/buccal periodontal pocket, demonstrating that an endo-perio lesion of endodontic origin associated with a root crack can resolve when the crack is sealed and the canal disinfected.
- Emphasizes adjunctive factors credited with success: early cusp-protective restoration (CAD-CAM onlay), operative magnification (clinical microscope), CBCT control, and a night occlusal plate for parafunction control.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Patient/tooth**: 37-year-old woman, tooth #36 (mandibular first molar), three roots including a supernumerary lingual root (radix entomolaris), five canals (two in mesial root). Pulp necrosis (negative thermal + electric tests), painful percussion, probing depth >5 mm mesial/buccal, periapical + furcation radiolucency, buccal cortical bone resorption; CBCT showed no root fracture (a crack, not a complete fracture).
- **History**: crack originally noted at the mesial marginal ridge by the general dentist while pulp was still vital and asymptomatic; managed then with glass ionomer pulp protection + CAD-CAM adhesive onlay for crack stabilization.
- **Endodontic protocol**: minimally invasive access through the onlay under rubber dam + gingival barrier; 2.5% NaOCl irrigation; Race Evo 4% rotary instrumentation; Root ZX2 apex locator; 17% EDTA + ultrasonic (Irrisonic) activation; UltraCal SX calcium hydroxide intracanal dressing.
- **Second visit (14 days)**: Ca(OH)2 removed ultrasonically; obturation with AH-Plus Bioceramic sealer + gutta-percha; mesial-canal filling removed to 2 mm beyond the crack's apical-most point.
- **Intraorifice barrier**: Biodentine (Septodont) mixed 30 s in amalgamator (4,200 rpm), carried with MTA carrier to the apical-most crack point, condensed dense; 15-min set; then Clearfil SE Bond primer/adhesive applied over the Biodentine and access sealed incrementally with Filtek Z350 composite; occlusal adjustment + night occlusal plate.
- **Crack measurement**: root extension measured with a periodontal probe under transillumination placed externally to the clamp wing.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **12-month follow-up**: clinical and radiographic healing of periapical tissues, no inflammatory signs, resolution of the periodontal pocket.
- **48-month follow-up**: radiograph + CBCT confirmed alveolar bone repair adjacent to the mesial root and complete periradicular healing; tooth fully functional, no patient complaints.
- Cited supporting evidence (from discussion, not this case's own data): intraorifice barriers with flowable resin achieved 53% survival at 5 yr (Malentacca) and 96.6% at 4 yr (Davis & Shariff); de Toubes' preliminary prospective study of 12 root-extended cracked teeth with Biodentine barrier reported 100% survival at 1–3 yr.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **n=1 case report** — lowest level of clinical evidence; no survival/success rate can be inferred. Authors explicitly frame it as hypothesis-generating.
- Outcome confounded by multiple favorable factors (single experienced operator, microscope, CBCT, early onlay, CAD-CAM) that cannot be disentangled in a single case.
- Generalizability untested: applies to a specific presentation (root-extended crack, no complete fracture on CBCT, endo-perio lesion of endodontic origin). Cracks that are complete fractures, or with deeper periodontal involvement, may behave differently.
- Authors call for controlled studies to assess Biodentine intraorifice-barrier efficacy and long-term effectiveness across clinical scenarios.

## 6. Related Work

- de Toubes — preliminary prospective study of the Biodentine intraorifice barrier in 12 root-extended cracked teeth (100% survival 1–3 yr).
- Malentacca; Davis & Shariff — flowable-resin intraorifice barriers for root-extended cracks (5-yr / 4-yr survival).
- Systematic review/meta-analysis (cited) reporting hydraulic repair cements offer advantages over composites as barrier materials.

## 7. Glossary

- **Cracked tooth (CT)**: incomplete fracture/crack line of uncertain direction crossing tooth structure, potentially reaching pulp and/or periodontal space; distinct from a complete root fracture.
- **Intraorifice barrier**: a plug of restorative material placed at/below the canal orifice (here, into the canal to seal the radicular crack segment) to improve coronal/marginal seal, fracture resistance, and resistance to bacterial infiltration.
- **Biodentine**: calcium-trisilicate-based hydraulic ("bioactive") restorative cement; releases calcium ions, bonds micromechanically to dentin, hardness similar to dentin; used as dentin substitute, in pulp therapy, perforation/resorption repair.
- **Endo-periodontal lesion**: combined endodontic and periodontal tissue destruction; here driven by biofilm colonization of the root crack communicating canal and periodontium.
- **Radix entomolaris**: supernumerary distolingual/lingual root of a mandibular molar.
- **CBCT**: cone-beam computed tomography, used here for crack/root assessment and to confirm bone healing.
