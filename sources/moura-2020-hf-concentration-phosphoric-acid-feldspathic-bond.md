## Why Ingested

HF 농도(5% vs 10%), 에칭 시간(60 vs 120초), 인산 처리+중화(소다수 세척) 조합이 장석질 도재의 레진 결합강도에 미치는 영향을 3요인 분산분석으로 평가한 연구. [[resin-bonding/unknown-2009-effect-of-silane-treatment-timing]]의 HF 처리 매개변수 논의를 보강하며, HF 농도만이 유일한 유의미한 변수임을 확인한다.

## One-line Summary

In vitro study (n=80, 8 groups, 3-way ANOVA): only HF concentration was significant — 10% HF yielded higher shear bond strength than 5%; etching time (60 vs 120 s) and phosphoric acid + sodium bicarbonate neutralization did not influence bond strength.

## 한줄요약

인 비트로 연구 (n=80, 8그룹, 3요인 분산분석): HF 농도만 유의미한 변수 — 10% HF가 5% HF보다 높은 전단결합강도; 에칭 시간(60 vs 120초)과 인산 처리+탄산수소나트륨 중화는 결합강도에 영향 없음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Brazilian Oral Research, 2020; 34:e018
- **DOI**: 10.1590/1807-3107bor-2020.vol34.0018
- **Study type**: In vitro experimental (3-way factorial design)
- **Institution**: Universidade Federal do Rio Grande do Norte (UFRN); UFPR; UNESP São José dos Campos, Brazil

## 2. Key Contributions

- Resolved the three-way interaction: HF concentration is the dominant variable; time and phosphoric acid post-treatment are not significant
- Showed 10% HF creates more substantial microporosity and higher bond strength than 5% HF at both 60 and 120 s
- Confirmed that phosphoric acid application + sodium bicarbonate neutralization (a technique used to manage HF residues) does not significantly reduce or improve bond strength — neutral effect on bond
- Established predominance of cohesive-in-ceramic (40%) and mixed (42.5%) failure modes, indicating the ceramic-cement interface exceeded ceramic fatigue resistance

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Material**: Vita Mark II feldspathic ceramic (SiO₂ ~60%; Al₂O₃ ~21%), cut into 80 blocks (10×12×2 mm)
- **Experimental groups** (n=10, 3 factors × 2 levels each = 8 groups):
  - HF concentration: 5% vs 10% (Condac, FGM)
  - Etching time: 60 s vs 120 s
  - Phosphoric acid post-treatment: with (37% PA/Condac 60 s → Na bicarbonate 1 min → ultrasonic 5 min) vs without (Na bicarbonate + ultrasonic only)
- **Silane**: Prosil (γ-MPS in ethanol + water, FGM)
- **Adhesive**: Ambar (UDMA, HEMA, methacrylic acid monomers, ethanol, FGM)
- **Cement**: AllCem Dual (dual-cure resin cement, FGM); cylinders Ø 2 mm × 2 mm
- **Storage**: 37°C distilled water for 90 days (longer aging than typical 24h studies)
- **Test**: Shear bond strength (50 kgF load cell, 1 mm/min); SEM + stereomicroscope failure analysis
- **Statistics**: 3-way ANOVA + Tukey's test (α=0.05)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **HF concentration** (p=0.02): Only significant factor
  - 10% HF > 5% HF across all conditions
- **Etching time** (60 vs 120 s): Not significant (p>0.05)
  - Longer etching does not improve bond strength; may even slightly damage ceramic surface
- **Phosphoric acid treatment** (with vs without): Not significant (p>0.05)
  - PA + NaHCO₃ neutralization does not impair or improve bond strength
- **Failure modes**: Cohesive in ceramic 40%, mixed 42.5%, adhesive 17.5%
  - High cohesive-in-ceramic rate suggests bond strength exceeded ceramic structural integrity at 90-day mark
- 90-day water storage provides durability insight not available in 24h studies

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- No thermocycling (pure water storage at 37°C for 90 days)
- Vita Mark II only — results may differ for lithium disilicate or leucite ceramics
- No fiber-optic transillumination or confocal analysis of HF microporosity morphology
- Phosphoric acid PA applied for only 60 s — different durations may matter

## 6. Related Work

- Cites Venturine et al.: HF concentrations 3/5/10% all produce stable adhesion after aging
- Cites Naves et al.: bond strength decreases with etching times beyond 60 s at 10% HF
- Cites prior work showing silica/fluoride salt precipitation in micro-retentions as concern (here found non-significant after Na bicarb neutralization)
- 90-day storage protocol longer than most comparable studies (typically 24h ± thermocycling)

## 7. Glossary

- **HF**: Hydrofluoric acid (5% or 10% concentration — Brazilian brand: Condac, FGM)
- **PA**: Phosphoric acid (37%, post-HF etching application to neutralize fluoride salts)
- **NaHCO₃**: Sodium bicarbonate — used for HF neutralization and residue removal
- **3-way ANOVA**: Three-factor analysis of variance used to evaluate main effects and interactions
- **Vita Mark II**: Feldspar-leucite ceramic CAD/CAM block (VITA Zahnfabrik)
- **AllCem Dual**: Dual-cure resin cement (FGM)
- **SEM**: Scanning electron microscopy for surface/failure characterization
