## One-line Summary

PRISMA systematic review (11 studies, n=15–85, mean age 60–68; none at low risk of bias) on supportive peri-implant care frequency for implant-supported full-arch prostheses, recommending professional oral hygiene every 6 months and prosthesis removal at least once per year, tailored to patient risk.

## 한줄요약

PRISMA 체계적 문헌고찰(11편, n=15–85, 평균연령 60–68세; 저비뚤림 연구 0편) — full-arch 임플란트 보철의 SPiT 빈도에 대해 6개월마다 전문 구강위생, 최소 연 1회 보철 제거를 권고하되 환자 위험도에 맞춰 조정.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR focused specifically on the *frequency* of supportive peri-implant care for **full-arch** (full-mouth fixed) implant prostheses — the hardest-to-clean reconstruction.
- Translates sparse evidence into a pragmatic default: professional OH every 6 months, prosthesis removal ≥ 1×/year.
- Flags that none of the 11 included studies were at low risk of bias — the recommendation is expert-pragmatic, not high-certainty.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PRISMA + PICO framework; PubMed search.
- Included RCTs, controlled clinical trials, and cohort studies with ≥ 1-year follow-up reporting supportive peri-implant care for full-arch prostheses.
- Blinded selection (100% agreement); quality assessed per Cochrane Handbook.
- 915 results screened → 11 included (8 reported care frequency, 3 detailed procedures).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Patients per study: 15–85; mean age 60.4–68.4.
- **None** of the 11 studies judged low risk of bias.
- No firm indication on optimal frequency could be derived; pragmatic recommendation: **professional oral hygiene every 6 months + remove the prosthesis at least once per year**, individualized to patient risk profile.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-database (PubMed only) search; small, heterogeneous body of evidence.
- All included studies at moderate/high risk of bias → low certainty.
- No standardized regimen for prosthesis removal/cleaning could be established.

## 7. Glossary

- **Full-arch prosthesis** — fixed full-mouth implant-supported reconstruction (e.g., All-on-4/6), removed only by the clinician.
- **Supportive peri-implant care** — professional maintenance: prosthesis removal, debridement, oral-hygiene reinforcement.
