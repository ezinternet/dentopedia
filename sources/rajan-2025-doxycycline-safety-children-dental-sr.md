## Why Ingested

소아(8세 미만)에서 독시사이클린(doxycycline) 단기 사용 시 치아 착색 위험이 실제로 얼마나 되는지 체계적으로 정리한 SR+MA가 없었으며, AAP의 RMSF 지침 개정(금기→권고) 이후 근거 기반이 필요했다. 기존 항생제 페이지 [[drug/bazsefidpay-2023-antibiotic-restrictive-use-adherence-recommendation]]가 항생제 사용 제한 근거를 다루는 한편, 본 리뷰는 특정 연령 금기의 재평가 근거를 제공한다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (5 studies, n=162 children <8 years): short-course doxycycline (median 8.5 days) caused dental staining in only 1/162 children (0.62%), with pooled adverse event proportion of 0.21 (95% CI: 0.13–0.28), supporting revised AAP/CDC guidance permitting doxycycline in young children for life-threatening indications.

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (5개 연구, n=162명, 8세 미만): 단기 독시사이클린(doxycycline, Doxy) 투여(중앙값 8.5일) 후 치아 착색(tooth staining)은 162명 중 1명(0.62%)에서만 발생했으며, 통합 이상반응 비율 0.21 (95% CI: 0.13–0.28)로, AAP/CDC의 생명위협적 적응증에서의 소아 사용 지침 개정을 지지한다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD42023494713)
- **Published**: 2025, Frontiers in Pharmacology
- **DOI**: [10.3389/fphar.2025.1646638](https://doi.org/10.3389/fphar.2025.1646638)
- **Search period**: Inception to November 2023 (PubMed, Cochrane Library, Google Scholar)
- **PRISMA compliant**: Yes
- **Included studies**: 5 studies (published 1969–2017)
- **Total pediatric patients**: 162 children aged 0–8 years

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First SR+MA specifically quantifying dental adverse event risk from short-course doxycycline in children under 8 years
2. Demonstrates that tooth staining occurred in only 1/162 children (0.62%), and that single case was a critically ill premature neonate
3. Among children with permanent teeth (n=137), zero cases of tooth discoloration
4. Supports revised AAP (Red Book 2021) and CDC guidance recommending doxycycline as first-line for RMSF in any age child, including those under 8
5. Highlights the mechanistic rationale: doxycycline has lower calcium-binding affinity (19%) compared to tetracycline (39.5%), reducing staining risk
6. Identifies the evidence gap motivating prescriber hesitancy — only 5 eligible studies in 50+ years of literature

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Study design**: SR+MA of observational and interventional studies
- **Population**: Children <8 years receiving doxycycline for any indication
- **Outcome**: Dental adverse events — tooth discoloration/staining, enamel hypoplasia
- **Minimum follow-up required**: ≥1 year post-treatment
- **Eligible designs**: RCTs, cohort, case-control, clinical trials
- **Meta-analysis method**: Freeman–Tukey double arcsine transformation for proportions; fixed-effect model (I² = 0%)
- **Bias assessment**: RoB2 (RCTs), JBI Critical Appraisal (observational studies)
- **Screening**: Two independent reviewers, consensus with third senior reviewer for disagreements
- **Software**: Jamovi version 2.4.14

**Included study characteristics**:

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Tooth staining rate**: 1/162 (0.62%) — single premature infant <2 months old
- **Enamel hypoplasia**: 2/162 (1.23%) — both from the RMSF cohort (Todd et al.)
- **Staining in permanent teeth**: 0/137 (0%)
- **Pooled adverse event proportion**: 0.21 (95% CI: 0.13–0.28)
- **Heterogeneity**: I² = 0%, Q = 0.954, p = 0.917 → fixed-effect model appropriate
- **Publication bias**: Fail-safe N = 49; Kendall's Tau = 0.600 (p = 0.233); Egger Z = 0.749 (p = 0.454) → no significant bias
- **RMSF-specific**: 0/68 children developed staining in two RMSF studies (both low/moderate bias)
- **Median treatment duration**: 8.5 days (IQR: 6–12.5)
- **Median dose**: 2.3 mg/kg/day (IQR: 1.525–5.438 mg/kg/day)
- **Median age at exposure**: 4.25 years (IQR: 2.065–5.563)

**Pharmacological rationale**:
- Doxycycline calcium-binding affinity: 19% (vs. tetracycline: 39.5%) — lower affinity = less dental incorporation
- Pharmacokinetics allow less frequent dosing and lower total daily doses compared to tetracycline

**Risk of bias summary**: 1 low, 3 moderate, 1 critical (Grossi et al. — the only study reporting staining)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 5 eligible studies across 50+ years of doxycycline use — extremely thin evidence base
- Small combined sample size (n = 162) limits statistical power and generalizability
- Majority retrospective; none designed prospectively to capture dental outcomes as primary endpoint
- The one case of staining came from the only study with critical bias (Grossi et al., premature infants)
- No data on gastrointestinal, photosensitivity, or other systemic adverse events — dental effects only
- Pooled proportion of 0.21 reflects any adverse event (staining + hypoplasia); not dental staining alone
- Follow-up periods varied (1 year to 22+ years), affecting detection of late effects
- **Calls for**: Large-scale prospective cohort studies; RCTs comparing doxycycline vs alternatives; follow-up ≥6–12 months post-treatment; diverse populations

## 6. Related Work

- AAP Red Book (2021): Revised guidance — doxycycline recommended as drug of choice for RMSF in children of any age
- CDC guidelines: Doxycycline 2.2 mg/kg BID (max 100 mg/dose) for RMSF in children <45 kg
- Todd et al. (RMSF cohort, USA): Key low-bias study showing 0/58 staining cases
- Vohra et al. (RMSF case-control, UK): 0/10 staining cases
- Peltola et al. (CNS infection cohort, Finland): 0/38 staining cases
- Grossi et al. (premature infants, Italy): 1/25 staining — only critical-bias study with positive finding
- Historical tetracycline staining literature (from 1958): Basis for original contraindication in <8 years

## 7. Glossary

- **독시사이클린 (Doxycycline, Doxy)**: 테트라사이클린계 광범위 항생제; RMSF, 스크럽 티푸스, 라임병 등 치료에 사용
- **치아 착색 (Tooth Staining)**: 테트라사이클린 계열이 칼슘과 결합해 치아 상아질에 침착, 변색을 일으키는 현상
- **법랑질 저형성 (Enamel Hypoplasia)**: 치아 발육 시 법랑질이 불완전하게 형성되는 구조적 결함
- **록키산 홍반열 (Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever, RMSF)**: Rickettsia 감염 — 소아에서 치명적이며 독시사이클린이 유일한 효과적 치료제
- **Freeman-Tukey 이중 역사인 변환 (Freeman–Tukey Double Arcsine Transformation)**: 소규모 연구에서 비율(proportion) 메타분석 시 분산을 안정화하는 통계 기법
- **PROSPERO**: 체계적 문헌고찰 국제 전향 등록 시스템 (International Prospective Register of Systematic Reviews)
- **고정효과 모델 (Fixed-effect Model)**: 연구 간 이질성(heterogeneity)이 낮을 때(I²=0%) 적용되는 메타분석 통합 모델
