## One-line Summary
Systematic review (Cureus 2024, 11 studies, 2010–2024): guided implant drilling generates significantly higher bone temperatures than non-guided osteotomy due to metal sleeves blocking irrigation, but in most reports peak temperatures remain below the 47°C/1 min necrosis threshold when irrigation, drill geometry, and speed are appropriately managed.

- Journal: Cureus 16(9):e70216, September 2024

## 2. Key Contributions
- Synthesizes head-to-head evidence on guided vs. non-guided osteotomy heat generation.
- Identifies the sleeve-irrigation interference mechanism as the principal driver of higher temperatures in guided surgery.
- Catalogs modifiable factors that mitigate guided-drilling heat: irrigation temperature, drill length/diameter, drilling speed.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Databases: PubMed, EBSCOhost, Google Scholar (2010–2024).
- MeSH terms + Boolean operators.
- 548 hits → 477 excluded by title/abstract → 26 duplicates → 15 unavailable full texts → 19 design exclusions → 11 included.
- Tabulated by: bone model, drill type/RPM, diameter/depth, irrigation type/temperature, conclusion.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Across studies: guided drilling temperature > non-guided drilling, by ~1°C to clinically meaningful margins depending on protocol.
- Most studies: peak temperatures stayed below 47°C necrosis threshold.
- Exception: Barrak 2018 — both 1500 and 2000 RPM under guided setting exceeded necrotic threshold.
- Cooled saline (10°C) consistently reduces peak temperature regardless of guided/non-guided technique.
- Irrigation volume increase did not always translate to lower temperature.
- Drill diameter, length, speed, and irrigation temperature all modulate the guided heat penalty.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- No meta-analysis due to study heterogeneity.
- All in vitro / ex vivo (bovine ribs, polyurethane foam, pig ribs, synthetic blocks) — no in vivo or RCT in humans.
- Guide sleeve geometry, material, and irrigation type heterogeneous across studies.
- Future: standardized comparison protocols; in vivo validation; engineered sleeves that permit better coolant flow.

## 6. Related Work
- Eriksson & Albrektsson — 47°C/1 min necrosis threshold.
- Bulloch 2012, Jeong 2013, Migliorati 2013 — early guided vs non-guided drilling temperature studies.
- Barrak 2018 — guided drilling exceeding necrotic threshold.
- Bone drilling thermal literature.

## 7. Glossary
- **Guided surgery**: implant placement via a surgical guide with metal/plastic sleeves directing the drill.
- **Sleeve interference**: irrigation fluid blocked from reaching osteotomy by the guide sleeve.
- **Necrotic threshold**: 47°C for 1 minute (classical Eriksson criterion).
- **PRISMA**: Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses.
