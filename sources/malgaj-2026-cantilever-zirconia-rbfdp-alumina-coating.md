## Why Ingested

The zirconia-bonding pages we hold ([[dental-materials/zirconia/lopes-2023-universal-adhesives-zirconia-long-term]], [[dental-materials/zirconia/comba-2021-chemical-bonding-cubic-zirconia]]) are all in-vitro bond-strength studies, and they establish that durable zirconia adhesion conventionally requires airborne-particle abrasion (APA) plus MDP. APA is mechanically aggressive and can introduce surface flaws into damage-prone translucent zirconia. This 8-year RCT (Malgaj 2026) provides rare *clinical* long-term evidence that a non-invasive nanostructured alumina coating (NAC) matches APA retention in cantilever RBFDPs, extending the bonding literature from bench to durable in-vivo outcome. It also complements survival data in [[dental-materials/zirconia/leitao-2022-monolithic-zirconia-single-crown-survival-sr]] by addressing a different prosthesis class (minimally invasive RBFDP).

## One-line Summary

8-year RCT (57 patients, 64 cantilever zirconia RBFDPs, mean 61.4 mo) — non-invasive nanostructured alumina coating (NAC) gave retention (88.4%) statistically equivalent to airborne-particle abrasion (89.1%, p=0.99), with 100% prosthesis survival.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides mid- to long-term (mean 61.4 mo, up to 97.4 mo / ~8 years) clinical RCT evidence for **nanostructured alumina coating (NAC)** as a non-invasive zirconia surface pretreatment alternative to the established airborne-particle abrasion (APA).
- Demonstrates **clinical equivalence of NAC and APA** for cantilever zirconia RBFDP retention (88.4% vs 89.1%, p=0.99) and survival (100% both groups).
- SEM analysis of debonded restorations identifies the NAC–zirconia interface as the weak link in NAC failures (adhesive delamination), distinguishing it from mixed cohesive–adhesive APA failures.
- Positions NAC as clinically relevant for damage-prone translucent high-yttria zirconia (4Y/5Y), where APA-induced surface flaws carry unquantified fracture risk.
- Largest RCT to date evaluating an alternative zirconia surface modification; provides complementary mid- to long-term evidence alongside Kern's 10–15 year APA-only cohort data.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Prospective, randomized, controlled, double-blind clinical trial; CONSORT-reported.
- **Setting**: Academic clinical center, Dept. of Prosthodontics, University of Ljubljana.
- **Sample**: 57 patients (28 women, 29 men; age range 13–71 years, mean 27.2 ± 16.0 years) requiring replacement of a missing maxillary or mandibular central or lateral incisor. 64 cantilever zirconia RBFDPs placed (6 patients received two restorations in a split-mouth sub-design). Dropout rate: 0%.
- **Inclusion criteria**: No bruxism, healthy abutment tooth (no active periodontal/periapical disease), enamel bonding surface ≥30 mm², adequate interocclusal space (≥0.7 mm retainer wing, proximal connector ≥2 mm × 3 mm), incisor-canine guidance without pontic guidance, adequate edentulous space.
- **Randomization**: APA group (n=32) — airborne-particle abrasion with 50-μm alumina (0.1 MPa, 10 mm distance), followed by ultrasonic cleaning in 96% ethanol for 3 min; NAC group (n=32) — immersed in NAC precursor solution (VALLBOND, Vall-cer, Ljubljana) then calcination-fired at 900°C for 30 min before veneering; coating verified by SEM. After try-in, NAC surfaces conditioned with 37% phosphoric acid.
- **Material**: 3Y-TZP frameworks (Ceramill Zolid, Amann Girrbach) milled by CAD/CAM; veneered with feldspathic porcelain (VITAVM9, VITA Zahnfabrik). Cemented under rubber dam with MDP-containing resin cement (Panavia 21 TC, Kuraray).
- **Pontic position sub-classification**: Group 1 — maxillary central incisors (APA n=13, NAC n=8); Group 2 — maxillary lateral incisors (APA n=13, NAC n=16); Group 3 — mandibular incisors (APA n=6, NAC n=8).
- **Outcomes**: Primary — RBFDP retention (restoration remaining functional without debonding). Secondary — prosthesis survival (restoration remaining functional throughout follow-up). Kaplan-Meier curves, log-rank tests (α=0.05). Failure mode assessed by SEM.
- **Follow-up**: 2 weeks, 6 months, then annually.
- **Statistics**: Mann-Whitney U (continuous outcomes), Fisher's exact test (categorical), Kaplan-Meier + log-rank. IBM SPSS v20.0. Bonding surface area computed with MeshLab 3D mesh processing.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Mean observation period**: 61.4 ± 22.1 months (range 8.3–97.4 months).
- **Prosthesis survival**: 100% in both APA and NAC groups.
- **Overall cumulative retention** (all 64 RBFDPs): 88.8%.
- **Retention by group**: NAC 88.4% vs APA 89.1%; log-rank p=0.99 (no significant difference).
- **Retention by pontic position**: Group 3 (mandibular incisors) 100%, Group 2 (maxillary lateral incisors) 88.8%, Group 1 (maxillary central incisors) 79%; log-rank p=0.18 (NS).
- **Debondings**: 6 total (3 APA, 3 NAC). All rebonded; all remained functional thereafter.
  - APA: 1 post-trauma (6.3 mo), 2 spontaneous (15.6 and 36.1 mo).
  - NAC: 2 post-trauma (2.1 and 22.9 mo), 1 spontaneous (30.6 mo).
- **Biological complications**: 2 gingivitis/tooth mobility cases (12 mo post-insertion); 1 occlusal trauma (6 mo, resolved after adjustment).
- **Technical complications**: 1 porcelain veneer chip at 4-year follow-up (polished, restoration retained).
- **SEM failure analysis**:
  - APA failures: mixed cohesive/adhesive; small cement remnants with delamination through thin cement layer.
  - NAC failures: predominantly adhesive at the NAC–zirconia interface; cement remnants plus areas of exposed NAC residues and zirconia grains.
- **No framework fractures** observed (3Y-TZP; contrast with concerns about translucent zirconia).
- **Bonding surface area**: comparable between groups (APA 50.8 ± 2.9 mm² vs NAC 54.1 ± 4.2 mm²; p=0.99).
- **Literature benchmark**: Kern's 15-year large cohort showed success/retention rates of 92.4% at 10 years declining to 82.6% at 15 years; present study at 8-year maximum provides complementary mid-to-long-term data consistent with these trajectories.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-center academic study; n=32 per group limits power to detect smaller differences.
- All debondings occurred within the first 3 years; longer observation (>10 years) is needed to confirm durability of both treatments.
- 3Y-TZP used (not high-translucent 4Y/5Y); APA's potential weakening effect on newer translucent zirconia remains clinically untested in an RCT.
- NAC failure mode (adhesive delamination at NAC–zirconia interface) suggests optimization of the calcination/deposition protocol may improve NAC adhesion to zirconia.
- Silicone-based try-in materials should be avoided with NAC surfaces (phosphoric acid ineffective against silicone contamination, per referenced evidence).
- Further trials with larger cohorts and longer observation are needed for definitive long-term conclusions.

## 7. Glossary

- **RBFDP**: Resin-bonded fixed dental prosthesis — minimally invasive adhesive bridge retained by thin retainer wings; requires no or minimal tooth reduction.
- **Cantilever RBFDP**: Single-retainer design; the pontic (replacement tooth) is cantilevered from one bonded wing, avoiding preparation of the adjacent tooth.
- **APA (airborne-particle abrasion)**: Sandblasting the zirconia bonding surface with alumina particles (here 50 μm, 0.1 MPa) to create micromechanical retention; the established clinical standard but potentially weakens translucent zirconia.
- **NAC (nanostructured alumina coating)**: Additive surface modification — zirconia immersed in alumina precursor sol and calcination-fired at 900°C, producing a nanolamellar (boehmite) surface that increases bonding area up to 7× while preserving strength and translucency.
- **MDP (10-methacryloyloxydecyl dihydrogen phosphate)**: Phosphate monomer in adhesive cements/primers that provides chemical bonding to zirconia oxide surface.
- **3Y-TZP**: 3 mol% yttria-stabilized tetragonal zirconia polycrystal — high-strength standard-opacity zirconia.
- **Retention**: Primary outcome — restoration remaining bonded and functional without debonding.
- **Survival**: Secondary outcome — restoration remaining in place and functional throughout follow-up (includes rebonded debonded restorations).
- **Adhesive failure**: Debonding at a substrate–cement interface (vs cohesive failure within cement or material).
- **Pontic position**: Location of the missing tooth being replaced (maxillary central incisor, lateral incisor, or mandibular incisor).
