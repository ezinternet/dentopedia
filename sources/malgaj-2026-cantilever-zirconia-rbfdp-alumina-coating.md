## Why Ingested

The zirconia-bonding pages we hold ([[dental-materials/zirconia/lopes-2023-universal-adhesives-zirconia-long-term]], [[dental-materials/zirconia/comba-2021-chemical-bonding-cubic-zirconia]]) are all in-vitro bond-strength studies, and they establish that durable zirconia adhesion conventionally requires airborne-particle abrasion (APA) plus MDP. APA is mechanically aggressive and can introduce surface flaws into damage-prone translucent zirconia. This 8-year RCT (Malgaj 2026) provides rare *clinical* long-term evidence that a non-invasive nanostructured alumina coating (NAC) matches APA retention in cantilever RBFDPs, extending the bonding literature from bench to durable in-vivo outcome. It also complements survival data in [[dental-materials/zirconia/leitao-2022-monolithic-zirconia-single-crown-survival-sr]] by addressing a different prosthesis class (minimally invasive RBFDP).

## One-line Summary

8-year RCT (57 patients, 64 cantilever zirconia RBFDPs, mean 61.4 mo) — non-invasive nanostructured alumina coating (NAC) gave retention (88.4%) statistically equivalent to airborne-particle abrasion (89.1%, p=0.99), with 100% prosthesis survival.

## 한줄요약

8년 RCT (환자 57명, 캔틸레버 지르코니아 RBFDP 64개, 평균 61.4개월) — 비침습적 나노구조 알루미나 코팅(NAC) 유지율 88.4%가 분사연마(APA) 89.1%와 통계적으로 동등(p=0.99), 보철물 생존율 100%.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides mid- to long-term (mean 61.4 mo, up to 97.4 mo) clinical RCT evidence for a **non-invasive zirconia surface pretreatment** (NAC) as an alternative to the established but aggressive airborne-particle abrasion (APA).
- Demonstrates clinical equivalence of NAC and APA for cantilever zirconia RBFDP retention.
- Positions NAC as especially suited to damage-prone translucent zirconia, where APA-induced flaws are a concern.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Prospective, randomized, controlled clinical trial.
- **Sample**: 57 patients needing replacement of a maxillary or mandibular incisor; 64 cantilever zirconia RBFDPs placed.
- **Groups**: APA (n=32) — air-abraded with 50-μm alumina; NAC (n=32) — coated with nanostructured alumina coating. Restorations further classified by pontic position.
- **Outcomes**: Retention and survival via Kaplan-Meier + log-rank; patient factors via Mann-Whitney U and Fisher's exact (α=0.05). Debonded surfaces examined by SEM.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Mean observation: 61.4 ± 22.1 months (range 8.3–97.4 mo).
- Six debondings total.
- **Overall prosthesis survival: 100%.**
- **Retention: NAC 88.4% vs APA 89.1% (p=0.99)** — no significant difference.
- No significant differences among pontic groups (p=0.18).
- Failures predominantly **adhesive**, with partial coating delamination at the NAC–zirconia interface (SEM).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only ingest; full methodological detail (cement type, framework design, drop-outs) not retrieved.
- Single-center; relatively small per-group n (32 each).
- NAC failures localized to the NAC–zirconia interface suggest coating adhesion is the weak link to optimize.
- Cantilever single-retainer RBFDP design only; generalizability to other prosthesis types untested here.

## 7. Glossary

- **RBFDP**: Resin-bonded fixed dental prosthesis (a minimally invasive "Maryland-style" bridge retained by adhesive wings).
- **Cantilever RBFDP**: Single-retainer design where the pontic extends from one bonded wing.
- **APA (airborne-particle abrasion)**: Sandblasting the bonding surface with alumina particles (here 50 μm) to roughen it for micromechanical retention.
- **NAC (nanostructured alumina coating)**: A non-invasive nanoscale alumina layer applied to zirconia to enhance adhesion without abrasive surface damage.
- **Adhesive failure**: Debonding at the cement–substrate interface (vs cohesive failure within cement/material).
