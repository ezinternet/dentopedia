사용자가 "무삭제 라미네이트(no-prep veneer)·접착·프렙" 주제를 PubMed 전문 감시로 인제스트 요청. 기존 [[wiki/veneers/ali-2023-conventional-minimally-invasive-veneers-sr]](conventional vs minimally invasive SR)와 [[wiki/veneers/gonzalez-martin-2024-ultrathin-feldspathic-veneer-sem-etching]](ultrathin enamel-etch bench)는 있지만, no-prep ↔ conventional 적응증·생존율을 **결정 트리 관점에서 직접 대비한 narrative review**가 없었다. 본 리뷰는 "법랑질에 본딩될 때 no-prep 성공률 ≤97.4%, 두 술식 생존율 91–100% (no-prep 97% vs conventional 92%)"라는 비교 수치와 case-selection 경계(intrinsic discoloration·misalignment·형태 불일치 → conventional)를 제공해 veneers overview 합성의 분기 근거로 활용.

## One-line Summary

Narrative review (Bioinformation 2026) comparing no-preparation vs conventional porcelain veneers: both show high survival (91–100%; no-prep mean ~97% vs conventional ~92%), no-prep success up to 97.4% when enamel-bonded, but no-prep is contraindicated for intrinsic discoloration, misalignment, or major morphological/contour correction — favoring a patient-specific, enamel-centered, complementary (not competing) treatment philosophy.

## 한줄요약

무삭제(no-prep) 비니어와 통상(conventional) 비니어를 비교한 narrative review (Bioinformation 2026). 두 술식 모두 생존율 91–100%로 높고(무삭제 평균 ~97% vs 통상 ~92%), 법랑질 접착 시 무삭제 성공률 최대 97.4%지만, 내인성 변색·치아 부정정렬·형태 큰 수정이 필요한 경우엔 무삭제가 부적응 — 환자별·법랑질 중심으로 두 술식을 경쟁이 아닌 상호보완으로 선택할 것.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Head-to-head indication map**: MPV/no-prep favored for well-aligned teeth with minimal esthetic demand; CV for difficult discolorations, morphological alterations, alignment deviations needing controlled reduction for masking/contour accuracy.
2. **Survival comparison**: both techniques 91–100%; no-prep slightly higher mean (97% vs 92%) with fewer catastrophic failures — caveated by methodological heterogeneity across studies.
3. **Enamel as the deciding substrate**: preparation design is the single most important determinant of MPV outcome; sound enamel bonding drives durable adhesion; additive approach preserves contour and reduces need for anesthesia/provisionals.
4. **Reframing**: MPV and CV are complementary, not opposing — enamel-centered, patient-specific selection.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Narrative review of peer-reviewed studies on no-prep vs conventional porcelain laminate veneers. No formal search/PRISMA protocol or pooled meta-analysis; representative studies summarized in a comparison table (clinical longevity, esthetic outcomes, tooth preservation). Evidence weight is **narrative-review** level — interpretive synthesis, not quantitative.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- No-prep success up to **97.4%** when bonded to enamel in well-selected cases.
- Survival **91–100%** both techniques; no-prep mean ~**97%** vs conventional ~**92%**.
- Conventional reduction range cited: **0.3–1.0 mm** for improved masking/contour control.
- No-prep advantages: enamel preservation, reduced postoperative sensitivity, low biological cost, less need for anesthesia/temporaries.
- No-prep contraindications: intrinsic discoloration, misalignment, significant morphological discrepancy (→ over-contouring risk).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative (no pooled estimate); survival figures drawn from heterogeneous studies with differing survival/failure definitions (some count trauma as failure), limiting cross-study comparison.
- Long-term (>10 yr) performance of no-prep specifically remains less well characterized than conventional.

## 7. Glossary

- **MPV** — minimal preparation veneer
- **CV** — conventional veneer
- **No-prep veneer** — additive ultra-thin veneer bonded to unreduced enamel ("contact lens")
- **Emergence profile** — cervical contour transition restoration→tooth; over-contouring is the key no-prep pitfall
