## Why Ingested

User's clinical interest in reading occlusal vertical dimension (OVD) errors off the face — mentalis strain, deepened mentolabial fold, lip support — in complete-denture and reconstruction cases. This 79-study Cureus review catalogs every OVD-determination method (pre/post-extraction) plus the functional/esthetic consequences of incorrect VD, complementing [[occlusion/bhambhani-2020-choosing-denture-occlusion-systematic-review]] (which addresses denture *occlusal scheme* rather than vertical relation) and grounding the OVD-elevation effects shown experimentally in [[tmj/wang-2024-tmj-ovd-elevation-occlusal-loss-rats]].

## One-line Summary

Narrative/comprehensive review (79 studies) cataloging OVD-determination methods from classical (facial/anthropometric measurement, phonetics, swallowing, physiologic rest position + freeway space, cephalometry) to digital (3D facial scanning, CBCT, CAD/CAM, AI), concluding no single method is universally superior and advocating a combined traditional + digital multifactorial approach.

## 한줄요약

종합 리뷰(79편) — 교합수직고경(OVD) 결정법을 고전적 방법(안면계측·인체계측, 음성학, 연하, 생리적 안정위 + freeway space, 두부계측)부터 디지털 방법(3D 안면스캔, CBCT, CAD/CAM, AI)까지 총망라하고, 단일 우월 방법은 없으므로 전통+디지털 다인자 접근을 권고.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type:** Open-access comprehensive literature review (Part II of a two-part series; Part I = conceptual evolution, Cureus 2024)
- **Journal:** Cureus 2025;17(10):e93987
- **DOI:** 10.7759/cureus.93987
- **Search:** PubMed/MEDLINE, Scopus, Web of Science, Cochrane Library; 1,259 → 903 after de-dup → 79 high-quality studies included.
- **Scope:** Methods of determining vertical dimension of occlusion (VDO/OVD) in prosthodontics, especially edentulous/complete-denture patients.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Two-stream taxonomy of OVD determination: **(a) pre-extraction record methods** and **(b) post-extraction assessment methods** (anthropometric/biometric, physiological/functional, mechanical, measurement devices, radiographic/imaging, esthetic).
- Historical arc from Leonardo da Vinci facial ratios (15th c.) and Hunter/Ivy facial-quarters → Niswonger physiologic rest position (1930s) → Willis Gauge (1935) → phonetics (Silverman closest speaking space) → cephalometry → EMG/neuromuscular → modern digital (3D scan, CBCT, CAD/CAM, AI).
- Introduces a recent radiographic formula (Fayad et al.) estimating OVD from a panoramic radiograph using the inter-mental-foramina distance as reference.
- States the **consequences of incorrect VD** (see Glossary / Results for excessive vs reduced signs).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Narrative comprehensive review (not a meta-analysis). Methods grouped functionally:

1. **Pre-extraction records** — transfer the natural-dentition OVD via intra-oral measurement, profile tracing, cephalometry, or the patient's existing denture.
2. **Physiological/functional** — physiologic rest position minus **freeway space** (~2–4 mm, Niswonger); swallowing technique (wax rim cut back until comfortable swallow); phonetic methods (sibilant "s"/"ch", "Mississippi"; closest speaking space ~1–2 mm; Igić vowel "O" ≈ 5.5 mm, "E" ≈ 7.5 mm interocclusal space); neuromuscular/tactile perception (Lytle central bearing device); mastication-based.
3. **Mechanical** — paralleling the ridges (Wright; criticized for variable resorption); existing complete denture (Massad).
4. **Measurement devices** — Willis gauge (subnasion→gnathion), calibrated calipers/dividers, Knebelman craniometer, Boos bimeter ("power point"; tends to overestimate VD), Precise Jaw Relation Recorder; electronic: EMG + jaw trackers, TENS, K7 kinesiography.
5. **Radiographic/imaging** — cephalometry, digital cephalometry, panoramic inter-mental-foramina formula, 3D facial scanning.
6. **Esthetic** — facial thirds proportion, lip support, lip–teeth relationship, digital smile design.
7. **Digital/AI** — virtual articulators, intraoral-scanner virtual occlusal records, AI for RPD design/edentulous-arch classification/outcome prediction.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **No single method is universally superior.** Traditional techniques retain clinical value but lack reproducibility; digital methods offer greater precision and documentation at the cost of equipment/training.
- **Freeway space ≈ 2–4 mm** is the classic RVD − OVD interocclusal rest distance (Niswonger), still used today.
- **Closest speaking space ≈ 1–2 mm** anterior clearance during sibilants (Silverman/Pound).
- **Signs of EXCESSIVE VD:** muscle fatigue, speech difficulties, bone resorption, prosthetic instability; clicking of teeth during speech; trauma to supporting tissues; stretched/strained facial appearance with the lower facial third too long.
- **Signs of REDUCED/decreased VD:** compromised esthetics, impaired masticatory function, temporomandibular disorders, patient dissatisfaction; an aged/overclosed appearance — shortened lower facial third, **deepened mentolabial (labiomental) fold**, drooping commissures, and increased **mentalis strain/chin prominence**, with reduced lip support.
- Willis-method assumption of upper = lower facial-third equivalence does **not** hold in all populations (e.g. Filipino sample: upper facial height exceeded lower by ~3 cm); Boos bimeter readings tend to **exaggerate** VD; calipers/Willis confounded by soft-tissue compression at skin markers.
- Recommended strategy: **combine multiple techniques + digital tools**, individualized to the patient.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative review — no quantitative pooling, no formal risk-of-bias scoring of the 79 studies.
- Most classical methods remain empirical, controversial, and population-dependent.
- Future: standardized protocols, validation of digital tools across populations, 4D-printed adaptive dentures, expanded AI into treatment planning.

## 7. Glossary

- **VD / Vertical Dimension:** facial height between two marked points (typically nose tip and chin), measured at occlusion (OVD/VDO) or at physiologic rest (RVD).
- **OVD / VDO:** occlusal vertical dimension — facial height with teeth in maximal intercuspation.
- **RVD:** rest vertical dimension — mandible at physiologic rest.
- **Freeway space (interocclusal rest space):** RVD − OVD, normally ~2–4 mm.
- **Closest speaking space:** minimal interocclusal clearance during sibilant phonation (~1–2 mm).
- **Mentolabial (labiomental) fold:** the horizontal groove between the lower lip and chin; deepens when OVD is reduced/overclosed.
- **Mentalis strain:** visible tension/dimpling of the chin (mentalis muscle) seen with reduced OVD and inadequate lip seal.
- **Willis gauge:** caliper-type device measuring subnasion→gnathion facial height.
