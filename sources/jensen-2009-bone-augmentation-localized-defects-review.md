## One-line Summary
ITI 4th Consensus background SR (108 studies, 11 RCTs) of bone augmentation in four defect types — dehiscence/fenestration, horizontal ridge, vertical ridge, maxillary sinus floor: no single superior protocol identified, but implant survival in augmented bone equals pristine bone (level A–B evidence).

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: International Journal of Oral & Maxillofacial Implants 2009;24 Suppl:218–236 (4th ITI Consensus Conference proceedings background paper).
- **Study type**: Systematic review (MEDLINE + hand-search), narrative synthesis stratified by defect type.
- **Authors**: Jensen (Copenhagen) + Terheyden (Kassel) — both senior bone-augmentation clinicians.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Stratifies augmentation evidence into 4 clinically meaningful defect categories — the framework still used in ITI consensus today.
- Establishes that implant survival in augmented bone is comparable to pristine bone (level A–B) — the key reassurance for clinicians considering augmentation prior to implant placement.
- Documents heterogeneity that prevented identification of a single best protocol in any defect type — useful caveat against dogmatic claims.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- MEDLINE search + hand-search of selected journals.
- Inclusion: any level of clinical evidence except expert opinion; English; ≥10 patients; ≥12 mo follow-up after implant loading.
- 4-category outcome stratification: (1) dehiscence/fenestration defects, (2) horizontal ridge augmentation, (3) vertical ridge augmentation, (4) maxillary sinus floor elevation (lateral window or transalveolar).
- Focus: (a) outcome of individual grafting protocols, (b) survival rates of implants placed in augmented bone.
- 2006 abstracts screened → 424 full-text → 108 included; 11 RCTs, majority prospective/retrospective with small n and short follow-up.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **No superior protocol identified** for any of the four defect categories — heterogeneity precluded ranking.
- **A series of grafting materials can be considered well-documented for different indications** based on the included evidence body.
- **Implant survival in augmented bone = pristine bone**, supported at level A–B evidence.
- Authors emphasize that the choice between autograft, xenograft (DBBM), allograft, alloplast, or combinations should be made by indication, not by universal ranking.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Pre-2009 literature — needs updating against subsequent SRs/network meta-analyses.
- Heterogeneity in defect classification, measurement, follow-up complicated quantitative synthesis.
- Few RCTs (11/108); most evidence is observational.
- Patient-reported outcomes (morbidity, comfort) not systematically addressed.

## 6. Related Work
- Akbarzadeh Baghban 2009 — Parallel 2009 SR/MA of Bio-Oss vs autogenous in intrabony defects.
- Bassir 2018 — ARP SR+MA covering postextraction socket subset.
- Mordenfeld 2014 — Long-term 10-yr clinical data for MSFA with DPBB + autogenous.
- Del Fabbro 2025 — Recent SR of sinus augmentation materials.

## 7. Glossary
- **Dehiscence/fenestration defect**: Localized buccal bone loss exposing implant surface; treated by GBR (guided bone regeneration) with graft + membrane.
- **Horizontal ridge augmentation**: Widening of buccolingual ridge dimension for implant placement.
- **Vertical ridge augmentation**: Increasing apico-coronal ridge height — technically the most demanding.
- **MSFA (Maxillary Sinus Floor Augmentation)**: Lateral window or transcrestal elevation of sinus floor with graft.
- **ITI**: International Team for Implantology — issues periodic consensus statements based on background SRs.
