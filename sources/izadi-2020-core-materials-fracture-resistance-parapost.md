## Why Ingested

ParaPost 제품(티타늄 vented/serrated)을 FRC post와 직접 비교한 fracture-resistance 실험. 기존 [[post-and-core/wang-2019-fiber-posts-vs-metal-posts-severely-damaged]]·[[post-and-core/tsintsadze-2022-survival-rates-glass-fiber-metal-posts]]의 임상 fiber > metal 근거를, 이 논문은 in-vitro 파절저항으로 재확인하면서 "core 재료 종류는 파절저항에 무관"이라는 별개의 결론을 추가한다.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro fracture-resistance study (108 mandibular premolars, 9 groups × 12): FRC-post groups pooled significantly higher fracture load than ParaPost groups (397.6 vs 265.6 N, P<0.001); among the three core build-up materials (Photo Core, LuxaCore, Core Max II) no material was superior.

## 한줄요약

인비트로 파절저항 연구 (하악 소구치 108개, 9군×12): FRC post군의 통합 파절하중이 ParaPost군보다 유의하게 높음 (397.6 vs 265.6 N, P<0.001); 3가지 core 재료(Photo Core, LuxaCore, Core Max II) 간 우열은 없음.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Full 2 (post: FRC vs ParaPost) × 4 (core material) factorial (108 teeth) isolating post effect from core-material effect on fracture resistance.
- FRC posts significantly outperform titanium ParaPosts on pooled fracture load (P<0.001).
- Core build-up material (Photo Core / LuxaCore / Core Max II ± bonding) does NOT significantly change fracture resistance.
- Bonding agent with Core Max II gives a small, non-significant increase.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 108 sound single-rooted mandibular first premolars (orthodontic extractions). Sample size 12/group.
- RCT-in-vitro randomization to 9 groups: Group 1 control (intact); Groups 2–5 FRC post + Photo Core / LuxaCore / Core Max II with bonding / Core Max II without bonding; Groups 6–9 ParaPost + same four core conditions.
- Root canal treatment (step-back, ISO 40, gutta-percha + AH26, lateral compaction); silicone PDL simulation; acrylic mounting; full-metal crown.
- ParaPost: titanium vented & serrated No. 4 (Coltène/Whaledent), #2 peeso-reamer then #4 ParaPost drill (12 mm). Posts cemented with Panavia F2. Load at 45° to long axis, crosshead 1 mm/min to fracture. ANOVA + Tukey (α=0.05).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Group means (N): G1 454.0±62.7; G2 410.8±48.3; G3 365.1±42.1; G4 423.7±111.7 (highest of post groups); G5 392.4±90.0; G6 292.3±83.9; G7 242.3±73.4 (lowest); G8 278.2±67.9; G9 247.3±49.6.
- Pooled FRC (Groups 2–5, n=46) 397.56±77.93 vs pooled ParaPost (Groups 6–9, n=45) 265.62±71.05; t=8.4, P<0.001.
- Highest overall: G4 (FRC + Core Max II + bonding); significant vs ParaPost and control, NS vs other FRC groups.
- Core material main effect within each post type: not significant.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro static loading (1 mm/min) unlike dynamic/fatigue oral loading; no thermocycling.
- Extracted-tooth variability (age, storage, root anatomy, pre-extraction status).
- Single geometry (mandibular premolar), single cement (Panavia F2).

## 6. Related Work

- Makade et al. (ParaPost highest fracture resistance vs control); Asadzadeh Aghadaee et al. (cast > FRC > ParaPost); Fokkinga et al. (metal > glass fiber > control ETT); Ahn & Sorensen (Photo Core > LuxaCore); Shahryarpanah (no Photo Core vs LuxaCore difference).

## 7. Glossary

- **FRC post**: fiber-reinforced composite post; hardness/modulus near dentin, double-tapered.
- **ParaPost (titanium, vented/serrated)**: parallel rigid metal prefabricated post (Coltène/Whaledent).
- **Core build-up material**: Photo Core (light-cure, high filler), LuxaCore (dual-cure), Core Max II (powder/liquid resin).
- **Fracture resistance**: peak load (N) at specimen fracture under a universal testing machine.
