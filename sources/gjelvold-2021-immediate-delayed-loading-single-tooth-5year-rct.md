## Why Ingested

기존 [[wiki/overviews/implant-loading-protocol-prosthesis-type-overview]]의 즉시부하 vs 지연부하 (Immediate Loading vs Delayed Loading) 결론이 single-tooth 심미영역에서 5년 장기 데이터로 지지되는지 확인이 필요했다. 본 RCT (Gjelvold 2021)는 상악 심미영역 단일치 50명을 5년 추적하며 생존율·변연골소실(MBL)·papilla index·PES/WES를 직접 비교해, [[wiki/immediate-implant/esthetic-soft-tissue/samet-2023-immediate-placement-loading-esthetic-zone]] 및 [[wiki/immediate-implant/esposito-2015-immediate-loading-vs-delayed-anterior-rct]]의 즉시부하 심미 안전성 주장을 장기 근거로 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

RCT (n=50, healed maxillary anterior/premolar sites): immediate loading (IL) and delayed loading (DL) of single-tooth implants gave statistically indistinguishable 5-year survival (100% vs 95.8%), marginal bone loss (−0.50 vs −0.54 mm), papilla index, and PES/WES — IL is as safe as DL esthetically and biologically at 5 years.

## 한줄요약

무작위대조시험 (RCT, n=50, 치유된 상악 심미영역): 단일치 임플란트의 즉시부하 (Immediate Loading, IL)와 지연부하 (Delayed Loading, DL)는 5년 생존율(100% vs 95.8%), 변연골소실(−0.50 vs −0.54 mm), 치간유두지수, 분홍·백색 심미점수(PES/WES) 모두 통계적 차이가 없어 — IL이 DL만큼 심미적·생물학적으로 안전함을 5년 시점에 입증.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **5-year head-to-head RCT of IL vs DL for single-tooth implants** in the maxillary aesthetic zone (teeth 15–25) — extends the same cohort's 1-year results to long-term follow-up with very low attrition (4% unaccounted at 5 years).
2. **No clinical or aesthetic penalty for immediate loading at 5 years**: survival, success, MBL, papilla index, PES, and WES were all statistically equivalent between groups.
3. **Papilla-index gap closes over time**: the 1-year study showed a significant DL advantage in papilla fill (distal papilla p=0.002 at definitive crown placement); by 5 years the difference was no longer significant (p=0.147 for complete bilateral papilla fill), supporting progressive papilla regeneration around immediately loaded single crowns.
4. **PES improves significantly between 1 and 5 years** in both groups (IL p=0.017, DL p=0.046), while WES stays stable — soft-tissue maturation drives long-term esthetic gains.
5. **Smoking, not loading protocol, drove bone loss**: the only significant MBL difference was smokers vs non-smokers (total −1.90 vs −0.40 mm, p=0.021); the single implant failure was in a smoker.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: prospective RCT, two parallel arms (IL vs DL), sealed-envelope randomization; surgeon (J.K.) blinded to allocation; MBL and esthetic assessment by an independent blinded examiner (B.R.C.) not involved in treatment.
- **Sample**: 62 patients recruited, 12 excluded (per prior 1-year report) → **50 patients randomized** (IL n=25, DL n=25). One DL implant failed at 3 months, so 5-year analysis is IL n=24, DL n=23 (47 implants). 2 patients (4%) unaccounted at 5 years (1 DL uncontactable; 1 IL declined visit but confirmed implant/restoration in place → counted as survival).
- **Inclusion**: ≥18 y, single-tooth edentulous space between maxillary teeth 15–25, **healed bone** (≥4 months post-extraction or agenesis), informed consent.
- **Exclusion**: general-health contraindications to surgery, poor oral hygiene (full-mouth plaque >25%), need for bone grafting/ridge augmentation.
- **Implants**: BioHorizons Tapered Internal (Laser-Lok collar), placed per manufacturer protocol (≥half the Laser-Lok collar in bone contact), none subcrestal. **All IL implants reached ≥30 Ncm insertion torque** (loading prerequisite).
- **Loading protocols**:
  - **IL**: immediate screw-retained temporary crown in light centric, free of eccentric contacts, for 2 months → definitive restoration.
  - **DL**: two-stage surgery, 4-month submerged healing → screw-retained temporary to shape emergence profile → definitive restoration.
- **Definitive crowns**: titanium base (Medentica) + individually fabricated zirconia abutment (I-butment, Biomain) + veneered (GC Initial), all by the same technician. Screw-retained preferred; cemented only when screw access compromised esthetics (excess-cement check with PA radiographs, inspection, probing).
- **Outcomes & timing**: clinical/radiographic follow-up at 3, 6, 12 months and 5 years after definitive crown. Baseline for MBL = implant surgery day; baseline for esthetics = definitive crown placement.
  - **Survival**: implants not lost (regardless of condition).
  - **Success** (Albrektsson criteria): MBL ≤1.0 mm first year and <0.2 mm/yr thereafter, no mobility/pain/peri-implant radiolucency/infection.
  - **MBL**: digital intra-oral PA radiographs (long-cone paralleling), measured in ImageJ from implant-abutment junction, calibrated to 1.00 mm inter-thread distance; mean of mesial+distal.
  - **Papilla index** (Jemt), mesial + distal.
  - **PES** (Fürhauser, pink esthetic score) and **WES** (Belser, white esthetic score) from intraoral photographs; (almost) perfect = PES≥12 & WES≥9; aesthetic failure = PES≤7 or WES≤5; complete failure = PES≤7 and WES≤5.
- **Statistics**: SPSS v26; normality by Kolmogorov–Smirnov, homoscedasticity by Levene; Student's t / Mann–Whitney; chi-square / Fisher exact for categorical; Wilcoxon signed-rank for within-group time changes; significance p<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Survival & success (5 years)**
| Outcome | IL | DL |
|---|---|---|
| Implant survival | **100%** | **95.8%** (1 failure at 3 mo, a smoker) |
| Implant success (MBL criterion) | **91.7%** | **83.3%** |

**Marginal bone loss (MBL, mm; negative = loss)**
| Time | IL | DL | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 year | −0.57 ± 0.53 | −0.70 ± 0.58 | 0.430 |
| 5 years | **−0.50 ± 0.73** | **−0.54 ± 0.65** | **0.782 (NS)** |

- Slight **mean bone gain** between 1- and 5-year follow-up in both groups.
- **Smoking effect**: total 5-yr MBL smokers −1.90 ± 0.90 (n=7) vs non-smokers −0.40 ± 0.58 (n=40), **p=0.021**. No significant MBL difference by sex or by cemented vs screw-retained.

**Papilla index (Jemt)** — complete bilateral papilla fill at 5 yr: IL 33.3% vs DL 56.5% (p=0.147, NS). Distal papilla showed a significant DL advantage only at definitive crown placement (1.21 vs 2.09, p=0.002); by 1 and 5 years all comparisons NS.

**Aesthetic scores (mean ± SD)**
| Score | Time | IL | DL | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| PES | crown placement | 8.58 ± 2.32 | 9.48 ± 3.03 | 0.260 |
| PES | 1 year | 10.38 ± 2.43 | 10.83 ± 2.42 | 0.517 |
| PES | 5 years | 11.00 ± 2.08 | 11.17 ± 2.39 | 0.501 |
| WES | 5 years | 7.63 ± 1.53 | 7.91 ± 1.53 | 0.365 |

- **PES improved significantly 1→5 yr** (IL p=0.017, DL p=0.046, Wilcoxon); **WES unchanged** (p=0.564 / 1.000).
- **Combined aesthetic outcome at 5 yr**: almost perfect (PES≥12 & WES≥9) IL 20.8% / DL 30.4%; acceptable IL 66.7% / DL 56.5%; aesthetic failure IL 12.5% / DL 13.0%; complete failure IL 4.2% / DL 8.7%.

**Complications (entire follow-up)** — only 2: one DL technical complication (loss of titanium-base retention at 52 mo, 4.3%); one IL crown replaced for esthetic reasons after veneering adjacent teeth (47 mo, non-technical). Peri-implant redness/edema + BOP at 5 yr: 8.5% overall (DL 4.3%, IL 12.5%).

**Bottom line**: in healed maxillary aesthetic-zone single-tooth sites with adjacent natural roots and ≥30 Ncm primary stability, **IL and DL are clinically and esthetically equivalent at 5 years**.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Small sample** (50 patients; only 2 smokers in IL, 5 in DL) → low power for subgroup analyses (e.g., IL smoker MBL p=0.060 likely under-powered, not truly null).
- **Restricted indication**: only **healed sites** (≥4 months post-extraction), no grafting/augmentation, single-tooth gaps **flanked by natural roots** — findings do not extend to immediate (fresh-socket) placement, multiple adjacent units, or compromised bone. Authors note adjacent natural roots likely aided good papilla/esthetic scores.
- **Single implant system** (BioHorizons Tapered Internal, Laser-Lok) and **single operator/technician** — limits external generalizability.
- **No ISQ / RFA data reported** — primary stability gated only by ≥30 Ncm insertion torque; the study does not quantify stability dip or use ISQ thresholds.
- 4% attrition is low but one "survival" was self-reported (patient declined clinical exam).

## 6. Related Work

- **Same cohort, 1-year**: Gjelvold et al. 2017 (Clin Implant Dent Relat Res 19:549–558, ref [3]) — showed a significant early DL papilla advantage that this 5-year report shows resolves over time.
- **Mandibular molar IL vs DL RCTs**: Chidagam 2017 (6-yr, ref [18]); Meloni 2018 (5-yr non-occlusal IL, ref [19]) — concordant lack of long-term MBL difference.
- **Smoking & implant failure**: Chrcanovic 2015 SR+MA (ref [12]), Chrcanovic 2016 early-failure analysis (ref [13]) — context for the smoker failure.
- **Esthetic scoring methods**: Fürhauser 2005 (PES, ref [8]); Belser 2009 (WES, ref [9]); Cosyn 2012 (failure thresholds, ref [10]); Jemt 1997 (papilla index, ref [7]).

## 7. Glossary

- **IL (Immediate Loading)**: prosthesis placed in functional/occlusal-light contact at or near implant placement; here a screw-retained temporary at surgery for 2 months.
- **DL (Delayed Loading)**: conventional loading after a submerged healing period (here 4 months, two-stage).
- **MBL (Marginal Bone Loss)**: radiographic crestal bone change from implant-abutment junction; negative = loss.
- **Implant success vs survival**: survival = implant still present; success = additionally meets MBL/health criteria (Albrektsson).
- **PES (Pink Esthetic Score)**: soft-tissue esthetic index (Fürhauser); max 14; ≥12 ≈ perfect.
- **WES (White Esthetic Score)**: crown/tooth esthetic index (Belser); max 10; ≥9 ≈ perfect.
- **Papilla index (Jemt)**: 0–3 scale of interproximal papilla fill.
- **Laser-Lok**: BioHorizons micro-grooved implant collar surface for soft/hard tissue attachment.
- **Insertion torque (≥30 Ncm)**: surgical primary-stability proxy used here as the IL eligibility threshold.
