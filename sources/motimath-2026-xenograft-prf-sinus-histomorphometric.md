## Why Ingested

Head-to-head histomorphometric comparison of xenograft alone vs xenograft+A-PRF in maxillary sinus augmentation using a split-mouth RCT design — adds high-quality PRF-adjunct evidence with histomorphometric and implant survival outcomes at 9 months. [[sinus-lift/lateral/sala-2024-sinus-perforation-outcomes-sr-ma]]

## One-line Summary

Split-mouth prospective RCT (n=30 patients, 60 sinuses, 9-month follow-up): A-PRF+DBBM vs DBBM alone — A-PRF significantly increased new bone formation (42.8% vs 31.2%, p=0.001) and reduced residual graft (28.4% vs 41.6%, p=0.001) with 100% implant survival in both groups.

## 한줄요약

분할구강 전향 RCT (30명 60개 동공, 9개월): A-PRF+DBBM이 DBBM 단독 대비 신생골 유의하게 증가(42.8 vs 31.2%, p=0.001), 잔류이식재 유의하게 감소(28.4 vs 41.6%, p=0.001), 양군 임플란트 생존율 100%.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Bioinformation
- **Volume**: 22(2): 912-916 (2026)
- **Year**: 2026
- **DOI**: 10.6026/973206300220912
- **Study design**: Prospective, randomized, controlled, split-mouth clinical trial
- **Duration**: March 2021 – December 2023
- **Setting**: Department of Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery, KLE VK Institute of Dental Sciences, Karnataka, India (and collaborating institutions)
- **N**: 30 patients, 60 sinuses (30 test: DBBM + A-PRF; 30 control: DBBM alone)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Demonstrates statistically significant histomorphometric advantage of A-PRF over DBBM alone: higher new bone formation and lower residual graft at 9 months
- Uses A-PRF (advanced PRF; lower centrifugation speed 2700 rpm, 12 min) — distinct from classical PRF or L-PRF protocols
- Split-mouth design minimizes inter-patient confounding
- Reports bone density (HU), graft height stability (CBCT), postoperative morbidity (VAS), and implant survival at 9 months
- 100% implant survival in both groups with early loading (4–6 months post-implant)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Inclusion criteria**: Adults 25–70 years; bilateral anterior maxillary edentulism; residual bone height ≤4 mm (CBCT; Kennedy Class I or IV); ASA I-II

**Exclusion criteria**: Uncontrolled systemic disease; >10 cigarettes/day; sinus pathology history; previous sinus surgery; bisphosphonate use; immunosuppression

**Randomization**: Computer-generated tables; concealed envelope allocation; intraoperative assignment

**Surgical technique**:
- Piezoelectric surgery (Mectron) for lateral window; Schneiderian membrane elevation
- Control: 2.0 g DBBM (Bio-Oss, Geistlich; 0.25–1 mm particles)
- Test: 1.5 g DBBM + 4 A-PRF membranes (cut into fragments) + 2 tubes liquid i-PRF
- Concomitant implant placement when residual bone height >3 mm and primary stability >25 Ncm

**PRF preparation** (Choukroun protocol; no anticoagulant, glass tubes; 80 mL blood/patient):
- A-PRF: 2700 rpm, 12 min → membranes compressed 8 min
- i-PRF: 700 rpm, 3 min

**Assessments**:
- CBCT: pre-op, immediate post-op, 9 months; sinus height at 3 levels (mesial, central, distal); HU bone density
- Postoperative morbidity: daily VAS pain and edema for 7 days
- Histomorphometry: 3.0 mm trephine biopsy at 9 months; 10% formalin fixation; H&E + Masson trichrome; Image-Pro Plus 7.0 at 100x; new bone %, graft particle %, connective tissue %
- Implants: Bone Level Tapered, Roxsolid SLActive (Straumann); prosthetic loading 4–6 months post-implant
- Success criteria: Albrektsson et al.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | A-PRF + DBBM | DBBM alone | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| New bone formation (%) | 42.8 ± 6.4 | 31.2 ± 5.9 | 0.001 |
| Residual graft (%) | 28.4 ± 4.7 | 41.6 ± 6.1 | 0.001 |
| Bone density (HU) | Improved | Lower | Significant |
| Graft height stability | Greater | Lesser | Significant |
| Implant survival | 100% | 100% | NS |
| Postoperative morbidity | Reduced | Higher | Significant |

- A-PRF+DBBM: ~37% relative increase in new bone formation versus DBBM alone
- A-PRF+DBBM: ~32% relative reduction in residual graft at 9 months
- Both groups achieved 100% implant survival at 9-month follow-up

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-center study from India
- 9-month follow-up only — longer-term implant survival and bone remodeling not assessed
- Sample size calculated based on prior literature (n=30 per group/sinus)
- A-PRF protocol: differences in centrifugation vs classical L-PRF may limit direct comparison with studies using other PRF generations
- No blinding of operator (surgical nature); histopathologist was blinded
- Reporting journal (Bioinformation) has moderate impact factor

## 6. Related Work

- PRF growth factor profile: PDGF, TGF-β, VEGF, IGF-1 gradually released over 10–14 days
- A-PRF and i-PRF: lower-speed centrifugation protocols with reportedly better biological properties in vitro vs classical PRF
- Contradictory RCTs on PRF+DBBM sinus augmentation — inconsistencies attributed to PRF preparation differences, centrifugation variation, surgical technique, implant timing
- DBBM slow incorporation: 30–45% residual graft volume at 9–12 months is a known limitation

## 7. Glossary

- **A-PRF**: Advanced PRF — Choukroun low-speed protocol (2700 rpm, 12 min); increased growth factor content vs classical PRF
- **i-PRF**: Injectable PRF — liquid form (700 rpm, 3 min); mixed with particulate graft
- **DBBM**: Deproteinized bovine bone mineral (Bio-Oss); osteoconductive xenograft
- **HU**: Hounsfield units — CT bone density measure
- **Schneiderian membrane**: Sinus mucosal lining elevated during sinus lift
- **Albrektsson criteria**: Classic implant success threshold (MBL, mobility, pain, infection)
- **Piezoelectric surgery**: Ultrasonic bone-cutting — reduces membrane perforation risk
