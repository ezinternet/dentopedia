## Why Ingested

기존 [[implants/isq/oh-2008-comparison-of-initial-implant-stability]]가 osteotome vs conventional drilling의 baseline ISQ 차이를 다뤘다면, 본 prospective cohort (Shokri 2013)는 mandibular SLA-coated ITI implant에서 11주간 주간 RFA 추적으로 **stability dip(4주째 최저)**의 시간 곡선을 직접 보여줘 loading timing threshold 논의를 보강한다. [[implants/isq/bavetta-2019-insertion-torque-isq-immediate-loading-extraction-socket]]의 IT↔ISQ↔immediate-loading 맥락과 짝을 이루는 secondary-stability dip 근거.

## One-line Summary

Prospective cohort (15 mandibular SLA ITI implants, 10 patients) tracked weekly by Osstell mentor RFA for 11 weeks: mean ISQ fell from 77.2 at placement to a 72.13 nadir at week 4, then significantly recovered to 75.6 by week 11 without reaching baseline — suggesting loading before week 4 may be disadvantageous.

## 한줄요약

전향적 코호트 연구 (Shokri 2013) — 하악 SLA ITI 임플란트 15개를 11주간 매주 공명주파수분석 (Resonance Frequency Analysis, RFA, Osstell mentor)으로 추적. 평균 임플란트 안정성 지수 (Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ)가 식립 당일 77.2에서 4주째 72.13으로 최저(stability dip)를 찍고 11주째 75.6으로 유의하게 회복(p<0.05)했으나 초기값엔 못 미침 → 4주 이전 하중은 불리할 수 있음을 시사.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Documents the **secondary-stability dip** longitudinally in human mandibular implants: a continuous decline in ISQ from placement to week 4, followed by recovery — captured at unusually fine weekly resolution (surgery day, weeks 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 7, 11).
2. Identifies **week 4 post-placement as the stability nadir** across all bone types studied, framing a "window when loading might be disadvantageous."
3. Reinforces RFA (Osstell mentor / magnetic ISQ) as a noninvasive, repeatable tool for tracking stability changes over the early healing period.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Prospective cohort study; sequential sampling.
- **Patients**: 10 healthy non-smoker patients >40 years old (4 male, 6 female; 42–65 yr) with ≥6 months of complete or partial edentulous mouth.
- **Inclusion**: bone height ≥12 mm, crest width ≥6 mm, bone density D2 or D3 (Friberg classification).
- **Exclusion**: diabetes, osteoporosis, hypertension, cardiac problems, psychological disorders, advanced periodontal disease, poor oral hygiene, lack of cooperation, occlusal discrepancies, insufficient ridge density/height, radiotherapy history, smoking, parafunctional habits.
- **Implants**: 15 threaded SLA-coated ITI (Straumann) implants placed by a one-stage protocol. Insertion torque 35 N/cm. Pre-op planning with NewTom VGI CBCT + panoramic X-ray. Drilling 400–600 rpm under saline irrigation, no additional pressure.
- **RFA measurement**: Osstell mentor (Integration Diagnostics, Sweden) at placement and weekly to week 5, then weeks 7 and 11. First two equal values accepted as authentic. A primary ISQ ≤47 considered a sign of questionable stability. Implants kept unloaded (no provisional prostheses) for the 11-week follow-up.
- **Statistics**: Microsoft Excel 2007; paired *t*-test, significance at *P*<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Mean ISQ (±SD, 95% CI) for the 15 implants over time (Table 1):

| Time | n | Range | Mean ISQ | ±SD | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Surgery day | 15 | 67–85 | **77.20** | 4.92 | 2.49 |
| Week 1 | 15 | 72–80 | 77.00 | 3.51 | 1.78 |
| Week 2 | 15 | 64–80 | 74.20 | 3.82 | 1.93 |
| Week 3 | 15 | 62–80 | 73.20 | 5.19 | 2.62 |
| Week 4 | 15 | 62–79 | **72.13 (nadir)** | 5.69 | 2.88 |
| Week 5 | 15 | 63–78 | 72.33 | 4.62 | 2.34 |
| Week 7 | 15 | 66–79 | 73.55 | 3.89 | 1.79 |
| Week 11 | 15 | 70–80 | **75.60** | 3.72 | 1.88 |

- Lowest mean stability occurred at **week 4** across all bone types.
- ISQ declined progressively from placement, then significantly (P<0.05) increased to 75.6 by week 11, **without returning to the baseline** 77.2.
- Authors conclude loading might be disadvantageous **prior to the 4th week** following placement.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Very small sample: 15 implants in 10 patients, single center, single operator (senior author placed all implants).
- All implants were one system (SLA-coated ITI/Straumann), one anatomic site (mandible), one insertion torque (35 N/cm) — limited generalizability to maxilla, other surfaces, or other protocols.
- Only D2/D3 bone included; bone-type subgroup ISQ trajectories are stated qualitatively, not stratified statistically.
- Statistics limited to paired *t*-tests in Excel; no regression/multivariable modeling, no per-bone-type significance testing.
- Authors explicitly note RFA's limited sensitivity to surrounding bone quality and call for further studies on different systems and variables.

## 6. Related Work

- **Friberg et al.** — correlation of cutting torque and RFA at placement; reported stability of implants in softer bone rises over time, with no bone-type difference at week 12.
- **O'Sullivan et al.** — cadaver insertion-torque vs bone-property study; high values for all bone types except type IV.
- **Boronat López et al.** — higher ISQ for implants in more compact bone.
- **Meredith; Sennerby & Meredith** — first proposed RFA as a qualitative implant-stability method.
- **Huang et al.** — RFA reliability across bone types.
- Cited thresholds from literature: ISQ 57–82 at one year indicates success; very low RFA at two months indicates risk of future failure; ISQ 57–70 indicates stability at early osseointegration.

## 7. Glossary

- **RFA (Resonance Frequency Analysis)**: noninvasive method quantifying implant stability via the resonance of a transducer/peg attached to the fixture.
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: 1–100 scale output of RFA; higher = stiffer implant-bone interface.
- **Osstell mentor**: magnetic RFA device used here for ISQ measurement.
- **Primary stability**: mechanical stability at placement (bone-implant friction/engagement).
- **Secondary stability**: biological stability from osseointegration replacing mechanical fixation during healing.
- **Stability dip**: transient ISQ decrease in early healing as primary stability is lost faster than secondary stability is gained (here, nadir at week 4).
- **D2/D3 bone (Friberg/Lekholm-Zarb context)**: moderately dense to less dense bone density classes.
- **SLA**: sandblasted, large-grit, acid-etched implant surface.
