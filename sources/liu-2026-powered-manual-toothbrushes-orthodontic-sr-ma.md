## Why Ingested

New 2026 SR+MA (European Journal of Orthodontics, search to Nov 2025) settling whether powered toothbrushes (PTBs) beat manual (MTBs) for plaque/gingivitis control specifically in fixed-appliance orthodontic patients — a population at high plaque-retention risk where four prior SRs (last updated 2021) gave conflicting verdicts. Extends the wiki's plaque-control evidence in [[periodontics/jo-2008-management-of-dental-biofilm-through]] from professional-care biofilm management to the home-care toothbrush-type question in the orthodontic context, and pairs with the OTM-periodontium biology in [[orthodontics/li-2021-periodontium-orthodontic-tooth-movement-biomechanics]] (why FA plaque control matters for periodontal stability during tooth movement). According to PubMed; DOI: https://doi.org/10.1093/ejo/cjag026

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (23 papers / 39 comparisons, fixed-appliance orthodontic patients): PTBs > MTBs on plaque (SMD -0.77), gingivitis (SMD -0.82), and bleeding (SMD -0.51) by incremental-difference meta-analysis, but evidence is LOW quality (I2 80–93%, possible publication bias, TSA inconclusive); descriptively most comparisons showed no difference, and PS/GS significance vanished when restricted to low-RoB or non-industry studies. Only counter-rotational PTBs beat MTBs on plaque by action mode (very low quality).

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (23편 / 39비교, 고정성 교정장치 환자): 전동칫솔(PTB)이 수동칫솔(MTB)보다 치면세균막(SMD -0.77)·치은염(SMD -0.82)·치은출혈(SMD -0.51)에서 우세하나 근거 수준은 낮음(I2 80–93%, 출판편향 배제 불가, TSA 미결). 서술적 분석에서는 대부분 비교에서 차이 없었고, 저편향·비산업지원 연구로 한정하면 PS·GS 유의성 소실. 작용방식별로는 역회전(CR) PTB만 치면세균막에서 우세(very low quality).

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First SR to add a **PTB action-mode subgroup analysis** (oscillating-rotating-pulsating ORP, oscillating-rotating OR, counter-rotational CR, sonic) for the fixed-appliance (FA) orthodontic population.
2. Most up-to-date synthesis (search to Nov 2025), resolving four prior SRs that conflicted (2 favored PTB, 1 found no difference, 1 found insufficient evidence; last update 2021).
3. Tightened inclusion to **rechargeable, single-head, motor-driven PTBs** with no concurrent interdental device — isolating the mechanical PTB-vs-MTB effect.
4. Documents an **evidence–product gap**: the only action mode that beat MTBs on plaque (CR) is no longer widely marketed; OR and sonic dominate the market but lack significant subgroup benefit.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **PICOS**: P = orthodontic patients with buccal FAs; I = powered toothbrush; C = manual toothbrush; O = plaque scores (PS), gingivitis scores (GS), gingival bleeding scores (BS); S = (R)CCTs.
- **Databases**: PubMed-MEDLINE + Cochrane-CENTRAL to Nov 2025 + hand-searched reference lists. Two independent reviewers, Rayyan screening.
- **Excluded**: replaceable-battery PTBs, multi-head PTBs, concurrent interdental cleaning (floss / water-jet), split-mouth, non-English; parallel and crossover designs allowed.
- **Risk of bias**: RoB 2 (RCTs), ROBINS-I (CCTs).
- **Synthesis**: descriptive vote-counting + random-effects meta-analysis on **incremental-difference** scores (end-trial minus baseline; SDΔ reconstructed assuming within-subject r = 0.5). SMD (Hedges' g) for different scales, MD for same scale. Heterogeneity by I2; sensitivity analysis; funnel-plot publication bias (Egger/Begg/trim-and-fill); trial sequential analysis (TSA); GRADE.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Inclusion**: 841 unique → 54 full-text → **23 papers, 39 comparisons** (all PS; 21 GS; 24 BS). Samples 20–144, ages 10–53. Only 9/23 met ADA ≥30/group. RoB: 19 RCTs (3 low / 4 some-concern / 12 high) + 4 CCTs (1 low / 2 moderate / 1 serious).
- **Descriptive (vote-counting)**: most comparisons showed **no difference** — PS 64%, GS 76%, BS 75% NS.
- **Overall incremental-difference meta-analyses (random-effects, SMD; negative favors PTB)**:
  - PS: 13 studies (14 comp), **SMD -0.77** (95% CI -1.33; -0.21), P = .007, I2 = 92% — medium
  - GS: 10 (10), **SMD -0.82** (-1.55; -0.09), P = .03, I2 = 93% — large
  - BS: 9 (10), **SMD -0.51** (-0.88; -0.13), P = .008, I2 = 80% — medium
- **Action-mode subgroup (PS)**: only **CR-PTB significant** — SMD -1.45 (-2.85; -0.05), P = .04 (2 comparisons, large, very low quality). ORP/OR, ORP, OR, sonic all NS. GS and BS action-mode subgroups all NS.
- **Same-index MD**: GI for GS MD -0.22 (-0.43; -0.02) P=.04; MPBI for BS MD -0.54 (-0.71; -0.36) P<.00001; most others (OMPI plaque, BOP, EIBI) NS.
- **Sensitivity**: BS stayed significant under all restrictions; RCT-only kept PS (-0.73), GS (-1.02), BS (-0.44) significant — BUT restricting to **low-RoB-only** or **non-industry-only** turned PS and GS **non-significant** (5 studies drove PS, 3 GS, 4 BS; 2 industry-supported, 5 moderate-high RoB).
- **TSA**: required information size NOT reached, Z-curve did not cross O'Brien-Fleming boundaries → meta-analyses **inconclusive**.
- **GRADE**: overall quality **LOW** for all three (downgraded for RoB, inconsistency, possible publication bias) → **no recommendation made**. Effect-size magnitude PS medium / GS large / BS medium.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small samples (only 9/23 met ADA ≥30/group); low-to-high RoB; incomplete data; RIS not reached (TSA inconclusive).
- Substantial-to-considerable heterogeneity (I2 80–93%) driven partly by 9 PS / 4 GS / 7 BS different indices → authors call for "common indices" (OMPI for PS, GI for GS, BOP for BS) and more objective digital/3D/AI plaque imaging.
- ORP-PTBs that help non-orthodontic patients showed no FA-population benefit — hard-to-reach FA plaque may sustain gingivitis regardless of brush.
- CR-PTB (the only PS-significant mode) is largely off-market; future trials should target OR and sonic modes, which dominate the market but lack significant subgroup evidence.

## 7. Glossary

- **PTB / MTB**: powered toothbrush / manual toothbrush.
- **FA**: fixed appliance (brackets/bands/wires).
- **PS / GS / BS**: plaque scores / gingivitis scores / gingival bleeding scores.
- **Action modes**: ORP = oscillating-rotating-pulsating; OR = oscillating-rotating; CR = counter-rotational; sonic.
- **SMD / MD**: standardized mean difference (Hedges' g; ≤0.2 none, >0.2–0.5 small, >0.5–0.8 medium, >0.8 large) / mean difference.
- **Incremental difference**: end-trial minus baseline (negative = clinical improvement).
- **OMPI / GI / BOP / MPBI / EIBI**: orthodontic-modified Silness-Löe plaque index / Löe-Silness gingival index / bleeding on probing / modified papillary bleeding index / Eastman interdental bleeding index.
- **TSA**: trial sequential analysis; **RIS**: required information size.
- **RoB 2 / ROBINS-I**: risk-of-bias tools for RCTs / non-randomized studies.
- **GRADE**: Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation.
