## One-line Summary
Meta-analysis of 21 RCTs (up to 60-month follow-up) showing that silorane, ormocer, and bulk-fill composites have similar clinical performance to conventional methacrylate composites, except for marginally inferior marginal adaptation at 12 months for modified-monomer composites.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Systematic Review and Meta-analysis
- **Published**: February 21, 2018 in PLOS ONE (open access, CC-BY)
- **PROSPERO Registration**: CRD42015023940
- **Funding**: State University of Maringá; CNPq (#307217-2014-0), Brazil
- **Affiliations**: State University of Maringá (Maringá, Paraná); State University of Campinas (Piracicaba, São Paulo), Brazil

## 2. Key Contributions
- First meta-analysis specifically comparing low-polymerization-shrinkage composites (silorane, ormocer, bulk-fill) as a group against conventional methacrylate composites in posterior Class I/II restorations
- Defined inclusion target: composites that (1) do not use Bis-GMA or traditional di-/methacrylates as main monomer, and (2) contain modified monomers allowing reduced shrinkage, modified incremental technique, or increased depth of cure
- Maximum follow-up in included studies: 60 months (5 years)
- Only statistically significant finding: conventional methacrylates showed better marginal adaptation at 12 months than modified-monomer composites

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Databases**: PubMed, Web of Science, Scopus, LILACS, EMBASE
- **Study type**: RCTs with ≥6 months follow-up
- **Target composites**: Modified-monomer types (silorane, ormocer, bulk-fill) — NOT Bis-GMA or conventional di-/methacrylates
- **Restorations**: Class I or II, permanent dentition
- **Outcomes**: Marginal integrity/adaptation, marginal discoloration, recurrent caries, retention, postoperative sensitivity
- **Initial search**: 14,217 studies → 21 articles after screening
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane Collaboration scheme; two independent reviewers
- **Result**: 6 of 11 studies "low risk"; 5 "unclear risk"

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Marginal adaptation at 12 months**: Methacrylate composites significantly better than modified-monomer composites — the only statistically significant result
- **All other outcomes** (marginal discoloration, recurrent caries, retention, postoperative sensitivity): No statistically significant differences
- Heterogeneity (I²): Not significant across parameters
- Excluding studies <24 months follow-up did not change the meta-analysis results
- Longest follow-up: 60 months
- Composite types evaluated: silorane-based, ormocer-based, bulk-fill (single-increment)
- **Key conclusion**: Low polymerization shrinkage is NOT the principal factor determining clinical superiority of a resin

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Heterogeneous group of "low-shrinkage" composites (silorane, ormocer, bulk-fill) pooled together — may mask material-specific differences
- Limited number of studies per composite type
- Maximum 60-month follow-up — very long-term behavior (>10 years) unknown
- RCT-level risk of bias: 5 of 11 "unclear" — some uncertainty remains
- Self-reported manufacturer monomer classification may not always reflect actual shrinkage behavior

## 6. Related Work
- Magno et al. 2016: Silorane-specific meta-analysis reaching same conclusion (SBC = MBC clinically)
- Boaro et al. 2019: Broader SR+MA covering physicochemical properties and RCT data
- Meereis et al. 2018: In vitro SR+MA on shrinkage stress reduction strategies
- Zailai et al. 2025: More recent SR+MA focusing on bulk-fill specifically

## 7. Glossary
- **Silorane**: Ring-opening monomer (siloxane + oxirane hybrid) used as low-shrinkage alternative to Bis-GMA; minimal volumetric shrinkage (~0.99% vs ~2–3% for methacrylates)
- **Ormocer**: Organically Modified Ceramic — inorganic-organic hybrid polymer with reduced shrinkage
- **Bis-GMA**: Bisphenol A-glycidyl methacrylate — traditional high-molecular-weight base monomer in conventional composites
- **Marginal adaptation**: Clinical criterion assessing continuity between restoration edge and tooth surface (typically scored with USPHS or similar criteria)
- **Methacrylate composite (MBC)**: Conventional composite using Bis-GMA, UDMA, or TEGDMA as main monomers
