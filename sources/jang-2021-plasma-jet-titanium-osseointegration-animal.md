## One-line Summary

In vivo study (6 mongrel dogs, 41 implants: 21 NTAPPJ-treated, 20 control, maxilla+mandible, 4 or 8 weeks): non-thermal atmospheric pressure plasma jet (NTAPPJ) significantly increased bone volume (BV) and BIC at 4 weeks (p<0.05), but the difference diminished at 8 weeks as controls caught up — benefit is concentrated in the early healing period.

## 한줄요약

개 in vivo(mongrel 6마리, 임플란트 41개: NTAPPJ 21·대조 20, 상·하악, 4 또는 8주): 비열 대기압 플라즈마 제트(NTAPPJ)는 4주에 골량(BV)·골-임플란트 접촉률(BIC)을 유의하게 높였으나(p<0.05), 8주에는 대조군이 따라잡아 차이가 줄었다 — 이득은 초기 치유기에 집중.

## 1. Document Information

- Journal: Materials 2021, 14(8):1942
- DOI: 10.3390/ma14081942
- Study type: in vivo animal (mongrel dog)
- Device: non-thermal atmospheric pressure plasma jet (NTAPPJ)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Clean demonstration of early-only benefit (4 wk significant, 8 wk converges).
- Maxilla + mandible placement; BV and BIC endpoints.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 41 implants (21 NTAPPJ, 20 control) in maxilla/mandible of 6 mongrel dogs; 4 or 8 weeks.
- BV and BIC by region of interest; Wilcoxon rank-sum test.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- 4 weeks: NTAPPJ significantly higher BV and BIC than control (p<0.05).
- 8 weeks: differences less significant — control caught up.
- Conclusion: NTAPPJ aids the critical initial healing/stability period.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Benefit transient (early only); animal model.
- Long-term predictability inferred, not directly shown.
