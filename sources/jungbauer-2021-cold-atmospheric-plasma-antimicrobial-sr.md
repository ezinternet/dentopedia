## One-line Summary

Systematic review of in-vitro studies (55 included; quality score 31–92%): cold atmospheric plasma (CAP) showed antimicrobial effect against dental pathogens with strongly varying reduction factors that clustered by target pathogen, working gas, and treatment time; a time-dependent increase of effect was consistent; heterogeneity precludes a specific protocol, and multi-species biofilm models are needed before RCTs.

## 한줄요약

in vitro 체계적 문헌고찰(포함 55편, 품질점수 31–92%): 저온대기압플라즈마(CAP)는 구강 병원균에 항균효과를 보였고, 감소폭은 병원균·작동가스·처리시간별로 군집을 이뤘으며 시간의존적 증가가 일관됨. 이질성 때문에 특정 프로토콜 권고는 불가, RCT 전 다균종 바이오필름 모델 필요.

## 1. Document Information

- Journal: Antibiotics 2021, 10(2):211
- DOI: 10.3390/antibiotics10020211
- Study type: systematic review of in-vitro studies (PubMed, Embase, Scopus)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Synthesizes CAP antimicrobial evidence across 55 in-vitro studies; introduces a standardized assessment tool for in-vitro comparison.
- Identifies the determinants of effect: pathogen, working gas, treatment time (time-dependent).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Databases: PubMed, Embase, Scopus.
- Extracted device parameters, experimental setups, microbial cultivation; quality scored with a newly designed tool.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- 55 studies included (quality score 31–92%).
- Reduction factors varied strongly; clusters by set pathogen, working gas, treatment time.
- Time-dependent increase of antimicrobial effect across studies.
- CAP a promising antimicrobial option within a clinically feasible application time.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro only; heterogeneous → no device/protocol recommendation.
- Multi-species biofilm models needed before randomized clinical trials.
