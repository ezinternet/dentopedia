## Why Ingested

Extends the screw-mechanics evidence anchored by [[prosthetic-materials/velez-2020-implant-connection-abutment-design-screw]] by directly testing whether *connection design* (external vs internal) governs preload maintenance. The clinically actionable finding — re-tightening the retaining screw a few minutes after insertion measurably restores preload (sedimentation/embedment relaxation), and connection design itself does NOT determine preload retention — reinforces why the retightening step matters more than the choice of connection geometry.

## One-line Summary

In vitro RTV study of 180 Klockner implants (4 connection designs, external SK2/KL & internal Vega/Essential) finding connection design did not influence preload maintenance, cyclic loading (500 N × 1000 cycles) significantly lowered removal torque for all connections, and multiple re-tightening yielded higher RTV than single tightening (except Vega) — hence clinically retighten retaining screws a few minutes after insertion.

## 한줄요약

180개 Klockner 임플란트(외부연결 SK2/KL·내부연결 Vega/Essential 4종)의 제거토크값(RTV) 실험. 연결방식(connection design)은 예압(preload) 유지에 영향이 없었고, 반복하중(500 N×1000회)은 모든 연결에서 제거토크를 유의하게 낮췄으며, 다회 재조임이 단회 조임보다 높은 RTV를 보였다(Vega 제외) → 임상적으로 보철나사는 식립 수 분 뒤 재조임 권장.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Direct head-to-head comparison of preload maintenance across 4 implant-abutment connection designs (2 external hexagon, 2 internal) under three sequential conditions: single tightening, multiple tightening, and multiple tightening + cyclic loading.
- Demonstrates that connection design (external vs internal) did NOT significantly influence preload (removal-torque) maintenance — countering the common assumption that internal connections inherently resist loosening better.
- Quantifies the sedimentation/embedment-relaxation effect: removal torque is always lower than insertion torque, and is significantly reduced by cyclic loading.
- Provides a mechanistic rationale and clinical recommendation for re-tightening the retaining screw a few minutes (3–10 min) after insertion, before masticatory loading.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Samples:** 180 Klockner implants (SOADCO, Andorra), all 4 mm diameter × 12 mm length, divided into 4 groups of 45 (each group split into 3 subgroups of 15 for the 3 phases):
  - **SK2** — external connection, hexagon 1.8 mm high, grade III cp-Ti; insertion torque 25 Ncm.
  - **KL (0.7)** — external connection, hexagon 0.7 mm high, grade III/IV cp-Ti; insertion torque 30 Ncm.
  - **Vega** — internal connection (OPTIMUM titanium); insertion torque 25 Ncm.
  - **Essential** — internal connection, grade III cp-Ti; insertion torque 30 Ncm.
- Each group used 45 implants / 45 straight titanium abutments / 45 titanium screws.
- Implants mounted in a custom stainless-steel metallic support, 2 mm of coronal portion exposed.
- **Tightening:** cordless prosthodontic screwdriver with torque calibration (NSK iSD900) at the manufacturer-recommended torque.
- **RTV measurement:** digital torque meter (Andilog Centor Touch Star TH).
- **Three phases:**
  - **Phase I (single tightening):** screw tightened once; RTV measured after 1 min.
  - **Phase II (multiple tightening):** tightened 10× with 15 s intervals; RTV measured after 1 min.
  - **Phase III (multiple tightening + loading):** tightened 10× (15 s intervals), then cyclic load 500 N × 1000 cycles applied (CS Dental fatigue machine, Idearum ID1-BAD); RTV measured afterward.
- **Statistics:** SPSS v27; Shapiro-Wilk normality, ANOVA; significance threshold p < 0.05. Sample-size calc (N Query, from Al-Otaibi data) required min 33 per group; power 80%.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Phase I (single tightening):** RTV < insertion torque for all connections, but statistically significant only for the two internal connections — Essential (30.497 → 27.692; p < 0.001) and Vega (26.496 → 25.562; p < 0.016). At equal theoretical torque (25 Ncm: Vega vs SK2; 30 Ncm: Essential vs KL), no significant difference between internal and external connections.
- **Phase II (multiple tightening):** RTV significantly lower than insertion torque for all connections. Multiple re-tightening produced HIGHER RTV than single tightening for all implants EXCEPT Vega (multiple 25.40 Ncm vs single 25.56 Ncm). Mean torque after 10 tightenings increased significantly over time for the other connections.
- **Phase III (multiple tightening + cyclic loading):** lowest RTV of all three phases, significantly below insertion torque for all connections (ANOVA F(3;56)=20.122, p<0.05). At 25 Ncm theoretical torque, SK2 (28.27→22.90) and Vega (27.21→25.16) differed significantly; at 30 Ncm, KL and Essential showed similar removal-torque means.
- SK2 and Vega differed from each other across all 3 phases; Essential and KL behaved similarly.
- **Overall:** connection design did not influence preload maintenance; loading significantly decreased RTV; multiple tightening > single tightening for preload retention.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In vitro condition — the oral environment (saliva, temperature, multidirectional masticatory forces) could not be exactly reproduced; results need clinical validation.
- Only axial cyclic loading (500 N, 1000 cycles) was applied; relatively low cycle count vs physiologic fatigue.
- Single manufacturer (Klockner) and fixed implant dimensions (4 × 12 mm) limit generalizability across systems.
- RTV is an indirect surrogate for residual preload, not a direct preload measurement.

## 6. Related Work

- Al-Otaibi et al., Jorge et al. — corroborate that removal torque < insertion torque in external hexagonal and internal connections.
- Breeding et al. — deformation/flow of components reduces torque 2–10% in first moments (sedimentation effect), basis for re-tightening recommendation.
- Kim et al. — preload loss from sedimentation can lead to screw loosening.
- Cashman et al. (no significant post-fatigue RTV loss) vs Arshad et al. (significantly lower post-loading RTV in conical-hex) — conflicting fatigue findings.
- Shin et al., Gil et al., Piermatti et al., Tsuruta et al., Tsuge et al. — no superiority of any connection design (internal/external/conical) in fatigue behavior, consistent with this study.

## 7. Glossary

- **Preload:** the clamping force generated within the screw joint when the screw is tightened to a given torque; only ~10% of insertion torque converts to preload, ~90% overcomes friction.
- **Removal torque value (RTV / detorque):** torque required to loosen the screw, used as an indirect measure of residual preload.
- **Sedimentation / embedment relaxation effect:** loss of preload shortly after tightening as machined microroughness on mating surfaces flattens, reducing screw elongation.
- **External vs internal connection:** external (hexagon on implant platform, e.g. SK2/KL) vs internal (hexagon/octagon/Cone Morse inside the implant, e.g. Vega/Essential).
- **Microgap:** the gap at the implant–abutment interface (reported 10–135 µm), a site for bacterial colonization and peri-implant bone loss.
