## Why Ingested

기존 [[wiki/implants/surface/smeets-2016-implant-surface-modifications-osseointegration]] 은 sandblasted/acid-etched (SLA) 표면을 포함한 broad한 표면 수정 리뷰이지만, sandblasting 단독 vs machined 비교의 고품질 메타분석 근거가 부재했음. 본 SR+MA (Czumbel 2019)는 RCT만 포함한 첫 메타분석으로 임플란트 실패율(80–74% 감소)과 변연골 소실 비교를 정량화함.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 7 RCTs (n=722 implants) found sandblasted surfaces reduce implant failure by ~80% at 1 year and ~74% at 5 years vs machined surfaces, with no significant difference in marginal bone level loss.

## 한줄요약

7개 RCT를 포함한 SR+MA (총 722개 임플란트): 샌드블라스팅 표면이 머신드 표면 대비 1년 임플란트 실패율을 약 80%, 5년 실패율을 약 74% 낮추었으나, 변연골 소실에는 유의한 차이가 없었다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: PLOS ONE
- **Published**: May 3, 2019
- **PROSPERO**: CRD42018084190
- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis (RCTs only)
- **Funding**: Hungarian national innovation grants (GINOP, EFOP); no competing interests declared

## 2. Key Contributions

- First meta-analysis restricted exclusively to RCTs comparing sandblasted vs machined (turned) implant surfaces
- Quantified implant failure risk reduction: RR = 0.20 (1-year) and RR = 0.26 (5-year) — both statistically significant
- Demonstrated no statistically significant difference in marginal bone level (MBL) change at either time point
- Highlighted low heterogeneity (I² = 0.0%) for both primary failure outcomes — high internal consistency
- Called for larger RCTs to establish definitive clinical guidelines

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**PICO framework**:
- **Population**: Edentulous or partially edentulous, systemically healthy adults (no systemic disease, no heavy smoking, no excessive alcohol)
- **Intervention**: Endosteal dental implants with sandblasted surface modification
- **Control**: Machined (turned) dental implants — no surface modification
- **Outcomes**: Implant failure rate (dichotomous), marginal bone level change (continuous)

**Search**: Cochrane Central Library, Embase, PubMed — up to August 20, 2018

**Screening**: 130 records after deduplication → 7 RCTs met inclusion criteria

**Statistical methods**:
- Pooled Risk Ratio (RR) with 95% CI for implant failures (random effects, DerSimonian-Laird)
- Weighted Mean Difference (WMD) with 95% CI for MBL change
- Heterogeneity: I² and chi² tests
- Risk of bias: Cochrane Handbook 8-domain framework

**Excluded**: Non-RCT designs, bone augmentation cases, growth factor use, neck-only surface modifications, systemic disease patients

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Implant Failure Rate

| Timepoint | RR | 95% CI | I² | p (heterogeneity) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 year | 0.20 | 0.06–0.67 | 0.0% | 0.986 |
| 5 years | 0.26 | 0.09–0.74 | 0.0% | 0.968 |

Interpretation: 80% lower failure risk at 1 year and 74% lower at 5 years for sandblasted vs machined implants.

### Marginal Bone Level Change

| Timepoint | WMD | 95% CI | I² | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 year | −0.10 mm | −0.20, 0.01 | 0.0% | >0.05 |
| 5 years | −0.01 mm | −0.12, 0.09 | 26.2% | >0.05 |

No statistically significant difference in MBL loss between surface types at either timepoint.

### Study sample
- 7 RCTs included; 362 sandblasted implants vs 360 machined implants
- All risk-of-bias assessments performed per Cochrane 8-domain protocol

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 7 RCTs met strict inclusion criteria — small evidence base
- Limited sample sizes within individual trials
- No subgroup analyses for implant type, loading protocol, or anatomical site
- MBL I² at 5 years (26.2%) suggests some methodological heterogeneity — not addressed in subgroup analysis
- Authors call for large, well-designed RCTs with standardized surface characterization
- Sandblasting parameters (particle type, size 25–250 µm, velocity) vary between manufacturers — not stratified in analysis

## 7. Glossary

- **Sandblasting**: abrasive blasting of implant surface with ceramic particles (Al₂O₃, TiO₂, SiO₂) producing Sa 1.2–2.2 µm (moderately rough to rough category)
- **Machined/turned surface**: lathe-machined Ti surface with shallow grooves, Sa 0.5–1 µm (minimally rough)
- **Sa**: average surface roughness (µm); classification: smooth <0.5, minimally rough 0.5–1, moderately rough 1–2, rough >2 µm
- **RR (Risk Ratio)**: ratio of failure probability in sandblasted vs machined group; RR <1 favors sandblasted
- **WMD**: weighted mean difference in MBL (mm); negative = more bone loss in sandblasted group
- **I²**: proportion of total variation attributable to heterogeneity; 0% = no heterogeneity detected
