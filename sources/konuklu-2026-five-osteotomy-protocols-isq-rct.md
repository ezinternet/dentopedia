## One-line Summary
RCT (n=100, 5 osteotomy protocols × 3-month ISQ trajectory): moderate-speed drilling 300rpm achieves best long-term ISQ; high-speed 800rpm yields highest initial IT in dense bone; osseodensification shows transient mid-healing gains; bone condensation shows progressive ISQ decline.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Clinical Implant Dentistry and Related Research 2026;28:e70125
- DOI: 10.1111/cid.70125
- Published: 10 February 2026
- Design: RCT, parallel 5-group (n=20/group), identical macrodesign implants
- n = 100 healthy adults; exclusion: IT<25 Ncm at placement (4 additional implants excluded from longitudinal analysis)
- Based on PhD thesis of first author (supervisor: Küçükkurt)

## 2. Key Contributions
- First RCT comparing all five major osteotomy protocols head-to-head on ISQ trajectory
- Moderate-speed (300 rpm) = best ISQ at T4 (3 months, Bonferroni-adjusted p<0.05)
- High-speed (800 rpm) = highest initial IT, especially in dense bone; but not best long-term ISQ
- Osseodensification = transient mid-healing ISQ gains (not sustained to T4)
- Bone condensation = progressive ISQ decline over 3 months
- Immediate postoperative VAS-anxiety: osseodensification = highest residual anxiety; bone condensation = greatest anxiety reduction
- All groups: mean IT >30 Ncm, baseline ISQ >80 (healthy patient selection)

## 3. Methodology
5 parallel groups (n=20 each): (1) high-speed drilling 800rpm, (2) moderate-speed 300rpm, (3) ultra-low-speed 50rpm, (4) osseodensification, (5) bone condensation. Single identical macrodesign implant per patient. IT recorded at placement. ISQ at T0 (placement), T1 (1 week), T2 (1 month), T3 (2 months), T4 (3 months). VAS-anxiety immediately postoperative. Primary endpoint: ISQ change T0→T4. Multiplicity: Bonferroni-adjusted pairwise comparisons.

## 4. Key Results
| Protocol | Initial IT | Baseline ISQ | ISQ trend T0→T4 | Residual Anxiety |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 800 rpm (high-speed) | Highest (dense bone) | >80 | Mid-range | Moderate |
| 300 rpm (moderate) | High | >80 | ↑ Best at T4 | Moderate |
| 50 rpm (ultra-low) | Moderate | >80 | Stable | Low |
| Osseodensification | Moderate | >80 | Transient mid-healing gain, not sustained | Highest |
| Bone condensation | Moderate | >80 | Progressive ↓ | Lowest |

## 5. Limitations
- Single implant macrodesign (results may not generalize across designs)
- n=20 per group; powered for ISQ primary endpoint but may miss adverse effects
- Bone density distribution across groups not fully reported
- Postoperative anxiety (VAS) as secondary outcome — novel but not validated in implant literature
