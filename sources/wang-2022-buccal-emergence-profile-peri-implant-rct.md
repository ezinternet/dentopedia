## Why Ingested

Emergence-profile design is the core "how to shape the abutment/restoration" lever for protecting the peri-implant buccal mucosal margin — directly informing the healing-abutment / emergence-profile batch. This RCT supplies the quantitative threshold (emergence angle ~32° vs 40°; W/H-ratio-driven design) that complements the sibling convex-vs-concave evidence in [[wiki/implants/siegenthaler-2022-convex-emergence-profile-mucosal-recession-rct]] and the customized-abutment workflow rationale in [[wiki/implants/ruhstorfer-2024-customized-vs-conventional-healing-abutments-sr]].

## One-line Summary

RCT (n=21, single mandibular molars, supracrestal mucosa ≥2 mm): a buccal emergence profile designed from the mucosal width/height (W/H) ratio (test) cut 12-month gingival recession to 0.13 ± 0.32 mm vs 0.63 ± 0.38 mm for the healing-abutment-derived profile (control, p=0.006), with a lower initial emergence angle (31.4° vs 40.0°, p=0.025).

## 한줄요약

무작위대조시험 (RCT, n=21, 하악 단일 대구치, 치조정상부 점막 두께 ≥2 mm): 협측 점막 폭/높이비 (Width/Height ratio, W/H)를 기준으로 설계한 출현윤곽 (Emergence Profile) 시험군은 12개월 치은 퇴축이 0.13 ± 0.32 mm로, 치유 지대주 윤곽을 유지한 대조군 0.63 ± 0.38 mm보다 유의하게 적었고 (p=0.006), 초기 출현 각도 (Emergence Angle)도 더 낮았다 (31.4° 대 40.0°, p=0.025).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Tests the hypothesis that the prosthetic buccal **emergence profile** is a modifiable factor in postsurgical mucosal recession etiology, and that a correctly designed restorative profile can reduce gingival margin recession.
- Introduces a **W/H-ratio-based emergence profile design** at molar sites and compares it head-to-head against simply maintaining the original emergence profile of the healing abutment.
- Provides a candidate **emergence-angle threshold** (~32° behaving better than 40°) for preserving the buccal gingival margin when supracrestal soft tissue is ≥2 mm.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design:** Parallel-group RCT.
- **Participants:** 21 patients each requiring a single mandibular molar replacement, with supracrestal mucosal thickness ≥2 mm.
- **Allocation:** Random assignment to either a prosthesis buccal emergence profile designed from the buccal mucosal W/H ratio (**Test Group**) or one maintaining the original emergence profile of the healing abutment (**Control Group**).
- **Time points:** T0 (before delivery of the definitive restoration), T1 (prosthesis placement), T2 (1 month after loading), T3 (12 months after loading).
- **Outcomes measured:** gingival margin level change (△GM), initial emergence angle, buccal mucosal W/H ratio, marginal bone loss (MBL), implant failure, and complications.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Gingival recession (△GM) at T3:** test 0.13 ± 0.32 mm vs control 0.63 ± 0.38 mm; significantly lower in test (p = 0.006).
- **Initial emergence angle:** test 31.4 ± 7.22° vs control 40.0 ± 7.60°; significantly lower in test (p = 0.025).
- **Buccal mucosal W/H ratio:** in the test group, higher at T2 than at T0 but stable thereafter; in the control group, a continued rising trend.
- **Conclusion (abstract):** with initial supracrestal soft tissue thickness ≥2 mm, a restorative emergence profile based on the W/H ratio significantly reduced gingival margin recession; an emergence angle of ~32.4° behaved better than 40° in maintaining the gingival margin.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Abstract-only ingestion** — full-text methods, exact MBL/failure/complication values, blinding, and statistical detail were not retrieved.
- Small sample (n = 21) and a single, specific anatomy (mandibular molars with supracrestal mucosa ≥2 mm) limit generalizability to anterior/esthetic zones, thin tissue biotypes, or maxillary sites.
- 12-month follow-up only; longer-term stability of the W/H-ratio-driven profile is unknown.
- The ~32° vs 40° angle threshold is a within-trial observation, not an externally validated cutoff.

## 7. Glossary

- **Emergence profile** — the contour of the implant restoration as it transitions from the implant platform through the soft tissue to the clinical crown.
- **Emergence angle** — the angle of the restoration's subgingival contour relative to the long axis; steeper angles are associated with worse peri-implant soft/hard-tissue outcomes.
- **W/H ratio (width/height ratio)** — buccal mucosal width-to-height ratio used here to drive the emergence profile design.
- **△GM (gingival margin level change)** — change in the buccal gingival margin position over time; positive change indicates recession.
- **MBL (marginal bone loss)** — peri-implant crestal bone level change.
- **Supracrestal soft tissue thickness** — vertical mucosal thickness above the bone crest (here ≥2 mm at inclusion).
