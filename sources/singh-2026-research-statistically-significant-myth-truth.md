## One-line Summary
Editorial (J Conserv Dent Endod) arguing that statistical significance does not equal clinical significance, and a non-significant result may still be clinically meaningful given small samples, low power, or high variability.

## 한줄요약
Editorial: 통계적 유의성은 임상적 의의와 같지 않으며, 표본 부족·검정력 부족·변동성으로 비유의 결과도 임상적으로 의미 있을 수 있음을 논함.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Plain-language reminder distinguishing statistical from clinical significance for clinicians.
- Notes p-value pitfalls: sample size, power, variability.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Editorial/commentary (no primary data).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- A statistically significant result is not necessarily clinically meaningful, and vice versa; clinicians should interpret beyond the p-value (effect size, CIs, clinical relevance).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Opinion piece; no systematic evidence.

## 7. Glossary
- Statistical significance: probability the observed result is not due to chance (p-value threshold).
- Clinical significance: magnitude of effect meaningful to patient care.
