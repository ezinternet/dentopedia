## Why Ingested

[[wiki/resin-bonding/mortazavi-2012-ethanol-wet-bonding-clinical-trial]] tested ethanol wet-bonding (EWB) as the durability-enhancing solvent strategy. This 2023 in vitro study refines that picture by introducing acetone as an alternative "water-chaser" solvent and showing acetone preserves bond strength after aging without harming immediate strength — qualifying the ethanol-centric assumption and narrowing when each solvent helps.

## One-line Summary

In vitro microtensile study (60 third molars, 4 solvent groups): acetone wet-bonding preserved bond strength after thermocycling/collagenase aging (p<0.05) without affecting immediate strength, with less nanoleakage and suppressed MMP collagenolytic activity vs water wet-bonding.

## 한줄요약

60개 제3대구치 미세인장 실험(4개 용매군): 아세톤 습윤접착이 즉시 접착강도는 유지한 채 열순환·콜라겐분해효소 노화 후 강도를 보존(p<0.05), 물 습윤접착 대비 나노누출 감소 및 MMP 콜라겐분해활성 억제.

## 1. Document Information

- Type: In vitro laboratory study (microtensile bond strength + mechanism assays)
- Published: Front. Bioeng. Biotechnol. 2023;11:1309503 (7 Dec 2023)
- DOI: 10.3389/fbioe.2023.1309503
- Substrate: 60 caries-free human third molars, mid-coronal dentin
- Adhesive: 3M Singlebond Universal; 35% phosphoric acid etch

## 2. Key Contributions

- First study to use acetone as the sole preconditioning agent ("acetone wet-bonding", AWB) with a full durability panel (aged MTBS, nanoleakage, in situ zymography, contact angle).
- Demonstrates acetone's "water-chaser" mechanism improves dentin wettability and removes residual hybrid-layer water.
- Shows AWB suppresses endogenous MMP collagenolytic activity, addressing a primary hybrid-layer degradation pathway.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 4 groups (15 teeth each): water wet-bonding (WWB), ethanol wet-bonding (EWB), 50% acetone aqueous (50%AWB), 100% acetone (AWB).
- Etch 15 s with 35% H3PO4, rinse, blot, 60 s primer solution, Singlebond Universal, light-cure; composite buildup (Charisma).
- MTBS tested at 24 h, after 10,000 thermocycles (5/55 °C), and after 1-month collagenase aging; 12 beams/tooth.
- Additional: failure-mode analysis, interface nanoleakage, in situ zymography, contact angle.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Acetone pretreatment preserved MTBS after thermocycling or collagenase aging (p<0.05).
- No effect on immediate (24 h) MTBS (p>0.05) — acetone does not penalize initial strength.
- AWB group showed fewer nanoleakage than WWB group.
- Contact angle of dentin surfaces significantly decreased (improved wettability) in AWB.
- Collagenolytic (MMP) activity within the hybrid layer suppressed in AWB.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In vitro only — no clinical performance/survival data (the EWB anchor has clinical-trial evidence; this does not).
- Single universal adhesive tested; generalization to other systems unknown.
- Third molars only; thermocycling/collagenase aging approximate but do not replicate the oral environment.
- Authors note clinical significance is inferential ("would increase lifespan").

## 6. Related Work

- Mortazavi 2012 RCT — ethanol wet-bonding clinical-trial evidence.
- Ekambaram et al. — ethanol wet-bonding mechanism / hydrophobic adhesives.
- Breschi/Tjäderhane — hybrid-layer degradation and MMP-mediated collagen breakdown.

## 7. Glossary

- AWB (Acetone Wet-Bonding): preconditioning etched dentin with acetone to chase residual water before adhesive.
- MTBS: microtensile bond strength.
- Nanoleakage: silver-traceable infiltration at imperfectly sealed hybrid-layer porosities.
- In situ zymography: localized assay of endogenous gelatinolytic (MMP) activity.
- Water-chaser: solvent (acetone) that displaces residual water from demineralized collagen.
