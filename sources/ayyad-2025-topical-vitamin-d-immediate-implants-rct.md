## Why Ingested

This RCT is distinct from the serum-deficiency literature on vitamin D and implants: instead of asking whether *systemic* hypovitaminosis D predicts failure, it tests **local/topical vitamin D3 (calcitriol) gel applied directly to the implant surface and osteotomy** as a bioactive adjunct at immediate placement — RCT-level human evidence for a delivery route that prior work covered mostly in animal/in-vitro models. It complements the systemic-deficiency syntheses: [[implants/vitamin-d/miron-2025-vitamin-d-deficiency-early-implant-failure]] (which also reviewed vitamin D implant coatings as an open question) and the osseointegration umbrella review [[implants/vitamin-d/tallon-2024-vitamin-d-osseointegration-umbrella-review]]. The 6-month ISQ endpoint (80 vs 75) is anchored to the loading-threshold framing in [[implants/isq/andersson-2019-rfa-factors-5year-neoss-survival]].

## One-line Summary

Split-mouth-style two-arm RCT (n=24 immediate anterior-mandible implants, 12 control vs 12 + topical vitamin D3/calcitriol emulgel): the vitamin D arm showed significantly greater 6-month reductions in probing depth (1.17 vs 1.83 mm) and bleeding index (0.58 vs 1.42), lower day-7 pain (1.00 vs 2.50 VAS, 81.2% reduction), higher ISQ (80 vs 75), and higher bone density (+18.1% vs +6.4%).

## 1. Document Information

- **Authors**: Ayyad BM, Hamed MS, Elrody A, Hanna PA, Younis AA
- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health, 2025
- **DOI**: 10.1186/s12903-025-06465-6
- **PMID**: 40618145 | **PMCID**: PMC12228272
- **Trial registration**: ClinicalTrials.gov NCT06806423 (Jan 28, 2025)
- **Setting**: Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery Dept, Faculty of Dentistry, Suez Canal University, Egypt
- **Study type**: Two-arm parallel RCT (CONSORT, examiner-blinded)

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First RCT-level human test of *topical/local* calcitriol on immediate implants** with no graft confound — bone grafting was deliberately omitted at the jumping gap in both arms to isolate the vitamin D effect (prior positive studies, e.g. Amr 2019, combined vitamin D with bone grafts).
2. Demonstrates significant benefit across **four independent endpoints** — soft tissue (PD, BI), pain (VAS), stability (ISQ), and radiographic bone density (GSV) — converging on improved osseointegration.
3. Provides a **fully characterized calcitriol emulgel** (pH 7.28, viscosity 3912.7 cps, biphasic rapid-then-sustained in vitro release, drug content ~98%) as a reproducible delivery vehicle.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Population**: ASA I, 21–40 y, thick soft-tissue phenotype, KTW ≥2 mm; non-restorable single-rooted mandibular anterior teeth. Excluded smokers, bruxers, severe periodontitis, periapical infection at site.
- **Sample size**: G*Power (effect size 0.39, partial η²=0.13, power 80%, α=0.05) → 24 implants, 12/group.
- **Intervention**: Sterile vitamin D3 (calcitriol) emulgel applied to both implant surface and osteotomy site immediately before placement; ROOTT R-line implants (TRATE AG). Osteotomy 950–1100 rpm, 35 Ncm.
- **Outcomes & timing**: PD/BI at 3, 6 mo; VAS pain at days 1, 3, 7; ISQ (Osstell/RFA) at immediate, 3, 6 mo; bone density (GSV, IDRISI Kilimanjaro on standardized periapical radiographs, Rinn XCP parallel) at baseline, 3, 6 mo.
- **Analysis**: Independent Student's t-tests per time point (SAS JMP 17.2), 95% CI, p<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Time | Control | Vit D | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Probing depth (mm) | 6 mo | 1.83 ± 0.72 | 1.17 ± 0.72 | 0.033* |
| Bleeding index | 3 mo | 2.50 ± 0.52 | 1.75 ± 0.45 | 0.0011* |
| Bleeding index | 6 mo | 1.42 ± 0.51 | 0.58 ± 0.51 | 0.0007* |
| Pain (VAS) | Day 3 | 3.83 ± 0.94 | 2.58 ± 0.67 | 0.0012* |
| Pain (VAS) | Day 7 | 2.50 ± 0.67 | 1.00 ± 0.74 | <0.0001* |
| ISQ | 3 mo | 60.58 ± 2.27 | 64.25 ± 3.39 | 0.0057* |
| ISQ | 6 mo | 75.00 ± 2.13 | 80.00 ± 3.86 | 0.0011* |
| Bone density (GSV) | 6 mo | 121.01 ± 13.03 | 139.01 ± 13.76 | 0.0257* |
| Bone density Δ (0–6 mo) | overall | +6.44% | +18.07% | <0.0001* |

- Baseline ISQ (50.0 vs 53.9, p=0.11) and baseline bone density (114.3 vs 118.1, p=0.55) were not significantly different — groups well balanced.
- Bone density *dipped* in both arms during 0–3 mo (remodeling phase; Vit D dipped more, −7.27% vs −2.33%) then surged 3–6 mo (Vit D +27.60% vs +9.26%).
- All 24 implants survived 6 months without failure or major complication.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small n (24); single center; short follow-up (6 mo) — no long-term survival/MBL data.
- Restricted to **anterior mandible** (dense, uniform bone) — limits generalizability to posterior mandible/maxilla with poorer bone.
- Periapical GSV is a relative, technique-sensitive density proxy; CBCT avoided (ALARA) so 3D bone evaluation lacking.
- Marginal bone loss and keratinized mucosa width not measured — flagged for future work.
- Per-time-point t-tests rather than a mixed/repeated-measures model.

## 6. Related Work

- Amr (2019): topical vitamin D3 + xenograft → increased buccolingual ridge width and implant stability (graft confound).
- Heeba et al. (2019): topical vitamin D in diabetic implant patients → reduced crestal bone loss, improved PD/BI.
- Salomó-Coll et al. (2018): 10% vitamin D solution on implant surfaces in Foxhound dogs → ~9% BIC increase.
- Bozoğlan & Dundar (2021): no significant BIC difference in rats — species/model variability.
- Systemic-deficiency line: Miron 2025, Tallon 2024, Werny 2022, Buzatu 2024 (all in `implants/vitamin-d`).

## 7. Glossary

- **Calcitriol (1,25(OH)₂D₃)**: biologically active form of vitamin D3; binds VDR on osteoblasts/osteoclast precursors, regulates RANKL and osteogenic markers.
- **Emulgel**: emulsion-in-gel topical delivery vehicle; here used for sustained local calcitriol release.
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: 1–100 scale from resonance frequency analysis (Osstell) quantifying implant stability.
- **GSV (Gray Scale Value)**: grayscale brightness on radiographs used as a relative bone density proxy.
- **BIC (Bone-to-Implant Contact)**: histologic measure of direct bone apposition on the implant surface.
- **Jumping gap**: the residual space between an immediate implant and the socket walls.
