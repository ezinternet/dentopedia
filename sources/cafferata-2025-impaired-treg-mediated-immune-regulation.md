## Why Ingested

사용자 요청 — "구강내 염증 육아조직(inflammatory granulation tissue)" 논문의 PMC 전문 인제스트. 임플란트주위염(Peri-Implantitis, PI) 수술·발거 시 채취한 **육아조직(granulation tissue)**을 직접 분석해, 그 조직이 왜 만성 비해소성(non-resolving) 염증으로 남는지 host 면역(조절 T세포, Treg) 관점에서 설명한다. 같은 프랑크푸르트 Goethe 그룹의 대식세포 극성화 연구 [[wiki/implants/peri-implantitis/galarraga-vinueza-2020-macrophage-polarization-peri-implantitis-lesions]]가 "M1 우세 = 파괴적 염증"을 보였다면, 본 논문은 그 육아조직 안에서 Treg 조절축(FOXP3↑·NRP-1↓·TGF-β1↓·IL-35↑)이 어떻게 무너져 있는지를 보강한다. PI 표면제염·재건술이 기계적·항균 치료만으로 완전 해소되지 않는 [[wiki/implants/peri-implantitis/baima-2022-surface-decontamination-protocols-surgical-periimplantitis]]의 "단일 우월 프로토콜 부재" 문제에 host-side 근거를 더한다.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective histological/molecular study of human peri-implantitis granulation tissue (PI surgical n=23, explantation PI-X n=23, healthy n=20): PI lesions show FOXP3↑ and IL-35↑ but NRP-1↓ and TGF-β1↓ — Tregs are present but functionally impaired, and these alterations correlate with probing depth and vertical defect depth.

## 한줄요약

사람 임플란트주위염 육아조직의 후향적 조직·분자 연구(수술군 23·발거군 23·건강 대조 20): 병소에서 FOXP3↑·IL-35↑이지만 NRP-1↓·TGF-β1↓ — 조절 T세포(Treg)가 존재하되 기능적으로 손상돼 있고, 이 변화가 탐침깊이·수직결손깊이와 상관.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Periodontology 2025;52(12):1779-1790
- **Design**: Retrospective histological + molecular analysis of archived human peri-implant tissue and crevicular fluid
- **Setting**: Department of Oral Surgery and Implantology, Carolinum, Goethe University Frankfurt (2022-2024; ethics N:2022-652)
- **Groups**: PI (surgical therapy, n=23 implants), PI-X (explantation, n=23), H (healthy, second-stage surgery, n=20)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First characterization of the T regulatory cell (Treg) profile in human peri-implantitis granulation tissue, integrating H&E histology, RT-qPCR, immunohistochemistry (FOXP3, NRP-1), and crevicular-fluid ELISA (IL-10, TGF-β1, IL-35).
- Identifies a **dissociated / impaired Treg signature**: FOXP3 and IL-35 up, but NRP-1 and TGF-β1 down — i.e., a regulatory response that is present but non-functional.
- First report of **IL-35 and NRP-1** in human PI lesions and crevicular fluid.
- Links molecular markers to clinical severity (PD, BOP, VDD), proposing NRP-1/IL-35 as candidate risk-progression markers and Treg-restoration as an immunomodulatory therapeutic direction.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Tissue**: In PI/PI-X, granulation tissue curetted directly from the implant surface and intrabony defect; in H, punch biopsy of mucosa over the cover screw at second-stage surgery. Because PI granulation tissue is fragmented and lacks epithelial landmarks, histological orientation was not feasible.
- **Histology**: H&E of all groups.
- **Molecular**: RT-qPCR for FOXP3, HELIOS, NRP1 (Treg transcription factors) and IL10, TGFβ1, IL35B (cytokines); 18S rRNA endogenous control. Immunohistochemistry for FOXP3 (% positive nuclei) and NRP-1 (% positive area).
- **Crevicular fluid**: paper-strip collection (30 s, mesial + distal), ELISA for IL-10, TGF-β1, IL-35.
- **Analysis**: Kruskal-Wallis / Mann-Whitney; Spearman correlation; multiple linear + logistic regression adjusted for age and smoking; n=20/group → 80% power (effect size 0.65).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Histology**: Healthy = intact epithelium, dense collagen, minimal immune cells. PI/PI-X = fragmented/disorganised collagen, abundant capillaries, dense lymphocyte + macrophage-like infiltrate. No PI vs PI-X morphological difference.
- **Transcription factors**: FOXP3 ↑ in PI (3.8-fold, p<0.001) and PI-X vs H; PI-X FOXP3 lower than PI (p=0.02). HELIOS ↑ in PI/PI-X (p<0.001). NRP1 ↓ in PI/PI-X (p=0.04).
- **Immunohistochemistry**: FOXP3+ cells ↑ in PI/PI-X vs H (p<0.001; PI vs PI-X ns, p=0.077). NRP-1+ area ↓ in PI/PI-X vs H (p<0.001); in health NRP-1 at epithelial membrane, in disease sparse cytoplasmic in macrophage-like cells.
- **Cytokine mRNA**: IL10 ns (p=0.052); TGFβ1 ns across groups (p=0.120); IL35 ↑ 2.3-fold in PI/PI-X (p=0.026).
- **Crevicular-fluid protein**: IL-10 unchanged (p=0.11); **TGF-β1 ↓** in PI/PI-X vs H (p<0.001); **IL-35 ↑** in PI/PI-X vs H (p=0.004; PI vs PI-X ns).
- **Clinical correlation**: FOXP3 ↔ PD (rho=0.59), BOP (OR 1.17), VDD (all p<0.05). NRP-1 inversely ↔ PD (rho=-0.61), BOP (OR 0.20), VDD (-0.40). TGF-β1 inversely ↔ PD (rho=-0.70). IL-35 directly ↔ PD (rho=0.53), BOP (OR 1.86). IL-10 no associations.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Retrospective, cross-sectional → no causality or progression directionality.
- No flow cytometry / single-cell → cannot resolve Treg subsets or true suppressive function.
- Modest sample size; inter-individual variability.
- Healthy controls harvested at second-stage surgery (unloaded, biologically earlier) — imperfect comparator; ethically unavoidable.
- Future: longitudinal/mechanistic studies; NRP-1 agonist and TGF-β1/IL-35 modulation as adjuncts to mechanical/antimicrobial PI therapy.

## 7. Glossary

- **Granulation tissue**: inflamed, vascular, fibroblast-rich connective tissue curetted from the peri-implant defect; here a chronic, non-resolving inflammatory tissue.
- **Treg (T regulatory cell)**: FOXP3+ CD25+ lymphocyte that suppresses inflammation and maintains immune homeostasis.
- **FOXP3 / HELIOS**: Treg lineage transcription factors.
- **NRP-1 (neuropilin-1)**: co-receptor stabilising Treg suppressive function; binds/activates latent TGF-β1.
- **IL-35 / TGF-β1 / IL-10**: Treg-associated immunoregulatory cytokines.
- **PD / BOP / VDD**: probing depth / bleeding on probing / vertical defect depth (clinical severity metrics).
- **PI-X**: peri-implantitis implants indicated for explantation (most severe / failing).
