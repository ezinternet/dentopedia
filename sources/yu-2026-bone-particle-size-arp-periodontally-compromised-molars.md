## Why Ingested

Existing ridge-preservation pages establish ARP efficacy and DBBM grafting in intact sockets, but say little about the posterior/molar, periodontally compromised scenario or how DBBM **particle size** (0.25–1 mm vs 1–2 mm) should be chosen. This retrospective cohort extends [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/lee-2020-compressive-force-dbbm-ridge-preservation-molar]] (molar DBBM ARP) and [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/arroteia-2025-alveolar-ridge-preservation-posterior-rct]] (posterior ARP) into the severe-periodontitis molar socket, and adds a particle-size decision factor not addressed by [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/majzoub-2019-influence-different-grafting-materials]].

## One-line Summary

Retrospective cohort (n=96 stage III/IV periodontitis molar sockets, 48 small- vs 48 large-particle DBBM ARP): large particle (1–2 mm) showed only a non-significant trend toward greater vertical central socket height (8.54 vs 7.76 mm, P=.077); ridge dimensions were maintained regardless of particle size, and socket integrity mattered more than particle size.

## 한줄요약

후향 코호트(중증 치주염 III/IV기 대구치 발치와 96개, 소입자 48 vs 대입자 48 DBBM 치조제보존술): 대입자(1–2 mm)가 중심 수직골높이에서 비유의한 증가 경향(8.54 vs 7.76 mm, P=.077)만 보였고, 입자 크기와 무관하게 치조제 치수는 유지됐으며 소켓 무결성이 입자 크기보다 더 중요했다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First study to evaluate radiographic ridge changes and implant placement feasibility after ARP in **periodontally compromised molar** extraction sockets, comparing two DBBM particle sizes.
- Provides clinical decision-making guidance on DBBM particle-size selection (0.25–1 vs 1–2 mm) for a poorly studied, high-prevalence patient group (severe periodontitis projected to affect 1.56 billion by 2050).
- Identifies **socket integrity** (number of walls with height ≥5 mm) and **jaw** as stronger determinants of central socket height than particle size.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Retrospective cohort, IRB-approved (SH9H-2025-T423-1), STROBE-adherent.
- **Patients**: 589 screened → 96 patients/96 molar sockets (37 F, 59 M; mean age 48.89 ± 10.66 y, range 25–73). 1:1 allocation by graft used: 48 small particle (SP, 0.25–1 mm) vs 48 large particle (LP, 1–2 mm).
- **Inclusion**: ≥18 y; stage III/IV periodontitis molar extraction; bone dehiscence on ≥1 wall over >50% root length; ≥1 adjacent tooth; CBCT within 3 mo of extraction (T0) and 4–8 mo post-ARP (T1).
- **Exclusion**: systemic disease; bone-metabolism drugs (bisphosphonates, immunosuppressants); chemo/radiotherapy in past 5 y; smoking; pregnancy/lactation; poor CBCT quality.
- **Procedure**: minimally traumatic flapless extraction, socket debridement, particulate DBBM (Bio-Oss 0.25–1 or 1–2 mm) + resorbable collagen membrane (Bio-Gide), 5-0 Vicryl sutures, secondary-intention healing.
- **Outcomes**: Linear (Romexis 6.4.7) — buccal/lingual plate thickness, horizontal width at 1/3/5 mm (HW1/HW3/HW5, Aimetti method), vertical buccal/lingual/mesial/distal height, primary outcome **vertical central socket height (VC)**. Volumetric (3D Slicer 5.7.0). Implant feasibility via 4-level LIPD criteria. Single calibrated examiner (ICC >0.9).
- **Stats**: G*Power (α=0.05, power 0.90, assumed VC diff 0.7 mm, ≥28/group); SPSS 30, MATLAB R2025A; t-test/Mann–Whitney, paired tests for T0→T1; multivariate linear regression on VC adjusting for age, gender, jaw, tooth position, healing duration, socket wall thickness, socket integrity, particle size.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **No significant intergroup differences** for any dimensional/volumetric variable. All VB, VL, HW1, HW3, HW5 decreased and volume increased significantly T0→T1 in both groups.
- **VC (primary outcome)**: LP 8.54 ± 2.34 mm vs SP 7.76 ± 1.93 mm — non-significant trend favoring LP (**P = .077**).
- **Volume gain**: LP 369.44 mm³ (23.78%) vs SP 239.13 mm³ (17.73%), non-significant.
- **Buccal > lingual resorption** in both groups (−1.90/−1.97 mm vs −0.97/−0.99 mm). Mean healing 6.4 months.
- **Implant feasibility (LIPD)**: Level I (standard placement, no augmentation) in 70/96 (73%); 26 sites needed augmentation (13 Level II, 13 Level III); slightly more Level III in SP (8/48) than LP (5/48); all Level III were maxillary requiring LSFE; no Level IV. Distribution similar between groups (P = .500).
- **Multivariate regression on VC** — significant: socket integrity (coeff 1.027/wall, SE 0.220, **P < .001**), jaw (coeff 1.077, SE 0.391, **P = .007**, favoring mandible: 8.844 vs 7.394 mm maxilla), particle size (coeff 0.788, SE 0.380, **P = .041**). Non-significant: age, gender, tooth position, healing duration, socket wall thickness.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Retrospective design — surgical procedures not calibrated, baseline characteristics not fully consistent.
- CBCT not taken immediately post-extraction; no soft-tissue, histomorphometric, or patient-reported outcomes.
- Limited sample size; large-sample RCTs warranted.
- Smokers excluded — applicability to smokers unknown.

## 6. Related Work

- Posterior/molar ARP: Arroteia 2025 (posterior RCT), Lee 2020 (compressive force DBBM molar ARP).
- DBBM grafting materials and particle-size effects at other sites: prior lateral sinus floor elevation and lateral ridge augmentation literature (LP improved augmentation stability after one-stage LSFE; no difference after LRA).
- Periodontally compromised socket healing — greater ridge-height reduction (~60%) vs intact (~35%).

## 7. Glossary

- **ARP** — Alveolar Ridge Preservation.
- **DBBM** — Deproteinized Bovine Bone Mineral (Bio-Oss).
- **SP / LP** — Small Particle (0.25–1 mm) / Large Particle (1–2 mm).
- **VC** — Vertical Central socket height (apex to most coronal grafted point at T1).
- **HW1/HW3/HW5** — Horizontal Width at 1/3/5 mm below crest.
- **LIPD** — Level of Implant Placement Difficulty (I–IV).
- **LSFE** — Lateral Sinus Floor Elevation.
- **Socket integrity** — number of socket walls with height ≥5 mm.
