## Why Ingested

A 2026 Chinese multi-center expert consensus covering the full clinical lifecycle of third molar management — from developmental classification through surgical technique, socket regeneration, severe-complication rescue, and functional utilization — represents the highest-level synthesis available on this topic. It directly complements and extends [[oral-surgery/damico-2025-third-molar-surgical-indications-guidelines]], which is limited to extraction-indication frameworks, by adding surgical protocol granularity, a modified Pell & Gregory Category D subclassification, coronectomy decision rules, and autotransplantation criteria. It also contextualizes [[oral-surgery/cervera-espert-2016-coronectomy-mandibular-third-molar-sr]] recommendations within a broader operative decision tree.

## One-line Summary

Chinese multi-center expert consensus (2026) covering the full clinical spectrum of third molar management: developmental trajectory, classification, extraction indications, preoperative preparation, surgical techniques (including coronectomy, root-removal-first, orthodontic traction, and digital navigation), socket graft materials, severe-complication rescue protocols, and functional utilization via autotransplantation or orthodontic mesialization.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Modified Pell & Gregory classification**: Introduces Category D for mandibular third molars whose crown lies below the apical one-third of the second molar or whose linear distance from the second molar exceeds the tooth length — a subclassification that improves prediction of extraction complexity beyond the existing A–C system.
2. **Archer classification for maxillary third molars**: Proposes four levels (A–D) based on crown position relative to the adjacent second molar's occlusal plane to cervical line to apical third, with added buccal/central/palatal buccopalatal sub-classification guiding approach selection.
3. **Tiered extraction-indication framework**: Differentiates definite pathologic indicators (recurrent pericoronitis, caries, periodontal damage, cyst/tumor, moderate/severe root resorption), therapeutic extraction needs (orthodontic, orthognathic, TMD, pre-radiotherapy/chemotherapy/immunotherapy, pre-pregnancy with deep involvement), and prophylactic removal of potentially risky but asymptomatic teeth.
4. **"Root Removal First" strategy**: For deeply embedded (level C/D) mesioangular or horizontal mandibular third molars with crown abutting the second molar root or roots near the inferior alveolar nerve — distal bone is removed, the tooth sectioned at 60° along the long axis, and the root elevated before the crown is retrieved distally, preserving second-molar periodontal health.
5. **Submaxillary sinus membrane space approach**: For level C/D maxillary impacted third molars with sinus protrusion — a 10×10 mm trapezoidal bone window above the second molar apex via ultrasonic osteotomy allows submucosal sinus space creation and tooth removal without sinus membrane perforation.
6. **Socket regeneration protocol**: Grades socket management by bleeding severity (mild / moderate / arterial) and preoperative bone defect status, recommending CGF alone, CGF + bone substitute, or bone lid technique based on clinical scenario.
7. **Functional utilization framework**: Establishes criteria for autotransplantation (root ≥3/4 complete, extracorporeal time ≤15 min, success rates ~95% in China) and orthodontic mesialization as alternatives to prosthetic replacement after adjacent molar loss.
8. **Severe-complication management protocols**: Provides stepwise guidance for inferior alveolar nerve injury, root displacement into the mandibular canal (<3 mm vs ≥3 mm), root/tooth displacement into lingual soft tissue space, tooth displacement into maxillary sinus, alveolar vascular hemorrhage, and dry socket.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

This document is an expert consensus produced by a Chinese multi-center panel and published in *International Journal of Oral Science* (Nature/Springer). It synthesizes available evidence and expert opinion across the following structured domains:

- Developmental trajectory and impaction characteristics (epidemiology in Chinese population: mandibular impaction rate 70.3% in 13,168 individuals aged 17–22; mesioangular predominant at 58.5%)
- Clinical and radiographic examination protocols (periapical, panoramic, CBCT; FBCT substitution rule)
- Classification systems (Winter, Pell & Gregory standard and modified, Archer for maxillary)
- Adverse effects of impacted third molars (pericoronitis 82.41% mandibular incidence, fascial space infections 50–70% attributable to third molars, osteomyelitis ~6% from chronic pericoronitis, distal second-molar caries ~19.5%, cyst/tumor ~5%)
- Extraction indications (3-tier framework)
- Preoperative preparation (imaging, laboratory, anticoagulant management, premedication)
- Anesthetic choices (lidocaine, articaine, mepivacaine; articaine infiltration for maxillary; inferior alveolar block for mandibular; nitrous oxide and general anesthesia indications)
- Surgical techniques (standard technique steps 1–8; germ extraction; root-removal-first; submaxillary sinus space approach; orthodontic traction; coronectomy; digital technology)
- Clinical decision trees for mandibular and maxillary third molars
- Socket management (hemostatic materials graded by bleeding severity; CGF; bone substitutes; bone lid technique)
- Postoperative follow-up and antibiotic use
- Management of severe complications (6 categories)
- Functional utilization (autotransplantation; orthodontic mesialization)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Domain | Key finding / recommendation |
|---|---|
| Impaction prevalence | 70.3% mandibular impaction in 13,168 Chinese individuals aged 17–22; mesioangular 58.5% |
| Sex difference | Female impaction prevalence 1.16–1.5× male |
| Pericoronitis incidence | 82.41% of pericoronitis is mandibular; level A > level B; class II > class I |
| Space infections | 50–70% of fascial space infections attributable to impacted third molars |
| Osteomyelitis | ~6% of chronic pericoronitis cases |
| Distal second-molar caries | ~19.5% of patients with impacted third molars |
| Cyst/tumor | ~5% of impacted third molar patients |
| Panoramic magnification error | 3%–10% — CBCT required for complex/high-risk cases |
| Coronectomy crown section | At or below pulp chamber floor, 3–5 mm below crestal bone margin |
| Root displacement threshold | <3 mm / <1/3 canal diameter → observe; ≥3 mm or ≥1/2 canal or neurological deficit → remove promptly |
| Autotransplantation success (China) | ~95% |
| Antibiotic duration | 3–5 days for complex extractions; not required for uncomplicated maxillary extractions |
| Bone substitute absorption target | Ideally absorbable within 3–6 months |
| PTFE sutures | Superior to silk for reducing bacterial biofilm (aerobic and anaerobic) |

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- No consensus reached on optimal extraction timing for asymptomatic impacted third molars or on the routine use of digital technology — the document explicitly acknowledges these as unresolved controversies.
- Special populations (pregnant women, elderly, systemic disease patients) are noted as requiring tailored plans beyond the decision-tree framework.
- The consensus reflects Chinese multi-center clinical practice and epidemiology; generalizability to other ethnic groups may differ.
- Evidence quality underlying specific recommendations is not uniformly graded (expert consensus, not systematic-review-based guidelines).

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Pericoronitis | Inflammatory response to soft tissue surrounding a partially erupted or impacted third molar |
| Winter classification | Axial position classification (vertical, mesioangular, horizontal, distoangular, lingual/palatal, buccal) |
| Pell & Gregory classification | Two-dimensional depth (A/B/C) and ramus relationship (I/II/III) classification |
| Category D (Sun & Zhao) | Modified P&G subclass: third molar below apical one-third of second molar or linear distance > tooth length |
| Archer classification | 4-level depth classification for maxillary impacted third molars (A–D) |
| Coronectomy | Deliberate retention of roots to avoid inferior alveolar nerve injury; crown removed to ≥3 mm below crestal bone |
| Root Removal First strategy | Surgical approach for deep horizontal/mesioangular impactions: root elevated before crown removed distally |
| CGF | Concentrated Growth Factor — third-generation platelet concentrate for socket regeneration |
| Bone lid technique | Bone window harvested during osteotomy, preserved in saline, reimplanted after tooth extraction |
| PTFE sutures | Polytetrafluoroethylene sutures — superior to silk for reducing biofilm in oral surgery wounds |
| Autotransplantation | Transfer of a tooth from one socket to another within the same individual; third molars are common donors |
| Orthodontic mesialization | Orthodontic forward movement of impacted/functional third molar to replace extracted first/second molar |
