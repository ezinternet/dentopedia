## Why Ingested

Primary prospective evidence with a striking dose-response — early implant failure jumps to 46.2% at serum 25(OH)D <10 ng/mL versus 2.3% (10–30) and 4.5% (>30) — measured alongside RFA/ISQ stability, CBCT bone density, and bone-biopsy histomorphometry. It supplies the patient-level signal that the SRs we hold synthesize, supporting their conclusion that vitamin D deficiency (VDD) raises early implant failure (EDIF) risk. Reinforces [[implants/vitamin-d/miron-2025-vitamin-d-deficiency-early-implant-failure]] and [[implants/vitamin-d/buzatu-2024-vitamin-d-osseointegration-human-studies-sr]]; anchors the RFA/ISQ stability link to [[implants/isq/andersson-2019-rfa-factors-5year-neoss-survival]].

## One-line Summary

Prospective study (143 implant sites / 53 patients, RFA-measured ISQ): overall early implant failure 7%, but 46.2% at serum vitamin D <10 ng/mL vs 4.5% (>30) and 2.3% (10–30 ng/mL); deficiency also associated with lower CBCT bone density and ISQ and higher immature/mature collagen ratio.

## 한줄요약

전향 연구(임플란트 143부위/환자 53명, 공명주파수분석 ISQ): 전체 조기 임플란트 실패 7%이나 혈청 비타민 D <10 ng/mL군에서 46.2%로 급증(>30: 4.5%, 10–30: 2.3%), 결핍군은 CBCT 골밀도·ISQ 낮고 미성숙/성숙 콜라겐 비율 높음.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies a steep, statistically significant dose-response between serum 25(OH)D category and early dental implant failure (EDIF): 46.2% in the deficient (<10 ng/mL) group vs 2.3% (insufficient, 10–30) and 4.5% (sufficient, >30); p=0.0007.
- Links VDD mechanistically to implant outcomes via three measured intermediates in the same patients: lower CBCT bone density, lower RFA/ISQ stability (both placement and 12 weeks), and elevated immature/mature collagen ratio on Masson-trichrome bone biopsies.
- Shows no significant association of EDIF with age, gender, smoking, or diabetes (HbA1c <6.5) in this cohort — isolating VDD as the dominant risk signal.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 53 adults, 143 implant sites, single implant system (controls for surface/design); ≥3 months post-extraction, adequate oral hygiene.
- Exclusions: ridge augmentation, sinus lift, immediate placement, bisphosphonates, head/neck irradiation, infection, pregnancy, immunocompromise, uncontrolled diabetes.
- Serum 25(OH)D by enzyme immunoassay (EIA) pre-op; categorized sufficient >30, insufficient 10–30, deficient <10 ng/mL.
- CBCT bone density as grey-scale values at 1 mm around virtual implant (PLANMECA verification tab).
- Trephine bone biopsy (1.7 mm) at implant site, Masson trichrome, histomorphometry of immature/mature collagen (IM/M).
- Primary stability (T1) and secondary stability (T2, 12 weeks) by Osstell resonance frequency analysis (ISQ).
- EDIF (≤12 weeks) defined by ≥1 of: loss of osseointegration/mobility, peri-implant infection, bleeding pocket >6 mm, or MBL >2.5 mm.
- Stats: independent t-test, one-way ANOVA (post-hoc Tamhane's T2/Bonferroni), Chi-square/Fisher's exact.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Overall EDIF**: 10/143 = 6.9% (~7%).
- **EDIF by vitamin D group**: deficient 6/13 (46.2%), insufficient 2/86 (2.3%), sufficient 2/44 (4.5%); p=0.0007. Deficient vs each other group p<0.001; sufficient vs insufficient not significant.
- **Bone density (CBCT GS)**: sufficient 660.3, insufficient 643.22, deficient 370.66; p=0.003 (deficient significantly lower).
- **ISQ T1 (placement)**: 77.52 / 75.33 / 71.54; p≈0.000003.
- **ISQ T2 (12 weeks)**: 78.02 / 76.09 / 73.50; p≈0.000002.
- **IM/M ratio** (mandible): 0.68% / 6.39% / 30.1%; (maxilla): 2.36% / 17.92% / 48.7%; both p<0.001 — far more immature collagen with deficiency.
- **No association** with age (p=0.968), gender (p=0.955), smoking (p=0.131; notably 0 failures among smokers), or diabetes (p=0.051).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Observational design, single center, modest deficient subgroup (n=13 implants / 6 patients) — 46.2% rests on 6 failures, so the CI is wide despite significance.
- Short 12-week horizon (early failure only); no late-failure or long-term survival data.
- Smokers limited to <10 cigarettes/day and small n (25 implants), so the null smoking finding is underpowered.
- Authors explicitly call for a better-designed randomized controlled trial on serum vitamin D and EDIF.

## 6. Related Work

- Mangano et al. (822 and 885 patients): no significant overall VDD–failure relationship, yet a rise in EDIF with falling vitamin D.
- Fretwurst et al.: vitamin D treatment improved implant success in VDD patients with prior failure.
- Nastri (2020) scoping review: strong correlation between VDD, poor osseointegration, and EDIF; supplementation warranted.
- Bone histomorphometry of osteoid increase in VDD (Bhan et al.; Priemel et al.; Parfitt on osteomalacia).
- CBCT bone-density vs osteoporosis (Sghaireen & Al Habib; Barngkgei et al.).

## 7. Glossary

- **EDIF** — Early Dental Implant Failure (within ~12 weeks, before functional loading).
- **VDD** — Vitamin D Deficiency (serum 25(OH)D <10 ng/mL by this study's cut-off).
- **ISQ** — Implant Stability Quotient, output of resonance frequency analysis (RFA).
- **RFA** — Resonance Frequency Analysis (Osstell device).
- **IM/M** — Immature collagen per Mature collagen ratio (histomorphometric marker of unmineralized osteoid).
- **CBCT GS** — Cone-Beam CT grey-scale value, used here as a bone-density proxy.
