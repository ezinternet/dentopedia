## Why Ingested

EFP S3 가이드라인([[periodontics/sanz-2020-treatment-stage-i-iii-periodontitis-efp]])이 항생제 보조요법을 일상적 사용으로 권장하지 않는 근거의 핵심 1차 자료로, 본 Cochrane SR+MA(45 RCT)는 SRP에 전신 항생제를 더해도 효과가 매우 낮은 확실성·임상적으로 미미함을 보여 항생제 스튜어드십 의사결정을 뒷받침한다. 임플란트 주위염 항생제 근거([[periodontics/grusovin-2022-antibiotics-non-surgical-peri-implantitis-ma]])와 짝을 이뤄 비외과적 항생제 보조요법 전반의 약한 근거 기반을 대비한다.

## One-line Summary

Cochrane SR+MA of 45 RCTs: adjunctive systemic antimicrobials (most studied: amoxicillin+metronidazole) added to SRP show only very-low-certainty, mostly clinically marginal benefits on CAL/PD/closed pockets/BOP for non-surgical periodontitis treatment, with no reliable evidence one antibiotic is superior.

## 한줄요약

Cochrane 체계적고찰+메타분석(RCT 45편): SRP에 전신 항생제(가장 많이 연구된 조합은 아목시실린+메트로니다졸)를 추가해도 비외과적 치주염 치료에서 CAL·PD·closed pocket·BOP 개선 효과는 모두 매우 낮은 확실성이며 임상적으로 미미하고, 특정 항생제가 더 우수하다는 신뢰할 만한 근거도 없음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews — Intervention Review (new, Issue 11, 2020)
- **Citation**: Khattri S, Kumbargere Nagraj S, Arora A, Eachempati P, Kusum CK, Bhat KG, Johnson TM, Lodi G. *Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews* 2020, Issue 11. Art. No.: CD012568.
- **DOI**: 10.1002/14651858.CD012568.pub2
- **Editorial group**: Cochrane Oral Health Group
- **Search date**: databases searched to 9 March 2020 (Cochrane Oral Health Trials Register, CENTRAL, MEDLINE, Embase; ClinicalTrials.gov and WHO ICTRP for ongoing trials)
- **Note**: The review follows the previous (pre-2017) classification of periodontitis (chronic / aggressive), because all included studies used this classification.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First comprehensive Cochrane review of **systemic** antimicrobials as an adjunct to scaling and root planing (SRP) across the full range of antibiotic agents, dosages, and combinations.
- Synthesizes **45 RCTs** comparing SRP + systemic antibiotic versus SRP alone/placebo, or one antibiotic versus another.
- Applies **GRADE** to grade certainty of evidence per outcome; consistently rates evidence as **very low certainty**.
- Provides a benchmark "minimally important clinical difference (MICD)" lens, flagging that most statistically significant effects do not reach clinical importance.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Systematic review with meta-analysis (random-effects mean differences).
- **Population**: Individuals with clinically diagnosed, previously untreated periodontitis (chronic or aggressive per old classification).
- **Intervention/comparator**: SRP + systemic antibiotic vs SRP alone/placebo, or vs another systemic antibiotic.
- **Included studies**: 45 RCTs (14 had multiple treatment arms; 7 trials did not contribute data to any meta-analysis). 58 studies excluded with reasons (e.g., 19 used adjunctive *local* antibiotics, not systemic).
- **Outcomes**: percentage of closed pockets, clinical attachment level (CAL), probing pocket depth (PD), and percentage of bleeding on probing (BOP), assessed at short-term (≤3 mo), intermediate-term (>3 mo), and long-term (≥1 year).
- **Analysis**: mean differences (MD) with 95% CI for continuous data; risk of bias assessed in duplicate across seven domains; certainty graded with GRADE.
- **Risk of bias**: most included trials at unclear or high risk of bias; ~23 trials at unclear, ~21 trials unclear for selection/detection domains — a major driver of downgrading.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

All effect estimates were of **very low certainty**. Selected long-term (≥1 yr) comparisons:

- **Amoxicillin + metronidazole + SRP vs SRP** (chronic/aggressive): closed pockets MD −16.20% (95% CI −25.87 to −6.53; 1 study, 44 pts); CAL MD −0.47 mm (−0.90 to −0.05; 2 studies, 389 pts); PD MD −0.30 mm (−0.42 to −0.18; 2 studies, 389 pts); BOP MD −8.06% (−14.26 to −1.85; 2 studies, 389 pts). Only **closed pockets and BOP** reached an MICD favouring the antibiotic arm.
- **Metronidazole + SRP vs SRP**: closed pockets MD −12.20% (−29.23 to 4.83; 22 pts); CAL MD −1.12 mm (−2.24 to 0; 71 pts); PD MD −1.11 mm (−2.84 to 0.61; 47 pts). Only **CAL and PD** reached an MICD.
- **Azithromycin + SRP vs SRP**: no evidence of difference in closed pockets, CAL, PD, or BOP (very low certainty).
- **Amoxicillin + clavulanate + SRP vs SRP**: no difference (1 study, 21 pts).
- **Doxycycline + SRP vs SRP** (aggressive): CAL MD −0.80 mm, PD MD −1.00 mm; MICD reached for PD only (22 pts).
- **Tetracycline + SRP vs SRP** (aggressive): CAL MD −2.30 mm (−2.50 to −2.10; 26 pts).
- **Clindamycin + SRP vs SRP** (aggressive): CAL MD −1.70 mm, PD MD −1.80 mm favouring clindamycin (21 pts).
- **Head-to-head**: doxycycline vs metronidazole (favoured metronidazole, 27 pts); clindamycin vs metronidazole (no difference, 26 pts); clindamycin vs doxycycline (no difference, 23 pts).
- **Adverse events**: nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea, mild GI disturbance, metallic taste reported; **no serious adverse events** in any trial.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Evidence is uniformly **very low certainty** — high/unclear risk of bias, imprecision (small single-study comparisons), and inconsistency (high I² in several pooled estimates) limit confidence.
- Most clinically meaningful comparisons rest on **single small trials**, preventing firm conclusions on long-term benefit.
- No reliable evidence to decide whether any antibiotic is superior to another alongside SRP.
- Uses the superseded pre-2017 chronic/aggressive classification, limiting direct mapping to the 2018 staging/grading framework.
- Authors call for **well-planned RCTs** that pre-define the MICD for closed pockets, CAL, PD, and BOP.

## 6. Related Work

- EFP S3-level clinical practice guideline (Sanz 2020) for stage I–III periodontitis — informs when adjunctive systemic antibiotics are (not) recommended.
- Cochrane/meta-analytic evidence on antibiotics for non-surgical peri-implantitis (Grusovin 2022) — parallel weak evidence base for a different antibiotic-adjunct setting.
- Antimicrobial stewardship literature cautioning against routine antibiotic use in periodontal therapy.

## 7. Glossary

- **SRP** — Scaling and Root Planing; mechanical debridement, the standard non-surgical periodontal treatment.
- **CAL** — Clinical Attachment Level; gain (mm) reflects periodontal attachment improvement.
- **PD / PPD** — (Probing) Pocket Depth, mm.
- **BOP** — Bleeding On Probing; inflammation indicator (%).
- **Closed pockets** — pockets reduced below a threshold depth (treatment success metric).
- **MICD** — Minimally Important Clinical Difference; threshold below which a statistically significant effect is not clinically meaningful.
- **GRADE** — system for rating certainty of a body of evidence (high → very low).
- **AMOX / MTZ / AZT** — amoxicillin / metronidazole / azithromycin.
- **MD** — Mean Difference (effect measure for continuous outcomes).
