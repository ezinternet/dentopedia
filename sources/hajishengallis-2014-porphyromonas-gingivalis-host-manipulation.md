## Why Ingested
Provides the molecular mechanistic depth underpinning the keystone-pathogen concept introduced in [[oral-microbiology/hajishengallis-2012-psd-model-periodontal-disease]], detailing how P. gingivalis serially subverts epithelial cells, complement, neutrophil killing, and adaptive immunity to orchestrate community-wide dysbiosis.

## One-line Summary
Narrative review mechanistically dissecting how P. gingivalis manipulates host immunity — via FimA/SerB invasion of gingival epithelial cells, C5aR–TLR2 crosstalk suppressing iNOS killing and IL-12/IFN-γ immunity, gingipain-mediated complement evasion, and Mfa1–SspA/B interspecies signaling — to function as a keystone pathogen enabling periodontal dysbiosis.

## 한줄요약
Narrative review: P. gingivalis의 분자적 면역 조작 기전 총정리 — FimA 침투, SerB의 NF-κB p65 탈인산화, C5aR-TLR2 crosstalk으로 iNOS·IL-12 억제, gingipain의 C3 분해로 보체 회피, Mfa1–SspA/B 종간 시그널링으로 keystone pathogen 역할 수행.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Detailed the FimA fimbria–β1 integrin invasion pathway and SerB-mediated cofilin dephosphorylation enabling P. gingivalis GEC entry.
- Identified C5aR–TLR2 crosstalk as a central subversion axis: cAMP-PKA pathway inhibits GSK3β and iNOS-dependent bacterial killing in macrophages.
- Showed P. gingivalis generates its own C5a locally via gingipain C5-convertase-like activity — an independent complement-activation bypass.
- Demonstrated gingipains degrade C3, blocking all three complement initiation pathways (classical, lectin, alternative), preventing C3b opsonization and membrane attack complex formation.
- Established that P. gingivalis regulates ~30% of its expressed proteome according to community/planktonic/epithelial cell context.
- Clarified that complement inactivation by P. gingivalis may protect otherwise complement-susceptible bystander organisms (community-wide benefit).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative review integrating in-vitro cell biology, mouse oral gavage model, and select human data.
- Key mechanistic studies cited: mouse C5aR-KO model; SerB-deficient P. gingivalis mutant; CR3-KO macrophage intracellular persistence assays; miRNA profiling in GEC.
- P. gingivalis proteome regulation: ~30% of expressed proteome differentially regulated in community vs. planktonic vs. epithelial cell conditions.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Invasion: FimA fimbriae bind β1-integrin on GECs; SerB serine phosphatase enters host cells, dephosphorylates cofilin (S536 on NF-κBp65 also dephosphorylated), enabling actin remodeling and cell entry.
- Anti-apoptosis: P. gingivalis activates JAK-Stat, PI3K-Akt; Ndk scavenges ATP to block P2X7-dependent apoptosis.
- miRNA reprogramming: miR-105 suppresses TLR2; miR-203 inhibits SOCS3/SOCS6 in GECs.
- Chemokine paralysis: SerB dephosphorylates NF-κBp65-S536 → prevents CXCL8 (IL-8) transcription; also inhibits CXCL9/10/11 via IRF-1/Stat1 downregulation — reduces Th1 immunity.
- SerB-deficient mutant: induces enhanced neutrophil recruitment and is less virulent (less bone loss) than WT.
- C5aR–TLR2 crosstalk in macrophages: synergistic cAMP-PKA activation → GSK3β inhibition → iNOS suppression (impairs intracellular killing); downregulates IL-12p35/p40 (via ERK1/2-IRF-1) → inhibits IFN-γ and cell-mediated immunity.
- Upregulates bone-resorptive cytokines (TNF, IL-1β, IL-6, IL-17A) while suppressing IL-12.
- Complement evasion: gingipains degrade C3 (all three initiation pathways); HRgpA/RgpB generate C5a (C5-convertase-like) locally activating C5aR; HRgpA captures C4b-binding protein to inhibit classical/lectin pathways; C5b degraded by gingipains to prevent MAC formation.
- CR3 transactivation: TLR2 → RAC1/PI3K/cytohesin-1 → CR3 high-affinity conformation; P. gingivalis enters via CR3 safely (CR3 not linked to strong microbicidal mechanisms); intracellular persistence longer in WT vs. CR3-KO macrophages.
- Interspecies: Mfa1–SspA/B (with S. gordonii) initiates community development; antagonistic S. cristatus arginine deiminase represses FimA fimbriae, negatively correlated in subgingival biofilm.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Mechanistic data predominantly from mouse models and in-vitro systems; clinical translation incomplete.
- Precise mechanisms by which P. gingivalis resists neutrophil non-oxidative killing remain unknown.
- Role of adaptive immunity (CD4+ T-cell subsets) in long-term dysbiosis needs clarification.
- Authors note therapeutic potential of C5aR antagonists (local periodontal application shown effective in mice).

## 7. Glossary
- SerB: P. gingivalis serine phosphatase; dephosphorylates NF-κBp65-S536 (CXCL8 suppression) and cofilin (facilitates invasion).
- Gingipains: cysteine proteases (HRgpA, RgpB = Arg-specific; Kgp = Lys-specific); degrade C3 and generate C5a.
- C5aR: complement receptor for C5a; required for P. gingivalis colonization and dysbiosis.
- CR3: complement receptor 3 (CD11b/CD18, β2 integrin); exploited by P. gingivalis for safe intracellular entry.
- iNOS: inducible nitric oxide synthase; microbicidal mechanism suppressed via C5aR–TLR2–PKA axis.
- Ndk: nucleoside diphosphate kinase; scavenges ATP to prevent P2X7-mediated apoptosis.
- GEC: gingival epithelial cell.
- MAC: membrane attack complex (C5b-9); degraded by P. gingivalis gingipains.
- FimA/Mfa1: two component fimbriae of P. gingivalis with distinct adhesin partners.
