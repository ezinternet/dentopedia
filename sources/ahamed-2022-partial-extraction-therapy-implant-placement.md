## One-line Summary

Narrative review of partial extraction therapy (PET) — socket shield, pontic shield, and root submergence — describing how retaining root structure preserves buccal bone and soft tissue, improves emergence profile, and reduces grafting needs at immediate implant placement, while noting the lack of standardized shield-design protocols.

## 한줄요약

부분발치술(PET — 소켓 쉴드·폰틱 쉴드·치근 매몰) 서술적 리뷰 — 치근 구조를 보존하면 협측골·연조직이 유지되고 emergence profile이 개선되며 골이식 필요가 줄지만, 표준화된 쉴드 디자인 프로토콜이 부재함을 지적.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Consolidates the family of partial extraction therapies (socket shield, pontic shield, root submergence) into one practitioner-oriented overview.
- Discusses shield design across case scenarios and stepwise procedure.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Narrative literature review (no quantitative synthesis).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Retained root structure preserves buccal bone → better emergence profile and esthetics.
- PET avoids/reduces hard- and soft-tissue grafting, shortening treatment time and recovery.
- Pontic shield: socket grafted before pontic pressure for ovate-pontic sites.
- Gap: no published study clearly standardizes PET shield design with proven success.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative (non-systematic); no pooled outcomes.
- Calls for standardized, evidence-based shield-design guidance.

## 6. Related Work

- Hürzeler socket shield; Gluckman pontic shield / partial extraction therapy classification.

## 7. Glossary

- **PET**: partial extraction therapy.
- **Pontic shield**: retained root fragment under an ovate pontic.
- **Root submergence**: intentional retention of a submerged root to preserve ridge.
