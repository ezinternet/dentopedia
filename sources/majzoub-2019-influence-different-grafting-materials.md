## Why Ingested

The largest single-SR ARP RCT pool (40 RCTs) quantifying horizontal resorption per material category, directly reinforcing [[overviews/socket-preservation-arp-overview]] and the no-material-superiority conclusion of [[avila-ortiz-2019-alveolar-ridge-preservation-interventions]]. Lets clinicians compare allograft/xenograft/alloplast numerically.

## One-line Summary
SR (40 RCTs) calculating inverse-variance weighted means by graft material — horizontal ridge resorption was 1.52 mm (allograft), 1.47 mm (xenograft), 2.31 mm (alloplast); ARP reduces resorption vs extraction alone, but inter-material differences are minimal.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Oral & Maxillofacial Research 2019;10(3):e6
- **Lead institutions**: University of Michigan (Majzoub, Ravida, Tattan), Aalborg University Hospital (Starch-Jensen), University of Oklahoma (Suárez-López del Amo)
- **Type**: Systematic review with weighted-mean synthesis (no formal MA)
- **Search up to**: December 2018

## 2. Key Contributions
- Largest **single-SR pool of ARP RCTs to date** (40 RCTs) at time of publication — exceeds Avila-Ortiz 2019's 22-RCT pool by using a broader inclusion (clinical + 3D imaging measurements).
- Quantifies horizontal resorption **per material category** (allograft / xenograft / alloplast), letting clinicians compare materials head-to-head numerically.
- Confirms previous conclusion: ARP works, but no material is clinically superior to another.

## 3. Methodology
- Search: PubMed/MEDLINE + EMBASE + CENTRAL + hand search of dental journals up to Dec 2018.
- Inclusion: RCTs in humans; clinical or 3D imaging measurements of dimensional change.
- Exclusion: non-RCT designs, 2D-only radiographic assessment.
- Two independent reviewers; third for disagreement (FS).
- Inverse-variance weighted means per material arm.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **40 RCTs** included.
- Mean horizontal ridge resorption (mm):
  - Allograft: 1.52 (SD 1.29)
  - Xenograft: 1.47 (SD 0.92)
  - Alloplast: 2.31
  - Extraction alone (control): higher than all ARP groups (specific value reported in tables)
- Inter-material differences statistically and clinically minimal between allograft and xenograft.
- Alloplast trend toward more resorption than allo/xeno — but heterogeneous (different alloplastic compositions pooled).
- Pre-op buccal bone thickness > 1 mm → better outcome (consistent with Del Fabbro 2022, Avila-Ortiz 2019).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Weighted-mean synthesis only (not formal random-effects MA) → wider real uncertainty than reported point estimates.
- Substantial heterogeneity in defect morphology, follow-up period (3–12 months), and outcome measurement (clinical caliper vs CBCT vs stent).
- Insufficient data to separate "graft alone" from "graft + membrane" effects.
- Long-term implant-level outcomes not assessed.

## 7. Glossary
- **Allograft**: Human-derived bone (FDBA, DFDBA).
- **Xenograft**: Bovine/porcine/equine-derived bone (e.g., Bio-Oss).
- **Alloplast**: Synthetic graft (β-TCP, HA, bioactive glass).
- **Inverse-variance weighted mean**: Weighting individual study means by their inverse variance (precision).
