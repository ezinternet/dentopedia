## Why Ingested

납복 착용 상태에서 TLD를 흉부(납복 위) 위치에 부착하여 실제 직원 피폭량을 측정한 연구 — 납복 착용 후에도 남는 산란선량 실측치를 제공. [[radiology/kim-2016-occupational-radiation-procedures-doses-korean-dentists]]의 한국 데이터와 비교 맥락에서 활용.

## One-line Summary

TLD dosimetry study in 30+ Basrah (Iraq) dentists and assistants: mean annual effective dose 5.623 mSv (dentists) / 5.279 mSv (assistants) measured above the lead apron, well below the 20 mSv limit; assistants in general hospitals received higher doses than dental-clinic staff.

## 한줄요약

이라크 바스라 치과 직원 TLD 측정 (납복 위 흉부): 연평균 유효선량 치과의사 5.623 mSv, 조무사 5.279 mSv — 직업한도(20 mSv/y) 이하이나 납복 없는 오픈 클리닉 환경에서 한국보다 훨씬 높은 수치.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies annual occupational dose in **open dental clinic** setting (no shielded room — reflects worst-case real-world condition)
- TLD placed at chest level **over the lead apron** — measures residual dose after apron protection
- Compares dentists vs assistants and dental-clinic vs general-hospital settings
- Demonstrates assistants in general hospitals receive higher doses due to higher patient throughput
- All doses below 20 mSv/y limit, confirming adequacy of protective measures even in open settings

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Study design**: Prospective TLD dosimetry study
- **Setting**: Open dental clinics, Basrah City, Iraq
- **Participants**: Dentists and dental assistants
- **Dosimeter**: TLD-200 (CaF₂:Dy) placed at chest level over the lead apron
- **Measurement period**: Annual (averaged)
- **Comparison**: Dentists vs assistants; dental clinics vs general hospitals

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Group | Mean Annual Effective Dose |
|---|---|
| Dentists | 5.623 mSv/year |
| Assistants | 5.279 mSv/year |
| Occupational limit (ICRP) | 20 mSv/year |

- Most measured doses were **well below 20 mSv/year**
- Assistants in general hospitals received **higher** doses than dentists or clinic-based assistants
- Interpretation: higher dose in open clinics compared to Korean shielded-room data (0.18 mSv/y) reflects absence of structural shielding, not failure of personal protective equipment

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only retrieval; sample size and exact clinic count not confirmed
- Iraq open-clinic setting may not generalize to shielded dental operatories in Korea or Western settings
- TLD placed **above** apron — does not quantify apron attenuation factor itself
- Radiograph modality breakdown (intraoral vs panoramic vs CBCT) not reported
- No control group without lead apron to isolate apron effectiveness

## 6. Related Work

- Kim et al. 2016 — Korean dentist dose 0.18 mSv/y (shielded rooms) vs this study's 5.6 mSv/y (open clinics): ~30× difference attributable to structural shielding, not personal protection
- Tamam et al. 2021 (PMID 34438276) — dental imaging staff dose in Saudi Arabia: 1.4 mSv/y (intermediate between Korea and Iraq)

## 7. Glossary

- **TLD-200 (CaF₂:Dy)**: calcium fluoride thermoluminescence dosimeter; high sensitivity for low-dose occupational monitoring
- **Open dental clinic**: clinic without dedicated radiation-shielded X-ray room (구분된 방사선 촬영실 없음)
- **ICRP**: International Commission on Radiological Protection (국제방사선방호위원회)
