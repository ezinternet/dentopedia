## One-line Summary

Animal study (90 short 6mm implants, porcine tibia) showing OD significantly increases insertion torque only for wide (5.4mm) implants vs standard drilling; narrow (4.2mm) implants show no IT advantage.

## 한줄요약

동물 연구(6mm 단임플란트 90개, 돼지 경골): OD는 광폭(5.4mm) 임플란트에서만 유의하게 높은 삽입 토크를 보이며, 세폭(4.2mm)에서는 표준 드릴링 대비 IT 이점이 없었다.

## 2. Key Contributions
- OD's IT benefit for short implants is diameter-dependent: significant only for 5.4mm (wide), not 4.2mm (narrow)
- OT (osteotome) also significantly increased IT for wide implants — OD advantage not exclusive
- Diameter effect within OD: W-OD (50.0 Ncm) > N-OD (31.5 Ncm, p=0.04)
- No histomorphometric differences (bone marrow space, connective tissue contact) between groups — IT improvement is mechanical, not biological at early stage
- Implication: selecting wider implants (≥5.4mm) when using OD in cancellous bone maximizes IT benefit

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Model:** Porcine tibia plateau (cancellous-dominant bone, Type III–IV analog)
- **Implants:** 90 Astra Tech EV (6mm length; Dentsply Sirona); 10 per group
- **Groups:** 3 diameters × 3 techniques = 9 groups (N-ST, N-OT, N-OD, R-ST, R-OT, R-OD, W-ST, W-OT, W-OD)
- **Diameters:** N=4.2mm, R=4.8mm, W=5.4mm
- **Techniques:** ST (standard drilling), OT (osteotome), OD (osseodensification/Densah)
- **Measurements:** IT (Ncm), ISQ; histomorphometry (bone marrow space%, connective tissue contact%) for N groups (n=4 per group)
- **Statistics:** not specified in abstract

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
| Group | IT (Ncm) | vs W-ST | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| W-OD (5.4mm, OD) | 50.00±14.14 | +79% | 0.005 |
| W-OT (5.4mm, osteotome) | 46.87±17.10 | +67% | 0.026 |
| W-ST (5.4mm, standard) | 28.00±10.85 | — | — |
| N-OD (4.2mm, OD) | 31.50±15.82 | N/A | NS vs N-ST |
| W-OD vs N-OD | 50.00 vs 31.50 | +59% | 0.040 |

- No significant IT differences for R (4.8mm) groups (not stated as significant)
- Histomorphometry: NS differences across all 3 N-group techniques

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Porcine model — no human in vivo validation
- Abstract-only; full ISQ values and R-group results not available
- Short-term — no healing/remodeling data
- Only narrow group (4.2mm) histomorphometrically evaluated; wide group histology not reported
- Need: clinical study of OD by diameter in Type III–IV bone, particularly for short implant indications

## 6. Related Work
- Rittipakorn 2025: Clockwise OD cadaveric tibiae — OD IT trend improvement; this study refines by adding diameter as co-variable
- Mercier 2022: OD cadaveric mandibles — OD significant IT increase; didn't separate by diameter
- Gaikwad 2022: SR+MA of OD animal studies — confirms IT/BIC advantages without diameter stratification

## 7. Glossary
- **Short implants:** Length ≤ 6mm; used in posterior regions with limited vertical bone
- **OD:** Osseodensification (Densah burs, Versah) in counterclockwise compaction mode
- **OT:** Osteotome — bone-condensing hand instrument
- **ST:** Standard drilling — conventional cutting
- **Porcine tibia plateau:** Cancellous-heavy porcine bone used as Type III–IV analog
