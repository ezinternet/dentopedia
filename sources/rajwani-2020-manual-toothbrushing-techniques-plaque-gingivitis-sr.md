## Why Ingested

This is the evidence backbone for the **toothpick method (Watanabe method) of manual toothbrushing** — the systematic review that benchmarks the toothpick method head-to-head against Bass / modified Bass / Charter's / Fones / scrub / roll / Stillman for plaque and gingivitis. It contextualizes our powered-vs-manual technique pages: where [[periodontics/liu-2026-powered-manual-toothbrushes-orthodontic-sr-ma]] and [[periodontics/luo-2026-electric-toothbrush-technologies-plaque-gingivitis-nma]] rank *powered* brush technologies, this Rajwani 2020 SR establishes that among *manual* techniques no single method has proven superior (excessive between-study heterogeneity), which is the comparator baseline those powered-brush SMDs are measured against.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (13 studies: 5 RCTs, 7 experimental non-RCT, 1 in vitro) comparing 10 manual toothbrushing techniques — no single technique is proven superior for plaque/gingivitis due to excessive methodological heterogeneity; the toothpick method beat Bass in the one study that tested it (Morita 1998).

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First SR focused specifically on manual toothbrushing *techniques*** (prior SRs covered manual-vs-powered or bristle-tuft/duration, not technique). 3190 articles screened → 40 full-text → 13 included.
2. **Head-to-head matrix of 10 manual techniques**: roll (6 studies), horizontal scrub (6), modified Bass (5), Bass (5), Charter's (3), Fones (3), modified Stillman (1), toothpick method (1), vertical (1), circular scrub.
3. **Toothpick method (Watanabe) benchmark**: tested in exactly one included study (Morita et al 1998), where it removed more plaque than the Bass method.
4. **Bottom line of insufficiency**: no single technique can be declared superior; excessive heterogeneity (10 techniques, 23 toothbrush brands, 10 plaque indices, varied training/surfaces) precludes pooling. Calls for CONSORT-compliant RCTs.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases**: Medline (Ovid), PubMed, EBSCO Dentistry & Oral Sciences Source; searched up to May 2018. 36 search terms; hand-searching of references.
- **Focused question**: efficacy of manual toothbrushing techniques on pre-/post-instruction bacterial plaque control and gingivitis.
- **Inclusion**: age ≥17, manual techniques (Charter's, modified Bass, Bass, Stillman, modified Stillman, Fones, scrub), baseline plaque/BOP, study designs RCT/split-mouth/observational/in vitro.
- **Exclusion**: <17 years, fixed ortho appliances, removable prostheses, compromised dexterity; electric brushes, interdental cleaning, mouthrinse; reviews/case reports/animal studies; non-English; full text unavailable.
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane Collaboration tool; two independent reviewers (SH, AT), blinded to title/authors/journal; disagreement resolved by consensus. PRISMA flow diagram.
- **Funding**: University of Western Australia postgraduate research component.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Included: 13 studies = 5 RCTs + 7 experimental non-randomised + 1 in vitro.**

Per-study findings:
- Frandsen 1970 (n=60, US Army, 17–25 yr): Charter's > roll/scrub for brusher A (left side/dentist); scrub better for brusher B (right side/hygienist).
- Frandsen 1972 (n=182, university students): instructor-dependent; Charter's & scrub slightly more effective; no significant differences between roll, scrub, Charter's.
- Arai & Kinoshita 1977 (n=52, dental students/dentists/hygienists, 20–34 yr): Fones and scrub more effective (judged unclear/high risk of bias due to vague methodology).
- Gibson & Wade 1977 (n=38, dental students): no significant difference roll vs Bass.
- Bergenholtz 1984 (n=24, previous periodontal patients, 20–49 yr): no difference between V-shaped and straight toothbrushes; Bass better and roll less effective (Index A, professionally applied).
- Kanchanakamol 1993 (n=100, soldiers, 20–21 yr): Bass ('Concept 45' brush) best mean plaque reduction (MNPI) vs roll.
- **Morita 1998 (n=20, male dental students, 20–26 yr): toothpick method removed more plaque than the Bass method (proximal and bucco-lingual surfaces, both supervised and self-brushing).**
- Poyato-Ferrera 2003 (n=46, secondary students 18–30 yr): modified Bass *statistically significantly* better than normal brushing at 21 days (TQHI).
- Harnacke 2012 (n=67, university students ~23 yr): Fones superior to modified Bass for oral hygiene **and** gingivitis (PBI). Computer-based training used.
- Schlueter 2013 (n=98, university students, mean 26.6 yr): no significant differences among any instruction groups (modified Bass; no instruction / leaflet / demonstration).
- Mastroberardino 2014 (n=61, university students 19–24 yr): vertical significantly better overall and interdental plaque removal than horizontal (RMNPI; by trained dental hygienist).
- Harnacke 2016 (n=70, random adults 22–23 yr): Fones (computer training) better than modified Bass for oral hygiene (70.3% Fones vs 77.9% modified Bass MPI reduction); **no** gingivitis difference (BOP) at 12 weeks.
- Jansiriwattana 2018 (in vitro, 11 tests × 3 brushes × 2 techniques = 66 tests): no difference among three toothbrushes with either scrub or modified Bass technique.

**Gingivitis**: only 2/13 studies used a true gingival index (both Harnacke) — conflicting results (2012 PBI favored Fones; 2016 BOP showed no difference), comparing only Fones vs modified Bass.

**Abstract tally of "statistically significantly superior" plaque removal**: Bass (1), modified Bass (1), Charter's (2), Fones (2), scrub (2), roll (1), modified Stillman (1), toothpick method (1); 4 studies showed no significant difference.

**Plaque heterogeneity drivers**: 10 plaque indices used across 13 studies — Silness & Löe PI, Turesky-modified Quigley & Hein (TQHI, 4 studies), Marginal Plaque Index (MPI, Deinzer), Rustogi Modified Navy Plaque Index (RMNPI), Modified Navy Plaque Index (MNPI), O'Leary, Proximal Marginal Index (PMI), Podshadley–Haley, Modified Volpe's, Bergenholtz index. Surfaces scored: Ramfjord 6 teeth (2 studies) vs full dentition; 4/6/8/18 surfaces per tooth.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Excessive heterogeneity** in design/methodology: 10 techniques, 23 toothbrush brands, 10 plaque indices, varied training methods (verbal/leaflet/video/computer/professional brushing), varied teeth/surfaces scored — no meta-analysis possible.
- Only 6/13 studies reported plaque reduction scores (percentage or mean), limiting comparability.
- Only 2/13 used a gingival index; gingivitis conclusions are weak and conflicting.
- Most participants were young (17–34) armed-forces/university/highschool cohorts; only one study used periodontal patients — limited generalizability to older/periodontally affected populations.
- Modified Stillman, toothpick method, vertical, and horizontal brushing each studied only once — insufficient evidence for single-technique conclusions.
- **Future work**: CONSORT-compliant RCTs; suggests Marginal Plaque Index (MPI, Deinzer et al) for higher sensitivity over TQHI. Study registration and CONSORT-compliant reporting needed.

## 7. Glossary

- **Toothpick (Watanabe) method**: brush head at 30° toward the crown, thrust into interproximal areas 8–9 times; first described 1984.
- **Bass / modified Bass**: bristles at 45° into the gingival sulcus with short back-and-forth strokes; modified adds a roll-off toward the occlusal.
- **Charter's**: bristles at 45° directed toward the occlusal, originally for orthodontic patients (1928).
- **Fones**: large circular motions across teeth and gingiva pressed together, 4–5 circles per set of teeth (1934, Alfred Fones).
- **Stillman / modified Stillman**: bristles partly on cervical tooth and gingiva at 45°; modified adds occlusal roll (Paul R. Stillman, 1932).
- **Scrub**: brush at 90°, horizontal/vertical/circular strokes — least technique-sensitive.
- **Roll**: bristles parallel to attached gingiva, swept toward the occlusal/incisal at 90° to the tooth surface.
- **TQHI**: Turesky modification of the Quigley & Hein plaque index.
- **MPI**: Marginal Plaque Index (Deinzer et al) — higher sensitivity than TQHI; 8 surfaces per tooth.
- **PBI / BOP**: Papillary Bleeding Index / Bleeding on Probing — gingivitis indicators.
- **CONSORT**: Consolidated Standards of Reporting Trials.
- **MNPI / RMNPI**: (Rustogi) Modified Navy Plaque Index.
