## One-line Summary

Systematic review (44/185 papers, 1922–2015, Ovid/Medline): cervical plexus accessory innervation — especially via the transverse cervical nerve and great auricular nerve — may explain persistent sensation after "successful" mandibular anesthesia, but evidence quality is mostly low (LOE 4–5 dominate; only 4 LOE-1 studies).

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(185편 중 44편 채택, 1922–2015, Ovid/Medline): 경부신경총(cervical plexus)의 부가신경 지배 — 특히 횡경부신경(transverse cervical nerve)·대이개신경(great auricular nerve) — 이 "성공적인" 하악마취 후에도 남는 감각을 설명할 수 있으나, 근거 수준은 낮음(LOE 4~5가 다수; LOE-1은 4편뿐).

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic (rather than narrative) synthesis of literature specifically on **cervical plexus (CP) accessory innervation** as a cause of dental anesthesia failure/persistent sensation.
- Applied a **methodological scoring index (MSI)** to grade studies by levels of evidence (LOE 1–5), exposing that the evidence base is overwhelmingly low-quality (LOE 4 n=20, LOE 5 n=14) versus high-quality (LOE 1 n=4, LOE 2 n=1).
- Flags that **CP anatomy itself needs re-examination** in the anatomical literature — the mechanism is plausible but under-studied.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Electronic + manual search (Ovid, Medline), articles from 1922 to March 2015, any language.
- Inclusion: humans/animals/cadavers AND anatomical/anesthetic-technique/clinical approach.
- Exclusion: maxillary buccal infiltration studies, no abstract/paper available, non-CP-branch studies, duplicates.
- 185 papers screened → 44 met inclusion criteria.
- Each included paper graded by LOE (1=highest, 5=lowest) via MSI.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Study-type breakdown of the 44 included: 1 RCT, 3 comprehensive reviews, 1 cohort study, 5 case series/reports, 16 poor-quality cohort/case series, 18 reviews/case reports/expert opinion.
- LOE distribution: LOE 4 (n=20) > LOE 5 (n=14) > LOE 3 (n=5) > LOE 1 (n=4) > LOE 2 (n=1).
- Conclusion: evidence for CP accessory innervation in dental anesthesia is real but predominantly low-quality; anatomical re-examination is needed.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only ingest — full methodological detail (specific nerve-by-nerve evidence tabulation) not retrieved.
- The review itself acknowledges its included evidence base skews toward low LOE (case reports/expert opinion) — the "accessory innervation explains anesthesia failure" claim should be read as plausible-but-not-rigorously-proven.
- No meta-analytic pooling — purely a quality-graded narrative synthesis.

## 7. Glossary

- **Cervical plexus (CP)**: nerve network from C1–C4 ventral rami in the neck; branches include great auricular, lesser occipital, transverse cervical, and supraclavicular nerves.
- **Accessory innervation (AI)**: sensory supply to a region from a nerve outside the "expected" (here, trigeminal/IAN) distribution.
- **LOE (Level of Evidence)**: 1 (highest, e.g. RCT) to 5 (lowest, e.g. expert opinion) evidence-quality grading.
- **MSI (Methodological Scoring Index)**: tool used in this review to standardize LOE grading across heterogeneous study types.
