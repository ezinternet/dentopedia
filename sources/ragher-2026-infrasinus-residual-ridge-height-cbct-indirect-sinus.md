## One-line Summary
Prospective observational study (n=50, CAS kit): CBCT underestimated infra-sinus residual ridge height by ~1.86 mm vs intraoperative measurement (4.66 vs 6.50 mm, p<0.001), arguing for dual-modality verification in borderline cases.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal of Indian Prosthodontic Society (JIPS) 2026. Prospective observational clinical study.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Quantifies systematic CBCT underestimation of residual bone height in posterior maxilla planning for transcrestal sinus lift.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 50 participants needing posterior maxillary implants. Preop CBCT RBH vs intraoperative measurement during indirect sinus lift using Crestal Approach Sinus (CAS) kit. Reliability of CBCT assessed.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- CBCT RBH mean 4.66 ± 0.80 mm; intraoperative mean 6.50 ± 1.07 mm; difference significant (p < 0.001).
- CBCT underestimates by ≈ 1.86 mm. Recommends CBCT + intraoperative verification (dual-modality), especially borderline cases.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Single-center; "underestimation" treats intraoperative measurement as gold standard (itself error-prone); CAS-kit-specific; observational, no implant outcome endpoint.

## 6. Related Work
- RBH-stratified OD-TSFE protocols: gaspar-2025; perforation risk by RBH: mazor-2024.

## 7. Glossary
- RBH: residual bone height. CBCT: cone-beam computed tomography. CAS kit: Crestal Approach Sinus kit (Hiossen/Osstem).
