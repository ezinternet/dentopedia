## One-line Summary
J Endod 2024;50(5):562-578 — scoping review (251 records screened, 35 included) mapping 10 AI use cases in endodontic education: radiographic interpretation, differential diagnosis, treatment planning, case difficulty, preclinical training, advanced simulation, real-time guidance, autonomous systems/robotics, progress evaluation, calibration.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Journal of Endodontics 2024;50(5):562-578
- Published: May 2024
- Multi-institution authorship (Case Western, USA; Sharjah, UAE; others)
- Type: Scoping review (PRISMA-ScR style)

## 2. Key Contributions
- First scoping review explicitly mapping AI into endodontic curriculum design
- Identifies 10 distinct domains where AI augments didactic and clinical endodontic teaching
- Aligned with Schwendicke et al. 2023 core AI curriculum framework for dentistry

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Electronic search up to December 2023: MEDLINE, Web of Science, Cochrane Library
- Manual reference search; grey literature and ClinicalTrials.gov included
- 251 records identified → 35 relevant to AI + endodontic education

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- 10 AI-aided learning domains identified:
  1. Radiographic interpretation
  2. Differential diagnosis
  3. Treatment planning and decision-making
  4. Case difficulty assessment
  5. Preclinical training
  6. Advanced clinical simulation and case-based training
  7. Real-time clinical guidance
  8. Autonomous systems and robotics
  9. Progress evaluation and personalised education
  10. Calibration and standardisation
- AI enables individualised feedback, automated detection/diagnosis, decision support, student progress tracking

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Educators must understand AI's limitations and ethical challenges
- Implementation will require curricular redesign
- Need for validated assessment tools tracking student-AI interaction outcomes
