## Why Ingested

Companion clinical reference to the just-ingested cadaveric clockwise-OD study [[wiki/implants/versah-protocols/rittipakorn-2025-clockwise-osseodensification-primary-stability-cadaveric]]: this is a prospective in-patient trial directly comparing osseodensification (OD) vs standard drilling (SD) on insertion torque (IT) and ISQ in the posterior maxilla, giving real-patient ISQ/IT numbers to set against the cadaveric model. It also feeds the IT-vs-ISQ dissociation question synthesized in [[wiki/implants/marzorati-2026-osseodensification-standard-osteotomy-torque-isq-sr-ma]] — OD raised IT ~37% but did NOT raise ISQ, the same split the SR+MA debates.

## One-line Summary

Prospective clinical trial (21 patients, 39 implants, posterior maxilla): OD raised insertion torque ~37% over SD (50.3 vs 36.1 Ncm, p<0.001) but produced NO significant ISQ difference at placement or 3 months — IT and ISQ dissociate.

## 한줄요약

전향적 임상시험 (환자 21명, 임플란트 39개, 상악 구치부): 골밀도화 (Osseodensification, OD)가 표준 드릴링 (Standard Drilling, SD) 대비 식립 토크 (Insertion Torque, IT)를 ~37% 높였으나 (50.3 vs 36.1 Ncm, p<0.001), 임플란트 안정성 지수 (Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ)는 식립 시점·3개월 모두 유의차 없음 — IT와 ISQ가 해리됨.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Comparison of osseodensification and standard drilling methods on implant stability quotient and insertion torque values of implants
- **Authors**: Moghaddas O, Banazadeh V
- **Journal**: Journal of Advanced Periodontology & Implant Dentistry, 2025;17(2):59–64
- **DOI**: 10.34172/japid.025.3475 | **PMID**: 40673121 | **PMCID**: PMC12261070
- **Study type**: Prospective clinical trial (test = OD, control = SD; CONSORT-styled, operator blinded to drilling protocol)
- **Setting**: Islamic Azad University, Dental Branch, Tehran (ethics ID IR.IAU.DENTAL.REC.1401.1402)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Real-patient (not cadaver/animal) head-to-head of OD vs SD on **both** IT and ISQ in the posterior maxilla, a low-density site where OD is most promoted.
- Replicates the now-common finding that **OD elevates insertion torque but not ISQ** — adding clinical-trial weight to the IT–ISQ dissociation.
- ISQ stayed above the 68 threshold for both groups at placement and 3 months, i.e. SD already achieved adequate stability in this ridge-restricted (height ≥9 mm, width ≥6 mm) cohort.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Sample**: 21 patients (10 F / 11 M, mean age 40); 39 implants in posterior maxilla. Group counts are reported inconsistently in the paper — abstract says 16 OD / 23 SD, body says 18 OD / 20 SD, with "20 implants in each group statistically analyzed" after excluding 2 non-cooperating patients at follow-up.
- **Implant**: MEGAGEN ANYONE (internal conical connection, tapered, sandblasted), all 4 × 10 mm, placed 1 mm subcrestal.
- **OD kit**: original Versah (USA) Densah burs, per densifying reference guide; SD per company protocol. Both at 1100 rpm, sequential similar-diameter burs, saline irrigation, max torque 50 Ncm during osteotomy.
- **Outcomes**: IT (max Ncm at insertion termination, manual torque wrench); ISQ via MEGA ISQ resonance frequency analysis (Smartpeg) at placement and 3 months.
- **Inclusion**: ≥18 y, ridge height ≥9 mm and width ≥6 mm, healed sites ≥4 months post-extraction, no augmentation needed. **Exclusion**: smoking, diabetes, bisphosphonates/steroids, immunocompromise, prior augmentation, etc.
- **Stats**: two-way repeated-measures ANOVA; significance set at 0.001.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Insertion torque**: OD **50.31 ± 5.90 Ncm** vs SD **36.09 ± 11.07 Ncm** — OD ~37% higher, p<0.001 (significant). IT correlated positively with ISQ at baseline.
- **ISQ at surgery**: OD 76.13 ± 6.07 vs SD 71.74 ± 2.42 — **no significant difference** (p≈0.134–0.176).
- **ISQ at 3 months**: OD 75.13 ± 6.44 vs SD 73.43 ± 1.67 — **no significant difference** (p≈0.471–0.488); values stable in both groups.
- All groups stayed **above the ISQ 68 threshold** at both timepoints.
- No inflammation, infection, or implant mobility at second-stage surgery.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Posterior maxilla only; anterior region not studied.
- Small sample; reporting inconsistency in per-group implant counts (16/23 vs 18/20 vs 20/20).
- Short follow-up (3 months) — no loading/survival endpoint.
- Single implant size (4 × 10) and one implant system; calls for long-term trials across bone types and macro-geometries.

## 6. Related Work

- Aligns with animal data (Huwais & Meyer; Lahens et al) showing OD doubles IT with no ISQ gain.
- Contrasts with Bergamo et al and Tretto et al review, where OD **did** raise ISQ significantly.

## 7. Glossary

- **OD (Osseodensification)**: non-subtractive osteotomy using Densah burs run counterclockwise to compact/autograft bone into the trabecular space, increasing peri-implant density.
- **SD (Standard Drilling)**: conventional subtractive osteotomy removing bone particles.
- **IT (Insertion Torque)**: maximum rotational torque (Ncm) at implant seating; a primary-stability surrogate.
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: 1–100 resonance-frequency-analysis index of implant stability; ≥68 considered favorable for early loading.
- **Spring-back effect**: residual elastic strain after OD compaction that re-engages bone against the implant threads.
