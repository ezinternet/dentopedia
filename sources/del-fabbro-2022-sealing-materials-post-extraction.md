## One-line Summary
SR + network meta-analysis (12 RCTs, 312 sites; IAO consensus paper) comparing biomaterials used to seal post-extraction sockets — autologous soft tissue grafts ranked best for horizontal ridge preservation; collagen membranes, d-PTFE, and acellular dermal matrices generally comparable.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Clinical Oral Investigations 2022;26(2):1137–1154 (online 2022)
- **Status**: Italian Society of Osseointegration (IAO) consensus conference proceedings
- **Type**: Systematic review with pairwise + network meta-analysis (NMA)
- **Funding**: IAO; open access

## 2. Key Contributions
- First **NMA dedicated to socket-sealing materials** (not the bone graft itself), addressing a gap in the ARP literature.
- Uses SUCRA + multidimensional ranking to handle the asymmetric, sparse evidence network.
- Provides treatment ranking (autologous soft tissue > membranes > collagen matrix > no coverage) for ridge preservation when a graft is already in place.
- Italian Society of Osseointegration (IAO) consensus weight — practice-shaping in EU prosthodontics.

## 3. Methodology
- Search: MEDLINE/PubMed + EMBASE + CENTRAL + Scopus up to 4 April 2021.
- Inclusion: parallel/split-mouth RCTs, ≥10 patients per study, sealing materials over extraction sockets.
- Excluded: studies altering both graft and coverage between arms, complete socket-wall destruction.
- Random-effects pairwise MA + Bayesian-style NMA; SUCRA for ranking; prediction intervals for clinical efficacy.
- Stata 16 with xtgee, metan, mvmeta, network commands.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **12 RCTs included; 312 extraction sites evaluated.**
- Pairwise:
  - Autologous soft tissue grafts → best horizontal preservation vs other sealing materials.
  - Resorbable collagen membranes vs d-PTFE: no significant difference (Arbab et al.).
  - Collagen membrane vs extracellular matrix (ECM): no significant difference (Parashis et al.).
- NMA SUCRA: autologous soft tissue > collagen matrix xenograft > membranes > no coverage (for horizontal dimensional change).
- Correlation between pre-op buccal plate thickness and outcome consistently observed (>1 mm thickness predicts better preservation).
- New bone formation %: insufficient consistent data for robust ranking.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Sparse network (some comparisons single-trial) → wide prediction intervals.
- High heterogeneity in tooth type (anterior vs posterior), defect morphology, and follow-up.
- Most trials co-administered a graft material → cannot fully isolate sealing effect.
- Long-term (>6 months) outcomes scarce; implant-level outcomes not assessed.

## 7. Glossary
- **NMA**: Network Meta-Analysis — combines direct and indirect comparisons across treatment network.
- **SUCRA**: Surface Under the Cumulative RAnking curve — relative ranking metric (0–1, higher = better).
- **d-PTFE**: Dense Polytetrafluoroethylene (e.g., Cytoplast) — non-resorbable membrane.
- **ECM**: Extracellular Matrix (acellular dermal substitute).
- **IAO**: Italian Society of Osseointegration.
