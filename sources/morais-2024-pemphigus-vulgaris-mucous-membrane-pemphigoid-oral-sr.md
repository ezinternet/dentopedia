## Why Ingested

Builds out the autoimmune blistering oral-disease branch of `oral-medicine`. Pemphigus vulgaris (PV) and mucous membrane pemphigoid (MMP) are the two mucocutaneous autoimmune diseases that most often present first in the mouth (desquamative gingivitis, blistering/erosive lesions), so the dentist is frequently the first to see them — this SR gives the clinical/demographic/diagnostic profile needed to tell them apart. Sibling to the autoimmune-disease implant page [[implants/esimekara-2022-dental-implants-autoimmune-diseases-sr]] (implant outcomes in autoimmune patients) and intended to pair with a future oral-manifestations-of-systemic-autoimmune-disease overview.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (32 articles; 50 PV + 123 MMP intraoral cases, all DIF-confirmed): mouth is the primary site in both (PV 71.4 %, MMP 91 %); cheek mucosa is the top PV site (30 %) vs gingiva for MMP (64.2 %); DIF was IgG-positive in 100 % of both; systemic corticosteroids dominate PV management (50 %), topical therapy dominates MMP (53.7 %).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Pools DIF-confirmed PV and MMP cases with intraoral involvement and contrasts the two diseases on demographic, clinical, diagnostic, and treatment axes — clinically the two are very similar but the SR isolates the features that separate them.
- Establishes that the oral cavity is the dominant presenting site for both diseases, reinforcing the dentist's role in first detection.
- Quantifies the intraoral site predilection difference: PV favors cheek (buccal) mucosa, MMP favors gingiva (consistent with desquamative gingivitis presentation).
- Confirms direct immunofluorescence (DIF) IgG positivity as a near-universal diagnostic anchor in both conditions.
- Maps the divergent treatment defaults: systemic corticosteroids for PV vs topical therapy for MMP.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Systematic review of the literature.
- **Inclusion criteria**: Studies describing cases of PV and MMP diagnosed by direct immunofluorescence (DIF) that exhibited intraoral manifestations.
- **Included studies**: 32 articles total — 18 on PV, 15 on MMP (overlap/dual-disease accounts for the totals).
- **Case counts**: 50 cases diagnosed as PV; 123 cases diagnosed as MMP.
- **Outcomes synthesized**: clinical and demographic profile, diagnostic methods, treatment choice.
- **Limitation (methodological)**: abstract-only retrieval — quality-appraisal tool, risk-of-bias method, and pooling/statistics not recoverable from the abstract.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Sex**: Most patients were women — PV 64 %, MMP 81.3 %.
- **Age**: PV peaked in the fifth decade; MMP in the sixth decade of life.
- **Primary site of involvement (mouth)**: PV 71.4 %, MMP 91 %.
- **Most frequent intraoral site**: PV → cheek (buccal) mucosa 30 %; MMP → gingiva 64.2 %.
- **Diagnosis**: Direct immunofluorescence positive for IgG in 100 % of cases of both conditions.
- **Treatment of choice**: PV → systemic corticosteroid therapy 50 %; MMP → topical treatment 53.7 %.
- **Bottom line**: differences in intraoral site predilection, extraoral involvement, and diagnostic-test results allow a clinical/demographic/diagnostic profile that supports differential diagnosis and therapeutic management.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only ingestion — full quality appraisal, individual-study heterogeneity, and any subgroup detail are not captured here.
- Evidence base is case reports/series (selection and reporting bias likely); percentages are descriptive, not comparative-statistical.
- DIF-confirmation inclusion criterion strengthens diagnostic certainty but may exclude clinically diagnosed cases, biasing the captured spectrum.
- No long-term outcome / recurrence data summarized.

## 7. Glossary

- **Pemphigus vulgaris (PV, 심상성 천포창)**: intraepithelial autoimmune blistering disease; autoantibodies against desmoglein (desmosomal adhesion) cause acantholysis and fragile blisters/erosions.
- **Mucous membrane pemphigoid (MMP, 점막 유천포창)**: subepithelial autoimmune blistering disease targeting the basement-membrane zone; frequently presents as desquamative gingivitis.
- **Direct immunofluorescence (DIF, 직접면역형광)**: tissue-biopsy assay detecting in-situ immunoglobulin/complement deposition; the diagnostic standard for autoimmune blistering disease (IgG-positive in both PV and MMP here).
- **Desquamative gingivitis (탈락성 치은염)**: clinical descriptor of erythematous, peeling, painful gingiva — a common oral presentation of MMP (and other vesiculo-erosive diseases).
- **Acantholysis (극세포해리)**: loss of keratinocyte-to-keratinocyte adhesion producing intraepithelial clefting; hallmark of pemphigus.
