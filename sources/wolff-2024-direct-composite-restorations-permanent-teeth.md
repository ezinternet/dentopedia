## Why Ingested

[[wiki/resin/astvaldsdottir-2015-posterior-resin-composite-longevity-sr]] provided the longevity evidence base for posterior composite but did not formalize indications. This 2024 German S3 (GRADE) clinical practice guideline extends that evidence into formal, panel-ratified indication recommendations spanning Class I–V, cusp replacement, and tooth-shape corrections — converting longevity data into actionable indication boundaries.

## One-line Summary

German S3 (GRADE) consensus guideline (20 societies, MEDLINE/Cochrane to Dec 2021, 6 PICO questions): direct composite is recommended for cavity Classes I–V, cusp replacement, and tooth-shape corrections, and should be preferred over indirect composite inlays in the posterior region.

## 한줄요약

독일 S3(GRADE) 합의 가이드라인(20개 학회, 2021.12까지 문헌, 6개 PICO): 직접 복합레진은 I–V급 와동·교두수복·치형교정에 권장되며, 후방부에서는 간접 복합레진 인레이보다 우선 선택되어야 함.

## 1. Document Information

- Type: Evidence-based clinical practice guideline (German S3 / AWMF 083-028, GRADE methodology)
- Published: J Adhes Dent 2024;26:185–200; accepted 30 July 2024
- DOI: 10.3290/j.jad.b5748881
- Evidence base: systematic MEDLINE + Cochrane (OVID) search to December 2021; 6 PICO questions
- Panel: dental professionals from 20 national societies/organizations

## 2. Key Contributions

- First comprehensive evidence-based guideline on indications for direct composite in permanent teeth.
- Formalizes that direct composite covers Classes I–V plus extended indications (cusp replacement, shape/position/shade corrections).
- Issues a directional preference: posterior direct composite over indirect composite inlays.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Methodology per AWMF Standing Guideline Commission + GRADE Working Group.
- Two methodologists ran the MEDLINE/Cochrane (OVID) search through December 2021.
- 6 PICO questions structured the evidence retrieval.
- Recommendations formulated by consensus among 20-society panel.
- Part 1 (this paper) covers indications; outcomes considered were survival, restoration quality, and process quality.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Composite is a viable option for direct restoration of Classes I–V.
- May be used for cusp-replacement restorations and tooth-shape corrections.
- Posterior region: direct composite should be preferred over indirect composite inlays.
- Class V: composite usable if adequate contamination control and adhesive technique are ensured.
- Context: ~47.1 million direct restorations in Germany in 2021; untreated adult caries ~29.4% globally.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Literature cutoff December 2021.
- Calls for further long-term clinical studies with comparators such as (modified) glass-ionomer cements.
- National (German) guideline; transferability to other care systems requires judgment.
- Part 1 limited to indications; implementation/technique handled separately.

## 6. Related Work

- Ástvaldsdóttir 2015 SR — posterior composite longevity evidence.
- Angeletaki 2016 SR+MA — direct vs indirect inlay/onlay composite.
- Boaro 2019 SR+MA — bulk-fill composite clinical/physical properties.

## 7. Glossary

- S3 guideline: highest German guideline development class (systematic evidence + structured consensus).
- GRADE: Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation framework.
- PICO: Population–Intervention–Comparison–Outcome question structure.
- Cavity Class I–V: Black's classification of cavity locations.
