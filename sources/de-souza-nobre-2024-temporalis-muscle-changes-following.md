## One-line Summary
Triple-blinded RCT (n=26 women) showing that BoNT-A injections in masseter for masseter hypertrophy (MH) cause compensatory increase in temporalis muscle thickness and EMG activity, with significantly greater changes after repeated injections; pain in temporalis emerged in 7/12 multiple-injection patients at 6 months.

## 1. Document Information
- Study type: Randomized, triple-blinded, placebo-controlled clinical trial (secondary analysis of a larger MH trial)
- Setting: Brazilian Dental Association (ABO-Goiás), Goiânia, Brazil; 2 Mar 2023 – 17 Oct 2023
- Ethics: Uningá University Research Ethics Committee (CAAE: 63135022.3.0000.5220)
- Registry: Brazilian Registry of Clinical Trials RBR-7tdjcn5 (24/01/2024)
- Reporting: CONSORT-compliant; Helsinki Declaration
- Level of Evidence: I (per journal designation)
- Primary outcome: Temporalis muscle thickness on ultrasound

## 2. Key Contributions
1. First clinical trial to comprehensively report **temporalis muscle thickness, EMG activity, and pain** changes after BoNT-A injection of the masseter for MH.
2. Demonstrates that the masseter and temporalis act as a coupled functional unit — masseter paralysis triggers measurable compensatory hyperactivity in the temporalis.
3. Quantifies the **dose-effect of repeated BoNT-A**: multiple injections (M-BoNT-A) produced significantly greater temporalis changes than a single injection (S-BoNT-A) at 6 months.
4. Identifies a clinically relevant adverse effect: **emergence of temporalis pain in the multiple-injection group** at 6 months (subjective pain location shift from masseter at baseline to temporalis at follow-up).
5. Provides level-I evidence supporting cautious use of repeated masseter BoNT-A for purely aesthetic indications.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Population: 26 Brazilian women, 25–50 years (mean 32.5 ± 8.04), Merz photonumeric masseter prominence scale levels II–V.
- Exclusions: prior BoNT-A, prior fillers/surgery, missing teeth, autoimmune/neuromuscular disease, pregnancy/breastfeeding, recent vaccinations.
- Intervention (Session 1, all participants): 75 U abobotulinumtoxinA (Dysport, reconstituted 500 U / 2 mL → 25 U/0.1 mL) into each masseter, 3 injection points × 25 U bilaterally = 150 U total.
- Randomization at 3 months (block size 4, online randomization):
  - S-BoNT-A group (n=13): saline (0.3 mL/side)
  - M-BoNT-A group (n=13): second BoNT-A injection identical to first
- Blinding: triple-blind (injector, examiner, participant); BoNT-A and saline visually identical.
- Follow-up: baseline, 1 mo, 3 mo, 6 mo (5 visits including screening).
- Outcomes:
  - Muscle thickness: ultrasound at MVC, SonoScape A6, L745 linear array 11.0–5.0 MHz, 0.01 mm accuracy; intra-observer Kappa 0.86; average of 3 measurements.
  - EMG: Miotool NG USB 4-channel, 10–700 Hz, 3000 samples/s, RMS over 5 s MVC × 3 trials with 2-min rest; bipolar Ag-AgCl on anterior temporalis 1 cm from external lateral region of eyebrows, reference on sternum.
  - Masseter prominence: 10-point Merz photonumeric scale (relaxed and contracted).
  - Subjective pain: VAS 0–10 plus pain-region drawing.
- Sample size: 9/group for β=0.9, α=0.01 assuming 25 ± 8 % thickness change; +4/group for dropouts → 13/group.
- Statistics: Shapiro-Wilk → Log10(x+1) transformation for thickness; two-way repeated-measures ANOVA with Sidak post-hoc for thickness/EMG/VAS; Friedman + Mann-Whitney for prominence; SPSS, α=0.05.
- Completers: 22 (S-BoNT-A n=10, M-BoNT-A n=12); 4 dropouts.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
### Right Temporalis Thickness (MVC)
- Intra-group: significant increase from baseline to 3 mo in both S- and M-BoNT-A (p < 0.02); increase persisted to 6 mo only in M-BoNT-A (p < 0.01).
- Inter-group: M-BoNT-A > S-BoNT-A at 6 mo (p < 0.02).

### Left Temporalis Thickness (MVC)
- Intra-group: significant increase from baseline to 3 mo in both groups (p < 0.01); to 6 mo only in M-BoNT-A (p < 0.001).
- Inter-group: no significant differences at any timepoint.

### Right Temporalis EMG (RMS μV at MVC)
- Intra-group: significant rise at 6 mo only in M-BoNT-A vs baseline (p < 0.03); no change in S-BoNT-A.
- Inter-group: M-BoNT-A > S-BoNT-A at 6 mo (p < 0.01).

### Left Temporalis EMG
- No significant intra- or inter-group differences.

### Masseter Prominence (subjective Merz scale)
- Intra-group: S-BoNT-A improved in rest position only at 3 and 6 mo (p < 0.03); M-BoNT-A improved in both rest (p < 0.02) and contracted (p < 0.0001) positions at all follow-ups.
- Inter-group: M-BoNT-A superior only at 1 mo (rest p < 0.02; contracted p < 0.03).

### Pain
- Baseline VAS: 3.4 ± 3.1 (S-BoNT-A), 2.6 ± 2.2 (M-BoNT-A); no significant inter- or intra-group VAS changes.
- Pain region shift: M-BoNT-A had **7 patients with pain isolated to temporalis at 6 months** vs masseter-pain pattern at baseline; inter-group prevalence of painful regions higher in M-BoNT-A at 6 mo (p < 0.02).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Female-only sample (cultural aesthetic indication); generalizability to males unknown.
- Single brand of toxin (abobotulinumtoxinA); not generalizable to onabotulinumtoxin / incobotulinumtoxin without dose-equivalence study.
- 6-month follow-up only — does temporalis hypertrophy and pain persist, normalize, or worsen with continued injections?
- Subjective pain measured by VAS + drawing; no quantitative algometry / pressure pain threshold.
- Asymmetry of findings (right > left for both thickness and EMG) is unexplained; possibly handedness-related.
- Surface EMG limited to anterior temporalis; deeper masticatory muscles not assessed.

## 6. Related Work
- Same trial cohort, different outcomes (masseter-focused): de Souza Nobre et al. *Scientific Reports* 2024;14:14522 (DOI 10.1038/s41598-024-65395-5) — paired companion paper.
- Lee et al. (cited as ref [8]): prior trial reporting temporalis volume increase after incobotulinumtoxinA in MH using 3D surface scanning (no thickness or EMG).
- Animal studies (refs [14–17]): show fatty infiltration, fiber-type shift (IIa→IIb), and atrophy markers after repeated BoNT-A.

## 7. Glossary
- **MH** — Masseter Hypertrophy (교근비대); idiopathic enlargement of masseter, often bilateral, contributing to a square lower face.
- **BoNT-A** — Botulinum Toxin Type A (보툴리눔 독소 A형); presynaptic acetylcholine-release blocker producing chemical denervation.
- **abobotulinumtoxinA** — Dysport formulation; conversion roughly 2.5 U Dysport ≈ 1 U Botox (onabotulinumtoxinA).
- **MVC** — Maximum Voluntary Contraction (최대수의수축); standard reference state for masticatory-muscle EMG and US.
- **Merz scale** — 10-point photonumeric masseter prominence scale (I–V), female-specific.
- **RMS** — Root Mean Square; standard amplitude metric for surface EMG.
- **S-BoNT-A / M-BoNT-A** — Single vs Multiple BoNT-A injection arms.
- **CONSORT** — Consolidated Standards of Reporting Trials.
- **Triple-blind** — Injector, examiner, and participant all blinded to randomization.
