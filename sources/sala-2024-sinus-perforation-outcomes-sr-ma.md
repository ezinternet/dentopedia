## One-line Summary
SR+MA (29 studies): sinus floor penetrating implants failure rate 2.1%; membrane-perforated vs intact augmentation OR 1.347 (p=0.197, NS) — perforation does not significantly increase implant failure risk.

## 한줄요약
SR+MA (29편): 상악동 저부 천공 임플란트 실패율 2.1%; 막 천공 상악동거상술 vs 비천공 간 임플란트 실패 OR 1.347 (p=0.197) — 천공 여부가 유의한 실패 위험인자 아님.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Systematic Review + Meta-Analysis (PRISMA 2020, PROSPERO CRD42024497046)
- **Published**: 22 February 2024, J. Clin. Med. 2024, 13(5):1253
- **Authors**: Yousef Mohamed Sala, Hans Lu (co-first), Bruno Ramos Chrcanovic (corresponding), Malmö University, Sweden
- **DOI**: 10.3390/jcm13051253
- **Quality**: All 29 included studies rated "good" (NIH Quality Assessment Tool ≥7 points)

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First SR+MA to analyse *both* scenarios in a single study: (a) implants protruding through the sinus floor without augmentation, and (b) implants placed in augmented sinuses where the Schneiderian membrane was perforated during sinus lift.
2. Quantifies estimated failure rate for sinus-penetrating implants at 2.1% (excluding Brånemark's machined-implant cohort: drops to 1.0%).
3. Pairwise meta-analysis (17 studies, 6,860 implants): OR 1.347 for membrane-perforated vs intact sinuses — not statistically significant (p=0.197).
4. Meta-regression shows the OR decreases non-significantly over longer follow-up (−0.004/month, p=0.500), suggesting perforations do not create worsening long-term disadvantage.
5. Confirms that sinusitis/infection risk exists but is low, and may be tied to perforation size or individual anatomical predisposition.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search**: PubMed/Medline, Web of Science, Scopus; September 2022 (last update October 2023); no time restriction
- **Inclusions**: Clinical human studies; cylindrical screw-type Ti/Ti-alloy implants in posterior maxilla; reporting sinus floor perforation by implants OR Schneiderian membrane perforation during sinus lift
- **Exclusions**: Case reports, animal studies, in vitro, reviews; mini-/zygomatic/orthodontic/zirconia/subperiosteal/hollow implants
- **29 studies included**: 11 for sinus floor perforation by implants; 18 for membrane perforation during sinus lift
- **Statistical methods**: Untransformed proportions (Group 1); pairwise meta-analysis using inverse variance method with random-effects model (heterogeneity I²=36.1%, p=0.069) for Group 2; meta-regression with follow-up as covariate; funnel plot for publication bias
- **Outcome**: Implant failure = implant removal for any reason (early or late); fracture also classified as failure

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Group 1: Sinus floor penetration by implants (11 studies, 1984–2022)

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Studies | 11 (10 unicenter, 2 multicenter) |
| Estimated failure rate (all implants) | **2.1%** (95% CI 0.2–4.1%, SE 1.0%, p=0.035) |
| Estimated failure rate (excluding machined implants) | **1.0%** (95% CI 0.0–1.9%, SE 0.5%, p=0.048, I²=0%) |
| Sinusitis reported | 1 case (transient) |
| Most common radiological sign | Mucosal thickening around apical portion |

Failure rates across individual studies: 28.9% (Brånemark 1984, machined implants), 4% (one study), 1.6%, 20%. Six studies reported 0 failures.

### Group 2: Membrane perforation during sinus augmentation (18 studies, 2006–2023)

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Total implants in perforated sinuses | 1,817 (73 failures) |
| Total implants in non-perforated sinuses | 5,043 (274 failures) |
| Odds Ratio (perforation vs no perforation) | **OR 1.347 (95% CI 0.857–2.117, p=0.197)** |
| Heterogeneity | I²=36.1%, τ²=0.241 |
| Meta-regression (follow-up covariate) | −0.004/month, p=0.500 (NS) |
| Funnel plot asymmetry | None apparent (low publication bias concern) |

Post-operative complications: sinusitis or infection reported in 8 studies; treated with antibiotics or incision/drainage in some cases.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Substantial confounders not controllable across studies: diabetes, smoking, bruxism, bisphosphonate use
- Retrospective study design predominant → data gaps
- Operator variability across multi-study pool
- Small cohort sizes and short follow-ups in several included studies
- Perforation *size* data inconsistently reported — could not be meta-analysed as subgroup

## 6. Related Work

- Díaz-Olivares 2021 (SR+MA): repair-focused, survival 97.68% perforated vs 98.88% intact (NS)
- Soares 2024 (SR+MA, 130 studies): repaired SMP implant loss 4%, perforation size not predictive of loss
- Manor 2024 (CBCT case-control): CBCT predictors of perforation
- Yang 2024 (SR+MA): septa OR 4.03 for perforation risk

## 7. Glossary

- **슈나이더 막 (Schneiderian Membrane)**: 상악동 내벽을 덮는 점막 (periosteum + respiratory epithelium)
- **상악동 저부 천공 (Sinus Floor Perforation)**: 임플란트가 상악동 내로 돌출된 상태 (막 천공 없을 수 있음)
- **슈나이더 막 천공 (Schneiderian Membrane Perforation, SMP)**: 거상술 중 또는 임플란트 식립 중 막이 찢어진 상태
- **OR (Odds Ratio)**: 오즈비 — 두 그룹 간 사건 발생 가능성 비교
- **I²**: 이질성 지표 — 36.1% (중등도)
