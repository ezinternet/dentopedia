## Why Ingested

Part of a focused collection on vertical ridge augmentation, covering the high-complexity scenario of simultaneous maxillary sinus graft + vertical ridge augmentation (often with Ti-mesh/membrane). It contextualizes the exposure and survival risks of vertical Ti-mesh augmentation from [[implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/chiapasco-2021-customized-cadcam-titanium-meshes-gbr]] in the technically hardest posterior-maxilla setting, and reinforces the soft-tissue-closure difficulty emphasized in [[implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/kaner-2017-pre-augmentation-soft-tissue-expansion-vertical-bone]].

## One-line Summary

Retrospective case series (15 patients, 33 implants, 2005–2010, Seoul National University Bundang Hospital): simultaneous maxillary sinus bone graft + vertical ridge augmentation (Ti-mesh/membrane in nearly all) gave 81.8% implant survival and success, low grafted-bone resorption (sinus 0.37 mm, ridge 0.20 mm at final), but elevated complications (4 Ti-mesh/membrane exposures) — authors recommend delayed implant placement.

## 한줄요약

후향적 증례 시리즈 (환자 15명, 임플란트 33개, 2005–2010, 분당서울대병원): 상악동 골이식 + 수직 치조제 증대술 동시 시행(거의 전 증례에서 티타늄 메시/차폐막)에서 임플란트 생존·성공률 81.8%, 이식골 흡수는 낮았으나(최종 상악동 0.37 mm, 치조제 0.20 mm) 합병증(티타늄 메시/막 노출 4건)이 많아 지연 식립을 권고.

- Affiliation: Seoul National University Bundang Hospital, Korea (Oral & Maxillofacial Surgery); IRB B-1811-505-103
- Study type: retrospective **case series** (note: PDF header is mislabeled "REVIEW"; the content is a single-center retrospective chart review of 15 patients).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Reports outcomes of the technically demanding **simultaneous** combination of maxillary sinus bone graft + vertical ridge augmentation in severely atrophied posterior maxilla.
- Documents the complication burden and lower survival of doing both procedures at once, and quantifies grafted-bone resorption over up to 3 years.
- Concludes in favor of a sufficient healing period and **delayed implant placement** for better long-term prognosis.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Patients (2005–2010) with severe alveolar atrophy from long-standing edentulism or progressive periodontitis, all operated by one surgeon, undergoing simultaneous vertical ridge augmentation with maxillary sinus bone grafting.
- 15 patients (11 men, 4 women), 33 implants placed. Mean age 54.2 ± 7.4 years.
- Bone grafts mostly particulate, mixed (autogenous bone, autogenous tooth bone graft [autoBT], allograft, xenograft, alloplast); block bone in 2 cases. Barrier membranes (Goretex, collagen, titanium mesh) used in all but one case; fibrin tissue adhesive (Greenplast) in 19 cases; Surgicel for sinus perforations in 2.
- Of 33 implants, 9 placed simultaneously with grafting; 24 delayed (mean healing 4.3 months).
- Outcomes from records + radiographs (periapical, panoramic): complications, success/survival (Albrektsson–Zarb 1986 criteria), ISQ, grafted-bone resorption at 1/2/3 years and final, marginal bone loss.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Implant survival = success = 81.8%** (6 of 33 failed). Three failed before loading (no osseointegration); three were late failures after prosthesis loading.
- Of the 6 failures, 5 were replaced and function well; 1 needed a third placement.
- **Postoperative complications**: 8 ecchymosis, **4 exposure of titanium mesh/membrane** (= 4 wound dehiscence), 3 peri-implantitis, 3 hematoma, 2 sinusitis, 2 fixture fracture, 1 bleeding, 1 numbness, 1 trismus, 1 fixture loss (counted as duplicates per implant).
- **Prosthetic complications**: 2 screw loosening, 1 abutment fracture, 1 food impaction.
- **Grafted bone resorption (sinus)**: 0.23 ± 0.40 mm at 1 yr; 0.37 ± 0.61 mm at final.
- **Vertical ridge augmentation resorption**: 0.12 ± 0.29 mm at 1 yr; 0.20 ± 0.37 mm at final.
- Marginal bone loss: 0.12 mm at 1 yr; 0.20 mm at final observation.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Retrospective, single-center, single-surgeon, small n (15 patients / 33 implants); heterogeneous graft materials and membranes.
- Doing sinus graft + vertical augmentation simultaneously raised complications and lowered survival (81.8%) vs typical implant survival; primary tension-free soft-tissue closure is especially difficult here and dual blood supply is limited.
- Authors recommend delayed implant placement after sufficient healing; longer follow-up and controlled comparison needed.

## 6. Related Work

- Simion et al. 2004 (vertical bone loss classification; sinus elevation when residual height <6 mm; vertical augmentation when loss >3 mm).
- Crown-to-implant ratio / occlusal overload literature (conflicting on its clinical impact).
- Reports that ~25% of grafted bone may resorb; delayed vs simultaneous placement debate (Cawood; McGrath).

## 7. Glossary

- **Vertical ridge augmentation (VRA)**: increasing alveolar bone height above the residual contour.
- **Sinus bone graft / sinus lift**: grafting beneath the elevated Schneiderian membrane to gain subantral bone height in the posterior maxilla.
- **Simultaneous approach**: performing sinus graft, ridge augmentation, and (in some) implant placement in one surgery — higher difficulty and complication risk.
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: RFA-derived implant stability index (Osstell).
- **autoBT (Autogenous Tooth Bone graft)**: graft processed from the patient's own extracted tooth.
- **Ti-mesh/membrane exposure**: dehiscence-driven exposure of the barrier — the key wound complication, here in 4 cases.
