## Why Ingested

치주염↔류마티스 관절염(Rheumatoid Arthritis, RA)의 양방향 자가면역 연결고리를 정량화하기 위해 인제스트. 자가면역질환 환자의 임플란트 결과를 다룬 [[wiki/implants/esimekara-2022-dental-implants-autoimmune-diseases-sr]]가 RA를 자가면역 적응증 중 하나로 다루지만, 치주염이 RA *발병 위험*을 끌어올린다는 방향의 근거는 별도로 필요했다. 본 SR+MA(Qiao 2020)는 13개 연구·100만 명 이상에서 치주염→RA 위험을 OR 1.69로 정량화하여, 공유 염증·시트룰린화(citrullination, *P. gingivalis* PPAD) 경로 가설을 역학적으로 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review + meta-analysis (13 case-control studies; 706,611 periodontitis patients vs 349,983 controls) finding periodontitis patients carry a 69% higher risk of rheumatoid arthritis (pooled OR 1.69, 95% CI 1.31–2.17, P<0.0001; I²=89%), with high heterogeneity explained partly by RA disease type, duration, and region.

## 한줄요약

치주염 환자가 대조군보다 류마티스 관절염(RA) 위험이 69% 높다는 13개 증례-대조 연구·치주염 70만6611명 vs 대조군 34만9983명 SR+MA로, 통합 OR 1.69(95% CI 1.31–2.17, P<0.0001; I²=89%), 신규 발병 RA와 이환기간 5년 초과 RA에서 연관성이 가장 강했다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First meta-analysis to specifically estimate the periodontitis → RA risk with comprehensive subgroup analysis restricted to case-control studies (to reduce structural heterogeneity), pooling 13 studies and >1 million subjects.
- Establishes a robust overall association: periodontitis confers 69% greater RA risk (OR 1.69, 95% CI 1.31–2.17, P<0.0001).
- Subgroup analysis by disease type: incident RA (OR 1.70, 95% CI 0.75–3.85) and mixed RA (OR 1.61, 95% CI 1.26–2.06) both elevated; prevalent RA (OR 0.93) not elevated.
- Subgroup by disease duration: longer-duration RA (>5 years) shows the highest point estimate (OR 2.88), consistent with cumulative inflammatory exposure.
- Regional subgroups reveal significant associations in Asia and Europe, but not North America (OR 1.19, P=0.264).
- Supports the shared inflammatory pathophysiology: HLA-DRB1 shared epitope, PAD (peptidylarginine deiminase) activity by *P. gingivalis*, ACPA/anti-CCP induction by *Aggregatibacter actinomycetemcomitans* (Aa) hypercitrullination via leukotoxin A (LtxA).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Systematic review with meta-analysis following PRISMA guidelines.
- **Databases searched**: PubMed, Cochrane Library, Embase, Web of Science, and Wanfang (Chinese); searched September 2019, no language or time restrictions.
- **Study selection**: two independent reviewers (WZ, LYF); disagreements resolved by third reviewer (CXP). From 4,445 identified records, 1,904 duplicates removed; final 13 studies screened.
- **Inclusion criteria**: observational case-control studies comparing periodontitis patients with controls; RA defined by internationally accepted criteria; periodontitis defined by clinical criteria (PPD/CAL thresholds), questionnaire, or periodontist diagnosis; extractable data.
- **Exclusion criteria**: animal/in-vitro studies; reviews, case reports, letters/comments; no extractable data.
- **Quality assessment**: Downs and Black 27-point tool covering study quality (10 pts), external validity (3 pts), study bias (7 pts), confounding/selection bias (6 pts), power (1 pt). Two independent reviewers.
- **Effect measure**: pooled odds ratio (OR) with 95% CI; Mantel-Haenszel method.
- **Heterogeneity**: I² test; fixed-effect model if I² ≤50%, random-effect model if I² >50%.
- **Sensitivity analysis**: sequential removal of one study at a time.
- **Publication bias**: Egger test.
- **Subgroup analyses**: by RA disease type (incident/mixed/prevalent RA), disease duration (>5 y, <5 y, mixed), and region (Asia, Europe, North America).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Included Studies
- 13 case-control studies published 2008–2019; total 1,056,594 participants.
- 706,611 periodontitis patients and 349,983 controls; 7,154 RA patients in the periodontitis group.
- Geographic distribution: 6 Asia (China, Korea, India), 4 Europe (Netherlands, Switzerland, UK, Germany), 3 North America (USA, Mexico).

### Overall Pooled Estimate
- OR 1.69 (95% CI 1.31–2.17; P<0.0001; I²=89% — high heterogeneity).
- Sensitivity analysis removing Chou et al reduced I² from 89% to 77% with minimal OR change (OR 1.48, 95% CI 1.20–1.83, P=0.0003).
- No significant publication bias (Egger test P=0.052).

### Subgroup Analyses

|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Disease type — mixed RA** | 11 | 1.61 (1.26–2.06) | <0.001 | 79% |
| **Disease type — incident RA** | 2 | 1.70 (0.75–3.85) | <0.001 | 96% |
| **Disease type — prevalent RA** | 1 | 0.93 (0.58–1.47) | — | 0% |
| **Duration >5 years** | 2 | 2.88 (0.66–12.62) | 0.018 | 82% |

| **Duration mixed** | 8 | 1.53 (1.05–2.22) | <0.001 | 89% |

### Quality Appraisal (Downs and Black)
- Strengths across studies: clearly stated hypothesis (13/13, 100%), representative participants (12/13, 92%), reliable outcome measures (13/13, 100%), appropriate statistical tests (13/13, 100%), adequate cohort size ≥50/group (10/13, 77%).
- Weaknesses: no adjustment for differential follow-up duration (0/13, 0%), limited assessor blinding (1/13, 8%), confounder adjustment varied (smoking, alcohol, antibiotics reported in 8/13, 62%).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Heterogeneous periodontitis definitions**: all studies used PPD and CAL, but thresholds varied, limiting direct cross-study comparison.
- **High residual heterogeneity**: I²=89% overall; sensitivity analysis and subgroup analyses did not fully explain it. Heterogeneity likely from: different diagnostic criteria for periodontitis/RA; differential adjustment for confounders (smoking, alcohol, antibiotics); geographic/genetic variation.
- **Sparse subgroup data**: incident RA (2 studies) and long-duration RA (2 studies) are underpowered; wide CIs cross 1 while P-values are <0.05, raising concern about subgroup reliability.
- **No causal inference**: all 13 included studies are observational case-control studies; temporal precedence (periodontitis predating RA) is assumed but not proven in most included studies.
- **Confounder control**: smoking, alcohol, and antibiotic use can affect both periodontitis and RA independently; adjustment varied across studies.
- **Geographic/database bias**: including only one Chinese-language database (Wanfang) may incompletely capture Asian literature; North America subgroup includes large population-based studies that dilute the OR toward null.
- **No meta-regression**: available data did not permit formal meta-regression to model sources of heterogeneity.

## 7. Glossary

- **Rheumatoid arthritis (RA)**: Chronic systemic autoimmune disease with synovial inflammation, joint destruction, functional disability, and elevated mortality.
- **Citrullination**: Post-translational conversion of arginine to citrulline by peptidylarginine deiminase (PAD); *P. gingivalis* carries PAD (PPAD) that citrullinates host proteins, generating neo-epitopes targeted by anti-citrullinated protein antibodies (ACPA/anti-CCP).
- **Anti-CCP / ACPA**: Anti-cyclic citrullinated peptide antibodies; serologic hallmark of RA, produced in response to citrullinated self-proteins.
- **Aggregatibacter actinomycetemcomitans (Aa)**: Periodontal pathogen that drives global hypercitrullination via leukotoxin A (LtxA); proposed molecular link between periodontitis and RA autoimmunity.
- **Shared epitope (SE)**: HLA-DRB1 allele conferring >80% of susceptibility for joint destruction in RA; also associated with alveolar bone destruction in periodontitis.
- **Incident RA**: Newly diagnosed RA arising after periodontitis exposure (prospective direction).
- **Downs and Black tool**: 27-item checklist for methodological quality assessment of both randomized and non-randomized health care intervention studies.
- **Mantel-Haenszel method**: Statistical method for pooling ORs across studies, with fixed-effect (I²≤50%) or random-effect (I²>50%) models.
- **Egger test**: Statistical test for publication bias in meta-analysis based on regression of effect estimates on their precision.
