## Why Ingested

치주염↔류마티스 관절염(Rheumatoid Arthritis, RA)의 양방향 자가면역 연결고리를 정량화하기 위해 인제스트. 자가면역질환 환자의 임플란트 결과를 다룬 [[wiki/implants/esimekara-2022-dental-implants-autoimmune-diseases-sr]]가 RA를 자가면역 적응증 중 하나로 다루지만, 치주염이 RA *발병 위험*을 끌어올린다는 방향의 근거는 별도로 필요했다. 본 SR+MA(Qiao 2020)는 13개 연구·100만 명 이상에서 치주염→RA 위험을 OR 1.69로 정량화하여, 공유 염증·시트룰린화(citrullination, *P. gingivalis* PPAD) 경로 가설을 역학적으로 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review + meta-analysis (13 studies; 706,611 periodontitis patients vs 349,983 controls; abstract-only) finding periodontitis patients carry a 69% higher risk of rheumatoid arthritis (pooled OR 1.69, 95% CI 1.31–2.17, P<0.0001), with the strongest association for incident RA and RA of >5-year duration.

## 한줄요약

치주염 환자가 대조군보다 류마티스 관절염(RA) 위험이 69% 높다는 13개 연구·치주염 70만6611명 vs 대조군 34만9983명 SR+MA(초록 기반)로, 통합 OR 1.69(95% CI 1.31–2.17, P<0.0001), 신규 발병 RA와 이환기간 5년 초과 RA에서 연관성이 가장 강했다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies the periodontitis → rheumatoid arthritis (RA) direction of the periodontal–systemic link, pooling 13 studies with very large populations (706,611 periodontitis patients, 349,983 controls).
- Establishes a statistically robust pooled association: periodontitis confers a 69% greater RA risk (OR 1.69, 95% CI 1.31–2.17, P<0.0001).
- Stratifies the association by RA disease type (incident vs mixed) and by RA disease duration (<5 y, >5 y, mixed), showing the risk elevation is not uniform.
- Supports a shared inflammatory and citrullination pathophysiology (the *Porphyromonas gingivalis* PPAD–anti-CCP/ACPA hypothesis) at the epidemiologic level.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Systematic review with meta-analysis.
- **Databases searched**: PubMed, Cochrane Library, Embase, Web of Science, and Wanfang (Chinese-language database).
- **Inclusion**: Studies comparing periodontitis patients with controls for the occurrence of RA.
- **Outcome**: Pooled odds ratio (OR) with 95% CI for the periodontitis–RA association.
- **Subgroup analyses**: by RA disease type (incident RA, mixed RA) and by RA disease duration (>5 years, <5 years, mixed).
- abstract-only — full text not retrieved; risk-of-bias tool, heterogeneity statistics (I²), and individual study weights are not recoverable from the abstract.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Included studies**: 13 (706,611 periodontitis patients; 349,983 controls).
- **Overall pooled RA risk**: OR 1.69 (95% CI 1.31–2.17; P<0.0001) — periodontitis patients had 69% greater RA risk.
- **By disease type**:
  - Incident RA: OR 1.70 (95% CI 0.75–3.85; P<0.001).
  - Mixed RA: OR 1.61 (95% CI 1.26–2.06; P<0.001).
- **By disease duration**:
  - >5 years: OR 2.88 (95% CI 0.66–12.62; P=0.018).
  - <5 years: OR 2.59 (95% CI 0.83–8.11; P<0.001).
  - Mixed: OR 1.53 (95% CI 1.05–2.22; P<0.001).
- **Conclusion**: Periodontitis patients carry an increased RA risk vs healthy controls; the association is higher for incident RA and for RA with >5-year duration.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- abstract-only — full text not retrieved; methodological detail (search dates, risk-of-bias appraisal, heterogeneity, publication bias) cannot be confirmed.
- Several subgroup estimates have very wide 95% CIs crossing 1 (incident RA 0.75–3.85; >5 y 2.88, 0.66–12.62; <5 y 2.59, 0.83–8.11) despite reported low P-values, signaling sparse data, high heterogeneity, or possible CI/P-value reporting inconsistency.
- Observational evidence establishes association, not causation; the periodontitis → RA temporal direction is inferred, not proven, by most pooled studies.
- Inclusion of a Chinese-language database (Wanfang) broadens coverage but mixes heterogeneous populations and case definitions of periodontitis and RA.

## 7. Glossary

- **Rheumatoid arthritis (RA)**: Chronic systemic autoimmune disease characterized by synovial inflammation and joint destruction.
- **Citrullination**: Post-translational conversion of arginine to citrulline; *P. gingivalis* peptidylarginine deiminase (PPAD) can citrullinate host proteins, generating neo-epitopes targeted by anti-citrullinated protein antibodies (ACPA/anti-CCP) — the proposed molecular bridge between periodontitis and RA.
- **Incident RA**: Newly diagnosed RA arising after periodontitis exposure (prospective direction).
- **Odds ratio (OR)**: Measure of association; OR >1 indicates higher odds of RA among periodontitis patients.
- **Anti-CCP / ACPA**: Anti-cyclic citrullinated peptide antibodies; serologic hallmark of RA.
