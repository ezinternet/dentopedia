## One-line Summary

Narrative review (Children, MDPI) synthesizing validated pediatric dental-anxiety assessment tools (selection varies by age/cognition/context) and non-pharmacological management — tell–show–do, modeling, parental guidance, audiovisual distraction, and VR-based methods show consistent effectiveness; structured assessment + multimodal behavioral strategies improve cooperation and clinical efficiency.

## 한줄요약

소아 치과불안 narrative review(Children, MDPI) — 검증된 평가도구는 연령·인지·맥락에 따라 선택 달라짐. 비약물 관리(tell-show-do, modeling, 보호자 안내, 시청각 distraction, VR)가 일관된 효과. 구조화된 평가 + 다중모드 행동전략이 협조도·임상효율을 높임. 몰입형 distraction 등 디지털 신경향 부각.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Children (MDPI) 2026;13(3):397

- Affiliation: University of Oradea, Romania (+ Apollonia University)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Pairs assessment-tool selection guidance with a catalog of non-pharmacological management strategies.
- Elevates emerging digital/immersive distraction (VR) as a promising modern direction.
- Argues structured anxiety assessment + multimodal strategy improves outcomes and chair efficiency.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Structured search across major databases; emphasis on validated anxiety scales, communication strategies, environmental adaptation, and digital interventions.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Multiple validated pediatric anxiety instruments exist; applicability varies by age, cognitive development, clinical context → age-appropriate selection required.
- Non-pharmacological methods (tell–show–do, modeling, parental guidance, audiovisual distraction, VR) consistently reduce anxiety and improve cooperation.
- Structured assessment + multimodal behavioral strategy enhances treatment outcomes and clinical efficiency.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative review — no pooled effect sizes or quality grading.
- Heterogeneous instruments/interventions; VR evidence still emerging.

## 7. Glossary
- **Tell–show–do**: behavior-shaping technique — explain, demonstrate, then perform.
- **Modeling**: reducing anxiety by observing a calm peer/model undergoing treatment.
- **Immersive/VR distraction**: digital diversion of attention during treatment.
