## Why Ingested

블랙 스테인 (Black Stain, BS)이 오히려 우식 경험을 낮춘다는 임상 관찰은 직관에 반하지만 중요한 임상 신호다. 이 리뷰는 그 메커니즘(미생물, 화학, 타액)을 체계적으로 정리한 가장 인용 빈도 높은 개요 문헌으로, [[caries/mousa-2022-black-stain-caries-children-sr-ma]]의 메타분석 결과를 해석하는 생물학적 기반을 제공한다.

## One-line Summary

Narrative review (2001–2014 literature) synthesizing etiology, prevalence (2.4–18%), microbiology (Actinomyces-dominant), chemistry (iron/sulfur complexes), and salivary factors explaining the inverse association between black stain and dental caries.

## 한줄요약

서사적 리뷰 (2001–2014): 블랙 스테인 (Black Stain, BS) 유병률 2.4–18%, 주요 원인균 Actinomyces, 철/황 복합체가 착색 원인이며 타액 칼슘·완충능 증가로 우식 경험 저하와 관련됨.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: BioMed Research International
- **Year**: 2015
- **Study type**: Narrative review
- **Literature scope**: Articles published 2001–2014
- **Population**: Primarily pediatric; some adult studies included

## 2. Key Contributions

- Synthesizes one century of debate on black stain etiology into a coherent framework
- Documents inverse association between black stain and caries across the reviewed literature
- Characterizes the distinctive microflora of black stain deposits (Actinomyces-dominated, low cariogenicity)
- Identifies iron/copper and sulfur complexes as the chromogenic agents responsible for dark coloration
- Highlights salivary differences (higher calcium, higher buffering capacity) in black stain patients
- Reviews contributing factors: diet, socioeconomic status, iron supplementation

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Narrative review of English-language literature published 2001–2014. Search terms related to "black stain," "dental pigmentation," and "caries" in pediatric and adult populations. No formal PRISMA protocol reported (narrative format). Key domains reviewed:
1. Definition and clinical characteristics
2. Prevalence and demographic distribution
3. Etiology (chemical, microbiological, dietary, systemic)
4. Association with dental caries
5. Salivary properties

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Prevalence**
- Range: 2.4–18% depending on population and diagnostic criteria
- Sex distribution: approximately equal (no strong male/female predominance)
- Most studies conducted in pediatric populations

**Clinical description**
- Dark line or incomplete coalescence of dark dots
- Located on the cervical third of the tooth crown
- More common on primary dentition; also found in permanent teeth

**Microbiological findings**
- Predominant organism: *Actinomyces* spp.
- Black stain microflora has lower cariogenic potential than non-discolored dental plaque
- Fewer *Streptococcus mutans* and *Lactobacillus* in black stain deposits compared to regular plaque

**Chemical/chromogenic mechanism**
- Iron (Fe) and copper (Cu) combine with sulfur (S) compounds from bacterial metabolism
- Ferrous sulfide complexes (FeS) and related compounds impart the black/dark coloration
- Extrinsic chromogens from diet (tea, coffee) may interact with these complexes

**Salivary properties in black stain patients**
- Higher calcium (Ca²⁺) concentration
- Higher buffering capacity
- Potentially higher pH — unfavorable environment for acidogenic/cariogenic bacteria

**Caries association**
- Majority of reviewed studies confirm significantly lower caries experience (dmft/DMFT) in children with black stain
- Proposed mechanisms: antimicrobial iron compounds, low-cariogenicity microflora, protective saliva chemistry

**Contributing factors**
- Dietary habits (tea, coffee consumption linked to stain formation)
- Iron supplementation (systemic or topical)
- Socioeconomic status (some studies show inverse correlation with SES, but confounders noted)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative review — subject to selection bias; no systematic search protocol or quality appraisal
- Heterogeneity of black stain diagnostic criteria across studies limits comparability of prevalence figures
- Most studies are cross-sectional; causality between microflora composition and low caries risk not established
- Salivary data largely from small, single-center studies
- Mechanism by which iron/sulfur complexes may inhibit cariogenic bacteria remains speculative
- Future longitudinal studies needed to establish whether black stain predicts sustained caries protection or is simply a co-occurring marker

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Black stain (BS) | Extrinsic dark pigmentation, cervical third, line or coalescent dots |
| Actinomyces spp. | Gram-positive filamentous bacteria dominant in BS microflora |
| Ferrous sulfide (FeS) | Iron-sulfur compound responsible for black coloration in BS |
| dmft/DMFT | Decayed, Missing, Filled Teeth index (lower case = primary; upper case = permanent) |
| Buffering capacity | Saliva's ability to neutralize acid — higher in BS patients |
| Chromogen | Pigment-producing compound; dietary chromogens (tea polyphenols) interact with Fe/S complexes |
