## Why Ingested

The clinical question of how to optimize in-office bleaching protocol (number of applications vs contact time) to maximize whitening while minimizing pulp HP penetration is unresolved. This controlled in vitro study with 11 protocol groups (n=154 premolars) directly maps the efficacy-safety trade-off, extending [[tooth-whitening/centenaro-2025-low-vs-high-hp-in-office]] by addressing the application time dimension independently of concentration.

## One-line Summary

In vitro RCT (n=154 human premolars, 11 protocol groups) found 2×8-min and 1×24-min in-office bleaching protocols optimally balanced whitening (significant WI after 2 sessions) and HP pulp penetration (intermediate levels), while 3×8 min maximized both whitening and penetration.

## 한줄요약

인비트로 RCT(n=154)에서 2×8분 및 1×24분 인체 소구치 미백 프로토콜은 미백 효과(2회 후 유의한 WI 변화)와 수분(HP 치수강 침투) 균형이 최적이었으며, 3×8분은 미백과 침투 모두 최대였다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic comparison of 11 in vitro application protocols: varying number of applications (1–3) and contact times (8, 16, 24 min) across two commercial bleaching gels
- Confirmed that WI improvement requires a minimum of 2 sessions — a single session regardless of protocol yields less improvement
- Identified 2×8 min and 1×24 min as the optimal "sweet spots": good WI + lower pulp HP penetration vs 3×8 protocol
- 3×8 min achieves maximum whitening but also maximum pulp HP exposure — highest sensitivity risk
- 1×8 min insufficient for both WI and HP penetration — minimal effect overall

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Specimens**: 154 human extracted premolars assigned to 11 groups (n=14/group)
- **Products**: Two commercial in-office bleaching gels: Pola Office+ and Pola Rapid (SDI)
- **Protocols tested** (each applied for 2 bleaching sessions, 7-day interval):
  - 3×8 min, 2×8 min, 1×8 min, 1×16 min, 1×24 min (for each gel)
  - Negative control (no bleaching)
- **WI (Whiteness Index)**: Digital spectrophotometer before and after 2 sessions
- **HP pulp penetration**: UV-Vis spectrophotometry (leaching into the pulp chamber)
- **Statistics**: Two-way ANOVA + Tukey's HSD + Dunnett's test vs control

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Whiteness Index (WI) — after 2 sessions:**
| Protocol | WI Change | Significance vs control |
|---|---|---|
| 3×8 min | Highest | p < 0.001 |
| 2×8 min | High (=3×8 stat.) | p < 0.001 |
| 1×24 min | High (=3×8 stat.) | p < 0.001 |
| 1×16 min | Intermediate | p < 0.001 |
| 1×8 min | Lowest | p < 0.001 |

**HP Pulp Penetration:**
| Protocol | HP level | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| 3×8 min | Highest | p < 0.0001 |
| 2×8 min | Intermediate | p < 0.0001 |
| 1×24 min | Intermediate-high | p < 0.0001 |
| 1×8 min | Lowest | p < 0.0001 |

**Gel comparison**: Both Pola Office+ and Pola Rapid produced similar patterns; no significant between-gel differences in most outcomes.

**Clinical translation**: The 2×8-min protocol achieves whitening equivalent to 3×8 while reducing HP pulp penetration — a safer and faster option (saves ~8 min chair time per session).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In vitro only — no pulp response, saliva, or tooth sensitivity data
- Extracted premolars; root canal morphology may differ from anterior teeth (primary bleaching targets)
- Two specific products only (Pola Office+ / Pola Rapid SDI); different gel formulations may behave differently
- pH changes during gel application not tracked (a known driver of efficacy and safety)
- Clinical validation RCT needed to confirm 2×8-min protocol superiority with sensitivity endpoints

## 7. Glossary

- **WI (Whiteness Index)**: Spectrophotometric measurement of perceived whiteness; WI = 0.511L* − 2.3424a* − 1.100b*
- **Pola Office+ / Pola Rapid**: SDI (Australia) in-office bleaching products; differing gel stability/concentration
- **UV-Vis spectrophotometry**: Measures HP concentration in pulp chamber leachate via absorbance at 450nm
- **Two-way ANOVA**: Statistical test for two independent factors (protocol × gel type)
- **Tukey's HSD**: Post-hoc test for all pairwise comparisons after significant ANOVA
- **n=154 premolars, 11 groups**: 14 teeth/group — adequate for in vitro power
