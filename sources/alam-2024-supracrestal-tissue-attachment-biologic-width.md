## Why Ingested

Provides the contemporary terminology (supracrestal tissue attachment, SCTA) and a worked clinical pathway — identification (bone sounding), evaluation, and surgical correction (crown lengthening) — for biologic-width violation, applying the dimensional concepts in [[wiki/periodontics/hamasni-2017-biologic-width-restorative-dentistry]] and operationalizing the SCTA principles reviewed in [[wiki/periodontics/ercoli-2021-fixed-dental-prostheses-restorations-periodontium]].

## One-line Summary

Case presentation + literature review defining supracrestal tissue attachment (SCTA, the renamed biologic width = junctional epithelium + connective tissue attachment) and detailing identification (bone sounding <2 mm margin-to-crest), evaluation, and correction (gingivectomy, surgical crown lengthening, extrusion) of SCTA violation in a subgingival-crown case.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Frames the modern terminology: biologic width → supracrestal tissue attachment (SCTA), the physiologic unit of junctional epithelium + connective tissue attachment.
- Defines ideal SCTA (≈1 mm connective tissue attachment + 1 mm junctional epithelium + 1 mm sulcus) and the diagnostic threshold for violation (margin-to-crest <2 mm on bone sounding).
- Tabulates correction strategies: surgical (gingivectomy, apically positioned flap ± osteotomy, surgical/orthodontic extrusion, intra-alveolar transplantation) vs conservative/ultraconservative; keyed to keratinized-tissue amount and bone-level relationship to CEJ.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Single case: 47-year-old female with discomfort, inflammation, and BOP at teeth 35/36 after recent prosthetic crowns; bone sounding showed margin-to-crest <2 mm and radiographic crestal bone loss → SCTA violation.
- Treatment: full-thickness envelope flap (34–36), osteotomy/osteoplasty under irrigation to re-create positive architecture and re-establish SCTA, silk suture, CHX 0.2%, uneventful healing.
- Literature review of SCTA biology, consequences of violation, and treatment-strategy tables.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- SCTA violation manifested as gingival inflammation, BOP, deepened pocket, crestal bone loss; surgical crown lengthening re-established the attachment with satisfactory healing.
- Reviewed evidence: SCTA violation → adverse subgingival microbiota shift, increased plaque index, progressive recession, deepened pockets.
- Decision logic: gingivectomy when ≥3 mm SCTA on bone sounding and adequate keratinized tissue (leaving ≥2 mm); crown lengthening when subgingival margins violate SCTA, short clinical crowns, cervical-third fracture; extrusion for single-rooted teeth.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single case report (lowest experimental evidence weight); no comparison group or long-term outcome data.
- Treatment-strategy tables are expert-synthesized rather than from pooled outcomes.

## 7. Glossary

- **Supracrestal tissue attachment (SCTA)**: renamed biologic width — junctional epithelium + supracrestal connective tissue attachment around each tooth.
- **Bone sounding**: probing to alveolar crest under anesthesia; margin-to-crest <2 mm indicates SCTA violation.
- **Surgical crown lengthening**: apically positioned flap ± osteotomy to relocate the attachment and create restorative space without impinging on SCTA.
