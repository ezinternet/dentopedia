## One-line Summary
Literature review (Cureus 2019) on orthopedic bone-drilling thermal osteonecrosis (50°C critical value): heat generation is multifactorial — drill parameters and drill characteristics matter — but **external irrigation is the most important single variable** for keeping bone temperature below the critical threshold.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Cureus 11(7):e5226, July 2019
- Affiliation: National University of Ireland, Galway; University Hospital Galway
- Type: Literature review (Level V evidence per authors)
- Search: PubMed, ScienceDirect, Cochrane Library through December 2017
- Field of application: orthopedic surgery (transferable to dental implantology)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Cross-specialty perspective: orthopedic surgery's framing of the same physical problem.
- States 50°C (not the more dental-centric 47°C) as the accepted critical value.
- Emphasizes external irrigation as the single most important mitigation.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- PubMed, ScienceDirect, Cochrane through Dec 2017.
- Boolean operator searches + thesaurus searches.
- Reference tracking on retrieved articles.
- Narrative review.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Factors affecting heat generation are multifactorial and interact.
- Both drill parameters (speed, feed rate, intermittence) and drill characteristics (geometry, sharpness, material) affect heat.
- **External irrigation = most important single variable** for maintaining temperature below critical 50°C.
- Lack of in vivo human bone studies identified as a major evidence gap.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Level V evidence (literature review).
- Search cut-off December 2017.
- Orthopedic focus — dental specifics need cross-translation.
- Future: parameter interaction studies; in vivo human bone studies.

## 6. Related Work
- Eriksson & Albrektsson — original 47°C/1 min dental threshold.
- Dental SRs by Chauhan 2018, Chakraborty 2024.
- Woods 2022 — orthopedic in vivo dog tibia model with saline irrigation.

## 7. Glossary
- **Thermal osteonecrosis**: bone death from excessive heat (~50°C threshold in this paper, 47°C in classical dental literature).
- **Level V evidence**: expert opinion / literature review (low rung of evidence hierarchy).
- **External irrigation**: coolant delivered outside the drill (vs internal through-drill irrigation).
