## One-line Summary

SR+MA with meta-regression (23 in vitro studies) — resin-based composites do change color after peroxide bleaching, but the change is not clinically significant; storage time, measuring device, and background color (not bleaching agent/time) drive the measured ΔE.

## 한줄요약

메타회귀 포함 SR+MA (in vitro 23편) — 과산화물 미백 후 레진 복합체는 색이 변하지만 임상적으로 유의하지 않음. 측정된 색차는 미백제·적용시간이 아니라 보관시간·측정기기·배경색이 좌우.

## 1. Document Information

- Study type: SR + meta-analysis with meta-regression (in vitro).
- Material: Resin-based composites (RBC); CIELAB color differences.
- Bleaching: HP and/or CP protocols.
- Included: 23 studies (from 1335 → 66 full-text → 23).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies whether bleaching damages/discolors existing composite restorations.
- Meta-regression separates true effect (bleaching) from confounders (methodology).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- MEDLINE/Scopus/Web of Science; English studies reporting CIELAB ΔE of RBC after bleaching.
- Meta-regression on study/experimental covariates.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- RBC color **does change** after bleaching but change is **not clinically significant**.
- Significant predictors of measured color change: **storage time** (p≤0.01), **color-measuring device** (p≤0.01), **background color** (p≤0.01).
- **Not** significant: bleaching agent and application time (p>0.01).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In vitro only — no oral aging, no marginal/surface degradation outcomes.
- Heterogeneous composites and measurement setups; abstract-only ingest.

## 6. Related Work

- de Geus 2025 — vital tooth bleaching efficacy/sensitivity (the tooth itself, not restorations).
- Resin color-stability literature in `wiki/resin/`.

## 7. Glossary

- **RBC**: resin-based composite (filling/restorative resin).
- **CIELAB / ΔE**: instrumental color space and color-difference metric.
- **Meta-regression**: regression across studies to find moderators of effect size.
