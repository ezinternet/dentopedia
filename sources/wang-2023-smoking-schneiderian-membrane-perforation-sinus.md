## Why Ingested

기존 [[wiki/sinus-lift/lateral/diaz-olivares-2021-schneiderian-membrane-perforation-sinus-lift]] 및 [[wiki/overviews/sinus-lift-lateral-2026-synthesis]]는 상악동거상술 (Sinus Floor Elevation, SFE)에서 슈나이더 막 천공 (Schneiderian Membrane Perforation, SMP)의 수복·해부학적 위험 예측을 다루지만, 흡연 (Smoking)이 천공 위험에 독립적으로 기여하는지에 대한 정량적 근거가 없었다. 본 SR+MA (Wang 2023)는 OR 1.58 (95% CI 1.10–2.25)를 제시해 흡연을 수술 전 조절 가능한 위험인자로 확립하며, OMS 개요의 예방 축에 직접 공급된다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 9 studies (n=1424); smoking is significantly associated with Schneiderian membrane perforation during maxillary sinus floor augmentation (OR 1.58, 95% CI 1.10–2.25, I²=5%).

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (9편, n=1,424): 흡연 (Smoking)이 상악동거상술 (Maxillary Sinus Floor Augmentation) 시 슈나이더 막 천공 (Schneiderian Membrane Perforation) 위험을 유의하게 증가시킴 — OR 1.58 (95% CI 1.10–2.25, I²=5%).

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First SR+MA quantifying smoking as a risk factor specifically for Schneiderian membrane perforation (슈나이더 막 천공, SMP) during maxillary sinus floor augmentation (상악동거상술, SFE).
2. Pooled OR 1.58 (95% CI 1.10–2.25) — a ~58% increase in perforation odds for smokers vs non-smokers.
3. Low heterogeneity (I²=5%, p=0.39) supports the consistency of this risk signal across studies.
4. No significant publication bias (Egger's test p=0.827).
5. Limitation: sparse data on cigarettes-per-day; dose–response relationship could not be established.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases searched**: PubMed, Web of Science, Embase, Cochrane Library
- **Inclusion criteria**: Adults (>18 years); studies with >10 participants; smoking status and perforation accurately recorded
- **Studies included**: 9 studies in systematic review; 8 in meta-analysis
  - 8 retrospective observational studies + 1 clinical trial
  - Total: 1,424 patients
- **Risk of bias**: Newcastle–Ottawa Scale (NOS); all 9 studies rated high quality
- **Statistical model**: Random-effects model (due to methodological differences across studies)
- **Heterogeneity statistic**: I² = 5% (low)
- **Effect measure**: Odds ratios (OR) with 95% confidence intervals
- **Software**: RevMan 5.4.1, Stata 15.0
- **Reporting standard**: PRISMA 2020

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Studies in SR | 9 |
| Studies in MA | 8 |
| Total patients | 1,424 |
| Pooled OR (smoking vs non-smoking) | 1.58 |
| 95% CI | 1.10–2.25 |
| I² | 5% |
| Egger's test p-value (publication bias) | 0.827 (non-significant) |

**Clinical interpretation**: Smokers face approximately 58% higher odds of sustaining a membrane perforation during sinus floor augmentation compared to non-smokers. The low I² indicates this is a consistent finding across the included retrospective cohorts.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Poor reporting of cigarettes per day — dose–response relationship not evaluable
- Most included studies are retrospective (8/9), limiting causal inference
- Heterogeneity in surgical techniques and sinus anatomies across included studies
- Authors call for prospective studies and standardized smoking-quantity reporting
