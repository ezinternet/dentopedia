## One-line Summary
J Clin Exp Dent 2018;10(12):e1192-7 (MBRU Dubai) — cross-sectional survey of 206 all-ceramic crown preparations by 154 Dubai private practitioners scanned with Carestream CS 3500; mean MD convergence 24.6° (SD 11.8), BL 32.6° — well above Shillingburg 6° ideal and even the 10-22° clinically realistic band.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Journal of Clinical and Experimental Dentistry 2018;10(12):e1192-1197
- Received: 04 September 2018
- Accepted: 25 October 2018
- Type: Cross-sectional clinical practice survey
- Affiliation: Hamdan Bin Mohamed College of Dental Medicine, Mohammed Bin Rashid University, Dubai, UAE

## 2. Key Contributions
- Real-world Dubai private-practice TOC values for all-ceramic bonded crowns
- Documents BL > MD convergence pattern in clinical setting
- Demonstrates persistent gap between textbook ideal (6°) and clinical reality

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Convenience sample of all-ceramic bonded crown preparations from private practitioners
- Cast scanning via Carestream CS 3500 intraoral scanner
- Digital image assessment for taper measurement
- MD and BL convergence calculated separately
- 154 dentists; 206 crown preparations (72 anterior, 134 posterior)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Mean MD convergence angle: 24.6° (SD 11.8°)
- Mean BL convergence angle: 32.6° (SD ≈11.8°)
- Both substantially exceed:
  - Shillingburg ideal 6°
  - Realistic 10-22° range (per Tiu 2015 SR)
- BL > MD direction pattern consistent with literature (anatomic + access constraints)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Convenience sample; not random
- Single geographic context (Dubai private practice)
- No outcome correlation (no link to crown survival/retention failure)
- All-ceramic bonded preps may tolerate higher TOC than cement-retained due to adhesive retention
