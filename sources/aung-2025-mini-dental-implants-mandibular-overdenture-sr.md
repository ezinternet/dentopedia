## One-line Summary
PRISMA SR+MA of mini dental implant (MDI; <3 mm diameter) retained overdentures (6 RCTs, 101 patients, 234 mini-implants): ball, bar, and ERA® attachments yielded similar marginal bone loss, while **telescopic attachments showed significantly more MBL (p<0.05)**; maximum bite force and implant survival were not significantly different across attachments; ball attachments had the lowest prosthetic maintenance.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First SR+MA dedicated to attachment systems specifically for **mini dental implant (MDI) retained overdentures**.
- Validates ball/bar/ERA as MBL-equivalent, **flags telescopic as worse for MBL on MDIs**.
- Confirms that MDIs offer a cost-effective, minimally invasive option for atrophic mandibular ridges.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Databases: PubMed, Cochrane Library, Scopus (search up to 25 Feb 2025).
- Inclusion: RCTs comparing ball vs other attachment in MDI overdentures.
- Risk-of-bias: Cochrane RoB tool for RCTs — 3 low-risk, 3 some-concern.
- Outcomes: primary = MBL; secondary = max bite force, survival, prosthetic maintenance/complications.
- Quantitative meta-analysis performed for MBL and bite force.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **6 RCTs included, 101 patients, 234 mini-implants** (search yield 561 papers).
- **MBL**: ball ≈ bar ≈ ERA® (no significant difference); **telescopic > others, statistically significant (p<0.05)**.
- **Maximum bite force**: no significant difference between attachments.
- **Survival rate**: high (consistent with prior MDI literature ~95%) and not influenced by attachment.
- **Prosthetic maintenance**: ball attachments lowest — telescopic required denture repair and nylon cap replacement multiple times over 1-yr follow-up; ball required only 2 events.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small body of evidence (6 RCTs, 101 patients).
- Heterogeneity in MDI brands, lengths, and prosthetic design.
- Three of six included studies had "some concerns" RoB.
- No long-term (≥5 yr) MA data on MDI-OD attachment comparison.

## 7. Glossary
- **MDI (Mini Dental Implant)**: dental implant <3 mm in diameter; FDA-approved since 1997 for both temporary and long-term use.
- **MBL (Marginal Bone Loss)**: radiographic crestal bone change at implant collar.
- **ERA® (Extra-coronal Resilient Attachment)**: stud-type attachment with replaceable nylon insert.
- **Telescopic attachment**: primary (fixed) + secondary (removable) crown pair; high satisfaction in full implants but more MBL on MDIs.
