## Why Ingested

This is the newest and largest systematic review (43 studies: 16 animal + 27 human, published up to May 2025) establishing the vitamin D–osseointegration subdomain, reporting that vitamin D deficiency is associated with up to a fourfold increase in early implant failures. It extends the earlier sibling SR [[implants/vitamin-d/werny-2022-vitamin-d-osseointegration-dental-implants-sr]] and the umbrella review [[implants/vitamin-d/tallon-2024-vitamin-d-osseointegration-umbrella-review]], and adds a modifiable systemic risk factor alongside the classic risk landscape in [[implants/moy-2005-dental-implant-failure-rates-risk]] and the smoking-EDIF evidence in [[implants/fan-2024-smoking-early-implant-failure-sr-ma]].

(Note: abstract-only — full text not retrieved from PMC; all claims below are grounded in the published abstract.)

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (43 studies = 16 animal + 27 human, search to May 2025) finding vitamin D deficiency associated with up to a fourfold increase in early implant failures, while pre-surgical supplementation enhanced osseointegration and bone-implant contact (BIC), even in high-risk diabetic patients.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(43편 = 동물 16편 + 사람 27편, 2025년 5월까지 검색)로, 비타민 D 결핍(Vitamin D deficiency)이 조기 임플란트 실패(Early Dental Implant Failure, EDIF)를 최대 4배까지 증가시키며, 수술 전 보충이 당뇨 등 고위험군에서도 골유착과 골-임플란트 접촉(Bone-to-Implant Contact, BIC)을 개선했다고 보고.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Largest synthesis to date of the vitamin D–osseointegration relationship: 43 included studies from 151 screened.
- Quantifies the deficiency signal: vitamin D deficiency associated with up to a fourfold increase in early implant failures.
- Separates the evidence by species: 16 animal and 27 human studies, with directionally concordant findings.
- Highlights supplementation as a modifiable, pre-operative intervention that improves osseointegration, BIC, and peri-implant bone preservation, including in diabetics.
- Recommends pre-operative screening and correction of vitamin D deficiency to optimize implant success.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Systematic review of in vivo experimental and clinical studies published up to May 15, 2025.
- **Screening**: 151 publications initially identified → 43 met inclusion criteria.
- **Included studies**:
  - Animal: 16 total — 9 investigating vitamin D supplementation before implant placement; 6 exploring vitamin D coatings on implant surfaces. Animal models included osteoporosis, diabetes mellitus, ultraviolet (UV) light deficiency, chronic kidney disease-induced uremia, and orchiectomy.
  - Human: 27 total — 3 case reports, 10 retrospective studies, 3 prospective case series, 8 prospective controlled trials (2–4 cohorts), and 3 randomized clinical trials (RCTs).
- **No meta-analysis** performed (narrative/systematic synthesis), hence `confidence: sr`.
- abstract-only — full text not retrieved; methodological detail beyond the abstract is not available.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Animal evidence**: A positive effect of vitamin D on implant osseointegration was found in 13 of the 16 studies.
- **Human evidence**: 22 of the 27 human studies supported a beneficial association between adequate vitamin D levels and improved implant osseointegration or reduced early implant failure.
- **Effect size signal**: Vitamin D deficiency associated with up to a fourfold increase in early implant failures.
- **Supplementation effects**: Pre-surgical vitamin D supplementation enhanced implant osseointegration, improved bone-implant-contact (BIC), promoted peri-implant bone preservation, and reduced early implant failures, even among high-risk populations (i.e., diabetics).
- **Implant-related parameters**: When pocket depths, radiographic marginal bone levels, or implant stability were measured, significantly poorer outcomes were consistently observed in vitamin D-deficient groups.
- **Mechanistic conclusion**: Evidence from both animal and human studies strongly indicates that vitamin D deficiency impairs both new bone formation and BIC.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- No meta-analysis; "up to fourfold" is a reported upper-bound association, not a pooled effect estimate.
- Human evidence is heterogeneous (case reports, retrospective, prospective, only 3 RCTs), limiting causal inference.
- Authors call for additional well-designed prospective clinical trials and RCTs to elucidate the extent of the correlation between serum vitamin D deficiency and implant failure risk.
- abstract-only here — risk-of-bias appraisal, exact serum 25(OH)D thresholds, and per-study effect sizes were not extracted from the full text.

## 7. Glossary

- **BIC (Bone-to-Implant Contact)**: Proportion of implant surface in direct contact with bone; a histomorphometric measure of osseointegration.
- **EDIF (Early Dental Implant Failure)**: Implant failure occurring before or shortly after loading, typically reflecting failed primary osseointegration.
- **Vitamin D deficiency**: Inadequate serum vitamin D status, here linked to impaired bone formation and reduced BIC.
- **Osseointegration**: Direct structural and functional connection between living bone and the surface of a load-bearing implant.
- **25(OH)D**: 25-hydroxyvitamin D, the standard serum marker of vitamin D status (referenced as the clinical screening target).
