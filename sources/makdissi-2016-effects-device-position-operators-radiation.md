## Why Ingested

Reinforces operator-positioning guidance for portable/handheld intraoral X-ray units — a held device removes the wall-mounted "nil exposure" baseline, so *how* the operator holds it (distance and orientation) directly governs scatter dose. Pairs with [[radiology/benavides-2023-patient-shielding-dentomaxillofacial-radiography]]: where that guidance modernizes patient-side shielding, this phantom/TLD study quantifies the *operator-side* dose consequence of device handling, closing the operator half of the dental radiation-safety picture for handheld units.

## One-line Summary

Phantom + TLD bench study of a NOMAD Pro handheld dental X-ray unit in 3 hold positions: operator scatter dose is negligible overall (highest 0.0310 mGy on the left palm, perpendicular-hold) and well below IRR-1999 limits, but device position significantly affects exposure — so positioning protocols, personal dosemeters, training and audits are required.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies **operator** (not patient) radiation dose for a handheld intraoral X-ray device, addressing a gap created because handheld units are held *within* the controlled area rather than fired from behind a barrier/wall-mount.
- Directly tests the clinical variable the operator controls: **how the device is held** (distance from body and orientation relative to the ground) across three defined positions.
- Translates bench measurements into a realistic occupational estimate using a workload assumption (100 intraoral radiographs/week × 46 weeks/year), framing significance against the Ionizing Radiation Regulations 1999 (IRR-1999) limits.
- Provides an evidence basis for operator-safety practice: personal dosimetry, positioning protocols, training, and regular audit.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Device**: NOMAD Pro Handheld X-ray system (Aribex Inc., Charlotte, NC) with integral lead shield (backscatter shield), fitted with a remote control and mounted on a tripod for reproducible geometry.
- **Patient simulation**: ATOM Max Dental and Diagnostic Phantom, model 711 HN (CIRS Inc., Norfolk, VA) simulating the patient head.
- **Operator simulation**: a mannequin carrying pre-calibrated thermoluminescent dosemeters (TLDs; Qados, Agar Scientific, Stansted, UK).
- **TLD placement**: near the eyes, and at thyroid, trunk, waist, hand (right finger + left palm) and feet; plus 3 TLDs for background radiation.
- **Three hold positions**:
  - **Position 1** — device close to operator's body, parallel to the ground.
  - **Position 2** — device away from the body, arms fully extended (~40 cm distance), parallel to the ground.
  - **Position 3** — device perpendicular to the ground, arms partially extended.
- **Exposure protocol**: 30 exposures of 1 s each per test scenario.
- **Occupational extrapolation**: estimated operator dose computed from an assumed workload of 100 intraoral radiographs/week, 46 weeks/year.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Background radiation**: 0.0110 mGy.
- **Peak measured dose** (after background subtraction): **0.0310 mGy on the palm of the left hand at Position 3** (perpendicular hold) — the highest of all body sites/positions.
- **Overall conclusion**: operator exposure with the handheld device is a **negligible increase**, remaining **well below IRR-1999 recommended levels**.
- **Key caveat**: this is nonetheless an increase relative to the **nil exposure** expected with a wall-mounted machine.
- **Position matters**: device position relative to the operator has a **significant effect** on overall operator exposure.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Bench/phantom design**: mannequin + phantom geometry with a tripod-mounted device idealizes the hold; real-world freehand variability (tremor, fatigue, awkward patient positions) is not captured.
- **Single device/unit**: results pertain to one NOMAD Pro unit with its integral backscatter shield; generalizability to other handheld brands/shield designs is untested.
- **Estimate dependence**: occupational dose is an extrapolation from an assumed workload (100/week, 46 weeks/year); higher-volume or different practice patterns would scale the estimate.

## 7. Glossary

- **Handheld/portable X-ray device**: a battery-powered intraoral X-ray unit held by the operator during exposure (e.g., NOMAD Pro), rather than wall-mounted.
- **Integral lead/backscatter shield**: the leaded ring/shield on the device aperture that intercepts backscattered radiation toward the operator.
- **TLD (thermoluminescent dosemeter)**: a passive radiation detector that stores absorbed dose and releases it as light when heated, used for personal/occupational dosimetry.
- **Controlled area**: a designated zone around an X-ray source with restricted access for radiation safety; handheld units complicate this because the source is held by the operator.
- **mGy (milligray)**: SI unit of absorbed radiation dose.
- **IRR-1999 (Ionizing Radiation Regulations 1999)**: UK regulations setting occupational and public dose limits and radiation-protection requirements.
- **ALARA**: "As Low As Reasonably Achievable" — the guiding principle for minimizing radiation exposure.
