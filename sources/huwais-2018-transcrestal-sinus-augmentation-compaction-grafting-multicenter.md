## One-line Summary
Multicentre retrospective study (3 centres, 222 patients, 261 implants, follow-up 6–64 months, mean 35 months) of osseodensification (OD) transcrestal sinus augmentation via compaction grafting: 7.0 ± 2.49 mm vertical gain from 5.4 ± 1.9 mm baseline RBH, zero membrane perforations, no late failures, 97% cumulative survival.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Multicentre retrospective clinical study
- **Journal**: International Journal of Oral & Maxillofacial Implants 2018;33(6):1305–1311
- **DOI**: 10.11607/jomi.6770; PMID 30427961
- **Authors**: Salah Huwais (inventor), Ziv Mazor, Andreas L. Ioannou, Howard Gluckman, Rodrigo Neiva
- **Sample**: 222 patients / 261 implants across three treatment centres, May 2012 – September 2017
- **Primary Question**: How effective and predictable is osseodensification transcrestal sinus augmentation with compaction grafting and simultaneous implant placement, over up to 5 years of follow-up?

## 2. Key Contributions
- Largest multicentre OD transcrestal sinus dataset to that date: 261 implants, mean follow-up 35 months, range 6–64 months — the headline clinical evidence cited in subsequent reviews (Cobo-Vázquez 2025, Starch-Jensen 2025).
- Baseline residual subsinus bone height (RBH): 5.4 ± 1.9 mm — i.e., the technique was applied to sites that would otherwise have required a lateral window in many traditional protocols.
- Significant mean vertical bone gain of 7.0 ± 2.49 mm after sinus augmentation — comparable to lateral-window grafting at substantially lower morbidity.
- **Zero sinus membrane perforations across 261 cases** — the most clinically striking safety claim and the basis for OD's "minimally invasive" positioning in sinus surgery.
- **No late implant failures** between 6 and 64 months of follow-up; cumulative implant survival 97%.
- Demonstrates the dual function of OD burs in sinus lift: simultaneously preparing the osteotomy and hydraulically elevating the Schneiderian membrane via the autografted compacted bone column.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
A retrospective chart review of consecutive patients treated at three centres between May 2012 and September 2017 using the OD transcrestal sinus augmentation protocol. All patients had simultaneous implant placement (one-stage). Summary statistics — means for continuous variables and percentages for categorical variables — are reported. Inclusion criteria, exclusion criteria, specific bone graft materials used, implant brands and macrogeometries, and adjudication of failures were not extractable from the available abstract/text (paywalled body).

The OD protocol uses tapered multi-fluted Densah burs run counterclockwise (densifying mode) with copious irrigation. The compacted bone column at the apex of the osteotomy is advanced through the sinus floor, gently lifting the Schneiderian membrane while simultaneously autografting bone (with or without added biomaterial) into the elevated space. The implant is placed in the same visit.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **n** = 261 implants in 222 patients across 3 centres
- **Follow-up**: 6–64 months; mean 35 months
- **Baseline RBH**: 5.4 ± 1.9 mm
- **Vertical bone gain**: 7.0 ± 2.49 mm (significant increase)
- **Sinus membrane perforations**: 0 / 261
- **Late implant failures (6–64 months)**: 0
- **Cumulative implant survival**: 97%
- Marginal bone loss (MBL), insertion torque, ISQ, complication breakdown, and graft-material subgroup analyses are not in the available text excerpt — paywalled body required.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Retrospective design — no randomisation, no control arm, and no comparison to alternative crestal techniques in this cohort.
- Investigator-led study with strong inventor involvement (Huwais is first author and patent holder) — conflict-of-interest considerations apply.
- Heterogeneous case mix across three centres; surgical and grafting protocols may have varied.
- Loss to follow-up and survival analysis methodology not extractable from the available text.
- Zero membrane perforations across 261 sites is a remarkable finding; some readers should consider whether silent perforations (radiographically occult) might have been under-detected.
- Patient-reported outcomes, sinus-related complications other than perforation (sinusitis, graft migration), and CBCT-based volumetric gain not reported in the available text.

## 7. Glossary
- **Transcrestal sinus augmentation**: Sinus floor elevation through the implant osteotomy itself, without lateral window osteotomy.
- **Compaction grafting**: Use of compaction-drilling kinematics (osseodensification) to autograft displaced bone particles along the osteotomy walls and into the elevated sinus space.
- **Residual bone height (RBH)**: Native subsinus alveolar bone height in mm pre-procedure; primary indication-defining metric.
- **Schneiderian membrane**: Sinus mucosal lining; perforation is the principal complication of any crestal sinus lift.
- **OD (Osseodensification)**: Huwais counterclockwise Densah bur compaction technique.
