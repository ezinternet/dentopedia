## One-line Summary

SR+MA (29 studies, n=29,212): uninterrupted DOACs carry 21% lower post-dentoalveolar-surgery bleeding risk than VKAs (RR 0.79, 95% CI 0.68–0.92), driven mainly by dabigatran (RR 0.40); both DOACs (RR 3.23) and VKAs (RR 3.35) raise bleeding vs no-anticoagulant controls, but severe bleeding is rare. GRADE very low.

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (29편, n=29,212): 비중단(uninterrupted) DOAC는 치조골 수술 후 출혈 위험이 VKA보다 21% 낮음 (RR 0.79) — dabigatran(RR 0.40)이 주도; DOAC(RR 3.23)·VKA(RR 3.35) 모두 비복용군 대비 출혈 증가하나 중증 출혈은 드묾. 근거수준 very low.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First meta-analysis to quantify the **DOAC vs VKA** post-dentoalveolar-surgery bleeding effect size: DOACs cut bleeding risk by **21%** (RR 0.79, 95% CI 0.68–0.92, P=0.002, I²=0%).
- Drug-level subgroup resolution: the DOAC advantage is **driven almost entirely by dabigatran** (RR 0.40); rivaroxaban, apixaban, edoxaban show no significant difference vs VKAs.
- Both anticoagulant classes roughly **triple** bleeding risk vs non-anticoagulated controls (DOAC RR 3.23; VKA RR 3.35), yet severe bleeding requiring hospitalization/causing irreversible harm was rarely reported.
- Concomitant antiplatelet therapy did **not** significantly raise bleeding risk in subgroup analysis.
- Supports **uninterrupted** anticoagulation with local hemostasis as a viable, relatively safer approach.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Databases: PubMed, Embase, Web of Science, CENTRAL through 28 Sep 2024 + manual reference-list search. PICOS framework, PRISMA.
- Population: adult DOAC/VKA users scheduled for dentoalveolar surgery (extraction, implant placement, alveolar bone reconstruction). Comparators: other anticoagulant class or non-anticoagulated patients.
- Two independent reviewers (screening, extraction, bias); third reviewer for disputes.
- Quality: **ROBINS-I** (7 domains); certainty by **GRADE**.
- Pooling: relative risk (RR) via **fixed-effects model** (RevMan 5.4); heterogeneity via Q test + I² (>50% moderate, >75% substantial). Sensitivity + subgroup analyses; publication bias by Begg funnel + Egger test.
- Included: 29 studies (13 cohort, 14 case-control, 2 cross-sectional), 29,212 patients total. No RCTs (ethical constraints).

- **GRADE certainty: very low** — observational designs, imprecision; interpret with caution.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- ~1/3 of studies retrospective (record-based) → inherent bias; no RCTs available.
- Bleeding definition varied across studies (over/under-estimation possible).
- Variable procedural invasiveness (simple extraction vs impacted-tooth removal) → residual heterogeneity.
- Edoxaban subgroup underpowered (few small studies, I²=60%).
- Authors call for higher-quality (ideally RCT) evidence.

## 6. Related Work

- Pharmacokinetic rationale: VKA half-life >55 h (no circadian variation) vs DOAC 7–17 h; dabigatran reversible (idarucizumab) and locally inhibits thrombin at wound, whereas factor Xa inhibitors are harder to reverse.
- Guideline divergence: American College of Surgeons (stop DOAC 24–48 h, resume 24 h post) vs European Heart Rhythm Association (last dose 18–24 h pre, resume 6 h post for minimal-risk procedures).
- Curto et al. bleeding-risk procedure stratification (low-risk: simple extraction, <45 min vs moderate–high: >3 teeth or >45 min).
- Thromboembolic rebound on discontinuation (Vene et al.: ~20-fold short-term thromboembolic risk after stopping dabigatran/rivaroxaban).

## 7. Glossary

- **DOAC** — Direct Oral Anticoagulant (dabigatran [thrombin inhibitor]; apixaban, rivaroxaban, edoxaban [factor Xa inhibitors]).
- **VKA** — Vitamin K Antagonist (warfarin, acenocoumarol, phenprocoumon).
- **RR** — Relative Risk (risk ratio); <1 favors first-listed group.
- **INR** — International Normalized Ratio; local hemostasis safe at INR ≤4.0.
- **ROBINS-I** — Risk Of Bias In Non-randomized Studies of Interventions.
- **Dentoalveolar surgery** — extraction, implant placement, alveolar bone reconstruction.
