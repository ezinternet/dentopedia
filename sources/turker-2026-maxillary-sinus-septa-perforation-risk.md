## One-line Summary
Retrospective CBCT analysis of 300 individuals: maxillary sinus septa were predominantly mediolateral (91.7%) and located in the premolar region (40.0%); using a modified seven-class radiographic system, the most frequent predicted Schneiderian membrane (SM) perforation risk classes were Class I (33.0%) and Class III (32.7%); moderate predicted risk was higher in females (p = 0.011) and septum location was significantly associated with predicted risk (p < 0.001), while posterior dentition status and septum orientation were not.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Characterizes septal morphology (presence, orientation, location) in a Turkish cohort using CBCT.
- Applies a **modified seven-class radiographic risk system** to stratify predicted SM perforation likelihood as low / moderate / high.
- Examines effect of **posterior dentition status (< 2 vs ≥ 2 remaining teeth)** on septal anatomy and perforation risk — finding no significant relationship.
- Identifies **septum location** (not orientation) as the morphologic feature most associated with predicted perforation risk.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Sample**: 300 individuals with adequate posterior maxillary visualization on CBCT.
- **Exclusions**: pathologic sinus formations, prior sinus surgery, fractures.
- **Variables recorded**:
  - Septum **presence**.
  - **Orientation**: mediolateral vs anteroposterior.
  - **Location**: premolar (anterior), premolar–molar, molar (posterior), or multiple.
  - **Posterior dentition status**: < 2 teeth vs ≥ 2 teeth in posterior maxilla.
- **Risk classification**: modified seven-class CBCT-based system, collapsed into low / moderate / high categories.
- **No intraoperative confirmation** — risk is *predicted*, not validated against actual SM perforation.
- **Statistics**: chi-square / Fisher; alpha 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Demographics**: mean age 43.5 ± 13.3 years; 54.7% male.
- **Septum orientation**: **91.7% mediolateral** (the surgically relevant orientation that crosses the lateral window).
- **Septum location**: premolar region most common at **40.0%**.
- **Predicted SM perforation risk distribution**:
  - **Class I**: 33.0% (most frequent).
  - **Class III**: 32.7%.
- **Sex effect**: moderate predicted perforation risk significantly higher in females (**p = 0.011**).
- **Location effect**: septum location significantly associated with predicted risk (**p < 0.001**).
- **No significant association**: posterior dentition status or septum orientation with predicted risk.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- **No intraoperative validation** — outcomes are *radiographically predicted*, not measured against actual perforation events.
- Single-center Turkish cohort; generalizability uncertain.
- Modified seven-class system is one of several proposed radiographic classifications; cross-system comparability not addressed.
- No CBCT measurement of septum height/thickness or lateral wall thickness — also known perforation risk factors.
- Authors recommend prospective CBCT-surgical correlation studies.

## 7. Glossary
- **Maxillary sinus septum (Underwood septum)**: bony partition projecting from sinus floor/walls into the antrum; complicates lateral window osteotomy and membrane elevation.
- **Mediolateral orientation**: septum running from medial to lateral wall — typically crosses the lateral access window and is the most surgically problematic.
- **Anteroposterior orientation**: septum running parallel to the alveolar crest; less obstructive of the lateral window.
- **SM (Schneiderian Membrane)**: respiratory mucosa lining the maxillary sinus.
- **Modified seven-class radiographic risk system**: CBCT-based classification scheme stratifying perforation likelihood by septal anatomy; collapsed into low / moderate / high.
- **Lateral window SFA**: lateral approach to maxillary sinus floor elevation.
- **CBCT**: cone-beam computed tomography.
