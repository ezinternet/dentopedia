## Why Ingested

Micro-CT comparison of collagen plug vs empty socket healing in a rabbit model — quantifies volumetric bone remodeling kinetics at 6 time-points (2, 7, 15, 30, 60, 90 days) with three-dimensional precision not achievable in clinical studies. Provides the mechanistic basis for understanding why collagen plug alone can preserve socket volume. [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/nisar-2020-collagen-plug-socket-preservation]]

## One-line Summary

Rabbit model micro-CT study (n=36, 6 time-points) comparing collagen plug vs empty socket: collagen plug group showed −4.6% volumetric bone change vs −58.1% in controls at 90 days, demonstrating near-complete prevention of alveolar collapse.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Medicine (MDPI)
- **Volume/Issue**: 11(9), 2493
- **Study design**: Animal experiment — randomized, two-arm (collagen plug vs natural healing)
- **Model**: 36 adult male New Zealand white rabbits (avg. 2 kg); lower incisor extraction
- **Time-points**: 2, 7, 15, 30, 60, 90 days post-extraction (6 groups of 6 animals each)
- **Imaging**: Micro-CT (IRIS scanner, Inviscan Sas; 59 µm voxel; 80 kV, 1 mA, 112 s)
- **Ethics**: Ethical Commission for Animal Welfare, Pisa, Italy (IRB 0035123/2017)
- **Part of larger study**: Histologic and molecular results already published separately

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides 3D micro-CT volumetric data at 6 time-points — resolves kinetics of bone remodeling in detail not possible with clinical CBCT or histology alone
- Collagen plug group showed near-zero volumetric bone loss (−4.6%) vs severe collapse in controls (−58.1%) at 3 months
- Non-destructive measurement allows longitudinal analysis of same bone block structure
- Establishes collagen plug (without bone graft or membrane) as highly effective in preventing socket volume loss in a controlled model

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Surgical procedure**:
- Single lower incisor extraction per rabbit under general anesthesia (alphaxolone) + local anesthetic (Xylocaine)
- Test group: Socket filled with native heterologous bovine collagen type 1 (lyophilized, sterile — ACE plugs); no suture applied
- Control group: Socket left empty; tension-free suture closure

**Micro-CT imaging**:
- Block resection of anterior mandible including extraction socket and contralateral socket (internal reference)
- Scanning: IRIS micro-CT, isotropic voxel 59 µm, 80 kV, 1 mA
- Reconstruction: Cone-beam filtered back projection (FBP), standard kernel, beam hardening correction
- Bone threshold: ~500 HU (between bone marrow and cortical bone)

**Measurements**:
- Primary endpoint: % bone volume change (ΔBV) from baseline to each time-point within cylindrical ROI (coronal to 10 mm apical)
- Secondary: Linear bucco-lingual dimension changes at 1 mm intervals from crest

**Sample size rationale**: E-value method (E = total n − total groups = 24; slightly >20, acceptable at 10% mortality risk).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Time-point | Control ΔBV | Collagen plug ΔBV |
|---|---|---|
| 2 days | modest loss | minimal |
| 15 days | progressive collapse | stable |
| 30 days | ongoing resorption | minimal change |
| 60 days | severe | near-stable |
| 90 days | −58.1% | −4.6% |

- Collagen group maintained near-baseline volume throughout 90-day observation
- Control group exhibited progressive buccal plate collapse beginning by day 15
- Linear bucco-lingual dimension changes were significantly smaller in collagen group at all time-points
- Collagen plugs resorb within ~15 days yet volumetric protection persisted — suggests early clot stabilization and vascularization scaffold role rather than physical space maintenance beyond resorption

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Animal model (rabbit) — incisor is continuously erupting, which differs from human molar socket biology
- No bone graft material in test group — cannot compare collagen plug to DBBM or other xenografts
- Terminal sacrifice design — no repeated measures; intra-animal variability not controlled over time
- No histomorphometric bone quality data from this paper (published separately)
- 59 µm voxel may underdetect very fine trabecular changes

## 6. Related Work

- Collagen plug resorbs in 15 days (from paper); yet volume protection is sustained — likely via clot stabilization role
- Supports use of collagen plug as a simple, low-cost scaffold without requiring graft material
- Histological companion paper published separately (same 36 rabbits)

## 7. Glossary

- **Micro-CT (micro-computed tomography)**: High-resolution 3D X-ray imaging of bone blocks ex vivo; gold standard for preclinical bone morphometry
- **ΔBV (% bone volume change)**: Primary endpoint — fractional change in bone voxel count within the ROI from baseline
- **ROI (region of interest)**: Cylindrical volume centered on socket centroid, from crest to 10 mm apically
- **ACE plug**: Native heterologous bovine collagen type 1, lyophilized and sterile — used as test material
- **E-value**: Statistical metric for sample size adequacy in ANOVA-designed animal studies (should be 10–20)
- **Bundle bone**: The thin layer of alveolar bone lining the tooth socket (lamina dura) — first lost after extraction
