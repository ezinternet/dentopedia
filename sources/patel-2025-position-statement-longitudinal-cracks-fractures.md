## Why Ingested

The cracked-tooth category has several primary-evidence and review pages but lacks an authoritative society consensus framing the whole "longitudinal crack/fracture" spectrum and its standardised terminology. This ESE position statement (Patel 2025) anchors definitions (craze line → cracked tooth → fractured cusp → split tooth → VRF → split root), supplies the consensus rationale for abandoning the "cracked tooth syndrome" label, and serves as the authoritative umbrella over [[cracked-tooth/raj-2025-cracked-tooth-syndrome-diagnostic-dilemma]] and [[cracked-tooth/li-2021-cracked-tooth-syndrome-etiology-review]]; it also frames the survival data synthesised in [[cracked-tooth/zhang-2024-cracked-teeth-treatment-outcomes-sr-ma]]. Sourced from PubMed/PMC (PMC11812625).

## One-line Summary

European Society of Endodontology (ESE) consensus position statement defining the spectrum of longitudinal cracks/fractures (craze line, cracked tooth, fractured cusp, split tooth, VRF, split root) and recommending early diagnosis, cuspal coverage restoration, occlusal/parafunction management, and abandonment of the term "cracked tooth syndrome."

## 한줄요약

유럽근관치료학회(ESE) 합의 입장문 — 치아 종축 균열·파절(crack/fracture) 스펙트럼(craze line, 균열치 CT, 파절교두, split tooth, 수직치근파절 VRF, split root)을 표준 정의하고, 조기 진단·교두피개 수복(cuspal coverage)·교합/이상기능 관리를 권고하며 "cracked tooth syndrome(균열치 증후군)"이라는 용어는 폐기할 것을 권고.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Standardised terminology** for the longitudinal crack/fracture spectrum (Table 1): craze line, cracked tooth (CT), fractured cusp, split tooth (ST), vertical root fracture (VRF), split root.
2. **Recommends abandoning "cracked tooth syndrome"** — the broad variability in presentation makes a single "syndrome" label misleading.
3. Separates **VRF in non-root-filled teeth (NRFT)** from **VRF in root-filled teeth (RFT)** as distinct entities with different epidemiology, aetiology, and management.
4. Provides an evidence-graded management framework keyed to **pulpal/periapical status** (normal pulp → reversible pulpitis → irreversible pulpitis/necrosis) and to **restorability/root number**.
5. Emphasises **occlusal force / parafunction management** as a cross-cutting (universal) consideration for all crack/fracture types.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Consensus view of an ESE expert committee, based on current clinical/scientific evidence plus the committee's collective reflective practice (not a systematic review or meta-analysis).
- Organised by entity: Cracked Teeth → Split Tooth → VRF in NRFT → VRF in RFT, each with Prevalence / Aetiology / Clinical features / Radiographic features / Management. Closes with "Universal considerations" (occlusion) and overall conclusion.
- Diagnostic aids endorsed: bite test (e.g., Tooth Sleuth) in vital teeth, fibre-optic transillumination, methylene-blue staining, magnification (dental operating microscope/loupes), removal of existing restorations, CBCT only when clinical/periapical radiograph (PR) assessment is inconclusive.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

(All figures as reported in the statement, citing its referenced literature.)

- **CT prevalence**: visible coronal CT reported as high as 70%; most prevalent >40 y; posterior teeth (maxillary pre/molars, mandibular molars). Increasing incidence in unrestored teeth over the last two decades; rise reported since COVID-19.
- **CT symptoms** (practice-based study, 2858 teeth / 209 US dentists): 45% symptomatic — pain to cold 37%, biting pain 16%, spontaneous pain 11%.
- **CT radiographs**: only 2% of CTs with vital pulps showed a crack radiographically.
- **CT periodontal probing**: narrow probing <6 mm associated with CT; deeper for VRF. 5+ mm probing associated with reduced survival.
- **Root-filled CT outcomes**: survival 84–88% over 1–5 y; success 76–82% over 1–2 y. Endodontic intervention after restorative management: 7.7–20%.
- **Intracanal barrier apical to crack + cuspal coverage + occlusal management** (Davis & Shariff): 4-year survival 96.6%.
- **VRF in NRFT**: incidence 6–37%; male predilection, >50 y; first molars (MB root maxillary, mesial root mandibular); majority of research in Chinese population.
- **VRF in RFT**: extraction prevalence 4–32%; VRF causes 13–21% of RFT extractions. Pathognomonic advanced sign = isolated narrow deep pocket on both sides of a root + multiple sinus tracts.
- **CBCT limitation**: cannot reliably detect VRFs within the root (fractures typically 50–100 μm vs insufficient image resolution); useful for periradicular bone-loss confirmation.
- **Root resection survival** (extrapolated from perio-affected teeth, no VRF-specific data): 90.6% at 10 y, 96.8% at 15 y.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Consensus/expert opinion document, not a systematic review — recommendations partly based on collective reflective practice.
- Unclear how long asymptomatic untreated CT remains stable (studies only monitored 1–3 years).
- No clear evidence on the single best restorative approach for CT.
- Limited evidence on VRF-in-NRFT treatment strategies; no survival data specific to root resection/amputation for VRF in RFT; limited evidence for intentional replantation/extraoral bonding.
- Most critical aetiological factor(s) for VRF remain unknown.

## 7. Glossary

- **Craze line**: superficial enamel-confined crack, no structural compromise; treat only for aesthetics.
- **Cracked tooth (CT)**: crack extending into dentine, unknown depth, may reach subcrestally; +/- pulpal involvement.
- **Fractured cusp**: (in)complete fracture along bucco/lingual groove and mesio-distal direction, usually involving marginal ridges.
- **Split tooth (ST)**: complete mesio-distal separation of the whole tooth into two parts; unfavourable prognosis → extraction.
- **Vertical root fracture (VRF)**: incomplete longitudinal (axial) root fracture involving cementum, dentine, canal space.
- **Split root**: complete VRF with visible separation into segments.
- **NRFT / RFT**: non-root-filled / root-filled teeth.
- **AP**: apical periodontitis.
- **Pericervical dentine**: cervical dentine whose preservation resists (para)functional occlusal forces.
- **Cuspal coverage restoration**: restoration covering cusps to limit crack propagation and improve longevity.
