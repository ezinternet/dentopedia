## One-line Summary
Narrative review covering UV photofunctionalization effects on osseointegration, soft tissue attachment, and bacterial biofilm inhibition on titanium implants; introduces VUV (1-minute) as clinical advancement.

## 1. Document Information
J Prosthodont Res 2025;69(2):136-152. UCLA Weintraub Center. Received 2024-03-27, accepted 2024-05-21.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive synthesis of UV photofunctionalization (PF) across three interfaces: bone, soft tissue, bacteria
- Removes hydrocarbon pellicles from Ti surfaces → restores superhydrophilicity and electropositivity
- VUV (vacuum UV, 1-minute) as next-generation PF method overcoming 15-min UV limitation
- Covers abutment/crown surfaces in addition to implant body

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Narrative review synthesising in vitro, animal, and clinical studies on UV-PF of titanium.

## 4. Key Results
- Osseointegration: UV-PF restores BIC toward ~100%; removes hydrocarbon (carbon content <20%); generates superhydrophilic surface (contact angle ≈0°)
- Soft tissue: UV-PF enhances epithelial cell attachment on abutment materials
- Bacteria: UV-PF reduces bacterial attachment and biofilm formation on titanium surfaces
- VUV (185+254 nm, 1 min): achieves equivalent surface transformation vs 15-min UV; clinically practical

## 5. Limitations
Narrative review; heterogeneous study designs; long-term RCT data on VUV absent.

## 6. Related Work
Ogawa 2025 (3D Theory), Hirota 2020 (7-yr clinical), Minamikawa 2016 (long-term aging), Dorigatti de Avila 2015 (bacteria).

## 7. Glossary
VUV: Vacuum ultraviolet (wavelength 185 nm + 254 nm); PF: photofunctionalization; hydrocarbon pellicle: carbon-containing contaminant layer on Ti surface.
