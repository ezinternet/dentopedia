## Why Ingested

[[wiki/drug/moore-2015-single-dose-oral-analgesics-cochrane-overview]]는 단회용량 경구진통제의 효능을 광범위하게 다루지만 etoricoxib 개별 약물의 발치 후 통증 효능은 따로 정량화하지 않았다. 본 2021 SR+MA는 사랑니 수술 후 etoricoxib 단독을 비선택 NSAID와 직접 비교해 그 gap을 확장(extends)하며, 한국 치과 발치 후 진통 프로토콜 결정에 직접 적용 가능하다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 8 high-quality trials (6 pooled) found etoricoxib (120 mg the dominant dose) significantly reduced the NUMBER of patients needing rescue analgesia after third molar surgery vs nonselective NSAIDs (p=0.0004; vs ibuprofen 400 mg p=0.00001), with no significant difference in adverse effects.

## 한줄요약

8편의 고품질 임상시험(6편 메타분석)을 종합한 SR+MA로, etoricoxib(주로 120 mg)가 사랑니 수술 후 비선택적 NSAID 대비 구제 진통제가 필요한 환자 수를 유의하게 줄였고(p=0.0004, 이부프로펜 400 mg 대비 p=0.00001), 이상반응에는 유의한 차이가 없었다.

- **Type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (Review Manager 5.3)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First meta-analysis to isolate the analgesic effectiveness of etoricoxib alone (vs nonselective NSAIDs) after third molar surgery — earlier work (González-Barnadas) pooled coxibs as a class.
- Demonstrates etoricoxib lowers the proportion of patients requiring rescue analgesia.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Search: PubMed + Google Scholar; English/Spanish; clinical trials to July 2020.
- PICO: patients undergoing third molar removal; etoricoxib vs COX-2 nonselective NSAIDs; outcomes = total rescue analgesic consumption, number of patients using rescue analgesics, VAS pain, global treatment assessment.
- Quality: Cochrane risk-of-bias tool; only low-RoB (high-quality) trials used for data extraction.
- Statistics: inverse variance + standardised mean difference (continuous); Mantel-Haenszel + odds ratio (dichotomous); p≤0.05 significant.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- 149 records → 11 met PICO → 8 high-quality (qualitative) → 6 in quantitative meta-analysis.
- Etoricoxib dose 120 mg used in all quality studies (range 60–240 mg); mostly single-dose.
- Qualitative: etoricoxib better than ibuprofen (2 trials) and diclofenac (1 trial); similar efficacy in 3/8 (2 vs ibuprofen, 1 vs naproxen sodium).
- Number needing rescue analgesia: significantly fewer with etoricoxib vs NSAIDs (p=0.0004); vs ibuprofen 400 mg p=0.00001.
- Total rescue consumption (Calvo, single study): mean difference −0.44 (95% CI −1.38 to 0.5; p=0.36, NS).
- Global treatment assessment: trend favoring etoricoxib, not statistically significant.
- Adverse effects (6 trials): no significant difference vs nonselective NSAIDs.
- Mechanistic context: etoricoxib COX-2 selectivity ratio = 106 (vs ibuprofen 0.2, diclofenac 3); ED50 = 3.27 mg/kg in mice.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Few high-quality trials; several outcomes from a single study (e.g., total rescue consumption).
- Limited databases (PubMed + Google Scholar only); possible publication bias.
- Predominantly single-dose, short-horizon postoperative analgesia data.

## 7. Glossary

- **Etoricoxib**: highly selective COX-2 inhibitor NSAID (COX-2 selectivity ratio ~106).
- **Rescue analgesia**: additional analgesic taken when the study drug is insufficient — a dichotomous efficacy proxy.
- **VAS**: Visual Analog Scale for pain intensity.
- **TOPAR**: total pain relief.
- **OR**: odds ratio (Mantel-Haenszel) for dichotomous outcomes.
