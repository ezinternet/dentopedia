## Why Ingested

블랙 스테인이 우식 경험 저하와 관련된다는 기존 서사적 리뷰([[caries/zyla-2015-black-stain-dental-caries-review]])의 주장을 최초로 메타분석 수준에서 정량화한 연구. OR 0.67 (95% CI 0.54–0.82)라는 구체적 수치를 제공하여 임상 인식 전환의 근거로 사용 가능.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (14 studies, children) finding black stain associated with lower odds of caries (OR 0.67, 95% CI 0.54–0.82) and fewer affected teeth (MD −0.98) and surfaces (MD −2.34), though causality direction remains uncertain.

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (14편, 소아): 블랙 스테인 (Black Stain, BS) 아동에서 우식 발생 교차비 (Odds Ratio, OR) 0.67 (95% CI 0.54–0.82) — 우식 경험 저하와 유의하게 관련; 인과 방향은 불명확.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of the Egyptian Public Health Association
- **Year**: 2022
- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Number of included studies**: 14
- **Population**: Children with and without black stain
- **Primary outcome**: Caries experience (likelihood, number of teeth, number of surfaces)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First meta-analysis to pool quantitative data on the black stain–caries association in children
- Provides OR, mean difference (MD) for teeth and surfaces affected — concrete clinical effect sizes
- Distinguishes between "BS is protective" vs "low-caries microbiome favors BS formation" as competing explanatory models
- Moderate heterogeneity (I²=37%) for the primary outcome (likelihood of caries) — more acceptable than surface/tooth counts (I²=79–85%)
- Supports the hypothesis that BS microflora (Actinomyces-dominated) and salivary chemistry mediate the association

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Systematic review following PRISMA guidelines
- Databases searched: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, and others (search details in abstract-level data)
- Inclusion: observational studies comparing caries experience between BS and non-BS children
- 14 studies included in quantitative synthesis
- Statistical pooling: random-effects model (DerSimonian-Laird)
- Heterogeneity assessed via I² statistic
- Outcomes:
  1. Likelihood of having caries (OR)
  2. Number of teeth affected (MD)
  3. Number of surfaces affected (MD)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Primary outcome — likelihood of caries:**
- OR = 0.67 (95% CI 0.54–0.82), p < 0.001
- I² = 37% (moderate heterogeneity — acceptable)
- Interpretation: children with BS have ~33% lower odds of having caries vs non-BS children

**Secondary outcome — number of teeth affected:**
- MD = −0.98 (95% CI −1.54 to −0.42)
- I² = 79% (substantial heterogeneity)
- Children with BS had approximately 1 fewer caries-affected tooth

**Secondary outcome — number of surfaces affected:**
- MD = −2.34 (95% CI −4.23 to −0.44)
- I² = 85% (substantial heterogeneity)
- Children with BS had approximately 2.3 fewer caries-affected surfaces

**Mechanistic interpretation:**
- Not established whether BS itself is directly protective (antimicrobial iron compounds, protective saliva) or whether children with constitutionally low-caries microbiome are simultaneously predisposed to BS formation
- Actinomyces-dominated plaque: lower acidogenic potential → reduced demineralization
- Salivary factors (calcium, buffering capacity) in BS patients may explain both the stain susceptibility and caries resistance

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only data available for this summary (full_text: false)
- High heterogeneity for tooth and surface count outcomes (I²=79–85%) limits confidence in precise MD estimates
- Most underlying studies cross-sectional — cannot establish causality
- Variation in BS diagnostic criteria across studies (visual, photographic, index-based)
- Publication bias not fully characterized
- Future: longitudinal RCTs or cohort studies tracking BS presence → caries incidence over time; microbiome sequencing to clarify whether BS microflora is mechanistically protective

## 6. Related Work

- [[caries/zyla-2015-black-stain-dental-caries-review]] — Narrative review (2001–2014) providing the biological mechanistic framework (Actinomyces microflora, Fe/S chemistry, salivary properties) that this SR+MA's quantitative findings build upon
- Koch et al.: prevalence data on black stain in Scandinavian pediatric populations (likely included in this SR+MA)

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Black stain (BS) | Extrinsic dark cervical pigmentation; line or coalescent dots |
| OR (Odds Ratio) | Ratio of odds of outcome (caries) in BS vs non-BS group; OR <1 = lower odds in BS group |
| MD (Mean Difference) | Pooled difference in continuous outcome (teeth/surfaces) between BS and non-BS groups |
| I² | Heterogeneity statistic; >75% = high; 37% for primary outcome = moderate |
| Actinomyces | Dominant genus in BS microflora; gram-positive filamentous bacteria with low cariogenicity |
| dmft/DMFT | Decayed, Missing, Filled Teeth index (primary / permanent dentition) |
| Random-effects model | Meta-analytic pooling that accounts for between-study variance; appropriate when I²>0 |
