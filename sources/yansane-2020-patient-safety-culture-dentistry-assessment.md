## Why Ingested

Empirical measurement of patient safety culture in dental settings, quantifying how dentistry lags medicine on most safety-culture dimensions while leading in teamwork and organizational learning. Reinforces [[practice-management/schmutz-2019-teamwork-performance-healthcare-sr-ma]] by treating teamwork as a measurable safety-culture dimension and providing the dental-clinic baseline that team-performance interventions would target. (Note: this is abstract-only — full text not retrieved.)

## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional survey (modified MOSPSC, 4 US dental institutions, n=656, 40.6% response) finding medical institutions outperform dental on 9/10 safety-culture dimensions, with dental clinics strongest in organizational learning (85%) and teamwork (79%).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Adapts the Medical Office Survey on Patient Safety Culture (MOSPSC) for the dental setting and applies it across 4 US dental institutions.
- Provides a direct dental-vs-medical benchmark of patient safety culture (medical 2016 comparator), establishing where dentistry trails its medical counterparts.
- Identifies organizational learning and teamwork as the relative strengths of surveyed dental clinics.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Cross-sectional survey study.
- **Instrument**: Medical Office Survey on Patient Safety Culture (MOSPSC), modified for dentistry.
- **Setting/timing**: 4 US dental institutions, administered in 2016; compared against medical-counterpart data (2016).
- **Sample**: All dental team members invited; 656 of 1,615 invited providers responded (40.6% response rate).
- abstract-only — full text not retrieved; detailed scoring and analysis methods not extracted.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Medical institutions outperformed dental institutions on **9 of 10** safety-culture dimensions.
- Medical outperformed dental on **6 of 6** overall quality items and **8 of 9** patient-safety/quality issues.
- Strongest dental average percentage-positive scores: **organizational learning (85%)** and **teamwork (79%)**.
- Conclusion: safety culture has progressed over time, but heterogeneity remains across academic dental, private (nonacademic), and medical clinics.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- 40.6% response rate raises potential non-response bias.
- Limited to 4 US dental institutions (academic-leaning); generalizability to private practice uncertain.
- Cross-sectional design — no causal or longitudinal inference.
- abstract-only — full text not retrieved; dimension-level detail and statistical comparisons not extracted.

## 7. Glossary

- **MOSPSC**: Medical Office Survey on Patient Safety Culture — AHRQ instrument for measuring safety-culture climate in ambulatory settings.
- **Safety culture**: shared values, attitudes, and behaviors that determine an organization's commitment to patient safety.
- **Percentage-positive score**: proportion of respondents answering favorably on a survey dimension.
