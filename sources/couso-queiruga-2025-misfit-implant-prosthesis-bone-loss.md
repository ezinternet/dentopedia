## Why Ingested

프레임워크 미스피트(misfit)가 임플란트주위 골 소실에 미치는 임상적 영향을 최초로 정량화한 연구. 한계값 ≥0.1 mm gap → DIB 증가를 수치로 제시. [[implants/katsoulis-2017-misfit-implant-prosthesis-clinical-outcomes-sr]] 의 "misfit이 골 소실에 미치는 영향 불명확" 결론을 업데이트.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective (n=301 patients, 505 implants, 10.6yr): ISPs with marginal gaps show higher bone loss (DIB 3.43 vs 3.22 mm, P=.001); gaps ≥0.1 mm are the threshold; each 0.1 mm increment adds 0.08 mm bone loss; smoking and periodontitis are the strongest independent risk factors.

## 한줄요약

후향 코호트 (n=505 임플란트, 10.6년): 변연 갭 있는 보철이 없는 것보다 골 소실 더 큼(DIB 3.43 vs 3.22 mm, P=.001); 갭 ≥0.1 mm가 임계값; 0.1 mm 증가마다 DIB +0.08 mm; 흡연·치주병력이 최강 독립 위험인자.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Clinical Oral Implants Research 2025;37(1):69-78
- **Study type**: Retrospective cohort (STROBE)
- **Institution**: University of Bern
- **n**: 301 patients / 505 soft-tissue level implants; 10.6 ± 0.7 years follow-up
- **Implants**: Straumann tissue-level only (3.3/4.1/4.8 mm diameter)

## 2. Key Contributions
- First study to quantify a specific misfit threshold (≥0.1 mm) linked to DIB increase
- Dose-response: each 0.1 mm gap → +0.08 mm bone loss
- Identifies biological confounders (smoking, periodontitis) as primary drivers in multivariate

## 3. Methodology
- Periapical radiograph at 10yr post-loading
- DIB = distance implant shoulder to most coronal crestal bone
- GapCAT: gap present/absent; GapDIM: 0 / >0–<0.1 / ≥0.1 mm
- Multivariable linear mixed-effects model with patient-level random intercepts

## 4. Key Results

| Gap category | Mean DIB (mm) | P vs no gap |
|---|---|---|
| No gap | 3.22 ± 0.8 | — |
| Gap >0–<0.1 mm | 3.36 ± 0.5 | 0.001 |
| Gap ≥0.1 mm | 3.45 ± 0.7 | 0.001 |

Dose-response: each 0.1 mm gap → +0.08 mm DIB (P=0.03)

Multivariate significant predictors:
- Smoking: +0.36 mm (P=0.002)
- History of periodontitis: +0.16 mm (P=0.001)
- GapDIM: NOT significant (P=0.28) in multivariate → confounders dominate

## 5. Limitations
- Tissue-level implants only → may underestimate impact at bone-level implants (gap closer to crest)
- Retrospective design, single center
- Radiographic misfit assessment (not direct measurement)
- GapDIM lost significance in multivariate — misfit likely acts via biological (biofilm) rather than pure biomechanical pathway

## 7. Glossary
- **DIB**: distance between implant shoulder and most coronal point of crestal bone (larger = more bone loss)
- **GapCAT**: presence/absence of radiographic gap at prosthesis-implant interface
- **GapDIM**: vertical dimension of gap (mm)
- **Passive fit failure mechanism**: gap → biofilm reservoir → inflammation → peri-implant bone loss
