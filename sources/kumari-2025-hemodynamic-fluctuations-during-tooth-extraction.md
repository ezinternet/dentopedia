## Why Ingested

Adds direct intraoperative hemodynamic time-course data (SBP/DBP/HR/SpO2 measured at 4 timepoints: preop → injection → extraction → postop) to the hypertensive-extraction monitoring evidence base, reinforcing [[drug/systemic-disease/yang-2025-tooth-extraction-hypertensive-ecg-monitoring]]. The study quantifies the magnitude and timing of BP/HR rise under 2% lidocaine + 1:100,000 adrenaline — peak at the extraction step, recovery below baseline by 5 min postop — giving a normotensive reference curve that frames how much steeper the response may be in true hypertensives.

## One-line Summary

Prospective observational study (n=50 normotensive adults) showing SBP, DBP and HR all rise significantly (P=0.0001) during local-anesthetic injection and tooth extraction under 2% lidocaine + 1:100,000 adrenaline, peaking at extraction and falling below baseline 5 min postoperatively; authors recommend anxiolysis/sedation caution for anxious and hypertensive patients.

## 한줄요약

전향적 관찰연구 (정상혈압 성인 50명): 2% 리도카인 + 1:100,000 아드레날린 하 발치 시 수축기·이완기 혈압과 심박수가 마취주사·발치 단계에서 유의하게 상승(P=0.0001)해 발치 시점에 최고치를 보이고 술후 5분에 기저치 이하로 회복 — 불안·고혈압 환자에서는 진정/항불안제 사용 권고.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides a four-timepoint intraoperative hemodynamic trajectory (T1 5-min preop → T2 injection → T3 extraction → T4 5-min postop) for routine dental extraction under lidocaine + adrenaline.
- Demonstrates that SBP, DBP and HR all peak at the extraction step (T3) and recover to below preoperative values by T4 in a normotensive cohort.
- Reports a gender split: females show larger BP rises; males have higher baseline SBP and greater pain tolerance (mean VAS pain only 2/10).
- Reinforces the clinical caution that anxious and hypertensive patients warrant anxiolysis/sedation, even though peak changes in this normotensive sample were modest.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Prospective, observational, single institution Dental OPD.
- **Sample**: 50 subjects by convenience (no a priori sample-size calculation); equal male/female; mean age 45.8 y (range 18–65).
- **Inclusion**: 18–65 y, requiring extraction, no systemic illness, not on medication.
- **Exclusion**: hypertension, diabetes, pregnancy, systemic illness, any medication — i.e., the cohort is **normotensive and otherwise healthy** (key caveat for interpreting the hypertensive recommendation).
- **Anesthetic**: lidocaine 2 mL, 2% solution, with 1:100,000 adrenaline.
- **Parameters**: SBP, DBP, HR, SpO2 measured at 4 timepoints (preop, injection, extraction, postop).
- **Operative duration**: mean 22 min (18–33). **Pain**: mean VAS 2/10.
- **Analysis**: SPSS v20.0.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Overall means (Table 2), all four parameters P=0.0001 across timepoints:

| Parameter | Preop (T1) | Anesthesia (T2) | Extraction (T3) | Postop (T4) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| SBP (mmHg) | 126.6±5.41 | 127.5±4.09 | 128.0±3.06 | 122.0±3.51 |
| DBP (mmHg) | 72.7±1.74 | 73.3±2.29 | 74.6±1.95 | 73.5±1.33 |
| HR (bpm) | 80.9±4.18 | 81.1±1.38 | 84.6±4.73 | 76.5±3.76 |
| SpO2 (%) | 97.5±0.67 | 97.9±0.48 | 98.0±0.53 | 98.1±1.08 |

- **SBP**: rises through injection and extraction, falls below baseline postop; larger swing in females.
- **DBP**: significant time-variation; greater rise in females.
- **HR**: peaks at extraction (84.6 bpm), then drops below baseline postop (76.5 bpm); pattern consistent across genders.
- **SpO2**: clinically stable (~97–98%) throughout, though statistically significant.
- The absolute magnitude of change is small in this normotensive cohort (SBP peak-to-trough ~6 mmHg overall).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Cohort is normotensive** — hypertensives were excluded, so the conclusion to "manage hypertensive patients with care" is extrapolated, not directly tested. This is the central interpretive caveat.
- Small sample (n=50), convenience sampling, no power calculation → authors themselves flag "low statistical power."
- Single center, single anesthetic protocol; no control arm (e.g., adrenaline-free).
- Confounders (age, gender, anxiety level) not adjusted.
- No continuous/ECG monitoring — discrete 4-point snapshots only.

## 6. Related Work

- Confirms prior reports of significant SBP/DBP rise with nonsignificant HR change during extraction; literature is mixed on whether peak BP occurs at the start (fear/anxiety) or at extraction (stress).
- Supports use of distraction, anxiolytics, or conscious sedation to mitigate anxiety-driven cardiovascular responses.

## 7. Glossary

- **SBP / DBP**: systolic / diastolic blood pressure.
- **HR**: heart rate (bpm).
- **SpO2**: peripheral capillary oxygen saturation.
- **VAS**: visual analog scale (pain, 0–10).
- **1:100,000 adrenaline**: epinephrine concentration in the local anesthetic (vasoconstrictor).
- **Normotensive**: normal blood pressure (the cohort condition here).
- **T1–T4**: study timepoints — 5-min preop, injection, extraction, 5-min postop.
