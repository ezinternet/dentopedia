## Why Ingested

기존 [[wiki/oral-medicine/psillas-2020-odontogenic-maxillary-sinusitis-comprehensive-review]]는 내러티브 리뷰로 병인론·치료 개요를 제공하지만, CBCT 기반 단면연구에서 치주골소실이 치근단병소보다 점막비후와 더 강하게 연관된다는 임상 수치 근거가 없었음. 본 연구(Bisla 2022)는 대조군 포함 213명, 404개 상악동 CBCT를 분석하여 periapical lesion의 크기·거리보다 치주골소실이 2.2배 더 강력한 점막비후 예측인자임을 보여줌.

## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional CBCT study (n=213 patients, 404 sinuses) found periodontal bone loss — not periapical lesion size or proximity — is the dominant predictor of maxillary sinus mucosal thickening (OR 2.2), with overall mucosal change prevalence of 49.5%.

## 한줄요약

단면연구(213명, 404개 상악동 CBCT)에서 치주골소실이 치근단병소 크기·거리보다 상악동 점막비후의 더 강한 예측인자(OR 2.2)이며 전체 점막변화 유병률 49.5% 확인.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of Oral Biology and Craniofacial Research, Vol. 12 (2022), pp. 645–650
- **Study design**: Cross-sectional analytical observational study
- **Sample**: 213 patients (404 maxillary sinuses) — 111 patients (208 sinuses) odontogenic pathology group; 102 patients (196 sinuses) control group
- **Setting**: Oral Medicine and Radiology Department, Post Graduate Institute of Dental Sciences, Rohtak, India
- **CBCT scanner**: CS 9300 (Carestream Health), 60–90 kVp, voxel size 90–300 µm, limited FOV

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Periodontal bone loss dominates over periapical lesions** as predictor of sinus mucosal thickening (OR 2.2, binary logistic regression).
2. **Periapical lesion size and distance from sinus floor were NOT significant** predictors of mucosal changes (p=0.646; p=0.49).
3. **Mucosal thickening prevalence increased with bone loss severity**: mild 58.1%, moderate 62.2%, severe 72% (p=0.008).
4. **Overall sinus mucosal change prevalence**: 49.5% (200/404 sinuses), with mucosal thickening most common (23%), followed by non-specific opacification (11.6%), sinus polyp (7.2%), antral pseudocyst (6.2%).
5. **Rhinosinusitis history was a non-confounding factor** — distributed equally between groups (35 patients total; p=NS).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Inclusion**: Patients referred for CBCT for posterior maxillary teeth/sinus evaluation; systemically healthy
- **Exclusion**: Prior sinusitis treatment within 1 year, pregnancy, metal artifacts, trauma/cysts/tumors
- **Sinus mucosal scoring**: MS evaluation score 0–6 (0=none; 1=thickening ≥3 mm; 2=polyp; 3=antral pseudocyst; 4=non-specific opacification; 5=periostitis; 6=antrolith)
- **Periapical lesion classification (CBCTPI)**: diameter-based score 1–5 (1: 0.5–1 mm; 5: >8 mm)
- **Periodontal bone loss**: mild <25%, moderate 25–50%, severe >50% of one tooth below sinus
- **Distance classification**: juxtaposed / 0–<2 mm / ≥2 mm from sinus floor
- **Statistics**: Chi-square, Shapiro-Wilk normality, binary logistic regression; SPSS software

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-center retrospective CBCT study limits generalizability
- Histopathological confirmation of sinus mucosal changes not performed
- Study group mean age (43y) vs control group (25y) significantly different — age confounding possible
- Inflammatory mediator levels not assessed
- Future work: longitudinal studies, microbiological confirmation of odontogenic origin

## 7. Glossary

- **Schneiderian membrane (슈나이더막)**: Sinus mucosal lining, normal thickness ~0.8–1 mm; thickening ≥3 mm considered pathological in this study
- **CBCTPI (CBCT 치근단지수)**: CBCT Periapical Index — diameter-based scoring of periapical lesions on CBCT
- **MS evaluation score (상악동 평가 점수)**: 0–6 scale classifying type of sinus mucosal change on CBCT
- **Antral pseudocyst (상악동 슈도시스트)**: Dome-shaped opacity on sinus floor, submucosal fluid accumulation
- **Antrolith (상악동석)**: Calcified body within maxillary sinus
- **Mucosal thickening (점막비후)**: Sinus mucosa ≥3 mm on CBCT, classified as score 1
