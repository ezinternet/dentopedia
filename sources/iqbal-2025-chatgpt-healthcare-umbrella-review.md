## One-line Summary
J Biomed Sci 2025;32:45 — PRISMA umbrella review of 17 reviews (15 SRs + 2 MAs) on ChatGPT in healthcare; AMSTAR-2 quality: 5 moderate, 12 low; most common theme — diagnosis/clinical decision-making; 52.9% general healthcare, 41.2% specialty-specific (incl. public health dentistry); methodology and funding-source reporting gaps flagged.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Journal of Biomedical Science 2025;32:45
- DOI: 10.1186/s12929-025-01131-z
- Type: Umbrella review (review of reviews; no quantitative synthesis)
- Search window: inception to February 2024
- Databases: Cochrane Library, PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, Google Scholar

## 2. Key Contributions
- First umbrella review synthesising ChatGPT-in-healthcare evidence base
- AMSTAR-2 grading flags pervasive methodological deficiencies in the underlying SR literature
- Maps thematic distribution across general healthcare, specialty domains, manuscript-review use cases

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- PRISMA-compliant umbrella review
- Two independent reviewers: title/abstract/full-text screening
- Quality assessment: AMSTAR-2 checklist
- 17 reviews included (15 SR + 2 MA); narrative synthesis due to heterogeneity

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- AMSTAR-2 quality distribution: 5 moderate, 12 low
- Methodological gaps: study design justification, funding source reporting
- Thematic dominance: ChatGPT in disease diagnosis or clinical decision-making
- 82.4% general usage focus; 17.6% unique topics (medical examinations, conducting SRs)
- Domain coverage: 52.9% general healthcare; 41.2% specialty-specific (radiology, neurosurgery, gastroenterology, public health dentistry, ophthalmology)
- 17.6% reviews discussed ChatGPT for manuscript review/writing

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- High proportion of low-quality SRs in evidence base; downstream conclusions weakened
- Ethical, legal, accuracy concerns require cautious integration
- Calls for policymaker involvement and continued evidence-generation on reliability

## 6. Related Work
