## One-line Summary
Health Sci Rep 2025;8(1):e70312 — narrative review of AI in broader healthcare (not dental-specific) covering ML, DL, NLP, robotics; documents publication growth 158 (3.54%) in 2014 → 731 (16.33%) in 2024 in Web of Science; flags data security and budget constraints as primary deployment barriers.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Health Science Reports 2025;8(1):e70312
- Published: 2025-01-05
- DOI: 10.1002/hsr2.70312
- Type: Narrative review (Web of Science, 2014–2024 corpus)
- Note: General healthcare focus (not dentistry-specific); included for broader AI context

## 2. Key Contributions
- Quantifies AI-in-healthcare publication growth trajectory (4.5× over decade)
- Maps AI core technology stack (robotics, ML, DL, NLP) to deployment domains (patient interaction, predictive analytics, remote monitoring)
- Names case studies (Google Health, IBM Watson Health) as deployment exemplars

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Web of Science search 2014–2024
- Keywords: AI, ML, healthcare applications
- Synthesis: recent literature + real-world case studies
- No PRISMA, no quality grading

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- AI-related healthcare publications: 158 articles (3.54%) in 2014 → 731 articles (16.33%) by 2024
- Core applications: remote monitoring, predictive analytics, personalized treatment, diagnostic imaging
- AI often outperforms human clinicians in cancer/cardiac/neurological imaging diagnostics
- Obstacles: data security, budget constraints, regulatory/ethical compliance

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Data security/privacy
- Resource and budget limitations
- Requires multidisciplinary cooperation (clinicians + computer scientists + regulators)
- Ethical, legal, social considerations remain underdeveloped

## 6. Related Work
- Najjar 2023 — broader AI in healthcare exemplar review
- IBM Watson Health, Google Health — frequently cited industrial deployments
