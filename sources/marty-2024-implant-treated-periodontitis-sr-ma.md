## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Dentistry Journal 2024, 12, 240
- **DOI**: 10.3390/dj12080240
- **Published**: 29 July 2024
- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis

## 2. Key Contributions
- Primary outcomes: peri-implantitis incidence, implant survival rate
- Secondary outcomes: mean pocket depth, mean peri-implant bone loss
- History of periodontitis significantly increases peri-implantitis risk (~4.8× higher odds vs non-periodontitis patients)
- Implant survival rate also reduced in treated-periodontitis patients vs healthy patients
- Search conducted across 5 databases: MEDLINE, Embase, ScienceDirect, LILACS, Cochrane Library (June 2024)

## 3. Methodology
- **Databases**: MEDLINE, Embase, ScienceDirect, LILACS, Cochrane Library; supplemented by manual search
- **Search date**: June 2024
- **Inclusion**: Studies evaluating implant health outcomes in patients with history of treated periodontitis vs periodontally healthy controls
- **Qualitative analysis**: 8 studies; **Quantitative synthesis**: 10 studies
- Screened from 10,775 potentially eligible articles

## 4. Key Results
| Outcome | Result |
|---|---|
| Peri-implantitis risk | ~4.8× higher in treated-periodontitis patients (statistically significant) |
| Implant survival | Reduced in periodontitis history vs healthy |
| Mean pocket depth | Increased in periodontitis history patients |
| Mean peri-implant bone loss | Greater in periodontitis history patients |

## 5. Limitations
- Heterogeneity in periodontitis classification and treatment protocols across included studies
- Limited control for SPT compliance, smoking, and systemic disease as confounders
- Most studies retrospective; few RCTs available
