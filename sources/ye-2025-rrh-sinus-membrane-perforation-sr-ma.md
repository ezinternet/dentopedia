## Why Ingested

측방창 (Lateral Window) 상악동거상술에서 잔류 치조골 높이 (Residual Ridge Height, RRH)가 슈나이더 막 천공 (Sinus Membrane Perforation, SMP)의 위험인자인지 처음으로 메타분석으로 집계한 SR+MA. 기존 [[wiki/overviews/sinus-lift-lateral-2026-synthesis]]의 "Cluster 2 — 해부학적 위험 예측" 축에서 잔류골 높이 항목이 누락되어 있었으며, 본 논문이 그 공백을 채우는 핵심 근거로 작용. [[wiki/sinus-lift/lateral/yang-2024-sinus-septa-wall-thickness-perforation-risk]] (격벽·측벽 두께), [[wiki/sinus-lift/lateral/manor-2024-cbct-prognostic-factors-sinus-perforation]] (CBCT 예측인자)와 함께 술전 위험 평가 체계를 보완.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (10 studies, 1,809 sinus lifts): lower residual ridge height is significantly associated with Schneiderian membrane perforation during lateral-window sinus floor elevation (MD −0.89 mm, 95% CI −1.47 to −0.31; small RRH OR 2.47, NS in primary analysis but significant after outlier removal).

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (10연구, 1,809회 상악동거상술): 잔류 치조골 높이 (Residual Ridge Height, RRH)가 낮을수록 측방창 (Lateral Window) 상악동거상술에서 슈나이더 막 천공 위험 증가 (MD −0.89 mm; 이분형 분석 OR 2.47, 이상치 제거 시 유의).

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic review and meta-analysis quantifying **잔류 치조골 높이 (Residual Ridge Height, RRH)** as a risk factor for 슈나이더 막 천공 (Sinus Membrane Perforation, SMP) in lateral-window sinus lift surgery.
- Pooled SMP rate: **19.2%** across 1,809 sinus lift procedures.
- Meta-analysis 1 (continuous RRH): Mean RRH significantly lower in perforation group — MD −0.89 mm (95% CI −1.47 to −0.31; I² = 90%).
- Meta-analysis 2 (small vs large RRH, cut-off ~3–4 mm): OR 2.47 (95% CI 0.49–12.21; I² = 88%) — tendency toward increased perforation risk with small RRH, not statistically significant; after exclusion of outlier (Tukel 2018), OR 4.68 (p < 0.05).
- Quality: All 10 included studies scored 7/9 on Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Included: adult patients, lateral-window sinus lift, reporting RRH by small/large classification or by perforation vs. non-perforation group data.
- Excluded: transalveolar/osteotome technique, duplicate data, non-English, abstract-only.
- 10 final studies (n = 1,601 patients, 1,809 procedures): prospective and retrospective designs.
- Statistical tool: RevMan 5.3, random-effects model; chi-square + I² for heterogeneity; funnel plots + Egger's test for publication bias; leave-one-out sensitivity analysis.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Analysis | Result | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| Overall SMP rate | 19.2% | (descriptive) |
| Mean RRH — continuous (MD) | −0.89 mm (95% CI −1.47 to −0.31) | p < 0.05 |
| Heterogeneity (continuous) | I² = 90% | High |
| Small vs large RRH (OR) | 2.47 (95% CI 0.49–12.21) | p = NS |
| Heterogeneity (categorical) | I² = 88% | High |
| After removing Tukel 2018 outlier | OR 4.68 | p < 0.05 |
| Publication bias (Egger's) | p = 0.62 | Not significant |

- "Small RRH" defined as 3–4 mm across included studies (no single standardized cut-off).
- Plausible mechanism: smaller RRH requires greater membrane elevation to achieve adequate implant length, increasing risk of tearing due to limited membrane extensibility.
- Impact on implant survival: review notes contrasting literature — Díaz-Olivares 2021 found no significant difference (97.68% vs 98.88%), but a 85-study review found 55% greater implant failure risk per 10% increase in perforation rate.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- High heterogeneity (I² = 88–90%) limits clinical generalizability.
- No adjusted/matched multivariate data — confounders (septa, smoking, membrane thickness, surgeon experience, instrument type) not segregated across perforation/non-perforation groups.
- No standardized RRH cut-off; included studies used 3–4 mm variably.
- Predominantly retrospective designs.
- Small number of studies per meta-analysis.
- Future studies should: (1) apply both 3 mm and 4 mm cut-offs, (2) provide multivariate-adjusted data, (3) segregate piezosurgery vs. rotary results.
