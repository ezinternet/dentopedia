## Why Ingested

Maxillary molar 즉시식립 (immediate implant placement, IIP)에서 임상적으로 구개근 소켓 (palatal root socket)에 식립하는 전략의 생역학적·생물학적 근거를 제공하는 기초 연구. [[immediate-implant/liu-2024-maxillary-immediate-molar-palatal-root-socket]]가 임상적으로 권고하는 "구개근 소켓 식립"이 왜 작동하는지를 마우스 모델에서 변형률(strain)·골세포 사멸·PDL 유래 Wnt 반응 세포 차원에서 설명한다. 또한 [[immediate-implant/araujo-2019-socket-healing-immediate-implant-review]]의 소켓 치유 개괄에 분자 메커니즘 층위를 더한다.

## One-line Summary

Murine first-molar extraction + palatal-root-socket osteotomy model showing that low-strain peri-implant zones (buccal, PDL-filled gap) osseointegrate via PDL-derived Wnt-responsive osteogenic cells, while high-strain zones (palatal, direct bone contact) suffer osteocyte death and bone resorption — and that exogenous liposomal WNT3A rescues osseointegration where PDL/Wnt cells are absent.

## 한줄요약

마우스 제1대구치 발치 후 구개근 소켓 (palatal root socket)에 골삭제·임플란트 식립한 동물 모델 연구. 변형률(strain)이 낮은 협측(PDL 잔존 gap)은 PDL 유래 Wnt 반응 골형성 세포로 골유착되고, 변형률이 높은 구개측(골 직접 압박)은 골세포 사멸·골흡수가 일어났으며, PDL/Wnt 세포가 없는 부위에 외인성 리포좀 WNT3A를 투여하면 골유착이 회복됨을 보여줌.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Established a murine immediate-postextraction model that creates two distinct peri-implant environments side-by-side.** First-molar extraction followed by an osteotomy positioned specifically in the **palatal root socket**, removing periodontal ligament (PDL) only on the **palatal aspect** and leaving the **buccal aspect** PDL undisturbed. This yields, in a single implant, a palatal aspect in direct bone contact and a buccal aspect with a PDL-filled implant–bone gap.

2. **Linked mechanical strain to biological outcome.** Finite element modeling showed **high strains on the palatal aspect** (bone compressed by the implant) → osteocyte death + bone resorption + loss of peri-implant bone; and **low strains on the buccal aspect** → minimal osteocyte death + robust peri-implant bone formation and osseointegration.

3. **Identified the PDL as a direct cellular source of osseointegration.** The buccal PDL remnants directly provided **Wnt-responsive cells** that were responsible for new bone formation and osseointegration — i.e., PDL is not merely a passive gap-filler but an inherently osteogenic tissue.

4. **Demonstrated a pharmacologic rescue.** On the palatal aspect (devoid of PDL and Wnt-responsive cells), adding **exogenous liposomal WNT3A** created an osteogenic environment for rapid peri-implant bone formation, substituting for the missing endogenous Wnt-responsive cell source.

5. **Clinical inference**: low strain and high Wnt signaling favor osseointegration of immediate postextraction implants; if PDL tissue is healthy, it is reasonable to preserve it during immediate implant placement.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Model**: Murine (mouse) first maxillary molar extraction, then immediate osteotomy site preparation in the **palatal root socket**.
- **Two-environment design**: osteotomy removes PDL only on the palatal aspect of the socket; buccal aspect PDL is left intact. Result — palatal = implant in direct contact with bone; buccal = PDL-filled gap between implant and bone.
- **Biomechanics**: finite element (FE) modeling of the peri-implant region to map strain distribution (high palatal vs. low buccal).
- **Biology/histology**: assessment of osteocyte viability/death, peri-implant bone resorption vs. formation, and identification of Wnt-responsive cells (consistent with this group's Wnt-reporter lineage-tracing approach used in related papers, e.g., Pei et al. 2017; Yuan et al. 2018 JDR socket-healing paper).
- **Intervention arm**: exogenous **liposomal WNT3A** applied to the PDL-deficient palatal aspect to test whether Wnt amplification restores osteogenesis/osseointegration.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Palatal aspect (high strain, direct bone contact, no PDL)**: osteocyte death and bone resorption predominated → **loss of peri-implant bone**.
- **Buccal aspect (low strain, PDL-filled gap)**: minimal osteocyte death and **robust peri-implant bone formation / osseointegration**.
- **Mechanism**: buccal PDL remnants directly supplied **Wnt-responsive cells** that drove new bone formation and osseointegration.
- **Rescue**: **liposomal WNT3A** applied to the PDL-/Wnt-deficient palatal aspect created an osteogenic environment for **rapid peri-implant bone formation**.
- **Bottom line**: osseointegration of immediate postextraction implants is favored by (1) **low mechanical strain** and (2) **high Wnt signaling**; the PDL is an inherent on-site source of osteogenic Wnt-responsive cells.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Species/scale**: murine model — small-scale, fast-healing rodent bone; direct quantitative translation to human maxillary molar sockets is limited (the reference list itself includes Seok et al. 2013 and Chen et al. 2017 noting human-vs-rodent inflammatory/site-viability differences).
- **Single-implant two-zone construct** is an experimental idealization; clinical immediate molar placement involves multiple roots, an interradicular septum, and variable buccal-plate integrity not modeled here.
- **PDL-preservation caveat**: the conclusion to preserve PDL applies only when PDL tissue is **healthy** — infected/diseased sockets are explicitly outside this recommendation.
- **WNT3A as therapeutic**: liposomal WNT3A is a proof-of-concept biologic; clinical translation (delivery, safety, dosing) remains future work.
- Captured PDF lacks full Methods/Results, so effect sizes and statistics could not be abstracted.

## 7. Glossary

- **PDL (Periodontal Ligament, 치주인대)**: connective tissue between tooth root and alveolar bone; here a source of Wnt-responsive osteogenic cells.
- **Palatal root socket (구개근 소켓)**: the socket of the palatal root of a maxillary molar; the osteotomy/implant site in this model and the clinically favored IIP site for maxillary molars.
- **Strain (변형률)**: relative mechanical deformation of peri-implant bone under implant load; high strain → bone damage, low strain → favorable healing.
- **Finite element modeling (FEM, 유한요소해석)**: computational method mapping stress/strain distribution around the implant.
- **Osteocyte (골세포)**: mechanosensing bone cell; its death (apoptosis) signals bone resorption.
- **Wnt-responsive cell (Wnt 반응 세포)**: cell with active Wnt/β-catenin signaling, inherently osteogenic; supplied by PDL remnants.
- **Liposomal WNT3A (리포좀 WNT3A)**: lipid-formulated exogenous WNT3A protein used to amplify Wnt signaling and induce peri-implant bone formation where endogenous Wnt-responsive cells are absent.
- **Osseointegration (골유착)**: direct structural/functional bone-to-implant connection.
- **IIP (Immediate Implant Placement, 즉시식립)**: implant placed into a fresh extraction socket at the time of extraction.
