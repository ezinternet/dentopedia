## Why Ingested
단일 내원 vs 다회 내원 근관치료(inter-appointment dressing 사용 여부)의 outcome 동등성을 직접 비교한 RCT로, 내원 간 수산화칼슘 dressing의 임상적 부가가치를 다룬 [[endodontics/irrigation/rossi-fedele-2023-irrigation-dressing-apical-periodontitis-sr-ma]]를 임상 결과(성공률·통증·chairside time) 관점에서 보강한다.

## One-line Summary
RCT (n=220 teeth, 18–45 mo follow-up): single-visit and multiple-visit non-surgical root canal treatment had statistically equivalent success rates (88.9% vs 87.4%, p=0.729) and postoperative pain, but single-visit took ~31 min less chairside time.

## 한줄요약
RCT (220개 치아, 18–45개월 추적): 단일 내원 vs 다회 내원 비외과적 근관치료는 성공률(88.9% vs 87.4%, p=0.729)과 술후 통증에 유의차가 없었고, 단일 내원이 의자시간을 약 31분 단축시켰다.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Randomised clinical trial (parallel two-arm, single-centre).
- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health (2015) 15:162. DOI 10.1186/s12903-015-0148-x.
- **Setting**: On-campus University Health Service dental clinic, University of Hong Kong; treatments performed by two general dentists (A and B; one used a 2.5x loupe).
- **Registration**: Chinese Clinical Trial Registry / WHO ChiCTR-IOR-15006117 (registered 20 March 2015).
- **Ethics**: IRB of University of Hong Kong / Hospital Authority Hong Kong West Cluster (HKU UW 09-303).

## 2. Key Contributions
- Direct head-to-head RCT comparing single-visit vs multiple-visit primary non-surgical endodontic treatment on three outcomes: success rate, postoperative pain, and chairside time.
- Demonstrates clinical equivalence of success and pain between the two protocols in a real-world general-dentistry clinic.
- Quantifies the chairside-time savings of single-visit treatment (~31 min less).
- Identifies prognostic factors (maxillary location; absence of preoperative apical periodontitis) associated with higher success via logistic regression.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Allocation**: Teeth randomly assigned to single-visit or multiple-visit groups via a calculator's random-number function; per-tooth randomisation when a patient had multiple teeth. Operators and independent assessor blinded to tooth code.
- **Inclusion**: Generally healthy patients needing primary non-surgical endodontic treatment, periodontally healthy tooth, no history of periodontitis, ≥50% remaining coronal structure, willing to return for follow-up.
- **Exclusion**: Severe acute pulpitis with facial swelling/systemic infection, severe systemic disease, TMJ/psychological stress, prior pulpotomy, <50% coronal structure.
- **Clinical procedure**: Local anaesthesia, rubber dam isolation; canals cleaned/shaped with ProTaper Ni-Ti rotary files (Dentsply Maillefer); 5.25% sodium hypochlorite irrigation; obturation by core-carrier technique. Single-visit teeth obturated immediately; multiple-visit teeth dressed with non-setting (calcium hydroxide) inter-appointment medicament and obturated at a later visit.
- **Follow-up**: Every 6 months (≥18 months) assessing pain, tenderness to percussion, sinus tract, mobility, abscess; periapical radiographs for periapical pathology.
- **Outcome definition**: Success = no clinical signs/symptoms AND no radiographic periapical radiolucency.
- **Sample size**: Powered (80%, α=0.05) to detect a 10% success-rate difference; 102/group needed, inflated to ≥128/group for 20% dropout → 256 teeth enrolled.
- **Analysis**: Chi-square / logistic regression; effect sizes reported as odds ratios.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Enrolment/analysis**: 256 teeth (228 patients) randomised → 220 teeth (194 patients) analysed; single-visit 117 completed/104? (analysis arm 117 single, 103 multiple). Mean age 46.4 ± 14.1 yr; mean follow-up 29.4 ± 9.3 months.
- **Success rate**: Single-visit 88.9% (104/117) vs multiple-visit 87.4% (90/103); p=0.729, OR=1.156. No significant difference.
- **Failure causes**: Mostly incomplete apical healing (single 8.5%, multiple 9.7%); few fractures/caries extractions and no signs of apical healing.
- **Chairside time**: Single-visit 62.0 min vs multiple-visit 92.9 min; mean difference −30.9 min (95% CI −39.4 to −22.4), p<0.001.
- **Postoperative pain (within 7 days)**: 21% (single) vs 12% (multiple); p=0.055, OR=2.061 — not significant.
- **Postoperative pain (≥18 months)**: 0.9% vs 1.0%; p>0.999.
- **Prognostic factors (logistic regression)**: Maxillary teeth OR=3.16 (95% CI 1.33–7.46; p=0.009); absence of preoperative apical periodontitis OR=4.35 (95% CI 1.43–13.24; p=0.010) — both associated with higher success.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Single-centre, two-operator study limits generalisability; operator skill/loupe use differed between dentists.
- Trend toward more early postoperative pain in single-visit (p=0.055) approached significance and may matter clinically.
- Core-carrier obturation and ProTaper-specific protocol may not generalise to other techniques/materials.
- Outcome assessed by periapical radiographs (2D), not CBCT, potentially under-detecting periapical lesions.
- Follow-up heterogeneous (18–45 months); some teeth lost to follow-up.

## 6. Related Work
- Systematic review/meta-analysis cited finding no significant difference in antimicrobial efficacy or postoperative complications between single- and multiple-visit treatment.
- Survey literature showing many clinicians prefer conventional multiple-visit protocols.
- Reviews reporting higher swelling/analgesic use after single-visit treatment, contrasted with meta-analytic null findings.

## 7. Glossary
- **Single-visit endodontics**: Cleaning, shaping and obturation of the root canal completed in one appointment.
- **Multiple-visit endodontics**: Treatment split across appointments with an inter-appointment intracanal medicament (e.g., calcium hydroxide) before obturation.
- **Apical periodontitis**: Inflammation/periapical pathology of the tissues around the root apex; preoperative presence lowers prognosis here.
- **Chairside time**: Total operator time spent completing the endodontic treatment.
- **Core-carrier obturation**: Filling technique using a carrier coated with gutta-percha (e.g., Thermafil-type).
- **Odds ratio (OR)**: Effect-size measure of association used in the logistic regression.
