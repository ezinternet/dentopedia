## Why Ingested

Collagen plug + PRP as a simple, low-cost socket preservation approach — split-mouth prospective study evaluating clinical and radiographic outcomes at 3 and 6 months. Complements PRF and xenograft evidence by testing a minimal-intervention collagen-only strategy with growth factor augmentation. [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/covani-2022-alveolar-bone-remodeling-collagen-socket]]

## One-line Summary

Split-mouth prospective study (n=30) comparing collagen plug + PRP vs extraction alone: significant crestal bone height preservation at 3 and 6 months (p<0.001) with collagen plug, but no significant difference in alveolar ridge width.

## 한줄요약

분할구강 전향적 연구 (n=30): 콜라겐 플러그+PRP군에서 6개월 치조골 높이 유의하게 보존 (p<0.001), 치조골 폭경은 양군 간 유의차 없음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of Dental Research, Dental Clinics, Dental Prospects (J Dent Res Dent Clin Dent Prospects)
- **Volume/Issue**: 14(2): 139–145
- **Study design**: Split-mouth comparative prospective study (clinico-radiographic)
- **Sample**: 30 patients (18–30 years) requiring bilateral extractions with similar root anatomy
- **Follow-up**: Immediate post-extraction, 10 days (suture removal), 3 months, 6 months
- **Setting**: Department of Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery, Krishna Institute of Medical Sciences, Karad, India
- **Ethics**: Institutional Ethics Committee approval

## 2. Key Contributions

- Demonstrates that a low-cost, widely available collagen plug + PRP combination preserves crestal bone height
- Uses clinically feasible double-centrifugation PRP method (3 mL blood) — accessible in most surgical settings
- First split-mouth design specifically using KOLSPON collagen plug with PRP
- Width dimension showed no significant advantage — highlights limitation of collagen plug without additional grafting material

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Participants**: 30 patients (18–30 years) requiring bilateral extractions with identical root anatomy in the same arch (maxilla or mandible), willing to participate and sign consent.

**Exclusion**: Acute periapical pathology, medically compromised patients, head/neck irradiation history.

**PRP preparation**: Double centrifugation method:
- 2 mL venous blood + sodium citrate anticoagulant
- Soft spin: 2500 rpm × 10 min → 1.3 mL buffy coat
- Hard spin: 3400 rpm × 10 min → ~1 mL PRP
- Activated with 0.1 mL calcium gluconate immediately before placement

**Test side procedure**:
1. Atraumatic extraction under 2% LOX (lignocaine 1:200,000 adrenaline)
2. Socket curettage
3. KOLSPON collagen plug saturated with activated PRP inserted to crestal level
4. Sutured with 3-0 silk interrupted sutures

**Control side**: Extraction + suturing only (no graft).

**Randomization**: Even serial number → right quadrant as test; odd serial number → left quadrant as test.

**Outcome measurements**:
- *Alveolar bone width*: Bone calipers at crestal, mid-root, and apical levels immediately post-extraction, 3 months, 6 months
- *Alveolar bone height*: Digital RVG with radiopaque millimeter grid; AUTOCAD-like measurement using CEJ line (AB) and perpendicular depth (CD); decrease in CD = increase in bone height

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Test (Collagen + PRP) | Control (extraction only) | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Crestal bone height at 3 months | increased (CD shortened) | decreased | p<0.001 |
| Crestal bone height at 6 months | maintained / improved | continued loss | p<0.001 |
| Alveolar ridge width (3 months) | no significant change | no significant change | p>0.05 (NS) |
| Alveolar ridge width (6 months) | no significant change | no significant change | p>0.05 (NS) |

- Collagen plug + PRP significantly preserved crestal bone height in both dimensions
- Width preservation was not significant — collagen plug alone (without particulate graft) cannot maintain buccal plate volume
- No complications reported; wound healing uneventful at 10 days

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample (n=30); young adults (18–30 years) — may not represent typical extraction demographics
- Radiographic assessment (RVG with grid) less accurate than CBCT for 3D volume analysis
- No PRP-only or collagen-only arm — cannot separate contributions of each component
- 6-month endpoint only; no implant placement or long-term follow-up
- Width not preserved — study supports combining collagen plug with particulate graft for complete ARP

## 6. Related Work

- KOLSPON: Collagen plug product used; resorbs rapidly, primarily serves as hemostatic scaffold
- PRP (double centrifuge) vs PRF (single centrifuge): PRP requires activation with calcium gluconate; PRF simpler but different growth factor profile
- Covani 2022 (animal micro-CT) confirms bone volume protection mechanism of collagen plug
- Width maintenance requires additional grafting material (xenograft, allograft) per Fischer 2022 SR+MA

## 7. Glossary

- **KOLSPON**: Collagen plug brand (Indian market) — resorbable hemostatic plug derived from collagen
- **PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma)**: Autologous plasma concentrated in platelets by double centrifugation; activated with calcium gluconate to release growth factors
- **CD measurement**: Radiographic socket depth measurement — distance from CEJ connecting line to lowest bone crest; decrease over time = bone fill
- **RVG**: Radiovisiography — digital intraoral X-ray sensor system
- **Socket plug technique**: Generalized term for socket preservation using a soft-tissue or collagen occlusal plug without membrane flap advancement
