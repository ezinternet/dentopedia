## Why Ingested

상악동 격벽 (Sinus Septa)의 **모집단 유병률 기준치**가 없으면 천공 위험 SR+MA (Yang 2024)의 OR을 임상 절대위험으로 변환할 수 없음. 본 SR+MA (Henriques 2022, 62편, 22,460개 상악동)는 분모(유병률)를 제공하는 유일한 대형 메타분석으로, [[wiki/overviews/sinus-lift-lateral-2026-synthesis]] Cluster 2 "Septa" 절의 해석 기반을 완성한다. Yang 2024가 *관계* (OR)를 정량화했다면, Henriques 2022는 *빈도* (유병률 33.2%/상악동, 41.0%/환자)를 정량화한다 — 두 페이지는 상보적이다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 62 studies (22,460 sinuses; 13,701 patients) reporting maxillary sinus septa prevalence of 33.2% per sinus and 41.0% per patient by CBCT/CT, with septa most common in the mid-sinus region with transverse orientation (86%).

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (62편, 22,460개 상악동): CBCT/CT 기반 상악동 격벽 (Sinus Septa) 유병률 상악동당 33.2%, 환자당 41.0%; 중간부 위치·횡단 방향 (86%)이 가장 흔하며 상악동저 거상술 성공에 직접 영향.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Establishes **pooled sinus septa prevalence** (33.2% per sinus; 41.0% per patient) from the largest sample assembled to that date (62 studies, 22,460 sinuses).
- Provides clinically actionable anatomy data: septa most often in the **mid-sinus** region and with **transverse orientation** (86%) — the orientation that bisects the lateral window and forces a two-window approach.
- Tests **sex difference** (OR 0.785; 95% CI 0.59–1.05; P = 0.098) — no statistically significant sex effect on septa presence.
- Quantifies substantial **heterogeneity** (I² ≈ 96–98%) and explores sources via meta-regression.
- Supersedes the earlier smaller SR+MA by Pommer et al. 2012 (J Clin Periodontol 39:769) in sample size; provides the benchmark against which single-population CBCT studies are calibrated.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

### Inclusion
- Cross-sectional or cohort studies reporting maxillary sinus septa prevalence by CBCT or CT.
- Search: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science; bibliographies; author contact if needed.
- Independent dual-reviewer quality assessment using Joanna Briggs Institute (JBI) levels of evidence.
- Inter-rater reliability: Cohen's κ reported.

### Meta-analytic approach
- Prevalence expressed as proportion (Freeman–Tukey double-arcsine transformation for stabilization).
- Per-sinus analysis: 35 studies (14,664 sinuses).
- Per-patient analysis: 42 studies (9,631 patients).
- OR for sex difference: reported with 95% CI and Cochran Q / I².
- Heterogeneity explored by meta-regression (imaging modality, study population, measurement protocol).
- Publication bias: funnel plot visual assessment.

### Quality appraisal
- JBI critical appraisal checklist for prevalence studies (10-item checklist, scored 0–10).
- Most included studies scored moderate–high quality.

- **Location:** Middle (mid) region of sinus — most frequent.
- **Orientation:** Transverse — 86.0% of septa.
- **Total literature reviewed:** 62 studies, 13,701 patients, 22,460 sinuses.

### Transverse orientation importance
Transversely-oriented septa bisect the intended lateral window, requiring either (a) a modified two-window technique or (b) piezoelectric removal of the septum — both are associated with higher intraoperative complexity and (per Yang 2024) OR >4× for Schneiderian membrane perforation.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Very high heterogeneity (I² >96%) limits the precision of pooled estimates; meta-regression identified study population and imaging protocol as partial sources but could not fully account for variance.
- Funnel plot asymmetry raises possibility of publication bias (smaller studies with higher prevalence preferentially published).
- Definition of "septum" varies across studies (height threshold: ≥3 mm vs ≥2.5 mm vs any osseous projection) — a primary driver of heterogeneity.
- Cannot determine whether septa morphology (height, thickness) independently predicts perforation risk — requires linkage to procedural outcomes data.
- Generalizability limited by predominantly European and East Asian cohorts.

## 7. Glossary

- **Sinus septa (Underwood septa):** Bony ridges projecting from the inferior sinus floor into the sinus cavity; first described by Underwood (1910). Classified by location (anterior/middle/posterior), orientation (transverse/sagittal/diagonal), and origin (floor/medial/lateral wall).
- **Transverse orientation:** Septum runs bucco-palatally across the sinus floor — the surgically most challenging orientation because it bisects the lateral window osteotomy.
- **Per-sinus vs per-patient prevalence:** A single patient with bilateral septa contributes 2 to per-sinus count but 1 to per-patient count; per-patient prevalence is always ≥ per-sinus prevalence.
- **I²:** Percentage of variance across studies attributable to heterogeneity rather than chance. I² ≥96% = almost all variance is between-study — pooled point estimates should be interpreted cautiously.
