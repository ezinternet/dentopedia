## One-line Summary

Narrative review (Head & Face Medicine 2014) covering definition, pathogenesis, epidemiology (prevalence up to 56%), risk factors (smoking, periodontitis history, systemic disease), and treatment hierarchy (CIST protocol) for peri-implantitis.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Operational definitions**: Distinguishes 점막염 (Mucositis) — reversible, soft tissue only — from peri-implantitis — progressive, irreversible, involving bone resorption, increased pocket depth, and purulence.
2. **Prevalence synthesis**: Reports mucositis incidence up to 80%; peri-implantitis between 28–56% (Lindhe & Meyle 6th EWP Consensus). Mombelli et al. estimate: 20% of patients, 10% of implants.
3. **Microbiology**: Identifies key pathogens (Prevotella intermedia, Porphyromonas gingivalis, Treponema denticola, Tannerella forsythia, Staphylococcus aureus). Notes 71.7% antimicrobial resistance in 120-patient cohort.
4. **Risk factor ranking**: Smoking (increases bone loss by 0.16 mm/year; 4.7× peri-implantitis risk with periodontitis history) > periodontitis history > poor oral hygiene > systemic disease > iatrogenic causes.
5. **CIST protocol**: Cumulative Interceptive Supportive Therapy as stepwise treatment guidance from non-surgical to regenerative/resective surgery.
6. **Implant surface**: Rough surfaces increase biofilm adhesion vs smooth; surface topography affects peri-implant inflammation progression.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Narrative review; no systematic search protocol or PRISMA flow reported.
- Sources drawn from European Workshop on Periodontology consensus reports, meta-analyses, and clinical studies.
- Covers: definition, pathogenesis (histological/molecular comparison of peri-implant mucosa vs periodontium), epidemiology, risk factors, conservative treatments, surgical treatments, and CIST protocol.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Parameter | Finding |
|---|---|
| Mucositis prevalence | Up to 80% |
| Peri-implantitis prevalence | 28–56% (Lindhe); 20% patients / 10% implants (Mombelli) |
| Periodontitis history risk | 6× higher incidence (Zitzmann) |
| Smoking + periodontitis risk | 4.7× increase (Wallowy) |
| Smoking — bone loss increment | +0.16 mm/year (meta-analysis) |
| Antimicrobial resistance | 71.7% in 120 patients (Rams) |
| Implant 16-year success | 82.9% (background figure) |
| MMP-8 elevation in lesions | Up to 971% vs healthy |

**Bone defect classification**: Schwarz — class I (intraosseous) vs class II (supra-alveolar); Spiekermann — horizontal (I), key-shaped (II), funnel/gap (IIIa/b), horizontal-circular (IV).

**Treatment hierarchy (CIST)**:
- Step A: Mechanical debridement + oral hygiene
- Step B: Antiseptics (chlorhexidine)
- Step C: Systemic/local antibiotics
- Step D: Surgical (resective or regenerative depending on defect morphology)

**Conservative options**: Manual debridement, Er:YAG laser, photodynamic therapy (PDT) ± antibiotics — effective for mucositis and moderate peri-implantitis.

**Surgical options**: Resective surgery for class II defects; GBR/regenerative for intraosseous class I defects.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative review without systematic methodology — risk of selection bias.
- No quantitative pooling; prevalence estimates vary enormously (5–63.4%) due to heterogeneous case definitions and study populations.
- Biomarker differentiation (osteocalcin, TRAP, DKK-1, OPG, CatK) insufficient to distinguish peri-implantitis from other periodontal inflammation in saliva.
- Authors note: more RCTs needed for treatment comparisons, especially laser vs conventional debridement.

## 7. Glossary

- **임플란트 주위염 (Peri-implantitis)**: Progressive bone-destructive infection around osseointegrated implant
- **점막염 (Mucositis, Peri-implant)**: Reversible soft-tissue inflammation without bone loss; analogous to gingivitis
- **CIST (Cumulative Interceptive Supportive Therapy, 누적차단지지치료)**: Stepwise treatment escalation protocol for peri-implant disease
- **BOP (Bleeding on Probing, 탐침출혈)**: Clinical indicator of peri-implant inflammation
- **MMP-8 (Matrix Metalloproteinase-8)**: Elevated up to 971% in peri-implant lesions; potential diagnostic biomarker
- **PDT (Photodynamic Therapy, 광역동치료)**: Laser-activated antimicrobial therapy for peri-implant disease
- **GBR (Guided Bone Regeneration, 골유도재생술)**: Barrier membrane technique to fill intraosseous peri-implant defects
