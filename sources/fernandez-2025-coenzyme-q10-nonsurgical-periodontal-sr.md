## Why Ingested

CoQ10 ("잇몸 영양제") is one of the most heavily marketed periodontal supplements, yet the wiki held **zero pages** on it. This Fernandez 2025 SR is the cited evidence behind the CoQ10 claim and the most current (search to May 2024), most methodologically careful answer: it **stratifies by administration route**, which is exactly what resolves the controversy — local CoQ10 gel shows no effect on PD/CAL, whereas oral 120 mg/day supplementation shows a small benefit, all at *very low* certainty. It directly contradicts the earlier pooled MA [[wiki/periodontics/rasoolzadeh-2022-coenzyme-q10-periodontitis-sr-ma]] (which endorses CoQ10 gel), and pairs with the omega-3 host-modulation anchor [[wiki/periodontics/heo-2022-omega-3-fatty-acids-periodontitis-ma]]. Ingested as the CoQ10 anchor of the periodontal-adjunct supplement evidence map.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review of 10 RCTs (SRP ± CoQ10): locally delivered CoQ10 gel had no significant effect on PD/CAL, but oral supplementation (120 mg/day) gave small significant gains (PD −0.41 mm, CAL −0.52 mm) at 12 weeks — very low certainty of evidence.

## 한줄요약

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Systematic review with meta-analysis (10 RCTs).
- **Journal**: European Journal of Oral Sciences (2025;133(2):e70002).
- **Registration**: PROSPERO CRD42022311286.
- **Search window**: five databases up to May 2024.
- **Population/intervention**: adults with periodontitis, subgingival SRP with or without adjunctive CoQ10 (antioxidant).
- **Outcomes**: probing depth (PD), clinical attachment level (CAL), baseline→follow-up mean difference.
- **GRADE**: very low certainty.
- **Abstract-only — full text not retrieved (no PMC open access).**

## 2. Key Contributions

- Resolves the CoQ10 controversy by **route stratification**: separates local (gel/intra-pocket) from systemic (oral) delivery rather than pooling them.
- Shows the marketed *gel* route is the one without evidence; only *oral* supplementation moved PD/CAL.
- Provides formal GRADE certainty (very low), tempering any clinical recommendation.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Inclusion: RCTs only, SRP ± CoQ10, adults with periodontitis.
- Subgroups: local (topical n=1; intra-pocket n=3) vs oral supplementation (n=6).
- Risk of bias and certainty of evidence formally assessed.
- Meta-analytic mean differences for PD and CAL.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Route | PD reduction | CAL gain | Significant? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Local CoQ10 gel (topical/intra-pocket) | — | — | No significant effect |
| Oral 120 mg/day | −0.41 mm (95% CI 0.02–0.80) | −0.52 mm (95% CI 0.26–0.78) | Yes (12 wk) |

Overall certainty: **very low**.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Very low certainty — no firm evidence-based recommendation possible.
- Small number of trials per route; oral arm only 6 studies, short follow-up (≈12 weeks).
- Directly contradicts Rasoolzadeh 2022's pro-gel pooled conclusion (which mixed routes and gingivitis-leaning indices).
- Abstract-only ingest; full-text per-study detail not extracted.

## 7. Glossary

- **CoQ10 (coenzyme Q10 / ubiquinone)** — endogenous antioxidant marketed as a periodontal supplement.
- **PD / CAL** — probing depth / clinical attachment level.
- **GRADE** — system rating certainty of evidence (here: very low).
- **Route stratification** — analysing local vs systemic delivery separately rather than pooling.
