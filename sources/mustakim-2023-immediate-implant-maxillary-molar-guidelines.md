## Why Ingested

Maxillary-molar immediate placement is uniquely hard (wide socket, poor bone, sinus proximity) and our existing molar pages lacked an explicit two-axis case-selection scheme tying socket morphology AND vertical bone to surgical decisions. This Seoul National University retrospective (106 patients, 148 implants) gives a concrete maxillary-molar guideline — an ABH grade (A/B/C, socket-lifting decision) crossed with an interradicular-septum type (I/II/III, graft/wide-implant decision) — that extends the molar-socket classification framing in [[immediate-implant/smith-2013-classification-molar-extraction-immediate-implant]] into the maxillary sinus context, and complements the CBCT septum-anatomy planning in [[immediate-implant/deporter-2021-cbct-immediate-maxillary-molar-implant]].

## One-line Summary

Single-center retrospective (106 patients / 148 immediate maxillary-molar implants, 26 fully analyzed with 29 tapered-SLA fixtures) proposing a paired classification — alveolar bone height (ABH) Grade A/B/C and interradicular-septum Type I/II/III — to drive socket-lifting, bone-graft, and wide-implant decisions; 93.10% survival (2 early failures reinstalled), with fluctuating then stable marginal bone (≈0.01–0.03 mm loss after 1 y loading).

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **A two-axis case-selection scheme** specific to the maxillary molar — the first published combination of (a) **alveolar bone height (ABH)** governing the vertical/apical decision and (b) **interradicular-septum morphology** governing the lateral decision.
2. **ABH classification** (crestal bone → sinus floor): Grade A >8.0 mm (no socket lift), Grade B 6.0–8.0 mm (socket lift for longer implants), Grade C <6.0 mm (socket lift mandatory).
3. **Interradicular-septum classification**: Type I (septum supports implant, no graft needed), Type II (weak septum, graft required), Type III (septum absent, use wide/ultra-wide implant to engage socket walls).
4. **Step-by-step protocol** with 8 representative cases, plus two documented salvage (reinstallation) pathways (wide implant ± sinus lifting after early failure).
5. Reinforces six-month re-entry timing and atraumatic/flapless septum-preserving technique for maxillary-molar immediate implants.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Cohort**: 106 patients, 148 immediate implants (2011–2019, Seoul National University Dental Hospital). After inclusion/exclusion (notably excluding simultaneous lateral-window sinus augmentation and incomplete records), the detailed analytic group = **26 patients, 29 implants**.
- **Implants**: tapered, sand-blasted large-grit acid-etched (SLA) internal implants — 14 Luna (Shinhung), 14 Straumann, 1 Anyone (Megagen 6.5×8.5 mm wide). 20 bone-level, 9 tissue-level. Sites: 10×#16, 5×#17, 10×#26, 4×#27.
- **Technique**: 2% lidocaine/1:100,000 epinephrine; atraumatic flapless extraction; diamond fissure bur for root separation (preserves septum + buccal bone, irrigation to remove metal debris); fixture at mid-septum, parallel to central-fossa line, placed below lowest extraction margin. Grade C → blunt osteotome socket (sinus floor) lifting without bone graft. Type II → particulate allogeneic Oragraft + Bio-Gide collagen membrane. 4-0 Vicryl sutures.
- **Failure criteria**: ICOI Pisa Consensus implant health scale (pain on function, mobility, radiographic loss >½ implant length, uncontrolled exudate, implant loss).
- **MBL**: panoramic radiographs at T0 (immediate), T1 (1 mo), T2 (re-entry, 6 mo), T3 (1 mo post-load), T4 (1 y post-load); implant shoulder → most coronal BIC mesial+distal, mean of 3; paired Student's t-test, p<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Survival**: 2 of 26 patients failed before re-entry (one peri-implantitis with fistula, one sinusitis) → **93.10% survival** after initial treatment. Both were reinstalled immediately with wider-diameter implants + graft (and sinus lifting in the sinusitis case) and continued to function.
- **Demographics**: mean age 64.88 y (range 16–86); 11 male, 15 female.
- **MBL pattern (fluctuating then stable)**:
  - Bone *gain* (negative values) on mesial at T1 (−0.44±0.62 mm); distal at T2/T3/T4 (−0.27±0.52 / −0.16±0.60 / −0.28±0.52 mm) vs T0.
  - Significant bone regeneration in the first month, bone loss beginning ~6 months post-installation, remodeling-driven.
  - **Stable after 1 year of loading**: average bone loss 0.01±0.01 mm distal, 0.03±0.03 mm mesial.
- **Implant length recommendation**: 6–10 mm (<6 mm risks loss of primary stability; >10 mm risks sinus-membrane perforation). Short dental implants (SDI <8 mm) used immediately in 5 patients with favorable outcomes.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small detailed analytic cohort (26 patients / 29 implants) from the larger 148-implant pool; single center, single surgical team — limited external validity.
- Retrospective design, no control arm (delayed placement or no socket lifting); the classification is descriptive/expert-derived, not validated prospectively.
- Short-to-mid follow-up (re-entry 6 mo; MBL to 1 y post-loading); no long-term survival.
- Mixed implant brands/lengths/diameters and mixed bone-level vs tissue-level confound MBL comparison.
- The ABH/septum grading thresholds need prospective validation and inter-operator reliability testing.

## 6. Related Work

- Builds on prior molar-socket lateral-stability classifications (Smith & Tarnow) but adds the **vertical (ABH/sinus) axis** unique to the maxilla.
- Aligns with CBCT-based septum/anatomy planning for maxillary-molar immediate implants.
- Consistent with literature on osteotome/socket "up-fracture" sinus lifting and wide-/ultra-wide-diameter implants (>6 mm) for type-III sockets and salvage.
- Cites the same group's prior work on early failed-implant surfaces (non-infectious metal/inflammatory failure).

## 7. Glossary

- **ABH (Alveolar Bone Height)**: vertical bone from crestal bone to maxillary sinus floor; the paper's Grade A/B/C axis driving the socket-lifting decision.
- **Interradicular septum**: bone between the molar roots; the lateral-primary-stability axis (Type I/II/III) driving graft/wide-implant decisions.
- **Socket lifting**: transcrestal (osteotome) elevation of the sinus floor through the fresh socket, here done simultaneously with immediate placement.
- **SLA (sand-blasted, large-grit, acid-etched)**: implant surface treatment favoring osseointegration; all implants here were tapered SLA.
- **MBL (Marginal Bone Level)**: peri-implant crestal bone change; negative = gain, positive = loss in this paper.
- **SDI (Short Dental Implant)**: implant <8 mm length, used to avoid sinus lifting/vertical grafting.
- **ICOI Pisa Consensus**: implant health scale used to define failure.
- **BL / TL**: bone-level vs tissue-level implant (neck design affecting MBL and plaque retention).
