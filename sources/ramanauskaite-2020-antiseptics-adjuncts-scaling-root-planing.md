## Why Ingested
SRP 단독의 한계(깊은 치주낭·치근이개부 잔류 치석 최대 30%)를 보완하는 화학적 보조요법의 근거를 정리한 SR+MA로, [[periodontics/smiley-2015-nonsurgical-periodontitis-srp-guideline]]의 비외과적 치주치료 권고를 소독제 전달방식(서방형 vs 세척)별로 세분화해 확장한다. [[periodontics/sanz-2020-treatment-stage-i-iii-periodontitis-efp]]의 단계별 치료 권고에서 보조 항균제 선택의 근거 보강에 활용.

## One-line Summary
Systematic review + meta-analysis (12 RCTs, follow-up ≥6 mo) finding that sustained-release subgingival antiseptics (gels/chips/varnish) as adjuncts to SRP significantly improve PD reduction, CAL gain, and BOP (all p=0.001), whereas subgingival antiseptic irrigation adds no significant benefit over SRP alone.

## 한줄요약
체계적 문헌고찰+메타분석(RCT 12편, 추적 ≥6개월): 서방형 치은연하 소독제(겔·칩·바니시)를 SRP에 병용하면 치주낭깊이(PD)·임상부착수준(CAL)·탐침시출혈(BOP)이 유의하게 개선(모두 p=0.001)되었으나, 소독제 세척(irrigation) 병용은 SRP 단독 대비 유의한 추가 효과가 없었다.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Synthesizes RCT evidence on chemical antiseptic adjuncts to scaling and root planing (SRP) in nonsurgical periodontal therapy.
- Stratifies the evidence by **delivery vehicle**: (a) sustained-release antiseptics (gels, chips, varnish) vs (b) subgingival irrigation with antiseptic solutions — a clinically actionable distinction.
- Demonstrates a clear divergence: sustained-release delivery yields significant clinical benefit; irrigation does not.
- Covers a range of agents: chlorhexidine (CHX) gluconate (chip, xanthan gel, varnish, solution), povidone-iodine (PVI), sodium hypochlorite (NaOCl), and boric acid gel.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Databases searched**: MEDLINE (Ovid) and Cochrane Central Register of Controlled Trials (CENTRAL).
- **Date range**: articles published 1 January 2000 – 22 September 2019.
- **Inclusion**: English-language RCTs comparing one or more antiseptic agents as adjuncts to SRP, with follow-up ≥6 months.
- **Outcomes**: probing depth (PD) reduction, clinical attachment level (CAL) gain, bleeding on probing (BOP).
- **Analysis**: meta-analysis using a random-effects model on qualifying articles.
- **Included studies**: 12 RCTs (parallel and split-mouth designs) from Holland, India, Belgium, Spain, Germany, Greece, Italy (×3), Turkey, UK — chronic periodontitis (CP) cohorts, follow-up 6–12 months.
- Tested products included: 0.5% NaOCl solution, 0.75% boric acid gel, 10% PVI solution, CHX-xanthan gel, 0.12% CHX solution, 7.5% PVI solution, CHX chip, CHX varnish.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Sustained-release antiseptics (gels, chips, varnish) adjunct to SRP** — significant improvements over SRP alone:
  - PD reduction: p = 0.001
  - CAL gain: p = 0.001
  - BOP: p = 0.001
- **Subgingival irrigation with antiseptics** adjunct to SRP — no significant benefit over SRP alone:
  - PD: p = 0.321
  - CAL: p = 0.7568
  - BOP: p = 0.3549
- Individual study signals: Kanoriya 2017 (0.75% boric acid gel) and Paolantonio (CHX-xanthan gel / CHX chip) showed significantly greater PD reduction and CAL gain in test groups; several CHX-solution / PVI / NaOCl irrigation studies (Bizzarro 2017, Denez 2016, Krück 2012, Matesanz 2013) showed no significant difference vs control.
- **Conclusion**: Adjunctive subgingivally delivered antiseptics with sustained-release delivery have significant clinical benefits compared to SRP alone.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small number of qualifying RCTs (12) with heterogeneity in agents, vehicles, periodontal case definitions, and designs (parallel vs split-mouth).
- Variable smoking-status handling (some studies included, some excluded smokers; some not reported) and variable sample sizes; several studies reported NR for key covariates.
- Outcomes pooled across different agents within the sustained-release category, limiting agent-specific recommendations.
- English-only inclusion introduces possible language bias.
- Follow-up limited to ≥6 months — long-term (multi-year) maintainability not addressed.

## 6. Related Work
- Builds on the cause-related therapy / SRP-as-cornerstone concept of nonsurgical periodontal therapy.
- Complements clinical practice guidelines for nonsurgical SRP and EFP staged periodontitis treatment.
- Relevant to the broader literature on locally delivered antimicrobials/antiseptics in periodontal pockets.

## 7. Glossary
- **SRP** — Scaling and Root Planing; mechanical removal of soft/hardened microbial deposits from root surfaces.
- **PD** — Probing Depth (mm).
- **CAL** — Clinical Attachment Level (mm); CAL gain reflects attachment recovery.
- **BOP** — Bleeding on Probing (%); inflammation marker.
- **CHX** — Chlorhexidine gluconate.
- **PVI** — Povidone-iodine.
- **NaOCl** — Sodium hypochlorite.
- **CP** — Chronic Periodontitis.
- **Sustained-release delivery** — local vehicle (gel, chip, varnish) that releases antiseptic over time in the pocket.
- **Irrigation** — single-time subgingival flushing with an antiseptic solution.
