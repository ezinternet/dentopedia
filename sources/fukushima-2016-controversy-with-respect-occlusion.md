## Why Ingested

조기접촉(premature contact)을 "안정적 교두감합위(ICP)의 종착 조건"이라는 개념 축에서 정면으로 다루는 review. 기존 위키의 조기접촉 1차연구 [[occlusion/jahanbin-2026-occlusal-adjustment-post-orthodontic-tscan-rct]] (교정후 조기접촉 T-Scan RCT)와 [[occlusion/velasquez-2022-occlusal-analysis-natural-dentition-sr]] (교합분석 SR)이 *측정·조정* 층위라면, 본 논문은 그 위의 *개념 층위* — centric relation(CR)의 한계와, 변형 과두(deformed condyle) 환자에서 습관성 폐구운동(HCM)이 조기접촉 없이 종료되는 안정 ICP를 어떻게 확보하는지 — 를 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

Narrative review (single clinical case illustration, 25-yr follow-up) arguing that centric relation is unreliable in patients with deformed/unstable condyles, and that a stable intercuspal position where habitual closing movements terminate **without premature tooth contact** — guided by muscle-driven jaw positions rather than condylar position — should be the reference for occlusal reconstruction.

## 한줄요약

서술적 종설(임상증례 1건, 25년 추적): 변형·불안정 과두 환자에서는 중심위(CR)가 신뢰할 수 없으며, 조기접촉 없이 습관성 폐구운동(HCM)이 종료되는 안정적 교두감합위(ICP)를 — 과두 위치가 아니라 근육 유도 하악위로 — 기준 삼아 교합을 재건해야 한다고 주장.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Frames the contemporary occlusion controversy as: **centric relation (CR) is valid for physiologically normal joints but not for deformed/unstable condyles** — a distinction the Glossary of Prosthodontic Terms (GPT) now reflects.
- Separates two clinically decisive axes: (a) condyle deformed vs not — clinically minor; (b) condyle **stable vs unstable** in the fossa — clinically decisive.
- Promotes **muscle-induced mandibular positions** — muscular position (MP) and the terminal points of **habitual closing movements (HCM)** — as a substitute reference for CR when condylar position cannot be trusted.
- Defines the practical target: a **stable ICP where HCMs terminate without premature tooth contact**, continually inspected.
- Documents a 25-year successful outcome maintaining function on an anteriorly-positioned, bone-unsupported deformed condyle, provided the ICP stayed stable.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Design: narrative/conceptual review, not a systematic review; no pooled data, no quantitative synthesis.
- Evidence base cited: Dawson's adapted centric posture (ACP) concept; Posselt, Kawaguchi, Shigeta et al., Klineberg & Jagger on jaw movement/closing-path measurement; author's own HCM measurements.
- Quantitative anchors drawn from cited measurement studies:
  - HCM terminal-point scatter ≈ **0.38 mm antero-posterior, 0.27 mm lateral** (Kawaguchi).
  - Condyle travels only **0.3–0.8 mm during the last 5 mm** of HCM toward ICP (Fukushima) → condylar end-position is tightly constrained.
  - Posterior guidance of mandible **> ~1.5–2.0 mm** behind ICP flags an **unstable** condylar position (excessive posterior space).
- Illustrated by one clinical case (37-yr-old woman, bilateral missing lower molars, bilateral deformed condyles with dislocated discs) managed via occlusal splint equilibrated to stable HCM, then transferred to definitive prostheses.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Stable-ICP rule of thumb: if a stable ICP exists, the stomatognathic system generally tolerates even an unstable/deformed condyle; disorders (open bite, progressive condylar resorption) tend to appear when **both** ICP and condylar position are unstable.
- Instability screen: backward guidance from ICP exceeding **~1.5–2.0 mm**, or marked working-side condyle retrusion on lateral excursion, signals an unstable condyle.
- Condylar imaging (panoramic TMJ, tomography, CT, MRI) detects bone change but **cannot by itself** establish whether the condyle is stable; symptoms also do not reliably indicate deformity/stability.
- Case outcome: ICP transferred from a stable-HCM splint remained positionally and functionally stable for **25 years** on a bone-unsupported anterior condyle.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Author-stated: the ICP cannot always be determined from HCMs; HCMs only delimit a small appropriate area, and biological adaptation has limits.
- Methodological (identified): single-author narrative review with one illustrative case — lowest analytic evidence weight; no controls, no cohort, selection/confirmation bias possible; quantitative thresholds are borrowed from heterogeneous older measurement studies, not validated prospectively here.
- No diagnostic-accuracy data for the 1.5–2.0 mm instability cut-off; reproducibility of HCM-based ICP determination across operators not quantified.

## 7. Glossary

- **ICP (intercuspal position)**: maximum-intercuspation tooth contact position; the functional occlusal reference.
- **CR (centric relation)**: maxillomandibular relation defined (post-1987 GPT) by antero-superior condylar seating; historically the prosthodontic reference.
- **ACP (adapted centric posture)**: Dawson's term for a deformed condyle still comfortably braced by bone at the medial pole — CR remains usable.
- **HCM (habitual closing movement)**: reflex/muscle-driven jaw closure path that terminates at the ICP; proposed CR substitute.
- **MP (muscular position)**: horizontal mandibular contact position set by the reflex muscle pattern as the closing-movement terminus from rest.
- **MOP (median occlusal position)**: occlusal position acquired by a snap jaw closure (Klineberg & Jagger).
- **PCR (progressive condylar resorption)**: ongoing condylar bone loss associated with unstable joint loading.
- **Premature (tooth) contact**: an occlusal contact reached before full, even intercuspation — its absence at HCM termination defines a stable ICP here.
