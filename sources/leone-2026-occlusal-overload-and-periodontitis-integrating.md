## One-line Summary
2026 scoping review integrating mechanistic, animal, and clinical evidence, concluding occlusal trauma is a modifying (not initiating) factor that may exacerbate periodontitis in susceptible patients, with aMMP-8 biomarker integration and selective (not routine) occlusal intervention recommended given modest effect sizes.

## 한줄요약

## 2. Key Contributions
- Integrates mechanistic (inflammatory/proteolytic pathways, ECM degradation), animal, and clinical evidence on occlusal overload and periodontitis.
- Positions occlusal trauma as a modifying factor, not an initiating cause.
- Proposes aMMP-8 (active matrix metalloproteinase-8) biomarker analysis alongside occlusal assessment.
- Advocates selective rather than routine occlusal intervention given modest clinical effect sizes.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Scoping review synthesizing clinical, animal, and in vitro studies on the mechanistic and clinical interplay of excessive occlusal loading and periodontal breakdown.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Excessive occlusal loading on inflamed tissues may accelerate periodontal breakdown via inflammatory and proteolytic pathways.
- Occlusal trauma is a modifying factor that may exacerbate progression in susceptible individuals — not a disease initiator.
- aMMP-8 and ECM-degradation markers proposed as objective adjuncts.
- Clinical effect sizes are modest → selective occlusal intervention optimizes outcomes.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Scoping review (no pooled estimates / risk-of-bias synthesis).
- Mechanistic claims partly from animal/in vitro models.
- aMMP-8-guided occlusal management not yet validated in trials.

## 7. Glossary
- aMMP-8: active matrix metalloproteinase-8, a collagenase biomarker of active periodontal tissue destruction.
- Modifying factor: a factor that alters progression/severity of an established disease without initiating it.
- Scoping review: maps the extent/range of evidence on a topic without formal effect synthesis.
