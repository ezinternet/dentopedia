## Why Ingested

OMS 클러스터([[wiki/oral-medicine/psillas-2020-odontogenic-maxillary-sinusitis-comprehensive-review]], [[wiki/oral-medicine/lin-2024-odontogenic-maxillary-sinusitis-expert-consensus]])는 구치 발치 후 구강상악동교통 (Oroantral Communication, OAC) 발생이 OMS의 주요 원인임을 확립했지만, 어떤 수술적 폐쇄 술식이 가장 우수한지는 다루지 않았다. 본 SR+NMA(Oliva 2024)는 9건(7 RCT, n=519)의 네트워크 메타분석으로 협측지방패드 (Buccal Fat Pad, BFP)의 우위를 직접 비교하여 OAC 수술 결정 알고리즘을 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

SR + network meta-analysis of 9 studies (7 RCTs, n=519 OACs) found buccal fat pad significantly superior to buccal advancement flap (OR 15; 95% CI 3.52–63.83) and palatal rotational flap (OR 15.29; CI 2.63–88.93) for OAC/OAF closure.

## 한줄요약

9건(7 RCT, n=519) SR+네트워크 메타분석: 협측지방패드(BFP)가 구강상악동교통·누공 폐쇄에서 협측전진피판(OR 15) 및 구개회전피판(OR 15.29) 대비 통계적으로 유의하게 우수.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Dentistry Journal (MDPI)
- **Published**: 2024-05-20 (e-pub)
- **Study type**: Systematic review + network meta-analysis (SR+NMA)
- **PRISMA compliant**: Yes
- **Databases**: MEDLINE (PubMed), Scopus, Google Scholar
- **Included studies**: 9 studies (7 RCTs, 2 retrospective)
- **Total patients**: 519 OACs/OAFs
- **Follow-up range**: 3 weeks to 12 months

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First network meta-analysis** comparing all four main OAC/OAF closure modalities (buccal advancement flap, buccal fat pad, palatal rotational flap, PRF) in a single analysis.
2. **BFP superiority confirmed quantitatively**: OR 15 vs buccal flap (p=0.0002) and OR 15.29 vs palatal flap (p=0.002).
3. **PRF comparisons inconclusive** due to high inconsistency and limited study numbers (only 1 study, n=21+15).
4. **Timing critical**: 90–95% success if treatment within 24–48 h; after 48 h, OAF formation risk rises sharply with only ~67% secondary closure success.
5. Risk-of-bias (OHAT): 6 studies high risk, 3 low risk — evidence quality is moderate overall.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search strategy**: Keywords included "oroantral communication (OAC)," "oro antral fistula (OAF)," "antro-oral communication," "sinus communication," and related terms
- **Inclusion criteria**: RCTs or retrospective comparative studies with ≥20 patients comparing ≥2 surgical techniques; primary outcome = closure success rate
- **Exclusion criteria**: Secondary-surgery studies, studies with <20 patients, no comparison group
- **NMA tool**: CINeMA v1 (University of Bern) for confidence assessment
- **Statistics**: Odds ratio, fixed-effects model; I² for heterogeneity; χ² global inconsistency test (χ²=1.694, df=4, p=0.792 — no global inconsistency)
- **Network arms**: Buccal flap (n=227, 44%), BFP (n=156, 30%), Palatal flap (n=115, 22%), PRF (n=21, 4%)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Buccal Flap vs. Palatal Flap
- OR: −0.77 (95% CI: 0.29–2.04); p=0.60 — **no significant difference**
- I²=50%; heterogeneity moderate

### Buccal Flap vs. Buccal Fat Pad
- OR: 15 (95% CI: 3.52–63.83); p=0.0002 — **BFP significantly superior**
- I²=43%; 3 low-risk studies, 2 high-risk studies

### Palatal Flap vs. Buccal Fat Pad
- OR: 15.29 (95% CI: 2.63–88.93); p=0.002 — **BFP significantly superior**
- I²=0% — consistent

### OAC Etiologies
- Dental extraction: 76.3%
- Cystectomy: 12%
- Failed implants: 6%
- Sinus lift: 2.2%
- Tumor resection: 2.2%
- Endodontic therapy: 0.7%

### Spontaneous healing threshold
- <3 mm defects without infection: may heal spontaneously via blood clot organization
- >3–5 mm with infection: surgical closure required

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Small included studies pool**: Only 9 studies eligible; most with ≥20 patients minimum but still small sample sizes
- **Mixed study designs**: Inclusion of 2 retrospective studies introduces confounding risk
- **High risk of bias** in 6/9 studies; limited blinding feasibility in surgical studies
- **PRF comparisons inconclusive**: Single PRF study (Bilginaylar 2019) insufficient for robust comparison
- **Follow-up heterogeneity**: 3 weeks to 12 months makes outcome comparison difficult
- **Grey literature excluded**: May introduce publication bias
- Future: Large multicenter RCTs; standardized follow-up (minimum 6 months); patient-reported outcomes (VAS pain documented in some studies: PRF=6, buccal flap=1.4)

## 6. Related Work

- Lin et al. 2024: OMS expert consensus — treatment sequencing including OAC closure before sinus management

## 7. Glossary

- **OAC (Oroantral Communication, 구강상악동교통)**: Pathological pathway between oral cavity and maxillary sinus; <3 mm may heal spontaneously; >5 mm requires surgical closure
- **OAF (Oroantral Fistula, 구강상악동누공)**: Epithelialized OAC; occurs when perforation persists >48–72 h; epithelialization prevents spontaneous closure
- **BFP (Buccal Fat Pad, 협측지방패드)**: Pedicled fat pad flap (Stensen duct landmark); full epithelialization in 4–6 weeks; main risk = trismus
- **Buccal Advancement Flap (협측전진피판)**: Rehrmann (1936) trapezoid flap; risk = sulcus depth reduction in 50% of cases
- **Palatal Rotational Flap (구개회전피판)**: Full-thickness palate-based flap; good blood supply via palatal artery; donor site secondary healing = painful
- **PRF (Platelet-Rich Fibrin, 혈소판풍부피브린)**: 2nd-generation platelet concentrate; membrane placed over socket without thrombin activation
- **NMA (Network Meta-Analysis, 네트워크 메타분석)**: Simultaneous comparison of multiple treatments using direct and indirect evidence; evaluated via CINeMA
- **Valsalva maneuver (발살바법)**: Diagnostic test for OAC — positive if air bubbles appear in nose during forced exhalation with closed mouth/nose
