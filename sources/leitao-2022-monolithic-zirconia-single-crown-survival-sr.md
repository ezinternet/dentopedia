## Why Ingested

First SR+MA specifically focused on monolithic zirconia (MZ) single crowns with CAD/CAM — fills the gap left by Pjetursson 2018, which explicitly excluded MZ due to insufficient follow-up. Distinguishes MZ from veneered zirconia and provides pooled survival estimates for clinical decision-making. [[dental-materials/warreth-2020-all-ceramic-restorations-narrative-review]]

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 9 studies (n=594 patients, n=1,657 restorations): monolithic zirconia CAD/CAM single crowns show survival rates 91–100% with high heterogeneity (I²=93.74%); no correlation between SR and cementation type or follow-up duration.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of Prosthodontic Research, 2022; 66(3):374–384
- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis
- **Registration**: PROSPERO CRD42020166112
- **Search**: PubMed/Medline, Embase, Cochrane Library; search period through May 2020
- **Quality assessment**: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS) — all studies moderate quality

## 2. Key Contributions

- First dedicated SR+MA on monolithic zirconia (MZ) tooth-supported single crowns
- Distinguishes MZ from veneered zirconia (which was the focus of prior SRs including Pjetursson 2018)
- Highlights scarcity of long-term (>5 year) clinical data on MZ
- Reports BOP (bleeding on probing) as the primary biological complication metric

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PRISMA-compliant, PROSPERO registered
- Databases: Medline/PubMed, Embase, Cochrane Library (searched February–May 2020)
- Inclusion: adult patients, MZ CAD/CAM tooth-supported SCs, minimum 3-month follow-up
- Exclusion: gray literature, congress abstracts, non-English publications
- Quality assessment: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS)
- Statistical: random effects model, I² for heterogeneity, Cochran Q test, linear regression (SR vs cementation type, SR vs follow-up duration)
- 1,298 initial references → 9 final included studies

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Survival**
- Range: 91% to 100% across studies
- Overall trend toward high survival; one study with bruxism patients showed marginal integrity SR of 31.60%
- Mean follow-up exposure: 1.07 years (range 0.3–2.1 years)
- Total: 594 participants, 1,657 restorations

**Biological complications**
- Bleeding on probing (BOP): mean 29.12%
- No major periodontal or bone loss events reported in short follow-up

**Technical complications**
- Failures/fractures (requiring replacement): observed in 3 studies
- Most fractures were complete, requiring restoration replacement
- High marginal integrity success rates — except in bruxism subgroup

**Statistical**
- High heterogeneity: I² = 93.74%, Q = 79.672
- No significant correlation between SR and cementation type (p=0.730)
- No significant correlation between SR and mean follow-up years (p=0.454)
- Wide CI due to heterogeneity; fixed-effect model not applicable

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Very short mean follow-up (1.07 years); no studies >2.1 years follow-up
- High heterogeneity (I²=93.74%) limits pooled conclusions
- All studies moderate quality (NOS); no RCTs
- Insufficient data to compare with veneered zirconia or metal-ceramic
- Need for longer-term RCTs and standardized success criteria

## 6. Related Work

- Pjetursson et al. 2018 — SR on veneered zirconia implant SCs (explicitly excluded MZ)
- Warreth 2020 — narrative review of all-ceramic systems including MZ

## 7. Glossary

- **MZ**: Monolithic zirconia — full-contour zirconia without veneering ceramic layer
- **CAD/CAM**: Computer-aided design / computer-aided manufacturing
- **NOS**: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale — quality assessment for non-randomized studies
- **BOP**: Bleeding on probing — periodontal health indicator
- **I²**: Inconsistency statistic for heterogeneity (>75% = substantial)
- **3Y-TZP**: 3 mol% yttria-doped tetragonal zirconia polycrystal — standard dental zirconia
- **5Y-PSZ**: 5 mol% yttria-stabilized partially stabilized zirconia — translucent variant
