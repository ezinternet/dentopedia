## One-line Summary

Overview of 18 systematic reviews: CAT is effective for mild-to-moderate malocclusion but inferior for severe cases and specific movements (torque, extrusion, rotation); periodontal health better and root-resorption risk lower with CAT, relapse greater, duration possibly shorter in mild cases; overall evidence moderate.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Single highest-level synthesis (SR-of-SRs) of CAT vs FAT clinical effectiveness.
- Maps the malocclusion-severity boundary: aligners viable for mild-moderate, not severe.
- Cross-domain summary (efficacy, periodontal, root resorption, relapse, pain, duration).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Search to 15 Jul 2021; 361 reviews screened -> 18 SRs included after excluding non-relevant/low-quality. Two-author screening, quality assessment, extraction. Narrative synthesis of moderate/high-quality reviews only.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Effective for mild-to-moderate malocclusion; inferior for severe cases and torque/extrusion/rotation.
- Periodontal health better with CAT; root-resorption risk tended lower with CAT.
- Relapse greater with CAT; duration conflicting but possibly shorter in mild-moderate.
- Pain unclear but CAT more comfortable, less impact on eating/chewing.
- Overall evidence level: moderate.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Umbrella inherits primary-review quality limits; abstract-only ingest (full text not retrieved).
- Calls for high-quality RCTs, esp. severe cases and specific movements.
