## Why Ingested

Extends the fluoride-varnish caries-prevention evidence base in [[wiki/caries/kumar-2026-fluoride-varnish-caries-prevention-cost-effectiveness-sr-ma]] into the high-risk radiation-caries population. Whereas the cost-effectiveness SR+MA addresses general caries prevention, this RCT tests whether fluoride varnish (and/or saliva substitute) actually halts the aggressive DMFS progression seen in irradiated head & neck cancer (HNC) survivors — a negative, sobering result that qualifies the optimism of single-agent fluoride prevention.

## One-line Summary

3-arm RCT (n=482 irradiated HNC patients) — DMFS increased significantly (P<0.05) in saliva-substitute, fluoride-varnish, and combined groups at all timepoints, with no intergroup difference (Bonferroni NS); neither agent halted radiation-caries progression.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Head-to-head 3-arm RCT in a large irradiated HNC cohort comparing **salivary substitute**, **fluoride varnish**, and **both combined** for radiation-caries (RC) prevention.
- Demonstrates that, in this high-risk population, **no single agent nor the combination halted DMFS progression** — a negative finding that reframes fluoride varnish from a stand-alone solution to one component of a multimodal protocol.
- Reinforces the clinical message that radiation caries is **multifactorial** and requires gland-sparing radiotherapy + professional care + stringent home care + regular follow-up + patient education together.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: 3-arm randomized controlled trial.
- **Population**: HNC patients meeting inclusion criteria, randomly assigned after comprehensive oral care.
- **Arms**:
  - Group I — salivary substitute (advised liberally at home before major meals)
  - Group II — fluoride varnish (applied professionally **before RT, immediately after RT, then at 3-month intervals**)
  - Group III — both
- **Outcome**: Decayed, Missing, and Filled Surfaces (**DMFS**) score recorded **before radiotherapy (RT)**, then at **3, 6, and 12 months post-RT**.
- **Statistics**: intergroup comparison by Bonferroni test.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Recruited**: 482 patients (407 males, 75 females; mean age 51.32 y, range 18–79).
- **12-month complete data**: 67 (Group I) / 73 (Group II) / 80 (Group III).
- **DMFS increase**: statistically significant (**P<0.05**) in all three groups across all time points (3/6/12 mo).
- **Intergroup comparison**: Bonferroni test **non-significant** — no group superior to another.
- **Conclusion**: Neither saliva substitute nor fluoride varnish, alone or combined, halted radiation-caries progression. Multimodal management (gland-sparing RT, professional care, home care, follow-up, education) is paramount.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Abstract-only ingest** — methods detail (randomization/blinding, fluoride concentration, saliva-substitute composition, adherence measurement) not available; quality appraisal limited.
- Substantial attrition by 12 months (482 recruited → 67/73/80 with complete data), reducing power and risking attrition bias in this morbid cancer population.
- No untreated/placebo control arm reported — all arms received an active agent plus comprehensive oral care, so the absolute preventive effect vs no intervention cannot be isolated.
- Single-center cohort; generalizability across RT modalities (IMRT vs conventional, gland-sparing) not stratified in the abstract.

## 7. Glossary

- **Radiation caries (RC)**: rapidly progressive cervical/incisal caries in head & neck cancer survivors, driven by radiation-induced hyposalivation, dietary change, and impaired oral hygiene.
- **DMFS**: Decayed, Missing, and Filled Surfaces — surface-level caries experience index.
- **HNC**: head and neck cancer.
- **Salivary substitute**: artificial saliva product used to relieve xerostomia and provide a moist oral environment.
- **Fluoride varnish**: professionally applied high-concentration topical fluoride for enamel remineralization/caries prevention.
- **Gland-sparing RT**: radiotherapy planning (e.g., IMRT) that limits dose to salivary glands to preserve salivary function.
- **Bonferroni test**: multiple-comparison correction used for intergroup statistical testing.
