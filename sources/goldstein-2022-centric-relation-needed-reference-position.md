## Why Ingested

This is the "CR is a needed, defensible reference position" counter-review responding directly to the abandon-CR movement; it rebuts [[occlusion/zonnenberg-2021-centric-relation-critically-revisited-clinical]], which argues CR should be discarded. Ingested to balance the CR/CO/MICP terminology batch with the pro-retention side: CR is reproducible and clinically validated for full-arch reconstruction, and the genuine problem is definitional/recording inconsistency rather than the concept itself.

## One-line Summary

Narrative review (abstract-only) arguing that centric relation is a reproducible, clinically validated reference position for diagnosis and full-arch reconstruction with a long track record; the real problem is lack of consensus on definition and recording method, not the concept, and any alternative position should be given a different name.

## 2. Key Contributions

(From abstract; full text not retrieved)

- Defends centric relation (CR) as a "needed reference position" against the movement to abolish it.
- Concedes the valid concerns: there is little consensus on CR's **definition** or its **method of recording**, and this ambiguity is what fuels calls for abolition.
- Asserts consensus that CR is an appropriate **restorative position for full-arch reconstruction**, with no substantive clinical research contradicting this use.
- Frames CR as **reproducible** and usable for both diagnostic and restorative procedures, supported by substantial scientific evidence.
- Notes numerous well-documented, all **intermaxillary** techniques can replicate the position.
- Concludes there is **little scientific evidence** to dictate where the exact condyle-in-fossa position should be.
- Normative rule: if a clinician uses a different position, it should carry a **different name** (preserve terminological precision rather than redefine CR).

## 7. Glossary

- **Centric relation (CR)**: maxillomandibular reference position with the condyles in their anterosuperior position against the articular eminences, independent of tooth contact; used as a reproducible starting position for diagnosis and full-arch restoration.
- **Reference position**: a reproducible jaw relationship used as a starting/registration point for diagnostic and restorative procedures.
- **Full-arch reconstruction**: comprehensive restoration of an entire dental arch, where a stable, reproducible jaw relation (CR) is needed to organize the occlusion.
- **Intermaxillary technique**: a CR-recording method relying on relating the maxilla and mandible (e.g., bimanual manipulation, leaf gauge, anterior deprogrammer) rather than instrumented condylar tracking.
- **Condyle-in-fossa position**: the spatial relationship of the mandibular condyle within the glenoid fossa; the abstract notes weak evidence for any single "ideal" position.
