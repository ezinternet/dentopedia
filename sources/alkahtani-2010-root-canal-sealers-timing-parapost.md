## Why Ingested

ParaPost 제품의 접착(resin cement) 밀폐성을 다룬 임상실험. 근관 sealer 종류(eugenol/non-eugenol)와 cementation 타이밍이 ParaPost 미세누출에 영향을 주는지 검증. 기존 [[post-and-core/naumann-2017-ferrule-first-post-second-systematic-review]]의 "post는 유지가 목적이지 밀폐가 아니다" 관점을 미세누출 데이터로 보강. abstract-only (full text not retrieved).

## One-line Summary

In-vitro fluid-filtration microleakage study (70 single-rooted teeth, 4 groups × 15 + 2 controls): microleakage of resin-cemented ParaPost increased significantly over 3 months regardless of sealer type (eugenol vs non-eugenol) or cementation timing (immediate vs delayed); immediate cementation with AH26 gave the least leakage.

## 한줄요약

인비트로 fluid-filtration 미세누출 연구 (단근치 70개, 4군×15 + 대조 2군): resin cement로 합착한 ParaPost의 미세누출은 sealer 종류나 cementation 타이밍과 무관하게 3개월에 걸쳐 유의하게 증가; AH26 즉시 합착이 가장 적은 누출.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Tests whether sealer chemistry and cementation timing modulate resin-cemented ParaPost seal — both found non-significant.
- Time is the dominant variable: microleakage rises significantly from 24 h → 2 mo → 3 mo in every group.
- Voids at the resin–dentin interface identified as a persistent failure feature.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 70 extracted single-rooted human teeth, crown-down instrumentation to ProFile size 50 .04 + Gates Glidden 2–5.
- Four experimental groups (n=15) by 2×2 factorial: sealer (eugenol vs non-eugenol/AH26) × timing (immediate vs delayed post cementation); resin cement luting of ParaPost.
- Two control groups (n=5). Microleakage assessed by fluid filtration at 24 h, 2 months, 3 months.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Microleakage significantly increased over time in all groups (24 h < 2 mo < 3 mo).
- Least microleakage: immediate cementation after AH26 (non-eugenol) obturation at all three time points.
- Sealer type and timing: no significant effect on overall leakage.
- Voids at resin–dentin interface present across groups.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro fluid-filtration surrogate for clinical seal; single post product (ParaPost) and one resin cement.
- Only abstract available in this record; group-level numeric leakage values not extracted.
- 3-month observation; no long-term or thermocycling data.

## 6. Related Work

- Eugenol–resin interference literature; AH26 non-eugenol sealer compatibility with resin cements; fluid-filtration microleakage methodology.

## 7. Glossary

- **Microleakage**: fluid/bacterial penetration along the restoration–tooth interface.
- **Fluid filtration**: quantitative method measuring fluid movement through a leakage pathway.
- **AH26**: epoxy-resin (non-eugenol) root canal sealer.
- **Eugenol**: phenolic component of ZOE sealers that can inhibit resin polymerization.
