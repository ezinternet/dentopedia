## Why Ingested

This is the foundational study that defined the subgingival microbial "complexes" (red, orange, yellow, green, purple) by which periodontal microbiology has been organized for 25+ years; ingested as the historical anchor against which later community-ecology models are read. It is the "red complex / specific-pathogen" paradigm that [[oral-microbiology/hajishengallis-2012-psd-model-periodontal-disease]] explicitly moves "beyond" toward polymicrobial synergy and dysbiosis, and it grounds the network/keystone organization synthesized in [[oral-microbiology/sedghi-2021-oral-microbiome-key-organisms-networks]].

## One-line Summary

Landmark cross-sectional study (185 subjects, 13,261 plaque samples, 40 taxa by checkerboard DNA-DNA hybridization) that defined five recurrent subgingival microbial complexes, with the "red complex" (P. gingivalis, T. forsythia, T. denticola) most tightly linked to pocket depth and bleeding on probing.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Defined **five reproducible microbial complexes** in subgingival plaque that emerged consistently regardless of the multivariate clustering/ordination method used (phi-coefficient clustering, principal components analysis, correspondence analysis).
- Introduced the now-canonical **color-coded complex nomenclature** (red, orange, yellow, green, purple, plus outliers) that has structured periodontal microbiology research and teaching ever since.
- Established the **"red complex"** — *Porphyromonas gingivalis*, *Tannerella forsythia* (then *Bacteroides forsythus*), *Treponema denticola* — as the tightly co-occurring group most strongly associated with clinical disease severity.
- Demonstrated an **ecological succession structure**: early/health-associated complexes (yellow streptococci, purple, green) versus a late/disease-associated bridge (orange) leading to the red complex.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Subjects**: 185 adults (mean age 51 ± 16 years); 160 with periodontitis, 25 without.
- **Sampling**: subgingival plaque from the mesial aspect of every tooth → **13,261 plaque samples**.
- **Detection**: presence and levels of **40 subgingival taxa** quantified using whole-genomic DNA probes and **checkerboard DNA-DNA hybridization** (a high-throughput method co-developed by this group enabling many samples × many species simultaneously).
- **Clinical data**: assessed at 6 sites per tooth (pocket depth, bleeding on probing, etc.).
- **Statistics**: pairwise species similarity by **phi coefficients**; clustering by **averaged unweighted linkage sort**; community ordination by **principal components analysis** and **correspondence analysis**.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

The five major complexes (with the standard color names by which they became known):

- **Red complex**: *Bacteroides forsythus* (now *Tannerella forsythia*), *Porphyromonas gingivalis*, *Treponema denticola* — tightly related; most strongly associated with **increased pocket depth and bleeding on probing**.
- **Orange complex** (the "bridge" / disease-associated core): *Fusobacterium nucleatum/periodonticum* subspecies, *Prevotella intermedia*, *Prevotella nigrescens*, *Peptostreptococcus micros*, with associated species *Eubacterium nodatum*, *Campylobacter rectus*, *Campylobacter showae*, *Streptococcus constellatus*, *Campylobacter gracilis*.
- **Yellow complex**: *Streptococcus sanguis*, *S. oralis*, *S. mitis*, *S. gordonii*, *S. intermedius* (early colonizers, health-associated).
- **Green complex**: three *Capnocytophaga* species, *Campylobacter concisus*, *Eikenella corrodens*, *Aggregatibacter (Actinobacillus) actinomycetemcomitans* serotype a.
- **Purple complex**: *Veillonella parvula*, *Actinomyces odontolyticus*.
- **Outliers** (little relation to each other or the five complexes): *A. actinomycetemcomitans* serotype b, *Selenomonas noxia*, *Actinomyces naeslundii* genospecies 2 (*A. viscosus*).

The red complex related **strikingly** to clinical measures of periodontal disease — specifically pocket depth and bleeding on probing.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Cross-sectional, association-only**: complexes describe co-occurrence and correlation with disease, not causation or temporal sequence; later longitudinal/mechanistic work was required to interpret them.
- **Restricted probe panel** (40 cultivable/known taxa): predates 16S/metagenomic surveys that revealed 700+ oral species and many uncultivated/unsuspected taxa, so the complex framework necessarily undersamples the true community.
- **Specific-pathogen framing**: the red-complex emphasis later proved reductionist — keystone-pathogen and polymicrobial-synergy-and-dysbiosis (PSD) models reinterpret these same organisms as low-abundance community remodelers rather than independent agents of disease.

## 6. Related Work

- Listgarten MA (1976, 1994) — structure of the microbial flora in periodontal health and disease (microscopic plaque architecture preceding this molecular survey).
- Haffajee AD & Socransky SS (1994) — microbial etiological agents of destructive periodontal diseases (Periodontol 2000).

- Successor frameworks: keystone-pathogen and PSD models (Hajishengallis & Lamont, 2012) that move "beyond the red complex."

## 7. Glossary

- **Checkerboard DNA-DNA hybridization**: a membrane-based method allowing simultaneous quantification of many bacterial species across many plaque samples using whole-genomic DNA probes; the workhorse technique of this study.
- **Microbial complex**: a group of bacterial species that co-occur in plaque more often than expected by chance, defined here by clustering/ordination of co-detection data.
- **Red complex**: *P. gingivalis* + *T. forsythia* + *T. denticola*; the disease-associated triad most tightly linked to pocket depth and bleeding.
- **Orange complex**: the disease-associated "bridge" community (notably *F. nucleatum*, *P. intermedia*, *P. micros*) whose presence precedes/accompanies red-complex emergence.
- **Phi coefficient**: a measure of association between two binary variables (here, co-detection of species pairs) used to build the similarity matrix.
- **Bleeding on probing (BOP)**: a clinical sign of gingival inflammation; one of the two clinical measures most associated with the red complex.
