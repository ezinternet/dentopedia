## Why Ingested

Provides direct SR+MA evidence comparing splinted (bar) vs unsplinted (ball, stud) overdenture attachments on marginal bone loss, prosthetic complications, and implant survival — a core decision point in mandibular implant overdenture prosthodontics. Complements [[complete-denture/prasad-2024-attachment-systems-mandibular-implant]] which covers individual attachment type comparisons (LOCATOR vs magnet vs ball) and [[complete-denture/bi-2022-attachment-type-implant-number-overdenture]] which addresses implant number and attachment interaction effects.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 9 studies (984 implants, 380 patients) found no significant difference between splinted and unsplinted overdenture attachment systems in marginal bone loss, prosthetic complications, or implant survival.

- **Authors**: R. S. Leão, S. L. D. Moraes, B. C. E. Vasconcelos, C. A. A. Lemos, E. P. Pellizzer
- **Journal**: Journal of Oral Rehabilitation
- **Year/Volume**: 2018, Volume 45, Issue 8, Pages 647–656
- **DOI**: 10.1111/joor.12651
- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis (PRISMA-compliant)

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Splinting vs unsplinting equivalence**: No statistically significant difference between splinted and unsplinted systems across all three primary outcomes (MBL, complications, survival).
2. **PICO-driven SR+MA**: Focused specifically on the clinical question of splinting — separates this from attachment-type comparisons in broader reviews.
3. **Marginal bone loss quantified**: MD −0.11 mm (95% CI: −0.37 to 0.14), P=.39 — favoring no difference.
4. **Prosthetic complications**: RR 1.26 (95% CI: 0.80–1.99), P=.31 — no significant difference.
5. **Implant survival**: RR 0.37% (95% CI: 0.10–1.36), P=.14 — no significant difference.
6. **Clinical implication**: Choice between splinted (bar) and unsplinted (ball/stud) attachments may be guided by patient preference, cost, and maintenance burden rather than bone or complication risk.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **PICO question**: Do splinted overdenture attachment systems promote better clinical results in comparison to unsplinted systems?
- **Databases**: PubMed/MEDLINE, Scopus, Cochrane (search up to October 2017)
- **Search terms**: "overdenture AND attachment OR overdenture AND bar OR overdenture splinted"
- **Inclusion criteria**:
  - RCTs and prospective studies with ≥10 participants
  - Minimum follow-up of 6 months
  - Studies comparing splinted vs unsplinted systems within the same study
  - Published in English
- **Exclusion**: Retrospective studies, case series <10, no comparison between splinted/unsplinted
- **Risk of bias**: Not explicitly specified in extracted text; PRISMA framework used
- **Statistics**: Random-effects model; 95% CI; Mean Difference (MBL) and Relative Risk (complications, survival)
- **Included studies**: 9 articles (from initial multi-stage screening)
- **Total**: 984 implants in 380 patients (mean age 62.8 years)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Primary Outcomes

| Outcome | Effect size | 95% CI | P-value |
|---------|------------|--------|---------|
| Marginal bone loss | MD: −0.11 mm | −0.37 to 0.14 | .39 |
| Prosthetic complications | RR: 1.26 | 0.80–1.99 | .31 |
| Implant survival rate | RR: 0.37% | 0.10–1.36 | .14 |

- All three outcomes showed **no statistically significant difference** between splinted and unsplinted systems.
- Both splinted and unsplinted systems achieved comparable clinical performance across bone preservation, prosthetic maintenance, and implant retention.

### Study Sample

- 9 studies included (qualitative + quantitative analysis)
- 984 total implants
- 380 patients
- Mean patient age: 62.8 years

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Heterogeneity risk**: Pooling multiple implant systems, follow-up durations, and surgical protocols may introduce heterogeneity.
- **Small number of included studies**: Only 9 articles passed inclusion criteria — limits statistical power.
- **Follow-up variability**: Minimum 6-month follow-up may not capture long-term divergence in outcomes.
- **Mostly mandibular focus**: Most overdenture literature concentrates on the mandible; maxillary overdenture evidence is sparse.
- **Unsplinted subtypes not separated**: Ball vs stud vs LOCATOR not distinguished within the "unsplinted" category — masks within-group variation.
- **Future**: RCTs with longer follow-up and standardized reporting on patient-reported outcomes (OHRQoL) and maintenance costs would strengthen the evidence base.

## 6. Related Work

- Naert et al. (2004): 10-year RCT comparing splinted vs unsplinted mandibular overdentures — foundational long-term study cited in this SR.
- Stoumpis & Kohal (2011): Earlier systematic review on splinting in overdenture therapy — this Leão 2018 SR updates and extends it with meta-analysis.
- Prasad et al. (2024): SR+MA of RCTs comparing individual attachment types (LOCATOR, ball, magnet, bar) — broader scope but confirms no splinting advantage on biological complications (P=.902).
- McGill Consensus (Feine et al. 2002): Two-implant mandibular overdenture as first-choice standard — contextual anchor for overdenture evidence reviews.

## 7. Glossary

- **Splinted attachment**: Bar connecting two or more implants before prosthetic retention (e.g., Dolder bar, Hader bar) — splints implants biomechanically
- **Unsplinted attachment**: Independent retention per implant (e.g., ball/stud, LOCATOR, magnet) — no rigid inter-implant connection
- **Marginal bone loss (MBL)**: Radiographic bone level change at implant neck — key periimplant health indicator
- **Prosthetic complications**: Maintenance events such as clip fracture, matrix wear, retentive insert replacement, denture fracture
- **RR (Relative Risk)**: Ratio of complication/failure probability between groups
- **MD (Mean Difference)**: Absolute difference in continuous outcome (MBL in mm) between groups
