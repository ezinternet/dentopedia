## One-line Summary
하악 제3대구치 발치 후 하치조신경 손상 문헌 고찰 (JOMR 2014): 14편 분석; 유병률 0.35–8.4%; 24세 초과, 수평매복, 레지던트 시술이 위험인자; 영구적 손상은 드묾.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Oral & Maxillofacial Research 2014, 5(4): e1
- **DOI**: 10.5037/jomr.2014.5401
- **Published**: October–December 2014 (accepted 22 November 2014)
- **Study type**: Literature review (systematic)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive overview of IAN injury after mandibular third molar extraction: incidence, risk factors, recovery, and treatment options
- Search: PubMed; January 2009–June 2014; English language; ≥6 months follow-up; injury assessed by patient report, radiographic, and neurosensory testing
- 84 sources reviewed; 14 most relevant selected
- IAN paresthesia incidence: **0.35–8.4%** (wide range depending on technique and patient population)
- Permanent injury: very rare; most cases recover
- Risk assessment: radiographic signs of intimate IAN-root relationship predictive of injury

## 3. Methodology
- **Database**: PubMed
- **Period**: January 2009–June 2014
- **Inclusion**: English language; ≥6 months follow-up; injury by patient report + radiographic + neurosensory testing
- **Outcomes**: Incidence of IAN paresthesia/injury, risk factors, recovery rates, treatment methods

## 4. Key Results
| Risk Factor | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Age >24 years | Increased IAN injury risk (root completion, closer nerve proximity) |
| Horizontal impaction | Highest impaction-type risk |
| Trainee surgeon | Increased risk vs experienced surgeon |
| Radiographic IAC contact signs | Predictive of injury |

- **Recovery**: Most paresthesias resolve; permanent dysesthesia rare
- **Treatment options reviewed**: Pharmacotherapy (steroids, B vitamins, low-level laser therapy); nerve repair surgery for severe cases

## 5. Limitations
- Literature search limited to 2009–2014; older landmark studies may be excluded
- No meta-analysis; qualitative synthesis
- Study heterogeneity in injury definition and assessment method
