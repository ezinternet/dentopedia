## Why Ingested

Denture hygiene is a critical aspect of removable dental prosthetics management, yet evidence on optimal cleaning protocols is scattered. This SR consolidates clinical evidence (44 studies, PROSPERO-registered) on biofilm removal efficacy and material property effects of cleaning interventions — directly relevant to [[wiki/complete-denture/srinivasan-2021-cad-cam-removable-complete-dentures]], where CAD/CAM dentures require evidence-based hygiene protocols.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review of 44 prospective clinical studies (1975–2020) showing that combined mechanical + chemical denture cleaning is significantly more effective than either approach alone for biofilm removal from removable dental prostheses.

## 한줄요약

44개 전향적 임상연구(1975–2020)의 체계적 문헌고찰로, 기계적+화학적 의치 세정의 병용이 단독 세정보다 생물막 제거에 유의하게 우수함을 확인한 연구.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of Evidence Based Dental Practice
- **Volume/Issue**: Volume 21, Issue 4, December 2021, 101644
- **Study type**: Systematic review (no meta-analysis)
- **PROSPERO registration**: CRD42020189649
- **Search period**: June 1975 – May 2020
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane (PubMed, Embase, ICTRP, ClinicalTrials.gov) + manual search
- **Total included studies**: 44 prospective clinical trials
- **Minimum sample size**: ≥10 participants per included study

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First PROSPERO-registered SR focusing exclusively on prospective clinical studies of denture cleaning for RDPs (removable dental prostheses)
2. Establishes combined mechanical + chemical cleaning as the gold standard for biofilm removal
3. Highlights severe gap in clinical evidence regarding the effect of cleaning on denture base material properties (color stability, surface roughness, dimensional stability)
4. Covers all major cleaning modalities: brushing, chemical soaking (effervescent tablets, alkaline peroxide, sodium hypochlorite, Ricinus communis), microwave disinfection, ultrasonic cleaning

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**PICO framework**:
- **P**: Patients wearing removable dental prostheses (RDPs) — complete and partial
- **I/C**: Various denture hygiene interventions (mechanical, chemical, or combined)
- **O1**: Efficacy in removing biofilms and microorganisms from RDP surfaces (in situ)
- **O2**: Effects of hygiene interventions on denture base material properties

**Inclusion criteria**:
- Prospective clinical trials
- Minimum 10 participants
- Investigating in situ biofilm/microorganism removal from RDPs or denture base materials
- English-language publications indexed in PubMed or Cochrane

**Exclusion**: retrospective studies, in vitro studies, studies with <10 participants, non-English publications

**Initial database yield**: 4,307 potentially relevant records
**Final inclusion**: 44 studies after title/abstract and full-text screening

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Biofilm Removal Efficacy

| Cleaning Method | Efficacy |
|---|---|
| Brushing alone | Effective mechanical removal but technique-dependent |
| Chemical soaking alone (alkaline peroxide) | Moderate; effective against Candida |
| Sodium hypochlorite | Effective antimicrobial; concentration/duration matters |
| Combined brushing + chemical | **Most effective** — superior to single modality |
| Microwave disinfection | Effective (supported by clinical data); used as adjunct |
| Ultrasonic cleaning | Effective; emerging as convenient alternative to brushing |

**Key conclusion**: The combined use of mechanical and chemical hygiene interventions is significantly more effective than single cleaning approaches for removing biofilms from RDP surfaces in situ.

### Material Properties

- **Limited clinical evidence** available on the effect of denture hygiene on material properties
- Some chemical agents (notably sodium hypochlorite at high concentrations) may adversely affect color stability of PMMA denture bases
- Alkaline peroxide tablets: generally considered safe for short-term use; long-term effects less studied in clinical settings
- Surface roughness changes: insufficient clinical data to draw firm conclusions
- Dimensional stability: minimal clinical evidence reviewed

### Candida Reduction

- Multiple included studies reported Candida albicans reduction with both chemical immersion and combined protocols
- Overnight storage in alkaline peroxide-based tablet solutions reduced biofilm mass and composition
- Relevant for management of denture stomatitis

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- No meta-analysis performed due to heterogeneity of outcome measures, assessment methods, and study designs
- Most included studies had small sample sizes
- Significant variation in chemical agent concentrations, immersion durations, and brushing protocols across studies limits direct comparison
- Lack of standardized outcome measures for both microbial reduction and material property assessment
- Very limited data on long-term effects of cleaning protocols on denture base materials
- Future needs: larger RCTs with standardized protocols; long-term clinical studies on material degradation; studies on implant-retained prostheses

## 7. Glossary

- **RDP (Removable Dental Prosthesis, 가철성 치과 보철물)**: Full or partial denture that can be removed by the patient
- **Biofilm (생물막)**: Structured community of microorganisms adhered to the prosthesis surface, including Candida and bacteria
- **Alkaline peroxide (알칼리 과산화물)**: Active ingredient in most effervescent denture cleaning tablets
- **PMMA (폴리메틸메타크릴레이트)**: Most common denture base resin material
- **In situ (구강 내)**: Studies measuring biofilm on prostheses worn in the mouth (as opposed to in vitro lab conditions)
- **PROSPERO (국제 체계적 문헌고찰 전망 등록소)**: International prospective register of systematic reviews
- **PICO**: Patient/Intervention/Comparison/Outcome — framework for systematic review question formulation
