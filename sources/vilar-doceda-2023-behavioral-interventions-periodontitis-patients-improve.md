## One-line Summary

Systematic review (no meta-analysis) of 21 studies (16 RCTs, 5 NRCTs; n 20–297) found social-cognitive-model interventions combining goal-setting, planning, self-monitoring and feedback may improve oral hygiene in periodontitis patients, and CBT combined with motivational interviewing may reduce plaque and bleeding scores.

## 한줄요약

21개 연구(RCT 16편, 비무작위 5편; n 20–297)를 종합한 체계적 문헌고찰(메타분석 없음)로, 목표설정·계획·자기점검·피드백을 결합한 사회인지모델 기반 중재가 치주염 환자의 구강위생을 개선할 수 있으며, 인지행동치료(CBT)와 동기면담(MI) 병용이 치면세균막·출혈 점수를 낮출 수 있음을 시사했다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Behavioral Interventions on Periodontitis Patients to Improve Oral Hygiene: A Systematic Review
- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Medicine (MDPI) 2023, 12, 2276
- **Authors**: Vilar Doceda M, Petit C, Huck O (University of Strasbourg)
- **DOI**: 10.3390/jcm12062276; published 15 March 2023
- **Type**: Systematic review (narrative synthesis; meta-analysis not performed due to heterogeneity)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Maps SIX psychological models used in periodontitis behavior change: Social Cognitive Theory, Theory of Planned Behavior, Health Action Process Approach (HAPA), Leventhal's self-regulatory theory, Motivational Interviewing, and Cognitive Behavioral Therapy.
- Identifies that social-cognitive models combining goal-setting + planning + self-monitoring + feedback are the most promising for oral-hygiene improvement.
- Suggests CBT + MI combination yields plaque/bleeding reductions.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Databases: PubMed/MEDLINE, Cochrane Library, Embase; searched by 2 blinded reviewers to October 2022.
- PICOS: adults >18 y with periodontitis (gingivitis-only and comorbidity/orthodontic patients excluded); behavioral/educational OH interventions vs no/regular OHI; plaque & bleeding indices; RCTs, NRCTs, cohort, case-control with ≥1-month follow-up.
- Quality: Cochrane RoB 2 for RCTs, Newcastle-Ottawa Scale for NRCTs.
- No meta-analysis due to high heterogeneity in protocols, outcomes, and follow-up times — narrative overview only.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- 5460 records → 55 full-text → 21 included (16 RCTs, 5 NRCTs).
- Sample sizes 20–297; follow-up 1 month–3 years; ages 18–80.
- 2 studies tested audio-visual modalities; 19 used six psychological models; 1 tested SMS triggers.
- Example trial (Williams 2018, computer-teaching vs self-care instructor, 4 weeks): plaque scores improved in both arms (test 68→79.8; control 65.8→76.5).
- Bottom line: social-cognitive interventions with goal-setting/planning/self-monitoring/feedback may improve OH and periodontal outcomes; CBT+MI may decrease plaque and bleeding.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- High heterogeneity prevented pooling — no quantitative effect estimate.
- Variable study designs and risk of bias; many short follow-ups.
- Notes Carra et al. SR+MA found cognitive-construct + MI interventions may reinforce OH but quantitative analysis showed no significant between-group difference.

## 6. Related Work

- Extends the MI focus of [[wiki/behavioral-dentistry/motivational-interviewing/kay-2016-motivational-interviewing-general-dental-practice]] to the full landscape of behavior-change models.
- Consistent with Zhan 2024's caution: MI alone shows weak/uncertain pooled effect, but combined cognitive approaches look more promising.

## 7. Glossary

- **SCM (Social Cognition Model)**: theories explaining health behavior via perception/self-regulation (e.g., Social Cognitive Theory, TPB, HAPA).
- **HAPA**: Health Action Process Approach.
- **CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy)**: model assuming emotions/behaviors are shaped by perception of events.
- **MI (Motivational Interviewing)**: counselling to increase intrinsic motivation by exploring the patient's own reasons for change.
- **NRCT**: non-randomized controlled trial.
- **BoP**: bleeding on probing.
