## Why Ingested

비외과적 치주치료 (Non-Surgical Periodontal Therapy, NSPT)가 치은연하 미생물군에 미치는 영향을 최초로 4가지 탐지 기술을 통합해 메타분석한 SR+MA로, 기존 [[oral-microbiology/socransky-1998-microbial-complexes-subgingival-plaque]]의 red/orange complex bacteria 개념이 치료 맥락에서 어떻게 변화하는지 정량적으로 확인하는 근거로 활용.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 115 studies (n=196 eligible) across four microbiological technologies confirms that NSPT significantly reduces mean counts of disease-associated subgingival species (A. actinomycetemcomitans, T. denticola, P. gingivalis, T. forsythia, E. nodatum, P. intermedia, C. rectus), with supportive periodontal care providing further reductions at 6 months.

## 한줄요약

115편(4가지 미생물 탐지기술)의 SR+MA로 NSPT가 치은연하 병원균(Pg, Td, Tf, Aa 등) 평균 개수를 유의미하게 감소시킴을 확인; 지지 치주치료(SPC) 추가 시 6개월 시점에 추가 감소 효과 입증.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of Periodontal Research, Vol. 60, Issue 10, pp. 963–993
- **Study design**: Systematic review and meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD42020167170)
- **Registration**: PROSPERO; PRISMA guidelines followed
- **Search databases**: Medline (via OVID), EMBASE, LILACS, Cochrane Library; search updated June 1, 2024
- **Included studies**: 115 (from 196 meeting full inclusion criteria after 3-stage screening of 3,067 retrieved)
- **Study span**: 1983–2024; 31 countries

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First comprehensive SR+MA** synthesizing NSPT microbiological data across all four dominant detection technologies (checkerboard DNA–DNA hybridisation, qPCR, bacterial culture, 16S rRNA gene sequencing).
2. **Meta-analysis confirms** significant post-NSPT reduction in mean counts of *A. actinomycetemcomitans* (Aa), *T. denticola* (Td), *P. gingivalis* (Pg), *T. forsythia* (Tf), *E. nodatum*, *P. intermedia*, and *C. rectus*.
3. **Supportive periodontal care (SPC)** provides further significant reduction in mean counts of Pg and Td at 6 months.
4. **16S rRNA studies heterogeneous**: consistent decrease of *P. gingivalis* at species level; genus-level increase in *Streptococcus* and *Rothia* (health-associated), decrease in *Treponema* (disease-associated).
5. **Methodological critique**: only ~5% of included studies used microbiological outcomes for sample size calculation; most used clinical outcomes or gave no information — likely underpowering microbiological analyses.
6. **Socransky complex shift**: qualitative analysis of 12 checkerboard studies showed decreased red and orange complex proportions with increase in blue (*Actinomyces*) and white complex species, maintained to 12 months.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Inclusion criteria (PICOS)**:
- Population: systemically healthy adults ≥18 years with periodontitis
- Intervention: NSPT (with or without adjunctive therapy); minimum 6 weeks follow-up
- Comparison: mean counts/relative abundance of subgingival microbiota before and after NSPT
- Studies: RCTs, CTs, prospective single-arm, longitudinal, case-control, split-mouth

**Exclusion criteria**: systemic disease, pregnancy, prior NSPT, antibiotic use within 3 months of or during study (except where antibiotic-free control arms were extractable).

**Microbiological detection technologies included**:
- Checkerboard DNA–DNA hybridisation: n=41 studies
- Real-time quantitative PCR (qPCR): n=30 studies
- Bacterial culture: n=36 studies
- 16S rRNA gene sequencing: n=8 studies

**Meta-analysis**: Longitudinal random-effects model (DerSimonian-Laird); effect size = Hedges' g; minimum 5 studies per species/technology/time-point for pooling. 16S studies were too few for meta-analysis — qualitative analysis only.

**Risk of bias**: RCTs assessed with Cochrane RoB2; non-randomised studies with ROBINS-I. Most RCTs had "some concerns" in randomisation or outcome measurement domains.

**Study design**: 80% RCTs; 70% whole-mouth design; 30% split-mouth. 80% RCTs in checkerboard cohort.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Meta-analysis findings (significant reductions after NSPT)**:
- *P. gingivalis* (Pg): significant decrease at 3 and 6 months (checkerboard and qPCR; also bacterial culture)
- *T. denticola* (Td): significant decrease at 1.5, 3, and 6 months (qPCR)
- *T. forsythia* (Tf): significant decrease at 1.5, 3, and 6 months (qPCR)
- *A. actinomycetemcomitans* (Aa): significant decrease at 3 and 6 months (qPCR); at 3 months (bacterial culture)
- *E. nodatum*: significant decrease (checkerboard)
- *P. intermedia*: significant decrease (checkerboard and qPCR)
- *C. rectus*: significant decrease (checkerboard)

**SPC subgroup analysis** (checkerboard studies at 6 months):
- Studies with SPC provision showed significantly higher effect sizes for Pg and Td; effect not significant for Tf in this subgroup

**CHX adjunct**: no significant additional effect on Pg mean counts vs. NSPT alone

**Adjunctive therapies overall**: similar microbiological profile of changes with or without adjuncts at 3 months (no significant subgroup differences)

**Smoking**: studies with smokers only showed no statistically significant changes in mean counts at 3 months; smoker subgroup based on few studies

**Split-mouth vs. whole-mouth**: smaller effect sizes in split-mouth designs for Pg and Tf in qPCR cohort (carry-across effect suspected; significant difference for Aa, p=0.03)

**16S rRNA findings (qualitative)**:
- Species: consistent significant decrease of *P. gingivalis* across studies; other species findings heterogeneous
- Genera: significant decrease of *Treponema* (4/7 studies); significant increase of *Streptococcus* (4/7) and *Rothia* (3/7)
- Alpha diversity (Shannon index): mostly no significant changes; two studies showed transient decreases (6 weeks and 3–6 months)
- Beta diversity: heterogeneous findings across Bray-Curtis, PCoA, PCA analyses

**Socransky complex shift** (qualitative; 12 checkerboard studies): decrease of red and orange complex bacteria; increase of blue (*Actinomyces*) and white complex species sustained to 12 months

**Clinical secondary outcomes** (across all technologies): reduction in FM-PPD, FM-CAL, FMBS, and FMPS at 3 months; heterogeneous FMPS (range 10%–64% at 3 months)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Antibiotic adjuncts (systemic/local) excluded from analysis — their specific microbiological effect vs. NSPT alone not evaluated
- Only 5% of included studies used microbiological outcomes for sample size calculation — most studies likely underpowered for microbiological endpoints
- Pooled sampling in majority of culture (60%) and 16S (67%) studies may mask site-specific changes
- Choice of 16S variable region (V3–V4 most common but less able to distinguish species like *Tannerella*, *Prevotella*, *Fusobacterium*) limits species-level resolution
- Qualitative analysis of 16S data only (insufficient studies for meta-analysis)
- Smoking not excluded but smoking-specific analysis limited by few studies

**Research recommendations**:
- Future microbiome studies: sample size calculation based on microbiological outcomes (e.g., Shannon index); site-specific sampling; clustered analysis by PPD at sampling sites post-treatment; parallel-arm (not split-mouth) design; standardise 16S variable region (V1–V2 recommended for oral samples)

## 6. Related Work

- Socransky et al. 1998: defined microbial complexes (red, orange, green, blue, yellow, white) referenced repeatedly for contextualizing post-NSPT complex shifts
- Hajishengallis et al. 2012/2014: Polymicrobial Synergy and Dysbiosis (PSD) model; *P. gingivalis* as keystone pathogen — theoretical framework for interpreting NSPT microbiological outcomes
- EFP S3 clinical guideline: recommends stepwise periodontal therapy; NSPT = Steps 1 and 2

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| NSPT | Non-Surgical Periodontal Therapy — subgingival debridement plus oral hygiene instruction and risk factor control (EFP S3 Steps 1–2) |
| SPC | Supportive Periodontal Care — maintenance phase after active therapy |
| Checkerboard DNA–DNA hybridisation | Multi-species DNA detection technology; simultaneously probes 40 species from subgingival plaque |
| qPCR | Real-time quantitative PCR — quantifies specific bacterial DNA copies |
| 16S rRNA gene sequencing | Next-generation sequencing of the 16S ribosomal RNA gene; profiles entire microbial community without pre-selection of species |
| OTU | Operational Taxonomic Unit — sequence cluster used as a proxy species in 16S analyses |
| Alpha diversity | Within-sample species richness/evenness (e.g., Shannon index, Chao 1, Simpson) |
| Beta diversity | Between-sample community dissimilarity (e.g., Bray-Curtis, UniFrac, PCoA) |
| Hedges' g | Standardised effect size measure, adjusted for small sample bias |
| PSD model | Polymicrobial Synergy and Dysbiosis model — community-level dysbiosis model replacing single-pathogen periodontal aetiology |
| Red complex | *P. gingivalis*, *T. denticola*, *T. forsythia* — strongly periodontitis-associated species per Socransky 1998 |
| Keystone pathogen | *P. gingivalis* — low-abundance organism that disrupts host innate immunity to enable dysbiotic community expansion |
