## Why Ingested

Part of the osseodensification (OD) primary-stability evidence base — the animal-model SR+MA arm showing OD raises insertion torque (WMD 2.27) but does not significantly improve BIC/BAFO at 3–6 weeks, a key nuance for the [[overviews/osseodensification-clinical-applications]] synthesis. Pairs with the human-data SR+MA [[implants/gaspar-2022-implant-stability-osseodensification-conventional-sr-ma]] to separate biomechanical torque gains from true histologic osseointegration benefit.

## One-line Summary
SR+MA of 9 animal studies (J Prosthet Dent 2022;127:61–70) — osseodensification significantly raised insertion torque (WMD 2.27, 95% CI 1.15–3.39, P < .001) but BIC and BAFO differences at 3 and 6 weeks were not statistically significant (mixed P values; small WMDs with wide CIs).

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (animal studies only)
- **Journal**: Journal of Prosthetic Dentistry 2022;127(1):61–70
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.prosdent.2020.07.004
- **Authors**: Amit M. Gaikwad, Amruta A. Joshi, Jyoti B. Nadgere
- **Search**: Medline/PubMed, Lilacs, Science Direct + manual search of reference lists, up to April 31, 2020
- **Sample**: 9 animal studies meeting inclusion criteria
- **Quality tools**: SYRCLE for risk of bias; ARRIVE for reporting quality
- **Primary Question**: In animal models, does osseodensification (counterclockwise compaction drilling) improve primary stability (insertion torque), bone-to-implant contact (BIC), and bone area frequency occupancy (BAFO) compared with conventional drilling?

## 2. Key Contributions
- First animal-focused SR+MA dedicated to OD outcomes — complementary to the human-focused Gaspar 2022.
- **Insertion torque**: pooled weighted mean difference (WMD) = 2.270 (95% CI 1.147 to 3.393; P < .001) favouring OD — the most robust effect in the meta-analysis.
- **BIC at 3 weeks**: WMD = 0.487 (95% CI 0.220 to 0.754; P = .114) — wide CI, P-value reported but interpretation ambiguous (the authors frame this as a positive trend; the P > .05 reading is more conservative).
- **BIC at 6 weeks**: WMD = 0.565 (95% CI 0.219 to 0.911; P = .448).
- **BAFO at 3 weeks**: WMD = 0.679 (95% CI 0.265 to 1.093; P = .073).
- **BAFO at 6 weeks**: WMD = 0.391 (95% CI -0.204 to 0.986; P = .027).
- Authors conclude that primary stability, BIC, and BAFO all *significantly improved* with OD, but reviewer reading of the P values shows only IT meets the conventional P < .05 threshold cleanly; the BIC/BAFO P values are mixed and CIs cross or approach zero.
- Quality appraisal via SYRCLE (animal risk of bias) and ARRIVE (animal reporting) — methodologically rigorous for an animal SR.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
A PRISMA-style systematic review limited to animal studies comparing OD with conventional drilling for endosseous implant placement. Three databases (Medline/PubMed, Lilacs, Science Direct) plus manual reference-list searching, with English-language restriction, up to 31 April 2020. The SYRCLE (SYstematic Review Center for Laboratory animal Experimentation) tool was used for risk of bias; ARRIVE guidelines were used for reporting quality.

Three primary outcomes were meta-analysed:
1. Insertion torque (proxy for primary stability)
2. Bone-to-implant contact (BIC) at 3 and 6 weeks
3. Bone area frequency occupancy (BAFO) at 3 and 6 weeks — proportion of trabecular bone within a defined region of interest around the implant

Weighted mean differences (WMD) with 95% confidence intervals were calculated via random-effects meta-analysis. Heterogeneity was assessed but specific I² values are not in the extracted abstract.

Nine studies were included after screening — most are in-vivo dog, sheep, or rabbit models. Implant healing periods of 3 and 6 weeks were chosen because they bracket early healing (peri-implant bone remodelling phase).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **n studies included**: 9 animal studies
- **Insertion torque (primary stability)**: WMD = 2.270 (95% CI 1.147 to 3.393; P < .001) — favours OD; CI excludes zero.
- **BIC, 3 weeks**: WMD = 0.487 (95% CI 0.220 to 0.754; P = .114) — CI excludes zero but P > .05.
- **BIC, 6 weeks**: WMD = 0.565 (95% CI 0.219 to 0.911; P = .448) — CI excludes zero but P > .05.
- **BAFO, 3 weeks**: WMD = 0.679 (95% CI 0.265 to 1.093; P = .073) — borderline.
- **BAFO, 6 weeks**: WMD = 0.391 (95% CI −0.204 to 0.986; P = .027) — CI crosses zero but P < .05.
- Quality appraisal: SYRCLE + ARRIVE; specific scores per study not in extracted text.
- Note: the P-value / CI mismatches in this paper deserve scrutiny; the abstract's conclusion ("primary implant stability, BIC, and BAFO significantly improved") is stronger than the raw P values support for BIC/BAFO.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Animal heterogeneity (dog, sheep, rabbit; jaw vs long-bone sites) limits clinical translation.
- Only 9 studies — small evidence base for stratified analysis.
- Acknowledged P-value / CI interpretive inconsistency: reading the data conservatively, only insertion torque reaches conventional significance.
- Heterogeneity (I²) and publication bias funnel-plot assessment not in extracted abstract.
- 3- and 6-week healing windows do not cover full osseointegration (8–12 weeks in most models).
- Authors call for additional laboratory and clinical studies for stronger evidence.

## 7. Glossary
- **SR+MA**: Systematic review with meta-analysis.
- **WMD (Weighted Mean Difference)**: Effect-size metric used when outcomes share the same scale across studies; weighted by study precision.
- **BIC (Bone-to-Implant Contact)**: Histomorphometric % of implant surface in direct bone contact.
- **BAFO (Bone Area Frequency Occupancy / Bone Area Fraction Occupancy)**: Histomorphometric proportion of trabecular bone within a defined region of interest around the implant.
- **SYRCLE**: SYstematic Review Center for Laboratory animal Experimentation — risk-of-bias tool for animal studies.
- **ARRIVE**: Animal Research: Reporting In Vivo Experiments — reporting guideline.
- **OD (Osseodensification)**: Counterclockwise compaction drilling with Densah burs.
