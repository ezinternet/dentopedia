## Why Ingested

SR specifically on PRF+xenograft (DBBM/BioOss) combination for sinus and ridge augmentation — integrates PRF adjunct evidence across two GBR contexts (sinus lift and mandibular horizontal/vertical GBR). Provides the systematic review-level synthesis needed to contextualize individual RCTs. [[bone-regeneration/wessing-2018-gbr-collagen-membranes-particulate-graft-sr-ma]]

## One-line Summary

SR of 7 RCTs (PROSPERO CRD42023411160; PRISMA 2020): PRF+DBBM shows no statistically significant benefit over DBBM alone in sinus lift histomorphometry, but may reduce graft resorption in mandibular GBR.

## 한줄요약

7편 RCT 대상 SR: PRF+DBBM은 상악동거상술 조직형태계측 결과에서 DBBM 단독 대비 유의한 이점 없음; 하악 수평 GBR에서는 골이식재 흡수 감소 가능성.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of Functional Biomaterials
- **Year**: 2023
- **DOI**: 10.3390/jfb14070389
- **Study design**: Systematic review (PROSPERO CRD42023411160; PRISMA 2020)
- **PICOS**:
  - P: Adult patients with atrophic posterior maxilla or posterior mandibular bone deficiency, candidates for implant therapy
  - I: Xenograft (DBBM/BioOss) + PRF or L-PRF
  - C: Xenograft alone
  - O: New bone formation (primary); residual graft, soft tissue %, bone maturation, radiological outcomes (secondary)
  - S: RCTs only
- **Databases**: PubMed/Medline, Cochrane Library, Web of Science (search through March 2023)
- **Included studies**: 7 RCTs

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR focused specifically on the PRF+DBBM/BioOss combination across both sinus lift and horizontal/vertical ridge augmentation contexts
- Demonstrates that PRF addition to DBBM does not yield statistically significant histomorphometric benefit in maxillary sinus lift
- Identifies a possible benefit in mandibular GBR: one study showed reduced bone graft resorption with PRF+DBBM
- Risk of bias assessed using Cochrane Collaboration tool; heterogeneity among studies limited meta-analysis

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Systematic review; no meta-analysis performed due to clinical and methodological heterogeneity
- PICOS-structured inclusion criteria; RCTs only (excluded case reports, in vitro/in situ, SR/MA)
- Three-database search without language or date restrictions until March 2023
- Manual reference search of eligible articles and specialist journals
- Duplicate removal via EndNote
- Risk of bias: Cochrane Collaboration tool

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Sinus lift context**: No statistically significant difference in new bone formation (%) between PRF+DBBM and DBBM alone across included sinus lift RCTs
- **Mandibular GBR context**: One study demonstrated significant reduction in bone graft resorption in the PRF+DBBM group
- **Soft tissue/connective tissue**: Results inconsistent across studies
- **Radiological outcomes**: No consistent advantage for PRF+DBBM
- **Implant survival**: Not systematically pooled; individual studies reported high survival rates in both groups
- Cochrane risk of bias: variable across studies; several with "some concerns" domains

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Heterogeneity in PRF preparation protocols (solid PRF vs L-PRF, centrifugation speeds/times) precludes meta-analysis
- Small sample sizes in individual RCTs
- Variable follow-up times and biopsy timing
- Meta-analysis not performed — qualitative synthesis only
- Authors call for standardized PRF preparation and reporting in future RCTs

## 6. Related Work

- PRF shown to benefit periodontal intrabony defect treatment with BioOss but evidence in pre-implantation bone grafting remains unclear
- BioOss established efficacy: sinus floor augmentation, socket preservation, horizontal/vertical augmentation, peri-implant defects
- PRF cytokine profile: TGF-β1, PDGF, VEGF, IL-1β, IL-4, IL-6

## 7. Glossary

- **DBBM**: Deproteinized bovine bone mineral (commercially: BioOss); particle size 0.25–2 mm
- **PRF**: Platelet-rich fibrin — autologous scaffold from centrifuged peripheral blood containing platelets, leukocytes, growth factors
- **L-PRF**: Liquid PRF — produced at lower centrifugation speed/shorter duration; easier mixing with particulate graft
- **GBR**: Guided bone regeneration
- **PROSPERO**: International prospective register of systematic reviews
