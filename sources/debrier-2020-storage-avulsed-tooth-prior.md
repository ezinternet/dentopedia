## Why Ingested

Seeds the new `dental-trauma` category (previously a gap) with the single highest-yield emergency decision in avulsion management: which storage medium a layperson should use before replantation. Directly supplies the periodontal-ligament-survival rationale that underlies IADT first-aid advice and pairs with the downstream splinting/replantation evidence in [[dental-trauma/kumari-2025-effect-splinting-outcomes-replantation-avulsed]].

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (33 studies, 10 in MA; GRADE low–very low): for storing an avulsed tooth before replantation, HBSS, propolis, oral rehydration salts, rice water, and cling film preserve PDL-cell viability at least as well as (or better than) milk; saline and tap water are worse than milk.

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (33편, MA 10편; GRADE 낮음–매우 낮음): 탈구치 재식 전 보관 매체로 HBSS·프로폴리스·경구수액염(ORS)·쌀뜨물·랩(cling film)이 우유 이상으로 치주인대(PDL) 세포 생존율을 보존하며, 식염수·수돗물은 우유보다 나쁘다.

## 1. Document Information

- Type: Systematic review + meta-analysis (Cochrane First Aid / Belgian Red Cross CEBaP).
- Journal: Dental Traumatology 2020;36(5):453-476.
- Outcome metric: in-vitro / ex-vivo PDL-cell viability (surrogate), not clinical replantation healing.
- Abstract-only — full text not retrieved (PMC unavailable).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Ranks layperson-accessible storage media against the two field standards (milk, saliva) on a single comparable surrogate (PDL-cell viability).
- Identifies practical, accessible alternatives (oral rehydration salts, rice water, cling film) beyond the classic HBSS/propolis.
- Confirms saline and tap water as inferior to milk — the most actionable "do not use" message.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Databases (Sept 2019): Cochrane Library, MEDLINE, Embase. 4118 references screened → 33 included, 23 comparisons, 10 pooled.
- Dual independent screening, extraction, risk-of-bias; GRADE certainty.
- Effect measure: standardized mean difference (SMD) in PDL-cell viability vs comparator (milk).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Better than milk (higher PDL viability):
- HBSS: SMD 2.47 (95% CI 1.59–3.34), P<.00001
- Propolis: SMD 1.73 (95% CI 1.12–2.33), P<.00001
- Oral rehydration salts: SMD 4.16 (95% CI 2.10–6.23), P<.0001
- Rice water, cling film

Worse than milk (lower PDL viability):
- Saline: SMD −4.35 (95% CI −7.55 to −1.14), P=.008
- Tap water, buttermilk, castor oil, GC Tooth Mousse, turmeric extract

Saliva: insufficient evidence to recommend for or against vs alternatives.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Certainty low to very low: indirect populations/outcomes (surrogate cell viability, not in-vivo healing), imprecision, design limitations.
- No clinical replantation-survival endpoints synthesized.
- Heterogeneous protocols (storage time, temperature) across primary studies.

## 6. Related Work

- IADT avulsion guidelines (milk / HBSS / saliva as recommended field media).
- [[dental-trauma/kumari-2025-effect-splinting-outcomes-replantation-avulsed]] — the post-replantation stabilization step.

## 7. Glossary

- PDL: periodontal ligament — the cells whose survival determines replantation prognosis (ankylosis/resorption risk).
- HBSS: Hank's balanced salt solution — pH/osmolality-balanced storage medium.
- ORS: oral rehydration salts — WHO-type electrolyte solution.
- SMD: standardized mean difference — pooled effect size for continuous outcomes.
