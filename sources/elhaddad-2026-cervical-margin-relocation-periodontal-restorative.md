## Why Ingested

First SR+MA pooling periodontal (PD, BOP) and restorative (marginal adaptation) outcomes for cervical margin relocation (CMR) / deep margin elevation (DME) — the standard answer to subgingival proximal margins of indirect adhesive restorations. It extends our immediate-dentin-sealing evidence base ([[resin-bonding/hardan-2022-immediate-dentin-sealing-sr-ma]]) into the adjacent "what to do when the box is subgingival" decision, and supports the deep-margin scenarios in partial-coverage literature ([[inlay/prott-2025-partial-coverage-restorations-posterior-scoping]]).

## One-line Summary

PRISMA SR+MA (abstract-only) finds cervical margin relocation does not harm periodontal health in healthy/stabilized patients — pooled PD reduction MD -0.96 mm (I2=0%) and BOP MD -0.52 (I2=27%→0% after one outlier removed); marginal adaptation depends on bonding strategy and material, with adhesively bonded glass-ceramics best.

## 한줄요약

PRISMA 체계적 문헌고찰+메타분석(초록만)에서 치경부 변연 재위치(Cervical Margin Relocation, CMR)는 치주 건강을 해치지 않으며(통합 탐침깊이 -0.96 mm, 탐침 시 출혈 -0.52 감소), 변연 적합도는 접착 전략과 수복 재료에 좌우되어 접착성 글라스세라믹이 가장 우수했다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA to quantitatively pool **periodontal outcomes** (probing depth and bleeding on probing) for CMR/DME, the technique addressing subgingival proximal margins.
- Establishes that **well-executed CMR is periodontally compatible** in periodontally healthy or stabilized patients — counters the long-standing concern that a subgingival composite base provokes inflammation.
- Synthesizes **restorative (marginal adaptation)** evidence, identifying bonding strategy and restorative material as the dominant determinants, with adhesively bonded glass-ceramic restorations performing best.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PRISMA-compliant systematic review across four databases.
- Meta-analyses computed for two periodontal endpoints: probing depth (PD) and bleeding on probing (BOP).
- Heterogeneity assessed with the I2 statistic; sensitivity analysis performed (exclusion of one clinically divergent study).
- Restorative outcome (marginal adaptation) summarized narratively across bonding strategies and materials.
- (Abstract-only: exact study count, designs, total sample, and risk-of-bias tool are not recoverable without full text.)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Probing depth (PD)**: sensitivity analysis MD **-0.96 mm** (95% CI -1.50 to -0.42; I2 = 0%) — significant reduction.
- **Bleeding on probing (BOP)**: pooled MD **-0.52** (95% CI -0.77 to -0.27; I2 = 27% moderate); after excluding one clinically divergent study, effect more pronounced and I2 = 0%.
- **Marginal adaptation**: influenced by bonding strategy and restorative material; adhesively bonded restorations — particularly glass-ceramics — showed superior marginal integrity under controlled conditions.
- Composite margin elevation effectively managed deep proximal cavities restored with ceramic onlays, with favorable periodontal outcomes.
- **Bottom line**: CMR is effective for subgingival margins and compatible with periodontal health when appropriate materials/techniques are used.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only ingest: no access to full inclusion list, GRADE/risk-of-bias, subgroup detail.
- Findings restricted to periodontally healthy or stabilized patients — active periodontitis not addressed.
- Authors stress that **long-term follow-up** is essential to judge restoration durability; most CMR evidence is short/medium term.
- Marginal-adaptation conclusions drawn "under controlled conditions" (largely in-vitro), limiting clinical generalizability.

## 7. Glossary

- **CMR (Cervical Margin Relocation)** / **DME (Deep Margin Elevation)** / **PBE (Proximal Box Elevation)**: placing a resin-composite base to raise a deep subgingival/sub-marginal proximal margin to a supragingival, more accessible level before a direct or indirect restoration.
- **PD (Probing Depth)**: distance from gingival margin to base of sulcus/pocket (mm).
- **BOP (Bleeding on Probing)**: bleeding elicited by gentle probing; inflammation marker.
- **MD (Mean Difference)**: pooled effect estimate for continuous outcomes.
- **I2**: statistic quantifying between-study heterogeneity (0% = none).
