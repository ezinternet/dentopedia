## 2. Key Contributions
- 20 qualitative / 17 quantitative studies included
- 1161 patients, 2933 implants (1427 TL vs 1506 BL)
- Survival rate >90% and success rate >90% for both implant types
- No statistically significant differences in radiological MBL between BL and TL
- Comprehensive search to September 2019

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Systematic review. MEDLINE, Embase, additional databases searched through September 2019. Two independent authors. English articles only. Inclusion: clinical studies comparing BL vs TL dental implants with radiographic outcome data. Qualitative and quantitative analyses performed.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- 1028 records → 20 qualitative, 17 quantitative studies
- 1161 patients, 2933 implants (1427 TL, 1506 BL)
- Survival rate: >90% for both BL and TL
- Success rate: >90% (2 studies: 88% and 86.2%)
- No significant group differences in radiological MBL
- No between-group survival differences reported

## 7. Glossary
- **PS**: Platform switching — use of smaller-diameter abutment on larger implant collar
- **PM**: Platform matching — abutment matches implant platform diameter
- **MBL**: Marginal bone loss — radiographic bone level change around implant
- **TL**: Tissue-level implant — transmucosal component integral to implant
- **BL**: Bone-level implant — implant platform at crestal bone level
