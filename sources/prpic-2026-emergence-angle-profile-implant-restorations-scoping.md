## Why Ingested

기존 [[implants/soft-tissue/raghoebar-2021-profilometric-mucosa-soft-tissue-augmentation-esthetic-sr-ma]]는 연조직 *증대*로 buccal contour를 다루지만, 보철 emergence angle/profile 자체가 변연골 흡수·중안면 퇴축에 미치는 영향은 보강이 필요했다. 본 scoping review (Prpic 2026)는 17편을 종합해 **emergence angle < 30° + concave profile**이 peri-implant 안정에 유리하다는 신호를 정리하며, `wiki/overviews/abutment-emergence-profile-peri-implant-tissue-overview` 종합 페이지의 일차 근거를 확장한다.

## One-line Summary

PRISMA-ScR scoping review (17 studies, incl. 2 RCT, 1 SR+MA, cross-sectional/retrospective) of implant-restoration emergence angle/profile: an emergence angle < 30° may reduce marginal bone loss (evidence inconclusive), and a concave emergence profile is consistently associated with greater peri-implant soft tissue margin stability and reduced recession.

## 한줄요약

임플란트 보철 emergence angle/profile에 관한 17편 scoping review — emergence angle < 30°가 변연골 흡수를 줄일 수 있으나(근거 불확실), concave(오목) emergence profile은 일관되게 peri-implant 연조직 안정·퇴축 감소와 연관된다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Synthesizes 17 studies on the prosthodontic **emergence angle** and **emergence profile** of implant-supported restorations and their effect on peri-implant hard/soft tissue.
- Provides the parametric design inputs (angle threshold, profile contour) intended to seed **generative design** of hybrid (zirconia + titanium) implant abutments.
- Frames the subgingival contour with the **EBC zone scheme** (E = esthetic, 1 mm below free gingival margin; B = bounded, 1–2 mm apical; C = crestal, 1–1.5 mm at platform).
- Explicitly scopes OUT platform-switching, connection type (internal/external), and esthetic-vs-posterior site (already covered elsewhere).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PRISMA-ScR-reported scoping review, OSF-registered. Two primary reviewers (V.P., S.G.) screened titles → abstracts → full text; disagreements adjudicated by two supplementary reviewers (S.S., A.C.).
- Inclusion: full-text, English, human-subject studies on emergence angle/profile, past 5 years. Exclusion: case series, editorials, interviews.
- Keywords: "emergence angle of implant restoration", "emergence profile of implant restoration".
- Flow: 791 retrieved → 650 removed pre-screen (640 automation, 10 duplicates) → 141 screened → 124 excluded → 17 included.
- No risk-of-bias / no meta-analysis performed (scoping design).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Emergence angle (10 studies):**
- 5 studies support **< 30°** as protective against marginal bone loss / peri-implant inflammation:
  - Neckel (cross-sectional, n=462): angle < 30° **+ concave profile** → significant reduction in annual peri-implant bone loss.
  - Lin (SR+MA): < 30° + concave/straight profile → reduced peri-implantitis risk.
  - Misch (retrospective, n=192): the >=30° angle risk becomes clinically significant **only when transmucosal abutment height < 2 mm**; abutment height > 2 mm mitigates it (bone-level implants).
  - Majzoub (retrospective, n=83): angle > 30° significantly associated with early (≤2 y) marginal bone loss in peri-implantitis-affected implants.
  - Yu (digital comparative, n=100): cantilever first-molar restorations have smaller angles, fewer exceeding 30°.
- 4 studies found **no clear correlation** + 1 review (Izzetti) found no consensus:
  - Tang (n=57, angle 36.7 ± 13.7°): no meaningful link to buccal vertical bone loss.
  - Maghsoudi: no difference between < 30° and >= 30° in probing depth (well-maintained patients).
  - Lops (×2): soft tissue/marginal bone stability unaffected by angle if a **concave profile** and a **mechanically stable abutment interface** are present, even for angles 30–50° (or >30°).

**Emergence profile (7 studies):**
- 4 studies favor a **concave** profile (reduced bone loss + soft tissue margin stability):
  - Neckel: profile shape matters most when angle > 30°; negligible effect when angle < 30°.
  - Lin (SR+MA): concave/straight → lower peri-implantitis risk.
  - Siegenthaler (RCT, n=47, anterior maxilla): concave provisional → greater mucosal margin stability vs convex at 1 y.
  - Endres (RCT, n=47, esthetic zone, 3-y): concave → superior soft tissue stability, fewer mid-facial recessions; encourages coronal soft-tissue creeping; recommended in esthetic zone.
- 3 reviews (Gervyte, Hamilton, Izzetti): emphasize careful profile management, esp. thin soft-tissue phenotype and ideal implant positioning.
- General observation: convex profiles tend to pair with **larger** angles; straight/concave with **smaller** angles.

**Bottom line**: angle **< 30°** = inconclusive but plausibly beneficial (needs RCTs); **concave profile** = consistent soft tissue stability benefit.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Heterogeneous study designs/methods/outcomes → limited generalizability.
- English-only, two-database search → possible missing data.
- No formal risk-of-bias appraisal (acknowledged; not required for scoping review).
- Future: personalized/generative-design abutment workflows; standardized protocols still undeveloped.

## 7. Glossary

- **Emergence angle**: angle between the average tangent of the transitional restoration contour and the implant long axis; > 30° linked to early marginal bone loss in several studies.
- **Emergence profile**: subgingival contour from alveolar bone to free gingival margin; classified convex / concave / straight.
- **EBC zones**: E (esthetic, 1 mm below margin), B (bounded, 1–2 mm), C (crestal, 1–1.5 mm at platform).
- **Hybrid abutment**: zirconia mesostructure on a titanium base — combines esthetics/biocompatibility with mechanical strength.
- **Generative design**: algorithm-driven parametric optimization of abutment geometry.
- **PRISMA-ScR**: PRISMA extension for Scoping Reviews.
