## Why Ingested

기존 [[endodontics/shaping/pui-yii-2021-glide-path-apical-bacteria-extrusion]]는 회전 vs 왕복(rotary vs reciprocating) kinematics가 근단 세균·debris 압출(apical extrusion)에 미치는 영향을 in-vitro로 본 반면, 그 압출이 실제 환자 술후통(postoperative pain)으로 이어지는지는 미확인이었다. 본 SR(Singh 2026)은 retreatment 한정 RCT 5편을 종합해, in-vitro 압출 차이가 임상 술후통 차이로 일관되게 번역되지 않음을 보여 그 기전 페이지의 임상적 함의를 보강·한정한다.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (PRISMA 2020; qualitative synthesis, no MA) of 5 RCTs (n=554) finding rotary and reciprocating NiTi systems produce comparable postoperative pain after nonsurgical endodontic retreatment; pain is mild, peaks at 24 h, and declines over time without consistent dependence on kinematics.

## 한줄요약

RCT 5편(n=554)을 정성종합한 체계적 문헌고찰: 비외과적 재근관치료에서 회전(rotary)·왕복(reciprocating) NiTi 시스템 간 술후통 차이는 대체로 없고, 통증은 경미하며 24시간에 최고였다가 시간이 지나며 감소 — kinematics에 일관되게 의존하지 않음.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic review to **exclusively** evaluate instrumentation kinematics (rotary vs reciprocating) on postoperative pain in **nonsurgical endodontic retreatment** (prior reviews addressed primary treatment; retreatment cannot be extrapolated because of filling-material removal and increased debris-extrusion risk).
- Synthesizes 5 RCTs (554 participants) and concludes rotary and reciprocating produce **comparable** postoperative pain.
- Establishes that postoperative pain after retreatment is multifactorial and that in-vitro apical-extrusion differences between kinematics do **not** consistently translate into clinical pain differences.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **PICO**: P = nonsurgical endodontic retreatment of permanent teeth; I = reciprocating NiTi; C = rotary NiTi; O = incidence and intensity of postoperative pain.
- **Databases**: PubMed/MEDLINE, Scopus, Embase, Web of Science (Feb 25 2016 – Feb 25 2026).
- **Eligibility**: human RCTs comparing reciprocating vs rotary NiTi during nonsurgical retreatment with clearly described pain assessment. Excluded: non-English, follow-up <48 h, no full text, lab/animal/case-report/review.
- **Selection/extraction**: two independent reviewers (P.S., A.B.), third reviewer (S.A.) verification.
- **Risk of bias**: Modified Cochrane RoB-2 (5 domains; low / some concerns / high).
- **Synthesis**: qualitative narrative only — heterogeneity in pain scales (VAS, NRS, verbal rating, 4-point verbal), timing, and reporting precluded meta-analysis. GRADE used for certainty.
- **Search yield**: 194 records → 78 duplicates removed → 116 screened → 8 full texts → 5 RCTs included.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Population**: 5 RCTs, 554 participants (1 Brazil, 4 Türkiye); single-rooted anterior, mandibular premolars, mixed maxillary/mandibular teeth; all with post-treatment endodontic disease.
- **Systems**: Rotary — Mtwo Retreatment, ProTaper Universal Retreatment, Hyflex EDM, One Shape, Revo-S, One Curve Mini. Reciprocating — Reciproc, Reciproc Blue, WaveOne Gold, WaveOne, One RECI. One trial also included hand SS files.
- **Protocols**: electronic apex locator + radiograph for working length; NaOCl irrigant (mostly 2.5%, one 5.25%); 17% EDTA final; mostly single-visit (one two-visit with Ca(OH)₂).
- **Pain over time**: highest within first 24 h, gradually declines; by 7–14 days minimal regardless of technique.
- **Rotary vs reciprocating**: most trials found **no statistically significant difference** at 24, 48, 72 h. Eyüboğlu & Özcan reported **higher early pain with reciprocating** (WaveOne) but **not significant (P>0.05) at later follow-up**. Hand-vs-rotary-vs-reciprocating RCT: **hand files significantly more painful at first 48 h (P<0.05)**; no significant difference between rotary and reciprocating (P>0.05).
- **Analgesic intake**: generally low and **comparable** across kinematics (ibuprofen/NSAID most prescribed); Comparin et al. and Çanakçi et al. found no significant difference in ibuprofen use.
- **Risk of bias**: low-to-moderate (2 low; 3 "some concerns" mainly from infeasible participant/operator blinding).
- **GRADE certainty**: low to very low.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 5 RCTs; heterogeneous pain scales (categorical vs continuous), follow-up intervals, sample characteristics, and protocols → no quantitative pooling, reduced comparability.
- Operator blinding infeasible (nature of instrumentation).
- Low-to-very-low GRADE certainty; further well-designed RCTs needed for definitive conclusions.

## 6. Related Work

- da Silveira et al. — rotary associated with lower severity of postoperative pain.

## 7. Glossary

- **Reciprocation**: alternating clockwise/counterclockwise NiTi file motion (vs continuous 360° rotation).
- **Apical extrusion**: displacement of debris/irrigant/microorganisms beyond the apical foramen during instrumentation; a proposed driver of postoperative pain/flare-ups.
- **RoB-2**: Cochrane Risk of Bias 2 tool for RCTs.
- **GRADE**: framework rating certainty of evidence (risk of bias, inconsistency, indirectness, imprecision, publication bias).
- **PAI**: Periapical Index (radiographic scoring of periapical status).
