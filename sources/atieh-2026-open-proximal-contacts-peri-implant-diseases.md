## Why Ingested

기존 food-impaction 페이지들([[food-impaction/kim-2025-factors-influencing-proximal-contact-loss]], [[food-impaction/sheba-2023-interproximal-contact-loss-systematic-meta]], [[food-impaction/fathi-2022-prevalence-proximal-contact-loss-umbrella]])은 근위접촉상실(Proximal Contact Loss, PCL/OPC)의 **유병률·위험인자**에 집중했고, "그래서 임플란트 주위조직 건강에 실제로 해로운가?"라는 임상 결과(MBL·PPD·주위염) 질문은 비어 있었다. 본 Atieh 2026 SR+MA(5편, 4882 임플란트)는 이 카테고리에서 **OPC→주위질환 결과를 직접 메타분석한 최고 근거 등급(sr+ma) pickup**으로, 개방접촉이 주위점막염(peri-implant mucositis) 위험지표(RR 1.74)이나 MBL·PPD·주위염은 비유의함을 정량화해 prevalence 중심 페이지들을 outcome 축으로 확장한다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (5 non-randomized observational studies, 4882 implants, 1050 with open proximal contact): open proximal contacts significantly raised peri-implant mucositis incidence (RR 1.74, 95% CI 1.06–2.86) but MBL change (MD 0.07), PPD change (MD 0.11), and peri-implantitis (RR 1.63) were all non-significant.

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (비무작위 관찰연구 5편·임플란트 4882개·개방접촉 1050개): 개방근위접촉은 임플란트주위점막염 발생을 유의하게 증가시켰으나(RR 1.74, 95% CI 1.06–2.86), 변연골소실·탐침깊이·주위염은 모두 비유의했다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA to specifically quantify the association between **open proximal contacts (OPC)** adjacent to implant restorations and **peri-implant disease outcomes** (most prior reviews addressed prevalence only).
- Pooled 5 observational studies (4882 implants; 1050 OPC) and stratified outcomes into MBL change, PPD change, peri-implant mucositis, and peri-implantitis.
- Identifies OPC as a **risk indicator for peri-implant mucositis** (the only statistically significant outcome), while marginal bone loss, probing depth, and peri-implantitis trended worse but were not significant.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **PICO**: adults ≥18 with implant-supported fixed prostheses; intervention = OPC between implant prosthesis and natural tooth OR two adjacent non-splinted implants; comparison = closed proximal contacts (CPC); outcomes = MBL change (primary), PPD change, peri-implant mucositis/peri-implantitis incidence (secondary); non-randomized observational designs; implants in function ≥1 year.
- Databases CENTRAL, MEDLINE, EMBASE, ClinicalTrials.gov up to 8 July 2025; PRISMA + Cochrane; duplicate screening/extraction.
- Proximal contact assessed with **dental floss** (closed/"tight" = resistance felt; open = no resistance).
- Risk of bias: Cochrane **ROBINS-I V2** (7 domains). Certainty: **GRADE**. Statistical unit = implant. RevMan 5.3, random-effects; MD for continuous, RR for dichotomous; leave-one-out sensitivity. Publication bias not formally assessed (<10 studies).
- 276 retrieved → 5 included: Alhakeem (Iran, retrospective cohort), Byun (Korea, cross-sectional), French (Canada, cross-sectional, n=5054 implants — largest), Ko (Taiwan, case-control), Latimer (USA, cross-sectional). Follow-up 24–84 months.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Studies | Effect (95% CI) | p | Heterogeneity | GRADE |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| MBL change (mm) | 4 | MD 0.07 (−0.09 to 0.24) | 0.38 | I²=48% | very low |
| PPD change (mm) | 3 | MD 0.11 (−0.29 to 0.51) | 0.59 | I²=68% | very low |
| Peri-implant mucositis | 1 (Latimer) | **RR 1.74 (1.06–2.86)** | **0.03** | — | low |
| Peri-implantitis | 2 (Alhakeem, Latimer) | RR 1.63 (0.88–3.02) | 0.12 | I²=0% | low |

- Only peri-implant mucositis reached significance (182 more cases per 1000). Peri-implantitis: 58 more per 1000 (non-significant).
- OPC prevalence across studies: 11.8% (8 yr, Alhakeem) → 54.2% (>10 yr, Latimer); ~50% lost by 9 yr (Byun). Strong positive correlation with follow-up length.
- Mesial > distal (Sheba: 2.1× mesial); mandible > maxilla (20% vs 15%); anterior > posterior (18% vs 14%) — French.
- Food impaction 2.2× more frequent at OPC sites (Byun: 63% vs 39% embrasures), but food impaction was NOT significantly linked to peri-implant outcomes.
- Leave-one-out (MBL): pooled MD 0.03–0.16, remained non-significant.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 5 studies; 4 at serious risk of bias (confounding, no assessor blinding), 1 moderate (Byun). Overall certainty low to very low.
- Cross-sectional designs preclude causality; retrospective bias; heterogeneous contact-assessment methods (different floss types).
- Mixed implant-tooth and implant-implant contacts; some treated-periodontitis patients (confounder), though all under supportive periodontal care — which may have mitigated OPC effects (contrast with Koori/Saber that found significant harm).
- Calls for well-designed longitudinal cohorts and validated preventive measures (splinting, occlusal guards/Essix retainers; screw-retained restorations for easy correction).

## 6. Related Work

- Prevalence/risk-factor body: Varthis (34–66% prevalence), Sheba 2023 SR (mesial 2.1×), Fathi 2022 umbrella, Ghasemi 2022 MA, Abduo 2022 qualitative SR.
- Contrast: Koori, Saber reported significant deeper PPD/CAL/MBL with OPC (this SR did not, attributed to good oral hygiene cohorts).

## 7. Glossary

- **OPC / Open Proximal Contact**: loss of contact between an implant restoration and the adjacent tooth/implant; floss passes without resistance.
- **CPC**: closed (tight) proximal contact.
- **MBL**: marginal bone level (radiographic; implant platform to first bone-to-implant contact).
- **PPD**: probing pocket depth.
- **Peri-implant mucositis**: reversible inflammation of peri-implant soft tissue without bone loss beyond initial remodeling.
- **Peri-implantitis**: inflammation with progressive bone loss + increased PPD.
- **ROBINS-I V2**: Cochrane risk-of-bias tool for non-randomized studies of interventions.
