## One-line Summary

Narrative review (Zurich 2026) synthesizing biological and clinical evidence that concave, polished emergence profiles with adequately tall abutments are the key modifiable prosthetic factor for preventing and reversing peri-implant inflammation.

## 한줄요약

내러티브 리뷰(취리히 2026): 오목하고 연마된 출현 프로파일(emergence profile)과 충분히 높은 지대주가 치주위임플란트 염증 예방 및 해소의 핵심 수정 가능한 보철 요소임을 종합.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Unified framework integrating four overlapping nomenclatures: Supracrestal Tissue Height (STH), Implant Supracrestal Complex (ISC), Supraplatform Complex, and Esthetic Biological Contour (EBC/critical-subcritical contour).
2. Demonstrates that emergence profile contour (concave vs. convex) and restorative angle are *modifiable* risk factors for peri-implantitis — independent of surgical accuracy.
3. Establishes that mucosal tunnel depth > 3 mm (driven by over-deep implant placement) is associated with elevated plaque, delayed inflammation resolution, and higher bleeding scores.
4. Defines the role of abutment height (≥ 2 mm titanium base) in repositioning the restorative interface coronally to protect the connective tissue zone.
5. Clinical case: resolving angular bone defect by replacing a convex short-abutment crown with a concave, tall-abutment monolithic zirconia crown — bone defect radiographically resolved at 4 months.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Study design: narrative review synthesizing histological, cross-sectional, observational, and randomized trial evidence.
- No systematic search strategy stated (narrative, not SR).
- Key framework sources: Gómez-Meda et al. (EBC), Su et al. (critical/subcritical contour), Pelekanos et al. (tripartite zone classification), Esquivel et al.
- Supplemented with clinical case report (maxillary lateral incisor implant: 4-year bone loss reversed by prosthetic redesign, plus second case of tissue reconditioning via provisionals).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Parameter | Threshold/Finding |
|---|---|
| Supracrestal complex height | ~3.5–4 mm (2 mm epithelium + 1.5–2 mm connective tissue) above implant platform |
| Ideal implant depth | 3–5 mm apical to future restorative zenith |
| Mucosal tunnel danger zone | > 3 mm tunnel → elevated plaque, delayed inflammation |
| Recommended EP shape | Concave (subcritical B-zone) + slightly convex critical E-zone |
| Abutment height recommendation | ≥ 2 mm titanium base for subcrestal implants |
| CAD/CAM polished surface target | < 0.2 μm Ra to reduce bacterial adhesion |
| Restorative angle risk zone | > 30° associated with peri-implant inflammation in RCTs |

- Concave emergence profile: preferred in most studies; supports hygiene access and soft tissue adaptation.
- Convex EP: acceptable in cases of palatally/lingually positioned implants or to mask soft-tissue deficiencies — but excessive convexity risks mucosal recession.
- Clinical case outcome: angular bone defect at 4-year follow-up resolved radiographically after prosthetic redesign (4-month follow-up post-replacement).
- Provisionalization: milled PMMA preferred for long-term provisionals; 3D-printed hybrid resins also acceptable. "Space, volume, and time" as perio-prosthetic principles.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative design: no systematic search, no GRADE assessment — susceptible to selection bias.
- Clinical case is n=1; cannot establish causality.
- Most supporting RCTs cited are small and short-term.
- No standardized definition of "restorative angle" across cited studies.
- Long-term RCT evidence on EP modification as peri-implantitis treatment is lacking.

## 7. Glossary

- **Emergence Profile (EP)**: Prosthetic contour of the transmucosal restoration portion from implant platform to cervical crown form
- **Restorative Angle**: Angular divergence of the prosthetic emergence relative to the implant axis
- **Supracrestal Complex / STH**: Soft tissue seal above the implant platform (~3.5–4 mm vertical dimension)
- **Critical Contour (E-zone)**: Coronal transmucosal zone near marginal mucosa; shape influences mucosal position
- **Subcritical Contour (B-zone)**: Apical transmucosal zone from platform to marginal mucosa; concavity here facilitates hygiene
- **Titanium Base (Ti-base)**: Prefabricated abutment component onto which a CAD/CAM crown is bonded; height determines interface position
- **PMMA**: Poly-methyl-methacrylate, used for milled provisional restorations
