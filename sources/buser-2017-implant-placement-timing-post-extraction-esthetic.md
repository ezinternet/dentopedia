## One-line Summary
Periodontol 2000 2017;73:84-102: ITI narrative review, history and clinical recommendations for implant timing post-extraction in esthetic zone — immediate (Type I) requires intact socket + thick biotype; early (Type II/III) safer; risk of >1mm recession 20-30% for immediate without selection criteria.

## 1. Key Results
- Historical analysis: pioneer (1975-89) → trial & error (1990-2003) → ITI classification (2003) → risk factor identification (2008) → selection criteria (2013)
- ITI Type I (immediate): highest esthetic risk; requires intact facial wall + medium-thick biotype
- ITI Type II (early, 4-8wk): soft tissue healed; reduced recession risk; recommended when biotype/bone borderline
- ITI Type III (early, 12-16wk): partial bone fill; best for compromised sockets
- ITI Type IV (late, >6mo): healed ridge; most predictable but requires bone augmentation often
- Esthetic complications: 20-30% risk of >1mm midfacial recession for Type I without selection criteria
- After 2008: proper selection criteria (intact wall + thick biotype) reduces this risk significantly
- All survival rates >95% regardless of timing

## 2. Risk Factors Identified
- Thin tissue biotype
- Facial malposition of implant (must be 2mm palatal from future crown margin)
- Thin (<1mm) or damaged facial bone wall
- High smile line
