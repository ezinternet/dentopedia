## Why Ingested
NCCL 병인 논쟁(특히 abfraction)에 대한 초미세구조 직접 증거. [[nccl/nascimento-2016-abfraction-etiology-diagnosis-treatment]]의 "abfraction 미입증" 주장에 대한 in-vitro 반례 데이터로 균형 확보.

## One-line Summary
SEM study of 10 extracted premolars with NCCLs finding microfracture lines in 4/10 (interpreted as supporting abfraction) plus linear scratches and exposed collagen (abrasion/erosion) in the rest.

## 한줄요약
NCCL 발치 소구치 10개의 SEM 연구로, 4/10에서 microfracture(abfraction 지지)·나머지에서 linear scratch와 노출 콜라겐(abrasion/erosion)을 관찰한 in-vitro 연구.

## 1. Document Information
- J Oral Maxillofac Pathol 2021;25(1):202. DOI 10.4103/jomfp.JOMFP_232_20. Open access (CC BY-NC-SA 4.0).
- Mahidol University, Dept of Oral and Maxillofacial Pathology.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Direct ultrastructural evidence: 4 of 10 lesions showed fracture lines/surfaces consistent with the abfraction (microfracture) mechanism.
- All 10 appeared crescent-shaped at low magnification, with upper edge on enamel and lower edge on cementum.
- Documents coexisting abrasion (parallel linear scratches) and erosion (exposed dentinal tubules, poorly fixed collagen network) features — supports multifactorial etiology.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 10 extracted human premolars (1 per patient), 10% formalin fixation, mesiodistal sectioning, NaOCl immersion, graded ethanol dehydration, gold sputter coating, JEOL JSM-6480 LV SEM at x40-x20,000.
- Morphologic mix: 1 concave, 2 shallow, 3 wedge-shaped, 2 notched, 2 irregular.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Microfracture evidence: 4/10 teeth.
- Remaining 6: linear scratches + dentinal tubule openings or exposed collagen fibers (no clear fracture).
- Conclusion: abrasion and erosion are associated etiologic factors; an ultrastructural finding supporting abfraction theory was observed.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small n (10), single operator/interpreter, descriptive (no quantitative fracture metrics). Ex vivo specimens; processing artifacts (NaOCl, dehydration) may alter surface. Cannot establish causation between occlusal stress and observed fractures.

## 6. Related Work
- Nguyen et al. (abrasion/erosion common), Daley et al. (no histopathologic abfraction evidence), Michael/Levrini SEM studies, Romeed FEA of canine abfraction.

## 7. Glossary
- SEM: scanning electron microscopy.
- Crescent-shaped lesion: low-magnification NCCL appearance, wide in middle and pointed at ends.
