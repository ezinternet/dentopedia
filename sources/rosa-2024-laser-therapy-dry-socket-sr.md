## One-line Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis of 3 selected studies assessing laser therapy (LLLT) for dry socket treatment; laser proves effective vs. Alvogyl but correlation not highly significant, suggesting it as a viable minimally invasive alternative.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health 2024;24:700
- **DOI**: 10.1186/s12903-024-04461-w
- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Institution**: University of Rome Tor Vergata (Departments of Chemical Science and Technologies; Biomedicine and Prevention; Clinical Sciences and Translational Medicine)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Evaluates laser therapy specifically for dry socket (alveolar osteitis) treatment, not just prevention
- Establishes laser as a viable alternative to conventional antiseptic dressings (Alvogyl — eugenol/chlorhexidine-based)
- Low-level laser therapy (LLLT): anti-inflammatory, wound healing, antimicrobial effects in a single-session procedure
- Discusses SaliCept patch (acemannan/Aloe vera hydrogel) as additional alternative treatment

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Databases: PubMed, Embase, Scopus, Google Scholar, Web of Science
- Search period: 1998 to January 31, 2024
- Search strategy: "laser" AND "dry socket" (boolean AND)
- Framework: PECO model
  - P: Individuals who have undergone extraction with dry socket
  - E: Patients with alveolar osteitis (AO) undergoing laser therapy
  - C: Patients with AO undergoing various other therapies
  - O: Efficacy of laser treatment for AO, secondary: preventive effectiveness
- Inclusion: English articles; clinical trials and RCTs with laser treatment
- Exclusion: non-PECO, duplicate, books, letters/editorials, experimental studies (non-clinical)
- 50 studies identified across databases; only 3 selected for systematic review and meta-analysis

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Meta-analysis: Laser treatment effective vs. Alvogyl for alveolar osteitis treatment
- Correlation not highly significant
- Laser advantages: significantly less pain, faster healing, fewer side effects vs. antiseptic dressing (from 60-patient RCT cited)
- LLLT mechanism: reduces inflammation, enhances wound healing, antimicrobial effects on oral mucosa
- Single-session procedure: minimally invasive
- SaliCept patch (acemannan) noted as another recent treatment option with antimicrobial properties

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Only 3 studies met inclusion criteria from 50 identified — very limited evidence base
- Non-highly significant correlation in meta-analysis
- Need for larger, well-designed RCTs specifically on laser therapy for dry socket
- Optimal laser parameters (wavelength, power, duration) not standardized

## 6. Related Work
- Camps-Font et al. 2024 (antibiotic prophylaxis NMA) — prevention of dry socket
- Conventional treatments: chlorhexidine and eugenol-containing dressings (Alvogyl)
- SaliCept patch (acemannan/Aloe vera) as alternative dry socket treatment

## 7. Glossary
- **Dry socket (alveolar osteitis, AO)**: Painful postoperative complication; premature blood clot loss after extraction resulting in exposed bone, severe pain, delayed healing, infection risk
- **Alvogyl**: Commercial antiseptic dressing containing eugenol; conventional first-line dry socket treatment
- **LLLT (Low-Level Laser Therapy)**: Low-power laser applied to tissue; uses photobiomodulation to reduce inflammation and promote healing without thermal damage
- **SaliCept patch**: Freeze-dried acemannan (Aloe vera-derived) hydrogel preparation with antimicrobial properties
- **PECO model**: Population, Exposure, Comparator, Outcomes — framework for systematic review inclusion
