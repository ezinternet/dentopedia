## One-line Summary
SR+MA (20 RCTs): single-dose ibuprofen 400mg shows large effect (TOTPAR6 SMD=3.009) for post-3rd molar extraction pain; acetaminophen 600mg+codeine 60mg shows moderate effect (TOTPAR6 SMD=0.796); ibuprofen clearly superior.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Pain Medicine 2022;23(6):1176–1185
- DOI: 10.1093/pm/pnab334
- Advance access: November 24, 2021
- Design: Systematic review + meta-analysis
- Databases: PubMed, ClinicalTrials.gov
- Inclusion: randomized, blinded, placebo-controlled; single-dose A/C (APAP 600mg+codeine 60mg) or ibuprofen; outcome TOTPAR6 or PR at 6h
- n studies = 20 (of 2,949 identified, 79 full-text reviewed)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Focused comparison: ibuprofen (400mg) vs acetaminophen/codeine (A/C) combination specifically in 3rd molar extraction model
- Ibuprofen TOTPAR6 SMD=3.009 = large effect (>0.8 threshold)
- A/C TOTPAR6 SMD=0.796 = moderate effect (borderline large)
- PR at 6h: ibuprofen SMD=0.854 (significant); A/C SMD=0.019 (not significant, p=0.059)
- Supports transition away from codeine-containing opioid combinations to ibuprofen monotherapy

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Keywords: "molar/molars," "tooth/teeth," "extraction," "pain" in PubMed + ClinicalTrials.gov
- Inclusion criteria: (1) randomized, blinded, placebo-controlled; (2) APAP 600mg+codeine 60mg or ibuprofen 400mg single dose; (3) TOTPAR6 or PR at 6h recorded
- Weighted standardized mean difference (SMD) calculated vs placebo
- 20 final studies included

## 4. Key Results
| Drug | TOTPAR6 SMD | 95% CI | p | PR at 6h SMD | 95% CI | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ibuprofen 400mg | 3.009 | 1.283–4.735 | 0.001 | 0.854 | 0.712–0.996 | <0.001 |
| APAP 600mg + Codeine 60mg | 0.796 | 0.597–0.995 | <0.001 | 0.019 | 0.007–0.378 | 0.059 (NS) |

SMD ≥0.8 = large effect size.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Single-dose design only; chronic dosing patterns not captured
- Ibuprofen doses in included studies may vary beyond 400mg fixed dose assumption
- Search limited to two databases; potential publication bias
- Did not evaluate combination ibuprofen+APAP (most relevant to current guidelines)

## 6. Related Work
- Miroshnychenko 2023 (NMA including ibuprofen+APAP combination, which outperforms ibuprofen alone)
- Feldman 2024 (RCT: ibuprofen+APAP vs hydrocodone+APAP, n=1,815)

## 7. Glossary
- TOTPAR6: Total pain relief summed over 6 hours (standardized scale)
- PR: Pain relief at single timepoint
- SMD: Standardized mean difference (vs placebo)
- A/C: Acetaminophen/codeine combination
