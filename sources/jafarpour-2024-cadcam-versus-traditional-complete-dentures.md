## Why Ingested

This SR+MA from JOR 2024 extends [[wiki/complete-denture/srinivasan-2021-cad-cam-removable-complete-dentures]] by incorporating clinician-reported outcomes and cost data not analyzed in the 2021 review, and by subgrouping results by manufacturing method (milled vs. 3D printed) — providing a more nuanced evidence base for digital complete denture adoption decisions.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (11 studies) finding CAD/CAM complete dentures comparable to traditional in patient satisfaction and OHRQoL, with milled CDs superior in clinician satisfaction and fewer adjustments, and CAD/CAM requiring significantly lower laboratory and total costs.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First SR+MA to separate patient-reported outcomes (PROs) from clinician-reported outcomes (CROs) and cost data for CAD/CAM vs. traditional complete dentures.
2. Subgroup analyses by manufacturing technique (milled vs. 3D printed) reveal divergent clinician satisfaction patterns not visible in pooled analyses.
3. Quantified cost advantage of CAD/CAM: significantly lower laboratory costs and total treatment costs vs. traditional workflow.
4. Pooled evidence for OHRQoL (OHIP instruments) shows no significant difference — CAD/CAM achieves parity, not superiority.
5. Highlights critical evidence gap: most included studies have small samples (<30) and moderate-to-high risk of bias; further well-designed RCTs needed.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Search strategy:**
- Databases: MEDLINE (Ovid), Embase, Scopus, Cochrane CENTRAL
- Date range: January 2000 – May 2022
- Languages: English, Arabic, French, Persian, Portuguese, Spanish
- Starting pool: 3,126 records; 2,271 after deduplication; 11 studies included

**Eligibility (PICOS):**
- Population: completely edentulous patients needing removable complete dentures
- Intervention: CAD/CAM CDs (milled or 3D printed)
- Comparison: conventionally fabricated CDs
- Outcomes: patient satisfaction, OHRQoL, clinician satisfaction, post-insertion adjustment visits, laboratory/total costs
- Study design: all clinical study designs (RCTs, observational, cross-sectional); excluded in vitro, case reports, reviews

**Included studies (n=11):**
- 3 cross-over RCTs, 1 parallel-arm RCT, 1 non-randomized controlled clinical trial, 2 prospective observational, 3 retrospective observational, 1 cross-sectional
- Publication years: 2012–2022

**Statistical methods:**
- Random-effects model (DerSimonian and Laird)
- Pooled as standardized mean differences (SMD) using Hedge's d
- Heterogeneity: Cochran Q, I², H², τ²; substantial heterogeneity defined as I² ≥ 50%
- No funnel plots (insufficient studies <10); fail-safe number calculated instead
- Cost data converted to USD for comparability; Likert data transformed to VAS (0–100 mm)

**Risk of bias:**
- Modified Jadad scale (RCTs) + Newcastle-Ottawa scale (observational)
- Most studies showed moderate-to-high risk; small sample sizes limit power

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Patient satisfaction (5 studies):**
- Traditional vs. 3D printed: ES = −0.88 (95% CI: −2.58 to 0.82); I² = 91% (favors traditional, NS)
- Traditional vs. milled: ES = 0.62 (95% CI: 0.95–1.89); I² = 0% (favors milled, NS)
- Pooled: no significant difference (pooled MD = −0.11; 95% CI: −1.15 to 0.93; p = .84)
- High heterogeneity (I² = 90%)

**OHRQoL (3 cross-over RCTs, OHIP instruments):**
- Pooled ES = 0.39 (95% CI: −0.28 to 1.05); no significant difference
- Instruments used: OHIP-G49, OHIP-EDENT, OHIP-EDENT-J; data collected at 6 weeks to 3 months post-insertion
- I² = 69% (high heterogeneity)
- Subgroup for 4 clinically relevant OHIP dimensions (physical pain, physical disability, psychological discomfort, handicap): no significant difference

**Clinician satisfaction (3 studies):**
- Milled CDs: ES = 1.42 (95% CI: 0.95–1.89; I² = 0%) — significantly superior to traditional CDs
- 3D printed CDs: ES = 0.56 (95% CI: −0.90 to 2.01; I² = 92%) — no significant difference from traditional
- Pooled (milled + 3D printed combined): no overall significant difference (pooled ES = 0.98; 95% CI: −0.06 to 2.03; p = .07), driven by high heterogeneity (I² = 93%)

**Post-insertion adjustment visits (4 studies):**
- Milled CDs required fewer adjustment visits than traditional CDs
- 3D printed CDs had comparable adjustments to traditional CDs
- Retrospective data (Kim et al., n=637 dentures) showed no significant difference in adjustment frequency or reline rates

**Costs (3 studies):**
- Laboratory costs: CAD/CAM significantly lower than traditional (p < .001 in most studies)
- Total costs: CAD/CAM significantly lower overall
- Clinical/chair time: CAD/CAM required fewer clinical visits; digital workflow averaged 205 min less chair time per case (Kattadiyil et al.)
- Material costs: higher for digital workflows, but offset by reduced labor/lab costs

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample sizes (most studies <30 participants) limit statistical power
- High heterogeneity (I² up to 93%) prevents firm conclusions from pooled data
- Only 11 studies met inclusion criteria; wide variation in CAD/CAM workflows, operators, and settings
- Short follow-up periods (days to months); no long-term (≥1 year) outcome data available
- Cannot fully separate effects of milling vs. 3D printing due to limited studies per subgroup
- Publication bias cannot be excluded (funnel plots not feasible <10 studies)
- Future needs: well-designed multicenter RCTs, standardized outcome measures, long-term follow-up, specific subgroup analyses by CAD/CAM system

## 7. Glossary

- **CAD/CAM**: Computer-Aided Design / Computer-Aided Manufacturing — digital workflow encompassing data capture (IOS or conventional impression + scan), software design, and either milling (subtractive) or 3D printing (additive) fabrication
- **OHRQoL**: Oral Health-Related Quality of Life — patient-reported impact of oral conditions on daily functioning
- **OHIP-EDENT**: Oral Health Impact Profile for EDENTulous patients — 20-item validated PRO instrument (score 0–80; higher = worse)
- **SMD (Hedge's d)**: Standardized Mean Difference — scale-free effect size; small ≥0.3, medium ≥0.5, large ≥1.0
- **Post-insertion adjustment visit**: Clinical appointment after denture delivery to relieve pressure areas, adjust occlusion, or address fit complaints
- **AvaDent**: Commercial milled complete denture system (milling workflow); used in several included studies
- **DENTCA**: 3D printing complete denture system (additive manufacturing)
- **Fail-safe number**: Statistical estimate of how many unpublished null studies would be required to reverse the pooled effect — used when funnel plots are not feasible
