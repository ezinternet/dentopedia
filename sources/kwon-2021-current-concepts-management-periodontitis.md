## Why Ingested
Concise clinical review covering the 2017 World Workshop staging/grading classification, risk factor quantification, and step-by-step treatment protocol from SRP through regenerative surgery and maintenance — a foundational reference complementing [[oral-microbiology/hajishengallis-2012-psd-model-periodontal-disease]] on host-pathogen dynamics.

## One-line Summary
Narrative review synthesising the aetiology, 2017 staging/grading classification, risk factors (smoking OR 3.25–7.28; diabetes OR 2.81–3.43), and treatment sequence (home care → SRP ± systemic antibiotics → surgical therapy → maintenance) for periodontitis.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Applies the 2017 World Workshop multidimensional staging (I–IV) and grading (A–C) system to clinical practice.
- Quantifies smoking risk: alveolar bone loss OR 3.25 (light) to 7.28 (heavy); attachment loss OR 2.05–4.07 vs non-smokers.
- Quantifies diabetes risk: CAL OR 2.81; alveolar bone loss OR 3.43 for type 2 diabetes.
- Adjunctive systemic antibiotics to SRP yield additional probing depth reduction of 0.448 mm and CAL gain of 0.389 mm at 6 months (meta-analysis of 28 RCTs).
- Reviews regenerative (GTR, bone grafts) and resective surgical options for residual pockets post-SRP re-evaluation.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Design: concise narrative clinical review (non-systematic).
- Scope: pathogenesis, natural history, diagnosis (staging/grading), risk factors, non-surgical therapy, surgical therapy, maintenance.
- Population: adult periodontitis patients; references large US NHANES surveys and global prevalence data.
- No formal systematic search or PRISMA; review-level evidence synthesis.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Prevalence: 42% of US adults (NHANES 2009–2014) have periodontitis; 7.8% severe; ~11% globally (743 million individuals).
- Smoking OR for alveolar bone loss: light smoker 3.25, heavy smoker 7.28 vs non-smokers.
- Smoking OR for attachment loss: light 2.05, heavy 4.07 vs non-smokers.
- Type 2 diabetes OR: 2.81 (CAL), 3.43 (alveolar bone loss).
- Adjunctive systemic antibiotics (meta-analysis, 28 double-blind RCTs): additional PD reduction 0.448 mm, CAL gain 0.389 mm at 6 months; PD 0.485 mm and CAL 0.285 mm at 12 months.
- Staging criteria: Stage I CAL 1–2 mm; Stage II 3–4 mm; Stage III/IV ≥5 mm with complexity escalation.
- Grading: Grade A (bone loss/age <0.25), Grade B (0.25–1.0), Grade C (≥1.0); modified by smoking ≥10 cigs/day and HbA1c ≥7.0%.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative design with no systematic search; conclusions largely consensus-based.
- Heterogeneity in antibiotic RCTs not fully addressed (type, duration, dosage variability).
- Regenerative procedure section lacks quantitative outcomes (CAL gain, bone fill data).
- Future work needed on optimal maintenance interval individualisation and long-term implant vs natural tooth retention data.

## 6. Related Work
- 2017 World Workshop classification (Papapanou et al. 2018) is the primary staging/grading reference.
- Builds on polymicrobial synergy models (Hajishengallis & Lamont) for red-complex pathogenesis.
- Antibiotic meta-analysis sourced from Herrera et al. 2002 / Rabelo et al. 2015 literature.

## 7. Glossary
- CAL: clinical attachment loss. SRP: scaling and root planing. GTR: guided tissue regeneration.
- HbA1c: haemoglobin A1c. BOP: bleeding on probing. AGE/RAGE: advanced glycation end products / their receptors.
- RANK-L / RANK: receptor activator of nuclear factor κB ligand / receptor — osteoclast activation pathway.
