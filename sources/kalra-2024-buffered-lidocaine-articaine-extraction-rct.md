## Why Ingested

기존 [[wiki/drug/karm-2017-lidocaine-epinephrine-200000-third-molar]]가 epinephrine 농도 변수(1:80,000 vs 1:200,000)를 다뤘다면, 본 RCT는 또 다른 LA 조정 변수인 **buffering(8.4% NaHCO3 chair-side alkalinization)**과 **drug choice(lidocaine vs articaine)**가 IANB onset/효과에 미치는 영향을 4-arm 비교로 정량화한다. Buffered-LA cluster의 onset-of-action anchor로서 sibling pages [[wiki/drug/hamad-2025-buffered-lidocaine-infiltration-extraction-rct]], [[wiki/drug/karunakar-2024-buffered-precooled-lidocaine-ianb-pulpitis]], [[wiki/drug/gandhi-2022-preheated-buffered-lignocaine-ianb-pulpitis-rct]]를 보강하며, [[wiki/drug/hwang-2023-proposal-clinical-guidelines-local-anesthesia-dentistry]]의 한국 LA 가이드라인 맥락에 buffering 근거를 추가한다.

## One-line Summary

Prospective 4-arm RCT (n=28, 7/group), mandibular extraction with IANB: chair-side buffering (0.2 mL 8.4% NaHCO3 into 2 mL cartridge) significantly accelerated onset of all anesthetic milestones (first symptom, lip numbness, subjective, objective) for both 2% lidocaine and 4% articaine; buffered articaine was fastest overall (objective onset 84 s vs 181 s for non-buffered lidocaine), while VAS pain during extraction did not differ across groups (χ²=4.107, p=0.250).

## 한줄요약

전향적 4-군 RCT (n=28, 군당 7명), 하악 발치 IANB: chair-side buffering(2 mL 카트리지에 8.4% 중탄산나트륨 0.2 mL 치환)이 2% 리도카인·4% 아티카인 모두에서 마취 발현(첫 증상·입술 마비·주관·객관)을 유의하게 단축; buffered articaine이 가장 빠름(객관적 발현 84초 vs non-buffered lidocaine 181초). 단, 발치 중 VAS 통증은 4군 간 차이 없음(χ²=4.107, p=0.250).

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First 4-arm head-to-head** comparing all combinations of buffered/non-buffered × lidocaine/articaine for mandibular IANB extraction (authors note no prior study compared all four simultaneously).
2. Quantified **onset acceleration from buffering** across four sequential milestones (first symptom, lip numbness, subjective, objective) with explicit second-level means.
3. Demonstrated **drug × buffering interaction**: articaine is intrinsically faster than lidocaine, and buffering compounds the speed advantage — buffered articaine being fastest, non-buffered lidocaine slowest.
4. Showed **onset benefit does not translate to a VAS pain difference** during the extraction itself — separating the "faster onset" claim from an "less intraoperative pain" claim.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: prospective randomized clinical study, single oral-and-maxillofacial-surgery department.
- **n = 28**, randomized into 4 groups of 7:
  - Group 1: non-buffered 2% lidocaine + 1:100,000 epinephrine
  - Group 2: non-buffered 4% articaine + 1:100,000 epinephrine
  - Group 3: buffered 2% lidocaine + 1:100,000 epinephrine (8.4% NaHCO3)
  - Group 4: buffered 4% articaine + 1:100,000 epinephrine (8.4% NaHCO3)
- **Buffering protocol**: 0.2 mL withdrawn from a 2 mL cartridge (octocaine; Septodont) and replaced with 0.2 mL 8.4% sodium bicarbonate via insulin syringe; cartridge inverted 5× to mix; no precipitation.
- **Technique**: single operator, IANB with 27-G 1.5-inch needle (Septoject), standard aspirating syringe.
- **Outcomes**: time of onset of (a) first symptom, (b) lip numbness, (c) subjective symptoms (tongue numbness), (d) objective symptoms (negative probing) by stopwatch; VAS pain during extraction. Missed blocks (no subjective symptoms) were excluded.
- **Exclusions**: <18 y, significant medical history, pregnancy, LA/sulfite allergy, interfering medications, unable to consent.
- **Statistics**: Shapiro–Wilk (normality), one-way ANOVA + Tukey post-hoc for onset times; Chi-square for VAS; α = 0.05; SPSS v16.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Mean onset times (seconds):

| Group | First symptom | Lip numbness | Subjective | Objective |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Non-buffered lidocaine | 58.71 | 78.71 | 128.00 | 181.00 |
| Non-buffered articaine | 36.14 | 70.00 | 97.00 | 136.28 |
| Buffered lidocaine | 30.57 | 51.85 | 82.72 | 100.00 |
| Buffered articaine | 16.85 | 38.57 | 67.42 | 84.14 |

- **First symptom**: p < 0.001 across groups. Tukey significant pairs: non-buffered lido vs buffered lido; non-buffered lido vs non-buffered articaine; non-buffered lido vs buffered articaine; non-buffered articaine vs buffered articaine.
- **Lip numbness**: p = 0.003. Significant pairs: non-buffered lido vs buffered articaine; non-buffered articaine vs buffered articaine.
- **Subjective symptoms**: p = 0.014. Significant pair: non-buffered lido vs buffered articaine.
- **Objective symptoms**: p = 0.009. Significant pairs: non-buffered lido vs buffered lido; non-buffered lido vs buffered articaine.
- **VAS during extraction**: χ² = 4.107, **p = 0.250 — NOT significant** across the four groups.

Direction: buffered < non-buffered for the same drug; articaine < lidocaine for the same buffering state.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Very small sample** (n = 7 per group, 28 total) — many pairwise Tukey comparisons were non-significant likely due to underpowering; the abstract's claim "efficacy significantly better in buffered" overstates what the per-pair data support (only some pairs reached significance).
- **Confounded comparison**: 2% lidocaine vs 4% articaine differ in both concentration and molecule, so "articaine fastest" cannot be cleanly attributed to the drug alone.
- **No injection-pain (VAS-on-injection) endpoint** — the most commonly cited benefit of buffering (less stinging) was not measured here; only post-injection onset and extraction-time VAS.
- **No duration-of-anesthesia or success-rate endpoint** — purely onset-focused.
- **Single operator, single center, no blinding detail** for outcome assessor reported.
- Future: larger powered trials; isolate buffering effect within a single drug; add injection-pain and duration endpoints.

## 7. Glossary

- **Buffering / alkalinization**: adding sodium bicarbonate (8.4%) to acidic LA cartridge to raise pH toward ~7.4, increasing the uncharged (base) fraction that crosses the nerve membrane → faster onset.
- **IANB (Inferior Alveolar Nerve Block)**: standard mandibular block at the mandibular foramen.
- **Subjective symptoms**: patient-reported numbness (lip, tongue).
- **Objective symptoms**: clinician-verified anesthesia (negative probing response).
- **VAS (Visual Analog Scale)**: 0–10 pain rating.
- **Onset**: time from injection to the anesthetic milestone, measured by stopwatch.
