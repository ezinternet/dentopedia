## One-line Summary
SR+MA of 46 studies (n=3,250 miniscrews) finding an overall orthodontic miniscrew failure rate of 13.5%, with gingival tissue type and smoking as the only statistically significant risk factors.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: European Journal of Orthodontics, Volume 40, Issue 5, October 2018, Pages 519–530
- **DOI**: 10.1093/ejo/cjx093
- **Published**: 05 January 2018
- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Search date**: Up to July 2017
- **Databases**: Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews, MEDLINE, Scopus, Ovid; plus grey literature and hand search

## 2. Key Contributions
- Provides a pooled failure rate estimate for orthodontic miniscrews across RCTs and prospective cohort studies
- Identifies statistically significant risk factors (gingival tissue type, smoking) vs non-significant factors (diameter, length, design, patient age, jaw of insertion)
- Establishes that miniscrews have an "acceptably low" failure rate of 13.5% with 95% CI 11.5–15.9%

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Eligibility**: RCTs and prospective cohort studies (PCSs), English language, reporting failure rate of miniscrews used as orthodontic anchorage, diameter <2 mm
- **Included**: 16 RCTs and 30 PCSs (46 studies total); 41 studies in meta-analysis (5 excluded due to insufficient statistical data)
- **Sample**: ~3,250 miniscrews pooled
- **Statistical model**: Random-effects model; heterogeneity assessed via I² and Chi² tests
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane Risk of Bias tool and Newcastle-Ottawa Scale
- **Subgroup and sensitivity analyses** performed for robustness

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Overall failure rate**: 13.5% (95% CI 11.5–15.9%)
- **Statistically significant risk factors**:
  - Gingival tissue type (keratinized vs mobile mucosa) — significant effect
  - Smoking — significant effect
- **Non-significant risk factors**:
  - Miniscrew diameter, length, and design — minimal effect
  - Patient age — minimal effect
  - Jaw of insertion (maxilla vs mandible) — minimal effect
- **Heterogeneity**: High I² across studies; results should be interpreted with caution
- **Publication bias**: Assessed but detailed results not reported in abstract

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- High level of heterogeneity among included studies
- Unbalanced group sizes across studies
- High-quality RCTs with large sample sizes needed to confirm findings
- Abstract-level access only; full quantitative subgroup data not available

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary
- **Miniscrew (mini-implant, TAD)**: Temporary anchorage device used in orthodontics for skeletal anchorage without patient compliance
- **TAD**: Temporary Anchorage Device
- **Random-effects model**: Meta-analytic model accounting for between-study variance
- **I²**: Measure of heterogeneity across studies; higher values indicate greater inconsistency
- **Keratinized gingiva**: Attached, non-mobile gingival tissue at orthodontic miniscrew insertion sites
