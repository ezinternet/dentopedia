## One-line Summary
J Clin Diagn Res 2015;9(7):ZC56-ZC60 (Triveni Institute, India) — in vitro evaluation of marginal seating of cast single crowns, 3-unit FPDs, and multi-unit FPDs with pier abutment at 0°, 6°, 12°, 20° convergence angles; convergence-marginal fit interaction documented across configurations.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Journal of Clinical and Diagnostic Research 2015;9(7):ZC56-ZC60
- Type: In vitro experimental
- Affiliation: Triveni Institute of Dental Sciences, Bilaspur, India

## 2. Key Contributions
- Extends convergence-angle research from single crowns to multi-unit FPDs with pier abutment
- Quantifies marginal seating across 4 convergence levels (0°, 6°, 12°, 20°)
- Addresses an underexplored configuration: pier abutment in multi-unit casting

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 3 dies in arch form per convergence configuration (single, 3-unit, multi-unit with pier)
- 4 arch forms × 4 convergence angles (0°, 6°, 12°, 20°)
- Cast Co-Cr/metal copings/FPDs
- Marginal seating measured pre/post cementation

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Marginal seating depends on convergence angle × FPD configuration interaction
- Multi-unit FPDs with pier abutment require careful convergence management
- Detailed numeric tables in original PDF (see paper)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- In vitro; cast metal only (no ceramic comparison)
- 0° convergence is impractical clinically — included for trend completeness
- Standard die material may not reflect tooth structure deflection

## 6. Related Work
- Tylman — "20-50 gingivocclusally" parallelism statement
- Johnston — "50-70 parallelism" alternative recommendation

## 7. Glossary
- Pier abutment: 중간 지대치 (FPD에서 양 끝이 아닌 중간 부위에 위치한 지대치)
- Marginal seating: 변연 (마진) 적합
- Marginal gap: 변연 간극 (Marginal Discrepancy)
- FPD: 고정성 부분의치 (Fixed Partial Denture)
