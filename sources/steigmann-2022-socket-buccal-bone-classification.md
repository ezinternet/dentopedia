## Why Ingested

발치와 협측 골 형태에 따른 치조골 보존술(ARP) 처치 결정 트리 제공 — 협측 골의 무결성, 두께, 골열개/천공 여부에 따라 임상 처치를 단계별로 안내하며 [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/araujo-2005-dimensional-ridge-alterations-tooth-extraction]]의 발치 후 치조골 변화 생물학을 임상 처치 알고리즘으로 연결.

## One-line Summary

Narrative review proposing a 3-type socket classification (ST1–ST3) based on buccal bone morphology with a clinical decision tree matching each type to the most predictable alveolar ridge preservation technique.

## 한줄요약

협측 골 형태에 따른 3형 발치와 분류(ST1–ST3)와 각 유형별 치조골 보존술 처치 결정 트리를 제안한 서술적 고찰.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Proposed the ST (Socket Type) classification: 3 main types based on buccal bone morphology
- Created a clinical decision tree matching each ST to specific ARP treatment modality
- Emphasized CBCT for pre-extraction planning and bone sounding as chair-side screening tool
- Highlighted buccal bone thickness and integrity as the strongest predictors of ridge change magnitude
- Excluded primary wound closure and immediate implant placement approaches (separate algorithms)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Literature review of RCTs, systematic reviews, and clinical studies on ARP outcomes. Classification developed by expert consensus (University of Michigan GBR group + Steigmann Institute). Decision tree validated against existing literature on ARP techniques.

**ST Classification System**:

| Type | Definition | Sub-class |
|---|---|---|
| ST1 | Intact buccal bone | ST1A: thickness ≥1.0 mm; ST1B: thickness <1.0 mm |
| ST2 | Buccal bone fenestration (regardless of thickness) | — |
| ST3 | Buccal bone dehiscence (compromised height) | ST3A: <1/3 buccal height; ST3B: 1/3–2/3; ST3C: >2/3 |

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Decision tree — ARP recommendations by socket type**:

- **ST1A** (thick intact bone ≥1 mm): No ARP needed. Optional collagen matrix for hemostasis only. Self-healing sufficient.
- **ST1B** (thin intact bone <1 mm): Particulate bone graft + collagen dressing/plug secured by cross suture. No membrane needed.
- **ST2** (fenestration, any thickness): Absorbable membrane ± bone graft covering the fenestration. Flap elevation optional but improves membrane placement.
- **ST3A** (dehiscence <1/3 height): Absorbable membrane + bone graft. Flap elevation recommended.
- **ST3B** (dehiscence 1/3–2/3): Non-resorbable membrane or reinforced membrane + bone graft + flap elevation mandatory.
- **ST3C** (dehiscence >2/3): Advanced regenerative approach — non-resorbable PTFE/Ti-mesh + bone graft + full flap elevation. Most demanding scenario.

**Key literature anchors**:
- Thin buccal bone → more extensive bone volume resorption (Avila-Ortiz et al.)
- Thick buccal bone → ARP effect minimal (bone resorbs less regardless)
- XV European Workshop on Periodontology 2019: ARP reduces resorption by 1–2.5 mm overall

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative review — not systematic; classification not yet clinically validated in prospective studies
- No inter-examiner reliability data for ST classification
- Decision tree does not include immediate implant placement — separate algorithm needed
- Buccal bone thickness threshold (1 mm) based on limited evidence; cut-off not universally agreed

## 7. Glossary

- **ARP (Alveolar Ridge Preservation)**: Procedures performed at time of extraction to reduce post-extraction ridge resorption
- **ST (Socket Type)**: Classification category based on buccal bone morphology (ST1–ST3)
- **Fenestration**: Window-like defect through buccal bone wall without loss of crestal bone height
- **Dehiscence**: Vertical bone defect involving loss of crestal bone height on buccal aspect
- **Non-resorbable membrane**: PTFE or Ti-mesh — provides prolonged space maintenance for more severe defects
