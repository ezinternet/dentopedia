## One-line Summary

Retrospective cast/radiograph analysis showing that posterior teeth left unopposed for ≥5 years develop positional changes (overeruption, tipping, drifting) that cause deviation from the ideal Broadrick occlusal curve, complicating prosthetic restoration of a harmonious occlusal scheme.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Demonstrates that when a posterior tooth has remained **unopposed for 5 years or more**, positional changes occur (overeruption, tipping, drifting) that cause **deviation from the Broadrick (ideal) occlusal curve**.
- States that the extent of this deviation **may be extreme**, potentially leading to difficulties in restoring a harmonious occlusal scheme.
- Shows that the Broadrick flag method can still provide an **accurate reproduction of the occlusal curve even when the posterior determinant tooth is tipped** — a **moderate degree of tipping** of that tooth has little effect on the radius of the Broadrick curve.
- Builds on the authors' prior validation work (Craddock et al. 2005, J Oral Rehabil 32:895–900) showing the Broadrick flag method is relatively accurate for most intact arches.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Uses the **Broadrick flag method** (a geometric/compass technique for constructing the ideal occlusal curve / curve of Spee on study casts), following Lynch & McConnell's prosthodontic application of the Broadrick flag.
- Compares the **existing** occlusal curve of dentitions deranged by posterior tooth loss against the **ideal Broadrick curve**.
- Examines the influence of tipping of the posterior determinant tooth on the radius/position of the constructed Broadrick curve.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Unopposed ≥5 years → deviation**: posterior teeth left unopposed for 5 years or more show positional changes causing deviation from the Broadrick curve; the deviation can be **extreme**.
- **Tipping tolerance**: a **moderate degree of tipping** of the posterior determinant tooth has **little effect** on the radius of the Broadrick curve — the method remains an accurate reproduction of the occlusal curve under moderate tipping.
- **Clinical implication**: extreme deviation complicates restoration of a harmonious occlusal scheme and presents challenges for the dental technician.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Retrospective observational design; no randomization or intervention.
- The captured text is the publisher landing page (abstract + references) — full-body method/result detail (sample, measurements, statistics) is not transcribed.
- The Broadrick method's tolerance was assessed for **moderate** tipping; extreme tipping/derangement may exceed the method's accuracy.
- Generalizability and the exact 5-year threshold derive from this specific Leeds dataset.

## 7. Glossary

- **Broadrick occlusal curve / Broadrick flag method**: A geometric technique (using a flag/compass and reference points) to construct the ideal anteroposterior occlusal curve (curve of Spee) on study casts for prosthetic/occlusal rehabilitation.
- **Curve of Spee**: The anteroposterior curvature of the occlusal plane from the canine tip through the buccal cusps of the posterior teeth.
- **Overeruption (supraeruption)**: Continued eruption of a tooth that has lost its antagonist, moving it beyond the occlusal plane.
- **Tipping**: Angular inclination of a tooth (often mesial) into an adjacent edentulous space.
- **Drifting**: Bodily migration of a tooth toward an edentulous space.
- **Posterior determinant tooth**: The most posterior tooth used as a reference point in constructing the Broadrick occlusal curve.
- **Harmonious occlusal scheme**: An occlusion in which the occlusal plane/curve permits balanced, interference-free contacts.
