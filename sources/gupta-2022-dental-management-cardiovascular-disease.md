## One-line Summary
Narrative review covering dental management of 7 major CVD categories: hypertension (BP thresholds, drug interactions), IHD/MI (no elective <30 days post-MI), arrhythmia, IE, heart failure, stroke, pacemaker.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Reviews in Cardiovascular Medicine 23(8):261 (July 2022)

- Design: Narrative review

## 2. Key Contributions
- Practical CVD-specific dental management protocols
- Drug interaction table (antihypertensives × NSAIDs, vasoconstrictors)
- Post-MI timing guidelines updated to 30-day window
- BP threshold table for dental procedure deferral

## 3. Disease-Specific Key Points

### Hypertension
- BP ≥180/120: Hypertensive Crisis — refer immediately, no dental treatment
- BP ≥140/90: Consult physician; delay elective care
- Drug interactions: NSAIDs ↓ antihypertensive effect; epinephrine → transient BP rise
- White coat effect: 15–30% of patients; verify with out-of-office readings

### Ischemic Heart Disease / Angina / MI
- No elective dental care within 30 days of MI (updated from prior 6-month guideline)
- Emergency care post-MI: hospital setting + physician consult only
- Stable angina: elective care OK; short appointments; avoid early morning/late afternoon
- Keep nitroglycerin available; sublingual on demand
- Maintain profound local anesthesia to minimize anxiety/pain → angina triggers

### Arrhythmia / Pacemaker
- Electrical dental devices (ultrasonic scalers, apex locators) may interfere with older pacemakers
- Consult cardiologist; keep appointment short; have defibrillator available

### Infective Endocarditis (IE)
- AHA 2007 prophylaxis protocol: only for high-risk conditions (prosthetic valve, prior IE, congenital cyanotic HD)
- Amoxicillin 2g PO 30–60 min before procedure

### Anticoagulants (CVD patients)
- Do not routinely stop anticoagulants for simple extractions
- Use local hemostatic measures (tranexamic acid, surgical, gelfoam)

## 4. Drug Interaction Summary
| Dental Drug | CVD Interaction |
|---|---|
| NSAIDs | ↓ antihypertensive effect; ↑ bleeding risk on anticoagulants |
| Epinephrine (1:100,000) | Transient BP ↑; safe in controlled hypertension (limit 2 carpules) |
| Amide local anesthetics | ↓ metabolism with some antihypertensives |
| Macrolide antibiotics | Prolonged QT with some cardiac drugs |

## 5. Limitations
- Narrative review (no systematic search, no quality grading)
- Guideline references mixed across years (AHA, ESC/ESH, ACC/AHA)
