## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 3 RCTs (n=259) — single-cone bioceramic obturation showed numerically higher periapical success than conventional techniques at 6/12/18 mo (88.7/87.1/92.0% vs 76.4/76.4/90.7%) but no statistically significant difference (OR 1.12–2.09, all p>0.05); GRADE certainty low.

## 한줄요약

RCT 3편(259명) SR+MA — 단일콘 bioceramic 충전은 6/12/18개월 치근단 성공률이 전통기법보다 수치상 높았으나(88.7/87.1/92.0% vs 76.4/76.4/90.7%) 통계적 유의차 없음(OR 1.12–2.09, 모두 p>0.05); GRADE 근거수준 낮음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (RCTs only), PRISMA 2020, PROSPERO-registered
- **Journal**: Journal of Oral Biology and Craniofacial Research 2025;15(6):1422–1432
- **DOI**: [10.1016/j.jobcr.2025.08.031](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jobcr.2025.08.031) · PMID 40948576 · PMC12424407
- **Group**: Ajman University, UAE (Elmsmari) + Nair Hospital Dental College, India (Pawar)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA restricted to **RCTs** comparing single-cone bioceramic obturation vs conventional obturation on **clinical + radiographic healing** endpoints (not lab surrogates).
- Pooled periapical success consistently favors bioceramic at every time point but **never reaches significance**; gap narrows to near-equivalence by 18 mo.
- Quantifies a genuine evidence gap: only **3 eligible RCTs** exist (one at high risk of bias), with short follow-up and no patient-reported outcomes.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Databases: PubMed, ScienceDirect, Cochrane to 31 Mar 2024; 4,812 records after dedup → 18 full texts → **3 RCTs** (Coşar, Kim, Bardini).
- Inclusion: permanent teeth, single-cone bioceramic vs conventional, ≥6-mo follow-up, ≥10/group, clinical + radiographic outcome.
- RoB 2.0: Coşar low; Kim some concerns (randomization); Bardini high (protocol deviation, unclear concealment; used BioRoot RCS).
- Random-effects model; OR + 95% CI at 6/12/18 mo; I² heterogeneity; Egger's test; GRADE.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Follow-up | Bioceramic | Control | OR (95% CI) | p | I² |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 6 mo | 88.7% | 76.4% | 2.09 (0.76–5.87) | 0.154 | 63.3% |
| 12 mo | 87.1% | 76.4% | 1.62 (0.50–5.31) | 0.424 | 57.5% |
| 18 mo | 92.0% | 90.7% | 1.12 (0.38–3.25) | 0.845 | 0% |

- Publication bias absent (Egger intercept −0.619, p=0.84).
- **GRADE: LOW** (risk of bias, inconsistency, imprecision).
- Concept framing: bioceramic→dentin HA bonding (secondary monoblock, Tay & Pashley); single-cone reduces technique sensitivity vs lateral condensation / warm vertical compaction.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 3 RCTs → no subgroup/meta-regression; cannot resolve heterogeneity drivers.
- Follow-up 6–18 mo is short-to-mid; ≥24 mo needed for periapical bone healing.
- Single-cone–only focus excludes WVC/carrier-based comparators (external validity).
- No PROs (postop pain, satisfaction) despite ESE S3 emphasis. Calls for multicentre long-term RCTs.

## 7. Glossary

- **Single-cone (one-cone) technique**: obturation with one master gutta-percha cone matched to apical preparation + sealer-dominant fill; relies on sealer properties rather than compaction.
- **Bioceramic / hydraulic CaSi sealer**: calcium-silicate sealer, hydrophilic, sets in moisture, releases Ca²⁺, forms hydroxyapatite, bonds to dentin.
- **Monoblock**: bonded sealer–core–dentin continuum (primary/secondary/tertiary) reducing interfacial gaps.
- **PAI**: Periapical Index, radiographic periapical-status score.
- **GRADE**: certainty-of-evidence rating framework.
