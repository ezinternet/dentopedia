## One-line Summary
SR+MA of 103 articles (lab + RCT) showing bulk-fill composites have less shrinkage, less polymerization stress, and less cusp deflection than conventional composites, but lower microhardness at ≤2 mm; clinical performance equivalent in RCTs over 1–10 years.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Systematic Review and Meta-analysis
- **Published**: October 2019 in Dental Materials
- **Search databases**: PubMed and Scopus
- **Statistical method**: Peto method via RevMan software
- **Studies included**: 103 articles (laboratory studies + RCTs)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive dual synthesis: both in vitro physicochemical properties AND clinical RCT performance in a single SR+MA
- Identified key trade-off: bulk-fill composites reduce stress and shrinkage (advantageous in vitro) but show lower microhardness at thin layers (≤2 mm, potential clinical concern)
- Demonstrated that flowable bulk-fill composites achieve greater DC than conventional composites at depths >2 mm — clinically relevant for deep cavity restorations
- Clinical equivalence confirmed over 1–10 year follow-up in RCTs

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Databases**: PubMed and Scopus
- **Study types included**: Laboratory studies and RCTs comparing bulk-fill vs. conventional composites
- **Outcomes**: Polymerization shrinkage, polymerization stress, cusp deflection, marginal quality, degree of conversion, microhardness, flexural strength, fracture strength, clinical performance
- **Statistical method**: Peto method for meta-analysis (appropriate for rare events / binary outcomes)
- **Software**: RevMan

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Polymerization shrinkage**: Bulk-fill < conventional ✓ (regular viscosity showed similar shrinkage — see Note below)
- **Polymerization stress**: Bulk-fill < conventional ✓
- **Cusp deflection**: Bulk-fill < conventional ✓
- **Microhardness**: Bulk-fill < conventional at ≤2 mm thickness ✗ (exception)
- **Marginal quality**: Similar between bulk-fill and conventional ≈
- **Flexural strength**: Similar ≈
- **Fracture strength**: Similar ≈
- **Degree of conversion**: Flowable bulk-fill ≈ conventional at ≤2 mm; flowable bulk-fill > conventional at >2 mm depth ✓
- **Clinical performance (RCTs, 1–10 yr)**: Similar between bulk-fill and conventional ≈
- Note: Graphical abstract contains a corrected statement — regular-viscosity bulk-fill showed similar shrinkage (text) vs. higher shrinkage (abstract), indicating an internal discrepancy in the published paper

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Internal abstract/text discrepancy regarding regular-viscosity bulk-fill shrinkage (abstract states higher; body states similar)
- Access-limited full text (ScienceDirect abstract-only extraction); some details of specific material comparisons are not available from abstract
- Heterogeneity among laboratory study designs may affect pooled estimates
- Microhardness deficiency at shallow depths warrants investigation into optimal placement depth and capping strategies

## 6. Related Work
- Zailai et al. 2025 SR+MA: updated clinical RCT analysis through November 2025 with same overall conclusion
- Parra-Gatica et al. 2023: detailed in vitro SR focused specifically on flowable BF-RBCs
- Meereis et al. 2018: composition strategies for shrinkage stress reduction
- Chaple-Gil et al. 2026: NMA for Class II restorations

## 7. Glossary
- **Cusp deflection**: Outward bending of cusp walls caused by polymerization shrinkage stress — indicator of stress magnitude
- **Peto method**: Fixed-effect meta-analysis method suitable for rare binary outcomes and small study sizes
- **RevMan**: Cochrane Collaboration's software for meta-analysis (Review Manager)
- **Regular viscosity bulk-fill**: High-viscosity/sculptable bulk-fill composite; different from flowable/low-viscosity type
- **Degree of conversion (DC)**: Extent of C=C bond conversion; higher = better cure; affected by depth and light transmission
