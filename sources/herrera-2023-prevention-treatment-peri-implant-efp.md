## One-line Summary
EFP S3 clinical practice guideline for prevention and treatment of peri-implant mucositis and peri-implantitis; 13 commissioned SRs; structured recommendations covering pre-implant to supportive care.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Periodontology 2023; 50(Suppl. 26): 4–76
- **Study type**: Clinical practice guideline (S3 level, EFP)
- **DOI**: 10.1111/jcpe.13823

## 2. Key Contributions
- First comprehensive EFP S3 CPG specifically for peri-implant disease prevention and treatment
- Covers entire implant lifecycle: planning → placement → loading → SPT → disease management
- 13 commissioned SRs synthesised
- Identifies risk factors for peri-implant mucositis and peri-implantitis

## 3. Methodology
- S3 level: AWMF methodology + GRADE evidence quality assessment
- Rigorous consensus process with leading experts and stakeholders (EFP workshop)
- Based on 2018 peri-implant disease classification (Berglundh et al. 2018)

## 4. Key Recommendations

### Prevention (pre-placement)
- Screen and treat periodontitis before implant placement
- Optimize modifiable risk factors (smoking cessation, glycaemic control)
- Ensure adequate keratinized mucosa; consider augmentation

### After placement/loading
- Structured supportive peri-implant care (SPIC) programme
- Regular BOP and PD monitoring; annual radiographs
- Oral hygiene instruction tailored to implant design

### Peri-implant mucositis treatment
- Mechanical debridement (professional + patient-performed) is sufficient
- Adjunctive antiseptics (CHX) may help in short term

### Peri-implantitis treatment
- Non-surgical debridement (mechanical ± antiseptics/antibiotics)
- Surgical access therapy for advanced cases
- Resective or regenerative approaches depending on defect morphology
- Implant surface decontamination essential

## 5. Risk Factors Identified
- History of periodontitis (strongest risk indicator for both PIDs)
- Poor plaque control; no regular SPIC
- Smoking; diabetes mellitus (less conclusive)
- Submucosal cement excess; inadequate keratinized mucosa
