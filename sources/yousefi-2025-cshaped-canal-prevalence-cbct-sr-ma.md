## Why Ingested

This 101-study SR+MA is the highest-evidence anchor for C-shaped canal prevalence in the wiki, pooling the kind of single-population cross-sectional data captured in [[endodontics/anatomy/song-2008-cross-sectional-morphology-and-minimum-canal]] and the sibling prevalence pages ([[endodontics/anatomy/abdalrahman-2022-cshaped-canal-second-molars-iraqi-cbct]], [[endodontics/anatomy/armenta-2024-cshaped-canal-radix-mandibular-molars-mexican-cbct]], [[endodontics/anatomy/brea-2021-cbct-cshaped-mandibular-premolars]]) into continent-, gender-, and tooth-type-stratified estimates. It supersedes the per-study scatter with a pooled mandibular-second-molar estimate of 17.3% and confirms the Asian + female predominance pattern.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review + meta-analysis of 101 CBCT studies (from 5548 screened): highest C-shaped canal prevalence in mandibular second molars (17.3%), lowest in maxillary first molars (0.8%); female predominance in mandibular second molars (23.6% vs 16.7%); highest in the Asian continent; right/left difference not significant (21.2% vs 23.1%).

## 1. Document Information

- Type: Systematic review + meta-analysis (PRISMA; PROSPERO CRD42024522710)
- Sponsor: Hamadan University of Medical Sciences, Iran (ethical code IR.UMSHA.REC.1403.517)
- Databases: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science (up to 2024)
- Included: 101 studies (of 5548 screened) — CBCT-based prevalence of C-shaped canals in premolars/molars
- Journal: BMC Oral Health (2025) 25:1657

## 2. Key Contributions

- Largest CBCT-based pooled estimate of C-shaped canal prevalence by tooth type, gender, geography, side.
- Updates and extends Martin et al. 2019 SR/MA — adds Fan-based morphological classification absent from the earlier review.
- Establishes mandibular second molar (17.3%) as the highest-prevalence site and maxillary first molar (0.8%) as the lowest.
- Quantifies the Asian-continent and female predominance effects.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PICO: patients 15–95y with intact maxillary/mandibular posterior teeth on high-quality CBCT; outcome = C-shaped canal prevalence.
- Inclusion: CBCT-diagnosed C-shaped prevalence studies, English, up to 2024.
- Exclusion: reviews/meta-analyses, in vitro studies, non-CBCT imaging (panoramic, MRI, micro-CT).
- Risk of bias: Newcastle-Ottawa scale (cross-sectional version).
- Heterogeneity: I² statistic; Comprehensive Meta-Analysis software; correlation coefficient with 95% CI as effect measure.
- Subgroups: tooth type, gender, left/right side, geographic region (continent), Fan C1–C5 type.
- Fan et al. classification used (C1 complete C, C2 comma/discontinuous, C3 2–3 separate canals, C4 round/oval near apex, C5 no visible lumen).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Highest prevalence: mandibular second molar 17.3%.
- Lowest prevalence: maxillary first molar 0.8%.
- Gender (mandibular second molar): female 23.6% vs male 16.7%.
- Geography: mandibular second molar C-shaped more common in Asian continent than other continents.
- Side (mandibular second molar): right 21.2% vs left 23.1% — not significantly different.
- Clinical implication: contralateral C-shaped canal likely if one side is found; CBCT recommended for preoperative morphology assessment.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- High heterogeneity across populations and CBCT protocols/voxel sizes.
- English-only inclusion; potential publication/language bias.
- CBCT resolution below micro-CT; subtle isthmuses underdetected.
- Variable classification reporting across primary studies limits some subgroup pooling.

## 6. Related Work

- Martin et al. 2019 (prior C-shaped canal prevalence SR/MA, no morphological classification).
- Cooke & Cox 1979 (first description); Fan et al. (C1–C5 classification).
- Single-population CBCT prevalence studies (e.g., Iraqi, Mexican, Venezuelan cohorts) feeding the pooled estimates.

## 7. Glossary

- C-shaped canal: root canal whose transverse section resembles the letter "C", arising from a defect in Hertwig's epithelial root sheath attachment.
- Fan classification (C1–C5): cross-sectional categorization of C-shaped canal shape.
- I² statistic: measure of between-study heterogeneity in meta-analysis.
- Newcastle-Ottawa scale: risk-of-bias tool for observational/cross-sectional studies.
- PROSPERO: international prospective register of systematic reviews.
