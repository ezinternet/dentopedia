## Why Ingested

PubMed 감시 인제스트(transcrestal sinus lift)로 발굴한 신규 RCT 재분석. 기존 [[sinus-lift/transcrestal/farina-2018-morbidity-transcrestal-lateral-sinus-floor-elevation]]가 전체 코호트의 morbidity를 비교했다면, 본 재분석은 동일 RCT를 **잔존골 높이(RBH) <4 mm vs ≥4 mm로 층화**해 술식 선택을 RBH 기준으로 구체화한다 — RBH≥4 mm에서는 tSFE가 chair time·비용·이환율 모두 우월, RBH<4 mm에서는 tSFE 초기 통증↑·lSFE 멍/비용↑로 trade-off가 역전된다. 방금 추가한 [[sinus-lift/transcrestal/fettouh-2025-graftless-lateral-transcrestal-sinus-rct]](RBH 4–6 mm graftless 비교)의 결정 축을 <4 mm 구간으로 확장한다. ## One-line Summary

Re-analysis of a parallel-arm RCT (ITT 29 tSFE vs 28 lSFE) stratified by residual bone height: both techniques reach median 100% implant-surface bone contact, but at RBH ≥4 mm tSFE wins on chair time, cost, and morbidity, whereas at RBH <4 mm tSFE causes more early pain while lSFE causes more bruising and higher cost.

## 한줄요약

평행-군 RCT 재분석(ITT tSFE 29 vs lSFE 28), 잔존골(RBH) 층화 — 양 술식 모두 임플란트면 골접촉 중앙값 100%, RBH≥4 mm에선 tSFE가 시술시간·비용·이환율 우월, RBH<4 mm에선 tSFE 초기 통증↑·lSFE 멍/비용↑로 trade-off.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Stratifies a tSFE-vs-lSFE RCT by RBH (<4 mm vs ≥4 mm), making technique selection RBH-conditional rather than averaged across all sites.
- Radiographic effectiveness (totCON%) is equivalent — median 100% in both arms, both RBH strata — so the decision pivots on morbidity/cost, not bone contact.
- RBH ≥4 mm: tSFE significantly fewer postoperative signs/symptoms, less chair time, lower cost → tSFE preferred.
- RBH <4 mm: tSFE significantly more day-0/1 pain (same analgesic dose) while lSFE has more bruising and higher cost → a genuine trade-off, with a practical recommendation to pre-plan analgesia if choosing tSFE.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Re-analysis of a parallel-arm RCT comparing tSFE and lSFE.
- ITT population: 29 tSFE, 28 lSFE.
- Stratification: RBH interval <4 mm vs ≥4 mm.
- Comparators within each stratum: chair time, surgery-related cost, morbidity (pain, signs/symptoms, bruising, analgesic dose), and radiographic totCON% (proportion of implant surface in contact with radiopaque area).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- totCON%: median 100% irrespective of RBH and technique.
- RBH <4 mm: tSFE pain significantly higher at days 0–1 (no difference in analgesic dose); lSFE significantly more bruising and greater cost.
- RBH ≥4 mm: tSFE significantly fewer postoperative signs/symptoms, less chair time, lower cost.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Secondary/re-analysis of a prior RCT — subgroup-by-RBH comparisons are post hoc and underpowered relative to the original primary endpoint.
- Abstract-only ingest — original trial n per stratum, effect sizes/CIs, and follow-up duration not extractable here.
- Cost figures are setting-specific (Italian academic practice) and may not transfer to other systems.
- Outcomes are early morbidity/cost and short-term radiographic contact — does not address long-term survival or MBL.

## 7. Glossary

- **tSFE / lSFE**: transcrestal vs lateral sinus floor elevation.
- **RBH (residual bone height)**: vertical bone below the sinus floor before augmentation; the principal site-selection variable.
- **totCON%**: proportion of the implant surface in direct contact with the radiopaque (bone/graft) area on radiographs.
- **ITT (intention-to-treat)**: analysis including all randomised patients as allocated.
