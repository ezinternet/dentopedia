## Why Ingested

기존 [[wiki/drug/sperotto-2024-antibiotic-prophylaxis-infective-endocarditis-sr-ma]]는 성인 위주 SR+MA(고위험군 pooled RR 0.41)였으나, 선천성 심장질환(CHD)을 가진 **소아** 환자에서 치과 시술 전 예방적 항생제가 IE를 예방하는지에 대한 별도 SR이 없었음. 본 SR(Loper 2025)은 소아 CHD에 특화된 최초 SR로, GRADE 근거가 매우 낮아 권고 근거가 취약함을 공식 확인한다는 점에서 보완적 근거로 활용.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review of 3 case-control studies (n up to 24,729 CHD children) found very low GRADE evidence that antibiotic prophylaxis prevents infective endocarditis (OR 3.63, ns) or bacteremia (OR 0.24, p<0.05) after dental procedures in pediatric congenital heart disease.

## 한줄요약

선천성 심장질환(Congenital Heart Disease, CHD) 소아 환자 대상 체계적 문헌고찰(3편 증례-대조연구) — 치과 시술 전 예방적 항생제(Antibiotic Prophylaxis, AP)가 감염성 심내막염(Infective Endocarditis, IE) 예방에 효과적이라는 근거 매우 낮음(GRADE ⊕◯◯◯); 균혈증(bacteremia) 감소에는 유효(OR 0.24, p<0.05)하나 역시 매우 낮은 근거.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First systematic review focused exclusively on **pediatric** patients with congenital heart disease (CHD) and antibiotic prophylaxis (AP) for dental procedures — prior reviews focused on adults or included children only as subgroups.
2. Identified only 3 eligible case-control studies from 1981 screened records — highlighting a critical evidence gap in this pediatric population.
3. Found **very low GRADE certainty** for both primary outcome (IE) and secondary outcome (bacteremia) — underscoring that current guideline recommendations rest on expert consensus and pathophysiological rationale rather than robust empirical evidence.
4. Bacteremia meta-analysis (2 studies): AP significantly reduces bacteremia post-extraction (pooled OR 0.24, 95% CI 0.14–0.42, p<0.05; I²=0%).
5. IE outcome (1 study): AP was not statistically associated with reduced IE risk in CHD children — OR for IE with AP was 3.63 (95% CI 0.98–13.47, p=0.05, ns), though likely confounded.
6. No RCTs found; ethical barriers make RCTs in high-risk pediatric CHD patients practically unfeasible.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Systematic review with attempted meta-analysis (PRISMA-compliant, PROSPERO-registered)
- **Databases**: Web of Science, Medline (via Ovid), Embase Classic, Scopus, CINAHL, PubMed — no date or language restrictions, search up to April 2024
- **Study types eligible**: RCTs, case-control studies, cohort studies
- **Population**: Pediatric patients with CHD (low, moderate, or high risk of IE) undergoing any dental procedure
- **Intervention/Exposure**: Antibiotic prophylaxis (any agent, any dose) before dental procedure
- **Control**: No AP (healthy patients or CHD patients without AP)
- **Primary outcome**: Incidence of IE
- **Secondary outcome**: Incidence of bacteremia
- **Risk of bias tool**: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS) for case-control studies (max 10 stars)
- **Evidence certainty**: GRADE approach using GRADEpro GDT
- **Statistical analysis**: Fixed effects Mantel-Haenszel (≤3 studies); Stata 18.0
- **Inter-rater agreement**: Cohen's kappa = 0.65 (moderate) at full-text reading stage

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Included Studies (n=3)

| Study | Country | n | Procedure | Antibiotic | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Elliott & Dunbar | Ireland | 217 | Extractions | Penicillin (various) | Bacteremia |
| Coulter et al. | Ireland | 58 | Extractions | Penicillin/amoxicillin/erythromycin | Bacteremia |
| Sun et al. | Taiwan | 24,729 CHD children (237 IE cases, 4,725 controls) | Extractions, endo, perio, oral surgery | Not specified | IE |

### Primary Outcome — IE (Sun et al. only)

- OR for IE with AP vs no dental procedure: **3.63** (95% CI: 0.98–13.47; p=0.05) — not statistically significant
- OR for IE without AP vs no dental procedure: 0.35 (95% CI: 0.11–1.17; p=0.088)
- Meta-analysis not possible (only 1 study)
- GRADE: **Very low** (⊕◯◯◯)

### Secondary Outcome — Bacteremia (2 studies)

- Pooled OR for bacteremia with AP vs no AP: **0.24** (95% CI: 0.14–0.42; p<0.05)
- Heterogeneity: I² = 0.00% (p=0.56) — homogeneous
- Elliott & Dunbar: 11% positive cultures in cardiac AP group vs 36% in healthy no-AP controls
- Coulter et al.: 35% positive cultures in AP group vs 63% in no-AP controls
- GRADE: **Very low** (⊕◯◯◯)

### NOS Quality Scores

- Elliott & Dunbar: 6/9 stars
- Coulter et al.: 5/9 stars
- Sun et al.: 8/9 stars

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Critical limitation**: Only 3 case-control studies eligible; no RCTs found — practically and ethically unfeasible to conduct RCTs withholding AP from high-risk CHD children
- Outdated antibiotic regimens in the two bacteremia studies (penicillin, erythromycin) — external validity for current amoxicillin-based protocols uncertain
- Controls in bacteremia studies were healthy non-cardiac patients (for ethical reasons) — not ideal comparators; could not control for underlying cardiac disease as confounders
- Subgroup analysis by antibiotic type/dosage, cardiac risk level, or follow-up duration was not possible
- Publication bias assessment (funnel plots) impossible with 3 studies
- Bacteremia not validated as surrogate marker for IE
- **Future research needed**: Large-scale RCTs or registry-based cohort studies; clinical validation of bacteremia as IE risk marker; cost-effectiveness analyses for pediatric CHD populations

## 6. Related Work

- Sperotto et al. 2024 (JAMA Cardiology) — SR+MA in adults: pooled RR 0.41 (95% CI: 0.29–0.57) for IE with AP in high-risk cardiac patients; protective but focused on adult populations
- European Society of Cardiology, AHA, NICE, Japanese Circulation Society guidelines all reviewed in companion SR: 3/4 recommend AP before dental procedures for high-risk cardiac conditions
- NICE 2008 guidelines (UK): dropped AP recommendation; 2015 modification re-added "consider" language; associated with ~35 additional IE cases/year in high-risk patients in England
- AHA recommendations for AP before dental procedures: established 1955, major revisions 1997, 2007, 2021 — currently recommends AP for specific high-risk cardiac conditions (prosthetic valves, prior IE, certain CHD lesions, cardiac transplant recipients)
- AP shown cost-effective in high-risk IE patients via decision-analytical models

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| AP (Antibiotic Prophylaxis, 예방적 항생제) | Antibiotics given before a procedure to prevent infection |
| IE (Infective Endocarditis, 감염성 심내막염) | Infection of the endocardium (heart's inner lining), often involving valves; high mortality |
| CHD (Congenital Heart Disease, 선천성 심장질환) | Structural heart defect present from birth |
| Bacteremia (균혈증) | Transient presence of viable bacteria in bloodstream; peaks 1.5–5 min post-dental procedure |
| GRADE (Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development, and Evaluation) | Framework for rating certainty of evidence (very low/low/moderate/high) |
| NOS (Newcastle-Ottawa Scale) | Risk of bias tool for observational studies (case-control, cohort); max 9–10 stars |
| AHA (American Heart Association) | US society recommending AP protocols since 1955 |
| NICE (National Institute for Health and Care Excellence) | UK body that controversially dropped AP recommendation in 2008 (modified 2015) |
| OR (Odds Ratio, 교차비) | Ratio of odds of outcome in exposed vs unexposed group |
| PECO | Population, Exposure, Control, Outcome — framework for observational review questions |
