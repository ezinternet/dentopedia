## One-line Summary
Critical narrative review challenges 9 "biomimetic restorative dentistry" (BRD) protocols (caries dyes, alumina air abrasion, enamel beveling, CHX MMP inactivation, "elected" gold-standard adhesives, IDS, deep margin elevation, decoupling, fiber inserts) — finding most lack RCT/SR-level evidence of superiority over standard adhesive techniques.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: J Appl Oral Sci 2024;32:e20240271
- **DOI**: 10.1590/1678-7757-2024-0271
- **Lead institutions**: Universidade Estadual de Ponta Grossa (Brazil), University of Iowa, Universidad de los Andes (Chile)
- **Type**: Narrative review with RCT/SR literature search
- **Databases**: PubMed, Embase, Cochrane Library CENTRAL

## 2. Key Contributions
- Rare evidence-skeptical voice in BRD literature dominated by case reports and laboratory studies
- Names the leading BRD proponents (David Alleman, Pascal Magne, Bio-Emulation Group™) and the Academy of Biomimetic Dentistry
- Identifies cognitive biases (novelty bias, authority bias) driving adoption of unvalidated protocols
- Provides RCT/SR-anchored verdict for each of 9 BRD protocols — useful as a counterweight reference for clinical decision-making

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: Critical narrative review
- **Strategy**: RCT and SR search on PubMed/Embase/Cochrane CENTRAL for each BRD protocol
- **Structure**: Origins of BRD → cognitive biases → protocol-by-protocol evidence assessment (9 protocols)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks — 9 BRD protocols evaluated

| # | Protocol | Reis et al. verdict |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Caries-detector dyes | Excessively removes carious dentin |
| 2 | Aluminum oxide air abrasion | Contributes to overtreatment; potential occupational health risk |
| 3 | Enamel beveling (posterior) | Technically difficult; no evidence of superiority over butt-joint |
| 4 | Chlorhexidine MMP inactivation | No clinical evidence of improved restoration longevity |
| 5 | "Elected" gold-standard adhesives | Perform no better than other good systems |
| 6 | Immediate dentin sealing (IDS) + resin coating | Similar post-op sensitivity and longevity to DDS in clinical data |
| 7 | Deep margin elevation | Viable alternative for subgingival margins (the only one largely supported) |
| 8 | "Decoupling" with time | Lacks scientific evidence |
| 9 | Fiber inserts on pulpal floor / axial wall | No benefits over current alternatives |

- Calls for rigorous RCTs focused on clinically meaningful outcomes (fracture, debonding, post-op sensitivity, esthetic quality, secondary caries, patient satisfaction)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative review — selection bias possible despite RCT/SR focus
- Some BRD protocols may show benefit in specific scenarios not captured in current RCTs
- Authors' position is intentionally polemical/corrective
- Future need: high-quality RCTs designed and conducted by BRD-neutral or skeptic groups
- Author conflict of interest: Reis/Loguercio group has long-standing position critical of biomimetic claims

## 6. Related Work
- Directly critiques Magne/Belser biomimetic textbook (2002, 2022)
- Contradicts Samartzi 2021 / Hardan 2022 IDS narrative + in vitro support — but argues clinical data does not show DDS inferiority
- Aligns with broader EBD critiques of laboratory-derived protocol claims
- Worth pairing with Hardan 2021 (universal adhesive bond strategies) for technique-level evaluation

## 7. Glossary
- **BRD (Biomimetic Restorative Dentistry, 생체모방 수복치과학)**: Movement claiming to mimic natural tooth biomechanics; led by Alleman, Magne
- **MMP inhibition with chlorhexidine**: Use of 2% CHX rinse to inactivate dentin-bound metalloproteinases
- **Deep margin elevation (DME)**: Composite buildup to relocate subgingival margin coronally before indirect restoration
- **Decoupling**: BRD concept that intermediate composite layer absorbs polymerization stress
- **Butt-joint preparation**: 90° cavosurface margin (no bevel)
- **Bio-Emulation Group™**: International think tank of dentists/researchers; trademarked term
