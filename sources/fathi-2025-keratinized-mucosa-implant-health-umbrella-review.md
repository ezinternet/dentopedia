## Why Ingested

각화점막 폭경(Keratinized Mucosa Width, KMW)이 임플란트 주위염 위험인자인지에 대한 문헌은 수십 년간 상충된 결과를 보여왔다. 이 우산 리뷰는 기존 10개 SR/MA를 집대성하여 KMW ≥2 mm 기준의 임상적 근거를 종합한다는 점에서, 기존 개별 SR 논문인 [[implants/peri-implantitis/roccuzzo-2025-keratinized-mucosa-peri-implant-20year-mandible]]에서 제시된 20년 장기 단일기관 관찰 결과를 보강하고 더 넓은 증거 기반으로 확장한다.

## One-line Summary

Umbrella review of 10 SRs/MAs (7,139 participants) finds that keratinized mucosa width ≥2 mm is consistently associated with reduced peri-implant inflammation, plaque accumulation, mucosal recession, and peri-implantitis risk, though evidence on bleeding and long-term survival remains inconclusive.

## 한줄요약

10개 SR/MA(7,139명) 우산 리뷰: 각화점막 폭경 ≥2 mm이 임플란트 주위 염증·치태·점막퇴축·주위염 위험 감소와 일관되게 연관되나, 출혈 및 장기 생존율 근거는 아직 불충분하다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First umbrella review synthesizing all available SR/MA evidence on the relationship between keratinized mucosa (KM) and dental implant health outcomes.
2. Synthesizes 10 SR/MA studies encompassing 7,139 participants and 132 primary studies (RCTs and non-RCTs).
3. Provides a structured AMSTAR 2 quality assessment of all included reviews (kappa = 0.9).
4. Consolidates evidence across multiple peri-implant outcomes: plaque index, probing depth, mucosal recession, peri-implantitis incidence, marginal bone loss (MBL), and gingival thickness.
5. Evaluates soft tissue augmentation methods (FGG, CTG, XCM) as interventions to increase KMW.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Design**: Umbrella review — a review of existing systematic reviews and meta-analyses.

**PICO question**: "Does the presence of peri-implant KMW contribute to peri-implant health and stability in adult human subjects?"
- Population: Adults with dental implants
- Intervention: KMW < 2 mm at implant placement
- Comparison: KMW ≥ 2 mm at implant placement
- Outcomes: Probing depth (PD), soft-tissue recession, mean gingival index (mGI), peri-implantitis incidence, marginal bone loss (MBL)

**Study selection**: From 47 identified articles → 26 after deduplication → 21 meeting eligibility → 10 selected for full data extraction. Eligibility required clearly comparative analyses of KM significance on implant health, published in English.

**Quality assessment**: AMSTAR 2 tool (16 questions). Low ROB: ≥8 affirmative; Moderate: 4–7; High: <3.
Inter-rater reliability: Kappa = 1.0 (selection) and 0.9 (quality assessment). Third investigator used for unresolved conflicts.

**Included reviews** (chronological):
| Author | Year | N participants | Study types | Key intervention |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Thoma et al | 2014 | – | 10 studies | Soft tissue grafting |
| Moraschini et al | 2017 | – | 4 RCTs | KMW <2 vs ≥2 mm |
| Longoni et al | 2019 | 1,031 | 15 RCTs | KMW <2 vs ≥2 mm |
| Moraschini et al | 2020 | 304 | 11 studies | XCM vs CTG |
| Montero et al | 2022 | – | 22 studies | KT <2 mm |
| Ravidà et al | 2022 | 220 | 9 studies | KMW <2 vs ≥2 mm |

**High heterogeneity** noted across studies; meta-analysis was not performed; narrative synthesis used.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Peri-implantitis risk**:
- Mahardawi et al (2023): KM deficiency significantly increases peri-implantitis occurrence (OR = 2.78)
- Montero et al (2022): KT prevalence ranged 20.5%–53% at 0 mm vs 5.1%–8% at ≥2 mm; significant difference favoring KT ≥2 mm
- All 10 reviews: consistently found positive association between KMW ≥2 mm and peri-implant health

**Plaque and inflammation**:
- Ravidà et al (2022): KMW ≥2 mm showed significantly reduced mean plaque index (P = .002)
- Longoni et al (2019): Adequate KT linked to reduced peri-implant inflammation (mGI/GI); no significant difference for plaque accumulation or bleeding but positive trend favoring adequate KT

**Marginal bone loss**: Narrower KT associated with greater MBL per multiple included reviews

**Mucosal recession**: Reduced KMW consistently linked to increased mucosal recession

**Soft tissue augmentation outcomes**:
- FGG > XCM for KM width augmentation and bleeding indices
- CTG > XCM for gingival thickness (GT) increase (P = .001)
- XCM comparable to CTG for KMW increase and percentage of attached soft tissue (PAS)
- XCM: viable alternative for esthetically demanding sites or when reduced surgical time is priority

**KMW threshold**: ≥2 mm established as clinically critical for preventing bone loss, soft-tissue recession, and facilitating effective oral hygiene

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- High heterogeneity across included studies; meta-analysis of the umbrella review was not feasible
- Most primary studies are observational, cross-sectional, or retrospective — cannot establish causation
- Insufficient long-term data on implant survival and success rates related to KMW
- Evidence from 2017 World Workshop still not definitively resolved: direct link between KM absence and peri-implantitis onset remains unclear
- Authors call for long-term interventional studies with proper sample sizes and extended follow-up periods

## 6. Related Work

- Mahardawi et al 2023: OR = 2.78 for peri-implantitis with KM deficiency (key quantitative anchor)
- Ravidà et al 2022: KMW as minor but significant risk factor; plaque index significantly better with ≥2 mm
- Ramanauskaite et al 2022: Autogenous grafts (FGG) superior to substitutes for KM augmentation
- Longoni et al 2019: Adequate KT linked to less peri-implant inflammation qualitatively
- Moraschini et al 2017: Earliest SR showing 100% positive association between KMW ≥2 mm and peri-implant health across all included SRs
- 2017 World Workshop on Periodontics: Acknowledged inadequate evidence for KMW–peri-implantitis link

## 7. Glossary

- **KM / KT**: Keratinized mucosa / keratinized tissue — the firm, bound oral mucosa surrounding implants and teeth
- **KMW**: Keratinized mucosa width — measured in mm; ≥2 mm considered adequate
- **Umbrella review**: A systematic review of existing systematic reviews and meta-analyses
- **FGG**: Free gingival graft — autogenous donor tissue from palate, most effective for KM width augmentation
- **CTG**: Connective tissue graft — autogenous subepithelial graft, superior for gingival thickness increase
- **XCM**: Xenogeneic collagen matrix — acellular porcine/bovine-derived substitute; comparable to CTG for KMW and PAS but inferior for GT
- **AMSTAR 2**: A Measurement Tool to Assess Systematic Reviews (16-item quality appraisal tool)
- **MBL**: Marginal bone loss — radiographically measured bone level change at implant
- **PD**: Probing depth — measured in mm at peri-implant sulcus
- **mGI / GI**: Mean gingival index / gingival index — clinical inflammation scoring
- **BOP**: Bleeding on probing — marker of peri-implant inflammation
- **OR**: Odds ratio — in Mahardawi et al (2023), OR = 2.78 for peri-implantitis with KM deficiency
- **PAS**: Percentage of attached soft tissue
