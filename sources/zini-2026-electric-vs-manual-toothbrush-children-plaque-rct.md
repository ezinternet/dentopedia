## Why Ingested

The electric-toothbrush evidence base we hold (e.g. [[wiki/periodontics/luo-2026-electric-toothbrush-technologies-plaque-gingivitis-nma]]) is largely adult-focused; this RCT adds direct pediatric (6–10 yo) head-to-head data on an advanced oscillating-rotating (OR) electric toothbrush versus a manual brush, reinforcing the OR-power-superiority signal in a child population where manual-brushing dexterity is still developing.

## One-line Summary

4-week examiner-blind parallel-group RCT (n=60, 6–10 yo children, Israel): an advanced oscillating-rotating electric toothbrush (Oral-B iO2) gave 51% greater whole-mouth plaque reduction than a manual toothbrush (TQHPI change 0.67 vs 0.44; p=0.003), with 42–64% greater reductions across all subregions (lingual, buccal, approximal, molar; p≤0.021); no adverse events.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First published clinical evaluation of the Oral-B iO2 advanced oscillating-rotating (OR) electric toothbrush in a paediatric population (6–10 years old).
- Demonstrates statistically significant whole-mouth plaque-reduction superiority (51% greater) for the OR electric toothbrush vs a manual toothbrush at 4 weeks, measured via TQHPI.
- Shows the benefit extends consistently across all measured subregions — lingual (+64.3%), approximal (+52.4%), whole mouth (+51.0%), molar (+42.8%), buccal (+41.8%) — including hard-to-reach interproximal and molar surfaces where 95% of deciduous caries occur.
- Both toothbrushes achieved significant within-group plaque reductions from baseline (p<0.001), confirming that supervised twice-daily brushing with either device improves plaque control; the electric toothbrush provides additive benefit.
- No adverse events observed or reported in either group.
- Contextualises the finding within the trajectory literature: high plaque at age 5 predicts four times greater tooth loss by early 30s (Broadbent et al.), making childhood plaque control a long-horizon investment.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

### Study Design
- 4-week, single-centre, randomised, controlled, two-treatment, parallel-group, examiner-blind clinical study.
- Conducted January–February 2025 at Hadassah–Hebrew University Medical Center, Jerusalem.

### Participants
- Enrolled: 60 children (30/group); 58 completed (2 excluded from manual group due to antibiotic use between visits).
- Age: 6–10 years; mean (SD) 7.7 (±1.33) years; range 6–10.
- Sex: 57% female, 43% male (equal distribution across groups).
- Race: 98.3% Caucasian, 1.7% Black.
- Inclusion criteria: ages 6–10; good general health; minimum 16 natural teeth with scorable facial/lingual surfaces; evidence of afternoon dental plaque.
- Exclusion criteria: systemic disease interfering with examination; urgent dental treatment needs; fixed orthodontic appliances; antibiotics within 2 weeks of study initiation; dental prophylaxis within 1 month.
- Groups were balanced for demographics (p≥0.848) and all baseline TQHPI scores (whole mouth, lingual, buccal, approximal, molar; all p≥0.252).

### Interventions
- **Test (Electric)**: Oral-B iO2 oscillating-rotating electric toothbrush with Oral-B iO Gentle Care brush head (OP030/OR017). Note: the toothbrush used was solid white (no compliance-enhancing features of the marketed Kids' version — no colourful handle, character, or 2-min music timer) to isolate mechanical plaque-removal efficacy. Manufacturer instructions: twice-daily brushing for 2 min per session.
- **Control (Manual)**: Paro Junior Soft manual toothbrush. Instruction: brush in customary manner twice daily.
- **Common**: Both groups used Colgate Junior fluoridated toothpaste (sodium monofluorophosphate, 1450 ppm F). Initial supervised brushing without mirror at the site.

### Stratification and Randomisation
- Stratified by baseline plaque score (≤4.0 vs >4.0) and gender using block size of 4.
- Randomised by encoded randomisation/balance program provided by the study sponsor.
- Examiner was blinded to group assignment throughout.

### Plaque Measurement (TQHPI)
- Turesky Modified Quigley-Hein Plaque Index (TQHPI) — scored at 6 sites per tooth (distobuccal, midbuccal, mesiobuccal, distolingual, midlingual, mesiolingual) on all deciduous and permanent teeth (excluding surfaces with restorations/malformations).
- Scores range 0 (no plaque) to 5 (plaque covering ≥2/3 of crown side).
- Red disclosing solution (Mira-2-Ton) applied with cotton swabs at both visits.
- Pre-brushing plaque examinations conducted in the afternoon; participants refrained from all oral hygiene after morning brushing and from eating/drinking (except small sips of water up to 45 min before visit) for 3 h prior.
- Same calibrated examiner at both time points.

### Primary Outcome and Statistical Analysis
- **Primary variable**: 4-week whole-mouth average pre-brushing TQHPI change from baseline (Baseline minus Week 4).
- **Power**: minimum 28 completers/group for ≥80% power to detect a 0.356 treatment difference (SD=0.467), two-sided α=0.05.
- **Main analysis**: ANCOVA with baseline TQHPI as covariate; interaction between covariate and treatment also evaluated.
- **Within-group significance**: paired difference t-test (vs zero).
- **Subregions**: lingual, buccal, approximal, molar (1st and 2nd permanent molars) analysed separately within and across treatments.
- **Safety**: absence of observed or self-reported irreversible adverse events.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Industry funding and conflicts**: Funded by Procter & Gamble; three authors are P&G employees; four academic authors received P&G grants. Introduces potential industry bias, though the study design (examiner-blind, ANCOVA, prospective registration) mitigates this.
- **No gingivitis assessment**: Gingival outcomes were not measured; plaque is a surrogate endpoint. Adult OR-brush data consistently show gingivitis benefits when plaque is reduced, and a traditional OR model showed gingivitis benefits in a 4-week paediatric trial, but direct evidence for this specific model is absent.
- **Short 4-week horizon**: No data on durability of benefit, long-term adherence, or novelty effects after extended use.
- **Single devices**: One OR electric model vs one manual brush — not generalisable to all powered or manual brush designs.
- **Compliance-enhancing features absent**: The tested toothbrush (solid white) lacked the colourful handle, character, and music timer of the marketed Oral-B iO Kids device; actual compliance and outcomes in real-world use may differ.
- **Supervised use**: Participants brushed under site staff supervision without a mirror; unsupervised home brushing may yield different results.
- **Small sample, single-centre**: n=60 from a single Israeli centre (predominantly Caucasian); generalisability is uncertain.
- **No comparison against other electric technologies**: Sonic vs OR comparison in children remains unstudied at this specificity.
- **Future work suggested**: Gingivitis outcomes; qualitative acceptability/behaviour-change studies; comparison with other electric brush technologies (traditional OR, sonic); real-world compliance studies with the marketed Kids' device features.

## 7. Glossary

- **OR (oscillating-rotating)**: Powered toothbrush mechanism with a round brush head that rotates alternately clockwise and counterclockwise, characteristic of the Oral-B platform. Distinct from sonic (high-frequency lateral vibration) and ultrasonic technologies.
- **TQHPI (Turesky Modified Quigley-Hein Plaque Index)**: A widely validated plaque-scoring index grading plaque extent on facial and lingual surfaces of each tooth on a 0–5 scale (0=no plaque; 5=plaque covering ≥2/3 of crown side).
- **ANCOVA (analysis of covariance)**: Statistical method comparing group outcome means while adjusting for a baseline covariate (here, pre-brushing TQHPI score), increasing precision and controlling for chance baseline imbalances.
- **Examiner-blind**: The clinician scoring plaque is unaware of the participant's group assignment, reducing detection/assessment bias.
- **Disclosing solution (Mira-2-Ton)**: Red dye that stains dental plaque, enabling visual scoring of plaque distribution before the examiner records TQHPI scores.
- **Paro Junior Soft**: A commercially available soft-bristle manual toothbrush marketed for children; used as the active control.
- **Oral-B iO2**: An advanced oscillating-rotating electric toothbrush in the Oral-B iO series, evaluated here in a version without the compliance-enhancing features (no character, no timer) to isolate mechanical efficacy.
