## Why Ingested

The electric-toothbrush evidence base we hold (e.g. [[wiki/periodontics/luo-2026-electric-toothbrush-technologies-plaque-gingivitis-nma]]) is largely adult-focused; this RCT adds direct pediatric (6–10 yo) head-to-head data on an advanced oscillating-rotating (OR) electric toothbrush versus a manual brush, reinforcing the OR-power-superiority signal in a child population where manual-brushing dexterity is still developing.

## One-line Summary

4-week examiner-blind parallel-group RCT (n=60, 6–10 yo children): an advanced oscillating-rotating electric toothbrush (Oral-B iO2) gave 51% greater whole-mouth plaque reduction than a manual toothbrush (0.67 vs 0.44; p=0.003), with 42–64% greater reductions across all subregions (p≤0.021). Abstract-only.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides direct pediatric (6–10 yo) head-to-head efficacy data for an advanced oscillating-rotating (OR) electric toothbrush vs a manual toothbrush, a population under-represented in the powered-vs-manual literature.
- Demonstrates statistically significant whole-mouth plaque-reduction superiority (51% greater) for the OR electric toothbrush at 4 weeks.
- Shows the benefit extends consistently across all measured subregions (lingual, buccal, approximal, molar), not just easy-access surfaces.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: 4-week randomised, controlled, two-treatment, parallel-group, examiner-blind clinical study.
- **Participants**: 60 children aged 6–10 years (30/group); mean (SD) age 7.7 (±1.33) years. 58 children completed the trial.
- **Test group**: Advanced OR electric toothbrush — Oral-B iO2 with Gentle Care brush head.
- **Control group**: Manual toothbrush — Paro Junior Soft.
- **Common protocol**: Both groups brushed twice daily with a fluoridated dentifrice.
- **Outcome measure**: Plaque assessed at baseline and 4 weeks using the Turesky modification of the Quigley-Hein Plaque Index.
- **Analysis**: ANCOVA.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Enrolment: 60 (30/group); 58 completed.
- Whole-mouth plaque reduction at week 4: test 0.67 vs control 0.44 — 51% greater for the OR electric toothbrush; p = 0.003.
- Subregion plaque reductions (lingual, buccal, approximal, molar): all statistically significantly greater for the test group (p ≤ 0.021), ranging from 42% to 64% greater than control.
- Conclusion: the advanced OR electric toothbrush produced significantly greater plaque reductions than a manual toothbrush in this pediatric population.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only; methodological detail (randomisation method, allocation concealment, dropout handling, exact subregion values, confidence intervals) not available.
- Short 4-week horizon — no data on durability of benefit, gingivitis outcomes, or long-term adherence/novelty effects.
- Single OR device (Oral-B iO2) vs a single manual brush; not generalisable to all powered or manual brush designs.
- Small sample (n=60) and single-centre; supervised-trial conditions may not reflect unsupervised home brushing in children.
- Plaque is a surrogate outcome; clinical endpoints (caries, gingival health) not assessed.

## 7. Glossary

- **OR (oscillating-rotating)**: Powered toothbrush mechanism with a round head that rotates back and forth, characteristic of the Oral-B platform.
- **Turesky modification of the Quigley-Hein Plaque Index**: A widely used plaque-scoring index that grades plaque extent on facial and lingual surfaces of each tooth (0–5 per surface).
- **ANCOVA (analysis of covariance)**: Statistical method comparing group outcomes while adjusting for a covariate (here, baseline plaque).
- **Examiner-blind**: The clinician scoring plaque is unaware of the participant's group assignment, reducing assessment bias.
