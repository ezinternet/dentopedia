## Why Ingested

즉시식립(immediate implant placement, IIP) 후 발치와의 개방형 창상(open wound) 관리법 비교가 위키에 부족하다. 기존 [[immediate-implant/zhou-2024-open-closed-flap-collagen-membrane-immediate-implant]]는 open vs closed flap을 collagen membrane으로 비교했고, [[immediate-implant/elgendi-2025-peri-implant-soft-tissue-conditioning]]는 CAD-CAM socket-sealing abutment로 연조직을 조절했다. 본 retrospective cohort는 자가 반응성 연조직 (Reactive Soft Tissue, RST) vs 흡수성 콜라겐 스펀지 (Absorbable Collagen Sponge, ACS)라는 제3의 개방창 sealing 선택지를 직접 대조해 이 결정 트리를 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective cohort (32 patients / 40 posterior immediate implants; 20 RST vs 20 ACS): autologous reactive soft tissue and absorbable collagen sponge gave comparable total soft-tissue healing scores, gingival recession, and hard-tissue (HBW, MBL) changes at ~6 months — ACS scored better tissue colour but worse fibrous repair.

## 한줄요약

후향적 코호트 (환자 32명 / 후방 즉시식립 임플란트 40개; RST 20 vs ACS 20): 자가 반응성 연조직 (Reactive Soft Tissue, RST)과 흡수성 콜라겐 스펀지 (Absorbable Collagen Sponge, ACS)는 약 6개월 시점에 총 연조직 치유 점수·치은퇴축·경조직(수평 골폭 HBW, 변연골 MBL) 변화가 서로 비슷했고, ACS가 조직 색조는 더 좋았으나 섬유성 회복은 더 나빴다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Directly compares two open-wound sealing strategies for **posterior** immediate implant placement — autologous **reactive soft tissue (RST)** versus **absorbable collagen sponge (ACS)** — a comparison the authors note had limited prior reporting.
2. Frames open (secondary-intention) wound healing as a legitimate alternative to the traditional ideal of flap-to-flap **primary** wound closure, which they state is often difficult to achieve over fresh extraction sockets.
3. Introduces a **novel soft-tissue healing scoring system** (combined with gingival recession measurement) to assess soft-tissue healing, alongside radiographic hard-tissue endpoints.
4. Reports that both materials achieved comparable overall outcomes, supporting RST and ACS as effective options for open wound closure in immediate posterior implants over short-term follow-up.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Retrospective observational cohort, single centre.
- **Recruitment**: 38 patients initially assessed; 6 excluded (did not meet inclusion criteria or had incomplete CBCT data); **32 patients** (18 female, 14 male; mean age 41.84 y, range 22–73) included, receiving **40 implants** total.
- **Groups**: 20 implants with autologous **RST**, 20 implants with **ACS** substitute (20 patients-worth of implants per group). All implants placed immediately into **posterior** sockets.
- **Soft-tissue assessment**: Novel soft-tissue healing **scoring system** (components include tissue colour and fibrous repair, among others) plus evaluation of **gingival recession** (assessed before tooth extraction and after restoration placement).
- **Hard-tissue assessment**: **Horizontal bone width (HBW)** and **interproximal marginal bone level (MBL)** measured on radiographs.
- **Follow-up**: Short-term, approximately **6 months**; soft-tissue scoring reported at **2 weeks** postoperatively.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Total soft-tissue healing score at 2 weeks**: no significant difference between RST and ACS.
- **Tissue colour**: ACS group **better** than RST (P = 0.016).
- **Fibrous repair**: ACS group **worse** than RST (P = 0.043).
- **Gingival recession**: comparable between groups, both before tooth extraction and after restoration placement.
- **Hard tissue (HBW and MBL changes)**: no intergroup differences.
- **Bottom line**: Within study limitations, both RST and ACS were effective for open wound closure, achieving ideal soft- and hard-tissue healing in immediate implant placement.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Retrospective, single-centre** design with a **small sample** (40 implants, 20 per group) — limits generalisability and statistical power.
- **Short follow-up** (~6 months) — long-term soft- and hard-tissue stability and implant survival not assessed.
- **Novel, non-validated scoring system** for soft tissue introduces measurement uncertainty.
- Restricted to **posterior** sockets; findings may not transfer to the esthetic zone.
- Full statistical detail (e.g., exact HBW/MBL values, effect sizes, confidence intervals) is in the paywalled full text and not recoverable from the available abstract/snippets.

## 7. Glossary

- **Immediate implant placement (IIP / Type 1)**: Implant inserted into a fresh extraction socket at the same visit as tooth removal.
- **Open wound healing (secondary intention)**: Wound left without flap-to-flap primary closure; the gap heals by granulation and epithelialisation. Contrasts with **primary (flap-to-flap) closure**.
- **Reactive soft tissue (RST)**: Autologous soft tissue (granulation/connective tissue) harvested and repositioned to seal the open socket/wound.
- **Absorbable collagen sponge (ACS)**: Resorbable collagen-based substitute placed in the socket to plug/seal the open wound and support healing.
- **Horizontal bone width (HBW)**: Buccolingual/buccopalatal width of the alveolar ridge measured radiographically.
- **Marginal bone level (MBL)**: Interproximal crestal bone height relative to the implant reference point, tracked over time.
- **CBCT**: Cone-beam computed tomography, used here for hard-tissue assessment/eligibility.
