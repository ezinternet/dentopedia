## Why Ingested

Direct head-to-head in-vitro comparison of the three main commercial saliva-decontamination cleaners (Ivoclean, Katana Cleaner, Zirclean) against air-particle abrasion on KATANA STML zirconia, with thermocycling. Reinforces the pooled finding in [[dental-materials/zirconia/silva-2022-zirconia-saliva-cleaning-sr-ma]] that dedicated cleaning restores bond to saliva-contaminated zirconia, and adds a single-bench comparator to [[dental-materials/zirconia/genc-2025-cleaning-protocols-zirconia-bond-strength]] showing mechanical re-abrasion still tops chemical cleaners.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro SBS study (n=100, KATANA STML, saliva-contaminated): air-particle abrasion gave the highest bond strength, while Katana Cleaner / Ivoclean / Zirclean were viable chemical alternatives; thermocycling reduced bond in all groups.

## 한줄요약

In-vitro 전단접착강도 연구(n=100, KATANA STML, 타액 오염): 공기입자분사(Air-Particle Abrasion)가 가장 높은 접착강도를 보였고, Katana Cleaner·Ivoclean·Zirclean은 유효한 화학적 대안이었으며, 열순환(thermocycling)은 모든 군의 접착강도를 감소시켰다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Single-bench head-to-head of four decontamination methods on saliva-contaminated zirconia: air-particle abrasion (APC) vs three commercial cleaners — Katana Cleaner (KC), Ivoclean (IC), Zirclean (ZC).
- Tested with a clinically relevant MDP-containing dual-cure cement (Panavia V5) and aged with 10,000 thermal cycles.
- Confirms a mechanical re-conditioning (air abrasion) ceiling above chemical cleaners while validating chemical cleaners as practical alternatives when re-abrasion is impractical (e.g., intraorally on a try-in restoration).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Specimens**: 100 zirconia specimens (KATANA STML, Noritake), randomized into 4 experimental + 1 control (n=20 each).
- **Contamination**: human saliva.
- **Cleaning arms**: air-particle abrasion (APC), Katana Cleaner (KC), Ivoclean (IC), Zirclean (ZC).
- **Bonding**: composite resin cylinders bonded with dual-cure resin cement (Panavia V5, Kuraray Noritake).
- **Aging**: 48 h distilled-water storage at 37°C; half of each group then underwent 10,000 thermal cycles.
- **Testing**: shear bond strength at 0.5 mm/min crosshead speed; failure mode by stereomicroscope.
- **Statistics**: one-way ANOVA + Bonferroni post hoc (α=0.05).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Significant SBS differences between non-aged and thermocycled conditions in all groups (p<0.05) — thermocycling reduced bond strength across the board.
- Air-particle abrasion (APC) showed significantly higher SBS than all other cleaning methods (p<0.05).
- Chemical cleaners (KC, IC, ZC) offered viable alternatives to air abrasion.
- (Numeric SBS means/SD and per-group failure-mode breakdown not available — abstract-only.)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro SBS; no clinical/long-term data.
- Abstract-only here — exact SBS values, ranking among the three chemical cleaners, and failure-mode distribution not retrieved.
- Single zirconia material (KATANA STML) and single cement (Panavia V5); generalizability to other zirconia/cement systems untested.

## 7. Glossary

- **APC (Air-Particle Abrasion)**: sandblasting with alumina particles to re-roughen/re-activate the bonding surface.
- **SBS (Shear Bond Strength)**: force per unit area at debond under shear loading.
- **Ivoclean (IC)**: ZrO₂-suspension cleaning paste (Ivoclar) that removes salivary phosphate contaminants.
- **Katana Cleaner (KC)**: MDP-salt cleaner (Kuraray Noritake) for intraoral decontamination.
- **Zirclean (ZC)**: alkaline cleaning gel for contaminated zirconia/glass-ceramic surfaces.
- **Thermocycling**: cyclic hot/cold water immersion to simulate intraoral thermal aging.
- **MDP (10-Methacryloyloxydecyl Dihydrogen Phosphate)**: functional monomer providing chemical adhesion to zirconia.
