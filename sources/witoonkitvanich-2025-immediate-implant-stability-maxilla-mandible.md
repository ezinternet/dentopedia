## Why Ingested

The immediate-molar implant pages we hold establish that fresh molar sockets can achieve good primary stability, but few directly contrast arch (maxilla vs mandible) ISQ trajectories under a controlled protocol. This 2025 prospective trial (computer-guided, tooth-supported guide, n=30) quantifies the maxilla-vs-mandible ISQ gap over 24 weeks and shows the stability dip is arch-dependent — extending [[immediate-implant/ragucci-2020-immediate-implant-molar-extraction-sockets]] (immediate molar feasibility) and reinforcing the ISQ-trajectory evidence in [[implants/isq/bavetta-2019-insertion-torque-isq-immediate-loading-extraction-socket]] for the immediate-loading decision in fresh sockets.

## One-line Summary

Controlled prospective non-randomized trial (n=30; 14 maxilla / 16 mandible) of computer-guided immediate molar implants: both arches achieved primary ISQ >73; mandible trended higher throughout and was significantly higher at 24 weeks (81.47 vs 78.23, P=0.018); a stability dip appeared in the maxilla at 2–4 weeks but not the mandible.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Direct maxilla-vs-mandible ISQ comparison** for immediate molar implants placed with computer-guided, tooth-supported surgical guides over a 24-week period — fresh molar sockets, not anterior/aesthetic-zone sites.
2. **Both arches reached high primary stability** (mean ISQ >73 at baseline), demonstrating that computer-guided placement using septal/inter-radicular bone can deliver primary stability >70 even in challenging fresh molar sockets.
3. **Arch-dependent stability dip**: a transient ISQ decline at 2 and 4 weeks occurred only in the maxilla (statistically significant in the GEE model); the mandible showed monotonic increase from baseline with no dip.
4. **Mandible significantly higher at 24 weeks** (81.47 vs 78.23, P=0.018) — confirming the study hypothesis that mandibular molar implants present superior stability, attributed to higher posterior-mandible bone density and greater implant length used.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Single-center controlled prospective non-randomized trial (Implant Centre, Mahidol University). Maxilla = test (clinical challenge), mandible = control (standard). Reported per CONSORT 2010.
- **Subjects**: 30 patients, each with a single unrestorable maxillary or mandibular molar (root fracture, endodontic failure, or unrestorable) requiring immediate implant placement. Sockets had intact buccal and lingual walls. Final groups: 14 maxilla, 16 mandible.
- **Inclusion**: age >18, healthy, able to undergo computer-guided surgery under local anesthesia, intact buccal/lingual socket walls.
- **Exclusion**: systemic disease/medication affecting healing, smoker, pregnancy, alcohol/drug abuse, mucosal disease (e.g., lichen planus), bone pathology, head/neck radiation or chemotherapy history, chronic periodontitis, insufficient apical bone, acute purulent infection, insertion torque <10 N·cm.
- **Digital workflow**: i500 intraoral scanner (Medit, STL) + CBCT (DICOM), merged in 3Shape Implant Studio. Prosthetically driven planning; tooth-supported digital surgical guide.
- **Implant**: SuperLine II bone-level tapered implant (Dentium, Seoul). Placed in inter-radicular septum or root socket (mostly mesial root) for ideal prosthetic position and maximal bone coverage.
- **Surgery**: One operator. Atraumatic simple extraction → guided bed preparation (Dentium guided kit) → implant placement → ISQ measured → gap filled with alloplastic graft (Osteon III, Genoss) → customized titanium healing abutment. Articaine 4% with 1:100,000 epinephrine.
- **Stability measurement**: Resonance frequency analysis (RFA). SmartPeg type 7 (Osstell), screwed at 4–5 N·cm; Mega ISQ monitor (Megagen). Probe held perpendicular in four directions (mesial/distal/buccal/lingual), repeated three times.
- **Time points**: Baseline (immediately after placement), 1, 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, and 24 weeks.
- **Statistics**: Independent t-test for between-arch comparison at each time point; generalized estimating equation (GEE) model for within-arch change vs baseline; Fisher's exact test for demographics. P < 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Baseline (primary stability):** Maxilla 73.98 ± 5.40; Mandible 73.59 ± 6.74; P=0.864 (no significant difference). Both >70.

**ISQ over time (mean, descriptive — maxilla vs mandible, between-arch P):**

| Time | Maxilla | Mandible | P |
|---|---|---|---|
| Baseline | 73.98 ± 5.40 | 73.59 ± 6.74 | 0.864 |
| 1 wk | 73.79 ± 6.67 | 74.28 ± 6.29 | 0.836 |
| 2 wk | 71.38 ± 8.39 | 74.25 ± 6.81 | 0.309 |
| 4 wk | 71.93 ± 6.73 | 74.77 ± 6.57 | 0.253 |
| 6 wk | 74.32 ± 5.47 | 77.06 ± 5.44 | 0.181 |
| 8 wk | 75.50 ± 4.59 | 78.19 ± 4.88 | 0.133 |
| 10 wk | 76.68 ± 4.09 | 79.06 ± 4.60 | 0.147 |
| 12 wk | 77.09 ± 3.66 | 79.92 ± 4.38 | 0.067 |
| 24 wk | 78.23 ± 3.73 | 81.47 ± 3.32 | **0.018 \*** |

- Mandible mean ISQ was higher than maxilla at **every** time point; the difference reached statistical significance only at 24 weeks (P=0.018).
- **GEE (within-arch vs baseline):** Maxilla showed a *significant reduction* at 2 wk (b=−2.607, P=0.009) and 4 wk (b=−2.054, P=0.024) — the stability dip — then significant increases at 10, 12, 24 wk. Mandible showed *no reduction*; significant increase from 6 wk onward (b at 24 wk = +7.875, all P<0.001 from 6 wk).
- **Stability dip** present only in maxillary molar region (2 weeks). No dip in mandible. Authors attribute the minimal/absent dip to the rigid tooth-supported guide and to posterior-mandible bone density.
- **No implant failures**; all achieved osseointegration with a one-stage protocol.
- **Demographic imbalances (significant):** implant length differed (maxilla used more 8 mm; mandible more 12 mm; P=0.011); implant area differed (maxilla 100% first molar; mandible 56% first / 44% second molar; P=0.007). Greater mandibular implant length is cited as a reason for higher mandibular stability.
- **Supporting bone-density rationale (cited):** posterior mandible 674.3 ± 227 HU vs posterior maxilla 455.1 ± 122 HU (Turkyilmaz et al.).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Small sample** (n=30; 14 vs 16) and **non-randomized** allocation by arch.
- **Confounded by implant length and tooth position**: maxilla used shorter implants and only first molars, mandible used longer implants and mixed first/second molars — these differences (not arch alone) partly drive the stability gap, as the authors acknowledge.
- **Bone class/density not reported**: septal bone volume was too low to interpret Hounsfield-unit density reliably, so Lekholm-Zarb bone classification and HU were not reported.
- **Computer-guided protocol limitations**: reduced tactile feedback of bone density during guided drilling; manufacturing/surgical guide deviations possible; learning curve for surgeon and technician.
- No clinical loading/prosthetic survival outcomes beyond 24-week stability; longer follow-up and RCT design recommended.

## 6. Related Work

- **Ragucci et al. (2020)** — immediate implants in molar extraction sockets (feasibility/outcomes); this trial extends the arch-specific stability question.
- **Bavetta et al. (2019)** — insertion torque and ISQ for immediate loading in extraction sockets; reinforces the RFA-based loading-decision framework.
- **Somraj et al. (25)** — reported primary stability 74.03 ± 5.63 for immediately placed posterior implants (concordant with this study's >73 baseline).
- **Rowan et al. (20)** — RFA of fresh-socket vs healed-site implants; immediate ISQ >65 but slightly below delayed (>70).
- **Gehrke et al. (28)** — comparable fresh-socket vs healed-site ISQ initially; healed sites superior during follow-up.
- **Gahona et al. (38)** — maxilla vs mandible RFA (57 patients); mandibular ISQ higher at placement, highly significant maxilla–mandible difference (P<0.001).
- **Turkyilmaz et al. (35)** — CT bone density: posterior mandible 674 HU vs posterior maxilla 455 HU, mechanistic basis for the arch difference.

## 7. Glossary

- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: RFA-derived index (1–100) of bone–implant interface stiffness; >60 commonly considered acceptable, ~70–75 indicating good primary stability.
- **RFA (Resonance Frequency Analysis)**: non-invasive method measuring implant stability via a SmartPeg/transducer and a frequency-reading probe (e.g., Osstell, Mega ISQ).
- **Primary stability**: mechanical bone-to-implant contact at placement (no biological bond yet); depends on bone density and preparation.
- **Secondary stability**: biological stability from osseointegration (bone remodeling/formation) developing after placement.
- **Stability dip**: transient ISQ decline (typically ~2 weeks) marking the transition from mechanical to biological stability during early remodeling.
- **GEE (Generalized Estimating Equation)**: regression model for correlated/repeated-measures longitudinal data; here used for within-arch ISQ change vs baseline.
- **Inter-radicular septum**: bone between molar roots; engaging it improves primary stability in immediate molar implants.
- **Tooth-supported surgical guide**: a guide seated on adjacent teeth (more rigid/stable than mucosa- or bone-supported guides), aiding precise placement and primary stability.
