## One-line Summary
Mini-review (in vivo 2021, 35:3011–3017) categorizing internal (drill properties, parameters) and external (cooling, irrigation, bone) factors influencing heat development during medical/dental drilling, plus a discussion of standardized ex vivo measurement methods to reduce animal experimentation.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: in vivo, 2021;35:3011–3017
- Affiliation: University Medical Center Rostock; BerlinAnalytix GmbH
- Type: Mini-review with preliminary inflammatory tissue reaction data
- Open access

## 2. Key Contributions
- Internal/external factor framework for drilling heat.
- Discussion of standardized ex vivo methods to reduce animal experimentation.
- Preliminary inflammatory tissue reaction data on inadequate drilling.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative review of literature on drilling-induced heat in bone.
- Categorization: internal factors (drill diameter, shape, material, speed, depth, feed, intermittence) vs external factors (cooling, irrigation, bone density, anatomy).
- Comparison of temperature measurement methods: thermocouples, IR thermography, fiber optic sensors.
- Brief preliminary in vivo data on tissue inflammation.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Heat is multifactorial; internal and external factors interact.
- 47°C threshold cited; clinical bone necrosis consequences detailed (implant loss, fractures).
- Drill diameter, shape, material identified as primary internal contributors.
- Standardized ex vivo measurement methods called for to enable reproducibility and reduce animal testing.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Mini-review without quantitative pooling.
- Preliminary inflammatory data only — not a primary study.
- Future: standardized measurement protocols, ex vivo replacement of animal models.

## 6. Related Work
- Chauhan 2018 — broader SR on biomechanical factors.
- Heuzeroth 2021 — minipig in vivo correlating thermal exposure to BIC.
- Timon 2019 — orthopedic perspective on thermal osteonecrosis.

## 7. Glossary
- **Internal factors**: drill-side variables (geometry, material, speed, feed).
- **External factors**: environment-side variables (irrigation, bone, anatomy).
- **Ex vivo**: outside the living organism but using biological tissue (e.g., excised bovine bone).
