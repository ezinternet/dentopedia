## Why Ingested

This GRADE systematic review is the evidence backbone for the overview's "risk-stratify, don't reflex-restore" thesis: it quantifies that occlusal changes after posterior tooth loss are mostly limited (on average up to ~2 mm) and rates the quality of evidence as VERY LOW, so tooth replacement should not be regarded as the default mainstay of therapy. Directly supports the ≤2 mm limited-change claim and the decision-tree framing in [[wiki/overviews/unopposed-tooth-overeruption-overview]] and reinforces the conservative-management direction of [[wiki/occlusion/livas-2016-fixed-retention-unopposed-molar-overeruption]].

## One-line Summary

GRADE systematic review (7 included studies of posterior bounded edentulous spaces in adults) finding that mesio-distal migration and overeruption after tooth loss are mostly limited (on average up to ~2 mm), on VERY LOW quality evidence — so prosthetic replacement should not be considered the default mainstay of therapy.

- **Journal**: Journal of Dentistry, Volume 39, Issue 2, February 2011, Pages 108–116

- **Source artifact**: ScienceDirect landing-page print (abstract + Introduction + Methods body present; full Results/Discussion text behind paywall).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Applies the **GRADE** system to a single, narrowly defined clinical question — whether posterior **bounded edentulous spaces (BES)** in adults need restorative replacement — making the quality-of-evidence and strength-of-recommendation judgments explicit and transparent.
- Quantifies the expected occlusal change: for most cases, mesio-distal narrowing and overeruption after tooth loss are **limited, on average up to ~2 mm**.
- Rates the **quality of evidence as VERY LOW**.
- Concludes that **tooth replacement should not necessarily be regarded as the mainstay of therapy** for posterior BES.
- Produces a **decision-tree-like scheme** to depict treatment options and aid clinician decision-making under low-quality evidence.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Systematic review following selected components of the PRISMA and AMSTAR statements; GRADE used for quality-of-evidence and strength-of-recommendation grading.
- **PICO question**:
  - P (participants): adult patients
  - I (intervention): restorative treatment
  - C (comparison): non-treatment
  - O (outcome): overeruption (and mesio-distal movement of adjacent teeth)
- **Databases searched (search up to and including February 2010)**:
  - PubMed
  - Cochrane Register of Controlled Trials (CENTRAL)
  - LILACS (searched by one author, CMF)
  - Google Scholar (in English, French, German, Greek, Italian, Portuguese, and Spanish)
  - Grey literature via OpenSIGLE (System for Information on Grey Literature in Europe)
  - Manual searching of reference lists of retrieved studies
- Searches conducted in triplicate for the primary databases.
- **Outcome measures**: quantitative mesio-distal changes (narrowing of the BES) and overeruption (extrusion of unopposed teeth).
- **Study selection**: 803 potential papers initially retrieved; **7 studies finally selected**. No study directly compared restorative treatment vs non-treatment; control groups in included studies were typically fully dentate patients.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Occlusal changes (overeruption and mesio-distal narrowing of posterior BES) were **limited**; for most cases the average change was **up to ~2 mm**, of questionable clinical relevance.
- **Severe occlusal changes affected only a small number of unopposed teeth**, implying other factors also determine the magnitude of BES changes.
- **Subject age** may be associated with the severity of change.
- **Quality of evidence: VERY LOW** (GRADE).
- **Bottom-line recommendation**: tooth replacement should not necessarily be the default/mainstay therapy for posterior BES; more robust studies are needed on the long-term effects of non-treatment.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Very low quality of available evidence; no head-to-head treatment-vs-non-treatment studies existed, so the comparison rests on indirect evidence.
- Only 7 studies met inclusion criteria from 803 screened.
- Long-term effects of leaving a posterior BES untreated remain insufficiently characterized — more robust (ideally prospective/controlled) studies are called for.
- Conflict of interest noted: the first author is a member of the GRADE Working Group.

## 7. Glossary

- **BES (bounded edentulous space)**: an edentulous gap bounded mesially and distally by remaining natural teeth (here, posterior).
- **GRADE**: Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation — a system for rating quality (certainty) of evidence and strength of recommendations.
- **Overeruption / supraeruption**: continued eruption (extrusion) of a tooth that has lost its opposing antagonist.
- **Mesio-distal migration**: drift of teeth adjacent to an edentulous space, narrowing the space.
- **PICO**: Participant, Intervention, Comparison, Outcome — framework for structuring a clinical question.
- **AMSTAR / PRISMA**: appraisal/reporting standards for systematic reviews.
- **OpenSIGLE**: System for Information on Grey Literature in Europe.
