## Why Ingested

기존 [[occlusion/kattadiyil-2021-relationship-centric-occlusion-maximal-intercuspal]] 페이지는 CO–MIP(=CR–ICP) discrepancy가 TMD와 연관된다는 주장을 담고 있으나, 그 연관 주장의 근거 강도를 평가할 1차 SR이 위키에 없었다. 본 SR (Jiménez-Silva 2017)은 바로 그 CR–ICP discrepancy–TMD 연관에 대한 직접적 systematic review로서, 개별 연구는 연관을 시사하나 low quality + heterogeneity로 확정 불가라는 근거 기반(evidence base)을 제공한다. ## One-line Summary

Systematic review (n=20 low-quality observational studies; NOS mean 3.36): most individual studies link CR–ICP (centric) discrepancy to muscle pain and joint disorders, but heterogeneity + low quality preclude any definite causal association with TMD.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(저질 관찰연구 20편, NOS 평균 3.36): 대부분의 개별 연구는 중심위–최대교두감합위 불일치(CR–ICP discrepancy)를 근육통·관절장애와 연관시키지만, 이질성과 낮은 질로 인해 측두하악장애(TMD)와의 확정적 인과 연관은 성립할 수 없다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic review to directly examine whether the **CR–ICP discrepancy** (the centric slide between centric relation [CR] and the intercuspal/maximum intercuspation position [ICP/MIP]) is associated with **temporomandibular disorders (TMDs)**.
- Pooled and quality-graded the observational evidence (1960–2016, all languages, 9 databases) using PICO + Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS).
- Bottom line: a frequently-cited clinical claim (centric discrepancy → TMD) rests on **low-quality, heterogeneous evidence** that does not support a definite association.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Systematic review of analytical observational clinical studies.
- **Search**: 1960–2016, all languages, 9 electronic databases (PubMed, Cochrane Library, Medline, Embase, Scopus, EBSCOhost, BIREME, Lilacs, Scielo).
- **Selection**: Two independent reviewers; PICO format for analysis.
- **Quality appraisal**: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS) for observational study quality.
- **Yield**: 467 potentially eligible articles identified → **20 studies** analyzed.
- **Grouping by intervention**: orthodontic patients (n=3) vs subjects without intervention (n=17).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **20 studies** included (3 orthodontic-intervention + 17 no-intervention).
- **Quality**: low — NOS **mean score 3.36**.
- **Direction of findings**: in most studies, CR–ICP discrepancy was **associated with**:
  - Muscle disorders → pain.
  - Joint disorders → noise, disc displacement, pain, crepitus, osteoarthritis, osteoarthrosis.
- **But**: lack of consistency across reported results reduces validity. Heterogeneity of design/methodology + low quality → **no definite conclusion possible**; an association between CR–ICP discrepancy and TMD cannot be established.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- (abstract-only — full text not retrieved; results limited to what the abstract reports)
- Included studies were low quality (NOS mean 3.36) and methodologically heterogeneous.
- Lack of validated, standardized diagnostic criteria for CR–ICP discrepancy and for TMD across studies.
- Cross-sectional/observational designs cannot establish causation or risk-factor status.
- **Future work**: well-defined and validated diagnostic criteria, rigorous methodologies, and **longitudinal studies** to test whether CR–ICP discrepancy is a risk factor for TMD.

## 7. Glossary

- **CR (Centric Relation)**: maxillomandibular relationship with the condyles in their anterior-superior position against the articular eminences, independent of tooth contact — a clinically reproducible reference position.
- **ICP / MIP (Intercuspal Position / Maximum Intercuspation)**: the occlusion of maximum tooth intercuspation (= maximum intercuspal position, MICP).
- **CR–ICP discrepancy ("centric slide")**: the difference (slide) between the CR contact position and the ICP/MIP; a non-coincidence of the two positions.
- **TMD (Temporomandibular Disorders)**: cluster of musculoskeletal and neuromuscular conditions of the masticatory muscles and TMJ (pain, noise, disc displacement, crepitus, osteoarthritis/osteoarthrosis).
- **NOS (Newcastle-Ottawa Scale)**: quality-assessment tool for non-randomized/observational studies.
- **PICO**: Population, Intervention, Comparison, Outcome — framework for structuring a systematic-review question.
