## One-line Summary

Retrospective ED case-control chart review (n=36; 18 metronidazole + 18 ethanol-matched controls): no documented disulfiram-like reaction in any metronidazole patient with detectable ethanol, and significantly LESS hypertension than controls (16.7% vs 61.1%, P<0.0001) — supporting absence of a clinically meaningful alcohol–metronidazole interaction.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: WMJ (Wisconsin Medical Journal) 122(3):171–, 2023
- **DOI**: not assigned by WMJ (unknown)
- **Institution**: Froedtert & the Medical College of Wisconsin, Milwaukee (Dept. of Pharmacy; Emergency Medicine/Medical Toxicology)
- **Design**: retrospective cross-sectional case-control chart review; IRB-approved with consent waiver

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides controlled clinical (not just experimental) data in the acute-care setting, where prior evidence was limited.
- Uses a control group matched for age, sex, and ethanol concentration to separate ethanol's own effects from any drug interaction.
- Searched records explicitly for the term "disulfiram" — found zero documented suspected reactions in the metronidazole group.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Single academic ED in Milwaukee (~72,000 visits/yr), Dec 1, 2010 – Dec 31, 2020.
- Included: ≥18 y, analytically confirmed (detectable) ethanol concentration, received metronidazole while ethanol predicted still present in serum.
- Comparator: ethanol-concentration-, sex-, and age-matched patients who did not receive metronidazole.
- Primary outcome: incidence of documented disulfiram-like effects (nausea, vomiting, flushing, tachycardia HR>100, hypertension SBP>160/DBP>105, hypotension SBP<90/MAP<65, or antiemetic use).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **n=36** (18 metronidazole + 18 matched control); mean age 46 y both groups; mean ethanol 0.21 g/dL.
- **Hypertension significantly LESS in metronidazole group: 16.7% vs 61.1%, P<0.0001.**
- No other significant difference in disulfiram-like effects between groups.
- **Zero** patients on metronidazole with detectable ethanol had a suspected disulfiram-like reaction documented.
- Consistent with controlled human/animal data showing no acetaldehyde rise with metronidazole + ethanol.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Very small sample (n=36), single center, retrospective chart review — relies on documentation completeness.
- Disulfiram-like symptoms are nonspecific and may be masked or mimicked by intoxication/illness.
- Acute-care ED population; may not generalize to outpatient multi-day dosing.
- Author conclusion: metronidazole should not be avoided over ethanol-interaction concern when it is the superior agent.

## 7. Glossary

- **Disulfiram-like reaction**: flushing/nausea/vomiting/tachycardia/hypertension when alcohol is combined with an ALDH-inhibiting drug.
- **ALDH (aldehyde dehydrogenase)**: converts acetaldehyde → acetate; its inhibition causes acetaldehyde buildup.
- **Case-control (matched)**: observational design comparing exposed vs unexposed matched on confounders (here age, sex, ethanol level).
- **Acetaldehyde**: toxic ethanol metabolite responsible for disulfiram-reaction symptoms.
