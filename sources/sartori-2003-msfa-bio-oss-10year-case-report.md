## One-line Summary
Single-patient case report with serial biopsies at 8 months, 2 years, and 10 years after MSFA with Bio-Oss alone: bone tissue (incl. medullary spaces) increased from 29.8% → 69.7% → 86.7%, with corresponding progressive resorption of Bio-Oss particles — a textbook visualization of long-term remodeling.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Clinical Oral Implants Research 2003;14(3):369–372 (published 20 May 2003).
- **Study type**: Case report with serial histomorphometric biopsies.
- **Setting**: Studio Dentistico Sartori-Bovenzi, Piacenza (Italy).

## 2. Key Contributions
- **Only published 10-year serial histomorphometric trajectory** of Bio-Oss in a single human sinus.
- Provides three-timepoint data (8 mo / 2 yr / 10 yr) — a continuous picture of remodeling unavailable elsewhere.
- Reports **progressive Bio-Oss resorption** with bone replacement: 70.2% → 30.3% → 13.3% Bio-Oss fraction over 10 years.
- Conflicts with later Mordenfeld 2010 11-year findings of "no significant DPBB resorption" — useful for understanding inter-study variability.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Single patient underwent MSFA with Bio-Oss alone for posterior maxillary atrophy.
- Serial trephine biopsies harvested from the augmented region at 8 months, 2 years, and 10 years post-graft.
- Twenty thin sections per biopsy analyzed by histomorphometry; mean ± SD reported per timepoint.
- Comparator: trend analysis across the three timepoints.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **8 months**: bone tissue (incl. medullary spaces) 29.8 ± 2.6%; Bio-Oss 70.2%.
- **2 years**: bone tissue 69.7 ± 2.7%; Bio-Oss ~30.3%.
- **10 years**: bone tissue 86.7 ± 2.8%; Bio-Oss ~13.3%.
- Highly significant increasing trend in bone formation paralleled by Bio-Oss resorption (p << 0.05 across timepoints).
- Bio-Oss progressively replaced by new bone over the decade.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- **n=1** — case report, no generalization possible.
- Single operator/center; no clinical implant outcomes reported.
- Bio-Oss alone (no autogenous component), differs from common 80:20 mixtures.
- Histomorphometric reporting style of 2003 may not match later non-decalcified plastic-embedded protocols (see Rogova 2025).
- Apparent conflict with Mordenfeld 2010 (n=11, no resorption at 11 yr with 80:20 mix) — possible explanations: graft composition difference, intersubject variability, methodological differences in particle vs total area measurement.

## 6. Related Work
- Mordenfeld 2010 — n=11 11-year DPBB+autogenous biopsies showing NO significant particle resorption.
- Mordenfeld 2014 — Companion 10-year clinical outcomes (CSR 86%).
- Tadjoedin 2003 — Retrospective histomorphometric study of Bio-Oss in sinus.
- Manfro 2013 — Comparative DBBM histomorphometry (animal).
- Rogova 2025 — Methodological SR of bone histomorphometry.

## 7. Glossary
- **MSFA (Maxillary Sinus Floor Augmentation)**: Lateral window or transcrestal elevation of sinus floor with graft for implant placement in atrophic posterior maxilla.
- **Bio-Oss**: Geistlich's deproteinized bovine bone mineral, prototypical DBBM product.
- **Serial biopsy / case-report trajectory**: Repeated sampling of same site over years to track remodeling — rare in human bone-substitute literature.
- **Bone tissue (incl. medullary spaces)**: Histomorphometric category summing mineralized bone + marrow — complementary to "graft particle" fraction.
