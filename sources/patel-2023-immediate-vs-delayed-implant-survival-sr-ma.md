## Why Ingested

The immediate-implant category and the timing-overview need a recent SR+MA that quantifies the survival gap between immediate and delayed placement at the pooled level. This 2023 Salford SR+MA (10 studies, 700 implants) provides the head-to-head pooled risk ratio (RR 0.99, no significant difference) that anchors the "survival is equivalent, the trade-offs live elsewhere" thesis. It reinforces [[overviews/implant-placement-timing-immediate-early-delayed]] and complements the site-specific molar evidence in [[immediate-implant/ragucci-2020-immediate-implant-molar-extraction-sockets]].

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (10 studies, 6 RCT + 4 non-randomised; 341 immediate vs 359 delayed implants) found no significant difference in implant survival between immediate and delayed placement (RR 0.99, 95% CI 0.96–1.02, p≈0.44, I²=0%), with immediate ~97.4% vs delayed ~97.5%.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Updated head-to-head SR+MA of **immediate vs delayed** implant placement, intended to refresh Chrcanovic et al. (2015); no comparable recent review was found in a scoping search.
- Pooled **fixed-effects meta-analysis of survival** across 10 studies → **risk ratio 0.99 (95% CI 0.96–1.02)**, statistically non-significant, with **zero heterogeneity (I²=0%)**, giving high confidence in the null result.
- Reports raw survival counts: **341 immediate implants → 332 survived (97.4%)**; **359 delayed implants → 350 survived (97.5%)**.
- Notes a directional (non-significant) signal: slightly more failures in the immediate group, with some immediate-group studies dipping to 90–95% survival while delayed-group studies mostly exceeded 95%.
- Surfaces persistent knowledge gaps (smoking, periodontal disease, medical comorbidities) not resolvable from the included evidence.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Question (PICO)**: Population — adults ≥18 y; Intervention — immediate placement into fresh extraction sockets; Comparator — delayed placement into healed sites (≥2 months, typically 3–4 months after extraction); Outcome — implant survival (implant still present at examination).
- **Databases**: CENTRAL, MEDLINE, Scopus (searched November 2022); supplemented by snowballing citation method and Google Scholar grey-literature search.
- **Eligibility**: RCTs and non-randomised comparative observational studies, 2014–2022, English, peer-reviewed. Excluded: case-control, case studies/series, editorials, non-English, children <18 y.
- **Quality appraisal**: Cochrane **RoB 2.0** for RCTs and **ROBINS-I** for non-randomised studies, via Review Manager 5.4.1.
- **Synthesis**: Meta-analysis in **RevMan v.5.3**, fixed-effects model, risk ratio as the effect measure; funnel plot for publication bias.
- **Included studies (n=10)**: Bömicke 2017, Cucchi 2017, Esposito 2015, Hakobyan 2020, Han 2016, Malchiodi 2016, S. Raes 2018, Slagter 2016, Tonetti 2017, Woods 2019. Six RCTs + four non-randomised (Hakobyan, Han, Raes, Woods).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Pooled survival (primary outcome)**: risk ratio **0.99, 95% CI 0.96–1.02**; **Z = 0.78, p = 0.44** (abstract also cites Z = 0.75, p = 0.45) → no significant difference. **I² = 0%** (heterogeneity p = 0.60).
- **Aggregate survival**: immediate **332/341 = 97.4%**; delayed **350/359 = 97.5%**.
- **Risk of bias**: 5 of 6 RCTs at low risk of bias; the 4 non-randomised comparative studies at moderate-to-serious risk.
- **Per-study survival (immediate vs delayed)**: Bömicke 94.7% vs 100%; Cucchi 95.9% vs 100%; Esposito 96.3% vs 100%; Hakobyan 97.8% vs 98.1%; Han 100% vs 96.9%; Malchiodi 100% vs 100%; Raes 93.8% vs 100%; Slagter 100% vs 100%; Tonetti 98.3% vs 100%; Woods 97.4% vs 90.5%.
- **Directional pattern**: delayed placement showed superior or equal survival in most trials (n≈6); the two studies where immediate exceeded delayed (Han, Woods) had limited follow-up or atypical populations (Woods = head/neck cancer + post-op radiotherapy patients, where 20% of Straumann SLActive implants failed).
- **Secondary signals from included studies (not pooled)**: mixed marginal/crestal bone loss and ISQ between groups; some studies report higher residual bone loss or more wound/prosthesis complications with immediate placement (e.g. Tonetti: wound failure ~5× more likely in immediate; Bömicke: prosthesis failure 15.8% immediate vs 31.3% delayed — direction varies by study).
- **Publication bias**: funnel plot showed minor asymmetry — small possible bias, judged unlikely to alter the pooled effect.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Short follow-up in several included studies (many ≈1 year); survival differences may emerge with longer observation.
- Small sample sizes per study; heterogeneous implant systems, sites (anterior/posterior, maxilla/mandible) and loading protocols.
- Non-randomised studies carried moderate-to-serious risk of bias (confounding, missing data).
- Confounders such as smoking, periodontal disease, and systemic comorbidities frequently controlled by exclusion in included trials, limiting generalisability to real-world comorbid patients.
- Minor funnel-plot asymmetry hints at possible publication bias.
- Authors call for larger, longer-term RCTs and exploration of modifying factors (smoking, periodontal status, gender, medical conditions).

## 7. Glossary

- **Immediate placement**: implant inserted into a fresh extraction socket at the same appointment as tooth extraction.
- **Delayed placement**: implant inserted into a healed site, ≥2 months (typically 3–4 months) after extraction.
- **Risk ratio (RR)**: ratio of the probability of an outcome (here survival/failure) in one group vs another; RR ≈ 1.0 indicates no difference.
- **I² (heterogeneity)**: proportion of variability across studies due to between-study differences rather than chance; 0% = no observed heterogeneity.
- **RoB 2.0 / ROBINS-I**: Cochrane risk-of-bias appraisal tools for randomised and non-randomised studies respectively.
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: resonance-frequency-analysis measure of implant stability (0–100).
- **PORT**: post-operative radiotherapy (relevant to the Woods head/neck cancer cohort).
