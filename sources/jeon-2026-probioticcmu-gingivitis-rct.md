## Why Ingested

Probiotic adjuncts for periodontal/gingival inflammation are an active area where the wiki holds a sibling RCT, [[wiki/periodontics/kim-2026-probiotics-nspt-periodontal-db-rct]] (probiotics adjunct to non-surgical periodontal therapy). This Jeon 2026 double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT tests an oral probiotic (OraCMU / ProbioticCMU) as a *standalone* daily supplement in gingivitis/incipient periodontitis — reinforcing the probiotic-for-gingival-inflammation evidence base with a clean placebo comparator and mechanistic (inflammatory-marker, microbiota) readouts. Ingested to strengthen the periodontics probiotic cluster and provide a non-NSPT (supplement-only) data point alongside the NSPT-adjunct sibling.

## One-line Summary

Double-blind placebo-controlled RCT (n=80) of 8-week OraCMU/ProbioticCMU oral probiotic tablets vs placebo in adults with gingivitis/incipient periodontitis: probiotic produced significantly greater reductions in gingival index (GI) and bleeding on probing (BOP) and modulated inflammatory markers and oral microbiota, with no serious adverse events.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides direct RCT evidence that an oral probiotic supplement (OraCMU / ProbioticCMU), taken as a standalone daily tablet, improves gingival health in adults with gingivitis and incipient periodontitis.
- Pairs clinical periodontal outcomes (GI, BOP, probing depth, clinical attachment level, gingival recession, plaque index) with mechanistic readouts: inflammation-related proteins and oral microbiota composition.
- Reports modulation of host inflammatory signaling — fibroblast growth factor-5 (FGF-5), thymic stromal lymphopoietin (TSLP), and the RANKL/OPG ratio — alongside microbiota shifts, supporting a host-modulation + microbiome-modulation mechanism.
- Establishes short-term (8-week) safety: no serious adverse events reported.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial.
- **Participants**: 80 adults with gingivitis and incipient periodontitis, randomized 1:1 — OraCMU tablets (n=40) vs placebo (n=40).
- **Intervention**: OraCMU tablets (2.0 × 10^? CFU/g — exact exponent not recoverable from abstract text) taken twice daily for 8 weeks; placebo on identical schedule.
- **Primary outcome**: Gingival index (GI).
- **Secondary outcomes**: Bleeding on probing (BOP), probing depth, clinical attachment level, gingival recession, plaque index, inflammation-related proteins, and oral microbiota.
- **Assessment sites**: Six preselected index teeth (#16, #12, #24, #32, #36, #44).
- **Timepoints**: Baseline and week 8.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

At week 8, the probiotic (OraCMU) group vs placebo:

- **Gingival index (GI)**: −0.19 ± 0.03 vs −0.08 ± 0.04; p = .035 (significantly greater reduction in probiotic group).
- **Bleeding on probing (BOP)**: −7.74 ± 1.54 vs −2.82 ± 1.60; p = .030 (significantly greater reduction in probiotic group).
- **Inflammatory markers significantly decreased** in the probiotic group: fibroblast growth factor-5 / FGF-5 (p = .003); thymic stromal lymphopoietin / TSLP (p = .017); RANKL/OPG ratio (p = .021).
- **Additional markers/taxa**: three measures significantly reduced (p = .001; p = .005; p = .046) and one measure significantly increased (p < .001) in the probiotic group — specific marker/taxon identities not recoverable from the abstract text.
- **Safety**: No serious adverse events reported during the study period.
- **Conclusion**: 8-week OraCMU supplementation improved gingival health and modulated the oral microbiota and inflammatory response, supporting OraCMU as a probiotic adjunct for managing gingivitis.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Abstract-only ingest**: full methods, exact probiotic strain identity, CFU dose exponent, complete inflammatory-marker panel, and specific microbial taxa are not available from the retrieved text.
- Short duration (8 weeks) — durability of GI/BOP improvement and any effect on probing depth / clinical attachment level over longer follow-up not established from the abstract.
- Single-trial evidence with modest sample (n=80); effect sizes for GI and BOP, while statistically significant, are clinically small in absolute terms.
- Secondary outcomes (probing depth, CAL, recession, plaque index) were listed but their between-group results are not reported in the abstract — cannot confirm whether the probiotic affected attachment/pocket measures.
- Product-specific (OraCMU); generalizability to other probiotic formulations is not established.

## 7. Glossary

- **OraCMU / ProbioticCMU**: The trade/product name of the oral probiotic tablet tested (developer-specific formulation).
- **GI (Gingival Index)**: Clinical score of gingival inflammation severity; primary outcome.
- **BOP (Bleeding on Probing)**: Proportion/score of sites bleeding on gentle probing; marker of active gingival inflammation.
- **CAL (Clinical Attachment Level)**: Distance from cemento-enamel junction to base of pocket; measure of periodontal attachment loss.
- **FGF-5 (Fibroblast Growth Factor-5)**: Growth-factor protein measured as an inflammation-related marker.
- **TSLP (Thymic Stromal Lymphopoietin)**: Epithelial-derived cytokine involved in inflammatory/allergic signaling.
- **RANKL/OPG ratio**: Receptor activator of nuclear factor κB ligand to osteoprotegerin ratio; a key determinant of osteoclastic bone resorption — elevated ratio favors bone loss.
- **Incipient periodontitis**: Early-stage periodontitis with minimal attachment/bone loss.
