## One-line Summary
Narrative review: bisphosphonates (oral/IV) mechanism, BRONJ risk stratification, dental procedure safety; topical BPs enhance osseointegration; systemic (IV especially) → high BRONJ risk; prevention = oral hygiene + pre-treatment dental clearance.

## 2. Bisphosphonate Types and Risk

| Route | Examples | Indication | BRONJ Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Oral | Alendronate, risedronate, ibandronate | Osteoporosis | Low-moderate (duration-dependent) |
| IV | Zoledronic acid, pamidronate | Malignancy, multiple myeloma | **High** |
| Topical | Experimental | Implant surface | Promotes osseointegration (beneficial) |

## 3. BRONJ Risk Factors
- IV bisphosphonate use (especially zoledronic acid): highest risk
- Duration >3 years (oral BP)
- Invasive dental procedures: extraction, implant placement, periodontal surgery
- Concomitant corticosteroids, chemotherapy
- Pre-existing oral inflammation/infection
- Mandible > maxilla risk

## 4. BRONJ Staging (AAOMS)
- Stage 0: no bone exposure; nonspecific symptoms (pain, imaging changes)
- Stage 1: exposed/necrotic bone; asymptomatic; no infection
- Stage 2: exposed bone + infection (pain, erythema, purulent discharge)
- Stage 3: Stage 2 + pathological fracture, orocutaneous fistula, osteolysis to inferior border

## 5. Prevention Strategies
- Pre-medication: dental clearance before starting IV BP (extractions, periodontal treatment)
- During oral BP therapy <3yr: routine dental care OK; avoid unnecessary surgery
- Oral BP >3yr or IV BP: drug holiday consideration + specialist evaluation for surgical procedures
- Post-procedure: CHX rinse, antibiotic prophylaxis, close follow-up

## 6. Topical BP — Positive Effect
- Topical application on implant surface → enhanced osseointegration
- Inhibits osteoclast activity locally → bone preservation around implant
- Does not carry systemic BRONJ risk (dose too low for systemic effect)
