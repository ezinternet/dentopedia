## One-line Summary
Retrospective case-control CBCT analysis (50 scans reviewed, 39 met criteria) of patients undergoing lateral-window maxillary sinus augmentation, comparing those with intraoperative Schneiderian membrane (SM) perforation vs no perforation; identifies thin sinus floor, retention pseudocyst, presence of septae, concave sinus floor, and triangular sinus shape as risk factors for SM perforation, with MS floor width being the only variable reaching statistical significance.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health 2024;24:1463
- **Pages**: 1–11 (open access)
- **Institution**: Department of Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery, Goldschleger School of Dental Medicine, Tel Aviv University, Israel
- **Study type**: Retrospective case-control CBCT analysis

## 2. Key Contributions
- Defines a **CBCT-readable risk panel** (5 anatomic features) for SM perforation during lateral-window sinus augmentation.
- Provides Israeli single-center evidence on which CBCT findings are *statistically* (MS floor width) vs *trend-level* (other 4) associated with perforation.
- Provides clinicians with **pre-op decision aids**: when CBCT shows triangular sinus + septae + thin floor → either modify approach (piezo, smaller window) or escalate consent.
- Independent inter-rater measurement (two physicians) — strengthens reproducibility of CBCT measurements.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 50 CBCT scans of patients undergoing lateral-window maxillary sinus augmentation; 39 met inclusion (intact scan, recorded intraop perforation status).
- Two groups: **study group** = SM perforation occurred; **control group** = no perforation.
- Two examiners (LK, AK) independently measured CBCT parameters using RadiAnt DICOM Viewer v5.5.
- CBCT parameters measured (coronal/sagittal cross-sections):
  1. MS lateral wall width
  2. MS floor width
  3. SM thickness
  4. SM shape (flat, concave, dome, irregular)
  5. Level of MS ventilation
  6. Presence of septae
  7. Presence of intraosseous blood vessels in MS lateral wall
  8. Geometric shape of MS (square, oval, triangular)
  9. Sinus floor geometry (flat, concave, convex)
  10. Presence of polyps / retention pseudocyst
  11. Oroantral communication
- Statistical analysis: between-group comparison, p<0.05 significance.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **n = 39 CBCTs** ultimately analyzed; perforation incidence consistent with literature (10–40%).
- **Statistically significant** between perforation vs control: **MS floor width** (perforation group narrower).
- Trend / qualitative risk factors (clinically meaningful, not all p<0.05):
  - Thin MS floor.
  - Retention pseudocyst.
  - Septae presence.
  - Concave sinus floor.
  - Triangular sinus shape.
- Recommendation: incorporate these 5 features into a **preoperative CBCT checklist**.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small n (39) — limited statistical power; only one variable cleared p<0.05.
- Single-center, retrospective; no blinding of surgeons during the original surgery.
- Outcome (perforation yes/no) is binary — did not stratify perforation size (small <5 mm vs large) which matters clinically.
- No quantification of inter-rater reliability (ICC) provided in abstract.
- Did not control for surgeon experience.

## 7. Glossary
- **Schneiderian Membrane (SM)**: the maxillary sinus mucoperiosteal lining; typically 0.3–0.8 mm thick; perforation is the most common intraoperative complication of sinus lift.
- **Retention pseudocyst**: dome-shaped, sterile fluid collection of the SM; usually benign but flagged as a perforation risk factor.
- **Septae (Underwood's septa)**: bony partitions within the maxillary sinus, prevalence ~30%, increase the perforation risk during lateral window osteotomy.
- **Lateral window approach**: surgical access to the sinus by trapdoor osteotomy through the lateral antral wall.
- **MS ventilation**: degree of air-filled sinus cavity, a proxy for sinus volume.
