## One-line Summary

Case series (n=5): a single-stage split-crest that longitudinally divides the ridge to provoke a greenstick fracture, spreads the cortical plates with a chisel, places immediate implants, and covers with e-PTFE (GTR) — yielding 1–4 mm width gain (greater in the maxilla) with histologic interpositional bone regeneration.

## 한줄요약

split-crest 고전 증례군(n=5): 치조제를 길이방향으로 분할해 녹색골절을 유발하고 끌로 피질판을 벌린 뒤 즉시 임플란트 식립·e-PTFE 막(GTR) 적용 → 폭 1–4 mm 증가(상악에서 더 큼), 조직학적으로 분할면 사이 골재생 확인.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Early description of the **split-crest** technique for horizontal ridge augmentation: longitudinal ridge division deliberately provoking a **greenstick fracture**, then chisel-spreading the buccal/lingual cortical plates.
- Combines **immediate implant placement** with the split, using the inter-cortical space for the fixtures.
- Adds **guided tissue regeneration** with expanded-PTFE membranes over implants and residual defects.
- Provides histologic evidence of bone regeneration between the two split portions — mechanistic support that the interposed gap heals with bone.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Design: clinical case series with biometric and histologic assessment.
- Population: 5 patients with adequate vertical bone height but insufficient ridge width for implants.
- Procedure: longitudinal split of the alveolar ridge into two parts (greenstick fracture) → fine chisel cut and lateral spreading of cortical plates → immediate implant placement → coverage of implants and defects with e-PTFE membranes (GTR).
- Outcomes: biometric ridge-width gain; histologic examination of regenerated bone.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Bone-width gain: **1 to 4 mm**.
- Maxillary sites showed **greater** ridge enlargement than mandibular sites.
- Histology: regeneration of bone tissue between the two portions of the split crest.
- Authors conclude the membrane-assisted split technique is effective and predictable for horizontal ridge augmentation with immediate implants.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small case series (n=5; confidence `case-report`); no control group; 1992-era e-PTFE (non-resorbable) membrane with attendant exposure/infection risk not quantified in abstract.
- Maxilla-vs-mandible difference is descriptive; no statistics.
- Abstract-only ingest (full text not retrieved) — exact follow-up duration, implant survival, and membrane-exposure rates unavailable from the abstract.

## 6. Related Work

- Foundational to later staged ridge-split refinements that predetermine the fracture line (Enislidis 2006) and to osseodensification-assisted ridge split (Guner 2025).
- Part of the GBR/GTR horizontal-augmentation lineage (barrier-membrane + space maintenance).

## 7. Glossary

- **Split-crest technique**: longitudinal division of a narrow ridge to widen it by greenstick-fracturing and spreading the cortical plates.
- **Greenstick fracture (alveolar)**: incomplete, hinged fracture of one cortical plate — the controlled goal of splitting.
- **GTR / e-PTFE membrane**: guided tissue regeneration using a non-resorbable expanded-polytetrafluoroethylene barrier to exclude soft tissue and maintain space for bone.
