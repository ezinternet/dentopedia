## Why Ingested

기존 [[wiki/veneers/sasse-2015-restoration-thickness-bonding-surface-fracture]] 에서 최소 0.7–1.0 mm 두께를 권장했으나, 0.5 mm 두께 가능성과 표면처리 방법(HF vs APF vs Monobond etch & prime)의 복합적 영향은 미탐구 상태였음. 본 in-vitro 연구(Essam 2023)는 두께 × 표면처리 교호작용을 동시에 분석해 HF 생물학적 위험 회피 근거(Monobond etch & prime 권장)를 제공한다.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro study (n=42 maxillary molars, 6 groups) of CAD/CAM lithium disilicate occlusal veneers at 0.5 mm and 1 mm thickness with three surface treatments: 1 mm + Monobond etch & prime achieved highest fracture load (1644.7 N); 0.5 mm veneers remain feasible (>962 N except APF group); Monobond etch & prime recommended over HF acid for safety.

## 한줄요약

In-vitro 연구 (n=42 대구치, 6군): CAD/CAM 리튬 디실리케이트 (Lithium Disilicate, LD) 교합면 비니어의 두께(0.5 mm vs 1 mm)와 표면처리(불산, APF, Monobond etch & prime) 교호작용 분석 — 1 mm + Monobond etch & prime군 최고 파절 하중 (Fracture Load, FL) 1644.7 N; 0.5 mm도 교합력 수준 상회하나 APF+0.5 mm군(962 N)은 이갈이 환자에 비권장; 생물학적 안전성을 고려해 Monobond etch & prime 권장.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First study comparing HF, APF, and Monobond etch & prime (all three) for their effect on fracture load of lithium disilicate occlusal veneers at 0.5 and 1 mm thickness simultaneously.
2. Demonstrated that 0.5 mm CAD/CAM lithium disilicate occlusal veneers can exceed molar masticatory force thresholds (400–800 N) for most surface treatments — validating minimally invasive use.
3. Recommended Monobond etch & prime as a safer alternative to HF acid, with equivalent or superior fracture resistance outcomes.
4. APF + 0.5 mm combination contraindicated for parafunctional patients (mean FL 962 N ≈ bruxism forces 800 N).
5. Both thickness and surface treatment significantly influenced fracture load (p<0.0001 and p=0.002 respectively); their interaction was not significant (p=0.785).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Specimens**: 42 sound maxillary molars extracted for periodontal reasons; 12±2 mm buccolingual / 10±2 mm mesiodistal; disinfected in 1% chloramine-T; embedded in epoxy resin.

**Groups** (n=7 each):
- HF-1 mm: 5% HF acid 20 s + Monobond N (universal silane)
- HF-0.5 mm: same, 0.5 mm thickness
- APF-1 mm: APF acid 2 min + Monobond N
- APF-0.5 mm: same, 0.5 mm
- MON-1 mm: Monobond etch & prime (self-etching primer, combines etching + silane)
- MON-0.5 mm: same, 0.5 mm

**Restorations**: CAD/CAM milled IPS e.max CAD (lithium disilicate, Ivoclar Vivadent) using Ceramill Map 400 scanner and CERAMILL MOTION 2 5-axis mill; Multilink N resin cement (Ivoclar Vivadent).

**Artificial aging**: Water storage 37°C 75 days + cyclic loading 240,000 cycles (49 N, 1.7 Hz, opposing natural molar) — simulating ~1 year clinically.

**Fracture test**: Universal testing machine (Instron 3345), 5 mm metal sphere on central fossa, 0.5 mm/min; first crack/fracture in Newtons.

**Failure analysis**: Binocular optical microscope + SEM fractography. Modified Al-Akhali criteria:
- Mode I: Extensive crack within veneer, no separation
- Mode II: Cohesive fracture with separation
- Mode III: Longitudinal fracture of restoration and tooth

**Statistics**: Two-way ANOVA + serial one-way ANOVAs + Post Hoc Tukey HSD; SPSS v25; significance p<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Fracture load (Mean ± SD, N)**:
| Group | Mean ± SD (N) |
|---|---|
| MON-1 mm | 1644.7 ± 155.3 (highest) |
| HF-1 mm | 1514.6 ± 212.5 |
| APF-1 mm | 1405.8 ± 172.7 |
| MON-0.5 mm | 1277.0 ± 114.0 |
| HF-0.5 mm | 1166.2 ± 211.0 |
| APF-0.5 mm | 962.0 ± 249.6 (lowest) |

**ANOVA results**:
- Veneer thickness: p<0.0001 (significant)
- Surface treatment: p=0.002 (significant)
- Interaction (thickness × surface treatment): p=0.785 (not significant)

**Pairwise significance** (same surface treatment, different thickness): HF (p=0.018), MON (p=0.011), APF (p=0.001) — 1 mm consistently higher than 0.5 mm.

**Within same thickness**: only MON-0.5 mm vs APF-0.5 mm was significant (p=0.042); MON-0.5 mm vs HF-0.5 mm not significant.

**Failure modes**: Mode I (extensive crack, no debonding) most common (27/42); Mode III (longitudinal crack) 10/42; Mode II (cohesive with debonding) 5/42. HF and MON groups = Mode I + III; APF groups = Mode II + III (separation, indicative of compromised bond).

**Clinical threshold**: Posterior masticatory forces 400–800 N; all groups except possibly APF-0.5 mm in bruxists exceed this. Bruxism forces to 800 N — APF-0.5 mm (962 N mean, SD 249.6 N) only marginally safe.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro only; does not replicate full intraoral complexity (saliva, temperature, occlusal anatomy variation)
- Small sample size per group (n=7)
- Cyclic loading simulates only ~1 year; long-term clinical follow-up needed
- Single cement type (Multilink N) — results may not generalize to all resin cements
- Only maxillary molars from periodontal patients — may not represent full patient population
- Normal masticatory forces can exceed 400–800 N; fracture resistance values should be interpreted with caution
- Authors note that HF weakening effect may be time-dependent; 20 s etching protocol used here

## 6. Related Work

- Sasse et al. (2015): 0.7–1.0 mm minimum for lithium disilicate occlusal veneers — present study extends this to 0.5 mm being feasible for most surface treatments
- Schlichting et al. (2011): Did not recommend 0.6 mm LD in patients with excessive occlusal force
- Ioannidis et al. (2019) and Maeder et al. (2019): Similar thickness results at 1,200,000 cycles
- Baldissara et al. (2019): Monobond etch & prime thickness effect — survival influenced by thickness; thicker = higher survival
- Román-Rodríguez et al. (2017) and Wille et al. (2017) and Maier et al. (2019): Bond strength of Monobond etch & prime equivalent to HF + silane
- Al-Akhali et al. (2017): Failure classification criteria used in present study

## 7. Glossary

- **Occlusal veneer**: Indirect restoration covering the occlusal surface to restore tooth wear; minimally invasive alternative to full crowns
- **TSL (Tooth Surface Loss)**: Combined attrition, abrasion, abfraction, and erosion
- **IPS e.max CAD**: CAD/CAM-milled lithium disilicate ceramic block (Ivoclar Vivadent)
- **HF acid**: Hydrofluoric acid — creates deep porous microstructure for ceramic bonding; biologically hazardous
- **APF**: Acidulated phosphate fluoride — milder etch, lower fluoride content, works more superficially on ceramic
- **Monobond etch & prime**: Self-etching primer combining ammonium polyfluoride + silane in one step; more biocompatible than HF
- **Multilink N**: Dual-cure adhesive resin cement (Ivoclar Vivadent)
- **Cyclic loading fatigue**: 240,000 cycles at 49 N / 1.7 Hz ≈ 1 year clinical simulation
- **Mode I failure**: Extensive crack within veneer without debonding (favorable — bond strength intact)
- **Mode II failure**: Cohesive fracture with debonding (indicates bond failure)
- **Mode III failure**: Longitudinal fracture of restoration + tooth (catastrophic)
