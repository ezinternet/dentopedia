## Why Ingested

Quantitative meta-analytic backing for the MMP-inhibition strategy that underpins hybrid-layer preservation: it converts the qualitative claim "collagenolytic enzymes degrade the hybrid layer over time" into a pooled effect showing chlorhexidine (CHX) preserves *aged* (but not immediate) resin-dentin bond strength. Reinforces the dentin bond-degradation mechanism described in the dentin-adhesion literature (sibling page [[wiki/resin-bonding/breschi-2025-adhesive-dentistry-evolution-review]], which frames hybrid-layer collagen breakdown and MMP-inhibitor adjuncts within the evolution of adhesive dentistry) and complements [[wiki/resin-bonding/anithakumari-2022-desensitizing-agents-bond-strength-sr]] on agents applied at the bonding interface. (Note: intended sibling `resin-bonding/liu-2011-limitations-bonding-dentin-bond-degradation-review` is not yet in the wiki — link to be added when that page is ingested.)

## One-line Summary

Systematic review + meta-analysis (740→43 studies; MA on 21 CHX articles) showing chlorhexidine (an MMP inhibitor) has no effect on immediate resin-dentin bond strength but significantly preserves aged bond strength at 6/12/24 months, with the advantage growing over time.

체계적 문헌고찰 + 메타분석 (740→43편; CHX 21편 메타분석) — 클로르헥시딘(기질금속단백분해효소 억제제, Matrix Metalloproteinase inhibitor, MMP inhibitor)은 즉시 결합강도에는 영향이 없으나 6·12·24개월 노화 후 레진-상아질 결합강도를 유의하게 보존하며, 노화가 길수록 이점이 커진다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First meta-analysis to quantitatively pool the effect of an MMP inhibitor (CHX) on resin-dentin bond strength across aging time and adhesive strategy.
- Establishes a time-dependent pattern: no benefit at baseline, significant benefit from 6 months onward, magnitude increasing with aging.
- Maps the breadth of MMP-inhibitor research (21 different inhibitors tested across 240 experimental groups) and identifies CHX as the dominant agent (32 of 43 studies).
- Provides a risk-of-bias assessment exposing systematic methodological weaknesses (no sample-size calculation; near-universal absence of operator blinding).
- Translates a laboratory mechanism (collagenolytic degradation of the hybrid layer) into a clinical recommendation: CHX can be recommended to increase resin-dentin bond longevity.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases**: PubMed + Scopus, searched to 5 July 2018.
- **Rejection criteria**: aging <6 months, thermocycling-only aging, no bond-strength data, no MMP inhibitor tested, no control group, reviews, carious teeth.
- **Screening**: 3-stage; 740 initial records → 43 accepted for full review (240 experimental groups; 21 different enzyme inhibitors).
- **Meta-analysis subset**: only 0.2–2% CHX data were pooled (heterogeneity of the other inhibitors was too wide); 21 articles allocated into comparison groups by aging time (6 / 12 / 24 months) and adhesive type (etch-and-rinse vs self-etch).
- **MMP inhibitors catalogued**: CHX, benzalkonium chloride (BAC), galardin, collagen cross-linkers (e.g., proanthocyanidin / grape-seed extract).
- **Risk of bias**: 6-item scale (randomization, intact teeth, per-manufacturer instructions, same operator, sample-size calculation, blinding) → high (1–2), medium (3–4), low (5–6).
- **Software**: MedCalc v19.2.1.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- 35/43 studies showed lower bond-strength loss with MMP inhibitors; 13 inhibitors gave ≥50% lower percentage loss vs control.
- Most common inhibitor: CHX (32 studies), then BAC (7 studies).
- **Meta-analysis (21 CHX articles):**
  - Baseline (immediate): CHX vs control — **no significant difference** (p=0.308; I²=21.7%, low heterogeneity).
  - 6 months: CHX significantly higher bond strength (**p<0.001**; I²=82.1%, high heterogeneity).
  - 12 months: CHX significantly higher (**p<0.001**; I²=75.0%, high heterogeneity).
  - 24 months: CHX significantly higher (**p<0.001**; I²=0%, extremely low heterogeneity).
- Fracture at the adhesive interface increased with aging regardless of inhibition.
- **Risk of bias**: 5/21 high, 16/21 medium, 0 low. No study reported a sample-size calculation; all but one lacked operator blinding.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Meta-analysis restricted to CHX (0.2–2%) only; the 20 other inhibitors were too heterogeneous to pool, so no quantitative conclusion for BAC, cross-linkers, or galardin.
- High statistical heterogeneity at 6 and 12 months (I²=82.1% and 75.0%) limits the precision of the pooled estimates at those timepoints.
- Overall low methodological quality: zero low-risk-of-bias studies, no sample-size calculations, near-universal lack of blinding.
- In-vitro bond-strength outcomes; no clinical (in-vivo restoration survival) endpoints.
- Authors call for more low-risk-of-bias, long-term studies.

## 7. Glossary

- **MMP (Matrix Metalloproteinase)**: host-derived collagenolytic enzymes that, once activated within demineralized dentin, degrade exposed collagen fibrils of the hybrid layer.
- **Cysteine cathepsin**: additional host-derived proteolytic enzyme contributing to collagen matrix breakdown.
- **Hybrid layer**: the resin-infiltrated demineralized dentin zone that mechanically interlocks adhesive resin with dentin collagen.
- **CHX (Chlorhexidine)**: cationic antiseptic that inhibits MMPs and cathepsins; applied as a dentin pretreatment to preserve the hybrid layer.
- **BAC (Benzalkonium chloride)**: quaternary ammonium MMP inhibitor, sometimes incorporated into etchants/primers.
- **Collagen cross-linker**: agent (e.g., proanthocyanidin / grape-seed extract) that stabilizes collagen and resists enzymatic degradation.
- **Etch-and-rinse vs self-etch**: two adhesive strategies differing in whether the smear layer is removed by a separate phosphoric-acid etch (etch-and-rinse) or modified in situ by acidic monomers (self-etch).
- **I² (heterogeneity)**: proportion of variability across studies due to true differences rather than chance.
- **Aging (6/12/24 mo)**: water/storage aging used to simulate long-term in-vivo degradation of the resin-dentin bond.
