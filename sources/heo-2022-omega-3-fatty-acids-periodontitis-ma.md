## Why Ingested

The wiki's periodontics cluster covers mechanical (SRP/GBT) and antimicrobial adjuncts ([[wiki/periodontics/smiley-2015-nonsurgical-periodontitis-srp-guideline]], [[wiki/periodontics/ramanauskaite-2020-antiseptics-adjuncts-scaling-root-planing]], [[wiki/periodontics/khattri-2020-adjunctive-systemic-antimicrobials-non-surgical-treatment]]) but held **no page on host-modulation / nutraceutical adjuncts** — the omega-3 PUFA and CoQ10 family a patient typically asks about as "gum supplements." This Heo 2022 JCP meta-analysis (13 RCTs) is the cited source behind the omega-3 claim and fills that gap. It directly contrasts with the EFP stage I–III guideline ([[wiki/periodontics/sanz-2020-treatment-stage-i-iii-periodontitis-efp]]), which issued a Grade A recommendation *against* omega-3 PUFA as an SRP adjunct on a smaller evidence base — making the guideline-vs-newer-MA conflict the key teaching point. Ingested as the omega-3 anchor of the periodontal-adjunct supplement evidence map.

## One-line Summary

Meta-analysis of 13 RCTs: omega-3 fatty acid intake (supplement or diet) as an intervention in periodontitis gave statistically significant PPD reduction (−0.44 mm), CAL gain (−0.51 mm), and BOP reduction (−9.45%); low risk of bias, no publication bias.

## 한줄요약

13편 RCT 메타분석 — 오메가-3 지방산(보충제·식이)이 치주염에서 통계적으로 유의한 치주낭 깊이 감소(−0.44 mm), 임상부착수준 획득(−0.51 mm), 탐침시 출혈 감소(−9.45%)를 보였고, 비뚤림 위험 낮고 출판 비뚤림 없음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (13 interventional RCTs).
- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Periodontology (2022;49(4):362–377).
- **Search window**: English-language studies 2010–2020; Cochrane Library, EMBASE, PubMed.
- **Population/intervention**: Periodontitis patients receiving omega-3 fatty acids — 11 trials used supplements, 2 used omega-3-rich diets.
- **Analysis**: Random-effects model; sensitivity analysis; subgroup analysis by intervention period.
- **Abstract-only — full text not retrieved (no PMC open access).**

## 2. Key Contributions

- First-line quantitative estimate of omega-3 effect size on the three core periodontal parameters (PPD, CAL, BOP).
- All included studies were RCTs → relatively strong design floor for a nutraceutical question.
- Reported low risk of bias and no publication bias — strengthens the positive signal.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Outcomes: PPD, CAL, BOP, indexed against amount of omega-3 intake.
- Effect measures: standardized mean difference (SMD) plus absolute mean difference (MD) in mm / %.
- Subgroup analyses stratified by intervention period.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Parameter | SMD | Absolute MD |
|---|---|---|
| PPD reduction | −0.81 | −0.44 mm |
| CAL gain | −0.77 | −0.51 mm |
| BOP reduction | −0.65 | −9.45% |

All statistically significant overall. Risk of bias low; no publication bias detected.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Absolute effect sizes are small (sub-millimetre) — clinical relevance is modest, host-modulation adjunct rather than a standalone therapy.
- Conflicts with the **EFP S3 stage I–III guideline (Sanz 2020)**, which recommended *against* omega-3 PUFA as a subgingival-instrumentation adjunct (Grade A) on a smaller RCT base — the guideline predates / under-weighted this MA.
- Heterogeneity in dose, formulation (EPA/DHA ratio), ± low-dose aspirin co-administration, and intervention duration.
- Abstract-only ingest; full-text subgroup/dose detail not extracted.

## 7. Glossary

- **PPD** — periodontal probing depth.
- **CAL** — clinical attachment level/loss.
- **BOP** — bleeding on probing.
- **Host modulation** — adjunctive therapy that dampens the host inflammatory response rather than targeting bacteria directly; omega-3 PUFA (via resolvins/pro-resolving mediators) is a candidate.
- **SMD** — standardized mean difference.
