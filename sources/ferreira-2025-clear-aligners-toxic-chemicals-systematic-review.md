## Why Ingested

The clear-aligner subcategory holds many efficacy and oral-health pages but no page on the **material-safety / chemical-leaching** question — whether the thermoplastic itself (BPA leaching, cytotoxicity) is a patient-safety concern. This SR fills that gap and complements [[orthodontics/clear-aligner/alhuwaizi-2026-clear-aligner-fixed-oral-hygiene-periodontal-grade-sr]] (which covers aligner oral-hygiene/periodontal effects but not chemical toxicity) and [[orthodontics/clear-aligner/thakur-2026-probiotics-clear-aligner-biofilm-rct]] (aligner biofilm/microbial side, the other half of "is wearing an aligner 20–22 h/day safe").

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (7 in-vitro/in-vivo studies, PRISMA/QUIN) concluding clear aligners are generally safe but with residual concern over thermoplastic chemical leaching (BPA below toxic limits), thermoforming-increased cytotoxicity, and moderate cytotoxicity in some materials (notably Biolon, 64.6% viability).

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(7편 in-vitro/in-vivo)로, 투명교정장치는 대체로 안전하나 열가소성 소재의 화학물질 용출(BPA는 독성 기준 이하), 열성형 시 세포독성 증가, 일부 소재(특히 Biolon 64.6% 생존율)의 중등도 세포독성이라는 잔존 우려가 있다고 결론.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Do Clear Aligners Release Toxic Chemicals?—A Systematic Review
- **Authors**: Ferreira et al.
- **Journal**: Journal of Functional Biomaterials (J Funct Biomater) 2025;16(5):173
- **DOI**: 10.3390/jfb16050173
- **PMID**: 40422837 / **PMCID**: PMC12112703
- **Study type**: Systematic review (PRISMA-guided; 7 in-vitro/in-vivo studies; no meta-analysis)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR focused **exclusively on clear aligners** (excludes retainers/expanders/other devices that prior reviews bundled in), isolating the aligner-material toxicity question.
- Synthesizes 7 cytotoxicity/biocompatibility studies into a single safety verdict: **5 of 7 concluded safe, 2 reported some cytotoxicity**.
- Ranks commercial aligner materials by cell viability and flags **thermoforming as a cytotoxicity amplifier**.
- Frames the BPA / bisphenol-derivative (BPS, BPF) leaching debate and the oral-environment modifiers (acidic pH, heat) that worsen leaching.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **PICO**: patients in orthodontic treatment (P) with clear aligners (I), changes/effects in the oral cavity (O), before vs after treatment (C).
- **Databases**: PubMed/Medline, Scopus, Cochrane. Filters: human, English/Portuguese, 2014–2024. Searched 21–22 Sept 2024.
- **Search string**: (clear aligners OR Invisalign) AND (biocompatibility OR toxicity OR biological effects).
- **Screening**: Rayyan; two independent reviewers (PL, MF), third adjudicator (AG); Cohen's Kappa for agreement.
- **Quality appraisal**: QUIN Tool (Quality Assessment Tool For In Vitro Studies, 12 items).
- **Flow**: 413 records (367 PubMed, 31 Scopus, 15 Cochrane) → 19 duplicates removed → 360 excluded on title/abstract → 34 full-text → 27 excluded → **7 included**.
- **Inclusion**: scientific research on clear aligners describing side effects; in-vitro and in-vivo; full text. **Exclusion**: case reports, systematic reviews, culture-method microbiota studies, retainers/expanders.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Cell viability / cytotoxicity (lower viability = more cytotoxic):**

| Material | Cell viability | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Duran | 84.6% | Highest viability, lowest cytotoxicity (Martina 2019) |
| SmartTrack (Invisalign) | 78.8% | (Martina 2019) |
| Zendura | 74.4% | (Martina 2019) |
| Biolon | 64.6% | **Most pronounced cytotoxicity** — caution (Martina 2019) |
| CA1 / CA2 thermoplastics | >82% | Safe per EpiDerm model (Dinu 2024) |

- **Alhendi 2022**: dose-dependent reduction in human gingival fibroblast viability across several commercial aligners (Invisalign, SureSmile) → slight-to-moderate toxicity; supports a dose–response relationship.
- **Nemec 2023**: saliva from Invisalign patients did NOT harm hGF or oral epithelial viability/proliferation and did NOT raise inflammatory mediators (unlike bracket patients), though IL-6/IL-8/MCP-1 rose in aligner-conditioned-saliva cell exposure with minimal before/after difference.
- **Yu 2022 / Yan 2024**: low cytotoxicity, preserved cell morphology (TPU; fluoride-coated antibacterial/remineralizing aligner respectively).
- **Thermoforming** significantly increases cytotoxicity, especially in TPU and Zendura.
- **BPA**: detected but generally within regulatory safety limits; long-term effects unclear for adolescents/pregnant women; BPA-free substitutes (BPS, BPF) may carry similar risk.
- **Bottom line**: generally safe; residual concerns = thermoplastic chemical leaching, bacterial accumulation from reduced saliva flow, mild inflammatory responses.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only **7 studies**, mostly in-vitro/in-vivo lab work — no clinical-outcome trials, no meta-analysis possible.
- Two included studies (Yu 2022, Yan 2024) had lower QUIN quality.
- Heterogeneous materials/methods preclude pooled estimates.
- Long-term and systemic effects of cumulative leachate exposure unquantified; calls for standardized toxicity-testing protocols and patient-specific factor studies (oral pH, saliva composition, hygiene).

## 6. Related Work

- Martina et al. 2019 (thermoforming cytotoxicity of Duran/Biolon/Zendura) — core data source.
- Alhendi 2022; Nemec 2023; Dinu 2024; Lo 2024; Yu 2022; Yan 2024 — the seven included primary studies.
- Katras et al. 2021 (BPA release below toxic levels); Ryokawa et al. (thermoplastic inflammatory response in hGFs).

## 7. Glossary

- **PET-G**: polyethylene terephthalate glycol — common aligner thermoplastic.
- **TPU**: thermoplastic polyurethane — common aligner material; thermoforming raises its cytotoxicity.
- **BPA / BPS / BPF**: bisphenol A and its substitutes; endocrine disruptors with estrogenic activity.
- **Thermoforming**: heat-molding of aligner sheet over a model; increases monomer leaching/cytotoxicity.
- **QUIN Tool**: 12-item quality-assessment tool for in-vitro dental studies.
- **hGF**: human gingival fibroblast — common cytotoxicity assay cell.
- **EpiDerm**: reconstructed-human-epidermis 3D model for irritation/cytotoxicity testing.
