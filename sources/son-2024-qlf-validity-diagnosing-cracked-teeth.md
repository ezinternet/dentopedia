## Why Ingested

[[cracked-tooth/raj-2025-cracked-tooth-syndrome-diagnostic-dilemma]] frames crack diagnosis as a clinical dilemma with no reliable global standard — this prospective STARD study (Son 2024) supplies hard validity/reliability numbers for one optical adjunct (Quantitative Light-induced Fluorescence, QLF), showing the 405 nm fluorescent channel beats natural-color imaging for localizing crack lines. It extends the diagnostic discussion in [[cracked-tooth/li-2021-cracked-tooth-syndrome-etiology-review]] with device-level evidence (kappa vs reference standard).

## One-line Summary

Prospective STARD diagnostic-accuracy study (26 cracked molars, 2 examiners): QLF fluorescent images localized crack lines with substantial agreement vs reference standard (kappa F-R 0.662, combination C-R 0.742), far better than natural-color images alone (N-R 0.164); inter-examiner kappa moderate for fluorescent/combination (0.449/0.493) vs fair for natural color (0.394).

## 한줄요약

전향적 STARD 진단정확도 연구 (균열치 26개, 평가자 2명): QLF 형광영상(405 nm)은 균열선 위치를 기준표준 대비 substantial 일치도로 검출(kappa 형광 0.662, 복합 0.742)했고, 자연색 영상 단독(0.164)보다 훨씬 우수. 평가자 간 일치도는 형광·복합 영상이 moderate(0.449/0.493), 자연색은 fair(0.394).

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Investigation of validity and inter examiner agreement of quantitative light induced fluorescent images in diagnosing cracked teeth
- **Authors**: Yulee Son et al.
- **Journal**: Scientific Reports
- **Year**: 2024 (published 2024-12-30)
- **DOI**: 10.1038/s41598-024-83803-8
- **PMID**: 39738743 | **PMCID**: PMC11685572
- **Study type**: Prospective clinical diagnostic-accuracy / reliability study (STARD 2015), n = 26 cracked teeth
- **Setting**: Department of Conservative Dentistry, Pusan National University dental hospital (IRB PNUDH-2021-006-MD)
- **Device**: Q-ray penC (AIOBIO, Seoul, Korea) — intraoral capture-type QLF, 405 nm blue light, single-shot natural-color + fluorescent images

## 2. Key Contributions

- First prospective clinical study (per authors) quantifying both **validity** (agreement vs reference standard) and **inter-examiner reliability** of QLF imaging for **localizing crack lines** in cracked teeth — not merely detecting crack presence.
- Establishes a head-to-head comparison of three image modes from a single device: **natural color**, **fluorescent (405 nm)**, and **combination** (both shown simultaneously).
- Reference standard built from QLF images taken **after** removing crack lines / old restorations by an experienced dentist, anchoring crack location against a post-removal internal view.
- Demonstrates the **405 nm fluorescent channel** is the operative element: it localizes cracks via red autofluorescence (microbial metabolites in micro-gaps) and light transmission, overcoming saliva/fog/reflection artifacts that degrade natural-color reading.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Recruitment**: 81 teeth / 59 patients screened → 37 diagnosed with cracked tooth or crack-related pulpitis (microscopy + bite test + pulp vitality hot/cold + radiographs) → **26 teeth finally selected** (those requiring restoration removal or root canal treatment).
- **Imaging**: Q-ray penC per manufacturer manual; natural-color + fluorescent images per tooth before treatment, plus interior QLF images after removing superficial cracks/old restorations. A W block aided mouth opening (no rubber dam).
- **Reference standard**: experienced dentist recorded crack locations across **five occlusal zones** (central, mesial, distal, buccal, lingual) by comparing before/after QLF images, distinguishing cracks from anatomical grooves and craze lines.
- **Examiners**: two first-year Conservative Dentistry residents, pre-trained on QLF capture principles and interpretation. Each independently read natural-color, fluorescent, and combination images (one-day intervals), marking crack presence per zone; then discussed to derive a final reading per mode.
- **Analysis**: Cohen kappa for (a) inter-examiner agreement A vs B per mode (each n=26) and (b) final reading vs reference standard — **N-R** (natural color), **F-R** (fluorescent), **C-R** (combination). False readings sub-classified as **over- or under-estimated** by crack count. Cross-analysis by restoration material and presence. SPSS v20.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Sample**: 57.7% female; modal age 50–59 (42.3%); maxillary first molar most common (11/26). Restorations: gold 38.5%, composite 23.1%, amalgam 9.2%, none 15.4% (4 teeth). Class I 53.8%, Class II 30.8%. Pulp dx: irreversible pulpitis 69.2%, necrosis 19.2%, reversible pulpitis 11.5%.

**Inter-examiner agreement (kappa)**:
- Natural color: **0.394** (fair, lowest)
- Fluorescent: **0.449** (moderate)
- Combination: **0.493** (moderate, highest)

**Agreement vs reference standard (kappa)**:
- C-R (combination): **0.742** — substantial, highest
- F-R (fluorescent): **0.662** — substantial
- N-R (natural color): **0.164** — slight, lowest

**By crack location**: N-R worst for lingual cracks (0.283), best for mesial (0.615); F-R and C-R both 0.923 for mesial cracks; C-R perfect (1.000) for lingual cracks.

**True/false by mode**: N-R 6/26 true (20 false; 19 underestimated — gold worst, 7 underestimations). F-R 18/26 true (false skewed to overestimation, 3:5; fluorescent over-reads cracks). C-R 20/26 true (gold highest true count; among false, 4 underestimated vs 2 overestimated).

**By restoration presence**: N-R judged **all 4 unrestored teeth false (100%)**; F-R judged all 4 unrestored teeth **true (100%)**; C-R 75% true for unrestored. Crack lines were confused with anatomical grooves in unrestored cases on natural-color reading.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample (**n = 26 teeth**) limits statistical power of the agreement estimates.
- No rubber dam; intraoral fog and imperfect/variable shooting angles (patient oral condition) introduce capture variability.
- Q-ray penC natural-color resolution lower than a DSLR, partly explaining poor natural-color performance.
- Examiners were first-year residents (limited experience), and the reference standard itself is QLF-based (not histology/microscopy of sectioned teeth), so it reflects optical, not gold-standard structural, crack mapping.
- Restoration material/type confounds reading (gold reflection inflates natural-color error and fluorescent overestimation) — future work should stratify by material with larger n.

## 6. Related Work

- Builds on prior reports that QLF detects crack lines on tooth surfaces, around restorations, and within cavities after restoration removal.
- Consistent with literature linking cracked teeth to Class I restorations and non-bonded restorative materials.
- Complements transillumination, microscopy, intraoral scanners, and CBCT as non-invasive crack-diagnosis adjuncts discussed by the AAE recommended workup (interview → visual/bite/probing/vitality tests).

## 7. Glossary

- **QLF (Quantitative Light-induced Fluorescence)**: optical method using ~405 nm blue light; loss of fluorescence + red autofluorescence reveal demineralization, microbial activity, and crack micro-gaps.
- **Red autofluorescence**: fluorescent emission from porphyrin metabolites of bacteria penetrating crack micro-gaps; marks crack location.
- **Reference standard**: independently established "truth" for crack location (here, post-removal internal QLF images read by an experienced dentist).
- **Cohen kappa (κ)**: chance-corrected agreement; ≤0 none, 0.01–0.20 slight, 0.21–0.40 fair, 0.41–0.60 moderate, 0.61–0.80 substantial, 0.81–1.00 almost perfect.
- **N-R / F-R / C-R**: agreement of Natural-color / Fluorescent / Combination image reading vs Reference standard.
- **STARD 2015**: Standards for Reporting of Diagnostic Accuracy Studies reporting guideline.
- **Over-/under-estimated**: false reading reporting more / fewer crack lines than the reference standard.
