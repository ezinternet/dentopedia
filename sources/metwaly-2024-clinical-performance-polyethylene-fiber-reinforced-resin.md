## Why Ingested

[[wiki/resin/boaro-2019-bulk-fill-composites-clinical-properties-sr-ma]] synthesized bulk-fill composite clinical/physical properties but lacked RCT data on bulk-fill performance specifically in structurally compromised endodontically treated molars, and on whether polyethylene-fiber reinforcement adds clinical benefit. This 2024 24-month RCT (n=240) reinforces the anchor by showing bulk-fill restorations perform well in ETT molars and that fiber reinforcement confers no detectable USPHS advantage.

## One-line Summary

24-month double-blind RCT (n=240 ETT lower molars): polyethylene-fiber-reinforced bulk-fill vs plain bulk-fill resin composite showed no significant difference on any modified USPHS parameter, with Alpha scores for retention, gross fracture, and secondary caries at all intervals.

## 한줄요약

24개월 이중맹검 RCT(n=240 근관치료 하악대구치): 폴리에틸렌섬유 강화 벌크필 vs 일반 벌크필 복합레진은 모든 modified USPHS 항목에서 유의차 없었고, 유지·파절·이차우식에서 전 시점 Alpha 점수 유지.

## 1. Document Information

- Type: Randomized controlled clinical trial (double-blind, two parallel groups, 1:1)
- Published: BMC Oral Health 2024;24:1285
- DOI: 10.1186/s12903-024-05009-8
- Registration: ClinicalTrials.gov NCT05180903 (registered 6 Jan 2022)
- Setting: Conservative Dentistry clinic, Faculty of Dentistry, Cairo University, Egypt (Jan 2022–May 2024)
- Sample: 240 participants (18–55 y) with endodontically treated lower molars, Class II cavities, moderate remaining tooth structure (≥2 mm wall thickness)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides RCT-level clinical data on bulk-fill composite in endodontically treated molars — a structurally high-risk indication.
- Tests whether polyethylene-fiber reinforcement (e.g., Ribbond-type) improves clinical outcomes over plain bulk-fill.
- Finds no clinical benefit of fiber reinforcement over a 2-year horizon, supporting simpler bulk-fill placement.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Two equal groups of 120: (1) polyethylene-fiber-reinforced bulk-fill resin composite; (2) plain bulk-fill resin composite. Both per manufacturer guidelines.
- Two experienced blinded assessors scored restorations with modified USPHS criteria at baseline, 6, 12, and 24 months.
- Statistics: Mann-Whitney U, Friedman test, Nemenyi post hoc; α=0.05; R software.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- No notable distinctions in any parameter or score between groups at any time point.
- Alpha scores recorded for retention, gross fracture, and secondary caries at all follow-up intervals.
- Both restoration types demonstrated satisfactory clinical outcomes over 24 months.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- 24-month follow-up only — short for restoration longevity conclusions.
- Single center, single material system per arm.
- Both arms are bulk-fill (the comparison is fiber vs no fiber, not incremental vs bulk-fill).
- Moderate-tooth-structure inclusion limits extrapolation to severely compromised teeth.

## 6. Related Work

- Boaro 2019 SR+MA — bulk-fill composite clinical/physical properties.
- 2021 in vitro SR on high-molecular-weight polyethylene fibers and fracture resistance in ETT.

## 7. Glossary

- ETT: endodontically treated teeth.
- Bulk-fill resin composite: composite placeable in increments up to ~4–5 mm with adequate cure.
- USPHS criteria (modified): United States Public Health Service clinical evaluation scoring (Alpha = ideal).
- Polyethylene fiber reinforcement: bondable high-modulus fiber ribbon embedded to improve composite toughness.
