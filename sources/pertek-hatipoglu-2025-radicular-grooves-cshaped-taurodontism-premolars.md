## One-line Summary

Multicenter cross-sectional study with meta-analytic pooling (20 countries, 6,000 participants / 12,000 bilateral mandibular first premolars, CBCT): C-shaped canal global prevalence 10% (95% CI 7–12%; Asia 19%, Europe 3%), radicular grooves 20%, taurodontism 7%; all three show strong bilateral symmetry and inter-correlation (shared developmental pathways).

## 한줄요약

다기관 단면연구+메타분석(20개국, 6,000명/12,000 양측 하악 제1소구치, CBCT): C-shaped 근관 전세계 유병률 10%(95% CI 7–12%; 아시아 19%·예멘 31% 최고, 유럽 3% 최저), radicular groove 20%, taurodontism 7% — 세 변이 모두 강한 양측 대칭성·상호 상관(공유 발생 경로)을 보임. I²=98%로 지역간 이질성 큼.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Largest standardized multinational CBCT dataset on mandibular first premolar anatomical variation: 6,000 participants, 12,000 bilateral teeth, 20 countries on 6 continents.
- Quantifies three clinically relevant variations together — radicular grooves (RGs), C-shaped canals, taurodontism — in the same population, enabling co-occurrence/correlation analysis.
- Pooled global prevalences with regional stratification and bilateral-symmetry analysis.
- Standardized AAE/AAOMR evaluation protocol + intra/inter-observer reliability across 20 observers.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Retrospective evaluation of previously acquired CBCT datasets, standardized protocol across all centers (simultaneous participation).
- Inclusion: bilateral mandibular first premolars present, no prior ortho/major craniofacial abnormality, written informed consent. Exclusion: missing bilateral premolars, open apices, resorption, prior RCT, crowns/coronal restorations, deep caries/periapical lesions/pyramidal roots, syndromes affecting dental anatomy (e.g., cleidocranial dysplasia).
- Outcomes: prevalence of RGs, C-shaped canals, taurodontism; stratified by region, gender, age.
- Statistics: chi-square then Cramer's V (association strength, robust in large n), subgroup/correlation/sensitivity analyses (outlier-country exclusion). Random-effects pooling.
- Reliability: intra-observer Cohen's kappa 0.59–0.87 (highest for C-shaped); inter-observer Fleiss' kappa RG 0.76 / taurodontism 0.72 / C-shaped 0.78 (substantial).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **C-shaped canal global prevalence: 0.10 (95% CI 0.07–0.12)**. Regional pooled: Africa 0.05 (Libya highest 0.06), **Asia 0.19 (95% CI 0.10–0.27; Yemen highest 0.31)**, Australia 0.12, Europe 0.03, North America 0.07, South America 0.09 (wide CI −0.05–0.23). Heterogeneity I²=98%; sensitivity analysis stable at ~12%.
- **Radicular grooves global prevalence: ~20%**, highest in Africa.
- **Taurodontism global prevalence: ~7%** (lowest of the three), marked regional variability.
- **Bilateral symmetry**: prominent for all three features; taurodontism highest bilateral rate (98.33%).
- **Inter-correlation** among C-shaped canals, RGs, and taurodontism — interpreted as shared developmental pathways.
- Demographics: female 55.2%, age peak 20–30 y (25.7%).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Very high heterogeneity (I²=98%) across countries — geographic/demographic/methodological differences; pooled global estimate must be read with regional context.
- Single study only for some regions (e.g., Australia n=1) → unstable regional estimates; South America CI crosses zero.
- Retrospective CBCT datasets, restricted to mandibular first premolars (not generalizable to molars where C-shaped is most common — see molar-focused pages).
- Authors call for population-specific imaging-based approaches.

## 7. Glossary

- **C-shaped canal**: ribbon-like canal with fins/isthmuses forming a "C" in cross-section; complicates shaping/obturation.
- **Radicular groove (RG)**: developmental groove on the root surface, a periodontal/endodontic risk.
- **Taurodontism**: enlarged pulp chamber with apically displaced furcation ("bull-like" tooth).
- **Cramer's V**: effect-size measure of association (0–1), robust at large sample sizes.
