## One-line Summary
Systematic review finding that platelet-rich fibrin (PRF) significantly reduces alveolar osteitis (dry socket), postoperative edema, restricted mouth opening, and pain following tooth extraction.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health 2025;25:869
- **DOI**: 10.1186/s12903-025-06238-1
- **Type**: Systematic Review
- **Year**: 2025

## 2. Key Contributions
- Evaluates clinical efficacy of PRF in preventing or reducing post-extraction complications: alveolar osteitis (dry socket), edema, trismus (restricted mouth opening), and pain
- Provides evidence synthesis from randomized controlled trials and controlled clinical trials on PRF use in extraction sockets
- Demonstrates that PRF, as a second-generation platelet concentrate, offers advantages over earlier concentrations (PRP) due to simplified preparation and autologous nature

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Systematic review following PRISMA guidelines
- Literature search in major databases (PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, Cochrane, etc.)
- Included RCTs and controlled studies comparing PRF placement in extraction socket vs. control (no PRF or conventional treatment)
- Outcomes assessed: incidence of alveolar osteitis, postoperative pain (VAS), edema (facial measurements), mouth opening (interincisal distance), and wound healing
- Risk of bias assessed using appropriate tools (e.g., Cochrane RoB tool)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Alveolar osteitis**: PRF significantly reduced the incidence of dry socket compared to control groups
- **Pain**: Postoperative pain scores were lower in PRF groups across multiple time points (24h, 48h, 72h, 7 days)
- **Edema**: Facial swelling was measurably reduced in patients receiving PRF
- **Trismus**: Mouth opening restriction was less severe and resolved faster with PRF
- **Wound healing**: PRF promoted faster soft tissue closure and socket healing
- PRF placement appears most beneficial in high-risk extractions (impacted third molars, multiple extractions)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Heterogeneity in PRF preparation protocols across studies (centrifugation speed, duration)
- Variability in outcome measurement methods and follow-up periods
- Some studies had small sample sizes or short follow-up
- Need for standardized PRF preparation protocols and longer-term RCTs

## 6. Related Work
- Related to literature on PRP (first-generation platelet concentrates) for socket preservation
- Connects to third molar surgery complication management literature
- Builds on autologous platelet concentrate research (Choukroun PRF protocol)

## 7. Glossary
- **PRF (Platelet-Rich Fibrin)**: Second-generation autologous platelet concentrate prepared by centrifuging patient's blood; contains growth factors (PDGF, TGF-β, VEGF) within a fibrin matrix
- **Alveolar Osteitis (Dry Socket)**: Post-extraction complication characterized by loss of blood clot, exposed bone, and severe pain; incidence ~2–5% overall, higher for mandibular third molars
- **Trismus**: Restricted mouth opening due to muscle spasm or edema following oral surgery
- **VAS**: Visual Analog Scale for pain measurement
- **PRP**: Platelet-Rich Plasma, first-generation platelet concentrate requiring two-step centrifugation and anticoagulants
