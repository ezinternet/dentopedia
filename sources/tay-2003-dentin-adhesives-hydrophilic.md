## One-line Summary
Narrative review (Tay & Pashley 2003): simplified self-etch and one-bottle adhesives are overly hydrophilic → water permeability → water blisters, bond degradation, incompatibility with dual-cure composites; 3-step systems remain gold standard.

## 1. Document Information
Georgia Health Sciences University (then Medical College of Georgia). JCDA 2003. Key conceptual paper by two of the most cited researchers in dentin adhesion science. Peer-reviewed clinical practice article.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Coined "too hydrophilic" framework for understanding simplified adhesive failure
- HEMA addition made adhesives moisture-tolerant but introduced water permeability problems
- Simplified adhesives (2-step single-bottle, 1-step self-etch) are more hydrophilic → more water absorption
- Water from underlying dentin permeates through polymerized adhesive layer → blisters, degradation
- Chemical/dual-cure composites incompatible with simplified acidic adhesives (tertiary amine inhibition)
- 3-step etch-and-rinse systems remain most durable/sealed

## 3. Methodology
Narrative review of published literature. No systematic search. Conceptual synthesis with clinical illustrations and SEM images.

## 4. Key Results
- Total-etch adhesives: technique-sensitive (wet bonding); postoperative sensitivity from water blisters
- Self-etching primers (2-step SE): more hydrophilic than 3-step TE systems
- 1-step self-etch: most hydrophilic → most permeable → fastest degradation
- Self-etch "ionized resin monomers" etch through smear layer but leave it as hybrid layer component
- Clinical implication: the more simplified the adhesive, the more compromised the seal

## 5. Limitations
- Narrative review — no systematic search
- 2003 — does not cover modern universal adhesives or MDP monomer generation
- Some conclusions from in vitro data extrapolated to clinical claims

## 6. Related Work
- Daneshkazemi 2013 — microTBS of Clearfil SE Bond after mechanical/thermal cycling
- Mortazavi 2012 — clinical RCT: ethanol wet bonding vs SE Bond
- Sharifian 2023 — µTBS comparison E&R, SE, and universal modes to dentin depth

## 7. Glossary
- **HEMA**: 2-hydroxyethyl methacrylate (hydrophilic monomer)
- **Wet bonding**: keeping acid-etched dentin moist for collagen matrix preservation
- **Hybrid layer**: resin-infiltrated demineralized dentin zone
- **Water blister**: fluid entrapment at adhesive interface from dentinal fluid permeation
- **3-step TE**: primer + adhesive applied separately after phosphoric acid etching (most sealed)
