## One-line Summary
Two-center retrospective case series evaluating the reamer-drilling lateral window approach for sinus augmentation: overall Schneiderian membrane perforation rate **12.5%**; thinner lateral walls (<1.25 mm) trended toward higher perforation; reamer drilling is concluded to be safe and effective when lateral wall thickness is ≥1.25 mm.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: International Journal of Periodontics & Restorative Dentistry 2016;36(4):548–556 (PubMed 27333013)
- **Institutions**: University of Michigan (USA), University of Granada (Spain), Hospital Universitario Infanta Cristina Badajoz (Spain)
- **Study type**: Retrospective double-center case series (in-vivo clinical)

## 2. Key Contributions
- First focused evaluation of the **reamer drilling lateral window technique** vs traditional piezo / rotary bur osteotomy.
- Quantifies perforation rate (12.5%) for this technique under routine clinical conditions across two centers.
- Identifies **lateral wall thickness <1.25 mm** as a clinically relevant cutoff increasing perforation risk.
- Provides anatomical and procedural risk factor analysis (Schneiderian membrane thickness, septa, sinus floor angulation).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Two-center retrospective case series of consecutive patients undergoing lateral window sinus augmentation with reamer drill.
- Inclusion: patients with adequate CBCT and lateral window planned for sinus augmentation in posterior maxilla.
- Procedure: lateral window prepared with a dedicated reamer drill rather than diamond bur or piezotome.
- Outcome: presence/absence and size of Schneiderian membrane perforation, intraoperatively recorded.
- Co-variables analyzed: lateral wall thickness, sinus membrane thickness, presence of septa, residual bone height, surgeon experience.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Overall Schneiderian membrane perforation rate**: 12.5% (lower than historical bur/piezo rates of 20–30%).
- **Lateral wall thickness <1.25 mm**: higher perforation rate (slightly elevated; cutoff identified).
- **Lateral wall thickness ≥1.25 mm**: reamer drilling safe and effective.
- No statistically significant association between membrane thickness alone and perforation in this series.
- Septa noted as an independent risk factor (consistent with prior literature).
- Authors conclude reamer drilling is a reliable alternative for lateral window opening.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Retrospective design — no randomization vs alternative techniques.
- Sample size moderate; perforation event count limits multivariate analysis power.
- No long-term implant survival data linked to perforation event.
- Authors recommend prospective comparative trials (reamer vs piezo vs bur).

## 6. Related Work
- Wallace 2005 — piezosurgery reduces perforation rate.
- Pjetursson 2008 — meta-analysis perforation rate 10–20%.
- Yang 2024 — septa and lateral wall thickness as perforation risk factors (SR+MA).
- Diaz-Olivares 2021 — perforation management outcomes.

## 7. Glossary
- **Reamer drill**: dedicated bone-cutting bur with internal cooling and trephine-like edge geometry, designed to cut bone while sparing soft tissue.
- **Lateral window approach (LWA)**: classical sinus elevation technique creating an osteotomy in the lateral wall to access and lift the Schneiderian membrane.
- **Lateral wall thickness**: CBCT-measured thickness of the antrolateral maxillary bone at the planned osteotomy site.
- **Schneiderian membrane perforation (SMP)**: most common intraoperative complication of sinus augmentation.
