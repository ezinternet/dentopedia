## One-line Summary
치주지지치료(Supportive Periodontal Therapy, SPT) 후 질환 진행 SR (JPIS 2019): 17편 14연구 964명, 5–15년 추적; 10/14 연구에서 CAL 손실 ≤0.5mm; 흡연·구강위생불량·SPT 불순응이 주요 예측인자.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Periodontal & Implant Science 2019, 49(2): 60–75
- **DOI**: 10.5051/jpis.2019.49.2.60
- **Study type**: Systematic review

## 2. Key Contributions
- Assessed periodontal disease progression after active treatment in patients receiving SPT
- Focused question: In adult treated-periodontitis patients, what is disease progression (CAL loss) over ≥5 years of SPT?
- 17 publications from 14 independent investigations; 964 patients; follow-up 5–15 years
- 10/14 studies: mean CAL loss ≤0.5 mm — generally stable disease in compliant SPT patients
- 3 studies: mean CAL loss 0.5–1.0 mm; 4 studies: >1.0 mm (outliers with poor compliance/smoking)
- Sites with CAL loss ≥2 mm: 3–20% range; associated with poor oral hygiene, smoking, poor SPT compliance

## 3. Methodology
- **Databases**: Not explicitly stated (systematic search)
- **Inclusion**: RCTs, prospective cohorts, longitudinal observational studies; ≥5-year follow-up post-treatment; reported CAL and PD changes; surgical or non-surgical treatment
- **Exclusion**: Studies <5 years follow-up; no SPT during follow-up
- **Outcomes**: CAL change from end of active treatment to latest follow-up; PD change; predictors of disease progression

## 4. Key Results
| CAL Loss Category | Number of Studies |
|---|---|
| ≤0.5 mm (stable) | 10/14 |
| 0.5–1.0 mm (moderate progression) | 3/14 |
| >1.0 mm (significant progression) | 4/14 (overlap possible across studies) |

Sites with CAL loss ≥2 mm: 3–20% across studies

**Predictors of progression**: poor oral hygiene, smoking, poor SPT compliance

## 5. Limitations
- Heterogeneous treatment protocols (surgical vs non-surgical); SPT intervals varied
- Patient baseline severity not standardized; mixing of periodontitis stages
- No meta-analysis; qualitative synthesis
