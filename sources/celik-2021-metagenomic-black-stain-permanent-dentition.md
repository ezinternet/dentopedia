## Why Ingested

One of the first NGS/metagenomic studies of black stain in adult permanent dentition — fills a critical gap in [[caries/zyla-2015-black-stain-dental-caries-review]] and [[caries/al-shareef-2025-black-stain-bacterial-origin-review]], which rely mainly on culture-based studies predominantly in children. Provides genus-level microbiome composition data for 4 BS×caries groups, directly supporting the complex-community (non-Actinomyces-only) and caries-protection hypotheses.

## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional NGS study (n=52 adults, 4 groups): Capnocytophaga, Neisseria, Corynebacterium dominate black stain plaque in permanent dentition; BS+ patients had significantly fewer carious teeth (p=0.007).

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Archives of Oral Biology
- **Year**: 2021
- **Study type**: Cross-sectional, metagenomic (next-generation sequencing)
- **PMID**: 34058724
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.archoralbio.2021.105171
- **Sample**: n=52 adults, permanent dentition, 4 groups

## 2. Key Contributions

- Among the first studies applying NGS/metagenomic sequencing to black stain biofilm in the adult permanent dentition (most prior studies used culture in children or mixed dentition)
- Documents complex multi-genus microbial community in BS+ plaque — Actinomyces NOT the only or necessarily dominant genus; Capnocytophaga, Neisseria, Corynebacterium emerge as highly abundant
- Confirms inverse BS–caries association in adults using ICDAS II standardized caries classification (p=0.007)
- Identifies genus signature specific to BS+/caries-free subgroup: Capnocytophaga (10.8%), Cardiobacterium (3.6%), Rothia (1.72%)
- Demonstrates that BS+ plaque microbial structure differs not only from BS− but also within BS+ between carious and caries-free patients

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Cross-sectional, 4-group parallel
- **Subjects**: 52 adults; groups — BS+/caries-free (n=13), BS+/carious (n=13), BS−/caries-free (n=13), BS−/carious (n=13)
- **Caries classification**: ICDAS II (International Caries Detection and Assessment System)
- **Microbial method**: Next-generation sequencing (NGS) / 16S rRNA metagenomic analysis of plaque samples
- **Statistical analysis**: Comparison of caries-free teeth counts across BS+/BS− groups; abundance comparisons (p<0.05 threshold)
- **Setting**: Turkey; adult permanent dentition exclusively

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Caries outcomes:**

| Group comparison | Finding |
|---|---|
| BS+ vs BS− | BS+ patients had significantly more caries-free teeth (p=0.007) |

**Microbial abundance — all BS+ plaques (carious + caries-free combined):**

| Genus | Relative abundance | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| Neisseria | 5.4% | p<0.05 |
| Capnocytophaga | 4.8% | p<0.05 |
| Corynebacterium | 3.9% | p<0.05 |

**Microbial abundance — BS+/caries-FREE patients specifically:**

| Genus | Relative abundance | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| Capnocytophaga | 10.8% | p<0.05 |
| Cardiobacterium | 3.6% | p<0.05 |
| Rothia | 1.72% | p<0.05 |

- Complex multi-genus structure in BS+ biofilm — not reducible to Actinomyces alone
- Genus-level differences between BS+/caries-free and BS+/carious suggest microbiome sub-structure within black stain may influence caries risk

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample per group (n=13); limits statistical power and generalizability
- Cross-sectional design: cannot determine if BS microbiome causes reduced caries or vice versa
- 16S rRNA sequencing provides genus-level resolution; species- and strain-level assignments require deeper sequencing or whole-genome approaches
- No salivary chemistry data — cannot correlate microbial findings with salivary iron/pH changes documented elsewhere
- Adult permanent dentition only; cannot be extrapolated directly to primary dentition (most prior literature)
- Turkish cohort only — geographic/dietary/genetic confounders not controlled

## 7. Glossary

- **Black stain (BS)**: External cervical tooth discoloration of bacterial origin; dark lines/spots
- **ICDAS II**: International Caries Detection and Assessment System — validated standardized scoring system for caries at multiple severity levels
- **NGS (Next-Generation Sequencing)**: High-throughput DNA sequencing enabling culture-independent metagenomic microbiome profiling
- **Capnocytophaga**: Gram-negative, capnophilic oral bacterium; elevated in BS+/caries-free biofilm; may produce antimicrobial bacteriocins
- **Cardiobacterium**: Gram-negative oral commensal; member of HACEK group; elevated in BS+/caries-free plaque
- **Rothia**: Gram-positive oral bacterium; associated with oral health; elevated in BS+/caries-free patients
- **Neisseria**: Gram-negative oral commensal; elevated in all BS+ plaque groups
- **Corynebacterium**: Gram-positive actinobacterium; elevated in BS+ plaque
- **16S rRNA**: Ribosomal RNA gene used as phylogenetic marker in bacterial identification
