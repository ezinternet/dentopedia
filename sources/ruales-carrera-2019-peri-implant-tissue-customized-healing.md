## Why Ingested

맞춤형 치유 지대주 (Customized Healing Abutment)를 즉시 식립 후 연조직 외형 보존 수단으로 사용하는 임상 케이스를 제공하며, [[immediate-implant/esthetic-soft-tissue]] 카테고리의 연조직 관리 옵션(임시 보철 없이 연조직 외형 유지)을 보강하는 임상 기법 근거로 활용.

## One-line Summary

Clinical case series (2019) demonstrating a chairside customized healing abutment technique for immediate implants to preserve peri-implant soft tissue contours without provisional restorations.

## 한줄요약

즉시 임플란트 식립 후 임시 보철 없이 맞춤형 치유 지대주를 이용해 연조직 외형을 보존하는 간소화된 임상 기법을 소개한 케이스 시리즈 (2019).

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of Esthetic and Restorative Dentistry, Volume 31, Issue 6, November/December 2019, Pages 533–541
- **DOI**: 10.1111/jerd.12512
- **Study type**: Clinical case report / technique article
- **First published**: 03 July 2019

## 2. Key Contributions

- Describes a simplified chairside protocol for fabricating customized healing abutments (CHA) at the time of immediate implant placement
- Demonstrates the technique in both anterior (esthetic) and posterior zones
- Proposes CHA as an alternative when immediate loading/provisionalization is not indicated or possible
- Eliminates the need for reopening surgery by maintaining the soft tissue tunnel/contour from extraction onward
- Reduces total clinical steps compared to conventional healing abutment + provisional restoration sequencing

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Study type: Clinical case series / technique description
- Material: PEEK (polyether ether ketone) blocks or PMMA used to mill customized abutments; titanium insert base
- Technique: Chairside fabrication or CAD/CAM milled customized healing abutment shaped to replicate the extracted tooth's emergence profile (critical and subcritical contour zones per Su et al.)
- Timing: Placed at the time of immediate implant insertion (Type 1 placement)
- Cases presented include anterior maxillary and posterior sites
- Key concepts applied: dual-zone therapeutic concept (Chu et al.), critical/subcritical contour (Su et al.)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Customized healing abutments successfully maintained soft tissue architecture (gingival tunnel / emergence profile contour) from day of extraction through prosthetic delivery
- No reopening surgery required
- Natural emergence profile achieved at final restoration delivery
- Reduction in overall treatment steps and number of clinical appointments
- Patient-reported satisfaction noted (qualitative)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Case series without control group; no quantitative soft tissue measurements reported (PES, recession depth, papilla score)
- Short follow-up (prosthetic delivery endpoint, no long-term data)
- Small number of cases presented
- Randomized controlled trials or prospective studies with pink esthetic score (PES) outcomes needed

## 6. Related Work

- Araujo & Lindhe (2005): dimensional ridge changes post-extraction (foundational reference)
- Chu et al. (2012): dual-zone therapeutic concept for immediate implant + provisional
- Su et al. (2010): critical and subcritical contour concept for abutment/crown design
- Slagter et al. (2014): SR of immediate placement in esthetic zone
- Alshhrani & Al Amri (2016): CAD-CAM customized healing abutment for delayed loading
- Proussaefs (2016): CAD/CAM healing abutment for non-esthetic zone guided soft tissue healing

## 7. Glossary

- **Customized Healing Abutment (CHA)**: A patient-specific healing abutment shaped to replicate the emergence profile of the extracted tooth, guiding soft tissue healing without provisional crowns
- **Critical contour**: The supragingival zone of the crown/abutment that shapes the gingival margin height and position
- **Subcritical contour**: The subgingival zone that determines soft tissue tunnel shape and papilla support
- **Dual-zone therapeutic concept**: Management of implant socket in two zones — hard tissue (apical/palatal) and soft tissue (facial/coronal)
- **PEEK**: Polyether ether ketone — high-performance polymer used for milling customized abutments/provisionals
- **Emergence profile**: The contour of a restoration from its implant connection to the gingival margin
