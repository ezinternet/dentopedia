## Why Ingested

The wiki holds insertion torque thresholds (≥30 Ncm for immediate loading) discussed in [[implants/isq/bavetta-2019-insertion-torque-isq-immediate-loading-extraction-socket]] and [[implants/isq/tisci-2026-isq-it-mbl-survival-sr-ma]], but has no RCT directly comparing implant macro-design (tapered vs straight) on immediate loading success over 3 years. This Yonsei RCT is the first head-to-head trial of this design question in the posterior dentition, providing long-term data on whether tapered self-tapping design confers durable advantage beyond primary stability.

## One-line Summary

3-year follow-up RCT (n=28 patients, 61 implants): tapered implants showed numerically higher patient-level survival (95.8% vs 78.3%, NS) and fewer composite protocol failures vs straight implants for immediate loading in posterior jaws; MBL was minimal and equivalent between groups.

## 한줄요약

3년 추적 RCT (28명, 61개): 테이퍼 임플란트는 즉시 부하 성공률에서 직선형보다 우위(프로토콜 실패 4 vs 13개), 생존율 차이는 비유의적(환자 수준 95.8% vs 78.3%); 변연골 소실은 양군 모두 1mm 미만으로 유의차 없음.

- **Institution**: Yonsei University College of Dentistry, Seoul, South Korea

## 2. Key Contributions

- First RCT directly comparing tapered (self-tapping) vs straight implants under immediate loading in posterior dentition with 3-year follow-up
- All implant failures occurred within the first year; zero late failures (years 1–3) in either group
- Composite immediate loading protocol success: TI 4/36 failures vs SI 13/43 failures — more than twice the failure rate in straight implants
- Insufficient primary stability (ITV <30 Ncm) was exclusively an SI problem (2 implants); tapered design reliably achieved threshold
- MBL was minimal (<1 mm mean) and statistically equivalent between groups at 3 years
- Peri-implantitis case in TI group attributed to convex emergence profile with angle >30°, not macro-design per se
- Mechanical prosthetic complications (bridge exfoliation, crown fracture) occurred only in SI group (possibly parafunction)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Implants**:
- TI: Yuhan Evertis SI (tapered, self-tapping cutting edge), SLA surface
- SI: Straumann Bone Level (straight, no cutting edge), SLA surface

**Protocol**:
- Multiple implants (≥2) in posterior maxilla or mandible
- GBR performed as needed
- Provisional splinted PMMA prosthesis within 7 days of surgery (= immediate loading)
- Definitive prosthesis delivered at 1 year (same design as provisional)
- Recall every 3–6 months

**Follow-up**:
- Original cohort: 48 enrolled, 41 completed original 1-year trial (TI: 23 pts/50 implants; SI: 18 pts/43 implants)
- 3-year cohort: 28 patients/61 implants (TI: 15/32; SI: 13/29)
- Drop-out: 13 patients (8 TI, 5 SI) — self-reported no discomfort; sensitivity analysis confirmed no statistically significant attrition bias

**MBL measurement**:
- Standardized extension cone paralleling technique with Rinn digital sensor
- ZeTTA PACS Viewer v2.0.0.4; mesial + distal average; calibrated to fixture length
- Single experienced examiner (S.J.Y.)
- Annual MBL = total MBL / observation years

**Statistics**:
- Kaplan-Meier survival curves; Cox proportional hazards regression (clustered by patient for implant-level)
- Linear mixed-effects model (LMM) with patient-level random intercept for MBL
- SPSS v26 + R v4.4.0; significance p<0.05

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Survival rates at 3 years** (Kaplan-Meier):

| Level | TI group | SI group | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Implant-level | 96.2% (90.1–99.5%) | 90.0% (81.4–99.5%) | 0.351 |
| Patient-level | 95.8% (88.2–100%) | 78.3% (63.1–97.1%) | 0.109 |

**Composite immediate loading protocol failures**:

| Failure reason | TI | SI |
|---|---|---|
| ITV <30 Ncm (ineligible for IL) | 0 | 2 |
| Early osseointegration failure | 2 | 5 |
| Annual MBL ≥0.2 mm | 2 | 6 |
| **Total unsuccessful** | **4** | **13** |

**MBL (estimated means from LMM)**:

| Time | TI group | SI group | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 year | 0.13 ± 0.18 mm | 0.21 ± 0.20 mm | 0.543 |
| 3 years | 0.23 ± 0.42 mm | 0.50 ± 0.64 mm | 0.729 |
| Annual rate | 0.13 ± 0.13 mm/yr | 0.11 ± 0.14 mm/yr | 0.868 |

**Complications**:
- Biologic: 2 TI-group implants (1 patient) — peri-implantitis; MBL reached ~7 mm at 3 years; managed conservatively
- Mechanical: 2 SI-group patients — bridge exfoliation (1), partial crown fracture (1); restored immediately

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Drop-out rate >30% at 3 years reduces statistical power (13/41 patients lost to follow-up)
- Sensitivity analysis confirmed no significant attrition bias, but borderline significance (patient-level p=0.109) may have been significant with full cohort
- Different implant manufacturers between groups (Yuhan Evertis vs Straumann Bone Level) — cannot isolate taper vs surface differences
- Peri-implantitis case maintained intraorally inflated survival rate and MBL in TI group
- Future work: longer follow-up (5+ years) incorporating the drop-out patients; controlled emergence angle in prosthetic design

## 6. Related Work

- Original 1-year RCT (same cohort): TI 96% vs SI 86% survival; TI achieved significantly higher ITV
- Tisci 2026 SR+MA: ISQ-IT moderate correlation (r=0.44) — ISQ adjunctive, not independent predictor
- Bavetta 2019: ITV ≥35 Ncm threshold for immediate loading in extraction sockets
- Nandini 2022: cylindrical vs tapered ISQ comparison — tapered advantage in primary stability
- Hamilton 2023 SR+MA: Type 1A immediate implant + immediate loading 3-year survival 97.5%; RCT subgroup 93%

## 7. Glossary

- **TI**: Tapered implant (self-tapping cutting edge; Yuhan Evertis SI)
- **SI**: Straight implant (no cutting edge; Straumann Bone Level)
- **ITV / IT**: Insertion torque value (Ncm) — measure of primary stability
- **MBL**: Marginal bone loss — vertical distance from implant shoulder to first bone-implant contact
- **Immediate loading**: Prosthesis delivery within 7 days of implant placement
- **LMM**: Linear mixed-effects model
- **GBR**: Guided bone regeneration
- **PMMA**: Polymethylmethacrylate — provisional prosthesis material
- **SLA**: Sandblasted, large-grit, acid-etched — standard surface treatment
- **Peri-implantitis**: Infectious inflammatory condition affecting peri-implant tissues with progressive bone loss
- **Emergence angle**: Angle of implant-supported crown profile at the soft tissue level; >30°–40° associated with poorer hygiene and higher MBL
