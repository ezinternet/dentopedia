## Why Ingested

Introduces PFAPA — the commonest childhood periodic-fever syndrome featuring recurrent aphthous stomatitis — linking the oral-ulcer differential in [[oral-medicine/mills-2017-aphthous-stomatitis-oral-medicine-review]] and [[oral-medicine/belenguer-guallar-2014-recurrent-aphthous-stomatitis-review]] to a systemic autoinflammatory cause.

## One-line Summary

Narrative review of PFAPA (periodic fever, aphthous stomatitis, pharyngitis, cervical adenitis) pathogenesis — recent genetic susceptibility loci link it to Behcet disease/recurrent aphthous ulcers, and tonsillectomy shows efficacy even in incomplete-criteria patients.

## 한줄요약

PFAPA(주기열·아프타구내염·인두염·경부림프절염) 병인 내러티브 리뷰 — 최근 유전 감수성 좌위가 베체트병/재발성 아프타궤양과 연결되며, 진단기준 미충족 환자에서도 편도절제술 효과 확인.

## 1. Document Information

- Current Opinion in Rheumatology 2023;35(6):423-428. NIH/NIAID intramural support; no conflicts.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Reframes PFAPA as a complex (polygenic) autoinflammatory disorder rather than monogenic.
- Connects PFAPA genetic susceptibility to Behcet disease and recurrent aphthous ulcers.
- Documents tonsillectomy efficacy beyond strict diagnostic criteria, with a distinct tonsillar immune profile.
- Raises nosological questions about PFAPA's boundaries with related recurrent-fever phenotypes.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Narrative review of recent genetic-association, linkage, and tonsillectomy-outcome studies.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- No causative rare mutations identified; susceptibility appears polygenic/common-variant driven.
- Phenotypic overlap with Behcet disease and recurrent aphthous ulcers via shared susceptibility loci.
- Tonsillectomy effective in broader cohorts, though predictors of response and tonsillar immunology differ.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative; PFAPA diagnostic definition remains unsettled; genetic architecture incompletely mapped.

## 6. Related Work

- Whole-exome/linkage studies of periodic fever syndromes.
- Behcet disease genetic-susceptibility literature.

## 7. Glossary

- PFAPA: Periodic Fever, Aphthous stomatitis, Pharyngitis, cervical Adenitis.
- Autoinflammation: innate-immune-driven inflammation without autoantibodies.
