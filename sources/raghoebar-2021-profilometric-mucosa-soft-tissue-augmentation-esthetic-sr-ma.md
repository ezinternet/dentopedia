## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 18 studies showing that soft-tissue grafting at esthetic-zone implant sites yields significantly less mid-buccal recession (0.34 mm gain, immediate) and greater mucosa thickness (0.66 mm, immediate) vs no graft, with stable medium-term outcomes.

## 한줄요약

전치부 임플란트에서 연조직 이식술은 즉시 식립 시 점막 퇴축 감소(0.34 mm)와 두께 증가(0.66 mm)를 유의하게 제공하며, 중기까지 안정적으로 유지됨을 18개 연구 SR+MA로 확인한 논문.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First EAO-endorsed SR+MA quantifying **both** linear (recession/level) and volumetric (thickness, profilometric) mid-buccal mucosa outcomes in the esthetic zone with a graft-vs-no-graft design.
2. Separate meta-analyses for **immediate** vs **delayed** implant placement subgroups — the distinction matters clinically because healing biology differs.
3. Confirmed medium-term stability of soft-tissue augmentation gains, addressing a key clinical concern about long-term durability.
4. Systematic search across MEDLINE, EMBASE, and Cochrane CENTRAL through June 2020 — 2,185 articles screened, 18 included.
5. Additional outcomes: implant survival, peri-implant health, and patient satisfaction reported alongside tissue dimension outcomes.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases**: MEDLINE, EMBASE, Cochrane Central Register of Controlled Trials (last search: 1 June 2020)
- **Inclusion criteria**: Studies reporting outcomes of different materials and timing of grafting at implant sites in the aesthetic zone; follow-up ≥ 1 year after implant placement
- **Population**: Patients undergoing soft-tissue augmentation at implant sites in the zone of aesthetic priority (anterior maxilla / esthetic zone)
- **Comparisons**: Graft (connective tissue graft, collagen matrix, or other soft-tissue substitute) vs no graft; immediate vs delayed implant placement subgroups
- **Primary outcomes**: Change in level of mid-buccal mucosa (vertical, mm); change in thickness of mid-buccal mucosa (linear/profilometric, mm)
- **Secondary outcomes**: Implant survival, peri-implant health parameters, patient satisfaction
- **Meta-analysis model**: Random-effects (implied by heterogeneity across included studies); mean difference (MD) with 95% confidence intervals
- **Included studies**: 18 out of 2,185 screened articles
- **Study designs**: Mix of RCTs, prospective cohort, and retrospective controlled studies (specific breakdown not extractable from abstract)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Immediate Implant Placement — Graft vs No Graft

| Outcome | MD (mm) | 95% CI | p-value | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Vertical mid-buccal soft tissue change | +0.34 | 0.13–0.56 | 0.002 | Significantly less recession with graft |
| Mid-buccal mucosa thickness | +0.66 | 0.35–0.97 | < 0.001 | Significantly greater thickness with graft |

### Delayed Implant Placement — Graft vs No Graft

| Outcome | MD (mm) | 95% CI | p-value | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mid-buccal mucosa level | +0.17 | 0.01–0.34 | 0.042 | Significantly less recession with graft |
| Mid-buccal mucosa thickness | +0.22 | −0.04–0.47 | 0.095 | NS — no significant thickness advantage with graft |

### Stability

- Observed tissue changes were **stable in the medium term** (exact follow-up interval not specified in abstract; see full text)

### Other Outcomes

- Implant survival, peri-implant health, and patient satisfaction were reported but detailed results require full text access

### Key Takeaway

Soft-tissue augmentation at esthetic-zone implants provides clinically meaningful, statistically significant benefits primarily for **immediate implant placement** (both recession and thickness). For **delayed placement**, only the recession benefit is significant; thickness increase does not reach significance.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **PDF access limitation**: This file is a Wiley HTML abstract print. The full paper (methods, study-level forest plots, risk of bias assessments, heterogeneity statistics, individual study data) is not accessible from this extraction. The above quantitative data are drawn exclusively from the abstract.
- 18 included studies is a modest number given the clinical question's breadth — heterogeneity across graft materials (connective tissue graft, xenogeneic collagen matrix), timing, and outcome measurement methods likely substantial.
- Pooling immediate and delayed implant placement studies (albeit separately) may mask further clinical heterogeneity (compromised vs intact sockets, different bone phenotypes).
- Follow-up definitions for "medium term" vary across included studies.
- Full volumetric (3D profilometric) analysis likely limited to a subset — many studies used linear caliper measurements.
- Patient-reported outcomes (satisfaction) not amenable to meta-analysis based on abstract.
- Systematic search cutoff June 2020 — subsequent RCTs and SR+MAs (e.g., Stefanini 2023, Thoma group RCTs) not captured.

## 7. Glossary

- **Zone of aesthetic priority**: Anterior maxillary region (incisors, canines, premolars visible on smile) where soft-tissue esthetics directly affect patient appearance
- **Mid-buccal mucosa level**: Vertical position of the gingival margin at the midpoint of the buccal surface of the crown/prosthesis
- **Mid-buccal mucosa thickness**: Horizontal (bucco-palatal) soft-tissue dimension measured at the midpoint of the buccal mucosa
- **Linear change**: 1D measurement of mucosal level (mm recession or gain)
- **Profilometric change**: Volumetric/surface contour analysis using 3D optical scanning or profilometric devices — captures buccal convexity loss/gain not detectable by linear measurement
- **CTG (Connective Tissue Graft)**: Subepithelial connective tissue harvested from the palate — gold standard soft-tissue graft material
- **Xenogeneic collagen matrix (XCM)**: Porcine- or bovine-derived acellular dermal matrix as CTG substitute (e.g., Mucograft)
- **Immediate implant placement**: Implant placed into fresh extraction socket at time of extraction (Type 1)
- **Delayed implant placement**: Implant placed after socket healing (Type 3 or 4)
- **Mean difference (MD)**: Statistical measure comparing continuous outcomes between two groups; positive MD = better outcome with graft
