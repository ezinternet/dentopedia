## Why Ingested

This 2026 SR+MA is the larger, newer counterpart to [[wiki/orthodontics/yu-2023-mandibular-advancement-aligner-vs-functional-class-ii-sr-ma]] on the identical CAMA-vs-functional-appliance Class II question. Both independently conclude comparable skeletal effect plus superior lower-incisor torque control with CAMA, so it **reinforces** (does not overturn) Yu, and adds a Herbst-specific comparison and quantified IMPA/overjet effect sizes.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 9 studies (1 RCT + 8 NRSI, n=465): clear aligner mandibular advancement (CAMA) achieves skeletal effects (SNA/SNB/ANB, vertical) statistically comparable to Twin Block and Herbst; CAMA gives marginally greater overjet reduction (MD -0.46 mm, clinically minimal) and significantly less lower-incisor proclination (IMPA MD -0.90°); certainty of evidence low (mostly retrospective).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Directly compares CAMA against the two dominant functional appliances (Twin Block AND Herbst) for growing skeletal Class II.
- Confirms skeletal-orthopedic equivalence (SNA/SNB/ANB, Wits, vertical) across both comparators.
- Quantifies the dental-control advantage of CAMA: significantly less lower-incisor proclination (IMPA MD -0.90°, P=0.0002).
- vs untreated controls, CAMA produces genuine skeletal change (SNB MD +1.00°; ANB MD -1.55° after leave-one-out), supporting CAMA as a true functional appliance, not merely a dental aligner.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

PubMed/Embase/Web of Science/Cochrane CENTRAL to Dec 31 2025; PRISMA 2020; PICOS. RoB 2 for the RCT, ROBINS-I for NRSIs. RevMan 5.4, random-effects WMD/SMD, leave-one-out and high-RoB-exclusion sensitivity analyses. 1589 records -> 60 full text -> 9 included (1 RCT + 8 NRSI), n=465 growing patients (mean age 11.2-13.0 y; observation 11-20 mo). CAMA systems: Invisalign MA, Angelalign A6, Clear Twin Block; comparators: Twin Block, Herbst, Vanbeek Activator, or untreated control.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**CAMA vs Twin Block (sagittal skeletal):** all NS — Wits MD -0.15 mm (I²=98%); SNA MD 0.01 (95% CI -0.66, 0.68); SNB MD -0.09 (-0.93, 0.74); ANB MD -0.10 (-0.36, 0.15, I²=0%).
**CAMA vs Herbst (sagittal skeletal):** all NS, all I²=0% — SNA MD 0.13 (-0.35, 0.62); SNB MD -0.50 (-1.22, 0.21); ANB MD 0.53 (-0.15, 1.21).
**CAMA vs untreated control:** Wits MD -2.51 mm (P=0.05); SNB MD +1.00° (0.34, 1.65; P=0.003); ANB MD -1.55° (-2.16, -0.94; P<0.00001 after excluding Al Subaie outlier); SNA NS.
**Sagittal dental:** overjet vs TB MD -0.46 mm (95% CI -0.86, -0.06; P=0.03, I²=0%) favoring CAMA but clinically minimal; overjet vs control NS (MD -2.16 mm, P=0.06). Lower-incisor inclination (IMPA) vs TB MD -0.90° (-1.38, -0.42; P=0.0002, I²=43%) — CAMA significantly less proclination.
**Vertical:** all NS — overbite vs TB MD -1.04 mm; ANS-Me vs TB MD -0.20 mm; overbite vs control MD -0.68 mm.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 1 RCT (high RoB, deviations from intended interventions); 8 NRSI — 5 high RoB (confounding from retrospective design), 3 moderate. Overall certainty **low**.
- Extreme heterogeneity in some skeletal parameters (I² up to 98%) from appliance-mechanic variability and CVM-timing differences.
- Physiologic mandibular growth near pubertal peak confounds appliance-induced effect; few ideal untreated controls.
- Vertical skeletal parameters and maxillary incisor inclination inconsistently reported.
- Calls for large multi-center RCTs with compliance monitoring (microsensors) and long-term post-treatment stability.

## 7. Glossary

- **CAMA**: clear aligner mandibular advancement — aligners with precision-wing/MA modules that posture the mandible forward.
- **IMPA (L1-MP)**: incisor-mandibular plane angle; lower-incisor inclination.
- **SNA/SNB/ANB**: cephalometric sagittal angles (maxilla/mandible to cranial base; intermaxillary).
- **Wits appraisal**: linear sagittal jaw-relationship measure.
- **CVM**: cervical vertebral maturation (skeletal-maturity staging).
- **ROBINS-I / RoB 2**: risk-of-bias tools for non-randomized / randomized studies.
- **Twin Block / Herbst**: removable / fixed functional appliances for mandibular advancement.
