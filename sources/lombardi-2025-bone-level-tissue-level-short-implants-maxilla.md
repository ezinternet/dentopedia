## Why Ingested

골-임플란트 접촉률 (Bone-to-Implant Contact, BIC) 및 변연골 안정성 (Marginal Bone Stability) 관련 문헌이 주로 표준 길이 임플란트에 집중되어 있어, 상악 구치부에서의 단축 임플란트 (Short Implant) 간 골 유지 차이를 다루는 비교 임상 데이터가 부족하였다. 본 연구는 골-수준 (Bone-Level) 대 조직-수준 (Tissue-Level) 단축 임플란트를 동일 시스템·동일 거시 형태 하에 직접 비교하는 최초의 전향적 케이스-대조 연구로, [[implants/emfietzoglou-2024-short-implants-6mm-survival-posterior-edentulism-sr]]의 6 mm 이하 단축 임플란트 생존율 체계에서 임플란트 배치 방식별 변연골 손실(MBL) 패턴을 보완하는 근거로 활용한다.

## One-line Summary

Multicenter prospective case–control study (n=58 patients, 71 implants, 1-year loading) found tissue-level short implants exhibited significantly less physiological bone remodeling than subcrestal bone-level short implants in posterior maxilla (PBR T0–T2: 0.30 vs. 0.55 mm, p=0.003), with 100% survival in both groups.

## 한줄요약

후향적 다기관 케이스-대조 연구(환자 58명, 임플란트 71개, 1년 추적)에서 상악 구치부 단축 임플란트의 변연골 개조량이 조직-수준 임플란트(0.30 mm)에서 골-수준 임플란트(0.55 mm)보다 유의하게 낮았으며(p=0.003), 두 군 모두 100% 생존율 달성.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Clinical Implant Dentistry and Related Research, 2025; 27:e13428
- **DOI**: 10.1111/cid.13428
- **Study type**: Multicenter prospective case–control study (STROBE guidelines)
- **Ethics**: Comitato Etico Regione Calabria—Sezione Area Centro (n. 194/2020)
- **ClinicalTrials.gov**: NCT05975138
- **Enrollment period**: June 2020 to May 2023
- **Follow-up**: 12 months post-loading (T2); timepoints T0 = implant placement, T1 = prosthesis delivery (5 months), T2 = 12 months loading
- **Implant system**: M2, Zeros (Busan, South Korea) — 4 mm diameter, conical hybrid connection, moderately rough surface (Sa 1–2 μm)

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First direct comparison** of bone-level vs. tissue-level short implants in posterior maxilla under identical macro-morphology and connection design — isolating only the implant-abutment junction position as the variable.
2. **Tissue-level implants preserve significantly more crestal bone** at both T1 (0.11 vs. 0.34 mm, p=0.004) and T2 (0.30 vs. 0.55 mm, p=0.003) — a clinically meaningful difference particularly relevant given the limited bony support of short implants.
3. **Thin mucosa (≤2 mm)** is the dominant predictor of early bone remodeling (T0–T1) in both implant types — more influential than smoking, periodontitis history, insertion torque, or ISQ.
4. **Short abutments (≤2 mm)** significantly increase bone remodeling from T1–T2 in both implant types; **crown emergence angle >30°** additionally worsens PBR in bone-level (but not tissue-level) implants.
5. **Bicortical engagement** of the implant apex in the sinus floor significantly improves primary stability (IT and ISQ at T0).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Participants**: 59 consecutive patients recruited across three centers; final analysis: 58 patients (25M/33F, mean age 63.3 ± 11.3 years), 71 implants (36 tissue-level, 35 bone-level). One patient lost to follow-up (received 1 TL + 2 BL implants).

**Inclusion** (local): available bone height 4.5–8.0 mm between crest and sinus floor; no prior/concomitant bone augmentation; tooth loss >6 months; IT ≥20 N·cm.

**Implants**:
- Tissue-level: 5 mm (n=18) or 6.5 mm (n=18) length; 2 mm machined transgingival neck; rough body submerged, neck at mucosal level
- Bone-level: 7 mm length; fully rough surface; placed 1 mm subcrestally; connected to healing abutment at placement
- Both: identical macro-morphology; one-stage protocol; prosthetics at 5 months

**Outcome — primary**: Physiological bone remodeling (PBR) = mean mesio-distal peri-implant bone level change from T0 on periapical radiographs (long-cone paralleling, customized Rinn holder). Positive = bone gain; negative = bone loss. Measured with ImageJ on 28-inch monitor; ICC intra-observer 0.99, inter-observer 0.87.

**Secondary outcomes**: IT (N·cm), ISQ (Osstell Smartpeg #27), implant failure, biological/mechanical complications.

**Predictors assessed**: mucosal thickness (thick >2 mm / thin ≤2 mm), IT (>50 / ≤50 N·cm), ISQ (≥60 / <60), bicortical engagement (yes/no), abutment height (long >2 mm / short ≤2 mm), crown–implant ratio, emergence angle (>30° / ≤30°), age, sex, smoking, periodontitis history.

**Statistics**: Shapiro–Wilk normality; t-test / Wilcoxon rank-sum for group comparisons; multiple linear regression (Enter method); α=0.05.

**Sample size**: 34 implants/group required for 80% power at 5% significance (based on prior PBR data: −0.03±0.74 vs. 0.26±0.55 mm).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Metric | Tissue-level | Bone-level | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| IT at T0 (N·cm) | 41.9 ± 14.0 | 40.8 ± 11.3 | 0.56 |
| ISQ at T0 | 66.8 ± 9.3 | 64.7 ± 10.3 | 0.42 |
| ISQ at T1 | 69.5 ± 7.0 | 71.4 ± 6.5 | 0.10 |
| PBR T0–T1 (mm) | 0.11 ± 0.27 | 0.34 ± 0.35 | **0.004** |
| PBR T0–T2 (mm) | 0.30 ± 0.23 | 0.55 ± 0.42 | **0.003** |
| Survival at 1 year | 100% | 100% | — |
| Complications | 0 | 0 | — |

**Mean residual bone height** (crest to sinus floor): 6.2 ± 2.3 mm.

**Multivariate PBR (T0–T1)**: Thin mucosa (≤2 mm) — significant predictor in both TL (B=−0.410, p<0.001) and BL (B=−0.480, p<0.001); all other variables NS.

**Multivariate PBR (T1–T2)**: Short abutment (≤2 mm) — significant in both TL and BL; emergence angle >30° — significant in BL only (mesial B=0.014, p=0.019; distal B=0.015, p=0.003); emergence angle NS in TL.

**Prosthetics**: 67 single crowns + 2 three-unit bridges; screwed metal-ceramic; 5-month healing before loading.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

1. **Short follow-up (1 year)**: physiological bone remodeling data only; long-term peri-implantitis risk, peri-implant tissue stability, and implant failure data not yet available.
2. **Small sample**: statistically adequate but n=36/35; larger multicenter RCTs needed.
3. **Bone-level implant position**: all placed 1 mm subcrestally per manufacturer recommendation; optimal crestal vs. subcrestal positioning remains debated and may confound PBR differences.
4. **Single implant system**: M2 Zeros; generalizability to other systems with different connection geometries unclear.
5. **Occlusal loading patterns and prosthetic design over time** not systematically analyzed.
6. No histological bone-implant contact data; PBR solely radiographic.

## 7. Glossary

- **PBR (Physiological Bone Remodeling)**: measurable change in crestal bone level from baseline on periapical radiographs; positive = bone gain, negative = bone loss
- **Tissue-level implant**: implant with a machined transgingival neck that positions the implant–abutment junction coronal to the bone crest, within the soft tissue
- **Bone-level implant**: implant with a fully treated surface placed at or below the bone crest; abutment connects at/below bone level
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: 1–100 resonance frequency analysis scale; higher = greater stability
- **IT (Insertion Torque)**: rotational resistance during implant placement (N·cm); indicator of primary stability
- **Bicortical engagement**: implant apex making contact with the cortical floor of the maxillary sinus
- **Supracrestal tissue adhesion (생물학적 폭경, Biological Width)**: soft tissue seal dimension (~2.7 mm) that forms around implants after exposure to oral cavity
- **Emergence angle**: angle between the abutment surface and the long axis of the implant body; >30° associated with increased bone remodeling in bone-level implants
- **Short implant**: generally accepted as ≤8 mm length; in this study: 5, 6.5, or 7 mm
