## Why Ingested
Anchor narrative review establishing the new glass-ionomer knowledge cluster; underpins [[glass-ionomer/ge-2024-gic-clinical-application-restorative-preventive]] and the planned GIC overview.

## One-line Summary
Narrative review of current evidence for GIC across restorative and preventive dentistry post-WHO Essential Medicines listing.

## 한줄요약
WHO 필수의약품 등재 이후 GIC의 적응증별 임상 근거를 정리한 narrative review.

## 1. Document Information
Journal of Dental Sciences 19 (2024) Suppl., pp. S1–S9. Received 29 Jun 2024; available online 27 Jul 2024. Open access (CC BY-NC-ND). Faculty of Dentistry, University of Hong Kong.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Post-2021 clinical-application synthesis of GIC.
- CGIC/HVGIC/RMGIC classification by setting reaction and clinical niche.
- Identifies liner/base as weakest-evidence indication.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Narrative review; no formal bias appraisal or meta-analysis. Structured by composition chemistry, setting reactions, and indication-by-indication evidence.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
Supported indications: restorative cement, luting cement, dental sealant. Debatable: liner/base. HVGIC preferred for ART (mechanical strength, marginal seal, durability).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
Non-systematic; subject to selection bias; no quantitative effect sizes.

## 6. Related Work
WHO Model List of Essential Medicines (2021); FDI amalgam-alternative policy; Minamata Convention.

## 7. Glossary
- CGIC: conventional glass ionomer cement.
- HVGIC: high-viscosity GIC (ART material).
- RMGIC: resin-modified GIC (Bis-GMA/UDMA/HEMA + acid-base reaction).
- ART: Atraumatic Restorative Treatment.
