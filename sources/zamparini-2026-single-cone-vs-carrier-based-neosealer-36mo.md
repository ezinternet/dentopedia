PubMed 감시(one-cone technique) — Bologna 그룹(Prati/Zamparini/Spinelli)이 [[endodontics/spinelli-2024-three-year-single-cone-ceraseal-cohort]]를 NeoSealer Flo·36개월·carrier-based 비교로 확장. 단일콘 vs carrier-based가 healing은 동등(93% vs 88%)하나 **apical extrusion은 carrier-based에서 유의하게 많다(75% vs 48%)**는 점이 임상 의사결정(어떤 충전법을 쓸지)에 직접 쓰인다. ## One-line Summary

Retrospective cohort (53 RCTs, 48 patients, NeoSealer Flo) — single-cone vs carrier-based had equivalent 36-mo healing (93% vs 88%, p>0.05) but carrier-based showed significantly more apical sealer extrusion (75% vs 48.2%); extrusion did not compromise healing.

## 한줄요약

후향 코호트(53건, 48명, NeoSealer Flo) — 단일콘 vs carrier-based의 36개월 치유율은 동등(93% vs 88%, p>0.05)했으나 carrier-based에서 치근단 실러 압출이 유의하게 많았다(75% vs 48.2%); 압출은 치유를 저해하지 않았다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Retrospective cohort (STROBE), 36-mo follow-up, 2 blinded examiners (κ=0.90)
- **Journal**: Clinical Oral Investigations 2026;30(6)
- **DOI**: [10.1007/s00784-026-06900-0](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00784-026-06900-0) · PMID 42113044 · PMC13161296
- **Group**: University of Bologna Endodontic Clinical Section (Prati group)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First clinical evaluation of **NeoSealer Flo in a carrier-based (Thermafil) technique** — manufacturer indicates warm + cold single-cone, but no carrier-based data existed.
- Head-to-head single-cone vs carrier-based with the **same premixed CaSi sealer** at 36 mo: healing equivalent, but extrusion differs.
- Adds the longest (36-mo) NeoSealer Flo cohort and a digital extrusion-measurement methodology.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 48 patients / 53 RCTs (2020–2022), 9 postgraduate master operators each trained on ONE technique (preference-allocated, tutor-supervised).
- Shaping: NiTi rotary (primary) / reciprocating (retreatment); irrigation 5% NaOCl + 10% EDTA, final sterile water.
- Obturation: NeoSealer Flo + Thermafil carrier-based (24) **or** cold single-cone (29); sealer to WL−3 mm.
- Outcome: PAI ≤2 + no signs/symptoms = healthy; PAI ≥3 / symptoms / retreatment = endodontic lesion. Chi-square; extrusion measured on PA radiographs (BlueSkyPlan).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Healing**: single-cone 27/29 = 93% (95% CI 78.0–98.1); carrier-based 21/24 = 87.5% (95% CI 69.0–95.7); absolute diff 5.6% (95% CI −10.5 to 21.7); NS.
- **Apical extrusion**: carrier-based 18/24 (75%) vs single-cone 14/29 (48.2%); mean size 1.49 mm. Significantly larger extrusion with carrier-based **and** preoperative PAI >3.
- **Radiographic modification** of extruded sealer: ~9.4% of all cases (5/53; abstract: 5/32 extruded = 15.6%); majority stable.
- Benchmarks cited: 88.6% (24 mo carrier-based + Ceraseal), 85.4% (3 y single-cone + Ceraseal), 90.9% (3 y single-cone + EndoSequence), 89.6% (4 y single-cone + BioRoot RCS).
- Thermal concern dismissed: in-canal temp rise with Thermafil <1 °C; 37–77 °C does not alter CaSi flow/setting.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Retrospective, heterogeneous selection, small n, very few failure events → no multivariable analysis, underpowered for small between-group differences.
- Operator-preference allocation (not randomized). Calls for prospective/RCT confirmation of temperature effects and long-term interfacial behavior.

## 7. Glossary

- **Carrier-based obturation (Thermafil)**: warm gutta-percha pre-mounted on a carrier, thermoplasticized and inserted; faster learning curve, denser fill, but higher hydraulic pressure → more extrusion.
- **Apical extrusion**: sealer pushed beyond the apical foramen.
- **NeoSealer Flo**: premixed tricalcium/dicalcium-silicate + calcium-aluminate bioceramic sealer (Avalon Biomed).
- **PAI**: Periapical Index radiographic score.
