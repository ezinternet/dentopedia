## One-line Summary
Sheep iliac-crest in vivo study (n=2 sheep, 20 implants, 2-month healing) — OD (Versah Densah) gave ~30% higher bone volume %, better removal torque, and less micromotion under lateral load vs conventional drilling.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Animal experimental (in vivo, biomechanical + histomorphometric)
- **Journal**: Implant Dentistry 2016;25(1):24–31
- **DOI**: 10.1097/ID.0000000000000358
- **Sample**: 2 sheep, 20 Dynamix (Cortex) implants total — 10 control (3.8 × 10 mm, conventional drilling, left ilium) vs 10 test (5 × 10 mm, OD/Versah, right ilium)
- **Primary Question**: Can osseodensification enhance bone density, ridge width, and secondary stability of implants in low-density bone vs conventional drilling?

## 2. Key Contributions
- **Foundational in vivo study** for OD in low-density bone — frequently cited in subsequent SR/MA.
- Quantifies a **~30% higher bone volume %** in the OD group vs conventional drilling at 2 months.
- Shows OD-prepared sites had significantly higher removal torque and lower micromotion under lateral loading.
- Demonstrates increase in **ridge width** with OD — first evidence of the bone-expansion effect.
- No implant failures observed in either group at 2 months.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Edges of both iliac crests were exposed in 2 sheep. Ten 3.8 × 10 mm Dynamix implants (Cortex) were placed in the left ilia using conventional drilling (control); ten 5 × 10 mm Dynamix implants were placed in the right ilia using OD (Versah Densah) — note that the test-group implants were of larger diameter, so OD also tested whether the lateral expansion enabled wider implants in the same starting bone. After 2 months of healing, sheep were euthanized and ilia harvested for biomechanical (removal torque, micromotion under lateral force) and histological (bone volume %, ridge width) analysis.

The study compares not just OD vs conventional, but also implicitly an osteotomy-diameter difference (3.8 mm conventional bed vs 5 mm OD bed). Healing duration was 2 months, longer than Witek 2019's 3 weeks.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **0/20 implant failures** at 2 months.
- Bone volume % (%BV) approximately **30% higher** in the OD group vs control.
- Removal torque significantly higher in OD vs control (specific N·cm values not extractable from available text).
- Micromotion under lateral force significantly lower in OD vs control.
- Ridge width significantly greater in OD sites.
- Specific numerical means, SDs, and p-values: Numerical detail not extractable from available text — see PDF tables.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Very small sample: n = 2 sheep, 20 implants.
- Test and control implants differ in **diameter** (5 mm vs 3.8 mm) — confounds the comparison: some of the test-group advantage may stem from a larger implant in the same bone, not OD per se.
- Both Cortex (implants) and Versah (burs) provided hardware — industry support disclosed in acknowledgements.
- Only 2 months of healing — no long-term remodeling or loading.
- Sheep iliac crest models low-density human jaw but is not anatomically equivalent.
- Authors are part of Pescara Biomaterial Clinical Research Association, a frequent OD publisher.

## 7. Glossary
- **%BV (bone volume percentage)**: histomorphometric ratio of mineralized bone tissue per unit of analyzed region around the implant.
- **Removal torque**: peak rotational torque required to unscrew the osseointegrated implant — a proxy for biomechanical anchorage at sacrifice.
- **Micromotion**: relative displacement between implant surface and surrounding bone under controlled load, related to risk of fibrous encapsulation.
- **Densah / Versah**: proprietary OD bur and manufacturer.
