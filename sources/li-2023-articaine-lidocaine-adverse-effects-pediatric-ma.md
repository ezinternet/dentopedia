## Why Ingested

Existing local-anesthesia coverage ([[wiki/drug/karm-2017-lidocaine-epinephrine-200000-third-molar]], [[wiki/drug/hwang-2023-proposal-clinical-guidelines-local-anesthesia-dentistry]]) centers on adult agent/vasoconstrictor choice; the wiki lacked any pediatric articaine-vs-lidocaine safety evidence. This SR+MA (8 RCTs, RR 1.08) supplies the pediatric adverse-event base rate and pairs with the broader articaine pharmacology in [[wiki/drug/becker-2012-local-anesthetics-pharmacological-considerations]] (4% articaine paresthesia risk).

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 8 RCTs (470 articaine vs 441 lidocaine subjects, ages 3–13): no difference in overall adverse-event risk between 4% articaine and 2% lidocaine in pediatric dentistry (RR 1.08, 95% CI 0.54–2.15, p=0.83), with high heterogeneity (I²=57%) and GRADE-rated "moderate" certainty.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First SR/MA focused specifically on adverse effects** (not efficacy) of articaine vs lidocaine in children — prior reviews only ran adverse-event subgroups inside efficacy syntheses.
2. **Pooled adverse-event risk ratio** for pediatric dentistry: RR 1.08 (95% CI 0.54–2.15, p=0.83) across 8 RCTs.
3. **Outcome-specific subgroup pooling** (postoperative pain, soft-tissue injury, edema) — all non-significant.
4. **GRADE-graded "moderate" certainty** and funnel plot showing no publication bias.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases**: Cochrane Library, PubMed, CBM, Embase, Web of Science, CNKI (last searched Nov 2021).
- **PICO**: In children undergoing dental treatment, is articaine safer than lidocaine?
- **Inclusion**: RCTs, healthy pediatric subjects age <14, raw adverse-event data, both buccal infiltration (BI) and inferior alveolar nerve block (IANB).
- **Exclusion**: non-RCT, observational, case reports/series, in-vitro, computerized delivery routes, supplementary anesthesia techniques.
- **Screening**: 333 records → 132 full-text → 8 included (7 English, 1 Chinese).
- **n**: 470 articaine vs 441 lidocaine subjects across 911 total.
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane Handbook 5.0.2 (7 domains); 2 low-RoB, 3 high-RoB, 3 moderate/unclear.
- **Statistics**: RevMan 5.3, random-effects model; heterogeneity by I² (≥50% substantial) and chi-square (p<0.1); GRADE for certainty; funnel plot for publication bias.
- **Primary outcome**: adverse-event rate (vital-sign change + adverse reactions, mostly elicited by postoperative phone follow-up).

- Articaine formulations: mostly 4% with 1:100,000 epinephrine (one 1:200,000), dose 5 mg/kg.
- Lidocaine formulations: 2% with 1:80,000 or 1:100,000 epinephrine, dose 4.4 mg/kg.
- GRADE certainty: **moderate** (downgraded for inconsistency/imprecision).
- Funnel plot: no publication bias.
- Most studies used articaine for infiltration and lidocaine for IANB — an asymmetry the authors flag as a confounder.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

1. **Small included sample sizes** → limited statistical power.
2. **Anesthetic delivery route not matched** — articaine mostly BI, lidocaine mostly IANB; safety equivalence therefore conditioned on articaine = infiltration use.
3. **Adverse events parent-/patient-reported via phone**, not clinically verified → reporting bias.
4. **Two RCTs included children <4 y** despite manufacturer recommendation against articaine <4 y.
5. **High heterogeneity** (I²=57%) for the primary outcome.
6. IANB articaine safety in children remains inconclusive; more RCTs needed.

## 6. Related Work

- Becker & Reed 2012 — pharmacology; 4% articaine paresthesia risk 7.3× (Garisto data).
- Brignardello et al. — articaine ≈ lidocaine in 3–4 y primary molar pulpotomy.
- Wright et al. — retrospective, 211 children <4 y on articaine, no adverse effects.
- Martin et al. — articaine infiltration success 2.78× lidocaine.

## 7. Glossary

- **Articaine** — amide local anesthetic with an ester side chain; rapid plasma-esterase hydrolysis; formulated 4%.
- **BI** — buccal infiltration.
- **IANB** — inferior alveolar nerve block.
- **RR** — risk ratio (relative effect).
- **I²** — heterogeneity statistic; ≥50% = substantial.
- **GRADE** — Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation (certainty rating).
