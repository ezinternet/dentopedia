## One-line Summary

SR+MA (8 studies, 6 meta-analyzed) — internal (walking-bleach) of endodontically treated discolored teeth produces large, significant shade change (ΔSGU 6.27, ΔE 12.83); carbamide peroxide 35–37%, HP 35%, and sodium perborate + HP outperform sodium perborate alone on ΔSGU (RoB moderate–high).

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (8편, 6편 메타분석) — 근관치료된 변색치의 내부(워킹블리치) 미백은 큰 색조 변화(ΔSGU 6.27, ΔE 12.83). ΔSGU 기준 CP 35–37%·HP 35%·과붕산나트륨+HP이 과붕산나트륨 단독보다 우수(근거 RoB 중–고).

## 1. Document Information

- Study type: SR + meta-analysis (subgroup analyses by agent).
- Population: Endodontically treated, discolored (non-vital) teeth.
- Outcome: Shade change (ΔSGU, ΔE).
- Included: 8 studies (6 in meta-analysis). PubMed, Web of Science, Cochrane.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First meta-analytic ranking of internal-bleaching agents.
- Establishes the magnitude of achievable shade change for the walking-bleach indication.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Two-reviewer search of clinical trials measuring pre/post color of non-vital teeth.
- Heterogeneity (Cochran Q, I²); publication bias (funnel plots); subgroup comparison of agents.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Overall: ΔSGU 6.27 (95% CI 5.36–7.17), ΔE 12.83 (95% CI 9.46–16.20) — large, significant.
- **ΔSGU**: CP 35/37%, HP 35%, and sodium perborate + HP (3% or 30%) > sodium perborate alone (adj. P ≤ .026).
- **ΔE**: no significant difference between CP 37%, HP 35%, sodium perborate + HP (P = .051).
- RoB: moderate to high.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 8 studies; moderate-to-high RoB; no long-term shade-stability/relapse data.
- Cervical resorption risk (a key internal-bleaching safety concern) not synthesized here.
- Abstract-only ingest.

## 6. Related Work

- de Geus 2025 — vital (external) bleaching.
- Endodontic restoration/esthetic literature in `wiki/endodontics/`.

## 7. Glossary

- **Internal / walking-bleach**: bleaching agent sealed inside the pulp chamber of a non-vital tooth between visits.
- **Sodium perborate**: classic walking-bleach agent; releases HP/oxygen on hydration.
- **ΔSGU / ΔE**: visual shade-unit change / instrumental color difference.
