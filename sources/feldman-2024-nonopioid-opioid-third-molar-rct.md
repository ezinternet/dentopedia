## One-line Summary
Multisite RCT (n=1,815): ibuprofen 400mg+APAP 500mg outperforms hydrocodone 5mg+APAP 300mg for pain on days 1–2 post-3rd molar extraction and yields higher overall satisfaction; opioid never outperformed nonopioid at any timepoint.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: JADA 2025;156(2):110–123
- DOI: 10.1016/j.adaj.2024.10.014
- Trial registration: ClinicalTrials.gov NCT04452344
- Design: Multisite, double-blind, randomized, stratified, noninferiority comparative effectiveness RCT
- Funding: NIDCR/NIH
- n = 1,815 adults (impacted mandibular 3rd molar extraction)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Largest RCT to date directly comparing nonopioid combination vs opioid for acute dental surgical pain
- Confirms ADA recommendation: ibuprofen+APAP as first-line; opioid prescribing after dental surgery not routinely supported
- Nonopioid superior to opioid on days 1–2 (primary endpoint); noninferior over entire postoperative period
- Higher patient satisfaction with nonopioid (85.3% vs 78.9%, p=0.006)
- Opioid did not outperform nonopioid at any timepoint

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Groups: (A) Hydrocodone 5mg + APAP 300mg q4–6h PRN; (B) Ibuprofen 400mg + APAP 500mg q4–6h PRN
- Outcome: NRS 0–10 pain scores (daily/nightly averages); interference; sleep; satisfaction
- Non-inferiority margin for full postoperative period; superiority tested for days 1–2, 2–3
- Stratified by sex; subgroup analyses for male vs female
- Multisite (≥6 academic dental centers), IRB: Rutgers; DSMB appointed by NIDCR

## 4. Key Results
| Timepoint | Mean Difference (nonopioid – opioid) | 95% CI | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Day 1–2 | −0.70 | −0.94 to −0.45 | <0.001 |
| Day 2–3 | −0.28 | −0.52 to −0.04 | 0.015 |
| Full period (noninferiority) | −0.20 | −0.45 to 0.05 (98.75% CI) | 0.172 (noninferior) |
| Overall satisfaction | 85.3% vs 78.9% | OR 1.21–1.98 | 0.006 |

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Fixed doses PRN; no dose-response optimization
- Extraction limited to bony mandibular impactions (may not generalize to simpler extractions)
- Sex differences not fully powered as primary subgroup
- Long-term opioid misuse outcomes not tracked
