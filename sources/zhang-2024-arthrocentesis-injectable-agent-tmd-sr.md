## One-line Summary
Systematic review from Chengdu on arthrocentesis (관절천자) combined with injectable agents (HA, corticosteroids, PRP, BTX) for TMD; finds hyaluronic acid and corticosteroids most evidence-supported, with emerging data on PRP.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health 2024;24:1108
- **DOI**: 10.1186/s12903-024-04858-7
- **Affiliation**: Chengdu University of Traditional Chinese Medicine
- **Study type**: Systematic review

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive review of arthrocentesis augmented with various injectable agents for TMD
- Compares: hyaluronic acid (HA), corticosteroids, platelet-rich plasma (PRP), BTX, and saline/lavage alone
- Evaluates added benefit of injectable agents beyond simple joint lavage
- Synthesizes evidence for agent selection in clinical practice
- Highlights PRP as emerging option with promising early data

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Search: PubMed, Embase, Cochrane, Scopus, Web of Science
- Inclusion: RCTs and controlled trials of arthrocentesis ± injectable agents for TMD
- Primary outcomes: pain (VAS/NRS), MMO (mm)
- Secondary: clicking/crepitus, jaw function, patient satisfaction
- Narrative synthesis; meta-analysis where feasible
- Risk of bias assessment using Cochrane RoB 2.0

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Hyaluronic acid (HA)**: Most evidence-supported injectable adjunct; significant pain reduction and MMO improvement vs. lavage alone in multiple trials
- **Corticosteroids**: Short-term pain benefit; risk of long-term joint damage with repeated injections
- **PRP**: Emerging evidence — comparable or superior to HA in some studies; variability in preparation protocols
- **BTX intra-articular**: Limited data; possible benefit for pain in disc displacement with pain
- **Saline lavage alone**: Significant benefit over no treatment, but inferior to HA augmentation
- Optimal number of injections and re-treatment interval remains unclear

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- High procedural heterogeneity (lavage volume, number of punctures, injectate concentration/volume)
- Variable follow-up (3–24 months)
- Most trials have moderate-to-high risk of bias
- Optimal HA molecular weight and dosing undefined
- Direct comparison between all agents lacking (network meta-analysis needed)

## 6. Related Work
- Ijoms 2023 arthrocentesis vs. conservative management SR+MA
- BMJ 2023 guideline (conditional against routine arthrocentesis as first-line)

## 7. Glossary
- **Arthrocentesis** (관절천자): Needle puncture and lavage of the TMJ superior joint space
- **Hyaluronic acid (HA)** (히알루론산): Viscosupplementation agent injected to reduce friction and inflammation; most studied TMJ injectable
- **PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma)** (혈소판풍부혈장): Autologous growth-factor concentrate; promotes tissue healing and anti-inflammatory effect
- **Corticosteroid**: Anti-inflammatory agent (typically triamcinolone, methylprednisolone); rapid pain relief but potential cartilage effects with repeated use
- **Intra-articular injection**: Injection directly into joint space; can be standalone or combined with arthrocentesis lavage
