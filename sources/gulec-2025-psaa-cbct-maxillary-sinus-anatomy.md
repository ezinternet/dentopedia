## Why Ingested

상악동 거상술 (Maxillary Sinus Floor Augmentation, MSFA) 측방 접근 시 술 중 출혈 합병증의 주요 원인인 후상치조동맥 (Posterior Superior Alveolar Artery, PSAA) 의 CBCT 해부학적 위치 데이터가 기존 wiki에 부재. 본 연구는 255명 510개 상악동 CBCT 코호트로 PSAA 유병률·직경·유형·알치조 거리 등 수술 계획에 직결되는 계측값을 제공하며, [[wiki/sinus-lift/lateral/yang-2024-sinus-septa-wall-thickness-perforation-risk]] 및 [[wiki/sinus-lift/lateral/turker-2026-maxillary-sinus-septa-perforation-risk]]의 CBCT 기반 천공 위험 예측 축을 혈관 해부학 측면으로 확장.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective CBCT study (n=255, 510 sinuses, Turkey) mapping the posterior superior alveolar artery (PSAA): bilateral prevalence 70.2%, predominantly intraosseous type, diameter <1 mm in ~55%, mean distance to alveolar crest ~16 mm — critical preoperative landmarks for lateral sinus lift vascular complication prevention.

## 한줄요약

후향 CBCT 연구 (255명, 510개 상악동): 후상치조동맥 (Posterior Superior Alveolar Artery, PSAA) 양측 유병률 70.2%, 골내형 (intraosseous) 우세, 직경 <1 mm 약 55%, 치조정까지 평균 거리 ~16 mm — 측방 상악동 거상술 혈관 합병증 예방을 위한 수술 전 CBCT 핵심 지표.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health (2025) 25:70
- **DOI**: 10.1186/s12903-025-05452-1
- **Design**: Retrospective CBCT cross-sectional study
- **Sample**: 255 patients (105 male, 150 female; mean age 47.5 ± 16.3 years), 510 maxillary sinuses
- **Setting**: Ahmet Kelesoglu Faculty of Dentistry, Karaman, Turkey; CBCT data from institutional archives (June 2024)
- **Conflict of interest**: None declared

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Quantified PSAA bilateral prevalence (70.2%) and anatomical distances (alveolar crest ~15.9–17.2 mm; sinus floor ~7.1–8.5 mm; medial wall ~13.1–13.3 mm) — directly actionable for lateral window incision planning.
2. Classified PSAA by type: intraosseous dominant (~63%), intrasinus (~10–13%), superficial (~3%).
3. Found PSAA diameter <1 mm is most common (~55%); diameter >1 mm correlates with reduced anatomical clearance distances (statistically significant — closer to sinus floor and alveolar crest).
4. Gender effect: sinus pathology prevalence higher in females (p = 0.002); males show greater PSAA-to-sinus-floor distance (p = 0.049).
5. No significant association between PSAA presence and sinus septa or sinus pathology (contradicts some prior hypotheses).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Imaging**: Kavo OP 3D Pro CBCT; 90 kVp, 8 mA, 4.5 s; 8×15 cm FOV; 0.35 mm voxel
- **Reviewers**: Oral radiologist (8 yr exp.) + oral/maxillofacial surgeon (10 yr exp.); discordance resolved by third specialist
- **PSAA typing** (Danesh-Sani classification): superficial / intraosseous / intrasinus on coronal sections at first molar level
- **Measurements**: PSAA diameter (digital ruler), distance to sinus floor / alveolar crest / medial wall (vertical reference lines)
- **Pathology definition**: any soft tissue thickening >2 mm
- **Statistics**: Kolmogorov-Smirnov normality; t-test / ANOVA / Tukey post-hoc; Chi-square + Cramér's V

Key ANOVA finding: PSAA diameter group significantly predicts anatomical distances (sinus floor F=6.54, p=0.002; alveolar crest F=8.46, p<0.001; medial wall F=3.28, p=0.039). Diameter <1 mm → greater clearance distances; diameter >1 mm → reduced distances → higher hemorrhage risk.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Retrospective single-center design; limits generalizability
- No intraoperative validation of CBCT findings against actual hemorrhage outcomes
- Metallic artifacts excluded but some PSAA course variation may still be missed
- Inter-observer agreement (ICC/kappa) not formally calculated
- Sinus pathologies not categorized by specific type (polyp vs. sinusitis vs. mucosal hypertrophy)
- Sample from Turkey only; geographic/ethnic variation not captured

## 7. Glossary

- **PSAA (Posterior Superior Alveolar Artery, 후상치조동맥)**: Branch of maxillary artery supplying lateral sinus wall; chief intraoperative bleeding risk in lateral sinus lift
- **Intraosseous type**: PSAA fully enclosed by cortical bone within lateral antral wall
- **Intrasinus type**: PSAA at the junction of lateral wall and sinus cavity
- **Superficial type**: PSAA on the outer surface of the lateral antral wall
- **Lateral window sinus lift (측방 접근 상악동 거상술)**: Surgical approach creating an osteotomy in the lateral maxillary sinus wall for bone grafting
