## Why Ingested

Part of a recent GBR / ridge-augmentation collection. This 7th ITI Consensus (Group 1) sets the authoritative buccal bone wall thickness (BBW) threshold of <1.5 mm and the role of simultaneous GBR for buccal dehiscence defects — it provides the consensus framework that contextualizes single-study work such as [[implants/cicciu-2023-buccal-palatal-bone-thickness-marginal]] and the simultaneous-GBR membrane evidence in [[bone-regeneration/choi-2017-single-double-layer-collagen-membrane-arp]].

## One-line Summary

7th ITI Consensus (Group 1, 2 underlying systematic reviews): in healed implant sites a buccal bone wall <1.5 mm predicts worse vertical bone loss and clinical/radiographic outcomes; simultaneous GBR over dehiscence defects and soft tissue augmentation (STA) with connective tissue grafts give stable peri-implant outcomes from 1–5 years.

## 한줄요약

7차 ITI 합의문(Group 1, 2개 SR 기반): 치유된 식립부에서 협측 골벽(BBW) <1.5 mm는 더 큰 수직 골소실·불리한 임상/방사선 결과를 예측하며, 열개 결손에 대한 동시 GBR과 결합조직이식(CTG)을 이용한 연조직증대(STA)는 1–5년간 안정적인 임플란트 주위 결과를 제공한다.

## 1. Document Information

- Type: Consensus report (7th ITI Consensus Conference, Working Group 1)
- Journal: Clinical Oral Implants Research 2023; 34(Suppl. 26): 43–49
- DOI: 10.1111/clr.14154
- Based on two pre-conference systematic reviews:
  1. Influence of buccal bone wall thickness on peri-implant hard/soft tissue dimensional changes (Monje, Roccuzzo, Buser, Wang) — 16 studies (12 clinical, 4 preclinical) out of 1700 records.
  2. Do soft tissue augmentation techniques provide stable, favorable peri-implant conditions medium/long-term? (Stefanini, Barootchi, Pispero, Grusovin, Mancini, Zucchelli, Tavelli) — 15 clinical studies (4 RCT, 5 non-randomized, 6 case series), 447 patients / 461 implants, follow-up 3–10 years (mean 8 y).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Defines a **"thin" buccal bone wall as <1.5 mm** for consensus purposes, and "initial healing" as ≤6 months after implant placement in healed sites.
- Establishes that the alveolar ridge undergoes buccolingual dimensional reduction after implant placement in healed sites; BBW reduces 0.3–1.8 mm (up to 72 months).
- Establishes that thin BBW sites suffer vertical bone loss during initial healing, while simultaneous GBR over buccal dehiscence defects reduces vertical bone loss vs non-augmented defects.
- Establishes that single implant sites remain stable 1→5 years with or without STA, but CTG augmentation maintains the soft tissue margin and increases/maintains soft tissue thickness and keratinized mucosa width, whereas non-augmented sites tend to migrate apically.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Two systematic reviews prepared and reviewed before the conference.
- Structured group discussion among Group 1 participants until consensus reached, then accepted by the entire plenum.
- Outputs: consensus statements, clinical recommendations, recommendations for future research, and patient perspectives.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Review 1 (BBW):
- Consensus statement 1: alveolar ridge undergoes buccolingual reduction during initial healing (2 prospective clinical + 1 preclinical). BBW reduction 0.3–1.8 mm up to 72 months (11 prospective clinical studies).
- Consensus statement 2: thin BBW → vertical bone loss during initial healing (5 prospective, 2 retrospective clinical, 1 preclinical).
- Consensus statement 3: implants with buccal dehiscence undergoing simultaneous GBR show less vertical bone loss and more favorable outcomes vs non-augmented defects (1 RCT, 22 patients / 28 implants).
- Patient guidance: bone should be ~1.5 mm thick buccal and ~1 mm palatal/lingual; a 4 mm-diameter implant therefore needs a ridge ~6.5–7 mm wide.
- Esthetic management options (besides bone augmentation): reduce implant diameter, place deeper in the crest, or flatten the posterior ridge.

Review 2 (STA):
- Statement 1: single implant sites may display stable interproximal bone levels medium/long-term with or without STA (12 studies; 3 RCT, 9 prospective).
- Statement 2: probing depths, bleeding, plaque at 1 year remain stable to 5 years ± STA (11 studies; 2 RCT, 9 prospective).
- Statement 3: CTG-augmented sites keep stable soft tissue margin to 5 years (10 studies; 2 RCT, 8 prospective); non-augmented sites may show apical margin migration long-term (5 studies; 1 RCT, 4 prospective).
- Statement 4: CTG sites display stable or increased soft tissue thickness and/or keratinized mucosa width 1→5 years.
- Statement 5: favorable esthetic outcomes (PES, VAS) maintained/improved 1→5 years after CTG or substitute; non-STA sites may show more discoloration.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Available data did not distinguish open-flap vs flapless approaches at implant placement (most implants placed with open flap).
- No meta-analysis possible for the STA primary outcome — only descriptive analyses (heterogeneous indications, limited evidence on soft tissue substitutes).
- Future research recommendations: anatomical/procedural factors driving buccal resorption (mandible vs maxilla, keratinized mucosa zone, open vs flapless, submerged vs transmucosal); long-term implant design/surface performance in thin BBW sites; long-term effect of augmenting thin BBW.

## 7. Glossary

- **BBW** — Buccal bone wall thickness; <1.5 mm defined as "thin."
- **STA** — Soft tissue augmentation.
- **CTG** — Connective tissue graft.
- **GBR** — Guided bone regeneration.
- **PROMs** — Patient-reported outcome measures.
- **PES** — Pink esthetic score.
- **Initial healing** — ≤6 months after implant placement in healed sites.
