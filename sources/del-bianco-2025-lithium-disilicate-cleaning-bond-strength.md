## Why Ingested

임상에서 리튬 디실리케이트 보철물 try-in 후 타액·실리콘 페이스트 오염을 제거하는 가장 효과적인 방법을 비교한 중요한 인 비트로 연구. [[resin-bonding/radhi-2023-lithium-disilicate-cleaning-resin-cement]]의 세척 프로토콜 논의를 직접 확장하며, Monobond Etch&Prime 자가 산처리 세라믹 프라이머가 기존 HF 재처리와 동등한 결합강도를 제공함을 보여준다.

## One-line Summary

In vitro study (n=70, 7 groups): after saliva + try-in silicone contamination of LiSi ceramic, self-etching ceramic primer (Monobond Etch&Prime) achieved bond strength equivalent to re-etching with 5% HF, outperforming phosphoric acid, ethanol, cleaning paste, and water rinse alone.

## 한줄요약

인 비트로 연구 (n=70, 7그룹): 타액+실리콘 페이스트로 오염된 리튬 디실리케이트 세라믹에서 자가 산처리 세라믹 프라이머(Monobond Etch&Prime)가 5% HF 재처리와 동등한 결합강도를 달성했으며, 인산·에탄올·세정 페이스트·수세만으로는 불충분했다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Demonstrated that self-etching ceramic primer (Monobond Etch&Prime, Ivoclar) restores bond strength of saliva+silicone-contaminated LiSi ceramic to levels comparable to re-etching with 5% HF
- Showed self-etching ceramic primer works regardless of whether initial HF pre-etching was performed (HFSEP ≈ SEP)
- Established that none of the tested cleaning methods completely removed contaminants per SEM analysis, yet HFSEP/SEP/HF still achieved clinically acceptable bond values
- Confirmed water rinse alone, phosphoric acid, ethanol, and Ivoclean cleaning paste are insufficient after combined saliva + try-in paste contamination

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Material**: IPS e.max CAD (Ivoclar Vivadent) — CAD/CAM LiSi ceramic blocks (14×7×3 mm, n=10)
- **Sintering**: Programat P500/G2 furnace (Ivoclar Vivadent)
- **Pre-etching**: 50 specimens etched with 5% HF (IPS ceramic gel) for 20 s; 20 specimens not pre-etched
- **Contamination protocol**: Fresh human saliva (cotton pellet, 60 s) + try-in silicone paste (Fit Checker, GC; finger-pressed 2 min)
- **Cleaning groups (HF-etched, n=10 each)**:
  - C (Control): Water rinse 20 s
  - PA: 37% H₃PO₄ for 20 s, rinse, dry
  - E: 70% ethanol brushed on 20 s, dry
  - CP: Ivoclean cleaning paste 20 s, rinse, dry
  - HFSEP: Monobond Etch&Prime rubbed 20 s, rinse, dry
- **Cleaning groups (no prior HF, n=10 each)**:
  - HF: 5% HF applied 20 s, rinse, dry
  - SEP: Monobond Etch&Prime rubbed 20 s (no prior HF)
- **Post-cleaning**: Monobond Plus silane 60 s (except HFSEP/SEP groups), Adhese Universal adhesive 20 s, Variolink DC resin cement
- **Storage**: 24 h at 37°C
- **Test**: Shear bond strength; fracture analysis by SEM

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Group | Prior HF | SBS Rank |
|-------|---------|---------|
| C (water rinse) | Yes | Lowest |
| PA (H₃PO₄) | Yes | Low |
| E (ethanol) | Yes | Low |
| CP (Ivoclean) | Yes | Low |
| HFSEP (MEP) | Yes | **High** |
| HF (re-etch) | No | **High** |
| SEP (MEP, no prior HF) | No | **High** |

- HFSEP, SEP, and HF groups significantly higher SBS than C, PA, E, CP (p<0.001)
- HFSEP ≈ SEP ≈ HF (no significant difference among top three groups)
- SEM: residual contaminants visible even after Ivoclean treatment (cleaning paste was least effective despite commercial claims)
- Conclusion: Surface etching + priming action of Monobond Etch&Prime bypasses need for re-HF while decontaminating

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single saliva donor; different donor microbiomes/protein composition may affect contamination
- Short 24-hour storage (no thermocycling) — durability of cleaning benefits not assessed long-term
- Only one LiSi material (IPS e.max CAD) tested
- Monobond Etch&Prime is a proprietary product; mechanism overlap with conventional HF+silane needs further characterization

## 7. Glossary

- **LiSi**: Lithium disilicate glass-ceramic (IPS e.max CAD, CAD/CAM version)
- **MEP**: Monobond Etch&Prime — self-etching ceramic primer (phosphoric acid methacrylate + silane methacrylate)
- **HFSEP**: HF-pre-etched group cleaned with MEP
- **SEP**: No prior HF, cleaned with MEP only
- **SBS**: Shear bond strength
- **Ivoclean**: Commercial cleaning paste (PMMA microsphere-based, Ivoclar Vivadent)
- **Try-in paste**: Silicone-based paste used for prosthesis fit assessment before cementation
