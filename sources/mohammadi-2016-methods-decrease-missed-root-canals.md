## One-line Summary
Narrative review of strategies to reduce missed canals: pre-op CBCT, access cavity design, ultrasonics, magnification (DOM), transillumination — each technique's role and limitations in canal orifice localization.

## 1. Document Information
Iranian Center for Endodontic Research + University of Western Australia. Narrative review article in IEJ.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive framework: 5 strategies to reduce missed canals
  1. Pre-operative radiographs (standard + angled)
  2. CBCT when anatomy complex or radiograph inconclusive
  3. Correct access cavity design (adequate dentin removal)
  4. Ultrasonic tips for calcification/bridge removal
  5. Magnification + illumination (DOM, loupes)
- Transillumination: useful adjunct for thin dentin over canal
- Missed canal = major cause of endodontic failure

## 3. Methodology
Narrative review — no systematic search reported. Literature synthesis of published evidence.

## 4. Key Results
- Pre-op CBCT: identifies extra canals, anomalies pre-surgery → reduces surprises
- Access cavity: must be conservative but complete; incomplete removal → orifice hidden
- Ultrasonics: removes calcified bridges without excessive dentin sacrifice
- DOM: increases detection rate for difficult canals (MB2, C-shape, etc.)
- Transillumination: identifies root anatomy by light transmission patterns

## 5. Limitations
- Narrative review — no systematic methodology
- 2016 — does not include post-2016 techniques (CBCT-guided, digital planning)

## 6. Related Work
- Dioguardi 2024 — access cavity design scoping review
- Floratos 2017 — intraoperative CBCT for calcified canals
- Sujith 2014 / Camacho-Aparicio 2022 — DOM + ultrasonics in vivo

## 7. Glossary
- **DOM**: Dental Operating Microscope
- **CBCT**: Cone Beam Computed Tomography
- **MB2**: Second mesiobuccal canal
- **Transillumination**: Passing light through root to reveal canal positions
