## One-line Summary
Cross-sectional CBCT study evaluating incisive canal dimensions and anterior maxillary bone thickness, with implications for immediate implant placement risk of canal perforation.

## 1. Document Information

## 2. Key Contributions
Highlights incisive canal as a frequently under-appreciated anatomic constraint in central incisor IIP, especially when palatal positioning is pursued for buccal bone preservation.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Retrospective CBCT measurements: incisive canal dimensions, position relative to central incisors, and labial/palatal bone thickness.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
Provides population-specific dimensional data. Quantifies how palatal IIP trajectories may approach canal walls.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
Cross-sectional; single population; no outcome data.

## 7. Glossary
- **IIP:** Immediate Implant Placement — placement at the time of tooth extraction.
- **IIPP:** Immediate Implant Placement and Provisionalization — IIP combined with same-visit provisional restoration.
- **SRP:** Sagittal Root Position — Kan I/II/III/IV classification of root-to-bone relationship.
- **CBCT:** Cone-Beam Computed Tomography.
- **PES/WES:** Pink/White Esthetic Score — esthetic outcome indices for anterior implants.
- **EMBL:** Early Marginal Bone Loss.
