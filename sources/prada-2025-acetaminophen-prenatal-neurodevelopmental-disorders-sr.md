## One-line Summary

Systematic review (Navigation Guide methodology, 46 studies): of studies on prenatal acetaminophen exposure and neurodevelopmental disorders (ASD/ADHD), 27 reported positive associations, 9 null, 4 negative; higher-quality studies more often positive — authors conclude the evidence supports an association and advise limiting acetaminophen in pregnancy. (Association, not causation; qualitative synthesis.)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Applies the Navigation Guide (environmental-health evidence-grading framework) to the prenatal acetaminophen–NDD literature.
- Stratifies 46 studies by direction of association and study quality.
- Concludes the body of evidence is consistent with an association, and recommends precautionary limitation of acetaminophen in pregnancy.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Systematic PubMed search through Feb 25, 2025, predefined inclusion criteria.
- Studies rated for risk of bias and strength of evidence (Navigation Guide).
- Qualitative synthesis chosen over meta-analysis due to substantial heterogeneity.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **46 studies** included.
- **27 positive** (significant link to NDD), **9 null**, **4 negative** (protective).
- Higher-quality studies more likely to show positive associations.
- Outcomes: ADHD, ASD, broader NDDs and related symptomatology.
- **Conclusion**: evidence consistent with an association between prenatal acetaminophen exposure and increased NDD incidence → advise pregnant women to limit acetaminophen.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Association, not causation** — observational evidence; confounding by indication (fever/infection/pain themselves linked to NDD) is a major unresolved threat.
- Qualitative (no pooled effect size); vote-counting by direction can overweight numerous small studies.
- Author group is associated with the acetaminophen-NDD hypothesis (potential interpretive bias); a 2024 large sibling-control Swedish cohort (not emphasized here) found no association after sibling adjustment.
- **ACOG and FDA continue to consider acetaminophen the preferred analgesic/antipyretic in pregnancy** — this SR is a precautionary signal, not a contraindication.

## 7. Glossary

- **NDD (neurodevelopmental disorder)**: conditions from atypical brain development (e.g., ASD, ADHD).
- **Navigation Guide**: a systematic, transparent framework for grading environmental-health evidence, adapted from clinical SR methods.
- **Confounding by indication**: bias where the reason a drug is taken (here fever/pain/infection) is itself associated with the outcome.
- **Acetaminophen (paracetamol)**: first-line analgesic/antipyretic in pregnancy per ACOG.
