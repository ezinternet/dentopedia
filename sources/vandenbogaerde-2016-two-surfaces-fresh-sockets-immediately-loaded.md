## Why Ingested

즉시식립+즉시부하 시나리오에서 임플란트 표면(rough/hydrophilic vs minimally rough)이 secondary stability 회복 속도를 좌우하는지에 대한 직접 within-patient 비교 근거. 기존 [[immediate-implant/hamilton-2023-type1a-immediate-implant-immediate-loading-esthetic-sr-ma]] (Type 1A 프로토콜 SR+MA)가 immediate placement+loading의 가능성을 종합하지만 표면별 stability dip 동역학을 보여주지 못하는 점을 보완하고, [[implants/isq/bavetta-2019-insertion-torque-isq-immediate-loading-extraction-socket]] (IT·ISQ와 즉시부하)의 baseline-stability 논의를 12주 ISQ trajectory로 확장한다.

## One-line Summary

Randomized within-patient case-series (11 patients, 22 implants — 11 bimodal control vs 11 proactive hydrophilic/rough test, Neoss) placed in fresh maxillary sockets and immediately loaded: both surfaces had a ~2-week ISQ dip, but the proactive test implant dipped less and recovered to significantly higher stability at 12 weeks (test +3.5±4.6 vs control −0.3±5.6 ISQ from baseline; p=0.021).

## 한줄요약

같은 환자에서 두 표면을 짝지어 비교한 무작위 케이스 시리즈(11명, 22개 — bimodal 대조 11 vs proactive 친수성·거친 표면 시험 11, Neoss)를 상악 발치와에 즉시식립·즉시부하: 두 표면 모두 2주째 ISQ 하강(stability dip)을 보였으나 proactive 시험 임플란트는 하강 폭이 작고 회복이 빨라 12주째 유의하게 높은 안정성에 도달(시험 +3.5±4.6 vs 대조 −0.3±5.6 ISQ, p=0.021).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Direct **within-patient (split-mouth) randomized comparison** of two implants with **identical macrodesign but different surface topography**, isolating the surface variable from patient/site confounders.
- Both surfaces are Neoss: **bimodal** (control, minimally rough, double particle-blasted, Sa ≈ 0.6 µm at body) vs **proactive** (test, moderately rough + hydrophilic, Ti-blast + acid-etch + chemically modified for electrowetting, Sa ≈ 1.0 µm at body). Neck roughness similar (Sa ≈ 0.4 µm) for both.
- Tracks the **ISQ "stability dip"** with high temporal resolution (baseline, 2, 4, 6, 12 weeks) in the highest-risk scenario: fresh extraction socket + immediate loading.
- Demonstrates that the **hydrophilic/rougher surface dips less and recovers faster**, reaching significantly higher 12-week stability — clinical evidence supporting rough/hydrophilic surface choice for immediate-loading protocols.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Patients**: 11 (4 M, 7 F; mean age 54.8 y, range 46–62). 8 had a history of periodontal disease; 3 smokers.
- **Inclusion**: planned extraction of ≥2 maxillary teeth (incisors, canines, or bicuspids) for immediate placement + immediate loading.
- **Implants**: 22 total — each patient got **one bimodal + one proactive** implant in two adjacent extraction sockets, **randomized by numbered sealed envelope** for position.
- **Surgery**: local anesthesia (mepivacaine/adrenaline); prophylactic amoxicillin. Flaps raised in 8 patients (no releasing incisions), flapless in 3. Piezotome/periotome atraumatic extraction; **buccal bone plates preserved**; implants positioned close to palatal wall leaving a buccal gap.
- **Gap management**: 9 implants had <1 mm residual peri-implant defects → left untreated; 13 had wider defects → regenerative procedure (12 with autologous bone chips from adjacent areas, **1 with synthetic substitute NanoBone**). No membranes.
- **Loading**: immediate screw-retained provisional bridge on day of surgery, **all occlusal contacts eliminated** in centric and lateral excursions.
- **Measurements**: peak **insertion torque (IT)** at placement; **RFA (Osstell ISQ)** at baseline, 2, 4, 6, 12 weeks — two readings each (mesiodistal MD + buccopalatal BP). Standardized intraoral radiographs (Rinn paralleling) read by independent radiologist; marginal bone measured from coronal shoulder to first bone contact.
- **Follow-up**: 36 months clinical; radiographs to 3 years.
- **Failure criterion**: any implant removed for any reason = failure.
- **Statistics**: Wilcoxon matched-pair signed-rank test (paired design); pooled 2–12-week ISQ also analyzed; significance p ≤ 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Survival / failure**
- 1 of 22 implants failed (a **test/proactive** implant, removed at 6 weeks) → overall **survival 95.5%**. The failed implant had the **lowest IT (20 Ncm)** and lowest baseline RFA (46–51 ISQ), a peri-implant defect grafted with the **synthetic substitute (NanoBone)**, and the extracted tooth had a periapical lesion. That patient excluded → **10 successful pairs** analyzed for stability.

**Insertion torque (NS between groups)**
- Control (bimodal): **38.0 ± 11.4 Ncm** (range 20–50)
- Test (proactive): **39.5 ± 9.1 Ncm** (range 25–50)

**ISQ trajectory (RFA over time)** — both surfaces similar at placement, then a **dip at ~2 weeks followed by recovery to 12 weeks**:
- The **2-week dip was more pronounced for the control (bimodal)** implant; the test (proactive) implant dipped less and recovered more rapidly.
- **Net change baseline → 12 weeks (mean ISQ)**: test gained **+3.5 ± 4.6 ISQ**; control **lost −0.3 ± 5.6 ISQ** (**p = 0.021**).
- Test implants significantly higher ISQ in **BP direction at 12 weeks (p = 0.021)** and when **pooling 2–12 weeks (p = 0.043)**. **No significant difference in MD direction.**
- Direction effect: control implants had significantly **lower BP than MD** values at most time points (except 2 weeks); test implants showed lower BP only at 12 weeks / pooled — consistent with progressive buccal-gap bone fill raising the coronal/buccal RFA signal.

**Marginal bone (no significant difference test vs control at any time)**
- Most bone loss occurred in the first 3 months: ~0.5 ± 0.7 mm (control) and 0.6 ± 0.4 mm (test) at 12 weeks.
- At **3 years**: control lost 0.4 ± 0.7 mm, test lost 0.6 ± 0.5 mm (NS). Repeated removal/reattachment of provisional for RFA did not increase bone loss vs same implant system.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Very small sample** (11 patients, 22 implants; 10 pairs for stability), single operator/clinic — case-series despite randomized within-patient allocation; underpowered for survival.
- **Heterogeneous gap management** (untreated, autologous chips, one synthetic graft) is a confounder; the only failure coincided with the lone synthetic-substitute site, conflating surface and graft variables.
- ISQ direction effects (MD vs BP) complicate interpretation; absolute ISQ values for each time point are presented graphically, not tabulated.
- Maxillary anterior/premolar sites only — not generalizable to mandible or posterior immediate loading.
- No histology; bone-to-implant contact inferred indirectly from RFA. Longer-term (>3 y) survival and esthetic/soft-tissue outcomes not reported.

## 7. Glossary

- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: 1–100 RFA-derived stability index from the Osstell device.
- **RFA (Resonance Frequency Analysis)**: non-invasive measurement of implant micromobility/stability via a transducer.
- **Stability dip**: transient decrease in ISQ (~2–3 weeks) as primary mechanical stability is replaced by secondary biological stability; the nadir is the highest-risk window under immediate loading.
- **Bimodal surface**: Neoss minimally rough (control) surface, double particle-blasted, Sa ≈ 0.6 µm at body.
- **Proactive surface**: Neoss moderately rough + hydrophilic (test) surface — Ti-blast + acid-etch + chemical modification for electrowetting, Sa ≈ 1.0 µm at body.
- **IT (Insertion Torque)**: peak torque (Ncm) at implant placement; surrogate for primary stability.
- **MD / BP**: mesiodistal / buccopalatal RFA measurement directions.
- **Sa**: arithmetic mean surface roughness (µm).
