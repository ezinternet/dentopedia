## Why Ingested

기존 [[complete-denture/srinivasan-2021-cad-cam-removable-complete-dentures]]과 [[complete-denture/avelino-2024-clinical-performance-digitally-fabricated-complete]]는 디지털 총의치의 임상 성능·결과(outcome)를 종합했으나, **어떤 임상·기공 절차**를 사용해야 하는지(테두리 형성 인상 vs IOS, 시적의치 필요 여부, 밀링 vs 3D 프린팅 재료 선택 등)에 대한 절차(procedure) 중심 체계적 리뷰가 없었다. 본 SR(JPD 2024)은 39편 임상연구를 PRISMA 방식으로 종합해 디지털 총의치 제작 절차의 변이와 그에 따른 합병증을 최초로 체계적으로 정리한다.

## One-line Summary

SR of 39 clinical studies on digital complete denture fabrication (2000–2022) found that border-molded impression and trial denture placement remain essential for optimal retention and fit, regardless of milling or 3D-printing workflow.

## 한줄요약

39편 SR: 디지털 총의치 제작 시 테두리형성 인상 채득 및 시적의치 시행이 유지력과 적합도를 위해 여전히 필수적이며, 밀링과 3D 프린팅 모두 유사한 환자만족도를 보임.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First SR to systematically map clinical and laboratory procedure variability across 39 digital complete denture studies (2000–2022).
2. Demonstrated that border-molded impression technique produces significantly higher maxillary denture base retention (14.5–16.1 N) than intraoral scanning (6.2–6.6 N).
3. Showed that only 13/39 studies performed trial denture placement — yet extra visits and remakes were most frequent when this step was omitted.
4. Established that digital CDs save 205–233 minutes of clinical time vs conventional workflow, but require ≥1 extra visit in most cases.
5. Confirmed patient satisfaction and OHRQoL are similar between conventional, milled, and 3D-printed complete dentures.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Study design**: PRISMA 2020 systematic review (no meta-analysis due to procedure heterogeneity)
- **Search**: PubMed/Medline, Embase, Web of Science; January 2000–September 2022; supplemented by Google Scholar and reference tracing
- **Screening**: 1,780 studies after duplicate removal → 266 full-text assessed → 39 included
- **Inclusion**: Clinical studies only, English language, subjective or objective outcome measurement; excluded case reports, short communications, reviews
- **Risk of bias**: 7 RCTs (fair-to-good quality); 13 cohort studies + 3 other designs (poor quality); remaining fair-to-good
- **Data extraction**: independent dual extraction; categories — clinical procedures, laboratory procedures/materials, outcomes/complications
- **Study types included**: 26 definitive CD studies, 7 denture base studies, 2 trial denture studies, 4 digital image analysis studies

**Procedures categorized:**
- Primary/definitive impression: border-molded vs intraoral scanning
- Jaw relation records: occlusion rim, gothic arch tracing, facebow
- Articulation: virtual vs mechanical semi-adjustable articulator
- Fabrication: milling (21 studies) vs 3D printing (17 studies)
- Trial denture: yes (13 studies) vs no

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Impression technique:**
- 24/39 studies used border-molded impression technique
- 3D printed custom trays used in some Avadent protocols
- Intraoral scanning (IOS) used in 6 studies — significantly lower retention than conventional impression

**Retention data (maxillary denture base):**
- Border-molded impression: 14.5–16.1 N
- Intraoral scan: 6.2–6.6 N (statistically lower, p<0.05)
- Milled bases generally higher retention than heat-polymerized (74.1 N vs 54.2 N in one study)
- 3D printed bases: comparable or slightly higher than milled in some RCTs (e.g., 14.1 N vs 13.4 N vs 10.5 N for 3DP/milled/conventional at baseline)

**Jaw relation records:**
- 7/39 studies used gothic arch tracing
- 3 studies used facebow records
- Virtual articulator mounting available in some CAD systems (3Shape, exocad)

**Trial denture:**
- Only 13/39 studies performed trial denture placement
- Studies omitting trial denture reported more esthetic/occlusal complications

**Fabrication:**
- Milling: 21 studies; materials primarily pre-polymerized PMMA blocks (e.g., Vipi Block Gum, Baltic Denture System)
- 3D printing: 17 studies; DLP and SLA technologies; materials include NextDent Denture Base, Dentca Denture Base II
- 1 RCT (Srinivasan 2021): milling vs 3D printing — similar patient satisfaction, milled dentures had slightly higher retention and lower OHIP scores

**Clinical time:**
- Digital workflow saved 205–233 minutes vs conventional complete denture workflow
- Average number of clinical visits: 2–6 (most common: 2–4)

**Complications:**
- Up to 37.5% of patients reported loss of retention
- Up to 31.3% required denture remake
- ≥1 extra visit needed in most protocols
- 1–4 unscheduled follow-up visits commonly required
- Complications included pressure sores (up to 66.7%), occlusal errors, esthetic issues

**Patient outcomes:**
- Maximum occlusal force similar across fabrication methods
- Patient satisfaction and OHRQoL (OHIP-EDENT) similar between conventional, milled, and 3D-printed CDs across multiple RCTs
- 3D printed CDs in some studies showed better OHIP improvement at 12–18 months

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- High heterogeneity of procedures across studies precluded meta-analysis
- Many included cohort studies rated poor quality (lack of controls, small samples)
- No standardized reporting of digital denture procedures across the literature
- Missing information on gothic arch tracing settings, specific articulator parameters, and tooth selection methodology
- Only 7 RCTs among 39 studies; more high-quality RCTs needed comparing specific procedure steps
- Studies limited to English; non-English literature may contain important data
- Future standardized reporting guidelines (similar to CONSORT for digital denture RCTs) are recommended

## 7. Glossary

- **CAD-CAM**: Computer-aided design and computer-aided manufacturing for complete denture fabrication
- **DLP**: Digital light processing — resin-based 3D printing technology
- **Gothic arch tracing (악궁 추적, GAT)**: Centric relation registration method using intraoral jaw tracing device
- **Facebow record (페이스보우 기록)**: Transfer of maxillary arch position relative to TMJ for articulator mounting
- **Border-molded impression (테두리 형성 인상)**: Impression technique capturing the functional border of the denture-bearing area with muscle trimming
- **IOS (구강 내 스캐너)**: Intraoral scanner — digital impression technique for edentulous jaws
- **OHIP-EDENT**: Oral Health Impact Profile for Edentulous patients (QoL measure)
- **PMMA**: Polymethylmethacrylate — primary denture base material for both milled and conventional CDs
- **Trial denture (시적의치)**: Wax or 3D-printed denture try-in step before final fabrication
- **Virtual articulator (가상 교합기)**: CAD software articulation simulation tool (e.g., 3Shape, exocad)
