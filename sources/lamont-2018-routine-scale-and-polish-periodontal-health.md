## One-line Summary

Cochrane systematic review + meta-analysis (2 RCTs, n=1711, UK general practice) finding that routine scale & polish makes little or no difference to gingivitis, probing depths, or oral-health-related quality of life over 2–3 years in adults without severe periodontitis (high-certainty), with only a small, clinically uncertain reduction in calculus.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Highest-certainty answer to a common practice question: for low-risk healthy adults, **scheduled routine scale & polish does not improve gingival health** vs no scheduled treatment over 2–3 years.
- Defines "routine scale and polish" = scaling/polishing of crown and root surfaces to remove plaque, calculus, debris, staining, **without** periodontal surgery, chemotherapeutic agents, or root planing — also called *prophylaxis*, *professional mechanical plaque removal (PMPR)*, or *periodontal instrumentation*.
- Directly evaluates **recall interval** (6-monthly vs 12-monthly) — no meaningful difference except a small calculus reduction favoring 6-monthly.
- GRADE-rated outcomes: high certainty for gingivitis, probing depth, calculus, QoL; low for plaque; very low for cost/oral-cleanliness perception.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Cochrane SR + fixed-effect meta-analysis; GRADE certainty rating.
- **Search**: Cochrane Oral Health Trials Register, CENTRAL, MEDLINE, Embase to 10 Jan 2018; no language/date restriction.
- **Inclusion**: RCTs of routine scale & polish (± oral hygiene instruction) in **healthy dentate adults without severe periodontitis**. Split-mouth trials excluded.
- **Included**: 2 studies, n=1711, both UK general dental practice, regular attenders; outcomes at 24 and 36 months.
- **Effect measures**: MD / SMD (continuous), RR (dichotomous), 95% CI.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Comparison 1 — routine scale & polish vs no scheduled treatment:**
- Gingivitis (6-monthly vs none): SMD **−0.01** (95% CI −0.13 to 0.11; 2 trials, 1087) — high certainty, no difference.
- Gingivitis (12-monthly vs none): SMD **−0.04** (95% CI −0.16 to 0.08; 2 trials, 1091) — high certainty, no difference.
- Calculus (6-monthly vs none): SMD **−0.32** (95% CI −0.44 to −0.20) — high certainty, small reduction.
- Calculus (12-monthly vs none): SMD **−0.19** (95% CI −0.31 to −0.07) — high certainty, small reduction.
- Probing depth, oral-health-related QoL: little/no difference (high certainty). Plaque: little/no difference (low certainty).

**Comparison 2 — 6-monthly vs 12-monthly recall:**
- Gingivitis: SMD **0.03** (95% CI −0.09 to 0.15; I²=0%) — high certainty, no difference.
- Calculus: SMD **−0.13** (95% CI −0.25 to −0.01) — 6-monthly reduces calculus slightly more; clinical importance unclear.

**Comparison 3 — dentist vs dental therapist/hygienist:** no studies.

- Patient self-reported oral cleanliness higher with scheduled treatment (low certainty). Costs uncertain (very low certainty). Neither trial measured adverse effects, attachment-level change, tooth loss, or halitosis.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 2 trials, both UK general practice, regular attenders — limited generalisability; no data on irregular attenders or higher-risk patients.
- No trial measured **adverse effects** of scaling (enamel/cementum wear, dentin hypersensitivity), a known concern.
- No head-to-head of operator type (dentist vs hygienist/therapist).
- Cost-effectiveness evidence very low certainty.
- Applies to routine prophylaxis in low-risk healthy adults — **not** to treatment of established gingivitis/periodontitis or supportive periodontal therapy in periodontitis patients.

## 7. Glossary

- **Routine scale and polish**: scaling/polishing of crown+root to remove plaque, calculus, debris, staining; no surgery, chemotherapeutics, or root planing.
- **PMPR (professional mechanical plaque removal)**: synonym for prophylaxis/periodontal instrumentation.
- **SMD**: standardised mean difference. **GRADE**: certainty-of-evidence framework.
- **Recall interval**: scheduled gap between routine dental hygiene visits (6- vs 12-monthly here).
