dental-anxiety 하위 카테고리에 "성인·생리학적 객관지표" 축이 필요. [[wiki/behavioral-dentistry/dental-anxiety/alhomoud-2023-behavior-anxiety-levels-pediatric-patient]]가 소아 행동척도라면, 본 관찰연구는 성인 근관치료(RCT) 중 MDAS·VAS·HR·SpO2를 6단계 측정해 불안→생리스트레스 연결을 정량화. 과거 부정적 경험이 불안을 높인다는 결과는 [[wiki/behavioral-dentistry/communication-relationship/ho-2025-overview-dentist-patient-communication-quality]]의 "소통이 불안 완화" 명제에 임상 근거 제공.

> 주의: 제목·stem의 "RCT"는 randomized controlled trial이 아니라 **root canal treatment(근관치료)**를 뜻함. 연구 설계는 단일군 관찰연구(observational).

## One-line Summary

Observational study (n=76 adults, single-visit root canal treatment) — preoperative MDAS anxiety and VAS pain with HR/SpO2 monitored at 6 stages; HR rose significantly during local anesthesia (93.9±8.2) and drilling (94.7±8.07), especially with moderate anxiety/severe pain (p<0.05); prior negative dental experience raised anxiety (p=0.018); moderate positive anxiety–HR correlation, SpO2 stable.

## 한줄요약

관찰연구(성인 76명, 단일내원 근관치료) — 술전 MDAS 불안·VAS 통증, HR/SpO2를 6단계 측정. 국소마취(93.9±8.2)·드릴링(94.7±8.07) 시 HR 유의 상승, 특히 중등도 불안·심한 통증군(p<0.05). 과거 부정적 치과경험자는 불안점수 유의 상승(p=0.018). 불안–HR 중등도 양의 상관, SpO2는 안정.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: The Journal of Contemporary Dental Practice 2025
- DOI: 10.5005/jp-journals-10024-3828
- Study type: Observational (single-cohort, prospective monitoring), n=76 adults aged 20–55 (mean 35.6±8.9)
- Instruments: MDAS (anxiety), VAS (pain); HR + SpO2 (pulse oximetry) at 6 procedural stages
- Analysis: descriptive, Pearson's correlation, Mann–Whitney

## 2. Key Contributions
- Links self-reported anxiety to objective physiological stress (HR) during a specific high-trigger procedure.
- Identifies the two highest-stress procedural moments: local anesthesia and drilling.
- Confirms prior negative experience as an anxiety amplifier — an actionable screening cue.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Six monitoring stages: preoperative, local anesthesia, access cavity, working-length determination, biomechanical preparation, 10-min post-procedure.
- Anxiety/pain stratification cross-tabulated with physiological response.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Moderate pain (VAS 4–6) in 47.36%.
- Significant HR elevation at local anesthesia (93.9±8.2) and drilling (94.7±8.07), esp. moderate-anxiety/severe-pain (p<0.05).
- SpO2 stable across all stages.
- Prior negative dental experience → higher anxiety (p=0.018); moderate positive anxiety–HR correlation.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Observational, single-cohort, modest n; no intervention/control.
- HR confounded by local-anesthetic vasoconstrictor (epinephrine) — physiological signal not purely anxiety.
- Single procedure type (RCT) → limited generalizability.

## 7. Glossary
- **MDAS**: Modified Dental Anxiety Scale.
- **VAS**: Visual Analog Scale (pain 0–10).
- **SpO2**: peripheral oxygen saturation (pulse oximetry).
- **RCT (here)**: root canal treatment (not randomized controlled trial).
