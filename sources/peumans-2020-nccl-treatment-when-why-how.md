## One-line Summary
Narrative review (abstract only) concluding NCCL management hinges on understanding multifactorial etiology, that adhesive composite restoration is the "last" option indicated by progression/sensitivity/esthetics, and that performance is adhesive- and operator-dependent (not composite-type-dependent).

## 2. Key Contributions
- Frames NCCL decision-making as when/why/how: monitor vs intervene based on progression, vitality, function, esthetics.
- Asserts composite-material type has no significant influence on clinical performance; the adhesive system and correct operator technique dominate.
- Positions adhesive restoration as the last option, optionally combined with root-coverage surgery.
- Stresses yearly maintenance with margin repolishing to extend restoration lifespan.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative clinical review. No systematic protocol (abstract-level information only).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- No quantitative outcomes in abstract; qualitative clinical guidance. General indication to place composite for NCCLs based on esthetics + clinical performance.
