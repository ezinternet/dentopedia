## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive narrative review of biomedical parameters influencing orthodontic tooth movement (OTM)
- Central molecular triad: OPG / RANK / RANKL — governs osteoclastogenesis and osteogenesis balance during OTM
- Osteoblasts produce OCN, ON, OPN, ALP, BSP, COL1A1/COL1A2; their signaling regulated by hormones, cytokines, prostaglandins, growth factors
- Bioengineering interventions reviewed: surgical (corticotomy, piezoelectric decortication), pharmacological (biomarker modulation), physical (LIPUS, vibration), gene therapy, stem cell approaches
- Vibration/LIPUS: promotes fracture healing and may accelerate OTM
- Limitation: scarcity of human studies; high cost and complexity limit clinical application

## 4. Key Results
| Intervention | OTM Effect |
|---|---|
| Corticotomy / decortication | Accelerates OTM (regional acceleratory phenomenon) |
| RANKL upregulation | Increases osteoclastogenesis → faster resorption |
| OPG | Inhibits RANKL → reduces bone resorption |
| LIPUS / vibration | Promotes bone healing; mixed OTM acceleration evidence |
| Stem cell approaches | Theoretically enhance regeneration; minimal human data |
| Gene therapy | Preclinical only; no accepted clinical application |

- No universally accepted biomarker for measuring or controlling OTM
- Human study data too scarce for definitive clinical recommendations

## 5. Limitations
- Narrative review; no systematic search protocol, no PRISMA, no risk of bias assessment
- Human studies underrepresented; most evidence from in vitro and animal models
- Rapidly evolving field; findings may be superseded
