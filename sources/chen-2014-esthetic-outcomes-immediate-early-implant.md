## One-line Summary
Systematic review (50 of 1,686 screened studies; 6 RCTs, 6 cohort, 5 cross-sectional, 33 case series of ≥5 patients on single-tooth maxillary anterior/premolar implants): immediate (type 1) placement carries a substantially higher rate of >1 mm midfacial mucosal recession (median 26%, range 9–41%, 1–3 years across 8 studies) than early (type 2/3) placement (no >1 mm recession in 2 studies); facial bone wall is undetectable on CBCT in 36–57% of type 1 grafted sites versus visible in >90% of type 2/3 contour-augmented sites.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Established the **quantitative esthetic-risk gap** between type 1 (immediate) and type 2/3 (early) placement at the level of objective indices and mucosal position changes.
- Documented that **midfacial recession >1 mm is common after immediate placement** (median ~1/4 of sites at 1–3 years), in contrast to early placement.
- Quantified the **frequency of absent/undetectable facial bone wall on CBCT** after type 1 placement with grafts: 36–57% — explaining the recession pattern observed clinically.
- Documented that **early placement combined with simultaneous contour augmentation (GBR)** preserves a detectable facial bone wall in >90% of cases.
- Served as evidence base for ITI consensus statements on immediate vs early placement and shaped subsequent case-selection criteria (thick biotype, intact facial wall).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Electronic + manual searches of dental literature for studies reporting **objective esthetic outcomes** of implants placed after extraction of maxillary anterior or premolar teeth.
- Inclusion: all levels of evidence; case series required ≥5 cases.
- 1,686 titles screened → 114 full-text reviewed → 50 records data-extracted.
- Outcomes: esthetic indices (predominantly the Pink Esthetic Score, PES), positional changes of peri-implant mucosa (especially midfacial mucosa), presence of facial bone on CBCT.
- Analysis: qualitative synthesis stratified by placement timing (type 1 immediate; type 2 early ~4–8 weeks; type 3 early with partial bone healing ~12–16 weeks); meta-analysis not performed due to heterogeneity in design, indices, and follow-up.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **No studies on multiple missing teeth**; evidence base is single-tooth implants.
- **Type 1 midfacial recession**: 8 studies, **9–41% of sites with >1 mm recession**, median 26%, 1–3 years post-placement.
- **Type 2/3 midfacial recession**: 2 studies, **0 sites with >1 mm recession**.
- **Facial bone visibility on CBCT after type 1 + graft**: 2 retrospective studies showed **facial bone undetectable in 36% and 57% of sites**; these sites had more midfacial recession than sites with detectable bone.
- **Facial bone visibility after type 2/3 + GBR contour augmentation**: 2 studies showed **>90% of sites with detectable facial bone**.
- Recent type 1 protocols imposed **selection criteria** (thick biotype, intact facial socket wall) to reduce esthetic risk.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- High heterogeneity (study design, indices, follow-up) precluded meta-analysis.
- Most evidence is case series; only 6 RCTs included.
- No multi-tooth data — conclusions limited to single-tooth replacements.
- Imaging artefact may cause CBCT to under-detect thin (<1 mm) facial bone.
- Outstanding questions identified: optimal biomaterials for facial bone reconstruction; long-term relationship between mucosal stability and facial bone presence/thickness/crestal position.

## 6. Related Work
- Araújo et al. 2006 — histologic basis of buccal collapse around immediate implants in dog model.
- Buser et al. — series of clinical studies on early placement with contour augmentation (type 2/3 + GBR).
- Subsequent SRs (Chen, Buser, Wismeijer post-2018) refining selection criteria for type 1.
- Pink Esthetic Score (Fürhauser 2005) — the dominant index used by included studies.

## 7. Glossary
- **Type 1 placement (immediate)**: implant placed in fresh extraction socket at the time of extraction.
- **Type 2 placement (early, soft-tissue healing)**: implant placed 4–8 weeks after extraction, after soft tissue closure but before significant bone fill.
- **Type 3 placement (early, partial bone healing)**: implant placed 12–16 weeks after extraction, after substantial bone fill but before full ridge maturation.
- **Type 4 placement (late)**: implant placed >6 months after extraction, in a healed ridge.
- **PES (Pink Esthetic Score)**: 0–14 scale for the esthetic quality of peri-implant soft tissue (Fürhauser 2005).
- **GBR (Guided Bone Regeneration)**: barrier-membrane-based augmentation, used here for "contour augmentation" alongside type 2/3 placement.
- **Contour augmentation**: simultaneous facial bone augmentation at early implant placement to over-build the facial contour for esthetic outcome.
