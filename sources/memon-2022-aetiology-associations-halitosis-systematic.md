## Why Ingested

구강 악취(halitosis)의 원인 분류 체계 — 구강내 요인(intra-oral) 80–90 % vs. 구강외 요인(extra-oral) 10–20 % — 를 근거 중심으로 정리하기 위해 수집. 현재 halitosis 카테고리를 개설하는 첫 번째 페이퍼로, 이후 역학·치료 SR들의 분류 참조틀(reference framework)을 제공한다. [[halitosis/memon-2022-aetiology-associations-halitosis-systematic]] 자체가 기반 페이지로 기능.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (19 studies, 2014–2020) concluding that 80–90% of halitosis is intra-oral in origin, with coated tongue, periodontal disease, and poor oral hygiene as the leading factors.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(19편, 2014–2020): 구강 악취의 80–90 %는 구강내 원인(설태·치주질환·구강위생 불량)이며, 10–20 %는 구강외 전신질환에 기인함.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Oral Diseases (Wiley)
- **Published**: 2022 (online); print 2023;29:1432–1438
- **DOI**: 10.1111/odi.14172
- **Study type**: Systematic review (no meta-analysis)
- **Databases searched**: MEDLINE (PubMed), Google Scholar, Wiley Online Library
- **Date range of included studies**: January 2014 – December 2020
- **Included studies**: 19 (12 cross-sectional, 2 RCTs, 5 case-control)
- **Funding**: None declared

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Provides a structured taxonomy of halitosis aetiology: physiologic vs. pathologic (intra-oral vs. extra-oral).
2. Confirms the 80–90% intra-oral origin figure with contemporary evidence from 2014–2020.
3. Identifies coated tongue (tongue coating) as the single most consistently cited intra-oral factor across study designs.
4. Maps extra-oral causes to ENT disorders, gastrointestinal/renal conditions, systemic diseases (Helicobacter pylori, viral hepatitis B, Parkinson's disease, allergic rhinitis).
5. Notes that VSC chemistry differs by source: methyl mercaptan + hydrogen sulphide → intra-oral; dimethyl sulphide → extra-oral.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search strategy**: Keywords 'halitosis', 'bad-breath', 'oral malodour causes and aetiology' in PubMed, Google Scholar, Wiley Online Library.
- **Inclusion criteria**: English-language articles, 2014–2020, empirical studies (case-control, RCT, cross-sectional) reporting aetiologic factors of halitosis.
- **Exclusion criteria**: Non-English articles (n=8), aetiology not defined (n=12), full text unavailable (n=3), short communications (n=2).
- **Screening**: Two independent authors screened titles/abstracts; full-text review when insufficient information.
- **Initial hits**: 1490 → 474 (date filter + deduplication) → 44 (after abstract screening) → 19 (final).
- **No quantitative synthesis** (heterogeneity in study designs and outcome measures precluded meta-analysis).
- **Quality assessment**: Not formally reported (a limitation acknowledged by the authors).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Intra-oral factors (80–90% of cases)
| Factor | Evidence base |
|---|---|
| Coated tongue (tongue coating) | Consistently identified across all 3 study designs (cross-sectional, RCT, case-control) |
| Periodontal disease | Cross-sectional + case-control; pathogens: *P. gingivalis*, *T. forsythia*, *T. denticola*, *Prevotella* spp. |
| Poor oral hygiene | Cross-sectional; associated with higher VSC levels |
| Higher saliva viscosity | Cross-sectional (Komori et al. 2016) |

### Extra-oral factors (10–20% of cases)
| Category | Specific conditions |
|---|---|
| ENT | Adenoid hypertrophy, allergic rhinitis, ENT disorders generally |
| Gastrointestinal | *Helicobacter pylori* infection |
| Hepatic/infectious | Viral hepatitis B |
| Neurological | Parkinson's disease |
| Iatrogenic/orthodontic | Fixed orthodontic appliances |
| Psychiatric | Depression (reduces oral hygiene motivation) |

### VSC chemistry
- **Methyl mercaptan + hydrogen sulphide** → predominantly intra-oral halitosis
- **Dimethyl sulphide** → elevated in extra-oral halitosis

### Detection methods noted
- Organoleptic scoring (1–5 scale) — gold standard
- Gas chromatography (GC) — saliva, breath, tongue debris
- Halimeter® — portable VSC measurement

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- No formal risk-of-bias or quality assessment tool applied to included studies.
- No meta-analysis; quantitative pooling not possible due to heterogeneity.
- Article search limited to 3 databases (PubMed, Google Scholar, Wiley); may miss conference proceedings or other databases.
- 2014–2020 publication window; older foundational studies excluded.
- 'Bad breath' and 'oral malodour' as isolated search terms without MeSH headings may have reduced sensitivity.
- Authors call for more mechanistic research on pathophysiological processes of oral malodour in otherwise healthy individuals.

## 6. Related Work

- Bollen & Beikler 2012 — organoleptic scoring as gold standard and VSC chemistry review
- Yaegaki et al. 2014 — classification framework (physiological vs. pathological; pseudo vs. genuine halitosis)
- Rösing & Loesche 2011 — food, tobacco, alcohol as halitosis contributors
- Takeuchi et al. 2017 — oral microbiome and VSC production mechanisms

## 7. Glossary

- **VSC (Volatile Sulphur Compounds)**: 휘발성 황화합물 — primary chemical mediators of oral malodour (H₂S, CH₃SH, (CH₃)₂S).
- **Organoleptic scoring**: 관능적 평가 — direct sniff test by trained examiner, scale 0–5; gold standard for halitosis assessment.
- **Coated tongue (tongue coating)**: 설태 — accumulation of bacteria, desquamated epithelial cells, and food debris on the dorsal tongue surface; primary site of VSC production.
- **Pseudo-halitosis**: 가성 구취 — patient-perceived malodour not confirmed by examiner; psychogenic component.
- **Halimeter®**: 휘발성 황화합물 측정기 — portable electrochemical device measuring total VSC concentration in expired breath.
- **Intra-oral halitosis**: 구강내 구취 — malodour originating within the oral cavity; 80–90% of cases.
- **Extra-oral halitosis**: 구강외 구취 — malodour from systemic, ENT, or GI sources; 10–20% of cases.
