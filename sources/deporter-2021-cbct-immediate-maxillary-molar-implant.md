## One-line Summary
Retrospective CBCT study of 427 maxillary molar sites in 223 patients evaluating inter-radicular septum (IRS) width and furcation-to-sinus-floor distance (FSD) for immediate molar implant (IMI) candidacy. Only 61.7% of first molars and 34% of second molars have IRS broad enough to fully contain a 5 mm-diameter implant; mean FSD ~9 mm suggests most cases need sinus elevation if longer implants are used.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Advanced Periodontology and Implant Dentistry 2021;13(2):49–55
- **Institutions**: University of Toronto (Canada); Islamic Azad University Isfahan; Lahijan maxillofacial radiology center (Iran)
- **Study type**: Retrospective CBCT-based cross-sectional cohort
- **Period**: 2016–2019, two centers (Toronto + Lahijan)

## 2. Key Contributions
- First study to provide **numerical IRS dimensions** at crestal, middle, and apical levels for maxillary first vs second molars in a large mixed-ethnicity cohort.
- Quantifies the percentage of maxillary molar sites suitable for immediate molar implantation under Smith & Tarnow Type A criteria.
- Reports root-by-root sinus intrusion rates (palatal vs buccal roots) — clinically useful for sinus perforation risk forecasting.
- Cross-population (Iran + Canada) comparison.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 223 patients, 427 dental sites (maxillary first and/or second molars).
- All CBCT scans: Sirona Galileos, 98 kVp / 12 mA, Galileos viewer 1.9 software.
- Measurements (mean of two reads, 4-week interval):
  - **IRS buccopalatal width (BPW)** and **mesiodistal width (MDW)** at three levels: crestal (0.5 mm apical to furcation), middle, apical.
  - **FSD**: furcation to sinus floor distance.
  - **Root apex to sinus floor distance** for each root.
  - **Sinus intrusion** of each root.
- Exclusions: periodontitis, periapical pathology, sinus pathology, prior surgery, metal restorations, drug abuse, chemotherapy.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **First molar IRS, coronal**: BPW 6.52 mm, MDW 7.33 mm.
- **Second molar IRS, coronal**: BPW 5.85 mm, MDW 6.86 mm (p = 0.008 vs first molar).
- **FSD**: first molar 9.69 mm (range 2.02–24.68); second molar 8.84 mm (range 1.48–25.09).
- **Mean root-apex-to-SF distance**: <3 mm for all roots — high sinus intrusion risk.
- **Sinus intrusion rates**:
  - First molar palatal roots: 28.85%
  - Second molar mesiobuccal roots: 37.65% (highest)
- **% with sufficient IRS for ≥5 mm IMI**: 61.7% of first molars, 34% of second molars.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Retrospective, no clinical outcome data — IRS sufficiency does not guarantee osseointegration.
- Mixed ethnicity (Iran + Canada) sample; not stratified.
- Healthy non-diseased sites — may overestimate IRS preserved in periodontally compromised molars.
- 5 mm implant chosen as cutoff; smaller-diameter implants would expand candidacy.

## 6. Related Work
- Smith & Tarnow 2013 — IRS Type A/B/C classification.
- Atieh 2010 / Mello 2017 — IMI survival meta-analyses.
- Lazzara 1989 — original immediate implant concept.
- Schropp 2003 — extraction socket dimensional change.

## 7. Glossary
- **IMI (Immediate Molar Implant)**: implant placed at time of molar extraction into the same socket.
- **IRS (Inter-Radicular Septum)**: bone between molar roots; primary anchorage source for IMI.
- **FSD (Furcation-Sinus-Floor distance)**: vertical bone height from furcation roof to sinus floor.
- **Smith & Tarnow Type A/B/C**: classification of IRS adequacy for IMI placement.
- **Sinus intrusion**: root apex protrusion above sinus floor; predictor of perforation risk on extraction.
