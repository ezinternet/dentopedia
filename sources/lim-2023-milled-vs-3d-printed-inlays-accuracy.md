## 2. Key Contributions
- Four fabrication methods compared: conventional, milled hybrid, milled zirconia, 3D-printed
- Marginal and internal fitness evaluated at 6 points per mesio-distal section
- 3D accuracy assessed using digital superimposition software
- All CAD/CAM methods showed acceptable marginal fit

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
In vitro comparison of marginal/internal fitness and 3D accuracy of Class II inlays fabricated by: (1) conventional resin (Tescera/TS), (2) milled hybrid ceramic (Lava Ultimate/LU), (3) milled zirconia (Zolid Fx/ZR), and (4) 3D-printed resin (NextDent C&B). n=13 per group (total 52). Digital microscope at 160× magnification for marginal/internal fit; 3D software for accuracy assessment.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Four fabrication methods compared: conventional, milled hybrid, milled zirconia, 3D-printed
- Marginal and internal fitness evaluated at 6 points per mesio-distal section
- 3D accuracy assessed using digital superimposition software
- All CAD/CAM methods showed acceptable marginal fit
