## Why Ingested

Attachment system selection for implant-supported mandibular overdentures is a core clinical decision in complete denture prosthodontics, yet no consensus existed on which retention type optimizes survival and minimizes complications. This SR+MA of 25 RCTs fills that gap and directly informs the clinical framework in [[complete-denture/alhajj-2017-occlusal-vertical-dimension-complete-denture-review]] by clarifying the prosthetic design decisions that affect long-term overdenture success.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 25 RCTs (2154 implants, 737 overdentures) found high implant (98%) and prosthesis (95.8%) survival regardless of attachment type, with LOCATOR having fewest prosthetic complications and magnets having 7.4× more than other attachments.

## 한줄요약

25개 RCT를 포함한 SR+MA (임플란트 2154개, 피개의치 737개)에서 어태치먼트 종류와 무관하게 임플란트 98%, 피개의치 95.8%의 높은 생존율을 보였으며, LOCATOR가 보철 합병증이 가장 적고 마그넷은 다른 어태치먼트보다 7.4배 높은 보철 합병증을 나타냈다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Comprehensive RCT-only evidence base**: First SR+MA restricted exclusively to RCTs comparing attachment systems, excluding retrospective/observational data.
2. **Attachment-specific complication ranking**: Quantified prosthetic complication rates by attachment type — LOCATOR < telescopic/Conus < bar < ball < magnet.
3. **Splinted vs unsplinted equivalence**: Demonstrated no significant difference in biological complications between splinted (bar) and unsplinted (ball, LOCATOR, magnet) overdentures (P=.902).
4. **Magnet complication burden quantified**: Magnet attachments had 7.4× higher prosthetic complications than all other attachment types combined.
5. **Overall high survival**: Implant failure rate 2.0% (95% CI 1.3–3.2%); overdenture failure rate 4.2% (95% CI 1.6–10.5%).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

### Search Strategy
- Databases: PubMed, Cochrane Library, Embase, Scopus
- Date range: Up to search cutoff (2021–2022)
- PRISMA-compliant reporting; PROSPERO-registered (CRD42021253566)
- Initial yield: 477 studies → 25 RCTs included after eligibility screening

### Eligibility Criteria
- Randomized controlled clinical trials only
- Mandibular 2-implant overdentures (primarily 2-implant, some multi-implant)
- Reported implant and/or overdenture survival/complication outcomes
- Minimum follow-up: varied (studies ranged from 1–10 years)

### Attachment Systems Compared
| System | Type |
|--------|------|
| Ball (stud) | Unsplinted |
| LOCATOR | Unsplinted |
| Magnet | Unsplinted |
| Bar-clip | Splinted |
| Telescopic / Conus | Splinted |

### Statistical Approach
- Pooled failure rates with 95% confidence intervals (random-effects model)
- Association analysis between attachment type and implant/overdenture survival
- RoB 2 tool for risk-of-bias assessment per included RCT

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Overall Survival
| Outcome | Failure Rate | 95% CI |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Implant failure | 2.0% | 1.3–3.2% |
| Overdenture failure | 4.2% | 1.6–10.5% |

### Attachment-Specific Implant Failure Rates
| Attachment | Failure Rate | 95% CI |
|------------|-------------|--------|
| Bar-type | 7.7% | 3.0–18.1% |
| Magnet | 7.6% | 2.2–22.7% |
| Ball | 6.8% | 3.0–14.3% |

*Note: Overlapping CIs indicate no statistically significant differences between attachment types for implant survival.*

### Prosthetic Complications Ranking (best to worst)
1. LOCATOR — fewest complications
2. Telescopic / Conus
3. Bar
4. Ball
5. Magnet — 7.4× higher prosthetic complications than others

### Biological Complications
- Splinted vs unsplinted overdentures: P=.902 (no significant difference)
- Marginal bone loss patterns similar across attachment systems

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Heterogeneity across RCTs in follow-up duration, patient demographics, implant systems used, and loading protocols limits direct comparability
- LOCATOR system underrepresented in early RCT literature; recent trials skew results
- Publication bias possible — studies with catastrophic failure may not be published
- Patient-reported outcomes (OHRQoL, satisfaction) not consistently reported across included RCTs
- Long-term (>5 year) RCT data sparse for newer attachment systems (LOCATOR)
- Cost-effectiveness not addressed in this meta-analysis
- Future RCTs needed with standardized outcome reporting (CONSORT) and longer follow-up

## 6. Related Work

- Cune et al. (2010): 10-year crossover RCT ball vs bar — foundational long-term data
- Naert et al. (2004): 10-year RCT splinted vs unsplinted peri-implant outcomes
- Thomason et al. (2009): York Consensus — mandibular 2-implant overdenture as standard of care
- Andreiotelli et al. (2010): Systematic review of prosthodontic complications with implant overdentures

## 7. Glossary

- **LOCATOR attachment**: Low-profile unsplinted retention system with replaceable nylon male inserts; uses two-part design (titanium abutment + nylon matrix)
- **Bar-clip (splinted)**: Metal bar connecting two or more implants; overdenture clips onto bar — distributes force across implants
- **Ball attachment (stud)**: Spherical male abutment fitting into rubber/metal female cap in denture base; simplest unsplinted design
- **Magnet retention**: Pairs of rare-earth magnets in abutment and denture base; relies on magnetic attraction for retention
- **Telescopic/Conus crowns**: Double-crown system with inner (primary) and outer (secondary) crowns; provides friction-based retention
- **Splinted overdenture**: Implants connected via bar or other rigid structure before prosthesis attachment
- **Unsplinted overdenture**: Each implant retains independently via individual stud/ball/LOCATOR/magnet attachments
- **Biological complications**: Peri-implant mucositis, peri-implantitis, marginal bone loss
- **Prosthetic complications**: Attachment fracture/wear, denture fracture, need for relining/rebasing, matrix replacement
- **RoB 2**: Cochrane revised risk-of-bias tool for randomized trials (Sterne et al., 2019)
- **PRISMA**: Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses
