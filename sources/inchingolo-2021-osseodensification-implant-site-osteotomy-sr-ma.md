## One-line Summary
SR + meta-analysis (16 articles, 11 clinical studies) — osseodensification raises insertion torque and BIC vs conventional drilling, with significant difference in BIC and torque between clockwise vs counter-clockwise OD mode and no difference in BAFO.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Systematic review with meta-analysis (animal studies pooled)
- **Journal**: Materials 2021, 14, 1147
- **DOI**: 10.3390/ma14051147
- **Sample**: 16 articles — 11 clinical (8 animal, 3 human), 4 literature reviews, 1 case report
- **Primary Question**: Effectiveness of OD drilling protocol for implant site preparation vs conventional drilling, with sub-comparison of clockwise vs counter-clockwise OD modes

## 2. Key Contributions
- Pools animal-study data for three outcomes: BIC %, BAFO %, and insertion torque, comparing clockwise (cutting) vs counter-clockwise (densifying) OD.
- Reports significant difference in BIC and insertion torque between clockwise vs counter-clockwise OD; **no difference in BAFO** between the two modes.
- Confirms OD > conventional drilling on insertion torque in vivo.
- Authors call for randomized clinical trials in humans — explicit acknowledgement that the strong evidence is animal-based.
- Updates the 2018-vintage Padhye review by adding three more years of literature and a quantitative pooling step.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
PubMed (Medline) database search identifying 16 papers: 11 clinical (8 animal, 3 human), 4 literature reviews, 1 case report. The meta-analytic component was restricted to animal studies because of homogeneity in outcome reporting (BIC %, BAFO %, insertion torque) and the scarcity of human data. The comparison structure pools clockwise OD vs counter-clockwise OD vs conventional drilling.

The narrative review portion situates OD as an alternative to conventional subtractive drilling and to Summers' osteotome condensation, with the proposed advantage that the Densah bur generates an autograft layer along the osteotomy wall via controlled lateral deformation rather than impaction. PRISMA reporting framework with screening and inclusion/exclusion stages documented in the body.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Included articles: 16 total (11 clinical, 4 reviews, 1 case report); meta-analysis run on the animal subset.
- Significant increase in **insertion torque** for OD vs conventional drilling.
- Significant difference in **BIC** between clockwise vs counter-clockwise OD modes.
- **No significant difference in BAFO** between clockwise vs counter-clockwise OD.
- Specific pooled effect sizes, 95% CIs, and I² heterogeneity statistics are not extractable from the available front/back text — see PDF tables for numeric results.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Meta-analysis restricted to animal studies; clinical evidence in humans is still limited (only 3 human clinical studies included).
- Search limited to PubMed-Medline (Padhye 2020 also searched Embase and Google Scholar) — narrower base.
- Substantial overlap of authorship/affiliation across the OD literature pool (Coelho/Neiva/Witek/Tovar cluster) — independence of included studies is a concern.
- Front/back text does not surface pooled effect magnitudes — readers must consult the body tables.
- Heterogeneity quantification (I², τ²) not extractable from the available text.

## 7. Glossary
- **Clockwise (cutting) mode**: Densah bur run in standard direction — subtractive entry to depth.
- **Counter-clockwise (densifying) mode**: reverse rotation — compacts bone laterally and apically along the osteotomy wall.
- **BIC, BAFO**: see Padhye 2020 source.
- **PRISMA**: SR reporting standard.
