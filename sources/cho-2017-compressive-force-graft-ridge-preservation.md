## Why Ingested

ARP에서 거의 다뤄지지 않은 변수 — graft 충전 시 가해지는 압축력(compressive force)이 신생골 형성에 미치는 영향. Park 그룹 open-healing 연작([[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/park-2016-hidden-x-suture-ridge-preservation]], [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/choi-2017-single-double-layer-collagen-membrane-arp]]) 중 술기 압력 변수 편이며, 후속 [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/lee-2020-compressive-force-dbbm-ridge-preservation-molar]]가 같은 질문을 DBBM 대구치에서 재검증.

## One-line Summary
RCT (20 completers): greater compressive force on graft biomaterials during ARP significantly increased new bone formation while preserving ridge dimensions equally to lower force.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: J Periodontal Implant Sci 2017 Feb;47(1):51
- First author: In-Woo Cho (corresponding: Jung-Chul Park)
- Type: randomized controlled clinical trial
- DOI: 10.5051/jpis.2017.47.1.51

## 2. Key Contributions
- Isolates compressive force during grafting as an ARP variable
- Histomorphometric evidence that greater force → more new bone
- Dimensional preservation unaffected by force level

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Design: RCT (4 dropouts from control; 20 finished).
- Test vs control compressive force during ARP grafting; CBCT + histomorphometry.
- Outcomes: ridge volume (CBCT), new bone ratio (histomorphometry), Periotest values (PTV).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Ridge volume preserved comparably vertically and horizontally in both groups (P>0.05).
- New bone formation ratio significantly greater in the greater-force (test) group (P<0.05).
- PTVs: no significant between-group difference (P>0.05).
- Conclusion: greater compressive force enhanced new bone formation while preserving dimensions; optimal force still to be defined.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small sample with dropouts; optimal compressive force not identified.
- Single-center, single-group surgeon.
