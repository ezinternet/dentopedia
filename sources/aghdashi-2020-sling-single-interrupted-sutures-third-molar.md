## Why Ingested

Part of a focused collection on suture techniques and primary wound closure after impacted mandibular third molar surgery. This split-mouth RCT is the only paper in the cluster that isolates the *suture geometry* variable (sling vs single interrupted) and reports periodontal endpoints (PPD/CAL at the adjacent second molar) rather than the usual pain/swelling/trismus triad — complementing [[suture-wound-closure/kumar-2022-suture-versus-sutureless-third-molar-impactions]] and [[suture-wound-closure/pachipulusu-2018-primary-secondary-closure-third-molar]], which report no periodontal difference by closure type.

## One-line Summary
Split-mouth RCT (n=25 patients) comparing sling vs single interrupted sutures after impacted mandibular third molar removal; sling sutures gave significantly better PPD (P=0.041) and CAL (P=0.016) at the adjacent second molar, with no significant long-term difference in wound dehiscence.

## 한줄요약
하악 매복 사랑니 발치 후 sling 봉합 대 단순 단속 봉합을 비교한 분할구강 무작위배정 임상시험(환자 25명). Sling 봉합이 인접 제2대구치의 치주낭 깊이(PPD, P=0.041)와 임상부착소실(CAL, P=0.016)에서 유의하게 우수했고, 봉합 파열(dehiscence)에는 장기적으로 유의한 차이가 없었다.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First reported head-to-head comparison of sling vs single interrupted suturing after impacted/semi-impacted mandibular third molar extraction, focused on periodontal outcomes of the adjacent second molar.
- Demonstrates that suture *geometry* (where the knot sits relative to the dehiscence-prone distal surface of the second molar) — not just presence/absence of sutures — affects periodontal recovery.
- Provides a mechanistic rationale: in the sling technique the knot lies away from the distal surface of the second molar where dehiscence usually occurs, reducing plaque-driven inflammation at that site.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: Split-mouth RCT; each of 25 patients with bilateral similar mandibular third molar impaction received sling suture on one side and single interrupted suture on the other; side allocation randomized; patient blinded.
- **Population**: Ages 18–25, non-smokers, systemically healthy, not pregnant/nursing, no medication in prior 6 months, good oral hygiene (OHI-S 0–1.2), plaque index 0–1, no acute pericoronitis/periodontal disease.
- **Surgery**: Same surgeon, local anesthesia (lidocaine + epinephrine, IANB), full-thickness triangular flap (#15 scalpel), sulcular incision, osteotomy with rotary instrument.
- **Outcomes**: Dehiscence (Michigan O probe, Williams markings) at days 7 and 14; PPD and CAL at distobuccal point of adjacent second molar at baseline and ~17 weeks (median; mean ~22 weeks) postop. Assessor (dental student) blinded.
- **Sample size**: Calculated 18–20 based on Cetinkaya et al (1 mm CAL difference, SD 0.9 mm, α=0.05, β=0.1).
- **Statistics**: Wilcoxon signed-rank test; generalized estimating equation (GEE) regression.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **PPD**: Sling suture significantly superior (P=0.041).
- **CAL**: Sling suture significantly superior (P=0.016).
- **Dehiscence at day 7**: Smaller in the single interrupted group (P=0.059 — borderline, reported as significant in abstract text).
- **Dehiscence at day 14**: No significant difference (P=0.852).
- **Conclusion**: Sling suture superior for PPD and CAL; suturing technique has no significant long-term effect on wound dehiscence.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small sample (25 patients), young (18–25), healthy, non-smoker population — limited generalizability.
- Single center, single surgeon.
- Borderline day-7 dehiscence finding (P=0.059) reported as significant in the abstract; interpret cautiously.
- DOI not printed in the article.

## 7. Glossary
- **PPD (Probing Pocket Depth)**: distance from gingival margin to pocket base (mm).
- **CAL (Clinical Attachment Loss)**: distance from CEJ to pocket base (mm).
- **Dehiscence**: separation of wound edges (buccal–lingual mucosa gap).
- **Sling suture**: continuous/looped suture wrapped around a tooth so the knot lies away from the at-risk distal surface.
- **OHI-S**: Simplified Oral Hygiene Index (debris + calculus index).
- **GEE**: Generalized Estimating Equation regression (for correlated repeated/split-mouth data).
