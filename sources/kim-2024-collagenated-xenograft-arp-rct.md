## Why Ingested

Three-arm RCT comparing collagenated DBBM (DBBM-C, Straumann XenoFlex) with and without socket seal (FGG) vs spontaneous healing — high-quality RCT evaluating a collagen-composite xenograft product with CBCT, histology, 3D volumetric analysis, and ISQ measurement at 180 days. Provides high-evidence clinical data on a specific formulation. [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/fischer-2022-bone-envelope-arp-dbbm-sr-ma]]

## One-line Summary

Three-arm RCT (n=50 completed, 180-day follow-up) showing DBBM-C with and without FGG socket seal preserved alveolar ridge volume better than spontaneous healing, with FGG seal enhancing horizontal ridge stability at 1 mm below crest; ISQ >70 in all groups.

## 한줄요약

3군 RCT (n=50, 180일): DBBM-C ± FGG 소켓실링 모두 자연치유 대비 치조골 부피 유의하게 보존, FGG 실링은 치조정 1 mm 하방 수평폭경 추가 유지 효과; 모든 군 ISQ ≥70.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: International Dental Journal (Seoul National University edition, Elsevier/FDI)
- **Volume/Issue**: 75 (2025): 1155–1164 (available online August 2024)
- **Study design**: Single-blind, parallel, 3-arm RCT
- **Sample**: 54 enrolled; 50 completed (4 dropouts: 2 loss-to-follow-up, 2 osseointegration failure)
- **Follow-up**: 180 days (ARP) + implant placement at 180 days + post-implant assessment
- **Setting**: Department of Periodontology, Seoul National University Dental Hospital, Korea
- **Period**: June 2021 – July 2023
- **Ethics**: IRB Seoul National University Dental Hospital; KCT0008266 (WHO ICTRP)
- **Material tested**: DBBM-C (OCS-B Collagen / Straumann XenoFlex, NIBEC, Korea) — deproteinized cancellous bovine bone mineral granules + 10% porcine collagen, block/syringe form

## 2. Key Contributions

- Evaluates DBBM-C (collagen-composite xenograft) vs spontaneous healing — addresses whether added collagen component improves conventional DBBM outcomes
- Three-arm design isolates FGG socket sealing as independent variable
- CBCT + 3D volumetric analysis + histology + ISQ — most comprehensive endpoint battery in recent ARP RCTs
- Between 84–180 days, DBBM-C groups maintained volume at 3 mm below crest; control group lost volume
- FGG seal specifically improved horizontal dimension at 1 mm below crest (p=0.049) — suggests sealing is most important at the crestal level

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Inclusion**: 18–80 years, single tooth requiring extraction + future implant, >2 alveolar bone walls, healthy systemic status, plaque index and BOP ≤20%.

**Exclusion**: Systemic disease (unstable angina, CVA within 6 months, renal/hepatic dysfunction, uncontrolled DM), investigator judgment.

**Randomization**: Computer-generated 1:1:1, non-involved researcher, ascending enrollment order.

**Groups**:
- Control: Spontaneous healing (no graft, no seal)
- DBBM-C: Collagenated xenograft placed in socket, open healing (no membrane/FGG)
- DBBM-C/FGG: Collagenated xenograft + free gingival graft from hard palate as socket seal

**ARP procedure**:
1. Atraumatic extraction with minimal flap reflection under local anesthesia
2. Socket irrigation and granulation tissue removal
3. DBBM-C hydrated with sterile saline, packed into socket
4. DBBM-C group: open healing, suturing
5. DBBM-C/FGG group: FGG from palate sutured over socket

**Imaging and analysis**:
- CBCT (Kodak CS 9300): 250 µm voxel, 80 kVP, 8 mA — immediately post-extraction, 84 days, 180 days
- 3D volumetric analysis of ridge contour
- Histological analysis of bone biopsy at implant placement (180 days)
- ISQ (implant stability quotient) at implant placement

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Control | DBBM-C | DBBM-C/FGG | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Volume change 84–180 days at 3 mm below crest | −0.37 ± 1.31 mm / −2.10% | +0.72 ± 0.80 mm / +6.05% | maintained | 0.026 |
| Horizontal width at 1 mm below crest (pre-extraction to 84 days) | reference | reference | −9.19 ± 5.09 mm / −73.67% | 0.049 |
| ISQ at implant placement | ≥70 | ≥70 | ≥70 | NS |
| Alveolar bone change overall | not significantly different between groups | | | NS for primary endpoint |

- Primary CBCT endpoint (total bone dimensional change) did not reach significance — but the 84–180 day subperiod analysis showed significant volume maintenance with DBBM-C at 3 mm below crest
- FGG seal reduced horizontal resorption at crestal 1 mm level (p=0.049) — clinically important for esthetic implant placement
- ISQ ≥70 in all groups: adequate osseointegration regardless of ARP method
- Histology: similar new bone formation across groups at 180 days; DBBM-C showed residual graft particles (slow resorption expected)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Study not registered prior to recruitment and randomization (post-hoc registration)
- Primary endpoint (total CBCT bone change) not statistically significant — risk of type II error with n=18/group
- Only 180-day follow-up — long-term graft resorption and implant survival not reported
- FGG harvesting is an additional surgical procedure with donor site morbidity
- Single center (Seoul National University) — may not generalize to different population bone densities

## 6. Related Work

- DBBM-C is a composite product (collagen + deproteinized DBBM granules); differs from pure DBBM (Bio-Oss) by handling characteristics and faster initial hemostasis
- Fischer 2022 SR+MA: DBBM-family xenografts consistently outperform allografts in ridge dimension preservation
- Socket seal with FGG consistent with prior evidence on soft-tissue-level protection

## 7. Glossary

- **DBBM-C**: Deproteinized bovine bone mineral with collagen — collagenated xenograft; here: OCS-B Collagen / Straumann XenoFlex (NIBEC, Korea), cancellous granules + 10% porcine collagen
- **FGG (Free Gingival Graft)**: Autogenous soft tissue graft from hard palate used to seal socket orifice (socket seal technique)
- **Socket seal technique**: Placing graft material followed by mucosal graft to close socket opening — avoids membrane exposure
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: Resonance frequency analysis output; >70 = high stability
- **3D volumetric analysis**: CBCT-based superimposition measuring net volume change of alveolar ridge from pre-extraction baseline
- **Open healing**: Socket graft exposed to oral cavity without membrane or graft coverage
