## Why Ingested

기존 [[immediate-implant/esthetic-soft-tissue/de-angelis-2021-immediate-implant-provisionalization-soft-tissue-retrospective]] 등 즉시식립·즉시 임시수복(immediate provisionalization) 페이지는 대부분 implant-supported provisional을 전제로 하고, 따라서 충분한 1차 안정성(primary stability)이 필수 조건이다. 본 RCT (Wu 2026)는 1차 안정성이 부족한 esthetic zone 외상치 증례에서 implant를 지지로 쓰지 않고 환자 자연치관(natural crown) + 섬유강화복합 스플린트(FRC splint)를 인접치에 고정하는 non-implant-supported 임시수복이라는 다른 분기를 평가해, 기존 immediate provisionalization 적응증을 "1차 안정성 미달 케이스"로 확장한다. PES 보존·술후 통증 감소 근거로 활용.

## One-line Summary

Exploratory RCT (n=20, anterior maxilla IIP, 4-5 mo) in which immediate non-implant-supported provisionalization using the patient's own natural crown fixed with an FRC splint to adjacent teeth preserved pink esthetic score (PES) and reduced early postoperative pain/swelling vs delayed restoration, with no significant difference in gingival height change.

## 한줄요약

탐색적 RCT (전치부 상악 즉시식립 IIP, n=20, 4-5개월): 환자 자연치관 + 섬유강화복합 스플린트(FRC)를 인접치에 고정하는 비-임플란트지지 즉시 임시수복이 지연수복 대비 분홍심미점수(PES)를 유지하고 술후 초기 통증·종창을 줄였으며, 치은 높이 변화에는 군간 유의차가 없었다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Tests a **non-implant-supported** fixed immediate provisional for IIP — the patient's own extracted natural crown, hollowed and resealed, bonded to adjacent teeth with an FRC splint on the **labial** surfaces (chair-side, no lab prep).
- Decouples immediate provisionalization from the usual hard prerequisite of high primary stability, since stabilization comes from adjacent teeth, not the implant. Extends the eligible population to esthetic-zone trauma cases where immediate loading would be contraindicated.
- Shows PES preservation (esthetic benefit) AND reduced early postoperative pain/swelling vs a delayed-restoration control over 4-5 months.
- Positions the technique against alternatives: superior to Essix/RPD (avoids transient edentulism, better soft-tissue support) and more practical than a Maryland bridge (labial FRC avoids occlusal interference; natural crown gives exact morphology/color/emergence match).
- Uses matched pre/post **intraoral optical scans** (3Shape Unite + ImageJ scale) for objective linear gingival-height measurement, reducing photographic-angle and probe-inclination error.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Population (n=20):** adults (mean 35.6 y, range 23-53; 7 M / 13 F) needing implant restoration of traumatized maxillary anterior teeth. 20 implants, all anterior maxilla.
- **Key inclusion:** Type I socket with intact labial bone wall; preoperative labial bone thickness >= 0.5 mm; adjacent teeth present mesiodistally; mouth opening >= 30 mm; stable adjacent occlusion. CBCT planning.
- **Exclusion:** insufficient space, uncontrolled adjacent caries/periodontitis, uncontrolled systemic disease, bone-metabolism-affecting drugs/radiotherapy, heavy smoking (>10/day).
- **Surgery (both groups, same surgeon):** crevicular incision, minimally invasive extraction, micro-crestal flap (mesial/distal papillae split labial/palatal), 3.3 mm bone-level tapered implant (Straumann) along palatal wall with shoulder at crest, Bio-Oss Collagen into jumping gap.
- **Randomization:** simple, SPSS 21.0 time-based seed, sealed opaque envelopes; operator blinded until extraction; odd = experimental, even = control. Two calibrated blinded examiners for PES + papilla height.
- **Experimental provisional:** root removed, natural crown preserved; pulp chamber cleaned/sealed with flowable composite; subgingival crown trimmed to ovoid covering surgical site, extending ~1-2 mm subgingivally; ~1-2 mm clearance from implant (no contact). Acid-etch + bond + flowable + FRC splint (RTD) on labial surfaces of crown and adjacent teeth; cured; occlusion adjusted out of all contacts.
- **Control:** primary soft-tissue closure, no immediate restoration (delayed restoration).
- **Final restoration:** both groups at 4-5 months.
- **Outcomes:** PES (Belser 5-parameter, 0-10) at baseline and final; gingival height change (mesial papilla, distal papilla, mid-labial margin) via intraoral scan; postoperative pain & swelling VAS (0-10) days 1-7; implant success.
- **Statistics:** independent t-test or Mann-Whitney U (intergroup); Wilcoxon signed-rank (within-group); two-sided p<0.05. Note: exploratory — no formal sample-size calculation (10/group by convenience).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Implant success:** 100% in both groups.
- **PES (primary esthetic outcome):**
  - Experimental: stable, 9.2 +- 1.1 (baseline) -> 9.0 +- 0.9 (final), p=0.666 (NS).
  - Control: significant drop, 9.3 +- 1.3 -> 6.3 +- 1.6, p=0.004.
  - Baseline comparable (p=0.853); **at final restoration experimental > control, z=2.799, p=0.005.**
- **Gingival height change (NO significant intergroup difference):**
  - Mesial papilla: 0.78 +- 0.62 mm (exp) vs 1.05 +- 0.76 mm (ctrl), p=0.389.
  - Distal papilla: 0.87 +- 0.59 mm vs 1.20 +- 0.59 mm, p=0.236.
  - Mid-labial margin: 0.82 +- 0.53 mm vs 0.35 +- 0.52 mm, p=0.062.
- **Postoperative VAS (experimental significantly lower):**
  - Day 2: pain 2.0 vs 5.1 (p=0.014), swelling 2.7 vs 5.1 (p=0.016).
  - Day 3: pain 1.1 vs 3.7 (p=0.035), swelling 1.9 vs 4.4 (p=0.039).
  - Day 7 pain 0.1 vs 0.8 (p=0.049). Day 1 and days 4-6 not significant.
- **Baseline labial bone thickness (CBCT):** 1.02 +- 0.29 mm (exp) vs 0.9 +- 0.19 mm (ctrl), p=0.269 (NS).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Exploratory, very small (n=20, 10/group):** no formal power calculation; the non-significant gingival-height differences may reflect underpowering rather than true equivalence (e.g., mid-labial margin p=0.062 trends toward more recession in the experimental group). Generalizability limited.
- **Short follow-up (4-5 months):** ends at final restoration; no medium/long-term esthetic, marginal bone, or recession data.
- **No radiographic bone-change or 3D volumetric soft-tissue analysis** — only linear (2D) papilla/margin measurements.
- **Single center, single surgeon** — operator-dependent results, no external validity check.
- **Micro-crestal flap used in both arms** may itself shape soft-tissue outcomes; the technique was not tested flapless.
- Narrow indication: Type I sockets, intact labial wall >= 0.5 mm, adjacent teeth present, trauma cases — not extrapolable to periodontally/endodontically compromised extractions or multi-tooth gaps.
- Larger RCTs with longer follow-up, radiographic + 3D soft-tissue assessment, and varied flap approaches needed.

## 6. Related Work

- Belser et al. — pink esthetic score (PES) 5-parameter framework used here.
- IADT dental-trauma guidelines — FRC splints for elastic fixation of luxated/avulsed teeth (the technique borrows trauma-splinting hardware for prosthetic use).
- Conventional implant-supported immediate provisionalization literature (Kan; De Angelis) — the comparison baseline this study deliberately sidesteps by removing the primary-stability requirement.
- Essix retainer / removable partial denture and Maryland bridge as alternative non-implant-supported provisionals — discussed and argued inferior on soft-tissue support / occlusal compatibility.

## 7. Glossary

- **IIP** — Immediate Implant Placement (Type 1, same-day extraction + placement).
- **Immediate provisionalization** — placing a provisional restoration at/around the time of implant surgery; conventionally implant-supported, here non-implant-supported.
- **FRC splint** — Fiber-Reinforced Composite splint; a fiber strip bonded with flowable composite, used here to fix the natural crown to adjacent teeth.
- **Natural crown (provisional)** — the patient's own extracted tooth crown, root amputated, pulp chamber cleaned/sealed, re-used as a fixed provisional pontic.
- **PES** — Pink Esthetic Score (Belser), 5 parameters x 0-2 = 0-10; higher = better peri-implant soft-tissue esthetics.
- **Type I socket** — intact extraction socket with intact labial bone wall.
- **Jumping gap** — buccal gap between implant surface and inner labial socket wall, here grafted with Bio-Oss Collagen.
- **Micro-crestal flap** — minimal crestal flap splitting papillae into labial/palatal portions for limited bone exposure.
- **VAS** — Visual Analogue Scale (0-10) for pain/swelling.
