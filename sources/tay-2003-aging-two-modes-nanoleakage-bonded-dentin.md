## Why Ingested

This is the foundational paper that **defines the two distinct modes of nanoleakage expression** in bonded dentin — (1) *reticular* silver deposits within and along the hybrid layer, and (2) *water-tree* (spotted) silver uptake within the water-binding domains of the adhesive layer — and shows that aging shifts the dominant mode toward water trees. It mechanistically grounds the water-treeing concept used by the sibling page [[wiki/resin-bonding/tay-2003-water-treeing-degradation-dentin-adhesives]] and supplies the morphological tracer (ammoniacal silver nitrate / TEM) that links water sorption to hydrolytic bond degradation across the bond-durability literature ([[wiki/resin-bonding/liu-2011-limitations-bonding-dentin-bond-degradation-review]]).

## One-line Summary

In-vitro TEM study (abstract-only): bonded human dentin aged ≤12 months in artificial saliva traced with 50 wt% ammoniacal silver nitrate shows reticular nanoleakage within the hybrid layer early on, progressively replaced by water-tree silver uptake in the adhesive's water-binding domains — defining two aging-dependent nanoleakage modes and marking water-filled channels as hydrolytic-degradation sites.

## 한줄요약

In-vitro TEM 연구(초록만): 인공 타액에서 ≤12개월 노화시킨 접착 상아질을 50 wt% 암모니아 질산은으로 추적하면, 초기에는 혼성층(hybrid layer) 내 망상형(reticular) 나노누출이 나타나다가 점차 접착제의 물 결합 영역에 워터트리(water tree)형 은 침착으로 대체된다 — 노화 의존적 두 가지 나노누출 양상을 정의하고, 물 채워진 채널을 가수분해 열화 부위로 지목.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Defines **two morphologically distinct modes of nanoleakage** in resin-dentin bonds: reticular (within/along the hybrid layer) vs water-tree (spotted, within the adhesive's water-binding domains).
- Demonstrates that **aging shifts the expression** from the reticular mode toward the water-tree mode over a 12-month period.
- Establishes **50 wt% ammoniacal silver nitrate uptake** as a tracer for hydrophilic domains and water-filled channels, providing a TEM-visible link between water sorption and incipient hydrolytic degradation.
- Frames **water trees (water-filled channels) as potential sites for hydrolytic degradation** of resin-dentin bonds.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Human teeth bonded with an **experimental filled-adhesive**.
- Specimens aged in **artificial saliva (experimental, aqueous)** vs **non-aqueous mineral oil (control)**.
- Specimens retrieved over a period of **up to 12 months**.
- Immersed in **50 wt% ammoniacal silver nitrate** to trace hydrophilic/water-filled regions.
- Examined by **transmission electron microscopy (TEM)** for changes in silver uptake over time.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Early (baseline):** reticular silver deposits identified within the **bulk of the hybrid layer** in the artificial-saliva (experimental) group.
- **Over time:** these bulk-hybrid-layer reticular deposits were **gradually reduced**, then **replaced by similar deposits along the hybrid layer–adhesive interface**.
- **Adhesive layer:** silver uptake in the **water-binding domains** of the adhesive **increased with aging**, producing **water tree formation** (spotted/dendritic channels).
- **Mechanistic conclusion:** these **water-filled channels may act as potential sites for hydrolytic degradation** of resin-dentin bonds.
- Mineral-oil (non-aqueous) control isolates water sorption as the driver of the aging-related changes.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Abstract-only**: methods detail (sample size, adhesive composition, time-point sampling, quantification) not available; results reported qualitatively at the morphological level.
- In-vitro / artificial-saliva aging — not a clinical durability outcome; no µTBS or survival data.
- Single experimental filled-adhesive; generalization to commercial total-etch/self-etch and universal adhesives requires extrapolation.

## 7. Glossary

- **Nanoleakage**: nanometer-scale penetration of tracer (silver) into resin-dentin interfaces independent of marginal gaps, indicating incompletely sealed/hydrophilic regions.
- **Reticular nanoleakage**: net-like silver pattern within or along the hybrid layer (Mode 1).
- **Water tree**: spotted/dendritic silver-filled channels within the adhesive's water-binding domains, increasing with aging (Mode 2).
- **Hybrid layer**: the resin-infiltrated demineralized dentin collagen zone forming the bond.
- **Ammoniacal silver nitrate**: silver tracer that penetrates hydrophilic domains and water-filled channels, visualized by TEM.
- **Hydrolytic degradation**: water-mediated breakdown of resin and exposed collagen, degrading bond strength over time.
- **TEM**: transmission electron microscopy, used here to image silver uptake at the interface.
