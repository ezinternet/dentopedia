## Why Ingested

레진 시멘트 대신 예열 복합레진을 간접 수복물 접착에 사용하는 대안적 프로토콜의 근거를 정립하기 위해 인제스트. PICN(폴리머 침투 도자)와 장석질 도재에 대한 예열 복합레진의 미세전단결합강도를 비교하는 드문 연구. [[resin-bonding/unknown-2009-effect-of-silane-treatment-timing]]과 함께 실란 처리 후 다양한 접착제 유형의 성능 비교 맥락에서 활용한다.

## One-line Summary

In vitro microshear bond strength study (n=180 per material, 12 groups): preheated composite (68°C) achieved highest µSBS to both feldspathic ceramic and PICN; thermocycling reduced all groups' bond strength, with preheated composite remaining superior after aging.

## 한줄요약

인 비트로 미세전단결합강도 연구 (재료당 n=180, 12그룹): 예열 복합레진(68°C)이 장석질 도재와 PICN 모두에서 가장 높은 결합강도를 달성했으며, 열순환 후에도 우위 유지; 유동성 복합레진은 모든 조건에서 가장 낮은 값을 보였다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of Adhesive Dentistry, 2023 (published as 2024); 25:159–166
- **DOI**: 10.3290/j.jad.b4279775
- **Study type**: In vitro experimental
- **Institution**: UNESP São José dos Campos, Brazil; Anhanguera University; Federal University of Pelotas

## 2. Key Contributions

- Established that preheated composite resin (68°C) achieves higher µSBS to both feldspathic ceramic and PICN than conventional resin cement or flowable composite
- Confirmed that thermocycling (5,000 cycles) uniformly reduces bond strength across all groups — aging significantly impacts all luting strategies
- Showed flowable composite consistently produced the lowest bond strength, particularly concerning given its low elastic modulus
- Provided comparative data supporting preheated composite as a clinically viable alternative to resin cement for adhesive cementation

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Materials**:
  - PICN: Vita Enamic (86% feldspathic ceramic + 14% polymer network: TEG-DMA, UDMA)
  - Feldspathic ceramic: Vita Mark II (SiO₂ 56–64%, Al₂O₃ 20–23%)
- **Luting agents**:
  - RC: Multilink N (Ivoclar Vivadent) — conventional resin cement
  - FC: Tetric N Ceram flowable (Ivoclar Vivadent) — flowable composite
  - PH: Filtek P60 (3M) preheated at 68°C for 10 min
- **Surface treatment**: 5% HF 60 s + ultrasonic cleaning 5 min + Monobond N silane 10 s active application + 60 s dwell
- **Specimen dimensions**: 10×8×2 mm plates; Tygon tubes (Ø 1.6 mm, h=2 mm) for cylinders
- **Groups**: 12 groups (2 materials × 3 luting agents × 2 time points) n=15 per group
- **Aging**: 5,000 thermal cycles (5°C–55°C, 30 s dwell)
- **Test**: Microshear bond strength (µSBS) with universal testing machine
- **Analysis**: Two-way ANOVA; failure modes by stereomicroscope + SEM

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Feldspathic ceramic (FEL)**:
- PH-baseline > RC-baseline ≈ FC-baseline (all significantly different)
- After aging: PH > RC ≈ FC
- Most frequent failures: adhesive and cohesive in ceramic

**PICN**:
- PH-baseline > RC-baseline > FC-baseline (all significantly different)
- After aging: PH > RC > FC
- Thermocycling reduced all groups significantly

**Cross-material comparison**:
- PICN generally showed slightly higher bond values than FEL across luting agents
- Preheated composite at 68°C reduces viscosity, enhancing flow into micro-retentions

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In vitro only — clinical conditions (salivary contamination, operator variability) not simulated
- Only Filtek P60 tested as preheated composite; other brands may differ
- Preheating protocol (68°C/10 min) requires dedicated equipment
- µSBS geometry (Tygon tubes) may not replicate full restoration cementation stress distribution

## 6. Related Work

- Feldspathic veneer survival: 87% at 9 years; PICN used for same indications as feldspathic (onlays, inlays, veneers, crowns)
- Cites evidence that low-elastic-modulus luting agents (flowable composites) can reduce ceramic fracture resistance
- Gold standard: HF etching + silane (as used here) confirmed as universal baseline for silica-based ceramics
- Preheating concept: reduces viscosity, increases filler loading effect vs flowable composites

## 7. Glossary

- **PICN**: Polymer-infiltrated ceramic network (e.g., Vita Enamic) — dual-network material (ceramic + polymer)
- **µSBS**: Microshear bond strength
- **RC**: Resin cement (conventional, dual-cure)
- **FC**: Flowable composite (low viscosity, low filler content)
- **PH**: Preheated regular composite (68°C → viscosity reduction)
- **Thermocycling**: Simulated aging via alternating temperature baths
- **TEG-DMA**: Triethylene glycol dimethacrylate (in PICN polymer phase)
