## One-line Summary
Single-arm clinical study (74 patients, 86 implants) — autonomous implant robot (ADIRS) achieved coronal 0.61 mm, apical 0.79 mm, angular 2.56° deviation.

## 한줄요약
단일군 임상연구(환자 74명·임플란트 86개): 자율임플란트로봇(ADIRS) 관상 0.61mm·첨부 0.79mm·각도 2.56° 편차로 높은 정확도.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Reports absolute accuracy of an autonomous dental implant robot system (ADIRS) without comparator.
- Region, implant length, and surgeon did not significantly affect accuracy.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Single-arm prospective clinical. 74 partially edentulous participants, 86 implants.
- Preoperative CBCT → ADIRS planning; robot performed osteotomy and placement under navigation.
- Postoperative CBCT for planned-vs-actual deviation. GLMM (α=.05).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Global coronal deviation: 0.61 ± 0.20 mm (95% CI 0.23–1.0).
- Global apical deviation: 0.79 ± 0.32 mm (95% CI 0.17–1.41).
- Angular deviation: 2.56° ± 1.10° (95% CI 0.42–4.70).
- No surgical complications. Accuracy unaffected by region, length, surgeon (p>.05).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- No control group; absolute accuracy only. Short term, no osseointegration/MBL outcomes. Single center.

## 6. Related Work
- sCAIS/dCAIS accuracy benchmarks; comparative robot-vs-navigation trials.

## 7. Glossary
- ADIRS: autonomous dental implant robot system. GLMM: generalized linear mixed model.
