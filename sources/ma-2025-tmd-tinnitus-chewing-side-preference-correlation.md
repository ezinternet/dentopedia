## Why Ingested

[[wiki/tmj/zheng-2023-unilateral-chewing-tmj-structure-tmd]]가 편측저작(chewing-side preference)이 TMD의 위험인자임을 정리했지만, 편측저작이 **이명(tinnitus)** 같은 이과(耳科) 증상과 연결되는지는 위키에 없었다. 본 횡단연구(Ma 2025, n=285)는 TMD 환자에서 이명-편측저작의 정적 상관(同側性 포함)과 이명-관절통의 연관을 직접 보여 편측저작→TMD→이명 축을 보강한다. 같은 연구진의 [[wiki/tmj/ma-2022-condyle-position-chewing-side-preference-cbct]](편측저작-과두위치 CBCT)와 짝을 이루는 편측저작 시리즈.

## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional study (n=285 TMD patients, PLA General Hospital) finding chewing-side preference is significantly more common in TMD patients with tinnitus (90.7% vs 76.0%), with tinnitus side positively correlated to the habitual chewing side and to TMJ-area pain — but not to clicking, limited opening, or disc-displacement type.

## 한줄요약

TMD 환자 285명 횡단연구: 이명을 동반한 TMD 환자에서 편측저작 비율이 유의하게 높고(90.7% vs 76.0%), 이명 발생측이 습관적 저작측 및 관절통과 정적 상관 — 단 관절잡음·개구제한·관절원판 변위 유형과는 무관.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First focused clinical correlation analysis (in this group's literature) of **chewing-side preference and unexplained tinnitus** within a TMD cohort.
2. Demonstrates a **side-specific (ipsilateral) association**: tinnitus side aligns with the habitual chewing side.
3. Confirms that **TMJ-area pain**, not clicking/limited opening/disc-displacement morphology, is the TMD symptom correlated with tinnitus.
4. Proposes chewing-side preference as a potential contributing factor to unexplained tinnitus, motivating chewing-habit correction as an adjunct in tinnitus workup.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Cohort:** 285 TMD patients (199 F / 86 M; age 18–60, mean 32.27 ± 10 y) diagnosed at the Dept. of Stomatology, retrospectively reviewed.
- **Grouping:** tinnitus group (n=43) vs non-tinnitus group (n=242).
- **Inclusion:** TMD signs/symptoms supported by CBCT and TMJ MRI; otolaryngology-confirmed **unexplained** tinnitus (with/without chewing-side preference).
- **Exclusion:** no TMD signs; systemic organic disease; MRI-incompatible (pacemaker/metal/claustrophobia); poor MRI quality; moderate–severe anxiety/depression or under anxiolytic/antidepressant treatment.
- **MRI:** 1.5 T Siemens Novus with TMJ surface coil; closed- and open-mouth oblique-sagittal/coronal PDWI and fat-suppressed T2WI; scan plane perpendicular to condylar long axis.
- **Chewing-side assessment:** chewing gum 2 min, interrupted every 15 s, observed 7 times; recorded gum side at the 1st/3rd/5th/7th observation; ≥3 same-side = chewing-side preference. Plus interview question ("Do you eat with one side?"). Same side across trials = chewing-side preference; alternating = bilateral. Classified as left / right / none.
- **Statistics:** SPSS 25.0; quantitative described as mean ± SD with t-test/ANOVA; qualitative as frequency/proportion with χ² test and Spearman correlation; P < 0.05 significant.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Baseline:** No age (t=0.13, P=0.966) or sex (χ²=0.124, P=0.726) difference between groups.

**Tinnitus × chewing-side preference (primary):**
- Tinnitus group: chewing-side preference 90.70% (39/43) vs none 9.30% (4/43).
- Non-tinnitus group: chewing-side preference 76.03% (184/242) vs none 23.97% (58/242).
- Higher in tinnitus group, χ²=4.613, **P<0.05**; positive Spearman correlation between tinnitus and chewing-side preference.

**Side concordance (39 tinnitus + chewing-side preference patients):**
- Left tinnitus (n=22): left chewers 54.55% (12/22) > right chewers 45.45% (10/22).
- Right tinnitus (n=11): right chewers 81.82% (9/11) > left chewers 18.18% (2/11).
- Bilateral tinnitus (n=6): left chewers 83.33% (5/6) vs right 16.67% (1/6).
- Distribution difference significant, χ²=7.282, **P<0.05**; tinnitus side positively correlated with chewing side.

**Clinical symptoms:**
- Pain: tinnitus 93.02% (40/43) vs non-tinnitus 73.55% (178/242), **P<0.05** (positive correlation with tinnitus).
- Clicking: tinnitus 76.74% (33/43) vs non-tinnitus 83.47% (202/242), P>0.05 (NS).
- Limited opening: tinnitus 48.84% (21/43) vs non-tinnitus 47.11% (114/242), P>0.05 (NS).

**Disc displacement (by laterality):** no-displacement / unilateral / bilateral — tinnitus 16.28% / 34.88% / 48.84% vs non-tinnitus 18.18% / 38.02% / 43.80%; all P>0.05 (NS).

**Disc displacement (by reducibility):** none / reducible / irreducible — tinnitus 16.28% / 34.88% / 48.84% vs non-tinnitus 18.18% / 28.93% / 52.89%; all P>0.05 (NS).

**Conclusion:** TMD with chewing-side preference may be a contributing factor to unexplained tinnitus; unexplained tinnitus is correlated with TMJ-area pain in TMD patients.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-center, retrospective design; small tinnitus subgroup (n=43) — side-stratified cells (n=6–22) are very small, limiting power.
- Possible methodological bias; other tinnitus causes not fully excluded despite ENT screening.
- Correlation only — no causal inference; mechanism (condylar displacement on auriculotemporal nerve, inflammation via petrotympanic/petrosquamous fissure, neural/vascular changes) remains hypothesized.
- Authors call for larger samples and advanced imaging/methods.

## 7. Glossary

- **TMD** — Temporomandibular disorder (颞下颌关节病).
- **Chewing-side preference (偏侧咀嚼)** — Habitual unilateral chewing.
- **Tinnitus (耳鸣)** — Perception of sound without external source; here "unexplained" (ENT-confirmed).
- **Disc displacement** — Reducible vs irreducible; unilateral vs bilateral, assessed on TMJ MRI.
- **PDWI** — Proton density-weighted imaging.
- **Auriculotemporal nerve / petrotympanic fissure** — Proposed anatomic conduits linking TMJ pathology to otologic symptoms.
