## One-line Summary
J Prosthodont 2025 (Early View, pub 10 Dec 2025) — SR+MA of 25 studies on LLMs (ChatGPT, Bard, Bing) answering dental patient inquiries; pooled accuracy 81.87% (95% CI 77.24–86.51%), clinical acceptability 69.9% (57.3–82.6%); ChatGPT-3.5 > Bing, NS vs ChatGPT-4.0/Bard.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Journal of Prosthodontics 2025 (Early View)
- Published online: 2025-12-10
- DOI: 10.1111/jopr.70068
- Type: PRISMA-compliant systematic review with meta-analysis

## 2. Key Contributions
- First quantitative pooled accuracy estimate for LLMs in dental patient communication
- Compares LLM versions (ChatGPT-3.5 vs ChatGPT-4.0 vs Bard vs Bing)
- Distinguishes accuracy from clinical acceptability as separate endpoints

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- PRISMA guidelines
- 7 databases (PubMed, Scopus, Cochrane, etc.); window: Nov 2022 to Jun 2024
- Quality: modified QUADAS-2 framework
- Accuracy data extracted; meta-analysis with subgroup + sensitivity analyses

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Studies included: 25
- Pooled accuracy score (all LLMs): 81.87% (95% CI 77.24–86.51%)
- Clinically acceptable responses: 69.9% (95% CI 57.3–82.6%)
- Subgroup: ChatGPT-3.5 significantly higher accuracy than Microsoft Bing
- ChatGPT-3.5 NOT significantly different from ChatGPT-4.0 or Google Bard

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Variability in accuracy across studies and dental topics
- Complex clinical scenarios still expose LLM brittleness
- Further research needed on standardised evaluation rubrics, longitudinal accuracy, multilingual performance
