## Why Ingested

Directly answers the core monomer-selection question that recurs across the resin-bonding category — *does the presence of the functional monomer 10-MDP, by itself, raise bonding performance over other acidic monomers?* This is the largest meta-analysis on the topic (206 studies, 64 adhesive systems) and quantifies the dentin/enamel benefit, so it anchors monomer-choice claims. It reinforces and quantifies the durability-focused sibling page [[wiki/resin-bonding/carrilho-2019-mdp-adhesive-interface-stability-sr]], which addresses 10-MDP interface *stability*; together they cover "stronger" (this paper) and "more durable" (Carrilho) sides of the 10-MDP rationale.

## One-line Summary

In vitro SR+MA (206 studies, 64 SE adhesive systems): 10-MDP-based self-etch adhesives gave significantly higher bond strength than most other acidic-monomer adhesives at both dentin (effect 6.98; 95% CI 5.61–8.36) and enamel (2.79; 95% CI 1.62–3.96), p<0.00001, though the benefit was conditional on test method, substrate, comonomer, and application category.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Largest synthesis to date isolating the **functional-monomer variable** (10-MDP vs other acidic monomers) on self-etch (SE) adhesive bond strength, holding substrate and test type as moderators.
- Confirms a significant **10-MDP bonding advantage on both dentin and enamel**, with dentin showing the larger effect.
- Provides a **monomer-by-monomer ranking**: which acidic monomers 10-MDP beats, which it ties, and the single monomer (GPDM) that outperforms it.
- Adds **Bayesian network meta-analysis (NMA)** indirect comparisons across SE application categories (1-step vs 2-step vs universal).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: SR + MA following PRISMA; registered PROSPERO CRD42020175715.
- **Databases**: PubMed, Web of Science, SciELO, Scopus, LILACS, IBECS, BBO; coverage through 30 June 2021.
- **Eligibility**: in vitro studies measuring bond strength (microtensile, microshear, tensile, or shear) to **sound dentin/enamel** of ≥2 distinct SE systems, with ≥1 containing 10-MDP and ≥1 with a distinct acidic composition.
- **Analysis**: RevMan 5.3.5, random-effects models, significance p<0.05; Bayesian NMA via MetaInsight V3.
- **Yield**: 740 full-text screened → **210 in SR, 206 in MA**; **64 adhesive systems**; microtensile was the dominant test (73.4%).
- **Risk of bias**: medium 56.7%, low 35.2%, high 8.1%.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Dentin** — favored 10-MDP group: overall effect **6.98** (95% CI 5.61–8.36; p<0.00001).
- **Enamel** — favored 10-MDP group: overall effect **2.79** (95% CI 1.62–3.96; p<0.00001).
- **10-MDP outperformed** (p≤0.01) adhesives based on: PENTA, 6-MHP, 4-META, 4-MET, pyrophosphate esters, mixed composition, and sulfonic-acid-derived monomers.
- **No significant difference** (p≥0.05) vs adhesives with: PEM-F, acrylamide phosphates, 4-AET, MAC-10, or polyacrylic-/phosphonic-acid-derived monomers.
- **Only GPDM-based adhesives exceeded 10-MDP** in bonding potential (p=0.03).
- **Application category**: dentin bonds favored **2-step 10-MDP-based** adhesives; enamel bonds favored **2-step versions regardless of 10-MDP presence**.
- **NMA indirect evidence**: 1-step 10-MDP-free adhesives performed worst in dentin; universal 10-MDP-free adhesives performed worst in enamel.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **In vitro only** — bond-strength surrogate; no clinical/longevity (retention, marginal integrity) outcomes; no aging/thermocycling stratification reported in the abstract.
- Benefit is **conditional** — depends on mechanical test type, substrate, comonomer composition, and SE application category, so "10-MDP = better" is not unconditional.
- Majority **medium risk of bias** (56.7%); heterogeneity across 206 studies/64 systems.
- Certainty of evidence grade (e.g., GRADE) not stated in abstract.
- Abstract-only ingest; full-text moderators, forest plots, and exact aging data not extracted.

## 7. Glossary

- **10-MDP** (10-methacryloyloxydecyl dihydrogen phosphate): acidic functional monomer that chemically bonds to hydroxyapatite Ca and forms stable nanolayers.
- **SE (self-etch) adhesive**: adhesive that conditions and primes simultaneously via acidic monomers, without a separate phosphoric-acid etch step.
- **GPDM** (glycerophosphate dimethacrylate): acidic functional monomer; here the only one outperforming 10-MDP.
- **Microtensile bond strength (μTBS)**: bond strength measured on small (~1 mm²) beams; the dominant test (73.4%) in included studies.
- **Network meta-analysis (NMA)**: synthesis combining direct + indirect comparisons to rank multiple treatments; here Bayesian (MetaInsight V3).
- **PENTA / 6-MHP / 4-META / 4-MET / MAC-10 / 4-AET / PEM-F**: alternative acidic functional monomers compared against 10-MDP.
