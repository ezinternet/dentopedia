## Why Ingested

Directly answers the core monomer-selection question that recurs across the resin-bonding category — *does the presence of the functional monomer 10-MDP, by itself, raise bonding performance over other acidic monomers?* This is the largest meta-analysis on the topic (206 studies, 64 adhesive systems) and quantifies the dentin/enamel benefit, so it anchors monomer-choice claims. It reinforces and quantifies the durability-focused sibling page [[wiki/resin-bonding/carrilho-2019-mdp-adhesive-interface-stability-sr]], which addresses 10-MDP interface *stability*; together they cover "stronger" (this paper) and "more durable" (Carrilho) sides of the 10-MDP rationale.

## One-line Summary

In vitro SR+MA (206 studies, 64 SE adhesive systems): 10-MDP-based self-etch adhesives gave significantly higher bond strength than most other acidic-monomer adhesives at both dentin (effect 6.98; 95% CI 5.61–8.36) and enamel (2.79; 95% CI 1.62–3.96), p<0.00001, though the benefit was conditional on test method, substrate, comonomer, and application category.

## 한줄요약

In vitro 체계적 문헌고찰+메타분석 (논문 206편, 자가산부식 접착제 64종): 10-MDP 함유 접착제가 대부분의 다른 산성 단량체 접착제보다 상아질(효과크기 6.98; 95% CI 5.61–8.36)·법랑질(2.79; 95% CI 1.62–3.96) 모두에서 유의하게 높은 결합강도를 보였으나(p<0.00001), 그 이점은 시험법·기질·공단량체·적용 방식에 따라 달라졌다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Largest synthesis to date isolating the **functional-monomer variable** (10-MDP vs other acidic monomers) on self-etch (SE) adhesive bond strength, holding substrate and test type as moderators.
- Confirms a significant **10-MDP bonding advantage on both dentin and enamel**, with dentin showing the larger effect (6.98 vs 2.79).
- Provides a **monomer-by-monomer ranking**: which acidic monomers 10-MDP beats (PENTA, 6-MHP, 4-META, 4-MET, pyrophosphate esters, sulfonic-acid derived), which it ties (PEM-F, acrylamide phosphates, 4-AET, MAC-10, polyacrylic/phosphonic-acid derived), and the single monomer that outperforms it (GPDM).
- Adds **Bayesian network meta-analysis (NMA)** indirect comparisons across SE application categories (1-step vs 2-step vs universal).
- Clarifies that **application category matters**: 2-step 10-MDP adhesives give the best dentin bonds; 2-step formulations (regardless of 10-MDP) give the best enamel bonds.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: SR + MA following PRISMA; registered PROSPERO CRD42020175715.
- **Databases**: PubMed, Web of Science, SciELO, Scopus, LILACS, IBECS, BBO; coverage through 30 June 2021.
- **Eligibility**: in vitro studies measuring bond strength (microtensile, microshear, tensile, or shear) to **sound dentin/enamel** of ≥2 distinct SE systems, with ≥1 containing 10-MDP and ≥1 with a distinct acidic composition.
- **Statistical analysis**: RevMan 5.3.5, random-effects models, significance p<0.05; Bayesian NMA via MetaInsight V3.
- **Literature yield**: 7,355 titles/abstracts retrieved → 770 selected for full-text access → 560 excluded (503 eligibility, 30 no access, 20 unavailable data, rest duplicate data) → **210 in SR, 206 in MA**.
- **Scale**: 64 adhesive systems analyzed; most frequently studied adhesive was Clearfil SE Bond (Kuraray).
- **Dominant test method**: microtensile bond strength (μTBS) — 73.4% of included studies.
- **Risk of bias**: medium 56.7%, low 35.2%, high 8.1%.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Overall effect by substrate
- **Dentin** — 10-MDP group favored: overall effect **6.98 MPa** (95% CI 5.61–8.36; p<0.00001).
- **Enamel** — 10-MDP group favored: overall effect **2.79 MPa** (95% CI 1.62–3.96; p<0.00001).

### Monomer-by-monomer comparisons
- **10-MDP outperformed** (p≤0.01): adhesives based on PENTA, 6-MHP, 4-META, 4-MET, pyrophosphate esters, mixed composition, and sulfonic-acid-derived monomers.
- **No significant difference** (p≥0.05): adhesives with PEM-F, acrylamide phosphates, 4-AET, MAC-10, or polyacrylic-/phosphonic-acid-derived monomers.
- **Only GPDM-based adhesives exceeded 10-MDP** in bonding potential (p=0.03) — the sole exception to 10-MDP superiority or equivalence.

### Application category (SE system type)
- **Dentin bonds**: favored **2-step 10-MDP-based** adhesives.
- **Enamel bonds**: favored **2-step versions regardless of 10-MDP presence**.

### Network meta-analysis (NMA) indirect evidence
- 1-step 10-MDP-free adhesives performed worst in dentin.
- Universal 10-MDP-free adhesives performed worst in enamel.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **In vitro only**: bond-strength surrogate; no clinical/longevity outcomes (retention, marginal integrity, restoration survival).
- Benefit is **conditional**: depends on mechanical test type, substrate, comonomer composition, and SE application category — "10-MDP = better" is not unconditional.
- Majority **medium risk of bias** (56.7%) across studies.
- **Moderate-to-high heterogeneity** across 206 studies/64 systems acknowledged.
- GRADE certainty-of-evidence assessment not reported.
- Aging/thermocycling stratification not prominently detailed (most studies used immediate testing).
- Future work: clinical RCTs isolating 10-MDP presence, aging/durability MA, and assessment of GPDM vs 10-MDP in head-to-head RCTs.

## 7. Glossary

- **10-MDP** (10-methacryloyloxydecyl dihydrogen phosphate): the dominant acidic functional monomer in modern SE adhesives; forms water-insoluble 10-MDP-Ca salt with hydroxyapatite; mild-etching with long hydrophobic spacer between methacrylate and phosphate group.
- **SE (self-etch) adhesive**: adhesive that conditions and primes simultaneously via acidic monomers, without a separate phosphoric-acid etch step; generations 6th, 7th, 8th.
- **GPDM** (glycerophosphate dimethacrylate): acidic functional monomer; the only one in this MA to exceed 10-MDP bonding performance (p=0.03).
- **Microtensile bond strength (μTBS)**: bond strength measured on small (~1 mm²) beams; dominant test (73.4%) in included studies.
- **Network meta-analysis (NMA)**: synthesis combining direct + indirect comparisons to rank multiple treatments; here Bayesian via MetaInsight V3.
- **PENTA**: dipentaerythritol pentaacrylate monophosphate — acidic monomer; outperformed by 10-MDP.
- **6-MHP / 4-META / 4-MET / MAC-10 / 4-AET / PEM-F**: other acidic functional monomers compared against 10-MDP in this MA.
- **Universal adhesive**: "multi-mode" or "multipurpose" adhesive that can be used in etch-and-rinse or self-etch mode.
- **PROSPERO**: International Prospective Register of Systematic Reviews; registration CRD42020175715.
