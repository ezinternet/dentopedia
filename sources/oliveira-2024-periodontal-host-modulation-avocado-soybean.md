## Why Ingested

인사돌(Insadol)·이가탄 계열 OTC 치주약 surveillance ingest의 일부. 인사돌의 유효성분은 옥수수 불검화물(Zea mays unsaponifiable)이며, 본 논문이 다루는 ASU(avocado/soybean unsaponifiables, Piascledine)는 같은 기전 계열의 국제판 자매 불검화물 host-modulation 화합물이다. 동물모델에서 ASU의 치주 골소실 감소·치유 개선 근거를 [[wiki/periodontics/kim-2024-magnoliae-cortex-zea-mays-extracts]]의 Zea mays 불검화물 기전과 짝지어 보강하기 위해 인제스트.

## One-line Summary

Animal study (rat ligature periodontitis + experimental arthritis, n=60): adjunctive avocado/soybean unsaponifiables (ASU, 0.6 mg/kg gavage) after scaling reduced periodontal bone loss and improved healing, blunting the worse outcomes seen in arthritic animals.

## 한줄요약

동물실험(쥐 결찰 치주염 + 실험적 관절염, n=60): 스케일링 후 보조 ASU(아보카도/대두 불검화물, 0.6 mg/kg 위관투여)가 치주 골소실을 줄이고 치유를 개선했으며, 관절염 동물에서 악화된 결과를 완화함.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Tests ASU (avocado/soybean unsaponifiables) as an adjunctive host-modulation therapy (HMT) for periodontitis, in the systemically compromised context of experimental arthritis.
- ASU is the international sister unsaponifiable to the Zea mays (corn) unsaponifiable that is the active in Korean OTC periodontal drug 인사돌(Insadol) — same mechanistic family of plant unsaponifiable host modulators.
- Provides preclinical evidence that an unsaponifiable adjunct, given after mechanical debridement (scaling), reduces periodontal bone loss and improves the healing pattern.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: 60 rats, 4 groups by treatment × systemic condition:
  - CTR-S: healthy, saline solution (SS)
  - ASU-S: healthy, ASU (0.6 mg/kg)
  - AR/ASU-S: induced arthritis, ASU
  - AR-S: induced arthritis, SS
- **Periodontitis induction**: ligatures kept for 15 days.
- **Treatment**: scaling with hand instruments; SS or ASU administered daily by gavage until euthanasia.
- **Euthanasia time points**: 7, 15, or 30 days after scaling (n=5 animals/group).
- **Outcomes assessed**: bone resorption, inflammatory infiltrate composition, osteoclastogenesis.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **AR-S (arthritis + saline) had the worst profile**: greatest bone loss, fewest fibroblasts, most inflammatory cells vs all other groups; and greater osteoclastogenesis than the healthy-animal groups.
- **ASU benefit**: ASU improved the healing pattern after periodontitis treatment in arthritic animals and reduced periodontal bone loss.
- Quantitative effect sizes, statistics, and per-timepoint values not recoverable (abstract-only — full text not retrieved).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Animal (rat) model — direct clinical extrapolation to human periodontal therapy limited.
- abstract-only — full text not retrieved; exact magnitudes, variance, and statistical thresholds unavailable.
- Single ASU dose (0.6 mg/kg); no dose-response reported in abstract.
- Comorbidity context (experimental arthritis) is specific; generalizability to other systemic conditions untested here.

## 6. Related Work

- Korean OTC periodontal HMT evidence on the corn (Zea mays) unsaponifiable analog: Kim 2018 and Kim 2024 (Magnoliae cortex + Zea mays extracts), and the 인사돌플러스 clinical RCT (Hong 2019).
- Same plant-unsaponifiable host-modulation family — ASU (Piascledine) is the international counterpart.

## 7. Glossary

- **ASU (avocado/soybean unsaponifiables)**: plant unsaponifiable fraction (brand Piascledine); host-modulation/anti-inflammatory agent used in osteoarthritis and tested here for periodontitis.
- **Unsaponifiable**: lipid fraction not hydrolyzed by alkali (the bioactive non-saponifiable plant extract); 인사돌's active is the Zea mays (corn) unsaponifiable.
- **HMT (host-modulation therapy)**: adjunct that modulates the host inflammatory/osteolytic response rather than targeting bacteria directly.
- **Osteoclastogenesis**: formation/activation of bone-resorbing osteoclasts; a driver of periodontal bone loss.
- **Ligature-induced periodontitis**: standard rodent model placing a ligature subgingivally to provoke plaque accumulation and bone loss.
