## One-line Summary
RCT (n=56): alternate ibuprofen 400mg / paracetamol 1000mg every 4h for 48h is superior to concurrent dosing every 8h for post-3rd molar extraction pain; 85% vs 53% required rescue medication (p=0.002).

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Scientific Reports 2025;15:11519
- DOI: 10.1038/s41598-025-89744-0
- Design: Single-center, double-masked, parallel-group RCT
- Trial registration: ClinicalTrials.gov NCT06514222 (registered 2024-07-23)
- n = 56 patients, impacted mandibular 3rd molar surgical extraction
- Institution: University of Sharjah, UAE

## 2. Key Contributions
- First RCT directly comparing concurrent vs alternate ibuprofen+APAP scheduling for dental extraction pain
- Alternate q4h schedule significantly reduces need for rescue medication vs concurrent q8h (p=0.002)
- Addresses a common clinical uncertainty about optimal dosing interval when combining two analgesics
- Both regimens well-tolerated (no major adverse events reported)
- Limitation: small n=56, single center; needs multicenter replication

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Group A (Control): ibuprofen 400mg + paracetamol 1000mg (2×500mg) together, q8h × 48h
- Group B (Study): ibuprofen 400mg and paracetamol 1000mg alternating q4h × 48h (total dosing interval maintained)
- Primary outcome: VAS 0–10 pain intensity
- Secondary: onset of analgesia, duration of pain relief, rescue drug frequency, time to rescue
- Blinding: investigators analyzing outcomes blinded to group assignment

## 4. Key Results
- Pain intensity "some pain" category: 50% (Group A) vs 35.7% (Group B); difference NS (p=0.495)
- "No pain" higher in Group B but NS
- Rescue medication needed: 53% Group A vs 15% Group B → Group B significantly better (p=0.002)
- Moderate complexity extractions: paradoxically higher pain scores in Group B (not explained mechanistically)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small n=56, single center (Sharjah, UAE); generalizability limited
- Pain "no significant difference" on VAS scores, but rescue medication significantly different — discrepancy worth noting
- No long-term follow-up (beyond 48h)
- Multicenter RCT with larger n and stratified complexity groups recommended

## 6. Related Work
- Feldman 2024 (both drugs combined, dosing every 4–6h PRN; nonopioid superior to opioid)
- Miroshnychenko 2023 (NMA: ibuprofen+APAP combination best overall)
- Samani 2025 (network MA on endodontic post-op pain timing)

## 7. Glossary
- VAS: Visual Analogue Scale (0–10)
- Concurrent: both drugs taken simultaneously at same interval
- Alternate: drugs staggered so each given at a different time, shortening effective analgesia interval
- Rescue medication: additional analgesic taken due to inadequate pain control
