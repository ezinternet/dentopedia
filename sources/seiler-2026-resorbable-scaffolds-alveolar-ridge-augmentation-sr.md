## Why Ingested

기존 GBR barrier 페이지들은 non-resorbable(Ti-mesh/PTFE)과 collagen membrane에 집중돼 있고, resorbable space-maintaining *scaffold*(PCL/PLGA/β-TCP/poly-D-L-lactide)가 Ti-mesh를 대체할 수 있는지 묻는 직접적 SR 근거가 비어 있었다. 본 Seiler 2026 SR은 resorbable scaffold가 second-stage mesh removal을 피하는 대안임을 제시하되 근거 수준이 낮음을 명시 — [[bone-regeneration/wang-2025-gelatin-polylactic-acid-pla-membrane-gbr-rct]]의 단일 PLA membrane RCT와 [[bone-regeneration/liu-2026-barrier-membranes-gbr-design-applications]]의 막 설계 리뷰를 묶는 상위 합성 근거로 활용. degradation kinetics gap은 [[bone-regeneration/kim-2025-serine-collagen-scaffold-degradation]]의 scaffold 분해 연구와 직접 맞닿는다.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (PRISMA/PROSPERO; 7 human studies, 39 patients, 45 ridges) finding resorbable scaffolds (PLA/PCL/PLGA/β-TCP) are a viable alternative to conventional grafting for alveolar ridge augmentation that avoids second-stage mesh removal, but evidence is too sparse and heterogeneous for meta-analysis (1 RCT, mostly case series; high risk of bias; degradation kinetics unreported in every study).

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(7개 임상연구, 환자 39명·치조제 45부위): resorbable scaffold(PLA/PCL/PLGA/β-TCP)는 2차 mesh 제거 수술을 피할 수 있는 conventional 골이식 대안으로 보이나, RCT 1편·대부분 증례군에 이질성이 커 메타분석 불가하고 비뚤림 위험이 높으며 모든 연구에서 분해속도(degradation time)가 보고되지 않았다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR focused specifically on **resorbable scaffolds** (biodegradable 3D constructs supporting GBR) for alveolar ridge augmentation, framed directly against **non-resorbable devices (titanium meshes, PTFE)**.
- Distinguishes **container-type scaffolds** (biological cage/barrier stabilizing particulate graft — the dominant clinical pattern reviewed) from **block-type osteoconductive scaffold matrices** (excluded from this review).
- Identifies **degradation kinetics** as the central, almost entirely unstudied determinant of long-term performance.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **PICO**: edentulous patients needing ridge augmentation (P); resorbable scaffolds (I); non-resorbable devices/Ti-mesh or conventional augmentation (C); bone gain, implant survival/success, complications (O).
- **Search**: PubMed/MEDLINE, Cochrane, Web of Science + Google Scholar grey literature; searched 06 Mar 2025, updated 22 Nov 2025; English only, no date restriction. Preclinical in vitro/in vivo animal studies excluded.
- **Selection**: 3704 records → 999 duplicates removed → 2705 screened → 39 full-text → **7 included** (+ manual search). Two reviewers, consensus, third-author adjudication.
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane RoB (RCTs), Newcastle–Ottawa Scale (non-randomized comparative), JBI tools (case series/reports).
- **Materials evaluated**: polylactide (PLA), polycaprolactone (PCL), poly(lactide-co-glycolide) (PLGA), poly-D-lactide, poly(lactic-co-caprolactone) P(LA/CL), β-tricalcium phosphate (β-TCP); customized (additive-manufactured) in 2 studies.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **7 studies, 39 patients, 45 sites.** 1 RCT (Bartols et al., Germany, 30 pts: autogenous block vs poly-D-L-lactide foil, 24 mo); 1 prospective comparative (Shido/Japan-type, 20 pts, collagen vs P(LA/CL), ≤6 mo); 3 case series; 2 case reports. Follow-up 5–48 mo, inconsistently reported.
- **Implant survival**: 4 studies 100%; 1 study 20% implant loss; 2 did not report.
- **Primary stability**: ~35 Ncm (Ivanovski, Hsu); equal ISQ across groups (Shido).
- **Complications**: 4 studies none (2 used customized scaffolds); others ranged from mucosal rubefaction/fistula to exposure, bone decrease, and graft loss (5 graft-loss cases total). Classification inconsistent.
- **Esthetics**: PES 7 / WES 9 (Ivanovski); "satisfying" (Schönegg).
- **Degradation time reported in ZERO studies** — residual scaffold particles noted at intermediate follow-up (slower resorption than anticipated), no overt foreign-body reaction.
- **No meta-analysis**: substantial clinical/methodological heterogeneity (volumetric vs linear bone gain, inconsistent SD/CI, variable follow-up, mixed materials) → descriptive synthesis only.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 7 studies, mostly case reports/small series; even the single RCT had incomplete outcome data (informative missingness → high risk in that domain). Overall moderate-to-high risk of bias.
- Heterogeneity precluded pooling; histology/long-term follow-up in only a minority; degradation kinetics absent.
- **Future**: adequately powered RCTs comparing defined resorbable scaffold systems against titanium meshes / autogenous block grafts, with CBCT volumetric + histomorphometric endpoints (vital bone %, residual scaffold %), prespecified complication categories, PROs, cost-effectiveness, and characterization of degradation products/host response.

## 6. Related Work

- Cites GBR as the most validated vertical augmentation technique (peri-implant bone loss 0.76/1.12/1.11 mm short/medium/long term).
- Non-resorbable ePTFE/dPTFE/Ti-mesh = superior space-making but require second-stage removal, risk crestal osseointegration of mesh, exposure/infection — the morbidity motivating resorbable alternatives.
- Vaquette et al. preclinical: pore architecture, interconnectivity, PCL–hydroxyapatite composition drive cell ingrowth/angiogenesis/mineralization.

## 7. Glossary

- **Resorbable scaffold**: biodegradable 3D biomaterial construct giving temporary structural support for GBR, gradually degraded and replaced by new bone.
- **Container-type (cage) scaffold**: contoured scaffold that stabilizes particulate graft and resists soft-tissue collapse, creating a protected osteoconductive chamber (combined with graft material). Contrasts with block-type scaffold matrix (osteoconductive 3D ingrowth lattice).
- **Degradation kinetics**: rate of scaffold resorption; ideally matched to bone-healing timeline — the review's flagged evidence gap.
- **PCL / PLGA / PLA / β-TCP**: bioresorbable polymers/ceramic used as scaffold materials.
