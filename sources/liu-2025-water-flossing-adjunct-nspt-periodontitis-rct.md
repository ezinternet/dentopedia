## Why Ingested

First RCT we hold testing daily home-use **water flossing** as an adjunct to **non-surgical periodontal therapy (NSPT)** in *actual stage I-II periodontitis* patients (not the orthodontic-hygiene population), reporting a clinical benefit plus a 16S rRNA subgingival-microbiome angle — distinct from [[periodontics/yiamwattana-2025-oral-irrigator-vs-floss-orthodontic-sr-ma]], whose oral-irrigator-vs-floss comparison in orthodontic patients found no superiority over floss. It extends [[periodontics/smiley-2015-nonsurgical-periodontitis-srp-guideline]] by characterizing a home-care adjunct layered on top of SRP. (abstract-only — full text not retrieved; no PMC OA.)

## One-line Summary

6-month RCT (n=72, stage I-II periodontitis, 3 arms post-NSPT) — daily home-use water flossing significantly improved plaque and periodontal indices and shifted subgingival microbiota; adding an herb-containing mouthrinse further altered microbiota but gave no extra clinical benefit over water flossing alone.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Tests **water flossing** as a home-care adjunct to NSPT in genuine **stage I-II periodontitis** patients (not gingivitis/orthodontic cohorts).
2. Shows a **significant improvement in dental plaque index and periodontal indices** with water flossing (with or without herb mouthrinse) over toothbrushing-only control.
3. Adds a **16S rRNA subgingival-microbiome** readout, characterizing taxon-level shifts associated with each adjunct.
4. Provides a **negative incremental result**: adding an herb-containing mouthrinse to water flossing changed the microbiota further but did **not** add clinical benefit beyond water flossing alone.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: 6-month, 3-arm randomized controlled trial.
- **Population**: 72 patients with stage I-II periodontitis.
- **Baseline therapy (all arms)**: full-mouth scaling and subgingival instrumentation (NSPT).
- **Arms** (post-NSPT home care):
  1. **Control** — toothbrushing only.
  2. **Water-flossing** — toothbrushing + water flossing.
  3. **Water-flossing / herb-containing mouthrinse** — toothbrushing + water flossing with herb-containing mouthrinse.
- **Timepoints**: baseline, 1, 3, and 6 months.
- **Measures**: periodontal parameters and subgingival plaque samples; subgingival microbiota analyzed by **16S rRNA sequencing**.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Water flossing with/without herb-containing mouthrinse produced **significant improvement in dental plaque index and periodontal indices** (vs control).
- **Microbiome**: depletion of *Gemella* in **both** water-flossing groups; the **water-flossing/herb-containing mouthrinse** group at 6 months showed **reduced *Aggregatibacter*** and **increased *Fretibacterium***.
- **Incremental herb mouthrinse**: further altered oral microbiota but provided **no additional clinical improvement** vs water flossing alone.
- **Bottom line**: daily home-use water flossing promotes plaque control, reduces periodontal inflammation, and contributes to periodontal stability maintenance — an effective adjuvant to NSPT; herb-containing mouthrinse on top of water flossing offers limited incremental benefit.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-center, modest sample (n=72 across 3 arms → ~24/arm).
- Stage I-II only — findings do not extend to stage III-IV periodontitis.
- 6-month horizon; longer-term periodontal-stability data not reported.
- Herb-containing mouthrinse composition not characterized here (abstract-only).
- Clinical effect magnitudes, adherence, and adverse events not extractable from the abstract.

## 7. Glossary

- **NSPT (Non-Surgical Periodontal Therapy)**: scaling and subgingival instrumentation without flap surgery.
- **Water flossing / oral irrigator**: pulsed-water interdental cleaning device.
- **16S rRNA sequencing**: amplicon sequencing of the bacterial 16S ribosomal RNA gene to profile microbial community composition.
- ***Gemella* / *Aggregatibacter* / *Fretibacterium***: subgingival bacterial genera; *Aggregatibacter* (incl. *A. actinomycetemcomitans*) is periodontitis-associated.
- **Stage I-II periodontitis**: early-to-moderate severity per the 2018 classification.
