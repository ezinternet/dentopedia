## Why Ingested

상악동 거상술 전 이비인후과의(ENT) 협진 기준을 체계화한 narrative review. 점막비후·MRC 등이 불필요한 ENT 의뢰를 유발하는 현실을 지적하고, 진짜 위험인자(골구내강 폐쇄, 감염성 상악동염)에 집중한 의뢰 알고리즘을 제시. [[sinus-lift/lateral/maska-2017-implant-grafting-success-mucosal-thickening-sinus]]와 [[sinus-lift/pseudocyst/kucukkurt-2025-maxillary-sinus-pathologies-cbct-prevalence]]의 임상적 해석을 실제 의뢰 결정으로 연결함.

## One-line Summary

Narrative review proposing a systematic ENT referral grading system (Felisati scale 1-5) for maxillary sinus elevation candidates; only OMC obstruction and overt sinus infection (grade ≥3) warrant otolaryngological evaluation — mucosal thickening and MRC usually do not.

## 한줄요약

상악동거상술 후보 환자에 대한 이비인후과 협진 등급 시스템(Felisati 척도 1-5) 제안 — 골구내강 폐쇄·감염성 상악동염(3등급 이상)만 ENT 의뢰, 점막비후·MRC는 대부분 의뢰 불필요.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Defines when ENT referral IS and IS NOT needed before maxillary sinus elevation
- Proposes Felisati grading scale (1-5) based on CT/CBCT findings and OMC patency
- Clarifies that mucosal thickening and MRC usually do NOT increase MSE complication risk
- Identifies true contraindications: active infectious sinusitis, fungal sinusitis, severe OMC obstruction, large polyps
- Recommends nasal endoscopy by ENT as the gold standard for sinonasal evaluation

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Narrative review of existing literature + expert consensus
- Framework based on: patient history, radiology (CBCT), nasal endoscopy
- Felisati grading system: combines OMC patency + sinus opacification (scale 1-5)
- Grade ≥3 → ENT referral; Grade 2 → optional for symptomatic patients; Grade 1 → no referral

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Key clarifications:**
- Mucosal thickening (incl. >2-6mm) → often "false alarm" — does NOT reliably predict MSE complications
- Mucus retention cysts + pseudocysts → generally do NOT adversely affect MSE success
- OMC patency → most critical determinant of sinus function and MSE success

**Felisati Grading Scale (proposed):**
| Grade | Finding | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Normal OMC, no pathology | No ENT referral |
| 2 | Mild mucosal thickening, small MRC | Optional ENT if symptomatic |
| 3 | Moderate OMC narrowing, mucosal disease | ENT referral recommended |
| 4 | Significant OMC obstruction or CRS | ENT referral mandatory |
| 5 | Complete obstruction, active infection, fungal | ENT referral + treat before MSE |

**Absolute contraindications to MSE (require ENT treatment first):**
- Active infectious sinusitis (pus in middle meatus)
- Fungal sinusitis
- Severe maxillary sinus ostial obstruction / scarring
- Large polyps obstructing OMC
- Incurable chronic rhinosinusitis

**NOT contraindications:**
- Physiologic mucosal thickening (no infection signs)
- Asymptomatic MRC / pseudocyst
- Mild mucosal thickening (<6mm without infection)

**Evaluation tools by otolaryngologist:**
- Nasal endoscopy: check middle meatus for pus, edema, OMC patency
- CBCT review: OMC patency, sinus opacification (complementary to dental provider's assessment)
- SNOT-22 / CRS-PRO questionnaires: symptom severity assessment

**Why ENT collaboration matters:**
- Mucociliary clearance disruption → risk of post-MSE sinusitis and graft infection
- Anti-gravity drainage of maxillary sinus through superomedial ostium = unique vulnerability
- Dental providers tend to misinterpret mucosal thickening as sinusitis requiring treatment

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- No prospective validation of the proposed grading system
- Most evidence from retrospective studies; no RCTs
- Felisati scale not yet externally validated
- Calls for well-designed prospective cohort studies

## 7. Glossary

- **OMC (Ostiomeatal Complex, 골구내강)**: functional drainage pathway where maxillary, anterior ethmoid, and frontal sinuses all drain; obstruction → sinusitis propagation
- **Mucociliary clearance**: mucus transport by ciliated epithelium toward ostium; disrupted in sinusitis and inflammation
- **Felisati grading scale**: novel 1-5 scoring system for ENT referral decision in MSE planning (Craig et al. 2024)
- **SNOT-22**: SinoNasal Outcome Test — 22-item rhinological symptom questionnaire
