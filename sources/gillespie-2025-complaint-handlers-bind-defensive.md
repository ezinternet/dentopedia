## Why Ingested
Explains WHY responders default to the defensive moves catalogued in [[complaint-management/12913_2021_Article_6733]]: contradictory organisational demands. Moves the response axis from blaming individuals to fixing the system -- the lever a clinic owner actually controls.

## One-line Summary
Mixed-methods study of UK hospital staff responding to online criticism, identifying six defensive tactics arising from contradictory work demands.

## 1. Document Information
- PLOS One 2025;20(6):e0325185. CC BY. No specific funding.
- LSE; data from Care Opinion online feedback platform. Coding manual on OSF.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Reframes defensiveness as a *structural* product of contradictory demands, not individual failing.
- Names six defensive tactics: redirecting to other channels, evading issues, psychologising concerns, invalidating concerns as incomplete, closing the feedback episode, individualising with bespoke workarounds.
- Links defensive tactics to lower-quality, less learning-oriented responses.
- Identifies the 'bind': handlers told to be open yet protect the organisation.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Mixed-methods explanatory sequential design. UK hospital staff responses to online criticism (Care Opinion). Quantitative reliability coding of tactics followed by qualitative explanation of the organisational tensions producing them.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
Six defensive tactics were reliably identified. They were generally associated with lower-quality engagement (less issue resolution, less learning). Defensiveness arose where handlers faced contradictory demands -- being responsive/transparent while also protecting organisational reputation and managing workload.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
Online public feedback context (Care Opinion), which differs from formal letters and from private dental practice. Single national system. Tactic taxonomy may not be exhaustive.

## 6. Related Work
Mechanistic counterpart to McCreaddie (2021). Extends the LSE complaint programme (Reader 2014; HCAT 2016) to the responder side.

## 7. Glossary
- **Defensive tactic**: a response move that protects the organisation rather than addressing the concern.
- **Explanatory sequential design**: quantitative phase followed by qualitative phase to explain results.
- **The bind**: contradictory mandate to be open yet self-protective.
