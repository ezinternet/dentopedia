## One-line Summary
Narrative overview of periapical (apical periodontitis) lesions covering etiology, diagnosis, and treatment, noting histopathology remains the diagnostic standard while CBCT/MRI/echography show promise for differentiating granuloma from cyst.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Consolidates etiology (microbial root-canal infection following pulp necrosis), diagnosis, and treatment of periapical lesions.
- Highlights imaging advances (CBCT, MRI, echography) for granuloma-vs-cyst differentiation that may change treatment strategy.
- Surveys treatment modalities for persistent periapical lesions, calling for less-invasive, more predictable approaches.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative review of etiology, diagnostic methods (clinical, radiographic, advanced imaging, histopathology), and treatment options.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Microorganisms are pivotal in initiation/progression of periradicular pathosis after pulp necrosis.
- Histopathology remains the gold standard for lesion-type diagnosis (granuloma vs cyst).
- CBCT, MRI, and echography are promising non-invasive adjuncts for lesion differentiation.
- Persistent apical periodontitis/true cysts need less-invasive, more-predictable treatment than current options.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative scope; no quantitative synthesis.
- Imaging-based granuloma/cyst differentiation not yet validated to replace histopathology.

## 6. Related Work
- Apical periodontitis microbiology; CBCT diagnostic-accuracy literature in endodontics.

## 7. Glossary
- Periapical lesion / apical periodontitis: periradicular inflammatory response to root-canal infection.
- Periapical granuloma vs radicular cyst: two common periapical lesion types differing in epithelial lining and management.
