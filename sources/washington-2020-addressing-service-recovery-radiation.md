## Why Ingested
Introduces 'service recovery' -- the active management practice of salvaging a dissatisfied patient's experience -- and shows it depends on measurement, feedback, and leadership training. Bridges the response axis to the education axis ([[complaint-management/10.1177_23743735251342611]]).

## One-line Summary
Mixed-methods project brief on radiation-oncology frontline managers, finding service recovery requires consistent measurement, feedback, and recurring leadership training.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal of Patient Experience 2020;7(6):889-895.
- Single US academic radiation-oncology setting.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Applies service-recovery theory to frontline healthcare management.
- Identifies enablers: consistent performance measurement, feedback loops, frequent leadership training.
- First service-recovery analysis specific to radiation oncology frontline managers.
- Connects dissatisfaction handling to satisfaction, reimbursement, and reputation.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Explanatory sequential mixed-methods: survey of frontline managers, document/artifact review, and probing interviews exploring managers' knowledge, motivation, and organisational-influence factors in service recovery.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
Effective service recovery depended on consistent performance measurement, structured feedback, and frequent leadership training emphasising the relevance of service recovery. Gaps in any of these undermined managers' ability to actively address dissatisfaction.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
Single specialty/site project brief; small qualitative base; limited generalisability. Manager perspective only (not patient outcomes).

## 6. Related Work
Operational complement to the expectation/response literature; precursor logic to the CODE training model (Elias 2025).

## 7. Glossary
- **Service recovery**: active intervention to salvage and restore a dissatisfied customer/patient's experience.
- **Frontline manager**: supervisor in direct contact with service delivery and complaints.
