## One-line Summary
MDPI Dentistry 2026 (Semmelweis): SR+MA in vitro (34 papers; PROSPERO CRD42023393091): digital vs conventional implant impressions in edentulous full-arch — no significant differences in trueness/precision (RMS); IOS accuracy remains debated in edentulous cases.

## 1. Key Results
- 34 in vitro studies; RMS deviation as primary metric for trueness and precision
- No significant differences between digital and conventional impression techniques
- Full-arch edentulous IOS accuracy remains debated — cumulative error with scan length
- PRISMA 2020 compliant; registered in PROSPERO
- Conventional impressions still viable; digital not yet demonstrably superior in edentulous

## 2. Clinical Implications
- Digital impressions for full-arch edentulous implants: comparable but not superior to conventional
- Larger scan field = higher cumulative error risk for IOS
- Scan body design and splinting techniques may improve digital accuracy
- Clinical decision should consider patient comfort, workflow, and lab capabilities
