## One-line Summary
3-arm RCT (n=42, NCT04366869) in implant-overdenture (OD) bruxer patients: BTX and occlusal splints both significantly improved patient satisfaction (TMD/NS) and Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI) vs. controls at 3, 6, 9, 12 months; BTX provided the largest gains at 9-12 months.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Saudi Dental Journal 2021;33(8):1004-1011
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.sdentj.2021.07.001
- **Published**: 10 July 2021 (online)
- **Trial registration**: NCT04366869 (Apr 2020)
- **Lead institution**: October University for Modern Sciences and Arts (MSA), Cairo; Taibah University; Ibb University
- **Design**: 3-arm parallel, single-blinded (assessor), pretest-posttest RCT, 12-month follow-up

## 2. Key Contributions
- First RCT to evaluate BTX vs. occlusal splints vs. nightly OD removal in implant-supported overdenture (OD) bruxers.
- Combines subjective sleep quality (PSQI), subjective satisfaction (TMD numeric scale), and objective prosthodontic (mechanical) complications.
- 12-month follow-up — longer than most BTX-bruxism RCTs.
- Bruxoff ambulatory EMG/ECG used as definite-bruxism diagnostic device.
- Mechanical complications recorded — relevant for implant prosthetics decision-making.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 42 adult patients with definite SB diagnosed by AASM criteria + Bruxoff (ambulatory EMG/ECG), implant-retained single-arch ODs opposing natural dentition or FPDs, loaded ≥1 year.
- Random 1:1:1 (n=14 per group):
  - Group I (control): instructed to remove OD overnight.
  - Group II: conventional occlusal stent.
  - Group III: BTX injection.
- Bilateral new ODs constructed and new nylon caps inserted for all groups before baseline.
- Sample size: power 80%, alpha 0.05, effect size 0.8 ± 0.9 (from Algabri 2017) → 42 sufficient.
- Outcomes (baseline, 3, 6, 9, 12 mo):
  - Subjective sleep quality: PSQI.
  - Patient satisfaction: TMD/numeric scale.
  - Mechanical (prosthodontic) complications recorded.
- Assessor-only single blinding; randomizer.org sequence; sealed envelopes; allocation concealment.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
**Patient satisfaction (TMD/NS, lower = better; Mean ± SD):**

| Timepoint | Control | Occlusal Splint | BTX |
|---|---|---|---|
| Baseline | 8.51 ± 1.1 | 8.72 ± 1.25 | 9.02 ± 1.05 |
| 3 months | 7.92 ± 1.84 | 6.02 ± 2.97 | 5.5 ± 0.88 |
| 6 months | 8.71 ± 0.88 | 5.1 ± 1.88 | 4.3 ± 0.87 |
| 9 months | 7.76 ± 1.06 | 4.7 ± 0.91 | 4.1 ± 1.77 |
| 12 months | 7.49 ± 1.87 | 4.3 ± 1.07 | 3.8 ± 1.02 |

p-values BTX vs. control: 0.001 (3 mo), 0.0001 (6 mo), 0.0013 (9 mo), 0.0001 (12 mo).

**PSQI (lower = better):**

| Timepoint | Control | Occlusal Splint | BTX |
|---|---|---|---|
| Baseline | 8.30 ± 1.78 | 8.17 ± 1.52 | 7.91 ± 2.25 |
| 3 months | 7.70 ± 3.42 | 6.83 ± 2.91 | 6.20 ± 2.34 |
| 6 months | 8.22 ± 1.23 | 6.63 ± 3.02 | 5.92 ± 2.11 |
| 9 months | 7.80 ± 1.52 | 7.13 ± 2.12 | 5.17 ± 1.92 |
| 12 months | 8.30 ± 1.78 | 6.94 ± 1.72 | 5.04 ± 2.12 |

- Different uppercase letters in original tables denote between-group significance (P < 0.05); different lowercase letters denote within-group temporal significance.
- BTX group achieved the lowest TMD/NS and PSQI at all post-baseline timepoints; advantage over splint widened at 9 and 12 months for PSQI.
- Mechanical complications: highest in control group (no protective intervention).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Single-blinded only (assessor); patients and providers necessarily unblinded.
- 14 per arm — modest sample.
- No direct pain (VAS) outcome; relies on sleep quality and satisfaction proxies.
- Mixed maxillary/mandibular OD locations.
- Authors note potential undiagnosed TMD as confounder; recommend EMG-based future studies (number of bruxing episodes from PSG).

## 6. Related Work
- Sahin 2024 — RCT in bruxism-myalgia where BTX matched DN/PT/MT.
- Coelho 2025 overview — bruxism SR landscape methodologically weak.
- Algabri 2017 — sample-size basis from same MSA group.
- Lobbezoo 2018 — current bruxism definition (sleep masticatory muscle activity).

## 7. Glossary
- **BTX** (보툴리눔독소, Botulinum Toxin).
- **OD** (임플란트 오버덴쳐, Implant Overdenture): Removable prosthesis retained by implants (ball attachments here).
- **SB** (수면이갈이, Sleep Bruxism): Masticatory-muscle activity during sleep.
- **PSQI** (피츠버그 수면질지수, Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index): 19-item self-reported global sleep quality score; higher = worse sleep.
- **TMD/NS** (TMD numeric scale): Patient-rated satisfaction/symptom severity used here.
- **Bruxoff**: Ambulatory EMG/ECG device for SB diagnosis (validated against PSG).
- **AASM** (미국수면학회, American Academy of Sleep Medicine): Diagnostic criteria source for SB.
- **PSG** (수면다원검사, Polysomnography): Gold standard for SB diagnosis.
