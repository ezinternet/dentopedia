## One-line Summary
Case series of 9 large Schneiderian membrane (SM) perforations during lateral-window sinus floor augmentation (SFA) repaired with leukocyte- and platelet-rich fibrin (L-PRF) membranes plus deproteinized bovine bone (Bio-Oss) and collagen barrier; 8-month re-entry placed 13 implants with satisfactory primary stability; 3–5 year radiographic follow-up showed osseointegration of all 13 implants and no signs of sinus infection or mucus.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Oral Implantology 2021;47(1):31–35
- **DOI**: 10.1563/aaid-joi-D-20-00044

- **Study type**: Clinical case series (long-term follow-up, n = 9 perforations / 13 implants)
- **Affiliation**: São Paulo State University (UNESP), Araraquara, Brazil

## 2. Key Contributions
- Demonstrates that **L-PRF membranes can repair "large" SM perforations** (typically defined > 10 mm or perforations where a collagen barrier alone is inadequate) without aborting the SFA procedure.
- Shows favorable long-term outcomes (3–5 years) with combined L-PRF + xenograft + collagen membrane protocol.
- Provides clinical workflow detail for using autologous fibrin biomaterial as an adhesive perforation patch.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Patient population: 9 patients undergoing lateral window SFA with intraoperative SM large perforation.
- Repair technique: **L-PRF membranes interposed over the perforated SM** until the rupture was no longer visible, providing both physical seal and biological adhesive.
- Grafting: maxillary sinus cavity filled with **deproteinized bovine bone mineral (DBBM, Bio-Oss; Geistlich, Switzerland)**.
- Closure: **collagen membrane** covering the lateral access window.
- Re-entry: implants placed at **8 months** post-SFA.
- Follow-up: CBCT or panoramic radiography at **3–5 years** post-implant.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Perforations treated**: 9/9 closed successfully with L-PRF.
- **Implants placed**: 13 implants total at 8-month re-entry; all achieved satisfactory **primary stability**.
- **Long-term outcomes**: osseointegration of all 13 implants confirmed radiographically at 3–5 year follow-up.
- **Sinus health**: no signs of postoperative sinusitis, mucus accumulation, or graft migration on CBCT/panoramic imaging.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small sample (n = 9 perforations); no control arm comparing L-PRF vs collagen-only repair.
- Outcomes assessed by **qualitative imaging** (CBCT/panoramic) rather than histology; no measurement of grafted bone volume.
- Exact perforation sizes were qualitatively described as "large" but quantitative dimensions were not standardized.
- No quantitative implant survival metrics (MBL, ISQ).
- Authors call for RCTs comparing L-PRF to collagen membrane and to L-PRF combined with collagen.

## 7. Glossary
- **L-PRF (Leukocyte- and Platelet-Rich Fibrin)**: Choukroun-protocol autologous fibrin matrix produced by single centrifugation of patient blood without anticoagulant; contains leukocytes, platelets, and growth factors (PDGF, TGF-β, VEGF).
- **SM (Schneiderian Membrane)**: respiratory epithelium-lined mucosa lining the maxillary sinus floor; integrity is critical for sinus floor augmentation success.
- **SFA (Sinus Floor Augmentation)**: bone augmentation under the SM to gain implant-supporting bone height in the posterior maxilla.
- **Lateral window approach**: SFA performed via osteotomy in the lateral sinus wall.
- **DBBM (Deproteinized Bovine Bone Mineral)**: xenograft; Bio-Oss is the most common commercial product.
- **Large SM perforation**: perforations typically > 10 mm (Vlassis–Fugazzotto class III–IV) that exceed the patch capacity of collagen membranes alone.
