## One-line Summary
SR+MA of 17 studies (12 RCTs) comparing four alternative site preparations vs conventional drilling — OD MD 10.25 ISQ (95% CI 4.97–15.52), osteotome MD 6.34, under-drilling MD 11.43; piezosurgery non-significant (MD 1.50).

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis
- **Journal**: Clinical Implant Dentistry and Related Research, 24(5):580-590, October 2022 (online 11 Aug 2022)
- **DOI**: 10.1111/cid.13127
- **Sample**: 17 studies meeting inclusion (12 RCTs); subgroups — OD 3 studies, osteotome (OT) 8 studies, piezosurgery (PS) 5 studies, under-drilling (UD) 4 studies
- **Primary Question**: Do alternative site-preparation methods (OD, osteotome, piezosurgery, under-drilling) improve primary implant stability (ISQ) over conventional drilling in low-density bone?

## 2. Key Contributions
- OD vs conventional: mean ISQ difference 10.25 (95% CI 4.97–15.52, P < 0.001).
- OT vs conventional: MD 6.34 (95% CI 2.26–10.42, P = 0.002).
- UD vs conventional: MD 11.43 (95% CI 5.17–17.68, P < 0.001).
- PS vs conventional: MD 1.50 (95% CI −2.54 to 5.54, P = 0.47) — not significant.
- Multi-database search including Chinese-language sources (Wanfang) — broader than English-only SRs.
- Funded by NSFC grants (52175422, 32101094) and Shanghai Ninth People's Hospital — not industry-linked.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
The authors searched MEDLINE/PubMed, Embase, Scopus, and Wanfang (Chinese) up to 31 March 2022 for studies comparing one of four alternative site preparations to conventional drilling on primary ISQ. Twelve of the 17 included studies were RCTs.

Outcome was primary implant stability via Osstell ISQ. Random-effects model was used for pooling. GRADE was applied to the overall body of evidence. Subgroup analyses were conducted by technique (OD/OT/PS/UD). The supporting appendix lists PRISMA checklist, excluded studies, quality assessment, subgroup analysis, and GRADE assessment.

The methodological caveat: this SR groups *all* "densification-like" techniques rather than restricting to counterclockwise OD. As a result the OD subgroup includes only 3 studies — small sample size for that subgroup.

|---|---|---|---|---|

| Piezosurgery (PS) | 5 | +1.50 | −2.54 – 5.54 | 0.47 (ns) |

- OD CI width (~10 ISQ units) reflects small subgroup (n=3 studies).
- UD shows the largest point estimate, similar magnitude to OD; PS effectively equivalent to conventional drilling.
- I² values not retrievable from the available text.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- OD subgroup has only 3 studies — the wide CI (4.97–15.52) indicates substantial uncertainty about the true magnitude.
- Marzorati 2026 (with tighter OD-only inclusion and 343 OD implants) finds a much smaller ISQ MD (3.24) — suggesting Yu's OD estimate may be inflated by the small-study effect or by inclusion of studies in which OD was tested in extremely low-density bone.
- Heterogeneity (I²) and individual study weights not retrievable here.
- Does not pool secondary stability, implant survival, or MBL — only primary ISQ.
- Conventional drilling protocols may have varied between included trials (drill diameter, irrigation, time-to-ISQ-measurement), inflating between-study variance.

## 7. Glossary
- **Under-drilling (UD)**: Site preparation with a final drill diameter smaller than the implant diameter; relies on bone elasticity for friction-fit.
- **Piezosurgery (PS)**: Site preparation using ultrasonic vibration of cutting tips — gentle on soft tissue, traditionally used for sinus and nerve-proximate surgery.
- **Osteotome (Summers)**: Hand-mallet-driven tapered osteotomes to compact bone laterally and apically.
- **Osseodensification (OD)**: Counterclockwise-rotating multifluted burs that plastically deform bone outward without extraction.
- **ISQ**: Implant Stability Quotient — Osstell resonance-frequency analysis, 0–100 scale.
