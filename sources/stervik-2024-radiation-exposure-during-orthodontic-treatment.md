## Why Ingested

Supplies a **second independent background-day data point** for the [[overviews/dental-imaging-effective-dose-background-equivalent-overview]]: a *full orthodontic imaging course* (≈1 panoramic + 1 lateral cephalogram + ~3 intraoral periapicals, ≈7 radiographs/patient over the whole treatment) is quantified as "5–10 days of natural background radiation" (against a Swedish 1–2 mSv/year baseline). This corroborates the overview's background-equivalent framing in a pediatric/orthodontic context and adds lateral-cephalogram dose data the overview otherwise lacked.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective record review (n=1,790 Swedish children/adolescents, two clinics) — a complete orthodontic imaging course (≈7 radiographs: 1 panoramic + 1 lateral ceph + ~3 intraorals) equals about 5–10 days of natural background radiation, with an estimated fatal-cancer risk well under 1 per 100,000.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Quantifies the **cumulative radiation burden of a full orthodontic imaging course** in children/adolescents — ≈7 radiographs per patient across treatment planning, treatment, and follow-up.
2. Expresses that burden as a **background-radiation equivalent**: about **5–10 days** of natural background radiation (Swedish baseline 1–2 mSv/year).
3. Provides **per-modality effective doses** including the **lateral cephalogram (5.6 µSv)** — a modality often missing from background-equivalent comparisons that focus on panoramic + intraoral.
4. Translates effective dose into **age-stratified fatal-cancer risk** (15%/Sv for <10 y, 10%/Sv for 10–20 y), confirming risk is age-dependent and higher in younger children.
5. Documents **poor justification recording** (5% at planning, 60% at treatment) as an ALARA/justification quality gap.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Retrospective review of patient records from two specialist Swedish orthodontic clinics (A: 2004–2005, n=1,125; B: 2011–2012, n=665). Total n=1,790; mean age 14.8 ± 2.57 y; 1,063 F / 727 M.
- **Exclusions**: cleft palate / craniofacial disorders, incomplete records, incomplete treatment, relocation.
- **Data captured**: number and type of radiograph by treatment stage (planning / treatment / follow-up), justification text, and supplementary (referred) tomographic exams.
- **Dose source**: effective doses drawn from Granlund et al. 2016, Ludlow et al. 2008, and Radiation Protection No. 172.
- **Risk model**: stochastic fatal-cancer risk = effective dose × age-specific risk factor (15%/Sv <10 y; 10%/Sv 10–20 y); linear non-threshold (LNT) model assumed for the low-dose (<100 mGy) range.
- **Statistics**: between-clinic comparison by t-test, p < 0.05 significant.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **~7 radiographs per patient** over the whole orthodontic course; only 1% (20/1,790) had no radiographs. Total 14,306 radiographs in the cohort.
- **Typical planning workup**: 1 panoramic + 1 lateral cephalogram + ~3 intraoral periapicals.
- **Per-image effective dose**: panoramic 14.2–36 µSv; lateral cephalogram 5.6 µSv; intraoral (single) 0.8 µSv; CBCT dento-alveolar 32–43 µSv.
- **Fatal-cancer risk** (induced per 100,000): one panoramic = 0.54 (<10 y) / 0.36 (10–20 y); lateral 0.10 / 0.06; intraoral 0.01; CBCT 0.65 / 0.32. Overall risk judged "low" (≈2 fatal cancers in a 400,000-patient population from a single panoramic each).
- **Tomography**: 8.2% (146) referred; CBCT small FOV (4×4 cm) in 6.1%; small-FOV in 85% of tomographic cases, consistent with EC recommendations.
- **Justification recording**: 5% (planning), 60% (treatment), 15% (follow-up) — a documentation gap.

**VERBATIM background-radiation-equivalent (with the natural-background reference value):**

> "Annual exposure from natural background radiation in Sweden has been calculated as 1–2 mSv []; thus, the additional radiation dose to children and adolescent in orthodontic treatment corresponds to 5–10 days of background radiation. Furthermore, a panoramic examination delivers a dose that is six times higher than a lateral cephalogram and 4–5 times higher than an intraoral image."

Abstract restatement: *"The radiation risk from orthodontic treatment was equivalent to about 5–10 days of natural background radiation."*

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- No assessment of the **diagnostic benefit** of the radiographs on treatment outcome — burden quantified, value not.
- Effective-dose risk factors are **derived for adults**; the authors note children's organs are closer together (smaller body diameter), so real pediatric risk may be **underestimated**.
- Data are historical (2004–2012) and from two clinics in one Swedish county — generalisability and currency limited; dose values predate some newer low-dose digital systems.
- LNT model is an assumption for the <100 mGy range; absolute risks are inherently uncertain at these very low doses.
- No Sweden-specific pediatric orthodontic radiographic guidelines existed at the time — a noted gap.

## 7. Glossary

- **Effective dose (Sv/µSv)**: tissue-weighted whole-body dose used to compare stochastic risk across imaging modalities.
- **Stochastic effect**: probabilistic radiation effect (e.g., cancer) with no dose threshold; probability (not severity) rises with dose.
- **Linear non-threshold (LNT) model**: assumes cancer risk increases linearly with dose with no safe threshold; standard for low-dose (<100 mGy) estimation.
- **ALARA**: As Low As Reasonably Achievable — radiation-protection principle requiring justification + optimisation of every exposure.
- **Lateral cephalogram**: standardised lateral skull radiograph for orthodontic cephalometric analysis (here 5.6 µSv).
- **CBCT**: cone-beam computed tomography; FOV = field of view (small FOV minimises dose).
- **Natural background radiation**: ambient ionising radiation (cosmic, terrestrial, radon); Swedish baseline 1–2 mSv/year.
