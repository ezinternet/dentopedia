## 2. Key Contributions
- 9 studies included: 475 PS vs 462 conventional platform implants
- PS: significantly less MBL (MD 0.255 mm, p<0.05)
- PS: probing depth increase MD 0.082 mm — not significant
- One Study Remove analysis: PD difference became significant (MD 0.190 mm)
- 2022 update providing more recent SR+MA evidence on PS

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
SR+MA following PRISMA. Multiple databases. Inclusion: studies comparing PS vs conventional (CM) platforms with peri-implant outcome data. Primary outcomes: peri-implant bone loss and probing depth. Meta-analysis: random-effects; mean difference (MD). Sensitivity: one-study-removed analysis.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- PS: bone loss MD 0.255 mm less than CM (statistically significant)
- PS: probing depth MD 0.082 mm greater — not significant (p>0.05)
- One-study-removed sensitivity: PD difference became significant (MD 0.190 mm)
- Both measures favour PS for bone preservation
- Probing depth finding warrants further investigation

## 7. Glossary
- **PS**: Platform switching — use of smaller-diameter abutment on larger implant collar
- **PM**: Platform matching — abutment matches implant platform diameter
- **MBL**: Marginal bone loss — radiographic bone level change around implant
- **TL**: Tissue-level implant — transmucosal component integral to implant
- **BL**: Bone-level implant — implant platform at crestal bone level
