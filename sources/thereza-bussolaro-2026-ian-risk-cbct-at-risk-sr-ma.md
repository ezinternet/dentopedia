## Why Ingested

기존 [[oral-surgery/li-2022-ian-position-nerve-injury-sr-ma]] 및 [[oral-surgery/thereza-bussolaro-2026-cbct-ian-predictive-factors-sr-ma]](OR 값)를 보완하는 동일 그룹의 companion SR+MA — 이 논문은 OR 대신 절대 위험률(%)을 CBCT 특징별로 계층화해 환자 상담과 수술 계획 결정에 직접 사용 가능한 수치를 제공한다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 11 prospective/retrospective cohorts with CBCT: overall IAN injury risk 9.52%, rising to 14.76% in high-risk cases; cortical bone absence elevates risk to 15.65%, lingual canal position ranges 1.34–27.47% by subclassification.

## 한줄요약

CBCT 보유 11개 코호트 SR+MA — 전체 하치조신경(IAN) 손상률 9.52%; 고위험군 14.76%; 피질골 소실 시 15.65%; 설측 위치에 따라 1.34–27.47% 범위.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Provides absolute IAN injury risk percentages stratified by CBCT feature (not just OR)
- 9.52% overall IAN injury risk in patients undergoing mandibular third molar surgery with preoperative CBCT
- High-risk CBCT profile elevates injury rate to 14.76%
- Cortical bone absence: 15.65% risk vs 0.39% with cortical bone present (40× difference)
- Mandibular canal (MC) position risk ranges 1.34–27.47% by subclassification
- MC shape risk: 6.56–18.36%; GRADE certainty: low to very low (limited sample sizes)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Inclusion**: Prospective + retrospective cohorts with CBCT imaging AND objective neurosensory testing (not subjective patient report)
- **Exclusion**: Subjective assessments only, no CBCT data, unrelated procedures
- **Studies included**: 11 (from 1,039 records screened; 1.05% inclusion rate)
- **Risk of bias tool**: Joanna Briggs Institute (JBI)
- **Statistical model**: Random-effects meta-analysis; I²/Tau² for heterogeneity; GRADE certainty

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| CBCT Stratification | IAN Injury Risk |
|---|---|
| Overall (all cases) | 9.52% (95% CI 7.18–12.53%) |
| High-risk cases (CBCT features) | 14.76% (95% CI 11.37–18.94%) |
| MC cortical bone absent | 15.65% |
| MC cortical bone present | 0.39% |
| MC position (range by subclassification) | 1.34–27.47% |
| MC shape (range) | 6.56–18.36% |

- GRADE certainty: low to very low due to limited high-quality prospective studies
- Substantial variation in risk by specific tomographic feature — underscoring the need for individualized CBCT-based assessment

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Only 11 studies met strict inclusion criteria (objective neurosensory testing + CBCT) — most published studies rely on subjective assessment
- Low to very low GRADE certainty limits clinical generalizability
- High heterogeneity in CBCT acquisition protocols and neurosensory testing methods
- Need for multi-center prospective studies with standardized CBCT and quantified neurosensory testing
