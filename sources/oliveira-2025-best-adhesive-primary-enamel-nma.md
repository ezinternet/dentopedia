## Why Ingested

PubMed full-text surveillance (레진접착) — 기존 enamel 접착 근거는 모두 영구치(permanent) 기반이고, 유치(primary/deciduous) 법랑질 전용 합성 근거가 위키에 없었다. 본 SR + network meta-analysis (Oliveira 2025, 14편)는 [[resin-bonding/hong-2021-universal-adhesive-etching-modes-sr-ma]]의 universal adhesive etch-mode 결론이 유치 법랑질에서도 성립하는지를 보강·한정하고, [[resin-bonding/assis-2023-acid-etching-universal-adhesive-nccl-sr-ma]] 및 [[resin-bonding/fehrenbach-2021-10-mdp-bonding-performance-sr-ma]]의 10-MDP/selective-etch 논의를 소아치과 substrate로 확장한다.

## One-line Summary

Bayesian network meta-analysis (14 in vitro studies, primary tooth enamel bond strength): 3-step etch-and-rinse ranked best (highest SUCRA); two-bottle universal adhesive WITH phosphoric acid etching was statistically equivalent to it; within the same number of steps, self-etch ≈ etch-and-rinse — suggesting clinical steps can be reduced for deciduous enamel.

## 한줄요약

베이지안 네트워크 메타분석 (in vitro 14편, 유치 법랑질 결합강도): 3단계 etch-and-rinse가 SUCRA 1위였고, 인산 산부식을 동반한 2병형 universal adhesive는 그와 통계적으로 동등했으며, 같은 단계 수에서는 self-etch ≈ etch-and-rinse — 유치 법랑질에서는 임상 단계 축소 여지가 있음을 시사.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR + network meta-analysis ranking adhesive systems specifically on **primary (deciduous) tooth enamel** bond strength — a substrate distinct from permanent enamel (thinner, less mineralized, ~30 μm aprismatic surface layer resisting acid etching).
- Establishes a SUCRA ranking of 8 adhesive treatment nodes against the 3-step etch-and-rinse reference.
- Tests whether step-count reduction is feasible for pediatric restorative/preventive (sealant) procedures.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **PICO**: P = primary tooth enamel; I = any dental adhesive; O = bond strength.
- Databases: PubMed/MEDLINE, Embase, Web of Science, Scopus + gray literature (Jan 2025); Rayyan; two calibrated reviewers (κ = 0.83).
- Excluded: pre-treatment before adhesion, primary-vs-permanent comparisons, etching-agent/time variations, bovine enamel, saliva contamination.
- 331 retrieved → **14 included** (2001–2024); **35 adhesive systems** tested.
- Risk of bias: QUIN tool — **all 14 graded low risk**; weakest domains = sample-size calculation, operator details, randomization.
- Statistics: mixed-treatment-comparison Bayesian NMA, mean difference, random-effects (lower DIC), 3-step etch-and-rinse as reference; node-split inconsistency; SUCRA ranking; MetaInsight. Only 24 h shear/microshear/microtensile data; thermocycled/aged studies excluded → **10 studies, 8 nodes** in the network.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- vs reference (3_Step_ER): **only 2-bottle universal + phosphoric acid etch (2b_Univ_PAE) was statistically equivalent**; all other systems inferior.
- All pairwise: 3_Step_ER superior to 2_Step_SE_noPAE, 2_Step_ER, 2b_Univ_noPAE, 1b_Univ_noPAE, 1b_Univ_PAE, 1_Step_SE_noPAE. 2_Step_ER and 2b_Univ_PAE also beat 1_Step_SE_noPAE.
- **SUCRA**: 3_Step_ER best; **1-step self-etch without PAE worst**.
- Within the same number of steps, self-etch ≈ etch-and-rinse (no significant difference).
- For two-bottle and one-bottle universal adhesives, adding phosphoric acid etching did NOT significantly change enamel bond strength.
- No network inconsistency (node-split p>0.05).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only **immediate (24 h) in vitro** bond strength — no hydrolytic/thermal/mechanical/salivary aging in the quantitative pool; not a clinical recommendation.
- Substrate heterogeneity (aprismatic vs prismatic/sanded enamel).
- Needs artificial-aging studies and RCTs on deciduous enamel.

## 7. Glossary

- **PAE**: phosphoric acid etching.
- **SUCRA**: surface under the cumulative ranking curve (probability of being best).
- **Aprismatic enamel**: prismless surface enamel layer (~30 μm in primary teeth) resistant to acid etching.
- **QUIN tool**: risk-of-bias tool for in vitro dental studies.
