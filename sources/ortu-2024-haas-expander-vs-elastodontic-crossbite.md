## Why Ingested

First head-to-head comparison we hold of an **elastodontic (myofunctional) device versus a rigid orthopaedic palatal expander (Haas)** for transverse maxillary deficiency in growing children — the authors note "there are no articles in the literature comparing the results obtained with elastodontic appliances and traditional orthopaedic appliances." It directly complements the sibling RCT [[wiki/orthodontics/myofunctional/ortu-2025-removable-devices-palatal-expansion-rct]] (removable elastodontic devices for palatal expansion) by adding a rigid-expander control arm, letting the wiki contrast functional vs skeletal expansion mechanisms and their equivalence on dentoalveolar transverse outcomes.

## One-line Summary

Single-centre prospective comparative study (n=64, ages 7–8; 32 elastodontic Equilibrator vs 32 Haas expander) finding **no statistically significant difference** in intermolar (IMW) or intercanine (ICW) width gain at 12 months — the two devices were equally effective, with the elastodontic device offering greater comfort and lower relapse/complication risk.

## 한줄요약

단일기관 전향적 비교연구(n=64, 7~8세; 탄성교정장치 Equilibrator 32명 vs Haas 확장장치 32명)에서 12개월째 상악 대구치간폭경(IMW)·견치간폭경(ICW) 증가량에 **통계적 유의차 없음** — 두 장치 효과는 동등하며, 탄성교정장치가 편안함·재발/합병증 측면에서 유리.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Comparison of the Haas Expander and the Elastodontic Device for the Resolution of Transverse Discrepancies in Growing Patients: A Single-Centre Observational Study
- **Authors**: Ortu E, Di Nicolantonio S, Cova S, Pietropaoli D, De Simone L, Monaco A
- **Journal**: Reports (MDPI), 2024;7(2):41
- **Year**: 2024 (published 2024-05-21)
- **DOI**: 10.3390/reports7020041 · **PMID**: 40729152 · **PMCID**: PMC12225328
- **Study type**: Single-centre prospective comparative ("observational") study with computer-generated 1:1 randomized allocation; self-described pilot study
- **Setting**: Dental Clinic, Dept. of Clinical Medicine, Public Health, Life and Environmental Sciences, University of L'Aquila, Italy (IRB 57/2021–22)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First reported direct comparison of an **elastodontic device** (Eptamed Equilibrator, EQ Series 00) against a **rigid orthopaedic rapid palatal expander** (Haas) for transverse maxillary deficiency in growing patients.
- Demonstrates **equivalence** of the two devices on dentoalveolar transverse gain (IMW, ICW) at 12 months — the elastodontic device achieves comparable expansion without the metal banded/bonded hardware, painful sutural distraction, or parental activation burden of the Haas.
- Positions the elastodontic appliance as a comfort/compliance-favourable option for **mild maxillary constriction**, where dentoalveolar (not skeletal) expansion suffices, with claimed lower relapse risk via re-education of perioral/lingual function.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Source population**: 120 patients aged 7–8 treated until March 2020, reviewed from archived plaster models database. 27 declined consent, 29 failed inclusion criteria → **64 enrolled**.
- **Groups** (1:1, 16F/16M each):
  - **Test (elastodontic)**: n=32, mean age 7.08 ± 0.44; orange Eptamed Equilibrator (00 series), medium hardness, worn overnight; monthly follow-up. 10 unilateral crossbite / 22 bilateral.
  - **Control (Haas)**: n=32, mean age 7.32 ± 0.50; Haas expander banded on first upper permanent/deciduous molars, cemented, chair-activated two quarter-turns (0.40 mm; Lamparski 2003), home activation 0.40 mm/day × 15 days, then blocked and left in situ 3 months. 14 unilateral crossbite.
- **Inclusion**: skeletal Class I, molar Class I, complete eruption of upper sixths, unilateral or bilateral crossbite (IOTN grade 3). **Exclusion**: IOTN > 4, caries, TMD, epilepsy, systemic disease, periodontal disease, no informed consent.
- **Randomization**: computer-generated (Sealed Envelope power calculator), stratified by centre, 1:1 allocation, random block sizes 4/6/8.
- **Measurement**: intraoral scans (iTero Element) at T0 (pre-treatment) and T1 (12 months); imported to OrthoCAD 5.9.1.50 for linear measures + 3D palate morphology. Intermolar width (IMW, permanent/deciduous first molars) and intercanine width (ICW, deciduous canines). Single operator; method error by Dahlberg's formula from double measurement of 30 random casts 1 week apart.
- **Statistics**: t-test (continuous), chi-square (sex). Shapiro-Wilk showed normality but non-parametric **Wilcoxon rank-sum test** adopted for conservativeness. Significance p < 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **No statistically significant difference** between Haas and Eptamed groups in IMW or ICW at either T0 or T1 — the two devices were **equally effective**.
- Baseline comparability: sex p = 1, age p = 0.143; IMW and ICW means/SD at T0 and T1 not statistically different between groups.
- Group differences in IMW/ICW depended only on device type (after Wilcoxon control for sex/physiological growth).
- **Intermolar width increased more than intercanine width** in both groups — attributed to posterior band anchorage promoting more distal enlargement (consistent with Boysen et al. and Petren et al.).
- No dropouts; all patients collaborative.
- Note: specific mm gain values and group means are presented only in figures/tables in the source (referenced but not transcribed in the available full text); the central reported finding is the absence of a significant between-device difference.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Self-described **pilot study** with small sample (n=64).
- Short follow-up — re-evaluation only at 12 months; longer follow-up needed (relapse claims for Haas vs elastodontic are hypothesized, not measured).
- Single centre, single operator, narrow age band (7–8 y).
- Outcomes limited to dentoalveolar transverse measures on casts/scans; no CBCT skeletal sutural separation quantification distinguishing dentoalveolar vs true skeletal expansion.
- Authors call for larger, more homogeneous cohort over a longer period.

## 6. Related Work

- **Lo Giudice et al.** — elastodontic devices significantly increased IMW and ICW vs untreated subjects (supports elastodontic efficacy).
- **Lippold et al.** — bonded Hyrax RME increased intercanine/intermolar width vs untreated.
- **Boysen et al.; Petren et al.** — slow expansion (quad-helix vs transverse plates): IMW increased more than ICW (concordant). **Bjerklin et al.** — opposite (ICW > IMW).
- **La Blonde et al.** — Hyrax activation protocol comparison (0.5 vs 0.8 mm/day): faster activation gave more transverse gain but more tooth tipping and cortical bone loss.
- **Idris et al.** — discordant: modified activators more effective than T4K myofunctional trainer for Class II div 1.
- **Ortu et al.** — EMG showed reduced muscle tension with elastodontic devices in mandibular retrusion.

## 7. Glossary

- **Elastodontic device**: removable, highly elastic (elastomeric/silicone) functional appliance covering both arches; activated by the bite/muscle contraction, guides tooth repositioning and re-educates perioral/lingual musculature (here: Eptamed Equilibrator / EQ Series 00).
- **Haas expander**: rigid banded/bonded rapid palatal expander (Haas 1961) that distracts the midpalatal suture via screw activation, remodelling the craniomaxillofacial complex.
- **IMW (intermolar width)**: transverse distance between upper first (permanent/deciduous) molars.
- **ICW (intercanine width)**: transverse distance between upper canines (deciduous).
- **IOTN**: Index of Orthodontic Treatment Need (Brook & Shaw 1989).
- **TDM / transverse jaw deficit**: reduced maxillary transverse dimension, typically with uni-/bilateral crossbite.
- **Palatine spot**: anterior palatal point of tongue rest contact promoted by the lingual ramp for proper palate development.
