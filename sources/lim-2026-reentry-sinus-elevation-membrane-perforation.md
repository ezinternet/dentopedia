## One-line Summary
JPIS 2026;56(2):161-171: Animal (rabbit, n=6): Re-entry SFE 8wk after complete membrane perforation — new bone area significantly smaller (5.8 vs 10.5 mm², P<0.05); complete perforation (>10mm) compromises subsequent bone formation.

## 1. Key Results
- n=6 rabbits; intentional complete perforation (≥10mm) + collagen matrix; re-entry SFE at 8 weeks
- SFE_ComPerf group vs intact SFE group
- Micro-CT: no significant difference in total augmented volume or mineralized tissue volume
- Histomorphometry: new bone area SFE_ComPerf 5.8±2.7 mm² vs SFE 10.5±1.9 mm² (P<0.05)
- Impaired bone formation near sinus membrane in ComPerf group

## 2. Clinical Implications
- Complete sinus membrane perforation (>10mm) may compromise bone formation if re-entry SFE is planned
- Staged approach with adequate healing time after large perforation critical before re-SFE
- Collagen matrix alone may not fully restore membrane function for bone formation
