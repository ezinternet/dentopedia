## Why Ingested

This is Part 2 (composite *processing* technique) of the German S3 direct-composite guideline whose Part 1 (indications) we already hold as [[resin/wolff-2024-direct-composite-restorations-permanent-teeth]]; it completes the indication→processing chain by giving step-by-step procedural recommendations (caries removal, isolation, matrix, adhesive, curing, polishing). It also provides a consensus anchor for our bulk-fill processing pages such as [[resin/boaro-2019-bulk-fill-composites-clinical-properties-sr-ma]] (statement: bulk-fill safely polymerized to 4 mm with adequate-power units).

## One-line Summary

German S3 consensus-based clinical practice guideline (Part 2; 6 PICO questions, panel from 20 societies) issuing 7 recommendations + 2 statements on composite processing: prefer one-stage selective caries removal near pulp, accept relative or absolute isolation (rubber dam may aid longevity), use anatomically preformed sectional matrix + ring/wedge for Class II, etch enamel with phosphoric acid, prefer 2-step self-etch / 3-step etch-and-rinse / universal adhesives, and polish all restorations.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: German S3 (highest tier) evidence- and consensus-based clinical practice guideline, Part 2 of 2.
- **Journal**: The Journal of Adhesive Dentistry 2024; 26: 201–212. DOI 10.3290/j.jad.b5749192. CC-BY 4.0.
- **German original** registered on AWMF (register 083-028).
- **Scope of Part 2**: the *manufacturing process* of direct composite restorations — caries removal, field isolation, matrix technique, adhesive technique, light polymerization, finishing/polishing. (Part 1 covered indications; DOI b5748881.)
- **Outcomes of interest**: restoration survival rate and restoration quality (quality indicators, surface gloss / surface discoloration).
- **Method note**: Part 2 covers PICO questions 6–11. For feasibility, systematic evidence grading was applied only to PICO 1–5 (Part 1); for PICO 6–11 a systematic literature search was run, relevant literature provided to the panel (Feb 2022), and recommendations were therefore **consensus-based**.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides a structured, voted consensus protocol for every step of placing a direct composite restoration, harmonizing 20 national societies/organizations.
- Seven consensus-based recommendations + two consensus-based statements (Part 2 totals; statement on bulk-fill counted), all reached by **strong consensus** (votes 16/0/0 or 17/0/0).
- Bridges procedural decisions (excavation, isolation, matrix, adhesive, curing, polishing) to the two clinical outcomes of survival and quality.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Systematic literature search by two methodologists in MEDLINE and Cochrane Library via OVID, studies up to December 2021; reference lists hand-searched.
- Inclusion: ≥12 months follow-up, ≥15 restorations, published since 1990, languages German/English/French/Russian; study designs varied by PICO (SR/MA, CCT/RCT, prospective/retrospective cohort).
- Six PICO questions (6–11): caries excavation, work field isolation, matrix technique, adhesive technique, light polymerization, finishing/polishing.
- Recommendations formulated by separate working groups per AWMF specifications, then voted in a **NIH Type 1 structured consensus conference** (present → questions/amendments → vote → re-vote if needed).
- Strength of recommendation and consensus levels classified per Tables 2–3; PRISMA flow diagrams (Fig 1) document selection.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Evidence base identified per topic and the resulting voted recommendations:

- **Caries excavation** (8 SRs): Both selective and non-selective excavation acceptable; for **dentin lesions close to the pulp, one-stage selective removal should be preferred** over stepwise or non-selective (Rec 1; 16/0/0). A Cochrane review found selective/staged removal more effective in deep lesions, though evidence quality low to very low.
- **Contamination control / isolation** (5 SRs, 6 clinical trials): Both relative and absolute isolation can be used successfully; **rubber dam (absolute) could have a positive long-term effect on longevity** (Rec 2; 16/0/0). Most reviews found no difference rubber dam vs cotton rolls; some favored rubber dam.
- **Matrix technique** (4 in-vivo studies): Both metal and acrylic matrices give sufficient proximal contact (Rec 3; 16/0/0). For Class II, an **anatomically preformed sectional matrix + wedge + ring system should be preferred** to optimize contact point and avoid excess (Rec 4; 16/0/0) — supported by in-vitro data on contact strength, marginal excess, marginal ridge stability.
- **Adhesive technique** (13 SRs): **Etch enamel of all direct composites with phosphoric acid** to improve enamel margin quality and prevent marginal discoloration (Rec 5; 16/0/0). **Prefer 2-step self-etch, 3-step etch-and-rinse, or universal adhesives** (Rec 6; 16/0/0). Older studies favored 2-step SE / 3-step E&R for durability and secondary-caries resistance; universal adhesives showed similar results.
- **Light polymerization** (1 SR + 8 clinical trials): Statement 1 (17/0/0) — light curing is decisive; correct handling (polymerization direction, distance, light-cone diameter), applied energy (power × time), and composite opacity/shade are relevant. Statement 2 (17/0/0) — **bulk-fill composites can be polymerized safely up to 4 mm depth with units of appropriate power**.
- **Finishing/polishing** (2 studies): Statement 3 (16/0/0) — restorations **should be polished** to improve surface and reduce plaque build-up.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Recommendations for PICO 6–11 are **consensus-based, not formally graded** (systematic evidence grading limited to Part 1's PICO 1–5 for feasibility).
- Underlying evidence is often weak: Cochrane caries-removal evidence low to very low; little in-vivo matrix evidence (only 4 studies); evidence on light polymerization and polishing described as "poor," so statements lean on standardized protocols/standard practice rather than clinical trials.
- German guideline context; generalization requires clinical judgment for non-German settings.

## 7. Glossary

- **S3 guideline**: highest AWMF methodological tier (systematic evidence + structured consensus).
- **PICO**: Population, Intervention, Comparison, Outcome — framework structuring each guideline question.
- **Selective caries excavation**: leaving caries-affected dentin near the pulp to preserve pulp vitality (vs non-selective removal to hard dentin).
- **Absolute vs relative isolation**: rubber dam (absolute) vs cotton rolls/suction/drying agents (relative).
- **Sectional matrix + ring/wedge**: partial matrix covering the proximal box, tensioned by a separating ring and wedge for tight contact in Class II.
- **Etch-and-rinse / self-etch / universal adhesive**: adhesive strategies differing in phosphoric-acid use and number of steps.
- **Bulk-fill composite**: composite formulated to be cured in increments up to 4 mm.
