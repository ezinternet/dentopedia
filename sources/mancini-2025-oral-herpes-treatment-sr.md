## One-line Summary

Systematic review (PRISMA, 2005-2025) of topical vs systemic therapy for oral HSV: topical agents (aciclovir cream, docosanol) shorten lesion duration when applied early but do not reduce recurrence; systemic antivirals (aciclovir, valaciclovir, famciclovir) address both resolution and recurrence prevention.

## 1. Document Information

- International Journal of Molecular Sciences 2025;26(17):8490. Published 2025-09-01. PRISMA-compliant.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Separates the distinct clinical goals of topical (local symptom/lesion control) vs systemic (recurrence prevention) HSV therapy.
- Supports short-course high-dose systemic regimens for episodic and suppressive use.
- Reviews novel delivery/combination strategies (aciclovir-hydrocortisone, photodynamic therapy).
- Identifies viral-latency/immune-modulation/gene-editing as future directions.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Databases: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science (2005-2025). Eligible: RCTs and observational studies with validated clinical outcomes for topical or systemic HSV treatment.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Topical: reduces lesion duration and local symptoms only when applied early; minimal systemic absorption; no effect on recurrence frequency.
- Systemic: aciclovir/valaciclovir/famciclovir effective for episodic and suppressive therapy; indicated for recurrent, disseminated, or high-risk infection.
- Combination/novel delivery may enhance local efficacy and symptom control.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Heterogeneous study designs and outcomes; no meta-analysis; need for standardized outcome measures and mechanism-based therapies.

## 6. Related Work

- Episodic vs suppressive antiviral trial literature.
- Docosanol and topical aciclovir herpes-labialis studies.

## 7. Glossary

- HSV: Herpes Simplex Virus.
- Suppressive therapy: continuous antiviral to prevent recurrence.
- PDT: Photodynamic Therapy.
