## Why Ingested

직전 세션의 MRONJ 임상 Q&A에서 "Prolia vs Xgeva MRONJ 치료 예후 차이"를 Jung 2022(n=98 Prolia-only)와 Yokoo 2025(n=324 Xgeva-only 암환자)로만 간접 비교해야 했음. Cho 2026은 같은 기관(KNUDH)에서 Prolia(G1), BP→Prolia 전환(G2), Xgeva(G3)를 직접 비교(n=178)하여 치료 성공률 차이를 통계적으로 확인한 최초의 3군 비교 한국 코호트. [[drug/mronj/jung-2022-denosumab-mronj-osteoporosis-5year]]를 직접 확장하고 [[drug/mronj/yokoo-2025-denosumab-mronj-breast-cancer-bone]]의 고용량 DRONJ 예후불량 소견을 골다공증 맥락에서 재확인.

## One-line Summary

Single-institution 5-year retrospective cohort (n=178 DRONJ patients, KNUDH) showing Xgeva 120mg had significantly worse treatment success (53.8%) vs Prolia 60mg (85.0%) or BP-to-Prolia switch (82.8%), with Dmab dose as the only significant predictor (OR 5.13).

## 한줄요약

경북대학교치과병원 5년 후향적 코호트(n=178 DRONJ) — Xgeva 치료 성공률 53.8% vs Prolia 85.0%(p=0.027), 고용량 Dmab만이 치료 실패의 유일한 독립 예측인자(OR 5.13), BP→Prolia 전환군은 발생 시점 앞당기되 예후 차이 없음.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First Korean 3-group comparison (Prolia vs BP→Prolia switch vs Xgeva) in DRONJ patients within a single cohort
- Quantifies treatment success difference by dose: high-dose Dmab (Xgeva) = 53.8% vs low-dose (Prolia) = 85.0% — OR 5.13 for low-dose success (P=0.028)
- BP→Dmab switch: accelerates DRONJ onset significantly (16.0 vs 23.2 months after starting Dmab, P<0.001) but does NOT worsen severity or prognosis — cumulative antiresorptive duration (mean prior BP: 56.7±46.4 months) explains early onset
- Dmab dose is the ONLY significant predictor in multivariate analysis; MRONJ stage, age, treatment duration, location, and treatment modality are NOT significant
- Peri-implantitis as DRONJ trigger in 12.4% of cases — third most common after extraction (45.5%) and periodontal disease (14.0%); critical for implant planning in antiresorptive-exposed patients
- Largest single-institution DRONJ cohort from Korea; contextualizes earlier studies that were predominantly high-dose/cancer-based
- In South Korea, denosumab was covered by national insurance for osteoporosis beginning in 2016 — rising DRONJ incidence explained by shift from BP to Dmab in the osteoporosis population

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Retrospective cohort, single institution
- **Period**: August 2019 – August 2024 (5 years)
- **n**: 178 (193 initially identified; 15 excluded for refusal or loss to follow-up beyond 3 months)
- **Groups**:
  - G1: Low-dose Dmab only — Prolia 60 mg/6 months (osteoporosis indication), n=60
  - G2: BP → low-dose Dmab transition (prior alendronate, ibandronate, pamidronate, risedronate, or zoledronate; then switched to Prolia), n=105
  - G3: High-dose Dmab only — Xgeva 120 mg/4 weeks (cancer bone disease indication), n=13
- **MRONJ definition and staging**: 2022 AAOMS position paper criteria
  - Stage 1: Exposed/probeable bone, asymptomatic, no infection
  - Stage 2: Exposed/probeable bone with pain and/or infection
  - Stage 3: Exposed/probeable bone with ≥1 of: pathological fracture, extra-oral fistula, osteolysis extending to inferior border, sinus involvement
- **Minimum follow-up**: 3 months post-treatment
- **Outcome categories**:
  - Complete healing: no exposed bone/fistula, no discharge/edema/pain, complete mucosal coverage
  - Partial healing: MRONJ stage improvement without complete mucosal coverage
  - No healing: unchanged or worsened stage
  - Recurrence: re-appearance of exposed bone ≥8 weeks after achieving complete healing
  - Treatment success = complete + partial healing
- **Statistics**: ANOVA + Tukey's HSD (continuous), Fisher's exact / chi-square (categorical), multiple logistic regression (SPSS v29.0, IBM); significance P<0.05

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Demographics**:
- Overall: Female 86.0%; Osteoporosis patients 92.7%
- Mean age: G1 77.4±6.7yr; G2 76.6±7.2yr; G3 63.5±8.9yr (P<0.001; cancer patients significantly younger)

**Dmab duration to MRONJ onset**:
- G1: 23.2±15.3 months
- G2: 16.0±11.3 months (P<0.001 vs G1 and G3)
- G3: 27.9±18.8 months
- G2 prior BP duration: 56.7±46.4 months before switching to Dmab

**Precipitating factors** (no intergroup difference, P=0.656):
- Tooth extraction: 45.5% (n=81)
- Periodontal disease: 14.0% (n=25)
- Peri-implantitis: 12.4% (n=22)
- Denture-related trauma: 9.6% (n=17)
- Implant surgery: 6.2% (n=11)
- Apical lesion: 2.2% (n=4)
- Dentoalveolar surgery: 1.1% (n=2)
- Idiopathic: 9.0% (n=16)

**MRONJ staging** (no intergroup difference, P=0.552):
- Stage 1: 18.0% (n=31)
- Stage 2: 60.7% (n=108)
- Stage 3: 21.3% (n=38)

**Location** (no intergroup difference, P=0.261):
- Mandible: 60.1% (posterior mandible 49.5% of all sites)
- Maxilla: 34.8%
- Both jaws: 5.1%
- Single-site involvement: 88.7%; Multiple sites: 11.3%

**Treatment**:
- Conservative (antibiotics, chlorhexidine gargling, minimal bone removal): 18.0% (n=32)
- Surgical (sequestrectomy ± curettage): 82.0% (n=146)
- Mean number of surgeries: G1 1.17±0.50; G2 1.23±0.52; G3 1.45±0.52 (P=0.231, NS)

**Treatment outcomes**:
| Metric | Overall | G1 (Prolia) | G2 (BP→Prolia) | G3 (Xgeva) | P |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Treatment success | 81.5% | 85.0% | 82.8% | 53.8% | **0.027** |
| Complete healing | 77.5% | 80.0% | 79.0% | 53.8% | 0.102 (NS) |
| Partial healing | 3.9% | 5.0% | 3.8% | 0% | — |
| No healing | 5.6% | 3.3% | 4.8% | 23.1% | — |
| Recurrence | 12.9% | 11.7% | 12.4% | 23.1% | — |

- Surgical vs conservative complete healing: 78.8% vs 71.9% (P=0.657, NS) — no significant difference

**Logistic regression** (predicting treatment success):
| Variable | OR | 95% CI | P |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dmab dosage | **5.13** | **1.19–22.14** | **0.028** |
| Age | 1.02 | 0.96–1.07 | 0.564 |
| Duration of Dmab | 1.00 | 0.98–1.03 | 0.761 |
| Location | 1.21 | 0.59–2.49 | 0.612 |
| MRONJ stage | 1.51 | 0.79–2.87 | 0.212 |
| Treatment modality | 1.02 | 0.34–3.05 | 0.976 |

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single institution (KNUDH), Daegu — referral bias likely; complex or advanced cases may be over-represented relative to community care
- G3 (Xgeva) n=13 is small — underpowered for subgroup analyses; the wide CI on OR (1.19–22.14) reflects this uncertainty
- Retrospective design; no randomization; treatment modality not randomized (selection bias possible)
- Minimum 3-month follow-up may undercount late recurrences
- Drug holiday status and rebound hypercalcemia after Dmab cessation not systematically analyzed
- Authors note this study's findings "need to be considered for treatment of DRONJ" but do not provide specific protocol recommendations

## 6. Related Work

- **Jung 2022 (JKAOMS)**: Same institution, Prolia 60mg, n=98 — this study extends with larger n and 3-group design
- **Yokoo 2025 (Cancers)**: Xgeva 120mg breast cancer MRONJ, n=324 — provides high-dose context; this study adds osteoporosis-population comparison
- **Pautke et al. 2022**: DRONJ n=87 (after excluding BP-ONJ from 132 total), surgical success 91.5% — comparable success rate to this study's surgical outcomes
- **Hoefert et al.**: Complete healing 80% with major surgery in MRONJ — consistent with this study's surgical results
- **de Oliveira et al.**: Osteoporosis/osteopenia most frequent primary disease (47%) — this study shows even higher proportion (92.7%) in Korean context
- **2022 AAOMS Position Paper (Ruggiero)**: Staging criteria and treatment philosophy used in this study
- **Marx 2003**: First ONJ report (pamidronate/zoledronate) — referenced as historical landmark
- **Taylor et al.**: First reported DRONJ cases — referenced for nomenclature history

## 7. Glossary

- **DRONJ**: Denosumab-Related Osteonecrosis of the Jaw (specific subset of MRONJ caused by denosumab)
- **MRONJ**: Medication-Related Osteonecrosis of the Jaw (2014 AAOMS term replacing BRONJ)
- **BRONJ**: Bisphosphonate-Related Osteonecrosis of the Jaw (pre-2014 term)
- **Prolia**: Denosumab 60 mg/6 months subcutaneous injection — osteoporosis/osteopenia indication (low-dose)
- **Xgeva**: Denosumab 120 mg/4 weeks subcutaneous injection — cancer bone metastasis/giant cell tumor indication (high-dose)
- **RANK/RANKL**: Receptor Activator of Nuclear Factor kappa B / its Ligand — osteoclast differentiation pathway; denosumab targets RANKL
- **Treatment success**: Complete + partial healing at ≥3 months post-treatment
- **BP→Dmab switch**: Patient previously on bisphosphonate switched to denosumab — cumulative antiresorptive burden concept; explains accelerated DRONJ onset
- **KNUDH**: Kyungpook National University Dental Hospital, Daegu, Korea
