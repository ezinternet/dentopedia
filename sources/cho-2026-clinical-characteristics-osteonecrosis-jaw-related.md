## Why Ingested

직전 세션의 MRONJ 임상 Q&A에서 "Prolia vs Xgeva MRONJ 치료 예후 차이"를 Jung 2022(n=98 Prolia-only)와 Yokoo 2025(n=324 Xgeva-only 암환자)로만 간접 비교해야 했음. Cho 2026은 같은 기관(KNUDH)에서 Prolia(G1), BP→Prolia 전환(G2), Xgeva(G3)를 직접 비교(n=178)하여 치료 성공률 차이를 통계적으로 확인한 최초의 3군 비교 한국 코호트. [[drug/mronj/jung-2022-denosumab-mronj-osteoporosis-5year]]를 직접 확장하고 [[drug/mronj/yokoo-2025-denosumab-mronj-breast-cancer-bone]]의 고용량 DRONJ 예후불량 소견을 골다공증 맥락에서 재확인.

## One-line Summary

Single-institution 5-year retrospective cohort (n=178 DRONJ patients, KNUDH) showing Xgeva 120mg had significantly worse treatment success (53.8%) vs Prolia 60mg (85.0%) or BP-to-Prolia switch (82.8%), with Dmab dose as the only significant predictor (OR 5.13).

## 한줄요약

경북대학교치과병원 5년 후향적 코호트(n=178 DRONJ) — Xgeva 치료 성공률 53.8% vs Prolia 85.0%(p=0.027), 고용량 Dmab만이 치료 실패의 유일한 독립 예측인자(OR 5.13), BP→Prolia 전환군은 발생 시점 앞당기되 예후 차이 없음.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First Korean 3-group comparison (Prolia vs BP→Prolia switch vs Xgeva) in DRONJ patients
- Quantifies treatment success difference by dose: high-dose Dmab (Xgeva) = 53.8% vs low-dose (Prolia) = 85.0% — OR 5.13 for low-dose success
- BP→Dmab switch: accelerates DRONJ onset (16.0 vs 23.2 months after starting Dmab) but does NOT worsen severity or prognosis — cumulative antiresorptive duration explains early onset
- Dmab dose is the ONLY significant predictor in multivariate analysis; MRONJ stage, age, treatment duration, location are NOT significant
- Peri-implantitis as DRONJ trigger in 12.4% — clinically important, third most common after extraction and periodontal disease

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Retrospective cohort, single institution
- **Period**: August 2019 – August 2024 (5 years)
- **n**: 178 (193 initially eligible; 15 excluded for refusal/loss to follow-up)
- **Groups**:
  - G1: Low-dose Dmab (Prolia 60 mg/6mo, osteoporosis), n=60
  - G2: BP→low-dose Dmab transition, n=105
  - G3: High-dose Dmab (Xgeva 120 mg/4wk, oncology), n=13
- **Staging**: 2022 AAOMS criteria
- **Minimum follow-up**: 3 months post-treatment
- **Outcomes**: Complete healing / partial healing / no healing / recurrence. Treatment success = complete + partial
- **Statistics**: ANOVA + Tukey HSD; Fisher's exact/chi-square; multiple logistic regression (SPSS v29.0)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Demographics**:
- Mean age: G1 77.4±6.7yr; G2 76.6±7.2yr; G3 63.5±8.9yr (P<0.001, cancer patients younger)
- Female: 86.0%; Osteoporosis: 92.7%

**Dmab duration before MRONJ onset**:
- G1: 23.2±15.3 months; G2: 16.0±11.3 months (P<0.001); G3: 27.9±18.8 months
- Prior BP duration in G2: 56.7±46.4 months before Dmab switch

**Precipitating factors**:
- Tooth extraction: 45.5%; Periodontal disease: 14.0%; Peri-implantitis: 12.4%; Denture trauma: 9.6%; Implant surgery: 6.2%; Idiopathic: 9.0%

**Staging**: Stage 1: 18.0% / Stage 2: 60.7% / Stage 3: 21.3% — no intergroup difference (P=0.552)

**Location**: Mandible 60.1% (posterior 49.5%); Maxilla 34.8%; Both 5.1%

**Treatment**: Surgical 81.6%; Conservative 18.0%

**Outcomes**:
| Metric | Overall | G1 (Prolia) | G2 (BP→Prolia) | G3 (Xgeva) | P |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Treatment success | 81.5% | 85.0% | 82.8% | 53.8% | 0.027 |
| Complete healing | 77.5% | — | — | — | — |
| Recurrence | 12.9% | 11.7% | 12.4% | 23.1% | — |

- Surgical vs conservative complete healing: 78.8% vs 71.9% (P=0.657, NS)

**Logistic regression** (predicting treatment success):
- Dmab dosage: OR 5.13 (95% CI 1.19-22.14; P=0.028) — low-dose significantly better
- Age, treatment duration, lesion location, MRONJ stage: NOT significant

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single institution (KNUDH), Daegu — referral bias (complex cases may be over-represented)
- Small G3 (Xgeva) n=13 — underpowered for subgroup analyses; wide CI on OR reflects this
- Retrospective; no randomization; treatment modality selection bias possible
- Minimum 3-month follow-up may undercount late recurrences
- Drug holiday status not systematically analyzed

## 7. Glossary

- **DRONJ**: Denosumab-Related Osteonecrosis of the Jaw (subset of MRONJ)
- **Prolia**: Denosumab 60 mg/6 months (osteoporosis indication)
- **Xgeva**: Denosumab 120 mg/4 weeks (cancer bone metastasis indication)
- **Treatment success**: Complete + partial healing at ≥3 months post-treatment
- **BP→Dmab switch**: Patient previously on bisphosphonate, switched to denosumab — cumulative antiresorptive burden concept
