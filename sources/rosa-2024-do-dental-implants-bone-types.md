## One-line Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis of 49 studies (29,905 implants) confirms that implants in bone type IV fail significantly more than in types I, II, and III, with type I and II showing equivalent survival rates and both superior to type III.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis (PRISMA / PROSPERO CRD42021229775)
- **Institution**: UNESP – Univ Estadual Paulista, Araçatuba, Brazil
- **Journal**: The Saudi Dental Journal, Vol. 36, pp. 428–442
- **Year**: 2024 (accepted Dec 2023)
- **Databases searched**: PubMed/MEDLINE, Scopus, Cochrane (through July 2021)
- **Studies included**: 49 (from 9,429 screened)
- **Patients**: 12,056 (mean age 41.56 years)
- **Implants**: 29,905 total

## 2. Key Contributions
- Confirms bone type IV significantly lowers implant survival vs. types I, II, and III using meta-analysis (not just narrative review)
- Establishes statistical equivalence of type I and type II bone for implant outcomes
- Adds ISQ (primary stability) data by bone type, linking bone quality to measurable stability values
- Highlights role of surface treatment in partially compensating for poor bone quality

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Classification used**: Lekholm-Zarb (1985/1995)
  - Type I: Homogeneous compact bone (highest density)
  - Type II: High-density spongy bone + thick cortical bone
  - Type III: High-density spongy bone + thin cortical bone
  - Type IV: Low-density spongy bone + thin cortical bone (worst)
- **PICO**: Do implants in type IV bone have lower success than in types I, II, III?
- **Inclusion**: RCTs + prospective/retrospective studies ≥10 patients, ≥1 year follow-up, Lekholm-Zarb classification used
- **Exclusion**: Radiotherapy/chemotherapy patients, bisphosphonate users, osteoporosis, patients <18y, studies mixing bone type classifications
- **Statistical methods**: Mantel-Haenszel and Inverse Variance; fixed or random effects depending on heterogeneity; hazard ratio (RR) with p < 0.05 significance threshold
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane tool for RCTs; Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS) for non-randomized studies (>6 stars = low risk)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Meta-analysis — Implant Failure Rate (RR vs. Type IV)
| Comparison | RR | 95% CI | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Type I vs. Type IV | 1.92 | 1.52–2.43 | <0.00001 |
| Type II vs. Type IV | 1.89 | 1.59–2.24 | <0.00001 |
| Type III vs. Type IV | 1.50 | 1.27–1.76 | <0.00001 |
| Type I vs. Type II | Not significant | — | NS |

### Implant counts by bone type
| Bone type | Implants placed |
|---|---|
| Type I | 2,570 |
| Type II | 12,434 |
| Type III | 12,240 |
| Type IV | 2,661 |

### ISQ (primary stability) values by bone type (from included studies)
| Bone type | ISQ range |
|---|---|
| Type I | 72–80 |
| Type II | 72–75 |
| Type III | 70–75 |
| Type IV | 58–65 |

- Follow-up range across studies: 1–19 years
- Surface-treated implants (SLA, oxidized) outperform machined implants especially in type IV bone
- Type IV bone predominantly found in posterior maxilla (sinus pneumatization + resorption)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Search limited to July 2021; newer technologies (e.g., short implants, bone condensation) not fully captured
- Heterogeneity in implant systems, surface treatments, and follow-up duration across 49 studies
- Bone type was often clinically assessed (tactile/visual), not always CT-based — measurement variability
- Only one RCT included; most evidence from prospective/retrospective studies
- Future: standardized CT-based bone classification and larger RCTs in type IV bone

## 6. Related Work
- Goiato et al. (2014) — earlier systematic review on bone type IV, lower survival (no meta-analysis); current paper extends this
- Jaffin & Berman (1991) — landmark study: 35% failure in type IV vs. 3% in types I–III
- Lekholm & Zarb (1985/1995) — original bone quality classification used throughout
- Andersson et al. (2019) — ISQ values by bone type, 5-year follow-up
- Sennerby et al. (2012) — primary stability by bone type using Osstell

## 7. Glossary
- **Lekholm-Zarb classification**: 4-type bone quality grading (I–IV) based on cortical/trabecular ratio and density
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: Resonance frequency analysis metric (0–100); higher = more stable. Measured with Osstell device
- **Osseointegration**: Direct bone-to-implant contact without fibrous tissue interface
- **Primary stability**: Mechanical stability at time of implant insertion; prerequisite for successful osseointegration
- **RR (Risk Ratio / Relative Risk)**: Here used as failure rate ratio; RR > 1 means type I/II/III fails less than type IV
- **SLA surface**: Sandblasted large-grit, acid-etched implant surface; improves osseointegration especially in soft bone
- **PROSPERO**: International registry for systematic review protocols
