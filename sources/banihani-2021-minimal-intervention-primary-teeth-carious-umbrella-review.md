## Why Ingested

Sealant 감시 인제스트 — 최소개입치의학(Minimal Intervention Dentistry, MID) 우산리뷰에서 fissure sealant(FS)/resin infiltration의 위치와 적응 한계를 확인하기 위함. 이 umbrella review는 SDF·Hall Technique·ART·선택적 우식제거가 유치 상아질 우식의 main MID 옵션이며, **fissure sealant과 resin infiltration은 유치 상아질 우식 관리에 권장되지 않는다**고 명시적으로 결론짓는다(효능 근거가 법랑질/상아질 외측 1/3 병소에 치우쳐 제외). 즉 sealant의 적응증이 cavitated dentine lesion에는 미치지 못한다는 경계를 그어주는 surveillance 근거. 기존 [[caries/garcia-miralles-2026-sdf-cavitated-primary-molars-sr]](유치 와동성 우식 SDF SR)를 우산 레벨에서 보강하고, [[caries/urquhart-2019-nonrestorative-treatments-caries-network-meta-analysis]](비복원 우식치료 NMA — SDF·sealant 비교)와 짝을 이룬다.

## One-line Summary

Umbrella review of 18 systematic reviews (95 studies) on minimal intervention dentistry (MID) for dentinal caries in primary teeth: 38% SDF, Hall Technique, selective caries removal, and single-surface ART arrest dentinal caries vs no treatment/conventional restoration — while fissure sealants and resin infiltration are explicitly NOT recommended for dentinal lesions.

## 한줄요약

유치 상아질 우식 최소개입치료(MID) 우산리뷰(SR 18편·연구 95편): 38% SDF·Hall Technique·선택적 우식제거·단면 ART는 무처치/통상복원 대비 상아질 우식을 정지시키나, fissure sealant과 resin infiltration은 상아질 병소 관리에 권장되지 않는다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Umbrella review (review of systematic reviews); Cochrane methodology v6; PROSPERO CRD42020202434
- **Journal**: European Archives of Paediatric Dentistry, 2021
- **Scope**: Primary teeth, dentinal carious lesions (ICDAS 4 & 5), MID without local anaesthetic, no pre-existing restorations
- **Evidence base**: 18 SRs (5 without meta-analysis, 13 with), reporting 95 underlying studies; included RCTs span 1969–2018
- **SR quality (ROBIS)**: 10 high risk of bias, 6 low (Ricketts, Innes, Dorri, Tedesco, Trieu, Tolba), 2 unclear (Gao, Tedesco 2018)

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Maps the MID landscape for primary-tooth dentinal caries** into: no-removal (FS, resin infiltration, 38% SDF, Hall Technique), non-restorative cavity control (NRCC), and selective removal (stepwise excavation, ART).
2. **Sealant verdict (the surveillance point)**: fissure sealants and resin infiltration are NOT recommended for managing *dentinal* caries in primary teeth — their efficacy SRs were excluded because pooled data mixed enamel and outer-third-dentine lesions, with most lesions limited to enamel. Sealants belong to *enamel* lesion control, not cavitated dentine.
3. Quantifies the four endorsed MID options (SDF, HT, selective removal, single-surface ART) with arrest/success rates and risk-of-pulp-exposure reductions.
4. Frames MID as a **mainstream option, not a compromise** for cooperative/cost-limited paediatric care.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Databases: MEDLINE, Embase, Cochrane CDSR, Epistemonikos, JBI, NIHR Journals Library; PROSPERO for forthcoming SRs.
- Search concepts: primary teeth AND caries AND minimal intervention dentistry AND systematic review/meta-analysis. English, Jan 2000–Aug 2020.
- Selection: 252 records → 177 after dedup → 181 eligible → 19 full text → 18 SRs included.
- Two independent reviewers (screening + extraction); ROBIS for SR risk of bias; overlap via Corrected Covered Area (CCA: 0–5 slight … >15 very high) — weak overlap found for SDF and ART clusters.
- SRs analysed by technique (SDF, HT, selective removal, stepwise, ART) separately due to differing methods.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Sealants / resin infiltration (verbatim verdict)**: *"both fissure sealants and resin infiltration are not recommended for the management of dentinal caries lesions in primary teeth"* — SRs on their sealing efficacy were excluded because *"pooled data reporting on caries arrest in both enamel and outer third of dentine with the majority of these carious lesions being limited to enamel."*

**38% SDF (8 SRs, 33 RCT/CCT)**:
- Significant caries arrest (p<0.05); more effective than 5% NaF varnish and GIC sealing.
- Single application 31–79%; biannual 53–91%. Mean arrested surfaces 2.5–4.5 (SDF) vs 1.3–1.8 (comparators).
- 38% SDF > 12% SDF (p<0.001). Meta-analyses: 81% overall arrest (Gao, p<0.001); OR 2.44–3.63 favouring SDF (Trieu).
- Comparator effectiveness: 5% NaF varnish 41%, GIC 82%, no treatment 15–34%.
- Adverse: black staining; one study 0.6% reversible white mucosal lesions. No SDF-vs-conventional-restoration trials.

**Hall Technique (2 SRs / 3 overlapping RCTs)**:
- Restoration failure 3% (HT) vs 37% (control); no difference in pulpal signs/symptoms.
- 77% children / 83% carers preferred HT; 89% HT children "no pain" vs 78% control.
- PMCs reduce major failure/pain, especially via HT; more gingival bleeding but inconclusive (RR 1.09, CI 0.42–2.86).

**Selective caries removal (1 SR / 3 RCTs)**:
- Stepwise excavation: 69% pulp-exposure risk reduction (RR 0.31, 95% CI 0.17–0.57, p=0.0002).
- One-step selective removal: 77% pulp-exposure risk reduction (RR 0.24, 95% CI 0.06–0.90, p=0.03).

**ART (8 SRs / 56 trials)**:
- Single-surface success: 95–100% (1y), 91–96.7% (2y), 86% (3y).
- Multi-surface significantly lower (p<0.0001): 73–100% (1y), 52–93% (2y), 48.7–88% (3y).
- Mean annual failure 17% (multi) vs 4.7–5% (single) over 3y. 3 SRs: ART ≈ amalgam/composite; 1 SR (Dorri): ART may increase failure (OR 1.60, CI 1.13–2.27).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Authors did not re-analyse primary studies; conclusions inherit the accuracy of the included SRs.
- Substantial heterogeneity (subjects, lesion depth, materials, outcomes); most underlying studies at considerable risk of bias.
- Outcomes largely clinical, limited post-operative radiographic data — may underestimate minor/major failures.
- HT and selective-removal evidence thin (single/overlapping SRs); ongoing PROSPERO HT reviews flagged.
- Sealant/resin-infiltration evidence not directly synthesised here (excluded by design) — their dentinal-lesion role is asserted absent, not quantified against the other options.

## 7. Glossary

- **MID (Minimal Intervention Dentistry)**: biological caries-control paradigm preserving maximal tooth structure; detect early, remineralise, minimally invasive operative only when needed, repair > replace.
- **SDF (Silver Diamine Fluoride, 38%)**: topical caries-arresting agent; black staining is the main drawback.
- **Hall Technique (HT)**: sealing a carious primary molar under a preformed metal crown (PMC) with no caries removal or local anaesthetic.
- **ART (Atraumatic Restorative Treatment)**: hand-instrument selective caries removal + HVGIC restoration, no rotary/LA.
- **NRCC (Non-Restorative Cavity Control)**: making a cavitated lesion cleansable and arresting it without restoration.
- **Selective / stepwise caries removal**: leaving soft/firm dentine over the pulp to avoid exposure (one-step or two-visit).
- **ICDAS 4 & 5**: dentinal carious lesions (4 = underlying dark shadow; 5 = distinct cavity with visible dentine).
- **ROBIS**: Risk Of Bias In Systematic reviews tool.
- **CCA (Corrected Covered Area)**: metric of study overlap across the SRs in an umbrella review.
