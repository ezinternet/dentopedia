## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional evaluation (rubric-based; 334 typodont preparations) of senior dental students' tooth preparations for CAD/CAM restorations finding finish line quality the most frequent error (136/223 prostheses), the criterion most critical for well-fitting CAD/CAM restorations.

## 한줄요약

단면연구(루브릭 기반, 타이포돈트 형성치 334개): 본과 4학년 학생이 CAD/CAM 수복용으로 형성한 치아에서 **finish line(변연선) 품질 오류가 가장 빈번(223건 중 136건)**했으며, 이는 CAD/CAM 수복물 적합도에 가장 결정적인 항목이다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Assessment of Teeth Prepared by Senior Dental Students for CAD/CAM Restorations
- **Authors**: Ramtin Sadid-Zadeh, Joseph T. DeLuca, Hema Arany, Rui Li
- **Journal**: Journal of Dental Education 2020;84(3):358-366 (first published 16 March 2020)
- **DOI**: 10.21815/JDE.019.178
- **Type**: Cross-sectional rubric-based assessment (dental education)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Develops and applies a rubric scoring tooth preparations on 4 factors: (1) retention/resistance form; (2) marginal/internal adaptation or manufacturing process; (3) biology/esthetics/durability of restorative material; (4) intraoral scanner impression.
- Quantifies the most common preparation error type in a predoctoral CAD/CAM context.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Academic year 2018–19; four calibrated faculty members applied the rubric.
- Sample: 111 first maxillary right premolars (tooth #4) prepared for lithium disilicate crowns; 223 specimens of second maxillary right premolar (#5) and first maxillary right molar (#3) prepared for monolithic zirconia partial fixed dental prostheses.
- Preparations performed on typodont teeth.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Finish line quality was the most common error: 136 errors across 223 prostheses** — the criterion deemed crucial for quality CAD/CAM restorations.
- Conclusion: to prevent poor-fitting CAD/CAM restorations driven by unacceptable finish line quality, students must understand the rationale of detailed rubrics and adhere to defined criteria.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Typodont (not patient) preparations; single-institution senior cohort.
- Rubric newly developed; broader validation across programs needed.

## 6. Related Work

- Connects to the preparation-factor → IOS accuracy literature: finish line is repeatedly identified as the dominant accuracy/fit driver.

## 7. Glossary

- **CAD/CAM**: Computer-aided design / computer-aided manufacturing.
- **Finish line**: Cervical margin of a tooth preparation.
- **Rubric**: Structured criterion-based scoring tool for assessment.
- **Lithium disilicate / monolithic zirconia**: Common CAD/CAM restorative ceramics.
