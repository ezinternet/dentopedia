## One-line Summary

Abstract-only SR+MA (10 studies, 711 cracked teeth, normal pulp/reversible pulpitis): external splinting yields 85% pooled 12-month pulp survival (95% CI 79–91%), higher without interim orthodontic banding (89% vs 79%) and with a direct vs stepwise approach (89% vs 81%); very low GRADE certainty.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA isolating **external splinting** as a management line for cracked teeth with vital pulp.
- Quantifies a 12-month pulp survival benchmark (85%) with protocol subgroup effects.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Search of 6 databases to Jan 31 2025; permanent mature cracked teeth (normal pulp or reversible pulpitis) with ≥12-mo follow-up. Newcastle-Ottawa risk of bias; GRADE QoE; random-effects pooling. (Abstract-level detail only.)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- 10 studies, 711 cracked teeth.
- Pooled 12-mo pulp survival **85% (95% CI 79–91%)**, I²=82.6%.
- Without interim orthodontic banding **89% (83–95%)** vs with banding **79% (67–90%)**.
- Direct approach **89% (82–97%)** vs stepwise **81% (72–90%)**.
- Risk of bias moderate; QoE very low; no publication bias.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only: forest plots, individual study weights, and per-tooth detail not extractable.
- Considerable heterogeneity (I²=82.6%), protocol variation, very low certainty → rigorous comparative studies needed.

## 7. Glossary

- **External splinting**: bonding/banding a cracked tooth to stabilize the crack and preserve pulp vitality.
- **Pulp survival rate**: proportion retaining vital pulp at follow-up.
- **Stepwise vs direct approach**: phased vs immediate definitive management.
