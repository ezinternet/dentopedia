## Why Ingested

상악동 임플란트 합병증 중 자연공 (natural ostium) 폐쇄 및 만성 부비동염 발생에 대한 ENT 전문의 관점의 프로토콜이 필요했음. 기존 [[wiki/sinus-lift/lateral/akbari-2022-maxillary-sinus-mucosal-thickening-ridge-height]]가 점막두께와 잔존골 높이의 관계를 다루지만, 골이식편 탈락에 의한 자연공 차단 및 수술 전·후 비내시경 평가와 FESS 적응증을 포함한 통합적 ENT-치과 공동 관리 프로토콜은 이 논문이 최초로 제시함. ENT 의뢰 기준 수립의 근거 문헌으로 활용.

## One-line Summary

Narrative review from otolaryngologists at Seoul St. Mary's Hospital outlining preoperative maxillary sinus assessment, sinus pathology classification, and ENT-dental joint management protocols to reduce post-implant sinusitis (~5% incidence).

## 한줄요약

서울성모병원 이비인후과 전문의 관점에서 상악 임플란트 전 부비동 평가·병리 분류·FESS 적응증을 정리한 서술 리뷰로, 골이식편 탈락에 의한 자연공 차단이 핵심 합병증 기전임을 강조.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Provides a structured CT-based evaluation and patient management protocol (Fig. 3) for maxillary sinus pathologies prior to implant placement — from the ENT specialist's viewpoint.
2. Identifies ostium plugging by a dislodged bone graft as a central mechanism for post-implant sinusitis.
3. Defines size thresholds for cysts/polyps (< 1/3–1/2 sinus height = acceptable; > 1/2 = refer for FESS before implant).
4. Clarifies acute vs chronic rhinosinusitis criteria (12-week threshold) and antibiotic selection (amoxicillin/amoxicillin-clavulanate first line).
5. Describes indications for FESS and endoscopic implant retrieval for dislocated implants.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Narrative review with illustrative CT images and endoscopic photographs. No systematic search or meta-analysis. Decision flowchart (Fig. 3) synthesizes CT findings into three management arms: (1) proceed with implant, (2) medical/surgical treatment first, (3) further workup (MRI/biopsy) for suspected malignancy.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Pathology | CT Threshold | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Mucosal thickening (no ostium block) | < 1/3–1/2 sinus height | Implant OK; repeat CT after medication if generalized |
| Mucosal thickening (blocks ostium) | Any | Treat before implant |
| Cyst/polyp (not near ostium) | 10–15 mm | Implant OK |
| Cyst/polyp (large, near ostium) | > 1/2 sinus height | FESS before implant |
| Acute rhinosinusitis | — | Resolve before implant |
| Chronic rhinosinusitis | ≥ 12 weeks symptoms | Antibiotics ± FESS |
| Fungal sinusitis | Hyperdense calcifications on CT | FESS; resolves ~1 month |
| Paranasal malignancy | Bone/tissue destruction | Stage and treat; no implant |

- Post-implant chronic sinusitis rate: **~5%**
- Incidental CT findings: mucosal thickening 23.7–28.2%, cyst/polyp 8.9–19.4%, sinusitis 3.6–6.5%
- Acute bacterial rhinosinusitis develops in only 0.5–2% of viral rhinosinusitis cases
- Antibiotic: amoxicillin or amoxicillin-clavulanate 5–10 days; high-dose (2 g BID) in high-resistance areas
- FESS: gold standard for restoring maxillary sinus clearance; also used for endoscopic implant retrieval through widened ostium (vs Caldwell-Luc for large dislocated implants)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative format; no quantitative evidence synthesis
- Size thresholds (1/3–1/2 sinus height) are expert-based, lacking strong clinical evidence — authors explicitly call for more clinical data
- No direct comparison of outcomes with vs without preoperative ENT clearance
- Does not address transcrestal approach–specific considerations

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| 상악동 자연공 (natural ostium) | Natural drainage opening of the maxillary sinus into the middle meatus; blockage leads to sinusitis |
| 상악동점막두께 (maxillary sinus mucosal thickening, MSMT) | Mucosal wall thickening visible on CT; threshold for intervention is 1/3–1/2 of sinus height |
| Ostiomeatal complex (OMC) | Common drainage channel connecting frontal sinus, anterior ethmoid, and maxillary sinus to the middle meatus |
| FESS (Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery) | Minimally invasive endoscopic procedure to restore sinus ventilation and drainage; gold standard for drug-resistant sinusitis |
| Schneiderian membrane | Periosteal lining of the maxillary sinus elevated during sinus lift procedures |
| Caldwell-Luc procedure | Open surgical approach to the maxillary sinus through the canine fossa; used for large dislocated implants inaccessible via ostium |
| Fungal ball | Non-invasive fungal sinusitis; appears as hyperdense calcification on CT; resolves with FESS |
| Odontogenic sinusitis | Sinusitis of dental origin; associated with unilateral disease |
