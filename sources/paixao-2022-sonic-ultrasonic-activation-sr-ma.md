## One-line Summary
Systematic review + meta-analysis of sonic vs. ultrasonic irrigant activation (in vitro): sonic is superior for apical smear layer removal; ultrasonic is superior for push-out bond strength at middle and apical thirds.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Acta Odontologica Scandinavica 80(8):588–595, 2022
- **DOI**: 10.1080/00016357.2022.2061591
- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (in vitro studies only)
- **PRISMA 2020 compliant**

## 2. Key Contributions
- First SR+MA directly comparing sonic vs. ultrasonic activation across smear layer removal and push-out bond strength outcomes
- Sonic activation superior for **apical smear layer removal** (SMD −0.48, 95% CI −0.93 to −0.02, p = .03)
- Ultrasonic activation superior for **push-out bond strength**: middle third (SMD −0.69), apical third (SMD −0.78)
- No significant difference for coronal/middle smear layer removal or coronal push-out bond strength

## 3. Methodology
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane, Embase, Web of Science (inception–2021)
- **Inclusion**: In vitro studies comparing sonic vs. ultrasonic irrigant activation; human single-canal teeth; final irrigant NaOCl/EDTA sequence; SEM-based smear layer scoring
- **Outcomes**: Smear layer removal (by canal third), push-out bond strength (by canal third)
- **Risk of bias**: Modified Cochrane RoB tool adapted for in vitro studies
- **Statistical**: Random-effects model; SMD with 95% CI

## 4. Key Results
| Outcome | Comparison | SMD | 95% CI | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Apical smear layer | Sonic > Ultrasonic | −0.48 | −0.93 to −0.02 | .03 |
| Middle smear layer | NS | — | — | NS |
| Coronal smear layer | NS | — | — | NS |
| Middle push-out | Ultrasonic > Sonic | −0.69 | (sig.) | <.05 |
| Apical push-out | Ultrasonic > Sonic | −0.78 | (sig.) | <.05 |
| Coronal push-out | NS | — | — | NS |

## 5. Limitations
- All included studies are in vitro; clinical translation uncertain
- Heterogeneity in activation devices (EndoActivator for sonic, various UIPs for ultrasonic)
- SEM smear layer scoring is semi-quantitative and operator-dependent
- Push-out bond strength measured with sealer/obturation materials, not isolated irrigation effect

## 7. Glossary
- **Sonic activation**: Low-frequency vibration (~1–6 kHz), e.g., EndoActivator; tip oscillates laterally
- **Ultrasonic activation (PUI)**: High-frequency vibration (25–40 kHz); passive ultrasonic irrigation
- **SMD**: Standardized mean difference (meta-analysis effect size)
- **Push-out bond strength**: Force required to displace sealer/gutta-percha from dentinal wall; indirect measure of sealer adhesion after irrigation
