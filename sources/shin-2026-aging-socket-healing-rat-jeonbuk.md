## One-line Summary

Animal study (rat, n=17, 3 age groups) showing that aging (18-month-old rats) significantly impairs alveolar socket healing — reduced bone volume/trabecular thickness, empty sockets with inflammatory infiltrate — compared to younger animals, emphasizing age-specific considerations in dental rehabilitation.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies the age-dependent impairment of alveolar bone healing after tooth extraction using three age groups (2M, 10M, 18M Sprague-Dawley rats)
- Demonstrates that 18-month-old (senescent) rats show significantly reduced bone volume and trabecular thickness at 2 weeks post-extraction
- Identifies an age-differential post-extraction TRAP5b (tartrate-resistant acid phosphatase 5b) response indicating altered osteoclastic activity
- Shows peak bone regenerative capacity at 10 months (middle-aged), not youth, as evidenced by maximal ALP expression filling the socket
- Highlights inflammatory cell accumulation in aged sockets as a mechanism of impaired healing

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Model**: Male Sprague-Dawley rats, mandibular tooth extraction
- **Groups**: 2M (n=6, young), 10M (n=6, middle-aged), 18M (n=5, senescent)
- **Timepoint**: 2 weeks post-extraction
- **Analyses**:
  - Micro-CT (bone volume, trabecular thickness)
  - Serum bone turnover markers (osteocalcin, CTX-I, ALP, TRAP5b)
  - Histology
  - Immunohistochemistry

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | 2M (young) | 10M (middle-aged) | 18M (senescent) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bone volume | High | Highest/favorable | Significantly reduced |
| Trabecular thickness | High | Favorable | Significantly reduced |
| ALP (socket filling) | High | Maximal | Compromised |
| Socket appearance | Regenerating | Best healing | Empty + inflammatory cells |
| Baseline osteocalcin/CTX-I | Highest metabolic activity | Intermediate | Lowest |
| TRAP5b response | Distinct post-extraction peak | Intermediate | Blunted/altered |

- 18M group: compromised healing characterized by empty sockets and inflammatory cell accumulation
- 10M group: most favorable bone regeneration with maximal alkaline phosphatase expression
- Younger rats (2M): highest baseline metabolic activity but 10M shows best socket healing response

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only data; specific numeric values not available
- Single timepoint (2 weeks) — longer-term healing trajectory not assessed
- Male rats only — sex differences in aging effects not addressed
- n=5 for 18M group (slightly smaller)
- Translation to human clinical aging requires further study
- Intervention strategies (grafting, biologics) for aged sockets not tested

## 6. Related Work

- Aging effects on alveolar bone biology and ridge preservation
- Cross-link with geriatric dentistry and implant planning in elderly patients
- TRAP5b and osteocalcin as bone turnover markers in extraction socket studies

## 7. Glossary

- **TRAP5b** (tartrate-resistant acid phosphatase 5b): Serum marker of osteoclast activity; used to assess bone resorption
- **Osteocalcin**: Serum marker of osteoblast activity / bone formation
- **CTX-I** (C-terminal telopeptide of type I collagen): Bone resorption marker
- **ALP** (alkaline phosphatase): Bone formation marker expressed by osteoblasts
- **Micro-CT**: Micro-computed tomography; quantifies 3D bone microarchitecture (BV/TV, Tb.Th)
- **Trabecular thickness (Tb.Th)**: Measure of bone trabecular strut width; reduced in aging/osteoporosis
