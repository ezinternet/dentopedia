## Why Ingested

Existing wiki pages on implant-supported overdentures (e.g., [[complete-denture/prasad-2024-attachment-systems-mandibular-implant]] and [[complete-denture/bi-2022-attachment-type-implant-number-overdenture]]) focus primarily on mandibular two-implant designs. This SR+MA (Lan 2025) provides medium-to-long-term comparative data specifically for four-implant maxillary IODs vs IFRs — filling a maxillary arch gap and extending the evidence base for full-arch implant rehabilitation decisions.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 16 studies (5,568 implants, ≥5y follow-up) comparing four-implant maxillary overdentures vs fixed complete dentures: IFRs showed higher implant (98.5%) and prosthesis (99.9%) survival but greater marginal bone loss (−1.20 mm), while IODs had less MBL (−0.27 mm) but more complications and lower prosthesis survival (85.0%).

## 한줄요약

16개 연구(임플란트 5,568개, 최소 5년 추적)를 메타분석한 결과, 상악 4-임플란트 고정성 완전의치(IFR)는 임플란트 생존율(98.5%)과 보철물 생존율(99.9%) 모두 피개의치(IOD, 94.5%/85.0%)보다 우수하였으나, 변연골 흡수는 IFR(−1.20 mm)이 IOD(−0.27 mm)보다 컸다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First SR+MA to comprehensively compare four-implant-supported maxillary IODs vs IFRs with ≥5-year follow-up across implant survival, prosthesis survival, marginal bone loss, and complications.
2. Subgroup analyses by attachment system (for IODs) and study design type provide clinically actionable stratification.
3. Quantifies the complication burden difference: IODs accumulate mechanical complications (retention loss 0.34/patient), while IFRs suffer acrylic tooth fracture/dislodgment (0.09/patient).
4. Demonstrates that despite heterogeneous baselines across studies, both IOD and IFR have predictable medium-term clinical outcomes.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases searched**: PubMed, Embase, Cochrane, plus manual search
- **Inclusion criteria**:
  - ≥10 maxillary edentulous patients
  - Four-implant-supported overdenture (IOD) or fixed complete denture (IFR)
  - Minimum 5-year follow-up
- **Studies included**: 16 total (9 on IODs, 7 on IFRs), representing 5,568 implants
- **Primary outcome**: Implant survival rate (ISR)
- **Secondary outcomes**: Prosthesis survival rate, marginal bone loss (MBL), complications
- **Risk of bias**: RoB 2 tool (for RCTs) and Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS) (for observational studies)
- **Statistical method**: Weighted pooled rates with 95% CIs; heterogeneity assessed by I²
- **Subgroup analyses**: Attachment system type and study design for the IOD subgroup

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Implant Survival Rate (ISR)
| Prosthesis Type | Weighted ISR | 95% CI | I² |
|---|---|---|---|
| IOD (overdenture) | 94.5% | 92.1%–96.9% | 84.22% |
| IFR (fixed) | 98.5% | 97.4%–99.5% | 77.88% |

### Prosthesis Survival Rate
| Type | Rate |
|---|---|
| IOD | 85.0% |
| IFR | 99.9% |

### Marginal Bone Loss at 5 Years
| Type | MBL (mean ± SD) |
|---|---|
| IOD | −0.27 ± 1.31 mm |
| IFR | −1.20 ± 0.76 mm |

### Most Common Complications
- **IOD**: Retention loss (0.34 per patient)
- **IFR**: Acrylic tooth dislodgment/fracture (0.09 per patient)

### Key Interpretations
- IFRs demonstrated superior implant and prosthesis survival despite greater MBL
- IODs preserved peri-implant bone better but at the cost of more mechanical complications and lower prosthesis survival
- High heterogeneity (I² >77%) across both groups reflects variability in patient selection, implant systems, and follow-up periods
- Both treatment modalities achieve "predictable medium-term clinical results"

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- High heterogeneity in both groups (I² >77%) limits precision of pooled estimates
- Abstract-only data available for this review (full text behind paywall); exact heterogeneity sources and subgroup breakdown not fully extractable
- Variability in baseline characteristics (bone quality, patient demographics, implant systems) across included studies
- "Medium-term" outcomes (≥5 years) — true long-term (≥10 year) data on four-implant maxillary prostheses remain sparse
- Future RCTs should standardize reporting of complications and prosthesis survival definitions
- Direct head-to-head RCTs comparing IOD vs IFR in matched populations are needed

## 7. Glossary

- **IOD (Implant-Overdenture / Implant-supported Overdenture)**: Removable prosthesis retained by implants via attachment systems (ball, locator, bar, magnet)
- **IFR (Implant-supported Fixed Restoration / Fixed Complete Denture)**: Non-removable implant-supported full-arch fixed prosthesis (All-on-4 style)
- **ISR (Implant Survival Rate)**: Proportion of implants surviving (in situ, functional) at follow-up
- **MBL (Marginal Bone Loss)**: Radiographic crestal bone loss around implants at follow-up
- **RoB 2**: Cochrane risk-of-bias tool version 2 (for RCTs)
- **NOS (Newcastle-Ottawa Scale)**: Quality assessment scale for observational cohort/case-control studies
- **I²**: Statistical measure of heterogeneity between studies in meta-analysis (>75% = high heterogeneity)
- **Retention loss**: Failure of overdenture attachment mechanism to maintain prosthesis retention
