## One-line Summary
Systematic review on exercise therapy after surgical/interventional treatment of TMJ internal derangement (악관절 내장증); finds postoperative exercise significantly improves MMO and reduces pain, supporting structured rehabilitation protocols.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Oral Rehabilitation 2024;51(10):2158–2168
- **DOI**: 10.1111/joor.13780
- **Study type**: Systematic review

## 2. Key Contributions
- First SR focused on exercise therapy specifically in the postoperative/post-interventional period for TMJ internal derangement (악관절 내장증)
- Evaluates exercise types: passive mobilization, active range-of-motion, resistance training, jaw-opening devices
- Outcome focus: MMO recovery, pain reduction, disc recapture outcomes
- Provides rehabilitation protocol guidance for clinical practice

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Search databases: PubMed, Embase, Cochrane, Web of Science
- Inclusion: studies evaluating exercise interventions in patients post-surgery (arthrocentesis, arthroscopy, open joint surgery) or post-splint therapy for TMJ internal derangement
- Primary outcomes: MMO (mm), pain (VAS/NRS)
- Secondary: disc position (MRI), patient satisfaction, jaw function
- PRISMA-compliant search and reporting
- Quality assessment using appropriate tools for study designs included

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Postoperative exercise therapy significantly improved MMO compared to surgery alone
- Pain reduction enhanced with structured exercise rehabilitation
- Early initiation of exercise (within 1 week post-procedure) generally favored
- Passive jaw mobilization devices (TheraBite, Dynasplint) showed consistent benefit for MMO
- Exercises should be individualized to procedure type and patient capacity
- Most studies limited to 3–6 month follow-up

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Heterogeneous surgical procedures and exercise protocols limit direct comparison
- Most included studies non-randomized (case series, prospective cohort)
- Lack of standardized exercise protocols and outcome measurement timing
- Blinding not feasible for exercise interventions
- Long-term outcomes (>1 year) inadequately reported

## 7. Glossary
- **TMJ internal derangement** (악관절 내장증): Abnormal positional relationship of the disc relative to the condyle/fossa; includes disc displacement with and without reduction
- **Arthroscopy** (관절경): Minimally invasive joint surgery using optical fiber; intermediate between arthrocentesis and open surgery
- **MMO** (하악 최대개구량): Maximum mouth opening; primary functional outcome in TMJ rehabilitation
- **TheraBite**: Commercial jaw mobilization device for postoperative jaw opening exercises
- **Disc recapture**: Restoration of normal disc position on condyle; goal of TMJ internal derangement treatment
