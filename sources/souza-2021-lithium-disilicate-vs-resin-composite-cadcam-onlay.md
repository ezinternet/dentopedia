## 2. Key Contributions
- Both materials excellent at baseline and 1 year with no failures
- No statistically significant difference in overall clinical performance (p>0.05)
- e.max CAD: surface lustre deterioration (p=0.020), color match/translucency change (p=0.039)
- SEM: no significant micromorphological differences at 1 year
- FDI criteria evaluated by two blind observers

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Split-mouth RCT (n=20 patients) comparing IPS e.max CAD (lithium disilicate) vs Lava Ultimate (resin composite) CAD/CAM onlays at 1 year. FDI criteria assessed by two blind observers; SEM evaluation of marginal interface. Both materials showed excellent results at baseline and 1-year with similar esthetic, functional, and biological properties (p>0.05). However, e.max showed deterioration in surface lustre (p=0.020) and color match/translucency (p=0.039) at 1 year. No failures in either group.
