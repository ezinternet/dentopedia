## Why Ingested

기존 [[immediate-implant/esthetic-soft-tissue/donker-2025-immediate-implant-provisionalization-digital-workflow-prospective]]가 디지털 워크플로우 기반 즉시 프로비저널라이제이션의 전향적 근거를 제공하는데, 본 retrospective cohort (Nechita 2026)는 거기에 더해 **무회전방지(non-antirotational) 5° Morse taper 인터페이스**라는 특이한 변형을 다룬다 — 가이드로 미리 제작한 프로비저널 크라운을 회전 적응 가능하게 두어 식립 위치 편차를 보상한다는 개념. 이로써 emergence profile shaping 프로비저널라이제이션의 연조직·골 결과 근거를 확장한다.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective single-arm cohort (n=20 single esthetic-zone implants) of a digitally planned, guide-delivered immediate provisionalization protocol on non-antirotational 5° Morse taper implants: provisional retention without major intervention 75%, 1-year MBL 0.37 mm, buccal contour gain 1.41 mm, complete papilla preserved in 70%.

## 한줄요약

심미부위 단일 임플란트 20개에 대한 후향 단일군 코호트 — 디지털 계획·가이드 전달의 즉시 프로비저널라이제이션(무회전방지 5° Morse taper) 결과: 주요 개입 없는 프로비저널 유지율 75%, 1년 변연골소실 0.37 mm, 협측 윤곽 증가 1.41 mm, 완전 유두 보존 70%.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Evaluates a distinctive workflow variant: **prefabricated, guide-delivered provisional crown screwed onto a non-antirotational (indexless) 5° Morse taper implant–abutment interface** in the esthetic zone. The non-antirotational design is proposed to allow intraoperative rotational adaptation of the crown so the planned emergence profile/facial contour can be optimally aligned despite minor guided-placement deviations (the standard antirotational index would lock a single — possibly suboptimal — rotation).
- First focused clinical evidence on this indexless conical-connection immediate provisionalization concept, distinct from conventional antirotational single-crown protocols.
- Reports retention, biologic/technical complications, papilla preservation, marginal bone change, and buccal-contour gain at one year of follow-up after definitive crown delivery.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Sample**: 20 consecutive single-implant esthetic-zone cases (one implant per patient) treated from January 2024, with ≥1 year follow-up after definitive crown by the February 2026 data-lock. 19 female, 1 male; mean age 38.1 ± 12.7 y (range 25–67). 18 anterior + 2 premolar sites. No a priori sample-size calculation (sample = eligible consecutive patients).
- **Cohort characteristics**: smoking negative 70%; periodontal history gingivitis 65% / chronic periodontitis 30% / unremarkable 5%; no bruxism; thin phenotype 60% / mixed 20% / thick 20%. Healed sockets 65% (13), immediate (post-extraction) placement 35% (7). Flapless 95% (19), single minimal flap 5%. Grafting: soft-tissue alone 50% (10), combined soft+hard 40% (8), none 10% (2).
- **Implants/protocol**: all placed with primary insertion torque ≥30 N·cm (mean 34.75 ± 2.55; 35 N·cm in 75%, 30 N·cm in 15%, 40 N·cm in 10%). Diameters 3.5 mm (55%) / 4.0 mm (45%); length 10–13 mm (mean 11.8 ± 1.0). All immediately restored with a digitally designed, screw-retained composite **non-antirotational** provisional crown on a 5° Morse taper connection. Contour management framed by critical/subcritical contour and Esthetic Biological Contour concepts.
- **Timepoints**: T0 = surgery (placement + provisional delivery); T1 = 1–2 wk (clinical eval, occlusal adjust); T2 = tissue-maturation visit + digital impression for definitive crown; T3 = definitive crown delivery; T4 = 12 mo after definitive crown.
- **Outcomes**: primary = provisional retention/survival without major intervention. Secondary = biologic complications, papilla score, marginal bone change (OPT/panoramic, ImageJ) at T0–T3 and T3–T4, buccal contour change (T0 vs T2 intraoral-scan superimposition in Exocad).
- **Statistics**: Wilson 95% CIs, Fisher's exact / Freeman–Halton, Mann–Whitney U, Wilcoxon signed-rank, Spearman; α = 0.05. Subgroup/association analyses interpreted as descriptive/hypothesis-generating only.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Implant survival**: 100% (20/20 integrated, no implant loss); definitive crown delivered in 100%.
- **Primary outcome — provisional retention without major intervention**: 75.0% (15/20; Wilson 95% CI 53.1–88.8%). No remake, no conversion to antirotational titanium base, no alternative provisional needed.
- **Provisional dislodgement**: 25.0% (5/20; 95% CI 11.2–46.9%) — all managed chairside by re-screwing or re-cementation, with no damage to the implant–abutment connection, no loss of soft-tissue conditioning, no compromise of the definitive restoration.
- **Biologic/technical complications (each 5.0%, 1/20)**: bleeding on probing, suppuration, midfacial mucosal recession (1 mm, managed with soft-tissue grafting), and chairside occlusal adjustment at delivery.
- **Papilla**: complete papilla (score 3) preserved in 70.0% (14/20; 95% CI 48.1–85.5%); partial fill (score 2) in 30% (6/20).
- **Marginal bone loss (panoramic)**: early T0–T3 0.24 ± 0.20 mm; late T3–T4 0.14 ± 0.03 mm; **cumulative 12-mo total 0.37 ± 0.20 mm** (median 0.33; range 0.14–0.96). Total <0.5 mm in 80% (16/20), <1.0 mm in 100% (20/20); no case >1.0 mm. Most remodeling occurred in early healing/conditioning phase (Wilcoxon W=53.5, p=0.054).
- **Buccal contour change (T0→T2 IOS superimposition)**: positive (gain) in all 20; **mean +1.41 ± 0.48 mm** (median 1.44; range 0.68–2.29). Mild <1.0 mm 25%, moderate 1.0–1.9 mm 60%, pronounced ≥2.0 mm 15%; no negative change. Authors caution this is the combined effect of the whole protocol (provisional design + grafting + socket healing + phenotype + guided placement), not the provisional alone.
- **Unplanned visits during maturation**: 0–4 (mean 1.0 ± 1.4; median 0); 60% had none, 40% ≥1 (mostly re-screwing).
- **Exploratory associations with dislodgement**: no significant categorical predictors (smoking, periodontal history, phenotype p=0.092, socket status, grafting, occlusal scheme, tooth position, flapless, systemic, insertion-torque category). Unplanned visits higher in dislodged cases (U=61.5, p=0.019, but mechanistic). No significant difference in papilla, age, torque, bone loss, or contour gain. Spearman: total bone loss not correlated with torque (ρ=−0.16), contour gain (ρ=−0.33), or age.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Retrospective, single-arm, single-operator/single-center, **no comparator** (no antirotational/conventional control) — cannot attribute outcomes to the absence of the antirotational index nor claim superiority.
- Very small sample (n=20), no a priori power; subgroup/association analyses are descriptive/hypothesis-generating only.
- Heavily female cohort (95%), narrow case selection; most sites grafted, so buccal-contour gain confounded by the full protocol.
- Marginal bone measured on **panoramic radiographs (OPT)**, not periapical/CBCT — lower precision; the 25% provisional dislodgement rate is non-trivial for an indexless screw-retained design.
- Future: prospective comparative (indexed vs indexless) trials, longer follow-up, CBCT-based bone assessment, larger and more balanced samples.

## 7. Glossary

- **Non-antirotational (indexless) connection**: implant–abutment interface lacking an anti-rotation feature, permitting free rotational seating of the restoration.
- **Morse taper (conical) connection**: friction-fit conical implant–abutment interface (here 5°) associated with reduced microgap and good torque maintenance.
- **Emergence profile**: transmucosal contour of the restoration shaping the peri-implant soft tissue; managed via critical (gingival margin/zenith) vs subcritical (tissue support/space) contour.
- **Papilla score**: ordinal interproximal papilla fill rating (here 3 = complete, 2 = partial).
- **Buccal/facial contour change**: change in facial mucosal profile measured by superimposing baseline vs post-conditioning intraoral scans; positive = volume gain.
