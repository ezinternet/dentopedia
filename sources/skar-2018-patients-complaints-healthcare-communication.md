## Why Ingested
Grounds the expectations axis in the relational content of complaints: what patients actually report about encounters and communication. Complements the quantitative Friele surveys and the [[complaint-management/bmjqs-2013-002437]] relationship domain with concrete encounter-level themes.

## One-line Summary
Qualitative content analysis of patient complaints finding that poor communication, not being listened to, and disrespect dominate dissatisfying encounters.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Centres the *encounter* and communication as the locus of complaint.
- Identifies themes: insufficient information, not being listened to/believed, and lack of respect.
- Reinforces that relational failures (not only clinical error) generate complaints.
- Links to person-centred care: meaningful relationship = individualised attention + involvement in decisions.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Qualitative content analysis of written patient complaints concerning healthcare encounters and communication submitted to a Swedish patients' advisory committee.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
Complaints clustered around communication failures -- patients not receiving adequate information, not feeling listened to or believed, and experiencing a lack of respect and individualised attention. Time pressure in encounters was a recurring obstacle to a caring relationship.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
Qualitative, single regional source; not generalisable in frequency terms. Nursing/encounter focus; transfer to dentistry interpretive. No outcome data.

## 6. Related Work
Empirically illustrates the relationship domain of Reader et al (2014) and the communication/listening categories of HCAT.

## 7. Glossary
- **Healthcare encounter**: a personal meeting between patient and professional, planned or unplanned.
- **Person-centred care**: care integrating 'being with' the patient and individualised attention.
