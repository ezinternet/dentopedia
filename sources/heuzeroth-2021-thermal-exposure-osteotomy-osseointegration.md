## One-line Summary
Minipig in vivo study (COIR 2021): novel test drills with optimized design and surface treatment, used in a shortened drill sequence, produced significantly lower peak osteotomy temperatures (35.4°C vs 46.5°C) and significantly higher BIC at 2 and 8 weeks compared to control drills.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Clinical Oral Implants Research, 2021;32(6):672–683
- Type: Preclinical in vivo (minipig mandible) experimental study
- 36 tapered implants, 4 groups, guided drilling
- Histomorphometric outcomes at 2 and 8 weeks
- First published online: 25 Feb 2021

## 2. Key Contributions
- Directly links osteotomy thermal profile to histomorphometric osseointegration (BIC, fBIC).
- Shows that an optimized drill design with shortened sequence can outperform standard drills with conservative sequence.
- Quantifies temperature differences (~11°C) translating to measurable BIC differences (+13.6% at 2 weeks).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Model: 36 tapered implants in mandibular minipig (Göttingen) bone.
- Groups:
  1. Control drills + conservative sequential drilling (CS)
  2. Control drills + shortened sequence ("PF", protocol followed)
  3. Novel test drill (optimized design + surface treatment) + PF
  4. Aged test drill + PF
- Intraosseous temperature measured with temperature probe during drilling.
- Histomorphometry at 2 and 8 weeks: BIC (bone-to-implant contact), fBIC (first BIC), tissue reactions.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
| Outcome | Test (3) | Control (2) | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Peak temperature (°C, 95%CI) | 35.4 (30.2–40.5) | 46.5 (41.0–52.0) | 0.0021 |
| Drill time (sec, 95%CI) | 4.5 (1.6–7.3) | 10.3 (7.3–13.4) | — |
| BIC at 2 weeks (%) | 44.9 (34.1–55.7) | 31.3 (20.5–42.2) | <0.0001 |
| BIC at 8 weeks (%) | 73.7 (64.2–83.2) | 66.2 (57.0–75.4) | 0.0455 |

- Control group reached the 47°C thermal necrosis threshold (CI upper bound 52°C).
- Test drill stayed below necrosis threshold throughout drilling.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Single animal model (minipig) — translation to human bone densities not directly validated.
- Sample size per group small (n=9).
- Aged-drill arm specifics for absolute number of prior uses limited.
- Healing period maximum 8 weeks — no long-term loading data.
- Sponsorship: industry-affiliated authors (Pippenger affiliated with implant manufacturer).

## 6. Related Work
- Branemark, Albrektsson foundational osseointegration literature.
- Eriksson & Albrektsson — 47°C/1 min thermal necrosis threshold.
- Augustin et al. — cortical bone drilling and thermal osteonecrosis (Clin Biomech 2012).
- Multiple in vitro thermal studies on drill geometry.

## 7. Glossary
- **BIC**: 골-임플란트 접촉률 (Bone-to-Implant Contact) — percent of implant surface in direct contact with bone, measured histomorphometrically.
- **fBIC**: first Bone-to-Implant Contact — distance from implant shoulder to most coronal BIC.
- **PF (Protocol Followed)**: in this paper, the shortened drill sequence recommended for the test drills.
- **Histomorphometry**: quantitative measurement of tissue structure from histologic sections.
