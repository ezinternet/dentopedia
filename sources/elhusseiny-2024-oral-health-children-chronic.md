## One-line Summary

Narrative review (12 included studies) of oral manifestations and dental management in pediatric CKD, hemodialysis, and renal transplantation — paradoxically LOW caries (high salivary urea/buffering) but HIGH enamel defects (31-83%), gingival/periodontal disease, calculus, xerostomia, and drug-induced gingival overgrowth; key management levers are dialysis-day-after timing, bleeding-risk control, fluoride contraindication, and the 6-month post-transplant elective-care moratorium.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Synthesizes the **paradoxical caries profile** of pediatric CKD: lower caries / more caries-free children despite high-carbohydrate (protein-sparing) diets, driven by elevated salivary urea (split to ammonia + CO₂, raising pH above the demineralization threshold), high salivary buffering capacity, antibacterial urea effects, and reduced cariogenic flora (S. mutans, lactobacilli).
2. Catalogues the **high-burden oral manifestations**: developmental enamel defects/hypoplasia (incidence 31-83%; from hypocalcemia, low 1,25-(OH)₂-vitamin-D, high phosphate/PTH/fluoride during mineralization), gingival/periodontal disease, heavy calculus (salivary urea/pH/phosphorus precipitating Ca-phosphate/oxalate), delayed eruption, xerostomia, uremic odor/metallic taste, and radiographic changes (loss of lamina dura, ground-glass bone, hypercementosis).
3. Provides a **dental-management protocol** spanning pretreatment (bleeding-risk assessment, drug-dose modification, antibiotic prophylaxis, amide LA, corticosteroid cover) and treatment-phase rules.
4. Gives **hemodialysis-specific timing/hemostasis guidance**: treat on the **day after dialysis** (heparin effect lasts only 3-5 h, intravascular volume optimal); layered hemostasis (topical thrombin, sutures, oxidized cellulose, desmopressin, tranexamic acid rinse); HIV/HBV/HCV screening; 3-6-month recall.
5. Gives **renal-transplant rules**: pre-transplant infection-focus elimination; **avoid elective care in the first 6 months post-transplant**; drug-induced gingival overgrowth (cyclosporine A, nifedipine) managed by cyclosporine→tacrolimus substitution + hygiene; mTOR-inhibitor (sirolimus/everolimus) stomatitis; HSV/candidiasis prophylaxis; elevated oral-cancer (Kaposi, SCC) surveillance.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: narrative review with a structured, PRISMA-flow-documented selection process (2 independent reviewers, GAE & WS).
- **Databases**: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, Google Scholar.
- **Selection funnel**: 54 records → 18 duplicates removed → 36 screened → 22 eligibility-assessed → 10 excluded → **12 included**.
- **Inclusion**: pediatric CKD/hemodialysis/renal-transplant oral manifestations; peer-reviewed; English. **Exclusion**: adult-only, case reports/series, off-topic.
- **Data extraction**: independent dual extraction of study characteristics + categorized oral manifestations; narrative synthesis, no meta-analysis.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Caries**: Al-Nowaiser — caries-free 40% (CRF) vs 8.5% (controls); salivary urea 11.6 vs 3.6 mmol/L; buffering pH 6.4 vs 5.6. Subramaniam — DMFT 0.5. Tuma — caries experience 42.5%, DMFT 0.85.
- **Enamel defects**: 31% (Koch) to 83% diffuse opacities (Nunn); 57-58% enamel defects (Al-Nowaiser, Subramaniam); localized primary-canine hypoplasia 19% vs 3% healthy.
- **Gingival/periodontal**: gingival bleeding 95.8% (Tuma); gingival index 1.88 vs 0.92 (Tadakamadla); periodontal pockets 70.3% vs controls; gingivitis tied to anemia (p=.0002) and dialysis duration (p=.007) (Nakhjavani).
- **Gingival overgrowth**: 13-22 children across studies; CsA-induced GO positively associated with total cyclosporine dose in first 6 post-transplant months (Wondimu); drug-induced GO 16.7% (Tuma).
- **Transplant infections**: oral HSV 0-11.3%; oral candidiasis 4-43%.
- **Hemostasis/timing**: treat day after dialysis; tranexamic acid 10-15 mg/kg/day in 2-3 doses; heparin effect 3-5 h.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Pediatric scope** — explicitly authors' stated limitation: few studies met inclusion in pediatric CKD/HD/transplant populations, limiting breadth and generalizability; adult extrapolation must be cautious.
- **Narrative, not meta-analytic** — no pooled effect estimates; heterogeneous study designs (cross-sectional, mixed age ranges, some studies span 4-70 y despite "pediatric" framing — e.g., Tadakamadla 16-70 y).
- **Selection bias** acknowledged; included studies may not represent population/setting diversity.
- Several cited prevalence ranges are wide (caries-free 8.5-40%, enamel defects 31-83%), reflecting heterogeneous classification systems and small samples.

## 7. Glossary

- **CKD** — Chronic Kidney Disease; gradual loss of renal function.
- **CRF** — Chronic Renal Failure (older term used in several cited studies).
- **DDE** — Developmental Defects of Enamel; quality/quantity alterations from disrupted enamel organ.
- **Renal osteodystrophy** — bone disorder from CKD-related mineral/hyperparathyroid disturbance; drives radiographic bone changes.
- **Gingival overgrowth (GO)** — drug-induced gingival enlargement (cyclosporine A, nifedipine, calcium-channel blockers).
- **mTOR inhibitors** — sirolimus, everolimus; cause transient oral stomatitis/ulceration.
- **Calcineurin inhibitors** — cyclosporine, tacrolimus; transplant immunosuppressants.
- **Sialagogue** — agent stimulating salivary flow (pilocarpine, cevimeline).
- **OHI-S** — Oral Hygiene Index-Simplified.
- **DMFT/dmft** — Decayed-Missing-Filled Teeth index (permanent/primary).
