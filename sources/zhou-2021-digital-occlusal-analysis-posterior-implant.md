## Why Ingested

Occlusal force evolution after single posterior implant crown delivery is poorly quantified; this prospective 6-month T-Scan pilot provides one of the few longitudinal datasets directly measuring how implant prosthesis bite force changes across time points. Reinforces and contextualizes [[wiki/occlusion/gozen-2025-occlusal-force-distribution-digital-analysis-implant-in-vivo]], which similarly uses digital occlusal analysis for implant force evaluation.

## One-line Summary

Prospective pilot (n=32 restorations, 30 patients) using T-Scan III at 5 time points found that single posterior implant prosthesis occlusal force increased ~2× from 2 weeks to 3 months post-placement, then stabilized, improving masticatory ability while maintaining light-contact guidelines.

## 한줄요약

후방 단일 임플란트 보철 32개를 T-Scan III로 5회 측정한 전향적 파일럿 연구에서, 임플란트 교합력은 장착 2주~3개월 사이 약 2배 증가 후 안정화되었으며, 즉시 교합 회복으로 저작 능력이 개선됨.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Longitudinal T-Scan III data at 5 time points (pre-placement, immediately, 2 weeks, 3 months, 6 months) quantifying occlusal force evolution after single posterior implant crown delivery.
2. Demonstrated that implant prosthesis bite force approximately doubles from 2 weeks (3.39%) to 3 months (6.90%) then plateaus to 6 months (7.31%).
3. Showed that posterior segment bite force significantly improved on both unilateral (+3.31%) and bilateral (+6.83%) defect restorations.
4. Identified that bilateral defect patients showed increased bite force discrepancy between sides and a 15.38% higher proportion of Level III bilateral deviation.
5. Confirmed that articulating paper + shimstock light-contact clinical standard does not prevent progressive occlusal force increase on implants over 6 months.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Participants**: 30 patients (18 women, 12 men; age 27–75 years); 32 single posterior implant restorations
  - Unilateral single-tooth defect: n=17
  - Bilateral teeth defects (one implant per side or sequential): n=15
- **Measurement instrument**: T-Scan Sensor III (Tekscan, South Boston, USA)
- **Measurement position**: Maximum intercuspation position (MIP), 3 recordings averaged per visit
- **5 measurement stages**:
  1. Pre-crown delivery (baseline)
  2. Immediately following crown placement
  3. 2-week follow-up (after final torque)
  4. 3-month follow-up
  5. 6-month follow-up
- **Occlusal parameters recorded**: Bite force % per tooth, per 6 segments (left/right anterior, left/right posterior, left/right half), center of occlusal force (COF) trajectory
- **Occlusal adjustment**: Performed at baseline and 2-week visits; NOT performed at 3- and 6-month visits before measurement (real-world evolution)
- **Clinical standard for implant occlusion**: Light contact at heavy bite; no contact at light bite; shim-stock foil passes more easily than on adjacent teeth; canine/anterior guidance in eccentric excursions
- **Sample size calculation**: Based on expected 2-unit bite force increase, σ=3, α=0.05, power=0.90 → n≥24
- **Statistics**: SPSSAU v20.0; Wilcoxon signed-rank test for within-group comparisons; Mann–Whitney U for between-group; Kruskal–Wallis for multi-group; Spearman correlation

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Implant prosthesis occlusal force over time:**
- Baseline (pre-delivery): significantly lower than control natural teeth (P=.000)
- Immediately post-delivery: significantly lower than mesial + distal adjacent teeth
- 3 months: no significant difference from mesial teeth; still lower than distal teeth
- 6 months: significantly lower than distal teeth (P=.000)
- **Critical change**: Implant bite force increased significantly from 2 weeks (3.39 ± 2.61%) to 3 months (6.90 ± 4.77%), mean increase ×2.04 (P=.008)
- From 3 months to 6 months: no significant change (6.90 → 7.31%; P=.900)

**Posterior segment bite force:**
- Unilateral defect: posterior segment bite force improved by +3.31% after restoration (P=.013)
- Bilateral defect: posterior segment bite force improved by +6.83% (P=.001)
- Bilateral defect: bite force discrepancy between sides increased from 3.52% to 5.02% (P=.039); proportion of Level III bilateral deviation increased by 15.38% (P>.05)

**Influencing factors:**
- Sex, implant diameter/length, region, crown material, and opposing tooth type did not significantly affect bite force changes
- Patients with bilateral defects showed greater asymmetry over time despite individual side improvement

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Pilot study design; small sample size (n=30/32)
- Short follow-up (6 months) — does bite force continue to rise beyond 6 months?
- No control group of natural tooth restorations for direct comparison
- T-Scan sensor film (100 µm) may slightly alter occlusion during measurement; reliability depends on proper calibration
- Occlusal adjustment was performed at baseline and 2-week visits but not at 3 and 6 months — this reflects clinical reality but means force changes include both biological adaptation and possible clinical drift
- COF trajectory analysis mentioned but not deeply reported in results
- Did not assess patient-reported outcomes (satisfaction, masticatory performance)
- Future: longer follow-up (1–5 years), larger multicenter RCT, comparison with fixed partial dentures

## 7. Glossary

- **T-Scan**: Digital occlusal analysis system recording bite force distribution and timing at MIP; displays force as % per tooth/segment
- **MIP (Maximum Intercuspation Position)**: Position of maximum cusp-fossa contact between maxillary and mandibular teeth
- **IPO (Implant-Protected Occlusion)**: Occlusal scheme minimizing biomechanical stress on implants via light contacts and no lateral excursive contacts
- **COF (Center of Occlusal Force)**: Dynamic centroid of occlusal force distribution trajectory during chewing cycle
- **FTU (Functional Tooth Unit)**: Two opposing teeth in occlusal contact; the smallest masticatory unit
- **Level III bilateral bite force deviation**: A severity classification of bite force asymmetry between left and right sides in the T-Scan system
- **Shimstock foil (8 µm)**: Thin metallic foil used to assess occlusal contact tightness; passes more easily than adjacent teeth through implant prosthesis gap = "light contact" confirmed
