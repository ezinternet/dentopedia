## One-line Summary
Systematic review + meta-analysis of 10 RCTs (n = 182 patients) comparing autogenous particulated dentin (APD) against blood clot healing (BCH) or other grafted materials (OGM) for alveolar ridge preservation (ARP); APD better maintained horizontal ridge width (MD 1.61 mm vs BCH; 1.28 mm vs OGM) and labial bone height (MD 1.75 mm vs BCH), with higher vital bone area (MD +10.51%) and lower residual graft area (MD -8.76%) versus OGM.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First meta-analysis to quantitatively pool RCT evidence for **autogenous particulated dentin (APD)** as an ARP graft.
- Provides parallel comparisons against **two clinically relevant controls**: spontaneous blood clot healing (BCH) and other grafted materials (OGM, including DBBM and β-TCP).
- Reports both **clinical (radiologic ridge dimensions)** and **histomorphometric (vital bone, residual graft, connective tissue)** outcomes.
- Establishes APD as biologically active (comparable or superior vital bone formation) with favorable resorption kinetics relative to xenograft.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- PICOS: systemically healthy patients undergoing ARP after extraction; intervention APD; comparator BCH or OGM; primary outcomes = Δ horizontal ridge width, Δ labial bone height, histomorphometry; design = RCT only.
- APD preparation: extracted tooth → removal of enamel/cementum/restorations → grinding → cleaning/disinfection/(partial)demineralization.
- 238 records screened; after deduplication and screening, 10 RCTs (182 participants) included.
- Two parallel meta-analyses (Group I: APD vs BCH; Group II: APD vs OGM); subgroup by reentry time and OGM type.
- Outcomes pooled as mean differences (MD) with 95% CIs in Review Manager 5.4; heterogeneity by I².

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Horizontal ridge width preservation**:
  - vs BCH: MD = **1.61 mm** (95% CI 0.76–2.46) favoring APD.
  - vs OGM: MD = **1.28 mm** (95% CI 1.08–1.48) favoring APD.
- **Labial bone height preservation**:
  - vs BCH: MD = **1.75 mm** (95% CI 0.56–2.94) favoring APD.
  - vs OGM: statistically significant in favor of APD (P < .05).
- **Histomorphometry (vs OGM)**:
  - Vital bone area: MD = **+10.51%** (95% CI 4.70–16.32).
  - Residual graft area: MD = **−8.76%** (95% CI −12.81 to −4.71) — i.e., APD resorbed/integrated more than OGM.
  - vs BCH: no significant difference.
- **Secondary outcomes** (primary ISQ at re-entry, postoperative complications): no statistically significant between-group differences.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small evidence base (10 RCTs, only 182 patients); heterogeneity of APD preparation protocols (demineralization vs not, particle size).
- Variable follow-up duration; few studies with > 6 month re-entry.
- Risk of bias unclear for blinding in several included trials.
- Authors call for larger RCTs with standardized APD protocol, longer follow-up, and head-to-head against DBBM as reference standard.

## 6. Related Work
- Tan 2012 — natural post-extraction ridge resorption (11–22% vertical, 29–63% horizontal at 6 months).
- Couso-Queiruga 2021 — systematic review on post-extraction dimensional changes.
- Bassir 2018 / Mardas 2023 / Troiano 2018 — meta-analyses of ARP with conventional bone substitutes (DBBM, allograft, FDBA).
- Schropp 2003 — landmark on socket dimensional change after single tooth extraction.

## 7. Glossary
- **APD (Autogenous Particulated Dentin)**: ground particles from a patient's own extracted tooth, used as autograft after cleaning ± demineralization.
- **ARP (Alveolar Ridge Preservation)**: post-extraction socket grafting to limit dimensional collapse.
- **BCH (Blood Clot Healing)**: unassisted socket healing without graft (negative control).
- **OGM (Other Grafted Materials)**: any non-APD graft used in the trials (DBBM, β-TCP, FDBA).
- **DBBM (Deproteinized Bovine Bone Mineral)**: xenograft (e.g., Bio-Oss).
- **β-TCP**: beta-tricalcium phosphate alloplast.
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: resonance frequency stability metric at re-entry implant placement.
