## One-line Summary
Doctoral study examining changes in plaque control ability through regular professional oral health care program using multi-visit intervention protocol.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Doctoral dissertation
- **Institution**: Chosun University, Graduate School of Dentistry
- **Year**: 2014 (submitted May 2014)
- **Dissertation Committee**: Lee Byung-jin (advisor), Jang Ki-wan, Kim Dong-ki, Kim Heung-joong, Choi Choong-ho
- **Pages**: 53
- **Research Design**: Prospective intervention study with pre/post measurement
- **Subject**: Impact of structured professional oral health care on patient's personal plaque control capability

## 2. Key Contributions
- Evaluates effectiveness of regular professional oral health care program on biofilm management
- Measures behavioral and clinical changes in patient plaque control ability
- Documents intervention protocol for professional oral hygiene maintenance
- Provides baseline data for preventive dentistry outcomes in Korean population

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Study Design**: Prospective observational study with periodic monitoring
- **Intervention**: Regular professional oral health care program (frequency/duration specified in methodology section)
- **Outcome Measures**: Plaque control index, patient compliance, clinical periodontal parameters
- **Assessment Points**: Baseline, multiple time points during intervention period
- **Control/Comparison**: Pre-intervention vs. post-intervention measurements

## 4. Key Results and Findings
- Professional oral health care program shows measurable improvement in patient plaque control ability
- Regular intervals of professional care reinforce patient motivation and technique
- Biofilm management capability demonstrates progressive improvement with program compliance
- Long-term maintenance of improved oral hygiene levels observed in compliant patients

## 5. Clinical Implications
- Structured professional oral health care programs enhance patient self-care capability
- Regular professional reinforcement is essential for sustained plaque control
- Prevention-focused approach reduces need for restorative/surgical interventions
- Patient education combined with professional care creates synergistic effect

## 6. Limitations and Future Work
- Study population limited to Korean dental care context
- Long-term follow-up data beyond intervention period not detailed
- Individual patient compliance factors may influence outcomes
- Future studies should examine cost-effectiveness and long-term maintenance rates

## 7. Glossary
- **치면세균막 (Dental biofilm/plaque)**: Bacterial community adherent to tooth surface
- **전문구강건강관리 (Professional oral health care)**: Professionally administered preventive services
- **관리능력 (Control ability)**: Patient's capability to maintain adequate oral hygiene
- **주기적 (Periodic)**: Regular, scheduled intervals
