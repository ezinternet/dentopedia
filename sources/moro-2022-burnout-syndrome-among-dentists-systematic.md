## One-line Summary
Pooled prevalence of burnout syndrome (Maslach Burnout Inventory) in dentists: overall **13%**, but the emotional exhaustion (EE) subscale runs **28%**, depersonalization (DP) 18%, reduced personal accomplishment (PA) 10% — the EE component is the dominant driver.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Journal of Evidence-Based Dental Practice, 22(3), Sep 2022, 101724
- DOI: 10.1016/j.jebdp.2022.101724
- Study type: Systematic review + proportion meta-analysis (sr+ma)
- Tool: Maslach Burnout Inventory (MBI) only — gold standard
- Search date: January 27, 2021

## 2. Key Contributions
- Provides the **first** SR+MA of dentist burnout prevalence using MBI only (excludes non-MBI tools, which would inflate heterogeneity)
- Distinguishes prevalence of **full syndrome** (~13%) from prevalence on individual MBI subscales (much higher on EE)
- Reports both proportion prevalence and continuous MBI scores (means)
- Documents very high heterogeneity (I² up to 98%), implying geography/specialty/timing all matter

## 3. Methodology
- Databases: Medline, Scopus, Web of Science, PsycINFO, EMBASE, LILACS + gray literature
- Inclusion: studies using MBI questionnaire and its 3 subscales (EE, DP, PA)
- Population: dentists (any specialty, any country)
- Exposure: burnout syndrome
- Quality: Joanna Briggs Institute (JBI) critical appraisal checklist — **no study met all positive criteria**
- Analysis: Stata 13.0 proportion meta-analysis, random-effects

## 4. Key Results
- Included studies: 37 narrative, 31 meta-analyzed
- **Overall burnout prevalence: 13%** (95% CI 0.6%–21%; I² = 97.07%)
- **Emotional exhaustion (EE) prevalence: 28%** (95% CI 24%–32%; I² = 20.70%) — most consistent and highest
- **Depersonalization (DP) prevalence: 18%** (95% CI 8%–28%; I² = 85.61%)
- **Reduced personal accomplishment (PA) prevalence: 10%** (95% CI 8%–13%; I² = 0%)
- High-level subscale findings:
  - 25% high EE (95% CI 19–31%; I² 92.58%)
  - 18% high DP (95% CI 10–26%; I² 96.62%)
  - 32% low PA (95% CI 20–45%; I² 97.86%)
- Continuous means:
  - EE: mean 17.90 (95% CI 9.36–26.43; I² 94.8%)
  - DP: mean 6.93 (95% CI 3.41–10.45; I² 80.2%)
  - PA: mean 34.69 (95% CI 23.82–45.55; I² 98.8%)

## 5. Limitations
- No study satisfied all JBI quality criteria — risk of bias is non-trivial
- I² consistently >90% — pooled estimates are statistically valid but practically heterogeneous
- Lower CI bound for overall prevalence is 0.6% (very wide) — point estimate of 13% should be read with this in mind
- Cross-sectional designs dominate — no causal direction
- Most data pre-COVID

## 7. Glossary
- **MBI (Maslach Burnout Inventory)**: 22-item, 3-subscale gold standard tool for occupational burnout
- **EE (Emotional Exhaustion)**: feeling drained, lack of enthusiasm — core symptom
- **DP (Depersonalization)**: cynicism, emotional detachment from patients/colleagues
- **PA (Reduced Personal Accomplishment)**: feelings of inefficacy and failure
- **I²**: heterogeneity statistic — >75% generally considered substantial
- **JBI checklist**: Joanna Briggs Institute critical appraisal for prevalence studies
