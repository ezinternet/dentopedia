## Why Ingested

This RCT fills the quantitative gap left by the sparse [[immediate-implant/galve-huertas-2026-inverted-body-shift-vs-conical-rct]] entry (DOI: medoral.27220) and the pilot-only evidence in [[immediate-implant/galve-huertas-2024-inverted-body-shift-design-pilot]]. Full-text retrieval from PMC provides complete ISQ/IT values, CBCT-volumetric bone remodeling data (horizontal at 1/3/5mm, vertical buccal/palatal), and PES aesthetic outcomes for both the Inverta co-axis 12° and conventional conical implant — enabling direct synthesis with ISQ-based loading decision literature.

## One-line Summary

RCT (n=30, 1-year) comparing inverted body-shift (co-axis 12°) vs conventional conical implants in immediate anterior maxillary placement: 100% survival, ISQ 56–62 in both groups, comparable MBL and PES, no statistically significant intergroup differences.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First full-data RCT** with complete CBCT-volumetric bone loss (horizontal at 1/3/5mm; vertical buccal/palatal) and ISQ values for the Inverta co-axis 12° inverted body-shift implant vs a conventional conical implant in IIP.
2. **ISQ loading threshold validation:** ISQ ≥62 used as cut-off for immediate provisionalization; test group achieved slightly higher median ISQ (62 buccal and palatal vs control 56/60) though not statistically significant.
3. **Insertion torque data:** Test group median IT 42.0 Ncm vs control 35.0 Ncm (p=0.074) — trend toward higher primary stability with inverted design.
4. **PES equivalence:** Comparable aesthetic outcomes at 1 year (median PES 12 control vs 11 test, no significant difference).
5. Confirms the inverted body-shift geometry's clinical viability without compromising peri-implant tissue maintenance vs conventional implants.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design:** Single-blind RCT. Computer-generated randomization with sealed opaque envelopes.
- **Population:** 30 ASA-I patients (≥18y) with maxillary anterior teeth requiring extraction (incisors, canines, premolars); Elian type I alveolus; intact buccal plate; adequate bone for primary stability.
- **Groups:**
  - Test (n=15): Inverta co-axis 12° implant (inverted body-shift design; 3.5–4.5mm diameter; body widens at middle third to 4.5mm; 12° angled neck; central socket positioning)
  - Control (n=15): Conventional internal conical implant (slightly palatal positioning)
- **Surgery:** Six postgraduate oral surgery students (operator variability); atraumatic extraction without flap; buccal gap ≥2mm filled with Bio-Oss®; subepithelial connective tissue graft (tuberosity/palate).
- **Provisionalization threshold:** ISQ ≥62 → immediate screw-retained provisional; ISQ <62 → customized healing abutment or Maryland bridge.
- **Bone assessment:** CBCT at T0 (baseline), T1 (immediately post-op), T2 (1 year). Volumetric superposition using LimaGuide software with individual bite fork (4 metal reference points). Horizontal: 1mm/3mm/5mm from implant platform level. Vertical: buccal and palatal bone height.
- **Stability:** RFA via Penguin device; SmartPeg screwed at 5Ncm; measurements in buccal (90°) and palatal directions; highest value recorded.
- **Aesthetics:** Pink Esthetic Score (PES) at 1 year; 7 parameters; 0–14 scale; compared to contralateral reference tooth.
- **Statistics:** Mann-Whitney U, Wilcoxon signed-rank, Student's t-test, Chi-square, Kruskal-Wallis, Brunner-Langer; p<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Baseline
- 30 patients: 11M (36.7%), 19F (63.3%); mean age 53.4±11.8 years
- Groups homogeneous (no significant differences in gender, age, tooth type, implant diameter/length)
- ICC for intra-examiner reliability: 0.91 (95% CI 0.86–0.95)

### Implant Survival and Success
| Outcome | Control | Test | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Survival (1 year) | 100% | 100% | — |
| Success (<2mm MBL) | 93.3% (n=14) | 73.3% (n=11) | 0.327 (ns) |
| Satisfactory survival (2–4mm MBL) | 6.7% (n=1) | 26.7% (n=4) | — |

### Primary Stability
| Measure | Control | Test | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| IT median (Ncm) | 35.0 (IQR 25–40) | 42.0 (IQR 27–55) | 0.074 (ns) |
| ISQ buccal (median) | 56.0 (IQR 50–71) | 62.0 (IQR 60–64) | ns |
| ISQ palatal (median) | 60.0 (IQR 51–71) | 62.0 (IQR 60–66) | ns |
| Immediate provisional rate | 20% | 40% | 0.426 (ns) |

| 5mm | -0.29mm | -0.20mm | p=0.070 | p=0.492 (ns) |

### Vertical Bone Loss (T1→T2)
| Site | Control (median) | Test (median) | Intergroup p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Buccal | -0.38mm | -0.86mm | p=0.124 (ns) |

### Aesthetics (PES at 1 year)
- Control: median 12 (IQR 10–12), mean 11.4; range 7–14
- Test: median 11 (IQR 9–13), mean 11.2; range 9–14
- No significant differences; comparable aesthetic outcomes

### Key Comparisons with Literature
- IT values align with: Brown et al. 38.6±8.1 Ncm (conical), Ma et al. 41.7±10.2 Ncm (tapered), Galve-Huertas 2024 pilot 40.5±15.7 Ncm (inverted)
- ISQ: Chu et al. 65.3±5.7; Brown et al. 64.7→69.5 at 1yr; Ma et al. 65.1→69.9 at 5yr; Galve-Huertas pilot 62.6/61.6 (closely matching current study test group)
- PES: Galve-Huertas pilot 11.5, Östman 12.63–13.0, Chu 12.5 — all consistent with current study

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample (n=30 total, 15/group) limits statistical power
- 1-year follow-up only; no long-term stability data
- Six different postgraduate operators → surgical variability
- Mixed tooth types (incisors, canines, premolars) → anatomical heterogeneity
- CBCT volumetric analysis may overestimate MBL vs periapical radiographs
- No blinding of outcome assessors for all measures
- Future: larger cohorts, longer follow-up, standardized protocols, advanced digital measurement

## 7. Glossary

- **IIP:** Immediate Implant Placement — placement at time of tooth extraction
- **ISQ:** Implant Stability Quotient — dimensionless RFA value (1–100); ≥62 used as immediate loading threshold in this study
- **IT:** Insertion Torque — maximum torque (Ncm) at implant placement; reflects bone-implant mechanical engagement
- **RFA:** Resonance Frequency Analysis — non-invasive stability measurement via vibration frequency of SmartPeg transducer
- **Co-axis 12°:** 12-degree angled platform (Inverta design) allowing central socket positioning with palatal screw access
- **Inverted body-shift:** Implant macro-design conical apically, widest at middle third, narrow coronally — opposite of conventional taper
- **PES:** Pink Esthetic Score — 7-parameter soft tissue esthetic index, scale 0–14
- **CBCT:** Cone-Beam Computed Tomography — used here for volumetric 3D bone change assessment
- **Bio-Oss®:** Deproteinized bovine bone mineral (xenograft) used to fill buccal gap ≥2mm
- **LimaGuide:** Software for CBCT superposition using individual bite fork with 4 metal reference points
- **Buccal gap:** Space between implant surface and buccal socket wall; 2–3mm recommended; filled with xenograft
- **Elian type I:** Socket classification (intact buccal plate, no soft tissue recession)
- **Misch 2008:** Four-category implant health scale: success/satisfactory survival/compromised survival/failure
