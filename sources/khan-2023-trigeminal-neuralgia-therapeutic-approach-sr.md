## Why Ingested

삼차신경통 치과 감시(surveillance) 인제스트 — 위키에 삼차신경통(Trigeminal Neuralgia, TN) 페이지가 부재했다. 안면통증으로 내원한 환자에서 치과의가 알아야 할 약물 사다리(1차 카르바마제핀/옥스카르바제핀 → 2차 라모트리진/바클로펜 → 난치성 BoNT-A/IV lidocaine → 수술 의뢰 MVD)와 ICHD-3 진단 분류를 한 페이지로 잡기 위함. 가장 가까운 기존 페이지는 신경병성 안면통 페이지들이다 — [[oral-medicine/jaaskelainen-2018-burning-mouth-syndrome-neuropathic-pain]] (구강작열감증후군의 신경병성 본태)과 [[oral-medicine/kouri-2024-small-fiber-neuropathy-burning-mouth-syndrome]] — 이 두 페이지가 삼차신경 신경병성 통증 기전을 다루므로, TN 페이지는 같은 삼차신경 신경병증 클러스터를 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

Umbrella/systematic review (11 review articles, 31 pharmacological interventions, PROSPERO CRD42023408948) confirming carbamazepine and oxcarbazepine as first-line drugs for classical trigeminal neuralgia, with lamotrigine/baclofen second-line and botulinum toxin A / IV lidocaine for refractory cases.

## 한줄요약

엄브렐러/체계적 문헌고찰 (리뷰 11편·약물 31종) — 전형 삼차신경통의 1차 약물은 카르바마제핀·옥스카르바제핀, 2차는 라모트리진·바클로펜, 난치성에는 보툴리눔독소 A와 정맥 리도카인, 혈관압박 전형 TN의 1차 수술은 미세혈관감압술(MVD).

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic review to recognize, organize, classify, and summarize the efficacy/safety of TN pharmacotherapy across multiple international review articles.
- Synthesizes 11 review articles covering 31 pharmacological interventions into a single management ladder.
- Frames TN by ICHD-style etiology: **classical** (vascular compression at root entry zone — most common), **secondary** (tumor/posterior fossa vascular abnormality, multiple sclerosis), **idiopathic** (no cause found).
- Names **microvascular decompression (MVD)** as the first-line surgical treatment for classical TN.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PICOS-defined inclusion/exclusion: included reviews and meta-analyses of RCTs of drug therapy for TN; excluded rapid reviews, non-English, lab studies, uncontrolled trials, qualitative studies, case reports/series.
- Two independent reviewers screened titles/abstracts; a third settled disagreements; data extracted with a standardized template (intervention, control, level of evidence, efficacy, adverse effects, conclusions).
- Screening flow: 588 titles → 370 after de-duplication → 127 full texts → **11 review articles** finally included; **31** pharmacological interventions evaluated.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**First-line (classical TN)** — Carbamazepine and oxcarbazepine.
- Daily recommended doses: **carbamazepine 0.4–1.2 g**; **oxcarbazepine 0.9–1.8 g**.
- Oxcarbazepine has fewer adverse effects than carbamazepine and may be first-line for secondary TN (monitor MS patients).
- Common side effects: sleepiness, vertigo, nausea, diplopia, reduced neuromuscular control, cognitive issues (esp. females); elevated transaminases and hyponatremia at higher doses.

**Second-line / refractory** — Lamotrigine and baclofen for non-responders or intolerable carbamazepine side effects.
- **Gabapentin**: meta-analysis of RCTs (n=1331) — efficacy comparable to carbamazepine with considerably fewer side effects; second-line/refractory; superior life-satisfaction index B.
- **Pregabalin**: GABA(B) analog similar to gabapentin; fewer side effects; pregabalin + carbamazepine effective in refractory TN; benefit at 1-year follow-up.
- **Baclofen**: effective for MS-secondary TN (relieves spasticity); levorotatory more effective than racemic; baclofen + carbamazepine significantly superior (low-grade evidence).
- **Eslicarbazepine** (dose 200–1200 mg/day) and **levetiracetam** (~3–4 g/day) — options with insufficient evidence.

**Combination / adjunct** — Carbamazepine can be combined with gabapentin, ropivacaine, tizanidine, or pimozide for better/equivalent results and improved tolerance. Ropivacaine (trigger-zone block) + oral gabapentin was the only combination with high-grade evidence.

**Refractory / acute** —
- **Intravenous lidocaine** for acute exacerbations; lidocaine also used topical (8% oral mucosa), nasal spray, trigger-zone infiltration.
- **Botulinum toxin A (BTX-A)**: intradermal/submucosal injection, **dose 25–75 U**; Hu et al. SR reported response in ~70–100% of patients, pain intensity/frequency reduced ~60–80%, no major adverse events — used as refractory therapy.
- **Sumatriptan** SC 3 mg + oral 100 mg/day improved analgesia (low-grade evidence).

**Ineffective / ruled out** — Proparacaine (0.5% ophthalmic drops), dextromethorphan, and tocainide reported inappropriate for TN.

**Surgical (referral)** — Microvascular decompression (MVD) first-line for classical TN. Novel agents awaited: vixotrigine, basimglurant (mGlu5 NAM), erenumab/Aimovig (anti-CGRP).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Except carbamazepine, most drugs were inadequately studied; few high-level-evidence trials, small samples, heterogeneous outcome measures — limits statistical pooling.
- Umbrella design inherits the low evidence grade of underlying reviews; no new meta-analytic effect estimate is generated.
- Future work: combination therapies and newer formulations (vixotrigine), anti-CGRP, mGlu5 modulators.

## 6. Related Work

- ICHD-3 TN classification (classical / secondary / idiopathic) and 2019 European Academy of Neurology survey on TN diagnosis/treatment.
- Di Stefano 2018 cited as the most comprehensive prior TN pharmacotherapy review.
- Burning mouth syndrome / trigeminal small-fiber neuropathy literature (neighboring trigeminal neuropathic-pain cluster in this wiki).

## 7. Glossary

- **TN (Trigeminal Neuralgia, 삼차신경통)** — paroxysmal, electric-shock-like facial pain in trigeminal distribution; classical / secondary / idiopathic.
- **MVD (Microvascular Decompression, 미세혈관감압술)** — surgical relief of vascular compression at the trigeminal root entry zone; first-line surgery for classical TN.
- **Classical TN** — caused by neurovascular compression at root entry zone (most common).
- **Secondary TN** — caused by tumor, posterior-fossa vascular abnormality, or multiple sclerosis.
- **Idiopathic TN** — no identifiable cause.
- **BTX-A (Botulinum Toxin type A, 보툴리눔 독소 A)** — refractory-TN option, 25–75 U intradermal/submucosal.
- **CGRP (Calcitonin Gene-Related Peptide)** — neuropeptide target of erenumab (Aimovig), trialed in TN.
