## Why Ingested

[[wiki/sinus-lift/transcrestal/felix-2022-subantral-sinus-augmentation-hydraulic-lift-system]]이 hydraulic lift 원리를 기술했으나 hyaluronic acid(HA)를 graft 대체제로 사용한 fluid-dynamic 접근은 없음. Scarano 2025는 HA 단독 사용 + 유체역학적 막거상의 12개월 CBCT 전향적 데이터로 새 카테고리 개방.

## One-line Summary
Prospective (n=58 patients): fluid-dynamic OSFE with 2% cross-linked hyaluronic acid achieves 7.5 mm average bone gain, no failures at 12 months CBCT; wider sinuses gain more bone.

## 한줄요약
전향적(58환자): 2% 교차결합 HA를 이용한 유체역학적 transcrestal SFE — 12개월 CBCT 평균 골이득 7.5mm, 실패 없음; 동공 폭 넓을수록 골이득 큼.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Materials 2025;18:2230
- **DOI**: 10.3390/ma18102230
- **Published**: 12 May 2025
- **Study type**: Prospective cohort
- **n**: 58 patients, 58 implants (simultaneous placement)
- **Device**: Flusilift system (fluid-dynamic membrane elevation device)
- **Graft material**: 2% cross-linked hyaluronic acid only (no granular graft)

## 2. Key Contributions
- HA as sole sinus fill material (non-granular, fluid) in transcrestal SFE — novel approach
- 12-month CBCT objective bone height quantification (mesial + distal measurement)
- ISQ at second-stage surgery (no initial ISQ reported; no ISQ dip data)
- Sinus width as positive predictor of bone gain (wider sinus → more HA volume → more gain)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Preop**: CBCT for RBH and sinus width assessment
- **Procedure**: fluid-dynamic device (Flusilift) for crestal membrane elevation → injection of 2% cross-linked HA → simultaneous implant placement
- **Healing**: undisclosed healing protocol; second-stage surgery for ISQ
- **Follow-up CBCT**: 12 months post-op
- **Outcome**: New bone height (mesial+distal), ISQ at second stage, complications, peri-implantitis

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Bone gain**: 7.5mm average at 12 months (CBCT)
- **Implant survival**: 58/58 (100%); no peri-implantitis
- **ISQ**: all 58 achieved primary stability (absolute values not extracted from abstract)
- **Sinus width correlation**: wider sinuses showed higher bone gain (statistical test not specified in abstract)
- **Complications**: none reported

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Single prospective arm; no control group
- 12-month follow-up only; HA resorption timeline and long-term bone maintenance unclear
- 8 authors from private practices — possible selection bias toward favorable cases
- Flusilift device-specific; technique not transferable to standard osteotome approach
- ISQ values at placement not reported; no stability dip characterization

## 6. Related Work
- Felix 2022: hydraulic sinus lift (water-based, similar principle but no HA graft)
- Sansupakorn 2024: alloplastic graft (BCP) vs no-graft (different graft philosophy)
- Scarano's broader HA body of work in dental biomaterials

## 7. Glossary
- **Flusilift**: proprietary device for controlled fluid injection to elevate Schneiderian membrane
- **HA** (Hyaluronic Acid, 히알루론산): here used as 2% cross-linked form (high-viscosity, slow-resorbing)
- **Fluid-dynamic SFE**: membrane elevation via hydraulic pressure from injected fluid rather than mechanical osteotomes
- **CBCT** (Cone Beam CT, 콘빔 전산화단층촬영): 3D volumetric bone assessment
