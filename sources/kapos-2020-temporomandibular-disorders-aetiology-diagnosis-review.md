## One-line Summary
Narrative review (Oral Surgery 2020): TMD is biopsychosocial/multifactorial; DC/TMD covers 12 diagnoses; OPPERA data — 4% annual incidence, 36% adult prevalence; conservative multidisciplinary treatment first-line.

## 한줄요약
서술 고찰 (2020): TMD는 생물심리사회적·다인성; DC/TMD 12개 진단 분류; 성인 36% 유병률; 보존적 다학제 치료 우선.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Oral Surgery 13(4):321–334 (2020); NIHMS preprint available
- **Study type**: Narrative review
- **Focus**: Aetiology, pathophysiology, epidemiology, DC/TMD diagnosis, management

## 2. Key Contributions
- DC/TMD covers 12 most common diagnoses (painful + non-painful)
- OPPERA study: 4% annual incidence (adults 18–44), 36% prevalence of painful TMD
- TMJ clicking (30% adults) vs disc displacement (8%): poor correlation with pain
- Biopsychosocial model — 3 domains: psychological profile, pain amplification, global symptoms
- Conservative management evidence: self-management + behavioural + physical + pharmacotherapy

## 3. Methodology
- Narrative review of TMD literature up to 2020
- Emphasis on OPPERA cohort, DC/TMD validation studies, biopsychosocial frameworks

## 4. Key Epidemiology Data (OPPERA)
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Annual incidence (adults 18–44) | 4% |
| Annual incidence (adolescent females) | 2–3% |
| Prevalence painful TMD (adults 20–49) | 36% |
| TMJ clicking prevalence | 30% |
| Disc displacement diagnosis | 8% |
| DJD (degenerative joint disease) | 17% |
| 8-month remission after incident TMD | 51% |
| 5-year remission | 49% |
| 8-year remission | 28% |

## 5. Aetiology — Biopsychosocial Domains
1. **Psychological profile**: anxiety, depression, somatization
2. **Pain amplification**: central sensitization, QST-measured pain sensitivity
3. **Global health/comorbidities**: IBS, insomnia, fibromyalgia, fatigue

Genetic factors (COMT, 5 SNPs) contribute to pain processing; hormonal/sex factors more important for chronicity than incidence.

## 6. DC/TMD Classification (12 diagnoses)
**Painful**: myalgia, arthralgia, headache attributed to TMD  
**Non-painful**: disc displacements (with/without reduction), DJD, subluxation

## 7. Management Principles
- Self-management education, jaw exercises
- Behavioural/psychological therapy for chronic cases
- Physical therapy (manual therapy, exercise)
- Pharmacotherapy (NSAIDs, muscle relaxants for muscle TMD, clonazepam for BMS)
- Avoid irreversible occlusal/surgical interventions as first-line

## 8. Limitations
- Narrative review; not systematic; potential selection bias
- Data primarily from OPPERA (US population); generalizability to other populations

## 9. Glossary
- **DC/TMD**: Diagnostic Criteria for Temporomandibular Disorders (2014 — gold standard)
- **OPPERA**: Orofacial Pain: Prospective Evaluation and Risk Assessment (NIDCR-funded cohort)
- **COMT**: Catecholamine-O-methyltransferase — enzyme regulating pain neurotransmitters
- **DJD**: Degenerative joint disease (골관절염)
- **QST**: Quantitative sensory testing (정량감각검사)
