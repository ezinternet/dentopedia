## One-line Summary
In vitro CNC-controlled study (PLOS ONE 2025): direct positive correlation between drill bit wear (assessed by SEM) and bone temperature increase across 150 holes per drill set — far beyond the manufacturer-recommended ~25–30 use limit; correlation with hole geometry is weaker.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: PLOS ONE 20(3):e0319492, 19 March 2025
- Type: In vitro experimental study
- Bone substitute: synthetic block reproducing human bone characteristics
- CNC-controlled drilling for reproducibility; no irrigation/lubrication
- Affiliations: University of Padova (Industrial Engineering, Dentistry); Malmö University

## 2. Key Contributions
- Demonstrates a quantitative, monotonic relationship between drill wear and intraosseous temperature rise.
- Tests drill bits up to 150 uses — 5–6× the manufacturer recommendation — providing direct evidence for the "replace your drills" claim.
- Combines real-time thermocouple temperature measurement, SEM wear assessment, and CMM geometric inspection.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Drill sets: AT and PT protocols, 4 steps each.
- Synthetic bone block (reproducing human bone characteristics).
- CNC drilling at 3200 rpm, 240 mm/min feed, peck-feed strategy, no irrigation.
- Three thermocouples (Type-K, 0.25 mm) at 1.5, 7, 12 mm depths, radially 0.8 mm from largest hole edge.
- SEM (FEI Quanta 450, 25 kV, BSED) wear assessment at baseline and every 10 holes.
- TiN-coated drill tips → BSED visualizes coating loss.
- Coordinate measuring machine (Zeiss O-Inspect) for hole geometry verification.
- 150 holes per set, 10–12 sec per drill, 5 min cooling between.
- Implant placement phase: torque + temperature measured.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Direct correlation: drill wear (TiN coating loss area, SEM) ↔ peak drilling temperature rise.
- Weaker correlation between wear and hole geometry deviation (CMM).
- Implantation phase temperatures and torques within acceptable ranges (no necrosis-level breach at insertion).
- Manufacturer recommendation (~25–30 holes) is conservative; performance degrades well before 150.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- In vitro synthetic bone — no live tissue, vascular cooling, or biological variation.
- No irrigation (deliberate, to isolate variables) — clinically all drilling uses coolant; absolute temperatures not directly applicable.
- CNC drilling removes operator-related variability that exists clinically.
- Two protocol sets compared, not all manufacturer designs.
- Future: in vivo validation with irrigation; correlation with osseointegration outcomes.

## 7. Glossary
- **SEM (BSED)**: Scanning Electron Microscope with Back-Scattered Electron Detector — distinguishes elements by atomic number; TiN coating shows bright vs underlying steel.
- **TiN coating**: titanium nitride hard coating on cutting tools for wear resistance.
- **CMM**: Coordinate Measuring Machine — high-precision 3D dimensional inspection.
- **Peck feed**: drilling strategy with periodic retraction to break chips and dissipate heat.
- **AT / PT**: two proprietary drill protocol designations compared in the study.
