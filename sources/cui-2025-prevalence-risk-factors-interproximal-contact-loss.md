## Why Ingested

food-impaction 카테고리의 후향 ICL 근거를 보강하기 위해 인제스트. 기존 [[food-impaction/liang-2020-prevalence-associated-factors-retrospective]] 및 [[food-impaction/pang-2017-prevalence-proximal-contact-loss-prospective]]가 mesial>distal 패턴과 기능기간 효과를 보고했는데, Cui 2025는 graded aluminum strip(20–100 μm)으로 interspace를 정량 측정하고 mesial/distal을 별도 logistic으로 분석해, bruxism·mechanical complication·peri-implantitis 같은 새 인자를 추가로 제시한다 (mesial vs distal 위험인자가 다르다는 점이 핵심 기여).

## One-line Summary

Retrospective study (204 patients, 445 posterior implant prostheses, ≤10y; ICL = aluminum-strip interspace >50 μm): patient-level ICL 59.8%, mesial 40.0% > distal 24.1%; mesial risk factors = food impaction (OR 4.99), function duration, adjacent-tooth status, bruxism; distal = mechanical complications (OR 7.04), peri-implantitis, food impaction, bruxism.

## 한줄요약

후향연구(204명, 구치부 임플란트 보철 445개, ≤10년; 알루미늄 strip interspace >50 μm를 ICL로 정의): 환자수준 ICL 59.8%, mesial 40.0% > distal 24.1%; mesial 위험인자=식편압입(OR 4.99)·기능기간·인접치상태·이갈이, distal=기계적 합병증(OR 7.04)·임플란트주위염·식편압입·이갈이.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Reports ICL prevalence at BOTH patient level (59.8%) and site level (mesial 40.0%, distal 24.1%) in one cohort, addressing the patient-vs-site analytical heterogeneity that confounded prior meta-analyses.
- Graded aluminum strips (20–100 μm), thickness >50 μm = ICL — an objective, incremental refinement of Pang's qualitative protocol.
- Analyzes mesial and distal contacts separately with distinct logistic models, showing the risk-factor profiles differ between sites.
- Adds bruxism, mechanical complications, and peri-implantitis to the recognized ICL risk-factor set.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Design: retrospective; n=204 participants (111 M / 93 F), 445 implant prostheses (204 maxilla, 241 mandible; 396 molar, 49 premolar; 289 single crowns, 69 bridges), placed Jan 2011–Dec 2020; 565 contact sites (345 mesial, 220 distal). Power calc target 215.
- Inclusion floor: ≥1 year function. Excluded: undocumented baseline contact, ongoing ortho, adjacent mobility ≥Grade II, defective crowns, edentulous arches.
- ICL measurement: aluminum strips inserted to resistance; recorded interspace >50 μm = ICL.
- Covariates: medical-record (location, site, retention, material, function duration, connection type) + questionnaire (bruxism, unilateral mastication, smoking, diabetes, periodontal maintenance) + clinical exam (probing, peri-implantitis BOP + bone loss >2 mm, Jemt Papilla Index, occlusion type, Tooth Wear Index, adjacent-tooth status, endodontic status, food impaction, mechanical complications) + parallel-X-ray bone loss.
- Analysis: chi-square / U tests, then logistic regression (independent factors) and multivariate linear regression (interspace thickness). SPSS 24.0; α=0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Patient-level ICL: 122/204 = 59.8% over up to 10y function; increased with time.
- Site-level: mesial 138/345 = 40.0%; distal 53/220 = 24.1%. Mesial:distal ratio ≈1.66; mesial interspace significantly thicker (P<0.001). Mesial 37.1% by 4y; distal 25.3% by 2y.
- Patient-level univariate: NO factor significant (age, gender, diabetes, smoking, periodontal maintenance, bruxism, unilateral mastication all NS).
- Mesial independent risk factors (logistic): function duration (OR 1.299/yr, P<0.001), adjacent-tooth status (P<0.001; implant/crown OR 4.062, P=0.042 vs implant/implant OR 0.314 protective), food impaction (OR 4.991, P<0.001), bruxism (OR 2.098, P=0.034), full-mouth wear (OR 1.875, P=0.04), keratinized-gingiva width (OR 1.131, P=0.015). Linear: external connection (B=34.927, P=0.003) and gingival papilla recession (B=26.532, P=0.001) added to interspace thickness; implant/implant protective (B=−34.241).
- Distal independent risk factors (logistic): mechanical complications (OR 7.041, P<0.001), peri-implantitis (OR 2.385, P=0.021), food impaction (OR 2.809, P=0.002), bruxism (OR 2.356, P=0.019). Occlusion (group function) significant univariate (P<0.001). Linear: mechanical complications (B=39.434, P=0.006), gingival papilla recession (B=38.150, P<0.001).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Aluminum-strip measurement disturbed by natural-tooth mobility and examiner insertion force (single examiner used to mitigate).
- ICL onset moment indeterminable → prevalence likely underestimated; longitudinal prospective studies recommended.
- Incomplete baseline records (floss type/thickness, baseline interspace, baseline food impaction/recession/caries, implant fit) → residual confounding; retrospective design limits causal inference (food impaction ↔ ICL direction unresolved; authors hypothesize synergistic loop).
- Single-center Chinese cohort; >3-year function subgroup small (n<30) with possible selection bias toward better hygiene.

## 7. Glossary

- **ICL (Interproximal Contact Loss / 인접면 접촉 상실)**: deterioration of physiologic proximal contact between an implant-supported prosthesis and the adjacent tooth/implant; here defined as aluminum-strip interspace >50 μm.
- **Mesial drift (근심 이동)**: physiologic mesial migration of natural teeth compensating for interproximal attrition; the implant's ankylosis prevents matching drift, opening the mesial contact.
- **Jemt Papilla Index**: 0–4 scale grading interproximal papilla fill.
- **Mechanical complication**: abutment dislocation, screw fracture, implant fracture, loose implant crown.
