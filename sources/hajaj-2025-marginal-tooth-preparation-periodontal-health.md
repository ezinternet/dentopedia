## Why Ingested

Extends the fixed-prosthesis/periodontal-health cluster from "where to place the margin" to "what geometry to prepare": horizontal finish lines (chamfer/shoulder) vs the vertical Biologically Oriented Preparation Technique (BOPT). Deep-dives the preparation-design dimension that [[periodontics/srimaneepong-2022-fixed-prosthetic-restorations-periodontal-health]] treats only at the margin-fit level, and gives a mechanistic counterpart to the abutment-tooth inflammation observed in [[periodontics/lamichhane-2025-crown-margins-marginal-adaptation-periodontal]].

## One-line Summary

Narrative review (112 studies) comparing horizontal preparations (chamfer/shoulder) with the vertical Biologically Oriented Preparation Technique (BOPT); BOPT is associated with greater gingival thickness, stable probing depths (<0.2 mm variation), lower BOP (<10%), and up to 10-year margin stability, but evidence is limited and superiority over horizontal designs is unproven.

## 한줄요약

내러티브 리뷰(112편): 수평 변연(chamfer/shoulder) vs 수직 변연 BOPT 비교. BOPT는 치은 두께 증가·안정적 probing depth(<0.2mm 변화)·낮은 BOP(<10%)·10년까지 변연 안정성과 연관되나, 근거 수준이 제한적이고 우월성은 입증되지 않음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Narrative review (J Clin Med, MDPI; open access), with scoping-review-like structured search
- **Authors**: Hajaj, Rominu, Constantin, Dobos, Veja ("Victor Babes" Univ. Medicine and Pharmacy, Timisoara, Romania; "Vasile Goldis" Western Univ., Arad)
- **Search**: PubMed/MEDLINE, Scopus, Web of Science, Google Scholar; Sep 2023–Jan 2025; included publications Jan 2000–Jan 2025; **112 studies** included
- **Comparison**: horizontal preparations (chamfer, shoulder) vs vertical preparation (BOPT, feather-edge)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Synthesizes periodontal, mechanical, esthetic, and digital-workflow evidence on tooth-preparation finish-line design.
- Foregrounds BOPT (Loi, 2008): a vertical, edgeless concept that eliminates the pre-existing emergence profile and uses immediate temporization to guide gingival adaptation.
- Frames preparation choice as individualized: BOPT for thin biotype / anterior esthetic zones; horizontal designs for posterior, lower-esthetic, higher-biomechanical demand sites.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Narrative synthesis (heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis); two independent reviewers for selection/extraction; no formal risk-of-bias tool (consistent with narrative scope).
- Findings organized into periodontal, mechanical, esthetic, and digital domains.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Periodontal**: prospective studies (n 40–85, 6–10y follow-up) report BOPT maintains stable probing depths (mean variation <0.2 mm) and increases gingival thickness vs horizontal designs; one 10-year/77-restoration trial confirmed superior periodontal stability and gingival-volume preservation for BOPT vs chamfer/shoulder. Chamfer/shoulder linked to progressive recession and papilla loss, especially anteriorly.
- **Gingival inflammation**: subgingival chamfer/shoulder margins → higher plaque indices and BOP >25% at 2–3y; BOPT → BOP <10% and reduced plaque over comparable follow-up.
- **Tooth reduction/biomechanics**: BOPT (and BOPTm) preserve more anterior tooth structure than chamfer/shoulder (Real-Voltas et al. 2025, in vitro).
- **Digital workflows**: intraoral scanning + CAD/CAM + virtual gingiva modeling improve reproduction/transfer of the BOPT emergence profile.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Evidence base limited and heterogeneous; no meta-analysis, no risk-of-bias appraisal.
- Studies do not allow firm conclusions on BOPT superiority over conventional designs.
- Calls for large-cohort RCTs with long-term follow-up before definitive recommendations.

## 6. Related Work

- Complements margin-position/material review (Srimaneepong 2022) and cross-sectional abutment-tooth data (Lamichhane 2025).
- Tied to biologic-width / emergence-profile and digital-workflow literature.

## 7. Glossary

- **BOPT (Biologically Oriented Preparation Technique)**: vertical, edgeless (feather-edge-type) preparation introduced by Loi (2008); no defined horizontal finish line, gingival contour guided by provisional.
- **Chamfer / shoulder**: horizontal finish-line geometries with a defined margin.
- **BOP**: bleeding on probing.
- **Emergence profile**: contour of the restoration/tooth as it emerges from the gingival sulcus.
- **Gingival biotype/thickness**: thin vs thick periodontal phenotype; thin biotype is recession-prone.
