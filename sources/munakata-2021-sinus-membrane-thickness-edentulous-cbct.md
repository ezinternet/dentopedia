## One-line Summary
Retrospective CBCT analysis of 40 edentulous maxillary molar sinuses (35 patients) preoperative to lateral-window sinus floor augmentation: mean sinus mucosal thickness (SMT) 1.09 ± 1.30 mm; SMT > 2 mm in 25%, SMT < 0.8 mm in 50%; significant factors influencing SMT were sex, time since extraction, reason for extraction, sinus septa, and nasal septum deviation.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: International Journal of Implant Dentistry, 2021; 7:16
- **Affiliation**: Department of Implant Dentistry, Showa University School of Dentistry, Tokyo, Japan
- **Study type**: Retrospective single-center observational CBCT study

## 2. Key Contributions
- Quantifies preoperative sinus mucosal thickness (SMT) specifically in **edentulous posterior maxilla**, a gap in the literature dominated by dentate-cohort data.
- Identifies five clinically observable factors that statistically associate with SMT before lateral-window sinus floor augmentation.
- Provides incidence rates for two **perforation-risk SMT thresholds**: > 2 mm (chronically swollen) and < 0.8 mm (thin and fragile).
- Reinforces the rationale for CBCT-based **preoperative risk stratification** prior to maxillary sinus floor augmentation.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 35 patients (40 sinuses; 11 male, 24 female) scheduled for lateral-approach sinus floor augmentation in an edentulous maxillary molar region.
- Mean age 58.90 ± 9.0 years (range 41–79).
- CBCT measurement of: SMT, residual alveolar bone height (RBH), presence/height of sinus septa, nasal septum deviation.
- Variables tested: age, sex, systemic disease, smoking, period after tooth extraction, reason for extraction, RBH, sinus septa, nasal septum deviation.
- Correlation analyses for age and RBH; group comparisons for categorical variables (α = 0.05).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Mean SMT**: 1.09 ± 1.30 mm.
- **SMT > 2 mm**: 25.0% of sinuses.
- **SMT < 0.8 mm**: 50.0% of sinuses.
- **Mean RBH**: 2.14 ± 1.02 mm.
- **Significant factors influencing SMT**: sex (P = .0078), period after tooth extraction (P = .0075), reason for tooth extraction (P = .020), sinus septa (P = .0076), nasal septum deviation (P = .038).
- **Higher SMT pattern**: male sex, < 6 months since extraction, periapical lesions, presence of sinus septa, nasal septum deviation.
- **SMT > 2 mm** specifically associated with: sex and reason for extraction.
- **SMT < 0.8 mm** specifically associated with: time since extraction and nasal septum deviation.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small single-center sample (40 sinuses, 35 patients); larger multicenter validation needed.
- Sex distribution skewed (11 male, 24 female); some subgroup analyses underpowered.
- Cross-sectional; SMT measured once preoperatively — no longitudinal mucosal trajectory after extraction.
- CBCT measurement of thin membranes (< 1 mm) is at the edge of voxel resolution; partial-volume artifacts may inflate measurement noise.
- Correlation does not establish causal mechanism (e.g., why nasal septum deviation co-occurs with thinner SMT remains hypothetical).

## 7. Glossary
- **SMT (Sinus Mucosal Thickness)**: Radiographically measured thickness of the Schneiderian membrane on CBCT.
- **Schneiderian membrane**: Pseudostratified ciliated columnar epithelium lining the maxillary sinus; mechanically integral to sinus floor augmentation.
- **RBH (Residual Bone Height)**: Vertical bone between alveolar crest and sinus floor; determines tSFE vs lateral SFE selection.
- **Sinus septum (pl. septa)**: Bony partition within the sinus cavity; raises perforation risk during membrane elevation.
- **OMC (Ostiomeatal Complex)**: Drainage outflow pathway of the maxillary sinus; obstruction predisposes to postoperative sinusitis.
- **Periapical lesion**: Inflammatory radiolucency around a tooth apex; can incite reactive mucosal thickening above the corresponding sinus floor.
