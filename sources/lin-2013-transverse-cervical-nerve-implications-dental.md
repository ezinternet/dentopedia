## One-line Summary

Fresh cadaver study (n=2, Sihler's staining technique): the transverse cervical nerve (C2-C3, cervical plexus) was found innervating the posterior mandible in one of two specimens, supporting accessory (non-trigeminal) innervation as a cause of IANB failure in posterior mandibular teeth.

## 한줄요약

신선 사체 연구(n=2, Sihler's 염색기법): 횡경부신경(transverse cervical nerve, C2-C3, 경부신경총 분지)이 사체 2례 중 1례에서 후방 하악을 지배하는 것으로 확인 — 하악 구치부 IANB 실패 원인으로서 삼차신경 외 부가신경 지배를 뒷받침.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Directly traces the **transverse cervical nerve (TCN)**, a branch of ventral rami from C2-C3 spinal nerves (cervical plexus), and shows it can supply **accessory sensory innervation to the posterior mandible**.
- Uses the **Sihler's staining technique** — a whole-mount nerve-staining method — to visualize small-caliber accessory nerve branches that conventional dissection typically misses due to their size and the barrier formed by mandibular accessory foramina.
- Provides a concrete anatomical mechanism (rather than only clinical inference) for IANB failure in posterior mandibular teeth, complementing purely clinical/statistical failure-rate literature.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Two fresh human cadaver specimens.
- Tissue fixed in neutralized formalin, macerated in potassium hydroxide, decalcified in acetic acid, stained in Ehrlich's hematoxylin, destained in acetic acid, cleared in glycerin (Sihler's technique).
- Skin dissected on both specimens; nerves delineated three-dimensionally via the staining protocol.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- TCN (stained blue) was found innervating the posterior mandible in **1 of 2** specimens.
- Confirms the cervical plexus **may** (not always/universally) supply accessory innervation to the inferior border of the posterior mandible via the TCN.
- Findings are proposed to account for some IANB failures in posterior mandibular teeth and support consideration of supplemental anesthetic techniques when standard IANB proves inadequate.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **n=2 cadavers** — extremely small sample; TCN mandibular innervation was present in only one specimen, so prevalence/generalizability is unknown from this study alone.
- Abstract-only ingest — full dissection imagery/detailed anatomical description not retrieved.
- Anatomical (ex vivo) finding only — no clinical correlation (e.g., anesthesia failure rate in patients with this variant) established in this paper.

## 7. Glossary

- **Transverse cervical nerve (TCN)**: cutaneous branch of the cervical plexus (C2-C3) classically described as supplying anterior neck skin; this study demonstrates a deeper accessory role reaching the posterior mandible.
- **Sihler's technique**: a nerve whole-mount staining method (decalcification + hematoxylin staining + clearing) that renders even fine nerve branches visible for three-dimensional tracing.
- **Cervical plexus (CP)**: nerve network from C1-C4 ventral rami.
