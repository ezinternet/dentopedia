## 2. Key Contributions

- Comprehensive organ/effective-dose table from post-2010 literature, stratified by dosimetry method, machine, age, technique.
- Mean effective dose: intraoral 1.32 (0.60–2.56) µSv; panoramic 17.93 (3.47–75.00) µSv; CBCT 121.09 (17.10–392.20) µSv.
- Child CBCT ~29% higher than adult; large FOV (>150 cm²) ~1.6× small FOV (<50 cm²); max child large-FOV CBCT 392.2 µSv ≈ 13% of annual natural background (3110 µSv). Free dentaldose.org calculator.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

PubMed review of post-2010 organ/effective-dose reports; summarization by sub-group; representative Monte Carlo simulations of intraoral, panoramic, CBCT to visualize head dose distribution; interactive web app.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Quantifies the intraoral≪panoramic≪CBCT dose hierarchy and the multiplicative effect of age and FOV, packaged as a patient-facing calculator comparing dental doses to natural/medical sources.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

Heterogeneous source studies (different dosimetry methods/phantoms); ranges wide; not a meta-analysis; values are literature aggregates not new measurements.
