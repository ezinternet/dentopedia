## Why Ingested

[[drug/vavro-2024-antibiotic-susceptibility-orofacial-infections]]의 amox-clav 최저 내성 소견에 대한 초기·독립 지역(인도, 2012) 감수성 근거로 reinforce. erythromycin 무용성도 함께 제시.

## One-line Summary
Susceptibility study (n=68 orofacial infections, India): isolates highly sensitive to amoxicillin-clavulanate (Streptococci 95%, Prevotella 92%, anaerobes 100%) and clindamycin; erythromycin shows substantial resistance and is of questionable benefit.

## 한줄요약
구강안면감염 68명 감수성 연구: amox-clav 감수성 우수(S. viridans 95%·Prevotella 92%·Peptostreptococcus 100%)·clindamycin 양호, erythromycin은 효용 의문.

## 1. Document Information
- Annals of Maxillofacial Surgery 2012;2(1):46-50. DOI 10.4103/2231-0746.95318 · PMID 23482901 · PMC3591083.
- Single-center pilot study, Department of Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery, Panineeya Dental College, Hyderabad, India.
- Study design: Evaluative/descriptive clinical microbiology study with antibiotic susceptibility testing.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First regional (South India) susceptibility dataset documenting amox-clav high efficacy across aerobic and anaerobic orofacial pathogens.
- Documents precise per-species susceptibility rates to five antibiotics (amoxicillin, amox-clav, erythromycin, clindamycin, levofloxacin) in 151 isolates from 68 patients.
- Provides evidence that erythromycin, commonly recommended for beta-lactam-allergic patients, has substantial resistance rates in this population (Prevotella only 62% susceptible, Fusobacterium 66%).
- Demonstrates anaerobic predominance (87 anaerobic vs 64 aerobic strains) in orofacial odontogenic infections obtained by aspiration.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Population**: 68 patients (37M/31F; mean age 32y, range 20-50y) presenting with orofacial infections; no ongoing antibiotic therapy, no systemic disease predisposing to infection.
- **Specimen collection**: Aseptic aspiration of abscess pus; immediate inoculation into Robertson cooked meat broth for anaerobe transport; plated on blood agar and MacConkey agar, incubated aerobically and anaerobically at 37°C for 48 hours.
- **Organism ID**: Conventional microbiological methods; selective media for subculture.
- **Susceptibility testing**: Kirby-Bauer disc diffusion method; antibiotics tested: amoxicillin, amoxicillin-clavulanate, erythromycin, clindamycin, levofloxacin.
- **Space involvement**: Submandibular space most common (34%), followed by buccal space (28%).
- **Statistics**: Descriptive; no inferential statistics. IRB exemption as no deviation from standard protocol.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Isolate count**: 151 strains from 68 patients (2.2 isolates/patient); 64 aerobic + 87 anaerobic.
- **Aerobic spectrum**: Streptococcus viridans 41 strains (64%), Staphylococcus 8 (13%), Corynebacterium 5 (8%), Candida albicans 4 (6%), Enterobacter 4 (6%), Haemophilus 2 (3%).
- **Anaerobic spectrum**: Prevotella species 38 total (Prevotella sp. 31/36%, P. oralis 3/3%, P. buccalis 2/2%, P. dentalis 2/2%), Peptostreptococcus 23 (26%), Fusobacterium 12 (14%), Porphyromonas 6 (7%), Bacteroides 4 (5%), Eubacterium 3 (3%), Veillonella 2 (2%). Total anaerobic = 88 (discrepancy in paper: 87 stated, 88 tabulated).
- **Overall distribution**: Anaerobic Gram-negative bacilli predominant (40%), aerobic Gram-positive cocci (34%).

**Susceptibility table (% susceptible by Kirby-Bauer)**:

| Pathogen | Amoxicillin | Amox-Clav | Erythromycin | Clindamycin | Levofloxacin |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Streptococci viridans | 90% (37/41) | 95% (39/41) | 59% (24/41) | 85% (35/41) | 83% (34/41) |
| Prevotella | 78% (29/37) | 92% (34/37) | 62% (23/37) | 92% (34/37) | 84% (31/37) |
| Peptostreptococcus | 91% (21/23) | 100% (23/23) | 78% (18/23) | 100% (23/23) | 87% (20/23) |
| Porphyromonas | 83% (5/6) | 100% (6/6) | 83% (5/6) | 100% (6/6) | 83% (5/6) |
| Fusobacterium | 75% (9/12) | 100% (12/12) | 66% (8/12) | 92% (11/12) | 75% (3/12)* |

*Note: Levofloxacin susceptibility rate 75% for Fusobacterium appears low vs MIC data — high MIC noted in text despite disc diffusion susceptibility.

- **Erythromycin**: Poor against Fusobacterium (66%) and Prevotella (62%); 59% against S. viridans — insufficient for reliable empirical coverage.
- **Levofloxacin**: High disc susceptibility rates but authors caution MIC values for levofloxacin were high, limiting clinical benefit for fluoroquinolone prescribing.
- **Beta-lactamase note**: Amox-clav superior to amoxicillin alone via beta-lactamase inhibition (clavulanate covers beta-lactamase-producing anaerobes, especially Prevotella).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Single center, single city (Hyderabad); pilot scale (n=68) — authors explicitly call for large-scale multicentric studies for national extrapolation.
- No MIC values systematically reported for all organisms; levofloxacin MIC concern noted only qualitatively.
- No statistical significance testing (descriptive only).
- Staphylococci rate (13%) possibly inflated by skin contamination during aspiration.
- No antibiotic history beyond exclusion criterion; no longitudinal resistance tracking.
- Candida albicans identified (4 strains) but not susceptibility-tested.
- IRB exemption reported without formal ethics documentation.

## 6. Related Work
- Vavro 2024 — large-scale multinational SENTRY data confirming amox-clav lowest resistance in orofacial infections (reinforces this finding at larger scale).
- Kuriyama et al. 2000 — foundational bacteriology of orofacial odontogenic infections; S. viridans 77% susceptible to penicillin (vs 90% amoxicillin here).
- Kuriyama et al. 2002 — Gram-positive cocci bacteriology: S. viridans 87% clindamycin, 77% erythromycin (present study: 85% and 59% respectively — erythromycin resistance higher).
- Gilmore et al. 1988 — strict anaerobe penicillin resistance 8.9–16% by genus.
- Sandor et al. 1998 — antimicrobial treatment options in odontogenic infections (key reference for beta-lactam use).

## 7. Glossary
- **amox-clav**: Amoxicillin-clavulanate (amoxicillin + beta-lactamase inhibitor).
- **MIC**: Minimum inhibitory concentration — the lowest concentration of antibiotic that prevents visible growth.
- **Kirby-Bauer disc diffusion**: Standard disk-on-agar susceptibility test; zone of inhibition interpreted as susceptible/intermediate/resistant.
- **GPC**: Gram-positive cocci (primarily S. viridans in this context).
- **GNR**: Gram-negative rods (primarily Prevotella, Porphyromonas, Fusobacterium, Bacteroides).
- **Odontogenic**: Originating from dental/periapical/periodontal source.
- **Robertson cooked meat broth**: Anaerobic transport/enrichment medium.
