NCCL 병인 이론(erosion·abrasion·abfraction)을 비교 정리한 narrative review. [[nccl/grippo-2012-abfraction-abrasion-biocorrosion-nccl-20year]] schema와 [[nccl/dioguardi-2024-abfraction-theory-controversy-scoping-review]]의 후속 scoping review를 잇는 같은 그룹(Foggia)의 개념 정리.

## One-line Summary
Narrative review comparing the competing etiopathological theories of NCCLs (erosion, abrasion, and the disputed abfraction), concluding erosion + abrasion are widely accepted while occlusal-load abfraction remains contested.

## 한줄요약
NCCL 병인 이론(erosion·abrasion·abfraction)을 비교한 narrative review — erosion·abrasion은 다수설, 교합부하 abfraction은 여전히 논쟁적.

## 1. Document Information
- International Journal of Dentistry 2023, Article ID 8838314, 8 pages. DOI 10.1155/2023/8838314. Open access (CC BY). Univ of Foggia.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Structured comparison of NCCL etiologic theories and their differential impact on enamel/dentin mineralized structures.
- Frames erosion (acidic) and abrasion (mechanical/brushing) as the two widely accepted mechanisms; abfraction (anomalous occlusal loads) as the contested third.
- Traces historical classification (G.V. Black 1908 onward).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative literature review (not systematic). Conceptual synthesis of theories.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- No pooled quantitative outcomes; qualitative thesis that single mechanisms cannot explain all NCCLs and a multifactorial model is required.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative design; no risk-of-bias appraisal. Sets up the group's 2024 scoping review for systematic treatment of abfraction.

## 6. Related Work
- Grippo 2012, Dioguardi 2024 (same group scoping review), Goodacre 2022.

## 7. Glossary
- Etiopathological: relating to cause + disease mechanism. Abfraction: occlusal-load microfracture hypothesis.
