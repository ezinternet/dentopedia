## One-line Summary
Retrospective pilot study (28 implants in 28 patients, Peking University Hospital of Stomatology, Oct 2019–Jul 2021): dynamic navigation combined with osteotomes and a piezoelectric device for transcrestal sinus floor elevation (TSFE) achieved angular deviation 3.66 ± 1.67°, entry-point horizontal deviation 1.07 ± 0.69 mm, and apical-point horizontal deviation 1.09 ± 0.67 mm — comparable to dynamic navigation in non-TSFE contexts; premolar sites showed smaller angular deviation than molar sites (p = 0.004), no surgeon effect, and no obvious sinus perforation on immediate postoperative CBCT.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First reported assessment of **dynamic computer-aided implant surgery (dCAIS) accuracy** specifically in the **transcrestal sinus floor elevation (TSFE)** context.
- Combines dynamic navigation with **piezoelectric** instrumentation to address the two main TSFE risk factors: limited visibility and membrane perforation.
- Provides benchmark deviation values for clinicians considering dynamic navigation for TSFE.
- Shows that premolar sites are more accurately targeted than molar sites — a generalizable finding for posterior implant planning.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 28 patients, 28 implants placed in posterior maxilla via TSFE with dynamic navigation.
- Implants: Straumann SP or BLT (4.1 or 4.8 mm diameter; 8 or 10 mm length; SLActive surface, Roxolid, Loxim).
- Workflow: digital planning on CBCT → dynamic navigation guidance for drills to within 1 mm of the sinus floor → TSFE completed with osteotomes and piezoelectric device → implant insertion under dynamic navigation.
- Outcomes measured by superimposing planned vs actual implant positions on postoperative CBCT:
  - **AD (angular deviation, degrees)**
  - **EPHD (entry-point horizontal deviation, mm)**
  - **APHD (apical-point horizontal deviation, mm)**
- Subgroup analyses: tooth type (premolar vs molar), surgeon effect.
- Sinus perforation assessed by immediate postoperative CBCT.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **AD**: 3.656 ± 1.665°
- **EPHD**: 1.073 ± 0.686 mm
- **APHD**: 1.086 ± 0.667 mm
- **Premolar < molar AD** (p = 0.004) — premolar sites more accurate.
- **No significant surgeon effect** — workflow appears robust across operators.
- **No obvious sinus perforation** on immediate postoperative CBCT.
- These deviation values are comparable to published dynamic-navigation accuracy in non-TSFE implant placement.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Retrospective, single-arm pilot — no static-guide or free-hand comparator.
- n = 28 — small, single-center (Peking University Hospital of Stomatology).
- "No obvious perforation" was a qualitative CBCT call, not endoscopic or histologic confirmation; subclinical micro-perforations cannot be excluded.
- Implant length capped at 10 mm; behavior with longer implants and severely atrophic ridges not assessed.
- Learning-curve effect not analyzed.
- Future work should be prospective, multicenter, comparator-controlled (dynamic vs static vs freehand) with membrane integrity verified endoscopically.

## 7. Glossary
- **TSFE (Transcrestal Sinus Floor Elevation)**: crestal/osteotome approach to sinus floor elevation through the implant osteotomy, suitable for residual ridge height ≥4–5 mm.
- **dCAIS (Dynamic Computer-Aided Implant Surgery)**: real-time optical tracking of drills and patient during implant placement, with on-screen visualization of planned vs actual trajectory.
- **Piezoelectric surgery**: ultrasonic bone-cutting modality that selectively spares soft tissue (e.g., the Schneiderian membrane).
- **AD / EPHD / APHD**: standard accuracy metrics for guided implant surgery — angular deviation, entry-point horizontal deviation, apical-point horizontal deviation.
- **SLActive, Roxolid, Loxim**: Straumann surface, alloy, and transfer-piece product terms.
