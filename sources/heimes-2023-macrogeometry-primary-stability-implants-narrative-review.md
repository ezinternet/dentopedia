## One-line Summary

Narrative review concluding that implant macrogeometry — larger diameter, conical/tapered shape, thread design, and roughened surface — increases bone contact area and primary stability, the basis for successful osseointegration.

## 한줄요약

서술적 고찰 — 임플란트 macrogeometry(큰 직경·원추형/테이퍼·나사 설계·거친 표면)가 골접촉 면적과 일차안정성을 높여 골유착 성공의 기반이 됨.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Organizes macro-geometric determinants of primary stability into one frame.
- Links diameter, taper/conicity, thread geometry, and surface roughness to bone-contact area.
- Situates macrogeometry as a controllable lever for low-density bone.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative review; literature search via PubMed, Embase, Cochrane Library.
- Studies screened/selected, quality assessed, data narratively synthesized.
- No meta-analysis (narrative).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Larger diameter and conical shape increase contact area → higher primary stability.
- Roughened surface and thread design further enhance bone engagement.
- Primary stability framed as prerequisite for osseointegration; influenced by implant design among other factors.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative (not systematic) — selection and synthesis not formalized.
- Macrogeometry effects entangled with site preparation and bone quality.
- Few head-to-head macrogeometry comparisons.

## 6. Related Work
- Macrodesign–ISQ studies (Stoilov 2023, Shiffler 2016); tapered-vs-cylindrical (Nandini 2022).

## 7. Glossary
- Macrogeometry: macroscopic implant design (diameter, taper, thread).
- Primary stability: mechanical stability at placement.
