## One-line Summary
SR of 7 studies comparing continuous vs discontinuous anticoagulant therapy for oral surgery: no significant difference in bleeding events; risk-scoring tools recommended for surgical decision-making.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Medicine and Pharmacy Reports, Vol. 96 No. 4, 2023: 346–357
- **DOI**: 10.15386/mpr-2519
- **Institution**: Iuliu Hatieganu University of Medicine and Pharmacy, Cluj-Napoca, Romania

## 2. Key Contributions
- SR comparing two anticoagulant management strategies for oral surgery
- Regimen 1: continuous vs discontinuous anticoagulant
- Regimen 2: continuous vs interruption + bridging therapy
- No significant difference in hemorrhagic or thromboembolic events between regimens
- Recommends use of validated risk scores (HAS-BLED, CHA₂DS₂-VASc)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- PRISMA 2020 checklist; databases: PubMed, ScienceDirect, Cochrane Library, Ovid; 4 specialized journals
- Publication range: January 2000 – March 2021
- Total 7 eligible articles; 5 on regimen 1, 2 on regimen 2
- Most common procedure: tooth extraction; some soft tissue procedures
- Anticoagulants covered: VKAs (warfarin, acenocoumarol), DOACs (rivaroxaban, apixaban, edoxaban, dabigatran), LMWH

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Regimen 1 (continuous vs discontinuous): no statistically significant difference in bleeding
- Regimen 2 (continuous vs bridging): no significant difference in bleeding or thromboembolism
- Continuous anticoagulation during tooth extraction: manageable bleeding with local hemostatic measures
- Bridging therapy associated with increased bleeding without reducing thromboembolic risk

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small number of eligible studies (n=7)
- Heterogeneity in anticoagulant types, procedures, and follow-up periods
- Risk of bias in included studies not fully quantified
- No meta-analysis performed

## 6. Related Work
- Closely related to dinkova-2025 on local hemostatic agents
- Complements moldovan approach: local hemostasis key regardless of anticoagulant strategy

## 7. Glossary
- **VKA**: Vitamin K Antagonist (warfarin, acenocoumarol)
- **DOAC**: Direct Oral Anticoagulant (rivaroxaban, apixaban, edoxaban, dabigatran)
- **LMWH**: Low Molecular Weight Heparin (bridging agent)
- **HAS-BLED**: bleeding risk score
- **CHA₂DS₂-VASc**: thromboembolic risk score
