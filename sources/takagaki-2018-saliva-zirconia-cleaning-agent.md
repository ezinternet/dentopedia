## One-line Summary
Dental Materials Journal 2018;37(5):734-739: Y-PSZ (high-translucency) zirconia saliva contamination reduces bond strength; phosphoric acid leaves phosphorous residue impairing MDP bonding; air abrasion + Ivoclean most effective; XPS analysis confirms cleaning efficacy.

## 1. Document Information
Journal: Dental Materials Journal 2018;37(5):734-739
Authors: Takagaki T et al., Tokyo Medical and Dental University
DOI: 10.4012/dmj.2017-376

## 2. Key Contributions
- Y-PSZ (yttria-partially stabilized, high-translucency zirconia) tested — distinct from standard Y-TZP
- Phosphoric acid cleaning leaves phosphorous residues → impairs MDP chemical bonding
- XPS analysis confirms surface elemental composition after various cleaning protocols
- Air abrasion alone superior to chemical cleaning alone

## 3. Key Results
- Air abrasion: most effective decontamination (XPS: lowest organic contamination)
- Ivoclean: good surface energy restoration; no phosphorous residue
- Phosphoric acid: residual phosphorous → MDP chemical bonding blocked
- Self-adhesive cement (MDP-containing): bond reduced proportionally to contamination level
