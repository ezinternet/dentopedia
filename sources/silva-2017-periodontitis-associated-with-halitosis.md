## Why Ingested

Foundational SR+MA establishing the quantitative association between periodontitis and halitosis (OR 3.16), directly relevant to the halitosis category as the primary periodontal etiology evidence base. Complements [[halitosis/memon-2022-aetiology-associations-halitosis-systematic-review]] on etiological pathways and [[periodontics]] disease-level clinical context.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 5 population-based observational studies found individuals with periodontitis have 3.16× higher odds of halitosis (OR 3.16; 95% CI: 1.12–8.95), with measurement criteria explaining ~45% (halitosis) and ~24% (periodontitis) of heterogeneity.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First pooled quantitative estimate of the periodontitis–halitosis association using meta-analytic methods (OR 3.16, 95% CI: 1.12–8.95).
2. Identified that heterogeneity between studies is substantially explained by diagnostic criteria: ~45% by halitosis assessment method and ~24% by periodontitis assessment criteria via meta-regression.
3. Highlighted that all included studies are cross-sectional, limiting causal inference.
4. Provided subgroup analyses demonstrating that different halitosis measurement approaches (organoleptic vs. instrumental) yield different effect estimates.
5. Identified need for prospective studies and standardized diagnostic criteria for both conditions.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search databases**: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, SciELO
- **Total articles screened**: 1,107 initially identified
- **Included studies**: 5 population-based observational studies
- **Study designs included**: Cross-sectional (population-based)
- **Statistical approach**:
  - Random-effects meta-analysis (DerSimonian–Laird method)
  - Meta-regression to explore sources of heterogeneity
  - Subgroup analyses by halitosis and periodontitis diagnostic criteria
- **Outcome measure**: Odds ratio (OR) for halitosis in individuals with vs. without periodontitis
- **PRISMA-compliant reporting**

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Value |
|---|---|
| Pooled OR for halitosis in periodontitis | 3.16 |
| 95% CI | 1.12–8.95 |
| Number of included studies | 5 |
| Variance explained by halitosis criteria | ~45% |
| Variance explained by periodontitis criteria | ~24% |

- Positive association confirmed: periodontitis is significantly associated with halitosis in pooled population-based data.
- High heterogeneity (I² not reported numerically in abstract, but meta-regression performed to explain it) — measurement variability is a dominant source.
- Subgroup analyses: halitosis assessment method (organoleptic vs. instrumental/volatile sulfur compounds) and periodontitis definition (CDC/AAP criteria vs. pocket depth thresholds) both contributed to between-study variance.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- All 5 included studies are cross-sectional — cannot determine temporal or causal direction of the periodontitis–halitosis relationship.
- Small number of included studies (n = 5) limits precision and subgroup analysis power.
- High heterogeneity driven substantially by inconsistent diagnostic criteria for both conditions.
- Lack of standardized halitosis measurement across the field (organoleptic scoring vs. portable VSC devices vs. Halimeter) impairs pooling.
- Future need: prospective cohort studies; agreed-upon standardized diagnostic criteria for both periodontitis and halitosis to reduce study heterogeneity.

## 7. Glossary

- **Halitosis**: Persistent malodor (bad breath) originating from oral or systemic sources; typically quantified via organoleptic score or volatile sulfur compound (VSC) measurement.
- **Periodontitis**: Inflammatory disease affecting the supporting structures of teeth (periodontium) — characterized by clinical attachment loss (CAL), probing pocket depth (PPD), and radiographic bone loss.
- **OR (Odds Ratio)**: Measure of association comparing odds of halitosis in those with vs. without periodontitis. OR > 1 indicates higher odds in the exposed group.
- **Meta-regression**: Extension of meta-analysis that models how study-level covariates explain between-study heterogeneity (I²).
- **VSC (Volatile Sulfur Compounds)**: Key odorant molecules in halitosis — hydrogen sulfide (H₂S), methyl mercaptan (CH₃SH), dimethyl sulfide — produced by anaerobic bacteria degrading sulfur-containing amino acids.
- **Organoleptic scoring**: Olfactory rating of breath odor by a trained examiner on a standardized scale (e.g., 0–5); the clinical gold standard despite subjectivity.
