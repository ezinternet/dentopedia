## One-line Summary

Editorial (Dent J 2018, Endodontic Microbiology special issue): frames pulpal/periradicular disease as biofilm-mediated, with biofilm structure and inter-microbial dynamics conferring extraordinary resistance — positioning targeted, biofilm-aware disinfection as the goal of endodontic therapy.

## 2. Key Contributions

- States the central premise: pulpal and periradicular infections are biofilm-mediated.
- Attributes microbial resistance to biofilm *structure* AND to the dynamics of interactions between biofilm microbial components (polymicrobial).
- Frames biofilm biology as essential for designing effective, efficient, targeted therapeutic modalities.

## 7. Glossary

- **Biofilm**: structured, matrix-embedded microbial community; structurally resistant to antimicrobials.
- **EPS matrix**: extracellular polymeric substance scaffold of biofilm.
- **Quorum sensing**: density-dependent inter-bacterial signalling.
- **Periradicular infection**: infection of tissues around the root apex.
