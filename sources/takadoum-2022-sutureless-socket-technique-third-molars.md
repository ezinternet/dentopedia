## Why Ingested

Part of a focused collection on suture techniques and primary wound closure after impacted mandibular third molar surgery. This is the cluster's largest, multicentric, full-text open-access RCT and the methodological anchor: it found no difference in pain or any secondary outcome between suture and sutureless arms, providing the equivalence baseline against which the more positive single-center trials [[suture-wound-closure/kumar-2022-suture-versus-sutureless-third-molar-impactions]] and [[suture-wound-closure/sen-2024-sutureless-multiple-suture-third-molar-inflammation]] should be read.

## One-line Summary
Multicentric open RCT (3 French hospitals, n=94 analyzed: 44 suture / 50 without) of suture vs sutureless extraction of four impacted third molars under general anesthesia; no difference in Day-3 pain (p=0.904) or any secondary outcome, with shorter operating time sutureless and smoking a risk factor for complications (3.65× higher, p=0.0244).

- **Authors**: Sarah Takadoum (corresponding, CHU Nîmes, Univ. Montpellier), Grégory Douilly, Marie de Boutray, Sarah Kabani, Eric Maladière, Christophe Demattei, Philippe Lapeyrie
- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health (2022) 22:256
- **DOI**: 10.1186/s12903-022-02287-y
- **Trial registration**: ClinicalTrials.gov NCT02583997 (registered 22/10/2015); CONSORT-compliant
- **Study type**: Multicentric, parallel, non-blinded (open) randomized controlled trial, 1:1 allocation
- **Note**: Full-text open-access PDF — all results in the captured text.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Largest multicentric RCT in this cluster, with follow-up extended to Day 31 (most prior trials stop at ≤7 days).
- Provides the equivalence baseline: sutureless removal is non-inferior on pain and all secondary outcomes while shortening operating time.
- Identifies smoking as a strong independent risk factor for postoperative complications.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: Parallel 1:1 RCT, non-blinded, electronic allocation, across CHU Nîmes, Montpellier, Perpignan (France).
- **Population**: Patients ≥14 y scheduled for extraction of all four impacted wisdom teeth under general anesthesia.
- **Exclusion**: antiplatelet/anticoagulant use, coagulation disorders, immunosuppression, teeth in normal/functional position, other planned orofacial surgery, emergency pain/infection, pregnancy/breastfeeding.
- **Surgery**: standardized — sulcular + retromolar incision, mucoperiosteal detachment, crown sectioning with cold irrigation, curettage, peri-coronary sac removal, saline wash. Suture group: vicryl 4.0 (single, two stitches, or a cross). Without-suture group: flap repositioned and held by jaw pressure (could be sutured if bleeding/poor flap repositioning).
- **Primary outcome**: pain on Day 3 (0–10 VAS).
- **Secondary outcomes**: pain Day 0 and Day 31, edema, trismus, complications, flap healing, painkiller consumption, quality of life (GOHAI), operating time, smoking impact.
- **Edema** = mean ear-lobe-to-commissure distance change vs baseline (%); **trismus** = max mouth-opening change vs baseline (%).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Enrollment**: 100 patients randomized (June 2016–November 2018); 94 analyzed — 44 Suture, 50 Without Suture.
- **Demographics**: mean age 16.5 y; 66.6% female.
- **Primary**: After adjustment for center, age, smoking — **no statistical difference in Day-3 pain (p=0.904)**.
- **Secondary**: No differences for swelling, trismus, painkiller consumption, healing, complications, or quality of life.
- **Smoking**: 3.65× higher complication rate (p=0.0244).
- **Operating time**: shorter without sutures.
- **Conclusion**: sutureless removal is a reliable technique with no negative consequence on outcomes and allows shorter operating time; smoking is a risk factor for postoperative complications.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Non-blinded (intervention visible); blinding not possible.
- Young cohort (mean 16.5 y), all four wisdom teeth under general anesthesia — limits generalizability to single-tooth, local-anesthesia, older patients.
- Crossover possible: Without-Suture patients could receive sutures if bleeding/poor flap repositioning, diluting the contrast.
- 6 of 100 randomized not analyzed.

## 6. Related Work
- Dubois et al — hermetic vs partial closure; half of sutured patients showed wound disunion by day 5.
- Petronis et al 2020 — review of wound-closure methods.
- A cited 5-study meta-analysis found reduced pain with secondary closure but high heterogeneity from differing incision techniques; insufficient evidence on alveolar osteitis/infection/bleeding.

## 7. Glossary
- **Sutureless / secondary-intention healing**: socket left open, flap held by cheek pressure.
- **GOHAI**: Geriatric Oral Health Assessment Index — 12-item quality-of-life score (12–60).
- **VAS**: Visual Analogue Scale (0–10) for pain.
- **Edema (%)**: standardized ear-to-commissure distance change vs baseline.
- **Winter classification**: third molar angulation/difficulty grading.
