## One-line Summary
Placebo-controlled RCT in 24 OA + stable IHD patients on aspirin 100 mg: ibuprofen 600 mg TID significantly raised serum TXB2 and platelet aggregation (aspirin effect abolished); celecoxib 200 mg BID had no effect on platelet COX-1 inhibition despite equivalent COX-2 suppression.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Clinical Pharmacology & Therapeutics 2006;80:264–74
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.clpt.2006.05.004
- **First published**: 02 September 2006
- **Study type**: Placebo-controlled, randomized study

## 2. Key Contributions
- Translated the Catella-Lawson 2001 findings from healthy volunteers to a real clinical population: patients with both OA (requiring analgesics) and stable IHD (requiring aspirin for cardioprotection)
- Confirmed ibuprofen 600 mg TID interferes with aspirin 100 mg daily in this high-risk population
- Demonstrated that celecoxib 200 mg BID, despite achieving equivalent COX-2 suppression (≥70% inhibition of LPS-stimulated PGE2), does NOT interfere with platelet COX-1 inhibition by aspirin
- Provided detailed pharmacodynamic data: serum TXB2 levels, platelet aggregation (AA-induced and ADP-induced), and platelet function analyzer (PFA-100) occlusion time

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Population**: 24 patients on long-term aspirin 100 mg daily for IHD (stable), also diagnosed with osteoarthritis
- **Design**: Placebo-controlled, randomized, three-arm crossover
- **Interventions**: 
  - Celecoxib 200 mg twice daily for 7 days
  - Ibuprofen 600 mg three times daily for 7 days
  - Placebo for 7 days
- **Primary outcome**: Serum TXB2 (index of platelet COX-1 activity)
- **Additional outcomes**: 
  - Platelet aggregation (arachidonic acid–induced and ADP-induced)
  - PFA-100 time to form occlusive thrombus (platelet function analyzer)
  - Urinary 11-dehydro-TXB2 (index of systemic thromboxane biosynthesis)
  - LPS-stimulated PGE2 generation (COX-2 activity ex vivo)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Ibuprofen group, day 7 (before morning dose)**: median serum TXB2 19.13 ng/mL [range 1–47.5] — significantly elevated vs baseline 1.65 ng/mL [0.55–79.8] (p < .001)
- **Ibuprofen group, 24 h after aspirin co-administration**: serum TXB2 22.28 ng/mL [4.9–44.4] — still significantly elevated (p < .001)
- **Ibuprofen group**: significant increase in AA-induced platelet aggregation (p < .01), ADP-induced aggregation (p < .05), and decrease in PFA-100 occlusion time (p < .01)
- **Celecoxib group**: serum TXB2 and platelet aggregation not significantly different from placebo — full aspirin antiplatelet effect preserved
- **Placebo group**: baseline aspirin inhibition maintained throughout 7 days
- **COX-2 inhibition**: ibuprofen ≥80% and celecoxib ≥70% suppression of LPS-PGE2 (comparable at steady state)
- **Urinary 11-dehydro-TXB2**: not significantly affected in either drug group (reflecting systemic, not just platelet, TXB2 production)
- Conclusion: COX-2 selectivity is the key determinant of whether an NSAID interferes with aspirin's platelet COX-1 inhibition

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small sample (n=24); limited statistical power for secondary outcomes
- 7-day treatment; longer co-administration not assessed
- Patients were already on chronic aspirin; the interaction may differ for patients initiating aspirin concurrently
- Ibuprofen dose (600 mg TID) is at the higher therapeutic range; lower doses may behave differently
- Only one COX-2 inhibitor (celecoxib) tested

## 6. Related Work
- Catella-Lawson 2001 (NEJM): foundational human pharmacodynamic study; ibuprofen vs. rofecoxib/diclofenac/APAP
- Hohlfeld 2008 (J Thromb Haemost): dipyrone/pyrazolinone class also interferes
- Yokoyama 2013: in vitro 9-NSAID screen; mefenamic acid also interferes

## 7. Glossary
- **Serum TXB2**: Stable metabolite of TXA2; used here as surrogate for platelet COX-1 activity
- **AA-induced aggregation**: Arachidonic acid triggers TXA2 production; if COX-1 is inhibited by aspirin, aggregation is suppressed
- **PFA-100**: Platelet function analyzer; measures time to form an occlusive platelet plug under high shear; sensitive to aspirin effect
- **11-dehydro-TXB2**: Urinary metabolite reflecting whole-body thromboxane biosynthesis (not only platelet-derived)
- **LPS-PGE2**: Lipopolysaccharide-stimulated prostaglandin E2 from monocytes; standard ex vivo marker for COX-2 activity
- **COX-2 selectivity**: The property of agents like celecoxib that allows them to suppress COX-2-mediated inflammation without blocking COX-1-mediated platelet thromboxane synthesis
