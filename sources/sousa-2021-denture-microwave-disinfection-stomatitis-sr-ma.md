## Why Ingested

Denture stomatitis (의치성 구내염)는 총의치 착용자에서 흔한 구강점막 병소로, 치료 방법 선택이 임상적으로 중요하다. 본 SR+MA는 마이크로웨이브 소독이 항진균제(nystatin) 치료와 비교하여 동등한 효과를 보이는지 GRADE 근거 수준으로 평가한다. [[complete-denture/srinivasan-2021-cad-cam-removable-complete-dentures]]의 디지털 의치 청결도 관련 논의에 근거 보강으로 활용.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (3 RCTs) showing microwave disinfection of complete dentures is as effective as topical nystatin for reducing Candida counts and clinical signs of denture stomatitis, with low certainty of evidence (GRADE).

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (3건 RCT): 총의치 마이크로웨이브 소독은 구강 항진균제(nystatin) 치료와 동등하게 칸디다 수 및 의치성 구내염 임상 소견을 감소시키며, GRADE 근거 확실성은 낮음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: International Journal of Dental Hygiene
- **Volume/Issue**: Vol. 19, Issue 1, February 2021, pp. 62–77
- **DOI**: 10.1111/idh.12477
- **First published**: 06 October 2020 (online)
- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis (SR+MA)
- **PICO**: P = complete denture wearers with denture stomatitis; I = microwave prosthesis disinfection; C = topical antifungal therapy (nystatin); O = Candida counts, clinical manifestation of denture stomatitis

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA specifically comparing microwave disinfection to topical antifungal therapy for denture stomatitis treatment
- Demonstrates statistical equivalence for most outcomes (Candida on denture, clinical signs)
- Identifies a single significant difference: Candida quantification on palate within 90-day follow-up (SMD = 0.47, 95% CI = 0.02–0.91) favoring microwave
- Applies GRADE methodology; rates overall evidence certainty as low
- Provides clinically useful alternative to pharmacological treatment, particularly important for patients with antifungal resistance concerns or medication side effects

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Search strategy**: Six databases searched (databases not fully enumerated in extracted text); 1524 records identified, 816 after deduplication
**Inclusion criteria**: Studies comparing microwave disinfection vs topical antifungal therapy in complete denture wearers with denture stomatitis
**Selection**: 3 articles for qualitative synthesis; 2 for meta-analysis
**Statistical method**: Random-effects meta-analysis for pooled effect estimates
**Effect measure**: Standardized Mean Difference (SMD) with 95% CI
**Evidence quality**: GRADE approach applied
**Outcomes evaluated**:
1. Candida counts on denture surface
2. Candida counts on palate
3. Frequency/clinical manifestation of denture stomatitis

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Result | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| Candida quantification – palate (≤90 days) | SMD = 0.47 (95% CI 0.02–0.91) | p < .05 (favors microwave) |
| Candida quantification – denture | No significant difference | p > .05 |
| Clinical signs of denture stomatitis | No significant difference | p > .05 |

- All included studies reported significant reductions in both Candida counts and clinical signs within both treatment groups (microwave and nystatin)
- GRADE certainty of evidence: **low**
- Clinical conclusion: Microwave disinfection is as efficient as antifungal therapy for denture stomatitis treatment

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Very small number of included studies (n = 3 qualitative; n = 2 meta-analysis) limits power
- Low GRADE certainty of evidence means results should be interpreted cautiously
- Further well-designed RCTs with standardized protocols are needed
- Heterogeneity in microwave disinfection protocols (wattage, duration, container) across studies may affect comparability
- Long-term follow-up data beyond 90 days is limited

## 7. Glossary

- **의치성 구내염 (Denture stomatitis, DS)**: Inflammatory condition of the oral mucosa under a removable denture, predominantly Candida-associated
- **마이크로웨이브 소독 (Microwave disinfection)**: Use of microwave irradiation to disinfect denture surfaces; effective against Candida spp.
- **SMD (Standardized Mean Difference)**: Effect size measure used in meta-analysis when outcomes are measured on different scales
- **GRADE**: Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation — framework for rating certainty of evidence
- **Nystatin**: Polyene antifungal agent commonly used topically for oral candidiasis/denture stomatitis
- **칸디다 (Candida)**: Opportunistic fungal pathogen; primary etiologic agent of denture stomatitis
