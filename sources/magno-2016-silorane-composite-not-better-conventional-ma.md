## One-line Summary
Meta-analysis of 11 RCTs showing that silorane-based composites (SBC) have statistically similar clinical performance to methacrylate-based composites (MBC) in posterior restorations, with no heterogeneity across all parameters; low polymerization shrinkage alone does not confer clinical superiority.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Systematic Review and Meta-analysis
- **Published**: 2016 in Journal of Adhesive Dentistry, 18(5): 375–386
- **PMID**: 27695714
- **Publisher**: Quintessence Publishing
- **Search**: PubMed, Scopus, Bireme, Science Direct, Web of Science, ClinicalTrials.gov, OpenGrey
- **Language/date restrictions**: None

## 2. Key Contributions
- Silorane-specific SR+MA — narrower focus than Kruly 2018 (which pooled silorane/ormocer/bulk-fill)
- Two meta-analyses performed: (1) all 11 studies; (2) studies with >24 months follow-up only — same conclusion both times
- Confirmed that I² (heterogeneity) not significant across all parameters — high internal consistency
- Key message: reduced polymerization shrinkage in silorane chemistry does NOT translate into measurable clinical superiority
- Included 544 initially identified studies → 17 full-text reviews → 11 included after exclusion

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Search databases**: PubMed, Scopus, Bireme, Science Direct, Web of Science, ClinicalTrials.gov, OpenGrey
- **Search strategy**: MeSH terms, synonyms, and keywords; no language or date restrictions
- **Study type**: RCTs only (PICOS framework)
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane Collaboration scheme
- **Software**: RevMan
- **Studies**: 544 identified → 17 full-text → 11 included
- **Meta-analyses**: Two — all studies, and ≥24 months subset

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Studies included**: 11 RCTs
- **Risk of bias**: 6/11 "low risk"; 5/11 "unclear risk"
- **Heterogeneity (I²)**: Not significant across all parameters
- **Primary outcome**: Silorane-based composites (SBC) = Methacrylate-based composites (MBC): statistically similar clinical performance
- Sensitivity analysis (>24 months follow-up only): Same result — SBC = MBC
- Main parameters analyzed: marginal integrity, marginal discoloration, retention, secondary caries, postoperative sensitivity
- **Key conclusion**: "Low polymerization shrinkage is not the principal factor that determines the superiority of a resin"

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Silorane composites limited to a small number of commercial products (primarily Filtek Silorane, 3M)
- Market shift: silorane-based composites have largely been discontinued; clinical relevance for new product selection is limited
- 5 of 11 studies "unclear risk" limits certainty
- No very long-term data (>5 years) available at time of publication
- The ring-opening mechanism of silorane (oxirane) may have biomechanical implications beyond pure shrinkage that were not measured clinically

## 6. Related Work
- Kruly et al. 2018: SR+MA pooling silorane, ormocer, and bulk-fill vs. methacrylates — same conclusion
- Meereis et al. 2018: In vitro SR+MA on shrinkage stress strategies including silorane chemistry
- Boaro et al. 2019: Broader SR+MA; silorane subset included
- Zailai et al. 2025: Bulk-fill-specific SR+MA (silorane largely supplanted by bulk-fill in clinical practice)

## 7. Glossary
- **Silorane**: Low-shrinkage monomer system combining siloxane backbone and oxirane ring-opening polymerization; approximate volumetric shrinkage ~0.99%
- **Oxirane (epoxide) ring-opening**: Polymerization mechanism in silorane that expands slightly on ring opening, partially offsetting typical shrinkage from monomer approximation
- **SBC (Silorane-Based Composite)**: Primarily Filtek Silorane (3M ESPE) — uses a dedicated self-etch primer/bonding system
- **MBC (Methacrylate-Based Composite)**: Any conventional composite using Bis-GMA, UDMA, or similar dimethacrylate monomers
- **PICOS**: Population, Intervention, Comparator, Outcome, Study design — framework for systematic review question formulation
