## Why Ingested

근관치료 방문 횟수(단일 방문 vs 복수 방문)에 따른 치료 성공률 차이를 직접 비교한 RCT로, 임상 프로토콜 결정의 핵심 근거가 된다. [[endodontics/ordinola-zapata-2022-intracanal-medicaments-status-directions]] 에서 다루는 방문 간 약제 사용(수산화칼슘) 의 임상적 타당성을 성공률·통증·시술 시간 측면에서 보강·검증하는 직접 근거로 활용.

## One-line Summary

RCT (n=220 teeth, mean follow-up 29 months) comparing single-visit vs multiple-visit primary non-surgical root canal therapy found no significant difference in success rate (88.9% vs 87.4%, p=0.729), with single-visit requiring significantly less chairside time (62 vs 93 min, p<0.001).

## 한줄요약

단일 방문 대 복수 방문 비수술적 근관치료를 비교한 RCT (n=220개 치아, 평균 추적 29개월)에서 성공률(88.9% vs 87.4%, p=0.729)과 술후 통증에 유의한 차이가 없었으나 단일 방문이 시술 시간을 유의하게 단축하였다 (62분 vs 93분, p<0.001).

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health (2015) 15:162
- **DOI**: 10.1186/s12903-015-0148-x
- **Trial Registration**: ChiCTR-IOR-15006117 (WHO Chinese Clinical Trial Registry), registered 20 March 2015
- **Setting**: University Health Service Dental Clinic, University of Hong Kong
- **Operators**: Two general dentists (Dentist A with 2.5x magnifying loupe; Dentist B without loupe)
- **Follow-up**: Minimum 18 months; mean 29.4 ± 9.3 months

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Demonstrates equivalence in success rates between single-visit and multiple-visit primary non-surgical endodontic treatment (RCT-level evidence).
2. Confirms that single-visit treatment significantly reduces chairside time (~31 minutes less per case) without compromising outcome.
3. Identifies prognostic factors — maxillary arch location (OR 3.16, 95% CI 1.33–7.46) and absence of preoperative apical periodontitis (OR 4.35, 95% CI 1.43–13.24) — as independent predictors of success regardless of visit protocol.
4. Finds no significant difference in early (within 7 days) or long-term (≥18 months) postoperative pain prevalence between the two groups.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Study design**: Randomized clinical trial (RCT), parallel-group, single-center.

**Participants**:
- Inclusion: Generally healthy patients requiring primary non-surgical endodontic treatment; periodontally healthy tooth; ≥50% coronal structure remaining; willing to return for follow-up.
- Exclusion: Severe acute pulpitis with facial swelling or systemic infection, severe systemic disease, TMJ/psychological stress history, teeth with prior pulpotomy.
- 256 teeth from 228 patients enrolled; 220 teeth from 194 patients completed ≥18 months follow-up (dropout 14.1%).

**Randomization**: Calculator random-number function; assignment unknown to operators and independent assessor. Per-tooth allocation if patient required multiple teeth treated.

**Interventions**:
- *Single-visit*: ProTaper NiTi rotary shaping + 5.25% NaOCl irrigation + Thermafil core-carrier obturation in one appointment.
- *Multiple-visit*: Same shaping/irrigation; inter-appointment calcium hydroxide paste (UltraCal XS) + IRM temporary restoration; obturation scheduled the following week (2–3 visits total); same Thermafil obturation.

**Outcomes**:
- Primary: Treatment success (no clinical signs/symptoms AND no periapical radiolucency at ≥18 months).
- Secondary: Postoperative pain prevalence (7 days and ≥18 months); chairside time.

**Statistical analysis**: Multiple logistic regression (backward stepwise) for success; chi-square / Fisher's exact for pain prevalence; ANCOVA for chairside time. Kappa statistics for intra-/inter-observer reliability of radiographic assessment (both κ = 1.000 intra-observer; inter-observer κ also high).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Single-visit | Multiple-visit | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Success rate | 88.9% (104/117) | 87.4% (90/103) | 0.729 |
| Postoperative pain ≤7 days | 21% | 12% | 0.055 (NS) |
| Postoperative pain ≥18 months | 0.9% | 1.0% | >0.999 (NS) |
| Mean chairside time (min) | 62.0 ± 23.5 | 92.9 ± 37.8 | <0.001 |

**Logistic regression — independent predictors of success**:
- Maxillary arch: OR 3.16 (95% CI 1.33–7.46), p=0.009
- Absence of preoperative apical periodontitis: OR 4.35 (95% CI 1.43–13.24), p=0.010
- Visit protocol (single vs multiple): NOT a significant predictor in final model

**ANCOVA — chairside time reduction**:
- Single-visit (vs multiple): significant reduction
- Use of magnifying loupe: reduction
- Single-canal tooth (vs multi-canal): reduction
- Non-vital tooth (vs vital): reduction

**Failure reasons (single-visit)**: Extraction due to fracture (1 tooth, 0.9%), incomplete apical healing (10 teeth, 8.5%), no signs of apical healing (2 teeth, 1.7%).
**Failure reasons (multiple-visit)**: Extraction due to fracture/caries (2 teeth, 2.0%), incomplete apical healing (10 teeth, 9.7%), no signs of apical healing (1 tooth, 1.0%).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-center study at a university dental clinic; generalizability to private practice settings uncertain.
- Moderate sample size (n=220); study powered for 10% success rate difference only; smaller differences may be undetectable.
- Gender imbalance between groups (male: 51% single-visit vs 24% multiple-visit; p<0.001) — potential confounding.
- Baseline differences: more teeth with tenderness to percussion in multiple-visit group (58% vs 42%, p=0.015); more preoperative pain in multiple-visit group (46% vs 28%, p=0.007).
- Minimum 18-month follow-up; longer-term data (e.g., ≥4 years) would strengthen conclusions.
- Both operators were general dentists (not endodontists); specialist results may differ.
- Thermafil core-carrier obturation used throughout — findings may not generalize to cold lateral condensation or warm vertical compaction protocols.
- Incomplete apical healing classified as failure (strict criteria) — some studies use "questionable success"; this affects direct comparison with other reports.

## 6. Related Work

- **Sathorn et al. (systematic review)**: No significant difference in antimicrobial efficacy or postoperative complications between single-visit and multiple-visit endodontic treatment (meta-analysis cited in background).
- **Chu et al. (2005)**: Radiographic assessment methodology adopted in this study; also provides the 88% baseline success rate used for sample size calculation.
- **Field et al.**: Comparable single-visit success rate reported with slightly different protocol.
- **Ordinola-Zapata et al. (2022)**: Review of intracanal medicaments (calcium hydroxide) — the inter-appointment medication used in the multiple-visit group; supports rationale for multiple-visit treatment historically.

## 7. Glossary

- **근관치료 방문 횟수 (Single-visit vs Multiple-visit Endodontics)**: Completing root canal obturation in one appointment (single) versus using inter-appointment medication and temporization over 2–3 visits (multiple).
- **ProTaper NiTi**: Nickel-titanium rotary file system (Dentsply) used for canal shaping.
- **Thermafil**: Core-carrier obturation system using a heated thermoplasticized gutta-percha-coated carrier.
- **수산화칼슘 (Calcium Hydroxide, Ca(OH)₂)**: Inter-appointment intracanal medicament; bactericidal, used in multiple-visit protocol to reduce microbial load between appointments.
- **근첨 방사선투과상 (Periapical Radiolucency)**: Radiographic sign of periapical pathology; used as a failure criterion in this study.
- **근첨 치주염 (Apical Periodontitis, AP)**: Inflammation of periapical tissues due to pulpal infection; presence identified as a negative prognostic factor.
- **IRM (Intermediate Restorative Material)**: Resin-modified zinc oxide eugenol temporary restorative cement used between appointments in multiple-visit protocol.
