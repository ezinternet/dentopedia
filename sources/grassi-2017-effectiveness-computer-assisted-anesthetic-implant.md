## One-line Summary

Prospective cohort study (n=45) found computer-assisted Single Tooth Anesthesia (STA) delivered markedly less pain (mean 1.6/10) than patients' prior experience with conventional anesthesia (mean 7.9/10) during dental implant surgery, using half the anesthetic volume.

## 한줄요약

전향적 코호트 연구(n=45)에서 컴퓨터 제어 국소마취(Single Tooth Anesthesia, STA)가 임플란트 수술 시 기존 전통마취(Conventional Anesthesia, CA) 대비 통증을 크게 낮췄으며(평균 1.6 vs 7.9/10), 마취제 사용량도 절반으로 감소했다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies patient-reported pain and comfort for a computer-assisted local anesthesia (CLA) delivery system — Single Tooth Anesthesia (STA) — specifically in the context of dental implant surgery (both immediate/post-extractive and delayed/healed-site placement).
- Uses a within-patient historical comparison design: each patient had prior implant surgery under conventional anesthesia (CA) within the past 3 years and no prior pain-relief treatment, allowing direct recall-based comparison of CA vs STA experience in the same individual.
- Reports that STA required only half the anesthetic volume normally used for CA, with a second (half-dose) injection needed in only 3 of 45 cases.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Prospective cohort study.
- **Population**: 45 consecutive patients whose treatment plan included immediate or late (delayed) dental implants.
- **Inclusion criteria**: Previous implant intervention under conventional anesthesia (CA) within the past 3 years; no previous pain-relief treatment.
- **Intervention**: All patients received the STA (Single Tooth Anesthesia) computer-controlled local anesthesia system, using half the anesthetic quantity normally used for CA.
- **Comparator**: Patients' own prior experience with conventional anesthesia (CA), rated retrospectively.
- **Outcome measures**: 0–10 scale ratings for pain (both prior CA experience and new STA experience) and comfort during STA; total quantity of anesthetic injected; need for a second injection.
- **Analysis**: Correlation analysis and regression models to explore associations and determinant variables; z-test for pain score comparison (CA vs STA).
- **Subgroups**: 27/45 patients received post-extractive (immediate) implant surgery; 18/45 received implant surgery on healed sites.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Reported pain: STA mean 1.6 (SD 0.7) vs CA mean 7.9 (SD 1.2); z = 5.873, p < 0.0001 — a large, statistically significant reduction in patient-reported pain.
- Perceived comfort during STA: range 7–10, mean 9.5 (SD 0.79) — high comfort ratings.
- A second injection (half of the initial dose) was necessary in only 3 of 45 cases.
- STA used half the anesthetic quantity normally required for CA across the cohort.
- Cohort composition: 27/45 post-extractive (immediate) implant sites, 18/45 healed (delayed) sites.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Abstract-only — full text not retrieved from PMC** (PMC full-text body was empty at time of ingest; only the structured abstract was available). Methodological details (exact regression model specification, blinding, statistical handling of the retrospective CA recall, injection technique/site details, adverse events) could not be verified beyond what is stated in the abstract.
- Pain comparison relies on patient recall of a prior CA experience (up to 3 years earlier), which introduces potential recall bias — patients rating a past experience they know differs from their current, more comfortable one.
- No randomization or concurrent control arm (each patient serves as their own historical comparator) — the design cannot fully separate device effect from expectation, order, or regression-to-comfort effects.
- Small single-arm sample (n=45) from what based on abstract appears to be a single center; generalizability to other implant patient populations, sites, and operators not established from the abstract alone.

## 7. Glossary

- **CLA (Computerized Local Anesthesia)**: Local anesthesia delivered via a computer-controlled device that regulates injection flow rate and pressure, intended to reduce injection pain compared to manual syringe delivery.
- **STA (Single Tooth Anesthesia)**: A specific computer-assisted local anesthesia delivery system (commercial device) capable of delivering intraligamentary/single-tooth anesthesia with controlled flow.
- **CA (Conventional Anesthesia)**: Standard manual syringe-based local anesthesia injection technique.
- **Intraligamentary anesthesia**: Local anesthetic injection into the periodontal ligament space, one of the delivery modes enabled by systems like STA.
