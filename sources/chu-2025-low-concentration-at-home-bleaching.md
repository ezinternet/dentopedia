## Why Ingested

Directly compares 3% HP (Invisalign whitening) vs 8% CP (Beyke iWhite) — two low-concentration at-home products with matched effective peroxide content — in a well-designed split-mouth RCT. Fills a specific gap not addressed by [[tooth-whitening/terra-2025-effect-at-home-agents-nma]] (which could not separate product-specific effects); the SEM and pH analysis add mechanistic depth.

## One-line Summary

Split-mouth triple-blind RCT (n=22) comparing 3% HP vs 8% CP at-home bleaching found 3% HP achieved greater ΔE (5.39 vs 4.75, p<0.05) while 8% CP caused less sensitivity (p<0.05); both improved OHRQoL and exceeded clinical acceptability (ΔE>1.8).

## 한줄요약

분할구강 삼중맹검 RCT(n=22)에서 3% HP는 8% CP보다 더 높은 색 변화(ΔE 5.39 vs 4.75, p<0.05)를, 8% CP는 더 낮은 민감도(p<0.05)를 보였으며 두 제품 모두 임상적 수용 기준을 초과했다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Randomized controlled trial (split-mouth, triple-blind)
- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health (2025), 25(1):1748
- **DOI**: [10.1186/s12903-025-07026-7](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12903-025-07026-7)
- **PMID**: 41194058
- **PMCID**: PMC12746643
- **Registration**: Chinese Clinical Trial Registry ChiCTR2400092807 (retrospectively registered 24/11/2024)
- **Institution**: Shanghai Ninth People's Hospital / Shanghai Jiao Tong University School of Medicine

## 2. Key Contributions

- First head-to-head split-mouth RCT of 3% HP (Invisalign) vs 8% CP (Beyke iWhite) — both low-concentration products with comparable effective HP content (~3% HP from 8% CP)
- Demonstrated within-patient control: split-mouth design eliminates inter-individual confounders
- 3% HP: superior ΔE (5.39 vs 4.75, p<0.05) but more enamel surface undulations on SEM
- 8% CP: lower sensitivity (p<0.05) and alkaline pH (neutralizing → less enamel alteration)
- Both products exceeded clinical acceptability threshold (ΔE > 1.8) after 2 weeks
- OHRQoL (OHIP + PIDAQ questionnaires) significantly improved with both products

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Participants**: 22 adults (split-mouth); each received both products on contralateral sides
- **Products**:
  - Invisalign whitening gel (3% hydrogen peroxide) — acidic pH, remains acidic during 8h use
  - Beyke iWhite (8% carbamide peroxide ≈ 2.8% HP equivalent) — initially alkaline, turns neutral
- **Duration**: 2 weeks; gel used daily 6–8h (overnight-style)
- **Outcomes**:
  - Color: spectrophotometer ΔE at baseline, post-bleaching, 1 month
  - Sensitivity: daily NRS + VAS
  - OHRQoL: OHIP-14 + PIDAQ questionnaires
  - Enamel morphology: scanning electron microscopy (SEM)
  - pH: in vitro tracking of gel pH over 8h on bovine enamel
- **Analysis**: Wilcoxon Signed Rank (sensitivity, questionnaires); paired t-test (ΔE); ANOVA (pH)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | 3% HP (Invisalign) | 8% CP (iWhite) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| ΔE (post-bleaching) | 5.39 ± 2.50 | 4.75 ± 2.25 | < 0.05 |
| ΔE clinical threshold (>1.8) | Exceeded | Exceeded | — |
| Tooth sensitivity (NRS/VAS) | Higher | Lower | < 0.05 |
| Sensitivity category | Mild (NRS < 1.5, VAS < 3) | Mild (lower) | — |
| OHRQoL improvement | Significant | Significant | < 0.05 |
| SEM enamel surface | More undulations | Less alteration | — |
| Gel pH | Acidic (stays acidic) | Alkaline → neutral | — |

- ΔE at 1-month follow-up maintained in both groups (color stability confirmed at 1 month)
- Both products well-tolerated; mild sensitivity only (no severe episodes)
- 8% CP alkaline pH likely contributes to enamel protection and lower sensitivity

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small n=22 — limits generalizability
- Retrospectively registered (ChiCTR2400092807)
- 2-week treatment window only; longer durations not studied
- SEM findings qualitative (not quantitative surface roughness measurement)
- Both products are low-concentration consumer/OTC products; findings may not apply to professional concentrations
- Future: larger sample, longer treatment, extended follow-up, profilometry for enamel roughness

## 7. Glossary

- **Split-mouth RCT**: Each participant receives both treatments on contralateral sides; eliminates between-subject variability
- **Invisalign whitening**: 3% HP at-home tray system (Align Technology); CE-marked for EU use
- **Beyke iWhite**: 8% CP gel (≈2.8% equivalent HP); alkaline formulation
- **OHIP-14**: Oral Health Impact Profile — 14-item OHRQoL questionnaire
- **PIDAQ**: Psychosocial Impact of Dental Aesthetics Questionnaire — 23-item scale for aesthetic self-confidence and social impact
- **SEM**: Scanning electron microscopy — enamel surface visualization at high magnification
