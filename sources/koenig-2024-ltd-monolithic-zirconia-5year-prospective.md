## Why Ingested

5-year follow-up of the Koenig 2021 LTD cohort — extends LTD documentation to 5 years, confirms slow but statistically significant progression, and raises a new safety concern: grain pull-out and potential 3Y-TZP nanoparticle release into the body. [[dental-materials/koenig-2021-ltd-monolithic-zirconia-prospective]]

## One-line Summary

5-year prospective follow-up (n=101 elements): LTD continues to progress significantly from baseline to 5 years in 3Y-TZP monolithic zirconia; grain pull-out raises nanoparticle release concern; fracture rate 4.5%; glazing still not protective.

## 한줄요약

3Y-TZP 모놀리식 지르코니아 5년 전향적 추적 (n=101): LTD 기저치 대비 5년 시점에서 유의하게 증가, 교합면 그레인 탈락으로 나노입자 체내 유입 우려 제기, 파절률 4.5%.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Dental Materials, February 2024
- **Study type**: Prospective clinical study with ex vivo monitoring (5-year results)
- **Registration**: ClinicalTrials.gov NCT02150226; CHU Liège Ethics Committee approval
- **Follow-up timepoints**: Baseline, 6 months, 1 year, 2 years, 3 years, 5 years
- **Relationship**: Extends Koenig et al. 2021 (2-year results from same cohort)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Extends LTD observation to 5 years in the same cohort as Koenig 2021
- Confirms statistically significant increase in monoclinic phase (Vm) from baseline to 5 years
- New safety concern: grain pull-out from monoclinic clusters raises question about release of 3Y-TZP nanoparticles into the body
- Prosthesis fracture rate: 4.5% at 5 years
- Glazing remains non-protective
- LTD remains confined to extreme surface — slow and non-uniform
- Effects of LTD aging on failure rate and nanoparticle biocompatibility require further investigation

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Design**: Prospective clinical study with ex vivo monitoring (5-year protocol)
**Same cohort as Koenig 2021**: 101 posterior tooth elements (molars + premolars)
**Additional timepoints**: 3 years and 5 years (added to baseline, 6m, 1y, 2y from prior report)

**Monitoring**:
- Raman spectroscopy: n=2,640 monoclinic phase measurement points per timepoint
- SEM + high-resolution SEM
- FIB-SEM (focused ion beam SEM) — added for 5-year samples (new vs 2021 paper)
- Raman mapping on selected samples

**LTD metrics**: Vm (monoclinic fraction), Vfm (monoclinic fraction in transformation-positive areas), MPs+ (proportion of monoclinic-positive measurement points)

**Compliance**: Strobe guidelines

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **LTD progression**: Tetragonal-to-monoclinic transformation significantly increased between baseline and 5 years (statistical significance confirmed)
- **Onset confirmed**: Transformation begins after 6 months, same as 2-year data
- **Kinetics**: Slow; remains confined to extreme surface; non-uniform (localized clusters)
- **Grain pull-out**: Tribological stress continues to cause grain pull-out from monoclinic clusters at occlusal areas — now flagged as potential source of nanoparticle release
- **Fracture rate**: 4.5% at 5 years
- **Glaze**: Still provides no protection
- **ISO threshold**: Values remained very low (Vfm low at all timepoints), suggesting ISO 25% Vm threshold not approached; clinical significance of detected LTD uncertain

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Access to ScienceDirect abstract/highlights/sections only (paywall) — full quantitative data not extractable
- Clinical impact of 5-year LTD on failure risk not established — 4.5% fracture rate may or may not be LTD-related
- Nanoparticle biocompatibility question raised but not answered — requires dedicated toxicological investigation
- 3M provided restorations and partial funding (potential conflict of interest noted; company had no authority over study design or publication)
- No control group with conventional zirconia (4Y-PSZ or 5Y-PSZ)

## 7. Glossary

- **LTD**: Low-temperature degradation — tetragonal-to-monoclinic phase transformation of 3Y-TZP in aqueous conditions
- **3Y-TZP**: 3 mol% yttria-doped tetragonal zirconia polycrystal
- **Vm / Vfm**: Monoclinic phase fraction metrics from Raman spectroscopy
- **MPs+**: Proportion of Raman measurement points with detectable monoclinic phase
- **Grain pull-out**: Ejection of zirconia grains from the surface due to masticatory tribological stress
- **FIB-SEM**: Focused ion beam SEM — cross-sectional nanoscale imaging tool added in 5-year analysis
- **Nanoparticle concern**: Sub-micron zirconia particles released by grain pull-out may enter soft tissues or systemic circulation — biocompatibility unknown
