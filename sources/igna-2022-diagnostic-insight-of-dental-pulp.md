## One-line Summary
Narrative review guiding pulp-test selection in pediatric dentistry, arguing sensibility tests are no longer recommended by pediatric specialists (subjectivity/cooperation) and vitality tests are preferred for primary and immature permanent teeth.

## 한줄요약
소아 치수검사 선택을 안내하는 narrative review — sensibility test는 주관성·협조문제로 소아 전문가 권고에서 빠지고, 유치·미성숙영구치에는 vitality test가 선호됨.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Synthesizes pulp-testing options specific to primary and immature permanent teeth.
- States that pediatric specialists no longer recommend sensibility tests (subjective; cooperation-dependent).
- Updates recommendations toward vitality tests (blood-flow based) for pediatric cases.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Literature review (narrative) of pulp testing methods and pediatric-specific recommendations.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Sensibility tests (EPT, thermal): unreliable in children due to subjectivity, immature apex/innervation, and cooperation issues.
- Vitality tests (pulse oximetry, laser Doppler flowmetry): more promising for primary/immature teeth, but not yet routine.
- Test selection should be tailored to tooth maturity (primary vs immature permanent).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative (non-systematic); no pooled estimates.
- Pediatric clinical-trial base for vitality tests still limited.

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary
- Immature permanent tooth: open apex, incomplete root development — alters pulp innervation/blood-flow signals.
- Laser Doppler flowmetry (LDF): vitality test quantifying pulpal microcirculation.
