## One-line Summary

Randomized crossover trial (n=5 edentulous, 2 mandibular implants each) showing a novel "centralized occlusal scheme" — maxillary canines reshaped to add centric contacts over the canine pillar — improved occlusal contact area by 21.22% and masticatory efficiency by 21.87% vs conventional bilateral balanced occlusion, with comparable satisfaction/speech/aesthetics (abstract-only).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Proposes a **centralized occlusal scheme (集中𬌗型)** for mandibular two-implant overdentures, designed to resolve the **mismatch between the support center and the occlusal load center** in implant-supported overdentures.
- The scheme **reshapes the maxillary canines** (referencing premolar form) to add **centric occlusal contacts** in the canine region, exploiting the supportive capacity of the anterior mandibular implants and the maxillary **canine pillar (buttress)** — while preserving labial contour and occlusal balance.
- Provides **preliminary evidence** that this design raises chewing efficiency and contact area without compromising patient-reported outcomes.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: randomized crossover; each patient received both prosthetic schemes in randomized sequence.
- **Population**: 5 completely edentulous patients, each with **2 mandibular implants**.
- **Prostheses**: mandibular implant-supported overdenture + complete maxillary denture, fabricated in two versions — (a) conventional **bilateral balanced occlusion**; (b) novel **centralized occlusal scheme** (maxillary canine lingual fossa modified for centric contact).
- **Outcomes / methods**:
  - Masticatory efficiency — **two-color chewing gum method**.
  - Occlusal contact area — digital **"virtual articulating paper" method**.
  - Patient satisfaction — validated questionnaires (overall satisfaction, speech, aesthetics).
  - Stress distribution — three-dimensional **finite element analysis (FEA)**.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Occlusal contact area**: centralized vs conventional improved by **21.22% (17.48%, 26.01%)**.
- **Masticatory efficiency**: improved by **21.87% (17.51%, 24.18%)**; centralized scheme reached **55.13% (43.73%, 59.09%)** of complete natural-dentition chewing efficiency.
- **Patient-reported outcomes**: centralized scheme **comparable** to conventional on overall satisfaction, speech, and aesthetics.
- **FEA**: centralized scheme **expanded the denture support area to the maxillary canine region**, consistent with the load-redistribution rationale.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Very small sample (n=5)** — preliminary feasibility study; effect estimates are imprecise.
- **Short-term**, surrogate functional endpoints (efficiency, contact area, FEA stress); no long-term implant/MBL, denture wear, or mucosal-tissue outcomes.
- **Abstract-only ingest** — methodology detail (implant system, attachment type, FEA loading conditions, statistics beyond reported medians/ranges) not extracted from full text.
- Larger RCTs needed before clinical adoption.

## 7. Glossary

- **Centralized occlusal scheme (집중교합형)**: novel design adding centric occlusal contacts in the canine region to align the occlusal load center with implant/pillar support.
- **Bilateral balanced occlusion**: conventional complete-denture scheme with simultaneous bilateral posterior contacts during excursions; the comparator here.
- **Canine pillar/buttress**: maxillary vertical bone support column at the canine, used here as a load-bearing region.
- **Support center vs occlusal load center mismatch**: the discrepancy between where the prosthesis is supported and where bite load concentrates, the problem this design targets.
