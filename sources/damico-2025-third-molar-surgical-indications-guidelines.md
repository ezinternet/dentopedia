## 2. Key Contributions
- Summarizes current clinical and surgical indications for third molar (wisdom tooth) removal
- Reviews NICE (National Institute for Health and Care Excellence) and AAOMS (American Association of Oral and Maxillofacial Surgeons) guidelines
- Addresses: angulation (mesioangular, horizontal, vertical, distoangular), depth of impaction (Pell & Gregory classification), and presence of symptoms
- Factors favoring extraction: pain, recurrent pericoronitis, infection, cysts, damage to adjacent second molar, root resorption
- Factors favoring retention: asymptomatic with no associated pathology; high IAN proximity risk
- Discusses general anesthesia and sedation indications for complex extractions

## 3. Methodology
- **Type**: Narrative review / guideline synthesis
- **Key guidelines reviewed**: NICE guidelines (UK); AAOMS position paper
- **Clinical evaluation criteria**: Radiographic assessment (OPG, CBCT when indicated), clinical examination, patient age, risk-benefit analysis

## 4. Key Results
- **Absolute indications for removal**: recurrent pericoronitis, abscess/cellulitis, cyst formation, root resorption of adjacent tooth, orthodontic necessity
- **Relative indications**: prophylactic removal in young patients (before root completion); operator judgment
- **Contraindications to removal**: high anesthetic risk; IAN proximity with high nerve injury risk (consider coronectomy)
- Coronectomy (intentional root retention) recommended when radiographic signs indicate high IAN contact risk

## 5. Limitations
- Proceeding paper format; not a full systematic review or meta-analysis
- Guidelines vary by country and institution; no universal standard
- Rapidly evolving evidence base (CBCT-guided risk assessment, coronectomy outcomes)
