## Why Ingested

기존 [[veneers/lim-2023-resin-composite-laminate-veneer-survival-sr-ma]]가 레진 비니어의 생존율을 다루지만 최소삭제 vs 전통적 준비 방식의 임상 결과 비교는 부재. 본 SR(Ali 2023)은 CVs 대 MPVs의 심미성, 주변 적합도, 색 안정성, 환자 만족도를 체계적으로 종합하여 준비 방식 선택 근거를 제공한다.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (4 comparative studies) on conventional veneers (CVs) vs minimal/no-preparation veneers (MPVs): MPVs demonstrated advantages in survival rates and mean success periods; microleakage, marginal fit, and color stability varied by preparation technique and material.

## 한줄요약

## 2. Key Contributions

- Synthesized 4 comparative assessments of CVs vs MPVs across esthetics, longevity, periodontal health, patient satisfaction, microleakage, marginal fit, color stability
- Found MPVs had enhanced survival rates and extended mean success periods
- Highlighted that meticulous patient management and optimal preparation technique are critical for successful outcomes
- Identified that MPV thickness ranges 0.2–0.5 mm (feldspathic porcelain preferred substrate) vs CV thickness 0.3–1.0 mm
- Noted no-prep veneers often eliminate need for anesthesia and temporary restorations

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Framework**: PRISMA-compliant; PECOS protocol (Population, Exposure, Comparison, Outcome, Study)
- **Databases**: PubMed, Embase, Scopus, Web of Science, Cochrane Library, CINAHL, ProQuest, Google Scholar (8 databases)
- **Search terms**: MeSH terms including "veneers," "conventional veneers," "minimal prep veneers," "no-preparation veneers," "prosthodontics"
- **Inclusion**: studies comparing CVs and MPVs in prosthodontics; minimum 1-year follow-up; clinical and in vitro studies
- **Studies included**: 4 comparative assessments (final synthesis)
- **Outcomes assessed**: esthetics, longevity, periodontal health, patient satisfaction, microleakage, marginal fit, cement thickness, color stability
- **Hypothesis tested**: CVs had better clinical outcomes than MPVs
- **Quality/bias assessment**: performed; specific tool not named in extracted text

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **MPV advantages**: higher survival rates, longer mean success periods vs CVs
- **Microleakage**: varied by preparation technique; MPVs showed favorable microleakage characteristics in some studies
- **Marginal fit**: depended on preparation technique — ultrathin/contact lens MPVs showed acceptable margins
- **Color stability**: material properties and fabrication method interaction significant; feldspathic MPVs maintained color integrity
- **Patient satisfaction**: MPVs offered advantages — no anesthesia required, no temporaries, preserved natural dental architecture
- **Periodontal health**: MPVs showed low bacterial plaque accumulation due to thin profile and biocompatibility
- **Preparation thickness**: MPVs 0.2–0.5 mm (feldspathic); CVs 0.3–1.0 mm; ultra-thin MPVs 0.2–0.3 mm (contact-lens type)
- **Hypothesis outcome**: rejected — MPVs demonstrated equivalent or superior outcomes in reported parameters vs CVs

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 4 studies included — small evidence base
- Diverse methodologies across included studies complicate direct comparison
- Dearth of clinical trials on genuine no-prep veneers (most literature focuses on minimal-prep, not true no-prep)
- Authors call for future research: interdisciplinary collaboration, evidence-based standardization of MPV outcome measures
- No meta-analysis performed due to study heterogeneity

## 6. Related Work

- Multiple references supporting MPV biocompatibility and plaque resistance (refs 11, 12 in original)
- Literature on feldspathic porcelain as material of choice for ultrathin MPVs (refs 12, 13)

## 7. Glossary

- **CV (Conventional Veneer)**: traditional laminate veneer requiring 0.3–1.0 mm enamel reduction for preparation
- **MPV (Minimal/No-Preparation Veneer)**: ultra-thin veneer (0.2–0.5 mm) requiring minimal or no enamel removal; also called "no-prep," "contact lens," or "ultra-thin" veneer
- **PECOS**: Population, Exposure, Comparison, Outcome, Study — framework for clinical research question structuring
- **Feldspathic porcelain**: glass-ceramic used for ultrathin MPVs (0.2–0.3 mm); allows acid etching for adhesive bonding
- **Microleakage**: penetration of bacteria, fluids, or ions at the margin between restoration and tooth structure
- **Marginal fit**: accuracy of the adaptation of the veneer margin to the prepared tooth margin
