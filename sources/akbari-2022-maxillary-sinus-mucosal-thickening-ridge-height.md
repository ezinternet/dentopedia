## Why Ingested

잔존 치조제 높이와 상악동 점막비후 간의 관계를 CBCT로 분석한 연구. 골 높이가 낮을수록 점막비후 위험이 높은지를 평가 — 임플란트 계획 시 사전 위험 계층화에 직접 활용 가능. [[sinus-lift/lateral/maska-2017-implant-grafting-success-mucosal-thickening-sinus]]와 대비되는 결과(ridge height 연관성 있음 vs 없음) 제공.

## One-line Summary

CBCT study (n=240 sinuses, 141 implant candidates) found significant association between lower residual alveolar ridge height and greater maxillary sinus mucosal thickening; membrane thickness >3mm was pathological threshold.

## 한줄요약

임플란트 후보 141명 CBCT 분석: 잔존 치조제 높이가 낮을수록 상악동 점막비후가 유의하게 심했으며, 점막두께 >3mm를 병적 기준으로 설정.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Retrospective cross-sectional CBCT analysis
- **Journal**: Future of Interdisciplinary Dentistry (2022)
- **DOI**: [10.18502/fid.v19i19.9965](https://doi.org/10.18502/fid.v19i19.9965)
- **Sample**: 240 CBCT images of maxillary sinuses from 141 implant candidates (62.1% male, 37.9% female); 99 bilateral, 42 unilateral

## 2. Key Contributions

- Established CBCT-measurable relationship between residual alveolar bone height and mucosal thickening
- Provides preoperative risk stratification guidance: low bone height → expect more mucosal thickening → plan accordingly
- Threshold: >3mm for pathological mucosal thickening (consistent with current literature consensus)
- Contradicts Maska 2017 finding of no ridge height association (different populations, thresholds)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Retrospective CBCT review, implant candidates
- Bilateral evaluation in 99 patients, unilateral in 42
- Mucosal thickness measured at point of maximum height on coronal sections
- Residual alveolar ridge height measured from alveolar crest to sinus floor
- Pathological threshold: mucosal thickening >3mm
- Statistical analysis: correlation and comparative tests

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Significant inverse correlation**: lower residual ridge height → greater mucosal thickening
- Patients with more advanced bone resorption (greater pneumatization, lower bone height) showed significantly higher rates of mucosal thickening
- Male-predominant sample (62.1%)
- Sinus mucosal thickening prevalence: majority had some degree of thickening
- Thin sinus membrane (<0.8mm) also noted as perforation risk: perforation risk lowest at 1.5-2mm membrane thickness

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Retrospective, cross-sectional — no causation
- Single point measurement of maximum thickening (vs multi-point measurement in Maska 2017)
- No clinical symptom correlation
- Geographic cohort (Iran) — may differ from Western populations

## 7. Glossary

- **Residual alveolar ridge height**: bone height remaining between alveolar crest and sinus floor; key parameter for implant placement decision
- **Sinus pneumatization**: post-extraction expansion of maxillary sinus into alveolar bone, reducing available bone height
- **Perforation risk**: studies suggest lowest risk at membrane thickness 1.5-2mm; >3mm or <0.8mm increases perforation rate
