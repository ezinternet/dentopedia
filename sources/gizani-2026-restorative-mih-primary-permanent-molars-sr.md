## Why Ingested

기존 [[wiki/caries/konukman-turker-2026-hall-vs-modified-hall-primary-molars-rct]] (Hall technique RCT)와 [[wiki/caries/chavhan-2026-zirconia-vs-ssc-primary-dentition-sr-ma]] (preformed crown SR-MA)는 정상 우식치 대상의 수복재 비교를 다룬다. 본 SR (Gizani 2026)은 동일한 수복 옵션(GIC, 레진, 세라믹, 기성금속관, Hall technique)을 **법랑질 저광화 (Molar Incisor Hypomineralization, MIH)**라는 별도 질환 맥락에서 성공률·치수/치주 영향·OHRQoL로 종합해, 우식 치아 근거를 MIH 표면 결함 치아로 확장·대비하는 근거로 활용.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (20 studies, 1,231 children, search to Sept 2025) of restorative interventions for MIH-affected first permanent molars (FPMs) and hypomineralized second primary molars (HSPM): overall clinical success 84% (12 mo) → 94% (18 mo) → 92% (24 mo) → 83% (36 mo); lowest for GIC (53% at 12 mo), highest (100%) for fabricated ceramic (18 mo) and preformed metal crowns/PMCs (24 mo); heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis and no evidence-based best-approach recommendation could be drawn.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰 (20편, 1,231명, 2025년 9월까지 검색): **법랑질 저광화 (MIH)** 침범 제1대구치(FPM)·저광화 제2유구치(HSPM)의 수복 중재 성공률 종합 — 전체 임상 성공률 84%(12개월)→94%(18개월)→92%(24개월)→83%(36개월); 글래스아이오노머(GIC) 최저(12개월 53%), 제작 세라믹(18개월)·기성금속관(PMC, 24개월) 최고(100%); 이질성으로 메타분석 불가, 근거 기반 최적 수복법 권고 불가.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Most up-to-date systematic synthesis (search to September 2025) pooling restorative-intervention clinical success specifically for **MIH-affected first permanent molars (FPMs)** plus the primary-dentition analogue, **hypomineralized second primary molars (HSPM)**.
- Adds three outcome domains beyond clinical survival not covered in prior SRs: **pulp health**, **periodontal health**, and **oral health-related quality of life (OHRQoL)**.
- Establishes a material success hierarchy across follow-up timepoints: GIC lowest (53% at 12 mo), fabricated ceramic and PMCs highest (100% at 18 and 24 mo respectively).
- Documents that restorative treatment of MIH-FPMs did **not** significantly aggravate pulp or periodontal health, and did not improve OHRQoL.
- Reports the only available HSPM data: Hall-technique PMCs (Declerck & Mampay 2021, n=39 PMCs, 15 children, mean age 4.6 yr) showed high 12-month success (64% fully successful, none lost) but ~1/3 of cases presented gingival condition deterioration, attributed to subgingival crown margins.
- Contextualizes MIH epidemiology: >17% of 8–10-year-olds have MIH; ~50% of these require restoration; MIH-affected children receive 10× more treatment than unaffected peers.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Reporting standard**: PRISMA; protocol pre-registered in PROSPERO.
- **Databases searched** (25 September 2025): Embase, Web of Science, Dentistry & Oral Sciences Source (DOSS), Medline/PubMed, Scopus, LILACS, Cochrane Library (CDSR + CMR). Plus ClinicalTrials.gov, OpenGrey, National Research Register, ProQuest Dissertation Abstracts. Manual reference-list search.
- **HSPM search**: from database onset; **MIH search**: from January 2020 onward.
- **Eligibility (PICO)**: Children/adolescents aged 3–18 with HSPM or MIH; any restorative or non-restorative intervention; follow-up ≥12 months; outcomes: clinical/radiographic success, pulp/periodontal status, OHRQoL, co-operation, acceptance. Excluded: in vitro, case reports, review articles, emergency treatment, anterior teeth, other developmental defects.
- **Selection**: Two independent, pre-calibrated reviewers (KS, SA) for title/abstract screening; discrepancies resolved by NK or SG; inter-examiner reliability k > 0.9.
- **Risk of bias tools**: RoB 2.0 for RCTs; ROBINS-I for non-randomized controlled studies; Newcastle-Ottawa Scale for cohort/cross-sectional studies. Evidence quality: GRADE.
- **Study flow**: 550 papers retrieved (131 HSPM + 419 MIH); after deduplication: 501; after title/abstract screening: 42 full-text; after full-text exclusion: **20 included** (19 MIH, 1 HSPM).
- **Study designs**: 10 RCTs, 5 retrospective cohort, 2 retrospective cross-sectional, 2 prospective cohort studies.
- **Sample**: Total 1,231 children (range 11–281 per study); mean age 6.8–11.6 years; ~1:1 M:F ratio.
- **Interventions covered**: non-restorative (fissure sealants); direct restorations (GIC, HVGIC, glass hybrid, resin composite — conventional, bulk-fill, short-fiber reinforced); indirect restorations (PMCs, cast metal crowns, zirconia crowns, lithium disilicate ceramic, resin composite onlays/endo-crowns); pulp treatment.
- **Heterogeneity assessment**: Chi² test and I² statistic (I² > 50% = substantial). Meta-analysis was planned but not executed due to clinical and methodological heterogeneity.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Overall clinical success rates (MIH-affected FPMs)

| Follow-up | Mean overall clinical success |
|---|---|
| 12 months | **84%** |
| 18 months | **94%** |
| 24 months | **92%** |
| 36 months | **83%** |

Re-treatment needs: 1% at 12 months → 23% at 24 months.

### Material-specific highlights

**Non-restorative (fissure sealants)**
- Resin-based sealants with etch-and-rinse (phosphoric acid): retention 68% at 12 mo; mean survival time 10.5 ± 3.2 months.
- Giomer sealants with self-etch primer: retention only 8% at 12 mo; mean survival time 4.0 ± 4.4 months.
- GI-based sealants: 83% retention at 12 mo; effective against caries (OR 0.2, 95% CI 0.06–0.95) but not against post-eruptive breakdown.
- Sealants more effective in teeth with white opacities and extension ≤ ¼ of occlusal surface.

**Direct restorative — GIC/glass hybrids**
- HVGIC (Durmus 2021, n=134 teeth): 87.5% survival probability at 24 mo.
- GIC via SMART technique (Bal 2024, n=19 teeth): 53% at 12 mo — lowest of all materials.
- Zirconia-reinforced GIC vs glass hybrid (Mahfouz 2025, n=88 teeth): 86% vs 84% at 12 mo; similar (NS difference).
- Glass hybrid (Sezer 2025, n=134 teeth): 78% failure rate at 72 months — significant long-term limitation.
- GIC + orthodontic bands (Bagattoni 2025, n=22 teeth): 98% at 12 and 18 mo — notably high when bands increase retention.

**Direct restorative — resin composites**
- Bulk-fill composite (Rolim 2021, n=64 teeth): 81% (etch-and-rinse) vs 62% (self-etch) at 12 mo.
- Short-fiber reinforced composite vs glass hybrid (Sen Yavuz 2024, n=62 teeth): ~97% vs ~90% at 12 mo; 96% vs 92% at 24 mo; comparable at 36 mo.
- Direct vs indirect composite (Hakmi & Dashash 2023, n=40 teeth): both 85–90% at 12 mo; indirect preferred by patients (shorter appointment).
- GIC temporary followed by composite: 99% at 6, 12, 18, 24 mo (Gaton-Hernandez 2020, n=272 teeth).
- Overall composite survival lower than for non-MIH teeth (Sekundo 2024).

**Indirect restorations**
- CAD/CAM ceramic restorations (Montaser 2023, n=68): endocrowns 100% at 18 mo; occlusal veneers 82% (VS) / 82% (VE) at 18 mo. All radiographic success 100%.
- Full-coverage crowns — zirconia, lithium disilicate, cast metal (Singh 2022, n=60): overall retention 96.7% at 24 mo (cast metal 100%, zirconia 94%, lithium disilicate 95%); 4 teeth required endodontic treatment.
- PMCs (de Farias 2022): significantly higher survival than direct composite at 24 mo.
- PMC (Al-Batayneh 2022, pulp therapy context): 100% clinical success at 6, 12, 24 mo.

**Pulp status**
- Single RCT (Al-Batayneh & Abdelghani 2022, n=50): excellent success >95% for all restoration types; radiographic success 100% at all intervals. Indirect pulp capping success not statistically different from cervical pulpotomy. Vital pulp therapy (VPT) considered a viable option for MIH-affected teeth requiring pulp treatment.

**Periodontal status**
- Three studies — results inconclusive. Treatment did not aggravate periodontal health. Two studies reported significant reduction in gingival/plaque indices up to 24 mo (Bagattoni 2025, Singh 2022), attributed to elimination of hypersensitivity enabling better brushing. Montaser 2023 (indirect ceramic) showed no significant effect at 18 mo.

**OHRQoL**
- Single high-quality RCT (Hajdarevic 2025, n=79): no statistically significant reduction in CPQ11-14 scores after treatment. No significant difference between extraction vs restorative treatment groups on OHRQoL or dental fear/anxiety long-term.

**HSPM (single study)**
- Declerck & Mampay 2021 (prospective cohort, n=15 children, 39 PMCs; mean age 4.6 ± 1.1 yr): Hall-technique PMCs — none lost at 12 months; 64% fully successful; small gingival condition deteriorations in 36% of cases (clinically acceptable); radiographic success 93%; well tolerated.

### Quality assessment
- 4 RCTs: low risk of bias (Hajdarevic, Mahfouz, Hakmi, Al-Batayneh); 6 RCTs: some concerns (Sen Yavuz, Montaser, Özgür, Singh, Rolim, Schraverus).
- Good-quality cohort studies: Bagattoni, Seremidi, Sekundo, de Farias. Concerns raised for: Bal, Biondi, Durmus, Sezer (selection/comparability/follow-up adequacy).
- GRADE: heterogeneity made overall evidence assessment questionable.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **No meta-analysis possible**: clinical + methodological heterogeneity (study designs, patient ages, MIH severity/diagnostic criteria, materials, adhesive protocols, outcome criteria) and unclear risk of bias in most studies.
- Only one study on HSPM; no controlled comparisons for primary molar MIH equivalent.
- Large variation in MIH diagnostic criteria used (EAPD criteria in 15/19 MIH studies; Ghanim criteria in 2; Hajdarević in 1; not reported in 3).
- Insufficient reporting of adhesive protocols and preparation design precluding subgroup analyses.
- Within-group sample sizes often small; studies lacked statistical power for definitive conclusions on lesion severity effect.
- Re-treatment rates, follow-up periods, and outcome criteria varied substantially across studies.
- OHRQoL measured at 11 years (long after treatment) — participants may have failed to recall positive effects.
- **Recommendation**: Larger, well-designed RCTs with standardized MIH diagnosis, material protocols, and ≥36-month follow-up are needed. MIH-specific OHRQoL instruments would provide more precise quality-of-life data.

## 7. Glossary

- **MIH (Molar Incisor Hypomineralization, 법랑질 저광화)**: qualitative developmental enamel defect affecting one or more first permanent molars (often with incisors); enamel is porous/weak, complicating bonding and restoration. Prevalence >17% in 8–10-year-olds.
- **HSPM (Hypomineralized Second Primary Molar, 저광화 제2유구치)**: the primary-dentition analogue of MIH affecting second primary molars; predictive of later MIH.
- **FPM (First Permanent Molar, 제1대구치)**: the permanent molar most commonly affected by MIH.
- **PMC (Preformed Metal Crown, 기성금속관)**: prefabricated stainless-steel crown; can be placed conventionally or via the Hall technique.
- **Hall technique**: sealing a preformed metal crown over a tooth without caries excavation or local anesthesia. Sub-gingival margins may cause gingival irritation.
- **GIC (Glass-Ionomer Cement, 글래스아이오노머 시멘트)**: fluoride-releasing restorative material; lowest clinical success in this MIH review (53% at 12 mo); adequate as interim material.
- **HVGIC (High-Viscosity GIC, 고점도 GIC)**: condensable GIC with improved mechanical properties; used in ART.
- **Glass hybrid**: enamel-reinforced GIC (e.g., EQUIA Forte); higher initial success than conventional GIC but 78% failure at 72 months in this review.
- **SFRC (Short-Fiber Reinforced Composite, 단섬유강화복합레진)**: composite containing short glass fiber fillers for improved fracture resistance.
- **OHRQoL (Oral Health-Related Quality of Life, 구강건강 관련 삶의 질)**: patient-reported impact of oral conditions on daily life; measured here by CPQ11-14.
- **MIH-TNI (MIH Treatment Need Index)**: standardized index classifying MIH severity and treatment need.
- **EAPD (European Academy of Paediatric Dentistry)**: governing body that published the 2022 best-practice guidance for MIH, which this SR was commissioned to update.
- **SMART technique (Silver-Modified ART)**: atraumatic restorative treatment using silver diamine fluoride; low 12-month success (53%) in MIH in this review.
- **ART (Atraumatic Restorative Treatment)**: hand-instrument-based caries removal and GIC placement; no bur, no injection.
- **VPT (Vital Pulp Therapy, 생활치수요법)**: direct pulp capping or pulpotomy preserving pulp vitality; viable option for MIH teeth with deep caries.
- **PROSPERO**: International register of systematic review protocols (York, UK).
- **GRADE**: Grading of Recommendations, Assessment, Development and Evaluation — framework to assess evidence certainty.
- **CPQ11-14**: Child Perceptions Questionnaire for ages 11–14; used to assess OHRQoL.
