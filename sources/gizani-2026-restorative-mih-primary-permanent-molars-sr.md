## Why Ingested

기존 [[wiki/caries/konukman-turker-2026-hall-vs-modified-hall-primary-molars-rct]] (Hall technique RCT)와 [[wiki/caries/chavhan-2026-zirconia-vs-ssc-primary-dentition-sr-ma]] (preformed crown SR-MA)는 정상 우식치 대상의 수복재 비교를 다룬다. 본 SR (Gizani 2026)은 동일한 수복 옵션(GIC, 레진, 세라믹, 기성금속관, Hall technique)을 **법랑질 저광화 (Molar Incisor Hypomineralization, MIH)**라는 별도 질환 맥락에서 성공률·치수/치주 영향·OHRQoL로 종합해, 우식 치아 근거를 MIH 표면 결함 치아로 확장·대비하는 근거로 활용.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (20 studies, searched to Sept 2025) of restorative interventions for MIH-affected first permanent molars (FPMs) and hypomineralized second primary molars (HSPM): overall clinical success 84% (12 mo) to 83% (36 mo), lowest for GIC (53% at 12 mo), highest (100%) for fabricated ceramic (18 mo) and preformed metal crowns/PMCs (24 mo); heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis and no solid evidence-based best-approach recommendation could be drawn. (abstract-only)

체계적 문헌고찰 (20편, 2025년 9월까지 검색): **법랑질 저광화 (MIH)** 침범 제1대구치(FPM)·저광화 제2유구치(HSPM)의 수복 중재 성공률을 종합 — 전체 임상 성공률 84%(12개월)→83%(36개월), 글래스아이오노머가 최저(12개월 53%), 제작 세라믹(18개월)·기성금속관(PMC, 24개월)이 최고(100%); 이질성 때문에 메타분석 불가, 근거 기반 최적 수복법 권고는 불가. (초록 전용)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic synthesis pooling restorative-intervention success specifically for **MIH-affected first permanent molars (FPMs)** plus the **hypomineralized second primary molar (HSPM)** counterpart, with three additional outcome domains: pulp health, periodontal health, and oral health-related quality of life (OHRQoL).
- Quantifies a material hierarchy: glass-ionomer cement (GIC) at the bottom (53% at 12 mo); fabricated ceramic restorations and preformed metal crowns (PMCs) at the top (100% at 18 and 24 mo respectively).
- Documents that restorative treatment of MIH-FPMs did **not** significantly worsen pulp or periodontal health, nor did it improve OHRQoL.
- Single HSPM study used the **Hall technique** for PMC placement — high success, but ~1/3 of cases showed deterioration of gingival condition — flagging a periodontal trade-off.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Seven electronic databases searched for studies published **up to September 2025**.
- Eligible-study selection and data extraction by independent, previously calibrated review authors.
- Methodological quality (risk of bias) assessed with the tool appropriate to each included study design.
- **20 papers included** (19 on MIH, 1 on HSPM).
- Clinical and methodological heterogeneity plus unclear risk of bias in most studies **precluded meta-analysis** (qualitative synthesis only).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Overall mean clinical success for MIH-affected FPMs: **84% at 12 months**, **83% at 36 months**.
- **Lowest** success: **glass-ionomer cements (GIC) — 53% at 12 months**.
- **Highest** success (**100%**): **fabricated ceramic restorations at 18 months** and **preformed metal crowns (PMCs) at 24 months**.
- Treatment of MIH-affected FPMs did **not significantly aggravate pulp or periodontal health**, nor **improve OHRQoL**.
- HSPM (single study): **Hall-technique PMCs** showed **high success rate**, but **~1/3 of cases presented deterioration of the gingival condition**.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Abstract-only ingest** — Results/Methodology captured at abstract granularity; numeric detail beyond the above (e.g., per-material n, CIs, longer follow-up) not available without full text.
- Clinical + methodological heterogeneity and unclear risk of bias in most included studies → no meta-analysis possible.
- Within the review's limitations, **clear evidence-based recommendations for the best restorative approach for HSPM and MIH-affected FPMs cannot be drawn** — higher-quality, lower-bias trials needed.
- Only one HSPM study, limiting primary-molar conclusions.

## 7. Glossary

- **MIH (Molar Incisor Hypomineralization, 법랑질 저광화)**: qualitative developmental enamel defect affecting one or more first permanent molars (often with incisors); enamel is porous/weak, complicating bonding and restoration.
- **HSPM (Hypomineralized Second Primary Molar, 저광화 제2유구치)**: the primary-dentition analogue of MIH affecting second primary molars; predictive of MIH.
- **FPM (First Permanent Molar, 제1대구치)**: the permanent molar most commonly affected by MIH.
- **PMC (Preformed Metal Crown, 기성금속관)**: prefabricated stainless-steel crown; can be placed conventionally or via the Hall technique.
- **Hall technique**: sealing a (preformed metal) crown over a tooth without caries removal or local anesthesia.
- **GIC (Glass-Ionomer Cement, 글래스아이오노머 시멘트)**: fluoride-releasing restorative material; lowest success in this MIH review.
- **OHRQoL (Oral Health-Related Quality of Life, 구강건강 관련 삶의 질)**: patient-reported impact of oral conditions on daily life.
