## Why Ingested

Part of a focused collection on vertical/horizontal ridge augmentation with titanium mesh. This paper provides the human clinical outcome data (mean bone gain, exposure rate, implant survival) for customized CAD/CAM Ti-mesh GBR, anchoring the more procedural [[implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/merli-2025-tacos-technique-alveolar-ridge-titanium-mesh]] technical note with retrospective evidence, and contrasting with the soft-tissue-focused mechanisms in [[implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/kaner-2017-pre-augmentation-soft-tissue-expansion-vertical-bone]] and [[implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/wang-2025-mucoperiosteal-flap-healing-vertical-bone-titanium-mesh]].

## One-line Summary

Retrospective human study (41 patients, 53 atrophic sites, 106 implants): customized CAD/CAM titanium mesh GBR with autogenous bone + bovine bone mineral achieved mean vertical gain 4.78 mm and horizontal gain 6.35 mm, 11/53 mesh exposures, 100% implant survival (mean 10.6 mo post-loading).

후향적 임상 연구 (환자 41명, 위축 부위 53곳, 임플란트 106개): 맞춤형 CAD/CAM 티타늄 메시 + 자가골/이종골(BBM) 골유도재생술로 수직 평균 4.78 mm, 수평 평균 6.35 mm 골증대를 얻었고, 53곳 중 11곳에서 메시 노출이 있었으나 임플란트 생존율은 100% (로딩 후 평균 10.6개월).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Reports clinical outcomes of guided bone regeneration (GBR) of severely atrophic edentulous ridges using **customized CAD/CAM titanium meshes** (patient-specific, digitally designed).
- Quantifies vertical and horizontal bone gain, graft volume change between augmentation and implant placement, biological complications (mesh exposure), and implant survival.
- Positions customized Ti-mesh as a tool that simplifies the surgical phases compared with conventional bending of stock meshes or block grafting.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 41 patients with 53 atrophic sites enrolled 2018–2019.
- GBR performed with titanium meshes filled with **autogenous bone chips + bovine bone mineral (BBM)**.
- Mesh removal after mean 7 months (range 5–12 months); 106 implants then placed.
- Implants uncovered after mean 3.5 months (range 2–5 months); prosthetic restoration started.
- Outcomes assessed: vertical and horizontal bone augmentation changes, biological complications, implant survival.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Mesh exposure: 11 of 53 sites.** Of these 11, eight had uneventful graft integration; three had partial bone loss.
- **Mean vertical bone gain after reconstruction: 4.78 ± 1.88 mm** (range 1.00–8.90 mm).
- **Mean horizontal bone gain: 6.35 ± 2.10 mm** (range 2.14–11.48 mm).
- Mean change of initial bone gain by time of implant placement: vertical −0.39 ± 0.64 mm (range −3.1 to +0.80 mm); horizontal −0.49 ± 0.83 mm (range −3.7 to +0.4 mm) — i.e. modest interim resorption.
- Reduction of bone volume was **significantly higher in exposed sites** (p < .001 for both dimensions).
- Mean implant follow-up after loading: 10.6 ± 6.5 months (range 2–26 months).
- **Implant survival rate: 100%.**

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Retrospective design, no control group, single-center.
- Short implant follow-up after loading (mean ~10.6 months) — survival figure is early.
- "Preliminary results"; authors frame mesh exposure (~21% of sites) as the principal biological risk, with exposed sites losing significantly more graft volume.
- Longer-term data and comparison with alternative augmentation methods (block grafts, non-resorbable membranes) needed.

## 6. Related Work

- Cited foundational/comparator work in the reference list includes: Roccuzzo et al. 2007 (autogenous bone ± Ti-mesh for vertical augmentation, CCT); Cucchi et al. 2017 (non-resorbable membranes vs Ti-mesh + resorbable membranes RCT); Sagheb et al. 2017 and Sumida et al. 2015 (individualized CAD/CAM Ti devices); Elnayef et al. 2017 (vertical ridge augmentation in atrophic mandible SR+MA); Esposito et al. 2009 (Cochrane review of vertical/horizontal augmentation).

## 7. Glossary

- **GBR (Guided Bone Regeneration)**: use of a barrier (membrane or mesh) over a graft to exclude soft tissue and allow bone formation in the protected space.
- **Customized CAD/CAM titanium mesh**: patient-specific titanium scaffold digitally designed from CBCT and manufactured (e.g. selective laser melting), pre-shaped to the planned bone volume.
- **BBM (Bovine Bone Mineral)**: deproteinized bovine bone graft (xenograft), slow-resorbing osteoconductive filler.
- **Mesh exposure / dehiscence**: soft-tissue opening over the mesh exposing it to the oral cavity — the main biological complication of Ti-mesh GBR.
