## Why Ingested

CBCT 감산 분석(CBCT subtraction)을 이용한 용적 측정을 도입한 3D-프린팅 Ti 메쉬 증례시리즈. 선형 측정 외에 cm³ 단위 용적 증대량(0.49 cm³)과 '증대 효율(augmentation efficacy 0.037 cm³/mm)'이라는 새로운 지표를 제공하며, [[wiki/implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/keller-2026-3d-printed-titanium-mesh-autologous-bone]](0% 노출)와 대비해 동일 3D-프린팅 기술임에도 77.8% 노출이 발생한 이유(술식 차이)를 분석.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective case series (n=9): 3D-printed Ti mesh; vertical gain 4.8 ± 2.4 mm, volumetric gain 0.49 cm³, graft stability 87.9%; mesh exposure 77.8% (7/9), complete early exposure in 22.2% → total graft loss.

## 한줄요약

후향 증례시리즈 (n=9): 3D 프린팅 Ti 메쉬; 수직골증대 4.8 ± 2.4 mm, 용적증대 0.49 cm³, 이식물 안정성 87.9%; 메쉬 노출 77.8%(7/9), 조기 완전노출 22.2% → 이식물 전손실.

## 1. Document Information

- **Study type**: Retrospective consecutive case series
- **Journal**: Clinical Advances in Periodontics
- **Year**: 2025 (epub 2025-10-10)
- **DOI**: 10.1002/cap.70015
- **PMID**: 41071184
- **N**: 9 cases (5 combined large, 2 vertical large, 2 combined medium defects)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Introduces CBCT subtraction volumetric analysis for Ti mesh GBR assessment
- First to report "augmentation efficacy" metric (cm³ gained / mm planned)
- Shows complete early exposure (22.2%) is catastrophic — total graft loss
- Defect size stratified outcomes (large vs medium)
- Identifies three failure factors: systemic compromise, improper mesh seating, complete early exposure

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Retrospective, consecutive case series, single institution (University of Florida College of Dentistry)
- Mesh: customized 3D-printed Ti mesh (next-generation)
- CBCT subtraction analysis: 3D digital models via spatial registration + semi-automatic segmentation
- Metrics: linear vertical bone gain (middle plane), volumetric gain (cm³), graft volume stability (%), augmentation efficacy (cm³/mm)
- Defect types: combined large (5), vertical large (2), combined medium (2)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Complications:**
- No complications: 2/9 (22.2%)
- Mesh exposure: 7/9 (77.8%)
  - Complete early exposure: 2/9 (22.2%) → total graft loss
  - Managed exposure: 5/9 (55.6%)

**Bone Gain:**

| Defect type | Linear vertical gain | N |
|---|---|---|
| Large defects | 5.7 ± 2.3 mm | 7 |
| Medium defects | 2.7 ± 0.5 mm | 2 |
| Overall | 4.8 ± 2.4 mm | 9 |

**Volumetric outcomes:**
- Volumetric gain: 0.49 ± 0.20 cm³
- Graft volume stability: 87.9 ± 19.5%
- Augmentation efficacy: 0.037 ± 0.012 cm³/mm

**Key factors for success:**
1. Thorough preoperative planning
2. Precise surgical technique
3. Effective management of postoperative mesh exposure

**Key factors for failure:**
1. Compromised systemic health
2. Improper mesh adaptation/seating
3. Premature and complete mesh exposure

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Very small n=9; no control group
- Single institution; possible selection bias
- Short-term; no implant survival outcomes
- Cannot pool volumetric data with literature (novel metrics)

## 7. Glossary

- **CBCT subtraction analysis**: volumetric comparison of pre- and post-op 3D bone models using spatial registration
- **Augmentation efficacy**: cm³ of bone gained per mm of planned augmentation height
- **Graft volume stability**: % of initial grafted volume retained at re-entry
- **Complete early exposure**: total mesh uncovering during early healing → invariably causes total graft loss
