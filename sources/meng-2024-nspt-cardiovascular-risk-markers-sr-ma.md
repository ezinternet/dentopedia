## One-line Summary
SR+MA of 21 RCTs: NSPT significantly reduces CRP, IL-6, and SBP (moderate certainty); no significant effect on LDL, HDL, TC, TG, or DBP.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health 2024; 24: 692
- **Study type**: Systematic review with meta-analysis of RCTs
- **PROSPERO**: CRD42022377565

## 2. Key Contributions
- Up-to-date SR+MA of RCTs specifically examining NSPT effect on CVD biomarkers
- Separates effect on inflammatory markers vs lipid profile vs vascular function
- GRADE certainty ratings for each outcome
- Found significant reductions in IL-6 and SBP (moderate certainty); CRP (low certainty)

## 3. Methodology
- PICOS: periodontitis patients; NSPT (OHI + SRP); vs no treatment/OHI only; 11 CVD markers; RCTs only
- 4 databases searched to April 2023
- 1353 studies screened → 21 RCTs included
- Random and fixed-effects models; WMD with 95% CI

## 4. Key Results
| Marker | Effect after NSPT | Certainty |
|--------|------------------|-----------|
| CRP | Significant decrease | Low |
| IL-6 | Significant decrease | Moderate |
| SBP | Significant decrease | Moderate |
| LDL | No significant change | Moderate |
| HDL | No significant change | Moderate |
| TC | No significant change | Moderate |
| TG | No significant change | Moderate |
| IL-1β | No significant change | Low |
| TNF-α | No significant change | Low |
| DBP | No significant change | Low |
| FMD | No significant change | Low |

## 5. Limitations
- Heterogeneity among RCTs in NSPT protocols
- Short follow-up in most included trials
- Causal relationship between periodontitis and CVD unproven

## 6. Related Work
- Sanz 2020 EFP guideline: NSPT as gold standard for Stage I–III; systemic effects acknowledged
- Meng 2024 supports perio-systemic link but shows limited lipid/vascular effects from NSPT
