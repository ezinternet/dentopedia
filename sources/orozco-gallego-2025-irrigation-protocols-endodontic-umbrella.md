## One-line Summary
Umbrella review (4 SRs + 9 MAs): PUI most effective for microbial reduction (RD −0.15, p=0.03) and apical healing (RD −0.09, p=0.01); high heterogeneity and variability across irrigant protocols.

## 1. Document Information
University of Antioquia, Colombia. Umbrella review following UR methodology. Synthesizes SRs and MAs on irrigation protocol effectiveness in endodontic therapy.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First umbrella review synthesizing SRs/MAs on endodontic irrigation effectiveness
- PUI (passive ultrasonic irrigation) shows strongest quantitative evidence for microbial reduction and apical healing
- High variability in irrigant type, concentration, volume, systems across studies
- 8 of 13 included articles high methodological quality

## 3. Methodology
- Umbrella review (UR) methodology, PRISMA-compliant
- Scientific + grey literature databases searched
- Included: 4 descriptive SRs + 9 MAs
- Quantitative comparability analysis between MAs
- Follow-up: hours to years

## 4. Key Results
- **PUI vs conventional irrigation**:
  - Microbial reduction: RD −0.15 (95% CI −0.28, −0.01; p=0.03; I²=60%)
  - Apical healing: RD −0.09 (95% CI −0.16, −0.02; p=0.01; I²=0%)
- Variability observed across irrigant types, concentrations, volumes
- Low overlap of primary studies across MAs
- Limitations: methodological heterogeneity

## 5. Limitations
- Umbrella review — dependent on quality of included SRs/MAs
- High heterogeneity in primary studies
- Short-term outcomes dominate; long-term data sparse
