## One-line Summary
Systematic review (8 studies, 445 implants, 135 subjects): 23% implant failure rate after antiresorptive drug initiation post-osseointegration; 83% of failures caused by MRONJ; mean latency 34 months; mandibular posterior most common site.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Medicine 2024, 13, 2091
- **Institution**: University of Hong Kong, Faculty of Dentistry (OMFS)
- **Search date**: up to 16 October 2023
- **Study design**: Systematic review (PRISMA 2020); not registered in PROSPERO
- **Evidence type**: 7/8 studies were case series → moderate risk of bias

## 2. Key Contributions
- Quantifies MRONJ-specific implant failure in patients who received ARD after osseointegration
- Establishes mean time from ARD initiation to MRONJ diagnosis: 34 months (range 3–192 months)
- Identifies peri-implantitis as potential MRONJ trigger
- Highlights mandibular posterior dominance: 65% mandible, 85% posterior
- Majority of MRONJ sites healed with treatment (75% complete healing)

## 3. Methodology (PICO)
- **P**: Patients with osseointegrated dental implants
- **I**: Initiation of antiresorptive drug therapy after osseointegration
- **C**: Patients not receiving antiresorptive drugs
- **O**: Incidence of MRONJ
- Databases: PubMed + Embase (inception to Oct 2023); manual search of IJOMS, JOMS, JOMI, J Craniofac Surg
- Inclusion: ≥5 implants, ARD commenced after osseointegration, ≥6 months follow-up post-MRONJ (or ≥4 years if no MRONJ)
- Meta-analysis not feasible due to heterogeneity → narrative synthesis

## 4. Key Results

**Overall**:
- 445 implants in 135 subjects across 8 studies
- Success rate: 77% (344/445)
- Failure rate: 23% (101/445)
- Of 92 failures with documented cause: MRONJ 83% (76/92), peri-implantitis 17% (16/92)

**Drug distribution** (of 75 subjects with reported ARD type):
- Bisphosphonates: 88% (66/75) — alendronate, zoledronate, risedronate
- Denosumab: 12% (9/75)
- Route: 61% oral, 39% IV/SC
- Indication: osteoporosis 93%, cancer 7%

**Timing**:
- Mean ARD-to-MRONJ: 34 months (range: 3–192 months)
- Latency distribution (Kim 2020): ≤12 months (11), 13–35 months (10), ≥36 months (13)

**Location of MRONJ**:
- Mandible: 65% (20/31 where specified)
- Maxilla: 36% (11/31)
- Posterior region: 85% of mandibular cases

**Severity** (2 studies, n=15):
- Stage 2: 12/15 implants
- Stage 3: 3/15 implants

**Treatment outcomes**:
- Sequestrectomy + antibiotics (amoxicillin/clavulanic acid) most common
- 75% complete healing, remaining sites stable/mucosal coverage
- ~36% of failed implants could be preserved in select studies
- No unhealed sites or serious complications reported

## 5. Limitations
- 7/8 studies are case series (moderate-high risk of bias); no RCTs
- No meta-analysis due to heterogeneity
- Small total sample (n=135 subjects)
- 88% bisphosphonate use → denosumab-specific conclusions limited
- Most subjects had osteoporosis-indication ARD; oncology-dose effects underrepresented

## 7. Glossary
- **ARD**: Antiresorptive Drug (bisphosphonate or denosumab)
- **Peri-implantitis**: Inflammatory condition of implant-supporting tissues; proposed MRONJ trigger
- **Osseointegration**: Direct structural/functional connection between living bone and implant surface
- **MRONJ latency**: Time from ARD initiation to MRONJ diagnosis (mean 34 months in this SR)
