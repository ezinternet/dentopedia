## Why Ingested

Part of a focused collection on vertical ridge augmentation (VRA). This SR/MA quantifies *how much* healing complications cut vertical bone gain in GBR — the mechanistic rationale behind comparing barrier devices and membrane coverage in [[implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/cucchi-2024-ptfe-mesh-ti-mesh-vertical-ridge-rct]] and [[implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/urban-2025-collagen-membrane-vertical-ridge-ptfe-mesh-rct]].

## One-line Summary

SR/MA of 31 publications: when membrane exposure (without suppuration) occurs in vertical GBR, the ratio of means of vertical bone gained is 0.65 (95% CI 0.47–0.91) vs uneventful healing (0.62 [0.45–0.85] for abscess without exposure); overall healing-complication incidence ~11% site-level and ~10.8% patient-level — uncommon but markedly bone-reducing.

## 1. Document Information

- Study type: Systematic review + meta-analysis (random- and fixed-effects), PRISMA, PROSPERO CRD42021226432.
- Journal: Clinical Implant Dentistry and Related Research, 2022; Volume 24, Issue 1, pp. 43–71. First published 19 January 2022.
- DOI: 10.1111/cid.13057
- Note: the ingested PDF is a publisher HTML export (abstract + reference list); abstract carries the full quantitative results.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies the detrimental effect of healing complications on bone gain in vertical GBR using ratio of means (RoM) of vertical bone gained — complication vs uneventful healing.
- Provides site- and patient-level incidence proportions for healing complications and complication subtypes.
- Examines influence of timing of implant placement (simultaneous vs staged) and regenerative device on complications.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PICO: vertically resorbed edentulous ridges needing implants (P); vertical GBR with healing complications (I) vs without (C); RoM of vertical bone gained (O).
- Databases: MEDLINE (PubMed), EMBASE, Cochrane CENTRAL, searched in duplicate up to November 2020.
- Included RCTs, controlled clinical trials, prospective and retrospective case series.
- Outcomes: patient- and site-level RoM of VBG between complication vs uneventful healing; incidences of complications.
- Random- and fixed-effects meta-analyses as appropriate.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- 31 publications included for qualitative + quantitative analysis.
- RoM of vertical bone gained:
  - Membrane exposure without suppuration: 0.65 (95% CI 0.47–0.91) vs uneventful healing.
  - Abscess formation without membrane exposure: 0.62 (95% CI 0.45–0.85) vs uneventful healing.
- Overall incidence proportion of healing complications: site-level 11.0% (95% CI 7.0–15.6); patient-level 10.8% (95% CI 6.6–15.7).
- Site-level subtypes:
  - Membrane exposure without suppuration: 8.7% (95% CI 4.2–14.2)
  - Membrane exposure with suppuration: 0.7% (95% CI 0.0–2.9)
  - Abscess without membrane exposure: 0.5% (95% CI 0.0–1.7)
- Neurologic complications at donor site (site-level weighted mean): 0.8% (95% CI 0.0–5.3).
- Patient-level: no significant differences between simultaneous vs staged approach, or by regenerative device.
- Conclusion: significant reduction in bone gain when healing complications occur, but healing complications are relatively uncommon after vertical GBR.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Search up to November 2020; pre-dates newer perforated PTFE mesh / CAD/CAM Ti-mesh evidence.
- Heterogeneous study designs (case series included); RoM derived across mixed devices.
- Ingested PDF is an HTML export — full methods/forest plots in the supplementary material, not in the extracted text.

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary

- GBR: guided bone regeneration (vertical)
- RoM: ratio of means (here, vertical bone gained with vs without complication)
- VBG: vertical bone gain
- Membrane exposure / suppuration / abscess: healing-complication subtypes
- Simultaneous vs staged: implant placed at augmentation vs at a later surgery
