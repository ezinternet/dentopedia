## Why Ingested

[[wiki/drug/karunakar-2024-buffered-precooled-lidocaine-ianb-pulpitis]]가 buffering을 precooling과 비교했다면, 본 대형 RCT(n=252)는 buffering을 **반대 방향 온도 조작인 preheating(42°C)**과 비교해 SIP "hot tooth"에서 두 물리·화학적 LA 강화법이 모두 conventional을 압도하고 서로 동등함(p=0.183)을 보인다. Buffered-LA cluster에서 가장 큰 표본의 SIP 근거로 sibling [[wiki/drug/karunakar-2024-buffered-precooled-lidocaine-ianb-pulpitis]]를 보강하고 [[wiki/drug/kalra-2024-buffered-lidocaine-articaine-extraction-rct]]의 buffering onset 주장을 SIP 효능·injection pain 차원으로 확장한다.

## One-line Summary

Double-blind RCT (n=252 enrolled, 241 analyzed), IANB in mandibular SIP teeth: both preheated (42°C) and buffered (8.4% NaHCO3) 2% lignocaine reduced intraoperative VAS far more than conventional (post-op VAS 1.59 and 1.69 vs 3.54, p<0.001) and were statistically equivalent to each other (p=0.183); preheated caused the least injection pain (VAS 1.35 vs buffered 2.08 vs conventional 3.19, all pairwise p<0.001).

## 한줄요약

이중맹검 RCT (등록 252명, 분석 241명), 하악 SIP 치아 IANB: preheated(42°C)와 buffered(8.4% 중탄산나트륨) 2% 리그노카인 모두 conventional보다 술중 VAS를 크게 감소(술후 VAS 1.59·1.69 vs 3.54, p<0.001)시키며 서로 동등(p=0.183); 주사 통증은 preheated가 최소(VAS 1.35 vs buffered 2.08 vs conventional 3.19, 모든 쌍 p<0.001).

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Largest SIP buffering RCT (n=252 enrolled)** comparing two opposite temperature/chemistry strategies — preheating (42°C) vs buffering — against conventional in the hard "hot tooth" case.
2. Demonstrated **preheated ≈ buffered** for intraoperative pain reduction (no significant difference, p=0.183), giving clinicians two interchangeable enhancement routes.
3. Showed both strategies **dramatically outperform conventional** on intraoperative pain (mean differences vs conventional both p<0.001).
4. Found **preheated lignocaine causes the least injection pain** of the three — a separate, clinically meaningful endpoint from pulpal efficacy.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: double-blind RCT, CONSORT-reported, allocation concealment via sealed opaque envelopes, computer randomization 1:1:1.
- **n = 252 enrolled** (from 521 assessed): Group A preheated n=84, Group B buffered n=80, Group C conventional n=88. After 11 anesthesia failures dropped, **241 analyzed** (A 81, B 77, C 83).
- **Groups** (all 1.8 mL 2% lignocaine/1:80,000 adrenaline, IANB, 27-G needle, slow over 2 min):
  - Group A (preheated): cartridge warmed to 42°C in composite warmer, administered at 37°C (body temp).
  - Group B (buffered): 0.18 mL withdrawn, replaced with 0.18 mL 8.4% NaHCO3, inverted 5×, refrigerated; freshly prepared every 2 days.
  - Group C (conventional): unmodified cartridge.
- **Population**: 18–60 y, mandibular SIP, preoperative VAS ≥5.
- **Outcomes**: VAS pre-op, VAS intraoperative (after pulp-chamber access, 15 min post-block), injection-pain VAS (immediately after IANB), EPT (negative = effective anesthesia). Patients needing supplementary intraligamentary injection were excluded as failures.
- **Statistics**: one-way ANOVA + Tukey post-hoc, paired t-test; α = 0.05; SPSS v21.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **No success-rate-as-percentage endpoint reported** — efficacy expressed as intraoperative VAS reduction; the supplementary-injection counts (3/3/5) are the only failure proxy.
- **Preheated vs buffered difference not significant (p=0.183)** — the trial cannot rank one over the other for pulpal anesthesia; only injection-pain separates them (preheated lowest).
- **No duration-of-anesthesia or onset-time endpoint** — purely pain-focused.
- **Preheating requires equipment** (composite warmer + thermometer), limiting practicality vs simple buffering.
- **Single LA (lignocaine/1:80,000)** — no articaine comparator.
- Future: combine endpoints (success %, onset, duration), test preheat+buffer combination, larger multi-center confirmation.

## 7. Glossary

- **SIP (Symptomatic Irreversible Pulpitis)**: "hot tooth"; IANB failure 44–81% per literature, driven by TTX-resistant sodium channels and lowered resting membrane potential.
- **Preheated LA**: cartridge warmed to 42°C, injected at 37°C; temperature lowers pKa → faster sodium-channel block.
- **Buffered LA**: 8.4% NaHCO3 added at 1:10 to raise pH toward 7.4, increasing the uncharged base fraction crossing the membrane.
- **EPT (Electric Pulp Test)**: negative response confirms effective pulpal anesthesia.
- **Intraligamentary injection**: supplementary technique given when primary block fails (these cases excluded).
