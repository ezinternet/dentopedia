## Why Ingested

Fills the gap between xenograft-centric SRs and biological agent SRs in the ridge-preservation category — directly compares rhBMP-2 vs L-PRF vs blood-derived growth factor preparations for socket preservation, a head-to-head absent from prior overviews. Extends [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/avila-ortiz-2019-alveolar-ridge-preservation-interventions]] which covered graft materials broadly but not growth-factor biologics comparison.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 29 RCTs (1,068 patients): rhBMP-2 significantly reduces both horizontal and vertical bone resorption after extraction; L-PRF shows nonsignificant changes vs natural healing but remains a cost-effective autologous option.

## 한줄요약

29개 RCT(1,068명) SR+MA: rhBMP-2는 수평·수직 골흡수를 모두 유의하게 감소시키며, L-PRF는 자연치유 대비 유의한 차이 없으나 자가유래·비용효과적 대안으로 제시됨.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Oral Surgery, Oral Medicine, Oral Pathology and Oral Radiology, 138(6):702–718
- **Publication date**: 2024-07-18
- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (SR+MA)
- **Databases searched**: PubMed, EMBASE, MEDLINE, Cochrane CENTRAL, Google Scholar
- **Timeframe**: January 2010 – December 2023
- **Inclusion**: English-language human RCTs only
- **Sample**: 29 articles, 1,068 participants
- **Note**: Abstract-only — full text not retrieved

## 2. Key Contributions

- Six separate meta-analyses conducted, segmented by biologic agent type:
  1. Blood-derived growth factor preparations (general)
  2. L-PRF (leukocyte- and platelet-rich fibrin) alone vs natural healing
  3. rhBMP-2 (recombinant human bone morphogenetic protein-2) vs comparators
  4. rhBMP-2 vs L-PRF head-to-head
- First SR+MA to directly pit rhBMP-2 against L-PRF in the socket preservation context
- Confirms radiographic height and width bone resorption as primary outcomes; provides p-values for each agent subgroup

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PICO: extraction socket patients receiving biologic/regenerative graft materials vs control (natural healing or other biologics); outcomes = radiographic alveolar width and height change
- Six meta-analyses run on subgroups by agent type; heterogeneity not fully reported in abstract
- Risk of bias (RoB): 6 low / 8 moderate / 15 high net risk among 29 included studies (high-RoB majority — limits certainty)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Comparison | Horizontal (width) | Vertical (height) |
|---|---|---|
| Blood-derived preparations vs control | NS | p = .001 (significant) |
| L-PRF vs natural healing | NS | NS |
| rhBMP-2 vs control | p = .01 (significant) | p < .0008 (significant) |
| rhBMP-2 vs L-PRF (width) | p < .0001 (rhBMP-2 better) | p = .057 (NS) |

- rhBMP-2 outperforms all other biologic agents tested for horizontal preservation
- L-PRF does not significantly differ from natural healing in either dimension; described as autologous and cost-effective alternative rather than a superior intervention
- Blood-derived preparations (non-L-PRF) show selective benefit — significant for height only

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- 15 of 29 included studies rated high risk of bias — overall certainty likely low-to-moderate
- Abstract-only; detailed heterogeneity statistics (I²), specific graft compositions within "blood-derived" category, and follow-up durations not available
- Future RCTs with standardized CBCT protocols and longer follow-up (≥12 months) needed
- Cost-effectiveness modeling for rhBMP-2 vs autologous alternatives (L-PRF) not addressed

## 6. Related Work

- Avila-Ortiz 2019 (ridge preservation overview) — provides graft material backdrop this SR+MA extends into biologics

## 7. Glossary

- **L-PRF** (Leukocyte- and Platelet-Rich Fibrin, L-PRF): autologous platelet concentrate derived from centrifuged peripheral blood; contains growth factors (PDGF, TGF-β, VEGF)
- **rhBMP-2** (recombinant human Bone Morphogenetic Protein-2, rhBMP-2): synthetic osteoinductive protein; promotes osteoblast differentiation; typically delivered on absorbable collagen sponge
- **Blood-derived growth factor preparations**: umbrella term covering PRP, PRF, CGF, and other centrifuged blood concentrates excluding specifically L-PRF
- **Alveolar ridge preservation (ARP)**: socket grafting procedures performed immediately after tooth extraction to reduce post-extraction dimensional bone loss
