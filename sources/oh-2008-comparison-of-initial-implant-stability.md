## One-line Summary
Comparative study of initial implant stability (ISQ values) across different implant systems using resonance frequency analysis.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Research article
- **Institution**: Jeonbuk National University, Department of Periodontology
- **Year**: 2008
- **Journal**: Korean Journal of Aesthetic Periodontology (presumed based on collection)
- **Methodology**: In vivo measurement of initial implant stability

## 2. Key Contributions
- Provides comparative ISQ data for different implant systems
- Validates resonance frequency analysis as non-invasive primary stability assessment
- Establishes baseline stability values for clinical decision-making
- Demonstrates relationship between implant design and initial stability
- Supports early loading protocols based on objective stability measurements

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Assessment Tool**: Resonance Frequency Analysis (RFA) using Osstell device
- **Primary Outcome**: Implant Stability Quotient (ISQ) measurements
- **Measurement Timing**: At implant insertion
- **Variables**: Different implant systems/designs compared
- **Methods**: 
  - Transducers attached to implant during measurement
  - Frequency analysis generating ISQ values (0-100 scale)
  - Multiple measurements per implant to ensure reliability

## 4. Key Results and Findings
- ISQ measurements show variation among different implant systems
- Initial stability values correlate with implant design features
- RFA provides objective, repeatable measurement of primary stability
- ISQ values >60 generally indicate adequate primary stability for loading considerations
- Implant design, bone quality interaction influences initial stability

## 5. Clinical Implications
- Objective stability assessment guides loading protocol selection
- Low ISQ values (<60) warrant extended healing period before loading
- High ISQ values (>70) may allow early loading approaches
- RFA measurement facilitates predictable implant treatment planning
- System selection based on clinical and bone conditions to optimize primary stability
- Non-invasive monitoring eliminates need for invasive removal torque testing

## 6. Limitations and Future Work
- Cross-sectional assessment at single time point
- Longitudinal stability changes during osseointegration not captured
- Relationship between RFA and actual bone implant contact (histological) incompletely understood
- Sample size and patient demographics may limit generalizability
- Future studies needed on RFA predictive value for long-term survival

## 7. Glossary
- **Resonance Frequency Analysis (RFA, 공명주파수분석)**: Non-invasive measurement technique using vibration analysis
- **Implant Stability Quotient (ISQ)**: Numeric value (0-100) derived from RFA measurement
- **Primary stability (초기안정성)**: Mechanical stability at time of implant insertion
- **Osseointegration (골유착)**: Direct bone-implant contact without fibrous tissue
- **ISQ 60 threshold**: Empirical value generally considered adequate for functional loading
