## Why Ingested

2008년 한국 임플란트 ISQ 데이터의 원본 출처로, 3종 임플란트 시스템(3i Osseotite, Replace Select Tapered, ITI SLA)의 초기 안정성 변화 패턴을 직접 비교한 드문 임상 연구. [[implants/isq/kim-2009-implant-stability-various-implant-systems-clinical]] 등 후속 시스템 비교 연구의 초기 근거로 활용.

## One-line Summary
Prospective in vivo cohort (n=54 patients, 104 implants) comparing ISQ across 3 implant systems (3i Osseotite, Replace Select Tapered, ITI SLA) at baseline, 2 months, and 4 months: significant baseline differences converge by 2 months via a "catch-up" effect.

## 한줄요약
54명(임플란트 104개) 전향적 코호트: 3가지 임플란트 시스템(3i, Replace, ITI) 기저시 ISQ 유의차 있으나 2개월 시점에 수렴(catch-up 효과); 표면처리·형태가 ISQ 변화 패턴에 영향.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First Korean comparative ISQ study across three distinct implant systems with different surface treatments and macro-designs
- Demonstrates that baseline ISQ differences across systems disappear by 2 months (catch-up effect)
- Type A (3i Osseotite, acid-etched) shows stable/slightly declining ISQ vs. Types B and C which increase — implicating surface and design in ISQ trajectory
- Type C (ITI SLA) lowest baseline ISQ explained by 2.8 mm supracrestal shoulder height (corroborates Meredith et al.)
- Highlights that system-specific ISQ normative ranges are needed for loading decisions

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: Prospective in vivo clinical cohort
- **Setting**: Periodontology department, Chonbuk National University Dental Hospital
- **Period**: January–November 2007
- **Patients**: 54 patients (36 male / 18 female), mean age 49 years (range 18–77), 18 smokers
- **Implants**: 104 total
  - Type A: 3i Osseotite® (acid-etched Osseotite™ surface) — 36 implants
  - Type B: Replace® Select Tapered (Nobel Biocare; TiUnite™ electrochemical oxide, tapered design) — 48 implants
  - Type C: ITI (Institut Straumann; SLA® surface, 2.8 mm supracrestal shoulder) — 20 implants
- **Implant dimensions**: Length 10–15 mm, diameter 3.5–6.0 mm
- **Distribution**: Anterior 19 (18.3%), premolar 14 (13.5%), molar 71 (68.2%); maxilla 49, mandible 55
- **Surgical approach**: 58 one-stage (55.8%), 46 two-stage (44.2%); Type C all one-stage
- **GBR concomitant**: 35 implants (33.7%) — autogenous bone, Bio-Oss®, or mixture
- **RFA instrument**: Osstell™ Mentor (magnetic device; Integration Diagnostics AB, Göteborg, Sweden), smartpeg per system, 2 buccal readings per timepoint
- **Measurement schedule**: Surgery (baseline), 2 months, 4 months (one-stage); surgery + abutment connection at 4 months (two-stage)
- **Statistics**: Mann-Whitney U test (between-group ISQ); paired t-test (within-implant change)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Baseline ISQ (at implant placement)
| System | Mean ISQ | Range |
|---|---|---|
| Type A (3i Osseotite) | 76.2 | 48.5–87.0 |
| Type B (Replace Select) | 72.7 | 45.0–84.0 |
| Type C (ITI SLA) | 67.6 | 41.0–84.0 |

Inter-system difference at baseline: statistically significant (p<0.05)

### 2-Month ISQ
| System | Mean ISQ | Range |
|---|---|---|
| Type A | 76.8 | 55.5–86.0 |
| Type B | 77.8 | 67.0–85.5 |
| Type C | 76.4 | 64.0–82.5 |

Inter-system difference at 2 months: NOT significant — convergence achieved

### ISQ Change Baseline → 2 Months (matched implants: A=14, B=14, C=16)
- **Type B** and **Type C**: statistically significant increase (p<0.05) — lamellar bone apposition replacing woven bone
- **Type A**: slight decrease (not statistically significant)

### 4-Month Trend (36 matched implants: A=13, B=15, C=8)
- Type B and C: continued increasing trend
- Type A: essentially stable (flat trajectory)
- No implant failures during the observation period

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small per-group n, especially Type C at 4 months (n=8), limits temporal analysis
- No blinding; single centre
- Type C implants mostly prosthetically loaded by month 3 → 4-month RFA unavailable for most
- Osstell™ Mentor gives ISQ readings ~8–12 points higher than earlier electronic Osstell device (acknowledged; affects cross-study comparison)
- Lack of failed implants precludes evaluation of RFA as failure predictor
- System-specific normative ISQ ranges not formally established — need future multi-centre data

## 6. Related Work
- Ersanli et al. — 3-system comparison including ITI SLA; lower baseline ISQ for ITI (ISQ ~55–60 on older Osstell device = comparable to 67.6 on Mentor after ≈8–12 adjustment)
- Meredith et al. 1996 — RFA methodology, ISQ 0–100 scale
- Glauser et al. 2004 — RFA and failed implants; ISQ decline pattern
- Barewal et al. — SLA ISQ nadir at 3 weeks, plateau ≥6 weeks; lamellar bone explanation
- O'Sullivan et al. — tapered design and higher RFA in animal/cadaver models
- Roberts et al. — callus formation 0–6 weeks → lamellar bone deposition from week 6 (rabbit model)

## 7. Glossary
- **공명주파수분석 (Resonance Frequency Analysis, RFA)**: Non-invasive vibration-based implant stability measurement
- **임플란트 안정성 지수 (Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ)**: 0–100 RFA output; higher = more stable
- **초기 안정성 (Primary stability)**: Mechanical stability at insertion, before osseointegration
- **골유착 (Osseointegration)**: Direct structural bone–implant interface without fibrous tissue
- **Catch-up effect**: Convergence of initially disparate ISQ values across systems by 2 months
- **Supracrestal shoulder**: The 2.8 mm ITI shoulder above the crestal bone — reduces ISQ by extending effective transducer height
- **Smartpeg™**: System-specific magnetic peg for Osstell™ Mentor RFA measurement
