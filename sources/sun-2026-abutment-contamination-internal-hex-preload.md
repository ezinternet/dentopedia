## Why Ingested
임플란트 어버트먼트 연결부 오염이 예비하중(preload) 및 하중 후 예비하중 손실에 미치는 영향을 정량적으로 평가한 연구로, 기존의 스크류 오염 위주 연구와 달리 어버트먼트 자체의 오염에 집중. [[wiki/prosthetic-materials/bulaqi-2015-dynamic-nature-abutment-screw-retightening]]의 screw 재조임 역학 맥락을 어버트먼트 오염 측면에서 확장하며, [[wiki/prosthetic-materials/pardal-pelaez-2017-preload-loss-abutment-screws-dynamic-fatigue]]의 피로하중 예비하중 손실 데이터를 오염 변수 추가로 심화.

## One-line Summary
In vitro study (n=90, 3 groups) on internal hexagonal implant-abutment assemblies showing that even partial abutment contamination significantly reduces initial preload, while extensive contamination further accelerates cyclic-loading-induced preload loss.

## 한줄요약
체외 연구 (n=90) 에서 내부 육각형 임플란트-어버트먼트 연결부의 부분 오염만으로도 초기 예비하중 (Preload)이 유의하게 감소하고, 완전 오염은 반복하중 후 예비하중 손실 (Preload Loss)을 추가로 가속화함.

## 2. Key Contributions
1. First study to systematically quantify the differential mechanical effects of abutment contamination (not screw contamination) within internal hexagonal connections on both initial preload formation and cyclic-loading-induced preload loss.
2. Demonstrates two distinct mechanical failure stages: (1) contamination → reduced initial preload (all contaminated groups); (2) extensive contamination → accelerated preload loss under fatigue loading (CT6 only).
3. Observes that partial contamination (3 of 6 hex surfaces) and extensive contamination (6 of 6 hex surfaces) produce equivalent initial preload reduction, but differ in post-loading torque loss.
4. SEM evidence of localized surface wear at line angles in contaminated specimens vs. minimal wear in clean controls.
5. Clinical prevalence data: 265 custom abutments examined, with meaningful proportions showing partial or full hexagonal surface contamination under stereomicroscope.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Implants**: 90 titanium implants (Ø4.5 × 11.5 mm; Neo Biotech), embedded in auto-polymerizing acrylic resin per ISO 14801:2016
- **Abutments**: Prefabricated titanium abutments (Ø4.5 × 5.5 mm; Neobiotech)
- **Screws**: Titanium abutment screws (Ø1.5 × 13 mm; Neobiotech), tightened to 30 N·cm
- **Groups (n=30 each)**:
  - CT0: No contamination (control)
  - CT3: Contamination on 3 alternating non-adjacent internal hexagonal surfaces
  - CT6: Contamination on all 6 internal hexagonal surfaces
- **Contamination agent**: Type III dental stone (Snow Rock) + distilled water mixture, applied with microbrush
- **Torque measurement**: Calibrated digital torque device (MGT-12; Mark-10 Corp)
  - Pre-RTV: reverse torque value after initial tightening + 10-min settling + re-tightening + 5-min wait
  - Post-RTV: reverse torque value after cyclic loading
  - ΔRTV = pre-RTV − post-RTV (absolute torque loss)
- **Cyclic loading**: ISO 14801:2016 protocol (90 N, 2 Hz, 240,000 cycles)
- **SEM**: Subset of CT0 and CT6; gold-sputter coated; S-3000N Hitachi; ×150, ×500, ×2000
- **Statistics**: Shapiro-Wilk normality; paired t-tests (pre vs post within group, Bonferroni correction); one-way ANOVA + Tukey HSD for intergroup comparisons (α=0.05)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Pre-RTV** (initial preload formation):
  - CT0 > CT3 ≈ CT6 (P < .001 for contaminated vs clean; no significant CT3 vs CT6 difference)
  - Even partial contamination significantly impairs preload formation
- **Post-RTV** (after cyclic loading):
  - Decreased progressively: CT0 > CT3 > CT6 (P < .001 across all pairwise comparisons)
- **ΔRTV** (torque loss magnitude):
  - CT6 > CT0 ≈ CT3 (P < .001 for CT6 vs others; no significant CT0 vs CT3 difference)
  - Extensive contamination accelerates fatigue-induced preload loss
- **SEM findings**:
  - CT6: Localized surface wear at line angles of hexagonal surfaces
  - CT0: Minimal surface alteration after 240,000 cycles
- **Clinical observation**: 265 custom abutments at university hospital prosthodontics dept showed significant proportion with visible contamination under ×20 stereomicroscope — contamination is a real clinical occurrence, not a hypothetical

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- In vitro study — cannot fully replicate intraoral environment (saliva, temperature cycling, actual occlusal forces)
- Contamination simulated with dental stone only; clinical contamination may include diverse materials (polishing residue, silicone, blood)
- Prefabricated titanium abutments used (not custom CAD-CAM), which may differ in surface texture and fit from clinical custom abutments
- Single tightening torque (30 N·cm) tested; clinical practice may vary
- ISO 14801:2016 loading (90 N) represents average occlusal load — does not capture extreme bruxism/parafunction
- Long-term in vivo outcome data needed
- Study did not assess whether cleaning/decontamination protocols can restore initial mechanical integrity

## 7. Glossary
- **Preload (예비하중)**: Clamping force generated in screw joint upon tightening; essential for joint stability
- **RTV — Reverse Torque Value (역토크값)**: Torque required to loosen abutment screw; used as surrogate for preload
- **Pre-RTV**: RTV measured before cyclic loading (reflects initial preload formation)
- **Post-RTV**: RTV measured after cyclic loading (reflects residual preload)
- **ΔRTV**: Absolute difference between pre- and post-RTV (magnitude of preload loss)
- **Internal hexagonal connection (내부 육각형 연결)**: Implant-abutment interface design where hexagonal anti-rotation feature is internal to the implant body
- **Cyclic loading (반복하중)**: Repeated mechanical loading simulating masticatory forces per ISO 14801:2016
- **SEM — Scanning Electron Microscopy (주사전자현미경)**: High-resolution surface imaging technique
- **ISO 14801:2016**: International standard for fatigue testing of dental implant assemblies (90 N, 2 Hz)
