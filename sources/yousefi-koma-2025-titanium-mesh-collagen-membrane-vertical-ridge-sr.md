## Why Ingested

Part of a focused collection on vertical ridge augmentation (VRA). This SR pools the small body of RCT/case-series evidence on whether titanium meshes / Ti-reinforced PTFE membranes need an accompanying collagen membrane, directly synthesizing the trials ingested as [[implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/cucchi-2024-ptfe-mesh-ti-mesh-vertical-ridge-rct]] and [[implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/urban-2025-collagen-membrane-vertical-ridge-ptfe-mesh-rct]].

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (3 RCTs + 1 case series, 119 patients across 9 articles): adding a collagen membrane on top of titanium meshes or Ti-reinforced PTFE membranes caused no significant difference in vertical bone gain or surgical/healing complication rates, suggesting the collagen membrane may be unnecessary; more RCTs needed.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(RCT 3편 + 증례군 1편, 9개 논문 119명): 티타늄 mesh 또는 Ti-보강 PTFE 막 위에 콜라겐막을 추가해도 수직골증가나 수술/치유 합병증률에 유의한 차이 없음 — 콜라겐막이 불필요할 수 있으며 추가 RCT 필요.

## 1. Document Information

- Study type: Systematic review (qualitative; no meta-analysis), PRISMA 2020, PROSPERO CRD420251002615.
- Journal: Journal of Functional Biomaterials (MDPI), 2025;16:246. Published 4 July 2025.
- DOI: 10.3390/jfb16070246
- Databases: MEDLINE/PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, Embase, Cochrane Library, up to 5 June 2025.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR specifically comparing titanium meshes and/or perforated Ti-reinforced dPTFE membranes with vs without collagen membranes in GBR-based VRA.
- Addresses the clinical question of whether the supplementary collagen membrane (to restore cell-occlusion lost via mesh perforations) is actually needed.
- Includes both randomized and non-randomized human studies (case reports excluded).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PICO: GBR-based VRA (I) using Ti-based meshes/membranes with vs without collagen membranes (C), in adults with vertical maxillary/mandibular atrophy candidates for implants (P), to gain vertical height (O).
- Eligible designs: RCTs, controlled clinical trials, case-controls, case series; case reports excluded; buccal dehiscence defects excluded.
- Two independent reviewers (AY, AM), third reviewer (MK) for disagreements.
- Primary outcome: vertical alveolar bone gain. Secondary: surgical/healing complications, bone density/quality, horizontal bone gain, regeneration rate, implant survival, implant stability.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Included: 119 patients from 3 human RCTs and 1 case series, reported across 9 articles.
- The addition of collagen membranes caused no significant differences in vertical bone gain or in surgical/healing complication rates.
- Conclusion: adding a collagen membrane on top of titanium meshes and Ti-reinforced membranes might not be necessary in GBR-based VRA; further human RCTs are required for a reliable conclusion.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Very small evidence base (3 RCTs + 1 case series); no meta-analysis performed — qualitative synthesis only.
- Heterogeneous devices (Ti-mesh vs perforated Ti-reinforced dPTFE) pooled under one question.
- Authors explicitly call for more human RCTs before a definitive recommendation.

## 6. Related Work

- Urban et al. 2021 (perforated PTFE + CM case series); Alotaibi et al. 2023 (~16% GBR complication); resorbable-vs-nonresorbable membrane comparisons; the Cucchi 2024 and Urban 2025 RCTs in this collection.

## 7. Glossary

- VRA: vertical ridge augmentation
- GBR: guided bone regeneration
- dPTFE: dense polytetrafluoroethylene (perforated, Ti-reinforced)
- CM: collagen membrane (cross-linked or non-cross-linked)
- Cell-occlusion: GBR principle of excluding epithelial/connective tissue cells from the graft space
