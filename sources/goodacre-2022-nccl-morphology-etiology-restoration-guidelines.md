## One-line Summary
Narrative review synthesizing NCCL morphology, progression, prevalence (10-90%), multifactorial etiology, and stepwise clinical guidelines for restoration including sclerotic dentin texturing and extended 30-s etching.

## 한줄요약
NCCL의 형태·진행·유병률·병인을 종합하고 경화상아질 표면처리·30초 산부식 등 수복 가이드라인을 제시한 narrative review.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal of Prosthodontics 2023;32(S2):e1-e18 (online 2022). DOI 10.1111/jopr.13585.
- Open access (CC BY-NC-ND). Munoz is affiliated with Kerr Corporation (industry tie noted).

## 2. Key Contributions
- Distinguishes two morphologic types with different progression: saucer-shaped (progress mainly in height) vs V-shaped (height + depth); annual progression increases with depth/height (D/H) ratio.
- Quantifies progression: >50 µm/yr height progression common in low-D/H lesions; mean volume loss 0.36 mm3/yr (one study) to 1.50 +/- 0.92 mm3/yr (another).
- Summarizes prevalence heterogeneity (9.1-93%); most affected: premolars (maxillary in most studies, mandibular first premolars in others), facial surfaces.
- Positions abfraction as controversial: publications both support and challenge; occlusal-factor association inconsistent.
- Practical restorative protocol: texture sclerotic dentin with fine diamond, bevel enamel, optional mechanical retention, 30-s dentin etching, adhesive applied with light scrubbing 20 s, flowable liner + sculptable composite layering; SDF for carious NCCLs in lieu of restoration.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- PubMed-based narrative literature search across morphology, progression, prevalence, etiology, pathophysiology, restoration. No systematic protocol, no quality grading (not a SR).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Prevalence examples: 13.1%, 16.6%, 17.1% vs 61.7%, 62.5%, 63%; per-tooth involvement 5.7-17.2%; at least one severe lesion in 29.9% (ages 26-30) vs 42.6% (ages 46-50).
- Progression risk factors: low salivary buffering capacity, older age, higher dietary acid intake.
- Restoration recommendation: delay restorative treatment until justified by progression, sensitivity, esthetics, QoL, food retention.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative design; no effect-size pooling; guideline statements rest partly on expert opinion.
- Author affiliation with manufacturer (Kerr) is a potential COI for material recommendations.

## 6. Related Work
- Grippo 2012 (biocorrosion schema), Roberts 2022 (demineralization pathophysiology), Peumans 2020 (treatment decision review), Bezerra 2020 SR+MA (GIC vs composite in NCCLs).

## 7. Glossary
- D/H ratio: lesion depth-to-height ratio used as progression predictor.
- SDF: silver diamine fluoride.
- Sclerotic dentin: hypermineralized, tubule-occluded dentin typical of NCCL floors that resists etching.
