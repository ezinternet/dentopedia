## Why Ingested

General-healthcare foundational review establishing the teamwork → patient-safety link across acute/dynamic domains (OR, ICU, ED, trauma). Ingested as an anchor for the dental team-communication axis — it provides the canonical taxonomy of *why* teamwork matters (incident causation/prevention, provider perceptions/well-being, observed communication-coordination-leadership patterns) that is extrapolated to the dental clinic. Reinforces [[overviews/dental-workforce-delegation-skill-mix-overview]]. This is general-healthcare (not dental-specific) evidence.

## One-line Summary

Narrative literature review (general healthcare, acute/dynamic domains) synthesizing three evidence streams — critical-incident analyses, provider-perception studies, and observational performance studies — that together establish teamwork as a determinant of quality and patient safety.

## 1. Document Information

- Type: Narrative literature review (general healthcare; not dental-specific).
- Journal: Acta Anaesthesiologica Scandinavica 2009;53(2):143-51.
- Author: Manser T (single author).
- PMID: 19032571 ; DOI: 10.1111/j.1399-6576.2008.01717.x.
- Scope: highly dynamic / acute domains of healthcare — operating rooms, intensive care, emergency medicine, trauma and resuscitation.
- Note: abstract-only — full text not retrieved.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Frames teamwork as a measurable determinant of quality and safety, not a soft adjunct.
- Organizes the field into three convergent evidence streams (incidents, perceptions, observed behaviors).
- Connects teamwork and leadership style to staff well-being, and well-being to the capacity to deliver safe care.
- Sets the research agenda: validated team-performance assessment instruments + integrative theoretical models → better team training and work-system design.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Narrative review of the literature (no formal systematic search/meta-analysis reported in the abstract). Evidence is grouped into three analytic categories: (1) critical-incident/adverse-event causation studies, (2) provider-perception and attitude studies, (3) observational studies of teamwork behavior vs. clinical performance.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Three evidence streams supporting the teamwork–patient-safety relationship:

1. **Critical incidents / adverse events** — teamwork plays an important role in both the *causation* and the *prevention* of critical incidents and adverse events.
2. **Provider perceptions** — staff perceptions of teamwork and attitudes toward safety-relevant team behavior relate to quality/safety of care; perceptions of teamwork and leadership style associate with staff well-being (which may in turn affect ability to provide safe care).
3. **Observational teamwork behaviors** — studies of high clinical performance identify recurring patterns of **communication, coordination, and leadership** that support effective teamwork.

(No pooled effect sizes — narrative synthesis; abstract-only.)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative (non-systematic) review; no quantitative synthesis.
- Evidence drawn from acute/dynamic domains — direct transfer to ambulatory/dental settings is an extrapolation.
- Stated future need: develop and validate team-performance assessment instruments and integrative theoretical models linking the three streams, to improve team training and clinical work-system design.

## 6. Related Work

- [[overviews/dental-workforce-delegation-skill-mix-overview]] — the dental synthesis this general-healthcare review anchors.
- Later systematic-review-and-meta-analysis work (e.g., Schmutz 2019) quantifies the teamwork → performance effect that this review described qualitatively.

## 7. Glossary

- **Dynamic / acute domains**: high-tempo, high-acuity clinical settings (OR, ICU, ED, trauma) where teamwork failures rapidly become safety events.
- **Critical incident**: an event with potential or actual patient harm, used to trace contributing factors.
- **Team-performance assessment instrument**: validated tool for rating team behaviors (communication, coordination, leadership) against outcomes.
