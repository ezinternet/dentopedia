## Why Ingested

Tissue-level implant crestal bone behavior is a recurring clinical question; this pre-clinical study provides BIC and fBIC benchmarks for a novel fully tapered self-cutting tissue-level design (Straumann TLX) across three diameters in moderate bone quality, directly extending the evidence base reviewed in [[wiki/implants/atieh-2025-tissue-vs-bone-level-peri-implantitis-sr-ma]] regarding tissue-level implant crestal bone preservation advantages.

## One-line Summary

Animal (minipig, n=48 implants, 12 wk) non-inferiority study comparing a novel fully tapered self-cutting tissue-level implant (Straumann TLX) to the established Straumann Standard Plus across small/medium/large diameters in moderate bone: BIC was non-inferior (≈45–61% test vs 52–55% control), fBIC showed superior crestal bone maintenance for test implants, and insertion torques were markedly higher for the tapered design.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Non-inferiority of BIC confirmed**: The Straumann TLX (test) achieved BIC statistically non-inferior to Straumann Standard Plus (control) across all three diameter groups at 12 weeks.
2. **Superior crestal bone maintenance (fBIC)**: Test implants showed consistently higher (more coronal) fBIC values vs control implants in all diameter groups; statistically significant for small-diameter implants (p = 0.047).
3. **High primary stability**: Insertion torque in polyurethane bone surrogates was 4–5× higher for test implants (23–54 Ncm) than control implants (5–11 Ncm), supporting candidacy for immediate/early placement.
4. **Microgap relocation advantage**: The tissue-level design relocates the implant-abutment microgap away from crestal bone, a design principle credited with favorable crestal bone outcomes.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Model**: Edentulous mandibular Sinclair Minipig model (n=8 female, 20–24 months old, 40–60 kg). Mandibular premolars (P2–P4) and first molar (M1) extracted 12 weeks prior to implant placement. Implants placed in fully healed sites to standardize baseline bone.

**Bone quality**: Type 2/3 bone (moderate), deliberately selected to challenge the novel design.

**Implants**:
- **Test (TLX)**: Straumann TLX — slim core, fully tapered endosseous design, wide-neck (WN Ø4.8 or Ø6.5 mm) supracrestal shoulder; self-cutting thread; VeloDrills osteotomy system. Three sizes: small (thread Ø3.75 mm, core Ø3.5 mm), medium (Ø4.5/3.5 mm), large (Ø5.5/4.5 mm).
- **Control (SP)**: Straumann Standard Plus — parallel-walled, regular/wide-neck design; conventional Twist drill PRO osteotomy.

**Groups**: 3 diameter subgroups (small/medium/large); split-mouth design; 8 implants per group (n=7 for control small-diameter due to anatomical constraint in one animal).

**Healing**: 12 weeks, submerged (titanium cover screws, 0 mm height).

**Outcomes**:
- Primary: BIC (% of endosseal implant surface in bone contact, histomorphometry)
- Secondary: fBIC (distance from rough-smooth surface margin to most apical crestal bone contact, μm; positive = crestal, negative = bone loss below reference)
- Bench: Maximum insertion torque in polyurethane plates (Ncm)

**Histology**: Non-decalcified methyl methacrylate sections (500 μm cut, ground to 30–50 μm), stained with paragon (toluidine blue + basic fuchsin), central bucco-lingual sections evaluated.

**Statistics**: Wilcoxon signed-rank tests for paired comparisons; mixed linear regression model adjusted for animal (random effect), mandible side, and mesio-distal position; Dunnett-Hsu adjustment for multiple comparisons; non-inferiority testing via 90% two-tailed CI (one-tailed 95%).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**BIC (%)** — mean ± SD:

| Diameter | Test (TLX) | Control (SP) | p (Wilcoxon) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Small | 61.30 ± 10.63 | 54.46 ± 18.31 | 0.99 |
| Medium | 60.91 ± 14.42 | 54.68 ± 9.16 | 0.55 |
| Large | 45.60 ± 14.67 | 52.52 ± 14.76 | 0.31 |

All BIC comparisons met non-inferiority criteria (average effects within 90% CI tolerance range).

**fBIC (μm)** — mean ± SD (negative = apical to reference margin):

| Diameter | Test (TLX) | Control (SP) | p (Wilcoxon) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Small | −19.01 ± 475.93 | −1026.23 ± 1603.23 | 0.047* |
| Medium | −136.85 ± 355.98 | −376.35 ± 478.01 | 0.25 |
| Large | −328.49 ± 379.04 | −764.94 ± 363.48 | 0.078 |

fBIC values were consistently higher (better crestal bone level) for test implants; non-inferiority accepted for all diameter groups. Large-diameter group non-inferiority confidence interval (5.59 to 824.00 μm) excluded zero on lower bound.

**Insertion torque (Ncm)**:

| Diameter | Test (TLX) | Control (SP) |
|---|---|---|
| Small | 23.0 ± 1.3 | 5.3 ± 3.1 |
| Medium | 53.8 ± 2.4 | 10.8 ± 5.9 |
| Large | 40.2 ± 1.3 | 8.7 ± 4.6 |

**Histologic findings**: All implants osseointegrated; no fibrous encapsulation in any specimen. No peri-implant inflammation at termination. Qualitative differences noted: control implants surrounded homogenously by bone apically; test implants showed some qualitative variation around apical regions related to osteotomy contour mismatch (under-/over-contoured osteotomy).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

1. **Pilot scale**: 8 animals, 48 implants — limited statistical power; single time point (12 wk).
2. **Non-randomized design**: Not a randomized controlled study; site allocation alternated across animals to mitigate position effects.
3. **Confounded variables**: Test vs control differed in implant shape, thread design, AND osteotomy protocol simultaneously — impossible to isolate which factor drove fBIC differences.
4. **Bench test simplification**: Insertion torque measured in polyurethane plates (PU), not biological bone — cannot directly extrapolate to clinical insertion torque values.
5. **No immediate placement protocol**: Used fully healed bone sites to standardize baseline; the primary clinical indication (immediate placement) was not tested in vivo.
6. **Animal-to-human translation**: Minipig bone metabolism is similar to humans but not identical; clinical translation requires RCT confirmation.
7. **Short observation period**: 12 weeks captures osseointegration but not long-term crestal bone stability.

## 7. Glossary

- **BIC (Bone-to-Implant Contact, 골-임플란트 접촉률)**: Percentage of the endosseous implant surface perimeter in direct contact with bone, measured histomorphometrically.
- **fBIC (First Bone-to-Implant Contact, 최초 골-임플란트 접촉점)**: Distance from the implant's rough-smooth surface junction to the most apical crestal bone-implant contact; negative values indicate bone loss below the reference margin.
- **Tissue-level implant (조직 수준 임플란트)**: Implant design where the implant-abutment microgap is positioned supragingivally / at mucosal level rather than at bone level.
- **TLX (Straumann TLX)**: Straumann's fully tapered self-cutting tissue-level implant with slim core and protruding thread geometry for primary stability maximization.
- **Non-inferiority testing (비열등성 검정)**: Statistical approach to confirm a new device is not worse than a reference by more than a pre-specified tolerance margin.
- **fBIC negative value**: Bone crest is apical to the rough-smooth surface junction — indicates crestal bone resorption below the reference landmark.
- **Active bone management**: Design principle in TLX thread geometry where bone is condensed laterally during insertion, limiting excessive cortical stress.
- **VeloDrills**: Straumann's drill system designed for use with TLX implants, featuring adapted sequences for tapered osteotomy preparation.
