## 2. Key Contributions

1. **KM width threshold**: ≥2 mm keratinized mucosa (KM) is the minimum required to facilitate oral hygiene, prevent plaque accumulation, reduce peri-implant mucositis incidence, and maintain marginal bone stability.
2. **PIS thickness threshold**: ≥2 mm peri-implant soft tissue (PIS) thickness is required to achieve esthetic appearance, mask prosthetic material color, and prevent marginal bone resorption triggered by biologic width establishment.
3. **Biologic width**: PIS forms a predetermined thickness of 2.5–4 mm (biologic width); if PIS <2 mm, bone resorption is initiated to create adequate space.
4. **Technique taxonomy**: Clearly classifies autogenous grafts (FGG vs CTG), pedicle grafts, and soft tissue substitutes with their respective indications.
5. **Graft biology**: Details plasmatic imbibition (days 1–3) → transvascularization (day 3–4 onward) as critical phases; conditions required: rigid immobilization, intimate graft-bed contact, adequate recipient site vascularity.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

This is a narrative review / educational book chapter. The authors:
- Synthesize published evidence on PIS augmentation indications and techniques
- Present each technique with step-by-step procedural description and clinical photographs
- Reference primary RCTs, case series, and systematic reviews (numbered bibliography)
- No meta-analysis, no PICO framework, no formal risk-of-bias assessment

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Technique Overview

| Technique | Primary Indication | Donor Site | Key Advantage |
|---|---|---|---|
| FGG (Free Gingival Graft) | Increase KM width | Palate / maxillary tuberosity | Predictable keratinization |
| CTG (Connective Tissue Graft) | Increase PIS volume/thickness | Palate (subepithelial) | Superior esthetic (color match) |
| sCTG (Subepithelial CTG) | Volume + recession coverage | Palate (trap-door/single-incision) | Dual blood supply → less shrinkage |
| De-epithelized FGG | KM width + volume | Palate FGG → extraoral de-epithelization | Larger graft volume |
| Pedicle graft (rotational/pedicle CTG) | Single-site augmentation | Adjacent soft tissue (blood supply preserved) | Minimal shrinkage, no separate donor site |
| APF (Apically Positioned Flap) | KM width increase (alternative to FGG) | Local tissue | Avoids palatal harvest |
| Roll technique | PIS volume (ridge augmentation) | Local palatal connective tissue (folded) | Avoids second surgical site |
| Tunneling | PIS thickness / recession coverage | CTG inserted via tunnel | Minimal flap trauma |
| Xenogeneic collagen matrix | KM width / volume | Commercial (no harvest) | No donor-site morbidity |

### FGG Key Points
- Harvested from palate or maxillary tuberosity
- Primarily increases KM width; less effective for volume augmentation
- Surface epithelium imprints the color of palatal mucosa → esthetic compromise in visible regions
- Thickness: ideally uniform to ensure intimate bed contact

### CTG Key Points
- Gold standard for volume augmentation and recession coverage
- Harvested via primary flap preparation (subepithelial / sCTG) or de-epithelization of FGG
- sCTG: trap-door or single-incision techniques — preserves palatal epithelium for faster donor healing
- Adipose and glandular tissue must be dissected from graft (impairs nourishment)

### Pedicle Grafts
- Graft remains attached to donor site on one side → blood supply preserved
- Less postoperative shrinkage than free grafts
- Used when adjacent tissue volume permits

### APF (Apically Positioned Flap)
- Splits mucosa; positions flap apically to expose periosteum coronally
- Periosteum keratinizes → increases KM width without palatal harvest
- Often combined with FGG or CTG for volume

### Roll Technique
- Palatal connective tissue is deepithelialized and "rolled" under buccal flap
- Increases ridge volume without separate donor site wound

### Tunneling
- A tunnel is prepared in the recipient tissue; CTG inserted without vertical incisions
- Reduced postoperative morbidity; good esthetic outcome for recession coverage around implants

### Xenogeneic Soft Tissue Substitutes
- Collagen matrices (e.g., Mucograft, Fibrogide) replace autogenous grafts
- Eliminate donor-site morbidity
- Evidence suggests comparable KM width gain to FGG; volume gain may be inferior to CTG
- Useful in patients refusing or unable to undergo palatal harvest

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Book chapter format: no formal systematic search, no risk-of-bias assessment
- Narrative evidence synthesis — susceptible to selection bias
- Long-term outcomes (>5 years) not extensively covered
- Comparative RCT data for newer substitutes (collagen matrices) still maturing at time of writing
- No quantitative pooling of outcomes across techniques

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| KM (Keratinized Mucosa) | Gingiva-like mucosa immediately around implant; resists mechanical trauma |
| PIS (Peri-Implant Soft Tissue) | Soft tissue envelope surrounding implant abutment/crown |
| FGG (Free Gingival Graft) | Full-thickness epithelium + connective tissue graft; harvested free from palate |
| CTG (Connective Tissue Graft) | Subepithelial connective tissue graft; gold standard for volume augmentation |
| sCTG (Subepithelial CTG) | CTG harvested with preservation of overlying palatal epithelium |
| APF (Apically Positioned Flap) | Mucosal flap displaced apically to create KM through periosteal exposure |
| Biologic width | Junctional epithelium + connective tissue attachment; 2.5–4 mm around implants |
| Plasmatic imbibition | Initial avascular nourishment of graft via plasma diffusion (days 1–3) |
| Transvascularization | Ingrowth of new blood vessels from recipient bed into graft (day 3–4+) |
| Pedicle graft | Graft remaining attached to donor site on one side, preserving native blood supply |
| Xenogeneic collagen matrix | Commercially derived (porcine/bovine) collagen substitute for autogenous soft tissue grafts |
| Roll technique | Local connective tissue folded and tucked under buccal flap to augment ridge volume |
| Tunneling | Minimally invasive recipient-site preparation allowing CTG insertion without vertical incisions |
