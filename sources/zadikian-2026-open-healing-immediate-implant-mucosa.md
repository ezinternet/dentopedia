## Why Ingested

Existing [[immediate-implant/esthetic-soft-tissue/pitman-2022-immediate-implant-provisionalization-sr-ma]] establishes that immediate provisionalization (IPP) of immediate implants supports midfacial soft tissue, but does not isolate the "open healing" socket-management variable (transmucosal seal + bone graft, NO barrier membrane, NO primary flap closure). This SR+MA (Zadikian 2026) extends that synthesis by asking specifically whether *open healing* — provisional/customized abutment as the only socket seal over a particulate bone graft — destabilizes the peri-implant mucosa, finding no penalty vs primary closure (MML SMD −0.26, NS).

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (12 studies, 431 patients / 438 implants; 4 RCTs) finds open healing — immediate transmucosal non-occlusal provisionalization + particulate bone graft without a barrier membrane or primary flap closure — yields limited midfacial mucosal change (MML SMD −0.26, 95% CI −0.71 to 0.18, NS) and comparable papilla/MBL/survival vs primary flap closure.

## 한줄요약

체계적 고찰·메타분석(12편, 환자 431명·임플란트 438개, RCT 4편): 즉시식립 시 비폐쇄 치유(차폐막 없이 임시보철/맞춤치유지대주로만 입자형 골이식재를 봉합 없이 덮는 방식, open healing)는 일차봉합 대비 정중협측 점막레벨(Midfacial Mucosal Level, MML) 변화가 미미(SMD −0.26, 유의차 없음)하고 유두·변연골소실·생존율도 동등했다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Isolates the **open-healing variable** (transmucosal seal over a particulate bone graft, **no barrier membrane, no primary flap closure**) by excluding protocols that add gingival grafts / soft-tissue substitutes — so the soft-tissue result is attributable to socket management, not to additional grafting.
- Demonstrates that omitting primary closure does **not** penalize midfacial mucosal stability vs primary flap closure (pooled MML SMD −0.26, NS).
- Signals a **mesial papilla advantage** for the open-healing/provisionalization arm (SMD 0.76, 95% CI 0.18–1.34, p=0.01), while distal papilla, MBL, KMW, and survival are comparable.
- Documents that **bone-graft particles become encapsulated in supracrestal connective tissue** without reported inflammatory/soft-tissue complications — flags this as an under-studied biological question.
- All 12 studies were **flapless**; 92% anterior sites — defines the evidence's current boundary (selected intact-buccal-wall, ideal-soft-tissue, mostly esthetic-zone cases).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Design: systematic review + meta-analysis; only RCTs entered the meta-analysis.
- PICO: P = systemically healthy adults ≥18 yr, single-tooth immediate implant; I = open healing (immediate transmucosal non-occlusal provisionalization, i.e., provisional restoration or customized healing abutment, + bone grafting, no membrane/no primary closure); C = bone grafting with primary flap closure; O = primary Midfacial Mucosal Level (MML); secondary = papilla, mucosa thickness, buccal horizontal alteration, PES, KMW, MBL, BBT, survival.
- Eligible designs: parallel RCTs + prospective case series (≥5 patients) with follow-up through final restoration. Excluded: no soft-tissue data; gingival graft / soft-tissue substitute protocols; retrospective/case report/review/abstract/opinion; **immediate functional occlusal loading** (provisional in occlusal contact <48 h) — so findings reflect soft-tissue management, not mechanical loading.
- RoB: Cochrane RoB2 (RCTs), Joanna Briggs Institute (cohort/case-control/case series); level of evidence by Oxford OCEBM.
- Statistics: Cohen κ for reviewer agreement; meta-analysis when >1 RCT with homogeneous data; χ² (α=0.05) + I² for heterogeneity (high >50%, low <25%); MML, mesial/distal papilla height, MBL pooled in RevMan 5.4.1 as standardized mean differences (SMD).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Screening: 362 records → 35 full-text → **12 included** (431 patients, 438 implants; 277 immediate implants with provisionalization + bone graft). Inter-reviewer κ = 0.91 (95% CI 0.78–1.04, p<0.001). 42% (5/12) from Italy.
- Evidence grade (OCEBM): 3×1B (RCT), 1×2B (cohort), 1×3B (case-control), 6×4 (case series). Discussion narrative cites 4 RCTs. Mostly **low RoB**.
- Follow-up: mean 10.6 months (95% CI 9.84–11.49); >50% of implants followed only through osseointegration (≤6 mo); only Cosyn 2011 reached 3 years.
- Grafts: 52% xenogenic bovine, 18% synthetic, 15% allograft, 8% autogenous.
- **Primary — MML**: SMD −0.26 (95% CI −0.71 to 0.18, p=0.25; 3 RCTs, 6–12 mo), no difference; I²=6% (low). Perez 2020 was the dissent — less recession with standard (control) abutment (+0.1±0.5 mm) than customized (test, −0.2±0.4 mm). Cosyn stable over 3 yr.
- **Mesial papilla**: SMD 0.76 (95% CI 0.18–1.34, p=0.01) favoring test (2 RCTs, I²=0%). **Distal papilla**: SMD 0.40 (95% CI −0.16 to 0.96, p=0.16), NS. PES 1.44–1.85, stable, no group difference (5 studies).
- **MBL**: SMD 0.14 (95% CI −0.32 to 0.59, p=0.55), NS (3 RCTs, I²=10%). Perez test 0.3±0.6 mm at 12 mo; Spinato grafted 0.94±0.51 vs non-grafted 0.90±0.49 mm.
- **KMW**: single study, limited change, no group difference. **BBT**: single study, no difference with vs without provisionalization. Mucosa thickness (Chu 2015): graft + provisional gave greater thickness than other sites.
- **Implant survival**: 92–100% over 4–58 months; no significant control-vs-test differences when reported.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 12 studies, only 3–4 RCTs; cohort/case-series predominance limits causal inference. Several outcomes (KMW, BBT, mucosa thickness) rest on a single study.
- Short follow-up — most ≤6 months (osseointegration window only); a single 3-year dataset.
- Anterior + posterior sites pooled despite location-specific resorption; 92% anterior, so generalizability to molars/degraded sites is weak.
- By design, gingival-graft / soft-tissue-substitute protocols were excluded — so the page speaks only to bone-graft-only open healing, not to CTG-augmented esthetic-zone protocols.
- Biological gap: bone particles encapsulated in supracrestal connective tissue need histologic/in-vitro/in-vivo study; effect on gingival phenotype unknown.
- Authors call for high-quality RCTs, especially in compromised buccal-wall and posterior scenarios.

## 6. Related Work

- Prior SR+MA on immediate-implant immediate provisionalization soft-tissue stability (Pitman 2022) — Zadikian's MML result aligns with and extends it by isolating the membrane-free open-healing variable.

## 7. Glossary

- **Open healing** — transmucosal management of the extraction socket with a provisional restoration or customized healing abutment as the only socket seal, WITHOUT primary flap closure and WITHOUT a barrier membrane (secondary-intention healing over the bone graft).
- **MML (Midfacial Mucosal Level)** — vertical position of the facial peri-implant mucosa margin; primary esthetic stability outcome.
- **Immediate provisionalization** — connection within 1 week of a transmucosal component (provisional or healing abutment) kept out of occlusion (distinct from immediate *loading*, in occlusal contact <48 h, which was excluded).
- **KMW (Keratinized Mucosa Width)** — apical-coronal distance from gingival crest to mucogingival junction.
- **PES (Pink Esthetic Score)** — peri-implant soft-tissue esthetic index.
- **MBL / BBT** — Marginal Bone Loss / Buccal Bone Thickness.
- **SMD** — standardized mean difference (pooled effect size used here because outcomes were measured on differing scales).
