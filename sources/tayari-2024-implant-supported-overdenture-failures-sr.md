## Why Ingested
임플란트 지지 피개의치 (Implant-Supported Overdenture, IOD)의 실패 위험인자를 체계적으로 정리한 SR이 기존 wiki에 없어 인제스트. [[complete-denture/prasad-2024-attachment-systems-mandibular-implant]]의 부착 시스템 비교를 보완하는 실패 위험인자 관점을 제공하며, 악궁·임플란트 수·부하 프로토콜별 실패율 벤치마크를 확립.

## One-line Summary
PRISMA systematic review (20 studies: 13 RCTs, 1 prospective CT, 3 prospective, 2 retrospective, 1 cross-sectional; 2018–2023) of implant-supported overdenture failures: variables associated with failure include the patient's general condition, edentulous arch (maxilla vs mandible), number of implants, and loading protocol; failures are minimized by careful case selection, regular follow-up, and clinician skill.

## 한줄요약
체계적 문헌고찰 (20편, 2018–2023): 임플란트 지지 피개의치 실패 위험인자는 전신질환, 상악 vs 하악 악궁, 임플란트 수, 부하 프로토콜이며 — 정기 유지관리와 술자 숙련도로 실패율을 최소화할 수 있다.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Maps the **factor landscape** of implant-supported OD failure rather than focusing on a single intervention.
- Covers RCTs and observational studies, giving a pragmatic view of clinical reality.
- Reaffirms the McGill / York consensus that mandibular two-implant OD remains a robust default, but qualifies it with risk factors that warrant attention.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Databases: MEDLINE/PubMed, Cochrane Library, LILACS + manual.
- Inclusion: CT, RCT, observational studies (2018–2023).
- Two independent screeners; Cochrane Collaboration RoB tool.
- 20 studies retained: 13 RCT, 1 prospective CT, 3 prospective, 2 retrospective, 1 cross-sectional.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Patient-level factors**: general medical condition (diabetes, smoking, bisphosphonates, head/neck irradiation) raise risk.
- **Arch**: maxillary OD shows higher failure than mandibular OD across studies; bone quality and number of implants matter more in the maxilla.
- **Implant number**: 2-implant mandibular OD is well-supported; maxillary OD typically needs ≥4 implants; splinting (bar) considered in low bone density.
- **Loading protocol**: immediate/early loading increases failure risk under poor primary stability; conventional loading remains safest in compromised hosts.
- **Attachment-related**: prosthodontic maintenance is a frequent "complication" but rarely causes implant failure itself.
- **Operator / follow-up**: regular maintenance visits and timely attachment service materially reduce failure rate.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Heterogeneity in failure definitions (implant failure vs prosthetic failure vs complication).
- Short follow-ups in many included studies; long-term predictors under-reported.
- Few studies comparing patient-level systemic conditions head-to-head.

## 6. Related Work
- Sutariya 2021, Patel 2025, Aung 2025: focus on attachment systems.
- Di Francesco 2021: maxillary OD number-of-implants question.
- McGill (2002) / York (2009) consensus statements on mandibular 2-implant OD as standard of care.

## 7. Glossary
- **OD failure**: Implant loss, OD loss, or major complication leading to prosthesis abandonment.
- **Prosthodontic maintenance**: Routine service (attachment matrix/insert change, reline, repair).
- **Conventional loading**: Functional loading after standard healing (≥3 mo mandible, ≥6 mo maxilla).
