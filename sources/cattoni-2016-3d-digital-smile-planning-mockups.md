## Why Ingested

기존 [[veneers/ali-2023-conventional-minimally-invasive-veneers-sr]]와 [[veneers/chandode-2026-no-preparation-conventional-veneers-clinical-considerations]]가 최소삭제 비니어의 임상 결과와 준비 원칙을 다루지만, 그 최소삭제를 가능케 하는 **mockup-guided / APT-style 사전평가 임시수복(aesthetic pre-evaluative temporary)** 워크플로우 자체를 다룬 페이지는 부재했다. 본 임상연구(Cattoni 2016, n=28, 108 PLVs)는 전통적 wax-up/실리콘 키 mockup을 **완전 디지털 3D-DSD → CAD-CAM 밀링 PMMA mockup**으로 대체하고, 그 mockup을 직접 prep guide로 사용해 법랑질 한정 최소삭제를 시행하는 프로토콜을 검증하여, 최소삭제 비니어를 "어떻게 예측가능하게 만드는가"의 디지털 워크플로우 근거를 제공한다.

## One-line Summary

Prospective clinical study (n=28 patients, 108 IPS e.max PLVs, 2-year follow-up) of a fully digital 3D Digital Smile Planning (3D-DSP) workflow: 2D facial photos + intraoral STL scan are fused into a 3D smile design, a CAD-CAM-milled PMMA mockup is fabricated and used as a position guide for enamel-only minimally invasive preparation; 2-year results showed 1 fracture, 2 sensitivities, 1 recession (0.9% each at veneer level) and no debonding/chipping/microleakage, with high patient satisfaction (mockup test rated very effective by 85.7%).

## 한줄요약

전향 임상연구(환자 28명, IPS e.max 도재 라미네이트 비니어 108개, 2년 추적): 안면 2D 사진과 구내 STL 스캔을 합쳐 3차원 스마일 디자인(3D Digital Smile Planning, 3D-DSP)을 만들고, CAD-CAM으로 밀링한 PMMA 목업을 법랑질 한정 최소삭제(Minimally Invasive)의 위치 가이드로 사용 — 2년 후 파절 1·지각과민 2·치은퇴축 1개(비니어 단위 각 0.9%)뿐이고 탈락·치핑·미세누출 0건, 목업 테스트 만족도 "매우 효과적" 85.7%.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Describes and clinically tests a **total digital smile planning (3D-DSP)** workflow that removes the analog laboratory steps (stone model, manual diagnostic wax-up, silicone-key mockup transfer)
- Fuses 2D facial/intraoral photographs (2D-Digital Smile System) with an intraoral-scanner STL file into a single 3D design environment (3D-Digital Smile System) for virtual previsualization
- Uses a **CAD-CAM-milled PMMA mockup as a direct prep guide** (cemented with spot-etch) to keep minimally invasive preparation confined to enamel and to maintain margins on enamel
- Reports 2-year clinical outcomes for 108 IPS e.max PLVs cemented on vital teeth, with very low complication rates
- Documents a patient-satisfaction questionnaire (VAS) for both the digital previsualization and the physical mockup test

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Participants**: 28 adult patients (9 male, 19 female), aged 19–53 (mean 36); no oral, periodontal, or systemic disease
- **Restorations**: 108 porcelain laminate veneers (PLVs) total — maxilla 84 (anterior 54, posterior 30), mandible 24 (anterior 10, posterior 14); all on vital teeth
- **Workflow steps**:
  1. Radiological, phonetic, and static/dynamic occlusal evaluation
  2. Three intra- and extraoral digital images taken with special calibrated eyewear (Digital Smile System Srl, Italy)
  3. Intraoral scan (Scanner 3D Progress, MHT, Italy) of maxilla and mandible (open + occlusal) → STL file
  4. Photos processed in **2D-Digital Smile System** software; STL + 2D design combined in **3D-Digital Smile System** (EGS Srl) to generate a virtual 3D prosthetic design
  5. After patient approval of the 3D view, a **PMMA mockup (Bredent)** was milled on a CAM system (Zirkonzahn)
  6. Mockup tried in mouth for esthetic consent; **cemented via spot-etch technique** and used as a **position guide for minimally invasive enamel preparation** (margins kept on enamel)
  7. Definitive impressions via double-cord technique with the intraoral scanner
  8. PLVs (IPS e.max, Ivoclar Vivadent) fabricated by CAD/CAM (Zirkonzahn)
  9. Adhesive cementation: 78 with Variolink Veneer (Ivoclar Vivadent), 30 with Clearfil Esthetic Cement (Kuraray)
- **Follow-up**: 2 years
- **Patient-reported outcome**: VAS questionnaire grading the design previsualization and the mockup test as very effective / effective / ineffective

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Preoperative indications** (by patient): discoloration 21.4%, bruxism 14.3%, abrasion 21.4%, crowding 14.3%, caries 14.3%, trauma 7.2%, diastema 7.1% (note: percentages tabulated per-patient and per-tooth differ in the source tables)
- **2-year complications (n=108 veneers)**: fracture 1 (0.9%), sensitivity 2 (1.8%), gingival recession 1 (0.9%); chipping 0, debonding 0, microleakage 0, secondary caries 0, discoloration 0, root canal therapy 0
- **Patient satisfaction (VAS)**:
  - Digital smile design previsualization: very effective 18 (64.3%), effective 10 (35.7%), ineffective 0
  - Milling mockup test: very effective 24 (85.7%), effective 4 (14.3%), ineffective 0
- **Interpretation**: the fully digital data transfer (clinical 3D planning → CAD/CAM mockup) is reported as simpler, faster, and more predictable than the analog wax-up/silicone-key route, with reduced operator error and reduced handling of hard dental tissue

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Small sample** (n=28 patients) — authors explicitly call for larger studies to confirm
- Single-arm design with **no concurrent control group** (no head-to-head vs analog APT/wax-up workflow); satisfaction is self-reported VAS without blinding
- Short follow-up (2 years) relative to PLV lifespan; survival/complication trends beyond 2 years not captured
- Proprietary, equipment-dependent workflow (specific scanners, DSD software, milling units) limits generalizability
- No quantitative measure of enamel preservation or preparation depth reported despite the "minimally invasive" claim

## 7. Glossary

- **3D-DSP / 3D-DSD (3D Digital Smile Planning / Design)**: workflow that fuses 2D facial-photo smile design with a 3D intraoral STL scan into a single virtual environment to previsualize the planned prosthetic outcome before any tooth reduction
- **PLV (Porcelain Laminate Veneer)**: thin bonded ceramic facing restoration for the esthetic zone
- **Mockup**: physical replica of the planned restorations placed in the mouth; here CAD-CAM-milled in PMMA rather than made from a wax-up via silicone key
- **APT (Aesthetic Pre-evaluative Temporary)**: Gürel/Coachman technique of using the approved mockup intraorally as both an esthetic preview and a depth/position guide for preparation
- **Spot-etch technique**: localized acid etching to temporarily retain a mockup intraorally without full bonding
- **STL (Stereolithography file)**: standard 3D surface-mesh format output by intraoral scanners
- **PMMA (Polymethyl methacrylate)**: acrylic resin milled for the trial mockup
- **Double-cord technique**: two-retraction-cord gingival displacement method for definitive impressions
