## Why Ingested

기존 GBR 차폐막 페이지들은 비흡수성(티타늄 메시)과 흡수성(콜라겐)의 trade-off — 메시는 안정적이나 노출률 15–50%·2차 제거 필요, 콜라겐은 노출은 적으나 비포위 결손에서 collapse — 라는 이분법에 머물러 있다 ([[bone-regeneration/liu-2026-barrier-membranes-gbr-design-applications]], [[bone-regeneration/sasaki-2021-barrier-membranes-tissue-regeneration-review]]). 본 prospective single-arm study는 3D-preformed resorbable PLGA membrane (3D-PRPM)이 메시의 강성과 콜라겐의 흡수성을 결합해 고정 없이·2차 제거 없이 ~3 mm 수평 증대를 달성하는지 검증해, 합성 흡수성 막 RCT ([[bone-regeneration/wang-2025-gelatin-polylactic-acid-pla-membrane-gbr-rct]])와 짝을 이루는 임상 근거를 추가한다.

## One-line Summary

Prospective single-arm clinical study (20 patients / 21 sites) of a 3D-preformed resorbable PLGA membrane (3D-PRPM, r-Builder, Osstem) with simultaneous implant placement and xenograft for horizontal ridge augmentation: mean horizontal bone augmentation 2.99 ± 1.15 mm, hard tissue gain 2.64 ± 1.10 mm at 5 months (HGR 87.2% ± 8.2%), with zero membrane exposure, infection, or dehiscence.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Simultaneous implant placement with guided bone regeneration for horizontal ridge augmentation using a 3D-preformed resorbable PLGA membrane: A prospective single-arm clinical study
- **Authors**: Sang-Hyun Son, Kyeong-Ok Lim, Seo-Hee Han, Gwi-Hyeon Min, Jooseong Kim, Tadeusz Morawiec, Won-Pyo Lee
- **Journal**: Clinical Oral Investigations 2026;30(6)
- **DOI**: 10.1007/s00784-026-06959-9 / PMID 42240865 / PMCID PMC13236836
- **Study type**: Prospective, single-arm clinical trial (Chosun University Dental Hospital, Gwangju, Korea; IRB CUDHIRB 2405-006)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First clinical study evaluating a 3D-preformed resorbable PLGA membrane (3D-PRPM) for GBR with simultaneous implant placement in localized horizontal ridge defects.
- Demonstrates a fixation-free, resorbable barrier achieving titanium-mesh-comparable horizontal augmentation (~3 mm) and retention (~87%) without the mesh's exposure/secondary-removal burden.
- Bony-envelope analysis quantifies contour stability beyond the native envelope through 5-month healing.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Membrane**: 3D-PRPM (r-Builder, Osstem) from PLGA (PURASORB PLG 8218; MW 90,000; L-lactide:glycolide 82:18), injection-molded, ~0.3 mm thick, 1.0-mm pores. In vitro: compressive strength > 16 N maintained through 4 months; >50% weight retained at 4 months; slower pH change/degradation than MW-50,000 PLGA control.
- **Patients**: 20 patients (21 sites), mean age 60.5 ± 16.6 y (25–83); 5 anterior, 16 posterior defects. Healthy, non-heavy-smokers, localized horizontal defects needing GBR at implant placement.
- **Surgery**: Single periodontist (WPL). Mid-crestal incision, flap, implant (TS III, Osstem) placement, 1-mm anchor screw on fixture, peri-implant defect filled with particulate xenograft (A-Oss, Osstem), covered by 3D-PRPM stabilized by cover cap, plus an additional resorbable collagen membrane (OssMem soft) over it; tension-free primary closure. 5-month healing then second-stage + restoration.
- **Imaging**: CBCT (voxel 0.2 mm) preop, immediately postop, 5 months by single blinded examiner (ICC > 0.90). BA = horizontal thickness immediately post-augmentation; HG = thickness at 5 months; BR = BA − HG; HGR = HG/BA × 100. Bony envelope = line joining most-labial bone of adjacent teeth (positive = within, negative = beyond).
- **Stats**: SPSS v27; Wilcoxon signed-rank (BA vs HG); Friedman + Bonferroni-corrected pairwise Wilcoxon (envelope across 3 time points); one-sample t-test of HGR vs 80% reference.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **BA**: 2.99 ± 1.15 mm (median 2.75; range 1.17–5.26).
- **HG (5 mo)**: 2.64 ± 1.10 mm (median 2.37; range 0.84–4.92).
- **BR**: 0.35 ± 0.23 mm (median 0.36; range 0.07–1.04). BA significantly > HG (Z = −4.016, p < 0.001, r = 0.88; Hodges–Lehmann median diff 0.35 mm, 95% CI 0.21–0.50).
- **HGR**: 87.2% ± 8.2% (95% CI 83.4–90.8%; range 71.2–97.4%). Significantly higher than 80% reference (t = 3.99, p < 0.001, Cohen's d = 0.87; mean diff 7.2%).
- **Bony envelope** (Friedman p < 0.001): baseline 2.60 ± 1.35 mm → post-aug −0.39 ± 1.18 mm → 5 mo −0.04 ± 1.16 mm. 14/21 sites (66.7%) extended beyond envelope after augmentation; 10/14 (71.4%) remained beyond at 5 months. Ridge dimensions at 5 months significantly greater than baseline (P < 0.001).
- **Safety**: No membrane exposure, infection, or wound dehiscence in any of the 21 sites.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample, single-arm (no control group, no membrane-type comparison).
- 3D-PRPM was always combined with an overlying collagen membrane — effects cannot be isolated.
- Short 5-month follow-up; no implant survival/long-term stability data.
- No histology. Authors call for larger RCTs, longer follow-up, histologic assessment, and exploration of vertical augmentation applicability.

## 6. Related Work

- Titanium mesh GBR: high stability/predictability but 15–25% (up to 40–50%) exposure; 3D-preformed Ti mesh 5–12%.
- Collagen-membrane GBR: low exposure but collapse in large/non-contained defects, contour regresses within envelope.
- Comparable Ti-mesh benchmarks cited: 2–4 mm gain, ~78.6% retention; recent 2.9 mm/83%, 3.1 mm/82%; SRs ~3–4 mm.

## 7. Glossary

- **3D-PRPM**: Three-dimensional preformed resorbable PLGA membrane.
- **PLGA**: Polylactic-co-glycolic acid, a synthetic resorbable copolymer with tunable degradation.
- **BA / HG / BR / HGR**: Bone augmentation / hard tissue gain at 5 mo / bone resorption (BA−HG) / hard tissue gain rate (HG/BA×100).
- **Bony envelope**: Reference line joining the most-labial alveolar bone of adjacent teeth; beyond it = negative value.
- **GBR**: Guided bone regeneration.
