## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 15 RCTs (n=1,092 primary teeth): diode-laser pulpotomy achieved clinical and radiographic success rates statistically equivalent to all comparators at 3–12 months (RR≈1.00 at every timepoint, p>0.05), while significantly reducing pathologic periodontal-ligament-space widening (RR=0.39, 95% CI 0.17–0.90, p=0.03); wavelength (632–660 nm vs 810–980 nm) and capping material had no effect on success.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First SR+MA to systematically evaluate both **efficacy** (clinical + radiographic success) and **safety** (7 postoperative adverse-event outcomes) of diode laser in primary-tooth pulpotomy.
2. Establishes that diode laser is **equivalent** to FC, MTA, FS, electrosurgery, and other agents at every follow-up through 12 months.
3. Identifies a specific **safety signal**: reduced pathologic widening of the periodontal ligament space (RR=0.39, p=0.03) — the single adverse-event outcome where diode laser outperformed controls.
4. Shows the ZOE subgroup further amplifies this safety advantage (RR=0.29, 95% CI 0.11–0.78, p=0.01) though the result is sensitive to exclusion of one study.
5. Demonstrates **wavelength independence** across 632–660 nm and 810–980 nm diode bands.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Search**: PubMed, Embase, Cochrane Library; inception to 31 August 2025; English only; MeSH terms "pulpotomy", "Lasers", "Lasers, Semiconductor".

**PICOS**:
- Population: children 3–10 years, primary molars, vital reversible pulpitis, no irreversible pulpitis/apical pathology
- Intervention: diode laser pulpotomy (partial or full coronal; any wavelength or parameter)
- Comparison: non-diode-laser pulpotomy (FC, FS, MTA, electrosurgery, NaClO, Er,Cr:YSGG, bioceramics, etc.)
- Outcomes: clinical + radiographic success rates; postoperative adverse events
- Study design: RCTs only; excluded n≤10, histologic outcomes, incomplete follow-up reporting

**Screening**: 348 identified → 138 duplicates removed → 210 title/abstract → 26 full-text → 9 excluded → **15 RCTs included**.

**Quality**: Cochrane RoB tool. 9/15 studies had adequate randomization; allocation concealment unclear in 12/15; blinding largely absent or unclear. Overall methodological quality limited.

**Statistics**: RR with 95% CI for dichotomous data; Cochran Q + I² for heterogeneity; fixed-effects when I²<50%; random-effects when I²≥50%. Subgroup analyses by wavelength, capping material, and control group. Sensitivity analysis (sequential study exclusion). Funnel plots for publication bias.

### Subgroup: ZOE capping material (PDL-space widening)
- N=8 studies, n=602 teeth; RR=0.29, 95% CI 0.11–0.78, p=0.01, I²=37%

### Sensitivity analysis caveat
- PDL-space widening result (RR=0.39, p=0.03) lost statistical significance when Yavagal 2022 was excluded (RR=1.05, p=0.93)
- ZOE subgroup PDL result also became NS without Yavagal 2022

### Wavelength subgroups (clinical success)
- 632–660 nm vs 810–980 nm: no significant difference at any timepoint across both clinical and radiographic endpoints

### Control-group subgroups
- FC (8 studies), MTA (3), FS (4), electrosurgery (2), SG/CH/ADM/C-MTA/CEM/Biodentine/NaClO/Er,Cr:YSGG (1 each): no significant difference in clinical or radiographic success vs diode laser in any subgroup

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Methodological quality: insufficient allocation concealment (12/15) and blinding (>half unclear); findings should be interpreted cautiously.
- Most single-comparator subgroups (SG, AMD, Biodentine, NaClO, Er,Cr:YSGG) supported by only one study each — evidence weak.
- Laser parameter heterogeneity: wavelength, power, frequency, energy density, irradiation mode all varied across studies; only wavelength was subgrouped; effects of other parameters remain unclear.
- PDL-space widening safety signal is statistically fragile (loses significance when Yavagal 2022 excluded).
- Subgroup analysis of pulp capping materials for safety was based on diode-laser group only — potential bias from inconsistent control-group capping materials.
- Need: large-sample, multi-center, methodologically rigorous RCTs with standardized laser parameters.

## 7. Glossary

- **Diode laser pulpotomy**: semiconductor laser applied to pulp stump for hemostasis, disinfection, and photobiomodulation during coronal pulpotomy of primary teeth.
- **Pathologic periodontal-ligament (PDL) space widening**: radiographic sign of periradicular inflammation — considered an adverse event / treatment failure indicator.
- **ZOE (zinc oxide eugenol)**: traditional pulpotomy capping material; used in 8 of 15 included studies.
- **FC (formocresol)**: historically the "gold standard" pulpotomy agent; known mutagenic/carcinogenic concerns.
- **FS (ferrous sulfate)**: hemostatic agent used as control in 4 studies.
- **MTA (mineral trioxide aggregate)**: biocompatible bioceramic capping material; used in 4 studies.
- **PBMT (photobiomodulation therapy)**: low-level laser bioeffect promoting stem cell proliferation, collagen synthesis, and tissue repair.
- **RoB (Risk of Bias)**: Cochrane quality assessment tool applied to all 15 RCTs.
