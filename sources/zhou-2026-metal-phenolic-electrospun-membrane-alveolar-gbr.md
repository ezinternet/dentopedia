## One-line Summary

In-vitro + rat alveolar bone defect study: a silk fibroin/PCL electrospun membrane loaded with curcumin–strontium metal-phenolic nanoparticles (Cur-Sr/SF/PCL) gave superior mechanical strength (~13.9 MPa) and slow degradation (<8% at 8 weeks), and synergistically drove osteogenesis (Wnt/Ca/CaN pathway), angiogenesis (VEGF/HIF-1), and M1→M2 macrophage reprogramming, yielding the highest BV/TV and new-bone fraction in vivo.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Designed a multifunctional **bioactive guided bone regeneration (GBR) membrane**: electrospun silk fibroin (SF) + polycaprolactone (PCL) loaded with **curcumin–strontium (Cur-Sr) metal-phenolic nanoparticles**.
2. Moved beyond passive barrier function: a single membrane simultaneously promotes osteogenesis, angiogenesis, and immune reprogramming (M1→M2).
3. Mechanistically tied the pro-osteogenic effect to the **non-canonical Wnt/Ca/calcineurin (CaN) signaling pathway** via RNA-seq + Western blot.
4. Positioned the membrane explicitly against the gold-standard Bio-Gide collagen membrane on mechanical strength and degradation matching.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Cur-Sr NPs**: synthesized via curcumin's phenolic-hydroxyl coordination with Sr²⁺ (metal-phenolic network), PVP stabilizer; ~12 nm globular NPs, organic content 69.34% (TGA); confirmed by SEM/TEM/FTIR/XPS.
- **Membranes (EFMs)**: co-electrospun SF/PCL, Cur/SF/PCL, and Cur-Sr/SF/PCL (Cur-Sr NPs optimized at 1 mg/mL); fiber diameter ~250–350 nm, membrane thickness ~60 μm. Hexafluoroisopropanol solvent.
- **In-vitro cells**: orofacial mesenchymal stem cells (OMSCs), human gingival fibroblasts (HGFs), HUVECs (angiogenesis), RAW 264.7 macrophages (immune; LPS 1 μg/mL to induce M1).
- **Assays**: CCK-8, live/dead, ALP (day 7), ARS mineralization (day 28), RT-qPCR + IF + Western (osteo/angio/immune genes & proteins), Transwell/scratch migration, tube formation, CLSM barrier (cell infiltration depth), tensile testing, degradation & drug-release profiling, RNA-seq (transcriptomics).
- **In-vivo**: 16 male SD rats (8-wk), 4 groups (sham, blank, SF/PCL, Cur-Sr/SF/PCL); standardized palatal maxillary first-molar alveolar defect (~3 × 1.5 × 2 mm); membrane placed; harvest at 8 weeks; micro-CT (BV/TV, CEJ–ABC distance), H&E, Masson, IF for OPN/VEGF/iNOS/CD206.
- **Statistics**: one-way ANOVA, n=3.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Membrane physical properties:**
- Ultimate tensile strength: SF/PCL 10.02±0.92 MPa, Cur/SF/PCL 13.12±1.00 MPa, **Cur-Sr/SF/PCL 13.92±0.30 MPa** (vs Bio-Gide ≈0.74 MPa reported).
- Elongation at break: 105–130% (Cur-Sr/SF/PCL 129.7±3.67%, vs Bio-Gide ≈29.9%); Young's modulus ~8.2–9.7 MPa.
- Degradation: <4% at 4 wk, <8% at 8 wk (vs Bio-Gide fragmenting at 5 days, reported) — matched to bone remodeling window.
- Drug release: ~70% cumulative at 42 days; Cur-Sr NPs gave more sustained/gradual release than free curcumin.
- Barrier: HGF infiltration depth ~30 μm < 60 μm membrane thickness over 7 days → effective soft-tissue barrier.
- Biocompatibility: no cytotoxicity to OMSCs/HGFs/HUVECs/RAW264.7; Cur-Sr/SF/PCL best proliferation.

**Osteogenesis (in vitro):** Cur-Sr/SF/PCL highest ALP (day 7) and mineralized nodules (ARS day 28); strongest upregulation of OPN, RUNX2, BMP2, OCN, COL1, BSP; highest RUNX2/OPN/OCN protein; strongest OMSC recruitment (Transwell).

**Angiogenesis (in vitro):** Cur-Sr/SF/PCL upregulated VEGF, ANG-1 in OMSCs and VEGF/HIF-1 in HUVECs; best tube formation (nodes/junctions/segments/meshes) and HUVEC migration (scratch + Transwell).

**Immune reprogramming (in vitro):** under LPS, Cur-Sr/SF/PCL suppressed M1 genes/proteins (iNOS, TNF-α, IL-6) and upregulated M2 markers (TGF-β, ARG-1) → strongest M1→M2 shift.

**Mechanism:** RNA-seq — 126 up / 87 down genes; upregulated osteogenic (Sp7, Runx2, Col1a1, Bmp2/4), Calcium and Wnt pathway genes (Cacna1d/Cacna1i, Wnt2b, Lgr5); KEGG enriched Calcium/MAPK/Wnt; Western confirmed ↑Wnt, CaM, CaN → **Wnt/Ca/calcineurin (CaN) pathway**; Sr²⁺ acts via calcium-sensing receptor (CaSR) synergistically.

**In vivo (rat, 8 wk):** Cur-Sr/SF/PCL gave greatest defect bone volume, most reduced CEJ–ABC distance (ridge-height restoration), highest BV/TV, highest new-bone area fraction and collagen volume fraction (Masson), minimal residual fibers (synchronized degradation), and strongest OPN/VEGF + lowest iNOS / highest CD206 (M2) in the defect.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Bench + small rat model (n=16; n=3 cell replicates); no large-animal or clinical data.
- Single 8-week in-vivo endpoint; long-term remodeling, full membrane resorption, and load-bearing performance untested.
- Mechanism (Wnt/Ca/CaN) inferred from RNA-seq + Western associations, not pathway-blockade causality.
- Comparison to Bio-Gide on degradation/mechanics is against literature-reported values, not a head-to-head arm.
- No infected/critical-size or vertical-augmentation challenge.

## 6. Related Work

- Bio-Gide collagen membrane (gold-standard GBR barrier) — comparator for degradation/mechanics.
- Strontium and curcumin osteogenic/angiogenic/immunomodulatory literature; metal-phenolic network (MPN) drug-delivery platforms.
- Wnt/Ca/CaN non-canonical Wnt signaling in MSC osteogenic differentiation.

## 7. Glossary

- **GBR**: Guided bone regeneration — barrier membrane excluding soft tissue from a bone defect.
- **EFM**: Electrospun fibrous membrane.
- **SF / PCL**: Silk fibroin / polycaprolactone (membrane polymers).
- **Cur-Sr NPs**: Curcumin–strontium metal-phenolic nanoparticles.
- **MPN**: Metal-phenolic network (polyphenol–metal coordination complex).
- **OMSC**: Orofacial mesenchymal stem cell.
- **HGF / HUVEC**: Human gingival fibroblast / human umbilical vein endothelial cell.
- **M1/M2**: Pro-inflammatory / anti-inflammatory (reparative) macrophage phenotypes.
- **Wnt/Ca/CaN**: Non-canonical Wnt → calcium → calcineurin osteogenic signaling cascade.
- **BV/TV**: Bone volume / tissue volume (micro-CT bone fraction).
- **CEJ–ABC**: Cementoenamel junction to alveolar bone crest distance (ridge-height proxy).
