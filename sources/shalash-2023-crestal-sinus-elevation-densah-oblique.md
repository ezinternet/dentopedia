## One-line Summary
Prospective study (n=16, RBH 4–7mm, oblique sinus floor): Densah bur osseodensification achieves mean lift 4.42mm, seating torque 35.5 Ncm; 1/16 perforation; 100% 1-year success.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: International Journal of Implant Dentistry 2023; 9:41
- **Study type**: Prospective clinical study

## 2. Key Contributions
- First study specifically addressing osseodensification CSFE in oblique sinus floor cases
- Demonstrates feasibility of OD technique in RBH 4–7mm with oblique anatomy
- Low perforation rate (6.25%) compared to osteotome technique in oblique floors

## 3. Methodology
- 16 patients; Group I: RBH 4–5.5mm, Group II: RBH 5.5–7mm
- Densah burs (counterclockwise, non-excavating OD technique) for CSFE
- Primary outcomes: membrane perforation, lift height, insertion torque
- CBCT verification of membrane integrity and bone gain at 1 year

## 4. Key Results
- Mean sinus lift: 4.42 mm
- Mean insertion torque: 35.5 Ncm
- 1 perforation (6.25%) — repaired intraoperatively
- All 16 cases: clinical success at 1-year follow-up, no sinus pathology
