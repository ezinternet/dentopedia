## Why Ingested

Counterpoint and mechanistic complement to [[wiki/implants/versah-protocols/rittipakorn-2025-clockwise-osseodensification-primary-stability-cadaveric]], which is a cadaveric study contrasting bur **rotation direction** (clockwise vs counter-clockwise) for bone compaction/primary stability. This Chen 2022 sawbone bench study addresses the same core mechanism — the **reverse (counter-clockwise) drilling technique** — but isolates its *ridge-expansion* effect rather than primary stability, showing that counter-clockwise rotation (osseodensification Densah bur) expands narrow ridges but at the cost of higher bone stress and shallower implant insertion. Together the two papers map both sides of the reverse-drilling concept: expansion gain vs. fracture/insertion-depth penalty.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro sawbone bench study (27 blocks, 3 ridge widths × 3 protocols): counter-clockwise (reverse) Densah osseodensification drilling expanded narrow ridges (6.75 mm) significantly more than standard forward drilling, but only in narrow bone, and produced shallower implant insertion depth due to higher induced bone stress and elastic rebound.

## 한줄요약

인공골(sawbone) 벤치 실험(27블록, 골폭 3종 × 드릴링 3종): 반시계(역회전, reverse) Densah 골밀도화(Osseodensification, OD) 드릴은 좁은 치조제(6.75 mm)에서만 표준 정회전보다 골폭을 유의하게 확장했고, 넓은 골폭에서는 차이가 없었으며, 더 큰 골 응력·탄성 반발 때문에 임플란트 식립 깊이는 더 얕았다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Directly compares three osteotomy protocols on standardized synthetic bone to isolate the **ridge-expansion** effect of reverse (counter-clockwise) drilling:
  1. **Osseodensification (OD)** — Densah bur (Versah), 1500 rpm **reverse** torque.
  2. **Modified osseodensification** — triple straight-blade bur, 200 rpm **reverse** torque.
  3. **Standard** — same triple-bladed drill, 1600 rpm **forward** torque.
- Demonstrates the reverse-drilling expansion benefit is **ridge-width dependent** — present only in narrow (6.75 mm) bone, absent at 7.25/7.75 mm.
- Quantifies a trade-off largely overlooked when promoting OD for expansion: OD gives higher expansion in narrow bone but **shallower implant insertion depth** (bone elastic rebound + higher induced stress).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Material**: Sawbones block 35 PCF (Sawbones Inc, Vashon Island, WA) cut to plates 5 mm thick × 40.5 mm long, three widths 6.75 / 7.25 / 7.75 mm (±0.05 mm), simulating different alveolar ridge widths.
- **Sample size**: Abstract states 27 samples (3 widths × 3 protocols × 3 each); the methods text also mentions "nine samples of each size" for the initial pilot marking. Each protocol group analyzed with n=3 per width.
- **Osteotomy sequence**: first a Lindemann drill at 1600 rpm forward, then one of the three protocols; final drill diameter standardized to 4.5 mm in all groups.
- **Implant**: IDEOSS (Taipei) dual sandblasted + anodically oxidized surface, φ5.0 × 8.0 mm, screwed at center until depth reached 5 mm **or** the bone fractured.
- **Outcomes**: (a) change in bone-block width (ridge expansion) by digital vernier caliper; (b) implant insertion depth; (c) presence/absence of bone fracture (surgical success).
- **Statistics**: ANOVA for insertion depth and width change; Tukey post-hoc pairwise; chi-square for surgery success (fracture) rate.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Ridge expansion (width change)**:
  - Significant difference among groups **only at narrow 6.75 mm** width (p < 0.05). Both reverse-torque groups (OD and modified OD) gave higher expansion, but in pairwise comparison **only the Densah/OD bur expanded significantly more than the standard forward drill**.
  - At 7.25 mm and 7.75 mm widths, no obvious expansion difference; no significant difference between Densah/OD and the modified-OD (counter-clockwise triple-blade) group.
- **Implant insertion depth**: OD group inserted at **significantly shallower depth** than the other two protocols (p ≈ 0.005). Standard and modified-OD allowed deeper placement before fracture → better drilling/placement performance.
- **Bone fracture / success**: fractures fewest in the standard forward group, but **no statistically significant difference** in fracture count among groups (chi-square).
- **Interpretation**: counter-clockwise (reverse) rotation can expand the ridge but induces higher stress in the bone structure; the bone's own elasticity causes the prepared site to rebound (smaller effective diameter), limiting how deep the implant can be driven.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Synthetic sawbone, not natural bone — no biology (no bone-cell vitality, no osseointegration, no healing); rigid/elastic behavior of sawbone may not match human cancellous/cortical ridges.
- Very small n (3 per cell); single implant geometry and one bur system (Densah/Versah) — limited generalizability.
- Only ridge **expansion** measured; the claimed OD effects on bone density/compaction and on simultaneous maxillary sinus lifting were explicitly **not** tested and need further study.
- Potential conflict of interest (co-author is shareholder of the implant manufacturer IDEOSS).
- Authors note low-speed (200 rpm, no irrigation) drilling preserves bone-cell viability and growth-factor (BMP-2, VEGF) expression vs high-speed irrigated drilling — cited as rationale for the modified-OD protocol but not measured here.

## 6. Related Work

- Osseodensification concept (Versah Densah bur, counter-clockwise non-cutting compaction) — the technique whose ridge-expansion claim is tested here.
- [[wiki/implants/versah-protocols/rittipakorn-2025-clockwise-osseodensification-primary-stability-cadaveric]] — cadaveric study contrasting clockwise vs counter-clockwise OD rotation direction for primary stability; same reverse-drilling mechanism, complementary outcome (stability vs expansion).
- Low-speed bone-harvesting / autograft viability literature (BMP-2, VEGF), titanium-mesh-guided ridge augmentation — cited motivation for the modified low-speed reverse protocol.

## 7. Glossary

- **Reverse drilling technique** — running the bur in counter-clockwise (non-cutting) rotation so it laterally compacts/displaces bone instead of cutting and removing it.
- **Osseodensification (OD)** — Densah-bur (Versah) protocol using counter-clockwise rotation to densify and laterally expand the osteotomy wall, raising bone density and primary stability.
- **Modified osseodensification** — common triple straight-blade cylinder drill run at low speed (200 rpm) in reverse, proposed to mimic OD's expansion effect.
- **Sawbones (PCF)** — synthetic rigid polyurethane foam bone substitute; 35 PCF = 0.56 g/cm³ density, used to standardize "bone" quality in bench tests.
- **Ridge expansion / splitting** — widening a narrow alveolar ridge so an implant of adequate diameter can be placed without grafting.
