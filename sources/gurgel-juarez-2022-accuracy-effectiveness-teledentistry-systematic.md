## Why Ingested

Opens a teledentistry (원격치과, teledentistry) sub-topic in the thin practice-management category — the service-delivery evidence base for remote referral, triage, screening, and treatment planning, sharpened by COVID-19. Pairs with the concurrently-ingested teledentistry cost umbrella review and extends practice-management beyond the in-clinic operational/legal focus of [[practice-management/nosrat-2025-endodontist-gp-nsrct-clinical-approaches-cohort]] toward how care is delivered at a distance.

## One-line Summary

Umbrella review (review of 6 SRs, >7,000 participants, 30 primary studies of interest) finding teledentistry is an effective means for dental referrals, treatment planning, and treatment monitoring, with sensitivity/specificity of 80-88% / 73-95% for referrals and diagnostic-treatment planning — though the included SRs were of low/critically low AMSTAR-2 quality.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Accuracy and effectiveness of teledentistry: a systematic review of systematic reviews
- **Authors**: Nalia Gurgel-Juarez, Cassius Torres-Pereira, Ana E Haddad, Lisa Sheehy, Hillel Finestone, Karen Mallet, Michael Wiseman, Kannika Hour, Heather L Flowers
- **Journal**: Evidence-Based Dentistry, 2022 (published 2022-07-08), pages 1-8
- **DOI**: 10.1038/s41432-022-0257-8 / **PMID**: 35804195 / **PMCID**: PMC9264296
- **Study type**: Systematic review of systematic reviews (umbrella/overview of SRs); narrative synthesis (no meta-analysis)
- **Affiliations**: University of Ottawa, Federal University of Parana, University of São Paulo, Bruyère Research Institute, McGill University, et al.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First umbrella synthesis (review of SRs) quantifying the **accuracy and effectiveness of teledentistry** vs in-person consultation across screening, diagnosis, and therapeutic management in children and adults.
- Establishes that teledentistry is **effective for dental referrals, treatment planning, and treatment monitoring** — the core service-delivery use cases for practice management.
- Identifies the **most feasible practical configuration**: asynchronous (store-and-forward) communication + smartphone image capture for routine implementation.
- Maps the evidence's concentration in **paediatrics and oral medicine** and flags a critical gap: no treatment-efficacy data and low methodological quality of the underlying SRs.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Review of systematic reviews (PRISMA-reported, pre-established protocol).
- **Search**: Medline (Ovid), Embase (Ovid), CINAHL (EBSCOHost), Web of Science from inception to June 2020; plus OpenGrey, Brazilian Ministry of Health Virtual Library, IADR conference archives (2001+). Reiterative reference-list/citation searching (Google Scholar, Scopus) into September 2020.
- **Eligibility**: SRs reporting virtual screening, diagnostic investigation, or therapeutic intervention via remote-communication technology. No restriction on population, setting, or language.
- **Selection**: 3-stage dual-reviewer (NG-J, KH, HLF) abstract → full-text → reference-chasing process; consensus for discrepancies.
- **Quality appraisal**: AMSTAR-2, with the diagnostic-accuracy AMSTAR-2 extension (modified items 1, 3, 8, 9, 11) for diagnostic/screening reviews.
- **Synthesis**: Narrative (data too heterogeneous in sample, purpose, and design for meta-analysis).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Screening (817 citations → 547 after dedup → 34 full-text → 6 SRs included)**. Reference-chasing added 3 of the 6.
- **Included SRs**: 6 (published 2013-2020, English), spanning **61 different primary studies, >7,000 unique participants**; 30 primary studies relevant to screening/diagnosis/treatment. 4 RCTs + 17 other designs (cross-sectional, pre-post, cohort).
- **Dominant modality**: store-and-forward (asynchronous), especially for screening/diagnosis (86% of cases); real-time (synchronous) used for treatment outcomes.
- **Settings**: mostly clinical (57%), rural 20%; >70% of primary studies from Europe, USA, Brazil.
- **Diagnostic accuracy** (6 primary studies): highest for DFS detection (sensitivity 94-100%, specificity 52-100%) and diagnostic-treatment planning (sensitivity 81-88%, specificity 82-95%). Abstract-level summary: referrals + diagnostic-treatment planning sensitivity/specificity **80-88% / 73-95%**, higher than other index/reference tests.
- **Screening accuracy** (3 studies, oral health indices): sensitivity 48-100%, specificity 81-98%; DFS best (sens 100%, spec 81%); dental caries/DMFT showed high specificity but low sensitivity.
- **Treatment** (2 studies): interceptive orthodontics under remote supervision (PAR-index improvement 36% remote vs 44% in-person — comparable) and paediatric treatment compliance (56-100%). No treatment-efficacy measures reported.
- **Quality**: 1 SR low-quality, 5 critically low (AMSTAR-2). Accuracy higher when remote and in-person assessors had similar education/experience; lower for mid-level/inexperienced professionals.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Heterogeneity of primary studies precluded meta-analysis → estimates remain descriptive ranges.
- Included SRs were low/critically low AMSTAR-2 quality and built on primary studies with high risk of bias.
- SRs often lacked key primary-study detail (design, technology, assessors), limiting data extraction.
- **No treatment-efficacy evidence** — only compliance/supervision outcomes for the treatment domain.
- Search ended mid-2020 (pre-dates much COVID-era teledentistry literature).

## 7. Glossary

- **Teledentistry (원격치과)**: Use of telehealth technologies to provide dental care at a distance (inception 1994).
- **Synchronous / real-time modality**: Live audiovisual interaction between provider and patient/practitioner.
- **Asynchronous / store-and-forward modality**: Health information collected at one time point and shared with a practitioner later — the dominant teledentistry mode.
- **AMSTAR-2**: A Measurement Tool to Assess systematic Reviews (v2); confidence ratings high/moderate/low/critically low.
- **DFS / DMFT**: Decayed-and-Filled Surfaces (primary teeth) / Decayed, Missing, Filled permanent Teeth — caries indices.
- **PAR index**: Peer Assessment Rating — validated orthodontic treatment-outcome measure.
- **Umbrella review / review of SRs**: A systematic review whose included units are themselves systematic reviews.
