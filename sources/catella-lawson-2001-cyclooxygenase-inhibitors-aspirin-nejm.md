## One-line Summary
Pharmacodynamic crossover RCT showing that ibuprofen (but not rofecoxib, diclofenac, or acetaminophen) blocks aspirin's irreversible COX-1 inhibition when taken before aspirin; aspirin-before-ibuprofen preserves antiplatelet effect.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: New England Journal of Medicine 2001;345:1809–17
- **DOI**: 10.1056/NEJMoa003199
- **Institution**: Center for Experimental Therapeutics, University of Pennsylvania School of Medicine, Philadelphia
- **Study type**: Pharmacodynamic crossover study (human volunteers)
- **Published**: December 20, 2001

## 2. Key Contributions
- First rigorously controlled human study to demonstrate pharmacodynamic interaction between ibuprofen and low-dose aspirin at the COX-1 active site
- Showed that the interaction is critically dependent on the **order of administration**: ibuprofen before aspirin blocks acetylation; aspirin before ibuprofen preserves full antiplatelet effect
- Demonstrated that a selective COX-2 inhibitor (rofecoxib 25 mg) does NOT interfere with aspirin's antiplatelet action
- Confirmed that diclofenac (delayed-release 75 mg BID) and acetaminophen (1000 mg) likewise do not antagonize aspirin
- Clinical implication: in patients on aspirin for cardioprotection, concurrent ibuprofen may abolish the cardiovascular benefit

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: Multiple crossover arms, 6-day treatment per arm, healthy volunteers
- **Drug combinations tested**:
  1. Aspirin 81 mg QAM → 2 h → ibuprofen 400 mg QAM
  2. Ibuprofen 400 mg QAM → 2 h → aspirin 81 mg QAM
  3. Aspirin 81 mg QAM → 2 h → acetaminophen 1000 mg QAM
  4. Acetaminophen 1000 mg QAM → 2 h → aspirin 81 mg QAM
  5. Aspirin 81 mg QAM → 2 h → rofecoxib 25 mg QAM
  6. Rofecoxib 25 mg QAM → 2 h → aspirin 81 mg QAM
  7. Enteric-coated aspirin 81 mg → ibuprofen 400 mg TID
  8. Enteric-coated aspirin 81 mg → delayed-release diclofenac 75 mg BID
- **Primary outcomes**: Serum thromboxane B2 (TXB2; index of COX-1 activity), platelet aggregation (measured 24 h after day-6 morning dose)
- **Mechanism context**: Aspirin irreversibly acetylates serine-529 in platelet COX-1. NSAIDs bind reversibly in the same hydrophobic channel; if ibuprofen occupies this channel before aspirin arrives, acetylation is sterically blocked.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Aspirin before any drug**: serum TXB2 and platelet aggregation maximally inhibited (full antiplatelet effect preserved)
- **Rofecoxib before aspirin**: no interference with TXB2 suppression or platelet aggregation (result: no interaction)
- **Acetaminophen before aspirin**: no interference (result: no interaction)
- **Ibuprofen 400 mg QD before aspirin**: TXB2 inhibition significantly blocked; platelet aggregation not suppressed
- **Ibuprofen 400 mg TID (multiple doses) with enteric-coated aspirin**: TXB2 inhibition similarly blocked
- **Diclofenac 75 mg BID with enteric-coated aspirin**: no significant interference
- The COX-2 selectivity of rofecoxib explains the absence of interaction: COX-2 selective agents do not occupy the COX-1 active-site channel that aspirin must access

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Study conducted in healthy volunteers; generalizability to patients with vascular disease or chronic arthritis is implied but requires further study
- Only single ibuprofen dose (400 mg) tested for QD arm; higher doses or longer durations not directly tested
- Only one COX-2 inhibitor (rofecoxib) tested
- Potential interaction with long-term enteric-coated aspirin formulations deserves further pharmacokinetic investigation

## 6. Related Work
- Renda 2006: confirmed the ibuprofen–aspirin interaction in patients with osteoarthritis + IHD; celecoxib (COX-2 selective) similarly did not interfere
- Hohlfeld 2008: extended findings to dipyrone/pyrazolinone class
- Yokoyama 2013: in vitro screen of nine NSAIDs; confirmed ibuprofen and mefenamic acid; naproxen and flurbiprofen have independent antiplatelet effects

## 7. Glossary
- **COX-1**: Cyclooxygenase-1; constitutively expressed in platelets; responsible for thromboxane A2 synthesis
- **Thromboxane A2 (TXA2)**: Platelet-activating prostanoid; promotes aggregation and vasoconstriction
- **Serum TXB2**: Stable metabolite of TXA2; surrogate marker for platelet COX-1 activity
- **Serine-529 (or -530)**: The serine residue in the COX-1 active site acetylated by aspirin (numbering varies by reference source)
- **Irreversible acetylation**: Aspirin covalently modifies the serine residue; because platelets are anucleate, this effect lasts the lifetime of the platelet (~7–10 days)
- **Rofecoxib**: Selective COX-2 inhibitor (Vioxx; withdrawn from market in 2004 for CV reasons unrelated to this study's findings)
