## Why Ingested

상악동석(Antrolith, 상악동석)은 상악동 거상술 전 CBCT에서 발견될 수 있는 방사선 불투과성 병변이다. 이를 MRC·pseudocyst·악성종양과 감별하고 임플란트 계획 전 처치 여부를 결정하는 것이 임상적으로 중요. [[sinus-lift/pseudocyst/shenoy-2013-maxillary-antrolith-recurrent-sinusitis-case]]와 함께 상악동석의 전체 임상 스펙트럼(무증상 소형 vs 증상성 대형)을 포괄.

## One-line Summary

Case report of an asymptomatic 3.1×3.6mm maxillary antrolith (incidental CBCT finding in a 67-year-old pre-Denosumab patient) managed with Caldwell-Luc excision; literature review confirms small asymptomatic antroliths do not require prophylactic removal.

## 한줄요약

67세 여성 데노수맙 치료 전 CBCT에서 우연히 발견된 3.1×3.6mm 무증상 상악동석 증례; 소형 무증상 상악동석은 예방적 제거 불필요, 증상성·합병증 동반 시 수술 제거.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Case report + literature review
- **Journal**: International Journal of Surgery Case Reports (2020)
- **DOI**: [10.1016/j.ijscr.2020.08.011](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijscr.2020.08.011)
- **Patient**: 67-year-old female, incidental finding during dental clearance before Denosumab

## 2. Key Contributions

- Well-documented CBCT-based diagnosis and Caldwell-Luc management of asymptomatic antrolith
- Comprehensive literature review of antrolith etiology, imaging, DDx, and management
- Highlights that histological evaluation is justified even for asymptomatic cases (especially cancer history)
- Confirms: prevalence 0.15–3.2%; female predisposition (55–60%)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Single case + narrative literature review
- CBCT imaging (Morita or equivalent) for localization and surgical access planning
- Caldwell-Luc procedure under LA; 5mm buccal window 20mm from cuspal edge of #17
- Histopathology: metaplastic bone and calcification

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Case details:**
- Size: 3.1mm × 3.6mm, floor of right maxillary sinus
- No mucosal thickening or opacification of sinus
- CT appearance: circumscribed dense radiopacity
- Histopathology: metaplastic bone and calcification = antrolith

**Literature review key points:**
- Prevalence in maxillary sinuses: 0.15–3.2% (rare)
- Most asymptomatic; incidental finding on routine radiography
- Symptoms (when present): epistaxis, facial pain, chronic sinusitis, nasal obstruction, foul-smelling discharge, halitosis
- CT: homogeneous, high-density periphery with central lower density (variable appearances)

**Differential diagnosis:**
- Impacted teeth, root fragments, calcified MRC/polyp
- Osteomyelitis, ossifying fibroma, fungal ball
- Benign/malignant tumour

**Management decision tree:**
- Small, asymptomatic: surveillance (prophylactic removal NOT necessary)
- Symptomatic / complications (sinusitis, fistula, ostial obstruction): surgical removal
- Surgical options: ESS, Caldwell-Luc, or combination
- Always with concurrent sinus infection treatment
- Prognosis: excellent; no recurrences documented

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single case; literature limited (~30 cases up to 2005)
- CT appearance is variable — histopathology needed for definitive diagnosis

## 7. Glossary

- **Antrolith (상악동석)**: calcified mass in the maxillary sinus; mineral salts depositing around an endogenous or exogenous nidus
- **Caldwell-Luc procedure**: surgical access via canine fossa to maxillary sinus; provides wide exposure for large lesions
- **Nidus**: central core (endogenous: tooth fragments/blood/mucus; exogenous: GP points/dental burs)
