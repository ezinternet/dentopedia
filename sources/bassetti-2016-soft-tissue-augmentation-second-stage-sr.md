## Why Ingested

The [[wiki/implants/zhang-2025-expert-consensus-km-augmentation-second-stage]] expert consensus cites earlier systematic review evidence as its evidentiary backbone. This 2016 SR by Bassetti et al. is one of the foundational references specifically scoped to **second-stage surgery** (implant uncovering) procedures — the narrower timing window that the 2025 consensus also targets. It anchors the KM-gain outcome benchmarks (APPTF/VP + FGG: up to 9.35 mm KT) and volume-gain benchmarks (REF: 2.41 mm; APPTF + SCTG: 3.10 mm) that later reviews and the consensus refined. Also extends [[wiki/overviews/keratinized-mucosa-peri-implant-health-overview]] by documenting what surgical options were available prior to widespread xenograft/collagen matrix adoption.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (10 studies, 2016) of soft-tissue augmentation at second-stage implant surgery: APPTF/VP + FGG or XCM best for KM width gain (up to 9.35 mm); REF or APPTF + SCTG best for volume gain (2.41–3.10 mm); all studies high risk of bias, no meta-analysis possible.

## 1. Document Information

- **Full citation**: Bassetti RG, Stähli A, Bassetti MA, Sculean A. Soft tissue augmentation procedures at second-stage surgery: a systematic review. *Clin Oral Investig* 2016;20:1369–1387.
- **DOI**: 10.1007/s00784-016-1815-2
- **Published**: 04 April 2016 (Volume 20, 2016)
- **Study type**: Systematic review (no meta-analysis due to heterogeneity)
- **PDF note**: This PDF is a Springer landing/abstract page — body text is paywalled. All content below is derived from the abstract, conclusions, clinical relevance statement, and reference list visible in the landing page.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Timing specificity**: First systematic review exclusively targeting **second-stage (uncovering) surgery** as the intervention time point for peri-implant soft-tissue augmentation — distinguishing it from pre-implant, simultaneous, and post-loading soft tissue procedures.
2. **KM width benchmark**: APPTF/VP combined with FGG or xenogeneic collagen matrix (XCM) provided the greatest KT enlargement across included studies (range −0.20 to +9.35 mm depending on technique and graft).
3. **Volume gain benchmark**: Roll envelope flap (REF) and APPTF + subepithelial connective tissue graft (SCTG) achieved mean soft-tissue volume increases of 2.41 mm and 3.10 mm, respectively.
4. **Jaw-location nuance**: Conclusions differentiated by jaw: APPTF alone favored in the maxilla for KM gain; APPTF/VP + FGG or XCM suitable for both jaws for KM gain; REF favored in the maxilla for volume gain; APPTF + SCTG effective in both jaws for volume gain.
5. **Evidence quality**: Only 8 prospective studies and 2 case series qualified; all had high risk of bias (Cochrane tool); heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis — highlighting the then-sparse evidence base.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search databases**: MEDLINE (PubMed) and EMBASE (OVID); hand search of related articles
- **Search horizon**: Inception to July 31, 2015
- **Population**: Human studies; submucosally osseointegrated (two-stage) implants undergoing second-stage (uncovering) surgery
- **Intervention**: Any soft-tissue augmentation or correction technique applied at uncovering
- **Outcomes**: Width of KM (keratinized mucosa / keratinized tissue) and/or soft-tissue volume gain
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane Collaboration's tool
- **Included studies**: 8 prospective studies + 2 case series (all high risk of bias)
- **Meta-analysis**: Not performed — excessive heterogeneity in study design, graft materials, and outcome measurement methods

**Surgical techniques evaluated** (from included studies, per abstract + references):
- Apically positioned partial-thickness flap (APPTF) alone
- APPTF / vestibuloplasty (VP) + free gingival graft (FGG)
- APPTF / VP + xenogeneic collagen matrix (XCM; e.g., Mucograft)
- Roll envelope flap (REF) / palatal roll technique
- APPTF + subepithelial connective tissue graft (SCTG)
- Rotated palatal flap
- Cultured mucosal epithelium (mentioned in references)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Technique | Primary outcome | Reported result |
|---|---|---|
| APPTF/VP + FGG | KM width gain | Range −0.20 to +9.35 mm (best KM gain) |
| APPTF/VP + XCM | KM width gain | Acceptable; comparable to FGG |
| APPTF (maxilla alone) | KM width gain | Acceptable in maxilla |
| REF (palatal roll) | Soft-tissue volume gain | Mean +2.41 mm |
| APPTF + SCTG | Soft-tissue volume gain | Mean +3.10 mm |

- All 10 included studies rated **high risk of bias**
- No study directly compared all techniques in the same RCT design
- Clinical relevance conclusion: jaw localization and individual clinical situation are the primary determinants of technique selection

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

1. **Landing-page only**: The PDF captured is the Springer web-landing page — full article body (tables, figures, detailed study-level data, forest plots if any) is behind paywall. Benchmarks cited above come from the abstract/conclusions only.
2. **All studies high risk of bias**: No RCTs with low bias were available at time of review; evidence base was primarily prospective case series.
3. **No meta-analysis**: Heterogeneity of designs, follow-up periods, and outcome measures prevented quantitative pooling.
4. **Short follow-up**: Included studies had variable, often short follow-up periods; long-term stability of KM gains and volume gains was not systematically addressed.
5. **Limited comparator data**: Few head-to-head comparisons between techniques; most studies were single-arm.
6. **Future need**: Authors implicitly called for RCTs with standardized outcome measures and longer follow-up — a gap partially addressed by later reviews (Montero 2022, Zhang 2025 consensus).

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| KM / KT | Keratinized mucosa / keratinized tissue — the band of attached, keratinized gingiva or mucosa around implants |
| Second-stage surgery | Implant uncovering procedure for two-stage (submerged) implant protocols |
| APPTF | Apically positioned partial-thickness flap — displaces keratinized tissue apically to expand KM zone |
| VP | Vestibuloplasty — deepening the vestibule to increase KM width |
| FGG | Free gingival graft — autogenous epithelialized graft from palate |
| SCTG | Subepithelial connective tissue graft — de-epithelialized palatal graft; best for volume |
| REF | Roll envelope flap / palatal roll — rotates palatal connective tissue to augment buccal volume |
| XCM | Xenogeneic collagen matrix (e.g., Mucograft, porcine-derived) — alloplastic FGG substitute |
| Risk of bias | Cochrane tool assessment; "high" = substantial methodological limitations |
