## One-line Summary
Systematic review (Indian J Dent Res 2018, 34 articles 1992–2015): catalogues the multifactorial determinants of osteotomy heat generation — drill design/material/wear, drilling method, irrigation, bone quality — concluding that no single factor dominates and management must be multidimensional.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Indian Journal of Dental Research, 2018
- Affiliation: Ahmedabad Dental College and Hospital, Gujarat, India
- Type: Systematic review (narrative synthesis, no meta-analysis)
- Search: PubMed + manual, 1992–December 2015
- 123 → 34 included

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive enumeration of biomechanical factors influencing osteotomy heat generation.
- Frames heat generation as multifactorial — pre-dating the more recent SRs by ~6 years.
- Foundational reading-list paper for any subsequent thermal-osteotomy review.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- PubMed + manual search, 1992–December 2015.
- 123 titles → 59 excluded (title/abstract) → 30 excluded (inclusion/exclusion) → 34 included.
- Narrative synthesis by factor category.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
Factor categories identified:
- Drill design (geometry, flute, helix, cutting edges)
- Drill material (SS, ceramic, coated)
- Drill wear
- Drilling method (intermittent vs continuous, sequential)
- Irrigation type (external, internal, none) and temperature
- Bone quality (D1–D4)
- Drilling speed and load

Findings: each factor independently affects heat, but interactions dominate; no isolated solution.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative synthesis without meta-analysis.
- Cut-off 2015 — predates osseodensification literature and recent piezo studies.
- No quality/bias appraisal beyond inclusion screening.
- Future: meta-analytic or network analysis of factor interactions.

## 6. Related Work
- Eriksson & Albrektsson — 47°C/1 min threshold.

## 7. Glossary
- **Multifactorial**: outcome jointly determined by multiple interacting variables.
- **D1–D4 bone**: Misch classification of bone density (D1 cortical dense → D4 cancellous sparse).
- **Sequential drilling**: standard implant protocol using progressively larger drills.
