## Why Ingested

기존 분석소염제 페이지가 ibuprofen·diclofenac·etoricoxib·rofecoxib 등 흔한 NSAID에 집중되어 있고 oxicam 계열(tenoxicam) 치과 술후통 근거가 비어 있었다. 본 SR(Altuhafy 2026, 9 RCT)은 tenoxicam이 다른 NSAID와 동등하거나 우월하며 1일 1회 투여·GI 내약성 이점을 제시해, [[drug/analgesics/moore-2015-otc-oral-analgesics-cochrane-overview]]의 단일용량 NSAID 순위와 [[drug/analgesics/kotowska-rodziewicz-2023-nsaids-role-dentistry-review]]의 치과 NSAID 개관에 oxicam 선택지를 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (PRISMA, 9 RCTs, n=35-100/study; no meta-analysis due to I²=62-89% heterogeneity): tenoxicam 20 mg (oral, once-daily) for postoperative dental pain was superior to placebo and comparable-to-superior versus ibuprofen, diclofenac, flurbiprofen, meloxicam, and methylprednisolone, but inferior to rofecoxib (which had more GI adverse events); GRADE moderate at 1-24 h, low at 48-72 h.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(PRISMA, RCT 9편, 연구당 35-100명; 이질성 I²=62-89%로 메타분석 불가): 치과 술후통에 tenoxicam 20 mg(경구 1일 1회)은 위약보다 우월하고 ibuprofen·diclofenac·flurbiprofen·meloxicam·methylprednisolone과 동등~우월, rofecoxib보다는 열등(단 rofecoxib는 위장관 부작용 더 많음); 1일 1회 투여·GI 내약성이 임상적 강점.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic review to consolidate RCT evidence on **tenoxicam** (an oxicam-class NSAID) specifically for postoperative **dental** pain — fills a gap not covered by ibuprofen/diclofenac/coxib-centric syntheses.
- Synthesizes 9 RCTs across heterogeneous procedures: third molar extraction (5), orthodontic adjustment (2), endodontic treatment (1), implant surgery (1).
- Provides a comparator-by-comparator efficacy map (placebo, ibuprofen, diclofenac K/Na, flurbiprofen, meloxicam, methylprednisolone, rofecoxib) and GRADE certainty by time window.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **PICO**: patients undergoing dental treatment; tenoxicam; other medication/placebo; postoperative pain; RCTs only.
- **Search**: PubMed, EMBASE, Scopus, ISI Web of Knowledge, Cochrane Library through August 2025. 92,787 records → 240 after dedup → 9 RCTs included (13 full-text assessed; 3 failed PICO, 1 non-RCT).
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane RoB 2.0 (5 domains), two independent reviewers.
- **Synthesis**: qualitative/descriptive; meta-analysis withheld because exploratory I² ran 62-89% across comparable VAS 0-100 outcomes.
- **Tenoxicam dosing**: most studies 20 mg oral (often single preoperative); one used 40 mg IV preop → 20 mg oral; extended regimens to day 7. Two studies IV-then-oral (Ilhan, Roelofse).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **vs placebo**: superior (orthodontic cohort P<0.001-0.044; endodontic significant at 6 h, not later).
- **vs ibuprofen 200 mg**: superior early but no difference beyond 6 h.
- **vs diclofenac potassium 50 mg**: significantly lower VAS from 8 h onward.
- **vs diclofenac sodium**: superior from postoperative day 3 (P<0.05); another trial significant only day 2.
- **vs meloxicam 15 mg**: no significant difference; both well tolerated.
- **vs methylprednisolone 80 mg IV**: comparable, no significant difference.
- **vs rofecoxib (50→25 mg)**: rofecoxib better overall pain control but more GI adverse events.
- **vs non-pharmacological chewing gum**: comparable, no clear tenoxicam superiority.
- **Adverse events**: generally mild/infrequent (nausea, GI discomfort, dizziness, skin irritation); no serious AEs attributable to tenoxicam; several trials reported none.
- **GRADE certainty**: moderate at 1-6 h and 12-24 h; low at 48-72 h; rescue-medication evidence low (inconsistent reporting).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Substantial clinical/methodological heterogeneity (I² 62-89%) precluded meta-analysis; effect sizes descriptive only.
- Inconsistent/incomplete reporting of rescue medication and adverse events → selective-reporting concerns in 8/9 studies, GRADE downgrades.
- Small samples (35-100), short follow-up variability, subjective VAS without objective biomarkers.
- Geographic concentration (Turkey, India, South Africa, New Zealand, Brazil) limits generalizability to North America, Europe, East Asia.
- Calls for standardized, larger, multicenter RCTs.

## 7. Glossary

- **Tenoxicam**: oxicam-class (enolic acid) non-selective COX-1/COX-2 inhibitor NSAID with long half-life (once-daily dosing); also inhibits lipoxygenase/leukotriene pathway.
- **Oxicam**: NSAID chemical subclass (tenoxicam, meloxicam, piroxicam).
- **VAS**: Visual Analogue Scale (0-10 or 0-100 pain self-report).
- **GRADE**: Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development, and Evaluation (certainty of evidence).
- **Preemptive analgesia**: analgesic given before the painful stimulus to blunt central sensitization.
