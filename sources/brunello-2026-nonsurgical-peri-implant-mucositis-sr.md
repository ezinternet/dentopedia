## Why Ingested

Extends the existing narrative review [[wiki/implants/peri-implantitis/mauriello-2026-peri-implant-mucositis-adjunctive-narrative-review]] by adding a systematic, risk-of-bias-appraised synthesis (21 studies, 1068 patients): mechanical debridement alone improves clinical parameters, and adjuncts (laser, air-polishing, antimicrobials, probiotics, antibiotics) add only minor improvements at 6 months. Also contextualizes prevention-focused [[wiki/implants/carra-2023-primordial-primary-prevention-peri-implant-sr-ma]] on the treatment side.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (21 studies — 19 RCTs, 2 NRCTs; 1068 patients) of nonsurgical peri-implant mucositis therapy: all protocols including mechanical debridement alone reduced BOP and improved clinical parameters, with mucositis resolution ranging 9%–100%; adjuncts yielded only minor additional benefit at 6 months, and a planned network meta-analysis could not be performed due to data scarcity and heterogeneity.

## 한줄요약

임플란트주위 점막염(peri-implant mucositis)의 비외과적 치료를 다룬 체계적 문헌고찰(21편 — RCT 19편·NRCT 2편, 환자 1068명): 기계적 변연부 청소(mechanical debridement) 단독을 포함한 모든 프로토콜이 출혈(BOP)을 줄이고 임상지표를 개선했으며 점막염 해소율은 9%~100%로 편차가 컸고, 보조요법(레이저·air-polishing·항균제·프로바이오틱스·항생제)은 6개월 시점에서 미미한 추가 효과만 보였다. 데이터 부족과 이질성으로 네트워크 메타분석(NMA)은 수행하지 못했다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Systematic appraisal of nonsurgical peri-implant mucositis therapy across 21 controlled studies (19 RCTs, 2 NRCTs; 1068 patients), search window May 2015–April 2025 across MEDLINE-PubMed, Cochrane CENTRAL, and Web of Science.
- Bottom line: mechanical debridement alone improves clinical parameters; adjuncts add only minor benefit at 6 months.
- Documents the wide heterogeneity in mucositis resolution (9%–100%, with only one study group reaching 100%) and the sparse, inconsistent reporting of microbiological/immunological outcomes and patient-reported outcomes (PROs).
- Calls for standardized outcome sets and routine PRO collection.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Question (PICO-style)**: how do clinical parameters change in implants affected by peri-implant mucositis following different nonsurgical treatment approaches (or no treatment), with >=6 months of monitoring?
- **Eligible designs**: RCTs and prospective controlled studies (NRCTs).
- **Databases**: MEDLINE-PubMed, Cochrane CENTRAL, Web of Science.
- **Search window**: May 2015 – April 2025.
- **Treatments compared**: curettes, (ultra)sonic scalers, air-polishing, laser, antimicrobials, probiotics, antibiotics — alone or in combination.
- **Planned synthesis**: network meta-analysis (NMA) — but it could NOT be performed due to limited data and heterogeneity (narrative/qualitative synthesis only).
- **Risk of bias**: only one-third of included studies judged at low RoB.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Included: 21 studies (19 RCTs, 2 NRCTs), 1068 patients.
- All treatments reduced bleeding on probing (BOP) to various extents.
- Peri-implant mucositis resolution achieved in a subset of cases, ranging 9% to 100% (only one study group reached 100%).
- Microbiological and immunological data, plus PROs, were sparsely and heterogeneously reported.
- Only one-third of studies had low risk of bias.
- NMA not performed (insufficient/heterogeneous data).
- (Abstract-level only — no further numerical effect sizes available without full text.)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Network meta-analysis infeasible due to limited and heterogeneous data — prevents quantitative ranking of treatment approaches.
- Two-thirds of included studies not at low risk of bias.
- Inconsistent/sparse reporting of microbiological, immunological, and patient-reported outcomes.
- Authors call for standardized outcome sets and routine PRO collection in future trials.

## 7. Glossary

- **Peri-implant mucositis**: reversible inflammation of soft tissue around an implant without supporting-bone loss; precursor to peri-implantitis.
- **BOP (Bleeding on Probing)**: key clinical marker of peri-implant soft-tissue inflammation.
- **Mechanical debridement**: nonsurgical removal of biofilm/deposits with curettes, (ultra)sonic scalers, or air-polishing.
- **NMA (Network Meta-Analysis)**: synthesis comparing multiple treatments simultaneously, including indirect comparisons.
- **RoB (Risk of Bias)**: methodological-quality appraisal of included studies.
- **PRO (Patient-Reported Outcome)**: outcome reported directly by the patient (e.g., comfort, satisfaction).
- **NRCT (Non-Randomized Controlled Trial)**: prospective controlled study without randomization.
