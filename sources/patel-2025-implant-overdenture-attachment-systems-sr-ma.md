## One-line Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis (14 RCTs/crossover trials, 2010–2020) comparing implant overdenture attachment systems: bar attachments showed superior retention, telescopic attachments highest patient satisfaction, locators best for limited interarch space / angulated implants, magnets the worst; implant survival did not differ significantly across attachments.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comparative synthesis of major mandibular overdenture attachment systems on three clinically meaningful outcomes (survival, tissue response, satisfaction).
- Ranks attachments by clinical role rather than declaring a single winner — clinician choice should depend on interarch space, implant angulation, hygiene access, and patient expectation.
- Confirms that attachment type does **not** drive implant survival once basic prosthetic principles are observed.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Databases: PubMed, Cochrane Central, ScienceDirect; manual search included.
- Inclusion: RCTs and crossover clinical trials 2010–2020, minimum 1-year follow-up.
- Risk-of-bias: Cochrane Collaboration tool (all 14 included studies = low risk).
- Statistical analysis: RevMan 5.4.
- Outcomes assessed: retention, peri-implant tissue response, prosthodontic complications, patient satisfaction, implant survival.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Bar attachments**: superior retention values overall.
- **Telescopic (double-crown) attachments**: highest patient satisfaction scores.
- **Locator attachments**: most flexible for limited interarch space (≤10 mm) and angulated implants (up to ~40°); easy retentive insert change.
- **Magnetic attachments**: lowest retention values; more soft-tissue changes (likely related to corrosion / housing wear).
- **Implant survival rate**: no significant association with attachment type.
- **Ball attachments**: intermediate retention but high prosthetic maintenance (matrix change).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Heterogeneity in attachment subtypes within the same family.
- Only 1-year minimum follow-up — long-term comparisons (≥5 yr) still limited.
- Patient satisfaction is multi-factorial and not standardized across studies.
- Most included studies addressed the mandible; maxillary OD evidence is sparser.

## 6. Related Work
- Builds on Sutariya 2021 (SR+MA on the same topic with fewer included studies).
- Complements Aung 2025 (mini-implant OD attachments, narrower scope).
- Should be read alongside Tayari 2024 (failure-risk factors of implant-supported OD).

## 7. Glossary
- **OD (overdenture)**: Removable prosthesis retained by implants or natural teeth via attachment elements.
- **Bar attachment**: Splinted bar connecting implants; clip in denture engages it.
- **Locator**: Low-profile resilient attachment; nylon inserts of varying retention.
- **Telescopic**: Inner primary (fixed to abutment) + outer secondary (in denture) crown pair.
- **Magnet attachment**: Magnetic keeper + magnet pair; low retention but easy seating.
