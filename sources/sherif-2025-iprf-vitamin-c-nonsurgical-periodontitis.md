## Why Ingested

Surveillance ingest on 이가탄(Igatan)-family OTC periodontal drug ingredients: 이가탄에프(CELC) lists **vitamin C (ascorbic acid)** among its actives, so this 3-arm RCT — testing vitamin-C-loaded I-PRF vs I-PRF vs mechanical therapy alone in non-surgical periodontal therapy — is a direct clinical-efficacy check on whether topically/locally delivered vitamin C adds benefit over mechanical debridement. It reinforces the recurring "nutraceutical adjuncts have limited clinical efficacy beyond mechanical therapy" finding seen in the 이가탄 CELC RCT [[wiki/periodontics/hong-2019-randomized-double-blind-placebo-controlled-multicenter]] and in CoQ10 adjunct evidence.

## One-line Summary

3-arm double-blind RCT (n=45, stage-II grade A periodontitis) showing that injectable platelet-rich fibrin (I-PRF) with or without vitamin C added NO clinical benefit (BOP/PD/CAL/PI/bone) over professional mechanical plaque removal (PMPR) alone at 6 months; the only adjunct advantage was lower post-operative pain.

## 한줄요약

3군 이중맹검 RCT (n=45, 2기 grade A 치주염): 비타민C를 넣은 주사형 혈소판농축피브린(I-PRF)이든 그냥 I-PRF든, 기계적 치석제거(PMPR) 단독 대비 6개월 시점에서 BOP·PD·CAL·PI·골 변화에 추가 임상이득이 없었고, 유일한 차이는 술후 통증이 낮았다는 점뿐이었다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Injectable platelet-rich fibrin with vitamin C as an adjunct to non-surgical periodontal therapy in the treatment of stage-II periodontitis: a randomized controlled clinical trial
- **Authors**: Sherif MA, Anter E, Graetz C, El-Sayed KF
- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health 2025;25(1):772
- **Year**: 2025 (published 2025-05-23)
- **DOI**: 10.1186/s12903-025-06115-x
- **PMID**: 40410805 / **PMCID**: PMC12100897
- **Study type**: Double-blinded, parallel-arm randomized controlled clinical trial (1:1:1), 6-month follow-up
- **Setting**: Oral Medicine and Periodontology, Faculty of Dentistry, Cairo University, Egypt (recruited Dec 2021–Jul 2023)

## 2. Key Contributions

- **First** trial to test vitamin-C-loaded I-PRF as a local adjunct in **non-surgical** periodontal therapy (NSPT) of stage-II grade A periodontitis (prior VitC-PRF evidence was surgical, stage-III intraosseous defects).
- Bottom line: apart from a reduction in post-operative pain, **I-PRF ± vitamin C did not add any clinical benefit over PMPR alone** — no intergroup difference in the primary outcome (BOP) or any secondary clinical/radiographic outcome.
- Provides a clean negative comparator showing that for shallow-to-moderate (stage-II) pockets, mechanical debridement alone captures most of the achievable improvement, leaving little headroom for biologic/nutraceutical adjuncts.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: 3 parallel arms, 1:1:1 allocation, computer-generated concealed randomization; outcome assessor + biostatistician blinded (operator/patient blinding not feasible).
- **Arms (n=15 each, total n=45)**:
  - Test: **PMPR + I-PRF/VitC** (250 µg/ml vitamin C added to blood; 2500 µg total into 10 ml)
  - Control 2: **PMPR + I-PRF**
  - Control 1: **PMPR alone**
- **Patients**: systemically healthy adults, stage-II (interdental CAL 3–4 mm, bone loss ≤33%, no tooth loss) grade A periodontitis; excluded smokers, pregnant, recent perio/antibiotic/anti-inflammatory use.
- **I-PRF prep**: low-speed centrifugation (60 g / 700 rpm, 3 min), upper liquid layer; delivered to the single deepest pocket via stent, retained 15 min.
- **Primary outcome**: bleeding on probing (BOP). **Secondary**: PD, CAL, gingival margin (GM), plaque index (PI), horizontal/vertical/total radiographic bone change, post-op pain (VAS days 2 & 3).
- **Sample size**: powered on BOP (effect size 1.177, α=0.05, power 0.8 → 13/group, inflated to 15). **No dropouts** (all 45 completed 6 months).
- **Stats**: repeated-measures ANOVA (PD, parametric); Kruskal-Wallis / Friedman / Dunn (non-parametric); binary logistic regression for BOP predictors.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Primary — BOP (% of sites positive):** No intergroup difference at 3 mo (p=0.185) or 6 mo (p=0.301). All groups changed over time (BOP rose at 3 mo, fell by 6 mo). At 6 mo BOP+: VitC 20% (3/15), I-PRF 33.3% (5/15), PMPR 46.7% (7/15) — numerically lowest in test group but NOT significant.

**PD reduction (mm, vs baseline):** no intergroup difference at any timepoint (3 mo p=0.833; 6 mo p=0.961).
| Time | I-PRF/VitC | I-PRF | PMPR |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3 mo | 1.73 ± 0.59 | 1.60 ± 0.51 | 1.67 ± 0.49 |
| 6 mo | 1.73 ± 0.59 | 1.67 ± 0.49 | 1.73 ± 0.59 |

**CAL gain (mm, vs baseline):** intergroup NS at 6 mo (p=0.056); a transient significant intergroup difference appeared at 3 mo (p=0.029) favoring the VitC group, but it disappeared by 6 mo.
| Time | I-PRF/VitC | I-PRF | PMPR |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3 mo | 1.33 ± 0.49 | 1.07 ± 0.59 | 0.93 ± 0.59 |
| 6 mo | 1.33 ± 0.49 | 1.20 ± 0.56 | 0.93 ± 0.59 |

**Change in GM (mm, recession):** intergroup NS (3 mo p=0.258; 6 mo p=0.205). At 6 mo: VitC 0.4 ± 0.51, I-PRF 0.33 ± 0.49, PMPR 0.73 ± 0.70.

**PI (plaque):** increased over time in all groups (compliance drift); no intergroup difference at any timepoint.

**Radiographic bone (total/horizontal/vertical):** no intergroup difference in *amount of change* at any timepoint. Some within-group bone gain (VitC and I-PRF arms gained total bone over time; PMPR did not), but between-group changes were NS. Baseline vertical-bone differed between groups (imbalance, a limitation).

**Pain (VAS, median):** significant intergroup difference — I-PRF arms lower than PMPR.
| Time | I-PRF/VitC | I-PRF | PMPR | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Day 2 | 1 (0–4) | 0 (0–6) | 4 (0–8) | 0.021 |
| Day 3 | 0 (0–2) | 0 (0–6) | 4 (0–8) | 0.001 |

**Regression (BOP at 6 mo):** only **gender** was a significant predictor (OR 0.059 for female, p=0.040); treatment group, age, baseline PD/CAL/GM/defect-angle/bone all non-significant.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Shallow-to-moderate stage-II defects with wide range of defect morphologies/angles → low absolute healing headroom (floor effect), reducing ability to detect adjunct benefit.
- Young, lower-socioeconomic, symptomatic-treatment-seeking cohort with **poor self-performed plaque control** (PI rose over time) and high baseline tissue responsiveness (first-ever perio therapy) — could mask adjunct effects.
- Baseline vertical-bone imbalance between groups; 2D radiography (not CBCT) for complex 3D defects.
- I-PRF chosen over newer formulations (C-PRF, Alb-PRF/e-PRF, horizontal centrifugation) that release more growth factors — may have underestimated PRF effect.
- Authors call for larger trials with better plaque control; note I-PRF's potential as an autologous drug carrier still deserves study.

## 6. Related Work

- Vitamin-C-loaded PRF improved CAL/PD when applied **surgically** to stage-III intraosseous defects (cited; contrast in disease severity explains the present null result).
- Enamel matrix derivative (EMD) as NSPT adjunct in moderate pockets — similarly failed to add benefit (concordant).
- Systematic reviews showing autologous platelet concentrates DO help — but mostly in stage-III / moderate-to-severe periodontitis, not stage-II.
- 이가탄 CELC RCT (Hong 2019) — vitamin-C-containing oral OTC adjunct; same theme of limited adjunctive efficacy beyond mechanical/baseline care.

## 7. Glossary

- **I-PRF** — injectable (liquid) platelet-rich fibrin; low-speed centrifugation (~60 g, 3 min) yields a fluid that stays injectable ~15 min before gelling, acting as an autologous growth-factor scaffold/drug carrier.
- **PMPR** — professional mechanical plaque removal (supra- + subgingival debridement); the evidence-based "gold standard" core of non-surgical periodontal therapy.
- **NSPT** — non-surgical periodontal therapy.
- **BOP / PD / CAL / GM / PI** — bleeding on probing / probing depth / clinical attachment level / gingival margin level / plaque index.
- **Stage-II grade A periodontitis** — moderate severity (interdental CAL 3–4 mm, bone loss ≤33%), slow progression rate.
- **VitC** — vitamin C (ascorbic acid); antioxidant + collagen-synthesis co-factor, here loaded into I-PRF at 250 µg/ml.
