## Why Ingested

Identifies the specific failure origins (strength-limiting defects) in CAD/CAM full-contour zirconia from milling and intrinsic processing — bridges material science to clinical fracture risk. Directly extends [[dental-materials/zirconia/chopra-2024-mechanical-characteristics-zirconia-dentistry]] by showing where and how fractures initiate in clinically processed zirconia, and informs which surface treatment (polishing vs. as-sintered) changes the flaw population driving strength and reliability.

## One-line Summary

In vitro fractographic study (n=168 specimens, 7 zirconia materials, 3–5 mol% Y) showing CAM-milling defects dominate as-sintered strength while polishing shifts failure to intrinsic subsurface pores and grain clusters.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic fractographic characterization of both CAM-milling (extrinsic) and processing (intrinsic) defects in dental CAD/CAM zirconia across multiple yttria contents (3Y, 4Y, 5Y)
- Demonstrates that as-sintered (AS) specimens have significantly LOWER flexural strength than high-polished (HP) counterparts due to milling-induced surface defects
- Shows that polishing shifts the flaw population from extrinsic (surface) to intrinsic (subsurface/volume): pores, large grain clusters, inclusions, corner-located critical flaws
- Reveals that Weibull modulus (reliability) generally decreases after polishing — more variable intrinsic flaw population despite higher mean strength
- 5Y-PSZ behavior is heterogeneous: two brands show no effect of polishing; one brand shows significant strength increase but reliability decrease

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 7 commercial CAD/CAM zirconia blanks: various yttria contents (3Y, 4Y, 5Y, including 3 high-translucent 5Y groups)
- Bend bars (2.0 × 4.0 × 25.0 mm³) milled from pre-sintered pucks using a 5-axis CAM system (DWX-51D, Roland)
- Sintering at 1530–1550°C for 2 h per manufacturer instructions
- Two groups: as-sintered (AS, n=12/material) and high-polished (HP, n=12/material) — 3-step chairside polishing system (Perla-Dia Z) + goat-hair brush + Zircon-Brite paste
- Four-point flexure testing (1/4-point configuration, inner/outer span 10/20 mm)
- Fractographic analysis: FE-SEM + EDS to identify fracture origins
- Statistical analysis: Weibull analysis (characteristic strength + Weibull modulus)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **AS flexural strength** < HP flexural strength across all tested materials (significant)
- **AS fracture origins**: predominantly extrinsic — CAM-milling gouges, scratches, edge chips with microcracks on sintered surface
- **HP fracture origins**: intrinsic — subsurface pores, large grain clusters, inclusions, corner-located critical flaws
- **Weibull modulus (HP)**: generally lower than AS, though not always statistically significant — indicates decreased reliability with polishing
- **5Y (high-translucent) group behavior**:
  - Two 5Y materials: strength and reliability unaffected by polishing (milling damage already minimal or self-healed during sintering)
  - One 5Y material: significant strength increase + significant reliability decrease after polishing
- **EDS analysis**: detected occasional contaminant inclusions (Al, Si, Ca, Fe) at fracture origins — suggests powder contamination or milling bur wear particles
- Clinical implication: milling-induced defects in full-contour zirconia restorations may act as fracture origins under occlusal loading if not properly addressed by polishing or glazing

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In vitro only — no clinical specimens or simulated aging (no LTD/fatigue cycling)
- Only one milling machine and one bur set per material; results may vary with different CAD/CAM systems
- Polishing protocol (chairside, manual) may not represent all clinical polishing workflows
- Authors recommend: optimized blank processing and milling protocols for pre-sintered zirconia; more standardized post-sintering surface finishing to reduce clinically critical flaw populations

## 7. Glossary

- **AS (as-sintered)**: Specimen as it comes from the sintering furnace, no further surface treatment
- **HP (high-polished)**: As-sintered specimen subjected to chairside polishing to mirror finish
- **Fractography**: Systematic analysis of fracture surfaces to identify failure origins and crack propagation patterns
- **Weibull modulus (m)**: Statistical measure of reliability/scatter — higher m = more consistent strength
- **Extrinsic defect**: Defect introduced during processing (milling, grinding)
- **Intrinsic defect**: Defect inherent to the material from powder/sintering (pores, grain clusters, inclusions)
- **5Y-PSZ**: 5 mol% yttria partially stabilized zirconia — high translucency, lower strength than 3Y-TZP
- **EDS**: Energy-dispersive X-ray spectroscopy — elemental composition analysis at fracture origin
