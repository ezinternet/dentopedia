## One-line Summary
Overview SR of 38 systematic reviews (~10,000 zirconia restorations) showing satisfactory 5-year survival for tooth- and implant-supported zirconia crowns and FDPs, with chipping of veneering ceramic as the predominant technical complication, addressable by monolithic design.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Dentistry, Volume 111, August 2021, Article 103723
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.jdent.2021.103723
- **Study type**: Overview/summary of systematic reviews
- **Search period**: MEDLINE, EMBASE, Trip Medical, Cochrane Library up to February 2021
- **Included reviews**: 38 systematic reviews published 2006–2021, based on ~128 in vivo studies, ~10,000 zirconia restorations

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive evidence synthesis for zirconia restorations across four prosthetic categories: tooth-supported (TS) single crowns (SC), TS multi-unit fixed dental prostheses (FDP), implant-supported (IS) SCs, and IS FDPs
- Identifies chipping of veneering ceramic as the most common technical complication
- Discusses long-term effects of hard, wear-resistant zirconia on the masticatory system — an underexplored area
- Notes that reviews on monolithic zirconia clinical outcomes are rare despite increasing clinical uptake

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Eligibility**: Systematic reviews on zirconia-based prosthodontic restorations
- **Study selection**: Two-author independent screening; Cohen's kappa = 0.917 (almost perfect agreement)
- **Bias assessment**: Assessed for included systematic reviews
- **Outcomes**: Cumulative survival rates (5-year), complication rates (technical and biological)
- **PRISMA** compliance for overview methodology

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
**5-year cumulative survival rates:**
- Tooth-supported single crowns (TS SC): 91.2%–95.9%
- Tooth-supported FDP (TS FDP): 89.4%–100%
- Implant-supported single crowns (IS SC): 97.1%–97.6%
- Implant-supported FDP (IS FDP): 93.0%–100%

**Technical complications (ranked by frequency):**
1. Chipping (veneering fracture) — most common
2. Framework fracture
3. Loss of retention
4. Marginal discrepancies/discoloration
5. Occlusal roughness
6. Abutment/screw loosening (IS only)

**Esthetic complication:**
- Color mismatch — the only esthetic complication identified

**Biological complications:**
- Caries, endodontic complications, tooth fracture, periodontal disease, abrasion/attrition, persisting pain, high sensitivity, peri-implantitis, soft tissue issues

**Masticatory system:**
- Impact of hard, highly wear-resistant zirconia on opposing teeth and TMJ remains unclear; bruxism patients understudied

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Clinical reviews on monolithic zirconia specifically are rare; most data on veneered zirconia
- Bruxism patients not adequately represented in included studies
- Long-term (>10 years) data scarce
- Heterogeneity in study designs and follow-up periods across included reviews
- No prospective registration of overview protocol

## 6. Related Work
- Pjetursson et al. 2026 — SR+MA with survival data including monolithic designs, direct material comparisons
- Waltenberger et al. 2025 — clinical guideline on all-ceramic implant restorations

## 7. Glossary
- **Chipping**: Fracture or spalling of veneering ceramic layer from zirconia coping/framework
- **TS SC**: Tooth-supported single crown
- **IS SC**: Implant-supported single crown
- **FDP**: Fixed dental prosthesis (bridge, multi-unit)
- **3Y-TZP**: Yttria-stabilized tetragonal zirconia polycrystal (classic high-strength zirconia)
- **Monolithic restoration**: Full-contour restoration without a separate veneering layer
- **Overview of reviews**: Systematic synthesis of multiple existing systematic reviews on the same topic
