## Why Ingested

[[radiology/stervik-2024-radiation-exposure-during-orthodontic-treatment]]의 "5–10일 배경방사선" 계산에 대한 대형 코호트 독립 보강. 소아에서 CBCT의 선량 기여도(집단의 70%)와 배경방사선 일수 환산을 명시해, 자연배경방사선·항공 피폭과의 직접 비교를 위한 앵커 제공.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective cohort (n=7,150 children, 12,252 dental X-rays): 2D exams median <20 µSv; CBCT median 127 µSv; highest CBCT = 34.1 days of natural background radiation.

## 한줄요약

후향적 코호트(소아 7,150명, 방사선 촬영 12,252건): 2D 검사 중앙값 <20 µSv; CBCT 중앙값 127 µSv; 최고 CBCT = 자연 배경방사선 **34.1일분**.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Largest pediatric dental dosimetry cohort study examining both 2D and CBCT exposures.
- Key finding: conventional 2D exams (intraoral, panoramic, cephalometric) remain in the low-dose range (<20 µSv median); CBCT dominates collective dose (70% contribution).
- **Background-equivalent anchors**: highest CBCT dose = 34.1 days of natural background radiation (ages 11–15 most affected).
- Life Attributable Risk (LAR) analysis confirms CBCT carries significantly higher stochastic risk than 2D for pediatric patients.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Pediatric population: 7,150 children and young adults over 2 years (single institution).
- 12,252 dental X-ray examinations: 4,220 intraoral, 1,324 cephalometric, 5,284 panoramic, 1,424 CBCT.
- Two groups: CBCT group vs 2D-only group.
- Effective doses corrected for age at exposure and device settings (mA, FOV) using logarithmic fit equations.
- Cumulative dose, per-caput collective dose, and LAR calculated.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Parameter | 2D group | CBCT group |
|---|---|---|
| Median effective dose | <20 µSv | 127.2 µSv |
| Median cumulative dose | <20 µSv | 156.5 µSv |
| Collective dose contribution | 30% | **70%** |
| Peak per-caput collective | — | 184 µSv |
| Highest dose (ages 11–15) | — | **≡ 34.1 days background** |

- CBCT cumulative dose increases significantly in ages 11–14 (orthodontic treatment period).
- LAR and Relative Radiation Level (RRL) significantly higher for CBCT under 18.
- Overall CBCT doses remain in the lower levels of medical-imaging risk, but 70% dose contribution from 11% of exams highlights prioritization need.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-center Romanian cohort — CBCT device mix and protocols may differ from other settings.
- Natural background radiation reference: ~2,400 µSv/year (Romania ~6.6 µSv/day) — varies by country/altitude.
- Abstract-only available; LAR calculation details not fully accessible.

## 6. Related Work

- Stervik 2024: independently confirms "days-of-background" framing for orthodontic imaging.
- Jacobs 2018: CBCT clinical indications in implant dentistry, dose-benefit context.

## 7. Glossary

- **LAR**: Life Attributable Risk — lifetime probability of radiation-induced fatal cancer
- **RRL**: Relative Radiation Level — categorical scale for comparing medical imaging doses
- **Natural background radiation**: ~2,400–3,000 µSv/year globally (~6.6–8.2 µSv/day); varies by altitude, geology (radon), latitude (cosmic)
