## One-line Summary
Systematic review of 13 studies finding that cortical bone thickness generally correlates positively with implant primary stability (ISQ, IT), though evidence is preliminary and MBL correlations are inconsistent.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Materials 2021, 14, 7183 (MDPI)
- **Type**: Systematic review (PRISMA)
- **Focus question**: Is there any relationship between cortical bone thickness/density/anchorage and primary/secondary implant stability/MBL?
- **Studies included**: 13 (8 prospective clinical, 4 retrospective clinical, 1 cadaver)
- **Search**: MEDLINE, EMBASE, Cochrane, Scopus; no language restriction

## 2. Key Contributions
- Demonstrates that cortical bone thickness is a stronger predictor of primary stability than trabecular bone density in most studies
- Bicortical anchorage produces significantly higher IT and ISQ than monocortical engagement
- Minimum 1.0 mm cortical thickness recommended for mini-implants (Motoyoshi 2007)
- ISQ values may depend on cancellous bone availability as well as cortical engagement (Waechter 2017)
- MBL correlation with cortical thickness remains inconclusive

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Outcomes measured**: Cortical bone via CT/CBCT/tactile sensation; stability via IT, ISQ (RFA), Periotest (PTV)
- **Exclusions**: Case series, case reports, in vitro, cross-sectional, studies without statistical correlation analysis
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane Handbook; generally good quality with "some concerns" in randomization/blinding

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Strong positive correlations (cortical thickness → primary stability):
- Miyamoto 2005: r = 0.84, p < 0.0001 (ISQ ↔ cortical thickness)
- Salimov 2014: IT ↔ cortical density r = 0.935 (p < 0.001); ISQ ↔ cortical density r = 0.888 (p < 0.001)
- Alsaadi 2007: ISQ and PTV significantly related to cortical bone grade (p = 0.02, p < 0.0001)
- Chatvaratthana 2017: p < 0.001
- Mantovani 2018: **Bicortical anchorage** → highest IT and ISQ (p < 0.05)

### Weak or negative correlations:
- Dias 2016: No significant relationship — ISQ ↔ cortical thickness (r = 0.145, p = 0.292); MBL ↔ cortical thickness (r = −0.029, p = 0.832)
- Waechter 2017: ISQ depends on cancellous bone availability (r = 0.32); IT related to cortical bone height (r = 0.32)

### Key thresholds:
- Cortical thickness ≥ 1.0 mm + IT ≤ 10 Ncm → improved mini-implant success (Motoyoshi 2007)
- MBL < 1 mm/year (year 1), < 0.2 mm/year (subsequent) = success criteria (Misch)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small sample sizes in most included studies
- Heterogeneity in cortical bone measurement methods (CT, CBCT, tactile)
- Inconsistent ISQ threshold definitions
- MBL–cortical thickness relationship needs prospective studies
- Future: standardized cortical thickness assessment protocols; integrate cortical thickness into implant planning software

## 6. Related Work
- Albrektsson osseointegration criteria
- Misch success scale (MBL thresholds)
- RFA/ISQ methodology (Oh 2008 in this wiki)
- Bone quality: Lekholm-Zarb classification; Rosa 2024 bone type survival data

## 7. Glossary
- **IT (Insertion Torque)**: Rotational force required during implant placement — clinical proxy for primary stability
- **ISQ**: Implant Stability Quotient via RFA (0–100 scale)
- **PTV (Periotest Value)**: Percussion-based damping measurement; inversely related to stability
- **Bicortical anchorage**: Implant engages both cortical plates (crestal + apical); higher stability
- **Primary stability**: Mechanical stability immediately post-insertion
- **Secondary stability**: Biologic stability from osseointegration/bone remodeling
- **MBL (Marginal Bone Loss)**: Radiographic peri-implant bone loss over time
