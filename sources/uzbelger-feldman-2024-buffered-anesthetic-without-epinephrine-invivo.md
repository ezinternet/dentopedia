## Why Ingested

The wiki's buffered-LA evidence is all clinical-onset RCTs ([[wiki/drug/kalra-2024-buffered-lidocaine-articaine-extraction-rct]], [[wiki/drug/karunakar-2024-buffered-precooled-lidocaine-ianb-pulpitis]], [[wiki/drug/hamad-2025-buffered-lidocaine-infiltration-extraction-rct]], [[wiki/drug/gandhi-2022-preheated-buffered-lignocaine-ianb-pulpitis-rct]]); none addresses a *vasoconstrictor-free* buffered formulation. This bench+animal development paper extends that cluster upstream — engineering duration via osmolality/viscosity instead of epinephrine — and contrasts with the epinephrine-concentration question in [[wiki/drug/karm-2017-lidocaine-epinephrine-200000-third-molar]].

## One-line Summary

In-vivo (Sprague-Dawley rat) development study of a buffered, epinephrine-free 2% lidocaine formulation (LW/O/E "Sample 3A": lactated Ringer's + dextrose + amino-acid bitter-blockers, pH 6.7–7.0, 590–610 mOsm/kg): viscosity, injectability, anesthetic duration (RHTFL and hot-plate paw-withdrawal latency) comparable to commercial 2% lidocaine+1:100,000-epinephrine (LW/E) with reduced bitterness and only minor transient erythema/edema.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Novel LW/O/E formulation** removing epinephrine yet preserving duration by engineering osmolality (590–610 mOsm/kg via dextrose/amino acids) and viscosity, using a **lactated Ringer's vehicle** that buffers in-cartridge pH to ~6.7–7.0 and metabolizes to bicarbonate in vivo.
2. **Built-in bitterness suppression** (dextrose, sodium saccharin, L-arginine, glycine, glutamic acid) verified on an electronic-tongue (ETongue) platform.
3. **In-vivo demonstration** that an epinephrine-free buffered lidocaine matches commercial LW/E for anesthetic duration in two rat nociception assays.
4. Addresses the three classic LW/E patient complaints in one product: **injection sting** (acidic pH), **jittering** (vasoconstrictor), **bitter aftertaste**.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Formulation**: 6 candidates screened → Sample 3 optimal → variants 3A (2% lidocaine), 3B (1.9%), 3C (1.5%); osmolality held at 590–610 mOsm/kg, pH 6.7–7.0 (10 N NaOH adjusted); vehicle QS to 250 mL lactated Ringer's.
  - Sample 3A composition: lidocaine HCl monohydrate 5.332 g, sodium saccharin 0.225 g, dextrose 5 g, glutamic acid 1.25 g, glycine 0.5 g, citric acid 0.25 g, L-arginine 0.5 g.
- **Characterization**: HPLC (Shimadzu, Luna C18, 230 nm) drug content + forced-degradation stability; accelerated stability 40 °C up to 6 mo / 25 °C up to 12 mo; viscosity (Ostwald viscometer); injectability (digital tester, 1 mm/s); taste (ETongue, vs water S1 and commercial LW/E Octocaine-100 S2).
- **In-vivo (Sprague-Dawley rats)**:
  - **Local toxicity**: single intra-oral injection (anterior maxillary fornix), Draize scoring of erythema/eschar (0–4) and edema (0–4) at 1 h / 6 h / 24 h / 96 h, plus histology; comparators D5NS, D10, 0.9% saline.
  - **Efficacy / duration — RHTFL** (radiant-heat tail-flick latency): 0.05 mL tail injection, latency cutoff 10 s, measured to 270 min.
  - **Efficacy / duration — Hot plate** (50 °C planta): paw-withdrawal latency, cutoff 15 s, at 0/1/5/10/20/30/40/50/60/90/120 min.
  - Comparator throughout: commercial 2% lidocaine + 1:100,000 epinephrine (LW/E).
- **Statistics**: Mean ± SD; 95% CI; significance p < 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Physicochemical / characterization (Sample 3A vs LW/E)**
- Viscosity and injectability **comparable** to commercial LW/E.
- ETongue: **bitterness reduced** in LW/O/E samples vs LW/E.
- pH 6.7–7.0 (near-physiologic; LW/E acidic ~3.3) → predicted lower injection sting.

**Efficacy / anesthetic duration**

| Assay | LW/E (commercial) | Sample 3A (LW/O/E) | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hot-plate paw withdrawal | max 12.33 s at 5 min, declines ~60 min | max 10 s at 1 min, declines ~40 min | comparable (NS except 20 min) |
| RHTFL (tail-flick) | max 12.6 s at 20 min, effect to 270 min; baseline ~4.1 s | comparable | NS except 20, 50, 60 min |

- Overall: **paw-withdrawal and tail-flick latency comparable** for Sample 3A vs LW/E (p < 0.05); Samples 3B/3C (lower drug) showed reduced duration vs commercial at later time points.

**Local toxicity (Draize, Sample 3A)**
- Erythema/eschar 1.4 ± 0.6 (slight-to-well-defined) at 6 h, **resolving by 24 h**; edema minimal/absent; histology mild. Signs "minor and transient."
- Comparable to D10 solution; slightly more erythema than D5NS, all transient.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Animal model only** (rat thermal nociception; tail/paw, not pulpal) — no human dental efficacy data; oral pulpal onset/duration not directly measured.
- Surrogate efficacy endpoints (thermal latency), not clinical pulpal anesthesia depth or onset.
- High osmolality (~600 mOsm/kg) raises theoretical tissue-irritation/neurotoxicity questions needing clinical confirmation.
- Lower-dose variants (3B/3C) underperformed → the dose-sparing aim was not achieved at 2% equivalence.
- Patent/proprietary procedure ("proprietary procedure," reference [35]) limits full reproducibility.
- Next step: human RCT vs LW/E and vs buffered LW/E.

## 6. Related Work

- Buffered-lidocaine clinical RCTs (Kalra 2024, Karunakar 2024, Hamad 2025, Gandhi 2022) — clinical onset/comfort side of buffering.
- Becker & Reed 2012 — vasopressor rationale (duration via delayed absorption) this paper tries to replace with osmolality/viscosity.
- Scott et al. 1972; Hersh et al. — serum-concentration / absorption pharmacokinetics underpinning the epinephrine-removal rationale.

## 7. Glossary

- **LW/E** — 2% lidocaine HCl with 1:100,000 epinephrine (standard since 1948).
- **LW/O/E** — lidocaine without epinephrine (this study's buffered formulation).
- **RHTFL** — radiant-heat tail-flick latency (spinal-reflex nociception assay).
- **HP test** — hot-plate paw-withdrawal latency (supraspinal nociception assay).
- **Draize score** — standardized 0–4 erythema/eschar and edema grading.
- **ETongue** — electronic tongue for objective taste/bitterness quantification.
- **Lactated Ringer's** — buffered crystalloid; lactate → bicarbonate in vivo.
- **mOsm/kg** — osmolality; here engineered to ~600 to substitute for vasoconstrictor duration effect.
