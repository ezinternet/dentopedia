## One-line Summary
Retrospective study of 745 Neoss implants in 334 patients (5yr CSR 97.3% implant-level / 95.8% patient-level): low ISQ (<70, <75) at placement or loading associated with higher failure. ⚠ METHODS CORRECTION (2026-06-14): analysis is UNIVARIATE (chi-square/Fisher + UNADJUSTED odds ratios at ISQ thresholds) — NOT multivariate logistic regression; implant-within-patient clustering not modelled. For survival, age/gender/jaw/position/bone quantity/bone quality/diameter/length showed NO significant effect; only full-arch (vs partial) rehab and low ISQ did.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: International Journal of Dentistry 2019, Article ID 3209872
- DOI: 10.1155/2019/3209872
- Design: Retrospective cohort, 745 Neoss implants, multivariate analysis, 5-year follow-up

## 2. Key Contributions
- ISQ <70 at placement = significant failure risk predictor
- ISQ <75 also significant (broader risk threshold)
- Large-scale (745 implants) UNIVARIATE analysis of RFA + clinical factors over 5 years (no multivariable adjustment)

## 3. Methodology
- 745 Neoss implants (various sites, bone types D1-D4)
- ISQ measured at placement, 10 weeks, and annual follow-up
- Actuarial/life-table CSR (Table 6); Pearson chi-square & Fisher exact (categorical associations); Kruskal–Wallis & Mann–Whitney (ISQ across groups); UNADJUSTED odds ratios + 95% CI at ISQ thresholds 60/65/70/75 (Table 10). NO multivariable logistic regression; patient-level clustering not modelled. Statistician employed by Neoss (declared COI).
- Factors analyzed: ISQ, bone type, implant location, length, diameter, loading protocol

## 4. Key Results
- 5-year cumulative survival rate (CSR): 97.3%
- ISQ <70 at placement: significantly higher failure risk (p<0.05)
- ISQ <75 at placement also significant risk threshold
- Bone quality affected ISQ (stability) but NOT survival (survival p=0.740); bone quantity nonsignificant for survival (p=0.126)
- For SURVIVAL only full-arch rehab (p=0.003) and low ISQ were significant: <70 OR 3.9 (1.6–9.7) at insertion, 6.3 (1.9–20.2) at loading; <75 OR 6.9 (2.0–23.8) and 17.9 (2.3–142.9). Length/position/diameter NOT significant. ORs are unadjusted; CIs very wide (sparse events, 20 failures)

## 5. Limitations
- Retrospective design (selection/treatment biases)
- Single implant system (Neoss) — may not generalize
- ISQ device/software version not standardized across 5 years
