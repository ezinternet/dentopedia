## One-line Summary

Human cadaver correlation study (48 stepped-cylinder implants in 4 maxillae/mandibles) relating implant primary stability to CT bone mineral density and histomorphometric parameters of the implant site.

## 한줄요약

인체 cadaver 상관 연구(stepped-cylinder 임플란트 48개, 상·하악 4구) — 임플란트 일차안정성을 CT 골밀도·식립부 histomorphometric 지표와 상관 분석.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Early, direct correlation of primary stability with both BMD and histomorphometry in human bone.
- Uses CT-measured BMD as an objective site descriptor.
- Foundational reference for stability–bone-quality links underpinning modern ISQ/torque work.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Ex vivo human cadaver study; 4 unfixed maxillae/mandibles.
- 48 stepped cylinder screw implants installed.
- BMD assessed by computed tomography; histomorphometric parameters measured.
- Correlation of primary stability with BMD and histomorphometry.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Primary stability correlated with bone mineral density and histomorphometric parameters of implant sites.
- Establishes the bone-quality basis of measured primary stability.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Ex vivo cadaver; no healing/secondary stability.
- Small donor number (4 specimens); device/implant-specific.
