## One-line Summary
JPIS 2025: Retrospective (n=528 implants, 412 patients, 3.5yr after ARP): PBE <1.1 mm = significant predictor of implant failure (HR 2.50); 89.0% cumulative success rate over 3.5 years.

## 1. Key Results
- n=528 implants, 412 patients (2014–2020, Kyung Hee University)
- Cumulative success rate 89.0% at 3.5±1.8 years; 43 failed implants
- Cox proportional hazards model: pristine bone engagement (PBE) <1.1 mm → HR 2.50 (95%CI 1.34–4.67, P=0.004)
- PBE ≥1.1 mm decreases probability of implant failure after ARP

## 2. Clinical Implications
- After ARP, ensure implant engages ≥1.1 mm of pristine native bone
- PBE is a modifiable factor — surgical planning should optimize implant depth in quality bone
- 89% success over 3.5 years confirms ARP as viable treatment pathway for implant sites
