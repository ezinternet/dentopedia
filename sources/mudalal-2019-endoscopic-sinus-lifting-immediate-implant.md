## One-line Summary
Case report of a 40-year-old female with severe posterior maxillary atrophy (residual bone height 3.53 mm): a PESS (PRF + Endoscope + Simultaneous implant placement + Sinus floor elevation) technique allowed transcrestal sinus elevation under direct endoscopic visualization with PRF as graft and a 12-mm implant placed simultaneously; ISQ increased from initial to 67.4 at 3 mo and 71.0 at 6 mo, with stable bone height and full-ceramic crown delivery at 3 months.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First report combining **endoscope-controlled** transcrestal sinus floor elevation with simultaneous implant placement using **PRF as sole graft** in a case with residual bone height (RBH) < 4 mm.
- Coins/popularizes the **PESS** acronym (PRF + Endoscope + Simultaneous implant + Sinus floor elevation).
- Reduces total treatment time from a conventional ~12 months to 3 months while maintaining adequate primary stability and uneventful healing.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 40-year-old female non-smoker, missing #16 (extracted years prior), RBH 3.53 mm on CBCT.
- Punch incision (no flap) using a 4.8-mm tissue bur (Nobel Biocare); pilot drill leaving 1 mm to sinus floor.
- Up-fracture of cortical sinus floor with 4.3 mm rounded osteotome (Kohler); endoscopic monitoring via POLYDIAGNOST endoscope.
- 3 PRF clots prepared (10 mL whole blood × 3, glass-coated tubes, no anticoagulant, 3000 rpm × 10 min) pressed into the sinus to fill elevated space; second elevation to total 12 mm height.
- Implant: Straumann 4.8 × 12 mm, torque 30 N·cm, healing cap placed.
- Postoperative: oral metronidazole 300 mg TID and amoxicillin 500 mg TID × 3 d; 0.12% CHX × 7 d.
- Follow-up: ISQ measurements (RFA) in 5 directions × 3 readings at 2, 3, and 6 mo; CBCT at 2 mo.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Sinus elevation height**: total 12 mm (from RBH 3.53 mm).
- **Insertion torque**: 30 N·cm.
- **ISQ**: 67.4 at 3 mo; 71.0 at 6 mo.
- **Healing**: uneventful; CBCT at 2 mo showed good osseointegration trajectory.
- **Restoration**: zirconia-ceramic crown delivered at 3 mo.
- **6-mo follow-up**: bone height stable, no peri-implant pathology.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Single case — no statistical inference.
- Short follow-up (6 mo).
- Endoscopic equipment (POLYDIAGNOST) not widely available; learning curve undocumented.
- PRF-only grafting may behave differently in larger lifts or with bony defects requiring more volume.

## 6. Related Work
- Summers 1994 — osteotome transcrestal technique.
- Tatum 1986; Boyne & James 1980 — lateral window foundations.
- Choukroun — PRF protocol.
- Ayubian Markazi 2018 — retrospective series of combined immediate + transcrestal lift.
- Liu 2019 — RCT comparing immediate vs delayed with transcrestal lift.

## 7. Glossary
- **PESS**: PRF + Endoscope + Simultaneous implant placement + Sinus floor elevation.
- **PRF (platelet-rich fibrin)**: Choukroun's autologous fibrin matrix from centrifuged whole blood, no anticoagulant.
- **RBH (Residual Bone Height)**: alveolar bone height below sinus floor before grafting.
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: 1–100 RFA-derived stability score.
- **Schneiderian membrane**: thin bilaminar membrane lining maxillary sinus.
