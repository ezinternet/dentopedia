[[implants/isq/]] 카테고리에 임플란트 거시형태(Macrogeometry, 매크로지오메트리) × ITV/ISQ 직접비교 RCT 부족; split-mouth 디자인이라 교란 최소화. 특히 healing chamber가 포함된 변형 매크로지오메트리가 ISQ 및 골표지자에 미치는 영향을 prospective double-blind split-mouth 설계로 평가한 점이 기존 관찰연구와 차별화됨.

## One-line Summary

Split-mouth RCT (n=18) — modified macrogeometry implants (healing chambers) showed similar ISQ/primary stability to conventional implants despite lower insertion torque, with significantly reduced peri-implant TNF-α at early healing.

## 한줄요약

Split-mouth RCT (n=18) — healing chamber가 있는 변형 매크로지오메트리 임플란트는 초기 삽입 토크가 낮음에도 기존 임플란트와 ISQ/일차안정성이 동등했으며, 초기 치유 시 peri-implant TNF-α는 유의하게 감소했다.

## 2. Key Contributions

abstract-only — full text not retrieved

- Demonstrates that modified macrogeometry (healing chambers) implants achieve equivalent ISQ-measured primary stability to conventional implants even when insertion torque (IT) is significantly lower.
- Provides peri-implant crevicular fluid data (at 7, 14, 30, 90 days) showing TNF-α reduction in the healing chamber group — a potential signal for favorable osteoclastic environment.
- Split-mouth design controls for patient-level confounders (bone density, surgical technique, healing capacity), strengthening causal inference.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

abstract-only — full text not retrieved

- **Design**: Prospective, controlled, randomized, double-blind, split-mouth RCT
- **Participants**: 18 patients with bilateral posterior edentulism
- **Groups**:
  - Test: implant with modified macrogeometry (healing chambers)
  - Control: conventional macrogeometry implant
- **Measurements**:
  - Insertion torque (IT): at surgery
  - ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient) via resonance frequency analysis (RFA): immediately post-placement, at 45 and 90 days
  - Peri-implant crevicular fluid bone markers: at 7, 14, 30, and 90 days (immunoenzymatic analysis)
- **Statistics**: Paired t test for IT; generalized linear mixed models (repeated measures) for ISQ and immunoenzymatic variables; α = .05

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

abstract-only — full text not retrieved

- **Insertion torque**: Significantly lower in test (modified macrogeometry) group (P<.05)
- **Primary stability (ISQ)**: No significant difference between groups at any time point (P>.05)
- **TNF-α**: Significantly lower in test group (P<.05)
- **Other bone markers**: No statistically significant differences between groups (P>.05)
- **Clinical implication**: Lower IT does not necessarily predict inferior ISQ stability; healing-chamber geometry may partially decouple torque from stability

## 7. Glossary

- **Macrogeometry (매크로지오메트리)**: overall three-dimensional shape of an implant — thread design (pitch, depth, width), body taper, and structural features such as healing chambers
- **Healing chamber (힐링 챔버)**: concave or recessed zones in implant body/thread designed to trap blood clot and promote bone ingrowth
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient, 임플란트 안정성 지수)**: dimensionless 1–100 scale from RFA; higher = greater stability
- **RFA (Resonance Frequency Analysis, 공명주파수분석)**: non-invasive method measuring implant-bone stiffness via transducer vibration
- **Insertion torque (IT, 삽입 토크)**: rotational resistance at final seating; reflects bone-implant friction but does not always predict later RFA-based stability
- **TNF-α (Tumor Necrosis Factor-alpha, 종양괴사인자-알파)**: pro-inflammatory cytokine associated with osteoclast activation; lower peri-implant levels may indicate reduced bone resorption pressure
- **Split-mouth design (스플릿-마우스 디자인)**: within-patient controlled trial where contralateral sites receive different treatments, minimizing inter-individual variability
