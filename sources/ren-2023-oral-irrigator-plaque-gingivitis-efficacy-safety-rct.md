## Why Ingested

Most water-flosser RCTs we hold are either orthodontic-population or periodontitis-adjunct contexts; this is a **general-population (gingivitis, non-orthodontic, non-periodontitis) 12-week efficacy AND safety RCT** that fills that gap and is first-of-kind in formally measuring oral-irrigator pain/dentin-hypersensitivity via VAS. It reinforces [[interdental-cleaning/mancinelli-lyle-2024-water-flosser-vs-interdental-brush-rct]] on oral-irrigator gingival-inflammation efficacy and pairs with the periodontitis-adjunct evidence in [[interdental-cleaning/liu-2025-water-flossing-adjunct-nspt-periodontitis-rct]].

## One-line Summary

12-week single-blind parallel RCT (n=90 gingivitis patients; FAS 88) — toothbrush + WaterPik oral irrigator significantly outperformed toothbrush alone on gingival indices (MGI/BI/BOP% from 4 weeks, all p<0.001 by 8–12 weeks) and on plaque (T-QH significant from 8 weeks), with no serious adverse events and no added pain/dentin hypersensitivity.

## 한줄요약

12주 단일맹검 평행 RCT(치은염 환자 90명; FAS 88) — 칫솔질 + WaterPik 구강세정기(Oral Irrigator)가 칫솔질 단독보다 치은지표(MGI/BI/BOP%)를 4주부터, 치태(T-QH)를 8주부터 유의하게 개선했고, 중대 이상반응 없이 통증·상아질과민증 증가도 없었다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: The Efficacy and Safety of Oral Irrigator on the Control of Dental Plaque and Gingivitis: A Randomized, Single-Blind, Parallel-Group Clinical Trial
- **Authors**: Ren X, He J, Cheng R, Chen Y, Xiang Y, Zhang Y, et al.
- **Journal**: International Journal of Environmental Research and Public Health, 2023;20(4):3726
- **DOI**: 10.3390/ijerph20043726
- **PMID**: 36834421 / **PMCID**: PMC9965011
- **Study type**: Randomized, single-blind, parallel-group clinical trial (12-week)
- **Setting**: West China Hospital of Stomatology, Sichuan University, Chengdu, China (ChiCTR2100054254)

## 2. Key Contributions

1. General-population efficacy evidence: in **gingivitis patients (not orthodontic, not periodontitis)**, an oral irrigator (OI) with adjustable 10–100 psi as a toothbrushing adjunct significantly improved both plaque and gingival inflammation over 12 weeks.
2. **Safety is a co-primary focus** — adverse events captured via electronic diary + clinical exam; pain and dentin hypersensitivity assessed by VAS (first study to do so for OIs), and gingival recession monitored.
3. Dose-response signal: average irrigation pressure positively correlated with BOP% reduction (rho=0.330, p=0.027); higher tolerable pressure → more gingival-inflammation benefit.
4. Longer follow-up (12 weeks) than the typical ~4-week OI trial, with both intention-to-treat (FAS) and per-protocol (PPS) analyses; FAS given precedence.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Participants**: 298 screened → 90 enrolled, gingivitis (≥20 BOP sites, T-QH >1.5 at baseline), 18–65 y, ≥20 permanent teeth. Excluded: smokers, periodontitis, gingival recession, regular adjunct-tool users, orthodontic appliances, relevant systemic disease/medications.
- **Groups (1:1)**: Test = manual toothbrush + OI (WaterPik ION Professional Cordless Water Flosser, GT17; 10–100 psi, 10 settings) used twice daily ~90 s before brushing, recommended comfortable pressure ≥ level 4. Control = identical toothbrush + toothpaste alone. Both used modified Bass brushing twice daily.
- **Blinding/randomization**: central computer randomization by independent third party; investigators/examiners blinded (single-blind — participants not blinded).
- **Outcome indices** (baseline, 4, 8, 12 weeks): Turesky-Modified Quigley-Hein Plaque Index (T-QH, after disclosing agent, 4 sites/tooth); Modified Gingival Index (MGI, 4 sites/tooth); Bleeding Index (BI, 0–5 scale); % sites with bleeding on probing (BOP%, 6 sites/tooth, CPI probe). Primary = gingivitis indices at 12 weeks; secondary = plaque indices, 4-week indices, safety.
- **Safety measures**: adverse events (electronic diary + exam), gingival recession (apical migration of margin), pain & dentin hypersensitivity (electronic VAS), soft/hard tissue trauma and allergic/mucosal lesions across all intraoral sites.
- **Analysis sets**: FAS (≥1 post-intervention assessment, LOCF imputation), PPS (completers without significant protocol deviation), SS (safety). Sample size 90 (76 needed for 10% MGI difference, 5% alpha, 80% power, +20% attrition).
- **Compliance**: defined as ≥2×/day brushing or irrigation on >80% of study days; tracked by electronic diary.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Disposition**: 2 control dropouts after baseline → FAS n=88 (test 45 / control 43). PPS n=71 (80.7%; test 33 / control 38). Exclusions mostly lost-to-follow-up (n=13), low compliance (3, test), prohibited medications (3).
- **Baseline**: well-balanced demographics and indices (mean age ~30.5 y, more females, no baseline index difference p>0.05).
- **Gingival inflammation (FAS, test vs control)**:
  - 4 weeks: MGI p=0.017, BI p=0.001, BOP% p=0.001 favoring test; T-QH not yet significant (p=0.102).
  - 8 & 12 weeks: all of MGI, BI, BOP% p<0.001 favoring test.
- **Plaque (T-QH, FAS)**: significantly lower in test at 8 weeks (p=0.033) and 12 weeks (p=0.006); not at 4 weeks. Note T-QH rose in both groups after 4 weeks; control T-QH increased significantly at 8–12 weeks (p=0.004) while test change non-significant.
- **Reduction-percentage**: higher reductions in test for BI and BOP% at baseline–4 weeks (both p<0.001); MGI and BOP% at 4–8 weeks (p=0.007, p<0.001); 8–12 week interval reductions similar between groups.
- **Pressure dose-response**: average used pressure 52.13 ± 11.04 psi (range 38.55–82.35); positively correlated with BOP% reduction (rho=0.330, p=0.027); no significant correlation with T-QH/MGI/BI reduction.
- **Safety**: 37 adverse events total, **none severe**. Transient gingival bleeding possibly OI-associated in 3 test participants (6.67%). Overall AE counts similar between groups (test 18/45 = 40.0%; control 19/43 = 44.2%; p=0.829). VAS pain/dentin hypersensitivity near 0 in both groups, no between-group difference (p>0.05). No gingival recession change, no OI-related soft/hard tissue trauma. OI compliance high (median 98.81% of days, 25th pct 96.45%).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-blind only — participants knew their group; possible Hawthorne effect (control also improved, partly attributable to taught modified Bass method).
- **No head-to-head comparator** against floss or interdental brush — only vs toothbrushing alone; superiority trials vs other interdental aids are still needed.
- Maximum-pressure (100 psi) safety not isolated; participants self-selected comfortable pressure. Gingival recession needs longer follow-up to manifest.
- Single-center Chinese gingivitis population; PPS analysis tends to exaggerate effect (FAS prioritized).

## 6. Related Work

- Systematic reviews report OIs improve gingival-inflammation indices but show inconsistent visible-plaque reduction as a brushing adjunct — this trial's 4-week plaque null but 8–12-week plaque benefit matches that mixed picture.
- American Academy of Periodontology position: irrigation pressure 80–90 psi tolerable; this trial's used range (38.55–82.35 psi) sits within safe/tolerable bounds for gingivitis patients.
- Mechanistic literature: OIs may disrupt plaque maturation, reduce biofilm thickness, lower subgingival pathogens, and reduce IL-1β/PGE2/ROS (with some conflicting GCF biomarker results).

## 7. Glossary

- **OI (Oral Irrigator / Water Flosser)**: device delivering a pulsating water jet to remove soft debris and unattached plaque from interdental/gingival-margin areas.
- **T-QH (Turesky-Modified Quigley-Hein Plaque Index)**: disclosing-agent-based plaque scoring index.
- **MGI (Modified Gingival Index)**: non-invasive visual gingival inflammation index.
- **BI (Bleeding Index)**: 0–5 ordinal gingival bleeding severity scale (0 healthy, 5 spontaneous bleeding).
- **BOP% (Bleeding on Probing %)**: percentage of probed sites that bleed.
- **FAS / PPS / SS**: full analysis set (ITT-based) / per-protocol set / safety set.
- **LOCF**: last observation carried forward (missing-data imputation).
- **VAS**: visual analogue scale (here for pain and dentin hypersensitivity).
- **psi**: pounds per square inch (irrigation water pressure unit).
