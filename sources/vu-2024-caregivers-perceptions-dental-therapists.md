## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional survey (J Dent Child 2024; n=202 caregivers of pediatric medical patients, urban teaching hospital) finding caregivers are receptive to midlevel dental therapists (DT) performing restorations/extractions/emergency care, with strong agreement (p<0.001) that procedures occur under dentist supervision; abstract-only.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies caregiver acceptance of a midlevel dental provider (dental therapist, DT) using validated DT-integration surveys.
- Links prior exposure to a midlevel medical provider (ML) with greater agreement toward DT-delivered dental procedures.
- Documents a strong, near-universal expectation that DT procedures be performed under dentist supervision.
- Identifies a public-awareness gap regarding DT education, scope of practice, and workforce integration.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Setting: urban teaching hospital; respondents were caregivers of pediatric medical patients.
- Instrument: validated DT-integration questionnaires.
- Respondents reported whether they had previously received care from a midlevel medical provider, then answered acceptance items on a midlevel dental provider (DT).
- Stratification: ML (recalled previous midlevel medical care) vs UML (unaware of/no previous midlevel medical care).
- n = 202 evaluable questionnaires.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- 71.2% recalled previous midlevel (ML) medical care; 28.7% were unaware (UML).
- ML respondents were significantly in agreement with DTs performing:
  - primary tooth restorations (p<0.001)
  - permanent tooth restorations (p=0.001)
  - primary tooth extractions (p=0.022)
  - emergency treatment (p<0.001)
- Both UML and ML significantly agreed procedures should be completed under the supervision of a dentist (p<0.001).
- Conclusion: caregivers are receptive to DTs and largely agree with them performing dental procedures, but information dissemination is needed to raise public awareness of DT education, scope of practice, and workforce integration.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only — full text not retrieved; detailed methods, exact agreement percentages per item, and confounder handling not extractable.
- Single urban teaching-hospital sample of pediatric medical caregivers — limited generalizability.
- Cross-sectional, self-reported acceptance ("theoretical acceptance"), not behavioral or clinical-outcome data.
- US workforce context (dental therapist role); not directly transposable to other jurisdictions/regulatory systems.

## 7. Glossary

- Dental therapist (DT): midlevel dental provider trained to perform a defined scope of restorative and basic surgical procedures, typically under dentist supervision.
- Midlevel provider (ML): non-physician/non-dentist clinician (e.g., nurse practitioner, physician assistant, dental therapist) delivering care within a defined scope.
- UML: respondent unaware of / without recalled previous midlevel medical care.
- Scope of practice: the set of procedures a provider is legally and professionally authorized to perform.
