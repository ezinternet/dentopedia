Deepens the OPMD-pathogenesis layer behind the malignant-transformation rates in [[oral-medicine/aguirre-urizar-2021-leukoplakia-malignant-transformation-sr-ma]] and complements the clinical overview [[oral-medicine/warnakulasuriya-2020-opmd-comprehensive-review]].

## One-line Summary

Narrative review of OPMD etiology, molecular pathogenesis, and the dysplasia-to-carcinoma transformation pathway, emphasizing tobacco/areca/alcohol and microbial/HPV drivers plus diagnostic-prognostic techniques to predict malignant transformation.

## 1. Document Information

- Frontiers in Pharmacology 2022;13:825266. Published 2022-04-20. Review (Pharmacology of Anti-Cancer Drugs section).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Compiles scattered OPMD knowledge (leukoplakia, erythroplakia, OSMF, proliferative verrucous leukoplakia, OLP) into one etiology-pathogenesis framework.
- Positions oral epithelial dysplasia (OED) as the key intermediate predicting malignant transformation.
- Reviews genetic, epigenetic, microbial, and habitual drivers of progression.
- Surveys advanced diagnostic/risk-stratification techniques.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Narrative review synthesizing epidemiology (Globocan 2020), histopathology, and molecular biology of OPMDs.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- ~0.37 million new lip/oral-cavity cancers and ~0.17 million deaths (Globocan 2020), highest burden in low-HDI South-Asian countries.
- Progression sequence: hyperplasia -> dysplasia -> carcinoma in situ -> invasive cancer; OED grade is the principal malignancy predictor.
- Habitual factors (tobacco/areca/alcohol) are primary reversible determinants at the benign/dysplastic stage.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Non-systematic; molecular-marker discussion outpaces clinically validated tools; no pooled transformation estimates.

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary

- OPMD: Oral Potentially Malignant Disorder.
- OED: Oral Epithelial Dysplasia.
- OSMF: Oral Submucous Fibrosis; PVL: Proliferative Verrucous Leukoplakia.
- MT: Malignant Transformation.
