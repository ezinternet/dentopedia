## One-line Summary
SR+MA (IJOMI 2022;36(6):1104–1110) of 3 NRSIs in humans comparing OD vs conventional drilling — OD produced a significantly higher mean ISQ at baseline (MD 13.1, 95% CI 10.0–16.1, P < .0001, I² = 0.0%) and at 4–6 month follow-up (MD 5.99, 95% CI 1.3–10.6, P < .0001, I² = 73.0%).

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Systematic review + random-effects meta-analysis (humans, NRSIs)
- **Journal**: International Journal of Oral & Maxillofacial Implants 2022;36(6):1104–1110
- **DOI**: 10.11607/jomi.9132
- **Authors**: João Gaspar, Luís Proença, João Botelho, Vanessa Machado, Leandro Chambrone, Rodrigo Neiva, José João Mendes
- **Search**: PubMed, Google Scholar, LILACS, EMBASE, CENTRAL through July 2020
- **Sample**: 3 non-randomised studies of interventions (NRSIs)
- **Quality**: All 3 included studies scored low risk of bias
- **Primary Question**: In human studies, does OD drilling produce higher ISQ values than conventional drilling at baseline and at follow-up?

## 2. Key Contributions
- First human-focused SR+MA on OD ISQ outcomes — three NRSIs pooled, all rated low risk of bias.
- **Baseline ISQ — large effect**: MD = 13.1 (95% CI 10.0 to 16.1; P < .0001) favouring OD, with complete homogeneity (I² = 0.0%) — a strikingly consistent across-study primary-stability gain.
- **Follow-up ISQ (4–6 months)**: MD = 5.99 (95% CI 1.3 to 10.6; P < .0001) favouring OD, but with high heterogeneity (I² = 73.0%).
- Both effects statistically significant; baseline effect more confidently positioned (homogeneous, tighter CI).
- Honest caveat acknowledged by authors: only three studies pooled — small evidence base limits inference.
- Authors call for RCTs to confirm consistency.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
A systematic review across five databases (PubMed, Google Scholar, LILACS, EMBASE, CENTRAL) up to July 2020. Eligibility: randomised clinical trials (RCTs) and non-randomised studies of interventions (NRSIs) comparing osseodensification drilling with conventional drilling in humans. Outcomes: ISQ at baseline and at follow-up; meta-analyses used standardised mean difference (SMD) with 95% confidence intervals and risk ratios where applicable.

Random-effects meta-analyses were performed on two timepoints: ISQ at baseline (primary stability immediately post-insertion) and ISQ at 4–6 months follow-up (transition into secondary stability). I² was calculated to quantify heterogeneity.

Three NRSIs met inclusion (no RCTs available at the search date). All three were rated low risk of bias.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **n studies**: 3 (all NRSIs, all low risk of bias)
- **Baseline ISQ**:
  - MD = **13.1** (95% CI **10.0 to 16.1**)
  - P < .0001
  - I² = **0.0%** (complete homogeneity)
- **Follow-up ISQ (4–6 months)**:
  - MD = **5.99** (95% CI **1.3 to 10.6**)
  - P < .0001
  - I² = **73.0%** (high heterogeneity)
- Both timepoints favour OD; primary stability effect (MD 13.1) larger and more homogeneous than secondary stability effect (MD 5.99).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Only 3 studies pooled — narrow evidence base, every study estimate influences the pooled effect.
- All NRSIs; no RCTs available at search date — risk of selection bias even with low individual RoB ratings.
- High I² at follow-up (73%) means the secondary-stability effect is variable across studies; clinical decisions should not rely on a single point estimate.
- Search ends July 2020 — newer studies (post-2020 RCTs) not captured; superseded by Lima Monteiro 2024 and Kalra 2025 for currency.
- Bone-density stratification (D1–D4) not analysed; the OD advantage may be largest in soft bone.
- Implant macrogeometry, surgical centre effects, and operator-skill variation not adjusted for.

## 7. Glossary
- **NRSI (Non-Randomised Study of Interventions)**: Cohort or comparative observational study where intervention allocation was not randomised — Cochrane's preferred label.
- **MD (Mean Difference)**: Pooled difference between OD and conventional ISQ means.
- **SMD (Standardised Mean Difference)**: Effect-size metric used when outcomes are on different scales across studies.
- **I²**: Percentage of variance in pooled estimate due to between-study heterogeneity (0–100%). 0% = full homogeneity; >50% typically considered substantial.
- **ISQ baseline vs follow-up**: Primary stability (immediately post-insertion) vs secondary stability (typically 4–6 months when osseointegration is consolidating).
- **OD (Osseodensification)**: Counterclockwise Densah bur compaction drilling.
