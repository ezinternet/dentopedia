## Why Ingested

Quantifies whether gingival phenotype (thin vs thick) drives implant survival, complementing the soft-tissue/keratinized-mucosa evidence in [[wiki/overviews/keratinized-mucosa-peri-implant-health-overview]] and the soft-tissue phenotype-modification work in [[wiki/implants/tavelli-2021-soft-tissue-phenotype-modification-peri-implant-nma]]; finds phenotype relates to survival only *indirectly* via peri-implantitis/MBL risk, not directly.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (8 studies): implant success >91% at up to 5 years for BOTH thin and thick gingival phenotypes; phenotype does not directly determine survival but the thin phenotype is a main risk factor for additional marginal bone loss, so its effect on survival is indirect (via peri-implantitis/MBL).

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(8편): 얇은·두꺼운 치은 표현형 모두 5년까지 성공률 >91%. 표현형이 생존율을 직접 결정하지는 않으나, 얇은 표현형은 추가 변연골소실의 주요 위험인자 — 따라서 생존율에 미치는 영향은 임플란트주위염/MBL을 통한 간접적 경로.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Synthesises gingival phenotype (thin vs thick) specifically against implant *survival* + MBL, not just esthetics.
- Concludes the phenotype–survival link is INDIRECT (mediated by peri-implantitis/MBL risk), with high success in both phenotypes.
- Flags thin phenotype as a principal risk factor for additional bone loss; flapless protocol gives better short-term esthetics.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PRISMA-guided; PICO question "Does gingival phenotype positively or negatively influence marginal bone loss around dental implants?"
- Databases: PubMed/MEDLINE, PubMed Central, B-On; searched April–September 2023; last-13-years filter.
- Inclusion: any clinical trial/comparative/prospective/retrospective/SR with ≥1-year follow-up and MBL assessment, reporting thin/thick phenotype.
- Exclusion: narrative/other reviews, letters, commentaries.
- Two independent reviewers + third adjudicator; JBI Critical Appraisal for risk of bias.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- 62 records → **8 included studies**.
- Implant success **>91%** for both thin and thick phenotypes at up to 5-year follow-up.
- Unclear whether tissue thickness *directly* influences marginal bone level.
- Thin phenotype = one of the main risk factors for additional bone loss; higher peri-implantitis risk suggested with thin phenotype.
- Flapless protocol → better short-term esthetic result.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 8 heterogeneous studies; conclusion on clinical parameters is "non-conclusive."
- Phenotype assessment methods varied; mostly ≤5-year follow-up.
- Indirect (mediated) relationship limits prognostic precision.

## 6. Related Work

- Tavelli 2021 (soft-tissue phenotype modification NMA) — intervention side of phenotype.

## 7. Glossary

- **Gingival phenotype (biotype)**: thin (delicate, recession-prone, dehiscence/fenestration) vs thick (dense, fibrous, flat bone).
- **MBL**: marginal bone loss (≥2 mm with pocket = peri-implantitis context).
- **Flapless protocol**: implant placement without raising a mucoperiosteal flap.
- **STA (supracrestal tissue attachment)**: former "biological width."
