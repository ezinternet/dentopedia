## Why Ingested

Existing AI-in-dentistry pages are task-specific systematic reviews — [[artificial-intelligence/abbott-2024-ai-platforms-dental-caries-detection]] (caries) and [[artificial-intelligence/khubrani-2025-periodontal-bone-loss-periodontitis-detection]] (periodontal bone loss). This 2023 overview-of-systematic-reviews (umbrella review, ROBIS-appraised, 12 SRs) sits one level above them, situating those task-specific reviews within a single panoramic-radiograph landscape that also spans osteoporosis, maxillary sinusitis, teeth numbering, and periapical lesions — providing umbrella-level context and cross-task accuracy anchors.

## One-line Summary

Overview of systematic reviews (umbrella review; 12 SRs, ROBIS-appraised) finding that recent AI models on dental panoramic radiographs reach high accuracy across caries (91.5%), osteoporosis (89.29%), maxillary sinusitis (87.5%), periodontal bone loss (93.09%), and teeth ID/numbering (93.67%), with periapical-lesion detection at 99.95% sensitivity / 92% specificity — but results must be read with caution given few, heterogeneous source studies.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides an umbrella-level (overview-of-systematic-reviews) synthesis of AI applied to dental panoramic radiographs (DPRs), sitting above the many task-specific systematic reviews.
- Establishes the current state of knowledge and traces the development of AI/information-technology methods for DPR analysis across 1988–2023.
- Uses the ROBIS tool to assess risk of bias of the included systematic reviews.
- Aggregates the latest reported accuracy across six distinct DPR analysis tasks into a single comparative view.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Overview of systematic reviews (umbrella review); qualitative synthesis, PROSPERO-registered (CRD42023416048).
- **Databases searched**: ACM (Association for Computing Machinery), BASE (Bielefeld Academic Search Engine), Google Scholar, and PubMed.
- **Risk of bias**: assessed with the ROBIS tool.
- **Included studies**: 12 systematic reviews qualified for qualitative synthesis.
- **Presentation**: results visualized with timelines, tables, and charts.
- **Note**: abstract-only — no further methodological detail (inclusion criteria specifics, per-SR ROBIS ratings, task-level review counts) was retrievable from the empty full text.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Latest analyzed AI models on dental panoramic radiographs achieved:

| Task | Reported performance |
|---|---|
| Caries detection | 91.5% accuracy |
| Osteoporosis detection | 89.29% accuracy |
| Maxillary sinusitis detection | 87.5% accuracy |
| Periodontal bone loss detection | 93.09% accuracy |
| Teeth identification and numbering | 93.67% accuracy |
| Periapical lesion detection | 99.95% sensitivity / 92% specificity |

- A significant development of information technologies for DPR analysis was observed over 1988–2023.
- Caution: due to the small number of heterogeneous source studies synthesized within the included systematic reviews, results should be interpreted cautiously.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Underlying evidence base is small and heterogeneous, limiting the strength of the pooled picture.
- Systematic reviews on AI become outdated quickly; the authors recommend regular updating.
- As an umbrella review, findings are only as reliable as the included SRs (ROBIS-appraised).
- Abstract-only ingest: task-level detail, per-review bias ratings, and any subgroup analysis are not captured here.

## 7. Glossary

- **DPR (Dental Panoramic Radiograph)**: single-image extraoral radiograph capturing both dental arches and surrounding structures.
- **Overview of systematic reviews / umbrella review**: a review whose unit of evidence is systematic reviews rather than primary studies.
- **ROBIS**: Risk Of Bias In Systematic reviews — a validated tool for appraising SR methodology.
- **Sensitivity / Specificity**: proportion of true positives correctly detected / proportion of true negatives correctly excluded.
- **AI (Artificial Intelligence)**: here, machine-/deep-learning models applied to radiographic image analysis.
