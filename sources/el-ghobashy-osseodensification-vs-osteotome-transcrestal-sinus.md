## One-line Summary
RCT (n=11, 12 implants, RBH 5–8mm, 6mo): Densah OD vs osteotome TSFE — OD significantly higher primary ISQ (66.17 vs 54.83) and secondary stability; operating time shorter in OD group.

## 1. Document Information
- **Institution**: Faculty of Dentistry, Alexandria University, Egypt
- **Study type**: Randomized clinical trial
- **Follow-up**: 6 months

## 2. Key Contributions
- RCT comparing osseodensification vs osteotome for transcrestal sinus lifting
- OD group: significantly higher primary stability ISQ (66.17 vs 54.83, p=0.043)
- Significant difference in secondary stability between groups
- No significant difference in postoperative pain or edema
- Shorter operation time with OD technique (p=0.002)

## 3. Methodology
- 11 patients, missing maxillary premolars/molars, RBH 5–8mm
- Group A: Densah bur osseodensification (6 implants)
- Group B: osteotome technique (6 implants)
- ISQ measured at placement (primary) and follow-up (secondary)

## 4. Key Results
- Primary stability ISQ: OD 66.17±9.57 vs osteotome 54.83±7.19 (p=0.043)
- Secondary stability: OD significantly higher
- Pain/edema: no significant difference between groups
- Operating time: OD significantly shorter (p=0.002)
