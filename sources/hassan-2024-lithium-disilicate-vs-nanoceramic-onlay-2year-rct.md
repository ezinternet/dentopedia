## 2. Key Contributions
- Both groups: 90% Kaplan-Meier survival at 2 years
- No significant difference between e.max CAD and Voco Grandio (p>0.05)
- Within-group: marginal discoloration, adaptation, surface texture, sensitivity changed over time
- Voco Grandio: color match within-group change observed
- Modified USPHS criteria at baseline, 1-year, 2-year

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Split-mouth RCT (n=20 patients) comparing IPS e.max CAD (lithium disilicate) vs Voco Grandio Blocks (hybrid resin nano-ceramic) CAD/CAM onlays over 2 years. Evaluated at baseline, 1-year, and 2-year using modified USPHS criteria. Both materials showed 90% Kaplan-Meier survival at 2 years. No significant differences between materials (p>0.05). Within-group: significant improvements in marginal discoloration, marginal adaptation, surface texture, postoperative hypersensitivity. Grandio: color match change within group.
