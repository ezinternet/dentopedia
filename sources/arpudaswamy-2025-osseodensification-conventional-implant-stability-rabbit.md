## One-line Summary
Rabbit femoral condyle (low-density D4) split-body RCT (n=8, 16 implants): OD raised insertion torque and 3-month ISQ vs conventional drilling, but ISQ at placement did not differ.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal of Contemporary Dental Practice (JCDP), published 2025-01-27 (accepted 2024-10-30).
- Animal experimental split-body RCT, female New Zealand white rabbits.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Isolates OD effect on stability in controlled D4 bone, separating primary (placement) from secondary (3-month) stability.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 8 rabbits, 16 implants (3.1 × 8 mm). Each animal: one femoral condyle prepared with conventional drills (Group I), contralateral with Densah OD burs (Group II).
- Outcomes: insertion torque (ITV), ISQ at placement and at 3 months.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Insertion torque: Group I 31.13 ± 1.73 Ncm vs Group II (OD) 33.00 ± 1.31 Ncm — significant (p = 0.028).
- ISQ at placement: 63.63 ± 5.93 (I) vs 63.62 ± 7.62 (II) — NOT significant.
- ISQ at 3 months: 67.25 ± 6.45 vs 75 ± 6.85 — significant (p = 0.0353); ISQ change over time significant (p = 0.001).
- Conclusion: OD gave superior secondary-stability progression; primary stability advantage was on torque, not placement ISQ.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Animal model (rabbit femur ≠ human jaw); very small n; short follow-up (3 mo); no histology of osseointegration reported in abstract.

## 6. Related Work
- OD ISQ SR: althobaiti-2023; OD osteotomy SR+MA: inchingolo-2021; null clinical split-mouth: al-ahmari-2022.

## 7. Glossary
- ISQ: Implant Stability Quotient. ITV: insertion torque value. D4 bone: low-density (Lekholm-Zarb) bone. OD: osseodensification (Densah burs).
