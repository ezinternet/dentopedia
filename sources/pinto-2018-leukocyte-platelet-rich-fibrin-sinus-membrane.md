## One-line Summary
Case report: a large intraoperative Schneiderian membrane perforation during lateral-window maxillary sinus floor elevation in a partially edentulous patient (sites #16, #17, #18) was repaired with **L-PRF (leukocyte platelet-rich fibrin)** combined with collagen membranes; uneventful healing and successful placement of two external-hexagon implants at 8 months.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Implant Dentistry 2018; 27(3): 375–380
- **Type**: Case report
- **Institutions**: UNESP – São Paulo State University, Araraquara, Brazil; Alfenas Federal University; Universidade de Araraquara (UNIARA), Brazil
- **Study type**: Single-patient case report (in-vivo clinical)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Demonstrates that **L-PRF** (a second-generation platelet concentrate) can effectively seal a large sinus membrane perforation, when used together with collagen membranes, and allow continued bone augmentation in the same session.
- Avoids the alternative — surgical abortion and reentry 6–8 weeks later (per Chanavaz protocol).
- Highlights mechanical advantages of L-PRF (good adherence to mucosa) and biological advantages (slow release of growth factors from trapped platelets/leukocytes).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Patient: partially edentulous, missing teeth #18, #17, #16, with insufficient vertical bone due to pneumatization of the maxillary sinus.
- Surgical plan: lateral-window maxillary sinus floor elevation (inlay bone graft).
- Intraoperative complication: large Schneiderian membrane perforation.
- Repair: **L-PRF membranes + collagen membranes** placed over the perforation to seal it; bone graft placed under the seal.
- Postoperative follow-up: 8 months.
- Second-stage surgery: placement of two external-hexagon implants at sites #16 and #17 with screw-retained prosthesis.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Sinus membrane perforation successfully sealed without aborting the procedure.
- 8-month healing uneventful — sufficient bone formation for implant placement.
- Two implants placed and restored with a screw-retained implant-supported prosthesis.
- Background literature context cited by authors: membrane perforation rate during SFE ranges from **3.6% to 56%**.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Single case — no controlled comparison.
- L-PRF preparation protocols and centrifugation parameters affect membrane properties and outcomes.
- No quantitative bone-height measurements or ISQ values reported in the abstract.
- Long-term (>5 yr) implant survival not reported.

## 6. Related Work
- Choukroun et al. — original L-PRF protocol.
- Tian 2026 — alternative tack-stabilized collagen membrane for large perforations.
- Estrin 2025 — extended PRF (E-PRF) as lateral window membrane.
- Histological PRF studies on sinus membrane healing (preclinical animal models).

## 7. Glossary
- **L-PRF (leukocyte and platelet-rich fibrin)**: autologous fibrin matrix with trapped platelets and leukocytes; second-generation platelet concentrate (Choukroun).
- **Schneiderian membrane**: respiratory epithelium lining of maxillary sinus.
- **Lateral window approach**: classical sinus floor elevation via the lateral antral wall.
- **External hexagon connection**: classical implant–abutment interface design.
