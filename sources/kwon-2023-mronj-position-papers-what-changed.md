## One-line Summary
Korean-language review of the AAOMS MRONJ position-paper evolution (2007 BRONJ → 2014 MRONJ → 2022 update), highlighting three clinically important 2022 changes: immune-modulator inclusion, operative therapy across all stages, and removal of pre-surgical drug holiday support.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative review comparing successive AAOMS position papers.
- Antiresorptives covered: bisphosphonate, denosumab (osteoporosis, osteopenia, bone metastases, multiple myeloma, Paget's).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Background**: antiresorptives control bone pain and reduce skeletal-related events (incl. pathologic fracture) but are linked to ONJ. First defined as BRONJ in the 2007 AAOMS position paper, renamed MRONJ in 2014, updated May 2022.
- **2022 change 1 — Definition**: immune modulators (e.g., Romosozumab) added as medications potentially related to ONJ. (Note: Romosozumab is a sclerostin inhibitor with anabolic action; its grouping here is per the position paper.)
- **2022 change 2 — Treatment**: operative therapy now included for **all** stages (1, 2, and 3), not reserved for advanced disease.
- **2022 change 3 — Drug holiday**: the previously mentioned pre-surgical drug holiday to prevent MRONJ is **not identified/supported** in the 2022 paper.
- Most 2022 statements were based on strict literature reviews; positions were revised where evidence for a specific diagnostic/therapeutic option was weak.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative summary of one organization's (AAOMS) position papers; other society guidelines not synthesized.
- Position-paper recommendations rest partly on low-level evidence.
