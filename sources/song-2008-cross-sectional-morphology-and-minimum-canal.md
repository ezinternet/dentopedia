## One-line Summary
Cross-sectional morphological analysis of C-shaped root canals in mandibular molars with measurement of minimum distances between canal and external tooth wall.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Research article
- **Institution**: Dankook University, College of Dentistry, Department of Conservative Dentistry
- **Corresponding Author**: Yong-Bum Cho (raindrop@dku.edu)
- **Year**: 2008 (approximately, based on PDF metadata)
- **Primary Focus**: Anatomical variation in mandibular molars—C-shaped canal system

## 2. Key Contributions
- Documents prevalence and characteristics of C-shaped canal system in mandibular molars
- Provides morphological classification based on cross-sectional analysis
- Quantifies minimum safe distances from canal to external root wall for clinical instrumentation
- Establishes baseline anatomical data for treatment planning of C-shaped canals
- Highlights clinical risks of over-instrumentation into dentin

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Research Design**: Observational morphological study
- **Sample**: Mandibular second molars with C-shaped canal anatomy
- **Analysis Method**: Cross-sectional ground sections at multiple levels
- **Measurements**: 
  - Canal morphology at apical, middle, and coronal thirds
  - Minimum distance from canal wall to external root surface
  - Canal configuration classification
- **Imaging/Visualization**: Microscopic analysis of sectioned specimens

## 4. Key Results and Findings
- C-shaped canal system represents significant anatomical variation in mandibular molars
- Minimum canal wall widths vary by root level and position
- High-risk zones identified where canal lies very close to external tooth surface
- Cross-sectional morphology shows continuous variation from coronal to apical
- Anatomical constraints may limit instrumentation diameter in certain regions

## 5. Clinical Implications
- Requires modified endodontic instrumentation technique for C-shaped canals
- Risk assessment essential before aggressive instrumentation to prevent perforation
- Preparation must respect minimal dentin thickness between canal and outer wall
- Radiographic diagnosis may underestimate canal complexity
- Treatment approach differs from conventional single-canal mandibular molars

## 6. Limitations and Future Work
- Sample limited to specific tooth type and population
- Cross-sectional analysis captures single points rather than continuous pathway
- Cadaver/extracted tooth specimens may not reflect in vivo variability
- Future studies should use 3D imaging (CBCT) to map canal pathway
- Clinical treatment outcome data for C-shaped canal cases needed

## 7. Glossary
- **C-shaped canal**: Anatomical variant where canal forms continuous C-shaped ring in cross-section
- **절단면 분석 (Cross-sectional analysis)**: Microscopic examination of tooth sections perpendicular to long axis
- **최소거리 (Minimum distance)**: Shortest measurement between two anatomical structures
- **근관형태 (Canal morphology)**: Shape and configuration of root canal space
- **치아외벽 (External tooth wall)**: Outer surface of root dentin
