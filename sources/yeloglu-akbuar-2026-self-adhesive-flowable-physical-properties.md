## Why Ingested

플로어블 레진의 **성분(레진 매트릭스 화학·filler composition)이 물성(미세경도·표면조도·색안정성)과 세균부착을 어떻게 결정하는지**를 직접 비교한 2026 in vitro 연구로, 기존 [[resin/parra-gatica-2023-flowable-bulk-fill-composites-sr]](flowable bulk-fill 물성 SR)와 [[resin/melo-2023-bioactive-dental-resins-restorative-dentistry]](bioactive resin 성분-기능 연결)의 "성분→물성" 축을 self-adhesive flowable 계열로 확장한다. 또한 [[resin-bonding/celik-2015-self-adhesive-flowable-nccl-6month]]의 self-adhesive flowable 임상 한계를 재료과학적으로 뒷받침하는 벤치 근거.

## One-line Summary

In vitro lab study (5 flowable composites, n=10 each): a conventional flowable composite (Filtek Ultimate Flowable) had the highest microhardness, while bacterial adhesion was composition-dependent and not explained by surface roughness alone — resin matrix chemistry and filler composition drive both physical properties and biofilm affinity.

## 한줄요약

In vitro 실험 (플로어블 레진 5종, 각 n=10): 통상형(Filtek Ultimate Flowable)이 미세경도 최고, 세균부착은 표면조도가 아니라 **레진 매트릭스·필러 성분**에 좌우 — self-adhesive flowable은 통상형보다 경도가 낮으나 일부는 색안정성·세균부착에서 유리.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Head-to-head bench comparison of **four self-adhesive flowable composites** (Vertise Flow, Fusio Liquid Dentin, Constic, Nova Compo SF) vs a **conventional flowable** (Filtek Ultimate Flowable) on the same protocol.
- Couples **physical properties** (microhardness, surface roughness, color stability) with **biological behavior** (S. mutans + L. casei adhesion) in one design — a material-science framing rather than a single-property bench test.
- Key mechanistic finding: **bacterial adhesion is composition-dependent and dissociated from surface roughness** — i.e., the smoother surface is not automatically the less colonized one.
- Translates rankings into clinical selection heuristics (microhardness → stress-bearing posterior; low adhesion → high-caries-risk patients; color stability → esthetic anterior).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: in vitro comparative laboratory study, n = 10 per material (5 materials).
- **Materials**: Vertise Flow (VF), Fusio Liquid Dentin (FL), Constic (Co), Nova Compo SF (NC) — all self-adhesive flowable; Filtek Ultimate Flowable (FU) — conventional flowable control.
- **Physical/optical outcomes**: surface microhardness (Vickers, VHN); surface roughness (Ra); color change after 24 h (ΔE1) and 7 days (ΔE2) immersion in coffee solution.
- **Biological outcomes**: bacterial adhesion + growth inhibition of *Streptococcus mutans* and *Lactobacillus casei* by crystal violet assay, with SEM corroboration.
- **Statistics**: one-way ANOVA + Tukey post hoc, α = 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Microhardness**: conventional FU highest (p < 0.05); self-adhesive flowables lower.
- **Surface roughness**: significant differences among self-adhesive materials; **FL highest Ra** (p < 0.05).
- **Color stability**: composition-dependent; **NC lowest ΔE** after prolonged staining (p < 0.05) → most color-stable.
- **Bacterial adhesion**: material-dependent; **FU and NC lowest** adhesion for both species; **FL highest** affinity (p < 0.05). Adhesion **not explained by surface roughness alone**.
- (Absolute VHN/Ra/ΔE and CFU/OD values not provided in the abstract.)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **In vitro**: static immersion + short-term (7-day) staining and 2-species biofilm do not reproduce intraoral aging, salivary pellicle, or polymicrobial dynamics.
- **Abstract-only artifact**: no per-group numerical values, no filler wt%/composition data, no degree-of-conversion measure — composition→property links are inferred, not quantified here.
- No mechanical strength (flexural) or wear endpoint; self-adhesive flowables are also load-limited clinically.
- Single coffee staining medium; one curing/finishing protocol.

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary

- **Self-adhesive flowable composite**: flowable resin composite with acidic functional monomers, applied without a separate adhesive step (no etch-and-rinse / self-etch primer).
- **VHN (Vickers Hardness Number)**: microhardness; proxy for filler load and degree of conversion.
- **Ra**: arithmetic mean surface roughness.
- **ΔE**: CIE color difference; higher = greater staining/instability (ΔE > ~3.3 commonly cited as clinically perceptible).
- **Crystal violet assay**: dye-binding quantification of adhered biofilm biomass.
