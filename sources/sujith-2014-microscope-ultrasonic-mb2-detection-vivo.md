## One-line Summary
In vivo (60 patients, maxillary first molar): naked eye MB2 detection 20%; +DOM 55%; +DOM+ultrasonic trough 70%; stepwise protocol statistically significant (p<0.001).

## 1. Document Information
Sri Jagadguru Murugarajendra Dental College, India. In vivo study evaluating sequential escalation of detection tools for MB2 canal in maxillary first molars.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Stepwise detection protocol: naked eye → DOM → DOM + ultrasonic trough
- Each step adds significant detection power
- Ultrasonics (3-mm trough toward palatal canal) reveals canals hidden by dentin bridges
- Statistical significance by ROC curve analysis (p<0.001)

## 3. Methodology
- 60 patients needing root canal therapy in maxillary first molar
- Step 1: endodontic explorer, naked eye
- Step 2: dental operating microscope (DOM)
- Step 3: ultrasonic tip — 3mm trough from MB1 toward palatal → examine under DOM

## 4. Key Results
| Method | MB2 detected (cumulative) |
|---|---|
| Naked eye | 12 / 60 (20%) |
| + DOM | 33 / 60 (55%) |
| + DOM + ultrasonic | 42 / 60 (70%) |

- p<0.001 between methods (ROC analysis)

## 5. Limitations
- Single center, India — MB2 prevalence may differ by ethnicity/population
- No CBCT gold standard (unlike Camacho-Aparicio 2022)
- Subjective endpoint (operator skill)

## 6. Related Work
- Camacho-Aparicio 2022 — DOM + ultrasonic vs CBCT gold standard in Mexican population
- Mohammadi 2016 — missed canal prevention strategies

## 7. Glossary
- **MB2**: Second mesiobuccal canal (maxillary first molar)
- **DOM**: Dental Operating Microscope
- **Trough**: 3-mm groove cut ultrasonically toward palatal canal orifice
