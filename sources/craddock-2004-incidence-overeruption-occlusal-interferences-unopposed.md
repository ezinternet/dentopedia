## Why Ingested

This is the foundational primary source behind the "~83% of unopposed posterior teeth over-erupt (range under 0.5 mm to 5.4 mm)" figure quoted throughout [[wiki/overviews/unopposed-tooth-overeruption-overview]]; that figure was previously cited second-hand and named as a sourcing gap. It provides the original 120-subject / 155-site clinical dataset that reinforces the CBCT-era over-eruption magnitudes in [[wiki/occlusion/wang-2022-unopposed-molar-overeruption-implant-cbct]] and adds the missing occlusal-interference dimension (51.6% of unopposed sites involved in an RCP contact or excursive interference).

## One-line Summary

Single-centre retrospective clinical study (120 adults, 155 unopposed posterior sites): 83.9% of unopposed sites over-erupted (0–5.39 mm; 95% CI 78–90), 51.6% were involved in an occlusal interference (RCP/protrusive/excursive), and the correlation between over-eruption extent and interference presence was weak and non-significant.

- **Received:** 2002-12-20; **Accepted:** 2003-05-20; **Published:** 2004-03-27
- **Study type:** Single-centre clinical retrospective analysis (cross-sectional measurement of an existing patient population)
- **Setting:** Combined Care Clinic, Leeds Dental Institute (patients under routine restorative care by final-year undergraduate students during 2000)

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Establishes the canonical over-eruption incidence figure**: 83.9% of unopposed posterior sites display over-eruption — the most-cited primary-source figure for this phenomenon.
2. **Quantifies the magnitude distribution**: over-eruption ranges from 0 to 5.39 mm, skewed toward smaller values, with the modal band 1.0–2.5 mm (the most commonly encountered values were 1–1.5, 1.5–2 and 2–2.5 mm).
3. **Adds an occlusal-interference dimension absent from earlier over-eruption studies**: 51.6% of unopposed sites are involved in an RCP contact or excursive interference (ICP/RCP being the most common type).
4. **Tests and largely rejects the intuitive over-eruption→interference link**: the Spearman correlation between extent of over-eruption and presence of occlusal interference is weak and statistically non-significant.
5. **Introduces a 3D-scanner + curve-of-Spee methodology** (with Orthlieb extrapolation for terminal-tooth sites lacking a distal reference), validated by good intra-examiner reliability.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Subjects:** 120 patients >18 yr with one or more unopposed posterior teeth; all invited patients consented. Max two unopposed sites per jaw. No patient had a BPE score >3. Mean age 50.9 yr (SD 13.94). 155 unopposed sites total (121 upper, 34 lower; 81 male sites, 74 female sites).
- **Definitions:** "Posterior" = upper/lower 1st or 2nd premolar or 1st/2nd/3rd molar. "Unopposed" = ≤20% of the occlusal surface in contact with an opposing tooth in intercuspal position. "Over-eruption present" = any measurement >0 from cusp tip to curve of Spee.
- **Over-eruption measurement:** Alginate impressions → Dentstone models, trimmed in 7-sided configuration, scanned on a Black Widow 9636 3D USB scanner with an in-image mm scale (1401 DPI, 50% scale). Over-eruption measured in QuickCAD from the curve of Spee to the most occlusally projecting cusp tip, along the tooth long axis. For terminal teeth with no distal reference, the curve of Spee was extrapolated using Orthlieb's progressive differential-angle method (e.g. +15° for a lower third molar) and superimposed from the opposing arch.
- **Occlusal-interference examination:** Single examiner, clinical, using 8-µm Hänel articulating foils in different colours to mark RCP, protrusive, working-side and non-working-side contacts; markings removed and procedure repeated to confirm. RCP reproducibly detected in all subjects.
- **Reliability:** 10% of sample (every 16th subject/site) re-examined 3–6 months later. Over-eruption agreement assessed by Bland–Altman plot (all values within 95% CI bands, mean difference near zero). Interference agreement assessed by Kappa: ICP/RCP 0.87 (very good), protrusive 0.70 (good), working-side 0.56 (moderate), non-working-side 0.63 (good).
- **Association test:** Spearman's rank correlation between over-eruption extent and interference presence. Sample size pre-calculated with statistician input.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Over-eruption (Table 6, Table 7):**
- 130/155 sites over-erupted = **83.9%** (95% CI 78–90); 25 sites (16.1%) had no over-eruption.
- Range **0 to 5.39 mm**; skewed distribution. Bin counts (mm: n): 0:26, 0.1–0.5:4, 0.51–1.0:14, 1.01–1.5:29, 1.51–2.0:29, 2.01–2.5:24, 2.51–3.0:14, 3.01–3.5:3, 3.51–4.0:3, 4.01–4.5:2, 4.51–5.0:1, 5.01–5.5:1. (Note: the 0-bin is reported as 26 in Table 7 vs 25 "no over-eruption" in Table 6 — a 1-site discrepancy in the source.)
- Modal/most common bands: 1.0–1.5, 1.5–2.0 and 2.0–2.5 mm.

**Occlusal interference (Tables 1–4):**
- **51.6%** of unopposed sites involved in interference (95% CI 44–60): 80/155 sites with interference, 75 without (table counts patients/sites).
- By arch: **upper 48.8%** (59/121; 95% CI 39.9–57.7) vs **lower 61.8%** (21/34; 95% CI 45.4–78.1) — arches not formally compared due to small lower n.
- Number of interferences per site (Table 3): 0:74, 1:47, 2:28, 3:4, 4:0. 32 sites had more than one interference. No site had all four.
- Type of interference (Table 4): ICP/RCP **42** (most common), protrusive **32**, working-side **23**, non-working-side **21**. An initial RCP contact was detected in 27% of cases with interferences; protrusive in 21% of the group.

**Association:** Spearman correlation between over-eruption extent and interference presence was **weak and statistically non-significant** — extent of over-eruption does not reliably predict interference.

**Context (prior literature cited):** Kiliaridis 2000 — over-eruption >2 mm in 24% of unopposed teeth, 18% with none (≈82% with some movement). Agerberg & Sandström 1988 — whole-mouth interference incidence 75–89%; this single-site incidence of 51.6% is therefore high for one occluding site.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single examiner only (justified by Vallon/Carlsson recommendations for longitudinal consistency, but limits generalisability of interference detection).
- Over-eruption measured **only in the occlusal/vertical direction**; tipping, drifting and rotation (which Kaplan and Smith showed contribute to interferences) were not measured.
- Curve-of-Spee extrapolation for terminal teeth (Orthlieb method) is complex and a potential error source; depends on tooth-dimension estimation.
- Cannot establish whether an interference pre-existed extraction of the opponent (some were "highly probable" pre-existing).
- Retrospective/cross-sectional: cannot establish rate or time-course of over-eruption (no longitudinal follow-up), only prevalence in an existing population.
- Lower-arch sample (n=34) too small for upper-vs-lower comparison.
- Authors suggest future research into the link between non-working-side interferences and cracked-cusp syndrome (citing Ratcliffe 2001).

## 6. Related Work

- **Kiliaridis et al. 2000** (Int J Prosthodont) — vertical position, rotation and tipping of molars without antagonists; ≈82% with some over-eruption, 24% >2 mm.
- **Smith 1996** (Br J Orthod) — extraction of upper 2nd molars and lower 2nd molar position; over-eruption confined mainly to the distal aspect.
- **Kaplan 1985** (J Prosthet Dent) — drifting/tipping/supra-eruption; horizontal/rotational component to supra-eruption.
- **Compagnon & Woda 1991** (J Prosthet Dent) — supra-eruption of unopposed maxillary first molar; most movement in early years, passive eruption/root exposure after ~10 yr.
- **Agerberg & Sandström 1988** (J Prosthet Dent) — whole-mouth occlusal-interference frequency 75–89% in teenagers/young adults.
- **Solnit 1988; Jepson & Allen 1999; Dahl et al. 1975** — preventive/management appliances (etched-metal splint, adhesive cantilever, Dahl appliance).
- **Ratcliffe et al. 2001** (J Prosthet Dent) — type/incidence of cracks; lateral excursive interference associated with cusp fracture.

## 7. Glossary

- **Over-eruption / supra-eruption:** Continued occlusal (and sometimes rotational/horizontal) movement of a tooth that has lost its antagonist; here measured vertically from the curve of Spee to the cusp tip.
- **Curve of Spee:** The anteroposterior curve of the occlusal plane along the buccal cusp tips; used as the baseline reference for vertical over-eruption measurement.
- **Unopposed tooth:** A tooth with ≤20% of its occlusal surface contacting an opponent in intercuspal position.
- **RCP (Retruded Contact Position):** First tooth contact when the mandible is in its retruded (hinge-axis) position; an RCP contact on an unopposed tooth is a form of occlusal interference.
- **Occlusal interference:** A tooth contact that obstructs smooth mandibular movement — classified here as ICP/RCP, protrusive, working-side, or non-working-side (NWSI).
- **Orthlieb method:** Technique to extrapolate a missing segment of the curve of Spee using the progressive differential angle of posterior teeth, used here for terminal teeth lacking a distal reference.
- **Bland–Altman plot:** Graphical method for assessing agreement between two sets of repeated measurements.
- **Kappa score:** Agreement statistic correcting for chance, used for the categorical interference observations.
- **Dahl appliance:** Localised anterior bite-platform appliance creating interocclusal space via relative axial movement (incisor intrusion + posterior eruption); proposed for managing over-erupted teeth before restoration.
