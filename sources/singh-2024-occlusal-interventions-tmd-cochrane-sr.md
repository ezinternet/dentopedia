## One-line Summary
Cochrane systematic review on occlusal interventions for TMD; found very low to low certainty evidence that occlusal splints and adjustments provide modest benefit, with insufficient data to support irreversible occlusal modifications.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews 2024;9:CD012850
- **DOI**: 10.1002/14651858.CD012850.pub2
- **Study type**: Cochrane systematic review

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive Cochrane review of all occlusal interventions: stabilization splints (교합안정장치), occlusal adjustment/equilibration, and irreversible occlusal modifications
- Covers both myofascial pain and disc displacement subtypes of TMD
- Provides GRADE-assessed certainty ratings for each comparison
- Explicitly evaluates safety of irreversible occlusal procedures
- Serves as the authoritative systematic evidence base for clinical guidelines

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Cochrane methodology: comprehensive search of Cochrane CENTRAL, MEDLINE, Embase, trial registries
- Inclusion: RCTs and quasi-RCTs on occlusal interventions for any TMD subtype (DC/TMD)
- Primary outcomes: pain intensity, jaw function, quality of life
- Secondary outcomes: adverse events, patient satisfaction, joint sounds
- Two independent reviewers; GRADE certainty assessment
- Sensitivity and subgroup analyses by TMD subtype

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Stabilization splints vs. no treatment**: Very low certainty evidence of pain benefit; effect sizes small
- **Stabilization vs. non-occluding splints**: No clinically meaningful difference (very low certainty)
- **Occlusal adjustment**: No benefit demonstrated; potential harm (irreversible)
- **Irreversible occlusal modifications**: Insufficient evidence; not recommended
- Overall certainty: very low to low across most comparisons
- Adverse events: generally mild (tooth soreness, muscle discomfort)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Most RCTs have high risk of bias, particularly in blinding
- Short follow-up (3–6 months predominant)
- Heterogeneous populations and diagnostic criteria
- Very few trials used current DC/TMD criteria
- Calls for well-designed RCTs with standardized outcomes

## 7. Glossary
- **Occlusal intervention** (교합 처치): Any treatment targeting the bite/occlusion to manage TMD
- **Stabilization splint** (교합안정장치): Full-arch acrylic appliance providing even bilateral occlusal contacts
- **Occlusal adjustment/equilibration**: Selective grinding of tooth surfaces to modify occlusal contacts
- **Irreversible occlusal modification**: Permanent alteration of occlusion (crowns, orthognathic surgery) for TMD
- **GRADE**: Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation — evidence certainty system
