## One-line Summary
Cochrane Database Syst Rev 2015 (updated 2017): 8 RCTs, 233 sockets, 184 participants — xenograft ARP vs extraction: height MD -2.60mm, width MD -1.97mm (moderate evidence); no convincing difference between graft materials; overall evidence low-to-moderate, high risk of bias.

## 1. Key Results
- 8 RCTs included; 233 extraction sites; 184 participants
- Xenograft vs extraction (2 RCTs, 70 participants, moderate evidence):
  - Height loss reduction: MD -2.60mm (95%CI -3.43 to -1.76)
  - Width loss reduction: MD -1.97mm (95%CI -2.48 to -1.46)
- Allograft vs extraction (1 RCT, 24 participants, low evidence): height MD -2.20mm, width MD -1.40mm
- Alloplast vs xenograft (2 RCTs, 55 participants, moderate evidence): no significant difference in height or width
- No difference between materials for: augmentation need, complications, implant failure, peri-implant MBL
- Quality: 7/8 trials high risk of bias; 2/8 no industry support
- Authors' conclusion: limited evidence for ARP benefit; no convincing difference between graft materials

## 2. Clinical Implications
- ARP is supported but evidence quality is low-moderate
- No material or barrier type proven superior over another
- Cochrane-level rigor: calls for better-designed long-term RCTs following CONSORT guidelines
