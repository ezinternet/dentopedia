## Why Ingested

Provides foundational in-vitro evidence comparing platinum foil vs. refractory die fabrication techniques for porcelain veneers — the marginal gap and microleakage data here underpin fabrication-technique comparisons cited in [[veneers/klein-2025-ceramic-laminate-veneer-survival-complications-sr-ma]]. Essential historical baseline for understanding why technique selection matters.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro study (JPD 1992) comparing marginal fidelity and microleakage of platinum foil vs. refractory die porcelain veneers on intraenamel-prepared incisors; platinum foil showed better vertical marginal fidelity but more overcontouring, while microleakage occurred universally at the tooth–composite resin interface.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Marginal Fidelity and Microleakage of Porcelain Veneers Made by Two Techniques
- **Authors**: John A. Sorensen, Judith M. Strutz, Sean P. Avera, Daniel Materdomini
- **Journal**: The Journal of Prosthetic Dentistry
- **Year**: 1992
- **Volume/Issue**: Volume 67, Issue 1, pp. 16–22
- **DOI**: 10.1016/0022-3913(92)90040-H
- **Study Type**: In-vitro laboratory study

## 2. Key Contributions

- Directly compared marginal fidelity and microleakage between two dominant porcelain veneer fabrication techniques of the era: platinum foil and refractory die.
- Established that platinum foil veneers achieved significantly better vertical marginal fidelity.
- Demonstrated that refractory die veneers had significantly less overcontouring.
- Found universal microleakage at the tooth–composite resin interface across both techniques; no difference between techniques in microleakage magnitude.
- Found no correlation between the magnitude of marginal opening and the degree of microleakage.
- Confirmed negligible microleakage at the porcelain–composite resin interface (internal bond holds).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Specimens**: Maxillary incisors matched for size and enamel quantity; uniform intraenamel preparation of 0.5 mm labial reduction.
- **Fabrication**: Veneers made by both platinum foil and refractory die techniques.
- **Cementation**: Veneers etched (HF), silanated, luted with composite resin; margins finished and polished.
- **Storage & thermocycling**: Teeth stored at 37°C water, thermocycled 1000 times.
- **Microleakage assessment**: Silver nitrate staining, embedded in resin, sectioned buccolingually and mesiodistally.
- **Marginal measurement**: Optical microscopy at ×250 magnification.
- **Measurement sites**: Vertical marginal gap and overcontour quantified at multiple sites per section.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Parameter | Platinum Foil | Refractory Die |
|---|---|---|
| Vertical marginal fidelity | Significantly better | Inferior |
| Overcontouring | Significantly more | Less |
| Microleakage (tooth–composite) | Universal | Universal |
| Microleakage (porcelain–composite) | Negligible | Negligible |

- No statistically significant correlation between marginal gap size and microleakage extent.
- The tooth–composite resin interface was the weak link in both systems.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro study: cannot account for intraoral fatigue, salivary aging, or occlusal loading.
- Only 1000 thermocycles — relatively low by modern standards (10,000+ cycles in later studies).
- Paper is a publisher web-page abstract/screenshot; full quantitative data tables not extractable from this PDF.
- The platinum foil technique is now largely obsolete (superseded by pressable and CAD/CAM methods).

## 7. Glossary

- **Marginal fidelity**: Accuracy of fit at the restoration margin (gap between veneer edge and prepared tooth margin).
- **Overcontouring**: Excess material extending beyond the planned contour at the margin.
- **Microleakage**: Penetration of fluids/stains into the micro-gap between restoration and tooth.
- **Silver nitrate staining**: Tracer method where silver ions penetrate marginal gaps and are reduced to black precipitate for visualization.
- **Intraenamel reduction**: Preparation depth contained entirely within enamel.
- **Platinum foil technique**: Veneer fabricated over a thin platinum foil matrix that is then removed; historically the first standard technique.
- **Refractory die technique**: Veneer fired directly on a refractory stone die replica of the preparation.
