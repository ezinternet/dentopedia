## Why Ingested

A large 2-year phase-3 RCT (n=6,000 children) showing 8.0% arginine dentifrice is a fluoride-free anticaries alternative that is *superior* to NaF (26% DMFS reduction) while 1.5% arginine is *equivalent* to NaF — directly relevant to fluoride-alternative counselling for caries-risk patients who are contraindicated for or prefer non-fluoride products. Extends the fluoride-centric preventive-caries evidence in [[wiki/caries/urquhart-2019-nonrestorative-treatments-caries-network-meta-analysis]] with a head-to-head non-fluoride active, and complements the fluoride-vehicle comparison in [[wiki/caries/chestnutt-2017-fissure-seal-fluoride-varnish-trial]].

## One-line Summary

2-year phase-3 double-blind 3-arm RCT (n=6,000 children, 10–14 y, China): 8.0% arginine dentifrice gave a 26.0% DMFS / 25.3% DMFT reduction vs 0.32% NaF (P<.001), while 1.5% arginine was equivalent to NaF — arginine is an effective fluoride-free anticaries alternative, dose-dependent.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Largest long-term (2-year) trial to date on the **dose-dependent anticaries efficacy of arginine-containing dentifrices in the absence of fluoride** (n=6,000 children).
- Establishes 8.0% arginine (calcium carbonate base) as **superior** to a 1,450-ppm NaF positive control, and 1.5% arginine as **equivalent** (noninferior parity) to NaF.
- Provides a head-to-head clinical evidence base for **fluoride-free caries prevention**, relevant where fluoride is contraindicated, refused, or where dental fluorosis is a regional concern.
- Demonstrates a **threshold/dose effect**: caries benefit appears only at the high (8.0%) concentration over and above fluoride-level protection.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: phase-3 randomized, double-blind, 3-cell parallel-group trial; ITT analysis.
- **Sites**: 3 centers in China — West China College of Stomatology (Chengdu); Chongqing Medical University; Xi'an Jiaotong University. April 15, 2019 → March 12, 2022.
- **Participants**: 6,000 healthy children aged 10–14 y (mean 12.35 y; 56.8% female), each with ≥2 active caries lesions (ICDAS ≥2) and DMFS ≥2.
- **Arms** (2,000 / 2,002 / 1,998): 8.0% arginine (CaCO₃ base), 1.5% arginine (CaCO₃ base), 0.32% NaF (silica base, 1,450 ppm) positive control. Brush twice daily; tubes overwrapped/coded, identical flavor; resupplied every 3 mo with leftover weighed for compliance.
- **Outcomes**: primary = incremental subject-wise DMFS and DMFT at 2 y; secondary = increments at 6 mo and 1 y. Severity scored with ICDAS by 6 calibrated examiners (intra-kappa >0.70, inter-kappa >0.61); only cavitated lesions (ICDAS 3–6) counted as "decayed". No radiographs.
- **Noninferiority margin**: 0.2545 (set per FDA guidance + historical Cochrane fluoride-toothpaste data, 2019–2020).
- **Statistics**: ITT; missing data imputed by MICE (predictive mean matching); bootstrapping (200×) + imputation (2× each = 400 datasets); baseline-adjusted via propensity-score weighting (baseline DMFS/DMFT imbalance noted); 2-sided α=0.05. Sensitivity analyses added sex, age, site.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Completion**: 5,347/6,000 (89.1%) completed in full; low attrition.
- **Baseline imbalance**: DMFS (8.0% 4.26, 1.5% 4.13, NaF 3.91; P<0.001) and DMFT (3.08, 3.15, 2.92; P<0.001) differed across arms — addressed by baseline-adjusted models.
- **2-y DMFS** (baseline-adjusted incremental vs NaF): 8.0% arginine **−0.16 (95% CI −0.22 to −0.10); 26.0% reduction; P<.001** (superior). 1.5% arginine −0.01 (−0.07 to 0.05); 0.7%; **P=.819** (no difference, noninferior parity).
- **2-y DMFT**: 8.0% arginine **−0.17 (−0.24 to −0.11); 25.3% reduction; P<.001** (superior). 1.5% arginine −0.01 (−0.07 to 0.05); 1.1%; **P=.739** (no difference).
- **Time course**: 6 mo — both arginine arms significant but noninferior vs control; 1 y — 8.0% superior, 1.5% parity. Dose-dependent benefit consolidates at 1–2 y.
- **Safety**: no serious adverse events. 37 nonserious side effects (75.7% unrelated); related/possibly related — occasional toothache (n=4), dry mouth (n=2), unpalatability (n=2), acne (n=1).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Baseline DMFS/DMFT imbalance** from simple (non-stratified) randomization; mitigated statistically but a design limitation.
- **Industry sponsorship** (Colgate-Palmolive) — risk-of-bias consideration.
- **Generalizability**: single-country (China) caries-risk adolescent population; confounders (diet, SES, dental access, other fluoride exposure, oral hygiene) acknowledged; further diverse-population research suggested.
- **No radiographs** — early/proximal enamel lesions undetectable; only cavitated lesions counted as decayed.
- Mechanism (arginolytic ammonia production, pH/biofilm modulation) inferred from prior literature, not directly measured here.

## 6. Related Work

- Prior trials: arginine + fluoride dentifrices superior to fluoride alone (additive effect).
- Earlier 2-y study (~600 adolescents): 1.5% arginine ≈ 0.24% NaF (comparable) — consistent with the parity finding here.
- Fluoride dose-response Cochrane reviews used to anchor the noninferiority margin.

## 7. Glossary

- **DMFS / DMFT** — Decayed, Missing, Filled Surfaces / Teeth; standard caries-experience indices.
- **ICDAS** — International Caries Detection and Assessment System; lesions scored 0–6 (3–6 = cavitated).
- **Arginine** — natural amino acid / prebiotic; metabolized by arginolytic bacteria to ammonia, neutralizing acid and modulating biofilm; non-fluoride anticaries active.
- **Noninferiority margin** — pre-set threshold (here 0.2545) below which the test product is deemed not clinically worse than control.
- **MICE / propensity-score weighting** — multiple-imputation and baseline-adjustment methods used to handle missing data and baseline imbalance.
- **NaF** — sodium fluoride; 0.32% ≈ 1,450 ppm fluoride positive control.
