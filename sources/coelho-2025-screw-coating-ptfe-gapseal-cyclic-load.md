## Why Ingested

Prior page [[prosthetic-materials/coelho-2024-preload-removal-torque-screw-coatings-laboratory]] tested PTFE vs GapSeal under static (non-cyclic) conditions. This 2025 follow-up by the same group adds the critical clinical dimension of cyclic loading (ISO 14801: 300,000 cycles, 37 °C distilled water, simulating 1 year of chewing), revealing that cyclic loading reverses PTFE's RTV deficit — making it the highest-RTV coating under oral simulation despite consistently lowering preload. Extends and qualifies the 2024 finding.

## One-line Summary

In vitro study (n=90, ±CL): GapSeal maintained best overall RTV without cyclic loading; PTFE uniquely reversed to highest RTV (31.50 Ncm) after 300,000-cycle ISO loading, though PTFE consistently reduced preload in both conditions.

## 한줄요약

체외 연구(n=90, 사이클 하중 유무): GapSeal은 비사이클 조건에서 RTV가 가장 높았으나, PTFE는 ISO 30만 사이클 하중 후 오히려 RTV가 가장 높아져(31.50 Ncm) 하중 전후 결과가 역전됨; 단, PTFE는 모든 조건에서 예압(preload)을 유의하게 감소시킴.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Materials (Basel) 2025;18(12):2921
- **DOI**: 10.3390/ma18122921
- **PMID**: 40573051 / **PMCID**: PMC12194849
- **Design**: In vitro, randomized, controlled laboratory study
- **Sample**: 90 external hex implant-abutment complexes (DIUIMPLANT, Busan, Korea); 3 groups × 2 loading conditions (nCL/CL), n=15 per subgroup
- **Key materials**: GapSeal® silicone sealing gel (Hager & Werken), PTFE tape 0.075 mm thick × 12 mm (MIARCO), uncoated control
- **Cyclic loading (CL)**: ISO 14801:2016, 20–200 N sinusoidal at 2 Hz, 300,000 cycles, 37 °C distilled water (~1 year simulated chewing)

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First study comparing PTFE tape and GapSeal screw coatings both with and without ISO-standardized cyclic loading in the same experimental framework.
2. Demonstrates that PTFE's effect on RTV is **loading-condition-dependent**: PTFE lowers RTV vs control without CL, but raises RTV above all other groups after CL.
3. Micro-CT confirms neither coating creates microgaps at the implant–abutment interface.
4. SEM shows PTFE tape sustains more tears/cuts post-CL than post-nCL, yet generates higher RTV — mechanical interlocking of fragmented PTFE debris as proposed mechanism.
5. GapSeal is evenly distributed in threads under nCL; uneven post-CL but still functional.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Tightening torque**: 30 Ncm (manufacturer recommendation); retightened at 10 min per protocol
- **Preload measurement**: Centor Touch Star TH dynamometer at 1000 Hz acquisition
- **RTV measurement**: same dynamometer, recorded after nCL or at end of CL protocol
- **Micro-CT**: ZEISS METROTOM 1500, 10 µm resolution; 2 samples per group, nCL only; 360° rotation
- **SEM**: QUANTA FEI 200 FEG-ESEM at 20 kV; 2 samples per group per CL condition (12 total)
- **Statistics**: One-way ANOVA with Tukey HSD; Shapiro-Wilk normality; Levene variance; η² effect size; α=0.05

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Non-Cyclic Loading (nCL) Group

| Variable | Control | GapSeal | PTFE |
|---|---|---|---|
| Preload (Ncm, M±SD) | 30.95±1.00 | **31.19±1.22** | 29.92±0.76* |
| RTV (Ncm, M±SD) | 27.98±1.20 | **28.48±1.47** | 15.30±1.21*** |

*Significantly lower than CG and GG (p=0.004, p=0.022); ***p<0.001 vs both

### Cyclic Loading (CL) Group

| Variable | Control | GapSeal | PTFE |
|---|---|---|---|
| Preload (Ncm, M±SD) | **31.72±1.16** | 31.42±0.98 | 30.29±0.83* |
| RTV (Ncm, M±SD) | 26.00±2.00 | 27.44±3.76 | **31.50±0.80***† |

*p=0.009 vs CG; ***p<0.001 vs CG and GG; †Reversal from lowest to highest RTV after CL

- **Micro-CT**: No microgaps detected at implant–abutment interface in any group (nCL)
- **SEM**: PTFE fragmented post-CL with visible tears, but fragments present in thread innermost areas; GapSeal retained but distributed unevenly post-CL
- **Effect sizes**: large throughout (η²=0.24–0.94)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- External hex implant only (DIUIMPLANT); results may not generalize to internal connections
- 300,000 cycles = 1 year simulated chewing — longer-term behavior unknown
- CL test performed in distilled water (not saliva — may miss enzymatic/pH effects on coatings)
- PTFE tape application method is technique-sensitive; uniform wrapping not guaranteed (observed in SEM)
- Authors note unknown mechanism for PTFE reversal under CL — in vitro only, no clinical data

## 7. Glossary

- **RTV (Removal Torque Value)**: Torque required to loosen the prosthetic screw after tightening; surrogate for retained preload
- **Preload**: Clamping force generated in the screw joint upon tightening
- **Settling effect**: Loss of preload due to micro-asperity flattening at screw-implant interface; ~2–10% of initial preload lost
- **GapSeal®**: Highly viscous silicone matrix with thymol; antimicrobial/sealing function; injectable into implant internal compartment
- **PTFE tape**: Polytetrafluoroethylene; biocompatible; low friction; wrapped twice around screw threads
- **CL / nCL**: Cyclic loading / non-cyclic loading (static)
- **ISO 14801**: ISO standard for fatigue testing of endosseous dental implants
- **η² (eta squared)**: ANOVA effect size (small ≥0.01, medium ≥0.06, large ≥0.14)
