## One-line Summary
Porcine tibia pilot histomorphometry (n = 18 osteotomies, 6 per group) — immediate BIC was 60.3% with osseodensification vs 40.7% with Summers osteotome and 16.3% with standard subtractive drilling, with most uniform trabecular condensation around OD.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Ex vivo bench histomorphometric pilot study (porcine tibia)
- **Journal**: Implant Dentistry 2018;27(4):424-428
- **DOI**: 10.1097/ID.0000000000000767
- **Sample**: Fresh porcine tibial plateau; n = 6 osteotomies per group × 3 groups = 18 osteotomies and 18 implants (4.7 × 13 mm tapered screw-vent)
- **Primary Question**: How do standard drilling (SD), Summers osteotome (SO), and osseodensification (OD) compare on immediate BIC, peri-implant bone volume %, and trabecular integrity in low-density bone?

## 2. Key Contributions
- Direct bench comparison of three osteotomy paradigms (subtractive cutting vs malleting compaction vs OD compaction) in the same low-density bone substrate.
- BIC at placement: OD 60.3% > SO 40.7% > SD 16.3%.
- OD produced uniform compacted bone zone ~0.7 mm laterally and apically around the implant.
- Apical compaction was a unique OD finding (not consistently produced by SD or SO).
- SO group showed evidence of trabecular fracture/microdamage; OD showed whole intact trabeculae being condensed.
- Bench rationale for the clinical hypothesis that OD provides better primary stability and BIC than osteotome or standard drilling.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Fresh porcine tibial plateau (low-density cancellous bone analogue) was used. Three osteotomy techniques were applied: (1) SD — standard drilling sequence with manufacturer's burs (pilot 1.7 mm, then sequence to final 4.7 mm); (2) SO — Summers osteotome compaction with progressive sizes I, II, III; (3) OD — Densah burs in CCW per manufacturer protocol. Eighteen 4.7 × 13 mm tapered screw-vent implants were placed (n = 6 per group). All specimens were fixed in 10% formalin, sectioned, stained, and analyzed histomorphometrically for BIC and percentage bone volume (BV%) within a 2-mm zone around the implant.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- BIC at placement: OD 60.3%, SO 40.7%, SD 16.3% (descriptive comparison; no formal inferential statistics extractable from available text — pilot n = 6/group).
- BV% in 2-mm zone around implant: SD ~54%, SO ~49%; OD reported as greatest (exact OD value not extracted from available text).
- OD compacted zone: ~0.7 mm uniform thickness laterally and apically, with intact whole trabeculae filling intrathread spaces.
- SO group: histologic evidence of trabecular fracture and microdamage.
- SD group: lowest BIC; visible gap between drill-cut bone and implant thread.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Ex vivo porcine tibia, not in vivo human; no healing, no remodeling captured.
- Pilot n = 6 per group; descriptive only — no p-values or CIs in extractable text.
- BIC measured at placement (mechanical contact), not post-osseointegration BIC.
- Author affiliations include the OD inventor/licensor city (Jackson, MI) — potential conflict.
- BV% values appear close between SD (54%) and SO (49%) — the headline BIC differences come from contact geometry, not raw bone volume.
- No randomization at the osteotomy level described.

## 7. Glossary
- **Osseodensification (OD)**: Densah-bur CCW osteotomy preserving and compacting bone.
- **Summers osteotome (SO)**: malleted progressive compaction technique introduced by Summers in 1994.
- **Standard drilling (SD)**: subtractive bur drilling that removes bone tissue.
- **BIC (Bone-to-Implant Contact)**: percentage of implant surface in direct contact with bone in histologic sections.
- **BV%**: bone volume per total volume in a defined peri-implant zone (here 2 mm wide).
- **Trabecular integrity**: degree to which native trabecular architecture is preserved (vs fractured/displaced).
