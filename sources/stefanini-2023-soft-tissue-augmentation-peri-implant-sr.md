## Why Ingested

Medium- and long-term stability of soft tissue augmentation (STA) at implant sites was a gap not yet covered in the wiki — existing pages address short-term STA outcomes and single-technique comparisons. This SR is the first in this wiki to directly answer whether early KMW/MT gains are preserved ≥36 months. Extends [[wiki/implants/thoma-2018-soft-tissue-augmentation-periimplant-health]] (Thoma 2018 short-term network MA) and contextualizes the clinical guidance in [[wiki/overviews/peri-implant-soft-tissue-dehiscence-prevention]].

## One-line Summary

Systematic review of 15 prospective studies (≥36-month follow-up) finds that soft tissue augmentation at implants yields high survival rates, stable soft tissue margins, and maintained KMW/MT gains — especially with CTG-based bilaminar techniques — while non-augmented sites show progressive apical recession.

## 한줄요약

15편 전향적 연구(추적 ≥36개월)를 분석한 체계적 고찰: CTG 기반 이중층 술식으로 연조직 증대 시 임플란트 생존율 높음(90.9–100%), 각화점막폭·점막두께 유지 또는 증가, 비증대 부위는 점진적 점막퇴축 발생.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Clinical Oral Implants Research, 2023;34(Suppl. 26):28–42
- **DOI**: 10.1111/clr.14150
- **Published**: 2023 (received Nov 2022, accepted Jul 2023)
- **Study type**: Systematic review (qualitative analysis only; no meta-analysis possible)
- **PROSPERO registered**: Yes
- **Search cutoff**: September 1, 2022
- **Databases searched**: MEDLINE (PubMed), EMBASE (OVID), Cochrane CENTRAL, LILACS, Web of Science, Scopus, ClinicalTrials.gov, OpenGrey
- **Included studies**: 15 prospective studies (4 RCTs, 5 non-RCTs, 6 case series)

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First SR specifically addressing **medium- and long-term (≥36 months)** outcomes of peri-implant STA, covering KMW, MT/volumetric stability, MBL, peri-implant health, and PROMs.
2. Documents that early STA gains are **maintained** over time: CTG-augmented sites show weighted mean soft tissue margin apical shift of only −0.06 mm over a mean 4.8-year observation period.
3. Demonstrates superior long-term margin stability at **CTG-augmented vs. non-augmented** implants (non-augmented: weighted mean apical displacement 0.96 mm over 6.2 years).
4. Provides evidence that CTG-augmented sites may exhibit **progressive KMW increase and MT gain** (creeping attachment analogue), with some sites showing volume gain up to 5 years.
5. Identifies persistent evidence gaps: qualitative analysis only possible due to heterogeneity; more RCTs needed for graft substitutes (ADM, CCM).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**PICOT framework**:
- P: Adults (≥18 yr) with ≥1 healthy dental implant
- I: STA using APF-based (APF + FGG) or bilaminar techniques (CTG, ADM, CCM)
- C: Non-augmented sites or different STA techniques
- O: Peri-implant health rate, biological complications, soft tissue margin stability, KMW/MT, MBL, PROMs, esthetics
- T: ≥36 months minimum follow-up

**Inclusion criteria**:
- Prospective interventional human studies only (no retrospective, no case reports, no animal)
- ≥10 participants at first follow-up ≥36 months
- STA using FGG, CTG, or graft substitutes (ADM, CCM)
- Healthy implants at baseline (no pre-existing peri-implant disease)

**Exclusion**: simultaneous hard + soft tissue augmentation; smoker-only populations; edentulous areas or natural teeth augmentation.

**Risk of bias**: 3 RCTs moderate, 1 RCT low; 4 non-RCTs low, 1 moderate; 4 case series low, 2 moderate (Cochrane tool used).

**Graft materials assessed**:
- Autogenous: CTG (connective tissue graft), FGG (free gingival graft)
- Substitutes: ADM (acellular dermal matrix, Mucoderm), CCM/XCM (xenogeneic cross-linked collagen matrix, Geistlich Fibrogide)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Included studies summary (15 studies, total cohort spanning 2004–2022)**:
- Bianchi & Sanfilippo 2004 (RCT, 96 pts, 36–108 mo), Cosyn 2016 (non-RCT, 22 pts, 60 mo), Eeckhout 2020 (case series, 15 pts, 36 mo), Eghbali 2018 (case series, 37 pts, 60 mo), Fenner 2016 (non-RCT, 36 pts, 86.4 mo median), Hanser & Khoury 2016 (non-RCT, 46 pts, 60 mo), Hosseini 2020 (non-RCT, 19 pts, 36–60 mo), Oh 2020 (RCT, 15 pts, 48 mo), Roccuzzo 2016 (non-RCT, 98 pts, 120 mo), Roccuzzo 2019 (case series, 16 pts, 60 mo), Seyssens 2020 (non-RCT, 18 pts, 120 mo), Stefanini 2016 (case series, 20 pts, 36 mo), Thoma 2020 (RCT, 17 pts, 36 mo), Thoma 2022 (RCT, 15 pts, 60 mo), Zucchelli 2018 (case series, 19 pts, 60 mo)

**Implant survival**: 90.9%–100% at augmented sites across all included studies.

**Peri-implant disease incidence**:
- Mucositis: 0%–50% (wide range, partly reflecting varying definitions)
- Peri-implantitis: 0%–7.14%

**Soft tissue margin stability (midfacial recession)**:
- CTG bilaminar: weighted mean apical shift −0.06 mm / mean 4.8 yr
- CCM/XCM bilaminar: weighted mean apical shift 0.58 mm / 3–5 yr
- Non-augmented controls: weighted mean apical displacement 0.96 mm / mean 6.2 yr
- APF + FGG: mean coronal migration +0.54 mm / 4 yr (Oh 2020)
- 10-yr data (Seyssens 2020): 100% of non-augmented sites developed ≥1 mm recession

**Marginal bone levels (MBL)**:
- Most studies: MBL change ≤0.6 mm at augmented sites
- CTG bilaminar weighted mean: 0.63 mm loss / ~5 yr; drops to 0.18 mm over ~4 yr in lower-loss cohorts
- Outlier: Fenner 2016 reported 2.2–2.5 mm mean bone loss at ≥5 yr
- APF + FGG (Oh 2020): significantly better MBL stability vs. non-augmented over 4 yr
- Some studies reported MBL gain (Eeckhout 2020, Oh 2020)

**KMW and MT / volumetric outcomes**:
- CTG-augmented: stable or increased KMW over time (creeping attachment effect noted in Stefanini 2016, Zucchelli 2018)
- CCM: maintained KMW at 3–5 yr (Thoma 2020, 2022); less data than CTG
- MT / volumetric gain: stable up to 3–5 yr with CTG and graft substitutes; CTG sites may progressively gain MT
- Hosseini 2020 (3D scanning): CTG sites gained volume to 5 yr; non-augmented lost volume at 3 and 5 yr
- ADM and CCM: only 2 cohorts; insufficient data for strong conclusions

**Plaque/BOP/PD**: No substantial changes between early and later time points in any study.

**Esthetic outcomes and PROMs**: No substantial change over time; patient satisfaction and professionally assessed esthetics maintained.

**Quantitative meta-analysis**: Not performed — heterogeneous study designs and outcome measures precluded pooling.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Qualitative analysis only — no meta-analysis possible; results cannot be pooled statistically.
- Heterogeneous outcome measures, flap approaches, implant systems, and follow-up definitions.
- Limited number of RCTs (only 4), mostly single-center, frequently self-supported funding.
- Lack of implant location data in several studies (anterior vs. posterior not always specified).
- ADM and CCM outcomes based on only 2 cohorts each — conclusions less robust than for CTG.
- No long-term RCTs comparing different STA techniques with standardized protocols.
- Future need: medium/long-term RCTs using mixed-modeling network meta-analysis designs.
- Smoking was variably included or excluded across studies.

## 6. Related Work

- Thoma et al. 2018 (short-term STA network MA): early KMW/MT gains established, this SR extends to medium/long term
- Thoma et al. 2021 (EAO consensus on soft tissue management)
- Tavelli et al. 2021 (network MA: STA improves short-term clinical/radiographic peri-implant parameters)
- Perussolo et al. 2018 (4-yr longitudinal: KMW <2 mm → more brushing discomfort + MBL)
- Seyssens et al. 2020 (10-yr: all non-augmented immediately placed implants developed ≥1 mm recession)
- Zucchelli et al. 2020 (bilaminar CTG techniques for implant esthetics)
- Roccuzzo et al. 2016 (10-yr: FGG benefit for brushing comfort and MBL)

## 7. Glossary

- **STA** (soft tissue augmentation, 연조직 증대술): surgical addition of volume/keratinized tissue to implant sites
- **KMW** (keratinized mucosa width, 각화점막폭): mm width of keratinized mucosa around implant
- **MT** (mucosal thickness, 점막두께): thickness of peri-implant soft tissue, measured by caliper or ultrasound
- **CTG** (connective tissue graft, 결합조직이식편): autogenous palatal graft used to augment MT/KMW
- **FGG** (free gingival graft, 유리치은이식편): full-thickness palatal graft for KMW augmentation
- **ADM** (acellular dermal matrix, 무세포진피기질): Mucoderm — porcine-derived graft substitute
- **CCM/XCM** (xenogeneic cross-linked collagen matrix, 이종교차결합콜라겐기질): Geistlich Fibrogide
- **APF** (apically positioned flap, 첨부변위판막): flap technique to expose/augment keratinized zone
- **Bilaminar technique** (이중층술식): flap coronally advanced to fully cover graft (primary intention healing)
- **PSTD** (peri-implant soft tissue dehiscence, 임플란트주위연조직열개): recession defect at implant
- **MBL** (marginal bone level/loss, 변연골): radiographic crestal bone level
- **Midf REC** (midfacial recession): apical shift of peri-implant soft tissue margin from crown margin
- **PROMs** (patient-reported outcome measures, 환자보고결과지표): brushing comfort, satisfaction VAS, OHIP
- **PES** (pink esthetic score, 핑크심미점수): standardized esthetic index for peri-implant soft tissue
