## Why Ingested
큰 근단치주염 병소(특히 ≥10 mm cyst-like 병소)에서 비수술적 근관치료가 외과적 개입 없이도 높은 치유율을 낼 수 있는지에 대한 실제 임상 근거를 보강한다. NaOCl 세정 기반 디스인펙션 프로토콜의 치유 결과를 다루므로 [[endodontics/irrigation/rossi-fedele-2023-irrigation-dressing-apical-periodontitis-sr-ma]]의 세정·드레싱이 근단치주염 치유에 미치는 영향 결론을 임상 outcome 측면에서 확장한다.

## One-line Summary
Retrospective study (199 teeth/184 patients, single operator, NaOCl single-visit protocol, >1–8 y follow-up): nonsurgical RCT of large apical periodontitis lesions healed/healing in 89% (loose) / 67% (rigid) for initial treatment and 79% / 47% for retreatment; previous abscess, antibiotic use, lesions ≥10 mm, and sinus tract worsened outcome, and CBCT-evaluated cases showed lower healed rates.

## 한줄요약

## 1. Document Information
- **Title**: Outcome of Nonsurgical Root Canal Treatment of Teeth With Large Apical Periodontitis Lesions: A Retrospective Study
- **Authors**: Liliana Artaza, Andrea F. Campello, Giuliana Soimu, Flávio R.F. Alves, Isabela N. Rôças, José F. Siqueira Jr.
- **Journal**: Journal of Endodontics, Volume 50, Issue 10, p1403–1411, October 2024
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.joen.2024.07.005 (article ID S0099-2399(24)00441-2)
- **Study type**: Single-operator retrospective outcome study
- **Topic**: Healing outcome and prognostic factors of nonsurgical root canal treatment/retreatment of teeth with large apical periodontitis lesions

## 2. Key Contributions
- Reports real-world outcomes of nonsurgical root canal treatment (RCT) and orthograde retreatment specifically for teeth with **large** apical periodontitis lesions, a clinical situation often defaulted to surgery.
- Demonstrates that high favorable (healed + healing) outcome rates are achievable nonsurgically: ~89% (loose criterion) for initial treatment and ~79% for retreatment.
- Identifies prognostic variables that worsen outcome: previous acute apical abscess, prior antibiotic use, very large lesions (≥10 mm), and presence of a sinus tract.
- Highlights that outcome assessment modality matters — cases evaluated with cone-beam computed tomography (CBCT) showed lower "healed" rates than those judged by periapical radiographs alone.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Sample**: 199 teeth from 184 patients, all with large apical periodontitis lesions, treated or retreated by a **single operator**.
- **Protocol**: Most teeth managed in a **single visit** using sodium hypochlorite (NaOCl) irrigation as the core disinfection step.
- **Follow-up**: Periodic recall from >1 year up to 8 years; median follow-up ~31.5 months (treatment) and ~32 months (retreatment).
- **Outcome assessment**: Clinical plus radiographic/tomographic (periapical radiograph and/or CBCT) criteria; each case categorized as **healed, healing, or diseased**.
- **Statistical handling**: Data dichotomized so that "healing" cases were counted as success under a **loose** criterion and as failure under a **rigid** criterion; influence of clinical variables on outcome evaluated.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Initial treatment cases**: 67% healed, 22.5% healing, 11% diseased. Success = 89% (loose) / 67% (rigid). Median follow-up 31.5 months.
- **Retreatment cases**: 47% healed, 32% healing, 21% diseased. Success = 79% (loose) / 47% (rigid). Median follow-up 32 months.
- **Negative prognostic variables**: previous abscess, prior antibiotic use, very large lesions (≥10 mm), and a sinus tract.
- **Imaging effect**: cases evaluated by CBCT had lower healed rates than those evaluated radiographically (CBCT detects residual disease more sensitively).
- **Bottom line**: nonsurgical treatment/retreatment can yield high favorable outcomes even for large lesions, supporting a nonsurgical-first approach in many such cases.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Retrospective, single-operator, single-center design — limits generalizability and introduces operator-skill confounding.
- No randomization or control arm; outcomes reflect one expert's protocol.
- Variable follow-up length and mixed imaging modalities (PA radiograph vs CBCT) complicate cross-case comparison; CBCT subset is not directly comparable to radiograph-only subset.
- "Healing" category is inherently interim; longer follow-up could reclassify some cases.

## 6. Related Work
- Builds on the classic endodontic outcome literature (Sjögren 1990; Ng/Mann/Gulabivala prospective outcome studies; Toronto Study phases).
- Relates to nonsurgical management of large cyst-like periapical lesions (Lin 2009; Caliskan 2004; Saini 2023 CBCT prospective cohort; Mosquera-Barreiro 2024 CBCT retrospective).
- Connects to lesion-size effect meta-analysis (Baseri 2023) and to the authors' prior sinus-tract outcome study (Artaza 2021).

## 7. Glossary
- **Apical periodontitis**: inflammatory disease of the periradicular tissues caused by root canal infection.
- **Healed / healing / diseased**: standard endodontic outcome categories — complete resolution, ongoing reduction, or persistent/worsening disease.
- **Loose vs rigid criterion**: outcome dichotomization counting "healing" as success (loose) or failure (rigid).
- **Sinus tract**: a drainage channel from a periapical infection, a sign of chronic abscess.
- **CBCT**: cone-beam computed tomography; 3D imaging with higher sensitivity for detecting residual periapical disease than 2D radiographs.
