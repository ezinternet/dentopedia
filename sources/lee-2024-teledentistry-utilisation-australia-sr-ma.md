## Why Ingested

Extends the global teledentistry diagnostic-accuracy evidence in [[practice-management/gurgel-juarez-2022-accuracy-effectiveness-teledentistry-systematic]] with an Australia-specific SR+MA. It pins down country-level caries-screening accuracy (sens 69.7% / spec 97.4%), and adds dimensions the global SR did not foreground: patient-vs-clinician acceptance (patients positive, clinicians mixed) and economic evaluations showing cost savings — directly relevant to regional/rural/remote access and Korean clinic teletriage policy.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (18 articles, Australia, to June 2024): teledentistry is an effective caries-screening tool (avg sensitivity 69.7%, specificity 97.4%), with patients positive but clinicians mixed, and economic evaluations suggesting cost savings — improving access in regional/rural/remote areas.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Country-specific (Australia) synthesis of teledentistry utilisation across diagnostic accuracy, acceptance, and economics.
- Quantifies caries-screening accuracy: average sensitivity 69.7%, average specificity 97.4%.
- Separates stakeholder acceptance: patients positive, clinicians mixed.
- Highlights cost savings for patients and healthcare providers, and access gains in regional/rural/remote areas.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Systematic search of four databases to June 2024.
- JBI Critical Appraisal Tools for quality/risk-of-bias assessment.
- Eighteen articles included, spanning study types: six diagnostic tests, six cross-sectional studies, four economic evaluations, one qualitative study, one expert opinion.
- Meta-analysis of diagnostic-accuracy data (caries screening), with sensitivity/specificity pooled.
- (abstract-only — full text not retrieved; detailed pooling methods, models, and per-study data not extractable.)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Caries screening accuracy**: average sensitivity = 69.7%, average specificity = 97.4%.
- Non-significant negative correlation between specificity and sensitivity (r = 0.432).
- **Acceptance**: clinician opinions MIXED; patient opinions POSITIVE.
- **Economics**: teledentistry may lead to savings for patients and healthcare providers.
- **Access**: increases healthcare access, especially in regional/rural/remote areas.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- abstract-only — full text not retrieved; included-study heterogeneity, pooling specifics, and barrier details are not fully extractable.
- High specificity but moderate sensitivity (~70%) means teledentistry caries screening may miss a meaningful fraction of lesions — confirmatory in-person examination remains needed.
- Clinician acceptance is mixed; implementation barriers identified (not enumerated in abstract).
- Single-country (Australia) scope limits direct generalisability.

## 7. Glossary

- **Teledentistry**: use of information-based/telecommunication technologies to deliver dental care, consultation, or screening remotely.
- **Sensitivity**: proportion of true caries cases correctly identified by the screening test.
- **Specificity**: proportion of true non-caries (healthy) cases correctly identified as negative.
- **JBI Critical Appraisal Tools**: Joanna Briggs Institute instruments for assessing study quality/risk of bias by study design.
