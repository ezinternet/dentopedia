## One-line Summary
SR+MA on TMD prevalence; pooled global prevalence approximately 31% for any TMD sign/symptom, with higher rates in women and younger adults; provides epidemiological foundation for TMD research and resource planning.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Clinical Oral Investigations 2021;25:441–453
- **DOI**: 10.1007/s00784-020-03710-w
- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis

## 2. Key Contributions
- Largest SR+MA on global TMD (악관절증) prevalence at time of publication
- Pools data from population-based studies using standardized diagnostic criteria
- Stratifies prevalence by TMD subtype (myofascial, disc displacement, arthralgia)
- Examines sex and age distribution of TMD prevalence
- Provides evidence base for understanding TMD burden

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Comprehensive database search (PubMed, Embase, Cochrane, Lilacs)
- Inclusion: cross-sectional and cohort studies with population-based sampling; standardized TMD diagnosis
- Primary outcome: prevalence of any TMD sign or symptom
- Secondary: subtype-specific prevalence (pain vs. disc displacement vs. arthralgia)
- Random-effects meta-analysis with inverse-variance weighting
- Meta-regression for age, sex, diagnostic criteria

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Any TMD sign/symptom**: pooled prevalence ~31% (95% CI varies by subtype)
- **Painful TMD** (myofascial pain + arthralgia): ~11–13% in general population
- **Disc displacement**: ~30% (includes asymptomatic disc displacement)
- **Female predominance**: women 1.5–2× more likely to have painful TMD than men
- **Peak age**: 20–45 years
- Heterogeneity was high (I² >80%) reflecting variability in diagnostic criteria and populations

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- High heterogeneity due to mixed diagnostic systems (RDC/TMD, DC/TMD, clinical criteria)
- Many older studies use non-validated criteria
- Publication bias toward clinical/specialty populations (overestimation risk)
- Population-based studies underrepresent elderly and children

## 6. Related Work
- BMJ 2023 guideline references epidemiology for burden estimation
- All TMD intervention studies use this prevalence data for sample size calculations

## 7. Glossary
- **TMD prevalence**: Proportion of population with one or more TMD signs or symptoms at a given time
- **Myofascial pain**: Most common painful TMD subtype; masticatory muscle tenderness and pain
- **Disc displacement** (원판 변위): Abnormal condyle-disc relationship; most common structural TMD finding
- **Arthralgia** (관절통): TMJ joint pain on palpation or movement; distinct from myofascial pain
- **RDC/TMD**: Research Diagnostic Criteria; earlier biaxial classification system
- **DC/TMD**: Diagnostic Criteria for TMD (2014); current standard with validated instruments
