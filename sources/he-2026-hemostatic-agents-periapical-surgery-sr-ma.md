## Why Ingested

근관외과(Periapical Surgery) 지혈제 선택에 SR+MA 근거 공백. 임상에서 황산제이철(Ferric Sulfate) vs 에피네프린(Epinephrine) 혼용 상황 정리. 네트워크 메타분석으로 에피네프린(Epinephrine)·알루미늄 클로라이드(Aluminum Chloride, AlCl₃)가 콜라겐(Collagen) 대비 우월함을 확인한 최초 SR+MA. See [[overviews/oral-surgery-decision-ladder]].

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 10 studies (388 teeth) on hemostatic agents in periapical surgery: average efficacy 63 ± 6.6%, no significant differences among agents, but network meta-analysis shows epinephrine and aluminum chloride superior to collagen (P < .05); smoking was the only clinical variable trending toward significance.

## 한줄요약

10편 SR+MA (388개 치아): 근관외과 지혈제 평균 효능 63 ± 6.6%로 양호하나 제제 간 유의차 없음; 네트워크 메타분석에서 에피네프린·알루미늄 클로라이드가 콜라겐보다 유의하게 우수, 흡연 습관만 예후 경향성(P=.074) 확인.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA to pool hemostatic efficacy rates across periapical surgery trials using global pooled effect, meta-regression, and network meta-analysis simultaneously.
- Quantified overall hemostatic efficacy at **63 ± 6.6%** across agents.
- Network meta-analysis finding: epinephrine and aluminum chloride > collagen (P < .05); no other pairwise differences were statistically significant.
- Identified **smoking** as the only patient-related variable trending toward significance (P = .074) for hemostatic outcomes.
- Found **no relationship** between hemostatic efficacy and periapical surgery prognosis.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

abstract-only — full text not retrieved

- **Design**: Systematic review + meta-analysis following PRISMA guidelines.
- **Databases searched**: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, Cochrane Library (up to October 2024).
- **Inclusion**: RCTs and controlled clinical trials analyzing hemostatic agent efficacy in periapical surgery with ≥12-month follow-up for prognosis.
- **Risk of bias tools**: RoB2 (for RCTs), ROBINS-I-V2 (for CCTs).
- **Meta-analysis types**: (1) global pooled effect meta-analysis, (2) meta-regression, (3) network meta-analysis.
- **N**: 10 studies, 388 teeth.
- Specific agents tested across included studies: epinephrine, ferric sulfate, aluminum chloride, bone wax, tranexamic acid, collagen — exact breakdown per study requires full text.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

abstract-only — full text not retrieved

- Only 10 studies with 388 teeth — small evidence base limiting statistical power.
- Exclusion of studies with <12-month follow-up for prognosis may reduce available data.
- Likely high heterogeneity in study protocols, agent concentrations, and surgical techniques.
- Full text needed for: agent-specific subgroup data, forest plots, risk of bias distribution, heterogeneity statistics (I²), and confidence interval widths.

## 6. Related Work

abstract-only — full text not retrieved

- Periapical surgery (apicoectomy/root-end surgery) relies on hemorrhage control for retrograde fill and visualization.
- Classic agents used: racemic epinephrine pellets, ferric sulfate (Astringedent), aluminum chloride, bone wax, collagen sponge, tranexamic acid.
- Prior narrative reviews and single RCTs existed; this is the first SR+MA quantifying comparative efficacy.

## 7. Glossary

- **Periapical surgery (근관외과)**: Surgical treatment of periapical lesions, including apicoectomy and retrograde root-end filling.
- **Hemostatic agent (지혈제)**: Agent applied to the bony crypt to control hemorrhage during surgery.
- **Epinephrine (에피네프린)**: Racemic epinephrine pellets (e.g., Racellet); vasoconstrictor.
- **Ferric sulfate (황산제이철)**: Astringedent; protein precipitation hemostatic; concerns re: tissue irritation.
- **Aluminum chloride (알루미늄 클로라이드, AlCl₃)**: Tissue astringent; used in raceway cords and surgical hemostasis.
- **Bone wax (골왁스)**: Mechanical hemostatic; plugs cancellous bone spaces.
- **Tranexamic acid (트라넥삼산, TXA)**: Antifibrinolytic agent applied locally or systemically.
- **Network meta-analysis (네트워크 메타분석)**: Simultaneously compares multiple treatments via both direct and indirect evidence.
- **RoB2 / ROBINS-I-V2**: Cochrane risk-of-bias tools for RCTs and non-randomized studies respectively.
