## Why Ingested

The wiki's existing alveolar-ridge-split pages document the *technique* (deliberate greenstick/buccal corticotomy in [[wiki/bone-regeneration/enislidis-2006-staged-ridge-splitting-implant-mandible]], chisel split-crest + GTR in [[wiki/bone-regeneration/simion-1992-jawbone-enlargement-split-crest-gtr]], and the case-level horizontal augmentation in [[wiki/bone-regeneration/ayoub-2018-ridge-splitting-horizontal-augmentation-case]]) but all rely on chisel/piezo instrumentation. This SR+MA is the missing *device-choice* evidence: it directly compares ultrasonographic (USG/piezo) vs motorized ridge expander (MRE) for ARSE and maps each device to its biology — USG to osteo-mobilization (precise osteotomy), MRE to minimally invasive osteo-condensation — telling the clinician which device fits which split strategy. It extends ayoub-2018's single-case horizontal-augmentation rationale to a pooled 24-study, 1287-implant evidence base.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (24 studies, 1287 implants in 634 patients) of modern ARSE devices: USG (piezo) yields 3.40 mm ridge width gain vs MRE 2.83 mm (p<0.0001), overall implant survival 98.07%; USG suits osteo-mobilization, MRE suits minimally invasive osteo-condensation.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First SR+MA to quantify **ridge width gain (RWG)** and **implant survival** specifically for *modern* ARSE devices (USG/piezo and MRE), filling the gap left by the prior Jha SR which only catalogued devices without outcomes.
2. Head-to-head pooled comparison of the two dominant device families: **USG RWG 3.40 mm vs MRE RWG 2.83 mm**, statistically significant (p<0.0001).
3. Pooled **overall implant survival 98.07%** across 1287 implants, with device-specific survival nearly identical (USG ~98.4%, MRE ~98.97%).
4. Maps device to ARSE biology/technique: **USG → osteo-mobilization (OM)** (precise vertical+horizontal osteotomy, technique-sensitive, delayed implant); **MRE → crestal-osteotomy + osteo-condensation (CO-OC)** (minimally invasive, immediate implant, fewer surgeries).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases**: PubMed/MEDLINE, Cochrane CENTRAL, Grey Open, hand search of reference lists and prior SRs; published until September 2020.
- **PICO**: P = horizontally deficient (2–6 mm) edentulous arch indicated for implant; I = ARSE with modern devices; C = conventional ARSE or GBR; O = ridge width gain, survival.
- **Inclusion**: RCT/CCT/cohort/case-control; ≥10 implants; modern ARSE device named; ≥3 months follow-up after prosthesis; English; abstract+full text.
- **Exclusion**: case reports; <10 implants.
- **PRISMA flow**: 1020 database + 10 other records → 110 after dedup → 79 excluded at abstract → 31 full-text → 7 excluded (<10 implants / case reports) → **24 included** (22 cohort + 2 RCT).
- **Risk of bias**: 22 observational via modified Newcastle-Ottawa; 2 RCT via modified Cochrane tool. **GRADE certainty: "very Low."**
- **Meta-analysis subset**: 10 USG-device articles + 7 MRE-device articles (continuous SMD for RWG, proportion for survival; fixed + random effects).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Population**: 634 patients (200 M / 286 F where reported), age 17–70 y, mean follow-up 13.23 months, 1287 implants.
- **Ridge width gain**:
  - USG: initial 3.76 mm → final 7.13 mm → **gain 3.40 mm**.
  - MRE: initial 3.78 mm → final 5.92 mm → **gain 2.83 mm**.
  - USG vs MRE difference significant, **p<0.0001**.
- **Implant survival**: overall **98.07%**; USG ~98.42% (pooled proportion 97.85%, 95% CI 95.83–99.06), MRE ~98.97% (pooled 98.50%, 95% CI 95.41–99.73).
- **Heterogeneity (RWG)**: high — USG I²=96.42% (Q=195.78, DF=7); MRE I²=97.20% (Q=178.70, DF=5). Abstract-level overall I²=88.39%.
- **Publication bias**: none on overall by Egger (intercept 6.6634, p=0.6142, NS); survival funnels symmetric (USG Egger p=0.8301, MRE p=0.4795).
- **Complications**: most common = implant failure, then buccal plate fracture / "bad split", peri-implantitis; some MRE studies noted buccal plate dehiscence.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **GRADE "very Low"** — predominantly observational (22/24), only 2 RCTs (both small; neither directly addressed the device-comparison objective: Mahmoud = flap type, Kheur = graft particle).
- **No study directly compared conventional vs modern devices** — comparison with chisel/osteotome baseline is inferential.
- Very high statistical heterogeneity in RWG meta-analyses (I²>96%).
- Inconsistent reporting of buccal dehiscence, bad split, cervical bone loss, and implant stability prevented their meta-analysis.
- USG vs MRE difference is confounded by technique (OM vs CO-OC) and implant-timing (delayed vs immediate), not a clean device-only contrast.
- Future: large RCTs with adequate sample and long follow-up.

## 7. Glossary

- **ARSE**: Alveolar Ridge Split and Expansion — pre-prosthetic procedure to widen a narrow ridge.
- **RWG**: Ridge Width Gain (mm).
- **USG**: Ultrasonographic / piezoelectric bone-cutting device (piezo).
- **MRE**: Motorized Ridge Expander.
- **HSE**: Hand Screw Expander. **CCBS**: Crest Control Bone Splitting system. **OD**: Osteodensification.
- **OC**: Osteo-condensation (lateral cancellous-bone condensation; ↑ peri-implant density).
- **CO-OC**: Crestal Osteotomy + Osteo-condensation (minimally invasive).
- **OM**: Osteo-mobilization (precise vertical + horizontal osteotomies). **DOM**: delayed (2-stage) OM. **SOM**: staged OM (graft + implant at 3rd stage).
- **III / DII / TSII**: Immediate / Delayed (2nd-stage) / Third-stage implant insertion.
- **SMD**: Standardized Mean Difference. **GRADE**: certainty-of-evidence rating system.
