## Why Ingested
Definitive mechanistic review of S. mutans Gtf biology — foundational reference for EPS matrix assembly models in [[oral-microbiology/koo-2013-exopolysaccharides-matrix-biofilm-virulence]] and [[oral-microbiology/karygianni-2020-biofilm-matrixome-extracellular-components]].

## One-line Summary
Comprehensive narrative review of S. mutans glucosyltransferases (GtfB, GtfC, GtfD) — their distinct glucan products, differential surface adsorption to pellicle and bacterial surfaces, activity enhancement upon adsorption (3–4x), and coordinated roles in cariogenic biofilm matrix assembly.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Establishes that glucan (from Gtf activity) comprises 10–20% dry weight of dental plaque; fructan ~1–2%.
- Differentiates the three S. mutans Gtfs: GtfB (primarily insoluble α-1,3-linked glucan; binds avidly to bacteria), GtfC (mixture of soluble and insoluble glucan; primary pellicle enzyme), GtfD (predominantly soluble glucan; serves as primer for GtfB).
- Demonstrates pellicle-adsorbed Gtf is enzymatically active within 1 minute of tooth surface placement in the mouth; activity is 3–4x enhanced compared to solution-phase enzyme.
- Shows GtfB converts non-Gtf producers (A. viscosus, L. casei, S. mutans) to glucan-forming organisms after adsorption to their surfaces.
- Demonstrates surface-adsorbed GtfB + GtfC cooperatively form insoluble glucan that promotes tight bacterial cell clustering into microcolonies (EPS-microcolony complexes).
- Reviews mutanase/dextranase (glucan-degrading enzymes) as plaque control strategies; notes that agents inhibiting Gtf in solution often have reduced/no effect on adsorbed enzymes.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Design: comprehensive narrative review.
- Evidence base: in vitro biochemical studies with purified Gtf enzymes on saliva-coated hydroxyapatite (sHA), in situ experiments with HA discs placed in human oral cavity, Scatchard binding analyses, dextranase/mutanase digestion structural analyses, and gene deletion studies.
- No formal clinical population; synthesises biochemical and microbiological literature.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Glucan: 10–20% dry weight of dental plaque; fructan ~1–2%.
- Dental plaque overall: ~80% water; ~40% dry-weight protein; variable Ca, P, Mg, F.
- GtfC has greatest affinity for sHA (highest binding sites by Scatchard plot); GtfD has relatively few sHA binding sites.
- Km values for all three Gtfs are lower by 2–8-fold following adsorption to sHA (enhanced activity).
- Pellicle-adsorbed GtfC activity: 3–4x enhanced vs. solution-phase enzyme (Schilling et al., Venkitaraman et al.).
- Active Gtf detected on HA discs within 1 min of oral placement.
- GtfB binds more avidly to oral bacteria (A. viscosus, L. casei, S. mutans) than GtfC or GtfD, even in ductal saliva.
- GtfC structural distinction: hydrophobic domain in C-terminal direct repeat units — associated with pellicle-binding activity via interactions with lysozyme and α-amylase.
- Glucan structure shifts dramatically when formed by surface-adsorbed vs. solution-phase enzyme: increase in α-1,3-linkages and branching (2,3-, 3,4-, 3,6-, 3,4,6-linked glucose).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Conformational changes of Gtfs upon surface adsorption remain mechanistically unclear.
- Chemotherapeutic inhibitors effective in solution often fail against adsorbed Gtfs — requires new strategies.
- Structure of glucan-binding domain (glucan-binding C-terminus repeat units) and its relationship to catalytic domain not fully elucidated.
- Role of (lipo)teichoic acids in modulating Gtf activity in situ deserves further study.

## 7. Glossary
- GtfB (GtfI): S. mutans glucosyltransferase B; synthesises primarily insoluble α-1,3-linked glucan; binds avidly to bacterial surfaces.
- GtfC (GtfSI): produces mixture of soluble (α-1,6) and insoluble glucan; primary pellicle-adsorbed enzyme.
- GtfD (GtfS): produces predominantly soluble glucan; serves as primer for GtfB; few sHA binding sites.
- sHA: saliva-coated hydroxyapatite; standard in vitro model for tooth pellicle.
- Mutanase: α-1,3 glucanase that degrades insoluble glucan (mutan); dismantles EPS matrix.
- Dextranase: α-1,6 glucanase degrading soluble dextran-type glucan.
- Scatchard plot: binding analysis method used to determine enzyme binding affinity and number of binding sites.
