## Why Ingested

치과 항생제 부적절 처방률(50–80%)과 항균제 내성(AMR) 문제가 반복적으로 등장하는 맥락에서, 기존 wiki에는 처방 현황 분석(sbricoli-2024, sutej-2024, mendez-millan-2024)만 있고 **중재 효과를 메타분석한 SR**이 없었음. 이 SR+MA(n=23 studies, k=10 in MA)는 감사(audit)·교육·디지털 도구가 부적절 처방을 70% 감소시킬 수 있다는 최초의 정량적 근거를 제공함. [[drug/mendez-millan-2024-antibiotic-overprescription-apical-periodontitis-sr-ma]]의 처방 현황 SR을 중재 효과 측면에서 확장.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 23 studies (1997–2023) finds that stewardship interventions — particularly audit+feedback and audit+education — reduce inappropriate antibiotic prescribing among dentists by 70% (95% CI 33.3%–86.4%), though evidence quality is low due to predominance of pre-post designs without control groups.

## 한줄요약

항생제 처방 중재 SR+MA (23개 연구, 1997–2023): 감사·교육·피드백 기반 항생제 청지기 프로그램 (Antibiotic Stewardship Program, ASP)이 치과의사의 부적절 처방을 70% 감소시키나, 대부분 대조군 없는 사전-사후 설계로 근거 수준은 낮음.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First meta-analysis** quantifying effectiveness of interventions to improve antibiotic prescribing specifically among dentists.
2. Established that audit+feedback (75% reduction, 95% CI 33%–91.4%) and audit+education (73.3%, 95% CI 44%–87.4%) are the most effective stewardship strategies.
3. Overall 70% reduction in inappropriate prescribing (95% CI 33.3%–86.4%) across all intervention types.
4. RCT-specific subgroup (n=2 RCTs): 63.9% reduction (95% CI 41%–78.1%, I²=0%) — lower heterogeneity but very few studies.
5. Identified major evidence gap: no high-quality RCTs from Latin America or other regions with high inappropriate prescribing rates.
6. Highlighted emerging interventions (e-health decision support, AI, nudges) as under-researched.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Design**: PRISMA-compliant SR+MA. Protocol registered in PROSPERO (CRD42023474664).

**Eligibility**: Original epidemiological studies on interventions to optimize antibiotic prescription by dentists. Excluded: non-dentists, primary care/hospital settings, reviews, conference abstracts.

**Meta-analysis inclusion**: Required reporting of 'appropriate prescriptions' pre- and post-intervention.

**Definition of appropriate prescription**: Compliance with clinical guidelines — correct antibiotic selection, dosage, duration, and consideration of patient allergies/comorbidities.

**Risk of bias tool**: The Evidence Project (applicable to RCT and non-RCT designs). Two independent reviewers; discordance resolved by third reviewer.

**Statistical approach**:
- Random effects model
- I² for heterogeneity
- Results expressed as relative risk reduction (analogous to vaccine efficacy %)
- Sensitivity analyses stratified by intervention type and study design

**Intervention categories identified**:
1. Audit + education (clinical audit with guideline feedback and educational component)
2. Audit + feedback (performance data provided to practitioners)
3. Online/virtual learning environments
4. Pharmacist visits / face-to-face expert feedback
5. Online prescribing decision-support tools
6. Multimodal (combined strategies)
7. Government/regulatory strategies
8. C-reactive protein-guided prescribing

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Overall meta-analysis (10 studies):**
| Subgroup | Reduction | 95% CI | I² |
|---|---|---|---|
| All interventions | 70% | 33.3%–86.4% | High |
| Pre-post designs (n=8) | 71% | 28.8%–88.1% | 99.2% |
| RCTs only (n=2) | 63.9% | 41%–78.1% | 0% |
| Audit + education | 73.3% | 44%–87.4% | 69% |
| Audit + feedback | 75.0% | 33%–91.4% | 99% |

**Key exemplar studies:**
- Steed & Gibson 1997 (UK): Consensus intervention → 50% prescription reduction
- Palmer 2001 (UK): Audit+feedback → appropriate prescriptions 57.4% → 70.5%
- Seager 2005 (UK, RCT): Pharmacist visits → 37% → 67% appropriate (guidelines alone ineffective)
- Goff 2022 (USA): Expert stewardship education+audit → 19% → 88% appropriate prescriptions
- Gross 2019 (USA): Multimodal intervention → 72% decrease in urgent-care antibiotic prescribing
- Kim 2017 (Korea): Prescribing guidelines + removal of prescription button → behavioral change and reduced rates
- Teoh 2020 (Australia): Education + online prescribing tool → 44.6% prescription reduction

**Background rates**: Dentists prescribe ~10% of all antibiotics; inappropriate prescribing estimated 50–80%.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

**Study-level limitations:**
- 20/23 studies are pre-post without control groups → regression to mean, confounding, Hawthorne effect
- Only 2 RCTs included in meta-analysis
- High heterogeneity in pre-post subgroup (I²=99.2%)
- Most studies from UK/Europe — limited global generalizability
- Absence of studies from Latin America and other high-prevalence inappropriate-prescribing regions
- Publication bias: positive/significant results overrepresented
- Cannot explore effect modifiers (years of training, practice type) — information not reported in source studies

**Future directions:**
- High-quality RCTs with parallel control groups
- Studies from underrepresented regions (Latin America, Asia outside Korea/Japan)
- Evaluation of 'nudge' interventions (commitment posters, defaults)
- e-Health decision support systems and AI-based prescribing tools
- Long-term follow-up (most interventions show reversal when discontinued)
- Direct comparison of active vs. passive feedback delivery

## 7. Glossary

- **항생제 청지기 활동 (Antibiotic Stewardship Program, ASP)**: Programs to optimize antibiotic use and combat antimicrobial resistance
- **항균제 내성 (Antimicrobial Resistance, AMR)**: Resistance of microorganisms to antimicrobial drugs, a global public health priority
- **감사+피드백 (Audit and Feedback, A&F)**: Intervention providing practitioners with data on their prescribing performance compared to standards
- **사전-사후 설계 (Pre-post design)**: Before-after study without concurrent control group; susceptible to regression to mean and Hawthorne effect
- **호손 효과 (Hawthorne effect)**: Behavior change due to awareness of being observed, independent of the intervention
- **부적절 처방 (Inappropriate prescription)**: Prescribing that does not comply with evidence-based guidelines — wrong antibiotic, dose, duration, or indication
- **무작위 효과 모델 (Random effects model)**: Statistical model accounting for between-study heterogeneity in meta-analysis
- **I² 통계량**: Measure of statistical heterogeneity in meta-analysis (high ≥75%, very high ≥90%)
- **넛지 (Nudge)**: Subtle behavioral intervention (e.g., commitment posters) that influences decision-making without restricting choices
