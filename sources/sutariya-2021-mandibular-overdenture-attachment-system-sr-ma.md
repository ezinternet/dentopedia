## One-line Summary
PRISMA SR+MA of mandibular implant overdenture attachment systems (6 RCTs/crossover trials, 2010–2020, all low risk of bias): ball attachments showed significantly fewer prosthodontic complications than locator (RR 0.55, 95% CI; p=0.03); peri-implant probing depth and marginal bone loss showed no significant difference among ball, magnet, and locator; retention and patient satisfaction reviewed qualitatively due to limited data.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Indian Prosthodontic Society 2021;21(4):319–327
- **DOI**: 10.4103/jips.jips_207_21
- **Institution**: College of Dental Sciences and Research Centre, Ahmedabad, India
- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (PRISMA)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Quantitatively confirms that **ball attachment has fewer prosthodontic complications and maintenance events than locator** in mandibular OD over ≥1-year follow-up.
- Confirms no meaningful peri-implant tissue difference (probing depth, MBL) between ball, magnet, and locator under standard care.
- Sets the stage for the larger, low-RoB SR+MA by Patel 2025, which broadened the comparison set.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Databases: PubMed, Cochrane Central, ScienceDirect; manual + reference-list search.
- Inclusion: RCTs and crossover clinical trials 2010–2020, >1-year follow-up.
- Cochrane Collaboration RoB tool — all 6 retained studies = low risk of bias.
- Statistical analysis: RevMan 5.4 (random- or fixed-effects per heterogeneity).
- Outcomes: prosthodontic maintenance & complications; peri-implant tissue; retention; patient satisfaction.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Ball vs Locator (prosthodontic complications & maintenance)**: ball significantly fewer (RR 0.55, 95% CI, p=0.03).
- **Ball vs Magnet (prosthodontic complications)**: statistically significant difference favoring ball.
- **Peri-implant tissue (probing depth, MBL)**: no significant difference among ball / magnet / locator.
- **Retention**: ball and locator both deliver clinically acceptable retention; magnet retention lowest.
- **Patient satisfaction**: equivalent for ball / locator; data too limited to meta-analyze formally.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Only 6 RCTs/crossover trials reached final inclusion — narrow evidence base.
- Many comparator combinations remain underpowered (telescopic, bar excluded by inclusion criteria here).
- Heterogeneous follow-up periods (1–3 yr).

## 6. Related Work
- Patel 2025 (Cureus): 14-study SR+MA on same family of questions, broader comparator set.
- Aung 2025: mini-implant OD attachment SR+MA.
- Tayari 2024: failure-risk SR for implant-supported OD.

## 7. Glossary
- **Prosthodontic complications**: matrix/insert change, denture fracture, attachment loosening, reline.
- **Probing depth**: distance from peri-implant mucosa margin to base of sulcus.
- **MBL**: marginal bone loss measured radiographically from implant shoulder.
