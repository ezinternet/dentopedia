## One-line Summary

SR+MA (17 studies, 10 meta-analyzed; RCTs) of implants placed with transcrestal sinus lift reporting a pooled 100% (95% CI 99–100%) implant survival, with follow-up time non-significant in meta-regression, but a hand-osteotome–specific risk of membrane perforation and patient vertigo.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Pools implant survival specifically for transcrestal (closed) maxillary sinus elevation across 17 RCTs → 100% (95% CI 99–100%) for both grafted and non-grafted hand-osteotome lifts.
- Meta-regression: follow-up time does not significantly affect implant survival.
- Subgroup analyses: no significant difference between bone-level vs tissue-level implants, nor one-stage vs two-stage placement.
- Flags a technique-dependent safety caveat: only hand-osteotome studies reported subsequent vertigo/dizziness, alongside a higher rate of sinus-lining perforation.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Databases: PubMed, Web of Science, Embase, Scopus searched to 21 Feb 2024.
- Inclusion: randomised clinical trials using transcrestal sinus lifts.
- 1807 records → 17 studies included, 10 entered meta-analysis.
- Elevation methods across studies: hand osteotomes, piezoelectric + hand osteotome combinations, drills, "smart lift" hydraulic systems.
- Random-effects model pooling survival of hand-osteotome transcrestal lifts (grafted and non-grafted), with meta-regression and subgroup analyses; publication bias via funnel plots and Egger's regression; α = 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Primary: pooled implant survival 100% (95% CI 99–100%), grafted and non-grafted hand-osteotome transcrestal lifts.
- Meta-regression: follow-up duration not a significant moderator of survival.
- Subgroups: bone-level vs tissue-level — no significant difference; one-stage vs two-stage — no significant difference.
- Safety signal: vertigo/dizziness reported only in hand-osteotome studies; higher sinus-lining perforation rate noted with hand osteotomes.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only ingest — pooled CIs, heterogeneity (I²), and per-study weights not extractable here; full text needed before quoting forest-plot detail.
- Survival ceiling (100%) reflects short-to-mid-term RCT populations and likely selection toward favourable cases; absolute survival has limited discriminative value between techniques.
- Heterogeneous elevation methods lumped under "transcrestal"; the hand-osteotome perforation/vertigo caveat is the clinically actionable signal.
- Survival is a coarse endpoint — endo-sinus bone gain, ISQ trajectory, and PROMs (covered by sibling syntheses) better discriminate techniques.

## 7. Glossary

- **Transcrestal (crestal/closed) sinus floor elevation**: sinus membrane lifted through the implant osteotomy via the alveolar crest, without a lateral bony window.
- **Hand osteotome**: manual malleting instrument (Summers technique) used to in-fracture and elevate the sinus floor.
- **Smart lift / hydraulic lift**: device-mediated atraumatic membrane elevation using fluid pressure.
- **Meta-regression**: regression of effect size on a study-level covariate (here, follow-up time) to test moderation.
