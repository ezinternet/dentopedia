## One-line Summary
Retrospective CBCT study of 349 edentulous posterior maxillary sites from 250 patients: 55.45% of molar and 54.42% of premolar sites had horizontal ridge width < 6 mm, 67.83% of molar and 44.86% of premolar sites had apico-coronal bone height < 8 mm, and 54.14% of all sites had oblique sinus-floor morphology — supporting routine CBCT pre-implant planning and a high need for sinus floor augmentation in this population.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Maxillofacial and Oral Surgery 2020;19(1):125–130

- **Affiliation**: Ceramco Dental Care, Mumbai; Clarus Dental Specialties, Pune; UNC Chapel Hill (adjunct)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Quantifies the **proportion of posterior maxillary edentulous sites that cannot accept a standard implant** without augmentation in an Indian patient cohort.
- Reports **sinus-floor obliqueness** as an additional anatomic variable beyond bone height/width — relevant for transcrestal vs lateral-window decision-making.
- Provides population-level prevalence data to support **routine pre-implant CBCT** in the posterior maxilla.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Sample**: 349 edentulous sites from 250 CBCT scans of patients referred for implant therapy.
- **Measurements**:
  - **Apico-coronal bone height** (vertical distance from alveolar crest to sinus floor) — sagittal slices.
  - **Bucco-palatal crest width** (horizontal ridge dimension) — coronal slices.
  - **Sinus-floor obliqueness** (flat vs oblique morphology) — assessed qualitatively from coronal slices.
- **Stratification**: by site type (premolar vs molar).
- **Cutoffs of clinical relevance**: horizontal < 6 mm (excludes standard-diameter implants without augmentation); vertical < 8 mm (excludes standard-length implants without sinus floor elevation).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Horizontal ridge width < 6 mm**:
  - Molar sites: 112/202 (**55.45%**).
  - Premolar sites: 74/136 (**54.42%**).
- **Apico-coronal height < 8 mm**:
  - Molar sites: 137/202 (**67.83%**).
  - Premolar sites: 61/136 (**44.86%**).
- **Oblique sinus floor**: 183/338 (**54.14%**) of all evaluated sites — increases SM perforation risk during lateral approach.
- **Implication**: more than half of edentulous posterior maxillary sites in this cohort require augmentation (sinus elevation, GBR, or both) for standard implant placement.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Single-center, Indian referral-population cohort — results may not generalize to other populations; selection bias toward patients already requiring implant evaluation.
- No longitudinal follow-up linking these anatomic findings to implant outcomes.
- Sinus-floor obliqueness classification was qualitative (no angle measurement protocol provided).
- No analysis of time since extraction, periodontal etiology, or systemic factors driving bone loss.
- Did not stratify by sinus septa, lateral wall thickness, or membrane thickness — known SM perforation risk factors.

## 7. Glossary
- **CBCT (Cone-Beam Computed Tomography)**: low-dose 3D dental imaging; standard for implant planning.
- **Edentulous posterior maxilla**: tooth-loss region distal to canines in the upper jaw; prone to ridge resorption + sinus pneumatization.
- **Apico-coronal height**: vertical bone available from crest to sinus floor.
- **Bucco-palatal width**: horizontal ridge dimension at the crest.
- **Oblique sinus floor**: sinus floor that is not parallel to the alveolar crest; raises SM perforation risk in lateral SFA.
- **Standard implant**: typically ≥ 3.5–4 mm diameter, ≥ 8–10 mm length; benchmarks against which the < 6 mm width and < 8 mm height cutoffs are defined.
