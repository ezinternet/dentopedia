## Why Ingested

BMS를 말초/중추 신경병성 통증으로 보는 [[oral-medicine/canfora-2025-burning-mouth-syndrome-pathogenesis-review]] 및 [[oral-medicine/jaaskelainen-2018-burning-mouth-syndrome-neuropathic-pain]]의 "탈억제(disinhibition)" 기전을 분자 수준에서 뒷받침하는 기초 substrate. GABA/glycine 억제가 NKCC1/KCC2 음이온 수송체에 의존하는 Cl⁻ 기울기에 좌우된다는 원리는 BMS topical clonazepam(GABA-A 작용제) 반응성과 직접 연결된다.

## One-line Summary

Narrative review of how cation-chloride cotransporters (NKCC1, KCC2) set the anion equilibrium potential that governs GABA-A/glycine-mediated synaptic inhibition, and how their dysregulation produces disinhibition-driven hyperalgesia and allodynia after inflammation or nerve injury.

## 한줄요약

내러티브 리뷰 — NKCC1·KCC2 음이온 수송체가 결정하는 Cl⁻ 평형전위가 GABA-A·glycine 억제의 방향과 세기를 좌우하며, 이 조절이 깨지면(탈억제) 염증·신경손상 후 통각과민·이질통이 생긴다는 기전을 종합.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Reframes the Gate Control Theory's "dynamic spinal inhibition" in modern molecular terms: inhibition is regulated not only by transmitter/receptor availability but by the transmembrane Cl⁻ gradient itself.
- Positions the cation-chloride cotransporters NKCC1 (Cl⁻ accumulating) and KCC2 (Cl⁻ extruding) as the central control point of the anion equilibrium potential (E_anion / E_Cl).
- Links downregulation of KCC2 (and/or upregulation of NKCC1) to the collapse of GABAergic inhibition and the emergence of chronic pain states — the same disinhibition logic implicated in epilepsy.
- Discusses therapeutic implications: modulating intracellular Cl⁻ gradients or targeting GABA receptors to restore inhibition.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Narrative/mechanistic review synthesizing electrophysiology, molecular biology, and behavioral pain pharmacology literature.
- Covers presynaptic and postsynaptic mechanisms of inhibition in the superficial dorsal horn; primary afferent depolarization; and the role of Cl⁻ homeostasis at the first central synapse.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- No quantitative meta-analytic outcome (narrative review).
- Core qualitative conclusion: pharmacologic block or genetic reduction of spinal inhibition reproduces hyperalgesia/allodynia; conversely, enhancing inhibition reduces them — consistent with Gate Control Theory predictions.
- Identifies Cl⁻ co-transporter dysregulation as a key pathophysiological substrate of disinhibition in chronic pain.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Predominantly spinal-cord–centric; peripheral primary-afferent Cl⁻ homeostasis is comparatively underdeveloped here (later addressed by [[orofacial-pain/wilke-2020-chloride-underrated-ion-nociceptors]]).
- 2009 vintage — predates much of the anoctamin/TMEM16 and CFTR nociceptor literature.
- Mechanistic, not clinical; no direct orofacial-pain or BMS data.

## 6. Related Work

- Melzack & Wall Gate Control Theory of Pain (1965) — theoretical anchor.
- Coull et al. (2003, 2005) on KCC2 downregulation and disinhibition after nerve injury (the foundational disinhibition papers this review synthesizes).

## 7. Glossary

- E_anion / E_Cl: anion (chloride) equilibrium/reversal potential — determines whether GABA-A/glycine receptor activation hyperpolarizes (inhibits) or depolarizes (can excite) the neuron.
- NKCC1: Na⁺-K⁺-2Cl⁻ cotransporter 1 — accumulates intracellular Cl⁻.
- KCC2: K⁺-Cl⁻ cotransporter 2 — extrudes Cl⁻; its downregulation is a hallmark of pathological disinhibition.
- Disinhibition: loss of inhibitory tone (here via shifted Cl⁻ gradient) producing net hyperexcitability.
