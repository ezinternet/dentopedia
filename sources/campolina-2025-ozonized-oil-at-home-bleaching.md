## Why Ingested

Ozone therapy is an emerging adjunct in dentistry (anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, analgesic) and this is the first RCT testing ozonized oil as a desensitizer during at-home bleaching — directly addresses the sensitivity limitation raised in [[tooth-whitening/terra-2025-at-home-bleaching-concentration-tooth-sensitivity]]. Negative result with important clinical message: ozonized oil does not reduce bleaching-induced sensitivity and has poor patient acceptance.

## One-line Summary

Double-blind RCT (n=40) found ozonized sunflower oil applied after 10% HP at-home bleaching did not reduce tooth sensitivity (p=0.536) or improve color change; patients reported unpleasant taste and odor.

## 한줄요약

이중맹검 RCT(n=40)에서 오존화 해바라기유는 10% HP 재가 미백 시 치아 민감도를 감소시키지 못했고(p=0.536) 불쾌한 맛·냄새로 환자 수용도가 낮았다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First double-blind RCT evaluating ozonized sunflower oil as a post-bleaching desensitizing agent
- Null result for sensitivity reduction — ozonized oil conferred no benefit over non-ozonized oil
- Null result for whitening enhancement — bleaching outcomes equivalent between groups
- Negative patient perception: significantly worse taste (p=0.002) and odor (p=0.010) with ozonized oil
- Establishes that ozone in oil form lacks the analgesic effect seen with ozone gas in earlier in-office studies

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Participants**: 40 adults randomized to ozonized sunflower oil (n=20) or non-ozonized sunflower oil placebo (n=20)
- **Bleaching protocol**: 10% hydrogen peroxide, 30 min/day for 21 days (at-home trays)
- **Intervention**: Oil applied in trays for 1 min after each bleaching session
- **Outcomes**:
  - Tooth sensitivity: daily VAS (0–10) and NRS (0–4 scale) for 21 days
  - Color change: spectrophotometer (ΔEab, ΔE00, ΔWID) at baseline, 1 week, 1 month; visual shade guide (ΔSGU)
  - Patient perception: Likert-scale questionnaire (taste, odor, handling, satisfaction, gum irritation)
- **Blinding**: Double-blind (patient + assessor)
- **Analysis**: Mann-Whitney test; significance level α = 0.05

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Ozonized Oil | Non-Ozonized (Placebo) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| TS risk (any episode) | 72.5% overall | — | — |
| TS intensity (VAS) | Similar | Similar | 0.536 |
| TS duration (days) | Similar | Similar | 0.256 |
| ΔEab at 1 week | No diff | No diff | > 0.05 |
| ΔEab at 1 month | Decreased vs 1 week | Decreased vs 1 week | 0.010 (both groups) |
| ΔE00 at 1 month | Decreased vs 1 week | — | 0.026 |
| Patient taste | Unpleasant | Neutral | 0.002 |
| Patient odor | Unpleasant | Neutral | 0.010 |
| Patient satisfaction | No difference | — | NS |

- Both groups experienced similar whitening outcomes; ozonized oil added no efficacy benefit
- Color relapse at 1 month (ΔEab decrease) was equivalent in both groups — consistent with expected color rebound
- The anti-inflammatory/analgesic effect of ozone gas does not translate when delivered via ozonized oil vehicle

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Relatively small n=40 (20/group); may be underpowered for small effect sizes
- Oil was applied for only 1 min after bleaching — longer application time may yield different results
- Ozone stability in oil form at room temperature (vs refrigerated) not controlled
- Ozonized oil concentration not explicitly reported
- Future work: higher ozone concentration, longer application time, different application timing

## 7. Glossary

- **Ozonized oil**: Vegetable oil (sunflower) reacted with ozone to form ozonides and peroxides; stable for up to 2 years refrigerated
- **ΔEab**: CIELab color difference (Euclidean distance in L*a*b* space); perceptibility threshold ΔEab = 1.2
- **ΔE00**: CIEDE2000 color difference formula; more perceptually uniform than ΔEab; threshold ΔE00 = 0.8
- **ΔWID**: Whiteness Index for Dentistry; formula: 0.511L* − 2.3424a* − 1.100b*
- **NRS**: Numerical Rating Scale (0=no sensitivity, 4=severe); binary threshold at NRS > 0
