## Why Ingested

Recent (2024) re-evaluation of the long-held wet/moist-bonding paradigm for the etch-and-rinse (ER) strategy — a clinically actionable test of whether dentin moisture state actually changes outcomes. Reinforces and broadens [[wiki/resin-bonding/mortazavi-2012-ethanol-wet-bonding-clinical-trial]] (single RCT, n=36 NCCLs, 12 mo, no significant dry-vs-wet difference) by pooling five split-mouth RCTs (n=195, up to 5 y) and finding the same null result with GRADE-rated moderate certainty.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 5 split-mouth RCTs (195 patients, NCCLs, up to 5-y follow-up): dry- vs wet-bonding under the etch-and-rinse strategy showed no significant difference in retention or postoperative sensitivity (moderate certainty, GRADE).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Pools the clinical (not in-vitro) evidence on whether **dentin moisture state** (dry vs moist/wet) affects outcomes of composite restorations placed with the etch-and-rinse (ER) strategy.
- Answers the focused question: *do retention rates and secondary outcomes of NCCL composite restorations differ between dry- and wet-bonding techniques?*
- Finds **no significant difference** between dry and wet bonding at any recall, with **moderate certainty** by GRADE — challenging the textbook insistence that ER dentin must be kept visibly moist.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Systematic review with meta-analysis of randomized clinical trials.
- **Population/intervention**: NCCLs restored with ER or universal adhesives applied to **dry vs moist dentin**.
- **Databases**: MEDLINE/PubMed, Web of Science, Cochrane Library, Brazilian Library in Dentistry (BBO), LILACS, Scopus, Embase, plus grey literature — no language or date restrictions; updated May 2024.
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane RoB 2.0.
- **Synthesis**: Random-effects meta-analysis; heterogeneity assessed; certainty of evidence by GRADE.
- **Included studies**: 5 split-mouth RCTs, all from Brazil, published 2005–2023; 195 adult patients; follow-up up to 5 years. RoB: 3 low, 1 some concerns, 1 high.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Retention rate, risk ratio (95% CI)** — no significant difference at any recall:
  - 18–24 months: RR 0.91 (0.45 to 1.84)
  - 36 months: RR 0.82 (0.38 to 1.78)
  - 60 months: RR 1.00 (0.42 to 2.38)
- **Postoperative sensitivity, risk difference (95% CI)**: 0.00 (−0.03 to 0.03) at all follow-ups.
- **Certainty of evidence (GRADE)**: moderate.
- **Bottom line**: retention and secondary outcomes were **not affected by dentin moisture** under the ER strategy.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- All 5 included trials originate from Brazil (single-country evidence base; limited generalizability).
- One trial had high RoB and one had some concerns, capping certainty at moderate.
- Restricted to NCCLs — does not address other cavity classes or substrate conditions.
- Wide confidence intervals (small pooled n=195) cannot exclude modest effects.
- Abstract-only ingest: full-text methods/forest plots not reviewed.

## 7. Glossary

- **Etch-and-rinse (ER)**: adhesive strategy where phosphoric acid is applied, rinsed off, then primer/adhesive infiltrates the demineralized dentin.
- **Wet/moist bonding**: keeping demineralized dentin visibly moist so the exposed collagen network stays expanded for adhesive infiltration (vs over-drying that collapses collagen).
- **Dry bonding**: air-drying the etched dentin before adhesive application.
- **NCCL**: non-carious cervical lesion — common clinical model for adhesive retention trials.
- **Split-mouth RCT**: each patient receives both interventions on contralateral teeth, controlling for patient-level confounders.
- **GRADE**: framework rating the certainty (high/moderate/low/very low) of a body of evidence.
- **RoB 2.0**: Cochrane Risk of Bias tool, version 2, for randomized trials.
