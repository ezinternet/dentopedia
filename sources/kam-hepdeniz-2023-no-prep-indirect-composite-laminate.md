## Why Ingested

기존 [[wiki/veneers/lim-2023-resin-composite-laminate-veneer-survival-sr-ma]]의 SR+MA가 간접 복합레진 비니어 생존율을 84%로 보고하였으나 no-prep 기법 특화 장기 임상 데이터(7년)는 포함되지 않았음. 본 전향적 증례 연구(Kam Hepdeniz 2023)는 무삭제 간접 복합레진 라미네이트 비니어의 84개월 생존율 91.3%를 보고하여 해당 SR+MA의 간접법 수치(84%)보다 높은 성과를 제시하며 enamel-bonded no-prep 전략의 근거로 활용.

## One-line Summary

Prospective case series (n=80 veneers, 35 patients, 84 months): no-prep indirect microhybrid composite laminate veneers on maxillary anterior teeth achieved 91.3% survival rate (Kaplan-Meier), with debonding (n=4) and fracture (n=3) as the only absolute failures; no secondary caries, antagonist wear, or tooth fracture observed.

## 한줄요약

전향적 증례 연구(n=80 비니어, 35명, 84개월): 무삭제 간접 마이크로하이브리드 복합레진 라미네이트 비니어(라미네이트 비니어, laminate veneer)의 생존율 91.3%(Kaplan-Meier); 실패는 탈락(디본딩, debonding) 4개·파절 3개에 한정, 이차 우식·대합치 마모·치아파절 없음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health 2023; 23:257
- **DOI**: 10.1186/s12903-023-02949-5
- **Study design**: Prospective case series, single operator, 7-year clinical follow-up
- **Ethics approval**: Local ethics committee approval 2014/420; informed consent obtained
- **Sample**: 80 no-prep indirect composite laminate veneers on maxillary anterior teeth in 35 patients (17 women, 18 men; age 19–45 years)
- **Indications**: Diastema (n=64), wedge tooth anomalies (n=9), re-shaping (n=7)
- **Material**: Indirect microhybrid composite Gradia (GC Dental), fabricated by one technician
- **Cement**: Light-cured resin cement Choice 2 (Bisco); adhesive All-Bond 3 (Bisco)
- **Evaluation intervals**: Baseline, 24 months, 84 months (7 years)
- **Evaluation criteria**: Modified USPHS criteria (7 categories: marginal adaptation, color match, marginal discoloration, surface roughness, fracture of restoration, fracture of tooth, wear)
- **Statistics**: Kaplan-Meier survival analysis; Wilcoxon Signed Ranks test (p<0.05)

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Longest prospective case series of no-prep indirect composite veneers**: 84 months follow-up provides rare long-term clinical evidence for this preparation-free technique.
2. **91.3% survival superior to prepared composite veneers**: Historical comparisons report 87% at 3 years and 75% at 10 years for composite veneers with preparation; the no-prep enamel-bonded approach outperformed these benchmarks.
3. **No tooth structure compromise**: All margins on intact enamel; no postoperative sensitivity, secondary caries, antagonist wear, or tooth fracture observed at any recall.
4. **Key failure modes defined**: 4 debondings (marginal adaptation score 4) and 3 fractures (fracture of restoration score 3); all fractures occurred after patient-reported biting hard food.
5. **Surface deterioration is polishable**: Marginal discolorations (n=15 at 84 months) resolved with polishing; no irreversible staining noted.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Patient selection**:
- Inclusion: Good general health, no allergy to resin-based materials, no active periodontal/pulpal disease, available for follow-up
- Exclusion: Bruxism/parafunctional habits, severe malocclusion, anterior crossbite, severe discoloration, hypoplasia, severe tetracycline staining, large cervical defects

**Restorative procedure (no preparation)**:
1. Shade matching (Vita Classic/Demetron shade light)
2. Polyvinyl siloxane impression (double-mix, Elite HD+)
3. Type IV die stone model (GC Fujirock); 12 micron die spacer applied
4. Diagnostic wax-up; silicone matrix
5. Gradia indirect composite fabrication via polychromatic layering + polymerization oven (GC)
6. Clinical try-in: marginal/gingival adaptation, contacts, shape, color verified
7. Rubber dam isolation; 32% phosphoric acid etch 15 s → rinse → air dry
8. All-Bond 3 (A&B 1:1) 1-2 coats to enamel → air dry 10 s → light-cure 10 s
9. All-Bond 3 to internal veneer surface (not cured); Choice 2 cement applied
10. Veneer seated, 3-5 s incisal stabilization light-cure, excess removed
11. Full light-cure 40 s (buccal/incisal/mesial/distal); radiometer verification after every 5 patients

**Evaluation**: Two independent calibrated clinicians using modified USPHS 7-category criteria; disagreements resolved by consensus.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Overall survival rate (Kaplan-Meier) | 91.3% |
| Mean retention time | 78.325 months |
| Total failures | 7/80 veneers |
| Debonding failures | 4 (2 patients; 11 months and 23 months after cementation) |
| Fracture failures | 3 (10, 21, 35 months after luting; all after hard food) |
| Veneers at 24 months | 74 |
| Veneers at 84 months | 73 (33 patients) |

**USPHS criteria at 84 months (n=73)**:
- Marginal adaptation: 66 score-0, 7 score-1, 0 score-2/3, 4 score-4 (debonded)
- Color match: 24 score-0, 34 score-1, 15 score-2 (significant p=0.000)
- Marginal discoloration: 58 score-0, 15 score-1 (polishable; p=0.000)
- Surface roughness: 32 score-0, 41 score-1 (significant p=0.000)
- Fracture of restoration: 63 score-0, 10 score-2 (minor chippings), 3 score-3
- Fracture of tooth: 73 score-0 (zero tooth fractures)
- Wear, secondary caries, postoperative sensitivity: all score-0 (100%)

**Significant deterioration vs baseline**: marginal adaptation (p=0.008), color match, marginal discoloration, surface roughness, fracture of restoration (all p<0.001 or p=0.001).
**Not significant between 24→84 months**: marginal adaptation (p=0.165).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single operator, single center, single technician → limits generalizability
- No control group (no comparison to prepared or ceramic veneers in same study)
- Convenience sample; attrition (80→73 veneers over 84 months)
- Composite material (Gradia, 2014 era) may not represent current generation composites
- No objective color measurement (spectrophotometry); USPHS criteria are observer-dependent
- No radiographic analysis of marginal bone or periodontal status
- Longer follow-up (10+ years) needed to match ceramic veneer comparison data
- Periodontal complications from over-contouring could not be ruled out given no-prep design, though not observed

## 6. Related Work

- Morimoto et al. (ceramic/porcelain veneer SR+MA) — referenced as gold standard benchmark; enamel bonding reduces failure
- D'Arcangelo et al. — noted challenges of thin feldspathic no-prep veneers (brittle fracture)
- Lim et al. SR+MA — reported 84% survival for indirect composite veneers (this study's no-prep approach exceeded this)
- Previous studies of composite veneers with preparation: 87% at 3 years, 75% at 10 years
- No-prep porcelain veneer study — 97.4% survival rate after 36–60 months
- 2-year study: no-prep vs minimal-prep porcelain veneers both reached 100% survival

## 7. Glossary

- **No-prep veneer (무삭제 비니어)**: Laminate veneer placed without any enamel reduction; reversible, conservative, enamel-bonded
- **Indirect composite veneer (간접 복합레진 비니어)**: Veneer fabricated outside the mouth in a laboratory using composite resin, then cemented adhesively
- **Gradia (GC)**: Indirect microhybrid composite material used for fabrication
- **Kaplan-Meier survival analysis (생존 분석)**: Statistical method accounting for censored data to estimate time-to-failure probability
- **Modified USPHS criteria (수정 USPHS 기준)**: Standardized clinical evaluation scoring for restoration quality (marginal adaptation, color, surface, fracture, etc.)
- **Choice 2 (Bisco)**: Light-cured resin cement used for veneer luting
- **All-Bond 3 (Bisco)**: Two-component adhesive resin used on both tooth enamel and veneer internal surface
- **Diastema (정중이개)**: Gap between anterior teeth; primary indication (80%) in this study
- **Debonding (탈락/디본딩)**: Failure mode where veneer detaches from tooth surface
- **USPHS criteria score 4 (marginal adaptation)**: Veneer debonded from both ends — absolute failure
