## Why Ingested

Healing-abutment batch — where [[wiki/implants/ruhstorfer-2024-customized-vs-conventional-healing-abutments-sr]] asks whether abutment *shape/customization* governs soft-tissue outcomes, this paper isolates the orthogonal variable: does abutment *surface* (machined vs anodized/laser/other modifications) drive peri-implant soft-tissue attachment, inflammation, and maintenance? Reinforces the sibling by showing that surface — not only macro-geometry — is a candidate determinant, with a meta-analytic answer (no short-term difference; contradictory long-term).

## One-line Summary

Systematic review + meta-analysis (10 studies; 6 pooled — 4 RCT/2 CCT, 118 patients, 182 implants): modified titanium abutment surfaces show no significant short-term difference vs controls in plaque index, bleeding on probing, or probing depth; long-term (5–6 y) studies report contradictory results depending on surface technique.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰 + 메타분석 (10편 포함, 6편 풀링 — RCT 4·CCT 2, 환자 118명·임플란트 182개): 변형된 티타늄 어버트먼트 표면은 단기적으로 플라크 지수·탐침 시 출혈(BoP)·탐침 깊이(PD)에서 대조군과 유의한 차이가 없었고, 장기(5~6년) 연구는 표면 처리 기법에 따라 상반된 결과를 보였다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Frames abutment **surface** (not shape) as the variable of interest for peri-implant soft-tissue healing, inflammation, and maintenance.
- Meta-analyzes modified vs control titanium healing-abutment surfaces on short-term soft-tissue inflammation outcomes (PI, BoP, PD).
- Reports a null short-term effect with formal random-effects pooling, and separately flags contradictory long-term (5–6 y) evidence.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Electronic database search through **30 April 2019**.
- Random-effects meta-analysis (DerSimonian–Laird estimator) per outcome parameter.
- **10 studies** included in the review.
- **6 studies pooled** for short-follow-up MA: **4 RCT + 2 CCT**, **118 patients**, **182 implants**, modified healing-abutment surface.
- **4 studies** with long follow-up (5–6 y) reported outcomes too heterogeneously / contradictorily for pooling.
- Mixed-effects models tested moderators: type of study, study design, number of implants, follow-up length.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Plaque index (PI)**: no significant difference between surfaces, **P = 0.091**.
- **Bleeding on probing (BoP)**: no significant difference, **P = 0.099**.
- **Probing depth (PD)**: no significant difference, **P = 0.488**.
- **Moderator analysis**: no statistical significance for any mixed-effects model moderator (study type, design, implant number, follow-up length).
- **Long-term (5–6 y, 4 studies)**: contradictory results depending on the surface-modification technique investigated.
- **Bottom line**: peri-implant soft tissue may not be affected by titanium abutment surface treatment in the short term; long-term evidence is conflicting.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only ingest — full-text methods, risk-of-bias, and forest plots not available.
- Small pooled sample (118 patients / 182 implants) and short follow-up for the MA subset.
- Long-term studies heterogeneous and contradictory; not poolable.
- Authors call for long-term validation before recommending modified titanium surfaces; clinicians should evaluate use carefully.

## 7. Glossary

- **Healing abutment**: transmucosal component placed after implant placement/uncovering to shape and maintain peri-implant soft tissue during healing.
- **PI (Plaque Index)**: clinical score of plaque accumulation around the implant.
- **BoP (Bleeding on Probing)**: presence of bleeding after gentle probing; marker of soft-tissue inflammation.
- **PD (Probing Depth)**: distance from soft-tissue margin to probe tip; peri-implant pocket measure.
- **CCT**: controlled clinical trial (non-randomized).
- **DerSimonian–Laird**: a random-effects meta-analysis estimator of between-study variance.
