## Why Ingested

The narrow (horizontally atrophic) alveolar crest is the exact "narrow ridge" scenario where bone expansion / compaction / osteodensification (osteotomes, threaded expanders, osseodensification drills) are deployed instead of staged GBR. This SR+MA (10 studies, 241 subjects) quantifies, in one place, how those techniques move the three decision-relevant numbers — bone density (BD, HU), crestal expansion (CE, mm), and implant stability quotient (ISQ) — and exposes where the evidence is fragile (high heterogeneity + publication bias for CE/ISQ). It reinforces and consolidates several single-technique pages we already hold: [[implants/chen-2022-reverse-drilling-technique-alveolar-ridge-expansion]] (reverse-drilling expansion), [[implants/tian-2019-alveolar-ridge-expansion-osseodensification-osteotome]] (OD vs osteotome expansion), [[implants/koutouzis-2019-alveolar-ridge-expansion-osseodensification-multicenter-retrospective]] (one of the 10 included studies), and [[implants/kalra-2025-implant-stability-crestal-bone-osseodensification-sr-ma]] (OD stability SR+MA). It also extends the ridge-split origin pages [[bone-regeneration/simion-1992-jawbone-enlargement-split-crest-gtr]] and [[bone-regeneration/enislidis-2006-staged-ridge-splitting-implant-mandible]] by setting expansion/densification head-to-head against crestal splitting.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (10 studies, n=241; PROSPERO CRD42025646738) — bone expansion/compaction/osteodensification in narrow (<=2.5 mm horizontal-atrophy) alveolar crests significantly favors the experimental group for BD (SMD -0.71, p=0.002), CE (SMD -1.12, p=0.04 after sensitivity test), and ISQ (SMD -8.88, p=0.0005), but with high heterogeneity and publication bias for CE and ISQ.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Pools three normally-separate technique families — alveolar ridge **expansion** (manual/motorized threaded expanders, crestal split/corticotomy), bone **compaction**, and **osteodensification (ODT, Huwais/Densah)** — under a single "narrow ridge" indication and quantifies their combined effect on BD, CE, and ISQ.
2. Provides a clean separation of evidence quality by outcome: BD is homogeneous (I²=0%) and trustworthy; CE and ISQ are heterogeneous (I²≥75% and 96%) with marked funnel-plot asymmetry (publication bias).
3. Surfaces the central contradiction in the literature — clinical SR+MA favors the experimental techniques, yet preclinical (sheep, porcine, murine) work repeatedly finds no BIC/osseointegration benefit and warns that over-densified narrow crests may suffer reduced blood supply, heat, and structural weakening (one RCT attributed 3 implant failures to this).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **PICOS**: P = adults with horizontal maxillary/mandibular ridge atrophy (dimension ≤2.5 mm); I = expansion/compaction/densification; C = vs other techniques; O = Δ BD (HU), Δ CE (mm), Δ ISQ; s = RCT, prospective, retrospective, case series.
- **Databases**: PubMed/MEDLINE, Embase, Cochrane Central, Dentistry & Oral Sciences Source, Web of Science + GreyNet (grey literature). English, last 15 years.
- **Inclusion**: >5 subjects, ≥18 y, horizontal atrophy ≤2.5 mm, means+SD reported.
- **Quality**: JBI MAStARI (≥7/13 = adequate); **Risk of bias**: Cochrane RoB2.
- **Analysis**: RevMan 5.4.1; SMD with 95% CI. Fixed-effect for homogeneous BD; random-effects for heterogeneous CE and ISQ. Sensitivity analysis applied to CE.
- **Yield**: 2,464 → 10 included (241 subjects: 5 RCTs, 2 prospective, 3 retrospective). Follow-up immediate to 6 months.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **BD**: 2 studies, I²=0%. **SMD -0.71, 95% CI [-1.15, -0.27], p=0.002** (favors experimental). Vaddamanu 2024 RCT: 821.3±485.5 → 1126.7±373.6 HU with ODT.
- **CE**: 8 studies, I²≥75%. Before sensitivity test NOT significant (0.41 [-1.02, 0.84], p=0.58). After removing Yadav, Koutouzis, Anitua & Alkhraisat (5 remain): **SMD -1.12, 95% CI [-2.21, -0.03], p=0.04** (moderate, favors experimental). Reported absolute crestal-width gains across primaries ~1.1–2.4 mm; e.g. Salman & Bede 4.04→5.3 mm, Yadav 3.64→5.62 mm (crestal split), Koutouzis mean 5.2 mm coronal, Anitua 3.1→5.1 mm.
- **ISQ**: 3 studies, I²=96%. **SMD -8.88, 95% CI [-13.85, -3.91], p=0.0005** (favors experimental). Bergamo 2021: ISQ 74±1.5 (osteodensifying drills) vs 66±1.5 (threaded osteotomes).
- **Publication bias**: none for BD (symmetric funnel); high for CE and ISQ (asymmetric funnels).
- **Head-to-head signals**: Rizk 2024 — drills > expanders for width; Tofan 2024 — no width difference but ODT faster; Ahmed 2022 — ODT higher insertion torque, shorter duration vs crestal split, 6-mo bone gain not different.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Mixed study designs (RCT + observational) inflate heterogeneity and selection/publication bias; lower-quality observational studies lacked control group, randomization, blinding (JBI 5–6).
- High publication bias specifically for the two most clinically cited outcomes (CE, ISQ) weakens those conclusions.
- Clinical benefit conflicts with preclinical findings (no BIC/stability advantage; possible structural damage/ischemia of over-densified narrow crests).
- Heterogeneous techniques, instruments, measurement methods, anatomic sites, and short follow-up (≤6 mo) — no survival endpoint. Authors call for well-designed prospective cohorts/RCTs.

## 7. Glossary

- **BD (Bone Density)**: radiographic density in Hounsfield Units (HU) on CT/CBCT.
- **CE / ARE (Crestal / Alveolar Ridge Expansion)**: gain in buccolingual ridge width (mm).
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: resonance-frequency-analysis stability index (Osstell), 1–100.
- **ODT (Osteodensification Technique)**: osseodensification with counter-clockwise burnishing drills (Huwais/Densah) that compact rather than excavate bone.
- **Threaded expander / osteotome**: manual or motorized screw/tapered device that laterally condenses bone to widen a narrow crest.
- **Crestal split / corticotomy**: longitudinal osteotomy of the crest creating a greenstick fracture to widen the ridge.
- **SMD (Standardized Mean Difference)**: effect size pooling outcomes measured on different scales (negative here favors the experimental group).
- **JBI MAStARI**: Joanna Briggs Institute appraisal checklist (≥7/13 = methodologically adequate).
