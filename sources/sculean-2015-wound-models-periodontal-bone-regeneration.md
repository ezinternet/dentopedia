## One-line Summary
Periodontol 2000 2015;68(1):7-20: narrative review of research models (cell culture → animal → human) used to study periodontal and bone regeneration; highlights translational gap and regeneration biologic principles.

## 1. Key Results
- Overview of three model tiers: in vitro cell culture, animal models, human clinical trials
- Animal models: rat/mouse calvaria, rabbit calvaria, dog periodontitis, swine
- Key regenerative approaches: GTR, bone grafts, growth factors (EMD, PDGF, BMP-2), scaffolds
- Translational challenge: animal results do not always transfer to humans
- Highlights necessity of vascularization, clot stability, and space maintenance for regeneration
- Evidence levels vary widely across procedures

## 2. Methodology
- Narrative review / perspective article (Periodontology 2000 series opener)
- No systematic search; expert commentary on research methodology
- Covers wound healing biology, research model selection rationale

## 3. Clinical Implications
- Clinicians should interpret animal-based evidence with caution
- GTR and EMD remain best-supported by combined animal + human evidence
- BMP-2 effective in vertical ridge augmentation but dose/carrier issues remain
- PDGF (GEM 21) supported by RCT data for osseous defects
