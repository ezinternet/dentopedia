## Why Ingested

[[wiki/veneers/gonzalez-martin-2024-ultrathin-feldspathic-veneer-sem-etching]] establishes the ultrathin feldspathic veneer as a minimally invasive workflow — but the thinner and more translucent the veneer, the more the underlying resin cement shade leaks through and dominates the final color. This SR (Sharma 2026) is the ultrathin-veneer sibling on the *cementation* axis: it quantifies that the cement is an active optical component (not a passive luting medium), with the effect most pronounced exactly in the thin/high-translucency veneers that the ultrathin SEM/etching page promotes. It also refines the survival/complication framing of [[wiki/veneers/klein-2025-ceramic-laminate-veneer-survival-complications-sr-ma]] by isolating shade/color stability as a distinct esthetic-outcome driver.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro systematic review (10 studies, 2011–2021; no meta-analysis) showing resin cement shade and translucency materially shift the final color of ceramic laminate veneers — white-opaque/high-chroma cements drive the largest ΔE (often above the 3.3 acceptability threshold), worsened by thin (0.3–0.5 mm) and high-translucency veneers, while try-in pastes poorly predict the cured cement.

## 한줄요약

In-vitro 계통고찰 (10편, 2011–2021; 메타분석 없음) — 레진 시멘트의 색조·투명도가 세라믹 라미네이트 비니어의 최종 색을 유의하게 바꾼다. 백색-불투명·고채도 시멘트가 ΔE를 가장 크게 (흔히 임상 허용역 3.3 초과) 올리고, 얇은(0.3–0.5 mm)·고투명도 비니어에서 효과가 증폭되며, try-in 페이스트는 경화된 시멘트 색을 잘 예측하지 못한다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Reframes the resin luting cement as an **active optical component** of the cemented ceramic laminate veneer, not a passive adhesive — its shade/translucency measurably alters final color.
2. Synthesizes 10 in-vitro CIE L*a*b* / spectrophotometric studies to rank the determinants of cement-driven color change: cement opacity/value/chroma > veneer thickness > ceramic translucency > substrate shade.
3. Documents the **try-in paste ↔ cured cement mismatch** (ΔE 0.32–11.49), warning that paste-based shade selection is unreliable.
4. Flags **long-term color instability** of some cements under aging (VarioLink ΔE = 10.4) as a distinct survival/esthetic risk.
5. Identifies a methodological gap: studies overwhelmingly use ΔE*ab; none consistently use the perceptually better ΔE00, and most lack sample-size justification or examiner blinding.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Reporting/conduct**: PRISMA 2020 + Cochrane Handbook.
- **Databases**: PubMed, Web of Science, ProQuest, Google Scholar (to March 2023).
- **Inclusion**: in-vitro studies of resin-based luting agents on **ceramic laminate veneers** using **objective** color measurement (CIE L*a*b*, spectrophotometry).
- **Exclusion**: full-coverage restorations; subjective/visual shade assessment.
- **Risk of bias**: QUIN tool (laboratory-study-specific).
- **Selection**: 229 records → **10 studies** included.
- **Synthesis**: **No meta-analysis** — substantial heterogeneity in cement systems, ceramics, thickness, aging protocols, and ΔE method forced qualitative synthesis.
- **Included material space**: ceramics feldspathic / IPS Empress / e.max HT & LT / ZLS / LDS; cements RelyX, VarioLink, Panavia, Maxcem, eCement, Choice 2; sample sizes 10–392 specimens.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Cement opacity/chroma drives ΔE**: white-opaque (WO) and high-chroma/high-value cements consistently produced the greatest color change — ΔE often **> 3.3** (clinical acceptability threshold) — worst with thin or high-translucency veneers. Translucent/low-chroma cements gave favorable outcomes (ΔE typically **< 3.3**).
- **Veneer thickness modulates**: thinner veneers (**0.3–0.5 mm**) amplify cement-shade influence vs ≥1.0 mm; high-translucency ceramics transmit more light, accentuating cement shade.
- **Cement–substrate interaction**: opaque/high-value cements raise ΔE most over light backgrounds (Jankar, Chen, Yildirim).
- **Aging**: significant time-dependent discoloration — Hoorizad reported VarioLink **ΔE = 10.4** after aging vs ceramic-alone ΔE = 0.9.
- **Try-in unreliable**: Aldahlawi found ΔE **0.32–11.49** between try-in pastes and cured final cements (curing changes refractive index/polymer matrix).
- **Per-study ΔE ranges**: up to ~3.0 (Alqahtani); 1.37–2.11 (Lee); <3.3 to >4.0 (Xing); 1.93–3.04 (Hernandes); 0.9–10.4 (Hoorizad, aging); 1.6–3.4 (Turgut); up to 7.16 (Chen).
- **Risk of bias**: 3 studies low, the rest moderate; common gaps = no sample-size justification, no examiner blinding. Most used ΔE*ab; none consistently used ΔE00.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Entire evidence base is **in-vitro** and of **moderate** overall quality — no clinical trials.
- Heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis; pooled effect sizes unavailable.
- Reliance on ΔE*ab rather than the perceptually weighted ΔE00 limits comparability and clinical interpretability.
- Frequent absence of sample-size justification and examiner blinding.
- Authors call for clinical trials and standardized cementation/ΔE measurement protocols.

## 7. Glossary

- **ΔE (Delta E)**: total color difference between two specimens in CIE color space; ~3.3 is a common clinical acceptability threshold (perceptible/unacceptable above it).
- **ΔE*ab / ΔE00**: ΔE*ab = older CIELAB Euclidean color difference; ΔE00 (CIEDE2000) = perceptually weighted, generally preferred.
- **CIE L*a*b***: color space (L* lightness, a* red-green, b* yellow-blue) used for objective shade measurement.
- **Chroma / value**: chroma = color saturation; value = lightness; high-value/high-chroma opaque cements push ΔE up.
- **Try-in paste**: water- or glycerin-based non-curing paste used to preview cement shade before luting; refractive index/matrix differs from the cured resin cement.
- **WO (white opaque)**: high-opacity cement shade used to mask dark substrates — biggest ΔE driver on light backgrounds.
- **QUIN**: Quality assessment tool specific to in-vitro dental studies.
- **ZLS / LDS**: zirconia-reinforced lithium silicate / lithium disilicate glass-ceramics.
