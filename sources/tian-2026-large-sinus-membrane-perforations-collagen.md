## One-line Summary
Technical note describing a tack-stabilized collagen membrane technique for repairing large (>10 mm) Schneiderian membrane perforations during lateral-window maxillary sinus floor elevation, permitting simultaneous bone augmentation rather than aborting/reentry.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: International Journal of Periodontics & Restorative Dentistry, 02/2026 (issue), pages 232–243
- **Type**: Technical note / case description
- **PMID**: 39808537
- **Institutions**: Peking University School of Stomatology, Beijing, China; collaborators incl. Tiziano Testori
- **Study type**: Case series / technical note (in-vivo clinical)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Describes a reproducible technique to manage large (>10 mm) Schneiderian membrane perforations encountered during lateral-window sinus floor elevation.
- Uses a collagen membrane stabilized with **fixation tacks on the mesial and lateral sinus walls** to create a stable barrier above the graft.
- Permits **simultaneous bone augmentation** at the same surgical session, avoiding abortion of the procedure and a delayed reentry.
- Provides decision-making rationale for surgeons who would otherwise abandon grafting when faced with large perforations.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Lateral-window access to the maxillary sinus.
- After encountering a large perforation (>10 mm), a resorbable collagen membrane is shaped and laid over the perforation, draping the residual Schneiderian membrane.
- The membrane is stabilized by **fixation tacks placed in the mesial and lateral bony walls** of the sinus cavity, creating a tented compartment.
- Particulate bone graft is placed into the new compartment beneath the stabilized collagen membrane.
- Implants are placed simultaneously when primary stability is achievable; otherwise staged.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Technical note format — limited case numbers, no controlled comparison.
- Requires sufficient mesial and lateral bone-wall thickness to anchor tacks; not applicable to all anatomies.
- Long-term comparative outcome studies vs. abort-and-reentry approach still needed.

## 7. Glossary
- **Schneiderian membrane**: the thin mucoperiosteal lining of the maxillary sinus.
- **Lateral window approach**: classical Caldwell-Luc–style access through the lateral antral wall for sinus floor elevation.
- **Sinus floor elevation (SFE)**: surgical procedure to gain vertical bone height in the posterior maxilla for implant placement.
- **Membrane perforation**: intraoperative tear of the Schneiderian membrane; the most common complication of SFE.
