## Why Ingested

기존 [[wiki/implants/surface/bonfante-2012-calcium-phosphate-coatings-plasma-spray]]를 포함한 표면처리 연구들이 주로 hydroxyapatite·SLA·산부식 코팅을 다룬 반면, 보론화(boronization) 표면처리의 생체내 골유착 결과는 보고된 바 없었다. 본 동물 연구(Witek 2020)는 양 장골(ovine ilium) 모델에서 보론화 티타늄 임플란트의 BIC/BAFO를 최초로 측정하여, 우수한 기계적 특성에도 불구하고 골유착을 억제한다는 예상치 못한 결과를 보고함으로써 표면처리 선택의 근거를 확장한다.

## One-line Summary

Animal study (5 sheep, ovine ilium, 3+6 weeks) found boronized CP-titanium implants (BM, BAA) produced significantly lower BIC and declining BAFO over time compared to non-boronized acid-etched controls, indicating boronization impairs osseointegration despite favorable mechanical properties.

## 한줄요약

양 장골 모델(n=5, 3·6주)에서 보론화 티타늄 임플란트(BM, BAA)의 BIC와 BAFO가 비보론화 산부식 대조군보다 유의하게 낮고 시간이 지날수록 감소하여, 보론화 표면처리가 우수한 기계적 특성에도 불구하고 골유착을 저해함을 확인하였다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First large-animal (ovine) in vivo evaluation of solid-state boronized titanium implant osseointegration.
2. Demonstrated that boronization decreases BIC and BAFO compared to acid-etched controls, directly contradicting in vitro findings of boron's osteogenic potential.
3. Showed a significant downward temporal trend (3→6 weeks) in BIC and BAFO for both boronized groups while control groups increased — indicating active inhibition of normal bone remodeling dynamics, not merely slower start.
4. Histology revealed demineralized woven bone foci (osteoid-level) adjacent to boronized surfaces, suggesting local toxic boron diffusion from the TiB/TiB2 surface layer impairs early bone healing.
5. Acid-etched boronized (BAA) implants showed worse outcomes than machined boronized (BM), attributed to greater surface area releasing more boron locally.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Animal Model**
- Species/site: 5 adult male sheep (~55 kg each), bilateral ovine iliac crest (hip); low-density bone chosen for translational relevance and to accommodate all 4 groups per animal
- Total implants: 40 (n=10 per group)
- Time points: 3 weeks and 6 weeks in vivo (right hip = 3 wk, left hip = 6 wk; staggered surgery)

**Implants**
- Material: Type II commercially pure (CP) titanium; 4 mm diameter × 10 mm length
- Four groups:
  - BM: boronized machined
  - BAA: boronized acid-etched
  - CM: control machined
  - CAA: control acid-etched

**Boronization Process (solid-state pack diffusion)**
- Powder mixture: 97 wt% amorphous boron (SB Boron 90) + 2 wt% boron carbide (B4C, 240 grit) + 1 wt% potassium fluoroborate (KBF4) activator
- Drying: 250°C × 2 h in box furnace
- Diffusion: vacuum furnace ramped to 1100°C × 8 h in argon (5 m³/h flow); cooled to 250°C under argon
- Post-treatment: cleaned with methanol in ultrasound tank
- Coating thickness: 10–15 µm (confirmed by Zeiss optical microscopy cross-section)
- Sterilization: gamma irradiation at 25 kGy (CellRad Faxitron)

**Surgery**
- Anesthesia: sodium pentothal (15–20 mg/kg IV) induction; isoflurane 1.5–3% in O2/N2O (50/50) maintenance
- Drilling: 1100 rpm under saline irrigation; final drill diameter 3.8 mm
- Antibiotic: cefazolin 500 mg IV pre- and post-operatively
- Euthanasia: Telazol + xylazine IM then sodium pentobarbital 120 mg/kg IV

**Histology & Histomorphometry**
- Processing: non-decalcified; progressive dehydration (ethanol) → infiltration (methyl salicylate) → MMA embedding
- Sectioning: slow-speed diamond saw (Isomet 2000), ~100 µm slices; ground/polished to ~75 µm final thickness
- Stain: Stevenel's Blue and Van Gieson's Picro Fuchsin (SVG)
- Imaging: Aperio automated slide scanning system
- Measurements: ImageJ — BIC (% implant perimeter in bone contact) and BAFO (% bone area within implant threads); single blinded examiner

**Statistics**
- Linear mixed model; fixed factors: surface texture (machined vs acid-etched), boronization (yes/no), time point (3 vs 6 wk)
- Data presented as mean ± 95% CI
- IBM SPSS v23

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**BIC at 3 Weeks**
| Group | BIC (mean ± 95% CI) |
|---|---|
| BM  | 16.44% ± 7.9 |
| BAA | 21.73% ± 7.9 |
| CM  | similar to BM/BAA (ns) |
| CAA | significantly higher (p<0.02 vs BM, BAA, CM) |

- At 3 weeks: only CAA (acid-etched control) was significantly higher; BM, BAA, CM did not differ from each other.

**BIC at 6 Weeks**
| Group | BIC (mean ± 95% CI) |
|---|---|
| BM  | 8.9% ± 9.7 (ns change from 3 wk) |
| BAA | 5.93% ± 9.7 (significant decrease from 3 wk, p<0.01) |
| CAA | significantly increased from 3 wk |
| CM  | significantly increased from 3 wk |

**BAFO at 3 Weeks**
| Group | BAFO (mean ± 95% CI) |
|---|---|
| BAA | 25.82% ± 6.6 |
| BM  | 27.29% ± 6.6 |
| CAA | 30.76% ± 6.6 |
| CM  | 19.92% ± 6.6 (significantly lower than BAA, BM, CAA; p<0.02) |

**BAFO over Time**
- Both boronized groups (BM, BAA): significant BAFO decrease from 3→6 weeks (p<0.03)
- Both control groups (CM, CAA): significant BAFO increase from 3→6 weeks
- This divergence is the core finding: bone actively retreats from boronized surfaces while consolidating around controls.

**Qualitative Histology**
- Control groups: normal intramembranous-like healing — progression from woven bone to organized lamellar bone; increasing density and contact over 3→6 weeks
- Boronized groups: absence of dynamic remodeling; demineralized regions (osteoid-level woven bone) noted focally near BAA surfaces, suggesting local boron diffusion induces demineralization
- All implants: clinically stable at necropsy, no signs of inflammation or infection

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

1. **Small sample size** (n=10 per group) limits statistical power; mixed model partially compensates.
2. **Short time points** (3 and 6 weeks only) — longer follow-up needed to determine if boron toxicity is transient or permanent.
3. **Unknown boron release kinetics** — the study does not quantify actual boron release rate or local tissue boron concentration; mechanistic link between TiB/TiB2 surface and osteoid demineralization is inferred but not directly demonstrated.
4. **Single animal species** (sheep) — ovine ilium is low-density; outcomes in denser mandibular/femoral bone unknown.
5. **No biomechanical testing** (removal torque not performed) — histomorphometry only.
6. **Future directions**: determine ideal boron concentration/dose to avoid toxicity; evaluate effect on osteoclast/osteoblast precursors and macrophages; longer time points; denser bone sites; modified boronization protocols.

## 6. Related Work

- **Albrektsson et al. 1981 (Acta Orthop Scand)** — foundational osseointegration requirements
- **Coelho & Jimbo 2014 (Arch Biochem Biophys)** — implant hardware design and osseointegration trends
- **Yao et al. 2016 (Materials)** — borided Ti-6Al-4V via SMAT + low-temperature boriding surface evaluation
- **Dogan et al. 2014 (Mater Sci Eng C)** — boron-containing PLGA scaffolds for bone tissue engineering (rat femur); showed boron increased bone healing in polymeric scaffold context
- **Gorustovich et al. 2006 (Biomed Mater)** — boron-modified bioactive glass in rat tibia; greater bone formation with higher Ca:P ratios
- **Hakki et al. 2013 (J Trace Elem Med Biol)** — dietary boron enhances bone strength in rabbits (systemic vs. local delivery distinction)
- **Lahens et al. 2016 (J Mech Behav Biomed Mater)** — osseodensification drilling in sheep ilium (same ovine model used as reference)
- **Baldassarri et al. 2012** — mechanical properties of human bone around plateau root form implants

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Boronization (boriding) | Thermo-chemical surface treatment — boron atoms diffuse into metal by solid-state diffusion forming TiB/TiB2 nanocrystalline hard layer; improves wear and corrosion resistance |
| BIC (Bone-to-Implant Contact) | Histomorphometric measure: % of implant perimeter in direct contact with mineralized bone |
| BAFO (Bone Area Fraction Occupancy) | Histomorphometric measure: % area of bone tissue within the implant thread spaces |
| Pack boronizing | Solid-state diffusion method using powder mixture (boron source + activator) in a sealed crucible at high temperature |
| TiB/TiB2 | Titanium boride phases formed at titanium surface during boronization |
| KBF4 (potassium fluoroborate) | Activator compound in the boronizing powder pack |
| MMA (methyl methacrylate) | Embedding resin for non-decalcified hard-tissue histology |
| SVG stain | Stevenel's Blue + Van Gieson's Picro Fuchsin — standard staining combination for bone histology |
| Woven bone | Immature, disorganized bone formed early in healing; subsequently remodeled to lamellar bone |
| Osteoid | Unmineralized bone matrix precursor; demineralized woven bone found adjacent to BAA indicates failed secondary mineralization |
| Intramembranous-like healing | Direct bone formation without cartilage intermediate; normal route for implant osseointegration |
| Ovine ilium | Sheep hip bone; low-density cancellous bone site used as surgical model |
