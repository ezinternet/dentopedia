## One-line Summary

Biomechanical clinical-repair study (n=21 closures, closing tensions 0.3-4.0 N): the basic lattice stitch dispersed 54.4 ± 3.2% more tissue tension away from the perpendicular plane of closure than a simple interrupted stitch (p<.005).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies the tension-dispersing property of the **basic lattice stitch** relative to a simple interrupted stitch.
- Demonstrates the lattice stitch removes ~54% of tissue tension from the perpendicular plane of closure — the plane along which wound edges are most likely to tear/dehisce.
- Provides rationale for using the lattice stitch in high-tension closures and in repair of atrophic/fragile skin.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

(From abstract only — full methods paywalled.)
- 21 wound closures studied, with closing tensions ranging from 0.3 to 4.0 N.
- Closure forces on structural components of the basic lattice stitch were measured and compared with the closing force of a simple interrupted stitch.
- Outcome: percent reduction in tissue tension in the perpendicular plane of closure.
- The specific force-measurement device and exact force-decomposition method are "not reported" in the available abstract.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- In 21 closures, the basic lattice stitch produced **54.4 ± 3.2% less tissue tension in the perpendicular plane of closure** than a simple interrupted stitch (p<.005).
- Conclusion: the lattice stitch significantly distributes tension away from the perpendicular plane and should be considered for any closure under tension or for atrophic-skin repair.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Available record is abstract-only; sample is modest (n=21) and outcome is a single biomechanical metric.
- No clinical healing/dehiscence/scar endpoints reported in the abstract.
- Tension range (0.3-4.0 N) is lower than the 5 N high-tension model used by Marsidi et al.

## 6. Related Work

- Conceptually aligned with tension-relieving/tension-dispersing suture literature (pulley, mattress variants).
- Complements pulling-force quantification (Marsidi 2020) and tensile-strength/dehiscence-force testing (Look 2022).

## 7. Glossary

- **Basic lattice stitch:** a suture configuration whose geometry redistributes wound tension away from the line perpendicular to the closure, reducing the risk of edge tearing.
- **Perpendicular plane of closure:** the plane across which wound edges are pulled together (the direction of maximal dehiscence force); the target of tension dispersion.
- **Simple interrupted stitch:** standard single-loop suture used as the comparison/control here.
- **Atrophic skin:** thinned, fragile skin (e.g., aged, steroid-affected) prone to tearing under suture tension.
