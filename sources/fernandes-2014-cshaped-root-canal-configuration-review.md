## One-line Summary

Narrative review (PubMed 1979–2012) synthesizing etiology, incidence, anatomy, classification (Melton/Fan), diagnosis, and management of C-shaped root canals, predominantly in mandibular second molars.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Consolidates the **etiology**: failure of Hertwig's epithelial root sheath to fuse on the buccal or lingual aspect creates a longitudinal groove; failure on both sides yields a conical/prism root. Genetic (racial) origin favored over earlier trauma/age theories.
- Tabulates **incidence** with strong ethnic predilection: high in East Asian (Chinese 0.6–41.27%, Korean 31.3–45.5%) and some West Asian (Lebanese 19.1%) populations; mandibular second molar range 2.7–45.5%. Also reported in mandibular premolars (Chinese up to 29.7%), maxillary first molars (0.12%), maxillary/mandibular third molars. Bilateral occurrence 70–81%. No correlation with gender or age.
- Reviews the **classification systems**: Cooke & Cox (first description, 1979), Manning, Melton et al. (categories I–III by orifice), and the more anatomically complete Fan et al. cross-sectional classification (C1 uninterrupted C; C2 semicolon; C3 separate canals; C4 single round/oval; C5 no lumen).
- Lays out **diagnosis** across the timeline: preoperative radiograph (radicular fusion/proximity, large distal canal, blurred third canal; Fan's radiographic types I–III), clinical exam under microscope (greatest yield when combined, 41.27%), pulpal-floor patterns (Min et al.), and working-length radiographs (most helpful).
- Details **management**: access modification, multi-file insertion strategy per orifice shape (continuous-C → 3 files; oval → 2; round → 1), small-file exploration (#8/10/15 K-file), ultrasonic/sonic-assisted cleaning of fins, and modified obturation for 3-D fill.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Narrative literature review. Manual and electronic PubMed searches (1979–2012) crossing the keywords above. No formal systematic-review protocol, risk-of-bias appraisal, or meta-analysis; evidence is summarized qualitatively with an incidence table compiled across population studies.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Mandibular second molar prevalence**: 2.7%–45.5% depending on population.
- **Korean**: 31.3%–45.5%; **Chinese**: 0.6%–41.27% (mandibular second molar); mandibular premolar up to 29.7% in Chinese.
- **Minimum canal wall thickness**: thinner lingual than buccal, lowest measured 0.26 mm (perforation/strip risk).
- **Accessory/lateral canals, inter-canal communications, apical delta**: present in 11–41% of the apical region.
- **Bilateral occurrence**: 70%–81%.
- **Diagnostic yield**: radiography 34.64%, clinical microscope 39.18%, combined 41.27% (Wang et al.).
- C-shaped premolar grooves start ~3 mm below CEJ, mean depth ~1.5 mm.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative (non-systematic) review: no quantitative synthesis, no risk-of-bias appraisal; incidence figures pooled from heterogeneous methods (cross-section, clearing, radiograph, early CBCT).
- Pre-2012 search predates the bulk of high-resolution CBCT prevalence literature now central to the topic.
- Classification multiplicity (Manning/Melton/Al-Fouzan/Fan/Gao/Min) is described but not reconciled into a single recommended scheme.

## 6. Related Work

- Cooke & Cox (1979) — first endodontic description of C-shaped canals.
- Melton et al.; Fan et al. — the dominant orifice and cross-sectional classification systems.
- Song et al. (2008) — minimum canal-wall thickness and cross-sectional morphology data cited herein.
- Subsequent CBCT prevalence studies (Shemesh 2017, Abdalrahman 2022, Armenta 2024) and SR+MA (Yousefi 2025) quantify the incidence this review surveys qualitatively.

## 7. Glossary

- **C-shaped canal**: root canal whose cross-section resembles the letter C, from a ribbon-shaped orifice arcing ≥180° from mesiolingual around the buccal to the distal.
- **Hertwig's epithelial root sheath (HERS)**: developmental structure whose failure to fuse buccally/lingually produces the C-shaped groove.
- **Melton classification**: C1 (continuous C), C2 (semicolon), C3 (separate canals) — orifice-level.
- **Fan classification**: C1–C5 cross-sectional categories (adds C4 round/oval, C5 no lumen).
- **Fan radiographic types I–III**: how the C-shape appears on a periapical radiograph.
- **Apical delta**: complex branching of multiple accessory canals near the apex.
- **Fin/isthmus**: thin web of canal connecting the main canals in a C-shaped system.
