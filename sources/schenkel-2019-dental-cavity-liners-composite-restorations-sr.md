## One-line Summary

Cochrane SR (8 RCTs, >700 participants) found inconsistent, low-quality evidence that a cavity liner under Class I/II posterior composite reduces postoperative hypersensitivity, and no evidence it improves restoration longevity.

## 한줄요약

코크란 SR(8 RCT, 참가자 700명 초과): Class I/II 구치부 컴포지트 밑에 라이너를 깔아도 술후 과민증 감소 근거는 일관성 없는 저질(low-quality)이고, 수복물 수명 차이는 근거 없음.

## 2. Key Contributions

- The highest-tier evidence (Cochrane SR) on the **indication for placing a cavity liner under posterior composite** — i.e., whether liner-bases such as Ionosit-Baseliner / RMGI / flowable / GI liners are clinically justified beneath Class I/II RBC.
- **Postoperative hypersensitivity**: 7 trials; inconsistent evidence — a benefit at *some but not all* time points; **low-quality** (all trials unclear/high risk of bias).
- **Restoration longevity**: 4 trials at 1 year + 3 trials at 2 years; **no difference** in failure rates (mostly zero failures in both arms; one trial RR 1.00, 95% CI 0.07–15.00); low-quality.
- **No adverse events** in any included study.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Cochrane systematic review (update of 2016), standard Cochrane methods; risk-of-bias and data extraction in duplicate.
- **Inclusion**: RCTs (parallel and split-mouth) of liner vs no liner under Class I/II posterior RBC restorations in permanent teeth, adults and children (all included participants were >15 years).
- **Databases**: Cochrane Oral Health Trials Register, CENTRAL (2018 Issue 10), MEDLINE Ovid (1946–), Embase Ovid (1980–), LILACS (1982–); searched to 12 Nov 2018; no language/date restriction.
- **Included studies**: 8 trials, >700 participants — USA ×2, Thailand ×2, Germany ×2, Saudi Arabia ×1, Turkey ×1 (mostly university dental schools; one USA trial in private practice).
- **Outcomes**: postoperative hypersensitivity (cold response and/or patient-reported), restoration longevity/failure, adverse events.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Trials | Finding | Certainty |
|---|---|---|---|
| Postoperative hypersensitivity | 7 | Inconsistent — benefit at some but not all time points | Low (GRADE) |
| Restoration failure @ 1 yr | 4 | No difference; 3/4 trials zero failures both arms; 4th RR 1.00 (95% CI 0.07–15.00) | Low |
| Restoration failure @ 2 yr | 3 | No failures in either group | Low |
| Adverse events | 8 | None reported | — |

**Authors' conclusion (verbatim):** "There is inconsistent, low-quality evidence regarding the difference in postoperative hypersensitivity subsequent to placing a dental cavity liner under Class I and Class II posterior resin-based composite restorations… Furthermore, no evidence was found to demonstrate a difference in the longevity of restorations placed with or without dental cavity liners."

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- All 8 included trials at **unclear or high risk of bias** → overall low certainty; effect estimates not robust.
- Short follow-up for longevity (1–2 years) with near-zero failure rates → underpowered for longevity differences.
- Participants all >15 years → does not address younger children / primary teeth.
- Heterogeneous hypersensitivity measurement methods limit pooling.
- Liner material types not stratified in the retrieved abstract (RMGI vs flowable vs GI vs calcium hydroxide effects not separable here).

## 7. Glossary

- **Cavity liner**: thin layer placed on the cavity floor/walls beneath the bulk restorative to protect the pulp and/or insulate against thermal conductivity (e.g., RMGI, flowable composite, glass ionomer, calcium hydroxide; product example Ionosit-Baseliner, DMG).
- **Base**: thicker liner layer used to block out undercuts / replace dentin bulk.
- **RBC (resin-based composite)**: tooth-colored direct restorative; thermal conductivity close to natural tooth.
- **Postoperative hypersensitivity**: transient sensitivity (often to cold) after restoration placement.
- **Class I / Class II**: occlusal-only (I) vs occlusal + proximal (II) cavity classifications.
- **GRADE low-quality**: limited confidence in the effect estimate; true effect may differ substantially.
