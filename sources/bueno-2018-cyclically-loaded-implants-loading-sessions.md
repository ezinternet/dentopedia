## Why Ingested

임플란트 주위 골치유에 대한 반복 하중의 용량-반응(dose-response) 관계를 동물 모델(쥐 경골)에서 직접 검증한 기초 연구. 교합 하중 빈도가 골-임플란트 접촉률(Bone-to-Implant Contact, BIC)과 골치유에 미치는 영향을 정량화해 [[wiki/occlusion/implant-occlusion-loading-biomechanics-overview]]의 과부하 역치 논의에 실험적 근거를 제공한다.

## One-line Summary

Rat tibia animal study (n=27, 3 groups) showing that doubling cyclic loading sessions (2× vs 1× per day, 60 cycles of 1.5 N, 7 days) significantly reduces BIC and increases bone-implant distance, demonstrating a dose-dependent detrimental effect of cumulative micromotion on early peri-implant bone healing.

## 한줄요약

쥐 경골 동물 실험(n=27, 3군)에서 하루 2회 반복 하중군이 1회군 및 무하중군에 비해 골-임플란트 접촉률(BIC) 유의감소·골-임플란트 거리(BID) 유의증가를 보여, 누적 미세동요(micromotion)의 용량 의존적 골치유 악화 효과를 규명하였다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First direct experimental demonstration that increasing daily loading sessions (1× → 2×) with the same peak force per cycle (1.5 N) significantly impairs early bone healing at the gap implant interface.
2. Establishes that one loading session per day (60 cycles, 1.5 N) produces no measurable difference in BIC, BID, or bone formation area compared to unloaded controls — defining a tentative "acceptable" loading threshold.
3. Finite element analysis (FEA) characterizes the biomechanical microenvironment: ~94 μm axial implant displacement with fibrin-filled gap; principal compressive strains reaching 60% at thread crests, 30% beneath apex.
4. DNA microarray gene expression analysis identifies the inflammatory/immune pathway as the predominant response across all groups, with greater up-regulation in the 2× group, implicating cumulative inflammation as the mechanobiological mechanism.
5. Single-case observation of fibrocartilage formation in the 2× group (at thread flank/apex base), consistent with local hydrostatic compression and distortional strain predicted by FEA.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

### Animal Model and Implant

- **Implant**: Machined screw-shaped cp Titanium Grade 2; 7 mm length, 0.45 mm pitch, 1.7 mm diameter
- **Interface model**: BIGI (Bone Implant Gap Interface) — implant (1.7 mm) placed in 2.0 mm drilled hole in rat tibia, leaving a ~0.15 mm circumferential gap filled with fibrin/healing tissue
- **Bone plate fixation**: Custom Delrin bone plate with titanium alloy retopins fixed to tibia; silicone O-ring (axial stiffness 0.016 N/μm) between implant head and plate to distribute force

### Experimental Groups

| Group | Daily Sessions | Cycles/Session | Force/Cycle | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Unloaded (control) | 0 | — | — | 7 days |
| Micromotion 1× | 1 | 60 | 1.5 N axial | 7 days |
| Micromotion 2× | 2 | 60 | 1.5 N axial | 7 days |

- Loading frequency: ~1 cycle/second (manual); animals under isoflurane (1–2%) during loading
- Each anesthesia ≤5 min; unloaded group also anesthetized once/day for wound cleaning

### Outcome Measures

- **Histomorphometry** (n=5/group, 3 sections/animal = 15 sections/group):
  - BFAo: Bone Formation Area within 0.15 mm of implant surface
  - BFAt: Bone Formation Area within 1.025 mm (trephine radius) of implant surface
  - BIC: % implant surface in direct contact with bone (threads 1–3 bilaterally)
  - BID: Mean bone-implant distance (90 measurements/animal, 30 evenly-spaced points × 3 sections)
- **DNA Microarray**: GeneChip Rat Gene 2.0 ST Array (Affymetrix); n=4/group; RIN ≥8.5; Gene Ontology (PANTHER) and Ingenuity Pathway Analysis (IPA); fold-change cutoff ≥2, p<0.05
- **FEA**: 3D finite element models (4 mm diameter composite cylinder: 0.6 mm cortical + 1.6 mm trabecular + 2 mm drill hole with 1.7 mm implant); material properties varied over time to simulate healing

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Histomorphometry

| Measure | Unloaded | Micromotion 1× | Micromotion 2× | Statistics |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| BFAo (% bone area near implant) | No significant difference | No significant difference | Significantly lower | p<0.05 (2× vs others) |
| BIC (%) | No significant difference | No significant difference | Significantly lower | p<0.05 (2× vs others) |
| BID (mean bone-implant distance) | No significant difference | No significant difference | Significantly larger | p<0.05 (2× vs others) |
| BFAt (overall trephine area) | No difference | No difference | No significant difference | p>0.05 |

Key finding: Unloaded and Micromotion 1× groups were statistically indistinguishable on all measures; only the 2× group showed significant impairment of interfacial bone healing.

### Histological Findings

- All groups: trabecular bone formation in marrow cavity; active bone deposition surfaces; no major inflammatory infiltrate; no osteoclast accumulation
- Micromotion 2× group: disrupted bone formation and connective tissue formation close to implant surface; fibrous layer along implant surface
- Single case in 2× group: cartilage (fibrocartilage) formation at thread flank and apex base — consistent with FEA-predicted negative hydrostatic stress + high distortional strain

### FEA Results

- Fibrin-filled gap (E~0.05 MPa): ~94 μm axial implant displacement at 1.5 N
- Compressive strain at thread crests: up to 60%; beneath apex: ~30%
- As interfacial tissue stiffened with healing, strain magnitudes decreased — healing self-protects, but repeated disruption prevents this stiffening

### Gene Expression

- All groups showed predominantly inflammatory/immune pathway activation
- 2× group showed enhanced inflammatory gene up-regulation compared to other groups
- Unknown gene categories also differed between groups
- Detailed differentially-expressed gene lists in Supplementary Tables S1–S6

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

1. **Single time-point**: 7-day endpoint only; does not capture evolution of healing over weeks or ultimate osseointegration outcome
2. **Short loading period**: 7-day loading protocol may not represent clinical loading durations for weeks to months
3. **Rat model**: Rat tibia BIGI is not equivalent to clinical implant placement in healed bone; healing is confounded by surgical insult-driven bone induction
4. **Anesthesia confound**: 2× group received twice-daily isoflurane; authors argue the effect is small (<5 min exposures), and isoflurane is generally anti-inflammatory (opposite direction of observed result), but the confound cannot be fully excluded
5. **Machined (non-treated) surface**: Results may not generalize to SLA or other surface-modified implants with higher osseointegration potential
6. **Gap interface only**: Only BIGI model studied; primary stability situations differ
7. Authors suggest future studies allowing initial healing before applying force, and investigating immune/inflammatory modulation as a therapeutic strategy

## 6. Related Work

- **Leucht et al., 2007**: Prior work in mouse tibia model with constant displacement — parent model for this rat tibia protocol
- **Wazen et al., 2013**: Constant micromotion in rat tibia BIGI — predecessor to this study examining loading sessions
- **Brunski, 1999**: Foundational in vivo bone response to biomechanical loading at bone-dental implant interface
- **Duyck et al., 2006, 2007**: Influence of micromotion and intermittent loading on tissue differentiation around immediately loaded implants
- **Vandamme et al., 2007a, 2007b**: Loading and bone chamber model studies supporting that some micromotion can benefit bone formation
- **Romanos, 2003**: Histomorphometric analysis in non-human primate under different loading conditions

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| BIGI | Bone Implant Gap Interface — implant placed in intentionally oversized hole, creating circumferential gap for studying gap-healing |
| BIC | Bone-to-Implant Contact — % of implant surface in direct bone apposition |
| BID | Bone-Implant Distance — mean distance from implant surface to first bone |
| BFA | Bone Formation Area — % bone area within defined radial zone around implant |
| Micromotion | Relative displacement of implant with respect to surrounding bone during loading |
| FEA | Finite Element Analysis — computational stress/strain modeling |
| BIGI | Bone Implant Gap Interface model used in this study |
| RIN | RNA Integrity Number — quality metric; >8.5 required here |
| GeneChip Rat Gene 2.0 ST | Affymetrix whole-transcriptome microarray for rat gene expression profiling |
| IPA | Ingenuity Pathway Analysis — bioinformatics tool for signaling pathway analysis |
| PANTHER | Protein Analysis THrough Evolutionary Relationships — Gene Ontology classification system |
| Fibrocartilage | Cartilage type associated with high compressive/distortional strain environments; abnormal finding at implant interface |
| O-ring | Silicone rubber ring in bone plate system that absorbs a fraction of applied axial force |
