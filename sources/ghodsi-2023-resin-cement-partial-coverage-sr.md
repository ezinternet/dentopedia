## One-line Summary
Narrative SR of 68 articles deriving resin cement selection criteria for partial coverage restorations (inlay/onlay/veneer/endocrown) based on restoration material, thickness, and translucency.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Clinical and Experimental Dental Research 2023;9:1096–1111
- **DOI**: 10.1002/cre2.761
- **Search period**: 1991–2023
- **Databases**: PubMed, Medline, Scopus, Google Scholar
- **Included**: 68 articles
- **Institutions**: Tehran University of Medical Sciences; Isfahan University of Medical Sciences

## 2. Key Contributions
- Decision framework matching resin cement type to PCR substrate and esthetic demands
- Comprehensive classification of resin cements by polymerization mode (self-cure / light-cure / dual-cure) and adhesion strategy (conventional EAR / self-etch / self-adhesive)
- Clear contraindications for self-adhesive/self-etch dual-cure cements in laminate veneers
- Tabulated indications/contraindications/advantages with referenced rationale

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: Narrative systematic review
- **Inclusion**: Articles on resin cement selection for PCRs (inlay, onlay, laminate veneer, endocrown)
- **Cement classifications analyzed**:
  - Polymerization: self-cure, light-cure, dual-cure
  - Adhesion: conventional (EAR), self-etch, self-adhesive
- **Outcome**: Selection criteria mapped to restoration material/thickness/translucency

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
**By polymerization mode**:
| Mode | Indication | Contraindication |
|---|---|---|
| Self-cure | Metal/PFM, thick (>2mm) ceramic, opaque ceramics | Esthetic anterior, thin restorations |
| Light-cure | Thin (<2mm) translucent metal-free, ceramic veneer <1.5mm | Thick or opaque restorations |
| Dual-cure | Most versatile; high bond strength, esthetic | None major; preferred for thicker translucent |

**By adhesion strategy**:
- Conventional (EAR) resin cements: Highest bond strength; technique-sensitive
- Self-etch resin cements: Reduced technique sensitivity; not recommended for laminate veneers
- Self-adhesive resin cements: Single-step; not generally indicated for laminate veneers (especially dual-cure type)

**Key clinical recommendations**:
- Metallic PCRs (PFM, cast metal) → self-cure or dual-cure
- Thin/translucent low-strength ceramics → light-cure conventional resin cement
- Laminate veneers → avoid self-etch and self-adhesive dual-cure types
- Thick (>2mm) ceramic → self-cure or dual-cure (light penetration insufficient for pure light-cure)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative format — no quantitative meta-analysis
- Heterogeneous study designs included
- Cement and ceramic material formulations rapidly evolving
- Long-term clinical comparative data on newer self-adhesive cements still limited

## 7. Glossary
- **PCR (Partial Coverage Restoration, 부분피개수복물)**: Inlay, onlay, laminate veneer, endocrown
- **PFM (Porcelain-Fused-to-Metal, 도재용착주조관)**: Metal substructure with porcelain veneer
- **EAR**: Etch-and-Rinse
- **Self-adhesive cement**: All-in-one cement with no separate primer/adhesive
- **Dual-cure**: Polymerization by both light activation and chemical (self-cure) initiator
- **Laminate veneer (라미네이트 베니어)**: Thin (<1mm) ceramic facing for anterior esthetic restoration
