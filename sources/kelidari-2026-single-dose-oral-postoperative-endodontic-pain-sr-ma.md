## Why Ingested

기존 [[drug/analgesics/samani-2025-nsaids-endodontic-postoperative-network]] (동일 2저자 Samani의 NSAID 중심 endodontic network meta-analysis)을 보강·확장한다. 본 Kelidari 2026 NMA는 NSAID 단독에 그치지 않고 단회투여(single-dose) 후처치 약물 전체 — diclofenac+acetaminophen, Novafen(ibuprofen+acetaminophen+caffeine), naproxen, tramadol, ibuprofen+alprazolam — 를 6–8h/12h/24h 시간대별로 직접 순위화하여, "어느 단회투여 약을 처방할지"라는 임상 질문에 시간대별 답을 제공한다. 단회투여로 한정해 환자 순응도·부작용·비용 부담을 줄이는 실용적 처방 가이드라인.

## One-line Summary

Frequentist network meta-analysis (10 RCTs qualitative, 5 quantitative, n=347): single-dose oral analgesics after non-surgical RCT — Diclofenac+Acetaminophen best for immediate (6–8 h) relief (MD −6.28), Novafen and Naproxen sustained at 12–24 h.

## 한줄요약

네트워크 메타분석 (RCT 10편 정성·5편 정량, n=347): 비수술 근관치료 후 단회투여 경구 진통제 중 디클로페낙+아세트아미노펜이 즉각(6–8h) 진통 최고(MD −6.28), 노바펜·나프록센이 12–24h 지속 효과 우수.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR/NMA to specifically isolate **single-dose POSTOPERATIVE** medications after non-surgical root canal treatment (RCT), excluding pre- and intra-operative dosing to give a clean prescribing answer.
- Provides **time-resolved drug rankings** (6–8 h, 12 h, 24 h) via P-scores rather than a single pooled estimate — the clinically useful framing because analgesic choice depends on the time window of breakthrough pain.
- Identifies a key pharmacokinetic split: **fast-but-short** agents (diclofenac+acetaminophen) vs **sustained** agents (naproxen, Novafen) — and flags that diclofenac+acetaminophen's effect reverses toward placebo by 24 h, requiring redosing.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: PRISMA 2020 SR + frequentist NMA (R v4.4.3, netmeta package).
- **PICO**: P = endodontic-treatment patients (moderate-to-severe preop pain, mostly irreversible pulpitis); I = single-dose oral medication after RCT; C = placebo; O = post-endodontic pain by VAS.
- **Search**: PubMed, Scopus, Embase, Cochrane CENTRAL up to Feb 19, 2025; no date/type/language limits; hand-searched references.
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane RoB 2 (5 domains).
- **Statistics**: Mean differences (MD) with 95% CI vs placebo. Random-effects model when I² > 50%. P-score ranking. Transitivity + design-by-treatment global inconsistency. Sensitivity analysis excluding Islam et al. (inconsistency source). Funnel plots + Egger. GRADE-for-NMA.
- **Included (qualitative, n=10 RCTs, 1999–2021)**: Iran, USA, Pakistan, Turkey, India, Sudan; sample 30–170.
- **Included (quantitative, n=5 RCTs, combined 347 participants)**: Menhinick 2004, Islam 2018, Mehrvarzfar 2012, Baradaran 2014, Makkar 2012. Five others (Nekoofar 2003, Rogers 1999, Akpinar 2021, Madani 2013, Elzaki 2016) excluded for missing SD / no reliable imputation.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**6–8 h (immediate)** — top reductions vs placebo:
- Diclofenac+Acetaminophen: MD −6.28 (95% CI −11.99 to −0.56) — greatest
- Novafen: MD −3.46 (−5.27 to −1.65)
- Ibuprofen+Acetaminophen: MD −3.05 (−4.80 to −1.30)
- Naproxen: MD −2.62 (−3.97 to −1.27)
- Ibuprofen+Alprazolam: MD −1.75 (−3.56 to 0.07) — NS

**12 h** — Novafen and Naproxen most pronounced:
- Novafen MD −2.70 (−3.87 to −1.52); Naproxen MD −2.58 (−3.44 to −1.73); Ibuprofen MD −1.64 (−2.57 to −0.71); Tramadol MD −1.46 (−2.35 to −0.57); Ibuprofen+Alprazolam MD −2.39 (−3.75 to −1.04, now significant — delayed onset).
- Diclofenac+Acetaminophen and Ibuprofen+Acetaminophen no longer significant.

**24 h** — only 4 remain superior to placebo:
- Novafen MD −2.89 (−4.14 to −1.63); Naproxen MD −2.48 (−3.42 to −1.54); Tramadol MD −1.39 (−2.41 to −0.38); Ibuprofen MD −1.32 (−2.28 to −0.37).
- Diclofenac+Acetaminophen MD +0.50 (p=0.93) — direction favors placebo. Ibuprofen+Acetaminophen and Ibuprofen+Alprazolam NS.
- **P-score top 3 at 24 h: Novafen, Naproxen, Tramadol.**

**Robustness**: Excluding Islam et al. left direction/magnitude unchanged and resolved global inconsistency (e.g. 6–8 h p 0.0038→0.3030). Funnel plots symmetrical. Most GRADE comparisons moderate-to-high certainty.

**Mechanistic note**: Novafen (ibuprofen + acetaminophen + **caffeine**) sustains efficacy where plain ibuprofen+acetaminophen fades — attributed to caffeine adjuvant (CNS stimulation, enhanced absorption). Alprazolam benefit is psychogenic (anxiety reduction), not true analgesia.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- High heterogeneity (I² > 70% at 6–8 h and 24 h): variable dosing (ibuprofen 400 vs 600 mg), pain types, study designs.
- Only 5 of 10 RCTs entered the quantitative NMA (small network, n=347); VAS subjective with overestimation risk; assessment intervals varied; Menhinick gave no scores beyond 8 h.
- Confounders: intracanal medicaments/irrigants, instrumentation technique (crown-down = less apical extrusion), tooth type, periapical status, residual local anesthesia, self/escape medication.
- Sex/age differences unaddressed. No long-term (>24 h) follow-up.
- Future: dose-response (Novafen vs matched monotherapy), pre/intra-operative dosing meta-analyses, 48–72 h follow-up, PROs, non-pharmacological adjuncts.

## 7. Glossary

- **NMA (network meta-analysis)**: pools direct + indirect comparisons to rank multiple treatments simultaneously.
- **P-score**: frequentist ranking metric (0–1) estimating probability a treatment is best.
- **MD (mean difference)**: difference in mean VAS pain vs placebo; more negative = greater pain reduction.
- **Novafen**: fixed-dose combination of ibuprofen + acetaminophen + caffeine.
- **VAS**: Visual Analog Scale for pain intensity.
- **RoB 2**: Cochrane risk-of-bias tool, version 2.
