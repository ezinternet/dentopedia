## One-line Summary
In vitro (42 molars, Clearfil SE Bond): thermal/mechanical cycling significantly reduces µTBS vs control (p<0.001); mechanical cycling effect significant from 100K cycles; combined cycling no additive effect at low cycle counts.

## 1. Document Information
Shahid Sadoughi University, Yazd, Iran. Dental Research Journal 2013. In vitro study evaluating fatigue effects on Clearfil SE Bond (2-step self-etch) to superficial dentin.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Demonstrates time/fatigue degradation of SE bond under simulated clinical loads
- Thermal cycling alone (5–55°C): significant bond reduction even at low cycle counts
- Mechanical cycling: significant effect begins at 100K cycles (50K not significant vs control without cycling)
- Combined 50K mechanical + 1K thermal: no additional effect vs 50K mechanical alone
- Failure mode shifts from adhesive → adhesive type with cycling (interface weakening)

## 3. Methodology
- 42 extracted molar teeth; flat dentinal surface; bonded with Clearfil SE Bond + Filtek Z250
- 7 groups: control + various combinations of thermal (1K, 2K cycles, 5–55°C) and mechanical (50K, 100K, 200K cycles, 125N, 0.5Hz) loading
- Specimens sectioned into hourglass shape; µTBS tested at 0.5mm/min
- SEM and stereomicroscope for failure mode analysis
- Statistics: 3-way ANOVA + T-test; p<0.05

## 4. Key Results
- All cycling groups: significantly lower µTBS than control (p<0.001)
- 50K mechanical cycles: not significantly different from 50K + 1K thermal
- Significant mechanical cycling effect threshold: **100K cycles**
- Predominant failure mode: adhesive type; frequency increases with cycling
- Clinical implication: SE bond durability decreases with oral function simulation

## 5. Limitations
- In vitro — alginate/lab cycling ≠ true oral environment
- Only Clearfil SE Bond tested — cannot generalize to other SE systems
- Superficial dentin only
- Short-term simulation relative to restoration lifespan

## 6. Related Work
- Tay & Pashley 2003 — conceptual framework for SE bond hydrophilicity and degradation
- Mortazavi 2012 — clinical RCT: SE Bond vs etch-and-rinse vs ethanol wet bonding
- Sharifian 2023 — µTBS E&R, SE, universal to superficial vs deep dentin

## 7. Glossary
- **µTBS**: Microtensile bond strength
- **Thermal cycling**: 5–55°C alternating temperature bath (simulates oral thermal stress)
- **Mechanical cycling**: Repetitive occlusal load simulation (125N, 0.5Hz)
- **Clearfil SE Bond**: 2-step self-etch adhesive (Kuraray); MDP-based primer + adhesive
- **Adhesive failure**: fracture at adhesive-dentin interface (vs cohesive = within resin/dentin)
