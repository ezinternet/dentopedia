## One-line Summary
CAD/CAM (zirconia, lithium disilicate) vs conventional SR+MA (13 studies, 1598 restorations): biological and technical complications NS; esthetic complications significantly higher in CAD/CAM (OR 16.88); survival rates comparable.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Cureus 2023, 15(4): e37888
- **DOI**: 10.7759/cureus.37888
- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane, Embase, Wiley Online; RCTs + cohort studies

## 2. Key Contributions
- 13 studies, 1598 restorations, 1161 patients; mean follow-up 3.6 years (range 1–9.3)
- Biological complications: OR 1.17 (0.64–2.17) — NS
- Technical complications: OR 1.14 (0.86–1.52) — NS
- **Esthetic complications: OR 16.88 (7.59–37.56) — significantly higher in CAD/CAM** (p<0.00001)
- Esthetic complication mainly = color mismatch, surface roughness after adjustment
- Survival and success rates comparable between CAD/CAM and conventional

## 3. Methodology
- **PICOS**: Adults requiring single crowns or FPDs; CAD/CAM (ZC or LD) vs conventional (metal-ceramic); biological/technical/esthetic outcomes
- **Study quality**: Cochrane RoB for RCTs; Newcastle-Ottawa Scale for cohorts
- **Meta-analysis**: Rev5 (Cochrane); random-effects

## 4. Key Results
| Outcome | OR | 95% CI | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Biological complications | 1.17 | 0.64–2.17 | NS |
| Technical complications | 1.14 | 0.86–1.52 | NS |
| Esthetic complications | **16.88** | **7.59–37.56** | **p<0.00001** |

## 5. Limitations
- Short mean follow-up (3.6 years); long-term data lacking
- Heterogeneous definitions of complications across studies
- Esthetic outcome definition varied (patient-reported vs clinician-rated)
