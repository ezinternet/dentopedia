## Why Ingested

A common defense of flossing's weak trial record is that participants simply floss badly — so "proper technique" should rescue efficacy. This study tests that assumption head-on by measuring whether instruction-improved flossing technique actually removes more plaque, directly extending the flossing-questioned theme in [[interdental-cleaning/min-2024-brushing-flossing-mouthrinsing-plaque-microbiota]] (where adding floss to brushing produced no detectable microbiome shift). It supplies a mechanistic counterpoint for the [[overviews/interdental-cleaning-devices-synthesis]] page.

## One-line Summary

Single-cohort pre/post intervention (n=37 young adults): a video instruction raised flossing technique score (FPS) from 2.0 to 2.83 (p<.001), but plaque removal did not improve (PSPI difference 0.17 vs 0.21, p=.112) and flossing performance was uncorrelated with plaque removed — suggesting even correct flossing does not substantially reduce interdental plaque.

## 한줄요약

단일 코호트 전후 중재연구(n=37, 젊은 성인): 동영상 교육 후 치실 술식 점수(Flossing Performance Score, FPS)는 2.0→2.83으로 유의하게 향상(p<.001)됐으나 치태 제거량은 차이가 없었고(PSPI 감소 0.17 vs 0.21, p=.112), 술식 숙련도와 치태 제거량 사이 상관도 없었다 — 올바른 치실 사용도 치간 치태를 의미 있게 줄이지 못한다는 근거.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Does proper flossing performance translate into effective plaque removal?
- **Authors**: Jung K, Böttge B, Kullmann M, Ganss C (Justus-Liebig-University Giessen, Germany)
- **Journal**: Clinical Oral Investigations 2025;29(9):438
- **DOI**: 10.1007/s00784-025-06505-z · **PMID**: 40906204 · **PMCID**: PMC12411584
- **Registration**: German clinical trials register DRKS00034902; Ethics ref. 63/22
- **Design**: Prospective single-cohort pre/post intervention (no control arm) — NOT an RCT
- **Population**: 37 university students (30F/7M; 23.1 ± 3.2 y), no dropouts

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Decouples flossing technique from cleaning outcome.** The central question is mechanistic: if poor application explains floss's weak trial record, then improved technique should yield better plaque removal. The study shows it does not.
2. **Objective dual measurement.** Flossing technique was scored from unobserved video (Flossing Performance Score, FPS), and plaque was quantified on aligned intraoral scans (Proximal Surface Plaque Index, PSPI) at four time points — sidestepping the subjective, dichotomous plaque indices used in older flossing studies.
3. **Null correlation finding.** No relationship between FPS and plaque removal, nor between flossing duration and plaque removal — instead, plaque level *before* flossing strongly predicted plaque *after* flossing.
4. Adds direct support to the body of evidence questioning floss efficacy, reframing the debate from "instruct better" toward "investigate alternative interdental approaches."

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Three appointments.** T0: screen + suspend interdental hygiene for 1 week. T1: habitual brushing → disclose plaque → intraoral scan 1 → videotape habitual flossing → scan 2 → watch instruction video → 1 week home practice. T2: videotape instructed flossing → scan 3 (before) and scan 4 (after).
- **FPS (flossing technique).** Coded dichotomously per interdental space on three criteria from the Flossing Dexterity Index: (a) interdental space reached (floss passed contact point), (b) correct adaptation (floss placed mesially and distally against tooth surfaces), (c) correct movement (vertical movements). Each is the fraction (0–1) of spaces meeting the criterion; summed to FPS (max 3 = perfect). 12 interdental spaces mesial/distal of Ramfjord teeth (16, 21, 24, 36, 41, 44) analyzed; flossing duration also coded.
- **PSPI (plaque).** Plaque disclosed (Mira-2-Ton), 3D intraoral scans (Carestream CS 3800) aligned in MeshLab; a fixed region of interest near each interdental space scored 0 (none) / 1 (<50%) / 2 (≥50%) on 4 areas per Ramfjord tooth (mesial+distal, oral+vestibular). PSPI = mean of all scores. Plaque removal = PSPI(before) − PSPI(after); higher = more removed.
- **Bias controls.** Video filmed behind a semi-transparent mirror with color-filter foil to hide red stains so participants weren't cued by visible plaque. Instruction video had achieved its effect in a prior study.
- **Statistics.** Non-parametric (Wilcoxon) due to non-Gaussian data; linear regression for FPS↔plaque associations. Values as median [95% CI], CIs bootstrapped (1000 samples), α=0.05. Sample size n=37 powered (effect size 0.48, α 0.05, power 0.80) on the *flossing-improvement* endpoint.
- **Reliability.** Inter-rater κ: reached 0.846, technique 0.663, adaptation 0.486; plaque-area scoring κ 0.679.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Technique improved markedly.** FPS 2.0 [1.48; 2.54] (habitual) → 2.83 [2.45; 2.95] (instructed), p<.001. Correct adaptation 0.42 → 0.92 (p<.001); vertical movements 0.64 → 1.00 (p=.012). Flossing time 60.3 s → 89.2 s (p<.001, ~7.5 s per interdental space). Most participants already reached all spaces at baseline; technique was the limiting component.
  - Residual deficits even after instruction: 8 subjects performed no vertical movement in any space; 5 achieved correct adaptation in <half of spaces.
- **Plaque removal did NOT improve.** PSPI difference (removal) 0.17 [0.04; 0.25] habitual vs 0.21 [0.13; 0.25] instructed, p=.112. Pre-floss PSPI ~1.29–1.33 (no difference between visits, p=.182). Habitual flossing reduced plaque ~13%; instructed ~16% — only ~3% more.
  - Site detail: a small but significant instruction benefit on buccal areas (0.08 → 0.17, p=.002) but none on oral areas (0.17 → 0.17, p=.332). Oral sites had higher plaque than buccal at all time points.
  - Flossing increased plaque-free areas and reduced score-2 (≥50%) areas (both p<.001) but did not change score-1 areas. Score-2 areas fell from 46% to 33%; only ~29% became plaque-free.
- **No correlation.** FPS not related to plaque removal or to post-floss PSPI. Duration not related (habitual R²=0.011, p=.544; instructed R²=0.001, p=.825). Instead, pre-floss PSPI strongly predicted post-floss PSPI.
- **Conclusion**: even a high level of flossing performance does not contribute to effective plaque reduction near the interdental surfaces — refuting the "inadequate-application" explanation for floss's weak efficacy.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Short-term observation with a single instruction; more frequent instruction / longer practice might widen effects.
- No control group (single cohort), small n=37, homogeneous young, mostly-female, highly-educated, well-motivated students — not generalizable to other ages or socioeconomic groups.
- No gingival-health outcomes measured; plaque is a surrogate, and interdental-space plaque can only be assessed indirectly via the tooth surface adjacent to the space.
- The ROI construction, though anatomically defined, is an arbitrary approximation.
- Authors note flossing may carry benefits independent of plaque mass (mechanical biofilm disruption, gingivitis effects) and call for research into alternative interdental movements, applied force, and direct interdental-space plaque quantification.

## 6. Related Work

- Systematic-review evidence cited by the authors: adding floss to brushing trended toward less plaque at 1 month (SMD −0.42, p=.06) but the benefit faded by 3 months and vanished by 6 months — and a network meta-analysis on gingival inflammation ranked floss near the bottom of 10 devices.
- Reinforces the flossing-questioned theme of [[interdental-cleaning/min-2024-brushing-flossing-mouthrinsing-plaque-microbiota]] (brushing + flossing did not shift the plaque microbiome vs brushing alone).
- Contextualizes device comparisons in [[interdental-cleaning/mancinelli-lyle-2024-water-flosser-vs-interdental-brush-rct]] and [[interdental-cleaning/thomassen-2025-airfloss-essential-oils-vs-floss-rct]].
- Feeds the cross-paper synthesis in [[overviews/interdental-cleaning-devices-synthesis]].

## 7. Glossary

- **FPS (Flossing Performance Score)**: 0–3 composite from % of interdental spaces (reached + correct adaptation + vertical movement); 3 = perfect.
- **PSPI (Proximal Surface Plaque Index)**: mean of 0/1/2 plaque-coverage scores on tooth surfaces adjacent to interdental spaces, read on intraoral scans.
- **Flossing Dexterity Index**: prior index from which the FPS criteria (reach, adaptation, movement) were drawn.
- **Ramfjord teeth**: standard six index teeth (16, 21, 24, 36, 41, 44) sampled to represent the whole dentition.
- **ROI (Region of Interest)**: fixed image area near the interdental space, transferred identically across the four aligned scans, where plaque was scored.
