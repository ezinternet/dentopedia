## Why Ingested

Provides the foundational large-n (300 implant) clinical evidence that CT bone density drives both insertion torque and ISQ, and that ISQ separates successful from failed implants — anchoring the bone-density → primary-stability link that [[implants/isq/chatvaratthana-2017-cortical-bone-thickness-isq-cbct]] and [[implants/di-stefano-2021-stability-dental-implants-cortical-bone]] later refine with cortical-bone specificity. Reinforces the success-threshold rungs of [[overviews/implants-isq-stability-ladder]].

## One-line Summary

Retrospective clinical study (300 Brånemark Mk III TiUnite implants, 111 patients) finding strong correlations between CT bone density (HU), insertion torque, and ISQ, with failed implants showing markedly lower values on all three parameters.

## 한줄요약

후향적 임상연구(임플란트 300개, 환자 111명)로, CT 골밀도(HU)·삽입토크·ISQ 사이에 강한 상관(r=0.76~0.88)을 확인했고, 실패한 임플란트는 세 지표 모두 현저히 낮았다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Influence of bone density on implant stability parameters and implant success: a retrospective clinical study
- **Authors**: Ilser Turkyilmaz (Univ. of Texas Health Science Center, San Antonio), Edwin A. McGlumphy (Ohio State University)
- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health 2008, 8:32
- **DOI**: 10.1186/1472-6831-8-32
- **Published**: 24 November 2008
- **Study type**: Retrospective clinical study
- **n**: 300 implants in 111 patients (55 F, 56 M; mean age 55 ± 11), placed 2003–2005, 3-year follow-up (avg 3.7 ± 0.7 yr)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies the relationship between **local bone density measured by CT in Hounsfield Units (HU)** and two primary-stability parameters: **insertion torque (Ncm)** and **resonance frequency analysis / ISQ**.
- Demonstrates that all three measures (HU, torque, ISQ) are significantly **lower in failed than in successful implants**, supporting bone density as a determinant of implant success.
- Reports site-specific bone density across the four jaw regions, consistent with prior CT density literature (Norton & Gamble; Shapurian et al.).
- Argues CT is a useful, objective preoperative tool to predict primary stability and modify treatment plans where bone quality is poor.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Patients/implants**: 111 patient files; 300 implants distributed as 100 anterior mandible, 60 posterior mandible, 70 anterior maxilla, 70 posterior maxilla. Inclusion: no uncontrolled medical conditions (e.g., diabetes), available for follow-up. Exclusion: uncontrolled diabetes, head/neck radiation, need for bone graft.
- **Bone density**: Spiral CT (Siemens Somatom AR-SP 40; 130 kV, 83 mA, 1 mm slice/interval). Surgical acrylic templates with 1 mm metal indicator rods. Mean density of the implant recipient area measured in HU via Siemens Somaris/4 software. Misch density bands cited: D1 >1250 HU, D2 850–1250, D3 350–850, D4 150–350, D5 <150 HU.
- **Implants**: 300 Brånemark Mk III TiUnite (NobelBiocare), one-stage technique, full-thickness mucoperiosteal flaps, saline irrigation, OsseoCare motor.
- **Insertion torque**: Max insertion torque recorded by OsseoCare motor, stepped from 20 Ncm in 5 Ncm increments; final values recorded at 20, 32, 45 Ncm.
- **RFA**: Osstell instrument, ISQ at implant placement and at 6- and 12-month follow-up (abutments removed for measurement).
- **Loading**: Conventional (3 or 6 mo) and early loading (1-, 6-, 8-week) protocols.
- **Success criteria**: Modified Albrektsson & Zarb — no peri-implant radiolucency, functioning as prosthesis anchor, confirmed stability, no suppuration/pain/pathology. Only implants failing **before prosthesis delivery** were counted as failures.
- **Statistics**: SPSS; non-parametric (Kolmogorov-Smirnov). Mann-Whitney U for group differences; Spearman's rho for correlations; p < 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Survival**: 20 of 300 implants lost in 15 patients → 6.7% failure (93.3% survival) over 3 years; no implant lost after prosthesis delivery.
- **All 300 implants at placement**: 620 ± 251 HU, 36.1 ± 8 Ncm, 65.7 ± 9 ISQ.
- **Correlations** (all p < 0.001): bone density ↔ insertion torque r = 0.768; bone density ↔ ISQ r = 0.882; insertion torque ↔ ISQ r = 0.764.
- **Successful (280) vs failed (20)** at placement (all p < 0.001):
  - Successful: 645 ± 240 HU, 37.2 ± 7 Ncm, 67.1 ± 7 ISQ
  - Failed: 267 ± 47 HU, 21.8 ± 4 Ncm, 46.5 ± 4 ISQ
- **ISQ over time (280 successful)**: 67.1 ± 7 at surgery → 66.9 ± 6 at 6 mo (slight decrease, p > 0.05) → 68.6 ± 7 at 12 mo (increase vs 6 mo, p < 0.001).
- **Site-specific bone density**: anterior mandible 846 ± 234 HU, posterior mandible 526 ± 107 HU, anterior maxilla 591 ± 176 HU, posterior maxilla 403 ± 95 HU. Overall range 199–1231 HU.
- **Sex differences (p < 0.001)**: females (145 implants) 542 ± 20 HU, 34.5 ± 8 Ncm, 64 ± 9 ISQ; males (155) 692 ± 271 HU, 37.6 ± 8 Ncm, 67.3 ± 8 ISQ.
- **Smoking**: smokers 11/121 lost (9.1%) vs non-smokers 9/179 (5.02%) — higher failure in smokers.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Retrospective design** — no randomization; confounding by mixed loading protocols (conventional + early) and pooled implant dimensions.
- Mixed loading protocols; only pre-prosthesis failures counted, so loading-related late failures were intentionally excluded.
- Single implant system (Brånemark Mk III TiUnite) limits generalizability to other surfaces/geometries.
- CT density values are scanner- and software-dependent (HU calibration varies between machines).
- Authors note laser Doppler flowmetry as an emerging alternative method for assessing bone quality/vascularity.

## 6. Related Work

- Turkyilmaz et al. (2006, J Oral Rehabil) — 158 implant sites, strong CT-density ↔ stability correlations (consistent prior work by same group).
- Norton & Gamble (2001) and Shapurian et al. (2006) — comparable site-specific CT bone density values.
- Degidi et al. (2006) — failed implants mean ISQ ~46 vs successful ~60 (consistent threshold pattern).
- Sjostrom et al. (2007) — successful 62.6 vs failed 54.9 ISQ at placement.
- Friberg et al. (1999) and Da Cunha et al. (2004) — torque ↔ RFA correlations in maxillary TiUnite implants.
- Misch (1999) — D1–D5 bone density classification used in this study.

## 7. Glossary

- **HU (Hounsfield Unit)**: CT-derived radiodensity scale (−1000 air to ~3000 enamel); used here to quantify local bone density.
- **Insertion / placement torque (Ncm)**: rotational resistance during implant insertion, an index of primary mechanical stability.
- **RFA (Resonance Frequency Analysis)**: non-invasive measurement of implant/bone interface stiffness via an oscillating transducer (Osstell).
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: 0–100 RFA output; higher = stiffer interface = higher stability.
- **D1–D5**: Misch bone-density classification (D1 >1250 HU … D5 <150 HU).
- **Primary stability**: mechanical implant fixation at placement, before osseointegration.
