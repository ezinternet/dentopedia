## One-line Summary

Open-access review arguing that chloride homeostasis in primary afferent (DRG/nociceptor) neurons — set by NKCC1/KCC2 and a large repertoire of Cl⁻ channels (GABA-A, glycine, anoctamin/TMEM16, bestrophin, CLC, CFTR, VRAC) — is a critical, underappreciated driver of nociceptor excitability and sensitization in neuropathic and inflammatory pain.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Contrasts spinal vs peripheral Cl⁻ physiology: in the dorsal horn Cl⁻ conductances are well studied, but DRG/primary afferents were neglected despite maintaining unusually **high intracellular Cl⁻**.
- Catalogs the full Cl⁻ channel repertoire of DRG neurons: ligand-gated GABA-A and glycine receptors; Ca²⁺-activated Cl⁻ channels (anoctamin/TMEM16, bestrophin, tweety-homolog); CLC channels/transporters; CFTR; and volume-regulated anion channels (VRAC).
- Explains the key paradox: because of high intracellular Cl⁻, opening the same Cl⁻ channels that hyperpolarize central neurons can **depolarize (excite)** sensory neurons — context-dependent on the actual Cl⁻ reversal potential and total efflux.
- Maps where specific Cl⁻ conductances act: signal transduction/amplification at the peripheral terminal, action-potential generation, and shaping of dorsal-horn synaptic transmission.
- Reviews how inflammatory mediators modulate Cl⁻ channels directly, via protein-protein interaction, or through signaling cascades.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Narrative mechanistic review of DRG-neuron chloride channel families and their regulation.
- Organized by channel family → expression in DRG → contribution to nociception/sensitization → modulation by inflammatory mediators.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- No quantitative pooled outcome (review).
- Central qualitative claim: Cl⁻ channels and the mediators modulating Cl⁻ flux are regulated in pain disorders and contribute to nociceptor excitation and sensitization — establishing Cl⁻ as a legitimate analgesic target in the periphery, not only centrally.
- Highlights anoctamin 1 (TMEM16A) as a prominent Ca²⁺-activated Cl⁻ channel in nociceptor excitation.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Mechanistic review; no clinical trial data and no orofacial-pain–specific evidence.
- Translational gap: peripheral Cl⁻–targeting analgesics remain largely preclinical.

## 7. Glossary

- DRG: dorsal root ganglion — houses primary afferent sensory neuron cell bodies.
- Anoctamin 1 (TMEM16A / Ano1): Ca²⁺-activated Cl⁻ channel implicated in nociceptor depolarization.
- VRAC: volume-regulated anion channel.
- CFTR: cystic fibrosis transmembrane conductance regulator (a Cl⁻ channel).
- Cl⁻ reversal potential: membrane voltage at which net Cl⁻ flux is zero; determines inhibitory vs excitatory effect of Cl⁻ channel opening.
