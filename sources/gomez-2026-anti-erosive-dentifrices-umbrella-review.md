## Why Ingested

Top-of-evidence-pyramid umbrella review (SR of SRs) answering *which* toothpaste chemistries actually protect against erosive tooth wear — stannous-containing formulations (stabilized SnF2 / multi-ion fluoride) come out ahead of conventional NaF/NaMFP, with calcium-based/biomimetic technologies still heterogeneous. This adds a prevention/management layer to [[wiki/dental-erosion/kanzow-2016-etiology-pathogenesis-dental-erosion]], which classifies ETW aetiology but leaves the dentifrice-choice question open. **Abstract-only — full text not retrieved (no PMC open access); all numbers below are from the published abstract.**

## One-line Summary

Umbrella review (8 SRs; PRISMA 2020, JBI, AMSTAR 2, ConQual): fluoridated > non-fluoridated dentifrices for enamel/dentin protection, with stannous-containing (SnF2 / multi-ion) formulations most consistently superior to NaF/NaMFP under erosive/erosive-abrasive protocols (certainty moderate for stannous, low/very-low for emerging tech), but evidence is dominated by in vitro/in situ short-term models — not proof of reduced clinical ETW.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Synthesizes and critically appraises the highest-level secondary evidence on anti-erosive dentifrice effectiveness on enamel and dentin, addressing the clinical-guidance gap created by heterogeneity across individual SRs.
- Eight systematic reviews met inclusion.
- Ranks chemistries: fluoridated > non-fluoridated overall; stannous-containing (stabilized SnF2, multi-ion fluoride systems) most consistently superior to conventional NaF/NaMFP comparators.
- Grades certainty (ConQual): **moderate** for stannous-based systems, **low or very low** for emerging (calcium-based / biomimetic) technologies.
- Foregrounds the key caveat: evidence is dominated by in vitro/in situ models with short follow-up; clinical ETW-specific patient-centered outcomes are scarce.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Umbrella review following **PRISMA 2020** and **Joanna Briggs Institute (JBI)** guidance.
- Searches in **PubMed, Embase, Scopus, Web of Science, Cochrane Library** up to **January 2026**.
- Inclusion: reviews evaluating **fluoridated and non-fluoridated** dentifrices under **in vitro, in situ, or clinical** conditions.
- Methodological quality assessed with **AMSTAR 2**; certainty of evidence assessed with **ConQual**.
- Findings synthesized **narratively** due to heterogeneity (no pooled meta-analysis across the included SRs).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Fluoridated vs non-fluoridated**: fluoridated dentifrices generally reduced enamel and dentin tissue loss vs non-fluoridated controls, predominantly in vitro and in situ.
- **Stannous formulations**: stabilized SnF2 and multi-ion fluoride systems most consistently showed greater protection than conventional NaF/NaMFP comparators under both erosive and erosive-abrasive protocols.
- **Calcium-based / biomimetic technologies**: heterogeneous and largely short-term experimental evidence.
- **Certainty (ConQual)**: moderate for stannous-based systems; low or very low for emerging technologies.
- **Translation to clinic**: clinical evidence is more established for **dentine hypersensitivity** outcomes than for ETW; ETW-specific patient-centered outcomes remain scarce.
- **Practice signal**: stannous-containing dentifrices may be preferred for patients at increased erosive risk, based on consistent experimental protection.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Evidence base dominated by **in vitro / in situ models with short follow-up** — should NOT be over-interpreted as proof of reduced clinical ETW progression.
- Heterogeneity among included SRs precluded quantitative pooling (narrative synthesis only).
- ETW-specific patient-reported outcomes are scarce.
- Authors call for **long-term, independently funded clinical trials** with standardized ETW and patient-reported outcomes.
- **Abstract-only ingest** — Methods/Results detail limited to abstract; per-SR effect sizes, AMSTAR 2 ratings, and overlap (CCA) not available here.

## 7. Glossary

- **ETW (Erosive Tooth Wear)** — irreversible, acid-related, non-bacterial loss of dental hard tissue.
- **SnF2 (Stannous fluoride)** — tin-containing fluoride; stabilized/multi-ion systems give the most consistent anti-erosive protection here.
- **NaF / NaMFP** — sodium fluoride / sodium monofluorophosphate; conventional fluoride comparators.
- **In situ** — intra-oral appliance-mounted specimens exposed to the real oral environment (between in vitro and clinical).
- **AMSTAR 2** — tool to appraise methodological quality of systematic reviews.
- **ConQual** — JBI approach to establishing confidence (certainty) in synthesized qualitative/aggregated evidence.
- **Umbrella review** — systematic review whose units of analysis are other systematic reviews/meta-analyses.
