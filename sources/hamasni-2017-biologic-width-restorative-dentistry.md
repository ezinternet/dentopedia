## Why Ingested

Provides the measured-dimension basis for the biologic-width concept that the perio-restorative batch relies on — it contrasts clinically measured biologic width with the textbook Gargiulo histologic mean (2.04 mm), directly informing how subgingival margins are positioned in [[wiki/periodontics/ercoli-2021-fixed-dental-prostheses-restorations-periodontium]] and the violation/correction approach in [[wiki/periodontics/alam-2024-supracrestal-tissue-attachment-biologic-width]].

## One-line Summary

Clinical study (42 patients, 504 sites/126 teeth, bone-sounding under anesthesia) finding mean clinical biologic width 1.13 mm (significantly less than Gargiulo's 2.04 mm histologic value) and mean sulcus depth 1.96 mm (significantly greater than 0.69 mm), arguing for site-specific rather than fixed-value margin planning.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Comparison of the Clinical Biological Width with the Published Standard Histologic Mean Values
- **Authors**: Fatme Mouchref Hamasni, Fady El Hajj (Dept. of Periodontology, Lebanese University, Hadat, Lebanon)
- **Journal**: Journal of International Society of Preventive and Community Dentistry 2017;7(5):264–271 (Sep–Oct 2017)
- **DOI**: 10.4103/jispcd.JISPCD_261_17
- **Study type**: Narrative-review-framed clinical measurement study (pilot, cross-sectional clinical measurement)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Measures *clinical* biologic width (BW) in living patients via bone sounding and compares it directly with the classic Gargiulo et al. histologic cadaver means (BW 2.04 mm, SD 0.69 mm).
- Finds clinical BW significantly lower (1.13 mm) and clinical SD significantly higher (1.96 mm) than the textbook histologic values.
- Argues a single "magic number" for BW should not be used as a treatment objective; recommends a patient/site-specific margin-to-crest distance when restoring subgingivally fractured/carious teeth.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 42 systemically healthy adults (21 M / 21 F), age 20–50 (mean 27.26), full-mouth bleeding and plaque scores <20%.
- 504 tooth sites on 126 teeth measured by two periodontists; sites on teeth already requiring infiltration anesthesia for adjacent procedures (no extra ethical burden).
- At 4 sites/tooth (mesial, midbuccal, distal, midlingual/palatal): sulcus depth (SD) and gingival-margin-to-bone-crest distance measured; clinical BW = (GM→bone crest) − SD.
- Statistics: Friedman ANOVA, independent-samples t-tests, one-sample t-tests (SPSS 20.0).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Mean clinical BW = 1.13 mm (significantly < Gargiulo 2.04 mm, p<0.05).
- Mean clinical SD = 1.96 mm (significantly > Gargiulo 0.69 mm, p<0.05).
- Conclusion: use the term *clinical biologic width* and create a site-specific margin-to-crest distance to preserve stable, healthy periodontal tissues, especially during crown lengthening.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Pilot study, single center / population (Lebanese), modest sample; bone sounding has measurement error vs histology.
- Clinical BW derived by subtraction (GM→crest − SD), propagating two measurement errors.
- Cross-sectional — no outcome follow-up on restored teeth.

## 6. Related Work

- Gargiulo et al. (histologic dentogingival junction, BW 2.04 mm); Vacek et al. (non-decalcified blocks, BW 1.91 mm); Waerhaug; Nevins & Skurow (limit subgingival extension 0.5–1 mm); Günay et al.; Kosyfaki et al. and Schmidt et al. systematic reviews.

## 7. Glossary

- **Biologic width (BW)**: per Cohen, the supracrestal gingival tissue between sulcus base and alveolar crest = junctional epithelium + connective tissue attachment.
- **Bone sounding**: probing to the alveolar crest under anesthesia to measure soft-tissue dimensions clinically.
- **Sulcus depth (SD)**: probing depth from gingival margin to base of the gingival crevice.
