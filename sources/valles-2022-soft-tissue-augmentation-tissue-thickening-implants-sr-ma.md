## Why Ingested

연조직 두께 증가(Soft Tissue Thickening, STT)에 특화된 SR+MA가 부족한 상황에서, 기존 [[wiki/implants/keratinized-mucosa-peri-implant-health-overview]]는 KM 폭(Keratinized Mucosa Width) 중심으로 서술되어 있어 buccal STT gain의 정량적 근거가 취약하다. 본 Valles 2022 SR+MA는 CTG 대비 무처치·대체재(XCM/ADM/PRF)를 두 개의 PICOS로 분리해 STT WMD를 직접 제시하므로, 개요 페이지의 STT 섹션을 보강하는 핵심 근거로 활용된다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 16 RCTs/CCTs (n=446 patients) showing CTG yields +0.64 mm STT gain vs no graft and +0.51 mm vs soft-tissue substitutes around dental implants, with better esthetic scores but no MBL benefit over substitutes.

## 한줄요약

16개 RCT/CCT(446명) SR+MA: 임플란트 주위 연조직 두께 증가에서 CTG가 무처치 대비 +0.64 mm, 연조직 대체재 대비 +0.51 mm 우월한 STT를 제공하며 심미 점수도 유의하게 높으나, 변연골 소실(MBL)에서는 대체재와 차이 없음.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First SR+MA focused exclusively on **peri-implant soft tissue thickening (STT)** — separating this endpoint from keratinized mucosa width studies.
2. Two PICOS questions answered separately: CTG vs no graft (FQ1) and CTG vs soft-tissue substitutes (FQ2).
3. Confirms that STT gain from CTG exceeds soft tissue substitutes by a clinically meaningful margin (WMD +0.51 mm), and that greater long-term STT benefit is seen at ≥1-year follow-up vs <1 year.
4. Demonstrates CTG superiority for pink esthetic score (PES) and soft-tissue margin stability (less recession) over substitutes, while MBL, PPD, plaque, and bleeding indices show no significant differences between techniques.
5. Identifies that medication intake (analgesic requirements) is significantly higher with CTG vs substitutes (WMD +1.68 tablets; p<0.001), the main disadvantage of the autogenous approach.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Design**: Systematic review + meta-analysis; PRISMA-compliant; random-effects model (REML).

**PICOS 1** — CTG vs no soft-tissue graft (no STG):
- Included studies: 8 trials (9 articles; Puzio 2018/2020 same cohort), n=254 patients, 297 implants
- Study types: 7 RCTs (parallel and split-mouth) + 1 CCT
- Follow-up: 3–60 months (mean 19.7 ±18.6 months)
- Included studies: Frizzera 2019, Hosseini 2020, Jiang 2020, Migliorati 2015, Papapetros 2019, Puzio 2018/2020, van Nimwegen 2018, Wiesner 2010

**PICOS 2** — CTG vs soft-tissue substitutes:
- Included studies: 8 trials, n=192 patients
- Substitutes tested: collagen matrix (XCM; Geistlich Mucograft), acellular dermal matrix (ADM), platelet-rich fibrin (PRF), titanium-PRF
- Included studies: Cairo 2017, Frizzera 2019, Hutton 2018, Kamal 2020, Puzio 2018/2020, Schmitt 2020, Thoma 2020, Ustaoğlu 2020

**STT measurement methods**: STL digital superimposition, transmucosal probing with endodontic file, ultrasonic device (Pirop), periodontal probe

**Subgroup analyses**: timing of implant placement (immediate vs delayed), follow-up duration (<1 year vs ≥1 year), timing of soft-tissue augmentation (simultaneous vs staged)

**Statistical**: WMD with 95% CI and 95% prediction interval (PI); I² heterogeneity; Begg's test for publication bias; leave-one-out sensitivity analysis

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### PICOS 1: CTG vs No Graft — STT (Primary Outcome)

| Comparison | n studies | WMD (mm) | 95% CI | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| CTG vs no graft (STT gain) | 8 | +0.64 | [0.16; 1.13] | 0.01 |
| CTG vs no graft (recession) | 4 | +0.50 less recession | [0.19; 0.80] | <0.001 |

- High heterogeneity (I²=78.6%) for STT; no significant subgroup effect by timing or protocol
- MBL: no significant difference between CTG and no graft

### PICOS 2: CTG vs Soft-Tissue Substitutes — STT (Primary Outcome)

| Comparison | n studies | WMD (mm) | 95% CI | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| CTG vs substitutes (STT gain) | 8 | +0.51 | [0.28; 0.75] | <0.001 |
| CTG vs substitutes (PES) | 3 | +1.02 | [0.29; 1.74] | 0.01 |
| CTG vs substitutes (recession) | 2 | +0.50 less recession | [0.10; 0.89] | 0.014 |
| CTG vs substitutes (MBL) | 4 | 0.10 | [−0.02; 0.23] | 0.11 (NS) |
| CTG vs substitutes (medication intake) | 2 | +1.68 tablets | [1.30; 2.07] | <0.001 |

- STT benefit of CTG over substitutes greater at ≥1-year follow-up (significant subgroup difference)
- No significant heterogeneity for MBL (I²=0%)
- Publication bias not detected (Begg's t=0.05; p=1)
- Sensitivity analysis: exclusion of any single study did not alter results

### STT threshold context (from Introduction)
- ≥2 mm STT recommended to minimize implant/prosthetic transparency through peri-implant mucosa
- Thicker STT associated with reduced mid-buccal recession risk, especially after immediate implant placement

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- High heterogeneity in PICOS 1 STT analysis (I²=78.6%); partially attributable to differences in STT measurement methodology and implant placement timing
- Short follow-up in several studies (minimum 3 months); long-term data sparse (only Hosseini 2020 at 60 months)
- Search restricted to studies published in English only
- Considerable variation in STT measurement technique (STL, probing, ultrasound) — not fully standardized
- Only 3 studies provided PES data for PICOS 2 meta-analysis — esthetic conclusions somewhat limited
- No study reported volumetric shrinkage of grafts over time
- Need for future standardized RCTs with ≥12 months follow-up, standardized STT measurement, and validated esthetic indices

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| STT (Soft Tissue Thickness) | Horizontal buccal mucosal thickness at implant site; measured in mm |
| CTG (Connective Tissue Graft) | Autogenous subepithelial graft harvested from palate/tuberosity; gold standard |
| XCM (Xenogeneic Collagen Matrix) | Porcine-derived collagen matrix (e.g., Geistlich Mucograft); most common substitute tested |
| ADM (Acellular Dermal Matrix) | Allogeneic dermal graft stripped of cells; alternative to CTG |
| PRF (Platelet-Rich Fibrin) | Autologous fibrin membrane derived from centrifuged blood |
| WMD | Weighted Mean Difference — primary meta-analytic effect size |
| PES (Pink Esthetic Score) | Standardized index for peri-implant soft tissue esthetics (Fürhauser 2005); max 14 |
| MBL (Marginal Bone Level) | Crestal bone height adjacent to implant, measured radiographically |
| PICOS | Population–Intervention–Comparison–Outcome–Study design framework |
| NSTG | No soft tissue graft (control group in PICOS 1) |
