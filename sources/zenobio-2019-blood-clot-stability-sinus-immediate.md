## One-line Summary
Controlled clinical pilot (10 patients, 10 implants): lateral-window maxillary sinus lift with immediate implant placement and no grafting; 15-day vs 180-day CBCT showed predictable but contracted bone neoformation (mesial 38%, apical 43%, distal 35% height reduction) and 16.52% ± 8.60% blood-clot volume contraction. Bone formed reliably around all implants.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Cranio-Maxillofacial Surgery 2019;47(11):1803–1808
- **Type**: Controlled clinical pilot study, in vivo human
- **Imaging**: CBCT at 15 days (T1) and 180 days (T2)
- **Software**: OsirixMD for measurements; Shapiro-Wilk, paired Student's t-test

## 2. Key Contributions
- Quantitative human CBCT evidence that blood-clot space maintained by an immediate implant under an elevated sinus membrane can yield reliable bone neoformation without graft material.
- Reports the magnitude of clot/bone height contraction at three reference points (mesial, apical, distal) over a 6-month window.
- Validates lateral-window sinus lift + immediate implant + collagen-membrane window closure as a feasible graftless protocol for residual bone heights 4–7 mm.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Ten patients, residual crestal bone height ≥4 and ≤7 mm in maxillary premolar or 1st/2nd molar sites.
- Lateral-window approach; sinus membrane elevated; implant placed simultaneously to act as a "tent pole" maintaining the elevated space.
- Window closed with resorbable bovine collagen membrane (Bio-Gide, Geistlich).
- No graft material placed.
- T1 (15 days) and T2 (180 days) CBCT compared for clot height, new-bone height, and volume.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Mesial bone-clot height**: 4.77 mm at T1 → 2.95 mm new bone at T2 (38% contraction).
- **Apical bone-clot height**: 0.77 mm at T1 → 0.44 mm new bone at T2 (43% contraction).
- **Distal bone-clot height**: 5.30 mm at T1 → 3.45 mm new bone at T2 (35% contraction).
- **Height changes T1 vs T2**: statistically significant, p < 0.05.
- **Volume T1**: mean 0.90 cm³, SD ± 0.60 cm³.
- **Volume T2**: mean 0.75 cm³, SD ± 0.62 cm³ — significant contraction (p < 0.005).
- **Mean blood-clot volume contraction**: 16.52% ± 8.60%.
- All implants showed consistent bone formation.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small sample (n = 10), pilot design.
- 6-month follow-up only; long-term prognosis of implants in graftless sinus lift unknown.
- CBCT is radiographic — histology not available.
- No control arm with grafting; cannot directly compare to grafted lateral sinus floor elevation (LSFE).
- Authors call for longitudinal studies stratifying by prosthetic loading.

## 6. Related Work
- Lundgren 2004 — original graftless lateral sinus lift concept.
- Lin 2011 — sinus tent-pole concept with immediate implants.
- Pjetursson 2008 — sinus elevation outcomes meta-analysis.
- Wen 2023 — long-term LSFE in patients with sinus floor defects.

## 7. Glossary
- **MSL (Maxillary Sinus Lift)**: lateral or transcrestal elevation of the Schneiderian membrane.
- **Lateral window approach**: surgical access through the lateral maxillary wall.
- **Schneiderian membrane**: thin mucoperiosteum lining the maxillary sinus.
- **CBCT (Cone Beam Computed Tomography)**: low-dose 3D dental imaging.
- **Graftless sinus lift**: blood clot only, no biomaterial filler — implant maintains the space.
