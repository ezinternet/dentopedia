## One-line Summary
In-vitro L929/MTT: all 3 dual-cure self-adhesive cements ↓viability; light-cure < auto-cure cytotoxicity; SpeedCem Plus least, then TheraCem Ca.

## 1. Document Information
J Prosthet Dent 2025; 134(1): 178.e1–178.e7. PII S0022-3913(25)00208-2. PMID 40118683. Published online 2025-03-20. Akdeniz University, Türkiye.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Shows polymerization mode (light vs auto) is a major cytotoxicity determinant.
- Comparative biocompatibility ranking incl. TheraCem Ca.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
In-vitro. L929 fibroblasts; MTT assay. Cements: Calibra Universal, SpeedCem Plus, TheraCem Ca. Light vs autopolymerization; 48-h DMEM extracts; multiple dilutions/incubation times.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
All cements significantly ↓cell viability. Light-cured viability > autopolymerized. Least cytotoxic: light-cured SpeedCem Plus, then TheraCem Ca. Composition + activation mode both matter.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
In-vitro single-cell-line model; extract-based MTT; clinical pulp-response correlation needed.

## 6. Related Work
Self-adhesive cement cytotoxicity literature; polymerization degree-of-conversion studies.

## 7. Glossary
- MTT assay: tetrazolium-based cell-viability test.
- L929: murine fibroblast cell line for cytotoxicity testing.
- Dual-polymerizing: cement curable by light and/or self-cure.
