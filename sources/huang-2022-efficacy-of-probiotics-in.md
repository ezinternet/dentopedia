## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 7 RCTs (n=23–68) showing probiotics significantly reduce organoleptic scores and VSC levels in the short term (≤4 weeks) but only organoleptic scores in the long term (>4 weeks).

## 한줄요약

7개 RCT를 포함한 SR+MA로, 프로바이오틱스가 단기(≤4주)에 관능검사 점수와 VSC 농도를 유의하게 낮추나 장기(>4주)에는 관능검사 점수만 유의하게 개선함.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First meta-analysis to stratify probiotic efficacy on halitosis by follow-up duration (short-term ≤4 weeks vs long-term >4 weeks).
2. Demonstrated significant short-term reductions in both OLP scores (SMD=−0.58, p<0.0001) and total VSC (SMD=−0.26, p=0.04).
3. Showed that VSC reduction is not sustained long-term — only OLP scores remain significantly improved (SMD=−0.45, p=0.03) beyond 4 weeks.
4. Identified key probiotic strains studied: *Lactobacillus salivarius*, *L. reuteri*, *Streptococcus salivarius* K12, *Weissella cibaria*.
5. Found no significant effect on secondary outcomes (tongue coating score, plaque index).
6. Confirmed no publication bias via Egger's test and funnel plots; leave-one-out sensitivity analysis showed stable pooled estimates.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Study design**: Systematic review and meta-analysis; PRISMA guidelines; RCTs only.

**PICOS**:
- Population: Systemically healthy patients diagnosed with halitosis (OLP score and/or VSC concentration)
- Intervention: Any probiotic therapy (any species, any delivery vehicle)
- Comparison: Placebo
- Outcomes: Primary — OLP scores, VSC concentrations; Secondary — tongue coating scores (TCS), plaque index (PI)
- Study type: RCTs and randomized crossover trials

**Included studies (7 RCTs)**:
| Study | n | Strains | Vehicle | Follow-up |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mousquer et al | 29 | *L. salivarius* G60 (1×10⁹ CFU) | Chewing gum | 2 weeks |
| Lee et al | 68 | *W. cibaria* (1×10⁸ CFU/g) | Tablet | 4, 8 weeks |
| He et al | 28 | *S. salivarius* K12 (1×10⁹ CFU) | Tablet | 4 weeks |
| Keller et al | 25 | *L. reuteri* DSM 17938 + ATCC PTA 5289 (1×10⁸ CFU each) | Chewing gum | 2 weeks |
| Suzuki et al | 23 | *L. salivarius* WB21 (6.7×10⁸ CFU) + xylitol | Tablet | 2 weeks |
| Penala et al | 29 | *L. salivarius* (2×10⁹) + *L. reuteri* (2×10⁹ CFU) | Rinse capsule | 4, 12 weeks |
| Kim et al | 58 | *W. cibaria* CMU (1×10⁸ CFU) | Powder | 2, 4, 8 weeks |

**Risk of bias (RoB2)**: 1 low risk, 5 some concerns, 1 high risk (incomplete OLP data).

**Statistical approach**:
- SMD + 95% CI (random-effects model)
- Heterogeneity: Q test + I² (threshold: I²>50% or p<0.10)
- Subgroup analysis: VSC subgroups (H₂S, CH₃SH, C₂H₆S)
- Publication bias: funnel plot + Egger's test
- Sensitivity: leave-one-out analysis
- Time stratification: short-term (≤4 weeks) vs long-term (>4 weeks)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Short-term (≤4 weeks)**:
- OLP scores: SMD=−0.58 (95% CI −0.87 to −0.30), p<0.0001 — significant reduction ✓
- VSC total: SMD=−0.26 (95% CI −0.51 to −0.01), p=0.04 — significant reduction ✓
- H₂S subgroup: SMD=−0.73 (95% CI −1.36 to −0.10), p=0.02 — significant; I²=75% (high heterogeneity)
- CH₃SH, C₂H₆S: not significant
- TCS: not significant
- PI: not significant

**Long-term (>4 weeks)**:
- OLP scores: SMD=−0.45 (95% CI −0.85 to −0.04), p=0.03 — significant reduction ✓
- VSC concentrations: not significant; I²=58% (substantial heterogeneity)

**Publication bias**: No evidence (Egger's test p>0.05); funnel plots symmetric.

**Sensitivity**: Leave-one-out confirmed stable pooled estimates.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 7 RCTs included with small sample sizes (n=23–68); results may lack power
- Heterogeneity in probiotic strains, vehicles, doses, and delivery frequencies limits direct comparison
- Different VSC measurement methods (Halimeter vs OralChroma) may contribute to heterogeneity
- Reporting forms varied across studies, increasing analytical heterogeneity
- Most studies had some concerns regarding risk of bias
- Long-term data insufficient — only 2 studies contributed to >4 week VSC analysis
- Cannot determine which specific strain is most effective for halitosis

**Future work**: Larger RCTs with standardized probiotic strains, doses, and follow-up protocols; head-to-head comparisons; mechanistic studies on competitive exclusion of VSC-producing bacteria.

## 7. Glossary

- **OLP (Organoleptic score)**: Subjective sensory assessment of breath odor by trained evaluators using the Rosenberg 0–5 scale; considered gold standard
- **VSC (Volatile Sulfur Compounds)**: H₂S, CH₃SH, C₂H₆S — primary chemical markers of halitosis produced by anaerobic oral bacteria
- **Halimeter**: Device measuring total VSC concentration in parts per billion (ppb) from exhaled mouth air
- **OralChroma**: Gas chromatography device measuring individual VSC components
- **SMD (Standardised Mean Difference)**: Effect size measure for continuous outcomes across studies with different scales
- **CFU**: Colony-forming units — measure of viable probiotic bacteria in a dose
- **RoB2**: Revised Cochrane risk-of-bias tool for randomized trials
- **TCS (Tongue Coating Score)**: Semi-quantitative assessment of tongue coating thickness/coverage
- **PI (Plaque Index)**: Quantitative measure of dental plaque accumulation
