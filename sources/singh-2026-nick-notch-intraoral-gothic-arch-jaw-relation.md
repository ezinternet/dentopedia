## Why Ingested

무치악 환자의 중심위 기록(jaw relation recording) 기법 선택 근거를 보강하기 위해 인제스트. 기존 [[wiki/complete-denture/fayad-2025-vertical-dimension-determination-prosthodontics-review]]가 수직고경(OVD/VDO) 결정에 집중했다면, 본 임상연구는 그 다음 단계인 수평적 악간관계(centric/protrusive relation) 기록 정확도를 nick-and-notch(정적 왁스 기록) vs 구내·구외 고딕아치 추적(기능적 그래픽 기록) 3방법으로 split-cast 검증해 정량 비교한다. 고딕아치 추적이 교합 안정성·기능적 위치 결정에서 갖는 위치를 다룬 [[wiki/occlusion/kwak-2024-masticatory-positioning-occlusal-stability-full-mouth]]의 맥락을 무치악 보철로 확장한다.

According to PubMed, the source article is "Jaw relation recording using nick and notch technique with intraoral gothic arch tracing in completely edentulous patients: A clinical study" (Bioinformation, 2026; PMID 42282410; PMCID PMC13252300), [DOI](https://doi.org/10.6026/973206300222521).

## One-line Summary

Within-subject clinical comparative study (n=20 edentulous, ages 45-60); split-cast verification on a semi-adjustable articulator showed Nick and Notch wax registration gave the lowest jaw-relation discrepancy (centric 0.15 mm, protrusive 0.23 mm), intraoral Gothic arch tracing intermediate (0.42 / 0.51 mm), and extraoral Gothic arch tracing the highest (0.74 / 0.86 mm); all pairwise differences significant (p<0.001).

## 한줄요약

무치악 환자 20명(45-60세) 자가대조 임상비교연구. 반조절성 교합기 split-cast 검증에서 nick-and-notch 왁스 기록이 악간관계 오차가 가장 작았고(중심위 0.15 mm·전방위 0.23 mm), 구내 고딕아치 추적이 중간(0.42/0.51 mm), 구외 고딕아치 추적이 가장 컸다(0.74/0.86 mm). 모든 쌍별 차이 유의(p<0.001), 전방위 오차가 중심위보다 일관되게 큼.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides a rare **three-way** head-to-head comparison of centric/protrusive jaw-relation recording methods in fully edentulous patients — Nick and Notch (static wax), intraoral Gothic arch tracing, and extraoral Gothic arch tracing — verified by **split-cast mounting** as the standard.
- Establishes a clear accuracy hierarchy at both centric and protrusive positions: **Nick and Notch < intraoral Gothic arch < extraoral Gothic arch** in discrepancy (smaller = more consistent).
- Quantifies the magnitudes against a clinical-acceptability frame: Nick and Notch within acceptable range; intraoral manageable with routine remounting/occlusal adjustment; extraoral potentially clinically perceptible and requiring larger correction.
- Confirms a biomechanical principle for intraoral superiority over extraoral: proximity of the recording plate to the transverse hinge axis reduces error magnification.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: clinical comparative study, single prosthodontics department, 6-month period; each patient acts as own control (within-subject, 3 records each → 60 records total).
- **Sample**: 20 fully edentulous patients, 45-60 years, both sexes, adequate mouth opening and good ridges; stratified random sampling. Excluded: compromised systemic health, TMJ disorders, severely resorbed ridges, inability/unwillingness to open mouth.
- **Standardization**: heat-cured acrylic record bases + wax occlusion rims; **plaster index duplication** to produce three identical record bases (one per technique) and identical occlusion rims per patient.
- **Mounting**: arbitrary face-bow → Hanau Wide-Vue semi-adjustable articulator; maxillary cast mounted via face-bow; mandibular cast first mounted with the **Nick and Notch centric record as the reference**.
- **Recording techniques**:
  - *Nick and Notch*: V-notches on rims, retruded closure, ZOE paste records; recorded 5x, most-repeated chosen.
  - *Extraoral Gothic arch*: upper bearing plate + forward lower tracing platform, lamp-black tracing, apex marked, type III stone records.
  - *Intraoral Gothic arch*: intraoral tracing device on rims, bearing pin to correct VD, inked upper plate, apex traced, type III stone records.
- **Verification**: split-cast — 3 reference points (anterior to incisive papilla; two posterolateral 10 mm anterior to posterior tuberosity borders); each technique's interocclusal record interposed, articulator closed, discrepancy measured with digital Vernier caliper (0.01 mm).
- **Outcomes**: RLD, LLD, AD, and overall (mean of the three) at centric and protrusive.
- **Statistics**: SPSS 22.0; Shapiro-Wilk, Levene; one-way ANOVA + Tukey HSD; p<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Centric position — overall mean discrepancy**
- Nick and Notch: **0.15 ± 0.03 mm**
- Intraoral Gothic arch: **0.42 ± 0.10 mm**
- Extraoral Gothic arch: **0.74 ± 0.12 mm**
- Overall ANOVA F = 200.90, p < 0.001 (RLD F=162.50, LLD F=83.27, AD F=91.21; all p<0.001)

**Protrusive position — overall mean discrepancy**
- Nick and Notch: **0.23 ± 0.08 mm**
- Intraoral Gothic arch: **0.51 ± 0.13 mm**
- Extraoral Gothic arch: **0.86 ± 0.14 mm**
- Overall ANOVA F = 146.70, p < 0.001 (RLD F=99.94, LLD F=53.13, AD F=53.20; all p<0.001)

- **Tukey post hoc**: all pairwise differences significant at both positions. Extraoral > intraoral > Nick and Notch in discrepancy at every site.
- Protrusive discrepancies consistently exceed centric for all three methods (eccentric registration is biomechanically more complex — bilateral condylar translation).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample (n=20); sufficient for significance but limited generalizability.
- Age restricted to 45-60 years — excludes older patients with the most challenging edentulous conditions.
- Single operator → possible operator bias, no inter-operator variability captured.
- Split-cast measures discrepancy in essentially one plane; may not capture full 3D positional error.
- Future: larger multi-operator samples, 3D analysis, correlation with clinical denture outcomes.

## 7. Glossary

- **Centric relation (CR)**: reproducible, bone-braced maxillomandibular reference position with condyles in the most anterior-superior position against the articular eminences.
- **Nick and Notch technique**: static interocclusal wax/ZOE registration recorded in habitual retruded closure.
- **Gothic arch tracing**: functional graphic technique; lateral excursions trace an arrowhead whose apex marks centric relation. Intraoral (device in mouth, near hinge axis) vs extraoral (forward-projecting platform).
- **Split-cast mounting**: technique to verify interocclusal-record accuracy by measuring separation between split cast surfaces when records are reseated.
- **RLD / LLD / AD**: right-lateral / left-lateral / anterior discrepancy reference-point measurements.
- **Protrusive record**: eccentric jaw record involving bilateral condylar translation.
