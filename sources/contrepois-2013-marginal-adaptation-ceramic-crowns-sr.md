## Why Ingested

Goodacre 2001 [[prosthetic-materials/goodacre-2001-tooth-preparations-complete-crowns-art-form]] concluded that finish-line type had no proven effect on marginal fit, but that predated all-ceramic CAD-CAM systems. This Contrepois 2013 SR (54 studies, 17 ceramic systems) re-opens and refines that conclusion for the ceramic era, identifying finish-line configuration, cement-space setting, veneering, and cementation as the real drivers of marginal adaptation — directly extending the prep-margin discussion that anchors [[overviews/crown-preparation-geometry-convergence-resistance-retention]].

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (J Prosthet Dent 2013;110(6):447-454; 469→54 studies, 17 ceramic systems, 48 in vitro + 6 in vivo, 1994-2012): 94.9% of measured marginal gaps were within clinical acceptability; heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis/ranking; four factors govern marginal adaptation — finish-line configuration, predefined cement space, veneering process, and cementation.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Systematic review (no meta-analysis) — J Prosthet Dent, Vol 110 Issue 6, December 2013, pp 447-454 (+ e10 online tables)
- **Authors**: Mathieu Contrepois, Arnaud Soenen, Michel Bartala, Odile Laviole (Bordeaux, France)
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.prosdent.2013.04.003 (article ID S0022-3913(13)00186-8)
- **Question**: What is the marginal fit of ceramic crowns across fabrication systems, and which factors influence marginal adaptation?

## 2. Key Contributions

- Pools the marginal-adaptation literature on **17 ceramic systems** (54 studies, 1994-2012) into one appraisal.
- Reports **94.9% of measured marginal gaps within clinical acceptability** (commonly cited ≤120 µm threshold per McLean & von Fraunhofer).
- Identifies **four factors** influencing marginal adaptation: finish-line configuration, value of the predefined cementing (cement) space, veneering process, and cementation.
- Concludes systems generally provide clinically acceptable fit but **cannot be ranked** against each other due to heterogeneity.
- Recommends **computer x-ray microtomography (micro-CT)** for future, non-destructive 3D marginal-gap research.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Electronic search of **PubMed and Scopus**: (discrepancy OR fit OR gaps OR adaptation) AND (disilicate OR ceramic) AND (copings OR crowns).
- Limited to English-language peer-reviewed articles published **before April 15, 2012**.
- Titles/abstracts screened against predefined inclusion criteria.
- **469 identified → 54 included** (all 1994-2012); **48 in vitro, 6 in vivo**.
- No meta-analysis performed — study heterogeneity (measurement methods, definitions of "marginal gap," cementation status) prevented pooling and system ranking.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **94.9%** of all measured marginal gaps fell within clinical acceptability.
- **17 ceramic systems** tested across the 54 studies.
- Four influential parameters:
  1. **Finish-line configuration** — geometry of the cervical margin affects measured gap.
  2. **Predefined cement space** — the internal/cement-space setting (die spacer / CAD parameter) influences marginal seating.
  3. **Veneering process** — application/firing of veneering porcelain can distort the coping margin.
  4. **Cementation** — luting agent and technique alter the final marginal gap.
- Cross-study comparison/ranking of systems was **not possible**.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Predominantly **in vitro** (48/54) — laboratory marginal gaps may not reflect intraoral, loaded, aged conditions.
- **No meta-analysis**: heterogeneous measurement protocols (number/location of measurement points, sectioning vs replica vs direct microscopy) and inconsistent "marginal gap" definitions.
- Search closed April 2012 — predates much current monolithic-zirconia and chairside-CAD-CAM data.
- Authors call for **micro-CT** to standardize non-destructive 3D marginal evaluation.

## 6. Related Work

- Extends Goodacre 2001's "finish-line type does not affect marginal fit" by showing finish-line configuration IS one of four marginal-adaptation factors in ceramic systems.
- Connects to in-vitro convergence/marginal-seating work (Vinnakota 2015, Kharat 2015) where TOC and seating interact.
- Relevant to contemporary ceramic-restoration marginal/longevity data (Warreth 2020, Miyazaki 2013 reviews; Lolos 2025 retrospective).

## 7. Glossary

- **Marginal adaptation / marginal fit**: closeness of the restoration margin to the prepared finish line; quantified as the marginal gap.
- **Marginal gap**: vertical/horizontal/absolute discrepancy between crown margin and tooth finish line (µm). Clinical acceptability often cited as ≤120 µm.
- **Cement space (die spacer / internal relief)**: the predefined gap left for luting agent; a CAD parameter that affects seating and marginal gap.
- **Veneering process**: layering/firing of veneering porcelain onto a coping/framework, which can introduce marginal distortion.
- **Micro-CT (computer x-ray microtomography)**: non-destructive 3D imaging recommended for marginal-gap measurement.
