## Why Ingested

Black stain (BS) is a clinical phenotype that appears caries-protective but whose microbial basis is poorly characterized. This 16S rRNA cross-sectional study in caries-free children directly characterizes the BS plaque and saliva microbiome, providing the molecular evidence base for [[caries/zyla-2015-black-stain-dental-caries-review]] (if present) or any overview on black stain's anti-caries mechanism. Actinomyces enrichment in BS+ plaque here serves as a candidate keystone taxon for future mechanistic work.

## One-line Summary

16S rRNA cross-sectional study (n=25 caries-free children) found that black stain plaque harbors a distinct, heterogeneous microbiota enriched in Actinomyces and depleted in Campylobacter, with significantly lower salivary microbial diversity vs controls.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Analysis of the Microbiota of Black Stain in the Primary Dentition
- **Journal**: PLoS ONE
- **Year**: 2015
- **Study type**: Cross-sectional, molecular microbiology (16S rRNA amplicon sequencing)
- **Population**: 25 Chinese children (10 BS+, 15 BS−), all caries-free, primary dentition
- **DOI**: 10.1371/journal.pone.0137030
- **PMID**: 26340752 / PMC4560370

## 2. Key Contributions

- First detailed 16S rRNA characterization of the BS microbiome in the primary dentition
- Demonstrated significantly reduced microbial diversity (Shannon/Simpson indices) in saliva of BS+ children
- Identified Actinomyces, Cardiobacterium, Haemophilus, Corynebacterium, Tannerella, and Treponema as enriched in BS+ plaque
- Identified Campylobacter as depleted in BS+ plaque
- PCA revealed that BS+ plaque samples did not cluster tightly (two subgroups, heterogeneous), while control plaque samples clustered tightly — suggesting BS is not a single microbiome phenotype

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Study design**: Cross-sectional; samples collected from children in Peking University School and Hospital of Stomatology
- **Groups**: BS+ (n=10, black stain present on ≥3 primary teeth, caries-free); BS− controls (n=15, caries-free, no stain)
- **Sample types**: Supragingival plaque and saliva from each child
- **Sequencing**: 16S rRNA V3–V4 hypervariable region amplicon sequencing (Illumina MiSeq)
- **Analysis**: OTU clustering, taxonomic assignment, alpha diversity (Shannon, Simpson), beta diversity (PCA/PCoA), relative abundance comparisons
- **Taxonomy detected**: 10 phyla, 19 classes, 32 orders, 61 families, 102 genera

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample (n=25); results need replication in larger cohorts
- Cross-sectional design — cannot determine causality (does BS microbiome cause the stain, or does stain select for this microbiome?)
- All participants from a single center in Beijing; may not generalize
- Sequencing limited to 16S rRNA — functional/metabolic capacity not assessed
- The two BS+ subgroups identified by PCA are not further characterized; future work should define them clinically or metabolically

## 6. Related Work

- Zheng 2023 (PMID 37065128): expanded BS microbiome characterization to include gut microbiome and co-occurrence with early childhood caries; replicated Actinomyces enrichment in EBS
- Soukos & Goodson 2011: proposed that iron sulfide precipitation by Actinomyces mediates black stain pigmentation
- Slots 1974: early description of black stain microbiota (culture-based); Actinomyces and Bacterionema (now Rothia) identified

## 7. Glossary

- **Black stain (BS)**: Dark extrinsic pigmentation on tooth surfaces of the primary or permanent dentition, associated with reduced caries prevalence
- **OTU (Operational Taxonomic Unit)**: Cluster of 16S rRNA sequences grouped at ≥97% similarity, approximating a bacterial species
- **Shannon/Simpson index**: Alpha diversity measures — higher values indicate greater species richness and evenness
- **PCA (Principal Component Analysis)**: Ordination method for visualizing community-level beta diversity differences
- **Actinomyces**: Gram-positive, filamentous oral bacteria; key iron sulfide-precipitating species in BS hypothesis
