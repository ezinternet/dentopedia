## One-line Summary
Prospective study (19 Conelog implants, CBCT correlation): crestal cortical thickness is the strongest ISQ predictor (p<0.001); buccolingual cortical at 3mm significant; depth 6–9mm not significant — ISQ reflects marginal bone more than apical bone.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: PLoS ONE 12(12):e0190293 (2017)
- DOI: 10.1371/journal.pone.0190293
- Design: Prospective, 19 Conelog implants, pre-implant CBCT measurement, ISQ correlation

## 2. Key Contributions
- Crestal cortical bone thickness = primary ISQ determinant
- Subcrestal (6–9 mm depth) bone has minimal ISQ influence
- ISQ is a marginal bone quality indicator, not a total bone volume indicator
- CBCT can predict ISQ (and thus loading potential) preoperatively

## 3. Methodology
- 19 Conelog implants in partially edentulous patients
- Pre-operative CBCT measuring cortical thickness at crest, 3mm, 6mm, 9mm depth (bucco-lingual and mesio-distal)
- ISQ measured at placement with Osstell ISQ
- Pearson/Spearman correlation analysis

## 4. Key Results
- Crestal cortical thickness: strongest ISQ correlation (p<0.001)
- Buccolingual cortical at 3mm: significant (p=0.018)
- Depth 6–9mm: NOT significant
- Mesio-distal measurements: less significant than bucco-lingual
- ISQ = "marginal stability" metric — reflects cortical engagement, not cancellous volume

## 5. Limitations
- Small sample (n=19)
- Single implant system (Conelog)
- No follow-up ISQ (only placement ISQ analyzed)
- CBCT radiation exposure as a limitation for routine use

## 6. Related Work
- Huang 2020: bone quality = top-ranked ISQ factor (literature review)
- Sennerby 2008: ISQ principles — cortical bone as primary stability contributor
