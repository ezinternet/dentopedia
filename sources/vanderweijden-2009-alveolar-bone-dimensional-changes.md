## One-line Summary
Systematic review of 12 human studies on post-extraction ridge change without preservation: weighted-mean horizontal width loss 3.87 mm, mid-buccal height loss 1.67 mm, crestal radiographic height loss 1.53 mm, and socket fill in height 2.57 mm — width loss exceeds height loss during natural socket healing.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Periodontology 2009;36(12):1048–1058
- **Institution**: ACTA, University of Amsterdam and VU University Amsterdam, Netherlands
- **Study type**: Systematic review (no meta-analysis); MEDLINE-PubMed + CENTRAL searched up to March 2009
- **Funding**: Self-funded; no conflicts declared

## 2. Key Contributions
- First systematic, weighted-mean estimate of post-extraction alveolar ridge dimensional change in **humans only** (prior consensus relied heavily on dog data).
- Establishes that the **horizontal (buccolingual) loss is greater than the vertical loss** — clinically critical for implant-site planning.
- Differentiates clinical height change (mid-buccal/lingual) from radiographic crestal change, recognising that radiographs underestimate buccal plate resorption.
- Quantifies that the socket fills approximately to within ~2.6 mm of the original alveolar floor over the healing period (typically 3–12 months).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Two independent reviewers screened 1244 PubMed + 106 Cochrane titles/abstracts; 12 publications met inclusion (extraction without preservation, dimensional outcomes reported with measurement method described).
- Outcomes pre-specified: (i) approximal height change, (ii) mid-buccal height change, (iii) mid-crestal change, (iv) mid-lingual change, (v) alveolar width change, (vi) socket fill (relative to original socket floor).
- Extraction of mean ± SD from each study; calculation of **weighted means** by sample size.
- No formal meta-analysis (heterogeneity in measurement methodology, time-points, tooth groups).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Width loss** (horizontal): weighted mean **3.87 mm**.
- **Mid-buccal height loss** (clinical): weighted mean **1.67 mm**.
- **Mid-crestal height loss** (radiographic): weighted mean **1.53 mm**.
- **Socket fill in height** (relative to original socket floor): weighted mean **2.57 mm**.
- Follow-up ranges across included studies: 3–12 months; majority 6 months.
- Conclusion: clinical width loss > clinical height loss; radiographic crestal change underestimates buccal-only loss.
- Precise CIs and per-study SDs distributed across 12 included papers (see PDF appendix).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- High heterogeneity in measurement instrument (calipers, radiographs, CBCT, stents), tooth type, time-point — precluded meta-analysis.
- Wide variation in surgical technique (flap vs flapless) not controlled.
- Most studies single-tooth; multiple-extraction sites underrepresented.
- Future need: standardised measurement protocol (e.g., CBCT pre/post with reference stent) and tooth-specific subgroup data.

## 6. Related Work
- Araujo & Lindhe 2005 — dog model showing buccal plate is largely bundle-bone and resorbs preferentially.
- Schropp 2003 — human prospective showing ~50% width loss in 12 months.
- Cardaropoli 2003 (dog) — histological timeline of socket healing.
- Tan 2012 — subsequent systematic review extending these numbers.
- Avila-Ortiz 2014 — meta-analysis quantifying effect of ARP on these baseline losses.

## 7. Glossary
- **Modeling**: change in size/shape of bone (e.g., resorption of buccal plate).
- **Remodeling**: coupled resorption-formation cycle replacing woven with lamellar bone.
- **Mid-buccal height**: vertical reading at the centre of the buccal aspect of the socket.
- **Socket fill**: amount of new tissue inside the socket measured from original socket floor toward the crest.
- **Bundle bone**: tooth-dependent bone layer lining the socket, lost rapidly post-extraction.
