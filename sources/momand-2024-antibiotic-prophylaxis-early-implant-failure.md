## Why Ingested

조기 임플란트 실패 surveillance 배치에서 "예방 가능한가?"라는 개입 측 질문을 담당하는 최신 SR+MA. 위험인자 코호트인 [[implants/yari-2023-risk-factors-early-implant-failure]]·[[implants/uesugi-2024-risk-factors-early-failure-all-on-four]]가 흡연·해부·로딩을 강조하는 반면, 본 연구는 항생제 예방이 조기 실패를 거의 줄이지 못함(NNT 143)을 보여 개입 우선순위를 재정렬한다. 기존 [[drug/antibiotics/torof-2023-antibiotic-dental-implant-procedures-sr-ma]](술전 단일 amoxicillin 권고)와 대비되는 결과 — placebo-RCT만 포함해 효과를 축소 추정.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review + meta-analysis (7 placebo-controlled double-blind RCTs, 1859 patients / 3014 implants): preoperative antibiotic prophylaxis did NOT significantly reduce early implant failure (RR 0.66, 95% CI 0.30-1.47; risk difference -0.007; NNT 143), GRADE moderate certainty — routine prophylaxis not supported for uncomplicated implant surgery.

## 한줄요약

체계적 고찰+메타분석(위약대조 이중맹검 RCT 7편, 환자 1859명 / 임플란트 3014개): 술전 항생제 예방은 조기 임플란트 실패를 유의하게 줄이지 못함(RR 0.66, 95% CI 0.30-1.47; 위험차 -0.007; NNT 143), GRADE 중간 — 비복잡 임플란트 수술에서 통상적 항생제 예방은 근거 부족.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA restricted to **placebo-controlled, double-blinded RCTs with low/moderate risk of bias** on this question, plus a formal **GRADE** certainty rating (absent from prior reviews).
- Finds **no significant** prophylaxis effect on early failure (RR 0.66) and an **NNT of 143** (95% CI 29-∞) — CI to infinity implies possible null effect.
- Directly contrasts prior positive reviews that included high-RoB / non-blinded / non-placebo trials, explaining their discordant conclusions.
- Subgroup excluding immediate-placement-into-socket studies flips direction (RR 1.10), isolating immediate post-extraction implants as the context where prophylaxis (really "treatment") may matter.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Design: SR + random-effects (Hedges) meta-analysis. PICO: antibiotic prophylaxis vs placebo/no antibiotics in overall healthy patients undergoing implant surgery; primary outcome early implant failure (loss before loading).
- Inclusion: RCTs, ≥20 patients, ≥2 months follow-up. Exclusion: mini-implants/mini-screws; immediate placement at sites with apical pathology; patients medically requiring prophylaxis.
- Screening: 1267 records → 1085 screened → 12 eligible for RoB → 7 in meta-analysis (5 high-RoB excluded). RoB-2 tool.
- Subgroups: amoxicillin-only (6 studies); excluding two immediate-socket studies (5 studies). Stata 16 SE. GRADE.
- Regimens: five RCTs 2 g amoxicillin 1 h preop; one 2 g preop + 500 mg ×3 days 1-3 postop; one 600 mg clindamycin 1 h preop. All double-blinded.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Pooled (7 studies): early failure 20/929 (2.2%) antibiotic vs 31/930 (3.3%) placebo. RR 0.66 (95% CI 0.30-1.47); RD -0.007 (95% CI -0.035-0.020); NNT 143 (95% CI 29-∞).
- Amoxicillin subgroup (6 studies): RR 0.60 (0.27-1.31); RD -0.011 (-0.029-0.006).
- Excluding immediate-socket studies (5 studies): RR 1.10 (0.35-3.45); RD 0.002 (-0.027-0.030).
- Overall implant failure across cohort: 51/1859 patients (2.7%); failure rates ≤6.5% in any group.
- GRADE certainty that the prophylaxis effect is small: **moderate**.
- The two immediate-post-extraction RCTs had higher placebo-group failure (5.1%, 4.7%) — the only signal favoring prophylaxis.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only seven low/moderate-RoB studies; two had small samples; low event rates → wide CIs.
- Heterogeneous design: follow-up timing 2-6 months, varied chlorhexidine use (confounder), inadequate periodontal reporting, varied surgical procedures, smoker proportion 9.3-34%.
- Timing of "early failure" not clearly reported in any included RCT.
- Does not address immunocompromised/medically complex patients or grafting-heavy/immediate-socket scenarios (where benefit may exist).

## 6. Related Work

- Earlier implant-prophylaxis SR+MA recommending preoperative single-dose amoxicillin (Torof 2023) — contrasted.
- Prior positive reviews (Lund 2015 NNT 50 = no benefit; Rodríguez-Sánchez 2018 NNT 67 = effective) and consensus statements (EAO 2015 vs Italian Academy) reconciled.
- Smoking as an early-failure risk factor (Fan 2024 SR+MA) cited as residual confounder.

## 7. Glossary

- **NNT (number needed to treat)**: patients given prophylaxis to prevent one early failure; here 143.
- **Risk difference (RD)**: absolute difference in failure proportion between arms; here -0.007.
- **GRADE**: certainty-of-evidence framework (high/moderate/low/very low).
- **RoB-2**: Cochrane risk-of-bias tool for randomized trials.
- **Early implant failure**: loss of implant within the first months after placement, before prosthetic loading (loss of osseointegration).
