## Why Ingested

치아형태학 감시 인제스트 — 하악 제1대구치의 부가 원심설측근(Radix Entomolaris, RE)/3근(three-rooted PMFM) 전세계 유병률을 정량화해, 근관치료 시 놓친 원심설측근관(missed distolingual canal) 위험을 인종·지역별로 보강한다. 기존 [[endodontics/anatomy/shekarian-2023-cshaped-canals-three-rooted-mandibular-iranian]]는 단일(이란) 인구의 3근·C-shaped 공존을 다루는데, 본 72-study 메타분석은 그 단일-인구 소견을 31개국·26,302명 규모의 글로벌 prevalence 그라디언트로 확장·맥락화한다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 72 CBCT studies (31 countries, 26,302 patients / 37,994 mandibular first molars): global tooth-level prevalence of a third root is 8.85%, patient-level 10.3%, with a steep East-Asia/Mongoloid → West gradient (East Asia 24% vs Europe 2% vs Africa 0.9%), ~46% bilateral symmetry, right-side dominance, and no sex difference.

## 한줄요약

72편 CBCT 연구(31개국, 환자 26,302명·치아 37,994개) 메타분석 — 하악 제1대구치 3근(원심설측근, RE) 치아수준 유병률 8.85%, 환자수준 10.3%, 동아시아(24%)에서 유럽(2%)·아프리카(0.9%)로 급감하는 인종 그라디언트, 양측대칭 약 46%, 우측 우세, 성차 없음.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Largest prevalence MA of three-rooted mandibular first molars (PMFM)** to date: 72 in-vivo CBCT studies, 31 countries, 26,302 patients, 37,994 teeth — vs prior reviews (35-study Tomaszewska MA = 8.9%; an early review = 13%).
2. **First MA to report patient-level prevalence and bilateral symmetry**, not just tooth-level (prior reviews reported tooth-level only).
3. Quantifies a **clear East→West / Mongoloid ethnic gradient**, and reports **Radix Entomolaris (RE) and Radix Paramolaris (RP) separately** by sensitivity analysis.
4. Restricts inclusion to **CBCT in-vivo** only (rejecting micro-CT/extraction and plain X-ray, which underestimate RE) — reduces methodological heterogeneity vs pooled-method prior reviews.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Eligibility**: in-vivo epidemiological studies using CBCT; PMFM with fully formed apices, no resorption/anomaly. Excluded: in-vitro, case reports, plain X-ray, extraction, micro-CT-only, mixed-method, pediatric (5–6 yr).
- **Search**: PubMed, Google Scholar, ResearchGate, ProQuest, LILACS to 30 Oct 2021; no language/time limit; 4 non-English studies translated (Chinese, Kurdish, Arabic, Japanese).
- **Quality**: JBI Critical Appraisal (8 of 9 items). 37 studies moderate RoB (avg JBI 53.38%), 35 high RoB (avg 32.14%).
- **Analysis**: proportion meta-analysis, random-effects, MetaXL v5.3. Three objectives (tooth-level, patient-level, bilateral symmetry) + subgroup (geography, side, gender) + sensitivity (RE vs RP) + publication bias (funnel, Doi plot, LFK index).
- **Designs**: 58 cross-sectional descriptive retrospective, 13 analytical retrospective, 1 analytical prospective.
- Country spread: South Korea (9), China (8), Turkey/Saudi Arabia (6 each), Taiwan/Iran (5 each), Brazil (4), Malaysia (3), India/Portugal/Chile (2 each), plus 20 single-study countries.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Pooled estimates (random-effects):**

| Objective | Pooled % (95% CI) | I² | Range | n studies | numerator/denominator |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| I. Tooth-level prevalence | 8.85% (6.60–11.39) | 99% | 0–29% | 68 | 5503 / 37994 teeth |
| II. Patient-level prevalence | 10.3% (6.9–14.4) | 98% | 0–35% | 44 | 2535 / 16836 patients |
| III. Patient-level bilateral symmetry | 46.22% (39.13–53.39) | 90% | 0–100% | 40 | 1311 / 2326 patients |

**Geographic (ethnic) gradient — tooth-level (objective I):** East Asia 24.1% (23–25.2) · Southeast Asia 13% (9–17.7) · South Asia 4.7% (2.5–7.6) · West Asia 4.4% (3.1–5.8) · Europe 2% (1.3–2.9) · America 1.8% (0.2–4.6) · Africa 0.9% (0.3–1.9). Highest single value 29% (Chinese populations).

**Patient-level subgroup:** East Asia 28.8% (27.3–30.4, range 22–35) · South Asia 5.6% · West Asia 4.4% · America 2.1% · Europe 1%. (Southeast Asia/Africa: insufficient data.)

**Bilateral symmetry subgroup:** East Asia 60.3% (56.3–64.2) · South Asia 38.6% · West Asia 37.5% · America 35.1% · Europe 18.7% (lowest).

**Side:** right 16% (12.2–20.2, range 1–34) vs left 12.1% (9.2–15.4, range 0–31) → **right-side predominance** (35 studies).

**Gender:** male 13.3% (9.8–17.3) vs female 13% (9.7–16.7) → **no sexual dimorphism** (35 studies).

**Sensitivity (RE vs RP):** Radix Entomolaris 12.3% (9.3–15.7), I²=99%, range 0–29%; Radix Paramolaris 0.1% (0.0–0.1), I²=30%, range 0–2%. 43 studies, 28,822 teeth (RE 5056, RP 21).

**Publication bias:** funnel/Doi major asymmetry for objectives I (LFK −4.02) and III (LFK −3.19); objective II symmetric (LFK 0.05).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- ~Half of included studies high RoB; >80% set no sampling frame, only 4% random sampling, 15% calculated sample size, 95% single-center — selection bias.
- Insufficient raw data for patient-level Africa and Southeast Asia subgroups; only 2 African studies.
- No Australian study eligible; ethnicity-level (vs geography) subgroup not done; internal canal morphology of the third root not assessed.
- Very high heterogeneity (I² up to 99%), driven mostly by geography (drops to 0% in Africa after subgroup).
- Future: MA of clinical factors of three-rooted PMFM and variation in third-root position around the circumference.

## 6. Related Work

- Tomaszewska et al. — 35-study MA, pooled 8.9% (tooth-level only); concordant with this MA's 8.85% but no patient-level/bilateral/RP analysis.
- Earlier scoping review — 13% global third-root prevalence (this MA finds ~4% lower at tooth level).
- Saudi Arabia SR — RE 2.9–6.07% (X-ray/combined method, likely underestimates RE).

## 7. Glossary

- **PMFM**: permanent mandibular first molar.
- **Radix Entomolaris (RE)**: accessory third root arising from the lingual (usually distolingual) root trunk; the clinically important "radix" — high missed-canal risk.
- **Radix Paramolaris (RP)**: third root on the buccal/mesiobuccal side; rarer (0.1% pooled), more anthropological than clinical significance.
- **Three-rooted PMFM**: umbrella term covering both RE and RP.
- **Tooth-level vs patient-level prevalence**: % of teeth vs % of people; patient-level is higher because bilateral teeth inflate the tooth denominator.
- **Bilateral symmetry**: a patient with the morphology shows it on both mandibular sides.
- **JBI Critical Appraisal**: Joanna Briggs Institute prevalence-study risk-of-bias tool.
- **LFK index / Doi plot**: small-study/publication-bias diagnostics (|LFK|>1 = bias).
