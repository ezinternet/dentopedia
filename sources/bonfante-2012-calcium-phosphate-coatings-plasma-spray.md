## Why Ingested

이 논문은 플라즈마 스프레이 방식으로 제조된 두 종류의 칼슘인산염 코팅(PSHA vs ACP)의 결정성 차이가 골유착 조직계측학적 결과(BIC, BAFO)에 실제로 영향을 미치는지를 생체 내(개 동물모델)에서 직접 비교한 희소한 연구다. 기존 [[wiki/implants/surface/yamada-2012-nanopolymorphic-hydroxyapatite-titanium-implant]]에서 나노스케일 HA 코팅의 골반응을 다루었으나, 플라즈마 스프레이법으로 생성된 비결정질 vs. 부분결정질 CaP 코팅의 위상 구성 정량분석(Rietveld)과 BIC/BAFO 비교는 다루지 않아 이를 보완한다.

## One-line Summary

Animal study (6 beagles, 36 implants, 6 and 12 weeks) comparing plasma-sprayed hydroxyapatite (PSHA) vs. amorphous calcium phosphate (ACP) coatings on Ti-6Al-4V plateau root form implants: ACP had significantly higher surface roughness (Sa, Sq) and unique carbonate groups, while PSHA had far greater crystalline HA content (71% vs. <1%), yet BIC and BAFO were statistically equivalent at both time points.

## 한줄요약

비글 6마리(임플란트 36개, 6·12주) 동물실험에서 플라즈마 스프레이 HA(PSHA) vs 비결정질 CaP(ACP) 코팅 간 표면 거칠기(ACP 우세)·결정상 조성(PSHA 71% HA vs ACP <1%)·탄산염 유무 차이에도 불구하고, BIC·BAFO 조직계측학적 지표는 두 군 간 유의차 없이 동등한 골전도성 확인.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title:** Physicochemical Characterization and In Vivo Evaluation of Amorphous and Partially Crystalline Calcium Phosphate Coatings Fabricated on Ti-6Al-4V Implants by the Plasma Spray Method
- **Journal:** International Journal of Biomaterials, Volume 2012, Article ID 603826, 8 pages
- **DOI:** 10.1155/2012/603826
- **Published:** 2012 (received 5 April 2012; accepted 4 July 2012)
- **Study design:** Animal in vivo study (beagle dog model) + physicochemical surface characterization
- **Institutions:** UNIGRANRIO University (Brazil); NYU Department of Biomaterials and Biomimetics; Tufts University School of Dental Medicine

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First rigorous four-method physicochemical characterization (SEM, IFM interferometry, XRD with Rietveld refinement, FT-IR) comparing PSHA and ACP plasma-spray coatings on the same implant macrogeometry.
2. Quantified crystalline phase composition by Rietveld analysis: PSHA contained 71.32% HA, 5.58% calcium phosphate, 23.01% calcium pyrophosphate; ACP contained only trace fractions (HA ~0.83%, calcium phosphate ~1.33%, calcium pyrophosphate ~7.53%).
3. Demonstrated that despite significant physicochemical differences, both coatings yield equivalent BIC and BAFO — supporting that the plateau root form implant macrogeometry enables intramembranous-like healing regardless of CaP coating type.
4. Identified FT-IR carbonate (CO3) groups exclusively in the ACP coating, absent from PSHA — a chemical distinction with potential resorption-rate implications that histomorphometry alone cannot detect.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Implants:**
- Plateau root form implants (Ti-6Al-4V), 4.5 mm diameter × 8.0 mm length (Bicon LLC, Boston, MA)
- Two surface groups: PSHA (plasma-sprayed hydroxyapatite) and ACP (plasma-sprayed amorphous calcium phosphate), n=18 per group
- 6 implants per group used for surface characterization only

**Surface Characterization Methods:**
- SEM (Philips XL 30) at 15–20 kV, intermediate and high magnification
- Optical interferometry (IFM, Phase View 2.6): 100 μm × 100 μm filter; Sa and Sq parameters; n=3 implants × 3 measurements; ANOVA for statistics
- XRD (Philips X'Pert): 2θ range 20–40°, step 0.02° at 3 sec/step; Rietveld refinement via MAUD software for phase quantification
- FT-IR (Nicolet Magna IR 550): 4000–400 cm⁻¹ scan; pellets from coating powder mixed with KBr

**Animal Model:**
- 6 adult male beagles, ~1.5 years old
- Ethics approval: École Nationale Vétérinaire d'Alfort (France)
- Implant site: radius epiphysis (cortical/trabecular mixed site)
- Right limb: 6 weeks in vivo; Left limb: 12 weeks in vivo
- Implant placement under general anesthesia (atropine sulfate + xylazine + ketamine)
- Sequential drills at 1,200 rpm under saline irrigation; implants alternated proximal-to-distal to minimize site bias

**Histological Processing:**
- Formalin fixation (10%, 24 h), graduated ethanol dehydration, methacrylate-based resin embedding (Technovit 9100)
- Diamond saw sectioning (~300 μm), grinding/polishing to ~50 μm (SiC papers 400–2400)
- Toluidine blue staining; optical microscopy (Leica DM2500M)
- BIC: regions of bone-to-implant contact / total implant perimeter (50×–200×)
- BAFO: bone area fraction between healing chambers / total area (100×)
- Statistical analysis: Friedman non-parametric test (P<0.05)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Surface Roughness (IFM):**
- PSHA: Sa = 3.22 ± 0.27 μm; Sq = 3.88 ± 0.28 μm
- ACP: Sa = 5.18 ± 0.95 μm; Sq = 6.35 ± 1.16 μm
- ACP significantly rougher than PSHA (Sa P<0.03; Sq P<0.02)
- Both surfaces fall in the "rough" classification (Sa > 2 μm per Albrektsson/Wennerberg criteria)

**Crystalline Phase Composition (Rietveld):**
- PSHA: HA 71.32%, calcium phosphate 5.58%, calcium pyrophosphate 23.01%, CaO 0.11%
- ACP: HA 0.83%, calcium phosphate 1.33%, calcium pyrophosphate 7.53%, CaO 0.29%
- All three major CaP phases significantly higher in PSHA vs ACP

**FT-IR:**
- Phosphate peaks (PO4): 1100–1040 cm⁻¹ and ~600–560 cm⁻¹ double peaks — present in both
- Carbonate groups (CO3): 850–800 cm⁻¹ peaks present only in ACP, absent in PSHA

**In Vivo Histomorphometry:**
- At 6 weeks: intimate bone-to-implant contact and woven bone formation in healing chambers observed for both groups
- At 12 weeks: lamellar bone replacing woven bone in healing chambers for both groups
- BIC: no significant differences between surfaces at 6 or 12 weeks (P>0.86)
- BAFO: no significant differences between surfaces at 6 or 12 weeks (P>0.75)
- Mean BIC ~65% at 12 weeks (consistent with human retrieval data for PSHA-coated plateau implants)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **No biomechanical testing:** Torque-to-interfacial-fracture data absent; authors note future biomechanical testing is warranted given the chemical differences detected
- **Short observation window:** Only 6 and 12 weeks; long-term degradation behavior of ACP (especially its carbonate groups and higher resorption potential) not evaluated
- **Manufacturer processing parameters unknown:** Neither coating's plasma-spray processing parameters (temperature, powder feed, spray distance) were disclosed
- **No machined control:** Lack of uncoated Ti-6Al-4V control limits comparison to the established machined baseline
- **Radius epiphysis model:** Non-oral site; may not fully translate to jaw bone microenvironment and loading conditions
- **Static morphometry only:** BIC/BAFO cannot distinguish dynamic differences in woven-to-lamellar bone remodeling kinetics between the two coating chemistries

## 6. Related Work

- Coelho et al. 2010 (Oral Surg Oral Med): Four-surface biomechanical/histomorphometric comparison in dogs — plasma-sprayed CaP showed highest torque-to-interfacial fracture among machined, AB/AE, bioceramic, and plasma-spray groups
- Granato et al. 2009 (J Biomed Mater Res): Ion beam bioceramic deposition on plateau root form implants — thin bioceramic layer improved torque values despite lower roughness
- Baldassarri et al. (retrieval): PSHA plateau implants retrieved at 2 months to 13 years — average BIC ~65%, lamellar bone and Haversian-like osteonic morphology regardless of time in function
- Wennerberg & Albrektsson 2009 (Clin Oral Implants Res): Sa 1–2 μm as "optimal" roughness range for osseointegration — both PSHA and ACP in this study exceeded that range

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| PSHA | Plasma-sprayed hydroxyapatite — partially crystalline CaP coating; high HA content (~71%) |
| ACP | Amorphous calcium phosphate — plasma-spray coating with negligible crystallinity (<1% HA) |
| BIC | Bone-to-Implant Contact — percentage of implant perimeter in direct contact with bone |
| BAFO | Bone Area Fraction Occupancy — percentage of healing chamber area occupied by bone |
| Sa | Arithmetic average height deviation — primary roughness parameter (ISO 25178) |
| Sq | Root mean square roughness — second roughness parameter |
| Rietveld refinement | XRD data analysis method for quantifying crystalline phase fractions in a mixture |
| MAUD | Materials Analysis Using Diffraction — Rietveld refinement software |
| FT-IR | Fourier Transform Infrared Spectroscopy — identifies molecular bond vibrations (CO3, PO4) |
| Calcium pyrophosphate | CaP phase (Ca2P2O7) present in partially crystalline plasma-spray coatings |
| Plateau root form implant | Non-threaded implant with horizontal plateaus creating discrete healing chambers |
| Healing chamber | Space between implant plateaus and surrounding bone where intramembranous-like bone forms |
| Intramembranous healing | Bone formation via direct woven → lamellar bone without cartilage intermediate (vs. endochondral) |
