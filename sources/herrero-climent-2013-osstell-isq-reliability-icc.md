## One-line Summary
Cross-sectional study (85 implants, 23 patients): Osstell ISQ ICC=0.97; single measurement sufficient; SmartPeg types I and II interchangeable; mean ISQ 72.4.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Medicina Oral Patologia Oral y Cirugia Bucal 18(6):e877-882 (2013)
- DOI: 10.4317/medoral.19120
- Design: Cross-sectional reliability/repeatability study, 85 implants, 23 patients

## 2. Key Contributions
- High ICC (0.97) confirms Osstell ISQ is highly reliable between measurements
- Single RFA measurement is statistically sufficient (no need for repeat averaging)
- SmartPeg types I and II produce equivalent ISQ values

## 3. Methodology
- 85 implants in 23 patients, measured by 2 operators
- Measurements: mesial, distal, buccal, lingual directions
- SmartPeg type I and type II compared
- Statistical: ICC (intraclass correlation coefficient), Bland-Altman analysis

## 4. Key Results
- ICC = 0.97 (excellent reliability)
- Mean ISQ across all implants: 72.4
- SmartPeg I vs II: no significant difference (NS)
- Measurement direction: some angular variation, but clinically non-significant
- One measurement = representative of implant stability

## 5. Limitations
- Cross-sectional (single timepoint per implant — no longitudinal tracking)
- No ISQ threshold testing (survival outcome not tracked)
- Single device brand (Osstell Mentor)

## 6. Related Work
- Kästel 2019: SmartPeg tightening torque variation does not affect ISQ
- Sennerby 2008: ISQ principles and thresholds
