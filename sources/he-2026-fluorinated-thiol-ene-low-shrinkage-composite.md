## One-line Summary
In-vitro + in-vivo (rat) study of a novel fluorinated methacrylate-thiol-ene composite (DST-1) using thiol-ene step-growth polymerization to reduce shrinkage stress, showing significantly lower microleakage, reduced bacterial (streptococcal) adhesion via low surface free energy, good wear/polish, and no adverse systemic/tissue response in Wistar rats.

## 한줄요약
In-vitro + in-vivo(rat): thiol-ene 단계성장 중합으로 저수축응력을 구현한 fluorinated methacrylate-thiol-ene 복합레진(DST-1)이 미세누출 유의 감소·연쇄상구균 부착 감소(낮은 표면자유에너지)·우수한 마모/연마성·Wistar rat 전신·조직 무해성을 보임.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Combines low polymerization shrinkage stress (thiol-ene step-growth → delayed gel point) with bacterial anti-adhesion (fluorination → low surface free energy) in one composite
- Links the mechanistic chain: low shrinkage stress → better marginal sealing (lower microleakage) → reduced secondary-caries risk
- Demonstrates reduced total bacterial + streptococcal adhesion without altering biofilm metabolic activity
- Provides in-vivo (Wistar rat) hematological/biochemical/histopathological biocompatibility evidence — rare for experimental composites
- Trade-off noted: modest reduction in diametral tensile strength, but improved polishing and hydrophobicity

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- DST via thiol-ene step-growth polymerization system (fluorinated methacrylate-thiol-ene matrix)
- Anti-adhesion: saliva-derived human dental plaque biofilms — CFU, CLSM, SEM, crystal violet, MTT
- Microleakage: modified Class II restorations, rhodamine B dye + CLSM
- Physicochemical: wear resistance, surface roughness, contact angle, surface free energy, diametral tensile strength
- In-vivo: Wistar rats — hematology, biochemistry, histopathology

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Significantly reduced total + streptococcal adhesion vs conventional composites; biofilm metabolic activity unaffected
- DST-1: significantly lower microleakage scores; enhanced wear resistance; lower surface roughness; reduced surface free energy
- Modest ↓ in tensile strength; improved polishing + hydrophobicity
- No adverse systemic or tissue responses in vivo
- (Precursor 2024 study: volumetric shrinkage + shrinkage stress reduced with thiol-ene 0-40 wt% addition, no conversion-degree loss)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Experimental material; in-vitro biofilm + rat model — no human clinical restoration data
- Tensile-strength reduction may limit high-stress applications
- Abstract-only ingest; quantitative shrinkage-stress values for DST-1 in this paper not extracted (see 2024 precursor)

## 7. Glossary
- **Thiol-ene step-growth polymerization**: delays gel point vs chain-growth methacrylate, reducing shrinkage stress. **Surface free energy (SFE)**: lower SFE → less bacterial adhesion. **Microleakage**: dye/fluid penetration at the tooth-restoration interface. **DST**: the experimental fluorinated methacrylate-thiol-ene composite.
