## Why Ingested

Rittipakorn-2025 clockwise OD cadaveric study의 reference surveillance 인제스트. OD가 실제 임상(ridge split 동시 임플란트)에서 ISQ ≥65 loading threshold를 달성하는지 확인. 상악 저밀도 골에서 유의한 ISQ 이점을 확인해 [[wiki/implants/versah-protocols/rittipakorn-2025-clockwise-osseodensification-primary-stability-cadaveric]]의 임상 번역을 지지.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective study (n=268 implants, 65 patients) comparing OD vs conventional Esset kit in ridge-split procedures: OD significantly improved stability in maxilla and mandibular posterior, with all OD implants maintaining ISQ ≥65; bone gain was equivalent.

## 한줄요약

후향적 연구(268개 임플란트, 65명): OD는 상악 및 하악 구치부에서 유의하게 높은 ISQ를 보였고 모든 OD 임플란트가 ISQ ≥65를 유지했으나, 골폭 획득은 기존 방법과 동등했다.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Demonstrates OD provides ISQ advantages over conventional drilling in ridge-split contexts, particularly maxilla and low-density regions
- All OD implants exceeded ISQ 65 loading threshold (minimum 68); conventional group minimum was 62
- Comparable horizontal bone gain (NS): OD offers stability benefits without sacrificing augmentation efficacy
- Ridge-split OD protocol: pilot at 800 rpm clockwise → Densah burs counterclockwise 1200 rpm (2.3–3.3mm)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design:** Single-center retrospective, Ordu University
- **Patients:** 65 (34M/31F; mean 51.8±9.2y; range 31–69); ASA I–II; horizontal ridge deficiency
- **Implants:** 268 Osstem implants (maxillary anterior n=82, maxillary posterior n=82, mandibular posterior n=82, mandibular anterior n=22)
- **Groups:** OD (n=133, Densah burs) vs conventional ridge split (n=135, Osstem Esset kit)
- **Stability:** RFA with Osstell at T0 (placement) and T1 (4 months, abutment connection); ISQ averaged mesiodistal and buccolingual
- **Bone width:** CBCT at preop and 4 months; coronal and apical measurements
- **Statistical:** Shapiro-Wilk normality; Mann-Whitney or t-test; Kruskal-Wallis with Bonferroni; α=0.05; power >0.99 post hoc

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
| Region | ISQ T0 (OD vs Conv) | ISQ T1 (OD vs Conv) | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Maxillary anterior | Higher | Higher | p=0.001 (both) |
| Maxillary posterior | Higher | Higher | p<0.01 |
| Mandibular anterior | NS | NS | p>0.05 |
| Mandibular posterior | NS | Higher | T1: p=0.001 |

- OD minimum ISQ: T0=68, T1=71; Conventional minimum: T0=62, T1=61
- Bone gain (coronal): OD 2.81mm vs 2.99mm (NS); apical: 0.73mm vs 1.37mm (NS)
- Both groups: significantly greater coronal than apical bone gain (p=0.001)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Single-center retrospective design; selection bias risk
- 4-month follow-up only — no long-term survival data
- Single implant system (Osstem); results may not generalize
- No standardized HU data; anatomical classification used as bone density proxy
- Patient-reported outcomes not assessed
- Need: multicenter prospective RCT with multiple systems, longer follow-up

## 6. Related Work
- Bergamo 2021: RCT of OD effect on primary/secondary stability — consistent with present findings
- Fontes Pereira 2023: SR on OD outcomes — supports OD for primary stability
- Rittipakorn 2025: Clockwise OD cadaveric study — same Osstem implants, IT-ISQ correlation confirmed

## 7. Glossary
- **OD (Osseodensification):** Densah bur technique compacting rather than removing bone in counterclockwise mode
- **Ridge split:** Controlled buccal plate separation for horizontal augmentation
- **ISQ:** Implant Stability Quotient (0–100 scale via RFA); ≥65 typically accepted for loading
- **RFA:** Resonance Frequency Analysis — ISQ measurement via Osstell device
- **Esset kit:** Osstem conventional ridge split bur set
