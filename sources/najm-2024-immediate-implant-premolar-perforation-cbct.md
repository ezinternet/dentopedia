## Why Ingested

Extends [[immediate-implant/kheur-2024-feasibility-iip-maxillary-first-premolars]] (which assessed feasibility of IIP at maxillary first premolars) into a larger CBCT virtual study covering BOTH first and second maxillary premolars (179 patients, 716 teeth) and, crucially, quantifies the prosthetically-driven vs bone-driven perforation gap (84.1% vs 40.5%) plus the mean buccal tilt required (ILAA 18.3°). Provides the anatomical-risk counterpart to the prep/drilling strategies in [[immediate-implant/sierra-rebolledo-2021-undersized-drilling-immediate-tapered-implants-maxilla]] and the sagittal-root-position logic of [[immediate-implant/kan-2011-sagittal-root-position-immediate-implant-anterior]], now applied to the premolar region.

## One-line Summary

CBCT virtual study (179 patients, 716 maxillary premolars): immediate-implant labial/nasal/sinus perforation occurred in 84.1% of prosthetically-driven placements vs 40.5% of bone-driven placements; female sex, older age, shorter tooth-apex distance, and smaller labial concavity angle raised perforation risk.

## 한줄요약

CBCT 가상 연구(환자 179명, 상악 소구치 716개): 즉시식립 시 보철주도(prosthetically-driven) 위치는 84.1%, 골주도(bone-driven) 위치는 40.5%에서 순측/비강/상악동 천공이 발생했고, 여성·고령·치근첨-구조물 거리 짧음·작은 순측 함몰각(LCA)이 천공 위험을 높였다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- **First investigation** into the risk of cortical bone perforation during immediate implant placement (IIP) specifically in the maxillary premolar region.
- Directly compares two virtual implant orientations on the same sockets: the **prosthetically-driven** ideal position (along the tooth long axis / line A) vs the **bone-driven** ideal position (shortest implant avoiding perforation with ≥4 mm apical native-bone anchorage).
- Quantifies the perforation penalty of prosthetic-driven placement: **84.1% perforation** vs **40.5%** bone-driven.
- Identifies four risk factors for perforation/safety-margin invasion in the bone-driven position via multivariate logistic regression: **female sex, older age, shorter tooth-apex-to-nasal/sinus distance, and smaller labial concavity angle (LCA)**.
- Introduces two purpose-built CBCT metrics: the **implant-line A angle (ILAA)** = buccal tilt needed to escape perforation (mean 18.3°) and the **labial concavity angle (LCA)** (mean ~156°).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Sample**: 574 maxillary CBCT exams (Belo Horizonte, Brazil, Q4 2014) screened; 179 included (716 premolar teeth: bilateral 1PM + 2PM). 387 excluded for missing premolars / implants / misalignment / artifacts; 8 for periapical bone destruction.
- **Cohort**: 83 male (332 teeth), 96 female (384 teeth); ages 14.2–78.1 y.
- **Hardware**: i-CAT CBCT (120 kV, 3–5 mA, 40 s, 136 µSv effective dose, 1.0-mm transverse slices, 6×14 cm FOV). Virtual placement in DeltalSlice / measurements in ImageJ.
- **Implant simulation**: parallel 3.75-mm-diameter implant, placed 1 mm subcrestal, ≥4 mm apical native-bone anchorage required for primary stability; 2-mm safety margin enforced from adjacent teeth, nasal/sinus floor, and labial/palatal cortical plates.
- **Two placements** per socket: (a) prosthetically-driven along line A (occlusal central fossa → root apex); (b) bone-driven (shortest non-perforating length).
- **Statistics**: Mann-Whitney / Pearson χ² / Fisher; Pearson & Spearman correlations; univariate + multivariate binary logistic regression (OR, 95% CI). SPSS v28, p<0.05. Calibration on 10 exams among 3 authors. Sample-size powered (80%, α=5%) to detect a 4-fold perforation difference (20% vs 5%).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Perforation incidence**: prosthetically-driven 84.1% perforation (+13.7% within 2-mm safety margin; only 2.2% clean); bone-driven 40.5% perforation (22.9% clean, 36.6% within 2 mm). Highly significant difference (p<0.001).
- **ILAA (buccal tilt to escape perforation)**: mean 18.3°±8.0° (range 0–40.1°); significantly larger for 2PM than 1PM (p<0.001). By tooth: 15=20.6°, 14=17.5°, 24=16.7°, 25=20.3°.
- **Minimum implant length (bone-driven)**: global 13.0±2.1 mm (range 6.5–19.0 mm); 1PM (13.1–13.3 mm) slightly > 2PM (12.7–12.8 mm), NS (p=0.127).
- **LCA**: global 156.5±10.3° (110.5–177.1°); significantly larger in males (158.2°) than females (155.3°), p<0.001.
- **Multivariate risk factors (bone-driven, OR per Table 6)**: Female sex OR 2.358 (1.492–3.725); Age OR 1.034/yr (1.018–1.049); Tooth-apex distance OR 0.697/mm (0.650–0.747); LCA OR 0.964/° (0.938–0.990). Tooth region NS in multivariate (OR 0.925, p=0.750) despite being significant univariately (2PM OR 2.031).
- **Clinical implication**: most premolar IIP single crowns would need a custom angled abutment (~18° buccal-palatal) or angled-screw-channel abutment; CBCT is essential preoperative planning.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Virtual / in-silico study — results depend on CBCT accuracy and do not capture surgical reality (drill deviation, soft tissue).
- Single-implant placement modeled; inter-implant spacing for simultaneous adjacent placements not considered.
- Single-center retrospective database (Brazil, 2014); generalizability to other populations untested.
- No clinical survival/outcome data — perforation is a geometric surrogate, not a confirmed failure endpoint.

## 6. Related Work

- Botermans et al. — ~5–5.6% perforation for IIP at the bone-driven ideal position in the **anterior maxilla** (this study reports 40.5% in premolars, far higher).
- Systematic review (163 studies): fresh-socket implant failure 3.60% vs 2.87% healed sites.

## 7. Glossary

- **IIP** — Immediate Implant Placement (type 1): implant installed directly into a fresh extraction socket.
- **Prosthetically-driven position**: implant aligned along the tooth long axis (line A) for ideal crown emergence.
- **Bone-driven position**: implant placed in the shortest non-perforating orientation that keeps ≥4 mm apical native bone and 2-mm margins.
- **ILAA (implant-line A angle)**: angle between the bone-driven implant axis and line A — i.e. the buccal tilt required to avoid perforation.
- **LCA (labial concavity angle)**: angle quantifying buccal-plate concavity; smaller LCA = deeper concavity = less buccal-palatal bone.
- **1PM / 2PM**: first / second maxillary premolar.
