## Why Ingested

구강 악취(Halitosis)의 세계적 유병률을 정량적으로 파악할 수 있는 기초 역학 데이터로, halitosis 카테고리의 첫 번째 SR+MA 페이지로 추가. 치주염-구취 연관 연구([[periodontics/silva-2017-periodontitis-halitosis-systematic-review]])와 쌍을 이루며 halitosis 역학의 baseline을 제공한다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 13 population-based studies (n=~10,000+) estimates global halitosis prevalence at 31.8% (95% CI 24.6–39.0%), with high heterogeneity driven by year of publication and country socioeconomic status.

## 한줄요약

13개 인구기반 연구를 메타분석한 결과 구강 악취(Halitosis) 유병률은 31.8%(95% CI 24.6–39.0%)이며, 출판 연도 및 국가 사회경제적 수준이 이질성의 주요 요인으로 확인됨.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Clinical Oral Investigations
- **Published**: 04 July 2017 (Volume 22, pages 47–55, 2018)
- **DOI**: 10.1007/s00784-017-2164-5
- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis + meta-regression
- **PRISMA compliant**: Yes
- **Databases searched**: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, SciELO (no date or language restrictions)

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First SR+MA** to estimate global halitosis prevalence using meta-regression to identify sources of heterogeneity.
2. Quantifies pooled prevalence at **31.8%** — approximately 1 in 3 adults and adolescents affected.
3. Identifies **year of publication** and **country socioeconomic status** (GDP-proxy) as significant meta-regression predictors — higher-income countries and more recent studies tend to show different prevalence.
4. Demonstrates **rising global trend** in reported halitosis prevalence.
5. Highlights need for clinical professional awareness for halitosis prevention and treatment.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search strategy**: Electronic searches in PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, SciELO without restrictions
- **Inclusion criteria**: Population-based observational studies reporting halitosis prevalence in adolescents and adults
- **Initial yield**: 584 articles screened by title/abstract
- **Final inclusion**: 13 articles met full inclusion criteria
- **Data synthesis**: 
  - Random-effects meta-analysis (DerSimonian & Laird method)
  - Meta-regression to explore heterogeneity sources (year of publication, socioeconomic status)
  - Sensitivity analyses conducted
- **Heterogeneity assessment**: I² statistics; high heterogeneity noted
- **Publication bias**: Assessed using Egger's test

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Value |
|---|---|
| Studies included | 13 population-based observational studies |
| Pooled halitosis prevalence | **31.8%** (95% CI 24.6–39.0%) |
| Heterogeneity | High (I² not specifically stated but acknowledged) |
| Meta-regression predictor 1 | Year of publication (significant) |
| Meta-regression predictor 2 | Country socioeconomic status (significant) |
| Trend | Worldwide rise in prevalence over time |

- Prevalence estimates varied widely across included studies, reflecting different measurement methods (organoleptic vs. VSC instruments vs. self-report) and populations.
- The high prevalence (nearly 1 in 3) underscores halitosis as a significant public health concern.
- Countries with lower socioeconomic status showed different prevalence patterns, possibly reflecting dietary habits, oral hygiene access, and healthcare behaviors.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **High heterogeneity** between studies limits precision of pooled estimate.
- Included studies used heterogeneous methods for halitosis assessment: organoleptic scoring, portable sulfide monitors (Halimeter), gas chromatography, and self-report — not directly comparable.
- Only 13 studies met inclusion criteria, suggesting a scarcity of population-level prevalence data.
- Meta-regression limited by small number of studies per covariate.
- Unable to fully adjust for confounders such as age distribution, oral health status, systemic conditions.
- Future research needed with standardized measurement protocols and representative samples from diverse global populations.

## 7. Glossary

- **Halitosis**: Bad breath / oral malodor; can be genuine (true), pseudo-halitosis (perceived but absent), or halitophobia (delusional)
- **VSC (Volatile Sulfur Compounds)**: H₂S, CH₃SH — primary odorants in genuine halitosis; measured by Halimeter or GC
- **Organoleptic scoring**: Clinician directly smells and scores breath intensity (gold standard reference method)
- **Meta-regression**: Regression of study-level effect estimates on study-level covariates to explain heterogeneity
- **SR+MA**: Systematic review with meta-analysis
- **Halimeter**: Portable VSC monitor (Interscan Corp.) commonly used in halitosis research
