## Why Ingested

기존 [[food-impaction/liang-2020-prevalence-associated-factors-retrospective]]는 식편압입·기능 5년 초과를 PCL 다변량 독립인자로 보고했지만, 인접 자연치의 치주지지(골소실)·잔존치조제 높이 차이는 다루지 않았다. 본 Liu 2025 후향 코호트(206명, 395 contact, Cox 모델)는 PCL의 다변량 독립인자로 식편압입(HR 2.75)에 더해 **잔존치조제 높이 차이(HR 2.51)와 인접치 진행성 골소실(>3/8 root, HR 5.83)** 이라는 해부학적 인자를 새로 식별해 기존 PCL 위험인자 근거를 확장·보강한다.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective cohort (206 patients, 395 implant-crown↔natural-tooth contact points, 2015–2025, Cox regression): PCL incidence 67.5% patient-level / 40.3% contact-level; independent predictors were food impaction (HR 2.75), residual ridge height discrepancy (HR 2.51), and severe adjacent alveolar bone loss >3/8 root length (HR 5.83).

## 한줄요약

후향 코호트(206명·395 contact·Cox 회귀): PCL 발생률 환자단위 67.5%·접촉점단위 40.3%; 다변량 독립인자는 식편압입(HR 2.75)·잔존치조제 높이 차이(HR 2.51)·인접치 중증 골소실 >3/8 root(HR 5.83). 인구·행동·보철 변수는 무의미.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Risk factors for proximal contact loss after implant-supported prosthetic restoration in adjacent natural teeth
- **Authors**: Lei Liu, Yue Jiang, Hui Xu, Yu Zheng, Xiao-Jun Han (Hebei Medical University Third Hospital / Shijiazhuang Second Hospital, China)
- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health 2025 Dec 17; 26(1):129
- **PMID**: 41402875 · **PMCID**: PMC12821840 · **DOI**: 10.1186/s12903-025-07480-3
- **Study type**: Single-center retrospective cohort, time-to-event (Cox proportional hazards)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies PCL incidence in a large cohort: 67.5% of patients, 40.3% of contact points (159/395).
- Uses **time-to-event Cox modeling** (rather than cross-sectional χ²) to identify *independent* predictors after adjustment.
- Identifies three modifiable/anatomic independent predictors: **food impaction (HR 2.75)**, **residual ridge height discrepancy (HR 2.51)**, and **advanced adjacent alveolar bone loss >3/8 root (HR 5.83)**.
- Demonstrates that demographic/behavioral/prosthetic variables (sex, age, smoking, bruxism, diabetes, restoration format, jaw arch, free-end span, opposing dentition) are **not** significantly associated with PCL — local biomechanics dominate.
- Introduces **residual ridge height discrepancy** as an under-discussed anatomic contributor.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 206 patients (86 M / 120 F; mean age 51, range 21–83), 395 implant-crown↔adjacent-natural-tooth contact points, June 2015–June 2025.
- Strict inclusion: single implant-supported crown, osseointegration (insertion torque ≥35 N·cm, no mobility), baseline intact contacts + periodontal health (PD ≤3 mm); excluded Stage III/IV periodontitis, adjacent mobility ≥Grade II, heavy smokers, bone-metabolism-affecting systemic disease/meds.
- **PCL diagnosis**: floss-resistance test (25-µm waxed floss / 8-µm shim-stock passes without resistance or with snap) AND/OR radiographic separation ≥0.2 mm at mid-contact.
- **PCL quantification**: 50 μm shim-stock strips × number seated = gap width.
- **Bone support**: (CEJ→crest) / (CEJ→apex) ratio on standardized periapical radiographs.
- **Residual ridge height discrepancy**: vertical crest-level difference >1 mm between implant platform and adjacent tooth.
- **Plunger cusp**: opposing/adjacent cusp projecting into embrasure.
- Unit of analysis = each contact point (independent). Univariate log-rank → variables P<0.05 entered into multivariate Cox. SPSS 26.0. 20% double-examiner reliability check.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| | Univariate | Multivariate Cox (HR, 95% CI, P) |
|---|---|---|
| Food impaction | 89/159 vs 27/236, χ²=90.83, P<0.001 | **2.75 (1.70–4.58), P<0.001** |
| Residual ridge height discrepancy | 33/159 vs 21/236, χ²=11.32, P<0.001 | **2.51 (1.37–4.58), P=0.003** |
| Alveolar bone loss >3/8 root | gradient χ²=12.51, P=0.005 | **5.83 (1.05–32.33), P=0.044** |
| Bone loss 2/8–3/8 | — | 2.48 (0.57–12.12), P=0.244 (ns) |
| Mesial contact location | 139/159 vs 138/236, χ²=38.00, P<0.001 | 1.46 (0.73–2.95), P=0.288 (ns) |
| Edentulism <5 y | 119/159 vs 142/236, χ²=9.13, P=0.003 | 0.67 (0.41–1.11), P=0.115 (ns) |
| Plunger cusp | 22/159 vs 11/236, χ²=10.45, P=0.001 | 1.75 (0.92–3.32), P=0.087 (ns) |

- PCL incidence: patient-level **67.5%** (139/206); contact-point level **40.3%** (159/395).
- **87% of PCL cases occurred at mesial contacts** (consistent with physiologic mesial drift), though mesial location lost significance in multivariate (likely collinearity).
- No association: sex, age, smoking, alcohol, diabetes, hypertension, bruxism, occlusal stability, placement site, jaw arch, free-end span, additional surgery, restoration format, pulp vitality, adjacent/opposing tooth type, opposing wear (all P>0.05).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Retrospective design → information/measurement bias; mild/early PCL may be underreported.
- Single-center → limited generalizability.
- Variable follow-up duration (partly addressed by Cox modeling).
- Unmeasured occlusal-force magnitude and 3D contact morphology; clinical/radiographic detection less precise than digital 3D.
- Risk identification only — prospective trials needed to test prevention/management strategies.

## 6. Related Work

- Liang et al. 2020 (PCL prevalence, mesial>distal, bridge susceptibility) — extended here with anatomic predictors.
- Wong et al. (43% PCL, mesial-mandible predominance); Byun et al., Pappous et al. (contact location inconsistent after adjustment); Koori et al., Pang et al. (mesial drift mechanism).

## 7. Glossary

- **PCL (proximal contact loss)**: opening of interproximal contact between an implant-supported crown and adjacent natural tooth.
- **Residual ridge height discrepancy**: vertical crest-level difference >1 mm between implant platform and adjacent natural tooth.
- **Plunger cusp**: opposing/adjacent cusp tip wedging into the interproximal embrasure during function.
- **Bone support ratio**: alveolar bone height ÷ total root length [(CEJ→crest)/(CEJ→apex)].
- **Shim stock**: thin (8–50 µm) metal foil strip used to gauge contact tightness.
