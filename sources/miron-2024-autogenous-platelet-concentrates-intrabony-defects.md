## Why Ingested

Periodontal 골내결손(intrabony defect) 재생에서 PRF(Platelet-Rich Fibrin)가 실제로 어디까지 근거가 있는지를 55-RCT 규모로 정리한 최신·최대 SR+MA. 기존 [[wiki/periodontics/stavropoulos-2021-periodontal-regeneration-intrabony-nma]]의 30-RCT NMA(재생술 > OFD, 병용요법 우세)에 "그 재생술의 자가 biologic 버전인 PRF는 EMD·bone graft·GTR과 대등하고, graft에 PRF를 더하면 추가 이득"이라는 층을 보강한다. iPRF 임상 RCT [[wiki/periodontics/sherif-2025-iprf-vitamin-c-nonsurgical-periodontitis]]의 상위 근거 우산 역할도 한다. 동시 인제스트되는 자매 PRF SR·MA — [[wiki/periodontics/miron-2024-prf-furcation-defects-sr-ma]] (분지부), [[wiki/periodontics/barootchi-2024-autologous-platelet-concentrates-root-coverage]] (치근피개) — 와 함께 PRF 적응증 지도를 완성한다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 55 RCTs on PRF for periodontal intrabony defects: adding PRF to OFD gives ~1.3 mm PPD reduction, ~1.2 mm CAL gain, ~1.6 mm radiographic bone fill vs OFD alone; PRF is equivalent to EMD/PRP/bone graft/barrier membrane; adding PRF to a bone graft (and adding metformin/bisphosphonates/statins to PRF) yields further significant gains.

## 한줄요약

치주 골내결손에 대한 PRF 55개 RCT SR+MA: OFD 단독 대비 PRF 병용 시 탐침깊이 약 1.3mm 감소·임상부착수준 약 1.2mm 획득·방사선 골충전 약 1.6mm 개선; PRF는 EMD·PRP·골이식재·차폐막과 대등하고, 골이식재에 PRF를 더하거나 PRF에 메트포르민·비스포스포네이트·스타틴을 더하면 추가 유의 개선.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Periodontology 2000 (2024); DOI 10.1111/prd.12598; PMID 39425513; PMCID PMC11808470.
- **Design**: PRISMA-compliant systematic review with meta-analysis; protocol registered INPLASY 202430001. Searches to Oct 1, 2023 (PubMed/MEDLINE, Cochrane CENTRAL, Scopus, Embase, Lilacs + gray literature), no language restriction.
- **Focused question**: efficacy of PRF alone or with other biomaterials in regenerative surgery of intrabony defects vs OFD alone or vs other established regenerative methods (PICOS: RCTs only).
- **Included**: 596 records screened → **55 RCTs** included, organized into **21 comparison categories** grouped into **five Groups (I–V)**.
- **Outcomes**: probing pocket depth (PPD), clinical attachment level (CAL), radiographic bone fill (RBF); weighted mean differences + forest plots.
- **Note on independence**: senior author Richard J. Miron discloses PRF-related intellectual property (patents/trademarks; founder, Miron R&D in Dentistry LLC).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Largest and most up-to-date PRF-in-intrabony-defect SR+MA to date (55 RCTs vs ≤21 in prior SRs), spanning OFD-adjunct, head-to-head vs other biologics, graft-adjunct, and PRF+biomolecule combinations.
- Quantifies the OFD/PRF benefit: ~1.3 mm PPD, ~1.2 mm CAL, ~1.6 mm RBF over OFD alone (23 RCTs).
- Establishes clinical **equivalence** of PRF to EMD, PRP, bone graft, and barrier membrane (Group III) — i.e., an autogenous, low-cost biologic matches commercial regenerative materials.
- Shows **adding PRF to a bone graft** (Group IV) significantly improves PPD/CAL/RBF vs graft alone, and adding small biomolecules (metformin, bisphosphonates, statins) to PRF (Group V) adds further benefit.
- Documents major **protocol heterogeneity and underreporting** (35% of studies didn't report the centrifuge system; only 3/55 included smokers).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Five Groups**:
  - **Group I** — OFD vs OFD/PRF (and one OFD vs OFD/T-PRF trial). 23 studies.
  - **Group II** — comparative PRF protocols: L-PRF vs Titanium-PRF (T-PRF). 2 studies.
  - **Group III** — comparisons *to* PRF: OFD/PRP, OFD/BG/PRGF, OFD/EMD, OFD/BG/EMD, OFD/BM (barrier membrane/GTR), OFD/BG/BM, OFD/BG each vs the PRF counterpart. 15 studies.
  - **Group IV** — *addition* of PRF: OFD/BG vs OFD/BG/PRF, OFD/GTR vs +PRF, OFD/EMD vs +PRF, OFD/BG/BM vs +PRF. 20 studies.
  - **Group V** — addition of biomaterial/biomolecule to PRF: PRF vs PRF/BG, /antibiotic, /metformin, /bisphosphonate, /statins, PRF/BG vs PRF/BG/statins, PRF vs PRF/LLLT. 13 studies.
- **Screening**: two independent reviewers (R.J.M., N.E.E.), Cohen's κ concordance, hand search + cross-referencing.
- **Centrifugation reporting**: Remi (R-4C) in 19/55 (35%), IntraSpin/Hettich PC-02 in 4/55 (7%), **not reported in 19/55 (35%)**; 32/55 used 3000 rpm × 10 min.
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane RoB; e.g., Group I = 5 low / 16 unclear / 1 high; Group III = 4 low / 9 unclear / 2 high.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Group I — OFD/PRF vs OFD alone (22 studies pooled):**
- PPD: overall MD **1.27 mm** (95% CI 1.03–1.51; p<0.00001; I²=87%). PRF subgroup MD 1.27 mm; T-PRF MD 1.33 mm.
- CAL: overall MD **1.22 mm** (95% CI 0.91–1.53; p<0.00001; I²=89%). PRF MD 1.24; T-PRF MD 0.89.
- RBF: overall MD **1.59 mm** (95% CI 1.35–1.82; p<0.00001; I²=90%). PRF MD 1.56; T-PRF MD 2.07.
- (23 RCTs narrative: 20 sig. for PPD, 16 for CAL, 18 for bone fill.)

**Group II — L-PRF vs T-PRF (2 studies):**
- PPD: MD **0.64 mm favoring T-PRF** (95% CI 0.18–1.09; p=0.006; I²=0%).
- CAL: no significant difference (MD 0.40 mm; 95% CI −0.19 to 0.99; p=0.19).

**Group III — comparisons to PRF (15 studies):** no overall advantage of PRF.
- PPD: overall MD **−0.11 mm** (95% CI −0.37 to 0.16; p=0.43, slightly favoring control).
- CAL: overall MD **−0.13 mm** (p=0.39). Two significant subgroups: OFD/BG/PRF > OFD/BG/BM by 0.54 mm (2 studies; p=0.02); OFD/BG > OFD/PRF by 0.40 mm (6 studies; p=0.004, i.e., graft > PRF for CAL).
- RBF: overall MD **−0.01 mm** (p=0.95). → PRF ≈ EMD ≈ PRP ≈ BM; graft slightly better for space-maintenance-dependent CAL.

**Group IV — addition of PRF (20 studies):** favors PRF.
- PPD: overall MD **0.59 mm** favoring PRF (95% CI 0.16–1.02; p=0.007; I²=92%). BG vs BG/PRF: MD 0.63 mm (p=0.009).
- CAL: overall MD **0.57 mm** favoring PRF (95% CI 0.03–1.12; p=0.04). BG vs BG/PRF MD 0.66 mm (p=0.03).
- RBF: overall MD **0.63 mm** favoring PRF (95% CI 0.40–0.86; p<0.00001). BG vs BG/PRF MD 0.60 mm (16 studies; p<0.00001).

**Group V — biomolecule addition to PRF (13 studies):** favors combinations.
- PPD: overall MD **0.68 mm** favoring test (95% CI 0.50–0.86; p<0.00001). PRF vs PRF/BG MD 0.52 mm (6 studies); PRF/BG vs PRF/BG/statins MD 0.66 mm.
- CAL: overall MD **0.85 mm** favoring test (95% CI 0.68–1.02; p<0.00001; I²=36%). PRF vs PRF/BG MD 0.88 mm; PRF/BG vs PRF/BG/statins MD 0.54 mm.
- RBF: PRF vs PRF/BG MD **0.80 mm** favoring the graft addition (5 studies; p<0.0001).
- PRF/antibiotic and PRF/LLLT showed **no** significant advantage; metformin, bisphosphonates, and statins did.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- High between-study heterogeneity (I² often 80–95%) and wide protocol variability (centrifuge/rpm/time), with 35% of studies not reporting the centrifugation system.
- Limited number of RCTs per specific comparison (many Group III/V subgroups rest on a single trial); several unclear/high risk-of-bias studies.
- Only 3/55 RCTs included smokers → limited external validity.
- No human histological confirmation of regeneration for PRF; no PROMs pooled.
- Conflict of interest: senior author holds PRF IP.
- Future needs: standardized PRF protocols, longer-term outcomes, human histology, PROMs, and further study of PRF + small-biomolecule combinations.

## 6. Related Work

- Stavropoulos et al. 2021 (JCPE) — 30-RCT network meta-analysis of intrabony-defect regeneration; PRF here is the autogenous analogue whose OFD-adjunct MDs align with that CPG evidence base.
- EFP S3-Level Clinical Practice Guideline for periodontitis (Sanz et al.) — regenerative surgery recommended for deep (≥3 mm) residual intrabony defects; this SR updates the PRF evidence for that recommendation.
- AAP best-evidence SR & network meta-analysis of periodontal regeneration — this SR reports outcomes exceeding it in magnitude/number of studies.
- Sibling PRF SR+MAs (Miron 2024 furcation; Barootchi 2024 root coverage) — extend PRF indications beyond intrabony defects.

## 7. Glossary

- **APC (autogenous platelet concentrate)**: blood-derived, autologous concentrate of platelets/growth factors (PRP = 1st gen; PRF = 2nd gen).
- **PRF (platelet-rich fibrin)**: anticoagulant-free centrifuged fibrin clot entrapping platelets + leukocytes, giving sustained growth-factor release. **L-PRF** = leukocyte-PRF; **T-PRF** = titanium-tube PRF; **iPRF** = injectable PRF.
- **OFD (open flap debridement)**: access-flap surgery without a regenerative material (the comparator "surgery alone").
- **EMD**: enamel matrix derivative (Emdogain). **BG**: bone graft. **BM**: barrier membrane / GTR. **PRGF**: plasma rich in growth factors.
- **PPD / CAL / RBF**: probing pocket depth / clinical attachment level / radiographic bone fill (primary outcomes).
- **Intrabony (infrabony) defect**: vertical/angular bony defect (1-, 2-, 3-wall) associated with Stage III/IV periodontitis.
- **MD**: mean difference (weighted, mm). **LLLT**: low-level laser therapy.
