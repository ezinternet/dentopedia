## One-line Summary
SR+MA of 6 RCTs (n=2,776) — Motivational Interviewing (MI) significantly prevents Early Childhood Caries (ECC) measured by dmfs; effect independent of child age, session count, but improves with **session quality** and ≥3-year follow-up.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Journal of Dentistry of Shiraz University of Medical Sciences, 23(1 Suppl):161–168, June 2022
- DOI: 10.30476/DENTJODS.2021.87985.1303
- Study type: Systematic review + meta-analysis (sr+ma) of RCTs
- Search period: 1989 → April 2020
- Databases: Web of Science, PubMed, Scopus, PsycINFO, Cochrane Library
- Statistical model: Fixed-effect, mean difference + 95% CI

## 2. Key Contributions
- First (or among first) SR+MA quantifying MI effect on ECC prevention specifically
- Uses **dmfs** (decayed-missing-filled surfaces in primary teeth) as outcome — clinically meaningful
- Sensitivity analysis confirms result is not driven by any single study
- Emphasizes **quality** of MI sessions over **quantity** as the operative variable
- 3-year minimum follow-up identified as practically necessary to see effect

## 3. Methodology
- PECO: Population (caregivers of children at ECC risk), Exposure (MI), Comparison (standard oral health education), Outcome (ECC measured by dmfs)
- RCT-only inclusion
- Egger's test for publication bias (visual inspection)
- Fixed-effect model

## 4. Key Results
- **6 RCTs, total 2,776 participants**
- MI had a statistically significant effect on preventing ECC
- No significant publication bias detected
- Sensitivity analysis: deleting any single study did not affect the significance of the pooled result
- Effect appears robust across child age groups (infants and children)
- Quality of sessions > number of sessions
- ≥3 years follow-up needed for full effect

## 5. Limitations
- Only 6 included RCTs — small evidence base
- Fixed-effect model assumes one true effect — may understate uncertainty if heterogeneity exists
- Heterogeneity statistics (I²) not prominently reported in the abstract
- Visual Egger inspection only — formal test not described
- Cultural/regional variation in MI delivery uncontrolled
- Journal: J Dent Shiraz — moderate-impact regional journal

## 6. Related Work
- Cartes-Velásquez 2024 — MI for adult oral morbidities (SR+MA)
- Lundahl 2013 — MI in medical care settings (SR+MA, cross-domain reference)
- Miller WR, Rollnick S (2008) Motivational Interviewing in Healthcare — foundational text
- Pediatric dentistry guidelines on caregiver education

## 7. Glossary
- **MI (Motivational Interviewing)**: counseling style developed by Miller & Rollnick to elicit behavior change through resolving ambivalence
- **ECC (Early Childhood Caries)**: caries in primary teeth of children under 71 months
- **dmfs**: decayed-missing-filled surfaces (primary dentition counterpart of DMFS)
- **PECO**: Population, Exposure, Comparison, Outcome framework
- **Fixed-effect model**: meta-analytic model assuming single true effect across studies
