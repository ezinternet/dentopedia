## One-line Summary
Systematic review (13 studies, transcrestal + lateral maxillary sinus elevation): CGF — alone or with graft — appears to enhance new bone formation, reduce postoperative sequelae and support high implant survival, but evidence is heterogeneous and RCT-poor.

## 한줄요약
체계적 문헌고찰(13편, 경치조골+측방창 상악동거상): CGF는 단독·이식재 병용 모두 신생골 형성·술후 후유증·임플란트 생존을 개선하는 경향이나, 근거가 이질적이고 RCT가 부족함.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First dedicated systematic review of CGF specifically in maxillary sinus augmentation (prior PC evidence focused on PRF/PRP).
- Synthesizes clinical, radiographic and histological outcomes across both transcrestal and lateral approaches.
- Maps CGF effects on vascularization, new bone quality/quantity, postoperative complications and implant survival.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Design: systematic review (no meta-analysis), PRISMA workflow.
- Inclusion: transcrestal or lateral MSFE with CGF, alone or combined with grafting materials; studies up to March 2024.
- 783 records screened → 13 studies met eligibility.
- Outcomes: postoperative complications, new bone formation (radiographic/histologic), implant survival.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- 13/783 studies eligible; designs heterogeneous (RCTs + non-randomized).
- Majority reported CGF positively influenced sinus augmentation: improved vascularization and regeneration, better quality/quantity of newly formed bone, reduced postoperative sequelae, high implant survival.
- No pooled effect size (qualitative synthesis); authors emphasize data variability precludes definitive conclusions.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Author-stated: high heterogeneity, limited number and quality of RCTs, no quantitative pooling, risk of reporting/publication bias toward positive results.
- Additional: outcome definitions (histomorphometry vs radiographic gain) inconsistent across studies; CGF preparation protocols not standardized; mixed transcrestal/lateral pooling limits approach-specific inference.

## 6. Related Work
- Guo 2020 (PC-TSFE meta-analysis — platelet concentration showed no significant bone gain advantage in TSFE).
- Shahood 2024 (CGF + Bio-Oss Collagen prospective OSFE).
- General CGF/Sticky bone regeneration literature.

## 7. Glossary
- CGF (Concentrated Growth Factor): centrifuged autologous fibrin/growth-factor concentrate, a later-generation platelet concentrate.
- MSFE / sinus augmentation: maxillary sinus floor elevation (transcrestal or lateral).
- New bone formation: histomorphometric % vital bone or radiographic vertical/volumetric gain.
