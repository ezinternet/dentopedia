## One-line Summary
Scoping review of 138 RCTs (368 toothpaste formulations) mapping active ingredients, fluoride type, and abrasives in desensitizing toothpastes, with potassium and CSP most common actives and MFP/NaF the dominant fluorides.

## 한줄요약
Scoping review(138 RCT·368 제형): 탈감작 치약의 활성성분·불소·연마제를 지도화 — 포타슘 화합물·CSP가 최다 활성성분, MFP·NaF가 주요 불소.

## 1. Document Information
- J Appl Oral Sci 2022;30:e20210410. PROSPERO CRD42018086815; search to Aug 2021.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Inventories the full landscape of desensitizing-toothpaste formulations from RCTs.
- Quantifies prevalence of each active ingredient, fluoride form, and abrasive base.
- Documents historical growth of formulations (sharp rise after 2010).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Scoping review of 138 RCTs; 368 formulations classified.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Actives: potassium (n=68, 19%), CSP (n=37, 10%), strontium (n=28, 8%), arginine (n=29, 8%), SnF2 (n=21, 6%), hydroxyapatite (n=9, 2%), others.
- Fluoride: MFP (n=105, 29%), NaF (n=82, 22%). Abrasive: silica (n=84, 23%), calcium (n=64, 17%).
- 34 placebo (9%), 98 fluoride-only controls (27%), 236 (64%) with active DH ingredients.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Scoping (not efficacy-ranking) review; no comparative effectiveness conclusion.

## 6. Related Work
- Companion to bioactive-toothpaste SR; supports formulation selection.

## 7. Glossary
- CSP: calcium sodium phosphosilicate. MFP: sodium monofluorophosphate. SnF2: stannous fluoride.
