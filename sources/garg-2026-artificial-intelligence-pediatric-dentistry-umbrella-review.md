## Why Ingested

This paper seeds the new `artificial-intelligence` category — the first AI/ML evidence-synthesis page in the wiki. As an umbrella review it sits one tier above primary SRs and anchors how AI evidence in (pediatric) dentistry should be appraised: AMSTAR-2 quality, GROOVE/CCA overlap, and CNN-imaging diagnostic metrics. It is paired with the sibling ML methodology review [[wiki/artificial-intelligence/gomez-rios-2025-machine-learning-data-analysis-pediatric-dentistry-sr]] (created alongside this one), which it reinforces by independently confirming that AI image-based diagnostics are promising but rest on low/critically-low-quality primary evidence.

## One-line Summary

Umbrella review (7 SRs, 109 primary studies) finding AI — chiefly CNNs for imaging — reaches pooled sensitivity/specificity 80–83% and AUC 0.87–0.91 in pediatric dentistry, but most underlying reviews are low/critically-low quality, so evidence is preliminary (abstract-only).

## 2. Key Contributions

- First umbrella review synthesizing systematic-review evidence on AI across pediatric dentistry (caries detection, risk prediction, anomaly identification, treatment planning).
- Quantifies methodological quality (AMSTAR-2) and primary-study overlap (corrected covered area, CCA, via the GROOVE tool) across the included SRs — a meta-epidemiological appraisal rather than a new pooled diagnostic estimate.
- Surfaces the central caveat for the field: diagnostic performance looks strong, but it is built on reviews of low/critically-low quality.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases:** PubMed/MEDLINE and the Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews, searched up to 31 August 2025.
- **Quality appraisal:** AMSTAR-2 applied to each included systematic review.
- **Overlap quantification:** corrected covered area (CCA) computed with the GROOVE tool.
- **Inclusion:** systematic reviews on AI applications in pediatric dentistry.
- Abstract-only; PRISMA flow, per-SR AMSTAR-2 domains, and the citation-matrix detail are not available from the abstract.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **7 systematic reviews** included, spanning **109 primary studies**.
- Topic distribution: **4** on early childhood caries; **1 each** on dental anomalies, cleft lip and palate, and caries risk prediction.
- **CNNs for imaging** achieved pooled **sensitivity/specificity 80%–83%** and **AUC 0.87–0.91**.
- **Methodological quality:** most reviews were **low or critically low** (lack of protocol registration; limited risk-of-bias assessment).
- **Overlap:** GROOVE indicated **moderate overlap, CCA = 8.27%**.
- **Conclusion:** AI shows promising diagnostic performance, particularly for image-based tasks, but current evidence remains preliminary and should be interpreted with caution.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Underlying SR evidence is predominantly low/critically-low quality — the umbrella review's conclusions inherit this weakness.
- Strong metrics concentrate on image-based diagnostic tasks (CNNs); non-imaging applications (risk prediction, treatment planning) are thinner.
- Lack of protocol registration and limited bias assessment in primary reviews; future SRs need pre-registration and standardized reporting.
- This wiki page is abstract-only — per-SR breakdowns, subgroup metrics, and full appraisal tables were not retrievable.

## 7. Glossary

- **Umbrella review:** an overview of systematic reviews — synthesizes SR-level rather than primary-study-level evidence.
- **AMSTAR-2:** A MeaSurement Tool to Assess systematic Reviews, version 2 — critical-appraisal instrument grading SR methodological quality.
- **GROOVE / CCA:** Graphical Representation of Overlap for OVErviews tool; quantifies the corrected covered area (CCA), the degree to which the same primary studies recur across the included SRs (slight <5%, moderate 5–10%, high 10–15%, very high >15%).
- **CNN (Convolutional Neural Network):** deep-learning architecture for image analysis; dominant in dental radiographic/image diagnostic AI.
- **AUC (Area Under the Curve):** area under the ROC curve; overall diagnostic discrimination (1.0 = perfect, 0.5 = chance).
- **Early childhood caries (ECC):** caries in children under 6 years; the most-represented topic (4/7 SRs) in this umbrella review.
