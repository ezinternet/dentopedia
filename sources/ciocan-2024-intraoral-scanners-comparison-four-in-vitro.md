## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Diagnostics 2024, 14, 1453
- **DOI**: 10.3390/diagnostics14131453
- **Published**: 8 July 2024
- **Study type**: In vitro; Carol Davila University of Medicine and Pharmacy, Bucharest, Romania

## 2. Key Contributions
- Head-to-head in vitro comparison of 4 different IOS devices on trueness (deviation from reference) and precision (reproducibility)
- Provides clinically relevant accuracy benchmarks across devices tested in identical conditions
- Identifies which scanners perform better for specific clinical scenarios

## 3. Methodology
- **Design**: In vitro; standardized dental model scanned with 4 IOS systems
- **Metrics**: Trueness (deviation from reference scan, µm) and precision (scan-to-scan repeatability, µm)
- **Reference**: Laboratory scanner or CT scan as gold standard
- **Setting**: Carol Davila University, Bucharest

## 4. Key Results
- Statistically significant differences in trueness and precision between scanners
- Specific performance ranking dependent on arch region (anterior vs posterior)
- All devices within clinically acceptable range for single-unit prosthetics but variation increases for full-arch scanning

## 5. Limitations
- In vitro setting; clinical (intraoral) performance may differ due to moisture, saliva, patient movement
- Limited to 4 devices; rapidly changing market
- Single model/typodont may not represent all clinical tooth preparations
