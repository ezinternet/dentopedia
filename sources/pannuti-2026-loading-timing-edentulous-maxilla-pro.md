## Why Ingested

기존 [[immediate-implant/qian-2024-anterior-maxillary-implants-placement-loading-nma]]와 [[immediate-implant/esposito-2015-immediate-loading-vs-delayed-anterior-rct]]가 즉시·지연 로딩의 임상·방사선 outcome(생존율, MBL)을 다뤘다면, 본 SR (Pannuti 2026)은 무치악 상악 full-arch에서 어떤 환자보고결과(Patient-Reported Outcome, PRO)·임상가보고결과(Clinician-Reported Outcome, ClinRO)와 측정도구(PROM/CROM)가 실제로 쓰였는지를 메타-방법론 관점에서 정리한다 — outcome 표준화 부재라는 근거 공백을 가시화해 향후 무치악 상악 로딩 결정 근거의 한계를 보강.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review of 5 prospective studies (4 RCT + 1 non-RCT; 121 patients, 724 implants) in edentulous maxilla comparing immediate vs early/delayed loading: VAS was the dominant PROM (80% of studies, 7 PROs), OHIP-19/20 and McGill Pain Questionnaire for QoL/pain, but the 12 identified clinician-reported outcomes (ClinROs) showed no standardized approach — high heterogeneity, standardization of PROM/CROM reporting needed.

## 한줄요약

무치악 상악 즉시 vs 조기/지연 로딩을 비교한 전향적 연구 5편(RCT 4 + non-RCT 1; 환자 121명, 임플란트 724개)의 체계적 문헌고찰: 환자보고결과(PRO) 측정은 시각아날로그척도(Visual Analog Scale, VAS)가 80%로 지배적(7개 PRO)이고 OHIP-19/20·McGill 통증설문이 삶의 질·통증을 보조 평가했으나, 도출된 12개 임상가보고결과(ClinRO)는 표준화된 방식이 전혀 없어 이질성이 높음 — PROM/CROM 보고 표준화가 필요하다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First focused synthesis of *which* PROs/ClinROs and *which* measurement tools (PROMs/CROMs) are actually used in maxillary edentulous loading-timing trials — distinct from prior SRs that pooled survival/marginal bone loss.
- Quantifies a measurement-standardization gap: VAS dominates patient-side reporting (80%) while 12 clinician-side outcomes are reported with no inter-study consensus.
- Maps PRO instrument hierarchy: VAS (broad, but response-bias/validation-limited) → OHIP-19/20 (validated, edentulous-relevant) → McGill Pain Questionnaire (pain-specific).
- Flags a calibration gap: only 1 of 5 studies reported assessor reliability testing, threatening comparability of subjective CROMs.
- Connects findings to ID-COSM (Implant Dentistry Core Outcome Set and Measurement) — satisfaction/comfort are mandatory domains, yet timing-specific validated PROMs do not exist.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Design: Systematic review of prospective interventional/observational studies; descriptive synthesis only (frequencies/percentages in STATA 16.1) — qualitative pooling not feasible.
- Focused questions: Q1 = which PROs/ClinROs used; Q2 = which PROMs/CROMs used.
- PICOS: P = completely edentulous maxilla (or scheduled for complete maxillary extraction) needing full-arch fixed/removable implant prosthesis; I = implant therapy with immediate (IL, ≤1 wk), early (EL, 1 wk–2 mo), or delayed (DL, >2 mo) loading; C = IL vs DL / IL vs EL / EL vs DL; O = primary PROs/PROMs, secondary ClinROs/CROMs; S = RCT, non-RCT, cohort, case series ≥10.
- Exclusions: retrospective, cross-sectional, case report, in vitro, preclinical; pre-placed implants; pterygoid/zygomatic implants; single-time-point or single-arm designs.
- Search: MEDLINE (PubMed), Embase, Scopus, Web of Science, Cochrane CENTRAL; Jan 2014 – May 2024; English.
- Screening: 2 reviewers via Rayyan; Cohen's kappa = 0.81.
- Risk of bias: Cochrane RoB 2 (RCTs); ROBINS-I (non-RCT).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Selection flow: 2232 records → 777 duplicates removed → 1455 screened → 68 full-text → **5 included** (4 RCT + 1 non-RCT); 121 patients, 724 implants; 4 compared IL vs DL, 1 compared IL vs EL.
- Settings: all 5 university-based, European (Serbia, Spain ×2, Belgium ×2); 6–8 implants/patient; mean age IL 45–71y, DL 49–67y; 80% externally funded.
- Follow-up range: 7 days (Vercruyssen) to 2 years (Bernard) — short relative to prosthesis lifetime.
- PROMs: VAS in 80% (4/5), capturing **7 PROs** — pain, overall satisfaction, comfort, speech, masticatory function, esthetics, self-esteem. OHIP-19 (Markovic) and OHIP-20 (Montero) for oral health-related QoL; McGill Pain Questionnaire (MPQ-DLV) + HRQOL-15 (Vercruyssen); condition-specific chewing ability/masticatory performance (Montero).
- ClinROs: **12 identified** across 4 studies, no consensus — implant stability (RFA Osstell/Penguin, ISQ 1–100), insertion torque, clinician esthetic-satisfaction VAS, surgical/prosthetic complications, occlusal force (Dental PRESCALE), EMG (MYOMED_932TM), bone loss, probing depth, BoP, plaque index, implant-placement accuracy (CT/CBCT matching), surgical time.
- Risk of bias: 4 RCTs "some concerns" overall (low across most domains); 1 non-RCT (Penarrocha-Oltra) moderate risk.
- Calibration: only Markovic reported inter/intra-rater reliability; Penarrocha-Oltra used a single trained clinician; remainder "not reported."

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Author-stated: small number of heterogeneous studies; varied outcome measures, assessment intervals, and loading-timeline definitions; limited geographic/cultural diversity (European university settings only); max follow-up 2 years; no PROM validated specifically to detect IL-vs-EL/DL timing differences.
- Methodological: descriptive synthesis only — cannot quantify whether loading timing changes PROs, only catalogs measurement practice; studies not weighted by risk of bias.
- Future work: develop/validate timing-sensitive PROMs; standardize/harmonize PROM-CROM reporting; report assessor training/calibration; longer follow-up with defined maintenance intervals.

## 6. Related Work

- Cites prior loading-protocol SRs focused on clinical/radiographic outcomes (Gallucci et al.; Papaspyridakos et al.) — this review fills the PRO/ClinRO measurement gap they did not address.
- Anchored to ID-COSM core outcome set (Tonetti et al.) and ID-COSM-related PROM guidance (Needleman et al.).
- Loading-timing definitions per Esposito et al. / Schimmel et al.

## 7. Glossary

- PRO / PROM — Patient-Reported Outcome / Patient-Reported Outcome Measure (structured tool capturing PROs directly from the patient).
- ClinRO / CROM — Clinician-Reported Outcome / Clinician-Reported Outcome Measure (clinician's clinical/objective assessment and the tool/scale used).
- IL / EL / DL — Immediate Loading (≤1 wk) / Early Loading (1 wk–2 mo) / Delayed Loading (>2 mo).
- VAS — Visual Analog Scale.
- OHIP-19 / OHIP-20 — Oral Health Impact Profile short forms (oral health-related quality of life), edentulous-relevant.
- ID-COSM — Implant Dentistry Core Outcome Set and Measurement.
- RFA / ISQ — Resonance Frequency Analysis / Implant Stability Quotient (1–100).
- BoP — Bleeding on Probing.
- RoB 2 / ROBINS-I — Cochrane risk-of-bias tools for RCTs / non-randomized intervention studies.
