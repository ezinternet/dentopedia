## Why Ingested

상악동거상술(Sinus Floor Elevation, SFE) 후 술후 부비동염(Postoperative Sinusitis) 발생률에 대한 정량적 근거가 [[wiki/overviews/sinus-lift-technique-selection]]에 누락되어 있으며, 협골 임플란트(Zygomatic Implant, ZI)와의 직접 비교 데이터는 [[wiki/overviews/odontogenic-maxillary-sinusitis-overview]]의 의인성 상악동염(Iatrogenic Sinusitis) 예방 축을 보완한다. 이 메타분석은 술식별 부비동염 유병률을 정량화해 술식 선택 위험편익 계산에 기여한다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (27 studies: 18 sinus lift, 9 zygomatic implant) comparing pooled sinusitis prevalence: sinus lift 1.11% vs zygomatic implants 3.76%; technique matters — zygomatic sinus slot 21.62%, extrasinus 0.00%, lateral window 1.35%, transcrestal 0.00%.

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (27편: 상악동거상술 18편, 협골 임플란트 9편) — 상악동거상술 후 부비동염 1.11% 대 협골 임플란트 3.76%; 술식 의존적: 협골 임플란트 내막하법(Sinus Slot) 21.62% vs 외막법(Extrasinus) 0.00%, 측방창법(Lateral Window) 1.35% vs 경치조골법(Transcrestal) 0.00%.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First meta-analysis to directly compare postoperative sinusitis prevalence between sinus floor elevation (SFE) procedures and zygomatic implant (ZI) placement in the same study.
2. Provides technique-level sinusitis rates within each procedure category (lateral vs transcrestal for SFE; sinus slot vs intrasinus vs extrasinus for ZI).
3. Establishes that sinusitis risk is technique-dependent, not procedure-dependent per se — the extrasinus ZI technique yields 0.00% sinusitis, lower than lateral SFE.
4. Quantifies the particularly high sinusitis risk of the sinus slot ZI technique (21.62%) compared to all other approaches.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Search strategy**: Six databases searched (likely PubMed, Embase, Cochrane, Scopus, Web of Science, LILACS); 2,419 references identified after deduplication.

**Inclusion criteria**: Clinical studies reporting sinusitis incidence/prevalence following sinus lift (lateral or transcrestal) or zygomatic implant placement. Studies in English or Portuguese.

**Exclusion criteria**: Non-human studies, case reports, reviews without primary data, studies not reporting sinusitis as an outcome.

**Final inclusion**: 27 studies — 18 sinus lift studies, 9 zygomatic implant studies.

**Risk of bias tools**:
- RoB 2.0 (for RCTs)
- ROBINS-I (for non-randomized studies)

**Statistical method**: Random-effects model (DerSimonian-Laird). Prevalence calculated based on total patients (not procedures). Subgroup analysis by technique within each procedure category. Heterogeneity assessed with I² and Cochran Q.

**Sinusitis definition**: Based on EPOS 2012 criteria (European Position Paper on Rhinosinusitis).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Sinus Lift Procedure — Pooled Sinusitis Prevalence

| Approach | Pooled Prevalence | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|
| **All sinus lift (overall)** | **1.11%** | 0.30–2.28% |
| Lateral window approach | 1.35% | 0.34–2.80% |
| Transcrestal technique | 0.00% | 0.00–3.18% |
| SALSA technique | 1.20% | 0.00–5.10% |

### Zygomatic Implant Placement — Pooled Sinusitis Prevalence

| Technique | Pooled Prevalence | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|
| **All zygomatic implants (overall)** | **3.76%** | 0.12–10.29% |
| Sinus slot technique | 21.62% | 9.62–36.52% |
| Intrasinus technique | 4.36% | 0.33–11.08% |
| Extrasinus technique | 0.00% | 0.00–1.22% |

### Key Comparisons

- Overall: ZI (3.76%) vs SFE (1.11%) — zygomatic implants show ~3.4× higher sinusitis prevalence.
- The sinus slot technique (ZI) drives the ZI risk upward; extrasinus ZI is the lowest-risk zygomatic technique.
- Transcrestal SFE has the lowest sinusitis prevalence of all approaches surveyed.

### Study Selection
- 2,419 references screened → 18 SFE studies + 9 ZI studies = 27 included

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

1. **High heterogeneity** across studies due to variable follow-up periods, patient populations, and sinusitis diagnostic criteria.
2. **Observational design** dominant in the included studies — most are prospective/retrospective cohorts, not RCTs; confounding is possible.
3. **Technique ascription**: Zygomatic implant techniques have evolved and overlap; operator skill and learning curve are not captured in prevalence pooling.
4. **Wide CIs for rare events**: Some subgroup estimates (e.g., transcrestal 0.00% CI 0.00–3.18%, extrasinus 0.00% CI 0.00–1.22%) have zero point estimates with wide intervals, reflecting small study counts.
5. **Publication bias** for rare complications cannot be excluded.
6. Future prospective studies should standardize sinusitis diagnosis and technique description to allow more precise pooling.

## 7. Glossary

- **SFE (Sinus Floor Elevation, 상악동저거상술)**: Augmentation procedure to increase vertical bone height in the posterior maxilla for implant placement.
- **Lateral window approach (측방창 접근법)**: Classical SFE technique using a lateral bone window to access the sinus.
- **Transcrestal technique (경치조골 접근법)**: SFE performed through the alveolar crest without a lateral window.
- **SALSA technique**: Subantral lateral sinus augmentation — a lateral window variant.
- **ZI (Zygomatic implant, 협골 임플란트)**: Extra-long implants anchored in the zygomatic bone for severe maxillary atrophy.
- **Sinus slot technique (내막하법)**: Original ZI technique passing through the sinus (highest sinusitis risk).
- **Intrasinus technique (사이너스내 경로법)**: ZI trajectory through the sinus, variant of slot technique.
- **Extrasinus technique (외막법)**: ZI trajectory fully outside the sinus (lowest sinusitis risk among ZI techniques).
- **EPOS**: European Position Paper on Rhinosinusitis and Nasal Polyps — defines sinusitis diagnostic criteria.
- **RoB 2.0 / ROBINS-I**: Risk of bias assessment tools for RCTs and non-randomized studies respectively.
