## Why Ingested

Scleroderma-specific implant evidence — systemic sclerosis (SSc) carries a unique surgical/prosthetic obstacle (progressive microstomia limiting access and removable-prosthesis use) that generic autoimmune-implant reviews do not isolate. Complements the broader sibling synthesis [[wiki/implants/esimekara-2022-dental-implants-autoimmune-diseases-sr]] by drilling into the SSc subpopulation and the disease-specific decision logic (fixed > removable, short implants/angled abutments, commissurotomy vs foldable prosthesis).

## One-line Summary

Systematic review of 14 case reports/series (37 SSc patients, 153 implants): implant survival 100% in case reports and 89.2% in the single case series, with SSc status showing no discernible impact on survival — but disease-specific risk assessment and microstomia management are essential.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First comprehensive systematic review compiling evidence on the influence of systemic sclerosis (SSc / scleroderma) on dental implant survival and clinical success.
- Aggregates 14 studies (1 retrospective case series + 13 case reports) covering 37 patients and 153 implants across 11 countries.
- Reports no significant negative effect of SSc on implant survival, while establishing SSc-specific clinical decision logic (prosthetic design, access management, interdisciplinary planning).
- Highlights microstomia (progressive restriction of mouth opening) as the dominant surgical/prosthetic limitation and catalogs management strategies (short implants, angled abutments, commissurotomy, foldable/collapsible prostheses, sectional impressions).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: PRISMA-adherent systematic review; protocol registered on Open Science Framework.
- **Databases**: PubMed, Scopus, Google Scholar; searched January 2023 by two independent authors (MeSH/keyword strings for "Dental Implants" AND "Systemic Sclerosis/Scleroderma").
- **Inclusion**: case reports/series from the last 30 years investigating the role of systemic scleroderma on dental implant survival and outcomes.
- **Exclusion**: non-English, reviews, posters, abstracts, insufficient-data studies.
- **Inter-rater reliability**: Cohen's Kappa κ = 0.86 (almost perfect agreement) for screening.
- **Quality assessment + data extraction**: two reviewers each, third author adjudicating disagreements; data captured in a standardized Excel datasheet (author, year, country, study type, age/sex/follow-up, sample size, survival rate, outcome).
- **Screening flow**: 593 records → 579 after de-duplication → 14 included (565 excluded as irrelevant on full-text review).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Included studies**: 14 (1 case series [Jackowski 2021], 13 case reports).
- **Patients**: 37 total, all with scleroderma. Age range 28–77 y; mean age 58.16 ± 12.88 y. 94.6% female (all case-report patients female; case series = 22 female + 2 male).
- **Implants**: 153 total (various systems — Straumann, Nobel Biocare, MIS, ITI, Odontit, etc.).
- **Follow-up**: range 1–10 years (several case reports did not report duration).
- **Survival**: 100% across all case reports; 89.2% (95% CI 79.1–95.6) in the case series.
- **Effect of SSc**: implant survival not significantly affected; no firm evidence that SSc negatively impacts survival.
- **Case-series detail (Jackowski 2021, n=24, 72 implants)**: mean peri-implant pocket depth 2.4–2.8 mm; mean interincisal distance 29.54 mm; positive correlations between sulcus bleeding index/plaque index and peri-implant pocket depth/plaque index.
- **Prosthetic pattern**: fixed restorations reported as superior to removable dentures in SSc; fixed designs indicated when interincisal distance has not dropped below ~3 cm.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Evidence base is exclusively case reports/series — no control groups, no RCTs, low specificity, small and highly heterogeneous patient collective.
- Outcomes often reported in aggregate, not patient-specific (e.g., implant loss per patient not consistently characterized).
- English-only inclusion may have omitted relevant data (language bias).
- Authors call for RCTs with larger SSc populations on dental implant outcomes.

## 7. Glossary

- **Systemic sclerosis (SSc) / scleroderma**: autoimmune connective-tissue disease with obstructive small-vessel vasculopathy and fibroblast-driven extracellular matrix deposition (skin, lung, heart, kidney, GI). Subtypes: limited (lSSc) and diffuse (dSSc).
- **Microstomia**: progressive restriction of mouth opening from perioral collagen deposition; key access/prosthetic limitation in SSc.
- **Sclerodactyly / Raynaud's phenomenon / calcinosis cutis / telangiectasia**: distinctive features of limited SSc.
- **Xerostomia / hyposalivation**: reduced salivary flow from gland fibrosis; raises caries/periodontal risk and impairs denture retention.
- **Commissurotomy**: surgical widening of the mouth corner to improve access (considered when opening < ~16 mm); risks scarring/recurrent microstomia.
- **FPD**: fixed partial denture.
- **Foldable/collapsible prosthesis**: pliable-silicone hinged removable design enabling insertion/removal through a constricted oral aperture.
