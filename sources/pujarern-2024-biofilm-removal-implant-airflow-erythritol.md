## Why Ingested

Peri-implantitis 비수술/유지 단계의 핵심은 임플란트 표면 생물막 제거인데, [[overviews/peri-implantitis-management-overview]]의 표면 decontamination 분기에서 air-polishing powder 선택(어떤 powder가 가장 효율적이고 표면 손상이 적은가)에 대한 직접 비교 근거를 보강하기 위해 인제스트. 본 in-vitro RCT-design 비교는 SB(40 µm) vs ERY(14 µm) 두 파우더의 생물막 제거 효율을 직접 대조해, 큰 입자가 더 잘 제거할 것이라는 통념을 반박하고 작은 입자(ERY) 쪽이 표면 손상이 적어 임상적으로 선호된다는 결론을 제공한다. 또한 [[implants/peri-implantitis/baima-2022-surface-decontamination-protocols-surgical-periimplantitis]]의 surface decontamination 프로토콜 논의와 짝을 이룬다.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro study (33 Dentium SuperLine II implants, 3 groups of n=11) showing both sodium bicarbonate (40 µm) and erythritol (14 µm) air-abrasion powders removed biofilm far better than untreated control (mean OD 0.130 and 0.129 vs 0.728; p<0.05) with no significant difference between the two powders (p>0.05) — supporting smaller-particle erythritol as the preferred choice for lower surface damage.

## 한줄요약

체외(in-vitro) 연구(임플란트 33개, 3군 각 n=11): 탄산수소나트륨(Sodium Bicarbonate, SB, 40 µm)과 에리스리톨(Erythritol, ERY, 14 µm) 두 air-abrasion 파우더 모두 무처치 대조군 대비 생물막을 훨씬 잘 제거(평균 광학밀도 OD 0.130·0.129 vs 0.728; p<0.05)했고, 두 파우더 간 차이는 유의하지 않았다(p>0.05) — 입자가 작아 표면 손상이 적은 에리스리톨이 임상적으로 선호된다는 근거.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Direct in-vitro head-to-head comparison of the two clinically common air-polishing powders on dental implant surfaces: **sodium bicarbonate (SB, 40 µm)** vs **erythritol (ERY, 14 µm)** — a ~4× particle-size difference.
2. Tested the hypothesis that the **larger particle (SB) would remove biofilm more efficiently** — and refuted it: no statistically significant difference in biofilm-removal efficacy between SB and ERY.
3. Quantified biofilm removal via crystal-violet optical-density (OD) assay against an untreated control, confirming both powders are highly and equally effective.
4. Synthesized prior literature on surface damage (SB causes crater-like defects; GLY/ERY barely alter the surface) to argue that, with equal cleaning, the **smaller particle (ERY) is the better clinical choice** for minimizing implant/peri-implant tissue damage.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Implants**: 33 bone-level implants, 4.0 × 8.0 mm (Dentium SuperLine II, Dentium Co., Suwon, South Korea), divided into 3 groups of n=11.
  - Group 1 (n=11): no treatment (control)
  - Group 2 (n=11): SB air abrasion (AIRFLOW Powder Classic Comfort, EMS, Nyon, Switzerland; 40 µm)
  - Group 3 (n=11): ERY air abrasion (AIRFLOW Powder Plus, EMS; 14 µm)
- **Biofilm formation**: Stimulated whole saliva (paraffin-chewing) from a single investigator, centrifuged (5,000 rpm, 15 min), supernatant diluted to 50%; implants pre-coated in saliva 24 h at 37 °C. Subgingival plaque (periodontal curette, distal of lower molars) inoculated in anaerobe basal broth, incubated 24 h anaerobically, adjusted to 0.5 McFarland. Implants then incubated with 0.5 McFarland broth 48 h in an anaerobic chamber, washed 3× in PBS.
- **Air abrasion treatment**: AIRFLOW Handpiece of AIRFLOW Prophylaxis Master, **15 s**, **power level 5**, ~**2 mm** handpiece-to-surface distance, sample manually rotated by hand — same settings for both powder groups.
- **Biofilm quantification**: Crystal violet 0.5% staining (15 min), PBS rinse ×3, dissolved in 20% acetone in ethanol (shaker 15 min), then **optical density (OD)** read on a microplate reader (Synergy H1, BioTek). OD = average of two readings minus negative-control OD (0.0635).
- **Surface characterization**: Stereomicroscope (all groups) + scanning electron microscope (JSM-6610LV, Jeol) at varying magnifications for qualitative topography.
- **Statistics**: One-way ANOVA with post-hoc Dunnett's T3; Shapiro–Wilk for normality; SPSS v28; significance p<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Biofilm-removal efficacy (mean OD ± SD; lower = less residual biofilm):**

| Group | Powder (particle size) | Mean OD ± SD |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — Control | none | 0.728 ± 0.188 |
| 2 — SB | sodium bicarbonate (40 µm) | 0.130 ± 0.068 |
| 3 — ERY | erythritol (14 µm) | 0.129 ± 0.049 |

- Both SB and ERY were **significantly better than control** (p<0.05).
- **No significant difference between SB and ERY** (p>0.05) — the larger-particle hypothesis was not supported.
- **Stereomicroscope**: control showed biofilm covering the entire implant; both treated groups showed only a few residual purple (crystal-violet) spots, including on hard-to-reach thread and valley surfaces; groups 2 and 3 showed similar topography.
- **SEM**: untreated surface smoother; all treated groups showed textured microstructure; **no marked differences between groups 2 and 3** at various magnifications.
- **Surface-damage synthesis (from cited literature, not measured here)**: SB is the most damaging powder (crater-like defects that may facilitate plaque accumulation and affect osseointegration), whereas glycine (GLY) and ERY barely alter the surface — basis for favoring smaller particles clinically.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **No surface-roughness measurement in this study**: surface topographical changes could not be assessed by stereomicroscope; microscopic differences between the two powders may exist but were not documented. SEM would have better visualized protocol effects on the surface.
- **Idealized in-vitro setting**: cannot conclude clinical superiority — in vivo, gingival crevice depth/width, implant location, and microflora vary; ideal accessibility cannot be achieved, so particle size might matter clinically.
- **Single-donor microbiome**: plaque from one individual; community diversity, thickness, and stickiness may not reflect real clinical biofilms.
- **Crystal-violet OD assay**: does not distinguish live vs dead cells (no antimicrobial readout) and may not be sensitive enough to differentiate minute residual biofilm — possible reason for the equal OD between powders.
- **Future work**: better replication of the clinical setting; SEM-based surface analysis; clinical/in vivo validation.

## 7. Glossary

- **Air abrasion / air polishing therapy**: mechanical debridement spraying an abrasive powder + water/air to remove biofilm; good accessibility, modest surface change.
- **SB (sodium bicarbonate)**: larger-particle (40 µm) air-polishing powder; effective but most surface-damaging (crater-like defects).
- **ERY (erythritol)**: smaller-particle (14 µm) sugar-alcohol air-polishing powder; effective cleaning, minimal surface alteration, biofilm-inhibiting properties.
- **GLY (glycine)**: intermediate small-particle air-polishing powder; previously considered superior; minimal surface damage.
- **OD (optical density / opacity density)**: spectrophotometric readout of crystal-violet-stained residual biofilm; higher OD = more residual biofilm.
- **Crystal violet assay**: dye-based biofilm-biomass quantification; stains total biomass (live + dead), not antimicrobial activity.
- **Peri-implantitis**: irreversible pathological inflammation of peri-implant tissues with progressive bone loss; prevalence ~10–31%.
- **0.5 McFarland standard**: turbidity standard for standardizing bacterial inoculum concentration.
