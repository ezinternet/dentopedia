## One-line Summary
RCT (n=52, Seville) — BTA (100 U) and percutaneous needle electrolysis (PNE) both significantly reduce chronic masticatory myalgia pain (VAS 6.5 → ~2.6) and improve MIO at 28 days, sustained through 90 days, with no superiority of one over the other.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Toxins 2023;15(4):278
- **DOI**: 10.3390/toxins15040278
- **Published**: 10 April 2023
- **Design**: Randomized controlled trial, head-to-head, single-center
- **Lead institution**: Virgen del Rocio University Hospital, University of Seville
- **Population**: Adults with refractory chronic localized masticatory myalgia (>12 months) and active trigger points; no signs of internal joint damage

## 2. Key Contributions
- Direct head-to-head RCT of BTA vs. PNE (percutaneous needle electrolysis) — two minimally invasive options for refractory chronic masticatory myalgia.
- Demonstrates that both interventions reduce pain to ≈2.5/10 by day 28 and stabilize through day 90.
- EMG and MRI baseline standardization rules out internal joint pathology.
- Reports DC/TMD-aligned "localized myalgia" phenotyping (as opposed to referred/myofascial).
- Adverse event signal: 4 mild AEs in PNE arm, 0 in BTA arm.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 52 patients (BTA n=26, PNE n=26) after initial 87 screened and 65 selected.
- Inclusion: chronic masticatory myalgia >12 mo, active TrPs on masticatory muscles, no internal joint damage on MRI.
- BTA: bilateral injection, total 100 U distributed across primary masticatory muscles (masseter, temporalis), single session.
- PNE: galvanic microcurrent 0.5 mA × 3 s × 3 consecutive applications, single session.
- Outcomes: pain (VAS 0-10), maximum interincisal opening (MIO, mm), lateral right/left, protrusion (mm), 100-point QoL questionnaire, patient/observer efficacy and tolerance (0-4).
- Timepoints: baseline (day 0), 28 days, 60 days, 90 days.
- Statistics: Mann-Whitney for intergroup, Friedman for intragroup, Wilcoxon between consecutive checkpoints.

- BTA vs. PNE intergroup comparison: not significant at any checkpoint for any variable.
- Bulk of within-group improvement occurred between day 0 and day 28; values stable thereafter.
- Adverse events: 4 mild events in PNE group (2 pain, 2 bruising); 0 in BTA group.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Single-center, single operator.
- 90-day follow-up — does not capture longer BTA pharmacodynamics (atrophy, bone effects) or PNE durability.
- No placebo arm — both interventions effective, but cannot rule out non-specific/placebo response.
- Authors clearly state the design is randomized (n=26/group), supporting RCT classification despite "clinical study" wording in some sections.

## 7. Glossary
- **BTA** (보툴리눔독소 A형): Used here as alternative abbreviation for Botulinum Toxin Type A.
- **PNE** (경피적 침전기분해, Percutaneous Needle Electrolysis): Insertion of needle delivering low-intensity galvanic current (e.g., 0.5 mA / 3 s) to promote tissue regeneration at trigger points.
- **TrP** (통증유발점, Trigger Point): Hyperirritable spot in taut muscle band associated with palpable nodule and referred pain.
- **Masticatory myalgia (MM)** (저작근 통증): DC/TMD-defined muscle-origin pain that worsens with function/parafunction.
- **MIO** (최대 절치간 개구, Maximum Interincisal Opening): Inter-incisor distance at maximum opening.
- **EMG** (근전도, Electromyography): Electrical activity recording of skeletal muscles.
- **DC/TMD localized myalgia**: Pain confined to muscle on palpation, no extra-muscular referral.
