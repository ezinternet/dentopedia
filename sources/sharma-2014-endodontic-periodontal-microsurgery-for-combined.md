## One-line Summary
Narrative overview arguing that microsurgical management of combined endodontic-periodontal lesions — endodontic microsurgery plus concurrent bone grafting, membrane barriers, and papilla-preserving flaps for primary closure — outperforms traditional surgical approaches.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Synthesizes microsurgical principles for combined endo-perio lesions (vs traditional surgery).
- Emphasizes concurrent regeneration: bone grafting + membrane barriers to prevent epithelial downgrowth.
- Highlights minimally invasive papilla-preserving flaps and graft submergence for GTR success.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative overview of endodontic and periodontal microsurgical techniques applied to combined lesions.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Microsurgery reports higher success rates than traditional endodontic/periodontal surgery for isolated defects.
- Combined endo-perio lesions need concurrent endodontic microsurgery + bone grafting + barrier membranes.
- Preventing epithelial downgrowth and regenerating cementum/fiber/bone determines case outcome.
- Achieving primary closure with graft submergence improves guided-tissue-regeneration (GTR) outcome.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative overview; no pooled outcome data or comparative trials.
- Technique-sensitive; operator microsurgical skill not quantified.

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary
- Endo-periodontal lesion (EPL): combined pulpal + periodontal pathology of a single tooth.
- GTR (guided tissue regeneration): barrier-membrane technique excluding epithelium to allow periodontal regeneration.
- Papilla-preserving flap: incision design retaining interdental papilla for primary closure/esthetics.
