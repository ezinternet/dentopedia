## Why Ingested

의료분쟁의 거시 추세(전문과목별 소송 빈도·합의율·판결 결과)를 16년 전수 데이터로 보여주는 국가 단위 근거. 한국 판례 미시분석 [[complaint-management/kwon-2017-analysis-of-the-legal-effect]]·[[complaint-management/park-2016-analysis-of-malpractice-claims-associated]]에 거시 맥락을 제공하고, 기존 medico-legal 적용층 [[complaint-management/JISPCD-14-295]]을 일본 데이터로 확장한다. 동아시아(일본) 의료소송이 의료진에 유리하게 종결되는 비율을 정량화.

## One-line Summary
Descriptive study of all 13,340 closed medical malpractice claims from Japan's Supreme Court (2006–2021): 52.9% settled, 35.3% judged (75.8% favoring physicians), claim volume declining — sharply in OB/GYN after the 2009 no-fault compensation system.

## 한줄요약
일본 최고재판소 종결 의료소송 전수 13,340건(2006–2021) 기술연구 — 52.9% 합의, 35.3% 판결(이 중 75.8% 의료진 승), 전체 건수 감소세; 특히 산부인과는 2009년 무과실 보상제 도입 후 급감.

## 1. Document Information
- PLoS ONE 2023;18(12):e0296155. Research Article. Published 2023-12-18.
- Shimane University (Environmental Medicine & Public Health; General Medicine Center) and University of Michigan Medical School.
- Open Access (CC BY). Data from Public Relations Section, Supreme Court of Japan.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First study reporting the actual prevalence of ALL closed malpractice claims in Japan (not just commercialized/high-risk subsets).
- Breaks down claims by medical specialty, settlement vs judgment, and outcome over 16 years.
- Documents an overall decline in claims and links the OB/GYN drop to a 2009 policy (Obstetrics/Gynecology Medical Compensation System).
- Quantifies the strong physician-favoring tilt of judged cases (75.8%).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Descriptive study of all closed malpractice claims from the Supreme Court of Japan, 2006–2021.
- Extracted trends and characteristics by medical specialty and outcome (settlement, judgment, dismissal/withdrawal).
- Normalized counts per 1,000 physicians for specialty comparison.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Total closed claims: 13,340 over 16 years.
- Settled: 7,062 (52.9%). Judged: 4,734 (35.3%), of which medical profession favored in 3,589 (75.8%).
- Plastic surgery and OB/GYN more likely resolved by settlement; psychiatry less likely to settle and notably higher rate of patient-unfavorable outcomes.
- Overall decline in closed claims vs 2006; OB/GYN and claims-per-1,000-physicians fell significantly more than other specialties, coinciding with the 2009 OB/GYN no-fault compensation system.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Closed-claims/court data only; disputes resolved before formal claim are unobserved.
- Descriptive — no causal inference on the 2009 policy beyond temporal association.
- Specialty coding from Supreme Court records; clinical detail of individual errors not available.

## 6. Related Work
- "To Err Is Human" (IOM, 2000) framing of medical error and patient safety.
- US National Practitioner Data Bank epidemiological studies of litigation trends by specialty.

## 7. Glossary
- Closed claim: a malpractice claim that has reached final resolution (settlement, judgment, dismissal, or withdrawal).
- OB/GYN Medical Compensation System (2009): Japanese no-fault compensation scheme for birth-related cerebral palsy.
