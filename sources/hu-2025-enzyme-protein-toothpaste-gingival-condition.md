## Why Ingested

이가탄(Igatan)-family 성분 감시(surveillance) 인제스트 — 이가탄에프의 active 중 하나인 **리소짐 (Lysozyme)**이 치약(국소 도포)으로 전달될 때 치은 건강에 효과가 있는지 검증하는 RCT다. 본 연구의 test 치약은 lysozyme·lactoferrin·colostrum(IgG)·lactoperoxidase 효소-단백 시스템을 담고 있어, [[wiki/periodontics/hong-2019-randomized-double-blind-placebo-controlled-multicenter]]의 전신 복용 이가탄(CELC, lysozyme 함유) tablet과 **동일 active(lysozyme)를 국소 vs 전신**으로 대비할 근거가 된다. 전신 이가탄의 제한적 efficacy와 달리 국소 치약은 GI·plaque에서 강한 우월성을 보여 전달 경로(local delivery) 논점을 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

Double-blind RCT (n=251, Chinese adults with gingivitis, 26 weeks): a fluoride toothpaste containing enzymes + proteins (lysozyme, lactoferrin, colostrum-IgG, lactoperoxidase + amyloglucosidase/glucose oxidase) reduced Gingival Index (between-product diff −0.53, p<0.0001) and supragingival plaque MQHPI (diff −0.96, p<0.0001) at 26 wk vs control fluoride toothpaste, raising non-bleeding sites from 35.7% to 93.8% (vs 45.3% control).

## 한줄요약

이중맹검 RCT (n=251, 치은염 중국 성인, 26주): 효소-단백(리소짐·락토페린·초유 IgG·락토퍼옥시다제 등) 함유 불소 치약이 대조 불소 치약 대비 치은지수(GI, between-product 차 −0.53, p<0.0001)와 치은연상 치태(MQHPI, 차 −0.96, p<0.0001)를 유의하게 낮췄고, 비출혈 부위를 35.7%→93.8%(대조 45.3%)로 끌어올렸다.

- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health 2025;25(1):1727
- **DOI**: 10.1186/s12903-025-07096-7 | **PMID**: 41184910 | **PMCID**: PMC12581499
- **Trial registration**: NCT06932211
- **Study type**: Double-blind (participant/examiner/statistician/investigator), randomised, two-group parallel design, 26 weeks
- **Setting**: University Dental Hospital, Xi'an, China; Oct 2018 – Apr 2019
- **Funding/sponsor implied**: Unilever (test product = Unilever Enzymatic technology toothpaste); control = Sensodyne Pronamel

## 2. Key Contributions

- Reproduces the gingival-health benefit of an enzyme + protein fluoride toothpaste — previously shown in UK and Denmark populations — in a **Chinese adult population with mild-to-moderate gingivitis**, establishing cross-population generalisability.
- Quantifies a large, dose-of-time effect: benefit appears by **4 weeks** and grows through **26 weeks**, with the between-product gap widening over time.
- Documents a striking shift in gingival-health *status*: proportion of participants with <10% bleeding sites ("healthy") rose 0.8% → 78.3% (test) vs 1.8% → 16.3% (control) at week 26.
- Ingredient relevance: the toothpaste contains **lysozyme** (plus lactoferrin, colostrum IgG, lactoperoxidase enzyme system) — the same lysozyme active found in the 이가탄/CELC systemic tablet, but delivered topically.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Participants**: 251 randomised (test 127 / control 124); 198 completed (test 106 / control 92); analysis population 235 (test 122 / control 113, excluding 16 with no post-baseline data).
- **Inclusion**: age 18–70, ≥20 natural teeth, mean GI ≥1.0 and mean MQHPI ≥1.5 at screening/baseline. **Exclusion**: pregnancy/lactation, untreated caries/significant perio, dentures, ortho, smokers, diabetics, recent anti-inflammatory/antimicrobial/statin use, recent prophylaxis.
- **Test toothpaste**: silica, 1450 ppm NaF + lactoferrin, colostrum, amyloglucosidase, glucose oxidase, lactoperoxidase, potassium thiocyanate (and lysozyme, per ingredient narrative). **Control**: Sensodyne Pronamel, silica + 1450 ppm NaF only (negative control — no antimicrobial active beyond fluoride).
- **Regimen**: brush ≥1 min twice daily for 26 weeks; supervised brushing at baseline, week 4, week 13. No prophylaxis or run-in before baseline (differs from prior UK design).
- **Outcomes**: Löe-Silness Gingival Index (GI, 0–3) and Modified Quigley-Hein Plaque Index (MQHPI, 0–5), 6 sites/tooth, all teeth except third molars. Assessments at baseline, 4, 13, 26 weeks by a single calibrated examiner (intra-examiner kappa 0.85).
- **Primary objective**: GI difference vs control at 13 weeks. Secondary: GI at 4/26 wk, MQHPI at 4/13/26 wk.
- **Statistics**: ANCOVA (baseline covariate; product + sex fixed effects; product×sex interaction retained for MQHPI only) for between-product; paired t-test for within-product. Stratified block randomisation (block 4) by sex. No imputation of missing data. Sample size 100/group to detect 0.2-unit GI difference (SD 0.5, 80% power, α 0.05).
- **Post-hoc**: % non-bleeding sites (GI 0–1 = non-bleeding; GI 2–3 = bleeding), logit-transformed ANCOVA.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Gingival Index (GI), between-product (Test − Control, negative favours test):**

| Timepoint | Test GI (SD) | Control GI (SD) | Between-product diff (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Baseline | 1.71 (0.36) | 1.69 (0.40) | — (NS) | — |
| Week 4 | 1.42 (0.19) | 1.58 (0.41) | −0.18 (−0.23, −0.12) | <0.0001 |
| Week 13 (primary) | 1.15 (0.09) | 1.31 (0.23) | −0.16 (−0.20, −0.11) | <0.0001 |
| Week 26 | 1.06 (0.11) | 1.59 (0.38) | **−0.53 (−0.60, −0.46)** | <0.0001 |

**Supragingival plaque (MQHPI), between-product:**

| Timepoint | Test MQHPI (SD) | Control MQHPI (SD) | Between-product diff (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Baseline | 4.12 (0.41) | 4.13 (0.44) | — (NS) | — |
| Week 4 | 3.73 (0.29) | 3.99 (0.42) | −0.29 (−0.38, −0.21) | <0.0001 |
| Week 13 | 3.25 (0.25) | 3.54 (0.41) | −0.35 (−0.45, −0.25) | <0.0001 |
| Week 26 | 2.85 (0.31) | 3.77 (0.40) | **−0.96 (−1.06, −0.85)** | <0.0001 |

- **% non-bleeding sites (test group)**: 35.7% baseline → 59.0% (4 wk) → 84.8% (13 wk) → **93.8% (26 wk)**, vs control 45.3% at 26 wk (between-product p<0.01 at all 3 timepoints).
- **"Healthy" (<10% bleeding sites)**: test 0.8% → 78.3%; control 1.8% → 16.3% (week 26).
- Both products improved significantly vs their own baseline at all timepoints. Notably the control group's week-26 GI (1.59) and MQHPI (3.77) regressed toward baseline relative to week 13 — attributed to the 3-month gap after the last (week-13) brushing instruction (Hawthorne effect waning).
- **Safety**: no product-related AEs. Mild cold-related AEs (control 10, test 4); 1 SAE (control, gastroenteritis hospitalisation, resolved). All resolved by study end.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Sex imbalance** (~70% female / 30% male) despite sex-stratified randomisation; authors argue product×sex was non-significant for GI and that the MQHPI interaction (weeks 4/13) was driven by greater plaque reduction in control-group females, not by differential test efficacy.
- **Differential dropout** (control 32 vs test 21), though reasons were non-product-related (time conflict, non-product AEs, protocol deviations); no imputation of missing data.
- **No prophylaxis / no run-in** before baseline (unlike the prior UK trial) → control toothpaste itself improved over baseline, indicating a substantial Hawthorne/brushing-instruction effect inflating the control arm and likely *under*-stating the absolute test benefit at intermediate timepoints.
- **Industry sponsorship** (Unilever test product; multiple industry-affiliated authors) — interpret effect sizes with appropriate caution.
- Cannot isolate the contribution of any single active (lysozyme vs lactoferrin vs the lactoperoxidase enzyme system) — the formulation is tested as a whole.

## 6. Related Work

- Prior UK double-blind RCT and Danish epidemiological study (≥12 months use) reported by the same group, both showing gingival-health benefit of the enzyme-protein toothpaste; the UK design used prophylaxis + 4-week control run-in to dampen the Hawthorne effect.
- Mechanism: amyloglucosidase + glucose oxidase generate H₂O₂ from polyglucans; lactoperoxidase converts salivary thiocyanate to hypothiocyanite (antimicrobial); lysozyme cleaves gram-positive peptidoglycan; lactoferrin sequesters iron / inhibits oral pathogen metabolism; colostrum IgG provides passive immunity. A 14-week microbiome study showed a shift toward gingival-health-associated species.
- **이가탄/CELC systemic tablet** ([[periodontics/hong-2019-randomized-double-blind-placebo-controlled-multicenter]]) — shares the **lysozyme** active but delivered orally/systemically for periodontitis adjunct; contrast topical (this paper, strong GI/plaque effect) vs systemic delivery.

## 7. Glossary

- **GI (Löe-Silness Gingival Index)**: 0–3 gingival inflammation score; 2–3 = bleeding on pressure / spontaneous bleeding.
- **MQHPI (Modified Quigley-Hein Plaque Index)**: 0–5 supragingival plaque extent score (disclosed with Red-cote).
- **Lysozyme**: enzyme hydrolysing peptidoglycan in gram-positive bacterial cell walls; salivary innate-immunity component and an active in 이가탄에프.
- **Lactoferrin**: iron-binding glycoprotein with antibacterial action; inhibits oral pathogen metabolism.
- **Lactoperoxidase system**: enzyme that oxidises thiocyanate → hypothiocyanite using H₂O₂; antimicrobial salivary defence.
- **Hawthorne effect**: behaviour change (improved brushing) from awareness of being observed/studied — inflates control-arm improvement.
- **ANCOVA**: analysis of covariance; here baseline-adjusted between-product comparison.
