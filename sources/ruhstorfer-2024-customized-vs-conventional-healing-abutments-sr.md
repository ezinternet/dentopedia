## Why Ingested

Seeds the healing-abutment function/usage evidence cluster — the wiki has emergence-profile and immediate-implant pages but no head-to-head appraisal of *customized vs conventional* healing abutments. Reinforces the one-abutment/one-time soft-tissue-preservation theme of [[wiki/implants/rios-santos-2020-one-abutment-one-time-rct]] (both argue for early, stable peri-implant tissue management that avoids repeated abutment disconnection / late compressive shaping), and pairs with the immediate-implant customized-abutment evidence in [[wiki/immediate-implant/dutra-2025-customized-healing-abutments-immediate-implants]].

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (5 studies — 2 RCTs, 2 prospective, 1 retrospective; 190 implants, 100% survival, 6–36 mo) finding customized healing abutments trend toward better peri-implant soft/hard tissue and esthetics with less treatment pain and no biological or esthetic disadvantage vs conventional titanium stock abutments.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic review to compare customized vs conventional healing abutments **using the same healing method** in both arms (non-submerged), addressing a confounder in the earlier Lenz et al. 2023 review (which compared non-submerged customized vs submerged conventional — methods differ, raising bias).
- Pools the still-thin evidence: 1,396 titles screened → 5 studies (190 implants; 91 customized, 99 conventional).
- All 5 included studies judged **low risk of bias** (RoB2 for RCTs, JBI Critical Appraisal for cohorts).
- Rejects all four null hypotheses: advantageous effects observed for peri-implant soft tissue, hard tissue, esthetics, and PROMs (pain), though as a *trend* given the small evidence base.
- Recommends incorporating anatomically designed (customized) healing abutments into the clinician's (digital) workflow.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **PICO**: In patients with dental implants, do customized healing abutments result in beneficial peri-implant conditions compared with conventional healing abutments?
- **Databases**: PubMed, PubMed Central, Cochrane, Web of Science; up to 2024-04-20. MeSH + free-text synonyms for "customized" and "conventional" healing abutment. Gray literature excluded; hand search of included-article references performed.
- **Screening / extraction**: independent dual review (TG, MR); disagreements resolved by consensus (JFG, TG, MR); corresponding authors emailed for missing data.
- **Quality assessment**: STROBE for observational studies; **RoB2** (Cochrane) for RCTs; **JBI** Critical Appraisal (11-item) for cohort studies.
- **Synthesis**: qualitative only — data too heterogeneous (different parameters, methods, follow-ups) for a meaningful meta-analysis.
- **Customized abutment materials in included studies**: 4 used hand-made resin healing abutments; 1 used CAD/CAM polyether ether ketone (PEEK). Conventional arms were all prefabricated titanium stock abutments.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Selection funnel**: 1,396 titles → 259 duplicates/ineligible removed → 991 excluded by title → 146 abstracts → 131 excluded → 15 + 1 hand-search full texts → **5 included**.
- **Designs**: 2 RCTs, 2 prospective cohorts, 1 retrospective; 190 implants (91 customized / 99 conventional); follow-up 6–36 months; **100% implant survival** in all groups.
- **Setting**: 4 studies = immediate implant after extraction (sometimes with simultaneous grafting); 1 study = late placement in healed sites.
- **Soft tissue**: 2/5 favored customized — Amato significantly lower bucco-palatal volumetric reduction of keratinized tissue (p<0.001); Perez significantly better papilla indices (p<0.001). Others showed stable, similar keratinized tissue in both groups. Hu noted customized abutments help close large posterior immediate sockets.
- **Hard tissue**: Chokaree, Hu, Perez found no significant vertical bone difference (6–12 mo). Amato horizontal ridge nearly stable at 6 mo. Crespi: significant alveolar-width gain baseline→3 mo in both groups, greater with customized (+2.5±0.5 mm vs +1.0±0.9 mm conventional, p<0.001); widths stable 3 mo→3 yr.
- **Peri-implant health**: Crespi found no significant difference in probing depth or bleeding on probing.
- **Esthetics (PES)**: mixed — Chokaree significantly better PES with customized at 6 mo (p=0.022); Perez no significant PES difference at 12 mo.
- **PROMs (pain)**: only Chokaree — NRS pain significantly higher with conventional at prosthesis insertion (p=0.003) and at 2 h (p=0.013); no difference at 24 h. Consistent with Beretta (referenced).
- **Bottom line**: trend toward better peri-implant soft/hard tissue and esthetics and less early treatment pain with customized abutments; **no biological or esthetic disadvantage** versus conventional.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 5 studies, low total implant count (190); evidence base thin.
- Mostly short-term follow-up (6–12 mo); only Crespi provided 3-year data.
- Data too heterogeneous for meta-analysis — conclusions are qualitative trends.
- PROMs (pain) examined in only 1 study with very small per-group n.
- Influence of individual customized-abutment materials (resin vs CAD/CAM PEEK/PMMA) could not be isolated.
- A 2022 Crespi report excluded as duplicate of the same 2023 study group.
- Authors call for larger samples and longer follow-up to confirm long-term outcomes.

## 6. Related Work

- Lenz et al. 2023 — earlier systematic review suggesting favorable peri-implant response, but compared non-submerged customized vs submerged conventional (mixed healing methods → higher bias) and only immediate placement; present review controls healing method and adds late-placement data.
- Troiano et al. (meta-analysis) — non-submerged technique had a slightly higher early-failure rate (~2%, p=0.008); late failure and marginal bone loss similar (p=0.22) — relevant because customized abutments are tied to non-submerged healing yet still match/beat conventional outcomes.
- Beretta et al. — corroborates lower early treatment pain with customized abutments.
- Single-arm studies (flowable composite, acrylic, PMMA CAD/CAM, PEEK CAD/CAM) report stable peri-implant outcomes up to 8 years.

## 7. Glossary

- **Customized (individualized) healing abutment**: a healing abutment shaped (hand-made resin or CAD/CAM) to mimic the natural emergence profile of the tooth, contouring peri-implant mucosa during healing and avoiding later compressive soft-tissue shaping.
- **Conventional healing abutment**: prefabricated round titanium stock abutment in standard gingival heights/diameters.
- **Emergence profile**: the contour of the restoration/abutment as it emerges from the implant platform through the peri-implant soft tissue.
- **Non-submerged healing**: healing abutment placed at implant insertion, transmucosal, no second uncovering surgery.
- **Submerged healing**: implant covered by mucosa during healing, requiring a later uncovering session.
- **One-abutment/one-time concept**: definitive abutment placed once (at surgery or uncovering) and never removed, reducing repeated soft-tissue disruption.
- **PES (Pink Esthetic Score)**: index scoring peri-implant soft-tissue esthetics (papillae, margin, contour, color, texture).
- **PROMs**: patient-reported outcome measures (here, pain on NRS).
- **RoB2 / JBI**: Cochrane Risk-of-Bias 2 tool (RCTs) / Joanna Briggs Institute Critical Appraisal (cohort studies).
- **PEEK**: polyether ether ketone, a polymer used for CAD/CAM customized healing abutments.
