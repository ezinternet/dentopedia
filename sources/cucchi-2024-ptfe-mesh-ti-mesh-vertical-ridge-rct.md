## Why Ingested

Part of a focused collection on vertical ridge augmentation (VRA) with Ti-mesh vs PTFE mesh GBR. This head-to-head non-inferiority RCT directly compares the two leading customized barrier devices, complementing the SR/MA evidence in [[implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/cucchi-2024-vertical-ridge-augmentation-peri-implant-bone-loss-sr-ma]] and the membrane-coverage RCT in [[implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/urban-2025-collagen-membrane-vertical-ridge-ptfe-mesh-rct]].

## One-line Summary

Non-inferiority RCT (n=48 treated of 50 randomized) comparing customized Ti-reinforced d-PTFE mesh vs customized CAD/CAM Ti-mesh for VRA: CAD/CAM Ti-mesh was non-inferior for complications; PTFE showed numerically higher VBG (5.79 vs 5.18 mm, p=.233) but significantly higher regeneration rate (99.5% vs 87.0%, p=.013); osseointegration ~98% in both.

## 한줄요약

비열등성 RCT (50명 무작위배정, 48명 수술): 맞춤형 Ti-보강 d-PTFE mesh vs 맞춤형 CAD/CAM Ti-mesh VRA 비교 — 합병증 측면에서 Ti-mesh 비열등 확인, PTFE가 수직골증가량(5.79 vs 5.18 mm, p=.233)은 비유의하게 더 높았으나 재생률(99.5% vs 87.0%, p=.013)은 유의하게 높음, 골유착률 양군 약 98%.

## 1. Document Information

- Study type: Non-inferiority, double-blind, parallel-group randomized clinical trial (CONSORT-conforming)
- Journal: Clinical Oral Implants Research, 2024;35:1616–1639
- DOI: 10.1111/clr.14350
- Registration: ClinicalTrials.gov NCT04257097; ethics protocol CMF 01/2019 (CE-AVEC CE 19143)
- Funding/access: Open access (CC BY-NC-ND)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First randomized head-to-head comparison of two customized digital barrier devices for VRA: Ti-reinforced d-PTFE mesh (RPM, Osteogenics) vs CAD/CAM Ti-mesh (Yxoss CBR, Reoss).
- Tested formal non-inferiority of CAD/CAM Ti-mesh (test) vs reinforced PTFE mesh (control) with a pre-set 10% non-inferiority margin on healing complication rate.
- Reports complication rates, vertical bone gain (VBG), regenerated bone volume (RBV), regeneration rates (RR, ERR), pseudo-periosteum, bone density, implant stability, and osseointegration.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 50 patients with vertical bone defects (VBD >3 mm), partial edentulism (≤5 teeth) in maxilla or mandible, randomized 25/25.
- Control ("PTFE group"): Ti-reinforced d-PTFE mesh (RPM, Osteogenics) + pericardium collagen membrane (Vitala, Osteogenics).
- Test ("Ti-mesh group"): custom CAD/CAM Ti-mesh (Yxoss CBR, Reoss) + native collagen membrane (Bio-Gide, Geistlich).
- Grafting material in both: 50:50 autogenous bone chips + deproteinized bovine bone matrix (DBBM, Bio-Oss, Geistlich).
- Blinded design (patients, clinicians, statistician). Digital workflow: CBCT/DICOM → virtual implant placement → virtual customized device design (STL).
- Sample size: non-inferiority margin 10%, power 80%, α 5% → minimum 17/group; inflated to 25/group for dropouts.
- Significance level α = 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- 48 of 50 patients underwent bone augmentation surgery.
- Surgical complication rate (SCR): PTFE 4% vs Ti-mesh 12%.
- Healing complication rate (HCR): PTFE 12.5% vs Ti-mesh 8.3%.
- Vertical bone gain (VBG): PTFE 5.79 ± 1.71 mm (range 3.2–8.8) vs Ti-mesh 5.18 ± 1.61 mm (range 3.1–8.0); p = .233 (not significant).
- Regenerated bone volume (RBV): PTFE 1.46 ± 0.48 cc vs Ti-mesh 1.26 ± 0.55 cc.
- Regeneration rate (RR): PTFE 99.5% vs Ti-mesh 87.0%; p = .013 (statistically significant difference favoring PTFE).
- Pseudo-periosteum, bone density, and implant stability: similar between groups.
- Osseointegration rates: PTFE 98.2% vs Ti-mesh 98.3%.
- Conclusion: non-inferiority of CAD/CAM Ti-mesh vs reinforced PTFE mesh confirmed for surgical and healing complications. PTFE showed higher VBG and regeneration rates, but no significant difference in VBG.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- VBG difference (PTFE higher) did not reach significance; RR difference did — sample size powered for complications (primary), not for VBG/RR.
- Single-/few-operator digital workflow; generalizability to less experienced operators uncertain.
- Follow-up focused on augmentation/implant phase; long-term peri-implant bone level data limited within this report.

## 7. Glossary

- VRA: vertical ridge augmentation
- RPM: reinforced PTFE mesh (Ti-reinforced dense PTFE)
- d-PTFE: dense polytetrafluoroethylene
- CAD/CAM Ti-mesh: customized titanium mesh from 3D laser sintering
- DBBM: deproteinized bovine bone matrix (Bio-Oss)
- VBG: vertical bone gain; RBV: regenerated bone volume; RR: regeneration rate; ERR: effective regeneration rate
- SCR/HCR: surgical / healing complication rate
