## Why Ingested

This is the landmark clinical SR that directly challenges the post-first assumption — it establishes that ferrule >> post in clinical evidence, redirecting clinical priorities. This is one of the most-cited conceptual papers in post-and-core dentistry. Directly extends [[wiki/post-and-core/]] category and provides the clinical evidence base for ferrule-effect discussions.

## One-line Summary

SR of 8 prospective clinical studies (≥5yr) in endodontically treated teeth: ferrule/cavity wall preservation is the dominant survival factor; 7/8 studies show no independent benefit from post placement.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR to systematically compare post vs. no-post restoration in endodontically treated teeth using only prospective long-term (≥5yr) clinical data
- Quantified ferrule-effect support: 2 of 3 studies that tested ferrule found it beneficial
- Quantified post-effect: 7 of 8 studies found no significant benefit from post placement
- Cochrane risk-of-bias assessment: 5 of 8 studies rated high risk of bias

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Inclusion criteria**: Prospective clinical studies in humans; comparing success/survival of endodontically treated teeth restored with vs. without posts; minimum 5-year observation period
- **Studies included**: 7 RCTs + 1 prospective clinical trial (n=8 total)
- **Quality assessment**: Cochrane risk-of-bias rating
- **Outcomes**: Tooth survival, restoration survival

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Ferrule effect: 2/3 studies support the ferrule concept; maintaining cavity walls is a predominant factor
- Post effect: 7/8 studies show no positive independent effect of post placement
- Conclusion: Ferrule/cavity wall preservation is the primary determinant of survival; post placement does not reliably add benefit in prospective long-term data

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- 5 of 8 studies rated high risk of bias (Cochrane)
- Abstract-only retrieval — detailed subgroup analysis (post material, crown type, tooth position) not available from this summary
- Small number of included studies reflects the scarcity of high-quality long-term prospective data

## 7. Glossary

- **Ferrule effect**: The encirclement of remaining coronal dentin by a crown margin, distributing occlusal forces and resisting root fracture
- **ETT**: Endodontically treated tooth
- **Post (endodontic post)**: An intraradicular retentive element placed to support a core build-up when insufficient coronal tooth structure remains
- **Cochrane risk of bias**: Standardized tool for assessing methodological quality of RCTs
