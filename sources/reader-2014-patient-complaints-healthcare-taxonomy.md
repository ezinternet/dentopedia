## Why Ingested
This is the foundational classification layer of the complaint-management pipeline (분류→기대→대응→교육). It defines the three-domain taxonomy (clinical / management / relationship) that [[complaint-management/bmjqs-2015-004596]] (HCAT) operationalises into a coded instrument, anchoring how a dental clinic can categorise the complaints it receives.

## One-line Summary
Systematic review of 59 studies (88,069 complaints) that synthesises 205 codes into a 7-category, 3-domain patient-complaint taxonomy.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First systematic synthesis of complaint *content* (not just volume) across 59 quantitative studies.
- Reduces 205 heterogeneous analytical codes to 29 subcategories -> 7 categories -> 3 conceptual domains.
- Establishes that complaints split roughly evenly across clinical safety/quality (33.7%), organisational management (35.1%), and staff-patient relationships (29.1%).
- Provides the conceptual backbone later turned into the HCAT coding instrument.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Systematic review of PubMed, ScienceDirect, Medline. Inclusion: primary quantitative data on the *content* of patient-initiated complaints. Data extracted on study characteristics, methodology, and the issues complained about. Codes were thematically grouped (bottom-up) into subcategories, categories, and domains.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
59 studies, 88,069 complaints, 113,551 underlying issues, coded with 205 distinct codes. Most common single issues: 'treatment' (15.6%) and 'communication' (13.7%). Final taxonomy: 3 domains -- Clinical (safety & quality of care, 33.7%), Management (organisation of care, 35.1%), Relationships (staff-patient communication & respect, 29.1%).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
Heterogeneous source coding (single vs multiple cause attribution) limits cross-study comparability. Predominantly hospital/secondary-care data; primary care and dentistry under-represented. Taxonomy is descriptive, not yet a reliability-tested instrument (addressed by HCAT 2016).

## 6. Related Work
Direct precursor to the Healthcare Complaints Analysis Tool (Gillespie & Reader 2016). Builds on earlier single-setting complaint coding schemes (e.g., Bark, Pichert/Hickson vanderbilt work).

## 7. Glossary
- **Domain**: top-level conceptual grouping (clinical / management / relationship).
- **Issue**: a single discrete problem underlying a complaint; one complaint may contain several.
- **Content analysis**: coding the substantive subject matter of complaints rather than counting them.
