## Why Ingested

Largest MDP–zirconia bonding-durability meta-analysis we hold (72 studies in SR / 68 in MA), it quantifies what [[wiki/resin-bonding/nagaoka-2017-mdp-zirconia-chemical-interaction-nmr]] explains mechanistically: the NMR work shows that 10-MDP forms a chemical (P–O–Zr) bond to the zirconia surface, and this MA confirms that bond degrades after aging yet is best preserved when 10-MDP is combined with additional surface treatments. It reinforces the network-meta-analytic ranking in [[wiki/resin-bonding/li-2024-zirconia-surface-treatments-monomers-nma]].

## One-line Summary

Systematic review + meta-analysis (72 studies SR / 68 in MA, abstract-only, Chinese-language): 10-MDP-pretreated resin-to-zirconia bond strength falls significantly after aging (MD 5.58 MPa), is unaffected by MDP application vehicle (primer/adhesive/cement), and is significantly higher when 10-MDP is combined with additional surface treatments (MD 10.17 MPa) vs MDP alone.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰 + 메타분석 (SR 72편 / MA 68편, 초록만, 중국어 논문): 10-MDP 전처리한 레진-지르코니아 결합강도는 노화 후 유의하게 감소(MD 5.58 MPa)하며, MDP 적용 매개체(프라이머/접착제/시멘트)에 따른 차이는 없고, 10-MDP를 추가 표면처리와 병용하면 MDP 단독 대비 노화 후 결합강도가 유의하게 향상(MD 10.17 MPa)된다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Largest synthesis of 10-MDP resin-to-zirconia bonding durability evidence we hold: 72 studies in the systematic review, 68 in the meta-analysis.
- Quantifies aging-induced bond degradation: significant drop in zirconia bond strength after aging (water storage and/or thermocycling).
- Establishes that the **vehicle** through which 10-MDP is delivered (primer vs adhesive vs resin cement) does **not** influence post-aging bond strength.
- Establishes that **combining** 10-MDP with additional surface treatments (alumina sandblasting, tribochemical silica coating, acid etching, laser etching, plasma) significantly improves post-aging bond strength over 10-MDP alone.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases searched**: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, CNKI, Wanfang.
- **Inclusion**: studies of resin-to-zirconia bonding after surface pretreatment with 10-MDP.
- **Outcome measure**: bonding strength of zirconia after 10-MDP surface pretreatment.
- **Subgroup categorization**:
  - Aging method: water storage, thermocycling, or both.
  - 10-MDP application method: within primer, adhesive, resin cement, or combinations.
  - Additional surface treatment: alumina sandblasting, tribochemical silica coating, acid etching, laser etching, plasma treatment.
- **Analysis software**: Review Manager (RevMan) 5.4.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **SR / MA size**: 72 studies in systematic review; 68 eligible for meta-analysis.
- **Aging effect**: zirconia bond strength decreased significantly after aging — MD 5.58, 95% CI 5.11–6.05, p<0.001.
- **MDP application method**: no significant difference among primer / adhesive / resin cement vehicles after aging (all p>0.05).
- **MDP + additional surface treatment vs MDP alone**: significantly higher post-aging bond strength — MD 10.17, 95% CI 8.20–12.14, p<0.001.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only ingestion; the full Chinese-language text (methods detail, risk-of-bias appraisal, heterogeneity statistics, individual study weights) was not retrieved.
- Bond-strength outcomes are in-vitro surrogates (SBS/μTBS); no clinical restoration-survival endpoints.
- Wide variety of aging protocols and surface-treatment combinations pooled together; heterogeneity not extractable from the abstract.

## 7. Glossary

- **10-MDP**: 10-methacryloyloxydecyl dihydrogen phosphate — functional acidic phosphate monomer that chemically bonds to zirconia (Zr–O–P) and metal oxides.
- **Aging**: laboratory durability challenge to bonded specimens via water storage and/or thermocycling.
- **MD (mean difference)**: pooled effect estimate; here in MPa of bond strength.
- **Tribochemical silica coating**: air-abrasion with silica-modified alumina that deposits a silica layer for silane coupling.
- **Network meta-analysis (NMA)**: meta-analysis comparing multiple treatments simultaneously via direct + indirect evidence (used in the related Li 2024 paper).
