## Why Ingested

Wiki coverage of occlusal veneers lacked a dedicated in-vitro mechanical performance summary for CAD/CAM resin vs. lithium disilicate. This SR synthesizes all fatigue and fracture resistance data on CAD/CAM occlusal veneers for severely worn posterior teeth and fills the gap in [[wiki/veneers/klein-2025-ceramic-laminate-veneer-survival-complications-sr-ma]], which covers clinical survival but not mechanical/in-vitro endpoints.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (6 in-vitro studies) finding comparable fracture resistance between CAD/CAM composite resin and lithium disilicate occlusal veneers, with two studies showing higher fatigue resistance for composite resin.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰 (in vitro 연구 6편): CAD/CAM 교합면 비니어에서 레진 복합체와 리튬 디실리케이트 세라믹의 파절저항성(Fracture Resistance)은 동등하며, 피로저항성(Fatigue Resistance)은 2편의 연구에서 레진이 더 우수한 결과를 보임.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First systematic review specifically focusing on fatigue AND fracture resistance of CAD/CAM occlusal veneers (composite resin vs. ceramic) for worn posterior teeth.
2. Demonstrates that CAD/CAM composite resin occlusal veneers achieve fracture resistance values (1,018.5–3,584.0 N) exceeding the maximum bite force without parafunctional habits (424–630 N), even at thin dimensions (0.5–1.5 mm).
3. Identifies that 2 of 3 fatigue studies show higher survival rates for CAD/CAM composite resin over lithium disilicate ceramic; 1 of 3 fatigue studies shows no significant difference.
4. Establishes that thermomechanical loading narrows the gap between materials — after thermomechanical fatigue, no significant difference between IPS e.max CAD and VITA ENAMIC in fracture resistance.
5. Provides a risk-of-bias profile: 4 of 6 studies high-risk, 2 medium-risk; no low-risk studies.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases**: PubMed, Embase, Web of Science, Scopus, Cochrane, OpenGrey, Redalyc, DSpace, Grey Literature Report; no time/language restriction; search through January 2022
- **PICO question**: In posterior teeth with severe tooth wear, do CAD/CAM composite resin occlusal veneers perform better mechanically (fatigue and fracture resistance) than CAD/CAM lithium disilicate occlusal veneers?
- **Eligibility**: In vitro studies, RCTs, non-randomized controlled trials comparing CAD/CAM composite resin vs. ceramic occlusal veneers on worn posterior teeth. Excluded: endodontically treated teeth, crowns/inlays/onlays/implants, case reports, literature/systematic reviews, FEA studies.
- **Study selection**: 400 initially identified → 25 duplicates removed → 352 excluded by title/abstract → 19 full-text reviewed → 6 included (3 fracture, 3 fatigue).
- **Quality assessment**: Modified CONSORT criteria (7 parameters); high bias = 1–3 items reported, medium = 4–5, low = 6–7.
- **Data extraction**: Authors, year, study design, material, sample size, objectives, testing machine, conclusions.

### Materials Evaluated

- **IPS e.max CAD**: Lithium disilicate glass ceramic (Ivoclar Vivadent); SiO₂ 57–80%, Li₂O 11–19%
- **VITA ENAMIC**: Hybrid ceramic — 86 wt% ceramic / 14 wt% polymer (UDMA + TEGDMA)
- **Lava Ultimate**: Resin nanoceramic (3M ESPE); silica + zirconia nanomers in Bis-GMA resin matrix
- **Paradigm MZ100**: 85 wt% zirconia-silica ceramic in Bis-GMA/TEGDMA resin matrix

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Fracture Resistance (3 studies)

- **IPS e.max CAD 0.6 mm**: 3,067 ±933 N; **1.5 mm**: 4,995 ±855 N
- **VITA ENAMIC 0.6 mm**: 2,973 ±635 N; **1.5 mm**: 3,540 ±986 N
- **Lava Ultimate 0.6 mm**: 3,384 ±922 N; **1.5 mm**: 3,584 ±954 N
- No statistically significant difference at 0.6 mm between all three materials (Andrade 2018, p = 0.050)
- IPS e.max CAD at 1.5 mm showed significantly higher fracture resistance than other 1.5 mm groups (p = 0.027)
- After thermomechanical loading: no significant difference between IPS e.max CAD and VITA ENAMIC at 0.5 mm (p ≤ 0.291)
- Without thermomechanical loading: IPS e.max CAD > VITA ENAMIC (p ≤ 0.015)
- All fracture values exceed maximum bite force for non-parafunctional patients (424–630 N)
- VITA ENAMIC 0.5 mm: fracture only at >800 N above maximum bite force

### Fatigue Resistance (3 studies)

- 2 studies: CAD/CAM composite resin (Paradigm MZ100, Lava Ultimate) showed significantly higher survival/fatigue resistance than IPS e.max CAD
- 1 study (Heck 2019): No statistically significant difference in survival rate at ultrathin 0.3–0.5 mm

### Clinical Thresholds

- Minimum recommended thickness: composite resin < 1 mm; lithium disilicate 0.7–1.5 mm
- Maximum bite force (non-parafunctional): 424–630 N

### Risk of Bias

- High risk: 4 studies (Magne 2010, Schlichting 2011, Al-Akhali 2019, Heck 2019)
- Medium risk: 2 studies (Andrade 2018, Al-Akhali 2017)
- No low-risk studies identified

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 6 studies met inclusion criteria — small evidence base
- All studies in vitro (no clinical RCTs on worn teeth with these materials); clinical translation limited
- High heterogeneity in testing conditions (loading cycles, thermocycling protocols, antagonist materials, thickness, tooth type) precluded meta-analysis
- Most included studies had high risk of bias (lack of operator blinding, no sample size calculations)
- VITA ENAMIC was used as the composite resin surrogate in most fracture studies, though it is technically a hybrid ceramic; true resin composites (Lava Ultimate, Paradigm MZ100) appeared mainly in fatigue studies
- Search cutoff January 2022; newer materials (e.g., 3D-printed composites) not captured
- Authors call for standardized in vitro testing protocols and clinical RCTs with longer follow-up

## 6. Related Work

- Referenced clinical evidence supporting lithium disilicate occlusal veneers (Vailati, Güth series)
- Maeder et al. (2019) — VITA ENAMIC 0.5 mm requires >800 N above maximum bite force to crack
- Ioannidis et al. — confirmed VITA ENAMIC 0.5 mm load capacity above normal force intervals
- Johnson et al. — compared Lava Ultimate and Paradigm MZ100 at 0.3–1 mm thickness
- Systematic review by Almeida et al. (referenced as [31]) — recommended thickness guidelines for resin (<1 mm) and lithium disilicate (0.7–1.5 mm) occlusal veneers

## 7. Glossary

- **CAD/CAM**: Computer-aided design / computer-aided manufacturing
- **Occlusal veneer**: Minimally invasive indirect restoration covering the occlusal surface of posterior teeth (0.4–1.5 mm preparation depth); also termed "table-top restoration"
- **Fatigue resistance**: Ability to withstand cyclic loading without fracture, measured as survival rate under chewing simulation
- **Fracture resistance**: Maximum load to failure under single static or post-cyclic loading
- **IPS e.max CAD**: Lithium disilicate glass-ceramic block (Ivoclar); high strength but more brittle than polymer-containing alternatives
- **VITA ENAMIC**: Hybrid ceramic — interpenetrating ceramic-polymer network; higher elastic modulus closer to dentin than pure ceramics
- **Lava Ultimate**: Resin nanoceramic (3M ESPE); contains zirconia and silica nanomers in resin matrix
- **CONSORT**: Consolidated Standards of Reporting Trials; modified version used for in vitro quality assessment
- **BEWE**: Basic Erosive Wear Examination — erosive wear index
- **TWES**: Tooth Wear Evaluation System — multifactorial wear scoring
- **Weibull modulus**: Statistical measure of material reliability/consistency under load
