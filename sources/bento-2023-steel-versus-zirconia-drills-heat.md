## One-line Summary
SR + meta-analysis (Saudi Dental J 2024, 36:1–10; 10 in vitro studies): zirconia drills produce significantly lower bone temperature variation during implant site preparation than stainless-steel drills.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: The Saudi Dental Journal 2024;36:1–10
- Type: Systematic review with meta-analysis (PRISMA)
- Quality assessment: JBI Critical Assessment Guidelines for Quasi-Experimental Studies
- Search: PubMed/MEDLINE, Embase, Cochrane Library through January 2023
- Open access (CC BY-NC-ND)

## 2. Key Contributions
- First meta-analysis on zirconia vs steel drill thermal performance.
- Quantitative pooled estimate where chakraborty-2024 found qualitative inconclusiveness for "material" generally.
- Inverse-variance method for pooling.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- PICO: Do zirconia drills generate less heat than steel drills during implant site preparation?
- 10 in vitro studies (zirconia vs steel ± coatings).
- Meta-analysis: inverse-variance (IV) method.
- Risk of bias and quality appraisal per JBI.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Pooled estimate: zirconia drills produce significantly lower temperature variation than steel drills.
- Heterogeneity present but direction of effect consistent.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- All included studies in vitro.
- Coating variants in steel comparator are heterogeneous.
- Long-term wear / cost / fracture toughness of zirconia drills not addressed.
- Future: in vivo / clinical RCT; durability and clinical survival data.

## 6. Related Work
- Chakraborty 2024 SR — "drill material vs heat" inconclusive in qualitative SR.
- Marenzi 2018 — drill microstructure characterization.
- Bernabeu-Mira 2020 — SS vs DLC comparison.

## 7. Glossary
- **Zirconia drill**: yttria-stabilized zirconia ceramic drill, hard and chemically inert.
- **Inverse-variance method**: standard meta-analytic pooling weighting studies by inverse of their variance.
- **JBI**: Joanna Briggs Institute quality appraisal tool.
