## Why Ingested

전해수 (Electrolyzed Water, EW)는 COVID-19 팬데믹 이후 치과 소독 분야에서 주목받은 신흥 소독제다. 기존 소독법([[infection-control/patino-marin-2025-sterilization-disinfection-dental-practices]])을 보완하는 novel approach로, 63개 연구를 망라한 SR이라 근거 수준이 높아 인제스트한다.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review of 63 studies (PROSPERO CRD42020206986) concluding that electrolyzed water is an effective broad-spectrum disinfectant for dental surfaces, materials, and equipment — including activity against SARS-CoV-2 — but with high overall risk of bias (only 35.18% NPQIP "Yes" entries).

## 한줄요약

63개 연구 체계적 고찰: 전해수(EW)는 치과 표면·재료·DUWL에서 광범위 항균·항바이러스(SARS-CoV-2 포함) 효과를 보이나, 전반적 비뚤림 위험이 높음(NPQIP "Yes" 비율 35.18%).

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First systematic review synthesizing evidence on electrolyzed water (EW) specifically for microbiologic control in the dental setting during COVID-19 pandemic.
2. Demonstrates broad-spectrum antimicrobial activity of EW: antibacterial, antifungal, and antiviral (including HBV, HIV, herpes simplex, poliovirus).
3. Confirms compatibility of EW with dental materials: impressions (PVS, alginate) and acrylic resin prostheses remain dimensionally stable and surface-accurate after EW disinfection.
4. Dental unit waterline (DUWL) decontamination with EW shows up to 98.1% microbial killing rate sustained for up to 6 weeks, comparable to glutaraldehyde.
5. Highlights critical methodological gap: only 35.18% of NPQIP items answered "Yes" across included studies, indicating widespread high risk of bias.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Systematic review per PRISMA 2020 checklist; quality assessed by AMSTAR-2.
- **Databases**: MEDLINE/PubMed, EMBASE, Scopus, Web of Science, Cochrane CENTRAL, LILACS; grey literature via OpenGrey and PQDT-ProQuest; manual journal search.
- **Inclusion**: All experimental studies (RCTs, non-randomized CTs, animal studies with control group, in vitro studies) on EW for microbiologic control in dental setting; case reports and editorial letters excluded.
- **Studies included**: 63 studies from 290 screened (218 excluded at title/abstract, 9 additional after full-text review).
- **Study composition**: 58 in vitro + 5 randomized clinical trials.
- **RoB tool**: Modified NPQIP (Nature Publication Quality Improvement Project) score sheet — 756 total entries analyzed.
- **Data synthesis**: Qualitative only (substantial heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis).

### Types of Electrolyzed Water

EW is produced by electrolyzing diluted salt or acid solutions; the major subtypes tested include:
- **Electrolyzed hydrogen water** (neutral-alkaline, high dissolved hydrogen)
- **Aqua oxidizing water / acid water / acidic electrolyzed water (AEW)** (pH ~2.5, high free available chlorine)
- **Superoxidized water** (near-neutral, high oxidation-reduction potential)
- **Oxidative potential water (OPW)**
- **Slightly acidic electrolyzed water (SAEW)** (pH 5–6.5)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Antibacterial activity (32 in vitro studies)

| Target bacteria | Outcome |
|---|---|
| *S. mutans*, *L. acidophilus* (caries) | Significant CFU reduction, biofilm inhibition |
| Periodontopathogens (*P. gingivalis*, *F. nucleatum*, *A. actinomycetemcomitans*, *T. denticola*, *P. intermedia*) | Significant viable cell % reduction or CFU decrease |
| Endodontic pathogens (*E. faecalis*, *C. albicans* in canal models) | Time-dependent CFU reduction, comparable to NaOCl |

### Antifungal activity (9 in vitro studies)
- *Candida albicans*: significant CFU reduction across multiple EW types.

### Antiviral activity (limited studies)
- HBV, HIV, poliovirus type 1, HSV-1: reduced infectivity potential in time-dependent manner.
- SARS-CoV-2: antiviral potential noted (mechanistic plausibility given broad virucidal data), though direct SARS-CoV-2 dental-setting RCT evidence was lacking at search cutoff.

### Clinical trials (5 RCTs)
1. **Caries**: EW oral rinse → significantly reduced *S. mutans* CFU vs. tap water.
2. **Gingivitis prevention**: AEW irrigation after halting oral hygiene → effective plaque control, gingivitis prevention.
3. **Plaque inhibition**: Acid water rinse ≈ chlorhexidine for plaque/bacteria in human dentine.
4. **Periodontitis**: Superoxidized water + SRP → reduced probing pocket depth and gingival bleeding at 30 days.
5. **Endodontics**: OPW vs. 1% NaOCl in necrotic primary teeth → no significant difference in bacterial load reduction.

### Dental office disinfection (15 studies)
- **DUWL** (high/low-speed handpieces, ultrasonic scalers): up to **98.1% microbial kill** for ≤6 weeks; antimicrobial activity comparable to glutaraldehyde.
- **Impression disinfection**: PVS and alginate impressions disinfected without dimensional or surface integrity compromise.
- **Acrylic resin prostheses**: Effective decontamination without material damage.
- **Titanium surfaces / dental implants**: Effective.
- **Toothbrushes and other surfaces/devices**: Effective.

### Risk of Bias
- 756 NPQIP entries total; only **35.18% "Yes"**, 48.15% "No", rest "NA".
- Major weaknesses: no sample size justification (85–82% "No"), no randomization/blinding specification (>80% "No"), no error bar definition, no normality testing, no variance analysis.
- All studies scored "Yes" for sample size and collection specification items (#1, #2).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Overwhelming majority of included studies are in vitro — only 5 RCTs; limits direct clinical extrapolation.
- Substantial heterogeneity (EW type, concentration, microorganism, exposure time, dental substrate) precluded meta-analysis.
- High risk of bias across all studies (NPQIP "Yes" rate 35.18%).
- Limited direct SARS-CoV-2 evidence specifically in dental aerosol/surface contexts.
- Future needs: standardized EW preparation protocols, clinical RCTs for DUWL and aerosol decontamination, head-to-head vs. currently recommended disinfectants.

## 7. Glossary

- **EW (전해수, Electrolyzed Water)**: Water produced by electrolyzing dilute salt or acid solutions; generates reactive oxygen/chlorine species.
- **AEW (산성 전해수, Acidic Electrolyzed Water)**: pH ~2–3, high free available chlorine; strong oxidizing antimicrobial.
- **SAEW (약산성 전해수, Slightly Acidic Electrolyzed Water)**: pH 5–6.5; milder, potentially less material-damaging.
- **DUWL (치과 유닛 급수관, Dental Unit Waterline)**: Narrow-bore plastic tubing supplying water to handpieces and scalers; biofilm-prone.
- **ORP (산화환원전위, Oxidation-Reduction Potential)**: Measure of oxidizing/reducing capacity; high ORP correlates with antimicrobial potency in EW.
- **NPQIP**: Nature Publication Quality Improvement Project — scoring tool adapted for in vitro and in vivo RoB assessment.
- **CFU (집락형성단위, Colony Forming Units)**: Measure of viable bacterial load.
- **PROSPERO**: International registry for systematic review protocols.
