## One-line Summary
J Clin Periodontol 2005;32(6):645-652: beagle dog study, immediate SLA implants in fresh sockets — buccal wall resorbed more than lingual; BIC established 2.6±0.4mm (buccal) below SLA; immediate implant does NOT prevent ridge remodeling.

## 1. Key Results
- 5 beagle dogs; lower 3rd/4th premolars extracted; SLA-surface implants placed immediately
- Buccal bone resorption significantly greater than lingual
- BIC found 2.6±0.4mm apical to SLA surface margin on buccal; 0.2±0.5mm on lingual
- Bundle bone (tooth-dependent bone) resorbs entirely after extraction
- Osseointegration confirmed histologically
- Conclusion: immediate implant placement does NOT prevent post-extraction ridge resorption

## 2. Methodology
- Animal (beagle dog) experimental study
- Bilateral split design; 5 dogs
- Histological/histomorphometric analysis at 3 months
- SLA implant surface
- Assessed bone-to-implant contact, marginal bone levels

## 3. Clinical Implications
- Companion to Botticelli 2004 (human study confirms same finding)
- Bundle bone loss is biologically inevitable — implant does not substitute the periodontal ligament
- Supports simultaneous GBR/contour augmentation approach
- Landmark study cited in virtually all immediate implant protocols
