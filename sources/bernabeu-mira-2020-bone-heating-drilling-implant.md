## One-line Summary
In vitro study (Materials 2020, 800 rpm, no irrigation, synthetic blocks): drill **design** (twisted < straight blades) is a stronger thermal driver than drill **material** (SS ≈ DLC overall); initial/pilot drills heat more than progressive/final; all groups show progressive temperature rise across 50 drillings.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Materials 2020, 13, 1921
- Type: In vitro experimental, controlled automated press
- Affiliation: University of Valencia, Faculty of Medicine and Dentistry, Oral Surgery Unit

- Published: 19 April 2020

## 2. Key Contributions
- Disentangles material, design, and wear contributions to drilling-induced bone heating in a single experimental design.
- Identifies twisted blade geometry as a robust thermal-mitigation feature.
- Shows that material choice (SS vs DLC) is statistically irrelevant at the overall mean level.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Three groups of drills:
  1. Stainless steel (SS), straight blades
  2. Diamond-like carbon (DLC) coated, straight blades
  3. DLC-coated, twisted blades
- Each group: 4 sequential drills (initial, pilot, progressive, final).
- Intermittent gradual drilling, 800 rpm, **no irrigation**, standardized synthetic blocks.
- Automatic press: 2 kg max axial load, fully controlled, no human intervention.
- 50 drillings per drill, sterilized every 5 uses.
- Thermographic camera for mean thermal change measurement.
- Statistics: Student's t-test, ANOVA, multiple linear regression; α = 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
| Comparison | Result | p-value |
|---|---|---|
| SS vs DLC overall mean | No significant difference | >0.05 |
| SS vs DLC pilot drill (specific) | Significant difference | <0.05 |
| Twisted vs straight blade — progressive drill | Twisted lower thermal change | <0.01 |
| Twisted vs straight blade — final drill | Twisted lower thermal change | <0.01 |
| Initial / pilot vs progressive / final | Initial/pilot higher thermal change | Significant |
| Across 50 drillings | All drills: gradual mean temperature increase | — |

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- In vitro synthetic blocks — not biological bone variability.
- **No irrigation** — magnifies effects and makes absolute values clinically inapplicable.
- Single operational rpm (800).
- Two coatings only (uncoated SS, DLC) — does not generalize to TiN, zirconia, or other surfaces.
- Future: with irrigation, multiple rpm, additional materials, prospective comparison to BIC.

## 7. Glossary
- **DLC (Diamond-Like Carbon)**: hard amorphous carbon coating with sp² and sp³ bonding, high hardness and low friction.
- **Twisted vs straight blade**: drill flute geometry; twisted flutes evacuate debris and dissipate heat more effectively.
- **Initial / pilot / progressive / final drill**: standard sequential drilling steps in implantology.
- **Thermographic camera**: infrared imaging for non-contact surface temperature measurement.
