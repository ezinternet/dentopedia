## Why Ingested

The wiki's oral mucosal-disease coverage in `oral-medicine` (aphthous stomatitis, lichen planus, BMS) had no entry on cancer-therapy-induced oral mucositis — a high-incidence (40–100%) inflammatory condition distinct from those entities. This SR fills that gap and pairs with [[oral-medicine/dean-2022-oral-chronic-gvhd-review]], which covers the adjacent oncology context (oral chronic GVHD after hematopoietic stem cell transplantation); both deal with debilitating oral mucosal injury in the cancer/transplant patient, but mucositis is the acute antineoplastic-therapy lesion whereas chronic oral GVHD is the late immunologic one. According to PubMed ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.3390/cancers16081548)).

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (PRISMA, PROSPERO CRD42022347208; 39 studies, n=14–148, mNOS quality) of pediatric cancer-therapy-induced oral mucositis therapies: chlorhexidine best for incidence, honey best for duration, olive oil best for pain, palifermin reduces incidence/severity/duration; calcium phosphate showed no effect — but overall evidence remains inconclusive for firm protocols.

## 한줄요약

소아 항암치료 구강점막염 치료를 다룬 체계적 문헌고찰 (PRISMA, 39편, n=14–148): 발생률엔 클로르헥시딘, 기간엔 꿀, 통증엔 올리브유가 가장 효과적이고 팔리퍼민은 발생률·중증도·기간을 줄였으나, 칼슘인산염은 효과 없음 — 전반적 근거는 아직 불충분.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Systematic review (PRISMA-compliant, PROSPERO CRD42022347208), no meta-analysis
- **Journal**: Cancers (Basel) 2024;16(8):1548
- **Affiliation**: Universidade Católica Portuguesa, Faculty of Dental Medicine, Viseu, Portugal
- **Search**: PubMed/MEDLINE, NICE, ICTRP, Embase, Scopus, Web of Science up to March 2024; English/Portuguese; last 20 years
- **Population**: Pediatric (≤18 y) undergoing/completed antineoplastic treatment (chemotherapy, radiotherapy, HSCT, or combination) who developed oral mucositis
- Source: PubMed full text (PMC11048343). DOI [10.3390/cancers16081548](https://doi.org/10.3390/cancers16081548)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First/recent SR to isolate the **pediatric** population for cancer-therapy oral mucositis management (most prior evidence is adult or mixed).
- Ranks 10 main intervention classes by which oral-mucositis outcome (incidence, severity, duration, pain) each best addresses.
- Frames the pediatric dentist as a member of the oncology care team for assessment and intervention.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PICO question: which treatments are most effective in treatment and prevention of cancer-induced oral mucositis in pediatric patients?
- Screening: 2655 unique records → 59 after title/abstract → **39 included**; Rayyan tool; two independent screeners (Cohen's κ = 0.82, almost perfect), third for consensus.
- Quality: **Modified Newcastle–Ottawa Scale** (0–9 stars: selection 0–4, comparability 0–2, outcome/exposure 0–3); bias high (≤5), moderate (6–7), low (≥8).
- Study geography: Brazil (8), Italy (6), India (3) most common; sample size 14–148.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Intervention frequency: **LLLT** most studied (8 studies, 20%), then palifermin (3), zinc (3), honey (3), calcium phosphate (3), olive oil (2), oral cryotherapy (2), vitamin E (2), glutamine (2), chlorhexidine (2). Treatment duration 3–29 days.

Efficacy by outcome (per SR synthesis):
- **Incidence** — chlorhexidine most effective; also reduced by glutamine, palifermin, olive oil.
- **Severity** — chlorhexidine, honey, palifermin, olive oil, vitamin E, glutamine similar.
- **Duration** — honey most effective; also palifermin.
- **Pain** — olive oil highest; honey, LLLT, zinc-based equally effective.
- **No effect** — calcium phosphate (all 3 studies); TGF-β2, chewing gum, ketamine mouthwash (single studies); plain oral cryotherapy alone, vitamin E (one study) inconsistent.

Per-therapy notes: palifermin (recombinant human KGF) reduces incidence/severity/duration in acute leukemia but has conflicting safety reports; chlorhexidine works via removal of pathogenic oral bacteria; honey/olive oil via decreased pro-inflammatory cytokines (manuka honey decreased severity+pain, natural honey severity+duration); zinc gluconate vs polaprezinc dose/effect undefined; glutamine 400 mg/kg/day reduced incidence in ALL after high-dose methotrexate; cryotherapy effective only with propantheline (compliance-limited). Quality: 9 high-bias, 16 moderate, 14 low-bias articles.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- No meta-analysis (heterogeneity of cancer types, protocols, outcome measures, durations precluded pooling).
- Many small-sample, design-biased, low-adherence studies; comparability across efficacy levels limited.
- Evidence still inconclusive to establish accurate clinical protocols; larger samples and standardized outcome measurement needed.
- English/Portuguese restriction is a potential language bias.

## 6. Related Work

- Adjacent oncology oral-injury context: oral chronic GVHD after HSCT.
- Aligns with broader MASCC/ISOO-type clinical practice guidelines for oral mucositis (cited but not pediatric-limited).

## 7. Glossary

- **Oral mucositis (OM)**: antineoplastic-therapy-induced oral mucosa inflammation; five stages (initiation, signaling, amplification, ulceration, healing).
- **LLLT / photobiomodulation**: low-power red/near-infrared light therapy.
- **Palifermin**: recombinant human keratinocyte growth factor (KGF).
- **Polaprezinc**: zinc L-carnosine complex.
- **HSCT**: hematopoietic stem cell transplantation.
- **mNOS**: Modified Newcastle–Ottawa Scale (quality/bias rating).
