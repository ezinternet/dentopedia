## One-line Summary
In-vivo/in-vitro study: acid-etching cpTi at 140°C creates hierarchical meso+micro+nano surface (Ra 6-12× higher); osteoblast differentiation ↑ without compromising attachment; bone-implant integration significantly enhanced vs micro-only.

## 1. Document Information
Int J Mol Sci 2020;21:783. doi:10.3390/ijms21030783. UCLA + Aichi Gakuin. Published 2020-01-25.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Novel acid-etching protocol (H2SO4, 140-150°C) creates three-scale hierarchical surface
- Meso-scale spikes (20-50 µm) formed exclusively at ≥140°C — the critical topographical feature
- Resolves proliferation-differentiation tradeoff of conventional microrough surfaces
- Regression analysis confirms meso-spike size and density as key predictors of osteoconductivity

## 3. Methodology
cpTi acid-etched at 120/130/140/150°C. In vitro: osteoblast attachment, proliferation, differentiation (ALP, mineralization). In vivo: rat femur model, biomechanical push-in test, histomorphometry.

## 4. Key Results
- 140°C optimal: meso+micro+nano structure; greatest osseointegration strength
- Ra 6-12× greater than micro-only surface
- Osteoblast attachment and spreading: NOT compromised despite higher Ra
- Differentiation (ALP, mineralization): significantly increased
- In vivo BIC and push-in force: significantly greater than micro-only

## 5. Limitations
Animal model only; no human clinical data; long-term durability of hierarchical structure under occlusal loading unknown.

## 6. Related Work
Ogawa 2025 (D2 topography as next frontier), Iwasa 2011 (nano-hybrid to sustain UV-PF benefits), Yamada 2012 (nano-HA on micro-Ti).

## 7. Glossary
Meso-scale: 20-50 µm; Ra: average surface roughness; hierarchical topography: multiple-scale roughness features on same surface.
