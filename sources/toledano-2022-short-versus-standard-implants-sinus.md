## One-line Summary
Meta-analysis of 14 RCTs (597 patients, 901 implants) finding similar implant survival but significantly lower MBL with short implants (≤6 mm) vs. standard implants (≥8 mm) + sinus lift in atrophic posterior maxilla.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Clinical Oral Investigations (2022) 26:6681–6698
- **Type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (PRISMA, PROSPERO #295642)
- **PICO**: Short implants ≤6 mm without sinus augmentation (I) vs. standard implants ≥8 mm + sinus lift (C) in posterior atrophic maxilla patients (P); implant survival + MBL (O)
- **Only RCTs included**
- **Sample**: 14 studies, 597 patients, 901 implants; search up to February 2022

## 2. Key Contributions
- Implant survival is **equivalent** between short implants and standard+sinus lift
- Short implants produce **significantly less MBL** at both ≤1 year and >1 year
- Short implants offer reduced postoperative discomfort, minimal invasiveness, shorter treatment time, lower cost
- Supports paradigm shift: sinus grafting with long implants → short implants as first-line

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Search**: PubMed, Cochrane Oral Health, EMBASE, Web of Science (1993–Feb 2022)
- **Short implant definition**: ≤6 mm (strict — excluded studies using >6 mm as "short")
- **Standard implant**: ≥8 mm + sinus elevation (lateral window or osteotome technique)
- **Statistics**: Random-effects model; RR for IS; weighted means for MBL; RevMan 5.4
- **Subgroups**: ≤1 year vs. >1 year follow-up
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane tool; most studies low risk

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Implant Survival (IS)
- RR = 1.02 (CI 95%: 1.00–1.05), p = 0.09
- **No significant difference** — short implants and standard+sinus lift are equivalent in survival
- Heterogeneity: I² = 0% (low)

### Marginal Bone Loss (MBL)
- ≤1 year: MBL difference = **0.11 mm** (CI 0.01–0.20), p = 0.03 — favors short implants
- >1 year: MBL difference = **0.23 mm** (CI 0.07–0.39), p = 0.005 — favors short implants
- High heterogeneity: I² = 78% (≤1 yr), 74% (>1 yr)

### Secondary Outcomes
- Postoperative discomfort: less with short implants
- Surgical time: shorter with short implants
- Patient satisfaction: favors short implants
- Complications: sinus lift associated with up to 38% complication rate; implant failure up to 17% within 3 years

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Relatively small total sample (597 patients)
- High MBL heterogeneity limits precision
- Short-term follow-up dominant; long-term (>5 years) data sparse
- Strictly ≤6 mm definition may not reflect clinical range
- Need high-quality long-term RCTs to confirm findings

## 6. Related Work
- Saenz-Ravello 2023 (this wiki): mandible-focused umbrella review; short implants vs. BA
- Yu 2021 (this wiki): extra-short implant meta-analysis (≤6 mm vs. ≥8 mm); both maxilla and mandible
- Sinus lift complications: GBR 5.8–27.3%, bone blocks 2.5–100%, distraction 6.8–57.1%

## 7. Glossary
- **Short implant (this review)**: ≤6 mm intrabony length
- **Standard implant**: ≥8 mm intrabony length
- **Sinus lift (sinus floor elevation)**: Surgical augmentation of maxillary sinus floor to increase bone height for implant placement
- **MBL**: Marginal bone loss — radiographic measurement of peri-implant bone level change
- **IS (Implant Survival)**: Implant remaining in situ at follow-up
- **RR (Risk Ratio)**: Relative probability of event between groups; RR = 1 means equal risk
