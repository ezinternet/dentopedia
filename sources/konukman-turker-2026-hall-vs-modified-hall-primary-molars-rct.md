## One-line Summary

RCT (268 primary molars, deep dentine caries) over 24 months: Hall technique (HT, seal caries under SSC, no caries removal) vs modified HT (selective removal of outer carious layer first) gave statistically equal 24-mo success (86.6% vs 92.8%, p=0.121); SSC fit was decisive in HT (insufficient fit → 40% major failure vs 7.3% with good fit).

## 한줄요약

RCT(유구치 268개, deep dentine caries) 24개월: Hall technique(HT, 우식제거 없이 SSC로 봉입) vs modified HT(바깥 우식층 선택제거 후 SSC) 성공률 통계적 동등(86.6% vs 92.8%, p=0.121). HT에서 SSC 적합도가 결정적 — 부적합 시 major failure 40% vs 적합 7.3%.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First wiki page on the Hall technique / SSC for primary molars.
- One of the first RCTs of a "modified HT" (selective caries removal before SSC) vs conventional HT in deep lesions.
- Identifies SSC marginal fit as a key success determinant.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Two-arm parallel RCT, 268 primary molars (189 children, 3–10 y), block-randomized 1:1 to HT (n=134) vs modified HT (n=134). Deep dentine caries (≥inner half of dentine) with a band of normal dentine over a vital, symptom-free/reversibly-inflamed pulp. Modified HT removed the necrotic/contaminated outer layer to soft dentine (ICCC selective removal) before crowning. Single operator; blinded assessor; outcomes (Innes 2007: success / minor / major failure) at 3/6/12/18/24 mo. Secondary by tooth group, caries surfaces, preoperative pain, SSC fit.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **24-mo success: HT 86.6% vs modified HT 92.8% — NS (p=0.121).**
- Major failures consistently fewer in modified HT (24-mo: 10.1% HT vs 3.6% modHT).
- **HT, SSC fit decisive (p=0.005)**: insufficient fit → major failure 40.0%, success 50.0%; sufficient fit → major failure 7.3%, success 89.9%.
- Modified HT by tooth (p=0.034): first primary molars more minor failures (9.8% vs 0%); second molars higher success (95.7% vs 87.8%).
- Preoperative pain 3% (NS); insufficient SSC fit ~8% both groups; no pulp exposures during modified-HT caries removal.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single examiner (observer bias, no inter-examiner reliability); no immediate postoperative radiograph (ALADAIP); non-standardized amount of caries removed; possible subclinical pulp inflammation in proximal lesions.
- Longer follow-up RCTs needed to confirm whether the modification reduces major failures.

## 7. Glossary

- **Hall technique (HT)**: cementing a preformed SSC over a carious primary molar without anaesthesia, prep, or caries removal — biological sealing.
- **Modified HT**: HT with selective removal of the outer carious layer to soft dentine before crowning.
- **SSC / PMC**: stainless steel / preformed metal crown.
- **Innes criteria**: success / minor / major failure classification for sealed-caries crowns.
