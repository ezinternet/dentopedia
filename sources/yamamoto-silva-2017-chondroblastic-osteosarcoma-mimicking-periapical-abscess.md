## Why Ingested

[[wiki/endodontics/diagnosis/karamifar-2020-endodontic-periapical-lesion-an-overview]]는 근단 방사선투과상(periapical radiolucency)이 비치성(non-endodontic) 병변일 수 있음을 일반론으로 다루지만, 악성 종양이 치근단 농양(periapical abscess)을 모방한 구체적 증례가 부족했다. 본 증례보고(Yamamoto-Silva 2017)는 생활치(vital pulp)에 동반된 근단 방사선투과상·치주인대강 확대가 실제로는 연골모세포성 골육종(chondroblastic osteosarcoma)이었던 사례로, 근관치료 전 비치성 악성 종양을 감별진단에 포함해야 함을 보강한다. 방사선학적으로는 [[wiki/radiology/mortazavi-2016-lesions-associated-with-periodontal-ligament]]의 PDL 확대 감별진단 목록(골육종 포함)을 임상 증례로 실증한다.

## One-line Summary

Case report (J Appl Oral Sci 2017): an 18-year-old male with a periapical radiolucency, PDL widening, and lamina dura loss at vital teeth #29/#30/#31 — initially suspected periapical abscess — was ultimately diagnosed by incisional biopsy as chondroblastic osteosarcoma, underscoring that non-endodontic malignancies must be in the differential of periapical lesions.

## 한줄요약

증례보고(J Appl Oral Sci 2017): 18세 남성에서 생활치 #29/#30/#31에 근단 방사선투과상·치주인대강 확대·치조백선(lamina dura) 소실이 보여 처음에는 치근단 농양으로 의심했으나, 절개생검 결과 연골모세포성 골육종으로 확진된 사례 — 근단병소 감별진단에 비치성 악성 종양을 반드시 포함해야 함을 보여준다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Documents a case where chondroblastic osteosarcoma of the mandible clinically and radiographically mimicked a periapical abscess at the apices of vital teeth.
- Emphasizes that **positive pulp sensibility (vitality) testing** in the presence of a periapical radiolucency should raise suspicion for a non-endodontic etiology, including intraosseous malignancy.
- Reinforces the importance of incisional biopsy and CBCT in working up periapical radiolucencies that do not fit an endodontic infectious picture.
- Adds osteosarcoma to the documented list of malignant/non-endodontic lesions that resemble periapical abscess or apical periodontitis.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Single-patient case report. An 18-year-old male was referred with a chief complaint of "discomfort on the right posterior gingiva" for over one month.

Diagnostic workup:
- **Clinical exam**: small expansion and redness of buccal and lingual cortical plates of the right mandible at teeth #29, #30, #31; overlying mucosa intact; no pain or inflammation signs.
- **Pulp vitality testing**: tetrafluoroethane spray (Endo-Ice; Hygenic Corp) — positive response in all teeth associated with the radiolucent lesion, so root canals were not treated.
- **Conventional imaging**: periapical and panoramic radiographs showed 4–5 mm periapical radiolucency in mesial and distal roots of #29 and #30, slight rarefaction of inter-dental alveolar bone, clear periodontal ligament (PDL) space widening, and evident loss of lamina dura.
- **CBCT**: undefined/ill-defined periapical osteolytic lesion — a hypodense lesion with hyperdense areas inside, thinning of the lingual (and buccal) cortical plates, and altered trabecular bone associated with roots of #29 and #30.
- **Incisional biopsy + histopathology**: proliferation of round to spindle-shaped cells with occasional cellular pleomorphism and variable osteoid production; a focal area of atypical chondroblastic cell proliferation; Ki-67 immunohistochemistry showed evident cellular activity → diagnosis of chondroblastic osteosarcoma confirmed.

No history of dental trauma; no crown cracks on #29/#30 marginal ridges; no prior restorations.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Diagnosis**: chondroblastic osteosarcoma (confirmed by microscopy + Ki-67 IHC).
- **Disease course**: 10 days after incisional biopsy the lesion enlarged considerably; patient referred to cancer hospital.
- **Treatment**: hemimandibulectomy with wide surgical margins + adjuvant chemotherapy, followed by right-mandible reconstruction with a fibular graft.
- **Follow-up**: at one year, bone scintigraphy showed no signs of bone metastases (tumor remission).
- **Background epidemiology cited**: osteosarcoma incidence ~0.7 per million; jaw osteosarcomas = 5–13% of all osteosarcomas; maxilla and mandible affected with roughly equal frequency; predominantly ages 10–20, slight male predominance; jaw lesions commonly show chondroblastic differentiation (possibly worse prognosis).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single case report — lowest evidence weight; no generalizable estimates.
- The initial diagnosis included periapical abscess based on location and prevalence of inflammatory lesions; the key disambiguating feature was the **vital pulp** response, which prompted further imaging and biopsy.
- Authors recommend that clinicians exercise exceptional care in endodontic diagnoses based on clinical/radiographic findings alone and consider non-endodontic malignancies before root canal treatment.

## 6. Related Work

The authors cite a series of prior reports of non-endodontic lesions mimicking apical periodontitis / periapical abscess, including mesenchymal chondrosarcoma (Bueno 2008), calcifying odontogenic cyst (Carvalhosa 2014), central giant cell lesion (Carvalhosa 2014), ameloblastoma (Faitaroni 2008), lymphangioma (Rodrigues 2011), and a review series (Pontes 2014, J Endod). Jaw osteosarcoma series cited: Bennett 2000 (30-year review), Paparella 2013 (74 cases, predominant chondroblastic pattern), Takahama 2003 (25 head/neck osteosarcomas, Ki-67+ in 88%).

## 7. Glossary

- **Chondroblastic osteosarcoma**: an osteosarcoma subtype in which malignant mesenchymal cells produce osteoid plus a predominant chondroid (cartilaginous) component; common differentiation pattern in jaw osteosarcomas.
- **Periapical abscess**: an acute/chronic suppurative inflammatory lesion at the root apex, almost always secondary to pulp necrosis and root canal infection.
- **Pulp sensibility (vitality) testing**: thermal/electric test of pulp nerve response; a positive (vital) result argues against an endodontic-origin periapical infection.
- **Lamina dura**: the radiopaque cortical bone lining the tooth socket; loss can indicate aggressive/infiltrative pathology.
- **PDL widening**: widening of the periodontal ligament space on radiographs; an early radiographic sign of osteosarcoma due to tumor infiltration, but also seen in benign conditions (occlusal trauma, orthodontic force).
- **Ki-67**: a nuclear proliferation marker used immunohistochemically to assess cellular (mitotic) activity.
