## Why Ingested

Ingested as the **contrasting half** of the CoQ10 evidence pair. This 2022 SR+MA (11 trials) reaches a *pro-gel* conclusion — "our findings encourage administration of coenzyme Q10 gel" — that directly conflicts with the newer, route-stratified [[wiki/periodontics/fernandez-2025-coenzyme-q10-nonsurgical-periodontal-sr]], which finds the gel route null. Holding both makes the conflict legible: Rasoolzadeh pooled local + systemic routes and gingivitis-leaning indices (Plaque/Bleeding/Gingival index) with very high heterogeneity (I² 72–89%) and noted high-risk-of-bias studies drove the larger effect. The pair teaches why "a meta-analysis says it works" needs route and bias scrutiny before it reaches a patient.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review + meta-analysis of 11 controlled trials: CoQ10 significantly reduced Plaque Index, Bleeding Index, Pocket Depth (SMD −0.96), CAL (SMD −0.73), and Gingival Index in periodontitis (intra-pocket > topical), but with very high heterogeneity (I² 72–89%) and a larger effect in high-risk-of-bias studies.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (11 controlled trials, random-effects).
- **Journal**: The Journal of Evidence-Based Dental Practice (2022;22(2):101710).
- **Registration**: PROSPERO CRD42020212424.
- **Search window**: 1980–August 2020; Embase, CENTRAL, PubMed/MEDLINE, Scopus, Web of Science, Google Scholar, ProQuest (269 records → 11 trials).
- **Abstract-only — full text not retrieved (no PMC open access).**

## 2. Key Contributions

- Pooled estimate across five indices (Plaque, Bleeding, Pocket Depth, CAL, Gingival Index).
- Subgroup signal that intra-pocket delivery beats topical.
- Transparent about the bias gradient: high-risk-of-bias trials reported larger CoQ10 effects.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Random-effects meta-analysis of controlled trials.
- Outcomes: Plaque Index, Bleeding Index, Pocket Depth, clinical attachment level, Gingival Index.
- Subgroup by delivery (intra-pocket vs topical); risk-of-bias subgrouping.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | SMD | 95% CI | I² |
|---|---|---|---|
| Plaque Index | −0.64 | −1.03 to −0.26 | 85.7% |
| Bleeding Index | −1.05 | −1.70 to −0.41 | 89.5% |
| Pocket Depth | −0.96 | −1.35 to −0.58 | 84.7% |
| CAL | −0.73 | −1.23 to −0.22 | 87.8% |
| Gingival Index | −0.63 | −0.97 to −0.28 | 72.8% |

Intra-pocket > topical; high-RoB studies showed greater effect.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Very high heterogeneity** (I² 72–89%) across all outcomes.
- Pooled local + systemic routes and gingivitis-leaning indices, inflating the apparent gel benefit — the methodological reason it conflicts with Fernandez 2025.
- Effect magnitude tracked with risk of bias (larger in high-RoB studies) → likely overstated.
- Authors call for higher-quality trials.

## 7. Glossary

- **CoQ10 / ubiquinone** — endogenous antioxidant marketed as a periodontal supplement.
- **I²** — heterogeneity statistic; >75% denotes considerable inconsistency across studies.
- **Intra-pocket vs topical** — subgingival placement vs surface application of the gel.
- **Risk of bias (RoB)** — methodological quality; effect inflation in high-RoB studies suggests overestimation.
