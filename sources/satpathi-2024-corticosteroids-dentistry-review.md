## One-line Summary
Narrative review: corticosteroids in dentistry — anti-inflammatory/immunosuppressive use across specialties (OLP, aphthous stomatitis, post-op edema); adrenal crisis risk from steroid-dependent patients; overcautious prescribing concerns.

## 2. Commonly Used Dental Corticosteroids

| Drug | Route | Relative Potency | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hydrocortisone | Systemic / Topical | 1× (reference) | Emergency (adrenal crisis), topical paste |
| Dexamethasone | Systemic / IV | 25× | Post-op edema, severe oral lesions |
| Methylprednisolone | Systemic | 5× | Post-surgical swelling |
| Prednisolone | Systemic | 4× | Oral lichen planus, pemphigus |
| Triamcinolone acetonide | Topical (paste) | 5× | Aphthous ulcers, OLP — Kenalog in Orabase |
| Clobetasol propionate | Topical | 500× | Severe OLP, recalcitrant lesions |

## 3. Clinical Applications
- **Oral Lichen Planus (OLP)**: topical clobetasol or triamcinolone first-line; systemic prednisolone for severe erosive
- **Recurrent Aphthous Stomatitis**: topical triamcinolone/betamethasone
- **Post-surgical edema**: dexamethasone 4–8mg PO/IM before/after extraction
- **Pericoronitis**: short course dexamethasone + antibiotics
- **Allergic reactions in dental office**: hydrocortisone IV (emergency)
- **Temporomandibular joint**: intra-articular corticosteroid injection

## 4. Adrenal Insufficiency — Dental Risk
- Patients on long-term systemic corticosteroids (>2 weeks at ≥5mg prednisolone/day): HPA axis suppression
- Dental stress protocol: supplemental corticosteroid dose before major/stressful procedures
- Hydrocortisone 25–50mg IV/IM for major oral surgery in steroid-dependent patients

## 5. Key Cautions
- Overcautious prescription concern highlighted: many clinicians prescribe corticosteroids for minor extractions without indication
- Systemic side effects of long-term use: osteoporosis, diabetes, hypertension, immunosuppression
- Short-course single doses (dexamethasone 4–8mg) for post-op: minimal systemic risk
