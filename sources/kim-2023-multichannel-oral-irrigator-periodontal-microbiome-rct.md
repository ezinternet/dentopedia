## Why Ingested

Extends the oral-irrigation evidence in our `interdental-cleaning` category from a clinical-index angle into a mechanistic, microbiome angle. [[interdental-cleaning/liu-2025-water-flossing-adjunct-nspt-periodontitis-rct]] also used 16S rRNA sequencing to link water flossing to microbial shifts in periodontitis patients; this Kim 2023 RCT provides the complementary "healthy-mouth + 3-day no-brushing challenge" model, showing how a multichannel oral irrigator (MCOI) blunts dysbiotic shifts (Prevotella, Bacteroidetes) that arise when mechanical cleaning is withheld. Reinforces the device-efficacy line of [[interdental-cleaning/mancinelli-lyle-2024-water-flosser-vs-interdental-brush-rct]] and the microbiome line of [[interdental-cleaning/min-2024-brushing-flossing-mouthrinsing-plaque-microbiota]].

## One-line Summary

Small preliminary RCT (n=29 healthy adults, 3-day no-brushing model): a multichannel oral irrigator (COMORAL, 45° gingival-margin jet + suction) kept PI/SBI stable and lowered % BOP, and prevented the Prevotella/Bacteroidetes increase seen in the no-cleaning control, enriching pioneer non-mutans streptococci instead.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Effect of a multichannel oral irrigator on periodontal health and the oral microbiome
- **Authors**: Kim JM, Yoo SY, An JS, Woo JJ, Cho YD, Park HE (Seoul National University Dental Hospital)
- **Journal**: Scientific Reports 2023 Jul 25; 13(1):12120
- **DOI**: 10.1038/s41598-023-38894-0 | **PMID**: 37491575 | **PMCID**: PMC10368725
- **Study type**: Randomized two-group preliminary/pilot clinical trial (n=29 analyzed)
- **Device**: Multichannel oral irrigator (MCOI) — COMORAL, SMDsolutions Co., Seoul, Korea (Class I medical device; WATERET mouthpiece)

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First evaluation of a **multichannel** oral irrigator (dozens of simultaneous water jets through a mouthpiece, with synchronized suction to prevent aspiration) — distinct from conventional one-channel pulsating irrigators.
2. Couples clinical periodontal indices (PI, SBI, BOP, swelling) with **16S rRNA amplicon + shotgun metagenome** sequencing of saliva, linking the device to oral-microbiome ecology, not just plaque scores.
3. Uses a **3-day no-brushing challenge model** to expose whether the irrigator alone can blunt dysbiotic shifts that appear when mechanical cleaning is withheld.
4. Identifies an ecological signature: MCOI enriches **pioneer non-mutans streptococci** (oral commensals) and suppresses the Prevotella/Bacteroidetes rise and cariogenic/acidogenic metabolic pathways seen in the control.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Participants**: 30 healthy adults recruited, randomized to MCOI vs control; 29 analyzed (1 lost). Mean age 33.7 ± 8.2 y; 16 women, 13 men. IRB Seoul National University Dental Hospital.
- **Inclusion**: 19–85 y, no periodontal/medical disease, ≥16 teeth (excl. third molars), no laminates/crowns on evaluated teeth. **Exclusion**: recent antibiotics, heavy smokers, pregnancy, severe periodontitis (>4 mm PD), caries needing restoration, cognitive impairment.
- **Sample size note**: originally planned as RCT of 42, but the IRB recommended reducing the sample (no prior preliminary study) — hence a small preliminary trial.
- **Device protocol**: MCOI delivers 500 mL tap water at 3.71 bar over 1.5 min, 60 dense streams up/down in four directions, water injected at a **45° angle to the gingival margin**.
- **Design**: At baseline visit all 30 scaled by two hygienists. 2 weeks later (phase 1): PI, SBI, BOP, swelling measured + saliva collected. Then for **3 days**: MCOI group used the device twice daily (morning + before bed, 3 min, medium intensity) and did **not brush**; control group **did not brush** for 3 days, lightly gargling tap water before bed. Phase 2: measurements + saliva repeated.
- **Indices**: PI = modified Quigley-Hein Plaque Index; SBI = Mühlemann-Son Sulcus Bleeding Index; single examiner.
- **Sequencing**: 16S rRNA amplicon (V3–V4, MiSeq) on 29 participants; shotgun metagenome (NovaSeq) on selected samples; MetaPhlAn4/HUMAnN3. Statistics: Wilcoxon signed-rank, p=0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Clinical**
- Control group: average PI and SBI **significantly increased**. MCOI group: no significant change in PI or SBI.
- % BOP **significantly decreased** in the MCOI group; swelling also decreased (not significant).
- Supragingival calculus appeared after 3 days only in the control group (4 mandibular anterior teeth in 2 of 14 controls, 14.28%); none in MCOI.

**Microbiome (16S rRNA)**
- No significant alpha-diversity difference between groups.
- Control: phylum **Bacteroidetes significantly increased** (15.3 ± 6.98% → 20.4 ± 5.19%; p=0.031); MCOI maintained near-basal distribution.
- Control: **Prevotella spp. increased 114.28% (p=0.003)** (opportunistic periodontal pathogens, possible disease-progression signal); no significant Prevotella change in MCOI.
- Beta diversity differed between groups (PCoA ANOSIM p=0.023 and 0.006).
- LDA: **pioneer species (oral commensals) significantly enriched in MCOI (LDA score 3.06)**. Cariogenic mutans streptococci seldom detected in either group.

**Microbiome (shotgun metagenome)**
- MCOI group: increased metabolic pathways (amino-acid synthesis, energy metabolism, nucleotide biosynthesis) almost exclusively in gram-positive bacteria (non-mutans streptococci, Actinomyces).
- Fermentation/acid-production pathways enriched but **no high-acidogenicity species (e.g., mutans streptococci) detected**.
- Multiple biosynthetic pathways **reduced in gram-negative bacteria** in the MCOI group.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **3-day no-brushing model** is short (ethical constraint) — too brief to detect precise periodontal-state change; the no-brushing design is artificial, not a real-world adjunct comparison.
- **Small pilot** (n=29; IRB cut from a planned 42) limits power and generalizability.
- **Healthy adults only** (no periodontitis patients) — findings may not transfer to diseased populations.
- MCOI = a **specific commercial product (COMORAL)**; results are not necessarily generalizable to other irrigators.
- Plaque accumulated in the MCOI group too — the device did not effectively remove attached supragingival biofilm; effectiveness in decreasing plaque could not be determined. Benefit appears to be on the subgingival/gingival-margin biofilm and gingival inflammation.
- Authors propose longer studies and additional arms (toothbrushing + MCOI, toothbrushing only, one-channel irrigator) and note potential utility for disabled/elderly/dysphagia patients where the suction function reduces aspiration risk.

## 7. Glossary

- **MCOI (Multichannel Oral Irrigator)**: oral irrigator with dozens of simultaneous water jets through a mouthpiece plus synchronized suction to prevent aspiration (here, COMORAL).
- **PI (Plaque Index)**: modified Quigley-Hein plaque-scoring index.
- **SBI (Sulcus Bleeding Index)**: Mühlemann-Son index of gingival sulcus bleeding.
- **BOP (Bleeding on Probing)**: percentage of sites that bleed on periodontal probing; a gingival-inflammation marker.
- **16S rRNA amplicon sequencing**: targeted sequencing of a bacterial marker gene to profile microbial community composition.
- **Shotgun metagenome**: untargeted sequencing of all DNA in a sample, giving species + functional-pathway resolution.
- **Pioneer species / non-mutans streptococci**: early-colonizing oral commensal bacteria associated with a healthy biofilm ecology.
- **Prevotella spp.**: gram-negative anaerobes; opportunistic periodontal pathogens whose increase can signal dysbiosis.
- **LDA score**: linear discriminant analysis effect-size score (LEfSe) ranking taxa that differ between groups.
