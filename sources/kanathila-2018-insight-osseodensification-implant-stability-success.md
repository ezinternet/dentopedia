## One-line Summary
Narrative review (JCDR 2018) of the osseodensification (OD) concept — explaining how Huwais's tapered multi-fluted Densah burs run counterclockwise to compact rather than excavate bone, the mechanistic rationale (autografting of bone debris into walls), and reported insertion-torque gains (≈49 Ncm in low-density bone vs ≈25 Ncm for conventional drilling).

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Narrative review article

- **Primary Question**: What is osseodensification, how does it differ from conventional and underprepared/osteotome osteotomy, and what evidence supports its claim to improve primary/secondary stability?

## 2. Key Contributions
- Frames OD as a non-extraction osteotomy paradigm: special tapered, multi-fluted Densah burs run counterclockwise (densifying mode) to compact rather than excavate bone, producing bone-debris autografting into the osteotomy walls.
- Cites Trisi et al. sheep iliac-crest study: 30% increase in bone volume percentage and visible ridge-width expansion in OD vs conventional control sites; bone trabeculae thickened in the most coronal implant zone.
- Cites Lahens et al. in-vivo work: OD drilling enhanced insertion torque and improved 6-week histometric BIC, in both clockwise and counterclockwise bur rotation; conclusion was that the bur geometry (not just direction) drove the effect.
- Quotes the Huwais-cited torque comparison: OD ≈ 49 Ncm vs conventional ≈ 25 Ncm in low-density bone — the most-quoted single number in the OD marketing literature.
- Mechanistic explanation: bone compaction is achieved by controlled deformation through viscoelastic and plastic mechanisms; spring-back of elastic strain produces an osteotomy slightly smaller than the final bur diameter, contributing to primary stability.
- Briefly compares OD to two other low-density-bone strategies: underpreparation (10% undersized last drill — sufficient; further undersizing offers no additional gain) and Summers osteotome bone condensation.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
This is a narrative review with no formal search strategy, inclusion criteria, or quality appraisal. The authors describe the OD concept developed by Huwais (US Patent 2013), summarise the bur geometry (tapered profile, multiple flutes, ability to lift away for irrigation), explain the kinematics (counterclockwise rotation densifies; clockwise extracts), and review two supporting studies (Lahens et al., Trisi et al.) plus the inventor's own torque comparison.

The review draws heavily on the inventor's bench and animal work and on early adopters' case series. It does not include a meta-analysis or quantitative synthesis. Some claims — particularly the 49 Ncm vs 25 Ncm torque comparison — appear to be cited from Huwais's own studies without independent verification.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Torque (per Huwais, in low-density bone)**: OD ≈ 49 Ncm vs conventional ≈ 25 Ncm.
- **Trisi sheep iliac-crest study (cited)**: 30% increase in bone volume %; visible ridge-width gain; trabeculae thickened coronally.
- **Lahens et al. (cited)**: OD enhanced IT and 6-week BIC; direction-independent within OD bur geometry.
- **Underpreparation alternative**: 10% undersized last drill sufficient for primary-stability gain in low-density bone; further undersizing offers no additional benefit.
- ISQ/RFA, MBL, or implant survival numbers are not provided in the extracted text — this is a concept review, not a quantitative synthesis.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative review with no systematic search — selection bias likely.
- Heavy reliance on inventor-affiliated (Huwais) studies and a small number of corroborating preclinical reports.
- The cited 49 vs 25 Ncm torque comparison appears in many OD reviews without consistent methodological context (bone type, implant macrogeometry, drill sequence).
- Published 2018 — predates the larger SR+MA bodies of work (Gaikwad 2022, Gaspar 2022, Lima Monteiro 2024, Kalra 2025) that should now be preferred for evidence synthesis.
- No discussion of complications (over-compaction, fracture risk, learning curve) or comparative cost analysis.

## 7. Glossary
- **OD (Osseodensification)**: Non-extraction osteotomy method using tapered multi-fluted burs (Densah) rotating counterclockwise to compact and autograft bone debris along the osteotomy walls.
- **Densah bur**: Proprietary tapered multi-fluted bur (Versah LLC) designed for densification (counterclockwise) and extraction (clockwise) modes.
- **Insertion torque (IT, Ncm)**: Peak rotational resistance during implant placement; used clinically as a primary-stability surrogate.
- **BIC (Bone-to-Implant Contact)**: Histomorphometric % of the implant surface in direct contact with bone; gold-standard osseointegration metric.
- **Spring-back / elastic recovery**: Viscoelastic shrinking of bone walls after bur removal, producing a final osteotomy diameter smaller than the bur — contributing to mechanical interlock at implant insertion.
