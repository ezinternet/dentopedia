## Why Ingested

Extends our SDM coverage with a dentistry-specific narrative review that frames informed consent and patient decision aids (PDAs) as integral to the SDM process. It reinforces the general SDM model in [[behavioral-dentistry/communication-relationship/elwyn-2025-shared-decision-making-primer-clinicians]] and grounds it in concrete dental fields, complementing the broader communication-quality picture in [[behavioral-dentistry/communication-relationship/ho-2025-overview-dentist-patient-communication-quality]].

## One-line Summary

Concise narrative review (PubMed/MEDLINE) finding that SDM is applied in dentistry but with limited published evidence, that informed consent intertwines with the SDM process, and that patient decision aids (PDAs) need further development across dental fields.

## 한줄요약

치과 분야의 공유의사결정(Shared Decision-Making, SDM)을 다룬 간결한 서술적 종설로, SDM이 치과에 적용되고는 있으나 출판된 근거는 제한적이며, 충분한설명 후 동의(informed consent)가 SDM 과정과 긴밀히 얽혀 있고, 환자 의사결정 보조도구(Patient Decision Aids, PDA)가 더 많은 치과 영역에서 개발될 필요가 있음을 정리했다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Surveys the current state of SDM implementation specifically within dental practice (rather than general medicine).
- Positions **informed consent** as intertwined with the SDM process, beginning with provision of high-quality information to patients.
- Highlights **patient decision aids (PDAs)** as tools that facilitate SDM, noting some have been developed for specific dental fields.
- Identifies the evidence gap: only a limited number of published studies address SDM implementation in dentistry.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Narrative (non-systematic) review of pertinent literature retrieved from PubMed and MEDLINE to determine the current position of SDM application in dentistry and the influence of informed consent and PDAs on this process. No formal systematic search protocol, risk-of-bias appraisal, or meta-analysis is reported (abstract-only — full text not retrieved, so methodological detail beyond the abstract is unavailable).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- A limited number of published studies on SDM implementation in dental practice is available.
- The available studies show that SDM in dentistry is influenced by **many factors**.
- Informed consent **intertwines** with the SDM process and begins with providing high-quality information and employing PDAs.
- PDAs have been **developed for some dental fields** to facilitate SDM.
- Conclusion: SDM is applied in dentistry and is multifactorially influenced; informed consent is an important part of the process; PDAs in more dental fields need further development.
- (No quantitative benchmarks reported — abstract-only.)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Design limitation**: narrative review without systematic methodology — selection and synthesis are not reproducible.
- **Evidence base**: only a limited number of primary dental SDM studies exist, constraining strength of conclusions.
- **Future work**: PDAs need to be developed for more dental fields; the abstract does not enumerate the specific factors influencing SDM (full text not retrieved).

## 7. Glossary

- **SDM (Shared Decision-Making)**: collaborative process in which clinician and patient jointly make care decisions using best evidence and the patient's values/preferences.
- **Informed consent**: process of providing the patient sufficient high-quality information to agree to treatment; here framed as intertwined with SDM rather than a one-off signature.
- **PDA (Patient Decision Aid)**: tool (leaflet, chart, interactive aid) that presents options, benefits, and risks to support patient participation in decisions.
