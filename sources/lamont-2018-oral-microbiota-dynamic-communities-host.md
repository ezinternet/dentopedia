## Why Ingested
The authoritative Nature Reviews Microbiology synthesis that unifies the PSD model of periodontitis (from [[oral-microbiology/hajishengallis-2012-psd-model-periodontal-disease]] and [[oral-microbiology/lamont-2014-polymicrobial-synergy-dysbiosis-inflammatory]]) with the ecological plaque/caries dysbiosis framework, covering ~1,000 known oral species, feedforward loops, inter-kingdom interactions with C. albicans, and therapeutic opportunities.

## One-line Summary
Nature Reviews Microbiology narrative review by Lamont, Koo, and Hajishengallis synthesizing current mechanisms of oral polymicrobial synergy and dysbiosis in periodontitis and dental caries, including feedforward loops between the microbiota and host factors (inflammation/dietary sugars), inter-kingdom interactions, and therapeutic prospects.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Integrates periodontitis (PSD, keystone pathogens) and caries (ecological plaque hypothesis) dysbiosis into a unified review for the first time at Nature Reviews level.
- Describes ~1,000 oral species capable of colonization; any one site contains ~50 species.
- Establishes the concept that disease-associated microbiota is "specialist" while health-associated microbiota is "generalist."
- Details feedforward loops: in periodontitis, dysbiosis → inflammation → nutrients → more dysbiosis; in caries, dietary sugars → EPS + acid → aciduric selection → more caries.
- Documents inter-kingdom synergy: C. albicans with S. mutans enhances EPS production and severe caries in vivo; C. albicans with Mitis streptococci enhances mucosal invasion.
- Reviews oral microbiome–systemic disease links: coronary artery disease, preterm delivery/low-birthweight, rheumatoid arthritis (PPAD citrullination of host proteins by P. gingivalis).
- Notes oral microbiome–oral cancer association and mechanistic interference with host cell viability/proliferation/differentiation signaling.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative (non-systematic) review with broad scope; ~75 primary references synthesized.
- Covers supragingival (tooth surface, caries) and subgingival (periodontitis) communities, mucosal epithelial surfaces, and systemic associations.
- Oral ecology framework: ~1,000 species capable of oral colonization; ~50 per site subset; primary colonizers are predominantly facultative anaerobes (Streptococcus, Actinomyces); subgingival anaerobes increase with reduced O2 (Bacteroidaceae, spirochaetes).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Oral microbiota: ~1,000 species capable of oral colonization; ~50 present per site; any individual may harbor ~200.
- Caries feedforward: sucrose → EPS (glucan/fructan) + organic acid (lactic acid) → aciduric selection (mutans streptococci, lactobacilli, bifidobacteria, Scardovia, Actinomyces) → demineralization → more sugar exposure.
- S. gordonii + P. gingivalis: more alveolar bone loss than P. gingivalis alone; S. gordonii = accessory pathogen.
- S. cristatus antagonizes P. gingivalis: negatively correlated in subgingival samples; reduces pathogenicity in animal models.
- C. albicans + S. mutans: inter-kingdom synergy elevates EPS production and increases caries severity in vivo.
- P. gingivalis PPAD (peptidylarginine deiminase): citrullinates host proteins uniquely → may trigger autoantibodies in rheumatoid arthritis.
- Oral cancer: oral microbiome influences oral squamous cell carcinoma (OSCC) by altering inflammatory microenvironment and host signaling (proliferation/differentiation/viability).
- Streptococci on epithelial surfaces: restrain pro-inflammatory responses, stimulate β-defensin production → homeostatic commensals.
- P. gingivalis activates Notch1 → PLA2-IIA production in epithelial cells → promotes dysbiosis.
- Gingivitis: almost inevitable with prolonged biofilm accumulation, but a controlled immune-inflammatory state that does not permanently compromise tissue integrity.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative scope limits depth on any single mechanism; no systematic literature search.
- Human mechanistic evidence for most dysbiosis models remains limited; causality inferred from animal models.
- Inter-individual microbiome variation in periodontitis is large — no discrete pathogenic cohort; functional gene sets better discriminators than species lists.
- Authors call for therapeutic strategies targeting interspecies signaling (e.g., Mfa1–SspA/B), complement (C5aR antagonists), and EPS inhibition.

## 7. Glossary
- PPAD: peptidylarginine deiminase — P. gingivalis enzyme citrullinating host proteins; implicated in RA.
- EPS: extracellular polymeric substances (glucans, fructans, eDNA) forming biofilm matrix.
- Nososymbiocity: combined pathogenicity of a microbial community (exceeds sum of parts).
- PLA2-IIA (PLA2G2A): antimicrobial phospholipase A2; induced by P. gingivalis Notch1 signaling.
- Ecological plaque hypothesis: caries results from ecological shifts under sustained sugar challenge, not introduction of novel pathogens.
- OSCC: oral squamous cell carcinoma.
- Feedforward loop: self-amplifying cycle in which dysbiosis promotes inflammation which provides nutrients sustaining further dysbiosis.
