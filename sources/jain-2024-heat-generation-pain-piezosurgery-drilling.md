## One-line Summary
Systematic review (Cureus 2024, 9 studies from 2,279 hits): piezosurgery significantly reduces postoperative pain but produces higher intraoperative bone temperatures during osteotomy than conventional rotary drilling — a clear pain-vs-heat trade-off.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Cureus, October 2024
- Affiliation: Manav Rachna Dental College, Faridabad (same group as Saxena 2024)
- Type: Systematic review (no meta-analysis)
- Search: PubMed via MEDLINE, EBSCO, Google Scholar
- 2,279 → 9 included

## 2. Key Contributions
- Establishes the piezo trade-off: lower post-op pain at the cost of higher intraoperative heat.
- Synthesizes both VAS pain and thermal outcomes in one review.
- Uses robust risk-of-bias tools (RoB 2.0, ROBINS-I, QUIN).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- PubMed/MEDLINE, EBSCO, Google Scholar.
- 2,279 → screened → 9 included.
- Risk of bias: RoB 2.0 (RCTs), ROBINS-I (non-randomized), QUIN (in vitro).
- Outcomes: intraoperative temperature; postoperative pain (typically VAS).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Significant reduction in postoperative pain with piezosurgery vs conventional drilling.
- Significantly higher intraoperative bone temperature during osteotomy with piezosurgery.
- Trade-off explicitly stated as the central clinical finding.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small final included sample (n=9).
- Heterogeneous protocols (insert design, load, irrigation, drilling speed).
- Trade-off mechanism not fully resolved.
- Future: standardized piezo protocols with manufacturer-recommended load and rotation; long-term outcomes.

## 6. Related Work
- Aquilanti 2023 — direct in vitro piezo vs rotary thermal comparison.
- Stubinger 2015 — clinical overview of piezosurgery in implant dentistry.
- Saxena 2024 — guided drilling heat SR (same group).

## 7. Glossary
- **Piezosurgery**: ultrasonic-driven bone-cutting technique using piezoelectric inserts.
- **VAS**: visual analog scale, 0–100 pain rating.
- **RoB 2.0 / ROBINS-I / QUIN**: bias appraisal tools for RCTs, non-randomized interventions, and in vitro studies.
