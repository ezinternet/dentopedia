## 2. Key Contributions
- Resin bonding mandatory for adhesion-dependent restorations (veneers, RBFDPs, thin ceramics)
- Conventional cementation acceptable for high-strength crowns with adequate retention
- Al₂O₃ air-particle abrasion provides durable bond to zirconia
- Silica/silane coating (Rocatec, CoJet) recommended for zirconia surface activation
- Single-retainer cantilever RBFDP: 5yr 92.3%, 10yr 94.4% survival reported
- Scarcity of long-term data on monolithic full-contour zirconia (FCZ)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Critical review in JDR discussing clinical evidence on resin bonding for long-term success of high-strength ceramic restorations (alumina and zirconia). Covers: cementation vs resin bonding protocols, surface pretreatment (sandblasting, silane, Rocatec/CoJet), clinical recommendations for different restoration types (crowns, FDPs, cantilever RBFDPs). Reviews clinical data on zirconia-based 3-unit FDPs showing 100% survival at 5 years when properly designed. Discusses differences between first- and second-generation zirconia. Notes scarcity of long-term clinical data on monolithic FCZ crowns.
