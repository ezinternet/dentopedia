## Why Ingested

구강 미생물 군집에서 공생세균의 역할을 다룬 기초 리뷰로, 기존 [[oral-microbiology/romund-2020-oral-microbiome-dysbiosis-keystone-pathogens]] 등 병원성 중심 문헌과 대비하여 건강-연관 공생세균(S. sanguinis)의 방어 기전을 체계적으로 정리한다. 우식 및 치주염 병인론 문헌에 공생세균 시각을 보완한다.

## One-line Summary

Narrative review summarizing *Streptococcus sanguinis* biofilm formation mechanisms and its antagonistic interactions with cariogenic (*S. mutans*) and periodontopathic (*P. gingivalis*, *A. actinomycetemcomitans*) species, while noting its dual role as an oral health commensal and infective endocarditis pathogen.

## 한줄요약

*Streptococcus sanguinis*의 생물막 형성 기전(필리, SrpA, GtfP, eDNA)과 우식원균(*S. mutans*) 및 치주병원균(*P. gingivalis* 등)에 대한 길항 상호작용을 정리하고, 구강 건강 공생세균이면서 동시에 감염성 심내막염 원인균이라는 이중 역할을 설명한 서술 리뷰.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Future Microbiology, vol. 13, issue 8, pp. 915–932
- **Publication date**: 2018-06-08 (e-pub)
- **Study type**: Narrative review
- **Topic**: *Streptococcus sanguinis* — biofilm formation factors and interspecies interactions in oral health and disease

## 2. Key Contributions

- Establishes *S. sanguinis* as a health-associated pioneer colonizer negatively correlated with dental caries and periodontal disease
- Comprehensively reviews biofilm formation genes: fimbriae (PilA/B/C), adhesins (SsaB, SrpA), glucosyltransferase (GtfP), eDNA via SpxB-generated H₂O₂
- Details competitive antagonism against *S. mutans*: H₂O₂ production by SpxB inhibits *S. mutans* growth; *S. mutans* counters with mutacins and CSP inactivation
- Documents arginine deiminase system (ADS) in *S. sanguinis* as pH buffer against cariogenic acid production
- Explains L-arginine as ecological caries intervention: L-arginine selectively reduces *S. mutans* biomass while enriching *S. sanguinis*
- Reviews ambiguous evidence re: periodontitis — *S. sanguinis* is health-associated by 16S rRNA but can serve as a binding scaffold for *P. gingivalis* (via fimbriae to GAPDH) and *F. nucleatum* (via RadD/Aid1 adhesins)
- Concludes that biofilm formation ability does NOT correlate with infective endocarditis virulence in animal models — sucrose-dependent glucan production is absent in blood

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Review of literature on *S. sanguinis* SK36 (sequenced reference strain) and clinical isolates
- Covers: biofilm attachment genes, maturation matrix (glucans, eDNA), interspecies competition assays (co-culture, dual-species biofilm), 16S rRNA epidemiology, endocarditis animal models
- Key experimental models reviewed: crystal violet biofilm assay, saliva-coated hydroxyapatite (sHA) adhesion assays, rabbit endocarditis model, FISH spatial mapping of dental plaque

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **H₂O₂ antagonism**: SpxB-derived H₂O₂ is the primary weapon against *S. mutans*; other genes (VicRK, spxR, etc.) modulate SpxB expression
- **L-arginine ecology**: 15 mg/ml L-arginine (clinically effective) decreased *S. mutans* proportion, increased *S. sanguinis* proportion; fluoride + arginine combination further suppressed *S. mutans*
- **P. gingivalis interaction**: *P. gingivalis* fimbriae bind *S. sanguinis* surface GAPDH; *S. sanguinis* suppresses *P. gingivalis* mfa1 gene (short fimbriae subunit)
- **F. nucleatum**: RadD adhesin of *F. nucleatum* mediates coaggregation with *S. sanguinis*; *S. sanguinis* H₂O₂ kills *F. nucleatum* but effect attenuated when *F. nucleatum* pre-forms coaggregates
- **A. actinomycetemcomitans**: *S. sanguinis* reduces soft-tissue colonization of *A. actinomycetemcomitans* under flow conditions
- **Endocarditis**: 8 biofilm-defective mutants screened; 4 showed reduced virulence, 4 retained virulence — no consistent correlation; sucrose absence in blood precludes glucan-dependent biofilm on heart valves

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Most mechanistic studies are *in vitro*; no adequate in vivo model of polymicrobial oral biofilm for these interactions
- Causal direction unclear: *S. sanguinis* may be a health indicator (survives at healthy sites) rather than a health promoter
- Intraspecies phenotypic variation in *S. sanguinis* not well characterized
- Mechanisms linking exogenous arginine to GtfP expression remain unknown
- Role of *S. sanguinis* in peri-implant disease unresolved

## 7. Glossary

- **SpxB**: Pyruvate oxidase producing H₂O₂ in *S. sanguinis*; key antagonist against *S. mutans*
- **GtfP**: Glucosyltransferase producing glucan matrix for biofilm maturation
- **SrpA**: Serine-rich repeat glycoprotein A; binds human sialoglycans and platelets
- **eDNA**: Extracellular DNA released via autolysis induced by H₂O₂; biofilm scaffold
- **ADS**: Arginine deiminase system; metabolizes arginine to maintain pH homeostasis
- **RadD / Aid1**: *F. nucleatum* adhesins mediating coaggregation with *S. sanguinis*; arginine-inhibitable
- **CSP**: Competence-stimulating peptide of *S. mutans*; induces mutacin production; inactivated by *S. sanguinis*
- **Mfa1**: Short fimbriae adhesin of *P. gingivalis*; expression suppressed by *S. sanguinis*
- **PilA/B/C**: Pilus proteins in *S. sanguinis*; mediate attachment to saliva-coated surfaces
- **Vegetation**: Platelet-fibrin nodule in infective endocarditis; resembles but is not a true biofilm
