## One-line Summary
2021 AHA scientific statement updating 2007 IE prophylaxis guidelines: no change — recommend prophylaxis only for 4 highest-risk cardiac categories; no evidence that relaxing 2007 guidelines increased VGS-IE frequency or mortality.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Circulation 143:e963–e978 (May 18, 2021)
- DOI: 10.1161/CIR.0000000000000969
- Design: AHA Scientific Statement (systematic evidence review + expert consensus)

## 2. Key Conclusions
- 2007 guidelines: **upheld unchanged** — still recommend AP only for 4 highest-risk categories
- No convincing evidence that VGS-IE frequency, morbidity, or mortality increased since 2007
- Good oral health + regular dental care emphasized as more important than prophylaxis for low/moderate-risk patients

## 3. 4 Highest-Risk Cardiac Conditions (AP Required)
1. Prosthetic cardiac valve (including transcatheter-implanted)
2. Prior history of infective endocarditis
3. Congenital heart disease:
   - Unrepaired cyanotic CHD
   - Repaired CHD with residual defects at site of prosthetic material
   - CHD repaired with prosthetic material within first 6 months post-repair
4. Cardiac transplant recipients with cardiac valvulopathy

## 4. Conditions No Longer Requiring Prophylaxis (vs pre-2007)
- Mitral valve prolapse
- Rheumatic heart disease
- Bicuspid aortic valve
- Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy
- Most congenital defects (unless meeting above criteria)

## 5. Antibiotic Protocol
| Situation | Drug | Dose | Timing |
|---|---|---|---|
| Standard | Amoxicillin | 2g PO (adults) | 30–60 min before |
| Penicillin allergic | Clindamycin 600mg OR cephalexin 2g | PO | 30–60 min before |
| Cannot take oral | Ampicillin 2g or cefazolin 1g | IM/IV | 30 min before |

## 6. Limitations
- Randomized controlled trials confirming/refuting efficacy not available (ethical reasons)
- Observational data used; causal inference limited
