## Why Ingested

Provides concrete 5-year survival rates disaggregated by tooth vitality, post type, AND crown material — exactly the cross-variable data needed for clinical decision-making in post-and-core planning. Directly extends [[wiki/post-and-core/overview-post-and-core-decision-framework]] by quantifying the material × post-type interaction on survival outcomes.

## One-line Summary

SR (26 studies, ≥24 mo): fiber-post + all-ceramic SCs achieve 95.0% 5-year survival on nonvital teeth vs 85.7% without post; tooth vitality significantly predicts FDP survival (84.9% vital vs 81.3% nonvital, P=.049).

## 한줄요약

체계적 고찰 (26편): 비활력치에 파이버포스트 + 올세라믹 단관은 5년 생존율 95.0%로 최우수; 포스트 없음은 85.7%; 지대치 활력 유무가 FDP 예후에 통계적으로 유의한 영향(P=.049).

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of Prosthodontics, 33(2):110-122
- **Publication date**: 2023-08-11
- **Study type**: Systematic review
- **Inclusion criteria**: 26 studies with ≥24 months observation period
- **Scope**: Full-coverage tooth-supported fixed prosthetic restorations (single crowns and fixed dental prostheses)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Disaggregates survival by three simultaneous variables: tooth vitality, post type, and prosthetic material
- First SR to directly compare all-ceramic vs metal-ceramic SCs in the context of post-and-core planning
- Demonstrates that crown material selection interacts with post type on survival outcome (fiber post + all-ceramic > fiber post + metal-ceramic)
- Quantifies the survival penalty of no-post restoration on nonvital teeth (85.7% vs 95.0%)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Systematic review design; 26 studies fulfilled inclusion criteria (≥24 months follow-up)
- Outcomes: 5-year survival rates for SCs and FDPs stratified by tooth vitality and post type
- abstract-only — full text not retrieved

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Single Crowns (SCs) — 5-year survival:**

| Restoration type | Survival |
|---|---|
| All-ceramic SC on vital teeth | 98.3% |
| All-ceramic SC on nonvital + fiber post | 95.0% |
| Metal-ceramic SC on vital teeth | 97.1% |
| Metal-ceramic SC + cast metal post | 90.7% |
| Metal-ceramic SC + fiber post | 91.3% |
| Metal-ceramic SC without post | 85.7% |

- All-ceramic SCs with fiber post (95.0%) showed **significantly higher survival** than metal-ceramic SCs with fiber post (91.3%)
- Vital teeth consistently outperform nonvital counterparts across all material types

**Fixed Dental Prostheses (FDPs) — 5-year survival:**
- Vital abutments: **84.9%**
- Nonvital abutments: **81.3%**
- Difference statistically significant (P=.049)

**Overall conclusion**: Tooth vitality contributes positively to survival of both SCs and FDPs.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only; detailed heterogeneity analysis, risk-of-bias data, and subgroup details not retrievable
- 26 studies; moderate evidence base for some subgroups (e.g., no-post nonvital)
- Follow-up period variation across included studies

## 7. Glossary

- **SC**: Single crown (단관)
- **FDP**: Fixed dental prosthesis / fixed partial denture (고정성 국소의치)
- **Fiber post**: Glass/quartz fiber reinforced resin post (파이버포스트)
- **Cast metal post**: Prefabricated or cast metal post-and-core (금속포스트)
- **Nonvital tooth**: Endodontically treated tooth (비활력치, 근관치료치)
