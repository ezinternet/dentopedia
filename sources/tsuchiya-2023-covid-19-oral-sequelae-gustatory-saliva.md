## Why Ingested

미각상실(taste loss/dysgeusia)과 침분비저하(hyposalivation/xerostomia)를 동시에 다루는 논문 요청에 대응. 기존 [[oral-medicine/poudel-2026-xerostomia-dental-treatment-outcomes-sr]]는 xerostomia가 치과치료 결과(보철·임플란트)에 미치는 영향을 다루지만, 두 증상이 **함께 발생하는 기전**(공통 병태생리)을 다룬 페이지는 없었다. 본 narrative review(Tsuchiya 2023)는 미각장애와 타액분비장애가 ACE2/TRPV1 수용체 발현 및 아연결핍이라는 공통 기전으로 동시 발생·지속됨을 제시해 공백을 메운다.

## One-line Summary

Narrative review (bibliographic synthesis of ~90+ studies, >65,000 COVID-19 patients/survivors combined) finding gustatory dysfunction (ageusia/dysgeusia) persists in 1–45% and saliva secretory dysfunction (xerostomia/hyposalivation) in 2–40% of COVID-19 survivors at 3-week–12-month follow-up, with the two symptoms correlated and pathogenically linked via ACE2/TRPV1 receptor expression in taste buds/salivary glands and SARS-CoV-2-induced zinc deficiency.

## 한줄요약

내러티브 리뷰(약 90편 이상의 연구, 코로나19 환자·완치자 총 6만5천여 명 종합) — 미각장애(ageusia/dysgeusia)는 완치 후 3주~12개월 추적에서 1~45%, 타액분비장애(xerostomia/hyposalivation)는 2~40%에서 지속되며, 두 증상은 서로 상관관계가 있고 타액선·미뢰의 ACE2/TRPV1 수용체 발현과 SARS-CoV-2 감염 유발 아연결핍이라는 공통 기전으로 설명됨.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First focused synthesis specifically on **persistent** (sequelae, not acute-phase) gustatory and saliva secretory dysfunction after COVID-19 recovery, updating an earlier acute-phase review by the same group.
- Quantifies prevalence ranges across follow-up windows (21–365 days for taste; 28–230 days for saliva) and shows sequelae can persist up to 1 year.
- Demonstrates geographic/ethnic gradient for gustatory sequelae (East Asia lowest, Middle East/Europe highest) but no clear gradient for saliva secretory sequelae.
- Proposes three converging pathogenic mechanisms (ACE2/Furin/TMPRSS2 cellular entry, TRPV1 interaction, zinc deficiency) common to both taste and saliva dysfunction, explaining their frequent co-occurrence.
- Extends mechanistic reasoning to variant-specific differences (Omicron's weaker ACE2 affinity correlating with lower reported ageusia/xerostomia rates).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: narrative/bibliographic literature review (not systematic; no meta-analysis performed due to study heterogeneity).
- **Search sources**: PubMed/Medline, LitCovid, ProQuest (cutoff September 30, 2022); preprint servers medRxiv/bioRxiv also searched.
- **Search terms**: SARS-CoV-2, COVID-19, sequelae, persistent, ageusia, dysgeusia, gustatory dysfunction, xerostomia, dry mouth, salivary secretion dysfunction (and combinations).
- **Exclusion criteria**: non-English articles; studies without RT-PCR/serological confirmation of SARS-CoV-2 infection; studies including primary Sjögren's syndrome patients (confound from independent xerostomia/dysgeusia etiology).
- **Scope**: 64 studies (23,476 survivors) for persistent gustatory sequelae; 86 studies (42,759 patients) for acute gustatory dysfunction; 7 studies (654 survivors) for persistent saliva secretory sequelae; 13 studies (2,283 patients) for acute saliva secretory dysfunction.
- Risk-of-bias judged qualitatively (referencing prior meta-analyses/SRs) as low-to-moderate overall; no formal GRADE or PRISMA framework applied (consistent with narrative-review design).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Gustatory sequelae prevalence**: 1–45% at 21–365-day follow-up; pooled ~13.6–13.8% at ~1–2 month follow-up, declining slightly but persisting to ~12.7% at ~6 months (not statistically different across follow-up windows) — i.e., once present, sequelae do not resolve quickly.
- **Geographic gradient (mean ± SD prevalence)**: Europe 16.2 ± 11.0%, North America 13.8 ± 8.8%, South America 11.5 ± 5.0%, East Asia 3.8 ± 2.0% (lowest, p<0.005–0.0005 vs all other regions), South Asia 10.0 ± 1.4%, Middle East 20.6 ± 7.5% (highest).
- **Gender**: mixed evidence — several cohorts (Saudi, Swiss, German) show female predominance in persistent ageusia (e.g., 69.5% female vs 30.5% male in one Saudi cohort, p=0.0001); others (South Korean n=900, Indian, Turkish) show no significant association.
- **Age**: mixed — some cohorts show higher persistence in older survivors (South Korean: age >50y, p=0.024) or specific middle-age brackets (Italian 40–54y, p=0.029); Indian cohort found no age association.
- **Disease severity**: largely independent of gustatory/saliva sequelae persistence in most cohorts examined.
- **Saliva secretory sequelae prevalence**: 2–40% at 28–230-day follow-up; lower than acute-phase prevalence (mean 18.5% vs 42.5%, p<0.001) but persists over months (16.2 ± 0.84% at 1–2 months; 20.5 ± 6.8% at >2–4 months).
- **Co-occurrence**: xerostomia and ageusia/dysgeusia prevalence moderately correlated across 20 studies spanning Italy, Colombia, China, India, Israel, Egypt, Turkey, Saudi Arabia — supporting a shared pathogenic mechanism.
- **Mechanistic evidence**: ACE2/Furin/TMPRSS2 co-expressed in taste buds and all major/minor salivary gland types (immunohistochemistry, postmortem biopsy data cited); TRPV1 co-expressed in tongue epithelium and salivary gland acinar/ductal cells; serum zinc significantly lower in COVID-19 patients (64.8 ± 7.5 μg/dL) vs healthy controls (88.3 ± 14.0 μg/dL); Omicron's weaker ACE2-RBD binding affinity correlates with its lower reported rates of ageusia/xerostomia versus earlier variants.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative (not systematic) review design — no formal quality appraisal tool (e.g., AMSTAR, GRADE), no meta-analysis, heterogeneous study designs and follow-up definitions pooled qualitatively.
- Author-acknowledged heterogeneity in study populations, follow-up duration, and outcome measurement tools (subjective questionnaires vs objective salivary flow/taste testing) across cited studies.
- Excludes primary Sjögren's syndrome patients but does not fully separate other confounding causes of xerostomia/dysgeusia (medications, comorbidities) in the underlying cited studies.
- Author notes zinc-deficiency and receptor-based mechanisms are speculative/correlational, not causally proven in the cited human data.
- Future work: standardized objective measurement (sialometry, validated taste-strip testing) at defined follow-up intervals; RCTs of zinc supplementation for persistent oral sequelae; studies stratifying by variant to confirm the Omicron-severity correlation.

## 7. Glossary

- **Ageusia**: complete loss of taste perception.
- **Dysgeusia**: qualitatively distorted/impaired taste perception.
- **Hypogeusia (amblygeustia)**: reduced (but not absent) taste sensitivity; graded severe/moderate/mild.
- **Parageusia**: wrong or inadequate taste sensation elicited by an actual taste stimulus.
- **Phantogeusia**: taste sensation occurring in the absence of any stimulus.
- **Xerostomia**: subjective complaint of oral/mouth dryness.
- **Hyposalivation**: objectively measured reduction in salivary flow rate.
- **ACE2**: angiotensin-converting enzyme 2, the primary host-cell receptor for SARS-CoV-2 spike protein.
- **TMPRSS2**: transmembrane serine protease 2; cleaves spike protein S2' site enabling membrane fusion/viral entry.
- **Furin**: host protease cleaving the spike protein S1/S2 site.
- **TRPV1**: transient receptor potential vanilloid 1 channel; implicated in viral cellular interaction and oral sensory/secretory function.
- **RBD**: receptor-binding domain of the SARS-CoV-2 spike protein S1 subunit.
