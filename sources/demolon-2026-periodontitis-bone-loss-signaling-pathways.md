## One-line Summary

Comprehensive 2026 narrative review of molecular signaling pathways (NF-κB, JAK/STAT, MAPK, PI3K/Akt) driving osteoclastogenesis and alveolar bone loss in periodontitis, with critical evaluation of emerging therapeutic strategies.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Maps 4 major intracellular signaling pathways (NF-κB, JAK/STAT, MAPK, PI3K/Akt) and their crosstalk in periodontal osteoclastogenesis; provides molecular framework for therapeutic targeting
- Delineates osteoclast transcription factors NFATc1 (master regulator), c-Fos, and c-Src as the terminal effectors downstream of RANKL/RANK signaling in periodontal bone destruction
- Introduces "trained osteoclastogenesis" — maladaptive innate immune memory (MITF-driven monocyte training) that amplifies bone resorption upon re-exposure to dysbiotic biofilm
- Critically evaluates therapeutic strategies: RANKL inhibitors (denosumab), JAK inhibitors (tofacitinib class), NF-κB inhibitors, natural compounds (curcumin, resveratrol, flavonoids), and site-specific drug delivery platforms
- Identifies absence of reliable clinical biomarkers for active vs. quiescent disease as the key barrier to precision-targeted biologic therapy

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Comprehensive narrative review
- **Scope**: Molecular and cellular mechanisms of periodontal bone loss + therapeutic strategies; ~700 million affected worldwide (~11% global population)
- **Pathways**: TLR/NF-κB, RANK/RANKL/OPG, JAK/STAT (IL-6/IL-17A/STAT3), MAPK (p38/JNK/ERK), PI3K/Akt/mTOR, Wnt/β-catenin (bone formation side), S1P (osteoclast migration), SPM (resolution failure)
- **Therapeutics reviewed**: Denosumab, OPG-Fc, anti-TNF-α, anti-IL-1β, JAK inhibitors, p38/JNK inhibitors, Bortezomib, natural compounds, injectable hydrogels/microspheres/mucoadhesive gels

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- RANKL/RANK/OPG axis is the **central axis**: OPG is the decoy receptor blocking osteoclastogenesis; dysbiotic biofilm tips RANKL/OPG ratio toward bone resorption
- **NFATc1** is the master transcription factor of osteoclastogenesis; activated by both NF-κB and MAPK pathways; required for expression of osteoclast-specific genes (TRAP, cathepsin K, MMP-9)
- **NF-κB**: Canonical (IKKβ/p65:p50) mediates acute inflammatory osteoclastogenesis; non-canonical (IKKα/RelB:p52) sustains chronic bone loss
- **JAK/STAT**: IL-6 → STAT3 accelerates NFATc1 expression; IL-17A → STAT3 upregulates RANKL in fibroblasts/osteoblasts
- **MAPK**: p38 promotes osteoclast survival and NFATc1 transcription; JNK modulates AP-1/c-Fos; ERK regulates osteoclast precursor proliferation
- **PI3K/Akt**: Downstream mTOR activates osteoclastogenesis; Akt-silencing siRNA blocks RANKL-induced osteoclast formation in vitro
- **Biomarkers for precision therapy**: Elevated GCF MMP-8, IL-1β — could guide timing of biologic intervention (not yet clinically validated)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Animal models (ligature/LPS) induce **acute synchronized** bone loss ≠ human periodontitis (chronic, episodic burst-quiescence pattern) — translation gap is fundamental
- No validated clinical biomarkers to distinguish active vs. quiescent disease phases
- Natural compounds: poor bioavailability, short study duration, small sample size; questionable stand-alone clinical impact without conventional therapy
- Biologics (anti-TNF-α, denosumab): MRONJ risk, immunosuppression, high cost; not suitable for long-term periodontal use without better risk stratification
- MAPK/JAK inhibitors: systemic off-target toxicity; local delivery essential for clinical translation

## 7. Glossary

- **NFATc1**: nuclear factor of activated T cells, cytoplasmic 1 — 파골세포 분화 마스터 전사인자
- **RANKL/RANK/OPG**: receptor activator of NF-κB ligand / receptor / osteoprotegerin — 파골세포 분화 3요소
- **NF-κB**: nuclear factor-κB — 핵심 전염증 전사인자; 치주염 만성화 핵심
- **JAK/STAT**: Janus kinase / signal transducer and activator of transcription — IL-6·IL-17A 신호 경로
- **MAPK**: mitogen-activated protein kinase — p38/JNK/ERK 세 갈래
- **PI3K/Akt**: phosphoinositide 3-kinase / Akt (protein kinase B) — 세포 생존·파골세포 활성
- **Trained osteoclastogenesis**: 훈련된 파골세포생성 — 단핵구가 선천면역기억으로 파골세포 분화 증폭
- **SPM**: specialized pro-resolving mediators — 리폭신·레졸빈·마레신; 염증 해소 실패 시 만성화
- **MMP-8**: matrix metalloproteinase-8 — 치은열구액 내 활성 바이오마커 후보
- **Denosumab**: 항-RANKL 단일클론항체; MRONJ 위험으로 치과 장기 사용 제한
