## Why Ingested

Provides the adult-oral-health evidence for motivational interviewing (periodontal outcomes), feeding the MI-efficacy rung of [[overviews/behavioral-dentistry-decision-ladder]] and the MI branch of [[overviews/behavioral-dentistry-overview]].

## One-line Summary
SR+MA of MI in adults — only 7 studies, all on periodontal outcomes; MI marginally favored: bleeding-on-probing (BoP) reduced by 6.41pp (significant), plaque index −3.59pp (not significant), gingival index −0.70 (not significant). Evidence base too thin to conclude effectiveness for "oral morbidities" broadly.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Community Dentistry and Oral Epidemiology, 52(1):84–92, 2024 (first published Sep 2023)
- DOI: 10.1111/cdoe.12904
- Study type: Systematic review + meta-analysis (sr+ma) of RCTs/cluster-RCTs
- Search domains: MEDLINE, Scopus, Web of Science, LILACS
- Code/data: meta-analysis code at https://github.com/tvarnetperez/Motiv-Interview-MA

## 2. Key Contributions
- First SR+MA of MI for **adult** oral health (parallel to Jahanshahi 2022 for pediatric/ECC)
- Documents that the entire MI-in-adults evidence base is **periodontal**, not caries/other
- Quantifies effect by clinical outcome (BoP, PI, GI)
- Open code repository for reanalysis

## 3. Methodology
- Inclusion: RCTs, cluster-RCTs, community-based randomized trials
- Population: adults 18–60 years
- Intervention: MI or MI-principles-based counseling (per Miller & Rollnick)
- Comparators: any oral health education or negative controls
- Outcomes: any oral morbidity
- 602 studies identified → 7 included in synthesis
- Random-effects model
- Subgroups: moderate-to-severe periodontitis patients (n=272), pregnant women (n=112), patients with mental disorders/alcohol problems (n=60)
- Follow-up: 1 month to 1 year

## 4. Key Results
- Total participants synthesized: ~444 across periodontal outcome analyses
- **Plaque index**: 4 studies (n=267), summary effect −3.59 percentage points (95% CI: −11.44, +4.25) — **not significant**
- **Bleeding on probing (BoP)**: 2 studies (n=177), summary effect −6.41 percentage points (95% CI: −12.18, −0.65) — **significant**
- **Gingival index**: 2 studies (n=166), summary effect −0.70 (95% CI: −1.87, +0.48) — **not significant**
- Patient mean age: 43.7 years
- All summary effects directionally favor MI

## 5. Limitations
- Only 7 studies — small evidence base
- All outcomes are periodontal (no caries, no implant maintenance, no other oral morbidities found)
- Heterogeneity among included studies
- Non-disclosure of some study data
- Follow-up ≤1 year — long-term effects unknown
- Only BoP reaches significance; PI and GI cross the null
- Selection bias risk: studies on niche populations (pregnant, mental disorders/alcohol)

## 6. Related Work
- Jahanshahi 2022 — MI for ECC (pediatric counterpart)
- Lundahl 2013 — MI in medical care settings (foundation)
- Miller WR, Rollnick S (2008) Motivational Interviewing in Healthcare
- Periodontal SR literature (separate category)

## 7. Glossary
- **MI (Motivational Interviewing)**: counseling method developed by Miller & Rollnick to elicit behavior change
- **BoP (Bleeding on Probing)**: periodontal inflammation indicator
- **PI (Plaque Index)**: oral hygiene measure
- **GI (Gingival Index)**: gingival inflammation grading
- **Percentage points (pp)**: absolute difference in a percentage outcome
- **Random-effects model**: meta-analytic model allowing true effect to vary across studies
