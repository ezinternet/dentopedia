## Why Ingested

Anterior maxillary implant placement requires thorough knowledge of 비비구개관 (Nasopalatine Canal, NPC) dimensions and morphological variants to avoid nerve/vascular injury. This cross-sectional CBCT study (n=102) provides sex- and age-stratified morphometric data that supplements [[wiki/radiology/yadav-2024-nasopalatine-canal-morphology-cbct]] and informs safe implant planning in the esthetic zone.

## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional CBCT study (n=102, Indian population) characterizing nasopalatine canal length, diameter, shape, orientation, and opening count across age groups and sexes, with statistically significant sex and age differences in several dimensions.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of Periodontal and Implant Science, 2025 Feb;55(1):62-71
- **Published online**: July 12, 2024
- **Accepted**: June 18, 2024
- **Study type**: Retrospective cross-sectional CBCT morphometric study
- **Population**: Southern Indian patients, Manipal College of Dental Sciences, Mangalore
- **Sample**: 102 CBCT scans (54 female, 48 male) from 150 reviewed; 48 excluded (poor quality, pathology, missing teeth, prior implant/bone graft)
- **Ethics**: MAHE/MCODSMLR/198/2015
- **CBCT unit**: Planmeca; FOV 8×8 cm, 0.16 mm resolution, 80–84 kVp, 10–12 mA; Romexis 4.6.2 software
- **Slice thickness**: 0.5 mm

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Sex differences: NPC significantly longer in males (mean 10.19 vs 9.16 mm; P=0.012); anteroposterior (AP) diameter at palatal opening significantly greater in males (3.53 vs 2.92 mm; P<0.001); NPC angle significantly greater in females.
2. Age differences: Mediolateral (ML) diameter at palatal opening significantly larger in older adults (≥61 yr vs ≤20 yr; P=0.027); canal length longest in ≤20 yr and 51–60 yr groups (P=0.002).
3. Shape distribution (sagittal): Cylindrical most common (25.5%), spindle least common (2.9%); no significant age/sex difference in overall shape distribution, but cylindrical predominant in females and funnel in males (P=0.012).
4. Coronal shape: Single opening most frequent, Y-shape least common.
5. Orientation: Slanted most prevalent; vertically curved canals rare; slanted predominant in females, slanted+vertical in males (significant sex correlation).
6. Number of openings: 2 nasal openings (Stensen's foramina) most common; 4 openings least common; variation correlated with age.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Reformatted CBCT in axial, sagittal, and coronal planes
- NPC length: midpoint of incisive foramen (IF) to midpoint of Stensen's foramen (SF)
- Diameters measured at 3 levels (nasal, middle, palatal) in axial sections
- Nasopalatine angle: intersection of long axis of canal with palatal plane (reference: anterior nasal spine)
- Protocol: Bornstein et al. standardized NPC measurement method
- Statistics: Student's t-test (sex comparisons), χ² test (categorical distributions by sex/age), Pearson correlation (dimension vs age); SPSS 17.0; significance P<0.05
- Age groups: ≤20, 21–30, 31–40, 41–50, 51–60, ≥61 years

- ML lower diameter significantly increased with age (≥61 yr mean 4.2 mm vs ≤20 yr 2.8 mm; P=0.027)
- Canal length significantly shorter in ≥61 yr group (7.15 mm) vs younger groups
- Shape distribution: cylindrical 25.5%, banana 17.6%, hourglass 18.6%, funnel 23.5%, cone 7.8%, tree branch 3.9%, spindle 2.9%
- 2 openings (SF) most common; 4 openings least common
- Complication risks highlighted: nasopalatine nerve injury → loss of sensation; vessel injury → bleeding; NPC cyst following canal trauma

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-center retrospective study (southern Indian population); limited generalizability
- 48 of 150 scans excluded, reducing sample size
- Specific ethnic/population characteristics may differ from other cohorts
- Future: larger multicenter studies, 3D volumetric NPC analysis, correlation with implant outcomes

## 7. Glossary

- **NPC (Nasopalatine Canal, 비비구개관)**: Bilateral paired canal in anterior maxilla connecting oral and nasal cavities; contains nasopalatine nerve and artery
- **IF (Incisive Foramen, 절치공)**: Oral opening of NPC beneath incisive papilla
- **SF (Stensen's Foramen, 스텐센공)**: Paired nasal openings of NPC on either side of nasal septum
- **Nasopalatine angle (비구개각)**: Angle between long axis of NPC and palatal plane; reflects canal obliquity
- **AP diameter (전후 직경)**: Anteroposterior dimension measured in axial section
- **ML diameter (내외측 직경)**: Mediolateral dimension measured in axial section
- **FOV (Field of View, 시야각)**: CBCT imaging volume coverage
