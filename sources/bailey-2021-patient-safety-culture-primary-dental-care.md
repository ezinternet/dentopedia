## One-line Summary

Narrative review/perspective (Prim Dent J 2021) arguing that patient safety in dentistry must shift from secondary-care/academic research into primary care (~95% of care), and offering tools — human factors, second-victim awareness, and a non-punitive culture — to build a patient safety culture in practice.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Frames patient safety as a system-level property: systems, teams, individuals, and environments must work in tandem for safety/quality.
- Highlights the evidence gap: dental patient-safety research is still early and predominantly from secondary care and academia, while **~95% of dental care is delivered in primary care** — so the evidence is being applied where it was not generated.
- Surveys five organizing concepts for primary dental care: **human factors**, **best practice**, the **second victim** concept, the **potential for over-regulation**, and **creating a patient safety culture**.
- Intends to be practical — giving the clinician tools to build a safety culture in their own practice rather than a purely academic overview.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Abstract-only — no quantitative results. Key qualitative points:
- **~95%** of dental care occurs in primary care, where most safety research has *not* been conducted.
- **Human factors**: errors arise from system/environment/team interactions, not solely individual fault.
- **Second victim**: the clinician/team involved in a patient-safety incident is themselves harmed (distress, guilt, loss of confidence) and needs support — a non-punitive response is part of a safety culture.
- **Over-regulation caution**: excessive top-down regulation can be counterproductive and may suppress the open reporting/learning that a true safety culture depends on.
- **Safety culture**: built through shared values, psychological safety, open incident reporting, and learning rather than blame.

## 7. Glossary

- **Patient safety culture** — shared organizational values/attitudes prioritizing safety, characterized by open reporting, learning from error, and a non-punitive ("just culture") response.
- **Human factors** — discipline examining how system, environment, equipment, and team design shape human performance and error.
- **Second victim** — a healthcare provider who is traumatized by involvement in an adverse patient event and requires support.
- **Over-regulation** — excessive prescriptive regulation that can undermine open reporting and the learning culture it aims to protect.
- **Primary dental care** — community/general dental practice setting where the large majority (~95%) of dental care is delivered.
