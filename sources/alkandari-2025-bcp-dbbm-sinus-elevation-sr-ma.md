## Why Ingested

Direct SR+MA comparing BCP vs DBBM (Bio-Oss) for sinus floor elevation — answers which bone substitute yields superior histomorphometric outcomes in sinus augmentation, addressing a frequently debated question in implant planning. Adds evidence on an understudied synthetic graft alternative to the xenograft-dominated sinus lift literature. Related to and reinforces the framework of [[wiki/overviews/sinus-lift-lateral-2026-synthesis]].

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 10 RCTs (328 patients, 389 implants) finding BCP superior to DBBM for new bone formation (MD +3.48%) and soft tissue percentage, with lower residual graft material, though high heterogeneity limits conclusions.

## 한줄요약

10개 RCT(328명, 389임플란트) SR+MA로 BCP가 DBBM 대비 신생골 형성률(MD +3.48%), 연조직 비율에서 우수하고 잔류 이식재가 적으나, 높은 이질성(I²=44–86%)으로 결론의 확실성이 제한된다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Cureus 17(7): e87573
- **Publication date**: Published July 9, 2025; Review period March–July 2025
- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis of RCTs
- **Institution**: Multiple polyclinics/health centers, Kuwait
- **Protocol adherence**: PRISMA guidelines; Cochrane Handbook

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA specifically comparing BCP vs. DBBM using only RCTs for sinus floor elevation
- Provides pooled quantitative estimates for three histomorphometric outcomes: new bone formation %, residual graft %, soft tissue %
- Establishes that BCP produces more new bone formation and less residual graft material than DBBM — consistent with BCP's higher resorption rate (TCP component) vs. DBBM's slow resorption
- Confirms clinical viability of synthetic BCP as an alternative to DBBM xenograft in sinus augmentation

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search databases**: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, Cochrane CENTRAL
- **Search period**: Inception to May 2025
- **Inclusion**: RCTs comparing BCP vs. DBBM in sinus floor elevation; histomorphometric outcomes
- **Exclusion**: Non-RCT designs, conference abstracts, unpublished data, non-BCP synthetic grafts
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane ROB-2 tool (two independent reviewers)
- **Statistical model**: DerSimonian-Laird random-effects model
- **Effect measure**: Mean difference (MD) with 95% CI
- **Software**: Stata/MP v18 (meta esize + forest plot packages)
- **Final inclusion**: 10 RCTs, 328 patients, 389 implants

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### New Bone Formation (primary outcome)
- BCP vs. DBBM: MD = +3.48% (95% CI: 0.89–5.97; p = 0.01; I² = 44.03%)
- BCP produces significantly more new bone
- Moderate heterogeneity (I² 44%) — interpretable with caution

### Residual Graft Material (primary outcome)
- BCP vs. DBBM: MD = −8.41% (95% CI: −13.12 to −3.72; p < 0.001; I² = 85.71%)
- BCP leaves significantly less residual graft — consistent with faster TCP resorption
- High heterogeneity (I² 86%) — likely reflects different BCP HA:TCP ratios across studies

### Soft Tissue (secondary outcome)
- BCP vs. DBBM: MD = +6.01% (95% CI: 2.39–9.63; p < 0.001; I² = 71.96%)
- BCP associated with significantly more soft tissue in the sinus — interpretation complex (could reflect faster space remodeling)
- High heterogeneity (I² 72%)

### Context
- DBBM functions primarily as osteoconductive scaffold; slow resorption provides volume stability
- BCP (typically 60% HA + 40% TCP): HA provides long-term scaffold; TCP resorbs and releases Ca/PO4 ions stimulating new bone
- BCP bioactivity and resorption rate tunable by modifying HA:TCP ratio

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- High heterogeneity for residual graft and soft tissue outcomes (I² 72–86%) reduces confidence in pooled estimates
- Differences in BCP composition (HA:TCP ratio), particle size, and brand across studies contribute to heterogeneity
- Lack of standardized histomorphometric protocols across included RCTs
- No survival/success rate data for implants placed in augmented sites — clinical bottom line (implant survival) not addressable from this SR+MA alone
- Short follow-up in some included studies
- Authors call for high-quality, long-term RCTs with standardized BCP protocols

## 7. Glossary

- **BCP**: Biphasic calcium phosphate — synthetic bone graft composed of HA + TCP (commonly 60:40)
- **DBBM**: Deproteinized bovine bone mineral — xenograft (Bio-Oss); highly osteoconductive, slow resorption
- **HA**: Hydroxyapatite — stable, slowly resorbing calcium phosphate phase
- **TCP**: Tribasic calcium phosphate (β-TCP) — resorbable phase; releases Ca/PO4 ions
- **Histomorphometry**: Microscopic quantification of tissue components (new bone %, residual graft %, soft tissue %) in biopsy sections
- **MD**: Mean difference — effect measure for continuous outcomes in meta-analysis
- **I²**: Measure of statistical heterogeneity across studies (0–100%); ≥50% considered high
- **Schneiderian membrane**: Epithelial lining of maxillary sinus; elevated during sinus floor elevation
- **ROB-2**: Cochrane Risk of Bias 2 tool for RCTs
