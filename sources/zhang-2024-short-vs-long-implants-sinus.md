## One-line Summary
Network meta-analysis (17 studies, 1,076 patients, 1,751 implants) comparing short implants (4–8 mm) vs long implants (≥8 mm) with sinus floor elevation, and immediate vs delayed long implants in the posterior maxilla. Long implants had a non-significant trend to lower failure (OR 1.26, 95% CI 0.53–3.00); short implants showed significantly less marginal bone loss; complication rates not significantly different.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health 2024;24:1574
- **Type**: Network meta-analysis (PRISMA-NMA, PROSPERO CRD42023495027)
- **Search**: Cochrane, PubMed, Embase, Scopus, Web of Science, Jan 2014–Nov 2024
- **Institution**: Authors from China (corresponding: Can Cao)

## 2. Key Contributions
- First network meta-analysis simultaneously comparing the three posterior-maxilla treatment strategies in one model: (a) short implants without sinus elevation; (b) long implants with sinus floor elevation (delayed); (c) long implants with sinus floor elevation (immediate).
- Quantifies marginal bone loss (MBL) advantage of short implants over long implants.
- Provides updated PROSPERO-registered evidence map through 2024.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- PRISMA-NMA reporting; random-effects Bayesian or frequentist NMA model.
- Outcome 1: implant failure rate (primary).
- Outcome 2: marginal bone loss.
- Outcome 3: biological complications (peri-implantitis, sinusitis, mucositis).
- Outcome 4: mechanical complications (screw loosening, fracture).
- Risk of bias assessed; heterogeneity reported.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Implant failure (long vs short)**: OR 1.26, 95% CI 0.53–3.00 (NS; trend favoring long).
- **Biological complications (short vs long)**: OR 0.47, 95% CI 0.19–1.18 (NS trend favoring short).
- **Mechanical complications (short vs long)**: OR 0.94, 95% CI 0.45–1.94 (NS).
- **MBL (short vs long immediate)**: MD = −0.17 mm in favor of short (precise CI/p; see PDF).
- **MBL (short vs long delayed)**: significant reduction with short implants.
- N = 17 studies; 1,076 patients; 1,751 implants.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Heterogeneity in implant systems, sinus elevation techniques, follow-up.
- Most included studies short to medium term (1–5 years).
- Trial sequential analysis not performed; treatment ranking with limited indirect evidence.
- Authors call for RCTs directly comparing immediate vs delayed long implants with sinus floor elevation.

## 7. Glossary
- **Short implant**: implant ≤8 mm in length (this study defines 4–8 mm).
- **NMA (Network Meta-analysis)**: combines direct and indirect treatment comparisons in one model.
- **MBL (Marginal Bone Loss)**: radiographically measured peri-implant crestal bone change.
- **OR (Odds Ratio)**: relative odds of an event; >1 means more events in the listed first group.
- **PROSPERO**: international prospective register of systematic reviews.
