## Why Ingested

This is the originating paper that introduced the "water treeing" concept to dentin adhesion, borrowing the term from the electrical high-voltage cable insulation literature where dendritic water-filled channels progressively degrade polymer insulation. It supplies the foundational mechanistic vocabulary that later degradation reviews and nanoleakage-classification papers build on, so it anchors the dentin-adhesion "hybrid layer / wet bonding / bond degradation" batch. It reinforces and is reinforced by [[wiki/resin-bonding/tay-2003-aging-two-modes-nanoleakage-bonded-dentin]] (the companion classification of two nanoleakage modes in aged bonded dentin) and is cited as a primary mechanism in [[wiki/resin-bonding/liu-2011-limitations-bonding-dentin-bond-degradation-review]] (the limitations-of-dentin-bonding degradation review).

## One-line Summary

In-vitro TEM tracer study (3 total-etch + 3 self-etch adhesives, conventional vs ammoniacal 50 wt% silver nitrate, 24 h): all adhesives showed nanoleakage within hybrid layers, and dendritic interconnecting silver "water trees" extended perpendicularly from the hybrid-layer surface into the adhesive — a proposed pathway for hydrolytic bond degradation. Abstract-only.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Introduced "water treeing" to dentin adhesion, importing the term from the electrical-engineering literature on polymeric high-voltage cable insulation, where dendritic water-filled channels progressively degrade the polymer.
- Provided TEM tracer evidence that water channels exist along the adhesive–dentin interface, detectable as silver deposits after silver-nitrate immersion and photodevelopment.
- Distinguished classic in-hybrid-layer nanoleakage from a second pattern — dendritic, interconnecting silver "trees" projecting perpendicularly from the hybrid-layer surface up into the adhesive resin.
- Showed the phenomenon is general across both total-etch and self-etch adhesives, not confined to one bonding strategy.
- Proposed water trees as a structural pathway for water uptake and hydrolytic degradation of resin–dentin bonds over time.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Hypothesis tested**: water channels are present along the adhesive–dentin interface and can be detected by TEM examination of tracer penetration.
- **Adhesives — total-etch**: Single Bond, Excite, PQ1.
- **Adhesives — self-etch**: Prompt L-Pop, Reactmer Bond, and an experimental self-etch adhesive.
- **Substrate**: adhesives bonded to dentin and enamel.
- **Tracer immersion**: bonded resin–tooth slabs immersed for 24 h in either 50 wt% conventional silver nitrate or 50 wt% basic (ammoniacal) silver nitrate.
- **Development & imaging**: tracer-loaded slabs exposed to a photodeveloping solution, then prepared for transmission electron microscopy (TEM).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- With **both** conventional and ammoniacal silver nitrate, **all** adhesives exhibited nanoleakage within the hybrid layers.
- **Water trees** appeared as interconnecting, dendritic silver deposits along the surface of the hybrid layers, extending **perpendicularly into the adhesive layers**.
- With **ammoniacal** silver nitrate, **additional isolated, unconnected silver grains** were observed within the adhesives (a second tracer pattern beyond the dendritic trees).
- Interpretation: the dendritic channels are water-filled pathways analogous to those degrading polymeric cable insulation, representing a potential route for hydrolytic degradation of resin–dentin bonds.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro TEM tracer study; tracer uptake indicates permeation pathways but is a surrogate for, not a direct measure of, clinical bond degradation.
- Abstract-only ingest — quantitative details, sample sizes, and statistics from the full text are not captured here.
- Mechanism is observational/correlative; longitudinal aging and bond-strength linkage are addressed in companion/later work.

## 7. Glossary

- **Water treeing**: formation of dendritic, water-filled channels within a polymer (originally described for high-voltage cable insulation) that progressively degrade it; here, analogous channels along/above the hybrid layer in dentin adhesives.
- **Hybrid layer**: the resin-infiltrated demineralized dentin zone that forms the bonded interface.
- **Nanoleakage**: nanometer-scale penetration of tracer (e.g., silver) into the bonded interface in the absence of gap formation, marking incompletely sealed/resin-deficient regions.
- **Conventional silver nitrate**: standard AgNO₃ tracer solution.
- **Ammoniacal (basic) silver nitrate**: ammoniated AgNO₃ tracer; smaller diammine-silver species can reveal additional permeation features (isolated grains here).
- **TEM**: transmission electron microscopy, used to image tracer distribution at the interface.
- **Total-etch / self-etch**: the two principal adhesive strategies (separate phosphoric-acid etch vs acidic self-etching monomers).
