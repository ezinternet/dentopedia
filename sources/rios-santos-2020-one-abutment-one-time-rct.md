## Why Ingested

The "one-abutment-one-time" (OAOT) concept — placing the definitive abutment at implant placement surgery and never disconnecting it, to avoid the repeated healing-abutment disconnections that disrupt the soft-tissue seal — is the clinical rationale behind much of the customized-healing-abutment literature. This multicenter RCT (147 patients, 231 implants) directly tests OAOT against repeated disconnection on marginal bone and stability, and its negative primary result (no significant difference) usefully tempers the OAOT enthusiasm. It reinforces and contextualizes [[wiki/implants/ruhstorfer-2024-customized-vs-conventional-healing-abutments-sr]] (which examines the same disconnection-avoidance principle through customized abutments) and relates to abutment-surface effects on peri-implant tissue summarized in [[wiki/implants/canullo-2020-titanium-abutment-surface-peri-implant-tissue-ma]].

## One-line Summary

Multicenter double-blind RCT (147 patients, 231 platform-switched subcrestal implants) finding no significant difference in 12-month peri-implant marginal bone loss or ISQ between the one-abutment-one-time protocol (definitive abutment at surgery, never removed; 0.36 ± 0.79 mm) and repeated healing-abutment disconnection (up to 3 times; 0.48 ± 0.71 mm) — but abutment height mattered: 2 mm abutments lost significantly less bone than 1 mm abutments (p = 0.002).

## 한줄요약

다기관 이중맹검 RCT(환자 147명, 플랫폼 스위칭·치조골하 식립 임플란트 231개)에서 원-어버트먼트-원-타임(수술 당일 최종 어버트먼트 장착 후 미분리, 0.36 ± 0.79 mm)과 반복적 치유 어버트먼트 분리(최대 3회, 0.48 ± 0.71 mm) 간 12개월 변연골소실·ISQ에 유의차가 없었으나, 어버트먼트 높이는 유의했음 — 2 mm 어버트먼트가 1 mm보다 골소실 적음(p = 0.002).

## 2. Key Contributions

1. One of the largest multicenter RCTs to test the "One Abutment—One Time" (OAOT) concept against conventional repeated healing-abutment disconnection, in **healed-ridge, subcrestal, platform-switched** implants — a configuration distinct from most prior OAOT RCTs (which were crestal and/or immediate placement).
2. Reports a **negative primary result**: OAOT did NOT significantly reduce 12-month peri-implant marginal bone loss vs. removing the healing screw up to three times.
3. Confirms an **abutment-height effect independent of group**: taller (2 mm) abutments produced significantly less marginal bone loss than short (1 mm) abutments (p = 0.002), supporting the "taller-abutment" / mucosa-tunnel concept.
4. Demonstrates **stable, high implant stability (ISQ)** in both groups across 12 months with no between-group or over-time significant difference.
5. Practical implication: because OAOT showed no bone-level advantage here, abutment selection (especially height, for esthetics) can be deferred until soft-tissue healing without bone-level penalty.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: randomized, double-blind, multicenter (20 centers), monitored by University of Seville; ethics approval Nº 0962-N-15 (March 2016). Recruitment April–September 2016.
- **Groups** (randomized AFTER implant insertion + primary-stability check, via sealed envelopes shipped with each implant; all of a patient's implants assigned to the same group):
  - **DEF (test = One Abutment One Time)**: definitive (Permanent) abutment placed on surgery day, never removed.
  - **HEA (control = repeated disconnection)**: 5 mm conical healing abutment placed at surgery; healing screw removed/replaced **up to three times** during the prosthetic phase (impression at 12 weeks, framework try-in at 14 weeks, prosthesis at 16 weeks).
- **Abutment height**: Permanent abutments were 1 or 2 mm high (chosen by clinician per mucosal thickness) in BOTH groups.
- **Implants**: Klockner VEGA, ContacTi bioactive surface (Sa 1.6 µm), bone-level, internal conical connection, platform switching (0.35 mm for Ø4.0; 0.60 mm for Ø4.5), lengths 8/10/12/14 mm. Placed ~1 mm **subcrestal**.
- **Site**: posterior partial edentulism (FDI 4-5-6-7, maxilla/mandible), healed ≥4 months post-extraction; ≥2 mm bone around implant; no GBR >25% of surface; smokers NOT excluded.
- **Loading**: screw-retained, definitive prosthesis at 16 weeks (15 Ncm screw torque); abutment final torque 25 Ncm in both groups.
- **Outcomes**:
  - Marginal bone level (mesial–distal mean), standardized long-cone paralleling radiographs at baseline, 2, 6, 12 months; calibrated with implant thread distance; measured in ImageJ by 2 calibrated, blinded operators (ICC 0.947). Baseline radiographs all taken with the 5 mm healing screw to blind the measurer to group.
  - Implant stability: RFA/ISQ (Osstell, Smart-Pegs Type 26 on implant at surgery, Type 72 on abutment at follow-ups); implants <60 ISQ excluded.
  - Modified plaque index and modified bleeding index (Mombelli) at 6 and 12 months.
- **Sample size**: powered (α 0.05, 80% power) at 70 per group based on Canullo radiographic data.
- **Statistics**: repeated-measures GLM (MANOVA), intra-subject factor = time (6/12 mo), inter-subject factor = group (HEA/DEF); Bonferroni post-hoc; chi-square/Fisher for qualitative; SPSS 26.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Enrollment/attrition**: 160 patients (252 implants) enrolled → 13 abandoned, 4 implant failures (survival 98.42%, all in first 2 months), additional drop-outs → final analyzed sample **147 patients (231 implants)**: HEA 76 patients / 128 implants; DEF 71 patients / 103 implants (no significant between-group imbalance).
- **Abutment-height distribution**: 1 mm → 119 implants (HEA 62 / DEF 57); 2 mm → 112 implants (HEA 66 / DEF 46). No significant group difference.
- **Population**: 81 women / 66 men; mean age 54.89 y (HEA 55.80 ± 10.93; DEF 53.85 ± 12.91, NS). Bone quality (Lekholm-Zarb): type I 5.6%, II 55%, III 38.1%, IV 1.3%. 57.6% single crowns, 42.4% multiple. 91.8% no periodontal history. No significant group differences in age, sex, smoking, bone quality, prosthesis type.

**Primary outcome — marginal bone level (HEA vs DEF):**
- Surgery day: HEA −0.70 ± 0.62 mm; DEF −0.79 ± 0.80 mm.
- 12 months: HEA −0.21 ± 0.71 mm; DEF −0.43 ± 0.79 mm.
- **Cumulative bone loss at 1 year: HEA 0.48 ± 0.71 mm vs DEF 0.36 ± 0.79 mm — NOT significant** (DEF numerically more favorable).
- Significant change only **over time** (0–6 mo and 0–12 mo, p < 0.001), regardless of group; bone loss occurred mainly in first 6 months, then stable 6–12 mo.

**Abutment height (1 mm vs 2 mm, pooled across groups):**
- Surgery day: 1 mm −0.65 ± 0.63 mm; 2 mm −0.84 ± 0.77 mm.
- 12 months: 1 mm −0.20 ± 0.78 mm; 2 mm −0.43 ± 0.70 mm.
- **Cumulative bone loss: 1 mm 0.45 ± 0.78 mm vs 2 mm 0.41 ± 0.70 mm — significant (p < 0.002); taller abutment = less bone loss.**

**Implant stability (ISQ):**
- Surgery: HEA 77.18 ± 9.30; DEF 78.36 ± 8.13.
- 6 months: HEA 79.67 ± 8.18; DEF 76.54 ± 9.41.
- 12 months: HEA 80.07 ± 9.01; DEF 76.51 ± 12.46.
- **No significant difference between groups or over time (p = 0.731);** slight DEF decrease after 6 mo (typical when primary stability is high).

**Soft tissue:** modified plaque index and modified bleeding index showed no significant HEA-vs-DEF difference at 6 or 12 months — so any DEF trend was not attributable to differential plaque control.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abutment-height selection was **not randomized** (clinician's esthetic discretion), confounding the height analysis.
- Mixed implant diameters → different platform-switch dimensions combined with different abutment heights.
- Subcrestal placement targeted 1 mm but varied between patients/centers (multicenter, real-world).
- Follow-up only 12 months; OAOT differences in some prior studies emerged at 3 years.
- Authors conclude more studies are needed to determine whether OAOT decisively influences peri-implant bone loss, given heterogeneous criteria across the literature.

## 6. Related Work

- **Abrahamsson 1997** (histology): connect/disconnect produced more bone loss and recession vs intact abutment — original biological-width rationale for OAOT.
- **OAOT human RCTs** (Canullo, Degidi, Grandi, Koutouzis, Molina, Praça, Luongo): mixed — several found less bone loss with OAOT (Grandi p<0.001; Molina p=0.028; Luongo 3-yr 0.07 vs 0.50 mm), others (Koutouzis, Praça) no significant difference; differences attributed to small samples, immediate vs healed placement, crestal vs subcrestal.
- **Abutment-height studies** (Galindo, White, Novoa, Blanco): consistently show taller abutments preserve more crestal bone — concordant with this study's height finding.
- **Praça**: comparable HEA/DEF methodology, similar ISQ values and non-significant bone-level result.

## 7. Glossary

- **One Abutment—One Time (OAOT)**: placement of the definitive prosthetic abutment at surgery, never disconnected, to preserve the soft-tissue/biological-width seal and crestal bone.
- **Platform switching**: connecting an abutment of smaller diameter than the implant, displacing the implant–abutment interface (and its inflammatory infiltrate) inward/away from the bone crest.
- **Biological width**: the soft-tissue dimension (epithelium + connective tissue) that re-forms around the abutment; repeated disconnection is thought to displace it apically with crestal bone loss.
- **ISQ / RFA**: Implant Stability Quotient measured by Resonance Frequency Analysis (Osstell).
- **Subcrestal placement**: implant platform positioned below the bone crest (negative values here), here ~1 mm.
- **Lekholm-Zarb classification**: bone quality types I–IV.
