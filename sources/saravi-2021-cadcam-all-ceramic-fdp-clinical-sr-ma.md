## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Materials 2021, 14, 2672
- **DOI**: 10.3390/ma14102672
- **Published**: 20 May 2021
- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis

## 2. Key Contributions
- Evaluated clinical performance of CAD/CAM all-ceramic (zirconia, lithium disilicate, feldspathic) tooth-supported FDPs
- Provides mid- and long-term survival and complication data for CAD/CAM all-ceramic FPDs vs metal-ceramic
- Framework fracture and chipping (veneering porcelain) are primary complications
- Lithium disilicate: favorable for single crowns; zirconia FPDs showing improving survival with newer generations
- Meta-analysis conducted on survival rates and biological/technical complications

## 3. Methodology
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane, Embase, Wiley Online Library
- **Study types**: RCTs and cohort studies
- **Interventions**: CAD/CAM all-ceramic tooth-supported FDPs (zirconia, lithium disilicate, conventional felspathic)
- **Outcomes**: Survival rate, success rate, biological and technical complications
- **Quality**: Cochrane RoB for RCTs; Newcastle-Ottawa Scale for cohorts

## 4. Key Results
- 5-year survival rates for CAD/CAM all-ceramic FDPs generally high (>90% for single crowns)
- Zirconia FPDs: framework fracture rare; chipping of veneering ceramic is main issue
- Lithium disilicate: higher fracture risk for long-span FPDs vs single crowns
- Biological complications (caries, endodontic): comparable to metal-ceramic
- Technical complications: chipping and fracture more frequent in all-ceramic than metal-ceramic FPDs

## 5. Limitations
- Heterogeneity among included studies in terms of CAD/CAM material generation, study design, follow-up duration
- Limited long-term (>10 year) data for newer CAD/CAM ceramics
- Operator skill and CAD/CAM system variability not controlled
