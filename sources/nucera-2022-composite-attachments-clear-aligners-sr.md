## One-line Summary

SR (5 clinical trials, medium RoB): composite attachments mostly increase aligner treatment effectiveness — improving anterior root torque, rotation, mesio-distal movement, and posterior anchorage; evidence for intrusion is weaker and lacking for extrusion and posterior expansion.

## 한줄요약

SR(임상 5편, medium RoB): 컴포지트 attachment는 대체로 교정 효과를 높임 — 전치부 root torque·rotation·근원심 이동·후방 앵커리지 개선. 정출(intrusion) 근거는 약하고, 함입/후방 확장 근거는 부족.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Maps which tooth movements attachments actually help (torque, rotation, M-D, anchorage).
- Flags the evidence gaps (intrusion weak; extrusion and posterior bucco-lingual tipping/expansion unstudied).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

8 databases to Mar 2020; two-author selection/extraction/RoB. 5 clinical trials, all medium RoB. Analysis across 6 movement groups (M-D tipping/bodily; anterior bucco-lingual tipping/torque; posterior bucco-lingual tipping/expansion; intrusion; extrusion; rotation).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Attachments improve anterior root torque, rotation, and mesio-distal movement; increase posterior anchorage.
- Some contradictory/non-significant results across studies.
- Intrusion possibly improved but evidence weak; extrusion evidence lacking.
- No studies on posterior bucco-lingual tipping/expansion.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 5 trials, all medium RoB; attachment number/size/shape/position effects unresolved. RCTs needed.

## 7. Glossary

- **Attachment**: bonded composite feature giving the aligner purchase for specific force systems.
- **Torque**: bucco-lingual root inclination control.
- **Bodily movement**: crown and root translate together (vs tipping).
