## One-line Summary

Narrative mini-review of oral chronic graft-versus-host disease (cGVHD) — a lichenoid/sicca/sclerotic complication of allogeneic HCT (30-50% of recipients) — covering NIH 2014 diagnostic criteria and a topical-to-systemic immunosuppressive management ladder.

## 1. Document Information

- Frontiers in Oral Health 2022;3:903154. Published 2022-05-20. Mini-review, Oral Cancers section.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Consolidates the three discrete oral cGVHD entities: lichenoid mucositis, immune-mediated salivary dysfunction, and tissue sclerosis/trismus.
- Summarizes NIH 2014 diagnostic/staging criteria for the mouth.
- Maps topical (corticosteroid rinses) to systemic immunosuppressive therapy with adverse-effect trade-offs.
- Flags cGVHD as a leading cause of late post-HCT morbidity/mortality and a malignant-transformation risk site.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Narrative literature review by two oral-medicine specialists; no quantitative pooling.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- cGVHD diagnosed in 30-50% of alloHCT recipients, >90% within 12 months.
- Lichenoid mucositis resembles OLP; salivary dysfunction resembles Sjögren/sicca; sclerosis causes trismus analogous to scleroderma.
- Mucosal ulceration raises oral-sourced bacteremia and secondary-malignancy risk; topical corticosteroids first line, escalating to systemic immunosuppression.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative, non-systematic; pathophysiology of trismus/sclerosis poorly understood; limited high-level evidence for specific topical regimens.

## 6. Related Work

- NIH 2005/2014 Consensus on cGVHD diagnosis and staging.
- Literature on oral-mucosal cGVHD and salivary dysfunction.

## 7. Glossary

- cGVHD: chronic Graft-Versus-Host Disease.
- alloHCT: allogeneic Hematopoietic Cell Transplantation.
- Trismus: restricted mouth opening.
