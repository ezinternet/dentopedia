## Why Ingested

The wiki's OLP pages center on local disease behaviour — diagnostic criteria, clinical subtypes, treatment ladder, and malignant transformation rate ([[oral-medicine/srivastava-2022-oral-lichen-planus-diagnosis-management-review]], [[oral-medicine/opmd/gonzalez-moles-2021-lichen-planus-malignant-transformation-sr]], [[oral-medicine/sandhu-2022-oral-lichen-planus-treatment-sr]]) — but had no page on the *systemic* comorbidity burden of OLP. This 2025 J Dent Res critical review (by the same Granada/WHO group behind several of those pages) synthesizes meta-analytic prevalence/risk estimates for ~12 systemic diseases linked to OLP (DM, thyroid, celiac, liver, emotional disorders), extending the local-disease picture into oral-systemic medicine and adding the management caveat that corticosteroid therapy interacts with diabetes and hypertension.

## One-line Summary

Critical review (J Dent Res 2025) synthesizing recent (≤3 y) meta-analyses: OLP patients carry significantly elevated prevalence and risk of diabetes mellitus (PP 9.77%, OR 1.64), Hashimoto thyroiditis (8.60%, OR 2.2), hypo/hyperthyroidism, celiac disease (8.66%, OR 18.44), hepatitis C (7.14%, OR 4.09) and B, steatohepatitis, cirrhosis, and emotional disorders (depression 31%, anxiety 55%, stress 41%) — many comorbidities are asymptomatic and act as cancer risk factors, so OLP warrants oral-systemic vigilance.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Consolidates, in one table, meta-analytic pooled prevalence and odds ratios for ~20 systemic conditions in OLP patients, drawn almost entirely from the authors' own group's SRs published in the prior ~3 years.
- Frames OLP not just as a local oral potentially malignant disorder (malignant transformation 1.14%–2.28%) but as a marker of a broader systemic disease burden, many components of which are asymptomatic and unrecognized.
- Pairs each association with a biological-plausibility mechanism (shared chronic inflammation, autoimmune cross-reactivity / molecular mimicry, viral antigen presentation) and a concrete clinical recommendation for the dentist.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Narrative critical review. The quantitative backbone is a set of meta-analyses (mostly De Porras-Carrique / González-Moles / García-Pola, 2022–2024) whose risk-of-bias was assessed with the Joanna Briggs Institute prevalence tool (10 domains, emphasis on confounding and selection bias). All cited primary studies required reticular white lesions for OLP diagnosis; most used biopsy confirmation. Sjögren's syndrome excluded for insufficient evidence. Not a fresh systematic search — confidence label is `narrative-review`.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Pooled prevalence (PP) and odds ratio (OR) of systemic disease in OLP vs general population:

| Comorbidity | PP | OR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Anxiety | 54.76% | 3.51 (2.10–5.85) | <0.001 |
| Stress | 41.10% | 3.64 (1.48–8.94) | 0.005 |
| Depression | 31.19% | 6.15 (2.72–13.89) | <0.001 |
| Hypertension | 24.17% | 1.28 (1.01–1.63) | 0.04 |
| Diabetes mellitus | 9.41% (abstract 9.77%) | 1.64 (1.34–2.00) | <0.001 |
| Celiac disease | 8.66% | 18.44 (2.40–141.43) | 0.005 |
| Hashimoto's thyroiditis | 8.60% | 2.23 (1.76–2.82) | <0.001 |
| Hypothyroidism | 8.14% | 1.65 (1.08–2.53) | 0.02 |
| Hepatitis C | 7.14% | 4.09 (2.77–6.03) | <0.001 |
| Steatohepatitis | 7.06% | 5.71 (0.97–33.60) | 0.05 |
| Liver cirrhosis | 4.27% | 5.58 (1.83–16.96) | 0.002 |
| Hepatitis B | 3.90% | 1.62 (1.01–2.40) | 0.02 |
| Hyperthyroidism | 2.84% | 2.11 (1.22–3.63) | 0.007 |

Non-significant / weak: fibromyalgia, IBD, rheumatoid arthritis, Sjögren's, lupus, psoriasis, vitiligo, hepatocellular carcinoma (OR 0.94).

Other notable figures: DM meta-analysis pooled 116 studies / 17,609 patients; thyroid 75 studies / 15,176; liver 146 studies / 21,187; emotional 51 studies / 6,815. HCV increases oral cancer risk in OLP (RR 3.67, p=0.005); direct-acting antivirals cut HCC risk 50%–80%. HCV prevalence in OLP highest in Europe (9.29%). Reverse association: HCV patients have OLP prevalence 9.42% (OR 4.48). Mechanism for thyroid link includes TSHR overexpression in OLP lesions.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Heterogeneous OLP diagnostic criteria across primary studies (WHO 1978, van der Waal, AAOMP); some excluded dysplasia (controversial for malignancy assessment).
- Causal direction undetermined for most associations (bidirectional for DM; unidirectional hepatitis→OLP and emotional→OLP proposed but not proven).
- No evidence base for comorbidity-tailored management; screening (e.g., celiac, liver) not yet justified.
- Potential detection bias (OLP patients may be more health conscious / more investigated).
- Calls for mechanistic studies and methodologically standardized future research.

## 7. Glossary

- **OLP** — oral lichen planus; T-cell-mediated chronic inflammatory oral potentially malignant disorder.
- **PP / pooled proportion** — meta-analytic pooled prevalence.
- **OR / RR** — odds ratio / relative risk.
- **Grinspan's syndrome** — proposed triad of OLP + diabetes + hypertension (1966); currently questioned.
- **TSHR** — thyroid-stimulating hormone receptor; overexpressed in OLP lesions of thyroid-disease patients.
- **Molecular mimicry** — structural homology between a foreign/self antigen and oral epithelial proteins driving cross-reactive autoimmunity (proposed for celiac, thyroid links).
- **NASH / steatohepatitis** — nonalcoholic steatohepatitis; cirrhosis and HCC risk factor.
- **DAA** — direct-acting antiviral (HCV therapy).
