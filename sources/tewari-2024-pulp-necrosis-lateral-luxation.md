## Why Ingested

Quantifies the pulp-necrosis and root-resorption risk that decides whether a laterally luxated tooth needs prophylactic endodontic intervention — the diagnostic-prognostic backbone of the new `dental-trauma` category. The maturity-stratified necrosis risk (immature 12% vs mature 58%) links to pulp-survival decision logic in [[overviews/vital-pulp-therapy-decision-ladder]] and to trauma-driven REP prognosis in [[dental-trauma/vitali-2025-preoperative-factors-prognosis-regenerative-endodontic]].

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (13 studies; GRADE very low): after lateral luxation of permanent anterior teeth, pooled pulp-necrosis prevalence is 57% overall — 58% in mature-apex vs only 12% in immature-apex teeth — and EIRR ~11%, so routine prophylactic pulp extirpation in mature teeth should be applied with caution.

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (13편; GRADE 매우 낮음): 영구 전치 측방탈구(lateral luxation) 후 치수괴사 통합 유병률은 전체 57%로, 성숙치근 58% 대 미성숙치근 12%이며 외부염증성치근흡수(EIRR)는 약 11%다. 따라서 성숙치에 대한 일률적 예방적 치수적출은 신중히 적용해야 한다.

## 1. Document Information

- Type: Systematic review + meta-analysis (authors include Lauridsen & Andersson, Dental Trauma Guide / IADT lineage).
- Journal: Dental Traumatology 2024;40(5):482-498.
- Registration: PROSPERO.
- Abstract-only — full text not retrieved (PMC unavailable).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Stratifies pulp-necrosis risk by apex maturity for lateral luxation specifically (not all luxation types pooled).
- Challenges any absolute recommendation for endodontic intervention in mature laterally luxated teeth (necrosis <60%, EIRR <20%).
- Pairs maturity with external inflammatory root resorption (EIRR) risk.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- A priori PROSPERO protocol; search (10 Jul 2023) of PubMed, LILACS, Web of Science, EMBASE, Scopus, Cochrane; no language/year limit.
- 13 studies (1985–2020), hospital settings. Risk-of-bias assessment; GRADE.
- Meta-analysis of proportions (pooled prevalence) + relative risks.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Pulp necrosis overall: 57% (95% CI 42–72%).
- Immature apex: 12% (95% CI 8–18%, I²=0%).
- Mature apex: 58% (95% CI 42–73%, I²=86%).
- EIRR: 11% (95% CI 4–27%, I²=95%); higher in mature apex (RR 1.26, 95% CI 1.12–1.42, I²=0%).
- ROB moderate/high in 9 studies; GRADE very low in 14/15 outcomes.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Very low certainty; high heterogeneity for mature-tooth necrosis and EIRR.
- Hospital-setting samples; variable observation periods.
- No standardized timing of follow-up pulp testing.

## 7. Glossary

- Lateral luxation: displacement of a tooth in a direction other than axial, usually with alveolar bone fracture.
- Pulp necrosis: death of the pulp tissue (an outcome to monitor after luxation).
- EIRR: external inflammatory root resorption — resorption driven by infected necrotic pulp + damaged root surface.
- Mature/immature apex: closed vs open root apex; immature retains greater revascularization potential.
