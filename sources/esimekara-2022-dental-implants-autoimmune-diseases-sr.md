## Why Ingested

Seeds the autoimmune-disease dental-treatment evidence cluster: this is the most comprehensive systematic review of dental implants across autoimmune diseases (55 studies, 9 distinct conditions) and serves as the hub of the batch, complementing the disease-specific sibling [[wiki/implants/almeida-2017-dental-implants-sjogren-syndrome-sr]] (Sjögren-only SR). It reinforces the broad "systemic disease rarely contraindicates implants if hygiene/recall are strict" position of [[wiki/implants/aghaloo-2019-systemic-diseases-medications-implant-osseointegration-sr]].

## One-line Summary

PRISMA systematic critical review (55 studies, 9 autoimmune diseases) finding dental implant survival comparable to the general population, though secondary Sjögren's and erosive oral lichen planus raise peri-mucositis and marginal bone loss risk.

## 한줄요약

PRISMA 체계적 비판적 고찰 (55편, 자가면역질환 9종): 치과 임플란트 생존율은 일반 인구와 동등하나, 이차 쇼그렌증후군과 미란성 구강편평태선에서 임플란트주위점막염(peri-mucositis)과 변연골소실(MBL) 위험이 증가.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Broadest single synthesis of dental implant outcomes across the autoimmune-disease spectrum (9 distinct diseases in one review), where prior reviews were typically disease-specific (e.g., Sjögren's only).
- Establishes that, despite methodological heterogeneity, implant survival in autoimmune-disease patients is comparable to the general population.
- Identifies two specific higher-risk subgroups — secondary Sjögren's syndrome and erosive oral lichen planus — as more susceptible to peri-mucositis and increased marginal bone loss.
- Frames scrupulous oral hygiene maintenance and long-term follow-up as the shared determinants of uneventful treatment across all conditions.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PRISMA-guided systematic critical review.
- Databases: Google Scholar + PubMed.
- Search stop date: September 2021.
- Inclusion: all full-text, human, English-language studies reporting on patients with autoimmune diseases treated with dental implants.
- Outcome focus: implant complications and survival rates.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **55 studies** analyzed, reporting on **9 distinct autoimmune diseases**:
  - Sjögren's syndrome (SS): 17 studies
  - Oral lichen planus (OLP): 11 studies
  - Type 1 diabetes mellitus (T1DM): 8 studies
  - Rheumatoid arthritis (RA): 6 studies
  - Systemic scleroderma / systemic sclerosis (SSc): 4 studies
  - Crohn's disease (CD): 3 studies
  - Systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE): 3 studies
  - Mucous membrane pemphigoid (MMP): 2 studies
  - Pemphigus vulgaris (PV): 1 study
- Implant **survival rates comparable to the general population** across the disease spectrum, despite heterogeneity and methodological limitations.
- **Secondary SS** and **erosive OLP** patients more susceptible to **peri-mucositis** and **increased marginal bone loss**.
- Conclusion: dental implants are a safe and viable option for edentulous autoimmune-disease patients; strict oral hygiene + long-term follow-up are common determinants of uneventful outcomes.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only ingest — full text not retrieved; effect sizes, per-disease survival percentages, and follow-up durations not extractable.
- Authors note marked heterogeneity and methodological limitations among included studies (largely case reports / small series for rarer diseases — e.g., only 1 PV study, 2 MMP).
- No meta-analysis; conclusions are narrative/critical synthesis rather than pooled estimates.
- Disease-control status, immunosuppressive medication effects, and smoking were not quantitatively adjusted across studies.

## 7. Glossary

- **SS (Sjögren's syndrome)** — autoimmune exocrinopathy causing dry mouth/eyes; "secondary SS" co-occurs with another connective-tissue disease (e.g., RA, SLE).
- **OLP (oral lichen planus)** — chronic T-cell-mediated mucosal inflammatory disease; "erosive" subtype is the symptomatic, ulcerative form.
- **Peri-mucositis (peri-implant mucositis)** — reversible inflammation of soft tissue around an implant without bone loss.
- **Marginal bone loss (MBL)** — crestal bone resorption around an implant over time.
- **SSc (systemic sclerosis / scleroderma)** — autoimmune connective-tissue disease causing fibrosis; microstomia limits oral access.
- **MMP / PV** — mucous membrane pemphigoid / pemphigus vulgaris: autoimmune blistering diseases affecting oral mucosa.
