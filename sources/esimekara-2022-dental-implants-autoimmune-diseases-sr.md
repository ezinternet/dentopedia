## Why Ingested

Seeds the autoimmune-disease dental-treatment evidence cluster: this is the most comprehensive systematic review of dental implants across autoimmune diseases (55 studies, 9 distinct conditions) and serves as the hub of the batch, complementing the disease-specific sibling [[wiki/implants/almeida-2017-dental-implants-sjogren-syndrome-sr]] (Sjögren-only SR). It reinforces the broad "systemic disease rarely contraindicates implants if hygiene/recall are strict" position of [[wiki/implants/aghaloo-2019-systemic-diseases-medications-implant-osseointegration-sr]].

## One-line Summary

PRISMA systematic critical review (55 studies, 9 autoimmune diseases) finding dental implant survival comparable to the general population, though secondary Sjögren's and erosive oral lichen planus raise peri-mucositis and marginal bone loss risk.

## 한줄요약

PRISMA 체계적 비판적 고찰 (55편, 자가면역질환 9종): 치과 임플란트 생존율은 일반 인구와 동등하나, 이차 쇼그렌증후군과 미란성 구강편평태선에서 임플란트주위점막염(peri-mucositis)과 변연골소실(MBL) 위험이 증가.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Dental implants in patients suffering from autoimmune diseases: A systematic critical review
- **Authors**: Esimekara JO, Perez A, Courvoisier DS, Scolozzi P
- **Journal**: J Stomatol Oral Maxillofac Surg 2022;123(5):e464–e473
- **Year**: 2022 (received 2021-12-31; accepted 2022-01-10; e-pub 2022-01-13)
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.jormas.2022.01.005
- **Study type**: Systematic critical review (PRISMA; narrative synthesis — no meta-analysis)
- **Affiliations**: University of Geneva and University Hospitals of Geneva (oral/maxillofacial surgery, Geneva, Switzerland)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Broadest single synthesis of dental implant outcomes across the autoimmune-disease spectrum: 9 distinct conditions evaluated in one review, where all prior reviews had been disease-specific.
- Demonstrates that implant survival is comparable to the general population across the entire autoimmune spectrum despite marked study heterogeneity.
- Identifies two specific higher-risk subgroups — secondary Sjögren's syndrome (SS) and erosive oral lichen planus (OLP) — as more susceptible to peri-implant mucositis (PIM) and increased marginal bone loss (MBL).
- Finds beneficial effect of low-dose prophylactic oral corticosteroids on implant survival and disease control in OLP patients (100% survival in the controlled group vs. 24% in uncontrolled patients).
- Highlights that glycemic control (HbA1c) is the key modifying variable for dental implant survival in Type 1 diabetes patients.
- Frames meticulous oral hygiene and long-term follow-up as the universal determinants of uneventful implant treatment across all autoimmune conditions.
- Formally argues against blanket contraindication of implants in autoimmune-disease patients.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: PRISMA-guided systematic critical review
- **Databases**: Google Scholar + PubMed
- **Search stop date**: September 2021
- **Eligibility criteria**: All full-text, human, English-language studies reporting on autoimmune-disease patients treated with dental implants
- **Primary outcomes**: Implant survival rate, implant complications (peri-implant mucositis, peri-implantitis, marginal bone loss)
- **Synthesis method**: Narrative/critical synthesis only; heterogeneity of study designs, sample sizes, follow-up durations, and outcome definitions prevented formal meta-analysis
- **Study design composition**: Largely retrospective cohort studies, case series, and case reports; few prospective controlled studies — particularly for rarer diseases (SLE, MMP, SSc, PV, CD)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Overall
- **55 studies** analyzed across **9 distinct autoimmune diseases**
- Implant survival comparable to the general population overall, despite substantial methodological heterogeneity

### By disease (study count, key findings):

**Sjögren's Syndrome (SS) — 17 studies**
- Overall survival rates similar to general population in most studies
- Korfage et al. (n=50, 140 implants): 97% survival at 46-month follow-up
- Albrecht et al. (OPCS, n=32, 104 implants): 95.2% survival
- Siddiqui et al.: 87% overall survival; all failures in preloading phase; loaded implants 100% at 40 months
- Chrcanovic et al. (n=19, 107 implants): 97.2% survival with increased MBL at 125-month follow-up
- Two distinct vulnerabilities in SS: (1) marked peri-implant soft-tissue susceptibility (high PIM prevalence — up to 94%) due to reduced salivary secretion and impaired oral self-clearance; (2) RA+SS co-occurrence associated with significantly increased MBL and bleeding index
- No significant difference in survival between primary and secondary SS, but secondary SS (especially with co-occurring RA) has worse peri-implant tissue status
- Conclusion: no contraindication, but meticulous oral hygiene + regular monitoring are essential

**Oral Lichen Planus (OLP) — 11 studies**
- Survival generally comparable or even higher than controls in some studies
- Hernandez et al. (n=18, 56 implants OLP vs controls): 100% survival in OLP group vs. 96.8% in controls
- López-Jornet et al. (n=16, 56 implants): 96.4% OLP vs. 92% control; PIM 17.9%, PID 25% in OLP
- Aboushelib & Elsafi: 24% survival in uncontrolled OLP patients vs. 100% in patients managed with oral corticosteroids + low-energy soft tissue laser irradiation
- Khamis et al.: low-dose systemic corticosteroids (LDSC) prevent increase in MBL; noncontrolled patients showed significant MBL increase and 13.7% OLP recurrence
- Anitua et al. (short implants ≤8.5 mm + prophylactic corticosteroids, n=23): 98% success rate (1 failure in erosive OLP with DG)
- Erosive OLP with desquamative gingivitis (DG): higher risk of PIM and PID
- Implant placement does not negatively influence the course of OLP disease itself (Czerninski et al.: 100% success, no OLP sign/symptom change)
- Notable: one case of oral squamous cell carcinoma arising adjacent to a peri-implantitis site in an OLP patient (Noguchi et al.)
- Key message: corticosteroid prophylaxis and OLP disease control are essential; implants not contraindicated in controlled OLP

**Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus (T1DM) — 8 studies**
- Most studies combined T1DM and T2DM; only 3 focused exclusively on T1DM
- Sannino et al. (n=53 T1DM vs 53 controls, 205 implants, 24-month follow-up): 95.2% vs. 97.0% survival, no statistically significant difference
- de Araújo Nobre et al.: T1DM significantly lower survival than T2DM (80% vs. 90.5%); T1DM higher MBL >0.2 mm/year between years 1–5
- Fiorellini et al. (n=40, well-controlled diabetes): 85.7% success at 6.5-year follow-up; most failures within year 1 of loading
- Alsaadi et al. (n=10 diabetics): 100% survival of 34 implants at 2 years post-abutment connection
- Alberti et al. (n=19 diabetics): 10-year survival 96.51% (T1DM subset: 94.74%)
- Only patient with poor glycemic control (HbA1c ≥8.0%) experienced multiple implant failures
- Perioperative glycemic crisis possible (case report: acute severe hypoglycemia during surgery)
- Key message: not explicitly contraindicated if comorbidities controlled; strict glycemic control + infection surveillance + maintenance are mandatory; failures cluster in early healing/loading phase

**Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA) — 6 studies**
- All 6 studies showed 100% survival rate
- El-Sherbini (prospective, n=14 RA vs controls): no difference in implant survival
- Three case reports all showed 100% survival
- Primary concern: RA+SS co-occurrence → increased MBL and higher bleeding index (not isolated RA)

**Systemic Scleroderma (SSc) — 4 studies**
- Majority case reports; all showed 100% survival
- Microstomia (limited mouth opening) is the main practical challenge for implant access but not a contraindication

**Crohn's Disease (CD) — 3 studies**
- Alsaadi et al. (retrospective): notably lower success rate in CD (66.7%) compared to general population
- Other CD studies: 100% survival rate
- Data too sparse for definitive conclusions

**Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE) — 3 studies**
- Case reports predominate; mostly 100% survival
- One patient with SLE + common variable immunodeficiency (CVID): 93% survival (15 implants, 18-month follow-up)

**Mucous Membrane Pemphigoid (MMP) — 2 studies**
- Both case reports; 100% survival at 8 and 180-month follow-up respectively

**Pemphigus Vulgaris (PV) — 1 study**
- Single case report: 100% survival (2 implants, 32-month follow-up)

### Clinical Conclusions
- No specific contraindications for dental implants in autoimmune-disease patients under current evidence
- Exceptions requiring specific management: (1) rigorous glycemic control for T1DM; (2) prophylactic corticosteroids for active OLP
- Universal requirements: scrupulous oral hygiene maintenance + long-term follow-up

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Methodological heterogeneity**: most studies retrospective, non-randomized, with small samples and lack of predetermined endpoints; high risk of bias across all included studies
- **No true meta-analysis**: quantitative pooling was not possible due to heterogeneity; outcomes are narrative synthesis
- **Confounding factors**: smoking, alcohol, multiple concomitant systemic diseases, pharmacologic therapy (immunosuppressants, corticosteroids) were not controlled across studies
- **Low evidence quality**: large proportion of case reports and small case series, particularly for rarer diseases (PV: 1 study; MMP: 2 studies; SLE: 3 studies)
- **T1DM isolation**: very few studies exclusively on T1DM; mixing of T1DM and T2DM data limits disease-specific conclusions
- **Future needs**: prospective controlled studies with larger, matched cohorts, pre-defined endpoints, and longer follow-up; standardized peri-implant outcome definitions across disease groups

## 7. Glossary

- **SS (Sjögren's syndrome)** — autoimmune exocrinopathy causing dry mouth/eyes; "primary SS" is isolated; "secondary SS" co-occurs with another connective-tissue disease (e.g., RA, SLE).
- **OLP (oral lichen planus)** — chronic T-cell-mediated mucosal inflammatory disease; "erosive" subtype is symptomatic/ulcerative; desquamative gingivitis (DG) is a high-risk OLP manifestation.
- **PIM (peri-implant mucositis)** — reversible inflammation of soft tissue around an implant without bone loss; characterized by bleeding on probing and increased pocket depth.
- **PID (peri-implantitis)** — implant-surrounding bone loss accompanying inflammation.
- **MBL (marginal bone loss)** — crestal bone resorption around an implant over time; >0.2 mm/year indicates implant failure per Albrektsson/Zarb criteria.
- **DG (desquamative gingivitis)** — mucosal stripping/ulceration at the gingival margin; a clinical presentation seen in erosive OLP and pemphigoid diseases.
- **SSc (systemic sclerosis / scleroderma)** — autoimmune connective-tissue disease causing progressive fibrosis; microstomia limits intraoral access.
- **MMP / PV** — mucous membrane pemphigoid / pemphigus vulgaris: autoimmune blistering diseases affecting oral mucosa.
- **HbA1c** — glycated hemoglobin; ≥8.0% reflects poor glycemic control and is associated with multiple implant failures in diabetic patients.
- **CVID (common variable immunodeficiency)** — primary humoral immune deficiency that may potentiate SLE-related immune dysfunction and implant complications.
- **LDSC (low-dose systemic corticosteroids)** — prophylactic corticosteroid regimen used in OLP patients during and after implant placement to control disease activity and prevent recurrence.
