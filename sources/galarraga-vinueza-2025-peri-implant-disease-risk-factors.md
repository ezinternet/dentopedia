## One-line Summary
SR+MA of 102 studies (13,030 patients): peri-implant mucositis 46%, peri-implantitis 21%; periodontitis and smoking are risk indicators for both; diabetes and obesity additionally relevant.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Periodontology 2025; 96: 587–633
- **Study type**: Systematic review with meta-analysis (AO/AAP Best-Evidence Consensus)
- **PROSPERO**: CRD4202346657800

## 2. Key Contributions
- Most comprehensive up-to-date prevalence/incidence meta-analysis for peri-implant diseases (102 studies, 13,030 patients)
- First SR+MA to comprehensively assess systemic, behavioral, and patient-related risk indicators concurrently
- 20-year incidence: mucositis 53%, peri-implantitis 22%
- Quantifies relative risk for key modifiable risk factors

## 3. Methodology
- PRISMA-compliant SR+MA
- Databases: MEDLINE-PubMed, EMBASE, Cochrane, ClinicalTrials.gov
- Random-effects pooled analyses for prevalence and incidence
- PECOS framework
- Only risk indicators could be identified (not true risk factors) due to study design limitations

## 4. Key Results
| Outcome | Prevalence | 20-yr Incidence |
|---------|-----------|----------------|
| Peri-implant mucositis | 46% (95% CI 41–51%) | 53% |
| Peri-implantitis | 21% (95% CI 17–24%) | 22% |

**Risk indicators for mucositis**: periodontitis, obesity, smoking
**Risk indicators for peri-implantitis**: periodontitis, diabetes mellitus, smoking, alcohol consumption

## 5. Limitations
- Heterogeneity of case definitions across included studies
- Predominantly observational studies → risk indicators, not confirmed causal risk factors
- Publication bias possible

## 6. Related Work
- Derks 2015: earlier SR+MA showing mucositis 43%, peri-implantitis 22%
- Berglundh 2018: case definitions applied throughout
- Herrera 2023 EFP guideline: uses this risk factor profile for prevention recommendations
