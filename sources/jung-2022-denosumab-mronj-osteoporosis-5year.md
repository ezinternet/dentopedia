## One-line Summary
5-year retrospective cohort (n=98, osteoporosis+tooth extraction): MRONJ rate 4.1%; no difference between denosumab-only vs BP+denosumab; rheumatoid arthritis was the only significant risk factor; MRONJ can arise from chronic periodontal inflammation without extraction.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Scientific Reports (2022) 12:8641
- **Institution**: Yonsei University Dental Hospital / Severance Hospital, Seoul
- **Study period**: October 2016 – March 2020
- **Study design**: Retrospective cohort (IRB: 2-2020-0071)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Reports MRONJ incidence and risk factors specifically for osteoporotic patients on Prolia® (60 mg SC q6mo)
- Demonstrates that MRONJ can develop without invasive dental treatment (spontaneous onset from periapical/periodontal inflammation)
- Identifies rheumatoid arthritis as a significant systemic risk factor (OR 35.4, p=0.033)
- No significant difference in MRONJ rate between denosumab-only (3.8%) vs BP→denosumab (4.3%) groups

## 3. Methodology
- Screened 159 patients; 98 included (exclusions: cancer-dose denosumab, Xgeva use, treatment at other institutions)
- Two groups: Dmab only (n=52) vs BP+Dmab (n=46)
- Mean age 70.5±10.3 years; 88.8% female
- Mean denosumab doses before extraction: 2.45±1.63
- Mean drug cessation to extraction: 6.26±4.22 months
- Variables: age, sex, comorbidities, BP history, number of Dmab doses, drug holiday duration, extraction location
- Statistics: χ² test, multiple logistic regression (n=40 randomly sampled for stability due to low MRONJ rate)

## 4. Key Results
- MRONJ occurred in 4/98 patients (4.1%)
  - Case 1: RA + prior BP (zoledronate) → MRONJ from periodontitis, Stage 2, mandible Rt
  - Case 2: CAD + prior 3 BPs → MRONJ from periodontitis, Stage 1, maxilla Lt
  - Case 3: DM + 1 Dmab dose → MRONJ after extraction 1 month post-administration, Stage 2, mandible Rt
  - Case 4: HT+DM+AMI → extraction 1 year BEFORE denosumab; MRONJ developed 10 months after 2nd Dmab, Stage 2, anterior mandible
- No significant difference between groups (p=1.00)
- Rheumatoid arthritis: OR 35.4, 95% CI 1.34–932.5, p=0.033 (only significant predictor)
- Timing of Dmab relative to extraction not significant (p=0.76 pre; 0.92 post)
- Comorbidities (cancer, HTN, DM) not significant

## 5. Limitations
- Small MRONJ event count (n=4); logistic regression used 40 randomly sampled cases
- Single institution
- No drug holiday protocol standardized
- Lacks long-term follow-up beyond extraction episode

## 6. Related Work
- AAOMS 2014/2022 position paper (Ruggiero et al.) — defines MRONJ criteria and staging used in this study
- Marx 2003 — first report of BRONJ
- FREEDOM trial — established denosumab fracture efficacy in postmenopausal women

## 7. Glossary
- **MRONJ**: Medication-Related Osteonecrosis of the Jaw — exposed bone >8 weeks in antiresorptive-treated patient, no radiation history
- **BRONJ**: Bisphosphonate-Related ONJ (older term, now subsumed under MRONJ)
- **Prolia®**: Denosumab 60 mg SC q6mo (osteoporosis dose)
- **Xgeva®**: Denosumab 120 mg SC q4w (oncology dose)
- **RANKL inhibitor**: Receptor activator of NF-κB ligand inhibitor — mechanism of denosumab
