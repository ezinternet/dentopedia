## Why Ingested

The wiki's oral-mucosal-disease coverage centers on oral lichen planus, RAS, BMS, and OPMD but holds no page on radiation-induced oral mucositis (RIOM) — a major HNC radiotherapy toxicity. This triple-blind RCT extends the oral-medicine symptom-management theme exemplified by [[oral-medicine/belenguer-guallar-2014-recurrent-aphthous-stomatitis-review]] (which discusses topical amino-acid/anti-inflammatory adjuncts for oral ulceration) by providing the first head-to-head evidence that L-arginine oral suspension is non-inferior to the better-established L-glutamine for RIOM severity, pain, weight maintenance, and OHRQoL.

## One-line Summary

Triple-blind RCT (n=69 HNC radiotherapy patients, 3 arms ×23) showing both L-arginine and L-glutamine oral suspensions reduced WHO mucositis severity, pain, and weight loss vs maltodextrin control by week 5–7, with L-arginine non-inferior to glutamine and a numerically higher week-7 healing rate (56% vs 13%).

## 한줄요약

삼중맹검 RCT (두경부암 방사선치료 환자 69명, 3군 ×23명) — L-아르기닌·L-글루타민 경구 현탁액 모두 maltodextrin 대조군 대비 5–7주차 WHO 점막염 중증도·통증·체중감소를 유의하게 줄였고, L-아르기닌은 글루타민에 비열등하며 7주차 완치율이 수치상 더 높았다 (56% vs 13%).

## 2. Key Contributions

- **First RCT to test L-arginine oral suspension for RIOM** and to benchmark it directly against the better-established L-glutamine.
- Used a true **neutral control (maltodextrin 10 g)** rather than placebo-absent comparison, isolating the amino-acid effect from generic caloric/nutritional support — a gap in prior glutamine trials.
- Both amino acids significantly outperformed maltodextrin on **WHO mucositis severity, Pain-VAS, BMI maintenance, and OHIP-14 quality of life**.
- Formal **non-inferiority analysis** (pre-specified margins) established L-arginine as not clinically worse than glutamine across all outcomes, positioning it as an alternative supportive therapy.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Population**: 69 head-and-neck-cancer patients (stages I–IV), aged 20–70, with RIOM appearing at 2nd–3rd week of RT, recruited at Ahmed Maher Teaching Hospital. All had surgical tumor removal then IMRT (2 Gy/fraction, 5/week, total 50–70 Gy over 5–7 weeks, ≥50 Gy to oral cavity). Excluded: diabetes, salivary gland tumors, renal/hepatic insufficiency, prior irradiation, sepsis, distant metastases.
- **Arms (n=23 each)**: Group I = L-arginine 5 g + maltodextrin 5 g; Group II = glutamine 5 g + maltodextrin 5 g; Group III = maltodextrin 10 g (neutral control). All swish-and-swallow, 30 min before meals, 3×/day, weeks 2–7 of RT.
- **Blinding**: triple-blind (patients, primary investigator, outcomes assessor); central computer-generated randomization, sealed opaque envelopes; identical-appearing treatments.
- **Outcomes** at weeks 2, 5, 7: WHO oral toxicity scale (0–4), Pain-VAS (0–10), Effectiveness Index (no/moderate/marked improvement/healed), BMI, OHIP-14 (Arabic).
- **Statistics**: Kruskal–Wallis / Friedman + Wilcoxon for ordinal; ANOVA / repeated-measures for parametric; pre-specified non-inferiority t-tests (margins: WHO 5, VAS 1.0, BMI 1.0, OHIP-14 3 points; binary −10%).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **WHO scale** (primary mucositis-severity outcome): no group difference at week 2 (p=0.6861); significant by week 5 (p<0.001) and 7. Arginine ≈ glutamine (pairwise NS); both significantly better than maltodextrin (p<0.001). Week-7 WHO stage 0 (complete): arginine 52%, glutamine 43%, maltodextrin 0% (70% of maltodextrin remained stage III). Favorable WHO (0/I) at day 7: arginine 82.6% vs glutamine 69.6% — L-arginine non-inferior (p=0.048).
- **Pain-VAS**: similar at week 2 (5.3/5.17/5.65, p=0.80); diverged at week 5 (4.2/4.3/6.3, p=0.004) and week 7 (1.6/2.4/4.9, p<0.001). Both amino acids better than maltodextrin; arginine ≈ glutamine.
- **Effectiveness Index**: week 7 healed — arginine 56% vs glutamine 13% vs maltodextrin 0% (descriptive difference, non-inferiority confirmed; not powered for superiority).
- **BMI**: maltodextrin dropped 21.56 → 18.93 (p=0.0005); arginine (p=0.87) and glutamine (p=0.170) stable — both prevented RT-associated weight loss.
- **OHIP-14**: arginine and glutamine improved at weeks 5 and 7; maltodextrin worsened (p<0.001). WHO–OHIP-14 correlation r=0.885 (p<0.001). Total radiation dose positively correlated with RIOM severity (p=0.012).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample (23/arm) underpowered for amino-acid-vs-amino-acid superiority; week-7 healing gap (56% vs 13%) was not statistically tested for superiority.
- Short follow-up (acute RT phase only) — long-term mucosal recovery and survival unknown.
- No biochemical/inflammatory-marker validation, so mechanistic claims (NO, cytokine modulation) are inferred.
- Exclusion of systemic comorbidities + single-center design limits generalizability.

## 6. Related Work

- Builds on glutamine RIOM literature (Ibrahim et al.; Nihei et al.; Pathak et al.; Pattanayak et al.) showing glutamine reduces OM frequency/intensity.
- Extends arginine's established wound-healing/anti-inflammatory role (Coburn et al.; Fujiwara et al.; Wittmann et al.) into the radiotherapy mucosal-injury setting.
- Effectiveness Index method drawn from recurrent aphthous stomatitis intervention trials (Rahmani et al.; Zhou et al.).

## 7. Glossary

- **RIOM**: Radiation-induced oral mucositis — inflammation/ulceration of oral mucosa from head-and-neck radiotherapy.
- **WHO oral toxicity scale**: Grade 0 (no lesion) → Grade 4 (oral feeding impossible, parenteral nutrition required).
- **OHIP-14**: Oral Health Impact Profile, 14 items, total 0–56; lower = better oral-health-related quality of life.
- **Effectiveness Index (EI)**: standardized % symptom relief — no (<30%), moderate (30–<70%), marked (70–<95%), healed (≥95%).
- **Non-inferiority margin**: pre-specified clinical tolerance below which a treatment is judged "not clinically worse" than comparator.
- **Maltodextrin**: polysaccharide neutral calorie source used as the non-therapeutic control.
