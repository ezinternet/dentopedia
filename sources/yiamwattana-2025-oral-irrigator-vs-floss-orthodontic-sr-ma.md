## Why Ingested

SR+MA testing whether an oral irrigator / water flosser (added to toothbrushing) beats dental floss for plaque and bleeding control in fixed-orthodontic patients — directly addresses the floss-vs-device interdental-cleaning question in the highest-risk plaque-retention population. Pairs with [[periodontics/liu-2026-powered-manual-toothbrushes-orthodontic-sr-ma]] (toothbrush type in the same orthodontic-hygiene domain) as the "interdental adjunct" arm of the orthodontic oral-hygiene evidence base.

> Note: **abstract-only** — full text not retrieved (no PMC open access). All numbers below are extracted verbatim from the PubMed abstract.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (4 studies eligible, 3 meta-analysed) — adding an oral irrigator to toothbrushing did NOT outperform dental floss for plaque-index (SMD −0.88, 95% CI −2.25 to 0.50, p=0.10, I²=98%) or bleeding-index (SMD −0.90, 95% CI −2.78 to 0.98, p=0.35) reduction in fixed-orthodontic patients; very high heterogeneity, weak evidence.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Direct SR+MA comparison of oral irrigator (OI, water flosser) vs dental floss (DF), both added to toothbrushing, in fixed-orthodontic-appliance patients — a high plaque-retention population where interdental cleaning is hard.
- Quantifies plaque-index (PI) and bleeding-index (BI) reduction as standardised mean difference (SMD).
- Bottom line: OI does NOT outperform DF on either PI or BI; however OI lowered PI and BI from baseline (i.e., it is an effective hygiene aid, just not superior to floss).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Comprehensive multi-database search up to May 7, 2024.
- Inclusion: studies evaluating PI and BI reduction in fixed-orthodontic patients comparing OI vs DF.
- Outcomes pooled as SMD of PI and BI from baseline to study end, random-effects model (DerSimonian–Laird), 95% CI.
- Screening yield: 975 records → 4 articles eligible → 3 studies meta-analysed.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Plaque index (PI)**: OI + toothbrushing vs DF — SMD −0.88 (95% CI −2.25 to 0.50, p=0.10); NOT significant. Very high heterogeneity I²=98%.
- **Bleeding index (BI)**: SMD −0.90 (95% CI −2.78 to 0.98, p=0.35); NOT significant.
- Conclusion: incorporating an OI into regular toothbrushing does not outperform DF for PI/BI reduction in orthodontic patients. OI does, however, lower initial PI and BI from baseline (useful adjunct, not a floss-beater).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 4 eligible studies, 3 pooled — small evidence base.
- Extreme statistical heterogeneity for PI (I²=98%) makes the pooled PI estimate unreliable; weak certainty of evidence.
- Wide CIs crossing the null for both outcomes (CI spans large negative to small positive).
- Abstract-only ingest: risk-of-bias detail, individual-study weights, subgroup/sensitivity analyses, and GRADE rating not captured here.

## 7. Glossary

- **OI (oral irrigator)**: water flosser / dental water jet delivering a pulsating stream to disrupt interdental plaque.
- **DF (dental floss)**: conventional interdental floss.
- **PI (plaque index)** / **BI (bleeding index)**: standard clinical hygiene/inflammation indices.
- **SMD (standardised mean difference)**: effect size pooling outcomes measured on different scales.
- **I²**: proportion of total variability across studies due to heterogeneity rather than chance (98% = extreme).
- **DerSimonian–Laird**: random-effects between-study variance estimator.
