## Why Ingested

기존 [[occlusion/velasquez-2022-occlusal-analysis-natural-dentition-sr]]는 T-Scan vs 교합지(articulating paper)의 신뢰도·재현성을 다뤘으나, 디지털 워크플로의 또 다른 축인 구강스캐너(IOS) 교합도(occlusogram)가 교합지와 일치하는지는 다루지 않았다. 본 임상 일치도 연구(Didier 2026, n=35)는 iTero occlusogram vs 8µm AccuFilm을 직접 비교해 κ 0.07–0.20 (poor agreement)을 보고 — IOS 단독으로 교합을 결정하면 안 되며 두 방법은 보완적이라는 근거를 추가해 velasquez 종합을 디지털 IOS 축으로 확장한다. 투명교정(ClinCheck) 마무리 시 후방 개교합(open bite) 위험과 직결되는 임상 함의.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective clinical agreement study (n=35 orthodontic patients, 840 teeth) finding poor agreement (Cohen kappa 0.07–0.20) between iTero Element 2 occlusogram and 8 µm AccuFilm carbon-paper occlusal records — the two methods are complementary, not interchangeable, and IOS alone cannot define occlusion.

## 한줄요약

후향적 임상 일치도 연구(교정 환자 35명, 치아 840개): iTero Element 2 구강스캐너 교합도와 8µm 카본지(AccuFilm) 교합 기록은 일치도가 낮아(카파 0.07–0.20) 서로 교환 불가하며 보완적으로 함께 써야 함 — IOS 단독으로 교합을 결정할 수 없다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Head-to-head IOS-vs-carbon agreement at the tooth-surface level.** Quantifies concordance between iTero Element 2 occlusogram and 8 µm AccuFilm carbon paper across 16 defined tooth surfaces, using each of 840 teeth (35 patients) as an independent sampling unit.
2. **Poor agreement is the headline.** Cohen kappa 0.07–0.20 on most surfaces (Landis & Koch "poor/none"); only LC of premolars (κ 0.38) and MLC of first molars (κ 0.27) reach "slight" when red+orange both count as contact.
3. **Resolves the "red vs orange" question for clinicians.** iTero red+orange overestimates contacts (~70% more than carbon); red-only underestimates vs carbon. Hypothesis: orange (0.1–0.2 mm proximity) likely represents *non-contact*, and 48.1% of "intense" red contacts are clinically ideal — cautioning against ClinCheck "overcorrection" / unnecessary posterior intrusion.
4. **Clinical bottom line**: IOS occlusogram alone cannot define the occlusal relationship; combine IOS with carbon paper.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Retrospective, single-center, paired within-patient comparison (each patient/tooth recorded by both methods).
- **Sample**: 35 patients (23 men, 12 women), 15–30 y, Class I malocclusion, moderate crowding 3–5 mm, no extractions; exclusions: missing permanent teeth, prior open bite/crossbite/orthodontic treatment. 840 teeth = sampling units.
- **Recording**: Seated at 45°, max habitual intercuspation at maximum intensity, occlusal surfaces air-dried. Carbon first (8 µm AccuFilm, single operator), then IOS (iTero Element 2, two trained operators, full-arch). Both transferred to occlusograms.
- **IOS contact definition**: colour scale red→dark blue by inter-surface distance; only **red (0.0–0.1 mm)** and **orange (0.1–0.2 mm)** counted as contact; analyzed three ways (red+orange / red only / orange only).
- **Surfaces (16)**: molar MBC/DBC/MLC/DLC/CG; premolar BC/LC/CG/MMR/DMR; canine MLS/DLS/TC; incisor MS/DS/IE.
- **Statistics**: SPSS v28; % agreement/disagreement + Cohen kappa (Landis & Koch interpretation). Mean κ 0.14; margin of error ±0.17 (canines/first molars, n=140) and ±0.12 (incisors/premolars, n=280); true κ ~0.02–0.26 (95% CI).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Total contacts**: carbon 917; scanning 1548 (red 745 = 48.1%, orange 803 = 51.9%).
- **Distribution by tooth (scan total / carbon)**: incisors 350/159; canines 157/133; premolars 597/365; first molar 444/260. Most contacts posterior (expected for stable occlusion).
- **Red-region prevalence** higher in incisors (60.3%), canines (54.8%), premolars (52.1%); **orange predominates in molars (69.1%)**.
- **Kappa (red+orange = contact)**: most surfaces 0.07–0.20 (poor). Best: premolar LC 0.38, molar MLC 0.27 (slight). Worst: premolar CG 0.04, canine MLS 0.06.
- **Kappa (red only = contact)**: same poor trend. Best: premolar LC 0.26, incisor DS 0.21, premolar BC 0.21. Premolar CG κ = −0.03 (worse than chance).
- **Magnitude**: scan red+orange ≈ +70% contacts vs carbon; scan red-only < carbon for canines/premolars/molars.
- **Accuracy gap**: articulating paper detects ~8–10 µm (matches patient occlusal perception threshold); iTero red minimum distance ~100 µm → potential ~10-fold lower accuracy.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single scanner model (iTero Element 2) — not generalizable to other IOS systems.
- Teeth with restorative materials not excluded (young cohort, mostly sound/small composite — impact judged minimal).
- Retrospective design precluded intra-examiner reliability testing (no repeat records possible).
- No defined gold standard for occlusal registration in the literature; carbon paper itself has operator variability and is an interposed object.
- Future: prospective, multi-scanner, standardized studies; possibly an intermediate IOS contact category (between red and orange) and between ClinCheck red/green.

## 6. Related Work

- **Reich et al.** — digital systems (T-Scan) underestimate contact number vs conventional, but higher reproducibility (esp. anterior).
- **Wei et al.** — 3D protocol; IOS consistency ~3× conventional impressions, improved occlusal tightness, occasional virtual intersections.
- **Rovira-Lastra et al.** — silicone transillumination reproducibility >85%; articulating films, virtual occlusion, T-Scan only moderate validity/reproducibility.
- **Landis & Koch** — kappa interpretation scale used.

## 7. Glossary

- **IOS** — Intraoral scanner (here iTero Element 2, Align Technology).
- **Occlusogram** — 2D map of occlusal contact points transferred from carbon or scan.
- **AccuFilm 8 µm** — thin articulating foil (carbon paper) used as the conventional reference.
- **Cohen kappa (κ)** — chance-corrected agreement; Landis & Koch: <0 none, 0.01–0.20 slight/poor, 0.21–0.40 fair, etc.
- **Red / orange (iTero)** — colour-coded inter-arch distance: red 0.0–0.1 mm, orange 0.1–0.2 mm.
- **ClinCheck** — Align Technology aligner planning software using the virtual occlusion model.
- **MIP** — maximum (habitual) intercuspation, the recording position.
