## One-line Summary

Feasibility study (Scotland) of ENGAGE, a <5-minute chairside intervention that communicates lifestyle-risk information (smoking/alcohol/diet) and signposts to free NHS helplines — patients overwhelmingly accept being asked (<10% would be offended), and 17/18 participating GDPs agreed it is deliverable in a routine check-up and an improvement over current practice.

## 한줄요약

스코틀랜드 ENGAGE 실행가능성 연구 — 정기검진 중 5분 이내로 생활습관(흡연·음주·식이) 위험을 알리고 무료 NHS 상담전화로 연결하는 개입. 환자 거부감 거의 없음(<10%만 불쾌), 참여 GDP 18명 중 17명이 "검진 중 실행 가능하고 현행보다 개선"이라 평가.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: British Dental Journal 2018;225(7):645–(October 12 2018)
- DOI: 10.1038/sj.bdj.2018.809
- Study type: Mixed-methods feasibility study (patient survey + GDP workshop/audit/survey)
- Setting: Scottish NHS dental primary care; aligned with UK opportunistic-health-promotion guidance
- Intervention: ENGAGE — communicate risk info → take-home handout signposting to free NHS lifestyle counselling helpline; designed for <5 min in routine check-up

## 2. Key Contributions
- Tests acceptance (not yet effectiveness) of expanding the dental visit beyond oral hygiene to general lifestyle-risk counseling.
- Provides a low-burden delivery model (risk-communication + signposting) rather than full behavior-change counseling, making it sustainable in busy practice.
- Quantifies the perceived-offense barrier that clinicians often assume blocks such conversations — and finds it small.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Study 1 (patients): N=1000 adults randomly selected from NHS database invited to online questionnaire; 200 completed (52% male; 90% visited dentist in prior 12 months).
- Study 2 (GDPs): 50 invited to a training workshop (first 20 enrolled); implement with next 20 adult check-up patients, audit, then survey. 18 GDPs across 9/14 health boards delivered to 335 patients (~18 each).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Patients: <15% recalled being asked about smoking/alcohol/diet at last check-up; <10% would be embarrassed/offended if asked; >70% would be reassured by the professionalism; <4% offended by receiving an NHS helpline leaflet.
- GDPs: 17/18 agreed ENGAGE is deliverable during a check-up, an improvement on current practice, and may change what patients think/feel/do about health risk.
- Conclusion: ENGAGE is feasible in Scottish dental primary care; to be developed and tested for effectiveness in a multi-centre UK trial.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Feasibility/acceptance only — no patient health outcomes measured.
- Self-selected GDPs (early adopters) and modest patient survey completion (200/1000) → optimism bias.
- Effectiveness for actual smoking/alcohol/diet change remains untested (the planned UK trial).

## 7. Glossary
- **Opportunistic health promotion**: using an unrelated clinical visit to raise general health risks.
- **Signposting**: directing patients to existing external services rather than delivering counseling in-chair.
- **GDP**: General Dental Practitioner.
