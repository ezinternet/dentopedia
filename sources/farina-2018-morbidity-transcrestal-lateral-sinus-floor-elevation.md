## One-line Summary
RCT (29 tSFE vs 28 lSFE, RBH 3-6 mm, xenograft + simultaneous implant): **lSFE = lower pain day 0, tSFE = lower swelling/bruising/nasal discharge** through 14 days. tSFE has overall more tolerable postoperative course.

## 1. Document Information
RCT registered on ClinicalTrials.gov (NCT02415946). Multi-center Italian study, JCPE. Direct head-to-head comparison of two sinus augmentation approaches with patient-reported morbidity outcomes.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First RCT directly comparing **patient morbidity** between tSFE and lSFE
- Both techniques used **same xenograft** and **simultaneous implant placement** to isolate the approach variable
- VAS pain trajectory (day 0 → 14)
- Functional impact metrics (swallowing, eating, speaking, work/school)

## 3. Methodology
- Enrollment: ≥1 edentulous maxillary posterior site with RBH 3-6 mm
- tSFE: xenograft + collagen matrix + simultaneous implant
- lSFE: xenograft + membrane covering antrostomy + simultaneous implant
- Postoperative questionnaires
- VAS_pain on 100 mm visual analogue scale

## 4. Key Results
- Day 0 (surgery day): VAS pain significantly **higher for tSFE** vs lSFE
- Day 1-14: VAS pain **similar** between groups
- **tSFE significantly lower** incidence of swelling, bruising, nasal discharge/bleeding
- **tSFE significantly less severe** limitation in swallowing, daily activities, eating, speaking, mouth opening, work/school (at specific intervals)

## 5. Limitations
- Single moderate sample size (29 + 28)
- RBH 3-6 mm range may not generalize to <3 mm or >6 mm
- Same xenograft used — graft variable not tested
- Short follow-up for morbidity (14 days)

## 7. Glossary
- **tSFE**: transcrestal sinus floor elevation
- **lSFE**: lateral sinus floor elevation
- **VAS**: Visual Analogue Scale
- **RBH**: Residual bone height
