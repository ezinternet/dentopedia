## Why Ingested

One of the two definitive human SRs (with Van der Weijden 2009) establishing the natural post-extraction ridge-change baseline — the "do-nothing" reference (horizontal 3.79 mm loss) against which ARP benefit is judged in [[overviews/socket-preservation-arp-overview]], and the quantitative anchor for the resorption biology discussed in [[overviews/bone-regeneration-socket-biology-and-arp-critique]].

## One-line Summary
Systematic review (20 human studies) establishing weighted-mean post-extraction ridge dimensional changes: horizontal reduction (3.79 ± 0.23 mm) substantially exceeds vertical reduction (buccal 1.24 ± 0.11 mm, mesial 0.84 ± 0.62 mm, distal 0.80 ± 0.71 mm) at 6 months; soft tissue gains 0.4–0.5 mm thickness; horizontal change 29–63% vs vertical 11–22% of baseline.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Clinical Oral Implants Research 2012;23(Suppl 5):1–21
- **Companion EAO consensus paper** to the 5th EAO Consensus Workshop (alongside Vignoletti 2012 on ARP protocols)
- **Institution**: University of Hong Kong (Tan, Wong) and University of Bern (Lang)
- **Study type**: Systematic review with descriptive synthesis and weighted means (no formal pooled MA)

## 2. Key Contributions
- One of the **two definitive human SRs** (with Van der Weijden 2009) anchoring the modern quantitative consensus on natural post-extraction ridge change in humans.
- Reports **hard tissue, soft tissue, and percentage change** in one harmonized dataset — earlier SRs covered hard tissue only.
- Explicit time-dependence: 3 months and 6 months separately tabulated.
- Includes RCTs and prospective cohorts only; sites left to heal spontaneously (no ARP, no implant).

## 3. Methodology
- MEDLINE + CENTRAL electronic search, supplemented by hand search of relevant dental journals.
- Inclusion: RCTs and prospective cohorts in humans reporting undisturbed post-extraction dimensional change relative to a fixed reference point.
- Two independent reviewers extracted data.
- Weighted means and percentages calculated descriptively (heterogeneity precluded formal pooled MA).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Search yield**: 3,954 titles → 238 abstracts → 104 full-text → 20 studies included.
- **Hard tissue at 6 months (absolute change)**:
  - Horizontal: −3.79 ± 0.23 mm
  - Vertical buccal: −1.24 ± 0.11 mm
  - Vertical mesial: −0.84 ± 0.62 mm
  - Vertical distal: −0.80 ± 0.71 mm
- **Percentage change**:
  - Vertical: 11–22% at 6 months
  - Horizontal: 32% at 3 months, 29–63% at 6–7 months
- **Soft tissue**: 0.4–0.5 mm gain in thickness at 6 months
- Greatest horizontal loss occurs on the buccal aspect, consistent with bundle-bone resorption.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Substantial heterogeneity prevented pooled meta-analysis (only weighted means).
- Most included studies measured a heterogeneous mix of tooth types (anterior vs posterior, single vs multiple sockets) → site-specific estimates remain uncertain.
- Soft tissue measurements scarce and inconsistently performed.
- Outcomes beyond 12 months not addressed.

## 7. Glossary
- **Buccolingual / horizontal dimension**: width measured perpendicular to the dental arch.
- **Crestal / vertical dimension**: height from a fixed apical reference to the crestal bone level.
- **Bundle bone**: tooth-dependent bone forming the inner socket wall; resorbs after extraction since it loses its functional role (PDL anchorage).
