## Why Ingested

This is one of the foundational early-2000s RFA studies that defined protocol-specific ISQ-at-placement (ISQi) cut-offs for osseointegration — a higher threshold for immediately loaded (IL, ≥54) than for delayed-loaded (DL, ≥49) implants — and described the now-classic ISQ-band-dependent stability trajectory (low-ISQi implants rise, mid-band 60–69 implants dip then recover, high-ISQi >69 dip early then stay stable). It anchors the historical/threshold basis for our newer ISQ pages. Extends and provides the early-evidence foundation for [[implants/isq/lee-2011-isq-loading-protocol-prediction]] (ISQ as a loading-protocol predictor) and reinforces the primary-stability → loading-decision logic explored for fresh sockets in [[implants/isq/bavetta-2019-insertion-torque-isq-immediate-loading-extraction-socket]].

## One-line Summary

Prospective clinical RFA study (36 patients, 106 ITI SLA implants: 63 IL + 43 DL) finding that Osstell RFA could NOT reliably detect mobile implants, but all DL implants with ISQi ≥49 and all IL implants with ISQi ≥54 achieved and maintained osseointegration at 1 year, with stability trajectories during healing depending on the ISQi band.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Protocol-specific ISQi cut-off thresholds for predicting osseointegration**: after 1 year of loading, all DL implants with ISQi ≥49 and all IL implants with ISQi ≥54 achieved and maintained osseointegration — the IL threshold is higher than the DL threshold.
2. **RFA is NOT a reliable diagnostic tool for detecting mobile implants** — a key negative finding qualifying enthusiasm for Osstell as a chairside mobility detector.
3. **Implant stability could nonetheless be reliably determined for implants displaying ISQ ≥47.**
4. **ISQi-band-dependent stability trajectory during healing** (see Results) — different ISQi bands follow different rise/dip/recover patterns over the first 8–12 weeks.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: prospective clinical study comparing two loading protocols.
- **Groups**:
  - **Immediately loaded (IL)**: 18 patients, 63 IL implants.
  - **Delayed loaded (DL)**: 18 patients, 43 DL implants (loaded after 3 months).
  - Total: 36 patients, 106 ITI SLA implants.
- **Measurement**: ISQ recorded with Osstell (RFA) at implant placement (ISQi) and after 1, 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, and 12 weeks.
- **Aims** (per abstract): (1) evaluate Osstell as a diagnostic tool able to differentiate stable vs mobile ITI implants; (2) evaluate a cut-off threshold ISQ at placement (ISQitv) predictive of osseointegration; (3) compare the predictive ISQitv of IL vs DL implants.
- **Outcome**: 1-year loading control / clinical osseointegration status.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Survival**: all implants passed the 1-year loading control. Two implants failed:
  - 1 DL implant with ISQ at placement (ISQi) of **48**.
  - 1 IL implant with ISQi of **53**.
- **RFA as mobility detector**: the RFA method was **not a reliable diagnostic tool to identify mobile implants**.
- **Reliable determination**: implant stability **could be reliably determined for implants displaying ISQ ≥47**.
- **Protocol-specific osseointegration thresholds (1 year)**:
  - All **DL implants with ISQi ≥49** achieved and maintained osseointegration.
  - All **IL implants with ISQi ≥54** achieved and maintained osseointegration.
- **Stability trajectory by ISQi band (by end of 3 months)**:
  - **ISQi < 60**: increase of stability during healing.
  - **ISQi 60–69**: stability decreased during ~8 weeks before returning to initial values (early dip then recovery).
  - **ISQi > 69**: stability decreased during the first ~4 weeks before remaining stable.
- **Clinical implication (authors)**: although preliminary, these data might orient the practitioner to choose among various loading protocols and to selectively monitor implants during the healing phase.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Authors describe the data as **preliminary**.
- Single implant system/surface (ITI SLA) and single RFA device (Osstell) — generalizability to other systems/surfaces is unestablished.
- The two failures sit just below the proposed thresholds (DL 48 vs threshold 49; IL 53 vs threshold 54), so thresholds are derived from a small number of failure events.
- Key negative finding: RFA cannot reliably flag a mobile implant, limiting its use as a standalone mobility test.

## 7. Glossary

- **RFA (Resonance-Frequency Analysis)**: non-invasive method that quantifies implant stability via the resonance frequency of a transducer attached to the implant.
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: 0–100 scale output of RFA (Osstell); higher = stiffer implant–bone interface.
- **ISQi (ISQ at implant placement / at insertion)**: the ISQ recorded at the moment of placement; a proxy for primary (mechanical) stability.
- **ISQitv (cut-off threshold ISQ value)**: the placement-time ISQ threshold the study evaluates as predictive of osseointegration.
- **IL (Immediately Loaded)**: implants restored/loaded immediately after placement.
- **DL (Delayed Loaded)**: implants loaded after a healing period (here, 3 months).
- **SLA (Sandblasted, Large-grit, Acid-etched)**: ITI implant surface treatment promoting accelerated osseointegration.
- **Osstell**: commercial RFA device producing the ISQ value.
- **Osseointegration**: direct structural and functional connection between living bone and the implant surface.
