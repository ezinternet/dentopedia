## Why Ingested

기존 [[cracked-tooth/ferracane-2023-cracked-tooth-registry-3year-network]] (NDPBRN crack registry)는 외부에서 보이는 균열(externally visible crack)만 포함해 표본이 편향됐다는 한계가 있었는데, 본 Renner 2025 cross-sectional은 외부 가시 균열을 inclusion 조건에서 빼고 수복물 제거 후 발견된 균열(55.1%가 제거 후에야 보임)까지 포착해 그 한계를 직접 보완·확장한다. 또한 [[cracked-tooth/raj-2025-cracked-tooth-syndrome-diagnostic-dilemma]]·[[cracked-tooth/li-2021-cracked-tooth-syndrome-etiology-review]]가 다루는 "균열치 진단의 어려움(증상 가변성·bite test 신뢰성)"에 일반 개원가 실제 데이터(균열의 약 55%가 무증상, 절반이 bite test 음성)를 제공해 임상 의사결정 근거를 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional practice-based study (147 patient records, single Australian general practice, 2014–2017): most cracked posterior teeth are asymptomatic (55.1%) and not visible before restoration removal (55.1%); cold sensitivity is associated with unrestored marginal ridges (OR 2.89), pre-removal-visible cracks (OR 3.04) and combined body+cusp cracks (OR 3.11), while stained cracks are inversely associated (OR 0.30); nearly half (51.7%) of cracked teeth gave a negative bite test.

## 한줄요약

단면 개원가 연구 (호주 일반치과 1곳 환자기록 147건, 2014–2017): 균열치의 55.1%가 무증상이고 55.1%는 수복물 제거 전엔 균열이 안 보였다. 한랭 민감성(cold sensitivity)은 비수복 변연융선(OR 2.89)·제거 전 가시 균열(OR 3.04)·체부+교두 복합균열(OR 3.11)과 연관됐고 착색 균열은 역연관(OR 0.30). 균열치의 절반(51.7%)은 bite test 음성 — 전문의 진료 문헌과 다른 일반 개원가 양상을 보여줌.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Captures cracks invisible before restoration removal.** Unlike the NDPBRN crack studies (~3000 teeth, all with ≥1 externally visible crack), this study did NOT require an external crack for inclusion. 55.1% of cracks were only visible AFTER removing the restoration — a population missed by prior network studies and likely underreported.
2. **Confirms most cracked teeth in general practice are asymptomatic (55.1%)**, matching NDPBRN (~55%) and contrasting sharply with the symptomatic, complex presentations reported in specialist endodontic literature.
3. **Quantifies cold-sensitivity and bite-test predictors** via logistic regression (marginal ridge status, crack visibility, body+cusp location, staining, tooth type, pain on biting).
4. **Clinical message**: after routine restoration replacement — especially in asymptomatic teeth — comprehensively examine remaining tooth structure (cavity floor AND base of cusps) to rule out cracks.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: retrospective cross-sectional, convenience sample of consecutive patient records (2014–2017), single Australian general dental practice; all examined by the same practitioner with 5.5× loupes + magnification light.
- **Crack definition**: thin surface disruption of enamel/dentin (± cementum) of unknown depth/extension. Cuspal crack (runs under a cusp), body crack (runs along middle, BL or MD), intersecting cracks (joining). Often categorised after restoration removal + pulp-floor and cuspal-base examination, aided by staged pre-/intraoperative macro photography.
- **Tests**: pulp sensibility = Frost Bite refrigerant on #2 cotton pellet, mid-buccal (no CO2 ice). Bite test = Tooth Slooth loading individual cusps; pain on release = positive.
- **Exclusions**: endodontically treated, split teeth, fractured cusps, vertical root fractures, anterior teeth, external cracks with no dentin extension, trauma, necrotic pulp, symptomatic irreversible pulpitis, periradicular pathosis.
- **Sample**: 152 records → 5 excluded (incomplete) → **147 analysed**.
- **Variables**: patient-specific (age, gender, symptoms, periodontal), tooth-specific (occlusion, excursive guidance, wear, tooth type/jaw, restoration type, crack visibility, pulp/bite tests, opposing tooth), crack-specific (direction, number, location, staining).
- **Analysis**: Jamovi. Descriptive stats + chi-square (symptom status). Stepwise model building; binomial and multinomial logistic regression. p<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Patient / tooth / crack descriptives**
- Mean age 62.3 (±10) y; 51.7% >60 y. Female 57.1%.
- Asymptomatic 55.1%. Symptomatic (n=66): thermal sensitivity 40% (27/66), pain on biting 33% (22/66), combined 25% (17/66).
- Most common tooth: mandibular 2nd molar (23.8%); molars 80.3% vs premolars 19.7%.
- Amalgam restoration 69.4% (single-surface occlusal most common); composite 23.8%.
- Cracks not visible before removal 55.1%. Cold test normal 94.6%. Bite test negative 51.7%.
- Crack direction: both MD+BL 50.3%, MD alone 48.3%, BL alone 1.4%. Single 44.2%; intersecting 27.9%. Body 72.8%, body+cusp 23.8%, standalone cusp 3.4%. Stained 46.3%.

**Symptom status (chi-square)** — significantly more symptomatic: age <60 y (52.4% vs 35.7%, p=0.04); crack visible before removal (57.6% vs 30.9%, p<0.001); unstained cracks (50.6% vs 33.8%, p=0.04); body & cusp location (60.0% vs 37.5%, p=0.01).

**Cold sensitivity (binomial LR)**: unrestored marginal ridge OR 2.89 (1.06–7.82); crack visible before removal OR 3.04 (1.14–8.12); body & cusp crack OR 3.11 (1.13–8.54); stained crack OR 0.30 (0.11–0.74, inverse). Antagonist status NS (OR 2.31, p=0.09).

**Positive bite test (binomial LR)**: 51.7% negative overall. Molar vs premolar OR 3.71 (1.16–11.78); pain on biting OR 36.96 (7.79–175.34); crack visible before removal OR 3.44 (1.49–7.92). Multiple cracks NS (OR 2.24, p=0.06).

**Crack number/type (multinomial LR)**: intersecting (vs single) — molar OR 8.79 (1.83–42.10), amalgam OR 4.81 (1.68–13.78); multiple (vs single) — mandibular jaw OR 0.40 (0.16–0.95, inverse).

**Location body & cusp (binomial LR)**: restored marginal ridge OR 3.22 (1.18–8.77); crack visible before removal OR 3.27 (1.40–7.63).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Cross-sectional, no control group → limited external validity (associations only, no causation).
- Predominantly restoration-associated cracks; unrestored teeth only 6.8% of sample (and mixed intact + unrestored carious due to low numbers). This restoration vs unrestored distinction is offered as the explanation for divergent symptom reports across the literature.
- No CO2 ice for pulp testing; no transillumination for crack detection.
- Single practitioner, single practice, convenience sample.
- Strength vs NDPBRN: external crack NOT an inclusion criterion → arguably a more realistic general-practice sample.

## 6. Related Work

- **NDPBRN crack registry** ([[cracked-tooth/ferracane-2023-cracked-tooth-registry-3year-network]]): both report ~55% asymptomatic and cold sensitivity as predominant symptom; Renner extends NDPBRN by capturing cracks invisible until restoration removal (NDPBRN required external visibility) and reports a lower combined-symptom prevalence (25% vs 35%).
- **Cracked tooth syndrome diagnosis** ([[cracked-tooth/raj-2025-cracked-tooth-syndrome-diagnostic-dilemma]]): provides empirical general-practice data on the diagnostic difficulty CTS reviews describe (symptom variability, ~half negative bite test).
- **CTS etiology/diagnosis framework** ([[cracked-tooth/li-2021-cracked-tooth-syndrome-etiology-review]]): supplies clinical observational counterpart to the narrative etiology framework (marginal ridge integrity, cuspal deflection, amalgam association).

## 7. Glossary

- **Body crack**: crack running along the middle of the tooth (buccolingual or mesiodistal), seen on the pulp floor.
- **Cuspal crack**: crack running under a cusp; visible only on specific examination of the cusp base.
- **Intersecting cracks**: multiple cracks that join one another.
- **Bite test (Tooth Slooth)**: individual cusps loaded; pain on RELEASE = positive.
- **NDPBRN**: National Dental Practice-Based Research Network (US crack-study series, externally-visible-crack inclusion).
- **LTM / algoneuron**: A-delta low threshold mechanoreceptors in the dentine-pulp complex stimulated by light pressure/vibration/air.
- **Thermo-TRP**: thermosensitive transient receptor potential ion channels implicated in temperature hyperalgesia.
- **Cuspal deflection / cuspal isolation**: loss of marginal ridge or body+cusp cracking allows cusps to flex; isolated cusps deflect maximally (Panitvisai & Messer).
