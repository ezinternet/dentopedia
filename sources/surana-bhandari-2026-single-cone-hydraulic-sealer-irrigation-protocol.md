## Why Ingested

PubMed 감시(one-cone technique) — Camilleri 그룹이 단일콘+hydraulic cement sealer에서 **세정 프로토콜(HEDP 지속적 킬레이션)이 dentin-sealer 계면과 항균성을 좌우**함을 보임. 단일콘 클러스터([[endodontics/elmsmari-2025-single-cone-bioceramic-clinical-success-sr-ma]], [[endodontics/song-2022-sealer-based-obturation-epoxy-calcium-silicate-rct]])가 다루지 않은 "충전 전 세정이 봉쇄·생체활성에 미치는 영향" 축을 추가한다.

## One-line Summary

Split-tooth in-vitro study (4 sealers × 3 irrigation sequences) — continuous chelation with HEDP caused less dentin erosion, a larger mineral zone at the hydraulic-cement/dentin interface, and improved sealer antimicrobial properties; all sealers lost antimicrobial efficacy after contact with irrigated dentin.

## 한줄요약

split-tooth in-vitro(실러 4종 × 세정 3종) — HEDP 지속적 킬레이션은 dentin 침식을 줄이고 hydraulic cement/dentin 계면의 미네랄 영역을 넓히며 실러 항균성을 향상; 모든 실러는 세정된 dentin 접촉 후 항균 효과가 감소.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Frames single-cone + hydraulic-cement obturation as a **system** (preparation × irrigation × obturation), not just sealer choice.
- Identifies **HEDP continuous chelation** as the irrigation that best preserves dentin and the mineralizing interface and boosts sealer antimicrobial action.
- Cautions that **irrigated dentin reduces every sealer's antimicrobial effectiveness** — a contact-dependent effect relevant to all single-cone bioceramic protocols.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Split-tooth model; 3 irrigation sequences: NaOCl, EDTA, HEDP (water control).
- 4 sealers: AH Plus, BioRoot RCS, BioRoot Flow, TotalFill; single-cone obturation.
- Interface ultrastructure/chemistry by SEM, EDS, FTIR before/after obturation and irrigation contact.
- Microbiology: direct contact test + minimum inhibitory concentration, with/without dentin contact. Two-way ANOVA + Tukey (p<0.05).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **HEDP**: less dentin erosion; larger mineral concentration zone at hydraulic-cement/dentin interface; improved antimicrobial properties of hydraulic-cement sealers (continuous chelation).
- **All sealers**: reduced antimicrobial effectiveness after contact with irrigated dentin.
- Conclusion: irrigation protocol selection materially affects interfacial characteristics and antimicrobial behavior → must be matched to the obturation system.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro split-tooth; no clinical outcomes. abstract-only ingest (quantitative interface/MIC values not extracted).
- Does not isolate single-cone fit quality independent of irrigation.

## 7. Glossary

- **Hydraulic cement sealer**: water-setting calcium-silicate sealer used as the bulk of single-cone obturation.
- **HEDP (etidronate)**: weak chelator mixed with NaOCl for "continuous chelation" — simultaneous deproteinization + mild demineralization without strong dentin erosion.
- **Continuous chelation**: single-step NaOCl+HEDP irrigation vs sequential NaOCl→EDTA.
- **DCT / MIC**: direct contact test / minimum inhibitory concentration (antimicrobial assays).
- **Split-tooth model**: tooth halved to expose the dentin–sealer interface for analysis.
