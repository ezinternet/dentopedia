## Why Ingested

Existing interdental-cleaning RCTs in the wiki (e.g. [[interdental-cleaning/mancinelli-lyle-2024-water-flosser-vs-interdental-brush-rct]]) compare device *efficacy* over short 2–4 week windows, but the limiting factor in primary prevention is whether patients keep using the device at all. This is the first RCT to directly compare the long-term *sustainability of regular use* (adherence over 24 weeks) of rubber interdental picks vs dental floss, reinforcing the overview thesis that real-world adherence — not intrinsic plaque-removal efficacy — drives interdental-cleaning outcomes.

## One-line Summary

Examiner-blinded RCT (n=54 completers, 24 weeks, periodontally healthy adults): rubber interdental picks were used regularly (≥3 days/week) for a mean 12.61/24 weeks vs 4.96/24 weeks for dental floss (p=0.003); plaque/bleeding scores were similar between groups but negatively correlated with the number of weeks of regular use. Note: "sustainability" here means adherence/consistency of use over time, NOT environmental sustainability.

## 한줄요약

검사자 맹검 무작위대조시험(RCT, n=54, 24주, 치주 건강 성인): 고무 치간 픽(rubber interdental pick)이 주 3일 이상 규칙적으로 사용된 주수는 평균 12.61/24주로 치실(dental floss) 4.96/24주보다 유의하게 높았다(p=0.003). 플라크·출혈 점수는 두 군이 비슷했으나 규칙적 사용 주수와 음의 상관관계를 보였다. 여기서 'sustainability(지속성)'는 환경적 지속가능성이 아니라 시간에 따른 사용 순응도/일관성을 의미한다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Sustainability of regular use of interdental cleaning tools in periodontally healthy individuals
- **Authors**: Mert Yılmaz, Timur Köse, Nurcan Buduneli (Ege University, Turkey)
- **Journal**: Journal of Periodontology, 2025; 96(11):1247–1256
- **DOI**: 10.1002/JPER.24-0551 · **PMID**: 40444858 · **PMCID**: PMC12671690
- **Study type**: Randomized, parallel-design, examiner-blinded RCT, 6-month (24-week) follow-up
- **Funding/COI**: Authors declare no conflicts of interest and no commercial relations.

> **Terminology caution**: "Sustainability" in this paper = the **number of weeks of regular use** (adherence/consistency of use over 24 weeks). It does NOT refer to environmental/ecological sustainability (a separate body of work, e.g. Abed 2022).

## 2. Key Contributions

- **First** RCT to directly compare the *sustainability of regular use* (adherence over time) of two interdental cleaning tools — rubber interdental picks vs dental floss — in periodontally healthy individuals as primary prevention.
- Reframes interdental-cleaning evidence away from short-term plaque-removal *efficacy* (the focus of prior 2–4 week RCTs) toward **long-term real-world adherence**, the actual rate-limiting factor in prevention.
- Deliberately mimics real life with **infrequent recalls (only weeks 8 and 24)** to minimize the Hawthorne effect that inflates home-care quality in studies with frequent visits.
- Demonstrates that within both groups, **more weeks of regular use correlate with lower plaque and bleeding** — quantifying the dose–response of adherence even in already-healthy mouths.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Randomized (coin toss by an off-study researcher), parallel two-arm, examiner-blinded RCT; 24-week follow-up.
- **Setting**: Ege University School of Dentistry; participants recruited among patients seeking non-periodontal dental treatment.
- **Power analysis**: Independent two-group Mann–Whitney test (2-sided), large effect size (d=0.8), α=0.05, 80% power → minimum 52 (26+26); 60 enrolled (30/group) to allow dropouts.
- **Participants** (inclusion): aged 18–70, ≥20 teeth with ≥4 sufficiently sized interdental areas per quadrant (open contacts/diastema allowed), manual toothbrush user, healthy periodontium, baseline full-mouth plaque & bleeding <15%.
- **Key exclusions**: systemic disease affecting oral tissues, gingival-hyperplasia drugs, prophylactic-antibiotic need, keratinized gingiva <2 mm, ortho appliances/dentures/overhangs, fractured/malposed teeth, periodontal history, prior regular interdental device use. **Dentists/dental students excluded** (they know how to use the tools).
- **Interventions**:
  - **Rubber interdental pick** group (GUM rubber interdental pick, small) — n=28 completers
  - **Dental floss** group (GUM dental floss) — n=26 completers
  - Both instructed to clean interdentally ≥once/day, brush twice daily with Modified Bass (medium-hard brush, fluoride paste). Standardized 10-min verbal instruction by one researcher (N.B.).
- **Adherence tracking**: participants received a **calendar** (dentition divided into 4 zones) to mark each use; recalled at weeks 8 and 24 and asked to **bring back the used-tool waste** to corroborate self-report.
- **Definitions**: **Regular use** = using the tool **≥3 days/week** (based on Cepeda/Marchesan literature). **Sustainability** = number of weeks meeting regular-use criterion.
- **Outcomes**: Primary = regular & sustainable use over 24 weeks. Secondary = full-mouth dichotomous plaque index (PI) and bleeding on probing (BOP) percentages at 4 sites/tooth (single calibrated blinded examiner, kappa >0.85), plaque-disclosing tablet, Williams probe.
- **Statistics**: SPSS v25 + R; Shapiro–Wilk (normality rejected → nonparametric); Brunner–Langer F1-LD-F1 / LD-F1 for repeated measures with Bonferroni; Mann–Whitney, chi-square/Fisher, Spearman correlation. α=0.05 two-sided.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Flow**: 60 enrolled → 54 analyzed (5 no-shows, 1 excluded for pregnancy). 28 rubber pick, 26 floss.
- **Baseline**: groups similar in sex, smoking, PI (~11.6%), BOP (~8.9–9.0%). Rubber-pick group **significantly older** (43.54 vs 34.15 yr, p=0.044) — a confounder favoring picks (older patients may handle picks more easily than floss).
- **Sustainability (primary outcome)**:
  - **8 weeks**: 5.14/8 weeks (rubber pick) vs 3.19/8 (floss), p=0.037.
  - **24 weeks**: **12.61/24 weeks (rubber pick) vs 4.96/24 (floss), p=0.003** — picks used regularly more than 2.5× as long as floss.
  - Neither tool showed a flat line; both fluctuated, but the number of participants reporting regular use stayed higher in the rubber-pick group throughout.
- **Clinical parameters (PI/BOP)**:
  - Between-group PI and BOP were **similar at every timepoint** (the study was not powered/designed to separate clinical efficacy in already-healthy patients; changes were "numerically minimal").
  - Rubber pick: BOP fell significantly T0→T8 (p<0.001), then no significant T0→T24 change (status maintained).
  - Floss: BOP rose significantly T8→T24 and T0→T24 (p<0.05 / p<0.001) — interpreted as declining application quality, consistent with floss's higher manual-dexterity demand.
  - PI fell significantly T0→T8 in both groups; slight non-significant rise by T24.
  - **Key correlation**: number of weeks of regular use was **negatively correlated with both PI and BOP** in both groups at both recalls (p<0.05) — more adherence → lower plaque/bleeding.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Self-reported adherence** via marked calendars (mitigated but not eliminated by returning used-tool waste); future work could use digital tracking.
- **Age imbalance** (picks older) is an uncontrolled confounder; age-balanced groups needed.
- **Periodontally healthy sample** with low baseline scores → ceiling/floor effect limits detection of clinical differences; future longitudinal studies in gingivitis/periodontitis patients with probing depth & attachment loss would test long-term efficacy.
- **No cost-effectiveness data** (authors note cost varies by region/brand/system).
- Single center; generalizability and external validity beyond the studied population unverified.

## 6. Related Work

- van der Weijden et al. SR — 6 short RCTs found no superiority of one interdental tool over another for plaque removal.
- Hennequin-Hoenderdos et al. — rubber interdental picks caused less gingival abrasion and were preferred over interdental brushes.
- Graziani et al. — rubber picks gave greater interdental bleeding reduction than floss.
- Cepeda et al. / Marchesan et al. — frequency basis for the ≥3 (or 2–4) days/week "regular use" threshold; ≥4×/week reduces periodontal/caries risk.

## 7. Glossary

- **Sustainability (this paper)**: the number of weeks a participant used the tool regularly over the follow-up — an adherence/consistency measure, NOT environmental sustainability.
- **Regular use**: using the interdental cleaning tool ≥3 days/week.
- **Rubber interdental pick**: a soft elastomeric cone-shaped pick used to clean interdental spaces; handling resembles an interdental brush, lower manual-dexterity demand than floss.
- **PI (Plaque Index)**: here, dichotomous (±) full-mouth percentage of sites with plaque (disclosing-tablet detected).
- **BOP (Bleeding on Probing)**: dichotomous (±) full-mouth percentage of sites bleeding on probing; <10% defines a healthy periodontium.
- **Hawthorne effect**: behavior improvement caused by being observed in a study — minimized here by infrequent recalls (weeks 8 and 24 only).
- **Primary prevention**: maintaining periodontal health in individuals who are already healthy.
