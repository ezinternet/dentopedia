## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Narrative review with meta-analysis data incorporated
- Covers all major membrane categories:
  **Non-resorbable:**
  1. e-PTFE (expanded, original Gore-Tex — discontinued clinically)
  2. d-PTFE (dense, bacteria-resistant, open-healing capable) — e.g. Cytoplast TXT-200, Cytoplast Ti-250XL
  3. Titanium mesh (Ti mesh) — highest space maintenance
  4. Ti-reinforced PTFE (Ti-PTFE, PTFE mesh) — e.g. RPM (Osteogenics), Smartmembranes
  **Resorbable:**
  5. Native (non-cross-linked) collagen — fastest resorption (4–6 wk), most common
  6. Cross-linked collagen — slower resorption, better barrier longevity; ribose (OSSIXPlus) vs glutaraldehyde (BioMend Extend)
  7. Synthetic polymers (PLGA, PLA) — limited clinical uptake
  8. Pericardium membranes (human, porcine, bovine); amnion/chorion; acellular dermal matrix

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**From abstract/keywords + established meta-analysis context:**

Space maintenance hierarchy:
- Titanium mesh > Ti-PTFE > d-PTFE > cross-linked collagen > native collagen ≈ synthetic

Exposure profile:
- d-PTFE: exposure tolerable (bacteria-resistant, open healing) — ~15% typical
- Ti mesh: exposure most damaging (bone loss 16.3% per cm²) — 7–66% range
- Cross-linked collagen: exposure HIGHER than native (~30% higher per Wessing SR+MA) — but tolerable via RCLC open healing

Second surgery required:
- Non-resorbable: YES (d-PTFE, Ti mesh, Ti-PTFE) — added cost, morbidity
- Resorbable: NO

Indications:
- Large vertical defects: non-resorbable preferred (Ti mesh, Ti-PTFE, d-PTFE)
- Lateral/combination defects: both types viable
- Ridge preservation/socket sealing: resorbable (collagen) adequate

## 7. Glossary

- **e-PTFE**: expanded polytetrafluoroethylene (microporous, Gore-Tex prototype — now largely discontinued)
- **d-PTFE**: dense PTFE (non-porous, bacteria cannot penetrate, open-healing capable)
- **Ti-PTFE**: titanium-reinforced PTFE mesh (PTFE with embedded Ti wire for space maintenance)
- **Cross-linked collagen**: collagen with chemical bridging (ribose or glutaraldehyde) for slower resorption
- **Ribose XL**: OSSIXPlus (porcine), slower resorption, no cytotoxicity vs glutaraldehyde
- **Glutaraldehyde XL**: BioMend Extend (bovine), slower resorption but residual cytotoxicity concern
