## One-line Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis of 21 studies on alveolar ridge preservation (ARP) versus extraction alone: ARP reduces horizontal ridge resorption by a mean of 1.86 mm (95% CI 1.44–2.28; P < .001). Outcomes are significantly modulated by socket morphology, wound closure type, grafting material, barrier membrane use, and growth factor application.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Quantifies the **average effect size** of ARP procedures across the literature: ~1.86 mm horizontal ridge preservation versus extraction-only control.
- Identifies **clinical modifiers** of ARP outcome through subgroup meta-analysis: wound closure technique, grafting material, barrier membrane use, growth factors, and socket morphology.
- Provides hard-tissue endpoints (horizontal and vertical buccal/lingual changes) usable as benchmarks when planning ARP cases.
- Frames ARP as an **evidence-supported standard** for sites destined for delayed implant placement, especially where socket walls are compromised.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- PICO-driven systematic search of controlled studies comparing ARP to extraction alone, reporting quantitative hard-tissue dimensional changes.
- Primary outcome: horizontal alveolar bone dimensional change (mm).
- Secondary outcomes: vertical buccal, vertical lingual, midcrestal changes, and other ridge dimension variables (seven total).
- Subgroup variables: wound closure (primary vs open), flap elevation (flap vs flapless), grafting material type (xenograft, allograft, alloplast, autograft), barrier membrane use, growth factor application, socket morphology (intact vs dehiscence/fenestration), and tooth position (anterior vs posterior).
- 21 studies included; pooled analyses by random-effects model with subgroup interactions.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Primary outcome (horizontal ridge change)**: ARP favored over extraction alone by **1.86 mm (95% CI 1.44 – 2.28; P < .001)**.
- **Six of seven outcome variables** favored ARP significantly.
- **Subgroup modifiers (P-values for interaction with horizontal outcome):**
  - Wound closure: P = .033
  - Grafting material: P = .001
  - Barrier membrane: P = .006
  - Growth factor: P = .003
  - Socket morphology: P < .001
- Flap elevation and tooth position were not significant modifiers in the primary outcome at the meta-analysis level.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Heterogeneity in graft materials, measurement methods (clinical caliper, CBCT, stent-based), and follow-up windows complicates direct comparisons.
- Many included trials are short-term (typically 4–6 months); long-term ridge stability after restoration loading is not addressed.
- Soft-tissue outcomes (keratinized tissue width, thickness) are not the focus.
- No formal cost-effectiveness analysis; clinical decision on whether ARP is justified for every socket remains case-specific.

## 7. Glossary
- **ARP (Alveolar Ridge Preservation)**: Surgical intervention at the time of (or shortly after) extraction to minimize postextraction ridge resorption — typically a graft material ± barrier membrane.
- **Horizontal ridge change**: Change in buccolingual width of the alveolar ridge, usually measured at a fixed apical depth.
- **Socket morphology**: Whether the buccal plate is intact or has dehiscence/fenestration; a key prognostic factor.
- **Random-effects meta-analysis**: Pooling model that assumes a distribution of true effects across studies; appropriate when between-study heterogeneity exists.
