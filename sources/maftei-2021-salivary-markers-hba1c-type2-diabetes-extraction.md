## Why Ingested

[[wiki/drug/enteghad-2024-diabetes-mellitus-periodontal-periimplant-disease-review]]는 당뇨–치주/임플란트주위 질환의 양방향 관계를 서술적으로 정리하지만, 발치 후 치유 시점에서 **타액 바이오마커가 HbA1c와 정량적으로 상관**하는지에 대한 임상 데이터는 부족하다. 본 case-control 연구(Maftei 2021, n=86, T0/T1 paired)는 비침습적 타액 산화스트레스·염증·골대사 마커가 당화혈색소를 예측·추적할 수 있음을 ROC/회귀로 보여줘 그 gap을 보강한다. [[wiki/drug/chen-2022-interpretation-hba1c-analytical-methodology-hematology]] 및 [[wiki/drug/weykamp-2013-hba1c-review-analytical-clinical-aspects]]의 HbA1c 해석 맥락과 짝을 이룬다.

## One-line Summary

Case-control study (n=86: 45 systemically healthy + 41 T2DM) measuring 8 salivary markers (OPG, RANKL, HGF, TNF-α, IL-18, MMP-9, TOS, TAC) plus HbA1c at baseline and 3 months post-extraction; T2DM had higher OPG/RANKL/TNF-α/MMP-9/IL-18/TOS, and baseline RANKL/TNF-α/IL-18/MMP-9/TOS/OPG were good ROC predictors of HbA1c >6.5% (RANKL AUC 0.999), with TOS positively (r=+0.777) and TAC negatively (r=−0.475) correlated to HbA1c post-extraction.

## 한줄요약

타액 8종 마커(OPG·RANKL·HGF·TNF-α·IL-18·MMP-9·TOS·TAC)와 HbA1c를 건강군 45명·2형 당뇨군 41명에서 발치 전(T0)·발치 3개월 후(T1) 측정한 case-control 연구로, 당뇨군은 염증·산화·골대사 마커가 더 높았고 baseline RANKL·TNF-α·IL-18·MMP-9·TOS·OPG가 HbA1c>6.5%의 좋은 ROC 예측인자(RANKL AUC 0.999)였으며, 발치 후 TOS는 HbA1c와 양의 상관(r=+0.777)·TAC는 음의 상관(r=−0.475)을 보여 타액이 당뇨 환자의 발치 후 치유 상태의 비침습 지표가 될 수 있음을 시사.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Demonstrates that a panel of **salivary immuno-biochemical markers correlates with glycemic control (HbA1c)** in T2DM patients, supporting non-invasive saliva-based monitoring.
2. Provides ROC-derived **cut-off values** for salivary markers as predictors of HbA1c >6.5% (dysglycemia threshold).
3. Tracks **post-extraction trajectory** (3-month) of bone-metabolism (OPG, RANKL), inflammatory (TNF-α, IL-18, MMP-9), growth (HGF) and oxidative-stress (TOS, TAC) markers in diabetic vs. healthy patients.
4. Reports that **HbA1c itself fell significantly** in the DM group 3 months after extraction (7.98% → 7.62%, p=0.05), alongside a significant TNF-α decrease.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Case-control study; subjects sampled at two time points — T0 (before any dental therapy) and T1 (3 months post tooth extraction).
- **Groups**: Control (C, n=45, no systemic pathology) vs. Diabetes Mellitus type 2 (DM, n=41, diagnosed ≥3 years per ADA, HbA1c 6.5–11%).
- **Recruitment**: 172 initially → 92 after inclusion/exclusion → 86 completed (6 lost to follow-up). Period Nov 2018–Nov 2019.
- **Inclusion**: >18 yrs, requiring ≥1 tooth extraction.
- **Exclusion**: antibiotics/anti-inflammatories in last 3 months; periodontal/orthodontic treatment in last 6 months; periodontitis stage 3–4; BMI >30; psychiatric disorders; pregnant/lactating; smokers; systemic diseases affecting immune response or bone metabolism (except T2DM).
- **Sample size**: G*power on TNF-α as primary variable; α=0.05, β=0.05, power 95% → min 38/group; +10% dropout → 41/group.
- **Saliva sampling**: ~3 mL unstimulated whole saliva (passive drooling), 8–10 am, fasting, no morning oral hygiene; venous blood for HbA1c same morning; stored at −80 °C.
- **Assays**: ELISA for OPG, RANKL, HGF, TNF-α, MMP-9, IL-18 (MyBioSource / Elabscience / Abcam kits); spectrophotometry for TAC and TOS (Rel Assay kits, Erel 2005 method).
- **Statistics**: SPSS v18, 95% threshold. ANOVA-derived indicators; Skewness/Kurtosis for normality; unpaired t-test (between groups), paired t-test (intragroup T0→T1); chi-square/Fisher for categorical; ROC curve (Se, Sp, AUC, cut-offs); linear regression for HbA1c determinism.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Baseline demographics**: Groups comparable in age (~52 yr), gender, urban/rural. HbA1c DM 7.98% vs. C 5.01% (p=0.001). Cause of extraction differed (p=0.019): periodontal disease predominant in DM (56.1%).

**Between-group (DM vs. C, both time points)**: DM had significantly higher OPG, RANKL, TNF-α, MMP-9, IL-18, and TOS. OPG/RANKL ratio and HGF higher in controls; TAC higher in controls at baseline only (equalized at follow-up).

**Intragroup T0 → T1 (paired)**:
- OPG ↑ in both groups (C 2.08→6.73; DM 3.79→7.58; p=0.001).
- RANKL ↓ in both (C 8.02→3.02; DM 36.70→31.70).
- OPG/RANKL ratio ↑ in both.
- HGF ↑ significantly only in controls (211.76→874.94; DM 13.29→26.38, ns p=0.181).
- TNF-α significant decrease **only in DM** (9.69→8.28, p=0.001; controls 4.81→4.57, ns).
- MMP-9 ↓ in both; IL-18 ↓ in both; TOS ↓ in both.
- TAC ↑ significantly in C; in DM increase to 1.12 was ns.
- **HbA1c**: DM 7.98%→7.62% (p=0.05, significant decrease); controls unchanged.
- % uncontrolled DM dropped 22.0% → 2.4% at follow-up.

**HbA1c correlations in DM at T1**: TAC negative moderate (r=−0.475, p=0.002); TOS positive strong (r=+0.777, p=0.001). OPG, HGF, TNF-α weak positive (ns). RANKL, OPG/RANKL, MMP-9, IL-18 independent.

**ROC for HbA1c >6.5% (baseline)** — good predictors: RANKL (cut-off 15, AUC 0.999, Se 99%, Sp 97.6%); TNF-α (6.02, AUC 0.907); IL-18 (198, AUC 0.901); MMP-9 (120, AUC 0.899); TOS (17.5, AUC 0.876); OPG (2.77, AUC 0.833). Linear regression indicated all studied salivary markers contribute to HbA1c determinism.

**Conclusion**: Several salivary oxidative-status and proinflammatory biomarkers are altered in diabetic patients and correlate with HbA1c, making them potential non-invasive indicators of post-extraction healing status.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-center, modest sample (n=86); 3-month follow-up only.
- Case-control design — associations, not causality; the post-extraction HbA1c drop cannot be attributed to extraction (confounding by improved oral care, regression to mean, diet/medication adherence).
- Some internal inconsistencies in reported descriptive HbA1c (Table 1 min–max 4.20–9.70 with mean 4.43 ± 1.65 for all cases) vs. group means; abstract claims TAC upward trend that is ns in DM.
- Cronbach's alpha reported unusually (−0.8960, adjusted to 0.600) for 9 simultaneous null-hypotheses.
- No periodontal-severity stratification within the (stage ≤2) cohort; smokers and BMI>30 excluded, limiting generalizability.
- Salivary marker units/assay-specific cut-offs may not transfer across laboratories.

## 7. Glossary

- **OPG** — Osteoprotegerin; decoy receptor for RANKL, inhibits osteoclast differentiation.
- **RANKL** — Receptor Activator of Nuclear factor Kappa-B Ligand; drives osteoclastogenesis.
- **OPG/RANKL ratio** — Bone-resorption balance indicator; higher = less resorption.
- **HGF** — Hepatocyte Growth Factor; epithelial–mesenchymal mediator in healing.
- **TNF-α** — Tumor Necrosis Factor alpha; proinflammatory cytokine, predictor of diabetic progression.
- **IL-18** — Interleukin 18; proinflammatory cytokine linked to T2DM risk.
- **MMP-9** — Matrix Metalloproteinase 9; tissue remodeling, osteogenesis/angiogenesis.
- **TOS** — Total Oxidant Status (µmol H2O2 Equiv/L); aggregate oxidant burden.
- **TAC** — Total Antioxidant Capacity (mmol Trolox Equiv/L); aggregate antioxidant defense.
- **HbA1c** — Glycated hemoglobin; 2–3 month glycemic control marker.
- **ROC / AUC** — Receiver Operating Characteristic curve / Area Under Curve; diagnostic accuracy metric.
- **T0 / T1** — Pre-extraction baseline / 3-month post-extraction follow-up.
