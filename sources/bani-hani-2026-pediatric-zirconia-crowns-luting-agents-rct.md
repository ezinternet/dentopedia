## Why Ingested

소아 지르코니아 크라운(paediatric zirconia crown)의 효능 근거는 늘고 있으나, **합착제(luting agent) 선택**에 대한 임상 근거는 빈약하다. [[wiki/caries/chavhan-2026-zirconia-vs-ssc-primary-dentition-sr-ma]]는 지르코니아 vs 스테인리스강 크라운(SSC)의 *크라운 종류* 비교를 다루지만 합착제 변수는 다루지 않는다. 본 36개월 split-mouth RCT (Bani-Hani 2026)는 글래스아이오노머시멘트(Glass Ionomer Cement, GIC) vs 자가접착 레진시멘트(self-adhesive resin cement)를 동일 환자 내에서 직접 비교해 그 합착제 공백을 메우는 자매 논문이다.

## One-line Summary

36-month split-mouth RCT (38 children, 64 pair-matched primary incisors / 128 crowns) — GIC (95.3% retention) vs self-adhesive resin cement (100% retention) for luting paediatric zirconia crowns showed no statistically significant difference in retention or gingival health; GIC is a clinically acceptable alternative.

## 한줄요약

36개월 split-mouth RCT (소아 38명, 짝지은 유전치 64쌍 / 크라운 128개) — 소아 지르코니아 크라운 합착에서 GIC(유지율 95.3%) vs 자가접착 레진시멘트(100%)는 유지율·치은 건강 모두 통계적 유의차가 없었고, GIC가 임상적으로 수용 가능한 대안이다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First (per the authors) direct head-to-head RCT comparing **GIC vs self-adhesive resin cement** for luting **paediatric zirconia crowns** on primary anterior teeth.
- Addresses a stated evidence gap: paediatric zirconia crowns are increasingly popular esthetically, but the cementation *technique/material* evidence is limited.
- Split-mouth design (pair-matched incisors in the same child) controls for patient-level confounders (oral hygiene, caries risk, diet, age).
- Long 36-month follow-up with structured recall schedule (1-week, 1-, 3-, 6-, 12-, 24-, 36-month).
- Clinical bottom line: GIC may be considered a clinically acceptable alternative to self-adhesive resin cement for paediatric zirconia crowns.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Split-mouth randomised clinical trial.
- **Participants**: Children aged 3–5 years with carious primary incisors requiring zirconia crown restoration.
- **Randomisation**: Pair-matched incisors randomly assigned within each child to either GIC or self-adhesive resin cement for crown cementation.
- **Sample**: 38 patients; 64 pair-matched incisors = 128 teeth.
- **Outcomes evaluated**: crown retention and gingival condition.
- **Recall timepoints**: 1-week, 1-, 3-, 6-, 12-, 24-, and 36-month.
- **Statistics**: McNemar's test for crown retention; Wilcoxon signed-rank test for gingival index scores; significance set at p < 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Enrolment**: 38 patients, 64 pair-matched incisors (128 teeth).
- **GIC group**: 3 debonding events over the study period → cumulative retention 96.9% (12-mo), 95.3% (24-mo), 95.3% (36-mo).
- **Resin-cement group**: 0 debonding events → 100% retention throughout.
- **Intergroup retention difference**: NOT statistically significant (p > 0.05; 95% CI for difference: −0.5% to +9.9%).
- **Gingival index**: no statistically significant difference between cement types at any follow-up (p > 0.05; effect size r = 0.02–0.11).
- **Conclusion**: both cements showed high retention and favourable gingival health; GIC is a clinically acceptable alternative to self-adhesive resin cement for luting zirconia crowns in primary teeth.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Abstract-only ingest** — methodological detail (zirconia crown brand, surface treatment, cement products, blinding, operator count, dropout handling) not available from the abstract.
- Small sample (38 children) → the non-significant retention difference may reflect limited power rather than true equivalence; the 95% CI upper bound (+9.9%) does not exclude a clinically meaningful resin advantage.
- Anterior primary incisors only — findings may not generalise to posterior primary molars (different occlusal load, retention form).
- 36-month follow-up is mid-term for primary dentition but does not span to natural exfoliation for all teeth.
- Single-centre; gingival index is the only soft-tissue outcome reported.

## 7. Glossary

- **Paediatric zirconia crown**: pre-formed tooth-coloured (zirconia ceramic) full-coverage crown for restoring carious or damaged primary teeth; esthetic alternative to stainless-steel crowns.
- **Luting agent / cement**: material used to bond (cement) the crown to the prepared tooth.
- **Glass Ionomer Cement (GIC)**: acid–base water-based cement with fluoride release and chemical adhesion to tooth structure.
- **Self-adhesive resin cement**: resin-based cement that bonds without a separate etch/primer step.
- **Split-mouth design**: each patient receives both interventions on contralateral/paired sites, serving as their own control.
- **McNemar's test**: statistical test for paired binary (yes/no) outcomes such as retained vs debonded.
- **Wilcoxon signed-rank test**: non-parametric test for paired ordinal data such as gingival index scores.
- **Gingival index**: ordinal score of gingival inflammation around the restoration.
