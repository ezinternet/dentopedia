## Why Ingested

Counterpoint reminder RCT for the no-show overview: in a university pediatric dental clinic, **voice messages beat SMS text** at reducing no-shows — the opposite-channel result qualifies the blanket "send a reminder" recommendation by showing channel choice (and population) matters. Refines [[overviews/dental-appointment-no-show-overview]] by adding a head-to-head channel comparison rather than reminder-vs-none.

## One-line Summary

RCT (n=318 caregiver/child dyads) in a dental-school pediatric clinic: voice-message reminders had a lower no-show rate (8.2%) than SMS text (17.7%); SMS was not as effective as voice for randomly assigned reminder type.

## 한줄요약

치과대학 소아치과 RCT (n=318 보호자/아동 쌍): 음성 메시지 알림의 미내원율(8.2%)이 문자(SMS) 알림(17.7%)보다 낮았다 — 무작위 배정 상황에서 SMS가 음성보다 효과적이지 않았다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- One of the early RCTs to directly compare **SMS text vs voice** appointment reminders in a dental setting (most reminder studies compare reminder vs no reminder).
- Found SMS **inferior** to voice messaging for no-show reduction in a university pediatric dental clinic — a counterpoint to the common assumption that text reminders are uniformly effective.
- Identified **younger caregiver age** as a predictor of nonattendance.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Setting: pediatric dentistry clinic, University of Washington.
- 543 caregiver/child dyads invited; **318 pairs (59% response)** enrolled and randomized.
- Randomized to **SMS text message (n=158)** or **voice message (control, n=160)** as the appointment reminder.
- Outcome: appointment attendance / no-show.
- Analysis: odds ratio for reminder type vs no-show, unadjusted and adjusted for caregiver age.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Voice-message group no-show rate: **8.2%**.
- SMS text group no-show rate: **17.7%** (P=.01 between groups).
- Unadjusted OR (reminder type vs no-show): **2.41** (P=.01).
- Adjusted OR (for caregiver age): **2.12** (P=.04).
- Younger caregivers were more likely to be nonattendees (P=.02).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-setting, dental-school pediatric clinic — limited external validity (university population may differ from private practice).
- Patients were **randomly assigned** the reminder channel; the authors note results may differ when patients **self-select** their preferred reminder type.
- Abstract-only ingest — full-text methodological detail (e.g., message content, delivery confirmation, sample power) not retrieved.

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary

- **No-show / nonattendance**: a scheduled appointment the patient fails to keep without prior cancellation.
- **SMS (Short Message Service)**: text-message reminder delivered to a mobile phone.
- **Voice message**: automated or recorded telephone-call reminder.
- **Odds ratio (OR)**: ratio of the odds of an outcome (no-show) between two groups; OR > 1 here means SMS had higher no-show odds than voice.
