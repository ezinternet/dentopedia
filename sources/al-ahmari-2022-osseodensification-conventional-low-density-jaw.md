## One-line Summary
Split-mouth in vivo study (20 patients, 40 implants, low-density bone): only immediate post-op bone density significantly favored OD; primary/secondary stability, PI, BOP, PD, and marginal bone loss did NOT differ significantly between OD and conventional.

## 1. Document Information
- Technology and Health Care 2022. doi 10.3233/THC-220048. Split-mouth in vivo clinical study.

## 2. Key Contributions
- A controlled human split-mouth design where OD's much-touted stability advantage was largely null — useful counterweight against OD-favorable pooled evidence.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 20 patients (40 split-mouth implants), age 40–59. OD side: Densah bur kit (20 implants); conventional side: standard burs (20 implants). Outcomes: primary stability, plaque index, BOP, pocket depth, CBCT bone density and marginal bone loss.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Bone density: significantly higher with OD immediately post-op.
- Primary and secondary stability, PI, BOP, PD, marginal bone loss: NO significant difference between groups.
- Conclusion (authors): OD gave better primary stability in low-density bone and improved peri-implant density — but the quantitative stability endpoints did not reach significance per the results statement.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small n; conclusion language is more OD-favorable than the null stability results support (interpret cautiously); single operator/center.

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary
- PI: plaque index. BOP: bleeding on probing. PD: pocket depth. MBL: marginal bone loss. OD: osseodensification.
