## Why Ingested

임플란트 주위 협측 연조직 열개(Buccal Soft Tissue Dehiscence, BSTD) 발생 빈도와 관련 위험요인에 대한 첫 포괄적 SR+MA로, [[wiki/overviews/peri-implant-soft-tissue-dehiscence-prevention]]의 근거 체계화 및 [[wiki/implants/tavelli-2025-soft-tissue-dehiscences-implant-sites-ao-aap]]의 선행 SR로서 CTG 보호 효과·협측 식립 위치의 OR을 정량화한 핵심 문헌.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 22 investigations (24 articles) on buccal peri-implant soft tissue dehiscence (BSTD ≥1 mm): thin biotype (OR 2.85) and buccal implant malposition (OR 14.37) were significant risk factors; CTG was strongly protective (OR 9.00 without CTG); buccal bone thickness and implant timing were not significant.

## 한줄요약

22개 임상연구(24편)에 대한 SR+MA: 얇은 표현형(OR 2.85)과 협측 임플란트 위치 불량(OR 14.37)이 BSTD 유의 위험요인이며, 결합조직이식(CTG) 미시행이 BSTD 위험 9배 증가, 협측 골판 두께와 식립 시기는 유의하지 않음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Clinical Oral Implants Research, 2022;33(Suppl. 23):109–124
- **DOI**: 10.1111/clr.13888
- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis (PRISMA; PROSPERO CRD42020203155)
- **Search period**: Databases (MEDLINE/PubMed, Embase, Cochrane) up to September 1, 2020
- **Included studies**: 24 articles from 22 clinical investigations (7 case series, 8 cohort studies, 1 CCT, 6 RCTs)
- **Population**: Partially edentulous patients with ≥1 implant; follow-up ≥6 months after final prosthesis; ≥10 patients per study
- **BSTD definition**: Apical migration ≥1 mm of peri-implant soft tissue margin from baseline (final restoration delivery) or vs. adjacent/contralateral homologous tooth

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First comprehensive SR+MA** on BSTD at implants—quantifying both frequency range and risk factor ORs from the same evidence base.
2. **Thin biotype confirmed as risk factor**: pooled OR = 2.85 [1.40–5.80], n = 5, p = .003.
3. **Buccal implant malposition as dominant risk factor**: pooled OR = 14.37 [4.58–45.14], n = 3, p ≤ .001—strongest single predictor.
4. **CTG as strong protective factor**: absence of CTG associated with OR = 9.00 [3.11–26.02], n = 5, p ≤ .001.
5. **Buccal bone plate thickness NOT significant**: OR = 1.29 [0.35–4.77], n = 2, p = .704.
6. **Immediate implant placement NOT significant**: OR = 1.56 [0.46–5.26], n = 4, p = .477.
7. **BSTD frequency range 0%–61%** across included studies; no pooled mean was calculated due to high inter-study heterogeneity.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**PICO**:
- Population: Partially edentulous patients with ≥1 implant
- Exposure: Any anatomic, surgical, or prosthetic risk factor
- Comparator: Non-exposure
- Outcome: Frequency of BSTD occurrence

**Risk of bias**: RCTs/CCTs assessed with Cochrane Handbook 5.1.0 (sequence generation, allocation concealment, blinding, follow-up completeness, selective reporting); observational studies with modified Newcastle–Ottawa Scale (NOS).

**Statistical analysis**: Random-effects meta-analysis (ORs with SEs); diagnostic plots (funnel, radial, Q-Q, standardized residuals) for model assumptions and publication bias. R with `metafor` package. Significance: p < .05.

**Inclusion criteria**:
- RCTs, CCTs, prospective and retrospective cohort studies, prospective case series
- ≥6 months follow-up after final prosthesis
- ≥10 patients
- English language only; cross-sectional designs excluded

**Screening**: Two independent reviewers (ISM, ER); disagreements resolved by third reviewer (DST); kappa agreement = 0.93 (title/abstract), 0.90 (full text).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Frequency of BSTD

BSTD frequency ranged from 0% to 61% across 22 studies; no pooled mean was estimable due to high heterogeneity in study design, site characteristics, and regenerative procedures. Notable individual study values:
- 0% (Tian 2019, flapless IIP with DBBM)
- 3.44% (Bianchi 2004, CTG used; Raes 2018)
- 61.32% (Oates 2002, flap elevation, no bone graft, anterior jaws)
- 57.14% (Bittner 2019)
- 53.33% (Cordaro 2009)

### Meta-analysis ORs

| Factor | OR [95% CI] | n studies | p-value | Interpretation |
|--------|-------------|-----------|---------|----------------|
| Thin biotype | 2.85 [1.40, 5.80] | 5 | .003 | Significant risk |
| Buccal implant position | 14.37 [4.58, 45.14] | 3 | <.001 | Strongest risk factor |
| No CTG (vs CTG) | 9.00 [3.11, 26.02] | 5 | <.001 | Significant (CTG protective) |
| Buccal bone plate <1 mm | 1.29 [0.35, 4.77] | 2 | .704 | NOT significant |
| Immediate vs delayed placement | 1.56 [0.46, 5.26] | 4 | .477 | NOT significant |

### Study characteristics

- Location: 14/22 studies—anterior maxilla; 4/22—anterior maxilla or mandible; 2/22—anterior and posterior combined
- Patient-level data: 17/22; implant-level data: 5/22
- No significant trend between BSTD frequency and follow-up duration or study type (Figure 7 of the paper)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- High heterogeneity in study design, implant protocols, and regenerative procedures precluded pooled frequency estimation.
- Study designs were not homogenized regarding implant placement protocols and treatment characteristics.
- ≥1 mm threshold for BSTD excluded publications reporting mucosal margin changes on continuous scales; some potentially informative studies were omitted.
- No analysis possible for buccal bone dehiscence influence (absent bone vs. present bone); only buccal bone plate thickness was analyzed.
- Limited evidence on advanced BSTD (prosthetic abutment or implant surface exposure), as these often involve iatrogenic factors incompatible with prospective trials.
- Keratinized mucosa (KM) quantity was not analyzable due to insufficient data across studies.
- Further studies needed to clarify the role of implant timing on BSTD.

## 6. Related Work

- Sanz-Martin et al. (2020): case–control study identifying buccal implant position as the single most important BSTD predictor (OR = 34.65 by CBCT); provided methodological basis for this SR+MA.
- Zucchelli et al. (2019): defined terminology—mid-facial recession, mucosal recession, soft tissue dehiscence around implants.
- Cosyn et al. (2012): systematic review on frequency of advanced recession after single immediate implant treatment—antecedent SR cited herein.
- Avila-Ortiz et al. (2020): peri-implant phenotype classification (basis for biotype categorization).
- Tavelli et al. (2025): subsequent AO/AAP SR on soft tissue dehiscences at implant sites, building on this evidence base. [[implants/tavelli-2025-soft-tissue-dehiscences-implant-sites-ao-aap]]
- [[implants/sanz-2022-keratinized-mucosa-around-implants-consensus]]: consensus on KM around implants (contextualizes KM-gap noted as limitation herein).

## 7. Glossary

- **BSTD (Buccal Soft Tissue Dehiscence)**: Apical migration of peri-implant mucosal margin ≥1 mm, exposing prosthetic components, implant shoulder, or surface; synonymous with mid-facial/mucosal recession in implant literature.
- **Thin biotype**: Peri-implant soft tissue phenotype with thinner mucosa; associated with higher susceptibility to recession.
- **Buccal implant position**: Implant shoulder positioned facially/buccally beyond the alveolar envelope, assessed by study casts or CBCT axial cuts.
- **CTG (Connective Tissue Graft)**: Subepithelial connective tissue graft used to augment peri-implant soft tissue volume and provide a protective effect against BSTD.
- **BPB (Buccal Plate of Bone)**: Thickness of the buccal cortical bone wall at the implant site.
- **IIP (Immediate Implant Placement)**: Implant placed into a fresh extraction socket at the time of extraction.
- **DIP (Delayed Implant Placement)**: Implant placed after socket healing (typically ≥3 months post-extraction).
- **KM (Keratinized Mucosa)**: Band of keratinized tissue at the peri-implant margin; quantity not analyzable in this SR due to data limitations.
- **NOS (Newcastle–Ottawa Scale)**: Risk-of-bias assessment tool for observational studies used in this review.
- **OR (Odds Ratio)**: Statistical measure of association; OR >1 indicates higher odds of BSTD with the factor present.
- **DBBM**: Deproteinized bovine bone mineral; used as socket fill material in many included studies.
