## One-line Summary
SR+MA of 18 studies finding that reused orthodontic miniscrews have no significant difference in insertion/fracture torque compared to new ones in vitro, but show a significantly higher clinical failure rate (RR 0.46), influenced by sterilization method, insertion technique, and patient anatomy.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of the World Federation of Orthodontists (J World Fed Orthod), Volume 14, Issue 2, Pages 97–110, April 2025
- **DOI**: S2212-4438(24)00069-9
- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Search date**: Up to February 2024
- **Databases**: MEDLINE, Web of Science, EMBASE, Scopus, Cochrane CENTRAL; grey literature; manual search of orthodontic journals 2010–2023

## 2. Key Contributions
- First SR+MA specifically addressing reuse of orthodontic miniscrews (retrieved after patient use, sterilized, and re-inserted)
- Separates in vitro mechanical outcomes (insertion/fracture torque) from clinical survival outcomes
- Quantifies the clinical failure risk ratio for reused vs new miniscrews
- Highlights the isolating effect of sterilization alone on mechanical properties

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Eligibility**: Studies on miniscrews after retrieval and sterilization; both in vitro and clinical trials; unused + sterilized screws also included to isolate sterilization effects
- **Included**: 18 studies total
  - 13 studies: retrieved and sterilized miniscrews
  - 5 studies: unused but sterilized miniscrews (sterilization-effect isolation)
- **Outcomes**: Insertion torque, removal torque, fracture torque (in vitro); clinical failure rate (clinical)
- **Statistical model**: Meta-analysis with random-effects; risk ratio for clinical studies; mean difference for in vitro studies

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
**In vitro results:**
- No significant difference in insertion torque between retrieved and unretrieved miniscrews
- No significant difference in fracture torque between retrieved and unretrieved miniscrews
- No significant difference in removal torque
- Sterilized (unused) miniscrews: statistically significant increase in insertion torque, but fracture resistance not affected

**Clinical results:**
- Reused miniscrews: significantly higher failure rate
- Risk Ratio = 0.46 (95% CI 0.24–0.69) — indicating ~54% higher relative failure likelihood on reuse
- Factors influencing outcome: sterilization method, insertion technique, patient-specific anatomical considerations

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Limited number of studies on reused miniscrews overall
- Heterogeneity in sterilization protocols across studies
- Lack of standardized outcome reporting between in vitro and clinical studies
- Full text not accessible; abstract-based extraction

## 6. Related Work
- Alharbi et al. 2018 — overall new miniscrew failure rates (baseline for comparison)
- Mohammed et al. 2018 — site-specific failure rates for new miniscrews

## 7. Glossary
- **Retrieved miniscrew**: A miniscrew previously used in a patient, removed, and subsequently sterilized for potential reuse
- **Insertion torque**: Force required to drive the miniscrew into bone; indicator of primary stability
- **Fracture torque**: Maximum torque before screw fractures; safety threshold
- **Sterilization**: Process (autoclave, EtO, etc.) to render retrieved instruments free from viable microorganisms
- **TAD**: Temporary Anchorage Device
