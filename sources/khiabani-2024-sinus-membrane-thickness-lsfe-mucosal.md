## Why Ingested

The current synthesis page [[wiki/overviews/sinus-lift-lateral-2026-synthesis]] identifies sinus mucosal thickness (SMT) as a preoperative risk variable but has no prospective trial directly testing the 5 mm cutoff as a management threshold. Khiabani 2024 fills that gap: it is the first controlled prospective trial comparing <5 mm vs >5 mm SMT groups through 6-month CBCT follow-up, with direct data on whether thickened mucosa is a contraindication to lateral SFE — addressing the "membrane cutoff" prevention axis in the overview.

## One-line Summary

Prospective controlled trial (n=40 sinuses, 20 per group) comparing preoperative SM thickness <5 mm vs >5 mm after lateral SFE: thickened mucosa resolves significantly (6.8 → 3.4 mm, p<.001) without rhinosinusitis, and is not a contraindication to LSFE.

## 한줄요약

전향적 대조 임상시험 (n=40 상악동, 각 군 20예): 수술 전 상악동 점막 두께 (Sinus Membrane, SM) <5 mm 대 >5 mm 비교; 두꺼운 점막은 수술 후 6개월 유의하게 감소 (6.8→3.4 mm, p<.001)하고 비부비동염 (Rhinosinusitis) 없이 적절히 골증대되므로 측방 상악동저 거상술 (Lateral Sinus Floor Elevation, LSFE)의 금기증이 아님.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides the first prospective controlled data on whether thickened sinus mucosa (>5 mm) contraindicates LSFE.
- Demonstrates that Group B (>5 mm SMT) undergoes significant, clinically favorable SM thickness reduction post-LSFE (6.8 → 3.4 mm, p<.001) — not pathological persistence.
- Establishes that augmentation adequacy (bone height and length) and membrane perforation rates are statistically similar between thin (<5 mm) and thickened (>5 mm) mucosal groups.
- Confirms zero rhinosinusitis incidence in both groups at 6 months, challenging the historical contraindication argument.
- Quantifies surgical difficulty differences: thickened mucosa requires more time and has higher intraoperative bleeding and resistance to membrane elevation.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Prospective controlled clinical trial, 6-month follow-up
- **Population**: 40 subjects undergoing unilateral LSFE; 72.5% male; mean age 48.8 ± 7.6 years
- **Group A** (n=20): Preoperative SM thickness < 5 mm
- **Group B** (n=20): Preoperative SM thickness > 5 mm
- **Intervention**: Lateral sinus floor elevation with simultaneous implant placement (52 implants total)
- **Imaging**: CBCT at baseline and 6 months postoperatively
- **Primary outcome**: SM thickness change (preoperative to 6-month CBCT)
- **Secondary outcomes**: Sinus health (rhinosinusitis — clinical + radiographic), augmentation adequacy (bone height + length), membrane perforation rate, bleeding during membrane separation, resistance to elevation, operation time

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Variable | Group A (<5 mm) | Group B (>5 mm) | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Preoperative SM thickness | 1.4 ± 0.9 mm | 6.8 ± 1.0 mm | — |
| 6-month SM thickness | 1.3 ± 0.6 mm | 3.4 ± 1.7 mm | — |
| SM thickness change significance | Not significant | **p < .001** | p < .001 (between groups) |
| Rhinosinusitis (clinical + radiographic) | 0/20 | 0/20 | — |
| Membrane perforation rate | Similar | Similar | NS |
| Augmentation adequacy | Similar | Similar | NS |
| Bleeding during separation | Lower | Higher | **p = .003** |
| Resistance to elevation | Lower | Higher | **p = .001** |
| Operation time | 8.64 ± 3.70 min | 12.08 ± 8.26 min | p = .097 (NS) |

- 52 implants placed simultaneously across 40 sinuses
- No radiographic or clinical evidence of rhinosinusitis at 6 months in either group
- Thickened mucosa (>5 mm but <10 mm) resolves toward normal range post-LSFE
- Main difference: surgical difficulty (bleeding, resistance) is greater with thickened mucosa

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample size (n=20 per group) limits power for rare complication detection
- 6-month follow-up only — long-term implant survival and late sinus complications not reported
- Upper bound of "thickened" group not specified beyond ">5 mm" (max values not reported)
- No histological data on membrane composition or inflammatory grade
- Single-center design; operator experience not formally stratified
- Did not evaluate the effect of ostium patency independently

## 6. Related Work

- Munakata 2021: SMT distribution in edentulous sinuses (mean 1.09 mm; 25% >2 mm threshold) — provides the baseline distribution context for Group A

- Manor 2024: CBCT prognostic factors for sinus perforation — complementary preoperative risk assessment

## 7. Glossary

- **SM**: Sinus membrane / Schneiderian membrane / sinus mucosal lining
- **LSFE**: Lateral sinus floor elevation (lateral window approach)
- **CBCT**: Cone-beam computed tomography — used for preoperative SMT measurement and 6-month outcome assessment
- **SMT**: Sinus mucosal thickness — measured from the sinus floor on axial/coronal CBCT
- **Rhinosinusitis**: Inflammation/infection of the sinus — the key safety endpoint; absent in both groups
- **Augmentation adequacy**: Whether achieved bone height and lateral bone length met implant site requirements
- **Resistance to elevation**: Intraoperative tactile difficulty separating the sinus membrane from the bony floor
