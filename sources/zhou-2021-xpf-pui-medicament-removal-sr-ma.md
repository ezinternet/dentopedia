## One-line Summary
SR+MA (in vitro) of XP-Endo Finisher vs. passive ultrasonic irrigation for intracanal medicament removal: PUI significantly superior overall (OR 3.09), but advantage disappears when both use NaOCl+EDTA combined (OR 1.51, NS).

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health 21:294, 2021
- **DOI**: 10.1186/s12903-021-01644-7
- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (in vitro)

## 2. Key Contributions
- **Overall**: PUI significantly superior to XPF for medicament removal (OR 3.09, 95% CI 1.96–4.86)
- **Key modifier**: When both methods use NaOCl+EDTA combined irrigation, the difference becomes non-significant (OR 1.51, 95% CI 0.71–3.20)
- **Dressing duration**: PUI significantly superior for **2-week** Ca(OH)2 dressing removal; no significant difference for 1-week dressing
- Apical third medicament removal remains the most challenging across both techniques

## 3. Methodology
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane, Embase, Web of Science, CNKI
- **Inclusion**: In vitro studies; Ca(OH)2 or other intracanal medicament; extracted human teeth; comparing XPF vs. PUI with quantitative removal measurement
- **Primary outcome**: Medicament removal score (microCT area/volume, SEM score, or direct weighing)
- **Statistical**: Random-effects model; OR with 95% CI; I² for heterogeneity

## 4. Key Results

### Overall comparison
| Comparison | OR | 95% CI | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| PUI vs. XPF (all studies) | 3.09 | 1.96–4.86 | <.001 |
| PUI vs. XPF (NaOCl+EDTA only) | 1.51 | 0.71–3.20 | NS |

### By dressing duration
| Duration | Winner | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| 1-week Ca(OH)2 | NS | p > .05 |
| 2-week Ca(OH)2 | PUI superior | p < .05 |

### By canal third
- Apical third consistently lowest removal rate for both techniques
- PUI advantage most pronounced at apical third

## 5. Limitations
- All studies in vitro; clinical applicability unconfirmed
- Heterogeneous measurement methods (microCT vs. SEM vs. direct weighing)
- Variation in XPF tip size/motion protocols across studies
- PUI frequency and activation time not standardized across included studies

## 7. Glossary
- **XPF (XP-Endo Finisher)**: NiTi file with unique B-Phase tip that expands during rotation, claimed to reach areas inaccessible to conventional instruments
- **PUI (Passive Ultrasonic Irrigation)**: Ultrasonic activation of irrigant without canal-shaping pressure; file vibrates at ~25–30 kHz
- **OR**: Odds ratio; OR 3.09 means 3× higher odds of complete medicament removal with PUI vs. XPF
- **Ca(OH)2 dressing**: Calcium hydroxide packed between appointments; hardened/adherent paste is difficult to remove completely
