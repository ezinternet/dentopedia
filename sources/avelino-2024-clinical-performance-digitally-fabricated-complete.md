## Why Ingested

디지털 총의치(digital complete denture)의 임상 퍼포먼스와 환자보고결과(PROM)를 종합하는 SR+MA가 필요했으며, 기존 [[wiki/complete-denture/khorasani-2024-3d-printed-vs-milled-complete]]는 3D 프린팅 vs 밀링 비교에 집중한 반면, 본 논문은 밀링·프린팅 모두 conventional CD 대비 비교하고 PROMs(만족도, OHRQoL, WTP)까지 포괄한 JPD 2024 SR+MA로 보완적 근거를 제공한다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 16 studies (n=1505 screened) found digitally fabricated complete dentures (milled or 3D-printed) had similar or better clinical performance vs conventional dentures, with no significant difference in patient satisfaction PROMs.

## 한줄요약

16편 SR+MA: 디지털 총의치(밀링·프린팅)는 기존 총의치와 임상 성능이 유사하거나 우수하며, 환자 만족도 PROM에서 유의한 차이 없음.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First comprehensive SR+MA comparing both milled and 3D-printed CDs against conventional CDs on **both** clinical performance and PROMs
2. Meta-analysis of denture retention separately for printed vs milled vs conventional CDs
3. Pooled analysis of patient satisfaction across different digital fabrication methods
4. Evidence certainty graded using GRADE approach
5. Covers 5 clinical outcomes (retention, adaptation/fit, clinician quality assessment, masticatory efficiency, occlusal force) and 4 PROMs (satisfaction, OHRQoL, final choice, willingness-to-pay)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search period**: Inception to September 2023
- **Databases**: Electronic databases (specifics from full text)
- **Included studies**: 16 articles (both RCTs and non-randomized studies)
- **PICO**: Population = complete denture wearers; Intervention = digitally fabricated CDs (milled or 3D-printed); Comparison = conventionally fabricated CDs; Outcomes = clinical performance + PROMs
- **Risk of Bias Tools**:
  - RoB 2 (Cochrane) for RCTs
  - ROBINS-I for non-randomized studies
- **Statistical analysis**: Mean difference (MD) with 95% CI for continuous outcomes
- **Evidence certainty**: GRADE

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Denture Base Retention (meta-analysis)
- **Conventional vs Printed CDs**: Conventional CDs showed significantly better retention (P=.02)
- **Conventional vs Milled CDs**: No significant difference in retention (P=.20)

### Patient Satisfaction (meta-analysis)
- **Printed CDs vs conventional**: No significant difference (P=.55)
- **Milled CDs vs conventional**: No significant difference (P=.08)

### Other Clinical Outcomes (qualitative synthesis)
- **Adaptation/fit**: Digital CDs showed comparable or better fit than conventional CDs
- **Clinician's quality assessment**: Digital CDs rated similarly or better by clinicians
- **Masticatory efficiency**: Similar between groups
- **Occlusal force**: Similar between groups

### PROMs (qualitative)
- **OHRQoL**: No meaningful difference between digital and conventional CDs
- **Final choice/willingness-to-pay**: Patients did not strongly prefer one method over the other

### Overall Conclusion
Digitally fabricated CDs showed similar or better clinical performance than conventional CDs; digital manufacturing method did not influence PROMs.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Limited number of high-quality RCTs available at time of search
- Heterogeneity across included studies in terms of digital protocols and conventional techniques used
- Most studies had relatively short follow-up periods
- Risk of bias concerns in several included studies (RoB 2 / ROBINS-I findings)
- GRADE certainty of evidence likely low-to-moderate for key outcomes
- Future high-quality RCTs with standardized protocols and longer follow-up needed

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary

- **CAD-CAM**: Computer-aided design and computer-aided manufacturing
- **CD**: Complete denture
- **PROM**: Patient-reported outcome measure
- **OHRQoL**: Oral health-related quality of life
- **WTP**: Willingness-to-pay
- **ROBINS-I**: Risk of Bias in Non-randomized Studies of Interventions
- **RoB 2**: Cochrane revised risk of bias tool for randomized trials
- **GRADE**: Grading of Recommendations, Assessment, Development and Evaluation
- **PRISMA**: Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses
- **MD**: Mean difference
