## Why Ingested

Super-Bond 4-META/MMA-TBB의 대표 적응증인 수직치근파절(VRF) 단편 접착(intraoral 또는 의도적 재식립 후 extraoral)의 최고근거(SR)를 정리하기 위해. 6개 study 중 4개가 4-META/MMA-TBB resin으로 파절선을 접착했고, intentional replantation 프로토콜(Okaguchi)에서 6/6 성공을 보고하는 등 Super-Bond fragment-reattachment의 임상 신호와 동시에 SR 수준의 evidence-quality caveat(전부 case series, low quality, MA 불가)을 함께 보여준다. 4-META adhesive monomer 성분/기전 reference로 [[resin-bonding/kodaira-2022-resin-bonding-titanium-adhesive-monomers-review]]에 연결.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (8 articles / 6 case series, ~64 teeth) of surgical management of confirmed vertical root fractures (VRF): 4 of 6 studies bonded fracture fragments with 4-META/MMA-TBB resin (Super-Bond), with widely heterogeneous survival; evidence is sparse and of low quality (all case series, no meta-analysis possible).

## 한줄요약

확진된 수직치근파절(VRF) 외과적 관리에 대한 체계적 문헌고찰(논문 8편/연구 6건, case series만): 6개 연구 중 4개가 4-META/MMA-TBB 레진(Super-Bond)으로 파절 단편을 접착했고 생존율이 매우 이질적이며, 전부 case series·low quality라 메타분석 불가·표준 술식 권고 불가.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First SR of surgical VRF management.** No prior systematic review/meta-analysis of surgical techniques for preserving roots with confirmed VRF existed; this fills that gap.
2. **Quantifies the 4-META/MMA-TBB (Super-Bond) footprint in the VRF-repair literature**: of the 6 included studies, **4 bonded fracture fragments with 4-META/MMA-TBB resin** (Hayashi 2002/2004, Sugaya 2001, Okaguchi 2019, and Selden 1996 used 4-META + silver glass-ionomer), 1 used MTA (Taschieri), 1 used resin-ionomer cement (Anderegg).
3. **Two technique families identified**: (a) extraction + extraoral 4-META/MMA-TBB resin bonding + (intentional) replantation; (b) direct in-situ repair via flap elevation with a bioceramic/sealing material (MTA) while the tooth stays attached.
4. **Evidence-quality verdict**: all included studies are observational case series with small samples and high heterogeneity → evidence "sparse and of low quality"; no quantitative synthesis possible; cannot recommend any technique as a standard approach.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Question (PICOS)**: efficacy of surgical techniques for preservation of a tooth with VRF.
- **Inclusion**: case series with ≥5 treated patients. **Case reports and series < 5 patients excluded.**
- **Databases**: MEDLINE (OVID), EMBASE, Cochrane Central + grey literature (Greylit, OpenGrey) + trial registers + manual journal search (issues since 2000). No language restriction. Last search 5 Jan 2024.
- **Selection**: two reviewers (ST, SC) independent screening; third reviewer (ER) for disagreement; Cohen's kappa for concordance.
- **Risk of bias**: JBI Critical Appraisal Tool for case series.
- **Synthesis**: comparative meta-analysis not feasible due to heterogeneity → qualitative single-study synthesis.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Yield**: 8 articles = 6 studies; all case series; two with comparative data. Countries: Japan ×2, USA ×2, Italy ×1, Taiwan ×1.

**4-META/MMA-TBB (Super-Bond) studies (verbatim numbers):**

| Study (year) | Material / technique | n teeth | Outcome | Follow-up |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Hayashi 2002/2004 (Japan) | 4-META/MMA-TBB resin, extraoral (extraction + replant) & intraoral | 26 teeth | **8 failed** (7 of bonded-after-extraction, 1 of bonded-without-extraction); 6 successes, 12 functional with periapical lesion; lower success for posterior teeth & fractures > 2/3 from cervical | mean 34.9 mo |
| Sugaya 2001 (Japan) | 4-META/MMA-TBB resin, extraoral bonding + replant (surgical group B) | 12 teeth | **9/12 functional, good prognosis**; 3 extracted (deep pocket/mobility, refracture). PD 7.4→4.6 mm; bleeding 91%→8.3%; radiographic bone level 18.8%→29.2%; no ankylosis/resorption | > 22 mo (PD/bleeding at 6 mo) |
| Okaguchi 2019 (Taiwan) | 4-META/MMA-TBB resin, **intentional replantation** + root-fragment bonding | 6 teeth | **6/6 clinically successful**; radiographic: 4 complete healing, 1 nearly complete, 1 not assessed | 33–74 mo (mean ~50 ± 15) |
| Selden 1996 (USA) | **4-META** + silver glass-ionomer + e-PTFE GBR, in-situ (incomplete VRF) | 6 teeth | **5 failed (2–11 mo); 1 survived to 12 mo then split** → all failed long-term | 2–12 mo |

**Non-Super-Bond studies:**
- Taschieri 2009/2010 (Italy, same cohort): MTA seal + calcium-sulfate bone fill, incomplete VRF, maxillary anterior. **5/7 successful at 33 mo; 2 extracted** (lateral incisors). Excluded teeth with PD > 4 mm and adverse radiographic signs.
- Anderegg 2018 (USA): resin-ionomer cement repair, 5 teeth. **All 5 failed within 6 months**; authors caution against resin-ionomer for VRF repair.

**Bottom line**: 4-META/MMA-TBB extraoral bonding + (intentional) replantation shows the most favourable case-series signals (Sugaya 9/12; Okaguchi 6/6), but heterogeneity is too high and evidence too low-quality to recommend any technique as standard. Resin-ionomer (Anderegg) and silver-glass-ionomer (Selden) repairs failed; glass-ionomer/resin-ionomer cements are NOT supported for VRF repair. Complete fracture with split tooth is the presumed failure mode.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **All included studies are observational case series**, small n, high heterogeneity → external validity low; no causal inference; no meta-analysis.
- JBI appraisal: incomplete demographic reporting across all studies; symptoms reported in only one study; statistics appropriate only in Sugaya.
- Repair feasible only for **incomplete** VRF, requires correct diagnosis (CBCT insufficient; deep narrow isolated probing defect on one side = standard diagnostic clue).
- **Future**: randomised comparative clinical trials with accurate case characterisation (even short follow-up) needed before any technique becomes standard.

## 7. Glossary

- **VRF (Vertical Root Fracture)**: axial fracture through cementum, dentin and root canal system; incomplete (one side) or complete (both sides); bucco-lingual usual direction.
- **4-META/MMA-TBB resin (Super-Bond / C&B Metabond)**: 4-methacryloxyethyl trimellitate anhydride / methyl methacrylate–tri-n-butylborane adhesive resin; flagship monomer for VRF fragment reattachment and intentional replantation bonding.
- **Intentional replantation**: deliberate extraction, extraoral repair/bonding of the fractured root, then replantation into the socket.
- **MTA (Mineral Trioxide Aggregate)**: bioceramic used to seal the fracture in-situ (Taschieri technique).
- **JBI Critical Appraisal Tool**: instrument for methodological quality of case-series studies.
- **PROSPERO**: international prospective register of systematic reviews (CRD42024524356).
