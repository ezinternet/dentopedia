## Why Ingested

기존 MSMT 기반 절대값 기준(Chan: 1/3 초과 시 이비인후과 협진; Chen: 1/2 초과 시 예방 FESS)에서 한 걸음 나아가, **MOD(자연공-동저 거리)**와 **MSMT/MOD 비율**이라는 상대적·기능적 지표를 처음 제시한 파일럿 연구. [[wiki/sinus-lift/lateral/chan-2011-sinusitis-risk-mucosal-thickening]] 등 기존 MSMT-only 권고를 확장해, 자연공 상태와 비율 기반 위험 분층을 통합하는 필요성을 실증한다.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective study (n=120) from a Korean tertiary center demonstrating that MSMT/MOD ratio — not MOD alone — significantly differentiates preoperative sinusitis-risk groups and should guide sinus augmentation planning alongside osteomeatal complex assessment.

## 한줄요약

한국 3차 병원 후향적 연구(n=120)로, 상악동점막두께(MSMT)/자연공-동저 거리(MOD) 비율이 군간 유의한 차이를 보임을 확인; MOD 단독은 유의하지 않으며 MSMT/MOD 비율 및 자연공 상태를 함께 평가해야 함.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Introduces **MOD** (distance between maxillary sinus ostium and sinus floor) as a measurable CBCT parameter — previously absent from preoperative risk checklists.
2. Proposes the **MSMT/MOD ratio** as a relative occupancy metric that captures how much of the sinus depth is occupied by mucosal disease — a more sensitive differentiator than absolute MSMT.
3. Demonstrates that MSMT/MOD ratio stratifies three clinically meaningful groups (no antibiotic, preop antibiotic, implant-after-FESS) with significant between-group differences at all three comparisons.
4. Highlights that **osteomeatal complex (OMC) status** independently predicts sinusitis: one case with low MSMT/MOD (0.23) but ostium obstruction still developed postoperative sinusitis.
5. Reports low postoperative sinusitis rate (1.6% vs. literature mean 5.1%), attributed to systematic dentist–ENT collaboration and preoperative CBCT review.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Setting**: Single tertiary center (Seoul St. Mary's Hospital); IRB-approved (KC19RESI0517)
- **Inclusion**: All periodontology → otolaryngology referrals for maxillary implants over 11 years
- **Imaging**: CBCT for all patients pre-implantation
- **Groups**:
  - Group A (n=95): No preoperative antibiotics
  - Group B (n=16): Preoperative antibiotics administered
  - Group C (n=9): Implant placed after FESS
- **Parameters measured**: MSMT (including cysts/polyps), MOD, MSMT/MOD ratio, ostium obstruction, anatomical variants (paradoxical middle turbinate, Haller cells, concha bullosa), postoperative sinusitis occurrence
- **Statistics**: Kruskal–Wallis test for group differences; post-hoc Mann–Whitney with Bonferroni correction; SAS v9.4

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Parameter | Group A (no Ab) | Group B (preop Ab) | Group C (post-FESS) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mean MSMT (mm) | 5.68 ± 5.74 | 11.84 ± 8.22 | 25.3 ± 11.1 |
| Mean MOD (mm) | 30.33 ± 5.08 | 29.98 ± 7.31 | 31.35 ± 6.81 |
| Mean MSMT/MOD ratio | 0.19 ± 0.19 | 0.39 ± 0.25 | 0.78 ± 0.24 |

- **MSMT group differences**: A vs B p=0.001; B vs C p=0.003; C vs A p<0.0001 ✓
- **MOD group differences**: A vs B p=0.804; B vs C p=0.502; C vs A p=0.637 (NS) ✗
- **MSMT/MOD ratio group differences**: A vs B p=0.001; B vs C p<0.0001; C vs A p<0.0001 ✓
- Overall mean: MSMT 7.97 ± 8.47 mm; MOD 30.36 ± 5.51 mm; MSMT/MOD ratio 0.26 ± 0.26
- Postoperative sinusitis: 2/120 (1.6%)
- Anatomical variants: concha bullosa 25.0%; Haller cells 11.0%; paradoxical middle turbinate 0.8%
- Ostium obstruction on CBCT: 2 cases (1.7%) — both among the sinusitis complication cases
- FESS threshold used at this institution: MSMT/MOD ratio ≈ 0.78 (mean of Group C) combined with antibiotic non-response

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Retrospective single-center design; no randomization or control arm
- Postoperative CT not routinely performed (radiation/cost); sinusitis detection may be underestimated
- Selection bias: tertiary center with strict preoperative protocol; not generalizable to general dental practices
- Small Group C (n=9) limits statistical power for FESS threshold derivation
- No ROC analysis to establish a definitive MSMT/MOD cutoff for clinical decision-making
- Authors call for multicenter prospective studies to establish evidence-based thresholds

## 6. Related Work

- Chen et al.: mucosal occupancy ≥1/2 sinus height → preventive FESS
- Chan et al.: mucosal thickening >1/3 → ENT consultation
- This study adds MSMT/MOD ratio as a relative metric and explicitly links MOD measurement to preoperative risk stratification

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| MSMT | Maxillary Sinus Mucosal Thickness — CBCT-measured thickness including mucosal disease (cysts, polyps, thickening) |
| MOD | Distance between the Maxillary sinus Ostium and the sinus floor — measures the functional drainage height |
| MSMT/MOD ratio | Proportion of sinus height occupied by mucosal disease; captures relative obstructive burden |
| OMC | Osteomeatal complex — the final common drainage pathway for the maxillary sinus |
| FESS | Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery — endoscopic surgery to restore sinus drainage |
| Haller cells | Infraorbital ethmoid air cells that may impinge on the maxillary ostium |
| Paradoxical middle turbinate | Lateral convexity of the middle turbinate, potentially narrowing the middle meatus |
| Concha bullosa | Pneumatization of the middle turbinate; associated with meatal narrowing |
