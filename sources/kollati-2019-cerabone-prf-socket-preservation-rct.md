## Why Ingested

Tests Cerabone (naturally derived bovine hydroxyapatite, BCHA) + PRF vs unassisted extraction alone in socket preservation — provides PRF adjunct evidence with a specific xenograft product that is commercially available. Fills a product-specific gap complementing the SR+MA evidence base. [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/fischer-2022-bone-envelope-arp-dbbm-sr-ma]]

## One-line Summary

Split-mouth RCT (n=25) comparing Cerabone + PRF + collagen plug vs atraumatic extraction alone: ridge width loss 1.47 mm vs 2.75 mm, bone fill 8.7% higher at test site at 6 months.

## 한줄요약

분할구강 RCT (n=25): Cerabone+PRF+콜라겐 플러그 처치군에서 대조군 대비 폭경 소실 1.47 mm vs 2.75 mm, 방사선학적 골충전 8.7% 향상 (6개월).

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of Indian Society of Periodontology
- **Volume/Issue**: 23(2):145–151
- **PMID**: 30983786; PMCID: PMC6434722
- **Study design**: Split-mouth RCT — within-patient control
- **Sample**: 25 patients (17–50 years), each contributing one test and one control extraction site
- **Follow-up**: 6 months
- **Ethics**: Declaration of Helsinki; Institutional Ethics Committee approval

## 2. Key Contributions

- Evaluated Cerabone™ (naturally derived BCHA) combined with PRF matrix and a collagen plug against unassisted natural healing
- Demonstrated significant reduction in ridge width loss and greater radiographic bone fill at treated sites
- Highlighted PRF's role as a growth-factor reservoir enhancing xenograft integration
- One of few RCTs using Cerabone (bovine HA) specifically, rather than sintered HA or DBBM

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Participants**: Patients requiring extraction of ≥2 non-adjacent teeth. Exclusions: smokers, uncontrolled systemic disease, pregnant women, radiation history, immediate implant candidates.

**Randomization**: Coin-flip method assigned which extraction site was test vs control within the same patient.

**Test group procedure**:
1. PRF preparation: 10 mL antecubital blood, centrifuged at 3000 RPM × 10 min; PRF clot collected from middle layer
2. Atraumatic extraction under 2% lignocaine + 1:200,000 adrenaline
3. Cerabone™ granules packed into socket
4. PRF membrane placed over graft
5. Collagen plug inserted at socket orifice; suturing

**Control group**: Atraumatic extraction only, primary closure suturing.

**Outcome measurements**:
- *Clinical*: Ridge width (buccal-lingual) at crestal level using Digital Vernier Caliper; ridge height via occlusal stent with pre-fabricated holes at mid-buccal, mid-lingual, mesial, distal
- *Radiographic*: Standardized IOPA (long-cone paralleling technique) at baseline and 6 months; bone fill % using 1 mm² counting grid and AUTOCAD software (Autodesk, San Rafael, CA)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Control (extraction alone) | Test (Cerabone + PRF) |
|---|---|---|
| Mean ridge width loss | 2.75 ± 1.49 mm | 1.47 ± 1.44 mm |
| Radiographic bone fill | baseline | +2.31 mm (8.7% increase) |

- Both outcomes statistically significant in favor of test group
- No adverse events or infections reported at test sites
- Collagen plug facilitated soft tissue sealing without membrane exposure

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample (n=25); single-center study at one Indian institution
- Smokers excluded — limits generalizability to a high-risk population
- Only 6-month follow-up; no implant placement outcomes reported
- No histomorphometric analysis of bone quality at biopsy
- No comparison arm testing Cerabone alone (without PRF) — cannot isolate PRF contribution
- AUTOCAD-based radiographic analysis is less standardized than CBCT or digital subtraction

## 6. Related Work

- Cerabone is a naturally derived BCHA — distinct from sintered products; slow resorption rate is a feature
- PRF + xenograft combinations reviewed in intrabony defect and sinus lift literature; this study extends evidence to ARP context
- Study excludes smokers, creating a gap addressed by Alrayyes 2022 (PRF in smokers)

## 7. Glossary

- **Cerabone™**: Naturally derived bovine hydroxyapatite (BCHA) xenograft; slow-resorbing particulate
- **PRF (Platelet-Rich Fibrin)**: Autologous fibrin matrix derived from centrifuged blood; contains PDGF, TGF-β, VEGF
- **PRF matrix**: Pressed PRF clot into membrane form
- **Bone fill %**: Radiographic measure of bone regeneration within extraction socket using counting grid method
- **Split-mouth RCT**: Study design where each patient serves as their own control
