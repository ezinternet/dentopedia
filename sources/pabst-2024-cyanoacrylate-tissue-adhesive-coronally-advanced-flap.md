## Why Ingested

Part of a focused collection on suture techniques & primary wound closure (this batch = suture biomechanics / tissue adhesive / gingival graft suturing). This ex-vivo study moves the suture-mechanics question into the intraoral periodontal-plastic-surgery domain — quantifying how cyanoacrylate tissue adhesive vs sutures affects coronally advanced flap (CAF) stability. It extends the dermatologic biomechanics of [[suture-wound-closure/marsidi-2020-measuring-forces-suture-techniques-wound-closure]] into oral flap fixation and pairs with the gingival-graft suturing RCT [[suture-wound-closure/shakiliyeva-2025-conventional-modified-sling-suture-free-gingival-graft]].

## One-line Summary

Ex-vivo biomechanical study (36 half-pig mandibles, n=12 per group): cyanoacrylate tissue adhesive (TA) gave significantly higher CAF marginal flap stability than sutures alone (5.20 vs 0.88 N maximum tensile force), and TA + sutures combined was highest (8.50 N; all p<0.001).

## 한줄요약

생체외 역학 연구 (반쪽 돼지 하악골 36개, 군당 n=12): 시아노아크릴레이트 조직접착제(TA)가 봉합 단독보다 치관변위판막(CAF) 변연 안정성을 유의하게 높였고(최대 인장력 5.20 vs 0.88 N), TA+봉합 병용이 가장 높았음(8.50 N; 모두 p<0.001).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies that cyanoacrylate-based tissue adhesive (CTA) markedly increases coronally advanced flap (CAF) marginal stability vs conventional suturing alone.
- Shows additive effect: combined suture + TA yields the highest pull-off resistance.
- Provides mechanistic rationale: polymerized CTA bonds over the entire flap extent (areal fixation) vs only punctual fixation by sutures.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Model:** 36 half-pig mandibles (pigs 6-8 months, 200-220 lb), 3 groups of n=12.
  - Group I: CAF fixed with sutures (sling and tag suture technique; vertical releasing incisions closed with single interrupted sutures).
  - Group II: CAF fixed with TA (three layers).
  - Group III: CAF fixed with sutures + TA combined.
- **Defect:** 3-mm gingival recession created at mandibular premolar (P3) by gingivectomy (15c blade), CEJ marked; CAF prepared per de Sanctis & Zucchelli split-full-split approach, advanced to 1 mm above CEJ.
- **Tissue adhesive:** PeriAcryl 90 HV (GluStitch), high-viscosity n-butyl + 2-octyl cyanoacrylate; three layers, ≥30 s between applications.
- **Biomechanical testing:** Instron Model 5942 universal testing machine, BlueHill software; mandible clamped occlusal-down, test suture looped to machine, pulled upward parallel to tooth long axis at 0.5 mm/s until marked CEJ became visible; maximum tensile force recorded.
- **Statistics:** Mann-Whitney U with continuity correction; linear regression for KT/GT vs tensile force.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Maximum tensile force (force to displace gingival margin apically):

| Group | Maximum tensile force (N ± SD) |
|---|---|
| I — Sutures alone | 0.88 ± 0.61 |
| II — Tissue adhesive | 5.20 ± 1.47 |
| III — Sutures + TA | 8.50 ± 2.30 |

- II > I: p<0.001; III > I: p<0.001; III > II: p<0.001 (all significant).
- KT and GT were **not** significantly correlated with maximum tensile force (p=0.19 and p=0.46).
- KTW: I 4.33, II 4.83, III 4.75 mm; only I vs III borderline (p=0.049); I vs II ns (p=0.076); II vs III ns (p=0.866).
- GT: I 2.13, II 2.71, III 2.29 mm; I vs II p=0.003; II vs III p=0.031; I vs III ns (p=0.313).
- Conclusion: cyanoacrylate-based TA can increase marginal flap stability vs sutures in CAF procedures; useful adjunct where high flap stability is required.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Ex-vivo pig-mandible model — no healing, vascular, or clinical recession-coverage endpoints; authors stress results transfer to clinic only with limitations and that long-term follow-up data are needed.
- Tensile force is an immediate mechanical proxy, not a wound-healing outcome.
- Single TA product and single suture technique (sling and tag) tested.

## 6. Related Work

- Cites prior demonstration that sling and tag (SAT) suture is superior to simple interrupted for CAF flap stability (ref 6).

## 7. Glossary

- **Coronally advanced flap (CAF):** root-coverage periodontal plastic surgery procedure repositioning the flap coronally over a recession defect.
- **Cyanoacrylate tissue adhesive (CTA/TA):** monomer adhesive (n-butyl + 2-octyl cyanoacrylate) that polymerizes in a humid/anionic environment, bonding tissue areally; here PeriAcryl 90 HV.
- **Marginal flap stability (MFS):** resistance of the flap margin to apical pull-off (maximum tensile force).
- **Sling and tag (SAT) suture:** flap-stabilizing suture technique used in group I.
- **CEJ:** cemento-enamel junction; reference landmark for flap positioning and the test endpoint (visible = failure).
