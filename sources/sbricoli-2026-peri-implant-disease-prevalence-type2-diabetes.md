## Why Ingested

Most existing peri-implantitis pages in our wiki frame T2DM as an established systemic risk factor and focus on keratinized mucosa, prosthetic design, and surface decontamination. This Italian single-center cross-sectional study (Sbricoli 2026) provides a direct **T2DM vs non-DM head-to-head prevalence** data point that *contradicts* the prevailing "diabetes as major risk factor" narrative — finding no statistical difference in peri-implant mucositis/peri-implantitis between well-controlled diabetics and non-diabetics. It contrasts the disease-association framing in [[implants/peri-implantitis/diaz-2022-what-is-the-prevalence]] and complements the risk-factor / prosthetic-design discussion in [[implants/peri-implantitis/lin-2025-influence-of-prosthetic-designs]].

## One-line Summary

Single-center cross-sectional study (70 subjects, 227 implants; 35 T2DM vs 35 non-DM): no statistically significant difference in peri-implant disease (80% vs 77%, p=0.99), mucositis (51% vs 63%, p=0.47), or peri-implantitis (51% vs 43%, p=0.63) — but underpowered, with high periodontitis history confounding.

## 한줄요약

단일기관 횡단연구(70명·임플란트 227개; 제2형 당뇨 35명 vs 비당뇨 35명): 임플란트주위질환(80% vs 77%, p=0.99)·점막염(51% vs 63%)·주위염(51% vs 43%) 모두 두 군 간 유의차 없음 — 단, 검정력 부족·치주염 과거력(83~94%) 교란 존재.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: "Prevalence of Peri-Implant Diseases in Patients With Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus: A Cross-Sectional Study"
- **Authors**: Sbricoli L, Feltracco G, Cavallin F, D'Arienzo D, Bergamin S, Stellini E, Bressan E (University of Padua / Castelfranco Veneto Hospital, Italy)
- **Journal**: Clinical and Experimental Dental Research 2026;12(2):e70352
- **DOI**: 10.1002/cre2.70352 — PMID 42035463 — PMCID PMC13110833
- **Type**: Single-center, cross-sectional, observational study

## 2. Key Contributions

- Direct T2DM-vs-non-DM comparison of peri-implant disease prevalence at both subject and implant level, using current EFP S3 (Herrera et al.) diagnostic definitions.
- Finds **no statistically significant association** between well-controlled T2DM (all on pharmacotherapy, HbA1c-confirmed) and peri-implant mucositis or peri-implantitis — challenging the "diabetes as major peri-implantitis risk factor" framing.
- Reinforces plaque control (PI), longer loading period, and cement-retention (cement remnants) as the operative drivers of peri-implant disease in this cohort, rather than diabetes per se.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Single-center, cross-sectional, observational; Saint Giacomo hospital, Castelfranco Veneto, Italy; Mar–Aug 2024; ethics CET-ANV 0005306/24.
- Eligible: ≥30 y, ≥1 implant in function ≥1 year. Exposed = T2DM (pre-existing diagnosis, HbA1c-confirmed, all on pharmacological therapy); unexposed = non-diabetic. Excluded: prediabetes, metabolic syndrome, gestational diabetes, pregnancy/breastfeeding.
- Six-site periodontal charting per tooth/implant (PCP-UNC 15); PPD, FMPS plaque index, FMBS bleeding on probing, suppuration, mucosal hyperplasia, mobility; periapical radiograph per implant with ImageJ-calibrated marginal bone loss (implant shoulder = reference, no baseline radiographs available). Single operator (G.F.).
- Diagnosis per EFP S3 (Herrera et al.); without prior data, peri-implantitis = BoP/suppuration + PPD ≥6 mm + bone level ≥3 mm apical of intraosseous portion.
- A priori sample size (R 4.4): 70 patients (35/35), 80% power to detect peri-implantitis 8%→35%. Subject-level: Mann-Whitney / Chi-square / Fisher. Implant-level: logistic regression with cluster-robust SE (implants nested in subjects). p<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Sample**: 70 subjects (32 M, 38 F; median age 73, IQR 66–80), 235 implants → 227 analyzed (8 excluded). Diabetic median age 77 vs non-DM 71 (p=0.004).
- **Subject-level prevalence** (T2DM vs non-DM): peri-implant disease 80% vs 77% (p=0.99); mucositis 51% vs 63% (p=0.47); peri-implantitis 51% vs 43% (p=0.63).
- **Implant-level** (104 vs 123 implants): peri-implant disease 62% vs 66% (p=0.67); mucositis 28% vs 31% (p=0.73); peri-implantitis 34% vs 35% (p=0.91).
- **Plaque index** higher in diseased subjects (median 27% vs 14%, p=0.003).
- **Loading period**: peri-implant disease associated with longer loading (13 vs 10 y, p=0.02); mucositis (14 vs 11 y, p=0.04); peri-implantitis NS (p=0.72).
- **Prosthesis** (screw n=61 vs cement n=166): peri-implantitis 21% vs 39% (p=0.08, trend); mucositis 39% vs 26% (p=0.08, trend); overall disease NS.
- History of periodontitis very high in both groups (83% diabetic, 94% non-DM); all periodontitis subjects on supportive peri-implant therapy.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Cross-sectional → no causal inference.
- Small sample; observed peri-implantitis prevalence much higher than the 8% planning assumption and nearly identical between groups → study **underpowered** to detect a true difference.
- No examiner calibration, no controlled probing force; no baseline radiographs (bone loss estimated from implant shoulder).
- Very high baseline periodontitis prevalence in both arms likely **confounds/masks** any specific T2DM effect.
- Authors call for larger prospective cohorts with standardized protocols.

## 6. Related Work

- Dalago et al. (183 pts, 916 implants): diabetes NOT associated with peri-implantitis (concordant).
- Weinstein et al. (248 pts, 1162 implants): significant diabetes–peri-implantitis association (discordant).
- Wagner et al. 2022 SR: implant rehabilitation in T2DM safe and predictable.
- Reis et al. SR: weighted mean peri-implant mucositis prevalence 63% (concordant with this cohort).

## 7. Glossary

- **T2DM**: Type 2 diabetes mellitus.
- **Peri-implant mucositis**: reversible inflammation confined to peri-implant soft tissue (BoP/suppuration, no bone loss beyond initial remodeling).
- **Peri-implantitis**: inflammation with progressive supporting bone loss (here BoP/suppuration + PPD ≥6 mm + bone ≥3 mm apical).
- **AGE / RAGE**: advanced glycation end-products and their receptor; hyperglycemia-driven inflammatory pathway.
- **PI / BoP / PPD**: plaque index / bleeding on probing / pocket probing depth.
- **EFP S3**: European Federation of Periodontology S3-level clinical practice guideline (Herrera et al.).
