## Why Ingested

Sealant-surveillance / seal-vs-restore 감시 인제스트 — minimal-intervention 우식관리(sealing, selective/stepwise removal, Hall technique, NRCC)의 최상위 근거인 Cochrane SR+NMA로, 기존 [[caries/urquhart-2019-nonrestorative-treatments-caries-network-meta-analysis]]의 비복원 NMA를 복원적 측면(절삭 정도·실링)으로 보완한다. Hall technique RCT 묶음([[caries/mohamed-2025-conventional-vs-hall-pmc-primary-molars-rct]], [[caries/konukman-turker-2026-hall-vs-modified-hall-primary-molars-rct]])의 상위 합성 근거 anchor로 활용.

## One-line Summary

Cochrane SR + network meta-analysis (27 RCTs, 3350 participants, 4195 teeth) of carious-tissue-removal strategies: for deep lesions, conventional complete removal (CR) had the highest probability of failure vs selective (SE), stepwise (SW), and Hall technique (HT); in primary teeth HT beat CR (OR 8.35 for CR-vs-HT failure) and NRCC.

## 한줄요약

코크란 SR+네트워크 메타분석(27 RCT·환자 3350명·치아 4195개): 깊은 우식에서 완전절삭 후 통상수복(CR)이 선택적절삭(SE)·단계적절삭(SW)·홀테크닉(HT) 대비 실패 확률이 가장 높았고, 유치에서는 HT가 CR(CR 실패 OR 8.35)·NRCC보다 우수 — 대부분 근거확실성은 낮음/매우낮음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Cochrane systematic review with pairwise meta-analysis + network meta-analysis (deep-lesion subgroup)
- **Journal**: Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews 2021, Issue 7, Art. No. CD013039
- **DOI**: 10.1002/14651858.CD013039.pub2 (PMID 34280957, PMCID PMC8406990)
- **Search date**: to 21 July 2020 (four bibliographic databases)
- **Evidence base**: 27 RCTs, 3350 participants, 4195 teeth/lesions, 11 countries, published 1977–2020
- **Designs**: 24 parallel-group, 3 split-mouth. 2 adults-only, 20 children/adolescents-only, 5 both
- **Dentition**: 10 permanent, 16 primary, 1 both
- **Lesion depth**: 3 studies non-cavitated; 12 cavitated-deep; 12 cavitated-not-deep / varying depth
- **Note**: PMC full-text JATS body was NOT retrievable (paywalled); structured abstract + plain-language summary only (`full_text: false`)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Six management strategies compared head-to-head: conventional restoration / non-selective removal (**CR**), selective excavation (**SE**), stepwise removal (**SW**), sealing with sealant materials, Hall Technique preformed-metal-crown sealing (**HT**), and non-restorative cavity control (**NRCC**).
- Primary outcome = **failure**, a composite of pulp exposure, endodontic therapy, tooth extraction, and restorative complications (incl. resealing of sealed lesions).
- First Cochrane NMA ranking carious-tissue-removal strategies for **deep lesions** — establishes that minimal-intervention (less/no carious-tissue removal) is at least non-inferior, and often superior, to complete removal.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- RCTs only; vital pulps (no irreversible pulpitis/necrosis); lesions conventionally judged to need restoration (cavitated/micro-cavitated, or occlusal non-cavitated extending radiographically into dentine).
- Dichotomous outcomes → odds ratios (OR) with 95% CI; pairwise random/fixed effects.
- **Network meta-analysis** for failure restricted to the deep-lesion subgroup (CR vs SE vs SW vs HT) to rank relative effectiveness.
- Risk of bias: all 27 studies at overall **high risk of bias**; GRADE certainty per comparison.
- 17 studies compared CR vs a less-invasive arm: SE (8), SW (4), HT (2), sealing with sealant materials (4), NRCC (1). Other comparisons: SE vs HT (2); SE vs SW (4); SE vs sealant (2); sealant vs no-seal (2).
- Follow-up: from none (intra-operative pulp exposure) to 120 months; most common 12–24 months.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Sealing with sealant materials (non-cavitated / cavitated-not-deep)** — all very-low certainty, insufficient evidence:
- Sealing vs CR: OR 5.00 (0.51–49.27); 1 study, 41 permanent teeth, cavitated
- Sealing vs SE: OR 3.11 (0.11–85.52); 2 studies, 82 primary teeth, cavitated
- Sealing vs no treatment: OR 0.05 (0.00–2.71); 2 studies, 103 permanent teeth, non-cavitated

**Cavitated-not-deep, primary teeth (HT / CR / SE / NRCC):**
- CR vs HT: OR **8.35** (3.73–18.68); 2 studies, 249 teeth — **low certainty** (CR fails more often than HT)
- HT vs NRCC: OR 0.19 (0.05–0.74); 1 study, 84 teeth — very low (HT better than NRCC)
- SE vs HT: OR 8.94 (0.57–139.67); 2 studies, 586 teeth — very low, insufficient
- CR vs NRCC: OR 1.16 (0.50–2.71); 1 study, 102 teeth — very low, insufficient

**Deep lesions (CR / SE / SW):**
- CR vs SW, permanent: OR **2.06** (1.34–3.17); 3 studies, 398 teeth — **moderate certainty** (CR worse)
- CR vs SW, primary: OR 2.43 (0.65–9.12); 1 study, 63 teeth — very low
- CR vs SE, permanent: OR **11.32** (1.97–65.02); 2 studies, 179 teeth — very low (CR worse)
- CR vs SE, primary: OR 4.43 (1.04–18.77); 4 studies, 265 teeth — very low (CR worse)
- CR vs SE, cavitated-not-deep: OR 0.62 (0.21–1.88); 204 teeth — very low, insufficient
- SW vs SE, permanent: OR **2.25** (1.33–3.82); 3 studies, 371 teeth — **moderate certainty** (SW worse than SE)
- SW vs SE, primary: OR 2.05 (0.49–8.62); 2 studies, 126 teeth — very low

**NMA ranking (deep lesions):** probability of failure **greatest for CR** vs SE, SW and HT.

**Bottom line:** vs CR, fewer failures with HT and SE in primary dentition, and with SE and SW in permanent dentition. SE ranks best for permanent deep lesions (beats both CR and SW).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- All 27 studies at high risk of bias; small samples and few failure events → wide CIs.
- Most comparisons low / very-low GRADE certainty (only CR-vs-SW and SW-vs-SE in permanent teeth reached moderate).
- Sealant-material comparisons all single/double-study, very imprecise — the seal-vs-restore question remains underpowered.
- Authors note future research could change conclusions; longer-term and lower-bias trials needed.
- This source ingested from PMC structured abstract only (full text paywalled).

## 7. Glossary

- **CR** — conventional restoration / non-selective (complete) carious tissue removal then filling.
- **SE** — selective excavation / selective carious tissue removal: soft dentine left over pulp, peripheral caries fully removed, then restore in one visit.
- **SW** — stepwise removal: leave soft dentine, provisionally restore, re-enter after months to remove more, then definitively restore.
- **HT** — Hall Technique: preformed metal crown cemented over a carious primary molar, sealing the lesion without any caries removal or local anaesthesia.
- **NRCC** — non-restorative cavity control: open/clean the cavity, biofilm control + diet/hygiene coaching to arrest, no restoration.
- **Sealing (sealant materials)** — resin or glass-ionomer coating over the lesion to inactivate it.
- **Failure (primary outcome)** — composite of pulp exposure, endodontic therapy, extraction, restorative complications (incl. resealing).
- **NMA** — network meta-analysis: combines direct + indirect comparisons to rank multiple interventions.
