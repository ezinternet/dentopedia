## One-line Summary
Materials 2021;14:960 (Mansoura/Zurich) — in vitro 2×2 factorial: zirconia crowns × (air-abrasion only vs air-abrasion + hot HF/HNO₃ etching) × (Panavia SA Cement vs GC LinkForce) on 40 premolars; hot acid etching significantly improves retention (p=0.001); cement type alone NS (p=0.882); best: air-abrasion + hot acid + Panavia SA.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Materials (MDPI) 2021;14:960
- Received: 15 January 2021; Accepted: 15 February 2021; Published: 18 February 2021
- Type: In vitro factorial study
- Affiliation: Mansoura and Horus U. (Egypt); University of Zurich (Switzerland)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Quantifies hot acid etching as an alternative/adjunct to air abrasion for zirconia bonding
- Compares two adhesive cement types head-to-head
- Identifies mode-of-failure differences between cements (Panavia SA → mixed; G-CEM LinkForce → adhesive)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 40 maxillary premolars prepared with proximal extensions
- Zirconia crowns CAD/CAM fabricated, divided 4 groups (n=10):
  - AP: air-abrasion + Panavia SA Cement
  - AL: air-abrasion + GC LinkForce
  - AHP: air-abrasion + hot acid etching (48% HF + 69% HNO₃) + Panavia SA Cement
  - AHL: air-abrasion + hot acid etching + GC LinkForce
- Retention: dislodgement load (N) → retention strength (MPa)
- Statistics: 2-way ANOVA + interaction

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Hot acid etching: significant retention improvement (p = 0.001)
- Cement type alone: NS (p = 0.882)
- Group AHP (hot acid + Panavia SA) — highest mean retention strength
- Group AL (no etching, GC LinkForce) — lowest mean retention strength
- Mode of failure:
  - Panavia SA Cement: mixed
  - GC LinkForce: adhesive

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- In vitro only; no thermocycling/fatigue
- Hot acid handling: significant clinical safety concern (concentrated HF + HNO₃)
- Limited to 2 cement types
- No comparison with primer-only (e.g. Z-Prime Plus, MDP only) protocols

## 6. Related Work
- Air abrasion as zirconia bonding baseline
- MDP-containing primers/cements as standard
- Piranha solution and hot acids — emerging surface treatment options

## 7. Glossary
- HF: 불산 (Hydrofluoric Acid)
- HNO₃: 질산 (Nitric Acid)
- 10-MDP: 10-Methacryloyloxydecyl Dihydrogen Phosphate (지르코니아 결합 단량체)
- Tribochemical: 트라이보케미컬 (실리카 코팅 + 실란)
- Air abrasion: 공기 분사 마모 (특히 알루미나 입자)
