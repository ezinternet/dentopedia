## One-line Summary
SR of 7 RCTs (PubMed/Scopus/Embase/Cochrane, search to Jan 2021): activation methods (PUI, XP-endo, EndoVac) significantly reduce biofilm vs conventional needle; NaOCl+chelating agent combinations not more effective antimicrobially.

## 1. Document Information
University of Brescia + CEU University. Frontiers in Oral Health open-access SR. RCT-only inclusion. Cochrane RoB2 for quality assessment.

## 2. Key Contributions
- RCT-only design → higher internal validity than mixed-design reviews
- Activation methods (PUI, XP-endo finisher, F-file, EndoVac) significantly outperform conventional needle for biofilm reduction
- NaOCl+HEDP and NaOCl+MTAD combinations: no significant antimicrobial advantage over NaOCl alone
- NaOCl vs CHX: broadly comparable antimicrobial efficacy

## 3. Methodology
- Search: PubMed, Scopus, Embase, Web of Science, Cochrane (to Jan 2021)
- 428 records → 7 RCTs included
- Cochrane RoB2 tool for quality
- Groups: irrigant comparisons (NaOCl vs CHX; NaOCl+HEDP; NaOCl+MTAD) + activation method comparisons

## 4. Key Results
| Comparison | Finding |
|---|---|
| NaOCl + HEDP vs NaOCl | No antimicrobial advantage |
| NaOCl + MTAD vs NaOCl | No antimicrobial advantage |
| NaOCl vs CHX | Broadly comparable |
| CNI vs PUI/XP-endo/EndoVac | Activation significantly better for biofilm reduction |

## 5. Limitations
- Only 7 RCTs — small evidence base
- Short follow-up in most RCTs
- Heterogeneous protocols across studies

## 7. Glossary
- **PUI**: Passive Ultrasonic Irrigation
- **CNI**: Conventional Needle Irrigation
- **NaOCl**: Sodium hypochlorite
- **CHX**: Chlorhexidine
- **HEDP**: 1-Hydroxyethylidene-1,1-diphosphonate (chelating agent)
- **MTAD**: Mixture of tetracycline/acid/detergent
- **RoB2**: Revised Cochrane risk-of-bias tool
