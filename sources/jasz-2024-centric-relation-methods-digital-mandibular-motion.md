## Why Ingested

Centric-relation registration sits at the core of jaw-relation determination for full-mouth rehabilitation and complete dentures, the same decision space as [[wiki/occlusion/kwak-2024-masticatory-positioning-occlusal-stability-full-mouth]] (gothic-arch / masticatory positioning) and [[wiki/complete-denture/fayad-2025-vertical-dimension-determination-prosthodontics-review]] (vertical-dimension determination). Those pages cover *where* the jaw should close vertically and how positioning stability is judged; this paper supplies the missing *anteroposterior/superior* CR side — a head-to-head ranking of seven CR techniques against a digital mandibular motion analyser, telling us which manual/intraoral method captures the condyle closest to a reproducible reference and which ones risk a forced posterior (retruded) seat. Source: According to PubMed, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12903-024-04131-x), PMID 38500122, PMCID PMC10946198.

## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional clinical comparison (n=34 dentate, stable MIP) using a KaVo Arcus Digma 2 ultrasound mandibular motion analyser: of 7 centric-relation determination methods, Dawson bimanual manipulation and tongue-tip at the hard/soft-palate border deviated least from MIP (no significant deviation on any axis), while active/passive chin retrusion drove the condyle posteriorly and the gothic-arch tracer captured it anteriorly — all deviations within ±1 mm.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Most comprehensive head-to-head of CR techniques to date** — seven everyday CR determination methods compared against a single digital reference instrument in the same dentate cohort.
2. **Digital motion-analyser reference (50 µm accuracy)** — replaces the imprecise articulator-mounting (mm scale) and expensive/radiation-laden CBCT/MRI used by prior comparative studies; condylar position read in a 3D coordinate system zeroed to MIP.
3. **Clinical ranking with a safety dimension** — distinguishes methods by *direction* of deviation: anterior (gothic-arch) vs posterior (chin retrusion) vs near-zero (Dawson, hard/soft-palate tongue), flagging retrusion methods as risking a forced posterior condylar seat despite high reproducibility.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: cross-sectional, single-examiner, single-center (Department of Prosthodontics, Semmelweis University, Budapest), 2014–2018. Ethics No. 92/2013.
- **Participants**: 34 volunteers (24 F, 10 M), mean age 29.1 ± 7.3 y; dentate with stable MIP, **negative TMD history** (DC-TMD screened), no bruxism/parafunction, no prior ortho. Edentulous patients deliberately excluded.
- **Reference instrument**: KaVo Arcus Digma 2 ultrasound mandibular motion analyser — 4 ultrasonic transmitters (mandible) + 8 receivers (maxilla via arch); paraocclusal clutch + magnet-mounted transmitter; facebow referenced to Frankfort horizontal plane (left infraorbital point); EPA module calibrated to MIP. Reported accuracy 50 µm.
- **Coordinate system**: origin = calibrated MIP. x = sagittal (+anterior/−posterior), y = vertical (+cranial/−caudal), z = transverse (+left/−right). Condylar positions exported via KaVo KiD.
- **Seven CR techniques**: (A) gothic arch tracer apex, (B) adduction field method, (C) Dawson bimanual manipulation, (D) tongue tip on palatal rugae, (E) tongue tip at hard/soft-palate border, (F) active chin retrusion by patient, (G) passive operator chin push at 20 N. (A and B registered at slightly elevated OVD because the intraoral gothic-arch tracer requires an open position.)
- **Reliability**: 2 patients re-examined (duplicates excluded); weighted kappa for intraexaminer reliability 0.88–0.93.
- **Statistics**: mean and 95% CI per axis (α 0.05); significant if CI excludes 0. GraphPad Prism.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Transverse (z) axis**: negligible for all methods (0 mm, 95% CI [−0.03, 0.03]); analysis reduced to sagittal+vertical vector.
- **Coincided with MIP on ALL axes (no significant deviation)**: **(C) Dawson bimanual manipulation** and **(E) tongue at hard/soft-palate border**.
- **5 of 7 methods significantly deviated on ≥1 axis**, but every deviation magnitude was **within ±1 mm**.
- **Anterior capture**: **(D) tongue at palatal rugae** had the **largest sagittal deviation** (right 0.78 mm [0.35, 1.22]*; left 0.79 mm [0.4, 1.19]*) → more protrusive than intended. Gothic-arch apex (A) and adduction field (B) also anterior-and-downward (e.g. A right sagittal 0.28*, vertical −0.5*) — attributed to condylar translation at the slightly open registration.
- **Posterior capture (clinical caution)**: **(F) active retrusion** (right sagittal −0.24*, left −0.28*) and **(G) passive 20 N chin push** (right −0.35*, left −0.46*) seated the condyle posteriorly vs MIP.
- **Concordance with prior evidence**: a 2021 systematic review (958 → 9 studies; 4 dentate) likewise concluded the **Dawson technique** was the most accurate in dentate patients.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Young, healthy, **TMD-free dentate** cohort (mean 29 y) with stable MIP — does not generalize to edentulous, full-arch-rehabilitation, or TMD patients where CR matters most clinically.
- MIP used as the theoretical zero/reference is a pragmatic, reproducible surrogate, **not an anatomic CR gold standard** — methods that "deviate from MIP" are not necessarily wrong; this is a relative ranking.
- Single examiner with 20 y gnathology experience → high reliability but limited transfer to average operators; small sample (n=34); single device/manufacturer.
- Gothic-arch and adduction-field registered at elevated OVD (device requirement), confounding their anterior-inferior deviation.

## 7. Glossary

- **Centric Relation (CR, 중심위)**: reproducible maxillomandibular relationship; current GPT definition = anterior-superior condylar position in the fossa (independent of tooth contact).
- **Maximum Intercuspal Position (MIP, 최대교두감합위)**: tooth-determined position of maximal cuspal interdigitation; used here as the reproducible reference zero.
- **Centric slide (중심위 활주)**: discrepancy between CR and MIP; debated TMD predisposing factor.
- **Gothic arch tracing (고딕아치 트레이싱)**: intraoral arrow-point tracing; apex = retruded contact point.
- **Linguomandibular homotrophy (설-하악 동조)**: theory that tongue-tip positioning guides the mandible to a physiologic closing position.
- **Dawson bimanual manipulation (Dawson 양수조작법)**: operator seats condyles superior-anteriorly via bilateral chin/mandible manipulation.
- **Mandibular motion analyser / axiography (하악운동분석기)**: ultrasound device tracking condylar position (here KaVo Arcus Digma 2, 50 µm accuracy).
