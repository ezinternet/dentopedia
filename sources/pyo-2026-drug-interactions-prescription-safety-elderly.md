## One-line Summary
Special-issue narrative review (J Korean Dent Assoc 2026, 64(4):138-142) summarizing the epidemiology of dental drug-drug interactions (DDIs) in elderly patients (40.7% prevalence; only 42.7% prescriber recognition) and prescribing strategies for NSAIDs, antibiotics, and local anesthetic vasoconstrictors in the context of polypharmacy and age-related pharmacokinetic changes.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Synthesizes Korean and international DDI epidemiology data into a single dentist-facing reference
- Quantifies the dentist-recognition gap (42.7% mean correct classification of clinically important DDIs; 18.2% for warfarin–cimetidine)
- Translates GFR/LFT-based dose-titration rules into a single dental prescribing table (Table 1)
- Compares dental antibiotics by ADR rate (amoxicillin 21.5 vs. clindamycin 337.3 per million prescriptions — 15.7× higher)
- Codifies epinephrine restriction in non-selective beta-blocker and TCA users (1:200,000, ≤2 cartridges, or substitute mepivacaine)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative review (not SR/MA, no PRISMA, no PROSPERO)
- Sources cited: real-world data (RWD) studies on dental DDIs, prescriber-knowledge surveys (Ko et al. US), and dental pharmacology guidelines
- Organization:
  1. Epidemiology of dental DDIs
  2. Age-related pharmacokinetic/pharmacodynamic changes
  3. Prescriber-awareness gap and information sources
  4. Drug-class–specific interactions (antibiotics → NSAIDs → vasoconstrictors → others)
  5. Practical prescribing strategy

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Epidemiology
| Study | n | Finding |
|---|---|---|
| de Oliveira 2022 | 320 records | 359 potential DDIs; analgesics 50.41%, local anesthetics 25.90%, antibiotics 23.67%; "monitor closely" 68.80%, "serious" 8.63%, "contraindicated" 0.83% |
| Real-world cohort | 105 patients / 1,332 drug pairs | 542 DDIs (40.7% prevalence); major 2.3%, moderate 25.0%; CVD patients median 18 vs. non-CVD 10 |
| Ko et al. (US prescriber survey) | — | Mean correct identification 42.7%; warfarin–cimetidine 18.2% |

### Polypharmacy and ADR risk
| Drugs concurrent | ADR risk |
|---|---|
| 2 drugs | 6% |
| 5 drugs | 50% |
| ≥8 drugs | ~100% |

### Antibiotic safety (ADR per million prescriptions)
| Drug | ADR rate |
|---|---|
| Amoxicillin | 21.5 |
| Clindamycin | 337.3 (15.7× higher; high *C. difficile* risk) |
| Metronidazole, erythromycin | CYP2C9/CYP3A4 inhibition → warfarin INR ↑ |

### Local anesthetic / vasoconstrictor cautions
- Non-selective beta blockers (e.g., propranolol) + epinephrine → unopposed α-vasoconstriction → hypertensive crisis + reflex bradycardia → limit 1:200,000, ≤2 cartridges, or use mepivacaine
- TCA users → increased catecholamine sensitivity → arrhythmia/hypertensive crisis risk; strict epinephrine cap

### Geriatric dose adjustments
- Age ≥85 or body weight <45 kg: start at 50% adult dose ("Start low, go slow")
- GFR <10 mL/min: extend amoxicillin/cephalosporin dosing interval to q24h
- LFT >4× elevated: avoid hepatically metabolized drugs (acetaminophen, lidocaine) or use minimal dose

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative review without systematic search or risk-of-bias appraisal → low evidence weight on its own
- Epidemiology data mixes Korean and US sources; not all directly applicable to Korean elderly cohort
- Practical screening tools (e.g., 약학정보원) referenced but not benchmarked for sensitivity/specificity
- Does not address interactions specific to Korean herbal supplements common in elderly patients
- Future: Korean dental DDI registry; prospective evaluation of screening-tool impact on prescribing errors

## 7. Glossary
- **DDI** (Drug-Drug Interaction): one drug altering another's effect (pharmacokinetic or pharmacodynamic)
- **ADR** (Adverse Drug Reaction): unintended harmful response to a drug at normal doses
- **pDI** (potential Drug Interaction): theoretically possible DDI, not necessarily clinically manifest
- **CYP450** (Cytochrome P450): hepatic enzyme family metabolizing most drugs; CYP2C9 and CYP3A4 most relevant here
- **GFR** (Glomerular Filtration Rate): renal function index; <10 mL/min triggers dosing-interval extension
- **LFT** (Liver Function Test): hepatic enzyme panel; >4× elevation triggers drug avoidance
- **INR** (International Normalized Ratio): standardized prothrombin time; warfarin monitoring
- **NSAIDs**: nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs; high-risk in elderly (renal, GI, cardiovascular)
- **TCA** (Tricyclic Antidepressant): older antidepressant class; sensitizes to catecholamines
- **Polypharmacy**: concurrent use of multiple drugs, conventionally ≥5
- **Start low, go slow**: geriatric prescribing principle (50% dose, gradual titration)
- **RWD** (Real-World Data): observational data from routine clinical practice
