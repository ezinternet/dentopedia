## One-line Summary
In vitro (Bioengineering 2024, custom drill press, IR thermography): sequential drilling does NOT eliminate the thermal damage zone — the first (pilot) drill peaks above 100°C, creates thermal trauma up to 10 mm from the osteotomy, and subsequent drills add to (rather than remove) the damage.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Bioengineering 2024;11:928
- Type: In vitro experimental study
- Affiliation: Stony Brook University (Oral Biology/Pathology + Engineering)
- Published: 16 September 2024
- Open access (CC-BY)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Directly challenges the assumption that sequential drilling protects from thermal damage.
- Quantifies thermal damage zone propagation up to 10 mm from osteotomy.
- Identifies the **pilot drill** as the dominant heat producer in the sequence (>100°C).
- Provides mitigation: peck drilling, reduced RPM, reduced load.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Custom drill press + artificial bone models.
- 5 drill bits tested under various protocols.
- Variables: load, spindle speed, peck drilling on/off.
- IR thermography recorded temperature at multiple depths around osteotomy.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Sequential drilling thermal damage zone: well over 70°C.
- Pilot drill (first in sequence): peak >100°C.
- Subsequent drill bits cannot remove the thermal trauma the pilot inflicted; they add additional heat.
- Heat spread: up to 10 mm from osteotomy.
- Mitigations that lowered heat: reduced RPM, reduced load, peck drilling — primarily by reducing friction.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Artificial bone (synthetic) — no biological cooling, no in vivo validation.
- No irrigation arm in this protocol — magnifies absolute temperatures.
- 5 drill bits only.
- Future: validate with irrigation; in vivo correlation with peri-implantitis and BIC.

## 6. Related Work
- Sorgato 2025 — wear–temperature direct correlation, also without irrigation.
- Bernabeu-Mira 2020 — initial/pilot drill heat > progressive/final.
- Heuzeroth 2021 — peak T 35.4°C (test) vs 46.5°C (control) in vivo with irrigation.
- Aquilanti 2023 — initial osteotomy heat by piezo/rotary.

## 7. Glossary
- **Pilot drill**: first, smallest-diameter drill in a sequence.
- **Peck drilling**: drill–retract cycle to break chips and dissipate heat.
- **Thermal damage zone**: region around osteotomy reaching damaging temperature.
- **Sequential drilling**: standard implant protocol using progressively larger drills.
