## One-line Summary
Narrative review (clinical studies context): irrigant activation methods (sonic, ultrasonic, ANP, laser) comprehensively discussed for biofilm disruption, smear layer removal, and clinical evidence quality — activation consistently superior to conventional needle for debridement.

## 1. Document Information
University of Hong Kong. Restorative Dentistry and Endodontics review. Focus on clinical evidence for activation methods. Senior author: Gary Shun Pan Cheung, Professor of Endodontology, HKU.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive clinical-evidence framework for all major activation methods
- Sonic (EDDY, VibraShaker) — effective, lower cost, no special equipment
- Ultrasonic (PUI) — strong evidence, minimal risk of smear layer push
- ANP (EndoVac) — superior apical delivery, prevents extrusion
- Laser (Er:YAG, diode) — emerging, promising but inconsistent clinical evidence
- Sodium hypochlorite + EDTA: essential combination; activation enhances both

## 3. Methodology
Narrative review of clinical studies. No formal SR methodology. Includes RCTs, clinical trials, and in vivo studies. Emphasis on clinical applicability and practical recommendations.

## 4. Key Results
| Method | Mechanism | Key Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| PUI | Acoustic microstreaming + cavitation | Consistently superior to CNI in RCTs |
| ANP (EndoVac) | Suction draws irrigant apically | Best for working-length delivery, minimal extrusion |
| Sonic | High-frequency oscillation | Effective, simpler setup |
| Laser | Photoacoustic streaming | Promising but limited RCT data |
- NaOCl activation → enhanced tissue dissolution + antimicrobial effect
- EDTA activation → improved smear layer removal

## 5. Limitations
- Narrative review — subject to selection bias
- Laser evidence quality lower than PUI/ANP
- Heterogeneous clinical protocols

## 7. Glossary
- **PUI**: Passive Ultrasonic Irrigation
- **ANP**: Apical Negative Pressure (EndoVac)
- **NaOCl**: Sodium hypochlorite
- **EDTA**: Ethylenediaminetetraacetic acid (chelating agent, smear layer removal)
- **CNI**: Conventional Needle Irrigation
