## Why Ingested

바늘없는 압력 침윤마취기(needle-free jet injector, NFI) 및 스프링식 압력주사기(PCJ) 관련 질의 대응을 위해 인제스트. 5종의 구개 침윤마취 전달 시스템(CDS·MCJ·PCJ·NFI·CCLAD)을 동일 환자군·동일 프로토콜에서 직접 head-to-head 비교한 최초의 RCT로, 기존 [[local-anesthesia/park-2024-articaine-infiltration-lidocaine-intraosseous-molars]]가 다룬 컴퓨터제어 전달장치(QuickSleeper5)의 임상적 이점을 구개 침윤이라는 다른 해부학적 맥락에서 재검증·확장한다.

## One-line Summary

RCT (n=200, 5-arm parallel, single-blind) comparing conventional syringe, manual pressure syringe (MCJ), spring-activated pressure syringe (PCJ), needle-free jet injector (NFI), and CCLAD for palatal infiltration: pain did not differ significantly across devices (p=0.380, all Hedges' g<0.20), but PCJ produced the largest fear reduction (ΔVAS -2.7) and CCLAD produced the largest transient pulse-rate increase.

## 한줄요약

RCT(n=200, 5군 병렬, 단일눈가림)로 구개 침윤마취에서 통상 주사기·수동압력주사기(MCJ)·스프링식압력주사기(PCJ)·바늘없는 분사주입기(NFI)·컴퓨터제어전달장치(CCLAD)를 비교 — 통증은 군간 유의차 없었으나(p=0.380, 모든 Hedges' g<0.20), PCJ가 공포 감소 폭이 가장 컸고(ΔVAS -2.7) CCLAD는 주사 중 맥박 상승이 가장 컸음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Comparison of five anesthetic delivery systems for palatal infiltration: A randomized clinical trial
- **Authors**: Küçükkurt S, Saadat A
- **Institution**: Department of Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery, Istanbul Aydın University, Turkey
- **Journal**: Medicina Oral, Patología Oral y Cirugía Bucal (Med Oral Patol Oral Cir Bucal), 2026;31(2):e259-e267
- **DOI**: 10.4317/medoral.27738
- **PMID**: 41108768 / **PMCID**: PMC12983378
- **Study period**: September 2023 – March 2025
- **Design**: Prospective, randomized, parallel-arm, single-blind superiority RCT; CONSORT-adherent

## 2. Key Contributions

- First RCT to directly compare all five common anesthetic delivery systems (CDS, MCJ, PCJ, NFI, CCLAD) head-to-head under standardized palatal infiltration conditions in adults.
- First study to evaluate the two Citoject variants (manual MCJ vs spring-activated push-button PCJ) side by side — previously assumed interchangeable in practice.
- Integrates objective physiological monitoring (pulse rate, SpO2) with subjective VAS pain/fear scores, plus multivariate statistics (regression, k-means cluster analysis, mediation testing) rarely applied in dental anesthesia trials.
- Identifies three psychophysiological response phenotypes via cluster analysis, including a "silent high-stress" cluster (high subjective pain/fear but low autonomic reactivity, predominantly older males using MCJ) — challenges reliance on vital signs alone to gauge patient comfort.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Participants**: 200 systemically healthy adults (ASA I/II, ≥18y; 100F/100M, mean age 37.9±14.1y) requiring maxillary molar extraction under palatal infiltration. Excluded: pregnancy/lactation, active infection at site, diagnosed needle phobia, prior exposure to any tested system, premedication/sedation/topical anesthetic use.
- **Randomization**: Computer-generated block randomization, n=40/group, allocation concealed via opaque sequentially-numbered envelopes prepared by independent staff.
- **Blinding**: Single-blind — outcome assessors and statistician blinded; operator and participant blinding not feasible due to device-specific mechanical/acoustic cues. Standardized instructions and visual barriers used to reduce expectation bias.
- **Anesthetic**: 0.4 mL articaine HCl 4% + epinephrine 1:100,000 (Ultracain D-S Forte), single calibrated OMFS operator, injection ~2mm anterior to greater palatine foramen adjacent to first molar. No topical anesthetic applied (isolates device effect).
- **Devices/protocols**:
  - CDS: 27G×50mm needle, manual, 15-20s, aspiration
  - MCJ (ASPIJECT, RØNVIG): 30G×16mm needle, manual, 15-20s
  - PCJ (PAROJECT, RØNVIG): spring-activated push-button, two calibrated 0.2mL doses, aspiration
  - NFI (Comfort-in, Mika Medical): jet injection <1s, no aspiration, 4-5 bar
  - CCLAD (CALAJECT, RØNVIG, Program I): 30G×16mm needle, AutoFlow 0.006-0.009 mL/s, auto-aspiration at 5s
- **Outcomes**: Primary = post-injection pain (10cm VAS). Secondary = injection-related fear (10cm VAS, pre/post, ΔVAS), pulse rate and SpO2 (fingertip oximeter) at T0 (baseline)/T1 (during injection)/T2 (3 min post).
- **Sample size**: G*Power a priori — 40/group to detect 1.0cm VAS difference (MCID) at 80% power, α=0.05, f=0.40.
- **Stats**: SPSS v25 + Python 3.11; Shapiro-Wilk normality; ANOVA/Kruskal-Wallis with Tukey/Bonferroni-adjusted post hoc; repeated-measures ANOVA/Friedman; chi-square for categorical; mean differences with 95% CI and Hedges' g; multivariable linear regression; ANCOVA adjusting for injection duration; k-means cluster analysis; Spearman correlation; intention-to-treat.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Injection duration** differed sharply by device (H=178.26, p<0.001): NFI ~1s (shortest) < CDS 27±3.2s < MCJ 32±4.3s < PCJ 36±4.8s < CCLAD 52±1.7s (longest).
- **Pain (primary outcome)**: No significant overall difference (Kruskal-Wallis H=4.20, p=0.380). Lowest: CCLAD 3.14±1.77 (g=-0.11), NFI 3.17±1.82 (g=-0.09); highest: MCJ 4.05±2.44 (g=+0.31). Regression: MCJ independently associated with higher pain vs CDS (β=+0.89, 95% CI +0.08–1.70, p<0.05). All other pairwise effect sizes small (Hedges' g<0.20); differences fell below the 1.0cm MCID threshold even where statistically flagged.
- **Fear reduction**: All systems significantly reduced fear (Wilcoxon p<0.05); intergroup difference significant (H=19.22, p=0.001). Largest reduction: PCJ (ΔVAS -2.70±2.57). PCJ > CDS (mean diff -2.10, 95% CI -3.15 to -1.05, g=-0.95, p<0.001); PCJ > NFI (mean diff -1.55, g=-0.68, p<0.01); MCJ and CCLAD each > CDS (g≈-0.53/-0.54, p<0.05). Regression confirmed PCJ independently associated with greater fear reduction (β=+2.08, 95% CI +0.72–3.44, p<0.01) regardless of age/gender/baseline anxiety.
- **Pulse rate**: Significant group differences in both injection-phase rise (T1-T0, H=19.16, p=0.001) and post-injection recovery (T2-T1, H=38.79, p<0.001). CCLAD showed the greatest rise vs CDS (+6.2 bpm, g=+0.28, p=0.025) and vs MCJ (+7.5 bpm, g=+0.32, p=0.003); CCLAD also recovered less than MCJ (-9.8 bpm difference in T2-T1, g=-0.31, p<0.001). MCJ had the most stable pulse profile overall. Effect sizes small-to-moderate (g 0.3-0.4).
- **SpO2**: Stable 97.3-98.8% across all groups/timepoints, no significant intra- or inter-group differences (Hedges' g<0.40), no desaturation episodes.
- **Cluster analysis** (3 phenotypes): (A) low pain/fear + elevated pulse reactivity — mainly PCJ, CCLAD, NFI; (B) moderate/balanced responders; (C) high fear/pain + low autonomic reactivity — predominantly older males in MCJ ("silent high-stress" cluster).
- **Subgroup**: In high-baseline-anxiety patients, CCLAD and MCJ produced greater fear reduction, while PCJ still gave the lowest pain scores. No adverse events or protocol deviations in 200 completers.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Operator blinding infeasible (device handling is visibly distinct); participant blinding limited especially for NFI (loud discharge) and CCLAD (audible "beep," longer duration) — both may bias fear self-report.
- Anesthetic onset time, duration, and depth of anesthesia were not measured — only injection-phase psychophysiological response, not clinical efficacy of the block itself.
- No behavioral coding (facial microexpression, body movement) to complement VAS self-report.
- Single-operator design (external validity to other clinicians unclear); single anesthetic agent/concentration/volume (4% articaine, 0.4 mL) — may not generalize to other agents or volumes.
- Most between-group pain/pulse differences, while statistically significant in places, fell below MCID (1.0cm VAS) — authors explicitly caution against overstating clinical relevance of the CCLAD/NFI pain advantage.
- Future work proposed: broader populations, pharmacodynamic endpoints (onset/duration), behavioral coding, and psychophysiologically-tailored device-selection algorithms.

## 6. Related Work

- Cites prior CCLAD literature (Berrendero et al., Romero-Gálvez et al.) supporting flow-regulated atraumatic injection benefits, and Patil et al. reporting no CCLAD-vs-conventional pain advantage — this study's CCLAD pain reduction is real but sub-MCID, reconciling the mixed literature.
- Cites Al-Moraissi et al. on needle gauge (27G vs 30G) pain paradox — this trial's finding that MCJ (30G) hurt more than CDS (27G) despite finer gauge reinforces that delivery mechanics (pressure/flow), not gauge alone, drive pain.
- Contrasts with prior NFI literature (jet injectors reducing needle-related fear via eliminating visual/tactile cues) — this trial found NFI gave the *least* fear reduction of all active devices, attributed to the palate's dense, low-compliance tissue amplifying the acoustic/pressure shock of jet discharge.

## 7. Glossary

- **CDS**: Conventional Dental Syringe
- **MCJ**: Manual pressure syringe (Citoject-type, manual variant; ASPIJECT)
- **PCJ**: Push-button/spring-activated pressure syringe (Citoject-type; PAROJECT)
- **NFI**: Needle-Free Jet Injector
- **CCLAD**: Computer-Controlled Local Anesthetic Delivery system (e.g., Wand/STA, CALAJECT)
- **VAS**: Visual Analog Scale (0-10cm)
- **MCID**: Minimal Clinically Important Difference
- **Hedges' g**: Standardized effect size measure, bias-corrected for small samples
- **SpO2**: Peripheral oxygen saturation
