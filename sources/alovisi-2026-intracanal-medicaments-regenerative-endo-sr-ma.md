## Why Ingested

기존 [[endodontics/regenerative/]] 논문들은 scaffold/barrier 위주; 약제 선택(TAP vs DAP vs Ca(OH)₂)이 REP 결과에 미치는 영향을 SR+MA로 정량화한 첫 메타분석. TAP·CH 동등, 두 약제 모두 DAP 대비 우월함을 pooled success rate으로 직접 수치화.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 24 studies (n=791) in permanent necrotic immature teeth finds TAP (96.7%) and Ca(OH)₂ (97.4%) equivalent for 1-year REP success, both superior to DAP (84.2%; GRADE: high for TAP/CH, low for DAP).

영구 미성숙 치아 REP에서 TAP(96.7%)과 수산화칼슘(97.4%)의 1년 성공률이 동등하며, 두 약제 모두 DAP(84.2%)보다 우월함을 24개 연구 SR+MA로 확인.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA to quantitatively compare TAP, DAP, and Ca(OH)₂ as intracanal medicaments specifically within REP protocols on permanent necrotic immature teeth
- Demonstrates TAP–CH equivalence: pooled difference −0.7% (95% CI −0.9% to +2.5%), entirely within the predefined ±2.5% equivalence margin
- Demonstrates both TAP and CH superiority over DAP: TAP−DAP +12.5% (95% CI 2.8%–22.1%); CH−DAP +13.2% (95% CI 3.3%–23.0%)
- Applies GRADE: high evidence for TAP and CH; low evidence for DAP due to few small studies (n=64, 3 studies)
- Covers literature through December 2025 across MEDLINE, Scopus, Embase, Web of Science, Google Scholar
- Employs naïve indirect comparison (no common head-to-head comparator available) with equivalence analysis using mixed clinical-statistical method for ΔEQ derivation
- Demonstrates scaffold type (blood clot vs PRP/PRF) does not significantly affect 1-year clinical success outcomes

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

### Study Design

As no clinical trials simultaneously compared all three protocols, direct comparisons were unavailable. The authors used naïve indirect comparison — a less robust but necessary approach when no common comparator exists for more formal network meta-analysis. Network meta-analysis and mixed-treatment comparison methods were ruled out due to the absence of a common comparator arm across studies.

### Search Strategy

- **Databases**: MEDLINE, Scopus, Embase, Web of Science, Google Scholar (last update December 2025)
- **Inclusion**: Clinical trials (RCTs and non-randomised studies) investigating REP success in permanent necrotic immature teeth using TAP, DAP, or CH as intracanal dressing
- **Exclusion**: Studies on primary teeth, case reports, studies without clinical follow-up ≥12 months

### Risk of Bias Assessment

Revised Cochrane Risk of Bias tool for randomised trials (RoB2) — 24 included studies rated average quality overall; no serious limitations detected for risk of bias, consistency, indirectness, or publication bias across TAP and CH arms.

### Statistical Methods

- **Pooled success rates**: Double arcsine transformation of proportions to normalise variances (required because several studies reported 100% success, causing conventional variance to be zero)
- **Model**: Random-effects model preferred when I² > 50%; fixed-effect model used for equivalence analysis (per pre-specification)
- **Heterogeneity**: Cochran's Q (formal test) + I² (quantification)
- **Publication bias**: Funnel plot using log odds on x-axis and sample size on y-axis; Peters regression test for asymmetry; pooled estimates adjusted for publication bias
- **Sensitivity analyses**: Study quality, publication bias, study inclusion (leave-one-out), meta-analytic method
- **Subgroup analyses**: Scaffold type (blood clot vs other), world region (as ethnicity proxy)

### Equivalence Analysis

- **Equivalence margin (ΔEQ)**: ±2.5% — derived from one-half the distance between 95% CI limits (97.5%–92.4% = 5.1%) of the overall pooled REP success rate (95.6%, Li et al. 2023), a mixed clinical-statistical approach
- Two protocols deemed equivalent when their pooled difference fell within −2.5% to +2.5%
- Fixed-effect model used for the equivalence comparison

### Evidence Grading

GRADE approach applied. Downgrading domains evaluated: risk of bias, consistency, indirectness, imprecision, publication bias. Upgrading domains: magnitude of effect, residual confounding direction. Dose-response gradient not assessable (dichotomous outcome). Recommendations deliberately not provided due to absence of direct comparisons.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Included Studies

| Arm | Studies (n) | Patients (n) | Study types |
|---|---|---|---|
| TAP | 19 | 544 | RCT, NRSI |
| DAP | 3 | 64 | RCT, NRSI |
| CH | 6 | 183 | RCT, NRSI |
| **Total** | **24** | **791** | |

### Pooled 1-Year Success Rates

| Medicament | Pooled Success | 95% CI | GRADE |
|---|---|---|---|
| TAP (triple antibiotic paste) | 96.7% | 94.8%–98.0% | High ⨁⨁⨁⨁ |
| DAP (double antibiotic paste) | 84.2% | 73.2%–92.0% | Low ⨁⨁◯◯ |
| CH (calcium hydroxide) | 97.4% | 93.9%–99.1% | High ⨁⨁⨁⨁ |

- Success defined as: asymptomatic tooth, not tender to percussion, without sinus tract/swelling, and radiographic absence/reduction of periapical radiolucency at ≥12 months

### Pairwise Comparisons (Naïve Indirect)

| Comparison | Pooled Difference | 95% CI | Conclusion |
|---|---|---|---|
| TAP vs DAP | +12.5% | 2.8%–22.1% | TAP superiority demonstrated |
| TAP vs CH | −0.7% | −0.9% to +2.5% | TAP/CH equivalence demonstrated |
| CH vs DAP | +13.2% | 3.3%–23.0% | CH superiority demonstrated |

### Blood Clot Subgroup Analysis Only

| Comparison | Difference | 95% CI | Conclusion |
|---|---|---|---|
| TAP vs DAP | +11.0% | 0.8%–21.3% | TAP superiority not shown |
| TAP vs CH | +2.0% | −2.8% to +6.8% | Equivalence not shown |
| CH vs DAP | +9.0% | −1.9% to +19.9% | CH superiority not shown |

Note: Blood clot subgroup results non-significant, attributed to reduced power from smaller sample sizes when stratified.

### GRADE Summary

DAP downgraded to Low due to serious limitations in consistency and imprecision (only 3 studies, n=64, wide CI). TAP and CH maintained High certainty across all GRADE domains.

### Additional Findings

- Scaffold type (blood clot vs PRP/PRF vs collagen membrane) did not significantly affect 1-year clinical success, consistent with Rahul et al. (2023) and Sabeti et al. (2024)
- Primary outcome deliberately limited to clinical/radiographic success; secondary outcomes (root wall thickening, lengthening, apical closure, pulp sensitivity response) excluded due to heterogeneity and contested clinical meaning
- Studies derived from Middle East, Asia, and few from Americas; no studies from Europe, Oceania, Africa — limits external validity
- Sample sizes in primary studies ranged from 7 to 76 patients

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **No direct head-to-head comparisons** between all three protocols — prevents more robust network meta-analysis
- **No common comparator** across all arms, necessitating naïve indirect comparison with lower robustness
- **DAP evidence base small**: 3 studies, n=64 — GRADE low; DAP estimate most uncertain
- **Statistical power limited** overall, especially for DAP comparisons
- **Short follow-up ceiling**: Primary outcome at 12 months; no long-term data beyond this; root maturation trajectory unknown
- **Geographic concentration**: Studies mostly from Middle East/Asia; none from Europe, Oceania, Africa — limits generalisability
- **Protocol heterogeneity**: Variable antibiotic concentrations, scaffold types, radiographic interpretation methods across trials
- **Secondary outcomes not pooled**: Root lengthening, wall thickening, apical closure, pulp sensitivity — too heterogeneous to quantify
- **Equivalence margin subjectivity**: ΔEQ of ±2.5% derived from mixed method; different margins would alter equivalence conclusions
- **Future directions**: Multicentre RCTs with direct protocol comparisons, standardised CONSORT reporting, larger DAP arms; exploration of novel medicaments (injectable gels, antimicrobial peptides) as alternatives to current TAP/DAP/CH (Noohi et al. 2025; Reis-Prado et al. 2025)

## 6. Related Work

- **ESE position (Galler et al. 2016)**: European Society of Endodontology REP guidelines recommending TAP, DAP, or CH — this paper provides the first pooled comparison of these three
- **AAE Clinical Considerations (2021)**: Recommends TAP at 1–5 mg·mL⁻¹ to avoid stem cell of apical papilla (SCAP) damage; this SR confirms TAP's high success consistent with AAE guidance
- **Li et al. 2023 SR+MA**: Prior overview of overall REP success (95.6%) — used as basis for equivalence margin derivation in this paper
- **Báez et al. 2022**: Previous meta-analysis presenting TAP as more effective for healing; CH higher for apical closure — partially contradicted by this paper's equivalence finding
- **Sabeti et al. 2024**: Reports scaffold type (PRP/PRF vs blood clot) does not differ significantly at >12 months, corroborating this paper's subgroup finding
- **Almutairi et al. 2019**: 79% of failed REP cases showed persistent infection — underscores the disinfection phase as decisive; motivates this medicament-focused SR+MA
- TAP = ciprofloxacin + metronidazole + minocycline (Hoshino formulation); minocycline associated with crown discoloration
- DAP = ciprofloxacin + metronidazole (minocycline omitted); safer for discoloration but less disinfection depth in dentinal tubules; ciprofloxacin may impair angiogenesis
- CH = calcium hydroxide — no discoloration, promotes growth factor release, lower stem cell toxicity; may have limited bactericidal depth in dentinal tubules

## 7. Glossary

- **REP** (Regenerative Endodontic Procedure, 재생근관치료): Two-visit protocol (Session 1: disinfection; Session 2: scaffold + barrier) aiming to restore pulp–dentin complex vitality in necrotic immature permanent teeth via stem cell ingrowth
- **TAP** (Triple Antibiotic Paste, 삼제항생제 페이스트): Ciprofloxacin + metronidazole + minocycline intracanal paste at 1–5 mg·mL⁻¹
- **DAP** (Double Antibiotic Paste, 이제항생제 페이스트): Ciprofloxacin + metronidazole (no minocycline — reduces discoloration risk)
- **CH** (Calcium Hydroxide, 수산화칼슘): Classic intracanal medicament; no antibiotic resistance concern; promotes growth factors; lower SCAP toxicity
- **CF-REP** (Cell-Free REP, 무세포 재생근관치료): Clinically practised REP approach relying on endogenous stem cell migration from apical papilla/periapical tissues
- **Naïve indirect comparison** (단순 간접 비교): Meta-analytic comparison of arms not directly randomised against each other; less robust than network MA; used here because no common comparator existed
- **Equivalence margin (ΔEQ)**: Predefined threshold within which two protocols are considered clinically equivalent; set at ±2.5% in this study
- **GRADE**: Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation — evidence quality framework (High/Moderate/Low/Very Low)
- **RoB2**: Revised Cochrane Risk of Bias tool for randomised trials
- **SCAP** (Stem Cells of the Apical Papilla, 근단유두 줄기세포): Primary regenerative cell source in CF-REPs; susceptibility to antibiotic concentrations is a safety concern
- **Periapical healing** (근첨부 치유): Radiographic and/or clinical resolution of periapical pathology — primary REP success criterion in this study
- **Double arcsine transformation**: Statistical method to normalise variance of proportions near 0 or 1, required when many studies report 100% success
