## Why Ingested

This is the SSRD/SEPES/PROSEC consensus that sits directly on top of the laminate-veneer SR+MA we already hold ([[veneers/klein-2025-ceramic-laminate-veneer-survival-complications-sr-ma]]) — it converts that meta-analysis's pooled survival/complication numbers into actionable clinical statements (enamel bonding, butt-joint preparation, material choice). It supplies the consensus-level "what to actually do" layer that the existing SR pages ([[veneers/morimoto-2016-feldspathic-glass-ceramic-veneer-survival-sr-ma]], [[veneers/albanesi-2016-incisal-coverage-ceramic-laminate-veneer-sr-ma]]) leave implicit, and extends scope to posterior partial coverage restorations (onlays/occlusal veneers).

## One-line Summary

Consensus statement (SSRD/SEPES/PROSEC; built on 2 SR+MA, 35 studies) recommending feldspathic/LRGC/LDS for ceramic laminate veneers and LDS/RMC/LRGC for posterior partial coverage restorations, with enamel bonding and butt-joint preparation as key success drivers.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Translates two pre-conference SR+MA (posterior PCRs; anterior ceramic laminate veneers) into formal, expert-ratified consensus statements and clinical recommendations.
- Confirms ceramic laminate veneers as a reliable long-term option across feldspathic/LRGC/LDS, with LDS having significantly fewer technical+biological complications.
- Establishes preparation guidance: bond to enamel, and when preparing, prefer flat incisal overlapping (butt-joint) over non-overlapping or palatal chamfer.
- Extends the synthesis to posterior partial coverage restorations (onlays, occlusal veneers, partial crowns) with LDS/RMC/LRGC all judged appropriate mid-term.
- Frames an ethics-of-preservation stance: minimally invasive first, transfer risk to the prosthetic restoration, prioritize tooth longevity and repairability over replacement.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Consensus methodology: systematic literature screening (PRISMA) identified 35 publications → two systematic reviews → consensus statements formulated by Working Group 1 and refined through plenary expert discussion.
- SR Paper 1 (posterior PCRs): meta-analysis of survival/complications; 1–3y follow-up, 252 restorations across 4 RCTs (loss of retention: 2–3y, 212 restorations, 3 RCTs).
- SR Paper 2 (ceramic laminate veneers): = Klein et al. 2024 SR+MA (29 studies; feldspathic, LRGC, LDS, zirconia).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Posterior PCRs (3-year):** LDS 93.7% vs RMC 89.3% survival (not significantly different). Failures/100 restoration-years: LDS 2.13, RMC 3.69. Chipping 1.89 vs 2.29; loss of retention 0.98 vs 2.76; endodontic 0.53 vs 0.92; bulk fracture 1.07 vs 0.32.

**Ceramic laminate veneers:** pooled survival 97.76% (2.6Y), 97.12% (5.0Y), 96.05% (10.4Y). By material at long term — feldspathic 96.13%, LRGC 93.7%, LDS 96.81%. LDS significantly fewer technical+biological complications than feldspathic (p=0.044) and LRGC (p=0.033). No significant difference in esthetic complications between materials (feldspathic vs LRGC p=0.972; feldspathic vs LDS p=0.117; LRGC vs LDS p=0.087).

**Clinical recommendations (veneers):** recommend feldspathic/LRGC/LDS; bond to enamel (dentin/existing-restoration bonding raises complication risk); if prepared, flat incisal butt-joint preferred over non-overlap or palatal chamfer.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Consensus is only as strong as the two underlying SRs — little long-term original data; few materials with primary RCT evidence.
- Veneers: no clear preferable fabrication method (conventional vs CAD/CAM), no definitive bonding/cementation protocol, indication/prep-design/prep-vs-non-prep/tooth-removal amount unresolved; mid/long-term zirconia performance uncertain; thickness and monolithic-vs-veneered design under-reported.
- Future: RCTs on analog/digital workflows and adhesive protocols (restoration-tooth-complex), split-mouth designs, larger samples, longer follow-up, zirconia veneer potential, PROMs + clinician-reported outcomes, FDI-criteria monitoring.

## 7. Glossary

- **PCR** — partial coverage restoration (onlay, occlusal veneer, partial crown).
- **LDS** — lithium disilicate ceramic.
- **LRGC** — leucite-reinforced glass ceramic.
- **RMC** — resin matrix ceramic.
- **Feldspathic** — feldspathic porcelain (silica-based ceramic).
- **Butt-joint** — flat incisal overlapping preparation design.
- **SSRD/SEPES/PROSEC** — Swiss/Spanish/(Portuguese-region) prosthodontic societies hosting the consensus conference.
