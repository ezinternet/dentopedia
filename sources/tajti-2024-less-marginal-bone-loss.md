## Why Ingested

Extends [[wiki/implants/mortazavi-2021-bone-loss-tissue-bone-level-implants]] by isolating one prosthetic variable — abutment height — within bone-level platform-switched implants under a one-abutment-one-time protocol, with a methodologically strict 2024 SR+MA that deliberately excludes the retrospective/animal data that biased earlier abutment-height reviews.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 8 studies (7 meta-analyzed; bone-level platform-switched implants): long abutments (≥2 mm) showed less marginal bone loss than short (<2 mm) — at 1 year, 6 studies/384 implants, MD 0.26 mm (95% CI −0.02 to 0.53, I²=73%), moderate GRADE certainty; no difference in probing pocket depth or bleeding on probing.

## 1. Document Information

- Journal: Periodontology 2000, 2024;94:627–638
- Design: Systematic review + meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD42022331923), PRISMA 2020 + Cochrane Handbook
- Search: MEDLINE, EMBASE, Web of Science, CENTRAL, Scopus; inception–May 2022, updated Jan 2023
- 8 studies in qualitative synthesis; 7 in meta-analysis
- Inclusion: human, ≥20 participants, ≥6 mo follow-up, titanium bone-level platform-switching implants, long (≥2 mm) vs short (<2 mm) abutments, single/partial (≤3-unit) fixed restorations
- Exclusion: GBR, tissue-level (one-piece) implants, zirconia implants, case reports/series, non-randomized retrospective studies
- Quality: RoB 2 (RCTs) + ROBINS-I; GRADE for certainty

## 2. Key Contributions

- A deliberately clean abutment-height meta-analysis: restricted to bone-level platform-switched implants with one-abutment-one-time and excluding the retrospective/animal data that inflated prior reviews.
- Confirms long abutments (≥2 mm) preserve marginal bone, with the signal strengthening over follow-up.
- Shows the benefit is bone-specific: no difference in probing pocket depth or bleeding on probing.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

PICO comparison of long vs short abutments; pooled mean difference (MBL, PPD) and risk ratio (BOP) with 95% CI, random-effects (Hartung-Knapp), Mantel–Haenszel pooling, R (meta/dmetar). Correlated split-mouth data handled with ICC=0 and ICC=0.5 sensitivity. Heterogeneity by Cochrane Q and I². GRADE certainty assessment.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- MBL at 6 months: 3 studies, 174 implants — long abutments favored, MD ≈0.63 mm but 95% CI crossed zero (CI [−0.16; 1.42], I²=93%).
- MBL at 1 year: 6 studies, 384 implants — long abutments lost ~0.26 mm less bone (MD 0.26, 95% CI −0.02 to 0.53, I²=73%); moderate GRADE certainty.
- Probing pocket depth (1 yr, 2 studies, 154 implants): no difference (MD −0.05, 95% CI −1.11 to 1.01, I²=0%, p=0.650).
- Bleeding on probing (1 yr, 4 studies, 256 implants): no difference (RR 0.97, 95% CI 0.76–1.23, I²=0%, p=0.927); moderate certainty.
- Overall risk of bias low-to-moderate; GRADE high-to-moderate certainty.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

Few eligible studies (8) and high heterogeneity at some timepoints (I²=73–93%); several pooled effects' CIs cross zero, so the bone-protective signal is directionally consistent but not always statistically conclusive. Long-term follow-up data sparse. Authors call for further trials to firm up the conclusion.

## 6. Related Work

Refines the abutment-height literature criticized for retrospective/animal contamination (Chen et al.; earlier SRs). Sits alongside platform-switching and one-abutment-one-time evidence; complements implant-type MBL work (Mortazavi 2021) and the broader prosthetic-design synthesis (Lin 2025).

## 7. Glossary

- **Abutment height (long/short)**: transmucosal abutment height ≥2 mm (long) vs <2 mm (short).
- **MBL**: marginal bone loss. **MD**: mean difference.
- **One-abutment-one-time**: definitive abutment placed once, never disconnected.
- **GRADE**: certainty-of-evidence grading framework.
- **Hartung-Knapp adjustment**: random-effects method guarding against false positives.
