근거평가 카테고리에서 "단일 연구그룹의 비현실적으로 큰 효과크기를 통계·보고질로 비판적 검증"하는 사례를 보강한다. [[evidence-appraisal/choi-2015-statistical-validity-insadol-clinical-effectiveness]]가 단일 그룹 인사돌 데이터의 통계적 타당성을 의심했듯, 본 part 2는 SRP 부가요법 RCT 32편의 CONSORT 보고질·trial registration 불일치를 정량화해 임상의가 증거를 무비판적으로 수용하지 말아야 함을 보여준다.

## One-line Summary

Methodological quality assessment (CONSORT + trial-registration audit) of 32 RCTs on SRP adjuncts published 2010–2017 by a single research group (GDCRI, Bangalore); all RCTs scored low on reporting quality, most CONSORT items <50% (including randomization, registration, blinding), only 38% had follow-up periods consistent with declared trial start/end dates — so the group's unusually large effect sizes should be viewed with extreme caution.

## 한줄요약

단일 연구그룹(인도 GDCRI)이 2010–2017년 발표한 SRP 부가요법 RCT 32편의 보고질(CONSORT)·임상시험 등록 일치성 감사로, 모든 RCT가 낮은 보고질을 보였고 대부분 CONSORT 항목이 50% 미만(무작위배정·등록·맹검 포함), 등록기록과 추적기간이 일치한 RCT는 38%뿐이어서, 이 그룹이 보고한 비정상적으로 큰 효과크기는 극도의 주의가 필요하다는 메타-연구(part 2).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides a structured, preregistered quality-and-integrity audit of a body of RCTs from one prolific research group whose effect sizes for SRP adjuncts (notably statins) were never reproduced by other groups.
- Demonstrates that low CONSORT reporting quality co-occurs with trial-registration discrepancies and implausible timelines, giving clinicians a concrete reason to discount the group's unusually large effects.
- Introduces a simple timeline-consistency check: comparing actual RCT duration (X, from published initiation/termination dates) against per-protocol declared follow-up (Y) to flag unrealistic (X=Y) or erratic (X<Y) reporting.
- Companion to Part 1 (meta-analysis), which could not explain the large effects statistically — Part 2 supplies the reporting/integrity explanation.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search/identification**: Scopus bibliometric search (and PubMed hand search) using SRP + adjunct keyword strings, restricted 2010–2017, to identify the single group with the highest RCT publication frequency on SRP adjuncts (GDCRI confirmed).
- **Inclusion**: RCTs (incl. cluster, crossover, noninferiority, superiority) on SRP vs SRP+adjunct in periodontitis, English language, baseline reported, ≥6-mo follow-up.
- **Exclusion**: nonrandomized/observational designs, gingivitis/tooth-sensitivity/peri-implantitis trials, furcation-defect trials, laser/photodynamic/platelet-fibrin adjuncts, non-SRP comparators.
- **Outcomes examined**: probing depth change, clinical attachment level, radiographic bone fill, plaque score, bleeding on probing.
- **Data extraction**: two reviewers (K.A., M.S.) independently, disagreements resolved by a third (E.I.); Cochrane Handbook–based extraction sheet capturing registration, population, intervention/disease type, primary/secondary outcomes, sample-size calculation, baseline and 6-mo results, journal/impact data, trial start/end and submission years.
- **Registration cross-check**: for registered trials, registry data cross-matched against the article; checked for prestudy power analysis and pre-specified primary/secondary outcomes.
- **Timeline variables**: X = actual RCT duration (from published initiation/termination); Y = per-protocol declared follow-up. X>Y = realistic; X=Y = unrealistic (implies all participants enrolled/randomized day 1 and all follow-up completed within the interval); X<Y = problematic/erratic.
- **Quality scoring**: CONSORT statement applied to each RCT; each item scored fulfilled/not-fulfilled (categorical), expressed as proportional percentage across studies. The "abstract" item and item 7b were excluded (abstracts have own CONSORT guidance; 7b not applicable).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Yield**: 92 studies retrieved (Scopus + PubMed); 32 RCTs included for CONSORT quality assessment.
- **Reporting quality**: all included RCTs characterized by low reporting quality; the majority of CONSORT items scored <50%, including critical items — randomization, registration, and blinding.
- **Registration discrepancies**: comparing registration records against published RCTs revealed several/systematic inconsistencies.
- **Timeline consistency**: only 38% of RCTs presented a follow-up period within the declared initiation and termination dates; the remaining RCTs showed inconsistent (unrealistic or erratic) follow-up relative to start/end dates.
- **Publication frequency**: the group's RCT publication rate was described as remarkable given that ~2 years is typical from trial to publication.
- **Bottom line**: poor adherence to reporting standards + systematic registration inconsistencies → the unusually large effect sizes from this group should be viewed with extreme caution.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-group focus by design (the analysis is intentionally targeted at GDCRI), so findings are about one research body, not SRP adjuncts in general.
- CONSORT scoring is dichotomous (fulfilled/not), which does not capture partial adherence gradients.
- Reporting-quality and registration problems indicate inability to replicate/verify, not direct proof of data fabrication; conclusions are about credibility/caution rather than definitive misconduct.
- Search window limited to 2010–2017; later or earlier outputs not assessed.

## 6. Related Work

- Smiley et al. 2015 — ADA Council on Scientific Affairs systematic review of SRP adjuncts; flagged statin trials confined to this single group with non-reproduced large effects.
- Part 1 (companion meta-analysis by the same authors) — meta-analyzed this group's RCTs and found no statistical explanation for the large effects.
- CONSORT (Moher/Schulz et al.) and ICMJE registration requirements — the reporting/registration standards used as benchmarks.

## 7. Glossary

- **SRP (Scaling and Root Planing)**: nonsurgical instrumentation of crown/root surfaces to remove plaque, calculus, and contaminated cementum/dentin.
- **CONSORT (Consolidated Standards of Reporting Trials)**: checklist of items an RCT report must contain (randomization, blinding, outcomes, etc.).
- **Trial registration**: prospective public registry entry (registry number) required by ICMJE; allows cross-checking declared vs reported design/outcomes.
- **Effect size**: magnitude of the treatment difference; "unusually large" here = far bigger than other groups reproduce.
- **GDCRI**: Government Dental College and Research Institute, Bangalore, India — the single research group under audit.
- **Variable X / Variable Y**: actual RCT duration vs per-protocol declared follow-up; their relation (X>Y, X=Y, X<Y) flags realistic/unrealistic/erratic reporting.
