## Why Ingested

This SR+MA fixes the evidence on how horizontal condylar guidance (HCG) angle is captured in the completely edentulous patient — the input that programs the semi-adjustable articulator for balanced complete-denture occlusion. It pairs with [[wiki/occlusion/kwak-2024-masticatory-positioning-occlusal-stability-full-mouth]] (which uses a Gothic arch tracer to establish a therapeutic/centric position) by characterizing the Gothic arch tracing method's accuracy as a reference, and it complements jaw-relation/VDO determination work [[wiki/complete-denture/fayad-2025-vertical-dimension-determination-prosthodontics-review]] by extending the same "how do we measure the maxillomandibular relationship records" question from vertical dimension to the sagittal condylar path.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (20 of 22 cross-sectional studies, n=325 completely edentulous patients) comparing extraoral Gothic arch tracing against radiographic and wax methods for measuring horizontal condylar guidance (HCG) angle: cephalogram and panoramic radiograph (OPG) yield significantly HIGHER HCG angles than Gothic arch tracing (cephalogram pooled MD ≈ +10.2–10.7°, OPG ≈ +5.1°, both p<0.05 bilaterally), while CBCT and protrusive interocclusal wax record show NO significant difference — authors conclude OPG is the best accurate alternative for programming the semi-adjustable articulator.

## 한줄요약

무치악 환자(n=325)에서 수평과로각(Horizontal Condylar Guidance, HCG)을 재는 구외 고딕아치 트레이싱(extraoral Gothic arch tracing)을 방사선·왁스 방법과 비교한 SR+MA(22편 중 20편 메타분석): 측방두부규격(cephalogram)과 파노라마(OPG)는 고딕아치보다 유의하게 큰 HCG 값(cephalogram 약 +10.2~10.7°, OPG 약 +5.1°, 양측 p<0.05)을 주고, CBCT와 단백 교합간 왁스기록(interocclusal wax record)은 차이 없음 — 저자는 반조절성 교합기 프로그래밍에 OPG가 가장 정확한 대안이라 결론.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA quantifying the accuracy gap between extraoral Gothic arch tracing (the reference clinical method) and four alternative methods of measuring HCG angle in the completely edentulous patient: panoramic radiograph (OPG), cephalogram, CBCT, and protrusive interocclusal wax record.
- Pooled effect sizes (mean difference, 95% CI) for each comparator on both right and left sides, with heterogeneity-driven fixed vs random effect model selection.
- Clinical bottom line: OPG and cephalogram give systematically higher condylar guidance values than the clinical tracer; OPG is named the best practical accurate alternative for articulator programming.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases**: EBSCOhost, Cochrane Library, PubMed/MEDLINE, Google Scholar; English-language studies Jan 2005–Dec 2022.
- **PICOS**: Population = completely edentulous patients >35 y with healthy ridges; Comparison = extraoral Gothic arch tracing via set protrusive interocclusal record; Outcome = HCG angle.
- **Study designs included**: cross-sectional, RCT, quasi-experimental (all 22 included were analytical cross-sectional studies in practice).
- **Risk of bias**: Newcastle–Ottawa (modified case–control scale); 20 studies "good," 2 "fair." Inter-rater Cohen kappa = 0.90.
- **Meta-analysis**: RevMan 5.4; forest plots from mean/SD/total at 95% CI; I² for heterogeneity (>50% → random effect, <50% → fixed effect); funnel plot for publication bias.
- **Sample**: 513 records → 22 studies qualitative (n=325; average age 45–60 y; 19 India, 2 Egypt, 1 Nepal) → 20 studies meta-analysis. 21/22 studies used Hanau Wide Vue semi-adjustable articulator.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Pooled mean difference (comparator − Gothic arch tracing), 95% CI, both sides:

| Comparator | n studies | Right side MD [95% CI], p | Left side MD [95% CI], p | Sig? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Panoramic radiograph (OPG) | 15 | +5.08 [2.17, 7], p<0.05 (I²=89%) | +5.07 [1.95, 8.18], p<0.05 (I²=90%) | YES (OPG higher) |
| Cephalogram | 2 | +10.65 [8.81, 12.49], p<0.05 (I²=0%) | +10.24 [8.65, 11.83], p<0.001 (I²=0%) | YES (cephalogram higher) |
| CBCT | 5 | +4.39 [−6.10, 14.87], p=0.41 (I²=99%) | +4.05 [−3.74, 11.84], p=0.31 (I²=98%) | NO |
| Protrusive interocclusal wax record | 4 | −0.45 [−9.62, 8.72], p=0.92 (I²=98%) | −1.21 [−7.86, 5.43], p=0.72 (I²=95%) | NO |

- **Significant difference**: cephalogram and OPG both give higher HCG angles than Gothic arch tracing, bilaterally.
- **No significant difference**: CBCT and protrusive wax record vs Gothic arch tracing (but CBCT/wax pools carry very high heterogeneity, I²=95–99%).
- **Conclusion**: based on mean difference and the larger number of contributing studies, panoramic radiograph is judged the best method to obtain a higher/more accurate HCG angle vs the clinical tracer.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only analytical cross-sectional studies; English-only; heavily India-weighted (19/22 studies) — geographic/operator clustering.
- Wide heterogeneity (I² up to 99%) for CBCT and wax-record pools makes the "no difference" conclusions fragile; few studies per comparator (cephalogram only 2, wax only 4, CBCT 5).
- "Accuracy" here = agreement with the Gothic arch tracing reference, not against a true anatomical gold standard — so "higher" cannot be equated with "more correct" in an absolute sense.
- Authors call for head-to-head trials comparing only two methods to reduce comparison-group mixing.

## 7. Glossary

- **HCG (Horizontal Condylar Guidance) angle / 수평과로각**: sagittal inclination of the condylar path from protrusive movement; programs the horizontal condylar element of a semi-adjustable articulator.
- **Extraoral Gothic arch (arrowhead) tracing**: central-bearing-point/plate device recording a protrusive interocclusal record to derive HCG clinically.
- **OPG (Orthopantomogram / panoramic radiograph)**: 2D radiograph used to trace condylar path landmarks.
- **Semi-adjustable articulator (Hanau Wide Vue)**: lab device whose condylar guidance is set from the patient's protrusive record to develop balanced complete-denture occlusion.
