## Why Ingested

Mechanical plaque control (toothbrushing + flossing) leaves up to ~50% of plaque even with proper technique; this paper tests a non-mechanical adjunct (home-use dual-light aPDT) against that ceiling. It contextualizes the mechanical-device evidence in [[wiki/periodontics/luo-2026-electric-toothbrush-technologies-plaque-gingivitis-nma]] (which compares powered-brush *technologies* for plaque/gingivitis) by asking whether a light-based chemical adjunct can add reduction on top of conventional self-care in already-healthy adults.

## One-line Summary

Crossover pilot RCT (n=30 healthy adults, 6-week design) — daily 10-min home-use dual-light (405+810 nm) aPDT with indocyanine green, added to conventional oral care, cut plaque (Greene-Vermillion) ~21% vs baseline (pooled p=0.0001) while conventional care alone showed no significant change (-6.5%, p=0.11).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Tests a home-use **dual-light antimicrobial photodynamic therapy (aPDT)** device (405 nm + 810 nm light + indocyanine green photosensitizer) as a daily adjunct to ordinary toothbrushing/flossing.
- Demonstrates that even in **healthy adults with good baseline oral hygiene**, an adjunctive aPDT produces an additional, statistically significant plaque reduction beyond mechanical self-care.
- Uses a **within-subject crossover** to control for individual hygiene differences — each subject acts as their own control across aPDT and conventional-only phases.
- Frames aPDT as a non-invasive preventive option for **plaque-retentive scenarios** (orthodontic appliances, implants, fixed prostheses, reduced manual dexterity).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Six-week randomized crossover, two parallel sequences with a 2-week crossover (washout/switch) phase.
- **Participants**: 30 young healthy adults, randomly assigned to two groups.
  - **Group 1**: 2 weeks daily dual-light aPDT + conventional oral care, then switched.
  - **Group 2**: continued regular oral care, then crossed over to aPDT.
- **Intervention**: daily **10-minute** dual-light aPDT (405 + 810 nm) with **indocyanine green** photosensitizer, alongside normal toothbrushing and flossing.
- **Outcome**: dental plaque measured with the **Greene-Vermillion index** at four time points.
- **Analysis**: per-phase group comparisons plus a **pooled analysis** of aPDT vs conventional care (medians [IQR]; nonparametric).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Baseline imbalance**: Group 1 median 1.0 [0.75–1.5] vs Group 2 1.0 [1.0–1.5], p = 0.026.
- **First treatment phase** (both groups improved hygiene):
  - Group 1: → 0.75 [0.5–1.0], **−28.4%**, p < 0.0001.
  - Group 2: → 1.0 [0.5–1.25], **−24.1%**, p < 0.0001.
- **Second treatment phase**:
  - Group 2 (now on aPDT): 1.125 [0.94–1.5] → 0.75 [0.5–1.25], **−14.1%**, p = 0.011 (significant).
  - Group 1 (standard care only): 0.5 [0.44–0.81] → 0.63 [0.5–1.19], **+22.4%**, p = 0.11 (no significant change).
- **Pooled analysis**:
  - Adjunct dual-light aPDT: 1.0 [1.0–1.5] → 0.5 [0.5–1.0], **−21.2%**, **p = 0.0001** (significant).
  - Conventional care alone: 1.0 [0.5–1.5] → 1.0 [0.5–1.5], **−6.5%**, p = 0.11 (no significant change).
- **Bottom line**: daily dual-light aPDT as an adjunct significantly enhances plaque control beyond toothbrushing + flossing in healthy adults.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Pilot study, small sample** (n=30 young healthy adults) — limited power and generalizability; not yet definitive.
- **Baseline plaque imbalance** between groups (p=0.026) complicates between-group interpretation; pooled within-subject analysis partly mitigates this.
- **Abstract-only retrieval** — full methods (blinding, compliance/adherence to the 10-min daily regimen, adverse events, exact statistical tests) not available here.
- Short follow-up (6 weeks total) — no data on long-term plaque control, gingival/clinical endpoints, or sustainability of the effect.
- Authors suggest benefit for plaque-retentive populations (orthodontics, implants, fixed prostheses, reduced dexterity) but these were **not** the tested population — extrapolation only.

## 7. Glossary

- **aPDT (antimicrobial Photodynamic Therapy)**: light-activated photosensitizer generates reactive oxygen species that kill bacteria in biofilm.
- **Dual-light**: simultaneous use of 405 nm (antibacterial blue light) and 810 nm (near-infrared, activates photosensitizer in deeper tissue).
- **Indocyanine green (ICG)**: near-infrared-activated photosensitizer used in the device.
- **Greene-Vermillion index**: clinical plaque/oral-hygiene scoring index (Simplified Oral Hygiene Index family).
- **Crossover design**: each participant receives both interventions in sequence, serving as their own control.
