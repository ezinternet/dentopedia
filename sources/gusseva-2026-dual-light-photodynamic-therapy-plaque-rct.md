## Why Ingested

Mechanical plaque control (toothbrushing + flossing) leaves up to ~50% of plaque even with proper technique; this paper tests a non-mechanical adjunct (home-use dual-light aPDT) against that ceiling. It contextualizes the mechanical-device evidence in [[wiki/periodontics/luo-2026-electric-toothbrush-technologies-plaque-gingivitis-nma]] (which compares powered-brush *technologies* for plaque/gingivitis) by asking whether a light-based chemical adjunct can add reduction on top of conventional self-care in already-healthy adults.

## One-line Summary

Crossover pilot RCT (n=30 healthy adults, 6-week design) — daily 10-min home-use dual-light (405+810 nm) aPDT with indocyanine green, added to conventional oral care, cut plaque (Greene-Vermillion) ~21% vs baseline (pooled p=0.0001) while conventional care alone showed no significant change (-6.5%, p=0.11).

## 한줄요약

교차설계 파일럿 RCT(건강 성인 30명, 6주) — 기존 구강관리에 매일 10분 이중광(405+810 nm) 항균 광역동치료(antimicrobial Photodynamic Therapy, aPDT, indocyanine green 광감작제)를 추가하니 치태(Greene-Vermillion 지수)가 기준 대비 약 21% 감소(통합 p=0.0001), 반면 기존 관리 단독은 유의한 변화 없음(-6.5%, p=0.11).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Tests a home-use **dual-light antimicrobial photodynamic therapy (aPDT)** device — the Lumoral® Treatment (Koite Health Ltd.) — as a **daily 10-minute adjunct** to ordinary toothbrushing and flossing in healthy adults.
- Demonstrates that even in **healthy adults with good baseline oral hygiene**, adjunctive aPDT produces an additional, statistically significant plaque reduction beyond mechanical self-care alone (~21% pooled reduction, p=0.0001).
- Uses a **within-subject crossover** (6-week, 2-week phases + 2-week washout) so each participant serves as their own control across aPDT and conventional-only periods.
- Shows tooth-level plaque reduction: significant decreases at premolars (−18.6%, p=0.0028) and incisors (−20.7%, p=0.0008) during aPDT, versus no change at those sites during standard care.
- Frames dual-light aPDT as a non-antibiotic, non-staining alternative to chlorhexidine, without the risks of taste alteration, tooth discoloration, or selection for antimicrobial resistance.
- Identifies candidate populations for future benefit: patients with orthodontic appliances, implants, fixed prostheses, or reduced manual dexterity.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Prospective, randomized, crossover pilot study. February–May 2024, Institute of Dentistry, University of Tartu, Estonia.
- **Participants**: 30 young healthy adults (age 20–25 years); Group 1: 80% female (n=12), 20% male (n=3); Group 2: 73% female (n=11), 27% male (n=4). No dropouts; all 30 completed the full study.
- **Crossover structure** (6 weeks total):
  - **Phase 1 (2 weeks)**: Group 1 → daily dual-light aPDT + conventional care; Group 2 → conventional care only.
  - **Crossover/washout phase (2 weeks)**: both groups revert to conventional care.
  - **Phase 2 (2 weeks)**: Group 1 → conventional care only; Group 2 → daily dual-light aPDT + conventional care.
- **Measurements**: Greene-Vermillion Oral Hygiene Index at four time points — Baseline 1 (week 0), week 2, Baseline 2 (week 4), week 6. Index teeth: 11, 14, 24, 31, 34, 44 (buccal surfaces; scores 0–3).
- **Intervention device**: Lumoral® — 48 LED mouthpiece delivering simultaneous 405 nm (antibacterial blue light, aBL) and 810 nm (near-infrared, NIR) light at 30 J/cm² radiant intensity for 10 minutes. Used with Lumorinse® effervescent tablet (indocyanine green 250 µg/mL final concentration), swished for 60 seconds prior to light activation. Total session ~12 minutes, performed daily **before** conventional oral hygiene.
- **Mechanism**: 405 nm aBL targets endogenous bacterial chromophores (porphyrins, flavins); 810 nm NIR activates ICG adhered to plaque → reactive oxygen species (ROS) → bacterial cell damage. Neither wavelength promotes antimicrobial resistance. The 810 nm component also has photobiomodulatory potential (microcirculation improvement, tissue healing).
- **Inclusion criteria**: GV index 0.3–1.9, no comorbidities, no daily medications (except oral contraceptives), no gum disease (BOP <10%, no probing depths ≥4 mm, no attachment loss >1 mm), no subgingival calculus, no active caries.
- **Exclusion criteria**: systemic disease, daily mouthwash use (CHX 0.12% or 0.2%), candidiasis, dental student status, pregnancy/breastfeeding.
- **Calibration**: Two calibrated investigators (L.G. and L.L.L.) under PI supervision (R.R.); joint scoring exercises before initiation; standardized intraoral photographs with fixed camera settings.
- **Blinding**: Baseline 1 assessment performed pre-randomization (blinded); data analyzed blinded to group allocation. Full participant/examiner blinding not feasible (no placebo device available).
- **Statistical analysis**: Nonparametric (ordinal data, small n). Within-group: Wilcoxon signed-rank test. Between-group: Mann-Whitney U test. Two-sided α=0.05. Data presented as median [IQR]; means ± SEM also reported descriptively.
- **Sample size**: Post-hoc estimation using prior periodontitis dual-light aPDT trial data (α=5%, power 80%) → 14/group; enrolled 30 to allow for 1 dropout/group. No dropouts occurred.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Baseline (Baseline 1)**:
- Group 1 median [IQR]: 1.0 [0.75–1.5]
- Group 2 median [IQR]: 1.0 [1.0–1.5]
- Between-group difference: p = 0.026 (significant baseline imbalance — Group 1 lower)

**First treatment phase (Baseline 1 → week 2)**:
- Group 1 (dual-light aPDT): 1.0 [0.75–1.5] → 0.75 [0.5–1.0], **−28.4%**, p < 0.0001
- Group 2 (standard care): 1.0 [1.0–1.5] → 1.0 [0.5–1.25], **−24.1%**, p < 0.0001
- (Note: Group 2's improvement attributed primarily to oral hygiene instruction effect)

**Second treatment phase (Baseline 2 → week 6)**:
- Group 2 (now on aPDT): 1.125 [0.94–1.5] → 0.75 [0.5–1.25], **−14.1%**, p = 0.011 (significant)
- Group 1 (standard care only): 0.5 [0.44–0.81] → 0.63 [0.5–1.19], **+22.4%**, p = 0.11 (no significant change)

**Pooled analysis (aPDT periods vs standard care periods)**:
- Adjunct dual-light aPDT: 1.0 [1.0–1.5] → 0.5 [0.5–1.0], **−21.2%**, **p = 0.0001** (significant)
- Conventional care alone: 1.0 [0.5–1.5] → 1.0 [0.5–1.5], **−6.5%**, p = 0.107 (not significant)

**Tooth-site analysis (aPDT periods)**:
- Premolars: 1.125 [0.75–1.25] → 0.75 [0.5–1.06], **−18.6%**, p = 0.0028
- Incisors: 1.0 [1.0–1.5] → 0.5 [0.5–1.0], **−20.7%**, p = 0.0008
- Standard care periods: no significant change at premolars (−11.4%, p = ns) or incisors (−2.0%, p = ns)

**Individual variability**: A small number of participants showed little/no improvement or slightly increased plaque during aPDT periods; plaque scores during standard-care periods showed greater variability than during aPDT periods.

**Bottom line**: Daily adjunctive dual-light aPDT significantly enhances plaque control beyond toothbrushing + flossing in healthy adults, with ~21% pooled reduction. The effect was consistent across premolars and incisors specifically.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

**Limitations**:
- **Small sample (n=30)** — pilot/exploratory; limits subgroup analyses and generalizability.
- **Baseline plaque imbalance** (Group 1 lower than Group 2, p=0.026) — complicates between-group interpretation; crossover design and pooled within-subject analysis mitigate but do not eliminate this.
- **Short follow-up (6 weeks total; 2-week treatment phases)** — insufficient for long-term adherence, durability of effects, or gingival/periodontal endpoint assessment.
- **Washout period (2 weeks) may be insufficient** — potential carry-over of behavioral or biological effects, particularly in a highly compliant population.
- **No blinding of participants or examiners** — no placebo device available; potential non-specific behavioral/placebo effects and detection bias.
- **Periodontal parameters** (probing depths, CAL, BOP) assessed only for eligibility, not systematically recorded — limits periodontal outcome description.
- **No microbiological data** — microbiota composition/ecology changes not assessed.
- **Retrospective registration** (ISRCTN registered after data collection was complete).
- **COI**: Corresponding author holds equity in device manufacturer (Koite Health Ltd.).

**Future directions**:
- Larger RCTs with extended follow-up to assess gingival and periodontal outcomes.
- High-risk populations: periodontitis, peri-implantitis, orthodontic appliances.
- Microbiological analyses (biofilm composition, resistance development).
- Long-term adherence and real-world practicality studies.

## 7. Glossary

- **aPDT (antimicrobial Photodynamic Therapy, 항균 광역동치료)**: light-activated photosensitizer generates reactive oxygen species (ROS) that kill bacteria in biofilm without inducing antimicrobial resistance.
- **Dual-light**: simultaneous emission of 405 nm (antibacterial blue light, aBL) targeting endogenous bacterial chromophores (porphyrins, flavins) and 810 nm (near-infrared, NIR) activating the exogenous photosensitizer ICG.
- **Indocyanine green (ICG, 인도시아닌 그린)**: near-infrared-activated photosensitizer used in Lumorinse® (250 µg/mL); adheres to plaque for targeted activation.
- **Lumoral® Treatment**: CE-marked home-use dual-light aPDT device (Koite Health Ltd., Finland); 48-LED mouthpiece, 30 J/cm², 10-minute sessions.
- **Lumorinse®**: effervescent tablet providing ICG photosensitizer; dissolved in 30 mL water, swished for 60 seconds before Lumoral® activation.
- **Greene-Vermillion Oral Hygiene Index (그린-버밀리언 구강위생지수)**: clinical plaque/debris scoring index for 6 index teeth (0=clean, 1=<1/3 surface, 2=<2/3, 3=full surface); higher = worse plaque control.
- **Crossover design (교차설계)**: each participant receives both interventions in sequence, serving as their own control; reduces inter-individual variability.
- **Photobiomodulation (광생체조절)**: non-thermal tissue effects of 810 nm NIR light — improved microcirculation, tissue healing; complementary to the antimicrobial aPDT mechanism.
