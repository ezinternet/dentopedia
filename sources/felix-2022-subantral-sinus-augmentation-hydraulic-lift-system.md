## One-line Summary
Case report (58F, atrophic right posterior maxilla) of indirect (transcrestal) sinus floor elevation using a **hydraulic lift system** with alloplastic **phosphosilicate putty** graft and **simultaneous implant placement** in the maxillary first molar region.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical and Translational Research 2022; 8(2): 86–92
- **Type**: Case report
- **Institution**: Sri Ramakrishna Dental College, Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu, India
- **Study type**: Single-patient case report (in-vivo clinical)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Demonstrates an indirect (crestal) sinus floor elevation using a **hydraulic pressure lift** system that delivers saline to atraumatically elevate the Schneiderian membrane through the osteotomy.
- Combines hydraulic elevation with an **alloplastic phosphosilicate putty** graft and **simultaneous implant placement** in a residual ridge of limited height.
- Argues the hybrid approach offers the controlled-elevation advantages of lateral window with lower morbidity and lower perforation rate of the crestal approach.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Patient: 58-year-old female, partially edentulous, atrophic posterior maxilla, planned single-implant rehabilitation of right maxillary first molar region.
- Preoperative imaging (CBCT) assessed residual bone height and sinus floor anatomy.
- Crestal osteotomy prepared with sequential drills under residual bone height; sinus floor breached.
- Hydraulic lift device introduced saline through the osteotomy to atraumatically elevate the Schneiderian membrane.
- Alloplastic **phosphosilicate putty** graft delivered into the elevated subantral space.
- Implant placed simultaneously in the same site; primary stability achieved.
- Conventional healing period followed by screw-retained prosthesis.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Successful simultaneous implant placement reported without sinus membrane perforation.
- Postoperative healing uneventful; implant integrated and prosthesis delivered.
- Reported reduction in perforation risk and minimal bone loss vs. classical osteotome technique (per author discussion of literature; not a controlled comparison in this case).
- Quantitative ISQ / radiographic bone gain values not reported in the abstract; full results require detailed read.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Single-case design — no statistical inference, no control group.
- Outcome dependent on operator experience and device selection.
- Long-term (>5 yr) implant survival and bone stability not reported.

## 6. Related Work
- Summers 1994 — osteotome-mediated transcrestal sinus elevation (origin).
- Boyne & James 1980 — first lateral window report.
- Farina 2023 — systematic review of minimally invasive transcrestal SFE.

## 7. Glossary
- **Indirect / transcrestal sinus floor elevation**: crestal approach through the implant osteotomy.
- **Hydraulic lift system**: device that uses controlled saline pressure to lift the Schneiderian membrane.
- **Phosphosilicate putty**: bioactive alloplastic bone graft.
- **Schneiderian membrane**: ciliated respiratory epithelium lining the maxillary sinus.
