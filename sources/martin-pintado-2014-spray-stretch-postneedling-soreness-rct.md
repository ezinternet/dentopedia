## One-line Summary

Single-blind RCT (n=70 healthy, latent upper-trapezius MTrP) — spray-and-stretch after dry needling reduced postneedling soreness but only short-term (<6 h); no effect on pressure pain threshold; psychological distress correlated with postneedling pain. Extra-masticatory (transferable) technique evidence; abstract-only.

## 한줄요약

단일맹검 RCT (n=70 건강인, 상부 승모근 잠복 트리거포인트) — 건침 후 분사신장이 건침통증을 줄였으나 효과는 단시간(<6시간)에 그쳤고 압통역치는 변화 없음; 심리적 디스트레스가 건침후 통증과 상관. 교근 외 전이가능 근거, 초록 기반.

- **Journal**: Archives of Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation 2014;95(10):1925–1932.e1
- **DOI**: [10.1016/j.apmr.2014.05.021](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.apmr.2014.05.021)
- **PMID**: 24928191
- **Study type**: Single-blind randomized controlled trial, 72-h follow-up
- **Site/muscle**: Upper trapezius (NOT masticatory) — transferable technique evidence
- **Source basis**: PubMed abstract (`full_text: false`)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies a **distinct clinical use** of spray-and-stretch: reducing the soreness that follows dry needling, not treating an active trigger point per se.
- Defines the **temporal boundary** of the effect — significant only immediately post-intervention, gone by 6 hours.
- Links **psychological distress** (somatization, anxiety, interpersonal sensitivity, hostility) to postneedling pain intensity.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 70 healthy volunteers (40 M / 30 F; mean 21±4 y) with a latent MTrP in one upper trapezius.
- All received dry needling; randomized to spray-and-stretch (ethyl chloride) over the needled muscle vs no intervention (control).
- Outcomes: VAS soreness (postneedling, posttreatment, 6/12/24/48/72 h), pressure pain threshold (pre/post, 24/48 h); psychological distress via SCL-90-R.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Significant group×time interaction for postneedling soreness (F=3.19; P<.05; ηp²=.04).
- Between-group difference significant **only immediately after intervention** (P=.002); no difference after 6 h (P>.05).
- **No significant effect** on mechanical hyperalgesia / pressure pain threshold (P=.131).
- Somatization, anxiety, interpersonal sensitivity, hostility correlated with postneedling pain (P<.05).
- **Conclusion**: spray-and-stretch gives a short-term (<6 h) reduction in postneedling soreness; PPT unchanged; psychological factors matter.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Latent** trigger point in **healthy** volunteers and **upper trapezius** — not symptomatic masticatory myofascial pain.
- Effect is brief (<6 h) and limited to soreness, not threshold; clinical relevance modest.
- Abstract-only ingest; vapocoolant = ethyl chloride.

## 7. Glossary

- **Postneedling soreness**: transient muscle soreness after dry needling.
- **Latent MTrP**: a trigger point that is tender on palpation but does not spontaneously refer pain.
- **PPT (Pressure Pain Threshold)**: pressure at which sensation becomes painful.
- **SCL-90-R**: Symptom Checklist-90-Revised, a psychological-distress inventory.
