## Why Ingested

대합치 없는 치아 정출(supraeruption) 클러스터를 확장하기 위한 감시 인제스트. 방금 추가한 [[occlusion/wang-2022-unopposed-molar-overeruption-implant-cbct]](발치~보철 ~9개월간 0.43mm 정출)은 정출이 *일어난다*는 데이터이고, 본 Livas 2016은 그 정출을 *막으려는 개입(고정 retainer)*이 실제로 의미가 있는가를 본다. 결론은 retention군·부분접촉 대조군 모두 정출이 임상적으로 무의미(~0.1mm)했다는 것 — 정출의 임상적 크기가 흔히 과대평가됨을 보강. 또한 background에 12년 코호트(≥2mm 정출 위험 4.9배), 18%는 전혀 정출 안 함, 상악>하악, 초기 가장 빠름 등 정출 역학의 핵심 수치를 집약.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective panoramic study (65 Class II div 1 cases): unopposed mandibular second molars retained with bonded sectional wires showed no statistically significant overeruption vs partial-contact non-retention controls — both moved only ~0.1 mm (clinically insignificant); partial occlusal contact restrained eruption as well as fixed retention.

## 한줄요약

후향적 파노라마 연구(Class II 1류 65명): 고정 sectional wire로 잡아둔 대합치 없는 하악 2대구치는 부분접촉 비고정 대조군과 비교해 통계적으로 유의한 정출이 없었고, 양쪽 모두 약 0.1mm(임상적으로 무의미)만 이동 — 부분 교합접촉도 고정 retainer만큼 정출을 억제했다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Retrospective comparative study (panoramic radiograph superimposition)
- **Journal**: Progress in Orthodontics 2016;17:6 (2016-01-21)
- **PMID**: 26798065 / **PMCID**: PMC4722044
- **DOI**: 10.1186/s40510-016-0119-z
- **n**: 65 Class II div 1 cases (retention group 30, control 35); mandibular second molars unopposed after bilateral maxillary first molar extraction (Begg technique)

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First clinical study** of whether bonded multistranded sectional retention wires actually prevent overeruption of nonoccluding mandibular second molars.
2. Finds **no statistically significant difference** between fixed retention and partial-contact controls — both ~0.1 mm movement, clinically insignificant.
3. Concentrates **overeruption epidemiology** from prior literature: ≥2 mm risk 4.9× over 12 y; 18% never overerupt; maxillary > mandibular; fastest in first years; younger age amplifies.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Panoramic radiographs at T1 (post-treatment) and T2 (follow-up) scanned 600 dpi, traced in Viewbox 3.0.
- Second-molar centroid from 77 digitized outline points; T2→T1 transfer by Procrustes + best-fit superimposition.
- Movement expressed as % of mesiodistal crown width (panoramic gives no absolute linear scale); 1% ≈ 0.11 mm at 11 mm width.
- Molar inclination vs mandibular plane; parametric/nonparametric tests; reproducibility on 25 re-traced pairs (Bland-Altman).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **No significant T1–T2 change** in inclination or vertical movement in either group (p > 0.05).
- Overeruption: **0.5–1.0% (retention) vs 1.1–1.2% (non-retention)** of crown width ≈ ~0.1 mm — clinically insignificant.
- All molars over-erupted slightly on average; tendency larger in non-retention but **not significant**.
- Partial occlusal contact (control) was **as efficient as fixed retention** in restraining eruption — contrasts with Craddock (≤30% overlap behaves like no contact).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Retrospective; no a-priori sample size; panoramic distortion limits absolute measurement.
- Assumes adjacent teeth did not move (may explain negative vertical values); molar root growth in young subjects; possible retention-wire deformation.
- Authors: fixed retention "may be effective but should be approached with caution"; cannot be fully warranted.
- No stratification by pulp vitality / restoration — does not address endo-vs-vital overeruption.

## 6. Related Work

- Wang et al. (BMC Oral Health 2022): CBCT cohort, 0.43 mm mean overeruption in ~9 months — companion "it does happen" data.
- Craddock & Youngson (Br Dent J 2004): 83% of unopposed posterior teeth over-erupt (0.5–5.4 mm).
- Craddock (J Oral Rehabil 2007): partial occlusal contact does not prevent overeruption; ≤30% overlap behaves like no contact.

## 7. Glossary

- **Overeruption / supraeruption (정출)**: occlusal-direction migration of a tooth lacking antagonist contact.
- **Sectional / multistranded retention wire**: bonded buccal wire spanning adjacent teeth to splint and resist vertical movement.
- **Centroid superimposition (Procrustes / best-fit)**: registering serial radiographs on stable structures to measure tooth displacement.
- **V/MD ratio**: vertical molar displacement expressed as a fraction of mesiodistal crown width (normalizes panoramic scaling).
