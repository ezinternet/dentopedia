## Why Ingested

전신질환 동반 치주염 환자에서 비외과적 치주치료(Non-Surgical Periodontal Therapy, NSPT)의 단독 효능을 무치료 대조군과 직접 비교한 44편 RCT의 SR+MA로, periodontal medicine 맥락의 RCT를 통합해 "전신질환자에서도 NSPT가 효과적"이라는 근거를 강화한다. [[periodontics/smiley-2015-nonsurgical-periodontitis-srp-guideline]]의 일반 SRP 효능 근거를 전신질환 동반 인구로 확장하며, [[periodontics/meng-2024-nspt-cardiovascular-risk-markers-sr-ma]]의 심혈관 바이오마커 중심 분석과 짝을 이루어 치주 임상지표(PD·CAL·BOP) 관점을 보완한다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 44 RCTs (search 1995–2023): in periodontitis patients with concurrent systemic conditions (diabetes, CVD, RA, pregnancy, etc.), NSPT vs untreated/SGS/OHI controls gave 0.55 mm lower mean PD at 3 months (95%CI −0.69 to −0.41, low/some-concern bias studies), plus reduced CAL and %BOP and increased %PD≤3 mm.

## 한줄요약

RCT 44편 SR+MA(1995–2023 검색): 전신질환(당뇨·심혈관·류마티스·임신 등) 동반 치주염 환자에서 비외과적 치주치료(NSPT)가 무치료/치은연상치석제거/구강위생교육 대조군 대비 3개월째 평균 탐침깊이(PD)를 0.55 mm 더 감소(95%CI −0.69~−0.41)시켰고 CAL·%BOP 감소 및 %PD≤3 mm 증가도 확인 — 전신질환자에서도 NSPT가 효과적임을 입증.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Pools 44 RCTs (published 1995–2023) testing **NSPT as monotherapy** (no local/systemic antibiotics, no adjuncts, no surgery) against untreated controls, supragingival scaling (SGS), or oral hygiene instruction (OHI) in periodontitis patients **with concurrent systemic conditions**.
- Exploits the "periodontal medicine" RCT design (delayed-treatment control arm, typically capped at 3 months for ethical reasons) to answer a question those trials were not primarily designed for: does NSPT improve periodontal parameters vs no treatment in comorbid patients?
- Provides comorbidity-specific subgroup estimates (type 2 diabetes, CVD, erectile dysfunction, pregnancy, rheumatoid arthritis).
- Directly informs health-policy/reimbursement debates (cites CADTH, German IQWiG uncertainty rulings) by supplying pooled effect sizes restricted to low/some-concern risk-of-bias studies.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search**: PubMed + Embase (1995-01-01 to 2023-09-30) plus Google Scholar and manual reference search; two independent reviewers (PJ, VP). English-only.
- **Eligibility**: RCTs, adults ≥18 y, periodontitis + a systemic disease/condition; NSPT monotherapy as test; no-treatment / OHI / SGS as control; mean PD reported with ≥3-month follow-up.
- **Outcomes**: Primary = mean probing depth (PD). Secondary = mean clinical attachment loss (CAL), %sites with PD ≤ 3 mm (%PD≤3 mm), %sites with bleeding on probing (%BOP). Means/SDs extracted at 3 and 6 months.
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane RoB2 (5 domains). Pooled estimates computed separately for low/some-concern vs high risk of bias; forest and funnel plots.
- **Synthesis**: Mean difference + 95% CI between test and control arms (negative favours NSPT, except %PD≤3 mm). Median→mean and missing-SD imputation per Cochrane handbook. Heterogeneity by I²; publication bias by Egger's test/funnel plots. Random-effects meta-regression on comorbidity type, risk of bias, and publication year.
- **Subgroups**: diabetes, CVD, erectile dysfunction, pregnancy, RA; conditions with <2 studies (obesity, PCOS, COPD, CKD) excluded from subgroup analysis.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Search: 2,403 hits → 1,565 title/abstract screened → 126 full text → **44 studies** included (1995–2023).
- Trial mix: mostly single-centre (all but 4); follow-up 3 m (25 trials), 6 m (20), 12 m (2), 24 m (1); multinational (Brazil, China, India, Iran, USA, etc.).
- **Primary outcome (low/some-concern bias studies)**: NSPT group had **0.55 mm lower mean PD at 3 months vs control (95% CI −0.69 to −0.41)**.
- Across outcomes vs untreated controls, NSPT **reduced mean PD, mean CAL, and %BOP, and increased %PD≤3 mm**.
- Conclusion: NSPT is an effective procedure for managing periodontitis even in patients with concurrent systemic conditions, supporting its inclusion in care/reimbursement for comorbid populations.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Evidence is drawn from patients **with** systemic conditions, not healthy individuals — generalisability to the general periodontitis population is indirect (authors note this limitation explicitly).
- Most control arms used short (≈3-month) delayed-treatment windows for ethical reasons; few RCTs exceeded 6 months, limiting long-term inference.
- English-only inclusion; heterogeneity across comorbidities, probes, and recording protocols (full-mouth vs 6 sites/tooth varied between trials).
- Some systemic conditions (obesity, PCOS, COPD, CKD) had too few studies for subgroup pooling.
- Variable risk of bias; high-bias studies analysed separately, reflecting uneven trial quality.

## 6. Related Work

- ADA/Smiley et al. 2015 nonsurgical SRP guideline — general efficacy of scaling and root planing.
- CADTH and German IQWiG appraisals — prior policy uncertainty on NSPT benefit, which this review addresses.
- Periodontal medicine literature (diabetes, CVD, pregnancy RCTs) repurposed here for periodontal-parameter efficacy.

## 7. Glossary

- **NSPT** — Non-Surgical Periodontal Therapy (scaling/root debridement + OHI; steps 1–2 of periodontitis treatment).
- **PD** — Probing Depth.
- **CAL** — Clinical Attachment Loss/Level.
- **%BOP** — percentage of sites with Bleeding On Probing.
- **%PD≤3 mm** — percentage of sites with probing depth ≤ 3 mm (closed pockets).
- **SGS** — Supragingival Scaling; **OHI** — Oral Hygiene Instruction (control comparators).
- **RoB2** — Cochrane risk-of-bias tool for randomized trials.
- **Comorbidities studied** — type 2 diabetes, CVD, erectile dysfunction, chronic kidney disease (CKD), rheumatoid arthritis (RA), PCOS, obesity, pregnancy, COPD.
