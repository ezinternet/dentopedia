## One-line Summary
SR of 14 studies on all-ceramic resin-bonded fixed dental prostheses (RBFDPs): cantilever single-abutment designs with zirconia or IPS e.max ZirCAD frameworks achieved 76–100% survival at 3–10 years; debonding then framework fracture remain dominant failure modes.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: J Appl Biomater Funct Mater 2024 (SAGE)
- **DOI**: 10.1177/22808000241250118
- **Search**: Web of Science, MEDLINE/PubMed, Scopus, Embase, Cochrane, Google Scholar (English, 2010–2020)
- **Included**: 14 studies
- **Lead institution**: Tehran University of Medical Sciences

## 2. Key Contributions
- Synthesizes evidence on full-ceramic RBFDP design choices (cantilever vs 2-abutment), preparation features (proximal box, groove, pinhole), framework materials, surface treatment, and cementation
- Cantilever single-abutment design demonstrates superior survival vs 2-abutment fixed-fixed design (different abutment movement causing debonding)
- IPS e.max ZirCAD, In-Ceram alumina, and zirconia CAD/CAM are dominant framework materials
- Air abrasion + silanization or HF etching are standard surface treatments
- Debonding leads failure modes, followed by framework fracture

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: SR
- **Eligibility**: English clinical studies 2010–2020 on full-ceramic RBFDPs
- **Variables analyzed**: design (cantilever vs 2-abutment), preparation (proximal box, groove, pinhole), framework material, surface treatment, cement type, survival, failure mode

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Survival (3–10 year follow-up)**:
  - All-ceramic RBFDPs overall: 76–100%
  - In-Ceram alumina: 85.3–94.8% (lower)
  - In-Ceram zirconia and IPS e.max ZirCAD: higher
- **Design**: Cantilever with single abutment > 2-abutment fixed
- **Preparation features**: Proximal box / groove / pinhole improve retention
- **Surface treatment**: Air abrasion + silanization, or HF etching for glass ceramics
- **Cement**: Adhesive resin cements dominant
- **Failure modes**: Debonding (most common) > framework fracture

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Most included studies are case series or small cohorts; few RCTs
- Long-term (>10y) data still limited
- Heterogeneity in design, preparation, cement protocols
- Larger sample sizes and standardized protocols needed
- Limited posterior/load-bearing data

## 7. Glossary
- **RBFDP (Resin-Bonded Fixed Dental Prosthesis, 레진본드 고정성 보철물)**: Conservative bridge bonded to minimally prepared abutments; Maryland bridge is metal-framework variant
- **Cantilever design**: Single abutment supporting pontic on one side (less stress from differential abutment movement)
- **In-Ceram**: Glass-infiltrated alumina or zirconia ceramic system
- **IPS e.max ZirCAD**: Zirconia framework for CAD/CAM
- **HF etching (불산 산부식)**: Hydrofluoric acid etching of glass ceramics for micromechanical bonding
- **Silanization (실란 처리)**: Application of silane coupling agent for chemical bonding between ceramic and resin cement
