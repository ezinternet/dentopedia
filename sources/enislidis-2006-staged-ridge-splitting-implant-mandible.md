"Alveolar Greenstick Fracture" PMC 전문 감시 인제스트의 핵심 결과물. 치조제를 의도적으로 녹색골절(greenstick fracture)시키는 ridge-split의 가장 canonical한 술식 논문으로, 기존 [[wiki/bone-regeneration/ayoub-2018-ridge-splitting-horizontal-augmentation-case]](단일-단계 ridge split)를 보강·대비한다 — 본 논문은 하악 피질골 두께 때문에 단일-단계에서 통제 불가능한 녹색골절 위치를 **2단계(corticotomy → 40일 치유 → split)로 미리 결정**하는 개념을 제시한다. ## One-line Summary

Prospective technical note (n=5 patients, 6 sites, 17 implants): a two-stage mandibular ridge-split that predetermines the greenstick-fracture line via a prior buccal corticotomy, keeping the buccal segment periosteally pedicled — all segments fractured as planned and all implants were stable and loaded at 6 months.

## 한줄요약

2단계 하악 치조제 분할(prospective 기술노트, 환자 5명·17 임플란트): 먼저 협측 corticotomy로 녹색골절 위치를 미리 정해 두고 40일 치유 후 분할 → 협측 분절을 골막 유경피판으로 유지, 전 분절이 계획대로 골절되고 6개월에 전 임플란트가 안정·부하 성공.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Reframes the single-stage ridge-split problem in the **mandible**: cortical thickness makes the greenstick fracture during osteotome widening uncontrollable, so malfracture (uncontrolled complete fracture) risk is high.
- Introduces a **staged** protocol: rectangular buccal corticotomy first, 40-day healing, then split — so the greenstick-fracture location is **predetermined** at the basal corticotomy line.
- Preserves perfusion of the lateralized buccal segment: vascularization shifts from internal (spongy-bone) supply after stage 1 to external (periosteal) supply after stage 2; the buccal cortical plate behaves as a **pedicled graft**.
- Demonstrates feasibility: all 6 sites fractured as planned; 17 implants stable and bone-surrounded at 6 months; fixed partial denture loading successful in all.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Design: prospective consecutive case series (technical note).
- Population: 5 consecutive patients with 6 long-span edentulous mandibular areas; narrow ridges (<5 mm wide) requiring horizontal augmentation for screw-type implants.
- Procedure: (stage 1) corticotomy outlining a rectangular buccal segment; (40-day healing); (stage 2) ridge split leaving buccal periosteum attached to the lateralized segment.
- 17 implants placed; implant–bone gap grafted with venous blood + porous algae-derived hydroxyapatite.
- Outcome assessment: planned-vs-actual fracture site; implant stability and bone coverage at 6 months; prosthetic loading outcome.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- All buccal segments fractured **as planned** at the basal corticotomy line during splitting (no reported malfracture).
- At 6 months: all 17 implants stable and surrounded by bone.
- Fixed partial denture prosthetic loading successful in all cases.
- (No inferential statistics; descriptive small-series report.)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Author-acknowledged: "preliminary report," small sample, short (6-month) follow-up; technical note rather than comparative trial.
- Additional: n=5 (case-series level evidence, confidence `case-report`); no control arm vs single-stage split; abstract-only ingest (full text not retrieved), so histology/biometric detail and exact stability metrics are unavailable.

## 6. Related Work

- Single-stage ridge-split / split-crest literature it improves upon (e.g., osteotome ridge expansion with its malfracture risk).
- Conceptually paired with split-crest + GTR (Simion 1992) and modern osseodensification-assisted ridge split (Guner 2025).

## 7. Glossary

- **Greenstick fracture (alveolar)**: incomplete fracture of the ridge in which one cortical plate is hinged/bent rather than fully separated — the controlled outcome sought in ridge-splitting.
- **Malfracture**: unintended complete/uncontrolled fracture of the buccal segment, the main complication of single-stage splitting.
- **Corticotomy**: a cut through cortical bone only (not full osteotomy), here used to predefine the fracture line.
- **Pedicled (cortical) graft**: a bone segment kept attached to its periosteal blood supply, preserving vascularity after lateralization.
