## One-line Summary
Narrative clinical review (Clin Cosmet Investig Dent 2015;7:115–124) of piezoelectric bone surgery in implant dentistry — principal claimed advantages: precise/selective cutting, avoidance of thermal damage, preservation of soft tissue; indications: implant site prep, bone grafting, sinus floor elevation, ridge splitting, IAN lateralization.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Clinical, Cosmetic and Investigational Dentistry 2015;7:115–124
- Affiliation: University of Basel (Switzerland), Columbia University
- Type: Narrative clinical review
- Open access (CC BY-NC 3.0)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive clinical overview of piezosurgery indications in implant dentistry.
- Historical and technical background (piezoelectric effect, Curie brothers 1880, Catuna 1953).
- Standardizes the clinical claims that later SRs (Jain 2024) test empirically.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative review of literature.
- Indications covered: implant site preparation, bone grafting, sinus floor elevation, edentulous ridge splitting, inferior alveolar nerve (IAN) lateralization.
- Comparative discussion with mechanical instruments.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Claims of piezosurgery: precise/selective cutting (cuts hard tissue, preserves soft tissue including nerves, vessels, schneiderian membrane), reduced thermal damage, better surgical visibility.
- Disadvantages: longer surgical time vs rotary; learning curve; insert wear; equipment cost.
- Verdict: piezo "superior to other methods that utilize mechanical instruments" in select indications, particularly when delicate tissues are at risk.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative review without quantitative pooling.
- Author claim of "avoidance of thermal damage" is contradicted by later studies (Aquilanti 2023, Jain 2024).
- Industry/manufacturer relationships not fully disclosed.
- Future: standardized comparison studies with manufacturer-recommended technique.

## 6. Related Work
- Aquilanti 2023 — direct in vitro thermal comparison.
- Jain 2024 SR — pain reduction yes, thermal benefit no.
- Kosior 2025 — piezo in 5-method histological comparison.

## 7. Glossary
- **Piezoelectric effect**: Curie 1880 — pressure on certain crystals creates electricity; reverse effect produces mechanical deformation from applied voltage.
- **Selective cutting**: piezo cuts mineralized hard tissue at low amplitude, but does not cut soft tissue at the same setting.
- **IAN lateralization**: surgical repositioning of the inferior alveolar nerve to allow implant placement.
