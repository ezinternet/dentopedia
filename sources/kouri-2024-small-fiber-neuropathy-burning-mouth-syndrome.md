## One-line Summary

PRISMA systematic review (PROSPERO CRD42024555839) of small fiber neuropathy in BMS: across included studies, tongue biopsies show 30–60% reductions in nerve fiber density plus altered TRPV1/P2X3/NGF expression, Nav1.7 overexpression, and sensory threshold abnormalities, strongly supporting SFN as a core pathophysiologic mechanism.

## 한줄요약

PRISMA 체계적 고찰(PROSPERO CRD42024555839) — BMS 환자 혀 생검에서 신경섬유밀도가 30~60% 감소하고 TRPV1·P2X3·NGF 발현 변화, Nav1.7 과발현, 감각역치 이상이 일관되게 관찰돼, 소섬유신경병증(SFN)이 BMS의 핵심 병태생리임을 강하게 지지.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First PRISMA-registered systematic synthesis dedicated to small fiber neuropathy (SFN) evidence in BMS.
- Consolidates histopathologic evidence: eight studies report reduced intraepithelial/tongue nerve fiber density (30–60%) with morphologic changes indicative of small-fiber damage.
- Aggregates molecular markers: increased TRPV1-positive, NGF-positive, and P2X3-positive fibers; overexpression of Nav1.7; slight underexpression of Nav1.9 mRNA; increased artemin mRNA.
- Aggregates functional evidence: QST abnormalities (reduced cool detection, reduced cold pain thresholds) in seven studies; blink reflex and corneal confocal microscopy indicating both peripheral and central small-fiber damage.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Design: systematic review following PRISMA; protocol pre-registered (PROSPERO CRD42024555839).
- Evidence streams synthesized: tongue/mucosal biopsy nerve fiber density and immunohistochemistry; QST; blink reflex; corneal confocal microscopy; mRNA expression of ion channels and neurotrophic factors.
- Narrative synthesis (no meta-analytic pooling due to heterogeneity).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Nerve fiber density reduction: 30–60% across eight biopsy studies.
- Molecular: ↑TRPV1, ↑NGF, ↑P2X3 fibers; ↑Nav1.7; slight ↓Nav1.9 mRNA; ↑artemin mRNA.
- QST (7 studies): reduced cool detection threshold and cold pain threshold — small-fiber dysfunction signature.
- Conclusion: evidence strongly supports SFN as a key factor in BMS pathophysiology, especially via reduced nerve fiber density and altered sensory thresholds.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- High between-study variability; small samples; heterogeneous methods preclude meta-analysis.
- Cannot establish causality (cross-sectional/observational source studies).
- Authors call for larger, standardized studies to establish causal relationships and guide therapy.

## 7. Glossary

- SFN (Small Fiber Neuropathy): degeneration/dysfunction of thinly myelinated Aδ and unmyelinated C fibers carrying pain/temperature signals.
- TRPV1: capsaicin/heat-sensitive transient receptor potential channel; marker of nociceptor sensitization.
- P2X3: ATP-gated ion channel on nociceptors.
- Nav1.7 / Nav1.9: voltage-gated sodium channels central to nociceptor excitability; Nav1.7 gain is pro-nociceptive.
- IENFD: intraepithelial nerve fiber density — quantitative biopsy measure of small-fiber integrity.
- Corneal confocal microscopy: non-invasive imaging of corneal small nerve fibers as an SFN biomarker.
