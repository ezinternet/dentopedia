## One-line Summary

Narrative literature review (8 articles, 2020–June 2023; abstract-only) concluding that elastodontic devices treat malocclusion in growing patients safely, simply, and comfortably, but with a noted lack of knowledge/evidence on the technique.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Verifies, from the most recent literature, the effectiveness of elastodontic devices in treating malocclusions in growing patients.
- Characterizes elastodontic appliances as a safe, simple, and more comfortable option for the young patient.
- Explicitly identifies a lack of knowledge about the technique and positions the review as supplying the scientific community with updated evidence.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- English-language literature search.
- Databases: PubMed, Web of Science, Scopus.
- Search window: 2020 to June 2023.
- Eight articles included after applying inclusion/exclusion criteria.
- (Abstract-only: detailed inclusion/exclusion criteria, individual study designs, and quality appraisal are not recoverable from the abstract.)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

1. Eight articles met the inclusion/exclusion criteria.
2. The included articles highlight the ability of elastodontic devices to act in cases of malocclusion.
3. Treatment is described as safe for the young patient.
4. Treatment is described as simple to deliver.
5. Treatment is described as more comfortable for the young (growing) patient.
6. A lack of knowledge about the technique is noted, motivating the review.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative review with a small evidence base (8 articles) — limited synthesis weight.
- Abstract-only ingest: no extractable risk-of-bias appraisal, effect sizes, or per-malocclusion-type breakdown.
- Authors explicitly note a lack of knowledge/evidence about elastodontic technique; more (and higher-quality) primary research is needed.

## 7. Glossary

- **Elastodontic appliance / device**: Prefabricated elastomeric (silicone/polyurethane) myofunctional appliance worn intra-orally to redirect orofacial muscle forces and intercept malocclusion in growing patients.
- **Malocclusion**: Misalignment or incorrect relationship between the dental arches.
- **Growing patient**: Pediatric/adolescent patient still in active craniofacial growth, the target population for interceptive/myofunctional therapy.
- **Interceptive orthodontics**: Early intervention aimed at correcting or stopping a developing malocclusion.
