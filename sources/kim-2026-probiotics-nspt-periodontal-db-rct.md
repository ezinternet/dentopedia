## Why Ingested

치주비수술치료(Non-Surgical Periodontal Treatment, NSPT) 보조제로서 프로바이오틱스(Probiotics)의 이중맹검 무작위대조시험(Double-Blind Randomized Controlled Trial, DB-RCT). 임상에서 "프로바이오틱스 효과 있나" 질문이 많음; J Clin Periodontol 수준의 고수준 근거로 [[periodontics]] 영역 빈 자리를 채움. 16S rRNA 시퀀싱 + 메타게노믹스 기반의 포괄적 미생물 평가라는 방법론적 강점이 있어 향후 overview synthesis에 핵심 근거가 될 논문.

## One-line Summary

Double-blind RCT (n=80; 61 completers); Lactobacillus PB01 + Enterococcus EB10 lozenges as adjunct to NSPT over 12 weeks reduced BoP% and PPD ≥5 mm sites significantly vs. placebo, with concurrent reduction in red/orange complex pathogens in subgingival and salivary microbiota.

## 한줄요약

이중맹검 무작위대조시험(n=80; 완료 61명); Lactobacillus PB01 + Enterococcus EB10 로젠지를 비수술치주치료(NSPT) 보조제로 12주간 사용 시 위약 대비 출혈양성률(BoP%)과 치주낭깊이(PPD) ≥5 mm 부위 수가 유의하게 감소하였고, 치주낭 및 타액 내 적색·오렌지색 복합군 병원균이 감소함.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First comprehensive DB-RCT** combining 16S rRNA sequencing (subgingival) + shotgun metagenomics (salivary) + clinical + immunological endpoints for probiotics adjunct to NSPT.
2. Demonstrated that Lactobacillus PB01 DSM14870 + Enterococcus EB10 DSM32307 lozenges produce significant reductions in BoP% and PPD ≥5 mm sites vs. placebo at 12 weeks post-NSPT.
3. Showed microbiome-level shifts: placebo group retained higher red complex (P. gingivalis, T. denticola) and orange complex species; probiotic group showed enrichment of commensal health-associated species (Rothia dentocariosa, Neisseria elongata).
4. Salivary metagenomics revealed broader immune-functional shifts in probiotic group: reduced genes for oxidative phosphorylation and anaerobic metabolic pathways.
5. Identified limits of salivary immunology (cytokines IL-1β, IL-8, MCP-1, MIF did not differ between groups) — suggests GCF may be more sensitive for future studies.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

### Trial Design
- **Randomisation**: 1:1 computer-generated (randomize.net); allocation concealed by single unblinded investigator (D.B.)
- **Blinding**: Double-blind (participants and clinical examiners); lozenges identical in appearance and flavour
- **Intervention**: Probiotic or placebo lozenge — one in morning, one in evening after oral hygiene, avoiding food/drink for 30 min afterward; duration 12 weeks
- **NSPT**: Steps 1–2 per EFP 2022 guideline (OHI, supragingival scaling, subgingival instrumentation with curettes + ultrasonic); no antimicrobials, host modulators, or chemical agents

### Probiotic Lozenge Composition
- Lactobacillus PB01 DSM14870 (≥1 × 10^9 CFU per lozenge)
- Enterococcus EB10 DSM32307 (≥1 × 10^9 CFU per lozenge)
- 491 mg xylitol per lozenge
- Manufacturer: ADM Denmark A/S

### Participants
- **N enrolled**: 80 (stage II or III periodontitis per 2017 Classification; ≥18 years; ≥20 teeth)
- **N completed**: 61 (probiotics n=32, placebo n=29); 19 dropouts (antibiotics 6, absence 7, consent withdrawal 2, extraction 2, surgery 1, non-compliance 1)
- **Exclusions**: Other oral diseases, immune/inflammatory systemic disease, dysregulated DM, cancer <4 yr, corticosteroids, antibiotics/SRP within 3 months, probiotic use within 1 month, pregnancy

### Clinical Assessments
- **Timepoints**: Baseline, Week 6 (PI, BoP only), Week 12 (full: PI, PPD, BoP, CAL — 6 sites/tooth, third molars excluded)
- **Examiner calibration**: ICC intra-rater PPD 0.864, CAL 0.867; ICC inter-rater PPD 0.800, CAL 0.797 (good–excellent)

### Microbiological Methods
- **Subgingival 16S**: V1–V3 region, Illumina MiSeq; HOMD v.15.2 reference; LEfSe (LDA >2) + relative abundance
- **Salivary metagenomics**: Nextera XT library prep; Illumina NovaSeq 6000 (150bp PE); Metaphlan annotation against eHOMD; DESeq2 differentially abundant taxa; KEGG pathway GSEA
- **α-diversity**: Shannon, Simpson, Richness; ANCOVA with baseline adjustment; β-diversity by Bray-Curtis + PERMANOVA

### Immunological Methods
- **Cytokines** (salivary supernatant): IL-8, IL-1β, MCP-1, MIF — bead-based immunoassay
- **Proteins/enzymes**: Albumin (ELISA), amylase activity, total protease activity (TPA), chitinase activity — fluorometric

### Statistics
- ANCOVA (between groups), ANOVA + Tukey (within groups); ITT analysis for clinical outcomes; Spearman correlations; Benjamini-Hochberg FDR correction for microbiome; p < 0.05 threshold

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Clinical (Table 1 summary)

| Parameter | Placebo BL → Wk12 | Probiotics BL → Wk12 | p (between) | p (smoking-adj) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| PI% | 84.4 → 35.1 | 83.3 → 37.4 | 0.65 | 0.51 |
| BoP% | 29.2 → 18.7 | 32.7 → 15.6 | **0.04** | **0.03** |
| PPD mean (mm) | 3.0 → 2.9 | 3.0 → 2.7 | **0.03** | 0.07 |
| PPD <5 mm sites | 136.9 → 144.5 | 138.8 → 150.3 | **0.03** | **0.02** |
| PPD ≥5 mm sites | 22.4 → 14.8 | 20.4 → 8.9 | **0.01** | **0.01** |
| CAL (mm) | 1.57 → 1.49 | 1.65 → 1.58 | 0.8 | 0.94 |

- At week 12: 5 placebo vs. 14 probiotic participants achieved BoP <10% (p=0.03)
- 0 placebo vs. 3 probiotic participants achieved full periodontal health (p=0.24, NS)
- ITT analysis: only BoP% remained significant between groups (p=0.014)

### Microbiological

**Subgingival (16S)**:
- Probiotic group enriched: Rothia dentocariosa, Neisseria elongata (health-associated)
- Placebo group retained higher: P. gingivalis, T. denticola (red complex), T. forsythia, F. nucleatum (orange complex)
- LEfSe identified 30 species differing between groups at 12 weeks
- α-diversity: decreased significantly more in probiotic group (Shannon p=0.01; Simpson p=0.03)

**Salivary (metagenomics)**:
- DESeq2: 23 differentially abundant species; P. gingivalis, T. denticola, F. nucleatum, T. forsythia all decreased more in probiotic group
- PERMANOVA: individual variation explained 77.4% of variance; NSPT 1%; probiotic intervention 2% (p<0.001)
- Functional: probiotic group showed greater reduction in oxidative phosphorylation, anaerobic metabolic pathways; increase in B6 vitamin and ascorbate metabolism genes

### Immunological
- No significant between-group differences for any cytokines (IL-8, IL-1β, MCP-1, MIF)
- Albumin increased more in placebo group (p=0.008) — interpreted as greater vascular leakage
- Chitinase decreased more in probiotic group (p=0.009) — suggests reduced tissue breakdown

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

1. **Short study duration** (12 weeks) — significant microbial and clinical changes in periodontitis often take 6–12 months
2. **Exploratory/pilot sample size** — powered for microbiological outcomes; immunological analyses likely underpowered
3. **Saliva vs. GCF** — cytokines diluted in saliva; GCF recommended for future immune endpoint studies
4. **Confounders**: smokers and diabetics included (mitigated by post-hoc sub-analyses with similar results)
5. **Single-centre** recruitment at university clinic — limits generalisability; patients may be unrepresentative of general population
6. **Probiotic heterogeneity** in prior literature (different strains, doses, frequencies) hampers meta-analytic comparison
7. **Future directions**: longer RCTs (≥6 months), multi-centre design, stratification for confounders, metatranscriptomic data, GCF immune profiling

## 6. Related Work

- Prior studies (same research group): probiotic effect on experimental gingivitis and sugar-induced microbiota changes (Lundtorp et al.; Lundtorp Olsen, Massarenti, et al.)
- EFP 2022 periodontal treatment guidelines (Sanz et al.) — NSPT protocol framework
- Multiple SRs/MAs on probiotics for periodontitis (Ho, Hu, Ikram, Li, Martin-Cabezas, Mishra, Ram, Song & Liu, Vives-Soler, Nguyen — equivocal results due to heterogeneity)
- Socransky red/orange complex species classification (Socransky et al.) — used as reference for pathogen categorisation
- HOMD v.15.2 (Escapa et al.) — microbial reference database

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| NSPT | Non-surgical periodontal treatment — Steps 1–2 EFP: OHI + supragingival + subgingival scaling/debridement |
| SRP | Scaling and root planing — subgingival instrumentation component of NSPT |
| BoP% | Bleeding on probing percentage — proportion of probed sites that bleed |
| PPD | Probing pocket depth — depth of sulcus/pocket in mm |
| CAL | Clinical attachment level — distance from CEJ to base of pocket |
| PI | Plaque index — proportion of surfaces with visible plaque |
| CFU | Colony-forming units — measure of viable probiotic bacteria per lozenge |
| LEfSe | Linear discriminant analysis effect size — method to identify differentially abundant taxa between groups |
| DESeq2 | R package for differential abundance analysis of sequencing count data |
| HOMD | Human Oral Microbiome Database — reference for 16S/metagenomic classification |
| Red complex | P. gingivalis, T. denticola, T. forsythia — strongly periodontitis-associated species (Socransky) |
| Orange complex | F. nucleatum, P. intermedia and others — intermediate periodontal pathogens (Socransky) |
| KEGG | Kyoto Encyclopedia of Genes and Genomes — functional pathway annotation |
| GSEA | Gene set enrichment analysis — identifies enriched functional modules |
| GCF | Gingival crevicular fluid — site-specific inflammatory exudate |
| MIF | Macrophage migration inhibitory factor — pro-inflammatory cytokine |
| MCP-1 | Monocyte chemoattractant protein-1 — chemokine |
| TPA | Total protease activity — measure of salivary proteolytic enzymes |
| ITT | Intention-to-treat analysis — includes all randomised participants regardless of completion |
