## Why Ingested

기존 [[tmj/zheng-2023-unilateral-chewing-tmj-structure-tmd-review]]는 편측저작(habitual chewing side)이 TMD 위험인자라는 narrative review였으나, 임상에서 "어느 쪽으로 씹는지가 통증 쪽과 일치하는가"의 직접 측정 근거는 부족했다. 본 cross-sectional double-blind 연구(Santana-Mora 2021)는 교합치료 이력이 없는 정상교합 만성 편측 TMD 환자 24명에서 저작측·과로(condylar path, CP)·측방유도(lateral guidance, LG)를 정량 측정하여, 통증측 = 습관저작측 + steep CP + flat LG의 측면 dimorphism을 실측 데이터로 보강한다. [[tmj/kapos-2020-temporomandibular-disorders-aetiology-diagnosis-review]]의 "etiology unknown" 명제에 말초 biodynamic 인자 후보를 추가한다.

## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional double-blind study (n=24, chronic unilateral TMD, untreated normal occlusion): the habitual chewing side coincided with the TMD-painful side in 15/17 (Kappa=0.767), with a steeper condylar path (50.5° vs 45.5°, P=0.002) and flatter lateral guidance (38.0° vs 47.5°, P<0.001) on the affected side.

## 한줄요약

교합치료 이력 없는 정상교합 만성 편측 TMD 환자 24명 단면연구: 습관저작측이 통증측과 일치(17명 중 15명, Kappa=0.767)했고, 통증측은 과로각이 더 가파르고(50.5° vs 45.5°, P=0.002) 측방유도각이 더 평평했다(38.0° vs 47.5°, P<0.001).

## 2. Key Contributions

- First study to assess dynamic/functional masticatory factors in TMD patients with **clinically normal, untreated occlusion** (no orthodontic/prosthetic/surgical occlusal therapy) — eliminating occlusal-therapy confounding of lateral guidance and condylar remodeling.
- Demonstrates a **sided dimorphism** in chronic unilateral TMD: the affected side shows (a) habitual chewing, (b) steeper condylar path (CP) angle = TMJ remodeling, and (c) flatter lateral guidance (LG) angle = dental-incline remodeling.
- Introduces **asymmetry indexes (AiCP, AiLG)** and a combined equation `[right CP × left LG] / [left CP × right LG]` that discriminate the affected side and predict habitual chewing side.
- Supports the "habitual chewing side syndrome" concept as a candidate peripheral biodynamic etiology, while acknowledging neither CP nor LG recording is diagnostically sensitive/specific for TMD on its own.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Cross-sectional, double-blind (participants unaware of purpose; clinicians/assessors blinded to condition). Declaration of Helsinki; ethics CAEI 2009/017.
- **Screening funnel**: 924 screened → 77 met inclusion → after excluding 26 bilateral TMD and 27 with prior orthodontic/prosthetic therapy → **final n=24** (17 women, 7 men; mean age 37.3 ± 11.7 y).
- **Inclusion**: chronic (>6 mo) substantial self-reported unilateral TMD pain (VAS 4–9/10, IMMPACT criteria); diagnosis per DC/TMD (Schiffman 2014); clinically normal stable Angle Class I occlusion (<2 mm horizontal displacement); complete dentition; no irreversible occlusal therapy.
- **Chewing side**: gum-chewing observation (first + subsequent cycles) + kinesiography + interview; agreement across tests = consistent chewing side.
- **Condylar path (CP)**: axiographic Gysi method with Gerber kinematic face-bow; ≥3 tracings/TMJ during lateral excursion; digitized; two independent assessors (ICC=0.87).
- **Lateral guidance (LG)**: K7 kinesiograph (Myotronics); 3 tracings; ImageJ measurement; two clinicians (ICC=0.94); differences >3° resolved by a third researcher.
- **Imaging**: MRI by two independent radiologists for disc displacement/degeneration, condylar hypoplasia/degeneration.
- **Statistics**: paired/unpaired two-sided Student t-tests (normality verified); Fisher exact, chi-squared, Kappa for dichotomous chewing/pain side. α=0.05. Sample size n=17 sufficient to test Kappa=0 (detect Kappa>0.7, α=0.05, power 0.8).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Chewing vs pain side**: 17/24 (71%) had a consistent habitual chewing side; in 15/17 it was the TMD-affected side. Pearson χ²=10.578, P=0.001; Fisher exact P=0.002; **Kappa=0.767, P=0.001**; risk estimate 4.5 (95% CI 1.326–15.277).
- **Condylar path (CP)**: affected 50.52° (9.98°) vs nonaffected 45.50° (7.98°), P=0.002. Right-vs-left of jaw NS (P=0.974). 17/22 (77%) had steeper CP on affected side (binomial P=0.017).
- **Lateral guidance (LG)**: affected 38.04° (8.15°) vs nonaffected 47.48° (13.10°), P<0.001. LG flatter on affected side in **all** participants. Right-vs-left of jaw NS (P=0.131).
- **Asymmetry indexes**: AiCP positive for right-pain / negative for left-pain (P<0.001); AiLG reversed sign (P<0.001); combined equation right-pain 1.54 vs left-pain 0.73 (P=0.002). No between-sex differences.
- **Interpretation**: in untreated normal occlusion, the TMD-painful side is characterized by habitual chewing + steeper CP (TMJ remodeling) + flatter LG (dental remodeling) — consistent with a self-reinforcing loop (flat LG → preferential chewing on that side → TMJ overload/remodeling → ipsilateral symptoms).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Small, severe, tertiary-referral sample (n=24)** — likely the more severe end of TMD prevalence; limited generalizability.
- **Cross-sectional / non-causal**: associations cannot establish whether flat LG → unilateral chewing → TMD, or the reverse; temporal direction unproven.
- 7/24 had **no identifiable habitual chewing side** (excluded from the chewing analysis).
- Two CP datasets excluded for aberrant condyle form/motion (severe internal derangement/osteoarthritis can flatten CP, opposing the general pattern).
- CP and LG recordings showed **neither sensitivity nor specificity** to detect TMD individually — proposed as evolutionary/dynamic descriptors, not diagnostic tests.
- Chewing function is not fixed (changes with caries, eruption, therapy over a lifetime), complicating measurement.

## 6. Related Work

- Builds on the authors' prior work (Santana-Mora 2013) on sided characteristics in TMD.
- Contrasts with non-patient studies showing no sided CP (Kordaß 2019) or LG (Ferrario 1992) differences.
- Animal models (Poikela 1997; Zhang 2003) support unilateral chewing → TMJ remodeling.

## 7. Glossary

- **TMD**: Temporomandibular (joint) disorder.
- **Condylar path (CP) angle**: inclination of the condylar movement path relative to the Frankfort horizontal during lateral excursion; steeper = TMJ remodeling/higher articular eminence.
- **Lateral guidance (LG) angle**: inclination of the dental guidance during lateral excursion; flatter = lower dental inclines (dental/occlusal remodeling).
- **Habitual chewing side**: the side an individual preferentially uses for mastication ("habitual chewing side syndrome").
- **Axiography**: graphical recording of condylar movement paths (Gysi method).
- **Kinesiography**: instrumented recording of mandibular movement (K7).
- **Asymmetry index (Ai)**: [right − left] / [right + left] of CP or LG, capturing left–right dimorphism.
- **ICC**: intraclass correlation coefficient (inter-observer reliability).
- **DC/TMD**: Diagnostic Criteria for Temporomandibular Disorders (Schiffman 2014).
