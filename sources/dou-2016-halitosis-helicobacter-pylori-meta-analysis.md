## Why Ingested

Clarifies the gastric/extra-oral etiology of halitosis by meta-analyzing the H. pylori–halitosis relationship, a question not addressed by the existing oral-etiology-focused wiki pages (e.g., [[halitosis/memon-2022-aetiology-associations-halitosis-systematic]]). Provides quantified odds ratios and eradication therapy data that directly inform clinical decision-making for refractory halitosis.

## One-line Summary

Meta-analysis of 21 studies (n varied by subgroup) found H. pylori infection significantly associated with halitosis (OR 2.85, 95% CI 1.40–5.83), and successful H. pylori eradication markedly reduced halitosis (RR 0.17, 95% CI 0.08–0.39).

## 한줄요약

21개 연구를 메타분석한 결과, 헬리코박터 파일로리(H. pylori) 감염은 구취와 유의하게 연관되었으며(OR 2.85), 제균 치료 성공 시 구취가 현저히 감소(RR 0.17)하였다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Medicine (Wolters Kluwer), 2016;95(39):e4223
- **DOI**: 10.1097/MD.0000000000004223
- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Search date**: Inception to December 2015
- **Databases**: Cochrane Library, MEDLINE, EMBASE, PubMed, Web of Science, Wanfangdata
- **Languages included**: English and Chinese
- **Included studies**: 21 (from 115 identified; 57 considered potentially relevant)
- **Software**: Review Manager 5.2
- **Reporting guideline**: MOOSE

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First meta-analysis to pool all available evidence (English + Chinese literature) on the H. pylori–halitosis association.
2. Quantified bidirectional association: H. pylori prevalence is higher in halitosis patients (OR 4.03), and halitosis is more prevalent in H. pylori-infected patients (OR 2.85).
3. Demonstrated that successful H. pylori eradication significantly reduces halitosis (RR 0.17), supporting eradication as a therapeutic option for refractory halitosis.
4. Pre-vs-post eradication comparison: halitosis prevalence before eradication was 4.78-fold higher than after (RR 4.78).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Study design groupings (4 subgroups):**
- Group 1: H. pylori infection rates in halitosis-positive vs. halitosis-negative patients
- Group 2: Halitosis rates in H. pylori-positive vs. H. pylori-negative patients
- Group 3: Halitosis rates in infected-halitosis patients after successful vs. unsuccessful H. pylori eradication
- Group 4: Halitosis rates in H. pylori-infected patients before vs. after successful eradication

**Inclusion criteria:** Case reports, case-control, cross-sectional, RCTs, quasi-RCTs, comparative clinical experimental trials investigating H. pylori–halitosis relationship or halitosis before/after eradication.

**Statistical approach:**
- Heterogeneity assessed via Cochrane Q-statistic and I² statistic (threshold: I² >50% or P <0.05)
- Random-effects model used when heterogeneity present
- Publication bias not assessed (fewer than 10 studies per subgroup)
- ORs for observational studies; RRs for interventional studies

**H. pylori detection methods across studies:** CLO-test (campylobacter-like organism test), urea breath test (UBT), histology, culture, serology

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Subgroup | Metric | Effect estimate | 95% CI | P-value |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Group 1: H. pylori in halitosis(+) vs. halitosis(-) | OR | 4.03 | 1.41–11.50 | 0.009 |
| Group 2: Halitosis in H. pylori(+) vs. H. pylori(-) | OR | 2.85 | 1.40–5.83 | 0.004 |
| Group 3: Halitosis after successful vs. unsuccessful eradication | RR | 0.17 | 0.08–0.39 | <0.0001 |
| Group 4: Halitosis before vs. after successful eradication | RR | 4.78 | 1.45–15.80 | 0.01 |

**Pathophysiological mechanism proposed:**
- H. pylori produces urease → ammonia (alkaline odor)
- H. pylori produces H₂S and methyl mercaptan (VSCs) directly via cystathionine β-synthase (CBS) and cystathionine γ-lyase (CSE)
- Gastric dysbiosis may alter the oral-GI microbial axis

**Key cited background:** ~80–90% of halitosis is intraoral; 10–20% is extra-oral (respiratory, gastrointestinal, systemic). H. pylori was first linked to halitosis by Tiomny et al. (case report). Conflicting results existed prior to this meta-analysis (Ierardi: eradication resolves halitosis; Tangerman: no association).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

1. **High heterogeneity** in some subgroups due to different study designs (RCTs, case-control, cross-sectional), different H. pylori detection methods, and different halitosis assessment methods (organoleptic, VSC measurement, patient self-report).
2. **Publication bias not formally assessed** because fewer than 10 studies per subgroup (no funnel plot possible).
3. **Confounding variables not fully controlled:** periodontal disease, oral hygiene, tongue coating — all independent halitosis causes — were not consistently excluded across studies.
4. **Chinese-language studies included** may have reporting differences.
5. **Causality not established:** association does not prove H. pylori is a primary halitosis cause; could be indirect via gastric pH, GERD, or microbiome disruption.
6. **Future needs:** well-designed RCTs with standardized halitosis measurement (VSC meters + organoleptic scoring), consistent oral-hygiene control, and standardized H. pylori eradication protocols.

## 6. Related Work

- **Tiomny et al.**: First case report linking H. pylori to halitosis
- **Ierardi et al.**: H. pylori eradication resolved halitosis
- **Serin et al.**: Halitosis is a frequent, treatable symptom of H. pylori-positive non-ulcer dyspepsia
- **Tangerman study**: No association found; concluded halitosis originates in oral cavity
- **Moshkowitz et al.**: Halitosis as extra-esophageal manifestation of GERD
- **Struch et al.**: GERD–halitosis association

## 7. Glossary

- **VSC (Volatile Sulfur Compounds)**: 황 함유 휘발성 화합물 (H₂S, methyl mercaptan, dimethyl sulfide) — primary odorants in halitosis
- **H. pylori (Helicobacter pylori)**: 헬리코박터 파일로리 — gram-negative spiral bacterium, primary colonizer of gastric mucosa
- **CLO-test (Campylobacter-Like Organism test)**: H. pylori rapid urease test from biopsy
- **UBT (Urea Breath Test)**: 요소 호기 검사 — non-invasive H. pylori detection
- **CBS (Cystathionine β-synthase)** / **CSE (Cystathionine γ-lyase)**: Enzymes producing H₂S from cysteine
- **OR (Odds Ratio)**: 오즈비 — used for case-control and cross-sectional studies
- **RR (Risk Ratio / Relative Risk)**: 상대위험도 — used for RCTs and interventional studies
- **GERD (Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease)**: 위식도역류질환
- **MOOSE**: Meta-analysis Of Observational Studies in Epidemiology reporting guideline
- **Refractory halitosis**: 난치성 구취 — halitosis unresponsive to standard oral hygiene measures
