## Why Ingested

기존 위키에는 치간청소 기기 간 직접 head-to-head 비교가 부족했다 — [[interdental-cleaning/thomassen-2025-airfloss-essential-oils-vs-floss-rct]]는 에어플로스 vs 치실(군간 무차이), [[interdental-cleaning/yiamwattana-2025-oral-irrigator-vs-floss-orthodontic-sr-ma]]는 구강세정기 vs 치실(NS)이었다. 본 RCT(Mancinelli-Lyle 2024)는 물세정기(WF) vs **치간칫솔(IDB)**을 4주간 BOPP/BOMP·치은마모로 직접 비교한 드문 동급 비교로, 치간청소 기기선택 질문에 핵심 근거를 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

RCT (single-blind parallel, n=78 young adults with moderate gingivitis): water flosser (WF, n=40) vs interdental brush (IDB, n=38) adjunct to manual brushing over 4 weeks — both reduced BOMP/BOPP (p=0.000), but WF was significantly more effective than IDB for marginal gingival health (all-sites BOMP p=0.003, BOPP p=0.030) with no difference in gingival abrasion.

## 한줄요약

RCT(단일맹검 평행, n=78 중등도 치은염 청년): 물세정기(WF, n=40) vs 치간칫솔(IDB, n=38)을 칫솔질 보조로 4주 비교 — 두 기기 모두 BOMP/BOPP 감소(p=0.000)했으나, WF가 변연치은 건강(전체부위 BOMP p=0.003, BOPP p=0.030)에서 IDB보다 유의하게 더 효과적이었고 치은마모 차이는 없었다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- One of the first 4-week RCTs directly comparing a **water flosser (WF)** against an **interdental brush (IDB)** — both as adjuncts to manual toothbrushing — for gingival bleeding (prior direct comparisons were only single-use and 2-week pilots).
- Dual bleeding indices: **bleeding on pocket probing (BOPP)** and **bleeding on marginal probing (BOMP)**, assessed in two contralateral randomly assigned quadrants (split-mouth-style design across indices).
- Added a **safety endpoint**: Gingival Abrasion Score (GAS) via plaque-disclosing solution to detect soft-tissue trauma.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Population**: systemically healthy non-smokers, 18-65 y, ≥20 natural teeth (5 evaluable/quadrant), moderate-severe gingivitis (≥50% BOPP), no pockets >5 mm. Buccal-accessible interdental spaces fitting the IDB in ≥4 spaces per quadrant (2 involving molars). Sample mostly university students.
- **Groups**: WF = Waterpik Water Flosser (500 mL lukewarm water, Classic Tip, 90° to tooth long axis, pressure dial 8, once daily) vs IDB = Interprox premium nano conical brush (1.9 mm brush / 0.38 mm core, 5 in-out strokes, buccal approach, once daily). All used a standard manual toothbrush (Bass technique, twice daily) + standard NaF toothpaste.
- **Outcomes**: BOPP (0.25 N plastic force-controlled probe, 6 sites/tooth), BOMP (WHO ball-ended probe, ~2 mm at ~60°, 6 sites/tooth) at baseline, 2 weeks, 4 weeks; GAS via Mira-2-Ton disclosing solution.
- **Blinding**: single-blind (examiners blinded to randomization; participants could not be blinded to device). 3 examiners (one per index).
- **Power**: 68 needed (34/group, 80% power, between-group diff 0.11, SD 0.16, BOPP); +15% for drop-outs. Final analyzed: 78 (WF 40, IDB 38). SPSS 23, p<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Both groups improved significantly from baseline (p=0.000) for BOMP and BOPP at W2 and W4, all sites and interdental sites.**

Bleeding on marginal probing (BOMP), all sites — between-group p:
- WF baseline 0.82 → W4 0.54; IDB baseline 0.95 → W4 0.75. **W2 p=0.002, W4 p=0.003** (WF lower; mean diff 0.21). Baseline NS (p=0.082).
- Interdental sites: **W2 p=0.003, W4 p=0.019** (WF lower; diff 0.18). Baseline NS (p=0.130).

Bleeding on pocket probing (BOPP), all sites — between-group p:
- WF baseline 0.60 → W4 0.50; IDB baseline 0.66 → W4 0.56. **W4 p=0.030** (WF lower). *Caveat*: baseline already differed (p=0.030, diff 0.06 ≈ W4 diff), so the all-sites BOPP advantage partly tracks the baseline imbalance.
- Interdental sites: **W4 p=0.219 (NS)** — no WF advantage for interdental BOPP.

Gingival abrasion (GAS): **no difference between groups at any time point** (all sites W4 p=0.256; small lesions p=0.230; interdental area p=0.362). Both devices equally non-traumatic.

Safety: 6 adverse events; only 1 (localized interdental inflammation + sensitivity, IDB-related) attributed to a study product, resolved without withdrawal.

**Bottom line**: In moderate gingivitis over 4 weeks, the WF was more effective than the IDB for **marginal** gingival health (BOMP), with equal soft-tissue safety; the BOPP advantage was weaker and confounded by a baseline imbalance, and absent at interdental sites.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Industry sponsorship** (Water Pik employee as first author; WF supplied by sponsor) — risk of bias favouring the test device.
- Only **one IDB size** used in select fitting locations; patients realistically need multiple sizes for full interdental coverage — may underrepresent IDB efficacy.
- **No blinding of participants** to device; WF and interdental devices uncommon in this Dutch student sample (novelty/behaviour effects); sample **not representative** of the general population.
- **Baseline BOPP imbalance** between groups undercuts the all-sites BOPP between-group claim.
- **Plaque was not measured** (only bleeding). Short 4-week duration; long-term persistence and reaching the <10% bleeding health threshold not addressed.

## 6. Related Work

- Meta-review and Bayesian NMA ranking IDB and WF as the top interdental devices for reducing gingival inflammation (floss/woodsticks near zero probability).
- Prior 2-week pilot RCT also favoured WF over IDB; RCTs favouring WF over dental floss and over air-floss devices.

## 7. Glossary

- **WF — Water Flosser (oral irrigator)**: power-driven device delivering a pulsating stream of pressurized water for interdental cleaning.
- **IDB — Interdental Brush**: conical/cylindrical bristle brush sized to fit interproximal spaces.
- **BOPP — Bleeding On Pocket Probing**: bleeding elicited by probing to the pocket base; indicator of periodontal inflammation/stability.
- **BOMP — Bleeding On Marginal Probing**: bleeding elicited by running a probe along the marginal gingiva (~2 mm); measures early gingival-margin inflammation. Not interchangeable with BOPP.
- **GAS — Gingival Abrasion Score**: number of soft-tissue abrasion sites disclosed by staining solution; a soft-tissue safety metric.
