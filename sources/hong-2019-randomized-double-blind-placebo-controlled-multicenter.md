## One-line Summary
Multicenter double-blind RCT (n=100) showing that an 8-week fixed-dose combination of vitamin C / vitamin E / lysozyme / carbazochrome (CELC; brand IGATAN F) added to SRP reduces gingival index (GI) significantly versus placebo in chronic periodontitis, but does not change probing depth, attachment level, or plaque index.

## 1. Document Information
- Authors: Hong J-Y (Kyung Hee Univ), Lee J-S, Choi S-H (Yonsei Univ), Shin H-S, Park J-C (Dankook Univ), Shin S-I, Chung J-H (corresponding)
- Journal/Year: BMC Oral Health 2019;19:40
- DOI: 10.1186/s12903-019-0728-2
- Trial registry: KCT0001366 (CRIS, Republic of Korea, retrospectively registered 29 Jan 2015)
- Funding: Myung-In Pharm, Seoul (CELC marketed as IGATAN F)
- Study design: Three-center, double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallel RCT with delayed crossover for control group
- IRB: KHDIRB1409-2, Yonsei 2014-0074, Dankook H-1411/011/003

## 2. Key Contributions
- First multicenter RCT of fixed-dose CELC (vitamin C 150 mg + vitamin E 10 mg + lysozyme 30 mg + carbazochrome 2 mg) for chronic periodontitis as adjunct to scaling and root planing (SRP).
- Demonstrates that CELC reduces gingival inflammation (GI by Löe & Silness) significantly more than placebo at 4 weeks (p = 0.015) and that the GEE odds ratio for GI improvement is 2.46 (p = 0.022) after adjusting for age, gender, and visit.
- Provides safety data: no specific TEAEs reported during 8 weeks of administration.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Subjects: 100 adults (19–80 yr) with generalized chronic incipient-to-moderate periodontitis, ≥20 natural teeth, ≥1 site per quadrant with PD 4–6 mm, recruited Oct 2014–May 2015 at Kyung Hee (55), Yonsei (43), Dankook (14).
- Randomization: stratified by center, block randomization via SPSS 12.0, 1:1 allocation off-site.
- Run-in: 4-week SRP + OHI for all subjects. After baseline, test group received CELC for 8 weeks; control group received placebo (anhydrous dibasic calcium phosphate matching color/shape) for first 4 weeks then crossed to CELC for last 4 weeks.
- Outcomes (Ramfjord teeth #16/21/24/36/41/44):
  - Primary: ΔGI baseline–4 weeks
  - Secondary: GI at 8 wk, PI, PD, CAL, 100 mm VAS at 4 & 8 wk
- Statistics: Per-protocol set n = 93 (test 48, control 45). Repeated-measures ANOVA; Wilcoxon signed-rank within-group; Mann-Whitney U between-group; GEE with age/gender/visit/group covariates. α = 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Completion: 93/100 (test 48, control 45). No specific adverse events.
- GI (Δbaseline–4 wk): test −0.18 ± 0.33 vs control +0.01 ± 0.38, p = 0.015. Δbaseline–8 wk: test −0.24 ± 0.38, control −0.10 ± 0.40.
- GEE for GI improvement: OR 2.457 (test vs control), p = 0.022.
- PI: significant within-group reduction in test at 8 wk (p = 0.045) but no between-group difference (p = 0.138).
- PD: test group baseline 2.63 → 8 wk 2.51 mm, within-group p = 0.018; between-group p = 0.381.
- CAL: no significant changes either within or between groups.
- 100 mm VAS: test group decreased baseline 18.46 → 8 wk 4.94 mm; between-group p = 0.027 at 8 wk but not significant in GEE.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Exploratory sample size (n = 48–49 per arm) with no a priori power calculation for hard endpoints (PD, CAL).
- Short 8-week observation insufficient to capture attachment-level or radiographic bone changes.
- Run-in period of SRP applied to both arms means real-world incremental benefit beyond SRP-only standard of care may be modest in magnitude (GI difference 0.19 units).
- Mechanism not directly demonstrated (no biomarker measurements such as CRP, IL-1β, MMPs).
- Manufacturer-funded, but trial was registered (retrospectively).
- 4-week crossover design for placebo arm precludes between-group comparison beyond week 4 on placebo-vs-active basis.

## 6. Related Work
- CELC formulation as host-modulating periodontal adjunct, alongside subantimicrobial-dose doxycycline (SDD), NSAIDs, bisphosphonates (Reynolds 2014; Preshaw 2008).
- Vitamin C/E and periodontitis (Chapple 2009; Van der Velden 2011) — antioxidant pathway.
- Lysozyme antimicrobial mechanism (Carter 1980s literature).
- Carbazochrome — hemostatic agent reducing vascular hyperpermeability; limited periodontal evidence.
- Other Korean periodontal adjunct drug class: Insadol (titrated unsaponifiable Zea mays L. fraction) — sibling category of plant-based periodontal supplements; see Kim 2018 (in vitro), Kim 2024 (canine), Choi 2015 (critical appraisal).

## 7. Glossary
- CELC: fixed-dose combination of vitamin C 150 mg + vitamin E 10 mg + lysozyme 30 mg + carbazochrome 2 mg (brand IGATAN F, Myung-In Pharmaceutical, Korea)
- GI: Gingival Index (Löe & Silness 1963), 0–3 ordinal score for gingival inflammation
- PI: Plaque Index (Silness & Löe 1964)
- PD: Probing depth (mm)
- CAL: Clinical attachment level (mm)
- GEE: Generalized estimating equations — regression accounting for repeated measurements
- SRP: Scaling and root planing
- TEAE: Treatment-emergent adverse event
- VAS: Visual analog scale (0–100 mm) for patient-reported discomfort
- Ramfjord teeth: standardized 6-tooth subset (#16, #21, #24, #36, #41, #44) for periodontal index measurement
