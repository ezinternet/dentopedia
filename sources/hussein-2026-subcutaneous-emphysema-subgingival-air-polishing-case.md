## Why Ingested

Documents a serious safety complication — subcutaneous facial emphysema (SFE) — of subgingival glycine air-polishing, a necessary caveat to the largely favorable Guided Biofilm Therapy / air-polishing evidence. Refines [[periodontics/cyris-2024-guided-biofilm-therapy-versus-conventional]] by adding a device-/protocol-specific safety qualifier: SFE can occur even during supportive periodontal therapy at sites without BOP or suppuration, so air-polishing safety must not be generalised across devices and protocols.

## One-line Summary

Case report: glycine-based subgingival air polishing with a subgingival nozzle in a 5 mm pocket caused subcutaneous facial emphysema (periorbital + cheek swelling), requiring 3-day hospitalisation and antibiotics, with full recovery. (abstract-only — full text not retrieved)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Reports subcutaneous facial emphysema (SFE) following subgingival air polishing using an air-polishing system with a dedicated subgingival nozzle — a setting with limited prior reports.
- Demonstrates that SFE can arise during routine subgingival biofilm removal (glycine-based air-powder abrasion in a 5 mm pocket), not only with classic air-driven handpieces.
- Highlights that the complication occurred in a supportive periodontal therapy context and (per the authors' message) at sites lacking bleeding on probing (BOP) or suppuration.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Single-patient case report. Glycine-based air-powder abrasion delivered subgingivally into a 5 mm deep periodontal pocket for biofilm eradication.
- Outcome documented clinically: development of periorbital and right-cheek swelling, hospitalisation for surveillance, and antibiotic administration to control microbial spread.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Periorbital and right-cheek swelling developed after the subgingival air-polishing procedure.
- Patient hospitalised for surveillance; antibiotics given to control microbial spread.
- Discharged stable after 3 days with no further complications (full recovery).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single case — no incidence/risk estimate; abstract-only, so procedural detail (air pressure, nozzle depth, duration, device brand) not captured here.
- Authors caution that air-polishing safety should not be generalised across devices and protocols; device-specific instructions and case selection matter.
- Early recognition of swelling/crepitus is emphasised as essential for safe management.

## 6. Related Work

- Refines the favorable air-polishing / Guided Biofilm Therapy evidence (see [[periodontics/cyris-2024-guided-biofilm-therapy-versus-conventional]]) with a device-/protocol-specific safety caveat.

## 7. Glossary

- **Subcutaneous facial emphysema (SFE)**: Abnormal entry of pressurised air/gas into subcutaneous facial tissue planes, causing swelling and crepitus.
- **Air polishing**: Removal of biofilm/stain using a pressurised stream of air, water, and abrasive powder (here glycine-based) — subgingival application uses a dedicated subgingival nozzle.
- **Crepitus**: Crackling sensation on palpation caused by gas in soft tissue.
- **BOP**: Bleeding on probing.
- **Supportive periodontal therapy (SPT)**: Maintenance phase of periodontal care.
