## One-line Summary
초기 탐침 깊이(Initial Probing Depth, IPD)와 치석제거·치근활택술(Scaling and Root Planing, SRP) 반응 전향적 연구 (JIUFD 2016): 1,672포켓, 15명; IPD=3mm 제외 모든 군 PD 감소 유의; 다근치 AG는 IPD≥7mm에서만 유의.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Istanbul University Faculty of Dentistry (JIUFD) 2016
- **DOI**: 10.17096/jiufd.40993
- **Received**: 12 August 2016; **Accepted**: 22 September 2016
- **Study type**: Prospective clinical study

## 2. Key Contributions
- Quantified the relationship between initial probing depth (IPD) and clinical response to non-surgical periodontal treatment (SRP)
- Large within-study sample: 1,672 periodontal pockets (3mm ≤ IPD ≤ 9mm) in 15 patients
- Key finding: deeper pockets respond better to SRP in terms of probing depth (PD) reduction
- Single-rooted teeth: significant attachment gain (AG) at all IPD levels except IPD=3mm
- Multi-rooted teeth: AG only significant at IPD ≥ 7mm — important clinical threshold

## 3. Methodology
- **Design**: Prospective; 15 chronic periodontitis patients; 1,672 periodontal pockets
- **IPD range**: 3–9 mm
- **Treatment**: SRP in 2 sessions; OHI included
- **Measurements**: PD, clinical attachment level (CAL), gingival recession (GR) at baseline and 8 weeks post-treatment
- **Groups**: By IPD (3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9 mm) and root number (single- vs multi-rooted)

## 4. Key Results
| Group | PD Reduction | AG | GR Increase |
|---|---|---|---|
| All single-rooted (IPD 4–9mm) | Significant (p<0.001) | Significant | Significant |
| Single-rooted IPD=3mm | Significant | Not significant | Significant |
| Multi-rooted IPD ≥7mm | Significant (p<0.05) | Significant | Significant |
| Multi-rooted IPD <7mm | Significant | Not significant | Significant |

- Positive correlation between IPD and PD reduction in single-rooted teeth (p<0.001)
- No correlation between IPD and AG in multi-rooted teeth (p>0.05)

## 5. Limitations
- Small patient sample (n=15); large pocket sample offsets but patient-level clustering not addressed
- 8-week outcome only; no long-term follow-up
- Single center; chronic periodontitis only (not aggressive)
