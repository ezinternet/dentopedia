## One-line Summary
Cross-sectional CBCT study (60 East Asian subjects, 30 Malay + 30 Chinese) characterizing the microanatomy of the maxillary permanent first molar socket and its relationship to the maxillary sinus floor; 95% had sinus floor extending anterior to first molar, 72% had sinus dipping between roots, ~50% had root apices intruding into the sinus, and mean socket height was only ~7 mm — implying that implants longer than 7 mm risk sinus perforation without adjunct procedures.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Surgical and Radiologic Anatomy 2015;37(10):1099–1108
- **Pages**: 1099–1108 (10 pages)
- **Institution**: Department of Oral Maxillofacial Surgery / Oral Diagnostics, University of Malaya, Kuala Lumpur
- **Study type**: Cross-sectional retrospective CBCT anatomy study

## 2. Key Contributions
- Quantitative CBCT mapping of the **maxillary first molar socket dimensions** in an East Asian population (where prior data was European/American-skewed).
- Reports the prevalence of **sinus pneumatization patterns** relative to first molar roots: anterior extension (95%), inter-radicular dipping (72%), and root-tip intrusion (~50%).
- Provides reference socket dimensions (width 11.42 mm, length 7.70 mm, height ~6.5–6.9 mm) usable for **immediate/early implant planning** and **socket preservation** decisions.
- Argues clinically that any implant >7 mm in a fresh first-molar socket has a fair probability of sinus perforation without adjunctive sinus lift or osseodensification.
- Mandates pre-extraction CBCT to avoid iatrogenic oroantral communication.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 60 CBCT scans (30 Malay, 30 Chinese; M:F roughly balanced) selected from >300 archived scans at the University of Malaya.
- Inclusion: presence of intact maxillary first permanent molar, no periapical pathology, no prior surgery in region.
- For each first molar site, the following were measured on coronal and sagittal CBCT reconstructions:
  - Socket mesiodistal width, buccolingual length.
  - Socket height (coronal and sagittal planes) from alveolar crest to sinus floor.
  - Presence and degree of inter-radicular sinus dipping.
  - Presence of root-tip protrusion into the sinus.
- Two examiners; inter-rater agreement assessed.
- Descriptive statistics; ethnic group comparisons.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Mean socket width**: 11.42 ± 0.86 mm.
- **Mean socket length (buccolingual)**: 7.70 ± 0.56 mm.
- **Mean socket height — coronal plane**: 6.48 ± 3.77 mm.
- **Mean socket height — sagittal plane**: 6.85 ± 3.67 mm.
- **Sinus floor anterior to first molar**: 95% of subjects.
- **Sinus floor dipping between roots**: 72%.
- **Inter-radicular bone present**: 75%.
- **Root-apex intrusion into sinus floor**: ~50%.
- Clinical implication: any implant ≥7 mm risks sinus perforation in this population without adjunct (sinus lift, OD).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Retrospective CBCT sample of two East Asian ethnicities only — generalizability to other populations limited.
- No correlation with age, sex, or dental status (e.g., periodontal bone level).
- Static measurement — does not predict post-extraction socket healing trajectory.
- Did not assess buccal plate thickness — a known critical determinant of immediate implant esthetics.

## 7. Glossary
- **Inter-radicular bone**: bone between the roots of a multi-rooted tooth; key for immediate molar implant primary stability.
- **Sinus pneumatization**: expansion of the maxillary sinus into the alveolar process after tooth loss.
- **Oroantral communication (OAC)**: pathological connection between oral cavity and maxillary sinus, often iatrogenic after molar extraction.
- **Sagittal/Coronal plane**: standard anatomical reference planes used in CBCT 3D reconstructions.
- **Socket height**: vertical distance from alveolar crest to sinus floor; primary determinant of usable implant length.
