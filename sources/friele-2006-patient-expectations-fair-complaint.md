## Why Ingested
The keystone of the expectations axis -- it answers 'what do complainants actually want?' using justice theory. It explains why purely defensive or apology-only responses fail, and sets the standard the response-axis papers ([[complaint-management/12913_2021_Article_6733]], [[complaint-management/pone.0325185]]) are measured against.

## One-line Summary
Survey of 424 hospital complainants showing the dominant goal is preventing recurrence; they prioritise impartial procedure, explanation over apology, and rarely seek money.

## 1. Document Information
- BMC Health Services Research 2006;6:106. Open access.
- NIVEL, Netherlands Institute for Health Services Research.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Frames complaint handling through justice theory: fair procedure, fair communication, fair outcome.
- Empirically ranks complainant priorities: recurrence prevention > impartial procedure > explanation > apology > restoring relationship > money.
- Shows explanation (65%) outranks apology (41%) as 'most important'.
- Documents that only 7% want financial compensation -- contradicting the litigation-first assumption.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Cross-sectional survey of 424 patients (75% response) who had lodged a complaint with complaint committees at 74 Dutch hospitals, completed at the *start* of the procedure. Items derived from justice theory measuring expectations of fair procedure, communication, and outcome.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
Predominant reason to complain: prevent recurrence. Impartial committee position most important to 87%; respectful treatment expected. Desired outcome = change in hospital performance (79%). Disclosure/admission of mistake expected. Explanation most important to 65% vs apology 41%. Restoring doctor-patient relationship important to only 32%; financial compensation wanted by only 7%.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
Dutch hospital complaint-committee population (formal complainants) -- may not represent informal or non-escalated complaints, or dental private-practice settings. Self-reported expectations at intake, not satisfaction with eventual outcome.

## 6. Related Work
Foundational for Friele's later expectation-gap survey (2015). Conceptual partner to HCAT's 'listening/communication/respect' relational domain.

## 7. Glossary
- **Justice theory**: framework distinguishing procedural, interactional, and distributive fairness.
- **Procedural fairness**: perceived fairness of the process (e.g., impartiality).
- **Distributive (outcome) fairness**: perceived fairness of the result.
