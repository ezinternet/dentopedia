## Why Ingested

사용자 요청 — 임플란트 유지관리(supportive peri-implant therapy, SPiT) 프로토콜 논문 감시·인제스트. 기존 위키는 주위염 *치료*(decontamination, GBR, PDT, minocycline) 페이지는 풍부하나 유지관리 *프로토콜*(recall 주기·자가관리) 자체를 다룬 1차 근거가 부족했음. 본 SR(25편, RCT 9편)은 SPiT 효과의 최신 종합으로, [[implants/peri-implantitis/jepsen-2015-primary-prevention-periimplantitis-managing-mucositis]]의 1차 예방 원칙을 정량적 근거로 보강하고 [[implants/peri-implantitis/mauriello-2026-peri-implant-mucositis-adjunctive-narrative-review]]의 mucositis 보조요법 논의를 SPiT recall 맥락으로 확장.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (25 studies: 9 RCTs, 13 cohorts, 3 case-control; narrative synthesis) finding that individualized, risk-based supportive peri-implant therapy (SPiT) recall intervals consistently improve probing depth and bleeding on probing versus fixed-interval or no maintenance — though high heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(25편: RCT 9·코호트 13·환자대조 3, 정성 종합) — 환자별 위험도 기반(risk-based) 개별화 SPiT recall 주기가 고정 주기나 무관리 대비 탐침깊이·탐침시출혈을 일관되게 개선했으나, 높은 이질성으로 메타분석은 불가.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Synthesizes the effectiveness of *professionally delivered* SPiT across the full disease spectrum (peri-implant mucositis + peri-implantitis), with primary focus on radiographic bone loss, clinical parameters (PD, BoP), and patient-reported outcomes (satisfaction scale 1–10).
- Core finding: **individualized risk-based recall** > standard fixed-interval maintenance > no maintenance, for all key clinical outcomes and implant longevity.
- Provides the largest and most up-to-date evidence summary for SPiT (search through April 30, 2025), addressing explicit gaps in prior reviews: (1) incomplete SPiT stratification by individualized risk-based intervals, (2) limited analysis of systemic comorbidities, (3) insufficient assessment of adjunctive therapies.
- Identifies the field's main weakness: high clinical/methodological heterogeneity, inconsistent documentation of systemic comorbidities, variable adjunctive therapies — limiting definitive conclusions and precluding meta-analysis.
- Antimicrobial photodynamic therapy (aPDT) is singled out as the only adjunct with repeated, independent evidence of meaningful additional probing-depth reduction (≈0.6–0.8 mm) beyond mechanical debridement.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search databases**: MEDLINE, Embase, Cochrane Library, Web of Science; search through 2025-04-30.
- **Eligibility**: RCTs, cohort studies, and case-control studies assessing professionally delivered SPiT for peri-implant mucositis or peri-implantitis.
- **Exclusion at full-text (95 articles)**: wrong study design (40), insufficient quantitative data (30), nonrelevant population or intervention (25).
- **Risk of bias tools**: Cochrane ROB 2.0 for RCTs; Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS, 0–9) for observational studies.
- **Synthesis**: Narrative only — formal meta-analysis was judged inappropriate due to high clinical and methodological heterogeneity (variable SPiT interventions, patient populations, adjunctive therapies, follow-up durations).
- **Included: 25 studies** — 9 RCTs, 13 prospective or retrospective cohort studies, 3 case-control studies.
- Sample sizes: 60–250 participants per study; follow-up: 3 months to 7 years.
- **SPiT modalities**: 19 studies used mechanical-only SPiT; 6 incorporated adjuncts at each recall visit (tetracycline fibers, air-polishing, laser decontamination, chlorhexidine rinse, antimicrobial ointment, powder-abrasive device).
- **Key geographic spread**: USA, Germany, Spain, Italy, Brazil, South Korea, Sweden, Switzerland, Singapore, Iran, Egypt.
- "No treatment" defined as no scheduled professional maintenance; "standard care" defined as fixed 6- or 12-month prophylaxis lacking risk stratification.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Clinical outcomes (Table 3 data)

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Jepsen 2015 | 3.4 (±0.5) | 4.2 (±0.5) | 14 | 28 | NR | 3 |
| Monje 2015 (risk-based) | 3.2 (±0.4) | 4.8 (±0.5) | 10 | 30 | NR | 3 |
| Heitz-Mayfield & Salvi 2016 | 3.5 (±0.5) | 5.0 (±0.6) | 15 | 35 | NR | 5 |
| Frisch 2019 (risk-based) | 3.0 (±0.4) | 4.5 (±0.5) | 12 | 32 | 1.02 | 4 |
| Costa 2019 | 3.1 (±0.4) | 4.0 (±0.6) | 10 | 20 | ~0.8 | 4 |
| Roccuzzo 2018 | 4.0 (±0.6) | 5.5 (±0.7) | 20 | 40 | ~0.7–1.1 | 10 |
| Lin 2019 (SPiT + aPDT) | 2.8 (±0.3) | 3.6 (±0.4) | 8 | 20 | NR | 2 |
| Ravidà 2020 (risk-based) | 3.2 (±0.4) | 4.5 (±0.5) | 12 | 30 | NR | 5 |

- **Probing depth reduction**: Mean additional PD reduction across SPiT vs control ranged from 1.0 to 1.5 mm; largest single-study gain was −1.5 mm (Heitz-Mayfield & Salvi, 5-year RCT).
- **Bleeding on probing**: 11 trials recorded BoP; SPiT groups showed relative decreases of 10–25 percentage points.
- **Marginal bone loss**: 7 cohort studies quantified bone loss — risk-based SPiT limited bone loss to ≤1 mm over ≥24 months; controls experienced ~1.5 mm.
- **Disease incidence/progression**: Long-term cohorts (≥36 months) reported 40–70% lower incidence of peri-implantitis or implant loss among SPiT-adherent patients.
- **Adjunctive therapies**: aPDT or growth-factor gels provided modest additional PD gains (≤0.8 mm) in 3 RCTs; 2 laser trials failed to outperform debridement alone.
- **Patient satisfaction**: Reported on a 1–10 scale in most studies; SPiT groups scored 8.0–9.5, consistently higher than controls.

### Systemic factors (Table 2 data)
- Only 5 of 25 studies (20%) explicitly quantified smokers; only 2 (8%) documented diabetes prevalence.
- Most trials excluded uncontrolled systemic illnesses.
- Smokers on quarterly risk-adjusted SPiT had fewer peri-implantitis recurrences than smokers receiving no maintenance.
- One prospective cohort including well-controlled type 2 diabetes patients recorded ~30% less marginal bone loss with risk-based SPiT vs fixed schedules.

### Risk of bias
- 7 of 9 RCTs rated low risk of bias across all domains; 2 RCTs flagged for "some concerns" (incomplete blinding of outcome assessors).
- NOS scores: 7–9 of 9 for cohort/case-control studies → overall low risk.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative synthesis only — no pooled effect estimates or formal statistical comparisons possible due to heterogeneity.
- Only 20% of studies explicitly quantified smokers; only 8% documented diabetes — limits evidence-based risk stratification.
- No included study assessed cardiovascular disease, osteoporosis, or immunosuppression — SPiT benefit in these populations is unknown.
- None of the included interventions involved simultaneous bone-graft reconstruction; most studies limited to implants supporting fixed restorations.
- Variable definitions of "risk-based SPiT" and "standard care" across studies.
- Calls for standardized reporting of systemic comorbidities, risk stratification criteria, adjunctive therapies, and long-term outcomes.
- Larger trials needed before broad recommendations for laser therapy or growth-factor gels can be made.

## 7. Glossary

- **SPiT** — Supportive Peri-implant Therapy: structured professional maintenance after implant placement or active peri-implant disease therapy.
- **Risk-based recall** — recall interval set individually by patient risk profile (periodontitis history, smoking, systemic health) rather than a fixed calendar.
- **BoP / PD** — Bleeding on Probing / Probing Depth.
- **aPDT** — Antimicrobial Photodynamic Therapy: adjunctive laser-activated photosensitizer treatment used during SPiT to reduce microbial load.
- **ROB 2.0** — Cochrane Risk of Bias 2.0 tool for RCTs.
- **NOS** — Newcastle-Ottawa Scale; 0–9 quality score for observational studies.
- **Narrative synthesis** — qualitative summary of results across studies without statistical pooling; used when meta-analysis is precluded by heterogeneity.
