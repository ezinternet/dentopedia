## Why Ingested

[[wiki/implants/versah-protocols/rittipakorn-2025-clockwise-osseodensification-primary-stability-cadaveric]]의 clockwise-OD cadaveric 실험은 저밀도골에서 초기 안정성을 끌어올리는 osseodensification(OD)을 평가하지만, 그 임상적 필요성을 떠받치는 "왜 저밀도골이 문제인가"의 기초 — D1–D4 골질 분류, 골밀도가 BIC/stress distribution/ISQ에 미치는 영향, 그리고 implant 재료·macro-design 선택 원칙 — 을 위키가 충분히 담고 있지 않았다. 본 narrative review (Khaohoen 2023, 119편 종합)는 D3–D4 soft bone에서 OD 같은 primary-stability 술식이 왜 필요한지를 biomaterial·implant-design·surgical-technique 관점에서 통합 설명해, OD 페이지에 골밀도 맥락(context)을 제공한다.

## One-line Summary

Narrative review (119 articles) concluding that in low-density (D3–D4) bone, implant material (Ti remains gold standard; zirconia/PEEK as alternatives), macro/microgeometry (conical, wider, longer, reverse-buttress self-tapping, small pitch, deep thread), and surgical technique (osseodensification/under-drilling) jointly determine primary stability and success.

## 한줄요약

내러티브 리뷰 (119편)로, 저밀도골(D3–D4)에서는 임플란트 재료(티타늄이 여전히 표준, 지르코니아·PEEK는 대안), 매크로·마이크로 형태(원추형·넓은 직경·긴 길이·역버트레스 self-tapping·좁은 pitch·깊은 thread), 수술 술식(골밀도화·under-drilling)이 함께 초기 안정성과 성공률을 좌우한다고 결론.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Synthesizes how **implant material, implant design, and surgical technique** interact with **bone density** from biomechanical and biological standpoints, to guide implant selection per bone state.
- Frames bone quality with **Misch's D1–D4 classification** and Hounsfield-unit ranges; collapses to high (D1–D2) vs low (D3–D4) for analysis.
- Provides a consolidated **low-density-bone optimization guideline**: conical macrogeometry, larger diameter, longer length, reverse-buttress self-tapping threads, small pitch, deep thread depth.
- Compares **titanium (gold standard), zirconia (esthetic/metal-free alternative), and PEEK (low-modulus, anti-stress-shielding alternative)** on mechanical and biological properties.
- Reviews clinical survival data stratified by bone density (D-I ~97.6% → D-IV ~88.8%) and confirms low bone quality consistently yields higher marginal bone loss (MBL).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **PICO question**: How do implant material, design, and surgical technique affect bone density in biomechanical and biological aspects?
- **P** edentulous area treated with implant surgery; **I** implant materials (titanium, zirconia, other); **C** bone densities D1 (hard) / D2–D3 (medium) / D4 (soft); **O** stress concentration, BIC, push-in/removal torque, fracture resistance, low-temperature degradation (LTD), MBL, success/survival rates; **S** RCT/non-RCT, case reports, animal, in vitro, FEA, reviews.
- Electronic search of PubMed/Medline, Scopus, Embase + gray literature, up to 2023, 10-year limit, English. 529 articles found → **119 included** after deduplication/filtering; **7 studies** carried bone-density clinical data. Reference management with EndNote v9; data tabulated in Excel by study design, bone type/density, material type, and outcomes.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Bone density & biomechanics**: D4 bone shows the highest stress (concentrated at the cervical/crestal implant socket); lower density and thinner cortical bone increase bone strain and lower implant stability, raising failure risk. Stress shielding arises from elastic-modulus mismatch between implant and bone.
- **Stability assessment**: insertion torque (IT) 30–50 Ncm acceptable; RFA → ISQ, with ISQ below ~70–75 flagged as higher failure risk; removal torque (destructive, non-clinical). Good osseointegration ~60% BIC (Albrektsson).
- **Materials (Table 5 functional properties)**: cpTi(IV) E≈104 GPa; Ti-6Al-4V ≈110 GPa (strongest Ti); Ti-Zr (Roxolid) ≈96 GPa, tensile ~953 MPa; 3Y-zirconia E≈210 GPa, flexural 900–1200 MPa; ATZ E≈220 GPa, 1800–2400 MPa; PEEK E≈3–4 GPa (bone-like), CFR-PEEK ≈18 GPa, GFR-PEEK ≈12 GPa. Titanium remains the gold standard; PEEK lowers stress shielding but has inferior osseointegration (lower BIC, less osteoblast differentiation).
- **Low-density-bone geometry (Table 6)**: conical shape, wider diameter (≥4 mm), longer length (9–11 mm), reverse-buttress/self-tapping threads, pitch lower than standard, thread depth higher than standard. Immediate placement: conical, ≥4 mm diameter, ≥11 mm, self-tapping. Hybrid (apical conical + crestal cylindrical) gives highest primary stability. Zirconia: diameter >3.25 mm advised to accommodate deep thread; sharp/deep-thread small-diameter zirconia not advisable (stress concentration).
- **Surgical technique for soft bone (D3–D4)**: undersized drilling, **osseodensification**, osteotome, piezoelectric (speeds healing but no primary-stability gain), magnetodynamic (higher IT/ISQ/RT vs conventional). OD/under-preparation/expander improved primary stability vs conventional drilling (lower ISQ).
- **Clinical survival**: zirconia 1-piece ~95% survival, 2-piece ~94% (2-piece not recommended due to limited data); narrow-diameter Ti-Zr ~>95% survival, MBL <1 mm. Survival by density: D-I 97.6%, D-II 96.2%, D-III 96.5%, D-IV 88.8%; treated (rough) surfaces survive better than machined in low-density bone (97.1%). Across the 7 included bone-density studies (2012–2022), low bone quality consistently showed higher MBL (e.g., Held 2013 D-IV ~1.46 mm vs ~0.2–0.7 mm in denser bone).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative (non-systematic) synthesis — no quantitative pooling, susceptible to selection bias; only 7 studies with explicit Misch bone-density data, mostly short follow-up (3–12 months).
- Sparse clinical evidence for zirconia and PEEK in defined low-density bone (only one zirconia study, n=18); PEEK data largely in-vitro/short-term; two-piece zirconia lacks long-term data.
- Authors call for further study to identify the optimal implant material for each bone state in clinical settings.

## 6. Related Work

- Misch bone classification (D1–D4); Albrektsson osseointegration/BIC and surface-roughness categories; Frost's mechanostat (microstrain windows); consensus MBL criteria (<1.5 mm first year, 0.2 mm/year thereafter).
- Reinforces the bone-density rationale behind primary-stability surgical techniques such as osseodensification: [[wiki/implants/versah-protocols/rittipakorn-2025-clockwise-osseodensification-primary-stability-cadaveric]].

## 7. Glossary

- **D1–D4**: Misch bone-quality classes from dense cortical (D1) to fine trabecular (D4).
- **BIC**: bone–implant contact (%); ~60% indicates good integration.
- **ISQ / RFA**: implant stability quotient from resonance frequency analysis; below ~70–75 = higher failure risk.
- **IT / RTQ**: insertion torque (30–50 Ncm acceptable) / removal torque (destructive).
- **Stress shielding**: under-loading of bone from implant–bone modulus mismatch → peri-implant bone loss.
- **OD (osseodensification)**: drilling technique that compacts/preserves bone to raise torque and primary stability in low-density bone.
- **LTD**: low-temperature degradation (zirconia aging, tetragonal→monoclinic).
- **PEEK**: polyetheretherketone, low-modulus polymer implant alternative.
