## One-line Summary
Periodontology 2000 narrative review (Jung et al., 2018): in the esthetic zone, after extraction the clinician must choose among spontaneous socket healing, immediate implant placement, or alveolar ridge preservation (ARP); three biologically distinct ARP windows (6–8 weeks soft-tissue; 4–6 months hard- and soft-tissue; >6 months hard-tissue) are described, with the choice driven by buccal bone plate height, soft-tissue phenotype, and timing strategy for implant placement.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Frames the postextraction esthetic-zone decision around four key clinical determinants: (i) chosen timing for implant placement, (ii) quality/quantity of peri-extraction soft tissue, (iii) remaining height of the buccal bone plate, and (iv) anticipated implant survival/success.
- Synthesizes published evidence into **three distinct ARP timing windows**, each targeted at preserving different tissue compartments before implant placement.
- Clarifies the rationale for soft-tissue-only preservation when the goal is delayed (Type 2/3) implant placement and the buccal plate is already compromised.
- Differentiates ARP indications from those of immediate implant placement (Type 1) in the esthetic zone.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative literature review covering experimental and clinical studies of post-extraction ridge alterations and ARP interventions (socket grafting, socket sealing, soft-tissue grafting).
- No formal systematic search, PRISMA flow, or quantitative meta-analysis described; selection is expert-driven.
- Organized by biological healing phase and corresponding clinical objective.
- Discusses materials: deproteinized bovine bone mineral (DBBM), allografts, collagen membranes, free gingival/connective tissue grafts, soft-tissue punch grafts.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Postextraction horizontal ridge reduction reported in the literature: predominantly buccal, ~50% of width within 12 months, mostly in the first 3 months (consistent with Schropp 2003, Araújo 2005).
- Three ARP windows defined (qualitative synthesis; no pooled CIs):
  - **Window A — soft-tissue preservation**: 6–8 weeks of healing post-extraction. Goal: improve soft-tissue thickness/keratinization before Type 2 implant placement.
  - **Window B — hard + soft tissue preservation**: 4–6 months. Goal: limit hard-tissue resorption AND optimize soft tissue contour. Indicated when buccal plate is intact or only partially compromised.
  - **Window C — hard-tissue preservation**: >6 months. Goal: full bone re-formation, used when staged augmentation or simultaneous GBR is anticipated.
- (Precise CIs/n not reported as primary outcomes; this is a narrative review.)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative format limits formal quality grading and pooled effect estimates.
- Most recommendations are expert opinion supported by heterogeneous case series and small RCTs.
- Buccal plate thickness measurement remains operator-dependent and inconsistently reported across cited studies.
- Long-term peri-implant tissue stability after each ARP window is not directly compared in head-to-head trials.

## 6. Related Work
- Araújo & Lindhe 2005 — dimensional ridge alterations after extraction in dog model.
- Schropp 2003 — clinical bone changes 12 months post-extraction.
- Chappuis 2017 — buccal plate phenotype and ridge alterations in esthetic sites.
- Avila-Ortiz 2014, 2019 — ARP meta-analyses informing magnitude of effect.
- Cardaropoli 2003 — modeling/remodeling of extraction sockets.

## 7. Glossary
- **ARP (Alveolar Ridge Preservation)**: any surgical intervention at extraction (graft, membrane, soft-tissue seal) aimed at reducing post-extraction ridge dimensional change.
- **Esthetic zone**: maxillary anterior region where labial gingival contour and crown emergence visibly determine outcome.
- **Buccal bone plate**: the thin (often <1 mm) facial cortical wall of the alveolus; bundle bone resorbs after extraction.
- **Type 1/2/3 implant placement (ITI)**: immediate / early (4–8 wk soft) / early with bone healing (12–16 wk) / late placement timing schemes.
- **DBBM**: deproteinized bovine bone mineral (e.g., Bio-Oss); slow-resorbing xenograft used in ARP/GBR.
