## Why Ingested

Sealant 감시 인제스트 — 치면열구전색 (Pit-and-Fissure Sealant, PFS) vs 불소바니시 (Fluoride Varnish, FV) 직접비교 SR+MA 근거. 동일한 head-to-head RCT 질문을 다룬 [[caries/chestnutt-2017-fissure-seal-fluoride-varnish-trial]]를 재확인·집계하고(둘 다 "2년 시점 유의차 없음"), 비용·시술 용이성 논거에서 [[caries/kumar-2026-fluoride-varnish-caries-prevention-cost-effectiveness-sr-ma]]와 짝을 이룬다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (4 RCTs, 1249 children, all 24-month follow-up): pit-and-fissure resin sealant and fluoride varnish show **no significant difference** in caries prevention of first permanent molars (caries-increment RR 0.65, 95% CI 0.31-1.35, p=0.26, I²=89%; DMFS MD 0.09, 95% CI -0.03 to 0.21), so FV is favored as cheaper/easier.

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (RCT 4편, 어린이 1249명, 모두 24개월 추적): 제1대구치 우식예방에서 치면열구전색(레진계)과 불소바니시는 **유의한 차이 없음**(우식증가 RR 0.65, 95% CI 0.31-1.35, p=0.26, I²=89%; DMFS MD 0.09, 95% CI -0.03~0.21) → 더 싸고 시술이 쉬운 불소바니시를 권장.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Pit and Fissure Sealant versus Fluoride Varnish for the Prevention of Dental Caries in School Children: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis
- **Authors**: Rashed TA (TR), Alsadhan AAA (AA), et al.
- **Journal**: International Journal of Clinical Practice, 2022
- **DOI**: 10.1155/2022/8635254 (PMID 36263239 · PMCID PMC9553663)
- **Type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (4 RCTs)
- **Registration**: PROSPERO CRD42022146807 (7 July 2022); PRISMA-reported

## 2. Key Contributions

- Head-to-head SR+MA of **resin-based pit-and-fissure sealant (PFS/RBS) vs fluoride varnish (FV)** for caries prevention in **first permanent molars (FPM)** of schoolchildren.
- Deliberately **narrow inclusion** to resolve conflict among prior MAs: only RCTs, ≥24-month follow-up, RBS only (GIC/glass-ionomer sealant comparisons **excluded** due to low GIC retention), split-mouth excluded (FV carryover contaminates the contralateral half).
- Bottom line: **no statistically significant difference**; recommends FV on cost/ease-of-application grounds.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **PICO**: schoolchildren 6-12 yr with sound occlusal FPM; PFS (resin-based) vs FV; outcome = caries incidence on FPM surfaces and DMFS increment.
- **Databases**: Embase, Google Scholar, Cochrane CENTRAL, MEDLINE via Ovid; Jan 1980-May 2022.
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane Collaboration tool, 7 domains.
- **Statistics**: RevMan 5.4; dichotomous → RR + 95% CI; continuous → weighted mean difference (MD) + 95% CI; heterogeneity I²; random-effects model when I² > 50%.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Screening**: 231 candidates → 140 duplicates removed → 64 excluded on title/abstract → 27 eligibility-assessed → **4 RCTs included** (published 1996-2014), **1249 subjects** aged 6-8.
- **Primary — caries incidence in FPM**: 3 trials, **2,622 FPMs**, all 24-month follow-up. Initial study favored PFS; more recent study no difference. Pooled **RR 0.65; 95% CI 0.31-1.35; p=0.26; I²=89%** → not significant, very high heterogeneity.
- **Secondary — DMFS increment**: 2 studies. Bravo et al. favored PFS (MD -0.64; 95% CI -1.07 to -0.21); Salem reported higher occlusal caries increment with PFS. Pooled (dominated by large Bravo n): **MD 0.09; 95% CI -0.03 to 0.21** (not significant); heterogeneity Chi²=11.69, **I²=91%**.
- **Risk of bias**: selective reporting low in all; half high-risk and half unclear for blinding of participants/personnel; Bravo and Salem did not describe randomization method.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Very high heterogeneity (I² 89-91%) despite strict criteria — interpret with caution; partly attributed to study quality.
- Most included studies did not control confounders (fluoride exposure, socioeconomic status, parental education).
- Only **4 studies, all 2-year follow-up** → long-term efficacy not established. Calls for high-quality RCTs with longer follow-up and trials in low-/middle-income populations.

## 6. Related Work

- Consistent with Li et al. (PFS or FV, no significant difference in caries incidence/DMFS at 2-3 yr).
- Contrasts prior MAs: Feifei et al. (mixed follow-up times + GIC-vs-FV), Wright et al. 2016 (included PFS-vs-water-fluoridation, mixed 5-yr/2-yr without subgroups, found PFS > FV); the cited Cochrane-style update concluded no certainty either direction.

## 7. Glossary

- **PFS** — Pit-and-Fissure Sealant (치면열구전색); here resin-based (RBS).
- **FV** — Fluoride Varnish (불소바니시); e.g., Duraphat.
- **FPM** — First Permanent Molar (제1대구치).
- **DMFS** — Decayed, Missing, Filled Surfaces (우식·상실·충전 치면 지수).
- **RR / MD** — Risk Ratio / Mean Difference.
- **GIC** — Glass Ionomer Cement; excluded as a sealant comparator due to low retention.
- **RBS** — Resin-Based Sealant.
