## Why Ingested

The long-term causal role of keratinized mucosa (KM) absence on peri-implant disease risk was debated but lacked >10-year prospective data; this 20-year cohort directly addresses that gap and extends the same group's earlier 10-year report. It reinforces and provides 20-year follow-up evidence for [[wiki/overviews/peri-implant-soft-tissue-dehiscence-prevention]], which synthesizes KM augmentation outcomes.

## One-line Summary

20-year prospective cohort (n=64, posterior mandible SLA tissue-level implants) shows implants lacking keratinized mucosa had 25% peri-implantitis rate vs. 4.2% with keratinized tissue, and 100% soft-tissue dehiscence (REC ≥1 mm) rate, with free gingival grafting conferring a significant protective effect.

## 한줄요약

20년 전향적 코호트(n=64, 하악 구치부 조직수준 임플란트)에서 각화점막 없는 임플란트는 각화점막 있는 임플란트 대비 임플란트주위염 발생률이 25% 대 4.2%로 유의하게 높았으며, 연조직 열개 발생률이 100%에 달해 유리치은이식(FGG)의 예방 효과가 입증되었다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of Periodontal Research, 2025; 60:1212–1221
- **DOI**: 10.1111/jre.70018
- **Study design**: 20-year prospective cohort study (STROBE compliant)
- **Setting**: Specialist periodontal private practice, Torino, Italy
- **Ethics**: Institutional Ethics Committee Nr. 168/2021; Helsinki Declaration 2018
- **Funding**: Geistlich-Stucki Foundation (Switzerland; J-C.I. and A.St.)
- **Implant system**: Straumann SLA tissue-level implants (sandblasted and acid-etched surface)
- **Original enrollment period**: December 1998 – December 2002

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First study to document 20-year clinical and radiographic outcomes of tissue-level implants stratified by keratinized mucosa (KM) status at placement.
2. Demonstrates that the absence of keratinized tissue (KT) is associated with significantly greater marginal bone loss, bleeding on probing, soft-tissue recession, peri-implant mucositis, and peri-implantitis at 20 years.
3. Provides evidence that free gingival grafting (FGG) applied during supportive care (years 1–10) confers a durable protective effect equivalent to having KT at placement, sustained to 20 years.
4. Shows 100% soft-tissue dehiscence (REC ≥1 mm) in AM (alveolar mucosa, no KT) group versus 35.4% in KT/AM+FGG group (OR 81.6, 95% CI 8.45–785.9, p<0.001).
5. Establishes OR 6.67 (95% CI 1.09–40.9, p=0.041) for peri-implantitis in AM vs. KT/AM+FGG implants.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Population**: Original cohort of 128 patients (128 implants). At 10-year follow-up, 98 attended; at 20-year follow-up, 64 patients (64 implants) attended (35% attrition).

**Groups** (defined at baseline and maintained through follow-up):
- **KT** (n=42): implants placed in the presence of keratinized tissue
- **AM** (n=16): implants placed in the absence of keratinized tissue, no subsequent grafting
- **AM+FGG** (n=6): implants initially placed in alveolar mucosa; received FGG during years 1–10 of SPC due to poor plaque control and self-reported soreness

**Inclusion criteria**: Single posterior mandible implant (premolar/molar) as distal element; implants in pristine bone (no concomitant GBR).

**Exclusion criteria**: Mucosal diseases, heavy alcohol/drug use, pregnancy, uncontrolled metabolic disorders, severe chronic/aggressive periodontitis (Armitage 1999), bone-regenerated sites.

**Prosthesis**: Cemented metal-ceramic fixed restorations after 6–10 weeks non-submerged healing. All patients enrolled in individualized SPC (supra-marginal instrumentation, oral hygiene reinforcement, treatment of peri-implant diseases as needed).

**KM assessment**: Visual assessment at T0 (baseline) at mid-buccal aspect by senior investigator (M. Roccuzzo). Binary: present (KT) or absent (AM). No systematic quantitative KM width measurement (width data were not reported as a categorical <2 mm vs. ≥2 mm metric — rather, absence vs. presence was the exposure).

**Outcome measures**:
- Probing pocket depth (PPD, mm; 4 sites)
- Bleeding on probing (BOP, %)
- Plaque index at implant level (PII, %)
- Soft-tissue recession (REC, mm; Castroviejo caliper, nearest 0.5 mm)
- Marginal bone loss (MBL, mm; periapical radiographs, ImageJ, long-cone technique; κ=0.906)
- Peri-implant health status: peri-implant health (PH), peri-implant mucositis (PM), peri-implantitis (PI) — 2018 Case Definitions (Renvert et al. 2018)
- Soft-tissue dehiscence: defined as REC ≥1 mm

**Statistics**: STATA BE v17.1; Shapiro-Wilk for normality; non-parametric: Chi², Fisher's exact, Mann-Whitney, Kruskal-Wallis; Spearman correlations; uni- and multivariable binary logistic regression (OR with 95% CI).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Cohort attrition**: 128 (baseline) → 98 (10-year) → 64 (20-year); 34 lost between 10–20 years (11 deceased, 23 refused). No implants lost between 10 and 20 years (100% implant survival in those followed).

**Group composition at 20 years**: KT=42, AM=16, AM+FGG=6.

**Peri-implant health status at 20 years**:

| Group | PH | PM | PI |
|---|---|---|---|
| KT (n=42) | 31 (73.8%) | 9 (21.4%) | 2 (4.8%) |
| AM (n=16) | 7 (43.8%) | 5 (31.2%) | 4 (25.0%) |
| AM+FGG (n=6) | 4 (66.7%) | 2 (33.3%) | 0 (0%) |
| KT+AM+FGG combined (n=48) | 35 (72.9%) | 11 (22.9%) | 2 (4.2%) |

**Peri-implantitis OR**: KT/AM+FGG vs. AM — OR 6.67 (95% CI 1.09–40.9, p=0.041)

**Additional treatment between 10–20 years**: 11/22 AM patients (50%) vs. 2/42 KT patients (5%) required additional treatment (p<0.01).

**Marginal bone loss (20-year cumulative)**:
- KT: mean 0.66 ± 0.70 mm (median 0.5)
- AM: mean 1.29 ± 0.99 mm (median 0.75) — significantly greater vs. KT (p=0.006)
- AM+FGG: mean 0.90 ± 0.44 mm (median 0.75) — not significantly different from KT

**Marginal bone loss change between 10 and 20 years**:
- KT: 0.32 ± 0.56 mm
- AM: 0.84 ± 0.80 mm (p<0.001 vs. KT)
- AM+FGG: 0.28 ± 0.17 mm (similar to KT)

**BOP at 20 years**:
- KT: 30.9 ± 23.9%
- AM: 51.6 ± 26.6% (p=0.015 vs. KT)
- AM+FGG: 25.0 ± 22.4% (similar to KT)

**Soft-tissue recession (REC) at 20 years**:
- KT: 0.43 ± 0.77 mm (median 0)
- AM: 2.69 ± 0.79 mm (median 3) — p<0.001 vs. KT
- AM+FGG: 1.50 ± 1.05 mm (median 1.5) — p=0.048 vs. KT, but better than AM

**Soft-tissue dehiscence (REC ≥1 mm) at 20 years**:
- AM group: 16/16 (100%)
- KT/AM+FGG combined: 17/48 (35.4%)
- p<0.001 (Chi²); OR 81.6 (95% CI 8.45–785.9, p<0.001)

**PPD at 20 years**: No significant difference among groups (KT: 2.95 mm, AM: 3.56 mm, AM+FGG: 2.83 mm; p=0.848).

**Plaque index**: No significant difference among groups (p=0.623).

**FMBS**: AM patients had significantly higher FMBS than KT patients at 20 years (21.8% vs. 18.8%, p=0.024).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

1. **Observational design**: Risk of confounding bias; causality cannot be definitively established.
2. **Attrition (35%)**: May reduce statistical power; however, consistent with other long-term studies with comparable follow-up.
3. **Small AM+FGG group (n=6)**: Wide confidence intervals; FGG protective effect findings should be interpreted cautiously.
4. **No calibration session for clinical measurements**: Same experienced hygienist, but formal calibration not documented.
5. **Periapical radiographs only**: No CBCT; bucco-lingual bone morphology not assessable.
6. **Single clinician / single implant system**: Reduces external validity; results may not generalize to other implant designs or operator skill levels.
7. **No PROMs**: Study designed in 1998 before PROMs were standard; patient-reported outcomes not assessed.
8. **Qualitative KT classification**: KT assessed as present/absent without quantitative width measurement; no <2 mm vs. ≥2 mm threshold analysis.
9. **Vestibular depth and mucosa mobility**: Not systematically recorded; may be confounders in posterior mandible sites.

Future work: Randomized studies comparing timing of FGG (prior to, concomitant with, or after implant placement); PROMs integration; quantitative KM width thresholds as predictors; multicenter designs.

## 7. Glossary

- **KT (Keratinized Tissue)**: Keratinized mucosa present at the peri-implant site at baseline; serves as the reference group.
- **AM (Alveolar Mucosa)**: Non-keratinized mucosa at the peri-implant site; absence of keratinized tissue at baseline, no grafting.
- **AM+FGG**: Implants initially placed in alveolar mucosa, subsequently treated with free gingival graft during years 1–10 of SPC.
- **FGG (Free Gingival Graft)**: Autogenous soft tissue graft from the palate applied to augment keratinized tissue width around implants.
- **SPC (Supportive Periodontal/Peri-implant Care)**: Individualized recall program including oral hygiene reinforcement, supra-marginal instrumentation, and treatment of peri-implant diseases.
- **MBL (Marginal Bone Loss)**: Radiographic bone loss measured from implant shoulder to most coronal bone-to-implant contact; cumulative from baseline.
- **BOP (Bleeding on Probing)**: Percentage of implant sites exhibiting bleeding; indicator of mucosal inflammation.
- **REC (Soft-Tissue Recession)**: Distance from implant shoulder to coronal mucosal margin; REC ≥1 mm = soft-tissue dehiscence.
- **PPD (Probing Pocket Depth)**: Measured in mm at 4 sites (mesial, buccal, distal, lingual) around each implant.
- **PH / PM / PI**: Peri-implant health / peri-implant mucositis / peri-implantitis (2018 Case Definitions).
- **SLA (Sandblasted and Acid-Etched)**: Surface treatment of Straumann tissue-level implants used throughout the cohort.
- **STROBE**: Strengthening the Reporting of Observational studies in Epidemiology — reporting guideline applied.
- **CIST (Cumulative Interceptive Supportive Therapy)**: Protocol for peri-implant disease management; C and D modalities refer to systemic antibiotics and surgical treatment.
