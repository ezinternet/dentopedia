## Why Ingested

[[wiki/orthodontics/mohammed-2018-miniscrew-anatomical-site-failure-sr-ma]] concluded anatomical site and several factors drive miniscrew survival. This 2024 SR+MA refines that picture specifically for adolescents/young adults: with univariate + multivariate meta-analysis it finds insertion site and implant size do NOT significantly affect failure, narrowing the conditions under which site-dependence holds.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (15 studies, 1981 patients, 3272 orthodontic mini-implants in young adults): success broadly consistent across demographics — gender and age not significant — but failure higher in males and in the maxilla; insertion site and implant size not significant; higher applied force correlated with LOWER failure.

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (15편·1981명·교정용 미니임플란트 3272개, 청소년·청년): 성공률은 인구통계에 무관하게 일관 — 성별·연령 무의미 — 그러나 남성·상악에서 실패 높음, 식립 부위·임플란트 크기는 무의미, 적용 힘이 클수록 실패 낮음(음의 상관).

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (univariate + multivariate), PRISMA, PROSPERO CRD42023411955
- **Journal**: The Saudi Dental Journal 2024;36:1149–1159 (online 7 Aug 2024)
- **Search**: PubMed-MEDLINE, Scopus, Cochrane, Web of Science, ScienceDirect; focus 2015–2023
- **Risk of bias**: ROBINS-I (Cochrane Bias Methods Group); Robvis visualization
- **Population**: adolescents and young adults

## 2. Key Contributions

- Age-focused SR+MA: directly tests whether OMI success differs between adolescents and adults — finds no significant age effect.
- Both univariate and multivariate meta-analyses, isolating single-factor and joint effects.
- Counter-intuitive force finding: higher applied force associated with lower failure.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 599 records → 448 after de-dup → 15 included.
- 1981 patients, 3272 OMIs; 10 retrospective + 5 prospective cohorts.
- Categorical variables via Cohen's method, random-effects; leave-one-out sensitivity; I² heterogeneity; Egger's test; forest/funnel plots; weighted regression for numerical variables.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Gender**: not a significant predictor overall, but **failure higher in males**.
- **Age**: not significant (adolescents vs young adults comparable).
- **Jaw**: **failure higher in the maxilla**.
- **Insertion site & implant size**: NOT significant for failure.
- **Force magnitude**: negative correlation with failure (higher force → lower failure).
- Implant dimensions across studies: diameter 1.2–4.1 mm, length 4–14 mm.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Mostly retrospective cohorts; ROBINS-I (no RCT-grade evidence).
- Heterogeneous force/loading reporting; force–failure finding needs prospective confirmation.
- 181 of 201 eligible-by-title articles were inaccessible, constraining the final set.

## 7. Glossary

- **OMI**: orthodontic mini-implant (synonym for TAD/miniscrew).
- **Univariate vs multivariate meta-analysis**: single-factor effect vs joint effect of co-varying factors.
- **ROBINS-I**: risk-of-bias tool for non-randomized intervention studies.
- **Robvis**: risk-of-bias visualization ("traffic light") tool.
