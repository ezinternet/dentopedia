## Why Ingested

Existing wiki pages on complete denture fabrication (e.g., [[wiki/complete-denture/khorasani-2024-3d-printed-vs-milled-complete]]) focus on digital/CAD-CAM methods; this SR+MA (n=19 RCTs) specifically benchmarks simplified chair-side impression/jaw-record techniques against conventional multi-appointment workflows, filling a gap in evidence on clinical time, cost, and patient-reported outcomes for resource-limited settings.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 19 RCTs found simplified complete denture fabrication saves ~77 min of clinical time and ~$86 USD in cost per case with no significant difference in patient satisfaction or OHRQoL at 6 months versus conventional technique.

## 한줄요약

19개 RCT의 SR+MA에서 간소화 총의치 제작법은 기존법 대비 임상시간 약 77분, 비용 약 86달러 절감, 환자만족도 및 구강건강관련 삶의질(OHRQoL)에서는 유의한 차이 없음.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First comprehensive SR+MA specifically pooling RCT data on clinical time and cost comparisons between simplified and conventional complete denture techniques.
2. Confirms that simplified techniques achieve equivalent patient satisfaction (OHIP, VAS-based instruments) with measurably lower resource consumption.
3. Highlights critical heterogeneity in how "simplified" is defined across studies (varying reductions in appointment number, impression technique, jaw record methods).
4. Provides quantitative pooled estimates with 95% CIs for three primary outcomes: patient satisfaction, clinical time, and fabrication cost.
5. Risk of bias assessment (Cochrane RoB tool) identifies high risk in the majority of included trials, warranting cautious interpretation.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search databases**: MEDLINE/PubMed, Cochrane Central Register of Controlled Trials (CENTRAL), Scopus; plus manual search of reference lists up to February 2020.
- **Inclusion criteria**: RCTs only; edentulous adults requiring complete denture treatment; comparison of simplified vs. conventional (traditional) technique.
- **Exclusion criteria**: Implant-retained overdentures, partial dentures, digital/CAD-CAM fabrication trials.
- **Studies included**: 19 RCTs met all inclusion criteria.
- **Meta-analysis method**: Random-effects model; outcomes reported as standardized mean difference (SMD) or mean difference (MD) with 95% CI.
- **Heterogeneity**: Assessed with I² statistic; high heterogeneity reported across studies.
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane RoB tool; majority rated high risk, primarily due to inability to blind participants/operators.

**Definition of "simplified technique"** (pooled from included studies): reduction in the number of clinical appointments (typically from 5–6 to 2–4), use of single-impression methods (stock tray + irreversible hydrocolloid or elastomeric materials), simplified jaw relation records (no facebow, single-step centric relation), and reduced try-in steps.

**Definition of "conventional technique"**: 5–6 clinical appointments including custom tray fabrication, two-stage impression (primary + final), facebow transfer, Gothic arch or clinical methods for centric relation, and wax try-in.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Patient Satisfaction (primary outcome)
- **SMD = 0.02** (95% CI: −0.22 to 0.27) — marginally favoring simplified method, **not statistically significant**
- Instruments used: OHIP-EDENT, OHIP-14, VAS satisfaction scales across studies
- No significant difference in OHRQoL between the two groups at 6-month follow-up

### Clinical Time
- **MD = −77.34 min** (95% CI: −122.45 to −32.24) — significantly favoring simplified method (p<0.05)
- Simplified techniques saved approximately 77 minutes of chair-side clinical time per case

### Fabrication Cost
- **MD = −85.89 USD** (95% CI: −170.02 to −1.77) — significantly favoring simplified method (p<0.05)
- Simplified techniques reduced cost by approximately $86 USD per case
- Wide CI reflects heterogeneity in currency/cost calculation methods across countries

### Secondary Outcomes
- Masticatory performance: limited data; no consistent significant difference
- Denture quality assessments: sparse reporting; cannot pool
- Follow-up durations: most studies 6–12 months; one 10-year follow-up (Kawai et al., 2018)

### Key finding
Simplified denture fabrication is cost-effective and time-efficient with equivalent patient-reported outcomes, but high risk of bias and study heterogeneity limit confidence in the estimates.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

1. **High heterogeneity (I²)**: Diverse definitions of "simplified" across studies make direct comparisons problematic.
2. **High risk of bias**: Most RCTs could not blind participants or clinicians; sequence generation and allocation concealment often unclear.
3. **Short follow-up**: Most outcomes assessed at 6 months; long-term denture performance and durability are unknown.
4. **Publication bias**: Funnel plot analysis inconclusive due to limited number of studies per outcome.
5. **Currency/cost variation**: Cost data pooled across different countries without purchasing-power-parity adjustment.
6. **Operator experience**: Skill level variation across centers may confound results.
7. **Future research needs**: Standardized definitions of simplified technique; longer follow-up RCTs; digital technique comparison arms.

## 6. Related Work

- Ye & Sun (2017) — earlier systematic review on simplified complete dentures (non-meta-analytic)
- Kawai et al. (2005, 2018) — landmark RCT comparing simplified vs. traditional with 10-year follow-up; no significant long-term difference
- Al-Ansari & Tantawi (2019) — meta-analysis on patient-reported outcomes of simplified dentures (partial overlap)
- Vecchia et al. (2014) — RCT cost analysis (included in this SR+MA)
- Regis et al. (2013) — RCT on simplified method (included in this SR+MA)

## 7. Glossary

- **Simplified technique (간소화 기법)**: Complete denture fabrication protocol reducing clinical appointments and procedural steps (stock tray impressions, simplified jaw records, no facebow).
- **Conventional technique (CT, 전통적 기법)**: Standard multi-appointment complete denture protocol with custom tray, two-stage impression, facebow transfer, wax try-in.
- **OHIP-EDENT (Oral Health Impact Profile for Edentulous patients)**: Patient-reported OHRQoL instrument validated for edentulous patients.
- **SMD (표준화 평균차)**: Standardized mean difference — effect size metric used when studies measure the same construct with different scales.
- **MD (평균차)**: Mean difference — used when studies report outcomes on the same metric (minutes, dollars).
- **OHRQoL (구강건강관련 삶의 질)**: Oral health-related quality of life.
- **Cochrane RoB (비뚤림위험)**: Cochrane risk-of-bias assessment tool for RCTs.
