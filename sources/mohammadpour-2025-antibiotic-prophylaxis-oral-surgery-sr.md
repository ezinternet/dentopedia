## Why Ingested

항생제 예방 투여 (Antibiotic Prophylaxis, AP)에 관한 기존 페이지들([[wiki/drug/de-angelis-2025-antibiotic-third-molar-extraction-prevention-sr]], [[wiki/drug/vidovic-juras-2024-antibiotic-prophylaxis-dental-procedures]])은 특정 술식(제3대구치 발치, 치과 처치)에 한정된 반면, 이 스코핑 리뷰는 구강악안면외과 전 술식(골절, 임플란트, 악교정, 종양, TMJ 치환 등)에 걸친 증거를 체계적으로 지도화하여 클리닉 의사결정을 위한 폭넓은 기준점을 제공한다.

## One-line Summary

Scoping review of 125 studies (25 RCTs, 60 cohort, 40 SRs) mapping antibiotic prophylaxis evidence across oral and maxillofacial surgery: perioperative prophylaxis (≤24 h) is supported for high-risk procedures, but extended postoperative courses beyond 24 h do not reduce SSI rates and increase antimicrobial resistance risk.

## 한줄요약

구강악안면외과 125편(RCT 25, 코호트 60, SR 40) 스코핑 리뷰: 제3대구치 발치·하악골절·TMJ 치환·복잡 임플란트·두경부종양 절제술에서 항생제 예방투여 (Antibiotic Prophylaxis, AP)가 지지되나, 술후 24시간 초과 연장 투여는 수술부위감염 (Surgical Site Infection, SSI) 감소 효과 없이 항균제 내성 (Antimicrobial Resistance, AMR) 위험만 증가시킨다.

- **Search period**: 2000–2025
- **Databases**: Ovid MEDLINE, PubMed, Embase, Google Scholar, Web of Science

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Comprehensive evidence map across the full spectrum of oral and maxillofacial surgery (fractures, implants, orthognathic, cleft, TMJ, oncological, salivary gland procedures)
2. Contamination-risk classification framework (Type I–IV) with procedure-specific antibiotic recommendations
3. Strong evidence against extended postoperative antibiotic prophylaxis beyond 24 h for mandibular fractures, maxillofacial fractures, and most other procedures
4. Antibiotic-of-choice clarification: amoxicillin/co-amoxiclav first-line; clindamycin reserved for penicillin-allergic patients only
5. Antimicrobial stewardship (Antimicrobial Stewardship, AMS) emphasis — extended courses paradoxically increase late infectious complications

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Large scoping review (PRISMA-ScR 2020)
- **Screening**: 542 papers identified → 261 excluded (duplicates, non-full-text, non-English) → 281 screened → **125 included**
  - 25 RCTs, 60 cohort studies, 40 systematic reviews
- **Inclusion**: RCTs and cohort studies on pre/peri/postoperative antibiotic prophylaxis in oral and maxillofacial surgical patients
- **Exclusion**: Case reports/series, non-English, conference abstracts, letters
- **Population**: All ages, male and female, undergoing oral or maxillofacial surgery
- **Evidence grading**: GRADE guidelines (Level I/IIa = strong recommendation; Level IIb majority = weak recommendation)
- **Limitation acknowledged**: Heterogeneity in study design, antibiotic type, fracture classification, follow-up duration (median ~6 weeks), SSI definition

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Contamination-Risk Classification

| Type | Description | SSI Rate | Prophylaxis Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| I (Clean) | No mucosal disruption | 1–4% | None or ≤24 h co-amoxiclav |
| II (Clean-contaminated) | Mucosal disruption | 5–15% | Co-amoxiclav 2 g; clindamycin+gentamicin if PCN-allergic |
| III (Contaminated) | Oncological oral/neck | 16–25% | Ampicillin/sulbactam or piperacillin/tazobactam |
| IV (Infected/dirty) | Active infection | >25% | Therapeutic antibiotics (not prophylaxis) |

### Procedure-Specific Findings

| Procedure | SSI Rate | Pre-op AP | Peri-op AP | Post-op AP | First-line Agent |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Third molar removal | 1–11% | Yes (Grade I) | Unclear | No | Amoxicillin 2 g single dose |
| Dental implant (simple) | 4–10% | No | No | No | Amoxicillin 2 g if indicated |
| Dental implant (complex/graft) | 4–10% | Yes | Yes | No | Amoxicillin 2 g |
| Mandibular fracture | 3.3–43.9% | Strongly Yes | Strongly Yes | 24 h IV only | Penicillin/co-amoxiclav |
| Maxillofacial fractures | 1–33% (pooled 5.6%) | Yes | Yes | No (>24 h) | Amoxicillin 2 g or penicillin V |
| Orthognathic surgery | 1.4–33.4% | Yes | Yes | Weak (5-day may reduce morbidity) | Co-amoxiclav, cefazolin |
| TMJ replacement | 1.5–4.5% | Weakly Yes | Strongly Yes | Limited evidence | Cefazolin, cefuroxime, clindamycin |
| Head and neck oncology | 22.7–46% | Yes | Yes | Until drainage ceases | Cefuroxime + metronidazole |
| Cleft lip/palate | 1.1–4.4% | Insufficient data | Cefuroxime IV | Amoxicillin 5 days (fistula prevention) | — |

### Penicillin-Allergic Patients
- Clindamycin is the recommended alternative across procedures
- NOT recommended as routine first-line due to resistance profile and adverse effects
- For clean-contaminated/contaminated: clindamycin + gentamicin (aminoglycoside covers Gram-negatives)

### Extended Prophylaxis Harms
- Extended prophylaxis >7 days: markedly higher infection risk (Zosa et al.)
- Extended prophylaxis >72 h: increased SSI incidence (Delaplain et al.)
- Prolonged courses → delayed infectious complications; AMR emergence

### Antibiotic Reduction Evidence
- Third molar extractions: prophylactic antibiotics reduce infection risk by **60–70%**
- Dental implants: preoperative amoxicillin reduces implant failure by **~2%**
- 24-h postoperative course = multi-day course for mandibular fractures in most RCTs

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Substantial heterogeneity across included studies (design, antibiotic choice, follow-up)
- Short follow-up (median ~6 weeks) likely misses late-presenting SSIs
- Many mandibular fracture studies did not specify open vs. closed fracture status
- No culture data available from infection diagnoses
- Evidence for cleft lip/palate, immunocompromised patients remains sparse
- Future need: RCTs for procedures with limited evidence (cleft surgery, immunocompromised, complex implant scenarios)

## 7. Glossary

- **AP (Antibiotic Prophylaxis, 항생제 예방투여)**: Administration of antibiotics before/during/after surgery to prevent infectious complications
- **SSI (Surgical Site Infection, 수술부위감염)**: Infection occurring within 30 days of surgery (or 1 year for implanted material)
- **AMS (Antimicrobial Stewardship, 항균제 관리)**: Coordinated program to ensure appropriate antibiotic use
- **AMR (Antimicrobial Resistance, 항균제 내성)**: Bacteria's ability to resist antibiotic effects
- **Co-amoxiclav (아목시실린/클라불란산)**: Amoxicillin + clavulanic acid; broad-spectrum beta-lactam + beta-lactamase inhibitor combination
- **PRISMA-ScR**: Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses extension for Scoping Reviews
- **GRADE**: Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation — evidence quality framework
- **ORIF (Open Reduction Internal Fixation, 관혈적 정복고정술)**: Surgical technique for fracture stabilization using plates and screws
- **Clean-contaminated (청정-오염)**: Wound class involving controlled mucosal incision without spillage; 5–15% SSI risk
- **Perioperative period (수술전후기)**: From 2 h before surgery to 24 h after surgery
