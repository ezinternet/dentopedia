## One-line Summary

Retrospective cohort (1323 implants / 738 patients; 53 early failures before final prosthetic loading): multivariate predictors of early implant failure were smoking (OR 1.84), posterior maxilla (OR 2.96), periodontal-extraction site (OR 2.53), type IV bone (OR 2.88), previously augmented site (OR 2.24), and immediate provisional prosthesis (OR 3.42).

## 한줄요약

후향 코호트(임플란트 1323개 / 환자 738명, 보철 로딩 전 조기 실패 53건): 조기 임플란트 실패의 다변량 위험인자는 흡연(OR 1.84)·후방 상악(OR 2.96)·치주문제 발치부위(OR 2.53)·4형 골(OR 2.88)·기존 골증대 부위(OR 2.24)·즉시 잠정보철(OR 3.42).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Defines early implant failure explicitly as failure **before final prosthetic loading** (the osseointegration window), separating it from late/peri-implantitis failure.
- Tests patient-, local-, surgical-, and implant-level variables in one multivariate logistic model rather than one factor at a time.
- Identifies six independent significant predictors, with **immediate provisional prosthesis (OR 3.42)** the strongest, ahead of posterior maxilla and type IV bone.
- Shows a univariate signal for narrow implants that **disappears** after adjustment (OR 2.32, P=0.068) — a caution against single-variable claims.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Design: retrospective cohort, consecutive patients receiving dental implants over a 4-year window.
- Sample: 1323 implants in 738 patients (mean 1.8 implants/patient).
- Variables: patient (age, sex, smoking, systemic disease); local (region, reason for extraction, bone quality); surgical (bone augmentation, placement timing, staging, antibiotic prophylaxis); implant (brand, length, diameter).
- Outcome: early implant failure = failure in the early stage before final prosthetic loading (53 implants / 52 patients).
- Analysis: bivariate analyses + multivariate logistic regression.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Early failure: 53/1323 implants (≈4.0%) in 52/738 patients (≈7.0% patient-level).
- Multivariate significant predictors (OR, P):
  - Immediate provisional prosthesis — 3.42, P=0.019
  - Posterior maxillary region — 2.96, P=0.006
  - Type IV bone — 2.88, P=0.008
  - Periodontal reason for extraction — 2.53, P=0.004
  - Previously augmented site — 2.24, P=0.014
  - Smoking — 1.84, P=0.031
- Narrow implants: significant in bivariate (P=0.012) but NOT in multivariate model (OR 2.32, P=0.068).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-center retrospective design; residual confounding; no 95% CIs reported in abstract.
- **Abstract-only ingest** — full methods/numbers (CIs, bone-quality classification, brand effects) not verifiable here.
- Immediate-provisional effect not separable from case selection (immediate loading often chosen in better bone/esthetic zone).

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary

- **Early implant failure**: loss of osseointegration before the implant is restored (before final prosthetic loading).
- **Type IV bone (Lekholm-Zarb)**: thin cortex around low-density trabecular core; lowest primary-stability bone class.
- **Immediate provisional prosthesis**: placement of a temporary restoration at/near surgery (immediate loading variant).
