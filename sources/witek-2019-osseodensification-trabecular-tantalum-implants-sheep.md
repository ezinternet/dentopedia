## Why Ingested

Tests whether the osseodensification (OD) autografting mechanism interacts with implant porosity — first in vivo pairing of OD with a porous trabecular tantalum implant, finding OD-CCW raises BAFO via bone chips trapped in the porous network. Extends the OD mechanism evidence of [[overviews/osseodensification-clinical-applications]] into the porous-implant/surface domain alongside [[overviews/implant-surface-comparison]].

## One-line Summary
Sheep ilium 3-week histology — three osteotomy modes (Regular, OD-CW, OD-CCW) for trabecular tantalum implants; OD-CCW showed higher BAFO% than Regular due to bone chips trapped in the porous network, with no other significant differences.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Animal experimental (in vivo histomorphometry)
- **Journal**: Medicina Oral Patologia Oral y Cirugia Bucal 2019;24(6):e764-9
- **DOI**: 10.4317/medoral.23108
- **Sample**: 3 female sheep, both ilia each, 3 osteotomy sites per ilium (Regular, OD-CW, OD-CCW); trabecular metal (Zimmer) implants 3.7 mm × 10 mm
- **Primary Question**: Do osseodensification drilling modes alter osseointegration parameters around porous trabecular tantalum metal implants in low-density bone?

## 2. Key Contributions
- First in vivo evaluation pairing osseodensification with a **porous trabecular tantalum (TM)** implant — tests whether OD's autograft-along-the-wall mechanism interacts with implant porosity.
- Sheep iliac crest model chosen specifically for low-density bone (analogous to maxillary posterior in humans).
- Standardized drilling at 1100 rpm with saline irrigation, 3-week healing.
- Direct head-to-head of Regular vs OD-CW (clockwise/cutting) vs OD-CCW (counterclockwise/densifying).
- Finds OD-CCW > Regular for BAFO% (bone area fraction occupancy), attributed mechanistically to bone chips trapped in the TM porous network acting as nucleation sites.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Three osteotomy sites (Regular, OD-CW, OD-CCW) were prepared in each ilium of three female sheep. Drilling at 1100 rpm with saline irrigation. Trabecular metal (Zimmer) implants 3.7 × 10 mm were placed in respective osteotomies. After a 3-week healing period the sheep were euthanized and ilia harvested. Samples were processed for non-decalcified histology and analyzed with ImageJ.

Primary outcomes: bone-to-implant contact (BIC%) and bone area fraction occupancy (BAFO%). The sheep iliac crest is justified as a translational model for low-density human jaw bone. The TM implant's porous network is the key variable that distinguishes this study from earlier OD work in solid-bodied implants.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- All implants showed successful bone formation in peri-implant environment and within the open spaces of the TM porous network.
- BAFO% was significantly higher in **OD-CCW vs Regular** — attributed to bone chips trapped within the trabecular network of the implant.
- With that exception, no other statistically significant histomorphometric differences between Regular, OD-CW, and OD-CCW.
- Specific numerical BIC% and BAFO% values, SDs, and p-values are not extractable from the available front/back text.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small sample: 3 sheep, n=6 ilia, 18 implants total — limited statistical power.
- 3-week healing only — captures early-stage osseointegration, not loading or long-term remodeling.
- Single rpm and irrigation protocol — not a sensitivity analysis on surgical parameters.
- TM implant findings are specific to porous-bodied designs; do not directly extrapolate to standard solid-screw implants.
- No clinical outcomes (loading, survival) — purely histomorphometric.
- Strong industry/affiliation cluster (Coelho/Witek NYU group + Zimmer hardware) — selection of comparators may not be neutral.

## 7. Glossary
- **Trabecular metal (TM)**: porous tantalum implant body designed to mimic cancellous bone architecture and allow bone ingrowth into the implant porous network.
- **OD-CW / OD-CCW**: clockwise (cutting) and counterclockwise (densifying) Densah bur modes.
- **Regular**: conventional subtractive drilling protocol.
- **BAFO%**: % of inter-thread area (or, here, porous-network area) occupied by bone.
- **BIC%**: % of implant surface in direct bone contact.
