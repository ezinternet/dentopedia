## One-line Summary
SR+MA (7 studies, 334 zirconia implant crowns, 3–10 yr): screw-retained significantly lower MBL at 6 months (SMD -0.63); long-term MBL comparable; SR crowns had 46% lower complication risk overall.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis
- **Journal**: Cureus (2025)
- **DOI**: 10.7759/cureus.83370
- **Studies**: 7 (168 cement-retained + 166 screw-retained zirconia crowns)
- **Follow-up**: 3–10 years

## 2. Key Contributions
- First SR+MA specifically for zirconia (monolithic/layered) implant crowns
- Identifies early MBL advantage for screw-retained at 6 months
- Demonstrates complication profile divergence (46% risk reduction with screw-retained)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: SR+MA (PRISMA)
- **Inclusion**: RCTs and prospective studies comparing CR vs SR zirconia implant crowns
- **Primary outcome**: Marginal bone loss (MBL)
- **Secondary outcomes**: Bleeding on probing (BOP), complications
- **Statistical**: Standardized mean difference (SMD) for continuous; relative risk (RR) for dichotomous outcomes

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Time | SR vs CR | Effect size | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| MBL | 6 months | SR lower | SMD -0.63 | 0.002 |
| MBL | 1 year | NS | — | NS |
| MBL | 3 years | NS | — | NS |
| MBL | 5 years | NS | — | NS |
| BOP | Any | Similar | — | NS |
| Complications | Any | SR lower | RR 0.54 | 0.02 |

- **Early peri-implant bone preservation**: Screw-retained crowns show less MBL at 6 months — clinically relevant for implants placed with GBR or limited bone volume
- **Long-term**: Both retention types converge; no significant MBL difference at 1–5 years
- **Complications**: SR reduces overall complication risk by 46% (RR 0.54)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Only 7 studies — moderate evidence base
- Heterogeneity in zirconia types (monolithic vs veneered)
- Cement types not standardized across CR groups
- Longer RCTs needed (>10 years)

## 6. Related Work
- Hamed 2020 (SR): consistent — screw biological advantage
- Khurshid 2025 (prospective): consistent — cement has more biological issues at 12 months

## 7. Glossary
- **MBL**: Marginal bone loss — crestal bone level change on periapical radiograph
- **SMD**: Standardized mean difference — pooled effect size for continuous outcomes
- **RR**: Relative risk — ratio of complication probability (SR/CR)
- **BOP**: Bleeding on probing — surrogate for peri-implant inflammation
