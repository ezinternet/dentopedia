## Why Ingested

기존 single-visit vs multiple-visit 근거([[endodontics/visit-protocol/mergoni-2022-single-versus-multiple-visits-cochrane-sr]], [[endodontics/visit-protocol/schwendicke-2017-single-visit-multiple-visit-rct-sr-ma]])가 "유의한 결과 차이 없음"이라는 SR/MA 결론을 제시했는데, 이 2026 RCT는 단근치·근단치주염 한정으로 PAI 기반 방사선학적 치유를 직접 비교해 그 결론을 1차 임상 데이터로 재확인(reinforce)한다.

## One-line Summary

Single-blind RCT (n=30, single-rooted teeth with primary apical periodontitis): single-visit vs multiple-visit RCT showed no significant difference in PAI-based radiographic periapical healing at 6/12 months (93.3% vs 86.7% success at 12 mo, P>0.05).

## 한줄요약

단일맹검 RCT (n=30, 1차성 근단치주염 단근치): 단일내원 vs 다회내원 근관치료가 PAI 기반 방사선학적 치근단 치유에서 6/12개월 모두 유의차 없음 (12개월 성공률 93.3% vs 86.7%, P>0.05) — 회내 횟수가 장기 예후를 좌우하지 않음을 시사.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Direct head-to-head RCT of single-visit vs multiple-visit RCT outcomes restricted to single-rooted teeth with primary apical periodontitis, using the validated Periapical Index (PAI) as the radiographic outcome.
- Adds primary-data confirmation that the inter-appointment calcium hydroxide dressing does not provide a measurable radiographic healing benefit when chemo-mechanical debridement is standardized.
- Reinforces the growing single-visit endodontics trend on patient-convenience and efficiency grounds without prognostic penalty.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Prospective, single-blind RCT; n=30 patients randomized 1:1 (Group A single-visit n=15, Group B multiple-visit n=15).
- **Allocation concealment**: Sealed opaque envelopes; PAI evaluator blinded and independent.
- **Inclusion**: Age 18–60, single-rooted teeth with primary apical periodontitis, pre-operative PAI 3/4/5, no prior RCT.
- **Exclusion**: Acute apical abscess, complicated anatomy, systemic disease, pregnant/lactating.
- **Operator**: Single experienced clinician; standardized canal preparation and irrigation for both arms.
- **Group A**: Prepared, irrigated, obturated in one appointment.
- **Group B**: Calcium hydroxide intracanal medicament at visit 1; obturated at visit 2 (~1 week later).
- **Primary outcome**: Radiographic healing by PAI at 6 and 12 months; success = PAI ≤2 at 12 months.
- **Statistics**: Chi-square and Mann–Whitney U; significance P<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Single-visit (n=15) | Multiple-visit (n=15) | P |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mean PAI at 6 mo | 2.1 | 2.2 | 0.58 |
| Mean PAI at 12 mo | 1.8 | 1.9 | 0.71 |
| Success (PAI ≤2) at 6 mo | 12/15 (80.0%) | 11/15 (73.3%) | 0.68 |
| Success (PAI ≤2) at 12 mo | 14/15 (93.3%) | 13/15 (86.7%) | 0.6 |

- No statistically significant difference between groups at either follow-up (all P>0.05).
- 12-month PAI distribution: PAI 1 (healed) 11 (73.3%) single-visit vs 10 (66.7%) multiple-visit.
- Authors conclude both protocols achieve comparable high healing; single-visit is a viable alternative.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Very small sample (n=30) → underpowered for non-inferiority/equivalence claims.
- Restricted to single-rooted teeth with primary apical periodontitis; not generalizable to molars or retreatment.
- 12-month follow-up only; longer-term recurrence not captured.
- Single operator/center; limited external validity.

## 7. Glossary

- **PAI (Periapical Index)**: 5-point ordinal radiographic scoring of periapical bone status; PAI 1–2 = healthy/healing, PAI 3–5 = apical periodontitis.
- **Single-visit RCT**: Root canal cleaning, shaping, and obturation completed in one appointment.
- **Multiple-visit RCT**: Treatment split over ≥2 appointments, typically with an inter-appointment Ca(OH)₂ dressing.
- **Intracanal medicament**: Antimicrobial dressing (commonly calcium hydroxide) placed between visits.
