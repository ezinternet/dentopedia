## One-line Summary
Prospective longitudinal study (n=14, 6 mo) comparing two xenograft-based ARP protocols — Bartee (defective socket, with dPTFE membrane) vs Bio-Col (intact socket, collagen plug + xenograft) — both with InterOss® DBBM: comparable dimensional preservation (~11–13% vertical, 20–22% horizontal resorption); Bartee yielded more new bone (31.10% vs 13.24%).

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: International Journal of Dentistry 2024, Article 3263011 (received Sep 2022, accepted Apr 2024, published 15 May 2024).
- **Study type**: Prospective longitudinal clinical study; two technique arms based on socket morphology.
- **Setting**: Popular Autonomous University of the State of Puebla (UPAEP), Mexico.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Direct comparison of two named ARP protocols (Bartee vs Bio-Col) using the same xenograft (InterOss® anorganic cancellous bone graft) — clarifies whether protocol-name differences translate to outcome differences.
- Demonstrates that both techniques produce equivalent dimensional preservation despite very different socket-wall starting conditions (Bartee = defective wall, Bio-Col = 4-wall intact).
- Provides 6-month CBCT-derived dimensional change values and histomorphometric numbers (new bone, residual particles) for clinical reference.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 14 single-root or premolar teeth with fused roots indicated for ARP and subsequent implant placement.
- **Group A — Bartee technique (n=7)**: sockets with ≥4 mm apico-coronal residual bone or buccal/lingual wall defect. Xenograft + dense PTFE membrane (Cytoplast singles, Osteogenics Biomedical) — membrane removed at 21 days; avoid excessive graft compaction; interrupted/mattress sutures.
- **Group B — Bio-Col technique (n=7)**: intact 4-wall sockets. Xenograft in coronal 3/4, resorbable collagen plug (CollaPlug, Zimmer Biomet) in apical 1/4; crossed suture + Periacryl 90-HV tissue adhesive.
- Both: amoxicillin 500 mg q8h × 7 d, ibuprofen, 0.12% chlorhexidine BID × 2 wk, no prosthesis 4 wk, no smoking.
- CBCT baseline + 6 months for dimensional analysis; trephine biopsy (2 mm × 4 mm) at implant placement for histomorphometry.
- Statistics: Wilcoxon signed-rank (related), Mann–Whitney U (between groups).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Vertical resorption**: Group A 11.48% vs Group B 13.24% (p = 0.482, NS).
- **Horizontal resorption**: Group A 21.95% vs Group B 20.55% (p = 0.949, NS).
- **Histomorphometry — new bone formation at 6 mo**: Group A 31.10% vs Group B 13.24% (p = 0.744, NS in this small sample).
- **Residual graft particles**: Group A 28.09% vs Group B 20.55% (p = 0.302, NS).
- Both techniques: dimensional stability sufficient for 6-month implant placement.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small sample (n=7 per arm); underpowered for histomorphometric comparison.
- 6-month outcome only; long-term implant survival not reported in this paper.
- Non-randomized assignment — groups defined by socket morphology, so a confounder by indication exists.
- Single xenograft brand (InterOss®); generalization to other DBBM uncertain.

## 7. Glossary
- **Bartee technique**: Open-membrane ARP with dPTFE membrane intentionally left exposed and removed at ~21 days; suited to compromised socket walls.
- **Bio-Col technique**: Coronal collagen plug over xenograft-filled socket sealed with tissue adhesive — minimal-invasive option for intact 4-wall sockets.
- **InterOss® anorganic cancellous bone graft**: DBBM xenograft (SigmaGraft, USA).
- **dPTFE (dense PTFE)**: Non-resorbable barrier membrane that can be left exposed without primary closure; pore size too small for bacterial penetration.
