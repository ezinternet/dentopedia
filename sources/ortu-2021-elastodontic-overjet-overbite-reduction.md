## Why Ingested

Supplies the head-to-head clinical efficacy evidence behind the EF-Line / elastodontic review pages: where [[wiki/orthodontics/myofunctional/ureni-2024-elastodontic-devices-sagittal-malocclusion-scoping-review]] synthesizes elastodontic sagittal/cephalometric outcomes at the review level, this prospective controlled study directly measures overjet (OJ) and overbite (OB) reduction in mixed-dentition Class II children with two commercial elastodontic devices (EQ Series II vs Occlus-o-Guide), assessed on digital 3D model superimposition. It is the quantitative OJ/OB-reduction data point the reviews abstract over, and it shows device-brand matters (Eptamed EQ significantly outperformed Occlus-o-Guide).

## One-line Summary

Prospective controlled clinical study (n=60 children aged 7–15, Class II div 1/2; 30 EQ Series II [Eptamed] vs 30 Occlus-o-Guide) showing both elastodontic devices reduced overjet and overbite over 12 months, with the EQ Series II achieving significantly lower OJ and OB at T1 than Occlus-o-Guide (p=0.0019), measured by iTero digital-model evaluation.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Efficacy of elastodontic devices in overjet and overbite reduction assessed by digital model superimposition (PMC title: "...assessed by computer-aid evaluation")
- **Authors**: Ortu E, Pietropaoli D, Cova S, Marci MC, Monaco A
- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health 2021;21(1):260
- **Year**: 2021 (published 2021-05-17)
- **DOI**: 10.1186/s12903-021-01628-7
- **PMID**: 34001097 | **PMCID**: PMC8130291
- **Study type**: Prospective controlled clinical study (two parallel groups, test vs control; within-group T0→T1 and between-group comparison)
- **Setting**: Dental Clinic, University of L'Aquila, Italy (Ethics Committee approval no. 16137/2016)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Direct head-to-head clinical comparison of two commercial elastodontic (myofunctional) devices for OJ/OB correction in growing Class II patients: **EQ Series II (Eptamed)** [test] vs **Occlus-o-Guide (Sweden & Martina)** [control].
- Outcome assessment by **digital 3D model superimposition** — alginate impressions → white-stone plaster models → iTero intraoral scanning → 3D virtual models — rather than manual caliper measurement.
- Demonstrates that the choice of elastodontic device brand/design materially affects OJ/OB reduction magnitude: the EQ Series II achieved significantly greater correction.
- Positions elastodontic appliances as easy-to-wear, neuromuscular-integrating interceptive tools for mixed dentition with fewer check-ups and lower microbiota impact than fixed appliances.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Screening**: 115 patients aged 6–16 examined; panoramic radiographs, extra-/intra-oral photos, alginate impressions of both arches; treatment plans per Brook & Shaw IOTN-based orthodontic treatment need index.
- **Exclusion criteria**: IOTN index > 4; epilepsy, systemic disease, TMD, or periodontal disease; lack of written informed consent from parent/guardian.
- **Inclusion criteria**: skeletal and dental Class II malocclusion (divisions 1 and 2); presence of OJ (OV) and OB.
- **Final sample**: 60 patients aged 7–15, split into two groups of 30:
  - **Test (EQ Series II / Eptamed)**: 30 patients (14 M, 16 F; mean age 10.66 ± 2.12 y) — medium-hardness orange EQ Class II device; mouthguard-like, covers both arches to last molars; sized by inter-first-premolar palatal-cusp distance; activated by biting (muscle-driven soft elastic forces); worn overnight.
  - **Control (Occlus-o-Guide / Sweden & Martina)**: 30 patients (15 M, 15 F; mean age 10.76 ± 2.52 y) — G-type elastomeric preformed activator (EGA, Eruptive Guidance Appliance) for early–late mixed dentition; sized by inter-upper-lateral-incisor distance; worn overnight; provides depressive force to front teeth + guides posterior eruption; myofunctional regulator (tongue posture, atypical swallow re-education, breathing).
- **Timepoints**: T0 (before therapy) and T1 (after 1 year / 12 months). Same orthodontist (AM) took all impressions.
- **Measurement**: OJ and OB on virtual 3D digital models (DTW lab, iTero scanning).
- **Statistics**: Wilcoxon test for within-group comparisons; significance set at p < 0.05. Data as mean (SD). Between-group difference at T1 reported p = 0.0019.
- **Monitoring**: control-group patients checked every 30 days to optimize device execution.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- At **T0**, OJ and OB were similar in both groups (groups had the same orthodontic features at baseline).
- At **T1 (12 months)**, both OJ and OB were **significantly lower for the Eptamed EQ Series II device than for the Occlus-o-Guide device (p = 0.0019)**.
- Both techniques improved OJ and OB between T0 and T1; the test group (EPTAMED) showed a statistically significant improvement in values.
- Numeric per-group means (SD) for OJ and OB at T0/T1 are presented only in the paper's Fig. (figure) — the full-text body reports the statistical comparison (p = 0.0019) and the direction of effect, not the exact mm values in-text. (The figure was not transcribed in the available PMC text export.)
- Reported secondary observation: removable elastodontic appliances permit good home oral hygiene; oral-microbiota alterations are registered ~1 month after start but impact oral bacteria less than fixed devices.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Small sample size** (60 patients, 30 per arm) and short **12-month** evaluation period (authors' stated limitations).
- Exact mm OJ/OB values reside in the figure, not the running text — magnitude of reduction not quantified numerically in the transcribed full text.
- Not randomized in the formal RCT sense (parallel test/control allocation under a per-patient treatment plan); single-center, single-clinician.
- Authors call for replication with a larger and more homogeneous cohort.

## 7. Glossary

- **Elastodontic device / appliance**: prefabricated elastomeric (silicone) myofunctional appliance worn (often overnight) that uses muscle-generated elastic forces to intercept malocclusion and rebalance the neuromuscular/stomatognathic system.
- **OJ (Overjet, OV in text)**: horizontal (antero-posterior) overlap of maxillary over mandibular central incisors; OJ ≥ 5 mm associated with Class II.
- **OB (Overbite)**: vertical (supero-inferior) overlap of maxillary over mandibular central incisors; ideal ~2 mm, deep bite ≥ 5 mm.
- **EQ Series II (Eptamed)**: medium-hardness elastodontic Class II device (test group), mouthguard-like, sized by inter-first-premolar palatal-cusp distance.
- **Occlus-o-Guide (Sweden & Martina)**: G-type EGA (Eruptive Guidance Appliance), preformed elastomeric activator for mixed dentition (control group).
- **EGA (Eruptive Guidance Appliance)**: positioner-type appliance correcting overbite and guiding posterior eruption while controlling crowding.
- **IOTN (Index of Orthodontic Treatment Need)**: Brook & Shaw index used here for case selection (excluded if > 4).
- **iTero**: intraoral scanner used to convert plaster models to 3D virtual models for digital OJ/OB measurement.
- **Wilcoxon test**: non-parametric within-group comparison test used for T0→T1 changes.
