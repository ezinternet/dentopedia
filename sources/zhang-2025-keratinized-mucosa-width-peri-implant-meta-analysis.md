## Why Ingested

이 SR+MA는 각화점막 폭경(KMW) ≥2 mm이 임플란트 주위 건강에 미치는 효과에 대해 기존의 소규모 메타분석(9개 연구)을 30개 연구로 대폭 확장하여 pooled effect estimate를 제시한다. 기존 [[wiki/implants/peri-implantitis/roccuzzo-2025-keratinized-mucosa-peri-implant-20year-mandible]]가 20년 장기 관찰로 KMW의 역할을 보여주지만 단일 코호트에 제한되는 반면, 이 MA는 4개 데이터베이스에서 추출한 30편을 통해 PI, GI, BI, BL에 대한 정량적 효과 크기를 확립하여 임상 의사결정에 직접 활용 가능한 수치를 제공한다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 30 studies (n=1,773 subjects, 5,241 implants) showing that adequate KMW (≥2 mm) significantly reduces plaque index (MD −0.30), gingival index (MD −0.26), bleeding index (MD −0.20), and marginal bone loss (MD −0.27 mm) compared to KMW <2 mm, while probing depth and CAL differences were non-significant.

## 한줄요약

30개 연구(1,773명, 임플란트 5,241개) 메타분석 결과, 각화점막 폭 ≥2 mm인 임플란트는 <2 mm 대비 치태지수(MD −0.30), 치은지수(MD −0.26), 출혈지수(MD −0.20), 변연골소실(MD −0.27 mm)이 유의하게 낮았으나 탐침깊이·임상부착수준 차이는 유의하지 않았다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Most comprehensive meta-analysis to date on KMW and peri-implant outcomes: 30 studies vs. 9 in the prior leading MA (Ravida 2022)
2. First MA to report pooled GI values for KMW comparison — prior MA (Ravida 2022) did not include GI
3. Quantified effect of KMW ≥2 mm on bone loss with trim-and-fill correction confirming robustness
4. Subgroup analyses across study design (prospective/cross-sectional/retrospective), systemic disease status, implant position, follow-up duration, and NOS quality score
5. Sensitivity analyses showed stability for all significant outcomes (PI, GI, BI, BL) when any single study was omitted
6. Confirmed PD was not significantly different between KMW groups after trim-and-fill adjustment raised the effect to MD −0.14 (95% CI −0.28 to −0.004), borderline non-significant

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**PECO framework**
- Population: patients with dental implants
- Exposure: KMW <2 mm (inadequate)
- Comparison: KMW ≥2 mm (adequate)
- Outcomes: plaque index (PI), gingival index (GI), bleeding index (BI), probing depth (PD), clinical attachment level (CAL), bone loss (BL)

**Search strategy**
- Databases: PubMed, Web of Science, CNKI, VIP Data; inception through May 2024
- Languages: English and Chinese
- Initial hits: 2,028 records; after deduplication 1,451; after title/abstract screening 51; after full-text review 30 included (κ=0.727 for full-text)

**Inclusion criteria**
- Human clinical studies (prospective cohort, retrospective cohort, or cross-sectional)
- At least one peri-implant clinical index measured
- KMW threshold ≥2 mm vs. <2 mm grouping
- Minimum 6-month follow-up

**Quality assessment**: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS) — mean score 6.43; 14 studies had low risk of bias (≥7 stars), 16 high risk of bias (4–6 stars); inter-reviewer κ=0.732

**Statistical model**: random-effects model used for all outcomes given significant heterogeneity; MD with 95% CI as effect measure; Q test and I² for heterogeneity; Begg's and Egger's tests for publication bias; trim-and-fill for adjustment

**Included study characteristics**
- 30 studies: 15 prospective cohort, 13 cross-sectional, 2 retrospective
- Published 1994–2024
- 1,773 total subjects (range 15–130 per study)
- 5,241 total implants
- Smoker prevalence 2.9–28% where reported (8 studies)
- Follow-up: 6 months to 25 years
- KMW measured predominantly at mid-buccal aspect

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Meta-analysis of clinical indices (plaque/inflammation)

| Outcome | Studies (n) | MD | 95% CI | I² | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Plaque Index (PI) | 28 | −0.30 | −0.42 to −0.17 | 95.5% | P<0.05 |
| Gingival Index (GI) | 18 | −0.26 | −0.38 to −0.13 | 91.5% | P<0.05 |
| Bleeding Index (BI) | 19 | −0.20 | −0.33 to −0.07 | 97.0% | P<0.05 |

All three indices favored adequate KMW (≥2 mm). Sensitivity analysis confirmed stability; removing any single study did not materially change results.

### Meta-analysis of clinical parameters (tissue destruction)

| Outcome | Studies (n) | MD | 95% CI | I² | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Probing Depth (PD) | 29 | −0.03 | −0.16 to +0.10 | 83.1% | NS |
| CAL/AL | 5 | −0.09 | −0.22 to +0.04 | — | NS |
| Bone Loss (BL) | 14 | −0.27 | −0.42 to −0.12 | 80.8% | P<0.05 |

BL was significantly reduced with adequate KMW; trim-and-fill analysis confirmed no change to the BL estimate (0 studies trimmed). For PD, trim-and-fill identified 4 potentially missing studies, shifting the adjusted MD to −0.14 (95% CI −0.28 to −0.004), now borderline non-significant.

### Publication bias summary

| Outcome | Studies trimmed | Adjusted 95% CI | Egger's P |
|---|---|---|---|
| PI | 0/28 | −0.42 to −0.17 | 0.65 |
| GI | 0/18 | −0.38 to −0.13 | 0.81 |
| BI | 4/19 | −0.43 to −0.19 | 0.12 |
| PD | 4/29 | −0.28 to −0.004 | 0.53 |
| AL | 0/5 | −0.22 to +0.04 | 0.62 |
| BL | 0/14 | −0.42 to −0.12 | 0.01* |

*Egger's test significant for BL, but trim-and-fill found 0 missing studies — no correction needed.

### Subgroup analyses

Both prospective cohort and cross-sectional study subgroups showed significantly lower PI, GI, BI, and BL in adequate KMW implants. Retrospective studies did not reach significance for PI and GI. Subgroup with follow-up >3 years lost significance for GI and BI. PD and BL showed significant association with KMW only in high-quality studies (NOS ≥7).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

1. **Language restriction**: only English- and Chinese-language studies included; potential exclusion of relevant literature in other languages
2. **High heterogeneity**: I² >80% for all outcomes; driven by variation in study design, implant system, patient population, outcome measurement methods, and follow-up duration
3. **Measurement variability**: KMW measured at different reference points (mid-buccal, mid-lingual, average of multiple sites) across studies
4. **Confounders**: most studies did not separately report smoker/non-smoker subgroups; cannot isolate smoking effect
5. **Study quality**: 16/30 studies had high risk of bias (NOS 4–6); mean NOS 6.43 — below the ≥7 threshold for low risk
6. **Publication bias**: significant Egger's test for BL (P=0.01), though trim-and-fill did not identify missing studies
7. **Future**: authors call for prospective clinical studies with homogeneous case definitions and standardized analysis methods

## 6. Related Work

- Ravida et al. 2022 (Clin Implant Dent Relat Res): prior leading meta-analysis on KMW, 9 studies, significant for PI only; BL and PD non-significant — this MA expands to 30 studies and adds GI, BI, BL significance
- Mahardawi et al. 2023: meta-analysis linking lack of KMW to peri-implantitis defined by BL ≥2 mm
- Lang & Loe 1972: foundational paper establishing ≥2 mm KMW threshold for periodontal health around natural teeth
- Gharpure et al. 2021 (J Periodontol): thin gingival phenotype and KMW <2 mm as risk indicators for peri-implant disease
- Mericske-Stern 1994: earliest included study (prospective, 5-year, mandibular implants)

## 7. Glossary

- **KMW (Keratinized Mucosa Width)**: 각화점막 폭경; width of the keratinized tissue band around implants, measured in mm
- **PI (Plaque Index)**: 치태지수; index scoring plaque accumulation around implant abutments/crowns
- **GI (Gingival Index)**: 치은지수; index scoring gingival inflammation severity
- **BI (Bleeding Index)**: 출혈지수; index scoring bleeding on probing at peri-implant sites
- **PD (Probing Depth)**: 탐침깊이; depth of the peri-implant sulcus/pocket measured in mm
- **CAL/AL (Clinical Attachment Level / Attachment Loss)**: 임상부착수준/부착소실
- **BL (Bone Loss)**: 변연골소실; marginal bone loss around implants measured radiographically in mm
- **MD (Mean Difference)**: 평균차; difference in means between adequate and inadequate KMW groups
- **NOS (Newcastle-Ottawa Scale)**: 뉴캐슬-오타와 척도; quality assessment tool for observational studies (0–9 stars)
- **PECO**: Population-Exposure-Comparison-Outcome framework for clinical question formulation in meta-analysis
- **Trim-and-fill**: statistical method to correct for publication bias by imputing potentially missing studies
