## One-line Summary
Triple-blinded RCT (n=26 women, same cohort as the temporalis-focused companion paper) showing BoNT-A is effective for masseter hypertrophy but **repeated injections cause persistent decreases in masseter thickness, EMG activity, and masticatory performance** — i.e. clinically relevant functional adverse effects.

## 1. Document Information
- Study type: Randomized, triple-blinded, placebo-controlled clinical trial (primary masseter-outcome analysis)
- Setting: ABO-Goiás dental clinic, Goiânia, Brazil; 2 Mar – 17 Oct 2023
- Ethics: Uningá University Research Ethics Committee (CAAE: 63135022.3.0000.5220)
- Registry: Brazilian Registry of Clinical Trials RBR-7tdjcn5 (24/01/2024)
- Reporting: CONSORT, Helsinki Declaration
- Primary outcome: Masseter muscle thickness on ultrasound

## 2. Key Contributions
1. Confirms BoNT-A effectiveness for MH on subjective Merz scale, but **with measurable functional cost**.
2. Distinguishes single vs multiple-injection effects on the masseter itself (single = transient ↓; multiple = persistent ↓).
3. Quantifies **masticatory performance decline** (color-mixing method, ViewGum) — present in both groups across all follow-ups.
4. Provides the masseter-side dataset that pairs with the temporalis-side companion paper (de Souza Nobre et al. 2024, *Aesthetic Plast Surg*).
5. Frames the central clinical question: *"Is it worthwhile to affect such an important muscle as the masseter muscle solely for aesthetic purposes?"*

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Population, intervention, randomization, blinding, statistics: identical to the companion paper (de Souza Nobre 2024, Aesth Plast Surg) — same 26 patients, same protocol. See that source for full design details.
- Distinct outcome instruments here:
  - **Masseter muscle thickness** (US, MVC, mid-level between zygomatic arch and gonial angle, close to occlusal plane) — primary outcome.
  - **Masseter EMG** (Miotool NG USB, RMS over 5 s MVC × 3, bipolar Ag-AgCl on masseter using same anatomical references as injection sites, sternal reference).
  - **Masticatory performance**: 20-cycle bicolor chewing-gum test (Vivident Fruitswing Karpuz), 1 mm flattening, 300 dpi scan, ViewGum colorimetric Variance of Hue (VOH) — higher VOH = worse mixing.
  - Masseter prominence: 10-point Merz photonumeric scale (rest and contracted).
- Group naming differs from the companion paper: **G1 = single (saline at 3 mo)**, **G2 = multiple (BoNT-A at 3 mo)**. (In the temporalis paper they are S-BoNT-A and M-BoNT-A.)
- Sample-size assumption: 25 ± 8 % thickness change → 9/group + 4 dropouts buffer = 13/group, 26 total.
- Completers: G1 n=10, G2 n=12.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
### Right masseter thickness (MVC, mm)
- G1: baseline 14.29 ± 1.73 → 1 mo 12.92 ± 2.61 → 3 mo 13.81 ± 1.74 → 6 mo 14.10 ± 1.85.
- G2: baseline 14.73 ± 2.14 → 1 mo 13.68 ± 2.31 → 3 mo 14.47 ± 2.10 → 6 mo 13.94 ± 1.61.
- Intra-group: both significantly ↓ at 1 mo vs baseline (p<0.05). No significant inter-group differences at any timepoint.

### Left masseter thickness (MVC, mm)
- G1: 13.95 ± 1.67 → 13.05 ± 2.30 → 14.56 ± 2.19 → 15.20 ± 1.45.
- G2: 14.68 ± 2.23 → 13.24 ± 2.81 → 15.00 ± 1.14 → 13.90 ± 2.45.
- Intra-group: both ↓ at 1 mo (p<0.05); G1 then ↑ from 1 mo to 6 mo (p<0.01).
- Inter-group: **G2 < G1 at 6 mo (p<0.02)** — multiple injections produced persistent thinning.

### Right masseter EMG (RMS μV, MVC)
- G1: 273.72 ± 174.64 (baseline) → 48.63 ± 20.29 (1 mo) → 88.39 ± 48.89 (3 mo) → 178.83 ± 93.15 (6 mo).
- G2: 226.40 ± 103.09 → 35.89 ± 15.69 → 83.99 ± 42.39 → 81.77 ± 48.69.
- Intra-group: G1 ↓ at 1 mo (p<0.02), then partial recovery; G2 ↓ at all follow-ups (p<0.001) and only modest recovery between 1 and 6 mo (p<0.044).
- Inter-group: **G1 > G2 at 6 mo (p<0.005)** — multiple injections suppress masseter EMG more durably.

### Left masseter EMG (RMS μV, MVC)
- G1: 243.23 ± 145.11 → 49.44 ± 51.85 → 78.23 ± 58.86 → 163.12 ± 118.70.
- G2: 267.54 ± 117.01 → 40.38 ± 15.98 → 91.21 ± 34.16 → 74.49 ± 48.29.
- Inter-group: G1 > G2 at 6 mo (p<0.044).

### Masticatory performance (Variance of Hue, higher = worse)
- G1: baseline 0.06 ± 0.02 → 1 mo 0.27 ± 0.17 → 3 mo 0.18 ± 0.12 → 6 mo 0.34 ± 0.06.
- G2: baseline 0.05 ± 0.01 → 1 mo 0.16 ± 0.13 → 3 mo 0.24 ± 0.17 → 6 mo 0.32 ± 0.13.
- Both groups significantly worse than baseline at all follow-ups (p<0.03). No inter-group difference.

### Masseter prominence (Merz, 10-point)
- G1 improvement vs baseline: 3 mo (p<0.02), 6 mo (p<0.01) in rest; no change in contracted.
- G2: significant improvement at all follow-ups in both rest (p<0.02) and contracted (p<0.0001).
- Inter-group: G2 superior to G1 only at 1 mo in both rest (p<0.020) and contracted (p<0.036).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Only women included; cultural/aesthetic framing biases generalization.
- Single brand (Dysport / abobotulinumtoxinA); requires bridging to onabotulinumtoxinA equivalents.
- 6-month observation window cannot capture long-term outcomes such as bony changes, fatty infiltration, or recovery after stopping.
- Masticatory performance test (color-mixing) reflects mixing efficiency but not bite force; complementary bite-force / occlusal force studies still needed.
- No assessment of patient satisfaction, psychological impact, or quality of life.
- Possible Hawthorne effect since the trial setting is overtly aesthetic.

## 6. Related Work
- Companion paper, same cohort: de Souza Nobre et al. *Aesthetic Plast Surg* 2024;48:3979-3987 (DOI 10.1007/s00266-024-04064-4) — temporalis outcomes (thickness, EMG, pain).
- Refs [9–11, 24]: prior efficacy trials of BoNT-A for MH establishing single-dose efficacy.
- Refs [14–17]: animal studies on muscle-fiber composition changes, atrophy markers, and fatty replacement after repeated BoNT-A.
- Ref [22]: prior study showing up to 20 % bite-force reduction with BoNT-A masseter injection.
- Ref [24]: long-term observational study showing masseter thickness ↓ persisting 4 years after last injection with repeat dosing.

## 7. Glossary
- **MH** — Masseter Hypertrophy.
- **BoNT-A** — Botulinum Toxin Type A.
- **abobotulinumtoxinA** — Dysport (Ipsen); equivalence ~2.5 U Dysport ≈ 1 U Botox.
- **MVC** — Maximum Voluntary Contraction.
- **Variance of Hue (VOH)** — color-mixing-based masticatory-performance metric from bicolor-gum test; ViewGum software.
- **Merz scale** — 10-point photonumeric masseter prominence scale (female-specific).
- **G1 / G2** — single / multiple injection groups (paper-specific naming).
- **Atrogin-1, MuRF-1** — molecular markers of muscle atrophy.
- **Type IIa → IIb fiber shift** — anti-fatigue (IIa) to non-anti-fatigue (IIb) shift seen after repeated chemodenervation in animal models.
