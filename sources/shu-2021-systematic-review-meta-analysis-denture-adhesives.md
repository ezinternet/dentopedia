## Why Ingested

의치접착제 (Denture Adhesive, DA)의 임상적 효능에 대한 체계적 근거가 필요하여 수집. 기존 wiki의 완전의치 카테고리에 OVD/VD 관련 논문은 있으나 의치접착제 관련 SR+MA는 없었음. 본 논문은 39개 RCT/CCT를 메타분석하여 DA의 유지력·저작력·저작효율 개선 효과를 정량화한 최초의 RCT/CCT 한정 SR+MA로, [[complete-denture/alhajj-2017-occlusal-vertical-dimension-complete-denture-review]]와 같이 완전의치 임상 의사결정을 보완한다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (39 studies, n=~1200) demonstrating that denture adhesives significantly improve retention (SMD 1.34), bite force (SMD 0.98), and masticatory performance (SMD 0.72) in complete denture wearers.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰 및 메타분석 (39개 연구): 의치접착제는 완전의치 착용자의 유지력(SMD 1.34), 최대교합력(SMD 0.98), 저작효율(SMD 0.72)을 유의하게 향상시킴.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of Dentistry
- **Volume/Issue**: Volume 108, May 2021, Article 103638
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.jdent.2021.103638
- **PROSPERO registration**: CRD42020164316
- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis
- **Databases searched**: Medline, Embase, CENTRAL + gray literature (up to March 2020)

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First SR+MA restricted to RCTs and CCTs only (previous 2015 SR included observational studies and mixed prosthesis types)
2. Meta-analysis quantifying DA effect on three primary mastication-related outcomes: retention, bite force, masticatory efficiency
3. Covers all DA types (cream/paste/gel/powder/strip/pad) applied specifically to complete dentures
4. Addresses conflicting trial results through pooled analysis with sensitivity analysis

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Inclusion criteria**: RCTs and CCTs evaluating DAs on complete dentures; primary outcomes = denture retention, maximum bite force, masticatory efficiency (objectively measured)
- **Secondary outcomes**: other objective variables + patient-reported outcomes (OHRQoL, oral microorganisms)
- **Records screened**: 1,729 records after deduplication → 77 full-text → 39 studies (43 articles) included
  - 23 RCTs, 16 CCTs; 2 multicenter trials (1 RCT + 1 CCT)
- **Statistical method**: Standardized mean difference (SMD) with random-effects model; heterogeneity assessed via I²; sensitivity analysis by removing outliers
- **DA types included**: Soluble (cream, paste, gel, powder — active ingredients: carboxymethyl cellulose [CMC] and polyvinyl methyl ether/maleic acid [PVM-MA]) and insoluble (strip, pad, wafer, cushion)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Primary Outcomes (Meta-analysis)

| Outcome | SMD | 95% CI | P-value | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Denture retention | 1.34 | 0.89–1.79 | <0.001 | Large effect |
| Maximum bite force | 0.98 | 0.50–1.47 | <0.001 | Moderate-large effect |
| Masticatory performance | 0.72 | 0.23–1.22 | 0.004 | Moderate effect |

- Retention showed the largest effect size, consistent with the primary mechanism of action of DAs
- Bite force and masticatory performance also significantly improved, though with smaller effect sizes
- Heterogeneity was substantial across studies (attributed to different measuring methods, denture qualities, ridge conditions, and DA types)

### Secondary Outcomes (Narrative)

- Patient-reported outcomes (comfort, confidence, satisfaction) generally improved with DA use
- Oral microorganism data were limited and heterogeneous; no definitive conclusions

### Clinical Significance

- Supports DA use as a conservative, non-invasive adjunct for edentulous patients with declining retention
- Particularly relevant for patients with xerostomia, compromised anatomic structures, or during CD adaptation period
- Addresses frail/handicapped patients for whom frequent dental visits are burdensome

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Focus on CDs only (avoids confounding from different prosthesis types, but limits generalizability to RPD/overdenture users)
- Substantial heterogeneity in meta-analysis (I² not specified in abstract) due to varying DA types, denture quality, ridge conditions, measurement methods
- Outliers retained (sensitivity analyses conducted but all studies kept in primary analysis)
- Only RCTs and CCTs included — excluded observational data that previous reviews incorporated
- Short follow-up in most included trials; long-term microbiome and tissue effects unclear

## 6. Related Work

- Previous SR (2015): included observational studies + mixed prosthesis types; no meta-analysis; concluded DAs improve retention and masticatory performance
- Torres-Sanchez (2017): RCT comparing masticatory efficacy between two DA types vs dentate controls
- Silva (2020): influence of DAs during adaptation period varying ridge resorption degree
- Nishi (2020): multicenter RCT on DA effect on oral moisture

## 7. Glossary

- **DA (Denture Adhesive, 의치접착제)**: commercially available product adhering denture to oral mucosa to improve retention/stability
- **CMC (Carboxymethyl Cellulose, 카복시메틸셀룰로오스)**: polymer salt active ingredient in soluble DAs
- **PVM-MA (Polyvinyl Methyl Ether/Maleic Acid, 폴리비닐메틸에테르/말레산)**: co-polymer active ingredient in soluble DAs
- **SMD (Standardized Mean Difference, 표준화 평균차)**: effect size metric; >0.8 = large, 0.5–0.8 = moderate, <0.5 = small
- **CCT (Controlled Clinical Trial, 대조 임상시험)**: non-randomized controlled clinical trial
- **Masticatory efficiency/performance (저작효율/저작기능)**: objective measurement of food particle size reduction after chewing cycles
- **Xerostomia (구강건조증)**: dry mouth condition reducing salivary lubrication; worsens CD retention
