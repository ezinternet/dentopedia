## Why Ingested

2024년 Clinical Oral Investigations에 게재된 SR+MA로, 구취(halitosis)와 치주염(periodontitis) 간 연관성을 OR로 정량화한 최신 근거. 기존 [[halitosis/silva-2017-periodontitis-associated-with-halitosis]]의 2017 SR+MA를 9편 관찰연구(585 cases, 1591 controls)로 업데이트하며, 측정방법(관능검사 vs VSC 측정)별 subgroup 분석을 추가해 임상적 해석을 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 9 observational studies (585 cases, 1591 controls) found a significant positive association between halitosis and periodontitis, with OR ~4 for both organoleptic and VSC-based assessments.

## 한줄요약

9편 관찰연구(585 증례, 1591 대조)의 SR+MA로, 구취와 치주염은 유의한 양의 연관성(OR≈4, 관능검사·VSC 측정 모두)을 보이나 이질성이 높아 추가 연구가 필요하다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First dedicated SR+MA focused specifically on the halitosis–periodontitis association using observational study design
- Quantified the association using OR with 95% CI across 9 eligible studies
- Performed subgroup analyses by halitosis assessment method: organoleptic test (OLT) vs volatile sulfur compound (VSC) measurement
- Identified statistically significant association in both subgroups
- Updated and extended prior SR+MA by Silva et al. (2017) with more recent evidence

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search period**: Up to August 18, 2023
- **Databases**: PubMed, Web of Science, Embase, Scopus, Cochrane Library
- **Inclusion criteria**: Observational studies reporting halitosis-periodontitis association
- **Studies included**: 9 observational studies
- **Sample**: 585 cases (halitosis), 1591 controls
- **Effect measure**: Odds ratios (ORs) with 95% confidence intervals (CIs)
- **Heterogeneity**: I² assessed; high heterogeneity noted
- **Subgroup analyses**: By halitosis assessment method (organoleptic vs VSC)
- **Risk of bias**: Assessed using standard tools for observational studies

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Overall association**: Positive association between halitosis and periodontitis (statistically significant)
- **Organoleptic test subgroup**: OR = 4.05 (95% CI: 1.76–9.30, p < 0.01)
- **VSC reading subgroup**: OR = 4.52 (95% CI: 1.89–10.83, p < 0.01)
- Both assessment methods showed comparable effect sizes (~4-fold increased odds)
- Significant heterogeneity across studies

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- High statistical heterogeneity limits the strength of conclusions
- Only observational studies included — causality cannot be established
- Diverse study populations, periodontal classification systems, and halitosis measurement protocols across studies
- Self-reported halitosis measures not consistently distinguished from objective measures
- Authors call for additional well-designed prospective studies to clarify the relationship

## 7. Glossary

- **Halitosis**: Oral malodor; unpleasant odor emanating from the mouth
- **Organoleptic test (OLT)**: Sensory assessment of breath odor by a trained examiner using a scoring scale (e.g., 0–5 Rosenberg scale)
- **Volatile Sulfur Compounds (VSC)**: Hydrogen sulfide (H₂S), methyl mercaptan (CH₃SH), dimethyl sulfide — primary chemical mediators of intra-oral halitosis, produced by bacterial proteolysis
- **Odds Ratio (OR)**: Measure of association in observational studies; OR > 1 indicates increased odds of halitosis in periodontitis patients
- **Heterogeneity (I²)**: Statistical measure of variation across studies in a meta-analysis
- **Periodontitis**: Inflammatory disease of the periodontium caused by dysbiotic microbial biofilm; associated with VSC-producing gram-negative anaerobes
