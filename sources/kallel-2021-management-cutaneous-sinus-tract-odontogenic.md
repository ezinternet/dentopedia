## Why Ingested

[[wiki/endodontics/anatomy/chen-2016-diagnosis-treatment-odontogenic-cutaneous-sinus]]·[[wiki/oral-surgery/bodner-2012-cutaneous-sinus-tract-dental]]의 치성 피부누공(odontogenic cutaneous sinus tract) 클러스터에, **오진→불필요한 피부과 수술·장기 항생제→근관치료로 치유**의 18개월 추적 증례를 추가. 항생제 스튜어드십 관점(불필요한 장기 항생제)과 치근단 병소 진단 양쪽을 보강하는 실증 사례.

## One-line Summary

Case report (n=1, 44-y female, 18-month follow-up): a chronically draining chin lesion repeatedly misdiagnosed and treated with cryotherapy, surgery, and prolonged antibiotics was actually an odontogenic cutaneous sinus tract; root canal treatment (+ surgery after a 2-week recurrence) resolved it, with clear periapical healing at 18 months — underscoring dentist–physician communication for correct diagnosis.

## 한줄요약

증례 보고(n=1, 44세 여성, 18개월 추적): 만성 배농되는 턱 병변이 반복적으로 오진되어 냉동치료·수술·장기 항생제를 받았으나 실제로는 **치성 피부누공(odontogenic cutaneous sinus tract)**; 근관치료(2주 후 재발로 외과적 처치 추가)로 해소되고 18개월째 치근단 치유 확인 — 정확한 진단을 위한 치과-의과 간 소통의 중요성 강조.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Illustrates the classic diagnostic pitfall: odontogenic cutaneous sinus tract mimics dermatologic/infective lesions (furuncle, osteomyelitis, congenital fistula), with no dental symptoms.
- Documents the harm of misdiagnosis: prior cryotherapy, surgical excision, and prolonged antibiotics targeting the skin — all futile until the dental source was addressed.
- Demonstrates definitive resolution via endodontic treatment (RCT) + surgery for recurrence, with radiographic periapical healing.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Single-patient case report; clinical + radiologic examination established dental origin.
- Treatment sequence: conventional root canal treatment → recurrence of pus discharge at 2 weeks → surgical (periapical) treatment.
- Outcomes assessed at 5 months (healing) and 18 months (sinus tract healing, periapical lesion regression, improved bone trabeculation).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- 5 months post-treatment: obvious healing of the cutaneous lesion.
- 18 months: comfortable patient, significant sinus-tract healing, periapical radiograph shows clear regression of the periapical lesion and improved bone trabeculation.
- Key lesson: correct diagnosis requires dentist–physician communication; the cutaneous lesion may be distant from the underlying tooth.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single case (n=1); no generalizable effect estimate.
- Outcome by radiograph + clinical exam only.

## 7. Glossary

- **Cutaneous sinus tract (odontogenic)**: pathologic canal starting in the oral cavity (periapical infection) but opening on the facial/neck skin.
- **Misdiagnosis dilemma**: mimics furuncle, osteomyelitis, traumatic injury, congenital fistula; often no dental symptoms.
- **Root canal treatment (RCT)**: removes the necrotic pulp source — the curative step here.
- **Periapical healing**: radiographic regression of the periapical radiolucency + bone trabeculation recovery.
