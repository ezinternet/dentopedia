## Why Ingested

Desquamative gingivitis (DG) is the gingival manifestation of mucocutaneous diseases — oral lichen planus (OLP), mucous membrane pemphigoid (MMP), and pemphigus vulgaris (PV) — which together account for 88–98% of DG cases. This SR (15 studies, MMAT mean 5.57) establishes the pharmacological treatment evidence base — first-line agents, dosing regimens, adverse-event profiles — complementing the wiki's existing mucocutaneous-disease pages. It extends [[oral-medicine/morais-2024-pemphigus-vulgaris-mucous-membrane-pemphigoid-oral-sr]] (PV/MMP SR) by providing the DG-specific treatment lens, and reinforces [[oral-medicine/lodi-2020-oral-lichen-planus-corticosteroids-cochrane]] (OLP corticosteroid Cochrane review) by independently confirming clobetasol and tacrolimus as the dominant agents across the entire DG spectrum regardless of underlying diagnosis.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review of 15 studies (2 RCTs, 5 NRCTs, 8 observational; MMAT mean 5.57) on pharmacological management of desquamative gingivitis: clobetasol propionate 0.05% (used in 53.3% of studies) and tacrolimus 0.1% are established first-line agents producing significant pain and lesion-severity reduction; topical routes carry mild/transient AEs; systemic agents carry greater risk (candidiasis, haemolysis, anaemia, alopecia); PRP and propolis/nano-vitamin gels are emerging adjuncts; QoL formally assessed in only 1 study (OHIP-14).

## 한줄요약

탈락성 치은염 (Desquamative Gingivitis, DG)의 약물치료에 관한 15개 연구(RCT 2건 포함) 체계적 문헌고찰: 클로베타솔 0.05%(53.3%의 연구에 사용)와 타크로리무스 0.1%가 1차 치료제로 유효하며 통증·병변 중증도의 유의한 감소를 보임; 국소도포는 경미·일시적 부작용, 전신투여는 칸디다증·용혈·빈혈 등 더 높은 위험; PRP 및 프로폴리스/나노비타민 젤은 보조치료로 유망; 삶의 질은 1개 연구에서만 OHIP-14로 정식 평가됨.

- **Authors**: Bandara DL, Padmakumari KMC, Jayasinghe YA, Peiris PM, et al.
- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health
- **Year**: 2025 (published online 2025-07-02)
- **DOI**: 10.1186/s12903-025-06369-5
- **PMID**: 40604637 | **PMCID**: PMC12220169
- **PROSPERO registration**: CRD42024556589
- **Study type**: Systematic review (PRISMA-adherent; Cochrane Handbook followed; MMAT v2018 quality appraisal)
- **Search period**: January 1990 – December 2023 across 7 databases (PubMed, SCOPUS, CINAHL Complete, CINAHL Ultimate, Dentistry and Oral Sciences Source, AMED, Web of Science)
- **Final inclusion**: 15 studies (14 from database search + 1 from reference list hand-searching)
- **MMAT quality**: Mean score 5.57/7; all studies rated "above average" (scores 5–7); 2 RCTs scored 7/7 and 6/7 respectively

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First comprehensive SR specifically addressing pharmacological efficacy AND safety for DG as a clinical entity (not restricted to a single underlying disease).
2. Establishes clobetasol propionate 0.05% (topical) and tacrolimus 0.1% (topical) as dual first-line agents — both validated across OLP, MMP, and PV presentations of DG.
3. Provides the first systematic evidence synthesis for emerging DG pharmacotherapies: heterologous PRP and propolis + nano-vitamin C + nano-vitamin E bio-adhesive gels.
4. Identifies a critical gap: QoL formally assessed in only 1/15 studies (OHIP-14; González-Serrano 2023); clinically meaningful outcomes beyond pain and lesion size are largely unmeasured.
5. Highlights that systemic treatments carry substantially higher AE burdens (candidiasis, haemolysis from SMXP, anaemia and alopecia from immunosuppressants) compared to the mild/transient profile of topical agents.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Study design breakdown (15 included)**:
- 2 RCTs (13.3%): González-Serrano 2023 (Spain; n=22), Corrocher 2006 (Italy; n=24)
- 5 NRCTs (33.3%): Torrezani 2024, Kavlakova 2022, Motta 2009, Guiglia 2007, Thornhill 2000
- 8 observational (53.3%): retrospective/prospective cohort, cross-sectional analytic, retrospective case series

**Population**: Adult patients (mean ages 42–62 years, majority ~60 years); strong female predominance (221 F vs 53 M across studies). Age range 21–90 years. Underlying diagnoses: OLP, MMP, PV, plasma cell gingivitis, erythema multiforme.

**Diagnostic criteria**: All 15 studies used clinical + histological criteria; 9/15 (60%) also used direct immunofluorescence (DIF); only 1 study cited standardized guidelines (2016 AAOMP for OLP; 2015 WWOM for MMP).

**PICO framework**:
- P: patients with DG (clinically/histologically confirmed)
- I: any pharmacological agent
- C: no treatment / placebo
- O: pain reduction, gingival improvement, QoL, recurrence, adverse effects

**Quality appraisal**: MMAT v2018 (5 design-specific questions + 2 screening questions; range 0–7). Scores: RCTs mean 6.5, NRCTs mean 5.75, descriptive/observational mean 6.17. Common weakness in non-randomized studies: failure to account for confounders (33.3% of studies rated "No" on confounder criterion).

**Data synthesis**: Narrative synthesis (meta-analysis not feasible due to high heterogeneity in agents, dosages, outcome measures, follow-up durations).

**Search completeness**: 624 records retrieved → 239 duplicates removed → 368 screened → 17 full-text assessed → 14 met criteria → 1 added by hand-search → 15 final.

**Geographical distribution**: Europe (Italy: 5; Spain: 2; UK: 1; Bulgaria: 1; Norway: 1), South America (Brazil: 2; one Brazil with Italy collaboration), Iran: 1. No studies from Africa, Australia, Oceania.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Most studied pharmacological agents

| Agent | Studies (n) | % of total |
|---|---|---|
| Clobetasol propionate 0.05% | 8 | 53.3% |
| Tacrolimus 0.1% | 4 | 26.7% |
| Systemic corticosteroids/immunosuppressants | ~3–4 | (adjunct/severe cases) |
| Sulphamethoxypyridazine (SMXP) | 1 | — |
| Fusidic acid 2% | 1 | — |
| Tetracycline/doxycycline | 2 (as adjunct or standalone) | — |
| PRP (heterologous) | 1 | — |
| Propolis + nano-vitamin gel | 1 | — |
| Sodium iodide + salicylic acid | 1 | — |

### Efficacy — key quantitative findings

**Clobetasol propionate 0.05%**:
- Gonzalez-Moles 2003: 100% complete pain remission; 93.9% resumed normal daily activities (95% CI 79.8–99.3%)
- Kavlakova 2022: VAS pain 7.80±1.22 → 3.30±1.94 (57% reduction in 3 weeks)
- Guiglia 2007: 22.94% reduction in bleeding-on-probing sites (p<0.0001)
- Motta 2009: symptom improvement in 77.2% vs 63.6% for placebo

**Tacrolimus 0.1%**:
- Kavlakova 2022: VAS pain 6.70±3.02 → 0.70±1.76 (74% reduction); not significantly different from clobetasol (p=0.435)
- Torrezani 2024 (custom tray): 60% pain reduction, 65.5% burning sensation reduction (p<0.01); 30.76% complete response, 69.24% partial remission
- Corrocher 2006 (RCT): tacrolimus achieved erythema/desquamation remission in 91.7% at week 4 (vs 0% for clobetasol in this study)

**PRP (heterologous, Gabusi 2024)**:
- VAS pain 6.4 → 2.0 (mean reduction 4.4±2, p<0.05 by Wilcoxon signed-rank test)
- Oral Disease Severity Score (ODSS) reduced 8.2±5.3 (p<0.05); 89% of patients showed improvement

**Propolis + nano-vitamin gel (González-Serrano 2023, RCT)**:
- Significant OHIP-14 improvement (p=0.002); physical pain (p=0.001), psychological disability (p=0.009), social disability (p=0.003)
- DG Clinical Score (DGCS) significant reduction — but not statistically significantly different from control gel at 4 weeks

**Fusidic acid 2% (Leite 2015)**:
- Pain score 5.4±1.12 → 1.16±0.97 at 6 weeks (sustained to 2.11±0.98 at 12 months)
- Pain frequency (>3×/week) reduced from 53.33% to 13.33%
- Lesion size 72.47% reduction at 6 weeks; 60.12% reduction sustained at 12-month follow-up
- No significant recurrence at 12 months

**Systemic SMXP (Thornhill 2000)**:
- 62% complete gingival resolution; 65% complete oral ulceration resolution; significant pain improvement (p<0.001)
- Used for patients previously unresponsive to topical steroids for MMP

**Rituximab (Balighi 2022 — pemphigus with gingival involvement)**:
- PDAI (gingiva) reduced from 4.76±0.74 to 3.26±0.63 at 6 months
- Complete resolution: 12.5%; partial resolution: 45.83%

### Safety profiles

| Agent | Key adverse effects |
|---|---|
| Clobetasol (topical) | Oral candidiasis, xerostomia (prolonged use) |
| Tacrolimus (topical) | Burning sensation, altered taste, oral candidiasis, abdominal discomfort |
| SMXP (systemic) | Haemolysis (mild to significant; treatment cessation in severe cases) |
| Fusidic acid (topical) | Allergic reaction with mucosal ulceration (1 case) |
| Tetracycline/doxycycline (systemic) | Abdominal discomfort, 1 oral candidiasis case |
| Systemic immunosuppressants (prednisone, azathioprine, MMF, methotrexate, rituximab) | Oral candidiasis, anaemia (dapsone/IVIG), alopecia (MMF) |
| Clobetasol + nystatin combination | No adverse effects; prevents secondary candidiasis |
| PRP | No adverse effects reported |
| Propolis + nano-vitamin gel | No adverse effects reported |

### QoL outcomes

- Only 1 study used a validated QoL instrument (OHIP-14): González-Serrano 2023
- Leite 2015 reported indirect QoL improvement (emotional well-being, social interactions) without validated tools
- Gonzalez-Moles 2003: 93.9% resumed daily activities (proxy for QoL)
- 13/15 studies did not formally assess QoL with validated tools

### Recurrence

- Clobetasol: Low recurrence; Kavlakova 2022 reported 0 relapses in clobetasol group vs 30% in tacrolimus group at 3 months
- Tacrolimus (Kavlakova 2022): 30% relapse rate within 3 months
- Fusidic acid (Leite 2015): No significant recurrence at 12 months
- 60% of studies did not report recurrence data

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

**Study-level limitations**:
- Heterogeneous study designs (2 RCTs only), small sample sizes, short follow-up durations
- Inconsistent diagnostic protocols: varied use of DIF, minimal reference to standardized guidelines
- Non-randomized studies did not account for confounders (33.3% rated "No" on confounder criterion)
- Varied outcome measures limit cross-study comparability

**SR-level limitations**:
- No meta-analysis possible (high clinical and methodological heterogeneity)
- Geographical gap: no studies from Africa, Australia, Oceania; predominantly European
- QoL measured in only 1/15 studies with validated instrument
- Emerging therapies (PRP, propolis gels) have very limited evidence base for long-term effectiveness

**Future research priorities**:
1. Larger multi-centre RCTs with standardized DG diagnostic protocols (DIF mandatory)
2. Validated QoL tools (OHIP-14 minimum) with extended follow-up (≥12 months)
3. Standardized recurrence assessment criteria
4. Studies in under-represented regions (Africa, Asia, Oceania)
5. Long-term safety and effectiveness of nanotechnology-based therapies and PRP

## 6. Related Work

- Cabras et al. 2019 SR (restricted to RCTs only; emphasized plaque control + topical corticosteroids)
- Scribante et al. 2023 scoping review (oral hygiene and autoimmune gingival diseases; patient-reported outcomes)
- Recent SR on DG treatment protocols (2024): emphasized personalized approach based on diagnosis
- No international guidelines dedicated specifically to DG as a distinct clinical entity; management derives from underlying OLP/MMP/PV guidelines

## 7. Glossary

- **DG (Desquamative Gingivitis, 탈락성 치은염)**: Clinical observation (not a diagnosis) of chronic epithelial loss/desquamation, erosion, and erythema of the gingiva; 88–98% of cases are caused by OLP, MMP, or PV
- **OLP (Oral Lichen Planus, 구강 편평 태선)**: Chronic inflammatory mucocutaneous condition; most common cause of DG
- **MMP (Mucous Membrane Pemphigoid, 점막 유사천포창)**: Autoimmune subepithelial blistering disease; second most common DG cause
- **PV (Pemphigus Vulgaris, 천포창)**: Autoimmune intraepithelial blistering disease; third DG cause
- **DIF (Direct Immunofluorescence, 직접 면역형광법)**: Gold standard for differentiating MMP from PV and OLP; used in 9/15 studies
- **Clobetasol propionate 0.05% (클로베타솔 프로피오네이트)**: Superpotent topical corticosteroid; most studied DG agent (53.3% of studies)
- **Tacrolimus 0.1% (타크로리무스)**: Topical calcineurin inhibitor; second most studied DG agent (26.7% of studies); used via ointment or custom trays
- **PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma, 혈소판 풍부 혈장)**: Emerging adjunct; heterologous application weekly for 2 months; no AEs reported
- **SMXP (Sulphamethoxypyridazine, 설파메톡시피리다진)**: Long-acting sulfonamide antibiotic; for topical-steroid-unresponsive MMP; haemolysis risk
- **MMAT (Mixed Methods Appraisal Tool)**: Quality appraisal tool for diverse study designs; 0–7 scale; ≤3.5 below average, =3.5 average, >3.5 above average
- **OHIP-14 (Oral Health Impact Profile-14)**: Validated 14-item QoL instrument measuring oral-health-related quality of life
- **VAS (Visual Analog Scale, 시각 아날로그 척도)**: 0–10 pain assessment scale used across most included studies
- **PDAI (Pemphigus Disease Area Index)**: Validated tool for quantifying pemphigus lesion extent; used in Balighi 2022
- **DGCS (Desquamative Gingivitis Clinical Score)**: Composite clinical scoring tool for DG severity
- **ODSS (Oral Disease Severity Score)**: Used in Gabusi 2024 to quantify lesion burden in MMP
- **PCG (Plasma Cell Gingivitis, 형질세포 치은염)**: Non-immune-mediated DG cause; distinguished by histology
