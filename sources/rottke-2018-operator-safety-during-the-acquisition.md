## Why Ingested

Quantifies the operator-side radiation dose and scatter geometry of a handheld/portable intraoral X-ray unit (Aribex NOMAD Pro 2), extending the patient-centred shielding evidence in [[radiology/benavides-2023-patient-shielding-dentomaxillofacial-radiography]] to the occupational (operator) side of dentomaxillofacial radiation protection. Pairs with the EADMFR handheld-device position paper being ingested concurrently to anchor a handheld-intraoral-X-ray safety cluster.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro phantom dosimetry of the Aribex NOMAD Pro 2 handheld intraoral X-ray showing operator scatter dose is negligible behind the device and that the control area (max 42 cm, mean 16.7 cm at 4000 simulated exposures) is far smaller than European/international standards specify, provided manufacturer instructions are followed. ## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides direct, instrumented measurement of operator-side radiation exposure for a handheld intraoral X-ray device, addressing the common occupational-safety concern that holding/operating an X-ray source in-room exposes the operator.
- Maps the **control area (CA)** spatial extent for the device and compares it against European and international radiation-protection standards, finding the real-world CA substantially smaller than regulatory specifications.
- Characterizes the scatter-radiation pattern around the phantom–device geometry, identifying that scatter concentrates between the phantom and the X-ray tube, with none detected on the operator side of a vertical plane through the focal spot or at the dorsal phantom region.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Equipment: Aribex NOMAD Pro 2 handheld intraoral X-ray device; RANDO male head phantom; a consistency-testing body; PTW NOMEX Multimeter; PTW Farmer Ionization Chamber Type 30010.
- Four investigated quantities: (1) dose–area products (DAP) across exposure times; (2) expansion/extent of the control area (CA); (3) the scattering pattern around the phantom–device geometry; (4) the potential operator risk.
- Up to 4000 simulated exposures were used to characterize the maximal control-area extent.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Dose–area products**: distributed linearly with exposure time, correlation factor > 0.9.
- **Control area (CA)** at 4000 simulated exposures: greatest extent 42 cm; mean 16.7 cm; SD 10.8 cm — significantly smaller than European/other international radiation-protection standards specify.
- **Scatter pattern**: highest scatter occurred between the RANDO phantom and the X-ray device; **no scattered radiation** measured at the dorsal part of the phantom or on the operator site of a virtual vertical plane through the focal spot.
- **Conclusion**: the Aribex NOMAD Pro 2 does not increase operator risk when operated per manufacturer specifications.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro phantom study — no in-vivo operator dosimetry (e.g., personal dosimeter badges on real operators across a working day).
- Single device model (Aribex NOMAD Pro 2); results may not generalize to other handheld units lacking equivalent backscatter shielding/leaded acrylic shield design.
- Abstract-only retrieval here: exact DAP values per exposure setting, detailed scatter isodose maps, and statistical detail are in the full text not captured on this page.
- Findings are conditional on adherence to manufacturer operating instructions (e.g., backscatter shield in place, correct stance).

## 7. Glossary

- **DAP (Dose–Area Product)**: product of absorbed dose and irradiated area; a measure of total radiation output proportional to exposure.
- **Control area (CA)**: regulatory zone around an X-ray source within which radiation-protection controls/access restrictions apply.
- **RANDO phantom**: anthropomorphic tissue-equivalent phantom used for dosimetry.
- **Handheld/portable intraoral X-ray**: a hand-supported intraoral radiography unit (e.g., Aribex NOMAD) with an integrated backscatter shield, operated in-room by the clinician.
- **Backscatter**: radiation scattered back toward the source/operator; the NOMAD's leaded acrylic shield is designed to intercept it.
