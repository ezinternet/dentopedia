## Why Ingested

Highest-evidence (RCT) paper in the EF-Line / elastodontic myofunctional-orthodontics batch — it head-to-head compares an elastodontic device against a conventional removable expander on a hard transverse-expansion endpoint, anchoring the batch's strongest claim that elastodontic appliances can match traditional removable mechanics. Pairs directly with the sibling transverse/expansion comparison [[wiki/orthodontics/myofunctional/ortu-2024-haas-expander-vs-elastodontic-crossbite]], extending it from a fixed-vs-elastodontic contrast (Haas) to a removable-vs-elastodontic contrast (Schwartz plate). Abstract-only — full text not retrieved.

## One-line Summary

RCT (n=70 children, mean age 11±1 y) comparing the Eptamed Equilibrator elastodontic device (series 00) vs the Schwartz plate for palatal expansion of posterior crossbite; the elastodontic device achieved expansion comparable to the conventional removable plate but with greater patient comfort and compliance.

- **Journal:** European Journal of Paediatric Dentistry (Eur J Paediatr Dent) 2025
- **PMID:** 40165619
- **DOI:** 10.23804/ejpd.2025.2230
- **Study type:** Randomised, open-label clinical trial (RCT)
- **Source:** PubMed abstract — **abstract-only; full text not retrieved (no open-access copy).**

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides RCT-level evidence that an **elastodontic (prefabricated elastomeric myofunctional) device** can produce palatal/transverse expansion comparable to a conventional removable expander (Schwartz plate) in growing children.
- Frames posterior crossbite — a very frequent malocclusion in the Caucasian population, most often linked to maxillary hypoplasia/retrusion — as treatable with elastodontic appliances rather than only fixed or screw-activated removable devices.
- Reports a patient-experience advantage (greater comfort, greater compliance) for the elastodontic device over the Schwartz plate.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design:** Randomised open-label two-arm clinical trial.
- **Participants:** 70 children (30 boys, 40 girls); average age 11 ± 1 years.
- **Groups:**
  - **Test group:** palatal expansion with the **Eptamed Equilibrator elastodontic device (series 00)**.
  - **Control group:** palatal expansion with the **Schwartz plate** (traditional removable expander).
- **Follow-up:** controls at **6 months** and **12 months** after start of treatment.
- **Primary measurement:** distance between the cusps of the **first upper premolars**, or the width between the **second deciduous molars** as the reference.
- **Statistics:** t-test or Wilcoxon signed-rank test for continuous variables; chi-square or Kruskal-Wallis test for categorical variables.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- The **Eptamed Equilibrator elastodontic device achieved palatal expansion comparable to that of the traditional expansion device** (Schwartz plate).
- The elastodontic device delivered this expansion with **greater comfort and greater patient compliance**.
- *Note:* abstract-only — numeric expansion magnitudes (mm of inter-premolar/inter-molar gain), between-group p-values, and per-timepoint values are **not reported in the abstract** and were not retrievable from full text.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Abstract-only:** no effect sizes, confidence intervals, or per-arm expansion values are available; only the directional conclusion (comparable expansion, better comfort/compliance) can be cited.
- **Open-label design:** no blinding of patients/operators — comfort/compliance outcomes are susceptible to expectation bias.
- Short follow-up (12 months); transverse-expansion stability/relapse beyond 1 year not assessed.
- Single device series (Equilibrator series 00) and single comparator (Schwartz plate) — generalisability to other elastodontic systems or to fixed expanders (e.g. Haas/RME) not established here.

## 7. Glossary

- **Elastodontic device:** prefabricated, soft elastomeric (silicone/polyurethane) myofunctional appliance worn intermittently; combines arch-form expansion with neuromuscular/functional effects (e.g. Eptamed Equilibrator, EF-Line).
- **Eptamed Equilibrator (series 00):** a specific elastodontic appliance size/series used here for the test arm.
- **Schwartz plate:** traditional acrylic removable expander with a midline expansion screw, activated periodically to widen the maxillary arch.
- **Posterior crossbite:** transverse malocclusion in which one or more maxillary posterior teeth occlude lingual to the mandibular antagonists, commonly due to maxillary hypoplasia/retrusion.
- **Palatal/transverse expansion:** widening of the maxillary dental arch (measured here at first-premolar cusps / second-deciduous-molar width).
- **Open-label trial:** randomised trial without blinding of participants/operators to the assigned device.
