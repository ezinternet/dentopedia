## Why Ingested

The existing single RCT page [[wiki/periodontics/cyris-2024-guided-biofilm-therapy-versus-conventional]] reports GBT favorably; this 2026 PRISMA SR (5 RCTs, n=231, GRADE very-low) qualifies that enthusiasm by showing GBT does NOT consistently outperform conventional SRD on primary clinical outcomes (PPD/CAL/BOP) — the benefit is patient-centered (comfort, less pain, reduced treatment time). It also contextualizes the supportive-care setting of [[wiki/periodontics/stahli-2024-clinical-evaluation-novel-protocol-supportive]].

## One-line Summary

PRISMA systematic review (5 RCTs, n=231, Cochrane RoB-2 + GRADE very-low) finds Guided Biofilm Therapy (GBT) improves patient satisfaction, pain, and treatment time over scaling/root debridement (SRD) but does NOT consistently outperform SRD on PPD/CAL/BOP — no clinical superiority on disease-related outcomes.

## 한줄요약

PRISMA 체계적 문헌고찰(RCT 5편, n=231, Cochrane RoB-2 + GRADE 매우 낮음): 유도생체막치료(Guided Biofilm Therapy, GBT)는 환자 만족도·통증·치료시간 면에서 전통적 스케일링·치근활택술(SRD)보다 우수하나, PPD/CAL/BOP 같은 1차 임상지표에서는 일관된 우위를 보이지 못함 — 질환 관련 지표에서 임상적 우월성 근거 없음.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic review to directly appraise GBT vs conventional SRD as a **standalone** non-surgical periodontal intervention (not as an adjunct).
- Synthesizes 5 RCTs (231 participants) across gingivitis, Stage III/IV periodontitis, and supportive periodontal care settings.
- Separates **patient-centered outcomes** (where GBT shows consistent benefit) from **primary clinical outcomes** (where GBT shows no consistent superiority) — the central clinical message.
- Rates overall certainty of evidence as **very low** via GRADE, tempering claims of GBT clinical superiority.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Guidelines**: PRISMA; PROSPERO-registered (CRD420251010127).
- **Databases (8)**: PubMed, Cochrane, LILACS, Scopus, Web of Science, Google Scholar, OpenGrey, ClinicalTrials.gov. No language/year restriction. Forward + backward citation searching.
- **PICOS**: Adults (>=18y) with periodontal disease; GBT standalone; vs conventional SRD/ultrasonic/hand instrumentation; primary outcome PPD, secondary CAL/BOP/GR/treatment time/plaque/pain; RCTs and non-randomized clinical trials.
- **Quality**: Cochrane RoB 2 (robvis) for RCTs; GRADE (Murad et al. adaptation for SR without meta-analysis).
- **Synthesis**: Descriptive only — meta-analysis NOT feasible due to heterogeneity in timelines and absence of a homogeneous dataset.
- **Screening reliability**: Cohen's kappa 0.86 (selection), 0.82 (extraction), 0.80 (RoB).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Flow**: 620 records -> 79 duplicates removed -> 541 screened -> 12 full-text -> **5 included** (231 participants, sample size 32-60). All 5 were RCTs (3 split-mouth, 2 parallel-arm). All used EMS erythritol/PLUS powder containing 0.3% chlorhexidine.
- **Included**: Vouros 2021 (n=50, Stage III SPC, Greece); Stahli 2024 (n=41, SPC, Switzerland); Mensi 2024a (n=32, gingivitis, Italy); Cyris 2024 (n=60, Stage III/IV, Germany); Mensi 2024b (n=41, Stage III/IV, Italy).
- **PPD**: decreased 3.8-6.2 mm -> ~2.9-3.3 mm (2-3 mm reduction), comparable both groups.
- **CAL**: 4.6-6.7 mm -> 4.0-4.1 mm, no clinically relevant between-group difference.
- **BOP**: largest reductions, 56-91% baseline -> 9-32% follow-up, similar magnitude both arms. Stahli 2024 noted BOP improvement significant in SRD; Cyris 2024 noted significant BOP reduction within GBT.
- **GR**: stable (~1.0-2.3 mm baseline -> 1.2-2.2 mm).
- **Plaque**: 83-91% -> 29-31% at 3M; 65% -> 17-19% at 12M; GBT slightly better in difficult-access areas.
- **Treatment time**: GBT showed reduction in some studies (Cyris: 4.3 min/quadrant, 12.43%; 30.3 vs 34.6 min) but not all (Stahli 42.4 vs 39.6; Mensi 2024a 55.44 vs 51.13 min).
- **Pain**: negligible (0.1 +/- 0.4 GBT vs 0.1 +/- 0.3 control overall), though Stahli reported VAS pain higher in GBT (15 vs 7.6) and satisfaction higher in SRD (97.3 vs 84.8).
- **Patient perception**: Mensi 2024b — GBT less discomfort (5.2% vs 33.4% at 12M), higher quality-of-treatment perception.
- **RoB**: 1 high (Mensi 2024a), 2 some concerns, 3 low (overall mix).
- **GRADE**: **very low** (⊕⊖⊖⊖) — downgraded for RoB, indirectness, imprecision (n=231), inconsistency (variable PPD/CAL/BOP findings across trials).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample sizes (max 60), reducing external validity; no meta-analysis feasible.
- Heterogeneous follow-up (6 weeks to 12 months; one unreported) and varied control-group protocols.
- Mixed disease severity (gingivitis only vs Stage III/IV) and mixed setting (active therapy vs supportive periodontal care).
- All studies used a single manufacturer's powder (EMS) — generalizability to other devices unknown.
- Aerosol/particulate contamination and rare subcutaneous emphysema/pneumocephalus risk with air-powder jets; high-vacuum suction recommended.
- Future: adequately powered, longer-term RCTs with standardized protocols.

## 7. Glossary

- **GBT (Guided Biofilm Therapy)**: protocolized supra/subgingival air-polishing with disclosing agent + piezo scaling (EMS AIRFLOW/PERIOFLOW/PIEZON).
- **SRD (Scaling and Root Debridement)**: conventional non-surgical mechanical instrumentation, the gold-standard comparator.
- **PPD / CAL / BOP / GR**: probing pocket depth / clinical attachment level / bleeding on probing / gingival recession.
- **GRADE**: certainty-of-evidence grading (here adapted for SR without meta-analysis).
- **SPC**: supportive periodontal care (maintenance phase).
