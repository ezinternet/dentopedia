## Why Ingested

The veneers category holds several SR/MA papers (klein-2025, morimoto-2016) on survival rates but lacks a Romanian private-practice cohort specifically comparing vertical-prep vs. no-prep feldspathic veneers across five thickness groups. This paper adds a 7-year retrospective cohort with 170 veneers and thickness-stratified failure data, directly reinforcing [[wiki/veneers/morimoto-2016-feldspathic-glass-ceramic-veneer-survival-sr-ma]] while extending it with detailed thickness-group comparisons.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective cohort (n=30 patients, 170 feldspathic ceramic veneers, 7 years) found 91.77% overall survival; fractures occurred at all thicknesses except 0.5 mm, with no significant difference between vertical-prep and no-prep methods.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Materials (MDPI), 2022; 15:2150
- **DOI**: 10.3390/ma15062150
- **Study type**: Retrospective cohort
- **Sample**: 30 patients (16 men, 14 women; mean age 36.7 ± 12 years), 170 feldspathic ceramic veneers (70 maxillary, 100 mandibular), treated 2013–2014 in a Romanian private clinic; evaluated February–June 2021
- **Follow-up**: Up to 7 years
- **Ethics**: Approved by Vasile Goldis Western University of Arad IRB (Protocol #15/28 February 2022)

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First Romanian private-practice cohort study comparing minimally invasive vertical-prep vs. no-prep feldspathic ceramic veneers over 7 years.
2. Demonstrates that 0.5 mm feldspathic veneers have zero fracture failures over the observation period, challenging the assumption that ultrathin veneers are more fragile.
3. Provides thickness-group (0.5/1/1.5/2/2.5 mm) pairwise comparisons using FDI World Dental Federation criteria, showing thicker veneers (>1.5 mm) are paradoxically more susceptible to fracture due to unsupported ceramic mass.
4. No-prep method produced significantly better FDI scores for esthetic anatomical form (C3), occlusal contour (C6), recurrent caries (C8), and periodontal response (C10) vs. prep method at 7 years.
5. No secondary caries, pulpal necrosis, or marginal adaptation problems observed in either group.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Patient selection**:
- Vital teeth without endodontic treatment or major crown destruction
- Exclusion: bruxism (ICSD criteria), severe malposition (referred for ortho first)
- All treated by same clinician (S.G.M.), all restorations by same technician

**Preparation groups**:
- Prep group (n=108 veneers): vertical/biologically oriented preparation — knife-edge/feather-edge without defined finish line, removing maximum convexity only
- No-prep group (n=62 veneers): additive approach, no tooth structure removed

**Thickness groups** (five subgroups): 0.5 mm, 1 mm, 1.5 mm, 2 mm, 2.5 mm

**Fabrication**: Refractory die technique; flexural strength 62–90 MPa, modulus of elasticity 69 GPa

**Cementation protocol**:
- Etching with 3–<7% hydrofluoric acid (60 s) + 36% orthophosphoric acid cleaning (60 s) + ultrasonic distilled water (5 min)
- Silanization (Monobond Plus, 60 s)
- Rubber dam isolation; tooth sandblasting
- Resin composite luting cement

**Evaluation**: FDI World Dental Federation criteria (11 criteria C1–C11, scored 1–5); evaluated by recall examination

**Statistics**:
- Mann–Whitney U test: prep vs. no-prep group comparisons per FDI criterion
- Kruskal–Wallis H test: five thickness groups per FDI criterion
- Wilcoxon Signed Ranks test: baseline vs. 7-year scores
- Log Rank (Mantel-Cox) test: survival time analysis (p<0.05 threshold)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Overall survival**: 156/170 veneers survived (91.77%); 14 failed from core fracture (replaced with lithium disilicate); 10 cases of chipping were polished without replacement.

**Prep vs. no-prep survival**: No statistically significant difference in survival curves (Log Rank p=0.247).

**Thickness group survival**: No statistically significant difference between five thickness groups (Log Rank p=0.065). Zero failures in the 0.5 mm group.

**FDI scores — prep vs. no-prep** (7-year evaluation):
- No-prep significantly better for C3 (esthetic anatomical form, p=0.038), C6 (occlusal contour, p<0.001), C8 (recurrent caries, p=0.001), C10 (periodontal response, p<0.001)
- No significant difference for other FDI criteria

**Thickness-group FDI comparisons** (7 years):
- 0.5 mm group scored significantly better (lower=better) than ≥1 mm groups for C1 (surface staining), C4 (fracture/retention), C6 (occlusal wear), C8 (recurrent caries), C10 (periodontal response)
- Thicker veneers (>1.5–2 mm) showed paradoxically worse fracture outcomes; fractures in 1 mm (fail time 3 months), 1.5 mm (33 months), 2 mm (2.3 months mean), 2.5 mm (2 months) groups
- No significant differences between 1.5 mm vs. 2.5 mm groups, or 2 mm vs. 2.5 mm groups for most criteria

**Esthetic outcomes** (7 years): All veneers rated score 1 (excellent) for color match/translucency (C2) and marginal adaptation (C5) in both groups

**Biological outcomes**: No secondary caries, no pulpal necrosis; adjacent mucosa rated excellent (score 1) in both groups; postoperative sensitivity excellent for no-prep method

**Failure pattern**: Most failures in first 6 months post-cementation

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single clinician, single technician, single private practice — limits generalizability
- Small sample (30 patients); no randomization; retrospective design
- No control group with conventional horizontal preparation or other ceramic types
- Patients with bruxism excluded — real-world performance unknown for parafunctional patients
- Thickness confounded by clinical indication (erosion/anatomy cases get thicker veneers)
- Recall bias: 100% recall rate not explicitly reported; patients evaluated 7 years post-placement
- No optical coherence tomography (OCT) for marginal seal assessment (identified as future direction)
- Authors suggest future use of intraoral scanners for refractory model impression as digital alternative

## 6. Related Work

- Layton et al. (2012) SR+MA: feldspathic veneer cumulative survival 87–89% (reference for comparison)
- Morimoto et al. (2016) SR+MA: feldspathic ceramic veneers — this paper's 91.77% sits at the upper range of that literature
- Vertical preparation concept from previous prosthodontic literature (cited as promising alternative to horizontal)
- Ostermann (cited): describes adhesive bonding methods for minimum-thickness all-ceramic restorations

## 7. Glossary

- **Feldspathic ceramic**: Glass-phase porcelain; flexural strength 62–90 MPa; no crystalline core material; highest translucency among ceramics
- **Vertical preparation (biologically oriented prep)**: Knife-edge/feather-edge crown preparation without defined finish line; removes only maximum tooth convexity
- **No-prep veneer**: Veneer placed without tooth structure removal; additive-only approach
- **FDI criteria**: FDI World Dental Federation evaluation system (C1–C11); scored 1 (excellent) to 5 (replacement necessary)
- **Log Rank (Mantel-Cox) test**: Statistical test for comparing time-to-event (survival) curves
- **Refractory die technique**: Laboratory fabrication method for feldspathic veneers using high-temperature-resistant die material
- **HF etching**: Hydrofluoric acid treatment (3–7%, 60 s) that creates microtextured surface on ceramic for adhesive bonding
- **Silanization**: Application of silane coupling agent (e.g., Monobond Plus) to create chemical bond between ceramic and resin cement
