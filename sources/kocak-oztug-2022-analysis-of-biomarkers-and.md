## Why Ingested

Reinforces the platform-switching MBL benefit reported in the SR+MA anchor [[wiki/implants/strietzel-2015-platform-switching-mbl-sr-ma]] with primary RCT-level evidence, and adds a mechanistic layer: a split-mouth randomized trial showing platform switching both preserves bone and shifts peri-implant crevicular fluid bone biomarkers (RANKL/OPG, MCP-1) in a bone-protective direction.

## One-line Summary

Split-mouth RCT (94 implants, 27 subjects, 12-month): platform-switched implants showed less marginal bone loss (0.51 ± 0.31 mm vs 0.75 ± 0.29 mm, p<0.001) and lower RANKL/OPG ratio and MCP-1 levels than nonplatform-switched implants.

## 1. Document Information

- Journal: BioMed Research International 2022, Article ID 2603287
- Design: Randomized controlled trial, split-mouth, single-blind (patient-blinded), CONSORT-reported; trial reg. TCTR20210721008
- 94 implants in 27 subjects; recruited 2014–2018, Istanbul University Periodontology
- Implants: Camlog, one-stage, crestal placement, ≥30 N·cm torque
- Timepoints: T1 (restoration) and T2 (12 months post-loading)
- Outcomes: PICF biomarkers (RANKL, OPG, IL-1β, MCP-1 by ELISA), peri-implant health (BOP, mPI, PPD), MBL on standardized periapical radiographs (ImageJ; IAI-to-IBC distance; intra-examiner R=0.937)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Direct randomized confirmation that platform switching reduces 12-month MBL (0.51 mm PS vs 0.75 mm NPS, p<0.001).
- Couples the radiographic benefit to biomarker shifts: RANKL/OPG ratio at T1, MCP-1 at T2, and MCP-1 change T1→T2 all lower in PS (p<0.05).
- Proposes a subclinical bone-remodeling mechanism (higher MCP-1, altered RANKL/OPG, lower OPG in NPS) for the platform-switching effect.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Implants randomly assigned (randomizer.org, balanced permuted blocks) to PS vs NPS in a split-mouth layout; PS abutments inset 0.3 mm relative to the implant neck, otherwise identical to NPS. Inclusion: ≥2 posterior implants, FMPI/FMBS ≤25%, adequate bone (≥7 mm width, ≥9 mm height), ≥2 mm keratinized tissue, nonsmoker, age 30–70. PICF sampled with PerioPaper at vestibulo-mesial/distal sites, analyzed by ELISA. MBL = mean of mesial/distal IAI-to-IBC corrected for scale. Power: 80% at α=0.05 (effect size d=0.712 from Guncu et al.).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- MBL change: PS 0.51 ± 0.31 mm vs NPS 0.75 ± 0.29 mm, p<0.001.
- IL-1β fell and RANKL, OPG, MCP-1 rose from T1→T2 in both groups (p<0.05).
- PS group had lower RANKL/OPG ratio at T1, lower MCP-1 at T2, and smaller MCP-1 increase T1→T2 (p<0.05).
- Clinical peri-implant health (BOP, mPI, PPD) negligibly different between groups.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

Short 12-month horizon; single-center, single implant system (Camlog); modest patient number (n=27); split-mouth confounding (same-patient PS/NPS adjacency); biomarker kits from mixed vendors. Longer follow-up needed to confirm whether early biomarker divergence predicts later clinical peri-implant disease.

## 6. Related Work

Adds RCT + biomarker evidence to the platform-switching MBL literature dominated by SR+MA (Strietzel 2015) and prior RCTs. Connects to RANKL/OPG osteoclastogenesis biology.

## 7. Glossary

- **PS / NPS**: platform-switched / nonplatform-switched abutment.
- **PICF**: peri-implant crevicular fluid.
- **RANKL / OPG**: receptor activator of NF-κB ligand / osteoprotegerin — opposing regulators of osteoclastogenesis; high RANKL/OPG favors bone resorption.
- **MCP-1**: monocyte chemotactic protein-1, can induce osteoclastogenesis.
- **MBL**: marginal bone loss. **IAI/IBC**: implant-abutment interface / coronal bone-implant connection.
