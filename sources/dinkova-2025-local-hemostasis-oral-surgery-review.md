## One-line Summary
Narrative review of 51 studies (1990–2023) on local hemostatic agents in oral surgery: oxidized cellulose and gelatin sponges control >85% of standard cases; tranexamic acid mouthwash reduces bleeding 50–60%; fibrin sealants achieve 70–90% reduction in high-risk patients.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Functional Biomaterials 2025, 16, 190
- **DOI**: 10.3390/jfb16050190
- **Institution**: Medical University-Plovdiv, Bulgaria

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive review of local hemostatic options for oral surgery
- Postoperative bleeding: 4–6% baseline; 9–12% in anticoagulated/antiplatelet patients
- Efficacy data for traditional agents, APCs (PRP, L-PRF), tranexamic acid, fibrin sealants

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative review; 51 studies 1990–2023
- Databases: Medscape, PubMed-MEDLINE, ScienceDirect, EBSCOhost
- Keywords: local hemostasis, bleeding, oral surgery, tranexamic acid, gelaspon, fibrin sealants, collagen sponges, oxidized cellulose, topical thrombin, APCs, PRP

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
| Agent | Efficacy |
|---|---|
| Oxidized cellulose / gelatin sponge | Bleeding control >85% standard cases |
| PRP / L-PRF (Autologous Platelet Concentrates) | Reduce bleeding time 30–50%; enhance soft tissue healing |
| Tranexamic acid mouthwash | Reduces postoperative bleeding 50–60% |
| Fibrin sealants | 70–90% bleeding reduction in high-risk patients |
| Topical tranexamic acid | ↓80% hemorrhagic events in anticoagulated patients (no thromboembolic risk increase) |

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Limited comparative studies in medically compromised populations
- Gaps in long-term outcomes and cost-effectiveness
- No standardized protocols for emerging agents (nanomaterials)
- Future: high-quality RCTs across diverse patient groups; integrated clinical guidelines

## 6. Related Work
- Moldovan-2023: SR on continuing vs stopping anticoagulants for oral surgery
- Complements: local hemostasis enables surgery without stopping anticoagulation
