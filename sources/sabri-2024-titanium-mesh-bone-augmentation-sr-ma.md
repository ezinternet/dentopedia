## Why Ingested

티타늄 메쉬의 호불호·성능·장단점을 종합하기 위해 수집. 22편 임상시험을 포함한 SR+MA로, [[wiki/implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/ragucci-2025-customized-titanium-mesh-vertical-ridge-augmentation-sr-ma]]와 상보적으로 conventional vs CAD/CAM Ti mesh 전반의 골증대량 및 합병증 풀드데이터를 제공한다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (22 trials, n≥196): Ti mesh VRA = 3.36 mm vertical / 3.26 mm horizontal bone gain; pooled complication rate 10.8%, most common = mesh exposure; non-inferior to collagen membrane for horizontal gain.

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (22편, n≥196): 티타늄 메쉬 골증대 = 수직 3.36 mm / 수평 3.26 mm; 전체 합병증률 10.8% (노출이 가장 흔함); 수평 증대 시 콜라겐막과 유의한 차이 없음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis
- **Journal**: International Journal of Oral Implantology (Berlin)
- **Year**: 2024
- **PMID**: 39283219
- **Inclusion**: RCTs and non-randomized prospective controlled trials; Ti mesh in ≥1 arm; outcomes = complications + vertical/horizontal bone gain
- **Studies included**: 22 articles (qualitative); subset for meta-analysis

## 2. Key Contributions

- Largest pooled dataset on Ti mesh bone augmentation outcomes (22 studies)
- First to pool BOTH vertical and horizontal bone gain separately
- Direct comparison of Ti mesh vs collagen membrane controlling for staged vs simultaneous placement
- Pooled complications rate across clinical trials
- Highlights scarcity of patient-reported outcomes data

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Databases: comprehensive electronic + manual search
- PICO: Ti mesh vs other membranes (collagen); outcomes = bone gain + complications
- Meta-analysis: vertical gain (196 subjects), horizontal gain (81 subjects)
- Complication pooling from clinical trials only
- No specific risk-of-bias tool stated; heterogeneity acknowledged as limitation

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Bone Gain (meta-analysis):**
- Vertical: 3.36 mm (95% CI 2.44–4.64 mm; range across studies 1.4–5.7 mm; n=196)
- Horizontal: 3.26 mm (95% CI 2.93–3.63 mm; range 2.6–3.7 mm; n=81)

**Complications (pooled, clinical trials):**
- Overall rate: 10.8%
- Most common: mesh exposure (all mesh types)
- Second most common: graft failure

**Ti mesh vs collagen membrane (horizontal gain):**
- No significant difference, controlling for staged vs simultaneous placement

**Clinical implication:** Ti mesh is feasible for defects requiring ~4 mm 3D reconstruction.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Heterogeneity among included studies (design, mesh type, defect type, graft)
- Small sample sizes in individual studies
- Patient-reported outcomes (PROMs) data scarce — future RCTs should include
- No differentiation of conventional vs CAD/CAM mesh outcomes (pooled together)

## 7. Glossary

- **Ti mesh**: titanium mesh barrier membrane for GBR
- **VRA**: vertical ridge augmentation
- **Graft failure**: complete loss of regenerated bone, usually after early mesh exposure
- **Pooled complication rate**: meta-analytically combined complication incidence across clinical trials
