## One-line Summary
Experimental single-arm study (n=10 female, ages 25-37) combining IncoBotulinumTox A pre-surgery + lip repositioning surgery + 3 repeat BTX injections at 2/4/8 months: gingival display 5.12 mm → 0 mm at 14 days through 8 months, with only 0.78 mm relapse at 12 months; interlabial distance 17.99 → 6.84 mm at 14 days, gradually increasing to 12.21 mm at 12 months. Combined approach proposed as longer-lasting solution than either modality alone.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First study combining IncoBotulinumTox A (Xeomin) pre-surgery + lip repositioning surgery + repeated BTX-A at 2/4/8 months
- Dose individualized to gingival display (1 U per mm at Yonsei point)
- Maintains zero gingival display through 8 months; minimal relapse (0.78 mm) at 12 months
- Hypothesis: BTX-A muscle relaxation stabilizes the surgical site, allowing fibrous tissue maturation in the newly reduced vestibular depth
- Avoids myotomy/myectomy (regarded as more aggressive with high muscle regenerative capacity)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Population: 10 White female patients, 25-37 y, gummy smile from upper lip hypermobility or short upper lip
- Upper lip length measured (subnasale to stomion superior); normal range 20-24 mm
- Upper lip hypermobility: (dentogingival exposure on spontaneous smile) - (incisal exposure at rest)
- Preoperative measurements: standardized photographs in Adobe Photoshop CS6 — gingival display (free gingival margin of upper central incisor to inferior border of upper lip) + interlabial distance (inferior border of upper lip to superior border of lower lip)

### Treatment stages
| Stage | Intervention | Timing |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Xeomin injection | T0 |
| 2 | Lip repositioning surgery | 2 weeks after stage 1 |
| 3 | Xeomin injection | 2 months post-op |
| 4 | Xeomin injection | 4 months post-op |
| 5 | Xeomin injection | 8 months post-op |

### Xeomin (incobotulinumtoxinA) injection protocol
- 100 U vial diluted with 2 mL saline (preservative-free)
- Injection points by subtype:
  - Anterior gummy smile: Yonsei point only (1 cm from ala)
  - Mixed: 3 points bilateral (Yonsei point + nasolabial fold deepest contraction point + mid-way between)
  - Some patients: additional vermilion point at orbicularis oris peripheralis/marginalis junction
- Dose at Yonsei = mm gingival display (e.g., 4 mm = 4 U); half-dose at other two points for mixed cases; 1 U intradermally at Cupid's bow tip for vermilion point
- Depth: 2 mm (superficial)

### Lip repositioning surgery
- Bilateral intraoral infraorbital block + vestibular infiltration with vasoconstrictor
- Full-thickness flap: inferior incision at mucogingival junction + superior at mucosa, connected laterally at premolars or first molar
- Suture: 5-0 Vicryl resorbable

### Follow-up
14 days, 4 months, 8 months, 12 months. Re-injection at 2 mo, 4 mo, 8 mo.

### Safety
- No paresthesia, no serious complications
- Edema in all patients post-surgery (controlled with medications, resolved within 1 week)
- All patients reported increased vermilion height of upper lip (cosmetic plus from lip repositioning + orbicularis BTX-A)
- High patient satisfaction at end of study

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Very small sample (n=10), single arm, no controls (BTX-A alone vs surgery alone vs combined)
- All female, all White, narrow age range — generalizability limited
- Short follow-up (1 year) — relapse trajectory between 12 months and next BTX cycle unknown
- Author has no disclosed financial interest but is sole author and operator (no blinding)
- Photographic measurement of interlabial distance may be confounded by lip vermilion change from surgery + BTX-A at orbicularis
- Multiple BTX-A injections — cost and antibody formation concerns not discussed
- Patients with vertical maxillary excess explicitly excluded — only hypermobility/short lip subtypes

## 7. Glossary
- IncoBotulinumTox A (Xeomin, Merz): incobotulinum toxin A, BTX-A formulation without complexing proteins (lower antigenicity)
- Lip repositioning surgery: removal of strap of vestibular epithelium + suturing of labial mucosa to mucogingival junction to reduce vestibular depth and limit elevator muscle pull
- Yonsei point: 1 cm from ala — confluence of LLSAN, LLS, zygomaticus minor
- Interlabial distance: vertical gap between upper and lower lips at smile
- Vermilion: red lip; orbicularis oris peripheralis / marginalis are anatomic subdivisions of orbicularis oris
- Mucogingival junction (MGJ): boundary between keratinized attached gingiva and oral mucosa
