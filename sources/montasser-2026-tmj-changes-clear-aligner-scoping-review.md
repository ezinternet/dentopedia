## Why Ingested

`clear-aligner` surveillance sweep의 **TMJ/악관절 형태 변화 축**. 기존 [[wiki/orthodontics/clear-aligner/almalki-2024-clear-aligner-masticatory-stomatognathic-sr]]가 저작·구강기능(stomatognathic) 측면을 다룬다면, 본 scoping review는 CBCT로 측정한 과두 위치·관절강·과두 형태 변화라는 구조적(영상) 축을 보강한다. CAT가 악관절에 구조적 변화를 일으키는지에 대한 근거 현황(4편·serious RoB·exploratory)을 정리해 연구 공백을 명시.

## One-line Summary

Scoping review (PRISMA-ScR) of 4 retrospective CBCT studies on TMJ morphology after clear-aligner treatment: no significant condylar-position or joint-space change in adult Class I (extraction + non-extraction) and Class II non-extraction; Class II div 2 non-extraction showed forward condylar repositioning; growth-driven changes in adolescent Class II div 1 functional aligners. All 4 studies serious RoB (ROBINS-I) → evidence exploratory, not confirmatory.

## 한줄요약

CBCT 기반 후향 4편 scoping review(PRISMA-ScR): 성인 Class I(발치·비발치)·Class II 비발치에서 과두 위치·관절강 유의 변화 없음. Class II div 2 비발치는 과두가 전방(중심)으로 재위치, 청소년 Class II div 1 기능성 얼라이너는 성장 관련 변화. 4편 모두 serious RoB → 근거는 확정적 아닌 탐색적.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First scoping review to map the CBCT evidence on TMJ morphological/positional change specifically after clear-aligner (CA) orthodontic treatment.
- Defines the outcome domains: primary = condylar position + joint spaces; secondary = condylar dimensions/inclination + mandibular fossa dimensions.
- Documents that the entire evidence base is 4 retrospective comparative studies, all with serious risk of bias (ROBINS-I) and institutional clustering (two paired institutions, overlapping teams).
- Establishes a clear research gap: no RCTs, no clinical TMD outcome measures (pain/sounds/function), CBCT-only.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PRISMA-ScR; PCC framework (Participants = CA orthodontic patients; Concept = TMJ changes; Context = RCT/CCT/retrospective comparative designs, any setting/language).
- Databases: MEDLINE (PubMed), Scopus, Web of Science, ProQuest Dissertations & Theses Global, ClinicalTrials.gov, WHO ICTRP. Searched 2025-05-27, updated 2025-11-17. Hand-searching of reference lists.
- Exclusions: surgical, cleft, TMJ-affecting syndromes, joint fractures, JIA, RA, psoriasis.
- Selection: 320 records → 287 after de-duplication → 6 after title/abstract → **4 included** after full-text.
- Risk of bias: ROBINS-I (3 domains / 7 sub-domains). Heterogeneity high → descriptive (narrative) synthesis only; no meta-analysis. Publication-bias test not performed (<10 studies).
- Imaging: all 4 studies used CBCT (T0 before vs T1 after treatment).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Included studies (all retrospective comparative, China; July 2023–Jan 2025):
- Al-Somairi 2025 — adult skeletal Class I, moderate crowding; FA (n=30) vs CA (n=30), each in non-extraction and extraction subgroups (total n=120).
- Al-Worafi 2024 — adult skeletal Class II (division unspecified), molar distalization; FA (n=35) vs CA (n=35).
- Zheng 2023 — adult skeletal Class II division 2; FA (n=25) vs CA (n=22).
- Zhang 2024 — adolescent skeletal Class II division 1; Twin-Block (n=24) vs functional clear aligners (n=25).

Findings (intergroup CA vs comparator unless noted):
- **Class I adult (non-extraction + extraction)**: CA showed no significant intra-group change in condylar position (APCP/VCP/MCP/APCJP) or joint spaces. FA showed some significant intra-group changes (e.g., APCP increase) but intergroup CA vs FA differences were largely non-significant; in the extraction subgroup APCP and APCJP changes did differ between groups.
- **Class II adult non-extraction (molar distalization)**: no significant condylar-position or joint-space change in either group.
- **Class II division 2 adult**: by Pullinger formula, posteriorly positioned condyles decreased from ~61% to ~14% (CA) and ~68% to ~24% (FA) → forward displacement toward fossa centre after mandibular unlocking in both groups; only the CA group showed increased superior joint space and glenoid-fossa depth (interpreted as adaptive condylar remodeling).
- **Adolescent Class II division 1 (functional CA vs Twin-Block)**: both groups moved condyles from posterior to neutral fossa position; significant increases in condylar dimensions and mandibular fossa width/depth in both, with greater condylar height (CH) and anteroposterior diameter (APDC) change in the comparator — growth modification effect, not appliance-specific.
- Condylar inclination: changes negligible/non-significant between CA and comparators across all malocclusion types.
- CBCT changes <1 mm regarded as possibly clinically insignificant (measurement error).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 4 studies, **all serious risk of bias** (ROBINS-I) — retrospective, no randomization, no allocation concealment/blinding, only successfully treated cases (selection bias → outcome overestimation).
- Institutional clustering: each pair from the same institution with overlapping teams → evidence not fully independent.
- High heterogeneity (different malocclusions, parameters) precluded meta-analysis.
- Growth-modification study lacked a true untreated control → cannot separate treatment from growth.
- No clinical TMD outcome measures (pain, joint sounds, functional limitation) in any study — CBCT-only.
- Conclusion: findings are **exploratory, not confirmatory**; research gap warrants high-quality studies (ideally RCTs) and future systematic reviews.

## 7. Glossary

- **CBCT**: cone-beam computed tomography.
- **Condylar position**: APCP (anteroposterior), VCP (vertical), MLCP/MCP (medio-lateral), APCJP (anteroposterior condylar joint position).
- **Joint spaces**: AJS (anterior), SJS (superior), PJS (posterior), MJS/IJS (medial/interior), EJS (exterior/lateral), TMJV (total joint volume).
- **Pullinger formula**: index classifying condyle as anterior/centric/posterior within the glenoid fossa.
- **Class II division 1 vs division 2**: div 1 = proclined upper incisors/overjet; div 2 = retroclined upper centrals, deep bite, condyles tend more posterior.
- **ROBINS-I**: Risk Of Bias In Non-randomized Studies – of Interventions.
- **PRISMA-ScR**: PRISMA extension for scoping reviews.
- **Functional clear aligner (FCA)**: aligner designed for mandibular advancement / growth modification (vs Twin-Block).
