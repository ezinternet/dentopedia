## Why Ingested

임플란트 주위 각화점막 (Keratinized Mucosa, KM) 폭경이 임플란트 건강에 미치는 영향에 대한 기존 SR/MA들을 총괄하는 우산형 체계적 고찰(umbrella review) + 메타-메타분석으로, KM 부족(< 2 mm)이 골흡수·치은퇴축·플라그지수 등에 미치는 영향을 정량화한 가장 상위 수준의 근거. 기존 [[wiki/implants/peri-implantitis/mahardawi-2023-lack-keratinized-mucosa-peri-implantitis-sr-ma]] 및 [[wiki/implants/peri-implantitis/ravida-2022-keratinized-mucosa-width-peri-implant-disease-sr-ma]]를 포함하는 10개 SR을 종합해 effect size를 통합 산출함.

## One-line Summary

Umbrella SR + meta-meta-analysis of 10 SRs (2012–2023) shows KM width < 2 mm is significantly associated with greater mucosal recession (eOR 4.05), higher plaque/gingival indices, and greater marginal bone loss (eOR 1.852), but not with bleeding on probing or pocket depth.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First umbrella review + quantitative meta-meta-analysis synthesising all existing SR/MAs on peri-implant keratinized mucosa (KM) significance.
2. Provides pooled effect sizes (Hedges' g, expressed as equivalent odds ratios) across outcomes: mucosal recession, gingival index, plaque index, and marginal bone loss.
3. Demonstrates that KM width < 2 mm is a significant risk factor for multiple soft- and hard-tissue outcomes around implants.
4. Identifies lack of sufficient evidence for impact of KM on bleeding on probing (BOP), pocket depth, implant survival, and disease prevalence.
5. Evaluates methodological quality of included SRs using AMSTAR-2 and evidence certainty with GRADE criteria.
6. Evidence mapping component visualizes consistency and heterogeneity across prior systematic reviews.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Study design**: Umbrella systematic review with evidence mapping and quantitative meta-meta-analysis
- **Search**: Comprehensive multi-database search strategy (specific databases not detailed in abstract)
- **Inclusion**: Systematic reviews comparing implant health outcomes between patients with adequate (≥ 2 mm) vs. inadequate (< 2 mm) keratinized mucosa width
- **Eligible SRs included**: 10 systematic reviews published between 2012 and 2023
- **Effect size measure**: Hedges' g, converted to equivalent odds ratios (eOR) for reporting
- **Case group**: Patients without adequate KM width (< 2 mm)
- **Control group**: Patients with adequate KM width (≥ 2 mm)
- **Quality assessment**: AMSTAR-2 (A MeaSurement Tool to Assess Systematic Reviews version 2)
- **Evidence certainty**: GRADE (Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation)
- **Outcomes assessed**: Mucosal recession, Gingival Index (GI) / modified Gingival Index (mGI), modified Plaque Index (mPI), marginal bone loss (MBL), bleeding on probing (BOP), pocket depth

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | eOR | P-value | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mucosal recession | 4.05 | 0.03 | Significant |
| Gingival Index / mGI | 3.131 | 0.001 | Significant |
| Modified Plaque Index | 5.34 | 0.005 | Significant |
| Marginal bone loss | 1.852 | 0.0007 | Significant |
| Bleeding on probing | — | NS | Not significant |
| Pocket depth changes | — | NS | Not significant |
| Pocket depth values | — | NS | Not significant |

- KM width < 2 mm threshold was consistently associated with worse soft- and hard-tissue outcomes.
- Follow-up time did not significantly modify the relationship between KM width and these outcomes.
- Implant survival and peri-implant disease prevalence: insufficient evidence or no significant association found.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Critical limitation**: The PDF file is a PubMed abstract-only page. Full methodological detail, AMSTAR-2 scores per included SR, GRADE certainty ratings per outcome, forest plots, evidence map figures, and individual SR-level data are not available from this source. Clinical interpretation should be supplemented with the full article.
- Umbrella review inherits the limitations and heterogeneity of included primary SRs.
- Definition of "adequate KM" (≥ 2 mm threshold) varies across included SRs; standardization issues may affect pooled estimates.
- Heterogeneity among original SRs (different populations, implant systems, follow-up durations, confounders) introduces uncertainty in meta-meta-analysis results.
- BOP, pocket depth, survival, and disease prevalence outcomes remain insufficiently evidenced — future prospective SR/MAs with standardized outcome reporting are needed.
- The review does not address KM height/thickness independently of width; three-dimensional KM volume may be a more complete predictor.

## 7. Glossary

- **Keratinized mucosa (KM)**: The band of gingiva/mucosa that is firmly attached to underlying bone, characterised by keratinised epithelium; adequate width conventionally defined as ≥ 2 mm around implants.
- **Umbrella review**: A synthesis of multiple systematic reviews on the same topic, providing a higher-order evidence summary.
- **Meta-meta-analysis**: Quantitative pooling of effect sizes from multiple previously published meta-analyses.
- **Hedges' g**: A standardized mean difference effect size measure, adjusted for small-sample bias; here converted to equivalent odds ratios for clinical interpretability.
- **eOR (equivalent odds ratio)**: Odds ratio derived from Hedges' g to facilitate interpretation of continuous-scale outcomes in OR terms.
- **AMSTAR-2**: A critical appraisal tool for systematic reviews of interventions, rating overall confidence in the review as critically low, low, moderate, or high.
- **GRADE**: A framework for rating certainty of evidence and strength of recommendations from very low to high.
- **Mucosal recession**: Apical migration of the soft-tissue margin, exposing the implant surface or abutment; a key soft-tissue adverse outcome.
- **Marginal bone loss (MBL)**: Peri-implant crestal bone resorption measured radiographically; key hard-tissue outcome for implant health surveillance.
- **Evidence mapping**: A structured visual/tabular display of the scope, quality, and direction of evidence across included reviews on a given topic.
