## Why Ingested

[[wiki/resin-bonding/mortazavi-2012-ethanol-wet-bonding-clinical-trial]] established ethanol wet-bonding (EWB) on coronal dentin. This 2024 in vitro study reinforces and extends that anchor to radicular dentin under fiber-post cementation with a modern multi-mode universal adhesive, adding direct push-out and in situ zymography evidence that ethanol pre-treatment raises bond strength and lowers MMP activity even at the root.

## One-line Summary

In vitro study (60 single-root teeth, 4 groups): ethanol pre-treatment significantly increased push-out bond strength of a multi-mode adhesive on radicular dentin (p<0.01) and reduced hybrid-layer MMP activity (p<0.01), while etching time (15 vs 30 s) did not affect bond strength.

## 한줄요약

60개 단근치 in vitro 실험(4군): 에탄올 전처리가 다목적 접착제의 근관내 상아질 push-out 접착강도를 유의하게 증가(p<0.01)시키고 혼성층 MMP 활성을 감소(p<0.01)시켰으며, 산부식 시간(15 vs 30초)은 접착강도에 영향 없음.

## 1. Document Information

- Type: In vitro laboratory study (push-out bond strength + in situ zymography)
- Published: J. Clin. Med. 2024;13:2474 (MDPI), 24 Apr 2024
- DOI: 10.3390/jcm13092474
- Substrate: 60 single-canal, round mono-radicular human teeth (donor age 20–40)
- Adhesive: Clearfil Universal Bond Quick (Kuraray, UBQ); fiber posts luted with DC Core dual-cure cement

## 2. Key Contributions

- First study to combine etching-time variation, ethanol pre-treatment, and MMP activity assessment for a multi-mode universal adhesive on radicular dentin.
- Confirms ethanol wet-bonding benefit extends from coronal to radicular dentin in a fiber-post context.
- Decouples etching time (no bond-strength effect) from solvent pre-treatment (significant effect).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 4 groups (n=15 each): G1 = 15 s H3PO4 + UBQ; G2 = 30 s H3PO4 + UBQ; G3 = 15 s + ethanol + UBQ; G4 = 30 s + ethanol + UBQ.
- Endodontics (WaveOne Gold, 5% NaOCl / 10% EDTA), obturation, 8 mm post space, fiber post (Rebilda #15), dual-cure cement light-cured 40 s.
- After 24 h artificial-saliva storage: 1 mm slices for push-out test and nanoleakage (coronal + apical); in situ zymography for MMP activity.
- Stats: ANOVA + Tukey, α=0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Push-out bond strength: significant effect of pre-treatment variable; ethanol groups higher (p<0.01). No significant effect of etching time.
- MMP gelatinolytic activity present in all groups; significant increase at the 30 s etching time.
- Ethanol pre-treatment significantly reduced MMP activity within the adhesive interface (p<0.01).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In vitro only, 24 h storage (no long-term aging or in vivo follow-up — authors explicitly call for these).
- Single adhesive (UBQ) and single cement; results not necessarily generalizable.
- Narrow donor age range; round single-canal teeth only.

## 6. Related Work

- Mortazavi 2012 RCT — ethanol wet-bonding on coronal dentin.
- Studies on multi-mode adhesive application modes (Munoz et al.).
- MMP-2/8/9 collagen degradation literature (Breschi, Tjäderhane).

## 7. Glossary

- Radicular dentin: dentin lining the root canal / post space.
- Push-out test: shear-type bond-strength test extruding a luted post from a dentin slice.
- Multi-mode (universal) adhesive: adhesive usable in etch-and-rinse, selective-etch, or self-etch modes.
- MMP: matrix metalloproteinase; endogenous collagen-degrading enzyme activated by acid etching.
