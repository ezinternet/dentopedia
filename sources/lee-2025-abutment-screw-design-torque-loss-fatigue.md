## Why Ingested

An Osstem TSIII vs KSIII head-to-head torque-loss study directly relevant to a Korean practice (both systems are in routine domestic use), reinforcing and quantifying the abutment-screw-design / preload theme of [[prosthetic-materials/velez-2020-implant-connection-abutment-design-screw]]. It supports the ~35 Ncm tightening recommendation by showing 35 Ncm gave the most stable preload in both systems, while demonstrating that screw geometry (KSIII modified screw) is itself a dominant determinant of preload maintenance independent of torque level.

## One-line Summary

In vitro fatigue study (50 implant–abutment assemblies, n=5 per torque group) showing Osstem KSIII keeps torque loss <25% at all torque levels, while TSIII loses 36.5%–51.8% (~2.5–3.0× more), with ~35 Ncm giving the most stable preload.

## 한줄요약

체외 피로하중 실험(임플란트-지대주 50쌍, 토크군당 n=5): 오스템 KSIII는 모든 토크에서 토크손실 <25%로 안정적이나 TSIII는 36.5%~51.8% 손실(약 2.5~3.0배 더 큼)을 보였고, 약 35 Ncm에서 가장 안정적인 전하중(preload)을 얻음.

- **Journal**: Bioengineering (Basel) 2025;12(10):1131
- **Date**: 2025-10-21
- **DOI**: 10.3390/bioengineering12101131 | **PMID**: 41155129 | **PMCID**: PMC12561121
- **Study type**: In vitro biomechanical (cyclic fatigue) study
- **Systems tested**: Osstem TSIII and KSIII (Osstem Implant Co., Ltd., Seoul, Korea)

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Direct head-to-head of two Osstem systems** (TSIII vs KSIII) with differing abutment-screw designs, under identical fatigue protocol — clinically pertinent to Korean practice.
2. **Quantifies torque loss across 5 tightening torques** (20/25/30/35/40 Ncm), separating the effect of tightening torque from the effect of screw geometry.
3. **Demonstrates screw design dominates preload maintenance**: KSIII (reduced head diameter, narrower thread profile, longer nominal length) held torque loss <25% irrespective of torque, whereas TSIII was torque-dependent and lost 36.5%–51.8%.
4. **Reinforces the ~30–35 Ncm optimum**: lowest TSIII loss occurred at 35 Ncm; 40 Ncm did not improve (and slightly worsened) preload retention — excessive torque is not protective.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Specimens**: 50 implant–abutment assemblies, n=5 per group; 10 groups (2 systems × 5 torque levels).
- **Implants**: Cylindrical Ø4.0 mm × 10 mm, straight abutments (Ø5.0 mm platform, 2.0 mm gingival height). Commercially pure Ti (Grade 4) fixtures; abutments and screws Ti-6Al-4V. Both systems share macro/micro-design; the stated difference is the implant–abutment connection geometry and the KSIII modified screw.
- **Tightening torques**: 20, 25, 30, 35, 40 Ncm, applied with calibrated digital torque gauge (MTT03-100, MARK-10) at ~1 rpm, by a single trained operator; lab conditions 22 ± 1 °C, 50 ± 5% RH.
- **T1 (initial removal torque)**: measured 5 min after tightening to capture settling-effect preload loss.
- **Cyclic loading**: after T1, specimens retightened to assigned torque, then loaded on ElectroPuls E3000 (Instron) per ISO 14801 — 150 N, 14 Hz, 100,000 cycles, at 30° off-axis. Stated to simulate ~1–2 months of mastication.
- **T2 (post-fatigue removal torque)**: measured immediately after loading. **Torque loss rate (%) = (T1 − T2)/T1 × 100**.
- **Statistics**: Shapiro–Wilk normality; independent t-tests (TSIII vs KSIII at each torque); one-way ANOVA + Tukey within each system; α = 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Initial removal torque T1 (Ncm, mean ± SD)** — KSIII > TSIII at every level (p < 0.05):

| Tightening | TSIII T1 | KSIII T1 |
|---|---|---|
| 20 Ncm | 16.4 ± 0.3 | 18.2 ± 0.3 |
| 25 Ncm | 19.3 ± 0.3 | 20.8 ± 0.3 |
| 30 Ncm | 23.1 ± 0.7 | 27.1 ± 0.2 |
| 35 Ncm | 30.3 ± 0.3 | 33.1 ± 0.3 |
| 40 Ncm | 29.3 ± 1.1 | 32.2 ± 0.6 |

**Post-fatigue removal torque T2 (Ncm)** — KSIII > TSIII at every level (p < 0.05):

| Tightening | TSIII T2 | KSIII T2 |
|---|---|---|
| 20 Ncm | 7.9 ± 0.2 | 14.0 ± 0.4 |
| 25 Ncm | 10.1 ± 0.5 | 17.4 ± 0.2 |
| 30 Ncm | 14.0 ± 0.6 | 21.5 ± 0.4 |
| 35 Ncm | 19.2 ± 0.6 | 26.9 ± 0.5 |
| 40 Ncm | 18.1 ± 0.7 | 24.7 ± 0.8 |

**Torque loss rate (%)**:

| Tightening | TSIII | KSIII |
|---|---|---|
| 20 Ncm | 51.8% | 23.1% |
| 25 Ncm | 47.7% | 16.3% |
| 30 Ncm | 39.4% | 20.7% |
| 35 Ncm | 36.5% | 18.7% |
| 40 Ncm | 38.2% | 23.3% |

- **TSIII**: torque-dependent loss; highest at 20 Ncm (51.8%), lowest at 35 Ncm (36.5%); 40 Ncm rose again to 38.2%. Significant differences among torque levels (ANOVA p < 0.05; 20 vs 35 Ncm Tukey p = 0.008).
- **KSIII**: all values <25%; no significant difference between torque groups (ANOVA p > 0.05) — geometry confers torque-insensitive stability.
- **Overall**: TSIII torque loss ~2.5–3.0× higher than KSIII at each level (p < 0.001).
- **Clinical bottom line**: ~35 Ncm gave the most stable preload; KSIII screw design inherently reduces loosening risk and may mitigate the need for frequent retightening.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In vitro only; 100,000 cycles ≈ ~1–2 months of function, not years — long-term loading/thermocycling needed.
- Only two systems examined → limited generalizability.
- Oral milieu (saliva, blood, thermal cycling, microbiology, contamination) not reproduced.
- No SEM/TEM surface characterization of screws post-test, so wear/deformation mechanism inferred not imaged.
- Prospective clinical trials required to confirm KSIII's biomechanical advantage translates to fewer loosening events and improved survival.

## 6. Related Work

- Velez 2020 — Morse-taper abutment-screw insertion torque (10/20/30 Ncm) vs misfit; same preload/screw-design theme, internal-connection focus.
- Ren 2024 — Morse-taper abutment subsidence/locking force; conical-connection mechanical behavior.
- Haack et al. — reverse torque can drop 20–30% within minutes of tightening (settling/embedment relaxation).
- Hung et al. — Morse-taper systems showed greater fatigue resistance than external hex.
- ISO 14801 — standardized implant fatigue testing (the loading framework used here).

## 7. Glossary

- **ASL (Abutment Screw Loosening)**: most frequent mechanical implant complication (5–15% incidence); causes microgaps, bacterial leakage, peri-implant disease.
- **Preload**: tensile clamping force generated when torque is applied; only ~8–10% of applied torque converts to preload, the rest dissipated as friction.
- **Settling effect (embedment relaxation)**: flattening of microscopic surface asperities after tightening → immediate preload reduction.
- **T1 / T2**: initial removal torque (5 min post-tightening) / post-fatigue removal torque.
- **Torque loss rate**: (T1 − T2)/T1 × 100 (%).
- **ISO 14801**: standard for dynamic fatigue testing of endosseous dental implants (off-axis cantilever loading).
