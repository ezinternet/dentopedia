## Why Ingested

치아형태학 감시 인제스트 — 하악 제1대구치 micro-CT 근관해부(중간근심관·isthmus)의 최고해상도 종합. micro-CT는 CBCT보다 voxel이 작아(9.9–50 µm) MMC·isthmus·accessory canal 같은 미세 구조를 "gold standard" 수준으로 정량화하므로, 위키의 CBCT 기반 하악 대구치 형태 페이지들에 해상도 상한선을 더한다. 짝을 이루는 mandibular-molar 근관형태 페이지 [[endodontics/anatomy/shemesh-2017-cshaped-canals-cbct-mandibular-molars]] (CBCT)와 C-shaped 단면 [[endodontics/anatomy/song-2008-cross-sectional-morphology-and-minimum-canal]]을 micro-CT 정량 데이터로 보강하고, MB2 검출·missed-canal 라인의 [[endodontics/anatomy/mohammadi-2016-methods-decrease-missed-root-canals]]에 mesial-root MMC/isthmus라는 누락 표적을 추가한다.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review + meta-analysis of 30 micro-CT studies (double-rooted permanent mandibular first molars): pooled Vertucci Type IV dominates the mesial root (58.7%) and Type I the distal root (83.0%); middle mesial canal prevalence up to 46%, mesial-root isthmus 10–100% — all with high heterogeneity (I²=86–87%) and "moderate" risk of bias in 27/30 studies (AQUA).

## 한줄요약

SR+MA, micro-CT 30편(이근성 영구 하악 제1대구치): 근심근 Vertucci Type IV(58.7%)·원심근 Type I(83.0%)가 우세, 중간근심관(MMC) 최대 46%, 근심근 isthmus 10–100% — 이질성 매우 높고(I²=86–87%) 27/30편이 AQUA "중간" 비뚤림위험.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Highest-resolution synthesis of mandibular first molar (MFM) root/canal anatomy — micro-CT (voxel 9.9–50 µm) is the stated "gold standard" over CBCT/clearing for isthmus, MMC, accessory canals, apical foramina.
2. Pooled prevalence meta-analysis of Vertucci main-canal configurations: **mesial Type IV (58.7%)**, **distal Type I (83.0%)**.
3. Meta-analyses of middle mesial canal (MMC) configurations and Fan's isthmus types, both with **high heterogeneity (I²=86% / 87%)**.
4. AQUA risk-of-bias appraisal: 27/30 "moderate", 2 "low", 1 "high"; systematic under-reporting of sample-size calculation (93%) and calibration (only 20% calibrated).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases**: Web of Science, PubMed, Scopus. Keywords: 'Mandibular first molar'/'Mandibular molar' AND 'Micro-CT'/'Micro-computed tomography'.
- **Screening**: 1358 identified → 921 after de-dup → 831 excluded by title → 56 by abstract → 34 eligible → 4 excluded on full-text (no MFM-specific data) → **30 included**. Interobserver Cohen's κ = 0.88.
- **Inclusion**: experimental micro-CT studies of permanent double-rooted MFM root/canal morphology. **Excluded**: three-rooted MFM, taurodontism, primary teeth, second molars, case reports, reviews, non-English, comparison-only (CBCT/clearing).
- **ROB tool**: AQUA (Anatomical Quality Assessment), 5 domains — (1) Objective(s) & Subject Characteristics, (2) Study Design, (3) Methodology Characterization, (4) Descriptive Anatomy, (5) Results Reporting.
- **Meta-analysis**: MetaXL v5.3; pooled prevalence for Vertucci configs, MMC (Pomeranz) configs, Fan's isthmus types; I² heterogeneity (>60% = substantial).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Vertucci main canal configuration (pooled prevalence)**
- Mesial root: **Type IV most prevalent — 58.7% (0.587; 95%CI 0.186–0.627)**; two canals the most frequent finding (Type IV two-separate or Type V single→split).
- Distal root: **Type I most prevalent — 83.0% (0.830; 95%CI 0.623–0.880)**; single canal most frequent. 3–4 distal canals reported in Brazilians, not Egyptians.
- High proportion of "non-classifiable" configurations in micro-CT studies; mesial anatomy more complex/variable than distal.

**Middle mesial canal (MMC)** — prevalence **up to 46%**; configurations independent / fin / confluent (confluent ± isthmus); confluent type predominant overall, fin most frequent in Burmese. Meta-analysis I²=86%. Keleş & Keskin: 77.41% of MMC orifices located at CEJ without troughing; 5.38% need 1-mm, 9.69% need 2-mm troughing; 7.52% inaccessible (>2 mm below CEJ). MMC major-foramen deviation from apex highest of all canals (>2 mm in six cases, up to 5.67–6.2 mm).

**Isthmus** — mesial-root prevalence highly variable **10–100%**; more frequent in middle/apical thirds; more common in mesial than distal canals; Fan types I (sheet) / II (separate) / III (mixed) / IV (cannular). Separate & mixed types more common in MFM; sheet connections more common in second molars. Meta-analysis I²=87%.

**Other** — root length ~13.16±1.24 mm (Chinese, two-rooted); thinnest mesial dentine ("danger zone") directed mesially in 48%, esp. apical third; accessory canals up to 85% (more mesial, more apical 3 mm, more in two-rooted); MAF-apex distance avg 0.85 mm (mesial); MB canal greater curvature than ML (largest apically). VRF in MFM 51.8%; ~2× the maxillary first molar in RCT-treated teeth.

**ROB** — 27/30 "moderate", 2 "low", 1 "high". Reporting: population/ethnicity 22/30 (73%), micro-CT specs 25/30 (83%), ethical approval 12/30 (40%), sample-size method missing 28/30 (93%), calibration only 6/30 (20%). Sample size 19–140 teeth.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Micro-CT studies on extracted teeth only — age/gender unknown, generalizability limited; English-only search; small sample sizes (cost-driven) feed high heterogeneity.
- **Three-rooted MFM excluded by design** — this page does NOT cover radix entomolaris prevalence (separate evidence stream).
- High I² (86–87%) → pooled MMC/isthmus estimates are population-sensitive, not single thresholds.
- Authors recommend PRIDE and PROUD 2020 reporting checklists and more prospective, multi-ethnic comparative studies.

## 7. Glossary

- **Micro-CT (micro-computed tomography)**: non-destructive 3D imaging, voxel here 9.9–50 µm; endodontic anatomy "gold standard", lab-only (not clinical).
- **Vertucci classification**: 8-type canal-configuration system; Type I single canal orifice→apex, Type IV two separate canals orifice→apex.
- **Middle mesial canal (MMC)**: third mesial canal between MB and ML, in the developmental groove/isthmus; configurations independent/fin/confluent.
- **Isthmus**: narrow ribbon-shaped communication between two canals containing pulp tissue; Fan types I–IV (sheet/separate/mixed/cannular).
- **AQUA (Anatomical Quality Assessment)**: 5-domain risk-of-bias tool for anatomical studies.
- **Danger zone**: thinnest dentine on the furcal/mesial aspect of the mesial root — strip-perforation risk.
- **VRF**: vertical root fracture.
