## Why Ingested

기존 GBR collagen membrane 동물실험 페이지들이 모두 mammalian(porcine/bovine) 또는 합성 막에 한정돼 있어 fish-derived(어류) collagen 대안에 대한 근거가 비어 있었다. 본 rat femoral defect study(Bujda 2026)는 European carp skin collagen membrane을 standard porcine membrane(Bio-Gide)·bovine xenograft(Bio-Oss)와 직접 비교해 그 공백을 메운다. EDC/NHS 가교 collagen 막을 rat에서 평가한 [[bone-regeneration/park-2015-edc-crosslinked-collagen-bcp-gbr-rabbit]] 및 rat 모델 collagen 막 [[bone-regeneration/verissimo-2014-collagen-membranes-gbr-rat-calvarial]]를 확장하며, "particulate graft에 막 추가 시 비critical-size defect에서 이득 없음"이라는 [[bone-regeneration/wessing-2018-gbr-collagen-membranes-particulate-graft-sr-ma]]의 임상 메시지를 동물 수준에서 보강한다. 동물모델 선택 논거는 [[bone-regeneration/lee-2026-animal-models-bone-regeneration-review]]와 맞물린다.

## One-line Summary

Animal study (21 Wistar rats, 4×2 mm cortical femoral metaphyseal defects, 4 treatments): a novel European-carp-skin EDC/NHS-crosslinked collagen membrane used ALONE showed a consistent (non-significant) trend toward higher healing scores, more new bone, and smaller residual defect, and was non-irritant throughout; adding Bio-Oss did not improve healing and performed worse than Bio-Oss + Bio-Gide.

## 한줄요약

동물실험(Wistar rat 21마리, 대퇴골 4×2 mm 피질골 결손, 4개 처치): 새 잉어피부 EDC/NHS 가교 콜라겐 막을 단독 사용 시 치유점수·신생골·잔존결손에서 일관된(통계적 비유의) 우세 경향과 우수한 생체적합성(비자극성)을 보였으나, Bio-Oss와 병용 시 이득이 없고 Bio-Oss+Bio-Gide보다 열등했다 — 비critical-size 모델 한계.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First in vivo bone-healing evaluation of a European-carp-skin collagen membrane (previously only characterized in a rat subcutaneous/soft-tissue model). Addresses zoonotic, ethical/religious, and cost concerns of mammalian (porcine/bovine) collagen membranes; fish collagen is lower-immunogenicity and producible from waste (skin/scales/fins).
- Head-to-head GBR design comparing carp membrane against the established porcine membrane Bio-Gide and bovine xenograft Bio-Oss in a standardized defect.
- Finds that the carp membrane ALONE trends best, but does NOT outperform Bio-Gide when both are paired with particulate Bio-Oss — reinforcing the "membrane-only sufficient in contained non–critical-size defects; graft adds no benefit" theme.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Animals**: 21 male Wistar rats, 12 weeks old; 3 groups (n=7) euthanized at 4, 12, and 16 weeks. Ethics: Charles University First Faculty of Medicine (MSMT-26,149/2022–4); Directive 2010/63/EU.
- **Membrane**: 4 wt% carp collagen dispersion, lyophilized, EDC/NHS crosslinked (4/1 wt/wt, 24 h), gamma-sterilized (25 kGy), cut to 8×6×2 mm (membrane type "A" from prior work).
- **Defects**: 4×2 mm cortical bone defects (depth = cortical thickness) made with 1 mm round burr under saline irrigation. Two defects per femur, four per animal at FIXED anatomical sites (to limit cross-contamination):
  - Proximal/right: untreated empty-defect control
  - Distal/right: carp collagen membrane alone
  - Proximal/left: Bio-Oss + carp collagen membrane
  - Distal/left: Bio-Oss + Bio-Gide (porcine collagen membrane)
- **Evaluation**: micro-CT (SkyScan 1272, 21 µm; semiquantitative healing score 1–4; regeneration ratio for Bio-Oss groups) + histology (Han et al. score: newly formed bone, cartilage, fibrous tissue, remnant defect → 0–40, 40 = complete healing). Soft-tissue irritancy via adapted Lindner scoring (final score = treated − empty control; non-irritant 0.0–2.9).
- **Statistics**: non-parametric (Kruskal–Wallis + Dunn's, Holm correction); medians/IQR; effect sizes η² (small <0.06, medium 0.06–0.14, large ≥0.14). Individual defects treated as independent units (anatomically separated).
- **Note**: femoral fractures at defect site in 4 animals → those data excluded; 14 defects excluded from histology total; reduced n (<7) at several cells.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **micro-CT healing score**: no significant between-group differences at any time point (4 wk p=0.055, η²=0.20 large; 12 wk p=0.060, η²=0.19 large; 16 wk p=0.731). Carp-membrane-alone medians trended highest (3→4→4). Only the carp-membrane-alone group showed a significant temporal increase (p=0.012; 4→12 wk p=0.010, 4→16 wk p=0.020).
- **Histology (0–40)**: carp-membrane-alone had highest medians at 4 wk (32/40; 5/6 defects 80–90% bone-filled) and 12 wk; at 12 wk between-group difference significant (p=0.020) with carp-alone > Bio-Oss+carp (post-hoc p=0.007), and 5/6 carp-alone defects ≥90% filled (score 10/10). Newly-formed-bone subscore differences non-significant but large effect at 12 wk (η²=0.24) and 16 wk (η²=0.18). By 16 wk groups converged (untreated controls largely healed spontaneously: 17.0→24.5→25.0).
- **Bio-Oss combinations**: Bio-Oss+carp consistently scored LOWER than Bio-Oss+Bio-Gide (new bone, residual defect) — non-significant trends, but carp membrane did not match the established porcine membrane when paired with particulate graft.
- **Soft-tissue biocompatibility**: carp-membrane-alone non-irritant at all time points (median 0, 0, 2). Bio-Oss+carp slight irritant at 4 wk (median 6) then non-irritant. Bio-Gide non-irritant throughout. Between-group irritancy significant at 4 wk (p=0.025) and 12 wk (p=0.018).
- **Histology detected differences earlier/more distinctly than micro-CT** (especially at 4 wk).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Non–critical-size metaphyseal defect**: substantial spontaneous healing in untreated controls by 16 wk compressed inter-group separation; most key comparisons non-significant despite large effect sizes.
- Small n per time point; femoral fractures and exclusions reduced evaluable samples; fewer histology than micro-CT samples.
- Multiple defects per animal → partial pseudoreplication; proximal vs distal metaphysis differ in vascularity/turnover.
- Four-walled metaphyseal defect does NOT replicate clinical vertical/horizontal ridge augmentation. Authors call for large-animal critical-size defect / true augmentation models. Animal-only data — no clinical generalization.

## 6. Related Work

- Builds on the authors' prior carp collagen membrane characterization (rat subcutaneous degradation model).
- Discussion cites Araújo et al. (Bio-Oss did not enhance new bone vs controls), Heberer et al. (xenograft in sockets could impair healing), and Chen et al. (barrier membrane alone sufficient for periodontal intrabony defects) — supporting the "graft adds no benefit in contained defects" interpretation.

## 7. Glossary

- **GBR (Guided Bone Regeneration)**: barrier-membrane technique to stabilize the clot and maintain protected healing space for bone formation.
- **Carp collagen membrane**: fish (European carp skin)–derived resorbable collagen barrier; alternative to porcine/bovine.
- **EDC/NHS crosslinking**: carbodiimide chemistry (N-ethyl-N′-(3-dimethylaminopropyl)carbodiimide / N-hydroxysuccinimide) to tune collagen degradation and mechanics.
- **Bio-Oss**: deproteinized bovine bone mineral (DBBM) particulate xenograft.
- **Bio-Gide**: native (non-crosslinked) porcine collagen barrier membrane — the comparator standard.
- **Critical-size defect**: defect too large to heal spontaneously within the study period; this study used a non–critical-size defect.
- **η² (eta-squared)**: effect-size measure; large ≥0.14 here flags clinically meaningful trends even when p>0.05.
