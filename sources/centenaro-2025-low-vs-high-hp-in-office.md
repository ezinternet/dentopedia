## Why Ingested

The EU 6% HP regulation for in-office bleaching demands direct head-to-head RCT evidence comparing 6% vs 35% HP efficacy. This is a powered equivalence trial (n=140) from the Loguercio group — the largest direct 6% vs 35% comparison to date. Extends [[tooth-whitening/de-geus-2025-at-home-vs-in-office-bleaching]] by providing in-office–specific concentration data.

## One-line Summary

Parallel double-blind RCT (n=140) found 35% HP had superior in-office bleaching efficacy vs 6% HP (ΔE MD=6.3, 90% CI 4.8–7.7), while 6% HP halved tooth sensitivity risk (44% vs 74%, p=0.0005); 6% HP is a valid low-sensitivity alternative with acknowledged efficacy trade-off.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Largest direct RCT comparing 6% vs 35% HP for in-office bleaching (n=140)
- Equivalence design: demonstrated non-equivalence — 35% HP achieves significantly greater whitening
- 6% HP halved sensitivity risk (44% vs 74%) with lower intensity up to 24h post-treatment
- Clinical framing: 6% HP is a real alternative for sensitivity-prone patients at the cost of inferior whitening outcome
- Contextualized within the EU regulatory framework limiting in-office HP to 6%

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Participants**: 140 adults randomly assigned 1:1 to 6% HP (Automixx 6%, FGM) or 35% HP (Automixx Plus 35%, FGM)
- **Bleaching protocol**: 3 sessions × 50 min; 1-week interval between sessions
- **Outcomes**:
  - Bleaching efficacy (BE): spectrophotometer (ΔE, ΔE00, ΔWI) + shade guides (Vita Classical, Vita Bleachedguide ΔSGU) at days 7, 14, 21, and 1 month
  - Tooth sensitivity (TS): VAS (0–10) immediately after each session, 1h, 24h, 48h post-treatment
- **Statistical analysis**:
  - BE: Paired Student's t-test (equivalence margins)
  - TS absolute risk: Fisher's exact test
  - TS intensity: Paired Student's t-test
  - α = 0.05

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Bleaching Efficacy (BE):**
| Metric | 35% HP | 6% HP | MD (90% CI) |
|---|---|---|---|
| ΔE | Superior | — | 6.3 (4.8–7.7) |
| All endpoints (days 7–21, 1 month) | Superior | Inferior | p < 0.05 across all |
| Equivalence verdict | Non-equivalent | — | 35% > 6% |

**Tooth Sensitivity (TS):**
| Metric | 35% HP | 6% HP | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Risk (any episode) | 74% | 44% | 0.0005 |
| Intensity (VAS, immediate) | Higher | Lower | < 0.01 |
| Intensity at 24h | Higher | Lower | < 0.01 |
| Intensity at 48h | No difference | No difference | NS |

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single bleaching product pair (Automixx 6% and 35% FGM) — results may not generalize to all formulations
- All three sessions used same protocol; clinical practice may individualize
- EU 6% HP regulation context: regulatory limit tested directly, relevant for EU practitioners
- No long-term follow-up beyond 1 month
- Future: identify minimum effective 6% HP protocol to maximize efficacy gap closure

## 7. Glossary

- **Automixx 6% / 35%**: Pre-mixed syringe HP products by FGM, Brazil; dual-syringe auto-mixing design
- **ΔE / ΔE00**: See campolina-2025 glossary; CIEDE2000 preferred for clinical applications
- **ΔWI**: Whiteness Index difference; perceptibility threshold ~3.3
- **Vita Classical / Bleachedguide**: Standard shade guide systems; Bleachedguide has 15 bleaching-specific shades
- **Equivalence margin**: Pre-specified threshold of acceptable difference between groups; crossing it = non-equivalent
