## Why Ingested

RCT 성공의 결정인자가 근관 내 술식뿐 아니라 coronal seal·술전 상태임을 보여주는 임상 prognostic 근거로, 세정·소독 프로토콜이 치유에 기여하는 맥락을 보완한다. [[endodontics/irrigation/llena-2020-outcome-root-canal-treatments-provided]] 자체 페이지로, 기존 근관치료 세정·해부 페이지들과 함께 RCT 예후 종합에 활용.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective study (585 teeth, 1–6 yr follow-up) of primary RCT by endodontic postgraduates: preoperative periapical/periodontal status and especially the quality of the coronal restoration governed complete periapical healing (overall ~90.9% healing).

## 한줄요약

대학원 수련의가 시행한 1차 근관치료 585개 치아(1~6년 추적) 후향연구 — 술전 치근단·치주 상태와 특히 최종 보철수복(coronal restoration)의 적합도가 완전 치유를 좌우했고, 전체 치유율은 약 90.9%였다.

## 1. Document Information

- Journal of Clinical Medicine, 2020, 9, 1994. DOI: 10.3390/jcm9061994 (MDPI, open access).
- Received 29 May 2020; Accepted 23 June 2020; Published 25 June 2020.
- Departament of Stomatology, University of Valencia (Valencia, Spain).
- Study type: retrospective observational study (ethics file H1445365577359).
- Setting: primary RCTs performed 2013–2018 by postgraduate endodontics students under specialist supervision; patients aged 18–81; follow-up of 1–6 years.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies preoperative, intraoperative, and postoperative factors associated with complete periapical healing after primary RCT in a teaching/postgraduate setting.
- Identifies the **quality (favorable fit) of the coronal restoration** as the single postoperative factor retained in the postoperative logistic regression model — i.e., the dominant modifiable postoperative predictor of healing.
- Confirms preoperative status (size/presence of periapical lesion, probing depth, RCT cause, abutment status) as strong predictors retained in the preoperative logistic regression model.
- Supports the principle that endodontic + restorative success are coupled: the coronal seal is as important as canal disinfection for long-term healing.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Target population: patients with primary RCT ≥1 year before exam; teeth with adequate preoperative radiograph identifying the apex.
- Exclusions: medication altering bone metabolism (immunosuppressors, corticosteroids, antiresorptives); periodontally diseased teeth; previously treated teeth; teeth extracted for non-endodontic reasons; missing pre/intra/postoperative data.
- Standardized protocol per European Society of Endodontology (ESE):
  - Local anesthesia, rubber dam isolation; access cavity; patency with #10 K file.
  - Working length by electronic apex locator (Root ZX, J Morita) confirmed radiographically.
  - Minimum apical instrumentation ≈ #30 caliber (no further widening if initially wider).
  - Irrigation: 2.5% or 5.25% NaOCl between files (3 mL, 27 G side-vented needles); final sequence = saline → 17% EDTA → saline → 2% chlorhexidine; ultrasonic activation (EMS / Satelec) with #15 K file at low power.
  - Interappointment medicament: calcium hydroxide (Ultracal) when needed.
  - Obturation: gutta-percha + AH Plus epoxy resin sealer (or BioRoot RCS in some) via lateral condensation / modified warm lateral / vertical compaction.
  - Coronal restoration: composite ± fiber post within max 2 weeks; Cavit as temporary.
- Outcome (per ESE): healing = absence of clinical (pain/swelling/sinus tract/mobility) and radiological signs of periapical pathology at follow-up.
- Statistics: chi-squared to screen associations, then variables significant on chi-squared entered into a logistic regression model (LRM) — separate preoperative and postoperative models.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Sample: 585 teeth (252 men 43.1%, 333 women 56.9%); overall complete healing 532/585 ≈ 90.9%.
- Preoperative factors significantly associated with healing (chi-squared):
  - ASA status (ASA I 92.5% vs ASA II 86%, p = 0.01).
  - Vital teeth 95.6% vs non-vital 82.3% (p < 0.01).
  - Periapical radiolucency: absent 97.4%, 1–4 mm 86.2%, >4 mm 47.1% (p < 0.01) — large lesion is the strongest negative preoperative predictor.
  - Absence of preoperative pain (p = 0.04); positive pulp test response; RCT cause = caries/pain/abscess/sinus tract favored healing; probing depth <4 mm; absence of mobility.
- Preoperative LRM retained: absence or <4 mm periapical lesion; probing depth <4 mm; RCT caused by caries/pain/abscess/sinus tract; tooth not a bridge abutment.
- Postoperative factors associated with healing: direct restoration; tooth not supporting a fixed prosthesis; favorable condition of the coronal restoration (p < 0.01).
- Postoperative LRM retained: only the **status (favorable fit) of the coronal restoration**.
- Tooth wall loss at presentation (0 / 1 / ≥2 walls) was NOT significantly associated with healing (p = 0.96).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Retrospective design; healing assessed on 2D periapical radiographs (no CBCT), risking underdetection of persistent lesions.
- Extracted teeth excluded from analysis (cause of extraction uncertain) — potential survivorship bias inflating healing rate.
- Operators were postgraduate students under supervision — results may not generalize to general practice or to specialists.
- Heterogeneous instrumentation/obturation techniques (operator preference) limit isolation of specific intraoperative variables.
- Wide follow-up window (1–6 years); ESE recommends ≥4 years for uncertain lesions, so some "healed" outcomes may be early.

## 6. Related Work

- Builds on prior outcome/prognosis literature reporting primary RCT success 68–85% (systematic review) and survival 86–93% when only clinical criteria applied.
- Reinforces the long-standing "coronal seal" literature linking quality of the final restoration to endodontic success.
- Complements ESE outcome-evaluation guidelines (≥1 yr follow-up, defined favorable/uncertain/failure categories).

## 7. Glossary

- **RCT (Root Canal Treatment)**: removal of infected pulp, canal disinfection, and obturation to treat/prevent apical periodontitis.
- **Apical periodontitis**: inflammatory periradicular process of polymicrobial origin reaching the periodontium via the root canal system.
- **Complete healing**: absence of clinical and radiological signs of periapical pathology at follow-up.
- **Coronal restoration quality / coronal seal**: integrity of the final restoration preventing re-contamination of the canal system.
- **ASA status**: American Society of Anesthesiologists physical-status classification (systemic health).
- **EAL (Electronic Apex Locator)**: device determining working length (here Root ZX).
- **LRM (Logistic Regression Model)**: multivariable model identifying independent predictors of a binary outcome (healing).
- **ESE**: European Society of Endodontology.
