## Why Ingested

기존 [[tmj/ijoms-2023-arthrocentesis-conservative-tmd-sr-ma]] (7개 RCT)는 관절천자(Arthrocentesis)가 보존치료 대비 하악 최대개구량(MMO)을 +1.12mm 유의 개선한다고 보고했다. 본 2026 SR+MA는 비교군을 보존치료뿐 아니라 관절내 주사·관절경·대체 천자 프로토콜까지 넓혀 32개 RCT(1247명)로 풀을 확장했고, 통증(VAS) 차이 없음 + MMO/MIO는 오히려 비교군 우세로 나와 기존 페이지의 modest-benefit 결론을 비교군 다양화 맥락에서 갱신·재맥락화한다. [[tmj/zhang-2024-arthrocentesis-injectable-agent-tmd-sr]]의 천자 프로토콜 비교(NMA)와도 짝을 이룬다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 32 RCTs (n=1247; 640 arthrocentesis vs 607 other) finds arthrocentesis is NOT superior to other modalities (conservative therapy, intra-articular injection, arthroscopy, alternative protocols): pain (VAS) no difference (MD −0.25, 95% CI −1.09–0.59, p=0.55, I²=96%); MMO and MIO statistically favored comparators; only masticatory efficiency (2 studies) favored arthrocentesis; all outcomes low-to-very-low GRADE certainty.

## 한줄요약

32개 RCT(1247명) SR+MA: 관절천자(Arthrocentesis)는 다른 치료(보존·관절내주사·관절경 등) 대비 통증(VAS) 차이 없고(MD −0.25, p=0.55, I²=96%), 최대개구량(MMO)·최대절치개구(MIO)는 오히려 비교군이 우세 — 우월성 입증 안 됨, 근거확실성 매우 낮음.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Largest direct head-to-head SR+MA of TMJ arthrocentesis **vs all comparator modalities** (not just conservative care): 32 RCTs, 1247 patients, published 1996–2023.
- Comparators pooled by **direct pairwise within-trial comparison** (no synthetic pooled comparator arm), covering conservative therapy (splints/physiotherapy/pharmacology), intra-articular injections (hyaluronic acid, PRP, i-PRF), arthroscopy, and alternative arthrocentesis protocols.
- Outcomes stratified into four pathology subgroups: disc displacement, osteoarthritis, arthralgia, and "other" (juvenile idiopathic arthritis, condylar fracture, TMJ dysfunction syndrome).
- Bottom line: arthrocentesis shows **no superiority** for pain or most functional outcomes; should be positioned as an adjunctive option in a stepwise strategy, not a definitive/superior intervention.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **PICO**: P = intra-articular TMD; I = TMJ arthrocentesis ± adjunctive intra-articular agents; C = conservative / injection / arthroscopy / alternative protocols; O = pain (VAS), MMO, MIO, mandibular movements, masticatory efficiency.
- **Databases**: MEDLINE(PubMed), EMBASE, SCOPUS, Web of Science, CINAHL, Google Scholar (first 200). Inception–December 2025. English/Spanish only.
- **Eligibility**: RCTs only (only RCTs met criteria). Diagnostic criteria per primary study — no single standardized RDC/TMD or DC/TMD applied across trials.
- **Screening**: 180 records → 91 after de-dup → 58 full-text → **32 RCTs** included.
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane RoB tool (7 domains); overall moderate quality; inter-reviewer κ=0.91. Blinding of participants/personnel was the most frequent high-risk domain (inherent to surgical/minimally-invasive interventions).
- **Synthesis**: RevMan 5.4, inverse-variance random-effects model; MD or SMD with 95% CI; GRADE for certainty. Funnel-plot publication-bias testing not done where <10 studies per comparison.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Pain (VAS):**
- Global (26 studies): MD **−0.25** (95% CI −1.09–0.59), **p=0.55, NS**, I²=96%. GRADE very low.
- Disc displacement (13): MD −0.79 (−2.10–0.51), NS, I²=98%.
- Osteoarthritis (7): MD 0.39 (−0.97–1.74), NS, I²=86%.
- Arthralgia (2): MD −0.22 (−1.53–1.09), NS, I²=0%.
- Other pathologies (4): MD 0.60 (−0.27–1.48), NS, I²=68%.

**Maximum mouth opening (MMO):**
- Global (14 studies): SMD **−0.67** (95% CI −1.14 to −0.20), **p=0.005** — statistically **favors comparators** (arthrocentesis gained less), I²=85%. GRADE very low.
- Subgroups (disc displacement, osteoarthritis, arthralgia) all NS; "other" (1 study) favored comparator (SMD −1.00, p=0.0003).

**Maximum incisal opening (MIO):**
- Global (11 studies): SMD **−2.93** (95% CI −4.54 to −1.32), **p=0.0004** — favors comparators, I²=96%. GRADE very low. (Disc-displacement subgroup SMD −5.07 favoring comparators.)

**Mandibular movement (overall, 22 studies):** MD −0.40 (−0.93–0.14), p=0.14, NS, I²=100%. All lateral/protrusive/ipsi-/contralateral subgroups NS.

**Masticatory efficiency (2 studies):** MD **+1.15** (95% CI 0.22–2.08), **p=0.02 — favors arthrocentesis** (only outcome favoring it), but I²=98%, GRADE very low, only 2 studies.

**Adverse effects:** Only 1/32 studies explicitly reported AEs — 4 patients (2 control, 2 experimental) had transient facial nerve paresis (<30 min, fully reversible), attributed to anesthetic diffusion / irrigation fluid extravasation near the facial nerve. Under-reporting prevents conclusions.

**Meta-regression:** Age positively associated with lateral TMJ movement (β=0.16, 95% CI 0.056–0.263, p=0.0024); other covariates K<3, not analyzable.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Extreme heterogeneity** (I² 85–100% in most pooled outcomes) — substantially limits interpretability; driven by diverse comparators, protocols, diagnostic criteria, follow-up.
- **Low-to-very-low GRADE certainty** across all primary outcomes.
- No standardized TMD diagnostic classification (RDC/TMD vs DC/TMD) across trials.
- High risk of performance bias (lack of blinding) inherent to the intervention type.
- Several outcomes rest on very few studies (masticatory efficiency = 2; some subgroups = 1).
- Authors call for well-designed RCTs with standardized diagnostic criteria, clearly defined comparators, and longer follow-up.

## 6. Related Work

- Zhang et al. — network meta-analysis of arthrocentesis regimens/adjuncts (HA short-term, i-PRF medium-term) — protocol variations, not vs alternative modalities (= our [[tmj/zhang-2024-arthrocentesis-injectable-agent-tmd-sr]]).
- Thorpe et al. — arthrocentesis vs conservative: no significant pain reduction, slight MMO improvement (the comparison anchored in [[tmj/ijoms-2023-arthrocentesis-conservative-tmd-sr-ma]]).
- Hu et al. — short/medium-term pain reduction favoring arthrocentesis, no MMO difference.
- Tang et al. — short-term pain/function benefit, MMO gains not clinically meaningful.

## 7. Glossary

- **Arthrocentesis**: minimally-invasive lavage of the upper TMJ joint space with sterile solution to flush inflammatory mediators, improve lubrication, and free disc mobility.
- **VAS**: visual analogue scale, 0–10 pain intensity.
- **MMO**: maximum mouth opening — greatest interincisal distance on unassisted opening (normal ~40–55 mm).
- **MIO**: maximum incisal opening — direct incisal-edge-to-edge measurement.
- **MD / SMD**: mean difference / standardized mean difference (used when scales differ across trials).
- **GRADE**: certainty-of-evidence grading (high → very low).
- **i-PRF / PRP**: injectable platelet-rich fibrin / platelet-rich plasma intra-articular adjuncts.
