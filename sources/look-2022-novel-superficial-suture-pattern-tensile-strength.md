## One-line Summary

Cadaveric human-skin biomechanical study (4 suture patterns × 12 samples each, 3-0 nylon): the novel Lindeque locking (LL) stitch had the greatest dehiscence force (198.60 N) and ultimate load-to-failure (211.13 N), significantly beating only the horizontal mattress (HM), which was the weakest of all patterns.

## 1. Document Information

- Journal: Injury, vol 53, issue 11, pp 3613-3616, November 2022
- Article identifier: S0020-1383(22)00685-4
- Authors: Nicole Look (a); Francisco Rodriguez Fontan (a,b); Todd H. Baldini (a,b); Bennie Lindeque (a,b)
- Study type: cadaveric human-skin biomechanical (tensile-strength) study

## 2. Key Contributions

- First study to evaluate **tensile strength of different suture patterns on human cadaveric skin** (prior work used animal/synthetic models).
- Introduces and tests the novel **Lindeque locking (LL)** superficial suture pattern.
- Identifies the **horizontal mattress as the mechanically weakest** of the four patterns tested.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Four skin-closure patterns tested: **simple (S), horizontal-mattress (HM), vertical-mattress (VM), and Lindeque locking (LL)**.
- Human cadaveric skin; **3-0 nylon** suture; four groups, **12 samples each**.
- A tensioning device applied **1 N of force per second** in a linear fashion.
- Primary outcomes: (i) **wound dehiscence force**, and (ii) **ultimate load-to-failure**.
- Statistics: one-way ANOVA with post-hoc Tukey tests.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Dehiscence force / ultimate load-to-failure (N):

| Pattern | Dehiscence force (N) | Ultimate load-to-failure (N) |
|---|---|---|
| Lindeque locking (LL) | 198.60 | 211.13 |
| Simple (S) | 186.90 | 195.08 |
| Vertical-mattress (VM) | 173.9 | 171.1 |
| Horizontal-mattress (HM) | 104.81 | 120.79 |

- LL had the greatest values on both outcomes but was **only significantly greater than HM** on both:
  - vs HM dehiscence: 95% CI 65.7 to 121.9; p<0.001.
  - vs HM ultimate load: 95% CI 63.5 to 117.2; p<0.001.
- LL vs S: **no significant difference** for either dehiscence or ultimate load.
- LL vs VM: significantly greater for ultimate load-to-failure, but **not** for dehiscence.
- Of all patterns, **HM had significantly less withstanding force**.
- Conclusion: the LL stitch demonstrated the greatest dehiscence force and tensile strength and may decrease the risk of wound dehiscence in high-tension wounds.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Cadaveric human skin — no living vascular/healing response; mechanical endpoints only.
- Small per-group sample (n=12); only one suture caliber/material (3-0 nylon).
- LL's advantage was robust only against HM; vs simple and vertical-mattress the benefit was partial or absent.
- Abstract-only extraction; full methods/figures not available.

## 6. Related Work

- Cites prior animal/synthetic suture-pattern perfusion and strength studies (Sagi pig model; Shannon, Shorten ankle-fracture RCTs on perfusion).
- Complements Marsidi 2020 (pulling force) and Knoell 2011 (tension dispersion) as the load-to-failure axis of suture biomechanics.

## 7. Glossary

- **Lindeque locking (LL) stitch:** novel superficial locking suture pattern introduced in this study; highest tensile/dehiscence resistance.
- **Wound dehiscence force:** force at which the closed wound edges begin to separate.
- **Ultimate load-to-failure:** maximum force the closure withstands before mechanical failure.
- **Horizontal mattress (HM):** traditional everting mattress pattern — weakest in this test.
