## Why Ingested

Existing water-jet evidence in the wiki splits by population: [[interdental-cleaning/yiamwattana-2025-oral-irrigator-vs-floss-orthodontic-sr-ma]] covers only orthodontic patients, and [[interdental-cleaning/mohapatra-2024-water-flosser-vs-floss-plaque-sr]] pools regular adults on plaque. This SR+MA spans BOTH regular AND orthodontic patients in one analysis with subgroup stratification by comparator (vs floss / vs brushing-alone) and by time (short/intermediate/long), giving a single contextual anchor that resolves where the device adds value. It also directly disputes Mohapatra's PI-superiority claim (attributing it to no-follow-up before/after assessments), and aligns with the orthodontic emphasis — a useful tension/agreement node for the [[overviews/interdental-cleaning-devices-synthesis]] page.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review + meta-analysis (18 RCTs, 15 in MA, n≈1005) of water jet (WJ) adjunct to manual brushing vs floss or brushing-alone in regular and orthodontic patients: WJ gives slight, time/population-dependent BI and GI benefits (moderate certainty for bleeding), no consistent plaque advantage, with benefits more pronounced vs brushing-alone than vs floss and in orthodontic patients.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First WJ review to span both regular AND orthodontic patients in one analysis** with a 2×2 comparator × population structure (vs floss / vs MTB-alone) — broader than the existing ortho-only SR ([[interdental-cleaning/yiamwattana-2025-oral-irrigator-vs-floss-orthodontic-sr-ma]]) and the regular-adult plaque SR ([[interdental-cleaning/mohapatra-2024-water-flosser-vs-floss-plaque-sr]]).
2. **Time-stratified subgroups** (short 2–3 wk, intermediate 4–6 wk, long >6 wk) reveal that most WJ benefits are short/intermediate and fade long-term.
3. **GRADE-graded bottom line**: BI gets moderate certainty; PI and GI are low to very low. The clinical message is calibrated, not over-claimed.
4. **Directly disputes Mohapatra (2023/2024)** — their finding of significant WJ-superior PI reduction is attributed to four of six included studies lacking a follow-up period (before/after-cleaning assessment), whereas this review required ≥2-week follow-up.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **PICO**: P = regular patients + fixed-orthodontic-appliance patients; I = WJ + manual toothbrushing (MTB); C = floss + MTB, or MTB alone; O = plaque index (PI), bleeding index (BI), gingival index (GI), at short/intermediate/long term.
- **Databases**: MEDLINE (PubMed), Cochrane Library, Google Scholar; studies 1990–July 2022; manual reference screening added.
- **Eligibility**: RCTs only; ≥2-week outcome timing; home irrigation device in intervention; floss or MTB-alone control. Excluded: peri-implantitis, impaired manual dexterity / uncontrolled systemic conditions, in-office/medicated irrigation, powered interdental devices other than WJ, powered brushes, non-floss interdental aids in control.
- **Screening yield**: 6464 + 30 manual → 6148 titles → 115 abstracts → 33 full text → **18 included; 15 in meta-analysis**.
- **Studies**: mostly parallel RCTs (1 crossover, 2 split-mouth); samples 20–117; **1005 participants** across included groups; ages 18–75 (regular) and 11–48 (orthodontic, included children).
- **Four comparison groups**: A = WJ vs floss, regular (4 studies); B = WJ vs floss, orthodontic (2); C = WJ vs MTB-alone, regular (9); D = WJ vs MTB-alone, orthodontic (5).
- **Analysis**: continuous outcomes as mean difference (MD) with 95% CI; fixed/random-effects per heterogeneity (I²); Egger/Begg publication-bias tests; sensitivity analysis; ROB 2; GRADE summary-of-findings tables.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Plaque Index (PI)** — no consistent WJ advantage:
- Group A (vs floss, regular): no significant difference at 2–3 wk (MD −0.04, p=0.92) or 4–6 wk (MD −0.29 [−0.65, 0.07], p=0.1; low certainty).
- Group B (vs floss, ortho): WJ slightly better at 2 wk (MD −0.62 [−0.74, −0.50], p<0.00001, 1 trial) but NS at 4 wk (MD −0.58 [−1.79, 0.64], p=0.35; low certainty).
- Group C (vs MTB-alone, regular): WJ very slight benefit at 2–3 wk, but **MTB-alone superior** at 4 wk (MD 0.90, p<0.00001) and 2–3 mo (MD 0.08, p=0.04) — inconsistent.
- Group D (vs MTB-alone, ortho): WJ superior at 4 wk (MD −0.36 [−0.67, −0.04], p=0.03, 5 trials); NS at 2–3 mo (MD −0.05, p=0.24; very low certainty).

**Bleeding Index (BI)** — the strongest signal (moderate certainty):
- Group A (vs floss, regular): WJ slightly better at 4–6 wk (MD −0.12 [−0.13, −0.10], p<0.00001, 3 trials; moderate); NS at 2–3 wk (p=0.75).
- Group B (vs floss, ortho): WJ better at 2 wk (MD −0.21 [−0.24, −0.18], p<0.00001); NS at 4 wk (MD −0.11, p=0.31; moderate).
- Group C (vs MTB-alone, regular): WJ better at 2 wk (MD −0.16, p<0.00001; moderate); equal at 1 mo (MD 0.00, p=1.00).
- Group D (vs MTB-alone, ortho): WJ superior at 1 mo (MD −0.67 [−0.77, −0.57]) and 2 mo (MD −0.19, p<0.00001; 1 trial, moderate).

**Gingival Index (GI)** — comparable to floss; slight edge over brushing-alone:
- Group A (vs floss, regular): no difference at 2–3 wk (MD −0.01, p=0.95) or 4–6 wk (MD −0.14, p=0.63; very low).
- Group C (vs MTB-alone, regular): WJ minimal improvement at 4 wk (MD −0.06 [−0.10, −0.02], p=0.004; moderate); NS at 2–3 mo (p=0.11).
- Group D (vs MTB-alone, ortho): little to no difference at 4–6 wk (MD −0.06 [−0.10, −0.02]) and NS long-term (MD −0.12, p=0.11; very low). No Group-B GI data reported.

**Overall**: WJ gives slight benefits in BI and GI vs floss or MTB-alone, more pronounced (a) vs brushing-alone than vs floss and (b) in orthodontic patients. No robust plaque superiority. Authors recommend WJ as an easy-to-use adjunct, NOT a brushing replacement.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Few and variable-quality studies; heterogeneity downgraded several GRADE ratings to low/very low.
- No periodontitis outcomes (probing depth) — too few studies reported them.
- Clinical significance uncertain: mean intergroup MDs may be too small to be clinically meaningful, and there is no established minimal-clinically-important-difference cutoff for PI/BI/GI.
- Several subgroup conclusions rest on a single trial (e.g., Group-D BI).
- Search ended July 2022; later RCTs not captured.
- Future work: adequately powered long-term RCTs, plus patient-centered outcomes (cost-effectiveness, compliance).

## 6. Related Work

- **Husseini et al. 2008** — irrigation-as-adjunct SR: trend favoring irrigation for BI/GI; concordant with this review.
- **Kotsakis et al. 2018** — network MA ranking 10 interdental tools; WJ ranked 2nd for GI/BI but no PI benefit vs brushing — concordant.
- **Worthington et al. 2019** — Cochrane-style MA: WJ reduced GI vs brushing/floss only at 1 mo, NS otherwise — broadly concordant.
- **Mohapatra et al. 2023/2024** — found WJ significantly superior to floss for PI; this review disputes it (their studies lacked follow-up) → see [[interdental-cleaning/mohapatra-2024-water-flosser-vs-floss-plaque-sr]].
- **Almoharib et al. 2023** — WJ more effective than MTB-alone for PI/bleeding in orthodontics — concordant with Group D.

## 7. Glossary

- **WJ (Water Jet / oral irrigator / water flosser)**: home device delivering a pulsating water stream for interdental cleaning.
- **MTB**: manual tooth brushing.
- **PI (Plaque Index)**: Silness & Löe plaque score.
- **BI (Bleeding Index) / BoP**: bleeding-on-probing or % bleeding sites.
- **GI (Gingival Index)**: Löe & Silness gingival inflammation score.
- **MD**: mean difference (continuous-outcome effect size).
- **ROB 2**: Cochrane Risk of Bias 2 tool.
- **GRADE**: certainty-of-evidence rating (high / moderate / low / very low).
- **Short/Intermediate/Long term**: 2–3 wk / 4–6 wk / >6 wk follow-up subgroups.
