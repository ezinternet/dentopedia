## Why Ingested

The ISQ literature in [[wiki/implants/isq/tisci-2026-isq-it-mbl-survival-sr-ma]] pools implants broadly without stratifying for diameter or systemic disease; this prospective case–control study fills the gap by isolating narrow-diameter implant (NDI, 3.3 mm TiZr SLActive) stability in uncontrolled T2DM (HbA1c > 6.5%) versus normoglycemic controls, extending the ISQ loading-threshold evidence base to a high-risk, narrow-diameter clinical scenario.

## One-line Summary

Prospective case–control study (n=32 patients, 48 NDIs, 3 months) showing ISQ rose significantly in both T2DM and normoglycemic patients from Day 3 to 3 months post-placement, with no significant between-group difference and no correlation to HbA1c, supporting the clinical use of 3.3 mm SLActive TiZr NDIs in posterior sites of uncontrolled T2DM patients.

## 한줄요약

전향적 환자-대조군 연구(32명, 48개 세경 임플란트, 3개월): 제2형 당뇨(HbA1c>6.5%) 환자와 정상혈당 환자 모두에서 식립 후 ISQ가 유의하게 증가했으며, 두 군 간 차이 및 HbA1c와의 상관관계는 없어 당뇨 환자에서 3.3mm SLActive TiZr 세경 임플란트의 임상적 사용 가능성을 지지한다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First prospective study specifically examining ISQ/RFA of narrow-diameter implants (3.3 mm TiZr Roxolid SLActive) in uncontrolled T2DM vs. normoglycemic patients.
2. Demonstrates that NDIs achieve steady ISQ improvement over 3 months in T2DM patients (HbA1c mean 7.34%) without significant compromise compared to normoglycemic controls.
3. No correlation found between HbA1c level and ISQ at any time point across all implant positions, contradicting earlier data from Oates et al. (2009) which used SLA (not SLActive) surfaces.
4. Suggests hydrophilic SLActive surface chemistry may counterbalance hyperglycemia-induced impairment of peri-implant bone healing around NDIs.
5. Final preload ISQ values (T2DM: 63.84 ± 6.05; Control: 63.84 ± 6.18) were equivalent between groups at 3 months.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Study design**: Prospective case–control study

**Participants**:
- 32 total (0 dropouts), mean age 67 years
- Test group: 16 patients with T2DM (HbA1c > 6.5%; mean 7.34% ± 0.73)
- Control group: 16 normoglycemic patients (HbA1c < 5.9%; mean 5.3% ± 0.4)
- Groups matched for age, gender, and implant localization
- Exclusion criteria: untreated periodontitis, insufficient oral hygiene, smoking, medications affecting blood perfusion or bone metabolism

**Implants**:
- 48 total (23 test, 25 control)
- 3.3 mm diameter TiZr Roxolid tissue-level implants, SLActive surface (Institut Straumann AG)
- Lengths: 8 mm (n=9), 10 mm (n=23), 12 mm (n=16)
- Posterior to canine, transmucosal healing protocol, no augmentation by protocol

**ISQ Measurement**:
- Osstell ISQ meter with SmartPeg Type 04 magnetic pegs
- Timepoints: Day 3 (V3), Day 7 (V4), Week 4 (V5), Month 3 (V6)
- Two measurements per implant (mesial + buccal), mean used
- Single calibrated investigator (M.W.)

**Statistical analysis**:
- Normality: Shapiro–Wilk, Kolmogorov–Smirnov, D'Agostino–Pearson
- Repeated-measures ANOVA + Dunnett's multiple comparisons (normal data)
- Friedman test + Dunnett's (non-normal data)
- Between-group: Sidak's multiple comparisons test
- HbA1c correlation: Pearson coefficient
- Software: GraphPad Prism 8; significance α = 0.05

**Ethics**: Witten/Herdecke University Ethics Committee (108/2012); informed consent obtained

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**ISQ over time — T2DM group** (n=23 implants):
- V3 (Day 3): 55.87 ± 5.99
- V4 (Day 7): 58.17 ± 7.09 (p = .3119 vs V3)
- V5 (Week 4): 60.13 ± 5.79 (p = .1550 vs V3)
- V6 (Month 3): 63.84 ± 6.05 (p = .0098 vs V3) — significant increase

**ISQ over time — Control group** (n=25 implants):
- V3 (Day 3): 51.41 ± 9.62
- V4 (Day 7): 58.17 ± 8.37 (p = .0238 vs V3) — most substantial jump
- V5 (Week 4): 61.53 ± 6.57 (p = .1469 vs V3)
- V6 (Month 3): 63.84 ± 6.18 (p = .0216 vs V3)

**Key findings**:
- Both groups reached equivalent ISQ at Month 3 (63.84 for both); no between-group significant difference at any time point (Sidak's test)
- T2DM group showed steadier, more gradual ISQ rise; control group showed its largest jump between Day 3 and Day 7
- No significant correlation between HbA1c and ISQ at any visit (Pearson)
- Exception: significant positive correlation between HbA1c and ISQ at V5 in maxillary implants in the T2DM group (r=0.7984, p=.0313), attributed to small sample size (n=7 maxillary patients) and considered incidental
- No difference in stability between maxillary and mandibular implants
- All 48 implants osseointegrated at the 3-month endpoint

**Contextual ISQ note**: Final ISQ values (63–64) are somewhat lower than typical values reported for regular-diameter implants with similar design, consistent with known diameter–ISQ relationship; within the ISQ 55–65 "safe" range for Straumann systems.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

1. **Small sample size**: Calculated minimum was n=26; enrolled n=32, but sub-group analyses (e.g., maxilla only, n=7) are underpowered.
2. **HbA1c range**: Mean T2DM HbA1c was 7.34% (< 8.1%); results may not generalize to patients with HbA1c > 8.1% where Oates et al. (2009) found stability impairment.
3. **Short observation period**: 3 months only; long-term survival and marginal bone loss data not collected.
4. **Implant-level focus**: Loading outcomes (prosthetic success, patient satisfaction) not assessed.
5. **No SLA comparator arm**: Cannot directly confirm whether SLActive surface is responsible for the positive outcomes over SLA in T2DM.
6. **Single implant system**: All implants were Straumann 3.3 mm TiZr Roxolid SLActive; findings may not generalize to other NDI systems.
7. Future studies should assess long-term survival of NDIs under hyperglycemic conditions with larger cohorts.

## 6. Related Work

- **Oates et al. (2009)**: Key predecessor; showed HbA1c > 8.1% correlated with reduced implant stability (SLA surface, not NDI-specific). Contradicted by this study.
- **Khandelwal et al. (2013)**: RCT comparing SLA vs. modified SLA implants in poorly controlled T2DM; noted tendency for higher ISQ in HbA1c > 9.6% group.
- **Cabrera-Domínguez et al. (2020)**: Prospective case–control of TiZr NDIs in controlled T2DM, 2-year results — corroborating evidence that SLActive surface supports osseointegration in diabetic patients.
- **Friedmann et al. (2021)**: Pilot study of NDIs in hyperglycemic vs. normoglycemic patients at 1 year (same research group; precursor to this study).
- **Schiegnitz & Al-Nawas (2018)**: SR+MA establishing clinical success of NDIs as an alternative to augmentation + regular-diameter implants.
- **Sennerby & Meredith (2008)**: Foundational ISQ/RFA review; ISQ 55–65 cited as safe range for Straumann.
- **Kim et al. (2017)**: Prospective clinical trial confirming positive correlation between implant diameter and ISQ values.

## 7. Glossary

- **NDI (Narrow-Diameter Implant, 세경 임플란트)**: Implant with diameter ≤ 3.5 mm; here specifically 3.3 mm Straumann Roxolid TiZr
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient, 임플란트 안정성 지수)**: Dimensionless unit (1–100) from RFA measurement; higher = greater stability
- **RFA (Resonance Frequency Analysis, 공명주파수분석)**: Non-invasive method using magnetic peg vibration to quantify implant stability
- **T2DM (Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus, 제2형 당뇨병)**: Metabolic disorder characterized by hyperglycemia due to insulin resistance or deficiency
- **HbA1c (당화혈색소)**: Glycated hemoglobin; reflects average blood glucose over ~3 months; >6.5% = T2DM diagnostic threshold
- **SLActive (화학적 개질 모래분사·산처리 표면)**: Straumann hydrophilic implant surface; accelerates protein adsorption and osteogenic gene expression vs. conventional SLA
- **TiZr (Roxolid)**: Titanium-zirconium alloy used by Straumann for NDIs; higher tensile strength than pure titanium
- **SmartPeg Type 04**: Magnetic resonance transducer peg used with Osstell ISQ meter for 3.3 mm Straumann tissue-level implants
- **V3/V4/V5/V6**: Study visit nomenclature — V3=Day 3, V4=Day 7, V5=Week 4, V6=Month 3
