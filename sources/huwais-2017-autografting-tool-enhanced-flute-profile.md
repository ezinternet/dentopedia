## One-line Summary

PCT patent (WO 2017/124079 A1; applicant Huwais IP Holding LLC, inventor Salah Huwais) for the osseodensification "Densah/Versah" rotary osteotome — a tapered multi-fluted bur whose key improvement is a *continuously negative rake angle*, with each flute carrying a cutting face and an opposing densifying face so the same tool cuts when rotated clockwise and condenses/autografts bone hydraulically when rotated counterclockwise.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Continuously negative rake angle flute (the claimed improvement)**: Claim 1's "wherein the improvement comprises: said flutes are formed with a continuously negative rake angle along the lengths thereof." This is the geometric heart of osseodensification.
2. **Dual-face flute / single bidirectional tool**: each helically spiraling flute has a *cutting face* (defining the rake angle) on one side and a *densifying face* (defining a heel-side angle) on the other; a land between adjacent flutes carries a *working edge* along the cutting face. → the same bur cuts in one rotation direction and condenses in the other.
3. **Hydraulic autografting mechanism**: flutes trap semi-viscous bone particles + collagen + blood under copious irrigation; the stopper section's piston-like effect plus the impeller-like outward force vectors of the negative rake build hydraulic pressure that deforms (expands) the osteotomy side-walls and autografts a bone slurry into them, forming a densified crust.
4. **Conically tapered body**: claim 9 — body tapers from maximum diameter near the shank to minimum diameter near the apical end (with stopper section).
5. **Non-medical applicability**: the specification states the tool/method also applies to expanding holes in metals and other non-organic host materials.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Independent claim 1 — a rotary osteotome comprising:
- a **shank** establishing a longitudinal axis of rotation;
- a **body** extending from the shank with an **apical end** remote from the shank;
- a plurality of **helically spiraling flutes**, each flute with a **cutting face** (rake angle) on one side and a **densifying face** (heel-side angle) on the other;
- a **stopper section** of the body between the flutes and the shank;
- a **land** between each adjacent pair of flutes, each land joining the densifying face of one flute and carrying a **working edge** along the cutting face of the adjacent flute, the working edge helically twisting about the body;
- improvement: flutes formed with a **continuously negative rake angle** along their lengths.

Dependent claims: 2–6 (negative rake angle regressive/progressive, total variance <30° or >30°); 7–8 (heel-side angle constant or fluctuating <30°); 9 (conically tapered body); 10 (working edges widen along the body away from a non-cutting direction); 11 (method claim).

Method claim 11: irrigate a precursor hole; rotate the body in a **cutting direction at >~200 RPM** (cutting direction = sweeping the cutting faces into the host material); the cutting faces release bone particles that are trapped in the flutes; hydraulic pressure builds and, via the working edges + stopper section, deforms/expands the side-walls and autografts the slurry — producing a clean, semi-condensed osteotomy in cutting mode alone (optionally intensified by the "DAC" protocol).

Mechanistic description (spec [0147]–[0148]): bone is visco-elastic — within a moderate strain (the straight-line, origin-to-B segment of the strain curve) it responds elastically; beyond B it deforms plastically. The densification crust is optimally formed when an expansion-oriented (radially outward) strain is imposed in the strain-hardening range (B-to-D). Negative rake angles let the osteotome transition from cutting to autografting/densifying with continued high-speed rotation as the stopper section plugs the osteotomy.

## 7. Glossary

- **Rake angle**: the angle of the cutting face relative to the work; a *negative* rake angle pushes/burnishes rather than slices cleanly — central to OD compaction.
- **Densifying face / cutting face**: the two opposing faces of each flute that make the bur bidirectional (condense vs cut).
- **Land / working edge**: the surface between two flutes and the helical edge along the cutting face that engages bone.
- **Stopper section**: a body section that plugs the osteotomy to build hydraulic pressure (piston effect).
- **Autografting**: hydraulically driving the patient's own bone slurry into the osteotomy walls (no external graft).
