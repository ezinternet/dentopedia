## Why Ingested

위축된 무치악 상악 즉시 로딩 전악 재건에서 경사식립(tilted) 임플란트가 축방향(axial) 임플란트 대비 생존율·변연골소실에서 불리한지에 대한 정량 근거. All-on-X·full-arch 즉시 로딩을 다룬 [[immediate-implant/yaghmai-2025-palatal-root-socket-all-on-x]]·[[immediate-implant/heimes-2025-immediate-implant-full-arch-rehabilitation]]와 보철 설계 측면에서 연결된다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (11 studies survival, 7 MBL) finding no significant difference between tilted and axial implants in immediately loaded atrophic edentulous maxillae at 3 years for implant survival (RR 1.00, 95% CI 0.98–1.01) or marginal bone loss (MD −0.02 mm, 95% CI −0.09 to 0.06).

## 한줄요약

SR+MA(생존 11편·MBL 7편) — 위축 무치악 상악 즉시 로딩 재건에서 3년 시점 경사식립과 축방향 임플란트 간 생존율(RR 1.00)·변연골소실(MD −0.02 mm) 차이가 없었다.

## 1. Document Information

- Journal: The Journal of Indian Prosthodontic Society 2021;21:217–228.
- College of Dental Sciences and Research Centre, Ahmedabad, India.
- PRISMA; Evidence Project risk-of-bias tool; RevMan 5.4; GRADEpro.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Pools survival and MBL evidence for tilted vs axial implants specifically in the immediately loaded atrophic maxilla.
- Supports tilted-implant designs (e.g., All-on-4-type) as biomechanically non-inferior at 3 years.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- SR+MA; MEDLINE, CENTRAL, Science Direct, Google Scholar + hand search; no date limit; English only.
- Outcomes: 3-year implant survival and marginal bone loss.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- 11 studies finalized (survival); 7 in MBL meta-analysis.
- Implant survival (3 yr): RR 1.00 (95% CI 0.98–1.01), P = 0.59 — no difference.
- Marginal bone loss (3 yr): MD −0.02 mm (95% CI −0.09 to 0.06), P = 0.69 — no difference.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- 3-year horizon; heterogeneous study designs; mostly observational evidence.
- Does not address full-arch prosthetic complications or longer-term outcomes.

## 6. Related Work

- All-on-4 / tilted-implant full-arch immediate-loading literature.

## 7. Glossary

- **Tilted implant**: implant placed at an angle (often to avoid anatomy or maximize anteroposterior spread), angle-corrected at the abutment.
- **Axial implant**: implant placed along the planned prosthetic long axis.
- **MBL**: marginal bone loss.
