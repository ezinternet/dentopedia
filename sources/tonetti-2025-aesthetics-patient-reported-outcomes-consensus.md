## Why Ingested

이 EFP 전문가 합의 보고서는 치주 및 임플란트 영역에서 PRO(Patient-Reported Outcome)가 임상가 평가 지표(CRO)와 어떻게 다르고 어떻게 통합되어야 하는지를 체계적으로 정의한 첫 번째 합의 문건으로, [[wiki/behavioral-dentistry/patient-reported-outcomes/arunyanak-2024-patient-related-outcomes-experience-periodontics]]의 개별 연구 결과를 정책·방법론 수준으로 확장한다.

## One-line Summary

EFP 2025 expert consensus (40 experts, 5 SRs) establishes that clinician-reported aesthetic scores and patient-reported outcomes (PROs) provide complementary but discordant perspectives on root coverage and implant soft-tissue procedures, and calls for their systematic integration in future trials.

## 한줄요약

EFP 2025 전문가 합의(전문가 40인, 체계적 고찰 5편): 치근피개술·임플란트 연조직 처치에서 임상가 심미점수와 환자보고결과(PRO)는 보완적이나 불일치하며, 향후 임상시험에서 PRO·CRO·PRE의 통합 보고가 필요하다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **PRO vs CRO discordance formalized**: For root coverage (RC) of single/multiple GRDs, a positive correlation exists between clinician-reported aesthetic scores and %RC, but patient-reported aesthetic perceptions do NOT correspondingly correlate — highlighting a systematic gap.
2. **Implant soft-tissue outcomes**: CTG at immediate implant placement significantly mitigates apical shift in mid-facial mucosal level, yet clinician- and patient-reported aesthetic scores failed to capture this benefit; neither captured volume improvement.
3. **Peri-implant dehiscence treatment**: Soft-tissue augmentation (STA) using CTG beneath CAF produced favorable outcomes in both CRO and PRO aesthetics — one scenario where both perspectives align.
4. **Terminology standardized**: Formally distinguishes PRO, PRE (patient-reported experience), CRO (clinician-reported outcome), PROM (patient-reported outcome measure), and minimally important difference (MID).
5. **Call for integrated trials**: Recommends future RCTs report PREs, PROs, and CROs together, including benefit and harm data.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Workshop format**: Two working groups — WG1 (GRDs around teeth; chair: Tonetti, Herrera) and WG2 (implants and peri-implant dehiscences; chair: Sanz, Chapple).
- **Evidence base**: Five commissioned systematic reviews covering PREs/PROs/CROs across five intervention categories:
  1. RC surgery for single GRDs (Cairo et al. 2025)
  2. RC surgery for multiple GRDs (Stefanini et al. 2025)
  3. STA adjunctive to immediate implant placement (Ramanauskaite et al. 2025)
  4. STA during early/delayed/late implant placement (Lambert et al. 2025)
  5. STA to correct peri-implant buccal soft-tissue dehiscences (Nart et al. 2025)
- **Clinical scenarios**: (i) GRDs, (ii) peri-implant buccal soft-tissue dehiscences (PSTD), (iii) immediate/early/delayed/late implant placement.
- **Outcome hierarchy**: PROMs considered the reference tool when validated; MID determination emphasized for clinical meaningfulness.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Gingival Recession Defects (Single)
- CAF+CTG: best CRO aesthetic outcomes; root coverage improvement correlates with clinician aesthetic scores.
- Patient-reported aesthetic perception: **not correlated** with %RC or clinician scores.
- GRD prevalence: 88% of adults ≥65 and 50% of adults 18–64 have ≥1 recession site; RT1 prevalence varies widely (0.3%–40.9% depending on population).
- Patients perceive only ~28% of diagnosed GRDs; 92% of recessions are asymptomatic or unnoticed.

### Gingival Recession Defects (Multiple)
- Similar discordance pattern between CRO and PRO.
- PROs measuring satisfaction and pain/discomfort remain inconsistently reported across trials.

### Immediate Implant Placement + Soft-Tissue Augmentation
- CTG at immediate implant placement: **significantly reduces** apical shift in mid-facial mucosal level.
- CRO and PRO aesthetic scores: **did not capture** this volumetric/marginal gain.
- Volume improvement from grafting: not reflected in standard aesthetic score tools.

### Early/Delayed/Late Implant Placement + STA
- PROs and CRO data sparse; heterogeneity limits conclusions.

### Peri-implant Soft-Tissue Dehiscences (PSTD)
- STA (CTG under CAF): favorable results in both clinician- and patient-perceived aesthetics.
- **Aligned CRO–PRO outcome** — only scenario where both measures tracked together.

### Methodological Findings
- MID for periodontal/implant PROMs not established in most contexts.
- Substantial heterogeneity in PROM instruments used across studies.
- PREs (treatment experience/satisfaction) systematically under-reported.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- PRO and PRE data currently sparse, inconsistently collected, and methodologically heterogeneous across RCTs.
- Validated, disease-specific PROMs with established MIDs are lacking for most periodontal indications.
- Socio-economic, cultural, and geographic determinants of patient concerns are under-studied.
- Need for standardized PROM battery for future periodontal/implant RCTs.
- Harm reporting (adverse events from patient perspective) largely absent in current literature.
- The commissioned SRs highlight the need for future trials with co-primary CRO and PRO endpoints.

## 6. Related Work

- Tonetti et al. 2014 (10th European Workshop) — first formal framing of PROs as 'true endpoints' in periodontics.
- Cairo et al. 2009 — Root Coverage Esthetic Score (RES), standard CRO tool.

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| PRO | Patient-Reported Outcome — health status reported directly by the patient |
| PRE | Patient-Reported Experience — assessment of the experience of treatment (e.g., satisfaction) |
| CRO | Clinician-Reported Outcome — assessment generated by a clinician/investigator |
| PROM | Patient-Reported Outcome Measure — validated, standardized tool to assess a PRO |
| MID | Minimally Important Difference — smallest change in PROM score meaningful to the patient |
| GRD | Gingival Recession Defect |
| RT1 | Recession Type 1 — not associated with periodontitis (Cairo classification) |
| CAF | Coronally Advanced Flap |
| CTG | Connective Tissue Graft |
| RC | Root Coverage |
| %CRC | Percentage of Complete Root Coverage |
| STA | Soft-Tissue Augmentation |
| PSTD | Peri-implant Soft-Tissue Dehiscence |
| KTW | Keratinized Tissue Width |
| GT | Gingival Thickness |
| NCCL | Non-Carious Cervical Lesion |
| EFP | European Federation of Periodontology |
| SIdP | Società Italiana di Parodontologia e Implantologia |
