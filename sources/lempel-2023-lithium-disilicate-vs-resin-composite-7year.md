## 2. Key Contributions
- LidiSi 96.8% survival vs RBC 84.9% at 7.8 years (p<0.05)
- Annual failure rate: LidiSi 0.2%, RBC 1.0%
- Both >98% survival at 6 years; RBC dropped to 60% by year 15
- Failure causes: secondary caries, fracture, endodontic complication
- LidiSi additional failure: tooth fracture; RBC: marginal discoloration

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Retrospective evaluation (n=91) of lithium disilicate (LidiSi) vs resin-based composite (RBC) inlay/onlay/overlay restorations placed 2007-2017. Mean observation 7.8±3.3 years. LidiSi survival 96.8% vs RBC 84.9%; annual failure rate 0.2% vs 1.0%. LidiSi survival remained >95% through 15 years while RBC dropped to 60%. Failure causes: secondary caries, fracture, endodontic complication. Modified USPHS criteria; Kaplan-Meier analysis.
