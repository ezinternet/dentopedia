## One-line Summary

Narrative review: the nerve to the mylohyoid — traditionally classified as purely motor — carries accessory sensory innervation to mandibular teeth; because of variable branching location and the barrier formed by the pterygomandibular fascia/sphenomandibular ligament, it may escape anesthesia during a standard inferior alveolar nerve block, contributing to IANB's high failure rate.

## 한줄요약

내러티브 리뷰: 전통적으로 순수 운동신경으로 분류된 이설골설근신경(nerve to the mylohyoid)이 실제로는 하악치아에 부가 감각신경을 공급하며, 분지 위치의 변이성과 익돌하악근막(pterygomandibular fascia)·접형하악인대(sphenomandibular ligament)가 만드는 장벽 때문에 표준 IANB로도 마취되지 않을 수 있어 IANB의 높은 실패율에 기여한다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Challenges the traditional teaching that the **nerve to the mylohyoid is purely motor** — synthesizes dissection and clinical evidence that it carries accessory sensory fibers to mandibular teeth.
- Explains the anatomical barrier mechanism: the nerve to the mylohyoid branches from the inferior alveolar nerve **within the infratemporal fossa** at a variable location, and the **pterygomandibular fascia** and **sphenomandibular ligament** can form physical barriers that keep it outside the spread of a standard IANB injection.
- Outlines techniques to specifically achieve neural blockade of the nerve to the mylohyoid when standard IANB proves inadequate.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Narrative synthesis of prior anatomical dissection studies and clinical anesthesia-failure literature on the nerve to the mylohyoid.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- The nerve to the mylohyoid can be anesthetized by an IANB **when** its branching point and course fall within the injected field — but variability in branching location means this is not guaranteed.
- Proposes this variability as a contributing (not sole) explanation for IANB's well-documented high failure rate.
- Reviews alternative injection approaches aimed at ensuring the nerve to the mylohyoid is blocked (e.g., depositing anesthetic more broadly/deeply relative to the pterygomandibular space).

## 7. Glossary

- **Nerve to the mylohyoid**: a branch of the inferior alveolar nerve (given off before it enters the mandibular foramen) that classically innervates the mylohyoid muscle and anterior belly of digastric — this review argues it also carries sensory fibers to mandibular teeth in some individuals.
- **Pterygomandibular fascia / sphenomandibular ligament**: anatomical structures near the mandibular foramen that can act as barriers limiting local anesthetic spread to accessory nerve branches.
