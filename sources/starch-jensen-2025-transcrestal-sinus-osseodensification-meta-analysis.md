## One-line Summary
SR+MA (6 RCTs, search to 2025-03-19, low GRADE): Osseodensification-mediated transcrestal sinus elevation (TSMEOD) yields **higher ISQ at placement and abutment connection** vs osteotome-mediated (OMSFE) or lateral window (MSFA), with comparable implant survival.

## 1. Document Information
JOMR open-access SR+MA. PRISMA-compliant. Database search: MEDLINE/Embase/Cochrane through March 19, 2025.

## 2. Key Contributions
- **TSMEOD significantly higher ISQ** at both placement and abutment connection vs OMSFE and MSFA
- Comparable implant survival across all three techniques (short-term)
- Reduced surgery duration, complications, analgesic days for TSMEOD
- Lower endo-sinus bone gain (ESBG) for TSMEOD vs MSFA; equal to OMSFE

## 3. Methodology
- 6 RCTs, ≥6 month observation
- Comparisons: TSMEOD vs OMSFE; TSMEOD vs MSFA
- Outcomes: survival (primary); ISQ, MBL, complications, ESBG, bone density, PROMs (secondary)
- All studies rated **high risk of bias**, GRADE **low**

## 4. Key Results
| Outcome | TSMEOD vs OMSFE | TSMEOD vs MSFA |
|---|---|---|
| Implant survival | Equal | Equal |
| ISQ (placement, abutment) | **TSMEOD higher** | **TSMEOD higher** |
| Surgery duration | TSMEOD shorter | TSMEOD shorter |
| Complications | TSMEOD fewer | TSMEOD fewer |
| Analgesic days | TSMEOD fewer | TSMEOD fewer |
| ESBG | Equal | TSMEOD less |

## 5. Limitations
- Only 6 RCTs, all short-term
- High risk of bias
- Low GRADE certainty
- No long-term RCT data
