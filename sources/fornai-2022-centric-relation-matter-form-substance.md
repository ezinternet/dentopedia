## Why Ingested

Direct published rebuttal to [[occlusion/zonnenberg-2021-centric-relation-critically-revisited-clinical]]. Provides the counter-argument that condylar position should be actively monitored when occlusion is changed (vs Zonnenberg's "ignore it" conclusion), supplying a key threshold (Pullinger ≈2 mm sagittal slide cut-off) and the GPT-9 CR-definition 29% consensus figure that quantify the terminology debate. Ingested to balance the occlusion category's CR coverage with the opposing clinical school.

## One-line Summary

Letter/commentary (rebuttal to Zonnenberg et al. 2021) arguing that although the term "centric relation" should be abandoned, condylar position is clinically meaningful and must be monitored when occlusion is altered, proposing "Reference Position (RP)" as the replacement.

## 한줄요약

Zonnenberg(2021)에 대한 반박 서신으로, "centric relation" 용어는 폐기하되 교합을 바꿀 때 과두 위치를 적극 모니터링해야 한다고 주장하며 대체 개념으로 "Reference Position (RP)"을 제안한다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Reframes the CR debate: agrees the CR *term* is unclear and should be abandoned, but reaches the OPPOSITE clinical conclusion to Zonnenberg — condylar position matters and should be monitored.
- Distinguishes "biological" (phenotypically expressed / occurring in nature) from "clinically normal": biological variation of the stomatognathic structures (malocclusion) can be pathological. Frequency ≠ functional normality.
- Quantifies the asymptomatic envelope of the CR–MIP sagittal slide via Pullinger, Seligman & Gornbein (~2 mm cut-off).
- Proposes "Reference Position (RP)" — an unstrained retral border position not exploiting retrodiscal shock-absorbing tissue — as the replacement concept.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Argumentative commentary; no new primary data. Marshals existing literature (Pullinger/Seligman/Gornbein on occlusal slide; Piehslinger/Celar/Slavicek on Reference Position; Gerber on iatrogenic risk of forced retrusion; GPT-9 consensus survey) to rebut the target review's clinical recommendation.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Pullinger, Seligman & Gornbein: sagittal occlusal-slide cut-off ≈ **2 mm** — in their sample **none** of the asymptomatic subjects had a slide >2 mm, and only **6%** had a slide length between 1 and 2 mm. A CR–MIP slide >2 mm is therefore atypical of asymptomatic individuals.
- The Glossary of Prosthodontic Terms-9 (GPT-9) CR definition received only **29%** expert consensus.
- TMJ adaptive remodelling is reduced in joint inflammation/degeneration and more limited in adults than in children — so relying on remodelling to absorb occlusal-treatment-induced condylar change is risky.
- TMJ skeletal morphology is associated with disc derangement; small occlusal-treatment-derived changes in condylar relationship can improve TMD symptoms.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

Opinion piece without new data; conclusions are interpretive and contested (the very nature of the CR debate). "Reference Position" reproducibility is asserted from cited sources rather than tested here. Threshold values (2 mm slide) are drawn from a single cited dataset.

## 7. Glossary

- **Centric relation (CR)**: maxillomandibular relationship with condyles in an anterosuperior braced position against the articular eminence; definition contested (only 29% GPT-9 consensus).
- **MIP / MICP (maximal intercuspal position)**: tooth-determined position of maximum occlusal contact; determines condylar position within the fossa.
- **Occlusal (CR–MIP) slide**: discrepancy/sliding movement from CR to MIP; sagittal component cut-off ≈2 mm in asymptomatic subjects.
- **Reference Position (RP)**: proposed replacement — unstrained physiologic retral border position with joint unloaded, ligaments not in tension, minimal muscle activity, not exploiting retrodiscal tissue.
- **Iatrogenic effect**: clinician-induced harm — here, altered condyle-to-fossa relation from unmonitored occlusal change or forced retrusion.
