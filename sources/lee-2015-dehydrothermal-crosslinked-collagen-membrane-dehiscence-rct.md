## Why Ingested

차폐막 가교 여부(cross-linking)가 임플란트 열개결손(peri-implant dehiscence) GBR에서 조작성·잔존성·노출률에 미치는 영향을 직접 RCT로 검증. 기존 [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/choi-2017-single-double-layer-collagen-membrane-arp]]의 콜라겐막 층수 논의와 [[bone-regeneration/wessing-2018-gbr-collagen-membranes-particulate-graft-sr-ma]] SR+MA의 "가교막 노출률 ~30%↑" 결론을 단일 RCT 수준에서 보강·대조하는 근거.

## One-line Summary
Randomized single-blinded RCT (n=28 dehiscence sites) comparing dehydrothermally cross-linked collagen membrane (DCM) vs non-cross-linked native collagen membrane (NCM) for peri-implant dehiscence GBR; both gave significant clinical/radiographic improvement at 16 weeks, DCM showed better handling/residual membrane without increased complications.

## 한줄요약
열탈수가교 콜라겐막(DCM)과 비가교 천연막(NCM)을 임플란트 열개결손 GBR에 비교한 단일맹검 RCT(n=28). 16주에 두 막 모두 임상·방사선 지표 유의 개선, DCM이 조작성·잔존성에서 우수하면서 합병증 증가는 없었음.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Journal of Periodontology & Implant Science (J Periodontal Implant Sci) 2015;45(6):229-237
- DOI: 10.5051/jpis.2015.45.6.229
- Institution: Yonsei University College of Dentistry (Periodontology), Kyung Hee University
- Design: Randomized single-blinded clinical trial

## 2. Key Contributions
- Head-to-head RCT of cross-linked (DCM) vs non-cross-linked (NCM) collagen membrane specifically at peri-implant dehiscence defects.
- Multi-domain outcome set: clinical defect width/height (probe), horizontal thickness + bone density (CBCT, 1/2/3 mm below platform), surgeon-rated handling (VAS), and histologic membrane degradation.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 28 patients with buccal dehiscence defects after implant placement.
- Defect width/height measured by periodontal probe at baseline and 16 weeks.
- Membrane manipulation/maintenance scored by VAS at baseline.
- Horizontal thickness change at 1/2/3 mm below platform + mean bone density via CBCT at 16 weeks.
- Membrane degradation observed histologically in peri-implant soft tissue before re-entry.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- 5 sites (2 NCM, 3 DCM) had soft-tissue dehiscence + membrane exposure in early healing, but no severe complications in either group.
- Significant clinical and radiological improvement in all parameters with both membranes.
- Partially resorbed membrane leaflets observed histologically only in DCM group (slower degradation → longer barrier function).
- DCM judged comparable-to-better in handling with similar safety profile.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small n; single 16-week timepoint; surrogate (CBCT thickness/density) rather than histomorphometric new-bone quantification.
- Exposure event count too small for statistical comparison of exposure risk.

## 6. Related Work
- Choi 2017 (single vs double-layer collagen membrane, ARP).
- Wessing 2018 SR+MA (cross-linking → higher exposure rate).
- Troiano 2018 (bone graft + membrane ridge preservation).

## 7. Glossary
- DCM: dehydrothermally cross-linked collagen membrane.
- NCM: non-cross-linked native collagen membrane.
- Dehiscence defect: buccal bone fenestration/exposure of implant threads requiring GBR.
