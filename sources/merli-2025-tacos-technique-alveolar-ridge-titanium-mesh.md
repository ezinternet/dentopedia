## One-line Summary

Technical note / stepwise protocol (no patient outcome dataset): describes the TACOS technique (Titanium Alveolar Customized Osteogenic Scaffold) — a ~14-step procedure for horizontal+vertical ridge augmentation with CAD/CAM/SLM customized titanium mesh, autogenous bone + DBBM (≥60:40), cortical perforations for RAP, CGF, and resorbable membrane over the mesh.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Defines and names the **TACOS Technique** (Titanium Alveolar Customized Osteogenic Scaffold) — a systematic, reproducible protocol for horizontal and vertical alveolar ridge augmentation using customized titanium mesh.
- Unlike outcome-focused papers, the explicit goal is **practical step-by-step execution** as a clinician's guide.
- Integrates digital planning (pre-planned bone volume, PBV), piezoelectric intraoral bone harvesting, biological accelerators (cortical perforations for regional acceleratory phenomenon, concentrated growth factors), and a layered barrier (resorbable membrane over the Ti-mesh).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Indications: patients needing implants with severe atrophic edentulous crest (upper or lower jaw). Preoperative CBCT to plan mesh design. Exclusions: systemic/local contraindications, smokers, pregnant/lactating women. Conscious sedation (midazolam, atropine) with antibiotic/analgesic protocol (ceftriaxone, tramadol, ketorolac, betamethasone).

Stepwise surgical protocol:
1. **Incision & flap**: articaine 1:100,000; mid-crestal (lower) or paracrestal buccal (upper) incision; mucogingival approach with split-thickness papillae anticipating coronally advanced flap, then full-thickness flap.
2. **Site preparation**: debride crest (Lucas/Volkmann spoons or piezoelectric inserts).
3. **Customized mesh fitting**: CAD/CAM mesh (YXoss CBR Fully Protect, ReOss) trial-fitted; pre-plan bone volume (PBV).
4. **Bone landmarks for screw fixation**: 0.8 mm drill creates landmarks to register planned bone volume.
5. **Autologous bone harvesting**: from ramus–corpus of homolateral mandible; safe-scraper and/or bone block via calibrated ultrasonic tips (4 osteotomy lines, OT12 straight + OT8L/R angled), respecting inferior alveolar nerve on CBCT.
6. **Mesh filling outside the mouth**: autologous bone mixed with DBBM (Bio-Oss) — text states autologous:DBBM at least 60:40 (figure legend notes ~80:20 in the illustrated case).
7. **Opening marrow spaces**: cortical perforations in recipient bone (0.8 mm drill) to activate regional acceleratory phenomenon (RAP) and blood supply; done dry at low speed (100–150 rpm), dusty bone left in situ.
8. **Mesh fixation**: filled mesh fixed with osteosynthesis screws (Ø1.3 × 6 mm, Stoma) at pre-marked landmarks.
9. **Bone pressed inside the mesh**: additional graft compacted through central tracks (the designed "weak region" for later removal).
10. **Bioactive agents — first layer**: concentrated growth factors (CGF) applied over the graft.
11. **Resorbable membrane**: Bio-Gide placed over the mesh, fixed buccally with titanium tracks (template for precise cutting).
(Subsequent steps in the protocol cover further CGF layering and tension-free primary flap closure / suturing.)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- This is a **technical note**: it reports no quantitative patient cohort, no augmentation-millimeter outcome dataset, and no survival statistics. The abstract states the protocol "has demonstrated predictable results in both clinical and radiographic evaluations" but provides no numeric outcomes in the extracted text.
- Stated practical benefits: pre-planning of graft volume during diagnostic phase, improved graft stability and defect adaptation versus conventional non-resorbable membranes, and a designed weak-track region for easier second-stage mesh removal.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- No comparative or outcome data — a procedural description, not evidence of efficacy; claims of predictability are not backed by a reported dataset here.
- Single-technique, single-group descriptive note; generalizability and complication rates (e.g. mesh exposure) not quantified.
- Materials and device choices (YXoss mesh, Bio-Oss, Bio-Gide, CGF) are brand-specific.

## 6. Related Work

- Builds on the customized-Ti-mesh GBR literature; conceptually paired with outcome series such as Chiapasco 2021. References cite CAD/CAM/SLM mesh fabrication, GBR membranes (resorbable vs non-resorbable), regional acceleratory phenomenon, and CGF rationale.

## 7. Glossary

- **TACOS**: Titanium Alveolar Customized Osteogenic Scaffold — the named protocol.
- **PBV (Planned Bone Volume)**: digitally pre-calculated graft volume the mesh is designed to contain.
- **DBBM (Deproteinized Bovine Bone Mineral)**: xenograft (Bio-Oss); mixed with autologous bone (≥60:40).
- **RAP (Regional Acceleratory Phenomenon)**: accelerated local healing/remodeling triggered by cortical perforation injury.
- **CGF (Concentrated Growth Factors)**: autologous platelet-derived fraction applied to enhance osteogenesis/soft-tissue healing.
- **SLM (Selective Laser Melting)**: additive manufacturing method for the customized titanium mesh.
