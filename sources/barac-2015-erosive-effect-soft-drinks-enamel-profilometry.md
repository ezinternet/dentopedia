## Why Ingested
Quantifies the erosive effect of five common soft drinks on human enamel via stylus profilometry,
providing pH and titratable-acidity benchmarks directly relevant to the dietary-erosion etiology
discussed in [[dental-erosion/kanzow-2016-etiology-pathogenesis-dental-erosion]] and the
acid-challenge methodology used in [[dental-erosion/souza-2020-citrus-sweets-enamel-erosion-invitro]].

## One-line Summary
In-vitro profilometry study (n = 96 enamel specimens from 24 third molars) comparing the
erosive potential of five soft drinks over 10 days (30 immersions), finding Coca-Cola (pH 2.67)
most erosive at 15 min and orange juice (pH 3.73, highest titratable acidity) equally erosive at
longer exposures, while strawberry yoghurt (pH 4.21) caused no significant enamel roughness change.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Measured initial pH and titratable acidity (TA) of five commercially available soft drinks.
- Assessed enamel surface roughness (Ra, Rq, Rz, Ry) after 15, 30, and 60 min immersion
  cycles over 10 days (30 immersions total) via diamond stylus profilometry.
- Demonstrated that pH alone does not predict erosive rank: Guarana (pH 2.52) had higher TA
  than Coca-Cola (pH 2.67) yet showed similar surface roughness, while orange juice (pH 3.73,
  TA 5.70 mL NaOH) equalled Coca-Cola at 30 and 60 min.
- Confirmed strawberry yoghurt (pH 4.21) does not erode enamel, attributable to its high
  calcium/phosphate content saturating hydroxyapatite dissolution.
- Surface roughness increased with exposure time for all erosive drinks (dose–response).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Design: Controlled in-vitro experiment, 10-day acid challenge model.
- Specimens: 96 enamel samples cut from 24 unerupted human third molars (4 samples/tooth);
  embedded in self-cured resin, polished with non-fluoridated pumice.
- Groups (n = 18 per drink, 6 per time-point): (1) Coca-Cola pH 2.67, (2) orange juice pH 3.73,
  (3) Cedevita pH 3.49, (4) Guarana pH 2.52, (5) strawberry yoghurt pH 4.21, plus control
  (filtered saliva, n = 6).
- Challenge protocol: 50 mL drink × 3 immersions/day × 10 days = 30 total immersions;
  between immersions samples stored in filtered saliva.
- Profilometer: Mitutoyo Surftest SJ-301, diamond stylus 4 mN contact force, stylus speed
  0.25 mm/s, measurement length 0.5 mm, cutoff 2.5 mm, at 25°C.
- Roughness parameters: Ra (arithmetic mean), Rq (root mean square), Rz (mean peak-to-valley
  over 5 equal lengths), Ry (maximum peak-to-valley distance).
- Statistics: one-way ANOVA, Tukey post hoc and Student-Newman-Keuls post hoc; p < 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**pH and Titratable Acidity (TA, mL 1.0727 M NaOH to raise to pH 7.0):**
| Drink | pH | TA to pH 5.5 | TA to pH 7.0 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Coca-Cola | 2.67 ± 0.06 | 0.56 ± 0.02 | 1.87 ± 0.09 |
| Orange juice | 3.73 ± 0.03 | 4.24 ± 0.01 | 5.70 ± 0.06 |
| Cedevita | 3.49 ± 0.03 | 2.30 ± 0.07 | 3.40 ± 0.08 |
| Guarana | 2.52 ± 0.06 | 3.41 ± 0.06 | 5.11 ± 0.01 |
| Strawberry yoghurt | 4.21 ± 0.17 | 2.02 ± 0.03 | 3.52 ± 0.03 |

**Enamel roughness Ra (μm) — selected values (control baseline Ra = 0.67 ± 0.02 μm):**
- 15 min: Coca-Cola 1.49 ± 0.08 > orange juice 1.27 ± 0.01 > Cedevita 1.09 ± 0.03 ≈ Guarana
  1.07 ± 0.02; yoghurt 0.67 ± 0.17 (ns vs control).
- 30 min: Coca-Cola 1.63 ± 0.05 ≈ orange juice 1.63 ± 0.02 > Cedevita ≈ Guarana ~1.11–1.14.
- 60 min: Coca-Cola 1.82 ± 0.01 ≈ orange juice 1.78 ± 0.07; Cedevita ≈ Guarana ~1.20.
- Rz at 15 min: Coca-Cola 10.55 ± 1.57 μm vs yoghurt 1.67 ± 0.07 μm.
- All drinks except yoghurt: roughness increased significantly with time (p < 0.05).
- No significant difference between Cedevita and Guarana at any time-point despite differing pH.

**Key finding:** Coca-Cola (phosphoric acid) had greatest early erosive effect; orange juice
(citric acid + multiple organics, highest TA) matched Coca-Cola at longer exposures.
Strawberry yoghurt did not erode enamel at any time-point (calcium/phosphate saturation effect).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- In-vitro model: no pellicle, no oral fluid dynamics, no mechanical stress (abrasion/attrition).
- Fixed beverage volumes (50 mL) and room-temperature exposure may not replicate oral sipping.
- Only surface roughness measured (no profilometric step-height wear in μm); cannot convert
  directly to volumetric or depth tissue loss.
- Single operator for all profilometry measurements (reduces variability but limits blinding).
- Future: in-situ or clinical studies combining erosive and abrasive challenge; add fluoride
  or CPP-ACP protective agents.

## 7. Glossary
- Ra: Arithmetic mean roughness (average deviation from mean profile line).
- Rq: Root-mean-square roughness.
- Rz: Mean peak-to-valley height over five equal sampling lengths.
- Ry: Maximum peak-to-valley height (maximum defect indicator).
- TA: Titratable acidity — volume of NaOH needed to raise pH to 5.5 or 7.0; reflects total
  buffering/acid reservoir beyond instantaneous pH.
- Critical pH: ~5.5 for hydroxyapatite dissolution onset in enamel.
