## One-line Summary
Meta-analysis of 21 RCTs comparing extra-short (≤6 mm) vs longer (≥8 mm) implants. AMSTAR-2 + GRADE quality appraisal. Outcomes: survival, MBL, biological/prosthetic complications.

## 1. Document Information

## 2. Key Contributions
High-quality SR+MA establishing the time-horizon-dependent equivalence/non-inferiority profile of extra-short implants vs augmentation+long implants.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
SR of PubMed/WoS/EMBASE/Cochrane through Nov 2020. Only RCTs with ≥1-year follow-up. Cochrane RoB 2 + GRADE. Quantitative meta-analysis.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
No survival difference at 1y (RR 1.002) or 3y (RR 0.996). Longer implants higher survival at 5y (RR 0.970, p<0.05) — but parity in subgroup with bone augmentation. MBL favored extra-short (WMD -0.22). Biological complications favored extra-short (RR 0.321). No difference in MBL from prosthesis or prosthetic complications.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
Heterogeneity across implant systems and protocols. Low-quality evidence on some MBL endpoints.
