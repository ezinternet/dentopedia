## Why Ingested

치과 임상에서 분쟁 발생 시 환자와 합의서(부제소 합의, covenant not to sue)를 작성하는 일이 잦은데, 그 합의서가 실제로 법적 효력을 갖는 조건이 무엇인지에 대한 실증 근거. 한국 법원 판례 287건을 분석한 첫 연구로, 기존 medico-legal applied 층 [[complaint-management/JISPCD-14-295]]·[[complaint-management/10-1055-s-0039-1700767]]을 "분쟁 후 합의서 작성" 단계로 확장한다. 성형외과 데이터지만 한국 의료법·민사 판례 구조가 동일해 치과에 직접 적용 가능.

## One-line Summary
Retrospective analysis of 287 Korean civil court precedents (2000–2015) on aesthetic-surgery disputes, identifying four factors that make a doctor–patient settlement agreement legally binding and showing fee-free reoperations are not credited by courts.

## 한줄요약
한국 성형수술 민사 판례 287건(2000–2015) 후향 분석 — 의사·환자 합의서가 법적으로 유효해지는 4대 요건을 도출하고, 무상 재수술은 법원이 배상액 산정에서 인정하지 않음을 확인.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First analysis of the legal effectiveness of doctor–patient settlement agreements using a Korean court database (287 civil cases).
- Identifies four major factors that make a settlement agreement legally effective.
- Shows fee-free reoperations are NOT considered by courts in determining compensation amounts.
- Finds no statistically significant relationship between effectiveness and surgery type, complication type, geography, settlement timing, or economic magnitude.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Retrospective review of 287 civil precedents on aesthetic surgery (2000–2015) from a Korean court database.
- 68 cases involved a covenant not to sue (부제소 합의). Of these, 18 dismissed (agreement recognized as effective), 50 forwarded to judgment on merits (agreement not recognized as effective).
- Classified by surgery type, complication type, geographic distribution, settlement timing, effectiveness, and economic impact.
- Statistical testing for associations among these factors.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- 68/287 cases had a covenant not to sue; effectiveness rate 18/68 (26.5%).
- No statistically significant relationship among surgery type, complication type, geography, timing, and effectiveness.
- Four factors making a settlement legally effective identified (content adequacy of the agreement, not the mere act of signing).
- Courts did not credit fee-free reoperations against compensation.
- Core conclusion: review the *contents* of the agreement; signing a settlement per se carries little legal weight.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Plastic-surgery–specific; generalization to other specialties (incl. dentistry) is by analogy of Korean civil-law structure, not direct.
- Court-database cases only — settlements that never reached litigation are unobserved (selection toward contested settlements).
- Descriptive; no multivariable model of which agreement clauses drive effectiveness.

## 6. Related Work
- Korean medical-dispute resolution literature (settlement vs litigation/mediation).
- ISAPS procedure-volume data establishing Korea's high aesthetic-procedure base rate.

## 7. Glossary
- Covenant not to sue (부제소 합의): contractual promise by the patient not to file or continue suit.
- Medical malpractice / dispute / litigation: error deviating from accepted norms / patient's request for redress / formal lawsuit, respectively.
