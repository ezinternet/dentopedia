## One-line Summary
RCT (NCT06055127) comparing OD vs piezoelectric internal sinus elevation for delayed implant placement: OD gave more bone gain, higher density, shorter surgery and higher satisfaction; PISE gave better day-0 primary stability.

## 1. Document Information
- BMC Oral Health 2024;24:1306. doi 10.1186/s12903-024-04964-6. Registered clinicaltrials.gov NCT06055127 (2023-09-26).

## 2. Key Contributions
- Direct head-to-head of two minimally invasive transcrestal sinus approaches (OD vs PISE) with delayed implant placement.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Randomized controlled clinical trial, posterior maxilla, transalveolar (transcrestal) sinus elevation to augment vertical bone height before delayed implant placement.
- Adjuncts mentioned: sticky bone, i-PRF.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- OD: greater bone gain, improved bone density, shorter surgical duration, faster healing, higher patient satisfaction.
- PISE: superior implant primary stability on day of surgery.
- Both effective; OD favored for bone gain/density/satisfaction, PISE for immediate primary stability.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Specific n, follow-up duration, and quantitative effect sizes not captured from abstract — verify in full text before citing numbers. Single-trial evidence.
