## Why Ingested
Sealant vs fluoride varnish(FV)의 *상대* 효과에 대한 최신 Cochrane 근거(pub5, 2020)로, 무처치 기준선을 다룬 [[caries/ahovuo-saloranta-2017-pit-fissure-sealants-permanent-teeth]]와 한 쌍을 이룬다. 기존 [[caries/rashed-2022-sealant-vs-fluoride-varnish-caries-prevention-sr-ma]]·[[caries/chestnutt-2017-fissure-seal-fluoride-varnish-trial]]의 상위 근거이며, pub4(2016, Ahovuo-Saloranta)를 대체(supersede)한다.

## One-line Summary
Cochrane SR+MA (11 RCTs, 3374 children aged 5–10): unable to determine whether resin sealant or fluoride varnish is superior for preventing occlusal caries (OR 0.67, 95% CI 0.37–1.19; very low certainty); sealant + varnish may beat varnish alone (OR 0.30; very low certainty).

## 한줄요약
코크란 SR+MA (RCT 11편, 5–10세 3,374명): 레진 전색 vs 불소바니시 우열 판정 불가(OR 0.67, 95% CI 0.37–1.19; 매우 낮은 확실성). 전색+바니시 병용이 바니시 단독보다 나을 가능성(OR 0.30; 매우 낮은 확실성).

## 2. Key Contributions
- Updates the sealant-vs-varnish comparison with 3 new trials (11 total); adds cost-effectiveness objective.
- Confirms that head-to-head superiority of sealant vs FV cannot be established on current evidence (all very low certainty).
- Strengthens the signal that sealant + FV combined may outperform FV alone (single split-mouth trial, 77% reduction at 2 yr, very low certainty).
- Notes 14 ongoing trials that may resolve the question in future updates.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: Systematic review of RCTs; random-effects meta-analysis when ≥4 studies combined. GRADE certainty.
- **Population**: participants <20 years at start; ≥12 months follow-up. All included trials enrolled children 5–10 years.
- **Comparisons / grouping**: by sealant material (resin-based vs glass ionomer-based) and follow-up period.
  - Resin sealant vs FV: 7 trials (5 contributing data)
  - GIC-based sealant vs FV: 3 trials (1 chemically cured GIC, 2 RMGI) — clinically diverse, no meta-analysis
  - Sealant + FV vs FV alone: 1 split-mouth trial
- **Effect measures**: OR (Becker-Balagtas OR for split-mouth); RR for one cluster-RCT; MD for DMFS/DMFT increment; all with 95% CI.
- **Included**: 11 trials, 3374 participants (3 new since 2016); 2 trials contributed no data.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Resin sealant vs FV, 2–3 yr**: OR 0.67 (95% CI 0.37–1.19), I²=84%, 4 studies, 1683 children — *uncertain*, very low certainty.
  - DMFS increment at 2 yr: MD −0.09 (CI −0.15 to −0.03); DMFT MD −0.08 (−0.14 to −0.02); 542 participants — small, possibly not clinically significant.
  - Single small high-RoB trial: 4 yr RR 0.42 (0.21–0.84); 9 yr RR 0.48 (0.29–0.79); 75 children — very low certainty.
- **GIC sealant vs FV**: no consistent benefit either way at 1/2/3 yr; one trial (with oral-health education) favored sealant in high-risk children — very low certainty.
- **Sealant + FV vs FV alone**: OR 0.30 (95% CI 0.17–0.55), 1 split-mouth trial, 92 children → ~77% caries reduction at 2 yr — very low certainty.
- **Adverse events**: 5 trials (1801 participants) reported none over 1–9 years; others did not mention.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- All conclusions rest on very low-certainty evidence; high heterogeneity (I²=84%) in the main resin-vs-FV pool.
- Sparse data: 2 of 11 trials contributed nothing; GIC arm too diverse to pool.
- Combination-therapy signal derives from a single split-mouth trial.
- 14 ongoing trials identified; firmer conclusions await future updates.
- Cost-effectiveness objective added but yielded limited usable economic data.

## 6. Related Work
- Ahovuo-Saloranta 2017 (CD001830.pub5) — sister review establishing sealant vs *no* sealant efficacy.
- Rashed 2022 — independent SR+MA on the same sealant-vs-FV question.
- Chestnutt 2017 (CDPP "Seal or Varnish?" trial) — large UK community RCT in this comparison space.
- Kumar 2026 — cost-effectiveness SR+MA of fluoride varnish.

## 7. Glossary
- **FV**: fluoride varnish. **GIC / RMGI**: glass ionomer cement / resin-modified glass ionomer.
- **OR / RR / MD**: odds ratio / risk ratio / mean difference. **DMFS/DMFT**: decayed-missing-filled surfaces/teeth.
- **Becker-Balagtas OR**: OR estimator for split-mouth (paired) designs. **I²**: heterogeneity statistic.
