## One-line Summary
Int J Dent 2022:7615892 (Casablanca, Morocco) — preclinical study of 140 preparations by 35 third-year students on typodont vs simulator scanned with IDENTICA HYBRID; mean TOC ~12° (typodont 11.99° ± 4.48; simulator 11.31° ± 4.16); both within recommended 6-16° range; simulator slightly tighter than typodont.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: International Journal of Dentistry 2022:7615892
- Published: 10 May 2022
- Type: Preclinical evaluation (in vitro)
- Affiliation: Faculty of Dentistry, Hassan II University of Casablanca, Morocco

## 2. Key Contributions
- Compares dental student preparation accuracy on typodont vs simulator
- Confirms 6-16° clinical achievability range across preclinical platforms
- Quantifies MD vs BL convergence differential

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 35 third-year students × 4 preparations each = 140 preparations total
- Scanner: IDENTICA HYBRID optical scanner
- STL files measured with 3D-TOOL-FREE software
- MD and BL convergence angles measured separately via MB-RULER
- Statistics: Student's t-test in SPSS

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Platform | Overall (Mean ± SD) | MD direction | BL direction |
|---|---|---|---|
| Typodont | 11.99° ± 4.48 | 11.40° ± 5.09 | 12.58° ± 4.74 |
| Simulator | 11.31° ± 4.16 | 10.81° ± 4.29 | 11.80° ± 5.44 |

- Both platforms produce preparations within the 6-16° recommended range
- BL slightly higher than MD on both platforms (anatomic + access-related)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Preclinical only; does not capture in-mouth clinician variability
- Single institution; cohort size 35
- The 6° ideal taper is rarely achievable even in controlled preclinical setting

## 6. Related Work
- Weed et al., Dodge et al. — 6° to 16° clinically achievable range
- Marghalani (King Abdulaziz Univ) — comparable student values
- Tiu et al. (Otago) — historical TOC trend
