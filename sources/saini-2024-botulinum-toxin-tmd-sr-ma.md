## One-line Summary
2024 SR+MA from King Khalid University confirms botulinum toxin (보툴리눔독소) significantly reduces pain in TMD, with updated evidence from a larger trial pool than prior reviews; published in PLoS ONE.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: PLoS ONE 2024
- **DOI**: 10.1371/journal.pone.0300157
- **Affiliation**: King Khalid University
- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis

## 2. Key Contributions
- Updated comprehensive SR+MA incorporating trials published after Zhu 2020
- Evaluates BTX effectiveness across multiple TMD subtypes (myofascial, disc displacement, arthralgia)
- Quantifies pain reduction and functional improvement (MMO, jaw function)
- Includes quality-of-life and patient-reported outcome measures
- Assesses evidence certainty using GRADE framework

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Search: PubMed, Embase, Cochrane CENTRAL, Scopus, Web of Science
- Inclusion: RCTs and controlled clinical trials, BTX (types A and B) vs. placebo/active comparator for TMD
- Primary outcomes: pain intensity (VAS/NRS), MMO
- Secondary: muscle tenderness, jaw function, quality of life
- Random-effects model; I² for heterogeneity
- Subgroup analyses by TMD subtype and BTX dose

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- BTX significantly superior to placebo for pain reduction across TMD subtypes
- Effect most pronounced for myofascial pain (masseter and temporalis injections)
- MMO: modest improvement with BTX in some subgroups (disc displacement)
- Consistent with Zhu 2020 findings but with broader evidence base
- Adverse effects: transient, mild (injection site discomfort, temporary functional limitation)
- GRADE certainty: low to moderate

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Heterogeneity in BTX product, dose, dilution, and injection protocol
- Variable follow-up periods
- Few long-term studies (>12 months)
- Need for standardized injection protocols and outcome measures
- Comparison with splint and physiotherapy combination therapies needed

## 6. Related Work
- Zhu et al. (2020) earlier BTX SR+MA for painful TMD
- Serrera-Figallo et al. (2020) narrative review of BTX in orofacial practice
- BMJ 2023 guideline

## 7. Glossary
- **Botulinum toxin** (보툴리눔독소, BTX): Neurotoxin used therapeutically to reduce masticatory muscle hyperactivity
- **Myofascial pain**: Muscle-origin TMD with characteristic trigger points and referred pain patterns
- **Arthralgia**: Joint pain component of TMD; may respond less to BTX than myofascial component
- **NRS**: Numerical Rating Scale (0–10) for pain intensity
- **GRADE**: Evidence grading system for systematic reviews and clinical guidelines
