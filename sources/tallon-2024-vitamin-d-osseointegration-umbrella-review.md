## Why Ingested

Highest-level synthesis (umbrella review of 5 systematic reviews) anchoring the new vitamin D–osseointegration subdomain `implants/vitamin-d`. It sits above and integrates the individual SRs we hold, giving the bottom line ("vitamin D positive effect on osseointegration and reduced implant failure, but evidence quality limited") plus a structured map of the methodological flaws that future studies must avoid. Pairs with the underlying SRs [[implants/vitamin-d/werny-2022-vitamin-d-osseointegration-dental-implants-sr]] and [[implants/vitamin-d/buzatu-2024-vitamin-d-osseointegration-human-studies-sr]], and reinforces the mechanistic/clinical signal in [[implants/vitamin-d/miron-2025-vitamin-d-deficiency-early-implant-failure]].

## One-line Summary

Umbrella review (PRISMA, JBI appraisal) of 5 systematic reviews (351 records → 5 SRs → 8 relevant primary studies); vitamin D appears to positively affect osseointegration, marginal bone loss, implant survival, and reduction of early dental implant failure, but the underlying evidence is methodologically weak (no meta-analysis, confirmation/selective-reporting bias, inconsistent reference levels, unreported doses).

## 한줄요약

우산형 종설(Umbrella Review, PRISMA·JBI 평가)로 체계적 문헌고찰(Systematic Review, SR) 5편(검색 351건 → SR 5편 → 관련 1차 연구 8편)을 통합. 비타민 D(Vitamin D)는 골유착(Osseointegration)·변연골소실(Marginal Bone Loss, MBL)·임플란트 생존·초기임플란트실패(Early Dental Implant Failure, EDIF) 감소에 긍정적으로 보이나, 근거 질이 낮음(메타분석 부재, 확증/선택적보고 편향, 기준치·보충용량 불일치).

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Umbrella review (review of systematic reviews); qualitative analysis, no meta-analysis performed.
- **Reporting standard**: PRISMA; protocol registered in the Open Science Framework.
- **Methodological quality tool**: Joanna Briggs Institute (JBI) critical appraisal checklist for SR (11 items).
- **Search period**: January–April 2023.
- **Databases**: PubMed, Web of Science, CINAHL Plus, Cochrane Library, Academic Search Complete.
- **Search string**: ["vitamin D" AND ("dental implant" OR "dental implants")]; no year limit; languages Portuguese, English, Spanish.
- **Journal**: Healthcare (Basel), 2024 (published 2024-09-17). PMID 39337208 / PMCID PMC11431482 / DOI 10.3390/healthcare12181867.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First **umbrella review** dedicated to vitamin D and osseointegration of dental implants — sits one tier above the existing SRs and integrates them.
- Quantifies the evidence funnel: 351 records → 300 de-duplicated → 5 SRs included; those 5 SRs cumulatively cite 51 primary studies (22 duplicated → 29 unique), of which only **8 primary studies (2014–2021)** actually met the umbrella's eligibility criteria.
- Exposes the methodological fragility of the field: none of the 5 SRs performed a meta-analysis; only one SR used dual independent reviewers and appraised primary-study quality; SR quality scores ranged 4–9 (JBI), i.e., low to high.
- Documents **confirmation/selective-outcome-reporting bias** — Werny (2022) and Hakim (2021) reported only positive findings of Mangano (2018) / Salomó-Coll (2016) while omitting non-significant results.
- Provides clinical guidance: consider measuring serum vitamin D before implant placement; supplement patients with severely compromised levels.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Two researchers performed search, selection, quality assessment, and data extraction independently; disagreements resolved by consensus or a third reviewer (inter-rater reliability not measured).
- Eligibility excluded SR/MA limited to diseased subjects, abstracts/commentaries, irrelevant outcomes, and duplicated/incomplete data.
- Because the included SRs were judged deficient, the authors went a step beyond standard umbrella methodology and re-analyzed the 29 underlying primary studies individually, isolating the 8 that genuinely addressed vitamin D and dental-implant (oral-bone) osseointegration.
- JBI 11-item appraisal: items least often satisfied were #6 (dual independent appraisal) and #7 (data-extraction error minimization); item #9 (publication bias) inapplicable as no SR performed MA.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Bottom line**: vitamin D *may* positively influence osseointegration, reduce marginal bone loss, increase implant survival, and reduce early dental implant failure (EDIF) — but results are inconsistent and often not statistically significant.
- **Mangano (2018)** (largest, n=885, 168-mo follow-up): tendency toward higher EDIF with vitamin D deficiency, NOT statistically significant (p = 0.105).
- **Tabrizi (2022)**: mean MBL higher with deficiency (1.38 ± 0.33 mm at 8.13 ng/mL) vs. sufficiency (0.78 ± 0.12 mm at 35.37 ng/mL); low serum vitamin D accounted for 50.6% of mean MBL (R = 0.506, p = 0.001); each 1-unit rise in serum vitamin D → MBL down 0.02 mm (p < 0.001). The 0.60 mm 1-year difference may not be clinically significant.
- **Hakim (2021)**: 6 of 12 studies found a significant deficiency–DIF association.
- **Supplementation RCTs**: Garg (2020) cholecalciferol 60,000 IU/month ×3 mo (≈2000 IU/day; cumulative ≈540,000 IU); Kwiatek (2021) 8000 IU/day ×12 wk (cumulative ≈672,000 IU) — both concluded supplementation improves osseointegration.
- **Guided surgery aside**: DIF threefold lower with digitally guided vs. freehand placement (2.25% vs. 6.42%, Bazal-Bonelli 2022).
- **Reference-level inconsistency**: e.g., severe deficiency <10 ng/mL; sufficient/optimal generally >30 ng/mL; infection rate correlated with vitamin D <20 ng/mL.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- No meta-analysis at any tier; conclusions remain qualitative.
- Underlying SRs were heterogeneous and methodologically weak (selection bias, missing quality appraisal, unreported supplementation doses, inconsistent vitamin D thresholds, selective reporting).
- Grey literature not searched (potential publication bias).
- Animal data could not be extrapolated to clinical practice and did not influence clinical conclusions.
- Calls for large-scale prospective human studies and especially RCTs, with larger samples, standardized dose (per national/continental guidelines), longer/varied follow-up, and control of confounders (age, diet, smoking, etc.). Recommends complementing p-values with confidence/credibility/prediction intervals and Bayesian/decision-theoretic methods.

## 7. Glossary

- **Umbrella review**: a review of systematic reviews on the same topic; the highest tier of evidence synthesis.
- **Osseointegration**: direct structural/functional connection between living bone and the surface of a load-bearing implant.
- **EDIF (Early Dental Implant Failure)**: implant failure before/around osseointegration, linked to impaired bone healing.
- **MBL (Marginal Bone Loss)**: crestal bone loss around the implant neck over time.
- **BIC (Bone-to-Implant Contact)**: histomorphometric percentage of implant surface in direct contact with bone.
- **25(OH)D (25-hydroxyvitamin D)**: the standard serum marker of vitamin D status.
- **JBI**: Joanna Briggs Institute, source of the SR critical-appraisal checklist used here.
