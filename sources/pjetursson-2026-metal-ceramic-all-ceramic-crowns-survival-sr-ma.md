## One-line Summary
SR+MA of 64 studies (n=11,560 crowns) showing that monolithic lithium-disilicate single crowns achieve the highest 5-year survival rate (98.5%), comparable to or exceeding metal-ceramic (97.1%), while monolithic designs significantly reduce ceramic fracture and chipping compared to veneered alternatives.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: The International Journal of Prosthodontics (Pre-print / published January 5, 2026)
- **DOI**: 10.11607/ijp.9633
- **PubMed ID (PMID)**: 41489982
- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Search period**: MEDLINE (PubMed), Embase, CENTRAL; 2014–2024; plus 30 studies from previous reviews
- **Sample**: 3,509 metal-ceramic SCs + 8,051 all-ceramic SCs = ~11,560 total

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive head-to-head comparison of all major crown material/design types: metal-ceramic, veneered all-ceramic (multiple subtypes), and monolithic all-ceramic (lithium-disilicate, zirconia)
- Demonstrates that monolithic lithium-disilicate achieves highest 5-year survival among all tested materials
- Quantifies that monolithic designs significantly reduce ceramic fractures and chipping vs veneered designs
- Provides statistical comparisons: feldspathic/silica-based, glass-infiltrated alumina, densely sintered alumina, and veneered glass-ceramic all significantly inferior to monolithic lithium-disilicate

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Eligibility**: Clinical studies on tooth-supported fixed dental prostheses; ≥3 years mean follow-up
- **Included**: 64 studies (12 metal-ceramic, 49 all-ceramic, 3 both)
- **Statistical model**: Robust Poisson regression models for pooled 5-year survival rate estimation
- **Outcomes**: 5-year survival rate, failure rate, biological complications, technical complications (ceramic fracture/chipping)
- **Region analysis**: Anterior vs posterior performance comparison

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
**5-year survival rates (ranked):**
1. Monolithic lithium-disilicate reinforced glass-ceramic: **98.5%**
2. Veneered densely-sintered zirconia: **97.3%**
3. Metal-ceramic: **97.1%**
4. Monolithic densely-sintered zirconia: **96.8%**
5. Veneered leucite/lithium-disilicate reinforced glass-ceramic: **95.7%**
6. Densely-sintered alumina: **94.5%**
7. Glass-infiltrated alumina: **94.3%**
8. Feldspathic/silica-based ceramic: **90.4%**

**Statistical significance vs monolithic lithium-disilicate:**
- Feldspathic/silica-based: p<0.0001
- Glass-infiltrated alumina: p=0.019
- Densely sintered alumina: p=0.002
- Veneered leucite/lithium-disilicate: p=0.021

**Ceramic fracture/chipping:**
- Monolithic lithium-disilicate and monolithic zirconia → significantly fewer fractures/chipping than veneered alternatives

**Regional performance:**
- Feldspathic/silica-based and metal-ceramic crowns performed differently in anterior vs posterior
- All other types showed similar performance in both regions

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Pre-print status (January 2026); awaiting formal publication volume/page assignment
- 10-year survival data limited for newer monolithic materials
- Biological complications require longer-term follow-up for full picture

## 7. Glossary
- **Metal-ceramic (PFM)**: Porcelain-fused-to-metal crown; traditional gold standard
- **Monolithic lithium-disilicate**: e.max-type glass-ceramic milled/pressed without veneering layer
- **Veneered zirconia**: High-strength zirconia coping with esthetic ceramic veneering layer
- **Monolithic zirconia**: Full-contour zirconia crown without veneering
- **Densely-sintered zirconia**: 3Y-TZP or similar high-strength zirconia
- **Robust Poisson regression**: Statistical model for estimating survival rates with correction for overdispersion
