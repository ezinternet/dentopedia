## One-line Summary

ADA evidence-based clinical practice guideline (expert panel, GRADE): for immunocompetent adults, antibiotics should NOT be prescribed for symptomatic irreversible pulpitis ± symptomatic apical periodontitis (strong, low certainty) nor for pulp necrosis with apical periodontitis/localized acute apical abscess (conditional, very low certainty) — definitive conservative dental treatment (pulpotomy/pulpectomy/RCT/I&D) is first-line; antibiotics only if no immediate access (delayed amoxicillin 500 mg TID) or systemic involvement.

## 한줄요약

미국치과의사협회 (American Dental Association, ADA) 근거기반 임상진료지침(전문가 패널, GRADE): 면역정상 성인에서 증상성 비가역적 치수염 (Symptomatic Irreversible Pulpitis, SIP) ± 증상성 치근단치주염 (Symptomatic Apical Periodontitis, SAP)에 항생제 처방 금지(강한 권고, 낮은 확실성), 치수괴사+치근단농양에도 비권장(조건부, 매우 낮은 확실성) — 1차 치료는 결정적 보존 치과치료 (Definitive Conservative Dental Treatment, DCDT: 치수절단·치수절제·근관치료·절개배농); 항생제는 즉시 치료 불가 시(amoxicillin 500 mg 1일 3회 지연처방)나 전신 침범 시에만.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of the American Dental Association (JADA). 2019;150(11):906–921.e12 (PMC manuscript available 2021-07-09)
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.adaj.2019.08.020
- **Body**: ADA Council on Scientific Affairs expert panel + ADA Center for Evidence-Based Dentistry (methodology)
- **Type**: Evidence-based clinical practice guideline (GRADE Evidence-to-Decision), with accompanying systematic review

## 2. Key Contributions

- First ADA guideline on systemic antibiotic use for urgent pulpal/periapical pain and swelling in US dentistry.
- Frames the antimicrobial-stewardship problem: dentists are the 3rd-highest outpatient antibiotic prescribers in the US; 30–85% of dental antibiotic prescriptions are suboptimal or not indicated.
- Issues strong/conditional recommendations against antibiotics, anchored on the physiopathology that inflamed pulp (pulpitis) is **not an infection** and does not require antibiotics.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Developed per AGREE-II and GIN-McMaster checklists; GRADE Evidence-to-Decision framework; consensus (vote only if consensus hard).
- Accompanying SR updated two pre-existing Cochrane reviews.
- Scope: immunocompetent adults (≥18 y), no comorbidities, with SIP±SAP, PN-SAP, or PN-LAAA, with or without immediate access to DCDT.
- Out of scope: cellulitis, immunocompromised patients, tooth extraction (covered elsewhere, e.g. Lodi 2021).
- Harm estimates drawn from observational data (antibiotic-resistant infection mortality, C. difficile, anaphylaxis) given indirectness.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Recommendation 1** — SIP ± SAP: dentists **do NOT prescribe** oral systemic antibiotics (Strong recommendation, low certainty). Refer for DCDT with interim monitoring. Rationale: inflamed pulp is not infected; antibiotics yield little/no benefit (low certainty) with potentially large harm (moderate certainty).

**Recommendation 2a** — Pulp necrosis + symptomatic apical periodontitis (PN-SAP): **suggest do NOT prescribe** (Conditional, very low certainty). Refer for DCDT; if DCDT not feasible, **delayed prescription** oral amoxicillin 500 mg three times daily.

**Recommendation 2b** — Pulp necrosis + localized acute apical abscess (PN-LAAA): higher systemic-progression risk; similar conditional guidance with delayed-prescription option when DCDT unavailable (penicillin allergy → alternative).

- **Good Practice Statement**: when immediate DCDT IS available, antibiotics are not indicated for these conditions.
- Escalate to antibiotics if **systemic involvement** (fever, lymphadenopathy, cellulitis, spreading infection).
- Delayed vs immediate prescription: no significant difference in patient satisfaction (91% vs 86%, moderate certainty).
- Harm context: ~23,000 annual US deaths from antibiotic-resistant infections; C. difficile burden; penicillin/cephalosporin anaphylaxis.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Beneficial-outcome evidence low to very low certainty; no RCTs for PN-SAP/PN-LAAA (indirect evidence borrowed from SIP).
- Scope excludes children, immunocompromised, cellulitis, extractions.
- Access-to-care and patient expectation may limit feasibility/acceptability (ED/rural settings).
- Planned update within 5 years or upon practice-changing evidence.

## 7. Glossary

- **SIP (Symptomatic Irreversible Pulpitis)**: painful, inflamed but non-infected pulp; antibiotics not indicated.
- **SAP (Symptomatic Apical Periodontitis)**: symptomatic inflammation of periapical tissues.
- **PN-SAP / PN-LAAA**: pulp necrosis with symptomatic apical periodontitis / with localized acute apical abscess (infectious processes).
- **DCDT (Definitive Conservative Dental Treatment)**: tooth-preserving treatment — pulpotomy, pulpectomy, nonsurgical root canal treatment, or incision & drainage; first-line.
- **Delayed prescription**: an antibiotic Rx given to use only if the condition fails to improve / DCDT cannot be done promptly.
- **GPS (Good Practice Statement)**: guidance based on indirect evidence with large net benefit, without a formal certainty grade.
