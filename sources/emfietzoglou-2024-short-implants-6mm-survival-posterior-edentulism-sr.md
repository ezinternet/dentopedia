## One-line Summary

Systematic review of RCTs comparing short (≤6 mm) vs longer (>6 mm) rough-surface implants in posterior partial edentulism, assessing survival, marginal bone loss, peri-implantitis, and technical complications (≥1-year post-loading).

## 한줄요약

RCT 체계적 문헌고찰 — 후방 부분무치악에서 short(≤6 mm) vs longer(>6 mm) 거친표면 임플란트의 생존율·변연골소실·주위염·기술적 합병증 비교(≥1년 하중 후).

## 2. Key Contributions
- Strict ≤6 mm definition of "short" — isolates the most aggressive length reduction.
- RCT-only inclusion with rough-surface restriction for comparability.
- Comprehensive outcome set: survival, MBL, peri-implantitis prevalence, technical complications.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Systematic review of RCTs; electronic search to September 2023.
- Inclusion: ≥1-year post-loading RCTs comparing ≤6 mm short vs >6 mm longer rough-surface implants in posterior jaws of systemically/periodontally healthy partially edentulous adults.
- Excluded studies with incomplete patient/follow-up data or vague "short" definition.
- Revised Cochrane risk-of-bias tool applied.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Short (≤6 mm) implants positioned as an alternative to bone-regeneration procedures for resorbed ridges.
- Outcomes compared: survival rates, peri-implant MBL, peri-implantitis prevalence, technical complications.
- (Detailed pooled figures in full text; this review is qualitative-leaning per strict inclusion.)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Strict inclusion narrows the evidence pool.
- Heterogeneous follow-up durations (≥1 year minimum).
- Technical-complication reporting inconsistent across RCTs.
