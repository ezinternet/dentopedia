## Why Ingested

Head-to-head examiner-blind RCT of a powered air-flosser charged with essential-oil mouthwash (AirFloss Ultra + Listerine, AFeo) versus conventional waxed dental floss as a toothbrushing adjunct — directly informs the floss-vs-device interdental-cleaning question. Pairs with [[periodontics/tsilingaridis-2026-biofilm-induced-gingivitis-children-adolescents]], whose SR concludes self-performed floss adds little for gingivitis: this RCT finds the powered air-flosser is *equivalent* to floss (neither superior), reinforcing that the device choice is driven by compliance/dexterity, not efficacy.

## One-line Summary

Examiner-blind parallel RCT (n=82 systemically healthy adults, ≥25% lower-jaw BOMP, no periodontitis; 3-wk experimental gingivitis then 4-wk reversal): AirFloss Ultra + essential oils (AFeo) vs waxed dental floss (DF), both adjunct to twice-daily fluoride toothbrushing — both reduced BOMP/MPI/GAS but NO between-group difference at any time point (p>0.05); no serious adverse events.

## 한줄요약

검사자 맹검 평행 RCT (n=82, 전신 건강 성인, 하악 BOMP ≥25%, 치주염 없음; 3주 실험적 치은염 유도 후 4주 회복): 정유 함유 에어플로스(AFeo) vs 왁스 치실(DF), 둘 다 1일 2회 불소 칫솔질 보조 — 두 군 모두 BOMP·치면세균막·치은마모 감소했으나 어느 시점에서도 군간 유의차 없음(p>0.05), 중대 이상반응 없음. 즉 동등·둘 다 안전.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First evaluation of the AirFloss Ultra charged with an essential-oil mouthwash (AFeo) head-to-head against waxed dental floss (DF) within a **reversal-of-experimental-gingivitis model** (21-day refrain → 4-week healing).
- Demonstrates **clinical equivalence**: AFeo and DF produce statistically indistinguishable reductions in gingival bleeding (BOMP), plaque (modified Silness-Löe MPI), and gingival abrasion (GAS).
- Reframes the device decision around **compliance, manual dexterity, and cost (value-based oral health care)** rather than efficacy, since neither device outperformed the other.
- Confirms **soft/hard tissue safety** for both modalities (no serious adverse events; the only minor events were six floss-related gingival irritations).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: parallel, single-blind (examiner), randomly assigned; reversal-of-experimental-gingivitis model (Van der Weijden). Conducted 2015 at ACTA, the Netherlands. CONSORT + TIDieR reporting.
- **Participants**: 104 screened → 82 completed (47 F / 35 M, mean age 22.9 ± 3.3 y). Inclusion: systemically healthy, ≥25% BOMP lower jaw, DPSI 0–3⁻ ("gingivitis" per 2017 EFP/AAP), floss fits ≥3 interdental spaces/quadrant (≥2 molar). Exclusion: overt caries, ortho appliances, removable dentures, piercings, oral lesions, pregnancy, recent trial.
- **Groups** (n=41 each):
  - **AFeo** — Philips Sonicare AirFloss Ultra + Listerine Cool Mint mouth rinse
  - **DF** — waxed dental floss (J&J Ultraclean)
  - Both + twice-daily manual toothbrushing (Lactona iQ Soft) with fluoride toothpaste (HEMA).
- **Phases**: familiarisation → experimental gingivitis (Day 0; professional prophylaxis lower jaw, then refrain from brushing lower jaw 21 days, brush upper jaw only) → treatment (Visits 3–6, Weeks 1, 2, 4). Lower-jaw partial-mouth model (Putt).
- **Outcomes**: Primary **BOMP** (6 sites/tooth, 0–2, WHO ball-ended probe). Secondary **MPI** (Van der Weijden modification of Silness-Löe, 6 surfaces/tooth, 0–3) and **GAS** (interdental/cervical/midgingival abrasions), plus product-appreciation questionnaire.
- **Sample size**: powered (80%, α=0.05) to detect 0.16 BOMP difference (SD 0.25) → 39/arm.
- **Allocation concealment**: atmospheric-noise randomization, SNOSE opaque envelopes; examiners blinded; instruction/use in a separate area.
- **Stats**: parametric + non-parametric, 95% CIs, ANOVA between groups, SAS; significance p<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **BOMP (primary)** — within-group reduction significant for both (p<0.01). Week-4 mean reduction: AFeo 0.60 (SD 0.32), DF 0.70 (SD 0.32). **Between-group: NS at every time point** (Day0 p=0.27, Wk1 0.37, Wk2 0.87, Wk4 0.72; Δ p=0.17).
- **MPI (plaque)** — significant within-group reduction both (p<0.01); Δ AFeo 1.04, DF 0.90. **Between-group NS** (Wk4 p=0.26, Δ p=0.19).
- **GAS (abrasion)** — significant within-group change both (p<0.01); **between-group NS** (Wk4 p=0.800, Δ p=0.49).
- **Product appreciation** — AFeo 6.06 (SD 2.09) vs DF 5.62 (SD 2.21); t(78)=0.91, p=0.365 (NS).
- **Safety** — six study-related adverse events, all **dental-floss** related (gum irritation, soreness, gingival lesions, pain, redness/swelling lower buccal gingiva); **no serious adverse events** in either arm.
- **Bottom line**: AFeo ≡ DF on bleeding, plaque, abrasion, and patient appreciation; both safe.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Participants could not be blinded to the device → potential **Hawthorne effect** favouring the novel AirFloss arm.
- No assessment of manual dexterity (assumed balanced by randomization).
- Floss-threading-needle use when required is atypical of normal flossing → generalisability caveat.
- **Asymmetric intervention**: AFeo combined mechanical + antiseptic (essential-oil) action, whereas DF was mechanical only — so the comparison is not a pure mechanical-vs-mechanical contrast; an AF-with-vs-without-essential-oils trial is warranted.
- Short-term experimental-gingivitis model (early-stage inflammation) — does not capture long-term/chronic effects.
- Studied AirFloss Ultra model may be superseded by newer devices (mode of action unchanged).
- **Cost/value**: AFeo (with essential oils) markedly more expensive than DF; authors note limited additional value-based benefit given no efficacy gain.

## 6. Related Work

- De Jager et al. — AF vs manual toothbrushing alone: AF reduced gingivitis over 4 weeks; 96% of irregular flossers used AF ≥4 days/week (compliance benefit).
- Mwatha et al. — AFeo and AF+BreathRx comparable to DF and safe (corroborates equivalence).
- Berchier et al. (SR/MA) & Sambunjak et al. (Cochrane SR) — weak/no evidence that floss adds benefit beyond toothbrushing; self-flossing ineffective for interproximal caries.
- Kotsakis et al. (NMA) — power-driven water flossers ranked second-best adjunct after interdental brushes.
- Bertl et al. — AirFloss nozzle/device bacterial colonisation after 3 weeks daily use → recommend one device per person.

## 7. Glossary

- **AFeo** — AirFloss Ultra with essential oils (Philips Sonicare AirFloss Ultra reservoir filled with Listerine Cool Mint essential-oil mouthwash).
- **DF** — waxed dental floss.
- **BOMP** — Bleeding On Marginal Probing index (0 = no bleeding, 1 = pin prick, 2 = excess), 6 sites/tooth.
- **MPI** — Modified Silness & Löe Plaque Index (Van der Weijden modification, 6 surfaces/tooth, 0–3 by plaque thickness).
- **GAS** — Gingival Abrasion Score (interdental/cervical/midgingival lesions by largest width).
- **Experimental gingivitis (reversal) model** — Löe-derived design: induce gingivitis by 21-day refrain from mechanical plaque control, then assess healing once oral hygiene resumes.
- **DPSI** — Dutch Periodontal Screening Index.
- **SNOSE** — sequentially numbered opaque sealed envelope (allocation concealment).
- **VBOHC** — value-based oral health care ("patient health outcomes achieved per dollar spent").
