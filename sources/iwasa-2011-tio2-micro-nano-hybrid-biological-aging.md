## One-line Summary
In-vitro study: TiO2 nanonodules (300 nm) on micropit Ti surface sustain bioactivity for ≥7 days after UV-PF (vs 30-50% decay on micropit-only); mechanism is sustained electropositivity, not hydrophilicity.

## 1. Document Information
Int J Nanomedicine 2011;6:1327-1341. doi:10.2147/IJN.S22099. UCLA LBIS.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First study showing nano-topography can slow post-UV biological aging
- Mechanism: TiO2 nanonodules confer sustained electropositivity → maintained cell bioactivity
- Separates electropositivity from hydrophilicity as independent aging regulators
- Model for "anti-biological aging" surface design

## 3. Methodology
Acid-etched Ti disks ± TiO2 nanonodules (300 nm, self-assembly). Aged 8 weeks, UV-treated 48 hours. Osteoblasts cultured at 0, 3, 7 days post-UV.

## 4. Key Results
- Micropit alone: bioactivity (attachment, spread, proliferation, ALP, Ca) decreased 30-50% over 7 days post-UV
- Micro-nano-hybrid: bioactivity maintained at 7 days equivalent to fresh surfaces
- Both surfaces superhydrophilic immediately post-UV; hybrid surface became hydrorepellent at day 7, yet maintained bioactivity
- Cl⁻ anion treatment (abolishes electropositivity) nullified the anti-aging effect of hybrid surface

## 5. Limitations
In vitro only; rat osteoblast model; 300 nm nodule size; manufacturing scalability to clinical implants not addressed.

## 6. Related Work
Minamikawa 2016 (long-term aging timeline), Ogawa 2025 (D3 aging theory), Park 2025 (VUV comprehensive review).

## 7. Glossary
Anti-biological aging: ability of surface to resist time-dependent bioactivity decay; electropositivity: positive surface charge on TiO2 nanonodules.
