## One-line Summary
Prospective clinical study (n=10) of BTX-A for gummy smile evaluated both clinically and with surface EMG (compound muscle action potential, C-MAP) — first reported use of EMG to quantify BTX-A effect on upper lip elevators; mean gingival display reduction 2.90 mm at 2 weeks (p<0.001), C-MAP dropped from 254.7 → 114.8 µV at 2 weeks, both gradually returned to baseline by 6 months.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First study to use electromyography (C-MAP — compound muscle action potential) to quantify BTX-A blockade of upper lip elevators
- Provides objective neurophysiological correlate to subjective/photographic gingival display measurement
- Subtype-tailored injection sites (anterior: LLSAN/LLS; posterior: ZM/ZMn origin + insertion; mixed: all four; asymmetric: ZM/ZMn on affected side bilateral)
- Dose 1.25 U per site (lower than many published protocols, 8 mL dilution per 100 U vial)
- Demonstrates statistical correlation between C-MAP decrement and clinical gingival display reduction

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Population: 10 patients (7F, 3M), 18-35 y, gingival display >3 mm, no neuromuscular drug interactions
- Subtype distribution: anterior n=3, posterior n=2, mixed n=3, asymmetric n=2
- Drug: BTX-A (SIAX, Aakar Pharmaceuticals, India), reconstituted with 8 mL 0.9% saline per 100 U vial → 1.25 U / 0.1 mL
- Anterior subtype: LLSAN (3-5 mm lateral to ala) + LLS (junction of upper 1/3 and lower 2/3 of nasolabial fold)
- Posterior subtype: ZM-ZMn at prominence of cheek (origin) + most lateral nasolabial fold while smiling (insertion)
- Mixed: all four sites; asymmetric: bilateral on affected side, only insertion on unaffected side
- Dose: 1.25 U per site
- C-MAP measurement: surface electrodes (ground = forehead; active = target muscle; inactive = chin); facial nerve stimulated at tragus; mean amplitude from peak-to-peak split into 5 subunits
- Follow-up: pre-op, 2 weeks, 3 months, 6 months
- Stats: repeated measures ANOVA + post hoc Bonferroni

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Gingival display (mm)
| Time | Mean ± SD | Diff vs baseline | p (Bonferroni) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-op | 6.20 ± 1.75 | — | — |
| 2 weeks | 3.30 ± 0.95 | 2.90 | <0.001 |
| 3 months | 4.40 ± 1.17 | 1.80 | 0.004 |
| 6 months | 5.60 ± 1.51 | 0.60 | 0.143 (NS) |

### C-MAP (µV)
| Time | Mean ± SD | Diff vs baseline | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-op | 254.70 ± 236.66 | — | — |
| 2 weeks | 114.80 ± 94.12 | -139.9 | 0.080 (NS) |
| 3 months | 145.10 ± 115.73 | -109.6 | 0.121 (NS) |
| 6 months | 229.45 ± 204.65 | -25.25 | 0.343 (NS) |
- 2 weeks vs 3 months: p=0.015 (significant)

### Satisfaction (6-month questionnaire)
- 70% fully satisfied, 30% satisfied
- 70% would undergo treatment again
- 50% no discomfort, 50% slight pain on injection
- One patient with 10 mm display received additional 1.25 U at 2 weeks
- No adverse reactions

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Very small sample (n=10), single arm, no control
- Wide standard deviation for C-MAP (236 µV) reflects subject heterogeneity → C-MAP differences vs baseline not statistically significant despite clear directional drop
- EMG-guided localization not used (palpation only)
- Indian cohort (SIAX brand, locally manufactured BTX-A), generalizability to other formulations uncertain
- Only one patient required re-injection
- 6-month gingival display did not return fully to baseline (5.60 vs 6.20), suggesting either residual effect or measurement variability

## 7. Glossary
- C-MAP: compound muscle action potential — simultaneous action potentials of a muscle fiber group measured via surface EMG
- LLSAN/LLS/ZM/ZMn: levator labii superioris alaeque nasi / levator labii superioris / zygomaticus major / zygomaticus minor
- Yonsei point: 1 cm lateral to ala, 3 cm from oral commissure — confluence of LLSAN, LLS, ZMn
- Repeated measures ANOVA: appropriate test for paired data across multiple time points
