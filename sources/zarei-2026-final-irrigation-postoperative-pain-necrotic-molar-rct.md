## Why Ingested

Prior irrigation-activation evidence on our shelf ([[endodontics/irrigation/paixao-2022-sonic-ultrasonic-activation-sr-ma]], [[endodontics/irrigation/orozco-gallego-2025-irrigation-protocols-endodontic-umbrella]]) mostly shows activation (PUI/sonic) reduces postoperative pain vs conventional needle irrigation but pools mixed pulpal diagnoses and rarely benchmarks activation against the *modern safe needle* (side-vented). This RCT isolates asymptomatic necrotic mandibular first molars (a high-risk flare-up subgroup) and directly compares PUI vs side-vented needle irrigation (SNI) vs conventional needle irrigation (CNI) — testing whether ultrasonic activation adds clinical value over an optimized needle, which refines the activation-superiority message in those reviews.

## One-line Summary

Single-blind RCT (n=45, 15/group) — in single-visit RCT of asymptomatic necrotic mandibular first molars, both PUI and side-vented needle irrigation lowered postoperative pain, swelling, and analgesic use vs conventional needle irrigation at 24–72 h, with no PUI-vs-SNI difference.

## 한줄요약

단일맹검 RCT (n=45, 군당 15명) — 무증상 괴사 하악 제1대구치 단일내원 근관치료에서 수동초음파세정(PUI)과 측방배출침세정(SNI) 모두 24~72시간 수술후 통증·부종·진통제 사용을 일반침세정(CNI)보다 낮췄고, PUI와 SNI 간 차이는 없었다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Direct head-to-head clinical comparison of three final-irrigation delivery methods — conventional needle irrigation (CNI), side-vented needle irrigation (SNI), and passive ultrasonic irrigation (PUI) — in a homogeneous, high-risk subgroup (asymptomatic necrotic mandibular first molars).
- Benchmarks activation (PUI) not only against outdated open-ended CNI but against the *contemporary safe standard* (side-vented needle), addressing a gap where most trials pool mixed pulpal diagnoses.
- Evaluates postoperative swelling as a co-primary patient-centered outcome (not just pain), plus analgesic consumption.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 45 patients, block randomization (blocks of 3), sealed opaque envelopes, allocation concealment; patient and outcome assessor blinded (operator unblinded by nature of intervention). Single 3-year endodontic resident operator at one center.
- Sample size a priori: 24 h VAS, Cohen d = 0.8, alpha 0.05, power 80% → 15/group.
- Standardized single-visit treatment: ProTaper Gold rotary (Sx, S1, S2, F1, F2; F3 single distal), 5.25% NaOCl during prep. Final irrigation per canal = 5 mL 5.25% NaOCl + 5 mL 17% EDTA; tip 1–2 mm short of working length; flow 1–2 mL/min; room temperature.
  - **CNI**: 30-G open-ended needle, NaOCl & EDTA each passive 3 min.
  - **SNI**: 30-G side-vented needle (Zenplus), NaOCl & EDTA each 3 min.
  - **PUI**: 5 mL NaOCl activated 30–60 s/canal with cordless ultrasonic (Ultra X, Eighteeth) silver tip #20/.00, then EDTA passive 3 min.
- Obturation: cold lateral condensation, gutta-percha + AH-26 (warm techniques avoided to limit overfill).
- Outcomes: VAS 0–10 pain and binary swelling at 6/24/48/72 h; binary analgesic (ibuprofen 400 mg PRN). Kruskal–Wallis (Dunn post-hoc), Fisher's exact, Cochran's Q; alpha 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Pain (median VAS)**: 6 h no difference (PUI 2.0 / CNI 3.0 / SNI 2.0; p=0.206). At 24 h (p=0.003), 48 h (p=0.008), 72 h (p=0.001) CNI significantly higher than both PUI and SNI; PUI=SNI throughout. Pain resolved faster and more completely in PUI/SNI.
- **Swelling (% present)**: 6 h NS (PUI 33.3% / CNI 46.7% / SNI 20%; p=0.30). At 24 h CNI 46.7% vs PUI 6.7% vs SNI 0% (p=0.002); CNI > PUI (p=0.035) and > SNI (p=0.003); PUI vs SNI NS. 48 h only CNI 6.7%; 72 h all resolved.
- **Analgesic use (% yes)**: 6 h NS (PUI 26.7% / CNI 46.7% / SNI 13.3%; p=0.162). At 24 h CNI 33.3% vs PUI 0% vs SNI 0% (p=0.007); CNI > both (p=0.042); PUI vs SNI NS.
- **Bottom line**: SNI and PUI clinically equivalent; both beat CNI from 24 h onward. Authors argue minimizing apical extrusion (side-vented design) may matter as much as acoustic agitation in this subgroup, making SNI a simpler/cheaper option.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample (15/group), single operator and single center → limited generalizability and power for subtle differences.
- Swelling and analgesic intake recorded by binary patient self-report (no objective palpation/measurement); pain is subjective VAS.
- No microbiological sampling and no long-term radiographic healing outcome; only asymptomatic necrotic mandibular first molars studied.
- Future: microbiological pre/post sampling, comparison of additional activation technologies (sonic, laser, multisonic), longer follow-up, broader tooth types/diagnoses.

## 6. Related Work

- Aligns with SR/MA and umbrella reviews reporting reduced postoperative pain from activated vs conventional irrigation (Adam & Wootton; Ali et al.; Chalub et al.; Orozco-Gallego et al.).
- Side-vented fluid-dynamics rationale (Boutsioukis et al.; Yost et al.) — lateral dispersion reduces apical pressure/extrusion.
- Conflicting trials: Topçuoğlu et al. (no difference in symptomatic irreversible pulpitis); Palanisamy et al. (PUI slightly favored in single-rooted necrotic teeth); Liang et al. (no long-term radiographic benefit from ultrasonic activation).

## 7. Glossary

- **CNI** — Conventional Needle Irrigation (open-ended 30-G needle).
- **SNI** — Side-vented Needle Irrigation (closed-end, lateral ports; reduces apical extrusion).
- **PUI** — Passive Ultrasonic Irrigation (acoustic streaming + cavitation via ultrasonic tip).
- **Flare-up** — moderate-to-severe postoperative pain and/or swelling after RCT.
- **VAS** — Visual Analogue Scale (0–10 pain).
