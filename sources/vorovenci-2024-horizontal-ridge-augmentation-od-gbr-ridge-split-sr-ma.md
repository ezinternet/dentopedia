## Why Ingested

This is the key HEAD-TO-HEAD synthesis for choosing a horizontal ridge augmentation modality for the narrow ridge — GBR vs ridge-split (RS) vs osseodensification (OD) ridge expansion — ranking them by mean horizontal bone gain in one meta-analysis. It directly ties together the wiki's previously separate technique pages: the ridge-split case literature ([[wiki/bone-regeneration/simion-1992-jawbone-enlargement-split-crest-gtr]], [[wiki/bone-regeneration/ayoub-2018-ridge-splitting-horizontal-augmentation-case]], [[wiki/bone-regeneration/enislidis-2006-staged-ridge-splitting-implant-mandible]]) and the OD-expansion literature ([[wiki/implants/chen-2022-reverse-drilling-technique-alveolar-ridge-expansion]], [[wiki/implants/tian-2019-alveolar-ridge-expansion-osseodensification-osteotome]], [[wiki/implants/koutouzis-2019-alveolar-ridge-expansion-osseodensification-multicenter-retrospective]]). It feeds the [[wiki/overviews/bone-regeneration-protocol-ladder]] with a quantitative gain hierarchy (GBR 4.04 > RS 3.66 > OD 2.15 mm), reinforcing rather than overturning the existing technique pages.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (18 studies, 17 meta-analyzed → 21 groups, 336 patients / 665 implants; Jan 2017–Dec 2022) ranking horizontal ridge augmentation by mean bone gain: GBR 4.04 mm > RS 3.66 mm > OD 2.15 mm (ANOVA P=0.002); implant survival ~99% for all; OD applied to widest ridges, RS to narrowest.

- **PICO**: In patients needing implant rehabilitation of horizontally deficient ridges, how much does horizontal ridge dimension increase with each of three surgical methods?

## 2. Key Contributions

- **First meta-analysis to quantify horizontal bone gain by osseodensification (OD) ridge expansion** and place it head-to-head against GBR and ridge-split (RS); no prior MA had pooled OD horizontal gain.
- Provides a single quantitative gain hierarchy for modality selection: **GBR (4.036 mm) > RS (3.661 mm) > OD (2.151 mm)**, statistically significant overall (ANOVA P=0.002).
- Pairs the gain hierarchy with the **initial ridge width each technique is applied to** (OD widest 4.37 mm, GBR 3.87 mm, RS narrowest 3.43 mm) — i.e. RS handles the thinnest ridges.
- Confirms **implant survival is technique-independent (~99%)**, shifting the choice to bone-gain need, ridge width, complication profile and operator skill rather than survival.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search**: PubMed-Medline, Cochrane CENTRAL, EMBASE, Cochrane Oral Health Group Trials Register, Web of Science + hand search of specialized journals. Jan 2017–Dec 2022 (5-yr window chosen because OD clinical trials only appeared from ~2018). English only, ≥10 subjects, must report pre- and post-op ridge width and ≥6-week follow-up.
- **Quality appraisal**: MINORS (modified by Slim) for non-randomized studies — mean 17/24 (comparative), 12.44 (single-arm); Cochrane RoB for 6 RCTs — 3 low risk, 2 medium risk. Inter-reviewer Cohen kappa 0.96.
- **Synthesis**: Comprehensive Meta-Analysis V3, random-effects model (DerSimonian-Laird; Pearson method of moments). Multi-group studies split into independent groups → 17 studies became 21 groups. Meta-regression with surgical technique and graft type as moderators. Heterogeneity by I², Q, τ²; bias by funnel plots + Egger's test.
- **Selection funnel**: 2,205 hits → 121 duplicates removed → 18 included → 17 meta-analyzed (1 excluded: no implant count, n=562). Final pool: 336 patients, 665 implants.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Final horizontal bone gain (primary, random effects)**: GBR **4.036 mm** (3.351–4.772 95%CI); RS **3.661 mm** (2.991–4.399); OD **2.151 mm** (1.327–2.975); global **3.305 mm** (2.211–4.399). ANOVA **P=0.002**. Pairwise: GBR vs OD P=0.001; **GBR vs RS P=0.09 (NS — overlap)**; RS vs OD P=0.004. Overall I²=94.6%. GBR group was bimodal (low 2.50 mm / high 4.99 mm, each I²=0%).
- **Initial ridge width (secondary)**: OD **4.373 mm** (widest), GBR 3.873 mm, RS **3.433 mm** (narrowest); global 3.875 mm. ANOVA P=0.03; RS vs OD P=0.001, GBR vs OD P=0.08, GBR vs RS P=0.18.
- **Implant survival (secondary)**: overall 99.1%; GBR 99.7%, RS 98.7%, OD 98% — differences NOT significant.
- **Graft-type moderator**: autologous/mixture 4.981 mm (highest) > synthetic 3.694 > xenogeneic 3.177 > allogeneic 2.731 > no graft 2.465 mm. Overall P<0.001; autologous vs no-graft P<0.001, autologous vs xeno P=0.014, no-graft vs xeno P=0.248 (NS).
- **Complications**: GBR — membrane dehiscence/exposure 20% (commonest), oedema 9.5%, hematoma 3.8%, peri-implant mucositis 0.9%. RS — augmented-site dehiscence 12.3%, paresthesia 7.7%, oedema 6.2%.
- **Indications cited**: minimum width GBR 3 mm, RS 2.5–3 mm; OD's primary indication is low-density bone (D3/D4), not narrow ridges, and its horizontal expansion depends on basal bone 5–10 mm from the crest.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Not all studies RCTs; unequal numbers per technique (OD only 4 studies — recent procedure, sparse data) → risk of bias and underpowered OD arm.
- Very high heterogeneity (I² ~95% for gain and initial width) not resolved by subgrouping; sources hard to isolate (graft, membrane, comorbidity, timing).
- Initial ridge width rarely reported as a statistical variable → interpret initial-width results with caution.
- Short 5-yr window; OD basal-bone dependence (5–10 mm) largely unaccounted for. Combination techniques (OD+GBR, RS+GBR) not analyzed and flagged as a future research direction.

## 7. Glossary

- **OD (Osseodensification)**: bur technique (Densah/Versah, Huwais 2013) that compacts bone via plastic deformation, raising density and primary stability and laterally expanding the ridge; primary indication is low-density (D3/D4) bone.
- **GBR (Guided Bone Regeneration)**: barrier membrane (resorbable/non-resorbable/Ti-mesh) + graft particles directing osteoprogenitor migration into a defect.
- **RS (Ridge-Split / split-crest)**: surgical splitting of the crest exploiting medullary viscoelasticity to widen the ridge and create implant space, filled with graft.
- **MINORS**: Methodological Index for Non-randomized Studies (0–24 comparative, 0–6 single-arm).
- **Initial ridge width / final bone gain**: horizontal crest width before surgery and the pre-to-post difference; primary outcome here is final bone gain.
