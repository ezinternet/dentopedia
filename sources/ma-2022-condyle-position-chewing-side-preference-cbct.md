[[wiki/tmj/zheng-2023-unilateral-chewing-tmj-structure-tmd-review]] cites a "Ma 2021/2021" CBCT study as the key human evidence that chewing-side preference (CSP) shifts the condyle posteriorly and increases articular eminence inclination — this is that primary study (Ma 2022, J Oral Rehabil), needed as the primary-data anchor under the narrative review. ## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional CBCT study (n=90 unilateral-symptom TMD patients [69 with chewing-side preference, 21 without] + 20 asymptomatic): condylar position did not strongly differentiate CSP-related from non-CSP-related TMD; TMD-without-CSP patients had significantly more posterior condyles than asymptomatic controls (57.1% vs 30.0%, p<0.05), while CSP patients only trended posterior (53.6% vs 30.0%, p=0.054) — concluding posterior condyle is not a reliable TMD indicator.

- **Authors:** Junli Ma, Jiazhu Wang (co-first authors), Dongzong Huang, Zhaowu Wang, Min Hu, Hongchen Liu, Hua Jiang
- **Journal:** Journal of Oral Rehabilitation, 2022; 49(2): 265–271 (Special Issue: Orofacial Pain in China)
- **First published:** 13 December 2021
- **DOI:** 10.1111/joor.13293
- **Study type:** Cross-sectional comparative CBCT imaging study (case-control structure)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First reported analysis of condylar position specifically in TMD patients stratified by **chewing-side preference (CSP)** status, addressing a stated gap ("the condylar position in TMD patients with CSP is unknown").
- Compares preferred-chewing-side vs unpreferred-side condyles within CSP patients, and symptomatic vs asymptomatic joints, against an asymptomatic control group.
- Challenges the long-held assumption that posterior condyle position is a reliable indicator of TMD: finds it is not a strong discriminator and is not specific to CSP-related TMD.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Participants:** 90 TMD patients with unilateral symptoms — 69 with CSP, 21 without CSP — plus 20 asymptomatic participants. All received cone-beam computed tomography (CBCT).
- **Condylar position determination:** based on measurements of **sagittal joint spaces** (anterior/centric/posterior classification of the condyle within the glenoid fossa). (Exact joint-space formula not recoverable from the abstract-only PDF.)
- **Comparisons:** intergroup (CSP patients vs non-CSP patients vs asymptomatic) and intra-group (preferred vs unpreferred side; symptomatic vs asymptomatic joints).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Asymptomatic controls:** condyles distributed nearly randomly across anterior, centric and posterior positions (posterior ~30.0%).
- **TMD without CSP:** significantly more posterior condyles than asymptomatic participants — **57.1% vs 30.0%, p<0.05**.
- **TMD with CSP:** posterior condyles in **50.7% on the preferred chewing side** and **42.0% on the unpreferred side**; neither reached significance vs asymptomatic or vs non-CSP patients (p>0.05).
- **Symptomatic vs asymptomatic joints:** no significant difference in condylar position within either CSP or non-CSP patients.
- Non-CSP patients had significantly more posterior condyles in symptomatic joints than asymptomatic participants (p<0.05); CSP patients showed only a **trend** toward more posterior condyles in symptomatic joints (**53.6% vs 30.0%, p=0.054**).
- **Conclusion:** condylar position is not a strong indicator to differentiate CSP-related from non-CSP-related TMD; posterior condyle should not be viewed as an indicator of TMD.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Cross-sectional design — no causal or temporal inference about CSP, condyle position and TMD.
- Modest sample, especially the non-CSP TMD subgroup (n=21) and controls (n=20); the borderline p=0.054 for CSP symptomatic joints suggests possible underpowering.
- Condylar position assessed only on sagittal joint spaces (2D-derived metric from 3D data); disc position (MRI) not assessed, so internal derangement status is unknown.
- Full methods, figures and tables not available in this PDF (abstract-only landing page).

## 7. Glossary

- **CSP (Chewing-Side Preference):** habitual preference for chewing predominantly on one side; here used to stratify unilateral-symptom TMD patients.
- **Condylar position:** classification of the mandibular condyle within the glenoid fossa (anterior / centric / posterior) based on sagittal joint-space measurements.
- **Sagittal joint space:** the gap between the condyle and the glenoid fossa measured on sagittal CBCT sections; basis for the anterior/centric/posterior classification.
- **CBCT (Cone-Beam Computed Tomography):** 3D radiographic imaging used here for TMJ osseous and joint-space assessment.
- **TMD (Temporomandibular Disorder):** disorders of the TMJ and masticatory muscles; here all patients had unilateral symptoms.
