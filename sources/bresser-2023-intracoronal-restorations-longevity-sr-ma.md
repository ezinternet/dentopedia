- Random-effects model; risk difference as primary outcome metric

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
SR+MA (PRISMA/Cochrane, PROSPERO CRD42021233185) comparing clinical success and survival of intracoronal indirect restorations: gold, lithium disilicate, leucite, and indirect resin composite. 12 eligible studies, 946 restorations, 1–7 years. Random-effects model, 95% CI. Risk difference used for meta-analysis. I² statistic for heterogeneity. Results: indirect resin composite showed higher failure rates compared to ceramic materials. Gold and lithium disilicate demonstrated superior longevity.
