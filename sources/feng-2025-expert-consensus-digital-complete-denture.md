## Why Ingested

사용자 요청으로 CAD/CAM 치과 기공 관련 논문을 확보하는 과정에서 입수. 기존 [[wiki/complete-denture/srinivasan-2021-cad-cam-removable-complete-dentures]]가 CAD-CAM 총의치의 기계적 물성·유지력을 메타분석으로 정량화한 반면, 본 전문가 합의문(Feng 2025)은 적응증 선정부터 디지털 인상·안모스캔·교합채득·CAD 디자인·CAM 제작·follow-up까지 표준화된 임상 워크플로우 프로토콜을 제시해 실행 수준의 공백을 메운다.

## One-line Summary

Expert consensus statement (Chinese multi-institutional prosthodontics panel) standardizing the digital complete denture workflow — case selection, intraoral/facial scanning, custom tray/jaw-relation digital design, CAD tooth arrangement, additive (SLA/DLP/material jetting/powder bed fusion) and subtractive (CNC milling) manufacturing, and evaluation/outcome metrics — with a synthesis of the current comparative evidence vs conventional dentures.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First unified, standardized clinical protocol for digital complete denture (CD) restoration spanning case selection through long-term follow-up, addressing the current lack of consensus in workflow implementation.
2. Detailed decision criteria for intraoral vs extraoral (facial) scanning based on anatomical and patient factors (gag reflex, mouth opening, soft-tissue mobility, saliva control).
3. Structured digital design guidance for custom tray complexes (tray body, tissue stops, occlusion rims, gothic arch tracers) and three explicit methods for jaw-relation transfer to virtual articulators.
4. Comprehensive comparison of additive (SLA, DLP, material jetting, powder bed fusion) vs subtractive (CNC milling) manufacturing, with practical parameters (e.g., dry vs wet milling).
5. Synthesis of accuracy/performance evidence: intraoral scanning deviations (<0.2 mm vs conventional impressions, <0.9 mm vs gypsum models), retention force ranges (13–20 N), occlusal force ranges (130–225 N), and time savings (58–233 min per case).
6. Explicit protocols for two under-addressed clinical scenarios: transitioning from RPD to CD, and CD re-fabrication using an existing denture as a scanning/reference template.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

This is a consensus statement, not an original clinical study. It was developed collaboratively by prosthodontic specialists and clinical experts across multiple Chinese institutions, synthesizing current clinical evidence and collective expert opinion. No formal Delphi round count or GRADE methodology is reported in the available text; the document presents itself as a narrative consensus review with embedded evidence citations (e.g., a cited systematic review of 39 studies on border molding vs intraoral scanning, and multiple meta-analyses on retention/mechanical/patient-satisfaction outcomes).

Scope covers the full digital workflow:
- Digital data acquisition (intraoral scanning, facial scanning — STL/OBJ/PLY export formats)
- Custom tray complex digital design and 3D-printed fabrication
- Definitive impression and jaw-relation recording (conventional, gothic arch tracer, jaw motion tracking system)
- Digital transfer to average-value or personalized virtual articulators
- CAD denture base and tooth arrangement (esthetic + occlusal design, bilateral balanced occlusion)
- Additive vs subtractive manufacturing
- Follow-up/maintenance intervals (6–12 months)
- Special scenarios: RPD-to-CD transition, CD re-fabrication

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Intraoral scanning accuracy**: deviations within 0.2 mm vs conventional impressions; <0.9 mm vs gypsum models — clinically acceptable, but direct scanning remains limited in movable-mucosa areas (soft palate, sublingual area, vestibular sulcus) critical for border seal/retention. A systematic review of 39 studies confirmed traditional border molding remains the predominant technique for denture base fabrication (better tissue adaptation/retention than intraoral-scan-only).
- **Retention**: digital dentures 13–20 N (force gauge/UTM); border-molded maxillary dentures up to 74 N; meta-analysis: conventional > 3D-printed but milled comparable to conventional; subtractive > injection-molded (less polymerization shrinkage).
- **Accuracy of manufacturing**: subtractive (milled) consistently more precise than additive across in vitro comparisons (lower SD across 7 fabrication systems compared).
- **Occlusal force**: digital CDs 130–225 N (T-scan/occlusal force meters); masticatory efficiency findings mixed across studies.
- **Patient satisfaction / OHRQoL**: meta-analysis found no significant difference between digital and conventional CDs, and no difference among conventional/milled/3D-printed.
- **Efficiency**: digital workflow saves 58–233 minutes of clinical time; fabrication time about one-third of conventional; fewer patient visits required.
- **Esthetics**: some patients rate 3D-printed dentures as less esthetic than milled or conventional (material uniformity favors milled).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- As a consensus statement (not primary research or SR/MA), recommendations rest on expert synthesis of heterogeneous underlying studies rather than pooled formal meta-analytic weighting for every claim.
- Denture stability (resistance to horizontal movement) lacks standardized objective evaluation criteria — acknowledged explicitly as a gap.
- No study has directly validated the superiority of facebows, gothic arch tracers, or articulators specifically within digital denture workflows for jaw-relation accuracy.
- High initial cost of digital equipment/training may limit adoption despite efficiency gains.
- The panel anticipates future integration of AI and augmented reality (AR) into CAD/CAM workflows for individualized esthetic customization and improved edentulous-arch scanning accuracy.

## 7. Glossary

- **CAD/CAM**: Computer-Aided Design / Computer-Aided Manufacturing
- **STL / OBJ / PLY**: 3D file formats (Stereolithography/Standard Tessellation Language; Object File Format; Polygon File Format) used for digital scan/design data exchange
- **CNC milling**: Computer Numerical Control subtractive manufacturing (removing material from a PMMA disc)
- **SLA / DLP**: Stereolithography / Digital Light Processing — vat-photopolymerization 3D printing technologies
- **PMMA**: Polymethyl methacrylate (common CNC-milled denture base/disc material)
- **Gothic arch tracing**: intraoral tracing method for recording centric relation via a registration pin and plates
- **OHRQoL**: Oral Health-Related Quality of Life
