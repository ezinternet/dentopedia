## Why Ingested

Head-to-head comparison of an elastodontic appliance against a conventional Class II interceptive device (high-pull headgear) — the kind of controlled comparator data that the broader elastodontic literature largely lacks. Extends [[wiki/orthodontics/myofunctional/ureni-2024-elastodontic-devices-sagittal-malocclusion-scoping-review]] (which scoped elastodontic effects on sagittal malocclusion but flagged weak comparative evidence) by providing a direct ED-vs-HPHD cephalometric outcome comparison in hyperdivergent growing patients.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective cohort (n=40, 20 ED vs 20 high-pull headgear; ages 7–11; 18-month follow-up) finding both appliances produced a comparable significant intragroup SNB increase with stable vertical (mandibular plane), sagittal (SNA/ANB), dentoalveolar, and soft-tissue parameters — no clinically meaningful between-group differences.

## 한줄요약

후향적 코호트 (n=40, 탄성교정장치 ED 20명 vs 고위견인 헤드기어 HPHD 20명; 7–11세; 18개월 추적): 두 장치 모두 SNB를 유의하게(군내) 증가시켰고 효과 크기는 비슷했으며, 수직(하악평면각)·시상(SNA/ANB)·치조·연조직 지표는 안정적으로 유지되어 군간 임상적 차이는 없었다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Direct head-to-head comparator data.** One of the few studies comparing an elastodontic device (ED) against a conventional, well-established Class II interceptive appliance (high-pull headgear, HPHD) on the same cephalometric outcome set in hyperdivergent growing patients.
2. **Comparable mandibular positional benefit.** Both ED and HPHD produced a statistically significant intragroup SNB increase, with moderate-to-large effect sizes, despite opposite biomechanical rationales (neuromuscular stimulation/mandibular advancement vs orthopaedic maxillary restraint).
3. **Vertical control without exacerbation.** Neither appliance worsened the hyperdivergent pattern — mandibular plane angles (SN^Go-Gn, AnsPns^Go-Gn, N-Go-Gn) stayed stable, though neither produced the ~1–2° mandibular plane reduction some prior HPHD studies report.
4. **Minimal collateral effects.** Dentoalveolar and soft-tissue measures were stable within groups, suggesting skeletal control with limited unwanted side effects for both protocols.
5. **Effect-size–oriented reporting.** Cohen's d reported alongside p-values to give a clinically interpretable magnitude (significant SNB = moderate-to-large d; non-significant vertical/dentoalveolar = negligible d).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Observational retrospective cohort; two parallel groups; T0 (pre-treatment) and T1 (18 months).
- **Sample**: 40 patients enrolled, equally split — 20 ED, 20 HPHD. No dropouts. Ages 7–11. Skeletal Class II, hyperdivergent growth pattern.
- **Sample-size calculation**: ClinCalc.com, α=0.05, power=80%, primary outcome SN^Go-Gn (expected mean 33.9, expected difference 3.9, SD 4.39) → 40 patients (20/group).
- **Interventions**:
  - **ED (trial group)**: removable preformed silicone elastomer elastodontic bioactivator; prescribed wear night-time plus 1 h/day.
  - **HPHD (control group)**: Bio-High-Pull Headgear (FORESTADENT); bilateral facebows on maxillary first-molar bands + occipital strap delivering upward/backward orthopaedic force; bands cemented with glass-ionomer band cement; prescribed wear ~14 h/day.
- **Imaging/analysis**: Same radiologic unit (Soredex Cranex-D); lateral cephalograms traced in Delta-Dent software per Giannì analysis. Cervical Vertebral Maturation (CVM) staging. All measurements by one operator; intra-rater ICC = 0.99.
- **Statistics**: R v3.1.3. Normality by Kolmogorov–Smirnov. Baseline between-group: Mann–Whitney U (non-normal baseline). Longitudinal: ANOVA + Tukey post hoc (repeated-measures correction for intragroup). Cohen's d for effect sizes (small 0.20–0.49, moderate 0.50–0.79, large ≥0.80). Bonferroni correction for multiple comparisons. Linear regression for age/CVM effects. α=0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **SNB (mandibular sagittal position)**: significant intragroup increase T0→T1 in **both** groups (p<0.05); moderate-to-large effect sizes — the principal positive finding. Literature ED SNB increments cited at ~0.8°–1.5°.
- **SNA**: no significant change in either group — neither appliance produced significant maxillary anterior displacement or restriction.
- **ANB**: unchanged in both groups — because SNB rose but SNA did not fall, ANB stayed stable; sagittal correction was modest and mandible-driven.
- **Vertical (mandibular plane) parameters**: SN^Go-Gn, AnsPns^Go-Gn, N-Go-Gn — stable; the only flagged comparison was control-group-T0 vs trial-group-T1 (p<0.05), no other intra/intergroup vertical differences. Second null hypothesis (no vertical difference between groups) **accepted**.
- **Dentoalveolar & soft tissue**: stable within groups over the period; third null hypothesis **accepted**. Effect sizes small/negligible.
- **Regression**: cephalometric scores not significantly influenced by patient age or CVM stage (p>0.05) — though authors note this may be underpowered.
- **Baseline**: Mann–Whitney U confirmed no significant baseline demographic/maturational differences between groups (p>0.05).
- **Hypotheses**: H1 (vertical skeletal) partially rejected; H2 (sagittal skeletal) accepted; H3 (dentoalveolar/soft tissue) accepted.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Unequal prescribed wear time** (ED night + 1 h/day vs HPHD ~14 h/day) and **no objective adherence measurement** — differential compliance may confound the comparison.
- **Non-randomized retrospective design** — selection bias cannot be excluded.
- **2D cephalometry only** — may not capture 3D skeletal/soft-tissue adaptation.
- **Residual growth confounding** — a small proportion of CVMS-3 patients existed only in the HPHD group.
- **Small sample** — regression on age/CVM likely underpowered; "no maturation effect" should be read with caution.
- **No long-term stability data** and **no PROMs** — authors call for future studies adding follow-up stability and patient-reported esthetic/functional/QoL outcomes.

## 6. Related Work

- Ureni et al. 2024 — scoping review of elastodontic devices on sagittal malocclusion; this study supplies the comparative-trial data that scoping review found scarce.
- Ortu et al. 2021 — elastodontic appliance overjet/overbite reduction; companion ED-effect evidence.
- Cardarelli et al. (cited internally; co-author here) — ED SNB increments comparable to Fränkel-2 functional appliances; stable vertical parameters.
- Di Vecchio et al. — preformed intraoral appliances suited to mild-to-moderate functional discrepancies; severe malocclusions need conventional/custom approaches.
- Franchi et al. — CVM/mandibular growth-spurt timing (females ~12.1±1.1 y, males ~13.2±0.8 y), relevant to the SNB-growth interpretation.

## 7. Glossary

- **Elastodontic device (ED)**: removable preformed silicone elastomer appliance exerting light elastic forces for orthopaedic/neuromuscular correction in growing patients.
- **High-pull headgear (HPHD)**: extraoral orthopaedic appliance applying posterior-superior force to restrict maxillary forward/downward growth and control vertical dimension.
- **Hyperdivergent**: vertical growth pattern with increased mandibular plane angle and clockwise mandibular rotation.
- **SNB / SNA / ANB**: cephalometric angles for mandibular sagittal position, maxillary sagittal position, and the maxillo-mandibular sagittal discrepancy.
- **SN^Go-Gn (mandibular plane angle)**: vertical skeletal parameter; here the study's primary outcome.
- **CVM (Cervical Vertebral Maturation)**: radiographic skeletal-maturity staging method.
- **Cohen's d**: standardized effect-size measure (small/moderate/large).
