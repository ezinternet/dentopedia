## One-line Summary
Scoping review (PRISMA-ScR, 10 studies from 3697): conservative access cavity (ConsAC) generates more debris; traditional/guided access (TradAC) shows superior canal orifice localization precision.

## 1. Document Information
University of Foggia group. PRISMA-ScR scoping review on access cavity design effect on canal orifice localization and debris presence.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First scoping review directly comparing ConsAC vs TradAC on canal orifice localization accuracy and debris
- Conservative techniques: potential trend toward more residual debris
- Guided/traditional techniques: superior precision in canal orifice identification

## 3. Methodology
- PRISMA-ScR scoping review
- 3697 records → 10 studies included
- Outcomes: canal orifice localization accuracy, debris/residue presence
- Designs: ConsAC (conservative/ninja/truss/contracted) vs TradAC (traditional/guided)

## 4. Key Results
- Conservative access → potential for more debris remaining
- Traditional/guided access → more reliable canal orifice localization
- 10 studies only — heterogeneous methods limit conclusions

## 5. Limitations
- Only 10 studies included (small evidence base)
- High heterogeneity
- Scoping review — no statistical pooling

## 6. Related Work
- Mohammadi 2016 — strategies for missed canal prevention
- Sujith 2014 / Camacho-Aparicio 2022 — magnification for canal detection

## 7. Glossary
- **ConsAC**: Conservative Access Cavity (ninja, truss, contracted)
- **TradAC**: Traditional Access Cavity
- **PRISMA-ScR**: PRISMA extension for Scoping Reviews
