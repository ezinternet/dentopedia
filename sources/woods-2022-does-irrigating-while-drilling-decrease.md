## One-line Summary
Preclinical in vivo dog tibial study (Iowa Orthop J 2022, 42:22, Level II): saline irrigation and sharp drill bits significantly reduce drilling-induced temperature rise and acute osteonecrosis; conventional trocar-tip Kirschner wires produced the largest temperature rise and the most necrosis.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Iowa Orthopaedic Journal 2022;42:22 (Volume 42, Issue 2)
- PMID: 35821907
- Type: Preclinical in vivo study, anesthetized research dogs
- Affiliation: University of Missouri Department of Orthopaedic Surgery; Thompson Laboratory for Regenerative Orthopaedics
- Disclosures: Crist is unpaid design consultant for Osteocentric Technologies and paid consultant for DePuy Synthes; in-kind funding from both companies.
- Level of evidence: II

## 2. Key Contributions
- In vivo (not in vitro) validation of saline irrigation benefit using viable cortical bone.
- Direct comparison of 5 drilling devices including K-wires (rare in dental literature).
- Quantitative link between sharpness and reduced osteonecrosis.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Animal: anesthetized research dogs.
- Procedure: bicortical drilling of each tibial diaphysis.
- Devices: 5 different (drills, K-wires of various tip designs).
- Conditions: ± saline irrigation.
- Outcomes: temperature change; acute osteonecrosis (histologic).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Saline irrigation: associated with smaller temperature increases and less acute osteonecrosis.
- Sharp drill bits: smaller temperature increases and less acute osteonecrosis.
- Conventional trocar-tip Kirschner wires: largest temperature increases and most acute osteonecrosis.
- Authors' conclusion: avoid dull drill bits and standard-tip K-wires; saline irrigation + sharp drill bits = clinically meaningful.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Canine cortical bone — translation to human alveolar bone requires caution.
- Industry disclosures (Osteocentric, DePuy Synthes) — sponsorship bias risk.
- Orthopedic application — dental implant osteotomy involves different drill diameters and protocols.
- Future: human RCT; correlation with implant survival.

## 6. Related Work
- Timon 2019 — orthopedic literature review (irrigation = most important variable).
- Eriksson & Albrektsson — 47°C/1 min thermal necrosis threshold.
- Dental SRs on irrigation: Chauhan 2018, Saxena 2024.
- Sorgato 2025 — wear effect (different methodology, no irrigation).

## 7. Glossary
- **K-wire (Kirschner wire)**: thin metal wire used in orthopedic surgery for fracture fixation; trocar tip = pointed three-edge geometry.
- **Bicortical drilling**: drilling through both cortices of long bone.
- **Acute osteonecrosis**: bone death observable within days of insult.
- **Level II evidence**: prospective controlled study (here, controlled animal model).
