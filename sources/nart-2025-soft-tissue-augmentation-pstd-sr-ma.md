## Why Ingested

기존 [[implants/soft-tissue/sanz-martin-2022-buccal-soft-tissue-dehiscence-implants-sr-ma]]는 협측 PSTD 치료의 임상 결과(피개율, MBL)를 합산했으나 심미 점수(전문가 평가·환자 보고)에 대한 메타분석은 포함하지 않았다. 본 Nart 2025 SR+MA는 동일 주제에서 심미 결과와 환자 만족도를 1차 outcome으로 삼아 10개의 meta-analysis를 수행, [[implants/soft-tissue/tavelli-2025-soft-tissue-dehiscences-implant-sites-ao-aap]]의 PSTD 유병율/위험인자 증거에 치료 심미 효과 데이터를 추가한다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (5 studies, 87 patients): coronally advanced flap (CAF) + CTG/substitutes for buccal PSTD achieves professional esthetic score 7.7/10 and 71% complete coverage with moderate-to-high heterogeneity.

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (5편/87명): 협측 임플란트 연조직 열개부 (Peri-implant Soft Tissue Dehiscence, PSTD)에 관상판막 전진술 (Coronally Advanced Flap, CAF) + 결합조직이식 (Connective Tissue Graft, CTG) 적용 시 전문가 심미점수 7.7/10, 완전 피개율 71% (중등도-높은 이질성).

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA to pool professional esthetic scores and patient-reported outcomes (VAS) specifically for buccal PSTD treatment
- Quantifies complete PSTD coverage rate at 71% (95% CI: 59–82%) via meta-analysis
- Demonstrates 2.2 mm PSTD depth reduction (95% CI: 1.76–2.69) with CAF-based STA
- Includes prosthetic component modification as adjunct in 2 of 5 studies
- Identifies moderate-to-high heterogeneity as a limitation — evidence base remains thin (5 studies, 87 patients)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Systematic review + meta-analysis, PRISMA protocol
- **Eligibility**: RCTs and prospective studies; buccal PSTD without peri-implantitis; follow-up ≥6 months
- **Databases**: Electronic + hand search (databases not specified in abstract)
- **Included**: 8 publications reporting 5 studies (2 RCTs, 3 prospective), published 2013–2024, total 87 patients
- **Intervention**: All studies evaluated CAF + CTG or substitutes; one RCT arm used tunnel procedure; 2 studies also modified the prosthetic component
- **Primary outcomes**: Professional esthetic assessment; patient-reported satisfaction (VAS 0–100)
- **Secondary outcomes**: Clinical variables (PSTD depth, coverage rate)
- **Statistical model**: Fixed- or random-effect model; 10 meta-analyses performed
- **Risk of bias**: 3 of 5 studies rated low risk

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Value | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|
| Professional esthetic score (0–10) | 7.7 | 6.63–8.83 |
| Patient-reported esthetics (VAS 0–100) | 60.8 | 46.56–75.01 |
| PSTD depth reduction (mm) | 2.2 | 1.76–2.69 |
| Complete PSTD coverage rate | 71% | 59–82% |

- Heterogeneity: moderate-to-high for esthetic outcomes
- Conclusion: STA procedures positively influence both professional and patient-reported esthetics (limited evidence)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Very small evidence base: only 5 studies, 87 patients
- Moderate-to-high heterogeneity limits precision of pooled estimates
- Only 3/5 studies at low risk of bias
- No subgroup analysis separating CTG vs substitutes (implied from abstract)
- Prosthetic modification confounds soft tissue-only STA effect in 2 studies
- Need for larger RCTs with standardized esthetic measurement tools

## 7. Glossary

- **PSTD (Peri-implant Soft Tissue Dehiscence)**: Apical migration of peri-implant mucosal margin exposing the implant surface/restoration buccally, analogous to gingival recession around natural teeth
- **CAF (Coronally Advanced Flap)**: Surgical technique repositioning the soft tissue margin coronally to cover recession/dehiscence defects
- **CTG (Connective Tissue Graft)**: Subepithelial palatal graft used under CAF to augment tissue volume and support coronal advancement
- **STA (Soft Tissue Augmentation)**: Broad term for surgical procedures increasing keratinized mucosa width/thickness or covering soft tissue defects around implants
- **PRO (Patient-Reported Outcome)**: Outcome measured directly from patient self-report (e.g., VAS esthetic satisfaction), without clinician interpretation
- **VAS (Visual Analogue Scale)**: 0–100 mm scale for subjective outcome measurement (e.g., esthetic satisfaction)
