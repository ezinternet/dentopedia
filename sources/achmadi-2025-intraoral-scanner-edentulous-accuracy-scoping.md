## One-line Summary
무치악 악궁 인상 정확도 IOS vs 기공실 스캐너 scoping review (BDJ Open 2025): PRISMA-ScR; 312건 검색 → 8편 선정; IOS는 무치악에도 유용하나 형태학적 차이가 스캔 결과에 영향.

- **Journal**: BDJ Open 2025, 11:13
- **DOI**: 10.1038/s41405-025-00300-4
- **Published**: 2025 (Received Sep 2024; Accepted Dec 2024)
- **Study type**: Scoping review (PRISMA-ScR; Arksey & O'Malley framework)
- **Institution**: Universitas Padjadjaran, Bandung, Indonesia

## 2. Key Contributions
- Compares accuracy of intraoral scanners (IOS) versus laboratory scanners for fully and partially edentulous arch impressions
- 312 articles identified across four databases (PubMed, Scopus, SpringerLink, ScienceDirect); 8 selected after screening
- IOS shows promise for edentulous cases but faces challenges: smooth featureless mucosal surfaces impede tracing/stitching algorithms
- Morphological differences between edentulous ridges and dentate arches reduce IOS accuracy compared to dentate conditions
- Laboratory scanners generally maintain higher accuracy in edentulous cases via indirect digitization of conventional casts
- Cumulative error increases with extended scanning distances in edentulous arches (consistent with IOS arch-size effect)

## 3. Methodology
- **Design**: Scoping review; PRISMA-ScR + Arksey & O'Malley 5-stage methodological approach
- **Databases**: PubMed, Scopus, SpringerLink, ScienceDirect; last decade of publications
- **Inclusion**: Studies analyzing accuracy of IOS and/or laboratory scanners in fully or partially edentulous cases
- **Output**: Thematic analysis; results tabulated for comparison

## 4. Key Results
- 8 articles met inclusion criteria from 312 retrieved
- Both fully and partially edentulous arches studied; most studies examined both
- IOS is extensively utilized and shows considerable promise for edentulous impressions
- Key accuracy limitations for IOS in edentulous: smooth surfaces → poor feature matching; reduced anatomical landmarks; longer scan path → error accumulation
- Laboratory scanners: indirect method (conventional impression → cast → scan) generally more accurate for edentulous
- Factors influencing digital scan accuracy: arch morphology, ridge height/form, software stitching algorithms, operator technique

## 5. Limitations
- Scoping review: no quality assessment, no meta-analysis, no risk of bias scoring
- Only 8 studies selected — small evidence base
- Rapid technology evolution; newer IOS generations may outperform reviewed devices
- Heterogeneous outcome metrics across included studies limit direct comparison
