## Why Ingested

Provides direct animal-experimental evidence for the "occlusal loading → alveolar bone density/microarchitecture" axis: when occlusal stimulus is removed, mandibular alveolar bone loses trabecular structure, and when occlusion is restored, the architecture recovers. This complements [[wiki/bone-biology/kondo-2022-current-perspectives-residual-ridge]] (which argues residual ridge resorption is pathological osteoclast activation rather than pure disuse atrophy) by showing the reversible disuse/reload microarchitectural response in growing rats, and reinforces [[wiki/bone-biology/tokavanich-2025-control-alveolar-bone-development]] on mechanical control of alveolar bone microarchitecture (BV/TV, Tb.Th, Tb.N).

## One-line Summary

Rat micro-CT study (n=48 growing male SD rats; normal n=24, hypofunction n=12, recovery n=12) showing occlusal hypofunction induced by an anterior bite-raising appliance reduces mandibular alveolar trabecular microarchitecture (lower BV/TV, Tb.Th, Tb.N; higher Tb.Sp and bone surface density), and that these changes reverse to normal after appliance removal and occlusal recovery.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Demonstrates that loss of occlusal stimulus (occlusal hypofunction) drives three-dimensional remodeling of mandibular alveolar bone in growing rats, captured by micro-CT trabecular indices.
- Demonstrates **reversibility**: after removing the bite-raising appliance, both masseter muscle weight and the altered alveolar bone architecture gradually return to normal levels.
- Couples a muscular outcome (masseter weight) with a structural bone outcome, linking masticatory/occlusal function to alveolar bone microarchitecture.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Animals**: 48 growing male Sprague–Dawley rats, randomized into three groups: normal/control (n=24), hypofunction (n=12), recovery (n=12).
- **Hypofunction model**: a bite-raising appliance inserted between the maxillary and mandibular incisors to reduce posterior occlusal contact/loading.
- **Recovery model**: two weeks after insertion the appliance was removed; the experiment continued for two additional weeks to assess recovery.
- **Sacrifice schedule**: experimental and control animals killed weekly.
- **Measurements**: body weight; masseter muscle weight; histomorphology and microstructure of mandibular alveolar bone scanned by micro-CT (trabecular cross-sectional area, BV/TV, Tb.Th, Tb.N, Tb.Sp, bone surface density; alveolar process height/width).
- **Statistics**: significance reported at P < 0.05 (differences) and P > 0.05 (return to normal in recovery group).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

(Directional findings from the abstract; numeric magnitudes not available in the extracted text.)

- **Masseter muscle**: lighter (atrophied) in the hypofunction group vs control (P < 0.05).
- **Alveolar process shape**: higher and narrower alveolar process in the hypofunction group vs control (P < 0.05).
- **Trabecular remodeling in hypofunction group** (especially marked at week 2, P < 0.05):
  - ↓ trabecular cross-sectional area
  - looser trabecular bone
  - ↓ bone volume fraction (BV/TV)
  - ↓ trabecular thickness (Tb.Th)
  - ↓ trabecular number (Tb.N)
  - ↑ bone surface density
  - ↑ trabecular separation (Tb.Sp)
- **Recovery group**: after appliance removal, masseter weight and alveolar bone architecture gradually reversed and reached normal levels by the end of the experiment (P > 0.05 vs control).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Growing-rat model; extrapolation to adult human alveolar bone and to clinical occlusal hypofunction (e.g., opposing-tooth loss, non-functional implants) is indirect.
- Anterior bite-raising appliance creates a specific (posterior) loss of occlusal contact; not identical to all clinical hypofunction scenarios.
- Two-week recovery window; long-term durability of recovery not assessed in the abstract.
- Quantitative effect sizes not recoverable from the abstract-only extraction.

## 7. Glossary

- **Occlusal hypofunction**: reduced or absent functional occlusal loading on a tooth/region (e.g., from antagonist loss or a bite-opening device).
- **Micro-CT (microcomputed tomography)**: high-resolution 3D X-ray imaging used to quantify trabecular bone microarchitecture.
- **BV/TV (bone volume fraction)**: ratio of mineralized bone volume to total tissue volume.
- **Tb.Th / Tb.N / Tb.Sp**: trabecular thickness / number / separation — core 3D trabecular indices.
- **Bone surface density (BS/TV or BS/BV)**: bone surface per unit volume; rises as trabeculae thin and fragment.
- **Masseter muscle weight**: surrogate for masticatory muscle bulk/activity, here a functional correlate of occlusal loading.
