## Why Ingested

이쑤시개법 양치질(칫솔모를 쓰는 와타나베법, [[periodontics/lee-2020-watanabe-toothpick-method-srp-diabetic-periodontitis-rct]])과 자주 혼동되는 **목재 이쑤시개(치간청소용)의 위해**를 분리해 기록하기 위해 인제스트. 와타나베법은 잇몸 치유를 촉진하는 반면, 나무 이쑤시개의 과도·수직 사용은 치간유두(interdental papilla)를 소실시켜 블랙트라이앵글을 만든다는 반대 방향 근거 — 토스픽법 페이지군과 짝을 이루는 안전성 경고. 치간 접촉소실/유두 관련 [[food-impaction/liang-2020-prevalence-associated-factors-retrospective]]와도 연결.

## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional study (n=69, ages 20–29; 87% toothpick users) — habitual wooden-toothpick interdental use is associated with significantly reduced interdental papilla presence (PPI, P<.05) in maxillary anterior teeth ("black triangles"), worse with the vertical up/down technique, frequency >3×/day, and duration >3 years; alveolar bone level and proximal contact length were unaffected (abstract-only).

## 한줄요약

단면연구(n=69, 20–29세, 87%가 이쑤시개 사용) — 나무 이쑤시개의 습관적 치간 사용은 상악 전치부 치간유두를 유의하게 소실시켜(PPI, P<.05) '블랙트라이앵글'을 유발하며, 수직(상하) 기법·하루 3회 초과·3년 초과 사용에서 더 심함. 치조골 수준·접촉면 길이는 무영향 (초록 기반).

- **Type**: Cross-sectional observational study (questionnaire + clinical measurement)
- **Journal**: Quintessence International 2026;57(5):432–442
- **PMID**: 41716131 · **DOI**: 10.3290/j.qi.b6912614
- **Artifact**: pubmed-text, **abstract-only** (Quintessence Int — no PMC open-access full text retrievable; Summary/Results limited to abstract level)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies a harm of a **near-universal habit** (87% of this young, otherwise-healthy sample used toothpicks) — links wooden-toothpick interdental cleaning to **interdental papilla loss / black triangles** in the esthetic zone.
- Isolates **technique** as a modifiable risk: vertical "up/down" insertion damaged papilla more than horizontal "in/out".
- Identifies **dose-response** signals: frequency >3×/day and duration >3 years → more severe papilla loss.
- Shows the damage is **soft-tissue specific** — no difference in alveolar bone level or proximal contact length between users and nonusers.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Cross-sectional, n=69, ages 20–29, all manual toothbrushers using **toothpicks exclusively** as their interdental aid (no floss/brushes), to isolate the toothpick effect.
- **Papilla Presence Index (PPI)** for interdental papilla integrity; clinical measurement of papilla height, proximal contact length, alveolar bone level; structured questionnaire for frequency, duration, technique.
- Users vs nonusers comparison.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- 87% reported toothpick use.
- Toothpick users had **significantly reduced papilla presence** vs nonusers (P < .05).
- **Vertical technique > horizontal technique** for papilla loss.
- **Frequency >3×/day** and **duration >3 years** significantly associated with **more severe** papilla loss.
- **No significant difference** in alveolar bone level or proximal contact length → hard-tissue structures unaffected.
- Conclusion: frequent/prolonged toothpick use (especially vertical) is associated with interdental papilla loss ("black triangles"); clinicians should advise **safer interdental cleaning alternatives**.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Cross-sectional → no causality**; association only.
- **Modest sample (n=69)**, narrow age band (20–29), single setting; self-reported habit data (recall bias).
- Abstract-only ingest — effect magnitudes (PPI distribution, odds ratios) not available beyond the abstract.
- Longitudinal/interventional studies and larger samples needed to confirm dose-response and establish causality.

## 7. Glossary

- **Interdental papilla (치간유두)**: gum tissue filling the embrasure between adjacent teeth.
- **Black triangle (블랙트라이앵글)**: open gingival embrasure when the papilla does not fill the interproximal space — an esthetic concern in the anterior zone.
- **Papilla Presence Index (PPI)**: ordinal index grading how completely the papilla fills the embrasure.
- **Proximal contact length (접촉면 길이)**: vertical length of the contact area between adjacent teeth.
