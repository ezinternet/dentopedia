## Why Ingested

The chairside risk-scoring tool for lateral window 상악동거상술 (Sinus Floor Elevation, SFE) perforation needs quantified hazard ratios — not just trend-level signals — for each risk factor (septa, mucous retention cyst, smoking, edentulous span). This prospective cohort (n=140) provides exactly those HR values (e.g. septa HR 8.07, smoking HR 25, mucous retention cyst HR 27.75) that can translate directly into a weighted risk checklist. Reinforces and numerically grounds [[wiki/sinus-lift/lateral/manor-2024-cbct-prognostic-factors-sinus-perforation]], which offered trend-only CBCT features without inferential risk quantification.

## One-line Summary

Prospective cohort (n=140, Iran) of lateral-window SFE: septa HR 8.07, mucous retention cyst HR 27.75, smoking HR 25, multi-tooth edentulous span HR 68.09 — all independent perforation risk factors (DOI: 10.1016/j.jcms.2023.06.010).

## 2. Key Contributions

- First prospective cohort study to simultaneously quantify multiple anatomical, habitual, and pathological perforation risk factors using hazard ratios (HR) in lateral-window SFE.
- Confirms sinus septa as a strong independent risk factor (HR 8.07).
- Identifies smoking as a major modifiable risk (HR 25, i.e. 25× higher perforation risk vs non-smokers).
- Identifies mucous retention cyst (MRC) as the strongest anatomical risk factor measured (HR 27.75).
- Demonstrates that multi-tooth edentulous span carries an extreme HR (68.09) compared with single-tooth gaps.
- Provides clinically actionable pre-operative CBCT-based risk stratification framework.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Prospective cohort study
- **Setting**: Department of Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery, Shahid Beheshti University of Medical Sciences
- **Period**: January 1, 2019 – September 31, 2021
- **Sample**: 140 subjects (81 male, 59 female); mean age 54.66 ± 11.73 years
- **Inclusion criteria**: Edentulous posterior maxilla with residual bone height < 5 mm, candidate for lateral-window SFE
- **Pre-operative assessment**: CBCT for all subjects
- **Outcome variable**: Presence/absence of Schneiderian membrane perforation (intra-operative)
- **Predictive factors assessed via CBCT**:
  - Presence of sinus septa
  - Presence of mucous retention cyst
  - Lateral wall thickness
  - Membrane thickness
  - Residual bone height (RBH)
- **Covariates**: Age, gender, smoking status
- **Statistical analysis**: Cox proportional hazards regression (hazard ratios with 95% CI)

### Sample Characteristics
- Mean residual bone height: 2.83 ± 0.94 mm (range 1–5 mm)
- Mean membrane thickness: 0.77 ± 0.56 mm
- Mean buccal bone (lateral wall) thickness: 1.54 ± 0.97 mm
- Septa present: 24 subjects (17.1%)
- Smokers: 36 subjects (25.7%)
- Mucous retention cyst: 21 subjects (15%)
- Single-tooth edentulous gap: 33 subjects (23.6%)
- Multi-tooth edentulous span (≥2 teeth): 107 subjects (76.4%)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Hazard Ratios for Membrane Perforation

| Risk Factor | HR (95% CI) | p-value |
|---|---|---|
| Sinus septa present | 8.07 (2.93–22.29) | < 0.001 |
| Mucous retention cyst present | 27.75 (8.73–88.23) | < 0.001 |
| Smoking | 25.00 (7.58–82.51) | < 0.001 |
| Multi-tooth edentulous span (≥2 teeth) | 68.09 (9.52–49.16)* | not reported separately |

*Note: CI bounds appear as published (9.52–49.16 likely a typographic issue in the original paper — the HR of 68.09 with lower-bound 9.52 is internally consistent but the upper CI may have been truncated in the abstract).

### Additional risk factors noted in conclusion (effect sizes not fully reported in abstract):
- Increasing age
- Lateral wall (buccal bone) thickness
- Residual bone height
- Membrane thickness

### Background prevalence context:
- Lateral-window SFE membrane perforation rate reported as 10%–60% in existing literature
- Study population had mean membrane thickness 0.77 mm (thin; perforation risk increases < 0.8 mm per Munakata 2021)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-level extraction only — full statistical tables (for continuous variables: membrane thickness cut-offs, lateral wall thickness threshold, RBH threshold) not accessible in this extraction.
- Single-center study (Iran); may not generalize across populations or surgeon experience levels.
- Surgeon experience/skill not controlled as a covariate.
- Lateral window technique standardization not fully described.
- Continuous variable HRs (for membrane thickness, RBH, lateral wall thickness) require full-text access.

## 7. Glossary

- **Schneiderian membrane**: The ciliated pseudostratified columnar epithelium lining the maxillary sinus floor; the tissue at risk during SFE.
- **Lateral window approach**: Surgical technique creating an osseous window in the lateral maxillary sinus wall to access and elevate the Schneiderian membrane.
- **Hazard ratio (HR)**: In a Cox regression, the instantaneous risk of the event (perforation) in the exposed group relative to the unexposed group.
- **Mucous retention cyst (MRC)**: Fluid-filled dome-shaped lesion arising from blocked serous glands in the sinus lining; predisposes to tearing during elevation.
- **Sinus septa**: Bony partitions within the maxillary sinus (Underwood's septa); most common in the posterior region; complicates membrane elevation.
- **Residual bone height (RBH)**: Vertical distance from the alveolar crest to the sinus floor; determines access geometry and instrument angulation.
