## Why Ingested

Super-Bond 4-META/MMA-TBB resin 화학의 성분·물성을 직접 특성화한 in-vitro 연구로, 4-META 기능성 모노머·접착 화학을 다루는 [[resin-bonding/delgado-2025-functional-monomers-dental-adhesive-technology]]를 물성(curing time, flexural strength, shear bond strength)·적응증(root caries 노인 수복) 측면에서 확장. Bondfill SB Plus(상용 4-META/MMA-TBB 수복재)의 filler 선택 근거를 제공하며, 노년치의학의 root caries 수복재 선택([[caries/schmidt-2024-root-caries-restorative-adhesive-nma]], [[caries/meyer-lueckel-2019-intervene-root-caries-process-sr-ma]])에 재료과학적 근거를 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro study (DSC + 3-point flexural + shear bond to root dentin) of 3 wt% fillers in 4-META/MMA-TBB resin (Super-Bond chemistry): organic composite filler shortened curing time and raised post-thermocycling dentin bond strength, while bioactive glass (45S5) and aluminosilicate glass prolonged curing and lowered flexural/bond strength — organic composite filler is the most suitable additive.

## 한줄요약

In-vitro 연구(DSC + 3점 굴곡강도 + 근면 상아질 전단접착): 4-META/MMA-TBB(슈퍼본드 화학) 수지에 3 wt% filler를 첨가했을 때, 유기 복합 filler는 경화시간을 단축하고 열순환 후 상아질 접착강도를 높인 반면, 생활성유리(45S5)·알루미노실리케이트 유리는 경화를 지연시키고 굴곡·접착강도를 낮춰 — 노인 root caries 수복에는 유기 복합 filler가 가장 적합.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Directly characterizes **4-META/MMA-TBB resin = Super-Bond chemistry** as a *restorative* material (not just adhesive) for the **root-caries / geriatric** indication.
- Compares three filler classes at a fixed **3 wt%** load against the commercial organic-filler product **Bondfill SB Plus (BSP, Sun Medical)**:
  - **Organic composite filler (OC)** — radiopaque polymer-coated, 15 µm, 7.0 g/cm³, 6.84 vol%.
  - **Bioactive glass (BG)** — 45S5, 5 µm, 2.8 g/cm³, 1.61 vol%.
  - **Aluminosilicate glass (AS)** — 1 µm, 2.4 g/cm³, 1.87 vol%.
- Four outcomes: **curing time** (DSC), **3-point flexural strength** (ISO 4049), **shear bond strength to root dentin** (24 h vs 10,000-cycle thermocycling), **mineralization ability** (SBF immersion, SEM/EDS/GIXRD).
- Identifies **organic composite filler as the optimal additive** (faster handling + retained/improved bond), and flags **bioactive + aluminosilicate glass as detrimental** to bonding/mechanics at this load.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Resin system**: 4-META/MMA-TBB (chemically cured, **not photocurable** — no light unit; tri-n-butylborane initiator). Cured at 37 °C.
- **Groups**: OC_multi, OC_opacious, BG_multi, BG_opacious, AS, plus commercial **BSP** control. (multi vs opacious differ only in <1 wt% pigment; >99 wt% identical.)
- **Curing time**: isothermal DSC at 37 °C; time of maximum exothermic peak = curing time (n=3).
- **Flexural strength**: 2×2×25 mm rods, brush-applied to Teflon molds per ISO 4049, stored 37 °C/100% RH ×1 wk, 3-point bend, 20 mm span, 1 mm/min (n=15). Weibull (MLE) analysis.
- **Mineralization**: 10 mm × 2 mm disks immersed in **simulated body fluid (SBF, ISO 23317)** at 37 °C for 4 or 8 wk; SEM, EDS, GIXRD (Cu Kα) for hydroxyapatite (n=10, BSP/BG groups only).
- **Shear bond to root dentin**: extracted human teeth, sound root dentin, **all caries removed**; Teeth Primer (Sun Medical) 20 s + air-dry, base+catalyst mix, Ultradent mold (Ø2.4 mm × 2.5 mm), cure 30 min RT. Two storage arms: **24 h ultrapure water 37 °C** vs **thermocycling 10,000× 5↔55 °C** (n=15). Linear mixed-effects model (tooth = random effect). Fracture mode by optical microscopy.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Curing time** (faster = better handling): OC_multi, OC_opacious, AS **significantly shorter** than BSP/BG groups (P<0.001). BG groups ≈ BSP (no diff). BG_opacious longer than BG_multi (P=0.00808). Mechanism: 45S5 raises pH → reduces acidic-monomer reactivity → inhibits TBB-initiated polymerization → slower cure.

**3-point flexural strength**: OC_opacious, BG_multi, BG_opacious, AS **significantly LOWER than BSP**. OC_multi lower than BSP but **not significant** (i.e., OC_multi essentially preserves flexural strength). AS slightly higher than the BG/OC_opacious groups but still below BSP.

**Shear bond strength to root dentin**:
- **24 h**: no significant differences among groups.
- **After 10,000-cycle thermocycling**: **OC_multi & OC_opacious significantly HIGHER** than all other groups (P<0.05).
- Intragroup (24 h → TC): **BSP** decreased (P=0.0041), **BG_multi** decreased (P=0.0050); BG_opacious & AS no change; **OC_multi INCREASED** (P=0.0376), **OC_opacious INCREASED** (P=0.0075).
- **Fracture modes**: no cohesive failures in any group. Adhesive-dominant at 24 h, shifting to more **mixed** failures after thermocycling (interface strengthened over thermomechanical load). e.g. OC_multi 24h adhesive 66.7% → TC 45.7%; BG_opacious mixed 46.7% → 86.7% after TC.

**Mineralization** (BG groups): EDS showed strong C, O, Yb but **Ca/P extremely weak**; SEM showed needle-like crystals on some BG specimens, but **GIXRD detected NO crystalline hydroxyapatite** at 4 or 8 wk. → No clear remineralization at 3 wt% BG (contrast Kohda et al. needing 30–50 wt% for measurable enamel effect).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro only; bonding to **sound root dentin after complete caries removal**, not infected/affected dentin (does not model real geriatric clinical field with bleeding/saliva contamination).
- Storage/thermocycling medium limited to **ultrapure water** (no PBS/SBF), which may understate bioactivity-mediated bond changes.
- Single **3 wt%** filler load — too low for BG to show mineralization; optimal BG concentration balancing remineralization vs cure/bond/mechanics not yet identified.
- Industry funding (Sun Medical provided materials + partial funding; authors declare no COI).

## 6. Related Work

- Saeki et al. — Bondfill SB (organic-filler 4-META/MMA-TBB) higher shear bond than conventional 4-META/MMA-TBB adhesive (reduced interfacial inhibition layer, better stress distribution).
- Kohda et al. — 0–50 wt% bioactive glass in 4-META/MMA-TBB; 30–50 wt% raised adjacent-enamel Knoop hardness / acid-neutralizing capacity; bond strength to enamel fell with rising BG.
- Yamamoto et al. — 4-META/MMA-TBB + BAC/CPC; ≤2.5 wt% no bond change, 5.0 wt% BAC reduced flexural & bond strength.
- Kim et al. — PMMA powder MW/particle size in 4-META/MMA-TBB affects degree of polymerization and Ti bond durability.

## 7. Glossary

- **4-META/MMA-TBB**: 4-methacryloxyethyl trimellitate anhydride / methyl methacrylate – tri-n-butylborane; the chemically-cured adhesive resin system marketed as **Super-Bond** (and as restorative **Bondfill SB / SB Plus**). 4-META is the functional adhesion-promoting monomer; TBB is the polymerization initiator.
- **45S5 bioactive glass (BG)**: Hench bioactive glass (SiO₂–Na₂O–CaO–P₂O₅), ion-releasing, can form hydroxyapatite/raise pH.
- **Aluminosilicate glass (AS)**: inorganic glass filler intended to raise flexural strength.
- **Organic composite filler (OC)**: pre-polymerized polymer-coated radiopaque filler; reduces polymerization shrinkage stress.
- **DSC**: differential scanning calorimetry — exothermic peak time used as curing-time proxy here.
- **SBF**: simulated body fluid (ISO 23317), used to test apatite-forming/mineralization ability.
- **GIXRD**: grazing-incidence X-ray diffraction — surface-sensitive crystalline-phase detection (hydroxyapatite).
- **BSP**: Bondfill SB Plus, commercial organic-filler 4-META/MMA-TBB restorative (Sun Medical), used as comparator.
