## One-line Summary

2011 InTech narrative review covering titanium implant surface types (machined, sandblasted, SLA, anodized, HA-coated, nano), their physicochemical properties, and mechanisms of bone response including inflammation, MSC recruitment, and osseointegration.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Comprehensive classification of implant surfaces by roughness: smooth (Sa < 0.5 µm), minimally rough (0.5–1.0 µm), moderately rough (1.0–2.0 µm), rough (>2.0 µm).
2. Systematic review of surface modification methods: machined → sandblasted → acid-etched → SLA → anodically oxidized → laser-modified → HA/BAG coated → nano-surface.
3. Biological framework: surface properties influence protein adsorption, platelet adhesion, complement activation, inflammatory cytokine expression (TNF-α, IL-1β), MSC recruitment (CXCR4/SDF-1α axis), and integrin-mediated attachment.
4. Emerging nanotechnology: discrete crystalline deposits (DCD), TiO₂ nanotubes, protein adsorption-modifying nanostructures — accelerate bone formation but lack RCT evidence at time of writing.
5. Bioactive coatings: HA, fluoride-modified HA, strontium-HA, biomimetic apatite, bioactive glass (BAG) — enhance early BIC but long-term clinical RCT evidence still limited as of 2011.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Narrative review synthesizing in vitro, in vivo animal, and available clinical evidence (no systematic search protocol reported). Uses rat tibia model data prominently (qPCR + immunohistochemistry + removal torque). References span Brånemark (1969) through 2010 literature.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Surface | Sa (µm) | Notable outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Machined | 0.3–1.0 | 56–85% BIC after 16 yr clinically; highest fibrinous response acutely |
| Sandblasted (25 µm) | 0.5–2.0 | Higher removal torque vs machined at 12 wk; optimal ≈1.5 µm |
| SLA (SLActive) | ~1.0–2.0 | Hydrophilic SLActive shows faster healing; clinically well-documented |
| Anodized (TiUnite) | ~1.0–2.0 | 11× higher CXCR4 expression vs machined at 12 h; lower pro-inflammatory cytokines |
| HA-coated | — | Higher early BIC; long-term delamination risk noted |
| BAG-coated | — | ≥ HA at 4/12/24 wk BIC in animal; 1-yr clinical pilot equivalent to HA |
| Nanostructured | varies | Enhanced osteoblast adhesion in vitro/vivo; no RCT evidence (2011) |

Key molecular finding: anodically oxidized surface upregulates CXCR4 (MSC recruitment receptor) 11-fold vs machined surface at 12 h post-implantation, coupled with predominance of mesenchymal stem cells at interface.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- No systematic review methodology; selection bias possible.
- Nano- and bioactive surfaces lack RCT-level clinical evidence as of 2011.
- Surface roughness vs chemistry confounding not resolved — unclear which drives improved bone response.
- Authors call for standardized roughness measurement guidelines (Sa parameters) for cross-study comparability.
- Future directions: nano-micro hierarchical surfaces, drug/growth-factor delivery coatings, antibacterial surfaces.

## 7. Glossary

- **Sa**: arithmetic mean surface roughness (µm) — primary roughness descriptor
- **Sdr**: developed surface area ratio vs flat — indicates functional surface area
- **BIC**: Bone-to-Implant Contact (골-임플란트 접촉률)
- **SLA**: Sand-blasted, Large grit, Acid-etched surface
- **TiUnite**: Nobel Biocare anodically oxidized implant surface
- **HA**: Hydroxyapatite (수산화인회석) coating
- **BAG**: Bioactive Glass coating
- **CXCR4/SDF-1α**: chemokine receptor/ligand axis for MSC recruitment (골수줄기세포 모집)
- **qPCR**: quantitative polymerase chain reaction — gene expression quantification
- **DCD**: Discrete Crystalline Deposits — calcium phosphate nano-modification
- **MSC**: Mesenchymal Stem Cell (중간엽줄기세포)
