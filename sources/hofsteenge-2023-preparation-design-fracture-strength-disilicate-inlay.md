## 3. Methodology and Architecture
In vitro + finite element analysis (n=64 human molars) comparing four preparation designs for lithium disilicate restorations on structurally compromised molars: Undermined Inlay (UI), Extended Inlay (EI), Restricted Overlay (RO), Extended Overlay (EO). All restored with IDS (Immediate Dentin Sealing) and subjected to thermomechanical fatigue (1.2×10⁶ cycles, 50N, 5–55°C) before load-to-failure testing. FEA assessed tooth deformation. Overlay designs showed superior fracture resistance. Polymerization-induced crack formation evaluated with optical microscopy.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Four designs tested: Undermined Inlay, Extended Inlay, Restricted Overlay, Extended Overlay
- Overlay designs (RO, EO) showed superior fracture resistance over inlay designs
- IDS (Immediate Dentin Sealing) used in all groups
- FEA confirmed reduced tooth deformation with cusp coverage designs
- Polymerization-induced cracks more frequent in deeper inlay preparations
