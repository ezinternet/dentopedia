## One-line Summary
Narrative review of 28 studies (out of 62 screened) on BTX-A for gummy smile: reviews subtype classification, indications/contraindications, dose adjustment (2.0-5.0 IU per region, gender-adjusted), Yonsei point technique, complications, and duration (4-6 months); supports BTX-A as effective non-surgical alternative for muscular-etiology gummy smile.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Compiles indications/contraindications, dosing, and injection sites across 28 studies
- Summarizes Mazzuco-Hexsel subtype classification (anterior, posterior, asymmetric, mixed)
- Tabulates 9 clinical trials with dose, n, technique, outcome (Duruel, Cengiz, Suber, Mazzuco-Hexsel, Aldhaher-Bede, Shemais, Skaria, Gong, Polo)
- Highlights novel adjuncts: zinc supplementation before BTX-A (Shemais 2021) enhances efficacy
- Discusses 3 BTX-A formulations (onabotulinum, abobotulinum, incobotulinum) with different potencies
- Provides a clinical recommendation framework: corrective (muscular cause), adjuvant (combined with ortho/lip augmentation), palliative (presurgical bridge)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Databases: PubMed, Google Scholar
- Keywords: botulinum toxin, Botox, gummy smile (Boolean combinations)
- No time restriction
- Inclusion: any study discussing BTX-A effect on gummy smile treatment
- Exclusion: poor methodology, insufficient data
- Initial screening: 62 papers → 28 included
- Format: narrative synthesis (not systematic, no risk-of-bias tool, no meta-analysis)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Gummy smile prevalence: 10.5-29% population (14% females, 7% males)
- Definition: >2-3 mm gingival exposure during smile
- Mazzuco-Hexsel subtypes: anterior (>3 mm between canines, LLSAN), posterior (>3 mm distal to canines, zygomaticus), asymmetric (one side), mixed (both)
- Vertical maxillary excess degrees: 1 (2-4 mm gingival exposure, orthodontic/perio/prostho); 2 (4-8 mm); 3 (>8 mm)
- Onset of BTX-A effect: 1-2 weeks
- Duration of effect: 4-6 months
- Standard dose: 2.0-2.5 U per muscle region
- Polo (2008, n=30, 2.5 IU at Yonsei): 5.2 mm mean gingival reduction
- Skaria (2020, n=20, 2.5 IU): reduction from 4.93 → 3.71 mm
- Aldhaher & Bede (2022): 4-point technique (5 IU/2 points/side) better than 2-point technique (2.5 IU)
- Shemais (2021): zinc supplementation before BTX-A enhances clinical efficacy
- Cengiz (2020): relapse potential observed at month 4
- Razmaite: up to 5 mm reduction; best outcome at week 2, effect ~3 months
- Reported complications: inflammation, nerve palsy, pain, hematoma, infection, bruising, edema, transient muscle weakness; overdose may cause ptosis

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative review (not systematic) — no PRISMA, no quality appraisal
- Single author, no second reviewer for inclusion decisions
- No quantitative pooling
- Heterogeneous dose, injection sites, and follow-up across cited studies prevent direct comparison
- Re-injection antibody formation risk mentioned but not quantified

## 6. Related Work
- Polo (2005, 2008): foundational BTX-A for gummy smile
- Hwang (2009): Yonsei point surface anatomy
- Mazzuco & Hexsel (2010): subtype-based dose protocol
- Zengiski et al.: SR on BTX-A longevity
- Chagas et al.: SR with significant effect through 8 weeks
- Costa et al.: increasing injection points → better persistence
- Makkeiah: BTX-A vs lip repositioning surgery — BTX-A superior

## 7. Glossary
- BTX-A: botulinum toxin type A
- LLSAN: levator labii superioris alaeque nasi
- LLS: levator labii superioris
- ZM/Zmn: zygomaticus major/minor
- SNAP-25: synaptosome-associated protein, cleaved by BTX-A
- Yonsei point: confluence of LLSAN/LLS/zygomaticus minor; ~1 cm lateral to ala, 3 cm from oral commissure
- Onabotulinum/abobotulinum/incobotulinum toxin A: FDA-approved BTX-A formulations with different potencies
