## Why Ingested

This is the keystone paper of a 6-paper CR/CO/MICP batch establishing the foundational treatment-position framework — when to restore in centric occlusion (condyle-seated) versus the maximal intercuspal position. It reinforces and gives the evidence-grade backbone for [[occlusion/kwak-2024-masticatory-positioning-occlusal-stability-full-mouth]], which examines masticatory positioning and occlusal stability in full-mouth rehabilitation; this consensus statement supplies the upstream "which treatment position" decision that paper builds on.

## One-line Summary

Best Evidence Consensus Statement (J Prosthodont 2021; literature review screening >15,000 → 313 SR/RCT/MA/clinical-trial articles): centric occlusion (condyle-seated) and maximal intercuspal position are usually NON-coincident in dentate/partially dentate patients, and complete mouth rehabilitation should be restored in centric occlusion.

## 한줄요약

베스트 에비던스 합의문(J Prosthodont 2021, >15,000편 검색 → 313편 분석): 대부분의 유치악·부분유치악 환자에서 중심교합(centric occlusion, CO; 과두안정위)과 최대교두감합위(maximal intercuspal position, MIP)는 일치하지 않으며, 전악 재건은 중심교합(CO)에서 회복하는 것이 권장된다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Answers two focus questions via structured literature appraisal:
  1. How often does centric occlusion coincide with the maximal intercuspal position in dentate and partially dentate populations?
  2. Should centric occlusion or maximal intercuspal position be the treatment position for dentate/partially dentate patients undergoing complete mouth rehabilitation?
- Establishes the evidence-based recommendation that complete mouth rehabilitation be restored in **centric occlusion**.
- Frames the clinical significance of the CO–MIP discrepancy: it is the norm, not the exception, and is associated with occlusal instability and TMD.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **CO–MIP coincidence**: Most dentate and partially dentate patients do NOT have coincident centric occlusion and maximal intercuspal position.
- **Preferred rehabilitation position**: There is support for CO–MIP coincidence (i.e., restoring so the two positions coincide, anchored to centric occlusion) as the preferred occlusal relationship in complete mouth rehabilitations.
- **Safety**: The literature does NOT report conclusive evidence of adverse prosthodontic outcomes when complete rehabilitations are done in either centric occlusion OR maximal intercuspal position in a **healthy** population.
- **Discrepancy associations**: There IS support for an association between CO–MIP discrepancies and (a) occlusal instability and (b) temporomandibular joint disorders.
- **Conclusion**: Partially and completely dentate patients requiring complete mouth rehabilitation should be restored in **centric occlusion**.
- *abstract-only — full text not retrieved*: no quantitative effect sizes, prevalence percentages, or discrepancy magnitude (mm) are available beyond the >15,000 / 313 article counts.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- abstract-only — full text not retrieved; the strength-of-evidence rating per focus question and the consensus voting/method detail are not captured here.
- "No conclusive evidence of harm in either position" is qualified to **healthy** populations; the discrepancy–TMD/instability association suggests caution in symptomatic patients.
- The recommendation rests on association evidence (discrepancy ↔ instability/TMD) rather than on direct comparative outcome trials of CO- vs MIP-restored rehabilitations.

## 7. Glossary

- **Centric occlusion (CO)** — here, the condyle-seated occlusal contact position (equivalent to occlusion in centric relation).
- **Maximal intercuspal position (MIP / MICP)** — position of maximum cusp interdigitation, independent of condylar position.
- **Centric relation (CR)** — maxillomandibular relationship with condyles in their anterior-superior braced position in the glenoid fossae; the seated condylar reference underlying CO in this statement.
- **CO–MIP discrepancy** — the slide/difference between the condyle-seated contact and maximum interdigitation; usually present, associated with occlusal instability and TMD.
- **Complete mouth rehabilitation** — full-arch restorative reconstruction re-establishing occlusion across all teeth.
