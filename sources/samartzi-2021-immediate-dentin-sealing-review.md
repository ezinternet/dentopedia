## One-line Summary
Literature review of 88 articles on Immediate Dentin Sealing (IDS) covering protocol, materials, and clinical evidence; concludes IDS is beneficial for bond strength, gap formation, microleakage, and dentin hypersensitivity with no major contraindications.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Clinical, Cosmetic and Investigational Dentistry 2021;13:233–256
- **Institution**: National and Kapodistrian University of Athens
- **Search**: MEDLINE, Cochrane, Ovid, Scopus (Jan 1990 – Dec 2020)
- **Initial records**: 26,222 → 88 included after screening
- **Inclusion**: Peer-reviewed English articles on IDS in human permanent teeth (in vitro + clinical)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Most comprehensive consolidated review of IDS protocol steps and rationale
- Identifies IDS terminology synonyms (prehybridization, dual bonding, resin coating)
- Step-by-step clinical recommendation: when to apply DBA, which adhesive type, polymerization, glycerin gel oxygen-inhibition layer management, conditioning before final cementation
- Identifies unresolved areas: interaction with impression materials, provisional cementation, conditioning before final luting

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: Literature review (not formal SR)
- **Search strategy**: Keywords "immediate dentin sealing", "dual bonding technique", "resin coating technique", "prehybridization"
- **Two-reviewer screening** with consensus resolution
- **Inclusion**: peer-reviewed English on IDS in human permanent teeth (in vitro + clinical)
- **Exclusion**: non-English, duplicates, primary/bovine teeth, off-topic

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
**IDS demonstrated benefits over DDS (Delayed Dentin Sealing)**:
- ↑ bond strength (multiple in vitro studies)
- ↓ gap formation at restoration–dentin interface
- ↓ bacterial microleakage during provisional phase
- ↓ postoperative dentin hypersensitivity
- Maturation of hybrid layer prior to occlusal loading

**Protocol summary**:
1. Apply DBA immediately after tooth preparation, before impression
2. Light-cure adhesive thoroughly
3. Apply glycerin gel before final cure to remove oxygen inhibition layer (optional)
4. Air abrade or pumice clean before impression
5. At delivery: clean with pumice/air abrasion + silane → bond + cement

**Unresolved issues**: impression material interaction, provisional cement choice, optimal conditioning protocol before luting

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Not a formal SR — selection bias possible
- Mix of in vitro and clinical evidence; clinical RCT data limited
- Long-term clinical comparison vs DDS still lacking
- Protocol heterogeneity across studies

## 6. Related Work
- Companion to Hardan 2022 IDS SR+MA (quantitative bond strength synthesis)
- Adjacent to Magne / Belser biomimetic protocols
- Critiqued in Reis 2024 (BRD myths) — protocol 6: IDS shows similar post-op sensitivity and longevity to DDS in clinical data

## 7. Glossary
- **IDS (Immediate Dentin Sealing, 즉시상아질봉쇄)**: Applying dentin-bonding agent immediately after tooth preparation, before impression
- **DDS (Delayed Dentin Sealing)**: Conventional approach — hybridization performed at final cementation
- **DBA (Dentin Bonding Agent)**: Adhesive system for dentin
- **Hybrid layer**: Interdiffusion zone of resin + demineralized dentin collagen
- **Oxygen inhibition layer**: Unpolymerized surface of adhesive due to atmospheric O₂; removed with glycerin gel or mechanical cleaning
