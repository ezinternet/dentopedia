## Why Ingested

기존 위키에 개별 항생제 SRs(de-angelis-2025, mendez-millan-2024 등)는 존재하나, 치주치료에서의 항생제 전반을 메타분석의 방법론적 질과 증거 강도 측면에서 종합 평가한 상위 증거(umbrella review)가 없었음. 항생제 스튜어드십(antimicrobial stewardship) 관점에서 처방 근거를 재점검하는 데 핵심 레퍼런스가 됨. [[drug/sandulescu-2024-antibiotic-prescribing-dental-best-practices-review]] 및 [[drug/bazsefidpay-2023-antibiotic-restrictive-use-adherence-recommendation]]과 함께 치과 항생제 적정사용 클러스터를 구성함.

## One-line Summary

Umbrella review of 44 SRs (221 meta-analyses) on systemic and local antibiotics in periodontal therapy: despite some statistically robust estimates, overall evidence is weak due to critically low methodological quality of underlying SRs, supporting antibiotic stewardship over routine use.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(Systematic Review, SR) 44편·메타분석(Meta-analysis, MA) 221개를 포함한 우산형 고찰(Umbrella Review): 전신·국소 항생제 모두 통계적으로 유의한 임상적 부착 수준(Clinical Attachment Level, CAL) 개선을 보였으나 기반 SR의 방법론적 질이 대부분 낮아 전체 근거등급은 "약함"으로 평가되었고, 항생제 스튜어드십(Antimicrobial Stewardship)을 지지함.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Umbrella review (systematic review of systematic reviews)
- **Journal**: Frontiers in Cellular and Infection Microbiology
- **Year**: 2025
- **PROSPERO**: CRD42024527222
- **Search period**: Up to March 2024
- **Databases**: PubMed/MEDLINE, Web of Science, EMBASE, CENTRAL (Cochrane), LILACS
- **Included SRs**: 44 systematic reviews with meta-analysis
- **Total meta-analytic comparisons**: 221

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First umbrella review synthesizing the methodological quality AND meta-analytical strength of all existing SRs on antibiotics in periodontal therapy, applying a combined AMSTAR-2 + evidence grading framework.
2. Demonstrates that despite numerically suggestive-to-strong meta-analytic estimates for systemic antibiotics, the methodological quality of the underlying SRs is predominantly critically low (65.6%), rendering the overall grading "weak."
3. Calculates Rosenberg's Fail-Safe Number (FSN) for 215 meta-analyses, showing 65.5% of suggestive-to-strong estimates are unlikely to change with additional future studies — yet the overall evidence remains weak due to SR quality.
4. Issues a strong antibiotic stewardship recommendation: antibiotics should NOT be routinely used in periodontal therapy; reserve for severe/aggressive periodontitis cases only.
5. Highlights that meta-analyses pooling multiple antibiotic types mask individual agent efficacy, calling for antibiotic-specific longitudinal RCTs.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Design**: Umbrella review (SR of SRs with MA)

**Eligibility**: SRs with MA assessing systemic or local antibiotics as adjuncts in periodontal therapy (surgical or non-surgical), human studies only. Excluded: SRs without pooled estimates, without controls, without heterogeneity statistics, or that were themselves umbrella reviews.

**Quality appraisal**: AMSTAR-2 tool — four tiers: High, Moderate, Low, Critically Low.

**Evidence grading** (meta-analytical strength, 4 tiers):
- **Strong**: >1000 cases, p ≤10⁻⁶, I² <50%, prediction interval excluding null, no small-study effects
- **Highly suggestive**: >1000 cases, p ≤10⁻⁶ (random effects), largest study significant
- **Suggestive**: >1000 cases, p ≤10⁻⁴
- **Weak**: all others

**Overall Grading**: Combines AMSTAR-2 tier with meta-analytical strength tier (both must be high/moderate for strong/highly suggestive overall grade).

**FSN calculation**: Rosenberg's FSN via Meta-Essentials package; median and range per evidence tier.

**Inter-examiner reliability**: AMSTAR-2 kappa 0.88 (95% CI: 0.84–0.92); full-text screening kappa 0.94 (95% CI: 0.92–0.96).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Meta-analytic evidence grading

| Evidence tier | Meta-analyses (n) | Key finding |
|---|---|---|
| Strong | 4 | Systemic antibiotics (combined types): CAL +0.3 mm, CAL deep pockets +0.4 mm, BoP -6.6%, pocket closure -14.5% |
| Highly suggestive | 15 | Systemic (combined types) for PPD/CAL; local antibiotics (combined types) for PPD/CAL |
| Suggestive | 10 | Systemic antibiotics (combined types) for PPD, CAL, BoP |
| Weak | 192 | The vast majority |

All strong/highly-suggestive/suggestive estimates came from SRs of **low** methodological quality → **overall grading: weak for all**.

### Methodological quality (AMSTAR-2)

| Quality tier | n | % |
|---|---|---|
| High | 4 | 6.6% |
| Moderate | 2 | 3.3% |
| Low | 15 | 24.6% |
| Critically low | 40 | 65.6% |

### Major AMSTAR-2 failures
- Funding source disclosure: 93.4% failed
- Study design selection explanation: 82.0% failed
- Excluded studies list with justification: 59.0% failed
- Accounting for RoB when interpreting results: 57.4% failed

### FSN statistics (suggestive-to-strong meta-analyses, n=29)
- Overall median FSN: 35 (range 0–175)
- Strong tier median FSN: 20 (range 1–126)
- Highly suggestive median FSN: 16 (range 0–175)
- Suggestive median FSN: 57 (range 35–160)
- 65.5% of these MA had FSN > number of included studies (unlikely to change)

### SR scope distribution
- Systemic antibiotics only: 72.1% (n=44)
- Local antibiotics only: 18.0% (n=11)
- Both systemic + local: 9.8% (n=6)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Umbrella reviews can only be as strong as the underlying SRs; the predominance of critically low-quality SRs fundamentally limits conclusions.
- Meta-analyses pooling diverse antibiotic types obscure agent-specific efficacy (metronidazole vs. doxycycline vs. azithromycin etc. cannot be separately assessed from this level).
- Outcome measures predominantly use average continuous values (PPD, CAL mean change) rather than threshold-based clinically meaningful metrics (% sites achieving ≥2 mm PPD reduction).
- Local vs. systemic head-to-head comparisons are sparse and underpowered.
- Future research needs: antibiotic-specific longitudinal RCTs with standardized threshold outcomes; mechanistic studies on local antibiotic biological rationale; studies in aggressive/severe periodontitis subgroups.

## 7. Glossary

- **Umbrella review**: A systematic review of existing systematic reviews and meta-analyses, providing the highest-level synthesis.
- **AMSTAR-2**: A MeaSurement Tool to Assess systematic Reviews — 16-item instrument for evaluating SR methodological quality; 7 items are "critical domains."
- **Fail-Safe Number (FSN)**: The number of additional null-effect studies needed to render a currently significant meta-analytic result non-significant; higher FSN = more robust finding.
- **CAL (Clinical Attachment Level, 임상적 부착 수준)**: Distance from the CEJ to the base of the sulcus/pocket; the primary periodontal outcome measure.
- **PPD (Probing Pocket Depth, 탐침 깊이)**: Distance from gingival margin to pocket base.
- **BoP (Bleeding on Probing, 탐침 시 출혈)**: Indicator of active gingival inflammation.
- **Antibiotic stewardship (항생제 스튜어드십)**: Coordinated programs to promote appropriate antibiotic use, minimizing resistance and adverse effects.
- **Eubiosis (공생 미생물 균형)**: A balanced, health-compatible microbial community; opposite of dysbiosis.
- **I² (이질성)**: Percentage of variability in a meta-analysis attributable to heterogeneity rather than chance; <50% considered acceptable.
