## Why Ingested

[[wiki/behavioral-dentistry/motivational-interviewing/kay-2016-motivational-interviewing-general-dental-practice]]가 "MI가 효과 있으니 팀이 훈련하라"고 권고하지만, 정작 졸업 후 위생사가 임상에서 MI를 실제로 쓰는지·효과를 어떻게 인식하는지의 implementation gap은 미해결. 본 cross-sectional(UMN 2010–2019 졸업생)은 위생사 교육 ROI와 MI 정착 결정요인(Theory of Planned Behavior)을 다뤄 위생사 교육 설계 근거로 직접 활용.

## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional survey of University of Minnesota dental-hygiene graduates 2010–2019 (n=58 analyzed, 28%) — 95% use MI and 98% perceive it effective; Theory-of-Planned-Behavior constructs (attitudes, subjective norms, perceived behavioral control) predicted MI use, though clinicians doubted patients' capacity to change.

## 한줄요약

미네소타대 치위생 졸업생 단면조사(분석 n=58, 28%) — 95%가 MI 사용, 98%가 효과적이라 인식. 계획행동이론(TPB) 3요소(태도·주관적 규범·지각된 행동통제)가 MI 사용을 예측. 단, 위생사들은 "환자가 정말 바뀔 수 있나"에 회의를 표함.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Journal of Dental Education 2022;86(7):909–917
- DOI: 10.1002/jdd.12919
- Study type: Cross-sectional electronic survey (Qualtrics), framed by Theory of Planned Behavior (TPB)
- Population: UMN DH graduates classes 2010–2019, invited n=208; 73 responses (35%), 58 analyzed (28%)
- Analysis: descriptive stats, cross-tabulations, one-way ANOVA

## 2. Key Contributions
- Moves MI evidence from "does it work" (efficacy SRs) to "do trained clinicians actually use it" (implementation/uptake).
- Applies TPB to explain MI use: attitudes, subjective norms, and perceived behavioral control all predicted clinical MI use.
- Documents a key barrier: clinician skepticism about patients' ability to change, despite high self-rated MI confidence.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Survey instrument mapped to the three TPB constructs plus demographics and current MI use.
- Single-program sample (UMN) with strong MI curriculum — high internal validity, limited generalizability.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- 95% reported using MI; 98% perceived MI as an effective behavior-counseling method.
- Respondents felt confident in MI skills and supported to use MI in their work environments.
- Significant association between age and perceived behavioral control (p=0.02).
- All three TPB constructs were predictors of MI use in the clinical environment.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Low response/analysis rate (28%) → non-response and self-report bias; self-reported use ≠ observed fidelity.
- Single institution with an unusually MI-intensive curriculum; not representative of hygienists trained elsewhere.
- No patient-outcome linkage — measures clinician behavior/perception, not patient change.

## 7. Glossary
- **Theory of Planned Behavior (TPB)**: behavior is predicted by intention, itself shaped by attitudes, subjective norms, and perceived behavioral control.
- **Perceived behavioral control**: belief in one's ability to perform the behavior (here, to deliver MI).
- **DH**: Dental Hygiene / dental hygienist.
