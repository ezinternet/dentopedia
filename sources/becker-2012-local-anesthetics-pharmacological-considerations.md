## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Mechanistic foundation**: Na⁺-channel state-dependent block; small rapid-firing fibers most sensitive (autonomic > sensory > motor; pain > pressure/proprioception).
2. **pKa → onset**: Henderson-Hasselbalch logic; all dental LAs pKa > 7.4 so most molecules are cationic/water-soluble at injection; inflamed tissue (low pH) worsens onset; bupivacaine pKa 8.1 = slowest.
3. **Protein binding → duration**: bupivacaine 95% vs mepivacaine 55% → longer block.
4. **Lipid solubility → potency**: bupivacaine more lipid-soluble/potent (0.5% formulation) than articaine (4%).
5. **Esters vs amides**: amides hepatic; esters hydrolyzed by plasma esterases; **articaine** = amide but its ester side chain gives plasma-esterase clearance (t½ 20–40 min vs >90 min for amides).
6. **Maximum-dose table** (mg/kg and absolute mg) — the most clinically reused content.
7. **Toxicity map**: CNS seizures threshold, bupivacaine cardiotoxicity, prilocaine methemoglobinemia, 4% concentration neurotoxicity/paresthesia, allergy demystification.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Narrative review (not systematic). Synthesizes classic pharmacokinetic studies (Scott et al. 1972; Hersh et al.) and paresthesia epidemiology (Haas & Lennon; Garisto et al.; Hillerup et al.). Dosage-calculation simplification: treat each cartridge as 2 mL (overestimates dose → safe).

**Dosage shortcut (Table 1)**: % concentration → mg/mL by moving decimal one space right (2% = 20 mg/mL; 4% = 40 mg/mL; 3% = 30 mg/mL; 0.5% = 5 mg/mL). Epinephrine: 1:100,000 = 10 µg/mL; 1:50,000 = 20 µg/mL; 1:200,000 = 5 µg/mL.

|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2% lidocaine (plain) | 4.5 (300) | 96 | ~5 min / — | ~2 h |
| 2% lidocaine + 1:100,000 or 1:50,000 epi | 7 (500) | 55–65 | 80–90 min | 3–5 h |
| 3% mepivacaine (plain) | 6.6 (400) | 114 | 20–30 min / 45–65 min | 2–3 h |
| 2% mepivacaine + 1:20,000 levonordefrin | 7 (550) | — | 40–60 min / 60–90 min | 3–5 h |
| 4% prilocaine (plain) | 6 (400)* | 96 | 10–15 min / 45–65 min | 3–4 h |
| 4% prilocaine + 1:200,000 epi | 6 (400)* | — | 35–45 min / 50–70 min | 3–6 h |
| 4% articaine + 1:200,000 or 1:100,000 epi | 5–7 (500)† | 20 | 60–75 min / 90–120 min | 3–5 h |
| 0.5% bupivacaine + 1:200,000 epi | (90)‡ | 210 | 30–45 min / 4–7 h | 8–12 h |

\* Prilocaine conservative; some references allow 8 mg/kg / 600 mg total.
† Articaine 7 mg/kg in US package insert; Canadian insert suggests 5 mg/kg for children; 500 mg total inferred at 70 kg.
‡ Bupivacaine not FDA-approved for children <16; total ~90 mg.

### Other quantitative anchors [합의수준]

- **Lidocaine toxicity**: signs may start at serum >5 µg/mL; convulsive seizures generally >10 µg/mL. 400 mg lidocaine submucosal stays well below toxic serum levels (~10-cartridge historical limit).
- **Peak serum**: 20–30 min post-injection (alone); delayed/reduced by adding 1:200,000 epinephrine.
- **Paresthesia risk** (Garisto, US 1997–2008, 248 cases; ~95% mandibular blocks, 89% lingual nerve): **7.3× with 4% articaine, 3.6× with 4% prilocaine** vs other LAs. Hillerup: 4% > 2% articaine neurotoxicity in sciatic-nerve prep.
- **Methemoglobinemia (prilocaine metabolite o-toluidine)**: symptomatic >15%, life-threatening 50–60%; treat with IV methylene blue; hereditary methemoglobinemia contraindicates prilocaine.
- **Combination LAs are additive** — if half the lidocaine max is given, halve the bupivacaine max.
- **Epinephrine cardiovascular** (Dionne; Hersh): 3 cartridges 2% lidocaine + 1:100,000 (~60 µg epi) ↑ cardiac output/HR/stroke volume, ↓ systemic resistance, MAP ~unchanged. >1:200,000 gives no onset/duration advantage for IANB; higher concentrations (1:50,000) only improve infiltration hemostasis.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative (non-systematic) CE review → no pooled estimates; relies on selected landmark studies.
- 2012 vintage; package-insert max doses and articaine pediatric guidance vary by jurisdiction (US 7 mg/kg vs Canadian 5 mg/kg children).
- Allergy literature heterogeneity acknowledged; true IgE confirmation rare.
- Companion "technical considerations" article promised separately (not in this paper).

## 7. Glossary

- **pKa** — acid-dissociation constant; lower pKa → more lipid-soluble base at pH 7.4 → faster onset.
- **Protein binding** — % drug bound; higher → longer duration (bupivacaine 95%, mepivacaine 55%).
- **Lipid solubility** — drives potency (and confounds onset clinically).
- **Amide vs ester** — metabolism class (hepatic vs plasma-esterase); articaine = amide with ester side chain (plasma clearance, t½ 20–40 min).
- **Methemoglobinemia** — prilocaine o-toluidine oxidizes Fe²⁺→Fe³⁺ in hemoglobin.
- **Levonordefrin** — vasopressor (1:20,000) alternative to epinephrine.
- **t½β** — elimination half-life (min).
