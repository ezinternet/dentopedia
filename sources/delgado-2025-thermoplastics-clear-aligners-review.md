## Why Ingested

The clear-aligner category holds clinical-outcome reviews (predictability, root resorption, biomechanics) but lacked a dedicated **material-science** anchor explaining *why* aligners behave as they do — the polymer chemistry (PETG vs hTPU vs multilayer vs 3D resin/SMP) underlying force delivery, comfort, staining, and biocompatibility. This review supplies that substrate. It directly extends [[orthodontics/clear-aligner/nakornnoi-2024-aligner-trimline-biomechanics-tooth-movement-sr]] (trimline geometry governs force, but material properties — yield point, stress relaxation, elastic range — set the force the trimline modulates) and contextualizes [[orthodontics/clear-aligner/nucera-2022-composite-attachments-clear-aligners-sr]] (PETG wears bonded attachments far more than TPU; material choice drives attachment efficacy/wear).

## One-line Summary

Narrative material-science review of clear-aligner thermoplastics (PETG/PET, hTPU, EVA, PP, PC, multilayers, blends) plus emerging shape-memory polymers (SMP) and direct-3D-print resins, mapping optical, mechanical, stress-relaxation, thermal, and biocompatibility properties to inform material selection.

## 한줄요약

투명교정 열가소성수지(PETG·hTPU·다층·블렌드)와 차세대 형상기억고분자(SMP)·직접 3D프린팅 레진의 광학·기계·응력완화·열·생체적합 특성을 재료선택 관점에서 정리한 서술적 종설 — hTPU는 탄성영역이 넓어 편안하고, PETG는 힘이 커 치근이동에 유리하며, 다층은 힘이 가장 부드럽고, 3D레진은 힘 유지가 거의 안 된다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Narrative review (descriptive overview of material science; no PRISMA protocol, no registered systematic-review search reported).
- **Journal**: Polymers, 2025, 17(12):1681; published 2025-06-17.
- **DOI**: 10.3390/polym17121681 | PMID 40574208 | PMCID PMC12196624.
- **Scope**: Properties of CAT thermoplastics and their effect on treatment effectiveness, comfort, biocompatibility, and environment; emerging SMP and direct-3D-print materials.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Material taxonomy for CAT.** PET/PETG = most common polyester; hTPU = second (used alone; mTPU/sTPU in multilayers); EVA/PP relegated to mouthguards; PC abandoned (hydrolysis, price, BPA reputation); multilayers (post-Invisalign patent expiry) and blends (PC as PETG/TPU compatibilizer) for enhanced performance.
2. **Force-delivery framework.** Aligners act in the elastic range near the yield point. hTPU has a broader "security range" (more uniform, lower, more comfortable force) than PETG. Three crucial parameters: yield stress, yield strain, elastic modulus. ~550–650 MPa elastic modulus suffices for most movements, but pain/force thresholds are patient-dependent.
3. **Multilayer force gentleness quantified.** Multilayer force is 2–3× lower than PETG and 1.5–2× lower than hTPU in models; 3D resins enact almost no force.
4. **PETG vs TPU clinical contrast.** PETG → larger root movements + higher attachment wear; TPU → mainly crown movement, less attachment wear.
5. **Emerging-material reality check.** 3D resins have similar elastic modulus but extremely poor stress-relaxation retention (TC-85: 10% vs PETG 80%) and Tg ≈ 37°C → low efficiency; SMP shape memory degrades after limited cycles.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Descriptive narrative synthesis organized by property domain: historical overview → thermoplastic types → general requirements → optical → aging/chemical resistance → mechanical (incl. force/strain measurement methods: OMSS, DIC, force-film, capacitive/piezoelectric sensors; stress relaxation & creep) → thermal (Tg, DSC, DMTA) → biocompatibility (in vitro cytotoxicity, bisphenol/estrogenic leaching, biofilm; in vivo irritation) → environment/recyclability → perspectives. Not a quantitative meta-analysis; no pooled effect estimates.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Optical**: PETG, TPU, PC, multilayers similarly transparent when naive; thermoforming ↑ roughness ↓ transparency; transmittance drops after 7 days; TPU most stain-susceptible (water sorption + porosity); coffee most damaging; Invisalign's soft-outer-TPU/polyester-core multilayer is especially stain-prone.
- **Aging**: higher water sorption → ↓ mechanical properties; PC hydrolyzes; TPU is among the most hygroscopic; blends/stacking ↓ water uptake; worn aligner modulus ↓ by 7 days, stable at 14 days.
- **Mechanical hardness**: PETG/PC/hTPU monolayers similar; multilayers softer; PP lowest. Water sorption dominates hardness change over cold-working + biofilm.
- **Activation**: standard 0.25 mm; higher activation only yields transient over-recommended forces that decay to the same threshold.
- **Thermal Tg**: PETG 75–80°C, TPU 80–95°C; approved direct-print resin Tg ≈ 37°C (cause of low efficiency).
- **Biocompatibility**: most materials low/null cytotoxicity; BPA mostly undetectable except higher in Invisalign TPU; all BPA-free polyesters (incl. PCTG) showed detectable in-vitro estrogenic activity, PETG did not; Invisalign isocyanate-TPU can cause histologic reaction; no biofilm difference between materials.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

Author-summarized gaps: no clinical evidence for claimed TPU/EVA comfort superiority; no post-usage studies of non-Invisalign thermoplastics; no aligner thermal data after usage; no LDH force–soft-tissue-damage study on aligners; no full life-cycle assessment (LCA) of CAT; unknown cytotoxicity/efficiency of 3D resins and SMPs in use; no guidelines for prescription duration (10/7 vs 14 days) or rigid-vs-soft staging. Narrative (non-systematic) design — no risk-of-bias appraisal or pooled estimates.

## 7. Glossary

- **CAT** — Clear Aligner Treatment.
- **PETG** — amorphous polyethylene terephthalate copolymerized with ethylene glycol; most common CAT polyester.
- **hTPU / mTPU / sTPU** — high/middle/soft-hardness thermoplastic polyurethane.
- **PCTG** — a copolyester (BPA-free); showed detectable in-vitro estrogenic activity.
- **SMP** — shape memory polymer (stable + temporary shape recoverable by heat/humidity).
- **OMSS** — orthodontic measurement and simulation system (3D force/torque on a movable-tooth model).
- **DIC** — digital image correlation (camera-based strain measurement).
- **DMTA / DSC** — dynamic mechanical thermal analysis / differential scanning calorimetry (Tg, storage/loss modulus).
- **Tg** — glass transition temperature (chain-mobility threshold; governs permanent deformation).
- **Stress relaxation** — viscoelastic force drop at fixed strain; the key CAT design property.
- **Activation** — designed aligner–dentition misfit in mm (standard 0.25 mm).
- **LDH** — lactate dehydrogenase; salivary marker of soft-tissue cell damage.
- **BPA / BPF / BPS** — bisphenol A / F / S; potential endocrine-disruptor leachates.
