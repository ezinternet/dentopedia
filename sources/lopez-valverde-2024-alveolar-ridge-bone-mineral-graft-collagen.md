## One-line Summary
Bioengineering 2024;11:565: SR+MA (12 studies); bone mineral graft + collagen membrane vs spontaneous healing — trend for preservation but no statistical significance (p>0.05); high heterogeneity I²>75%; more RCTs needed.

## 1. Key Results
- PubMed/Medline, WoS, Embase, Cochrane; up to January 2024
- 561 results → 12 studies included
- Intervention: bone mineral graft + resorbable collagen membrane vs spontaneous healing
- Meta-analysis: high heterogeneity (I²>75%) → random-effects model
- Result: trend for better alveolar ridge preservation but p>0.05 (no statistical significance)
- Risk of bias: Cochrane RoB Tool; quality: JBI
- University of Salamanca; University of Coimbra

## 2. Clinical Implications
- Bone mineral graft + collagen membrane: biologically plausible, widely used, but statistical evidence inconclusive
- High heterogeneity limits conclusions — material type, defect size, follow-up vary widely
- Low immunogenicity + no second surgery = practical advantages of collagen membranes
- More homogeneous RCTs needed
