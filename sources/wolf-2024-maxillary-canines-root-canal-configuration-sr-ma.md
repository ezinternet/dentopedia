## Why Ingested

치아형태학 감시 인제스트 — 상악 견치 근관형태(Vertucci) SR+MA. 위키의 근관형태 데이터가 대구치(MB2, C-shaped) 중심으로 편중되어 전치부(single-rooted) Vertucci 분포 근거가 빈약했는데, 본 22-study SR+MA가 상악 견치의 Type I 우세(75.4–100%)와 ~25% 변이라는 정량 기준을 보강한다. 기존 근관형태 페이지 [[endodontics/anatomy/song-2008-cross-sectional-morphology-and-minimum-canal]]의 근관 단면형태 데이터와 짝을 이루는 상악 전치부 anchor.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 22 studies on maxillary canine (MxC) root canal configuration: Vertucci Type I (1-1-1/1) dominates at 75.4–100%, but anatomical variation reaches up to ~25% of cases; CBCT is the most-used method, and males show significantly more Vertucci III and V configurations.

## 한줄요약

상악 견치 근관형태 22편 SR+MA — Vertucci Type I(단일 근관) 75.4–100% 우세이나 최대 약 25%에서 변이; CBCT가 최다 연구법, 남성에서 Vertucci III·V 유의하게 많음.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA dedicated to the internal morphology / root canal configuration (RCC) of **maxillary canines (MxC)** — a tooth type usually treated as a simple single-canal anchor but here quantified.
- Pooled RCC frequency distribution across Vertucci (Ve), Weine, and Briseño Marroquín classifications:
  - **1-1-1/1 (Ve I)**: 75.4–100% (dominant)
  - **2-2-1/1 (Ve II)**: 0.1–20%
  - **1-2-1/1 (Ve III)**: 0.1–11.6%
  - **2-2-2/2 (Ve IV)**: 0.1–0.4%
  - **1-1-2/2 (Ve V)**: 0.1–2.4%
  - **2-1-2/2 (Ve VI)**: 0.5–1.2%
  - **1-2-1/2 (Ve VII)**: 0.1–0.2%
- Quantifies the clinical bottom line: despite Type I dominance, **up to ~25% of MxC carry anatomical variation** (connecting/accessory canals not mechanically preparable → chemical irrigation emphasis).
- Sex-difference meta-analysis (6 studies, Europe vs Asia).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Five databases: MEDLINE/PubMed, Embase, Cochrane, LILACS, Scopus + grey literature, up to June 2024.
- Screening funnel: 200 identified → 73 duplicates removed → 127 screened → 113 removed by title/abstract → 14 full-text (4 excluded) → 12 added by hand search → **22 included**.
- Inclusion: RCTs, cross-sectional, comparative, validation, evaluation studies of MxC internal morphology/RCC. Excluded: case reports, reviews.
- RCC classified by Vertucci (1984), Weine et al., and Briseño Marroquín et al. (2015).
- Risk of bias: **AQUA** tool (Anatomical QUality Assessment); quality: NHLBI customized tool. Two independent reviewers (T.R., A.L.W.), third reviewer (T.G.W.) for consensus.
- Meta-analysis: RevMan 5.4, odds ratio effect size, I² heterogeneity (I²<35% fixed-effects, >35% random-effects), 95% CI, p≤0.05.
- Research-method mix in included studies: **CBCT most used**; radiographic, staining-and-clearing, and micro-CT less frequent.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Type I (1-1-1/1) dominance**: 75.4–100%; lowest reported by Weng et al. (75.4%) and Somalinga Amardeep et al. (81.6%).
- Second most common across half of the 22 studies: **2-2-1/1 (Ve II)**, 0.1–20%.
- Nearly all studies report **1-2-1/1 (Ve III)**, 0.1–11.6% (note: Weine classification lacks this type).
- Sex differences (meta-analysis, OR [95% CI], males as reference direction):
  - 2-2-1/1 (Ve II): OR 1.34 [0.53, 3.41] (NS)
  - **1-2-1/1 (Ve III): OR 2.07 [1.01, 4.26]** — significantly more in males
  - **1-1-2/2 (Ve V): OR 2.93 [1.07, 8.07]** — significantly more in males
  - 2-2-2/2 (Ve IV): OR 0.08 [0.00, 4.00] — directionally more in females (CI wide)
  - **No sex difference** in 1-1-1/1 (Ve I) and 1-2-1/2 (Ve VII)
- Type I most observed in both sexes: up to 99.5% men, up to 100% women.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Methodological heterogeneity and CBCT/micro-CT artifacts; subjective RCC evaluation criteria.
- Age not incorporated into analysis despite being available in some CBCT studies — secondary dentin from aging/caries/wear narrows canals but has minimal effect on main-canal morphology.
- Some studies lacked sex and age data.
- No age-stratified MxC analysis exists (molar data suggest complex-RCC frequency may decrease with age) — future direction.

## 7. Glossary

- **RCC** — Root Canal Configuration; classification of canal number/pattern from orifice to apex.
- **Vertucci Type I (1-1-1/1)** — single canal running pulp chamber to apex (one orifice, one apical foramen).
- **MxC** — Maxillary Canine.
- **AQUA tool** — Anatomical QUality Assessment tool for risk-of-bias in anatomical SR/MA.
- **CBCT** — Cone Beam Computed Tomography (in vivo 3D imaging).
- **micro-CT** — micro-computed tomography; ex vivo gold standard, too high radiation for in vivo human use.
- **Briseño Marroquín classification** — modern RCC system capturing intra-canal detail beyond Vertucci/Weine.
