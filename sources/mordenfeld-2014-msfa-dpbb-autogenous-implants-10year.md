## One-line Summary
10-year prospective clinical/radiographic follow-up of 108 turned-surface implants in MSFA sites grafted with 80% DPBB + 20% autogenous bone: cumulative survival 86%, mean MBL 1.6±1.0 mm; first 2 years are critical; thereafter excellent stability irrespective of smoking or grafted-vs-residual placement site.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Clinical Implant Dentistry and Related Research 2014;16(3):435–446 (online 15 Oct 2012; print issue 2014).
- **Study type**: Prospective long-term cohort (10-year follow-up of an earlier 3-year report — Hallman 2005).
- **Setting**: Swedish multi-center; Public Dental Health (Gävle, Uppsala).

## 2. Key Contributions
- Provides the longest published clinical follow-up (10 years) of MSFA with the 80:20 DPBB:autogenous mixture.
- Identifies the **first 2 years post-loading as the critical window** for turned-surface implants in MSFA sites — most failures occur early.
- Confirms **long-term stability** of marginal bone level, ISQ, and pocket depth — equal between grafted and residual sites and between smokers and non-smokers at 10 years.
- Demonstrates that graft height reduction occurs between 3 months and 2 years and then stabilizes (no further significant change to 10 years).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 20 patients with severe posterior maxillary atrophy underwent two-stage MSFA with 80% DPBB (Bio-Oss) + 20% autogenous bone (intraoral).
- 22 sinuses augmented (6 unilateral + 8 bilateral); 53 implants placed in grafted bone + 15 in non-grafted (residual) sites; turned-surface implants.
- 14 of 20 patients followed throughout 10 years.
- Outcomes: cumulative survival rate (CSR), marginal bone level (MBL), pocket depth, ISQ (RFA), graft height change.
- Subgroups: grafted vs residual bone, smokers vs non-smokers.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Cumulative survival**: 86% at 10 years (15 of 108 implants lost).
- **Mean MBL at 10 yr**: 1.6 ± 1.0 mm.
- **No significant difference** in MBL, pocket depth, or ISQ between:
  - Grafted vs residual bone placement
  - Smokers vs non-smokers
- **Graft height**: significant reduction between 3 months and 2 years; no further significant change between 2 and 10 years.
- **Critical window**: first 2 years post-placement (most losses); after that, excellent outcomes.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Single graft mixture (80:20 DPBB:autogenous); no comparator arm.
- Turned-surface implants — modern moderately rough surfaces likely perform better; results may underestimate current outcomes.
- 6 of 20 patients lost to follow-up (30% attrition over 10 years).
- Subgroup analyses (smoking) underpowered.
- Same surgeon/center; transferability to other settings unclear.

## 7. Glossary
- **MSFA (Maxillary Sinus Floor Augmentation)**: Surgical elevation of sinus floor membrane and grafting to enable implant placement in atrophic posterior maxilla.
- **DPBB (Deproteinized Bovine Bone) / DBBM**: Anorganic bovine bone xenograft; Bio-Oss is prototypical.
- **80:20 mixture**: 80% DPBB + 20% autogenous bone — combines osteoconductive scaffold with osteogenic/osteoinductive component.
- **Turned-surface implants**: Machined (smooth) surface implants — predecessor to moderately rough (SLA, TiUnite) surfaces.
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: Resonance frequency analysis-derived stability index (1–100).
