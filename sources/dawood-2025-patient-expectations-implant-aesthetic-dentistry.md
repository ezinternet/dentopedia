## One-line Summary

Expert narrative/opinion (BDJ) on managing patient expectations in implant and aesthetic dentistry — social media inflates expectations away from biological reality; transparent communication, informed consent and empathetic care align expectations and reduce dissatisfaction, while complex/repeat-failure cases threaten both patient and clinician wellbeing.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: British Dental Journal 2025
- DOI: 10.1038/s41415-025-8725-0
- Author: Andrew Dawood (implant/prosthodontic practice, 4 decades)
- Study type: Expert narrative/opinion article (not empirical)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Frames expectation management as a core clinical competency, not soft skill, in implant/aesthetic dentistry.
- Connects digital-media/celebrity culture to a measurable gap between perceived and biologically achievable outcomes.
- Extends the duty-of-care frame to clinician wellbeing and team cohesion when facing unrealistic expectations and prior failures.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Reflective expert essay drawing on long clinical experience; organized around psychological concepts (e.g., cognitive dissonance from unmet expectations), consent, and communication.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks (key points)
- Social media/digital culture heighten expectations, disconnecting patients from treatment realities.
- Clear communication + informed consent are essential to align expectations and reduce dissatisfaction.
- Patients often misunderstand the complexity/limitations of implant treatment → clinicians must clarify and correct false assumptions.
- Unrealistic expectations and past failures stress both patients and clinicians → emotional resilience and tailored care needed.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Single-author opinion; no data, no comparative evidence.
- Practice-context specific (referral-heavy complex implant practice) → may not generalize to routine GDP caseloads.

## 7. Glossary
- **Expectation management**: aligning a patient's anticipated outcome with the biologically/technically achievable result.
- **Cognitive dissonance**: psychological discomfort when outcome conflicts with expectation.
- **Informed consent**: process ensuring the patient understands options, risks, and realistic outcomes.
