## One-line Summary
SEM in vitro study (3 groups, n=20) comparing NovaMin (calcium sodium phosphosilicate) and Gluma against control for dentinal tubule occlusion after a single application.

## 한줄요약
SEM in vitro(3군·각 20): 단회 도포 후 NovaMin(칼슘 소듐 포스포실리케이트)과 Gluma의 상아세관 폐쇄율을 대조군과 비교한 기초 연구.

## 1. Document Information
- J Periodontal Implant Sci 2013;43(6):269. Korean Academy of Periodontology, Open Access.

## 2. Key Contributions
- SEM quantification of completely/partially occluded vs open tubules per agent.
- Compares a powder-based bioactive glass (NovaMin) with a liquid HEMA/glutaraldehyde agent (Gluma).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Extracted sound human premolars; dentin discs etched with 6% citric acid 2 min.
- 3 groups (n=20): control, Gluma, NovaMin; 2 extra longitudinally fractured per test agent.
- SEM analysis of occlusion proportions; non-parametric statistics.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Both agents increased tubule occlusion vs control; ratios of completely/partially occluded tubules computed per group (NovaMin forms a mineral-like surface layer; Gluma precipitates within tubules).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Single application, no thermocycling/acid challenge, no permeability/clinical correlation.

## 6. Related Work
- Mechanistic basis for bioactive-glass toothpaste reviews and Gluma clinical trials.

## 7. Glossary
- NovaMin: calcium sodium phosphosilicate bioactive glass.
- Gluma: HEMA + glutaraldehyde desensitizer.
