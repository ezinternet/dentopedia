## 2. Key Contributions

- Distills the heterogeneous dental-school-clinic satisfaction literature into **five recurring measurement dimensions**: **quality, interaction, access, environment, cost**.
- Frames patient satisfaction as a driver of treatment cooperation and, downstream, long-term oral health — making it an operational concern for clinic administrators, not just a courtesy metric.
- Provides a screening funnel (730 → 41 → 9 articles) that documents how sparse the rigorously appraised evidence base is in this niche.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Source**: PubMed only.
- **Window**: 1980 through March 2014.
- **Selection**: multi-stage screening — 730 records identified, 41 selected, then critical appraisal narrowed to **9 retained articles**.
- **Synthesis**: identified satisfaction dimensions were categorized across the retained studies (qualitative/narrative comparison; no meta-analysis).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Five dimensions identified**:
  1. **Quality** — perceived clinical/technical quality of care.
  2. **Interaction** — patient–provider communication and relationship.
  3. **Access** — availability, waiting, ease of obtaining care.
  4. **Environment** — physical setting / clinic facilities.
  5. **Cost** — financial aspects of care.
- These dimensions are offered as the constructs dental institutions should measure to improve care quality.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single database (PubMed) and a window ending March 2014; later instruments not captured.
- Narrative comparison, not a quantitative synthesis — no pooled estimates or reliability/validity meta-data.
- Only nine appraised articles, limiting generalizability across dental-education systems.

## 7. Glossary

- **Patient satisfaction**: a patient's subjective evaluation of care relative to expectations.
- **Dimension (of satisfaction)**: a distinct measurable construct (e.g., quality, access) aggregated into overall satisfaction.
- **PROM / PREM**: patient-reported outcome / experience measure — structured instruments capturing the patient's perspective.
- **Critical appraisal**: systematic assessment of a study's methodological quality and risk of bias.
