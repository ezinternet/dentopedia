## Why Ingested

Seeds the wiki's first CeraSeal / bioceramic-sealer biocompatibility cluster, complementing the clinical CeraSeal cohort and obturation papers already held ([[endodontics/spinelli-2024-three-year-single-cone-ceraseal-cohort]], [[endodontics/zamparini-2023-premixed-calcium-silicate-carrier-based-2year]]). Whereas those track clinical/survival outcomes, this paper supplies the cell-level (MC3T3 pre-osteoblast) cytotoxicity and osteo/odontogenic gene-expression evidence underpinning why premixed calcium-silicate sealers like CeraSeal are chosen over epoxy-resin AH Plus.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro MC3T3 pre-osteoblast study (ISO 10993-5, n triplicate): CeraSeal, BioRoot RCS and AH Plus were all non-cytotoxic at 48 h (BioRoot transiently cytotoxic at 24 h, 1:10), and differentially induced inflammation/mineralization genes (AH Plus → Tnf; CeraSeal/BioRoot → Ptgs2 and Dmp1) without changing 28-day mineralization-nodule formation.

## 한줄요약

In-vitro MC3T3 전조골세포 연구 (ISO 10993-5, 3회 반복): CeraSeal·BioRoot RCS·AH Plus 모두 48시간 시점에서 비독성 (BioRoot만 24시간·1:10에서 일시적 독성), 염증·광화 유전자를 차등 유도 (AH Plus는 Tnf↑, CeraSeal/BioRoot는 Ptgs2·Dmp1↑) 했으나 28일째 광화 결절 형성에는 차이가 없었다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First report that the epoxy-resin gold-standard **AH Plus** is **not cytotoxic** to MC3T3 osteoblast-lineage cells.
- Direct head-to-head of the premixed calcium-silicate bioceramic **CeraSeal** against BioRoot RCS (tricalcium silicate) and AH Plus in an osteoblastic model.
- Dissociates **cytotoxicity** (all three safe at 48 h) from **bioactivity** (divergent inflammation/mineralization gene profiles) — showing "non-cytotoxic" does not equal "osteoinductive nodule formation."

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Standard**: ISO 10993-5:2009 extraction method.
- **Cells**: MC3T3 (ATCC CRL-2594) mouse pre-osteoblast, passages 5–10, α-MEM + 5% FBS.
- **Materials/extracts**: CeraSeal (calcium silicate, ZrO2, thickener; MetaBiomed Korea), BioRoot RCS (tricalcium silicate + ZrO2 + povidone; Septodont), AH Plus (Bis-A/Bis-F epoxy resin; Dentsply). 24 h extract in α-MEM, 0.22 µm filtered, serial dilutions 1:10 and 1:100.
- **Viability**: MTT at 24 h and 48 h; absorbance 570 nm; DMSO positive control, FBS-free negative control.
- **Gene expression**: qRT-PCR (TaqMan, 2^-ΔΔCt, Gapdh/β-actin reference) at 1:100, 24 h — inflammation (Tnf, Ptgs2) and mineralization/osteo-odontogenic (Runx2, Msx1, Ssp1, Dmp1).
- **Mineralization**: Alizarin Red S at 28 days (β-glycerophosphate + ascorbic acid medium), quantified by cetylpyridinium chloride elution at 570 nm.
- **Stats**: one-way ANOVA + Tukey, α=0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Viability 24 h**: BioRoot RCS reduced viability at 1:10 (p<0.05); CeraSeal similar to / AH Plus higher than control.
- **Viability 48 h**: no sealer cytotoxic vs control (p>0.05) except CeraSeal and DMSO showing a difference.
- **Tnf (TNF-α)**: up-regulated by **AH Plus** (p<0.05); CeraSeal/BioRoot ≈ control.
- **Ptgs2 (COX-2)**: up-regulated by **CeraSeal and BioRoot** vs control and AH Plus (p<0.05).
- **Runx2**: induced by AH Plus (and BioRoot per abstract) (p<0.05).
- **Dmp1**: higher for **CeraSeal and BioRoot** vs AH Plus/control (p<0.0001).
- **Msx1, Ssp1**: not modulated by any sealer (p=0.1769).
- **Mineralization nodules (28 d)**: similar across all sealer groups vs control (p>0.05); only DMSO reduced (p<0.0001).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro single cell line (MC3T3 mouse pre-osteoblast); limited extrapolation to clinical practice.
- Bioactivity inferred from gene expression at 24 h; mineralization-nodule endpoint showed no between-sealer difference, so transcriptional signals did not translate to functional mineralization in this 28-day model.
- Molecular mechanisms of mineralized-tissue formation remain to be elucidated.

## 7. Glossary

- **MC3T3-E1**: well-characterized mouse pre-osteoblast cell line used to model osteoblast differentiation and mineralization.
- **MTT assay**: colorimetric viability assay (formazan formation) read at 570 nm.
- **ISO 10993-5**: international standard for in-vitro cytotoxicity testing of medical devices.
- **Tnf / Ptgs2**: inflammation markers (TNF-α; cyclooxygenase-2/COX-2).
- **Runx2 / Msx1 / Ssp1 / Dmp1**: osteo/odontogenic markers — early osteogenesis transcription factor; homeobox; osteopontin (SPP1); dentin matrix protein 1.
- **Alizarin Red S**: stain quantifying calcium in mineralized extracellular matrix.
