## Why Ingested

Provides 5-year retrospective clinical data on full-mouth adhesive rehabilitation of severely worn teeth using occlusal, vestibular, and palatal veneers — filling a gap in medium-term outcome evidence for this high-complexity scenario. Directly extends [[wiki/veneers/maldonado-2024-cadcam-occlusal-veneers-fatigue-fracture-sr]] (which assessed in-vitro mechanical performance) with real in-vivo clinical survival data across composite and ceramic materials.

## One-line Summary

5-year retrospective case series (8 patients, 212 restorations) reporting 90.1% overall survival for adhesive full-mouth rehabilitation in severe tooth wear, with occlusal veneers showing lower survival (79.1%) than vestibular (94.1%) or palatal (100%) veneers.

## 한줄요약

후향적 증례군 연구 (8명, 212개 수복물, 60개월): 중증 치아마모(Tooth Wear) 환자에서 접착성 전악 수복(Total Rehabilitation) 생존율 90.1%, 교합면 비니어(Occlusal Veneer) 79.1% vs 순측 비니어(Vestibular Veneer) 94.1% vs 구개측 비니어(Palatal Veneer) 100%.

- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Medicine (MDPI)
- **DOI**: 10.3390/jcm12165222
- **Published**: 10 August 2023
- **Study type**: Retrospective case series
- **Ethics**: Catholic University of Cuenca Ethics Committee (CEISH-UCACUE-2023-037); Declaration of Helsinki compliant
- **n**: 8 patients (6 men, 2 women; mean age 46.6 years); 212 restorations; 60 months follow-up
- **Operator**: Single experienced operator (AFC)

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Medium-term in vivo data**: Provides 5-year survival data for all three veneer types (occlusal, vestibular, palatal) in full-mouth severe tooth wear rehabilitation — a scenario with limited published clinical evidence.
2. **Material comparison**: Demonstrates comparable but non-statistically-significant survival difference between composite resin (86.3%) and ceramic (93.1%) restorations (p = 0.107).
3. **Risk predictor identification**: Posterior tooth location (posterior veneer) is a statistically significant risk factor for failure (HR 0.143, CI 0.034–0.601, p = 0.008); restoration type was also significant (p < 0.000).
4. **Repairability principle**: 18 of 21 complications were resolved with direct composite resin repair — validates the reparability advantage of minimally invasive adhesive approaches.
5. **Semi-additive protocol**: Describes a step-by-step clinical sequence integrating DSD, VDO determination via Kois analyzer, posterior occlusal restoration first, then anterior guidance, then esthetic veneers.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Study design**: Retrospective case series at a private dental clinic in Spain.

**Inclusion criteria**:
- Main chief complaint: esthetic problem
- Severe generalized tooth wear grade 2–4 per TWES 2.0 (Tooth Wear Evaluation System)
- Previous indirect restorations in anterior/posterior sectors
- Restorative treatment in place for ≥5 years
- Complete medical records with initial/final photographs and models

**Exclusion criteria**:
- Localized wear grade 1 (TWES 2.0)
- Periodontitis
- Severe gingival inflammation
- Non-compliant oral hygiene

**Restorations performed** (n=212):
- 66 occlusal veneers (CAD-CAM milled indirect composite resin: Cerasmart, GC)
- 26 palatal veneers (CAD-CAM nanoceramic resin)
- 120 vestibular veneers (feldspathic ceramic: Creation; or lithium disilicate: E.max Press, Ivoclar)

**Adhesive cementation**: Light-cured resin cement (Calibra Veneer Bleach, Dentsply Sirona)

**VDO management**: Semi-additive restorative approach with VDO increase; determination via Kois dentofacial analyzer and articulator setup; intermaxillary relations via power centric manual induction technique.

**Outcome assessment**: Modified USPHS criteria at each follow-up; survival analysis via Kaplan–Meier + log-rank (Mantel-Cox); Cox regression for hazard ratios; SPSS 24.0.

**Failure definitions** (modified USPHS):
- Absolute failures: restoration fracture score 3–5, tooth fracture score 4, secondary caries score 1
- Non-absolute failures: minor chipping (score 2), surface roughness score 2

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Restoration type | Survival at 60 months |
|---|---|
| Overall (all 212) | 90.1% (mean survival time 57.6 months) |
| Occlusal veneers | 79.1% |
| Vestibular veneers | 94.1% |
| Palatal veneers | 100% |
| Composite resin | 86.3% |
| Ceramic | 93.1% (p=0.107 vs composite) |

**Failure timeline**: Most complications at year 2 (5%, 10 restorations) and year 5 (6%, 6 restorations); year 3 = 2%; year 4 = 1%.

**Total complications**: 21 restorations
- 17 chipping (USPHS score 2) — repaired with direct composite
- 1 debonding (USPHS score 5) — recemented
- 1 tooth fracture (extraction + implant)
- 2 secondary caries (1 needed root canal, 1 composite repair)

**No marginal microleakage or postoperative sensitivity** observed in any restoration type throughout 60 months.

**Significant risk factors** (Cox regression):
- Restoration type (posterior vs anterior veneer): HR 0.143 (CI 0.034–0.601), p = 0.008
- Treatment type (occlusal/facial/palatal veneer): p < 0.000
- Material (composite vs ceramic): p = 0.401 (NS)
- Bite guard use: HR 1.91, p = 0.149 (NS but positive trend)
- TWES class 3–4 vs 2: HR 1.362, p = 0.522 (NS)
- Parafunction/bruxism: NS

**Orthodontic pre-treatment**: 4 of 8 patients (50%) received pre-restorative orthodontics for better sagittal/transverse/vertical positioning; 6/8 had parafunctional habits.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample (n=8 patients) — limits statistical power and generalizability
- Single operator (AFC) — reduces external validity; high-skill protocol
- Retrospective design — selection and recall bias; no randomization
- Non-standardized material mix (composite + multiple ceramics) — may confound material-level comparisons
- No control group — no comparison with conventional crown-based rehabilitation
- Moderate-quality evidence by definition of study type (case series, no control)
- Authors recommend prospective RCTs with larger cohorts to confirm these findings

## 6. Related Work

- Torosyan et al. (2022): 406 restorations, 99% survival at 6 years — higher than current study
- Fradeani et al. (2021): 99.15% cumulative survival, 96.5% at 10 years with MIPP; supports adhesive approach
- Faus V et al. (2020): bruxism patients with bite guards — 89.1% survival at 7 years; without guards — 63.9% (p<0.05)
- Mesko et al. (2016) SR: no strong evidence favoring one material over another for severe wear
- Gúth JF et al. (2020): lithium disilicate shows significantly less occlusal wear than CAD-CAM composites at 2 years
- Loomans & Opdam (2018): restorations modify rate/location/nature of wear but don't prevent it
- European consensus: advocates adhesive/direct/indirect techniques for severely worn teeth

## 7. Glossary

- **TWES 2.0**: Tooth Wear Evaluation System version 2 — graded taxonomy for severity classification of tooth wear (grades 1–4)
- **VDO**: Vertical Dimension of Occlusion — vertical relationship of maxilla to mandible at defined closure; increased as part of semi-additive rehabilitation
- **USPHS criteria**: Modified United States Public Health Service criteria — standardized clinical rating scale for restorations (marginal adaptation, surface roughness, fracture, secondary caries, sensitivity)
- **DSD**: Digital Smile Design — digital planning tool for esthetic diagnosis and treatment simulation
- **Semi-additive approach**: Restorative strategy that adds material (via wax-up/veneers) without reducing tooth structure, relying on increased VDO to create space
- **Occlusal veneer**: Indirect restoration covering the occlusal surface of posterior teeth; used to restore lost vertical dimension in worn dentitions
- **Kaplan–Meier survival analysis**: Non-parametric method to estimate survival function from time-to-event data; used here for restoration survival at 60 months
- **Cox regression**: Proportional hazards model for identifying predictor variables of time-to-failure; used to compute hazard ratios for each restoration variable
- **MIPP**: Minimally Invasive Preparation Procedure — concept of no/minimal tooth preparation using additive adhesive restorations
