## Why Ingested

골-임플란트 접촉률 (Bone-to-Implant Contact, BIC)과 표면처리별 osseointegration 속도 차이를 다루는 기존 페이지들의 생물학적 기반 근거가 부족하여 ingest. 이 리뷰는 Ti/TiZr/지르코니아(Zirconia) 임플란트의 골유착 시간적 순서, 표면처리 효과, 미래 과제를 종합적으로 정리하여 [[implants/surface/buser-2017-slaactive-vs-sla-early-loading]] 등 표면처리 페이지들의 생물학적 배경 참조원으로 활용.

## One-line Summary

Narrative review (Periodontology 2000, 2017) by Bosshardt, Chappuis & Buser synthesizing preclinical osseointegration evidence for cpTi grade 4, TiZr, Ti6Al4V, and zirconia implants — covering wound-healing sequence, surface modification effects (%BIC), multinucleated giant cells, and open questions on marginal bone loss.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Periodontology 2000, Vol. 73, 2017, pp. 22–40
- **DOI**: 10.1111/prd.12169
- **Study type**: Narrative review (predominantly preclinical/animal data)
- **Authors**: Dieter D. Bosshardt, Vivianne Chappuis, Daniel Buser (University of Bern)
- **Focus**: Temporal sequence of osseointegration; surface modification effects on %BIC; comparative osseointegration of Ti, Ti alloys, and zirconia; open clinical questions

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Wound-healing sequence**: Detailed account of the 4-phase osseointegration cascade — hemostasis → granulation tissue → woven bone → parallel-fibered → lamellar bone → remodeling. Bone formation starts within the first week in both animals and humans.
2. **Primary vs. secondary stability**: Primary stability (mechanical) decreases over 3–4 weeks while secondary stability (biological) rises; the transient "stability dip" occurs ~3–4 weeks post-placement.
3. **Surface roughness hierarchy**: SLA (sandblasted large-grit acid-etched) > TPS (titanium plasma-sprayed) > machined/smooth for %BIC. Hydroxyapatite-coated implants showed high %BIC but consistent signs of coating resorption.
4. **SLActive superiority**: Chemically modified SLActive surfaces (nitrogen-rinsed, isotonic saline-stored) showed significantly higher %BIC vs. SLA at 2 and 4 weeks in miniature pigs and humans.
5. **TiZr alloy**: TiZr with modified SLA surface shows comparable osseointegration to cpTi grade 4. Ti6Al4V peaked at lower %BIC and had significantly more multinucleated giant cells (MNGCs) than TiZr and cpTi.
6. **Zirconia**: Surface-modified zirconia (sandblasted + acid-etched) achieves bone apposition. Acid-etching alone increased %BIC over sandblasting alone; alkaline-etching reduced %BIC. MNGCs appeared on all zirconia surfaces. Alumina-toughened zirconia showed lower %BIC than cpTi at 4 and 8 weeks.
7. **Multinucleated giant cells (MNGCs)**: Present on all biomaterials but in significantly higher numbers on Ti6Al4V and zirconia than on cpTi/TiZr. MNGCs are part of normal osseointegration; their presence does NOT predict future implant loss.
8. **Marginal bone loss**: Identified as an unsolved problem; likely multifactorial — immune/inflammatory factors, patient medication (polypharmacy, SSRIs, antiresorptives, antiangiogenics), titanium particle/ion release, peri-implantitis distinct from "unexplained" loss.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Predominantly preclinical evidence: miniature pig, canine, and rat models
- Histomorphometry (%BIC) as primary osseointegration metric
- Wound chamber model (Berglundh design) for standardized early-healing analysis
- Human biopsy data incorporated where available (SLA vs. SLActive human studies)
- In vitro and clinical data discussed selectively
- Soft tissue integration explicitly excluded from scope

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Biomaterial | Surface | Outcome vs. cpTi |
|---|---|---|
| cpTi grade 4 | SLActive | Reference gold standard |
| cpTi grade 4 | SLA | Lower early %BIC vs. SLActive |
| TiZr (Roxolid) | mod-SLA | Comparable to cpTi, not significantly different |
| Ti6Al4V | Sandblasted + nitric acid | Lower peak %BIC; significantly more MNGCs |
| Zirconia (Y-TZP) | SB + acid-etch | Bone apposition confirmed; MNGCs present |
| Alumina-toughened ZrO₂ | SB + acid-etch | Lower %BIC than Ti at 4 and 8 weeks |

- SLActive: significantly greater %BIC vs. SLA at 2 and 4 weeks (miniature pig and human)
- Stability dip: transient loss of total stability at ~3–4 weeks is physiologically normal
- Gene expression switches at 4 days–2 weeks: immuno-inflammatory → angiogenesis + osteogenesis

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Predominantly animal data; inter-species variability complicates translation
- Long-term osseointegration maintenance not testable in current animal models (studies too short)
- MNGC role remains unclear — present in normal healing but elevated on some materials
- Titanium ion/particle release and clinical relevance of cell-induced corrosion understudied
- Medication effects (SSRIs, antiresorptives, antiangiogenics) on osseointegration poorly characterized
- Surface chemistry vs. topography effects virtually inseparable — mechanistic clarity lacking
- Marginal bone loss unrelated to peri-implantitis: etiology and prevention unresolved

## 7. Glossary

- **골-임플란트 접촉률 (Bone-to-Implant Contact, BIC)**: % of implant surface covered by bone — primary histomorphometric endpoint
- **1차 안정성 (Primary Stability)**: Mechanical fixation immediately post-placement; decreases over time
- **2차 안정성 (Secondary Stability)**: Biological stability from new bone apposition; increases over time
- **안정성 강하 (Stability Dip)**: Transient decrease in total stability at ~3–4 weeks
- **SLA (Sandblasted Large-grit Acid-etched)**: Standard micro-rough titanium surface
- **SLActive**: Chemically modified SLA — hydrophilic, biologically more active
- **TiZr (Roxolid)**: Titanium-zirconium alloy with enhanced mechanical strength vs. cpTi
- **Ti6Al4V**: Titanium-6aluminum-4vanadium alloy; adequate %BIC but more MNGCs
- **Y-TZP (지르코니아)**: Yttria-stabilized tetragonal zirconia polycrystal — ceramic implant material
- **다핵거대세포 (Multinucleated Giant Cell, MNGC)**: Fused macrophages; present on all biomaterials during osseointegration; elevated on Ti6Al4V and zirconia
- **골재형성 (Bone Remodeling)**: Continuous resorption + apposition cycle at bone-implant interface; exposes implant surface transiently
