## Why Ingested

광중합 레진 시멘트를 사용한 리튬 디실리케이트와 고투명 지르코니아 비니어의 접착 강도를 정량적으로 비교한 SR+MA가 부재하여 인제스트. [[veneers/klein-2025-ceramic-laminate-veneer-survival-complications-sr-ma]]에서 다루는 세라믹 비니어 임상 생존율을 접착 메커니즘 측면에서 보완한다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 6 in vitro studies (n=64 specimens): light-cured resin cement bond strength was higher for lithium disilicate veneers (25.4 MPa) than translucent zirconia (12.1 MPa), with enamel substrate (21.1 MPa) outperforming composite core (11.3 MPa).

## 한줄요약

6편 인비트로 연구 SR+MA: 광중합 레진 시멘트 접착 강도는 리튬 디실리케이트(25.4 MPa)가 고투명 지르코니아(12.1 MPa)보다 높고, 에나멜 기질(21.1 MPa)이 복합재 코어(11.3 MPa)보다 우수.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First quantitative SR+MA specifically comparing LDS vs translucent zirconia veneers under light-cured cementation protocols
- Demonstrated substrate type (enamel vs composite core) significantly affects bond strength
- Exploratory meta-regression found inverse trend between veneer thickness and bond strength
- Applied REML random-effects model with Hartung–Knapp–Sidik–Jonkman adjustment (appropriate for <10 studies)
- Used adapted GRADE framework for in vitro studies

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Study design**: PRISMA 2020-compliant SR+MA of in vitro studies
- **Databases**: PubMed, Google Scholar, ScienceDirect, Wiley Online Library, SpringerLink (Jan 2010–Mar 2025)
- **Studies screened**: 1,936 records identified; 94 full-texts assessed; 7 qualitative, 6 quantitative
- **Inclusion**: LDS or translucent zirconia veneers, light-cured resin cement only, bond strength in MPa, enamel or composite substrate
- **Exclusion**: crowns/inlays, dual-cure or self-adhesive cements, no extractable SD data, FEA studies, case reports, narrative reviews
- **Statistical model**: Random-effects (REML), Hartung–Knapp adjustment; I² and Tau² for heterogeneity
- **Subgroup analyses**: ceramic material type, substrate type
- **Meta-regression**: veneer thickness vs bond strength (exploratory)
- **Risk of bias**: adapted framework for in vitro studies (5 domains: specimen randomization, blinding, surface treatment standardization, outcome reporting, statistical analysis)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Overall pooled bond strength**: 15.9 MPa (all materials combined)
- **Lithium disilicate (LDS) subgroup**: 25.4 MPa
- **Translucent zirconia subgroup**: 12.1 MPa
- **Enamel substrate subgroup**: 21.1 MPa
- **Composite core substrate subgroup**: 11.3 MPa
- **Heterogeneity**: I² = 98.2% (considerable)
- **Meta-regression**: inverse trend — thicker veneer → lower bond strength (exploratory, limited dataset)
- **Evidence certainty**: low (in vitro indirect evidence, high heterogeneity, small n)
- **Overall conclusion**: LDS + enamel substrate combination yields highest bond strength under light-cured protocol

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- All included studies are in vitro — cannot be directly extrapolated to clinical performance
- Only 6 studies with complete data for meta-analysis; very small n (64 specimens total)
- Considerable heterogeneity (I² = 98.2%) limits pooled estimate reliability
- Bond strength test methods mixed (shear, microtensile, push-out) — mechanically non-equivalent despite MPa standardization
- No prospective PROSPERO registration (deliberate, as in vitro design)
- Future: clinical RCTs comparing LDS vs translucent zirconia veneers with standardized light-cure protocols needed

## 7. Glossary

- **LDS (Lithium Disilicate)**: glass-ceramic with crystalline lithium disilicate phase; etchable with HF acid; allows silanization for strong chemical+micromechanical bonding
- **Translucent zirconia**: high-translucency zirconia variant (reduced alumina content); improved light transmission vs conventional zirconia; not HF-etchable
- **Light-cured resin cement**: resin-based luting agent polymerized exclusively by light (no self-cure component); color stability advantage but relies on adequate light penetration
- **10-MDP (10-methacryloyloxydecyl dihydrogen phosphate)**: phosphate monomer in adhesive systems that bonds chemically to zirconia via ionic interaction with ZrO₂
- **REML (Restricted Maximum Likelihood)**: statistical estimation method for random-effects variance component; recommended for small meta-analyses
- **Hartung–Knapp adjustment**: correction for confidence interval calculation in random-effects meta-analysis with <10 studies
- **I²**: proportion of total variation attributable to heterogeneity rather than chance; 98.2% = very high
