## Why Ingested

근관치료 후 보철 수복 시 글래스 파이버 포스트(Glass Fiber Post, GFP) 접착 프로토콜 선택에 대한 임상적 의문 — 전산부식(Total Etch, TE), 자가산부식(Self-Etch, SE), 자가접착(Self-Adhesive, SA) 시스템 중 근관 내 push-out 결합강도(Bond Strength, BS)가 가장 우수한 것을 직접 비교. 기존 wiki의 이중경화형 레진시멘트 논문 [[wiki/resin-bonding/li-2025-curing-modes-dual-cure-dentin]]이 경화 방식에 초점을 맞춘 반면, 본 연구는 접착 전처리 전략을 비교하여 보완적 근거를 제공.

## One-line Summary

In vitro push-out bond strength study (n=60 mandibular premolars, 3 groups) comparing total-etch, self-etch, and self-adhesive resin cement systems for glass fiber post cementation: self-etch demonstrated highest bond strength (131.03 N) vs total-etch (115.25 N) vs self-adhesive (75.04 N), p=0.001.

## 한줄요약

in vitro 연구(하악 소구치 60개, 그룹당 n=20): 글래스 파이버 포스트 접착 시 자가산부식(SE) 131.03 N > 전산부식(TE) 115.25 N > 자가접착(SA) 75.04 N 순으로 push-out 결합강도 유의한 차이(p=0.001).

## 2. Key Contributions

- First direct head-to-head comparison of TE vs SE vs self-adhesive system using identical glass fiber post (ParaCore/Solocem, Coltene) and identical tooth preparation protocols
- Confirms that SE (self-etch) adhesive provides statistically significantly superior push-out bond strength to root dentin compared to TE and self-adhesive systems
- Clinically actionable: SE system recommended as optimal adhesive strategy for fiber post cementation in endodontically treated teeth
- Demonstrates that self-adhesive resin cements, despite technical simplicity, yield significantly lower bond strength

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Sample**: 60 freshly extracted human mandibular premolars (single-rooted, caries/crack-free, extracted for orthodontic purposes)
- Decoronated to uniform 15 mm root length; standardized chemo-mechanical preparation (K-files to 25, Neo Endo Flex 35/4%, 5% NaOCl + 17% EDTA irrigation)
- Obturation: sectional technique with gutta-percha + Endoseal sealer
- Post space: prepared with fiber post drills (size 3)

**Group allocation** (n=20 each):
- Group A (TE): 37% phosphoric acid 15 s → rinse → air-dry → ParaCore adhesive (ParaBond) + ParaCore resin cement
- Group B (SE): non-rinse conditioner → ParaBond adhesive A + B → ParaCore resin cement
- Group C (SA): self-adhesive Solocem cement (Coltene) without separate etch or primer

**Push-out test**:
- Samples sectioned into three 5 mm segments (apical, middle, cervical); middle segment tested
- Universal testing machine at 0.5 mm/min; 1 mm plunger apical-to-coronal direction
- Peak force to dislodge fiber post recorded in Newtons

**Statistics**: One-way ANOVA + Tukey's HSD post hoc; Shapiro-Wilk normality test; Stata 18; significance p<0.05; G*Power sample size (power 90%, α 5%, effect size 0.59)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Group | Adhesive System | Mean Bond Strength | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| B | Self-etch (SE) | **131.03 N** | Highest — p=0.001 vs others |
| A | Total-etch (TE) | 115.25 N | Intermediate |
| C | Self-adhesive (SA) | 75.04 N | Lowest |

- ANOVA confirmed highly significant difference between all groups (p=0.001)
- Null hypothesis rejected
- Middle root third used (consistent post-space diameter; 5 mm slice thickness replicates clinical forces)

**Mechanistic interpretation**:
- SE advantage: self-etching primer penetrates smear layer to form thin hybrid layer without over-drying/over-wetting risk
- TE disadvantage: requires precise moisture control; over-wetting → hydrophilic monomer dissolution; over-drying → collagen fibril collapse → incomplete hybrid layer
- SA disadvantage: mild demineralization insufficient for strong micromechanical interlocking; relies on chemical bond to hydroxyapatite alone

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In vitro study; no simulation of aging, cyclic loading, or oral environment (saliva, temperature cycling)
- Single brand of cement (ParaCore/Solocem, Coltene) — results may not generalize across manufacturers
- Middle root third only; apical and cervical thirds not analyzed separately
- No fractographic analysis of failure modes (adhesive vs cohesive)
- No long-term follow-up; durability of SE advantage not assessed
- Single-root premolar model; multi-canal anatomy not represented

## 7. Glossary

- **GFP (Glass Fiber Post, 글래스 파이버 포스트)**: Prefabricated post made of glass fibers in resin matrix; modulus of elasticity similar to dentin (~18 GPa), reducing root fracture risk
- **Push-out test (Push-out 시험)**: Shear stress test where plunger displaces bonded post apico-coronally; preferred over microtensile for post-bond testing
- **Monoblock effect (모노블록 효과)**: Uniform stress distribution concept achieved when fiber post + resin cement + root dentin have similar elastic moduli
- **Hybrid layer (하이브리드 층)**: Dentin-resin interdiffusion zone formed during bonding; quality determines bond strength longevity
- **SE (Self-Etch, 자가산부식)**: Adhesive system using acidic primer that simultaneously conditions and primes dentin without rinsing
- **TE (Total-Etch, 전산부식)**: Three-step adhesive using separate phosphoric acid etch, rinse, and adhesive application
- **SA (Self-Adhesive, 자가접착)**: All-in-one resin cement requiring no separate etching or bonding step
