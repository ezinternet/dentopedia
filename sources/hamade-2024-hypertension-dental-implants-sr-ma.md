## One-line Summary

SR+MA (24 studies, 21,066 implants) finding that hypertensive patients show no higher dental-implant failure odds than normotensive patients (OR 1.100, 95% CI 0.709-1.707, p=0.671).

## 한줄요약

24편·임플란트 21,066개 SR+MA — 고혈압 환자의 임플란트 실패 위험은 정상혈압 환자와 차이 없음 (OR 1.100, 95% CI 0.709-1.707, p=0.671).

## 2. Key Contributions

- First broad SR+MA (4 databases, no medication-type restriction) directly quantifying the effect of hypertension on dental-implant failure, superseding a prior 3-study SR that only included studies reporting antihypertensive-drug type.
- Pooled 24 studies (published 2005-2021): **4874 implants (257 failures) in hypertensive** vs **16,192 implants (809 failures) in normotensive** patients (21,066 implants total).
- Bottom line: **hypertension does NOT raise implant failure odds** (OR 1.100, 95% CI 0.709-1.707, p=0.671); effect did not change with follow-up time (meta-regression p=0.824); no clear publication bias.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PICO: hypertensive vs normotensive patients rehabilitated with dental implants; outcome = implant failure (statistical unit = implant).
- Databases: PubMed/Medline, Web of Science, Scopus, Ebsco + manual journal/reference search; no time restriction; search Aug 2022, updated Oct 2023.
- Inclusion: clinical human studies; cylindrical screw-type modern Ti (c.p.Ti or alloy) implants. Exclusion: case/technical reports, animal/in-vitro/review; mini/zygomatic/orthodontic/zirconia/subperiosteal/hollow implants.
- Definitions: hypertension >= 130/80 mm Hg; normotension < 120/80 mm Hg. Failure = implant removal (early or late) or fracture.
- Quality: NIH Quality Assessment Tool; "good" = >= 7 points — all 24 studies rated "good".
- Analysis: random-effects inverse-variance model (heterogeneity I2 = 72.96%, p<0.001); OR effect measure; meta-regression on follow-up; funnel plot.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Low specificity in classifying hypertension; included studies rarely reported whether patients were properly controlled (antihypertensive intake with effective clinical results) or had other cardiovascular disease.
- Confounders: diabetes, smoking, bruxism, radiotherapy/chemotherapy, SSRIs, PPIs, bisphosphonates — all potential implant-failure modifiers.
- Many studies retrospective with incomplete data, small samples, short follow-up; multiple operators across studies.
- Antihypertensive drugs may counteract bone effects of hypertension, but causal bone-health associations remain unestablished.
- Authors recommend future studies with rigorous hypertension diagnostic criteria and clear pharmacological-management reporting.

## 7. Glossary

- **Hypertension**: blood pressure >= 130/80 mm Hg (study definition).
- **OR (Odds Ratio)**: relative effect measure; OR ~1 = no association.
- **Osseointegration**: direct structural/functional bone-to-implant connection.
- **RANKL/RANK/OPG**: bone-remodeling signaling triad; PTH-driven RANKL upregulation drives osteoclastogenesis.
- **Microvascular rarefaction**: hypertension-associated loss/constriction of microvessels reducing tissue perfusion.
