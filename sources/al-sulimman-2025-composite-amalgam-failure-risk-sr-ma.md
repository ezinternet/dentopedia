복합레진 vs 아말감 실패위험 SR+MA (2025, Int Dent J): 13편; RR 0.96 (0.68–1.34), 두 재료 간 유의한 차이 없음; 아말감 0–50%, 레진 0–62.7% 실패비율.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: International Dental Journal (Elsevier/FDI), 2025
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.identj.2025.100871
- **Available online**: 26 June 2025
- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (PRISMA)

## 2. Key Contributions
- 13 studies included; primary outcome = restoration failure (replacement, fracture, secondary caries, postoperative sensitivity, toothache)
- **Meta-analysis result**: RR 0.96 (95% CI 0.68–1.34) — no statistically significant difference between composite and amalgam
- Failure proportions: amalgam 0–50%, composite 0–62.7% (wide between-study variation)
- No significant publication bias (Egger's test p > 0.05)
- Conclusion: existing evidence insufficient to declare one material superior; future reviews need to control for confounders (caries risk, operator skill, tooth position)

## 3. Methodology
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane, Google Scholar
- **Inclusion**: Clinical trials + observational studies; English-language; ≥12 months follow-up; studies from 1990 onward; permanent posterior teeth
- **Quality assessment**: Cochrane RoB (RCTs); Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (observational)
- **Meta-analysis**: Random-effects RR; publication bias via Egger's test

## 4. Key Results
| Outcome | RR | 95% CI | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Failure risk (composite vs amalgam) | 0.96 | 0.68–1.34 | NS |

Failure proportion range: amalgam 0–50%, composite 0–62.7%

## 5. Limitations
- Heterogeneous failure definitions across 13 studies
- Did not account for caries risk category, tooth position (single vs multi-surface), operator experience
- No subgroup analysis by surface number
- Short follow-up in some included studies
