## Why Ingested

This RCT applies the toothpick method as one component of a structured, hygienist-delivered dental hygiene care programme for patients with mental disorders — a special-needs population — extending the method beyond healthy adults and beyond the periodontal-maintenance setting. It complements [[periodontics/lee-2020-watanabe-toothpick-method-srp-diabetic-periodontitis-rct]], which tested the Watanabe toothpick method in diabetic periodontitis patients, by showing the technique can be embedded in a broader behavioural/educational care package for a psychiatrically vulnerable group.

Caveat: this is an **abstract-only** record (full text not retrieved). The toothpick method was just **one element** of a multi-part programme (10-min flash-based video + brochures + toothpick method), so its isolated contribution to the plaque reduction cannot be separated from the educational components.

## One-line Summary

RCT (n=73, 3 groups, 12 weeks at 4-week intervals): a multi-component dental hygiene care programme (10-min flash video + brochures + toothpick method, delivered by 5 hygienists) significantly reduced plaque index after each session in patients with mental disorders; oral dryness improved, but stimulated saliva and caries activity did not.

## 한줄요약

정신질환 환자 73명 대상 RCT(3군, 12주·4주 간격): 10분 플래시 동영상 + 브로셔 + 토스픽법(toothpick method)을 묶은 다요소 구강위생 관리 프로그램이 매 세션 후 치면세균막 지수(plaque index)를 유의하게 감소시켰고(P<0.0001) 군간 차이도 유의(P=0.036). 구강건조감은 개선됐으나 자극타액과 우식활성도는 변화 없었다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Develops and tests a dental hygiene care programme tailored to the specific needs of patients with **mental disorders** (a special-needs / psychiatrically vulnerable population).
- Embeds the **toothpick method** within a multi-component educational/behavioural package (short flash-based video + brochures), delivered by dental hygienists.
- Demonstrates that a brief, repeatable (every 4 weeks), low-intensity programme can reduce plaque index in a population that typically has poor oral self-care.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: RCT.
- **Participants**: 73 patients with mental illness, randomly allocated to **three groups**.
- **Follow-up**: 12 weeks, sessions at **4-week intervals**.
- **Intervention**: a newly designed dental hygiene care programme using a **flash-based video (~10 min)**, **brochures**, and the **toothpick method**, implemented by **five dental hygienists**.
- **Outcome variables**: plaque index, stimulated saliva, subjective oral dryness, dental caries activity.
- *(Abstract-only: details of group differentiation, randomization, blinding, and plaque index used are not recoverable from the abstract.)*

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Plaque index**: significantly decreased after each session in all three groups (**P < 0.0001**); significant differences also found **between groups** (**P = 0.036**).
- **Subjective oral dryness**: decreased significantly.
- **Stimulated saliva**: did not improve.
- **Dental caries activity**: did not improve.
- **Authors' conclusion**: the programme — a short 10-min flash-based video plus brochures every 4 weeks — was effective in reducing plaque index in patients with mental disorders.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Multi-component intervention**: the toothpick method was one element among video and brochures; its isolated effect cannot be separated.
- **Abstract-only record**: numeric magnitudes (mean plaque change, group definitions, baseline characteristics) are not available.
- Special-needs population with potential confounders (psychotropic medication–induced xerostomia, motivation, caregiver dependence) not detailed in abstract.
- Short 12-week horizon; durability of the behaviour change not assessed.

## 7. Glossary

- **Toothpick method (Watanabe method)**: an interdental plaque-control technique using a toothbrush in a horizontal, toothpick-like insertion motion into interproximal spaces.
- **Plaque index**: clinical score quantifying dental biofilm accumulation on tooth surfaces.
- **Stimulated saliva**: salivary flow rate measured under stimulation (e.g., chewing); a marker of salivary gland function.
- **Subjective oral dryness**: patient-reported sensation of dry mouth (xerostomia symptom).
- **Dental caries activity**: a test-based estimate of an individual's current caries-promoting microbial/biochemical activity.
