## One-line Summary
SR+MA on botulinum toxin type A for painful TMD; found BTX-A superior to placebo for myofascial pain reduction but no significant difference in maximum mouth opening; 20 RCTs included qualitatively, 6 meta-analyzed.

## 1. Document Information
- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Year**: 2020
- **Authors**: Mengjiao Zhu, Ziwei Huang, Yeye Wang, Jing Qin, Mingyue Fan

## 2. Key Contributions
- Largest synthesis at time of publication: 20 RCTs included in qualitative review, 6 in meta-analysis
- Demonstrates statistically significant pain reduction with BTX-A for myofascial TMD
- Shows no significant benefit of BTX-A on MMO compared to placebo
- Clarifies that BTX-A effect is primarily on muscle pain, not joint mechanics
- Dose and injection site heterogeneity identified as major research gap

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Comprehensive database search (PubMed, Embase, Cochrane)
- Inclusion: RCTs comparing BTX-A injection to placebo/saline in patients with painful TMD
- Primary outcomes: pain (VAS), MMO (mm)
- Subgroup analysis by TMD subtype (myofascial vs. arthrogenous)
- Random-effects meta-analysis for 6 trials with poolable data
- Cochrane RoB assessment

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Pain (VAS)**: BTX-A significantly superior to placebo (pooled SMD favoring BTX-A, p<0.05)
- **MMO**: No significant difference between BTX-A and placebo (pooled MD not significant)
- Myofascial pain subtype showed most consistent benefit
- Arthrogenous/disc displacement subtypes: insufficient evidence
- 20 RCTs total included; 6 provided poolable quantitative data
- Adverse effects: mild (injection site pain, temporary masticatory weakness, asymmetric smile)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- High heterogeneity in dose protocols, injection sites, and number of injections
- Most RCTs small sample size (<40/arm)
- Short-to-medium follow-up (≤6 months)
- Lack of standardized outcome measures
- Optimal dosing (masseter: 25–100 U; temporal: 15–50 U) remains unclear

## 6. Related Work
- Saini et al. (2024) comprehensive BTX SR+MA for TMD
- Serrera-Figallo et al. (2020) narrative review of BTX in orofacial practice
- BMJ 2023 guideline — conditional recommendation for BTX in refractory myofascial TMD

## 7. Glossary
- **Botulinum toxin type A** (보툴리눔독소 A형, BTX-A): Neurotoxin that blocks acetylcholine release at neuromuscular junction, causing temporary muscle paralysis/weakness
- **Myofascial TMD**: TMD subtype involving masticatory muscle pain (masseter, temporalis, pterygoid)
- **SMD**: Standardized mean difference — effect size for pooled data with different scales
- **Masseter**: Primary chewing muscle; main target for BTX-A in bruxism/TMD treatment
- **Temporalis**: Secondary masticatory muscle; secondary BTX-A injection target
