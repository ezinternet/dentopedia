## Why Ingested

임플란트 교합 부하 방식(즉시 기능 부하 vs. 지연 부하 vs. 무부하)이 골-임플란트 접촉률 (Bone-to-Implant Contact, BIC)에 미치는 영향을 조직형태계측학으로 비교한 고전 동물 실험. [[wiki/occlusion/di-fiore-2022-periimplant-bone-loss-overload-occlusal-analysis]]의 교합 과부하-골소실 연구의 생물학적 기전을 뒷받침하는 초기 근거로 활용.

## One-line Summary

Animal study (9 Macaca fascicularis monkeys, 48 implants) showing that both immediately loaded and delayed-loaded titanium implants achieved significantly higher BIC and bone area within threads than unloaded submerged implants, with no significant difference between the two loading groups.

## 한줄요약

마카크 원숭이 9마리, 임플란트 48개 대상 조직형태계측 연구: 즉시 기능 부하군과 지연 부하군 모두 무부하 대조군보다 골-임플란트 접촉률(BIC) 및 나사산 내 골면적(BA)이 유의하게 높았으며, 두 부하군 간 유의한 차이는 없었음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of Periodontology
- **Volume/Issue**: Vol. 74, Issue 10, October 2003, pp. 1483–1490
- **DOI**: 10.1902/jop.2003.74.10.1483
- **Study type**: Animal experimental study (in vivo, non-human primate)
- **Animal model**: Macaca fascicularis (crab-eating macaque, 9 adults)

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Demonstrated histomorphometrically that functional occlusal loading — whether immediate or delayed — stimulates significantly greater bone formation at the bone-implant interface compared with submerged non-loading.
2. Showed that immediately loaded implants can achieve osseointegration comparable to delayed loaded implants when progressive thread design implants are used and occlusion is optimized.
3. Provided histological evidence that loading promotes compact cortical bone and extensive bone trabeculae formation rather than the loose connective tissue seen around unloaded implants.
4. All 48 implants osseointegrated with no gap at the metal-bone interface, supporting implant survival under both loading protocols.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Animal model and surgery:**
- 9 adult Macaca fascicularis monkeys
- Second premolars and first and second mandibular molars extracted bilaterally; 3 months healing before implant placement
- 48 titanium implants: 8 mm long, 3.5 mm diameter, progressive thread design

**Groups:**
- **Group A (unloaded control, n=2 monkeys, 6 implants):** Submerged healing for 3 months; no loading. Sacrificed at 3 months.
- **Group B (delayed loading, n=7 monkeys, 21 implants):** Submerged for 3 months, then loaded with temporary resin bridges simultaneously with Group C. Bridges replaced with metal bridges at 1 month; loaded for additional 2 months. Total loading period: 3 months.
- **Group C (immediate loading, n=7 monkeys, 21 implants):** Placed in contralateral mandible of same Group B monkeys; loaded immediately after placement with resin bridges. Same bridge replacement protocol. Same sacrifice timepoint as Group B.

**Occlusal verification:** Optimal occlusal relationship of resin bridges checked and adjusted at placement and bridge replacement.

**Endpoints:** Histological and histomorphometrical analysis — bone-to-implant contact (BIC) and bone area (BA) within threads and around apices.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Parameter | Group A (unloaded) | Groups B & C (loaded) |
|---|---|---|
| BIC | Lower (cancellous bone, loose connective tissue at interface) | Significantly higher; compact cortical bone in contact with implant |
| BA within threads | Lower | Significantly increased area |
| BA around apices | Lower | Increased in loaded groups |
| B vs. C difference | — | No significant difference (P <0.05 threshold not met) |
| Osseointegration (all) | Yes (no interface gap) | Yes (no interface gap) |

**Key finding:** Functional loading (immediate or delayed) significantly elevated BIC and BA versus non-loading. Immediate loading produced equivalent histomorphometric outcomes to delayed loading with progressive thread design implants.

**Histological characteristics:**
- Group A: Cancellous bone with loose connective tissue at the interface
- Groups B & C: Thick cortical plate with extensive bone trabeculae formation

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Small group A (n=2 monkeys, 6 implants):** Statistical comparison with loaded groups is limited by low power in the control arm.
- **Single implant design:** All implants were progressive thread titanium; generalizability to other surface treatments or macro-geometries requires separate study.
- **No ISQ/RFA measurement:** Implant stability not quantified at placement or loading; primary stability metrics not reported.
- **Short observation period:** 3-month loading period may not capture long-term remodeling differences.
- **Immediate loading protocol limited to optimal occlusion cases:** Results may not generalize to poor primary stability or parafunctional loading scenarios.
- **Animal-to-human translation:** Non-human primate models approximate human bone biology well but direct clinical extrapolation requires human RCT confirmation.

## 7. Glossary

- **BIC (Bone-to-Implant Contact, 골-임플란트 접촉률):** Percentage of implant surface in direct contact with mineralized bone; primary histomorphometric index of osseointegration quality.
- **BA (Bone Area, 골면적):** Area of mineralized bone within implant threads or defined peri-implant zone; supplementary histomorphometric measure.
- **Progressive thread design (점진적 나사산 디자인):** Implant macro-geometry with varying thread pitch/depth to improve primary stability and stress distribution.
- **Osseointegration (골유착):** Direct structural and functional connection between living bone and implant surface without intervening connective tissue.
- **Immediate loading (즉시 기능 부하):** Functional occlusal loading within 48–72 h of implant placement, before conventional healing.
- **Delayed loading (지연 부하):** Loading applied after a conventional submerged healing period (typically 3–6 months).
- **Submerged healing (매몰 치유):** Two-stage protocol where the implant is covered by mucosa and isolated from the oral environment during initial osseointegration.
- **Macaca fascicularis:** Crab-eating macaque; mandibular bone composition closely approximates that of humans, making it a preferred model for implant research.
