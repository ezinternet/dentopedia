## Why Ingested

[[wiki/endodontics/vpt/fasoulas-2023-pulpotomy-direct-pulp-capping-sr-ma]]의 재료 비교를, 본 Frontiers in Dental Medicine (Elmsmari 2026)는 Bayesian network meta-analysis + CINeMA 확실성 등급으로 확장해 "어떤 비교가 high-certainty인가"라는 의사결정 차원을 추가한다 — anchor의 효과 비교를 certainty-graded, modality·시점별 의사결정 근거로 확장.

## One-line Summary

Bayesian network meta-analysis (35 RCTs; 24 in mature permanent teeth, 2,906 teeth) finding VPT success generally >93% across modalities, with hydraulic calcium-silicate cements (MTA, Biodentine) beating Ca(OH)2 (high-certainty for MTA, moderate for Biodentine) but no clear difference between silicate cements.

## 한줄요약

35편 RCT(성숙영구치 24편, 2,906개 치아)의 베이지안 네트워크 메타분석으로, VPT 성공률은 modality 전반 대체로 >93%였고, 수경성 규산칼슘시멘트(MTA·Biodentine)가 Ca(OH)2보다 우월(MTA는 high-certainty, Biodentine은 moderate)했으나 규산칼슘시멘트 간 차이는 명확하지 않았다.

## 1. Document Information

- Journal: Frontiers in Dental Medicine, 2026;7:1780755 (published 2026-04-20)
- DOI: 10.3389/fdmed.2026.1780755
- Design: Systematic review with Bayesian network meta-analysis (PRISMA 2020 / PRISMA-NMA); PROSPERO CRD420251074110
- Search: six databases; CINeMA certainty grading
- Included: 35 RCTs (2,906 teeth); 24 RCTs in mature/permanent teeth

## 2. Key Contributions

- Adds CINeMA-graded certainty to a network meta-analysis — "decision-ready" framing distinguishing high- vs low-certainty contrasts.
- Confirms uniformly high VPT success (>93%) across FP/PP/DPC/IPC modalities.
- Explicitly addresses three clinical axes (diagnostic frame, apex maturity, restoration interface) shaping prognosis.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PRISMA 2020 / PRISMA-NMA; arm-based Bayesian random-effects NMA + frequentist sensitivity (REML–Hartung–Knapp).
- RCTs evaluating full pulpotomy (FP), partial pulpotomy (PP), direct pulp capping (DPC), indirect pulp capping (IPC).
- Primary outcome: composite (clinical + radiographic) success at 6/12/24/36 months.
- Certainty graded by CINeMA; network coherence p>0.10; heterogeneity τ²=0.09 (low).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Primary analysis 35 RCTs / 2,906 teeth; 24 RCTs mature permanent teeth.
- Success across all VPT types/time points generally **>93%**.
- Hydraulic calcium-silicate (MTA & Biodentine) > Ca(OH)2 in DPC and partial pulpotomy.
- Certainty: **high** for MTA vs Ca(OH)2; **moderate** for Biodentine vs Ca(OH)2.
- Between silicate cements (e.g., MTA vs Biodentine): credible intervals overlap, moderate certainty — no explicit difference.
- Adjunctive therapies (PRP, laser): promising but low-certainty (sparse network).
- Network coherence met (p>0.10); τ²=0.09.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Silicate-vs-silicate contrasts imprecise (overlapping credible intervals).
- Adjunctive-therapy evidence sparse/low-certainty.
- Inconsistent reporting of moderators (diagnosis, tooth type, apex status, hemostasis); durability beyond 1 year less documented.

## 6. Related Work

- Extends pulpotomy/DPC material SR+MA (Fasoulas 2023) with certainty grading.
- Converges with prior VPT network meta-analyses (Komora 2024) and SR+MA (Pusa 2025): MTA > Ca(OH)2, MTA ≈ Biodentine.
