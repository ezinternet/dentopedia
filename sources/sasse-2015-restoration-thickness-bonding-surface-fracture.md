## Why Ingested

기존 [[wiki/veneers/hajeer-2026-lithium-disilicate-zirconia-veneer-retention-sr-ma]]는 접착 강도 측면에서 법랑질 접착이 복합레진 코어보다 우수하다는 SR+MA 결과를 제공했지만, 교합면 비니어의 최소 두께 기준에 대한 in-vitro 근거는 제시하지 않았다. 본 연구(Sasse 2015)는 0.3–0.7 mm 협착부터 0.6–1.0 mm 교두까지 세 가지 두께와 세 가지 접착 기질(법랑질 단독, 법랑질+상아질, 법랑질+복합레진)을 교두 시뮬레이터 600,000 사이클 동안 직접 비교하여 임상 최소 두께 권고값(0.7–1.0 mm)의 in-vitro 근거를 제공한다.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro study (n=72 molars, 9 groups) of IPS e.max CAD occlusal veneers: only the thickest group (0.7 mm fissure / 1.0 mm cusp) survived all 600,000 chewing cycles undamaged; minimum thickness of 0.7–1.0 mm recommended.

## 한줄요약

In-vitro 연구 (n=72 구치, 9개 군): IPS e.max CAD 교합면 비니어 (리튬 디실리케이트 세라믹, Lithium Disilicate Ceramic, LDS)에서 두꺼운 군(협착부 0.7 mm/교두 1.0 mm)만 교합 시뮬레이터 600,000 사이클을 무손상 완주; 최소 두께 0.7–1.0 mm 권고.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Dental Materials (Elsevier)
- **Publication Date**: August 2015
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.dental.2015.04.017
- **Study Type**: In-vitro laboratory study
- **Sample Size**: 72 extracted human molars (3 groups × 8 subgroups × 3 thickness levels, N=24 per main group)
- **Funding/COI**: Materials provided free of charge by Ivoclar Vivadent; restorations manufactured free by Absolute Ceramics/Biodentis GmbH

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Established that 0.7 mm (fissure) / 1.0 mm (cusp) is the minimum viable thickness for full-coverage occlusal LDS veneers under masticatory simulation.
2. Demonstrated that bonding to dentin or composite resin provides significantly higher fracture resistance than enamel-only bonding when ceramic thickness is reduced.
3. Showed that only the thickest subgroup achieved 100% survival through 600,000 loading cycles without any macroscopic damage; thinner groups had survival rates of 50–100% (with some damage) and 12.5–75% (without damage).
4. Median final fracture loads ranged 610–3390 N across groups, with thickness being the most influential variable.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Specimen Groups (3 main × 3 thickness subgroups)**:
- Group 1 (Enamel only): Preparation entirely within enamel
- Group 2 (Enamel + Dentin): Preparation involving enamel and dentin
- Group 3 (Enamel + Composite): Preparation into an existing occlusal composite filling

**Thickness Subgroups**:
- Thin: 0.3 mm fissure / 0.6 mm cusp
- Medium: 0.5 mm fissure / 0.8 mm cusp
- Thick: 0.7 mm fissure / 1.0 mm cusp

**Restoration Material**: IPS e.max CAD (lithium disilicate glass-ceramic, Ivoclar Vivadent)

**Adhesive Protocol**:
- Ceramic etched with 5% HF acid + silanation
- Self-etching primer (Multilink Primer A/B) + composite luting resin (Multilink Automix, Ivoclar Vivadent)

**Aging Protocol**:
- Water storage at 37°C for 3 days
- Thermal cycling: 7,500 cycles at 5–55°C
- Chewing simulator: 600,000 loading cycles at 10 kg load (combined thermal cycling)

**Fracture Testing**: Universal testing machine on unfractured specimens post-simulation

**Statistics**: Kruskal-Wallis + Wilcoxon tests with Bonferroni-Holm correction

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Thickness | Bonding Surface | Survival (no damage) | Median Fracture Load |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0.7/1.0 mm (thick) | All groups | 100% | 3200–3390 N |
| 0.5/0.8 mm (medium) | Enamel only | ~50% | ~610 N |
| 0.5/0.8 mm (medium) | Dentin/composite | ~75–87.5% | ~1800–2200 N |
| 0.3/0.6 mm (thin) | Enamel only | 12.5% | ~610 N |
| 0.3/0.6 mm (thin) | Dentin/composite | ~50% | ~1200–1700 N |

- Thickness had a statistically significant influence on fracture resistance (p ≤ 0.05)
- In thinner groups, dentin or composite bonding was significantly stronger than enamel-only (p ≤ 0.05)
- No significant differences between dentin and composite bonding surfaces

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro study; clinical long-term outcomes may differ
- Only one ceramic material (IPS e.max CAD) tested; results may not generalize to other LDS or ceramic systems
- Chewing simulator loads (10 kg / 600,000 cycles) may not fully replicate in-vivo masticatory forces in parafunction
- Artificial periodontal membrane used (gum resin) — approximates but not equivalent to natural PDL
- Ivoclar Vivadent material sponsorship — potential industry bias
- No data on surface texture of composite resin fillings prior to preparation (may affect bond quality)

## 7. Glossary

- **Occlusal veneer**: Ceramic restoration covering the occlusal surface only; non-retentive, adhesively retained
- **IPS e.max CAD**: CAD/CAM lithium disilicate glass-ceramic block (Ivoclar Vivadent); high-strength all-ceramic
- **Self-etching primer**: Adhesive primer that conditions tooth surface without separate acid etching step; combines etchant + primer
- **Multilink Automix**: Dual-cure composite resin luting cement (Ivoclar Vivadent)
- **Masticatory simulation (chewing simulator)**: In-vitro device simulating cyclic occlusal loading; 600,000 cycles at 10 kg ≈ roughly 2 years of clinical use
- **Thermal cycling**: Alternating hot/cold water baths to simulate oral thermal stresses and accelerate material aging
