## Why Ingested

[[wiki/prosthetic-materials/tiu-2015-clinical-tooth-preparations-measuring-sr]] established the ideal-preparation benchmarks (TOC, occluso-cervical height, finish line) but stopped short of quantifying how far real-world clinician preparations stray from them for the now-dominant monolithic zirconia crown. This 2021 cross-sectional STL audit fills that currency/applicability gap by measuring 392 actual lab-submitted preparations against the textbook standards Tiu et al. catalogued, reinforcing the recurring conclusion that preparation guidelines are widely missed in practice.

## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional STL audit (n=392 posterior preparations from 3 labs) found most monolithic zirconia preparations exceeded 20° TOC and >50% had undercut, unsupported enamel, and/or unacceptable finish-line quality.

## 한줄요약

392개 후방치아 모놀리식 지르코니아 크라운 프렙(STL 파일) 단면조사: 대부분 총교합수렴각(TOC) 20° 초과, 50% 이상에서 언더컷·미지지 법랑질·부적합 변연선 품질이 확인되어 임상 프렙이 이상 기준에서 크게 벗어남.

## 1. Document Information

- Type: Cross-sectional in vitro/observational digital model audit
- Published: Dent. J. 2021, 9, 112 (MDPI), 27 Sept 2021
- DOI: 10.3390/dj9100112
- Setting: 3 of 19 contacted CAD-CAM dental laboratories, Buffalo NY, USA
- Sample: 392 STL files of posterior tooth preparations submitted for monolithic zirconia crowns, 1 May–1 June 2017
- Assessment: Two calibrated evaluators using 3Shape 3D Viewer; descriptive analysis

## 2. Key Contributions

- First quantification of adherence to preparation principles specifically for monolithic zirconia crowns (prior work covered lithium disilicate / generic zirconia).
- Documents the gap between recommended preparation geometry (TOC 10–20°, occluso-cervical ≥4 mm molars / ≥3 mm premolars) and what clinicians actually submit.
- Confirms CAD-CAM–specific requirements (rounded line angle, smooth finish line) are frequently unmet.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Inclusion: posterior preparations for monolithic zirconia (intraoral or desktop scanned). Exclusion: anterior teeth, layered zirconia, other materials.
- Parameters: finish line design (chamfer/shoulder/modified shoulder/feather edge); finish line width (averaged over 8 locations); occluso-cervical dimension; total occlusal convergence (TOC); intercuspal angulation; finish line quality (rough/irregular/stepped); line angle form; presence/absence of undercut and unsupported enamel lip.
- TOC and occluso-cervical dimension measured on sectioned STL files; analyzed by tooth location.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Occluso-cervical dimension below the recommended threshold (<3 mm premolars / <4 mm molars): 39% of premolars, 77% of first molars, 91% of second molars.
- Most preparations had TOC >20° (exceeding the recommended 10–20°).
- >50% of preparations had undercut, unsupported enamel, and/or unacceptable finish-line quality.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single metropolitan area, only 3 of 19 labs participated → selection/generalizability limits.
- Purely descriptive (no inferential statistics, no clinical outcome linkage).
- One-month sampling window; cannot capture operator-level variability over time.
- Authors call for evaluation of preparation quality at the lab-intake stage as a quality gate.

## 6. Related Work

- Tiu et al. 2015 SR — ideal preparation benchmarks and measuring methods.
- Prior preparation-quality audits for lithium disilicate / CAD-CAM crowns (refs 13,14,43–45).
- Studies on TOC effect on fracture resistance and internal adaptation of CAD-CAM crowns (refs 15,16).

## 7. Glossary

- TOC (Total Occlusal Convergence): angle between two opposing axial walls of a preparation; governs retention/resistance.
- Occluso-cervical dimension: vertical preparation height from finish line to occlusal surface.
- Unsupported enamel lip: enamel margin lacking dentin support, prone to fracture.
- STL: Standard Tessellation Language; mesh file format for 3D scanned preparations.
