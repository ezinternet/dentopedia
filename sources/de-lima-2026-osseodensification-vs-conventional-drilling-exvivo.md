## Why Ingested

The versah-protocols category needed a direct head-to-head comparison of the reference Densah®/Versah kit against a Brazilian-made competitor (Bone Reamer Drills®/WF) on the same outcome measures (insertion torque, removal torque, ISQ, temperature, SEM). This ex vivo study fills the gap and extends [[wiki/implants/versah-protocols/moghaddas-2025-osseodensification-standard-drilling-isq-itv]] by adding a second densification system into the comparison and qualifying *which* outcome metric (torque vs ISQ) actually separates osseodensification from conventional drilling in type IV bone.

## One-line Summary

Paired ex vivo study (n=16 bovine rib blocks, 48 osteotomies) found both osseodensification kits (Densah®/Versah and Bone Reamer Drills®/WF) produced significantly higher insertion and removal torque than conventional drilling in type IV bone, while ISQ and heat generation did not differ significantly.

## 한줄요약

소 늑골 블록(n=16)을 이용한 ex vivo 쌍대 비교 연구에서 골밀도화 (Osseodensification, OD) 두 키트(Densah®/Versah, Bone Reamer Drills®/WF) 모두 기존 드릴링 대비 삽입·제거 토크가 유의하게 높았으나, 임플란트 안정성 지수 (Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ) 및 온도는 세 군 간 유의한 차이 없었음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: PLOS ONE
- **Published**: January 5, 2026
- **Study type**: Paired comparative ex vivo study
- **Sample**: 16 bovine rib bone blocks; 3 osteotomies per block = 48 total osteotomies (16 per group)
- **Institution**: Federal University of Minas Gerais (UFMG), Belo Horizonte, Brazil; Federal University of Vales do Jequitinhonha e Mucuri, Diamantina, Brazil
- **Groups**:
  - G1: Conventional (Biomorse® burs, clockwise)
  - G2: Osseodensification with Densah®/Versah (VS) burs, counterclockwise + oscillating pumping
  - G3: Osseodensification with Bone Reamer Drills®/WF burs, counterclockwise + oscillating bombardment

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First direct comparison of Densah®/Versah vs. a Brazilian-manufactured densification kit (WF) using standardized ex vivo bovine bone model.
2. Demonstrates that both OD systems improve primary stability via torque (insertion and removal) but do NOT significantly improve ISQ over conventional drilling in type IV bone.
3. SEM analysis confirms bone compaction at the implant interface for OD groups; WF burs showed more extensive surface wear compared to Densah® burs.
4. Establishes that neither OD kit generates dangerous heat increases above the conventional technique threshold.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Bone model**: Bovine rib blocks (50×12×30 mm), stored at −18 to −20°C, thawed 5h at room temperature, fixed in bench vise.
- **Implants**: Biomorse® 4×10 mm (Bio Implante, Bauru) — same surface treatment and shape across all groups.
- **Torque measurement**: Lutron TQ-8800 digital torque meter (accuracy 0.1 Ncm); insertion torque at final seating position, removal torque = peak unscrewing value.
- **ISQ/RFA**: Osstell® with SmartPeg type 01; 4 measurements per implant (distal, vestibular, mesial, lingual faces); probe at 45° angle, 2 mm from SmartPeg.
- **Temperature**: Thermocouple inserted 1 mm lateral from final osteotomy diameter edge; drilling depth standardized at 5 mm; all under 0.9% saline at 4–6°C.
- **SEM**: FEI Quanta 200 FEG at 40×, 100×, 500×, 1000×; burs before/after use; bone beds after implant removal.
- **Mass loss**: Analytical balance (accuracy 0.001 g) weighing OD burs pre/post drilling.
- **Statistics**: Repeated-measures ANOVA with Bonferroni post-hoc for continuous outcomes; Shapiro-Wilk normality test; paired t-test for mass loss; α = 0.05 (SPSS v21).
- **Sample size**: OpenEpi v3.01; n=16 per group (power 0.80, α = 0.05), based on Barberá-Millán 2021 methodology.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Insertion Torque (Ncm, mean ± SD)
| Group | Mean ± SD |
|---|---|
| G1 Conventional | 61.25 ± 29.6 |
| G2 VS (Densah®) | 77.62 ± 31.6 |
| G3 WF (Bone Reamer) | **95.25 ± 45.8** |

Pairwise: G1 vs G2 p=0.018; G1 vs G3 p=0.004; G2 vs G3 p=0.016. Overall p=0.007. WF outperformed Versah on insertion torque.

### Removal Torque (Ncm, mean ± SD)
| Group | Mean ± SD |
|---|---|
| G1 Conventional | 58.18 ± 32.7 |
| G2 VS | 69.62 ± 35.6 |
| G3 WF | **89.62 ± 48.4** |

Pairwise: G1 vs G2 p=0.043; G1 vs G3 p=0.001; G2 vs G3 p=0.009. Overall p=0.008.

### ISQ (mean ± SD)
| Group | Mean ± SD |
|---|---|
| G1 Conventional | 79.42 ± 6.81 |
| G2 VS | 82.40 ± 4.03 |
| G3 WF | 80.68 ± 5.00 |

No significant difference (p=0.157). All groups in high-stability range (ISQ ~80).

### Maximum Temperature (°C, mean ± SD)
| Group | Mean ± SD |
|---|---|
| G1 Conventional | 27.81 ± 2.94 |
| G2 VS | 30.62 ± 4.61 |
| G3 WF | 28.37 ± 2.68 |

No significant difference (p=0.087). All well below the 47°C bone necrosis threshold.

### Mass Loss of OD Burs
No significant mass loss for either VS (p=0.667) or WF burs (p=0.208). Mean mass changes: VS 0.333 mg ± 1.155; WF 0.600 mg ± 0.894.

### SEM Findings
- G2 (Versah): bone bed showed more crack propagation compared to G3; Densah® burs had smoother surfaces suggesting surface coating/treatment on active tip; slight structural loss at VT2838 tip-body interface after use.
- G3 (WF): more extensive bur surface irregularities and wear vs Densah®; machining lines visible pre-use; different manufacturing process.
- Both OD groups: compacted bone microparticles visible at implant interface vs conventional.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Ex vivo bovine bone does not replicate human oral surgical conditions (blood supply, healing response, patient variables).
- Type IV bone only — results may not extrapolate to higher-density bone types where OD benefit may differ.
- Thermocouple captures local temperature 1 mm from osteotomy; does not detect overall thermal profile or heat leakage.
- Single implant system (Biomorse® 4×10 mm) — different implant macrogeometries may yield different torque profiles.
- SEM evaluated only one cutting surface per bur; gross deformation on other surfaces cannot be excluded.
- No clinical follow-up — ISQ parity post-insertion does not address long-term osseointegration outcomes or bone remodeling.
- Authors note need for clinical studies to confirm ex vivo findings.

## 7. Glossary

- **골밀도화 (Osseodensification, OD)**: Bone site preparation technique using counterclockwise-rotating densification burs to compact, rather than excavate, bone microparticles into the osteotomy wall.
- **삽입 토크 (Insertion Torque, IT)**: Rotational force required to seat an implant to final position; proxy for primary stability.
- **제거 토크 (Removal Torque, RT)**: Maximum rotational force required to unscrew an implant from bone; reflects mechanical interlocking.
- **임플란트 안정성 지수 (Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ)**: 1–100 scale from resonance frequency analysis (RFA); ≥65 generally considered stable for loading.
- **SEM (주사전자현미경, Scanning Electron Microscopy)**: High-magnification surface imaging of bur wear and bone compaction morphology.
- **Type IV bone**: Softest bone density classification (Lekholm & Zarb); thin cortex, very low-density trabecular core — scenario where OD benefit is hypothetically greatest.
