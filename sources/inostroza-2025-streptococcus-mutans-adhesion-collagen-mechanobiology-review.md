## Why Ingested

Mechanism-level review of how *Streptococcus mutans* — the keystone cariogenic bacterium — adheres to host tissue via collagen-binding proteins (CBPs: SpaP, WapA, Cnm, Cbm), providing the molecular and mechanobiological basis of cariogenic biofilm initiation on dentin/ECM and identifying CBP–collagen bonds as a target for anti-biofilm caries prevention. Extends the existing *S. mutans* virulence coverage in [[wiki/oral-microbiology/bowen-2011-streptococcus-mutans-glucosyltransferases]] (Gtf/EPS-matrix axis) toward the upstream adhesin/attachment axis measured by single-molecule and single-cell force spectroscopy.

## One-line Summary

Narrative review (Crit Rev Microbiol 2025) of *S. mutans* collagen-binding proteins (SpaP, WapA, Cnm, Cbm) and their mechanobiology, characterized by AFM single-molecule (SMFS) and single-cell (SCFS) force spectroscopy, as drivers of dentin/ECM adhesion, coaggregation, virulence, and as targets for anti-biofilm caries prevention.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Synthesizes recent evidence on the mechanical properties of the four relevant *S. mutans* collagen-binding proteins (CBPs): **SpaP, WapA, Cnm, Cbm**.
- Frames CBP–collagen attachment as a **mechanobiological** process — bond behavior under physiological force — rather than a static binding event.
- Positions **AFM-based single-molecule force spectroscopy (SMFS)** and **single-cell force spectroscopy (SCFS)** as the key tools that characterize this attachment in real time.
- Proposes SMFS/SCFS as a screening route for **novel anti-biofilm molecules** against *S. mutans* for caries prevention.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Narrative review. Methodological focus is on atomic force microscopy (AFM) force-spectroscopy approaches:
- **SMFS (single-molecule force spectroscopy)** — measures the rupture/mechanical behavior of individual CBP–collagen bonds.
- **SCFS (single-cell force spectroscopy)** — measures whole-cell *S. mutans* attachment to collagen substrates under physiological conditions in real time.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Abstract-level only (no quantitative force values retrieved):
- CBPs mediate *S. mutans* attachment to **dentin**, **bacterial coaggregation**, and **extracellular matrix (ECM) invasion**.
- The mechanobiological/adhesive characteristics of SpaP, WapA, Cnm, and Cbm contribute to **virulence toward the host**.
- Understanding CBP–collagen mechanics is presented as crucial for preventing biofilm formation in oral **and remote** tissues (systemic dissemination context).

## 7. Glossary

- **CBP** — collagen-binding protein.
- **SpaP / WapA / Cnm / Cbm** — *S. mutans* surface adhesins; Cnm and Cbm are the classic collagen-binding adhesins.
- **SMFS** — single-molecule force spectroscopy (AFM).
- **SCFS** — single-cell force spectroscopy (AFM).
- **Mechanobiology** — study of how mechanical forces regulate biological/adhesive behavior.
