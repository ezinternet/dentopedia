## Why Ingested

바늘없는/압력제어 침윤마취기(computer-controlled delivery system) 관련 질의 맥락에서, WANDSTA를 이용한 single tooth anesthesia (STA, intra-ligamentary injection)가 매복 제3대구치 외과적 발치에서 전통적 IANB의 대안이 될 수 있는지 확인하기 위해 인제스트. IANB 실패기전을 다루는 기존 [[local-anesthesia/malamed-2011-mandibular-nerve-block-passe]], [[local-anesthesia/haas-2011-alternative-mandibular-nerve-block-techniques]]과 대비되는, computer-controlled intraligamentary system 기반의 대안 주사 기법 비교 RCT로서 근거를 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

RCT (n=60) comparing WANDSTA computer-controlled single tooth anesthesia (intra-ligamentary, 4% articaine) versus conventional IANB (4% articaine) for surgical removal of impacted mandibular third molars: STA had faster onset (2.2 ± 0.25 min difference, p<0.05) and lower postoperative pain/trismus, but required more supplemental long buccal nerve blocks (50% vs 23.3%) and showed higher intra-operative VAS during tooth elevation.

## 한줄요약

RCT(n=60): 매복 하악 제3대구치 외과적 발치에서 WANDSTA 컴퓨터 제어 단일치아마취(치주인대내 주사, 4% articaine)가 전통적 IANB(4% articaine) 대비 발현 시간이 2.2(±0.25)분 더 빠르고(p<0.05) 술후 통증·개구제한은 낮았으나, 장협신경 추가블록 필요율이 더 높았고(50% vs 23.3%) 발치 시 VAS는 더 높았음.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Direct head-to-head comparison of a computer-controlled intra-ligamentary delivery system (WANDSTA, single tooth anesthesia) against conventional IANB for a surgical (not just restorative) indication — impacted third molar removal.
- Quantifies onset-of-action difference between STA and IANB (2.2 ± 0.25 min, statistically significant).
- Reports supplemental-injection burden by technique: STA required additional lingual block in 6.7% and long buccal nerve block in 50% of cases; IANB required repeat long buccal block in 23.3%.
- Documents postoperative outcomes (pain, trismus) at 24 h, favoring the STA group.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Randomized comparative study, two parallel groups.
- **Sample**: 60 patients undergoing surgical removal of impacted mandibular third molars, randomized 1:1.
  - Group I (study, n=30): STA via WANDSTA computer-controlled intra-ligamentary injection system, 4% articaine.
  - Group II (control, n=30): Conventional inferior alveolar nerve block (IANB), 4% articaine.
- **Outcomes assessed**: onset of action, anesthesia failure, intra-operative pain (VAS, including specifically during tooth elevation), need for supplemental injections (lingual block, long buccal nerve block), incidence of lip numbness, postoperative pain and trismus at 24 h.
- Abstract does not specify blinding, randomization method, or statistical tests beyond significance thresholds — full methodological detail unavailable without full text.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Onset of action**: STA vs IANB mean difference 2.2 (± 0.25) minutes, statistically significant (p<0.05) — direction implied faster onset for STA per abstract framing.
- **Intra-operative VAS**: significant difference only during tooth elevation, with Group I (STA) reporting *higher* VAS than Group II (IANB) at that step.
- **Supplemental injections**:
  - Group I (STA): lingual block needed in 6.7%; long buccal nerve block needed in 50%.
  - Group II (IANB): long buccal nerve block repetition needed in 23.3%.
- **Postoperative outcomes**: pain and trismus were higher in Group II (IANB) than Group I (STA).
- **Overall conclusion (authors')**: despite some limitations in extent of anesthesia achieved, WANDSTA STA achieved adequate anesthesia for third molar surgical removal and is a viable alternative, particularly where nerve blocks are contraindicated by systemic conditions.

## 7. Glossary

- **STA (Single Tooth Anesthesia)**: A computer-controlled intra-ligamentary (periodontal ligament) injection technique/system intended to anesthetize a single tooth with minimal soft-tissue numbness.
- **WANDSTA**: A specific computer-controlled anesthetic delivery device/system used to administer STA.
- **IANB (Inferior Alveolar Nerve Block)**: The conventional regional block technique anesthetizing the inferior alveolar nerve, producing wider mandibular quadrant anesthesia including lip/soft-tissue numbness.
- **Long buccal nerve block**: Supplemental injection anesthetizing the buccal soft tissue/periosteum adjacent to mandibular molars, often needed to supplement IANB or STA for surgical procedures involving buccal flap manipulation.
- **VAS (Visual Analog Scale)**: Patient-reported pain intensity scale used intra- and post-operatively.
- **Trismus**: Restricted mouth opening, commonly assessed as a postoperative morbidity measure after third molar surgery.
