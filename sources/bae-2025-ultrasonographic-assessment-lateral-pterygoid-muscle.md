## One-line Summary
Cross-sectional ultrasonographic anatomy study (120 hemifaces from 60 healthy Korean female volunteers) establishing depth and surface-landmark coordinates of the lateral pterygoid muscle (LPM) for safe extraoral BoNT-A injection through the mandibular-notch "window".

## 1. Document Information
- Study type: Cross-sectional anatomical / ultrasound imaging study (Communication)
- Setting: Yonsei University College of Dentistry, Seoul, Republic of Korea
- Ethics: IRB Yonsei University Dental Hospital (IRB no. 2-2023-0050); Helsinki Declaration
- Funding: Yonsei University College of Dentistry
- Population: Healthy Korean female volunteers (n=60); 120 hemifaces

## 2. Key Contributions
1. Establishes a **practical extraoral US-guided injection protocol** for the LPM via the mandibular-notch window with a 15 MHz linear transducer.
2. Defines and quantifies three reproducible landmarks relative to the ala-tragus line: **CorP** (coronoid process), **ConP** (lateral condylar process), **MP** (midpoint).
3. Reports muscle-layer depths at the MP for needle planning: skin+subcutaneous 9.7 ± 1.0 mm, masseter 10.3 ± 1.3 mm, temporalis 10.9 ± 1.6 mm, **LPM depth 30.9 ± 1.9 mm**.
4. Demonstrates that masseter, temporalis, and LPM can be visualized in **all 60 subjects (100%)** via the mandibular-notch window — confirming the feasibility of US guidance.
5. Provides Doppler-compatible, real-time, radiation-free alternative to CT/MRI guidance for LPM injection — relevant to repeated BoNT-A treatment regimens.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 60 healthy Korean female volunteers, mean age 36.7 ± 3.5 y; 120 hemifaces total.
- Exclusions: TMJ disorder, obvious facial asymmetry, prior plastic surgery, BoNT injection within the preceding 6 months.
- Equipment: real-time 2D B-mode US, 15 MHz high-frequency linear transducer (E-CUBE 15 PLATINUM, ALPINION, Seoul).
- Setup:
  - Skin landmarks and reference lines pre-marked with waterproof eyeliner.
  - Generous US gel layer to avoid tissue compression.
  - Subject seated upright; mouth opened 2 cm to facilitate mandibular-notch access.
- Imaging protocol:
  1. Transducer perpendicular to skin, then aligned/rotated until both coronoid and condylar processes were on the same plane.
  2. US image captured; transducer position recorded; side-view photograph taken.
  3. Three reference points defined relative to ala-tragus line:
     - CorP — coronoid process location.
     - ConP — condylar process location.
     - MP — midpoint between CorP and ConP.
  4. Measurements at MP (ImageJ):
     - Skin + subcutaneous layer thickness.
     - Masseter muscle thickness.
     - Temporalis muscle thickness.
     - LPM depth (skin → LPM at MP).
- Anatomical order (superficial → deep): masseter → temporalis → LPM, observed in 100% of cases.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Layer thicknesses / depth at MP (mean ± SD)
| Layer | Thickness/Depth (mm) |
|---|---|
| Skin + subcutaneous | 9.7 ± 1.0 |
| Masseter | 10.3 ± 1.3 |
| Temporalis | 10.9 ± 1.6 |
| LPM depth (from skin) | **30.9 ± 1.9** |

### Landmark locations relative to ala-tragus line (mean ± SD)
| Landmark | Anterior to tragus (mm) | Inferior to ala-tragus line (mm) |
|---|---|---|
| CorP | 54.0 ± 3.9 | 10.7 ± 2.8 |
| MP   | 39.6 ± 3.3 | 7.8 ± 1.6 |
| ConP | 25.2 ± 3.0 | 4.9 ± 1.9 |

### Practical takeaway
The mandibular-notch window is **39.6 ± 3.3 mm anterior and 7.8 ± 1.6 mm inferior to the tragus**, with the LPM lying ~31 mm deep beneath the skin. A 35–40 mm 22-G needle can therefore reach the LPM center safely via this corridor under US guidance.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Korean female-only sample — sex differences and ethnicity-related variation in fossa size and overlying tissue thickness are not addressed.
- Healthy volunteers (TMD-excluded) — depths in TMD patients with hypertrophic or atrophic LPM, asymmetric anatomy, or facial swelling may differ.
- Mean age 36.7 y; pediatric and elderly anatomical variations not measured.
- No measurement of the maxillary artery or pterygoid venous plexus locations within the field, despite Doppler capability being mentioned — this is a follow-up safety question.
- LPM was not subdivided into superior vs inferior heads; the trial-relevant question of selective head injection is unaddressed.
- Cross-sectional snapshot only; no validation in actual injection cases (no clinical accuracy data).
- 15 MHz transducer may not be available in all clinics — feasibility with lower-frequency probes not assessed.
- Static (mouth 2 cm open) measurements only; dynamic LPM behavior during protrusion/lateroduction not captured.

## 7. Glossary
- **LPM** — Lateral Pterygoid Muscle (외측익돌근); deep masticatory muscle in the infratemporal fossa with two heads; upper head fibers continuous with TMJ disc/capsule.
- **CorP / ConP / MP** — Coronoid Process / Condylar Process / Midpoint between them — surface-landmark coordinates established in this paper.
- **Ala-tragus line** — anatomical reference line from the ala of the nose to the tragus of the ear; standard maxillofacial landmark.
- **Mandibular notch (sigmoid notch)** — concavity between coronoid and condylar processes; the "acoustic window" used for US/extraoral injection of LPM.
- **B-mode US** — brightness-mode 2D real-time ultrasonography.
- **Doppler mode** — US mode visualizing blood flow direction/velocity; useful to identify and avoid maxillary artery and pterygoid plexus during LPM injection.
- **TMD** — Temporomandibular Disorder.
- **BoNT-A** — Botulinum Toxin Type A.
- **Infratemporal fossa** — anatomical space inferior and deep to the zygomatic arch containing LPM, medial pterygoid, maxillary artery, pterygoid venous plexus, mandibular nerve branches.
