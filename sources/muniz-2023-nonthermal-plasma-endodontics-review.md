## Why Ingested

비전통적 근관 소독 보조 수단으로서 비열 대기압 플라즈마(Non-Thermal Atmospheric Pressure Plasma, NTPP)의 근관치료 적용 가능성을 체계적으로 개관한 리뷰. 기존 NaOCl/CHX 세정의 한계를 NTPP의 ROS/RNS 메커니즘으로 보완하는 근거를 제공하며, [[wiki/endodontics/irrigation]] 및 미래 복합 프로토콜과의 접점을 형성한다.

## One-line Summary

Integrative review (17 studies, 2007–2022) demonstrating non-thermal atmospheric pressure plasma's antimicrobial efficacy against endodontic pathogens (especially E. faecalis), with optimal effects at ≥8 min direct exposure, enhanced when combined with conventional irrigants.

## 한줄요약

2007–2022년 17편 통합 리뷰: 비열 대기압 플라즈마(NTPP)는 E. faecalis 등 근관 병원균에 강한 살균력(≥8분 직접 노출에서 최대 효과)을 보이며, 기존 세정제와 병용 시 상승 효과가 확인됨.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Integrative literature review
- **Journal**: Biomedicines 2023, 11, 1401
- **Institution**: São Paulo State University (UNESP), São José dos Campos, Brazil
- **Included studies**: 17 manuscripts (2007–2022); in vitro and ex vivo; 15 direct exposure, 2 indirect (plasma-activated liquid)
- **Databases searched**: Science Direct, Lilacs, PubMed, Ebsco

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First integrative review specifically mapping NTPP device types (DBD, plasma jet) and parameters (gas, distance, exposure time) to endodontic antimicrobial outcomes.
2. Establishes ≥8 minutes of direct plasma exposure as the threshold for maximum antimicrobial effect.
3. Identifies plasma-activated liquid (PAL/PAW) as an emerging indirect delivery route with clinical promise but limited data.
4. Documents synergistic efficacy when NTPP is combined with NaOCl or CHX — same antibacterial endpoint achievable with shorter plasma exposure times.
5. Highlights safety concerns: cytotoxicity at prolonged exposures; need for standardized protocols before clinical adoption.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Study design**: Integrative literature review; keyword search (Nonthermal Plasma, Endodontics, Cold Plasma, Treatment) across 4 databases; 279 papers screened → 17 included after criteria filtering.

**NTPP device types covered**:
- **Dielectric Barrier Discharge (DBD)**: high-voltage discharge between two electrodes, one coated with dielectric material; produces diffuse, large-area plasma.
- **Plasma jet (OAUGDP / kINPen type)**: flowing noble gas (He, Ar) excited by RF or kHz voltage; generates directed cold plasma plume, suitable for root canal geometry.
- **Microwave plasma jets**: less common; mentioned in passing.

**Exposure modes**:
- *Direct*: plasma plume applied in contact with or proximate to root canal substrate (dentinal wall, biofilm).
- *Indirect*: NaOCl, saline, or distilled water pre-activated by NTPP then used as irrigant (PAL).

**Key variables assessed**: working gas type (He, Ar, air, N₂), flow rate, distance (apparatus-to-substrate), treatment duration, combination with irrigants, biofilm age/maturity.

**Target microorganisms**: E. faecalis (primary), C. albicans, multispecies biofilm models.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Endpoint | Finding |
|---|---|
| E. faecalis planktonic killing | Significant reduction across all direct-plasma studies |
| E. faecalis biofilm | 4–6 log reduction possible; older/thicker biofilm more resistant |
| C. albicans | Notable antifungal effect; comparable to antibacterial response |
| Exposure time threshold | ≥8 min for maximum antimicrobial effect in direct mode |
| Plasma + NaOCl combination | Superior to either alone; shorter plasma time needed |
| Plasma + CHX combination | Similar synergy; reduced exposure requirement |
| Plasma-activated liquid (PAL) | Only 2 studies; promising but insufficient for conclusions |
| Cytotoxicity | Concentration/time-dependent; short exposures generally biocompatible |
| Dentinal tubule penetration | Limited data; direct plasma less effective in tubules vs. canal surface |

**Reactive species implicated**: ROS (H₂O₂, ·OH, O₃, ¹O₂) and RNS (NO, NO₂, ONOO⁻) — damage microbial membranes, DNA, and proteins; do not induce resistance unlike antibiotics.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **No standardization**: wide variation in device type, gas, distance, power density, treatment time across studies — direct comparison impossible.
- **Mostly in vitro / ex vivo**: no in vivo (animal or human clinical) RCT data in endodontic context.
- **Few PAL studies**: indirect method has practical delivery advantages but only 2 papers evaluated.
- **Cytotoxicity boundary unclear**: maximum safe exposure duration not established for periapical tissues.
- **Canal geometry**: how to deliver plasma to apical third in narrow, curved canals remains unsolved.
- **Standardization needed** for: generator parameters, working gas, distance, exposure time, combined-protocol sequences.

## 6. Related Work

- Conventional irrigant limitations motivating NTPP research: NaOCl toxicity, CHX staining, limited dentinal tubule penetration.
- Plasma medicine in broader dentistry: implant surface decontamination, wound healing, tooth whitening — established fields.
- Photodynamic therapy (PDT) as parallel adjunct disinfection strategy (not directly compared in this review).

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| NTPP | Non-Thermal Atmospheric Pressure Plasma: ionized gas at near-room temperature and atmospheric pressure |
| DBD | Dielectric Barrier Discharge: plasma generated between two electrodes, one insulated by dielectric material |
| Plasma jet (OAUGDP) | Flowing-gas plasma plume; kHz or RF excitation; directed application |
| ROS | Reactive Oxygen Species: H₂O₂, ·OH, O₃, ¹O₂ — key antimicrobial mediators |
| RNS | Reactive Nitrogen Species: NO, NO₂, peroxynitrite — synergistic with ROS in microbial killing |
| PAL / PAW | Plasma-Activated Liquid / Water: irrigant pre-treated by plasma to carry reactive species |
| E. faecalis | Enterococcus faecalis: Gram-positive, biofilm-forming; responsible for ~80% of persistent endodontic infections |
