## Why Ingested

Bench (SEM) evidence on how adjunctive erythritol- vs glycine-based air polishing combined with 24% EDTA alter periodontitis-affected root surfaces after SRP — directly relevant to professional biofilm-management / subgingival instrumentation protocols. This applies the EFP step-2 instrumentation recommendations of [[periodontics/sanz-2020-treatment-stage-i-iii-periodontitis-efp]] at the root-surface micromorphology level (do adjuncts to SRP improve or harm the root surface?).

## One-line Summary

In-vitro SEM study (10 periodontitis-affected human teeth, 20 samples) showing SRP+EDTA leaves a continuous smear layer, adjunctive erythritol air polishing lowers instrumentation-mark scores (Se<S, p=0.001) but raises crack scores (Se>S, p=0.001), while smear-layer scores are comparable across all protocols (p=0.950) and CEJ behaves like mid-root.

## 한줄요약

치주염 발치치 10개(시편 20개) SEM 인비트로 연구 — SRP+EDTA는 연속적 smear layer를 남기고, 에리스리톨 에어폴리싱 추가군(Se)은 기구흔 점수가 더 낮지만(Se<S, p=0.001) 균열 점수는 더 높았으며(Se>S, p=0.001), smear layer 점수는 모든 프로토콜에서 차이 없음(p=0.950), CEJ는 치근 중간부와 유사하게 반응.

## 2. Key Contributions

- One of few in-vitro studies investigating the **combined** root-surface effect of SRP + 24% EDTA ± erythritol/glycine subgingival air polishing (most prior work isolated each agent).
- Uses **clinically relevant substrate**: periodontitis-affected human roots with real calculus deposits (not bovine teeth or healthy donors).
- Semi-quantitative SEM scoring (ordinal 0-3) of three outcomes: instrumentation **marks** (×100), surface **cracks** (×100), **smear layer**/residual detritus (×1000).
- Adds a CEJ-region comparison (Js group) to ask whether the anatomically challenging cementoenamel junction responds differently to instrumentation.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Samples**: 10 extracted periodontitis-affected human teeth (premolars, molars) → 20 SEM samples (~4×4×2 mm). 2 teeth as descriptive controls.
- **Groups (8 evaluations/group/parameter)**:
  - **Ct** — uninstrumented controls (descriptive).
  - **S** — SRP + 24% EDTA (mid-root).
  - **Sg** — SRP + AIR-FLOW PERIO (glycine-based, EMS) + 24% EDTA.
  - **Se** — SRP + AIR-FLOW PLUS (erythritol-based, EMS) + 24% EDTA.
  - **Js** — CEJ region, SRP + 24% EDTA.
- **SRP**: ultrasonic scaler (1S tip, Acteon) then Gracey 5/6 curettes (Hu-Friedy), modified pen grasp, 45-90° angulation, 4 strokes, light standardized force; single trained operator.
- **Air polishing**: 5 s at 5 mm working distance (per published protocols).
- **EDTA**: immersion in 24% EDTA for 2 min, then water-jet rinse.
- **SEM**: graded ethanol dehydration, gold-palladium sputter coating, 100×-15,000×, single blinded reader; two fields scored per sample (near mark + middle).
- **Statistics**: Kruskal-Wallis (S vs Se vs Sg) + Bonferroni pairwise; Mann-Whitney U (S vs Js); p<0.05. No power calculation (exploratory).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Instrumentation marks**: significant difference among S/Se/Sg (Kruskal-Wallis **H = 13.411, df = 2, p = 0.001**); **Se < S (p = 0.001)**. Sg vs S p = 0.054 (NS); Se vs Sg p = 0.646 (NS).
- **Surface cracks**: significant difference among S/Se/Sg (Kruskal-Wallis **H = 12.038, df = 2, p = 0.002**); **Se > S (p = 0.001)**. Sg vs S p = 0.314 (NS); Se vs Sg p = 0.018 (NS after Bonferroni).
- **Smear layer**: **NO significant difference** among S/Se/Sg (Kruskal-Wallis **H = 0.102, df = 2, p = 0.950**). EDTA did not eliminate the smear layer; S group showed a continuous smear layer obscuring the substrate.
- **CEJ (Js) vs mid-root (S)** (Mann-Whitney): significant **only for marks** (U = 9.000, Z = -2.604, **p = 0.009**); cracks p = 0.116 (NS), smear layer p = 0.913 (NS).
- Cementum appeared preserved in all treated groups (pebble-like morphology); no residual calculus microislands on instrumented samples; dentinal tubules not identified.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Very small n (2 teeth per group); no formal power calculation; exploratory.
- No formal intra-/inter-examiner calibration.
- Ex-vivo design; SEM specimen preparation (fixation/dehydration/coating) may itself produce cracking artifacts — authors attribute most cracks to dehydration of cementum.
- Semi-quantitative ordinal scoring is partly subjective; cannot reflect 3D surface alterations.
- Multiple samples from the same tooth introduce statistical dependence.
- Lower marks in Se may be partly masked by residual smear layer (confounding).
- Authors call for larger studies to clarify clinical relevance.

## 6. Related Work

- EFP S3 guideline on staged treatment of stage I-III periodontitis (subgingival instrumentation as step 2): [[periodontics/sanz-2020-treatment-stage-i-iii-periodontitis-efp]].
- Prior SEM/instrumentation literature: hand instrumentation produces grooves/deep marks; air polishing minimal hard-tissue damage; EDTA exposes collagen matrix by removing inorganic smear-layer component (endodontic chelation rationale).
- Cobb et al. and others reported SRP+EDTA leaving 14-18% residual calculus vs 45-53% after SRP alone.

## 7. Glossary

- **SRP** — Scaling and Root Planing (subgingival mechanical instrumentation; periodontal gold standard).
- **EDTA** — Ethylenediaminetetraacetic acid; chelating agent binding Ca²⁺ at neutral pH, removes inorganic smear-layer component.
- **Smear layer** — residual detritus (infected cementum, toxins, biofilm, calculus remnants) on the instrumented root surface; barrier to periodontal cell attachment.
- **Air polishing (GBT)** — guided biofilm therapy with low-abrasive powders (glycine, erythritol) for subgingival biofilm removal.
- **CEJ** — Cementoenamel junction.
- **SEM** — Scanning Electron Microscopy.
- **EPS** — Extracellular polymeric substance (biofilm matrix).
