## Why Ingested

Part of a focused collection on suture techniques and primary wound closure after impacted mandibular third molar surgery. This RCT pairs a small modified Szmyd V-shaped flap with the suture-vs-sutureless question, and reports a strong early-phase (24–48 h) pain/swelling/trismus advantage for sutureless — a more pronounced effect than the equivocal results of [[suture-wound-closure/takadoum-2022-sutureless-socket-technique-third-molars]] and the trismus-only benefit in [[suture-wound-closure/sen-2024-sutureless-multiple-suture-third-molar-inflammation]].

## One-line Summary
RCT (n=50, 25 per arm) of suture vs sutureless closure with a small modified Szmyd V-flap after impacted mandibular third molar removal; sutureless significantly reduced early postoperative pain, swelling, and trismus (p<0.001), with no difference in periodontal sequelae or alveolar osteitis.

## 2. Key Contributions
- RCT pairing a deliberately small modified Szmyd V-shaped flap with the sutureless approach, framing sutureless surgery as less invasive, time-saving, and cost-effective.
- Quantifies a strong early-phase advantage (24 h and 48 h) for sutureless on pain, swelling, and trismus.
- Confirms no penalty in periodontal sequelae or alveolar osteitis when sutures are omitted.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: RCT; 50 patients with asymptomatic impacted mandibular third molars randomized into two groups of 25.
- **Group I**: suture used to close the flap. **Group II**: sutureless (no suture).
- **Flap**: small modified Szmyd V-shaped flap in all cases; radiographs assessed angulation and degree of eruption.
- **Outcomes**: pain, swelling, trismus, hemorrhage, periodontal pocket, alveolar osteitis at 24 h, 48 h, day 5, day 7, and 2 weeks.
- **Statistics**: Chi-square, "t" test and independent samples "t" test.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Pain, swelling, and trismus **significantly reduced** in the sutureless group, especially in the immediate postoperative period (p<0.001).
- **No** intra-operative or post-operative hemorrhage in any case.
- **No difference** in periodontal sequelae or alveolar osteitis between groups.
- Conclusion: sutureless surgery with a small flap is less invasive, time-saving, cost-effective, and significantly reduces the early crucial phase of patient discomfort.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small sample (n=50), single setting.
- Full numeric data tables paywalled in captured PDF.
- Outcome of "p<0.001" reported in aggregate; per-timepoint detail not extractable here.
- Sutureless benefit confounded with the small modified Szmyd flap design (cannot separate flap size from closure technique).

## 7. Glossary
- **Modified Szmyd flap**: small V-shaped mucoperiosteal flap designed to minimize tissue trauma.
- **Sutureless surgery**: flap repositioned without sutures; socket heals by secondary intention.
- **Alveolar osteitis**: "dry socket" — painful loss of the protective blood clot.
- **Periodontal sequelae**: pocket formation / attachment changes at the adjacent second molar.
