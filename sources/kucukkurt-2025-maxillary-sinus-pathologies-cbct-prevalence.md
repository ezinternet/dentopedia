## Why Ingested

상악동 점막비후의 유병률과 치성 위험인자를 대규모 CBCT 코호트(n=1,000)로 정량화한 최신(2025) 연구. 기존 소규모 연구들([[sinus-lift/pseudocyst/yeung-2022-cbct-maxillary-sinus-pathology-review]], [[sinus-lift/pseudocyst/salari-2021-maxillary-sinus-pathologic-findings-cbct-implant]])의 유병률 추정치를 보강·확장함.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective CBCT study (n=1,000 scans) found 68% of dental implant candidates had maxillary sinus pathologies, with mucosal thickening most prevalent (47%), strongly linked to odontogenic factors including periapical lesions and periodontal disease.

## 한줄요약

임플란트 후보 환자 1,000명 CBCT 분석: 68%에서 상악동 병변, 점막비후(47%)가 가장 흔했으며, 치근단 병변·치주질환과 강력한 연관성 확인.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Retrospective cross-sectional study
- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health (2025)
- **DOI**: [10.1186/s12903-025-06079-y](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12903-025-06079-y)
- **Sample**: 1,000 CBCT scans (500 males, 500 females), dental implant candidates

## 2. Key Contributions

- Largest single-center CBCT prevalence study of sinus pathology in implant candidates to date
- Quantified association between odontogenic conditions and specific sinus pathology types
- Mucosal thickening defined as >3 mm (pathological threshold per current literature)
- Provided statistical associations, not just frequency counts

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Retrospective CBCT review, single center
- 1,000 scans (500 M / 500 F), dental implant candidates
- Pathology categories: mucosal thickening, mucus retention cysts, polyps, mucoceles, non-specific opacifications
- Odontogenic risk factors: periapical lesions, periodontal disease, root proximity to sinus floor
- Statistical analysis: chi-square, logistic regression for risk factor associations

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Overall pathology prevalence**: 68% of sinuses showed pathologic findings
- **Most common pathology**: Mucosal thickening (~47%)
- **Mucus retention cysts**: second most common
- **Odontogenic associations**: periapical lesions and periodontal disease significantly associated with mucosal thickening
- Pathological threshold used: >3 mm (Schneiderian membrane)
- Male and female prevalence similar

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-center retrospective design
- Cross-sectional — cannot establish causation
- Selection bias (all implant candidates, not general population)

## 7. Glossary

- **Schneiderian membrane**: pseudostratified ciliated columnar epithelium lining the maxillary sinus; normally <1mm
- **Mucosal thickening**: membrane thickness >2-3mm on imaging; most common sinus pathology
- **Mucocele**: mucus-secreting cyst caused by ostium obstruction
