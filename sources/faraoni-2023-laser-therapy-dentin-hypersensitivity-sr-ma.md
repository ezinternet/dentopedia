## One-line Summary
SR+MA (34 studies included, 11 in meta-analysis) finding laser therapy (high- or low-power) significantly reduces DH pain vs baseline at 3 months, with no significant superiority of high- over low-power and no defined protocol.

## 한줄요약
SR+MA(34편 포함·11편 정량): 레이저 치료(고출력/저출력)가 3개월 시점 DH 통증을 유의하게 감소시키나, 고출력의 저출력 대비 우월성은 없고 표준 프로토콜 미확립.

## 1. Document Information
- Dental and Medical Problems 2023;60(1):153–166. Cite: Pion LA, Matos LLMd, Gimenez T, Palma-Dibb RG, Faraoni JJ.
- Search up to April 2020; human clinical trials only.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Combines high-power and low-power laser DH trials in one SR+MA.
- Indirect comparison of high- vs low-power laser effect at 3 months.
- Flags heterogeneity of assessment methods preventing a defined protocol.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 562 records to April 2020; 160 full-text read; 34 included, 11 quantitative.
- Independent extraction + risk of bias; meta-analysis of pre/post pain at 3 months.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Statistically significant pain reduction before vs after 3 months for high- and low-power lasers.
- Indirect comparison: high-power laser trended toward greater pain reduction but not significantly over low-power.
- 55% of studies followed patients ≤6 months.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Heterogeneous outcome measures; no standardized protocol derivable; mostly short follow-up.

## 6. Related Work
- Broader-scope sibling to LLLT-only meta-analysis; supports clinical laser trials.

## 7. Glossary
- High-power laser: e.g., Nd:YAG, Er,Cr:YSGG, diode at ablative/photothermal settings.
- Low-power laser / LLLT: sub-ablative photobiomodulation.
