## One-line Summary
Int J Prosthodont 2025 Suppl: Narrative review (Mainz/Berlin): full-arch immediate implant placement + restoration — implant survival >97%, success >95%; MBL 0.29–1.48mm over 1–5yr; prosthesis survival >98% despite mechanical complications; QoL improved significantly.

## 1. Key Results
- Implant survival rate consistently >97% in full-arch immediate protocols
- Success rates surpassing 95% when proper patient selection applied
- MBL: 0.29–1.48 mm over 1–5 years — within acceptable range
- Prosthesis survival often >98%; mechanical complications: veneer fractures, screw loosening
- Patient satisfaction and quality of life improved significantly post-treatment
- Challenges: anatomical variation, primary stability thresholds, augmentation needs

## 2. Clinical Implications
- Full-arch immediate implant placement/restoration is reliable in well-selected patients
- Primary stability threshold and bone volume are critical selection criteria
- Mechanical complications (veneer fracture, screw loosening) expected — maintenance protocols essential
- Further research needed for higher-risk populations (thin bone, compromised ridge)
