## One-line Summary
Book chapter (IntechOpen, "Human Teeth – From Function to Esthetics"): narrative review of immediate placement and immediate loading in the anterior esthetic zone — when done correctly and in selected cases, immediate placement helps preserve buccal bone and gingival architecture, with provisional crown shaping the emergence profile; conditions, contraindications, and surgical/prosthetic protocols are detailed.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Connects biology (post-extraction bone remodeling) with practice (case selection, surgical sequence, provisional design) in the anterior esthetic zone.
- Reviews the **socket-shield** technique briefly and explains why it remains case-dependent.
- Frames immediate placement as a bone- and soft-tissue-**preserving** option — not a shortcut.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative review structure: rationale → diagnosis → surgical sequence → provisional → final restoration → references.
- Cites foundational papers on post-extraction ridge alterations (Araújo & Lindhe), immediate placement esthetic outcomes (Evans & Chen; Khzam et al; De Rouck et al), and inter-implant papilla biology (Tarnow et al).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks (Clinical Take-aways)
- **Indication**: intact buccal plate, thick biotype, no apical pathology, sufficient apical bone for primary stability.
- **Contraindications / caution**: thin / fenestrated buccal wall, active infection, severe biotype thinness.
- **Surgical sequence**: atraumatic extraction → socket inspection → palatal axis preparation → primary stability via apical + palatal bone → bone graft into peri-implant gap → connective-tissue graft when indicated.
- **Provisional restoration**: immediate ovate pontic-shaped provisional ideal for emergence-profile development; out-of-occlusion.
- **Risks**: midfacial recession (~0.5–1 mm typical), papilla loss, color mismatch — all reduced by case selection and immediate provisionalization.
- **Outcome reference**: immediate implant placement with immediate provisionalization yields good esthetic outcomes in ~80–90% of well-selected cases.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative review — no quantitative pooling; relies on cited primary evidence.
- Esthetic outcome metrics (PES, recession) vary across cited studies.
- Long-term (≥10 yr) data on immediate placement + immediate loading remain limited.

## 7. Glossary
- **Immediate placement (Type 1)**: implant placed in fresh extraction socket on the same day.
- **Immediate loading**: provisional or definitive prosthesis loaded within 1 week of placement.
- **Ovate pontic**: convex emergence shape of a provisional restoration; sculpts peri-implant soft tissue.
- **Socket-shield**: root fragment retained on buccal aspect to maintain bundle bone vasculature.
- **PES (Pink Esthetic Score)**: 7-item soft tissue esthetic scoring.
