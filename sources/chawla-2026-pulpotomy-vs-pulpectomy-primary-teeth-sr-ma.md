## One-line Summary

Abstract-only SR+MA (only 2 studies: 1 RCT + 1 cohort) of pulpotomy vs pulpectomy for irreversible pulpitis in primary teeth (without swelling/periapical signs): no significant difference in radiographic (RR 1.35, 95% CI 0.67–2.73; I²=96%) or clinical success (RR 1.06, 0.95–1.18; I²=66%) at ≤12 months; very low certainty.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Directly compares pulpotomy vs pulpectomy for the contested indication of irreversible pulpitis in primary teeth.
- Finds no significant difference, supporting pulpotomy as a minimally invasive alternative — but on only 2 studies / very low certainty.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

6-database search to April 2025; included primary teeth with symptomatic/asymptomatic irreversible pulpitis, EXCLUDING swelling, sinus tract, furcal/periapical radiolucency, root resorption; ≥12-mo follow-up. Two studies met criteria (1 RCT + 1 retrospective cohort). Random-effects meta-analysis. (Abstract-level only.)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Radiographic success: pooled RR **1.35 (95% CI 0.67–2.73; p=0.40)**, I²=96% (very high heterogeneity) — NS.
- Clinical success: pooled RR **1.06 (95% CI 0.95–1.18; p=0.28)**, I²=66% — NS.
- Certainty of evidence: very low for both outcomes.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 2 included studies; extreme heterogeneity (radiographic I²=96%); very low GRADE certainty.
- Excludes teeth with swelling/periapical pathology — applies only to the "clean" irreversible-pulpitis subset.
- Abstract-only: forest plots / per-study data not extractable.

## 7. Glossary

- **Pulpotomy**: removal of coronal pulp, retaining vital radicular pulp.
- **Pulpectomy**: complete removal of coronal + radicular pulp (primary-tooth root canal).
- **Irreversible pulpitis**: pulp inflammation not expected to resolve — traditionally an indication for pulpectomy/extraction.
