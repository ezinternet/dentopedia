## One-line Summary
Korean special-feature review proposing the implant surveyed crown–retained removable partial denture (IC-RPD) as an oral rehabilitation option for severely disabled patients who cannot use conventional dentures, with discussion of marginal bone loss (MBL) and survival.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: 대한치과의사협회지 (J Korean Dent Assoc) 61(8):528–, 2023
- Author: Soo-Yeon Yoo, Dept. of Prosthodontics, School of Dentistry, Seoul National University
- Type: clinical special-feature review
- DOI: 10.22974/jkda.2023.61.8.003; accepted 2023-07-07
- Key words: Disabled patients, Implant, removable partial denture (RPD), Implant surveyed crown retained RPD (IC-RPD)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Describes IC-RPD as a middle-ground option between conventional RPD and full implant-fixed rehabilitation
- Targets severely disabled patients for whom conventional dentures fail and full fixed implant therapy is impractical
- Reviews MBL and survival considerations for the approach

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative clinical review (special feature); likely includes case illustration.
- Concept: implant-supported surveyed crowns act as abutments providing rests/guide planes/retention to improve RPD stability and retention.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Rationale: aging population + rapid spread of systemic disease increases severely disabled patients who tolerate neither conventional removable dentures nor extensive fixed implant prostheses.
- IC-RPD provides improved retention/stability via implant surveyed crowns while remaining removable (hygiene-accessible, repairable) — a pragmatic compromise.
- [미검증] 구체적 MBL·생존율 수치는 본문(한국어) 정밀 추출 필요 — 요약은 개념·적응증 수준.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative review/case-level evidence; no comparative trial.
- Specific quantitative MBL/survival data require full-text reading.
