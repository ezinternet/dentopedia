## Why Ingested

Independent SR+MA that replicates Moro 2022's ~13% pooled dentist-burnout prevalence and adds a geographic gradient and temporal trend — reinforcing the prevalence backbone of [[overviews/dentist-burnout-mental-health-overview]].

## One-line Summary
Independent SR+MA (15 studies, n=6,038) — overall dentist burnout prevalence **13%**, matching Moro 2022; adds geographic gradient (Europe highest, Americas lowest), longitudinal > cross-sectional, and a downward temporal trend.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Psychology, Health & Medicine, 28(7):1767–1782, Jul-Dec 2023
- DOI: 10.1080/13548506.2023.2208364

- Study type: Systematic review + meta-analysis (sr+ma)
- Search end date: October 28, 2021

## 2. Key Contributions
- **Independent corroboration** of Moro 2022's 13% headline prevalence using a different study set and search date
- Adds **geographic subgroup analysis**: Europe > Asia > Americas
- Documents that **longitudinal studies report higher prevalence than cross-sectional** — design choice biases the headline number
- Reports a **declining temporal trend** — burnout prevalence in the last decade lower than the decade before

## 3. Methodology
- Databases: PubMed, PsycINFO, Embase, Cochrane, Web of Science (inception → 2021-10-28)
- Random-effects model, forest plots
- Subgroup analyses: geography, study design, era

## 4. Key Results
- 15 studies, **n=6,038** dentists
- **Overall pooled prevalence: 13% (95% CI 6–23%)**
- Subgroup by region:
  - Europe: highest
  - Asia: middle
  - Americas: lowest
- Subgroup by design:
  - Longitudinal > cross-sectional (cross-sectional underestimates)
- Subgroup by era:
  - Last decade < previous decade (downward trend)

## 5. Limitations
- Wide CI (6–23%) — point estimate has substantial uncertainty
- Cross-sectional designs dominate the sample
- No causality possible from prevalence data
- Subgroup analyses are exploratory, multiplicity risk

## 6. Related Work
- Moro 2022 — independent SR+MA, same headline ~13%
- Negucioiu 2024 — pre/during/post-COVID prevalence
- Afrashtehfar 2023 — commentary on Moro
- Maslach C. 1981 — MBI tool

## 7. Glossary
- **Random-effects model**: meta-analytic model assuming true effect varies across studies
- **Forest plot**: visualization of individual study effect sizes and pooled estimate
- **Cross-sectional vs longitudinal**: snapshot vs follow-up — different burnout-detection sensitivity
