## Why Ingested

This study extends [[caries/li-2015-microbiota-black-stain-primary-dentition]] by (1) adding a large epidemiologic prevalence survey (n=2,675) to anchor EBS frequency, (2) comparing EBS microbiota across four plaque phenotype groups including caries co-occurrence, and (3) being the first published study to simultaneously characterize the gut microbiome of EBS children — opening a systemic axis not covered by Li 2015 or any existing wiki page.

## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional study (epidemiologic survey n=2,675 + microbiome n=4 groups) found EBS prevalence of 12.10% in preschool children, with EBS associated with reduced caries (lower dmft), Actinomyces/Rothia enrichment in EBS+ caries-free plaque, and a unique Lactobacillus-enriched gut microbiome signature in EBS+ children.

## 한줄요약

취학 전 아동 2,675명의 역학 조사에서 EBS(조기 흑색 착색) 유병률은 12.10%이었으며, EBS는 우식 감소와 관련되고 EBS+ 무우식 치태에서 Actinomyces·Rothia가 증가하였으며 EBS+ 아동의 장내 미생물에서 Lactobacillus 고유 신호가 처음 보고되었다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Oral microbiome and gut microbiome of early black stain and dental caries in preschool children
- **Journal**: Frontiers in Microbiology
- **Year**: 2023
- **Study type**: Cross-sectional (epidemiologic survey + 16S rRNA microbiome, oral plaque + fecal)
- **Population**: Epidemiologic: 2,675 children aged 3–6 years; Microbiome: subset divided into 4 plaque and 4 fecal groups
- **DOI**: 10.3389/fmicb.2023.1081629
- **PMID**: 37065128 / PMC10103904

## 2. Key Contributions

- First study to simultaneously profile oral and gut microbiomes in EBS children
- EBS prevalence in Chinese preschool children: 12.10% (significant benchmark)
- EBS children had significantly lower caries prevalence and lower dmft scores (p<0.01), supporting the caries-protective phenotype hypothesis
- Actinomyces and Rothia enriched in BP group (EBS+, caries-free) vs other groups — replicating Li 2015 Actinomyces finding
- Streptococcus mutans enriched in CP group (caries, no EBS) — expected caries-associated signal
- P group (healthy, no EBS, no caries) showed the most complex/diverse plaque microbiome
- First gut microbiome finding in EBS: BF group (EBS+, caries-free) had Lactobacillus as a distinctive gut biomarker
- EBS proposed as a potential protective factor against early childhood caries (ECC)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Phase 1 — Epidemiologic survey**: Cross-sectional, 2,675 preschool children aged 3–6 years; dental examination for EBS and caries (dmft index)
- **Phase 2 — Microbiome**: Subset enrolled; supragingival plaque and fecal samples collected
- **Plaque groups**: BCP (EBS+caries+), BP (EBS+caries−), CP (EBS−caries+), P (EBS−caries−)
- **Fecal groups**: BCF (EBS+caries+), BF (EBS+caries−), CF (EBS−caries+), F (EBS−caries−)
- **Sequencing**: 16S rRNA amplicon sequencing (oral plaque and fecal samples)
- **Analysis**: Alpha/beta diversity, differential abundance, biomarker identification (LEfSe or equivalent)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Result |
|---|---|
| EBS prevalence (n=2,675) | 12.10% |
| EBS vs caries prevalence | EBS significantly lower caries, lower dmft (p<0.01) |
| BP plaque biomarkers | Actinomyces ↑, Rothia ↑ |
| CP plaque biomarker | Streptococcus mutans ↑ |
| P group plaque complexity | Most complex (highest diversity) |
| BF fecal biomarker | Lactobacillus (unique EBS gut signature) |

- EBS appears to select for a plaque microbiome distinct from both caries-active and healthy microbiomes
- Gut microbiome data suggest a systemic or host-level factor may modulate EBS susceptibility

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Cross-sectional: causality between EBS microbiome, gut microbiome, and caries protection cannot be determined
- EBS diagnosis likely clinical (visual); inter-examiner reliability not detailed in abstract
- Microbiome subset size not specified in available abstract text — may be small relative to the 2,675 epidemiologic cohort
- Mechanism linking gut Lactobacillus to oral EBS formation is unexplained; could be dietary/host immune
- Longitudinal follow-up needed to determine if EBS microbiome predicts future caries-free status

## 6. Related Work

- Li 2015 (PMID 26340752): first 16S rRNA characterization of BS microbiota in primary dentition; Actinomyces enrichment replicated by Zheng 2023
- Soukos & Goodson 2011: iron sulfide precipitation by Actinomyces as the pigmentation mechanism
- Slots 1974: culture-based early description of BS microbiota

## 7. Glossary

- **EBS (Early Black Stain)**: Black extrinsic stain on primary teeth of preschool children; associated with reduced caries prevalence
- **dmft**: Decayed, missing, filled teeth index for primary dentition
- **BCP/BP/CP/P**: Four plaque phenotype groups combining EBS and caries status
- **BCF/BF/CF/F**: Four fecal phenotype groups combining EBS and caries status
- **LEfSe**: Linear discriminant analysis effect size — method for identifying differentially abundant taxa
- **Actinomyces**: Gram-positive filamentous oral bacteria; iron sulfide precipitation candidate for BS pigmentation
- **Rothia**: Gram-positive oral commensal; found enriched in EBS+ caries-free plaque
- **Lactobacillus**: Gram-positive lactic acid bacteria; found as gut biomarker in EBS+ caries-free children
