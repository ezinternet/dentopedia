## One-line Summary
Systematic review of 20 RCTs/controlled studies showing PRF significantly improves early socket healing (pain, soft tissue, bone fill) within 2–3 months but offers no clear advantage at 6 months.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: International Journal of Implant Dentistry 2021;7:117
- **DOI**: 10.1186/s40729-021-00393-0
- **Study type**: Systematic review (no meta-analysis due to data heterogeneity)
- **Institution**: FORM, Frankfurt Oral Regenerative Medicine, Goethe University, Frankfurt, Germany

## 2. Key Contributions
- Focused PICO question: efficacy of PRF vs. no PRF for pain prevention and soft/hard tissue regeneration after fresh tooth extraction
- 20 randomized and/or controlled studies included from PubMed, Web of Knowledge, and hand search
- Demonstrates PRF benefit is time-limited: most effective in first 2–3 months

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Electronic database search: PubMed, Web of Knowledge of Thomson Reuters; manual hand search of relevant journals
- Inclusion: randomized and/or controlled studies on PRF in extraction sockets
- No meta-analysis performed due to heterogeneity in study design, PRF preparation protocols, and outcome assessment methods
- Outcomes evaluated: postoperative pain, soft tissue healing, dimensional bone loss, socket fill/bone regeneration

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Pain**: 66.6% of studies showed PRF significantly reduced postoperative pain, especially days 1–3
- **Soft tissue healing**: Significantly improved in PRF group vs. spontaneous healing at 1 week in 75% of evaluated studies
- **Bone loss (dimensional)**: Significantly lower in PRF group at 8–15 weeks but NOT at 6 months
- **Socket fill**: 85% of studies showed significantly higher socket fill in PRF group vs. spontaneous healing
- Conclusion: PRF most effective in early healing period (2–3 months); no benefit in longer healing periods
- No data available on long-term implant success in PRF-treated sockets

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- High heterogeneity in PRF preparation protocols, centrifugation speeds, and time
- No meta-analysis possible; qualitative synthesis only
- Inconsistent outcome measurement timepoints across studies
- Lack of data on implant outcomes in PRF-treated sockets
- Future RCTs needed with standardized PRF protocols and longer follow-up

## 6. Related Work
- Socket preservation literature using bone substitute materials (BSMs) with 3–6 month healing protocols
- Immediate implant placement as alternative approach to limit bone resorption
- Ridge preservation as prophylactic intervention for larger defects

## 7. Glossary
- **PRF (Platelet-Rich Fibrin)**: Autologous blood-derived biomaterial produced by centrifugation; contains growth factors embedded in fibrin matrix
- **Socket fill**: Radiographic measure of new bone formation within extraction socket
- **Alveolar bone atrophy**: Progressive bone loss following tooth extraction; 50–60% of alveolar bone lost in first 3 months
- **Socket preservation**: Prophylactic intervention applying BSMs into extraction socket to preserve alveolar bone dimension
