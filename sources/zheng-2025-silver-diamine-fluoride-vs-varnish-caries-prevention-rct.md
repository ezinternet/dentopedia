## Why Ingested

A large 30-month double-blind superiority RCT (730 children) directly comparing 38% silver diamine fluoride (SDF) against the standard-of-care active comparator 5% sodium fluoride **varnish** (FV) for *preventing* (not arresting) early childhood caries on sound primary upper anterior teeth — the first long-duration head-to-head of the two leading professional topical agents on sound surfaces. Extends [[wiki/caries/urquhart-2019-nonrestorative-treatments-caries-network-meta-analysis]], whose NMA ranked SDF and sealant+fluoride highest for lesion arrest/reversal, by adding direct preventive-effect (sound-surface) evidence with a 39% prevented fraction favoring SDF.

## One-line Summary

30-mo double-blind superiority RCT (n=730→621, Hong Kong kindergartens): semiannual 38% SDF prevented new caries on sound primary upper anterior teeth better than 5% NaF varnish — new carious surfaces/child 0.35±1.09 (SDF) vs 0.54±1.50 (FV), p=0.048; GEE adjusted OR 0.61 (p=0.029), 39% prevented fraction, effect emerging after 24 mo on proximal & smooth surfaces.

## 한줄요약

30개월 이중맹검 우월성 RCT (홍콩 유치원, 730명→621명): 반년마다 38% SDF 도포가 5% NaF 바니시(FV)보다 건전한 상악 전치 유치의 신규 우식을 더 잘 예방 — 아동당 신규 우식면 0.35±1.09(SDF) vs 0.54±1.50(FV), p=0.048; GEE 보정 OR 0.61(p=0.029), 예방분율 39%, 효과는 24개월 이후 인접면·평활면에서 나타남.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First long-term (30-mo) RCT testing the **preventive** (not arresting) effect of 38% SDF on *sound* primary upper anterior teeth, against the standard-of-care active comparator 5% sodium fluoride varnish.
- Demonstrates SDF superiority over FV at both the child level (new carious surfaces/child) and the tooth-surface level (GEE adjusted OR), with a 39% prevented fraction.
- Shows the SDF advantage emerges only after **24 months** of semiannual application — short (12-mo) trials are insufficient to detect the preventive benefit.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Double-blind, stratified-randomized (by caries risk: dmft >0 vs =0), 1:1 parallel 2-arm superiority RCT in 13 Hong Kong kindergartens; block randomization (blocks of 8); opaque sealed-envelope allocation concealment.
- **Participants**: Children aged 3–4 y, first year of kindergarten, generally healthy; 730 recruited at baseline (365 per arm). Hong Kong water fluoridation 0.5 ppm.
- **Intervention**: Semiannual application of either 38% SDF (Advantage Arrest, Elevate Oral Care) or 5% NaF varnish (Duraphat, Colgate-Palmolive) to all surfaces of the 6 primary upper anterior teeth (~18 surfaces/child). Per 6-mo session: ~4.8 mg F (SDF) vs ~9.1 mg F (FV). SDF F concentration 44,800 ppm vs FV 22,600 ppm.
- **Outcomes**: Primary = mean new carious tooth surfaces per child at 30 mo (cavitation-level dmfs). Secondary = incidence (odds ratio) of new carious surfaces, surfaces categorized smooth (buccal/lingual) vs proximal (mesial/distal).
- **Examiner**: Single trained/calibrated examiner; intra-examiner kappa ≥0.90 at every period; 10% re-examined per visit.
- **Analysis**: Modified intention-to-treat (final-examination completers); GEE with logit link and exchangeable correlation to cluster surfaces within child; adjusted for tooth surface type. P < 0.05 two-sided. SPSS 28.0.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Retention**: 621/730 completed 30 mo (15% loss; SDF 307, FV 314); no significant baseline differences between arms. Loss 16% SDF vs 14% FV (p=0.84).
- **Primary outcome (new carious surfaces/child, 30 mo)**: SDF 0.35 ± 1.09 vs FV 0.54 ± 1.50 (Mann-Whitney p=0.048) — difference 0.19 surfaces.
- **Child-level caries incidence**: 15.6% (48/307) SDF vs 19.7% (62/314) FV (p=0.262, NS).
- **Tooth-surface incidence**: 1.5% (108/7,224) SDF vs 2.2% (166/7,384) FV.
- **GEE adjusted OR (30 mo)**: 0.61 (95% CI 0.39–0.95, p=0.029) favoring SDF. At 24 mo aOR 0.58 (0.37–0.93, p=0.023). Sensitivity (433 non-dentist-visiting children): aOR 0.58 (0.34–1.00, p=0.048) — same result.
- **Prevented fraction**: 39% (SDF vs FV).
- **Time course**: Child-level mean differences not significant at 6/12/18 mo; significant from 24 mo (p=0.028) and 30 mo (p=0.048).
- **Tooth surface type**: Proximal surfaces aOR 2.38 (1.73–3.27, p<0.001) vs smooth — proximal at much higher caries risk.
- **Restorations**: No difference in fillings received (21/307 SDF vs 20/314 FV, p=0.492). No adverse effects reported.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Low overall caries incidence in both arms → reduced power, risk of type II error.
- 2-arm design with **no no-treatment control** (ethical) → cannot quantify either agent's benefit over no treatment.
- Findings limited to primary **upper anterior** teeth; not generalizable to posterior/occlusal surfaces (a separate 30-mo molar-fissure SDF trial is underway).
- May not apply to children younger than 3–5 y. SDF metallic taste / esthetic blackening of carious (not sound) lesions noted as practical considerations.
- Single examiner → potential examiner effect, though mitigated by calibration.

## 6. Related Work

- Cochrane SR: FV reduces primary-tooth caries development by ~37%.
- 2019 SR+MA: annual 38% SDF gave 54% caries reduction vs quarterly 5% FV (heterogeneous: 12–36 mo, 12–38% SDF, primary/permanent, ages 3–15).
- Two 12-mo SDF ECC-prevention RCTs (2023) — too short to detect preventive effect.
- WHO Model List of Essential Medicines now includes SDF (children and adults).

## 7. Glossary

- **SDF (38% silver diamine fluoride)**: 44,800 ppm fluoride solution with silver; dual remineralizing + antibacterial action.
- **FV (5% sodium fluoride varnish)**: 22,600 ppm fluoride; standard-of-care professional topical applied 2–4×/y.
- **ECC (early childhood caries)**: caries in primary dentition of preschool children.
- **dmfs / dmft**: decayed-missing-filled surface / tooth indices for primary dentition.
- **Prevented fraction**: 1 − relative risk; proportion of caries prevented relative to comparator.
- **GEE (generalized estimating equation)**: regression accounting for within-child clustering of tooth surfaces.
