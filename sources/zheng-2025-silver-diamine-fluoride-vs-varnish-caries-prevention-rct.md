## Why Ingested

A large 30-month double-blind superiority RCT (730 children) directly comparing 38% silver diamine fluoride (SDF) against the standard-of-care active comparator 5% sodium fluoride **varnish** (FV) for *preventing* (not arresting) early childhood caries on sound primary upper anterior teeth — the first long-duration head-to-head of the two leading professional topical agents on sound surfaces. Extends [[wiki/caries/urquhart-2019-nonrestorative-treatments-caries-network-meta-analysis]], whose NMA ranked SDF and sealant+fluoride highest for lesion arrest/reversal, by adding direct preventive-effect (sound-surface) evidence with a 39% prevented fraction favoring SDF.

## One-line Summary

30-mo double-blind superiority RCT (n=730→621, Hong Kong kindergartens): semiannual 38% SDF prevented new caries on sound primary upper anterior teeth better than 5% NaF varnish — new carious surfaces/child 0.35±1.09 (SDF) vs 0.54±1.50 (FV), p=0.048; GEE adjusted OR 0.61 (p=0.029), 39% prevented fraction, effect emerging after 24 mo on proximal & smooth surfaces.

## 한줄요약

30개월 이중맹검 우월성 RCT (홍콩 유치원, 730명→621명): 반년마다 38% SDF 도포가 5% NaF 바니시(FV)보다 건전한 상악 전치 유치의 신규 우식을 더 잘 예방 — 아동당 신규 우식면 0.35±1.09(SDF) vs 0.54±1.50(FV), p=0.048; GEE 보정 OR 0.61(p=0.029), 예방분율 39%, 효과는 24개월 이후 인접면·평활면에서 나타남.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First long-term (30-mo) RCT testing the **preventive** (not arresting) effect of 38% SDF on *sound* primary upper anterior teeth, against the standard-of-care active comparator 5% sodium fluoride varnish.
- Demonstrates SDF superiority over FV at both the child level (new carious surfaces/child) and the tooth-surface level (GEE adjusted OR 0.61), with a 39% prevented fraction.
- Shows the SDF advantage emerges only after **24 months** of semiannual application — short (12-mo) trials are insufficient to detect the preventive benefit on sound surfaces.
- Confirms large safety margin: 18 applications over 30 mo are well below fluorosis-risk thresholds; no adverse effects reported.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Double-blind, stratified-randomized (by caries risk: dmft >0 vs =0), 1:1 parallel 2-arm superiority RCT in 13 Hong Kong kindergartens; block randomization (blocks of 8); opaque sealed-envelope allocation concealment. CONSORT 2010 compliant.
- **Participants**: Children aged 3–4 y, first year of kindergarten, generally healthy. Excluded if uncooperative or on long-term medications (e.g., methylphenidate). 730 recruited at baseline (365 per arm). Hong Kong water fluoridation 0.5 ppm. First child enrolled October 22, 2020; last evaluated October 11, 2023.
- **Intervention**: Semiannual application of either 38% SDF (Advantage Arrest, lot 21109, Elevate Oral Care) or 5% NaF varnish (Duraphat, Colgate-Palmolive) to all surfaces of the 6 primary upper anterior teeth (~18 surfaces/child). Each tooth surface dry-brushed before application; proximal surfaces of adjacent teeth shared a single application. Per 6-mo session: ~4.8 mg F (SDF) vs ~9.1 mg F (FV). SDF F concentration 44,800 ppm vs FV 22,600 ppm.
- **Outcomes**: Primary = mean new carious tooth surfaces per child at 30 mo (cavitation-level dmfs). Secondary = incidence (odds ratio) of new carious surfaces; surfaces categorized smooth (buccal/lingual) vs proximal (mesial/distal). Also: proportion at each interim time point.
- **Examiner**: Single trained/calibrated examiner (F.M.Z.); intra-examiner kappa ≥0.90 at every period; 10% re-examined per visit; biannual recalibration against gold-standard examiner (C.H.C.).
- **Sample size**: Based on prior 2002 RCT (SDF 0.47±0.87 vs FV 0.70±0.84 at 30 mo → 33% prevented fraction). Power 0.9, α=0.05, two-sided → 292/arm. Inflated to 365/arm for 20% anticipated loss → total 730.
- **Analysis**: Modified intention-to-treat (final-examination completers); last observation carried forward for secondary outcomes prior to final visit. GEE with logit link and exchangeable correlation to cluster surfaces within child; adjusted for tooth surface type. SPSS v28.0. Two-sided p < 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Retention**: 621/730 completed 30 mo (15% overall loss; SDF 307/365 [16%], FV 314/365 [14%]; p=0.84). No significant baseline differences between arms (demographics, dmft, VPI, toothbrushing habits).
- **Primary outcome (new carious surfaces/child, 30 mo)**: SDF 0.35 ± 1.09 vs FV 0.54 ± 1.50 (Mann-Whitney U, p=0.048) — difference 0.19 surfaces.
- **Child-level caries incidence**: 15.6% (48/307) SDF vs 19.7% (62/314) FV (chi-square p=0.180, NS). Overall 17.7% (110/621) developed new carious lesions on 274 surfaces (1.9% of 14,344).
- **Tooth-surface incidence**: 1.5% (108/7,224) SDF vs 2.2% (166/7,384) FV.
- **GEE adjusted OR (30 mo)**: 0.61 (95% CI 0.39–0.95, p=0.029) favoring SDF. At 24 mo: aOR 0.58 (95% CI 0.37–0.93, p=0.023). Sensitivity (433 children who did not visit a dentist, 10,680 surfaces): aOR 0.58 (95% CI 0.34–1.00, p=0.048) — same result.
- **Predicted probability of caries at 30 mo**: SDF group 0.83% vs FV (reference) 1.36%.
- **Prevented fraction**: 39% (SDF vs FV).
- **Time course**: Child-level mean differences NS at 6 mo (p=0.292), 12 mo (p=0.168), 18 mo (p=0.089); significant at 24 mo (p=0.028) and 30 mo (p=0.048).
- **Tooth surface type**: Proximal surfaces aOR 2.38 (95% CI 1.73–3.27, p<0.001) vs smooth — proximal at much higher caries risk.
- **Restorations**: No difference in fillings received (21/307 SDF vs 20/314 FV, p=0.492). No adverse effects reported; 18 applications provide large safety margin against fluorosis.
- **Noncarious tooth loss** (exfoliation): mean 0.12±0.48 (SDF) vs 0.10±0.41 (FV), p=0.429 — NS.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Low overall caries incidence in both arms → reduced statistical power, increased risk of type II error.
- 2-arm design with **no no-treatment control** (ethical constraint) → cannot quantify either agent's absolute benefit over no treatment.
- Findings limited to primary **upper anterior** teeth; not generalizable to posterior/occlusal surfaces. A separate 30-mo molar-fissure SDF trial is underway (Duangthip et al. 2022; NCT not specified).
- May not apply to children younger than 3–5 y (ECC is age-dependent in its distribution).
- Single examiner → potential examiner effect, though mitigated by rigorous calibration (kappa ≥0.90) and blinding.
- Modified ITT (final-exam completers only) rather than full ITT with multiple imputation.
- SDF metallic taste / aesthetic blackening of carious lesions (not sound teeth) noted as practical considerations for acceptance.

## 6. Related Work

- Oliveira et al. 2019 SR+MA (Caries Research): annual 38% SDF gave 54% (95% CI 27–73%) caries reduction vs quarterly 5% FV — but heterogeneous populations (12–36 mo, 12–38% SDF, primary/permanent, ages 3–15) and SDF was applied to carious not sound teeth.
- Marinho et al. 2013 Cochrane SR: FV reduces primary-tooth caries development by 37%.
- Jain et al. 2023 (Int J Clin Pediatr Dent) and Zheng et al. 2023 (Int Dent J): two 12-mo SDF ECC-prevention RCTs — too short to detect preventive effect on sound surfaces.
- Chu et al. 2002 (J Dent Res): original HKU 30-mo RCT on *arrest* of dentine caries; provided the pilot data for this trial's sample size calculation.
- Worthington et al. 2024 Cochrane: SDF for arrest in children/adults; highlights detection-bias risk when using black-staining as an outcome — not an issue here since this trial focused on cavitation-level detection on sound surfaces.
- WHO 2021 Model List of Essential Medicines: SDF included for children and adults.

## 7. Glossary

- **SDF (38% silver diamine fluoride)**: 44,800 ppm fluoride solution with silver; dual remineralizing + antibacterial action.
- **FV (5% sodium fluoride varnish)**: 22,600 ppm fluoride; standard-of-care professional topical applied 2–4×/y.
- **ECC (early childhood caries)**: Caries in primary dentition of preschool children.
- **dmfs / dmft**: Decayed-missing-filled surface / tooth indices for primary dentition.
- **Prevented fraction**: 1 − relative risk; proportion of caries prevented relative to comparator.
- **GEE (generalized estimating equation)**: Regression accounting for within-child clustering of tooth surfaces (logit link, exchangeable correlation structure, binomial distribution).
- **Modified ITT**: Analysis limited to participants who completed the final examination (vs full ITT which includes all randomized participants).
- **Visible Plaque Index (VPI)**: Ainamo & Bay 1975; percentage of surfaces with visible plaque.
