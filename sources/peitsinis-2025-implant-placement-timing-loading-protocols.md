## One-line Summary
JCM 2025: Narrative review (Aristotle University): immediate (93.8-100% survival, high complication risk), early, delayed placement timing comparison + loading protocols; immediate has highest esthetic risk — strict patient selection criteria essential.

## 1. Key Results
- Immediate implant: survival 93.8–100%; increased risk of gingival recession and implant exposure
- Early placement (4–8 wk): good outcomes with less esthetic risk
- Delayed placement (>6 mo): most predictable, lowest esthetic risk
- Immediate loading: viable when ISQ/IT criteria met; not universally applicable
- No single superior timing — patient selection determines outcome

## 2. Clinical Implications
- Immediate placement requires intact socket walls, thick biotype, no infection
- Loading protocol must be matched to primary stability achieved
- Esthetic zone: early or delayed placement preferred unless optimal conditions met
