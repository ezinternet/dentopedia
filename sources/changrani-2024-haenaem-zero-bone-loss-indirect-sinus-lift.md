## One-line Summary

Prospective single-arm study (n=12) demonstrating statistically significant bone height gain at all four CBCT aspects (p<0.01) at 4 months following indirect transcrestal sinus lift using the HaeNaem Zero Bone Loss osseodensification kit with simultaneous implant placement and no graft material.

## 한줄요약

전향적 단일군 연구(n=12): HaeNaem Zero Bone Loss OD 버 키트로 무이식 경치조골 간접 거상 동시 임플란트 식립 후 4개월 CBCT에서 4개 측면(근심/원심/협측/구개측) 모두 유의한 골 높이 증가(p<0.01).

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First (or among very few) clinical data on CW-rotation OD bur for indirect sinus lift**: HaeNaem Zero Bone Loss Kit operates in a clockwise (forward) direction, contrasting with the counter-clockwise (CCW) motion of Densah burs. This study provides the only known prospective clinical data on this alternative OD bur system for transcrestal sinus elevation.
2. **Graft-free OD sinus lift with CBCT documentation**: Demonstrates that simultaneous implant placement with no graft material can achieve measurable bone height gain at 4 months when membrane is elevated via OD compaction forces alone.
3. **Four-aspect CBCT measurement**: Records bone height change at mesial, distal, buccal, and palatal aspects of the implant, providing a more complete spatial picture than single-point measurement.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Patient selection**: Maxillary posterior edentulous patients, residual crestal bone height (RCBH) 6–8 mm, requiring single-implant placement
- **Exclusion**: Uncontrolled systemic disease, active infection, smoking (not clearly specified in summary — typical for OD studies)
- **Procedure**: Sequential HaeNaem Zero Bone Loss burs of increasing diameter used in CW (clockwise/forward) rotation to create osseodensified osteotomy; indirect sinus membrane elevation achieved via bone compaction pressure without hydraulic or balloon assist; simultaneous implant placement; no grafting material placed
- **Implant system**: Not specified in summary (Indian academic context, likely generic or specified in full text)
- **CBCT protocol**: Pre-operative and 4-month post-operative CBCT scans; bone height measured at mesial, distal, buccal, and palatal aspects at implant site
- **Statistics**: Paired t-test comparing pre- vs. post-op bone height at each of the 4 aspects; significance threshold p<0.05

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Statistically significant increase in alveolar bone height at all 4 measurement aspects (mesial, distal, buccal, palatal): paired t-test p<0.01 for each
- Implant stability confirmed at placement (ISQ or clinical stability — specific values not available from extraction)
- No sinus membrane perforation reported (implied by successful outcome)
- No graft material required; bone gain attributed to OD compaction and blood clot organization under elevated membrane

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **No control group**: Single-arm design prevents comparison to other techniques (osteotome, Densah, balloon)
- **Small sample (n=12)**: Insufficient statistical power to detect complications or compare subgroups
- **Short follow-up (4 months)**: Bone height gain is early; long-term stability, implant survival, and marginal bone level changes not assessed
- **Single center, single country**: Bharati Vidyapeeth Dental College, Pune — results may not generalize
- **RCBH 6–8 mm only**: Does not address more atrophic (<6 mm) or less atrophic (>8 mm) scenarios
- **No ISQ/RFA data reported in summary**: Stability documented clinically but without quantified values
- **CW vs CCW bur comparison lacking**: The claimed advantage of HaeNaem over Densah (reference 12 in corrected version) is cited but not tested within this study
- **Future need**: RCT comparing HaeNaem CW vs Densah CCW, with longer follow-up and larger n

## 7. Glossary

- **HaeNaem Zero Bone Loss Kit**: Proprietary OD bur system using clockwise (CW/forward) rotation; "Zero Bone Loss" refers to no bone removal during osteotomy preparation (bone is compacted, not excised)
- **Osseodensification (OD)**: Osteotomy preparation technique that compacts bone laterally rather than removing it, increasing bone density and primary stability; originally developed with CCW Densah burs
- **CW (clockwise/forward) rotation**: HaeNaem bur direction — contrasts with CCW (counter-clockwise/reverse) used by Densah for OD effect
- **Indirect (transcrestal) sinus lift**: Sinus membrane elevation via crestal approach using compaction forces rather than lateral window
- **RCBH**: Residual crestal bone height — vertical bone available between alveolar crest and sinus floor
- **CBCT**: Cone beam computed tomography — used here for pre/post bone height measurement at 4 aspects
