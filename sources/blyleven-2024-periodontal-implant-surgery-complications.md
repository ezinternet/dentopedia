## One-line Summary
Retrospective single-investigator chart review of 1135 periodontal/implant surgeries (8 residents, 2018–2022): intraoperative complications 2.8% and postoperative complications 15.2%; lateral sinus elevation drives intraoperative complication risk (OR 102.5, 95% CI 12.3–852.9, p<0.001) and connective tissue grafting drives postoperative complication risk (OR 3.2, 95% CI 1.6–6.1, p<0.001).

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Clinical and Experimental Dental Research 2024;10:e849, 14 pages
- **Institution**: Department of Periodontics, Army Postgraduate Dental School, Fort Eisenhower (Augusta GA, USA)
- **Funding**: Defense Health Agency
- **Study type**: Retrospective single-centre cohort/chart review (residency clinic)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Largest single-investigator record-review of periodontal/implant complications to date (n=1135 procedures).
- Reports **procedure-stratified ORs** for both intraoperative and postoperative complications — useful for chair-side risk discussion.
- Applies a **standardised severity grading** to postoperative complications rather than binary present/absent.
- Confirms long-standing intuition that **procedure type, not patient or operator factors, dominates complication risk**.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Single investigator audited records from 8 periodontal residents across 4 academic years (July 2018 – June 2022).
- For each procedure, recorded intraoperative and postoperative complications (or none).
- Explanatory covariates: operator (resident), procedure type, patient (age, sex, ASA, smoking, systemic disease), site (tooth/region).
- Statistical analysis: logistic regression for occurrence; postoperative severity assessed with a standardised grading system.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Total procedures**: 1135.
- **Intraoperative complication rate**: 2.8%. Most common: Schneiderian membrane perforation 1.3%; gingival flap perforation/tear 1.0%.
- **Postoperative complication rate**: 15.2%. Most common: dentin hypersensitivity 2.6%; excessive pain 2.5%; infection 2.2%.
- **Postoperative ORs (vs reference scaling/root-planing)**:
  - Subepithelial connective tissue graft (CTG): **OR 3.2 (95% CI 1.6–6.1, p<.001)**.
  - Guided bone regeneration (GBR): **OR 3.0 (95% CI 1.4–6.5, p=.004)**.
  - GBR with implant placement: **OR 3.1 (95% CI 1.3–7.6, p=.011)**.
- **Intraoperative ORs**:
  - Lateral sinus elevation (SES): **OR 102.5 (95% CI 12.3–852.9, p<.001)**.
  - Transalveolar sinus elevation: **OR 22.4 (95% CI 2.2–224.5, p=.008)**.
  - Open flap debridement: **OR 36.4 (95% CI 3.0–440.7, p=.005)**.
  - Surgically facilitated orthodontic therapy: **OR 20.5 (95% CI 1.2–358.4, p=.039)**.
- Procedure type dominates; operator/patient covariates contributed less.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Single-centre, single-investigator audit → risk of detection bias and limited generalisability.
- All operators are periodontal residents — learning-curve effects.
- Wide CIs on some ORs (e.g., lateral SES) reflect rare events; need multi-centre confirmation.
- Severity grading is standardised but novel; cross-study comparability is limited.

## 6. Related Work
- Tan 2008 — systematic review of sinus membrane perforation incidence (10–56%).
- Goodacre 2003 — classification of implant complications.
- Schwartz 2008 — risk factors for soft-tissue grafting failure.
- Other residency-based chart audits (Bilhan, Khoury) generally show 5–25% postoperative complication ranges, congruent with present 15.2%.

## 7. Glossary
- **Intraoperative complication**: any unintended deviation during the surgical procedure (perforation, bleeding, fracture, etc.).
- **Postoperative complication**: any adverse event after the procedure (infection, swelling, dehiscence, hypersensitivity, etc.).
- **Schneiderian membrane**: thin epithelial lining of the maxillary sinus; perforation is the commonest intraoperative complication in sinus lift.
- **Open flap debridement (OFD)**: full-thickness flap elevation for direct subgingival instrumentation.
- **CTG**: subepithelial connective tissue graft for soft-tissue augmentation/root coverage.
