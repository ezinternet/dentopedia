## One-line Summary

Systematic review + single-arm meta-analysis (Int Dent J 2024; 7 studies, 202 patients / 215 immediate implants) of digital-template-guided immediate implant placement accuracy: pooled coronal 0.74 mm, apical 1.01 mm, depth 0.50 mm, angular 2.34° deviation — generally acceptable but with very high heterogeneity (I² 75-99%); timing (immediate vs delayed) did not materially change accuracy.

## 한줄요약

SR + 단일군 메타분석 (Int Dent J 2024, 7연구·202명·215 즉시 임플란트): 디지털 템플릿 가이드 즉시식립의 풀링 편차는 coronal 0.74 mm·apical 1.01 mm·depth 0.50 mm·angular 2.34°로 임상적으로 수용 가능하나 이질성 매우 높음(I² 75~99%). 즉시 vs 지연 식립 시점은 정확도에 유의한 차이를 주지 않음.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic review focused specifically on accuracy of **immediate** implant placement guided by **digital templates** (prior SRs/NMAs centred on healed-ridge or mixed timing).
- Pooled single-arm deviation estimates for immediate template-guided placement: coronal 0.74 mm, apical 1.01 mm, depth 0.50 mm, angular 2.34°.
- Direct delayed-vs-immediate comparison: timing did not significantly change coronal/apical/depth deviation; angular difference small but significant.
- Catalogues accuracy-modifying factors: guide support type (tooth > bone/mucosa), fabrication (CAD/CAM vs lab-manual), arch (mandible > maxilla), flap vs flapless, fully vs half guided, and the immediate-specific buccal-drift tendency.
- Flags a unique immediate-placement limitation: guides cannot be tried in before extraction, so fit cannot be pre-verified.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Design: systematic review with random-effects single-arm meta-analysis (Stata v16.0); meta-regression for between-group comparisons; heterogeneity by I².
- PICO: P = edentulous patient receiving implant treatment; I = immediate implantation using digital guides; C = preoperative software-planned implant; O = accuracy (3D deviation of placed vs planned implant).
- Search: PubMed, Cochrane CENTRAL, Embase, Web of Science, inception–July 2023, English only. RCTs + retrospective/prospective cohorts.
- Selection: 436 records → 321 after de-dup → 7 included (5 retrospective, 2 prospective). n = 202 patients, 215 implants.
- Outcomes: coronal, apical, depth, angular deviation (placed vs planned).
- Risk of bias: Newcastle–Ottawa scale (cohorts; scored 6–8 → moderate-to-high quality), Cochrane tool (RCTs). Sample-size calculations only in Feng, Han, Huang.
- Study mix: maxilla 5 / mandible 2; intraoral scan 5, extraoral 1, unspecified 1; lab-manual guides dominant (1 CAD/CAM); flap 4 / flapless 4; fully guided 4, full+half 1, unspecified 2.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Global pooled accuracy (all CIs exclude 0 → significant):**
- Coronal deviation: 0.74 mm (95% CI 0.41–1.08), I² 99.0%, p = .000
- Apical deviation: 1.01 mm (95% CI 0.83–1.20), I² 94.0%, p = .000
- Depth deviation: 0.50 mm (95% CI 0.36–0.65), I² 75.3%, p = .007
- Angular deviation: 2.34° (95% CI 1.68–3.00), I² 94.5%, p = .000

**Delayed vs immediate timing (5 studies):**
- Coronal 0.07 (95% CI −0.09–0.22), apical 0.05 (95% CI −0.12–0.22), depth −0.18 (95% CI −0.38–0.02): all CIs include 0 → no significant difference.
- Angular 0.16 (95% CI 0.01–0.31): small significant difference.

**Benchmark vs healed-ridge guided surgery (Schneider et al):** lower coronal (0.74 vs 1.16 mm) and apical (1.01 vs 1.96 mm) deviation, suggesting comparable-to-better accuracy — but heavily confounded by heterogeneity and predominant fully-guided protocols.

Subgroup (maxillary, anterior) estimates are reported but the published text contains label–metric mismatches; values reproduced verbatim in the source `.txt`, interpret with caution.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Author-stated: few included studies (7), small patient numbers, very high heterogeneity (I² up to 99%); some studies lacked p-values for precision.
- Methodological caveats (additionally identified): all included evidence is non-RCT (retrospective/prospective cohorts) → confidence is observational; single-arm pooling of absolute deviation with I² near 99% makes the pooled point estimate weakly interpretable; subgroup tables in the published article have internal label inconsistencies.
- No patient-centred (satisfaction/comfort/recovery) or socioeconomic outcomes — accuracy only.
- Future work: adaptable guide designs and improved preoperative planning to overcome the no-try-in limitation; multidimensional evaluation including cost and compliance.

## 6. Related Work

- Gargallo-Albiol et al — static computer-assisted guides more accurate than freehand; fully guided > half guided.
- Schneider et al — accuracy SR in non-immediate (healed ridge) guided surgery (comparator benchmark).
- Putra et al — CAD/CAM template-guided lower angular deviation than conventional.
- Yolanda et al — tooth-supported guides more accurate than bone/mucosa-supported.
- Zhou et al — mandibular guided placement more accurate than maxillary.
- Cassetta et al; Chen et al — fully guided more accurate than half-guided.

## 7. Glossary

- **Coronal deviation** — 3D distance between planned and placed implant at the implant platform/coronal point.
- **Apical deviation** — 3D distance between planned and placed implant at the apex.
- **Depth deviation** — vertical (apico-coronal) error between planned and placed implant.
- **Angular deviation** — angle between planned and placed implant long axes (degrees).
- **Static CAIS** — static computer-assisted implant surgery using a sleeve-bearing surgical template (vs dynamic navigation / robotic).
- **Fully guided vs half guided** — fully: guide directs all steps incl. implant insertion; half: osteotomy guided, then guide removed and implant placed freehand.
- **NOS** — Newcastle–Ottawa Scale for cohort study risk of bias (max 9).
