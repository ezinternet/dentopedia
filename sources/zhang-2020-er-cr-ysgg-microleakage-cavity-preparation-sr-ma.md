## Why Ingested

사용자 요청: 용도별 핸드피스 적응증·bur 종류별 역할 풀텍스트 인제스트. `dental-handpiece` 카테고리에서 회전식 bur의 대안 cutting instrument(Er,Cr:YSGG 레이저) 적응증을 정량 비교한 첫 SR+MA — "언제 레이저, 언제 bur"라는 instrument 선택 질문에 직접 답한다. 같은 카테고리의 Er:YAG 레이저 vs bur caries removal을 다룬 [[dental-handpiece/matys-2020-dental-aerosol-hazard-risk-dental]]를 수복 marginal seal 결과로 확장.

## One-line Summary

First SR+MA (13 in-vitro studies, 11 meta-analyzed) finding Er,Cr:YSGG laser cavity preparation produces microleakage equivalent to traditional burs, and that adjunctive acid etching significantly improves self-etch adhesive sealing after laser preparation.

## 한줄요약

첫 SR+MA(인비트로 13편, 11편 메타분석) — Er,Cr:YSGG 레이저 와동형성은 전통적 bur와 미세누출이 동등하며, 레이저 형성 후 자가부식 접착제에 산부식을 추가하면 미세누출이 유의하게 감소.

## 1. Document Information

- Journal: BMC Oral Health 2020 Oct 6;20(1):269

- PRISMA SR+MA; databases PubMed, EBSCO, Embase, Cochrane CENTRAL to July 2019.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic synthesis of Er,Cr:YSGG laser vs traditional bur for cavity-preparation microleakage.
- Establishes laser ≈ bur on marginal seal (no significant difference) — neither instrument superior for microleakage.
- Quantifies that prior acid etching strongly benefits SELF-ETCH adhesives after laser preparation (RR=2.69) but not etch-and-rinse.
- Frames the instrument-indication trade-off: laser offers less vibration/noise/pain/heat but no microleakage advantage and parameter-sensitive risks.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Design: PRISMA systematic review + meta-analysis of RCT/quasi-RCT, human teeth.
- Inclusion: bur vs Er,Cr:YSGG microleakage; laser ± acid etching; dropout <20%.
- 13 included (11 meta-analyzed); 2001–2018; 5 primary-teeth studies; Class V in 10/13.
- RevMan 5.3; RR/MD + 95% CI; I²>50% random-effects; Cochrane risk-of-bias (7 domains); reviewer agreement κ=0.93.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Microleakage (laser vs bur), random-effects, high heterogeneity (I²=80%): NS overall — dentin margin RR=1.26 (0.67–2.38, p=0.47); whole margin RR=1.27 (0.44–3.67, p=0.66); enamel margin RR=0.87 (0.60–1.27, p=0.47).
- Acid etching after laser (self-etch): RR=2.69 (1.74–4.15, p<0.00001; I²=0%); enamel RR=3.0 (1.54–5.83); dentin RR=2.44 (1.38–4.34).
- Acid etching after laser (etch-and-rinse): NS, RR=1.18 (0.63–2.22, p=0.60; I²=81%).
- All included studies in vitro, dye-penetration microleakage.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- All in-vitro; moderate risk of bias across all studies (poor randomization/allocation/blinding reporting).
- High heterogeneity (tooth/cavity/restoration/adhesive/laser-parameter variability).
- Mostly sound (non-carious) teeth as models; under-reported laser parameters.
- Future: higher-quality RCTs, optimal Er,Cr:YSGG parameters, long-term restoration outcomes.

## 6. Related Work

- Er:YAG/Er,Cr:YSGG caries-removal & cavity-prep literature (erbium family lasers as bur alternatives).
- Self-etch vs etch-and-rinse adhesive bonding to laser-modified dentin/enamel.

## 7. Glossary

- Er,Cr:YSGG: erbium, chromium-doped YSGG laser (2780 nm), high water absorption; hard/soft tissue ablation.
- Microleakage: passage of bacteria/fluid/ions through tooth–restoration marginal gap.
- Self-etch vs etch-and-rinse adhesive: adhesive systems differing in whether a separate acid-etch step is used.
- Smear layer: debris layer on cut dentin; absent after laser ablation.
- Dye penetration: common in-vitro microleakage assay.
