## One-line Summary
In-vitro bench study (4 bulk-fill composites, n=3-5) finding that different bulk-fill types show comparable polymerization shrinkage, that filler content (wt%) strongly correlates with both shrinkage and fracture toughness, and that sonication (SonicFill 3) and pre-heating (Viscalor) enhance flowability without increasing shrinkage or reducing fracture toughness.

## 한줄요약
In-vitro (벌크필 4종, ATR-FTIR + bonded-disk): 벌크필 종류 간 중합수축은 비슷하고, 충전율(wt%)이 수축·파괴인성과 강하게 상관하며, sonication(SonicFill 3)·예열(Viscalor)은 수축 증가나 파괴인성 저하 없이 흐름성만 개선.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Direct head-to-head of real-time polymerization kinetics (DC, RPmax) + shrinkage strain kinetics (PS, PS Rmax) + fracture toughness (KIC) for four contemporary bulk-fills with built-in viscosity-reduction features
- Showed bulk-fill PS is broadly comparable across types (only OBF highest), but PS Rmax (rate) differs significantly (p=0.047; SF3 highest)
- Established strong filler content (wt%) ↔ PS and ↔ KIC correlations
- Demonstrated sonication and pre-heating are "free" flowability boosts — no shrinkage or toughness penalty
- Pre-heating duration (30 s vs 3 min) of Viscalor had no significant effect on any property

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 2-mm-deep mold specimens; SF3 sonic insertion; VC pre-heated at 68 °C (T3-30s, T3-3min)
- Irradiation: Elipar S10 LED, 1200 mW/cm², 40 s, zero distance
- DC at 5 min and 24 h by ATR-FTIR (n=3); PS by bonded-disk on 1-mm discs over 1 h (n=3); PS Rmax by numerical differentiation
- KIC: single-edge-notched 32×6×3 mm, three-point bending after 7 d water storage (n=5)
- One-way ANOVA, independent T-tests, Tukey (p<0.05)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- DC/RPmax: SF3 highest (p<0.05), then OBF; VC ≈ BBR regardless of pre-heating
- PS: no significant difference except OBF highest; PS Rmax significantly varied (p=0.047), SF3 highest
- KIC: BBR (giomer) lowest (p<0.05); others similar
- Strong filler wt% correlations with PS and KIC
- Pre-heating time: no significant effect on DC, RPmax, PS, PS Rmax, KIC of VC

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small n (3-5), single irradiance protocol/distance, bench-only (no tooth/cavity, no C-factor variation)
- Abstract-only ingest; full numerical tables not extracted
- No clinical extrapolation; degree-of-conversion vs shrinkage trade-off not modeled across materials

## 7. Glossary
- **DC**: degree of conversion. **RPmax**: maximum polymerization rate. **PS**: polymerization shrinkage (%). **PS Rmax**: maximum shrinkage strain rate. **KIC**: mode-I fracture toughness. **Giomer**: S-PRG-filler-containing composite (BBR).
