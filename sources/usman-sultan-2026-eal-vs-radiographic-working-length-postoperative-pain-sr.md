## Why Ingested

기존 [[endodontics/eal/kaur-2024-eal-vs-radiograph-working-length-sr-ma]]는 EAL vs 방사선 비교에서 정확도·obturation 적합성을 1차 결과로 삼고 통증은 부차적(추출치 포함, 3편, very low certainty)으로만 다뤘다. 본 SR (Usman Sultan 2026)은 *standalone* EAL vs *standalone* 방사선만으로 좁혀 **술후 통증을 1차 결과로** 직접 합성한 첫 리뷰로, kaur-2024의 통증 endpoint를 RCT-only로 확장·정교화한다. 결론적으로 두 방법 간 통증 차이가 없다는 점을 RCT 근거로 보강.

## One-line Summary
Systematic review (4 RCTs, n=414, single-visit RCT, vital teeth) found no statistically significant difference in postoperative pain incidence, intensity, analgesic consumption, or time to resolution between standalone EAL and radiographic working length determination (4 h–7 days); one small three-arm trial showed lower early pain (6–48 h) with a hybrid EAL+radiograph method; very low certainty.

## 한줄요약
SR (RCT 4편, n=414, 단일내원 근관치료·생활치): standalone EAL과 방사선 근관장측정 간 술후 통증·진통제 사용·통증 소실시간 차이 없음(4시간–7일); 소규모 3-arm 1편에서 EAL+방사선 hybrid가 초기(6–48시간) 통증 더 낮음; 근거 확실성 매우 낮음.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First systematic review restricted to RCTs directly comparing **standalone** EAL vs **standalone** radiographic working length (WL) determination with **postoperative pain as the primary outcome**.
- Distinguishes its scope from prior SRs: Kaur et al. (accuracy/obturation focus, pain secondary, extracted teeth) and Kumar et al. (conflated hybrid + standalone methods → heterogeneity).
- Concludes EAL and radiographic WL determination yield **equivalent postoperative pain** — gives clinicians method freedom based on patient context (radiation sensitivity, anatomy).
- Flags a hybrid/simultaneous-length-control (SLC) signal: one small three-arm trial showed lower early pain.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- PRISMA 2020; PROSPERO CRD420251247626.
- Databases: PubMed (MEDLINE), Cochrane CENTRAL, ScienceDirect; Jan 1, 2000 – Nov 17, 2025. English, human, peer-reviewed RCTs only; no grey literature.
- PICOS: permanent teeth needing RCT; EAL (any generation) vs conventional/digital radiography; primary outcome postoperative pain (VAS/NRS/verbal scale at 24 h, 48 h, 7 d); secondary WL accuracy, procedural time, radiation, healing, survival, side effects.
- Screening in Rayyan (blinded), 2 reviewers, Cohen's kappa; RoB 2.0 (5 domains).
- 368 records → 330 after dedup → 22 full-text → **4 RCTs** (Kara Tuncer & Gerek; Khan; Naeem; Saha).
- No meta-analysis performed due to heterogeneity (pain scales, tooth types, follow-up).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **4 RCTs (2014–2024), Egypt/Pakistan/India/Turkey, total n = 414** (female 200, male 214); sample sizes 54–220; ages 14–60; mostly single-rooted teeth (Naeem: multi-rooted mandibular molars); pulp mostly vital/asymptomatic irreversible pulpitis (Saha: symptomatic irreversible pulpitis).
- EAL devices: third-generation DentaPort ZX, Root ZX (J. Morita), Propex Pixi (Dentsply), one unspecified resistance-based. Radiographic arms: digital systems (DIGORA Optime, Sopix2, Kodak RVG 5100). All single-visit, rotary NiTi, NaOCl + EDTA, rescue analgesia only (ibuprofen/flurbiprofen).
- **Primary (postoperative pain): no statistically significant difference EAL vs radiographic at any time point (4 h–7 d).**
  - Naeem: cumulative VAS EAL 0.96±1.25 vs digital radiograph 0.73±1.37, p=0.29.
  - Khan: 48 h VAS EAL 4.27±0.48 vs radiographic 4.35±0.39, NS.
  - Kara Tuncer & Gerek: 4-point scale first 48 h EAL 1.26±0.31 vs radiographic 1.20±0.31, p>0.05.
- **Hybrid SLC arm (Saha, n=60, symptomatic):** lower pain at 6/24/48/72 h vs standalone methods, significant at select early time points — but small single trial.
- **Secondary:** analgesic intake NS (Naeem p=0.529; Saha 8 radiographic vs 3 EAL vs 2 SLC, NS); pain resolution 24–26 h to ~1.35–3.88 days, NS by method.
- **Safety:** no flare-ups or serious adverse events; only minor self-limiting discomfort.
- **RoB 2.0:** all four trials "some concerns" (randomization, blinding, outcome measurement); none high-risk. **Overall certainty: very low.**

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Only 4 small studies; no meta-analysis (heterogeneity); mostly single-rooted teeth; English-only post-2000; no grey literature; inconsistent reporting of procedure time/radiation.
- All trials carry "some concerns" RoB (blinding/randomization) → possible detection/performance bias that may overestimate equivalence.
- Future: large multi-center blinded RCTs, standardized pain protocols, long-term outcomes (healing/survival); subgroups (necrotic, curved, pediatric); EAL vs CBCT cost-effectiveness.

## 7. Glossary
- **WL (Working Length)**: distance from a coronal reference to the apical termination point of instrumentation/obturation.
- **EAL (Electronic Apex Locator)**: device detecting the apical constriction via electrical impedance/resistance; radiation-free.
- **SLC (Simultaneous Length Control)**: hybrid method combining EAL reading + radiographic confirmation.
- **RoB 2.0**: Cochrane Risk of Bias 2.0 tool (5 domains).
- **VAS / verbal rating scale**: validated postoperative pain measurement instruments.
