## One-line Summary
SR+MA of 21 longitudinal studies showing furcation involvement (FI) approximately doubles the risk of molar tooth loss during SPT over 10–15 years, with degree II and III FI showing gradual risk increase, yet most furcation-involved molars can be retained with periodontal therapy.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Periodontology, 2016; 43(2):156–166
- **DOI**: 10.1111/jcpe.12497
- **First published**: 21 December 2015 (online)

- **Funding**: No specific funding declared; no conflicts of interest

## 2. Key Contributions
- Quantifies relative risk of tooth loss attributable to furcation involvement during maintenance therapy
- Demonstrates time-dependent risk: RR doubles from borderline significance at ≤10 years to highly significant at 10–15 years
- Provides evidence that most furcation-involved molars (including grade III) can be successfully maintained with periodontal therapy
- Advocates for tooth preservation over extraction even for advanced furcation involvement
- Highlights need for long-term studies with patient-reported outcomes

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: Systematic review with meta-analysis
- **Search**: Ovid Medline, Embase, LILACS, Cochrane Library
- **Initial search**: 1,207 titles
- **Included studies**: 21 longitudinal studies with ≥3 years follow-up
- **Inclusion criteria**: Longitudinal studies including FI measures and tooth loss data; ≥3-year follow-up
- **Subgroups analyzed**: 
  - Studies up to 10 years follow-up
  - Studies with 10–15 years follow-up
  - Degree I, II, III FI separately
  - Third molars included vs excluded

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
| Follow-up | Relative Risk | 95% CI | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Up to 10 years | 1.46 | 0.99–2.15 | 0.06 (borderline) |
| 10–15 years | 2.21 | 1.79–2.74 | <0.0001 |

- Gradual increase in risk for degree II and III FI compared to degree I
- Most molars even with grade III FI respond well to periodontal therapy
- Third molar inclusion/exclusion did not substantially alter results (separate funnel plots)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Heterogeneity in FI classification systems across studies
- Most studies retrospective in nature; few prospective designs
- Limited patient-reported outcome data
- Variation in SPT protocols across included studies
- Third molar data complicates some analyses
- Need for long-term prospective studies with standardized FI measurement

## 7. Glossary
- **Furcation involvement (FI)**: Extension of periodontal disease into the furcation area of multi-rooted teeth
- **Degree I FI**: Early involvement; probe penetrates slightly into furcation
- **Degree II FI**: Moderate involvement; probe penetrates >3 mm horizontally but does not pass through
- **Degree III FI**: Through-and-through involvement; probe passes completely through furcation
- **Relative risk (RR)**: Ratio of probability of tooth loss in FI group vs non-FI group
- **SPT**: Supportive periodontal therapy — maintenance phase after active treatment
- **LILACS**: Latin American and Caribbean Health Sciences Literature database
