## Why Ingested

Contributes an Iranian-population CBCT data point covering all three mandibular molars (first, second, third) and jointly reports C-shaped canals *and* three-rooted (radix entomolaris) prevalence, adding a second high-impact anatomical variant to the same cluster. It reinforces the C-shaped morphology baseline of [[endodontics/anatomy/song-2008-cross-sectional-morphology-and-minimum-canal]] and pairs with sibling prevalence studies [[endodontics/anatomy/shemesh-2017-cshaped-canals-cbct-mandibular-molars]], [[endodontics/anatomy/singh-2022-cshaped-canal-second-molars-indian-cbct]], and [[endodontics/anatomy/yu-2012-cbct-mandibular-premolars-root-canal-morphology]].

## One-line Summary

CBCT cross-sectional study (292 Iranian patients; 291 first, 402 second, 200 third mandibular molars): C-shaped canal prevalence 1.7% (first), 2% (second), 2% (third molars); three-rooted prevalence highest in first molars (2.7%), with most molars two-rooted.

## 한줄요약

이란인 환자 292명 CBCT 단면 연구(하악 제1대구치 291·제2대구치 402·제3대구치 200) — C-shaped canal 유병률 제1 1.7%·제2 2%·제3 2%, 3근치는 제1대구치(2.7%)에서 가장 높고 대부분의 대구치는 2근치였음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Clinical and Experimental Dental Research, 2023, 9(5):906–912
- **Study type**: Descriptive cross-sectional CBCT study
- **Population**: Iranian (Isfahan), 292 patients (141 M / 151 F), scans 2013–2021
- **Sample**: 291 first + 402 second + 200 third mandibular molars (893 teeth)
- **Device**: Sirona D-64625, 85 kV / 5.2 mA, 0.180 mm voxel, 1 mm slice; Fan et al. (2004) classification

## 2. Key Contributions

- Iranian-population prevalence for two clinically important variants in one dataset: C-shaped canals and three-rooted (radix entomolaris) mandibular molars.
- Covers all three molar positions including the often-overlooked third molar.
- Addresses prior conflicting Iranian reports by using a larger sample and high-resolution CBCT.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Descriptive cross-sectional design; simple random sampling from archival CBCT (1248 initially screened → 292 eligible).
- Inclusion: Iranian patients, fully erupted molars, mature apices; excluded resorption, immature roots, calcification, fracture, artifacts, restorations/crowns, prior RCT.
- Three calibrated examiners (graduate student, endodontist, oral/maxillofacial radiologist) assessed all three planes; radiologist arbitrated; 25% re-evaluated for intra/inter-examiner agreement.
- C-shaped class assigned per Fan et al. (2004); χ² test for gender correlation.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Mandibular first molars (n=291):**
- Roots: single-rooted 0.7%, two-rooted 96.6%, three-rooted 2.7%.
- C-shaped canal: 1.7%.

**Mandibular second molars (n=402):**
- Roots: single-rooted 8.5%, two-rooted 90.5%, three-rooted 1%.
- C-shaped canal: 2%.

**Mandibular third molars (n=200):**
- Roots: single-rooted 21.5%, two-rooted 77.5%, three-rooted 1%.
- C-shaped canal: 2%.

- Most mandibular molars were two-rooted; three-rooted molars (radix entomolaris) were most common in first molars.
- Second and third molars showed the higher C-shaped prevalence.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-center archival sample; referral and regional bias possible.
- 1 mm slice thickness limits detection of fine isthmuses/accessory canals.
- Cross-sectional prevalence only — no treatment-outcome data.

## 6. Related Work

- Fan et al. 2004 — C-shaped canal anatomical classification (method reference).
- Tu et al. 2007 — high (~21%) three-rooted first molars in Taiwanese/Chinese population (comparator).
- Rahimi et al. 2017; Kuzekanani et al. 2017 — prior Iranian three-rooted / radix entomolaris prevalence.

## 7. Glossary

- **C-shaped canal**: Ribbon-/C-shaped canal cross-section linking canals by a fin; complicates debridement and obturation.
- **Three-rooted mandibular molar / radix entomolaris (RE)**: Additional (usually distolingual) root on a mandibular molar; missed roots cause endodontic failure.
- **Radix paramolaris**: Additional buccally located supernumerary root (counterpart to radix entomolaris).
