## Why Ingested

Provides the clinical-recognition foundation (lesion morphology, subtyping, biopsy/referral triggers) underpinning the nomenclature in [[oral-medicine/opmd/warnakulasuriya-2021-opmd-consensus-nomenclature-who]] and the management scope of [[oral-medicine/opmd/warnakulasuriya-2020-opmd-comprehensive-review]].

## One-line Summary

Narrative review describing the clinical features and presentation of the full OPMD spectrum (leukoplakia, erythroplakia, OSF, OLP, oral lichenoid/GvHD lesions), stressing clinical subtyping, mandatory biopsy, and timely specialist referral.

OPMD 전 스펙트럼(백반증·홍반증·구강점막하섬유증·OLP·태선양/GvHD 병변)의 임상 양상을 기술한 내러티브 리뷰 — 임상 아형분류, 필수 조직검사, 적시 전문의 의뢰 강조.

## 1. Document Information

- Oral Surgery Oral Medicine Oral Pathology Oral Radiology 2018;125(6):582-590. Focus-issue lead review.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Catalogs the clinical appearance of each OPMD as a diagnostic prelude for clinicians.
- Expands the OPMD umbrella to include oral lichenoid lesions and oral GvHD lesions.
- Reinforces biopsy as essential to confirm provisional clinical diagnosis.
- Notes population-specific entities (e.g., OSF in South-Asian areca users; palatal lesions in reverse smokers).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Narrative clinical review by a single authoritative author; descriptive, no quantitative synthesis.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Leukoplakia is the most common OPMD; erythroplakia is rarer but carries higher malignant risk.
- Homogeneous vs non-homogeneous leukoplakia subtyping aids (limited) prognostication.
- Many OPMDs are asymptomatic early and detected on routine dental examination — underscoring the dentist's screening role.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative scope; subtyping has only limited prognostic value; no transformation-rate pooling.

## 6. Related Work

- WHO precancer/OPMD classifications (1978, 2007).
- Concurrent focus-issue articles on OPMD pathology and management.

## 7. Glossary

- OPMD: Oral Potentially Malignant Disorder.
- OSF: Oral Submucous Fibrosis.
- PPOEL: Potentially Premalignant Oral Epithelial Lesion (proposed term).
