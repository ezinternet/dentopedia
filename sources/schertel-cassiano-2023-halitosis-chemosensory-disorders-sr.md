## One-line Summary

Systematic review (Oral Diseases, 2023) examining the bidirectional association between halitosis and chemosensory disorders (olfactory and gustatory), finding evidence that halitosis patients may report altered smell/taste perception, likely via shared oral/nasal pathophysiological mechanisms.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First systematic review** to specifically address the association between halitosis and chemosensory (olfactory and gustatory) disorders.
2. Identifies shared pathophysiological pathways linking oral malodor production and impaired chemosensory function — volatile sulfur compounds (VSCs) and microbial metabolites may directly affect olfactory receptors.
3. Highlights that clinicians assessing halitosis patients should consider co-existing smell or taste disturbances as part of the clinical evaluation.
4. Provides a bibliographic foundation linking oral health conditions (periodontal disease, tongue coating, xerostomia) to chemosensory complaints.
5. Examines the bidirectionality: halitosis may cause or worsen olfactory/gustatory disturbance, and chemosensory dysfunction may alter patient perception of their own oral malodor (self-reporting bias).

- **Databases searched**: Likely PubMed/MEDLINE, Scopus, Web of Science (consistent with other SRs from this Aarhus group)
- **Inclusion criteria**: Studies reporting on the association between halitosis (objectively measured or self-reported oral malodor) and chemosensory disorders (olfactory or gustatory disturbance, including hyposmia, anosmia, hypogeusia, dysgeusia)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Domain | Finding |
|---|---|
| Association direction | Bidirectional — halitosis patients show altered chemosensory function; chemosensory-impaired patients may not perceive their own halitosis |
| Olfactory findings | Prior study (Altundag et al., 2015, Chemical Senses) found impaired olfactory function in chronic halitosis patients |
| Gustatory pathway | Oral microbial products (VSCs, indole, skatole) disrupt taste bud homeostasis (via tongue film composition changes) |
| Saliva/xerostomia link | Reduced salivary flow (xerostomia) reduces chemosensory stimulus delivery to taste receptors, linking halitosis-associated dry mouth to taste disturbance |
| Oral health — food perception | Oral health status influences food flavor perception (Batisse et al., 2017, J Oral Rehab SR) |
| Non-oral etiology | Non-oral causes of halitosis (systemic disease) can also alter chemosensation (Preti et al., 1992) |
| Population prevalence | Smell/taste alterations common: ~13–23% in NHANES 2011–2012 (Rawal et al., 2016) |

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Short communication**: Condensed format limits detailed methodology and GRADE assessment reporting
- **Heterogeneity**: Included studies likely differ in halitosis measurement methods (organoleptic scoring, VSC gas chromatography, portable sulfide monitors) and chemosensory assessment tools (Sniffin' Sticks, UPSIT, electrogustometry, taste strips)
- **Self-report bias**: Patients with anosmia/hyposmia may underreport their own halitosis, complicating prevalence estimates
- **No meta-analysis**: Qualitative synthesis only; quantitative pooling not possible due to study heterogeneity
- **Future directions**: Need for prospective studies with standardized simultaneous measurement of VSC levels and validated chemosensory tests; COVID-19 anosmia cohorts provide a new natural experiment to test this association

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Chemosensory disorder | Disturbance in olfaction (smell) or gustation (taste); includes anosmia, hyposmia, parosmia, phantosmia, hypogeusia, dysgeusia |
| Halitosis | Oral malodor; unpleasant odor emanating from the oral cavity, predominantly from volatile sulfur compounds (VSCs: H₂S, CH₃SH) |
| VSC (Volatile Sulfur Compound) | H₂S, methyl mercaptan, dimethyl sulfide — main chemical mediators of oral malodor, produced by anaerobic gram-negative bacteria |
| Anosmia / Hyposmia | Complete / partial loss of the sense of smell |
| Dysgeusia / Hypogeusia | Distorted / reduced sense of taste |
| Organoleptic assessment | Sensory evaluation of oral malodor by trained examiner (hedonic scale 0–5); the clinical gold standard |
| JBI methodology | Joanna Briggs Institute evidence synthesis framework for systematic reviews of etiology/risk |
| Olfactory desensitization | Adaptation/reduction in olfactory sensitivity with prolonged exposure to an odor (Stuck et al., 2014) — relevant to self-perception of halitosis |
