## Why Ingested

Most ridge-preservation (ARP) evidence reports linear/horizontal changes; true volumetric (mm³) human data with collagenated xenograft is scarce. This 80-subject CBCT cohort quantifies residual resorption *despite* Bio-Oss Collagen and contrasts site-specific behavior (maxillary molar worst), extending the DBBM/collagenated-xenograft ARP picture in [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/van-orten-2024-species-specific-collagen-dbbm-arp]] and [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/sanchez-perez-2025-dbbm-alveolar-ridge-preservation-histologic]] with a volumetric outcome and posterior (molar/premolar) focus.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective CBCT cohort (n=80; 60 with complete imaging) finds ridge preservation with Bio-Oss Collagen still incurs ~3–18% residual alveolar volume loss vs pre-extraction root volume (worst in maxillary molars ~18%, best in maxillary premolars ~3%); 78/80 sites achieved implant placement without further augmentation.

## 한줄요약

후향적 CBCT 코호트(n=80, 영상 완전 60)에서 Bio-Oss Collagen 치조제보존 후에도 발치 전 치근 체적 대비 약 3~18%의 잔여 골흡수가 남았고(상악 대구치 최대 ~18%, 상악 소구치 최소 ~3%), 80건 중 78건은 추가 증대 없이 임플란트 식립이 가능했다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- One of the largest human samples (n=80) reporting **volumetric (mm³)** rather than linear alveolar change after ARP with a collagenated bovine/porcine xenograft (Bio-Oss Collagen, BO).
- Quantifies **site-specific residual resorption (3–18%)** despite grafting, identifying maxillary molar sockets as the worst (~18%) and maxillary premolars best (~3%).
- Demonstrates clinical endpoint relevance: 78/80 sites proceeded to implant placement **without additional augmentation**.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: retrospective cohort; predictor = pre-extraction tooth-root volume (apex to crestal bone) as surrogate for "ideal" alveolar volume; outcome = post-preservation alveolar volume (socket + BO segments).
- **Inclusion**: age >18; carious/periodontally compromised premolars or molars; ARP with BO; no other augmentation in ROI. **Exclusion**: severe periodontitis, current antiresorptive medication, missing/incomplete imaging.
- **Protocol**: atraumatic extraction, socket curettage, dense BO fill (100 or 250 mg) without pressure, gingival approximation + silk suture; antibiotics at surgeon discretion; ibuprofen 600 mg tid.
- **Volumetry**: 3D Slicer v5.3.0 manual segmentation; BO distinguished by higher radiopacity. When no pre-extraction CBCT, root volume estimated from panoramic radiograph (PR) via validated correlation (molar factor validated; premolar factor 5.38 ± 1.94 derived from 8 teeth with both CBCT+PR).
- **Analysis subset**: 20/80 had only analog PR/periapical (non-evaluable) → comparative analyses on **n=60** with complete imaging. Stats: SPSS v31, paired t-test/ANOVA, Pearson, multivariable regression; post hoc G-Power.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Sample**: 80 subjects (43 male), mean age 59 ± 12.5 y; 20 premolars, 60 molars (full cohort wording "61 teeth molars"). Comparative subset n=60 (33 M / 27 F; 15 premolars, 45 molars; mean age 59 ± 12.7 y).
- **Subset (n=60) volumes**:
  - Predictor (root volume): 451.64 ± 186.86 mm³ (premolars 192.57 ± 42.60; molars 538.04 ± 125.31).
  - After ARP: 394.86 ± 174.85 mm³ (premolars 187.92 ± 57.22; molars 463.84 ± 143.15); mean 334.70 ± 175.36 mm³ filled with BO.
- **Site-specific (pre → post, mm³)**:
  - Maxillary premolar 195.20 → 192.07 (~3% loss, lowest)
  - Maxillary molar 470.41 → 381.96 (~18% loss, highest)
  - Mandibular premolar 220.42 → 199.86
  - Mandibular molar 544.76 → 475.85
- **Significance**: predictor vs outcome volume decrease significant (p<0.001, t-test); 3–18% range depended on tooth type / medical condition (p<0.008).
- **Covariates**: multivariable regression — **none significant** (all p>0.05); anticoagulant therapy largest coefficient (B=110.1, p=0.075, NS). Initial univariate "medical condition" signal attributed to confounding by tooth type/defect size.
- **Clinical endpoint**: implant placement without further augmentation in **78/80** (2 failed for low bone quality / primary stability).
- **Context**: 3–18% loss compares favorably to non-preserved sockets (bucco-oral ~40–60%, vertical ~10–20%).
- **Power**: Cohen's d = 0.63; ≥22 matched pairs needed for 80% power; 60 pairs analyzed → adequately powered.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Retrospective; uneven tooth-type/arch distribution; selection bias from imaging availability (only 17 pre-extraction CBCT; 43 digital PR; 20 excluded).
- Root-volume surrogate excludes PDL space and periapical/periodontal osteolytic lesions → may underestimate true defect, **overestimating** ARP success; 13 cases showed apparent volume *gain* (likely measurement artifact from septum loss / PDL overfill / lytic-area bone fill).
- Premolar PR→volume calibration (5.38 ± 1.94) from only 8 teeth.
- Implant-without-augmentation rate is cohort-specific; authors caution against universal interpretation. Prospective studies needed.

## 7. Glossary

- **ARP (Alveolar Ridge Preservation)**: socket grafting to limit post-extraction dimensional loss.
- **Bio-Oss Collagen (BO)**: deproteinized bovine bone mineral combined with ~10% porcine collagen, block/sponge form.
- **Volumetric analysis**: 3D mm³ measurement (here via 3D Slicer on CBCT) capturing both horizontal and vertical change, unlike linear measures.
- **Predictor (root-volume surrogate)**: pre-extraction tooth-root volume used as proxy for the "ideal" alveolar volume to be preserved.
