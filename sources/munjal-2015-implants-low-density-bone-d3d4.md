## One-line Summary
Clinical study (n=14, D3/D4 bone, Maestro™ implants, 12-month follow-up): 92.3% survival rate; mean bone loss not statistically significant; specially designed implants viable in poor bone quality.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Contemporary Clinical Dentistry 2015; 6(1):40–44
- **Study type**: Prospective clinical study

## 2. Key Contributions
- Demonstrates viability of specifically designed (square-thread) implants in D3/D4 bone
- 92.3% survival rate at 12 months — clinically acceptable for low-density bone
- Mean bone loss not statistically significant at 12 months
- Supports that poor bone quality is not an absolute contraindication

## 3. Results
- 14 patients, 13 implants (Maestro™ — square thread, D3/D4 specific)
- Survival rate: 92.3%
- Bone loss: statistically not significant at 12 months
- Square thread design reduces shear forces 10× vs V-thread
