## Why Ingested

The clear-aligner library has multiple pages on specific TAD-assisted movements — [[orthodontics/clear-aligner/bhate-2025-cat-maxillary-molar-distalization-class-ii-sr]] (maxillary molar distalization, where TADs are a key anchorage adjunct) and [[orthodontics/clear-aligner/inchingolo-2023-clear-aligner-distalization-predictability-sr]] (distalization predictability) — but no page synthesizing the **combined aligner + TAD modality across all movement types**. This Marinelli 2025 SR is the dedicated cross-indication review of that combination, extending the distalization-focused pages to intrusion/extrusion/asymmetry/open-bite and the surgery-first protocol. It also links to the orthodontic-miniscrew (TAD) stability literature in [[orthodontics/alharbi-2018-miniscrew-failure-rate-orthodontics-sr-ma]] by motivating why anchorage stability matters in aligner cases. Note: shares senior authors (Inchingolo F., Dipalma G.) with [[orthodontics/clear-aligner/inchingolo-2023-clear-aligner-distalization-predictability-sr]].

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (PRISMA, 14 studies — mostly case reports/small series, ROBINS-I: 1 low / 9 some-concerns / 4 high risk of bias) finding that combining clear aligners with temporary anchorage devices (TADs/miniscrews) improves precision and control of complex movements (intrusion, extrusion, distalization), but the evidence base is weak (no controlled comparisons, scarce RCTs).

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰 (PRISMA, 14편 — 대부분 증례보고/소규모 시리즈, ROBINS-I 비뚤림: 1편 낮음 / 9편 일부우려 / 4편 높음): 투명교정장치 (Clear Aligner)에 임시고정원장치 (Temporary Anchorage Device, TAD; 미니스크류)를 병용하면 압하·정출·후방이동 같은 복잡한 치아이동의 정밀도·조절력이 향상되나, 대조군·무작위배정연구가 거의 없어 근거 수준은 낮다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First/among the few dedicated SRs synthesizing the **combined clear-aligner + TAD modality** rather than aligners or skeletal anchorage separately.
- Maps the **indication space** where aligner+TAD outperforms aligners alone: molar distalization (esp. Class III lower-molar and Class II upper-molar), intrusion/vertical control in hyperdivergent cases, anterior open-bite, alveolar protrusion, impacted-canine traction, dental asymmetry, and surgery-first skeletal-discrepancy cases.
- Formal risk-of-bias appraisal via ROBINS-I across 14 included studies, making explicit that the modality currently rests on case reports/small series.
- Flags a research-gap agenda: comparative effectiveness, patient-reported outcomes, aligner material behavior under load with TADs, and long-term stability.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases**: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science; English; 1 Jan 2004 – 17 Jul 2024.
- **Search**: Boolean (aligners / clear aligners / transparent aligners / Invisalign) AND (TADs / miniscrews / skeletal anchorage / micro-implants / orthodontic anchorage systems, etc.).
- **Eligibility (PICOS)**: clinical studies (RCT, retrospective, case series, case reports) of orthodontic treatment with **both** aligners and TADs; full text in English. Excluded: animal, in vitro, off-topic, reviews/letters/comments, non-English.
- **Screening**: 458 records → 369 after de-duplication → 355 excluded (348 off-topic, 7 reviews) → **14 included**.
- **Selection/extraction**: three reviewers (G.M., A.D.I., G.D.) independently; Zotero v6.0.15; disagreements adjudicated by senior reviewer (F.I.).
- **Quality**: ROBINS-I (7 domains) by two reviewers (M.C., P.A.), third-reviewer adjudication.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Yield**: 14 studies in qualitative synthesis (no meta-analysis — heterogeneous designs/protocols/outcomes preclude pooling). Predominantly case reports and small case series.
- **Risk of bias (ROBINS-I)**: 1 study low risk, 9 some concerns, 4 high risk (remainder unclear). High bias driven by confounding and selective reporting of results; measurement and participant-selection domains generally low risk.
- **Clinical signal**: combining TADs with aligners improves precision and control for movements aligners struggle with alone — distalization, intrusion, extrusion, vertical control. TADs supply tooth-independent anchorage (with coil springs, elastics, or direct attachment), reducing aligner stages and reciprocal/anchorage-loss movements.
- **Representative cases cited**: Greco (G-Block posterior skeletal anchorage after aligner distalization; impacted canines), Lu (preset intrusion bulbs, hyperdivergent skeletal Class II, 4-yr follow-up stability), Auladell (mini-implant molar distalization), Kottemann (dental asymmetry), Ojima (non-extraction Class II), Lin (3D analysis of mandibular-molar distalization), Iodice (gummy smile / mandibular retrognathia, surgery-first), De Almeida (forces of aligners with TSADs; aligner thermoplastics — polyurethane, PETG).
- **Patient-centered**: high satisfaction from aligner aesthetics/comfort; challenges = need for precise miniscrew placement and insertion discomfort.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Evidence dominated by case reports/small series; **no controlled comparisons, scarce RCTs** → low certainty; conclusions not generalizable.
- Substantial heterogeneity (designs, protocols, outcome measures); no quantitative synthesis possible.
- Short follow-up in most reports; selective-reporting bias common.
- Authors call for large-scale, long-term, standardized prospective studies; comparative effectiveness; PROs; aligner material-under-load behavior with TADs; defined case-selection criteria and standardized miniscrew-placement protocols.

## 6. Related Work

- Distalization-specific aligner reviews: Bhate 2025 (maxillary molar distalization Class II), Inchingolo 2023 (distalization predictability).
- Orthodontic miniscrew/TAD stability and failure literature (alharbi-2018, katyal-2024, jahanbin-2025) underpins the anchorage-reliability assumptions of this review.
- General CAT efficacy-vs-fixed overviews (yassir-2022) for the broader limitation context that motivates TAD adjuncts.

## 7. Glossary

- **TAD (Temporary Anchorage Device)** — small titanium miniscrew inserted temporarily into palatal/alveolar bone to provide tooth-independent anchorage; removed after treatment.
- **TSAD** — temporary skeletal anchorage device (synonym used by some included authors).
- **Clear aligner therapy (CAT)** — sequential transparent thermoplastic (e.g., polyurethane, PETG) appliances designed via digital workflow (intraoral scan → ClinCheck).
- **ROBINS-I** — Risk Of Bias In Non-randomized Studies of Interventions; 7-domain tool.
- **Distalization** — posterior (backward) movement of molars; a movement where aligners alone are biomechanically weak without skeletal anchorage.
- **Hyperdivergent** — high mandibular-plane-angle vertical skeletal pattern; vertical control is anchorage-demanding.
