## Why Ingested

This paper isolates the source of water entrapment in one-step self-etch adhesives by an elegant control: bonding to caries-affected dentin with *occluded* tubules removes the transdentinal water flux while leaving residual-solvent and substrate porosity intact. The result — occluded tubules eliminate water-treeing and water-droplets — directly shows that **transdentinal water flux from patent tubules**, not just incompletely evaporated solvent, drives water-treeing degradation. It reinforces the sibling mechanism page [[wiki/resin-bonding/tay-2003-water-treeing-degradation-dentin-adhesives]] (which characterizes water-treeing as a degradation pathway) by providing the causal "knock-out" experiment for the water source.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro TEM/silver-impregnation study (experimental single-bottle one-step self-etch adhesive): bonding to caries-affected dentin with occluded tubules eliminated water-treeing and water-droplets, whereas sound dentin showed both — demonstrating transdentinal water flux (not just residual solvent) drives water-treeing.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides a "knock-out" experiment isolating the *source* of water entrapment at resin–dentin interfaces of one-step self-etch adhesives.
- Demonstrates that **occluded dentinal tubules** (in transparent/caries-affected dentin) prevent water-treeing and water-droplet formation, while adjacent **sound dentin** (patent tubules) shows variable water-treeing and water-droplets.
- Establishes that water-treeing and water-droplets are manifestations of **evaporative and convective water fluxes**, and that the convective component depends on patent tubules conducting transdentinal water.
- Implicates **transdentinal water flux** — not merely incompletely removed adhesive solvent — as a primary driver of water entrapment.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- An **experimental single-bottle, one-step self-etch adhesive** was applied to flat surfaces of **caries-affected (transparent) dentin** containing **occluded dentinal tubules**, surrounded by a rim of **sound dentin** with patent tubules.
- Bonding was performed **with or without pulpal pressure** to manipulate the convective transdentinal water flux.
- Resin–dentin interfaces were examined by **transmission electron microscopy (TEM)** after **silver-impregnation** (silver acts as a tracer for water-filled channels/voids — water-treeing).
- The occluded-vs-sound contrast within the same specimen controls for solvent removal and bonding technique, isolating the tubular water-flux variable.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Although caries-affected dentin was **highly porous**, adhesive layers over it were **devoid of silver deposits when tubules were occluded** — i.e., no water-treeing.
- In bonded **sound dentin** (patent tubules), **variable extents of water-treeing and water-droplets** were identified within the adhesive layers.
- Water-treeing and water-droplet formation, being manifestations of **evaporative and convective water fluxes**, **can be eliminated** during bonding to occluded transparent carious dentin.
- Caveat: the **highly porous nature** of this clinically relevant (caries-affected) substrate after bonding may itself lead to **potentially undesirable consequences** (e.g., compromised bond durability), despite the absence of water-treeing.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only ingest — no quantitative figures (bond strength, silver-uptake quantification, sample sizes) recoverable from the abstract.

## 7. Glossary

- **Water-treeing**: Branching, tree-like silver-impregnable channels within the adhesive layer, marking water permeation/percolation paths; a hallmark of interfacial degradation.
- **One-step self-etch adhesive**: A single-bottle "all-in-one" adhesive combining etchant, primer, and bonding resin; hydrophilic and prone to water uptake.
- **Transdentinal water flux**: Movement of water from the pulpal side through patent dentinal tubules toward the bonding surface; driven by pulpal pressure (convective) plus diffusion.
- **Caries-affected dentin**: Partially demineralized but remineralizable dentin with mineral-occluded ("sclerotic"/transparent) tubules; here used as a naturally tubule-occluded substrate.
- **Silver-impregnation (nanoleakage tracer)**: Ammoniacal silver nitrate staining that deposits metallic silver in water-filled nanovoids, visualized under TEM.
- **Evaporative vs convective water flux**: Evaporative = residual solvent/water escaping; convective = bulk water transported through tubules under pulpal pressure.
