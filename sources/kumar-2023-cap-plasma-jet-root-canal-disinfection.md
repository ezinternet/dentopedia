## Why Ingested

냉플라즈마(CAP) 제트의 근관 내 E. faecalis 항균 효과를 NaOCl 및 QMix와 직접 비교한 최초 in-vitro RCT급 연구로, 기존 [[endodontics/irrigation/ardila-2026-intracanal-medicaments-enterococcus-faecalis-sr]]의 E. faecalis 제거 전략에 CAP 플라즈마라는 신규 방법론을 추가한다.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro study (n=210 premolars) comparing CAP plasma jet vs 5.25% NaOCl vs QMix for E. faecalis root canal disinfection at 2, 5, and 10 min exposures; NaOCl was most effective, CAP plasma required 5 min for substantial CFU reduction.

## 한줄요약

단근 소구치 210개를 이용한 시험관 연구에서 냉대기압 플라즈마(CAP) 제트·5.25% NaOCl·QMix의 E. faecalis 사멸 효과를 2·5·10분 노출 시간별로 비교; NaOCl이 가장 효과적이며 CAP 플라즈마는 5분 이상 노출 시 유의한 CFU 감소.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Biomaterial Investigations in Dentistry, 2023, Vol. 10, No. 1, Article 2193214
- **DOI**: 10.1080/26415275.2023.2193214
- **Received**: 5 January 2023; **Accepted**: 16 March 2023
- **Institution**: All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS) Jodhpur; IIT Jodhpur
- **Study type**: In-vitro experimental study (single-blind, controlled)
- **Sample size**: 210 single-rooted mandibular premolars (n=21 per subgroup)

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First direct comparison of CAP plasma jet vs 5.25% NaOCl vs QMix at three time intervals (2, 5, 10 min) in E. faecalis-infected root canals.
2. Establishes minimum contact time thresholds: NaOCl needs ≥5 min for zero bacterial growth; QMix needs ≥10 min for optimal CFU reduction; CAP plasma needs ≥5 min for substantial CFU reduction.
3. Demonstrates CAP plasma jet as a viable antibacterial adjunct in endodontics, though inferior to NaOCl at all time points.
4. Uses Zero-Inflated Poisson (ZIP) statistical model suitable for excess-zero CFU count data.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Specimen preparation**: 210 human caries-free single-rooted mandibular premolars; decoronated to 12 mm standard length; shaped with ProTaper Universal to F3; irrigated with 3% NaOCl + 17% EDTA; apical foramen sealed with composite resin.

**Infection protocol**: Teeth immersed in BHI broth containing 10^5 CFU/mL E. faecalis (ATCC 29212) for 7 days, anaerobically.

**CAP plasma jet design**: Dielectric barrier discharge plasma jet; 5 kV peak-to-peak sinusoidal HV source; helium working gas at 2.5 L/min; plume delivered via 23-gauge needle.

**Groups** (n=21 each):
- CAP plasma jet: 2, 5, 10 min subgroups
- 5.25% NaOCl: 2, 5, 10 min subgroups
- QMix: 2, 5, 10 min subgroups
- Control (0.9% saline): 10 min

**CFU assessment**: Barbed broach churning + F3 paper point collection → BHI broth culture → blood agar plating → CFU counting.

**Statistics**: ANOVA + Tukey's post-hoc; Zero-Inflated Poisson (ZIP) model; p<0.05 significance threshold.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **5.25% NaOCl** showed significantly greater antibacterial efficacy (p<0.001) vs all groups except QMix at 2 and 10 min exposures.
- **NaOCl at 5 min**: achieved zero bacterial growth (0 CFU) in E. faecalis canals.
- **QMix at 10 min**: required for optimal CFU reduction; less effective than NaOCl at 2 and 5 min but comparable at 10 min in some analyses.
- **CAP plasma jet at 5 min**: achieved substantial (significant) CFU reduction, but could not achieve zero growth even at 10 min.
- **CAP plasma jet at 2 min**: minimal antibacterial effect, not significantly different from control in some models.
- Both ANOVA and ZIP models showed statistically significant differences among treatment groups at all three time points (p<0.05).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro model: cannot replicate complex root canal anatomy (lateral canals, isthmuses, dentinal tubule penetration depth).
- Helium-based CAP plasma jet: helium is costly and not readily available in clinical settings; argon or air-based plasma devices may be more practical.
- CAP plasma plume delivery via 23-gauge needle: may not reach all areas of the root canal system, especially apical thirds.
- E. faecalis monoculture: clinical root canal infections are polymicrobial.
- No assessment of dentin microhardness changes or cytotoxicity after CAP plasma exposure.
- Future work: in-vivo studies, combination protocols (CAP + NaOCl), plasma-activated water/saline as irrigant, different plasma gas compositions.

## 6. Related Work

- Zehnder (2006): NaOCl as gold-standard endodontic irrigant for >70 years.
- QMix (chlorhexidine 2% + EDTA + surfactant): one-step irrigant; as effective as 17% EDTA for smear layer removal.
- CAP plasma in medicine: sterilization, wound healing, blood coagulation, tumor apoptosis well-documented.
- Bekeschus et al.: CAP plasma in oncology and infection control.
- Laroussi (2005): Cold plasma for biomedical applications — foundational reference.

## 7. Glossary

- **CAP plasma**: Cold Atmospheric Pressure plasma — partially ionized gas (electrons, ions, reactive oxygen/nitrogen species, UV photons) generated at room temperature and atmospheric pressure.
- **E. faecalis**: Enterococcus faecalis — Gram-positive facultative anaerobe; most common persistent pathogen in failed root canal treatments; highly resistant to antimicrobials due to biofilm formation.
- **CFU**: Colony-Forming Unit — measure of viable bacteria in a sample.
- **QMix**: Commercial endodontic irrigant (chlorhexidine 2% + EDTA + surfactant); one-step solution.
- **Dielectric barrier discharge**: Type of plasma generation using insulating material between electrodes to prevent arc formation and generate stable plasma.
- **ZIP model**: Zero-Inflated Poisson regression — statistical model for count data with excess zeros (many samples with 0 CFUs).
- **BHI broth**: Brain-Heart Infusion broth — nutrient medium for bacterial culture.
- **ProTaper Universal F3**: Rotary NiTi file sequence for root canal shaping; F3 = finishing file size #30/.09 taper.
