## One-line Summary
In vitro PU foam study: diameter > length for primary stability; tapered (TE) macro-design outperforms parallel in all bone densities; drilling protocol must adapt to simulated bone quality for optimal IT and ISQ.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Journal of Functional Biomaterials 14:469 (2023)
- DOI: 10.3390/jfb14090469
- Design: In vitro, polyurethane foam 35/25/15/10 PCF, 3 implant systems × multiple lengths/diameters

## 2. Key Contributions
- Diameter is a stronger determinant of primary ISQ than length
- Tapered Effect (TE) macro-design consistently produces higher ISQ and IT than parallel-walled
- Drilling protocol must be adapted to simulated bone quality (PCF density)

## 3. Methodology
- PU foam blocks of 4 densities: 35 PCF (D1), 25 PCF (D2), 15 PCF (D3), 10 PCF (D4)
- 3 implant systems tested (tapered vs parallel macro-designs)
- ISQ measured immediately post-placement with Osstell ISQ
- IT measured with digital torque wrench
- Variables: length (8–16mm), diameter (3.5–5.5mm), macro-design, bone density

## 4. Key Results
- Tapered (TE) optimal IT: 13 Ncm (35 PCF), 40 Ncm (25 PCF); parallel: 8–9 Ncm across groups
- Diameter increase: most significant driver of ISQ increase (p<0.01)
- Length increase: smaller marginal effect than diameter
- Dense foam (D1/D2): macro-design differences largest; soft foam (D3/D4): differences attenuate

## 5. Limitations
- In vitro PU foam ≠ clinical human bone (no cortical layer differentiation)
- Only immediate placement ISQ (no healing phase)
- Limited to 3 implant systems

## 6. Related Work
