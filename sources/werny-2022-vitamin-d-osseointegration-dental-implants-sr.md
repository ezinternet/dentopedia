## Why Ingested

Foundational systematic review establishing the vitamin D–osseointegration evidence base, which is animal-dominant (13 of 18 included studies are experimental animal work). It anchors the `implants/vitamin-d` category by mapping what is actually known vs. assumed, and is later extended by the [[implants/vitamin-d/tallon-2024-vitamin-d-osseointegration-umbrella-review]] umbrella review and by [[implants/vitamin-d/miron-2025-vitamin-d-deficiency-early-implant-failure]] (early implant failure focus). Connects to the classic implant-failure risk-factor anchor [[implants/moy-2005-dental-implant-failure-rates-risk]] by adding low serum vitamin D as a candidate modifiable failure risk.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (18 studies: 13 animal experimental + 2 clinical + 3 case reports; titanium implants 24–1740) finding vitamin D deficiency negatively affects new bone formation/BIC and supplementation enhances osseointegration in animals (especially with systemic disease), but only slight, high-risk-of-bias evidence in humans.

체계적 문헌고찰(18편: 동물실험 13 + 임상 2 + 증례보고 3; 타이타늄 임플란트 24~1740개) — 비타민 D 결핍은 신생골 형성·골-임플란트 접촉(Bone-to-Implant Contact, BIC)을 저하시키고, 보충은 특히 전신질환 동물에서 골융합을 향상시키나, 사람에서는 근거가 미약하고 비뚤림 위험이 높다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic mapping of the vitamin D ↔ osseointegration evidence across both animal and human subjects, framed by two PICO questions (deficiency → inferior osseointegration? supplementation → superior osseointegration?).
- Establishes that the evidence base is overwhelmingly preclinical: 13 of 18 included studies are animal experimental; human evidence is 2 clinical studies + 3 case reports only.
- Documents a consistent deficiency signal (3/3 animal deficiency studies showed reduced BIC/new bone formation) and a supplementation signal in disease models (8 animal studies showed enhanced BIC/NBF), while flagging that healthy-subject supplementation has only weak effect.
- Surfaces the human early-implant-failure association (Mangano: low serum 25(OH)D → higher early dental implant failure tendency) as the clinically actionable human-side finding.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Guideline**: PRISMA; two PICO-structured focus questions.
- **Databases**: PubMed/Medline, Cochrane Library, Google Scholar; searched 1969 → March 2021.
- **Search terms**: combinations of "implant", "bone to implant contact", "vitamin D", "osseointegration".
- **Eligibility**: original studies or case reports (clinical/experimental) with a control group (osseointegration without vitamin D supplementation/deficiency); English and German only. Excluded letters, historic reviews, commentaries.
- **Screening**: initial 60 hits → 42 excluded → 18 included; data qualitatively summarized (no meta-analysis).
- **Subjects**: mice, rats (8 studies), rabbits (3), dogs (1) for animal arm; humans for 2 clinical studies + 3 case reports.
- **Outcomes**: BIC, new bone formation (NBF), implant resistance (biomechanical), BV/TV; assessed by histology (gold standard), histomorphometry, micro-CT (3D), 2D radiography, SEM, wound-fluid analysis, and ISQ (one clinical study).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Implant count**: titanium implants ranged 24–1740 across studies (animal studies 24–168; clinical 4–1740). Follow-up 1–8 weeks (animal) and 12 weeks–7 months (human).
- **Deficiency (animal)**: 3/3 studies → significantly decreased BIC and/or new bone formation vs. control.
- **Supplementation (animal)**: 8 studies → enhanced BIC and/or NBF; ~75% of systemic-supplementation studies showed enhanced new bone formation.
- **Null results**: 4 studies showed no significant difference (vitamin D coated implants or systemic supplementation). Pimentel et al. — the only study in *healthy* supplemented animals — found no significant effect, supporting the "supplementation helps deficient, not replete" interpretation.
- **Systemic disease models**: nephrectomized mice → significant NBF/BIC gain with vitamin D; ovariectomized rats → improved implant resistance + bone area density with vitamin D + alendronate; diabetic rats → no significant effect from vitamin D alone, but insulin + vitamin D combination matched healthy controls.
- **Local/coated implants**: only 50% of animal coating studies showed enhanced BIC; effect of coatings remains controversial (Satué: opposing cellular effects at different coating concentrations — ALP/osteocalcin higher at low dose, LDH higher at high dose).
- **Human**: Mangano — low serum 25(OH)D associated with higher early dental implant failure (EDIF, failure within 4 months before abutment connection); Kwiatek — supplementation increased peri-implant bone level change (also used ISQ); case reports (Fretwurst, Flanagan) — deficient/ESRD patients successfully re-treated after supplementation.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Evidence base is animal-dominant; human evidence is only 2 clinical studies + 3 case reports, all weak and at high risk of bias (case reports especially).
- No meta-analysis; methods varied remarkably across studies (different species, assessment modalities, supplementation timing/dose), precluding pooling.
- Very short animal follow-up (≤12 weeks) — almost nothing known about long-term effects on implant survival.
- No consensus on how/when to supplement or measure 25(OH)D, in animals or humans.
- Authors call for longitudinal RCTs in humans, especially in patients with diabetes, osteoporosis, vitamin D deficiency and CKD.

## 7. Glossary

- **Osseointegration**: functional ankylosis — direct structural and functional connection between vital bone and implant surface.
- **BIC (Bone-to-Implant Contact)**: proportion of implant surface in direct contact with bone; primary osseointegration histomorphometric outcome.
- **NBF (New Bone Formation)**: amount of newly formed bone around the implant.
- **BV/TV (Bone Volume / Tissue Volume)**: micro-CT measure of trabecular bone fraction.
- **25(OH)D**: 25-hydroxyvitamin D — circulating metabolite used as the standard marker of vitamin D status (long half-life); the active form is 1,25(OH)₂D.
- **EDIF (Early Dental Implant Failure)**: implant failure within 4 months of placement, before prosthetic abutment connection.
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: resonance-frequency-derived index of implant stability.
- **CKD / ESRD**: chronic kidney disease / end-stage renal disease.
- **Micro-CT**: 3D micro-computed tomography, high-resolution bone density/architecture imaging.
