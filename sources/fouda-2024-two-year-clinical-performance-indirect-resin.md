## Why Ingested

[[wiki/inlay/angeletaki-2016-direct-vs-indirect-inlay-onlay-sr-ma]] compared direct vs indirect composite inlay/onlay broadly but did not resolve, within indirect CAD/CAM restorations of endodontically treated molars, whether cuspal coverage (overlay) outperforms cusp preservation (inlay). This 2024 2-year RCT reinforces the indirect-restoration evidence base by directly testing inlay vs overlay cavity design and favoring cuspal reduction in ETT.

## One-line Summary

2-year double-blind RCT (30 participants, MOD ETT molars): CAD/CAM nano-hybrid composite overlay (with cuspal reduction) showed better marginal adaptation, less discoloration/fracture, and less caries than the inlay (no cuspal reduction) design at 24 months.

## 1. Document Information

- Type: Randomized controlled clinical trial (superiority, double-blind, two parallel arms, 1:1)
- Published: BMC Oral Health 2024;24:1009
- DOI: 10.1186/s12903-024-04725-5
- Registration: ClinicalTrials.gov NCT04561167 (23 Sept 2020)
- Setting: Conservative Dentistry outpatient clinic, Faculty of Dentistry, Cairo University, Egypt
- Sample: 30 participants with MOD cavities in endodontically treated permanent molars (15/group; 26 completed)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides RCT evidence on cavity-design choice (inlay vs overlay) for CAD/CAM indirect composite in ETT molars.
- Generates prospective clinical data on CAD/CAM nano-hybrid composite (Brilliant Crios) — previously mostly inferred from SRs.
- Resolves the cuspal-coverage controversy in favor of cusp reduction for mutilated ETT.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 30 MOD ETT molars randomized 1:1: Group 1 inlay (no cuspal reduction); Group 2 overlay (cuspal reduction). Pulp chambers filled with bulk-fill flowable; restorations milled from nano-hybrid composite blocks (Brilliant Crios, Coltène).
- Modified USPHS evaluation at baseline, 6, 12, and 24 months.
- Power analysis: α=0.05, power 80%, base n=24 → +20% dropout buffer → 30.
- Normality by Shapiro-Wilk/Kolmogorov-Smirnov; P≤0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- 26 of 30 completed follow-up.
- At 6 and 12 months: overlay had significantly better marginal adaptation, less discoloration/tooth-or-restoration fracture, and similar marginal integrity and caries to inlay.
- At 24 months: overlay still had better marginal adaptation, less discoloration/fracture, and less caries than inlay; marginal integrity no longer differed between designs.
- Conclusion: cuspal reduction outperformed cusp preservation → overlay is more reliable in ETT.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample (n=30, 26 completed); single center.
- 2-year follow-up; longer-term survival unknown.
- Single CAD/CAM composite material (Brilliant Crios).
- USPHS is a relatively coarse clinical scoring instrument.

## 6. Related Work

- Angeletaki 2016 SR+MA — direct vs indirect inlay/onlay composite restorations.
- SR concluding indirect resin-based composites are as reliable as partial indirect restorations / glass-ceramics.

## 7. Glossary

- MOD: mesio-occluso-distal cavity.
- Inlay: intracoronal indirect restoration without cuspal coverage.
- Overlay: indirect restoration covering all cusps (cuspal reduction).
- PIR: partial indirect restoration (inlay/onlay/overlay).
- CAD/CAM nano-hybrid composite: milled composite block combining ceramic-like and composite advantages.
