## One-line Summary

Narrative review (Saudi Dental Journal 2020, PubMed search 2010–2019) providing a comprehensive classification of dental ceramics by composition (glass-based, glass-infiltrated, polycrystalline) and fabrication method (stacking/sintering, heat-pressing, slip-casting, dry-pressing, CAD/CAM), with clinical selection guidance.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Comprehensive overview of dental ceramics enabling material selection for specific clinical situations
- Three-tier composition-based classification: (1) glass-based, (2) glass-infiltrated (In-Ceram group), (3) non-glass-based (polycrystalline)
- Glass-based ceramics further subdivided: predominantly glass (feldspathic, <17% particles, 70–90 MPa), moderately filled glass (leucite, 17–25%, improved strength), highly filled glass (leucite/lithium disilicate 45–70%, 120–500 MPa)
- Fabrication method classification: stacking/sintering, heat/hot-pressing (lost-wax), slip-casting + glass infiltration, dry-pressing (Procera), CAD/CAM (soft-machining vs. hard-machining)
- Comparison of PFM vs. all-ceramic restorations covering aesthetics, light transmission, metal allergy risks
- Clinical guidance linking ceramic type to indication (veneers, inlays, onlays, crowns, FPDs) and location (anterior vs. posterior)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Composition classification highlights:**
- Feldspathic (predominantly glass): 70–90 MPa flexure strength; used for veneers, inlays, onlays, veneering frameworks
- Leucite-based (moderately/highly filled glass): 120–160 MPa; inlays, onlays, veneers, anterior/posterior crowns
- Lithium disilicate (highly filled glass): 300–500 MPa; inlays, onlays, veneers, crowns, short-span 3-unit FPDs
- In-Ceram Alumina (glass-infiltrated): ~600 MPa; single crowns, 3-unit anterior bridges
- In-Ceram Spinell: ~350 MPa; translucent but weaker; limited to anterior crowns
- Alumina (polycrystalline): ~650 MPa
- Zirconia (polycrystalline, Y-TZP): 800–1500 MPa; monolithic posterior restorations or core/framework

**Fabrication methods:**
- Stacking/sintering: conventional powder-liquid technique (VM13, Ceramco 3)
- Heat-pressing (lost-wax): IPS Empress family; familiar workflow for technicians
- Slip-casting + glass infiltration: In-Ceram group; CAD/CAM variant available
- Dry-pressing (Procera): enlarged die + sintering shrinkage compensation for alumina/zirconia
- CAD/CAM soft-machining: partially sintered blocks milled oversized, then sintered (Lava, IPS e.max ZirCAD)
- CAD/CAM hard-machining: fully sintered blocks; superior fit but tool wear and lengthy process

**PFM vs. All-ceramic comparison:**
- PFM failure: 4% at 5y, 12% at 10y, 32% at 15y (Valderhaug 1991)
- PFM disadvantage: opaque (blocks light transmission); metal allergy risk (nickel)
- All-ceramic advantage: superior aesthetics, light transmission, biocompatibility
- All-ceramic limitation: brittle, especially glass-based; bi-layered type prone to veneer delamination/fracture
- Monolithic ceramic: more durable than bi-layered but potentially inferior aesthetics; preferred for posterior

**Success factors**: material selection, restoration design, occlusion, cementation media.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative review design — no systematic PRISMA process, potential selection bias in included studies
- 10-year search window (2010–2019) excludes older foundational literature and post-2019 advances (e.g., newer translucent zirconia generations)
- No survival/failure rate synthesis or statistical pooling
- CAD/CAM section is brief given the fast-evolving nature of digital workflows
- No discussion of newer high-translucency multi-layer zirconia (4Y/5Y-PSZ) that emerged prominently after 2015

## 7. Glossary

- **Feldspathic ceramic**: Predominantly glass ceramic (<17% particles); silica/alumina-based feldspar; weakest but most aesthetic
- **Leucite**: Crystalline potassium aluminosilicate particle; reinforces glass matrix (moderately/highly filled)
- **Lithium disilicate**: Highly filled glass ceramic (Li₂Si₂O₅ crystals, 45–70 vol%); 300–500 MPa; IPS e.max
- **Y-TZP**: Yttrium-oxide partially stabilized zirconia — polycrystalline, 3–5 mol% Y₂O₃; 800–1500 MPa
- **In-Ceram**: Glass-infiltrated ceramic system (Vita); alumina/spinell/zirconia variants
- **PFM**: Porcelain fused to metal — metal coping + overlying ceramic veneer
- **CAD/CAM**: Computer-aided design / computer-aided manufacturing; soft-machining (partially sintered) vs. hard-machining (fully sintered)
- **Monolithic restoration**: Single-material ceramic restoration without separate core-veneer layers
- **Bi-layered restoration**: Core/framework + aesthetic veneer ceramic; prone to delamination
- **CTE**: Coefficient of thermal expansion — must be matched between PFM metal alloy and ceramic
