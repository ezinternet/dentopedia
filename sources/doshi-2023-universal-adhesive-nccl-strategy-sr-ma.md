## One-line Summary
Updated systematic review and meta-analysis of 17 clinical studies showing no statistically significant difference between total-etch and self-etch strategies for universal adhesives in NCCLs across any of five clinical outcome measures.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: BDJ Open (npj Oral Health), 2023; 9:6
- **DOI**: 10.1038/s41405-022-00124-6
- **Institution**: Saveetha Dental College and Hospitals, SIMATS, Chennai, India
- **Received**: 28 March 2022; Accepted: 23 September 2022
- **Databases**: PubMed, Scopus, Cochrane, Web of Science, Open Gray, Clinical Registries

## 2. Key Contributions
- Updated SR+MA specifically for NCCLs comparing total-etch (TE) vs self-etch (SE) strategies with universal adhesives
- Provides 18-, 24-, and 36-month follow-up data using both USPHS and FDI criteria separately
- Finds no significant difference between TE and SE across all five outcome measures (p > 0.05, I² = 0%)
- Identifies strongly suspected publication bias in the retention domain at 18-month follow-up under FDI criteria
- Adds to the evidence that universal adhesives perform acceptably regardless of etching strategy in NCCLs

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: Systematic review with meta-analysis (PRISMA)
- **Inclusion criteria**: RCTs of NCCLs restored with universal adhesives comparing total-etch and self-etch strategies
- **Databases**: PubMed, Scopus, Cochrane, Web of Science, Open Gray, Clinical Registries
- **Included studies**: 17 in systematic review; 13 in meta-analysis
- **Follow-up periods analyzed**: 18, 24, 36 months
- **Outcome measures**: Retention, marginal adaptation, marginal discoloration, secondary caries, postoperative sensitivity
- **Criteria**: Both USPHS and FDI criteria analyzed separately
- **Heterogeneity**: I² = 0% for all comparisons

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Overall finding**: No significant difference between total-etch and self-etch strategies for any of five outcome measures at any follow-up period (p > 0.05, I² = 0%)
- **USPHS criteria**: No significant difference in retention, marginal adaptation, marginal discoloration, secondary caries, or postoperative sensitivity
- **FDI criteria**: No significant difference across outcomes; strongly suspected publication bias in retention domain at 18-month follow-up
- **Implication**: Both adhesive strategies are clinically acceptable for NCCL restorations with universal adhesives

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Publication bias suspected in retention domain at 18-month FDI criteria
- Limited number of studies at longer follow-up periods (36 months)
- Heterogeneity in universal adhesive products across studies
- Need for longer-term (>3-year) follow-up data
- No assessment of specific tooth types or severity of NCCL abrasion/erosion influence

## 6. Related Work
- Hong et al. 2021 — meta-analysis showing E&R superiority for universal adhesives overall (not NCCL-specific)
- Assis et al. 2023 — NCCL-focused SR+MA finding E&R advantage for medium-term retention
- Bresser et al. 2019 — earlier SR on universal adhesives in NCCLs
- Litonjua et al. — review of NCCL etiology and management

## 7. Glossary
- **NCCL (Non-carious cervical lesion)**: Hard tissue defect in cervical region not caused by bacterial caries; includes abrasion, erosion, abfraction
- **Total-etch strategy**: Phosphoric acid conditioning of both enamel and dentin before adhesive application
- **Self-etch strategy**: Acidic adhesive monomers condition and infiltrate simultaneously
- **USPHS criteria**: United States Public Health Service criteria for clinical evaluation of restorations
- **FDI criteria**: World Dental Federation criteria for clinical evaluation of direct and indirect restorations
- **HEMA**: 2-hydroxyethyl methacrylate, a hydrophilic monomer in adhesive systems
- **Hybrid layer**: Zone of resin-impregnated demineralized collagen formed at the adhesive-dentin interface
