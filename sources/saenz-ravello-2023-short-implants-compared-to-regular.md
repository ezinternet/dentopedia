## One-line Summary
Umbrella review and meta-analysis comparing short implants (<10 mm) vs. regular implants with bone augmentation in the atrophic posterior mandible, finding short implants may reduce failure, MBL, and complications.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: International Journal of Implant Dentistry (2023) 9:18
- **Type**: Umbrella review + meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD42022333526)
- **PICO**: Short implants <10 mm in native bone (I) vs. regular implants ≥10 mm in augmented bone (C) in atrophic posterior mandible patients (P); implant success outcomes (O)
- **Search date**: June 29, 2022; databases 2012–2022
- **Sample**: 595 implants, 281 hemiarches/patients across 15 primary studies (14 RCTs + 1 cohort); all conducted in Italy

## 2. Key Contributions
- First umbrella review specifically addressing short vs. regular+BA in atrophic mandible
- Quantitative synthesis across 18 SRs/MAs and 15 primary studies
- Meta-regression showing correlation between bone height, MBL, and biological complications
- GRADE assessment of certainty: mostly very low to low
- Trial Sequential Analysis (TSA) applied to each meta-analytic model

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Phase 1**: Identified 21 SR/MAs via 7 databases + 2 registries; assessed with AMSTAR-2
- **Phase 2**: Extracted 15 primary studies from SR/MAs; risk of bias via RoB 2.0 and ROBINS-I
- **Overlap management**: Corrected Coverage Area (CCA) = 31.62% (72% when grouped by cohort threads)
- **Statistics**: Random-effects meta-analysis (Mantel-Haenszel for RR; inverse variance for MD); meta-regression with Hartung-Knapp method
- **Subgroups**: Follow-up period, bone regeneration type, implant length (<10, <8, <6, <4 mm)
- **Short implant definition**: <10 mm (study-level definitions ranged from 4–10 mm across included SRs)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Implant Failure (primary outcome)
- Short implants **may reduce** implant failure at **1-year** follow-up vs. regular+BA
- Evidence certainty: **very low** (GRADE)

### Marginal Bone Loss (MBL)
- Short implants **may reduce** MBL at **3-, 5-, and 8-year** follow-up
- Meta-regression: bone height is a significant predictor of MBL
- Evidence certainty: **low** (GRADE)

### Biological Complications
- Short implants **likely reduce** biological complications at **1-, 3-, 5-, and 8-year** follow-up
- Evidence certainty: **low** (GRADE)

### Patient-Reported Outcomes
- Short implants **may be patient's preferred alternative** (less morbidity, lower cost, shorter treatment time)

### Bone Height Correlation
- Positive correlation between available bone height, MBL, and biological complication rate

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Most included SRs were "critically low" or "low" confidence (AMSTAR-2)
- High heterogeneity in short implant definition (4–10 mm cutoffs vary)
- All primary studies from Italy (single-setting bias)
- Short implant group: 6 mm average; regular group: 11.5–15 mm — not clinically uniform
- No maxilla data (exclusion criterion)
- Need for multicenter RCTs with standardized definitions and longer follow-up (>8 years)

## 6. Related Work
- Compares to 34 PROSPERO SR/MAs showing short implants may be more predictable than BA
- Lekholm-Zarb and Cawood classifications used for bone quality/quantity characterization
- AMSTAR-2, RoB 2.0, ROBINS-I, GRADE, TSA all applied for evidence quality

## 7. Glossary
- **Short implant**: <10 mm (this review's definition); other definitions range from <4 to <8 mm in literature
- **Bone augmentation (BA)**: Distraction osteogenesis, inlay/onlay bone block, or guided bone regeneration (GBR) to increase bone volume before regular implant placement
- **MBL (Marginal Bone Loss)**: Radiographic bone level change around implant neck — key long-term success indicator
- **Umbrella review**: Systematic review of systematic reviews (SRs/MAs)
- **CCA (Corrected Coverage Area)**: Index measuring overlap of primary studies across included SRs
- **TSA (Trial Sequential Analysis)**: Statistical method to assess whether cumulative evidence is sufficient to draw conclusions
- **GRADE**: Framework for rating certainty of evidence (high/moderate/low/very low)
- **AMSTAR-2**: Tool for assessing methodological quality of systematic reviews
