## Why Ingested

기존 [[wiki/behavioral-dentistry/motivational-interviewing/kay-2016-motivational-interviewing-general-dental-practice]]는 MI의 일반 치과 적용에 대한 초기 근거를 다뤘으나, 치주질환 특이적 임상 지표(PI/BI/GI/PD)에 대한 정량 효과는 미확정 상태였다. 본 2024 SR+MA는 2023년 5월까지의 최신 RCT를 모아 치주 특이 결과를 정량화하여 currency gap을 메운다 (kay 2016 이후 출판된 Arnett 2022 등 최신 RCT 반영).

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 7 RCTs (n=474; 6 in meta-analysis) found NO significant difference between motivational interviewing and standard oral health instruction on plaque, bleeding on probing, or gingival inflammation; evidence insufficient and mostly high risk of bias.

## 한줄요약

7개 RCT(n=474, 6편 메타분석)를 종합한 SR+MA로, 동기면담(Motivational Interviewing, MI)이 표준 구강위생교육 대비 치면세균막·탐침시출혈·치은염증에서 유의한 차이를 보이지 않았으며, 근거 수준이 불충분하고 대부분 비뚤림 위험이 높았다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Title**: Motivational Interviewing on Periodontal Treatment Outcomes: A Meta-Analysis (Concise Review)
- **Journal**: International Dental Journal 74 (2024) 669–678
- **Authors**: Zhan C, Qu W, Fok MR, Jin L, Lin Y (University of Hong Kong, Faculty of Dentistry)
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.identj.2024.01.003
- **Registration**: PROSPERO CRD42023458021
- **Type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (random-effects)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First updated SR+MA incorporating recent (post-2022) RCTs (e.g., Arnett 2022) that earlier reviews missed.
- Restricted to MI delivered WITHOUT other psychological co-interventions, isolating MI's independent effect.
- Concludes current evidence is insufficient to support MI as an adjunct for improving oral hygiene / periodontal outcomes.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Databases: Medline (PubMed), Embase, Cochrane Library, Web of Science, Scopus + gray literature (OpenGrey, ProQuest), ClinicalTrials.gov, WHO ICTRP. Search to May 2023.
- PICOT: noninstitutionalised patients ≥12 y with gingivitis/periodontitis/peri-implantitis; MI alone vs standard oral health instruction; outcomes PI, BI, GI, probing depth, attachment loss; RCTs only, no follow-up restriction.
- Comorbidity patients (diabetes, hypertension) and orthodontic patients excluded.
- Risk of bias: Cochrane RoB 2. Effect measure: standardised mean difference (SMD) with 95% CI, random-effects model; heterogeneity via Tau² and I².

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- 2108 records screened → 7 RCTs (n=474) in qualitative synthesis; 6 in meta-analysis.
- Risk of bias: 5/7 studies HIGH RoB, 2/7 "some concerns."
- Pooled result: NO significant difference between MI and control on plaque level, bleeding on probing, or gingival inflammation.
- Insufficient evidence for any significant effect on attachment loss or probing depth.
- Individual studies conflicting: one trial (Arnett 2022) reported significant gingival index improvement with MI; another reported a HIGHER plaque score in the MI group than control.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small number of trials, small total n (474), high heterogeneity in MI protocol/format/duration/follow-up.
- Majority high risk of bias; non-standardised MI delivery and provider training.
- Authors call for high-quality RCTs with standardised MI interventions before definitive conclusions.

## 6. Related Work

- Reinforces the cautious read of MI efficacy seen in [[wiki/behavioral-dentistry/motivational-interviewing/kay-2016-motivational-interviewing-general-dental-practice]].
- Complements broader behavioral-intervention reviews (Vilar Doceda 2023) that also found heterogeneity precluding pooled MI benefit.

## 7. Glossary

- **MI (Motivational Interviewing)**: client-centred, non-confrontational counselling to resolve ambivalence and evoke intrinsic motivation for behaviour change.
- **PI / BI / GI**: Plaque Index / Bleeding Index / Gingival Index.
- **SMD**: standardised mean difference, effect-size metric pooled in random-effects meta-analysis.
- **RoB 2**: revised Cochrane risk-of-bias tool for randomised trials.
