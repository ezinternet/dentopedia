## Why Ingested

[[wiki/orthodontics/baneshi-2024-clear-aligners-malocclusion-sr-ma]] already links TO this paper as the "periodontal outcome, more cautious" counterpart. Baneshi 2024 (RCT-only) reports relatively optimistic pro-CA periodontal effect sizes; this Crego-Ruiz SR+MA refines that optimism by showing only 2 of many pooled estimates reached significance (PI mid-term, PPD long-term), both with extreme heterogeneity (I²=99%), and concluding there is insufficient evidence that clear aligners (CA) maintain better periodontal health than fixed appliances (FA). It anchors the periodontal axis of the clear-aligner limitations batch and supplies the cautious bottom line that balances [[wiki/orthodontics/baneshi-2024-clear-aligners-malocclusion-sr-ma]] and the [[wiki/orthodontics/yassir-2022-cat-vs-fat-overview-systematic-reviews]] umbrella.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (12 studies, 8 in MA, 612 patients; Cochrane RoB) finding clear aligners only slightly favor periodontal health — significant only for plaque index mid-term (MD −0.99) and pocket depth long-term (MD −0.93 mm), both at I²=99% — so evidence is insufficient to claim CA maintains better periodontal health than fixed appliances.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA to **simultaneously** address (a) periodontal-health maintenance and (b) gingival-recession development under CA vs FA, with corrected search strategy and inclusion criteria (prior MA by Jiang et al. had pooled studies not actually using CA).
- Pooled 612 patients (291 CA, 321 FA) across 12 studies (3 RCTs, 8 prospective cohorts, 1 retrospective), 8 of which entered quantitative MA.
- Stratified each periodontal index by **follow-up window** (short ≤2–3 mo, mid 6–9 mo, long ≥12 mo), exposing that significance appears only at specific windows, not uniformly.
- Delivered a deliberately **cautious bottom line**: appliance type itself has little-to-null effect on periodontal health when adequate oral-hygiene programs are in place; the apparent CA advantage is small, heterogeneous, and of limited clinical relevance.
- Identified the near-total absence of gingival-recession evidence (only 1 study, Madariaga et al., 3-month follow-up).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases**: MEDLINE (PubMed), Scopus, Cochrane Library, Web of Science — searched to 30 Sept 2022; supplemented by ClinicalTrials.gov, OpenGrey, greylit.org and manual reference screening.
- **PICOS**: Population = orthodontic patients; Intervention = CA; Comparison = buccal FA; Outcomes = PI, PPD, GI, BoP (primary) and gingival recession (secondary); Studies = RCTs, CCTs, prospective/retrospective cohorts.
- **Exclusions**: cross-sectional studies, <2-month follow-up, no control group, case series/reports, lingual appliances, retainers, orthognathic surgery.
- **Screening**: two independent reviewers; Cohen's κ = 0.914 (high agreement); third reviewer for disagreements; most recent report kept for duplicate samples.
- **RoB**: ROBINS-I for non-randomized studies; RoB 2.0 for RCTs.
- **Synthesis**: random-effects pairwise MA; MA performed only when ≥2 studies shared the same index at the same follow-up window; change-from-baseline (follow-up minus baseline) used as outcome to reduce bias; GI/PI treated as continuous per Cochrane Handbook; missing SDs imputed.
- **Heterogeneity**: I² and χ² (Q); I²>50% = significant; publication bias and sensitivity not assessed (each MA had ≤4 studies, below the 10-study threshold).
- **Software**: Stata 14, RevMan 5.3; α=0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Yield**: 129–145 potential → 12 included → 8 in MA. 612 patients total (291 CA, 321 FA). Mostly young (15–30 y); Han et al. enrolled older periodontitis patients (mean 52.9 ± 9.42 y). All studies delivered at least baseline oral-health promotion.
- **Plaque index (PI)** — 6 studies in MA:
  - Mid-term: **MD −0.99 (95% CI −1.94 to −0.03; P=.04; I²=99%)** — significant, favors CA.
  - Short- and long-term: no significant difference, but consistent trend favoring CA.
- **Gingival index (GI)** — 4 studies in MA:
  - No significant difference at any window; trend favoring CA, strongest long-term (MD −0.46; 95% CI −1.03 to 0.11; P=0.11; I²=96%).
- **Pocket probing depth (PPD)** — 5 studies in MA:
  - Short/mid-term: no significant difference.
  - Long-term: **MD −0.93 mm (95% CI −1.16 to 0.7; P<0.0001)** — significant, favors CA, **but based on a single study**.
- **Bleeding on probing (BoP)** — 8 studies, but heterogeneous indices → no MA. Most studies (except Miethke and Vogt) reported significantly less bleeding with CA; timing of significance varied (Karkhanechi & Srinath: only at 12 mo; Chhibber: at 9 mo but not 18 mo).
- **Gingival recession** — only Madariaga et al. (3-mo follow-up): FA group developed significant recession from baseline (−0.85 ± 0.45 → −0.67 ± 0.51 mm); CA group unchanged (−1.16 ± 0.18 mm at both timepoints).
- **RoB**: only 1 study (Chhibber et al., 18-mo RCT) judged low risk. Main weakness = inability to blind the outcome assessor (FA not removed); several RCTs had high randomization-process risk; age mismatch between groups in 2 studies.
- **Clinical-relevance caveat**: PI/GI are ordinal — MDs between 0 and 1 likely yield the same clinical score; the <1 mm PPD difference is practically negligible.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Mostly observational studies; few RCTs; **extreme heterogeneity (I²>90% in all MAs)** driven by differing oral-hygiene habits, frequency of OHI/maintenance programs, and participant age (teens to adults).
- Could not run subgroup meta-regression or sensitivity analysis (too few studies, low quality); publication bias not assessed.
- Long-term outcomes rest on very few studies (only 4 with ≥12-mo follow-up; long-term PPD on a single study); Ristic et al. note periodontal indices peak ~3 mo post-appliance and recede by 6 mo, so short-window snapshots can mislead.
- Gingival-recession evidence is a single 3-month study — needs confirmation by larger, long-term prospective trials.
- Calls for CONSORT-adherent RCTs with long-term follow-up.

## 7. Glossary

- **CA (Clear Aligners)** — removable transparent thermoplastic orthodontic aligners.
- **FA (Fixed Appliances)** — conventional buccal fixed braces (brackets + wires).
- **PI (Plaque Index)** — ordinal score of dental plaque accumulation.
- **GI (Gingival Index)** — ordinal score of gingival inflammation.
- **PPD (Pocket Probing Depth)** — sulcus/pocket depth in mm.
- **BoP (Bleeding on Probing)** — presence/extent of bleeding when probing the sulcus.
- **Gingival recession** — apical migration of the gingival margin (mm change vs baseline).
- **MD (Mean Difference)** — pooled difference in means between CA and FA.
- **I²** — proportion of variability across studies due to heterogeneity rather than chance; >50% = significant, >90% = extreme.
- **ROBINS-I** — Cochrane risk-of-bias tool for non-randomized intervention studies.
- **RoB 2.0** — Cochrane risk-of-bias tool for randomized trials.
