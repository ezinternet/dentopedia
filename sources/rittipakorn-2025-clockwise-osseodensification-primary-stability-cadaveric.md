## Why Ingested

기존 OD 근거는 모두 제조사 표준인 **반시계방향 (counter-clockwise, CCW) 1,200 rpm** 프로토콜을 다룬다 ([[implants/huwais-2017-novel-osseous-densification-osteotomy-primary-stability]], [[implants/marzorati-2026-osseodensification-standard-osteotomy-torque-isq-sr-ma]]). 본 사체연구(Rittipakorn 2025)는 densah bur를 **절삭방향(시계방향, clockwise, CW) 800 rpm**으로 돌리는 단순화·저감도 변형 프로토콜을 처음 검증해, "OD = CCW만이 정답인가"라는 versah-protocols 카테고리의 미해결 질문을 보강·확장한다.

## One-line Summary

Paired-site human cadaveric tibia study (40 Osstem TS III implants, D3/D4 low-density bone) comparing a novel **clockwise osseodensification (CW-OD, 800 rpm)** vs standard drilling (SD): OD gave higher but non-significant mean ISQ (67.5 vs 62.9, p=0.077) and insertion torque (34.0 vs 29.5 Ncm, p=0.052), with tighter IQR and a significant IT–ISQ correlation only in OD (ρ=0.577, p=0.0077).

## 한줄요약

사람 경골(tibia) 사체 짝지음-부위 연구(임플란트 40개, D3/D4 저밀도골)로 **시계방향 골밀도화(CW-OD, 800 rpm)** vs 표준 드릴링(SD) 비교: OD가 평균 ISQ(67.5 vs 62.9, p=0.077)·삽입토크(34.0 vs 29.5 Ncm, p=0.052)가 더 높았으나 통계적으로 비유의했고, 사분위범위(IQR)는 더 좁았으며 IT–ISQ 상관은 OD에서만 유의(ρ=0.577, p=0.0077)했다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First investigation of a clockwise (CW) OD protocol.** Standard Versah OD uses non-cutting **counter-clockwise (CCW) burs at ~1,200 rpm**; this study reverses bur direction to the cutting direction (CW) at a lower **800 rpm**, hypothesizing that the bur's negative rake angle / non-sharp flute geometry still generates lateral compaction rather than material removal — a simpler, less technique-sensitive alternative.
2. Demonstrates CW-OD with an **Osstem bone compaction kit** (not Versah Densah) — i.e. the densifying-bur principle generalized to another manufacturer's burs.
3. Adds **predictability** as an outcome dimension: OD not only trended higher but produced tighter dispersion (narrower IQR, no low torque outliers) and a significant IT–ISQ correlation, strengthening RFA reliability in low-density bone.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Specimens**: 9 formalin-fixed human tibiae (epiphyseal region; 6 F / 3 M; mean age 77 ± 9.1 y) — low-density (D3/D4) cancellous bone model.
- **Design**: paired-site, within-cadaver. 40 sites = 20 pairs (each pair ≥5 mm apart in the same epiphysis), randomized to SD (n=20) vs CW-OD (n=20).
- **Implants**: Osstem Taper TS III, 4.0 × 10 mm; all final osteotomy 4.0 mm Ø / 10 mm depth; no countersinking.
- **SD protocol**: drills 2.2 → 3.5 → 4.0 mm at 800 rpm (manufacturer instructions).
- **CW-OD protocol**: Osstem bone compaction kit densifying burs 2.2 → 3.5 → 4.0 mm, **clockwise rotation, 800 rpm**. Constant 0.9% saline irrigation, single calibrated operator.
- **Insertion**: torque ramped 20 → 30 → 35 → 40 Ncm at 30 rpm until seated; max IT recorded.
- **Outcomes**: maximum insertion torque (IT) and ISQ (Osstell ISQ, RFA; mean of mesial/distal/buccal/lingual ×2). Imaging — CBCT (75 µm voxel, 80 kVp), periapical radiography, and **synchrotron-based x-ray tomographic microscopy (SR-µCT, 3.61 µm pixel, 13.5 keV)** for qualitative bone–implant interface morphology.
- **Statistics**: Shapiro–Wilk; Mann–Whitney U (group comparison); Spearman ρ (IT–ISQ); α=0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | SD (n=20) | CW-OD (n=20) | Difference (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ISQ | 62.9 ± 9.3 | 67.5 ± 6.5 | (−0.52, 9.75) | 0.077 |
| Insertion torque (Ncm) | 29.5 ± 7.6 | 34.0 ± 6.6 | (−0.06, 9.06) | 0.052 |

- Both stability metrics **trended higher with OD but neither reached significance** (p>0.05). OD showed **narrower IQR** and **no low torque outliers** → more consistent outcomes.
- **IT–ISQ correlation**: significant moderate positive in OD (**ρ=0.577, p=0.0077**); weak/non-significant in SD (ρ=0.208, p=0.378). Pooled ρ=0.414.
- **Imaging (qualitative only)**: OD sites visibly narrower/constricted after drilling; CBCT/PA showed denser, more compact peri-implant bone with less interfacial radiolucency; SR-µCT showed condensed trabecular architecture and close thread apposition in OD vs porous structure with micro-gaps in SD.
- IT in OD (34.0 Ncm) closely matched Mercier et al. CCW-OD (34.9 Ncm), aligning with Padhye SR conclusion that OD raises IT.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Ex vivo cadaveric, formalin-fixed** — no biological healing/osseointegration; mechanical primary stability only.
- **Small n (20/group)** and inter-specimen bone-density variability likely underpowered the near-significant differences (p=0.052–0.077).
- **Imaging is qualitative/descriptive only** — no quantitative BIC, BV/TV, or densification-zone measurement despite SR-µCT capability.
- Single operator; single implant system (Osstem). No CCW-OD arm — cannot say CW is equivalent/superior to the established CCW protocol, only vs SD.
- Authors call for **in vivo studies** to confirm trends and assess long-term biological effects (remodeling, MBL).

## 7. Glossary

- **Osseodensification (OD)**: osteotomy technique that compacts (densifies) rather than excavates bone, using specially fluted burs run in non-cutting direction to autograft bone laterally into the osteotomy wall.
- **CCW vs CW**: standard Versah OD runs burs counter-clockwise (non-cutting) at ~1,200 rpm; this study tests clockwise (cutting-direction) at 800 rpm.
- **ISQ / RFA**: Implant Stability Quotient from Resonance Frequency Analysis (Osstell); 0–100 scale of implant micromobility.
- **SR-µCT**: synchrotron-based x-ray tomographic microscopy — micron-resolution 3D imaging of the bone–implant interface.
- **D3/D4 bone**: Misch low-density bone classes (thin cortex, sparse trabeculae), common in posterior maxilla/elderly.
