## Why Ingested

기존 [[dentin-hypersensitivity/qiu-2026-stannous-fluoride-toothpaste-dentin-hypersensitivity-rct]]는 단일 활성성분(SnF₂) 치약을 표준 불소치약과만 비교했지만, 가정용 탈감작제(home-use desensitizing agent)들 사이의 직접 우열은 다루지 못했다. 본 RCT(Ayan 2025)는 NovaMin·포타슘 질산염(Sensodyne 양대 flagship 기술)을 포함한 5종 가정용 탈감작제를 표준 불소치약 대조군과 head-to-head로 비교해, 상아질 과민증(Dentin Hypersensitivity, DH) at-home 1차 치료 선택지의 상대적 효능 근거를 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

RCT (n=180 enrolled / 164 randomized / 150 analyzed, ages 18–70, DH in ≥2 non-adjacent teeth): five home-use desensitizing agents (CPP-ACP, Arginine, NovaMin, Propolis, Potassium nitrate) vs standard fluoride control over 8 weeks — all test agents significantly improved DHEQ-15/SSS/VAS at weeks 4 & 8, control improved only on SSS+VAS; CPP-ACP (brushing + finger inunction) gave the greatest reduction by week 8.

## 한줄요약

무작위 대조 임상시험(180명 등록 / 164명 무작위배정 / 150명 분석, 18–70세, 비인접 2치 이상 DH): 5종 가정용 탈감작제(CPP-ACP, 아르기닌, 노바민, 프로폴리스, 포타슘 질산염)를 표준 불소치약 대조군과 8주간 비교 — 모든 시험군은 4·8주 DHEQ-15/SSS/VAS에서 유의 개선, 대조군은 SSS+VAS만 개선; CPP-ACP(칫솔질+손가락 도포)가 8주째 가장 큰 감소를 보임.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First study to **head-to-head compare five distinct home-use desensitizing agents** (Arginine, NovaMin, Propolis, Potassium nitrate, CPP-ACP) against a standard fluoride toothpaste control, rather than testing one agent in isolation.
- Triangulates effectiveness with **three measurement instruments simultaneously** — DHEQ-15 (OHRQoL, Turkish-validated), Schiff Sensitivity Scale (SSS, evaporative/air stimulus), and VAS (subjective pain) — across baseline, week 4, week 8.
- Demonstrates **CPP-ACP (GC Tooth Mousse, brushing + nightly finger inunction) as the most effective** agent by week 8, with the largest score reductions across all three scales.
- Shows the standard **fluoride control did not significantly improve DHEQ-15** (quality-of-life) over time, though it improved SSS and VAS marginally by week 8 — separating QoL impact from raw sensitivity response.
- Provides evidence that **fluoride addition alone does not explain efficacy**: Potassium nitrate (used with fluoride) underperformed expectations, while fluoride-free Propolis matched Potassium nitrate on DHEQ-15/VAS.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: parallel-group RCT, six arms, 8-week intervention, single independent examiner for outcome assessment.
- **Participants**: 180 assessed (ages 18–70, DH in ≥2 non-adjacent teeth) → 26 excluded (inclusion criteria) → **164 randomized** into six groups; dropouts/protocol violations reduced to **25 per group (150) for final analysis**. 62% female, mean age 40.9 ± 15.1.
- **Sample size**: G*Power 3.1, total 144 (alpha 0.05, power 0.85, effect size 0.3287, medium).
- **Exclusions**: pregnancy/lactation; advanced periodontal disease; relevant chronic disease; major oral pathology; anticonvulsants/antihistamines/antidepressants/sedatives/tranquilizers; desensitizing toothpaste use in past 3 mo; periodontal treatment in past 12 mo; pulpitis/caries/cracked enamel/RPD abutments.
- **Groups** (active ingredient / product): CPP-ACP (GC Tooth Mousse, n=28) · Arginine 8% (Colgate Sensitive Pro-Relief, n=28) · NovaMin = calcium sodium phosphosilicate 5% (Sensodyne Repair & Protect, n=27) · Propolis (Eyup Sabri Tuncer, fluoride-free, n=27) · Potassium nitrate 3% (Sensodyne Cool Mint Mouthwash, n=27) · Control = standard fluoride toothpaste 1450 ppm (Colgate Total, n=27).
- **Protocols**: toothpaste groups brushed twice daily 2 min; CPP-ACP applied by finger 3 min nightly after fluoride brushing; Potassium nitrate rinsed twice daily after fluoride brushing. All used soft brushes; standardized preventive instructions (avoid acidic/sugary food, Modified Stillman technique).
- **Outcomes**: DHEQ-15 (15–105), SSS (0–3, air 1 cm/1 s, mean of 2 teeth), VAS (0–10) at baseline/4wk/8wk.
- **Statistics**: Cronbach's alpha; Shapiro-Wilk, Levene, Mauchly; ANOVA/Kruskal-Wallis (groups); Repeated Measures ANOVA (Greenhouse-Geisser)/Friedman (time); Bonferroni post-hoc; SPSS 25.0; p<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **All test agents** significantly improved DHEQ-15, SSS, and VAS at weeks 4 & 8 vs baseline (p<0.005). The **control** group improved significantly only on SSS and VAS (not DHEQ-15, p=0.068) and showed the smallest changes.
- **CPP-ACP greatest reduction at week 8** — change from baseline: **DHEQ-15 56.68 ± 17.87, VAS 6.52 ± 1.48, SSS 2.32 ± 0.56**.
- **DHEQ-15 (baseline → 8wk)**: CPP-ACP 77.84 → 21.16; Arginine 77.84 → 37.52; Potassium nitrate 74.6 → 36.36; Novamin 72.68 → 39.16; Propolis 77.68 → 41.56; Control 82.36 → 68.72 (NS over time). All test agents < control at wk 8.
- **SSS (baseline → 8wk)**: CPP-ACP 2.5 → 0.18; Arginine 2.28 → 0.56; Potassium nitrate 2.36 → 0.62; Novamin 2.12 → 0.68; Propolis 2.22 → 0.88; Control 2.38 → 1.9. All test agents < control at wks 4 & 8; CPP-ACP < Novamin at wk 4.
- **VAS (baseline → 8wk)**: CPP-ACP 7.44 → 0.92; Arginine 7.36 → 2.52; Novamin 6.84 → 2.52; Potassium nitrate 6.84 → 2.76; Propolis 7.2 → 3.2; Control 7.4 → 4.96 (highest). CPP-ACP change > Potassium nitrate/Novamin/Propolis.
- **Ranking** (by week-8 reduction): CPP-ACP > Arginine ≈ Potassium nitrate > Novamin ≈ Propolis > Control.
- DH most common in premolars (~38.6%); higher prevalence in women.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Many uncontrolled factors influence patient-based pain measurement; standardized DH product-testing procedures not yet sufficiently developed (limits cross-product comparability).
- No control over participants' oral hygiene status during the trial.
- DH-contributing factors (acidic diet, improper brushing, erosive/intrinsic factors) not identified or controlled at baseline.
- **Only 8 weeks** — no long-term durability assessment; authors cite a 12-month observation precedent and call for longer follow-up.
- CPP-ACP and Potassium nitrate were used *with* fluoride toothpaste (two-product combinations), unlike Arginine/Novamin/Propolis — a protocol asymmetry, though the authors argue fluoride co-use did not by itself explain efficacy.

## 6. Related Work

- Compares against single-agent home-use RCTs (e.g., stannous fluoride vs standard fluoride control).
- Aligns with bioactive-glass (NovaMin/CSP) SR evidence and CPP-ACP remineralization literature.
- DHEQ-15 (Turkish version) used as an OHRQoL outcome alongside SSS and VAS.

## 7. Glossary

- **DH (Dentin Hypersensitivity)** — short sharp pain from exposed dentin to thermal/tactile/osmotic/chemical stimuli, no other pathology; explained by the hydrodynamic theory.
- **CPP-ACP** — casein phosphopeptide–amorphous calcium phosphate; milk-derived, occludes tubules via Ca/PO4 deposition.
- **NovaMin** — calcium sodium phosphosilicate; bioactive glass that precipitates Ca-phosphate to occlude tubules.
- **Arginine** — amino acid (8% Pro-Argin) forming calcium-carbonate/phosphate plugs in tubules.
- **Potassium nitrate (KNO3)** — nerve-transmission blocker (depolarization) rather than a tubule occluder.
- **Propolis** — bee resin with antimicrobial/antioxidant properties and a tubule-blocking effect.
- **DHEQ-15** — 15-item Dentin Hypersensitivity Experience Questionnaire (short form), OHRQoL measure, total 15–105.
- **SSS (Schiff Sensitivity Scale)** — 0–3 evaporative (air) stimulus response score.
- **VAS** — Visual Analog Scale, 0–10 subjective pain.
