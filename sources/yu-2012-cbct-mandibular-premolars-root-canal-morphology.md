## Why Ingested

Mandibular premolars are an under-recognized site of C-shaped canal anatomy (usually thought of as a mandibular-2nd-molar finding), and this CBCT study quantifies how rare it actually is in a western Chinese population, complementing the molar-focused C-shaped cluster. It reinforces the broader canal-morphology baseline anchored by [[endodontics/anatomy/song-2008-cross-sectional-morphology-and-minimum-canal]] (C-shaped cross-sectional morphology and minimum wall widths) and pairs with the molar-prevalence siblings [[endodontics/anatomy/shemesh-2017-cshaped-canals-cbct-mandibular-molars]], [[endodontics/anatomy/shekarian-2023-cshaped-canals-three-rooted-mandibular-iranian]], and [[endodontics/anatomy/singh-2022-cshaped-canal-second-molars-indian-cbct]].

## One-line Summary

CBCT cross-sectional study (149 patients, 356 mandibular premolars) in a western Chinese population: mandibular first premolars 98% one-rooted with predominant Vertucci Type I (86.8%) and Type V (9.8%); C-shaped canal prevalence only 1.1% in first and 0.6% in second premolars.

## 한줄요약

서부 중국인 149명·하악 소구치 356개 CBCT 단면 연구 — 하악 제1소구치 98%가 단일근, Vertucci Type I(86.8%)·Type V(9.8%)가 주류이고 C-shaped canal은 제1소구치 1.1%·제2소구치 0.6%로 드물게 관찰됨.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: BMC Medical Imaging, 2012, 12:18
- **Study type**: Cross-sectional CBCT morphology study
- **Population**: Western Chinese (West China Hospital of Stomatology, Chengdu; multi-ethnic Western China region), 2009–2010
- **Sample**: 149 CBCT scans → 178 mandibular first premolars + 178 mandibular second premolars (356 total)
- **Device**: 3D Accuitomo (MCT-1 EX-2 F, Morita), 80 kV / 5.0 mA, voxel 0.125 mm, slice 1.0 mm

## 2. Key Contributions

- 3D CBCT documentation of root number, canal number, and Vertucci configuration of mandibular first and second premolars in a western Chinese (largely non-Han, Asian) population.
- Quantified C-shaped canal prevalence in premolars: 1.1% (first), 0.6% (second) — confirming C-shaped anatomy is uncommon but not absent outside molars.
- Localized the canal-bifurcation site: middle-to-apical third in most bicanal premolars (87% of first, 75% of second premolar variations).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Inclusion: fully erupted permanent mandibular first/second premolars with mature apices, no fillings/posts/crowns.
- Two independent endodontists assessed roots, canals, bifurcation location, and Vertucci classification; a third oral radiologist resolved disagreements.
- Axial/coronal/sagittal sections reviewed on a calibrated workstation in a darkened room.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Mandibular first premolars (n=178):**
- Roots: one root 98% (174), two roots 2% (4).
- Canals: one canal 87.1%, two canals 11.2%, three canals 0.6%; C-shaped 1.1%.
- Vertucci (174 single-rooted): Type I 86.8% (151), Type III 1.7% (3), Type V 9.8% (17), Type VIII 0.6% (1), C-shaped 1.1% (2).

**Mandibular second premolars (n=178):**
- Roots: all one root (100%).
- Canals: one canal 97.2%, two canals 2.2%; C-shaped 0.6%.
- Vertucci: Type I 97.2% (173), Type II 0.55% (1), Type V 1.7% (3), C-shaped 0.55% (1).

- Canal bifurcation in Type V/VIII teeth occurred predominantly at the middle-apical third.
- Compared with a Taiwanese-Chinese study reporting 18% C-shaped in mandibular first premolars, this western Chinese sample showed far lower variation (1.1%), attributed to ethnic/regional and methodological differences.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-center archival CBCT sample; selection by referral may not represent the general population.
- 1.0 mm slice thickness and 0.125 mm voxel limit detection of very fine isthmuses/accessory canals.
- Cross-sectional prevalence only — no clinical-outcome correlation.

## 6. Related Work

- Song 2008 — cross-sectional morphology and minimum canal-wall widths in C-shaped roots (safety-margin context).
- Chai & Thong 2004; Fan et al. 2004 — C-shaped canal anatomy in mandibular molars (cited comparators).
- Zhang et al. 2011 — CBCT root/canal morphology of mandibular molars in Chinese individuals (same group's molar companion study).

## 7. Glossary

- **C-shaped canal**: Ribbon-/C-shaped canal cross-section formed when buccal and lingual canals are connected by a fin or isthmus; complicates debridement and obturation.
- **Vertucci classification**: Eight-type system describing root canal configurations from pulp chamber to apex (Type I = single canal, Type V = 1→2, Type VIII = 1→3, etc.).
- **Voxel size**: 3D pixel dimension of a CBCT volume; smaller voxels = higher spatial resolution.
