## Why Ingested

하악 구치부 임플란트 식립 후 발생하는 감각이상(altered sensation / paresthesia)의 이환율, 원인, 경과, 치료에 대한 체계적 문헌 고찰이며, 국내 구강악안면외과 전문 석사논문으로서 한국 임상 맥락의 데이터(치과의사 설문 포함)를 포함한다. 기존 [[oral-surgery/lee-2015-inferior-alveolar-nerve-injury-management]] 페이지가 단일 증례(제3대구치 발거 후 IAN 손상)를 다루는 반면, 이 논문은 임플란트-특이적 IAN/정신신경/설신경 손상을 이환율 관점에서 종합한다.

## One-line Summary

Narrative review (15 articles, PubMed 1990–2009 + Korean OMFS journal) of altered sensation following posterior mandibular implant placement: incidence 0–55%, mostly transient (resolves within 6 months), involving IAN, mental nerve, or lingual nerve, managed with NSAIDs non-surgically and microneuroanastomosis surgically.

## 한줄요약

서사적 문헌 고찰(15편, 2010) — 하악 구치부 임플란트 식립 후 감각이상(Altered Sensation)의 이환율은 0–55%로 문헌마다 상이하며, 대부분은 1차 수술 후 발생하고 일시적 손상은 6개월 이내 회복; 비외과적 치료는 NSAIDs, 외과적 치료는 미세신경문합술(Microneuroanastomosis).

## 2. Key Contributions

- First Korean OMFS master's thesis synthesizing the incidence, symptoms, causes, duration, and treatment of altered sensation following mandibular posterior implant placement
- Collates international data (PubMed 1990–2009) and domestic Korean OMFS journal data into one review
- Identifies the wide range (0–55%) of reported altered sensation incidence and attributes it to study design differences (retrospective vs. prospective), measurement variability, and patient subjective perception
- Provides practical guidance: safety zone ≥2 mm above mandibular canal on panoramic, ≥1 mm on CT; panoramic magnification correction formula (L = H/M – c – s) for implant length planning
- Includes indications and contraindications for microneurosurgery from Hegedus & Diecidue

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Narrative literature review (서사적 문헌 고찰)
- **Databases**: PubMed (search terms: "altered sensation", "mandibular implant"), Korean OMFS journal (대한 구강악안면 외과 학회지)
- **Date range**: Articles published 1990–2009; full-text articles published October 1992 – September 2008 from key journals
- **Journals searched**: Journal of Korea Oral & Maxillofacial Surgery, Journal of Prosthetic Dentistry, Practical Periodontics & Aesthetic Dentistry, International Journal of Oral & Maxillofacial Implants, British Journal of Oral & Maxillofacial Surgery, Implant Dentistry, Clinical Implant Dentistry & Related Research, Clinical Oral Implants Research, Journal of Periodontology
- **Inclusion criteria**: Clinical studies; involving inferior alveolar nerve (IAN), mental nerve, or lingual nerve injury; incidence data reported; prospective or retrospective investigation of patients or dentists
- **Exclusion criteria**: Maxillary sensory disturbance, anterior mandible sensory disturbance; studies on surgical techniques to avoid nerve injury; legal-topic papers; other narrative reviews
- **n**: 15 articles selected; 5 specifically on incidence (3 retrospective + 2 prospective)
- **Outcomes assessed**: Incidence, timing of onset, symptom type, recovery duration, management

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### 4.1 Incidence (신경손상의 발생율)

| Study | n / Implants | Incidence | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Kwon et al. (retrospective, Korean) | 47 dentists surveyed | 55% experienced nerve injury | 79% were surgeons with ≥5 years experience; mostly IAN; 1 lingual nerve |
| Ellies & Hawker 1992–1993 (retrospective questionnaire) | 112 patients | 36% altered sensation | 23% resolved; 13% persistent; 81% after 1st surgery; 18% after 2nd surgery or prosthetic attachment |
| Ellies 1992 (retrospective) | 212 respondents | 37% altered sensation | 24% transient; 13% permanent; 82% resolved within 6 months; 8% persisted >1 year |
| Bartling et al. (prospective) | 94 patients, 405 implants | 8.5% (8 patients) | Zone 1 (anterior to mental foramen): 1 patient; Zone 2 (posterior): 3 patients; Both zones: 4 patients; no permanent altered sensation |
| Lydia Vazquez et al. 2007 (prospective) | 1527 patients, 2584 implants | 0.08% (2 patients) | Both transient; resolved within 3–6 weeks |

**Overall range**: 0–55% across studies (major source of variation: study design, measurement, patient subjectivity)

### 4.2 Nerves Involved

- **Inferior alveolar nerve (하치조신경, IAN)**: most common
- **Mental nerve (이신경)**: anterior loop at risk; mental foramen ~3.5 mm in size, oval/round shape, located above mandibular canal between 2nd premolar and 1st molar
- **Lingual nerve (설신경)**: least common; injured during flap manipulation

### 4.3 Symptoms

- Hypoaesthesia (hypoaesthesia 48% in Kwon et al.)
- Numbness (마취감): 68–74%
- Tingling (이상감각): 13–33%
- Painful sensation: 22–29%
- Swollen sensation: 19–26%
- Thick sensation: 18–32%
- Itching: 5–13%
- No hypersthesia or dysesthesia reported in Bartling et al.
- Female sex: significantly higher risk (42.4–43.2% in Ellies studies; p-value not stated)
- Diabetes mellitus: significantly higher risk (75% in Ellies 1992)

### 4.4 Timing and Duration

- Mostly after 1st surgery (81% in Ellies & Hawker; majority in other studies)
- Transient cases: 90% resolve within 6 months (Ellies & Hawker); 82% within 6 months (Ellies 1992)
- Recovery at 1 year: 5–8% still with altered sensation
- Bartling et al.: all 8 cases resolved; 1 case complete anesthesia resolved by 4 months

### 4.5 Treatment

**Non-surgical (비외과적)**:
- NSAIDs: ibuprofen 800 mg for 21 days (Bartling protocol), repeat if not resolved
- Adeno-cortical steroid + dexamethasone (anti-inflammatory)

**Surgical (외과적)**:
- Microneuroanastomosis (미세신경문합술)
- Nerve grafting
- Removal of implant or back-out 1/4 turn (bone compression reduction)
- Pressure relief procedures

**Microneurosurgery indications (Hegedus & Diecidue)**:
- Observed nerve severance
- Total anesthesia >3 months
- Dysesthesia >4 months
- Severe hyposthesia without improvement >4 months

**Contraindications**: Sensation improving, dysesthesia abolished by local anesthetic nerve block, excessive delay after injury, medically compromised patient

### 4.6 Prevention / Safety Zone

- Panoramic: implant ≥2 mm above mandibular canal / mental foramen (Bartling, Misch & Crawford)
- CT scan: implant ≥1 mm above canal
- Mental foramen anterior loop (이신경의 전방 루프): locate mesially → implant placed mesially
- Panoramic magnification correction: **L = H/M – c – s** (Worthington formula; H = apparent bone height above IAC; M = magnification factor; c = crestal bone too thin; s = safety zone)
- Screw-type implant: reverse 1/4 turn at final insertion to reduce apical bone compression
- Flap: tilt lingually in severely resorbed mandible to protect mental nerve

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative (not systematic) review; no meta-analysis; heterogeneous study populations and methods preclude quantitative pooling
- Heavy reliance on retrospective questionnaire data (surgeon and patient self-report); subjective perception differs person to person
- Wide variation in incidence (0–55%) reflects methodological and sampling differences, not necessarily true biological variation
- No standardized objective assessment tool for altered sensation existed at time of writing
- Prospective studies (Bartling, Vazquez) show markedly lower incidence than retrospective questionnaire studies — design bias likely
- Legal implications noted (16 cases in Israel 1992–1999; 4 compensations, 6 lawsuits)
- Authors recommend: future objective, standardized criteria for evaluating and classifying altered sensation following mandibular implant surgery

## 6. Related Work

- Ellies (1992) / Ellies & Hawker (1993): foundational retrospective studies, incidence 36–37%
- Bartling et al.: lower-incidence prospective study, 8.5%; rigorous safety-zone protocol
- Lydia Vazquez et al. (2007): largest prospective study (1527 patients, 2584 implants); extremely low incidence (0.08%)
- Kwon et al.: Korean dentist survey, 55% experienced nerve injury
- Misch & Crawford: safety zone definition using panoramic
- Hegedus & Diecidue: microneurosurgery indications/contraindications
- Hirsch & Branemark: 4-category classification of nerve injury mechanisms (direct mechanical, vessel compression, intravascular injury → intravascular clot, osteoma formation)
- Yosue & Brooks: 4-category classification of mental foramen appearance on panoramic

## 7. Glossary

- **감각이상 (Altered Sensation / Paresthesia)**: Abnormal sensation including hypoesthesia, hyperesthesia, dysesthesia, anesthesia, or numbness following nerve injury
- **하치조신경 (IAN, Inferior Alveolar Nerve)**: Main sensory nerve of mandibular posterior teeth and lower lip
- **이신경 (Mental Nerve)**: Terminal branch of IAN exiting at mental foramen; supplies lower lip, chin, gingiva
- **이공 (Mental Foramen, MF)**: Opening in mandibular buccal cortex; ~3.5 mm; located between PM2 and M1
- **이신경 전방 루프 (Anterior Loop)**: Anterior extension of IAN before exiting mental foramen; located mesially
- **설신경 (Lingual Nerve)**: Sensory nerve to anterior 2/3 of tongue; at risk during lingual flap elevation
- **안전구역 (Safety Zone)**: Buffer distance between implant apex and mandibular canal/mental foramen
- **미세신경문합술 (Microneuroanastomosis)**: Microsurgical repair/anastomosis of injured nerve
- **이환율 (Incidence / Prevalence)**: Proportion of patients developing altered sensation post-implant
- **후향적 조사 (Retrospective Study)**: Study looking back at past events; susceptible to recall bias
- **전향적 조사 (Prospective Study)**: Prospective follow-up of patients; generally lower incidence reported
