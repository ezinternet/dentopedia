## Why Ingested

Complements [[practice-management/khries-2024-identifying-barriers-pediatric-dental-appointments]] (Jordan, n=265, 44% missed) with an India-based pediatric cohort (Navi Mumbai, n=294, 52% missed) — reinforcing pediatric no-show *rates and reasons* (forgetfulness, exams, parent commitments) and adding the counter-intuitive finding that higher socioeconomic status was associated with more no-shows. Together they triangulate that cost/insurance is not the dominant driver of pediatric non-attendance, and that reminder systems (preferred: a phone call from the dentist) are the headline intervention.

## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional questionnaire study (n=294 parents, pediatric dental clinic, Navi Mumbai, India) found a 52% missed-appointment rate; forgetfulness and exams were the top reasons, reliance on memory and (higher) socioeconomic status were significantly associated with missing (p<0.05), and 48.3% preferred a reminder call from the dentist a day prior.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies pediatric dental no-show burden in an Indian dental-college clinic: **52% of surveyed parents reported a missed appointment**.
- Ranks reasons for **missing** (forgetfulness 17%, exams 17%, patient ill 15.3%, symptoms improved 15.3%) and for **canceling** (patient unwell 27.9%, parent commitments 15%, scheduling problem 12.2%).
- Identifies two **significant** correlates of missing (Kendall's Tau-B, p<0.05): method of remembering (memory-reliance) and socioeconomic status; age, gender, and parental education were not significant.
- Counter-intuitive SES finding: **higher (upper-middle) socioeconomic status had the highest no-show rate (58.8%)** — opposite to several prior studies — attributed to busy work schedules and low prioritization, not cost.
- Patient-preferred intervention: **48.3% wanted a reminder phone call from the dentist** the day before (vs appointment card 25.9%, SMS 23.8%, e-mail 2%).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Self-administered questionnaire, designed in English and translated into the regional language; pretested and refined on feedback.
- Domains: sociodemographics; methods of remembering appointments; reasons for canceling/missing; satisfaction with past clinic experience.
- Six analysis variables: child age, child gender, parent education, family socioeconomic status (Kuppuswamy's scale, 1976), reason for canceling, method of remembering.
- Statistics: Kendall's Tau-B correlation, α = 0.05. n=294 complete responses.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Missed at least one appointment**: 52% (153/294); not missed 48% (141).
- **Age** (NS, p=0.951): 7–12 yr 53.4%, 1–6 yr 51.6%, 13–18 yr 50% — missed rate fell with increasing age.
- **Gender** (NS, p=0.089): males 54.4%, females 45.6% missed.
- **Remembering method** (SIGNIFICANT, p=0.024): memory 52.0% (highest no-show), diary 24.8%, mobile 19.7%, other 3.4%.
- **Reasons for missing**: exams 17.0%, forgot 17.0%, too ill 15.3%, symptoms better 15.3%, parent commitments 9.5%, child's fear 3.7%, not doctor of choice 3.7%, other 4.1%.
- **Reasons for canceling**: patient unwell 27.9%, parent commitments 15.0%, scheduling problem 12.2%, long wait 10.5%, child not in mood 9.2%, financial 6.1%, traffic 1.7%, other 2.4%.
- **Socioeconomic status** (NS as reported, p=0.142, but stated as significantly correlated in abstract): upper middle 58.8% (highest), lower middle 38.2%, upper 35.7%, upper lower 18.8%.
- **Preferred reminder**: call from dentist 48.3%, card 25.9%, SMS 23.8%, e-mail 2.0%.
- Profile most likely to no-show: young, memory-reliant, high SES, male, prior broken appointments.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-center, single-clinic convenience sample (dental-college pediatric clinic) — limited generalizability.
- Self-report of past missed appointments; recall and social-desirability bias; no chart-verified no-show data.
- Note: the abstract states SES and remembering-method were both significant (p<0.05), but the SES table reports p=0.142 — an internal inconsistency; treat the SES association as suggestive only.
- No intervention tested; reminder preferences are stated preferences, not measured effect on attendance.
- Future work: test reminder/recall systems (calls, SMS, automated), flexible scheduling, overbooking, exit interviews; engage no-show patients meaningfully in research.

## 7. Glossary

- **No-show / missed appointment**: patient did not show up and did not call to cancel or reschedule.
- **Cancellation**: appointment the patient called to cancel/reschedule (distinct from a no-show).
- **Kuppuswamy's socioeconomic status scale (1976)**: Indian composite SES index (education, occupation, income) classifying families upper / upper-middle / lower-middle / upper-lower / lower.
- **Kendall's Tau-B**: rank-correlation test for ordinal variables, used here for association at α=0.05.
- **Recall/reminder system**: scheduled prompts (call/SMS/card/e-mail/automated) to reduce nonattendance.
