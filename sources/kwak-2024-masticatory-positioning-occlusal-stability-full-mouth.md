## Why Ingested

Adds a long-term (10-year) human clinical anchor for the centric-relation / therapeutic-position concept that underpins occlusal-scheme choices — extends [[occlusion/sippy-2021-condylar-incisal-guidance-canine-group-function-schemes]]'s static guidance-scheme framing into a longitudinal, function-driven (chewing-pattern) definition of the therapeutic position, and complements digital concordance-measurement work like [[occlusion/didier-2026-occlusal-contacts-ios-vs-carbon-paper-concordance]] by reporting concordance of mandibular movements (CM-H vs MICP) over a decade.

## One-line Summary

Prospective 10-year follow-up case series (n=16) using an intraoral Gothic arch tracer to set the normalized hard-food chewing position (CM-H) as the therapeutic position for full-mouth rehabilitation; 63% concordance between CM-H and MICP at both timepoints, with overall stomatognathic recovery improving 19%, supporting long-term stability of chewing-position-centered occlusal reconstruction.

## 한줄요약

전악 수복 환자 16명을 약 10년 추적한 전향적 증례군 — 구내 고딕아치 트레이서로 정상화한 단단한 음식 저작위치(CM-H)를 치료위(therapeutic position)로 설정. CM-H와 최대교두감합위(MICP) 일치율이 1차·2차 모두 63%로 유지되고 4가지 운동 전체 일치율은 19% 개선되어, 저작위 중심 교합 재건의 장기 안정성과 구강악계 회복을 임상적으로 뒷받침.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Function-defined therapeutic position.** Instead of defining centric relation (CR) by the most-posterior condylar (border-movement) position à la Gysi, the therapeutic position is set at the apex of a *normalized chewing pattern* (CM-H), i.e., a physiologic, neuromuscular- and TMJ-harmonized position rather than a ligament-guided border position.
2. **Decade-long human longitudinal data.** Same 16 patients retraced with the same intraoral Gothic arch device after a mean 10 years 3 months — rare long-term reproducibility evidence for an occlusal-reconstruction philosophy.
3. **Movement-concordance as a system-health readout.** CM-H vs CM-S → masticatory muscle health; CM-H vs MICP → chewing-position/occlusion long-term stability; CM-H vs BM → TMJ-complex health; all-four concordance → overall stomatognathic recovery.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Device**: N-GAT (BIOCR Co., Seoul) intraoral Gothic arch tracer — mandibular recording pin (fixed or spring-loaded stylus) + maxillary recording plate.
- **Sample**: 16 patients (6 M / 10 F), age 10–70 (mean 39.75 y at first visit), all full-mouth rehabilitation using chewing position (CP) as the therapeutic position. Four groups of 4:
  - G1: normal TMJ + prosthetic Tx + occlusal adjustment
  - G2: TMD Tx + prosthetic Tx + occlusal adjustment (2 with mild nocturnal bruxism)
  - G3: TMD Tx + orthodontic Tx (no occlusal adjustment)
  - G4: TMD Tx + orthodontic Tx + occlusal adjustment
- **TMD pre-treatment**: splint therapy 3–6 months before recording CP for patients with joint noise.
- **Recorded movements** (twice, ~10 y apart, on a single layered plate via sequential Arti-spray): CM-S (soft-food chewing), CM-H (hard-food chewing), BM (border movement, ligament-guided, opposite to chewing), MICP (max intercuspation + tooth guidance). Tapping movements (T-U up-right, T-S supine) recorded but **excluded** for high variability.
- **Reference / quantification**: CM-H apex set as origin (0,0); discrepancies measured in 0.1 mm units along X (lateral L-R) and Y (anteroposterior). Concordance classified per movement and scored as progression (○○ / ○ improvement), maintenance, or decline (×) between 1st and 2nd recordings.
- **CP selection rule**: when CM-H and CM-S apices differed, CM-H apex was chosen as the therapeutic position.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **CM-H vs MICP (chewing-position & occlusion long-term stability)**: 10/16 (63%) concordant at 1st recording and 10/16 (63%) at 2nd — stable over the decade (the specific concordant subjects shifted but the rate held).
- **CM-H vs CM-S (masticatory-muscle health)**: 11/16 (69%) → 13/16 (81%), a 13% (≈+2 subjects) improvement.
- **CM-H vs BM (TMJ-complex health)**: 1/16 (6%) → 5/16 (31%), a 25% improvement.
- **All-four concordance (overall stomatognathic recovery)**: complete concordance 1/16 (6%, subject B) → 4/16 (25%; A, B, C, P), a 19% improvement. Across the cohort: 6 maintained (37%) + 7 progressed (44%) = 13/16 (81%) recovery rate; 3 declined (19%).
- **Group ranking**: best recovery generally in G1 (normal TMJ, prosthetic + occlusal adjustment) reaching 100% on several metrics; for CM-H=MICP stability G1 > G4 > G2 > G3; G3 (orthodontic, no occlusal adjustment) weakest for CM-H=MICP (steady 25%).
- **Interpretation**: occlusion reconstructed around the normalized chewing position (point-centric fossa + canine guidance → mutually protected occlusion) remains stable and supports recovery of masticatory muscle and TMJ function over 10 years, compensating for age-related chewing decline.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small n (16), four heterogeneous treatment subgroups of 4 each → group comparisons underpowered, descriptive only (no inferential statistics / CIs reported).
- Single-operator, single-device (N-GAT) Gothic-arch method; reproducibility of the tracing and of "normalized chewing pattern" judgment is operator-dependent.
- Concordance thresholds (0.1 mm units; ○/× classification) are author-defined; no blinded measurement.
- No control group reconstructed by a conventional (border-movement / posterior-condylar) CR method, so the superiority of the chewing-position approach is inferred, not directly compared.
- Tapping movements excluded for variability — limits the "stomatognathic health" panel.

## 6. Related Work

- Gysi's classical Gothic-arch / border-movement method of determining CR (contrasted: this study uses chewing-pattern apex, not the most-posterior position).
- Point-centric (Gnathology) vs long-centric / freedom-in-centric (PMS) CR concepts — the paper situates its therapeutic position within this long-standing debate.
- Chewing-pattern-generator / proprioceptive-feedback neurophysiology of the masticatory system (PDL, TMJ, muscle mechanoreceptors).

## 7. Glossary

- **CP (Chewing Position)** — therapeutic position set at the apex of the normalized chewing pattern (here, CM-H).
- **CM-H / CM-S** — chewing movement for hard / soft food.
- **BM (Border Movement)** — ligament-guided, non-physiologic movement opposite in direction to chewing; basis of Gysi-style CR.
- **MICP** — maximum intercuspation position (+ tooth guidance), recorded at natural vertical dimension with spring stylus.
- **N-GAT** — BIOCR intraoral Gothic arch tracer used to record the movements.
- **Mutually protected occlusion** — final occlusal scheme (point-centric fossa + canine guidance) resembling natural dentition.
- **Stomatognathic system** — TMJ complex + teeth/occlusion + masticatory muscles under central masticatory control.
