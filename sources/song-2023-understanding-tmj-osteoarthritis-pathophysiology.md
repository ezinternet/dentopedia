## One-line Summary
Korean special-feature narrative review on TMJ OA pathophysiology (overload → hypoxia/HIF-1 → VEGF/MMP → cartilage & bone destruction), epidemiology (female-predominant, age-related but inconsistent), and diagnosis (CT is gold standard; cortical-continuity changes outweigh shape changes).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Anatomy**: mandibular condyle covered by dense fibrocartilage (not hyaline); zones — proliferative (undifferentiated mesenchymal reservoir), fibrocartilaginous (type II collagen + proteoglycan/aggrecan + hyaluronic acid aggregates), calcified cartilaginous (chondrocytes → osteoblast formation).
- **Pathophysiology**: physical overload exceeding physiologic resistance → intra-articular pressure ↑ → hypoxia → HIF-1 → VEGF → osteoclast migration + MMP (MMP-1/-3/-9 elevated in OA synovial fluid) + aggrecanase → collagen/proteoglycan breakdown. Reperfusion → ROS → hyaluronic acid degradation. Inflammatory cytokines (IL-1β, IL-6, TNF) elevated.
- **Epidemiology**: more common in women, age-related — but study results inconsistent.
- **Diagnosis / imaging reliability**: panorama κ=0.16 (poor), MRI κ=0.46 (fair), CT κ=0.71 (excellent); inter-examiner positive agreement panorama 19% / MRI 59% / CT 84% → CT is gold standard for bony change.
- **Bony change categories (5)**: flattening, sclerosis, erosion, subchondral cyst, osteophyte.
- **DC/TMD (2014, revised from RDC/TMD 1992)**: clinical criteria sensitivity 0.55 / specificity 0.61 → imaging essential. Imaging dx requires ≥1 of subchondral cyst, erosion, generalized sclerosis, or osteophyte. Flattening and/or cortical sclerosis = indeterminate (may be normal variant/aging/remodeling), excluded from dx criteria.
- **Clinical reading principle**: simple flattening or cortical-layer-confined sclerosis with intact cortical continuity is insufficient as a pathologic OA sign; erosion/subchondral cyst (loss of cortical continuity) better reflect disease stage. On CT, attend to cortical integrity more than condylar shape.
