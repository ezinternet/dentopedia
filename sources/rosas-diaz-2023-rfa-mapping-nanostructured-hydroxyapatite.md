## Why Ingested

The ISQ wiki currently contains RFA time-course data predominantly for SLA/CA/UV-treated surfaces. This prospective case series is the first mapped RFA dataset for a nanostructured hydroxyapatite (nano-HA) coated implant (SIN-UNITITE), documenting the dip timing and recovery pattern week-by-week over 20 weeks — filling a direct gap in [[wiki/overviews/implants-isq-stability-ladder]] which generalizes the primary→secondary stability dip without surface-specific ISQ trajectories for nano-HA implants.

## One-line Summary

Prospective case series (n=23 implants, 8 patients; 21 analyzed) mapping ISQ weekly over 20 weeks for nanostructured hydroxyapatite-coated implants (SIN-UNITITE), demonstrating a universal stability dip at weeks 2–3 followed by full recovery to or above baseline by week 8–10.

## 한줄요약

나노구조 하이드록시아파타이트(nano-HA) 코팅 임플란트(SIN-UNITITE) 23개(8명, 21개 분석)에서 20주간 매주 ISQ를 추적한 전향적 증례군 연구로, 식립 후 2–3주에 안정성 저하(dip)가 발생하고 8–10주에 기저치 이상으로 회복됨을 기록.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of International Society of Preventive and Community Dentistry
- **Volume/Issue**: 13(5):365–72
- **Published**: November 2022 (online); September–October 2023 (print issue)
- **Study design**: Prospective observational case series
- **Setting**: Teaching clinic, Faculty of Stomatology, Universidad Inca Garcilaso de la Vega, Lima, Peru (2017–2019)
- **Ethics**: Approved by ethics committee, Faculty of Stomatology, Universidad Inca Garcilaso de la Vega, resolution No. 007-2020-DFE-UIGV
- **Implant system**: SIN-UNITITE (S.I.N. Implant System, São Paulo, Brazil) — nanostructured hydroxyapatite surface
- **Measurement device**: Osstell (Integration Diagnostics AB, Göteborg, Sweden) using SmartPegs; buccal-lingual and mesial-distal readings averaged
- **Monitoring protocol**: Day 0 (placement), suture removal, then weekly through week 20
- **Follow-up duration**: 20 weeks

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First dedicated ISQ mapping study for nano-HA coated implants** — prior RFA literature focused on SLA, CA-SLA, or UV-activated surfaces; nano-HA provides osteoconductive nano-crystalline HA coating on titanium.
2. **Universal stability dip at weeks 2–3** regardless of length, diameter, torque, or jaw location — confirms the primary-to-secondary stability transition gap in nano-HA implants as in conventional-surface implants.
3. **Subgroup ISQ trajectories**: diameter 4.3 mm and torque ≥40 N-cm confer higher initial and final ISQ values; long implants (>10 mm) dip earlier (week 2) and recover faster (week 8) vs short implants (≤10 mm, dip week 3–4, recovery week 10).
4. **Jaw location**: Maxilla dips at week 1 (earlier, more abrupt) and recovers by week 8; mandible dips at weeks 3–4 and recovers by week 10 — no jaw-based difference in final ISQ at week 20.
5. **Survival**: 2/23 implants failed (lost osseointegration); 21/23 (91.3%) successfully integrated over 20 weeks.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

### Patients and Implants
- 8 patients (5 female, 3 male; age range 20–79 years) with partial edentulism
- 23 SIN-UNITITE implants placed; 21 analyzed (2 failures excluded from ISQ data)
- Implant dimensions: lengths 8, 10, 11.5, 13, 15 mm; diameters 3.5 mm (n=12) and 4.3 mm (n=11)
- Bone quality (Lekholm/Zarb): Type I (n=3), Type II (n=10), Type III (n=6), Type IV (n=4)
- Placement: 17 mandible (73.9%), 6 maxilla (26.1%); 16 posterior (69.6%), 7 anterior (30.4%)
- Healed sites: 22/23; 1 alveolar ridge preservation site
- Insertion torque: 11 implants <40 N-cm, 12 implants ≥40 N-cm (range 10–69 N-cm)

### ISQ Measurement Protocol
- SmartPeg screwed onto each implant; 3 consecutive measurements per direction (B-L and M-D)
- ISQ = average of B-L and M-D values
- Measured at: placement (day 0), suture removal (~1 week), then weekly through week 20
- Healing screws unscrewed at each measurement; no provisional prostheses during observation

### Grouping Strategy
Implants grouped proportionally by: (1) length ≤10 mm vs >10 mm; (2) diameter 3.5 mm vs 4.3 mm; (3) torque <40 vs ≥40 N-cm; (4) maxilla vs mandible.

### Surgical Protocol
- All implants placed by single surgeon, conventional drilling protocol
- Postoperative ibuprofen 400 mg TID × 3 days; 0.12% chlorhexidine rinse × 4 weeks

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Overall ISQ Time Course
- **Baseline ISQ range**: 30–79 (8 implants 70–79; 3 implants 30–49)
- **Stability dip**: all implants decreased ISQ in first 3 weeks after placement
- **Recovery**: ISQ increased from week 3–5 onward, reaching or exceeding baseline values by week 8–10
- **Week 20 values**: 68–74 ISQ range across subgroups

### By Implant Length
| Parameter | ≤10 mm | >10 mm |
|---|---|---|
| Baseline ISQ (mean) | 58.1 | 62.5 |
| Minimum ISQ (dip) | 41.6–41.9 at weeks 3–4 | 49.4 at week 2 |
| Recovery peak | 64.7 at week 10 | 69.4 at week 8 |
| Week 20 ISQ | 68.8 | 71.8 |

### By Implant Diameter
| Parameter | 3.5 mm | 4.3 mm |
|---|---|---|
| Baseline ISQ | 54.3 | 69.0 |
| Minimum ISQ (dip) | 35.3 at week 3 | 57.2 at week 2 |
| Recovery plateau | 66.8 at week 12 | 70.8 at week 8 |
| Week 20 ISQ | 68.1 | 73.7 |

### By Insertion Torque
| Parameter | <40 N-cm | ≥40 N-cm |
|---|---|---|
| Baseline ISQ | 52.6 | 67.9 |
| Minimum ISQ (dip) | 39.7 at week 3 | 50.8 at week 2 |
| Converge point | ~week 5–10 | ~week 5–10 |
| Week 20 ISQ | 68.6 | 72.2 |

### By Jaw Location
| Parameter | Maxilla | Mandible |
|---|---|---|
| Baseline ISQ | 60.5 | 60.7 |
| Minimum ISQ (dip) | 42.7 at week 1 | 45.7–46.1 at weeks 3–4 |
| Recovery plateau | 66.3 at week 8 | 68.0 at week 10 |
| Week 20 ISQ | 68.1 | 71.1 |

**Key finding**: Maxilla dips earlier and more steeply (week 1) than mandible (weeks 3–4); both converge to similar final ISQ by week 20.

### Directional Mapping
Measurements taken from two orthogonal directions (B-L and M-D); averaged per implant. No directional-anisotropy analysis was reported beyond the averaging protocol.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Small sample**: 8 patients, 23 implants (21 analyzed); no statistical hypothesis testing performed — purely descriptive/mapping
- **Case series design**: no control group (no comparison to conventional SLA or CA-SLA surfaces under same protocol)
- **Subgroup sizes unequal and small**: proportional grouping means some subgroups have very few implants
- **No prosthetic loading data**: follow-up ended at week 20 without prosthetic loading or clinical outcome reporting
- **Bone quality subjectivity**: Lekholm/Zarb classification is subjective; limits cross-study reproducibility
- **2 implant failures** not analyzed in ISQ time course — survival rate 91.3% over 20 weeks
- **Authors recommend**: future randomized clinical studies with larger samples and adequate follow-up to validate nano-HA surface effects

## 6. Related Work

- Sartoretto et al. (2020, Int J Nanomedicine): nanostructured HA surface in low-density bone animal model — positive early osseointegration effects cited by authors
- Martinez et al. (2018, Int J Oral Maxillofac Implants): in vitro study — HA-coated titanium promotes osteoblast proliferation, collagen I and osteopontin secretion
- Berglundh et al. (2003, Clin Oral Implants Res): sequence of healing events around implants — mechanical → resorption → apposition → secondary stability (foundational reference for the dip pattern)
- Lages et al. (2018): SR on ISQ vs insertion torque correlation — cited for insertion torque/ISQ independence
- Monje et al. (2019): primary/mechanical vs secondary/biological stability — cited for dip mechanism
- Sim & Lang (2010): factors influencing RFA (length, position, bone structure) — cited for length effect

## 7. Glossary

- **RFA (Resonance Frequency Analysis, 공명주파수분석)**: non-invasive method measuring bone-implant interface stiffness via vibration frequency of a transducer screwed to the implant
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient, 임플란트 안정성 지수)**: dimensionless score 1–100 derived from RFA; higher = greater stability
- **Osstell (Osstell 장치)**: commercial RFA measurement system using magnetic SmartPegs
- **SmartPeg**: magnetic peg screwed to implant fixture for Osstell measurement
- **Nanostructured Hydroxyapatite (나노구조 하이드록시아파타이트)**: nano-crystalline HA coating applied to implant surface to enhance osseoconduction and early bone bonding
- **SIN-UNITITE**: brand name of implant with nano-HA surface (S.I.N. Implant System, Brazil)
- **Primary stability (1차 안정성)**: mechanical retention at implant placement via thread-bone contact
- **Secondary stability (2차 안정성)**: biological union through osseointegration (new bone apposition)
- **Stability dip**: transient ISQ decrease during bone resorption phase (weeks 1–4) before secondary stability rises
- **Lekholm/Zarb classification**: subjective bone quality grading (Types I–IV based on cortical/trabecular composition)
- **B-L (bucco-lingual)**: buccal-to-lingual direction for Osstell measurement
- **M-D (mesio-distal)**: mesial-to-distal direction for Osstell measurement
