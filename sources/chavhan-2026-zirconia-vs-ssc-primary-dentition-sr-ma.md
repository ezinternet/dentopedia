## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 7 RCTs (700 crowns; 6 pooled for retention): prefabricated zirconia crowns (PZCs) and stainless steel crowns (SSCs) showed equivalent retention in primary molars (OR 0.96, 95% CI 0.40–2.32; p=0.93; I²=0%), with PZCs consistently demonstrating better gingival health and less plaque accumulation, possible increased antagonist wear due to zirconia hardness, and strongly superior parental esthetic satisfaction despite higher cost.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA (2018–2024) to comprehensively compare PZC vs SSC across retention, gingival health, plaque accumulation, antagonist wear, and parental satisfaction in a single synthesis using strict RCT-only inclusion.
- Pooled retention meta-analysis (fixed-effect; 6 RCTs; 166 ZR vs 170 SSC crowns): OR 0.96 (95% CI 0.40–2.32; p=0.93; I²=0%) — statistically equivalent. Justifies PZC as a reliable alternative to SSC for primary molars.
- PZC gingival advantage: most studies (Mathew 2020, Abuelniel 2018, Taran 2018) reported significantly better gingival health, likely due to PZC's polished, nonporous glazed surface reducing bacterial adherence and gingival inflammation.
- Antagonist wear caveat: 2 of 3 studies assessing this outcome found higher wear of opposing dentition with PZC (Prabhu 2022, Taran 2018), reflecting zirconia's hardness — clinical relevance low in most children but warrants monitoring in parafunctional habits.
- Parental satisfaction: uniformly and strongly favored PZC across all studies assessing this outcome (Murali 2022, Mathew 2020, Vishnu 2024, Prabhu 2022), driven by natural tooth-like appearance.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Protocol**: PRISMA 2020 guidelines; PROSPERO registered (CRD420251016795).

**PICOS**:
- P: Pediatric patients aged 3–10 years requiring full-coverage restorations on primary molars
- I: Prefabricated zirconia crowns (PZCs)
- C: Stainless steel crowns (SSCs) or other full-coverage restorations
- O: Crown retention, gingival health, plaque accumulation, antagonist wear, marginal integrity, esthetic/parental satisfaction
- S: RCTs with ≥6-month follow-up

**Search**: PubMed, Cochrane Library, ProQuest, Google Scholar, K-Hub (2018–2024); English-only; manual reference screening; expert contact for unpublished data. Search date: January–March 2025. (Note: CENTRAL/Embase/Scopus excluded due to institutional subscription restrictions; Google Scholar, ProQuest, K-Hub substituted.)

**Selection**: 233 records identified → 35 duplicates removed → 198 screened → 157 excluded on title/abstract → 41 full texts assessed → 34 excluded (irrelevant outcomes n=12, ineligible comparisons n=9, non-RCT/in vitro n=7, incomplete data n=3, duplicates n=3) → **7 included in qualitative synthesis; 6 contributed to quantitative meta-analysis** (Mathew 2020 excluded from meta-analysis because 100% retention in both groups made OR non-estimable).

**Risk of bias**: Cochrane RoB 2 tool. Most studies: low risk in randomization and outcome measurement. Some concerns in Prabhu 2022 and Vishnu 2024 (allocation concealment, participant blinding). Overall methodological quality: acceptable.

**Statistical model**: Fixed-effect (justified by I²=0%, χ²=3.73, df=5, p=0.59). Funnel plot showed generally symmetric distribution, no substantial publication bias.

**Quality appraisal**: QATSDD (Quality Assessment Tool for Studies with Diverse Designs) and GRADE criteria applied.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Crown retention (meta-analysis)**:
- Pooled OR 0.96 (95% CI 0.40–2.32; Z=0.09; p=0.93; I²=0%) — no statistically significant difference
- Most studies showed point estimates numerically favoring ZR, but with wide CIs
- Exception: Taran 2018 slightly favored SSC; Vishnu 2024 OR=8.20 (CI 0.40–169.90) favoring ZR but NS due to small sample

**Gingival health**:
- Majority of studies: PZC superior (Mathew 2020 p=0.047; Abuelniel 2018; Taran 2018)
- Vishnu 2024: no significant difference
- Agrawal 2022: slightly better SSC (the minority finding — likely influenced by short 3-month follow-up and operator technique)
- Mechanism: PZC's glazed, nonporous surface reduces plaque adhesion and gingival inflammation

**Plaque accumulation**:
- Lower with ZR: Abuelniel 2018, Taran 2018, Mathew 2020
- Higher with ZR: Agrawal 2022 (contrary finding; possibly technique/patient-related)
- Not reported: Prabhu 2022, Vishnu 2024

**Antagonist (opposing) tooth wear**:
- Higher with ZR: Prabhu 2022, Taran 2018 (material hardness)
- No significant difference: Murali 2022
- Clinically: slight but not functionally significant in most cases; greatest concern in parafunctional bruxers

**Parental/child satisfaction**:
- Uniformly and strongly favored PZC across all reporting studies (Murali 2022, Mathew 2020, Vishnu 2024, Prabhu 2022)
- Primary driver: natural tooth-like esthetic appearance
- Despite higher cost and technique sensitivity, caregivers consistently preferred PZC

**Bottom-line equivalence**: SSC success rates 88–96% over 10 years (established gold standard); PZCs match this with added gingival and esthetic benefits but introduce cost, technique demands, and antagonist wear considerations.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Short follow-up in most studies (6–12 months); Mathew 2020 is the exception at 36 months; long-term outcomes beyond primary tooth exfoliation not assessed
- Variation in ZR crown brands (NuSmile, KinderKrown, Kedo Zr) and luting cements across studies; limits cross-study comparability
- Only 6 of 7 RCTs contributed to the meta-analysis (Mathew 2020 excluded due to 100% retention in both arms — non-estimable OR)
- Blinding: difficult to blind participants/operators due to visible crown differences; subjective outcomes (gingival indices, parental satisfaction) prone to performance/detection bias
- Institutional subscription restricted access to CENTRAL, Embase, Scopus — substituted databases may have missed some eligible RCTs
- Calls for future high-quality standardized RCTs with longer follow-up, uniform reporting, and assessment of long-term antagonist wear effects

## 7. Glossary

- **Prefabricated zirconia crown (PZC)**: Preformed full-coverage crown made of yttria-stabilized tetragonal zirconia polycrystals (Y-TZP); tooth-colored, biocompatible, smooth-surfaced; introduced 2008; technique-sensitive, requires precise preparation and longer chairside time; more expensive than SSC. Brands: NuSmile, KinderKrown, Kedo Zr.
- **Stainless steel crown (SSC)**: Preformed metallic full-coverage crown introduced by Humphrey 1950; gold standard for primary molars; 88–96% success rate over 10 years; durable, cost-effective, operator-friendly; esthetically inferior.
- **Antagonist wear**: Wear of opposing natural teeth or restorations caused by contact with a harder crown material (zirconia harder than natural enamel and metal).
- **RoB 2**: Cochrane Risk of Bias 2 tool for randomized trials — domain-based assessment of bias risk.
- **Fixed-effect model**: Statistical pooling model assuming a single true effect size; appropriate when heterogeneity is low (I²≈0%).
- **I²**: Measure of statistical heterogeneity in meta-analysis; 0% indicates no between-study variance (consistent findings across studies).
- **QATSDD**: Quality Assessment Tool for Studies with Diverse Designs — multidimensional quality appraisal instrument.
