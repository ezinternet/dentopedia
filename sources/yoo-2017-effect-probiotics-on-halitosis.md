## Why Ingested

구취 임상 관리에서 프로바이오틱스의 효과가 기존 연구마다 상이하여, 이를 종합한 SR+MA가 필요한 상황. 본 리뷰는 OLT 점수(기관능관능 평가)와 VSC(휘발성 황화합물) 두 지표를 메타분석하여 프로바이오틱스의 구취 억제 효과를 정량화한 최초의 SR+MA 중 하나임. [[wiki/halitosis/jkda-2022-60-4-001]] 페이지의 한국 구취 클리닉 코호트와 대비하여 치료 효과 근거를 제공함.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 3 RCTs (2017): probiotics (mainly Lactobacillus) significantly reduce organoleptic halitosis scores (SMD −1.93) but do not significantly lower volatile sulfur compound concentrations (SMD −0.02).

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (RCT 3편, 2017): 프로바이오틱스(주로 Lactobacillus)는 기관능관능 구취 점수를 유의하게 낮추나(SMD −1.93), VSC 농도에는 유의한 차이 없음(SMD −0.02).

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First SR+MA to quantify probiotics' effect on halitosis using two primary outcomes: OLT score (organoleptic) and VSC (volatile sulfur compounds) concentration.
2. Demonstrated a moderate but statistically significant reduction in OLT scores with probiotic use (SMD = −1.93).
3. Showed that VSC reduction was not significantly improved by probiotics (SMD = −0.02), suggesting a discordance between subjective/panelist perception and instrumental measurement.
4. Identified a very small evidence base (only 3 RCTs met inclusion criteria out of 153 screened) highlighting the critical lack of high-quality evidence in this area.
5. Provided guidance for future study design: longer follow-up, standardized probiotic administration methods, and pretreatment protocols.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Inclusion criteria**: RCTs on any probiotic treatment ≥ 2-week duration, any age, measuring OLT or VSC
- **Exclusion criteria**: Non-RCT designs, follow-up < 2 weeks, no quantitative halitosis outcome
- **PRISMA**: Reported following PRISMA guidelines
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane Collaboration's tool for assessing risk of bias in RCTs
- **Statistical method**: Standardized mean difference (SMD) with 95% CI; random-effects model
- **Heterogeneity**: I² statistic (Higgins 2003)
- **Included studies** (3 RCTs):
  - Penala et al. (2016): scaling/root planing + probiotics vs scaling alone in chronic periodontitis; n not specified in abstract
  - Jamali et al. (2016): CHX pretreatment + Streptococcus salivarius K12 in children
  - Suzuki et al. (2014): Lactobacillus salivarius WB21 tablets (double-blind, crossover)
- **Primary outcomes**: OLT score, VSC concentration

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | SMD | 95% CI | P-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| OLT score | −1.93 | −2.85 to −1.02 | < 0.0001 |
| VSC concentration | −0.02 | −2.12 to 2.07 | 0.98 (NS) |

- OLT: Probiotics significantly reduced organoleptic halitosis scores vs placebo — moderate effect size
- VSC: No significant difference in volatile sulfur compound levels between probiotic and placebo groups
- Duration: average treatment duration ~2 weeks across included studies
- Main probiotic strains studied: Lactobacillus salivarius WB21, Streptococcus salivarius K12, Lactobacillus-containing probiotic adjunct to scaling
- Current evidence described as "quantitatively and qualitatively insufficient" for further recommendations on administration strategies and pretreatment

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 3 RCTs met inclusion criteria; evidence base is extremely limited
- Short follow-up (~2 weeks); long-term effects unknown
- Heterogeneity in probiotic strains, administration routes, and doses across studies
- VSC data had wide CI and high variability, limiting conclusions
- No dose-response data; optimal probiotic strain, dose, and duration undefined
- Future studies should standardize administration protocols, include longer follow-up, and assess pretreatment effects

## 6. Related Work

- Iwamoto et al. (2010): open-label pilot of Lactobacillus salivarius WB21 on halitosis — one of the precursor studies
- Keller et al. (2012): Lactobacillus reuteri chewing gum and oral malodour — screened, not included (non-RCT or <2 weeks)
- Marchetti et al. (2015): Lactobacillus brevis lozenges and halitosis monitoring — multi-sensor approach
- Zupancic et al. (2017): Streptococcus salivarius K12 and oral/ear health SR
- Muniz et al. (2017): chewing gum impact on halitosis — parallel SR examining different intervention category

## 7. Glossary

- **OLT (Organoleptic Test)**: Sensory panel assessment of breath odor; subjective but gold-standard clinically
- **VSC (Volatile Sulfur Compounds)**: Hydrogen sulfide (H₂S), methyl mercaptan (CH₃SH), dimethyl sulfide — primary chemical mediators of halitosis, measured by gas chromatography or Halimeter
- **SMD (Standardized Mean Difference)**: Effect size metric for comparing continuous outcomes across studies with different scales
- **Probiotics**: Live microorganisms that, when administered in adequate amounts, confer a health benefit; mainly Lactobacillus strains studied here
- **Streptococcus salivarius K12**: Probiotic strain known to produce BLIS (bacteriocin-like inhibitory substances) that inhibit VSC-producing bacteria
