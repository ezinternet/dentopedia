## Why Ingested

PubMed 감시 인제스트(transcrestal sinus lift)로 발굴한 신규 head-to-head RCT. 기존 [[sinus-lift/transcrestal/farina-2018-morbidity-transcrestal-lateral-sinus-floor-elevation]]가 측방 vs 경치조골의 morbidity를 비교했다면, 본 RCT는 **둘 다 graftless** 조건에서 4–6 mm 잔존골 부위의 12개월 ISQ·수직골증가·MBL을 직접 비교해 "안정성은 동등, 수직골증가는 측방이 우월, 합병증·불편감은 측방이 많음"이라는 trade-off를 정량화한다. graft 없는 transcrestal 적응증 논의를 [[sinus-lift/transcrestal/sansupakorn-2024-osfe-bcp-graft-versus-no-graft-rct]]의 graft-vs-no-graft 축과 보완적으로 확장한다. ## One-line Summary

RCT (n=26, RBH 4–6 mm, graftless) comparing lateral window vs transcrestal/crestal maxillary sinus floor elevation: no significant 12-month ISQ difference (80.92 vs 80.19) and no MBL difference, but significantly greater intra-sinus vertical bone gain with the lateral window (3.82 vs 2.42 mm) at the cost of more postoperative complications and patient discomfort.

## 한줄요약

무이식(graftless) 측방 vs 경치조골 상악동거상 RCT (n=26, 잔존골 4–6 mm) — 12개월 ISQ(80.92 vs 80.19)·변연골소실 차이 없음, 그러나 수직골증가는 측방이 유의하게 큼(3.82 vs 2.42 mm), 대신 측방이 합병증·환자 불편감 더 많음.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Direct, head-to-head RCT of **graftless** lateral window vs transcrestal MSFE under matched RBH (4–6 mm) — isolates the approach variable with grafting removed.
- Stability equivalence: ISQ at 12 months not significantly different between approaches.
- Quantifies the bone-gain trade-off: lateral window yields significantly more intra-sinus vertical bone gain, but transcrestal incurs fewer complications and less discomfort.
- MBL equivalence at 12 months → both approaches periodontally stable peri-implant in the short term.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Design: parallel-arm RCT, single posterior maxillary missing teeth, delayed implant placement.
- Population: 26 sites, residual ridge height 4–6 mm.
- Groups: graftless lateral window (control) vs crestal/transcrestal implant approach (test).
- Outcomes: implant stability (resonance frequency analysis / ISQ); intra-sinus vertical bone gain and marginal bone loss via CBCT at 6 and 12 months; clinical parameters and patient-reported outcomes.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- ISQ at 12 months: lateral 80.92 ± 4.57 vs crestal 80.19 ± 7.28 — not significant.
- Intra-sinus vertical bone gain: lateral significantly higher (p < 0.001) at 6 mo (3.64 ± 0.84 mm) and 12 mo (3.82 ± 0.84 mm) vs crestal (1.66 ± 1.27 mm; 2.42 ± 1.39 mm).
- Marginal bone loss: no intergroup difference (p = 0.31) at 12 months.
- Postoperative complications and patient discomfort: more evident in the lateral window group.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample (n=26) and single-center; 1-year follow-up only — survival/MBL durability not yet established.
- Abstract-only ingest — randomization/allocation detail, drop-outs, and exact PROM instruments not extractable here.
- "Graftless" design narrows generalizability; clinical relevance of greater lateral bone gain when transcrestal stability is already equivalent is debatable for the 4–6 mm RBH window.
- Restricted to RBH 4–6 mm — does not inform the <4 mm decision zone where approach choice is most contested.

## 7. Glossary

- **Graftless sinus floor elevation**: membrane elevation relying on the blood clot / implant-tenting space for new bone, no exogenous graft material placed.
- **Lateral window approach**: sinus access via a bony window in the lateral maxillary wall (open technique).
- **Intra-sinus vertical bone gain (endo-sinus bone gain)**: apical new bone formed above the original sinus floor around the protruding implant.
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: resonance frequency analysis index of implant stability (1–100).
