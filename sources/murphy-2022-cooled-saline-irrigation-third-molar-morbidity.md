## Why Ingested

Part of a recent third-molar / post-operative-morbidity collection. This Evidence-Based Dentistry summary review appraises a split-mouth RCT showing cooled (4°C/10°C) saline irrigation reduces pain, trismus and swelling after third molar surgery — a low-cost intraoperative measure that complements the morbidity-reduction adjuncts in [[oral-surgery/pattnayak-2024-prf-prp-third-molar-extraction-sr]] and the surgical-technique evidence in [[oral-surgery/bailey-2020-surgical-techniques-mandibular-wisdom-teeth-sr]].

## One-line Summary

Evidence-Based Dentistry summary review of a double-blind, split-mouth RCT (48 patients): irrigating with cooled saline (4°C or 10°C) during mandibular third molar surgery significantly reduced post-operative pain, analgesic use, trismus and swelling versus 25°C saline, with the 4°C group performing best.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Critically appraises a primary RCT testing whether **intraoperative cooled saline irrigation** reduces early post-operative morbidity after mandibular third molar removal.
- Highlights a simple, low-cost intraoperative modification (cooling the irrigant) as effective for reducing pain, trismus, and swelling.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture (of the appraised primary RCT)

- Design: double-blind, single-centre, split-mouth, prospective randomized controlled trial.
- 48 medically fit adults (28 female, 20 male; mean age 24.6 ± 3.8 y) with bilateral asymptomatic mandibular third molars, removed in two operations ≥21 days apart by the same maxillofacial surgeon.
- Control operation: saline irrigation at 25°C.
- Test operation: randomized to saline at 10°C (n=24) or 4°C (n=24).
- Local anaesthetic, flap design, burr design, and sutures held constant.
- All patients: amoxicillin, chlorhexidine, paracetamol as needed. Patients blinded to test group and to control/test order.
- Outcomes: self-recorded analgesia use and VAS pain daily for 7 days; a second surgeon assessed swelling (soft-tissue point distances vs pre-op) and trismus (maximum inter-incisal opening vs pre-op) on days 1, 3, 7.
- Statistics: Shapiro-Wilk; chi-square; Duncan, Kruskal-Wallis, Dunn, Friedman tests (p<0.05).
- Exclusions: 2nd molars with probing depth >4 mm, third molars with cysts/tumours; antibiotics in the prior month or analgesics within 12 h pre-op.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Operation durations similar between control and test (p=0.051).
- Test group: lower VAS pain and less analgesic use vs control (p=0.001), lowest in the **4°C group** (p<0.001).
- Greater decrease in trismus on days 3 and 7 in test groups (p<0.001) vs control (p=0.07).
- Swelling greatest in the control group (p<0.001), reduced by day 7 in all groups (p<0.001); 10°C lower than control (p<0.001), lowest at all time points in the **4°C group** (p<0.001).
- Conclusion: cooled saline irrigation at 4°C and 10°C was more effective than 25°C saline at reducing post-operative pain, trismus, and swelling.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-centre, single-surgeon primary trial; relatively young, medically fit, asymptomatic cohort (limited generalizability).
- This wiki page is built from the EBD summary review (abstract-level); detailed appraisal text is paywalled.
- Mechanism (local vasoconstriction / reduced inflammatory response from cooling) inferred, not directly measured.

## 7. Glossary

- **Split-mouth design** — Each patient serves as their own control (one side test, other side control).
- **VAS** — Visual analogue scale for self-reported pain.
- **Trismus** — Restricted mouth opening (reduced maximum inter-incisal opening).
- **EBD summary review** — Structured critical appraisal of a primary study published in Evidence-Based Dentistry.
