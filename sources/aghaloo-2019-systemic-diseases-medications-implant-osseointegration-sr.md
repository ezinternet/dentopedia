## Why Ingested

Provides broad systemic-disease and medication context for osseointegration risk in medically compromised (including autoimmune) implant candidates, reinforcing the sibling autoimmune-specific review [[wiki/implants/esimekara-2022-dental-implants-autoimmune-diseases-sr]]. This ITI consensus SR anchors the wider systemic-risk landscape (diabetes, osteoporosis, RA, HIV, CVD, neurologic, hypothyroid, plus SSRI/PPI medications) into which the autoimmune-implant evidence is situated.

## One-line Summary

ITI consensus systematic review (abstract-only) finding that most systemic diseases — including well-controlled diabetes, osteoporosis, HIV, CVD, neurologic disorders, hypothyroidism, and rheumatoid arthritis — show no direct evidence of reduced implant osseointegration, while SSRIs and PPIs show preliminary negative effects and antiresorptives warrant ONJ caution.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Synthesizes the evidence on whether common systemic disorders and concomitant medications impair dental implant osseointegration, framed as an ITI consensus systematic review.
- Distinguishes the effect on the **osseointegration rate** itself (largely not reduced) from the effect on **long-term peri-implant complications** (which can increase, e.g., in diabetes).
- Flags **medications** (SSRIs, PPIs) as an emerging, under-recognized risk signal distinct from the underlying systemic disease.
- Highlights antiresorptive-associated osteonecrosis of the jaws (ONJ) as the principal caution in the osteoporosis/bone-malignancy context rather than failure of osseointegration per se.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Systematic review conducted under the ITI consensus framework, evaluating systemic disorders (diabetes, osteoporosis), additional conditions (neurocognitive disease, cardiovascular disease, HIV, hypothyroidism, rheumatoid arthritis), and medications (SSRIs, PPIs, antihypertensives) for their effect on implant osseointegration.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Diabetes**: Literature does NOT demonstrate that diabetes negatively affects osseointegration — most studies involved well-controlled diabetics with prophylactic antibiotics; however, increased long-term bone and soft-tissue complications were shown.
- **Osteoporosis**: Recent studies/reviews fail to demonstrate a lower osseointegration rate; caution is warranted for osteonecrosis of the jaws (ONJ), especially with bone malignancies and antiresorptive medications.
- **HIV, cardiovascular disease, neurologic disorders, hypothyroidism, rheumatoid arthritis**: No direct evidence of a decreased osseointegration rate.
- **Medications**: Preliminary evidence that SSRIs or PPIs may negatively affect osseointegration (recent finding requiring validation).
- **Conclusion**: Disease control, concomitant medications, and comorbidities complicate osseointegration and must guide treatment approaches and clinical guidelines.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only ingest — full-text methodology, heterogeneity, GRADE/quality appraisal, and quantitative pooling are not captured.
- Evidence for SSRI/PPI effects is preliminary and requires validation in prospective studies.
- Many included conditions (HIV, neurologic, hypothyroid, RA) are supported by limited direct implant data, so "no evidence of reduced osseointegration" reflects scarcity as much as safety.
- Long-term complication risk (vs. initial integration) is the more clinically relevant endpoint for several conditions and is less well quantified.

## 7. Glossary

- **Osseointegration**: Direct structural and functional connection between living bone and a load-bearing implant surface.
- **ONJ (Osteonecrosis of the Jaws)**: Exposed, non-healing necrotic jawbone, classically associated with antiresorptive/antiangiogenic medications.
- **Antiresorptive**: Drug class (bisphosphonates, denosumab) that suppresses osteoclastic bone resorption.
- **SSRI (Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitor)**: Antidepressant class linked in preliminary data to impaired bone metabolism around implants.
- **PPI (Proton Pump Inhibitor)**: Acid-suppressing drug class with preliminary signals of negative effect on osseointegration.
- **RA (Rheumatoid Arthritis)**: Chronic autoimmune inflammatory joint disease; no current evidence of reduced osseointegration.
- **ITI (International Team for Implantology)**: Academic organization that issues consensus statements on implant dentistry.
