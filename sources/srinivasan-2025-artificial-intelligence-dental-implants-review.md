## One-line Summary
IJDM 2025;7(4):120-124 (Chettinad): AI in implant dentistry narrative review — diagnostic efficiency, treatment planning, patient outcomes; accuracy, rapidity, precision highlighted; drawbacks limit current clinical use; further clinical trials needed.

## 1. Key Results
- Narrative review; databases: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, Google Scholar
- AI roles: implant identification, planning, prediction, complication management
- Impacts: diagnostic efficiency↑, treatment planning accuracy↑, patient outcome↑
- Key advantage: accuracy + rapidity + precision of clinical decision-making
- Current drawbacks: data dependency, limited interpretability, cost, regulation
- Conclusion: AI as excellent supportive tool pending further clinical trial evidence

## 2. Clinical Implications
- AI can support (not replace) clinical decision-making in implant dentistry
- Integration into digital workflows shows promise across all phases
- Regulatory approval and standardization remain barriers to widespread adoption
