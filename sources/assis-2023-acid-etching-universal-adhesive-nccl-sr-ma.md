## One-line Summary
SR+MA of 20 RCTs showing that etch-and-rinse strategy for universal adhesives in NCCLs provides better medium-term (12–36 months) retention, marginal adaptation, and marginal discoloration, while self-etch leads to lower postoperative sensitivity.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Operative Dentistry, 2023; 48(4):373–390
- **DOI**: 10.2341/22-067-LIT
- **Publication type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis (Literature Review)
- **Published online**: 03 July 2023
- **Databases**: PubMed/MEDLINE, Scopus, Web of Science, Cochrane Library (up to December 2021)
- **Registration**: PROSPERO

## 2. Key Contributions
- Largest SR+MA to date on acid etching influence for universal adhesives in NCCLs (20 RCTs included)
- Demonstrates E&R strategy provides better medium-term (>12–36 months) retention of NCCL restorations
- E&R also results in lower marginal discoloration, better marginal adaptation, lower secondary caries rates
- Self-etch strategy yields lower postoperative sensitivity — a clinically significant trade-off
- Conducted per PRISMA guidelines and registered in PROSPERO

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: Systematic review with meta-analysis (PRISMA, PROSPERO-registered)
- **Databases**: PubMed/MEDLINE, Scopus, Web of Science, Cochrane Library (up to December 2021)
- **Initial records**: 170 articles after duplicate removal
- **Screening**: 146 excluded after title/abstract review; 24 full-text evaluated; 4 further excluded
- **Included studies**: 20 RCTs (both SR and meta-analysis)
- **Inclusion criteria**: Only RCTs comparing E&R and SE strategies using universal adhesive systems in NCCLs
- **Outcomes**: Retention, marginal discoloration, marginal adaptation, secondary caries, postoperative sensitivity

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Retention**: E&R strategy showed better medium-term (>12–36 months) retention than SE
- **Marginal discoloration**: E&R resulted in lower percentages than SE
- **Marginal adaptation**: E&R showed better performance than SE
- **Secondary caries**: E&R associated with lower secondary caries incidence
- **Postoperative sensitivity**: Self-etch strategy associated with lower postoperative sensitivity
- Key trade-off: E&R superiority on most outcomes vs SE advantage on sensitivity

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Limited access to full text for some studies (journal paywall noted in extraction)
- Heterogeneity in NCCL etiology (abrasion, erosion, abfraction) not consistently reported
- Short follow-up in some included studies (<12 months)
- Need for >36-month follow-up RCTs with universal adhesives
- Sensitivity analyses limited by number of available studies per subgroup

## 6. Related Work
- Doshi et al. 2023 — updated SR+MA finding no significant difference between TE and SE in NCCLs
- Hong et al. 2021 — SR+MA showing E&R superiority in general restorations
- Rosa et al. — meta-analysis on bond strength of universal adhesives
- Bresser et al. 2019 — earlier SR on universal adhesives in NCCLs

## 7. Glossary
- **NCCL**: Non-carious cervical lesion — hard tissue defect at the CEJ not caused by bacteria
- **Etch-and-rinse strategy**: Prior phosphoric acid etching and rinsing before universal adhesive application
- **Self-etch strategy**: Universal adhesive applied without prior acid conditioning
- **PRISMA**: Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses guidelines
- **PROSPERO**: International prospective register of systematic reviews
- **Medium-term**: Follow-up period of >12 to 36 months in this context
- **Marginal discoloration**: Staining at the tooth–restoration interface
