## Why Ingested

임플란트 유형(tissue-level vs bone-level)과 보철 유형(단일 크라운 vs 3유닛 고정성 부분 보철)이 변연골 소실(Marginal Bone Loss, MBL)에 미치는 영향을 2년 추적한 비교 연구로, 수렴형 경부(convergent neck)를 가진 tissue-level 임플란트의 골 보존 효과를 정량화한다. [[implants/mortazavi-2021-bone-loss-tissue-bone-level-implants]]에서 제기된 tissue-level 우위 가설을 임상 데이터로 직접 뒷받침하며, [[implants/atieh-2025-tissue-vs-bone-level-peri-implantitis-sr-ma]]의 SR+MA 메타분석에 포함될 원시 데이터 유형의 대표 연구이다.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective clinical study (n=120 implants, 53 patients, 2-year follow-up) showing tissue-level implants with convergent neck had significantly less peri-implant MBL (0.31 ± 0.48 mm) than bone-level implants (0.97 ± 0.91 mm), independent of prosthesis type.

## 한줄요약

53명 환자 120개 임플란트 2년 추적 후향적 연구에서 수렴형 경부 조직 수준 임플란트(MBL 0.31 mm)가 골 수준 임플란트(MBL 0.97 mm)보다 변연골 소실이 유의하게 적었으며, 보철 유형(단일 vs FPP)의 영향은 없었다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Quantified MBL advantage of tissue-level over bone-level**: Tissue-level implants with convergent transmucosal neck (PRAMA RF, Sweden & Martina) showed 0.31 ± 0.48 mm MBL vs 0.97 ± 0.91 mm for bone-level (KOHNO, Sweden & Martina) — a highly significant difference (p < 0.001).
2. **Prosthesis type does not affect MBL**: Single crowns (0.55 ± 0.68 mm) vs fixed partial prostheses (FPP; 0.73 ± 0.90 mm) showed no significant difference (p = 0.338). This holds within both implant types.
3. **Arch location (maxilla vs mandible) does not affect MBL**: No significant difference overall (p = 0.079) or within either implant group.
4. **Position within FPP matters for bone-level but not tissue-level**: Anterior bone-level implants in FPP showed MBL 1.44 ± 1.32 mm vs posterior 0.74 ± 0.47 mm (p = 0.001). Tissue-level FPP showed no such positional effect (0.40 vs 0.35 mm, p = 0.606).
5. **Convergent neck biology**: The convergent transmucosal neck provides more space for supracrestal collagen fiber attachment, displacing the microgap away from the bone crest and reducing bacterial filtration to the peri-implant bone.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

### Study Design
- Retrospective comparative study of patients treated at the Dental Clinic, University of Valencia (June 2016 – June 2017)
- Single surgeon (R.A.-P., 15 years experience)
- All implants placed in posterior zones (molars, premolars), both maxilla and mandible

### Sample
- 53 patients (mean age 54.2 years; range 20–75 years)
- 120 implants total: 60 tissue-level + 60 bone-level
- 4 subgroups (n=30 each):
  - Group Ia: Tissue-level + Single Crown (TSSC)
  - Group Ib: Tissue-level + FPP (TFPP)
  - Group IIa: Bone-level + Single Crown (BSSC)
  - Group IIb: Bone-level + FPP (BFPP)
- 46 implants maxilla; 74 implants mandible

### Implant Systems
- **Tissue-level (Group I)**: PRAMA RF (Sweden & Martina, Padua, Italy) — machined neck 2.8 mm height (0.8 mm straight + 2 mm hyperbolic profile), internal hex connection 3.4 mm, supracrestal placement with convergent transmucosal neck
- **Bone-level (Group II)**: PREMIUM/KOHNO (Sweden & Martina, Padua, Italy) — internal hex connection 3.8 mm, peripheral ring at juxta-osseous level, crestal placement

### Inclusion Criteria
- Adults in good general health (ASA I)
- Partial edentulism in posterior zones
- Non-smokers or light smokers (<10 cigarettes/day)
- No parafunctions
- Minimum 24 months follow-up post-loading
- Complete periapical radiographic series (surgery, loading, 1 year, 2 years)

### Exclusion Criteria
- Age <18 years
- Poor oral hygiene, systemic disease, bisphosphonate treatment
- Smokers >10 cigarettes/day
- Prior bone regeneration at measurement site
- Missing control radiographs

### Radiographic Measurement
- Digital periapical radiographs with Rinn paralleling positioners (Dentsply)
- Carestream RVG digital system (Atlanta, GA)
- Measurements via Rhinoceros® software (Robert McNeel & Associates) — scaled using known prosthetic platform width
- Reference height (0 mm): prosthetic platform edge (bone-level) or junction of machined neck and implant body (tissue-level)
- Mesial and distal bone loss measured perpendicular from reference to bone or radiolucent zone edge
- Single observer (R.L.-M.) for all measurements

### Statistical Analysis
- Wald chi-square test for group comparisons
- Generalized Estimating Equations (GEE) for interaction analysis
- Bonferroni correction for multiple comparisons
- Significance level α = 0.05, 95% CI

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

1. **Retrospective design**: Cannot fully control for confounders; no randomization
2. **Single operator and single institution**: Limits generalizability
3. **Short follow-up (2 years)**: Long-term MBL progression, survival rates, and peri-implantitis incidence not assessed
4. **Radiographic measurement only**: No probing depth, bleeding on probing, or soft-tissue measurements; no true peri-implantitis diagnosis
5. **Single-observer radiographic measurement**: Risk of systematic bias despite standardized protocol
6. **No soft-tissue outcomes**: The paper does not report probing pocket depth, bleeding indices, or mucosal recession
7. **Imbalanced jaw distribution**: 46 maxillary vs 74 mandibular implants; subgroup power may be limited
8. **No survival/failure reporting**: No implants reportedly failed but no explicit kaplan-meier data provided

## 6. Related Work

- Agustín-Panadero et al. (prior work, ref [13]): compared 6 implant groups (3 TL, 3 BL) — all TL groups showed significantly less bone loss
- Bilhan et al. (ref [23]): 105 TL vs 36 BL — confirmed lower MBL in TL group
- López et al. (refs [11,12]): found no significant difference between TL and BL — contrasting result
- Wallner et al. (refs [19,20]): similar methodology/samples, also used periapical paralleling radiographs
- Firme et al. (ref [29]): meta-analysis of crowns vs FPP — no significant MBL difference (consistent with this study)
- Sola-Ruiz et al. (ref [21]): anterior implants → greater bone loss — consistent with this study's FPP findings
- Two meta-analyses cited (refs [14,27]): convergent necks show less MBL than divergent necks due to greater soft tissue accommodation space (>2 mm biologic width)
- Histological dog studies (ref [28]): abutment geometry influences circular collagen fiber orientation, affecting peri-implant bone stability

## 7. Glossary

- **Tissue-level implant**: Implant with transmucosal collar placed supragingivally; microgap located away from bone crest
- **Bone-level implant**: Implant placed at crestal bone level; microgap at or near bone, requiring abutment connection
- **Convergent neck**: Transmucosal collar with hyperbolic/convergent profile that creates more space for supracrestal collagen fiber attachment
- **BOPT (Biologically Oriented Preparation Technique)**: Philosophy by Dr. Loi; guides soft tissue management for stable peri-implant biology; convergent neck implants follow this concept
- **MBL (Marginal Bone Loss)**: Radiographic measurement of crestal bone change from baseline (implant placement) to follow-up
- **Microgap**: Junction space between implant and abutment/prosthesis — site of potential bacterial colonization and inflammation
- **FPP (Fixed Partial Prosthesis)**: 3-unit fixed bridge supported by 2 implants
- **GEE (Generalized Estimating Equations)**: Statistical method for correlated data (multiple implants per patient)
- **PRAMA RF**: Sweden & Martina tissue-level implant with 2.8 mm machined convergent neck (0.8 mm straight + 2 mm hyperbolic), internal hex
- **KOHNO / PREMIUM**: Sweden & Martina bone-level implant with peripheral ring at juxta-osseous level, internal hex
