## Why Ingested

OMS 클러스터 내 구강상악동루 (Oroantral Fistula, OAF) 외과적 폐쇄 술식 지식 공백을 채우기 위해 수집. 기존 [[wiki/oral-medicine/lin-2024-odontogenic-maxillary-sinusitis-expert-consensus]] 및 [[wiki/oral-medicine/kim-2019-definition-management-odontogenic-maxillary]]는 OMS 진단·치료 개요를 다루지만, 실제 OAF 폐쇄 플랩 선택 알고리즘과 각 술식의 적응증·장단점을 상세히 제공하지 않는다. 본 리뷰는 협측 전진 피판 (Buccal Advancement Flap), 협측 지방 패드 (Buccal Fat Pad, BFP), 구개 회전 피판 (Palatal Rotational Flap) 세 가지 주요 국소 피판 술식을 비교해 임상 의사결정 근거를 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

Narrative review of local flap techniques for oroantral fistula (OAF) closure — buccal advancement flap, buccal fat pad, and palatal rotational flap — with a size-based decision algorithm and illustrative case series from a Korean oral and maxillofacial surgery department.

## 한줄요약

구강상악동루 (Oroantral Fistula, OAF) 폐쇄를 위한 협측 전진 피판, 협측 지방 패드, 구개 회전 피판 세 가지 국소 피판 술식의 적응증·장단점과 크기 기반 치료 알고리즘을 정리한 서술적 문헌고찰.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Size-based treatment algorithm**: A practical flowchart for OAF management based on (a) fistula size and (b) presence of sinusitis:
   - < 3 mm: observe for spontaneous closure
   - < 5 mm without sinusitis: simple suture
   - ≥ 5 mm or failed suture: flap procedure
   - Sinusitis present: infection control first (medication/irrigation for acute; ESS/Caldwell-Luc for chronic), then flap closure

2. **Comparative review of three local flap techniques** (buccal flap, BFP, palatal rotational flap) with advantages, disadvantages, and clinical case illustrations.

3. **Infection control priority**: Emphasizes that the OAF tract itself acts as drainage — closure before infection control may exacerbate sinusitis. Acute sinusitis: amoxicillin/clavulanate 1 g/125 mg TID × 10-14 days + nasal decongestants + NSAIDs.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PubMed narrative literature review
- Illustrative case series from the Kyung Hee University Department of Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery
- Covers buccal flap (Rehrmann 1936 + Moczair sliding variant), buccal fat pad (Egyedi technique), and palatal rotational flap

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Buccal Advancement Flap (Rehrmann)
- Oldest and most common technique (Rehrmann 1936)
- Trapezoid mucoperiosteal flap advanced over defect
- **Advantage**: Good blood supply; proximity to defect; ease of use — preferred for small OAFs
- **Disadvantage**: Reduction in buccal sulcus depth → decreased denture retention
- Buccal sliding flap (Moczair variant): minimizes sulcus reduction but risks periodontal disease/gingival recession from extensive dentogingival detachment

### Buccal Fat Pad (BFP)
- First described by Egyedi (1977) for OAF closure
- Blood supply: buccal + deep temporal branches of maxillary artery, transverse branch of superficial temporal artery, branches of facial artery
- Harvest: vestibular incision at maxillary tuberosity, blunt dissection under periosteum, gentle traction
- **Advantages**: Rich multi-source blood supply; negligible donor-site morbidity; does not reduce buccal sulcus depth; exposed fat self-epithelializes within 2–6 weeks
- **Disadvantages**: Risk of graft necrosis and new fistulae for large defects; requires careful manipulation; prior head/neck radiation → decreased BFP size and mobility (contraindication relative)
- Double-layered suturing (BFP + oral mucosa overlay) recommended to minimize postoperative complications
- **Indicated for**: Medium-to-large OAFs; cases where buccal sulcus preservation is needed

### Palatal Rotational Flap
- Preferred for OAFs > 10 mm or late presentations
- Full-thickness mucoperiosteal flap including the greater palatine artery (essential for vascularity)
- **Advantages**: Abundant keratinized mucosa; best for large defects; preserves buccal vestibule depth
- **Disadvantages**: Stiffness limits rotation; requires subepithelial dissection on rotating region to release tension and prevent arterial twist/necrosis; exposed donor-site bone requires secondary healing (~2 weeks with collagen sponge or palatal splint)
- Circular fistula tract excision often reveals larger defect than apparent at entrance

### Diagnostic Notes
- CBCT confirms OAF tract and extent of sinus infection
- Panoramic + Waters' view: initial screening for sinus opacity
- Valsalva test, cheek-blowing test, probing: useful but small perforations may be missed → caution to avoid fistula widening

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative review only — no systematic literature search; no quantitative outcome data
- Case series not formally reported with n and follow-up statistics
- Does not cover distant flap procedures (tongue flap, auricular cartilage, temporalis muscle flap) in depth
- Does not address bone graft options or synthetic materials for OAF closure

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| OAF (Oroantral Fistula) | Abnormal epithelialized tract connecting oral cavity and maxillary sinus |
| OAC (Oroantral Communication) | Acute (non-epithelialized) connection between oral cavity and maxillary sinus |
| BFP (Buccal Fat Pad) | Lobulated fatty tissue in buccal space; used as pedicled graft for OAF closure |
| ESS (Endoscopic Sinus Surgery) | Functional endoscopic sinus surgery; treatment for chronic maxillary sinusitis |
| Greater Palatine Artery | Terminal branch of descending palatine artery; essential vasculature for palatal rotational flap |
| Caldwell-Luc Operation | Surgical approach to maxillary sinus via anterior wall; used for chronic sinusitis |
