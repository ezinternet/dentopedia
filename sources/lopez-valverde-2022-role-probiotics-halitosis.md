## Why Ingested

구강 유래 구취(halitosis)의 비항균제 치료 대안으로서 프로바이오틱스의 임상적 유효성을 평가한 SR+MA. 기존 [[halitosis/memon-2022-aetiology-associations-halitosis-systematic]]의 원인론·역학 관점을 보완하는 치료 개입 근거로 활용.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (4 RCTs, n=283): probiotics (Streptococcus salivarius K12/M18, Weissella cibaria) showed no statistically significant reduction in oral halitosis measures (p=0.53), though individual trials suggest potential benefit.

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (4 RCT, n=283): 프로바이오틱스(Streptococcus salivarius K12/M18, Weissella cibaria)가 구강 구취를 통계적으로 유의하게 줄이지 못했으나(p=0.53) 일부 개별 연구는 긍정적 효과를 시사.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA specifically evaluating probiotics for genuine oral halitosis
- Fixed-effects meta-analysis of 4 RCTs (283 participants) using Volatile Sulfur Compounds (VSC) and Organoleptic Test (OLT) as primary outcomes
- Identified Streptococcus salivarius K12 and M18 as the most studied probiotic strains in oral malodor
- Highlighted Weissella cibaria as a novel probiotic with potential anti-halitosis properties
- Documented significant heterogeneity in methods across trials and called for standardization

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**PICO question**: Are probiotics effective for the reduction or elimination of oral halitosis?
- P: Subjects suffering from halitosis
- I: Probiotic administration
- C: Subjects not receiving probiotics
- O: Reduction of halitosis (VSC levels, OLT score)

**Inclusion criteria**:
- English-language RCTs on probiotics and oral halitosis
- Minimum 2-week follow-up

**Exclusion criteria**:
- Animal/in vitro studies, non-RCT designs, duplicate or informative studies
- Studies on probiotics for other oral pathologies

**Statistical method**: Fixed-effects model; mean difference (MD) ± SD; 95% CI; RevMan v5.4.1
**Risk of bias**: Cochrane Collaboration tool (independent duplicate assessment)

**Studies included (n=4)**:
1. Jamali et al. — n=99, 12 weeks, SS K12; post-chlorhexidine disinfection
2. Benic et al. — n=64, 12 weeks, SS M18; orthodontic patients (triple-blind, placebo-controlled)
3. He et al. — n=28, 4 weeks, SS K12; SS K12 did not significantly reduce halitosis
4. Lee et al. — n=92, follow-up unspecified, Weissella cibaria; only study to measure "Bad Breath improvement"

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Overall meta-analysis**: p=0.53 — no statistically significant effect of probiotics on oral halitosis
- **Individual trials**:
  - Jamali et al.: SS K12 after chlorhexidine disinfection may reduce halitosis severity over longer periods (12 weeks)
  - Benic et al.: SS M18 reduced halitosis in orthodontic patients
  - He et al.: SS K12 did NOT significantly reduce halitosis
  - Lee et al.: Weissella cibaria showed Bad Breath improvement
- **Primary outcomes used**: VSC levels (Volatile Sulfur Compounds), OLT (Organoleptic Test scores)
- **Total participants**: 283; follow-up range 2–12 weeks
- **Risk of bias**: Mixed across studies; details per Cochrane tool

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 4 eligible RCTs identified from 14 candidate studies — very small evidence base
- High variability in probiotic strains, dosages, administration forms, and follow-up durations
- Heterogeneous outcome measures (VSC, OLT, self-reported BB) limit pooling
- Short follow-up periods (4–12 weeks) insufficient to assess durability of effect
- No blinding consistency across studies
- Authors concluded: "more clinical trials are needed to establish real evidence"

## 6. Related Work

- Halitosis affects 30–50% of the population; main cause is putrefaction of sulfur-containing amino acids (H₂S, methylmercaptan, dimethylsulfide)
- ISBOR classification: genuine halitosis, pseudohalitosis, halitophobia
- Probiotics mechanism: reduce intestinal/oral pH, reduce pathogenic colonization, modulate host immune response
- Streptococcus salivarius (K12, M18) and Bifidobacterium are the most studied oral probiotic species
- Probiotics have been studied for plaque control and microbial homeostasis maintenance

## 7. Glossary

- **VSC (Volatile Sulfur Compounds)**: hydrogen sulfide, methylmercaptan, dimethylsulfide — primary chemical markers of halitosis
- **OLT (Organoleptic Test)**: direct sensory scoring of breath odor by a trained examiner
- **SS K12/M18**: Streptococcus salivarius strains K12 and M18 — lactic acid bacteria used as oral probiotics
- **Weissella cibaria**: a lactic acid bacterium naturally found in the oral cavity; studied for anti-halitosis effect
- **Fixed-effects model**: meta-analytic method assuming a single true effect size across studies; appropriate when studies are homogeneous
- **ISBOR**: International Society for Breath Odor Research
- **PRISMA**: Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses
