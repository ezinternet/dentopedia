## One-line Summary
Multi-center prospective study of 90 implants in maxillary premolar area. Buccal and palatal bone thickness measured at placement (T0) and 3-month uncovering (T1). Showed significant horizontal resorption (mean -0.5 mm buccal, -0.4 mm palatal) but minimal vertical bone loss during submerged healing. Inverse correlation between initial thickness and EMBL.

## 2. Key Contributions
Quantifies the buccal/palatal envelope thickness threshold (~2 mm) required to keep submerged-healing EMBL within negligible range. Adds prospective evidence to the rule of thumb of ≥2 mm buccal bone.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Multi-center prospective. Periodontal probe measurements at T0 (placement) and T1 (3-month uncovering). Kruskal-Wallis + multivariate linear regression on bone changes.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
Mean buccal thickness 2.42 ± 0.64 mm at T0 → 1.92 ± 0.71 mm at T1 (p=0.000). Palatal 1.31 → 0.87 mm. Vertical resorption non-significant (~0.03–0.04 mm). Initial thickness inversely correlated with horizontal loss.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
Submerged healing only (no functional loading); 3-month follow-up; manual probe measurements with 0.5 mm precision.

## 7. Glossary
- **IIP:** Immediate Implant Placement — placement at the time of tooth extraction.
- **IIPP:** Immediate Implant Placement and Provisionalization — IIP combined with same-visit provisional restoration.
- **SRP:** Sagittal Root Position — Kan I/II/III/IV classification of root-to-bone relationship.
- **CBCT:** Cone-Beam Computed Tomography.
- **PES/WES:** Pink/White Esthetic Score — esthetic outcome indices for anterior implants.
