## Why Ingested

기존 occlusion 카테고리는 교합력↔치조골의 임상·생역학적 관찰(occlusal trauma, overload-induced bone loss)에 집중돼 있고, 그 *분자 기전*은 비어 있었다. 본 rat 연구는 교합저하(occlusal hypofunction)가 sclerostin↑ → Wnt/β-catenin 억제 → 골형성↓·골흡수↑로 치조골 소실을 일으킨다는 분자 경로를 제시해, [[occlusion/fan-2018-occlusal-trauma-and-excessive-occlusal]]의 임상 case-definition 논의와 [[bone-biology/kondo-2022-current-perspectives-residual-ridge]]의 잔존 치조제 흡수(RRR) 기전 논의를 분자 수준에서 연결한다. "occlusion → 치조골 밀도/부피" 테마의 mechanism anchor.

## One-line Summary

Rat split-mouth study (n=14 male Sprague-Dawley, unilateral maxillary molar extraction, 8 wk) showing occlusal hypofunction decreases BMD and BV/TV and increases osteoclast number, accompanied by upregulated sclerostin and RANKL and downregulated β-catenin — implicating sclerostin-mediated inhibition of Wnt/β-catenin signaling as the molecular driver of disuse alveolar bone loss.

## 한줄요약

Rat split-mouth 연구(수컷 Sprague-Dawley 14마리, 편측 상악 대구치 발치로 교합저하 유도, 8주) — 교합저하 측에서 골밀도(BMD)·BV/TV 감소, 파골세포 수 증가가 sclerostin·RANKL 상승 및 β-catenin 저하와 동반됨을 보여, sclerostin이 Wnt/β-catenin 신호를 억제해 disuse 치조골 소실을 일으키는 분자 기전임을 시사.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First report of sclerostin expression in an occlusal-hypofunction (occlusal unloading) jaw-bone environment.** Prior unloading–sclerostin work was in long bone (tail suspension); this extends the mechanism to alveolar bone.
2. Establishes a within-animal **split-mouth occlusal hypofunction model**: unilateral maxillary molar extraction (+ ipsilateral upper incisor abrasion) removes the antagonist load on the contralateral-mapped mandibular alveolar bone, with the non-extraction side serving as paired control.
3. Links the disuse bone-loss phenotype (↓BMD, ↓BV/TV, ↑osteoclasts) to a coherent molecular signature: **↑sclerostin, ↑RANKL, ↓β-catenin**, with OPG unchanged.
4. Proposes anti-sclerostin therapy as a potential strategy to protect against occlusal-hypofunction–induced alveolar bone loss in prosthetic rehabilitation contexts.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Animals**: 14 male Sprague-Dawley rats, 10 weeks old; powder diet, free water, 12-12 h light-dark, 23°C. Ethics-approved (West China Hospital of Stomatology).
- **Model**: Left maxillary molars extracted; homolateral upper incisors abraded → occlusal hypofunction (unloading). Anesthesia: 0.5% chloral hydrate IP. Non-extraction side = paired control.
- **Endpoint**: Week 8 post-extraction; mandibles harvested, stored in 0.5% buffered formalin.
- **Assays**:
  - High-resolution X-ray radiography (Faxitron MX-20) → BMD.
  - Micro-CT (µCT 35, Scanco) → BV, TV, BV/TV.
  - Histology: H&E.
  - TRAP staining → osteoclast count (two independent investigators).
  - Immunohistochemistry: sclerostin, β-catenin, OPG, RANKL; quantified as mean optical density (MOD = integrated OD / area) via Image-Pro Plus 6.0.
- **Statistics**: mean ± SD; paired t-test (within-animal comparison); SPSS 17.0; P<0.05 significant.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Bone density / architecture**: BMD markedly decreased and BV/TV significantly decreased at the hypofunction side vs control (P<0.001); radiography, micro-CT and H&E all showed bone loss and architecture deterioration.
- **Osteoclasts**: Significantly increased osteoclast number at the hypofunction side (TRAP, P<0.001).
- **Sclerostin**: Increased protein expression at the hypofunction side.
- **RANKL**: Significantly increased in osteocytes at the hypofunction side (P<0.001).
- **β-catenin**: Decreased at the hypofunction side.
- **OPG**: No significant difference between sides (authors hypothesize stronger OPG staining at hypofunction side reflects a negative-feedback attempt to control RANKL-driven osteoclastogenesis).
- **Interpretation**: occlusal hypofunction (an unloading environment) → ↑sclerostin → antagonism of Wnt/β-catenin (↓β-catenin) → ↓bone formation, plus ↑RANKL-driven osteoclastogenesis → net alveolar bone loss.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample (n=14), single time point (8 wk), single species (rat) — no dose/time-course or sex comparison (males only).
- Associative immunohistochemistry (MOD), not causal: sclerostin "essential" claim is inferred, not tested by sclerostin knockout/antibody intervention in this study.
- Occlusal unloading differs from tail-suspension unloading; mechanical stimulus is distributed across the tooth–PDL–alveolar bone complex, so PDL atrophy and PDL-fibroblast mechanoresponses (not assayed here) may also contribute.
- Future: test anti-sclerostin antibody to prevent occlusal-hypofunction alveolar bone loss; investigate additional signaling pathways.

## 6. Related Work

- Lin et al. 2009 (J Bone Miner Res) — sclerostin mediates long-bone response to tail-suspension unloading by antagonizing Wnt/β-catenin (the long-bone precedent this paper extends to alveolar bone).
- Wijenayaka et al. 2011 (PLoS One) — sclerostin stimulates osteoclast support via a RANKL-dependent pathway.
- Nakashima et al. — osteocytes as a crucial in vivo source of RANKL for osteoclastogenesis.
- Elovic et al. 1994/1995 — maxillary molar extraction decreases mandibular stiffness and increases bone loss in ovariectomized rats (occlusal-unloading model precedent).

## 7. Glossary

- **Occlusal hypofunction**: reduced or absent occlusal (bite) loading on a tooth/alveolar bone region, e.g. after loss of the antagonist tooth — a jaw-bone "disuse"/unloading state.
- **Sclerostin (SOST gene product)**: osteocyte-secreted Wnt antagonist; binds LRP5/6 to inhibit Wnt/β-catenin signaling, suppressing bone formation. Secretion falls under mechanical loading and rises under unloading.
- **Wnt/β-catenin signaling**: canonical osteogenic pathway driving osteoblast differentiation and function; β-catenin is its intracellular effector.
- **RANKL / OPG**: receptor activator of NF-κB ligand (pro-osteoclastogenic) and osteoprotegerin (decoy receptor, anti-osteoclastogenic); their ratio sets osteoclast activity.
- **BV/TV**: bone volume / total volume — micro-CT trabecular bone fraction.
- **BMD**: bone mineral density.
- **TRAP**: tartrate-resistant acid phosphatase — osteoclast marker stain.
- **MOD**: mean optical density — IHC quantification (integrated OD / area).
