## Why Ingested

This split-mouth RCT extends UV photofunctionalization (UVP) evidence into the **controlled-diabetic population** — a compromised-healing group not covered by the general-population stability data in [[implants/surface/hirota-2020-uv-photofunctionalization-dental-implant-7year]] (where pathophysiologically compromised cancer-resection cases failed) and the early-loading case reports in [[implants/isq/seol-2017-uv-implant-resonance-frequency-early-loading]]. Directly relevant to early-implant-failure risk in compromised hosts ([[overviews/early-implant-failure-risk-prevention-overview]]) and to the broader surface-activation synthesis ([[overviews/plasma-surface-treatment-dental-implants]]).

## One-line Summary

Split-mouth RCT in controlled diabetic patients (J Oral Implantol 2025): 20-s pre-placement UV photofunctionalization gave significantly higher 3-month ISQ gain (4.40 vs 2.60, P<.029), higher osseointegration index (OSI 1.42 vs 0.84, P=.032), and less 9-month distal crestal bone loss (0.35 vs 0.64 mm, P=.003) than non-UV implants.

## 한줄요약

조절된 당뇨 환자 대상 split-mouth RCT (J Oral Implantol 2025): 식립 전 20초 자외선 광기능화 (UV Photofunctionalization, UVP) 시 비처리군 대비 3개월 임플란트 안정성 지수 (Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ) 상승폭(4.40 vs 2.60, P<.029)·골유착지수 (Osseointegration Index, OSI 1.42 vs 0.84, P=.032)가 유의하게 높고 9개월 치조정 골소실 (0.35 vs 0.64 mm, P=.003)이 유의하게 적었다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: The Journal of Oral Implantology (2025), e-pub 2025-08-08
- **DOI**: 10.1563/aaid-joi-D-24-00160 ; **PMID**: 40420652
- **Type**: Split-mouth randomized clinical trial (RCT)
- **Population**: Controlled diabetic patients (stratified random sampling for age/gender/subgroups)
- **Groups**: UVP (20-s UV activation pre-placement) vs NUVP (non-UV control), site-randomized split-mouth
- **Outcomes**: ISQ, osseointegration index (OSI) at placement & 3 months; crestal bone change at 3 & 9 months
- **Note**: abstract-only — full text not retrieved

## 2. Key Contributions

- First/among-first RCT to test UV photofunctionalization specifically in **controlled diabetic** patients, a compromised-healing host group
- Split-mouth design controls for patient-level confounders (each patient is own control)
- Demonstrates UVP benefit on three distinct axes simultaneously: secondary stability (ISQ), osseointegration speed (OSI), and crestal bone preservation
- Extends UVP evidence from anatomically/general cases to **metabolically compromised** healing

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Split-mouth RCT in controlled diabetics; sites randomly allocated to UVP vs NUVP
- **Sampling**: Stratified random sampling for proportional representation (age, gender, subgroups)
- **Intervention**: UVP implants treated in a UV activator for **20 seconds** immediately before placement; NUVP placed conventionally
- **Measurements**:
  - ISQ and OSI: immediately after placement and at 3 months
  - Crestal bone change: at 3 and 9 months postplacement
- **Statistics**: Descriptive statistics + paired t-tests (intragroup and intergroup comparisons)
- *(Exact n, implant system, and diabetic control thresholds not available — abstract-only.)*

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | UVP | NUVP | P-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| ISQ change (baseline → 3 mo) | **4.40 ± 1.89** | 2.60 ± 1.17 | **<.029** |
| Osseointegration index (OSI) | **1.42 ± 0.62** | 0.84 ± 0.39 | **.032** |
| Distal crestal bone loss at 9 mo (mm) | **0.35 ± 0.08** | 0.64 ± 0.18 | **.003** |

- UVP group showed significantly **greater secondary stability gain**, **higher OSI**, and **less crestal bone loss** than NUVP in controlled diabetic patients.
- Direction of all three effects favors UVP; effect on crestal bone loss (distal) had the strongest significance (P=.003).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **abstract-only — full text not retrieved**: sample size, implant system, exact diabetic-control criteria (HbA1c), CIs, and ISQ absolute baselines unavailable.
- Crestal bone change reported for the distal aspect only in the abstract; mesial/buccal-lingual not summarized.
- Short follow-up (9 months for bone, 3 months for stability) — no survival/long-term data.
- Single-population (controlled diabetics); generalization to uncontrolled diabetics or other compromised hosts untested.
- ISQ gain magnitude (~4.4) is modest in absolute terms; clinical loading-decision relevance needs the full data.

## 7. Glossary

- **UV photofunctionalization (자외선 광기능화, UVP)**: UV irradiation of titanium just before placement → super-hydrophilic, positively charged surface with reduced surface carbon, restoring osteoconductivity lost by aging.
- **ISQ (임플란트 안정성 지수, Implant Stability Quotient)**: resonance-frequency-analysis stability score (1–100).
- **OSI (골유착지수, Osseointegration Index)**: index of osseointegration progress used in this trial.
- **Crestal bone loss (치조정 골소실)**: marginal bone height change around the implant neck over time.
- **Controlled diabetic (조절된 당뇨)**: diabetic patient with glycemic control within defined limits.
- **Split-mouth design (구강내 분할 설계)**: each patient receives both interventions at different sites, serving as own control.
