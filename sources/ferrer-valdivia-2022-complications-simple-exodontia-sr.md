## One-line Summary
Systematic review of 3 included studies on complications after simple (non-surgical) tooth extraction; trismus, alveolitis, and retained roots were most prevalent, linked to surgeon experience and surgery duration.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Dental and Medical Problems (Dent Med Probl) 2022;59(4):593–601
- **DOI**: 10.17219/dmp/144596
- **Study type**: Systematic review
- **Institution**: University of Antofagasta, Chile; methods adapted from Cochrane Handbook and PRISMA
- **Published online**: December 14, 2022 (received July 2021, accepted December 2021)

## 2. Key Contributions
- One of few systematic reviews focused exclusively on simple (non-surgical) exodontia complications, distinct from third molar (surgical) extractions
- Identifies the most prevalent complications: trismus, alveolitis, retained roots
- Links complication prevalence to modifiable factors: surgeon experience, surgery duration, tissue trauma

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Databases: PubMed, Scopus, ScienceDirect
- Search terms: "Exodontia" AND "Complications"
- Search period: from database start to January 31, 2020
- Inclusion: simple exodontia studies, CPES prevalence studies, human studies
- Exclusion: third molar exodontia complications, narrative reviews, systematic reviews, book chapters, animal studies, generalities
- Initial search: 1,446 articles (725 PubMed, 96 Scopus, 631 ScienceDirect)
- After deduplication: 948 articles; 9 read in full; 3 included in final review

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Most common complications post-simple exodontia (CPES): trismus, alveolitis, pain, dehiscence, infections, retained roots
- **Most prevalent**: trismus of chewing muscles, alveolitis, retained roots
- These complications most likely related to: surgeon's experience, surgery duration, tissue trauma during surgery
- Complications classified as intraoperative (during surgery) or postoperative
- Postoperative complications: infectious (wound infection, abscess, necrotizing fasciitis) and non-infectious (pain, hemorrhage, edema, alveolitis, paresthesia, sinus communication, TMD, trismus, tissue emphysema)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Very small number of qualifying studies (n=3) reflects scarcity of research on simple exodontia complications
- Most complications literature focuses on third molar/surgical extraction
- Heterogeneity in complication definitions and reporting across included studies
- Need for prospective studies specifically targeting simple exodontia

## 6. Related Work
- Bailey et al. 2020 Cochrane review (surgical techniques for mandibular wisdom teeth)
- Extensive literature on third molar extraction complications (excluded from this review)
- CBCT and imaging techniques for pre-extraction assessment

## 7. Glossary
- **Simple exodontia**: Extraction of fully erupted teeth without surgical flap; also called non-surgical exodontia
- **Alveolitis (dry socket)**: Painful postoperative complication due to premature blood clot loss; also called alveolar osteitis
- **CPES**: Complications Post Simple Exodontia
- **Trismus**: Restricted mouth opening due to chewing muscle spasm
- **Dehiscence**: Wound opening/separation after closure
