## Why Ingested

Provides rare 15–17-year RCT follow-up data comparing one-piece (Straumann SLA transmucosal) vs two-piece (Brånemark TiUnite) implants — the longest comparative RCT data currently available for this design question. Extends and contextualizes [[implants/atieh-2025-tissue-vs-bone-level-peri-implantitis-sr-ma]] (1–5y SR+MA showing TL vs BL comparable outcomes) with an ultra-long-term perspective revealing diverging technical complication profiles.

## One-line Summary

RCT 15–17-year follow-up (n=60 patients, 151 implants; Zurich) comparing one-piece (STM, Straumann SLA) vs two-piece (BRA, Brånemark TiUnite) implants: overall survival 95%, one-piece superior marginal bone preservation (0.08 mm vs 1.53 mm MBL) but 6× higher technical complication rate (35.4% vs 5.8% implant-level).

## 한줄요약

RCT 15–17년 추적(60명, 151개 임플란트): 1조각 임플란트(STM) 변연골 소실 (Marginal Bone Loss, MBL) 우수(0.08 mm vs 1.53 mm)하나 기술적 합병증(Technical Complication) 발생률 6배 높음(35.4% vs 5.8%); 전체 생존율 (Implant Survival Rate, ISR) 95%.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Longest published RCT follow-up comparing one-piece vs two-piece implant systems (15–17 years)
- Reveals a trade-off: one-piece (STM) = better marginal bone preservation; two-piece (BRA) = fewer technical complications
- Overall cumulative survival 95% (STM 91.84%; BRA 98.04%)
- One-piece MBL 0.08 mm vs two-piece 1.53 mm at 15–17y — clinically meaningful difference
- Technical complications: STM 35.4% implant-level vs BRA 5.8% implant-level
- Peri-implantitis prevalence 13.25% (STM patient-level 23.33% vs BRA 16.67%)
- Cautions that BRA represents older-generation two-piece design; modern internal-connection/platform-switching systems may differ

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design:** 15–17-year follow-up of a previously conducted RCT
- **Setting:** University of Zürich, Switzerland
- **Population:** 60 adults requiring implant therapy; randomized to one-piece Straumann SLA (STM, n=30, 65 implants) or two-piece Brånemark TiUnite (BRA, n=30, 86 implants)
- **Randomization:** Computer-generated list
- **Implant specs:** Lengths 6–15 mm; diameters 3.3–5 mm
- **Restoration types:** 6 removable (17 implants), 93 fixed restorations; 103 screw-retained, 31 cement-retained
- **Follow-up visits:** Baseline (final restoration delivery), 1y, 5y, 10y, 12y, 15y, 17y
- **Final cohort:** 39 patients, 95 implants (STM: 22 patients/45 implants; BRA: 17 patients/50 implants); mean FU 16.43 years
- **Attrition:** 21 patients (56 implants) lost to follow-up — no significant baseline differences from completers
- **Blinding:** Not feasible (implant type identifiable clinically)
- **Outcomes:**
  - Biological: PPD (probing pocket depth, 치주낭 탐침 깊이), PCR (plaque control record), BOP (출혈 지수)
  - Radiographic: MBL (marginal bone level) via paralleling technique + Image J
  - Technical: all prosthetic complications continuously documented
  - Esthetic: mucosal shimmering, crown margin visibility, peri-implant mucosal margin position
- **Statistics:** Primarily descriptive due to limited final-visit sample size; independent samples t-tests and chi-square for attrition analysis

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Survival

| Group | n implants | Lost | Survival Rate |
|-------|-----------|------|---------------|
| STM (one-piece) | 65 | 4 | 91.84% |
| BRA (two-piece) | 86 | 1 | 98.04% |
| Overall | 151 | 5 | 95.0% |

All implant losses due to peri-implant disease with progressive bone loss.

### Marginal Bone Levels (implant-level, positive = bone loss)

| Timepoint | STM Mean (SD) | BRA Mean (SD) |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|
| Baseline | 0.61 mm (0.82) | 1.07 mm (0.51) |
| 5 years | 0.62 mm (0.77) | 1.39 mm (0.44) |
| 10 years | 0.03 mm (1.08) | 1.38 mm (0.52) |
| 15–17 years | 0.08 mm (1.15) | 1.53 mm (0.81) |

### Biological Parameters (implant-level, 15–17y)

| Parameter | STM | BRA |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| PPD | 3.79 mm (±1.01) | 3.51 mm (±1.06) |
| PCR | 14.34% (±24.82) | 13.18% (±19.77) |
| BOP | 45.63% (±30.81) | 38.33% (±25.37) |

- Peri-implant mucositis (cumulative): STM 70.77% / BRA 33.72% implant-level; resolved in many cases
- Peri-implantitis (cumulative): STM 12/65 implants (18.46%), BRA 8/86 (9.30%); patient-level STM 23.33% vs BRA 16.67%

### Technical Complications

| Metric | STM | BRA |
|--------|-----|-----|
| Implant-level rate | 35.38% (23/65) | 5.81% (5/86) |
| Patient-level rate | 30% (9/30) | 13.33% (4/30) |
| Total events | 34 | 6 |

STM complication breakdown: 11 matrix renewals, 10 screw loosenings, 5 minor chippings, 2 major chippings, 4 screw access hole restorations lost, 1 debonding, 1 ball attachment renewal.
No implant fractures, screw fractures, or framework fractures in either group.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- High attrition: 21/60 patients (35%) lost to follow-up at final visit; may introduce attrition bias despite comparable baseline characteristics
- BRA system is an older-generation two-piece implant (TiUnite surface, external connection); modern internal-connection/platform-switching two-piece implants may behave differently
- Protocol heterogeneity: implant depth, bone augmentation, healing protocol, and restoration type varied — reflects real-world practice but limits internal comparisons
- No blinding possible — potential examiner bias for clinical/esthetic parameters
- Small final-visit sample limits statistical power for rare events (peri-implantitis, specific complication types)
- Single-center university study — generalizability uncertain

## 7. Glossary

- **STM**: One-piece Straumann SLA implant (sandblasted large-grit acid-etched surface, transmucosal design)
- **BRA**: Two-piece Brånemark implant (TiUnite anodized surface, Nobel Biocare)
- **MBL (Marginal Bone Level, 변연골 수준)**: Bone level measured relative to implant reference point; positive values = bone loss
- **PPD (Probing Pocket Depth, 탐침 깊이)**: Clinical measure of peri-implant sulcus/pocket depth
- **BOP (Bleeding on Probing, 탐침 시 출혈)**: Indicator of peri-implant tissue inflammation
- **PCR (Plaque Control Record, 치태 조절 기록)**: Plaque accumulation measure
- **Peri-implant mucositis (치주낭 점막염)**: Reversible inflammatory lesion of peri-implant mucosa
- **Peri-implantitis (임플란트 주위염)**: Inflammatory lesion with progressive bone loss
- **Implant-abutment interface (임플란트-지대주 연결부)**: Junction between implant body and prosthetic abutment in two-piece systems; potential site for microbial colonization and micro-movement
- **Brånemark Rule**: Historical expectation of ~1 mm first-year bone loss and ~0.1–0.2 mm/year thereafter around two-piece implants
