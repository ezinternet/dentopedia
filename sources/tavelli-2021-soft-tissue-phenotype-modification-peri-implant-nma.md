## Why Ingested

기존 [[wiki/implants/thoma-2018-soft-tissue-augmentation-periimplant-health]] (Thoma 2018 SR+MA, COIR)이 연조직 증대술과 임플란트 주위 건강의 관계를 다루었으나, 기술 간 직접 비교(Network MA)는 포함하지 않았다. Tavelli 2021은 최초로 CTG/FGG/ADM/CM/APF 등 5가지 접근법을 NMA로 직접 순위화해 [[wiki/overviews/keratinized-mucosa-peri-implant-health-overview]]의 기술 선택 근거를 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

SR + network meta-analysis (52 studies, 23 RCTs) ranking soft tissue phenotype modification techniques around implants: CTG/ADM bilaminar best for mucosal thickness gain; APF+FGG best for keratinized mucosa width gain; both improve peri-implant health markers.

## 한줄요약

52편(RCT 23편) 포함 SR+네트워크 메타분석 — 점막두께 증가에는 CTG·ADM 이층술이, 각화점막폭 증가에는 APF+FGG가 가장 효과적이며 두 접근 모두 임플란트 주위 건강 지표를 개선한다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First NMA on PSP modification**: Directly compared five categories of soft tissue augmentation techniques via network meta-analysis — a significant methodological advance over previous pair-wise MAs.
2. **Dual-pathway framework**: Separated the evidence base into two mechanistically distinct surgical pathways: (a) bilaminar techniques (primarily for mucosal thickness, MT) and (b) apically positioned flap (APF) techniques (primarily for keratinized mucosa width, KMW).
3. **Technique ranking for MT**: CTG and ADM bilaminar > collagen matrix (CM) bilaminar > non-augmented for mucosal thickness gain.
4. **Technique ranking for KMW**: APF+FGG > APF alone; APF+CTG/CM/ADM did not significantly outperform APF alone for KMW.
5. **Peri-implant health impact**: KMW augmentation via APF significantly reduced probing depth, soft tissue dehiscence, and plaque index regardless of graft material; bilaminar CTG or CM had beneficial effects on marginal bone level (MBL) stability.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Study design**: Systematic review + network meta-analysis (NMA)
- **Search**: Comprehensive electronic + manual search (databases not specified in abstract; typical: MEDLINE, Embase, Cochrane)
- **Inclusion criteria**: Clinical studies reporting soft tissue augmentation around endosseous dental implants with outcomes on KMW, mucosal thickness (MT), and/or supracrestal tissue height (STH); health outcomes also assessed
- **Qualitative analysis**: 52 articles total
- **NMA**: 23 RCTs only; classified into bilaminar (n=16 RCTs) vs APF-based (n=7 RCTs) networks
- **PSP definition**: Peri-implant soft tissue phenotype (PSP) = KMW + MT + supracrestal tissue height (STH)
- **Interventions compared**:
  - Bilaminar network: CTG, CM (xenogeneic collagen matrix), ADM (acellular dermal matrix) vs non-augmented
  - APF network: APF+FGG, APF+CTG, APF+CM, APF+ADM vs APF alone
- **Outcomes**: KMW change, MT change, MBL change, probing depth (PD), soft tissue dehiscence, plaque index

Full NMA ranking tables (SUCRA values, league tables) and forest plots are in the supplementary materials (Tables S1–S8) which are not accessible in the retrieved file. The conclusions above are drawn from the structured abstract.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Bilaminar techniques — Mucosal Thickness (MT)

| Comparison | Finding |
|---|---|
| CTG vs non-augmented | Significant MT gain |
| ADM vs non-augmented | Significant MT gain |
| CM vs non-augmented | Significant MT gain |
| CTG vs CM | CTG superior |
| ADM vs CM | ADM superior |
| CTG vs ADM | No significant difference |

- No significant differences in KMW across different bilaminar techniques
- CTG and CM bilaminar approaches showed beneficial effects on MBL stability

### APF-based techniques — Keratinized Mucosa Width (KMW)

| Comparison | Finding |
|---|---|
| APF+FGG vs APF alone | FGG significantly superior for KMW |
| APF+CTG vs APF alone | No significant difference for KMW |
| APF+CM vs APF alone | No significant difference for KMW |
| APF+ADM vs APF alone | No significant difference for KMW |

- APF with any evaluated soft tissue graft: significant reduction in PD, soft tissue dehiscence, plaque index vs non-augmented
- Evidence on MBL effect of APF-based approaches: inconclusive

### Clinical summary ranking

- **Best for MT gain**: CTG ≈ ADM > CM (bilaminar approach)
- **Best for KMW gain**: APF + FGG
- **Best for MBL protection**: Bilaminar CTG or CM
- **Best for inflammation/hygiene metrics**: APF-based approaches with any graft

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- PDF is an HTML abstract print — full NMA SUCRA rankings, heterogeneity statistics, and forest plots could not be verified from the retrieved file
- Network meta-analyses of soft tissue procedures are limited by heterogeneity in implant type, follow-up period, and PSP measurement methods across included RCTs
- Only 23 RCTs eligible for NMA from 52 included studies, limiting network density
- The bilaminar and APF networks are treated separately — no cross-network comparison possible
- Evidence on APF-based approaches and MBL outcomes was deemed inconclusive, requiring future RCTs with longer follow-up
- STH outcomes were not separately analyzed in the NMA

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| PSP (Peri-implant Soft tissue Phenotype) | Composite of KMW, mucosal thickness (MT), and supracrestal tissue height (STH) |
| KMW (Keratinized Mucosa Width) | Width of keratinized mucosa coronal to the mucogingival junction around implants |
| MT (Mucosal Thickness) | Thickness of the peri-implant mucosa measured horizontally |
| STH (Supracrestal Tissue Height) | Vertical dimension of soft tissue coronal to the alveolar crest (≈ biologic width equivalent) |
| CTG (Connective Tissue Graft) | Subepithelial connective tissue autograft harvested from palate |
| FGG (Free Gingival Graft) | Full-thickness epithelialized gingival autograft; gold standard for KMW increase |
| ADM (Acellular Dermal Matrix) | Processed dermal allograft (e.g., AlloDerm); xenogeneic soft tissue substitute |
| CM (Collagen Matrix) | Xenogeneic 3D collagen scaffold (e.g., Mucograft); used as CTG substitute |
| APF (Apically Positioned Flap) | Flap technique that repositions the keratinized tissue apically, exposing the implant and allowing vestibular deepening |
| Bilaminar technique | Two-layer approach: flap + graft placed beneath (tunnel or envelope), primarily increases MT |
| NMA (Network Meta-Analysis) | Statistical method allowing indirect comparison of multiple treatments via a common comparator |
| MBL (Marginal Bone Level) | Crestal bone level adjacent to implant; change over time = proxy for peri-implant health |
| PD (Probing Depth) | Pocket depth at peri-implant sulcus; elevated PD signals mucositis/peri-implantitis |
