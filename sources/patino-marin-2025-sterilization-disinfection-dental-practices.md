## One-line Summary
Narrative review comprehensively covering sterilization and disinfection methods in dental practices — instrument washing, chemical/heat/radiation sterilization, biological indicator protocols, and sterile storage guidelines.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive taxonomy of sterilization methods (moist heat, dry heat, chemical, radiation, gas) and their applicability to dental materials
- Classification of disinfection levels (low / high / critical) mapped to Spaulding device categories (noncritical / semicritical / critical)
- Detailed biological indicator (BI) protocols: organism, cycle type, frequency per ADA/CDC/WHO/NOM-013 guidelines
- Evidence-based storage duration guidelines by packaging type (pouch ≤6 mo, rigid container ≤12 mo, cloth ≤1 mo) and event-related vs time-related sterility
- Review of advanced decontamination technologies: vaporized H₂O₂ (HPV), far-infrared radiation (FIR), antimicrobial coatings (Ag, Cu, TiO₂), RFID/barcode labeling

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Narrative review drawing on peer-reviewed studies, international guidelines (CDC, WHO, ADA, OSAP, ISO 11607-1), and Mexican standard NOM-013-SSA-2015. No systematic search protocol or PRISMA reported; broad coverage organized by topic. Published in Cureus (open-access, peer-reviewed).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Instrument Washing
- Manual or automated; washer-disinfectors achieve >7-log₁₀ reduction of vegetative and spore-forming bacteria
- Cleaning must precede all disinfection/sterilization

### Disinfection by Level
| Level | Device category | Agents |
|---|---|---|
| Low | Noncritical (intact skin) | QACs, alcohols |
| High | Semicritical (mucous membrane) | Glutaraldehyde 2%, OPA, H₂O₂, peracetic acid, chlorine |
| Sterilization | Critical (sterile tissue) | All sterilization methods; liquid chemical sterilants (3–12 hr) only when heat-incompatible |

### Heat Sterilization
- **Autoclave (moist heat)**: 121–132°C at 12 psi ≥15 min; types N/B/S by air-extraction method; BI: *Geobacillus stearothermophilus* spores
- **Dry heat**: 160°C ≥60 min; lower corrosion risk; BI: *Bacillus atrophaeus*; cannot use for plastics/fabrics/liquids

### Chemical Sterilization
- **Unsaturated chemical vapor**: alcohol + formaldehyde at 270°C, 20–40 min; low corrosion; instruments must be dry pre-cycle
- **Glutaraldehyde 2%**: widely used for impressions and heat-sensitive instruments; effective against HIV and coronaviruses; health-risk if mishandled; BI: *Bacillus subtilis* / *G. stearothermophilus*
- **Hydrogen peroxide vapor (HPV)**: 20-min cycle (3 min application + 17 min chemotoxic); 6-log reduction vs <4-log for aerosolized H₂O₂; no toxic residues; BI: *G. stearothermophilus*

### Radiation
- **UVC (220–290 nm)**: damages DNA, prevents replication; up to 9-log₁₀ reduction on Petri dishes, 3.23–6.25 log on dental tools; limitations: cannot penetrate shadows/cracks, damages some plastics; BI: *Bacillus pumilus*
- **Gamma rays**: high penetration, effective for complex devices/grafts; dose must be controlled; BI: *Bacillus pumilus*; gamma, X-ray, and e-beam equivalent at 1–6 kGy

### Gas Sterilization
- **Ethylene oxide (EtO)**: ~2.5-hr cycle; excellent material compatibility (plastics, electronics); carcinogenic/explosive — high cost, strict environmental controls required; BI: *Bacillus atrophaeus*
- **Ozone**: antimicrobial via oxidation; no toxic residues; toxicity to humans at high concentrations — area must be vacated; BI: *Bacillus subtilis*

### Biological Indicator Protocols
- ADA/OSAP/CDC: weekly verification; WHO: weekly for autoclave and dry heat
- Mexico NOM-013-SSA-2015: bi-monthly (every 2 months) minimum — legally mandatory
- Growth detected → inspect equipment, identify root cause (human error is most common: incorrect temperature/time/preheating, lack of training)

### Sterile Storage
- CDC recommended: 20–25°C, 30–60% relative humidity
- Event-related sterility supersedes time-related sterility (damage/moisture = contamination event)
- Pouches (paper/plastic): ≤6 months; rigid containers with intact seals: ≤12 months; cloth wraps: ≤1 month
- Advanced technologies: FIR in storage areas → 0 CFU vs 68.2% contamination in controls at 30 days; HEPA + UVC for airborne pathogen control; antimicrobial coatings (Ag, Cu, TiO₂) on surfaces/storage furniture

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative review format; no systematic literature search or PRISMA — susceptible to selection bias
- Primarily reflects Mexican regulatory context (NOM-013) alongside international guidelines; frequency requirements vary by country
- Some referenced studies are small (single-clinic audits) or experimental rather than large RCTs
- Future: standardized validation protocols for novel technologies (HPV, FIR, antimicrobial coatings); sustainability research (reuse of sterilization pouches, carbon footprint of packaging)
