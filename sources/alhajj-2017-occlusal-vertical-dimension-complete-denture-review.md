## One-line Summary

Updated narrative review (J Oral Rehabil 2017) classifying OVD-determination methods for complete-denture patients into two streams — pre-extraction (intra-oral measurement, profile tracing, cephalometry) and post-extraction (mandibular rest position, facial esthetics, swallowing, craniofacial-landmark measurement, cephalometry, phonetics, existing dentures) — concluding all methods are empirical and controversial, with no single accurate technique, so combining techniques best approximates OVD.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type:** Updated narrative review article (web-print PDF — abstract + reference list captured; full body behind paywall).
- **Journal:** Journal of Oral Rehabilitation 2017;44(11):896–907.
- **DOI:** 10.1111/joor.12522 (first published 18 May 2017).
- **Scope:** Methods to determine the occlusal vertical dimension (OVD) in complete-denture (edentulous) patients.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Two-stream classification of OVD determination for complete dentures:
  - **(i) Pre-extraction methods** — transfer the natural-dentition OVD to the new dentures via **intra-oral measurements, profile tracing, and cephalometric analysis**.
  - **(ii) Post-extraction methods** — **mandibular rest position, facial (esthetic) appearance, swallowing pattern, craniofacial-landmark measurements, cephalometric analysis, phonetics, and existing dentures**.
- Critically appraises each method's merits and limitations.
- Bottom line: all techniques are **empirical, controversial, and lack scientific support**; **no single accurate method** exists, so the clinician benefits from **combining techniques** to approximate OVD.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Narrative review of the literature on OVD determination, organized by the pre-/post-extraction dichotomy. The reference list (≈70+ entries) spans classic pre-extraction record methods (Smith 1971; Turrell; Bissasu), lingual-frenum guides (Bissasu 1999), anterior/posterior tooth-measurement methods (Fayz/Eslami), Willis bite-gauge facial measurement (McMillan & Imber), cephalometric estimation (Orthlieb; Strajnic; Sheppard & Sheppard 1975), and phonetic/closest-speaking-space methods (Silverman; Pound; Burnett). No meta-analysis.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Each stream and method carries trade-offs; pre-extraction records require foresight and are often unavailable in long-term edentulous patients, while post-extraction physiologic/esthetic methods are subjective.
- Recommendation: a **combination of techniques** is more reliable than any single approach for routine clinical OVD determination in complete dentures.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative review — no pooled estimates or formal risk-of-bias scoring.
- Underlying primary methods are empirical and population-dependent; calls implicitly for more rigorous, validated, reproducible methods.

## 7. Glossary

- **OVD (occlusal vertical dimension):** facial height with teeth/occlusal rims in maximal intercuspation.
- **Pre-extraction methods:** record OVD while natural teeth are still present, then transfer to the denture.
- **Post-extraction methods:** establish OVD after teeth are lost, using rest position, esthetics, swallowing, landmarks, cephalometry, or phonetics.
- **Profile tracing:** a pre-extraction facial-profile record used to reproduce the original vertical relation.
- **Craniofacial-landmark (anthropometric) measurement:** estimating OVD from facial/skeletal distances (e.g. eye–rima oris, finger length).
