## Why Ingested

치주치료(SRP/subgingival instrumentation)가 단순 구강 결과를 넘어 전신 대사 지표(HbA1c)를 임상적으로 의미 있게 개선한다는 최고 등급(Cochrane SR+MA) 근거로, 당뇨 환자의 치주관리 정당화에 핵심. [[periodontics/smiley-2015-nonsurgical-periodontitis-srp-guideline]]의 비외과적 SRP 효과 근거를 전신 건강(혈당 조절) 측면으로 확장하며, [[periodontics/meng-2024-nspt-cardiovascular-risk-markers-sr-ma]]의 NSPT 전신영향 주제와 짝을 이룬다.

## One-line Summary

Cochrane SR+MA (35 RCTs, 3249 randomised participants, mostly type 2 diabetes): periodontal treatment by subgingival instrumentation produced a moderate-certainty absolute HbA1c reduction of 0.43% (4.7 mmol/mol) at 3-4 months vs no treatment/usual care, sustained at 6 and 12 months.

## 한줄요약

Cochrane 체계적 문헌고찰+메타분석(RCT 35편, 무작위배정 3249명, 대부분 제2형 당뇨): 치은연하 기구조작(subgingival instrumentation, SRP)에 의한 치주치료가 무처치/일상관리 대비 3-4개월 시점 당화혈색소(HbA1c)를 0.43%(4.7 mmol/mol) 절대 감소시켰고(중등도 근거), 6·12개월에도 유지됨.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews — Intervention Review (systematic review + meta-analysis), 2022, Issue 4, Art. No. CD004714.pub4.
- **Editorial group**: Cochrane Oral Health.
- **Update history**: Update of a review previously published 2010 and 2015; the 2022 update doubled the number of included studies and participants, changing the conclusion and certainty for the primary outcome.
- **Search currency**: Evidence current to 7 September 2021 (six databases searched).
- **Scope**: This is "Part one" — periodontal treatment versus no active treatment/usual care. (Part two will compare different types of periodontal treatment.)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides the highest-tier (Cochrane SR+MA) evidence that treating periodontitis improves glycaemic control in people with diabetes.
- 2022 update upgraded certainty: now **moderate-certainty** evidence of a **clinically significant** improvement in glycaemic control vs no treatment/usual care.
- Concludes that further RCTs of periodontal treatment vs no treatment/usual care are unlikely to change the overall conclusion.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Systematic review with meta-analysis of parallel-group RCTs only.
- **Population**: People with diabetes mellitus (predominantly type 2; one study included type 1 or type 2) and periodontitis; baseline metabolic control mixed (good/fair/poor); mostly secondary-care settings.
- **Intervention**: Periodontal treatment by subgingival instrumentation (scaling and root planing / "deep cleaning"), sometimes supplemented by oral hygiene instruction, antimicrobials, or other adjuncts.
- **Comparator**: No active treatment or "usual care" (oral hygiene instruction/support and/or supragingival plaque removal).
- **Inclusion**: RCTs lasting ≥3 months that measured HbA1c.
- **Primary outcome**: Blood glucose as glycated haemoglobin (HbA1c), reported as % of total haemoglobin or mmol/mol.
- **Secondary outcomes**: Adverse effects, periodontal indices (BOP, CAL, gingival index, plaque index, PPD), quality of life, cost, diabetic complications.
- **Process**: ≥2 review authors independently screened, selected, extracted data, and assessed risk of bias (Cochrane RoB); sensitivity analysis restricted to low-risk-of-bias studies.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Included**: 35 studies, 3249 randomised participants; all parallel RCTs; follow-up 3–12 months.
- **Primary outcome (HbA1c, treatment vs no treatment/usual care):**
  - **3–4 months**: absolute reduction **0.43%** (4.7 mmol/mol); 95% CI −0.59% to −0.28% (−6.4 to −3.0 mmol/mol); 30 studies, 2443 analysed participants; **moderate-certainty**.
  - **6 months**: absolute reduction **0.30%** (3.3 mmol/mol); 95% CI −0.52% to −0.08% (−5.7 to −0.9 mmol/mol); 12 studies, 1457 participants.
  - **12 months**: absolute reduction **0.50%** (5.4 mmol/mol); 95% CI −0.55% to −0.45% (−6.0 to −4.9 mmol/mol); 1 study, 264 participants.
- **Risk of bias**: 2 studies low risk, 14 high risk, 19 unclear; low-risk-of-bias sensitivity analysis supported the main findings.
- **Adverse effects**: Where measured, generally no or only mild harms; serious adverse events similar between arms — but most studies did not evaluate harms.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Most included studies were at high or unclear risk of bias.
- Adverse-effect data were sparse, precluding a robust evaluation of harm.
- 12-month estimate rests on a single study (264 participants).
- Authors judge that further no-treatment/usual-care comparison trials are unlikely to change the conclusion; future work shifts to comparing different periodontal treatment types (Part two).

## 6. Related Work

- Builds on the 2010 and 2015 versions of this Cochrane review.
- Complements guideline-level SRP efficacy syntheses (e.g., AAP non-surgical periodontitis guideline) by adding a systemic (glycaemic) endpoint.
- Aligns with broader NSPT systemic-effect literature (e.g., cardiovascular risk-marker meta-analyses).

## 7. Glossary

- **HbA1c**: Glycated (glycosylated) haemoglobin; reflects average blood glucose over the preceding ~3 months; reported as % of total haemoglobin or mmol/mol.
- **Subgingival instrumentation**: Scaling and root planing ("deep cleaning") below the gumline.
- **PPD / CAL / BOP**: Probing pocket depth / clinical attachment level / bleeding on probing — periodontal indices.
- **Usual care**: Oral hygiene instruction/support and/or supragingival plaque removal used as the control condition.
- **Moderate-certainty evidence**: GRADE rating indicating moderate confidence the true effect is close to the estimate.
