## One-line Summary
Retrospective cohort (35 patients, 36 sinuses with preexisting sinus floor defects confirmed by CBCT, treated 2008–2020 by lateral sinus floor elevation using a split-thickness palatal-crestal-incision flap and resorbable collagen membrane to seal the defect before graft placement, then implants placed staged at 8 months in 81% or simultaneous in 19%); first-attempt completion 97.2%, Schneiderian membrane perforation 27.8%, cumulative implant survival 96.5% at 1/3/5/7 years and 64.3% at 8 years — sinus floor defects do not compromise lateral SFE outcomes when properly managed.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Clinical Oral Implants Research 2023;34(10):1141–1150
- **Pages**: 1141–1150
- **Institution**: Department of Implantology, Peking University School and Hospital of Stomatology, China
- **Study type**: Retrospective single-center cohort (1- to 9-year follow-up)

## 2. Key Contributions
- First retrospective series specifically examining **lateral SFE in patients with pre-existing sinus floor defects** (often from prior surgery, oroantral fistula, or pathology).
- Defines a reproducible **technique**: split-thickness palatal crestal-incision flap + resorbable collagen membrane sealing the defect from crestal side **before** bone substitute placement.
- Reports **first-attempt completion** = 97.2% even in this complex anatomy.
- Long follow-up (up to 9 years) — robust survival data at 1/3/5/7 years; sharp drop at year 8 suggests caution about late biologic complications.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- 2008–2020 inclusion at Peking University.
- 35 patients (36 sinuses) with CBCT-confirmed sinus floor defects.
- Surgical technique:
  - **Split-thickness palatal crestal incision** to handle tissue adhesion in defect area.
  - Lateral window osteotomy → identify defect.
  - **Resorbable collagen membrane** placed from crestal side to seal defect *before* placing bone substitute.
  - Bone graft inserted.
  - Implant timing: 47/58 implants (81.0%) placed at 8-month staged second-stage; 11/58 (19.0%) simultaneous.
- Outcomes:
  - Surgical completion in first attempt.
  - Schneiderian membrane perforation (SMP) — repaired or aborted.
  - Cumulative implant survival rate (CSR) at 1/3/5/7/8 years.
  - Marginal bone loss (MBL).
- Statistics: Kaplan-Meier survival.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **First-attempt completion**: 35/36 sinuses (97.2%).
- One case suspended due to complicated SMP — successful re-entry LSFE 4 months later.
- **SMP incidence**: 10/36 (27.8%) — within reported range.
- 9 of 10 SMP repaired intraoperatively; 1 aborted.
- **Cumulative implant survival rate (CSR)**:
  - 1-year: 96.5%
  - 3-year: 96.5%
  - 5-year: 96.5%
  - 7-year: 96.5%
  - **8-year: 64.3%** (sharp drop — small at-risk sample at this timepoint).
- MBL data reported but not in extracted abstract.
- Conclusion: sinus floor defects do not compromise LSFE outcomes when proper management is used.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Retrospective single-center; selection bias (operator experience, patient selection).
- "Sinus floor defect" is heterogeneous — does not stratify defect size/etiology.
- 8-year CSR drop from 96.5% to 64.3% may reflect small denominator rather than true survival; need larger long-term cohort.
- No randomized comparison with conventional LSFE without preexisting defect.
- No histological assessment of graft maturation in defect repair area.

## 7. Glossary
- **LSFE (Lateral Sinus Floor Elevation)**: trapdoor osteotomy through lateral antral wall, SM elevation, bone graft placement; gold-standard for severely atrophic posterior maxilla.
- **Sinus floor defect**: preexisting discontinuity of the bony sinus floor (e.g., from prior surgery, oroantral communication, pathology) complicating LSFE.
- **SMP (Schneiderian Membrane Perforation)**: intraoperative tear of the sinus mucoperiosteum.
- **Split-thickness flap**: surgical flap leaving periosteum on bone — used to manage adherent tissue over defects.
- **CSR (Cumulative Survival Rate)**: Kaplan-Meier survival of implants over time.
- **MBL (Marginal Bone Loss)**: radiographic measure of crestal bone change post-implant loading.
