## One-line Summary
Systematic review + meta-analysis + trial sequential analysis (7 studies) on alveolar ridge preservation (ARP) using allogenic/xenogenic graft covered by a resorbable membrane vs spontaneous healing: combination therapy reduced horizontal ridge resorption by 2.19 mm (95% CI −2.67 to −1.71) and vertical resorption by 1.72 mm (95% CI −2.14 to −1.30); TSA confirmed the evidence base is sufficient to validate these effects despite high heterogeneity.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Periodontology 2018;89(1):46–57.
- **Type**: Systematic review, meta-analysis, and trial sequential analysis (TSA).
- **Affiliation**: Department of Clinical and Experimental Medicine, University of Foggia, Italy (and co-authors).
- **Reporting**: PRISMA framework; random-effects inverse-variance pooling.

## 2. Key Contributions
- One of the first ARP meta-analyses to apply **trial sequential analysis (TSA)** — adjusting for type I/II errors and assessing whether the cumulative evidence base is adequately powered.
- Provides pooled estimates for both horizontal ridge width reduction (HRWR) and vertical ridge height reduction (VRHR) for the specific clinically relevant combination (graft + resorbable membrane vs spontaneous healing).
- Confirms statistical and informational sufficiency of the existing literature despite heterogeneity — clinicians do not need to await further RCTs to act on this evidence.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Electronic database screening (independent dual reviewers); inclusion: comparative studies of allogenic/xenogenic bone graft + resorbable membrane vs spontaneous healing after extraction.
- Primary outcomes: **HRWR** (mm) and **VRHR** (mm); secondary outcome: volumetric change (VC).
- Statistical synthesis: random-effects model, inverse-variance weighting.
- Additional analyses: heterogeneity (I²), trial sequential analysis with monitoring boundaries to evaluate power and adjust for repeated testing.
- 7 studies met inclusion criteria for quantitative synthesis.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **HRWR (combination vs control)**: −2.19 mm (95% CI −2.67 to −1.71 mm).
- **VRHR**: −1.72 mm (95% CI −2.14 to −1.30 mm).
- **VC**: insufficient data for meta-analysis.
- **TSA**: information size and study count sufficient — evidence is conclusive despite high heterogeneity.
- Heterogeneity: high across included studies (different grafts, membranes, follow-up).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- High heterogeneity in graft material (allograft vs xenograft), membrane type (collagen vs others), tooth site, and follow-up.
- Volumetric data sparse — needs standardized CBCT-based ARP outcome reporting.
- Cannot rank specific graft × membrane combinations.
- Patient-reported and functional outcomes (need for additional grafting at implant placement) not synthesized.

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary
- **ARP (Alveolar Ridge Preservation)**: grafting and/or membrane procedures at extraction to limit post-extraction ridge resorption.
- **HRWR (Horizontal Ridge Width Reduction)**: change in bucco-lingual ridge width after extraction.
- **VRHR (Vertical Ridge Height Reduction)**: change in vertical ridge height after extraction.
- **TSA (Trial Sequential Analysis)**: meta-analysis method adjusting for repeated significance testing and assessing whether the required information size has been reached.
- **GBR (Guided Bone Regeneration)**: barrier-membrane based bone regeneration principle.
