## Why Ingested

Maps the histomorphometric methodology landscape (models, stains, parameters) behind the bone-substitute efficacy claims synthesized in [[overviews/dbbm-bone-substitute-overview]] — a methods reference for interpreting the new-bone/residual-graft numbers reported across DBBM studies. Highlights the gap between proliferating biomaterials and standardized assessment.

## One-line Summary
Systematic review (118 studies, 2015–2024) of histomorphometric methodology in bone regeneration research using non-decalcified plastic-embedded specimens: rat most common animal model; toluidine blue most common stain; new bone formation, residual graft, MAR (mineral apposition rate) with calcein green dominate parameters.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Materials 2025, 18, 119 (received Nov 2024, published 30 Dec 2024).
- **Study type**: Systematic review (PCC framework — Population, Concept, Context); methodology-focused.
- **Setting**: Medical University of Varna (Bulgaria). 10-year scope (2015–2024).

## 2. Key Contributions
- First SR mapping the methodological landscape of histomorphometric bone-regeneration research over the past decade.
- Identifies dominant study models, staining choices, and parameter sets — useful for protocol design and study comparison.
- Highlights the gap between proliferating biomaterials and standardized assessment protocols.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- PCC framework: P = animal/human models for bone regeneration with bone substitutes; C = histomorphometric parameters; C = articles 2015–2024.
- Inclusion: English research articles, non-decalcified plastic-embedded specimens, reported histomorphometric parameters.
- Exclusion: reviews, case reports/series, no bone substitutes, semi-quantitative only, decalcified-only, SEM-only.
- Two independent reviewers (V.R., R.Y.) for screening; full-text retrieval and data extraction.
- Final: 118 included studies (98 animal + ~20 human, per table 1 vs human tables).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Animal models (frequency)**: Rat > Rabbit > Sheep > Dog > Mini-pig > Goat > Mouse.
- **Bone substitute materials**: DBBM (Bio-Oss) most studied xenograft; β-TCP, HA (Maxresorb, Ostim), allografts, autografts also common.
- **Staining methods**: Toluidine blue dominant; Goldner's trichrome, methylene blue/basic fuchsin, H&E for decalcified subgroup.
- **Bone-related parameters**: ~50% of all recorded parameters. New bone formation (NB%) is the single most reported.
- **Other parameters**: Residual graft (RG%), non-mineralized tissue (NMT%), particle osseointegration (OI%), bone-to-implant contact (BIC%).
- **Dynamic histomorphometry**: Mineral apposition rate (MAR) dominant; calcein green most-used fluorochrome label.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Methodology review — does not pool clinical outcomes or compare materials.
- Plastic-embedded non-decalcified scope excludes paraffin/decalcified studies; full literature is broader.
- No quality assessment of included studies; descriptive synthesis only.
- Recommends standardization of histomorphometric reporting (parameter definitions, ROI, sectioning) but no formal consensus framework offered.

## 6. Related Work
- Manfro 2013 — DBBM comparative histomorphometry (decalcified, not in this SR's scope).
- Mordenfeld 2010 — DPBB particle measurements in non-decalcified human biopsies at 11 yr.
- Sartori 2003 — Histomorphometric trajectory of Bio-Oss to 10 yr (case report).
- ARRIVE / PRISMA — Reporting guidelines applicable to histomorphometric SRs.

## 7. Glossary
- **Non-decalcified plastic embedding**: Sectioning bone without prior acid demineralization, preserving the mineralized phase — required for accurate residual graft quantification and BIC measurement on metallic implants.
- **Toluidine blue**: Metachromatic dye; stains osteoid pink and mineralized bone blue; standard for non-decalcified histology.
- **MAR (Mineral Apposition Rate)**: Distance between two fluorochrome labels divided by interval (µm/day); measures bone formation rate.
- **Calcein green**: Fluorescent tetracycline-class chelator binding newly mineralizing bone front.
- **BIC (Bone-to-Implant Contact)**: % of implant surface in direct contact with bone — primary integration metric.
