## Why Ingested

This 2020 dog study is the direct sequel to [[wiki/occlusion/podaropoulos-2016-bone-reactions-progressive-static-load-dogs]], which showed progressive loading alone increases BIC; this paper adds an overloading phase to test whether progressive loading conditions bone to better resist damage. Ingested to complete the Podaropoulos progressive-loading series and to ground [[wiki/overviews/implant-occlusion-loading-biomechanics-overview]] with animal-level mechanobiological evidence on overloading thresholds.

## One-line Summary

Animal RCT (n=5 beagle dogs, 40 implants): progressive orthodontic pre-loading before overloading significantly raised implant success rate (87.5% vs 67.5%) but did not further increase bone-to-implant contact compared with overloading alone.

## 한줄요약

동물실험(비글 5마리, 임플란트 40개): 점진적 교정력 전처치 후 과부하 적용 시 임플란트 성공률이 87.5%로 과부하 단독군(67.5%)보다 유의하게 높았으나, 골-임플란트 접촉률(BIC)은 두 군 간 유의한 차이 없음.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Demonstrates that prior progressive static loading raises implant survival under subsequent overloading (87.5% PL+OL vs 67.5% OL-only), providing the first controlled animal evidence that mechanically conditioning peri-implant bone improves overload resilience.
2. Shows that overloading forces — whether or not preceded by progressive loading — produce significantly higher BIC than no loading (P=.006 for OL vs UL; P<.001 for PL+OL vs UL), contradicting concern that functional load always harms osseointegration.
3. Establishes that both loaded groups exhibited significantly less crestal bone resorption than unloaded controls, suggesting functional stimulus is protective against marginal bone loss in the beagle model.
4. Finds no additional BIC benefit of progressive pre-loading over overloading alone (P=.225), indicating bone-contact adaptation may already reach a ceiling under direct overloading.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Animal model**: Five beagle dogs; bilateral edentulous ridges created surgically in the posterior maxilla; 8-week healing before implant placement; 40 implants total.

**Three experimental groups**:
- **UL (Unloaded control, n=8 implants)**: inserted, left uncovered/unloaded for 32 weeks.
- **OL (Overloading, n=16 implants)**: osseointegrated 16 weeks; metal crowns with supraocclusal contacts (antagonist interference); dynamic overloading for 16 weeks.
- **PL+OL (Progressive loading + overloading, n=16 implants)**: osseointegrated 8 weeks; custom abutment cores paired with Ni-Ti orthodontic springs; ascending static forces 100 g (3 weeks) → 200 g (3 weeks) → 300 g (3 weeks) = 9-week progressive loading phase; then metal crowns with supraocclusal contacts for 16-week overloading as in OL group.

**Outcome measures**: Bone-to-implant contact (BIC%); bone density at 1 mm and 2 mm from threads; crestal bone resorption; implant success rate.

**Analysis**: Histologic sections, histomorphometric measurement, and statistical testing (P values reported; significance threshold P<.05).

| Outcome | UL | OL | PL+OL |
|---|---|---|---|
| BIC (vs UL) | Reference | Higher (P=.006) | Higher (P<.001) |
| BIC (OL vs PL+OL) | — | No difference (P=.225) | — |
| Bone density 1 mm | No significant difference between groups | | |
| Bone density 2 mm | No significant difference between groups | | |
| Crestal bone resorption | Reference (highest) | Lower (P=.006) | Lower (P=.004) |
| Implant success rate | 87.5% | 67.5% | 87.5% |

**Key finding**: Progressive pre-loading rescued success rate (87.5% PL+OL = UL) without further improving BIC over OL alone; overloading alone depressed success rate to 67.5%.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Abstract-only data**: Full histomorphometric tables, individual BIC values, and detailed statistical model are not accessible from the available PDF (publisher abstract page).
- **Small sample**: n=5 beagle dogs; limited statistical power for subgroup effects.
- **Animal-to-human extrapolation**: Beagle dog maxillary bone may not replicate human bone density distribution or masticatory force magnitudes.
- **Force magnitude ambiguity**: "Supraocclusal contacts" in the OL phase are not quantified numerically; exact overloading force is not reported in the abstract.
- **Single implant system**: Implant type (Lazzara is a co-author; likely BioHorizons or related) not specified in abstract; generalizability unclear.
- **Future**: Clinical RCT in humans to test whether a gradual loading protocol prior to full occlusal loading reduces implant failure in bruxers or heavy-loading patients.

## 6. Related Work

- **Podaropoulos 2016** (`podaropoulos-2016-bone-reactions-progressive-static-load-dogs`): Same first author, same beagle model — examined progressive loading without the subsequent overloading phase; the 2020 paper is the direct extension.
- **Romanos 2003** (`romanos-2003-bone-implant-interface-loading-conditions-monkey`): Primate study showing bone-implant interface histomorphometry under different loading conditions — provides comparative primate evidence.

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Progressive static load (PSL) | Stepwise increasing orthodontic force applied via Ni-Ti springs to condition bone before full occlusal loading |
| Overloading (OL) | Supraocclusal contact crowns creating forces exceeding physiologic occlusal range |
| BIC (Bone-to-implant contact) | Percentage of implant surface in direct contact with mineralized bone on histologic sections |
| Crestal bone resorption | Marginal bone loss measured from the implant shoulder to the first bone-to-implant contact on histology |
| Ni-Ti orthodontic spring | Nickel-titanium spring used to deliver controlled, measurable static forces |
| Osseointegration | Direct structural and functional connection between living bone and the implant surface |
| Beagle dog model | Standard animal model for implant loading studies; posterior maxilla edentulous ridge created surgically |
