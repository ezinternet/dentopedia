## Why Ingested

Existing pages on drilling speed and thermal injury (e.g., [[wiki/implants/gehrke-2021-healing-chambers-macrogeometry-low-density-drilling]]) focus on macrogeometry or low-density bone in general, but no page directly examines how combined multifactorial surgical instrumentation variables (RPM × irrigation presence) interact specifically for narrow- vs. wide-diameter short plateau-root-form implants across two healing periods. This Witek/Coelho group sheep study fills that gap and provides in-vivo BIC/BAFO data that complements [[wiki/implants/di-stefano-2018-undersizing-tapping-bic-primary-stability-bovine-histomorphometric]] (undersizing/tapping effects on BIC) and [[wiki/implants/slete-2018-histomorphometric-comparison-three-osteotomy-techniques]] (osteotomy technique comparison in vivo).

## One-line Summary

Sheep in-vivo study (n=12 sheep, 144 implants, 3.5 mm narrow vs. 6.0 mm wide plateau-root-form Ti-6Al-4V) shows irrigation is most beneficial for BIC at low drilling speed (50 RPM) for narrow implants at 6 weeks, while wide implants benefit from irrigation at 500 and 1,000 RPM; BAFO differs only between 3- and 6-week healing periods regardless of instrumentation variables.

- **Publication date**: February 2021
- **DOI/PII**: S0278-2391(20)31210-6
- **Implant system**: Plateau-root-form healing-chamber titanium implants (Ti-6Al-4V), Bicon LLC, Boston, MA
- **Animal model**: 12 skeletally mature female sheep
- **Study type**: In-vivo animal (sheep), histomorphometric

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Multifactorial drilling protocol analysis**: First systematic evaluation combining three drilling speeds (50, 500, 1,000 RPM) × irrigation (present vs. absent) × implant diameter (3.5 mm narrow vs. 6.0 mm wide) × healing time (3 vs. 6 weeks) in the same in-vivo ovine model.
2. **Diameter-specific irrigation effect on BIC**: Irrigation matters most at low speed (50 RPM) for narrow implants; for wide implants, irrigation significance emerges at intermediate and high speeds (500, 1,000 RPM) at 6 weeks.
3. **BAFO driven by healing time, not instrumentation**: Bone area fraction occupancy within healing chambers did not differ by drilling speed or irrigation at either diameter — only healing duration (3 vs. 6 weeks) drove BAFO differences, suggesting the healing-chamber macrogeometry governs bone infill independently of osteotomy thermal variables.
4. **Clinical safety confirmation**: No inflammatory response, infection, or implant failure observed at either time point, validating both narrow (3.5 mm) and wide (6.0 mm) short plateau-root-form implants as safe across all instrumentation protocols tested.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

### Animal model
- 12 skeletally mature female sheep
- 144 plateau-root-form healing-chamber Ti-6Al-4V implants total
- Equal distribution: 72 narrow (3.5 mm diameter) + 72 wide (6.0 mm diameter)

### Instrumentation variables (multifactorial design)
- **Drilling speeds**: 50 RPM, 500 RPM, 1,000 RPM
- **Irrigation**: present vs. absent
- **Healing periods**: 3 weeks vs. 6 weeks

### Outcome measures
- **BIC** (Bone-to-Implant Contact): histomorphometric linear measure of direct bone apposition on implant surface
- **BAFO** (Bone Area Fraction Occupancy): area percentage of newly formed bone within healing chambers / peri-implant region

### Implant type
- Plateau-root-form design with healing chambers (Bicon LLC)
- Diameters: 3.5 mm (narrow) and 6.0 mm (wide)
- Material: Ti-6Al-4V titanium alloy

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### BIC — Narrow 3.5-mm implant (6-week healing)
- **50 RPM with irrigation**: 30.6 ± 6.1%
- **50 RPM without irrigation**: 19.7 ± 6.1% — statistically significant difference (p value not reported in abstract)
- **500 RPM and 1,000 RPM**: no statistical difference between irrigation and no-irrigation groups

### BIC — Wide 6.0-mm implant (6-week healing)
- **500 RPM and 1,000 RPM**: irrigation groups showed higher BIC vs. no-irrigation; statistically significant
- **50 RPM**: not highlighted as significant in abstract

### BAFO — Both diameters
- Only healing period (3 weeks vs. 6 weeks) produced statistically significant differences
- No significant effect of drilling speed or irrigation on BAFO at either diameter

### General safety
- No inflammation, infection, or implant failure at 3 or 6 weeks in any group

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

1. **Abstract-only access**: The PDF is an HTML abstract print from joms.org. Full methodological details, statistical tables, histological images, and exact p-values are unavailable from the extracted text. Clinical conclusions should be verified against the full article.
2. **Animal model (sheep)**: Ovine cortical/cancellous bone differs from human jawbone in density, vascularity, and trabecular architecture; direct extrapolation to clinical outcomes requires caution.
3. **Short healing periods**: Only 3 and 6 weeks examined; longer healing (12+ weeks) may equalize or shift the observed instrumentation-variable effects.
4. **Plateau-root-form specificity**: Results apply to Bicon healing-chamber design; effects on standard threaded implants may differ because thread design modulates heat generation and bone condensation independently.
5. **No ISQ / insertion torque data**: Primary stability metrics absent; BIC/BAFO only reflect post-osseointegration histological outcomes, not initial stability.
6. **Single implant system / manufacturer**: Generalizability to other implant macrogeometries is untested.

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| BIC (Bone-to-Implant Contact) | Linear percentage of implant surface in direct contact with mineralized bone; primary histomorphometric osseointegration metric |
| BAFO (Bone Area Fraction Occupancy) | Percentage of bone area within a defined peri-implant region or healing chamber; reflects volumetric bone infill |
| Plateau-root-form implant | Implant macrogeometry featuring flat plateau surfaces and integral healing chambers (Bicon LLC design) to retain blood clot and promote bone ingrowth |
| Healing chamber | Recessed chamber in implant macrogeometry designed to be filled with bone during osseointegration |
| Ti-6Al-4V | Grade 5 titanium alloy (6% aluminum, 4% vanadium); higher strength than commercially pure Ti |
| RPM (Rotations per minute) | Drilling speed parameter; lower RPM generates less heat but may reduce cutting efficiency |
| Irrigation | Saline coolant delivered during osteotomy preparation to dissipate frictional heat |
| Narrow-diameter implant | Implant with ≤3.5 mm diameter; used in limited-width ridges |
| Wide-diameter implant | Implant with ≥6.0 mm diameter; used in larger extraction sockets or high-load sites |
