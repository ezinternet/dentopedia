## Why Ingested

Provides pilot SEM evidence on optimal HF etching time for ultrathin feldspathic veneers by fabrication method — directly relevant to the surface-treatment / cementation step discussed in El-Mowafy 2018 and to bonding durability considerations for veneers in [[veneers/klein-2025-ceramic-laminate-veneer-survival-complications-sr-ma]]; no prior source in the wiki addresses etching-protocol differences by fabrication technique for ultrathin (<0.5 mm) specimens.

## One-line Summary

Pilot in-vitro SEM study (Int J Periodontics Restorative Dent 2024; DOI 10.11607/prd.6574) evaluating the effect of HF acid etching time (0, 90, 120, 150 s; 9.6% HF) on intaglio surface microroughness of ultrathin feldspathic veneers made by platinum foil vs. refractory die techniques; platinum foil required 120 s for optimal roughness vs. 90 s for refractory die.

## 한줄요약

파일럿 SEM 연구(2024): 9.6% HF로 초박형 장석 비니어의 내면 에칭 시간을 비교한 결과, 백금박 기법은 120초, 내화 다이 기법은 90초에서 최적 미세거칠기 패턴을 보였으며, 150초는 추가적인 이점이 없었음.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First study to directly compare the effect of HF etching duration on intaglio surface microroughness of ultrathin feldspathic veneers differentiated by fabrication technique (platinum foil vs. refractory die).
- Established that optimal etching time is fabrication-technique dependent: platinum foil requires longer etching (120 s) to achieve comparable microroughness to refractory die at 90 s.
- Confirmed that 150 s etching provides no additional roughness benefit over the respective optimal protocol — avoiding over-etching guidance.
- Described under-etching (0 s control) intaglio as structurally smooth, supporting the necessity of HF treatment.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Specimens**: Replicas of an ultrathin feldspathic ceramic veneer for a maxillary central incisor.
- **Fabrication groups**: Platinum foil technique vs. refractory die technique.
- **Etching protocols**: 0 s (no etch), 90 s, 120 s, 150 s — 9.6% HF acid for all etching groups.
- **Evaluation**: Scanning electron microscopy (SEM) of intaglio surfaces after each etching protocol.
- **Outcome measure**: Qualitative and descriptive assessment of microroughness surface pattern by SEM.
- **Design**: Pilot study — descriptive/observational, not powered for statistical inference.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Etching Time | Platinum Foil | Refractory Die |
|---|---|---|
| 0 s (no etch) | Smooth — inadequate for bonding | Smooth — inadequate for bonding |
| 90 s | Suboptimal roughness pattern | **Optimal roughness pattern** |
| 120 s | **Optimal roughness pattern** | Comparable to 90 s |
| 150 s | No additional benefit | No additional benefit |

- Platinum foil intaglio surfaces required 30 additional seconds of HF etching (120 s vs. 90 s) to achieve comparable microroughness to refractory die surfaces.
- This difference is attributed to the different surface characteristics created by the respective fabrication methods.
- The 9.6% HF concentration was held constant; only etching duration varied.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Pilot study: small specimen number, descriptive SEM only — no quantitative roughness measurements (Ra, Sa) or bond strength testing.
- No clinical bonding outcomes; SEM pattern ≠ proven bond strength difference.
- 9.6% HF is higher than commonly used concentrations (4.9–9%); results may not directly extrapolate to all commercially available HF products.
- PDF is a publisher landing page; full data figures, specimen counts, and SEM images not available from this PDF.
- Future work should include profilometry, bond strength testing, and evaluation of additional concentrations.

## 7. Glossary

- **Intaglio surface**: The inner (fitting) surface of a veneer that bonds to the tooth.
- **HF (hydrofluoric acid) etching**: Chemical treatment of ceramic intaglio surface to create microroughness for mechanical bonding.
- **Microroughness**: Microscale surface irregularity that increases bonding surface area and promotes resin tag formation.
- **SEM (scanning electron microscopy)**: High-resolution imaging technique used to visualize surface topography at the micro/nano scale.
- **Platinum foil technique**: Veneer fabricated over a thin platinum matrix; known to produce a different intaglio surface texture vs. refractory die.
- **Refractory die technique**: Veneer fired directly on a refractory stone die; intaglio surface exhibits different porosity/texture.
- **Ultrathin veneer**: Veneer with thickness typically ≤0.3 mm, allowing minimal or no-preparation protocols.
