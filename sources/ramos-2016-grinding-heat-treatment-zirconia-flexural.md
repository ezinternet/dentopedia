## One-line Summary
Braz Oral Res 2016;30:e12: Y-TZP grinding × grit × heat treatment 1200°C — grinding increases monoclinic phase + characteristic strength; heat treatment reverses monoclinic transformation; finer grit = smoother surface; heat treatment recommended after heavy grinding.

## 1. Key Results
- 7 groups: control + grinding at 200μm(G80), 160μm(G120), 25μm(G600) × untreated or heat-treated 1200°C 2h
- No correlation between Ra/Rz roughness and flexural strength
- Grinding: increases monoclinic (m) phase; increases characteristic strength regardless of grit
- Heat treatment: reverses monoclinic transformation; m-phase returns to control level
- G80A (coarsest + heat treated): similar strength to control; other heat-treated groups: higher strength
- Finer grit (G600): smoother surface — use for final adjustment
