## Why Ingested

"Oral prophylaxis" 요청의 두 번째 코어 논문. 21st European Workshop on Periodontology(EFP) Working Group 1 SR로, [[periodontics/lamont-2018-routine-scale-and-polish-periodontal-health]](저위험 성인 루틴 프로필락시스 무효)과 대비되는 **established gingivitis 치료 맥락**에서 PMPR의 역할을 규명한다 — OHI가 1차, PMPR은 OHI에 대한 adjunct일 때만 이득. 또한 [[periodontics/cyris-2024-guided-biofilm-therapy-versus-conventional]]·[[overviews/professional-biofilm-management-gbt-air-polishing-overview]]의 air-polishing vs rubber-cup 논쟁에 SR+MA 근거(동등 효능·짧은 시간)를 더한다. ## One-line Summary

EFP 21st Workshop systematic review + meta-analysis (11 studies; mostly RCTs) showing that, for dental biofilm-induced gingivitis, OHI is first-line and professional mechanical plaque removal (PMPR) adds benefit only as an adjunct to OHI (low certainty), with air polishing + ultrasonics as effective as but faster than ultrasonic + rubber-cup polishing (very low certainty) and diode laser offering no adjunctive benefit.

## 한줄요약

치태-유발 치은염에서 구강위생교육(OHI)이 1차 치료이고 전문가 기계적 치태제거(PMPR)는 OHI에 더해질 때만 이득(low certainty)을 주며, 에어폴리싱+초음파가 초음파+러버컵 폴리싱과 동등하면서 더 빠르고, 다이오드 레이저는 부가 이득이 없다는 EFP SR+MA(11편).

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR to comprehensively evaluate **PMPR modalities and adjuncts specifically for naturally occurring biofilm-induced gingivitis** (2018 classification), in adults free of plaque-retentive factors and systemic modifiers.
- Structured around 3 focused questions (FQ): FQ1 = PMPR vs no-treatment/OHI; FQ2 = PMPR delivery modality; FQ3 = professionally administered local adjuncts.
- Establishes an **OHI-first hierarchy**: PMPR alone has no efficacy when patients continue ineffective self-care; benefit emerges only as adjunct to OHI, and mainly in generalised gingivitis.
- Modality: air polishing + ultrasonics ≈ ultrasonic + rubber-cup polishing in efficacy, but **shorter treatment time** and greater patient preference; glycine air polishing adds superior stain removal.
- Adjuncts (diode laser, topical oils, etc.) show no robust clinical benefit; diode laser explicitly not recommended.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: SR + pairwise meta-analysis; common-effect model when <5 studies; Cochrane ROB 2.0 (RCTs) / ROBINS-I (CTs); GRADE certainty.
- **Search**: MEDLINE, Scopus, Embase (inception–2 May 2025), Cochrane Oral Health register, grey literature (ProQuest); no language restriction.
- **Population**: adults with naturally occurring dental biofilm-induced gingivitis; gingival inflammation measured at **patient level within 2–6 weeks** post-treatment (long-term >6 weeks reported separately).
- **Included**: 11 studies — FQ1: 4 (3 RCTs + 1 CT); FQ2: 5 RCTs; FQ3: 2 RCTs.
- **Primary outcome**: gingival inflammation scores (BOP, GBS, GI, PBI); endpoint of interest = conversion to health (full-mouth BOP <10%).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **FQ1 — PMPR vs no treatment**: no significant effect size on gingival indices (GBS, PBI) at 2–6 weeks in patients continuing ineffective oral hygiene; significant plaque-score reduction only. At 3 months, bleeding relapsed toward baseline (PMPR group) or above baseline (no-treatment group). → PMPR alone insufficient.
- **FQ1 — PMPR + OHI vs OHI alone**: meta-analysis **favours PMPR + OHI** for gingival inflammation (**low certainty**), driven by generalised-gingivitis cases (baseline BOP ~33–63%); plaque-score trend toward benefit but 95% CI crossed no-effect by 0.01. Heterogeneous (one study each favouring PMPR+OHI, OHI, and no difference).
- **FQ2 — modality**: US + rubber-cup polishing vs air polishing + US (US for calculus) → **no significant difference** in gingival inflammation or plaque (very low certainty). Air polishing + US significantly **shorter treatment time** (e.g., 24.9 vs 34.1 min; 18.4 vs 20.3 min) and preferred by patients; glycine air polishing superior for stain removal.
- **FQ2 — diode laser** (810 nm) adjunct to US: **no adjunctive benefit**.
- **FQ3 — local adjuncts** (topical coconut/sesame oil microneedling, etc.): isolated positive signals but heterogeneous, not meta-analysable; limited clinical applicability and uncertain cost-effectiveness.
- **Certainty**: FQ1 low; FQ2 very low; most included RCTs rated "some concerns" on ROB 2.0.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Few studies per question; substantial heterogeneity; most trials "some concerns" risk of bias → certainty low/very low.
- Short primary follow-up window (2–6 weeks); long-term gingival-health maintenance under-reported.
- FQ3 adjuncts too heterogeneous for pooled analysis or GRADE.
- Calls for well-powered RCTs using 2018-classification case definitions, full-mouth BOP conversion as primary outcome, proper randomisation/allocation concealment and examiner blinding.

## 6. Related Work

- Builds on Suvan et al. and Figuero et al. (self-performed mechanical/chemical plaque control for gingivitis) and Chapple et al. (gingivitis as primary-prevention target for periodontitis).
- Complements the Cochrane routine scale-and-polish review (Lamont 2018) by shifting focus from low-risk prophylaxis to treatment of established gingivitis.
- Extends air-polishing / guided-biofilm-therapy evidence base.

## 7. Glossary

- **PMPR**: professional mechanical plaque removal (prophylaxis / periodontal instrumentation).
- **OHI**: oral hygiene instruction (self-performed brushing + interdental cleaning ± chemical agents).
- **BOP / GBS / GI / PBI**: bleeding-on-probing / gingival bleeding score / gingival index / papillary bleeding index.
- **FQ**: focused question. **US**: ultrasonic scaling. **Air polishing**: powder-jet (e.g., glycine, sodium bicarbonate) biofilm/stain removal.
- **Gingivitis health endpoint**: full-mouth BOP < 10%.
