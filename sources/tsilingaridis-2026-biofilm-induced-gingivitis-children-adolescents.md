## Why Ingested

New 2026 JCP systematic review (EFP/EAPD-commissioned, 269 included studies) characterizing the occurrence, determinants, management and OHRQoL of plaque/biofilm-induced gingivitis in systemically-healthy children & adolescents (< 18 yr) — it defines the **pediatric baseline** for the 2017 World Workshop gingivitis case definition. Extends [[periodontics/caton-2018-classification-scheme-periodontal-periimplant-diseases]] (the 2017 WW framework this review applies) into the under-18 population, and notes that none of the 269 studies actually used the new WW gingivitis criteria — a measurement gap.

## One-line Summary

EFP/EAPD-commissioned systematic review (269 studies, 47 countries; 22 large low-bias studies pooled) — dental biofilm-induced gingivitis affects ~52% of children/adolescents (95% CI 42–62%, I²>99%; 34% CPI to 80% bleeding-index), rises with age/puberty, is socioeconomically patterned, and is best controlled by supervised fluoride toothbrushing + school/caregiver education (flossing adds nothing; CHX is an adjunct only).

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First EFP/EAPD-commissioned synthesis** of biofilm-induced gingivitis across the entire under-18 population, framed by the 2017 World Workshop classification.
2. **Pooled occurrence estimate of 52%** (95% CI 42–62%) from 22 large, low-risk-of-bias studies, with explicit decomposition of how diagnostic method drives the estimate (CPI 34% → bleeding index 80%).
3. **Documents a measurement gap**: across all 269 included studies, none used the 2017 World Workshop gingivitis case definition (bleeding-on-probing threshold), so the pediatric baseline for that definition is still unmeasured.
4. **Maps determinants** (socioeconomic, behavioural, pubertal/biological, malocclusion) and **management evidence** (supervised toothbrushing as cornerstone; flossing ineffective; CHX adjunctive only).
5. Finds **no eligible studies** on non-biofilm gingival diseases, on gingivitis *diagnosis*, or on tobacco exposure in this age group — explicit evidence voids.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases**: PubMed Central, Scopus, EMBASE, LILACS, to January 2025; MeSH + free-text; PRISMA + ENTREQ; OSF-registered.
- **Population**: < 18 years, no known systemic disorder.
- **Screening funnel**: 33,180 records → 20,228 after de-duplication → 2264 (title) → 433 (abstract/full text) → **269 included** (all biofilm-induced gingivitis).
- **Split by outcome**: 99 occurrence, 63 determinants, 89 management, 18 OHRQoL; 0 diagnosis.
- **Occurrence meta-analysis**: random-effects proportion model (Stata 19, `meta`/`esize proportion`); studies < 1000 participants and high-risk-of-bias excluded (64 and 38 respectively) → 22 studies pooled.
- **Risk of bias**: RoB 2.0 (RCTs), ROBINS-I (non-randomised), JBI checklists (observational); GRADE for overall strength; post hoc exclusion of high-RoB studies.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Pooled gingivitis occurrence: 52% (95% CI 42–62%)**, heterogeneity I² > 99%.
- **By method**: 34% (CPI-based) up to 80% (bleeding index) — method, not biology, dominates spread.
- **Largest occurrence study**: n = 118,601, 12–15-yr Chinese schoolchildren, 47% prevalence (WHO criteria) (Wang et al.).
- **Largest OHRQoL study**: n = 89,582 (Wu et al., China, 12–15 yr) — teeth with gingival bleeding significantly associated with worse C-OIDP scores.
- **Age**: occurrence rises with age through childhood/early adolescence (≥ 9 cross-sectional studies concordant); attributed to pubertal sulcular microflora shifts + elevated sex steroid hormones amplifying host inflammatory response.
- **Gender**: boys > girls in most studies.
- **Socioeconomic**: lower parental education, public-school attendance, immigrant/minority status positively associated.
- **Management — what works**: supervised toothbrushing + fluoridated toothpaste twice daily (cornerstone); school-/caregiver-based oral health education reduces inflammation in already-affected children; 0.1–0.2% CHX and herbal mouthwashes reduce GI (adjunct only).
- **Management — what doesn't add value**: supervised flossing added to brushing gave no improvement vs brushing alone (Rich et al. RCT); electric vs manual toothbrush inconclusive (methodological limits); professional cleaning effective but logistically unrealistic at this prevalence.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Extreme heterogeneity (I² > 99%)** in occurrence — cross-study prevalence comparison is not meaningful; pooled 52% is illustrative of method-driven variation rather than a precise figure.
- **Determinants are 61/63 cross-sectional** — associations only, no temporality/causation.
- **2017 WW gingivitis criteria never applied** in any included study — pediatric baseline for the current case definition remains unmeasured.
- **Evidence voids**: no studies on gingivitis diagnosis, non-biofilm gingival diseases in healthy children, or tobacco exposure in under-18s.
- **OHRQoL associations inconsistent** and concentrated in younger (12-yr) children.

## 7. Glossary

- **Biofilm-induced gingivitis**: reversible gingival inflammation from dental plaque, without periodontal attachment loss.
- **GI (Löe and Silness Gingival Index)** / **CPI (Community Periodontal Index)** / **BOP (bleeding on probing)** / **GBI (gingival bleeding index)**: gingival inflammation measures; threshold/method choice drives prevalence estimates.
- **C-OIDP (Child Oral Impacts on Daily Performance)** / **CPQ11–14 (Child Perceptions Questionnaire)**: OHRQoL instruments for children.
- **2017 World Workshop**: AAP/EFP classification of periodontal and peri-implant diseases (current gingivitis case definition).
- **EFP / EAPD**: European Federation of Periodontology / European Academy of Paediatric Dentistry.
