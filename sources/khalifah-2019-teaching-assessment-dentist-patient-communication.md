## One-line Summary
SR of 50 studies (2000–2016) — identifies 26 dentist-patient communication skills in 4 categories (generic, case-specific, time-specific, emerging), and synthesizes teaching/assessment methodologies; field uses a mix of passive and active strategies, all assessment measures deemed valid and reliable, but study designs are weak.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Identifies **26 specific communication skills** relevant to dentistry
- Groups skills into **4 categories**: generic, case-specific, time-specific, emerging
- Reviews teaching methodologies (passive vs. active)
- Reviews assessment methodologies (all deemed valid/reliable)
- Highlights the need for stronger research designs in dental communication education

## 3. Methodology
- Databases: MEDLINE, EBM-Cochrane, Embase, ERIC, BIOSIS, CAB Abstracts, PsycINFO via OVID
- Plus manual search of relevant journals
- Inclusion: studies on teaching and assessment of dental communication skills
- Period: 2000–2016
- Thematic analysis, categorization, quality review
- Narrative synthesis

## 4. Key Results
- **50 articles** included
- Most studies = comparative design
- Largest sub-types: single-group pre/post designs, cross-sectional studies
- **26 communication skills** identified across the literature
- Skills grouped into **4 categories**:
  1. **Generic skills** — applicable across all clinical scenarios
  2. **Case-specific skills** — tied to particular clinical presentations
  3. **Time-specific skills** — tied to encounter phase (opening, mid, closing)
  4. **Emerging skills** — newer in the dental communication literature (e.g., shared decision-making, motivational interviewing techniques, technology-mediated communication)
- Teaching methods: combination of passive and active strategies
- Assessment measures: all considered valid and reliable
- Identified need: more robust study designs

## 5. Limitations
- Most included studies have weak designs (single-group pre/post, cross-sectional)
- No comparative effectiveness data on teaching methods
- Heterogeneity of communication skill definitions
- Search ended 2016 — newer evidence not captured
- No outcome data on patient-level effects (only learner-level)

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary
- **Generic skills**: communication skills applicable across all clinical scenarios (e.g., active listening, empathy)
- **Case-specific skills**: skills tied to particular clinical presentations (e.g., breaking bad news in oncology dentistry)
- **Time-specific skills**: skills tied to encounter phase (greeting, history-taking, closing)
- **Emerging skills**: newer in the literature (shared decision-making, MI, technology-mediated communication)
- **OSCE**: Objective Structured Clinical Examination — typical assessment format
- **Active vs. passive teaching**: experiential/practice-based (active) vs. lecture-based (passive)
