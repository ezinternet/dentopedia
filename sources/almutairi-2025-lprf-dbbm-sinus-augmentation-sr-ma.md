## Why Ingested

SR+MA of RCTs specifically on L-PRF + DBBM combination in sinus augmentation — highest evidence level for this adjunct approach. Resolves the inconsistency seen in earlier systematic reviews (Idiri 2023 found no significant effect) by restricting to RCTs and using meta-analysis with pooled MDs. [[sinus-lift/lateral/sinus-lift-lateral-2026-synthesis]]

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 5 RCTs (n=140 patients): L-PRF+DBBM significantly increases new bone formation (MD +7.07%, 95% CI 2.20–11.93, p<0.001, I²=37%) and reduces residual graft (MD −7.93%, 95% CI −11.20 to −4.66, p<0.001, I²=0%) vs DBBM alone in lateral maxillary sinus augmentation; ISQ and soft tissue NS.

## 한줄요약

5편 RCT SR+MA(140명): L-PRF+DBBM은 DBBM 단독 대비 신생골 형성 유의 증가(MD +7.07%, p<0.001)와 잔류이식재 유의 감소(MD −7.93%, p<0.001); ISQ 및 연조직 비율은 유의차 없음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Cureus
- **Volume/Issue**: 17(6): e85306 (June 2025)
- **Year**: 2025
- **DOI**: 10.7759/cureus.85306
- **Study design**: Systematic review and meta-analysis (PRISMA guidelines; Cochrane Handbook)
- **Search databases**: PubMed, Web of Science, Scopus, Cochrane Central (inception through May 2025)
- **Included studies**: 5 RCTs
- **Total patients**: 140 (76 L-PRF+DBBM; 64 DBBM alone)
- **Statistical method**: Der-Simonian-Laird random-effects model; pooled MD with 95% CI; STATA MP v18

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA restricted to RCTs specifically comparing L-PRF+DBBM vs DBBM alone in lateral window sinus augmentation with histomorphometric outcomes
- Demonstrates statistically significant and clinically meaningful increase in new bone formation (MD +7.07%) and decrease in residual graft (MD −7.93%) at pooled level
- Low heterogeneity for residual graft outcome (I²=0%, p=0.91) — highly consistent finding
- Moderate heterogeneity for new bone (I²=37%, p=0.07) — acceptable for clinical population
- Leave-one-out sensitivity analysis confirms robustness — no single study drives the effect
- ISQ at implant loading not significantly different (MD −2.25, 95% CI −9.76 to +5.27, p=0.56) — functional osseointegration equivalent despite better histomorphometry

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Eligibility**:
- P: Patients undergoing lateral maxillary sinus augmentation
- I: L-PRF + DBBM
- C: DBBM alone
- O: New bone formation %, residual bone graft %, soft tissue %, ISQ at loading
- S: RCTs only; English; 2000 onward

**Exclusions**: Non-L-PRF adjuncts, non-DBBM grafts, observational designs, conference abstracts, unpublished data

**Included studies**:
1. Pichotano et al., 2019: n=12; 8-month follow-up; Dohan L-PRF technique
2. de Almeida Malzoni et al., 2023: n=36; 8-month follow-up; Dohan L-PRF; two L-PRF:DBBM mixing ratios (DBBM4, DBBM8)
3. Nizam et al., 2018: n=22; 6-month follow-up; Dohan L-PRF
4. Zhang et al., 2012: n=10; 6-month follow-up; Choukroun L-PRF
5. Tatullo et al., 2012: n=60; 6-month follow-up; Choukroun L-PRF

**Baseline**: Mean age 50.42 ± 7.3 years; 62% female; residual bone height <5 mm (3 studies) or <4 mm (2 studies)

**Risk of bias**: Cochrane ROB-2 tool; 4 studies "some concerns" (randomization process, outcome measurement); 1 study low risk

**Statistics**: Random-effects model (Der-Simonian-Laird); heterogeneity by Cochrane Q test and I²; leave-one-out sensitivity; STATA 18MP

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Primary outcomes — histomorphometry**:

| Outcome | MD | 95% CI | p | I² | Heterogeneity p |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| New bone formation (%) | +7.07 | 2.20 to 11.93 | <0.001 | 36.86% | 0.07 |
| Residual graft (%) | −7.93 | −11.20 to −4.66 | <0.001 | 0.00% | 0.91 |

**Secondary outcomes**:

| Outcome | MD | 95% CI | p | I² |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ISQ at implant loading | −2.25 | −9.76 to 5.27 | 0.56 | 48.67% |
| Soft tissue (%) | −3.67 | −10.94 to 3.60 | 0.32 | 89.21% |

- New bone: significant positive effect; moderate, acceptable heterogeneity
- Residual graft: significant reduction; zero heterogeneity — highly consistent across all 5 RCTs
- ISQ: no significant difference — osseointegration quality unaffected by L-PRF adjunction
- Soft tissue: no significant difference; very high heterogeneity (89%) — likely reflects different L-PRF preparation protocols affecting fibrin matrix residue

**Sensitivity analysis**: Leave-one-out for new bone formation: no single study disproportionately drives the effect — result is robust

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- All 5 included RCTs are small (n=10–60); total 140 patients
- Heterogeneity in L-PRF protocols: Dohan vs Choukroun centrifugation; different DBBM mixing ratios (DBBM4 vs DBBM8); variable follow-up (6–8 months)
- 4 of 5 studies rated "some concerns" on ROB-2 (randomization, blinding)
- Short follow-up (6–8 months) — long-term implant survival not assessable
- ISQ and soft tissue secondary outcomes limited by high heterogeneity and small k (studies)
- Authors call for standardized L-PRF protocols in future high-quality RCTs

## 6. Related Work

- L-PRF biological mechanism: dense fibrin matrix scaffold; slow sustained release of VEGF, PDGF, TGF-β1 over 10–14 days; three-dimensional fibrin network from slow polymerization during centrifugation
- Choukroun et al.: L-PRF+DBBM reduced healing duration without compromising bone neoformation (4-month healing)
- Zhang et al. (included): 1.4× increase in new bone, 0.5× reduction in residual graft with L-PRF
- Contrasting result: Nizam et al. (included): no significant difference at 6 months — protocol variability implicated
- Idiri et al. 2023 SR (no meta-analysis; heterogeneous PRF types): found no significant effect in sinus lift — explains the discrepancy with the present RCT-restricted MA

## 7. Glossary

- **L-PRF**: Leukocyte-platelet-rich fibrin — second-generation platelet concentrate; dense fibrin matrix enriched with platelets, leukocytes, VEGF, TGF-β1, PDGF
- **DBBM**: Deproteinized bovine bone mineral (Bio-Oss); slow-resorbing osteoconductive xenograft
- **MD**: Mean difference — primary effect measure for continuous outcomes in random-effects meta-analysis
- **I²**: Measure of statistical heterogeneity; 0–25% low, 25–50% moderate, ≥50% high
- **ROB-2**: Cochrane Risk of Bias 2 tool for RCT quality assessment
- **ISQ**: Implant Stability Quotient — RFA-derived 1–100 scale for osseointegration assessment
- **DBBM4/DBBM8**: Mixing ratios used in Malzoni et al. (4 or 8 L-PRF membranes per DBBM volume)
