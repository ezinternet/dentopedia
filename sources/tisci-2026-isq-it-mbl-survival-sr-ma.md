## One-line Summary
SR+MA (48 studies): ISQ correlates moderately with insertion torque (pooled r=0.44, p<0.001) but shows high heterogeneity; current evidence does not support ISQ as an independent predictor of MBL or implant survival.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Clinical Implant Dentistry and Related Research 2026;28:e70156
- DOI: 10.1111/cid.70156
- Design: Systematic review + meta-analysis (PRISMA)
- Databases: MEDLINE, Scopus, Web of Science
- PIO: Systemically healthy patients undergoing implant placement — are ISQ values associated with IT, MBL, implant survival/success?
- 48 studies included; 20 eligible for ISQ-IT quantitative analysis
- Risk of bias: design-specific methodological tools

## 2. Key Contributions
- ISQ-IT pooled correlation: r=0.44 (moderate, significant) — supports ISQ as complementary primary stability indicator
- Surviving implants showed higher baseline ISQ (mean diff=10.22) but pooled estimate NOT statistically significant
- Quantitative synthesis for MBL and success not feasible due to reporting inconsistency
- High I²>90% across ISQ-IT network — limits clinical interpretability
- Conclusion: ISQ is useful but cannot be used alone to predict MBL or implant survival

## 3. Methodology
PRISMA-compliant SR+MA. Searched MEDLINE, Scopus, Web of Science. 48 studies included; 20 for ISQ-IT meta-analysis; survival/MBL analyses descriptive only. Pooled Pearson r calculated. Heterogeneity: I².

## 5. Limitations
- High heterogeneity (I²>90%) — pooled r clinically uncertain
- ISQ-MBL and ISQ-success: insufficient standardized reporting across studies
- Mostly cross-sectional ISQ snapshots; longitudinal ISQ trajectory not well represented
