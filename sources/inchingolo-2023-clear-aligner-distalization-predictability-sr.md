## One-line Summary

SR (19 studies): molar distalization is achievable with clear aligners alone, but anterior anchorage loss is highly probable; predictability and stability improve markedly with skeletal anchorage (TADs) and IPR — a hybrid approach gives the best success, especially with large overjet.

## 한줄요약

SR(19편): 투명교정 단독으로 대구치 distalization은 가능하나 전치부 앵커리지 손실이 매우 흔함. TAD(골성 앵커리지)·IPR 병용 시 예측성·안정성이 크게 향상 — 특히 큰 overjet에서 hybrid 접근이 최선.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Frames distalization predictability as an anchorage problem, not just an appliance property.
- Identifies TADs + IPR as the predictability multipliers; flags large-overjet patients as high-risk for midcourse corrections.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Scopus/WoS/PubMed, last 10 years; 146 studies -> 19 included. Distalization movement, anchorage management, auxiliary use synthesized qualitatively.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Distalization possible with aligners alone but anterior anchorage loss probable.
- Skeletal anchorage (TADs, direct/indirect) + IPR stabilize results and reduce unwanted effects.
- Hybrid (aligner + TAD) shows greatest orthodontic success.
- Large-overjet molar distalization needs corrective measures/auxiliaries to avoid midcourse corrections.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Heterogeneous designs; no meta-analysis; evolving technique.

## 7. Glossary

- **Distalization**: posterior (distal) movement of teeth, typically molars, to relieve crowding/Class II.
- **TAD**: temporary anchorage device (miniscrew).
- **IPR**: interproximal (enamel) reduction.
