## Why Ingested

기존 [[tmj/singh-2024-occlusal-interventions-tmd-cochrane-sr]]와 [[tmj/zhang-2021-exercise-vs-splint-tmd-sr-ma]]는 splint·exercise 같은 능동 치료에 초점을 두지만, 환자교육·자기관리 (Education and Self-Management, ED/SM)가 이들 대비 어느 위치에 서는지는 정량 합성이 없었다. 본 SR+MA (Ferland 2026, JOR)는 ED/SM을 비수술 치료와 직접 비교한 첫 메타분석으로, TMD 1차 보존치료 의사결정 트리에서 "교육만으로 충분한가"를 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

First SR+MA (47 RCTs, n=3238) comparing patient education + self-management (ED/SM) to other non-surgical treatments for adult TMD: other interventions may beat ED/SM-alone on short-term pain (SMD 0.67) and HRQoL (SMD 0.61), but no consistent superiority/inferiority overall — all very low to low certainty (no RCT at low risk of bias).

## 한줄요약

성인 측두하악장애 (Temporomandibular Disorders, TMD)에서 환자교육·자기관리 (ED/SM)를 다른 비수술 치료와 직접 비교한 첫 SR+MA (47 RCT, n=3238). 단기 통증·삶의질은 다른 치료가 ED/SM 단독보다 나을 수 있으나(SMD 0.67·0.61) 전반적으로 우열은 일관되지 않으며 모든 근거가 very low~low (저위험 RCT 0편).

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA to systematically appraise education + self-management (ED/SM) effectiveness *against* other non-surgical TMD treatments (splints, manual therapy, electrotherapy, multimodal, supervised/unsupervised exercise).
- Maps ED/SM intervention content against the Durham (Delphi consensus) and van der Meer conceptual frameworks, exposing how poorly defined/standardized most ED/SM "control" arms are.
- GRADE-rated certainty across pain, function, HRQoL at short/medium/long term — finds it uniformly very low to low.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 6 databases (MEDLINE, PubMed, Embase, Cochrane Central, CINAHL, PsycINFO) to March 2025; PRISMA; Covidence Cochrane RCT Classifier pre-screen; dual-reviewer.
- Inclusion: adults with RDC/TMD or DC/TMD diagnosis; ED/SM (education, jaw/joint guidance, posture, lifestyle/psychosocial, unsupervised exercise) per Durham/van der Meer, alone or combined; outcome on pain/function/HRQoL; RCT in English or French. Excluded: dysphagia/tinnitus/migraine; psychologist-delivered CBT or interventions needing advanced training.
- RoB 1 (7 domains). Random-effects MA in RevMan 5.4; SMD (negative = favours ED/SM). Time strata: short 1–6 wk, medium 7–26 wk, long 27–52 wk. SMD interpretation: <0.2 trivial, 0.2–0.49 small, 0.5–0.79 moderate, ≥0.8 large. GRADE certainty.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Search/yield**: 17,859 records → 47 RCTs included; n=3238; 76.8% female; mean age 34.4±7.3 y; sample sizes 19–186. 62% university clinics. 19 trials myofascial-pain-only, 22 mixed TMD, 5 disc displacement, 15 persistent pain (≥3 mo).
- **Providers/delivery**: dentists/hygienists (n=21) or physiotherapists (n=12); 91% brief one-on-one sessions, 57% leaflets; only 6% (n=3) individualized; most SM = unsupervised exercise (Carlsson & Magnusson / Rocabado 6×6) often without rationale.
- **ED/SM vs other interventions (15 RCTs; 8 pooled)**:
  - Short-term pain: other interventions superior — SMD 0.79 (95% CI 0.27–1.32, I²=78%, 7 studies, 359 pts); GRADE summary SMD 0.67 (0.13–1.20, 6 RCTs, 323 pts), very low.
  - Medium-term pain: SMD 0.43 (−0.25–1.10), NS. Medium-term function: SMD 0.36 (−0.46–1.18), NS.
  - Short-term HRQoL: other interventions superior — SMD 0.61 (0.20–1.01, I²=0%, 2 studies, 124 pts).
- **ED/SM added to another tx vs that tx alone (2 RCTs)**: short-term pain SMD −0.44 (−2.11–1.23, I²=89%, 61 pts), NS.
- **ED/SM + another intervention vs ED/SM alone (34 high-RoB RCTs; 19 pooled)**: NS for all outcomes/follow-ups EXCEPT short-term HRQoL (combination superior, SMD 0.50, 0.18–0.82, I²=0%, 3 studies, 154 pts). Short-term pain SMD 0.25 (−0.06–0.56, 15 RCTs, 816 pts) NS; long-term pain SMD −0.10 (−0.44–0.25, 3 RCTs) NS; short-term function SMD 0.41 (−0.03–0.85) NS.
- **Risk of bias**: 0/47 low; 3 unclear; 44 high. GRADE certainty very low for most outcomes, low for long-term pain & long-term function.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- No subgroup analysis by diagnosis or delivery method possible (heterogeneity); most trials myofascial → limited generalizability to disc-displacement/arthrogenic TMD.
- English/French language restriction. RoB 1 (less granular than RoB-2).
- ED/SM arms were mostly generic, non-individualized "control" conditions weakly grounded in best-practice frameworks — likely biased findings against demonstrating ED/SM benefit. Calls for high-quality, theory-informed, patient-centered trials with medium-/long-term follow-up.

## 6. Related Work

- Durham et al. (Delphi consensus) & van der Meer et al. (scoping review) — ED/SM conceptual frameworks used for coding.
- Prior SRs concluding ED/SM may reduce pain/disability (low-moderate quality) — this MA is first quantitative head-to-head vs other non-surgical care.

## 7. Glossary

- **ED/SM**: Education and Self-Management — patient education + self-guided behavioural/exercise strategies.
- **TMD**: Temporomandibular disorders. **HRQoL**: Health-related quality of life.
- **SMD**: Standardized mean difference (negative favours ED/SM here).
- **RoB 1**: Cochrane Risk of Bias tool version 1. **GRADE**: certainty-of-evidence framework.
- **RDC/DC-TMD**: Research/Diagnostic Criteria for TMD.
