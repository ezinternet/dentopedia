## Why Ingested

This is the landmark in-vitro paper that establishes the chemical-bonding basis of 10-MDP — self-assembled "nanolayering" of MDP-calcium salts on tooth substrate, following the adhesion-decalcification (AD) concept. It resolves the wiki's standing [[10-MDP]] concept gap by grounding *why* MDP-based mild self-etch and universal adhesives bond chemically (not just micromechanically) to enamel and dentin. It pairs directly with the sibling page [[wiki/resin-bonding/nagaoka-2017-mdp-zirconia-chemical-interaction-nmr]], which demonstrates the analogous MDP chemical interaction on zirconia — together they anchor the MDP chemical-bonding mechanism across both tooth-substrate (HAp) and ceramic (ZrO₂) targets.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro XRD + TEM study (experimental 10-MDP primer vs Clearfil SE Bond primer) showing self-assembled MDP-Ca nanolayering occurs on enamel and dentin under a clinical protocol — significantly greater on dentin than enamel, proportional to 10-MDP concentration, and enhanced by active rubbing.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Demonstrates MDP nanolayering occurs on natural tooth substrate (enamel and dentin), not just synthetic HAp** — extending the previously HAp-only "adhesion-decalcification" evidence to a clinically relevant context.
2. **Establishes the self-assembled MDP-calcium salt nanolayer as the chemical-bonding mechanism** underlying mild self-etch (and by extension MDP-universal) adhesion to tooth structure.
3. **Provides the mechanistic explanation for the known clinical weakness of mild self-etch adhesives on enamel** — lower chemical reactivity (less nanolayering) with enamel HAp compared with dentin.
4. **Identifies nanolayering as concentration- and technique-dependent** — proportional to 10-MDP concentration and enhanced by active rubbing/scrubbing application.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Materials**: an **experimental 10-MDP primer** and a **commercial control 10-MDP-based primer** — Clearfil SE Bond primer (C-SE, Kuraray) — applied to enamel and dentin following a common clinical application protocol.
- **Interfacial characterization**: **X-ray diffraction (XRD)** to detect the periodic nanolayer structure (MDP-Ca salt), and **transmission electron microscopy (TEM)** to image the interfacial ultrastructure.
- **Concentration effect**: XRD was used to study the effect of varying **10-MDP concentration** on the degree of nanolayering on dentin.
- **Technique effect**: nanolayering compared with vs without **active rubbing** of the primer on the surface.
- **Nanolayer stability / functional relevance**: **tensile bond strength** to enamel and dentin measured **with vs without an added photoinitiator** in the experimental primer.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

(Abstract-level — no numeric values reported in the retrieved abstract.)

- XRD **confirmed nanolayering on both enamel and dentin**.
- Nanolayering was **significantly GREATER on dentin than on enamel**.
- Nanolayering was **greater when the surface was actively rubbed** with the primer.
- The degree of nanolayering was **proportional to the 10-MDP concentration** in the primer.
- The experimental primer **required the photoinitiator to achieve dentin tensile bond strength comparable to the control C-SE primer**, but **not on enamel**.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Abstract-only retrieval** — full quantitative data, sample sizes, and statistical detail are not available here; re-read the PDF/full text to extract bond-strength numbers and XRD/TEM quantification if needed.
- In-vitro design — no aging, no thermocycling, no long-term clinical durability data; the relationship between nanolayer thickness and clinical bond longevity is inferential.
- Single MDP control (Clearfil SE Bond) — generalizability across the wider universal-adhesive market (which varies in MDP purity, solvent, and pH) is not established here.

## 7. Glossary

- **10-MDP (10-methacryloyloxydecyl dihydrogen phosphate)**: a functional acidic methacrylate monomer with a long carbon spacer; its phosphate group ionically bonds to calcium in hydroxyapatite and to zirconia.
- **Nanolayering**: self-assembly of MDP molecules into ordered, periodic layers of MDP-calcium salt at the adhesive–substrate interface; the structural hallmark of MDP chemical bonding.
- **Adhesion-decalcification (AD) concept**: framework predicting that a functional monomer first decalcifies (etches) HAp but, if its calcium salt is stable, remains ionically bonded — the basis for choosing chemically bonding monomers like 10-MDP.
- **HAp (hydroxyapatite)**: the mineral phase of enamel and dentin; provides the calcium that MDP bonds to (denser/more in enamel, less accessible reactive surface than dentin in this context).
- **Mild self-etch**: adhesives with relatively high pH that only partially demineralize tooth structure, leaving HAp available for chemical (MDP) bonding — strong on dentin, weaker on enamel.
- **XRD (X-ray diffraction)**: technique detecting the periodic nanolayer spacing, used here to confirm and quantify nanolayering.
