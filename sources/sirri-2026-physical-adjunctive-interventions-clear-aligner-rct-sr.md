## Why Ingested

Covers the **acceleration / adjunct axis** of clear-aligner-treatment (CAT) limitations: can vibration or low-level laser shorten treatment, improve tracking, or reduce pain? The efficacy anchor [[wiki/orthodontics/clear-aligner/baneshi-2024-clear-aligners-malocclusion-sr-ma]] establishes CA≈FA occlusal equivalence in simple cases; this paper tests whether physical adjuncts move that needle and answers no (low/very-low certainty). It also contextualizes the [[wiki/orthodontics/clear-aligner/yassir-2022-cat-vs-fat-overview-systematic-reviews]] umbrella by showing the aligner-change cadence — not the device — drives tracking/time.

## One-line Summary

First RCT-only SR (7 RCTs, n=266) of physical adjunctive interventions (vibration, LLLT) in clear aligner treatment: no consistent benefit on alignment (Little's Irregularity Index MD 0.08 maxilla p=0.869, 0.44 mandible p=0.487), case completion (p=0.999), compliance (~77%, p=0.390) or QoL; only transient day-1–3 pain reduction; GRADE low to very low.

## 한줄요약

투명교정(CAT)에 물리적 보조요법(진동·저출력레이저)을 더한 RCT 7편(n=266) 첫 SR: 정렬(Little 불규칙지수 상악 MD 0.08 p=0.869, 하악 0.44 p=0.487)·완료율(p=0.999)·순응도(~77%, p=0.390)·삶의질 모두 일관된 이득 없음. 통증만 1~3일 일시 감소. GRADE 확실성 low~very low.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic review focusing **exclusively on physical adjunctive interventions (PAIs) in CAT**, restricted to human RCTs, with **outcome-level** RoB 2 (not just study-level).
- Reframes the question: across six outcome tracks, the **aligner-change cadence and adherence — not the adjunct device — appear to drive tracking, treatment time, and pain**.
- Bottom line: randomized evidence does **not** demonstrate a consistent, clinically meaningful adjunct-specific benefit; clinical utility of PAIs in CAT remains unconfirmed.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PICOS: healthy CAT patients of any age; PAIs (vibration devices, LLLT/photobiomodulation, bioelectrical stimulation) vs no-adjunct or sham; outcomes = treatment time, Little's Irregularity Index, PCPDI, tracking, pain, OHRQoL, biomarkers, periodontal indices, root resorption; RCTs only (parallel or split-mouth).
- Excluded: nonrandomized designs, secondary syntheses, animal/in-vitro, PAI-vs-other-acceleration (corticotomy/drugs), and reports with insufficient data.
- Search inception→2025-06-30, run 2025-08-25: PubMed, Embase, Scopus, WoS, CENTRAL, plus TRIP, Google Scholar, grey literature (OpenAIRE, EBSCO Open Dissertations), four core ortho journals, reference hand-search. No language/date limits.
- 357 records → 217 after dedup → 14 full-text → **7 RCTs included (n=266)**.
- Certainty: GRADE per outcome domain. Synthesis narrative (SWiM); no meta-analysis.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Included trials**: 7 RCTs, 266 participants. Modalities: vibration (AcceleDent ×5; VPro5 ×1) + LLLT (×1). All addressed leveling/alignment stage only.
- **Objective tooth movement (GRADE very low)**: Little's Irregularity Index — AcceleDent no effect, MD 0.08 maxilla (p=0.869), 0.44 mandible (p=0.487). Bragassa: 4-day vs 14-day schedule ↑PCPDI efficiency (18.9%→23.8%→29.1% with vibration, p=0.003) but ↓accuracy (incisor alignment 48%→36%/37%, p=0.023/0.047). Lombardo: 7-day+vibration ≈ 14-day; vibration on 14-day improved only selected upper-arch movements. Alansari: vibration on 7-day improved tracking vs sham (p=0.003) and matched 14-day control.
- **Process / completion (GRADE very low)**: Katchooi — weekly-regimen completion identical active vs sham (p=0.999). Pescheret — shorter total time with vibration (0–12 mo p=0.010) but confounded by 7-day cadence + grey-literature thesis. Caccianiga (LLLT, 12-h wear) — 40±2 wk completion vs control discontinued at 7.2±1.6 wk; correctly-fitted aligners 22.1±1.0 vs 3.6±0.8 (p=0.001).
- **Compliance (GRADE very low)**: no active–sham difference (Katchooi p=0.390); mean ~77% (Pescheret, range 12–121%); adherence declines over time (novelty effect).
- **Pain / PROs (GRADE very low)**: only transient early benefit — Alansari days 1 & 3 (p<0.020, p<0.034); Katchooi day-3 only (p=0.026). No durable analgesic effect (Bragassa, Pescheret p>0.05). No OHIP-14/QoL benefit (Katchooi p>0.05).
- **Biomarkers (GRADE very low)**: inconsistent — Alansari ↑GCF inflammatory/osteoclastogenic markers with vibration (p<0.05); Idarraga no RANKL/OPG between-arm difference (p>0.05).
- **Periodontal indices (GRADE low)**: Idarraga — no PI/GI/BOP within- or between-group difference (p>0.05).
- **Overall GRADE**: low to very low across all domains; downgrades for RoB, inconsistency, indirectness (short follow-up, surrogate outcomes), imprecision (few small trials).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Few small RCTs (7, n=266); heterogeneous protocols/outcome definitions precluded meta-analysis.
- Blinding to a physical intervention is difficult; outcome-level RoB 2 flagged "some concerns"/"high" in domains 2–4; compliance often subjectively measured.
- Aligner-change cadence is a pervasive confounder — most "benefits" track the accelerated schedule, not the device.
- Future trials: prospective registration, sham control with blinded assessment, standardized adjunct "dose" with objective logging, designs that isolate adjunct from change cadence (factorial), harmonized outcomes, adequate follow-up + ITT.

## 7. Glossary

- **PAI (physical adjunctive intervention)**: non-invasive modality (vibration, LLLT/photobiomodulation, bioelectrical stimulation) intended to accelerate orthodontic tooth movement.
- **CAT**: clear aligner treatment.
- **LLLT / photobiomodulation**: low-level laser therapy applied to modulate bone remodeling.
- **Little's Irregularity Index**: linear sum of anterior contact-point displacements; alignment outcome metric.
- **PCPDI**: proximal contact point discrepancy index (tooth-movement efficiency metric).
- **Tracking**: degree to which achieved tooth position matches the ClinCheck plan.
- **RoB 2**: Cochrane risk-of-bias tool v2 for RCTs (here applied at outcome level).
- **GRADE**: certainty-of-evidence framework (high/moderate/low/very low).
- **RANKL/OPG**: bone-remodeling biomarkers (osteoclastogenesis vs inhibition) in gingival crevicular fluid.
- **SWiM**: Synthesis Without Meta-analysis reporting guideline.
