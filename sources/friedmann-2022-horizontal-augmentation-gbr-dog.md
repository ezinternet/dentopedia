## Why Ingested

수평 골증대술에서 막 유형(가교 콜라겐 vs 비가교 콜라겐 vs 심낭막)과 이식재 조합이 신생골 형성에 미치는 영향을 무작위 개 모델에서 분리하여 평가 — [[bone-regeneration/buser-2023-gbr-implant-35years-basic-principle-review]]가 제안한 막-이식재 조합 원칙을 조직형태계측으로 검증.

## One-line Summary

Randomized dog study (5 beagles, 20 defects, 4 treatment groups) showing DBBM + ribose cross-linked collagen membrane yields significantly greater new bone area than DBBM + native collagen membrane + tacking.

## 한줄요약

개 5마리 20개 만성 하악 결손부 무작위 연구 — 리보스 가교 콜라겐막+DBBM이 4핀 고정 비가교 콜라겐막+DBBM보다 신생골 면적이 유의하게 컸음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Materials (MDPI)
- **Volume/Issue**: 15(1): 238
- **Publication date**: 29 December 2021 (published 2022)
- **Study type**: Randomized animal study (beagle dog)
- **Ethics**: Directorate of Foodchain Security and Animal Health Care Pest County Government Office, Hungary (PEI/001/961-1/2013)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Isolated the effect of membrane type and fixation strategy on horizontal GBR outcomes in chronic mandibular defects
- Demonstrated that prolonged-barrier collagen membranes (ribose cross-linked; RCLC) outperform native collagen membranes even without mechanical pin fixation
- Showed membrane tacking/fixation is redundant when using biomaterials with adequate intrinsic stability
- Ca-sulfate (CS) + RCLC showed least residual non-bone graft area (favorable for graft resorption and bone replacement)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Animals**: 5 adult male beagle dogs (8.5–11.5 kg, ≥12 months)
- **Defect creation**: Bilateral premolar extraction (P2, P4) → surgical grinding to L-shaped buccal defect (~6–7 mm × 8–10 mm × 2.5–3 mm) → 6-week healing → chronic defect
- **Treatment groups** (n=5 defects each):
  - a. DBBM + NCM (Bio-Oss + Bio-Gide) + 4-pin tacking
  - b. DBBM + RCLC (Bio-Oss + Ossix Plus)
  - c. DBBM + NPPM (Bio-Oss + Smartbrane native porcine pericardium)
  - d. Ca-sulfate + RCLC (3DBond + Ossix Plus)
- Groups b/c/d: no pin fixation — membrane stabilized by CAF soft tissue only
- **Wound closure**: Coronally advanced flap (CAF), tension-free, Monocryl 5.0
- **Healing time**: 3 months
- **Histology**: Non-decalcified processing, toluidine blue staining, histomorphometry by ImageJ
- **Outcome measures**: New bone area (primary), residual non-bone graft area (secondary), ridge width at crest and 3 apical levels
- **Statistics**: ANOVA post hoc (data as medians and IQR)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Comparison | New bone area | p-value |
|---|---|---|
| b (DBBM+RCLC) vs a (DBBM+NCM+tacking) | b significantly greater | 0.001 |
| b vs c (DBBM+NPPM) | b significantly greater | 0.002 |
| b vs d (CS+RCLC) | b significantly greater | 0.046 |

| Comparison | Residual non-bone graft area | p-value |
|---|---|---|
| d (CS+RCLC) vs a | d significantly less | 0.026 |
| d vs c | d significantly less | 0.021 |

- Group b (DBBM+RCLC, no tacking) produced the most new bone — prolonged membrane barrier function combined with DBBM's space-maintaining properties appears synergistic
- Membrane fixation (4-pin tacking) with NCM did NOT improve outcomes over unfixed cross-linked membranes
- Ca-sulfate (CS) resorbed more completely — less residual graft material, potentially allowing faster bone ingrowth
- Note: 2 of 5 specimens lost in group b due to processing error — slightly reduces power for that group

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample (5 dogs, 20 defects); 2 specimens lost in one group
- Beagle dog chronic mandibular defects — may not fully translate to human clinical scenarios
- 3-month endpoint — longer follow-up needed to assess volumetric stability and remodeling
- No implant placement — does not assess if new bone supports osseointegration
- Membrane exposure rates not separately analyzed

## 7. Glossary

- **NCM (Native Collagen Membrane)**: Non-cross-linked collagen membrane — shorter barrier function (e.g. Bio-Gide)
- **RCLC (Ribose Cross-Linked Collagen Membrane)**: Prolonged degradation membrane (Ossix Plus)
- **NPPM (Native Porcine Pericardium Membrane)**: Xenogeneic membrane from pig pericardium (Smartbrane)
- **CS (Calcium Sulfate)**: Resorbable graft material with faster resorption than DBBM
- **CAF (Coronally Advanced Flap)**: Tension-free wound closure technique
- **Histomorphometry**: Quantitative analysis of tissue sections using image analysis software
