## Why Ingested

First systematic review + meta-analysis quantifying PDT efficacy specifically for halitosis using VSC (H2S, CH3SCH3, CH3SH) as outcomes. Establishes the evidence base for PDT as a halitosis management modality. This is a new category (halitosis) with no prior wiki pages — foundational ingest.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 8 RCTs (n=345): PDT and PDT+tongue scraper reduce H2S immediately post-treatment better than tongue scraper alone, but the advantage disappears at 7–90-day follow-up.

## 한줄요약

8개 RCT(n=345) SR+MA: 광역동치료(PDT) 및 PDT+혀클리너는 시술 직후 H2S 감소에서 혀클리너 단독보다 우수하지만, 7~90일 추적에서는 차이가 없어진다.

- **Included studies**: 8 RCTs, n=345 patients

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First dedicated SR+MA to quantify PDT efficacy for halitosis using volatile sulfur compound (VSC) measurements as primary outcomes
2. Demonstrates significant short-term (immediate post-treatment) superiority of PDT over tongue scraper (TS) alone for H2S reduction
3. PDT+TS combination shows additive immediate benefit over TS alone
4. No sustained benefit of PDT over TS alone at 7-, 14-, 30-, or 90-day follow-up
5. PDT shows no significant advantage over TS for CH3SCH3 (dimethyl sulfide) or CH3SH (methanethiol) reduction
6. Supports PDT as a promising adjunctive short-term treatment for halitosis

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Search strategy**: Five databases (PubMed, Cochrane Library, Embase, Web of Science, Scopus) searched from inception to August 10, 2022.

**Inclusion criteria**: RCTs comparing PDT with tongue scraper (TS) for halitosis treatment; VSC concentration reported as outcome.

**Comparison groups**:
- PDT vs. TS (tongue scraper control)
- PDT + TS vs. TS alone

**Outcome measures (VSC measurements)**:
- H2S (hydrogen sulfide) — primary
- CH3SCH3 (dimethyl sulfide, DMS)
- CH3SH (methanethiol, methyl mercaptan)

**Time points analyzed**: Immediately post-treatment, 7-day, 14-day, 30-day, 90-day follow-up

**Statistical synthesis**: Mean differences (MD) with 95% confidence intervals (CI); random-effects model implied by heterogeneity reporting

**PDT protocol typical parameters**: Methylene blue (MB) photosensitizer; LED light source (red spectrum); application to tongue dorsum (primary reservoir of VSC-producing bacteria)

**Sample**: 8 eligible RCTs, 345 patients total

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### H2S reduction — Immediate post-treatment
| Comparison | MD | 95% CI | P-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| PDT vs. TS | −34.49 | [−66.34, −2.64] | 0.03 |
| PDT+TS vs. TS | −67.72 | [−101.17, −34.28] | <0.001 |

- Both PDT alone and PDT+TS significantly outperformed TS alone immediately after treatment
- PDT+TS combination showed approximately double the H2S reduction versus PDT alone

### H2S reduction — Follow-up (7, 14, 30, 90 days)
- No significant differences between TS, PDT alone, and PDT+TS at any follow-up time point
- The immediate advantage of PDT does not persist

### DMS (CH3SCH3) and CH3SH
- No significant differences between PDT and TS for reduction of dimethyl sulfide or methanethiol
- H2S appears to be the primary VSC target of PDT

### Clinical interpretation
- PDT demonstrates bactericidal action via reactive oxygen species (ROS) generation, targeting VSC-producing bacteria on the tongue dorsum
- The immediate effect is consistent with PDT's mechanism (acute photochemical killing of bacteria)
- Lack of long-term benefit suggests recolonization occurs, limiting standalone PDT efficacy
- PDT may be best used as an adjunct to mechanical cleaning rather than as monotherapy

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

1. **Abstract-only data**: This source was retrieved as a web page preview; full methodology and risk-of-bias tables not accessible
2. **Small number of included studies**: Only 8 RCTs; underpowered for subgroup analysis by PDT parameters (wavelength, power density, photosensitizer concentration, exposure time)
3. **Short-term follow-up dominant**: Most evidence at immediate and 7-day; 90-day data sparse
4. **No difference in DMS/CH3SH**: Unclear if PDT genuinely does not affect these VSCs or if measurement sensitivity was insufficient
5. **Heterogeneity**: PDT protocols vary across trials (LED vs. laser, MB concentration, irradiation time)
6. **Future directions**: Standardized PDT protocols; longer follow-up (≥6 months); combined PDT + oral hygiene instruction RCTs

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| PDT (Photodynamic Therapy, 광역동치료) | Use of photosensitizer + light to generate ROS, killing target microorganisms |
| VSC (Volatile Sulfur Compounds, 휘발성황화합물) | H2S, CH3SH, CH3SCH3 — primary chemical mediators of oral malodor |
| H2S (Hydrogen sulfide, 황화수소) | Most prevalent VSC in halitosis; produced by anaerobic bacteria on tongue dorsum |
| TS (Tongue Scraper, 혀클리너) | Mechanical device to remove tongue coating and VSC-producing bacteria |
| MB (Methylene Blue, 메틸렌블루) | Cationic photosensitizer commonly used in aPDT for oral bacteria |
| aPDT (Antimicrobial PDT) | PDT applied for antimicrobial purposes, synonym of PDT in this context |
| MD (Mean Difference) | Continuous outcome effect size measure in meta-analysis |
| ROS (Reactive Oxygen Species) | Cytotoxic oxygen radicals generated during PDT activation |
