## Why Ingested

인사돌(Insadol)-family OTC 치주 생약제 감시(surveillance) 인제스트. 본 논문은 ISD(=Zea mays L. 추출물 + Magnolia cortex 추출물, 즉 인사돌플러스 처방 그 자체, 250 mg/kg/day)를 양성대조약(positive control)으로 직접 사용해, 새 한약 추출물 PBE의 효능을 ISD에 벤치마킹한다 — 인사돌의 기준 효능을 동물 모델 안에서 외부 비교군으로 못박은 드문 paper. [[wiki/periodontics/kim-2024-magnoliae-cortex-zea-mays-extracts]]가 보고한 ISD/인사돌의 항염·골보호 효과를 제3의 약물 평가 맥락에서 재확인한다.

## One-line Summary

Rat ligature-induced periodontitis study (6 groups, n=5–7/group): Pimpinella brachycarpa extract (PBE, 150–300 mg/kg/day) dose-dependently reduced alveolar bone loss, TRAP+ osteoclasts, and CD45/IL-1β/TNF-α, with efficacy comparable to the positive control ISD (250 mg/kg/day, the Insadol Plus formula = Zea mays + Magnolia cortex extract).

## 한줄요약

쥐 결찰-유발 치주염 모델(6군, 군당 n=5–7)에서 한약 추출물 PBE(150–300 mg/kg/day)가 치조골 소실·TRAP+ 파골세포·CD45/IL-1β/TNF-α를 용량 의존적으로 줄였고, 그 효과가 양성대조약 ISD(250 mg/kg/day, 옥수수+후박 추출물 = 인사돌플러스 처방)와 비슷했다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First in vivo study to evaluate *Pimpinella brachycarpa* extract (PBE; Korean "Chamnamul," 참나물) in experimental periodontitis, showing dose-dependent reduction of alveolar bone loss, osteoclast activity, and inflammatory cytokine expression.
2. **Direct head-to-head benchmarking against ISD (Insadol Plus)** — the *Zea mays* L. extract + *Magnolia cortex* extract herbal formulation (Dongkook Pharmaceutical, Insadolplus) at **250 mg/kg/day** as the positive-control reference drug. PBE's efficacy was reported as "comparable" to ISD across bone-loss, osteoclast, and inflammatory readouts.
3. Component dissection: green tea catechin (GTC, 20 mg/kg), chlorogenic acid (CGA, 20 mg/kg), and their 1:1 combination (MIX, 20+20 mg/kg) each independently reduced bone loss/osteoclasts, indicating CGA (the most abundant PBE constituent, 13.7 mg/g) and catechins account for part of PBE's effect.
4. HPLC phytochemical profile of PBE: chlorogenic acid 13.7 mg/g, luteolin-7-O-glucuronide 3.6 mg/g, apigenin-7-O-glucuronide 3.8 mg/g, luteolin-3-O-glucuronide 2.5 mg/g; GTC not detected in the extract.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Model**: 7-week-old male Sprague-Dawley rats; 4-0 silk ligature tied around **maxillary second molars bilaterally** to induce periodontitis; test substances by **oral gavage once daily × 14 days**; euthanized day 15, maxillae harvested.
- **Groups (6 total)**: Normal control (n=5); LIG (ligature only, n=7); and treatment groups (n=7 each). Sample size reported as n=5–7/group.
- **Dose-response arm**: PBE at 150, 200, 300 mg/kg/day vs **ISD 250 mg/kg/day (positive control)**.
- **Component arm (separate comparison)**: PBE 150 mg/kg/day vs GTC 20 mg/kg, CGA 20 mg/kg, MIX (GTC+CGA, 20+20 mg/kg, 1:1).
- **Readouts**:
  - Gross morphology — alveolar bone loss area (CEJ→bone crest), ImageJ.
  - H&E histology — CEJ-ABC vertical distance.
  - TRAP staining — TRAP+ multinucleated osteoclasts (≥3 nuclei) per ROI.
  - IHC — CD45 (leukocyte infiltration), IL-1β, TNF-α (% positive area).
  - HPLC — phytochemical quantification (Waters, C18, 330 nm).
- **Statistics**: one-way ANOVA + Dunnett's post hoc vs LIG; p<0.05 significant.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Alveolar bone loss**: LIG group = most severe resorption. **All PBE doses (150/200/300) and ISD visibly preserved bone**; quantitatively all PBE-treated groups significantly reduced bone-loss area vs LIG (p<0.05–0.001).
- **Histology (CEJ-ABC)**: ISD and all PBE doses significantly reduced CEJ-ABC distance vs LIG, preserving periodontal architecture.
- **Osteoclasts (TRAP+)**: LIG markedly elevated; PBE reduced TRAP+ cell number **dose-dependently** (higher doses strongest).
- **Inflammation (IHC)**: PBE reduced CD45, IL-1β, TNF-α positive area vs LIG (especially CD45 and IL-1β).
- **ISD benchmark statement (Discussion)**: "When compared with the positive control ISD, which has demonstrated anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects in periodontal therapy, PBE exhibited **comparable efficacy** in suppressing inflammatory markers and osteoclast presence." → PBE ≈ ISD; ISD = active reference, not a weak control. The paper treats ISD/Insadol Plus as an established, efficacious plant-based periodontal therapeutic baseline.
- **Component arm**: GTC, CGA, MIX, and PBE all significantly reduced bone loss, CEJ-ABC, and TRAP+ counts vs LIG. CGA (most abundant in PBE) and catechins each contribute; MIX suggested additive/synergistic effect.
- **Quantitative note**: paper reports significance vs LIG (Dunnett) but does not tabulate exact mean ± SD bone-loss values or a head-to-head PBE-vs-ISD statistical contrast — comparison is qualitative ("comparable").

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Molecular signaling pathways (NF-κB/MAPK/RANKL) not directly assayed — inferred from component literature (CGA, luteolin-7-O-glucuronide).
- Short duration (14 days) and small sample size (n=5–7) limit generalizability.
- Bioavailability/pharmacokinetics of PBE phytochemicals not measured; rodent dose (150–300 mg/kg) far exceeds human dietary intake (100–300 mg/day) — translational gap.
- No exact numeric effect sizes for PBE vs ISD head-to-head; "comparable" is a qualitative claim.
- Luteolin-7-O-glucuronide and apigenin-7-O-glucuronide not tested in vivo (limited compound availability).

## 6. Related Work

- **ISD / Insadol Plus** (*Zea mays* + *Magnolia cortex*) — used as the positive-control reference drug; corresponds to the wiki's Insadol-family pages [[wiki/periodontics/kim-2024-magnoliae-cortex-zea-mays-extracts]] and [[wiki/periodontics/kim-2018-magnoliae-cortex-zea-mays-modulate]].
- GTC (green tea catechin) and CGA (chlorogenic acid) — established natural anti-periodontitis comparators (Lee et al.; Nishida et al.; Gennaro et al.).
- Ligature-induced periodontitis is the standard rat model for alveolar bone loss / host-modulation testing.

## 7. Glossary

- **PBE** — *Pimpinella brachycarpa* extract (참나물/Chamnamul, edible Korean herb).
- **ISD** — herbal formulation = *Zea mays* L. extract + *Magnolia cortex* extract = **Insadol Plus (인사돌플러스)**, Dongkook Pharmaceutical; positive control here at 250 mg/kg/day.
- **GTC** — green tea catechin. **CGA** — chlorogenic acid. **MIX** — GTC+CGA 1:1 combination.
- **TRAP** — tartrate-resistant acid phosphatase; osteoclast marker.
- **CEJ-ABC** — cementoenamel junction to alveolar bone crest distance (bone-loss metric).
- **CD45** — pan-leukocyte marker (immune infiltration). **IL-1β / TNF-α** — pro-inflammatory cytokines driving osteoclastogenesis.
- **LIG** — ligature-only periodontitis group (untreated disease control).
