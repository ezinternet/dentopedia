## Why Ingested

Part of a focused collection on vertical ridge augmentation with titanium mesh, contributing direct evidence on the flap-management problem (mesh exposure, keratinized gingiva loss). This beagle study tests whether an open-healing approach without periosteal releasing incision can heal over a Ti-membrane and *widen* keratinized gingiva — directly addressing the exposure risk reported in [[implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/chiapasco-2021-customized-cadcam-titanium-meshes-gbr]] and complementing the soft-tissue-expansion mechanism in [[implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/kaner-2017-pre-augmentation-soft-tissue-expansion-vertical-bone]].

## One-line Summary

Randomized-block animal study (6 beagle dogs, 4 surgical protocols, ~12 sites/group): a mucoperiosteal flap left open without periosteal releasing incision (PRI) healed over a clean titanium-membrane surface during vertical bone augmentation with the lowest dehiscence (group C 8.3% vs PRI groups 41.67–50%) and a +1.77 mm gain in keratinized gingiva (vs −4.37 mm with PRI + closure, p<.001).

## 한줄요약

무작위블록 동물 연구 (비글견 6마리, 4개 프로토콜, 군당 약 12부위): 골막이완절개(PRI) 없이 일차봉합을 하지 않은 점막골막피판이 깨끗한 티타늄막 위에서 치유되어 열개율이 가장 낮았고(C군 8.3% vs PRI군 41.67–50%) 각화치은이 +1.77 mm 증가(PRI+봉합군 −4.37 mm, p<.001).

- Journal: International Dental Journal, Vol. 75, 2025, article 100823

- Affiliation: Affiliated Stomatological Hospital, Southwest Medical University, Luzhou, China
- Study type: controlled **animal** experiment (beagle dogs), randomized block design; ARRIVE-compliant.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Tests whether **open healing without a periosteal releasing incision (PRI)** is viable over a titanium membrane in vertical bone augmentation — challenging the routine PRI + primary closure paradigm.
- Shows the open, non-PRI protocol both **minimizes wound dehiscence** and **widens keratinized gingiva (KG)** rather than narrowing it.
- Provides histological comparison of new gingiva and Ti-membrane surface analysis across protocols.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 6 male beagle dogs (12 ± 2 kg). Bilateral mandibular P2, P3, P4, M1 extracted; 3-month healing.
- 4 sites per mandibular side (8 mm wide, >4 mm apart, >2 mm from natural teeth) randomized by block design into 4 groups (≈12 sites/group by sample-size calc):
  - **Group A**: Bio-Oss + Ti-membrane + Bio-Gide + PRI, closed flap.
  - **Group B**: Bio-Oss + Ti-membrane + Bio-Oss (over mesh) + Bio-Gide + PRI, closed flap.
  - **Group C**: Bio-Oss + Ti-membrane + Bio-Oss (over mesh) + Bio-Gide + **no PRI, open (unclosed) flap** (3 mm fissure covered with gelatin sponge, 8-character + interrupted sutures).
  - **Group D**: Ti-membrane + Bio-Gide + no PRI, closed flap (membrane directly on bone, no graft).
- Granular bone piled 3 mm high (groups A/B/C); porous Ti-membrane (CTi-mem, Neobiotech, Korea) 7×5 mm; collagen membrane (Bio-Gide) fixed by horizontal mattress suture.
- Sample size: 12/group computed (KG width mean difference 1.2 mm, SD 1.03, α=0.05, power 0.8).
- Outcomes: wound healing rate & dehiscence rate, KG width change, gingival histology, Ti-membrane surface analysis. Penicillin 7 days post-op.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Wound dehiscence**: group A 50%, group B 41.67%, group D 8.3%; no significant difference between A and B (p=1.000). In **group C (open, no PRI), only 1 site (8.3%)** showed incomplete soft-tissue closure.
- **Keratinized gingiva width change**: **group C +1.77 ± 0.37 mm** (KG widened) vs **group B −4.37 ± 0.45 mm** (KG narrowed); difference highly significant (p<.001).
- New gingiva in group C showed better histological performance than group B.
- Demonstrates a mucoperiosteal flap can heal on the **clean surface of the Ti-membrane** without PRI/primary closure, and KG can widen.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Animal (beagle) model; small n (6 dogs); short-term healing focus on soft tissue/KG (the extracted text emphasizes soft-tissue and membrane-surface outcomes rather than millimeters of vertical bone gain).
- Group C's open healing depends on a *clean* membrane surface and gelatin-sponge coverage; clinical translation and infection risk need human study.
- Brand/device-specific (CTi-mem, Bio-Oss, Bio-Gide).

## 6. Related Work

- Reports of Ti-mesh exposure rates up to 51% (cited); clinical reports that gingiva can heal over Ti-mesh; PRI drawbacks (KG narrowing, vestibular shallowing, periosteal blood-supply interruption); angiogenesis/CD31 in wound healing.

## 7. Glossary

- **MPF (Mucoperiosteal Flap)**: full-thickness flap (mucosa + periosteum) raised to access bone.
- **PRI (Periosteal Releasing Incision)**: cutting the periosteum to release flap tension for tension-free primary closure; drawbacks include KG narrowing and interrupted periosteal blood supply.
- **KG (Keratinized Gingiva)**: keratinized mucosa around the site; its width can narrow (with PRI + coronal advancement) or widen (open healing here).
- **Open healing**: deliberately leaving the wound unclosed over a clean Ti-membrane to heal by secondary intention.
- **Ti-membrane / Ti-mesh**: rigid titanium space-maintaining device for GBR (here porous CTi-mem).
