## Why Ingested

Xerostomia (구강건조증, incl. Sjögren·radiation·medication causes) is the connective thread across our autoimmune/dry-mouth dental pages, but until now we held only implant-specific syntheses ([[wiki/implants/almeida-2017-dental-implants-sjogren-syndrome-sr]], [[wiki/implants/esimekara-2022-dental-implants-autoimmune-diseases-sr]]). This 16-study SR is the first page we hold that spans **all** treatment modalities — restorations, implants, periodontal therapy, prostheses, and PROs — and quantifies the restoration-longevity penalty (HR ~2.6–2.9) that the implant-only SRs do not cover. It reinforces almeida-2017's high-implant-survival conclusion for Sjögren's while adding the radiation-induced caveat.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (16 studies, 1227 patients) showing xerostomia roughly doubles–triples restoration failure (HR ~2.6–2.9, recurrent caries) while implant survival stays >90% except in radiation-induced cases; no major periodontal-therapy penalty; evidence GRADE low-to-moderate, RoB moderate-to-serious.

## 2. Key Contributions

- **First cross-modality synthesis** of xerostomia's impact on dental outcomes — restorations, implants, periodontal therapy, prostheses, and patient-reported outcomes (PROs) — across all etiologies (Sjögren's, radiation, medication, systemic disease, mouth-breathing).
- **Restoration longevity is the clearest casualty**: two large registry/EHR cohorts (Leinonen 2021; Gomez 2024) show HR ~2.6–2.9 for restoration failure vs controls, driven by **recurrent caries at margins**; 5-year survival ~55% (Sjögren's) vs ~75% (controls) in Gomez.
- **Material guidance for radiation-induced xerostomia**: fluoride-releasing glass ionomer cements (GIC) outperform composite/amalgam in fluoride non-users; fluoride compliance is itself a strong effect modifier.
- **Implants remain viable** (~94% survival, moderate certainty) for autoimmune xerostomia, but **radiation-induced xerostomia carries a distinctly higher implant failure risk** (failures within first year; success ~86.7% under Albrektsson criteria).
- **Periodontal therapy** shows delayed short-term healing but equivalent long-term outcomes with diligent maintenance.
- Flags the near-total absence of **prosthesis-retention** evidence (single n=8 study) as a key research gap.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **PICO question**: clinical outcomes of dental treatment in patients with xerostomia.
- **Databases**: PubMed, Embase, Google Scholar (first 200 results, incognito); reference-list screening; up to June 2025; English, adults ≥18 y.
- **Inclusion**: human subjects with xerostomia/hyposalivation of any etiology reporting ≥1 dental treatment outcome (restoration longevity, implant survival, periodontal healing, prosthesis retention, OHRQoL). RCTs, cohort, case-control, cross-sectional eligible.
- **Exclusion**: animal/in vitro, reviews, abstracts without data, case series <5 patients, prevalence/pathophysiology-only papers.
- **Selection**: two independent reviewers (S.P, P.K), third (P.P) for disputes; PRISMA flow.
- **Risk of bias**: RoB 2 for the single RCT; ROBINS-I for non-randomized studies.
- **Certainty**: GRADE (high/moderate/low/very low) per outcome.
- **No meta-analysis** — substantial heterogeneity in design, outcome measures, and xerostomia etiologies.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Screening**: 244 records → 217 after de-dup → 48 full-text → **16 included** (10 database + 6 reference screening); 8 excluded for being case series <5.
- **Designs**: 1 RCT, 1 non-randomized interventional, 5 prospective cohorts, 7 retrospective cohorts, 2 cross-sectional.
- **Total**: **1,227 patients**; sample sizes 8–292; mean ages 50–70 y; women predominate in Sjögren's cohorts.
- **Restoration survival (5 studies, GRADE low)**: HR ~2–3 for failure in xerostomia. Gomez 2024 HR ≈2.9 (95% CI 2.3–3.7), failures ~1 y sooner, 5-y survival ~55% vs ~75%. Leinonen 2021 HR ≈2.6 (p<0.001); Sjögren's/radiation worse than unspecified hyposalivation. GIC reduced recurrent caries vs composite (McComb 2002, De Moor 2011; p<0.05) but more marginal erosion. Haveman 2003: fluoride users had no recurrent caries regardless of material.
- **Implant survival (8 studies, ~300 implants, GRADE moderate)**: Sjögren's cohorts 97–100% at 18 mo–5 y (Maarse 2022 100% SS vs 96% control p=0.06; Hosseini 2025 100% both; Korfage 2015 97% pSS vs 100% control ns; Albrecht 2016 ~100% both). Radiation-induced worse: ~95.2% survival at 1 y, success ~86.7% (Albrektsson). Siddiqui 2017 implant survival 87.6% vs 95.7% controls (p<0.001); Bidyasagar 2023 10% vs 0% failure. **Overall average ~94%**.
- **Periodontal (2 studies, GRADE low)**: Kaur 2018 slower early gingival-index improvement in mouth-breathers, equalized by 12 weeks; Sparrow 2021 no difference in probing depth/plaque during maintenance. **No major long-term periodontal-therapy difference.**
- **PROs (GRADE moderate)**: consistent OHRQoL gains post-implant rehab. Hosseini OHIP-49 47.5→30.4; Maarse OHIP-14 33.7→28.8 over 18 mo; but Sjögren's scores persistently worse than controls.
- **Prosthesis retention (very low)**: only Isidor 1999 (n=8) — no implant-retained dentures lost/remade over 2 y.
- **Risk of bias**: 10/16 (62.5%) at serious RoB; single RCT (McComb 2002) high RoB; a few prospective cohorts (Maarse, Hosseini) moderate.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Predominantly retrospective/small prospective cohorts; non-random allocation, unblinded assessment, limited confounder control.
- Heterogeneity across etiologies, treatments, and outcome measures precluded meta-analysis.
- Most studies did not stratify outcomes by xerostomia cause, weakening etiology-specific conclusions.
- Radiation-implant higher-failure signal came at short (1-y) follow-up and stricter success criteria — interpret cautiously.
- Near-absent prosthesis-retention evidence (single n=8 study).
- Future: prospective trials stratified by severity/cause, standardized blinded outcomes, larger samples, and evaluation of salivary stimulation/substitution therapies on treatment outcomes.

## 7. Glossary

- **Xerostomia**: subjective sensation of dry mouth; may occur with or without measurable hyposalivation.
- **Hyposalivation**: objectively reduced salivary flow.
- **Sjögren's syndrome (pSS/sSS)**: autoimmune destruction of salivary/lacrimal glands; primary vs secondary.
- **Sicca syndrome**: dryness of eyes and mouth (sicca complex).
- **OHRQoL / OHIP**: Oral Health-Related Quality of Life / Oral Health Impact Profile (OHIP-14, OHIP-49).
- **HR**: hazard ratio (relative rate of an event over time).
- **GIC / RMGIC**: glass ionomer cement / resin-modified GIC; fluoride-releasing restoratives.
- **ROBINS-I**: Risk Of Bias In Non-randomized Studies of Interventions.
- **RoB 2**: Cochrane Risk of Bias tool for randomized trials.
- **GRADE**: Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation (certainty of evidence).
- **Albrektsson criteria**: stricter implant success criteria (vs survival-only).
