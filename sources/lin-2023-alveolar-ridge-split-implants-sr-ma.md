## Why Ingested

The wiki holds ridge-split *case reports* ([[bone-regeneration/ayoub-2018-ridge-splitting-horizontal-augmentation-case]], [[bone-regeneration/enislidis-2006-staged-ridge-splitting-implant-mandible]], [[bone-regeneration/simion-1992-jawbone-enlargement-split-crest-gtr]]) but lacked synthesis-level quantitative evidence for the alveolar ridge split (ARS) technique. This 25-study SR+MA supplies the pooled numbers — horizontal width gain 3.348 mm and implant survival 98.1% — that turn the anecdotal case-report picture into a defensible evidence base for narrow-ridge horizontal augmentation.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 25 studies (24 observational + 1 RCT) on the alveolar ridge split (ARS) technique: pooled gained horizontal ridge width 3.348 mm (95%CI 2.533-4.163) and pooled implant survival 98.1% (95%CI 96.9-98.9), establishing ARS as a reliable horizontal augmentation method for narrow crests.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA to pool both horizontal width gain AND implant survival specifically for the ARS (split-crest / ridge-split) technique across the 2010-2023 literature.
- Pooled gained horizontal width = **3.348 mm** (95%CI 2.533-4.163), 14 studies.
- Pooled implant survival = **98.1%** (95%CI 96.9-98.9), 17 studies — comparable to implants placed in native bone.
- Catalogues seven distinct complication types and maps the maxilla-vs-mandible difficulty gradient driven by buccal cortical thickness and bone density.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Databases: PubMed, Embase, Cochrane CENTRAL, Web of Science, CNKI, SIGLE (grey literature). Jan 1, 2010 - Nov 1, 2023, no language restriction.
- PICOS: completely/partially edentulous patients undergoing implant placement with ARS; primary outcome = increased horizontal width; secondary = implant survival rate.
- Exclusions: nonhuman studies, n<10, no width/survival reported, no mean/SD, reviews.
- 235 records after de-duplication -> 42 full-text -> 25 included.
- Risk of bias: ROBINS-I (NRCTs), RoB 2.0 (the single RCT). Random-effects model (Comprehensive Meta-Analysis 3.0). Begg/Egger for publication bias; one-study-removed sensitivity analysis.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Gained horizontal width 3.348 mm** (95%CI 2.533-4.163); individual study range 1.6-5.3 mm (Korsakova 1.6 mm low; Moro 5.3 / Khoury 5.2 mm high).
- **Implant survival 98.1%** (95%CI 96.9-98.9); individual range 93.9-100%.
- Study mix: 24 observational (12 prospective, 12 retrospective) + 1 RCT (Mahmoud 2020). RoB: 9 high, 16 moderate.
- Flap approach: 20 full-thickness, 3 partial-thickness, 2 flapless. Graft used in 21/25 (12 autologous, 9 xenograft).
- Maxilla gains more width than mandible (thicker/more viscoelastic maxillary buccal wall, D2/D3/D4 bone vs harder D1/D2 mandible).
- ARS (3.63 mm) yields slightly less width than bone block grafting (4.25 mm) but with shorter treatment time and lower morbidity; FPCS cut operative time >50% vs autologous bone block grafting (Mahmoud).
- Complications (7 types in 7 studies): exposure, infection, bad split, dehiscence, fracture, paresthesia, soft tissue retraction. Buccal plate fracture is the most frequent intraoperative complication; risk rises sharply when ridge width <3 mm.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Almost all evidence is non-randomized, no control group (only 1 RCT, itself high RoB).
- Egger test flagged publication bias for the survival-rate outcome.
- Short follow-up, small samples, and heterogeneity in surgical site, age, split technique, and clinician experience are confounders.
- Authors call for long-term RCTs and stratified comparisons (anterior vs posterior, maxilla vs mandible).

## 6. Related Work

- Reinforces ridge-split case reports already in the wiki: ayoub-2018 (horizontal augmentation case), enislidis-2006 (staged mandibular ridge split predetermining greenstick fracture line), simion-1992 (classic split-crest origin with immediate implant + e-PTFE GTR).
- The mandible two-stage / greenstick-fracture discussion (Goyal/Iyer, Anitua) maps directly onto enislidis-2006's staged corticotomy rationale.

## 7. Glossary

- ARS (Alveolar Ridge Split) — splitting the cortical bone to expand a narrow ridge buccolingually for implant placement (a.k.a. split-crest, ridge expansion).
- Greenstick fracture — controlled incomplete buccal-cortex fracture that hinges the segment outward; harder to control in dense mandibular cortex.
- ROBINS-I — Risk Of Bias In Non-randomized Studies of Interventions tool.
- FPCS — Flapless Piezotome Crest Split.
- ABBG — Autologous Bone Block Grafting.
