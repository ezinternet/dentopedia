## Why Ingested

Direct counterpoint to the foundational nanolayering claim. [[wiki/resin-bonding/yoshihara-2011-nanolayering-mdp-enamel-dentin]] established that 10-MDP self-assembles into ordered nanolayers at the resin-dentin interface and hypothesized this drives bond durability. Tian et al. (2015) tested whether seven *commercialized* 10-MDP self-etch/universal adhesives actually reproduce this structure on human dentin — and found nanolayering is RARELY present, challenging its real-world clinical relevance for bond longevity. Ingested to balance the Yoshihara mechanism page with the dissenting commercial-product evidence (concentration dependence: profuse at 15% experimental, sparse in commercial formulations).

## One-line Summary

In-vitro TEM + thin-film XRD study (7 commercial 10-MDP adhesives, n=6 TEM / n=4 XRD; experimental 15/10/5 wt% primers): profuse ~3.7 nm-periodicity nanolayering appears at 15% 10-MDP but is rarely seen in commercial adhesives, challenging nanolayering as a clinical bond-longevity mechanism.

## 한줄요약

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic test of whether *commercialized* 10-MDP adhesives actually produce the self-assembled nanolayering structure reported with experimental 10-MDP solutions.
- Directly challenges the hypothesis (rooted in Yoshihara 2011) that 10-MDP nanolayering is a meaningful mechanism for resin-dentin bond longevity in clinical adhesives.
- Establishes a concentration dependence: nanolayering is profuse at 15 wt% 10-MDP, reduced at 10% and 5%, and rarely present in commercial formulations.
- Provides paired TEM (morphology) + thin-film XRD (crystallographic periodicity) evidence with characteristic 2θ peak fingerprints.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Commercial adhesives (7)**, all applied in self-etch mode on midcoronal human dentin per manufacturer instructions:
  - Adhese Universal (Ivoclar-Vivadent)
  - All-Bond Universal (Bisco)
  - Clearfil SE Bond 2 (Kuraray Noritake)
  - Clearfil S3 Bond Plus (Kuraray Noritake)
  - Clearfil Universal Bond (Kuraray Noritake)
  - G-Premio Bond (GC Corp.)
  - Scotchbond Universal (3M ESPE)
- **Experimental control primers**: 15 wt%, 10 wt%, and 5 wt% 10-MDP (DM Healthcare Products) in mixed solvent (ethanol:water 9:8 by weight) with photoinitiators; applied to dentin for 20 s, then covered with a hydrophobic resin layer.
- **TEM**: bonded specimens (n=6) covered with flowable resin composite, processed for transmission electron microscopy, examined at 30 random sites WITHOUT staining.
- **XRD**: thin-film glancing-angle X-ray diffraction (n=4) to detect characteristic nanolayering peaks.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **15% 10-MDP experimental primer**: profuse nanolayering with highly ordered periodicity (~3.7 nm wide) adjacent to partially dissolved apatite crystallites in dentin.
- **Thin-film XRD (15%)**: three characteristic peaks — 2θ 2.40° (d = 3.68 nm), 4.78° (d = 1.85 nm), 7.18° (d = 1.23 nm).
- **10% and 5% 10-MDP primers**: reduced extent of nanolayering and lower-intensity XRD peaks (concentration dependence).
- **7 commercial adhesives**: nanolayering and characteristic XRD peaks were RARELY observed.
- **Conclusion**: the sparsity of nanolayering in commercial resin-dentin interfaces challenges its clinical effectiveness as a mechanism for improving dentin bond longevity.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro only; no bond-strength or aging/durability outcome was measured, so the link between nanolayering and clinical longevity is argued by absence, not directly disproven on a longevity endpoint.
- PMC export available to this wiki contained only the abstract — exact per-adhesive nanolayering scoring, statistics, and figures are not captured here.
- Self-etch mode only on midcoronal dentin; etch-and-rinse mode and enamel/other substrates not examined.
- Does not resolve WHY commercial adhesives nanolayer sparsely (formulation co-monomers, solvent, 10-MDP purity/concentration, functional-monomer competition all plausible).

## 6. Related Work

- Yoshihara K, et al. (2011) — original demonstration of 10-MDP self-assembled nanolayering at enamel/dentin interfaces; this paper is the counterpoint.
- Van Meerbeek / Kuraray adhesive durability literature on 2-step vs 1-step self-etch (e.g., Clearfil SE Bond family).

## 7. Glossary

- **10-MDP** — 10-methacryloyloxydecyl dihydrogen phosphate; functional acidic monomer that ionically bonds to calcium in hydroxyapatite.
- **Nanolayering** — self-assembled, ordered lamellar structures of 10-MDP–calcium salts at the resin-dentin interface; ~3.7 nm periodicity reported.
- **Self-etch (SE) mode** — adhesive application without separate phosphoric-acid etching; the acidic monomer demineralizes and infiltrates simultaneously.
- **Thin-film glancing-angle XRD** — surface-sensitive X-ray diffraction that detects the crystallographic periodicity (Bragg peaks) of thin ordered layers.
- **2θ** — diffraction angle in XRD; peak position maps to a d-spacing (lattice/layer spacing) via Bragg's law.
- **Apatite crystallites** — hydroxyapatite mineral crystals of dentin partially dissolved by acidic monomers during self-etch bonding.
