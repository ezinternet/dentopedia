## One-line Summary
Narrative review from Sevilla covering the full range of botulinum toxin applications in orofacial clinical practice, including TMD, bruxism, facial pain, and cosmetic uses; summarizes injection protocols and dosing recommendations.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Toxins 2020;12(2):112
- **DOI**: 10.3390/toxins12020112
- **Affiliation**: University of Sevilla, Spain
- **Study type**: Narrative review

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive overview of BTX indications in orofacial/dental practice
- Covers: TMD myofascial pain, bruxism (수면이갈이), trigeminal neuralgia, sialorrhea, gummy smile, facial aesthetics
- Summarizes clinically applicable injection protocols (muscles, anatomical landmarks, doses)
- Discusses BTX subtypes (OnabotulinumtoxinA, AbobotulinumtoxinA, IncobotulinumtoxinA) and their equivalence
- Provides practical dosing reference for masseter, temporalis, and lateral pterygoid

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative review of published literature and clinical experience
- No systematic search methodology
- Organized by clinical indication
- Includes anatomical diagrams and injection site descriptions
- Covers both therapeutic and cosmetic applications

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Masseter injection for bruxism/TMD myofascial: 25–75 U OnabotulinumtoxinA per side
- Temporalis injection: 15–50 U per side
- Effect onset: 3–5 days; peak: 2–4 weeks; duration: 3–6 months
- Repeated injections may cause muscle atrophy with volume reduction (20–40% masseter reduction reported)
- Safety profile: generally favorable; rare systemic effects
- Gummy smile correction: 2.5–5 U per injection site (upper lip levators)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative design; no systematic evidence synthesis or risk of bias assessment
- Clinical recommendations based on heterogeneous evidence levels
- Dosing recommendations are expert consensus-level
- Individual variation in muscle anatomy requires customized approach
- Long-term effects of repeated injections need prospective study

## 7. Glossary
- **OnabotulinumtoxinA** (Botox): Most widely studied BTX-A formulation; unit dosing reference standard
- **Masseter** (교근): Main jaw-closing muscle; primary target for bruxism and TMD BTX treatment
- **Temporalis** (측두근): Temporal fossa muscle; secondary masticatory target for BTX
- **Lateral pterygoid** (외측익돌근): Deep masticatory muscle involved in jaw opening and disc displacement
- **Sialorrhea**: Excessive salivation; treated with BTX injection to salivary glands
- **Gummy smile**: Excessive gingival display; managed with BTX to upper lip elevator muscles
