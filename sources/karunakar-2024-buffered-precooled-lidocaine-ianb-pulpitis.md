## Why Ingested

Symptomatic irreversible pulpitis (SIP)의 "hot tooth" IANB 실패율(literature 44–81%)은 [[wiki/drug/hwang-2023-proposal-clinical-guidelines-local-anesthesia-dentistry]]가 다루지 않은 임상 난제이며, 본 double-blind RCT는 buffering과 precooling(intraoral cryotherapy)이 SIP success rate를 conventional 38.9% → buffered 86.2% → precooled 78.9%로 끌어올림을 직접 비교한다. Buffered-LA cluster의 SIP-success anchor로 sibling [[wiki/drug/gandhi-2022-preheated-buffered-lignocaine-ianb-pulpitis-rct]](동일 SIP 모델, preheated/buffered 비교)와 짝을 이루고 [[wiki/drug/kalra-2024-buffered-lidocaine-articaine-extraction-rct]]의 onset 데이터를 SIP 맥락으로 확장한다.

## One-line Summary

Double-blind RCT (n=45, 15/group), IANB in mandibular molar with symptomatic irreversible pulpitis: buffered 2% lidocaine gave the highest anesthetic success (84.2% per Table 2; abstract states 86.2%) vs precooled 72.0% (abstract 78.9%) vs conventional 36.8% (abstract 38.9%), with buffered also showing fastest onset (61.1 s vs 79.6 s precooled vs 139.6 s conventional, p<0.001) and longest duration (313.8 min vs 278 vs 193.3 min).

## 한줄요약

이중맹검 RCT (n=45, 군당 15명), 증후성 비가역치수염(SIP) 하악 대구치 IANB: buffered 2% 리도카인이 마취 성공률 최고(Table 2 기준 84.2%, 초록은 86.2%) > precooled 72.0%(초록 78.9%) > conventional 36.8%(초록 38.9%); buffered가 발현도 가장 빠르고(61.1초 vs 79.6초 vs 139.6초, p<0.001) 지속도 가장 김(313.8분 vs 278분 vs 193.3분).

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Three-arm comparison** of conventional vs buffered vs precooled (intraoral cryotherapy) 2% lidocaine specifically in the SIP "hot tooth" model where IANB notoriously fails.
2. Quantified a **>2-fold success jump** from buffering over conventional (84.2% vs 36.8%) in the inflamed-tissue setting that most resists anesthesia.
3. Positioned **precooling as a viable intermediate** — better than conventional, slightly below buffered (difference between buffered and precooled NOT significant for pain, efficacy, duration; significant only for onset, p=0.034).
4. Confirmed buffering improves the full quartet: less injection-site pain, faster onset, longer duration, higher success.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: prospective, double-blind RCT, allocation 1:1:1, CONSORT-adherent; second investigator and patients blinded.
- **n = 45** (15/group) from 80 assessed; mandibular first molar with SIP, VAS >5, age 18–45, ASA I/II.
- **Groups**:
  - Group I (control): conventional 1.8 mL 2% lignocaine + 1:80,000 epinephrine, IANB over 2 min.
  - Group II (buffered): 3 mL 8.4% NaHCO3 added to 3 mL of 2% lignocaine/1:80,000 epi (1:10 ratio, freshly prepared) → IANB.
  - Group III (precooled): ice stick at injection site 15 s, then 1.8 mL refrigerated (4–6°C) 2% lidocaine/1:80,000 epi.
- **Diagnosis**: SIP with apical periodontitis by history, clinical/radiographic exam, positive EPT.
- **Outcomes**: pain at injection site (VAS), onset (EPT every minute until two consecutive negative responses), duration, anesthetic efficacy (VAS during access/RCT), success rate.
- **Statistics**: Kruskal–Wallis + post-hoc for pain/onset/efficacy/duration; Chi-square for success rates; p ≤ 0.05; SPSS 23.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Table 1 (mean ± SD; Kruskal–Wallis, all p < 0.001):

| Group (n=15) | Injection pain (VAS) | Onset (s) | Efficacy score | Duration (min) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conventional | 4.42 ± 0.8 | 139.6 ± 68 | 4.2 ± 0.7 | 193.3 ± 14.4 |
| Buffered | 1.84 ± 0.7 | 61.1 ± 8.7 | 1.6 ± 0.6 | 313.8 ± 36.4 |
| Precooled | 2.41 ± 0.6 | 79.6 ± 19 | 2.5 ± 0.9 | 278 ± 39.3 |

Pairwise (post-hoc): Conventional vs Buffered and Conventional vs Precooled all p<0.001. **Buffered vs Precooled**: pain p=0.359 (NS), onset p=0.034 (significant), efficacy p=0.092 (NS), duration p=0.250 (NS).

Success rate (Table 2, Chi-square = 1.462, p = 0.03):

| Group | Success n (%) |
|---|---|
| Conventional | 5/15 (36.8%) |
| Buffered | 12/15 (84.2%) |
| Precooled | 10/15 (72.0%) |

**Note on internal inconsistency**: the abstract and conclusion report success as 38.9% / 86.2% / 78.9%, while Table 2 (the actual count data, n=15/group) reports 36.8% / 84.2% / 72.0%. The Table 2 percentages (5/12/10 of 15) are taken as primary; the abstract figures appear to be a transcription discrepancy.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Single bicarbonate concentration (8.4%)** tested — authors flag this; varying ratios not explored.
- **Small per-group n (15)** and a **borderline Chi-square (p=0.03) on success** — modest statistical robustness.
- **Internal data inconsistency** between abstract and Table 2 success percentages (see above) reduces confidence in the exact success numbers.
- **Buffered vs precooled differences mostly non-significant** except onset — so the ranking buffered > precooled is weakly supported beyond onset.
- Buffered solution **instability** (precipitation if held too long) limits chair-side practicality.
- Future: multiple cryotherapy temperatures, multiple buffer concentrations, larger samples.

## 7. Glossary

- **SIP (Symptomatic Irreversible Pulpitis)**: inflamed "hot tooth"; inflammation can reduce anesthetic efficacy up to ~8-fold via nociceptor sensitization and TTX-resistant sodium channels.
- **Intraoral cryotherapy / precooling**: applying cold (ice stick 15 s + refrigerated 4–6°C LA) to deactivate A-delta fibers and raise lignocaine pKa, increasing the active cationic fraction.
- **EPT (Electric Pulp Test)**: vitality test; two consecutive negative max-reading responses confirm pulpal anesthesia onset.
- **Buffered lidocaine**: 8.4% NaHCO3 + 2% lidocaine/epi at 1:10 ratio, pH ~7.4.
- **Anesthetic success**: minimal/no pain on access and instrumentation.
