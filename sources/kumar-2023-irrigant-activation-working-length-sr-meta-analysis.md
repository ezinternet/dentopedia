## One-line Summary
SR+MA on irrigant activation techniques (IATs) vs conventional needle irrigation (CNI): ANP best at working-length delivery, followed by PUI > SI > MDA; IATs routinely recommended.

## 1. Document Information
KLE Academy, India. European Endodontic Journal open-access SR+MA. Searched MEDLINE, Scopus, Cochrane. Focus: irrigant penetration to working length (WL) in straight and curved canals.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive comparison of 4 IATs: MDA, SI, PUI, ANP
- ANP (apical negative pressure / EndoVac) most effective for WL delivery
- PUI second most effective
- All IATs superior to CNI (conventional needle injection)
- Recommends IAT incorporation in routine endodontic practice

## 3. Methodology
- SR+MA, MEDLINE/Scopus/Cochrane
- Outcomes: irrigant penetration depth to WL in straight and curved canals
- Comparators: MDA, SI (sonic), PUI (passive ultrasonic), ANP (apical negative pressure)

## 4. Key Results
**Irrigant delivery to WL ranking**: ANP > PUI > SI > MDA > CNI
- Both straight and curved canals: ANP consistently best
- PUI reliable second-line
- All IATs significantly better than CNI

## 5. Limitations
- Heterogeneity across studies
- Most studies in vitro / ex vivo
- ANP system not universally available

## 7. Glossary
- **IAT**: Irrigant Activation Technique
- **CNI**: Conventional Needle Irrigation
- **ANP**: Apical Negative Pressure (EndoVac system)
- **PUI**: Passive Ultrasonic Irrigation
- **SI**: Sonic Irrigation
- **MDA**: Manual Dynamic Activation
- **WL**: Working Length
