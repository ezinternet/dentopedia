## Why Ingested

냉플라즈마(cold plasma)와 광역동치료(PDT)를 직접 비교한 최초의 근관 항균 연구로, He/O₂ 플라즈마가 *E. faecalis* 바이오필름에 대해 PDT를 능가함을 정량적으로 확인했다. [[wiki/endodontics/irrigation/overview-root-canal-irrigation]] 페이지의 대체 항균법 섹션을 확장하고, 냉플라즈마 카테고리 신설의 근거 논문으로 활용한다.

## One-line Summary

In vitro study (n=100 extracted teeth) comparing He, He/O₂ cold plasma jet, and PDT against 1-week E. faecalis biofilm in root canals; He/O₂ plasma achieved the greatest CFU reduction (log 5.39 → 0.2) and complete biofilm elimination confirmed by SEM.

## 한줄요약

시험관 연구(n=100)에서 He/O₂ 냉플라즈마가 E. faecalis 바이오필름 제거 효과(CFU log 5.39→0.2)에서 PDT 및 He 플라즈마를 능가함을 처음으로 직접 비교·확인했다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First head-to-head comparison** of cold plasma jet (He and He/O₂) vs. PDT for E. faecalis root canal disinfection.
2. He/O₂ plasma delivered the strongest antibacterial effect — log CFU reduction of >5 log units at 8 min — outperforming both PDT and pure He plasma.
3. SEM confirmed **complete biofilm disappearance** and **obliteration of dentinal tubule openings** in the He/O₂ group.
4. Plasma's gas-phase delivery is an advantage over PDT: it can penetrate complex root canal anatomy where photosensitizer distribution may be uneven.
5. Dose–response relationship confirmed for both plasma types: longer exposure (2→8 min) progressively reduced CFU.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Specimen preparation**:
- 100 single-rooted extracted human teeth; crowns cut, standardised length 12 mm.
- Canals prepared with crown-down technique; sterilised with 5.25% NaOCl.
- Contaminated with *Enterococcus faecalis* (ATCC 29212); incubated 1 week to form biofilm.

**Phase 1 — dose–response (n=60)**:
- He plasma group (n=20): 5 subgroups × 4 teeth — control, 2, 4, 6, 8 min treatment.
- He/O₂ plasma group (n=20): same exposure time subgroups.
- PDT group (n=20): control vs. PDT treatment (2-s light activation of photosensitiser).

**Phase 2 — direct comparison (n=40)**:
- Four groups (n=10 each): control, 8-min He plasma, 8-min He/O₂ plasma, PDT.
- CFU/mL counting (bactericidal endpoint); SEM for structural evaluation.

**Plasma source**: Low-temperature atmospheric pressure plasma jet device; feeding gases: pure He and He/O₂ mixture.

**PDT protocol**: Photosensitiser (not fully specified in abstract), activated by light of specific wavelength in the presence of O₂.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Group | Baseline log CFU/mL | 8-min / treatment log CFU/mL | Reduction |
|---|---|---|---|
| He plasma | 5.55 | 3.42 | ~2.1 log |
| He/O₂ plasma | 5.39 | 0.20 | ~5.2 log |
| PDT | Not fully reported | Significant reduction | Between He and He/O₂ |
| Control | 5.5 (approx.) | No change | — |

- **Ranking (best → weakest)**: He/O₂ plasma > PDT > He plasma, all better than control.
- **SEM**: He/O₂ group showed complete biofilm elimination; dentinal tubule openings obliterated — suggests deeper reactive species penetration.
- Increasing plasma exposure time correlates with increasing bactericidal effect (dose-response confirmed for both gases).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In vitro only — no clinical or animal validation; root canal anatomy more complex in vivo.
- PDT protocol details (photosensitiser concentration, light dose) not fully disclosed in abstract — reproducibility concern.
- Plasma device not commercially standardised; parameters vary across studies (limits direct cross-study comparison).
- Did not test effects on dentin microstructure or pulp tissue biocompatibility.
- Future work: in vivo studies, optimise gas composition/exposure time, assess effect on smear layer, biocompatibility of plasma on periapical tissue.

## 6. Related Work

- Li et al. (prior): Ar/O₂ plasma more effective than Ca(OH)₂, 2% CHX, and Ca(OH)₂+CHX in 3-week biofilm.
- Ureyen Kaya et al. (prior): plasma and NaOCl had similar results in 4-week biofilms.
- Chen et al. (prior): He/O₂ plasma more effective than He plasma vs. E. faecalis — consistent with this study's findings.
- Pan et al. (J Endod 2013): cold plasma therapy against E. faecalis biofilms in vitro.
- Williams et al. (J Dent 2006): photoactivated disinfection (PAD) in endodontic bacteria (planktonic + root canal model).

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Cold plasma jet (냉플라즈마 제트) | Low-temperature atmospheric pressure plasma delivered via a nozzle; contains reactive oxygen/nitrogen species, UV, charged particles |
| He plasma | Plasma generated with pure helium gas as carrier |
| He/O₂ plasma | Plasma generated with helium + oxygen mixture; more reactive oxygen species (ROS) produced |
| PDT (광역동치료) | Photodynamic therapy: photosensitiser + light → reactive oxygen species → cell lysis |
| E. faecalis | Enterococcus faecalis; Gram-positive facultative bacterium; dominant pathogen in persistent root canal infection |
| CFU/mL | Colony-forming units per milliliter; quantitative measure of viable bacteria |
| SEM (주사전자현미경) | Scanning electron microscopy; used to visualise biofilm structure |
| Dentinal tubule | Microscopic canal in dentin; harbours bacteria resistant to conventional irrigation |
| ROS (활성산소종) | Reactive oxygen species; primary antibacterial mechanism of both plasma and PDT |
