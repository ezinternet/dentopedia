## One-line Summary
Narrative review surveying endodontic irrigants (NaOCl, CHX, EDTA, TetraClean, calcium hydroxide, distilled water, herbals), concluding no single irrigant is ideal and case-by-case selection plus a CHX final rinse (substantivity) are advisable.

## 한줄요약
Narrative review: NaOCl·CHX·EDTA·calcium hydroxide·herbal 등 세정제 총괄 — 이상적 단일 세정제는 없으며 증례별 선택과 CHX 최종 세척(잔류성) 권장.

## 1. Document Information
- World J Clin Cases 2024;12(21):4460–4468. Invited review, externally peer reviewed.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Broad single-reference comparison of irrigant classes and roles.
- Notes NaOCl+CHX precipitate (parachloroaniline / chemical smear) risk.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative review (non-systematic).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- No ideal irrigant; case-individualized selection.
- CHX final rinse beneficial for sustained (substantive) antimicrobial action; NaOCl+CHX forms orange-brown precipitate that can occlude tubules and impair sealing.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Narrative (low evidence weight); calls for further comparative research.

## 6. Related Work
- Umbrella narrative tying together smear-layer, activation, and antimicrobial irrigation pages.

## 7. Glossary
- CHX: chlorhexidine. Substantivity: prolonged residual antimicrobial activity.
