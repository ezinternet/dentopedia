## Why Ingested

The 2021 Cochrane update is the highest-tier evidence node for [[overviews/socket-preservation-arp-overview]] — its GRADE downgrade to "very low" certainty tempers the ARP-positive conclusions and supplies the critical-appraisal backbone for [[overviews/bone-regeneration-socket-biology-and-arp-critique]]. Doubles the trial pool (8→16 RCTs) while confirming no graft-material superiority.

## One-line Summary
2021 Cochrane update (.pub3) of the 2015 ARP review — 16 RCTs, 524 extraction sites, 426 participants; xenograft vs extraction alone shows reduced width loss (MD −1.18 mm) and height loss (MD −1.35 mm), but **certainty downgraded to "very low"** by GRADE; conclusions changed from 2015 — limited certainty about meaningful clinical benefit and no superiority among graft materials.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews 2021, Issue 4
- **Lead institution**: Mohammed bin Rashid University of Medicine and Health Sciences (Atieh)
- **Editorial group**: Cochrane Oral Health Group
- **Status**: New search & content updated (conclusions changed) — supersedes Atieh 2015 (.pub2)

## 2. Key Contributions
- **Doubled trial count** (8 → 16 RCTs) and added 6 new ARP-vs-extraction trials + 3 new material-vs-material trials.
- **GRADE certainty downgraded** from "moderate" (2015) to "very low" (2021) for the primary comparison → tempering of prior optimistic conclusions.
- Explicitly notes 7/8 included trials at unclear risk of bias and 4 at high risk → methodological caution is the headline.
- Confirms no convincing evidence for any single graft material superiority.

## 3. Methodology
- Cochrane Handbook protocol; risk of bias in duplicate; corresponding-author contact for missing data.
- 16 RCTs included; 524 extraction sites; 426 adult participants.
- Outcomes: alveolar ridge width/height loss, need for additional augmentation, implant failure, complications.
- Random-effects MD/RR with 95% CI; GRADE for certainty.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Xenograft vs extraction (7 trials → 6 in MA, 184 participants, 201 sites; very low certainty)**:
  - Width loss MD −1.18 mm (95% CI −1.82 to −0.54), p = 0.0003
  - Height loss MD −1.35 mm (95% CI −2.00 to −0.70), p < 0.0001
  - Need for additional augmentation: RR 0.68 (95% CI 0.29 to 1.62) — NS
  - Implant failure: RR 1.00 (95% CI 0.07 to 14.90) — uninformative
- **Alloplast vs extraction (1 trial, 15 participants, 60 sites; very low certainty)**:
  - Height loss MD −3.73 mm (95% CI −4.05 to −3.41) favouring alloplast
- **Different graft materials (allograft vs xenograft, alloplast vs xenograft, etc.)**:
  - 3 trials allograft vs xenograft (87 sites); inconsistent results; no clear superiority.
- **Quality**: 4 trials high risk of bias, 12 unclear; only 2/16 industry-free.
- **Authors' conclusion (changed from 2015)**: Limited certainty about meaningful clinical benefit of ARP over extraction alone; no convincing evidence for material superiority.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Heterogeneous defect morphology, surgical technique, follow-up period across trials.
- Most trials too small to detect clinically meaningful differences in patient-centred outcomes.
- Implant failure data nearly absent (long-term endpoints rarely reported).
- Need for adequately powered RCTs with standardized outcomes and longer follow-up.
