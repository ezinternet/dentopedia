## Why Ingested

Super-Bond 성분(4-META) surveillance — the wiki holds 10-MDP / IDS / titanium-bonding / saliva-contamination pages but had no canonical reference for the **4-META (4-methacryloxyethyl trimellitic anhydride)** monomer chemistry that defines the Super-Bond / 4-META-MMA-TBB resin family. This narrative review by Van Meerbeek's group lays out 4-META structure, its hydrolysis to 4-MET, pKa/acidity, etching behaviour, and HAp/collagen interaction, and benchmarks it head-to-head against 10-MDP and GPDM. It reinforces [[resin-bonding/fehrenbach-2021-10-mdp-bonding-performance-sr-ma]] (10-MDP superiority quantified) and feeds the cross-paper picture in [[overviews/adhesive-bonding-strategies-overview]].

## One-line Summary

Literature-based narrative review (PubMed/Scopus/LILACS, 2005–2025) of the physico-chemical properties of acidic functional monomers in contemporary adhesives; 10-MDP dominates (~50% of marketed adhesives) and outperforms 4-META/GPDM on bonding, hydrolytic stability and collagen interaction, while 4-META (carboxylic, pKa of 4-MET ≈ 2.9, hydrolyzes to 4-MET) remains a structurally weaker but still-used monomer.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Synthesizes the chemistry of the three "key player" acidic functional monomers — **10-MDP, GPDM, 4-META** — plus a wide roster of others (phenyl-P, 6-MHP, MAC-10, PENTA, Pyro-EMA, HEMA-P, MMEP, 4-AETA, PEM-F, MDTP).
2. Tabulates physico-chemical descriptors (MW, Log P, Log S; pKa) so monomers can be compared on a common basis.
3. Maps each monomer's chemical structure (acidic moiety, spacer-chain length, hydrophilic/hydrophobic balance) onto its etching efficacy, bond strength, and hydrolytic durability.
4. Establishes **10-MDP as the most reliable monomer** (present in ~50% of marketed adhesives; superior bonding, nano-layering, collagen complexation, hydrolytic stability), with **4-META and GPDM as structurally weaker but still-used alternatives**.
5. Provides the canonical positioning of **4-META** within the adhesive monomer landscape, including its conversion to 4-MET and its carboxylic (vs phosphate) bonding chemistry.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### 4-META / 4-MET chemistry (focus)

- **Identity & structure**: 4-META = 4-methacryloxyethyl trimellitic **anhydride**; a **carboxylic-acid functional monomer** that presents **two carboxylic acid groups** as its chemically active moiety (contrast with the phosphate group of 10-MDP, GPDM, phenyl-P). It carries the polymerizable methacrylate end plus the acidic trimellitic end.
- **Hydrolysis**: "**4-MET forms upon reaction of 4-META in aqueous solutions**" — i.e., the anhydride hydrolyzes to **4-MET** (4-methacryloxyethyl trimellitic **acid**) in water. The two are listed together (4-META/4-MET) in commercial composition tables.
- **Physico-chemical (Table 2)**:
  - 4-META — MW 304.2 g/mol, Log P 2.00, Log S −3.04 mol/L.
  - 4-MET — MW 322.3 g/mol, Log P 1.71, Log S −2.74 mol/L.
  - (cf. 10-MDP — MW 322.3, Log P 4.09, Log S −2.65; 10-MDP is far more hydrophobic, Log P 4.09 vs 2.00.)
- **pKa / acidity (Table 4)**: orthophosphoric acid 2.0; **10-MDP 2.2; 4-MET 2.9**; MMEP 3.1; MAC-10 4.7. So 4-MET is a **weaker acid than 10-MDP** (higher pKa → lower mineral-dissolution / etching power). Lower pH (more acidic monomer / higher concentration) improves etching but harms bottle shelf-life stability.
- **Etching / application time**: as with GPDM and 4-META, ~**20 s** active application is most efficient; <10 s gives inferior bond strength.
- **HAp interaction**: 4-MET (4-META) bonds to HAp via its **carboxylic** groups (ionic interaction), but its calcium salt is **less hydrolytically stable** than 10-MDP_Ca; phenyl-P_Ca and 4-META_Ca are more soluble than the very insoluble, nano-layering 10-MDP_Ca.
- **Collagen interaction**: 10-MDP was described to **stably interact with collagen, as opposed to 4-META** (10-MDP forms phosphate-collagen complexes / phosphorylated collagen; 4-META lacks this stable complexation).
- **Concentration / degree of conversion (Amaral et al 2016)**: experimental E&R adhesives with 12/20/30/40 wt% 4-META — 12 wt% gave the lowest DC; only **40 wt% 4-META maintained bonding performance after 1 y** (all maintained 6-month bond stability). High 4-META content improved 1-y bond stability.
- **Hydrolytic degradation**: both 4-META and 10-MDP undergo **ester cleavage** to hydrolyzed by-products with little/no function; 10-MDP documented to **outperform 4-META, GPDM and phenyl-P** on hydrolytic stability (immediate-to-HAp tests + thermocycling 30k/50k/100k of commercial adhesives).
- **Cytotoxicity**: 4-META has a **56× lower IC50 than UDMA, 170× lower than Bis-GMA (0.03 mmol/L), and ~2× higher IC50 than HEMA** (i.e., less cytotoxic than UDMA/Bis-GMA, somewhat more than HEMA); 4-META incorporated into experimental pulp-capping agents enhanced cell viability vs TheraCal LC.

### MMA / TBB context (Super-Bond family)

- **MMA** (methyl methacrylate) appears as a bulk/diluent monomer; TBB-initiated **10-MDP/MMA-TBB** self-polymerizing resins (Hiraba et al 2023) used as luting agents to enamel — 1.7–2.0 mol% 10-MDP optimal for non-etched enamel bond durability.
- **TBB (tri-n-butylborane)** appears as the self-cure initiator of the MMA-TBB resin system (the chemistry underpinning Super-Bond / C&B Metabond–type 4-META-MMA-TBB resins). Borate-based initiators and butanedione are noted as **more efficient than CQ/amine in the presence of acidic monomers** (acidic monomers inactivate amine co-initiators via acid-base reaction; TPO/BAPO phosphine-oxides also less sensitive).

### 10-MDP / GPDM benchmark (context)

- **10-MDP** = most prevalent (~50% of marketed adhesives), introduced 1980s; long linear 10-carbon spacer separates phosphate from methacrylate → self-assembles into ~1.9-nm nano-layers (10-MDP_Ca), superior bonding + hydrolytic stability + stable collagen complexation. Meta-analytic data (64 adhesives) — **10-MDP significantly outperforms 4-META, 4-MET, 6-MHP, PENTA and Pyro-EMA (P<0.01)**.
- **GPDM** — short spacer, small, hydrophilic; efficient for E&R but tends to **etch rather than adhere**, lower bonding efficacy.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative/qualitative synthesis — no pooled effect estimates; mixes peer-reviewed data with manufacturer technical sheets.
- Acidic-monomer **concentration** in commercial products is usually undisclosed, limiting cross-product comparison.
- Insufficient long-term clinical data on several newer monomers (PEM-F, 4-AET, MAC-10) to confirm equivalence to 10-MDP.
- Degradation pathways, biocompatibility, and long-term bonding efficacy flagged as open challenges for future formulation work.

## 7. Glossary

- **4-META** — 4-methacryloxyethyl trimellitic anhydride; carboxylic acidic functional monomer; hydrolyzes to 4-MET in water.
- **4-MET** — 4-methacryloxyethyl trimellitic acid; the hydrolyzed (di-carboxylic) acid form of 4-META (pKa ≈ 2.9).
- **10-MDP** — 10-methacryloyloxydecyl dihydrogen phosphate; long-spacer phosphate monomer, gold-standard (pKa ≈ 2.2).
- **GPDM** — glycerophosphate dimethacrylate; short-spacer hydrophilic phosphate monomer.
- **MMA** — methyl methacrylate; bulk/diluent monomer of the Super-Bond resin system.
- **TBB** — tri-n-butylborane; self-cure (chemical) polymerization initiator of MMA-TBB resins.
- **pKa** — acid dissociation constant; lower pKa = stronger acid = greater mineral-dissolution / etching capacity.
- **Log P / Log S** — octanol-water partition coefficient (hydrophobicity) / aqueous solubility coefficient.
- **HAp** — hydroxyapatite, tooth mineral phase.
- **AD/AE model** — adhesion-decalcification model of acidic-monomer interaction with mineralized tissue.
- **E&R / SE** — etch-and-rinse / self-etch bonding modes.
