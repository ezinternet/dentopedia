## One-line Summary
J Adv Prosthodont 2019;11:162-8: Y-TZP (Lava/Bruxzir) × 5 surface conditions × 500,000 cycles — glazed: roughness increases after simulation (glaze layer partially removed); polished zirconia caused MORE enamel wear than glazed/PVZ; material surface > finishing technique.

## 1. Key Results
- 2 Y-TZP brands (Lava, Bruxzir); 5 conditions: CPZ (control polished), GRZ (ground), RPZ (repolished), GZ (glazed), PVZ (porcelain-veneered)
- 500,000 cycles; 80N; artificial enamel (steatite) antagonist
- No brand effect (p=0.216); no brand effect on steatite loss (p=0.064)
- GZ roughness INCREASED after chewing simulation (glaze layer partially removed)
- Steatite loss: CPZ 0.09mm³ > PVZ 0.02mm³ = GZ 0.02mm³
- Polished zirconia caused MORE wear on artificial enamel than glazed or porcelain-veneered
- Intraoral polishing system did not reduce roughness adequately
