## Why Ingested

Provides the longest-term (15-year) RCT evidence on the fiber-vs-metal post question, complementing the SR+MA [[post-and-core/tsintsadze-2022-survival-rates-glass-fiber-metal-posts]]. The clinically important detail — metal posts fail more often by catastrophic root fracture while fiber posts fail more repairably — reinforces the modulus-matching rationale behind fiber posts that also motivates fiber-post bonding work in [[resin-bonding/dhawan-2026-bonding-total-self-etch-fiberpost]].

## One-line Summary

15-year RCT (131 patients, 182 restorations): glass-fiber posts trended toward higher success/survival (prefab 59.2%/68.5%) than metal posts (48.0%/53.6%), but post material had NO statistically significant effect; metal posts failed more by root fracture.

## 한줄요약

15년 추적 RCT(환자 131명, 수복 182건): 유리섬유 포스트가 금속 포스트보다 성공·생존율이 높은 경향(기성 59.2%/68.5% vs 금속 48.0%/53.6%)이었으나 포스트 재료 자체의 통계적 유의 효과는 없었고, 금속 포스트는 치근파절로 실패하는 비율이 더 높았음.

## 1. Document Information

Randomized controlled trial with 15-year follow-up, *Journal of Dentistry* (2025;156:105694). Abstract-only ingest. Control = metal post (Parapost XT); test = glass-fiber post (prefabricated Parapost Fiberlux for small canals; custom Everstick for wide oval canals). Outcomes by Kaplan-Meier success/survival curves corrected for clustering. Mean follow-up 179.6 ± 7 months; recall 91.6%.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Rare 15-year prospective randomized clinical data on post material.
- Showed a consistent trend favoring (especially prefabricated) glass-fiber posts in both success and survival.
- Documented differential failure mode: metal posts → more frequent root fracture (often non-restorable); fiber posts → more repairable failures.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

131 patients, 182 post + single-crown restorations randomized to metal vs fiber; fiber arm subdivided by canal morphology (prefab for small RC, custom for wide oval RC). Follow-ups at 3, 5, 15 years.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Success: metal 48.0% · prefab fiber 59.2% · custom fiber 49.5%.
- Survival: metal 53.6% · prefab fiber 68.5% · custom fiber 55.3%.
- No statistically significant effect of post material (low effect size).
- Metal-post failures more often due to root fracture.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

Trend did not reach significance (low effect size of material). Custom fiber posts (wide canals) performed closer to metal than prefab fiber did — canal morphology is a confounder. Abstract-only ingest precludes extraction of full Kaplan-Meier and subgroup detail.

## 6. Related Work

Long-term clinical confirmation of [[post-and-core/tsintsadze-2022-survival-rates-glass-fiber-metal-posts]]. The root-fracture failure mode echoes the catastrophic-failure pattern of rigid/metal restorations described in [[post-and-core/mously-2024-anterior-endocrowns-alternative-core-crown]].

## 7. Glossary

- **Prefabricated glass-fiber post** — factory-made fiber post for narrow round canals.
- **Custom glass-fiber post** — chairside-adapted fiber bundle (e.g. Everstick) for wide/oval canals.
- **Success vs survival** — success = original post still present; survival = tooth/restoration present after any reintervention.
