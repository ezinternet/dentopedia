## One-line Summary
SR finds only 3 interventions targeted at problem-orientated (pain-only) dental attenders — dental anxiety clinic, advertising campaign of free dental update, free dental check-ups (Scotland policy change). Reporting is poor; key future intervention targets identified: dentist communication, dentist-patient relationship, raising awareness, free check-ups.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Journal of Oral Rehabilitation, 48:1183–1191, 2021
- DOI: 10.1111/joor.13244
- Study type: Systematic review (no MA — only 3 studies meet inclusion)
- Open Access (CC-BY)
- Affiliation: Newcastle University School of Dental Sciences, UK
- Funding: NIHR

## 2. Key Contributions
- First SR specifically focused on **problem-orientated attenders** (the ~1/3 of UK population who only attend with pain)
- Surfaces the **interventional vacuum** in this patient segment
- Uses Theoretical Domains Framework (TDF) to map intervention design
- Names the 4 specific future intervention targets backed by what little evidence exists

## 3. Methodology
- Eight electronic databases
- Inclusion: any intervention targeted at adult problem-orientated/irregular dental attenders to encourage regular attendance
- Data extraction mapped to Theoretical Domains Framework (TDF)
- Effectiveness data and patient views extracted where available
- Quality assessment performed

## 4. Key Results
- **Only 3 studies** met inclusion criteria
- Identified interventions:
  1. Attendance at a dental anxiety clinic
  2. Large advertising campaign promoting a free dental update where members of the public could visit local dental practices and meet dentists
  3. Policy change introducing free dental check-ups in Scotland
- Interventions were **poorly reported** — significant omissions in description, lack of clarity about intervention components
- Future intervention targets identified from synthesis:
  - Dentist communication
  - Dentist-patient relationship
  - Increasing the awareness of need
  - Effect of free dental check-ups (cost barrier)

## 5. Limitations
- Only 3 included studies — extremely sparse evidence base
- Quality of included studies limited
- Heterogeneity of interventions precludes pooling
- UK-centric (Scotland policy specific)
- Problem-orientated attender definition varies across studies

## 7. Glossary
- **Problem-orientated attender**: patient who only attends dentist with acute pain or specific problem, not for regular check-ups
- **TDF (Theoretical Domains Framework)**: framework for understanding factors influencing behavior change
- **NIHR**: UK National Institute for Health Research
- **NHS**: UK National Health Service
