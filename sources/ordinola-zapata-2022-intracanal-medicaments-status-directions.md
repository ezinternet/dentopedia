## Why Ingested
Companion IEJ 2022 supplement review covering the inter-appointment phase of root canal treatment — when and why to use intracanal medicaments — directly extending [[endodontics/irrigation/boutsioukis-2022-irrigants-irrigation-methods-status-directions]] and providing the post-irrigation clinical decision framework not covered by [[endodontics/irrigation/khoury-2024-endodontic-irrigants-comprehensive-perspective-review]].

## One-line Summary
Narrative review examining calcium hydroxide and other intracanal medicaments, proposing a staging system for apical periodontitis severity to guide medicament selection and multi-appointment strategy, with ~19% of patients developing severe postoperative pain at 1 week cited as a key clinical trigger.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Proposes a four-stage classification of apical periodontitis (analogous to oncology staging) to stratify when intracanal medicaments are indicated.
- Argues that the binary AP model (present/absent) inadequately captures severity — staging should include bone loss extent, furcation involvement, cortical plate erosion, systemic modifiers.
- Establishes that calcium hydroxide remains the primary intracanal medicament: high pH (~12.5) disrupts bacterial cell membranes, inactivates LPS, and dissolves necrotic tissue.
- Quantifies postoperative pain incidence: ~19% of root canal patients develop severe pain 1 week post-treatment; chronic pain (6 months) reported in ~10%.
- Identifies AP staging-relevant prognostic factors from an 800-patient prospective study (Ng et al. 2011): AP presence, lesion size, preoperative sinus tract, interappointment flare-up, diabetes/steroid therapy, preoperative pain, narrow/deep periodontal probing.
- Antimicrobial peptides (AMPs) highlighted as promising next-generation medicaments; triple antibiotic paste (TAP) reviewed for regenerative endodontics.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Design: narrative review commissioned for the IEJ 2022 supplement.
- Focus: clinical indications for intracanal medicaments, calcium hydroxide mechanism and limitations, alternatives (triple antibiotic paste, AMPs, LSTR), AP staging model, future research directions.
- Key data sourced from in vitro models, animal studies, and cohort/prospective clinical studies (Ng et al. 2011, Yoldas et al. 2004, Law et al. 2015, Nixdorf et al. 2016).
- No formal systematic search or PRISMA.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Stage 1 AP: no or minimal apical changes; Stage 2: periapical lesion >5 mm with sinus tract; Stage 3: through-and-through bone loss, furcation involvement, J-shaped lesions; Stage 4: imminent tooth loss.
- Ng et al. (2011) cohort, n=800: prognostic factors for failure include AP presence, lesion size >5 mm, preoperative sinus tract, interappointment flare-up, diabetes or systemic steroid use, preoperative pain, narrow/deep probing depth.
- Law et al. (2015): ~19% of root canal patients experience severe pain at 1 week; predictors: baseline pain intensity, stress-worsened pain, pain interfering with daily activities, symptomatic AP.
- Nixdorf et al. (2016): chronic pain (≥6 months) following root canal treatment ~10%; days of preoperative pain predictive.
- Yoldas et al. (2004): two-visit retreatment with calcium hydroxide reduces postoperative pain and flare-up in previously symptomatic teeth (n=218).
- Calcium hydroxide pH ~12.5: bactericidal against most endodontic pathogens; less effective against Enterococcus faecalis and Candida albicans in biofilm.
- Medicament efficacy is unpredictable in the presence of debris — thorough debridement is a prerequisite.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Most antimicrobial data from laboratory/animal models; clinical outcome prediction is limited.
- Cohort designs (Ng et al.) prone to selection bias — complex cases more likely assigned multi-visit with medicament.
- Proposed AP staging system not yet validated in prospective RCTs.
- Future: validated AP staging RCTs; AMP clinical translation; biofilm-penetrating medicament formulations.

## 6. Related Work
- Boutsioukis & Arias-Moliz 2022 (IEJ): companion irrigation review — establishes the framework into which medicaments are inserted between appointments.
- Khoury 2024 (WJCC): mentions calcium hydroxide as one irrigant class in a broader overview.
- Sjögren et al. 1997, Byström et al. 1985: foundational studies on calcium hydroxide antimicrobial activity.

## 7. Glossary
- Ca(OH)₂: calcium hydroxide — high-pH (12.5) intracanal medicament. AP: apical periodontitis.
- LPS: lipopolysaccharide (endotoxin) — inactivated by high pH. TAP: triple antibiotic paste (ciprofloxacin + metronidazole + minocycline).
- AMP: antimicrobial peptide. LSTR: lesion sterilisation and tissue repair (Hoshino protocol).
- Flare-up: acute exacerbation between appointments with pain and/or swelling requiring unscheduled care.
