## Why Ingested

Ti mesh 대안의 핵심 비교 논문. **d-PTFE Ti 강화막 vs Ti mesh + 가교화 콜라겐막** 을 직접 무작위 비교한 유일한 RCT. 두 술식의 수직골증대량(4.2 vs 4.1 mm, NS)·합병증률(15.0 vs 21.1%, NS) 동등성을 확인하며, d-PTFE막의 조작 용이성과 Ti mesh의 신경 손상 위험을 문헌화. [[wiki/implants/vertical-ridge-augmentation/cucchi-2024-ptfe-mesh-ti-mesh-vertical-ridge-rct]] 이전 동일 저자의 선행 파일럿 RCT.

## One-line Summary

Pilot RCT (n=40, posterior mandible): d-PTFE Ti-reinforced membrane vs Ti mesh + cross-linked collagen → VBG 4.2 vs 4.1 mm (NS); complications 15.0 vs 21.1% (NS); d-PTFE simpler to handle (fewer surgical complications 5% vs 15.8%).

## 한줄요약

파일럿 RCT (n=40, 후방 하악): d-PTFE Ti 강화막 vs Ti 메쉬+가교화 콜라겐 → 수직골증대 4.2 vs 4.1 mm(NS); 합병증 15.0 vs 21.1%(NS); d-PTFE가 조작 더 간편(술중 합병증 5% vs 15.8%).

## 1. Document Information

- **Study type**: Pilot, parallel, double-blind RCT
- **Journal**: Clinical Implant Dentistry and Related Research
- **Year**: 2017 (epub 2017-07-26)
- **DOI**: 10.1111/cid.12520
- **PMCID**: PMC5655714
- **N**: 40 patients, 108 implants (simultaneous with GBR)
- **Healing**: 9 months submerged; posterior mandible only

## 2. Key Contributions

- First (and few) RCT directly comparing d-PTFE Ti-reinforced membrane vs Ti mesh + XL collagen for VRA
- Establishes clinical equivalence of both approaches for VBG and complication rates
- Quantifies Ti mesh handling disadvantage: more surgical complications (3× higher rate, NS) and surgical time
- Documents 16.3% VBG deficit per cm² of Ti mesh exposed
- Shows late exposure (>4 weeks) does not affect bone in either group
- Ti mesh: implants can be retained even when mesh removed early (vs d-PTFE where implants may be lost)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Groups: A = d-PTFE Ti-reinforced (Cytoplast Ti-250XL) vs B = Ti mesh (Trinon) + XL collagen (Osseoguard)
- One-stage: implants placed simultaneously with GBR at T0
- Graft: 50:50 autogenous bone (bone scraper) + allograft (EnCore)
- Decortication: yes
- Fixation: mini-screws; collagen membrane over Ti mesh in Group B
- VBG: UNC-15 probe at 4 sites per implant (mesial/distal/buccal/lingual)
- Complication classification: Fontana 2011 (surgical + healing; major vs minor)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Parameter | Group A (d-PTFE) | Group B (Ti mesh + XL collagen) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Surgical complications | 5.0% (1/20) | 15.8% (3/19) | 0.34 NS |
| Healing complications | 15.0% (3/20) | 21.1% (4/19) | 0.69 NS |
| Major complications | 10.0% | 15.8% | NS |
| VBG (mean) | 4.2 ± 1.0 mm | 4.1 ± 1.0 mm | 0.58 NS |
| ISQ at T0 | 87.0 | 84.5 | NS |
| ISQ at T1 | 71.0 | 66.5 | NS |

Ti mesh exposure: 16.3% deficit in bone volume per cm² of mesh exposed.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Pilot study — underpowered for definitive conclusions (designed to detect 35% difference in complications)
- Single center; single surgeon; Bologna posterior mandible only
- 9-month healing period (long)
- No long-term implant outcomes reported (data to T2 only)
- Cross-linked collagen used as COVER over Ti mesh — not standalone for VRA space maintenance

## 7. Glossary

- **d-PTFE**: dense polytetrafluoroethylene — non-porous, bacteria-resistant, open-healing capable
- **Osseoguard**: cross-linked collagen membrane (Zimmer Biomet)
- **Cytoplast Ti-250XL**: titanium-reinforced d-PTFE membrane (Osteogenics Biomedical)
- **Trinon**: Ti mesh manufacturer (Karlsruhe, Germany)
- **IBD**: initial bone defect (at T0) — distance from implant shoulder to first bone contact
- **FBD**: final bone defect (at T1 reopening)
- **VBG**: vertical bone gain = IBD − FBD
