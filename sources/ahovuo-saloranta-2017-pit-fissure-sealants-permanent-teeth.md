## Why Ingested
Cochrane 우식 예방 근거의 기준선(sealant vs no sealant)을 위키에 고정하기 위해 인제스트. 기존 [[caries/rashed-2022-sealant-vs-fluoride-varnish-caries-prevention-sr-ma]] 및 [[caries/chestnutt-2017-fissure-seal-fluoride-varnish-trial]]는 sealant와 fluoride varnish의 *상대* 효과를 다루나, 그 전제가 되는 "sealant가 무처치 대비 효과적"이라는 근거의 원전이 본 리뷰다. 자매편 [[caries/kashbour-2020-sealants-versus-fluoride-varnishes-decay]]와 한 쌍으로 묶인다.

## One-line Summary
Cochrane SR+MA (38 RCTs, 7924 children aged 5–16): resin-based fissure sealants reduced occlusal caries by 11–51% vs no sealant at 24 months (moderate-certainty evidence).

## 한줄요약
코크란 SR+MA (RCT 38편, 5–16세 아동 7,924명): 레진계 열구전색이 무전색 대비 24개월 시점 교합면 우식을 11–51% 감소시켰다(중등도 확실성).

## 2. Key Contributions
- Establishes the reference estimate that resin-based sealants prevent occlusal caries in first permanent molars vs no sealant (OR 0.12 at 24 months).
- Quantifies absolute benefit across baseline caries risk strata (16% / 40% / 70% control decay rates).
- Confirms benefit persists to ~48 months, with declining evidence quantity/quality thereafter.
- Documents that glass ionomer sealant vs no sealant, and one sealant vs another, remain unresolved (very low certainty).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: Systematic review of RCTs; random-effects meta-analysis when >3 trials, fixed-effect otherwise.
- **Population**: Children/adolescents ≤20 years; ≥12 months follow-up required.
- **Included**: 38 trials, 7924 children (7 trials new this update, 1693 participants).
  - Resin-based vs no sealant: 15 trials (3620 participants + 575 tooth pairs)
  - Glass ionomer vs no sealant: 3 trials (905 participants)
  - One sealant vs another: 24 trials (4146 participants)
- **Exclusions**: compomer vs resin/composite comparisons.
- **Outcome**: caries/no caries on occlusal surfaces (OR or RR); mean caries increment (MD); all with 95% CI.
- **Certainty**: GRADE. Blinding of outcome assessment rated high risk of bias in *all* trials (sealant is visible to assessor).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Resin-based sealant vs no sealant (primary)**:
  - 24 months: OR 0.12 (95% CI 0.08–0.19); 7 trials, 1322 children evaluated; **moderate certainty**.
  - Absolute effect by baseline risk: control decay 16% → 5.2% (CI 3.13–7.37); 40% → 6.25% (CI 3.84–9.63); 70% → 19% (CI 12.3–27.2).
  - 48–54 months: OR 0.21 (95% CI 0.16–0.28), 4 trials, 482 children; RR 0.24 (0.12–0.45), 203 children.
  - Headline: 11–51% relative caries reduction at 24 months.
- **Glass ionomer sealant vs no sealant**: inconclusive at 24 months (very low certainty).
- **One sealant vs another**: relative effectiveness unknown (very low certainty; 24 heterogeneous trials).
- **Adverse events**: 4 trials assessed; none reported.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- All trials at high RoB for outcome-assessment blinding (intrinsic to sealant visibility).
- Most resin-vs-no-sealant data derive from 1970s trials; only 2 modern (2010s) trials in the pooled estimate.
- Background fluoride exposure and baseline caries prevalence rarely reported → limits transportability to high-fluoride contemporary populations.
- GIC sealant and head-to-head material questions remain unresolved.

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary
- **OR/RR**: odds ratio / risk ratio. **MD**: mean difference. **DMFS/DMFT**: decayed-missing-filled surfaces/teeth.
- **GRADE**: evidence-certainty grading. **RoB**: risk of bias.
- **Resin-based sealant generations**: 2nd (UV-cured) / 3rd (visible-light) / 4th (fluoride-releasing).
