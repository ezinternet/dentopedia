## Why Ingested

Our cracked-tooth restorative-design evidence is dominated by comparative trials and syntheses — [[cracked-tooth/wang-2025-crown-vs-porcelain-inlay-cracked-teeth-rct]] (crown vs partial-coverage inlay) and [[cracked-tooth/zhang-2024-cracked-teeth-treatment-outcomes-sr-ma]] (pooled outcomes) — but we lacked a worked single-case illustration of the *minimally invasive* end of that spectrum: a bonded occlusal/laminate veneer placed in one visit immediately after root canal therapy. This case report documents the morphology-driven preparation technique (MDPT) with a lithium-disilicate occlusal veneer and short-fiber-reinforced composite (SFRC) access build-up, fleshing out the conservative restorative option that the comparative literature only references in aggregate. Single case (n=1) — illustrative, not evidence of effectiveness.

## One-line Summary

Single case report (n=1): a cracked endodontically treated maxillary first molar restored in one visit with a morphology-driven, minimally invasive lithium-disilicate occlusal veneer (butt-joint proximal margins + hollow-chamfer axial walls) over an SFRC access build-up; complete pain resolution and intact restoration at 15-month follow-up.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Case report (n=1), full text retrieved from PubMed Central (JATS).
- **Journal**: Case Reports in Dentistry, 2026, vol. 2026, article 5525524.
- **Published**: 2026-02-27.
- **Patient**: 36-year-old male, otherwise healthy, no reported bruxism.
- **Tooth**: maxillary right first molar (Tooth #16), cracked tooth syndrome with reversible pulpitis.
- **Ethics**: Fujian Provincial Governmental Hospital (Approval No. RL2024-02); written informed consent.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Worked clinical illustration of the **morphology-driven preparation technique (MDPT)** for a cracked molar — a minimally invasive, anatomy-guided alternative to uniform full-crown reduction.
- Demonstrates a **single-visit** workflow combining microscopic root canal therapy + chairside-fabricated bonded ceramic occlusal veneer, motivated by a patient-driven constraint (imminent relocation).
- Shows a specific material/margin combination: lithium-disilicate (IPS e.max Press) occlusal veneer with **butt-joint proximal margins (1.2–1.3 mm)** and **hollow-chamfer axial walls (20°–30° inclination)**, over a short-fiber-reinforced composite (SFRC) access build-up placed 1 mm sub-occlusally.
- Reports a 15-month follow-up with intact restoration and healthy periapical/periodontal status.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Single patient, descriptive case report. Diagnosis: cracked tooth syndrome + reversible pulpitis (#16); cold (+), no percussion/probing response, no mobility; crack confirmed by methylene blue staining; AAE classification — crack extending into the pulp chamber but not the pulpal floor (confirmed microscopically).

**Restorative — MDPT:**
- Proximal box: tapered cylindrical diamond bur (medium grit, 1.2-mm tip), walls reduced 1.2–1.3 mm, 90° cavosurface margins without bevel, internal angles rounded, natural proximal contacts preserved.
- Occlusal: anatomic reduction 1.0–1.5 mm with rounded-end bur following fissure pattern, silicone-index-guided.
- Buccal/palatal axial: hollow chamfer (20°–30°) coronal to the equator to expose enamel prisms for bonding; butt-joint if apical to the equator; transitions blended with curved "slip roads."
- Digital impression (3Shape TRIOS 5); monolithic e.max Press, min thickness 1.2 mm; shade 2R2.5 (VITA 3D-Master). Chairside fabrication during the endodontic phase.

**Endodontics:** rubber dam; access with diamond fissure bur + ultrasonic refinement (P5 XS Newtron); 16× microscope; working lengths MB-16, MB2-16, DB-17, P-19 mm (Raypex 6 + radiographs); Reciproc Blue R25/.06; 17% EDTA gel lubricant; irrigation 0.5% NaOCl + 17% EDTA with ultrasonic activation (Irrisafe); hybrid warm obturation (System B 200°C + Thermafil, 4-mm apical seal) with AH Plus sealer.

**Cementation:** intaglio etched 5% HF (20 s) + Monobond Plus silane (90 s); enamel 37% phosphoric acid (30 s); Single Bond Universal Adhesive; flowable composite + SFRC access build-up to 1 mm sub-occlusal (light-cured 20 s at 1200 mW/cm²); dual-cure RelyX Ultimate under finger pressure; sectional polywave LED curing; Sof-Lex finishing.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Trial verification: even occlusal contacts (8-µm articulating paper), marginal discrepancy < 40 µm (20× magnification), proximal contact passing floss with moderate resistance.
- Outcome: complete resolution of masticatory and spontaneous pain; functional and esthetic restoration achieved in a single visit.
- **15-month follow-up**: optimal restoration integrity, preserved tooth structure, healthy periodontal tissues; periapical radiograph normal.
- No quantitative comparative outcomes — single uncontrolled case; "benchmarks" cited (e.g., lithium-disilicate fracture toughness 2.75 MPa·m^½, SFRC modulus 12–15 GPa vs dentin ~18 GPa) are drawn from the authors' referenced literature, not measured here.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **n=1, uncontrolled, short follow-up (15 months)** — cannot establish effectiveness or compare against full-coverage crowns; subject to publication/selection bias inherent to case reports.
- No long-term data on the SFRC–ceramic interface under functional loading (authors flag this explicitly).
- Single operator, single center; generalizability across tooth types, crack depths, and patient populations is unknown.
- Authors call for comparative studies (MDPT vs conventional preparation) to validate the approach.

## 7. Glossary

- **MDPT (morphology-driven preparation technique)**: anatomy-guided, minimally invasive preparation customizing reduction to each tooth's morphology rather than a uniform reduction pattern.
- **Occlusal veneer**: bonded indirect ceramic restoration covering the occlusal surface with minimal axial reduction (here 1.0–1.5 mm), preserving more structure than a full crown.
- **Butt-joint margin**: 90° cavosurface margin without bevel (used proximally and apical to the equator).
- **Hollow chamfer**: concave bevel margin (20°–30° inclination) used coronal to the tooth equator to expose enamel prisms for adhesion.
- **SFRC (short-fiber-reinforced composite)**: fiber-filled resin composite with higher fracture resistance and a modulus (12–15 GPa) closer to dentin (~18 GPa), used here for the access-cavity build-up.
- **Lithium disilicate (IPS e.max Press)**: glass-ceramic with high translucency and HF-etchable/silanizable surface enabling adhesive "monoblock" bonding.
- **Monoblock effect**: integration of restoration, cement, and tooth into a mechanically unified complex through adhesion.
