## Why Ingested

기존 BMS 페이지들([[oral-medicine/canfora-2025-burning-mouth-syndrome-pathogenesis-review]], [[oral-medicine/gupta-2016-burning-mouth-syndrome-review]], [[oral-medicine/chmieliauskaite-2020-burning-mouth-syndrome-icd11-rename-delphi]])이 BMS를 신경병성으로 다루지만, "말초형 vs 중추형" 2-subtype 분류와 각 형의 치료 반응성(말초형=lidocaine block·topical clonazepam 반응, 중추형=무반응+정신과 동반)을 명시적으로 제시한 권위 있는 NeuPSIG 리뷰가 없었다. 임상 치료 선택 분기의 근거로 ingest.

## One-line Summary

NeuPSIG (PAIN) review concluding that primary BMS is a neuropathic pain condition with neurophysiologically verifiable lesions, divisible into peripheral and central subtypes (often overlapping) that differ in treatment response and psychiatric comorbidity.

## 한줄요약

NeuPSIG(PAIN) 리뷰 — 일차성 BMS는 신경생리 검사로 확인되는 신경병성 통증이며, 치료 반응과 정신과 동반이 다른 말초형·중추형 두 아형(개인 내 중첩 가능)으로 나뉜다고 결론.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Argues that, despite the absence of overt clinical neuropathy signs, sensitive diagnostic methods reveal neuropathic involvement at multiple levels of the neuraxis in primary BMS.
- Proposes a clinically actionable **two-subtype** model:
  - **Peripheral** neuropathic type — responds to peripheral lidocaine blocks and topical clonazepam.
  - **Central** type — does NOT respond to local treatment; frequently associated with psychiatric comorbidity (depression/anxiety) and deficient striatal dopamine function.
  - Subtypes may overlap within individual patients.
- Articulates a unifying pathophysiologic hypothesis: in postmenopausal women, decline in neuroprotective gonadal hormones + rise in stress hormones renders vulnerable small fibers and basal ganglia susceptible to neurotoxic damage.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Narrative/expert review synthesizing neurophysiologic evidence:
  - Peripheral small-fiber damage: thermal quantitative sensory testing (QST), electrogustatometry, epithelial nerve fiber density.
  - Trigeminal lesions (peripheral or brainstem): brainstem reflex recordings, trigeminal neurography, evoked potentials.
  - Decreased central inhibition: deficient brainstem reflex habituation, positive QST signs, neurotransmitter–PET indicating deficient striatal dopamine.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Qualitative review; no pooled statistics.
- Electrogustatometry abnormalities implicate small Aδ taste afferents in addition to somatosensory small fibers.
- Key clinical translation: subtype determines treatment — peripheral type is the one expected to respond to topical/peripheral interventions; central type needs centrally acting therapy and psychiatric attention.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Expert review, not systematic; small sample neurophysiologic studies underpin the subtype model.
- Subtype assignment requires specialized neurophysiologic testing not available in most dental settings.

## 7. Glossary

- BMS (Burning Mouth Syndrome): intraoral burning/dysaesthetic sensation recurring daily >3 months without clinically evident causative lesion (IHS 2013).
- QST (Quantitative Sensory Testing): standardized psychophysical testing of sensory/pain thresholds.
- Electrogustatometry: electrical measurement of taste function; abnormal in small Aδ taste afferent involvement.
- NeuPSIG: Neuropathic Pain Special Interest Group of IASP.
