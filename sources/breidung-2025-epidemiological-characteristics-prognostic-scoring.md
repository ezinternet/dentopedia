## Why Ingested

환자가 "피린계 진통제 알러지"를 호소할 때 그 임상적 무게를 근거로 답하기 위해 인제스트. 피린계(pyrazolone) 약물 과민반응의 가장 위중한 표현형은 SJS/TEN인데, 본 17년 화상센터 후향연구(n=68)에서 **메타미졸(metamizole)이 SJS/TEN의 단일 최다 원인약물(8건)**로 확인됐다 — 피린계 알러지가 단순 두드러기를 넘어 치명적 SCAR로 이어질 수 있음을 실증한다. 기존 진통제 부작용 페이지 [[drug/analgesics/magesty-2026-adverse-events-oral-analgesics-third-molar-nma]]를 위중도 측면에서 확장하고, 피린계 약리 페이지 [[drug/anticoagulants/hohlfeld-2008-pyrazolinone-aspirin-antiplatelet-pharmacology]]에 안전성 짝을 제공하며, SJS/TEN 감별 스펙트럼인 [[oral-medicine/kechichian-2024-erythema-multiforme-review]]와 연결된다.

## One-line Summary

17-year single-center retrospective (n=68 histopathology-confirmed SJS/TEN) in which metamizole (a pyrazolone analgesic) was the most frequent culprit drug (8 cases, ahead of allopurinol's 7); overall mortality 51%, and a CHAID classification tree (AUC 0.88) outperformed SCORTEN/Re-SCORTEN/ABCD-10 for mortality prediction.

## 한줄요약

17년 단일기관 후향연구(조직검사 확진 SJS/TEN 68명)에서 **피린계 진통제 메타미졸(metamizole)이 단일 최다 원인약물(8건, 알로푸리놀 7건보다 많음)**, 전체 사망률 51%, 분류트리 모델(AUC 0.88)이 기존 SCORTEN 계열 점수보다 사망예측 우수.

## 1. Document Information

- Journal: *Medicina (Kaunas, Lithuania)* 2025;61(1):66. Published 2025-01-03. MDPI, open access.
- Design: retrospective single-center cohort, Burn Unit, Klinikum Nuremberg Hospital, Paracelsus Medical University (Germany).
- Period: January 2006 – July 2023 (17 years).
- Funding/COI: not flagged in retrieved text (MDPI OA).
- PMID 39859048 · PMCID PMC11767146 · DOI 10.3390/medicina61010066.

## 2. Key Contributions

- **Metamizole = single most frequent SJS/TEN trigger (8/68 cases)** in this German cohort, ahead of allopurinol (7); pip/tazo and ciprofloxacin 4 each; ~20% idiopathic (no identifiable drug).
- Documents that the high metamizole share **contrasts with international literature** (where metamizole is rarely reported) and reflects regional prescribing — a pharmacovigilance/geography caveat.
- Independent mortality predictors at admission: higher body surface area (BSA), lower platelet count, higher creatinine and BUN (renal dysfunction).
- Novel gender-stratified predictors (hemoglobin, base excess) and COPD entered a CHAID classification tree (AUC 0.88) that beat SCORTEN (0.61), Re-SCORTEN (0.66), ABCD-10 (0.56).
- Among legacy scores, only **Re-SCORTEN** separated survivors vs non-survivors (3.0 vs 4.2; p=0.01).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- n=68 (33 survivors, 35 non-survivors); inclusion required **histopathological confirmation** of SJS/TEN (clinical diagnosis alone insufficient).
- Identified via ICD L51.20 (<30% BSA) / L51.21 (≥30% BSA).
- Severity split: SJS <10% BSA, SJS/TEN overlap 10–30%, TEN >30%.
- Variables from day of admission: demographics, comorbidities, presumed culprit drugs, clinical + laboratory parameters, prognostic scores.
- Statistics: Fisher's exact/Pearson, independent t-tests, Monte Carlo randomization, bootstrap logistic regression (OR, 95% CI), CHAID classification tree with 10-fold cross-validation, ROC/AUC comparison. p<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Mortality 51% (35/68).
- BSA at admission: survivors 43.4%±29.7% vs non-survivors 56.8%±26.2% (p<0.05).
- Distribution by severity: SJS 6% in each group; SJS/TEN overlap 36% (surv) vs 11% (non-surv), p=0.02; TEN 58% (surv) vs 83% (non-surv), p=0.02.
- Labs: platelet count differed (160.2 vs 221.4); creatinine 1.3±1.0 vs 1.9±1.8 mg/dL (p=0.05); BUN 30.9±19.7 vs 45.2±30.6 mmol/L (p=0.02). Hb, RDW, RDW/Hb, AST, ALT, CRP, procalcitonin, glucose, lactate, base excess, bicarbonate NS.
- Prognostic scores: Re-SCORTEN 3.0 vs 4.2 (p=0.01, only significant one); SCORTEN p=0.20; ABCD-10 p=0.65.
- Culprit drugs (26 identified across 68 pts): **metamizole 8**, allopurinol 7, piperacillin/tazobactam 4, ciprofloxacin 4; 14 cases (~20%) no definite drug.
- Classification tree (COPD, gender, Hb, base excess): accuracy 89%, NPV 81%, PPV 95%, sensitivity 93%, specificity 86%; AUC 0.88.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small single-center sample → limited generalizability; no external validation.
- Classification tree superiority **not proven** (95% CIs of AUCs overlap); authors explicitly say not yet for clinical decision-making.
- Possible uncaptured culprit drugs; ~20% idiopathic.
- Causality of metamizole is "presumed triggering medication," not challenge-confirmed — epidemiologic association, not mechanistic proof of pyrazolone-specific SCAR.
- Regional prescribing (Germany's heavy metamizole use) limits transfer of the metamizole-rank finding to other countries.

## 6. Related Work

- SCORTEN (Bastuji-Garin 2000), ABCD-10 (Noe 2019), Re-SCORTEN (Koh 2022) — the prognostic scores benchmarked.
- Torres-Navarro meta-analysis — SCORTEN overestimation, motivating reappraisal.

## 7. Glossary

- **SJS / TEN**: Stevens–Johnson syndrome / toxic epidermal necrolysis — severe cutaneous adverse reactions (SCAR) on a severity continuum by % BSA detachment (SJS <10%, overlap 10–30%, TEN >30%).
- **Metamizole (dipyrone)**: pyrazolone-class (피린계) non-opioid analgesic/antipyretic; the culprit drug ranked #1 here.
- **SCORTEN / Re-SCORTEN / ABCD-10**: validated severity-of-illness mortality scores for SJS/TEN.
- **Nikolsky sign**: epidermal separation on light tangential pressure; diagnostic hallmark.
- **CHAID classification tree**: chi-square automatic interaction detection decision-tree method used to model in-hospital mortality.
- **BSA / BUN**: body surface area / blood urea nitrogen.
