## One-line Summary
Cochrane overview of Cochrane reviews on single-dose oral analgesics for acute postoperative pain — 메타-메타분석으로 약물별 NNT (number needed to treat) 비교. Ibuprofen 400mg + APAP 1000mg 최저 NNT (~1.5); 단독 APAP·codeine·tramadol NNT 4-5.

## 1. Document Information
- Authors: Moore RA, Derry S, Aldington D, Wiffen PJ (Oxford pain group)
- Type: Cochrane overview of Cochrane reviews
- Citation: Cochrane Database Syst Rev 2015;9:CD008659.pub3

## 2. Key Contributions
1. Single most-cited evidence base for single-dose acute pain analgesic choice.
2. NNT framework: ibuprofen + APAP combination delivers lowest NNT.
3. Establishes APAP/codeine and tramadol as weak monotherapies for acute postoperative pain.

## 3. Methodology
Overview of multiple Cochrane SRs comparing single oral doses across all common analgesics for ≥50% pain relief vs placebo at 4-6 hours postoperatively.

## 4. Key Results
[근거강함] NNT for ≥50% relief at 4-6 h vs placebo:
- Ibuprofen 200mg + APAP 500mg: ~1.5 (most effective)
- Ibuprofen 400mg + APAP 1000mg: ~1.5
- Ibuprofen 400mg: ~2.5
- APAP 1000mg + codeine 60mg: ~2.2
- APAP 1000mg: ~3.5-4
- Codeine 60mg: ~12+ (very poor monotherapy)
- Tramadol 75mg: ~9

## 5. Limitations
- Mostly third molar / postoperative dental pain populations
- Single-dose only — does not address sustained dosing
- Pre-DOAC era; published 2015
