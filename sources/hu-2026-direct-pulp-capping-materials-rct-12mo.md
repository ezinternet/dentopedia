## Why Ingested

기존 보유 [[endodontics/vpt/]] 논문들이 Ca(OH)₂ vs MTA 중심; iRoot BP Plus(bioceramic)가 실제 임상에서 늘고 있어 3-way RCT 직접비교 근거 필요. [[endodontics/vpt/kundzina-2017-mta-vs-calcium-hydroxide-dpc-rct]]가 MTA vs CH 2-way RCT인데 본 논문이 iRoot BP Plus를 추가한 3-way 비교로 확장한다.

## One-line Summary
Single-blind RCT (n=120, Shanghai) comparing iRoot BP Plus, MTA, and Ca(OH)₂ for direct pulp capping in mature permanent teeth with caries-induced reversible pulpitis: no significant difference at 12 months (iRoot BP Plus 88.5%, MTA 93.1%, Ca(OH)₂ 84.6%; p=0.6); proximal lesion location was the dominant risk factor for failure (OR=18.18).

## 한줄요약
성숙 영구치의 우식 유발 가역성 치수염(Reversible Pulpitis)에서 직접치수복조(Direct Pulp Capping, DPC) 재료 3종 — iRoot BP Plus(바이오세라믹, Bioceramic), MTA(Mineral Trioxide Aggregate), 수산화칼슘(Calcium Hydroxide, CH) — 을 비교한 단맹 무작위 대조 연구(Randomized Controlled Trial, RCT)(n=120, 상하이): 12개월 성공률 iRoot BP Plus 88.5%, MTA 93.1%, CH 84.6%로 세 군 간 유의한 차이 없음(p=0.6); 근심면 국한 우식(Proximal Lesion)이 실패의 가장 강력한 예측인자(OR=18.18).

## 1. Document Information

- **Title:** Clinical Evaluation of Three Direct Pulp Capping Materials in Caries‐Induced Pulpitis of Mature Permanent Teeth: A Randomized Controlled Trial
- **Authors:** Hu et al. (corresponding institution: Department of Endodontics, Shanghai Ninth People's Hospital, Shanghai Jiao Tong University School of Medicine, China)
- **Year:** 2026
- **Journal:** Clinical and Experimental Dental Research (Clin Exp Dent Res)
- **DOI:** [10.1002/cre2.70367](https://doi.org/10.1002/cre2.70367) — Based on PubMed (PMID: 42188992, PMCID: PMC13239863)
- **Trial Registration:** ChiCTR2100047588 (Chinese Trials Registry)
- **Study Design:** Single-center, single-blind, parallel-group, superiority RCT
- **Recruitment:** July 2021 – July 2024
- **Data Cutoff:** November 30, 2025
- **Median Follow-up:** 364 days (12 months primary endpoint; 24-month secondary data available)

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First 3-way RCT** directly comparing iRoot BP Plus (bioceramic), ProRoot MTA, and Calxyl Ca(OH)₂ in mature permanent teeth with caries-induced pulp exposure.
2. **No material superiority at 12 months**: all three materials achieved clinically acceptable success rates (84.6%–93.1%; p=0.6). iRoot BP Plus performs equivalently to MTA without its handling and esthetic disadvantages.
3. **Proximal lesion location dominates failure risk** (OR=18.18 at 12 months; p=0.001): shifts clinical attention from material selection to case selection criteria.
4. **Additional risk factors at 6 months**: exposure size >1 mm (OR=9.2; p=0.024) and anterior tooth location (OR=10.7; p=0.018).
5. **Kaplan–Meier survival**: overall 89.3% at 12 months (95% CI: 82.8%–96.4%); no log-rank difference between materials (p=0.66).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

### Patient Selection
- **Inclusion:** Adults ≥18 years; mature permanent teeth; deep caries >2/3 dentin thickness without spontaneous pain; reversible pulpitis symptoms (cold/sweet discomfort resolving within seconds); normal periapical status on X-ray; pulp exposure confirmed intraoperatively
- **Exclusion:** Non-restorable teeth, vertical root fracture, root resorption, pulp necrosis, periapical lesions, furcation involvement, pregnancy, hemostasis failure within 5 min intraoperatively
- **n=120** (40 per group): powered for 90% power at α=0.05 to detect differences based on expected rates of 98% (iRoot BP Plus), 86% (MTA), 65% (CH); 20% dropout assumed → 40/group enrolled

### Procedure (Standardized)
- Single experienced endodontist (>10 years VPT experience) performed all DPC
- Dental operating microscope used throughout
- Rubber dam isolation mandatory
- Hemostasis: 3% NaOCl soaked sterile cotton pellet; exclusion if >5 min
- Capping layer: minimum 1.5 mm thickness
- Restored with light-cure GIC (Vitrebond) + temporary GIC (Fuji II); permanent restoration at 1 month if asymptomatic
- Materials used: Calxyl (Ca(OH)₂), ProRoot MTA (Dentsply Sirona), iRoot BP Plus (Innovative Bioceramix)

### Blinding
- Single-blind: patients and outcome assessors blinded; operator not blinded
- Two independent calibrated endodontists evaluated outcomes independently

### Follow-up Schedule
- 1 week (telephone, VAS pain), 1 month, 3, 6, 12, 24 months
- Clinical: palpation, percussion, cold test, EPT
- Radiographic: PAI score (periapical index) by 2 blinded examiners

### Success Criteria (Combined Clinical + Radiographic)
- **Clinical success:** No spontaneous pain, no tenderness to percussion, no swelling/sinus tract, positive pulp vitality response (cold/EPT)
- **Radiographic success:** No periapical lesions, no internal/external root resorption

### Statistics
- Chi-square / Fisher's exact for group comparisons
- Kaplan–Meier + log-rank for survival analysis
- Per-protocol analysis; drop-outs censored in survival analysis
- R version 4.5.0

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Primary Outcome: 12-Month Success

| Material | n analyzed | Success | Failure | Success Rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| iRoot BP Plus | 26 | 23 | 3 | **88.5%** |
| MTA | 29 | 27 | 2 | **93.1%** |
| Ca(OH)₂ | 26 | 22 | 4 | **84.6%** |
| **Overall** | **81** | **72** | **9** | **88.9%** |

p=0.6 (no significant difference between groups)

### Success Over Time (All Groups Combined)

| Timepoint | Success Rate |
|---|---|
| 3 months | 96.0% |
| 6 months | 93.2% |
| 12 months | 88.9% |
| 24 months | 72.7% |

### Kaplan–Meier Survival (12 months)

| Material | Cumulative Survival (95% CI) |
|---|---|
| iRoot BP Plus | 87.7% (74.6%–100%) |
| MTA | 93.5% (85.3%–100%) |
| Ca(OH)₂ | 85.7% (73.5%–100%) |
| **Overall** | **89.3% (82.8%–96.4%)** |

Log-rank p=0.66 — no significant difference between materials

### Prognostic Factors

| Factor | Timepoint | p-value | OR / Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| Proximal surface only (lesion location) | 12 months | **0.001** | OR=18.18 |
| Proximal surface only (lesion location) | 6 months | **0.002** | all failures proximal |
| Proximal surface only (lesion location) | 3 months | **0.012** | — |
| Anterior vs posterior tooth | 6 months | **0.018** | OR=10.7 |
| Anterior vs posterior tooth | 3 months | **0.049** | — |
| Exposure size >1 mm | 6 months | **0.024** | OR=9.2 |
| Age, sex, arch, bleeding time, cold test | all | NS | — |

Note: Anterior teeth were strongly correlated with proximal lesion location (OR=11.85), so these two risk factors cannot be interpreted independently.

### Dropout
- 39 of 120 patients lost to follow-up (32.5%) — exceeds the planned 20% allowance; attributed largely to COVID-19 disruptions in follow-up scheduling. Baseline characteristics remained balanced between retained and lost participants.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

1. **High dropout rate (32.5%)** exceeded planned 20%; reduced statistical power to detect smaller between-group differences. COVID-19 disrupted routine follow-up scheduling.
2. **Short follow-up**: Most patients have only 12-month data; material stability and delayed complications not fully evaluable. 24-month data available for a subset only.
3. **Underpowered for multivariable modeling**: small final cohort prevented regression controlling for confounding (especially the anterior teeth–proximal lesion correlation).
4. **Single center and single operator**: limits generalizability; selection bias cannot be excluded.
5. **Future studies** should stratify randomization by lesion location, enroll larger samples, and extend follow-up to ≥5 years to assess material durability and pulp calcification patterns.

## 6. Related Work

- **Barthel et al.**: CH long-term failures — 37% success at 5 years, 13% at 10 years in caries-related DPC (historical context for why newer materials were needed)
- **Kundzina et al.**: MTA 85% vs CH 52% at 3 years in RCT — one RCT avoided per 3 MTA-treated cases; contrast with this study's equivalent results, possibly due to CH formulation differences (Dycal in Kundzina vs Calxyl here)
- **Çalışkan and Güneri**: MTA vs CH no significant difference at 24 months (93% vs 90%), consistent with this study
- **Brizuela et al.**: MTA, CH, Biodentine no difference in young permanent teeth at 12 months
- **Chen et al. (iRoot BP Plus cohort)**: 92% at 1 year, 72% at 5 years in 354 teeth — consistent with this RCT's 88.5% at 12 months
- **Prasertsuksom et al. (SR)**: 83% overall DPC success in permanent teeth over 2 decades — this RCT is above that benchmark

## 7. Glossary

- **직접치수복조 (Direct Pulp Capping, DPC)**: Application of a capping material directly onto mechanically or caries-exposed vital pulp tissue to stimulate reparative dentin formation and preserve pulp vitality
- **생활치수요법 (Vital Pulp Therapy, VPT)**: Umbrella term for procedures preserving pulp vitality: indirect pulp capping, direct pulp capping, partial pulpotomy, full pulpotomy
- **바이오세라믹 재료 (Bioceramic Material)**: Calcium silicate-based materials (e.g., iRoot BP Plus, Biodentine) that are biocompatible, bioactive, and promote apatite formation; do not cause tooth discoloration unlike MTA
- **iRoot BP Plus**: Premixed, ready-to-use calcium silicate bioceramic capping material (Innovative Bioceramix, Vancouver, Canada); main advantage over MTA: no discoloration, easier handling, no mixing required
- **MTA (Mineral Trioxide Aggregate)**: Gold-standard bioactive endodontic cement for pulp capping; drawbacks include high cost, long setting time, technique sensitivity, potential tooth discoloration
- **수산화칼슘 (Calcium Hydroxide, CH)**: Traditional pulp capping material; highly alkaline (antimicrobial), but dissolves over time causing microleakage and reduced long-term success
- **가역성 치수염 (Reversible Pulpitis)**: Pulp inflammation where vitality can be preserved if the irritant is removed; characterized by brief pain response to cold/sweet stimuli
- **근첨주위지수 (Periapical Index, PAI)**: Radiographic scoring system (1–5) for periapical health; PAI 1–2 = healthy/minimal changes; PAI ≥3 = pathology
- **지혈 (Hemostasis)**: Cessation of bleeding at the pulp exposure site; required within 5 min using 3% NaOCl in this protocol; failure indicates excessive pulpal inflammation → exclusion
- **근심면 우식 (Proximal Caries)**: Caries located on the mesial or distal surface of a tooth; in this study, lesions confined to the proximal surface had dramatically higher DPC failure rates (OR=18.18)
