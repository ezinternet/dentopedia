## Why Ingested

[[endodontics/irrigation/mohammadi-2019-smear-layer-irrigants-review]]의 irrigant 항균 논의에 lubricant(윤활제/chelating paste)의 항균성 데이터를 추가. Glyde·RC-Prep 등 paste가 항균 측면에서 irrigant에 비빌 수 있는지 in vitro 비교.

## One-line Summary
In vitro study comparing antibacterial activity of 10 root-canal lubricants and 7 irrigants against E. faecalis (and others); several lubricants matched some irrigants, with activity not correlated to pH (2.9–10.3).

## 한줄요약
In vitro: 근관 윤활제 10종과 세정제 7종의 항균력(E. faecalis 등) 비교 — 일부 윤활제가 일부 세정제에 필적, 활성은 pH(2.9–10.3)와 무관.

## 1. Document Information
- Australian Endodontic Journal 2014;40(3):111–115.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Quantifies antibacterial properties of lubricants alongside irrigants.
- Shows antibacterial activity independent of pH.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Serial dilutions in tryptic soy broth inoculated with E. faecalis (and others); 10 lubricants (KY Jelly, RC-Prep, File-Eze, Glyde, SlickGel, etc.) vs 7 irrigants.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Several lubricants showed antimicrobial activity comparable to some irrigants.
- Three irrigants (incl. Consepsis) strongest; activity did not correlate with pH (2.9–10.3).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- In vitro planktonic; not biofilm/clinical; single-species focus.

## 6. Related Work
- Lubricant-focused complement to irrigant antimicrobial reviews.

## 7. Glossary
- Root canal lubricant: gel/paste (often EDTA-based) easing instrumentation.
- E. faecalis: persistent endodontic pathogen.
