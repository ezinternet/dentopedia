## One-line Summary
J Dent Res 2014: SR+MA of RCTs; alveolar ridge preservation (socket grafting) vs extraction alone — buccolingual width +1.89mm, midbuccal height +2.07mm; flap elevation + membrane + xenograft/allograft associated with superior outcomes.

## 1. Key Results
- Multiple electronic databases; RCTs only; non-molar teeth
- ARP vs extraction alone:
  - Buccolingual width: +1.89mm (95%CI: 1.41–2.36; p<.001)
  - Midbuccal height: +2.07mm (95%CI: 1.03–3.12; p<.001)
  - Midlingual height: +1.18mm (95%CI: 0.17–2.19; p=.022)
  - Mesial height: +0.48mm (95%CI: 0.18–0.79; p=.002)
  - Distal height: +0.24mm (NS; p=.102)
- Subgroup analyses: flap elevation, membrane use, xenograft/allograft = superior midbuccal and midlingual height preservation
- University of Iowa

## 2. Clinical Implications
- ARP is effective; all dimensions improved except distal height
- Flap elevation + membrane + xenograft/allograft = optimal ridge preservation protocol
- Non-molar sites only — results may not generalize to molar extraction
