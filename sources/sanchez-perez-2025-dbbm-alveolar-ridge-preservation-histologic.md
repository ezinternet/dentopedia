## One-line Summary
Human split-mouth study of techBiomat bone® DBBM for alveolar ridge preservation: 87% of treated sockets achieved >75% bone fill (vs 7% untreated); new bone 42% vs 25%; residual graft <9% at 6 months.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Biomedicines 2025, 13, 1358 (received 29 Apr 2025; accepted 28 May; published 31 May).
- **Study type**: Randomized split-mouth, human, histologic + radiographic (CBCT) outcomes at 6 months.
- **Setting**: University of Murcia (Spain). Manufacturer (Technology in Biomaterials S.L., Barcelona) supplied graft only; no role in design/analysis.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Validates techBiomat bone® (alternative DBBM) for alveolar ridge preservation with both CBCT bone fill grades and histologic new bone fractions.
- Provides a 4-grade CBCT bone fill scoring system (>75%, 50–75%, 25–50%, <25%) usable in clinical practice.
- Confirms low residual graft (<9% at 6 months) — favorable resorption profile.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- ASA I/II adults requiring ≥2 non-contiguous extractions; bilateral split-mouth randomization (random.org sealed envelope).
- Test socket: completely filled with techBiomat bone® (DBBM, 0.25–1.68 mm granules), prehydrated saline; coronally advanced flap for primary closure.
- Control: untreated socket, same closure.
- 6-month healing → CBCT (90 kV, 8 mA, 0.16 mm voxel) + 2 mm trephine bone core during implant osteotomy → 10% formaldehyde → histology.
- Endpoints: CBCT bone fill grade (I–IV); histologic new bone (%), residual graft (%), non-mineralized tissue (%).
- Implant system: Ticare Inhex® (20 Ncm IT, 800 rpm under irrigation).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Radiographic (CBCT)**: 87% of treated sockets ≥ Grade I (>75% bone fill) vs 7% of untreated.
- **Histologic new bone**: 42% (test) vs 25% (control).
- **Residual graft**: <9% at 6 months — near-complete replacement.
- **Non-mineralized tissue**: lower in test — improved healing quality.
- Resorption rate of techBiomat bone® classified as "moderate, conducive to optimal regeneration."

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Small sample (split-mouth design, manufacturer-supplied material).
- Single timepoint (6 months) — long-term remodeling and implant outcome data needed.
- Single product (techBiomat bone®) — generalization to other DBBM brands not direct.
- No head-to-head against Bio-Oss; comparator is untreated socket only.

## 6. Related Work
- Atieh 2015 (Cochrane) — xenograft ARP vs extraction: -2.60 mm height, -1.97 mm width.
- Bassir 2018 — ARP SR+MA: horizontal preservation 1.86 mm.
- Manfro 2013 — DBBM products vary substantially in performance.
- Signoriello 2025 — DBBM ± calcium sulphate for socket preservation.

## 7. Glossary
- **DBBM (Deproteinized Bovine Bone Mineral)**: Anorganic bovine bone, osteoconductive scaffold; Bio-Oss is prototypical.
- **ARP (Alveolar Ridge Preservation)**: Graft ± membrane at extraction to limit physiologic ridge resorption.
- **Split-mouth design**: Same patient receives test and control interventions on contralateral sites — controls for inter-patient variability.
- **Bone fill grade (this study)**: I >75%, II 50–75%, III 25–50%, IV <25%.
