## Why Ingested

이 논문은 수중방전 플라즈마(Underwater Discharge Plasma, UDP)를 근관세정제로 임상 적용한 **최초의 RCT**로, 기존 in vitro 연구들의 임상 번역 가능성을 직접 검증한다. 기존 근관치료 세정제 관련 wiki 페이지 [[endodontics/irrigation]]에서 NaOCl의 한계(세포독성, 해부학적 복잡 부위 침투 불가)를 다루는 맥락에서, UDP가 임상적으로 동등한 효과를 보이는지 판단할 근거를 제공한다.

## One-line Summary

Prospective RCT pilot (n=28, 4-month follow-up) comparing underwater discharge plasma vs. 6% NaOCl as root canal irrigant — comparable VAS pain reduction and periapical healing (PAI), no adverse effects with UDP.

## 한줄요약

전향적 무작위 대조 파일럿 RCT (n=28, 4개월 추적): 수중방전 플라즈마(UDP)와 6% 차아염소산나트륨(NaOCl) 근관세정 비교 — 통증(VAS)과 치근단 치유(PAI) 모두 동등하며 UDP 부작용 없음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Biomedicines 2025, 13, 2343
- **Institution**: Hallym University Sacred Heart Hospital / Graduate School of Clinical Dentistry, Hallym University, Republic of Korea
- **Study design**: Prospective, single-operator, parallel-group randomized controlled pilot trial
- **Ethics**: IRB No. 2024-03-016 (Hallym University Sacred Heart Hospital); Declaration of Helsinki
- **Registration**: Not specified
- **Follow-up**: 4 months (preop, 1 week post-fill, 2 months, 4 months)

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **First prospective clinical RCT** evaluating underwater discharge plasma (UDP) as a root canal irrigant in human patients.
2. Demonstrated that UDP achieves **short-term clinical efficacy and safety comparable to 6% NaOCl** — VAS pain and PAI healing outcomes non-inferior at all time points.
3. Validated **real-time impedance feedback safety mechanism** (cutoff >5000 Ω) that prevents thermal injury; no thermal damage reported in any UDP case.
4. Quantified 4-month healing rates: UDP 71.4% vs. NaOCl 92.9% (p=0.326, not significant) — numerically lower for UDP, warranting larger trials.
5. Positioned UDP device (PLAZEN RCT®; Dentory, Seoul) as a biocompatible alternative with acceptable safety profile for clinical use.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Participants**: 30 adults recruited at Hallym University Sacred Heart Hospital (Aug–Dec 2024); 28 completed (2 lost to follow-up, 1 per group). Inclusion: age 19–90, restorable teeth requiring RCT. Exclusion: uncontrolled diabetes, immunocompromised, pregnancy, pacemaker/cochlear implant, moderate-severe periodontitis.

**Groups**:
- UDP group (n=14): Root canal filled with saline → K-file 3 mm short of WL → plasma discharge via PLAZEN RCT® device → NiTi rotary shaping (VDW.ROTATE, up to 25/0.06) → second plasma discharge before obturation.
- NaOCl group (n=14): 6% NaOCl (Sense Cleaner; Sunjin Bio) with same rotary protocol.
- Both groups: MTA-based sealer (Ceraseal®), calcium hydroxide intervisit if needed, resin composite final restoration 1 week post-obturation.

**UDP mechanism**: High-frequency, high-voltage electrical discharge in aqueous medium generates hydroxyl radicals (•OH), H₂O₂, ozone (O₃) through water molecule ionization, cavitation (microbubble formation/collapse), and filamentary discharge. Temperature <40°C. Impedance monitoring auto-stops at >5000 Ω; discharge limited to 1.5 s per activation.

**Outcome measures**:
- Primary: VAS pain (0–10) at preop, 1 week, 2 months, 4 months
- Primary: PAI score (1–5) at preop, immediately post-fill, 2 months, 4 months
- Secondary: Clinical success rate (PAI ≤2), inter-rater reliability (quadratic-weighted Cohen's kappa)

**Statistics**: Kolmogorov-Smirnov normality test → non-parametric; Friedman test (within-group VAS change); Mann-Whitney U (between-group VAS/PAI); Fisher's exact (healing rates). Alpha=0.05.

**Sample size**: G*Power v3.1.9.4; n=30 achieves power=0.65, effect size=0.9, α=0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Pain (VAS)**:
- Both groups showed significant within-group VAS reduction over time (UDP: χ²=11.28, p=0.010; NaOCl: χ²=10.17, p=0.017).
- No significant between-group difference at any time point overall (all p>0.05).
- At 4 months: UDP 0.07±0.27 vs. NaOCl 0.14±0.54 (p=0.000 — note: statistically significant but clinically negligible, both near zero).

**Periapical Healing (PAI)**:
- PAI scores decreased over time in both groups.
- No significant between-group difference at any time point (all p>0.05).
- 4-month mean PAI: UDP 1.66±0.79 vs. NaOCl 1.43±0.62 (p=0.401).

**Clinical Success (PAI ≤2)**:
- Canal filling: UDP 50.0% vs. NaOCl 78.6% (p=0.236)
- 2 months: UDP 57.1% vs. NaOCl 78.6% (p=0.420)
- 4 months: UDP 71.4% (10/14) vs. NaOCl 92.9% (13/14) (p=0.326)
- Overall healing rate: 82.1% (23/28)

**Inter-rater reliability**: Quadratic-weighted κ=0.721 (substantial agreement) for PAI radiographic readings.

**Safety**: No adverse effects or thermal damage reported in UDP group. Impedance feedback system successfully prevented overheating.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Pilot study**: Small sample (n=28) → underpowered (0.65) for definitive non-inferiority conclusions.
- **Short follow-up**: 4 months insufficient to assess long-term periapical healing (standard RCT follow-up is ≥1 year).
- **Single operator**: Limits generalizability across operators with different skill levels.
- **No microbiological sampling**: Bacterial cultures not taken — antimicrobial efficacy inferred from clinical/radiographic outcomes only, not direct microbiological confirmation.
- **Device-specific**: Results apply to PLAZEN RCT® system; other UDP devices may differ in output parameters.
- **Future**: Larger multicenter RCTs with ≥1-year follow-up and direct microbiological endpoints recommended.

## 6. Related Work

- In vitro UDP antimicrobial studies: Akiyama et al. (pulsed discharge plasma ROS generation), prior E. coli / V. cholerae studies.
- NaOCl cytotoxicity and dentin weakening at high concentrations — established limitation driving alternatives.
- Non-thermal plasma (NTP) antimicrobial effects vs. E. faecalis and C. albicans (cited prior in vitro evidence).
- PAI scoring system: Ørstavik et al. (PAI 1–5 classification).
- ESE S3 clinical practice guidelines: pain and PAI as core post-treatment outcome parameters.
- Hallym University / Dentory (Seoul) — device developed and studied at same institution (potential COI).

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| UDP (Underwater Discharge Plasma, 수중방전 플라즈마) | Plasma generated by high-frequency, high-voltage electrical discharge within an aqueous medium inside the root canal |
| NaOCl (Sodium Hypochlorite, 차아염소산나트륨) | Standard chemical root canal irrigant with antimicrobial and tissue-dissolving properties; 6% concentration used in this study |
| PAI (Periapical Index, 치근단 지수) | 5-point radiographic scoring system for periapical health (1=normal, 5=severe periodontitis with exacerbating features) |
| VAS (Visual Analog Scale, 시각적 상사 척도) | 0–10 pain intensity scale |
| ROS (Reactive Oxygen Species, 활성산소종) | Oxidative molecules (•OH, H₂O₂, O₃) generated by plasma discharge with antimicrobial activity |
| Cavitation (공동화) | Localized formation and collapse of microbubbles in the aqueous medium, generating heat and mechanical shockwaves |
| Impedance feedback (임피던스 피드백) | Safety mechanism auto-stopping power delivery at >5000 Ω to prevent thermal injury |
| PLAZEN RCT® | Commercial UDP device (Dentory, Seoul) used in this study; IEC 60601-2-2 compliant |
| NiTi rotary (니켈-타이타늄 회전 파일) | Nickel-titanium rotary instrument for root canal shaping (VDW.ROTATE used here) |
| MTA sealer (MTA 실러) | Mineral trioxide aggregate-based canal sealer (Ceraseal® used in this study) |
