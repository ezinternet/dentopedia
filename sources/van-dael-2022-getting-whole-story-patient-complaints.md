## Why Ingested
Shows the downstream payoff of structured complaint classification ([[complaint-management/bmjqs-2015-004596]]): coded complaints linked to staff incident reports surface safety signals staff miss. Reframes complaints as a patient-safety data source, not just a service grievance.

## One-line Summary
Retrospective linkage of 5 years of complaints and staff incident reports at a London hospital; overlapping cases (n=446) carried higher harm and revealed events staff missed.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal of Health Services Research & Policy 2022;27(1):41-49.
- NIHR Imperial Patient Safety Translational Research Centre + LSE.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Demonstrates patient-level data linkage between complaints and staff incident reports.
- Shows complaints capture surrounding safety events (n=582) that staff reports omit.
- Finds complaints describe greater harm in 60% of linked cases and contesting accounts in 46%.
- Argues for triangulating heterogeneous viewpoints to understand unsafe care.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Five years of patient complaints and staff incident reports from a large multi-site London hospital. Retrospective patient-level data linkage identified overlapping reports. Mixed quantitative coding + in-depth qualitative comparison of harm level, missed adjacent events, and combined narratives.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
Overlapping incidents n=446 (7.6% of complaints, 0.6% of incident reports) but disproportionately Serious Incidents and severe harm. Linked complaints reported greater harm in 60% of cases, surfaced 582 surrounding safety events missed by staff, gave contesting causal accounts in 46% and complementary accounts in 26%.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
Single institution; linkage relies on identifiable overlap (small fraction of records). Retrospective. Generalisability to primary care/dentistry untested.

## 6. Related Work
Applies HCAT-style coding (Gillespie & Reader 2016) to safety science. Same group as the national policy critique (Van Dael 2022, JRSM).

## 7. Glossary
- **Data linkage**: matching records about the same patient/event across separate systems.
- **Serious Incident (SI)**: NHS designation for events requiring formal investigation.
