## Why Ingested

Part of a focused collection on flap design and tension-free primary wound closure over GBR / titanium mesh. This RCT quantifies how a coronally advanced lingual flap (CALF) eliminates titanium-mesh exposure in the posterior mandible, complementing periosteal-release-based advancement methods such as [[suture-wound-closure/moslemi-2016-periosteal-releasing-incision-diode-laser-gbr]] and the broader flap-design discussion in [[suture-wound-closure/lee-2018-flap-management-alveolar-ridge-preservation]]. It pairs directly with its sibling Ti-mesh closure technique [[suture-wound-closure/wang-2024-poncho-technique-titanium-mesh-bone-regeneration]].

## One-line Summary

RCT (n=14, posterior mandible horizontal GBR with titanium mesh) showing the coronally advanced lingual flap (CALF) achieved markedly greater lingual flap advancement (14.4 vs 3.9 mm) and zero titanium-mesh exposure vs 83.3% early exposure without CALF.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Introduces and tests the coronally advanced lingual flap (CALF) technique to safely advance the lingual flap coronally during posterior-mandible horizontal ridge augmentation with titanium mesh (TM).
- Demonstrates that adding CALF to buccal advancement converts an 83.3% early TM-exposure rate to 0% exposure.
- Provides quantitative flap-advancement values for lingual and buccal flaps in both arms.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 14 patients randomly allocated to two groups:
  - Control (NO-CALF): buccal flap advancement without CALF.
  - Test (CALF): buccal flap advancement plus the CALF technique.
- Setting: horizontal ridge augmentation in the posterior mandible with titanium mesh.
- Outcomes monitored: wound healing weekly for the first 4 weeks, then at 2, 4, 6, and 9 months postoperatively for any soft-tissue dehiscence (titanium-mesh exposure) along the incision line.
- Measured: extent of lingual and buccal flap advancement; intraoperative and postoperative CALF-related complications.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Titanium-mesh exposure: NO-CALF 83.3% of cases showed early Class II exposures; CALF group showed no exposure (P < .0001).
- Mean lingual flap advancement: 3.9 ± 1.1 mm (NO-CALF) vs 14.4 ± 3.8 mm (CALF) (P < .0001).
- Mean buccal flap advancement: 15.8 ± 2.1 mm (NO-CALF) vs 10.5 ± 1.4 mm (CALF) (P < .0001).
- No complications related to the CALF technique were reported.
- Conclusion: Tension-free primary wound closure was facilitated and maintained during healing by CALF; a reliable technique to safely advance the lingual flap coronally.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample (n=14), single anatomical site (posterior mandible).
- Full-text methodology, follow-up bone-gain outcomes, and statistical detail are not available in the ingested abstract-only PDF.
- Short reporting horizon emphasizes early soft-tissue outcomes (exposure) rather than long-term augmentation or implant survival (not reported here).

## 7. Glossary

- CALF: Coronally Advanced Lingual Flap — technique to mobilize and advance the lingual flap coronally for primary closure.
- TM: Titanium Mesh — rigid barrier for guided bone regeneration.
- GBR: Guided Bone Regeneration.
- Class II exposure: a graded category of soft-tissue dehiscence exposing the mesh along the incision line (as used by the authors).
