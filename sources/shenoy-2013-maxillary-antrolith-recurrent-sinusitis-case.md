## Why Ingested

상악동석이 재발성 상악동염과 구강상악동루(Oroantral Fistula)를 유발한 증례. Caldwell-Luc 수술 과거력이 있는 환자에서 잔류 골편이 상악동석의 nidus가 된 메커니즘을 설명. [[sinus-lift/pseudocyst/tan-2020-maxillary-antrolith-case-report-management]]의 무증상 소형 증례와 대비되는 증상성 대형(2×1cm) 증례.

## One-line Summary

Case report of a large (2×1cm) symptomatic maxillary antrolith causing recurrent sinusitis and oroantral fistula, arising from residual bone chips post-Caldwell-Luc; managed with repeat Caldwell-Luc and endoscopic sinus surgery.

## 한줄요약

과거 Caldwell-Luc 수술 후 잔류 골편을 nidus로 형성된 2×1cm 대형 상악동석이 재발성 상악동염·구강상악동루를 유발한 증례; 반복 Caldwell-Luc+ESS로 제거.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Case report + literature review
- **Journal**: Case Reports in Otolaryngology (2013)
- **DOI**: [10.1155/2013/527152](https://doi.org/10.1155/2013/527152)
- **Patient**: 47-year-old woman; prior left Caldwell-Luc (1984)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Demonstrates large/symptomatic antrolith requiring combined ESS + Caldwell-Luc
- Establishes prior sinus surgery residual bone chips as nidus for antrolith formation
- Recommends thorough sinus irrigation after ESS to prevent future antrolith formation
- Pathogenesis review: role of poor drainage, chronic infection, and foreign body

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Single case report
- CT of osteomeatal complex and paranasal sinuses (opacification bilateral maxillary; bone-density amorphous area left sinus)
- Diagnostic nasal endoscopy: thick pus in middle meatus
- Wide antrostomy → antrolith found → too large for ESS → repeat Caldwell-Luc
- Histopathology: decalcification + PAS staining; excluded osteoma, malignancy, rhinoscleroma, fungal concretions

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Case details:**
- Size: 2 × 1 cm (large)
- Appearance: brownish, hard, gritty, freely mobile in antrum
- Predisposing factor: residual bony chips from 1984 Caldwell-Luc as endogenous nidus
- Culture: Pseudomonas spp. (resistant to ampicillin)
- Complication: oroantral fistula + mobile #27

**Pathogenesis of antrolith:**
1. Nidus formation (endogenous/exogenous)
2. Long-standing infection + poor sinus drainage
3. Purulent fluid concentration
4. Precipitation of calcium phosphate + calcium carbonate
5. Progressive encrustation = antrolith

**Background statistics:**
- Only ~30 cases in world literature until 2005
- Maxillary sinus most common site (> frontal sinus)
- Almost always unilateral; bilateral (Kharoubi) is very rare

**Nidus classification:**
- *Endogenous*: tooth/bone fragments, blood, pus, mucus, fungi
- *Exogenous*: cotton, paper, dental burs, GP points, silver points, bullets, wood, grass, match sticks

**Treatment:**
- Endoscopic sinus surgery ± Caldwell-Luc (large antroliths need combined approach)
- Caldwell-Luc provides better exposure, ventilation, drainage
- + Treatment of concurrent sinus infection

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single case; very rare entity (~30 world cases until 2005)
- Diagnosis by exclusion (histopathology after decalcification)

## 7. Glossary

- **Oroantral fistula (구강상악동루)**: pathological communication between oral cavity and maxillary sinus
- **Antrolith/sinolith**: calcified mass within maxillary sinus = antrolith; also called rhinolith when originating from nasal cavity
- **Endoscopic sinus surgery (ESS)**: functional endoscopic approach to paranasal sinuses; limited for large antroliths requiring Caldwell-Luc access
