## Why Ingested

소켓 쉴드(SS) 기법의 생역학적 안전성 — 잔존 치근편이 주위골에 가하는 응력 — 을 유한요소분석(FEA)으로 정량. 임상 결과·심미 중심의 [[immediate-implant/socket-shield/velasco-bohorquez-2021-socket-shield-failure-mbl-pes-sr-ma]]·[[immediate-implant/socket-shield/oliva-2023-socket-shield-buccal-bone-preservation-sr]]에 생역학 관점을 더한다.

## One-line Summary

Finite element analysis comparing socket shield (SS, 2.0-mm dentin fragment), heterologous bone graft (HBG), and a fully bone-embedded control (C) under oblique loading; both SS and HBG produced higher peri-implant bone stress than the control, with SS showing the highest stress concentration in peri-implant tissues.

## 한줄요약

유한요소분석 — 소켓 쉴드(SS, 2.0 mm 치근편)·이종골이식(HBG)·완전 골내 대조(C) 비교에서 SS와 HBG 모두 대조군보다 주위골 응력이 높았고, SS가 주위 조직 응력 집중이 가장 컸다.

## 1. Document Information

- Journal: Journal of Indian Society of Periodontology 2023 Jul;27(4):392–398. PMCID PMC10431233.
- In-vitro 3D finite element analysis (computational model).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies the biomechanical stress consequences of retaining a buccal root fragment (socket shield) vs grafting or full bone embedment.
- Adds a mechanical-risk perspective to the predominantly clinical/esthetic socket-shield literature.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 3D model: maxillary central incisor implant-supported crown.
- Three conditions: SS (2.0-mm root dentin fragment between buccal wall and implant), HBG (bovine bone graft), C (implant fully in healed bone).
- Loads (simultaneous, oblique 45°): 100 N lingual (maximal intercuspation) + 25.5 N incisal (protrusion).
- Analyzed: tensile/shear/compressive stress and displacement in cortical/trabecular bone, dentin fragment, graft; von Mises in implant/prosthetic components.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- SS and HBG bone-tissue stresses > control.
- Only slight differences between models for dentin fragment, graft, implant, and prosthetic components.
- SS condition = highest stress concentration in peri-implant tissues.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro FEA: idealized geometry, static loads, assumed material properties; no biological remodeling.
- Single tooth/loading scenario; mechanical stress ≠ clinical failure.

## 6. Related Work

- Hürzeler et al. (2010) socket shield concept; socket-shield clinical outcome reviews.

## 7. Glossary

- **FEA**: finite element analysis.
- **Socket shield (SS)**: retention of a buccal root fragment in contact with the buccal plate at immediate implant placement.
- **von Mises stress**: scalar measure of combined stress used for failure prediction in ductile components.
