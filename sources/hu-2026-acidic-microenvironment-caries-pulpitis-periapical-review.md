## Why Ingested

This narrative review unifies the acidic-microenvironment mechanism that drives the whole disease continuum: a low-pH milieu selects for **acidogenic and aciduric cariogenic bacteria**, demineralizes dental hard tissue, and propagates damage from caries through pulpitis into apical periodontitis while blunting treatment effectiveness — the core acid-mechanism behind caries. It reinforces the biofilm/EPS acidogenesis substrate in [[wiki/oral-microbiology/jakubovics-2021-dental-plaque-biofilm-matrix]] and extends the caries-detection/management framing in [[wiki/caries/foros-2021-early-caries-detection-methods-sr-ma]] by supplying the upstream pH-driven pathophysiology. **Abstract-only — full text not retrieved (no PMC OA).**

## One-line Summary

Narrative review (Sichuan University / HKU): an acidic microenvironment is the unifying pathophysiologic driver across dental caries (selecting acidogenic/aciduric cariogenic bacteria and accelerating demineralization), pulpitis (low pH impairs pulpal function and raises bacterial virulence), and apical periodontitis (acid disrupts osteoblast/osteoclast/enzymatic processes to increase bone resorption), and it also compromises endodontic treatment effectiveness — yet acid-mitigation strategies remain far better developed for caries than for pulpal/periapical disease.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Frames the **acidic microenvironment** as a single unifying mechanism spanning dental caries, pulpitis, and apical periodontitis, rather than treating them as separate disease entities.
- **Dental caries**: the acidic milieu promotes proliferation and adaptation of acidogenic and aciduric cariogenic bacteria, accelerating disease progression and impairing treatment outcomes.
- **Pulpitis**: when pH drops, normal pulpal functions are impaired and pathogenic bacteria exhibit heightened virulence.
- **Apical periodontitis**: the acidic periapical microenvironment affects osteoblast, osteoclast, and enzymatic processes, increasing bone resorption.
- Highlights that the acidic environment also **compromises the effectiveness of endodontic treatment**.
- Identifies a translational gap: many acid-mitigation strategies exist for caries, but comparable approaches have received less attention for pulpal and periapical diseases.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Abstract-only — full text not retrieved. Reported conceptual findings:

- Acidic environment causes **demineralization of dental hard tissues** and disrupts the function of host cells and microorganisms.
- This promotes progression of caries → pulpitis → apical periodontitis and reduces treatment effectiveness across the continuum.
- Acid-related adaptation favors acidogenic/aciduric bacteria; low pulpal pH heightens bacterial virulence; acidic periapical tissue shifts bone homeostasis toward resorption.

## 7. Glossary

- **Acidogenic bacteria**: microorganisms that produce acid (e.g., from carbohydrate fermentation), lowering local pH.
- **Aciduric bacteria**: microorganisms that tolerate and remain metabolically active in a low-pH (acidic) environment.
- **Demineralization**: loss of mineral (hydroxyapatite) from enamel/dentin driven by acid exposure.
- **Apical periodontitis**: inflammation of periapical tissues, often with bone resorption, secondary to pulpal infection.
