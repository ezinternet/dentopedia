## One-line Summary
2017 World Workshop consensus establishing case definitions and classification for peri-implant health, peri-implant mucositis, peri-implantitis, and soft/hard tissue deficiencies.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Periodontology 2018; 45(Suppl. 20): S286–S291
- **Study type**: Consensus statement (2017 AAP/EFP World Workshop, Workgroup 4)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Standardised case definitions for peri-implant health, mucositis, and peri-implantitis (now universally adopted)
- Defined peri-implantitis as plaque-associated with progressive bone loss + clinical signs of inflammation
- Clarified that peri-implant health can exist around implants with reduced bone support
- Baseline radiographic measurements at prosthesis delivery recommended for all implants

## 3. Methodology
- Five position papers commissioned prior to workshop
- Workgroup 4 of joint AAP/EFP classification workshop
- Expert consensus process with structured deliberation

## 4. Key Definitions
**Peri-implant health**: absence of erythema, BOP, swelling, suppuration; no BOP threshold for "normal" probing depth
**Peri-implant mucositis**: BOP on gentle probing as main clinical feature; erythema/swelling/suppuration may be present; strong evidence plaque is etiological factor; reversible
**Peri-implantitis**: plaque-associated; progressive bone loss; BOP and/or suppuration; increased PD or recession + radiographic bone loss
**Soft/hard tissue deficiencies**: loss of alveolar ridge volume; keratinized mucosa evidence equivocal for long-term health

## 5. Key Clinical Points
- Probing of peri-implant tissues is safe and mandatory
- No generic implant — case definitions must account for design variability
- Keratinized mucosa may benefit patient comfort and plaque control
- Baseline probing depths and radiographs at prosthesis delivery are essential reference points

## 6. Related Work
- Herrera 2023 EFP guideline uses these definitions as foundation
- Galarraga-Vinueza 2025 applies 2018 classification criteria for prevalence estimates
