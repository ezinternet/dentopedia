## Why Ingested

Existing OD primary-stability evidence ([[implants/versah-protocols/mercier-2022-osseodensification-primary-stability-cadavers]], [[implants/versah-protocols/moghaddas-2025-osseodensification-standard-drilling-isq-itv]]) compares OD vs conventional drilling but does not pin down *which* OD spin-speed/feed/irrigation settings are optimal — Huwais and others never specified exact parameters. This CNC-controlled in vitro study (Tao 2025) varies spindle speed, feed per tooth, and irrigation on Type IV polyurethane foam to recommend a concrete OD parameter set (1500 rpm, 0.04 mm/z, with irrigation), giving the parameter-selection layer those clinical/cadaver comparisons leave open.

## One-line Summary

In vitro study (48 polyurethane foam blocks, 0.160 g/cm³ Type IV bone analog, CNC DMU machine): osseodensification drilling (OD, Densah) vs conventional drilling (CD, BLT tapered) — OD gave higher insertion/removal torque (IT 11.73 vs 7.77 N·m, RT 9.28 vs 6.65 N·m, both p<0.001) and fewer site-wall defects despite comparable ISQ (47.1 vs 46.7, NS); recommended OD parameters: 1500 rpm, 0.04 mm/z feed, with 4°C saline irrigation.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First study to systematically **optimize the OD drilling parameter set** (spin speed × feed per tooth × irrigation) rather than only comparing OD vs CD as a binary technique — addressing the gap that Huwais et al. never specified exact clinical OD parameters.
- Used a **CNC machine (DMU)** to remove operator hand-pressure variability, giving controlled measurements of thrust force, real-time intra-osteotomy temperature, IT, RT, and ISQ.
- Recommends concrete settings for Type IV bone: **1500 rpm, 0.04 mm/z feed, with 4°C saline irrigation**.
- Quantified the **IT/RT–ISQ dissociation** in vitro: OD strongly raised torque metrics but not ISQ.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Bone analog**: Polyurethane foam blocks (PFB, Sawbones) 0.160 g/cm³ (PCF 10), simulating Type IV (low-density) bone per ASTM; block dimensions 80 × 40 × 20 mm.
- **Implants**: Straumann BLT, 4.1 mm diameter, 10 and 16 mm lengths.
- **Drills**: CD = BLT tapered drills (2.2 mm pilot, 2.8, 3.5 mm); OD = Densah burs (Versah) (1.6 mm pilot, 1828, 2838) run in densifying (reverse/counterclockwise) mode per Versah protocol.
- **CNC osteotomy**: DMU computer numerical control machine varying 3 spin speeds (300, 800, 1500 rpm per Versah protocol), 2 feeds per tooth (Fz), and 2 irrigation conditions (with / without 4°C saline).
- **Measurements**: thrust force via Kistler 9272 dynamometer; intra-hole real-time temperature via K-type thermocouples at 5/10/16 mm depth (NI LabVIEW); surface temperature via FLIR A615 infrared imager; hole-wall morphology via laser confocal microscope (Keyence VK-X3000) and SEM; IT/RT via Tohnichi torque-testing machine; ISQ via Osstell resonance frequency analysis.
- **Sample size**: 48 samples (G*Power; effect size 0.92, α 0.05, power 0.8). Statistics: Shapiro–Wilk normality, independent-samples t-test, p<0.05; SPSS 26.0.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Thrust force**: OD higher than CD, maximum at 300 rpm (2.99 ± 0.22 N vs 2.77 ± 0.17 N, p<0.01).
- **Temperature**: elevation lower in OD than CD under irrigation (3.35°C vs 4.67°C); irrigation essential in both to prevent thermal damage.
- **ISQ**: comparable between groups (CD 46.71 ± 8.56 vs OD 47.08 ± 5.95, p=0.86, NS).
- **Insertion torque (IT)**: OD markedly higher — 11.73 ± 0.45 vs 7.77 ± 0.21 N·m, p<0.001.
- **Removal torque (RT)**: OD higher — 9.28 ± 0.45 vs 6.65 ± 0.19 N·m, p<0.001.
- **Morphology**: OD produced fewer hole-wall defects (tears/potholes) than CD, i.e. less iatrogenic damage to the osteotomy walls.
- **Bottom line / recommendation**: For Type IV bone, OD at **1500 rpm and 0.04 mm/z with irrigation** maximizes primary stability while preventing thermal damage; OD outperforms CD on bone density, thrust force, and torque.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **In vitro / synthetic bone only**: PFB lacks the cellular, vascular, viscoelastic, and lamellar architecture of living bone; no remodeling, healing, or osseointegration captured.
- **Single bone density** (0.160 g/cm³); no gradient of Type III/II densities tested.
- **CNC drilling** removes the surgeon's hand variability — generalizability to freehand clinical drilling is limited.
- **ISQ/IT dissociation** unexplained mechanistically; no loading/survival endpoint (impossible in a bench model).
- Future work: in vivo / clinical validation of the recommended 1500 rpm, 0.04 mm/z parameter set.

## 7. Glossary

- **OD (Osseodensification Drilling)**: implant-site preparation using Densah burs run in reverse (counterclockwise/densifying) mode to laterally compact and preserve bone, increasing density at the implant interface.
- **CD (Conventional Drilling)**: sequential subtractive drilling removing bone to create the osteotomy.
- **PFB (Polyurethane Foam Block)**: standardized synthetic bone analog (ASTM) used to simulate cancellous bone of defined density.
- **Type IV bone**: lowest-density jawbone (Lekholm & Zarb), thin cortex + low-density trabecular core, common in posterior maxilla.
- **IT / RT (Insertion / Removal Torque)**: rotational resistance during implant insertion / removal; primary-stability surrogates.
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: resonance-frequency-analysis stability metric (Osstell), 1–100 scale.
- **Feed per tooth (Fz)**: CNC machining parameter — axial advance of the drill per cutting edge per revolution (mm/z).
