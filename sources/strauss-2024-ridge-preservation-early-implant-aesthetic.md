## Why Ingested

[[wiki/bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/jung-2018-alveolar-ridge-preservation-esthetic-zone]]가 esthetic-zone ARP의 원리·적응증을 narrative로 정리했다면, 이 RCT 2차분석(Strauss 2024)은 그 esthetic-zone ARP를 **early implant placement(8주 지연식립)** 맥락에서 radiographic·profilometric outcome으로 정량 검증해 보강한다. 핵심 발견은 ARP가 implant 식립 시 추가 GBR 필요(68% → ~30%)를 줄이지만, **추가 GBR 자체가 1년 buccal convexity의 최강 결정인자**라는 점 — ARP의 "GBR을 대체한다"가 아니라 "GBR 부담을 줄인다"로 한정한 점에서 [[wiki/bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/couso-queiruga-2021-post-extraction-dimensional-changes-systematic]]·[[wiki/bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/mardas-2023-alveolar-ridge-preservation-overtreatment]]의 ARP 적응증 논쟁에도 직접 연결된다.

## One-line Summary

RCT secondary analysis (n=75, 3 groups 1:1:1; 55 datasets analyzed at 1y) of early implant placement (8-wk delayed) in the anterior maxilla: ARP (DBBM-C + collagen matrix or palatal graft) cut the need for additional GBR at implant placement from 68.4% (no ARP) to ~30%, but simultaneous GBR at placement — not ARP alone — was the dominant driver of restored buccal convexity at 1 year (72.7% with GBR vs 36.0% ARP-only).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies how esthetic-zone ARP interacts with **early implant placement** (type 2, ~8 weeks), a timing distinct from the immediate or late protocols most ARP-implant studies use.
- Separates two interventions usually conflated: **ARP at extraction** vs **additional GBR at implant placement**, and shows the latter dominates 1-year buccal contour.
- Reports both **radiographic (CBCT)** and **profilometric (surface-scan/STL)** outcomes on the same patients, with a tendency for the two methods to agree.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: 75 patients with a failing single tooth in the anterior maxilla, randomized 1:1:1 to:
  - (a) **ARP-CM** — DBBM containing 10% collagen (DBBM-C) covered by a collagen matrix (CM);
  - (b) **ARP-PG** — DBBM-C covered by a free palatal graft (PG);
  - (c) **Control** — unassisted (spontaneous) socket healing.
- **Implant timing**: early implant placement performed in **all** patients 8 weeks after tooth extraction.
- **Additional GBR**: performed at implant placement when needed (a per-protocol clinical decision), then quantified by group.
- **Imaging/measurement**: CBCT and impressions/scans taken at 8 weeks (pre-implant) and at 1-year post-loading; radiographic ridge dimensions and profilometric (volumetric/contour) changes evaluated.
- **Analysis**: adjusted regression models for predictors of 1-year alveolar change; outcome of interest included presence of **buccal bone convexity**.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Datasets analyzed at 1y**: 55 of 70 available (ARP-CM 19; ARP-PG 17; Control 19).
- **Need for additional GBR at implant placement**: ARP-CM 31.6%, ARP-PG 29.4%, **unassisted healing 68.4%** — ARP roughly halved the GBR requirement.
- **Predictors (adjusted models)**: residual **buccal bone height** and **additional GBR at placement** significantly influenced 1-year alveolar change (p < 0.05).
- **Buccal convexity at 1y**:
  - ARP without additional GBR: **36.0% (9/25)**.
  - ARP **with** additional GBR: **72.7% (8/11)** (p = 0.042).
- **Profilometric vs radiographic**: a tendency toward agreement between the two measurement modalities.
- **Conclusions**: early implant placement with ARP attenuates 1-year ridge changes (both radiographic and profilometric), **but** early placement with simultaneous GBR consistently yields superior contour regardless of whether ARP was performed.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Secondary analysis; subgroup sizes are small (11 in the ARP+GBR convexity cell), limiting precision.
- "Additional GBR" was a clinician decision rather than randomized, so confounding by indication is plausible (worse sites get GBR).
- Single timepoint (1 year); longer-term contour stability and esthetic (PES/soft-tissue) endpoints not the focus here.
- Two ARP techniques (CM vs PG) were not powered for head-to-head superiority.

## 7. Glossary

- **ARP** — Alveolar Ridge Preservation (치조제보존): grafting the socket at extraction to limit dimensional loss.
- **DBBM-C** — Demineralized Bovine Bone Mineral containing 10% Collagen (Bio-Oss Collagen–type xenograft).
- **CM / PG** — Collagen Matrix / Palatal Graft used as the socket-sealing cover.
- **Early implant placement** — type 2 timing, ~4–8 weeks post-extraction after soft-tissue healing.
- **GBR** — Guided Bone Regeneration: barrier-membrane + graft augmentation at implant placement.
- **Buccal convexity** — restored facial bone/contour bulge, an esthetic-zone success surrogate.
- **Profilometry** — superimposition of surface scans (STL) to measure volumetric/contour change.
