## Why Ingested

[[wiki/prosthetic-materials/tiu-2015-clinical-tooth-preparations-measuring-sr]] catalogued preparation geometry and measuring methods generically; it predates the fully-digital (IOS → CAD → mill) zirconia workflow and does not resolve which finish-line / taper / occlusal-reduction design minimizes marginal and internal misfit in that workflow. This 2025 PROSPERO-registered systematic review (27 in vitro studies) extends Tiu by translating preparation-design choices into specific marginal-discrepancy and internal-gap recommendations for digitally fabricated zirconia prostheses.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review of 27 in vitro studies: rounded-shoulder finish line favored marginal fit, chamfer favored internal adaptation, and increased taper plus planar occlusal reduction improved fit of digitally fabricated zirconia prostheses (all within the ~60–120 µm clinically acceptable range).

## 1. Document Information

- Type: Systematic review of in vitro studies (PRISMA, PROSPERO CRD42024615390)
- Published: J Prosthet Dent 2025;134:616-627 (September 2025)
- DOI: 10.1016/j.prosdent.2025.04.030
- Databases: Ovid (Medline/Embase), PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, Google Scholar + manual search
- Quality tool: QUIN tool for in vitro studies (12 parameters)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR specifically isolating tooth-preparation design (finish line, taper, occlusal reduction) as the determinant of misfit in fully digital zirconia prostheses.
- Disentangles which design optimizes marginal fit vs internal adaptation (they diverge).
- Adopts Holmes et al. standardized misfit terminology (absolute marginal discrepancy, internal adaptation, vertical/horizontal marginal discrepancy).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 130 records → 93 after de-duplication → 39 full-text → 27 in vitro studies included (qualitative + quantitative synthesis).
- Study mix: 17 on finish lines, 9 on taper, 1 on occlusal reduction, 1 on both occlusal reduction + finish line, 2 on FPDs (majority single crowns).
- Clinically acceptable thresholds adopted: marginal gap 60–120 µm; internal adaptation 50–100 µm (McLean & von Fraunhofer: ≤120 µm acceptable).
- Null hypothesis: preparation design does not affect marginal fit / internal adaptation.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Selected studies generally reported clinically acceptable marginal fit and internal adaptation.
- Marginal fit: favored the rounded-shoulder finish line.
- Internal adaptation: favored the chamfer finish line.
- A planar occlusal reduction combined with a chamfer finish line gave better marginal fit.
- Taper: positive effect on both marginal fit and internal adaptation.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- All evidence is in vitro — no clinical/in vivo outcomes (survival, secondary caries).
- Heterogeneous study designs, scanners, zirconia types, and measurement methods limit pooled certainty.
- Very few FPD studies (2); generalization to multi-unit prostheses is weak.
- Risk-of-bias scoring by a single investigator.

## 6. Related Work

- Tiu et al. 2015 SR — preparation geometry and measuring methods.
- Holmes et al. — standardized misfit terminology.
- McLean & von Fraunhofer — 120 µm clinical-acceptability threshold.

## 7. Glossary

- Absolute marginal discrepancy: total marginal gap including over/underextension errors.
- Internal adaptation/gap: distance from crown intaglio to axial wall away from the margin.
- Taper: convergence of axial walls (≈ TOC); higher taper here aided digital fit.
- QUIN tool: quality assessment instrument for in vitro dental studies.
