## Why Ingested

Head-to-head RCT comparing Guided Biofilm Therapy (GBT) against conventional curette + ultrasonic debridement, measuring gingival crevicular fluid (GCF) inflammatory biomarkers (IL-1β, IL-10, TNF-α, MMP-8) — adds biomarker-level (immunological) evidence to the GBT-vs-conventional question. It reinforces the clinical-index RCT [[wiki/periodontics/cyris-2024-guided-biofilm-therapy-versus-conventional]] (GBT comparable/favorable for plaque control), while refining the more cautious systematic-review conclusion in [[wiki/periodontics/yein-2026-guided-biofilm-therapy-periodontal-efficacy-sr]] that GBT is not clinically superior — here GBT does beat conventional on GI, PI, and IL-1β but not on the deeper inflammatory markers (TNF-α, IL-10, MMP-8).

## One-line Summary

Prospective RCT (n=80, Stage 1–2 periodontitis) finds GBT significantly outperforms conventional curette+ultrasonic debridement on Gingival Index, Plaque Index, and GCF IL-1β at 1 and 3 months (p<0.05), with no between-group difference in TNF-α, IL-10, or MMP-8 (abstract-only — full text not retrieved).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Direct head-to-head comparison of Guided Biofilm Therapy (GBT) vs conventional therapy (Gracey curette + ultrasonic device) in non-surgical periodontal therapy.
- Couples standard clinical periodontal indices with GCF immunological biomarkers (IL-1β, IL-10, TNF-α, MMP-8), giving a molecular read-out of the inflammatory response beyond clinical scoring.
- Demonstrates a GBT advantage specifically on plaque control (PI), gingival inflammation (GI), and the pro-inflammatory cytokine IL-1β.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Population**: 80 nonsmoking, systemically healthy volunteers with Stage 1 or Stage 2 periodontitis.
- **Randomization**: randomly divided into two groups — GBT vs conventional therapy (CT: Gracey curette + ultrasonic device).
- **Timepoints**: baseline, 1 month, and 3 months after therapy.
- **Outcomes**: pocket depth (PD), Gingival Index (GI), Plaque Index (PI), Bleeding on Probing (BOP); GCF levels of IL-1β, IL-10, TNF-α, MMP-8.
- Note: abstract-only — full text not retrieved; detailed protocol, GCF sampling/assay method, and statistics not available.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Both modalities significantly improved all clinical and biomarker indicators from baseline (p<0.001).
- At 1 and 3 months, GBT showed a **greater decrease in GI, PI, and IL-1β** than conventional therapy (p<0.05).
- **No significant between-group difference** in TNF-α, IL-10, or MMP-8.
- Conclusion: GBT is a viable non-surgical option, especially for managing plaque and lowering the IL-1β inflammatory response.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only; full methods, effect sizes, and confidence intervals not retrieved.
- Short follow-up (3 months); authors call for larger and longer studies.
- Limited to nonsmoking, systemically healthy, Stage 1–2 patients — not generalizable to severe periodontitis, smokers, or medically compromised patients.
- Biomarker advantage is confined to IL-1β; deeper markers (TNF-α, MMP-8) showed no GBT-specific benefit, tempering claims of immunological superiority.

## 7. Glossary

- **GBT (Guided Biofilm Therapy)**: protocol-driven biofilm management (disclosing + air-polishing with erythritol/glycine powder ± piezo) marketed as an alternative to hand/ultrasonic debridement.
- **GCF (Gingival Crevicular Fluid)**: serum-derived exudate from the gingival sulcus; carries host inflammatory mediators usable as periodontal biomarkers.
- **IL-1β**: pro-inflammatory cytokine central to periodontal tissue destruction.
- **MMP-8**: collagenase-2, a key matrix metalloproteinase in periodontal connective-tissue breakdown.
- **Stage 1/2 periodontitis**: mild-to-moderate severity per the 2017 periodontitis staging classification.
