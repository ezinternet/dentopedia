## Why Ingested

This is the unifying landmark review (JDR 2011, by the Tay–Pashley–Breschi–Tjäderhane axis) that ties together every strand of the resin-dentin durability problem covered separately in our other pages: water-driven hydrolytic degradation of hydrophilic adhesives, MMP/cysteine-cathepsin collagenolysis, and the rescue strategies (anti-MMP monomers, collagen cross-linkers, ethanol wet-bonding, biomimetic remineralization). It reinforces the hydrophilicity/water-degradation thesis of [[wiki/resin-bonding/tay-2003-dentin-adhesives-hydrophilic]], extends the collagen-cross-linker/MMP-silencing evidence toward the meta-analytic synthesis in [[wiki/resin-bonding/zhou-2025-collagen-crosslinkers-naocl-dentin-bond-strength-sr-ma]], and provides the mechanistic rationale behind the ethanol wet-bonding RCT in [[wiki/resin-bonding/mortazavi-2012-ethanol-wet-bonding-clinical-trial]]. It is the conceptual hub that [[wiki/resin-bonding/breschi-2025-adhesive-dentistry-evolution-review]] later updates.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Unifies the two mechanistic drivers of resin-dentin bond degradation into one frame: (a) **hydrolysis** of suboptimally polymerized hydrophilic resin components, and (b) **enzymatic collagenolysis** of water-rich, resin-sparse collagen matrices by **matrix metalloproteinases (MMPs)** and **cysteine cathepsins**.
2. Reviews three years of data across research groups on **five experimental strategies** to extend resin-dentin bond longevity (see §4).
3. Reframes "water" as the central villain — it both enables hydrolysis and keeps endogenous collagenolytic enzymes mobile/active in the hybrid layer.
4. Argues that **combining several strategies** is the realistic path to overcoming the durability barrier, rather than any single approach.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Critical narrative review of the preceding ~3 years of literature on resin-dentin bond degradation and preservation. The review organizes the field around a single degradation model (hydrolysis + MMP/cathepsin collagenolysis of the water-rich hybrid layer) and evaluates five mechanistically distinct counter-strategies developed by different groups. No quantitative pooling (not a meta-analysis). *(Methodological detail beyond the abstract was not retrieved — abstract-only.)*

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

The **five strategies** to prevent bond degradation (faithfully preserved):

1. **Increasing the degree of conversion and esterase resistance** of hydrophilic adhesives — better polymerization and ester-bond stability reduce hydrolytic breakdown of the resin phase.
2. **Broad-spectrum inhibitors of collagenolytic enzymes**, including novel inhibitor functional groups **grafted onto methacrylate resin monomers** to produce **anti-MMP adhesives**.
3. **Cross-linking agents** that **silence MMP and cathepsin activity** by irreversibly altering the 3-D structures of their **catalytic/allosteric domains**.
4. **Ethanol wet-bonding with hydrophobic resins** to **completely replace water from the extrafibrillar and intrafibrillar collagen compartments** and **immobilize the collagenolytic enzymes**.
5. **Biomimetic remineralization** of the water-filled collagen matrix using **analogs of matrix proteins** to progressively replace water with **intrafibrillar and extrafibrillar apatites**, excluding exogenous collagenolytic enzymes and **fossilizing endogenous collagenolytic enzymes**.

Bottom line: a **combination** of several strategies should overcome the critical barriers currently encountered in dentin bonding.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Abstract-only retrieval** — quantitative benchmarks, figures, and group-by-group comparisons in the full text were not obtained.
- The five strategies were **experimental** at time of publication (2011); clinical translation/durability data were still maturing.
- The review itself concludes the durability barrier is unsolved by any single strategy — combination approaches and long-term clinical validation are the open work (later addressed by ongoing adhesive-chemistry evolution).

## 7. Glossary

- **Hybrid layer**: the resin-infiltrated demineralized dentin collagen zone that mechanically interlocks adhesive with dentin.
- **MMP (matrix metalloproteinase)**: zinc-dependent endogenous collagenolytic enzymes (e.g., MMP-2, -8, -9) exposed/activated during acid-etching that degrade exposed collagen.
- **Cysteine cathepsins**: lysosomal cysteine proteases (e.g., cathepsin K, B) that also contribute to endogenous collagen breakdown in dentin.
- **Degree of conversion**: fraction of methacrylate C=C double bonds converted to polymer; higher = denser, more hydrolysis-resistant network.
- **Esterase resistance**: resistance of resin ester bonds to enzymatic/hydrolytic cleavage by salivary/bacterial esterases.
- **Ethanol wet-bonding**: technique replacing rinse water with ethanol to keep collagen expanded while admitting more hydrophobic resin, reducing residual water.
- **Biomimetic remineralization**: bottom-up reprecipitation of intra-/extrafibrillar apatite using matrix-protein analogs to restore mineral and "fossilize" enzymes.
- **Fossilize (enzymes)**: render endogenous collagenolytic enzymes inactive by encasing the collagen matrix in mineral, removing the water they require.
