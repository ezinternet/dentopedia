## One-line Summary
Systematic review (25 clinical studies, 1989–2024): local hemostatic measures sufficient for anticoagulated patients; do not routinely stop VKAs/DOACs for routine dental procedures; tranexamic acid mouthwash as first-line adjunct.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Healthcare 12(15):1537 (August 2024)
- DOI: 10.3390/healthcare12151537
- Design: Systematic review (25 clinical trials; PubMed, Scopus, WoS; ROBINS-I quality assessment)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Confirms: anticoagulants should NOT be routinely interrupted for dental procedures
- Tranexamic acid mouthwash: evidence-based hemostatic adjunct since 1993
- DOACs vs VKAs (warfarin): different management strategies
- Education gap identified: dentists/patients uncertain about anticoagulant management

## 3. Drug Classes Covered

| Class | Examples | Mechanism | INR Monitoring |
|---|---|---|---|
| Vitamin K Antagonists (VKA) | Warfarin | Inhibit factors II, VII, IX, X | Required (INR target 2–3) |
| Direct Oral Anticoagulants (DOACs) | Dabigatran (anti-IIa); Rivaroxaban, Apixaban (anti-Xa) | Direct factor inhibition | Not routine |
| Low Molecular Weight Heparins (LMWH) | Enoxaparin | Anti-Xa | Not routine |
| Antiplatelet | Aspirin, Clopidogrel | COX-1 / P2Y12 inhibition | — |

## 4. Key Clinical Recommendations

### VKA (Warfarin) Patients
- Do NOT stop warfarin for: simple extractions, scaling, minor surgical procedures
- Proceed if INR ≤3.5 (some guidelines ≤4.0)
- INR >3.5: defer elective, consult physician for dose adjustment
- Use local hemostatic measures (tranexamic acid 4.8% mouthwash, oxidized cellulose, sutures, gelfoam)

### DOAC Patients
- Do NOT stop for: simple procedures
- For invasive surgery: skip morning dose on day of procedure (discuss with prescribing physician)
- Dabigatran: renal function dependent — longer interval if CrCl ↓
- No reversal agent needed for simple extractions with local hemostasis

### Antiplatelet Patients (Aspirin / Clopidogrel)
- Do NOT stop: stopping causes thrombosis rebound risk (stents, ACS)
- Local hemostasis + pressure pack sufficient

## 5. Hemostatic Agents Evidence
| Agent | Evidence Level | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Tranexamic acid 4.8% mouthwash | Strong (since 1993) | 4× daily × 7 days; reduces post-op bleeding |
| Oxidized cellulose (Surgicel) | Moderate | Local socket packing |
| L-PRF (fibrin) | Emerging | Sammartino 2011: reduced hemorrhagic complications |
| N-butyl-2-cyanoacrylate | Alternative | Al-Belasy 2003: comparable to conventional hemostasis |

## 6. Limitations
- Non-randomized studies dominate (ROBINS-I assessment)
- Heterogeneous outcome measures across 25 studies
- DOACs underrepresented in older studies
