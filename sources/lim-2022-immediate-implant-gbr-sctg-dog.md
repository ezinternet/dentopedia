## Why Ingested

The clinical SR/MAs on immediate-implant soft-tissue grafting ([[immediate-implant/esthetic-soft-tissue/seyssens-2021-immediate-implant-connective-tissue-graft-sr-ma]], [[immediate-implant/esthetic-soft-tissue/de-angelis-2021-soft-tissue-augmentation-immediate-implant-sr-ma]]) consistently favor SCTG but lack histologic/histomorphometric mechanism for *why* and for *how SCTG compares head-to-head with GBR and with their combination*. This randomized preclinical canine study provides the missing histometric layer — directly comparing GBR vs SCTG vs GBR+SCTG vs control on the same animals — and adds the counter-intuitive finding that combining the two is not additive.

## One-line Summary

Randomized canine study (5 dogs, 4 sites each: GBR / SCTG / GBR+SCTG / control, 4-month non-submerged healing) — SCTG alone gave the greatest peri-implant mucosa height and thickness at/above the implant shoulder, GBR worked only below the shoulder, and combining GBR+SCTG was the *worst*, not additive.

## 한줄요약

무작위 개 실험 (개 5마리, 마리당 4부위: GBR / SCTG / GBR+SCTG / 대조군, 4개월 비침수 치유) — 즉시식립에서 결합조직이식(SCTG) 단독이 임플란트 어깨 높이·두께에 가장 유리했고, 골유도재생(GBR)은 어깨 하방에서만 효과가 있었으며, GBR+SCTG 병용은 오히려 가장 불리해 더하기 효과가 없었다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Head-to-head histometric comparison** of the three augmentation strategies clinicians actually choose between at IIP — GBR, SCTG, GBR+SCTG — plus a no-treatment control, within the same animals (split-mouth-like clustering).
2. **SCTG > GBR for the visible coronal soft tissue.** SCTG alone produced the greatest peri-implant mucosa height (MM-IS median 1.99 mm) and the greatest soft-tissue thickness at and above the implant shoulder (1.14 mm at IS, 0.78 mm 1 mm above).
3. **GBR works only apically.** Bone grafting was ineffective at or coronal to the implant shoulder; mineralized tissue was essentially absent at/above the IS in nearly all specimens.
4. **Combination is NOT additive (and tended worst).** GBR+SCTG gave the *lowest* mucosa height (MM-IS 1.24 mm) and the *smallest* thickness at the IS (0.72 mm), plausibly from increased flap tension/compression over the stacked graft.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Model**: 5 mongrel dogs (>2 yr, 12–17 kg). Distal roots of maxillary P2 and P3 hemi-sectioned and extracted atraumatically (buccal/palatal plates preserved); mesial roots retained with pulpotomy + Ca(OH)₂. **4 IIP sites per dog.**
- **Implants**: 2-piece Ø3.2×7 mm narrow-diameter (NR line, Dentium), shoulder at palatal crest, ~0.5–1.0 mm buccal gap. Non-submerged (transmucosal) healing throughout.
- **Randomized 4 groups** (one per site per dog): **GBR** (biphasic calcium phosphate / Osteon III in gap + buccal overbuild 3–4 mm high, cross-linked collagen membrane secured by healing abutment + 2 tacks); **SCTG** (palatal subepithelial graft, ~2× implant diameter wide, 5 mm high, ≥1.5 mm thick, sutured to palatal flap); **GBR/SCTG** (SCTG on top of the collagen membrane); **Control** (none).
- **Healing**: 4 months, then sacrifice. Central bucco-oral non-decalcified sections (40–50 μm, MMA-embedded, Masson-Goldner trichrome).
- **Histomorphometry**: vertical MM-IS, MM-fBIC; horizontal total/hard/soft tissue thickness at IS and ±1 mm. Mixed linear models (group factor, dog clustering); descriptive emphasis (sample too small for confirmatory testing).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **2 implant losses** (1 GBR/SCTG, 1 GBR); remaining implants osseointegrated. All implant apices protruded into maxillary sinus to varying degrees. Buccal plate moderately resorbed; bone substitute particle spaces often collapsed.
- **Peri-implant mucosa height (MM-IS, median)**: SCTG 1.99 mm > GBR 1.45 > Control 1.40 > GBR/SCTG 1.24 mm (p=0.72, NS).
- **MM-fBIC (median)**: GBR 4.06 > Control 3.97 > SCTG 3.86 > GBR/SCTG 3.06 mm (p=0.78, NS).
- **Tissue thickness (median)**:
  - 1 mm above IS: SCTG 0.78 (max) > Control 0.57 > GBR 0.43 > GBR/SCTG 0.30 mm (min) (p=0.80).
  - At IS: Control 1.17 ≈ SCTG 1.14 > GBR 0.87 > GBR/SCTG 0.72 mm (smallest) (p=0.71).
  - 1 mm below IS: SCTG 1.57 (greatest) > GBR 1.19 > GBR/SCTG 1.09 > Control 1.05 mm (p=0.56).
- **No mineralized tissue at/above the IS** in nearly all specimens; mineralized tissue 1 mm below IS in only 5 specimens (HT 0.28–1.56 mm). All between-group differences NS (p>0.05).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Small sample** (5 dogs); 2 losses further reduce per-cell n (some cells n=3). Underpowered for inferential testing — findings are descriptive directional signals, not proven differences.
- **Maxillary premolar site** chosen (atypical for canine bone-augmentation models) — less bone quantity, shallow vestibulum, sinus proximity forcing narrow Ø3.2 implants and ≤1 mm gaps; augmentation often fell *outside the bony envelope*, biasing GBR toward failure.
- **Behavioral control** poorer than human patients (graft/flap disruption). Authors call for favorable-bone-housing models (box-type defects) and clinical confirmation.

## 6. Related Work

- Clinical SR/MAs favoring SCTG at IIP: Seyssens 2021, De Angelis 2021, Rondone 2024, Torra-Moneny 2024.
- Soft- vs hard-tissue grafting and combined hard+soft augmentation (volume-stable collagen matrix, Chappuis 2018, cited) for IIP esthetic outcomes.

## 7. Glossary

- **IIP** — immediate implant placement (type 1, into fresh extraction socket).
- **GBR** — guided bone regeneration (bone substitute + barrier membrane).
- **SCTG** — subepithelial connective tissue graft (palatal donor).
- **MM / IS / fBIC** — margo mucosae / implant shoulder / first bone-to-implant contact (histometric reference points).
- **MM-IS** — vertical mucosa height from margin to shoulder; **MM-fBIC** — margin to first BIC.
- **TT / HT / ST** — total / hard / soft tissue thickness (horizontal, on buccal aspect).
