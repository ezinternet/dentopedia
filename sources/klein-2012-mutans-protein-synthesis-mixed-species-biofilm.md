## Why Ingested
Provides the only high-throughput in-vitro proteomics dataset quantifying S. mutans behavior in mixed-species biofilms, directly grounding the EPS-matrix virulence concepts described in [[oral-microbiology/koo-2013-exopolysaccharides-matrix-biofilm-virulence]] and [[oral-microbiology/karygianni-2020-biofilm-matrixome-extracellular-components]].

## One-line Summary
In-vitro MudPIT proteomics study of S. mutans UA159 in 3-species biofilms (with A. naeslundii and S. oralis on saliva-coated hydroxyapatite) showing that mixed-species context up-regulates glucan-synthesis proteins (GtfB/C, DexA), glucan-binding protein GbpB, acid-stress adaptations (F1F0-ATPase, FabM, GroEL), and detects up to 60% of the S. mutans proteome.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First MudPIT-based quantitative proteomics of S. mutans within a 3-species ecological biofilm model.
- Detected up to 60% of S. mutans UA159 proteome within biofilms.
- Demonstrated that mixed-species context augments glucan-synthesis (gtfB, gtfC, dexA, gbpB) and acid-tolerance (atpD/F1F0-ATPase, fabM, groEL) protein abundance vs. single-species biofilms (P<0.05).
- Confirmed sucrose-triggered ecological shift: S. oralis is predominant at 29 h; S. mutans becomes dominant by 115 h after 1% sucrose introduction.
- Proteome and RT-qPCR (n=12 from 6 experiments in duplicate) data are concordant.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Design: in-vitro, batch-culture, saliva-coated hydroxyapatite (sHA) disc model.
- Organisms: S. mutans UA159 (10² CFU/mL), A. naeslundii ATCC 12104 (10⁶ CFU/mL), S. oralis ATCC 35037 (10⁷ CFU/mL).
- Conditions: UFTYE + 0.1% sucrose (0–29 h initial community); 1% sucrose introduced at 29 h for cariogenic challenge; samples at 67 h and 115 h.
- Proteomics: MudPIT (12-step SCX/C18 LC-MS/MS; LTQ-Orbitrap); ProLuCID/DTASelect; 0.1% FPR; abundance by spectral count normalized to S. mutans CFU.
- Gene expression: RT-qPCR at 43, 67, 91, 115 h; genes: gtfB/C, ftf, dexA, gbpB, atpD, fabM, groES, nox, glgP, manL; n=12.
- Stats: ANOVA with Tukey post-hoc; P<0.05 threshold.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- MudPIT detected up to 60% of proteins encoded by S. mutans UA159 genome within biofilms.
- Glucan-synthesis/remodeling proteins (GtfB, GtfC, DexA) and glucan-binding protein GbpB: significantly higher spectral counts in mixed-species vs. single-species biofilms at 67 h and 115 h (P<0.05).
- Acid-tolerance cluster: F1F0-ATPase subunits, FabM (unsaturated fatty-acid biosynthesis), branched-chain amino acid metabolism enzymes, and molecular chaperone GroEL all elevated in mixed-species context.
- Ecological shift confirmed: at 29 h S. oralis is predominant; by 115 h (after 1% sucrose) S. mutans is the dominant species.
- RT-qPCR corroborated proteomic findings for gtfB/C, dexA, gbpB; expression levels generally augmented in mixed-species vs. single-species biofilms (P<0.05).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- In-vitro model (3 species only) — does not capture full complexity of human dental plaque (~700 species).
- Spectral counting is a semi-quantitative abundance metric; absolute protein concentrations not determined.
- S. oralis and A. naeslundii proteomes were not fully profiled (focus was S. mutans UA159).
- Authors call for in-vivo validation and metabolomics complementation.

## 7. Glossary
- MudPIT: Multidimensional Protein Identification Technology — shotgun proteomics combining SCX and reverse-phase LC with tandem MS.
- sHA: saliva-coated hydroxyapatite — tooth-surface surrogate.
- gtfB/C: glucosyltransferase B/C genes encoding GtfB and GtfC — primary enzymes synthesizing water-insoluble and water-soluble glucans.
- dexA: dextranase A gene; gbpB: glucan-binding protein B gene.
- FabM: enzyme for unsaturated fatty-acid synthesis aiding membrane fluidity under low pH.
- F1F0-ATPase: proton-extruding ATPase central to acid tolerance in S. mutans.
- FPR: false positive rate (0.1% in DTASelect filtering).
