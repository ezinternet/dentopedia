## Why Ingested

기존 [[immediate-implant/esthetic-soft-tissue/staas-2026-fiipp-palatal-position-cbct-3year]]가 "협측에서 ≥2 mm 구개측" 위치 규칙을 3년 CBCT로 양적 뒷받침했다면, 본 전향연구(Fettouh 2026)는 그 구개측 위치를 *어떻게* 안정적으로 구현할지를 다룬다 — "Safe Angle Concept" 가상계획 + plateau drill(평면 밀링 커터) 2-guide 프로토콜로 socket 구개 경사면을 평탄화해 드릴 slippage·labial 편향을 줄인다. 동일 1저자의 기존 연조직 RCT [[immediate-implant/esthetic-soft-tissue/fettouh-2024-soft-tissue-volume-midfacial-gingival-immediate-implant]]와는 별개 논문(연조직 부피 vs 가이드 정확도)이며, 전상악 IIP의 facial 편향이라는 공통 문제를 다른 각도에서 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

Prospective single-arm clinical trial (n=26 implants/26 patients, anterior maxilla IIP) using the "Safe Angle Concept" virtual plan + a novel plateau-drill 2-guide protocol: planned-vs-actual deviation was 4.56 ± 3.14° angular, 0.91 ± 0.61 mm platform, 1.47 ± 0.88 mm apex; all implants stayed within the safe angle, none needed angled abutments, PES 11.89 ± 1.75.

## 한줄요약

전상악 즉시식립 26증례 전향연구: "Safe Angle Concept" 가상계획 + plateau-drill 2-guide로 계획-실제 편차 각도 4.56°, platform 0.91 mm, apex 1.47 mm — 전 증례 safe angle 내, 각도형 지대주 불필요, PES 11.89.

- **Type**: Prospective clinical trial (single-arm, no comparison group), ClinicalTrials.gov NCT05750004 (reg. 19-Feb-2023)
- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health 2026;26(1)
- **DOI**: 10.1186/s12903-026-08408-1 · **PMID** 42087162 · **PMCID** PMC13159268
- **Setting**: Misr International University outpatient clinic + private practice, Cairo, Egypt (Feb–Aug 2023)
- **Source**: PubMed Central full text (JATS)

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First clinical (in-patient) test of the **Safe Angle Concept** (previously a CBCT planning proposal) for immediate implant placement (IIP) positioning in the anterior maxilla.
2. Introduces a **novel 2-guide "plateau drilling" protocol**: a plateau guide drives a flat milling cutter down the same long axis to the socket apex, flattening the palatal socket slope *before* conventional guided drilling, to counter the slippage that pushes anterior immediate implants facially.
3. Reports planned-vs-actual deviation (angular/platform/apex) achievable with this protocol and links it to 9-month esthetic (PES) and labial-bone outcomes.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Sample**: 26 patients (19F/7M, age 32.69 ± 6.27, range 20–42), 26 implants. Sites: lateral incisor 15 (57.7%), central incisor 7 (26.9%), canine 4 (15.4%). Non-restorable maxillary anterior teeth, thin (<1 mm) intact labial plate, ≥3–5 mm apical bone.
- **Planning**: Two-step silicone impression → stone cast → lab scan (Medit T700, STL); small-volume CBCT (Cranex SOREDEX) → DICOM. Superimposed in Implant Studio (3Shape) via 3-point cloud + ICP. Virtual crown → implant plan by Safe Angle Concept.
- **2-Guide System**: (1) plateau guide → flat milling cutter to socket apex → flat palatal-slope surface; (2) drilling guide → subsequent osteotomy drills. Tapered bone-level Ti-Zr implant (Straumann BLT Roxolid SLA) placed **free-hand**, engaging 3–4 mm apical/palatal bone, ≥35 Ncm, non-functional immediate loading. Customized healing abutment (flowable composite on Variobase).
- **Outcome capture**: 3-month open-tray impression + scan body → actual implant position; planned vs actual superimposed (Exocad, ICP best-fit). 9-month CBCT for labial bone (platform/2mm/5mm) and safe-angle verification; root inclination angle. PES at 9 months (2 examiners, intra-examiner k=0.82). FMPS/FMBS at baseline vs 9 months.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Deviation (planned vs actual, mean ± SD)**: angular **4.56 ± 3.14°**, global platform **0.91 ± 0.61 mm**, global apex **1.47 ± 0.88 mm**. (Comparable to a guided-surgery SR+MA: ~1 mm entry / 1.6 mm apex / 5–6° axis.)
- **Safe angle**: ALL 26 implants lay within the safe angle on 9-month CBCT; root inclination 26.64 ± 5.43°. No angled abutments required (all straight abutments).
- **Labial bone (9 mo)**: platform 2.45 ± 1, 2 mm level 2.14 ± 1.03, 5 mm level 1.79 ± 1.03; average 2.13 ± 0.99 mm.
- **PES (9 mo)**: 11.89 ± 1.75, median 12.5 (range 8–14) — above the acceptable threshold of 7.
- **FMPS** 15.54 → 10.0 (p<0.001, d=1.71); **FMBS** 11.08 → 7.04 (p<0.001, d=1.73).
- **Predictors (linear regression)**: implant site (lateral/canine > central) and female gender were significant positive predictors of platform deviation (R=0.593, p=0.003) and apical deviation (R=0.355, p=0.036). Angular-deviation model was unfit (ANOVA p=0.114).
- **Survival**: baseline table lists 26/26 (100%); narrative reports "two implants that failed" — an internal inconsistency in the published paper.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single surgeon; **no comparison/control group**; small sample (n=26). Single-arm before-after design — no direct evidence the plateau protocol outperforms standard guided IIP.
- Deviation measured by cast/scan-body workflow (not intraoral scan); authors note IOS would be more reliable.
- 2-guide protocol needs experienced clinicians and adds cost. Authors call for RCTs with larger samples, longer follow-up, multiple operators.

## 6. Related Work

- Safe Angle Concept originated as a CBCT planning study (incisal root angle defines the safe shoulder range).
- Contrasts with apico-palatal free-hand guiding-slot and dynamic-navigation slot techniques for slippage control.
- Botermans et al.: 80% labial perforation with purely prosthetic-driven IIP positioning; ~76–84% angled-abutment need in simulation studies.

## 7. Glossary

- **IIP** — immediate implant placement (type 1, into fresh extraction socket).
- **Safe Angle Concept** — CBCT-based planning rule using the incisal root angle to define a safe range for the implant shoulder, aiming to avoid labial malposition and angled abutments.
- **Plateau drilling** — flattening the palatal slope of the socket with a flat milling cutter (slightly narrower than implant) before conventional drilling, to reduce drill slippage.
- **Global platform / apex deviation** — linear 3D displacement between planned and actual implant at the platform / apex center.
- **PES** — Pink Esthetic Score (7 domains, 0–14).
- **FMPS / FMBS** — full-mouth plaque / bleeding score.
