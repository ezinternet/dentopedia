## Why Ingested

한국 치과의사의 방사선 피폭량을 국가 선량 레지스트리 데이터로 실측한 유일한 국내 코호트. [[radiology/benavides-2023-patient-shielding-dentomaxillofacial-radiography]]의 납복 불필요 논의와 [[radiology/gijbels-2005-dosimetry-digital-panoramic-occupational-exposure]]의 이론적 선량 추정치를 실제 한국 임상현장 데이터로 맥락화.

## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional survey of 658 South Korean dentists linked to national dose registry: mean annual effective dose 0.18 mSv (male) / 0.13 mSv (female), far below the 20 mSv limit, with panoramic radiography being the most frequent procedure.

## 한줄요약

한국 치과의사 658명 국가선량레지스트리 연계 조사: 연평균 유효선량 남성 0.18 mSv, 여성 0.13 mSv — 직업한도(20 mSv/y)의 1% 미만; 파노라마가 가장 빈번한 방사선 시술.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First large-scale Korean cohort linking dentist survey data to National Dose Registry dosimetry
- Documents that panoramic radiography is the most common radiation procedure among Korean dentists, followed by intraoral and portable
- Quantifies sex differences: male dentists perform more radiation procedures and receive higher annual doses
- Identifies provincial vs metropolitan disparity in female dentists
- Provides Korea-specific baseline for occupational dose benchmarking

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Study design**: Cross-sectional survey + registry linkage
- **Population**: 658 dentists (78% male; 51% under 40 years)
- **Survey period**: April 2012 – May 2013
- **Data source**: Survey questionnaire linked with National Dose Registry (Korea Food and Drug Administration)
- **Outcome**: Annual effective dose (mSv), procedure frequency, demographic correlates
- **Statistics**: Multiple linear regression to identify factors associated with radiation dose

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Mean annual effective dose — male | 0.18 mSv |
| Mean annual effective dose — female | 0.13 mSv |
| % employed after 2000 | 61% |
| Most frequent procedure | Panoramic radiography |
| 2nd/3rd most frequent | Intraoral → Portable dental |
| Lead apron use for diagnostic radiology | Higher proportion in male dentists |
| Cumulative dose — older/longer-employed dentists | Significantly greater |

All reported doses were far below the Korean occupational dose limit (20 mSv/year).

Female dentists in provincial areas had **significantly higher** average annual and cumulative effective doses than metropolitan female dentists (sex × location interaction).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only retrieval; detailed dose distributions not confirmed
- Cross-sectional: cannot establish causal relationships
- Survey self-report bias for procedure counts
- Does not separately quantify dose contribution by panoramic vs intraoral modalities
- No data on lead apron use rates or effect on dose

## 6. Related Work

- Lee WJ et al. 2009 (Radiat Prot Dosimetry) — Korean diagnostic radiation workers 1996–2006; dentists included as one of 6 occupational categories
- Gijbels et al. 2005 — panoramic scatter dose at 1 m; provides the physics basis for why Korean dentist doses are so low

## 7. Glossary

- **National Dose Registry (NDR)**: Korea MFDS-maintained individual radiation dose monitoring database for occupationally exposed workers
- **Annual effective dose**: whole-body radiation dose accounting for organ sensitivity weighting (mSv/y)
- **Portable dental radiography**: handheld X-ray unit (휴대용 X선 장치)
