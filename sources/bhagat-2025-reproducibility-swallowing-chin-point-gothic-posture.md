## Why Ingested

Centric relation (CR) recording method 선택 — swallowing vs chin-point guidance vs gothic-arch tracing — 의 재현성과 체위(자세) 영향에 대한 직접 비교 임상 근거를 확보하기 위해 인제스트. Gothic-arch tracing은 [[wiki/occlusion/kwak-2024-masticatory-positioning-occlusal-stability-full-mouth]]의 저작·하악위 안정성 논의와 직접 연결되는 jaw-relation 측정 기법이며, CR/jaw-relation record는 [[wiki/complete-denture/fayad-2025-vertical-dimension-determination-prosthodontics-review]]의 수직고경(VD) 결정과 함께 무치악 보철의 악간관계 기록 단계를 구성한다. 어떤 CR 기법이 가장 재현성이 높고 환자 체위에 둔감한지에 대한 정량 근거(신뢰도 %, 재현성 계수)를 wiki에 보강.

PubMed (PMCID PMC12563705). DOI: [10.4103/jpbs.jpbs_787_25](https://doi.org/10.4103/jpbs.jpbs_787_25).

## One-line Summary

In-vivo clinical comparison (n=30 edentulous/overdenture patients, customized digital intraoral gothic-arch tracer): digital gothic-arch tracing was the most reproducible CR-recording method (~0.98, ~99% reliability), light chin-point guidance second (~0.79, ~88%) and posture-robust (reliable supine), while swallowing was least reproducible (~0.62, ~77%) and only dependable in the upright position.

## 한줄요약

생체 임상 비교 (무치악·오버덴처 환자 30명, 자체 제작 디지털 구내 고딕아치 트레이서): 디지털 고딕아치 트레이싱이 중심위(CR) 기록 재현성이 가장 높았고(~0.98, 신뢰도 ~99%), 라이트 턱끝유도(chin-point)가 두 번째(~0.79, ~88%)이면서 앙와위(supine)에서도 신뢰할 만했으며, 삼킴법(swallowing)은 재현성이 가장 낮고(~0.62, ~77%) 직립(upright) 자세에서만 믿을 만했다.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Direct head-to-head reproducibility comparison of three centric-relation (CR) recording techniques — **swallowing (Method 1)**, **chin-point / light chin positioning guidance (Method 2)**, and **gothic-arch tracing (Method 3)** — in the same edentulous/overdenture patients.
2. Quantifies CR reproducibility as agreement coefficients and reliability percentages, with statistical significance (all p=0.000).
3. Adds the **body-posture variable** (upright vs supine): which methods stay reliable when the patient's posture changes during recording.
4. Uses a **custom digital intraoral gothic-arch tracer** (intraoral sensor pad + MAX232 digitizer + MATLAB analysis) recording X/Y coordinates of mandibular position, giving an objective digital readout rather than visual/scratch-plate interpretation.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Participants**: 30 patients with well-formed ridges and adequate inter-arch space, no TMJ disorders. Two groups:
  - **Group 1**: one edentulous arch + opposing overdenture-indicated arch — alloy copings over RCT-treated teeth, permanent record bases with occlusal rims.
  - **Group 2**: both arches edentulous — complete denture bases from secondary casts after zinc-oxide-eugenol impressions.
- **Device**: Custom digital intraoral gothic-arch tracing system — intraoral sensor pad + central bearing plate, extra-oral MAX232 digitizer circuit, MATLAB analysis software. Outputs X and Y coordinates of the mandibular position.
- **Methods compared**: (1) swallowing; (2) chin-point guidance (light chin positioning / jaw positioning); (3) gothic-arch tracing.
- **Posture conditions**: each method recorded in **upright** and **supine (back)** body postures.
- **Outcome**: reproducibility/agreement coefficients and reliability %, plus stability of X/Y coordinates across postures. Swallowing performed with sealed oral cavity and closed lips.
- **Ethics**: DY Patil Dental School ethics committee (G-148/IECDYPDS/2024); informed consent obtained.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

Reproducibility (Table 1; coefficient / reliability %, all p=0.000, significant at 0.05):

| Method | Coefficient | Reliability |
|---|---|---|
| **Method 1 — Swallowing** | 0.62 | 76.54% |
| Method 1 vs Method 3 | 0.69 | 81.65% |
| **Method 2 — Chin positioning** | 0.79 | 88.26% |
| Method 2 vs Method 3 | 0.78 | 87.64% |
| Method 2 vs Method 3 (alt) | 0.79 | 88.26% |
| **Method 3 — Gothic arch tracing** | 0.98 | 98.98% |

- **Gothic-arch tracing (Method 3)** = most reproducible (~0.98 / ~99%), **excellent reproducibility regardless of posture** (no significant change between upright and supine).
- **Chin-point guidance (Method 2)** = second best (~0.79 / ~88%); high reproducibility and consistency, **reliable in the supine posture**; Y-coordinate stable in the back position.
- **Swallowing (Method 1)** = least reproducible (~0.62 / ~77%); **most reliable only in the upright position**.
- **Clinical bottom line**: among posture-sensitive bedside methods, **light chin-point guidance is more reproducible than swallowing and is dependable supine**, making it the preferable clinical choice over swallowing; gothic-arch tracing remains the overall reproducibility benchmark and is posture-independent.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample (n=30), single center, no long-term prosthesis-outcome correlation — reproducibility is a surrogate, not a clinical success endpoint.
- Custom (non-commercial) digital tracer; results may not transfer directly to other devices.
- Full-text body is abbreviated in the PMC version (some procedural and statistical detail compressed); exact CI/SD per posture not fully tabulated.
- Heterogeneous groups (overdenture vs fully edentulous) pooled.
- No blinding/operator-variability analysis reported in the retrieved text.

## 7. Glossary

- **Centric relation (CR)**: maxillomandibular relationship in which condyles are in their most anterosuperior braced position; a reproducible reference jaw position for prosthesis construction.
- **Gothic-arch (arrow-point) tracing**: lateral/protrusive mandibular border-movement tracing producing an arrow whose apex marks CR.
- **Chin-point guidance**: operator lightly guides the mandible (chin) to seat the condyles for CR registration.
- **Swallowing method**: patient swallows with lips sealed to reflexively position the mandible at CR.
- **Reproducibility coefficient / reliability %**: degree to which repeated recordings of the same method land on the same X/Y mandibular position.
