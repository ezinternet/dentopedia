## Why Ingested

[[immediate-implant/kim-2016-immediately-placed-implant-without-primary]] already shows a single immediate implant with no primary stability can osseointegrate; this 16-case retrospective extends that anecdote into a cohort with a 100% survival rate at 65 months, directly testing whether the insertion-torque/ISQ thresholds emphasized by [[immediate-implant/stanley-2020-five-thread-guideline-predicting-primary]] are hard prerequisites or merely favorable conditions for immediate flapless placement.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective case series (16 flapless immediate SLA implants, ITV <10 Ncm and mean initial ISQ <55) — mean ISQ rose from 42.2 to 68.7 and survival was 100% at mean 65.18 months, suggesting low/no primary stability is not an absolute contraindication for immediate placement.

## 한줄요약

후향 증례군 (16개 무피판 즉시 SLA 임플란트, 식립토크 <10 Ncm·초기 ISQ <55) — 평균 ISQ가 42.2에서 68.7로 상승하고 평균 65.18개월에 생존율 100%로, 낮거나 없는 초기 안정성이 즉시식립의 절대 금기는 아님을 시사.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Directly addresses an under-studied niche: immediately placed implants with **low OR no primary stability** (most literature covers high-stability immediates or low-stability *delayed* placements).
- Demonstrates 100% survival at mean 65.18 months despite all implants having insertion torque <10 Ncm and mean initial ISQ <55.
- Quantifies the primary→secondary stability transition: mean ISQ 42.2 → 68.7 over ~13 weeks (Wilcoxon signed-rank, p<0.01; 95% CI of ISQ difference 17.2–32.9, large effect size).
- Reinforces the clinical principle that the **trend/trajectory** of ISQ (rising) matters more than the absolute initial value.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Retrospective chart review of implants placed Apr 2014–May 2022; all by a single surgeon, flapless (no flap elevation).
- **Cohort**: 16 SLA-surfaced implants in 16 patients (7 M, 9 F; mean age 48.8 y). Sites: 13 maxilla / 3 mandible; 6 anterior, 2 premolar, 8 molar (per body: 6 maxillary anterior, 2 maxillary premolar, 5 maxillary molar, 3 mandibular molar).
- **Inclusion**: low primary stability immediately after flapless placement.
- **Low primary stability definition**: insertion torque value <10 Ncm **AND** mean initial ISQ <55.
- **Surgical modifications**: one-step undersized drilling per manufacturer protocol; bone grafting in all but one case; submerged protocol in only one patient.
- **Loading**: prosthetic loading applied like natural dentition; opposing dentition was natural teeth or implants.
- **Stability measurement**: Osstell ISQ (RFA, 3rd-gen), recorded in 4 directions (M/D/B/L) at placement and at impression-taking; cases that spun at placement (unmeasurable ISQ) treated as missing values.
- **MBL**: panoramic radiographs at prosthesis delivery and final follow-up; measured implant shoulder to crestal bone at mesial/distal.
- **Statistics**: Wilcoxon signed-rank test (SPSS v25).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Survival**: 100% at mean follow-up 65.18 months (range 12–128 months).
- **ISQ**: mean rose 42.2 ± 7.5 → 68.7 ± 7.6 (p<0.01); 95% CI of difference 17.2–32.9; large effect size.
- **Initial ISQ distribution**: 8 implants <50 (mean 39.6), 2 implants ≥50 (mean 51.0), 6 unmeasurable (spinning).
- **Impression timing**: mean ~13 weeks post-placement (range 8–25 weeks); 6 implants reached ≈70+ ISQ by impression, others rose but did not reach target yet still favorable.
- **Extended healing cases**: Patients 3, 5, 10 had persistently low ISQ requiring 6–14 week extensions before adequate stability.
- **MBL**: positive (bone loss) in most cases; 4 cases showed negative values (slight bone gain) of –0.1 to –0.3 mm.
- No clinical signs of infection, inflammation, or bleeding at final follow-up.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample (n=16), single center, single surgeon.
- Heterogeneity in extraction causes, implant sites, fixture surfaces, and implant sizes.
- Inclusion of cases with short follow-up periods (min 12 months).
- No control group (high-stability immediates or delayed placements).
- Authors call for larger, long-term systematic studies.

## 7. Glossary

- **Primary stability**: mechanical fixation achieved from pre-existing bone at the moment of placement.
- **Secondary stability**: biological fixation from osseointegration with newly formed bone.
- **Stability dip**: ~4–6 week window where falling primary stability is not yet replaced by rising secondary stability.
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: 1–100 scale from resonance frequency analysis (RFA); higher = more stable.
- **ITV (insertion torque value)**: rotational resistance (Ncm) during placement; surrogate for primary stability.
- **SLA**: sandblasted, large-grit, acid-etched implant surface.
- **MBL (marginal bone level)**: peri-implant crestal bone height change; positive = bone loss here.
- **Flapless**: implant placed without elevating a mucoperiosteal flap.
