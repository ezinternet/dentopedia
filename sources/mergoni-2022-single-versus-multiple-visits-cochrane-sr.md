## Why Ingested

근관치료의 단회 vs 다회 내원 프로토콜 비교는 임상 의사결정의 핵심 질문이며, 기존 위키에 이 주제의 SR+MA 페이지가 없어 근거 공백이 있었음. [[endodontics/visit-protocol/mergoni-2022-single-versus-multiple-visits-cochrane-sr]]는 47 RCT(5805명)를 종합한 Cochrane pub4 업데이트로, [[wiki/endodontics/ordinola-zapata-2022-intracanal-medicaments-status-directions]]에서 논의되는 interappointment medication의 임상적 가치를 성과 관점에서 직접 보완한다.

## One-line Summary

Cochrane SR+MA pub4 (47 RCTs, n=5805 teeth): single-visit and multiple-visit root canal treatment show equivalent radiological outcomes; single-visit has modestly higher pain within 1 week (RR 1.55, moderate certainty).

## 한줄요약

Cochrane SR+MA pub4 (47건 RCT, 5805명): 단회·다회 근관치료의 방사선학적 성공률은 동등하며, 단회군에서 1주 내 통증 보고 비율이 약간 높음 (RR 1.55, 중등도 근거).

- **Journal**: Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews
- **Year**: 2022, Issue 12 (pub4; edited reprint published Issue 1, 2023)
- **DOI**: 10.1002/14651858.CD005296.pub4
- **Study type**: Systematic review with meta-analysis (Cochrane Intervention Review, 4th edition)
- **Search date**: 25 April 2022
- **Previous versions**: pub1 (2007), pub2/pub3 (2016)

## 2. Key Contributions

- Provides the most current Cochrane-level evidence synthesis on single-visit vs multiple-visit root canal treatment (RoCT) for permanent teeth
- Updated exclusion criteria: 5 studies from prior versions were dropped for not meeting current standard of care (rubber dam isolation + NaOCl irrigation)
- Applied GRADE certainty-of-evidence framework across all outcomes, replacing older narrative-only certainty assessments
- Confirmed primary finding of no clinically meaningful difference in tooth survival or radiological failure between the two protocols
- Identified single-visit RoCT as having higher short-term pain (within 1 week) for vital teeth and when mechanical instrumentation was used — a clinically actionable subgroup finding
- Found no difference in analgesic use (contrary to pub3 conclusions), suggesting post-treatment pain, while statistically more frequent, may not differ in analgesic requirement

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Systematic review + meta-analysis following Cochrane methods
- **Population**: People aged >10 years needing RoCT for permanent teeth (irreversible pulpitis or pulp necrosis)
- **Intervention**: Single-visit RoCT (instrumentation + irrigation + obturation in one appointment)
- **Comparator**: Multiple-visit RoCT (obturation at a separate, later appointment, with or without interappointment intracanal medication)
- **Study inclusion**: RCTs and quasi-RCTs only
- **Primary outcomes**:
  1. Tooth extraction due to endodontic problems
  2. Radiological failure (periapical radiolucency) at ≥1 year
- **Secondary outcomes**:
  3. Postoperative / post-obturation pain (dichotomous and continuous)
  4. Swelling or flare-up
  5. Analgesic use
  6. Presence of sinus tract or fistula at ≥1 month
- **Statistical approach**: Risk ratio (RR) with 95% CI for dichotomous outcomes; mean difference (MD) for continuous pain; I² for heterogeneity; GRADE for evidence certainty
- **Subgroup analyses**: Pulp vitality (vital vs non-vital), instrumentation type (manual vs mechanical), use of interappointment medication, preoperative diagnosis (normal vs periapical pathology)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Comparison | Result | Certainty |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tooth extraction | Single vs multiple | RR 0.46 (95% CI 0.09–2.50); I²=0%; 2 studies, 402 teeth | Very low |
| Radiological failure | Single vs multiple | RR 0.93 (95% CI 0.81–1.07); I²=0%; 13 studies, 1505 teeth | Moderate |
| Pain within 1 week (proportion) | Single vs multiple | RR 1.55 (95% CI 1.14–2.09); I²=18%; 5 studies, 638 teeth | Moderate |
| Pain ≤72 h post-obturation (proportion) | Single vs multiple | RR 0.97 (95% CI 0.81–1.16); I²=70%; 12 studies, 1329 teeth | Low |
| Pain intensity ≤72 h (VAS/NRS) | Single vs multiple | MD 0.26 (95% CI −4.76–5.29); I²=98%; 12 studies, 1258 teeth | Low |
| Pain at 1 week post-obturation | Single vs multiple | RR 1.05 (95% CI 0.67–1.67); I²=61%; 9 studies, 1139 teeth | Very low |
| Swelling / flare-up | Single vs multiple | RR 0.56 (95% CI 0.16–1.92); I²=0%; 6 studies, 605 teeth | Very low |
| Analgesic use | Single vs multiple | RR 1.25 (95% CI 0.75–2.09); I²=36%; 6 studies, 540 teeth | Very low |
| Sinus tract / fistula | Single vs multiple | RR 1.00 (95% CI 0.24–4.28); I²=0%; 5 studies, 650 teeth | Very low |

**Subgroup findings for pain within 1 week:**
- Vital teeth (single vs multiple): RR 2.16 (95% CI 1.39–3.36); I²=0%; 2 studies, 316 teeth
- Mechanical instrumentation (single vs multiple): RR 1.80 (95% CI 1.10–2.92); I²=56%; 2 studies, 278 teeth
- No subgroup difference for non-vital teeth or manual instrumentation

**Study quality**: 10 studies low risk of bias; 17 high risk; 20 unclear. Largest study: 390 teeth. Smallest: 26 teeth. Most studies from India (12).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 2 studies reported tooth extraction — the most clinically meaningful primary outcome is severely underpowered
- High heterogeneity in pain outcomes (I² up to 98%) reduces confidence in pooled pain estimates
- Most studies are small; largest single study has n=390
- Geographic skew (12/47 studies from India) may affect generalizability
- Blinding of participants and operators is inherently difficult for procedural trials, inflating risk of bias
- Review explicitly excludes studies not using rubber dam and NaOCl, which excludes historical evidence but aligns with current standard of care
- Future RCTs should standardize: outcome timing, pain measurement tools, definition of "multiple visits," and follow-up duration (ideally ≥2 years)
- Quality of life, patient preference, and treatment cost outcomes are absent from current evidence base

## 6. Related Work

- Previous Cochrane editions: Figini L et al. (2007, 2016) — same review, earlier versions; conclusions consistent
- Ordinola-Zapata R et al. (2022): narrative review on intracanal medicaments — provides mechanistic rationale for multiple-visit approach with medication
- Halvorsen SE et al. (meta-analyses on post-endodontic pain): cited in background for postoperative pain context
- AAE guidelines on single-visit endodontics: position aligns with review finding of equivalence

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| RoCT (Root Canal Treatment) | Endodontic procedure to remove pulpal tissue, disinfect canals, and obturate the root canal system |
| Single-visit RoCT | Instrumentation, irrigation, and obturation completed in one appointment without interappointment medication |
| Multiple-visit RoCT | Treatment completed over ≥2 appointments; obturation deferred to final visit; may include interappointment intracanal medication (e.g., calcium hydroxide) |
| Radiological failure | Presence of periapical radiolucency on radiograph at ≥1 year post-treatment |
| Flare-up | Acute exacerbation of symptoms (severe pain ± swelling) requiring unscheduled visit within 48–72 h of treatment |
| GRADE | Grading of Recommendations, Assessment, Development and Evaluations — framework for rating certainty of evidence (high/moderate/low/very low) |
| RR (Risk Ratio) | Ratio of event probability in the experimental group to event probability in the control group |
| I² | Measure of statistical heterogeneity across studies in a meta-analysis (0–100%); values >50% indicate substantial heterogeneity |
| Rubber dam isolation | Latex/non-latex sheet placed around tooth to isolate operative field from oral contamination — required standard of care for RoCT |
| NaOCl | Sodium hypochlorite — primary antimicrobial irrigant used in root canal disinfection |
