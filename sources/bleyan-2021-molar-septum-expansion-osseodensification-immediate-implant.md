## One-line Summary
Retrospective multicenter cohort (131 patients, 145 immediate molar implants, 5 centers, 2015–2020) where OD expanded the interradicular septum from mean 3.3 mm to 4.65 mm osteotomy diameter; cumulative implant survival 93.1% (10 failures: 7 healing-phase, 3 post-loading).

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Retrospective multicenter cohort study
- **Journal**: Journal of Functional Biomaterials 2021;12:66 (MDPI, open access)
- **DOI**: 10.3390/jfb12040066
- **Sample**: 131 patients receiving 145 immediate molar implants across 5 centers, August 2015 – September 2020, follow-up up to 5 years
- **Primary Question**: Is osseodensification a predictable method for immediate molar implant placement via interradicular septum expansion, and can a new molar-socket classification organize case selection?

## 2. Key Contributions
- Largest multicenter dataset to date on OD for immediate molar implant placement (n = 145 implants).
- Quantifies septum geometry change: baseline mean septum width 3.3 mm → mean post-instrumentation osteotomy diameter 4.65 mm.
- Reports cumulative implant survival 93.1% with up-to-5-year follow-up.
- 10 total failures: 7 during healing, 3 post-loading.
- Introduces a new molar socket classification system for immediate implant case selection (specific class definitions in the body of the paper, not extractable from front/back).
- Positions OD as alternative to standard drilling in the interradicular septum where conventional drills tend to skid off the septum into a root socket.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Retrospective multicenter chart review across five centers. Inclusion criterion: molar tooth extraction followed by immediate implant placement using the osseodensification technique. Primary outcome: pre-instrumentation septum width and post-instrumentation osteotomy diameter (mm). Secondary outcomes: insertion torque (text labels this "(ISQ)" but the context indicates IT and ISQ recorded), implant survival rate. Follow-up between August 2015 and September 2020 (up to 5 years). No comparator group, no blinding, no formal randomization. Survival reported as cumulative rate.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Mean baseline septum width: 3.3 mm.
- Mean post-instrumentation osteotomy diameter: 4.65 mm.
- Implants placed: 145 (in 131 patients).
- Cumulative implant survival: 93.1%.
- Failures: 10 total — 7 within the healing period, 3 after loading.
- Insertion torque and ISQ values were collected but specific mean values are not extractable from front/back text.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Retrospective; no comparator (no head-to-head vs conventional drilling or vs delayed placement).
- Cumulative survival 93.1% is below typical molar-implant benchmarks reported in cited literature (Smith & Tarnow 97.3%, Ketabi SR 98%, Ragucci SR+MA 93.3% at 1 year). Bleyan's 93.1% with up-to-5-year follow-up is broadly in line with the 1-year SR+MA figure but below the high-end retrospective reports.
- "Up to 5 years" mixes follow-up windows — the survival curve over time is not given in extractable text.
- Inventor/licensor (Huwais) and Versah-affiliated authors are co-authors — conflict of interest.
- New molar socket classification introduced but no inter-rater reliability data extractable.
- Septum-width-to-osteotomy-diameter difference (3.3 → 4.65 mm = ~1.35 mm expansion) is modest; for sockets with very thin septa this may still be insufficient for primary stability without additional augmentation.
- No MBL data foregrounded in extractable text.

## 7. Glossary
- **Interradicular septum**: bone wall between the roots of a multi-rooted tooth (e.g., between MB, DB, and palatal roots of an upper molar; between mesial and distal roots of a lower molar). Often the only sound bone available for implant anchoring in a fresh molar socket.
- **Immediate implant placement (IIP)**: implant placement into a fresh extraction socket at the same surgical visit.
- **Cumulative survival rate**: proportion of implants still in function at the end of follow-up, accumulated across the observation window.
- **Smith-Tarnow molar socket classification (cited Ref 14)**: prior classification by Smith and Tarnow (2013) for molar IIP case selection that this paper proposes to update with an OD-aware system.
