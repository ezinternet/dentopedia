## Why Ingested

상악동거상술 후 상악동염(Post-Sinus-Lift Maxillary Sinusitis, PSLMS) 예방 축이 현재 [[wiki/overviews/odontogenic-maxillary-sinusitis-overview]] 에서 다뤄지지 않는 "고위험 환자의 비침습적 내과적 예방" 관점을 보완한다. 본 증례는 비중격만곡(Septal Deviation), 함기화 중비갑개(Concha Bullosa), 할러 세포(Haller Cell), 알레르기성 비점막 부종이 복합된 환자에서 국소 스테로이드(Fluticasone) + 류코트리엔 수용체 길항제(Leukotriene Receptor Antagonist, LTRA) + 주술기 항생제를 조합해 수술 없이 합병증을 예방한 유일한 보고로, 이비인후과–치과 협진의 임상 프로토콜 근거로 활용한다.

## One-line Summary

Case report (n=1, 48M) demonstrating noninvasive prevention of post-sinus-lift maxillary sinusitis in a high-risk patient (septal deviation, concha bullosa, Haller cell, allergic rhinitis) via preoperative topical steroid + leukotriene receptor antagonist + perioperative antibiotics, with uneventful bilateral sinus lift outcome at 7 months.

## 한줄요약

증례 보고 (n=1, 48세 남성): 비중격만곡·함기화 중비갑개·할러 세포·알레르기 비점막 부종이 복합된 상악동거상술 고위험 환자에서 국소 스테로이드 + 류코트리엔 수용체 길항제 + 주술기 항생제 조합으로 수술 없이 상악동염 합병증을 예방하여 양측 상악동거상술 후 7개월 경과 이상 없음.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First reported use of leukotriene receptor antagonist (pranlukast hydrate) combined with topical steroid as part of a non-surgical sinusitis prevention regimen before sinus lift
- Demonstrates that otorhinolaryngology consultation + medical conservative therapy can circumvent the need for presinus lift surgical intervention (FESS/septoplasty) even in a multi-factor high-risk patient
- Operationalizes the Pignataro "reversible contraindication" classification into a concrete medical protocol
- Highlights the rationale for avoiding vasoconstrictors/nasal decongestants: risk of further reducing sinus oxygen tension

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Single case report
- **Patient**: 48-year-old Japanese male; planned bilateral sinus lift for implant placement by periodontist; referred to otorhinolaryngology for nasal congestion (1 year duration)
- **Diagnostic workup**: Preoperative CT + nasal endoscopic examination revealing:
  - Septal deviation (left)
  - Concha bullosa of middle turbinate (left)
  - Haller cell (left)
  - Nasal mucosal swelling (allergic rhinitis)
  - Maxillary sinus accessory ostium (left)
  - No active sinusitis at baseline
- **Risk classification**: "Reversible otorhinolaryngological contraindication" (Pignataro criteria)
- **Treatment options presented**: (1) Medical conservative therapy vs (2) FESS + septoplasty ± turbinectomy/minimally invasive middle meatal antrostomy (Kunihiro). Patient selected (1)
- **Medical protocol**:
  - Topical nasal steroid: fluticasone propionate aqueous nasal spray (preoperative, 2 months)
  - Leukotriene receptor antagonist (LTRA): pranlukast hydrate (preoperative, 2 months)
  - Perioperative antibiotic prophylaxis: amoxicillin (Sawacillin®)
- **Surgical procedure**: Bilateral maxillary sinus floor elevation with Cytrans Granules® (GC Company, Tokyo) bone graft material
- **Follow-up**: 2-month panoramic radiography post-bilateral sinus lift; 7-month uneventful clinical follow-up

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Nasal congestion resolved almost completely after 2 months of medical therapy
- Bilateral sinus lift performed without intraoperative complications
- No maxillary sinusitis detected on 2-month postoperative panoramic radiography
- No clinical symptoms at 7-month follow-up
- Graft material bilaterally visible and appropriately positioned on postoperative panoramic

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single case report (n=1); no comparative data, no control arm
- 7-month follow-up only; longer monitoring needed
- Pranlukast-specific dosing/duration not individually tailored — no titration protocol described
- Authors explicitly call for further clinical trials to validate the "noninvasive" regimen
- The specific contribution of LTRA vs steroid alone cannot be disentangled

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary

- **Septal Deviation (비중격만곡)**: Asymmetric displacement of the nasal septum impairing unilateral sinus drainage
- **Concha Bullosa (함기화 중비갑개)**: Pneumatization of the middle turbinate, narrowing the ostiomeatal complex
- **Haller Cell (할러 세포)**: Infraorbital ethmoid air cell that encroaches on the maxillary ostium
- **Leukotriene Receptor Antagonist (류코트리엔 수용체 길항제, LTRA)**: Anti-inflammatory agent (e.g., pranlukast, montelukast) blocking CysLT1 receptor; reduces nasal mucosal eosinophilic inflammation in allergic rhinitis
- **Ostiomeatal Complex (사골누두·골막복합체)**: Anatomic region comprising the middle meatus, infundibulum, and maxillary/frontal/anterior ethmoid ostia — the critical drainage pathway
- **Reversible Contraindication (가역적 금기증)**: Per Pignataro: conditions correctible by ENT treatment (anatomic impairments, allergic/infective processes), contrasted with irreversible (Wegener's, malignancy, etc.)
- **FESS (기능적 내시경 부비동 수술, Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery)**: Surgical restoration of sinus drainage via endoscopic removal of obstructing anatomy
- **Perioperative Antibiotic Prophylaxis (주술기 항생제 예방요법)**: Antibiotics given around the surgical event to reduce infection risk; standard in sinus lift
