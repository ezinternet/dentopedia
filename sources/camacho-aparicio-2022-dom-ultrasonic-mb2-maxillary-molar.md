## One-line Summary
In vivo validity study (84 patients, CBCT gold standard): MB2 prevalence 79%; DOM alone sensitivity 82%; DOM+ultrasonic 86% sensitivity; specificity 100% all stages; CBCT confirms ground truth.

## 1. Document Information
UNAM, Mexico City. In vivo 3-stage clinical study using CBCT as gold standard. Kappa=91% between examiners.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Rigorous validity assessment using CBCT as objective gold standard
- MB2 prevalence in Mexican population: 79% by CBCT
- Each additional technique improves sensitivity:
  - Explorer alone: 79% sensitivity
  - + DOM: 82% sensitivity  
  - + DOM + ultrasonic: 86% sensitivity
- Specificity 100% and PPV 100% at all stages — no false positives
- NPV improves with each stage: 56% → 60% → 67%

## 3. Methodology
- 84 patients (maxillary first molar for RCT, 91 initial − 7 excluded)
- CBCT: gold standard, read by blinded expert observer
- Stage 1: explorer + mirror (naked eye)
- Stage 2: DOM
- Stage 3: DOM + selective dentin removal with ultrasonic tips
- Validity metrics: sensitivity, specificity, PPV, NPV, PLR, NLR

## 4. Key Results
| Stage | Sensitivity | Specificity | PPV | NPV |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Explorer | 79% | 100% | 100% | 56% |
| DOM | 82% | 100% | 100% | 60% |
| DOM + ultrasonic | **86%** | 100% | 100% | **67%** |

MB2 prevalence by CBCT: 79%

## 5. Limitations
- Single center (Mexico City)
- Clinical location overall: 68% (vs 79% CBCT) — highlights systematic under-detection without tools
- Stage 3 adds ultrasonic dentin removal — irreversible; should be justified by anatomy

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary
- **MB2**: Second mesiobuccal canal of maxillary first molar
- **DOM**: Dental Operating Microscope
- **PPV / NPV**: Positive / Negative Predictive Value
- **PLR / NLR**: Positive / Negative Likelihood Ratio
- **CBCT**: Cone Beam Computed Tomography
