## One-line Summary
Review introducing the "3D Theory of Osseointegration" — three interdependent dimensions (material, topography, time/aging) — UV photofunctionalization resolves time-dependent biological aging of titanium to restore osteoconductivity.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: International Journal of Implant Dentistry 2025; 11:49
- **Study type**: Narrative review

## 2. Key Contributions
- Proposes the 3D Theory of Osseointegration: Dimension 1 (material), Dimension 2 (topography), Dimension 3 (time/aging)
- Identifies biological aging of titanium (hydrocarbon accumulation, loss of hydrophilicity) as the critical unresolved factor
- UV photofunctionalization fully reverses time-dependent degradation
- Dimensions 1 and 3 approaching maturity; Dimension 2 (topography) identified as next frontier

## 3. Key Concepts
- **Biological aging**: titanium surfaces lose hydrophilicity and accumulate hydrocarbons under sterile storage
- **UV photofunctionalization**: UV light removes hydrocarbons, restores super-hydrophilicity → enhanced osseointegration
- **Why BIC never reaches 100%**: partially explained by biological aging at implant surface
- **8% failure plateau**: suggests material/chemistry factors largely exhausted; topography innovation needed
