## One-line Summary
SR+MA of 48 RCTs (n=9,618) — MI in medical care settings shows modest but robust advantage (OR 1.55, 95% CI 1.40–1.71), effective even in brief consultations; promising areas include HIV viral load, dental outcomes, weight, alcohol/tobacco, sedentary behavior. Reference paper for MI's general medical evidence.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Patient Education and Counseling, 93(2):157–168, Nov 2013
- DOI: 10.1016/j.pec.2013.07.012
- PMID: 24001658
- Study type: Systematic review + meta-analysis (sr+ma) of RCTs
- Authorship note: Stephen Rollnick (co-developer of MI) is on this paper — reader should be aware of advocate authorship
- Subsequent commentary: Mullin 2013 (PEC), van der Wouden & Rietmeijer 2014 (PEC) — both criticize/clarify aspects of the meta-analysis

## 2. Key Contributions
- Largest SR+MA of MI in medical (non-mental-health) settings at time of publication
- Quantifies overall MI effect across diverse medical outcomes
- Demonstrates MI works in brief consultations (relevant to medical/dental encounters)
- Identifies high-yield outcome categories
- Maps moderators (location, patient characteristics) — finds MI robust across them

## 3. Methodology
- Database searches for RCTs comparing MI to comparison conditions
- Outcomes isolated to MI's unique effect (vs. mixed interventions)
- Medical care settings only (excludes pure mental health/substance abuse)
- Random-effects meta-analysis
- Subgroup analyses by outcome type, delivery setting, patient characteristics

## 4. Key Results
- **48 studies, n=9,618 participants**
- **Overall effect: OR = 1.55 (95% CI 1.40–1.71), z = 8.67, p < 0.001** — statistically significant modest advantage
- MI particularly effective in:
  - HIV viral load
  - **Dental outcomes** (specifically called out)
  - Death rate
  - Body weight
  - Alcohol and tobacco use
  - Sedentary behavior
  - Self-monitoring
  - Confidence in change
  - Approach to treatment
- MI NOT particularly effective for:
  - Eating disorder behaviors
  - Self-care behaviors
  - Some medical outcomes (e.g., heart rate)
- Robust across moderators (delivery location, patient characteristics)
- Effective even in brief consultations

## 5. Limitations
- **Author bias risk**: Rollnick is co-author and MI co-developer
- Meta-analysis methodology critiqued by Mullin 2013 and van der Wouden 2014 (subsequent commentaries)
- Heterogeneous outcomes pooled (OR comparable across HIV load and body weight is questionable)
- Publication bias possible
- Pre-2013 evidence — newer trials may shift estimates
- "Medical care settings" definition allows broad inclusion

## 7. Glossary
- **MI (Motivational Interviewing)**: counseling method by Miller & Rollnick to elicit behavior change
- **OR (Odds Ratio)**: ratio of odds of outcome between exposed and unexposed groups
- **Brief consultation**: short clinical encounter (typically <20 min)
