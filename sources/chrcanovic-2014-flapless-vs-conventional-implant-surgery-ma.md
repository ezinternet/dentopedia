## Why Ingested

임플란트 수술 시 flap 거상 여부(flapless vs. open flap)가 임플란트 실패율에 미치는 영향을 정량화한 최초 수준의 메타분석으로, 수술 접근법 선택 근거가 필요할 때 핵심 참고문헌. [[wiki/implants/benic-2014-loading-protocols-single-implant-crowns-sr-ma]]와 함께 임플란트 프로토콜 결정 근거로 활용.

## One-line Summary

Meta-analysis of 23 studies (10 RCTs, 7 CCTs, 6 retrospective) found flapless implant surgery associated with significantly higher implant failure rate (RR 1.75, 95% CI 1.07–2.86) compared to open flap, but no significant difference in postoperative infection or marginal bone loss.

## 한줄요약

23개 연구 메타분석에서 무피판(flapless) 임플란트 수술은 개방 피판술 대비 실패율 유의하게 높았으나(RR 1.75), 감염률 및 변연골 소실에는 유의한 차이 없었음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Meta-analysis (PRISMA guidelines)
- **Journal**: PLoS ONE 9(6): e100624
- **Published**: June 20, 2014
- **Institution**: Malmö University, Sweden; Göteborg University, Sweden
- **Search date**: March 2014

## 2. Key Contributions

- First comprehensive meta-analysis directly comparing flapless vs. open flap implant surgery on three clinical outcomes: failure rate, postoperative infection, and marginal bone loss
- Demonstrates that flapless surgery is associated with 75% higher relative risk of implant failure
- Sensitivity analysis reveals that the significant difference in failure rates disappears when only low-risk-of-bias studies are considered
- Confirms no statistically significant effect of flapless technique on postoperative infection or marginal bone loss
- Identifies high heterogeneity among studies and predominantly high risk of bias in the literature

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Study design**: Systematic review + meta-analysis following PRISMA
- **Databases searched**: PubMed, Web of Science, Cochrane Oral Health Group Trials Register
- **Included studies**: 23 publications (10 RCTs, 7 CCTs, 6 retrospective)
- **Risk of bias assessment**: Cochrane recommended approach — 4 criteria: sequence generation, allocation concealment, incomplete outcome data, blinding
  - 16 studies: high risk of bias
  - 2 studies: moderate risk of bias
  - 5 studies: low risk of bias
- **Statistical methods**:
  - I² statistic for heterogeneity (25% = low, 50% = moderate, 75% = high)
  - Inverse variance method; random-effects model where heterogeneity P < 0.10, otherwise fixed-effects
  - Dichotomous outcomes: Risk Ratio (RR) with 95% CI
  - Continuous outcome (MBL): Mean Difference (MD) in mm with 95% CI
  - Funnel plots for publication bias assessment
  - Software: RevMan 5.2.8

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Implant failure rate:**
- Flapless vs. open flap: RR = 1.75 (95% CI 1.07–2.86), P = 0.03 (significant)
- Sensitivity analysis (low risk of bias studies only): difference no longer significant
- Sensitivity analysis (high risk of bias studies only): results driven by this subgroup

**Postoperative infection:**
- RR = 0.96 (95% CI 0.23–4.03), P = 0.96 (not significant)
- No apparent effect of surgical approach on infection rates

**Marginal bone loss:**
- MD = −0.07 mm (95% CI −0.16 to 0.03), P = 0.16 (not significant)
- No clinically meaningful difference between techniques

**Funnel plots**: No indication of publication bias for all three outcomes

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Majority of included studies (16/23) at high risk of bias
- High heterogeneity across studies due to diverse patient populations, implant systems, follow-up periods, surgical protocols
- Sensitivity analysis diminishes the significance of the failure rate finding
- Many studies lack standardized reporting of outcomes
- Need for well-designed RCTs with adequate power and standardized protocols
- Authors caution that results must be interpreted carefully given the bias landscape

## 7. Glossary

- **Flapless surgery**: Implant placement through mucosal tissue without reflecting a mucoperiosteal flap
- **Open flap / conventional flapped surgery**: Traditional technique with flap elevation for direct visualization
- **RR (Risk Ratio)**: Ratio of probability of event in flapless group vs. open flap group
- **MD (Mean Difference)**: Arithmetic difference in means between two groups
- **I²**: Percentage of total variation across studies attributable to heterogeneity rather than chance
- **CCT**: Controlled clinical trial (non-randomized)
- **MBL**: Marginal bone loss — change in crestal bone level around implant
