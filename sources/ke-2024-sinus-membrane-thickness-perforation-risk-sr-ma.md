## Why Ingested

Membrane thickness is repeatedly cited as a Schneiderian-membrane perforation risk factor, but the direction and any usable cutoff have been unclear. This SR+MA isolates membrane thickness specifically (11 CBCT studies, lateral window only) and shows thinner membranes in perforation cases yet *no* significant cutoff relationship — directly reinforcing the sibling multi-factor review [[wiki/sinus-lift/lateral/alalshaikh-2024-membrane-perforation-risk-factors-lateral-sinus-sr-ma]] by quantifying one of its risk factors in detail.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 11 CBCT-based observational studies (1841 lateral sinus lifts): the Schneiderian membrane is significantly thinner in perforation cases (MD −0.91 mm, 95% CI −1.48 to −0.33), but no thickness cutoff (2 / 1.5 / 1 mm) predicts perforation rate — so thickness matters as a continuous trend but offers no clean clinical threshold.

## 1. Document Information

- Type: systematic review + meta-analysis of observational studies (Newcastle-Ottawa Scale; all NOS=7)
- Journal: Med Oral Patol Oral Cir Bucal 2024;29(4):e568-e574
- DOI: 10.4317/medoral.26545 | PMID 38794940 | PMCID PMC11249380
- PROSPERO: CRD42023483303; PRISMA-reported; search completed 4 Dec 2023 (Embase, PubMed, Web of Science)
- Question (PECO): in patients undergoing lateral window sinus lift, does sinus membrane thickness (thin vs thick / per-mm) influence intraoperative Schneiderian membrane perforation rate?

## 2. Key Contributions

- Isolates membrane thickness as a single perforation risk factor for the **lateral window technique only** (excludes osteotome/transcrestal), unlike prior reviews that pooled techniques or cutoffs.
- Runs two distinct meta-analyses: (1) continuous membrane thickness in perforation vs non-perforation cases, and (2) subgroup pooling by thickness cutoff (2, 1.5, 1 mm).
- Documents the apparent paradox: thinner membranes in perforation cases on the continuous analysis, yet no significant cutoff-based association.
- Surfaces the proposed **U-shaped** thickness–perforation relationship (very thin AND very thick membranes perforate more; lowest risk ~1–1.5 mm) as an explanation for null cutoff results.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Inclusion: lateral window sinus lift patients; studies comparing thick vs thin membranes reporting perforation rate, OR reporting membrane thickness in perforation/non-perforation cases.
- Exclusion: osteotome sinus lift, studies not reporting thickness or perforation, conference abstracts, unpublished data.
- Screening: 1104 citations → 482 after dedup → 27 relevant → 11 included (reviewer agreement kappa=0.95).
- Included studies: published 2014–2023; 2 prospective, 9 retrospective; China, Iran, Austria, USA, Korea, Taiwan, Turkey, Switzerland; cumulatively 1841 sinus lift procedures; mean age 46.3–57 y.
- All 11 studies measured membrane thickness with preoperative **CBCT**. Perforation rate across studies 10–37%.
- Random-effects model (RevMan 5.3); continuous → mean difference (MD), binary → odds ratio (OR), 95% CI; I² for heterogeneity (>50% substantial); leave-one-out and funnel plot for outliers/publication bias.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Continuous thickness (7 studies):** membrane significantly THINNER in perforation cases — MD −0.91 mm (95% CI −1.48, −0.33), I²=94%. 6 of 7 studies showed thinner membranes in perforation cases. Leave-one-out: no outlier, significance unchanged. Funnel plot: no gross asymmetry (no publication bias).
- **Cutoff 2 mm (4 studies):** OR 0.97 (95% CI 0.44, 2.17), I²=56% — non-significant.
- **Cutoff 1.5 mm:** OR 0.66 (95% CI 0.29, 1.48), I²=72% — non-significant.
- **Cutoff 1 mm:** OR 0.93 (95% CI 0.34, 2.56) — non-significant.
- Interpretation: thinner membrane associates with perforation on a continuous scale, but dichotomizing at any tested cutoff loses the signal — likely from few studies per cutoff, inconsistent cutoffs, and a possible U-shaped (non-monotonic) relationship.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small number of studies per meta-analysis; inconsistent cutoffs and reporting precluded pooling all 11 studies in either analysis.
- Crude (unadjusted) data — perforation also depends on edentulous span size, lateral window thickness, instrument type, septae, graft volume, and operator experience, none adjusted for.
- Perforation **size** not accounted for (small perforations manageable; very large may abort the procedure).
- All thickness measured by CBCT, which may not equal histological thickness.
- Calls for further studies on the role of membrane thickness (ideally modeling the U-shaped relationship rather than arbitrary cutoffs).

## 6. Related Work

- Monje (meta-analysis of 6 studies): no significant difference by thick/thin lining but a trend toward fewer perforations with thicker membranes; pooled all cutoffs together (no subgroup analysis).
- Fang: combined lateral + osteotome techniques, lower perforation with thickened sinuses (normal 14% vs thick 6%).
- Lin: described the U-shaped curve — perforation 17% at ≤0.5 mm, 25% at >3 mm, lowest 7% at 1–1.5 mm.
- Pommer (cadaver, n=20): membrane stretches to 125–133% of original size; >7.3 N/mm² causes perforation; thicker membranes tolerate higher load.

## 7. Glossary

- **Schneiderian membrane**: the maxillary sinus lining (pseudostratified columnar epithelium + vascular lamina propria + periosteum); must be elevated intact during sinus augmentation.
- **Lateral window technique**: sinus augmentation via a bony window in the lateral maxillary wall; used when residual bone height <5 mm.
- **Membrane perforation**: intraoperative tear of the Schneiderian membrane (~30% of sinus lifts); can cause graft dislodgement and postsurgical sinusitis.
- **Mean difference (MD)**: pooled effect for a continuous outcome (here, mm of membrane thickness).
- **U-shaped relationship**: non-monotonic risk where both very thin and very thick membranes carry higher perforation risk, with a low-risk midrange.
- **CBCT**: cone beam computed tomography, used preoperatively to measure membrane thickness.
- **NOS**: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale for observational study quality (all included studies scored 7).
