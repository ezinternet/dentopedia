## Why Ingested

교합력 (Bite Force, BF)과 임플란트 안정성 지수 (Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ) 간의 직접적 연관성은 기존 문헌에서 불분명했다. 이 전향적 코호트 연구 (n=80, 18개월)는 BF와 ISQ의 상관관계를 시간·위치·성별 변수로 분석한 최초 연구 중 하나로, [[wiki/implants/isq/andersson-2019-rfa-factors-5year-neoss-survival]]에서 제시한 ISQ 영향 요인 목록에 교합력이라는 새로운 변수를 추가한다.

## One-line Summary

Prospective cohort (n=80, 18 months) showing that bite force (BF) and implant stability (ISQ) are significantly associated and dynamically co-influenced by time, jaw location (posterior > anterior), and sex (males > females).

## 한줄요약

전향적 코호트 연구 (n=80, 18개월): 교합력 (Bite Force, BF)과 임플란트 안정성 지수 (ISQ)는 유의하게 연관되며, 시간·위치(구치부>전치부)·성별(남>여) 모두가 두 지표에 복합적으로 영향을 미친다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: PLOS ONE
- **Published**: January 20, 2026
- **Study type**: Prospective cohort study
- **Sample**: 80 participants (25–40 years), 235 implants (Straumann® / Medintika®), 4 groups (anterior/posterior × male/female), 3 follow-up visits
- **Follow-up**: Immediately post-crown loading, 6 months, 18 months
- **Setting**: Maxillofacial Surgery Department, College of Dentistry, Al-Ameed University, Karbala, Iraq
- **Ethics**: Ethics Committee #52017, University of Al-Ameed (February 2021); Declaration of Helsinki

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First study to quantify the direct association between maximum bite force (MBF) and ISQ using concurrent measurement in a controlled prospective design.
2. Demonstrates that BF–ISQ correlation is dynamically modulated by time, jaw location, and sex — none of these factors can be considered in isolation.
3. Males exhibit significantly higher posterior BF and higher posterior ISQ than females across all three time points.
4. Posterior implants show greater stability (ISQ) than anterior implants, mirroring the higher bite forces in the posterior region.
5. Provides mixed-effects regression model (ISQ ~ BF + time + location + gender) formalising the multi-variable relationship.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Bite force measurement**: Loadstar™ sensor (DI-100U, 16-bit load cell interface; 100–400 N range); maximum bite force (MBF) recorded in Newtons at the first molar region bilaterally; 3 trials per side, 2-minute rest between trials; intra-operator reliability confirmed.
- **Stability measurement**: Resonance Frequency Analysis (RFA) using Osstell ISQ Mentor (Smartpeg per implant; measurements from 3 directions; ISQ scale 0–100).
- **Implant systems**: Straumann® and Medintika® implants; diameters 3.3–4.0 mm; lengths 9–11 mm.
- **Loading protocol**: Immediate loading (crown placed day of surgery) for all subjects.
- **Statistical analyses**: One-way repeated-measures ANOVA (within-group time effects); Two-way and three-way ANOVA (time × location × gender interactions); Bonferroni post-hoc; Pearson correlation (BF vs ISQ); Shapiro-Wilk normality; Mixed-effects regression (ISQ ~ BF + time + location + gender with 95% CIs); Sphericity tested with Mauchly, violations handled with Greenhouse-Geisser correction.
- **Sample size**: G*Power calculation; effect size F = 0.4, α = 0.05, power = 80% → 18/group minimum; 80 enrolled to offset dropout.
- **Exclusion**: Systemic disease affecting bone metabolism, periodontitis, smoking, alcohol, parafunctional habits, delayed loading protocol.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Posterior BF (males)**: Significantly higher than females at all three visits (p < 0.05).
- **Posterior ISQ (males)**: Significantly higher than females at all three visits.
- **Two-way ANOVA**: BF and ISQ values significantly influenced by interaction of time × location × gender (p < 0.05).
- **BF–ISQ association**: Significant positive correlation confirmed by Pearson r and regression coefficient (b), modulated by time, location, and gender.
- **Temporal trend**: Both BF and ISQ change dynamically from immediate loading through 18 months, suggesting that osseointegration maturation alters the BF–ISQ relationship over time.
- **Anterior vs. posterior**: Posterior implants consistently show higher ISQ, consistent with higher posterior BF providing mechanical stimulation to bone.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-centre study (Iraq); geographic/ethnic generalizability unclear.
- Immediate loading only — no delayed-loading comparison group.
- Age range restricted (25–40 years); findings may not extrapolate to older patients or those with bone loss.
- Implant brand variation (Straumann + Medintika) introduces confounding surface/design variables.
- No marginal bone level (MBL) or radiographic measurements; long-term bone remodelling not assessed.
- Future studies should include diverse populations, multiple loading protocols, and integrate MBL/CBCT outcomes.

## 7. Glossary

- **BF (Bite Force)**: Maximum occlusal force generated by jaw musculature during biting; measured in Newtons (N).
- **ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)**: Dimensionless index (0–100) from resonance frequency analysis (RFA); higher values indicate greater implant-bone stiffness.
- **RFA (Resonance Frequency Analysis)**: Non-invasive method measuring implant–bone interface stiffness via magnetic excitation of a Smartpeg transducer.
- **MBF (Maximum Bite Force)**: Peak occlusal force recorded at the molar region during maximum voluntary clenching.
- **Immediate loading**: Crown placement on the same day as implant insertion.
- **Osseointegration**: Direct structural and functional contact between living bone and the implant surface.
