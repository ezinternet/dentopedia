## Why Ingested

단일 임플란트 유지 하악 피개의치(single implant-retained overdenture)의 임플란트 생존율, 합병증, 부하 프로토콜 및 어태치먼트 유형별 성능을 정량화한 SR+MA로, [[complete-denture/prasad-2024-attachment-systems-mandibular-implant]]의 2개 임플란트 중심 근거를 단일 임플란트 맥락으로 보완하고, [[complete-denture/bi-2022-attachment-type-implant-number-overdenture]]의 임플란트 수 비교 근거를 확장한다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (17 studies, 11 in meta-analysis) reporting 5-year implant survival of 91.93% and 10-year survival of 84.62% for single implant-retained mandibular overdentures, with delayed loading showing highest survival and locator abutments requiring most maintenance.

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (17편, 11편 메타분석): 단일 임플란트 하악 피개의치의 5년 생존율 91.93%, 10년 생존율 84.62%이며, 지연 부하 시 생존율 최고, 로케이터 어태치먼트는 유지관리 빈도 최다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of Prosthodontics, Vol. 29, Issue 4, April 2020, pp. 287–297
- **DOI**: 10.1111/jopr.13133
- **First published online**: 18 December 2019
- **Study design**: Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **PICO question**: Which surgical and prosthetic protocols result in the highest survival and complication rates of implants and prostheses employed in a single implant-retained overdenture for the rehabilitation of a completely edentulous mandibular ridge?

## 2. Key Contributions

- Establishes survival estimates for single implant-retained mandibular overdentures using Poisson regression modeling
- Compares loading protocols (immediate, early, delayed) for implant survival
- Quantifies complication/maintenance event rates by attachment type (ball abutment vs. locator abutment)
- Confirms single implant overdenture as a cost-effective, minimally invasive treatment modality
- Identifies delayed loading as the optimal loading protocol for implant survival

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search databases**: PubMed, ScienceDirect, COCHRANE, LILACS, IndeMED, OVID, EMBASE, NIH Clinical Trials
- **Inclusion criteria**: English-language articles with minimum 1-year follow-up on single implant-retained overdentures
- **Initial yield**: 2,083 titles/abstracts screened
- **Systematic review**: 17 studies selected
- **Meta-analysis**: 11 studies included
- **Statistical model**: Poisson regression to estimate implant and prosthesis survival rates
- **Risk of bias tools**: ROBINS-I (non-randomized studies), RoB 2 (randomized trials)
- **Outcome measures**: Implant survival, prosthesis survival, complication rates by attachment type and loading protocol

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Implant Survival
- **5-year estimated survival**: 91.93%
- **10-year estimated survival**: 84.62%

### Loading Protocol Comparison
- **Delayed loading**: Highest survival rates overall
- **Immediate loading** vs. early loading: Higher survival at 5 years (p = 0.849) and 10 years (p = 0.464), but differences not statistically significant

### Attachment Type Complications
- **Ball abutment**: Fracture event rate 10.8 (95% CI: 10.5–11.09) per 100 prosthesis years
- **Locator abutment**: Maintenance event rate 16.84 (95% CI: 16.01–17.66) per 100 prosthesis years — highest maintenance burden

### Overall Conclusion
- Single implant-retained overdenture is cost-effective, minimally invasive, and clinically viable
- High implant and prosthesis success rates with minimal complications relative to benefit provided

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Heterogeneity in study designs, implant types, and follow-up periods across included studies
- Limited number of RCTs among included studies; many prospective/retrospective cohorts
- The p-values for loading protocol comparisons (0.849 and 0.464) indicate no statistically significant difference — larger RCTs needed
- Short-to-medium follow-up in many studies; long-term (>10 year) data limited
- Need for standardized outcome reporting in future single-implant overdenture trials

## 7. Glossary

- **Single implant-retained overdenture (SIRO)**: A removable complete denture stabilized by a single endosseous implant placed in the mandibular symphysis region
- **Ball abutment**: Spherical retention attachment on implant; prone to fracture over time
- **Locator abutment**: Nylon-insert retention attachment; high retention but frequent matrix replacement
- **Delayed loading**: Implant loading ≥3 months after placement (conventional protocol)
- **Early loading**: Implant loading 1 week to 3 months after placement
- **Immediate loading**: Implant loaded within 1 week (often same day) of placement
- **Poisson regression**: Statistical model for count/rate data; used here to model implant survival rates as event rates per time unit
- **ROBINS-I**: Risk Of Bias In Non-randomized Studies of Interventions assessment tool
- **RoB 2**: Revised Cochrane risk of bias tool for randomized trials
