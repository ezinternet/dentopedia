## Why Ingested

Ionosit(DMG) 감시 인제스트 — Ionosit를 **명시적으로** 측정한 성분·응력거동 벤치데이터. 본 in-vitro 연구는 Ionosit를 compomer(polyacid-modified composite)로 분류하고, 모든 시험재 중 가장 높은 수분 흡수(~3 wt%)와 56일 후 유의한 radial pressure(hygroscopic expansion stress)를 측정했다. 기존 [[glass-ionomer/ge-2024-gic-clinical-application-restorative-preventive]]의 cGIC/RMGIC/HVGIC/compomer 재료군 임상 개관에 정량적 응력거동 데이터를 보강하며, RMGIC의 ion-release/water-sorption 화학을 다룬 [[glass-ionomer/singh-2024-gic-rmgic-biological-response-sr]]과 짝을 이룬다.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro photoelastic + ISO 4049 study of 10 ion-releasing polymeric restoratives (RMGIC, compomer, giomer) showing material-dependent polymerization contraction stress relaxed (and sometimes reversed to expansion stress) by hygroscopic water sorption; Ionosit (compomer) had the highest water sorption (~3 wt%) and produced significant radial pressure after 56 days.

## 한줄요약

이온유리 폴리머 수복재 10종(RMGIC·컴포머·지오머)을 광탄성 + ISO 4049로 평가한 in-vitro 연구 — 중합수축응력이 수분흡수(hygroscopic expansion)로 완화·역전됨이 재료별로 다르며, Ionosit(컴포머)이 전 시험재 중 최고 수분흡수(~3 wt%)·56일 후 유의한 radial pressure를 보임.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Directly compares polymerization contraction stress vs. hygroscopic expansion stress across three ion-releasing material classes — RMGIC, compomer (polyacid-modified composite), and giomer — using a modified photoelastic method tracked out to 84 days.
- Provides named-material bench data for **Ionosit** (DMG): classified as a **compomer**, highest water sorption of all tested materials, and significant radial (expansion) pressure after 56 days of water storage.
- Demonstrates that the magnitude of contraction-stress relief by water sorption is material-dependent and tied to filler content/composition; the photoelastic method is shown inadequate for RMGIC (Fuji II LC, Ionolux).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Materials (n=10)**: RMGIC — Fuji II LC, Ionolux; compomers/polyacid-modified composites — Dyract eXtra, Compoglass Flow, **Ionosit**, Glasiosite, Twinky Star; giomers — Beautifil Bulk Fill Flow, Beautifil Flow Plus F00, Beautifil Flow F02.
- **Water absorption dynamic**: ISO 4049, n=5 cylinders (15 mm × 1 mm), weighed daily for 30 days plus at 1344 h and 2016 h.
- **Water sorption & solubility**: ISO 4049, n=5 (15 mm × 1 mm), desiccator constant-mass → 7-day 37 °C water immersion → reconditioning.
- **Photoelastic study**: 3 mm diameter × 4 mm deep orifices in photoelastic epoxy (Epidian 53) plates mimicking a tooth cavity; strains read in circular transmission polariscope (FL200), stress computed via Timoshenko equation, n=3 per material.
- **Time points**: 0.5, 24, 72, 96, 168, 240, 336, 504, 672, 1344, 2016 h. Light cure 1250 mW/cm², 2 mm increments, curing per manufacturer.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- All ion-releasing materials shrank during hardening (contraction stress), then showed significant stress decrease due to hygroscopic expansion. Absolute stress changes ranged **6.2 to 21.4 MPa** across materials.
- **Ionosit (compomer)**: "showed the highest values of water sorption among all tested materials." Absorption rose up to ~3 wt% (alongside Beautifil Flow F02). Volume increased after polymerization, compensating polymerization contraction. "Ionosit (compomer) produced a significant radial pressure after 56 days of water storage" — consistent with McCabe's compomer (F2000) high displacement/radial pressure.
- Most materials reached total (100%) contraction-stress reduction on water aging; four materials additionally developed opposite-direction (expansion) stress.
- **Low-sorption materials (no total stress reduction)**: Beautifil Bulk Fill (lowest sorption, 13.6 µg/mm³), Dyract eXtra (slow reduction, only up to 1.6 MPa after 21 days), Glasiosite, Twinky Star.
- RMGIC water sorption >115 µg/mm³. No significant overall difference between giomers and compomers; differences driven by filler content/composition (higher filler → lower sorption). Beautifil Flow F02 (giomer) showed greatest swelling/radial pressure via S-PRG osmotic effect.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro photoelastic model in epoxy plates, not real dentin/enamel; n=3 for photoelastic measurement.
- Photoelastic method inadequate for RMGIC (Fuji II LC, Ionolux) — contraction isochromes persisted through the contraction→expansion transition; authors call for tensiometer, strain gauge, or stress–strain analyser methods for RMGIC.
- No clinical (marginal-gap, restoration-longevity) endpoints; stress-to-clinical-failure link is inferential.

## 6. Related Work

- McCabe et al. — compomer (F2000) high displacement force / radial pressure after 1 month water storage (cited corroboration for Ionosit behavior).
- Prior work on hygroscopic-expansion relief of composite polymerization stress (material-dependent stress reduction).

## 7. Glossary

- **Compomer (polyacid-modified composite)**: resin-based material with dimethacrylate monomers bearing acid functional groups + partially silanized ion-leachable glass; classified internally as a composite. Ionosit is a compomer.
- **RMGIC (resin-modified glass-ionomer cement)**: GIC chemistry combined with methacrylate photopolymerization; acid/base reaction predominates.
- **Giomer**: resin-based material with surface pre-reacted glass-ionomer (S-PRG) fluorosilicate particles for anticariogenic activity.
- **Hygroscopic (water-sorption) expansion stress**: expansion generated by water uptake that relaxes (and can reverse) initial polymerization contraction stress at the restoration–tooth interface.
- **Photoelastic analysis**: strain visualization in birefringent epoxy via polariscope; stress quantified by Timoshenko equation.
