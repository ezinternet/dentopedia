## Why Ingested

Our intracanal-medicament pages — [[endodontics/irrigation/rossi-fedele-2023-irrigation-dressing-apical-periodontitis-sr-ma]], [[endodontics/irrigation/shunmugavelu-2025-intracanal-medications-periapical-abscess-sr]], and [[endodontics/irrigation/ardila-2026-intracanal-medicaments-enterococcus-faecalis-sr]] — frame Calcium Hydroxide (CH) as the default inter-appointment dressing. This RCT (Otaify 2026, n=46) is the first head-to-head of low-level laser therapy (LLLT) vs CH for symptomatic apical periodontitis, adding objective ELISA biomarkers (IL-8, Substance P) on top of VAS pain. It extends those SR pages by testing whether a single-visit LLLT protocol can replace CH for acute symptom/inflammation control — relevant to the single-visit-vs-multi-visit debate.

## One-line Summary

Parallel RCT (n=46, single-canalled symptomatic apical periodontitis) found low-level laser therapy and Calcium Hydroxide intracanal medication gave statistically comparable postoperative pain, percussion pain, and reduction of periapical IL-8 and Substance P (all intergroup p>0.05), supporting LLLT as a single-visit alternative for acute symptom control — but CH's antimicrobial role was not tested.

## 한줄요약

단일근관 증상성 치근단치주염 RCT (n=46)에서 저출력레이저치료 (Low-Level Laser Therapy, LLLT)와 수산화칼슘 (Calcium Hydroxide, CH) 근관내 첩약은 수술후 통증·타진통·치근단 IL-8·물질P 감소가 통계적으로 동등(군간 모두 p>0.05)했고, LLLT가 단일내원 대체 옵션이 될 수 있으나 CH의 항균 역할은 평가하지 않았다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- **First direct comparison** of LLLT vs Calcium Hydroxide intracanal medication for symptomatic apical periodontitis.
- Combines **patient-reported pain** (modified VAS) with **objective periapical inflammatory biomarkers** (IL-8 and Substance P by ELISA) — clinical and biological outcomes in one trial.
- Tests whether LLLT can support a **single-visit endodontic** workflow (empty canal + laser) as an alternative to multi-visit CH dressing.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: parallel, 1:1 RCT; computer-generated randomization, concealed allocation (sealed opaque envelopes); molecular biologist and statistician blinded; participants not blinded.
- **Sample**: n=48 enrolled (24/group); 46 analyzed (23/23) after 2 lost to follow-up. Sample size powered on primary outcome (postoperative pain), 80% power, alpha 0.05, 15% dropout.
- **Inclusion**: age 20–55, healthy, mature single-canalled permanent teeth (anterior + single-rooted posterior), symptomatic apical periodontitis with percussion pain.
- **Endodontic protocol (both groups)**: 4% Articaine buccal infiltration; rubber dam; 5.25% NaOCl field disinfection; M-Pro rotary to WL (apex 0.5 mm short); irrigation 2.5% NaOCl (total 25 ml) + saline flush; final 2.5% NaOCl + 17% EDTA; obturation modified cold lateral compaction with Adseal sealer.
- **LLLT group**: 976 ± 20 nm diode (Woodpecker LX16 plus), 0.5 W, 10 Hz, 200-µm tip, ~10 mm from buccal apex, circular movement, 30 s/tooth (per Arslan 2018), empty canal.
- **CH group**: Metapaste placed 1–2 mm short of WL; removed at 1 week with saline irrigation + Ultra-X ultrasonic activation, confirmed clear by loupes + radiograph (equivalent intracanal conditions before PS-2).
- **Sampling**: periapical fluid PS-1 (post-instrumentation) and PS-2 (1 week) via size 20 paper points 2 mm beyond terminus; ELISA for SP and IL-8.
- **Outcomes**: PRIMARY = pain VAS at 6/12/24/48 h + percussion pain at 1 wk; SECONDARY = SP and IL-8 reduction; EXPLORATORY (post-hoc) = biomarker–pain correlation.
- **Stats**: SPSS v25.0; Shapiro-Wilk; Mann-Whitney U, Wilcoxon, Friedman + Dunn; chi-square; alpha 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Postoperative pain (intergroup)**: NS at every time point — 6 h p=0.773, 12 h p=0.751, 24 h p=0.885, 48 h p=0.644. Numerically lower in LLLT at all intervals. Both groups significant reduction from baseline; 48 h < 6 and 12 h.
- **Percussion pain**: NS intergroup (preop p=0.297; 1 wk p=0.708). Both significant within-group: LLLT 5.9→2.9 (p<0.001), CH 5.1→2.8 (p=0.039).
- **IL-8 reduction (intergroup)**: NS (p=0.123). Intragroup: LLLT 111.0→95.7 ng/L NS (p=0.064); CH 86.7→61.9 ng/L significant (p=0.044).
- **Substance P reduction (intergroup)**: NS (p=0.385); numerically greater in CH (38.7 vs 25.2 ng/L). Intragroup: BOTH significant — LLLT 137.3→112.1 (p=0.04), CH 97.5→58.8 (p=0.013).
- **Correlations**: no significant association between IL-8/SP and pain or percussion pain at any time point.
- **Confounders noted**: posterior teeth more frequent in LLLT (p=0.049); higher preoperative SP in LLLT (p<0.001).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- No participant blinding (performance bias).
- Single-canalled teeth only — limited generalizability to multi-rooted teeth.
- Subjective pain assessment; sample powered for pain, likely underpowered for biomarkers; only 2 biomarker time points (may miss inflammatory peaks).
- **CH antimicrobial role NOT evaluated** — CH remains gold-standard disinfectant; LLLT is a single-visit symptom/inflammation option, not a direct substitute for CH's long-term disinfecting role.
- LLLT used empty canal vs CH-filled canal (different sampling environment) — deliberate to isolate laser bio-stimulation.
- Future: multi-rooted teeth, placebo-laser blinding, larger biomarker-powered cohorts, additional time points, LLLT + CH synergy, asymptomatic lesions.

## 7. Glossary

- **LLLT (Low-Level Laser Therapy)** — non-thermal photobiomodulation (600–1000 nm) modulating mitochondrial ATP/oxidative stress; analgesic and anti-inflammatory.
- **CH (Calcium Hydroxide)** — standard inter-appointment intracanal medicament with antimicrobial action.
- **Symptomatic apical periodontitis** — periapical inflammation with pain/percussion sensitivity.
- **IL-8 (Interleukin-8)** — proinflammatory chemokine recruiting neutrophils, promoting periapical bone resorption.
- **Substance P (SP)** — neuropeptide of neurogenic inflammation; vasodilation, vascular permeability, pain signaling (TRPV1-linked).
- **PS-1 / PS-2** — periapical fluid samples post-instrumentation / at 1 week.
