## One-line Summary

Narrative review (Periodontology 2000, 2017) tracing 50 years of osseointegration-based implant dentistry — from Brånemark/Schroeder foundations to moderately rough surfaces, bone augmentation, digital workflows, and open controversies (peri-implantitis, zirconia implants).

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Historical synthesis**: Documents the parallel and independent origins of modern implant dentistry via Brånemark (Gothenburg) and Schroeder (Bern) — the two founding schools that replaced blade/subperiosteal implants with scientifically validated osseointegrated titanium implants.
2. **Surface evolution**: Narrates the paradigm shift from machined (turned) surfaces to moderately rough (SLA, TiUnite) surfaces and the consequent acceleration of osseointegration and expansion of loading protocols (immediate/early loading).
3. **Bone augmentation integration**: Reviews guided bone regeneration (GBR) and sinus floor elevation (SFE) as enabling technologies that widened implant indications to anatomically deficient sites.
4. **Extraction socket protocols**: Summarizes evidence for immediate (Type 1), early (Types 2–3), and late (Type 4) implant placement timing following tooth extraction.
5. **Digital implant dentistry**: Discusses CBCT for 3D preoperative planning, computer-guided surgery, and prosthetic CAD/CAM workflows as current trends.
6. **Open questions and controversies**:
   - Whether marginal bone loss around implants reflects a foreign-body reaction (Albrektsson hypothesis) vs. peri-implantitis (biofilm-driven disease).
   - Evidence-based appraisal of peri-implantitis prevalence and case definitions.
   - Second-generation zirconia (Y-TZP) ceramic implants as an emerging alternative to titanium.
   - Aging implant population and modified protocols for older patients.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Narrative review commissioned for Periodontology 2000's 50th anniversary volume; no systematic search protocol or PRISMA flow.
- **Scope**: Covers the full arc from 1960s origins through 2016 evidence on implant surgery, surfaces, bone augmentation, placement timing, loading protocols, esthetics, digital dentistry, and controversies.
- **Evidence base**: Cites landmark RCTs, SRs, long-term cohort studies, and preclinical research (>100 references spanning 1981–2016).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Topic | Key Finding |
|---|---|
| Osseointegration definition | Direct structural and functional connection between living bone and the surface of a load-bearing implant (Brånemark 1985) |
| Surface roughness threshold | Sa 1–2 µm (moderately rough) = optimal for bone-implant contact (BIC); machined surfaces (Sa ~0.5 µm) inferior |
| SLA surface | Sand-blasted, Large-grit, Acid-etched (SLA; Straumann) — gold standard moderately rough surface, 10+ years RCT/cohort evidence |
| Loading protocols | Moderately rough surfaces enabled early loading (3–6 wk) and immediate loading without compromising OI in good bone quality |
| GBR evidence | Simultaneous GBR at implant placement for horizontal defects well-documented; staged GBR for large vertical defects; DBBM + resorbable collagen membrane most studied combination |
| Sinus floor elevation | Lateral window approach with DBBM reliable for residual bone height ≥4 mm; transcrestal approach for ≥6 mm |
| Immediate implant placement | Type 1 (immediate): predictable in ideal sockets with ≥1 mm buccal bone, experienced surgeon; gap ≤2 mm → fill with DBBM |
| CBCT | Better 3D anatomy vs. dental CT; lower radiation dose; opened door for digital planning and guided surgery |
| Peri-implantitis prevalence | Wide variation in reported rates due to lack of standardized case definition; estimated patient-level prevalence ~10–20% at ≥5 years |
| Foreign-body reaction debate | Albrektsson et al. proposed marginal bone loss as physiologic foreign-body reaction; countered by biofilm-centric peri-implantitis model |
| Zirconia implants | First-generation (Al₂O₃ composite) failed; second-generation Y-TZP showing early promise; long-term data lacking as of 2017 |

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative design: selection bias; not reproducible via systematic search.
- Focuses primarily on Brånemark and ITI (Bern) systems; other systems discussed selectively.
- Peri-implantitis section acknowledges lack of consensus case definition as a major evidence gap.
- Zirconia implant data limited to short-term series; authors urge RCTs with ≥5-year follow-up.
- Digital workflow evidence largely based on accuracy studies, not long-term implant survival RCTs at time of writing.

## 7. Glossary

- **골유착 (Osseointegration, OI)**: Direct bone-to-implant contact without intervening fibrous tissue
- **SLA 표면 (Sand-blasted Large-grit Acid-etched, SLA)**: Moderately rough titanium implant surface (Sa ≈ 1.7 µm) by Straumann
- **유도골재생 (Guided Bone Regeneration, GBR)**: Barrier membrane technique to compartmentalize and regenerate bone defects
- **상악동저 거상술 (Sinus Floor Elevation, SFE)**: Augmentation of the posterior maxillary sinus to increase bone height for implant placement
- **즉시 식립 (Immediate Implant Placement, Type 1)**: Implant placed on the day of tooth extraction
- **CBCT (Cone-Beam Computed Tomography)**: 3D dental imaging with lower dose than medical CT
- **지르코니아 임플란트 (Zirconia Implant, Y-TZP)**: Ceramic implant alternative to titanium using yttria-stabilized tetragonal zirconia polycrystal
- **주위염 (Peri-implantitis)**: Inflammatory condition with bone loss around a functioning implant
- **이물반응 가설 (Foreign-Body Reaction Hypothesis)**: Albrektsson's proposal that marginal bone loss is a physiologic response to titanium as a foreign body
- **골-임플란트 접촉률 (Bone-to-Implant Contact, BIC)**: Histomorphometric measure of direct bone apposition to implant surface
