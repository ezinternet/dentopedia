## Why Ingested

발치 후 합병증인 건성 발치와(Dry Socket, Alveolar Osteitis)의 예방·치료에 어떤 국소 중재가 근거를 갖는지 정리하기 위해 ingest. 직전 클레임 상담(사랑니 발치 후 통증·음식물 끼임)에서 드라이소켓 가능성을 다뤘고, chairside에서 "예방·처치 무엇이 효과 있나"에 근거 기반 답이 필요. 발치 합병증 관리의 표준 근거로 [[oral-surgery]] 카테고리를 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

Cochrane SR+MA (49 RCTs, n=6,771): chlorhexidine rinse (OR 0.38) and intrasocket gel (OR 0.44) probably reduce dry socket (moderate-certainty); benefit (NNT) scales with baseline risk — NNT≈7 at 30% prevalence (surgical third molars); Alvogyl > zinc oxide eugenol for pain at day 7 (very low-certainty).

## 한줄요약

코크란 SR+MA (RCT 49편, n=6,771): 클로르헥시딘 (Chlorhexidine, CHX) 가글 (OR 0.38)·발치와 내 젤 (OR 0.44)이 건성 발치와 (Dry Socket)를 줄일 가능성(중등도 확실성). 효과는 기저 위험에 비례 — 하악 사랑니 등 고위험(유병률 30%)에서 NNT≈7. 치료로는 Alvogyl(구 제형)이 산화아연유지놀 (Zinc Oxide Eugenol, ZOE)보다 7일째 통증 감소(매우 낮은 확실성).

- **Journal**: Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews, 2022, Issue 9, Art. No.: CD006968 (pub3)
- **DOI**: 10.1002/14651858.CD006968.pub3
- **Publication**: New search + content updated (conclusions changed), Issue 9, 2022; search completed 28 Sept 2021
- **Institution**: Cochrane Oral Health Group; Dublin Dental University Hospital, Trinity College Dublin; University of Manchester
- **Type**: Cochrane intervention systematic review with meta-analysis

## 2. Key Contributions

- Pooled 49 trials: 39 prevention trials (6,219 participants) + 10 treatment trials (552 participants).
- Quantified chlorhexidine (CHX) prevention effect for both rinse and intrasocket gel with GRADE moderate-certainty evidence.
- Translated odds ratios into NNT across a realistic baseline-risk gradient (1% routine extraction → 30% surgical third molar) — clinically the headline message.
- Updated conclusions from prior versions; notes the old Alvogyl formulation studied is no longer marketed (now "Alveogyl").

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Cochrane methodology; RCTs comparing antiseptic rinses/intrasocket gels vs placebo/no treatment for **prevention**, and obtundent dressings vs placebo/other agents for **treatment**.
- Outcomes: incidence of dry socket (prevention), pain (treatment, VAS day 7), adverse effects.
- GRADE certainty assessment; risk of bias largely from study conduct + imprecision.
- Most prevention studies involved mandibular third molar extractions by oral surgeons (all but 5 studies).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Comparison | Effect | n / trials | Certainty |
|---|---|---|---|
| CHX rinse (0.12%/0.2%) vs placebo (prevention) | OR 0.38 (95% CI 0.25–0.58; P<0.00001) | 1,547 / 6 | Moderate |
| CHX gel (0.2%) intrasocket vs placebo (prevention) | OR 0.44 (95% CI 0.27–0.71; P=0.0008) | 753 / 7 | Moderate |
| CHX gel vs CHX rinse | RR 0.74 (0.46–1.20; P=0.22), NS | 383 / 2 | Low |
| Platelet-rich plasma (PRP) vs placebo | RR 0.51 (0.19–1.33; P=0.17), NS | 127 / 2 | Very low |
| Alvogyl (old) vs ZOE, pain day 7 (treatment) | MD −1.40 (−1.75 to −1.04; P<0.00001) | 80 / 2 | Very low |

- **NNT (CHX rinse)**: 162 / 33 / 7 for baseline dry-socket prevalence of 1% / 5% / 30%.
- **NNT (CHX gel 0.2%)**: 180 / 37 / 7 for the same baseline gradient.
- Dry socket prevalence: 1–5% routine extractions, up to >30% surgically extracted third molars.
- Adverse effects: CHX rinse — minor (taste alteration, tooth staining, stomatitis); CHX intrasocket gel — none reported.
- 21 further prevention interventions and 9 further treatment interventions each evaluated in single studies → insufficient evidence.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Evidence dominated by mandibular third molars → limited generalisability to routine extractions.
- Risk of bias in study conduct; imprecision when pooled.
- Alvogyl evidence relates to a discontinued formulation.
- Most single-study interventions lack replication.

## 7. Glossary

- **Alveolar osteitis (dry socket)**: painful post-extraction condition from partial/total loss of the blood clot in the socket; most common after mandibular third molar removal. Not bacterial in origin, but bacterial load increases risk.
- **Chlorhexidine (CHX)**: antiseptic; used as 0.12%/0.2% rinse or 0.2% intrasocket gel.
- **NNT (Number Needed to Treat)**: patients to treat to prevent one event; here scales inversely with baseline risk.
- **Obtundent**: soothing medicated dressing used to reduce pain in an established dry socket.
- **Alvogyl/ZOE**: medicated dressings for treating (not preventing) dry socket; Alvogyl old formulation discontinued.
