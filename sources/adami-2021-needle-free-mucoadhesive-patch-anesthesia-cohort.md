## Why Ingested

Prospective clinical evidence for a needle-free mucoadhesive patch (lidocaine + prilocaine) that can replace infiltrative anesthesia in medium-complexity procedures — directly addresses the needle-phobia population (59% here) and the injection-pain problem that conventional agent-selection pages don't solve. Extends the injection-pain-minimization discussion in [[wiki/drug/zhou-2023-pregnancy-physiology-oral-local-anesthesia-review]] (which flags emerging needle-free formulations as promising-but-understudied) with actual clinical-cohort data.

## One-line Summary

Prospective one-arm open clinical cohort (USP Ribeirão Preto, n=58 adults, 65% women, mean age 50.7y): a tri-layered mucoadhesive patch delivering lidocaine + prilocaine HCl (1:1, 30 mg/patch) gave needle-free anesthesia in medium-complexity dental procedures, completing 90% of cases without any injection; mean onset ~13.2 min, effect 50–90 min, no side effects at 2 h or 6-month follow-up; 59% of subjects were needle-phobic/anxious (82% of them women).

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **Clinical validation of a needle-free buccal patch** as primary pre-operative anesthesia (not just topical pre-injection) for medium-complexity procedures — 90% of cases needed no injection at all.
2. **Tri-layer design** (drug-release + mucoadhesive + backing) gives unidirectional release, resists salivary washout, adheres throughout the procedure, and avoids the throat-isthmus numbness of the earlier monolayer prototype.
3. **Targets the needle-phobia population**: 59% of the cohort self-reported needle-phobia/anxiety; every patient needing supplemental injection was in this group, but even they did not feel the needle puncture (patch acted as effective pre-anesthetic).
4. **Lidocaine + prilocaine synergy**: lidocaine's lipophilicity/vasodilation drives fast onset; prilocaine's lower vasodilation drives the peak and longer duration.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Patch**: three film-cast layers — drug release (9 mm Ø), mucoadhesive (12 mm Ø), backing (12 mm Ø); mean mass 42.5 mg, thickness ~510 µm; PCl + LCl 1:1, 30 mg total/patch.
- **Administration**: two patches per patient (one vestibular, one palatal/lingual) at the involved teeth, kept in place throughout the procedure (86% maxillary sites, 14% mandibular).
- **Population**: n=58 healthy adults (>18 y, good systemic condition, pulp-vital teeth needing medium-complexity treatment). Exclusions: nonvital/endo-treated teeth, pregnant women, smokers/drinkers, CNS-drug users, anesthetic allergy, menstruating women.
- **Procedures**: Class I–V restorations, gingival retraction (staple/clamp facilities), supra/subgingival prep, scaling/root planing, gingivoplasty, occlusal adjustment.
- **Supplementation** (if pain): 2% mepivacaine + norepinephrine 1:100,000 (Mepinor).
- **Outcomes**: onset time (patient-reported anesthetic sensation, palpation-confirmed); need for supplemental injection; supplemental volume; safety (tissue color/ulceration at 2 h and 6 months).
- **Stats**: descriptive frequencies; Mann-Whitney U for gender subgroups; SPSS v20.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Value |
|---|---|
| Patients | 58 (65% women, 35% men) |
| Mean age | 50.7 ± 14.4 y (range 18–78) |
| Needle-phobia/anxiety self-report | 59% overall (74% of women, 30% of men; 82% of phobics were women) |
| Mean onset of anesthesia | 13.2 ± 6.3 min (range 5–40; women 13.5, men 12.6, NS p=0.615) |
| Procedures completed with patch alone (no injection) | 90% |
| Procedures needing supplemental injection | 10% (6 cases, all maxillary, all in anxious/needle-phobic patients; 83% women) |
| Mean supplemental volume | 0.63 ± 0.23 mL (~⅓ cartridge, ~12 mg drug) |
| Duration of useful anesthesia | 50–90 min (procedures completed within these windows) |
| Maximum onset effect | reached within 15–25 min |
| Side effects | None reported during/after, at 2 h, or over 6-month follow-up |
| Mandibular cases | 0% needed supplementation (all completed with patch) |

- Procedure mix: 40% cavity prep/restoration, 29% staple/clamp facilities, 10% gingival retraction, 9% subgingival scaling, remainder gingivoplasty/supragingival/occlusal/subgingival.
- Of the 6 supplemented cases: 50% restorations, 33% supragingival prep, 17% gingivoplasty.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **One-arm, open (non-blinded, no comparator)** prospective cohort — convenience sample, self-controlled; no randomized control vs conventional injection, so efficacy is descriptive not comparative.
- **Subjective onset endpoint** (patient-reported sensation, palpation-confirmed); no EPT.
- **Exact procedure-completion time not recorded** (authors' stated limitation).
- Pulp-vital teeth only, healthy adults, single center; excludes pregnancy, smokers, allergic patients.
- Authors note formulation/design optimization, long-term stability, and manufacture upscaling are still needed before full product validation.

## 6. Related Work

- Couto 2017 (J Control Release) — the team's prior mucoadhesive monolayer film (PCl+LCl 1:1, 22 mg) establishing 5-min onset, 15–25 min peak, ≥50 min duration in simulated needle-puncture trials.
- Amorim 2020 — palatal needle-free lidocaine-prilocaine liposome anesthesia for maxillary molar extraction (~26 min injection-free anesthesia).
- George 2018 (Cochrane) — injectable LA agents for dental anesthesia (comparator context).

## 7. Glossary

- **PCl / LCl**: prilocaine hydrochloride / lidocaine hydrochloride (aminoamide salts, combined 1:1).
- **Mucoadhesive patch**: tri-layer polymeric film (drug-release + mucoadhesive + backing) adhering to oral mucosa for transbuccal drug delivery.
- **Onset time**: interval from patch application to patient-reported anesthetic sensation (palpation-confirmed).
- **Needle-phobia**: anxiety/fear of injections; affects ~20% of the population, more prevalent in women.
- **One-arm open cohort**: single-group, non-blinded prospective study where each patient serves as own reference.
