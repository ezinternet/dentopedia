## One-line Summary

Split RCT (124 primary molars, Class II) over 12 months: a highly viscous glass-ionomer (EQUIA Forte HT) and a giomer injectable composite (Beautifil Flow Plus X) had statistically comparable clinical success (88.3% vs 93.4%, NS) and equal functional/biological FDI outcomes; the giomer was significantly better only in esthetics (surface luster p=0.002, color match p<0.001).

## 2. Key Contributions

- Head-to-head 12-mo RCT of HVGIC (glass hybrid) vs giomer injectable composite in stress-bearing Class II cavities of primary molars.
- FDI-criteria evaluation across esthetic, functional, biological domains.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

124 primary molars with Class II cavities, randomized 1:1: Group I EQUIA Forte HT Fil (glass hybrid GIC), Group II Beautifil Flow Plus X F00 (giomer injectable composite). FDI criteria at 3/6/12 months; FDI score 4 (repair) or 5 (replacement) = failure. Mann-Whitney U (intergroup), Wilcoxon signed-rank (intragroup), p≤0.05. Ain Shams University.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- 12-mo clinical success: **EQUIA Forte HT 88.3% vs giomer 93.4% — no significant difference (p>0.05)**.
- Giomer superior esthetics: surface luster (p=0.002), color match (p<0.001).
- Functional (fracture & retention, marginal adaptation): comparable, no significant difference.
- Biological: comparable; no recurrent caries or postoperative sensitivity in either group.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single-center, 12-month follow-up; primary teeth (limited remaining lifespan).
- Longer follow-up and multi-center confirmation needed.

## 7. Glossary

- **HVGIC / glass hybrid**: highly viscous glass-ionomer (EQUIA Forte HT); bulk-placeable, fluoride-releasing.
- **Giomer**: resin composite containing pre-reacted glass-ionomer (S-PRG) filler, fluoride-releasing, esthetic.
- **FDI criteria**: standardized esthetic/functional/biological restoration evaluation.
- **Class II (primary molar)**: proximal cavity, stress-bearing — a demanding test of restorative durability.
