## One-line Summary

Narrative review (Zahedan Univ, Iran): odontogenic infection/pain affects >43% of pregnant women; endodontic treatment with appropriate antibiotics/analgesics/local anesthesia is safe and should not be delayed — first-line antibiotics amoxicillin/penicillin/cephalexin (erythromycin or clindamycin if penicillin-allergic), acetaminophen first-line analgesic, 2nd trimester safest for elective care.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Consolidates pregnancy-specific guidance for endodontic pharmacotherapy (antibiotics, analgesics, local anesthesia, intracanal drugs).
- Aligns with ADA (2012/reaffirmed 2019) + ACOG position that timely oral care is integral to a healthy pregnancy.
- Provides a trimester-by-trimester treatment framework.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Narrative (non-systematic) review of the literature on antibiotics/drugs in endodontic treatment during pregnancy.
- No formal search protocol, inclusion criteria, or risk-of-bias assessment reported.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Burden**: >43% of pregnant women report oral health problems including odontogenic infection/pain; 25–50% (Canada/Netherlands data) received antibiotics in pregnancy.
- **First-line antibiotics** (mild cellulitis): cephalexin, penicillin, amoxicillin; **penicillin allergy** → erythromycin or clindamycin; **severe cellulitis** → hospital IV clindamycin or cephalosporin.
- **Analgesia**: acetaminophen for toothache; controlled use of oxycodone and ibuprofen (ibuprofen avoided in 3rd trimester per general guidance).
- **Local anesthesia**: considered safe with proper dose; epinephrine 1:100,000 with aspiration, minimum necessary dose; stress of untreated pain more dangerous than extra anesthetic.
- **Radiography**: intraoral X-ray safe with ALARA, lead apron, collimation, high-speed film.
- **Intracanal**: chlorhexidine/metronidazole, calcium hydroxide usable; hypochlorite/fillers not reported harmful to fetus.
- **Timing**: 1st trimester emergency only (consult obstetrician if organogenesis incomplete); **2nd trimester safest** for elective + emergency; 3rd trimester emergency, defer definitive care postpartum if possible.
- **Bottom line**: antibiotics can be used safely during pregnancy; odontogenic infection must be treated promptly at any time.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative review — no systematic search, no risk-of-bias, no GRADE; potential selection bias.
- Drug-class recommendations are general and not always tied to FDA categories or trimester-specific contraindications (e.g., ibuprofen 3rd-trimester ductus arteriosus risk not detailed).
- Single-institution authorship; older references.

## 7. Glossary

- **Odontogenic infection**: infection originating from a tooth (pulp/periapical) or its supporting structures.
- **RCT (Root Canal Therapy)**: endodontic treatment removing infected pulp and sealing the canal.
- **ALARA**: As Low As Reasonably Achievable — radiation-dose minimization principle.
- **Cellulitis**: diffuse soft-tissue bacterial infection; in dentistry, spread of odontogenic infection.
