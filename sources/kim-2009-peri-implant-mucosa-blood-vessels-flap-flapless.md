## Why Ingested

기존 피판 수술(flap surgery)이 임플란트 주위 연조직의 혈관을 손상시킬 수 있다는 임상적 의문에서 출발한 동물실험. [[implants/flapless-implant-surgery-vascularity]] 주제와 연관되며, 무피판 수술의 생물학적 근거를 혈관분포 측면에서 직접 검증한 최초 동물 연구로 가치 있음. 기존 [[implants/berglundh-1994-vascular-topography-peri-implant-dog]] 계열 연구를 확장한다.

## One-line Summary

In a 6-dog canine model, flapless implant placement produced significantly higher peri-implant mucosal vascularity (vessel fraction 1.7% vs 1.2%; vessel number 51.4 vs 38.2) than conventional flap surgery after 3 months of healing.

## 한줄요약

성견 6마리 동물실험에서 무피판 임플란트 수술 후 임플란트 주위 점막의 혈관단면적 비율(1.7% vs 1.2%)과 혈관 수(51.4 vs 38.2)가 피판 수술 대비 유의하게 높았음(p<0.05).

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First animal study to directly compare peri-implant mucosal vascularity between flap and flapless implant surgery.
2. Quantified vessel fraction (%) and vessel number per unit area using morphometric analysis (image analysis, IBAS system).
3. Demonstrated that flapless surgery preserves supraperiosteal vasculature, resulting in significantly richer vascularization.
4. Provided histological evidence that flapless soft tissue healing involves less scar formation and thinner, better-vascularized peri-implant mucosa.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Animal model**: 6 mongrel dogs (18–24 months, 15–20 kg). Bilateral edentulous ridges created by premolar/molar extraction; 3 months healing; alveolar crest flattened; 3 months further healing.

**Implant placement**: 2 Osstem GS II Φ4×10.0 mm implants placed per side; side randomized to:
- **Flap group**: Full-thickness mucoperiosteal flap raised, drilled, implant placed, flap sutured (4-0 suture)
- **Flapless group**: 3 mm diameter soft tissue punch; drilling through punch hole; implant placed; no suture

Healing abutments connected immediately; antibiotic coverage (cefazolin) for 3 days; 3-month healing period.

**Histomorphometry**: Specimens fixed in 10% formalin (10 days), decalcified in EDTA (2 weeks), implants removed, paraffin-embedded, 5 µm buccolingual sections, H-E staining. Measurement zone: height = junctional epithelium base to alveolar crest; width = implant surface to 500 µm laterally. Measured: vessel number, vessel cross-sectional area fraction (%). Image analysis: IBAS (Contron, Germany). Statistics: Wilcoxon signed-rank test, SPSS (p<0.05 threshold).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Outcome | Flapless | Flap | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Vessel fraction (%) | 1.7 ± 0.7 | 1.2 ± 0.3 | <0.05 |
| Vessel number (n) | 51.4 ± 9.2 | 38.2 ± 8.1 | <0.05 |

**Clinical observations at 3 months**:
- Flapless sites: no mucosal inflammation signs
- Flap sites: 5/12 implants showed redness and bleeding on probing
- Flapless mucosa was thinner than flap mucosa overall

**Histological observations**:
- Flap group: vessels ≤30 µm diameter, sparse near implant surface
- Flapless group: some vessels >40 µm diameter; more numerous overall

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Small sample size (n=6 dogs, 12 implant pairs)
- Animal model (dogs); extrapolation to humans requires clinical studies
- No measurement of mucosal thickness objectively (clinical observation only)
- Single implant system (Osstem GS II); results may vary by implant surface
- No functional loading — only 3-month submerged/non-loaded healing assessed
- No microbiological analysis correlating vascularity with bacterial defense

## 6. Related Work

- Berglundh et al. 1994 (J Clin Periodontol): vascular topography of peri-implant vs periodontal tissues in dogs — foundational reference showing reduced vascularity around implants vs natural teeth
- Berglundh et al. 1991 (Clin Oral Implants Res): soft tissue barrier at implants and teeth
- Abrahamsson et al. 1996 (Clin Oral Impl Res): peri-implant hard/soft tissues at different implant systems — confirmed scar-like avascular zone 300–600 µm from implant surface
- Buser et al. 1992 (J Periodontol): soft tissue reactions to non-submerged implants — emphasized weak defense due to low vascularity
- Jeong et al. 2007 (Oral Surg): flapless implant surgery experimental study — higher osseointegration rate and crestal bone height with flapless approach

## 7. Glossary

- **무피판 수술 (Flapless surgery)**: Implant placement without raising a mucoperiosteal flap; uses a soft tissue punch instead
- **피판 수술 (Flap surgery)**: Conventional full-thickness mucoperiosteal flap raised to expose alveolar bone
- **혈관분포 (Vascularity)**: Distribution and density of blood vessels in tissue
- **혈관단면적 비율 (Vessel fraction %)**: Cross-sectional area of vessels as a proportion of total measurement area
- **접합상피 (Junctional epithelium)**: Epithelium forming the base of the sulcus, directly attached to implant surface
- **결합조직 (Connective tissue)**: Supracrestal fibrous tissue lateral to the implant
- **골막 상부 혈관 (Supraperiosteal vessels)**: Vessels above the periosteum supplying peri-implant mucosa; damaged by flap elevation
- **반흔조직 (Scar tissue)**: Relatively avascular fibrous tissue formed after wound healing, especially pronounced after flap surgery
