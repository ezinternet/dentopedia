## Why Ingested

기존 [[periodontics/qiao-2020-rheumatoid-arthritis-risk-periodontitis-sr-ma]]는 치주염→RA *위험* 방향(OR 1.69)을 다루며 "RA 환자에서 치주 치료가 RA 질병활성도를 낮춘다"는 점을 별도로 언급만 했다. 본 Dolcezza 2024 SR+MA(RCT 6편)는 바로 그 **치료(중재) 방향**을 정량화 — 비외과적 치주치료가 DAS28과 CAL을 유의하게 개선함을 보여 qiao-2020을 **보강(reinforces)**한다 (역학적 연관 → 중재적 근거로 확장).

## One-line Summary

Systematic review + meta-analysis (6 RCTs; periodontal-treatment n=124 vs control n=131) showing non-surgical periodontal therapy in patients with both periodontal disease and rheumatoid arthritis significantly reduces RA activity (DAS28 mean difference −0.56, 95% CI −0.82 to −0.31, p=0.001; I²=39%) and improves clinical attachment level (CAL −0.41 mm, 95% CI −0.70 to −0.12, p=0.006; I²=7%), trim-and-fill adjusted to −0.39 DAS28 points and −0.30 mm.

## 한줄요약

치주질환과 류마티스 관절염(RA)을 함께 가진 환자에서 비외과적 치주치료(scaling/root planing)가 RA 활성도(DAS28)를 유의하게 낮추고(평균차 −0.56, 95% CI −0.82~−0.31, p=0.001; I²=39%) 임상부착수준(CAL)을 개선한다(−0.41 mm, 95% CI −0.70~−0.12, p=0.006; I²=7%)는 RCT 6편(치료군 124 vs 대조군 131) SR+MA, trim-and-fill 보정 후 −0.39 DAS28점·−0.30 mm.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies the **interventional** direction of the periodontitis↔RA link: does treating periodontal disease lower RA disease activity? Pools 6 RCTs of non-surgical periodontal therapy (scaling/root planing + oral hygiene) in patients carrying both diseases.
- Dual outcome pooling: DAS28 (RA activity) and CAL in mm (periodontal status), both via random-effects inverse-variance.
- Reports publication-bias-adjusted (trim-and-fill) estimates that retain significance, strengthening robustness.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases**: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science (Cochrane also searched), up to March 2024.
- **PICO**: Population — patients with periodontal disease + RA; Intervention — non-surgical periodontal treatment; Comparison — treated vs untreated; Outcome — CAL and DAS28.
- **Inclusion**: RCTs only, any age/language/year. **Exclusion**: reviews/case reports/series/editorials; other oral or autoimmune disease confounding.
- **Screening**: 29 records → 3 duplicates removed → 20 excluded on title/abstract → **6 RCTs** in quantitative synthesis. Sample sizes <100 per study; no dropouts.
- **Risk of bias**: Jadad scale (0–5; <3 = low quality). Most studies described losses; only Silvestre-Rangil et al. and Thilagar et al. were double-blind; most randomized.
- **Analysis**: random-effects inverse-variance; mean difference (treatment − control); z test; heterogeneity by Q and I²; α=0.05; trim-and-fill funnel-plot adjustment for publication bias. Follow-up window ~2 months.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**CAL (mm), treatment n=124 vs control n=131**
- Mean difference **−0.41 mm**, 95% CI −0.70 to −0.12 (z=−2.73; p=0.006), favoring treatment. **No heterogeneity** (I²=7%; Q=5.38, p=0.372).
- Trim-and-fill (3 studies added): **−0.30 mm**, 95% CI −0.55 to −0.046 (z=−2.31; p=0.021) — remains significant.

**DAS28**
- Mean difference **−0.56**, 95% CI −0.82 to −0.31 (z=−4.33; p=0.001), favoring treatment. Heterogeneity slight (I²=39%; Q=8.19, p=0.146).
- Trim-and-fill (3 studies added): **−0.39 DAS28 points**, 95% CI −0.46 to −0.31 (z=−10.3; p<0.001) — remains significant.

**Clinical interpretation**: DAS28 categorizes RA activity as low (≤3.2), moderate (3.2–5.1), high (>5.1); enrolled patients started at moderate activity, and treatment groups shifted moderate→low while controls worsened. CAL threshold 4 mm marks active periodontitis; controls progressed while treated patients improved. r=0.97 between DAS28 and original DAS.

**Note on label discrepancy**: the paper's abstract/discussion swap the labels — it states "−0.56 mm for CAL" while the results section assigns −0.56 to DAS28 and −0.41 mm to CAL. The internally consistent reading (Results §3.4): pre-adjustment CAL = −0.41 mm (I²=7%), DAS28 = −0.56 (I²=39%); trim-and-fill adjusted CAL = −0.30 mm, DAS28 = −0.39 points.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only **6 small RCTs** (each <100 patients), short ~2-month follow-up — durability of RA benefit unknown.
- Heterogeneous blinding (most not double-blind); Jadad-based quality mixed.
- Authors paradoxically state the null hypothesis (no association) is "supported," yet the significant DAS28/CAL treatment effects argue for a pathogenic association — the conclusion text endorses the association and a multidisciplinary approach. Treat the "H0 supported" sentence as an internal inconsistency.
- Effect on RA is an *adjunctive* signal; cannot replace antirheumatic therapy. Larger, longer multidisciplinary RCTs needed.

## 6. Related Work

- Complements [[periodontics/qiao-2020-rheumatoid-arthritis-risk-periodontitis-sr-ma]] — Qiao 2020 establishes the epidemiologic risk direction (periodontitis → 69% higher RA risk, OR 1.69); Dolcezza 2024 adds the interventional direction (treating periodontitis lowers RA activity).

## 7. Glossary

- **CAL (Clinical Attachment Level)**: distance from cementoenamel junction to base of periodontal pocket; primary periodontal-destruction metric; ≥4 mm flags active disease.
- **DAS28 (Disease Activity Score, 28 joints)**: composite RA activity index (0–9.4); ≤3.2 low, 3.2–5.1 moderate, >5.1 high.
- **Non-surgical periodontal treatment**: scaling and root planing (SRP) + oral hygiene reinforcement, without flap surgery.
- **Trim-and-fill**: funnel-plot method imputing "missing" studies to test/adjust for publication bias.
- **Jadad scale**: 0–5 RCT quality score (randomization, blinding, withdrawals); <3 = low quality.
