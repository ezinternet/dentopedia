## Why Ingested

OMS (구강원성 상악동염, Odontogenic Maxillary Sinusitis) 클러스터 보강 목적 — 기존 [[oral-medicine/psillas-2020-odontogenic-maxillary-sinusitis-comprehensive-review]] 및 [[oral-medicine/lin-2024-odontogenic-maxillary-sinusitis-expert-consensus]]가 리뷰/consensus 레벨인 반면, 본 De Corso 2022 논문은 262례 대규모 후향적 코호트로 내시경적 접근의 실제 치료 성과(96.5% 성공률, SNOT-22 43.4→6)와 OAF 동시 처치 프로토콜을 제공하여 임상 의사결정 근거를 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective cohort (n=262) from an Italian academic ENT center demonstrates 96.5% success rate for endoscopic endonasal surgery (FESS) in managing sinonasal complications of dental disease or treatment (SCDDT), with SNOT-22 improvement from 43.4 to 6 at 15 days; 55.34% were iatrogenic (implant/extraction), 44.65% from dental pathology.

## 한줄요약

이탈리아 3차 병원 ENT과의 후향적 코호트(262례)에서 치성 시노비강 합병증(SCDDT)에 대한 내시경 비내 수술의 전체 성공률 96.5%, SNOT-22가 43.4에서 6으로 감소(술 후 15일); 원인의 55.34%는 의인성(임플란트·발치), 44.65%는 치성 병변.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Large real-world retrospective series (n=262) of SCDDT managed exclusively with endoscopic endonasal approach, supporting FESS as the primary treatment modality
2. Validates the Felisati et al. (2013) SCDDT classification (Groups I–III) with full patient distribution across subclasses
3. Demonstrates simultaneous ENT + dental treatment is feasible and safe in 58% of patients (152/262)
4. Reports rapid QoL improvement: SNOT-22 drops from mean 43.4 to 6 within 15 days post-surgery
5. Describes implant-sparing policy for peri-implantitis complications (Group IIa) — removal only if frank dislocation
6. Addresses frontal sinusotomy question: authors advocate opening all diseased sinuses including frontal when pre-existing conditions preclude spontaneous resolution

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Monocentric retrospective cohort
- **Setting**: Tertiary academic ENT department
- **Diagnosis criteria**: CT, nasal endoscopy, and dental evaluation in all patients
- **Classification**: Felisati et al. 2013 SCDDT taxonomy (Group I = pre-implantologic; Group II = implantologic; Group III = classic dental disease/treatment)
- **QoL instrument**: SNOT-22 questionnaire (0–110; higher = worse) administered pre-op and at 15 days post-op
- **Surgical approach**: Endoscopic endonasal mini-invasive conservative approach (general anesthesia, oro-tracheal intubation); all patients received pre/post-operative antibiotics (5 days pre, 10 days post)
- **Outcome**: Treatment success = symptom resolution + endoscopically observed sinus healing
- **Dental timing**: Simultaneous (58%) vs. postponed dental treatment based on patient characteristics

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Demographics**:
- 262 patients (female 59.55%, male 40.45%); mean age 50.84 years (range 21–78)
- Mean baseline SNOT-22: 43.4; mean post-op SNOT-22 (15 days): 6 (p < 0.05)

**Symptoms at presentation**:
- Anterior discharge 86.2%, posterior discharge 74.8%, nasal obstruction 61.4%
- Cacosmia 43.5%, facial pressure 50.8%, hyposmia 21.7%
- Purulent exudate on endoscopy: 85.5%

**Disease extension (CT)**:
- Maxillary only: 38.5%
- Maxillary + ethmoid: 50.4%
- Maxillary + ethmoid + frontal: 9.9%
- Maxillary + ethmoid + sphenoid: 1.1%

**Etiology (Felisati classification)**:
- Group III (classic dental): 67.9% — 3a with OAF: 21.7%, 3b without OAF: 46.2%
- Group II (implant-related): 23.7% — 2a (peri-implantitis ± OAF): 13.8%, 2c (implant dislocation without OAF): 5.7%, 2b (dislocation + OAF): 2.3%, 2d (dislocation only): 1.9%
- Group I (pre-implantologic surgery): 8.4%

**Iatrogenic vs. pathologic**:
- 55.34% iatrogenic (dental implant complications 42.7%, extraction-related 34.5%, foreign bodies 13.1%)
- 44.65% dental pathology (endodontic complications 35%, dentigerous cyst/tooth intrusion 29.9%, radicular cyst 24.8%, dental caries 10.2%)

**Most commonly involved teeth**: 2nd molar > 1st molar > 2nd premolar+1st molar

**Treatment outcomes**:
- Overall success rate: 96.5%
- OAF repair success: 95.8%
- Poor outcomes: 4 patients required prolonged antibiotics; 3 required re-surgery for antrostomy closure/OAF revision; 2 required revision for frontal sinus re-stenosis

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Monocentric retrospective design — selection and referral bias (tertiary center)
- Short-term QoL follow-up (only 15 days post-op); long-term outcomes not reported
- No comparison group (no randomization between FESS-first vs. dental-first)
- Frontal sinusotomy indication not standardized — authors acknowledge this needs further study
- No standardized microbiological data collected
- Pre-existing sino-nasal conditions not fully characterized

## 6. Related Work

- Felisati et al. (2013) SCDDT classification: foundational taxonomy used throughout this paper
- Craig et al. (prospective cohort): primary FESS led to faster resolution (7–12 days) vs. primary dental treatment (35–56 days) — cited as supporting evidence
- Fredriksson et al.: 40% of unilateral maxillary sinusitis had odontogenic origin
- Ly et al.: SCDDT in 48% of unilateral maxillary sinusitis; 1/3 had no dental evaluation
- Ungar et al.: maxillary antrostomy alone sufficient for OMS without frontal sinusotomy — compared with authors' broader approach

## 7. Glossary

- **SCDDT**: Sinonasal Complications of Dental Disease or Treatment — integrative term by Felisati 2013 that encompasses implant and sinus augmentation etiologies
- **OMS**: Odontogenic Maxillary Sinusitis — sinusitis of dental origin, ≈10–14% of all maxillary sinusitis
- **OAF**: Oroantral Fistula (구강-상악동 누공) — communication between oral cavity and maxillary sinus
- **FESS**: Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery (기능적 내시경 부비동 수술) — endoscopic endonasal approach used exclusively in this series
- **SNOT-22**: Sinonasal Outcome Test-22 — validated 22-item QoL questionnaire for rhinosinusitis (score 0–110)
- **Schneiderian membrane**: The mucoperiosteal lining of the paranasal sinuses; its integrity is central to SCDDT pathogenesis
- **Group I/II/III**: Felisati SCDDT classification — I=pre-implantologic, II=implantologic, III=classic dental disease/treatment
