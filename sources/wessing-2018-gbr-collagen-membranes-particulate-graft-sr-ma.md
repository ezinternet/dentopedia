## Why Ingested

Part of a recent GBR / ridge-augmentation collection. This SR+MA quantifies how cross-linking, implant-placement timing, membrane fixation, and decortication affect collagen-membrane GBR outcomes — providing pooled evidence that complements single grafting-material studies like [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/majzoub-2019-influence-different-grafting-materials]] and the membrane-design evidence in [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/choi-2017-single-double-layer-collagen-membrane-arp]].

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 20 RCTs/prospective controlled studies (1980–2014): collagen-membrane + particulate-graft lateral ridge GBR is effective; cross-linked membranes exposed ~30% more often than non-cross-linked, simultaneous and staged implant placement had similar survival (so simultaneous is recommended when possible), and membrane fixation + decortication offer additional vertical/horizontal bone-gain benefit.

## 한줄요약

20편 RCT·전향적 대조연구(1980–2014) SR+MA: 콜라겐막 + 입자형 이식재를 이용한 측방 치조제 GBR은 효과적이며, 교차결합막은 비교차결합막보다 노출률이 약 30% 높았고, 동시 식립과 지연 식립의 생존율이 유사해 가능하면 동시 식립이 권장되며, 막 고정과 피질골 천공(decortication)은 추가적 수직·수평 골증대 이점을 준다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Pools controlled evidence (RCTs and prospective controlled studies only) on GBR using **collagen membranes + particulate grafts** for lateral alveolar ridge augmentation in implant dentistry.
- Quantifies four moderators: membrane cross-linking, timing of implant placement (simultaneous vs subsequent/staged), membrane fixation, and decortication.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Electronic database + hand search for articles 1980–2014.
- Inclusion limited to randomized clinical trials and prospective controlled studies.
- Primary outcomes: survival rates, membrane exposure rates, bone gain / defect reduction, vertical bone loss at follow-up.
- Meta-analysis of cross-linking presence, implant timing, membrane fixation, decortication.
- 20 studies met inclusion criteria.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Implant survival was **similar between simultaneous and subsequent (staged) implant placement**.
- Membrane exposure rate of **cross-linked membranes was ~30% higher** than non-cross-linked membranes.
- Anorganic bovine bone mineral (ABBM) produced sufficient newly regenerated bone and high implant survival.
- **Membrane fixation** was weakly associated with increased vertical bone gain.
- **Decortication** led to higher horizontal bone gain (defect depth reduction).
- Conclusion: GBR with particulate grafts + resorbable collagen membranes is effective for lateral ridge augmentation; simultaneous implant placement is recommended when feasible; membrane fixation and decortication may be beneficial adjuncts.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Search window ends 2014; newer membranes/grafts not captured.
- Only the abstract was available at ingest (paywalled full text) — heterogeneity, pooled effect sizes, and confidence intervals not extractable here.
- "Weak" association for membrane fixation flagged by the authors.

## 7. Glossary

- **GBR** — Guided bone regeneration.
- **ABBM** — Anorganic bovine bone mineral (deproteinized bovine bone mineral).
- **Cross-linking** — Chemical treatment to slow collagen membrane resorption (associated with higher exposure here).
- **Decortication** — Perforating the cortical plate to open marrow access for the graft.
- **Membrane fixation** — Securing the membrane (e.g., pins/tacks) to stabilize the graft.
