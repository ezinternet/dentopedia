## Why Ingested

Extends the `implants/vitamin-d` subdomain from the early-failure question into marginal **bone preservation** around the implant platform, and adds a second independent variable — soft-tissue (gingival) thickness — that affected bone preservation *regardless of* vitamin D status. This bridges the vitamin D evidence to the peri-implant soft-tissue literature. Reinforces [[implants/vitamin-d/miron-2025-vitamin-d-deficiency-early-implant-failure]] (which also touched peri-implant bone preservation) and [[implants/vitamin-d/buzatu-2024-vitamin-d-osseointegration-human-studies-sr]]; anchored to the broader failure-risk literature in [[implants/moy-2005-dental-implant-failure-rates-risk]].

## One-line Summary

Retrospective observational study (72 patients / 115 implants); vitamin D deficiency was associated with significantly greater peri-implant bone loss, and gingival (soft-tissue) thickness independently affected alveolar bone preservation around the implant platform regardless of vitamin D3 status.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Retrospective observational study (chart review)
- **Sample**: 72 patients, 115 dental implants
- **Setting**: Single private medical facility, January 2022 – February 2023
- **Exposures assessed**: Preoperative serum vitamin D3 level; soft-tissue (gingival) thickness over the implant site
- **Outcome**: Preservation of alveolar bone near the implant platform (peri-implant bone loss)
- **Note**: abstract-only — full text not retrieved from PMC. Quantitative effect sizes, deficiency thresholds, and measurement methods are not available in the abstract.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Demonstrates an association between preoperative vitamin D deficiency and increased peri-implant marginal bone loss in a clinical (human, retrospective) sample.
- Identifies soft-tissue (gingival) thickness as an **independent** factor for bone preservation — its effect held regardless of vitamin D3 status.
- Proposes combined preoperative monitoring of serum 25-hydroxycholecalciferol (25(OH)D) plus radiological assessment of gingival tissue thickness as a simple, reliable predictive workflow in implantology.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Records of 72 patients (115 implants) were analyzed retrospectively.
- Each implant had **simultaneous preoperative determination** of (a) serum vitamin D3 level and (b) soft-tissue thickness over the implant site.
- Patients were grouped by vitamin D status (deficiency vs normal) and gingival thickness was evaluated as a separate factor.
- Outcome was alveolar bone preservation near the implant platform.
- *abstract-only* — statistical methods, deficiency cut-offs, follow-up duration, and bone-loss measurement technique are not stated in the abstract.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Patients with **vitamin D deficiency showed significantly higher peri-implant bone loss** than those with normal vitamin D levels.
- **Gingival tissue thickness over the implant site had a significant effect on alveolar bone preservation, regardless of vitamin D3 status** (independent factor).
- Authors conclude that monitoring 25(OH)D concentration alongside radiological assessment of gingival thickness may be a simple, reliable method for predictable implant outcomes.
- (Numerical effect sizes not available — abstract-only.)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Retrospective, single-center design → confounding and selection bias likely; no randomization.
- abstract-only: cannot appraise sample characteristics, deficiency thresholds, follow-up time, or magnitude of effects.
- Association, not causation — supplementation benefit cannot be inferred from this design.
- Future work: prospective/interventional confirmation and standardized definitions of "deficiency" and bone-loss measurement.

## 7. Glossary

- **25(OH)D / 25-hydroxycholecalciferol**: Major circulating form of vitamin D; the standard serum marker of vitamin D status.
- **Vitamin D deficiency**: Low serum vitamin D; here associated with greater peri-implant bone loss.
- **Soft-tissue / gingival thickness**: Thickness of mucosa over the implant site; an independent factor for bone preservation in this study.
- **Marginal / peri-implant bone loss**: Resorption of alveolar bone around the implant platform; the primary outcome.
- **Implant platform**: Coronal interface of the implant where marginal bone level is measured.
