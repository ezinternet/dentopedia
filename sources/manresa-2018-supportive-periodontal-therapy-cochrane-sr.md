## One-line Summary
Cochrane systematic review on supportive periodontal therapy (SPT) showing limited RCT evidence comparing different SPT protocols, with comparisons including specialist vs non-specialist delivery, antimicrobial adjuncts, and photonic adjuncts.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews, 2018; Issue 1, Art. No.: CD009376
- **DOI**: 10.1002/14651858.CD009376.pub2
- **Type**: Cochrane systematic review (pub2 update)
- **Organization**: The Cochrane Collaboration

## 2. Key Contributions
- Provides the highest-level evidence synthesis on SPT interventions for periodontitis-treated adults
- Compares three types of SPT comparisons: specialist vs non-specialist delivery, antimicrobial + mechanical vs mechanical alone, and photonic + mechanical vs mechanical alone
- Highlights the limited and low-quality RCT evidence base for SPT protocols
- Establishes reference framework for clinical outcomes: bleeding on probing (BOP), probing depths (PD), and clinical attachment level (CAL)

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: Cochrane systematic review (no meta-analysis due to heterogeneity)
- **Comparisons evaluated**:
  1. SPT by specialists vs non-specialist clinicians
  2. Antimicrobial + mechanical debridement vs mechanical debridement alone
  3. Photonics (laser/photodynamic therapy) + mechanical debridement vs mechanical debridement alone
- **Outcomes measured**: BOP (%), full-mouth mean probing depths (mm), clinical attachment level (mm), tooth loss
- **Analysis structure**: Multiple sub-comparisons (Analysis 1.1, 1.2, 2.1, 2.2, 2.3, 3.1, 3.2)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Comparison 1 (Specialist vs non-specialist)**:
  - BOP and PD outcomes compared (Analysis 1.1, 1.2)
  - Evidence was very limited and uncertain
- **Comparison 2 (Antimicrobial + mechanical vs mechanical alone)**:
  - BOP (Analysis 2.1), CAL change (Analysis 2.2), PD final scores (Analysis 2.3)
  - Antimicrobial adjuncts showed some additional benefit but evidence quality was low
- **Comparison 3 (Photonics + mechanical vs mechanical alone)**:
  - CAL and PD outcomes (Analysis 3.1, 3.2)
  - Insufficient evidence to make definitive conclusions
- Overall: very limited high-quality RCT evidence for SPT; most included studies had unclear or high risk of bias

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Very few RCTs available for SPT comparisons
- High risk of bias in most included studies
- Heterogeneity in SPT protocols, populations, and follow-up periods
- Lack of patient-reported outcome measures
- Long-term tooth loss data inadequate
- Need for well-designed RCTs comparing SPT intervals, providers, and adjunctive therapies

## 6. Related Work
- Nibali et al. 2016 — tooth loss in furcation-involved molars during SPT
- Stavropoulos et al. 2021 — medium/long-term outcomes of periodontal regeneration

## 7. Glossary
- **SPT (Supportive Periodontal Therapy)**: Maintenance phase of periodontal care following active treatment; includes reassessment, professional debridement, and reinforcement of oral hygiene
- **BOP (Bleeding on Probing)**: Indicator of gingival inflammation; percentage of sites bleeding upon gentle probing
- **PD (Probing Depth)**: Distance from gingival margin to base of sulcus/pocket
- **CAL (Clinical Attachment Level)**: Distance from CEJ to base of the pocket; reflects cumulative attachment loss
- **Mechanical debridement**: Scaling and root planing, professional tooth cleaning
- **Photonics**: Laser or photodynamic therapy used as adjunct to mechanical treatment
