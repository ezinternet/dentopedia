## One-line Summary
PRISMA-compliant SR+MA (15 studies qualitative, 11 quantitative, 1250 → 15 screening): BTX-A reduces gummy smile by pooled -3.22 mm at 2 weeks (95% CI -4.43 to -2.01) and -2.70 mm at 3 months (95% CI -4.52 to -0.88); pre-treatment exposure 3.5-7.2 mm; LLSAN/LLS/zygomaticus minor injected at 1.25-7.5 U/side; effect duration mostly 4-6 months with no return to baseline at 12 weeks.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First SR+MA with meta-regression on BTX-A units vs gummy smile reduction
- Pooled estimate at 2 weeks: -3.22 mm (95% CI -4.43 to -2.01)
- Pooled estimate at 3 months: -2.70 mm (95% CI -4.52 to -0.88)
- Subgroup analysis: no significant difference between 2-week and 3-month reduction (Q=0.221, p=0.638)
- BTX-A dose does NOT significantly affect reduction (meta-regression p=0.781 at 2 wk, p=0.357 at 3 mo)
- Provides a treatment algorithm based on anterior gingival exposure (≤4 mm vs >4 mm)
- ROBINS-I risk-of-bias assessment: 12/13 studies moderate risk, only 1 low risk

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Databases: PubMed, Scopus, Embase, Web of Science, Cochrane, OpenGrey (grey lit) + hand-searched references
- Updated June 2021
- Keywords: 5 MeSH (gingiva, smile, smiling, hypermobility, botulinum toxin) + 13 uncontrolled descriptors
- Inclusion: sample ≥10 adult patients, gingival exposure >2 mm, treated with BTX-A; RCTs, cohorts, case-controls, prospective/retrospective
- Exclusion: pure altered passive eruption, gingival thickening, overeruption; case reports, narratives, n<10
- 1250 references → 742 after duplicates → 49 full-text → 15 included (14 prospective + 1 retrospective); 11 in meta-analysis
- Kappa inter-reviewer agreement: 0.74 (95% CI 0.56-0.93)
- Bias: ROBINS-I tool
- Pooling: random effects, inverse variance method
- Heterogeneity: Q test, I² (>95% — very high)
- Publication bias: funnel plot, Duval-Tweedie trim-and-fill, classic fail-safe N (1156 at 2 wk; 530 at 3 mo)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Pre-treatment gingival exposure
3.5 - 7.2 mm across studies.

### Reduction at 12 weeks
Up to 6 mm (Al Wayli).

### Quantitative pooled estimates
| Endpoint | n studies | n patients | Mean diff (mm) | 95% CI | I² |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2 weeks | 9 | 213 | -3.22 | -4.43, -2.01 | 95.3% |
| 3 months | 5 | 182 | -2.70 | -4.52, -0.88 | 97.9% |

### Meta-regression (dose effect)
- 2 weeks: p=0.781 (no significant dose-response)
- 3 months: p=0.357 (no significant dose-response)

### Subgroup analysis (time)
- 2 weeks vs 3 months: Q=0.221, p=0.638 (no significant difference)
- → Effect substantially preserved through 3 months

### Per-study reduction at 2 weeks
- Range -1.22 mm (Hexsel) to -5.11 mm (one study)
- Significant in 7/9 (NS in Hexsel et al. and Sucupira-Abramovitz)

### Per-study reduction at 3 months
- Range -1.00 mm (Somaiah) to -5.95 mm (Al Wayli)
- All but Somaiah significant

### Doses and muscle targets
- 1.25-7.5 U per side
- Yonsei point (LLSAN/LLS/zygomaticus minor confluence) most common
- Other studies inject only LLS

### Bias
- 12/13 studies moderate risk (ROBINS-I)
- 1 study low risk
- No high-risk studies

### Publication bias
Trim-and-fill: no imputed studies; fail-safe N = 1156 (2 wk), 530 (3 mo).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- High heterogeneity (I² >95%) — pooled estimates have wide CI
- Most included studies prospective single-arm, not RCT
- Only 1 retrospective vs 14 prospective; no RCTs
- 92% of studies moderate ROBINS-I risk (selection, classification, outcome measurement issues)
- Follow-up rarely beyond 6-9 months — long-term durability not addressed
- Direct comparison with hyaluronic acid fillers limited (only 2 studies)
- Authors propose a treatment algorithm based on anterior exposure but it is not externally validated

## 6. Related Work
- Polo (2005, 2008): foundational BTX-A studies
- Mazzuco & Hexsel (2010): subtype protocol
- Hwang (2009): Yonsei point anatomy
- Al Wayli: 9-month follow-up
- Rajogopal: 7-14 month follow-up (after re-injection)
- Diaspro et al.: hyaluronic acid in piriform fossa (-1.37 mm at 2 wk)
- Two prior SRs (one with MA) cited — this paper adds short-term focus and meta-regression

## 7. Glossary
- ROBINS-I: Risk Of Bias In Non-randomized Studies of Interventions (Cochrane tool)
- I²: percentage of variability across studies due to heterogeneity rather than chance
- Meta-regression: regression analysis to assess influence of study-level covariates (e.g., dose)
- Trim-and-fill: method to detect/correct for publication bias
- Fail-safe N: number of NS studies needed to nullify a significant pooled estimate
- LLSAN/LLS: levator labii superioris alaeque nasi / levator labii superioris
- BTX-A: botulinum toxin type A
