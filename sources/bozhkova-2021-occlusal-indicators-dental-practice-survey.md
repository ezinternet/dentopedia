## Why Ingested

심스탁(shim stock foil)을 포함한 교합 지시재(occlusal indicator) 전반의 임상 사용 실태를 조사한 설문연구. 기존 [[occlusion/rovira-lastra-2026-articulating-paper-accuracy-occlusal-points]]가 교합지의 정확도를 정량화했다면, 본 논문은 "현장에서 무엇을 실제로 쓰는가"를 보여준다 — 교합지가 압도적 1위(52%)이고 shim stock은 2.45%로 거의 안 쓰임. shim stock이 접촉 유무만 판정(표면 시각화 불가)한다는 도구 특성과, 정량(T-Scan/OccluSense) 디지털 전환 의향(59%)을 함께 정리해 교합 지시재 선택 맥락을 제공.

## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional survey of 228 Bulgarian dentists: articulating paper is by far the most-used occlusal indicator (52.4%), followed by articulation foil (26.2%) and silk (16.8%); shim stock foil (2.45%), T-Scan (1.4%), and OccluSense (0.7%) are rarely used, while 58.7% expressed willingness to adopt quantitative/digital occlusion methods.

## 한줄요약

불가리아 치과의사 228명 단면 설문: 교합지가 압도적 1위 교합 지시재(52.4%)이고 교합박(foil 26.2%)·실크(16.8%)가 뒤를 이으며, 심스탁(2.45%)·T-Scan(1.4%)·OccluSense(0.7%)는 거의 안 쓰이고, 58.7%가 정량·디지털 교합 분석 도입 의향을 보였다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Quantifies real-world usage frequency of occlusal indicators among practicing dentists, with articulating paper dominant (52.4%).
- Documents that shim stock foil is rarely used (2.45%) — and explains why: it only detects WHETHER an antagonist contact exists, without visualizing the contact on the tooth surface.
- Captures appetite for digital/quantitative occlusion (T-Scan, OccluSense): 58.7% willing to adopt, despite current near-zero usage.
- Links possession of a specialty to attitudes toward quantitative methods (χ²=14.73, df=4, p=0.005).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Anonymous electronic questionnaire to 2014 randomly selected dentists nationwide; 228 returned (response rate 14.87%).
- Self-developed instrument: sociodemographic/professional status + opinions on positives/negatives/effectiveness of occlusal indicators.
- Analysis: SPSS Statistics 25.0; descriptive statistics and nonparametric tests; significance p<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Respondents: 228 (131 F / 97 M), mean age 50.35±9.85; 31.1% with a specialty.
- Indicator usage: articulating paper 52.45±2.95%; articulation foil 26.22±2.6%; silk 16.78±2.21%; shim stock foil 2.45±0.91%; T-Scan 1.40±0.69%; OccluSense 0.70±0.49%.
- Willingness to use quantitative indicators: 58.74±2.11%.
- Specialty vs opinion on quantitative methods: χ²=14.73, df=4, p=0.005 (significant).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Low response rate (14.87%) → non-response/selection bias; single-country sample (Bulgaria).
- Self-reported usage and attitudes, not validated against observed clinical behavior.
- Survey instrument self-developed; usage categories qualitative, no accuracy data generated.
- OccluSense newly marketed (2019) at survey time — low usage may reflect availability, not preference.

## 6. Related Work

- Echoes literature that articulating-paper mark interpretation is subjective and cannot determine occlusal force/timing (qualitative vs quantitative indicator dichotomy).
- Cites silk as high color-capacity/accurate, articulation foil (thinnest) as more accurate than paper or silk (Sharma et al.), and T-Scan as the preferred quantitative tool (Afrashtehfar & Qadeer 2016).
- Notes T-Scan must be paired with articulating paper for visualization, whereas OccluSense stains contacts directly on the tooth.

## 7. Glossary

- Occlusal indicator: material/system used to register the location (and sometimes force/timing) of occlusal contacts.
- Qualitative indicator: marks location/number only (paper, foil, silk, shim stock).
- Quantitative indicator: measures force sequence/magnitude/timing (T-Scan, OccluSense).
- Shim stock foil: thin (≈8–12 µm) metal/polymer foil pulled between antagonist teeth to test only whether a contact holds the foil (presence/absence), without marking the tooth.
- OccluSense: digital pressure-sensor foil (introduced 2019) that both quantifies force and stains contacts on the tooth surface.
