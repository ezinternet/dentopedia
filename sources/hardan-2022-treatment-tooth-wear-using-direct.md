## Why Ingested

기존 [[prosthetic-materials/hjerppe-2025-minimally-invasive-glassceramic-fullmouth-12year]]가 단일 보철재료(glass-ceramic)로 마모치 전악수복을 다룬 데 비해, 본 SR(Hardan 2022)은 직접 레진복합레진 vs 간접(세라믹·금속도재·지르코니아) 수복 전반을 망라해 "tooth wear 수복재 선택" 상위 근거 맵을 제공한다. 직접 vs 간접의 우열 근거가 없다는 결론은 보존적·가역적 접근을 우선하라는 임상 지침과 직접 연결된다.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (16 clinical studies, follow-up up to 10 years, PRISMA 2020) of direct vs indirect restorations for tooth wear: high heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis and no restoration technique or material was shown superior for clinical performance.

## 한줄요약

치아 마모(tooth wear) 수복에서 직접 vs 간접 수복을 비교한 체계적 문헌고찰(16편 임상연구, 최대 10년 추적, PRISMA 2020): 이질성이 높아 메타분석은 불가했고, 어떤 수복 기법·재료도 임상 성적에서 우월하다는 근거는 없었다.

- **Authors:** Hardan L, Mancino D, Bourgi R, Cuevas-Suárez CE, Lukomska-Szymanska M, Zarow M, Jakubowicz N, Zamarripa-Calderón JE, Kafa L, Etienne O, Reitzer F, Kharouf N, Haïkel Y
- **Journal:** Bioengineering (MDPI), 2022, 9(8):346
- **DOI:** 10.3390/bioengineering9080346
- **Type:** Systematic review of clinical studies (no meta-analysis; qualitative synthesis)
- **Registration:** Open Science Framework; reported following PRISMA 2020.
- **Search date:** Databases searched up to 29 April 2022 (PubMed/MedLine, Scopus, ISI Web of Science, Scielo, EMBASE).

## 2. Key Contributions

- First systematic review focused specifically on **which technique (direct vs indirect) and material (ceramic vs resin-based)** is favored for restoring worn dentition, rather than on the procedural steps of treatment.
- Synthesizes 16 clinical studies (RCTs + observational) covering anterior and posterior worn teeth, follow-up up to 10 years.
- Catalogues the full range of materials used clinically for tooth wear: ceramo-metal crowns (CMCs), full gold crowns, lithium disilicate, zirconia, polymer-infiltrated ceramic networks (PICN), and direct/indirect resin composites.
- Concludes there is **no evidence for the superiority of any single restoration technique or material** for tooth-wear rehabilitation.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Framework (PICOS):** Population = dental substrate (worn dentition); Intervention = indirect restorations; Control = direct restorations; Outcomes = FDI or USPHS criteria; Study design = clinical trials.
- **Research question:** "What is the best treatment for treating tooth wear: direct or indirect restorations?"
- **Eligibility:** clinical studies of direct/indirect restorations for tooth wear; minimum 6-month follow-up; English language. Excluded: in vitro studies, case series, reviews, pilot studies, case reports.
- **Screening:** two blinded investigators (Rayyan platform); inter-examiner agreement by kappa; disagreements resolved by a third author. Duplicates removed in Mendeley.
- **Risk of bias:** assessed across selection bias, performance/detection bias, incomplete-data bias, reporting bias, and other bias.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Search yield:** 2776 records → 16 studies included in qualitative analysis.
- **Meta-analysis NOT performed** due to high heterogeneity of designs, materials, follow-ups, and outcome criteria.
- Most studies reported **survival rate** and clinical features graded with the **USPHS** (or FDI/World Dental Federation) evaluation system.
- **Etiology** of wear split mainly into chemical (erosion/GERD/diet/bulimia) and mechanical (attrition/bruxism); no study compared restoration outcomes by etiology.
- Representative included studies and findings:
  - **Milosevic 2016** (164 patients, nano-hybrid composite, 8 y): first-year failure rate 5.4%; longer time-to-failure in older subjects and with adequate posterior support; bruxism and increased OVD not associated with failure.
  - **Taubóck 2021** (13 patients, 11 y): direct composites at increased vertical dimension of occlusion show suitable long-term performance in severe wear.
  - **Mehta 2021 / 2021(b)** (34 participants): molar, posterior-mandibular, and multi-session anterior restorations had significantly higher failure risk; premolars lower risk than molars.
  - **Smales 2007**: RBCs typically failed by fracture (often repaired/replaced); ceramo-metal crowns failed by complete loss (often requiring RCT or extraction).
  - **Oudkerk 2020** (PICN, 2 y): elevated survival/success.
  - **Vailati 2013**, **Redman 2003**, **Hemmings 2000**: resin-based composites give worthy short-to-medium-term survival for localized anterior wear; veneers preserve tooth structure and vitality.
- **Contradictory findings** across studies on which restoration type performs best.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Severe clinical and methodological **heterogeneity** prevented quantitative pooling (meta-analysis).
- Many included studies carried **high risk of bias** (especially performance/detection and reporting bias).
- No study directly compared **anterior vs posterior** outcomes or outcomes stratified by **etiology** (chemical vs mechanical).
- Variable, often non-standardized outcome reporting and follow-up durations.
- Future need: standardized treatment protocols and head-to-head trials of direct vs indirect, material- and region-specific.

## 6. Related Work

- Builds on a 2014 systematic review that focused on the procedural steps (diagnostic waxing, occlusal positioning, VDO increase, restoration, follow-up) for treating tooth wear.
- Connects to the broader literature on resin composite as a conservative/additive approach vs full-coverage crowns (metal-ceramic, lithium disilicate, zirconia) for severely worn dentition.

## 7. Glossary

- **Tooth wear:** irreversible non-carious loss of tooth structure (erosion + abrasion + attrition).
- **Erosion / Abrasion / Attrition:** chemical dissolution by acids / wear by exogenous materials / tooth-to-tooth contact.
- **Direct restoration:** restoration placed and cured chairside (e.g., resin composite).
- **Indirect restoration:** lab- or CAD/CAM-fabricated restoration (crown, veneer, overlay) cemented intra-orally.
- **PICN:** polymer-infiltrated ceramic network (e.g., Vita Enamic).
- **USPHS / FDI criteria:** standardized clinical evaluation systems for restoration quality.
- **VDO / OVD:** vertical dimension of occlusion.
- **CMC:** ceramo-metal crown.
