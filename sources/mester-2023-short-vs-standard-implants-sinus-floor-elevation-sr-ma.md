## Why Ingested

Short implant vs sinus floor elevation 비교를 ≥5년 장기 RCT로 한정해 근거 수준을 높이기 위해 인제스트. [[sinus-lift/lateral/chaware-2021-short-vs-long-implant-sinus-graft-sr-ma]] 의 혼합 추적 결과를 장기 subset으로 정제하며, ISR에서 standard+SFE가 수치상 높으나 유의차 미달임을 보여 short implant 비열등 thesis를 조건부로 강화.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 5 RCTs (≥5-year follow-up) showing no significant difference in implant survival between short implants and standard implants + sinus floor elevation, with significantly lower marginal bone loss and fewer biological complications favoring short implants.

## 한줄요약

≥5년 추적 RCT 5편 메타분석 — short implant vs standard implant+상악동거상술의 생존율 유의차 없음(RR 0.97, p=0.07), 변연골소실은 short implant가 유의하게 적고 생물학적 합병증도 적음.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Restricts to ≥5-year RCTs — directly addresses the long-term durability gap.
- Significant MBL and biological-complication advantages for short implants quantified.
- Tightens the equivalence claim to long-term horizons.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- SR+MA of RCTs with ≥5-year follow-up; 1619 screened, 5 RCTs included.
- Comparison: short implants vs standard implants + sinus floor elevation in atrophic posterior maxilla.
- Outcomes: implant survival rate (ISR), marginal bone loss (MBL), biological and prosthetic complications.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- ISR: RR 0.97 (95% CI 0.94–1.00), P=0.07 (standard+SFE numerically higher, not significant).
- MBL: WMD −0.29 (95% CI −0.49 to −0.09), P=0.005 (favors short implants).
- Biological complications: RR 0.46 (95% CI 0.23–0.91), P=0.03 (fewer with short implants).
- Prosthetic complications: RR 1.51 (95% CI 0.64–3.55), P=0.34 (ns).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Only 5 RCTs; ISR difference borderline (p=0.07) — underpowered for survival.
- Authors note standard+SFE showed numerically higher survival; longer RCTs needed.
- Heterogeneity in implant systems/graft materials.

## 6. Related Work
- Short-implant RCT literature; Esposito, Thoma trials.

## 7. Glossary
- ISR: implant survival rate. SFE: sinus floor elevation.
- WMD: weighted mean difference.
