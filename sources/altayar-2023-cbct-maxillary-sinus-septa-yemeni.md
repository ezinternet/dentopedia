## One-line Summary
Cross-sectional CBCT study of 880 maxillary sinuses (440 Yemeni patients): sinus septa present in 63.9% of patients and 47% of sinuses; mean septum height 5.2 mm; most originated from the floor (54.5%), located in the middle region (43%), with coronal orientation (66%) and complete configuration (58.2%) — among the highest prevalence figures reported in the literature.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health 2023; 23: 466
- **Type**: Retrospective cross-sectional analysis
- **Institutions**: West China School of Stomatology, Sichuan University, China; Taiz University, Yemen
- **Study type**: Cross-sectional CBCT-based observational

## 2. Key Contributions
- First CBCT-based study of maxillary sinus septa anatomy in a Yemeni population.
- Reports one of the highest prevalence values for sinus septa documented in the literature (63.9% of patients, 47% of sinuses).
- Characterizes 3D septa parameters relevant to lateral-window sinus floor elevation planning: origin wall, location (anterior/middle/posterior), orientation (coronal/sagittal/transverse), and configuration (complete vs incomplete).
- Tests associations with age, gender, and dental status; reports no significant effect on septa presence.
- Reports no significant association between sinus septa and Schneiderian membrane pathology.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Retrospective cross-sectional design.
- 880 maxillary sinuses (440 patients) imaged by CBCT.
- Image analysis with **Anatomage Invivo v6**.
- Variables: septa prevalence, height, location (anterior/middle/posterior), orientation (coronal/sagittal/transverse), origin (floor/lateral/medial wall), configuration (complete/incomplete).
- Covariates: age, gender, dental status (dentate/partial/edentulous).
- Statistics: descriptive + analytical; significance threshold p < 0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Septa prevalence**: 63.9% of patients, 47% of sinuses.
- **Mean septum height**: 5.2 mm.
- **Laterality**: 15.7% right only, 18% left only, 30.2% bilateral.
- **Origin**: 54.5% arise from the sinus floor.
- **Location**: 43% in the middle region of the sinus.
- **Orientation**: 66% coronal.
- **Configuration**: 58.2% complete (full-height) septa.
- Gender, age, and dental status: no statistically significant effect on septa presence.
- Schneiderian membrane pathology: no significant association with septa.
- Background context cited: Schneiderian membrane perforation occurs in 11–56% of SFE cases.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Single-population (Yemeni) — generalizability requires comparable cohorts elsewhere.
- Retrospective design; selection bias possible (patients referred for CBCT).
- No correlation with intraoperative perforation outcomes (CBCT only, not surgical follow-up).
- Software-dependent measurements.

## 6. Related Work
- Underwood 1910 — first description of maxillary sinus septa.
- Multiple population-specific CBCT septa prevalence studies (Korean, Chinese, European) for comparison.
- Evaluation of maxillary sinus septa and predicted Schneiderian membrane perforation risk — related CBCT studies.

## 7. Glossary
- **Maxillary sinus septum (Underwood septum)**: cortical bone projection arising from any internal sinus wall; can compartmentalize the sinus and obstruct membrane elevation.
- **Sinus floor elevation (SFE)**: surgical augmentation to gain vertical bone for posterior maxillary implants.
- **Schneiderian membrane**: respiratory mucosa lining the maxillary sinus.
- **CBCT**: cone-beam computed tomography — standard 3D imaging for SFE planning.
