## Why Ingested

The safety of peroxide bleaching on enamel integrity is the most commonly cited concern limiting clinical use and regulatory approval (EU 6% limit). This 2026 SR+MA directly quantifies enamel microhardness change using ISO 28,399-aligned methodology and meta-regression on exposure time — the first to use ROM + multilevel random-effects — extending and formalizing the evidence base touched on in [[tooth-whitening/de-oliveira-2024-otc-bleaching-color-adverse-effects]].

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 81 in vitro studies (9 in synthesis) found peroxide bleaching causes small but statistically significant enamel microhardness reductions (ROM = 0.89; 95% CI 0.84–0.94), unlikely to be clinically meaningful; exposure time but not concentration was a significant moderator.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA to use ISO 28,399-aligned eligibility criteria for enamel microhardness studies
- Applied multilevel random-effects meta-analysis with log-transformed ratio of means (ROM)
- Demonstrated that the pooled microhardness reduction (ROM = 0.89) does not exceed the 10% threshold considered clinically meaningful
- Showed exposure time — not peroxide concentration — is the key moderator of enamel damage
- Concluded that current evidence does not support strict regulatory peroxide concentration limits (e.g., EU 6% cap) on the basis of enamel hardness alone

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search**: Four databases searched per PRISMA 2020 and Cochrane Handbook
- **Inclusion**: In vitro studies measuring enamel Vickers or Knoop microhardness before and after peroxide bleaching (hydrogen peroxide or carbamide peroxide); ISO 28,399 alignment as an eligibility anchor
- **Exclusion**: In vivo studies; studies without pre/post measurements
- **Statistics**: Multilevel random-effects meta-analysis; log-transformed ratio of means (ROM); meta-regression on prespecified moderators (concentration, exposure time, indentation load, dwell time)
- **Eligible/Synthesized**: 81 eligible studies; 9 entered quantitative synthesis (strict ISO-aligned criteria)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Pooled ROM | 0.89 (95% CI: 0.84–0.94; p < 0.01) |
| Clinical significance threshold | ROM < 0.90 (10% reduction) |
| I² (heterogeneity) | 50.8% (moderate) |
| tau | 0.074 |
| Exposure time as moderator | 10× increase → 6.8% decrease in Knoop hardness (p = 0.044) |
| After exclusion of outlier | Effect non-significant (1.9% decrease; p = NS) |
| Concentration as moderator | Not significant |
| Time-course analysis finding | Larger changes at lower concentrations (unexpected direction) |

- The pooled ROM of 0.89 places the reduction just below the 10% threshold, suggesting clinical borderline significance; outlier sensitivity is high
- Exposure time was significant but sensitive to a single influential study
- Concentration did not significantly predict enamel change, undermining rationale for concentration-based regulation

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 9 of 81 studies entered quantitative synthesis — most studies failed ISO 28,399 alignment (highlights widespread methodological heterogeneity)
- In vitro only; clinical translation is limited (saliva, remineralization, thermal cycling are absent)
- Moderate residual heterogeneity (I² = 50.8%) despite the strict inclusion criteria
- Results sensitive to one influential study
- Authors call for ISO 28,399-compliant studies as a research standard

## 7. Glossary

- **ROM (Ratio of Means)**: Log-transformed effect size expressing post/pre ratio; ROM = 0.89 means 11% reduction
- **Vickers / Knoop hardness**: Indentation-based enamel microhardness tests; Knoop is elongated, Vickers pyramidal
- **ISO 28,399**: International standard for the evaluation of tooth whitening products
- **Multilevel random-effects**: Meta-analytic model accounting for multiple outcomes per study
- **PROSPERO**: International prospective register for systematic reviews
