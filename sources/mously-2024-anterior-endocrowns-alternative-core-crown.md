## Why Ingested

Brings the modern minimally-invasive alternative (endocrown) into the new `post-and-core` category, directly framing when NOT to use a post — the conservative counterpoint to the post-necessity finding in [[post-and-core/alenezi-2024-endodontically-treated-teeth-post-placement-survival]]. Full-text PMC ingest gives detailed material/ferrule/extension-depth decision factors and connects to fracture-stress logic in [[resin/susita-2026-comparative-analysis-stress-distribution-teeth]].

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (12 in-vitro/FEA studies, narrative — no meta-analysis): endocrowns gave comparable-or-greater fracture resistance and more repairable failures vs post-core-crown in anterior ETT; ferrule was the dominant fatigue-resistance factor, lithium disilicate the best material.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(in-vitro/FEA 12편, 메타분석 없음·서술적): 전치부 근관치료치아에서 엔도크라운이 포스트-코어-크라운 대비 동등하거나 더 높은 파절저항과 더 수리 가능한 실패양상을 보였고, 페룰이 피로저항의 결정적 인자, 리튬디실리케이트가 최적 재료였음.

## 1. Document Information

Systematic review (PRISMA, QUIN risk-of-bias), *International Dental Journal* (2025;75(1):59-74), PMC full text. PICO: anterior ETT, endocrown vs post-and-core, biomechanical/fracture outcomes. Search Jan 2014-Mar 2024 → 12 studies (8 in-vitro fatigue + load-to-failure, plus FEA). All studies medium risk of bias (QUIN 50-70%). Heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis → narrative synthesis.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Synthesizes endocrown vs post-core-crown biomechanics specifically for anterior teeth (most reviews are posterior-focused).
- Reframes the post's role: its main benefit is core RETENTION, not tooth reinforcement; rigid (metal/zirconia) restorations concentrate root stress → catastrophic failures.
- Identifies ferrule as the dominant fatigue-resistance factor regardless of technique, and lithium disilicate as the best-performing material; gives intracanal-extension guidance (3-5 mm).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

1307 records → 1107 unique → 30 full-text → 12 included. QUIN tool for in-vitro bias. Qualitative comparison across fracture resistance, failure pattern, pull-out bond strength, FEA stress distribution.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Most studies: endocrowns ≈ or > post-core-crown (PCC) fracture resistance; endocrown failures more repairable, PCC failures more catastrophic (vertical root fracture).
- Ferrule (1-2 mm) dominant for fatigue resistance regardless of material/technique; without ferrule, endocrown with 3 mm intracanal extension gave best stress distribution.
- Lithium disilicate (flexural ~400 MPa, toughness 3.3 MPa·m^1/2) best biomechanics; resin nano-ceramic ~7% fewer catastrophic failures (lower modulus); zirconia highest fracture resistance but rigid (210 GPa vs dentin 18.6 GPa) → root stress → catastrophic failures.
- Intracanal extension 3-5 mm; deeper (5-6 mm) raises pull-out bond strength but may worsen marginal gap. Long posts (10 mm) gave most non-repairable fractures.
- One 1-yr double-blind RCT (e.max endocrown vs e.max PCC): no significant gross-fracture difference; endocrown 33% marginal discrepancy (NS); authors still recommended PCC for anterior ETT.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

Predominantly in-vitro/FEA — limited clinical generalisability; only one short (1-yr) RCT. All studies medium risk of bias; heterogeneity (tooth type, material, extension depth, load angle/magnitude) precluded meta-analysis. Calls for standardised methodology and longer clinical trials.

## 7. Glossary

- **Endocrown** — single-unit adhesive monolithic restoration using the pulp chamber for macro-mechanical retention + adhesive cementation; no intracanal post.
- **Post-core-crown (PCC)** — conventional intracanal post + core buildup + crown.
- **Catastrophic vs repairable failure** — catastrophic = vertical root fracture (often unrestorable); repairable = debonding / horizontal coronal fracture amenable to re-treatment.
- **Modulus mismatch** — high-modulus restoration (zirconia/metal) vs dentin causes stress concentration in the root.
