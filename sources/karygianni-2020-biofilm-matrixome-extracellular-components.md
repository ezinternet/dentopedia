## Why Ingested
Introduces and defines the 'matrixome' concept — the complete inventory of EPS biomolecules — that provides the unifying framework for understanding matrix-related virulence across [[oral-microbiology/jakubovics-2021-dental-plaque-biofilm-matrix]], [[oral-microbiology/koo-2013-exopolysaccharides-matrix-biofilm-virulence]], and [[oral-microbiology/ponde-2021-candida-albicans-biofilms-polymicrobial]].

## One-line Summary
Perspective review coining 'matrixome' to describe the complete inventory of EPS biomolecules (polysaccharides, proteins, eDNA, lipids) across mono- and polymicrobial biofilms, emphasising targeting the matrixome for precise antibiofilm strategies.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Proposes the term 'matrixome' (adapted from 'matrisome' in eukaryotic biology) to denote the entire inventory of known EPS biomolecules in biofilms — their molecular, structural, and functional diversity.
- Provides a comprehensive taxonomy of EPS classes across model organisms: polysaccharides (glucans/fructans in S. mutans; Psl, Pel, alginate in P. aeruginosa; VPS in V. cholerae; mannans/β-glucans in C. albicans), proteins (TasA, BslA, Gtf/Ftf, Als, Hwp), eDNA/eRNA, and lipids/lipoproteins.
- Demonstrates that biogenic minerals (CaCO3, calcite) are matrixome components providing structural support in B. subtilis and Mycobacterium biofilms; Proteus spp. and P. aeruginosa generate calcium/magnesium mineral encrustations.
- Highlights that Gtf enzymes from S. mutans bind the surface of many oral microbes (A. viscosus, L. casei, C. albicans), converting them to glucan producers — key polymicrobial virulence mechanism.
- Advocates combinatorial treatment strategies targeting both EPS and embedded microbial cells.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Design: perspective/narrative review (Trends in Microbiology format).
- Model organisms used as illustrative examples: B. subtilis, S. aureus, S. mutans, P. aeruginosa, V. cholerae, C. albicans, E. coli.
- Incorporates confocal laser scanning microscopy (CLSM) and FISH data for oral biofilm structure visualisation.
- Identifies urgent need for in-depth polymicrobial EPS studies.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- EPS classes catalogued in Table 1: polysaccharides, proteins (fibrous and globular, including extracellular enzymes), lipids, nucleic acids (eDNA, eRNA).
- Six-species oral biofilm visualised by CLSM (S. mutans, S. oralis, A. oris, F. nucleatum, V. dispar, C. albicans): exopolysaccharides (blue), eDNA (red), proteins (yellow), bacteria (green).
- S. mutans Gtfs bind A. viscosus, L. casei, and C. albicans surfaces, making non-Gtf producers de facto glucan formers.
- CaCO3 demonstrated in matrix of B. subtilis and M. smegmatis; Proteus mirabilis/vulgaris/Providencia rettgeri cause catheter encrustation via calcium/magnesium minerals.
- Flagella and type IV pili serve as mechanosensors in V. cholerae and B. subtilis, triggering EPS production during surface contact.
- Matrix assembly model (3 phases): surface EPS polymer formation → in-situ expansion → microcolony scaffold development — conserved across P. aeruginosa, B. subtilis, V. cholerae, and S. mutans.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Knowledge of EPS matrix in polymicrobial communities is limited — authors identify this as urgent unmet need.
- Most mechanistic data from monospecies model organisms; cross-kingdom data sparse.
- Further mechanistic in vivo studies required to link matrixome to clinical infection outcomes.

## 6. Related Work
- Builds directly on Flemming & Wingender (2010) EPS review.
- Incorporates Bowen & Koo (2011) and Koo et al. (2013) glucan/virulence data.
- Extends dental biofilm matrixome findings to cross-kingdom and environmental biofilm contexts.

## 7. Glossary
- Matrixome: complete inventory of known biomolecules (EPS) and their molecular, structural, and functional diversity associated with biofilm assembly and virulence.
- eRNA: extracellular RNA — newly recognised EPS component alongside eDNA.
- Emergent properties: biofilm characteristics not predictable from planktonic cell study (adhesion, spatial organisation, antimicrobial tolerance, chemical heterogeneity).
- EPS degradation: therapeutic use of exogenous enzymes (e.g., DNase, glucanase) to disassemble biofilm matrix.
- Biogenic minerals: calcium carbonate/calcite produced by or within biofilm matrix providing structural support.
