## Why Ingested

Establishes the restoration–periodontium interface as a periodontics topic in its own right: margin placement (supra- vs subgingival), material choice (zirconia/CAD-CAM vs metal-ceramic), surface finish, and contour all modulate biofilm accumulation and gingival inflammation. Anchors the new fixed-prosthesis/periodontal-health cluster and is reinforced by the cross-sectional clinical data in [[periodontics/lamichhane-2025-crown-margins-marginal-adaptation-periodontal]] and the preparation-design focus of [[periodontics/hajaj-2025-marginal-tooth-preparation-periodontal-health]].

## One-line Summary

Narrative review (search 1981–2021) concluding that supragingival margins and CAD/CAM zirconia restorations preserve periodontal health better than subgingival margins and conventional cast-alloy restorations, with marginal discrepancy driving biofilm, GCF flow, recurrent caries and bone loss.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Synthesizes technical, clinical, and molecular aspects of how fixed prosthetic biomaterials interact with periodontal tissues.
- Argues marginal discrepancy is the central failure pathway: thick cement → cement dissolution → biofilm/microleakage → margin discoloration, increased GCF, recurrent caries, pulp infection → periodontal lesion and bone loss.
- Positions zirconia/CAD-CAM as periodontally favorable vs conventional alloys; positions supragingival margins as periodontally favorable vs subgingival.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- Narrative synthesis structured by theme: marginal/internal fit, margin position, restorative materials (metal-ceramic, all-ceramic, zirconia, Co-Cr), surface roughness/finish, biologic width, and maintenance.
- No formal risk-of-bias appraisal or quantitative pooling (narrative format).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Margin gap**: no consensus optimum; cited thresholds <120 µm, <100 µm, and an "adequate" range of 20–75 µm. Larger gaps worsen biofilm and periodontal sequelae.
- **Margin position**: supragingival > equigingival > subgingival for oral-hygiene maintenance; supragingival gaps do not lead to secondary caries or periodontal disease, subgingival gaps impede hygiene.
- **Materials**: CAD/CAM zirconia gives better marginal fit, less inflammation, easier maintenance, and better restoration of periodontal health/oral hygiene than conventional-method or other-alloy constructions.
- **Surface finish**: higher surface roughness favors microbial growth; good polish/finish from proper technique is protective.
- Emphasizes pre-prosthetic assessment of oral hygiene, gingival and periodontal status, plus prosthodontist–periodontist collaboration.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative (not systematic); no meta-analysis, selection/extraction not formally protocolized, no risk-of-bias tool.
- Heterogeneous source studies; conclusions are directional rather than quantitative.

## 6. Related Work

- Couples with cross-sectional abutment-vs-non-abutment data (Lamichhane 2025) and preparation-design reviews (Hajaj 2025).
- Relates to biologic-width / supracrestal-tissue-attachment literature and to periodontal maintenance protocols.

## 7. Glossary

- **GCF**: gingival crevicular fluid — exudate from the gingival sulcus; flow rises with inflammation.
- **Marginal discrepancy / margin gap**: vertical/horizontal misfit between restoration margin and tooth finish line.
- **CAD/CAM**: computer-aided design/manufacturing of restorations (e.g., milled zirconia).
- **Biologic width (supracrestal tissue attachment)**: junctional epithelium + connective tissue attachment coronal to alveolar crest; violation by deep margins causes chronic inflammation/bone loss.
