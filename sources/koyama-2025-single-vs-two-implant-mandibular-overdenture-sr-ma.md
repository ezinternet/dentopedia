## Why Ingested

오버덴쳐 임플란트 감시 인제스트 — 하악 1 vs 2 임플란트 IOD를 직접 비교한 17-RCT SR+MA로, 임플란트 개수 결정의 핵심 근거. 임플란트 생존율은 5년까지 차이 없지만 보철 합병증(의치 파절·재제작·메탈하우징 재부착)은 1-IOD에서 ~2배 잦다는, 갯수 선택의 trade-off를 정량화한다. 기존 [[implants/park-2023-narrow-regular-diameter-mandibular-overdentures]](직경 비교)와 짝을 이뤄 하악 오버덴쳐 임플란트 설계 변수(직경 vs 개수)를 보강하며, [[implants/liu-2021-immediate-delayed-loading-overdenture-mbl-sr-ma]](부하 시기)와 함께 오버덴쳐 의사결정 축을 채운다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 17 RCTs comparing single (1-IOD) vs two (2-IOD) implant mandibular overdentures: no difference in implant survival through 5 years, but denture fracture, denture remake, and metal-housing reattachment are ~2× more frequent with 1-IOD.

## 한줄요약

하악 단일(1-IOD) vs 2개(2-IOD) 임플란트 오버덴쳐를 비교한 17편 RCT SR+MA — 5년까지 임플란트 생존율은 차이 없으나, 의치 파절·재제작·메탈하우징 재부착 같은 보철 합병증은 1-IOD에서 약 2배 더 잦다.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First MA to update the 1-IOD vs 2-IOD comparison with the most recent RCTs (published since 2017), overturning an earlier SR (Ahmed et al.) that had reported higher implant survival for 1-IOD.
- Quantifies that **implant survival is equivalent** between 1- and 2-IOD at 1, 3, and 5 years — including across loading-protocol subgroups (immediate/early/conventional).
- Quantifies the **prosthetic-complication penalty of 1-IOD**: denture fracture (5Y RR 2.10), new denture fabrication (5Y RR 2.57), and metal-housing reattachment (3Y RR 2.29; 5Y RR 2.31) are all ~2× more frequent than 2-IOD.
- Shows that **relining and O-ring replacement frequencies do NOT differ** between groups, supporting a structural-strength (not mobility) explanation for the extra fractures and a metal-framework reinforcement recommendation for 1-IOD.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: PRISMA-reported SR+MA; PROSPERO-registered (CRD420250644169).
- **PICOT**: edentulous patients with 1- or 2-IOD; I = 1-IOD, C = 2-IOD; outcomes implant + prosthesis failures; follow-up > 12 months.
- **Eligibility**: RCTs only.
- **Databases**: PubMed, Google Scholar, CENTRAL, Web of Science; final search April 2025.
- **Screening**: 1,083 identified → 289 duplicates removed → 794 → title/abstract screen → 32 full-text → **17 RCTs included** (15 excluded: 2 no full text, 6 duplicate data, 6 follow-up < 1 year, 1 insufficient data).
- **Included studies**: follow-up 12–60 months; ages mid-50s to mid-60s; ball or Locator attachments; loading immediate / 2W / 21D / 6W / 3M. Systems: Neobiotech, Sterngold, Dentis, Straumann (SLA/SLActive/Roxolid), ADIN, Neodent, Brånemark TiUnite, Conexão, Implantium.
- **Synthesis**: dichotomous outcomes → RR (95% CI); continuous → MD (95% CI); random-effects model; heterogeneity by chi-square + I²; RevMan 5.4.1; funnel plots. Zero-event studies (both groups) excluded from the relevant MA.
- **RoB**: Cochrane RoB 2.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Implant survival (1Y / 3Y / 5Y)** — no statistically significant difference between 1- and 2-IOD at any timepoint; loading-protocol subgroups also NS.

| Outcome | 1Y | 3Y | 5Y |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Denture fracture** | RR 2.28 (1.01–5.14), p=0.05, I²=0% | RR 2.08 (0.82–5.28), p=0.12, I²=40% (NS) | RR 2.10 (1.21–3.64), p=0.008, I²=0% |
| **New denture fabrication** | no events (no MA) | RR 2.29 (0.76–6.90), p=0.14, I²=0% (NS) | RR 2.57 (1.11–5.94), p=0.03, I²=0% |
| **Metal-housing reattachment** | NS | RR 2.29 (1.04–5.04), p=0.04, I²=56% | RR 2.31 (1.03–5.18), p=0.04, I²=84% (substantial heterogeneity) |
| **Relining** | NS | NS | NS |
| **O-ring replacement** | NS | NS | NS |

- RR > 1 = higher event rate in **1-IOD** group. Denture fracture in 1-IOD is "approximately twice as high" as 2-IOD at 5Y.
- **Patient satisfaction / function** (not meta-analyzed — no standardized metric): 1-IOD significantly improves satisfaction vs conventional complete denture; vs 2-IOD, OHRQoL/OHIP results mixed (some favor 2-IOD, some NS). Two-colored-gum mixing-ability test: both 1- and 2-IOD improve vs baseline CD with no significant 1-vs-2 difference; almond test: both improve vs CD, 2-IOD greater improvement than 1-IOD.
- **MBL**: not separately meta-analyzed in this review (outcomes were implant survival + prosthetic complications; marginal bone loss not a reported pooled endpoint).
- **Mechanistic support (cited FEA)**: El-Zawahry et al. — 1-IOD total deformation ~50% greater than 2-IOD; higher Von Mises stress in 1-IOD overdenture; metal framework reduced tensile stress around the implant housing by **61.8%**.
- **RoB**: mostly low; "some concerns" for randomization in some studies and for missing outcome data in 5-year studies. Funnel plot symmetrical → low publication-bias risk.
- **Cost** (cited): 1-IOD ≈ 75% of 2-IOD cost (Hartmann et al.); but long-term complications + metal-framework reinforcement may narrow the gap.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Most RCTs had follow-up ≤ 5 years → long-term prognosis uncertain.
- Reports on prosthetic complications limited; metal-housing 5Y MA had substantial heterogeneity (I²=84%).
- Insufficient sample sizes precluded subgroup analyses by implant length, diameter, and type.
- Patient-satisfaction/OHRQoL not poolable (no standardized instrument).
- Authors call for further long-term RCTs focusing on prosthetic-complication incidence.

## 6. Related Work

- McGill (2002) / York (2009) consensus statements — recommended 2-IOD as first-choice for edentulous mandible (the standard this review re-examines).
- Ahmed et al. SR — earlier review reporting higher 1-IOD implant survival, now contradicted by this updated MA.
- El-Zawahry et al. — FEA of deformation/stress in 1- vs 2-IOD and metal-framework effect.

## 7. Glossary

- **1-IOD / 2-IOD**: single / two implant-supported overdenture.
- **IOD**: implant-supported overdenture.
- **RR**: risk ratio (RR > 1 = more events in 1-IOD).
- **Metal-housing reattachment**: recementing/recapturing the metal housing that holds the attachment (Locator/ball) when it debonds from the denture base.
- **O-ring / relining**: O-ring = elastic retention insert (ball attachment); relining = re-adapting the denture intaglio to the mucosa.
- **OHRQoL / OHIP**: oral health-related quality of life / Oral Health Impact Profile.
- **FEA**: finite element analysis.
- **CD**: conventional complete denture.
