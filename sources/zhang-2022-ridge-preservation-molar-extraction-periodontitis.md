## Why Ingested

Most high-quality ARP evidence comes from anterior/intact sockets; whether ARP helps at periodontally compromised MOLAR sockets (severe stage III/IV periodontitis, multi-wall infrabony defects) is the open question this protocol targets — it extends the scope-of-ARP debate in [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/mardas-2023-alveolar-ridge-preservation-overtreatment]] (when is ARP overtreatment?) and complements the posterior-only RCT [[bone-regeneration/ridge-preservation/arroteia-2025-alveolar-ridge-preservation-posterior-rct]] by adding a severe-periodontitis molar indication with histology and 3D soft-tissue endpoints. Ingested as the registered design (ChiCTR2200056335); a protocol with planned methodology only — no outcomes reported yet.

## One-line Summary
Study protocol (BMC Oral Health 2022): 2-arm parallel RCT design (n=70 molar sites), ARP vs natural healing at severe-periodontitis (stage III/IV) molar extraction sockets; primary outcomes = CBCT vertical/horizontal ridge change, 3D soft-tissue contour & mucosa thickness, histology, periodontal parameters — no results yet.

## 한줄요약
연구 프로토콜(BMC Oral Health 2022): 중증 치주염(stage III/IV) 대구치 발치와에서 치조제보존(ARP) vs 자연치유를 비교하는 2군 평행 RCT 설계(n=70 발치 부위); 1차 결과 = CBCT 수직·수평 변화, 3D 연조직 윤곽·점막 두께, 조직학, 치주 지표 — 아직 결과 없음.

## 1. Document Information
- **Type**: Study protocol (RCT protocol — no results yet), SPIRIT-compliant
- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health 2022;22:511
- **DOI**: 10.1186/s12903-022-02544-0
- **Trial registration**: Chinese Clinical Trial Register (ChiCTR) ChiCTR2200056335 (registered 2022-02-04, v1.0)
- **Institution**: Peking University School and Hospital of Stomatology (Beijing, China); University of Washington (co-author Chung)
- **Funding/Design**: Two-group parallel RCT; reporting follows SPIRIT statement

## 2. Key Contributions
- Addresses a sparse-evidence niche: ARP at **periodontally compromised MOLAR** sockets (severe stage III/IV periodontitis), where most ARP evidence is from anterior/intact sockets.
- Combines **hard tissue (CBCT)**, **3D soft-tissue (intraoral scanning / STL superimposition)**, and **histology** (biopsy at implant surgery) in one trial — soft-tissue profile change at infected molar sites is specifically under-reported.
- Planned 1-year-post-loading follow-up window for soft-tissue/mucosa endpoints; aims to define the most beneficial and least traumatic treatment for these patients.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: Two-group parallel RCT. Test group = ridge preservation; control = natural (unassisted) healing.
- **Sample size**: 70 molar extraction sites total (power analysis).
- **Setting**: Peking University School and Hospital of Stomatology.
- **Inclusion**: age > 25; good compliance/oral hygiene; stage III/IV periodontitis; ≥1 hopeless molar with severe bone loss requiring extraction; ≥2 socket walls beyond apex or ≥3 mm of the socket; ≥1 adjacent proximal tooth.
- **Exclusion**: pregnancy/lactation; meds/disease complicating bone healing; surgical contraindications (uncontrolled HTN/DM); head-neck radiotherapy; smoking >10 cig/day; absence of both adjacent teeth; acute pathology; teeth extracted due to caries/endo failure/fracture.
- **Randomization**: Excel-generated random numbers, sealed sequentially numbered opaque envelopes (SNOSE).
- **Procedures**: atraumatic extraction; ARP in test group; CBCT + intraoral STL scans + periodontal exam through follow-up; bone biopsy obtained during implant surgery (≈6-month healing).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
**This is a protocol — no outcomes are reported.** Planned endpoints:
- **Primary outcomes**: (i) vertical & horizontal alveolar ridge change on CBCT (baseline → 6 mo post-extraction/ARP); (ii) soft-tissue contour change by superimposed digital impressions; (iii) mucosa thickness change (CBCT + STL superimposition); (iv) histological features of implant sites (6-month healing); (v) periodontal parameter changes over follow-up.
- **Secondary outcomes**: patient-reported post-operative reaction; need for simultaneous bone graft or sinus lifting during implant placement.
- Background cited: ARP indications widening from anterior intact sockets to infected molar sockets; Kim et al. and Wei et al. report ARP at periodontally compromised sockets is safe and may decrease need for further regenerative/sinus procedures; DBBM ARP showed lower new-bone formation vs natural healing at 3–6 mo (motivating histology + longer healing).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- No results yet — clinical conclusions cannot be drawn until the trial reports.
- Single-center; molar sites only; severe-periodontitis indication limits generalization to intact/anterior sockets.
- ARP grafting/membrane material details and exact ARP protocol are only partly specified in the protocol; histology benchmark (DBBM lower new bone) flagged as needing longer healing.

## 7. Glossary
- **ARP** — Alveolar Ridge Preservation (치조제보존)
- **Stage III/IV periodontitis** — severe periodontitis with extensive attachment/bone loss (2017 classification)
- **CBCT** — Cone Beam Computed Tomography
- **STL** — Stereolithography file (3D surface from intraoral scan)
- **DBBM** — Deproteinized Bovine Bone Mineral
- **SPIRIT** — Standard Protocol Items: Recommendations for Interventional Trials
- **SNOSE** — Sequentially Numbered Opaque Sealed Envelopes
- **USH** — Unassisted Socket Healing (natural healing control)
