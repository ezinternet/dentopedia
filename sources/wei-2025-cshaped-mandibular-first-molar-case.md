## Why Ingested

Documents a rare C-shaped variant in the mandibular FIRST molar (the wiki's prevalence pages center on second molars), with the first reported case of four separate canals within a single fused C-shaped first-molar root, confirmed by CBCT. Applies the Fan C1–C5 classification and management principles from [[wiki/endodontics/anatomy/fernandes-2014-cshaped-root-canal-configuration-review]] and the thin-wall morphology of [[wiki/endodontics/anatomy/song-2008-cross-sectional-morphology-and-minimum-canal]] to an uncommon tooth, and extends the second-molar-focused CBCT prevalence picture in [[wiki/endodontics/anatomy/shemesh-2017-cshaped-canals-cbct-mandibular-molars]], [[wiki/endodontics/anatomy/armenta-2024-cshaped-canal-radix-mandibular-molars-mexican-cbct]], and [[wiki/endodontics/anatomy/yousefi-2025-cshaped-canal-prevalence-cbct-sr-ma]] into the rarer first-molar territory.

## One-line Summary

Case report (CBCT-confirmed) of bilateral C-shaped mandibular first molars — the first documented mandibular first molar with four separate canals (two mesial + two distal) inside a single fused C-shaped root.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Reports a **rare C-shaped configuration in mandibular first molars** (vs the typical second molar), bilaterally, in a 35-year-old male.
- **First documented mandibular first molar with four separate canals (2 mesial + 2 distal) within a single fused C-shaped root**, confirmed by CBCT.
- Provides a literature-review **prevalence table** of C-shaped configuration in mandibular first molars: range 0.16%–24.33% across populations (e.g., 0.2% Mexican [Armenta 2024], 0.16% Israeli [Shemesh 2017], 24.01–24.33% in some Brazilian/Saudi CBCT series).
- Illustrates the **clinical risk** of missed canals (a tooth with ≥1 missed canal has ~3.66× greater odds of periapical lesion) and thin-wall strip-perforation/fracture susceptibility in C-shaped systems.
- Demonstrates a CBCT-guided, microscope-assisted treatment with controlled-memory NiTi and sonic activation.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Single-patient case report. Right mandibular first molar: symptomatic irreversible pulpitis. Diagnosis via periapical radiograph + dental operating microscope (Carl Zeiss) revealing a fused C-shaped chamber with four orifices (two mesial, two distal, buccal merging), confirmed by **CBCT** showing C3c coronally, two mesial + two distal canals in the middle third, and individual mesial/distal canals apically — the fused C-shaped root extending buccally to the apex. Negotiation with 06#/08#/10# C-files; working length by Raypex 6 apex locator; chemomechanical prep with pre-bendable controlled-memory NiTi (PLEX-V) to size 25/.06; 3% NaOCl + 17% EDTA with sonic activation. Left molar separately exhibited a single oval mesial canal + semicolon-shaped distal canal.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Mandibular first molar C-shaped prevalence (review table)**: 0.16%–24.33% across CBCT/dentascan studies; many populations <1% (Portuguese 0.6%, Israeli 0.16%, Saudi 0–0.19%, Thai 0.39%, Mexican 0.2%), some markedly higher (Brazilian 24.01%, Saudi 24.33%).
- **Mandibular second molar** prevalence cited as high as 44.5–48.7% (e.g., Korean 40%, female 47% vs male 32%).
- **Right molar (index)**: four separate canals in a fused C-shaped root; C3c → C3(two mesial/two distal) → C3c along coronal/middle/apical thirds.
- **Left molar**: single oval mesial + semicolon (C2-type) distal.
- **Missed-canal risk**: ~3.658× greater probability of periapical lesion with ≥1 missed canal (cited).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single case; no long-term outcome/recall data reported in the extracted text.
- First-molar C-shaped prevalence figures are pooled from heterogeneous populations and methods (in vivo CBCT vs in vitro dentascan), with wide variance.
- Rarity limits generalizability; CBCT is emphasized as essential to avoid missed canals in atypical first-molar anatomy.

## 6. Related Work

- Fan et al. — C1–C5 cross-sectional classification used to describe the canals.
- Shemesh et al. (2017), Armenta et al. (2024) — CBCT prevalence studies tabulated, including mandibular first molars.
- Fernandes et al. (2014) — review of etiology/classification/management referenced conceptually.

## 7. Glossary

- **Mandibular first molar**: tooth in which C-shaped anatomy is rare (0.16–24.33%), unlike the second molar where it is common.
- **C3c / C3d (Fan)**: C3 with two (C3c) or three (C3d) separate canals; both cross-sectional angles <60°.
- **Controlled-memory NiTi**: pre-bendable nickel-titanium files that retain a bent shape, aiding negotiation of curved/narrow C-shaped canals.
- **Strip perforation**: lateral perforation of a thin canal wall (danger zone) during shaping — heightened risk in thin-walled C-shaped roots.
- **Missed canal**: an uninstrumented canal acting as a bacterial reservoir; ≥1 missed canal ≈3.66× higher periapical-lesion odds.
