## One-line Summary
Original in-vitro study (porcine tibia, 72 osteotomies): osseodensification (OD) significantly increases insertion/removal torque, BIC ×3 vs standard drilling, and creates compacted bone layer; OD is the foundational paper for the Densah bur technique.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: International Journal of Oral & Maxillofacial Implants 2016; doi:10.11607/jomi.4817
- **Study type**: In vitro (porcine tibial plateau bone)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Foundational paper introducing the osseodensification (OD) concept and Densah bur technique
- OD uses reversed (counterclockwise) multi-fluted burs to compact bone rather than excavate
- Significantly increased BIC: ~3× vs standard drilling
- Increased insertion and removal torques vs both standard drilling and extraction drilling
- Spring-back effect creates smaller osteotomy diameter → more bone contact

## 3. Key Results
- Insertion torque: OD > extraction drilling > standard drilling (all significantly different)
- ISQ: no significant difference among three groups
- Bone mineral density: OD group showed compacted layer at osteotomy periphery (µCT)
- BIC: OD ×3 improvement vs standard drilling (histomorphometry)
