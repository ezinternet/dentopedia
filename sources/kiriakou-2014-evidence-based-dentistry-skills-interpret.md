## One-line Summary
Open-access review (Progress in Orthodontics 2014;15:58) teaching dentists how to apply RCT and SR critical appraisal to clinical practice, anchored on the 5-step EBD framework.

## 1. Document Information
- Journal: Progress in Orthodontics 2014, 15:58
- DOI: 10.1186/s40510-014-0058-5
- Affiliations: University of Athens School of Dentistry (Greece); University of Bern (Switzerland)
- Open Access (CC-BY)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Adapts evidence-based medicine machinery to dental practice as "evidence-based dentistry" (EBD).
- Defines EBD as a three-domain integration: best available scientific evidence + clinician judgment + patient preferences.
- Operationalizes EBD into 5 steps: ask focused question → search → critically appraise → apply → audit.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Narrative review with worked appraisal frameworks for two study types:
  - RCT appraisal: randomization, allocation concealment, blinding, intention-to-treat, completeness of follow-up.
  - SR appraisal: a priori protocol, comprehensive search, study selection criteria, quality assessment, heterogeneity, publication bias.
- References CONSORT-style RCT reporting and PRISMA-style SR reporting checks.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Educational — no empirical results.
- Reproduces canonical hierarchy of evidence (SR+MA > RCT > cohort > case-control > case series > expert opinion).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Pre-PRISMA 2020 / pre-GRADE-mainstream version of EBD instruction.
- Does not cover network meta-analysis, real-world evidence, or AI-augmented appraisal.

## 6. Related Work
- Companion reading for any of the dental SR+MA pages in this wiki (the EBD framework underlies all clinical decision pages).

## 7. Glossary
- EBD: Evidence-Based Dentistry — clinical decision integration of evidence + expertise + patient values.
- CONSORT: Consolidated Standards of Reporting Trials.
- PRISMA: Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses.
