## Why Ingested

Seeds the wiki's first Ceraseal / calcium-silicate-bioceramic sealer cluster with a bench measurement of the sealer–dentin interface property (wettability/contact angle) that underpins seal quality, complementing the clinical-outcome anchor [[endodontics/spinelli-2024-three-year-single-cone-ceraseal-cohort]] (which reports Ceraseal single-cone survival but not the wetting physics). Provides the contact-angle numbers needed to reason about why epoxy-resin vs bioceramic vs silicone sealers adapt differently to radicular dentin.

## One-line Summary

In vitro contact-angle (Ossila goniometer) study on 60 NaOCl/EDTA/CHX-treated human mandibular premolar root-dentin halves (n=20/group): AH Plus wet dentin best (mean 47.40°) < Ceraseal (61.64°) < Guttaflow bioseal (95.64°, nonwetting); all pairwise differences highly significant (P<0.001).

## 2. Key Contributions

- First reported comparison of the wetting ability (contact angle) of Ceraseal (calcium-silicate bioceramic) and Guttaflow bioseal (silicone) against AH Plus (epoxy resin) on human root-canal dentin.
- Quantifies a three-tier wettability ranking driven by sealer chemistry: epoxy resin > bioceramic > silicone.
- Establishes that Guttaflow bioseal is *nonwetting* (contact angle >90°) on irrigant-treated radicular dentin, whereas AH Plus and Ceraseal are wetting (<90°).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 30 intact single-rooted human mandibular premolars (orthodontic extractions), decoronated and apically resected, split longitudinally buccolingually → 60 root-dentin halves.
- Inner dentin polished with Sof-Lex disks (to standardize roughness/surface energy), mounted on cold-cure resin blocks.
- All specimens surface-treated: 10 mL 3% NaOCl (5 min) + 10 mL 17% EDTA (5 min) + 2% CHX (10 min), then blot-dried.
- Randomized to 3 groups (n=20 each): AH Plus, Ceraseal, Guttaflow bioseal.
- 0.1 mL sealer dispensed by micropipette; contact angle measured 5 min later with an Ossila goniometer.
- One-way ANOVA + Tukey post hoc; significance P<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Group | Sealer (type) | Mean contact angle (°) | SD | SE |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | AH Plus (epoxy resin) | 47.3995 | 5.73024 | 1.28132 |
| 2 | Ceraseal (Ca-silicate bioceramic) | 61.6353 | 4.53076 | 1.01311 |
| 3 | Guttaflow bioseal (silicone) | 95.6425 | 3.89777 | 0.87157 |

- ANOVA F = 537.74, P = 0.000 (highly significant).
- Tukey pairwise mean differences (all P = 0.000): AH Plus vs Ceraseal −14.236°; AH Plus vs Guttaflow bioseal −48.243°; Ceraseal vs Guttaflow bioseal −34.007°.
- Lower contact angle = higher wettability. AH Plus < Ceraseal < Guttaflow bioseal.
- Guttaflow bioseal >90° = nonwetting; AH Plus and Ceraseal <90° = wetting.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Single time point (5 min post-dispensal); no measurement over the full setting reaction.
- No load applied during measurement — sealers may behave differently under obturation pressure.
- Influence of different irrigants/irrigation techniques on wettability not isolated (a fixed NaOCl/EDTA/CHX protocol used for all).
- Bench measurement of one interface property — does not capture in-vivo sealing, push-out bond, or clinical outcome.

## 6. Related Work

- Tummala et al. — AH Plus showed significantly lower contact angle (better wetting) than ZOE and Guttaflow bioseal.
- Tammineedi et al. — AH Plus showed lower contact angle / better wettability than MTA Fillapex.
- Mechanistic basis cited: AH Plus penetrates microirregularities and bonds chemically to collagen amino groups; Ceraseal creeps along dentin and into lateral micro-canals via small grain size and water affinity; Guttaflow's silicone/hydrophobic gutta-percha raises surface tension and resists spreading.

## 7. Glossary

- **Wettability**: ability of a liquid to spread on a solid surface; quantified by contact angle.
- **Contact angle**: angle between a sealer droplet and dentin; <90° = wetting, >90° = nonwetting, 0° = complete wetting. Lower angle = greater surface free energy = better adaptation.
- **Ceraseal**: pure calcium-silicate bioceramic sealer (zirconium oxide, calcium aluminate, tricalcium silicates) that sets by absorbing dentinal-tubule water and bonds to hydroxyapatite.
- **Guttaflow bioseal**: silicone-based injectable root-filling paste containing gutta-percha particles.
- **AH Plus**: epoxy-resin paste-to-paste sealer; reference/control sealer.
- **Goniometer (Ossila)**: instrument measuring droplet contact angle.
