## Why Ingested

고투명 지르코니아(high-translucency ZC), ZC+부분 베니어, LDS 세 가지를 동일 RCT 안에서 직접 비교한 드문 3-arm 다기관 RCT. [[dental-materials/zirconia/ban-2023-dental-zirconia-types-development-review]]의 5Y/UHTZ grade 임상 근거를 실제 RCT 3년 데이터로 보강하고, [[overviews/zirconia-types-clinical-selection]] 축 2(grade × indication)에 고투명 ZC 임상 결과를 추가한다.

## One-line Summary
Multicenter RCT (n=71, 84 crowns, 3y): high-translucency ZC, ZC+partial buccal veneer, LDS show 98.8% survival with no fractures; ZC and LDS perform equivalently in posterior single crowns.

## 한줄요약
다기관 RCT(n=71, 3년): 고투명 지르코니아·부분베니어 ZC·리튬디실리케이트 후방 단관 생존율 98.8%로 동등, 파절 없음.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Direct 3-arm RCT comparing three ceramic types in the same trial
- High-translucency zirconia confirmed as a viable posterior crown material at 3 years
- No crown fractures in any group — critical for safety validation of 5Y-type zirconia
- Patient-reported esthetics favorable across all three types; patients rated more favorably than dentists

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- n=71 patients, 90 crowns randomized to three groups:
  - **ZC**: High-translucency monolithic zirconia crown
  - **ZC-V**: High-translucency zirconia + partial buccal veneer
  - **LDS**: Lithium disilicate glass-ceramic
- Four general dentists performed all treatments (blinded to material)
- Evaluations: baseline + annual for 3 years; modified CDA criteria
- 66 patients / 84 crowns available at 3-year follow-up (7 dropouts)
- Patient-reported outcomes via questionnaire

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Survival rate**: 98.8% across all groups at 3 years
- **Fractures**: None in any group
- **Failures**: 1 ZC-V (loss of retention); 1 ZC (loss of retention)
- **Complications**: 2 ZC crowns required endodontic retreatment (biological)
- **Marginal integrity, surface, anatomical form**: No significant difference between groups
- **Esthetics ratings**: All types rated favorably; patients rated higher than dentists

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Abstract-only; detailed statistical data not available
- Short 3-year follow-up; longer data needed for fracture differentiation
- High-translucency ZC likely 5Y-PSZ grade, but grade not specified in abstract
- Biological complications (endo retreatment) in ZC group warrants investigation
