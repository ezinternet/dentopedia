## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 10 clinical studies (PROSPERO CRD42021284190) on implant-supported overdentures (IODs) retained by CAD-CAM milled bars: ~100% implant survival across Ti/PEEK/BioHPP/zirconia bars, CAD-CAM milled titanium bars showed minimal complications (pooled bar-level complication 7.4%, biological 2.8%, MBL 0.324 mm) and best OHIP/satisfaction, while polymer (PEEK/BioHPP) bars carried peri-implantitis signal.

## 한줄요약

임플란트 지지 오버덴쳐를 CAD-CAM 밀링 bar로 유지한 임상연구 10편 SR+MA: 모든 bar 재료(티타늄/PEEK/BioHPP/지르코니아)에서 임플란트 생존율 ~100%, 밀링 티타늄 bar가 합병증 최소(bar 합병증 7.4%, 생물학적 2.8%, MBL 0.324 mm)·OHIP/만족도 최상이고, 폴리머(PEEK/BioHPP) bar는 임플란트주위염 신호를 보임.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA focused specifically on **CAD-CAM milled bars** (not attachments) for implant-supported overdentures, separating clinical performance by **bar material**: milled titanium, PEEK, BioHPP (PEEK-based high-performance polymer), zirconia.
- Quantifies pooled event rates for milled titanium bars: mechanical/bar-level complications, biological complications, and marginal bone loss.
- Direct head-to-head meta-analysis of CAD-CAM milled titanium bars vs conventional soldered gold bars (no significant difference in complications).
- Surfaces a **material-dependent biological signal**: peri-implantitis reported with polymer (PEEK/BioHPP) bars but not titanium or zirconia bars.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **PICO**: edentulous patients with IODs (population); IODs retained by CAD-CAM printed/milled bars (intervention); conventional-method IODs and CAD-CAM prostheses with baseline (comparison); survival, MBL, biological/prosthodontic complications, OHIP, plaque/bleeding, satisfaction (outcomes).
- **Databases**: MEDLINE, PubMed, Cochrane Library, SciELO, Web of Science (to May 2023). Selection kappa k=1.0.
- **Quality/bias**: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS scores 6–9), ROBINS-I (nonrandomized), RoB tool (RCTs), GRADE (very low to moderate; technical complications and MBL rated low).
- **Statistics**: random-effects models, Comprehensive Meta-Analysis v3.0; heterogeneity by Q and I². Significance p<0.05.
- **Selection flow**: 127 references → 91 de-duplicated → 13 full-text → **10 included studies**.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Included studies (n=10)** spanned RCT (Srinivasan 2020), pseudorandomized (Altonbary & Emera), prospective (Katsoulis 2011; Toia 2019; Pozzi 2016; Mangano 2019; Zuercher 2022; Al-Asad 2023), and retrospective (Katsoulis 2015; Cordaro 2013); mean ages ~55–75y; follow-up 3 months to 4 years.

**Implant survival**: nine studies reported **100% implant survival** across all CAD-CAM milled bar types (titanium, PEEK, BioHPP, zirconia), follow-up up to 4 years.

**Meta-analysis — complications, milled Ti (n=113) vs conventional gold bar (n=128)**: 9 vs 13 complications; **RR=0.638 (95% CI 0.108–3.755; p=0.619)** — no significant difference; Q=1.804 (p=0.179), I²=44.561.

**Meta-analysis — bar-level (mechanical) complication event rate, milled Ti**: 4 studies, 171 IODs, 11 complications; rates 4.2–12.6%; **pooled 7.4% (95% CI 4.2–12.6)**; Q=1469 (p=0.689), I²=0.0.

**Meta-analysis — biological complication rate, milled Ti**: 3 studies, 159 IODs, 4 events; rates 1.1–6.9%; **pooled 2.8% (95% CI 1.1–6.9)**; Q=0.026 (p=0.987), I²=0.0.

**Meta-analysis — peri-implant MBL (Ti or zirconia bars)**: 4 studies, **324 implants**, follow-up 12–24 months; per-study mean rate 0.061–0.587; **pooled 0.324 mm (95% CI 0.061–0.587)**; high heterogeneity Q=120.308 (p=0.0), **I²=97.506**. Zirconia ≈ titanium ≈ conventional; bone loss independent of bar metal. Per-study MBL: Pozzi 2016 Ti 0.64±0.21 mm (n=72); Toia 2019 Ti 0.27±0.35 mm (n=185); Zuercher 2022 zirconia 0.20±0.67 mm (n=30); Srinivasan 2020 Ti+gold clip 0.137±0.671 mm (n=38).

**Material-specific findings**:
- **Titanium (milled)**: lowest complication rates; OHIP improved as early as week 2; highest patient/practitioner satisfaction.
- **Zirconia**: no bar/prosthesis fractures; no plaque/bleeding (Zuercher); minor sore spots and lingual brown discoloration in some; higher patient satisfaction vs Co-Cr (appearance/comfort/hygiene). (Abstract notes zirconia higher plaque/bleeding indices than Ti.)
- **PEEK**: no fractures, but 3/15 bars lacked passive fit (intraoral scanning); peri-implantitis (2 fixtures, one patient).
- **BioHPP**: 2 prosthesis fractures; peri-implantitis in 2 bar patients; no significant difference vs BioHPP fixed hybrid (p=0.531).

**OHIP**: 4 studies — all significant OHRQoL improvement with milled Ti bars (implant-fixed prosthesis still scored lower/better in Katsoulis 2011). **Satisfaction**: 5 studies — high patient and prosthodontist satisfaction with milled Ti; Locator favored for hygiene ease in one study.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Short follow-up across cohorts (mostly 1–2y, max 4y); scarcity of RCTs/prospective trials directly comparing CAD-CAM vs conventional IODs.
- Very high heterogeneity (I²≈97.5) in the MBL meta-analysis — pooled 0.324 mm should be read cautiously (baseline bone-loss subgroups, surgical technique, learning curve).
- GRADE evidence very low to moderate; polymer-bar (PEEK/BioHPP) peri-implantitis signal needs longer-term confirmation (microbial roughness over time).
- Authors call for long-term randomized/prospective studies on PEEK, BioHPP, zirconia bars.

## 7. Glossary

- **IOD**: Implant-supported (implant-retained) overdenture.
- **CAD-CAM milled bar**: Bar machined from a single block (metal/polymer), eliminating soldering — better passive fit and mechanical stability vs soldered gold bars.
- **BioHPP**: High-Performance Polymer based on PEEK (polyether-ether-ketone).
- **OHIP**: Oral Health Impact Profile — OHRQoL questionnaire.
- **MBL**: Marginal bone loss (peri-implant).
- **RR**: Risk ratio. **NOS**: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale.
