## Why Ingested

Head-to-head animal histomorphometry showing "anorganic bovine xenografts" are NOT interchangeable — manufacturing differences produce large efficacy gaps (Bio-Oss/Bone-Fill > Gen-Ox), a product-variability caveat for [[overviews/dbbm-bone-substitute-overview]]. Provides absolute new-bone benchmarks usable across DBBM comparisons.

## One-line Summary
Rabbit cranial-vault titanium-cylinder model (n=8, 8/12 wk) head-to-head comparing three anorganic bovine xenografts: Bone-Fill and Bio-Oss were comparable and both clearly superior to Gen-Ox and blood clot in new bone formation.

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Journal of Maxillofacial and Oral Surgery 2014;13(4):464–470 (received Apr 2013, e-pub Aug 2013).
- **Study type**: In-vivo experimental, animal (rabbit, n=8 New Zealand whites), 4-arm parallel within-animal design.
- **Setting**: Brazilian implantology research — comparing two domestic (Brazilian) DBBM products against Bio-Oss as gold standard.

## 2. Key Contributions
- First direct head-to-head histomorphometric comparison of Bone-Fill and Gen-Ox (Brazilian DBBM products) against Bio-Oss using a standardized cylinder model.
- Demonstrates that not all "anorganic bovine xenografts" are equivalent — manufacturing/processing differences produce large efficacy gaps.
- Provides absolute mm² new bone values usable as benchmarks for other DBBM comparisons.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Eight New Zealand rabbits, four titanium cylinders implanted on cranial vault per animal; each cylinder randomly filled with Bio-Oss, Bone-Fill, Gen-Ox anorganic, or blood clot only.
- Sacrifice at 8 weeks (n=4) and 12 weeks (n=4).
- Cylinder contents removed with 4.3 mm trephine including adjacent cranial vault bone, decalcified, H&E stained, evaluated by optical microscopy.
- Histomorphometry at UNICAMP: new bone area, residual particles, non-mineralized tissue. Z-test for two means.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **New bone (mm²) at 8 weeks**: Bio-Oss 8.43, Bone-Fill 7.24, Gen-Ox 2.78, blood clot 0.65.
- **New bone (mm²) at 12 weeks**: Bio-Oss 9.32, Bone-Fill 9.01, Gen-Ox 3.02, blood clot 0.63.
- Bone-Fill closed the small gap with Bio-Oss between 8 and 12 weeks (osteoconductive maturation).
- Gen-Ox showed scant new bone, large residual material throughout the cylinder, abundant collagen fibers — clinically inferior.
- Blood clot alone formed only primary woven bone, no significant maturation by 12 weeks.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Animal cranial-vault cylinder model — not a loaded jaw site; cannot translate directly to socket or sinus.
- Small n (4 per timepoint); single histomorphometric sample per material per animal.
- No mechanical/biomechanical testing; no longer-term remodeling data.
- Decalcified histology limits residual particle quantification precision.

## 6. Related Work
- Sartori 2003 — Bio-Oss 10-year human histomorphometric trajectory (sinus).
- Mordenfeld 2010 — DPBB particle stability at 11 years in humans.
- Bassir 2018 — ARP meta-analysis: grafting material type is a significant outcome modifier.

## 7. Glossary
- **DBBM (Deproteinized Bovine Bone Mineral)**: Anorganic bovine bone graft; Bio-Oss is the prototypical product. Osteoconductive scaffold with slow resorption.
- **Bone-Fill, Gen-Ox**: Brazilian anorganic bovine xenograft products positioned as Bio-Oss alternatives.
- **Histomorphometry**: Quantitative measurement of bone tissue components (new bone, residual graft, non-mineralized tissue) on histologic sections.
