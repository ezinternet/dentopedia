## Why Ingested

[[wiki/endodontics/irrigation/do-2020-eryag-laser-pips-endodontic-irrigation-review]] established Er:YAG/PIPS laser-activated irrigation as a mechanism to enhance smear-layer and biofilm removal; this newer 15-study SR (2015–2025) extends that evidence base with SWEEPS and adds the clinically attractive finding that laser activation enables **lower NaOCl concentrations** without losing efficacy. Tempering the enthusiasm, the protocol/parameter heterogeneity it documents echoes [[wiki/endodontics/irrigation/boutsioukis-2022-irrigants-irrigation-methods-status-directions]]'s caution that no activation method is yet proven to improve clinical outcomes.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (15 studies, 2015–2025) finding Er:YAG laser-activated irrigation (PIPS/SWEEPS) consistently improved root canal disinfection, biofilm disruption, and smear-layer removal over conventional and ultrasonic methods, and enabled lower NaOCl concentrations — though heterogeneous protocols limit pooling and clinical generalization.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Systematic review (PRISMA 2020; PROSPERO CRD42025638319), no meta-analysis
- **Journal**: Dentistry Journal (Dent. J.) 2025, 13, 101 (MDPI)
- **Authors**: Jakub Fiegler-Rudol et al. — Medical University of Silesia & Wroclaw Medical University, Poland
- **Databases**: PubMed/MEDLINE, Embase, Scopus, Cochrane Library; English, 1 Jan 2015 – 3 Jan 2025
- **Included studies**: 15 articles (antibacterial efficacy, biofilm disruption, or smear-layer removal)
- **Risk of bias**: 10-item checklist based on Cochrane Handbook guidelines

## 2. Key Contributions

- Synthesizes the Er:YAG laser-activated irrigation (LAI) evidence centered on **PIPS** (Photon-Induced Photoacoustic Streaming) and **SWEEPS** (Shock Wave-Enhanced Emission Photoacoustic Streaming).
- All 15 studies reported Er:YAG activation **significantly improved disinfection** over conventional or ultrasonic methods, with superior **apical** bacterial reduction.
- Notable clinical lever: laser activation enabled **lower NaOCl concentrations** without sacrificing efficacy, and some studies reported **reduced post-operative discomfort**.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- PICO: P = patients undergoing root canal therapy; I = Er:YAG laser technology; C = conventional disinfection; O = disinfection effectiveness.
- Er:YAG at 2940 nm — wavelength strongly absorbed by water → photoacoustic streaming/cavitation with **lower thermal risk** than diode/Nd:YAG (shorter penetration depth).
- Two reviewers screened title/abstract/full text; reference lists hand-searched.
- Outcomes extracted: laser settings (pulse duration, frequency, energy), irrigants, antibacterial/biofilm/smear-layer outcomes.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Consistent superiority**: every included study found Er:YAG (PIPS/SWEEPS) improved bacterial reduction vs conventional or ultrasonic activation, especially apically and in dentinal tubules.
- **Mandras 2020**: PIPS + 5% NaOCl/10% EDTA enhanced bacterial-load reduction and reduced post-op discomfort vs steel-needle irrigation (not all strain differences statistically significant).
- **Yavagal 2021** (primary teeth): laser-activated NaOCl completely eliminated E. faecalis, outperforming syringe irrigation.
- **Neelakantan 2015**: Er:YAG activation destroyed mature E. faecalis biofilm and improved tubule disinfection vs syringe/ultrasonic; NaOCl+etidronic acid or NaOCl-EDTA-NaOCl most effective.
- **Shan 2022**: Er:YAG and ultrasonic both significantly better than conventional irrigation; efficacy similar regardless of minimally-invasive vs conventional access.
- **Rahmati 2022** (regenerative): Er:YAG, EDTA, QMix supported SCAP adhesion similarly; MTAD impaired it.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Substantial heterogeneity in laser parameters (energy, frequency, pulse duration, tip placement), irrigants, study designs, and outcome measures → no meta-analysis, limited direct comparison.
- Mix of in-vitro and limited clinical studies; few long-term clinical trials.
- Calls for standardized protocols and long-term RCTs before firm clinical guidelines.

## 7. Glossary

- **Er:YAG laser**: Erbium:Yttrium-Aluminum-Garnet, 2940 nm; highly water-absorbed → strong photoacoustic streaming with low thermal penetration.
- **PIPS (Photon-Induced Photoacoustic Streaming)**: Low-energy Er:YAG pulses in the pulp chamber generating shockwaves/cavitation that propel irrigant 3-dimensionally through the canal system.
- **SWEEPS (Shock Wave-Enhanced Emission Photoacoustic Streaming)**: Evolution of PIPS using paired pulses to amplify cavitation shockwaves.
- **LAI (Laser-Activated Irrigation)**: Activation of irrigants by laser-induced cavitation/streaming.
- **Smear layer**: Debris/organic layer on instrumented dentin walls that impedes irrigant penetration into tubules.
