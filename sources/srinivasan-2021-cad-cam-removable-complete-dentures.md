## Why Ingested

CAD-CAM 기반 총의치(디지털 제작) 비교 근거가 wiki에 없어 공백을 채우기 위해 수집. 기존 [[complete-denture/alhajj-2017-occlusal-vertical-dimension-complete-denture-review]]가 다루는 수직교합고경(VDO) 결정과 함께, 실제 의치 제작 방식(디지털 vs 전통)의 임상·기계적 특성을 비교한 SR+MA로 complete-denture 카테고리의 디지털 워크플로우 축을 보강한다.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA (73 studies) comparing CAD-CAM (milled/3D-printed) vs conventional complete dentures: milled CDs showed superior flexural strength, surface roughness, and toughness; CAD-CAM CDs had better retention and lower chairside time/cost.

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (73개 연구): 밀링 CAD-CAM 총의치는 굴곡강도·표면조도·인성에서 우수하고, CAD-CAM 총의치 전반이 기존 방식보다 유지력이 높으며 의자 시간과 비용이 적게 든다.

## 1. Document Information

- **Journal**: Journal of Dentistry
- **Volume/Issue**: 113 (2021), 103777
- **Publication date**: Available online 13 August 2021
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.jdent.2021.103777
- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis
- **PICO question**: In completely edentulous patients, are CAD-CAM removable complete dentures (CDs) inferior to conventional CDs with respect to trueness of fit, biocompatibility, mechanical properties, surface characteristics, color stability, time-cost efficiency, clinical and patient-reported outcomes?
- **Inter-rater reliability**: Kappa 0.897–1.000

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First comprehensive SR+MA combining multiple outcome domains (mechanical, surface, clinical, PROs, cost-time) for CAD-CAM complete dentures.
2. Meta-analysis demonstrates 3D-printed CDs are superior in trueness of fit vs conventional (p = 0.039).
3. Milled CDs have statistically superior flexural strength vs conventional and 3D-printed (p < 0.0001); also superior flexural modulus, yield strength, toughness, and surface roughness.
4. CAD-CAM CDs (pooled) demonstrate better retention vs conventional (p = 0.015).
5. CAD-CAM fabrication requires less chairside time (p = 0.037) and lower overall costs (p < 0.0001).
6. Conventional CDs had higher strain at yield point than milled (p < 0.0001) and superior esthetics vs 3D-printed (p < 0.0001).
7. Rapidly-prototyped CDs showed inferior color stability (p = 0.029).

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search databases**: PubMed (MEDLINE), Embase, CENTRAL (Cochrane); supplemented by Google, ResearchGate, hand searches.
- **Search date**: Final search 15 March 2021.
- **Inclusion criteria**: Studies on CAD-CAM CDs (milled or 3D-printed) reporting any of the specified outcomes; all study designs included.
- **Exclusion criteria**: Fixed prostheses, partial removable dental prostheses, software/FEA studies, review articles.
- **Number of included studies**: 73
- **Fabrication methods compared**: Conventional (heat-polymerized, injection-molded), milled (subtractive CAD-CAM), 3D-printed (additive — SLA, DLP, FDM, PolyJet/rapid prototyping).
- **Outcomes assessed**: Trueness of fit (surface deviation), biocompatibility (cytotoxicity, residual monomer), mechanical properties (flexural strength/modulus, yield strength, toughness, hardness, strain), surface properties (roughness, contact angle, staining), color stability, time-cost, retention, clinical outcomes (denture quality assessments), patient-reported outcomes (OHRQoL, satisfaction).
- **Statistical analysis**: Random-effects meta-analyses with subgroup analyses by fabrication method; heterogeneity reported.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

### Trueness of Fit
- 3D-printed > conventional (p = 0.039) in trueness.
- Optimal 3D-print orientation: 45° build angle for mandibular dentures.
- Post-polymerization conditions: 30 min at 40°C optimal for clear resin.

### Mechanical Properties
- **Flexural strength**: Milled > conventional (p < 0.0001); milled > 3D-printed (p < 0.0001).
- **Flexural modulus**: Milled > 3D-printed (p < 0.0001).
- **Yield strength**: Milled > injection-molded (p = 0.004); milled > 3D-printed (p = 0.001).
- **Toughness**: Milled superior (p < 0.0001).
- **Strain at yield**: Conventional > milled (p < 0.0001) — conventional CDs are more ductile/flexible.

### Surface Characteristics
- **Surface roughness**: Milled superior to conventional and 3D-printed (p < 0.0001).
- **Color stability**: Rapidly-prototyped inferior (p = 0.029).
- **Esthetics**: Conventional > 3D-printed (p < 0.0001).

### Clinical Outcomes
- **Retention**: CAD-CAM > conventional (p = 0.015).

### Time-Cost Analysis
- **Chairside time**: CAD-CAM requires less (p = 0.037).
- **Overall cost**: CAD-CAM lower (p < 0.0001).

### Patient-Reported Outcomes
- OHRQoL and patient satisfaction showed no significant inferiority for CAD-CAM CDs vs conventional in clinical studies.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- High heterogeneity across included studies due to variety of CAD-CAM systems, materials, and fabrication protocols.
- Many in-vitro studies included; limited RCT data for clinical and PRO outcomes.
- Long-term clinical data (≥5 years) lacking for CAD-CAM CDs.
- Rapid evolution of 3D-printing materials and technologies means some findings may be quickly outdated.
- Biocompatibility data limited; residual monomer data for 3D-printed CDs underreported.
- Need for standardized testing protocols across studies.
- Future research should focus on long-term RCTs and geriatric patient populations with limited access to care.

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| CAD-CAM | Computer-Aided Design / Computer-Aided Manufacturing |
| Milled CD | Subtractive CAD-CAM: block of pre-polymerized PMMA milled by CNC |
| 3D-printed CD | Additive manufacturing: layer-by-layer resin polymerization (SLA, DLP, FDM, PolyJet) |
| Trueness of fit | Closeness of fabricated denture to the digital design (surface deviation, mm) |
| Flexural strength | Maximum stress before fracture under bending load (MPa) |
| Flexural modulus | Stiffness under bending load (GPa) |
| Yield strength | Stress at onset of permanent deformation (MPa) |
| Toughness | Energy absorbed before fracture (J/m² or MPa·√m) |
| Surface roughness | Ra value — average deviation from mean surface plane (μm) |
| OHRQoL | Oral Health-Related Quality of Life |
| Rapidly-prototyped | PolyJet/inkjet 3D-printing technologies used in earlier generation systems |
| SLA | Stereolithography Apparatus (photopolymerization 3D printing) |
| DLP | Digital Light Processing (photopolymerization 3D printing) |
