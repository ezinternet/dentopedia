## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: International Journal of Oral Science 2021, 13:20
- **DOI**: 10.1038/s41368-021-00125-5

- **Study type**: Narrative review (structured literature selection: 6,808 → 170 studies)
- **Institution**: West China Hospital of Stomatology, Sichuan University

## 2. Key Contributions
- Proposes new hypothesis: PDL cells (PDLCs) and osteocytes serve primary mechanosensing roles in OTM — not previously emphasized at this level
- Organizes OTM mechanisms into 4 sequential steps:
  1. **Extracellular mechanobiology**: biological, mechanical, material changes in acellular periodontium components under force
  2. **Cell strain**: sensing, transduction, and regulation of mechanical stimuli in PDLCs and osteocytes
  3. **Cell activation/differentiation**: osteoblast/osteoclast activation; force-induced sterile inflammation; communication networks (sensors ↔ effectors)
  4. **Tissue remodeling**: bone and PDL remodeling on compression vs tension sides; root resorption
- Clinical implications: optimal orthodontic force (OOF), OTM acceleration strategies, root resorption prevention
- Research models categorized: in vivo (rat most common), in vitro, analytical (FEM)

## 3. Methodology
- Initial search: 6,808 papers; 2,863 after duplicate removal; 170 final after quality/relevance filtering
- Focus on studies published in the 2010s (new decade update)
- Multi-model synthesis: in vivo animal, in vitro cell culture, FEM analytical models

## 4. Key Results
| Step | Key Mechanisms |
|---|---|
| Extracellular mechanobiology | ECM remodeling; collagen fiber tension/compression; fluid dynamics in PDL |
| Cell strain (sensing) | PDLCs + osteocytes as primary mechanosensors; cilia, integrins, ion channels |
| Cell activation | RANKL↑ compression → osteoclastogenesis; Runx2↑ tension → osteogenesis; sterile inflammation (TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-6, PGE2) |
| Tissue remodeling | Compression: bone resorption + PDL compression; Tension: new bone + PDL widening; Root resorption risk with excessive force |

- Optimal orthodontic force: sufficient to activate remodeling without causing sustained hyalinization or root resorption
- Acceleration methods: corticotomy, LIPUS, vibration, pharmacological RANKL modulation
- Root resorption prevention: avoiding heavy/continuous forces; monitoring with CBCT

## 5. Limitations
- Narrative structure despite systematic literature selection; no formal risk of bias
- Animal model findings may not translate directly to human periodontium
- FEM models require accurate PDL material property inputs which vary across studies
