## Why Ingested

This SR+MA (Kumar 2026, 23 economic evaluations) pools the **cost-effectiveness** (incremental net monetary benefit, INMB) of professional fluoride varnish (FV) for childhood caries prevention vs other preventive measures, finding NO significant economic advantage of FV over comparators in high-income countries (total INMB $124.1, I²=0%). It adds the economic-evidence layer to [[wiki/caries/chestnutt-2017-fissure-seal-fluoride-varnish-trial]], whose RCT found FV clinically equivalent to fissure sealant yet "cost-effective in a program" — Kumar's pooled economic finding qualifies that single-trial cost claim as uncertain. It also complements the clinical FV/varnish-composition material in [[wiki/caries/jkda-2023-61-7-004]]. NOTE: abstract-only — full text not retrieved (no PMC OA).

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 23 cost-effectiveness studies (836 screened): pooled incremental net monetary benefit (INMB) of fluoride varnish vs other caries-preventive interventions in children was $124.1 (I²=0%), showing NO significant economic difference — cost-effectiveness of FV remains uncertain in high-income countries from the healthcare provider/payer perspective.

## 한줄요약

23개 경제성평가 연구 SR+MA (836편 스크리닝): 소아 우식예방에서 불소바니시(FV) vs 다른 예방중재의 통합 증분순화폐편익(INMB)이 $124.1 (I²=0%)로 비용효과 차이 없음 — 고소득국 의료공급자/지불자 관점에서 FV의 비용효과는 불확실. (임상효능이 아닌 경제성 분석)

## 1. Document Information

- Type: Systematic review + meta-analysis of health-economic (cost-effectiveness) studies
- Registration: PROSPERO CRD42023483985
- Databases: PubMed, Web of Science, Scopus, Cochrane — searched to January 2024
- Risk of bias: modified Economic Evaluations Bias (ECOBIAS) checklist; two independent reviewers
- Funding: none
- Source artifact: abstract-only (full text not retrieved; no PMC open access)

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA to pool the **economic value** (not clinical efficacy) of fluoride varnish for childhood caries prevention using incremental net monetary benefit (INMB).
- Pooled total INMB of FV vs other interventions = **$124.1** (95% CI crossing zero; I²=0%) → no statistically significant cost-effectiveness difference.
- Maps the comparator landscape: FV studied against oral health education (OHE), treatment as usual (TAU), pit-and-fissure sealant, toothbrushing, and no intervention.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- 836 studies screened → **23 included** economic evaluations.
- Comparators: OHE (n=5), TAU (n=3), pit-and-fissure sealant (n=5), toothbrushing (n=5), no intervention (n=7).
- Pooling restricted to **model-based FV studies in high-income countries (HICs)**, from a **healthcare provider/payer perspective**, vs OHE / TAU / no intervention. (Sealant and toothbrushing comparisons, non-model-based or non-HIC studies were not pooled.)
- Outcome: total incremental net monetary benefit (INMB).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Overall pooled FV vs other interventions: **total INMB $124.1** (95% CI: −1.3×10, 1.3×10 as reported; CI crosses zero), **I²=0%** → no significant difference in cost-effectiveness.
- Interpretation: cost-effectiveness of FV is **uncertain** in HICs from the provider/payer perspective.
- The authors note potential economic value of FV especially in **settings lacking robust local cost-effectiveness data**.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Abstract-only ingest — full methods/results not retrieved.
- Limited and heterogeneous economic evidence; pooling restricted to a narrow subset (model-based, HIC, payer perspective, vs OHE/TAU/no intervention).
- Reported CI is very wide despite I²=0%, reflecting sparse and imprecise economic data.
- Findings are about **economic cost-effectiveness, NOT clinical caries-prevention efficacy** — FV clinical efficacy is established elsewhere and is not contradicted here.

## 7. Glossary

- **INMB (Incremental Net Monetary Benefit)** — economic metric converting health gains and costs to a single monetary value; positive favors the intervention, CI crossing zero = no significant difference.
- **HIC** — high-income country.
- **OHE / TAU** — oral health education / treatment as usual.
- **ECOBIAS** — Economic Evaluations Bias checklist (risk-of-bias tool for economic evaluations).
