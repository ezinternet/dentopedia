## Why Ingested

라미네이트 비니어 절단연 피개(incisal coverage) 여부가 생존율에 미치는 영향에 대한 임상 근거가 없어 수집. [[veneers/morimoto-2016-feldspathic-glass-ceramic-veneer-survival-sr-ma]]에서 절단연 피개 OR 1.25 (NS) 결과를 보고했으나, 이 Albanesi 2016은 동일 주제에 특화된 SR+MA로 준비 디자인 선택 근거를 더 구체적으로 제시함.

## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 8 clinical studies (n=1145 screened): incisal coverage vs non-coverage showed 88% vs 91% survival, OR 1.25 (non-significant); both preparation designs are safe for ceramic laminate veneers.

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (8편): 절단연 피개 비니어 생존율 88% vs 미피개 91%, OR 1.25 (비유의), 두 준비 디자인 모두 안전하고 효과적.

- **Title**: Incisal coverage or not in ceramic laminate veneers: A systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Authors**: Rafael Borges Albanesi, Mônica Nogueira Pigozzo, Newton Sesma, Dalva Cruz Laganá, Susana Morimoto
- **Year**: 2016
- **Journal**: Journal of Dentistry, Vol. 52, pp. 1–7 (September 2016)
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.jdent.2016.06.004
- **PROSPERO**: CRD42015016606
- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis

## 2. Key Contributions

- First SR+MA focused exclusively on incisal coverage as the primary clinical question for ceramic veneer preparation design
- Demonstrated that incisal coverage does not significantly affect survival rates of ceramic laminate veneers
- Evaluated PRISMA-compliant systematic search across MEDLINE (PubMed) and Cochrane databases from 1977 to June 2016
- Identified a gap in RCT evidence on preparation design comparison
- Concluded both preparation designs (with and without incisal coverage) are safe and effective conservative treatment options

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search databases**: MEDLINE (PubMed) and Cochrane Central Register of Controlled Trials
- **Search period**: 1977 to June 5, 2016
- **Language restrictions**: None
- **Initial articles**: 1145 screened
- **Included studies**: 8 clinical studies (RCTs and cohort studies)
- **Risk of bias tool**: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS)
- **PICOS framework**:
  - P = patients receiving laminate veneers
  - I = ceramic veneers
  - C = not applicable (no direct comparison)
  - O = survival rate
  - S = RCTs and cohort studies
- **Statistical methods**: Cochran Q test and I² for heterogeneity; metaregression, meta-analysis
- **Preparation design types**: Feathered incisal edge (window), butt joint/incisal bevel, palatal chamfer; prepless veneers with increased incisal coverage also considered
- Note: No studies on crystalline ceramic veneers were identified that met inclusion criteria

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

| Preparation Design | Estimated Survival Rate | OR vs No Coverage |
|---|---|---|
| With incisal coverage | 88% | 1.25 |
| Without incisal coverage | 91% | Reference |

- OR 1.25 for incisal coverage: not statistically significant
- No randomized controlled studies directly comparing incisal coverage vs non-coverage designs were identified
- Significant differences in dental structure removal between preparation designs noted by referenced studies
- Ceramic veneers remove approximately 3%–30% by weight of coronal tooth structure (least invasive designs)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 8 studies included due to strict preparation-design reporting requirements
- No RCTs directly comparing incisal vs non-incisal coverage identified
- No studies for crystalline ceramic veneers meeting inclusion criteria
- Wide variation in preparation description across included studies
- Authors strongly recommended future RCTs with standardized description of preparation designs
- Retrospective studies included to increase sample size, which introduces potential bias

## 6. Related Work

- Companion paper: Morimoto et al. 2016 (IJP) — broader SR+MA on all ceramic veneer outcomes including incisal coverage as one variable
- Prior SR by Peumans et al. addressed preparation design but included only laboratory studies
- Preceded by narrative reviews (Gresnigt et al.) noting lack of clinical evidence on incisal design
- Followed by Klein et al. 2025 JERD which also recorded preparation designs but did not specifically meta-analyze incisal coverage effect

## 7. Glossary

- **Incisal coverage**: Preparation design where the ceramic veneer extends over and covers the incisal edge
- **Feathered/window preparation**: Preparation ending at or near the incisal edge without extending to the palatal surface
- **Butt joint**: Preparation where the incisal edge is reduced to provide a 90° margin at the incisal
- **Palatal chamfer**: Preparation extending to the palatal surface with a chamfer finishing line
- **Prepless veneer**: Veneer placed with minimal or no tooth preparation; may or may not have incisal coverage
- **PROSPERO**: International Prospective Register of Systematic Reviews (National Health Institute for Research)
- **Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS)**: Tool for assessing risk of bias in observational studies
