## Why Ingested

기존 [[endodontics/irrigation/]] 논문들은 sonic/ultrasonic activation (Passive Ultrasonic Irrigation, PUI / Apical Negative Pressure, ANP) 중심; 레이저 활성화 세정(Laser-Activated Irrigation, LAI)의 umbrella SR이 없었음. 본 논문은 9개의 SR을 통합한 umbrella review로, Er:YAG, Er,Cr:YSGG, Nd:YAG, diode 레이저를 근관 세정 보조제로 임상적으로 평가한 첫 종합 합성 근거다.

## One-line Summary

Umbrella SR of 9 systematic reviews (through Dec 2024) on laser-activated irrigation in endodontics: erbium lasers (Er:YAG, Er,Cr:YSGG) showed the most consistent benefit for canal decontamination, diode lasers for postoperative pain reduction, Nd:YAG lasers carry microcrack risk, and overall evidence quality was predominantly low to critically low by AMSTAR 2.

## 한줄요약

9개 체계적 문헌고찰을 통합한 Umbrella SR: 에르븀 레이저(Er:YAG, Er,Cr:YSGG)가 근관 오염 제거에 가장 일관된 효과, 다이오드 레이저는 술후 통증 감소에 유효, Nd:YAG 레이저는 상아질 미세균열 위험, 전반적 근거 질은 AMSTAR 2 기준 낮음~매우 낮음.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Umbrella review of systematic reviews
- **PROSPERO**: CRD42025599352
- **Databases searched**: PubMed/MEDLINE, Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews, SciELO, Web of Science, LILACS (inception through December 2024)
- **Included reviews**: 9 systematic reviews (from 319 initially identified titles; 22 full texts screened; 13 excluded)
- **Quality assessment tool**: AMSTAR 2 (16-item tool)
- **Interexaminer agreement**: κ = 1.00 (full-text screening); κ = 0.78 (risk of bias)
- **Language restrictions**: English and Portuguese
- **Journal**: International Dental Journal

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First umbrella review synthesising the clinical evidence base for laser-activated irrigation (LAI) across all major laser types used in endodontics.
2. Demonstrates that erbium-based lasers (Er:YAG 2940 nm; Er,Cr:YSGG 2780 nm) have the most biologically consistent mechanism for irrigant activation — photoacoustic and cavitation effects that enhance penetration into lateral canals, isthmuses, and apical ramifications.
3. Differentiates clinical roles by laser type: erbium (canal disinfection/decontamination), diode (postoperative pain via photobiomodulation), Nd:YAG (deep dentinal tubule penetration, but microcrack risk).
4. Critically documents that only 1 of 9 included SRs reached high AMSTAR 2 quality; 4 were critically low — the field suffers from rapid adoption without methodological standardisation.
5. Identifies that ~60% of canal surface remains uninstrumented by rotary files, providing the mechanical rationale for any activation modality.
6. Notes laser-induced microcrack formation is parameter-dependent and not consistently worse than ultrasonic instruments.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

**Design**: Umbrella review (synthesis of systematic reviews with/without meta-analysis), PRISMA 2020 compliant.

**Search**: 5 databases, inception–December 2024, English and Portuguese, Boolean operators using "endodontic treatment," "root canal treatment," "laser therapy," "laser."

**Screening**: Dual-blinded independent review in Rayyan platform; disagreements resolved by consensus; reference list hand-search.

**Inclusion**: Systematic reviews of clinical studies evaluating laser use in endodontic treatment. Exclusion: exclusively in vitro studies; limited clinical relevance.

**Quality assessment**: AMSTAR 2. Rating distribution across 9 included SRs:
- High: 1
- Moderate: 2
- Low: 2
- Critically low: 4

**Outcomes evaluated per SR**:
- Microbial reduction / canal disinfection
- Periapical lesion healing (radiographic)
- Postoperative pain (VAS or equivalent)
- Dentinal microcrack formation
- Pulpotomy outcomes in primary teeth (1 SR)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Erbium lasers (Er:YAG 2940 nm; Er,Cr:YSGG 2780 nm)**:
- Most consistent benefits; mechanism = photoacoustic + cavitation effects from strong absorption by water and hydroxyapatite.
- Improved microbial reduction and radiographic periapical healing reported across multiple SRs.
- Performance not consistently superior to passive ultrasonic irrigation (PUI) — may be an alternative, not a clearly better option.
- Er,Cr:YSGG assessed in both dry and liquid-filled canals; periapical lesion reduction demonstrated radiographically.

**Diode lasers (810–980 nm)**:
- Primary clinical value: postoperative pain reduction in the first 48–72 hours.
- Mechanism: photobiomodulation → reduced proinflammatory mediators, modulated neural transmission.
- Limited efficacy for irrigant activation, smear layer removal, biofilm disruption.
- Evidence weak (small samples, variable pain assessment methods, short follow-up).

**Nd:YAG laser (1064 nm)**:
- Can penetrate dentinal tubules → bacterial reduction beyond reach of irrigants.
- Koba et al (dry canals): pain reduction + periapical healing (radiographic).
- Verma et al (liquid-filled canals): periapical healing (CBCT).
- Key safety concern: microcrack formation at high-energy/continuous-emission settings.
- Risk-benefit balance uncertain without standardised irradiation parameters.

**Dentinal microcrack formation**:
- Most frequently associated with Nd:YAG (also CO2 in continuous mode).
- Laser-induced cracks are parameter-dependent; not consistently worse than ultrasonic instruments.
- Critical methodological gap: only 9 of 22 primary studies in one SR assessed baseline cracks before intervention.
- Mitigation: proper dentin cooling and correct irradiation parameters.

**Laser-assisted pulpotomy (primary teeth)**:
- 1 SR included; 7 studies; patient age 4.5–7.9 years.
- Laser types: Nd:YAG (3 studies), diode 632 nm (1), diode 980 nm (1), Er:YAG (1), CO2 (1).
- Most on asymptomatic vital pulps; 1 study included symptomatic vital pulpitis.
- No definitive conclusions: heterogeneity in laser types, pulp dressings, follow-up periods.

**Overall evidence verdict**: Limited and heterogeneous. Laser-assisted irrigation = adjunct with potential but unproven definitive clinical benefit.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

**Current limitations**:
- 4 of 9 included SRs are critically low AMSTAR 2 quality; only 1 is high.
- No standardised laser protocols (wavelength, energy, pulse duration, fibre tip design, activation time).
- Inconsistent outcome reporting; mixture of in vitro and clinical data within SRs.
- Short follow-up periods in most primary studies.
- Lack of baseline dentinal defect assessment in primary studies undermines microcrack causality analysis.

**Future research priorities**:
1. Well-designed RCTs with standardised laser parameters and clinically relevant outcomes (postoperative pain, tenderness to percussion, swelling, sinus tract resolution, radiographic healing).
2. Standardised imaging modalities (periapical radiographs or CBCT) with long-term follow-up.
3. Head-to-head comparison of laser-activated irrigation vs PUI/sonic irrigation using equivalent irrigant regimens.
4. Clarify whether LAI offers genuine clinical advantage beyond existing activation modalities.

## 6. Related Work

- Passive Ultrasonic Irrigation (PUI) and Apical Negative Pressure (ANP) — the current comparative benchmarks against which erbium laser performance was assessed.
- Meire et al SR — evaluated Nd:YAG and Er,Cr:YSGG in dry vs liquid-filled canals, reporting periapical healing outcomes.
- Oliveira Tavares et al SR — evaluated laser-induced structural changes to root canal dentin; most comprehensive crack formation data.
- Er:YAG photon-induced photoacoustic streaming (PIPS) literature — provides mechanistic basis for erbium LAI.

## 7. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| LAI (Laser-Activated Irrigation) | Use of pulsed laser energy to activate root canal irrigants, generating photoacoustic/cavitation effects |
| PIPS (Photon-Induced Photoacoustic Streaming) | Er:YAG-specific LAI modality using subablative parameters at the canal orifice |
| Er:YAG | Erbium-doped yttrium aluminium garnet laser; 2940 nm; highly absorbed by water and hydroxyapatite |
| Er,Cr:YSGG | Erbium-chromium doped yttrium scandium gallium garnet laser; 2780 nm; similar photoacoustic mechanism to Er:YAG |
| Nd:YAG | Neodymium-doped yttrium aluminium garnet laser; 1064 nm; near-infrared, photothermal mechanism, deep dentinal tubule penetration |
| Diode laser | Semiconductor laser; 810–980 nm; primarily for soft tissue, photobiomodulation |
| Photobiomodulation | Low-level laser effect on tissue → anti-inflammatory, analgesic response |
| Photoacoustic effect | Rapid laser energy absorption by fluid → bubble formation → mechanical irrigant agitation |
| Cavitation | Bubble collapse generating shockwaves that disrupt biofilm |
| AMSTAR 2 | 16-item tool for methodological quality assessment of systematic reviews |
| Umbrella review | Synthesis of existing systematic reviews on a topic |
| AP (Apical Periodontitis) | Inflammatory lesion at root apex from endodontic infection; global prevalence 16–86% |
