## Why Ingested

Existing buccal-dehiscence IIP pages ([[immediate-implant/esthetic-soft-tissue/qian-2023-iip-buccal-bone-dehiscence-anterior-maxilla]], [[immediate-implant/esthetic-soft-tissue/pohl-2022-iipp-anterior-maxilla-buccal-dehiscence-pilot]]) demonstrate that IIP can work in dehisced sockets but, as Cosyn notes, almost never report the *vertical and lateral extent* that actually determines regenerative potential — so they remain proof-of-principle. This Cosyn 2026 conceptual review (Ghent) supplies the missing decision layer: an 11-morphotype classification (vertical Elian/Chu Type II-a/b/c × lateral contained/PC/NC + Type III) that maps each socket to one of three escalating regenerative strategies (modified trimodal → four-pillar non-supportive barrier → four-pillar supportive barrier + composite graft), and names the two absolute IIP contraindications. It extends the single-paper evidence into a usable case-selection framework.

## One-line Summary

Conceptual review (Cosyn & De Bruyckere, Ghent) proposing 11 buccal-dehiscence morphotypes (vertical Type II-a/b/c × lateral contained/partially-contained/non-contained, plus Type III & extended defects) for IIP; 2 are absolute contraindications and the other 9 map to 3 escalating regenerative strategies by regenerative potential.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. **New lateral-extent classification** of a buccal dehiscence by regenerative potential: contained (facial only, highest potential) → partially contained (mesial OR distal) → non-contained (mesial AND distal) → extended (to adjacent tooth, no potential).
2. **11 buccal-dehiscence morphotypes** synthesized from vertical extent (Elian Type II / Chu II-a <4 mm, II-b 4-6 mm, II-c >6 mm), lateral extent (C/PC/NC), and facial soft tissue support (Type III).
3. **Two absolute IIP contraindications**: Type III (dehiscence + facial recession) and extended defects.
4. **Three escalating regenerative strategies** mapped to nine treatable morphotypes by regenerative potential.
5. **Safe / Danger / Failure zone** model for 3-D implant positioning (vertical: safe starts 4 mm below soft-tissue margin; horizontal: buccal gap >2 mm).
6. A clinical **decision tree** for IIP in compromised sockets.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Conceptual review — no primary data collection, systematic search, or meta-analysis. Framework is built by synthesizing prior classifications (Elian, Chu, Kan), biological healing principles (structural stability of the regeneration chamber + mesenchymal stem cell migration from surrounding bone/periosteum), and the authors' clinical/expert experience, illustrated with clinical case photographs and CBCT/3-D reconstructions. Authors explicitly state the regenerative concepts require future clinical validation. Hence `confidence: narrative-review` (the article proposes, but does not test, a classification).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Positioning zones**
- Safe zone: vertical begins 4 mm below facial soft-tissue margin; horizontal buccal bone gap >2 mm → maximal preservation, ideal emergence profile.
- Danger zone: 2 mm vertical × 1 mm horizontal — shoulder may lack bone, thin buccal wall expected, soft tissue thins at abutment.
- Failure zone: 2 mm vertical (to soft-tissue margin) × variable horizontal — shoulder exposed, no buccal wall after healing → advanced recession / esthetic failure.

**Morphotype → regenerative-strategy decision framework** (core takeaway)

| Regenerative potential | Morphotypes | Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Contraindicated** (high esthetic-failure risk) | Type III (dehiscence + facial recession); Extended defect (to adjacent tooth) | Do **not** place IIP |
| **Relatively high** | Type II-a (all lateral); Type II-b contained | **Modified trimodal** — flapless mandatory, barrier omitted, wider buccal gap + slightly **thicker CTG** (± extra soft-tissue aug for II-a non-contained) |
| **Moderate** | Type II-b partially contained; Type II-c contained | **Four-pillar + non-supportive barrier** (native collagen membrane; defect still has surrounding bone support) |
| **Low** | Type II-b non-contained; Type II-c partially contained; Type II-c non-contained | **Four-pillar + supportive barrier + composite bone graft** (autogenous cortico-cancellous shell from tuberosity OR porcine supportive barrier, placed over bony edges; moldable graft + autogenous bone chips) |

- Regenerative potential is **inversely related to complication risk**.
- Diagnosis: CBCT over-reports dehiscence (thin walls invisible → low specificity/PPV); bone sounding and pocket probing closer to truth; intrasurgical confirmation always required. Lateral extent read on axial CBCT slice just below anticipated buccal crest (level set at contralateral tooth).
- Flapless mandatory in any compromised socket (flap disrupts buccal-wall blood supply → bone loss). Computer-assisted surgery + bone-track drill recommended as standard of care to avoid buccal malpositioning.
- Supporting datum: a 10-year RCT treated dehisced sockets (vertical ≥5 mm) with a tuberosity cortico-cancellous shell — successful regeneration to 10 y, midfacial recession only 0.13 mm with concomitant CTG.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- All nine treatable-morphotype strategies are **unvalidated** — need clinical studies with CBCT correlation, outcome tracking, and histology.
- Regenerative-potential tiers (high/moderate/low) are an **estimated model** that may need adjustment.
- Strategies framed only for IIP — not compared against early placement or ARP + late placement (which the authors note has stronger RCT/MA evidence and is the preferable, easier option for inexperienced clinicians).
- Apical-fenestration and acutely infected sockets are out of scope.

## 6. Related Work

- Builds on Elian et al. (vertical Type I/II/III), Chu et al. (II-a/b/c subdivision), Kan et al. (V/U/UU lateral) classifications.
- Aligns with single-paper IIP-in-dehiscence evidence: Qian 2023, Pohl 2022 (pilot), and buccal-bone-wall/esthetic outcome work (Martins 2024).
- Trimodal-approach pillars (gap grafting, CTG soft-tissue augmentation, immediate restoration) draw on the broader esthetic-soft-tissue IIP literature.

## 7. Glossary

- **IIP / IR**: Immediate implant placement / immediate restoration.
- **Buccal dehiscence**: Loss of buccal bone wall at the cervical aspect of the socket (vs. fenestration, which leaves the crest intact).
- **Trimodal approach**: Buccal-gap grafting + soft-tissue augmentation (CTG) + immediate provisional — state of the art for intact-socket IIP.
- **Four-pillar approach**: Trimodal + a barrier membrane (non-supportive or supportive).
- **Contained / partially contained / non-contained / extended defect**: Lateral-extent categories by remaining interproximal bone (decreasing regenerative potential).
- **Supportive vs non-supportive barrier**: Rigid barrier (autogenous bone shell / stiff porcine) that maintains regeneration-chamber space vs. flexible native collagen membrane.
- **CTG**: Connective tissue graft.
- **SAC classification**: Straightforward/Advanced/Complex case-difficulty scheme.
