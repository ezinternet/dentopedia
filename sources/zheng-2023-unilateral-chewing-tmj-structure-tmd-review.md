## One-line Summary

Narrative review: chewing-side preference is a major risk factor for TMD Рђћ preferred-side condyles become shorter/displaced with reduced posterior-superior joint space, articular eminence inclination and glenoid fossa depth increase, and clinical studies show TMD patients have higher rates of unilateral chewing; proposed mechanisms span biomechanical (BMP-2/VEGF condylar cartilage remodeling), masticatory-muscle (CaN-driven Type IIРєњI fiber shift, EMG asymmetry), and neurogenic (substance P / CGRP upregulation) pathways.

## 2. Key Contributions

1. Synthesizes Chinese and international literature establishing chewing-side preference (defined: chewing cycles on one side >30% more than the other) as an important, biomechanically-plausible TMD risk factor Рђћ adding a *functional behavioral* variable to the contested occlusionРђЊTMD debate.
2. Structures the evidence into a three-level chain: (a) effect of unilateral chewing on TMJ structure (condyle, glenoid fossa, articular disc); (b) clinical association with TMD signs/symptoms (Table 1, 15 studies); (c) candidate mechanisms (biomechanical, masticatory-muscle, neurogenic).
3. Highlights three mechanistic pathways: BMP-2/VEGF-mediated condylar cartilage remodeling under altered loading; calcineurin (CaN)-driven masticatory-muscle fiber-type transformation (Type IIРєњType I) with EMG asymmetry; and substance P / CGRP upregulation producing TMJ neurogenic inflammation.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

Narrative review (not systematic). The authors collate human imaging studies (mostly CBCT), clinical cross-sectional/case-control studies, animal (rat) experiments, and finite-element analyses. Table 1 tabulates 15 clinical studies on the chewing-side-preferenceРєћTMD relationship (study aim, subjects, method, key result, reference). No formal search strategy, risk-of-bias, or pooled estimate is reported.

**Definition.** A chewing cycle (closingРєњocclusionРєњopening) is the basic mastication unit; when one side's cycle count exceeds the other by >30%, it is termed unilateral chewing / chewing-side preference. Etiology is multifactorial Рђћ central factors (lateralization akin to handedness) and peripheral factors (missing teeth, caries, periapical disease, joint and masticatory-muscle factors). Reported prevalence ~87% (deciduous), ~82% (mixed), ~76% (permanent dentition).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Effect on TMJ structure**
- *Condyle:* On the preferred (working) side Рђћ reduced posterior, posterior-superior, lateral and superior joint spaces; greater condylar neck width; overall smaller condyle that migrates superiorly. Non-preferred side shows compensatory anterior joint-space enlargement. (Jiang; Lyu; Li Jia.) One CBCT study (Costa 2021) found no significant condylar/coronoid volume difference Рђћ conflicting evidence.
- *Glenoid fossa / articular eminence:* Greater articular eminence inclination on preferred side (Jiang 2015); TMD-with-unilateral-chewing group has significantly higher eminence inclination than TMD-without and healthy groups, with greater fossa depth (Ma 2021). Working-side condyle sits posterosuperiorly during normal chewing; repeated unilateral loading drives adaptive remodeling.
- *Articular disc:* Increased eminence inclination + fossa depth predispose to disc displacement during translation. In anterior-disc-displacement patients with unilateral chewing, the preferred side shows significantly higher rates of non-reducing anterior disc displacement, pain and joint noise (Huang 2021).

**Clinical association (Table 1, selected)**
- Wu 2021 (754 medical students): TMD prevalence 31.7%; 56.5% of TMD patients had unilateral chewing Рђћ significantly higher than healthy; a high-risk factor.
- Yu 2015 (616 male civilian pilots): TMD prevalence 33.3%; anxiety and unilateral chewing the most significant associated factors.
- Yal├Д─▒n 2017 (519 university students): unilateral chewing rate rose with TMD severity Рђћ 36.8% (no TMD), 46.9% (mild), 54.6% (moderate), 65.0% (severe).
- Other study (109 TMD vs 109 healthy): unilateral chewing 69.7% vs 54.1%.
- Symptom correlations: unilateral chewing significantly associated with mouth-opening pain, joint pain, orofacial pain, limited jaw movement, joint clicking (Chua; Diernberger; Ma; others). TMJOA-with-unilateral-chewing patients more prone to pain and to condylar surface bony destruction/flattening (Zhai 2022) Рєњ possible TMJOA-promoting factor.

**Mechanisms**
- *Biomechanical:* In unilateral-chewing rats, chewing-side condylar cartilage shows higher BMP-2, IL-1╬▓, TNF-╬▒, Type II collagen, IL-1, VEGF and increased cartilage thickness; non-chewing side thins. Working-side condyle rotates (compression near initial position), non-working side translates (compression on anterior fossa) Рєњ long-term anterior remodeling of non-chewing fossa and deepening of chewing-side fossa. FEA: non-chewing-side disc receives greater medial-lateral pressure Рєњ retrodiscal/muscle pain. Animal histology: trabecular osteocyte loss, empty lacunae, thinned cartilage, disc collagen disorganization, synovial proliferation/damage.
- *Masticatory muscle:* Long-term unilateral chewing raises intracellular Ca┬▓РЂ║ Рєњ activates calcineurin (CaN) pathway Рєњ transcription of slow-fiber genes Рєњ Type IIРєњType I fiber transformation on preferred side; preferred-side masseter/temporalis EMG activity higher than non-preferred; focal inflammatory infiltrate Рєњ prostaglandin E2 Рєњ fiber rupture and muscle pain; positively correlated with asymmetry of bite force and occlusal contact area.
- *Neurogenic:* Trigeminal ganglion synthesizes substance P and CGRP, delivered to TMJ; both enhance nociceptive transmission and vascular permeability Рєњ local edema, inflammatory-cell infiltration, neurogenic inflammation. Unilateral-chewing rats show upregulated substance P / CGRP in trigeminal ganglion, with higher substance P synthesis on the chewing side.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Narrative review without systematic search, risk-of-bias appraisal, or quantitative pooling.
- Human structural studies are limited in sample size/type; age, sex, preferred side, central vs peripheral cause and duration of unilateral chewing are rarely stratified Рђћ directionality (cause vs consequence) remains uncertain.
- Conflicting CBCT volume data (Costa 2021 null).
- Mechanistic data (BMP-2, CaN, substance P/CGRP) are largely from rat models; human translation and specific signaling mechanisms need further study.
- Clinical message: clinicians should attend to unilateral chewing and its causes in both healthy and TMD patients, and correct it early to help prevent/limit TMD.

## 7. Glossary

- **Chewing-side preference / Unilateral chewing (тЂЈСЙДтњђтџ╝):** habitual chewing where one side's cycle count exceeds the other by >30%.
- **TMJ (жбъСИІжбїтЁ│Уіѓ):** temporomandibular joint Рђћ glenoid fossa, articular disc, condyle, capsule, ligaments.
- **TMD (жбъСИІжбїтЁ│Уіѓу┤іС╣▒):** temporomandibular disorders Рђћ group of conditions of masticatory muscles, TMJ and related structures.
- **TMJOA:** temporomandibular joint osteoarthritis.
- **Articular eminence inclination (тЁ│Уіѓу╗ЊУіѓТќют║д):** slope of the articular eminence governing condyle/disc translation path.
- **BMP-2:** bone morphogenetic protein 2 Рђћ anabolic factor inducing bone/cartilage formation.
- **VEGF:** vascular endothelial growth factor.
- **CaN (calcineurin):** Ca┬▓РЂ║-dependent signaling molecule regulating skeletal-muscle fiber-type remodeling.
- **Substance P / CGRP:** neuropeptides from trigeminal ganglion mediating nociception and neurogenic inflammation.
