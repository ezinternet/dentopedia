## Why Ingested

Part of a focused collection on suture techniques and primary wound closure after impacted mandibular third molar surgery. This RCT frames the question as primary vs secondary *closure* (full suturing vs leaving a drainage window) and reports the recurring pattern that secondary closure reduces pain/swelling/trismus while periodontal healing is unaffected — the same closure-type signal seen in [[suture-wound-closure/kumar-2022-suture-versus-sutureless-third-molar-impactions]], with periodontal equivalence echoed by [[suture-wound-closure/aghdashi-2020-sling-single-interrupted-sutures-third-molar]].

## One-line Summary
RCT (n=60, 30 per arm) comparing primary vs secondary closure after impacted mandibular third molar removal; secondary closure gave significantly less pain and swelling and greater mouth opening (p<0.05), with no difference in periodontal healing at 6 months (alveolar osteitis in 1 secondary-closure patient, 3.3%).

## 2. Key Contributions
- Direct primary-vs-secondary closure RCT with both inflammatory (pain, swelling, trismus) and periodontal (6-month healing near adjacent second molar) endpoints.
- Reinforces that secondary closure (leaving a drainage window) reduces early morbidity without harming periodontal outcomes.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- **Design**: RCT, 60 patients randomized into Group A (primary closure, n=30) and Group B (secondary closure, n=30).
- **Outcomes**: postoperative pain (VAS), swelling, trismus at days 1, 3, 7; periodontal healing near the adjacent second molar at 6 months.
- **Statistics**: between-group comparison, significance p<0.05.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- Swelling and pain **greater in Group A (primary closure)**, statistically significant (p<0.05).
- Mouth opening **greater in Group B (secondary closure)** — i.e., less trismus.
- **No significant difference** in periodontal healing between groups at 6 months.
- Alveolar osteitis in **1 patient (3.3%)** in Group B (secondary closure).
- Conclusion: secondary closure was better than primary closure for postoperative pain, swelling, and trismus; periodontal healing did not differ by closure technique.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Moderate sample (n=60), single center.
- Full numeric tables paywalled in captured PDF.
- 6-month periodontal endpoint only; longer-term not assessed.

## 7. Glossary
- **Primary closure**: complete mucoperiosteal coverage of the surgical site with sutures.
- **Secondary closure**: a window/gap left open for drainage; heals by secondary intention.
- **Trismus**: restricted mouth opening (measured as maximum interincisal opening).
- **Alveolar osteitis**: dry socket.
- **VAS**: Visual Analogue Scale for pain.
