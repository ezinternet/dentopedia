## One-line Summary
RCT (one-year) in maxillary molar region: immediate implant placement + transalveolar (transcrestal) sinus floor elevation with nonsubmerged healing (test) vs delayed implant placement after staged sinus lift (control); 100% survival in both groups, but test group preserved significantly more buccal ridge dimensions (horizontal shrinkage 0.65 vs 1.23 mm, vertical resorption 0.60 vs 1.53 mm; P < .0001).

## 1. Document Information
- **Journal**: Clinical Implant Dentistry and Related Research 2019;21(3):462–472.
- **Type**: Randomized controlled trial (parallel-group, one-year follow-up).
- **Funding**: Qingdao Municipal Science and Technology Bureau (15-9-2-73-nsh).
- **Conflict of interest**: None disclosed.

## 2. Key Contributions
- One of the first RCTs directly comparing **immediate** vs **delayed** implant placement when both arms use transalveolar sinus floor elevation and nonsubmerged healing in molar sites.
- Quantifies ridge-preservation benefit of immediate placement on the buccal aspect.
- Validates that initial implant stability adequate for nonsubmerged protocol is achievable in fresh molar sockets when interradicular bone supports it.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- Patients requiring extraction of a failing tooth in the maxillary molar region randomized to:
  - **Test**: immediate implant placement + transalveolar sinus floor elevation (Summers / osteotome) + nonsubmerged healing.
  - **Control**: delayed implant placement + transalveolar sinus floor elevation + nonsubmerged healing.
- Outcomes: implant survival; CBCT-measured dimensional changes at buccal and palatal aspects (horizontal shrinkage W1/W2 and vertical resorption H1/H2); patient satisfaction (VAS).
- Time points: time of permanent restoration (W1, H1); after 1 year of loading (W2, H2).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- **Initial stability**: adequate in all implants.
- **Implant survival**: 100% in both groups at 1 year.
- **Buccal horizontal shrinkage (W1)**: test 0.65 ± 0.12 mm vs control 1.23 ± 0.32 mm (**P < .0001**).
- **Palatal horizontal shrinkage**: 0.30 ± 0.10 vs 0.28 ± 0.08 mm (NS, P = .515).
- **Buccal vertical resorption (H1)**: test 0.60 ± 0.18 mm vs control 1.53 ± 0.19 mm (**P < .0001**).
- **Palatal vertical resorption**: 0.24 ± 0.12 vs 0.29 ± 0.13 mm (NS, P = .190).
- **W2/H2 after 1-year loading**: no significant inter-group differences.
- **VAS satisfaction**: 8.36 ± 1.01 (test) vs 8.14 ± 1.35 (control), NS (P = .638).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Single-center RCT; sample size and exact n per arm not detailed in abstract.
- 1-year follow-up — peri-implant bone stability beyond loading is unknown.
- Operator effect not controlled; no blinding of clinical assessment.
- Residual bone height ranges and case selection criteria critical for generalization to lower-RBH sites.

## 7. Glossary
- **Transalveolar / transcrestal approach**: sinus floor elevation through the alveolar crest using osteotomes (Summers' technique).
- **Nonsubmerged healing**: healing abutment placed at the time of implant surgery (single-stage).
- **W1, H1**: horizontal and vertical alveolar dimensional changes measured at permanent restoration.
- **VAS**: Visual Analog Scale (patient-reported satisfaction).
