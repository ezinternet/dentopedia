## Why Ingested

Extends the fabrication-technique comparison to include cast glass ceramic veneers at two thicknesses alongside feldspathic veneers, providing a four-way marginal fit comparison that complements [[veneers/klein-2025-ceramic-laminate-veneer-survival-complications-sr-ma]] by grounding fabrication-method differences in measurable marginal accuracy.

## One-line Summary

In-vitro study (Int J Prosthodont 1993, PMID 8507328) comparing marginal fit of platinum foil, refractory die, and cast glass ceramic (0.5 mm and 1.0 mm) porcelain veneers; platinum foil had the best fit, and thicker cast ceramic (1.0 mm) had the worst.

## 2. Key Contributions

- First study to directly compare four veneer fabrication methods in the same in-vitro experiment: platinum foil feldspathic, refractory die feldspathic, cast ceramic 0.5 mm, cast ceramic 1.0 mm.
- Established a clear hierarchy of marginal fit: platinum foil > refractory die > cast ceramic 0.5 mm > cast ceramic 1.0 mm.
- Measured fit both before and after sectioning of specimens to separate elastic deformation from permanent marginal opening.
- Confirmed that fabrication technique (p < .001) and measurement site (p < .05) both significantly affect marginal fit.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Model tooth**: Maxillary central incisor, labial surface preparation.
- **Groups**: (1) Feldspathic / platinum foil, (2) Feldspathic / refractory die, (3) Cast glass ceramic 0.5 mm, (4) Cast glass ceramic 1.0 mm.
- **Fit measurement**: Marginal opening measured before sectioning (intact veneer seated) and after sectioning (to eliminate elastic spring-back).
- **Statistical analysis**: Effects of fabrication technique and measurement site evaluated; significance thresholds reported (p < .001 and p < .05).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Marginal opening hierarchy (intact)**: platinum foil < refractory die < cast ceramic 0.5 mm < cast ceramic 1.0 mm.
- Same rank order maintained in sectioned specimens.
- Both technique (p < .001) and measurement site (p < .05) were statistically significant predictors of marginal fit.
- Cast glass ceramic veneers (both thicknesses) showed the greatest marginal discrepancy; thicker specimens were worse.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- In-vitro study on a single tooth model; no clinical outcomes or bonding evaluated.
- PDF is a PubMed abstract page only; full quantitative gap measurements not extractable from available text.
- Cast glass ceramic (Dicor-type) is largely obsolete; findings may not translate directly to modern pressable or CAD/CAM ceramic veneers.
- No assessment of microleakage or cementation effects on marginal gap.

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary

- **Marginal fit / marginal opening**: The gap at the interface between the restoration margin and the prepared tooth surface.
- **Platinum foil technique**: Veneer built up over a thin platinum matrix; historically superior marginal accuracy.
- **Refractory die technique**: Veneer fired directly on a refractory die; slightly inferior marginal fit to platinum foil.
- **Cast glass ceramic**: Cast-ceramic material (e.g., Dicor) fabricated by lost-wax casting; now largely discontinued.
- **Sectioned specimen**: Specimen cut after measurement to release elastic deformation and reveal permanent marginal discrepancy.
