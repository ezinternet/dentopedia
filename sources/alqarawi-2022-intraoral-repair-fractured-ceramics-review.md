## Why Ingested

Addresses the clinical management of ceramic fractures/chipping — a frequent complication documented in zirconia and metal-ceramic SRs (e.g., Pjetursson 2018). Bridges ceramic bonding and surface treatment principles to a practical intraoral repair scenario; complements bonding evidence in the resin-bonding category. [[resin-bonding/kheirkhah-2021-universal-adhesive-silane-porcelain-repair-bond]]

## One-line Summary

Narrative review (21 studies, 2017–2022): intraoral ceramic repair is viable and cost-effective; for veneered zirconia, optimal protocol is HF acid etching of veneering ceramic + silane + composite resin; glazing requires no HF but needs alternative surface treatment.

## 한줄요약

구강 내 세라믹 수리 문헌고찰 (21편, 2017–2022): 지르코니아 베니어 파절 시 HF 산부식 + 실란 + 복합레진이 최선; 직접 수리는 비용 효율적이고 최소침습적.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Reviews 21 eligible studies (17 in vitro, 3 case reports, 1 prospective clinical study) from PubMed, 2017–2022
- Synthesizes intraoral repair techniques for multiple ceramic types: glass-based (feldspathic, leucite, lithium disilicate), glass-infiltrated (In-Ceram), polycrystalline (alumina, zirconia)
- Identifies optimal protocol for veneered zirconia fracture: HF acid etching of veneering ceramic → silane coating of zirconia core → composite resin repair material
- Evaluates surface conditioning options: HF, sandblasting/airborne abrasion, laser (Nd:YAG, Er:YAG), combinations
- Highlights that bond strength between repair material and substrate is the critical determinant of repair longevity

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Search**: PubMed; January 2017 – August 2022; English language; keywords: "intraoral repair," "ceramics," "porcelain," "porcelain fused to metal," "efficacy"
- **Inclusion**: Studies on intraoral repair techniques for ceramic fractures
- **Exclusion**: Non-English, >5 years prior, no full text
- **Process**: Title/abstract screening → full-text screening → data extraction
- **No statistical analysis** (narrative synthesis only)
- **21 included studies**: 17 in vitro, 3 case reports, 1 prospective clinical study

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Ceramic types studied**:
- Glass-based ceramics: feldspathic, leucite-reinforced (IPS Empress), lithium disilicate (IPS e.max)
- Zirconia-reinforced lithium silicate (Celtra Duo)
- Polycrystalline zirconia (pure zirconia, veneered zirconia)

**Surface conditioning methods compared**:
1. HF acid etching — gold standard for glass-phase ceramics
2. Sandblasting/airborne particle abrasion (Al₂O₃) — mechanical roughening
3. Nd:YAG laser ± HF — clinically favorable for some ceramics
4. Er:YAG laser ± HF
5. Universal adhesive primers (MDP-containing)
6. Silane coupling agents

**Key findings by ceramic type**:

*Veneered zirconia (zirconia core + veneering ceramic)*:
- Best protocol: HF etching of veneering ceramic + silane on zirconia core surface + composite resin
- For each layer of cracked zirconia-ceramic: specific surface treatment required for each substrate exposed
- Universal adhesives without silane: inferior bond strength vs HF + silane + traditional adhesive

*Lithium disilicate (LD)*:
- HF + silane = standard; grit blasting + rubber dam improves bond for intraoral repair
- Self-etching glass-ceramic primer (MEP) + composite: alternative

*Leucite-reinforced ceramics*:
- HF + silane + traditional adhesive > HF + universal adhesive alone (higher SBS)
- Laser combinations may serve as alternatives

*Zirconia (no veneering layer — monolithic or exposed core)*:
- No HF (glass phase absent); requires sandblasting or laser conditioning
- Silane: limited benefit alone on pure zirconia; requires phosphate monomer (MDP) adhesive
- Selective infiltration etching (SIE) or laser creates microroughness for mechanical bonding

**Clinical outcomes**:
- Intraoral repair is temporary but effective; longevity depends on technique adherence and clinical skill
- Avoids crown removal risks (abutment damage, tooth structure loss, cost)
- Most clinical failures due to technique deviation, contamination, or inadequate substrate preparation

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Mostly in vitro studies (17/21) — limited clinical translation evidence
- Short-term data; only 1 prospective clinical study included
- Heterogeneous protocols across studies limit direct comparison
- No long-term survival data for intraoral repair outcomes
- Future: standardized clinical protocols, RCTs with ≥3 year follow-up, comparison of repair vs replacement cost-effectiveness

## 7. Glossary

- **HF**: Hydrofluoric acid — etches glass phase in ceramics, creating microretentive surface for silane + bonding
- **Silane**: Bifunctional coupling agent (organosilane) — bonds between ceramic surface and composite resin
- **SBS**: Shear bond strength — standard in vitro metric for repair bond strength
- **MDP**: 10-methacryloyloxydecyl dihydrogen phosphate — phosphate monomer in universal adhesives; chemically bonds to zirconia
- **MEP**: Self-etching glass-ceramic primer (methacryloyloxypropyl trimethoxysilane-based)
- **FDP**: Fixed dental prosthesis
- **Chipping**: Cohesive fracture of veneering ceramic layer, not through the core
- **Selective infiltration etching (SIE)**: Surface treatment for zirconia using glassy matrix infiltration + HF to create microroughness
