## Why Ingested
Shows the analytic frontier of the response axis: mining the *emotional* language of complaints (NLP) to drive hospital quality improvement. Connects complaint handling to organisational development and previews how a clinic could analyse its own complaint text. Extends the manual coding of [[complaint-management/bmjqs-2015-004596]] (HCAT) toward automated sentiment, within the [[overviews/complaint-management-pipeline-classification-expectation-response-education]] response axis.

## One-line Summary
Case study of a Chinese tertiary-hospital service centre using an emotional-language analysis model of complaints to inform high-quality hospital development.

- Frontiers in Health Services 2025;5:1610004. CC BY.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Applies an emotional/sentiment language-analysis model to complaint narratives.
- Treats patient complaints as a driver of 'high-quality hospital development', not just risk.
- Demonstrates systematic analysis of patients' perceptions, opinions, and emotional responses.
- Bridges complaint analytics and management strategy at an organisational level.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
Single-site case study at a tertiary-hospital service centre. Systematic analysis of patient perceptions, opinions, and emotional responses within the complaint-handling process using an emotional-language (sentiment) analysis model. (Mixed quantitative text analysis + organisational case interpretation.)

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
The emotional-language model characterised patients' affective responses across the complaint-handling process and linked complaint-handling quality to indicators of high-quality hospital development, supporting use of complaint sentiment data to target service improvement. (Specific model metrics reported in full text.)

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
Single tertiary hospital case study; model performance metrics not fully abstracted here [미검증]. Chinese-language sentiment model -- transfer to Korean dental complaints would need re-training. Limited external validity.

## 6. Related Work
Analytic extension of structured complaint coding (HCAT) toward automated sentiment; aligns with Li (2024) on system-level benefit.

## 7. Glossary
- **Emotional/sentiment language analysis**: NLP method classifying affective tone in text.
- **High-quality development**: Chinese health-policy term for quality-led hospital improvement.
