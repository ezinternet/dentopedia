## One-line Summary
SR (56 of 538 articles) comparing chelating agents — 17% EDTA most effective for smear-layer removal and growth-factor release, but 9–18% HEDP advantageous for NaOCl compatibility and avoiding smear-layer formation.

## 한줄요약
SR(538편 중 56편): 킬레이트제 비교 — smear layer 제거·성장인자 방출은 17% EDTA 우수, NaOCl 양립성·smear layer 예방은 9–18% HEDP(에티드론산)가 유리.

## 1. Document Information
- J Clin Exp Dent 2024;16(4):e516–538. Universitat de Barcelona; search to Jan 2023.

## 2. Key Contributions
- Multi-axis comparison: efficacy, erosive potential, cytotoxicity, interaction, antimicrobial, sealer adhesion, growth-factor release.
- Positions HEDP as a NaOCl-compatible alternative to EDTA.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture
- MEDLINE/Cochrane/Scopus to 14 Jan 2023; agents: 17% EDTA, 9/18% HEDP, 10/20% citric acid, 2–2.25% PAA, 7% maleic acid.
- 538 → 56 included; modified CONSORT + Cochrane tool; moderate-low evidence.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks
- 17% EDTA: best smear-layer removal + growth-factor release (regenerative endodontics).
- 9/18% HEDP: optimal NaOCl compatibility; avoids smear-layer formation.
- EDTA greater cytotoxicity/inflammation than alternatives.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work
- Moderate-low evidence; heterogeneous protocols.

## 6. Related Work
- Chelator-focused companion to maleic-acid scoping review and herbal-vs-EDTA SR.

## 7. Glossary
- HEDP/etidronic acid: chelator co-usable with NaOCl in a single irrigant.
- Maleic acid / PAA: alternative chelators/conditioners.
