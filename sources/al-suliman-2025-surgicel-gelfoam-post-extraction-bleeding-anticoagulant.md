## Why Ingested

[[drug/anticoagulants/moldovan-2023-anticoagulant-oral-surgery-bleeding-sr]] establishes that extractions can proceed without interrupting anticoagulation when a local hemostatic measure is used, but does not adjudicate which topical agent is superior. This split-mouth trial directly head-to-heads the two most common socket dressings (oxidized cellulose Surgicel vs gelatin sponge Gelfoam) in aspirin/warfarin patients, refining the "use a local hemostat" recommendation into a concrete agent choice.

## One-line Summary

Prospective single-blinded split-mouth clinical trial (40 patients, 80 sockets; 20 aspirin + 20 warfarin) — Surgicel (oxidized cellulose) outperformed Gelfoam (gelatin sponge) on the same patient's contralateral socket for hemostasis (VIBe), delayed 24-h bleeding, gingival healing, and pain (all p<0.05); warfarin patients bled and hurt more than aspirin patients.

## 한줄요약

전향적 단일맹검 분악(split-mouth) 임상시험 (환자 40명, 발치와 80곳; 아스피린 20 + 와파린 20) — 같은 환자의 반대쪽 발치와 비교에서 Surgicel(산화셀룰로오스)이 Gelfoam(젤라틴 스폰지)보다 지혈(VIBe)·24시간 지연출혈·치은치유·통증 모두에서 우수(전부 p<0.05); 와파린군이 아스피린군보다 출혈·통증 더 많음.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Direct intra-patient (split-mouth) head-to-head of two widely used socket hemostatic dressings — oxidized regenerated cellulose (Surgicel) vs absorbable gelatin sponge (Gelfoam) — in anticoagulated extraction patients.
- Stratified by anticoagulant class: aspirin 80 mg/day (antiplatelet) vs warfarin (INR 2–3.5), allowing comparison of bleeding/healing burden between the two drug classes.
- Used the FDA-validated VIBe intraoperative bleeding scale plus Gingival Healing Index and VAS pain, evaluating hemostasis, delayed bleeding, healing, and comfort together.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **n = 40** (20 aspirin, 20 warfarin), **80 sockets** (paired teeth per patient): 48 molars (60%), 20 premolars (25%), 12 anterior (15%), balanced across sides.
- Split-mouth allocation **fixed by side**: Gelfoam → right, Surgicel → left in both groups (not randomized side allocation — hence prospective rather than RCT).
- Warfarin patients required stable INR 2–3.5 (mean cohort INR 2.58 ± 0.32); anticoagulant doses kept unchanged throughout.
- Atraumatic periotome extraction; dressing placed in socket, figure-of-8 3.0 silk suture.
- Outcomes: VIBe at T0 (immediate) and T1 (2 h, reactionary); 24-h delayed bleeding via clinic + phone; Gingival Healing Index at D3/D7; VAS pain at D1/D3/D7. Sample size via G*Power (f=0.47, α=0.05, 95% power).

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Hemostasis (VIBe, lower=better)**: Surgicel < Gelfoam in both groups, at T0 and T1 (paired t-test p<0.0001). Aspirin T0 Gelfoam 2.0 vs Surgicel 1.8; Warfarin T1 Gelfoam 2.65 vs Surgicel 2.4. Warfarin > aspirin bleeding at all time points (p<0.0001).
- **Delayed bleeding (24 h)**: Aspirin Gelfoam 10% (2/20) vs Surgicel 5% (1/20); Warfarin Gelfoam 15% (3/20) vs Surgicel 10% (2/20). Surgicel significantly better (Chi-square p<0.05); warfarin > aspirin (p<0.05).
- **Gingival Healing Index (higher=better)** at D7: Aspirin Gelfoam 3.6 vs Surgicel 3.85; Warfarin Gelfoam 3.25 vs Surgicel 3.55. Surgicel > Gelfoam within group (p<0.05); warfarin lower than aspirin at D7 (p<0.05).
- **Pain (VAS)**: Surgicel < Gelfoam at all days (p<0.0001); D1 warfarin Gelfoam 8.25 vs Surgicel 6.9; D7 aspirin Gelfoam 2.65 vs Surgicel 1.65. Warfarin > aspirin (p<0.0001).
- INR subgroup (2.1–2.5 vs 2.6–3.1): trend to more bleeding at higher INR, NOT significant (p>0.05).

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- **Not randomized side allocation** (fixed Gelfoam-right/Surgicel-left) — coded as prospective, not RCT; risk of side/handedness confounding.
- **No suture-only (no-hemostat) control arm** — added benefit of dressing vs no dressing not quantified.
- Split-mouth single-session design compromises pain localization (overlapping perception between sides).
- Postoperative bleeding is rare even in anticoagulated patients; low event counts limit generalizability.
- **No DOAC patients** (only aspirin + warfarin) — authors call for inclusion of direct oral anticoagulants.
- Single-center, Syria; small n; retrospective trial registration.

## 7. Glossary

- **Surgicel**: oxidized regenerated cellulose hemostatic dressing; activates clotting via platelet interaction and lowers local pH (also antimicrobial).
- **Gelfoam**: absorbable gelatin sponge; mechanical scaffold/barrier promoting clot formation, resorbable.
- **VIBe scale**: Validated Intraoperative Bleeding scale (FDA-validated), 0–5 grading of bleeding severity.
- **Gingival Healing Index (Landry)**: 5-level (very poor → excellent) clinical wound-healing score.
- **INR**: International Normalized Ratio; warfarin dental therapeutic range typically 2–3.5.
