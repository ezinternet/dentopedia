## Why Ingested

Part of the clear-aligner-limitations evidence base: documents that clear aligner therapy (CAT) is NOT inert on the masticatory/stomatognathic system — it transiently raises muscle activity/discomfort and reduces bite force in the first days/weeks. Pairs directly with the EMG sibling [[wiki/orthodontics/lekaviciute-2024-clear-aligner-masticatory-muscle-emg-sr-ma]] (masseter/temporalis sEMG) and contextualizes the overall efficacy/safety anchor [[wiki/orthodontics/baneshi-2024-clear-aligners-malocclusion-sr-ma]].

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (no meta-analysis; 6 clinical studies, n=10–31, ROB-2) finding clear aligner therapy causes a transient early increase in masticatory muscle activity/discomfort that normalizes on follow-up, plus a significant bite-force reduction; all 6 studies were of good methodological quality (low overall risk of bias).

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(메타분석 없음; 임상연구 6편, n=10–31, ROB-2): 투명교정(Clear Aligner Therapy, CAT)은 착용 초기에 저작근 활성도·불편감을 일시적으로 증가시키나 추적 시 정상화되고, 교합력(bite force)은 유의하게 감소함. 6편 모두 방법론적 질 양호(전체 비뚤림 위험 낮음).

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Systematic review without meta-analysis (qualitative synthesis only)
- **Journal**: BMC Oral Health 2024;24(1):350
- **PMID/DOI**: 38504207 / 10.1186/s12903-024-04029-8 (PMC10949793)
- **Registration**: PROSPERO (509370)
- **Framework**: PRISMA + Cochrane handbook; PEOS question — "Does aligner orthodontic treatment affect muscles of mastication and the stomatognathic system?"
- **ROB tool**: ROB-2

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First focused SR isolating clear aligner therapy (rather than fixed appliances) effects on the masticatory musculature and stomatognathic system.
2. Synthesizes the **temporal pattern**: early (T0→T1) rise in muscle activity / discomfort that reverts to baseline on later follow-up (attributed to muscle plasticity / occlusal adaptation).
3. Flags **bite-force reduction** during CAT as a consistent secondary finding.
4. Concludes CAT is broadly **TMD-neutral** (no causal link to TMD onset), consistent with the wider orthodontics-TMD literature.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases**: PubMed, Embase, Web of Science, Scopus, Cochrane Library, CINAHL, PsycINFO, Google Scholar (English-only).
- **PEOS**: P = humans (any age/sex); E = clear aligner orthodontic treatment; C = none; O = masticatory muscle activity change / TMD; S = clinical studies.
- **Screening**: 437 records → 276 screened → 134 assessed for eligibility → **6 included** (qualitative synthesis; meta-analysis not feasible due to heterogeneity).
- **Excluded**: reviews, editorials, commentaries, case reports, conference abstracts; patients with RA or prior jaw trauma.
- **Bias**: ROB-2; two independent reviewers; third reviewer for disputes.
- Note: a heterogeneity meta-analysis was planned but not performed; limited/heterogeneous data precluded pooling.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

**Six included studies** (region, year, n, mean age, follow-up):

| Study | Region | Year | n | Mean age (y) | Follow-up | Key inference |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Liu et al. | China | 2017 | 23 | 26.8±2.4 | 6 mo | Anterior temporalis (TA) activity ↑ T0→T1 (P<0.05); TA & SCM ↑ at MVC at T1; OBC score ↓ (fewer parafunctions); insignificant after 3 mo (muscle plasticity) |
| Manfredini et al. | Italy | 2018 | 19 | 28.3±2.4 | Unspecified | Invisible retainers did NOT significantly affect sleep-time masticatory muscle activity (sMMA) / SB index |
| Nota et al. | Italy | 2021 | 16 | 22.5±3.5 | 3 mo | Masseter sEMG at rest ↓ at T1, returned to baseline at T2; palpation pain 12.5%→25% at T1; no change at clenching |
| Paes et al. | Brazil | 2023 | 10 | 29.9±5.5 | 8 mo | sEMG (RMS) ↑ ~30% (p=0.001); **bite force ↓ <20% (p<0.05)**; more clenching reported |
| Pittar et al. | New Zealand | 2023 | 31 | 22±4.3 | 9 days | Passive clear aligners → mean contraction-episode amplitude ↓ (p=0.003); occlusal discomfort ↑ (p=0.048, esp. high-parafunction); TMD symptoms persisted in both groups |
| Tran et al. | Canada | 2020 | 27 | 35.3±17.6 | 4 wk | Mild tooth pain + masticatory muscle soreness across phases; aligner fit perceived more painful than active phase; pain correlated with trait anxiety (r=0.423; p=0.008) |

- **Demographics**: all studies female-predominant; smallest n=10 (Brazil), largest n=31 (New Zealand); follow-up 9 days–8 months.
- **Synthesis**: short-term change in muscle activity from T0→T1, reverting to baseline on later follow-up; pain/discomfort increased; bite force significantly reduced. One study (Manfredini) showed no significant change throughout.
- **Risk of bias**: overall **low**; Liu "unclear" in selection (all-female recruitment); Paes flagged for small sample (n=10). All studies "good methodological quality."

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Only 6 small, heterogeneous studies → low statistical power, no meta-analysis possible (variable design, sample size, follow-up, outcome measures).
- English-only; no grey literature → potential publication/language bias.
- No comparator group (uncontrolled exposure design per PEOS).
- The paper's limitations paragraph internally says "eight studies," but its abstract, PRISMA flow, and Tables 3–4 consistently report **six** — six is the correct count.
- Future work: larger, longer, controlled studies with standardized outcome measurement.

## 7. Glossary

- **CAT** — Clear Aligner Therapy.
- **sEMG** — surface electromyography.
- **MMA / sMMA** — masticatory muscle activity / sleep-time masticatory muscle activity.
- **TA** — anterior temporal muscle; **MM** — masseter; **SCM** — sternocleidomastoid.
- **MVC** — maximal voluntary contraction; **MPP** — mandibular postural position.
- **OBC** — Oral Behavior Checklist; **OD** — occlusal discomfort; **SB** — sleep bruxism.
- **PCA** — passive clear aligners; **DC/TMD, RDC/TMD** — (Research) Diagnostic Criteria for Temporomandibular Disorders.
- **ROB-2** — Cochrane Risk of Bias 2 tool.
- **PEOS** — Population, Exposure, Outcome, Study design (review question framework).
