## Why Ingested

기존 [[implants/soft-tissue/tavelli-2021-soft-tissue-phenotype-modification-peri-implant-nma]]와 [[implants/soft-tissue/jensen-2023-iti-consensus-bone-dimensions-soft-tissue]]는 점막 두께·각화점막 등 연조직 표현형(supracrestal tissue height, STH) 보강을 다루지만, 경점막 지대주 높이(TmAH)와 보철 emergence angle(REA)의 **상호작용**이 변연골소실(MBL)·임플란트주위염에 어떻게 작용하는지는 비어 있었다. 본 후향연구(Misch 2025)는 TmAH×REA를 4그룹으로 교차분류해 "REA ≥30°는 TmAH < 2 mm일 때만 위험인자"라는 조건부 결론을 제시 — STH(=TmAH) 확보가 emergence angle 위험을 상쇄한다는 supracrestal tissue adhesion 개념을 임상 데이터로 확장한다.

## One-line Summary

Retrospective radiographic analysis (192 bone-level implants / 119 patients, mean 7.4-yr follow-up) crossing trans-mucosal abutment height (TmAH) × restoration emergence angle (REA) into 4 groups: Short/Wide-Angle (TmAH <2 mm + REA ≥30°) had the greatest MBL (+0.48 mm vs Long/Narrow, p<0.001) and ~4× higher peri-implantitis (OR 4.19 vs Long/Wide, p=0.013); REA was a significant MBL factor ONLY when TmAH <2 mm, and every 1 mm TmAH increase cut odds of any MBL by 37% (OR 0.63, p=0.003).

## 2. Key Contributions

1. First study to **cross-classify** trans-mucosal abutment height (TmAH, cutoff 2 mm) with restoration emergence angle (REA, cutoff 30°) into 4 groups (LN, LW, SN, SW) and analyze their combined effect on MBL and peri-implantitis around bone-level implants.
2. Demonstrates that **REA ≥30° is a significant MBL risk factor only when TmAH < 2 mm** — reframing emergence angle as a conditional rather than independent risk factor.
3. Quantifies TmAH's protective effect: every 1 mm increase reduces odds of any MBL > 0 mm by ~37% (OR 0.63, p=0.003); REA alone non-significant (OR 0.99, p=0.991).
4. Links short abutment + wide angle (SW) to ~4× higher peri-implantitis and the bulk of implant failures.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: single-center retrospective radiographic analysis (University of Michigan, implants placed/restored 2012-2020).
- **Sample**: 192 bone-level implants in 119 partially edentulous patients; mesial + distal sites grouped independently → 384 sites (78 LN, 61 LW, 83 SN, 162 SW). Mean age 64.1 yr; total follow-up 7.43 ± 2.73 yr.
- **Groups**: LN (TmAH >2 mm, REA <30°), LW (TmAH >2 mm, REA ≥30°), SN (TmAH <2 mm, REA <30°), SW (TmAH <2 mm, REA ≥30°).
- **Measurements**: one calibrated examiner (JM); periapical radiographs at crown placement, T0 (12-18 mo post-crown), T1 (>1 yr after T0); MBL = T0→T1 difference on mesial & distal; calibrated by charted implant length; MiPACS/axiUm; duplicate blinded measurements ≥30 days apart, ICC 0.95.
- **PI definition**: 2017 World Workshop — progressive bone loss >0.5 mm + deepening PD + clinical inflammation.
- **Statistics**: Generalized Estimating Equations (GEE) for non-independence; linear regression (MBL) and binary logistic regression (PI, MBL>0 yes/no); multivariate adjustment for sector, arch, implant length, connection type (the factors non-homogeneous across groups). Post hoc power 86.2%.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Mean MBL by group**: LN 0.26 mm, LW 0.27 mm, SN 0.46 mm, **SW 0.79 mm** (95% CI 0.63-0.94). Group effect p<0.001 (univariate), p=0.001 (multivariate).
- **Adjusted pairwise MBL (vs reference)**: SW vs LN +0.48 mm (p<0.001); SW vs LW +0.43 mm (p=0.001); SW vs SN +0.25 mm (p=0.013); SN vs LN +0.29 mm (p=0.004); LN vs LW NS (+0.08 mm, p=0.444).
- **Probability of MBL > 0 mm**: TmAH only significant covariate, OR 0.63 per mm (95% CI 0.46-0.85, p=0.003, ~37% odds reduction). REA NS (OR 0.99, p=0.991). Interaction REA×TmAH NS (p=0.276). Prevalence MBL>0: LN 35.1%, LW 47.5%, SN 69.9%, SW 67.7%.
- **Peri-implantitis**: prevalence 19.3% patient-level, 18.8% implant-level. SW accounted for 65.7% of all PI; intra-group PI rate SW 25.6% vs LN 13.3%/LW 9.1%/SN 13.2%. SW vs LW OR 4.19 (p=0.013); SW vs LN OR 4.04 (p=0.091). Other PI predictors: periodontitis history (OR 4.81, p=0.018), maintenance visits/yr (OR 2.64, p=0.002); internal hex protective vs external hex (OR 0.17, p=0.024).
- **Failure**: 5 failures, all SW sites (9/10 sites SW), severe PI main cause.
- **Interaction (model interpretation)**: protective effect of +1 mm TmAH on pMBL>0 drops as REA rises — 41% at REA 20°, 30% at 40°, 16% at 60°. TmAH effect is multiplicative.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Retrospective, single-center; initial crown emergence level (and convergent vs divergent transmucosal contour) not documented — a likely confounder.
- Radiographic beam-angulation error inherent (mitigated by Rinn film holders, well-matched pairs, ICC 0.95); all negative bone-level change counted as MBL.
- Paradoxical association of more maintenance visits with greater MBL/PI interpreted as reverse causation (worse disease → more recall).
- Only 5 failures — failure inference limited.
- Future: prospective design, 3D imaging, control for crown emergence position and transmucosal contour.

## 6. Related Work

- Galindo-Moreno et al. 2014 — TmAH >2 mm gives significantly less MBL at 6/18 mo (founding observation).
- Katafuchi et al. — REA >30° → 2× peri-implantitis in bone-level implants.
- Yi et al. — 0.56 mm greater MBL with ≥30° REA (bone-level), NS in tissue-level.
- Strauss et al. — REA effect on MBL time-dependent (significant year 1, gone at 5 yr).
- Derks et al. — ≤1.5 mm prosthetic-margin-to-crest distance → 2.3× moderate/severe PI.
- Linkevicius et al. — vertical mucosal thickness as alternative protective factor (contrast).

## 7. Glossary

- **TmAH (trans-mucosal abutment height)**: vertical height of the prosthetic abutment from implant platform to crown margin through the mucosa; >2 mm protective.
- **REA (restoration emergence angle)**: angle of the crown profile emerging from the implant/abutment relative to the long axis; ≥30° plaque-retentive.
- **MBL (marginal bone loss)**: radiographic loss of crestal bone around the implant from T0 to T1.
- **STAd / STH (supracrestal tissue adhesion / supracrestal tissue height)**: biologic soft-tissue seal between mucosa and implant components; needs vertical space (abutment height) to establish.
- **GEE (Generalized Estimating Equations)**: regression method accounting for non-independence of multiple implants/sites per patient.
- **PI (peri-implantitis)**: pathological progressive peri-implant bone loss with inflammation (2017 World Workshop).
