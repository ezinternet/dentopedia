## Why Ingested

Pairs with the Gothic-arch / jaw-relation-record cluster — [[wiki/occlusion/kwak-2024-masticatory-positioning-occlusal-stability-full-mouth]] (where mandibular positioning underpins full-mouth occlusal stability) — by answering the upstream question: *which CR-recording technique most reproducibly captures the condylar position?* It complements [[wiki/complete-denture/fayad-2025-vertical-dimension-determination-prosthodontics-review]]: VD fixes the vertical jaw relation, CR fixes the horizontal/centric jaw relation, and both are needed for a maxillomandibular record. The SR's bottom line (bimanual ≥ chin-point guidance for reproducibility in dentate, non-TMD patients) gives the wiki a technique-level evidence anchor for jaw-relation records.

According to PubMed, this article (de Moraes Melo Neto et al., 2021, *European Journal of Dentistry*; [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1055/s-0041-1735903); PMID 34921385; PMCID PMC9339936) is a systematic review comparing CR techniques within the same method-of-obtaining-CR.

## One-line Summary

Systematic review (6 low-quality clinical studies, complete-dentition non-TMD subjects) comparing centric-relation recording techniques *within the same method*: every included study compared only bimanual manipulation vs chin-point guidance (guided method) — 3 found bimanual more reproducible, 2 found them equivalent, 1 favored chin-point — so bimanual manipulation is more recommended.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(6편, 모두 저질·완전치열·비TMD 대상)로 중심위(CR) 획득 술식을 같은 방법(method) 내에서 비교 — 포함된 6편 모두 유도법(guided)의 양손조작(bimanual) 대 턱끝유도(chin-point guidance)만 비교했고, 3편은 양손조작이, 2편은 동등, 1편은 턱끝유도가 과두위치 재현성이 더 높다고 보고 → 양손조작법이 더 권장됨.

## 1. Document Information

- **Type**: Systematic review (PRISMA; PROSPERO CRD42018097285)
- **Journal**: European Journal of Dentistry, 2021
- **DOI**: [10.1055/s-0041-1735903](https://doi.org/10.1055/s-0041-1735903) · PMID 34921385 · PMCID PMC9339936
- **Source**: PubMed Central full text (JATS), retrieved via PubMed
- **Question (PICO)**: In individuals undergoing CR-recording techniques, comparing techniques *within the same method* (guided / graphic / physiologic), which technique yields the greatest reproducibility of condylar (mandibular) position in CR?
- **Companion review**: A separate de Moraes Melo Neto SR compared techniques *across different* methods; this one is restricted to *within-method* comparisons.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Establishes that, despite three theoretical CR-recording method families, the *within-method* evidence base is almost entirely the guided method, and within it the bimanual-vs-chin-point question.
- Synthesizes the direction of evidence: bimanual manipulation is most often the more (or equally) reproducible technique for the condylar position in CR.
- Exposes evidence gaps: no studies compare graphic (intraoral vs extraoral gothic arch) or physiologic (swallowing vs tongue-retrusion) techniques head-to-head, and none study edentulous patients.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane Library, SciELO, Scopus, Web of Science; up to May 5, 2021.
- **Search terms**: "dental centric relation" (MeSH) × each of "reproducibility of findings", "jaw relation record", "chin point", "gothic arch", "bimanual manipulation", "swallowing", "jig".
- **Methods & their techniques considered**:
  - Guided — bimanual manipulation; chin-point guidance (traditional + modified three-finger).
  - Graphic — intraoral and extraoral gothic-arch tracing.
  - Physiologic — swallowing; tongue retrusion along the palate.
- **Inclusion**: English clinical studies; no-TMD subjects; complete/near-complete dentition or complete edentulism; ≥5 subjects; ≥2 techniques of the same method compared on CR reproducibility.
- **Exclusion**: RPD wearers; undisclosed dental condition; incomplete data; reviews/SRs/case reports.
- **Quality**: Jadad scale (0–2 low, 3–5 high). Reviewer agreement: Kappa.
- **Data extracted**: sex/age, sample size, dental condition, techniques, randomization, period of day, patient position, number of records, apparatus, number of operators.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- **Screening**: 1692 identified → 12 full-text (Kappa 1.00) → **6 included** (Kappa 1.00).
- **Quality**: all 6 studies **low quality** (Jadad max 2). Only Simon & Nicholls and Teo & Wise randomized. Double-blinding not applicable (operator must know the technique).
- **Population**: all complete-dentition; **none edentulous**.
- **All 6 comparisons = bimanual vs chin-point guidance (guided method only).**

| Study | n | Finding |
|---|---|---|
| Kantor et al | 15 | Bimanual > chin-point |
| Hobo & Iwata | 10 | Bimanual > chin-point |
| Keshvad & Winstanley | 14 | Bimanual > chin-point |
| Simon & Nicholls | 5 | No difference |
| Galeković et al | 32 | No difference |
| Teo & Wise | 7 | Chin-point > bimanual |

- **Tally**: 3 bimanual-superior, 2 equivalent, 1 chin-point-superior.
- **Mechanistic rationale**: bimanual uses both hands to stabilize/guide the mandible (lower manipulation-error chance) vs one-handed chin-point. Guided-method techniques are more operator-dependent than graphic/physiologic; operator experience matters.
- **Methodological notes**: patient position (supine → mandible more posterior via gravity) and time-of-day can shift condylar position; one operator should perform all techniques; a Lucia jig (5–20 min across studies) deprograms proprioceptive memory and relaxes masticatory muscles before recording.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- All included studies low quality; only 2/6 randomized.
- Heterogeneous measurement devices → no statistical pooling / meta-analysis (qualitative synthesis only).
- No studies comparing graphic or physiologic techniques; no studies on edentulous patients.
- Frequent missing reporting (operator count, patient position, jig duration, period of day).
- Future studies should standardize operator, position, time-of-day, and report jig duration.

## 7. Glossary

- **Centric relation (CR)**: maxillomandibular relationship, independent of tooth contact, with condyles in the anterior-superior position against the posterior slopes of the articular eminences; a repeatable reference position.
- **Bimanual (bilateral) manipulation**: guided CR technique stabilizing the mandible with both of the operator's hands (Dawson technique).
- **Chin-point guidance**: guided CR technique stabilizing/guiding the mandible via the chin with one hand.
- **Gothic-arch tracing**: graphic CR method recording mandibular border movements (intraoral or extraoral) as an arrow/apex.
- **Lucia jig**: anterior deprogramming device that removes posterior occlusal contacts to relax muscles and deprogram proprioceptive memory before CR recording.
- **Jadad scale**: 5-point trial-quality scale (randomization, double-blinding, dropouts).
