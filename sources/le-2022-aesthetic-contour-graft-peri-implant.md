## Why Ingested

기존 [[implants/soft-tissue/sanz-martin-2022-buccal-soft-tissue-dehiscence-implants-sr-ma]]는 연조직 결손 관리에서 CTG 등 연조직 이식 위주의 근거를 제공하지만, GBR을 통해 하방 골 윤곽을 보강함으로써 연조직 두께와 출현 윤곽을 개선한다는 "Aesthetic Contour Graft" 개념은 별도로 정리가 필요함. 본 임상 논문은 GBR이 연조직 이식을 대체 또는 보완할 수 있다는 임상적 프레임을 제시한다.

## One-line Summary

Clinical article describing the "Aesthetic Contour Graft" concept — using GBR (particulate allograft + collagen membrane) to augment underlying labial/ridge bone contour and thereby enhance peri-implant soft tissue emergence profiles and pontic sites, sometimes obviating separate soft tissue grafts.

## 한줄요약

임상 논문: GBR로 순측 골 윤곽을 증대("Aesthetic Contour Graft")함으로써 임플란트 주위 연조직 출현 윤곽 및 폰틱 부위 심미성을 개선하며, 경우에 따라 별도의 연조직 이식 없이도 2-3 mm 연조직 두께 확보 가능.

## 2. Key Contributions

- Coins the term "Aesthetic Contour Graft" for GBR procedures specifically designed to improve labial bone contour in support of peri-implant and pontic-site soft tissue architecture.
- Establishes the principle: **most peri-implant soft tissue deficiencies represent an underlying bone defect** — correcting the bone corrects the soft tissue.
- Demonstrates that GBR with particulate mineralized allograft + collagen membrane can achieve and maintain ≥2–3 mm labial soft tissue thickness, potentially eliminating the need for connective tissue grafts.
- Covers both staged (GBR before implant placement) and simultaneous (GBR at time of implant placement) approaches.
- Addresses pontic site development: GBR to support ovate pontic emergence profiles in edentulous spans.

## 3. Methodology and Architecture

- **Design**: Clinical article with technique description, illustrative cases, and supporting literature review.
- **Key thresholds cited**:
  - Minimum 2–3 mm vertical soft tissue thickness for emergence profile.
  - ≥2 mm vertical mucosa to protect against crestal bone loss.
  - 2–3 mm horizontal soft tissue thickness for reduced recession and esthetics.
  - ≥2 mm labial bone labial to implant platform after remodeling (overcorrect for anticipated resorption).
- **Technique elements**:
  - Particulate mineralized allograft + resorbable collagen membrane (GBR).
  - Tenting screws to maintain ridge contour and prevent graft collapse.
  - Healing abutment (non-submerged approach) to tent peri-implant soft tissue.
  - Staged: GBR → implant placement after healing.
  - Simultaneous: implant + GBR in one stage (suitable for mild-moderate defects).
  - Pontic site: GBR ridge augmentation prior to or at time of bridge delivery.

## 4. Key Results and Benchmarks

- Case examples show stable peri-implant soft tissue contours at 8-year follow-up after simultaneous bone grafting with healing abutment (congenitally missing lateral incisor cases).
- Literature cited shows bone regeneration of 100% and 79.3% for small and medium buccal dehiscences respectively using mineralized allograft + collagen membrane; large defects showed only partial improvement.
- High correlation between labial crestal soft tissue thickness and underlying labial bone thickness around maxillary anterior implants — supporting the bone-drives-soft-tissue thesis.
- Implants with cingulum angulation had significantly more crestal soft tissue thickness (2.98 mm) vs labial angulation (1.71 mm) — implant position directly affects soft tissue.

## 5. Limitations and Future Work

- Case series / technique article only — no controlled RCT data comparing Aesthetic Contour Graft vs CTG in matched patients.
- Graft resorption variability means outcomes depend on extent of overcorrection; quantified outcomes not systematically reported.
- No histological data in this article.
- Author calls for further prospective studies on GBR-only vs GBR+CTG combinations.

## 6. Related Work

- Labial bone–soft tissue correlation: Tsuda et al. (referenced, high correlation between labial bone thickness and crestal soft tissue thickness).
- Simultaneous bone grafting at implant placement: Benic & Hämmerle (submerged protocol); Cosyn et al. (non-submerged with healing abutment).
- CTG for soft tissue augmentation: classic references for 2 mm labial tissue thickness with CTG.
- Pontic site development: traditional ovate pontic tissue conditioning.

## 7. Glossary

- **Aesthetic Contour Graft**: Author-coined term for GBR performed to restore/enhance labial ridge contour and thereby support peri-implant soft tissue volume and emergence profile.
- **Emergence profile**: The contour of a restoration as it transitions from the implant platform through the peri-implant tissue to the visible crown.
- **GBR (Guided Bone Regeneration)**: Barrier membrane + bone graft technique to promote localized osseous regeneration.
- **Tenting screw**: Fixation screw placed to physically tent the membrane/graft away from the bone surface, maintaining space for regeneration.
- **Ovate pontic**: A pontic design with a convex tissue surface that presses into the edentulous ridge to create a lifelike soft tissue contour.
- **Staged approach**: GBR performed as a separate procedure before implant placement; allows maximum augmentation volume without compromising implant position.
- **Non-submerged healing**: Implant placed with a healing abutment exposed through the soft tissue (vs buried beneath it), reducing the number of surgical stages.
