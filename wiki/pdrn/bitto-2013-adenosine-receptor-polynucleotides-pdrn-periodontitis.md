## One-line Summary
Rat experimental periodontitis study showing PDRN gel reduces periodontal inflammation and apoptosis via A2A adenosine receptor stimulation; the A2A antagonist DMPX abolishes the effect — the foundational mechanistic anchor for PDRN's anti-inflammatory action.

## 한줄요약
랫 실험적 치주염 모델: PDRN 젤이 A2A 아데노신 수용체를 통해 염증·세포자멸을 억제(항염), A2A 길항제 DMPX가 효과를 소거 — PDRN 항염 기전의 원전 근거.

## Summary
Rat experimental periodontitis study demonstrating that PDRN reduces periodontal inflammation and apoptosis through A2A adenosine receptor stimulation; receptor blockade (DMPX) abolishes the effect, proving the mechanism is receptor-mediated.

## Key Contributions
The mechanistic anchor for PDRN's anti-inflammatory action: A2A adenosine receptor agonism. Later dental PDRN literature (bone/soft-tissue regeneration, peri-implant adjunct) repeatedly cites this A2A mechanism.

## Methodology
Experimental periodontitis induced by ligating the lower-left first molar cervix in rats (sham: no ligature). After 7 days: PDRN gel vs vehicle ± DMPX (A2A antagonist). Histology and inflammatory/apoptotic protein expression.

## Results
- Vehicle EPD: severe gingival/PDL infiltrate; ↑ p-JNK, p-ERK, TNF-α, IL-6, HMGB-1, BAX; ↓ Bcl-2.
- PDRN gel: restored histology, blunted inflammatory/apoptotic proteins, preserved Bcl-2.
- DMPX abrogated PDRN's effects → A2A-receptor mediated.
- Limitation: rat periodontitis model; mechanism generalization to peri-implant/sinus contexts is inferential.
