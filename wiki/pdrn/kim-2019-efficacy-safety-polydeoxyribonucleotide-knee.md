## One-line Summary
SR/MA of 5 RCTs: intra-articular PDRN beat hyaluronic acid on knee-OA pain at 1 and 2 months (P=0.04, 0.02) but not 4 months; function (KOOS/KSS) and adverse events were equivalent. Non-dental — dental extrapolation is interpretive.

## Summary
This systematic review and meta-analysis pooled five RCTs comparing intra-articular PDRN with hyaluronic acid (HA) for knee osteoarthritis. PDRN produced significantly better pain relief than HA at 1 and 2 months, with the difference gone by 4 months; function (KOOS, KSS) and adverse events were equivalent. It is the highest-grade clinical evidence for PDRN overall — five RCTs — but in a non-dental joint. It is recorded here as cross-domain support for PDRN's short-term analgesic effect and benign safety profile behind the dental TMJ intra-articular prolotherapy cohort, not as a dental indication.

## Key Contributions
- RCT-level pooled evidence that intra-articular PDRN is at least as good as HA, with short-term pain advantage.
- Establishes a benign safety signal (adverse-event RR n.s.) relevant to any injectable PDRN use.
- Bounds the analgesic benefit to ≤2 months.

## Methodology
- MEDLINE/EMBASE/Cochrane (2018-11); intra-articular PDRN vs HA RCTs.
- Primary VAS; secondary KOOS, KSS, adverse events; 5 RCTs pooled.

## Results
- Pain: PDRN > HA at 1 mo (P=0.04) and 2 mo (P=0.02); n.s. at 4 mo.
- Function (KOOS/KSS): n.s. at all timepoints.
- Adverse events: RR 2.15 (95% CI 0.17–26.67, P=0.55).
