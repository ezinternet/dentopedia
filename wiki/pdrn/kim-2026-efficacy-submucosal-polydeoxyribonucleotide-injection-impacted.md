## One-line Summary

Split-mouth RCT of submucosal PDRN injection after bilateral impacted mandibular third molar (IMTM) extraction: postoperative pain was significantly lower on the PDRN side at POD3 and POD7 (P < 0.05), while pain duration did not differ (a split-mouth attribution limitation) — the first human evaluation of PDRN for oral-surgery postoperative outcomes.

## Summary

This split-mouth randomized controlled trial evaluates whether submucosal PDRN injection after impacted mandibular third molar (IMTM) extraction reduces postoperative pain, swelling, and complication burden. Patients with bilateral IMTM received PDRN on the experimental side and saline (or no injection) on the control side. Postoperative pain intensity was significantly lower on the PDRN side at POD3 and POD7. Pain duration did not differ — likely a methodological consequence of split-mouth design, since patients struggle to attribute pain duration to a specific side. Swelling was measured using the Laskin method. The authors note this is the **first human evaluation of PDRN for oral-surgery postoperative outcomes**, where prior evidence was confined to animal histomorphometry of bone formation.

## Key Contributions

- First human RCT of PDRN in oral surgery — bridges the gap from animal-model bone histomorphometry to human postoperative clinical outcomes.
- Statistically meaningful pain reduction at POD3 and POD7 supports an analgesic / anti-inflammatory adjunctive role for PDRN beyond its osteogenic indications.
- Identifies a split-mouth limitation for pain-duration measurement that future trials should avoid (e.g., parallel-group design).

## Methodology

- Design: split-mouth RCT (bilateral IMTM).
- Intervention: submucosal PDRN injection on the experimental side, saline/no injection on the control side, after IMTM extraction.
- Outcomes: pain intensity (VAS at POD1, POD3, POD7), pain duration, swelling by Laskin method (sum of two linear facial measurements).
- Setting: Wonju Severance Christian Hospital, OMS department.

## Results

- Pain intensity: PDRN side significantly lower at POD3 and POD7 (P < 0.05).
- Pain duration: no significant difference between sides — attributed to split-mouth attribution difficulty.
- Swelling: assessed by Laskin method; full numerical results in main text.
- No serious adverse events reported.
