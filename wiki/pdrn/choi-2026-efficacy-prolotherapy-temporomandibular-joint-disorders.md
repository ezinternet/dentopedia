## Summary
A retrospective chart review of 66 conventional-treatment-refractory TMD patients who received prolotherapy with hypertonic dextrose or PDRN (average 2.3 sessions, March–December 2024). Pain fell from VAS 4.34±2.12 to 1.00±1.58 and maximum mouth opening rose from 31.0±8.7 to 40.8±4.55 mm (both p<0.001). Joint sounds, jaw displacement (69.6%→13.0%) and deflection (52.2%→8.7%) improved significantly. PDRN and dextrose groups both improved with no significant intergroup difference. This is the first human cohort backing the same group's narrative claims, but it is uncontrolled and does not isolate PDRN from dextrose.

## Key Contributions
- First human cohort quantifying TMD prolotherapy outcomes; elevates the TMJ indication from narrative to retrospective evidence.
- PDRN ≈ dextrose efficacy → supports PDRN as an interchangeable proliferant with better tolerability.
- Documents structural/functional gains (joint sounds, deviation, deflection) beyond pain.

## Methodology
- 66 TMD patients, classified soft-tissue-related vs bone-related.
- Intra-articular dextrose or PDRN; VAS, MMO, joint sounds, S deviation/L deflection at baseline, per session, final follow-up.
- Paired t-test, McNemar, ANOVA, regression.

## Results
- VAS 4.34→1.00; MMO 31.0→40.8 mm (p<0.001).
- Joint sounds resolved in 23; displacement 69.6→13.0%, deflection 52.2→8.7% (p<0.001).
- No significant PDRN vs dextrose difference.
