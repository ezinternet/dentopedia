## 한줄요약

Mongrel dog 4마리 IIP 모델에서 SCTG / VCMX / VCMX+PDRN / control 비교. PDRN은 soft-tissue volume gain에는 효과 없음. KT height에서만 우위. Wound dehiscence가 빈번해 결과 해석 제한.

## Summary

This canine study compared four soft-tissue augmentation modalities in the immediate implant placement (IIP) protocol: no augmentation, subepithelial connective tissue graft (SCTG), volume-stable collagen matrix (VCMX), and VCMX with polydeoxyribonucleotide (PDRN). Four mongrel dogs received implants in distal sockets of the third and fourth mandibular premolars after extraction, with buccal gaps filled with synthetic bone substitute. After 4 months, histomorphometry and STL superimposition were analyzed. SCTG produced the highest tissue thickness at and above the implant platform; VCMX-based groups behaved similarly below it. PDRN did not produce additional volume gain — its main contribution was in KT height (VCMX + PDRN > SCTG, VCMX). Wound dehiscence occurred in more than half of test sites, likely from over-grafting.

## Key Contributions

- **Conflicts with the box-type defect signal** ([[pdrn/lim-2023-xenogeneic-collagen-matrix-polydeoxyribonucleotide-gingival-phenotype]]): in the IIP scenario, PDRN did not add volume gain.
- Confirms PDRN's KT-height advantage seen elsewhere ([[pdrn/kim-2025-preclinical-investigation-collagen-matrix-polydeoxyribonucleotide]]).
- Identifies wound dehiscence as a dominant confounder in IIP soft-tissue augmentation studies — calls for restraint on graft volume.

## Methodology

- 4 mongrel dogs, IIP after distal root extraction (3rd/4th mandibular premolars).
- Buccal gap filled with synthetic bone substitute.
- 4 split-mouth groups: control / SCTG / VCMX / VCMX + PDRN.
- 4 months later: histomorphometry + STL superimposition for tissue change.

## Results

- Above and at implant platform level: tissue thickness highest in SCTG.
- Below implant platform: SCTG ≈ VCMX-based groups.
- KT height: VCMX + PDRN > SCTG, VCMX.
- STL: all groups showed soft-tissue volume **decrease**, including PDRN-treated sites.
- Wound dehiscence in more than half of test sites; secondary wound healing achieved.
- Authors' conclusion: "PDRN appears not to have a positive effect on the soft-tissue volume gain."
