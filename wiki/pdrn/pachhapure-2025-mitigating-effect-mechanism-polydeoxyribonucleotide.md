## One-line Summary
In vitro (HGF-1 gingival fibroblasts): PDRN 100 µg/mL prevents and reverses zoledronic acid (50 µM)-induced growth suppression by inhibiting TBK1 phosphorylation, partially restoring p-PKB, and attenuating ROS — a BRONJ-relevant cytoprotective mechanism.

## Summary
Zoledronic acid (ZA), a nitrogen-containing bisphosphonate, suppresses oral mucosal cells and is linked to BRONJ. In HGF-1 human gingival fibroblasts, ZA (50 µM, 48 h) inhibited growth with reduced p-PKB and p-STAT3 and increased p-TBK1; TBK1 silencing restored growth, identifying TBK1 as a toxicity driver. PDRN co-treatment (100 µg/mL) prevented and reversed ZA cytotoxicity, inhibited ZA-induced TBK1 phosphorylation, partially restored p-PKB (but not p-STAT3), and attenuated the ZA-induced ROS rise; delayed PDRN still reversed growth inhibition dose- and time-dependently. This provides the first molecular mechanism for PDRN cytoprotection in a MRONJ-relevant model.

## Key Contributions
- First mechanistic (TBK1/PKB/ROS) account of PDRN protecting gingival fibroblasts from bisphosphonate toxicity.
- Identifies TBK1 phosphorylation as the actionable node and PDRN as a TBK1-suppressing, ROS-attenuating agent.
- Even delayed PDRN reverses ZA damage — relevant to treating, not just preventing, mucosal toxicity.

## Methodology
- HGF-1 cells; ZA 50 µM 48 h ± PDRN 100 µg/mL (pre/co/delayed).
- Western blot (p-PKB, p-STAT3, p-TBK1), siRNA silencing, ROS assay, NAC comparison.

## Results
- ZA: ↓growth, ↓p-PKB, ↓p-STAT3, ↑p-TBK1; TBK1 silencing rescues growth.
- PDRN: prevents/reverses ZA toxicity, ↓p-TBK1, partial ↑p-PKB, ↓ROS; delayed PDRN dose/time-dependent rescue.
