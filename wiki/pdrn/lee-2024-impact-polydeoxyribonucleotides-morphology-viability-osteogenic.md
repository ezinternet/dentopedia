## 한줄요약

GMSC spheroid in vitro에서 PDRN의 osteogenic effect 검증. D14, 75 μg/mL에서 calcium deposition 유의 증가. RUNX2는 25 μg/mL, COL1A1은 75 μg/mL에서 peak — marker별로 최적 농도가 다름.

## Summary

This in vitro study tested polydeoxyribonucleotide (PDRN) on the morphology, viability, and osteogenic differentiation of human gingiva-derived mesenchymal stem cell (GMSC) spheroids. Spheroid size decreased over time and cell viability declined as expected in long-term culture, independent of PDRN concentration — i.e. no cytotoxicity. Calcium deposition increased significantly at PDRN 75 μg/mL by Day 14, indicating enhanced mineralization. mRNA expression revealed a **marker-specific dose-response**: RUNX2 peaked at 25 μg/mL while COL1A1 peaked at 75 μg/mL. RNA sequencing identified osteogenesis-related clusters of differentially expressed genes between PDRN-treated and control spheroids.

## Key Contributions

- Confirms PDRN is **non-cytotoxic** across the tested dose range in GMSC spheroids.
- Demonstrates a **marker-specific dose-response** for osteogenic differentiation — undermines any notion of a single "optimal" PDRN concentration.
- Adds mechanistic depth (RNA-seq pathway enrichment) to the otherwise tissue-level evidence in the PDRN literature.

## Methodology

- GMSC isolated and cultured as 3D spheroids.
- PDRN concentrations: control / 25 / 50 / 75 / 100 μg/mL (approximate, per abstract).
- Time points: Day 7 and Day 14.
- Outcomes: spheroid diameter, cell viability, ALP staining, calcium deposition (alizarin red), qPCR (RUNX2, COL1A1), RNA-seq with hierarchical clustering + functional enrichment.

## Results

- Spheroid size: progressive decrease over time, no concentration effect.
- Cell viability: long-term decline, no concentration effect → no cytotoxicity.
- Calcium deposition: significantly higher at D14 with PDRN 75 μg/mL.
- RUNX2 mRNA: peak at 25 μg/mL.
- COL1A1 mRNA: peak at 75 μg/mL.
- RNA-seq: distinct osteogenic gene clusters with PDRN treatment.
