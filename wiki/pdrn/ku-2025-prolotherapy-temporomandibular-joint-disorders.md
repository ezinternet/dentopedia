Narrative review of TMJ prolotherapy: dextrose is the most validated proliferant, PDRN gives comparable efficacy with less discomfort and shorter intervals; pain reduction and improved mouth opening are consistent, adverse events minimal — but no RCTs.

## Summary
This narrative review summarizes prolotherapy — intra-/peri-articular injection of proliferants such as hypertonic dextrose or PDRN — for refractory temporomandibular joint disorders. Mechanistically, prolotherapy promotes fibroblast activation, collagen synthesis, and ligamentous stabilization. Dextrose is the most validated agent; PDRN provides comparable efficacy with less injection discomfort and shorter treatment intervals. Clinical data consistently report reduced pain and improved maximum mouth opening, with preliminary imaging of subchondral bone remodeling and minimal, transient adverse events. The author frames prolotherapy as a conservative TMD modality pending controlled trials for long-term and structural outcomes.

## Key Contributions
- Positions PDRN against dextrose as a TMJ proliferant: equal efficacy, better tolerability, shorter intervals — but dextrose better evidenced.
- Catalogs the mechanism (fibroblast/collagen/ligament) and the conservative-care placement of prolotherapy.
- Pairs with a same-group retrospective cohort that supplies the supporting numbers.

## Methodology
- Narrative review; no PRISMA search, no inclusion table, no risk-of-bias assessment.

## Results
- Qualitative: pain ↓ and MMO ↑ across chronic/degenerative TMJ cases; adverse events minimal/transient; PDRN ≈ dextrose.
