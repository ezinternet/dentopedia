## Summary

This canine preclinical study tested whether xenogeneic collagen matrix (XCM) soaked with polydeoxyribonucleotide (PDRN, 2.0 mg/mL) can match the gingival-phenotype-modification performance of subepithelial connective tissue graft (SCTG). Five mongrel dogs received bilateral box-type gingival defects on maxillary canines, allowed to heal for 8 weeks, then augmented with either SCTG or XCM + PDRN under a coronally positioned flap. Twelve weeks later, STL superimposition and histometry showed no significant difference between groups across gingival thickness increase, supracrestal gingival tissue height, KT height, tissue thickness, or rete peg density. The authors concluded XCM + PDRN can produce SCTG-comparable phenotype modification — with the caveat that an XCM-only positive control was not included.

## Key Contributions

- First direct head-to-head of SCTG vs PDRN-loaded XCM on phenotype modification in a controlled defect model.
- Findings support the idea that PDRN may compensate for the inherent disadvantage of substitute materials relative to autograft for soft-tissue augmentation.
- Notes the methodological gap (no XCM-only arm) — limits attribution of effect to PDRN alone.

## Methodology

- 5 mongrel dogs; bilateral box-type gingival defects on maxillary canines.
- 8-week defect maturation, then augmentation with SCTG or XCM + PDRN (2.0 mg/mL) under coronally positioned flap.
- 12-week sacrifice.
- Outcomes: intraoral scan + STL superimposition (thickness change); histometry for STGTH, KT height, tissue thickness, rete peg density.

## Results

- Gingival thickness increase (STL): SCTG 0.69 ± 0.25 to 0.80 ± 0.31 mm; PDRN 0.48 ± 0.25 to 0.85 ± 0.44 mm. P > 0.05.
- Histometric parameters: all comparable between groups.
- One animal had wound dehiscence (analyzed n=4).
