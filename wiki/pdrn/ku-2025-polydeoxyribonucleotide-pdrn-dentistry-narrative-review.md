## One-line Summary

Narrative review of PDRN (50–1500 kDa salmon/trout sperm-derived DNA fragments) in dentistry: maps its dual mechanism (A2A receptor signaling + nucleotide salvage pathway) onto adjunctive applications across GBR, soft-tissue, TMJ, peri-implantitis, and MRONJ, concluding that consistent preclinical signals are not yet matched by RCT-grade evidence for protocolization.

## Summary

PDRN is a mixture of DNA fragments (50–1500 kDa) extracted from salmon/trout sperm. Its biological activity is attributed mainly to two converging pathways: (1) generation of adenosine that activates the A2A receptor — anti-inflammatory, pro-angiogenic, vasodilatory; (2) provision of nucleotides through the salvage pathway — supporting DNA synthesis in proliferating cells. In dentistry, this dual mechanism has been translated into adjunctive applications across regenerative bone surgery, soft-tissue management, joint disorders, and inflammatory bone diseases. The review concludes that while preclinical signals are consistent, the absence of well-designed RCTs prevents evidence-based protocolization.

## Key Contributions

- Defines the operational boundary between **PDRN** (<1500 kDa, short/medium chains) and **PN** (>1500 kDa, long chains).
- Maps PDRN action onto two molecular axes: **A2A receptor signaling** (functional anti-inflammatory + angiogenic effect) and **salvage pathway** (substrate provision for proliferation).
- Catalogs dental indications:
  - GBR / bone graft scaffold soaking → enhanced osteogenesis + vascularization in preclinical bone defects
  - TMJ disorders → anti-inflammatory + chondroprotective signal
  - Neuropathic / postoperative pain modulation
  - Peri-implantitis adjunct
  - MRONJ — supportive role in soft- and hard-tissue healing
- Frames the evidence gap: most reports are preclinical or small clinical series; no large RCT has yet defined optimal dose, vehicle, or timing.

## Methodology

Narrative format. No PRISMA-style search, no inclusion/exclusion table, no risk-of-bias assessment. Reference selection at author discretion. This is the principal methodological limitation.

## Results

No quantitative synthesis. The review summarizes mechanism evidence (in vitro adenosine signaling, A2A receptor pharmacology), preclinical evidence (animal bone regeneration with PDRN-soaked grafts), and selected case reports / small clinical series for each indication.
