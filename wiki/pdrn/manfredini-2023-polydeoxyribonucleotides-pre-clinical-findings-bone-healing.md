## One-line Summary

Scoping review of PDRN preclinical bone-healing evidence (in vitro + animal): PDRN consistently raised osteoblast proliferation, ALP activity, and mineralization in vitro, and improved new-bone area, vascularization, and BIC in animal defect models, but heterogeneity in dose, vehicle, and timing precluded meta-analysis.

## Summary

This scoping review maps the preclinical evidence base for polydeoxyribonucleotide (PDRN) in bone healing, including in vitro osteoblast/MSC studies and animal models. Across in vitro work, PDRN consistently raised osteoblastic proliferation, alkaline phosphatase activity, and mineralization. In animal work, PDRN-soaked grafts produced higher new-bone area, vascularization, and BIC than controls in calvarial, femoral, and peri-implant defect models. The authors emphasize heterogeneity in PDRN dose, application vehicle, and outcome timing — meta-analysis was not attempted.

## Key Contributions

- Positions PDRN within the broader landscape of GBR adjuncts, contrasting it with autogenous graft limitations (donor morbidity, intraoral availability).
- Separates **in vitro** and **animal** evidence streams to clarify what is known mechanistically versus what is known at tissue scale.
- Documents the methodological heterogeneity that currently blocks formal meta-analysis.

## Methodology

- Scoping review framework with defined database search and inclusion criteria (PRISMA-ScR style).
- No quantitative synthesis. No formal RoB assessment (consistent with scoping format).

## Results

- In vitro: consistent reports of increased osteoblast proliferation, ALP activity, and mineralization with PDRN.
- Animal: PDRN-soaked grafts outperformed control grafts on new-bone metrics in multiple defect models.
- Substantial heterogeneity in concentration (μg/mL to mg/mL), vehicle (soaking vs. injection vs. scaffold loading), and follow-up.
