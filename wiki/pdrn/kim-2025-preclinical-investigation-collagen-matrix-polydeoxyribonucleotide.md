## 한줄요약

Canine 5마리 buccally positioned implant 모델에서 FGG/XCM/XCM+PDRN 2mg/4mg 비교. PDRN 2 mg/mL이 FGG와 비슷한 KT 형성을 보였으나 FGG가 clinical KT height에서 여전히 우위. 4 mg/mL은 추가 이득 없음.

## Summary

A canine preclinical study testing whether xenogeneic collagen matrix (XCM) loaded with polydeoxyribonucleotide (PDRN) can match the keratinized-tissue (KT) regeneration achieved by autogenous free gingival graft (FGG) at buccally positioned implants with a thin buccal plate. Four implants per mandible were placed in five mongrel dogs and randomized to one of four soft-tissue protocols: FGG, XCM alone, XCM + PDRN 2 mg/mL (PDRN2), XCM + PDRN 4 mg/mL (PDRN4). Histomorphometric KT formation in PDRN2 approached FGG; PDRN4 offered no additional benefit. FGG still produced the greatest clinically measured KT height.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates that **PDRN concentration does not scale linearly** with KT regeneration outcome — 2 mg/mL ≈ 4 mg/mL on KT measures.
- Suggests a viable PDRN-loaded XCM combination as an alternative to autograft when palatal donor morbidity is a concern.
- Adds a dose-response data point to the [[pdrn/lim-2023-xenogeneic-collagen-matrix-polydeoxyribonucleotide-gingival-phenotype]] line of work.

## Methodology

- 5 mongrel dogs, edentulous mandible.
- 4 implants per mandible (n=20), intentionally placed buccally to thin the buccal plate.
- 4 groups, split-mouth: FGG / XCM / PDRN2 / PDRN4.
- Outcomes: clinical KT, histometric KT height, histometric KT length.

## Results

- Clinical KT height: FGG 4.70 ± 1.00 mm > XCM 3.53 ± 0.60 mm.
- Histometric KT height: FGG 3.64 ± 0.76 mm > XCM 3.19 ± 0.50 mm.
- Histometric KT length: FGG 5.22 ± 1.43 mm > XCM 4.71 ± 1.15 mm.
- PDRN2 reached histomorphometric outcomes comparable to FGG; PDRN4 did not add further improvement.
- XCM initial dimension exceeded FGG (8 vs 6 mm) to anticipate higher shrinkage — direct group comparisons must therefore account for this.
