## One-line Summary
Beagle ARP (8 dogs, 2/4/12 wk): PDRN-soaked graft+membrane increased new bone (coronal/middle/total) at 2 wk and new blood vessels at 2–4 wk versus saline-soaked ARP — an early bone/angiogenesis acceleration.

## 한줄요약
비글 8마리 발치와보존술. PDRN soaking이 2주 신생골과 2~4주 신생혈관을 대조보다 증가 — sinus·dehiscence 연구와 같은 초기 가속 패턴.

## Summary
In eight beagles, mandibular premolar sockets received alveolar ridge preservation with collagenized alloplastic graft and a bilayer collagen membrane soaked for 10 minutes in either saline (ARP) or PDRN (ARP/PDRN), with 2/4/12-week subgroups. The PDRN group showed greater new bone formation at coronal, middle, and total positions at 2 weeks, and a higher number of newly formed blood vessels at 2 and 4 weeks. The signal concentrates early, echoing the early-bone-acceleration pattern reported in immediate-implant dehiscence and rabbit endosinus models.

## Key Contributions
- First preclinical evidence for PDRN as an ARP/socket adjunct.
- Documents an angiogenesis component (more new vessels at 2–4 wk), bridging the A2A pro-angiogenic mechanism to a socket-healing outcome.
- Adds a third independent dataset (socket) to the recurring "early acceleration, narrowing later" PDRN bone pattern.

## Methodology
- 8 beagles, mandibular P2/P3/P4 sockets.
- ARP with collagenized alloplastic graft + bilayer collagen membrane, soaked 10 min in saline vs PDRN.
- 2/4/12-wk subgroups; histomorphometry (primary) + micro-CT + 3D volumetry.

## Results
- New bone (histomorphometry): ARP/PDRN > ARP at coronal/middle/total, 2 wk.
- New blood vessels: ARP/PDRN > ARP at 2 and 4 wk.
- micro-CT mean new bone higher with PDRN at early timepoints.
