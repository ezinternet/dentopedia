## 한줄요약

## Summary

This study tested whether soaking a collagenated biphasic calcium phosphate (CBCP) graft in polydeoxyribonucleotide (PDRN) improves early bone formation when bone augmentation is performed simultaneously with immediate implant placement into a buccal dehiscence defect. Six male beagles received 3D-printed implants in P3/P4 sites with surgically created 5-mm dehiscence defects, grafted with either CBCP + collagen membrane (control) or CBCP soaked in PDRN + collagen membrane (test). Outcomes were assessed by micro-CT and histomorphometry at 2 and 6 weeks. PDRN significantly increased new-bone area and proportion in the buccal augmented zone, but did not improve bone-to-implant contact (BIC) or bone area fraction occupancy (BAFO).

## Key Contributions

- Targets a clinically common scenario: dehiscence defect at immediate implant placement.
- Adds a positive signal for **early** (2w, 6w) bone formation when PDRN is added to a synthetic graft.
- Notes a dissociation: PDRN improves the **fraction of new bone** but not the **bone-implant interface** parameters in this short follow-up.
- Documents feasibility of 3D-printed implants in a dehiscence + augmentation model.

## Methodology

- 6 male beagles; P3/P4 extracted; immediate implant placement.
- 5-mm buccal dehiscence created surgically.
- Two arms: (i) CBCP + collagen membrane, (ii) CBCP soaked in PDRN + collagen membrane.
- Sacrifice at 2 and 6 weeks.
- Outcomes: micro-CT, histomorphometry (BIC, BAFO, new-bone area, new-bone proportion).

## Results

- Micro-CT: no significant difference between CBCP and CBCP/PDRN at either time point.
- BIC and BAFO: no significant difference in buccal augmented or lingual non-augmented zones.
- New bone area and proportion in buccal augmented zone: significantly higher with CBCP/PDRN at both 2 and 6 weeks.
- Suggests PDRN's osteogenic action is most visible in early-phase tissue composition rather than interface integration.
