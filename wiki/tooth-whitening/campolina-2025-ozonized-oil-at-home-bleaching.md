## One-line Summary

Double-blind RCT (n=40) testing ozonized sunflower oil as a post-session desensitizer during 21-day at-home 10% HP bleaching: no reduction in sensitivity (VAS p=0.536) or improvement in whitening; ozonized oil rejected by patients for taste/odor.

## Summary

This double-blind parallel RCT from the Federal University of Uberlândia, Brazil, tested whether ozonized sunflower oil applied for 1 minute after each of 21 bleaching sessions (10% HP, 30 min/day in custom trays) could reduce bleaching-induced tooth sensitivity. Despite theoretical plausibility (ozone gas has shown anti-inflammatory/analgesic effects), ozonized oil had no effect on sensitivity intensity (VAS p=0.536) or duration (p=0.256). Whitening outcomes were identical between groups. Patients rated the ozonized oil significantly worse for taste (p=0.002) and odor (p=0.010). The ozone in oil vehicle does not replicate the analgesic effects of ozone gas observed in in-office protocols.

## Key Contributions

1. **Negative result for ozone oil as desensitizer**: Ozonized sunflower oil does not reduce at-home bleaching-induced sensitivity — gas ≠ oil delivery
2. **No whitening benefit either**: Color outcomes identical; ozonized oil adds no efficacy
3. **Patient rejection**: Poor sensory properties (taste, odor) would limit real-world adoption even if efficacious
4. **72.5% baseline sensitivity rate** with 10% HP at-home bleaching (consistent with literature ~51–80% range)
5. **Color rebound confirmed**: ΔEab and ΔE00 both decreased at 1-month vs 1-week in both groups (expected relapse)

## Methodology

- **Design**: Double-blind, parallel-group RCT (n=40)
- **Protocol**: 10% HP, 30 min/day for 21 days; oil (ozonized or non-ozonized sunflower) applied 1 min in trays after each session
- **Primary outcome**: Tooth sensitivity (VAS 0–10; NRS 0–4) daily for 21 days
- **Secondary outcomes**: Color change (ΔEab, ΔE00, ΔWID, ΔSGU at baseline, 1 week, 1 month); patient perception questionnaire
- **Registration**: RBR-6V7M9ZK

## Results

| Outcome | Result | p-value |
|---|---|---|
| Any TS episode | 72.5% (both groups) | — |
| TS intensity (VAS) | No difference | 0.536 |
| TS duration | No difference | 0.256 |
| Color change (ΔEab, ΔE00) | No difference between groups | > 0.05 |
| Color at 1 month | Both decreased vs 1 week | 0.010 / 0.026 |
| Patient taste | Ozonized worse | 0.002 |
| Patient odor | Ozonized worse | 0.010 |
| Patient satisfaction | No difference | NS |

**Clinical bottom line**: Ozonized sunflower oil cannot be recommended as a bleaching adjunct. Other desensitizing approaches (potassium nitrate, fluoride, nano-hydroxyapatite) have better evidence.
