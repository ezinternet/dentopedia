## One-line Summary

In vitro RCT (n=154 premolars, 11 protocol groups) across two sessions found that 2×8-min and 1×24-min in-office protocols achieve equivalent whitening to 3×8 min with lower pulp HP penetration; 1×8 min is insufficient; second session is essential for clinically meaningful WI change.

## 한줄요약

인비트로 RCT(n=154 소구치)에서 2×8분과 1×24분 인체 프로토콜은 3×8분과 동등한 미백 효과를 보이면서 치수 HP 침투가 더 낮았고, 1×8분은 효과 부족, 2회 세션이 임상적 WI 변화에 필수적이었다.

## Summary

This controlled in vitro study from the Loguercio/Reis group systematically evaluated 11 in-office bleaching application protocols using two commercial products (Pola Office+, Pola Rapid) in extracted human premolars. The key design question: how many applications and for how long? Across two bleaching sessions (7-day interval), WI improvement was significant for all protocols except 1×8 min. The 2×8-min and 1×24-min protocols achieved WI gains statistically equivalent to the standard 3×8-min protocol while producing intermediate levels of HP pulp penetration — not as low as 1×8, but significantly lower than 3×8 (the maximum). Both gel types behaved similarly across all protocols.

## Key Contributions

1. **2×8-min protocol: the optimal sweet spot**: Equivalent whitening to 3×8 min, lower pulp HP penetration, shorter chair time (saves ~8 min/session)
2. **1×24-min: viable alternative**: Also achieves good whitening with intermediate HP penetration — useful when fewer gel changes are logistically preferable
3. **3×8 min: maximum whitening, maximum penetration**: The highest whitening but highest HP exposure to pulp — highest theoretical sensitivity risk
4. **1×8 min: avoid**: Minimal WI, minimal penetration — the protocol achieves neither whitening nor safety advantage
5. **Two sessions essential**: Single-session WI changes are modest regardless of protocol; second session drives clinically meaningful improvement

## Methodology

- **Design**: In vitro controlled study with 11 groups (n=14 premolars each; total n=154)
- **Products**: Pola Office+ and Pola Rapid (SDI), each tested in 5 protocols + shared control
- **Protocols**: 3×8 min, 2×8 min, 1×8 min, 1×16 min, 1×24 min; 2 sessions separated by 7 days
- **Whitening**: WI (digital spectrophotometer), pre/post 2 sessions
- **HP penetration**: UV-Vis spectrophotometry of pulp chamber leachate
- **Statistics**: Two-way ANOVA + Tukey's HSD + Dunnett's vs control

## Results

**Protocol decision table (after 2 sessions):**

| Protocol | WI (Whitening) | HP Penetration | Clinical verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3×8 min | Highest | Highest | Max whitening, max risk |
| **2×8 min** | **= 3×8** | **Intermediate** | **Optimal balance** |
| **1×24 min** | **= 3×8** | **Intermediate-high** | **Good balance** |
| 1×16 min | Intermediate | Moderate | Acceptable |
| 1×8 min | Lowest | Lowest | Insufficient |

**Gel comparison**: Pola Office+ vs Pola Rapid showed no significant differences across most outcomes; protocol effect dominates.

**Key implication**: The standard 3×8-min protocol can be safely replaced by 2×8 min in most cases — same result, lower pulp HP exposure, 8 fewer chair minutes per session.
