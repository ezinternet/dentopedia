## One-line Summary

SR + frequentist NMA (37 studies qualitative, 10 meta-analyzed, n=1932; GRADE low) — OTC bleaching works short-term vs placebo: 6% HP strips (≥14 h) best on instrumental ΔEab*, at-home 10% CP (≥14 h) best on shade-guide ΔSGU, with little-to-no tooth sensitivity or gingival irritation.

## 한줄요약

SR + 빈도주의 NMA (정성 37편, 메타 10편, n=1932) — OTC 미백은 단기적으로 위약보다 효과적: ΔEab*는 6% HP 스트립(≥14h), ΔSGU는 10% CP(≥14h)가 최고이며 민감도·치은 자극은 거의 없음(근거 낮음).

## Summary

This NMA covers the consumer/unsupervised whitening route — strips, paint-on gels, prefilled trays — which is the most common entry point patients ask about. The headline: low-concentration OTC products do produce real short-term lightening over placebo and are generally well tolerated (minimal sensitivity, minimal gingival irritation), with longer daily wear time being the key driver of effect. Format matters by metric: **6% HP strips win on instrumental color (ΔEab*)** while **10% CP trays win on visual shade units (ΔSGU)**. The "contradicts" relation to de Geus is nuanced — OTC is not meaningfully more harmful than professional bleaching as marketing fears suggest, but the authors stress short-term-only, low-certainty evidence and the absence of professional supervision (missed pathology, unrealistic expectations, misuse). Position OTC as a practical low-strength option, not an equivalence claim to clinician-supervised bleaching.

## Key Contributions

- First NMA ranking OTC formats (strip concentration × wear time, vs CP trays) on color AND adverse effects together.
- Quantifies the favorable short-term safety profile (low sensitivity / gingival irritation) of low-strength OTC products.

## Methodology

- 37 RCTs qualitative, 10 in meta-analysis, n=1,932; nine databases + grey literature.
- Frequentist NMA through common comparators (ΔEab*, ΔSGU, sensitivity); RoB2; GRADE.

## Results

| Outcome | Best protocol | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| ΔEab* (instrumental) | 6% HP strips (≥14 h) | significantly higher |
| ΔSGU (visual shade) | 10% CP at-home (≥14 h) | then 6% HP strips, then 3% HP strips (all ≥14 h) |
| Tooth sensitivity | 10% CP (7–13 h) ≈ placebo | low risk, no significant difference |
| Adverse effects | minimal | little-to-no dentin hypersensitivity / gingival irritation |

Certainty (GRADE): low; short-term outcomes only.
