## One-line Summary

SR+MA (8 studies, 6 meta-analyzed; RoB moderate–high) — internal (walking-bleach) of endodontically treated discolored teeth achieves large, significant lightening (ΔSGU 6.27, ΔE 12.83); on ΔSGU, carbamide peroxide 35–37%, HP 35%, and sodium perborate + HP all outperform sodium perborate alone, while ΔE shows no significant agent difference.

## Summary

Single-tooth discoloration in a non-vital (root-canal-treated) tooth is a distinct whitening indication that external bleaching cannot solve — the chromogen is inside the dentin, so the agent must be sealed inside the pulp chamber (the "walking-bleach" technique). This meta-analysis confirms the technique works well (large ΔSGU/ΔE) and gives an agent hierarchy: **higher-strength carbamide peroxide (35–37%), hydrogen peroxide 35%, or sodium perborate mixed with HP beat sodium perborate alone** for shade-unit improvement, though instrumentally measured ΔE doesn't separate the top agents. Evidence is thin (8 studies) and risk of bias moderate-to-high, and the abstract does not report long-term shade stability or the well-known external cervical resorption risk — both warrant clinical caution.

## Key Contributions

- First meta-analytic comparison of walking-bleach agents for non-vital teeth.
- Quantifies the large shade improvement achievable and ranks agents on ΔSGU.

## Methodology

- Two reviewers searched PubMed, Web of Science, Cochrane for clinical trials of internal bleaching with pre/post color (ΔSGU or ΔE).
- 8 studies included (6 meta-analyzed); subgroup analyses by agent; heterogeneity (Q, I²); funnel-plot publication bias.

## Results

- Overall shade change: **ΔSGU 6.27 (95% CI 5.36–7.17)**, **ΔE 12.83 (95% CI 9.46–16.20)** — large and significant.
- ΔSGU: CP 35/37%, HP 35%, sodium perborate + HP (3%/30%) all **> sodium perborate alone** (adjusted P ≤ .026).
- ΔE: no significant difference among CP 37%, HP 35%, sodium perborate + HP (P = .051).
- RoB moderate–high; long-term recall data lacking.
