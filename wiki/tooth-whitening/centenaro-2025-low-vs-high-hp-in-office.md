## One-line Summary

Parallel double-blind RCT (n=140, 3 sessions of 50 min) found 35% HP superior to 6% HP for in-office whitening (ΔE MD=6.3; 90% CI 4.8–7.7) but 6% HP halved tooth sensitivity risk (44% vs 74%, p=0.0005); 6% HP is a valid EU-compliant sensitivity-reducing alternative.

## Summary

This 2025 RCT (Loguercio group, State University of Ponta Grossa) directly compared 6% and 35% hydrogen peroxide for in-office bleaching using an equivalence design. The study was motivated by the EU's restriction of in-office HP to 6%, demanding evidence about what is actually lost or gained at that concentration. Results demonstrated non-equivalence: 35% HP achieved significantly greater ΔE across all time points (3 sessions × 50 min, 1-week interval). However, 6% HP reduced tooth sensitivity risk from 74% to 44% (p=0.0005) and lowered intensity at up to 24 hours post-treatment. The 35% HP group had higher sensitivity at all immediate and 24h time points; at 48h, the groups were comparable. Both concentrations produced clinically significant whitening.

## Key Contributions

1. **35% HP superior — not equivalent — to 6%**: The non-equivalence finding directly informs the EU regulation debate; some whitening efficacy is lost at 6%
2. **44% vs 74% sensitivity risk**: A clinically meaningful 30-percentage-point reduction in sensitivity risk with 6% HP
3. **Sensitivity resolves equally at 48h**: Any additional sensitivity from 35% HP is short-lived
4. **Both protocols achieve significant whitening**: 6% is not ineffective — it just achieves less than 35%
5. **Informed consent framework**: Results enable clinicians to offer patients a clear efficacy-sensitivity trade-off choice

## Methodology

- **Design**: Parallel RCT, double-blind, equivalence design (n=140; 70/group)
- **Products**: Automixx 6% (FGM) vs Automixx Plus 35% (FGM); 3 sessions × 50 min; 1-week intervals
- **BE assessment**: Vita Easyshade spectrophotometer (ΔE, ΔE00, ΔWI) + shade guides at days 7, 14, 21, 1 month
- **TS assessment**: VAS (0–10) immediately after, 1h, 24h, 48h post-session

## Results

**Bleaching Efficacy:**

| Endpoint | 35% HP | 6% HP | MD (90% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ΔE (day 21) | Superior | — | 6.3 (4.8–7.7) | < 0.05 |
| All endpoints | Non-equivalent at all time points | — | — | — |

**Tooth Sensitivity:**

| Time point | 35% HP | 6% HP | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Risk (any TS) | 74% | 44% | 0.0005 |
| Intensity (immediate + 24h) | Higher | Lower | < 0.01 |
| Intensity (48h) | Equal | Equal | NS |

**Decision framework:**
- Patient with high sensitivity risk → **6% HP**: significant whitening, halved sensitivity
- Patient prioritizing maximal whitening → **35% HP**: superior efficacy, higher short-term sensitivity
- EU-regulated practice → **6% HP** is the only available option; outcomes are acceptable
