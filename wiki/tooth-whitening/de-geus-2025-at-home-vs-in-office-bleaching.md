## One-line Summary

Updated SR+MA (32 RCTs, 23 meta-analyzed; GRADE low) — at-home and in-office vital bleaching produce equivalent shade-guide change (ΔSGU); at-home has significantly lower sensitivity intensity (SMD −0.78) and slightly greater instrumental ΔE, but sensitivity *risk* does not differ by technique.

## 한줄요약

갱신 SR+MA (RCT 32편) — 자가미백과 진료실미백의 색조 단위(ΔSGU) 변화는 동등; 자가미백이 민감도 강도는 유의하게 낮고 ΔE는 약간 큼. 민감도 발생 위험 자체는 두 방식 간 차이 없음(근거 수준 낮음).

## Summary

This is the most current (2025) head-to-head synthesis of the two delivery modes for vital tooth bleaching. The clinically important message: choosing in-office vs at-home does **not** change how much the teeth lighten in shade-guide units, and does **not** change whether the patient gets sensitivity — only the *intensity* of sensitivity is modestly lower with at-home (likely reflecting lower peroxide concentration and slower delivery). All conclusions sit on **low-certainty (GRADE)** evidence, so they should guide preference/convenience discussions rather than be stated as strong efficacy claims.

## Key Contributions

- Updates a 2016 SR with six new (2024) studies → 32 studies total, the broadest current comparison.
- Decouples the two decision drivers — **efficacy** (ΔSGU, ΔE) and **adverse effect** (sensitivity risk vs intensity).
- Provides GRADE certainty, flagging the low evidence quality behind common marketing claims.

## Methodology

- RCTs in adults comparing in-office vs at-home bleaching; seven databases + grey literature/citation searching.
- Risk-of-bias assessment; GRADE for certainty.
- Effect measures: SMD for continuous outcomes (ΔE, sensitivity intensity), RR for sensitivity risk, ΔSGU for shade.

## Results

| Outcome | Result | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Sensitivity intensity | SMD −0.78 (95% CI −1.53 to −0.03; p=0.04) | Significantly **lower** for at-home |
| Sensitivity risk | RR 0.82 (95% CI 0.61–1.10; p=0.19) | **No difference** |
| Efficacy ΔSGU | SMD 0.04 (95% CI −0.17–0.25; p=0.68) | **No difference** |
| Efficacy ΔE | SMD 0.49 (95% CI 0.14–0.84; p=0.006) | Slightly **higher** for at-home |
| GRADE certainty | Low | Interpret cautiously |
