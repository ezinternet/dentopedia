## One-line Summary

Bayesian NMA (77 studies, 50 meta-analyzed; GRADE low) — at-home tooth sensitivity rises with peroxide concentration: CP 20–22% and HP 14–16% carry ~2.8–2.9× the sensitivity risk of HP 2–4%, and CP 20–22% ~2× that of CP 10–11%; but on average sensitivity is mild for every concentration.

## Summary

Bleaching-induced tooth sensitivity is the most common adverse effect of whitening, and this NMA turns the vague "higher concentration = more sensitivity" intuition into a ranked, quantified gradient for at-home agents. The practical takeaway: **CP 10–11% is the comfort sweet spot** — meaningfully lower sensitivity risk than CP 20–22% or HP 14–16%, while still effective (efficacy addressed in companion at-home NMAs). For sensitivity-prone patients, prescribe the lowest effective concentration and supervise higher concentrations more closely. Because average sensitivity is mild across the board and certainty is low, concentration choice is a comfort-optimization lever, not a safety gate.

## Key Contributions

- First network meta-analysis to **rank at-home concentration tiers** by sensitivity (risk and intensity), not just active vs placebo.
- Concentration-stratified safety gradient directly usable for prescribing.
- Highlights scale-dependence (NRS detected differences the VAS did not).

## Methodology

- 77 RCTs (50 in meta-analysis), permanent dentition, at-home systems only. PROSPERO CRD42021265220.
- Bayesian NMA across HP/CP concentration nodes; outcomes = sensitivity risk (RR) and intensity (VAS 0–10, NRS 0–4).
- RoB 2.0 (most studies high RoB); GRADE certainty = low.

## Results

| Comparison | Outcome | Effect (95% CrI) |
|---|---|---|
| CP 20–22% vs HP 2–4% | TS risk | RR 2.8 (1.15–8.16) |
| HP 14–16% vs HP 2–4% | TS risk | RR 2.9 (1.06–9.21) |
| CP 20–22% vs CP 10–11% | TS risk | RR 2.0 (1.07–4.31) |
| CP 20–22% vs CP 10–11% | TS intensity (NRS) | MD 0.51 (0.14–0.91) |
| HP 7–8% vs CP 10–11% | TS intensity (NRS) | MD 0.50 (0.10–0.84) |
| (any) | TS intensity (VAS) | no significant difference |

Average sensitivity mild for all agents/concentrations; QoE low.
