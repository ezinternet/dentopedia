## One-line Summary

Systematic review comparing external HP vs CP bleaching gels found CP superior for spectrophotometric color change (ΔE) but equivalent on shade guide units; higher-concentration CP caused less sensitivity than higher-concentration HP; moderate quality evidence throughout.

## Summary

This systematic review (PROSPERO CRD42023483434) from King Khalid University compared hydrogen peroxide (HP)-based and carbamide peroxide (CP)-based external bleaching gels for whitening performance, longevity, and adverse effects. Searches covered four major databases (PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, Cochrane) through mid-2024. The key finding is that CP gels outperformed HP on spectrophotometric ΔE, though both were equivalent on shade guide unit changes (ΔSGU). Additionally, higher-concentration CP gels induced less tooth sensitivity than comparable-concentration HP gels, attributed to CP's slower peroxide release via its two-step decomposition (CP → urea + HP). The quality of evidence for all key outcomes was rated as moderate.

## Key Contributions

1. **CP > HP on ΔE, equal on ΔSGU**: Carbamide peroxide provides slightly better spectrophotometric whitening; clinically, both are equivalent on shade guide assessment
2. **Higher-concentration CP safer than HP**: The urea-mediated buffering in CP (alkaline pH) and slower peroxide delivery reduce peak pulp exposure, resulting in less sensitivity
3. **Longevity addressed**: Included whitening durability as a secondary outcome — an underexplored dimension in prior bleaching SRs
4. **Moderate evidence quality**: GRADE assessment throughout; high-quality RCT evidence remains lacking for direct agent comparisons

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review (no meta-analysis)
- **Search**: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, Cochrane Library (through August 2024)
- **Eligibility**: RCTs measuring tooth color change (ΔE, ΔSGU) before and after external bleaching
- **Comparison**: HP-based vs CP-based bleaching gels; various concentrations
- **Quality appraisal**: Cochran Q + I² for heterogeneity; GRADE for evidence quality
- **Registration**: PROSPERO CRD42023483434

## Results

| Outcome | CP gels | HP gels | Finding |
|---|---|---|---|
| ΔE (spectrophotometric) | Superior | Reference | CP > HP |
| ΔSGU (shade guide) | Equivalent | Equivalent | No difference |
| Tooth sensitivity (high conc.) | Lower | Higher | CP safer |
| Quality of evidence | Moderate | Moderate | — |

**Key clinical implication**: When choosing between CP and HP for external bleaching, CP offers marginally better spectrophotometric outcomes and less sensitivity at comparable concentrations. However, shade-guide–based outcomes (more relevant clinically) are equivalent. Agent selection should consider patient sensitivity profile, treatment duration, and patient compliance.
