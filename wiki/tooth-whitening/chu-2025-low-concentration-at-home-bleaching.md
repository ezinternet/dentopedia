## One-line Summary

Split-mouth triple-blind RCT (n=22, 2 weeks) found 3% HP (Invisalign) achieved greater ΔE than 8% CP (iWhite) (5.39 vs 4.75, p<0.05), while 8% CP caused less sensitivity (p<0.05) and less enamel alteration on SEM; both products clinically acceptable and improved OHRQoL.

## Summary

This split-mouth RCT from Shanghai Ninth People's Hospital directly compared Invisalign whitening gel (3% HP, acidic) vs Beyke iWhite (8% CP, alkaline formulation) in 22 adults over 2 weeks (6–8 h/day). The split-mouth design provides within-patient control, making results highly reliable despite the small sample. Key findings: 3% HP achieved greater spectrophotometric whitening (ΔE 5.39 vs 4.75) but caused more enamel surface undulations (SEM) and higher sensitivity. 8% CP's alkaline pH is protective — it starts alkaline (buffering enamel acid attack) and shifts to neutral over 8 hours. Both products exceeded the ΔE > 1.8 clinical acceptability threshold, improved OHRQoL, and caused only mild sensitivity.

## Key Contributions

1. **3% HP whitens more than 8% CP**: Even at matched effective HP content, formulation matters — 3% HP is faster/more active
2. **8% CP: less sensitivity + less enamel alteration**: The alkaline pH of 8% CP acts as an enamel buffer and reduces peak pulp peroxide exposure
3. **Both products clinically acceptable**: ΔE > 1.8 and > 3.3 (visible) — validates low-concentration products for clinical use
4. **pH mechanism confirmed in vitro**: Invisalign gel remains acidic throughout 8h; iWhite turns neutral — explains differential enamel outcomes
5. **OHRQoL improves with both**: Both OHIP and PIDAQ showed significant improvement — low-concentration bleaching has real psychosocial benefit

## Methodology

- **Design**: Split-mouth, triple-blind RCT (n=22 adults; contralateral treatment allocation)
- **Products**: Invisalign 3% HP (Align) vs Beyke iWhite 8% CP (≈2.8% equivalent HP)
- **Duration**: 2 weeks; 6–8 h/day application in custom trays
- **Assessments**: Spectrophotometer ΔE (baseline, post, 1 month); daily NRS/VAS sensitivity; OHIP-14 + PIDAQ; SEM enamel; in vitro pH tracking
- **Registration**: ChiCTR2400092807 (retrospective)

## Results

| Outcome | 3% HP (Invisalign) | 8% CP (iWhite) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| ΔE post-bleaching | 5.39 ± 2.50 | 4.75 ± 2.25 | < 0.05 |
| Clinical acceptability (ΔE > 1.8) | Both | Both | — |
| Tooth sensitivity (NRS/VAS) | Higher (mild) | Lower (mild) | < 0.05 |
| SEM enamel | More undulations | Minimal | Qualitative |
| Gel pH (8h) | Acidic throughout | Alkaline → neutral | — |
| OHRQoL (OHIP + PIDAQ) | Improved | Improved | < 0.05 both |
| 1-month color stability | Maintained | Maintained | — |

**Clinical selection guide:**
- Sensitivity-prone patient → **8% CP (alkaline)**: equally acceptable whitening, less sensitivity, less enamel alteration
- Fastest low-concentration result → **3% HP**: slightly superior ΔE but more sensitive
