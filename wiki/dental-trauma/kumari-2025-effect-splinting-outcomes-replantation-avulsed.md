## One-line Summary

SR (7 studies; 4 in MA; 708 patients, 975 teeth; GRADE very low): trends favor flexible splints and shorter durations for replanted avulsed permanent teeth in children, tentatively supporting IADT guidance, but most effects were not statistically significant.

## 한줄요약

SR (7편, MA 4편; 환자 708명·치아 975개; GRADE 매우 낮음): 소아 영구치 탈구치 재식 후 탄성(flexible) 스플린트와 짧은 고정 기간이 유리한 경향을 보여 IADT 권고를 잠정 지지하나, 대부분 통계적 유의성에는 도달하지 못함.

## Summary

After a replanted avulsed permanent tooth, stabilization is required, but splint TYPE (rigid vs flexible) and DURATION have long lacked a firm evidence base. This pediatric SR pools the available studies and finds the data point toward flexible, short-term splinting for better periodontal/pulp outcomes — consistent with IADT guidance — while cautioning that most differences are not statistically significant and overall certainty is very low. It is the stabilization step that follows storage/replantation in the avulsion chain, and is a distinct indication from periodontal/mobility splinting.

## Key Contributions

- Trauma-specific focus on splint type and duration for replanted avulsed permanent teeth (pediatric).
- Provides a GRADE behind the IADT preference for flexible, short-term splinting.

## Methodology

- PRISMA-guided; PROSPERO CRD42023414888.
- Databases: PubMed, Cochrane Library, Embase, Google Scholar; 734 records → 7 included, 4 pooled.
- RoB: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale; meta-analysis: RevMan v5.4; certainty: GRADE.

## Results

- 708 participants, 975 avulsed teeth.
- Trend: flexible splints + shorter durations → better periodontal and pulp outcomes.
- Most comparisons NOT statistically significant.
- Overall evidence: GRADE very low.
