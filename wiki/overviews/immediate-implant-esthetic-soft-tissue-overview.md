> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - **결론(bottom line)**: 전치부 즉시식립(Immediate Implant Placement, IIP)의 생존율은 모든 식립시점에서 >95%로 거의 동등하지만, **승패는 생존이 아니라 정중협측 퇴축(midfacial recession)·심미**에서 갈린다. 무선별 IIP는 >1 mm 퇴축 위험이 20–30%다 (Buser 2017).
> - **퇴축의 3대 동인**: ① 얇은 협측골벽(≤1 mm) ② 얇은 생체형(thin biotype) ③ 협측 임플란트 위치(buccal shoulder). 이 셋이 위험을 곱셈으로 키운다 (Chen 2009, Buser 2017).
> - **협측골벽 두께가 1차 게이트**: ≤1 mm 박벽형은 발치 후 8주에 수직골 중앙값 7.5 mm 소실(후벽형 1.1 mm의 ~3.5배) — 박벽형이면 IIP 자체를 재고하고 치조제보존술(Alveolar Ridge Preservation, ARP)+지연식립으로 (Chappuis 2013).
> - **임플란트 위치가 직접 동인**: 협측 숄더는 설측 대비 정중협측 퇴축이 ~3배(1.8 vs 0.6 mm). 구개측·심부 식립이 필수 — 안전구역은 안면 연조직연 4 mm 하방·협측 간극 >2 mm (Evans 2008, Cosyn 2026).
> - **즉시 임시수복(immediate provisionalization)의 가치**: 생존율은 안 바꾸지만(~97% 동등) **연조직 윤곽·유두·emergence profile 보존**에 유리. 협측 점막레벨·핑크심미점수(Pink Esthetic Score, PES) 보존 경향 (Pitman 2022, Qin 2023, Sutariya 2022).
> - **임시수복의 전제조건**: 충분한 일차안정성(보통 ≥35 N·cm 또는 임플란트안정지수 ISQ ≥70). 안정성이 부족하면 비-임플란트지지 대안(자연치관+섬유강화복합 FRC 스플린트)으로 PES 유지 가능(9.0 vs 6.3, p=0.005) (Wu 2026).
> - **결합조직이식(Connective Tissue Graft, CTG/SCTG)의 효과**: 정중협측 퇴축을 줄이고(~0.4–0.7 mm 보존; Seyssens 2021) 협측조직두께·PES를 높이며(상피하 SCTG: 두께 +0.74 mm, PES +0.79; Aldhohrah 2022), 1년 RCT에서 부피손실을 ~5배 감소(퇴축 −0.74 mm vs 골이식 −0.98, 맞춤치유지대주 단독 −1.54; Fettouh 2024). 단 RCT-only 합성에서는 협측 치은레벨 차이가 비유의 경향(Torra-Moneny 2024) — CTG는 레벨을 '증가'보다 '유지'시킨다.
> - **CTG는 두께가 얇을 때 우선**: 네트워크 메타분석에서 STT(연조직두께)를 유의하게 늘린 것은 CTG뿐이나 대부분 재료간 차이는 비유의; 단층 이종 콜라겐 매트릭스가 PES 순위 1위 — 얇은 케이스만 CTG, 그 외엔 대체재로 충분 (Azadi 2025).
> - **협측 간극(buccal gap) 관리가 경조직의 핵심**: 간극 >2 mm는 Type 1A 생존을 +3.1 PP 올린다(근관감염 부재 +2.6 PP; Hamilton 2023). 간극은 이식재로 채우고, 열개(dehiscence) 소켓은 morphotype에 따라 재생전략을 단계화하되 Type III(열개+안면퇴축)와 인접치까지 확장된 결손은 **절대금기** (Cosyn 2026).
> - **임상 takeaway**: IIP 심미성공 = 케이스선별(협측벽 intact·중간/두꺼운 생체형·간극 ≥2 mm·감염 없음) × 구개측 식립 × 간극 이식 × (얇으면) CTG × (안정성 충분하면) 즉시 임시수복. 비폐쇄 개방치유(open healing)도 선별된 전치부에서 일차봉합과 동등(정중협측 점막 SMD −0.26, 비유의; 근심유두는 오히려 유리; Zadikian 2026).

## One-line Summary

In esthetic-zone immediate implant placement, survival is near-universal (>95%) but esthetic success hinges on preventing midfacial recession; the dominant drivers are thin buccal wall (≤1 mm), thin biotype, and buccal implant shoulder position, and the controllable levers are palatal 3-D positioning, buccal-gap grafting, connective tissue grafting (CTG) for thin phenotypes, and immediate provisionalization (when primary stability allows) to preserve soft-tissue contour and papillae.

## 한줄요약

전치부 즉시식립의 생존율은 거의 보편적(>95%)이지만 심미 성패는 정중협측 퇴축 방지에 달려 있고, 핵심 동인은 얇은 협측골(≤1 mm)·얇은 생체형·협측 임플란트 위치이며, 조절 가능한 지렛대는 구개측 3차원 식립·협측간극 이식·(얇으면) 결합조직이식·(안정성 충분하면) 즉시 임시수복이다.

## Thesis

The esthetic-zone immediate implant is not a survival problem — it is a **soft-tissue stability problem**. Across timing-classification reviews (Buser 2017; Chen 2009), survival exceeds 95% for immediate (Type 1) just as for early (Type 2/3) and late (Type 4) placement, yet Type 1 carries a **20–30% risk of >1 mm midfacial mucosal recession** when patients and sites are unselected. The clinically meaningful outcome is therefore the **midfacial mucosal level (MML)**, the **papillae**, and the composite **Pink Esthetic Score (PES)** — not the implant-survival column.

Three risk factors recur in every analysis and behave **multiplicatively**, not additively:

1. **Buccal bone wall thickness** — the upstream gate. Chappuis 2013 quantified that a ≤1 mm thin-wall phenotype loses a median **7.5 mm of vertical bone** by 8 weeks post-extraction versus **1.1 mm** for thick walls (~3.5×), and most esthetic-zone patients are *already* thin-walled at the central buccal site. A wall that resorbs takes the overlying mucosa with it.
2. **Tissue biotype/phenotype** — thin biotype trends toward more recession (Evans 2008 found 1.0 vs 0.7 mm, NS; Buser 2017 lists it as a top-3 driver). Its main effect is to make wall loss visible and to limit the soft-tissue reserve a graft can build on.
3. **Implant 3-D position** — the one fully operator-controlled driver. Evans 2008 showed a **buccal shoulder yields ~3× the recession of a lingual shoulder (1.8 vs 0.6 mm, p<0.001)**. Cosyn 2026 formalizes this as a *safe zone* (≥4 mm below the facial soft-tissue margin vertically, buccal gap >2 mm horizontally) vs *danger* and *failure* zones.

Against this risk substrate, the literature converges on a small set of **controllable levers**:

- **Buccal-gap grafting** is the principal hard-tissue lever. A facial gap **>2 mm raised Type 1A survival by +3.1 percentage points** (Hamilton 2023) and is the space into which graft, blood clot, and biology fit; Pitman 2022 found jumping-gap management *more predictive of hard-tissue outcome than provisionalization itself*.
- **Connective tissue grafting (CTG/SCTG)** is the principal soft-tissue lever for thin phenotypes. Seyssens 2021 (SR+MA) found CTG preserves the midfacial level by ~0.4–0.7 mm; Aldhohrah 2022 found subepithelial CTG significantly raised buccal thickness (+0.74 mm), gingival level (+0.5 mm), and PES (+0.79); Fettouh 2024 (1-yr RCT) showed CTG cut midfacial recession to −0.74 mm and volumetric loss ~5-fold vs bone graft (−0.98) or customized healing abutment alone (−1.54). The caveat: RCT-only pooling (Torra-Moneny 2024) found the buccal-level benefit non-significant (MD 0.09 mm, p=0.05) — **CTG maintains rather than increases** the level, and a network meta-analysis (Azadi 2025) found CTG uniquely raised soft-tissue thickness but most between-material differences were not significant, with a uni-layer xenogeneic collagen matrix ranking best for PES. Reserve CTG for thin tissue; substitutes suffice otherwise.
- **Immediate provisionalization** is the principal contour/papilla lever. It does **not** change survival (~97% in both arms; Pitman 2022) but trends toward better MML preservation and papilla stability (Qin 2023, Sutariya 2022) by shaping the emergence profile and supporting the soft tissue during healing. Its prerequisite is **primary stability** — when that is lacking, a non-implant-supported provisional (natural crown on an FRC splint) can still preserve PES (Wu 2026), and a guide-delivered indexless protocol can shape emergence profile with 0.37 mm 1-yr MBL and 1.41 mm buccal-contour gain (Nechita 2026). An **open-healing** seal (provisional/customized abutment + graft, no membrane, no primary closure) matches primary flap closure for MML (SMD −0.26, NS) and *favors* the mesial papilla (Zadikian 2026).

The synthesis: **case selection sets the ceiling; technique fills the room under it.** A site with an intact buccal wall, medium/thick biotype, and an achievable ≥2 mm gap can be driven to an excellent result with palatal positioning + gap graft + (thin → CTG) + (stable → immediate provisional). A thin-walled or dehisced site should either be regenerated by a morphotype-matched strategy (Cosyn 2026) or — the better-evidenced default for less-experienced operators — deferred to ARP + late placement.

## Clinical Decision Points

### 1. Case selection — is this socket an immediate-implant candidate at all? (set the ceiling)

Assess buccal wall + biotype + infection + gap **before** committing.

- **Intact buccal wall + medium/thick biotype + no active infection + achievable gap ≥2 mm** → IIP defensible (Hamilton 2023, Chen 2009).
- **Thin buccal wall (≤1 mm)** → expect ~7.5 mm vertical loss potential (Chappuis 2013); strongly consider **ARP + early/late placement (Type 2/3)** rather than forcing IIP — Type 2 is the more esthetically predictable default (Chen 2009).
- **Active endodontic/periodontal infection** → defer (−2.6 PP survival; Hamilton 2023).
- **Buccal dehiscence present** → classify by morphotype (Cosyn 2026). Type III (dehiscence + facial recession) and extended (interproximal-to-adjacent-tooth) defects are **absolute contraindications** → ARP + late placement.

### 2. 3-D position — where does the shoulder go? (the operator's dominant lever)

- Place the **shoulder palatally and deep**: safe zone is ≥4 mm below the facial soft-tissue margin and ≥2 mm buccal gap (Cosyn 2026). A buccal shoulder triples midfacial recession (Evans 2008).
- Engage apical/palatal bone for primary stability; this also positions the gap on the buccal aspect where graft is needed.

### 3. Buccal gap — graft it (the hard-tissue lever)

- A facial gap **>2 mm is favorable** (+3.1 PP survival; Hamilton 2023) and should be filled with a slow-resorbing graft (e.g., DBBM/xenograft) — gap management outweighs provisionalization for hard-tissue outcome (Pitman 2022).
- For dehisced sockets, match graft + barrier to morphotype-tiered regenerative potential (Cosyn 2026): modified trimodal (barrier omitted) for high-potential, four-pillar + barrier (± composite/autogenous bone) for moderate/low.

### 4. Soft-tissue graft — CTG or not? (the thin-phenotype lever)

- **Thin biotype / thin labial bone / high esthetic demand** → add CTG/SCTG. It preserves midfacial level (~0.4–0.7 mm; Seyssens 2021), raises thickness/PES (Aldhohrah 2022), and cuts volumetric loss ~5× (Fettouh 2024).
- **Adequate thickness** → CTG benefit is small/non-significant on level (Torra-Moneny 2024); a xenogeneic collagen matrix is a reasonable, lower-morbidity substitute (best PES rank; Azadi 2025).
- **Do not rely on a customized healing abutment alone** to maintain contour in thin sites (−1.54 mm midfacial change; Fettouh 2024).

### 5. Provisionalize or not? (the contour/papilla lever)

- **Primary stability adequate (≈≥35 N·cm / ISQ ≥70)** → place an immediate **non-occlusal** screw-retained provisional to shape emergence profile and support papillae (Kan 2003, Pitman 2022, Qin 2023). Survival is unchanged; the gain is soft-tissue contour.
- **Primary stability inadequate** → use a non-implant-supported provisional (natural crown + FRC splint; Wu 2026) or a customized healing abutment / open-healing seal — open healing matches primary closure for MML and favors the mesial papilla (Zadikian 2026).
- **Guided/digital workflow** → guide-delivered (even indexless) provisionals are feasible with favorable 1-yr tissue stability (Nechita 2026).
- **Always keep the provisional out of occlusion**; immediate functional occlusal loading is a separate, higher-risk decision (excluded from the soft-tissue evidence base; Zadikian 2026).

## Gaps & Future Research

- **Esthetic/functional success (not just survival) is under-reported.** Hamilton 2023 and the ITI consensus both flag that PES/contour outcomes are inadequately characterized in the primary literature; survival ceilings hide esthetic variance.
- **CTG level-maintenance is RCT-fragile.** Seyssens 2021 (RCT+NRS) found a benefit that Torra-Moneny 2024 (RCT-only) reduced to a non-significant trend — the true effect size and the threshold thickness at which CTG stops adding value are unresolved (Azadi 2025 shows most material differences are NS).
- **Substitute vs autograft.** Whether xenogeneic collagen matrices truly equal CTG for long-term level (not just PES) needs powered head-to-head RCTs.
- **Dehiscence framework is unvalidated.** Cosyn 2026's 11-morphotype → 3-strategy scheme is conceptual; it needs prospective CBCT/histology/outcome validation before it can be a standard.
- **Open healing evidence is shallow and short.** Zadikian 2026 is ~92% anterior, intact-wall, mostly ≤6-month follow-up — long-term and compromised-socket data are missing.
- **Provisionalization alternatives are exploratory.** Wu 2026 (n=20) and Nechita 2026 (n=20, single-arm, 95% female) are feasibility signals, not comparative proof.
