## 한국어 핵심요약

> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - 핵심 명제: 최소침습 도재 라미네이트 비니어(Porcelain Laminate Veneer, PLV) 삭제는 "기존 치아를 깎는 것"이 아니라 **"최종 보철 형태를 먼저 디자인하고 그것을 관통해 삭제하는 것"** — Gürel의 "설계에 의한 최소삭제(minimal preparation by design)" 원칙이 전 종합의 척추.
> - 도구는 심미 사전평가 임시수복물 (Aesthetic Pre-evaluative Temporary, APT) / 목업(mock-up): 환자 승인 받은 임시보철을 입에 붙인 뒤 그 표면에 depth groove를 내고 *관통하여* 삭제 → 삭제량이 최종 도재가 놓일 위치를 기준으로 결정됨(법랑질 보존 극대화).
> - 삭제량을 정하는 변수는 치아가 아니라 **기질-목표색 차이 + 도재 두께/투명도**: Coachman의 "Do the Math" 수식 **P = LT − EV** (라미네이트 두께 − 추가부피 = 삭제량)으로 부위별(치경/중앙/절단 1/3) 정량화.
> - 색 변화 규칙(Coachman 2014): 1~2 shade tone 변화 → ~0.3mm 박형 라미네이트, ≥3 tone 또는 심한 변색 → 더 침습적 삭제 + intrasulcular 변연 연장(색 전이 은폐).
> - 정량 사례(A2→A1): 중앙·절단 1/3 삭제량 **0mm**(법랑질 무손상), 협면 0.2~0.5mm만, 최종 리튬디실리케이트(Lithium Disilicate, LDS) ~0.3mm — 추가부피가 충분하면 P가 0으로 붕괴(=진정한 무삭제).
> - 패러다임 진화 3세대: ① depth-guide 세대(고정깊이 버를 치아에 직접 — 과삭제 경향) → ② 실리콘 인덱스 세대(왁스업+freehand) → ③ **목업/APT 세대(권장)** → 이후 디지털화.
> - 아날로그→디지털(Cattoni 2016, 3D Digital Smile Planning, 3D-DSP): 석고모형·수기 왁스업 체인 제거, 안면 2D 사진 + 구내 STL 스캔 융합 → CAD-CAM 밀링 PMMA 목업을 prep guide로; 108 PLV 2년 추적 합병증 미미(파절 1·과민 2·치은퇴축 1, 탈락/치핑/미세누출 0), 목업 만족도 85.7%.
> - 생존 검증(Ali 2023 SR): 최소삭제 비니어(MPV 0.2~0.5mm)가 전통 삭제(CV 0.3~1.0mm) 대비 동등 또는 우수 — 초박형 contact-lens형은 마취·임시치 불필요.
> - 무삭제 vs 통상 적응증(Chandode 2026): 둘 다 91~100% 생존(무삭제 ~97% vs 통상 ~92%, 법랑질 본딩 시 무삭제 성공률 ≤97.4%); **무삭제 부적응 = 내인성 변색·부정정렬·큰 형태 수정**(overcontour 위험) → 경쟁이 아닌 상호보완.
> - 장기 단일사례 실증(Reis 2025): 법랑질 한정 ~0.2mm 치경부 삭제 + <0.5mm 장석 라미네이트가 12년+ 추적에서 파절·우식·과민·재수복 0.
> - 절단연 피개(incisal coverage) 디자인은 생존율에 무관(Albanesi 2016, OR 1.25, NS) → 심미·교합 기준 선택 가능, 삭제 디자인 자유도 확대.
> - **비협상 원칙**: 모든 삭제 디자인의 성공 전제는 법랑질 한정 변연 — 상아질 변연 노출 시 실패율 ~10배(El-Mowafy 2018, Gürel 580 비니어 12년). 최소침습의 목적이 곧 법랑질 confinement.
> - 임상 bottom line: "얼마나 깎을까"는 목업을 만들고 색 차이를 계산해 결정한다. 추가부피가 클수록(부가형 왁스업) 삭제는 작아지고(종종 0), 법랑질만 남으면 재료·절단연 디자인은 비교적 자유롭다.

## One-line Summary

Synthesis of 6 papers (1 prospective clinical study, 1 SR, 1 SR+MA, 1 narrative review, 2 case reports) establishing the mock-up/APT-guided "preparation by design" paradigm for minimally invasive porcelain laminate veneers: reduction is referenced to the patient-approved final contour and quantified by the substrate-to-target color gap (P = LT − EV), evolving from analog (Gürel 2007) to color-math (Coachman 2014) to fully digital CAD-CAM mock-ups (Cattoni 2016), with survival evidence (Ali, Reis, Chandode, Albanesi) confirming minimal/no-prep equals or exceeds conventional preparation provided enamel-confined margins are preserved.

## Summary

This page synthesizes the **preparation-design** axis of laminate veneer therapy — distinct from the material/survival/bonding axis covered in [[overviews/veneer-material-survival-protocol-overview]]. The question here is not *which ceramic* or *how long it survives*, but **how to remove the right amount of tooth structure in the right place** to achieve the planned esthetic result while preserving the maximum possible enamel for adhesion.

The unifying thesis, originating with **Gürel (2007)**, is "**minimal tooth preparation by design**": conservative reduction is not achieved by free-handing on the existing (often worn, dark, or malpositioned) tooth, but by first *designing the final restorative outcome* and then preparing **through** a patient-approved provisional that already carries those final contours. The physical instrument is the **Aesthetic Pre-evaluative Temporary (APT)** / mock-up, which serves double duty — an esthetic-phonetic-functional "test drive" for the patient *and* the depth reference for the bur.

**Coachman et al. (2014)** made this computable. Their "**Do the Math**" technique recognizes that how much to reduce depends on the **color gap between the remaining substrate and the target shade** (plus the ceramic thickness/translucency needed to mask it), not on the tooth alone, and expresses it region-by-region as **P = LT − EV, clamped at 0** (laminate thickness for the desired color − extra volume added by the mock-up = preparation depth; EV ≥ LT → 0). When the additively waxed-up volume meets or exceeds the ceramic thickness, P collapses to zero and enamel is never touched.

**Cattoni et al. (2016)** digitized the entire chain: a fully digital 3D Digital Smile Planning (3D-DSP) workflow fuses 2D facial photographs with an intraoral STL scan, mills a PMMA mock-up by CAD-CAM, and uses it directly as the position guide for enamel-confined preparation — eliminating the analog stone-model/hand-wax-up/silicone-key sequence.

Three lines of clinical evidence validate the paradigm's safety: **Ali (2023)** (SR) found minimal-prep veneers equal or superior to conventional in survival; **Reis (2025)** documented a 12-year failure-free minimally invasive feldspathic case; **Chandode (2026)** mapped the no-prep/conventional indication boundary; and **Albanesi (2016)** (SR+MA) showed the incisal-coverage design choice does not affect survival — widening latitude in prep design. Across all of them the single non-negotiable constraint is **enamel-confined margins**: dentin-margin exposure carries roughly a 10× failure penalty (El-Mowafy 2018, from Gürel's 580-veneer 12-year data), which is precisely *why* minimal preparation matters — its purpose is enamel preservation, not reduction for its own sake.

### The core procedural paradigm — "prepare through the mock-up"

1. **Design the endpoint first** — 3D smile analysis + functional evaluation (can the teeth bear the planned restorations? what occlusal scheme?), then an **additive diagnostic wax-up**.
2. **Transfer it intraorally** as a bis-acrylic mock-up / APT (analog) or a CAD-CAM-milled PMMA mock-up (digital), and obtain patient + team approval.
3. **Cut depth grooves on the mock-up surface** (single-ringed depth burs 0.3 / 0.5 / 0.7 mm) and prepare *through* the provisional, so reduction is referenced to where the porcelain will ultimately sit — not to the existing enamel.
4. **Keep all margins on enamel**; finish cervical/interproximal lines under magnification.

### The three generations of veneer preparation (Coachman 2014 taxonomy)

| Generation | Method | Reference for reduction | Limitation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — Depth-guide | Preset-depth diamond burs directly on the tooth | The existing tooth surface | Standardized but non-individualized; tends to over-reduce |
| 2 — Silicone-index | Diagnostic wax-up + silicone indexes guide freehand prep with re-evaluation | The wax-up, checked indirectly | Operator-dependent; iterative |
| 3 — Mock-up / APT (recommended) | Prepare *through* an intraoral APT carrying the final contours | The planned final restoration | Unsuitable alone for malformed/crowded teeth needing reorientation |
| 3-digital — CAD-CAM mock-up (3D-DSP) | Mill the mock-up from a fused 2D-face + 3D-STL design; use as prep guide | The digital final design | Equipment-dependent; short-term clinical data |

### Evidence propositions

1. **Reduction depth is governed by the substrate-to-target color difference, not the tooth alone.** P = LT − EV, applied per region (Coachman 2014). In the A2→A1 case, middle and incisal thirds required **0 mm** reduction; buccal 0.2–0.5 mm; final LDS ~0.3 mm. [case-report]
2. **Color-change magnitude sets the strategy.** 1–2 shade tones → thin ~0.3 mm laminate, often near-zero prep; ≥3 tones or markedly dark substrate → more invasive prep and intrasulcular margin extension to hide the color transition (Coachman 2014). [case-report]
3. **The mock-up/APT works as the preparation guide, analog or digital.** Gürel (2007) established the analog mock-up→wax-up→APT→silicone-index chain; Cattoni (2016) showed a CAD-CAM PMMA mock-up performs the same role with low 2-year complications (108 PLVs: 1 fracture, 2 sensitivities, 1 recession; 0 debonding/chipping/microleakage). [narrative / prospective n=28]
4. **Minimal-prep veneers (0.2–0.5 mm) equal or exceed conventional (0.3–1.0 mm) in survival.** Ali (2023), SR of 4 comparative studies, refuting prior assumption of conventional superiority; ultra-thin contact-lens feldspathic types add no-anesthesia / no-temporary benefits. [SR — no comparative RCT]
5. **No-prep and conventional are complementary, not competing; indication is enamel- and case-driven.** Both survive 91–100% (no-prep ~97% vs conventional ~92%; no-prep success ≤97.4% when enamel-bonded). No-prep is contraindicated for intrinsic discoloration, malalignment, or major morphology change (overcontour risk) (Chandode 2026). [narrative review]
6. **Incisal-coverage design does not affect survival.** OR 1.25 (NS), 8 studies (Albanesi 2016); choose incisal extension on esthetic/occlusal grounds, not prognosis — expanding prep-design freedom. [SR+MA]
7. **Enamel-confined margins are the non-negotiable success condition.** Dentin-margin exposure ≈ 10× failure rate (El-Mowafy 2018, from Gürel 580 veneers/12 yr); long-term enamel-limited cases survive without failure (Reis 2025, 12+ yr). [narrative / case-report]

### Decision logic — how much to cut

- **Build the additive wax-up / mock-up first.** The more volume it adds, the less you cut.
- **Compute P per region** (LT − EV). Where EV ≥ LT → **prepare nothing** (true no-prep, enamel intact).
- **Match LT to the color change**: small color change → thin ceramic → minimal prep; large color change/dark substrate → thicker ceramic (more masking) → more prep + consider intrasulcular margins.
- **Keep every margin on enamel** regardless of the above — if a planned margin would fall on dentin, the design (not the constraint) must change.
- **Material/incisal-coverage choice is comparatively free** once enamel confinement is met (survival is material- and coverage-insensitive within ceramics; see [[overviews/veneer-material-survival-protocol-overview]]).

### Evolution: analog → quantitative → digital

| Year | Paper | Advance | Form of the prep guide |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2007 | Gürel | "Preparation by design"; APT as prep guide | Analog mock-up → wax-up → APT → silicone index |
| 2014 | Coachman et al. | Color-driven quantification (P = LT − EV); 3-generation taxonomy | APT + per-region math, DSD/Visagism planning |
| 2016 | Cattoni et al. | Fully digital chain; CAD-CAM-milled mock-up; 2-yr outcomes | 3D-DSP (2D face + 3D STL) → milled PMMA mock-up |

### Limitations and gaps

- The two foundational technique papers are a **narrative** (Gürel; here only abstract + section snippets, paywalled) and a **case report** (Coachman) — high conceptual influence, low evidence weight; no comparative trial tests P = LT − EV against freehand prep.
- Cattoni is a **single-arm prospective series** (n=28, 2 yr, proprietary equipment, no control, no quantitative enamel-preservation metric).
- The survival validation (Ali 2023) rests on only 4 heterogeneous comparative studies; **genuine no-prep evidence remains scarce** versus minimal-prep.
- No study quantifies how accurately "preparing through the mock-up" actually limits dentin exposure in routine hands — the enamel-preservation claim is mechanistic, not directly measured at scale.
