## 한국어 핵심요약

> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - 결론: 총의치 수직고경(Occlusal Vertical Dimension, OVD)을 정확히 잡는 **단일 신뢰 기법은 없다** — 여러 방법을 교차 검증해 합의점을 찾는 것이 표준 (Alhajj 2017, Fayad 2025).
> - 방법 분류: **발치 전(pre-extraction)** — 기존 기록·측면 윤곽·두부계측(cephalometric) / **발치 후(post-extraction)** — 안정위+자유공극(rest position + freeway space), 안모·심미, 발음(phonetics), 연하(swallowing), 안면계측(anthropometric), 두부계측.
> - 안정위(rest position) 단독은 불안정: 두부계측 50명 연구에서 **안면 연조직이 실제 하악 위치 변화를 가리고**, 안정위가 변동하며 의치 장착 시 이동 → 무치악 안정위만으로 OVD 결정은 부적합 (Sheppard 1975).
> - 안면계측(anthropometric) 타당도 SR+MA(13편/3275명/11기법): **엄지 길이(thumb length)**가 전체 최강 상관(r≈0.63), 남성은 외안각–구열(distal canthus–rima oris ≈0.57), 여성은 **새끼손가락 길이(little-finger ≈0.46)** — 단 I²≈99%, 비뚤림 위험 높아 보조 지표로만 (Goyal 2026).
> - OVD 오류는 실제 결과를 부른다: 즉각 **+5 mm 증가** 시 뇌파(EEG) 부정 심리지표 상승, **−3 mm 감소** 시 교합력 저하 (Matsuda 2014) — 잘못된 VD는 심리·기능에 측정 가능한 악영향.
> - **수직고경 과다(증가, over-opening) 징후**: 긴 하안면, 입술 다물기 곤란·긴장, 치아 부딪힘(clicking), 연하·발음 곤란, 잔존치조제 외상, 근피로 (Fayad 2025).
> - **수직고경 과소(감소, over-closing) 징후**: 짧은 하안면, 구각부 처짐·구각구순염(angular cheilitis), **이순구(mentolabial fold) 깊어짐**, 턱끝 돌출, **이근(mentalis muscle) 과활성으로 턱끝이 오톨도톨·뭉치는 모양**, 볼·혀 깨물기 (Fayad 2025).
> - 질문의 "하악 턱끝 복숭아 형태"는 위 **과소 VD(over-closing)에서 이근 과활성으로 턱끝 피부가 자갈/뭉친 복숭아씨처럼 보이는 소견**에 해당할 가능성이 높음 — 즉 VD를 너무 낮게 잡았다는 신호로 읽고, 안정위·자유공극·발음·안모를 재평가해 VD를 적정 회복하는 방향 (단, 본 위키 논문은 "복숭아"라는 용어 자체를 명시하진 않음 — Fayad의 mentalis strain 기술에 근거한 해석).
> - 임상 워크플로: 한 가지에 의존하지 말고 ①안정위−자유공극(2–4 mm) ②발음(/s/ closest speaking space, /m/) ③안모·입술지지 ④필요시 안면계측·두부계측을 **교차 확인**, 환자 적응 고려해 점진 조정.
> - 용어: 자유공극(Freeway Space, 안정위-교합위 차 ~2–4 mm), 안정위(rest position), 발음공간(closest speaking space).

## One-line Summary

Across one comprehensive review (Fayad 2025), one method-classification review (Alhajj 2017), one anthropometric-validity SR+MA (Goyal 2026), one EEG consequence study (Matsuda 2014) and a classic cephalometric rest-position study (Sheppard 1975), the consistent message is that **no single method reliably establishes complete-denture OVD** — clinicians must cross-check several (rest position + freeway space, phonetics, esthetics/facial, anthropometric, cephalometric), because the rest position alone is unstable and a wrong VD produces measurable functional and psychological harm; reduced VD specifically presents as a shortened lower face with mentalis strain (the "bunched chin" sign), and increased VD as a strained, elongated face.

## 한줄요약

총의치 OVD는 단일 기법으로 정확히 못 잡으며(안정위는 특히 불안정, Sheppard 1975), 여러 방법 교차검증이 표준 — 안면계측은 보조 지표(엄지/새끼손가락, Goyal 2026), VD 오류는 심리·교합력에 실제 악영향(Matsuda 2014); VD 과소 시 짧은 하안면·이근 과활성(턱끝 뭉침), 과다 시 긴 하안면·입술 긴장 (Fayad 2025).

## Thesis

Determining the occlusal vertical dimension (OVD) in an edentulous patient is the step with the least intrinsic landmark — there are no teeth to dictate it — and the five papers here triangulate the problem from four angles.

**1. There is no single accurate method (Alhajj 2017, Fayad 2025).** Alhajj classifies methods into *pre-extraction* (pre-extraction records, profile/silhouette tracing, cephalometry) and *post-extraction* (physiologic rest position minus freeway space, esthetics/facial appearance, swallowing, craniofacial/anthropometric landmarks, cephalometry, phonetics, pre-existing dentures). Fayad's comprehensive review extends this from classical to digital methods (3-D scanning, CBCT, CAD/CAM, AI) and reaches the same verdict: all are empirical with error, so **combine techniques and seek convergence**.

**2. The physiologic rest position — the workhorse method — is itself unstable (Sheppard 1975).** On cephalometric radiographs of 50 denture wearers, facial soft tissue *masked* skeletal mandibular movement, the edentulous rest position varied over time and usually *shifted on denture insertion*, and its range widened with denture age/experience. Conclusion: the edentulous rest position is "not particularly suitable" for OVD, least of all via facial measurements. This is the historical anchor for the modern "don't rely on rest position alone" caution.

**3. Anthropometric (facial-measurement) methods are usable but only as adjuncts (Goyal 2026).** This SR+MA (13 cross-sectional studies, 3275 participants, 11 facial methods) found *thumb length* the strongest overall correlate of OVD (r ≈ 0.63 common-effect), with sex-specific best methods (men: distal outer-canthus–rima-oris ≈ 0.57; women: little-finger length ≈ 0.46). But heterogeneity was extreme (I² up to 99%) and risk of bias high — so these are corroborating, not standalone, references.

**4. Getting VD wrong has measurable consequences (Matsuda 2014).** In 21 denture wearers given duplicate dentures at −3 mm and +5 mm VDO, an acute **+5 mm increase raised an EEG negative-mood indicator** after chewing, and a **−3 mm decrease lowered occlusal force** — objective evidence that VD error perturbs both psychology and function, justifying the care the determination methods demand.

## Method matrix

| Method | Class | Evidence here | Practical note |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-extraction records / profile / cephalometry | pre-extraction | Alhajj 2017, Fayad 2025 | Best when available; rarely is in fully edentulous |
| Physiologic rest position − freeway space (~2–4 mm) | post-extraction | Alhajj 2017; **caution** Sheppard 1975 | Workhorse but rest position is variable/maskable |
| Esthetics / facial appearance / lip support | post-extraction | Fayad 2025 | Judges over- vs under-opening (see signs below) |
| Phonetics (closest speaking space, /s/, /m/) | post-extraction | Alhajj 2017, Fayad 2025 | Functional cross-check |
| Swallowing | post-extraction | Alhajj 2017 | Adjunct |
| Anthropometric / facial measurements (thumb, finger, canthus–rima) | post-extraction | **Goyal 2026 SR+MA** | Adjunct only; high heterogeneity |
| Cephalometric | both | Alhajj 2017, Sheppard 1975 | Skeletal > facial markers |

## Clinical signs of incorrect VD (answering the "chin" question)

Grounded in the Fayad 2025 review of incorrect-VD signs:

- **Excessive / increased VD (over-opening)**: elongated lower face; strained, parted, or incompetent lips; clicking of teeth during speech; difficulty swallowing; trauma/soreness of residual ridges; muscle fatigue; reduced freeway space.
- **Reduced / decreased VD (over-closing)**: shortened lower face; drooping oral commissures with angular cheilitis; **deepened mentolabial (chin) fold**; prominent ("witch's") chin; **mentalis muscle strain/hyperactivity producing a bunched, dimpled chin**; cheek/tongue biting; excessive freeway space.

The clinical picture of a **"peach-pit / bunched" appearance over the mandibular chin** corresponds to **mentalis hyperactivity from a reduced (over-closed) VD** — i.e. a signal that the vertical dimension has been set too low. The corrective reading is to re-evaluate VD with the multi-method cross-check above (rest position + freeway space, phonetics, lip support/esthetics) and restore an appropriate dimension. (Note: the source papers describe mentalis strain / deepened mentolabial fold but do not use the word "peach" — this is the literature-grounded interpretation of that descriptive sign.)
