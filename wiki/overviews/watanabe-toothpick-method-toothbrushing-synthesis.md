## 한국어 핵심요약

> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - 핵심: 와타나베 이쑤시개법(Watanabe Toothpick Method, TPM)은 2열모(double-row) 칫솔모 끝을 치간(interdental)에 이쑤시개처럼 눌러 넣어 닦는 수동 칫솔질 기법으로, 위키 7편을 원전(1998)부터 최신(2025)까지 종합.
> - 두 가지 차별적 기전: ① 인접면(proximal) 치면세균막(dental plaque) 제거력 우수, ② 단순 청소가 아니라 치은 창상치유(wound healing) 자극 — 치은 기저세포 증식 스케일러 대비 약 2.5배↑·섬유아세포 활성화(Lee 2020 인용).
> - 원전 근거: Morita 1998 RCT(n=20)에서 TPM이 Bass법보다 **인접면** 플라그를 유의하게 더 제거 — 와타나베(T. Watanabe)가 공저자인 명명 논문.
> - 그러나 기법 비교 근거 자체는 약함: Rajwani 2020 SR(13편)은 "어느 수동 칫솔질법도 우월하다 결론 불가"(이질성 과도), TPM은 13편 중 단 1편(Morita)에서만 검증.
> - 지도 빈도가 중요: Ishikawa 1995(지역사회)에서 TPM 지도 3회군만 치은 발적·종창 유의 개선, 1회군은 1년 후 무변화 — 반복 교육 > 단일 교육.
> - 특수·고위험 집단 확장: 2형 당뇨(T2DM) 치주염서 SRP에 TPM 추가 시 치은출혈(BOP) −16.5% vs SRP단독 −7.3%, 혈청 내독소(serum endotoxin) 유의 감소한 유일 군(Lee 2020); 정신질환자 위생프로그램의 한 요소로 플라그 감소(Mun 2013, 단독효과 분리 불가).
> - 임플란트 주위 점막염(Peri-Implant Mucositis, PIM): TPM은 약제 전달 수단으로 유효하나 **기계적 단독(식염수-TPM)은 12균종 중 5종을 오히려 증가** — 항균제(천연 Cibotium 추출물 또는 클로르헥시딘) 병용 필수(Kim 2025).
> - 입증된 효과는 대부분 "전문가 시술" 기준(Lee·Kim·Mun 모두 훈련된 술자가 시행) — 환자 자가 수행의 동등성은 미확인, 추가 내원·짧은 추적이 교란.
> - 명명 주의(중요): TPM(칫솔질법, 유익) ≠ **목재 이쑤시개**(치간청소 도구)의 과용. 후자는 치간유두(interdental papilla) 소실·블랙트라이앵글 유발(El Haddad 2026, 수직기법·>3회/일·>3년 악화) — 같은 "toothpick"이지만 반대 방향.
> - 임상 결론: TPM은 인접면 청소 + 치은 치유 + 출혈 감소라는 강점으로 당뇨·출혈경향·임플란트 유지관리에서 부가가치가 있는 **보조·전문가 술식**이나, 비교근거가 빈약하고 단독 항균력이 부족해 "표준 1차 자가 칫솔질법"으로 단정할 근거는 아직 없음.

## One-line Summary

Synthesis of 7 wiki papers (1995–2026) on the Watanabe toothpick method (TPM) of toothbrushing — a double-row-brush interdental technique with a dual mechanism (superior proximal plaque removal + gingival wound-healing stimulation, ~2.5× basement-cell proliferation): the origin RCT shows TPM > Bass on proximal plaque (Morita 1998), benefit extends to diabetic periodontitis (less bleeding, lower serum endotoxin) and peri-implant mucositis (as an agent-delivery vehicle, but mechanical-alone allows bacterial regrowth), instruction frequency matters, yet technique-comparison evidence is weak (no manual technique proven superior; Rajwani 2020 SR) and the *wooden toothpick* as an interdental tool is the opposite — a cause of papilla loss/black triangles (El Haddad 2026).

## 한줄요약

와타나베 이쑤시개법(TPM, 칫솔질법) 위키 7편(1995–2026) 종합 — 2열모 칫솔로 치간을 닦는 기법으로 인접면 플라그 제거 + 치은 치유 자극(기저세포 증식 약 2.5배)의 이중 기전. 원전 RCT는 TPM>Bass(Morita 1998), 당뇨 치주염·임플란트 주위 점막염으로 효과 확장(단, 기계적 단독은 세균 재증식→항균제 병용 필요), 지도 빈도가 중요하나 기법 비교근거는 약함(Rajwani SR). *목재* 이쑤시개(치간도구)는 반대로 치간유두 소실·블랙트라이앵글 유발(El Haddad 2026)이라 명명 혼동 주의.

## Summary

The **Watanabe toothpick method (TPM, 이쑤시개법)** is a manual toothbrushing technique using a **double-row (2-row) toothbrush** whose bristle tips are pressed into the interdental embrasures in a toothpick-like motion. This page synthesizes the 7 papers the wiki holds, spanning the method's **origin (1998)** to its most recent **peri-implant application (2025)**, plus the **naming-confusion counterpoint** about wooden toothpicks (2026).

The method's distinctive claim is a **dual mechanism**: it is not only a plaque-removal technique but a **gingival wound-healing stimulus**. Histology cited in Lee 2020 reports TPM raises gingival basement-cell proliferation roughly **2.5× versus a scaler** and activates fibroblasts and junctional epithelium, producing repair-phase changes rather than mere mechanical cleaning. This biology frames why TPM's measured benefits cluster on **gingival bleeding and inflammation**, not just plaque scores.

**Evidence arc.**

| Paper | Design | Population | Toothpick-method finding |
|---|---|---|---|
| [[periodontics/morita-1998-toothpick-method-bass-supragingival-plaque-rct]] | RCT, n=20 | dental students | **Origin RCT** (Watanabe coauthor): TPM removed significantly more **proximal** plaque than Bass |
| [[periodontics/ishikawa-1995-toothpick-method-instruction-frequency-community-perio]] | community comparative, 1 yr | town residents | TPM instruction **3× > 1×**: only repeated instruction improved gingival redness/swelling + CPITN |
| [[periodontics/mun-2013-toothpick-method-dental-hygiene-mental-disorders-rct]] | RCT, n=73 | mental-disorder patients | TPM as one component of a video+brochure+TPM program → plaque index ↓ (P<0.0001); isolated effect not separable |
| [[periodontics/rajwani-2020-manual-toothbrushing-techniques-plaque-gingivitis-sr]] | SR, 13 studies | adults | **No manual technique proven superior** (excess heterogeneity); TPM tested in only 1 study (Morita) where it beat Bass |
| [[periodontics/lee-2020-watanabe-toothpick-method-srp-diabetic-periodontitis-rct]] | RCT, n=60 | T2DM periodontitis | SRP + weekly professional TPM vs SRP alone: **BOP −16.5% vs −7.3%**; only TPM arm cut **serum endotoxin** (−0.78 EU/mL, p=0.003) |
| [[periodontics/kim-2025-toothpick-method-cibotium-peri-implant-mucositis-rct]] | RCT, n=60 | peri-implant mucositis | All arms TPM-delivered; **saline-TPM (mechanical only) increased 5/12 bacteria** — needs an antimicrobial agent (CB extract / CHX) |
| [[periodontics/el-haddad-2026-toothpick-use-interdental-papilla-loss-cross-sectional]] | cross-sectional, n=69 | toothpick users | **Wooden toothpick** (NOT the brushing method): overuse → interdental papilla loss / black triangles |

**What the evidence supports.**
- **Proximal plaque removal** superior to Bass — but on a single small RCT (Morita 1998), echoed only weakly by the Rajwani SR.
- **Gingival bleeding/inflammation control**, especially as a **professional adjunct** in higher-risk patients — diabetic periodontitis (Lee 2020) and community programs (Ishikawa 1995).
- **Systemic signal**: lower serum endotoxin in diabetics — consistent with the wound-healing/sealing mechanism.
- **Vehicle role** in peri-implant maintenance — TPM delivers an antimicrobial agent effectively (Kim 2025).

**What the evidence does NOT support.**
- TPM as a **proven-superior standard self-care technique** — the technique-comparison literature is too heterogeneous to crown any manual method (Rajwani 2020).
- **Mechanical TPM alone** as adequate antimicrobial control around implants — saline-TPM allowed bacterial regrowth; an agent is required (Kim 2025).
- Self-performed equivalence — proven benefits came from **trained/professional delivery**, with extra-visit and short-follow-up confounds (Lee, Kim, Mun).

## Decision Points

1. **Consider TPM when** the goal is interproximal plaque, gingival inflammation/bleeding control, or a healing stimulus — particularly in **bleeding-prone / diabetic** patients and **peri-implant** maintenance.
2. **Delivery & teaching**: benefit is strongest with **professional delivery and repeated instruction** (Ishikawa: 3× > 1×). Treat self-performed TPM as unproven-equivalent and reinforce technique.
3. **Around implants, pair with an antimicrobial** — mechanical TPM alone is insufficient (Kim 2025).
4. **Do not conflate with wooden toothpicks.** Counsel against frequent/vertical **wooden-toothpick** interdental poking, which is associated with **papilla loss / black triangles** (El Haddad 2026). The brushing method and the wooden tool share a name only.

## Limitations of the Evidence Base

- Small RCTs (n=20–73), several **abstract-only** (Morita, Ishikawa, Mun, El Haddad) and **Japan/Korea-centric**.
- The single SR (Rajwani 2020) could not pool data; TPM rests on one direct comparison.
- Mechanism data (2.5× basement-cell proliferation) is cited secondhand, not independently re-verified here.
- Most outcome gains are **professional-delivery**, short follow-up (≤12 wk in the systemic RCTs).
