## 한국어 핵심요약

> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - 핵심 명제: 지지 임플란트주위 치료(Supportive Peri-Implant Therapy, SPiT)는 효과가 있으나, "고정 주기"가 아니라 **위험도 기반 개별화 recall**이라야 한다 — 진단 측정(measurement)이 recall 주기를 *계산*하고, 그 recall이 임플란트주위 질환을 늦춘다는 측정→결정→유지의 폐회로(closed loop)가 4편을 관통하는 뼈대.
> - 근거 사다리: 체계적 고찰 2편(Mojaver 2025·25편, Lanzetti 2024·11편) ▸ 내러티브/임상 2편(Ramseier 2024 진단 도구 카탈로그, Barclay 2026 임상 framing) — RCT 단독·메타분석은 없음(이질성으로 pooling 불가).
> - 효과 정량(Mojaver 2025): 위험도 기반 SPiT는 무관리·고정주기 대비 탐침깊이(Probing Depth, PD) **1.0~1.5 mm** 더 감소, 탐침시출혈(Bleeding on Probing, BoP) **10~25%p** 더 감소, 변연골소실 **≤1 mm vs ~1.5 mm**(≥24개월), 장기 코호트에서 임플란트주위염·임플란트 상실 **40~70%** 더 낮음.
> - recall 주기: 고위험 환자는 흔히 **3~4개월**(Mojaver 2025), Ramseier 2024는 잔존 치주낭 분포·%BoP·흡연·당뇨로 **3~12개월** 개별 산출(perio-tools.com/spt, 883명·11,842 SPT 방문 기반).
> - 검증된 monitoring 측정: 탐침·%BoP·방사선이 핵심. 임플란트 탐침은 **≤0.15 N**(치아 0.25 N)로 — 0.15→0.25 N 증가 시 임플란트 BoP가 +13.7% 위양성(치아는 +6.6%). 흡연자는 출혈이 억제돼 %BoP 임계값을 더 낮게(**<16%** vs 비흡연 <20~23%).
> - 임플란트주위 안정(Ramseier 2024): PD <5 mm, BoP·배농 없음, 기준선 대비 골소실 <2 mm(초기 remodeling 1.5 mm까지는 생리적).
> - full-arch의 특수성(Lanzetti 2024): 고정성 full-arch 보철은 가장 청소하기 어려운 재건 — 전문 구강위생 **6개월마다** + 보철 제거 **최소 연 1회**가 실용적 기본값(11편 중 저비뚤림 0편이라 확정 주기는 불가).
> - 인구 변화 framing(Barclay 2026): 임플란트가 무치악 소수에서 일반 인구로 확산 → **환자 맞춤형**(uniform 아님) 유지관리가 필수, 2018 치주 신분류가 임플란트주위 질환을 처음 공식 포함한 것을 틀로.
> - 보조요법(Mojaver 2025): 광역동 항균치료(antimicrobial Photodynamic Therapy, aPDT)만 기계적 debridement 대비 추가 PD 감소(~0.6~0.8 mm)를 반복 입증 — 레이저·성장인자 겔은 일관된 추가 이득 없음.
> - 근거 한계: 4편 모두 메타분석 부재(이질성/저비뚤림 연구 부족), 전신질환 보고 빈약(Mojaver — 흡연 정량 20%·당뇨 8%만). 임상 takeaway: ① 고정 연 1회 recall 폐기, **위험도 기반 3~12개월** ② 탐침·%BoP·방사선으로 stability criteria 충족 여부 측정 ③ full-arch는 6개월 OH + ≥연 1회 보철 제거 ④ 흡연·당뇨는 더 잦게 — [[overviews/peri-implantitis-management-overview]]의 *치료*에 대응하는 *예방·유지* 축.

## One-line Summary
Synthesis of 4 papers (2 SRs, 2 narrative/clinical reviews): supportive peri-implant therapy (SPiT) works, but only as **individualized risk-based recall** rather than a fixed annual schedule — diagnostic monitoring (probing, %BoP, radiographs) should compute the recall interval (3–12 months), which then attenuates disease (PD −1.0–1.5 mm, BoP −10–25 pp, 40–70% lower peri-implantitis incidence in adherent patients); full-arch fixed prostheses are the hardest case (6-month OH + ≥annual prosthesis removal); the evidence base is consistent in direction but heterogeneous (no meta-analysis, few low-RoB studies).

## 한줄요약
4편 종합(SR 2·내러티브/임상 2): 지지 임플란트주위 치료(SPiT)는 효과가 있으나 고정 연 1회가 아닌 **위험도 기반 개별화 recall**이라야 한다 — 진단 측정(탐침·%BoP·방사선)이 recall 주기(3~12개월)를 산출하고 그 recall이 질환을 늦춤(PD −1.0~1.5 mm, BoP −10~25%p, 순응 환자 임플란트주위염 40~70%↓); full-arch는 6개월 OH + ≥연 1회 보철 제거; 근거는 방향 일관하나 이질성 커 메타분석 불가.

## Summary

Peri-implant mucositis and peri-implantitis are increasingly prevalent as implant therapy spreads from the early-edentulous minority into the wider general population (Barclay 2026). The clinical question is no longer *whether* to maintain implants but *how* — and the four papers held here converge on a single architecture: **maintenance is a closed loop of measurement → decision → care**. Diagnostic measures do not merely flag disease; they compute the recall interval and the risk tier (Ramseier 2024), individualized risk-based recall then measurably slows disease (Mojaver 2025), and the hardest-to-clean reconstructions (full-arch fixed prostheses) need a dedicated removal cadence (Lanzetti 2024).

**The unifying thesis: abandon one-size-fits-all annual recall in favor of genuinely individualized, risk-based maintenance.** This is asserted independently from three angles — the efficacy SR (Mojaver), the diagnostic-framework review (Ramseier), and the clinical-framing article (Barclay) — and the full-arch SR (Lanzetti) operationalizes it for the worst-access case.

### What monitoring measures are validated (Ramseier 2024)

The diagnostic system that *triggers* maintenance is the load-bearing part. Ramseier catalogs the full toolkit but the validated, decision-driving core is **probing, %BoP, and radiography**:

- **Peri-implant probing safety**: use non-metallic probes; force **≤0.15 N around implants** (vs 0.25 N around teeth). Raising force from 0.15→0.25 N inflates BoP by +13.7% at implants vs only +6.6% at teeth — over-forceful probing manufactures false-positive bleeding.
- **Smoking-adjusted %BoP thresholds**: stability at **<20–23%** (non-/former smokers) but **<16% for smokers**, because smokers suppress gingival bleeding and mask active inflammation.
- **Peri-implant stability criteria**: PD <5 mm, no BoP/suppuration, <2 mm bone loss from baseline (initial remodeling up to 1.5 mm may be physiological).
- **Interval algorithm**: residual PPD distribution + %BoP + furcation + smoking + diabetes → an individualized **3–12 month** recall (perio-tools.com/spt; derived from 11,842 SPT visits across 883 patients).
- **Compliance matters**: patients attending >50% of SPT visits *earlier* than scheduled lost 0.60 teeth vs 1.45 for later attenders over 20 years (p<0.0001) — adherence is itself an outcome lever.
- Biomarkers (chair-side aMMP-8 / ImplantSafe, PICF IL-1β & MMP-8), RFA/ISQ, and ultrasound are catalogued as emerging adjuncts but do **not** replace probing + bone-level radiography.

### Does SPiT actually work — and how much (Mojaver 2025)

Across 25 studies the signal is consistent and clinically meaningful, with **risk-based recall outperforming both fixed-interval and no maintenance**:

- **Probing depth**: SPiT groups −1.0–1.5 mm vs control; largest single gain −1.5 mm (Heitz-Mayfield & Salvi 2016 5-yr RCT: 3.5 vs 5.0 mm). Risk-based protocols 3.0–3.2 mm vs 4.5–4.8 mm standard care.
- **BoP**: −10–25 percentage points (e.g., Frisch 2019 12% vs 32%; Monje 2015 10% vs 30%).
- **Marginal bone loss**: ≤1 mm over ≥24 months vs ~1.5 mm in controls (Costa 2019 ~0.8 vs ~1.4 mm; Frisch 2019 1.02 vs 1.53 mm).
- **Disease/implant loss**: long-term (≥36 months) cohorts show **40–70% lower** peri-implantitis incidence or implant loss in adherent vs non-adherent patients.
- **Adjuncts**: aPDT is the only adjunct with repeated independent evidence of additional benefit (~0.6–0.8 mm PD); laser and growth-factor gels show no consistent gain over mechanical debridement.

High clinical/methodological heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis — the synthesis is narrative.

### The full-arch problem (Lanzetti 2024)

Fixed full-arch reconstructions are the hardest restoration for a patient to keep clean, so access drives a distinct sub-question: how often to *remove the prosthesis* for professional care. The 11-study PRISMA SR could not derive a definitive interval (none of the 11 studies was at low risk of bias) but offers a pragmatic default: **professional oral hygiene every 6 months + prosthesis removal at least once per year**, tailored to risk. This is the cleansability-limited corner of the general risk-based rule, not a separate principle.

### Reconciling the tension — direction agrees, certainty does not

There is **no genuine contradiction across the four** — all four point the same way (individualized maintenance reduces peri-implant disease). The real tension is **strength of evidence vs strength of recommendation**:

- Mojaver's efficacy signal is consistent and quantified, but heterogeneity blocked meta-analysis, and systemic-comorbidity reporting is poor (only 20% of studies quantified smokers, 8% diabetes).
- Lanzetti's full-arch recommendation rests on **zero low-RoB studies** — an explicitly low-certainty default.
- Ramseier is a single-author narrative review (no systematic search or grading), and Barclay is an abstract-only clinical commentary with no quantitative data.

So the **action** (risk-based recall, validated monitoring, full-arch removal cadence) is well-motivated and internally consistent, but the **evidence grade** is moderate-to-low. The defensible stance is to adopt the framework while acknowledging that exact intervals are pragmatic, not proven.

### Clinical decision — recall and monitoring protocol

1. **Replace fixed annual recall with risk-based intervals.** Default to **3–12 months** computed from residual PPD, %BoP, furcation, smoking, and diabetes (Ramseier algorithm); high-risk patients commonly land at **3–4 months** (Mojaver).
2. **Measure correctly at every visit.** Probe implants with non-metallic probes at **≤0.15 N**; judge stability against PD <5 mm, no BoP/suppuration, <2 mm bone loss from baseline; apply smoking-adjusted %BoP thresholds (<16% smokers, <20–23% otherwise).
3. **Full-arch fixed prostheses**: professional OH every 6 months + remove prosthesis **≥1×/year** for thorough access (Lanzetti default).
4. **Smokers and diabetics**: shorten intervals and reinforce home care; these groups have the strongest risk amplification and the weakest reporting (Mojaver gap).
5. **When mechanical debridement fails to quell inflammation**, add **aPDT** as the best-supported adjunct; do not rely on laser or growth-factor gels for additive benefit.
6. **Treat this as the prevention/maintenance axis** that sits upstream of active disease treatment in [[overviews/peri-implantitis-management-overview]] — measurement and risk-based recall keep mucositis from progressing to peri-implantitis.
