> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - **핵심 결론**: 구취(Halitosis)는 전 세계 성인 약 31.8%에서 발생하는 흔한 구강 문제로, 80–90%는 구강내(설태·치주염·구강위생 불량), 10–20%는 구강외(이비인후과·소화기·약물) 원인에서 비롯된다.
> - **주요 수치**: 유병률 31.8% (95% CI 24.6–39.0%, n=13 인구기반 연구; Silva 2017); 치주염 환자에서 구취 위험 약 4배 증가 (OR 4.05–4.52; Wang 2024, Silva 2017); H. pylori 감염 시 구취 위험 2.85배, 제균 성공 후 83% 감소(RR 0.17; Dou 2016).
> - **측정 문제**: 관능검사(Organoleptic Test, OLT)가 현재 유일한 임상 기준이며, 구취측정기(할리미터, OralChroma, GC)는 OLT와 중등도 상관(r=0.65–0.76)에 불과해 대체 불가(Szalai 2023). 특히 할리미터는 이질성이 극히 높음(I²=95%).
> - **분기점 1 — 구강내 원인**: 황화수소(Hydrogen Sulfide, H₂S) + 메틸메캅탄(Methyl Mercaptan, CH₃SH) 상승 → 설태·치주 관리 우선. 관능점수 ≥2이면 치주 평가 필수.
> - **분기점 2 — 구강외 원인**: 이황화디메틸(Dimethyl Sulfide, DMS, (CH₃)₂S) 상승 시 소화기·신장·폐 원인 의심. 약물 복용력 반드시 확인(아미노티올계는 최대 100% 발생; Mortazavi 2020).
> - **프로바이오틱스(Probiotics)**: 단기(≤4주) VSC 및 OLT 점수 유의 감소(SMD −0.58/−0.26; Huang 2022), 4주 이후 VSC 효과 소실. López-Valverde 2022의 4 RCT 메타분석은 전체 효과 비유의(p=0.53). 현 근거는 일관성 부족 — 보조요법으로 사용 가능하나 단독 표준치료로 권고 불가.
> - **광역동요법(Photodynamic Therapy, PDT)**: 즉시 H₂S 감소(MD −34.49; Wang 2022)에 유의하나 7일 이후 혀클리너 대비 차이 소실. 재집락화로 인한 지속 효과 없음 → 술전·단기 처치에 적합.
> - **삶의 질(OHRQoL)**: 성인에서 구취가 있으면 구강건강관련삶의질(OHRQoL) 유의하게 저하(SMD 0.51; Cassiano 2021). 청소년에서는 유의한 연관 없음. 청소년·젊은 성인에서도 불안·우울·사회적 고립과 일관된 연관(Briceag 2023).
> - **화학감각(Chemosensory) 문제**: 후각 둔화 환자는 자신의 구취를 스스로 인지하지 못해 자가보고 기반 유병률이 과소 또는 과대 추정될 수 있음(Schertel Cassiano 2023).
> - **임상 핵심**: 구취 평가 시 ① 설태 제거 + 구강위생 지도, ② 치주 검사, ③ 약물력·전신질환 확인, ④ 지속 시 H. pylori 검사 고려, ⑤ 심리적 부담(할리토포비아 포함) 스크리닝 순으로 접근한다.

## One-line Summary

Halitosis affects ~32% of adults globally; 80–90% is intra-oral (coated tongue, periodontitis), measurement by halitometer correlates only moderately with the gold-standard organoleptic test, and current evidence supports short-term benefit from probiotics and PDT but neither replaces mechanical oral hygiene for sustained control.

## 한줄요약

구취는 전 세계 성인 약 32%에서 발생하며 80–90%는 구강내 원인(설태·치주염)이고, 구취측정기는 관능검사와 중등도 상관에 불과하며, 프로바이오틱스·광역동요법은 단기 효과가 있으나 지속적 관리는 기계적 구강위생이 핵심이다.

## 1. Etiology

### Intra-oral (80–90%)

Memon et al. (2022) SR of 19 studies (2014–2020) confirmed that the vast majority of halitosis originates within the mouth. The hierarchy of intra-oral contributors is:

1. **Coated tongue (tongue coating)** — the single most consistently reported factor across all study designs. Anaerobic bacteria residing in tongue fissures metabolize sulfur-containing amino acids (cysteine, methionine) into volatile sulfur compounds (VSC).
2. **Periodontal disease** — *Porphyromonas gingivalis*, *Treponema denticola*, *Tannerella forsythia*, and *Prevotella* spp. are the primary VSC producers in periodontal pockets.
3. **Poor oral hygiene** — interproximal debris and subgingival plaque accumulation.
4. **Reduced salivary flow (xerostomia)** — salivary hypofunction removes the buffering and mechanical clearance of VSC precursors.

**VSC chemistry — intra-oral signature:**
- Hydrogen sulfide (H₂S) and methyl mercaptan (CH₃SH) predominate.
- Produced via degradation of cysteine, methionine, and glutathione by gram-negative anaerobes.

### Extra-oral (10–20%)

| Source category | Specific condition |
|---|---|
| ENT | Adenoid hypertrophy, allergic rhinitis, post-nasal drip |
| Gastrointestinal | *Helicobacter pylori* (gastric urease + VSC production) |
| Hepatic / infectious | Viral hepatitis B, liver failure (dimethyl sulfide, trimethylamine) |
| Neurological | Parkinson's disease (reduced swallowing, dry mouth) |
| Iatrogenic | Fixed orthodontic appliances, drug-induced (see Section 5) |
| Psychiatric | Depression (impairs oral hygiene motivation) |

**Extra-oral VSC marker:** Dimethyl sulfide (DMS, (CH₃)₂S) elevation is the chemical discriminator of a systemic/extra-oral origin — distinguishing it from intra-oral H₂S/CH₃SH predominance (Memon 2022).

### Microbiome Role

The VSC-producing microbiome is not limited to classic periodontal pathogens. Salivary bacteria, tongue dorsum communities, and even gastric *H. pylori* (via cystathionine-β-synthase / cystathionine-γ-lyase pathways) all contribute. Dysbiosis of the tongue microbiome — which parallels but is not identical to subgingival dysbiosis — is the proximal driver of physiological halitosis in otherwise periodontally healthy individuals.

## 2. Prevalence

Silva et al. (2017) conducted the first SR+MA estimating global halitosis prevalence from population-based data (13 studies, random-effects model):

| Statistic | Value |
|---|---|
| Pooled prevalence (all measurement types) | **31.8%** (95% CI 24.6–39.0%) |
| Heterogeneity | High |
| Meta-regression predictor 1 | Year of publication (more recent studies → higher prevalence) |
| Meta-regression predictor 2 | Country socioeconomic status |

This ~1-in-3 burden estimate combines clinically-measured and self-reported halitosis across studies. The rising temporal trend likely reflects both methodological evolution (more objective measurement over time) and genuine epidemiological change (dietary shift, antibiotic use, population aging). Importantly, self-reported prevalence substantially exceeds clinically-measured prevalence in studies that include both — olfactory desensitization causes many patients to underreport, while anxiety/halitophobia causes others to overreport (Schertel Cassiano 2023).

## 3. Measurement — Why Organoleptic and Halitometric Assessments Do Not Agree

Szalai et al. (2023) SR+MA (76 studies, n=14,635) is the definitive evidence that **current halitometers cannot replace the organoleptic test (OLT)**:

| Device | Pooled Spearman r vs. OLT | I² | Clinical implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| Halimeter (electrochemical) | 0.65 | 95% | High variability; limited generalizability |
| OralChroma (portable GC) | 0.69 | 12% | Most consistent device evidence |
| Bench gas chromatography | 0.76 | 0% | Best correlation; impractical for routine use |

No device achieves strong (r > 0.80) clinical agreement with OLT. SROC analysis shows no device is significantly superior to another. The Halimeter's extremely high I² (95%) means its readings are inconsistent across clinical contexts.

**Why they diverge:**
- OLT captures the full olfactory signature — including non-VSC volatile compounds (indole, skatole, short-chain fatty acids, amines) that halitometers ignore.
- Halimeter measures total electrochemical VSC, without speciation.
- OralChroma speciates H₂S, CH₃SH, and DMS — more informative but still misses non-VSC odors.
- Patient-side factors (mouth breathing vs. nasal, morning vs. afternoon sampling, recent food) introduce measurement variability.

**Clinical bottom line:** OLT (trained examiner, Rosenberg 0–5 scale, standardized conditions) remains the required reference standard. Devices serve as adjuncts for monitoring VSC trends or differentiating intra- vs. extra-oral sources, not as replacements for sensory assessment.

**Chemosensory confound:** Approximately 13–23% of the adult population reports some smell or taste alteration (NHANES data). Halitosis patients with co-existing anosmia/hyposmia fail to perceive their own malodor, systematically biasing self-reported prevalence downward. Conversely, patients with halitophobia may over-perceive absent odor (Schertel Cassiano 2023).

## 4. Systemic Associations

### Periodontitis

Two SR+MAs quantify the periodontitis–halitosis link:

| Study | n studies | OR (OLT method) | OR (VSC method) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Silva et al. 2017 | 5 (n=population-based) | 3.16 (95% CI 1.12–8.95) | — |
| Wang et al. 2024 | 9 (n=2176) | 4.05 (95% CI 1.76–9.30) | 4.52 (95% CI 1.89–10.83) |

The ~3–4-fold OR is consistent across two independent SR+MAs a decade apart and across both measurement methods (OLT and VSC). Meta-regression in Silva 2017 showed that ~45% of between-study heterogeneity is explained by the halitosis diagnostic criteria and ~24% by the periodontitis case definition — confirming that standardization of both definitions is the key methodological priority.

**Causal mechanism:** Gram-negative periodontal anaerobes in deep pockets produce H₂S and CH₃SH. Periodontal pocket probing depth and bleeding on probing correlate with VSC levels. Successful periodontal treatment reduces halitosis scores in interventional studies (not covered in our current SR+MA base).

### Helicobacter pylori

Dou et al. (2016) meta-analysis (21 studies, English + Chinese literature):

| Clinical question | Effect |
|---|---|
| H. pylori prevalence in halitosis patients vs. controls | OR 4.03 (95% CI 1.41–11.50) |
| Halitosis in H. pylori(+) vs. H. pylori(−) patients | OR 2.85 (95% CI 1.40–5.83) |
| Halitosis after successful vs. unsuccessful eradication | RR 0.17 (83% reduction) |
| Halitosis before vs. after successful eradication | RR 4.78 (5× more prevalent pre-eradication) |

**Biochemical mechanism:** *H. pylori* expresses cystathionine-β-synthase and cystathionine-γ-lyase, producing H₂S and CH₃SH in the gastric mucosa. Urease-generated ammonia contributes additional odorous volatiles. These reach the breath via pulmonary excretion. Importantly, H. pylori can also colonize the oral cavity directly (dental plaque), providing a local intra-oral source.

### OHRQoL Impact

Cassiano et al. (2021) SR+MA (10 cross-sectional studies, n=2,692):

- **Overall SMD 0.51** (95% CI 0.27–0.75) — moderate effect, statistically significant
- **Adults only**: effect is significant regardless of OHRQoL instrument (OHIP-14, HALT, GOHAI) or halitosis measurement method
- **Children/adolescents**: no statistically significant association in pooled analysis
- Methodological quality explains ~20% of heterogeneity (higher risk-of-bias studies overestimate the effect)

Briceag et al. (2023) SR specifically in adolescents/young adults (age 10–26, 6 studies):
- Self-reported halitosis prevalence 23.1–77.5% (mean 44.7%) — wide range reflecting measurement heterogeneity
- Consistent associations with: elevated anxiety and depression (SCL-9-R), social isolation and avoidance behaviors, reduced self-esteem, and stigma
- **Crucially:** the psychological burden is present even in pseudo-halitosis and halitophobia cases where no objective malodor is detectable

The age-stratified divergence between Cassiano 2021 (no OHRQoL effect in children/adolescents in pooled SR+MA) and Briceag 2023 (consistent psychosocial harm in the same age group in a dedicated SR) likely reflects different outcome instruments: Cassiano measured OHRQoL instruments primarily validated in adults; Briceag captured anxiety, depression, and social behavior — more sensitive measures for adolescent burden.

## 5. Management

### Mechanical Oral Hygiene (First-line)

Tongue cleaning (tongue scraper or tongue brush) targeting the dorsal tongue coating is the single highest-yield intervention for intra-oral halitosis. Combined with twice-daily toothbrushing and interdental cleaning, it addresses the primary substrate for VSC-producing anaerobes. All pharmacological and adjunctive interventions in the literature are compared against mechanical hygiene as the comparator (Wang 2022 PDT trials use tongue scraper as control; probiotic trials use standard oral hygiene as background).

### Probiotics

Three SR+MAs exist with partly discordant conclusions, reflecting different included RCTs and outcome definitions:

| Study | RCTs (n) | Primary finding |
|---|---|---|
| Yoo et al. 2017 | 3 | OLT score significantly reduced (SMD −1.93, p<0.0001); VSC not reduced (SMD −0.02, p=0.98) |
| López-Valverde et al. 2022 | 4 (n=283) | No statistically significant overall effect on halitosis (p=0.53) |
| Huang et al. 2022 | 7 | Short-term (≤4 wk): OLT SMD −0.58 (p<0.0001) and VSC SMD −0.26 (p=0.04) both significant; long-term (>4 wk): only OLT remains significant; VSC effect lost |

**Strains with most evidence:** *Lactobacillus salivarius* WB21, *L. reuteri* DSM 17938 + ATCC PTA 5289, *Streptococcus salivarius* K12, *Weissella cibaria* CMU/regular.

**Synthesis:** The evidence base is small (maximum 7 RCTs), heterogeneous in strain, dose, delivery vehicle, and follow-up duration. The most internally consistent message from the largest and most recent SR+MA (Huang 2022) is: probiotics reduce OLT scores and VSC in the short term (≤4 weeks), VSC reductions do not persist beyond 4 weeks, and the mechanism appears targeted to VSC metabolism rather than general plaque reduction (tongue coating and plaque index are not significantly changed). No current evidence supports probiotics as a standalone or long-term treatment for halitosis.

### Photodynamic Therapy (PDT)

Wang et al. (2022) SR+MA (8 RCTs, n=345), comparator = tongue scraper alone:

| Comparison | Immediate H₂S reduction (MD) | 95% CI | P |
|---|---|---|---|
| PDT vs. tongue scraper | −34.49 ppb | −66.34 to −2.64 | 0.03 |
| PDT + tongue scraper vs. tongue scraper | −67.72 ppb | −101.17 to −34.28 | <0.001 |

**Follow-up (7, 14, 30, 90 days):** No significant H₂S, CH₃SH, or DMS differences between any groups at any follow-up time point.

**Interpretation:** PDT produces an acute bactericidal effect via reactive oxygen species (ROS) targeting VSC-producing bacteria on the tongue dorsum (methylene blue photosensitizer + red LED). The combination of PDT+TS doubles immediate H₂S reduction versus PDT alone. However, bacterial recolonization of the tongue dorsum restores VSC levels within days, negating the initial advantage. PDT is therefore best suited for: acute social situations requiring rapid short-term halitosis reduction, or as an intra-appointment treatment before impression-taking in sensitive clinical contexts — not as a substitute for mechanical oral hygiene maintenance.

PDT shows no benefit for CH₃SH (methyl mercaptan) or DMS (dimethyl sulfide) at any time point, suggesting H₂S-producing bacteria are photodynamically more sensitive.

### Drug-related Halitosis

Mortazavi et al. (2020) SR (34 studies, n=30,736) identified 10 pharmacological groups causing extra-oral halitosis through systemic mechanisms:

| Drug group | Key agent | Halitosis incidence | Primary mechanism |
|---|---|---|---|
| Aminothiols | Cysteamine (≥15 mg/kg) | Up to 100% | Direct volatile sulfur metabolite production |
| Anticholinergics | Oxybutynin 5–15 mg/day | 15.5% | Xerostomia → decreased VSC clearance |
| Anticholinergics | Glycopyrrolate | 4.5% | Xerostomia |
| Acid reducers | Ranitidine 150 mg/day | 0.9% | Unclear |
| Antidepressants | Multiple classes | Variable | Xerostomia |
| Chemotherapeutic agents | Multiple | Variable | Mucosal disruption + microbiome alteration |
| Organosulfur substances | Garlic, DMSO | Variable | Direct allyl mercaptan/DMS production |
| Antifungals, antihistamines/steroids, antispasmodics, dietary supplements | Various | Reported | Xerostomia / metabolite |

**Clinical implication:** Drug-induced halitosis is a systemic side effect — non-oral routes (IV, intranasal, topical, eye drops) can produce halitosis via bloodborne delivery of volatile compounds to the lungs. A thorough medication history is mandatory in any refractory halitosis evaluation, particularly in patients on anticholinergics, antidepressants, or chemotherapy.

### H. pylori Eradication

For patients in whom intra-oral causes have been adequately addressed but halitosis persists, H. pylori testing (urea breath test or stool antigen) should be considered. Successful eradication yields ~83% reduction in halitosis risk (RR 0.17; Dou 2016). This represents the strongest evidence-based indication for a systemic pharmacological approach to refractory extra-oral halitosis.

## 6. Clinical Decision Tree

```
Patient presents with halitosis complaint
        │
        ▼
Step 1: OLT (organoleptic) score ≥2 (Rosenberg scale, standardized conditions)
        │
        ├─ No objective malodor (OLT 0–1) ──→ Consider pseudo-halitosis or halitophobia
        │                                      Screen for anxiety/depression; refer psychology
        │
        └─ Objective halitosis confirmed (OLT ≥2)
                │
                ▼
        Step 2: Chemical differentiation (OralChroma preferred for VSC speciation)
                │
                ├─ H₂S + CH₃SH elevated ──→ INTRA-ORAL PATHWAY
                │       │
                │       ├─ Tongue coating present? → Tongue scraping instruction (primary)
                │       ├─ BOP / pocket depth ≥4mm? → Full periodontal assessment
                │       │   • If periodontitis: treat periodontal disease first
                │       ├─ Orthodontic appliances? → Enhanced hygiene protocol
                │       └─ Persistent after oral hygiene optimization:
                │           • Short-term: consider probiotic trial (≤4 weeks)
                │           • Pre-appointment: consider PDT+TS combination
                │
                └─ DMS elevated OR H₂S/CH₃SH do NOT explain clinical severity
                        │
                        ▼
                Step 3: EXTRA-ORAL PATHWAY
                        │
                        ├─ Full medication review:
                        │   aminothiols, anticholinergics, antidepressants, chemotherapy?
                        │   → Discuss with prescribing physician about alternatives
                        │
                        ├─ ENT symptoms (rhinitis, post-nasal drip, tonsil stones)?
                        │   → ENT referral
                        │
                        ├─ GI symptoms / refractory halitosis despite oral control?
                        │   → H. pylori test (urea breath test or stool antigen)
                        │   → If positive: eradication therapy
                        │
                        └─ Systemic disease (hepatic, renal, metabolic)?
                            → Internal medicine referral
```

**Psychological screen at any stage:** If patient concern is disproportionate to objective OLT findings, screen for dental anxiety, halitophobia, and body dysmorphic disorder (BDD). Adolescents and young adults with halitosis warrant specific psychosocial assessment regardless of malodor severity (Briceag 2023).

## 7. Knowledge Gaps

1. **No standardized halitosis definition.** Across 13 prevalence studies, halitosis was defined by organoleptic, Halimeter, GC, or self-report criteria — precluding like-for-like pooling. A consensus case definition (minimally: OLT ≥2 under standardized conditions) is the foundational prerequisite for all further epidemiological work (Silva 2017, Szalai 2023).

2. **Halitometer cannot replace OLT — but OLT is impractical for post-COVID infection control.** Szalai 2023 explicitly raises the unresolved problem of close-range sensory examination during infectious disease outbreaks. Development of contactless or electronic nose (eNose) devices with r > 0.80 vs. OLT remains an active gap.

3. **Probiotic protocol undefined.** The maximum 7 included RCTs span 6 different strains, 5 delivery vehicles, and follow-up of 2–12 weeks. There is no established strain, dose, or duration for clinical recommendation. VSC effects do not persist beyond 4 weeks (Huang 2022), raising a fundamental question about maintenance protocols.

4. **PDT sustainability gap.** Bacterial recolonization restores VSC levels by day 7 consistently across 8 RCTs (Wang 2022). No trial has tested repeated PDT sessions at intervals designed to stay ahead of recolonization. Optimal PDT session frequency for sustained benefit is unknown.

5. **Causal direction of periodontitis–halitosis association.** Both SR+MAs are based exclusively on cross-sectional observational data. Whether treating periodontitis reliably and durably reduces halitosis (and by how much) requires randomized or at minimum prospective cohort designs.

6. **Age-stratified psychosocial burden paradox.** Cassiano 2021 found no OHRQoL association in children/adolescents; Briceag 2023 found consistent anxiety, depression, and social harm in the same age group. The discrepancy may reflect instrument insensitivity (OHRQoL tools are adult-validated) rather than genuine absence of harm in youth. Age-appropriate outcome instruments for halitosis-related psychosocial impact in pediatric populations are needed.

7. **Drug-related halitosis monitoring.** Only 11 of 34 studies in Mortazavi 2020 used objective halitosis measurement; 21 relied on patient complaint. Prospective pharmacovigilance studies with OLT-confirmed halitosis endpoints would provide more accurate incidence data for high-risk drug classes.
