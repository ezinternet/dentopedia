## 한국어 핵심요약

> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - 핵심 명제: 각화점막 폭(Keratinized Mucosa Width, KMW) <2 mm는 해결해야 할 임플란트 위험인자로 다루되, 결론을 둘러싼 논쟁의 대상이 아니다 — 문헌은 방향성에서 일관된다("많을수록 좋다").
> - ~2 mm KMW 역치는 모든 근거 등급(전문가 합의·SR+MA·장기 코호트)이 수렴. DGI/SEPA/Osteology 2022 합의(Sanz 2022)는 <2 mm를 biofilm·BOP·퇴축·MBL 증가 역치로 조작화, 단 >2 mm 광폭은 추가 보호 없음.
> - 근거는 비대칭: 연조직 대리지표(치태·염증·퇴축)에는 강하고 일관(OR 2.78–eOR 5.34), 경성 endpoint(임플란트주위염 발생·변연골소실)에는 방향성은 있으나 근거 수준 제한적. 이는 부재의 증거가 아니라 저빈도·고검정력 필요 endpoint의 방법론적 현실.
> - 최대 SR+MA(Mahardawi 2023, 22편, 13,265 임플란트): 임플란트주위염 OR 2.78. 통일된 정의(MBL ≥2 mm)로 제한 시 이질성 I²=0%·OR 1.96, 교란보정 시 OR 3.68. 정기 유지관리 내에서도 효과 유지(OR 2.08) → 독립적 위험인자.
> - TSA(Trial Sequential Analysis) 가장 확정적 연관 = 치태 축적(Ravidà 2022, 개입연구만): 치태가 유일하게 TSA 확인(MD 0.37, p=0.002). MBL은 통상유의(MD 0.17mm, p=0.03)지만 TSA는 검정력 부족(inconclusive)으로 재분류 → null은 부재가 아니라 under-powered.
> - 최대 MA(Zhang 2025, 30편, 5,241 임플란트): 치태·치은지수·출혈지수·MBL(MD −0.27mm) 모두 유의, 단 이질성 매우 높음(I²=80–97%). 탐침깊이(PD)는 양쪽 MA·umbrella에서 비유의 → KM 결핍에 둔감한 지표.
> - 유일 장기 전향 코호트(Roccuzzo 2025, 20년, n=64, 하악 구치부)가 임상가에게 논쟁 종결: KM 없음 → 임플란트주위염 25% vs KT군 4.2%(OR 6.67), 연조직 열개 100%. SPC 중(1–10년) 적용한 유리치은이식(FGG)이 원발 KT와 통계적으로 구별 불가한 20년 결과 산출.
> - FGG가 표준 증대술식: 근단변위피판 동반 시 1.5–6.5 mm KMW 획득, 20년 내구성(Roccuzzo). 이종이식(XCM)은 KMW에서 CTG와 비열등(WMD 0.8mm, p=0.062)·수술시간 18.5분 단축·통증 적음. 동종이식(ADM)은 수축 19.5% 더 커 하위 옵션.
> - 술식 선택 결정트리(Zhang 2025 합의): 하악 잔존 KMW <2 mm → 2차 수술 전 FGG / 2–3 mm → SFGG 또는 FGG / 상악 전치(심미) → SFGG+대체재(색조 불일치 회피) / 상악 구치 → 근단변위피판(ARF, 구개측 KM 충분).
> - 증대 시점은 식립 시뿐 아니라 유지관리 중에도 효과적·내구적 — 2차 수술 시기가 실용적·생물학적으로 선호 창. 임플란트주위 연조직은 PDL 혈관 결여·저혈관저세포 영역으로 치은보다 취약(Bhatavadekar 2012)해 증대 역치를 낮춰야 함.
> - 임상 bottom line: 매 recall에서 KM 측정, <2 mm이면서 병적 징후 동반 시 증대. 하악 <2 mm 부위는 2차 수술 후가 아니라 전(前)에 FGG 시행.
> - 수술 방식 선택: 무피판 임플란트 수술(Flapless Implant Surgery)이 피판 수술 대비 임플란트 주위 점막 혈관단면적 비율(Vessel Fraction, VF) 유의 증가(1.7% vs 1.2%, p<0.05) — 골막상 혈관(Supraperiosteal Vessel) 보존이 기전; 5/12 피판 부위 발적·출혈(BOP) vs 무피판 0 (Kim 2009, 성견 6마리).
> - 생리식염수(Saline) 세척: 0.9–1.8% NaCl이 치은 섬유아세포(Human Gingival Fibroblast, hGF) 이동(Migration)을 유의 촉진(p<0.05); 활성 이온은 Cl⁻(염소이온) — FAK/F-액틴(F-actin) 경로; 1.8%가 최적, 7.2%는 억제; 각화세포(Keratinocyte)에는 무반응; 임플란트주위 연조직 창상치유 프로토콜에 생물학적 근거 제공 (Huynh 2016, in vitro).

## One-line Summary

Synthesis across 10 papers (2 umbrellas, 3 SR+MAs, 1 prospective, 2 expert consensuses, 1 SR, 1 narrative) establishes ~2 mm keratinized mucosa width as a practical threshold: inadequate KM is most consistently and strongly associated with soft-tissue surrogates (plaque, inflammation, recession; OR 2.78–eOR 5.34), with a directionally present but evidence-quality-limited link to hard endpoints (peri-implantitis incidence, marginal bone loss), while FGG is the reference augmentation technique and can restore outcomes equivalent to native KM even when applied years after placement.

## Summary

This synthesis spans 10 papers addressing two clinically intertwined questions: (1) does inadequate keratinized mucosa (KM) width around dental implants constitute a genuine risk factor for peri-implant disease, and (2) when and how should KM be surgically augmented?

The overarching finding is asymmetric: evidence for KM's protective role is strong and consistent for soft-tissue/surrogate outcomes, but weaker and contested for hard clinical endpoints. This asymmetry is not evidence of absence; it reflects the methodological reality that peri-implantitis and marginal bone loss (MBL) are low-base-rate, hard-to-power outcomes requiring large, long-term, prospective samples that the field has not yet generated at RCT scale. The 20-year Roccuzzo cohort — the longest follow-up available — resolves this ambiguity in the observational direction: implants without KM develop peri-implantitis at 6× the rate and 100% soft-tissue dehiscence over two decades, with FGG providing durable rescue.

**Core propositions:**

1. **The ~2 mm KMW threshold is agreed across all evidence tiers: expert consensus, SR+MAs, and long-term cohort data converge on <2 mm as the operative risk cutoff.** The DGI/SEPA/Osteology 2022 consensus (Sanz 2022, 34 statements, 2 SRs) operationalized KT <2 mm as the threshold associated with higher biofilm, BOP, recession, and MBL — while explicitly stating wider bands (>2 mm) do not provide incremental protection beyond that minimum. The Chinese expert consensus (Zhang 2025, 29 authors) uses the same ≤2 mm threshold to gate technique selection at second-stage surgery, targeting a minimum of ≥4 mm total KM (≥2 mm buccal + ≥2 mm lingual/palatal). [합의수준]

2. **Inadequate KM is a consistent, independently confirmed risk factor for peri-implantitis, but the effect size and evidence quality vary by study design and outcome definition.** The largest SR+MA (Mahardawi 2023, 22 studies, 4,044 patients, 13,265 implants) yielded an overall OR of 2.78 (95% CI 2.07–3.74). Crucially, when restricted to studies using a uniform peri-implantitis definition (MBL ≥2 mm), heterogeneity collapsed to I²=0% and OR=1.96 (95% CI 1.41–2.73); confounder-adjusted subgroup yielded OR=3.68. The independence of the effect survived regular maintenance (OR=2.08 within maintenance programs). The highest-level evidence synthesis (Sabri 2025 umbrella meta-meta-analysis, 10 SRs) found eOR for MBL=1.852 and eOR for recession=4.05 with significance, while BOP and pocket depth were non-significant at the umbrella level. [근거강함 for surrogate endpoints; 합의수준 for peri-implantitis incidence]

3. **The most TSA-conclusive association is with plaque accumulation — the only outcome that has met the required information size in a methodologically strict interventional SR+MA.** Ravidà 2022 (9 studies, 685 implants, interventional designs only, TSA applied) found plaque index as the sole TSA-confirmed outcome (MD 0.37, p=0.002; RIS 310 met by 430 implants). MBL showed a conventionally significant signal (MD 0.17 mm, p=0.03, I²=0%) but TSA reclassified it as inconclusive (required 424 implants; only 257 available). PD required 2,171 implants. This TSA reversal is a methodological discipline that reframes the controversy: the null results are not evidence of absence — they are evidence of under-powered studies. [근거강함 for plaque only; claude해석/미검증 for "absence of evidence" reframing]

4. **The larger, more inclusive MA (Zhang 2025, 30 studies, 5,241 implants) demonstrates significant associations for plaque (MD −0.30), gingival index (MD −0.26), bleeding index (MD −0.20), and marginal bone loss (MD −0.27 mm), at the cost of very high heterogeneity (I²=80–97%).** This is the largest meta-analysis on the topic to date, including study types excluded from Ravidà 2022 (cross-sectional, retrospective). The significant BL finding (MD −0.27 mm) contrasts with Ravidà's TSA-underpowered MBL result and the Sabri umbrella's significant eOR=1.852 for MBL, forming a consistent directional signal across three separate syntheses. Probing depth remained non-significant in both MA (Zhang 2025: MD −0.03 mm, NS) and the Sabri umbrella (NS), suggesting PD is an insensitive outcome for KM deficiency. [합의수준 — directional convergence across methods; heterogeneity limits precision]

5. **The only long-term prospective cohort (Roccuzzo 2025, 20 years, n=64, posterior mandible) resolves the debate for the clinician: no KM → 25% peri-implantitis vs. 4.2% in the KT group (OR 6.67, 95% CI 1.09–40.9) and 100% soft-tissue dehiscence rate, while FGG applied during supportive care years 1–10 produced 20-year outcomes statistically indistinguishable from native KT.** This is the largest effect size seen in any single study in this corpus, consistent with a genuine biological relationship that cross-sectional and short-term studies are too noisy to resolve. Additional treatment needs (CIST C/D protocol) were 10× higher in the AM group over the final decade (50% vs. 5%). MBL progression accelerated between 10 and 20 years in the AM group (0.84 mm decade 2 vs. KT 0.32 mm). [근거강함 — longest prospective cohort; single-center, small n limits generalizability]

6. **Apparent contradictions between Ravidà's TSA-null results and the positive MBL signals from Zhang 2025 and Sabri 2025 are resolvable by study design differences, not biological inconsistency.** Ravidà 2022 restricted to interventional designs with comparison arms (1 RCT, 3 non-RCTs, 5 prospective cohorts) to avoid reverse-causation bias — this is methodologically correct but drastically limits sample size, leaving most outcomes underpowered. Zhang 2025 included cross-sectional and retrospective designs, trading causal clarity for statistical power, and found significant MBL. Both results are logically compatible: the KM–MBL relationship likely exists (Zhang, Sabri, Roccuzzo directionally consistent) but has not been confirmed at TSA-level power in high-quality prospective studies. This is a literature gap, not a refutation. [claude해석/미검증]

7. **FGG is the reference augmentation technique for KM width gain and long-term peri-implant stability; xenogeneic substitutes are non-inferior to CTG in width gain and offer lower morbidity; technique selection should be site- and biotype-driven.** Sanz 2022 consensus: FGG via apically positioned flap yields 1.5–6.5 mm KMW gain; xenografts gain 3.5 mm (non-inferior to CTG, WMD 0.8 mm, p=0.062) with 18.5 min less surgical time and lower post-operative pain; allogeneic substitutes show 19.5% greater shrinkage than autogenous (p=0.009) and are a lower-tier option. Stefanini 2023 SR (15 prospective, ≥36-month follow-up, up to 10 yr) showed CTG-based bilaminar approaches achieve a mean soft-tissue margin shift of only −0.06 mm over 4.8 years versus −0.96 mm progressive recession in non-augmented controls over 6.2 years; at 10 years, 100% of non-augmented immediately placed implants had ≥1 mm recession vs. 0% of CTG-augmented (Seyssens 2020 data). Zhang 2025 consensus operationalized a decision tree: mandible with residual KM <2 mm → FGG before second-stage surgery; 2–3 mm residual → SFGG or FGG; maxillary anterior → SFGG + substitute to avoid color mismatch; maxillary posterior → ARF (palatal KM ample). A 2024 SR+MA restricted to the cleaner *no-concomitant-bone-graft* condition (Dadlani et al., 9 studies, 6 meta-analyzed) sharpens the autograft-vs-substitute hierarchy: porcine XCM was inferior to autogenous grafts for KMW (pooled MD −0.96 mm at 6 mo; −1.16 mm at 12 mo) and soft-tissue thickness (MD −0.35 mm), with esthetics comparable and XCM offering lower morbidity, less analgesic need, and shorter surgical time — but the autograft advantage was graft-type-dependent (CTG ≈ XCM for KMW; FGG > XCM), echoing the "FGG > XCM, CTG-XCM closer" pattern across Sanz 2022, Montero 2022, and Fathi 2025. [근거강함 for FGG; 합의수준 for substitute hierarchy]

8. **KM augmentation begun during supportive care — not only at the time of implant placement — is effective and durable.** Bhatavadekar 2012 framed the conceptual imperative: the peri-implant soft tissue lacks PDL vascularity and has a hypovascular-hypocellular connective tissue zone, making it biologically more vulnerable than gingiva, which justifies a lower threshold for augmentation. Roccuzzo 2025 provides the empirical confirmation: FGG applied years 1–10 during SPC in patients with AM (alveolar mucosa) yielded 20-year peri-implantitis rates (0%), BOP (25.0%), and MBL (0.90 mm) statistically equivalent to native KT. This establishes that augmentation opportunity is not restricted to the pre-implant or immediate-implant window. [근거강함 for rescue FGG; narrative-review level for biologic rationale]

## Clinical Bottom Line

The practical synthesis is this: treat KM width <2 mm as a modifiable implant risk factor requiring attention, not a debate requiring resolution. The literature is consistent in the direction of its findings — more KM is better — even where individual studies diverge on which specific endpoint crosses the significance threshold at TSA-level power. The strongest signal is for surrogate outcomes (plaque, inflammation, recession), which are also the most clinically tractable: inadequate KM makes oral hygiene harder, tissues less resilient to mechanical insult, and the path to peri-implantitis shorter.

For hard endpoints specifically: the Ravidà TSA correctly identifies that MBL and PD differences are underpowered in interventional designs — this means the field needs larger RCTs, not that the relationship is absent. The convergent finding of significant MBL differences in Zhang 2025 (MD −0.27 mm, 5,241 implants), Sabri 2025 (eOR 1.852, p=0.0007), and Roccuzzo 2025 (0.63 mm absolute difference at 20 years) is mechanistically coherent: KM deficiency → greater plaque → greater inflammation → greater crestal bone resorption over time.

For management, the algorithm is well-agreed: measure KM at every recall visit; when <2 mm AND associated with pathological signs, augment. FGG via apically repositioned flap remains the reference standard for KMW gain and the longest documented durability (20 years). Xenogeneic substitutes are a clinically sound, lower-morbidity alternative particularly in esthetic zones. Second-stage surgery is the preferred augmentation window for practical and biological reasons; for mandibular sites with <2 mm residual KM, FGG should precede uncovering, not follow it.

## Peri-implant Soft Tissue Wound Healing & Surgical Technique

Two additional lines of evidence address how peri-implant soft tissue responds to surgical approach and post-operative management — both relevant to achieving and preserving the vascular and cellular milieu that underpins KM health.

### Flapless surgery preserves peri-implant mucosal vascularity (Kim 2009)

The only controlled animal study comparing peri-implant mucosal blood vessel distribution between conventional flap and flapless implant surgery (Kim 2009, 6 mongrel dogs, split-mouth, 12 implant pairs) found significantly richer vascularity at flapless sites after 3 months of healing: vessel fraction 1.7 ± 0.7% vs 1.2 ± 0.3% (p<0.05) and vessel number 51.4 ± 9.2 vs 38.2 ± 8.1 (p<0.05). Clinically, 5/12 flap-procedure sites showed redness and bleeding on probing; flapless sites were uniformly inflammation-free.

The proposed mechanism is dual: (1) flapless surgery preserves the supraperiosteal vessel network — identified by Berglundh et al. 1994 as the primary blood supply to the supracrestal connective tissue — which is severed during mucoperiosteal flap elevation; and (2) the smaller, cleaner punch wound heals with less scar tissue formation; scar tissue is inherently hypovascular. The higher vascularity at flapless sites implies better immune surveillance capacity and is consistent with the clinical absence of BOP at those sites. This evidence reinforces the biological rationale for preferring flapless protocols when bone volume and angulation permit: soft-tissue defense capacity is enhanced, not just convenience.

**Evidence note**: Animal model (canine, n=6); histomorphometric endpoints only; clinical translation requires caution, but mechanism-of-vascularity data are consistent with established vascular anatomy principles.

### Saline rinsing promotes gingival fibroblast wound healing via Cl⁻/FAK pathway (Huynh 2016)

The century-old practice of post-operative saline rinsing has a cellular-level mechanistic basis. Huynh et al. (PLOS ONE 2016, primary human gingival fibroblasts) demonstrated that 3×/day, 2-minute rinsing at 0.9–1.8% NaCl significantly enhances hGF scratch closure and transwell migration (p<0.05 at 0.9%; p<0.01 at 1.8% optimal), without affecting proliferation — the entire benefit is migration-driven. The active mediator is the chloride ion (Cl⁻): KCl replicated the effect while equimolar sodium-phosphate solutions did not. Mechanistically, 1.8% NaCl upregulated COL1 (>3-fold), fibronectin, and FAK mRNA, and induced FAK redistribution to focal adhesion clusters with F-actin stress fibers — the cytoskeletal signature of lamellipodia-driven directed migration. Human oral keratinocytes were entirely unresponsive, indicating fibroblast-specific chloride sensitivity. Concentrations ≥7.2% NaCl are inhibitory.

This study defines a therapeutic window for post-operative rinsing protocols: 0.9–1.8% NaCl (isotonic to mildly hypertonic). Importantly, MMP1, MMP2, and TGF-β were unchanged — saline does not trigger proteolytic overremodeling. For peri-implant soft tissue wound healing, this provides a molecular rationale for incorporating 0.9–1.8% saline rinsing into the post-surgical protocol following KM augmentation procedures (FGG, CTG), where gingival fibroblast migration is critical to wound closure and collagen matrix integration.

**Evidence note**: In vitro only (hGF, scratch/transwell assay); no animal or clinical RCT evidence for peri-implant-specific outcomes. The salt concentration studied is lower than that in commercial saline rinse products, which warrants clinical translation caution.

### Integrating the two: biological chain from technique to wound quality to long-term KM stability

Flapless surgery (Kim 2009) → higher supraperiosteal vascularity → better-perfused wound bed → optimal microenvironment for saline-enhanced fibroblast migration (Huynh 2016) → more efficient wound closure and ECM deposition. While this chain is inferential across two independent studies, it aligns mechanistically with Bhatavadekar 2012's framing of peri-implant soft tissue as a hypovascular-hypocellular zone requiring deliberate management: every measure that increases vascularity or accelerates fibroblast response is additive.

- [[implants/peri-implantitis/ravida-2022-keratinized-mucosa-width-peri-implant-disease-sr-ma]] — SR+MA+TSA (9 studies, 685 implants, interventional only): plaque-index the only TSA-confirmed finding; MBL TSA-inconclusive; methodological discipline anchor
- [[implants/peri-implantitis/zhang-2025-keratinized-mucosa-width-peri-implant-meta-analysis]] — MA (30 studies, 5,241 implants): largest meta-analysis to date; significant PI, GI, BI, BL; high I² but stable on sensitivity analysis
- [[implants/peri-implantitis/sabri-2025-keratinized-mucosa-implant-health-umbrella-review]] — umbrella SR + meta-meta-analysis (10 SRs): highest evidence tier; eOR recession 4.05, MBL 1.852, GI 3.131, mPI 5.34; BOP/PD NS; follow-up time not a moderator
- [[implants/peri-implantitis/fathi-2025-keratinized-mucosa-implant-health-umbrella-review]] — umbrella review (10 SRs, 7,139 participants): consistent positive association across all included reviews; FGG > XCM for KMW; CTG > XCM for gingival thickness; confirms OR 2.78 as key risk signal
- [[implants/peri-implantitis/roccuzzo-2025-keratinized-mucosa-peri-implant-20year-mandible]] — 20-year prospective cohort (n=64, posterior mandible): 25% vs 4.2% peri-implantitis (OR 6.67); 100% soft-tissue dehiscence in AM group; FGG rescue equivalent to native KT at 20 yr — longest follow-up anchor
- [[implants/sanz-2022-keratinized-mucosa-around-implants-consensus]] — DGI/SEPA/Osteology 2022 consensus (34 statements, 2 SRs): operationalizes 2 mm threshold; FGG standard of care; xenografts non-inferior to CTG for KMW (WMD 0.8 mm, NS); routine measurement at every recall mandated
- [[implants/zhang-2025-expert-consensus-km-augmentation-second-stage]] — Chinese multi-center expert consensus (29 authors): site- and residual-KMW-driven decision tree for ARF/FGG/SFGG/substitute selection at second-stage surgery; mandible <2 mm → FGG before uncovery
- [[implants/stefanini-2023-soft-tissue-augmentation-peri-implant-sr]] — SR (15 prospective studies, ≥36 months): CTG bilaminar −0.06 mm margin shift / 4.8 yr vs −0.96 mm progressive recession in non-augmented controls; 100% non-augmented immediate implants → ≥1 mm recession by 10 yr (Seyssens 2020)
- [[implants/bhatavadekar-2012-peri-implant-soft-tissue-management-narrative]] — narrative (2012): conceptual framing of peri-implant soft tissue fragility (PDL-absent vascularity, hypovascular-hypocellular zone); KM as "layer of protection"; soft tissue management begins at extraction, not placement
- [[implants/oh-2024-keratinized-mucosa-augmentation-functioning-implants-sr-ma]] — SR+MA (11 studies, 290 participants) on *functioning* implants: quantifies the augmentation upside — FGG weighted mean KM gain +2.6 mm (↓inflammation, crestal bone stable ≤4 yr); CTG weighted mean recession −2 mm. Autogenous-only (no substitutes)
- [[implants/montero-2022-soft-tissue-substitutes-vs-autogenous-keratinized-mucosa-sr]] — SR (11 studies, 282 subjects), companion to Sanz 2022: FGG > substitutes overall for KM gain (WMD −0.9 mm), but xenogeneic collagen matrix non-inferior when analyzed alone; substitutes save ~18.5 min operative time
- [[implants/soft-tissue/dadlani-2024-efficacy-xenogeneic-collagen-matrices-augmenting]] — SR+MA (9 studies, 6 meta-analyzed), restricted to *no-bone-graft* condition: porcine XCM inferior to autograft for KMW (pooled MD −0.96 mm at 6 mo, −1.16 mm at 12 mo) and STT (−0.35 mm), aesthetics comparable, XCM lower morbidity; autograft advantage graft-type-dependent (CTG ≈ XCM, FGG > XCM) — refines the substitute hierarchy
- [[implants/zwanzig-2025-titanium-pins-fgg-arf-vestibuloplasty-technique]] — Technique report (n=3): titanium-pin fixation of FGG/apically repositioned flaps during vestibuloplasty achieves KM gain with minimal shrinkage where suturing is impractical (abstract-only source)
- [[implants/sanz-martin-2022-buccal-soft-tissue-dehiscence-implants-sr-ma]] — SR+MA (buccal soft tissue dehiscence): dominant risks = buccal implant malposition (OR 14.37), CTG absence (OR 9.00), thin phenotype (OR 2.85) — frames KM/soft-tissue as the dehiscence-prevention axis
- [[implants/valles-2022-soft-tissue-augmentation-tissue-thickening-implants-sr-ma]] — SR+MA: CTG soft-tissue thickness +0.64 mm vs no graft, +0.51 mm vs substitutes; superior PES; MBL equal — thickness (MT) axis effect sizes
- [[implants/cairo-2019-soft-tissue-augmentation-implant-sites-rct-sr-ma]] — SR+MA of 14 RCTs: CTG > xenogeneic matrix for soft-tissue thickness (WMD −0.30 mm); augmentation improves KT & STT vs none
- [[implants/tavelli-2021-soft-tissue-phenotype-modification-peri-implant-nma]] — SR + network MA (52 studies): technique ranking — CTG≈ADM best for mucosal thickness, APF+FGG best for KM width; bilaminar grafts protect MBL
- [[implants/raghoebar-2021-profilometric-mucosa-soft-tissue-augmentation-esthetic-sr-ma]] — SR+MA (esthetic zone, profilometric): grafting at immediate placement gains +0.34 mm recession & +0.66 mm thickness vs no graft
- [[implants/bassetti-2016-soft-tissue-augmentation-second-stage-sr]] — SR: augmentation at second-stage surgery; APF/VP+FGG best for KM width, REF/APF+SCTG best for volume (all high bias) — historical anchor for the second-stage window
- [[implants/yadav-2025-soft-tissue-substitutes-patient-reported-outcomes-commentary]] — EBD commentary: soft-tissue substitutes reduce post-op pain/analgesic use/surgery time vs SCTG with equivalent satisfaction — the patient-reported-outcome (PROM) axis
- [[implants/kim-2009-blood-vessels-peri-implant-mucosa]] — Animal study (6 dogs, split-mouth): flapless implant surgery produces significantly higher peri-implant mucosal vascularity than flap surgery (vessel fraction 1.7% vs 1.2%, p<0.05); supraperiosteal vessel preservation identified as mechanism — surgical-technique axis for soft-tissue vascular defense
- [[periodontics/huynh-2016-saline-rinsing-gingival-fibroblast-wound-healing]] — In vitro (PLOS ONE 2016, primary hGFs): 0.9–1.8% NaCl rinsing promotes hGF migration via Cl⁻/FAK/F-actin pathway; 1.8% optimal; COL1 >3-fold upregulation; keratinocytes unresponsive; 7.2% inhibitory — post-operative wound management evidence base
