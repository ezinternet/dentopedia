## 한국어 핵심요약

> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - 핵심 명제: 이 묶음에서 가장 강력한 임상 레버는 약물 분자가 아니라 산-염기 화학(pH)이다. 체어사이드 중탄산나트륨(8.4% Sodium Bicarbonate) 완충이 핵심 변수.
> - 완충의 최대 효과 지점은 증상성 비가역성 치수염(Symptomatic Irreversible Pulpitis, SIP)의 하악대구치 하치조신경 전달마취(IANB) — 마취 성공률 약 37% → 84%로 거의 2배 (Karunakar 2024).
> - 반면 건강한 발치 조직(Kalra 2024)에서는 완충이 발현(onset)만 단축할 뿐 발치 통증 VAS·최대 성공률은 변하지 않는다(χ²=4.107, p=0.250). 발현은 항상 움직이는 endpoint, 성공률은 baseline이 나쁠 때만 움직인다.
> - 기전: 국소마취제는 약염기로, 이온화 양이온(BH⁺, 수용성)과 비이온화 자유염기(B, 지용성·신경 투과) 평형 상태. 카트리지 pH ~3.5–4.5에서는 대부분 이온화 → 발현 지연·통증. 완충은 주사 전에 자유염기 비율을 높임. 염증 조직은 산성이라 통상 용액의 자유염기 비율이 더 붕괴 → SIP에서 완충 효과 집중.
> - 가온(~42°C, Gandhi 2022)은 발현·편안함에서 완충과 거의 동등(술후 VAS 1.59 vs 1.69, p=0.183). 예냉(precooling)은 더 약한 3번째 옵션.
> - 골내마취(Intraosseous, 예: QuickSleeper, Park 2024): 리도카인 골내 100% 무보충 성공 vs 아티카인 협측 침윤 70.8%, 발현 빠르고 지속시간 짧음(118.6 vs 231.3분).
> - 소아 아티카인 vs 리도카인 안전성 동등(RR 1.08, 0.54–2.15, Li 2023) — 단 '침윤용 아티카인'에 한정. 4% 아티카인 IANB의 감각이상(paresthesia ~7.3배) 우려는 별개.
> - 임신: 2% 리도카인 + 1:200,000 에피네프린이 표준(Zhou 2023). 확진된 amide 알러지(매우 드묾): 알레르기내과 검사 후 diphenhydramine 기반 대체(Bina 2018). 바늘공포: 점막부착 패치 또는 무에피네프린 완충 제형.
> - 임상 의사결정 순서: (1) 건강 조직 일상 처치 → 통상 용액 충분, 더 빠른 발현·편안함 원하면 완충, (2) hot tooth 하악대구치 → 완충 1차(최고 가성비), (3) 완충 어려우면 가온이 거의 동등 대체, (4) 임신 → 1:200,000 에피네프린, 14–20주 선택적.
> - 한계: 대부분 소규모 단일기관 RCT(n=28–252), 치과 특이적 완충 SR+MA 부재. Kalra 2024는 VAS null에 대해 검정력 부족(7/arm).

## One-line Summary

Synthesis of 12 papers (4 buffering RCTs, 1 SR+MA, 1 paired SR+MA, 1 in-vivo development study, 1 narrative pharmacology review, 1 retrospective technique comparison, 2 special-population reviews, 1 allergy case, 1 needle-free cohort): chair-side sodium-bicarbonate **buffering reliably accelerates onset and reduces injection pain**, and in inflamed pulpitis tissue **substantially raises anesthetic success** (conventional IANB ~37% → buffered ~84%), while in healthy extraction tissue it speeds onset but does **not** change peak success or extraction VAS — with warming, precooling, intraosseous delivery, and epinephrine-free/needle-free formulations as situational adjuncts.

## 한줄요약

12편 종합 (완충 RCT 4편, SR+MA 1편, 쌍 SR+MA 1편, in-vivo 개발 1편, 약리 narrative 1편, 후향 술기비교 1편, 특수집단 리뷰 2편, 알러지 증례 1편, needle-free 코호트 1편): 체어사이드 중탄산나트륨 **완충은 발현 속도를 일관되게 단축하고 주사통을 줄이며**, 염증(불가역성 치수염) 조직에서는 **마취 성공률을 크게 끌어올린다** (전통 하치조신경 전달마취 ~37% → 완충 ~84%). 반면 건강한 발치 조직에서는 발현만 빨라질 뿐 최대 성공률·발치 VAS는 변하지 않는다. 가온·예냉·골내마취·무에피네프린/무바늘 제형은 상황별 보조 수단.

## Summary

The dominant clinical lever in this batch is **acid–base chemistry, not the drug molecule**. Commercial dental local anesthetics are bottled at low pH (~3.3–4.5 with epinephrine, lower still because the vasoconstrictor needs an acidic antioxidant) to maximize shelf stability and keep the drug in its water-soluble ionized (cationic) form. But only the **uncharged base** crosses the nerve membrane. Chair-side buffering with 8.4% sodium bicarbonate to physiologic pH (~7.3–7.4) increases the fraction of free base available at the moment of injection — which is why the consistent, reproducible finding across the four buffering RCTs is **faster onset and less injection sting**, and why the effect is *largest precisely where the conventional solution fails*: acutely inflamed pulpitis tissue, where the already-acidic inflamed milieu cripples the low-pH commercial solution.

Reading the 12 papers together gives a clean clinical hierarchy:

1. **Buffering's biggest payoff is in symptomatic irreversible pulpitis (SIP), not routine extraction.** [근거강함] In SIP mandibular molar IANB, buffered lidocaine roughly *doubles* anesthetic success vs conventional (Karunakar 2024: 84% vs 37%). In healthy extraction tissue, buffering speeds onset but the conventional solution already reaches adequate success, so extraction VAS is unchanged (Kalra 2024: χ²=4.107, p=0.250).
2. **Buffering reliably cuts onset time and injection pain everywhere.** [근거강함] Across Kalra 2024, Karunakar 2024, Hamad 2025, Gandhi 2022 the onset and injection-pain advantages are statistically robust regardless of indication.
3. **Warming (~42 °C) is roughly equivalent to buffering for onset/comfort; precooling is a weaker third option.** [근거강함] Gandhi 2022 found preheated ≈ buffered (post-op VAS 1.59 vs 1.69, p=0.183), preheated lowest injection pain. Karunakar 2024 ranked buffered > precooled > conventional.
4. **Agent swap (articaine) and route change (intraosseous) are parallel levers when infiltration/IANB underperforms.** [합의수준] Park 2024 shows lidocaine intraosseous reaches 100% no-supplemental success vs 70.8% for articaine buccal infiltration in deep-caries mandibular molars.
5. **Safety is not the differentiator between articaine and lidocaine in routine pediatric use.** [근거강함] Li 2023 SR+MA: adverse-event risk equivalent (RR 1.08, 0.54–2.15) — but the equivalence is *conditioned on articaine being used for infiltration*, not block.
6. **For special populations and edge cases, agent/vasoconstrictor selection dominates.** [합의수준] Pregnancy → 2% lidocaine + 1:200,000 epinephrine (Zhou 2023, reinforcing [[drug/uritu-2025-vasoconstrictor-dental-anesthesia-pregnancy-sr]]); true amide allergy → diphenhydramine-based alternative (Bina 2018); needle-phobia → mucoadhesive patch or epinephrine-free buffered formulations (Adami 2021, Uzbelger-Feldman 2024).

## 1. Mechanism — why pH is the lever

[합의수준] Local anesthetics are weak bases existing in equilibrium between an ionized cation (BH⁺, water-soluble, stays in solution) and an un-ionized free base (B, lipid-soluble, penetrates the nerve sheath). The Henderson–Hasselbalch relationship sets the ratio by the gap between solution pH and the drug's pKa (lidocaine pKa ≈ 7.9; articaine ≈ 7.8 — see [[drug/becker-2012-local-anesthetics-pharmacological-considerations]]).

- At cartridge pH ~3.5–4.5, the overwhelming majority of molecules are ionized → little free base → slow diffusion → delayed onset and an acidic "sting."
- Buffering to ~7.4 shifts a clinically meaningful fraction to free base *before* injection, rather than waiting for tissue buffering to do it slowly.
- **Inflamed tissue (pulpitis, abscess) is acidic**, so the conventional solution's free-base fraction collapses further at exactly the site that needs anesthesia — the mechanistic reason buffering's success benefit is concentrated in SIP.

[claude해석] This also explains why buffering helps onset/comfort but rarely changes *maximum attainable* anesthesia in healthy tissue: once enough free base eventually accumulates, peak block depth converges. Buffering buys speed and a better first-pass success rate, not a higher ceiling in already-favorable tissue.

## 3. Adjacent levers when block/infiltration underperforms

- **Warming to body temperature (~42 °C)** — [근거강함] Gandhi 2022: statistically equal to buffering for comfort/onset; attractive where a cartridge warmer is available and bicarbonate mixing is not.
- **Precooling / intraoral cryotherapy** — [근거강함] real but smaller benefit than buffering (Karunakar 2024); useful as a no-added-drug adjunct to blunt needle-stick pain.
- **Intraosseous delivery (e.g., QuickSleeper)** — [합의수준] Park 2024 (SNU Bundang, retrospective n=48): lidocaine intraosseous 100% no-supplemental success vs articaine buccal infiltration 70.8%, with faster onset and *shorter* duration (118.6 vs 231.3 min) — a feature, not a bug, for short restorative visits. Trade-off: technique sensitivity and equipment.
- **Agent escalation to 4% articaine for infiltration** — [합의수준] effective mandibular infiltration alternative, but see §4 safety conditioning.

## 4. Safety & agent selection

- **Pediatric articaine vs lidocaine** — [근거강함] [[drug/li-2023-articaine-lidocaine-adverse-effects-pediatric-ma]]: no adverse-event difference (RR 1.08, 95% CI 0.54–2.15, I²=57%, GRADE moderate). [claude해석] The equivalence holds for **articaine-as-infiltration**; do not read it as license for 4% articaine IANB in children, where paresthesia signal (4% articaine ~7.3× per [[drug/becker-2012-local-anesthetics-pharmacological-considerations]]) is the standing concern.
- **Maximum doses & toxicity baseline** — [합의수준] Becker 2012 is the reference page for mg/kg ceilings (lidocaine+epi 7 mg/kg / 500 mg; 4% articaine 5–7 mg/kg; bupivacaine cardiotoxicity; prilocaine methemoglobinemia), independent of buffering.
- **True amide allergy** — [claude해석] genuinely rare; most "allergy" is metabisulfite/methylparaben additive reaction, epinephrine response, or vasovagal. [[drug/bina-2018-true-allergy-amide-local-anesthetics-case]] documents a workable diphenhydramine + 1:100,000 epinephrine fallback (short-acting, volume-capped) for the rare confirmed case — refer for allergist testing before assuming.

## 5. Special populations & novel delivery

- **Pregnancy** — [합의수준] [[drug/zhou-2023-pregnancy-physiology-oral-local-anesthesia-review]]: standard oral LA is safe throughout pregnancy; **2% lidocaine + 1:200,000 epinephrine** best balances maternal/fetal safety+efficacy; lidocaine/prilocaine FDA category B, articaine/mepivacaine/bupivacaine category C; elective timing weeks 14–20, semi-supine to avoid aortocaval compression. Reinforces [[drug/uritu-2025-vasoconstrictor-dental-anesthesia-pregnancy-sr]] (vasoconstrictor not contraindicated).
- **Needle-phobia / atraumatic delivery** — [근거강함 for the cohort] [[drug/adami-2021-needle-free-mucoadhesive-patch-anesthesia-cohort]]: lidocaine+prilocaine mucoadhesive patch completed 90% of medium-complexity procedures injection-free (n=58, 59% needle-phobic). [claude해석] single-arm, no comparator — promising for low-/medium-complexity buccal work, not a block substitute.
- **Epinephrine-free formulation development** — [합의수준 / animal] [[drug/uzbelger-feldman-2024-buffered-anesthetic-without-epinephrine-invivo]]: a buffered, epinephrine-free 2% lidocaine (lactated Ringer's vehicle, pH 6.7–7.0) matched commercial lidocaine+epi anesthetic duration in rats with less bitterness — relevant for patients where vasoconstrictor load is the limiting factor, but still pre-clinical.

## 6. Clinical bottom line (decision order)

[claude해석] For a clinician deciding how to get profound anesthesia:

1. **Routine restorative/extraction in healthy tissue** — conventional solution is adequate; **buffer if you want faster onset and a more comfortable injection** (no downside, modest chair-time gain). Don't expect a success-rate change.
2. **Symptomatic irreversible pulpitis (hot tooth), mandibular molar** — **buffer first-line**; it is the single highest-yield change (success ~37%→84%). If still inadequate → supplemental intraosseous or intraligamentary; agent escalation/articaine infiltration as parallel option.
3. **Warming** is a near-equivalent substitute for buffering when bicarbonate mixing is impractical.
4. **Pregnancy** → 2% lidocaine + 1:200,000 epi, weeks 14–20 elective.
5. **Confirmed amide allergy** → allergist testing, then diphenhydramine-based fallback.
6. **Needle-phobia** → topical/patch for minor work; behavioral + buffered injection for definitive work.

## 7. Limitations & living-document notes

[claude해석]
- Most buffering RCTs are small single-center trials (n=28–252) with heterogeneous buffering ratios and onset definitions; a current dental-specific buffering SR+MA would strengthen the success-rate claim beyond the SIP signal.
- Karunakar 2024 carries an internal abstract-vs-Table-2 discrepancy (success 86.2/78.9/38.9% abstract vs 84.2/72.0/36.8% table) — the wiki page uses the table figures; treat the exact percentages as approximate.
- Kalra 2024 is underpowered (7/arm) for the VAS null — read its "no VAS difference" as *consistent with* onset-only benefit, not a definitive equivalence.
- Buffering shelf-life: bicarbonate-mixed solution must be used promptly; the evidence base is chair-side, not pre-mixed storage.
- [wiki-living-document] If a chair-side automated buffering device or a stable pre-buffered cartridge enters the Korean market, the practical barrier ("extra mixing step") changes and the cost/benefit of routine buffering shifts toward broader use — revisit §6.1.
