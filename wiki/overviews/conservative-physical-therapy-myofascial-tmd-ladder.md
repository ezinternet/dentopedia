## 한국어 핵심요약

> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - 근육형/근막성 턱관절장애(Myogenous/Myofascial Temporomandibular Disorder, TMD)의 **보존적 물리치료 모달리티**(운동치료·도수치료·건침·분사신장·근육내주사/보툴리눔)를 머리-대-머리로 비교한 16편 종합 — 기존 5축 [[overviews/tmd-management-evidence-ladder]]의 물리치료 축을 모달리티 단위로 심화.
> - **최상위 근거 결론**: 능동적(active) 치료가 수동적(passive)·약물 치료를 이긴다 — Yao 2023 BMJ 네트워크 메타분석(Network Meta-Analysis, NMA; 233 RCT, n=8,713, 59개입)에서 통증감소 TOP3가 인지행동치료(Cognitive Behavioral Therapy, CBT)+바이오피드백(위험차 Risk Difference, RD 36%), 하악 가동화(jaw mobilization, 36%), 수기 트리거포인트 치료(manual trigger-point therapy, 32%)로 모두 중등-높은 근거; 약물은 낮은 근거.
> - **1차(foundation)**: 환자교육+자가관리+치료적 운동(therapeutic exercise) — 운동치료는 교합안정장치(Occlusal Splint)와 통증 동등·최대개구량(Maximal Mouth Opening, MMO)에서 일부 우월(Zhang 2021), 내장증 시술 후 조기 운동이 MMO·통증 회복(Zhu 2024).
> - **2차(core)**: 도수치료(Manual Therapy, MT) 추가 — 저작근 근막이완·마사지+하악 가동화가 대조군 대비 통증·MMO·압통역치(Pressure Pain Threshold, PPT) 개선(Calixtre 2015, GRADE 저~고), **MT+운동 병용이 최선**(González-Sánchez 2023 SR 15 RCT); 단 MT가 상담·보툴리눔보다 우월하진 않음(de Melo 2020).
> - **3차(trigger-point 표적)**: 건침(Dry Needling, DN) ↔ 분사신장(Spray-and-Stretch)은 상호교환적 보조 — DN vs MT 간접비교 유의차 없음(Menéndez-Torre 2023 NMA, MD −0.263)이나 순위는 MT(SUCRA 0.932) > 심부건침(0.775); 분사신장은 DN에 비열등(Ustun 2024)하고 즉시 MMO↑(Koole 2019)·건침후통증 완화 보조(Martín-Pintado 2014, 단 효과 <6h).
> - **4차(escalation)**: 근육내 주사 — 보툴리눔독소(Botulinum Toxin, BTX-A)는 근막통에서 위약 대비 통증 유의 감소(Zhu 2020·Saini 2024 SR+MA)하나 **MMO 개선은 없음**; 저작근 주사는 교근(masseter) 65%·측두근 27% 집중(Nowak 2021). MT와 BTX 효과 동등(Calixtre·de Melo) → BTX는 1차 아님.
> - **약한 단독근거(보조로만)**: 경피전기신경자극(TENS)·저출력레이저(Low-Level Laser Therapy, LLLT) 단독은 효과 제한적(Alowaimer 2024); 능동치료·다학제 병용의 부가요법으로만.
> - **심리행동 overlay(전 단계 관통)**: CBT+바이오피드백이 NMA 공동 1위(Yao 2023); 만성·기분장애/불안 동반 시 예후 악화(Vázquez-Delgado 2010)·심리 디스트레스↔처치후 통증 상관(Martín-Pintado 2014) → 환자선택·동반 평가 필수.
> - **임상 bottom line**: 근막성 TMD 1차는 *운동+도수치료+행동치료*(능동), trigger-point 표적엔 DN/분사신장(상호교환), 불응 시 BTX(통증엔 효과·개구엔 무효), 수동 전기물리치료는 보조. TMD 80%+ 비수술 관리 가능(Alowaimer 2024).
> - **근거 공백**: 저작근 대상 분사신장 무작위 시험 0편(승모근 근거 전이), 모달리티 직접비교 head-to-head 부족, 대부분 단기 추적·이질성으로 MA 제한. 모달리티 우열보다 **순응도·다학제·능동참여**가 결과를 좌우.

## Scope

This page is the **modality-level deep dive** under the physical-therapy axis of the broader [[overviews/tmd-management-evidence-ladder]]. Where that page maps the whole 5-axis TMD landscape (epidemiology · conservative · interventional · bruxism · BTX), this one asks a narrower clinical question: *for myofascial / myogenous TMD — ~45% of all TMD (Chan 2022) — which conservative physical-therapy modality, in what order?* It also subsumes and ranks the standalone [[overviews/spray-and-stretch-tmd-myofascial-synthesis]] within the full modality ladder.

The unifying signal across every high-quality source is the same: **active, patient-engaged therapy (exercise, manual therapy, behavioral) outperforms passive modalities and pharmacology.**

## The evidence ladder (by certainty)

The strongest single anchor is **Yao 2023 (BMJ SR+NMA, 233 RCTs, n=8,713, 59 interventions)** — the largest comparative dataset in TMD:

| Rank tier | Intervention | Effect (pain RD vs placebo/no-Tx) | Certainty |
|---|---|---|---|
| Top | CBT + biofeedback | ~36% | Moderate–high |
| Top | **Jaw mobilization (manual therapy)** | ~36% | Moderate–high |
| Top | **Manual trigger-point therapy** | ~32% | Moderate–high |
| Mid | Therapeutic exercise / other PT | meaningful | Low–moderate |
| Low | Pharmacological interventions | variable | Low |

This NMA's verdict — active manual + behavioral therapies on top, drugs at the bottom — frames the whole ladder below.

## Decision tree (myofascial / myogenous TMD)

**Step 0 — Confirm myogenous subtype.** DC/TMD myalgia / myofascial pain / tendonitis / spasm; palpation + referral pattern (Chan 2022, Pal 2014). ~80%+ of TMD is manageable non-surgically (Alowaimer 2024).

**Step 1 — Foundation: education + self-care + therapeutic exercise.**
- Exercise therapy = occlusal splint for pain, with some MMO superiority (Zhang 2021 SR+MA).
- Early exercise restores MMO and reduces pain after derangement procedures (Zhu 2024 SR).

**Step 2 — Core: add manual therapy.**
- Myofascial release / massage of masticatory muscles + jaw mobilization beat control for pain, MMO, PPT (Calixtre 2015 SR, GRADE low–high); thoracic manipulation ineffective.
- **MT + therapeutic exercise = best combination** (González-Sánchez 2023 SR, 15 RCTs).
- Caveat: MT is *not* superior to counseling or BTX, and the literature carries uncertain risk of bias (de Melo 2020 SR).

**Step 3 — Trigger-point–targeted: dry needling ⇄ spray-and-stretch (interchangeable).**
- DN vs MT: no significant difference (Menéndez-Torre 2023 NMA, MD −0.263 [−1.517, 0.992]); ranking favors MT (SUCRA 0.932) > deep DN (0.775).
- Spray-and-stretch is non-inferior to DN (Ustun 2024 RCT, trapezius), produces an immediate MMO gain (Koole 2019), and reduces post-needling soreness as an adjunct — but the effect is short (<6 h) (Martín-Pintado 2014). See [[overviews/spray-and-stretch-tmd-myofascial-synthesis]].

**Step 4 — Escalation: intramuscular injection / botulinum toxin.**
- BTX-A reduces myofascial TMD pain vs placebo (Zhu 2020, Saini 2024 SR+MA) **but does not improve MMO** (Zhu 2020).
- Masticatory injections concentrate in masseter (65%) and temporalis (27%); BTX is the most-studied agent (Nowak 2021).
- Since MT ≈ BTX for pain (Calixtre 2015, de Melo 2020), BTX is escalation — not first-line — and carries masticatory-atrophy / dose caveats (see [[overviews/bruxism-muscle-overload-axis]]).

**Adjuncts with weak standalone evidence:** TENS and LLLT alone are limited (Alowaimer 2024) — use only as add-ons.

**Overlay across all steps — psychobehavioral care.** CBT + biofeedback co-tops the NMA (Yao 2023). Chronicity + mood/anxiety worsen prognosis (Vázquez-Delgado 2010), and psychological distress correlates with post-treatment pain (Martín-Pintado 2014) → screen and co-treat.

## Clinical bottom line

- **First-line for myofascial TMD = active therapy**: therapeutic exercise + manual therapy (jaw mobilization + myofascial release) + behavioral care. These carry the highest certainty and beat or match everything else.
- **Trigger-point adjuncts (DN, spray-and-stretch) are interchangeable** — choose by availability, patient tolerance, and cost; spray-and-stretch is the lowest-cost, lowest-risk option but the shortest-acting.
- **BTX / intramuscular injection is escalation**, effective for pain but not range of motion, reserved for refractory cases.
- **Passive electrophysical modalities (TENS/LLLT) are not standalone treatments.**
- The recurring meta-lesson: **modality choice matters less than active patient engagement, adherence, and a multidisciplinary (incl. psychosocial) frame.**

## Evidence gaps / future work

- **No randomized trial of spray-and-stretch on masseter/temporalis** — masticatory recommendation borrows from trapezius RCTs.
- Few direct **head-to-head** comparisons; most evidence is indirect (NMA) or vs control.
- Short follow-up and high heterogeneity limit meta-analysis across modalities; durable (≥6-month) comparative outcomes are scarce.
- Optimal **sequencing/combination** (e.g., exercise+MT vs exercise+MT+DN) under-tested.
