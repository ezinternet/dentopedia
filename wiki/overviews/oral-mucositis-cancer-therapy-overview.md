> - **주제 분리:** 항암치료 유발 구강점막염 (Oral Mucositis, OM)은 위키의 다른 구강내과 병소(재발성 아프타 구내염 RAS, 구강편평태선 OLP, 작열감증후군 BMS)와 **기전·관리 체계가 다른 별개 토픽** — 항암제·방사선의 직접 점막 독성이며 MASCC/ISOO 지지요법 틀로 관리. 그래서 [[oral-medicine-decision-ladder]]에서 분리해 별도 overview로 둠.
> - **부담(burden):** 소아 항암 OM 발생률 최대 **91.5%**, 전체 항암 OM 40–100%; 두경부암(Head & Neck Cancer, HNC) 방사선치료에서는 **용량제한 독성(dose-limiting)** — 총 방사선량과 중증도가 양의 상관(p=0.012).
> - **소아 — 유일하게 메타분석으로 지지되는 약제는 꿀(honey):** Andriakopoulou SR+MA(RCT 34편 중 5편 풀링)에서 국소 꿀이 중증 OM **입원기간 −4.33일**(p=0.002) 단축. Braguès SR(39편)에서도 **지속기간(duration)** 최우수.
> - **저출력레이저(Low-Level Laser Therapy, LLLT)는 소아 풀링에서 효과 없음**(RR=0.99) — 성인/타 점막질환 데이터와 대비되는 핵심 반전. 단 통증(pain) 완화에서는 보조적 언급.
> - **결과지표별 최적 약제(Braguès):** 발생률(incidence)→클로르헥시딘(chlorhexidine); 지속기간→꿀; 통증→올리브유(olive oil); 중증도(severity)→클로르헥시딘·꿀·팔리퍼민·올리브유·비타민E·글루타민 유사.
> - **팔리퍼민(palifermin, 재조합 KGF):** 발생률·중증도·지속기간 모두 감소(급성백혈병) — 그러나 **안전성 보고가 상충**, 무비판적 적용 금지.
> - **효과 없음으로 일관:** 칼슘인산염(calcium phosphate, 3편 모두 무효), TGF-β2·껌·케타민 가글(단일연구).
> - **방사선 OM(성인 HNC) — 아미노산:** Hassanein 삼중맹검 RCT(n=69)에서 **L-아르기닌·L-글루타민 모두** maltodextrin 대조군 대비 WHO 중증도·통증·체중감소·삶의질(OHIP-14) 유의 개선; **L-아르기닌은 글루타민에 비열등**(7주 양호 WHO 0/I: 82.6% vs 69.6%, p=0.048), 7주 완치율 수치상 더 높음(56% vs 13% vs 0%).
> - **근거 수준 주의:** 두 SR 모두 "확정 프로토콜 도출엔 근거 불충분" 결론(이질성↑, 표본 작음, Andriakopoulou는 abstract-only). 약제 선택은 **목표 결과지표 + 비용 + 환자 연령**으로 개별화.
> - **임상 takeaway:** 저비용·접근성 좋은 **꿀을 1차 후보**로, 결과지표별로 클로르헥시딘(발생률)·올리브유(통증)를 조합; 방사선 OM 성인은 아르기닌/글루타민으로 중증도·영양 동시 관리; 소아치과의는 종양 치료팀의 일원으로 OM 평가·중재에 참여.

## One-line Summary

Synthesis of 3 papers (2 pediatric SRs + 1 adult HNC radiotherapy RCT) on cancer-therapy-induced oral mucositis: topical honey is the only meta-analytically supported agent (hospital-stay −4.33 days in severe pediatric OM) while LLLT shows no pooled pediatric benefit (RR=0.99); agent choice is best matched to the target outcome (incidence→chlorhexidine, duration→honey, pain→olive oil), and for adult radiation-induced OM both L-arginine and L-glutamine suspensions beat a neutral control with arginine non-inferior to glutamine — but overall evidence remains too heterogeneous for firm protocols.

## Summary

Cancer-therapy-induced oral mucositis (OM) is an inflammatory ulcerative injury of the oral mucosa caused directly by the cytotoxicity of chemotherapy, radiotherapy, hematopoietic stem-cell transplantation (HSCT) conditioning, or their combinations. It is mechanistically and managerially **distinct from the immune-mediated / idiopathic mucosal diseases** that the [[oral-medicine-decision-ladder]] organizes (recurrent aphthous stomatitis, oral lichen planus, burning mouth syndrome): OM is a predictable, dose-dependent treatment toxicity governed internationally by the **MASCC/ISOO** supportive-care framework, with its own agent-prevention literature. This overview opens that sub-topic (`oral-medicine/mucositis`).

The burden is large: chemotherapy-induced OM incidence in children reaches **91.5%**, the broader cancer-therapy OM range is **40–100%**, and in head-and-neck-cancer (HNC) radiotherapy OM is a **dose-limiting toxicity** whose severity correlates with total radiation dose. The three papers split cleanly into a **pediatric prevention/management axis** (two systematic reviews) and an **adult radiation-induced OM (RIOM) treatment axis** (one RCT).

## Axis 1 — Pediatric OM: which agent for which outcome

Two complementary pediatric SRs converge on the same practical message: no single agent wins everything, so the choice should be **matched to the target OM outcome** (incidence, severity, duration, pain), and **honey is the standout low-cost candidate**.

| Outcome target | Best-supported agent(s) | Evidence | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Incidence** | Chlorhexidine | Braguès SR (most effective); also glutamine, palifermin, olive oil | Acts by removing pathogenic oral bacteria |
| **Severity** | Chlorhexidine ≈ honey ≈ palifermin ≈ olive oil ≈ vitamin E ≈ glutamine | Braguès SR (comparable) | Honey/olive oil ↓ pro-inflammatory cytokines |
| **Duration** | **Honey** (also palifermin) | Andriakopoulou MA: severe-OM hospital stay **MD −4.33 d, p=0.002** (2 RCTs); Braguès: best for duration | Only meta-analytically supported agent |
| **Pain** | Olive oil (also honey, LLLT, zinc-based) | Braguès SR | LLLT helps pain but not grade ≥II risk |
| **No effect** | **Calcium phosphate** (all 3 studies); TGF-β2, chewing gum, ketamine mouthwash (single studies) | Braguès SR | Do not rely on calcium phosphate |

**The LLLT reversal.** Although LLLT / photobiomodulation is the *most-studied* agent (8/39 studies, 20% in Braguès), the pooled pediatric meta-analysis found **no benefit for OM grade ≥II (RR=0.99, p=0.99)**; the therapeutic-use point estimate was favorable but non-significant (RR=0.48, p=0.58). This contrasts with adult/other-mucosal-disease LLLT data and is a key caution against extrapolating adult photobiomodulation enthusiasm to children.

**Palifermin** (recombinant human keratinocyte growth factor, KGF) reduced incidence, severity, and duration in acute leukemia — but carries **conflicting safety reports**, so it is not a default. **Glutamine** 400 mg/kg/day reduced incidence in ALL after high-dose methotrexate. **Oral cryotherapy** was effective only when combined with propantheline (compliance-limited).

## Axis 2 — Adult radiation-induced OM (RIOM): amino-acid suspensions

Hassanein 2025 is a **triple-blind, 3-arm RCT (n=69 HNC patients, 23/arm)** testing **L-arginine 5 g** vs **L-glutamine 5 g** vs **maltodextrin 10 g** (a *neutral control* that isolates the amino-acid effect from generic caloric support — a design gap in prior glutamine trials), swish-and-swallow 3×/day during weeks 2–7 of IMRT.

- **WHO mucositis severity:** no difference at week 2; significant by weeks 5–7 — both amino acids ≫ maltodextrin (p<0.001), arginine ≈ glutamine. Week-7 complete (WHO 0): **arginine 52% / glutamine 43% / maltodextrin 0%** (70% of control remained stage III). Favorable WHO 0–I at day 7: arginine 82.6% vs glutamine 69.6% → **L-arginine non-inferior** (p=0.048).
- **Pain-VAS** (wk7): 1.6 / 2.4 / 4.9 — both amino acids better than control.
- **Effectiveness (healed, wk7):** arginine 56% / glutamine 13% / control 0% (descriptive; non-inferiority confirmed, not powered for superiority).
- **Weight:** maltodextrin BMI 21.56→18.93 (p=0.0005); arginine and glutamine stable → **prevented RT-associated weight loss**.
- **QoL:** OHIP-14 improved with both amino acids, worsened with control; WHO–OHIP-14 r=0.885.

Clinical implication: for adult HNC RIOM, an amino-acid oral suspension addresses **severity, pain, nutrition, and QoL simultaneously**, and **L-arginine is a viable alternative to the better-established glutamine**.

## Evidence quality & gaps

- Both SRs explicitly conclude the evidence is **inconclusive for firm protocols** — heterogeneity of cancer types, regimens, outcome measures, and durations precludes confident pooling (Braguès: no meta-analysis possible; Andriakopoulou: only 5 of 34 RCTs poolable, honey estimate rests on 2 RCTs, and this record is **abstract-only**).
- Sample sizes are small (n=14–148 across the pediatric SR), bias is often high/moderate (Braguès modified Newcastle–Ottawa: 9 high-, 16 moderate-, 14 low-bias).
- The RIOM RCT is single-center (n=69), not powered for amino-acid superiority, and excludes diabetic/renal/hepatic patients.
- **Open questions:** head-to-head honey vs chlorhexidine vs palifermin by outcome; whether the pediatric LLLT null replicates with standardized dosimetry; arginine/glutamine durability and cost-effectiveness; pediatric RIOM (this RCT is adult-only).

## Clinical takeaway

1. **Frame OM as a predictable, dose-dependent toxicity** of the cancer regimen, managed proactively within the oncology team — the pediatric/dental clinician is part of supportive care, not a bystander.
2. **Choose the agent by the target outcome.** Honey is the best-evidenced, low-cost first choice (duration/severity); add chlorhexidine where incidence is the priority and olive oil where pain dominates. Avoid calcium phosphate.
3. **Do not assume LLLT works in children** — pooled pediatric data show no benefit for grade ≥II OM.
4. **For adult HNC radiation OM,** an L-arginine or L-glutamine suspension reduces severity/pain and preserves weight/QoL vs neutral control; arginine is non-inferior to glutamine.
5. **Temper expectations:** the evidence base cannot yet support a single rigid protocol — individualize by outcome target, cost, and patient age, and document response.
