## 한국어 핵심요약

> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - 핵심 명제: 근기능교정(Myofunctional Orthodontics) = **탄성교정장치(Elastodontic Appliance, EA)**(EF Line, AMCOP, Eptamed Equilibrator, Occlus-o-Guide 등 기성 실리콘 bio-activator)는 성장기·혼합치열 환자의 **조기차단(interceptive)** 치료 도구로, Class II 시상교정·횡적 확장·구치부 반대교합·개방교합·기도에 작용. 위키 12편을 7축으로 합성.
> - **장치 정체**: 기성(prefabricated) 폴리디메틸실록세인(PDMS)+실리카 점탄성 엘라스토머; 야간+주간 1~2시간 착용. 고정장치 없이 근기능(혀·구순·저작근) 재훈련 + 치아유도 (Inchingolo 2022 AMCOP).
> - **Class II 시상**: 탄성교정이 SNB↑·ANB↓·overjet 감소 유의(scoping 16편, overjet −2.1~−4.58mm, Ureni 2024)하나 **twin-block 등 전통 기능장치보다 효과 작음**. EQ Series II가 Occlus-o-Guide보다 우세(p=0.0019, Ortu 2021).
> - **vs 전통장치 동등성**: 고각 Class II서 탄성 bio-activator와 high-pull headgear 모두 SNB 동등 상승(Sfondrini 2026, n=40, retrospective).
> - **횡적 확장**: 탄성교정 구개확장이 Schwartz plate(Ortu 2025 RCT, n=70)·강성 Haas 확장기(Ortu 2024, n=64)와 **동등**하나 주로 **치조성(dentoalveolar)**; 편의·협조도는 우수.
> - **구치부 반대교합**: AMCOP가 기능성 구치부 반대교합 교정, 구개대칭 81.1%→92.3%(12개월) vs 대조군 무변화 (Lo Giudice 2023, n=39).
> - **개방교합·신경근(sEMG)**: AMCOP OPEN 개방교합 증례군서 overbite 0.1→2.2mm·SN-GoGn −2.8°·근활성 지표 개선 — 단 **대조군 없음**(Dipalma 2026, n=9).
> - **기도**: 인두기도 증가폭 **TB > RPE > AMCOP**(TB SPAS +2.1mm d=1.35 vs AMCOP +0.85mm d=0.55) — 형태적 변화, 기능적 입증 아님 (Inchingolo 2025, n=50).
> - **재료 한계**: EA는 노화 시 peak stress↓·compression set↑(알칼리 타액서 최악), 대략 **6개월까지 구조 안정** → 정기 교체 필요 (Cui 2024, in-vitro).
> - **확신도(중요)**: **근거 수준 전반적으로 약함** — 대부분 후향연구·증례군, RCT는 구개확장 1편(Ortu 2025)뿐, 다수가 단일 이탈리아 연구군·단기·무대조. SR들도 "근거 부족" 명시(Ronsivalle 2023, Ortu 2024). 효과 방향은 일관되나 전통장치 대비 열등/동등이며 장기·골격 효과 미확립.
> - **임상 한 줄**: 협조적 성장기 아동의 경증~중등도 Class II·반대교합·개방교합 **조기차단**에 편의·심미 이점으로 고려 가능하나, 골격성·중증 증례의 고정/강성장치를 대체하지 못하며 ~6개월 장치 교체 전제. 근거가 약해 신중한 적응증 선택 필요.

## One-line Summary

Synthesis of 12 wiki papers on myofunctional/elastodontic orthodontics (EF Line, AMCOP, Eptamed Equilibrator, Occlus-o-Guide prefabricated silicone bio-activators): in growing/mixed-dentition patients these appliances achieve interceptive Class II sagittal correction, dentoalveolar transverse expansion, posterior-crossbite and open-bite correction, and modest airway gains — generally comparable to conventional functional/expansion appliances with better comfort/compliance but smaller skeletal effects, on a weak evidence base (mostly retrospective/case-series, a single RCT) with appliances that degrade by ~6 months.

## 한줄요약

위키 12편 종합: 탄성교정장치(EF Line·AMCOP 등)는 성장기 혼합치열의 조기차단(Class II·반대교합·개방교합·횡적확장)에 효과를 보이나 주로 치조성·전통장치 대비 동등~열등이고, 근거는 약함(후향/증례 위주, RCT 1편), 장치는 ~6개월 노화로 교체 필요.

## Synthesis

According to the 12 myofunctional/elastodontic papers held in this wiki (`orthodontics/myofunctional`), the clinical bottom line is: **elastodontic appliances are an esthetic, comfortable, removable option for early interceptive correction of developing malocclusions in compliant growing children — delivering effects that are directionally consistent and often comparable to conventional appliances, but smaller for skeletal change and supported by weak evidence.** The seven axes below organize the data.

### 1. What the appliance is

Elastodontic appliances (EAs) — marketed as EF Line (Eptamed), AMCOP bio-activators, Eptamed Equilibrator, Occlus-o-Guide — are **prefabricated silicone (PDMS + silica filler) viscoelastic bio-activators** worn nightly plus 1–2 daytime hours, without fixed appliances. They retrain orofacial myofunction (tongue posture, lip seal, masticatory muscles) while guiding erupting teeth ([[orthodontics/myofunctional/inchingolo-2022-amcop-elastodontic-protocol-interception-review]]). Mechanically they are characterized in vitro by [[orthodontics/myofunctional/cui-2024-elastodontic-appliance-mechanical-characterization-fea]]: aging reduces peak stress and raises compression set (worst in alkaline artificial saliva), with structural integrity maintained to roughly 6 months — implying **periodic replacement**.

### 2. Class II sagittal correction

EAs produce mandibular advancement / Class II improvement: the scoping review [[orthodontics/myofunctional/ureni-2024-elastodontic-devices-sagittal-malocclusion-scoping-review]] (16 studies, n=345) reports SNB increase (+0.61° to +2.82°), ANB reduction (−0.38° to −2.42°), and overjet reduction (−2.1 to −4.58 mm) — but with effects **weaker than the twin-block**. Head-to-head, EQ Series II outperformed Occlus-o-Guide for overjet/overbite reduction at 12 months ([[orthodontics/myofunctional/ortu-2021-elastodontic-overjet-overbite-reduction]], prospective, n=60, p=0.0019), and in hyperdivergent Class II an elastodontic bio-activator matched high-pull headgear for SNB gain while keeping vertical/sagittal/soft-tissue measures stable ([[orthodontics/myofunctional/sfondrini-2026-elastodontic-bioactivator-vs-headgear-class-ii]], retrospective, n=40).

### 3. Transverse expansion & posterior crossbite

For transverse deficiency, EAs achieve expansion **comparable to conventional devices but mainly dentoalveolar**: an RCT found elastodontic palatal expansion equivalent to a Schwartz plate with better comfort/compliance ([[orthodontics/myofunctional/ortu-2025-removable-devices-palatal-expansion-rct]], n=70), and a controlled study found no significant intermolar/intercanine difference vs a rigid Haas expander at 12 months ([[orthodontics/myofunctional/ortu-2024-haas-expander-vs-elastodontic-crossbite]], n=64). For functional posterior crossbite, AMCOP restored palatal symmetry (81.1%→92.3% over 12 months) vs no change in controls ([[orthodontics/myofunctional/lo-giudice-2023-elastodontic-functional-posterior-crossbite-palatal]], n=39).

### 4. Vertical dimension & open bite (neuromuscular)

In anterior open bite, AMCOP OPEN improved overbite (0.1→2.2 mm) with vertical reduction (SN-GoGn −2.8°) and improved masticatory-muscle balance on surface EMG, though as an uncontrolled single-arm case series ([[orthodontics/myofunctional/dipalma-2026-elastodontic-vertical-skeletal-neuromuscular-balance]], n=9 cephalometric / 6 sEMG) — exploratory only.

### 5. Pharyngeal airway

For airway, gains are real but **smaller than conventional appliances**: pharyngeal airway increase ranked twin-block > rapid palatal expander > AMCOP (TB SPAS +2.1 mm, d=1.35 vs AMCOP +0.85 mm, d=0.55), and the changes are morphological, not proven functional ([[orthodontics/myofunctional/inchingolo-2025-elastodontic-pharyngeal-airway-functional-treatment]], retrospective, n=50).

### 6. Indications (what they treat)

The systematic/narrative reviews map the indication range: AMCOP and similar EAs are used for Class I/II/III, deep bite and open bite in mixed dentition without fixed appliances ([[orthodontics/myofunctional/inchingolo-2022-amcop-elastodontic-protocol-interception-review]]); the hybrid systematic review [[orthodontics/myofunctional/ronsivalle-2023-elastodontic-appliances-malocclusion-interception-children-review]] maps indications across six clinical domains; and the literature review [[orthodontics/myofunctional/ortu-2024-elastodontic-appliances-malocclusion-effectiveness-review]] concludes EAs are safe, simple and comfortable for malocclusion in growing patients.

### 7. The evidence caveat (the dominant theme)

Every synthesis paper flags the same limitation: the evidence base is **weak**. Designs are predominantly retrospective cohorts and case series; only one RCT exists (palatal expansion, Ortu 2025); several outcome studies have no control group (Dipalma 2026); much of the literature originates from a small number of (largely Italian) research groups; follow-up is short; and reviews (Ronsivalle 2023, Ortu 2024) explicitly note the lack of high-quality evidence. Effect *direction* is consistent, but EAs are generally **comparable-to-inferior to conventional/rigid appliances** for skeletal change, and long-term stability is unestablished.

### Clinical bottom line

Reasonable to offer EAs for **early interception of mild-to-moderate Class II, functional crossbite, or open bite in compliant growing children** who value a removable, esthetic, comfortable device — recognizing the correction is largely dentoalveolar/postural, effects are smaller than twin-block/Haas/headgear, appliances need replacing (~6 months of material integrity), and the supporting evidence is low-certainty. They are **not** a substitute for fixed or rigid appliances in skeletal or severe malocclusion.
