## 한국어 핵심요약

> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - 핵심 명제: 치아 흑색착색 (Black Stain, BS) — 치경부 1/3에 생기는 외인성 검은 선·점 — 은 병이 아니라 **저(低)우식성 생물막 + 보호적 타액 화학**을 표지하는 양성 현상으로, 우식과 **일관된 역상관**을 보인다. 6편 종합(서사적 고찰 2 + 미생물·횡단 3 + SR+MA 1).
> - **유병률**: 2.4–20% (Żyła 2.4–18%, Al-Shareef 2.4–20%); 대규모 역학에서 취학전 아동 조기흑색착색(Early Black Stain, EBS) 12.10% (Zheng 2023, n=2,675). 성별 차 없음, 전 연령 발생.
> - **착색 기전**: 세균이 만든 황화수소 (H₂S) + 타액의 철 이온 (Fe³⁺) → 불용성 황화철 (Ferric/Ferrous Sulfide, FeS) 침착이 검은색의 본체 (Al-Shareef 2025, Żyła 2015). 식이 chromogen(차·커피 polyphenol)·철분 보충제가 촉진.
> - **우식 역상관 (정량)**: SR+MA에서 BS 아동의 우식 교차비 (Odds Ratio, OR) **0.67** (95% CI 0.54–0.82), 우식치아 평균차 −0.98개·우식면 −2.34면 (Mousa 2022, 14편). 성인 영구치에서도 무우식 치아 유의하게 많음 (Çelik 2021, p=0.007), 취학전 dmft 유의 낮음 (Zheng, p<0.01).
> - **미생물 논쟁 — "Actinomyces 단독" 서사는 흔들린다**: 고전·배양·소아 16S에서는 Actinomyces 우점 (Żyła, Li 2015 유치, Zheng 치태 Actinomyces·Rothia↑). 그러나 **성인 영구치 차세대염기서열분석 (NGS)** 에서는 Capnocytophaga·Neisseria·Corynebacterium가 우점하고 Actinomyces는 우점이 아님 (Çelik 2021). BS는 단일 균이 아닌 **이질적 다균종 phenotype** (Li PCA 두 아군).
> - **타액 signature**: 철·나트륨·구리·칼슘·인산 농도↑, pH↑, 유량↓ (Al-Shareef); 칼슘·완충능↑ (Żyła). 과포화·고완충 환경이 탈회를 억제 → 보호의 한 축.
> - **인과 미해결 — 두 모델 경쟁**: ① BS 생물막/철·황 화합물이 우식균을 **직접 억제** vs ② 저우식성 체질(Actinomyces 우세·S. mutans 적음·고완충 타액)이 BS와 우식저항을 **동시에 유발**하는 교란. 관찰연구로는 구분 불가 (Mousa).
> - **장(腸) 축 (신규)**: EBS+ 무우식 아동에서 장내 Lactobacillus 바이오마커가 처음 보고됨 (Zheng 2023) → 구강에 국한되지 않은 **전신·숙주 면역 성분** 가능성 시사.
> - **치료**: 1차 = 반복 전문가 초음파 스케일링(재발 흔함); PDT·코코넛오일·과산화물·lactoferrin·probiotics는 근거 약함. 과도한 기계적 제거는 법랑질 마모 위험 → 보존적 접근.
> - **임상 takeaway**: BS는 **제거 대상 병변이 아니라 낮은 우식위험의 표지**. 보호자에게 안심시키고, 심미 목적의 스케일링 외 공격적 처치는 피하며, BS 아동이라도 식이·불소 예방의 끈은 놓지 말 것(역상관 ≠ 면역).
> - **확신도**: 역상관(축 4)·착색 기전(축 2)은 근거 강함(SR+MA + 다수 일치); 미생물 정체(축 3)·인과 방향은 미결(이질성·NGS vs 배양 불일치).

## One-line Summary

Synthesis of 6 papers (2 narrative reviews, 3 microbiome/cross-sectional studies, 1 SR+MA) on dental black stain (BS): an extrinsic ferric-sulfide cervical pigmentation (bacterial H₂S + salivary Fe³⁺ → FeS) that is consistently and inversely associated with caries (pooled OR 0.67, 95% CI 0.54–0.82; Mousa 2022), driven by a low-cariogenic biofilm and protective salivary chemistry (↑Ca/phosphate, ↑pH, ↑buffering). The classic Actinomyces-dominance narrative holds in primary dentition (Li 2015) and large pediatric epidemiology (Zheng 2023, n=2,675, EBS 12.10%) but is challenged in adult permanent dentition by NGS showing Capnocytophaga/Neisseria/Corynebacterium dominance (Çelik 2021). Causality (direct protection vs constitutional confounding) is unresolved; a novel gut-microbiome signature (Lactobacillus in EBS+ caries-free children) hints at a systemic component. Clinically, BS is a benign marker of low caries risk, not a lesion to aggressively remove — first-line treatment is repeated ultrasonic scaling, with caution against enamel wear.

## Summary

Dental black stain (BS) is an extrinsic dark discoloration — a thin line or coalescent dots — on the cervical third of the tooth, distinct from chromogenic staining caused by tobacco, chlorhexidine, or iron drops. Across 6 papers spanning two decades, BS emerges as a coherent biological phenotype rather than a cosmetic nuisance: it has a characteristic pigment chemistry, a debated but distinctive microbiome, a reproducible salivary signature, and — most clinically important — a **consistent inverse association with dental caries**.

This overview integrates the foundational narrative reviews (Żyła 2015, covering 2001–2014; Al-Shareef 2025, the modern synthesis), the microbiome characterizations across the lifespan (Li 2015 primary dentition; Çelik 2021 adult permanent dentition via NGS; Zheng 2023 large preschool epidemiology + gut microbiome), and the quantitative SR+MA establishing the caries link (Mousa 2022).

### Axis 1 — Pigment chemistry (well-established)

The unifying mechanism is iron-sulfide precipitation: sulfur-metabolizing bacteria generate hydrogen sulfide (H₂S), which reacts with elevated salivary ferric iron (Fe³⁺) to precipitate insoluble ferric/ferrous sulfide (FeS) — the black deposit (Al-Shareef 2025, Żyła 2015). Dietary chromogens (tea/coffee polyphenols), iron supplementation, and copper contribute. This chemistry is the least controversial part of the picture.

### Axis 2 — Caries inverse association (strong, quantified)

The signal is robust and quantified. Mousa 2022 (SR+MA, 14 studies, children) found BS children had **33% lower odds of caries (OR 0.67, 95% CI 0.54–0.82, I²=37%)**, ~1 fewer affected tooth (MD −0.98) and ~2.3 fewer affected surfaces (MD −2.34). The signal replicates outside meta-analysis: Çelik 2021 found significantly more caries-free teeth in BS+ adults (p=0.007), and Zheng 2023 found significantly lower caries prevalence and dmft in EBS+ preschoolers (p<0.01, n=2,675). The narrative reviews independently report that the majority of primary studies link BS to lower DMFT/dmft.

### Axis 3 — Microbiome identity (contested — the key open question)

The classic teaching — *Actinomyces*-dominant, low-acidogenic biofilm with reduced *S. mutans*/*Lactobacillus* — holds in the **primary dentition** (Li 2015: Actinomyces, Haemophilus, Treponema enriched) and in **large pediatric epidemiology** (Zheng 2023: Actinomyces + Rothia enriched in EBS+/caries-free plaque; S. mutans confined to the caries-only group). But it is directly challenged in the **adult permanent dentition**: Çelik 2021's NGS profiling found *Capnocytophaga*, *Neisseria*, and *Corynebacterium* dominant, with Actinomyces **not** the leading genus. Li 2015's PCA further split BS+ plaque into two distinct subgroups. The reconciliation: BS is a **heterogeneous, multi-genus phenotype** whose dominant taxa shift with age, dentition, and method (culture/16S vs shotgun NGS) — "Actinomyces only" is an oversimplification of a low-cariogenic community.

### Axis 4 — Salivary host environment (protective)

BS individuals show a distinct salivary chemistry: elevated iron, sodium, copper, calcium, and phosphate; higher pH; and lower flow rate (Al-Shareef 2025), with elevated calcium and buffering capacity (Żyła 2015). Calcium/phosphate supersaturation and high buffering resist demineralization — a plausible **direct protective pathway** that is independent of the biofilm.

### Axis 5 — Causality (unresolved) and the gut axis (emerging)

The central unresolved question is **direction of causation**. Two models compete (Mousa 2022): (1) BS biofilm and its iron/sulfur compounds *directly* suppress cariogenic bacteria; (2) a constitutionally low-cariogenic phenotype (Actinomyces-rich, low S. mutans, high-buffer saliva) *simultaneously* favors both BS formation and caries resistance — i.e., confounding by constitution. Observational data cannot separate them. Zheng 2023 adds a provocative new dimension: EBS+ caries-free children carry a distinctive **gut** microbiome (Lactobacillus biomarker), suggesting a systemic or host-immune component beyond the local plaque.

### Treatment and clinical stance

First-line treatment is **repeated professional ultrasonic scaling**; recurrence is common. Alternatives (photodynamic therapy, virgin coconut oil, peroxide, lactoferrin, probiotics) have only limited evidence and none has shown superiority. All removal must be weighed against **enamel wear risk**. The overarching clinical posture: BS is a benign marker of *lower* caries risk, not a pathology — caregivers should be reassured, removal is esthetic (not therapeutic), and aggressive abrasion is contraindicated. Crucially, the inverse association is *not* immunity: BS children still require dietary counseling and fluoride, because the protection is a correlation of constitution, not a guarantee.

## Key Contributions

- Frames BS across 5 axes (pigment chemistry · caries inverse association · microbiome identity · salivary environment · causality/gut axis) rather than as an isolated cosmetic finding
- Quantifies the caries-protective signal (OR 0.67) and shows it replicates across age groups (primary, mixed, adult permanent) and study designs (SR+MA, cross-sectional, large epidemiology)
- Surfaces the **microbiome controversy**: Actinomyces-dominance (pediatric/culture/16S) vs Capnocytophaga/Neisseria-dominance (adult permanent/NGS) — a method- and age-dependent split, not a contradiction to be averaged away
- Separates the two competing causal models (direct biofilm/chemistry protection vs constitutional confounding) and flags them as observationally indistinguishable
- Integrates the novel gut-microbiome dimension (Zheng 2023) as the first hint of a systemic component
- Translates to a conservative clinical stance: treat BS as a low-caries-risk marker, remove only for esthetics, avoid enamel-damaging abrasion, and keep prevention active
