> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - **주제**: 고딕아치 트레이싱(Gothic Arch Tracing, GAT = arrow-point tracing)을 중심으로 한 **수평 악간관계 = 중심위(Centric Relation, CR) 기록** 술식 7편(complete-denture 6 + occlusion Kwak)의 재현성·정확도·디지털화 종합. 수직 차원(OVD)은 [[complete-denture-ovd-determination-overview]]가 담당 — 이 페이지는 그 **수평 짝**.
> - **3대 술식 계열** (de Moraes Melo Neto 2021): ① 유도법(guided: 양손조작 Bimanual·턱끝유도 Chin-point) ② 도해법(graphic: 구내/구외 고딕아치) ③ 생리법(physiologic: 삼킴 Swallowing·혀 위치). 임상 선택은 이 3계열 안에서 이뤄진다.
> - **재현성(reproducibility) 위계**: 디지털 고딕아치(~0.98, 체위 무관) > 턱끝유도(~0.79, 앙와위 신뢰) > 삼킴(~0.62, 직립 한정) (Bhagat 2025). 유도법 내에서는 **양손조작 ≥ 턱끝유도** (de Moraes Melo Neto 2021 SR: 3편 우세·2편 동등·1편 열세).
> - **정확도(reference 대비 일치) 위계는 reference에 따라 뒤집힌다**: 무치악 split-cast 검증에서는 **nick-and-notch(0.15/0.23mm) < 구내 고딕아치(0.42/0.51) < 구외 고딕아치(0.74/0.86)** 로 정적(static) 왁스기록이 최소 오차 (Singh 2026). 즉 GAT은 **재현성은 최고지만 정적 기준 대비 오차는 더 큼** — "재현성 ≠ 정확도"의 핵심 모순.
> - **오차의 방향(direction)이 크기만큼 중요**: 디지털 운동분석기(MIP 0점) 기준 Dawson 양손조작·혀끝(경구개·연구개 경계)이 최근접(전 축 유의차 無), 고딕아치 정점은 **전방+하방**(개구 OVD 인공물), 턱 후퇴법은 **후방 압박** — 다만 전부 ±1mm 이내 (Jász 2024). 후방 압박(능동/수동 후퇴)만 피하면 임상 허용.
> - **구내 > 구외 고딕아치**: 추적판이 **횡적 회전축(hinge axis)에 가까울수록** 각→선형 오차증폭이 적고 혀·근육 간섭이 적다 — Singh 2026 일관 + 생역학적 근거.
> - **구외 GAT vs 교합기 프로그래밍**(반조절성, 수평과로각 HCG): SR+MA(Potdukhe 2023, n=325)에서 cephalogram(+10°)·OPG(+5°)가 GAT보다 유의하게 큰 HCG 산출, CBCT·전방위 왁스는 차이 無 → 저자는 **OPG를 GAT의 실용적 대체**로 제시. 단 "기준이 GAT 자체"라 높은 값=더 정확이 아님 + 인도 표본 편중·이질성 큼.
> - **디지털화**: CAD/CAM 간이 구내 고딕아치 장치가 전통 구내장치와 CR 일치(편차 <0.5mm)하며 의자시간 57.5→41.9분 단축 (Zhou 2023, abstract-only). 자체제작 디지털 트레이서도 재현성 벤치마크 (Bhagat 2025).
> - **장기·기능 정의 확장**: GAT을 단발 기록이 아니라 **정상화된 저작위(CM-H)=치료위** 설정 도구로 쓴 10년 추적(Kwak 2025, n=16) — CM-H↔MICP 일치 63% 유지, 전체 회복 81%. Gysi의 "최후방 인대유도 경계위" CR 정의에서 **신경근 조화 기능위**로의 재정의.
> - **원장 결정 요지**: 무치악 정밀·표준 케이스 → nick-and-notch/정적기록이 오차 최소; **신경근 제어 저하·습관성 전방폐구·심한 흡수릉·전악 기능재건**에는 고딕아치(특히 구내·디지털)가 시각적 정점확인·기능위 포착으로 유리. 후방 압박 술식(턱 후퇴)은 회피.

## One-line Summary

A 7-paper synthesis (6 complete-denture + Kwak 2025 occlusion) on Gothic arch (arrow-point) tracing and horizontal jaw-relation / centric-relation recording: it separates **reproducibility** (digital Gothic arch ≈ 0.98, posture-independent > chin-point > swallowing; bimanual ≥ chin-point) from **accuracy against a reference** (in edentulous split-cast, static nick-and-notch < intraoral Gothic arch < extraoral Gothic arch in discrepancy), shows that error *direction* matters (Gothic arch biases anterior at open OVD, retrusion methods seat posteriorly, all within ±1 mm), establishes intraoral > extraoral Gothic arch, positions OPG as a programming alternative for condylar guidance, and extends the tracer from an instant record to a decade-stable function-defined therapeutic position.

## 한줄요약

고딕아치(arrow-point) 트레이싱과 수평 악간관계(중심위) 기록 7편 종합 — **재현성**(디지털 고딕아치 ~0.98·체위무관 > 턱끝유도 > 삼킴; 양손조작 ≥ 턱끝유도)과 **기준 대비 정확도**(무치악 split-cast에서 nick-and-notch < 구내 고딕아치 < 구외 고딕아치)를 분리하고, 오차의 **방향**(고딕아치=전방, 턱후퇴=후방, 모두 ±1mm 내)과 **구내>구외** 우위, OPG의 교합기 프로그래밍 대체 가능성, 그리고 단발 기록→10년 안정 기능위 확장을 정리.

## Summary

Determining the **horizontal jaw relation — centric relation (CR)** is the most error-sensitive step of complete-denture and full-mouth occlusal reconstruction: edentulism removes periodontal proprioception, leaving mandibular position to the TMJ and musculature. **Gothic arch (arrow-point) tracing (GAT)** — a stylus on a mandibular plate scribing an arrowhead whose apex marks CR — is the classical *graphic* method. This synthesis assembles the current evidence on how GAT compares with the other recording families, and resolves a recurring confusion: **reproducibility and accuracy are different axes and they rank GAT differently.**

Following de Moraes Melo Neto 2021, CR techniques fall in three families — **guided** (bimanual manipulation, chin-point guidance), **graphic** (intraoral/extraoral GAT), and **physiologic** (swallowing, tongue position). GAT is prized because a patient *re-traces the same apex repeatedly* (high reproducibility), but the apex is recorded at an opened vertical dimension and reflects a functional border, not a static habitual closure — so against a static or MIP reference it can sit slightly off. The clinically useful questions are therefore: which method re-finds the same point (reproducibility), which point is "right" and against what reference (accuracy), in which *direction* do methods err, intraoral vs extraoral, and how does digitization change the trade-off.

## Results

### Axis 1 — The three CR-recording families and the evidence map

de Moraes Melo Neto 2021 (PRISMA SR, 6 low-quality studies, **all dentate, none edentulous**) is the framework anchor. Critically, the within-method evidence is almost entirely **guided**: every one of the 6 studies compared only **bimanual manipulation vs chin-point guidance** — none compared the two *graphic* (Gothic arch) techniques head-to-head, and none enrolled edentulous patients. Tally: bimanual superior in 3, equivalent in 2, inferior in 1 → **bimanual ≥ chin-point** for condylar-position reproducibility in dentate non-TMD subjects. The take-home is twofold: a technique-level anchor (two-handed stabilization beats one-handed guidance) **and an evidence gap** — graphic and physiologic methods, and the edentulous population, were essentially unstudied at SR level. The papers below fill exactly those gaps.

### Axis 2 — Reproducibility: digital Gothic arch leads, swallowing trails

Bhagat 2025 (in-vivo, n=30 edentulous/overdenture, custom digital intraoral tracer) ranks reproducibility cleanly: **digital Gothic arch tracing 0.98 (≈99%, posture-independent) > light chin-point guidance 0.79 (≈88%, dependable supine) > swallowing 0.62 (≈77%, reliable only upright)** (all p≈0.000). Two clinical messages: GAT is the reproducibility benchmark and *tolerates posture change*, and among bedside guided/physiologic methods **chin-point guidance is preferable to swallowing** (more reproducible and posture-robust). This complements de Moraes Melo Neto 2021's guided-method finding by adding the graphic and physiologic arms it lacked.

### Axis 3 — Accuracy is reference-dependent, and it inverts the ranking

Here the two non-GAT-friendly results live, and they are not contradictions but different *reference standards*:

- **Singh 2026** (within-subject, n=20 edentulous, **split-cast on a semi-adjustable articulator** as the objective check) found a discrepancy hierarchy **Nick and Notch 0.15 mm (centric) / 0.23 mm (protrusive) < intraoral Gothic arch 0.42 / 0.51 < extraoral Gothic arch 0.74 / 0.86** (all pairwise p<0.001; protrusive error > centric for all). Against a static split-cast reference, the **static** wax registration (nick-and-notch) is most consistent and GAT carries more discrepancy.
- **Jász 2024** (cross-sectional, n=34 dentate, **KaVo Arcus Digma 2 motion analyser, MIP = 0 point**) found that **Dawson bimanual** (0/0.01 mm sagittal, ns all axes) and **tongue-tip at the hard/soft-palate border** deviated least, while the **gothic-arch apex sat anterior + downward** (0.28–0.30 mm sagittal, −0.47 to −0.5 mm vertical) and retrusion methods sat posterior — but **all seven methods stayed within ±1 mm**.

The reconciliation: GAT is highly *reproducible* (Bhagat 2025) yet shows larger *discrepancy versus a static/MIP reference* (Singh 2026, Jász 2024) because its apex is a functional border captured at an **opened OVD** (Jász 2024 explicitly flags the open-registration confounder for GAT's anterior-inferior bias). "Reproducible" ≠ "coincident with habitual closure." Reading these three papers on one axis is the single most important clinical insight of this overview.

### Axis 4 — Direction of error, and intraoral > extraoral

Two convergent sub-themes:

- **Direction matters more than magnitude** (Jász 2024): the methods to *avoid* are the **posterior-seating** ones (active retrusion, passive 20 N chin push) that force a retruded condyle; GAT's bias is anterior and partly an OVD artifact, so it is clinically safer in direction even when larger in magnitude. Non-retrusive methods (Dawson, tongue-at-border, GAT, adduction field) are all acceptable.
- **Intraoral beats extraoral GAT** (Singh 2026, mechanistic, and consistent with biomechanics): a tracing plate **near the transverse hinge axis** suffers less angular-to-linear magnification than a forward-projecting extraoral platform, and the smaller intraoral device disturbs tongue/musculature less. When GAT is chosen, prefer the **intraoral** form.

### Axis 5 — Extraoral GAT vs articulator programming (condylar guidance)

Potdukhe 2023 (SR+MA, 20 of 22 studies, n=325 edentulous, PROSPERO CRD42022283974) addresses a *different* use of GAT — programming the **horizontal condylar guidance (HCG)** angle of a semi-adjustable articulator. Versus extraoral GAT as the reference: **cephalogram (+10.2–10.7°) and panoramic OPG (+5.1°)** gave significantly higher HCG angles (p<0.05 bilaterally; cephalogram pool tightest at I²=0%), while **CBCT and protrusive interocclusal wax record showed no significant difference** (but with I²=95–99%, so "no difference" is weakly supported). The authors name **OPG the best practical accurate alternative**. Two caveats anchor the interpretation: "accuracy" is agreement *against GAT itself* (a higher reading is not automatically more anatomically correct), and 19/22 studies were from India (generalizability). Net: a radiograph can substitute for the technique-sensitive extraoral tracer for *articulator programming* specifically.

### Axis 6 — Digitization and the function-defined therapeutic position

- **Digitization narrows the cost of GAT** (Zhou 2023, prospective paired n=10, abstract-only): a CAD/CAM **simplified intraoral Gothic arch device** reproduced the conventional intraoral tracer's CR to **<0.5 mm** (midline + molar, vertical + horizontal by STL best-fit) while cutting chair time **57.5 → 41.9 min** (P<0.01). Combined with Bhagat 2025's custom digital tracer, the trajectory is clear: digital intraoral GAT keeps the reproducibility advantage and removes much of the chairside-time penalty.
- **From an instant to a position** (Kwak 2025, prospective 10-year follow-up, n=16, occlusion): a Gothic arch tracer (N-GAT) was used not to capture a single CR but to set the **normalized hard-food chewing position (CM-H) as the therapeutic position** for full-mouth rehabilitation. At ~10 years, **CM-H↔MICP concordance held at 63%**, overall stomatognathic recovery reached **81% (37% maintained + 44% progressed)**, and recovery was strongest when occlusal adjustment accompanied reconstruction. This reframes the classical Gysi CR (most-posterior, ligament-guided border) toward a **neuromuscular function-harmonized position**, and supplies the rare long-term human validation that a GAT-defined occlusion is durable.

## Clinical Decision Threads [Claude interpretation]

- **Default standard edentulous case (good ridges, cooperative patient)** → a well-made **static record (nick-and-notch / careful retruded wax)** gives the smallest discrepancy against split-cast (Singh 2026); guided **bimanual** is the reproducibility-validated manual adjunct (de Moraes Melo Neto 2021, Jász 2024).
- **Impaired neuromuscular control, habitual protrusive closure, or severely resorbed ridges** → choose **Gothic arch tracing** for its visual apex confirmation and high reproducibility (Singh 2026 author caveat; Bhagat 2025). Prefer the **intraoral** form over extraoral (less error magnification).
- **Chairside method choice when not using GAT** → **chin-point guidance over swallowing** (Bhagat 2025); chin-point is dependable supine. **Avoid forced posterior retrusion** methods (Jász 2024).
- **Programming a semi-adjustable articulator's condylar guidance** → extraoral GAT works but is technique-sensitive; **OPG (panoramic) is an acceptable, more practical alternative** for the HCG angle (Potdukhe 2023).
- **Going digital** → a CAD/CAM intraoral Gothic arch device preserves accuracy (<0.5 mm) and saves ~15 min of chair time (Zhou 2023); reasonable to adopt where the workflow exists.
- **Full-mouth reconstruction / defining the therapeutic position** → consider the GAT-derived **chewing-centered functional position** (Kwak 2025), and pair reconstruction with **occlusal adjustment** for best long-term stomatognathic recovery.

## Cross-cutting caveat — references are not comparable across studies

Each study judges "accuracy" against a *different* benchmark: split-cast/habitual retruded closure (Singh 2026), MIP via motion analyser (Jász 2024), GAT-as-reference (Potdukhe 2023), or internal reproducibility with no external truth (Bhagat 2025, de Moraes Melo Neto 2021). There is **no anatomic CR gold standard** in this literature. Therefore cross-study magnitude comparisons are illegitimate; the durable conclusions are the *within-study rankings and directions* synthesized above, not the absolute millimetre values.

## 원장 메모 체크리스트

- 재현성과 정확도를 분리해 말하라 — "고딕아치가 제일 재현성 좋다"는 맞지만 "제일 정확하다"는 기준에 따라 틀린다.
- 무치악 표준 케이스: 잘 만든 정적 왁스(nick-and-notch)가 split-cast 오차 최소.
- 고딕아치를 쓸 거면 **구내** 장치로 (구외보다 오차증폭·간섭 적음).
- 턱끝유도 > 삼킴 (의자 옆에서). **턱 후퇴 압박은 금지** (후방 변위).
- 교합기 수평과로각만 필요하면 OPG로 대체 가능.
- 디지털 구내 고딕아치 = 정확도 유지 + 의자시간 단축.
- 전악재건 치료위는 저작위 기반 기능위 + 교합조정 동반 시 장기 안정.
