> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
>
> - **핵심 메시지**: 비수술치주치료(Non-Surgical Periodontal Therapy, NSPT)에 대한 모든 보조요법(adjunct)은 6–12개월 임상부착수준(Clinical Attachment Level, CAL) 이득 면에서 서로 우열이 없으며, 추가 이득은 평균 ~0.3 mm에 불과하다 (존 2017 네트워크 메타분석, NMA; 9종 보조요법, RCT 61편).
> - **프로바이오틱스 (Probiotics, 생균제) — 주목할 만한 2026년 근거**: 이중맹검 RCT (n=80, 12주)에서 Lactobacillus PB01 + Enterococcus EB10 로젠지가 NSPT 단독 대비 출혈양성률(Bleeding on Probing, BoP%) 유의 감소(p=0.03)와 치주낭깊이(Probing Pocket Depth, PPD) ≥5 mm 부위 수 유의 감소(p=0.01)를 달성했다 (Lundtorp-Olsen 2026).
> - **프로바이오틱스 — 미생물 기전**: 프로바이오틱스 군에서 적색복합군·오렌지복합군(Red/Orange complex) 병원균 (*P. gingivalis*, *T. denticola*, *F. nucleatum*) 감소 + 건강 관련 공생균 (*Rothia dentocariosa*, *Neisseria elongata*) 증가 — 16S rRNA 서열분석·샷건 메타게노믹스로 확인.
> - **프로바이오틱스 — 치은염 단독 근거**: 8주 OraCMU 경구 정제 RCT (n=80)에서 치은지수(Gingival Index, GI) −0.19 vs −0.08(위약, p=.035), 탐침시출혈(BOP) −7.74 vs −2.82(p=.030) — NSPT 없이 보충제 단독으로도 유의한 치은 개선 (Jeon 2026).
> - **프로바이오틱스 — 임상부착수준(CAL)**: 두 RCT 모두 CAL에서 유의한 군간 차이 없음 — 프로바이오틱스는 염증 지표(BoP, GI)는 개선하지만 부착 수준 개선은 불분명.
> - **항균광역동치료 (antimicrobial Photodynamic Therapy, aPDT) — 이중광 프로토콜**: 405+810 nm 이중광 + 인도시아닌 그린(indocyanine green, ICG) 광감작제를 매일 10분 가정용으로 사용 시 치태(plaque) 21.2% 추가 감소(p=0.0001), 기존 구강위생 단독은 유의한 변화 없음 — 소형 파일럿 RCT(n=30), 가설 생성 수준 (Gusseva 2026).
> - **aPDT 기전**: 405 nm 청보라빛이 치면세균막(biofilm) 내 내인성 포르피린(porphyrin) 광활성화 → 활성산소종(Reactive Oxygen Species, ROS) 생성; 810 nm 근적외선이 조직 침투 깊이 증가 → 치은열구(gingival sulcus) 심부 도달.
> - **보조요법 비교 틀 (NMA)**: 독시사이클린 하이클레이트 겔(doxycycline hyclate gel)과 다이오드 레이저 광역동치료(PDT)가 SUCRA 상위 1–2위이나 신뢰구간이 겹쳐 임상적 선택 근거로 삼기에 불충분 — 비용·접근성·환자 선호를 함께 고려해야 함.
> - **치태 공시제 (plaque disclosing agent)**: 미국 FDA의 에리트로신(erythrosine) 식품·경구약 금지(2025.01)로 D&C Red No. 33이 실용적 대안 — 치태 제거율은 동등, 잔류 착색·맛 면에서 우월 (Sooampon 2026).
> - **전동칫솔 (powered toothbrush)**: 6–10세 아동에서 회전진동(oscillating-rotating, OR) 전동칫솔이 수동칫솔보다 치태 감소 51% 우월(p=0.003) — 기계적 자가구강위생의 기반을 보강하는 맥락 근거 (Zini 2026).
> - **치은연하 약제 세척 (subgingival irrigation, 化學洗淨) — 음성**: NSPT 중 약제(포비돈요오드 PVP-I·클로르헥시딘 CHX·정유 essential oils·오존수·붕산)를 치은연하 세척제로 추가해도 물/식염수 대비 PPD(MD 0.01 mm)·CAL(MD 0.09 mm)·BOP에 추가 이득 없음 — 16편 RCT(712명) SR+MA, 근거수준 low~very low, 항균제 관리(antimicrobial stewardship) 관점에서 일상적 사용 비권고 (da Silveira 2026). NMA의 ~0.3 mm 천장효과를 재확인하며 *세척 전달경로*만 분리한 첫 업데이트.
> - **"clean and seal" (AA-NaOCl + 가교 히알루론산, xHA) — 유지치료 잔존 포켓에서 양성**: 단일기관 RCT(n=42)에서 재기구조작(subgingival re-instrumentation, SRI)에 아미노산-차아염소산나트륨 겔 + 가교 히알루론산 겔을 보조 시 6개월에 PD 추가 −0.50 mm·CAL 추가 +0.57 mm, 포켓폐쇄율 88.1% 대 38.1%(p<0.001), 8개 중 5개 치주병원균 유의 감소; **깊은 포켓일수록 효과 큼** → 선택적 사용 (Jungbauer 2026). 단, 생체표지자(IL-1β·MMP-8)는 불변.
> - **세척 vs 점착-봉합의 역설**: 단순 약제 *세척*(da Silveira: 음성)과 효소분해+클롯안정화 *겔 봉합*(Jungbauer: 양성)의 대비는 — 전달방식(접촉시간·기질분해·클롯안정화)이 약제 종류보다 결정적임을 시사. NaOCl은 세척용이 아니라 생체막/기질을 분해하는 겔 형태로, HA는 봉합제로 작용.
> - **임상 결론**: 프로바이오틱스(특히 Lactobacillus + Enterococcus 복합)는 NSPT 후 BoP 감소 목표에 부가적 가치가 있으며, 가정용 이중광 aPDT는 기계적 접근이 어려운 부위에서 보조 가능성이 있다. 단순 약제 치은연하 세척은 이득 없으나, 유지치료 단계 깊은 잔존 포켓에 한정한 "clean and seal" 겔 프로토콜은 의미있는 부가효과를 보인다. CAL 개선 근거는 세척 계열에서 여전히 미약하고 장기 데이터가 없다.

## One-line Summary

Synthesis of 2026 probiotic and aPDT adjunct RCTs against the 2017 NMA benchmark: all adjuncts to NSPT/SRP yield ~0.3 mm extra CAL gain, probiotics improve BoP and PPD ≥5 mm sites but not CAL, and home-use dual-light aPDT adds meaningful plaque reduction on top of conventional oral hygiene — no adjunct is yet dominant.

## 한줄요약

NSPT/SRP 보조요법 2026년 프로바이오틱스·이중광 aPDT RCT를 2017 NMA 벤치마크와 종합: 모든 보조요법의 추가 CAL 이득은 ~0.3 mm에 불과하고, 프로바이오틱스는 BoP와 PPD ≥5 mm 부위를 개선하되 CAL은 미개선, 가정용 이중광 aPDT는 기존 구강위생에 추가적 치태 감소를 제공하며, 아직 우월한 단일 보조요법은 없다.

## Thesis

The best-available evidence (2017 NMA, 9 adjuncts, 61 RCTs) establishes a ceiling effect: no adjunct to SRP is superior to any other for clinical attachment gain, and the collective increment is modest (~0.3 mm). Within this landscape, 2026 double-blind RCTs show that specific probiotic combinations (Lactobacillus PB01 + Enterococcus EB10) reliably reduce BoP and deep pockets as NSPT adjuncts through a documented microbiome-remodelling mechanism — but leave CAL unchanged. Dual-light aPDT (405+810 nm) offers an additional plaque-control pathway in patients where mechanical access is limited, though evidence remains pilot-level. Clinicians should frame adjunct selection around achievable targets (BoP and pocket count rather than attachment) and patient-specific barriers to mechanical self-care.

## The Adjunct Landscape — Context from NMA

John et al. (2017) performed a star-shaped frequentist network meta-analysis reusing the 2015 ADA Clinical Practice Guideline's primary RCT library: 61 RCTs, 74 SRP+adjunct vs SRP-alone comparisons, 9 adjuncts (systemic antimicrobials, sub-antimicrobial doxycycline [SDD], chlorhexidine chips, doxycycline hyclate gel, minocycline microspheres, diode-laser PDT, diode laser non-PDT, Nd:YAG laser, erbium laser). The Guideline had compared each adjunct only against SRP alone, never head-to-head; this NMA generated 36 indirect comparisons.

**Key finding**: no adjunct was statistically superior to any other. All pairwise differences carried wide, overlapping confidence intervals. Ranking placed doxycycline hyclate gel first and diode-laser PDT second by SUCRA probability, but the differences are not clinically actionable given the interval widths. Collectively, adjuncts added approximately 0.3–0.35 mm of CAL gain over SRP alone at 6–12 months — a clinician-measured surrogate whose patient-perceived benefit is uncertain. A contour-enhanced funnel plot detected publication bias that inflated observed effects by ~20%.

**Benchmark implication**: any new adjunct RCT (probiotic, aPDT, or other) should be interpreted against this ~0.3 mm ceiling and with the knowledge that surpassing the ceiling on CAL is unlikely. Reframing to patient-relevant outcomes — BoP resolution, pocket count reduction, patient-reported outcomes — may be more appropriate.

## Probiotics as NSPT Adjunct

### Rationale and Mechanism

Probiotic bacteria compete with periodontal pathogens for colonization niches, displace red/orange complex species, and may downregulate the pro-inflammatory cytokine milieu. The precise mechanisms include competitive exclusion, co-aggregation interference, short-chain fatty acid modulation, and immunomodulation via innate signalling pathways.

### Kim/Lundtorp-Olsen 2026 — Probiotics + NSPT in Stage II–III Periodontitis

The most methodologically comprehensive probiotic + NSPT RCT to date (double-blind, n=80, 12 weeks; University of Copenhagen). Participants with stage II–III periodontitis received NSPT per EFP 2022 Steps 1–2 and were randomized 1:1 to probiotic lozenges (Lactobacillus PB01 DSM14870 + Enterococcus EB10 DSM32307, each ≥1 × 10^9 CFU, 491 mg xylitol, twice daily) or identical placebo for 12 weeks.

**Clinical results** (probiotic vs placebo, Week 12):

- BoP%: 15.6% vs 18.7% — significant advantage probiotic (p=0.03, smoking-adjusted)
- PPD ≥5 mm site count: 8.9 vs 14.8 — significant advantage probiotic (p=0.01, both models)
- PPD mean: 2.7 vs 2.9 mm — nominally significant (p=0.03), lost significance after smoking adjustment (p=0.07)
- CAL: 1.58 vs 1.49 mm — no significant difference (p=0.8 / 0.94)
- Plaque index: no significant difference (p=0.65)
- Participants achieving BoP <10%: 14 probiotic vs 5 placebo (p=0.03)

**Microbiological results**: Subgingival 16S rRNA sequencing (V1–V3, MiSeq) showed decreased α-diversity in the probiotic group (Shannon p=0.01; Simpson p=0.03) and LEfSe-identified differential abundance of 30 taxa. *P. gingivalis* and *T. denticola* enriched in placebo; *Rothia dentocariosa* and *Neisseria elongata* enriched in probiotics at Week 12. Salivary shotgun metagenomics (NovaSeq 6000) identified 23 differentially abundant species: all four major red/orange complex pathogens (*P. gingivalis*, *T. denticola*, *F. nucleatum*, *T. forsythia*) decreased more in the probiotic group. Functional gene-set enrichment (GSEA) showed the probiotic group had greater reductions in anaerobic metabolic pathways (oxidative phosphorylation, fatty acid biosynthesis, anaerobic amino acid catabolism) and increases in vitamin B6 and ascorbate metabolism — mechanistic evidence beyond species-level shifts.

**Immunological results**: No significant between-group differences in salivary IL-8, IL-1β, MCP-1, or MIF. Chitinase decreased more in the probiotic group (p=0.009). The null cytokine finding is informative: saliva is a diluted matrix; GCF is the recommended matrix for future immunological endpoints.

See [[periodontics/kim-2026-probiotics-nspt-periodontal-db-rct]].

### Jeon 2026 — Standalone Probiotic in Gingivitis/Incipient Periodontitis

Double-blind placebo-controlled RCT (n=80, 8 weeks), testing OraCMU/ProbioticCMU oral tablets twice daily in adults with gingivitis and incipient periodontitis — notably, **no concurrent professional mechanical debridement** required. This design isolates the probiotic signal from NSPT.

**Results** (probiotic vs placebo, Week 8):

- GI (primary): −0.19 ± 0.03 vs −0.08 ± 0.04 (p=.035)
- BOP: −7.74 ± 1.54 vs −2.82 ± 1.60 (p=.030)
- Inflammatory markers: FGF-5 (p=.003), TSLP (p=.017), RANKL/OPG ratio (p=.021) — all significantly decreased in probiotic group
- Oral microbiota: several taxa significantly modulated (specific identities not recoverable from abstract-only ingest)
- Safety: no serious adverse events

The strain composition and CFU dose of OraCMU are partially recoverable (2.0 × 10^? CFU/g — exponent not confirmed from abstract). This limits direct comparability with the Lactobacillus PB01 + Enterococcus EB10 formulation in Kim 2026.

**Synthesis**: Both 2026 RCTs converge on the BoP/BOP signal. Standalone probiotics (Jeon) in gingivitis and probiotics as an NSPT adjunct (Kim) both significantly reduce gingival inflammation markers. Neither shows CAL benefit. This suggests the active mechanism operates at the inflammatory/microbiome level rather than producing structural attachment gain — consistent with the NMA ceiling.

See [[periodontics/jeon-2026-probioticcmu-gingivitis-rct]].

## Antimicrobial Photodynamic Therapy (aPDT)

### Mechanism

Antimicrobial photodynamic therapy works by activating a photosensitizer (exogenous dye such as indocyanine green, toluidine blue O, or methylene blue) with a specific light wavelength to generate reactive oxygen species (ROS) — singlet oxygen, superoxide, hydroxyl radicals — that damage bacterial membranes, nucleic acids, and proteins. ROS-based bactericidal action is non-specific, minimizing the risk of targeted resistance development, a theoretical advantage over antibiotic adjuncts.

### Dual-Light Protocol — Gusseva 2026

Crossover pilot RCT (n=30 healthy adults, 6-week design) testing daily 10-minute home-use dual-light aPDT (405 nm + 810 nm) with indocyanine green photosensitizer added to conventional toothbrushing and flossing.

**Why dual-light**: 405 nm blue-violet light activates biofilm-resident endogenous porphyrins (produced by *P. gingivalis* and other anaerobes) directly, supplementing the exogenous ICG photosensitizer. 810 nm near-infrared light penetrates more deeply into tissue, reaching subgingival zones that 405 nm cannot access.

**Results** (pooled within-subject analysis):

| Group | Plaque change | p |
|---|---|---|
| Dual-light aPDT + conventional | −21.2% (1.0 → 0.5, Greene-Vermillion) | 0.0001 |
| Conventional care alone | −6.5% (no significant change) | 0.11 |

Individual phase results: Group 1 (aPDT first, 2 weeks): −28.4% (p<0.0001); after crossover to conventional only, Group 1 showed +22.4% plaque rebound (p=0.11, not significant). Group 2 (conventional first): −24.1% (p<0.0001) on initial conventional phase; after crossing to aPDT: −14.1% (p=0.011). Note: baseline imbalance between groups (Group 1 1.0 [0.75–1.5] vs Group 2 1.0 [1.0–1.5], p=0.026) is a methodological limitation of this pilot.

**Clinical interpretation**: The ~21% plaque reduction versus zero with conventional care alone is a statistically robust within-subject signal even in a small pilot, with a plausible mechanism. However, this was tested in healthy adults with good baseline hygiene — real-world uptake and effect size in periodontitis patients or systemically compromised patients awaits investigation.

**Caveat**: This is a **pilot study only**. The authors describe it as hypothesis-generating, and the sample size, 2-week treatment phases, and simplified plaque endpoint (healthy subjects, no clinical periodontal outcomes) mean the result should not be used to recommend aPDT as standard of care. It does justify a powered RCT in periodontitis patients measuring PPD/CAL.

See [[periodontics/gusseva-2026-dual-light-photodynamic-therapy-plaque-rct]].

### aPDT in the NMA Context

The John 2017 NMA included diode-laser PDT as one of its nine adjuncts and ranked it second by SUCRA — but this referred to in-office delivery devices (typically single wavelength, applied by clinician). The Gusseva 2026 protocol extends this toward home-use, daily self-administration, which is a fundamentally different delivery model with different adherence and cost implications.

## Subgingival Irrigation & Local "Clean and Seal" Adjuncts

The probiotic and aPDT data above target the biofilm or microbiome ecology. A separate adjunct family acts at the moment of instrumentation itself — delivering a chemical agent into the pocket during scaling or re-instrumentation. Two 2026 studies bracket this family and, read together, deliver a sharp lesson: **delivery mode, not agent identity, governs whether a local chemical adjunct works.**

### Chemical-Agent Subgingival Irrigation — the Null Result (da Silveira 2026)

da Silveira et al. (2026) performed a PRISMA SR+MA isolating one specific clinical habit: flushing a chemical agent (CA) into the pocket as a subgingival *irrigant* during NSPT, versus a water/saline control. Sixteen RCTs (712 patients; 13 parallel, 3 split-mouth) were pooled, with strict eligibility (≥3-month follow-up; gel vehicles, full-mouth disinfection, mouthwash, extracts, and systemic antibiotics/corticosteroids all excluded). Agents spanned povidone-iodine (PVP-I), chlorhexidine (CHX), essential oils, ozonated water, and boric acid.

**Result — no benefit:**

- PPD change: MD **0.01 mm** (95% CI −0.11 to 0.14; P=0.82; I²=73%)
- CAL gain: MD **0.09 mm** (95% CI 0.00 to 0.18; P=0.05; I²=0%)
- BOP reduction: non-significant (MD −1.48; P=0.51)
- No agent (PVP-I, CHX, EO, BA) rescued the result; no difference by irrigation method (syringe vs ultrasonic) or in furcation studies.
- The single ozonated-water study showed a marginal ~0.40 mm PPD reduction, but its own source RCT found no significant effect — interpret with caution.

This **updates and partially overturns Van der Sluijs 2016**, which had reported a slight PVP-I CAL gain not confirmed here. da Silveira frames the negative finding under **antimicrobial-stewardship**: with no demonstrated benefit and given CHX tolerance/resistance concerns, routine adjunctive subgingival irrigation is not supported. The MD of ~0.01–0.09 mm sits *below* even the ~0.3 mm John 2017 NMA ceiling — irrigation as a delivery channel adds nothing.

See [[periodontics/dasilveira-2026-subgingival-irrigation-chemical-agents-nspt-sr-ma]].

### "Clean and Seal" (AA-NaOCl + Cross-linked HA) — the Positive Counterpoint (Jungbauer 2026)

Against that null backdrop, Jungbauer et al. (2026) tested a fundamentally different local protocol — not a flush but an enzymatic-degradation-plus-clot-stabilization sequence — in a setting where adjuncts had previously failed: residual pockets during **supportive periodontal care (SPC)**. The "clean and seal" protocol applies amino-acid-buffered sodium hypochlorite gel (AA-NaOCl, Perisolv) to degrade biofilm and organic matrix, followed by cross-linked hyaluronic acid gel (xHA, hyaDent BG) to stabilize the clot, re-applied at 1 week, as an adjunct to subgingival re-instrumentation (SRI).

Single-center RCT, n=42 (21/arm), stage III/IV grade B/C in SPC, the two highest-PD non-adjacent pockets per patient as study sites:

- **Additional ΔPD (test vs control):** +0.43 mm at 3 mo (p=0.007); **+0.50 mm at 6 mo** (p=0.008)
- **Additional ΔCAL at 6 mo:** **+0.57 mm** (p=0.017)
- **Pocket closure (PD ≤4 mm + BOP−) at 6 mo:** **88.1% test vs 38.1% control** (p<0.001) — roughly doubled
- **BOP+ at study sites (6 mo):** 78.6% → 19.0% reduction in test (p<0.001)
- **Microbiology:** significant decrease of **5 of 8 periopathobionts in test only** (incl. *A. actinomycetemcomitans*); control stable
- **GCF IL-1β, MMP-8:** no significant change, no intergroup difference
- **Effect modifier:** PD reduction more pronounced in deeper pockets → use selectively
- Magnitude is smaller than when the same protocol is used during *initial* therapy (where subgingival instrumentation added 1.1–2.1 mm), because SRI sites are previously-treated non-responders.

See [[periodontics/jungbauer-2026-naocl-hyaluronic-acid-subgingival-reinstrumentation-rct]].

### Reconciling the Two — Delivery Mode Trumps Agent

The contrast is the synthesis point. A passive chemical *irrigation* (da Silveira: many agents, all null) adds nothing, while a structured *gel-based degrade-and-seal* sequence (Jungbauer: AA-NaOCl + xHA, positive) produces a real ~0.5 mm PD and ~0.57 mm CAL increment and doubles pocket closure. The difference is not the chemistry per se but the delivery: contact time, matrix degradation, and clot stabilization. Practically, this means do **not** add a CHX/PVP-I flush during routine SRP (no benefit, stewardship cost), but **do** consider the "clean and seal" gel protocol selectively for deep residual pockets in maintenance, where the gain is largest and justifies the added material cost and chairtime. The biomarker null (IL-1β/MMP-8 unchanged in both Kim 2026 saliva and Jungbauer 2026 GCF) recurs across this overview — clinical/microbiological endpoints move before, or independently of, measurable local cytokine shifts.

## Clinical Decision Points

### When to consider adding probiotics to NSPT

**Appropriate candidate profile**:
- Stage II–III periodontitis undergoing NSPT (Steps 1–2)
- Residual BoP or high PPD ≥5 mm site count after initial SRP
- Patient seeking non-antibiotic adjunct (antibiotic-averse, or where antibiotic stewardship is a concern)
- Smokers: both Kim 2026 smoking-adjusted analyses retained significance, suggesting effect is not abolished by smoking

**Formulation note**: The evidence base for Kim 2026 specifically supports Lactobacillus PB01 DSM14870 + Enterococcus EB10 DSM32307 (each ≥1 × 10^9 CFU, lozenge, twice daily, 12 weeks). OraCMU in Jeon 2026 supports the broader class signal but strain-specific conclusions require caution with non-identical products.

**Realistic outcome expectations**: Probiotics will likely improve BoP resolution and reduce the count of residual deep pockets. They are unlikely to produce clinically meaningful CAL gain beyond what NSPT alone achieves. Set this expectation explicitly with patients.

**Duration**: Both trials used 8–12 weeks of probiotic supplementation. Longer-term benefit and relapse after discontinuation are unknown; microbiological relapse is the primary concern given short follow-up in both trials.

### When to consider aPDT

**Appropriate candidate profile** (extrapolated from Gusseva 2026; not yet studied in periodontitis patients):
- Patients with mechanical access limitations: fixed orthodontic appliances, implant-supported prostheses, furcation areas, reduced manual dexterity
- Adjunct for patients with demonstrated good brushing technique but persisting plaque on specific surfaces
- As a daily home-use hygiene supplement where patient motivation and device access exist

**Critical caveat**: The Gusseva 2026 data are from healthy adults without periodontitis, using plaque as the outcome, over only 2-week active treatment phases. Application to clinical periodontal management requires a powered RCT measuring PPD/CAL/BoP. Do not present aPDT as equivalent to antibiotic or chemotherapeutic adjuncts based on current evidence.

**In-office aPDT**: The NMA context (John 2017, diode-laser PDT ranked second) refers to clinician-applied, single-session delivery — a different context than home use. Both modalities may be complementary.

### Adjunct selection framework

| Goal | First-line adjunct | Evidence level | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reduce BoP after NSPT | Probiotics (Lactobacillus + Enterococcus) | rct (n=80) | 12-week course; no CAL benefit |
| Reduce residual deep pockets (PPD ≥5 mm) | Probiotics adjunct to NSPT | rct (n=80) | PPD count, not depth magnitude |
| CAL gain | Doxycycline hyclate gel or systemic antimicrobials | sr+ma NMA | ~0.3 mm; no head-to-head winner |
| Plaque control where mechanical access limited | Dual-light aPDT (home use) | pilot rct (n=30) | Hypothesis-generating only |
| Overall adjunct choice | No single adjunct dominant | sr+ma NMA | Cost, access, patient preference govern |

## Peripheral Evidence

### Plaque Disclosure — Sooampon 2026

A crossover RCT (n=11) compared 3% erythrosine (FD&C Red No. 3), 3% D&C Red No. 33, and a mixed two-tone D&C Red No. 33/green agent for plaque disclosure. Erythrosine produced the highest plaque visibility but equivalent percentage plaque-index reduction post-brushing across all three agents. D&C Red No. 33 had the least residual mucosal and lip staining, best taste, and easiest removal.

**Regulatory context**: The U.S. FDA banned erythrosine in food and orally ingested drugs effective 15 January 2025 (carcinogenicity in male rats). D&C Red No. 33 is already FDA-approved in oral-care products and is the practical replacement. This is relevant in any periodontal hygiene instruction workflow where plaque disclosure aids patient feedback.

See [[periodontics/sooampon-2026-dc-red-33-erythrosine-plaque-disclosure-rct]].

### Electric vs Manual Toothbrush in Children — Zini 2026

4-week parallel-group RCT (n=60, ages 6–10): advanced oscillating-rotating electric toothbrush (Oral-B iO2) vs manual (Paro Junior Soft) — the electric brush produced 51% greater whole-mouth plaque reduction (0.67 vs 0.44 index units; p=0.003), with 42–64% greater reductions across all subregions (p≤0.021).

**Relevance**: mechanical plaque control is the foundational layer on which all adjuncts (probiotics, aPDT, chemical agents) layer. Ensuring patients use effective mechanical tools is prerequisite; adjuncts add marginal increments on top. In pediatric patients whose manual dexterity is developing, powered brush superiority may be clinically meaningful before any adjunct is considered.

See [[periodontics/zini-2026-electric-vs-manual-toothbrush-children-plaque-rct]].

## Gaps & Limitations

1. **Short follow-up**: Kim 2026 (12 weeks) and Jeon 2026 (8 weeks) do not address microbiological relapse, recolonization by pathogens, or durability of BoP improvement after probiotic discontinuation.

2. **CAL as primary endpoint**: Both 2026 probiotic RCTs are underpowered for CAL (no significant between-group differences). Given the ~0.3 mm NMA ceiling, future trials need very large samples to detect CAL differences or should pivot to patient-reported and pocket-count outcomes.

3. **Strain specificity**: The two probiotic RCTs use different formulations (Lactobacillus PB01 + Enterococcus EB10 vs OraCMU). Class-level generalizations about "probiotics for periodontitis" risk conflating distinct strain-effect profiles. Clinical implementation should specify the studied formulation.

4. **Jeon 2026 abstract-only**: CFU dose exponent, exact strain identifiers, probing depth and CAL results, and specific microbial taxa are not recoverable. Conclusions must be held at the level of GI and BOP outcomes only.

5. **aPDT pilot limitations**: Gusseva 2026 is a 2-week per-phase crossover in 30 healthy adults without periodontitis, measuring plaque only. Generalization to periodontal patients, longer treatment durations, and hard clinical endpoints (CAL, PPD, BoP) is speculative.

6. **Gusseva 2026 baseline imbalance**: Groups showed a significant difference at baseline (p=0.026), complicating between-group phase comparisons despite the crossover design. Within-subject pooled analysis is the more reliable estimate.

7. **Publication bias**: John 2017 quantified ~20% effect inflation in the adjunct literature overall. The 2026 RCTs are each single-centre, small-to-moderate scale, and positive — the same inflation risk applies.

8. **Immunological sampling matrix**: Salivary cytokine panels in Kim 2026 were uniformly non-significant. Future studies should use GCF as the immunological matrix to detect local inflammatory changes that systemic saliva dilutes away.

9. **Head-to-head adjunct comparison**: No trial has directly compared probiotics vs aPDT. The NMA framework provides indirect comparison for earlier adjunct classes but does not yet include the 2026 probiotic or home-use aPDT data.
