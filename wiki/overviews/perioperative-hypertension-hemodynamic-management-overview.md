## 한국어 핵심요약

> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - 핵심 명제: 고혈압 (Hypertension)은 치과 구강수술 환자에서 가장 흔한 전신질환이며, **기저 혈압 (Blood Pressure, BP) 상태 자체가 수술 중 혈역학 변동의 최대 결정인자**다 (Perozo 2026, eta²=0.33 — 마취제·수술시간·불안보다 영향 큼). 그러나 안정적으로 조절된 고혈압이라도 **장기 임플란트 실패 위험은 정상혈압과 차이 없다** (Hamadé 2024 SR+MA, OR 1.100, p=0.671).
> - 발치 중 혈역학 곡선 (Kumari 2025, 정상혈압 50명): 수축기혈압 (Systolic BP, SBP)·이완기혈압 (Diastolic BP, DBP)·심박수 (Heart Rate, HR)가 마취주사→발치 단계에서 유의 상승(P=0.0001), **발치 시점에 최고치**(HR 80.9→84.6 bpm) 후 술후 5분에 기저치 이하로 회복 — 정상혈압에서 SBP 변동 폭은 ~6 mmHg로 작다.
> - 고혈압 환자는 곡선이 훨씬 가파르다 (Perozo 2026): 술전 SBP 159 vs 정상혈압 128, 수술 중 최고 170 vs 136 mmHg.
> - **술전 SBP ≥159 mmHg = 발치 중 혈압강하제 개입 예측 임계값** (Yang 2025, n=2,059, AUC 0.91, 민감도 84%·특이도 89%) — 가장 실용적인 단일 컷오프.
> - 발치 중 일과성 신규 심전도 (Electrocardiogram, ECG) 이상이 **37.2%**에서 발생(거의 다 심방·심실 조기수축, 전부 비치명적, 2,059건 중 2건만 중단) → 고령 고혈압군에서 지속 ECG 모니터링 정당화 (Yang 2025).
> - 시술 연기 임계값 (Perozo 2026 Phase I): 매우 높음 **≥180/110 mmHg → 연기 + 즉시 내과 의뢰**; 높음 160–179/100–109 → 짧은 시술·엄격 모니터링; 중등 140–159/90–99 → 소수술만; 낮음 <140/90 → 통상 진행. Yang 2025의 수술 중 개입 트리거는 SBP ≥180 또는 SBP >170 2회 연속.
> - 임플란트+골증대술 围术期 고혈압 발생률 26.77% (Liu 2024, n=728); 위험인자는 **남성(RR 1.74)·60세 이상(RR 2.35)·수술시간 >60분(RR 1.65)**, 골유도재생술 (Guided Bone Regeneration, GBR)에서 세 인자 모두 유의, 상악동 내측거상은 연령만, 외측창 거상은 모두 비유의.
> - 마취제 영향: 에피네프린 함유 리도카인이 무혈관수축제 메피바케인보다 BP/HR 더 상승 (주로 고혈압군) → Perozo 프로토콜은 조절된 고혈압에서 **에피네프린 0.04 mg(1:100,000 2 카트리지)로 상한**.
> - 임상 종합: 술전 BP 스크리닝(특히 SBP ≥159) → 위험 stratify → 고위험군에 지속 ECG·반복 BP·짧고 분할된 세션·불안 조절 → 수술 중 SBP ≥180 시 약물 개입(니트로글리세린 86% 사용) → 술후 회복실 모니터링·심혈관계 위험 때문에 NSAID 회피. 단 Perozo 프로토콜은 n=39 파일럿 미검증, Liu 2024는 초록만, 모니터링 근거는 단일센터 후향연구 위주 — 근거 수준 한계 명시.

## One-line Summary

Synthesis of 5 papers on perioperative hypertension in dental/oral surgery: baseline BP status is the dominant intraoperative hemodynamic determinant (Perozo 2026, eta²=0.33) — hypertensives run ~30 mmHg higher SBP throughout and a 7-fold hemorrhage rate (66.7% vs 9.5%) — yet stable hypertension does not raise long-term implant failure (Hamadé 2024 SR+MA, OR 1.100, NS). The practical triage cutoff is preoperative SBP ≥159 mmHg (Yang 2025, AUC 0.91) predicting need for intraoperative BP drugs; transient new ECG abnormalities occur in 37.2% of hypertensive extractions (all non-lethal), and implant+bone-augmentation surgery triggers perioperative hypertension in 26.77% (older males, >60 min cases, GBR). Defer at ≥180/110 mmHg.

## Summary

Arterial hypertension is the most common systemic disease among oral-surgery patients (Perozo 2026), so the perioperative question is not *whether* to treat hypertensives but *how to read and manage their hemodynamics chairside*. Five papers in the wiki triangulate this from three angles: (1) **what BP/HR actually do during a procedure** (Kumari 2025 in normotensives, Perozo 2026 in hypertensives), (2) **who needs intervention and at what threshold** (Yang 2025 SBP cutoff + ECG, Liu 2024 augmentation risk factors), and (3) **whether any of this changes the durable outcome** (Hamadé 2024 implant survival).

The synthesized thesis has four load-bearing claims:

1. **The intraoperative hemodynamic spike is real, peaks at the surgical act, and recovers fast.** In normotensives, SBP/DBP/HR all rise significantly through injection and peak at extraction, then drop *below* baseline by 5 min postop (Kumari 2025) — but the absolute swing is small (~6 mmHg SBP).
2. **Baseline hypertension dominates and amplifies that response.** Hypertensive status, not anesthetic choice or anxiety, is the largest determinant of variability (Perozo 2026, eta²=0.33); hypertensives run ~30 mmHg higher SBP throughout and bleed far more (66.7% vs 9.5%).
3. **There is now an actionable preoperative cutoff.** Preoperative SBP ≥159 mmHg predicts the need for intraoperative antihypertensive drugs with AUC 0.91 (Yang 2025), the most clinically useful number in this cluster, filling the gap left by ACC/AHA generically labeling dental procedures "low-risk."
4. **Stable, controlled hypertension does not penalize the durable outcome.** Across 21,066 implants, hypertensives had no higher failure odds than normotensives (Hamadé 2024, OR 1.100, p=0.671) — so the management target is *intraoperative safety*, not long-term osseointegration.

The unifying clinical message: screen BP preoperatively, stratify risk, monitor the high-risk patient continuously (ECG + serial BP), keep sessions short and divided, and reserve drug intervention and deferral for the genuinely elevated — while reassuring the controlled hypertensive that the implant itself is not at extra risk.

## 1. The intraoperative hemodynamic curve — what BP/HR do during surgery

Kumari 2025 supplies the **normotensive reference curve** (prospective, n=50, 2% lidocaine + 1:100,000 adrenaline). All parameters varied significantly across time (P=0.0001), peaking at the **extraction step (T3), not at needle insertion**, then falling below baseline 5 min postop:

| Parameter | Preop (T1) | Injection (T2) | Extraction (T3) | Postop (T4) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| SBP (mmHg) | 126.6 | 127.5 | **128.0** | 122.0 |
| DBP (mmHg) | 72.7 | 73.3 | **74.6** | 73.5 |
| HR (bpm) | 80.9 | 81.1 | **84.6** | 76.5 |
| SpO₂ (%) | 97.5 | 97.9 | 98.0 | 98.1 |

Two takeaways: the surge is small in normotensives (~6 mmHg SBP swing; females show larger BP rises), and the **postoperative dip below baseline** means the danger window is intraoperative, centered on the surgical act itself.

Perozo 2026 shows how that same curve **shifts up and steepens in hypertensives** (controlled/treated, n=18 vs 21 normotensive):

| Variable | Normotensive | Hypertensive | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Preop SBP | 128 | 159 | 0.006 |
| Intraop SBP (peak) | 136 | 170 | 0.004 |
| Intraop DBP | 82 | 97 | 0.013 |
| Peak HR | 86 | 85 | 0.71 (NS) |
| Hemorrhagic complications | 9.5% | 66.7% | 0.008 |

The hemorrhage signal (Cramer's V=0.55) is the most surgically consequential finding — controlled hypertensives still bled ~7× more. Notably HR did not differ (the difference is pressure, not rate), and **anxiety acted differently by group: it drove SBP in hypertensives but HR in normotensives** (Perozo 2026). Morning procedures peaked higher than afternoon (circadian surge: morning HTN peak SBP >250 vs afternoon 187 mmHg).

## 2. BP risk stratification & defer thresholds

The clearest framework is the **Perozo protocol Phase I** (Perozo 2026), aligned with the intervention/defer numbers from Yang 2025:

| Tier | Preop BP (SBP/DBP, mmHg) | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Low | <140 / <90 | Proceed routine |
| Moderate | 140–159 / 90–99 | Minor procedures only; reduce anxiety; monitor BP |
| High | 160–179 / 100–109 | Short procedures only; strict BP monitoring |
| **Very high** | **≥180 / ≥110** | **Defer + urgent medical consultation** |

Cross-check with the data-derived cutoffs:

- **Preoperative SBP ≥159 mmHg** predicts need for intraoperative antihypertensive drugs (Yang 2025): AUC 0.91, sensitivity 84.2%, specificity 88.8%. This is the single most actionable triage number — it sits exactly at the Perozo Moderate/High boundary, lending the otherwise-unvalidated protocol empirical support.
- **Intraoperative intervention trigger** (Yang 2025 institutional rule): intraoperative SBP ≥180 mmHg, *or* SBP >170 mmHg on ≥2 readings → temporary antihypertensive. In practice 8.01% of hypertensive extractions crossed this; nitroglycerin was used in 86%, the rest captopril/metoprolol.
- **Operative-time risk**: estimated operative time was a strong independent predictor of needing BP intervention (Yang 2025, OR **3.62**) — duration matters as much as baseline pressure.

Reassurance bound: even in this elderly hypertensive cohort (mean 74.6 y), only **2 of 2,059 procedures were terminated** (Yang 2025) — deferral mid-procedure is rare when patients are pre-screened.

## 3. Intraoperative monitoring protocol (ECG + serial BP)

Yang 2025 (n=2,059 extractions, 1,314 hypertensives under continuous intraoperative ECG) is the monitoring backbone:

- **New-onset transient ECG abnormality in 37.20%** — almost entirely supraventricular premature atrial contraction (44.4%) + ventricular premature contraction (42.3%) = 86.7% combined. **All were non-lethal.**
- Predictors of ECG abnormality form a **distinct set** from BP-intervention predictors: older age (OR 1.024), female sex (OR 0.796, females higher risk), cerebrovascular disease (OR 1.301), SBP at operation start, **number of teeth extracted (OR 1.182)**, and operative time (OR 1.236).
- Monitoring cadence used: continuous ECG + BP every 5–10 min at preop / operation-start / intraoperative-max, with cardiologist co-management.

Practical monitoring rules synthesized:

| Patient/procedure profile | Monitoring intensity |
|---|---|
| Controlled HTN, preop SBP <159, short single-tooth | Baseline + post-op BP; standard vigilance |
| Preop SBP ≥159, or long/multi-tooth, or elderly | **Continuous ECG + serial BP (q5–10 min)**, cardiologist available |
| Cerebrovascular disease / female / age ≥76 | Continuous ECG (higher arrhythmia risk per Yang 2025) |
| Implant + bone augmentation, esp. GBR >60 min | Proactive intraoperative BP monitoring (Liu 2024) |

Session design: because tooth count and operative time both drive risk, **fewer extractions per visit and shorter sessions** lower intraoperative cardiovascular stress (Yang 2025).

## 4. Implant & bone-augmentation considerations

Two papers separate the *intraoperative* risk from the *durable outcome* — a key distinction for consenting hypertensive implant patients.

**Intraoperative surge during implant + simultaneous bone augmentation** (Liu 2024, n=728, abstract-only): perioperative hypertension occurred in **26.77%**, with SBP variability averaging ~10% (max ~50%). Whole-cohort risk factors:

| Factor | RR (95% CI) |
|---|---|
| Male sex | 1.74 (1.21–2.50) |
| Age ≥60 y | 2.35 (1.54–3.58) |
| Surgical time >60 min | 1.65 (1.15–2.38) |

The **risk-factor profile shifted by augmentation approach** (overall incidence similar across the three): in **GBR** all three factors were significant; in **transalveolar/transcrestal sinus lift** only age ≥60 y; in **lateral-window sinus lift** none reached significance (Liu 2024). So an older male in a long GBR case is the highest-surge profile, dovetailing with Yang's operative-time and Perozo's circadian/baseline findings.

**Durable outcome is not penalized** (Hamadé 2024 SR+MA, 24 studies, 21,066 implants): hypertensive vs normotensive implant failure OR **1.100 (95% CI 0.709–1.707, p=0.671)** — non-significant, stable across follow-up (meta-regression p=0.824), no publication bias. Despite mechanistic concerns (hypertension elevates PTH/RANKL and causes microvascular rarefaction), **controlled-or-not, hypertension by itself does not raise implant failure odds.** The limitation is poor reporting of control status and confounders.

**Synthesized implant message**: manage the *intraoperative* spike (older male, long GBR cases are highest risk — monitor, keep <60 min where possible), but reassure the patient the *implant survival* itself is not compromised by their hypertension.

## 5. Anesthesia & vasoconstrictor considerations

- **Epinephrine-containing lidocaine raised BP/HR more than plain mepivacaine**, chiefly in hypertensives (Perozo 2026). Kumari 2025's normotensive curve used 2% lidocaine + 1:100,000 adrenaline and still saw only modest swings — the vasoconstrictor effect is amplified by baseline hypertension, not by the drug alone.
- **Perozo cap**: epinephrine limited to **0.04 mg = two cartridges of 1:100,000** in controlled hypertensives (Perozo 2026).
- **Anxiety management** matters group-specifically: anxiolysis/conscious sedation/distraction is recommended for anxious and hypertensive patients (Kumari 2025), and because anxiety drives SBP specifically in hypertensives (Perozo 2026 r=0.58, p=0.01), anxiety control is itself a BP-control lever in this group.

**Perozo protocol — intra/postoperative drugs** (Perozo 2026, unvalidated): captopril 25 mg SL (hypertensive crisis), nifedipine bitten capsule (acute surge), nitroglycerin SL (coronary symptoms), clonidine (refractory). **Postoperative: avoid NSAIDs** (cardiovascular risk), monitor vitals in recovery, structured follow-up with warning-sign education. (Yang 2025's real-world acute drug of choice was nitroglycerin, 86% of interventions.)

## 6. Evidence-quality caveats (living-document)

- **Perozo protocol is a hypothesis-generating pilot** (n=39, two Venezuelan centers, retrospective analysis of prospectively collected data) — explicitly unvalidated; do not adopt as a validated standard. Awaits multicenter prospective testing.
- **Liu 2024 is abstract-only** (Chinese article, PMC body not retrievable): threshold definitions, anesthesia/sedation protocol, and confounder adjustment are not captured — verify against full text before clinical reliance.
- **Yang 2025 & Kumari 2025 are single-center** (Yang retrospective; Kumari normotensive-only so hypertensive recommendations are *inferred*, not directly tested, and the study notes low statistical power).
- **Hamadé 2024 is the highest-evidence node** (SR+MA) but is limited by poor characterization of hypertension control status and unadjusted confounders (diabetes, smoking, bruxism, bisphosphonates).
- Numbers to refresh as evidence matures: the SBP ≥159 cutoff (single-center), the 26.77% augmentation incidence (single Beijing center), and any future validation of the Perozo tiers.
