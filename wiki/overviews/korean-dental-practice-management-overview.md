## Dentist Workforce: Regional Distribution 2012–2022

**JKDA 2024 (Yim & Jeon, cross-sectional, KDA Health Policy Institute):**

Key findings:
- **Annual average growth rate: 2.39%** in active dentist count (2012–2022)
- Significant regional distribution disparity exists (Seoul metropolitan vs non-metropolitan areas; ANOVA confirmed difference)
- International metric: WHO indicator (active dentists per 100,000 population) applied for cross-national comparability

Policy context: this is the primary quantitative anchor in current debates over dental school quota (치과대학 정원) expansion. Both the "supply surplus" and "new school needed" positions use this dataset as a common reference. [cross-sectional; descriptive only — causality not inferable]

Clinical relevance for solo practitioners:
- 2.39% annual supply growth outpaces population growth in most regions
- Regional concentration (수도권 쏠림) means competitive density is markedly higher in metropolitan areas
- Long-term planning (branch clinic location, specialty niche selection) should incorporate regional density data
