## One-line Summary
Synthesis of 11 VPT papers (3 SR+MA/NMA, 2 RCT, 1 consensus, 1 prospective, 1 in-vitro, 1 narrative-review, 1 clinical-lecture): Biodentine and MTA are first-line materials (OR 4.41 and 3.02 vs Ca(OH)₂); overall VPT success >93%; diagnosis of reversible vs irreversible pulpitis is the critical gate.

## Summary

Vital pulp therapy (생활치수요법, VPT) is the collective term for procedures that preserve viable pulp tissue to maintain tooth vitality and stimulate tertiary dentin formation. The evidence base has matured substantially since 2017: three network meta-analyses (Fasoulas 2023, Komora 2024, Hatipoglu 2025) and one Bayesian NMA with CINeMA grading (Elmsmari 2026) converge on the same material hierarchy, the ESE 2019 position statement provides the procedural framework, and clinical data now cover both pediatric (Chailertvanitkul 2014) and adult (Kundzina 2017, Taha 2017) populations.

The most important clinical takeaway is that diagnosis drives outcome more than technique. Selecting teeth with reversible pulpitis or incidental mechanical/carious exposure in the absence of signs of irreversible inflammation remains the dominant success predictor — materials and technique optimise outcomes only within a correctly selected case.

## VPT Modality Hierarchy (ESE 2019)

Four modalities, ordered from least to most invasive:

| Modality | Indication | Material |
|---|---|---|
| Indirect pulp capping (IPC) | Near-pulp exposure, no exposure | Liner (Biodentine/GIC) |
| Direct pulp capping (DPC) | Point exposure, reversible pulpitis | Biodentine or MTA |
| Partial pulpotomy | Shallow carious/traumatic exposure | Biodentine or MTA |
| Full pulpotomy | Deeper exposure, symptomatic reversible pulpitis | MTA; vital pulpectomy (Jang 2026) |

Ca(OH)₂ is NOT recommended as a first-line material for any VPT modality (ESE 2019 [근거강함], confirmed by 3 NMA [근거강함]).

## Material Evidence: Key Numbers

**Hatipoglu 2025 (NMR, 21 RCTs, n=1,733) — DPC:**

| Material | OR vs Ca(OH)₂ | 95% CrI |
|---|---|---|
| Biodentine | 4.41 | 1.40–17.66 |
| MTA | 3.02 | 1.19–8.30 |
| TheraCal | intermediate | included |
| Ca(OH)₂ | 1.00 (ref) | — |

SUCRA ranking: Biodentine > MTA > TheraCal > Ca(OH)₂ [근거강함]

**Komora 2024 (NMA, 21 RCTs, mature permanent teeth) — failure rate OR vs MTA:**

| Material | 6 mo | 12 mo | 24 mo | Sig. |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ca(OH)₂ | 2.26 | 2.53 | 2.46 | ✓ |
| Biodentine | 1.09 | 1.21 | 1.47 | NS |
| Totalfill | 1.19 | 1.43 | NS | NS |

Conclusion: Ca(OH)₂ fails at 2.3–2.5× the rate of MTA across all time points; Biodentine and Totalfill are equivalent to MTA. [근거강함]

**Elmsmari 2026 (Bayesian NMA + CINeMA, 35 RCTs, n=2,906):**
- VPT success >93% across all modalities
- MTA vs Ca(OH)₂: HIGH certainty superior
- Biodentine vs Ca(OH)₂: MODERATE certainty superior
- Silicate-vs-silicate (MTA vs Biodentine): no clear winner (overlapping credible intervals) [근거강함]

**Pusa 2025 (SR+MA, 21 RCTs, n=1,816):**
- DPC MTA vs Ca(OH)₂: RR 1.21 (95% CI 1.02–1.42), p=0.03 — MTA superior [근거강함]
- DPC MTA vs Biodentine: NS
- Partial vs full pulpotomy: NS

**Reparative dentin bridge — Nowicka 2015 (in vitro, micro-CT, 44 teeth):**
- Bridge formation: Biodentine > MTA > Ca(OH)₂ >> Single Bond Universal
- SBU showed toxic/inflammatory response — DPC 금기 [in-vitro]

## Clinical Pearl: Vital Pulpectomy (Jang 2026)

A clinical concept distinct from full root canal treatment: removes coronal + upper-root pulp while preserving apical vital pulp to stimulate tertiary dentin. Key protocol:

- Material: Endoseem (bioceramic), ≥3 mm barrier thickness mandatory
- Working length: 2–3 mm SHORT of apex (not at apex)
- Irrigation: NaOCl 5.25% + ultrasonic; EDTA not used (NaOCl handles smear layer)
- Hemostasis: sustained pressure; Endoseem for persistent bleeding
- Restoration: crown mandatory (Endoseem dissolves without crown seal)

Indications: cracked teeth, geriatric patients, over-erupted antagonist after implant.

---

## Diagnostic Gate

Before selecting VPT modality, confirm:

1. **Pulp sensibility**: positive response to cold/EPT suggests vital pulp
2. **Symptom pattern**: spontaneous pain, lingering pain >30 s → irreversible pulpitis → root canal treatment
3. **Radiograph**: no periapical pathology, no internal resorption
4. **Bleeding quality at exposure**: bright red, controlled hemostasis within 5 min → favorable [합의수준]
5. **Patient age/root maturity**: immature apex favors VPT (Jang 2026); mature apex also acceptable with correct diagnosis (Taha 2017)

---

## Follow-up and Failure Recognition

All three NMA confirm success rates decline over time regardless of material (Hatipoglu 2025) — follow-up is mandatory at 6, 12, and 24 months. Failure signs: pain, swelling, sinus tract, periapical pathosis, internal resorption.
