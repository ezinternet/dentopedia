> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - **핵심 명제**: 치과 진료시장은 경제학적으로 비정상적인 시장이다 — 정보 비대칭(asymmetric information), 공급자 유발 수요(Supplier-Induced Demand, SID), 시장 실패(market failure)가 동시에 작동한다. 그래서 가격·제도 같은 단순한 레버(lever)를 당겨도 직관과 다르게 반응한다.
> - **Listl 2019(narrative review)**가 이론 틀을 제공한다: 구강건강의 경제적 결정요인, 정보 비대칭 하 수요, 시장 실패 → 효율(efficiency)·형평(equity) 함의, 자원 제약 하 경제성 평가, 행동경제학적 수요·공급, 치과 인력 계획(workforce planning).
> - **정보 비대칭의 핵심 결과**: 환자는 치료의 필요성·질을 스스로 판단할 수 없어 의사가 대리인(agent)이 되고, 이때 의사가 수요 자체를 만들어내는 **공급자 유발 수요(SID)**가 발생한다 — 치과 시장 왜곡의 근본 원인.
> - **수요측 자연실험 (Ono 2022, DiD, retrospective)**: 일본에서 만 10세에 소아 치과 보조금(childhood subsidy)을 중단해도 12개월 단기 이용량·비용·급성 중증 치과상태에 **유의한 변화 없음** (초진 mean ratio 1.01, 95% CI 0.97–1.04). 아동 1108명·내원 6276건.
> - **Ono의 맥락 조건**: 일본은 **전국민 의료보장(universal coverage)** 체계라 잔여 본인부담(residual cost-sharing)이 이미 미미했다 → 검증된 가격 변화 자체가 작았다. "가격이 무의미하다"가 아니라 "이 맥락에서 현 보조금의 한계효용이 작다"는 뜻 — 미국식 고(高)본인부담 맥락과 구별해야 한다.
> - **공급측 자연실험 (Chen 2024, DiD, MEPS 2001–2014)**: 치과위생사(dental hygienist) 자율성(autonomy)을 **중간 수준**으로 높이면 예방치료(preventive care) 증가로 전체 방문↑, 그러나 **최고 수준** 자율성에서는 치료(treatment) 이용↓ — **비단조(non-monotonic) 용량-반응** 패턴.
> - **취약지 증폭 효과**: Chen의 두 효과 모두 치과의료 취약지(provider-shortage area)에서 더 크게 나타나, 진료 자원이 위생사 쪽으로 재배분된다 → scope-of-practice(업무범위)는 **접근성(access) 레버**다.
> - **효율 vs 형평**: 시장 실패는 두 가지를 동시에 훼손한다 — 효율(자원이 최고가치 진료로 못 감)과 형평(필요가 아닌 지불능력이 이용을 좌우). 정책 레버는 둘 사이 trade-off를 만든다.
> - **인력 계획 원칙**: 인력은 공급 관성(supply inertia)이 아니라 **시스템 목표(필요·수요)**에서 도출해야 한다 (Listl). Chen은 이를 실증한다 — 위생사 자율성은 인력 정책 레버이지 단순 규제완화가 아니다.
> - **임상·정책 시사점**: 치과 수요 변화는 항상 정보 비대칭·SID 렌즈로 해석할 것; 본인부담 효과는 보장체계(universal vs US)에 따라 다르다; 업무범위 확대는 용량 trade-off를 동반하는 접근성 레버다; 한국 데이터는 없어 직접 적용 전 맥락 보정 필수.

## One-line Summary

A 3-paper synthesis framing dental care as an economically atypical market (asymmetric information, supplier-induced demand, market failure → efficiency/equity trade-offs): Listl 2019 supplies the health-economics framework, and two difference-in-differences natural experiments instantiate it — Ono 2022 (removing a childhood subsidy did NOT change utilization in universal-coverage Japan) on the demand side and Chen 2024 (hygienist autonomy raises prevention but maximal autonomy lowers treatment use, strongest in shortage areas) on the supply side.

## 한줄요약

논문 3편 종합: 치과 진료는 정보 비대칭·공급자 유발 수요·시장 실패가 작동하는 경제학적 비정상 시장이며(효율·형평 trade-off), Listl 2019가 그 이론 틀을, Ono 2022(수요측 보조금 중단 → 이용 변화 없음, 전국민보장 일본)와 Chen 2024(공급측 위생사 자율성 → 예방↑·최고자율성에서 치료↓, 취약지에서 증폭)라는 두 이중차분(DiD) 자연실험이 그 실증을 제공한다.

## Thesis

**Dental care is not a textbook market, so textbook levers misbehave.** The defining distortion is **asymmetric information**: a patient cannot reliably judge whether care is needed or whether it was done well, so the provider acts as the patient's *agent*. That agency relationship is what makes dentistry economically atypical — it opens the door to **supplier-induced demand (SID)** (the provider can shape the quantity of care demanded) and produces **market failure**, which degrades both **efficiency** (resources fail to reach the highest-value care) and **equity** (utilization tracks ability to pay rather than need). Listl 2019 lays out this framework and adds the corollary that the dental workforce should be planned from system goals (need/demand), not from supply inertia.

Because of these distortions, the obvious policy levers do not behave as naive intuition predicts — and the two empirical papers are natural experiments that test exactly this, one on each side of the market:

- **Demand side (price/cost-sharing).** Ono 2022 removes a public childhood dental subsidy at age 10 in Japan and finds *no* short-term change in utilization, cost, or severe conditions. Standard demand theory predicts that raising patient price lowers utilization — but in a **universal-coverage** system where residual cost-sharing was already minimal, the price change being tested was small, and the effect was null. The lesson is not that price is irrelevant; it is that the *effect of a cost-sharing lever is entirely context-dependent* (a small price change off an already-low base in a universal system behaves nothing like large out-of-pocket exposure in the US).

- **Supply side (scope of practice).** Chen 2024 expands what dental hygienists may do without dentist supervision and finds a **non-monotonic** response: *moderate* autonomy raises total visits via more preventive care, but the *highest* autonomy tier *reduces* treatment use — both effects amplified in provider-shortage areas. Loosening supervision is a workforce/access lever, but it has a dose–response with a turning point, not a straight line.

Together they show the same underlying point from two directions: in a market shaped by asymmetric information and SID, **demand-side and supply-side levers both produce context-dependent, sometimes counterintuitive effects** — exactly why Listl's framework (and not naive supply-and-demand) is the right lens.

## Policy & Clinical Decision Points

1. **Read demand changes through the asymmetric-information / SID lens.** When utilization shifts after a price, insurance, or provider-supply change, do not assume a simple demand-curve response. Provider agency and supplier-induced demand can mute, amplify, or reverse the expected effect — interpret the change against Listl's framework, not against textbook supply-and-demand.

2. **Cost-sharing effects are context-dependent (universal vs US).** A null result like Ono's says the *current* subsidy has limited marginal value in a universal-coverage system with already-minimal residual charges — it does NOT generalize to high-out-of-pocket settings (e.g., the US) where the same lever can move utilization substantially. Match the evidence to the financing context before importing a conclusion.

3. **Scope-of-practice is an access lever with a dose trade-off.** Expanding dental-hygienist autonomy can raise preventive-care access, especially in shortage areas — but the response is non-monotonic: at the highest autonomy tier treatment use falls. Calibrate the *degree* of delegation to the intended outcome (prevention access vs. total treatment capacity), rather than assuming "more autonomy = more care."

4. **Plan workforce to system goals, not supply inertia.** Listl's prescription, operationalized by Chen: derive workforce/scope policy from need and demand (and where access gaps are — shortage areas), not from the existing distribution of providers. The hygienist-autonomy evidence shows the workforce mix is a genuine, tunable policy instrument.

## Gaps & Future Research

- **Evidence weight is thin.** The two empirical papers are both `retrospective` difference-in-differences natural experiments; the framework paper is a `narrative-review`. There is no RCT, SR, or meta-analysis in this set — conclusions are directional, not pooled.
- **Abstract-only ingest (2 of 3).** Both Ono 2022 and Chen 2024 are `full_text: false`; Chen's point estimates, confidence intervals, model specification, and the mechanism behind the highest-autonomy treatment-use decrease are not captured. Effect sizes are therefore largely unquantified for the supply-side finding.
- **Short-term only / single setting.** Ono is a 12-month, single-prefecture (Kumamoto) result using a claims-based caries proxy — no long-term or multi-region replication.
- **No Korean data.** All evidence is from Japan (universal coverage) and the US (mixed financing, MEPS). The financing context, fee schedule, and scope-of-practice regulation in Korea differ, so none of these conclusions transfers directly — Korean-context studies are the key missing piece.
- **Demand/supply levers studied in isolation.** No paper here examines the *interaction* (e.g., how cost-sharing changes interact with workforce/scope changes), which is where the asymmetric-information framework predicts the most counterintuitive effects.
