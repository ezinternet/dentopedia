> - 적응증: 중증 치아마모·침식(Tooth Wear Evaluation System, TWES 2~4), 흔히 교합고경(Vertical Dimension of Occlusion, VDO) 상실 동반 → semi-additive DSD/Kois로 VDO 회복(schlichting·ferrando-cascales).
> - 기계적 타당성: 초박형 세라믹이 생리적 구치 교합력을 상회 — 임상 기준 **저작 400~800N·이악물기 ~800N**(essam), **최대교합력 424~630N**(maldonado). al-akhali는 0.5/0.8mm 세라믹이 **120만 회·98N 저작 후 100% 생존**.
> - 재료 순위 (파절저항 정적): 세라믹(리튬디실리케이트 LD/e.max, ZLS) **>** 폴리머(PICN, PMMA) — al-akhali LD>PICN·PMMA(P≤0.015). 단 **생존율로는 세라믹≈복합레진**(임상적 유의차 없음).
> - 임상 생존 (재료 무의미): schlichting RCT 3년 세라믹 100% vs 복합레진 84.7%(P=.124 NS), ferrando-cascales 5년 세라믹 93.1% vs 복합레진 86.3%(p=0.107 NS).
> - **최소두께 논쟁이 핵심 미해결**: sasse는 **0.7/1.0mm만 안전**(thin 0.3/0.6mm enamel-only 생존 12.5%), essam·al-akhali는 **0.5mm 타당**(적절 컨디셔닝 시), maldonado는 **복합레진 <1mm·LD 0.7~1.5mm** → 기질·재료·이악물기에 따라 0.5~1.0mm.
> - 접착 기질 보상: 얇은 세라믹은 **상아질/복합레진 본딩이 법랑질보다 우수**(sasse, thin/medium에서 p≤0.05), 치아측은 즉시상아질밀봉(Immediate Dentin Sealing, IDS)이 표준(schlichting).
> - 표면처리: **모노본드 Etch&Prime ≥ 불산(HF) >> APF** — essam 파절하중 MON-0.5mm 1277N > HF 1166N > APF 962N, APF는 debonding(Mode II) 유발 → **0.5mm·이악물기 환자에 APF 금기**; 세라믹 HF+실란, 복합레진 airborne-particle abrasion+실란.
> - 피로(fatigue) vs 정적파절 순위 상충: maldonado는 3편 중 2편 복합레진 피로 우위, al-akhali는 세라믹 정적 우위, schlichting은 복합레진 표면 열화(거칠기 p=.003)↑ — 피로·정적·표면내구성 순위가 갈림.
> - 실패는 경미·수리가능: 대부분 chipping(schlichting 5건 전부 부분 chipping·수복가능, ferrando-cascales 21건 중 18건 직접 수리) → additive 접착 철학의 가역성이 강점.
> - 위치 위험인자: **후방/교합면 위치가 유일한 유의 실패예측인자**(ferrando-cascales HR 0.143, p=0.008; 교합면 79.1% vs 구개 100%·순면 94.1%).
> - 탈착·재사용: Er,Cr:YSGG 레이저 탈착 시간은 재료 무관(~87초, p=0.995)이나 **e.max·지르코니아는 무손상, 고결정 LiSi(GC Initial)는 60% 손상** → LiSi 레이저 재활용 고위험(el-sheikh).
> - 근거 한계: RCT·case series 소표본(n=11·n=8), SR 6편 전부 high/medium 편향 — 결론은 방향성 신호이지 강한 확정 아님.

## One-line Summary

Synthesis of 7 papers (4 in-vitro, 1 SR, 1 RCT, 1 retrospective case series) on ultrathin occlusal (table-top) veneers for tooth wear and erosion — converging on ultrathin ceramic being mechanically viable beyond physiologic posterior forces, ceramic and CAD/CAM composite being clinically equivalent for survival, an unsettled 0.5–1.0 mm minimum-thickness threshold, Monobond etch & prime ≥ HF ≫ APF for conditioning, and repairability as the additive philosophy's core advantage.

## Summary

The erosive-wear patient presents a problem anterior-laminate veneers do not address: posterior occlusal surfaces lost to acid and attrition, often with reduced vertical dimension, that must be rebuilt **additively** — without the aggressive full-crown reduction that would sacrifice remaining sound structure. **Occlusal (table-top) veneers** answer this, and this synthesis assembles the full evidence ladder behind them: four in-vitro bench studies (al-Akhali 2017, Essam 2023, Sasse 2015, El-Sheikh 2024), one systematic review (Maldonado 2024), one RCT (Schlichting 2022), and one 5-year retrospective case series (Ferrando-Cascales 2023).

Four clinical questions organize the evidence. **(1) Are ultrathin occlusal veneers mechanically viable?** Yes — al-Akhali et al. (2017) showed 0.5/0.8 mm CAD/CAM restorations survived 1.2 million chewing cycles at 98 N with 100% survival across all four material classes, and both Essam and Maldonado benchmark measured fracture loads far above the 400–800 N of physiologic posterior mastication. **(2) Which material?** For static fracture, ceramic (lithium disilicate, ZLS) beats polymer, but for clinical *survival* ceramic and CAD/CAM composite are indistinguishable (Schlichting, Ferrando-Cascales, Maldonado). **(3) How thin can you go?** This is the field's live disagreement — 0.5 mm to 1.0 mm depending on substrate, material, and parafunction. **(4) How do you bond, repair, and retrieve?** Monobond etch & prime rivals HF and both crush APF; immediate dentin sealing prepares the tooth; chipping failures are directly repairable; and laser retrieval is material-dependent.

**핵심 명제 (Evidence Propositions)**

1. **Ultrathin (0.5 mm) occlusal veneers withstand physiologic posterior loads across every CAD/CAM material class.** Al-Akhali et al. (2017), an in-vitro study of 64 premolars with enamel-only 0.5 mm fissure / 0.8 mm cusp preparations, recorded 100% survival after 1.2 million cycles at 98 N for lithium disilicate (e.max CAD), ZLS (Suprinity), PICN (Enamic), and PMMA (Telio) — establishing that the *minimally invasive* geometry itself is not the weak link. [근거강함 — in-vitro]

2. **Ceramic outperforms polymer on static fracture, but material type is non-significant for clinical survival.** Al-Akhali et al. (2017) found LD > PICN and LD > PMMA (P ≤ 0.015), yet the two clinical studies show no material effect on survival: Schlichting et al. (2022) RCT reported 3-year Kaplan-Meier survival of ceramic 100% vs composite 84.7% (P = .124, NS), and Ferrando-Cascales et al. (2023) found ceramic 93.1% vs composite 86.3% (p = 0.107, NS). Maldonado et al. (2024) concurred that fracture resistance is comparable between CAD/CAM composite and LD. [근거강함 — RCT + 후향 + SR 수렴]

3. **The minimum-thickness threshold is unsettled between 0.5 and 1.0 mm and depends on substrate.** Sasse et al. (2015) found only the 0.7/1.0 mm group survived undamaged (100%), while thin 0.3/0.6 mm enamel-only fell to 12.5% survival — arguing a 0.7/1.0 mm minimum. Essam et al. (2023) validated 0.5 mm LD (fracture loads 962–1277 N with APF/HF/Monobond) as sufficient for molar forces, and Maldonado et al. (2024) recommended composite < 1 mm and LD 0.7–1.5 mm. The safe minimum therefore ranges ~0.5–1.0 mm by material, substrate, and parafunction. [상충 — 미해결]

4. **Bonding to dentin or composite compensates for thin ceramic; enamel-only thin restorations are the failure-prone case.** Sasse et al. (2015) showed dentin- and composite-bonded groups significantly outperformed enamel-only in thin/medium ceramics (p ≤ 0.05; dentin vs composite NS), with thin enamel-only surviving only 12.5% vs ~50% on dentin/composite. This tempers the "bond only to enamel" ideal when preparations are ultra-minimal. [근거강함 — in-vitro]

5. **Surface conditioning ranks Monobond etch & prime ≥ HF ≫ APF, and APF is contraindicated at 0.5 mm in parafunction.** Essam et al. (2023) recorded fracture loads at 0.5 mm of Monobond 1277 N > HF 1166 N > APF 962 N (surface-treatment p = 0.002), with HF/Monobond producing Mode I (crack, bond intact) failures and APF producing Mode II (debonding). Monobond etch & prime is thus a biologically safer HF substitute of equal or better performance. Composite restorations are conditioned by airborne-particle abrasion + silane (Schlichting 2022). [근거강함 — in-vitro]

6. **Failures are predominantly minor, repairable chipping — the additive philosophy's decisive advantage.** Schlichting et al. (2022) reported zero complete failures; all 5 failures were partial chipping in composite, all restorable. Ferrando-Cascales et al. (2023) had 21/212 complications, of which 18 were minor chipping repaired directly (1 debonding, 1 tooth fracture, 2 secondary caries). [근거강함]

7. **Posterior/occlusal location is the dominant failure predictor — not material, parafunction score, or bite guard.** Ferrando-Cascales et al. (2023), a 5-year full-mouth adhesive rehabilitation series (8 patients, 212 restorations, 90.1% overall survival), found occlusal restorations survived 79.1% vs palatal 100% and vestibular 94.1%; posterior location was the only significant predictor (HR 0.143, 95% CI 0.034–0.601, p = 0.008), while TWES grade, parafunction, and bite guard were NS. [후향 — 소표본]

8. **Laser retrieval is safe for e.max and zirconia but high-risk for highly-condensed lithium silicate.** El-Sheikh et al. (2024) debonded 0.5 mm occlusal veneers with an Er,Cr:YSGG laser in statistically equal time across materials (~87 s, p = 0.995), but e.max and Katana zirconia emerged 100% intact while GC Initial LiSi was 60% damaged (20% cracked, 40% fractured) — a retrieval/reuse caution specific to that crystal architecture. [근거강함 — in-vitro]

### 공백·미해결 (Open Questions)

- **최소두께 합의 부재**: 0.5mm(essam·al-akhali) vs 0.7/1.0mm(sasse) — 기질·재료·이악물기 층화된 임상 RCT 필요.
- **피로 vs 정적파절 vs 표면내구성 순위 상충**: maldonado(복합레진 피로 우위) vs al-akhali(세라믹 정적 우위) vs schlichting(복합레진 표면 열화). 어느 실패양식이 임상 지배인지 미해결.
- **근거 강도**: 임상 근거가 소표본 RCT(n=11)·case series(n=8)에 국한, SR 6편 전부 high/medium 편향 — long-term 대규모 비교시험 부재.
- **LiSi 이상치**: 고결정 LiSi의 레이저 취약성(el-sheikh)이 다른 재료비교에서 다뤄지지 않음.
