## 한국어 핵심요약

> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - 핵심 명제: 좁은 치조제(narrow ridge)의 수평 골증대에서 치조제 분할·확장술(ridge split/expansion)은 협·설측 피질판을 길이방향으로 갈라 벌리는 한 가족의 술식으로, 공여부 없이 약 3.3–3.7 mm 폭 증가와 임플란트 생존율 약 98–99%를 신뢰성 있게 달성한다(치조제 분할술(Alveolar Ridge Split, ARS) SR+MA Lin 2023: 폭 +3.348 mm, 생존 98.1%).
> - 세 가지 경쟁 술식의 골증대량 서열(Vorovenci 2024 SR+MA, 직접 비교): 골유도재생술(Guided Bone Regeneration, GBR) 4.04 mm > 치조제 분할술(Ridge Split, RS) 3.66 mm > 골밀도화 치조제확장(Osseodensification expansion, OD) 2.15 mm (ANOVA P=0.002), 단 생존율은 세 술식 모두 ~99%로 동등 → **선택 기준은 생존이 아니라 시작 골폭·필요 증대량·합병증·술자 숙련도**.
> - 골폭 기반 결정: GBR 적응 ≥3 mm, RS 적응 2.5–3 mm로 RS가 가장 좁은 치조제(평균 3.43 mm)에 쓰이고, OD는 역설적으로 가장 넓은 치조제(4.37 mm)·저밀도골(D3–D4)에 쓰인다 — OD의 낮은 폭 증대는 진짜 narrow ridge가 아니라 저밀도골이 본 적응증이기 때문(Vorovenci 2024).
> - 골이식 여부(횡적 치조제 확장술(Transverse Ridge Expansion, TRE) SR Abdallaoui 2025): ≥3 mm 치조제는 확장 단독으로 신뢰 가능, <3 mm 또는 장기 부피 보존이 목표일 때만 선택적 이식 — 이식의 추가 이득은 통계·임상적으로 미미(이식 4.5–6.1 mm vs 비이식 3.2–5.0 mm, 생존 동등).
> - 단계 결정(Khanum 2024 SR+MA): 1단계(one-stage, 분할+동시식립)가 2단계(two-stage)보다 우수(통합 표준화평균차(Standardized Mean Difference, SMD) ~0.89, one-stage 유리) — 단 11편 전부 중–고 비뚤림위험·이질성 불량·출판편향 가능성으로 근거 강도는 낮음(low-certainty).
> - 상악 vs 하악: 상악은 점탄성·낮은 피질밀도(D2–D4)로 분할이 더 예측 가능, 하악(D1–D2)은 두꺼운 피질로 녹색골절(greenstick fracture) 제어가 어려워 협측분절 완전골절(malfracture) 위험 → 하악 치밀골은 **2단계 또는 지연식립** 권장(Lin 2023).
> - 2단계 고전 해법(Enislidis 2006): 1차 협측 피질골절단(corticotomy)으로 골절선을 미리 정하고 40일 치유 후 분할, 협측분절을 골막 유경피판으로 유지 → 6개월 17개 임플란트 전부 안정·부하 성공(소규모 n=5, case-report 수준).
> - 기구 선택(Manekar 2022 SR+MA): 초음파/피에조(USG)가 골폭 증가 3.40 mm로 전동확장기(Motorized Ridge Expander, MRE) 2.83 mm보다 큼(p<0.0001)이나 생존은 동등(~98%) — USG는 골이동술(osteo-mobilization, 지연식립), MRE는 최소침습 골응축(osteo-condensation, 즉시식립)에 적합. GRADE는 very low.
> - OD 보조 분할(Guner 2025 후향, n=268): OD가 분할 맥락에서 상악·하악구치부 ISQ를 유의하게 높이고(전부 ISQ ≥65 달성) 골폭 증대는 동등 — 즉 OD는 폭을 더 늘리진 않아도 저밀도골 1차 안정성을 보강. 단 in-vitro(Chen 2022)는 OD 확장 이득이 좁은 골(6.75 mm)에서만 나타나고 탄성반발로 식립깊이가 얕아지는 trade-off를 보임.
> - 합병증·안전 경고: 주된 술중 위험은 협측판 골절(buccal plate fracture, 4–10%)·열개(dehiscence)이며 골폭 <3 mm에서 급증(Lin 2023); 과도한 OD 압축은 혈류 감소·발열로 임플란트 실패를 유발할 수 있음(Rizk 2024 RCT 3건 실패, López-Valverde 2025 인용). 임상근거 전반은 비무작위 위주·GRADE 낮음 → 동의서에 근거 수준 명시 권장.

## One-line Summary

Hub synthesis of 10 wiki pages (4 SR+MA, 1 qualitative SR, 1 SR+MA on staging, 2 classic case-series, 1 in-vitro bench, 1 retrospective) on alveolar ridge split / expansion for horizontal augmentation of the narrow ridge. Core thesis: ridge split reliably delivers ~3.3–3.7 mm width gain and ~98–99% implant survival without a donor site (Lin 2023); against its alternatives the bone-gain hierarchy is GBR 4.04 > RS 3.66 > OD 2.15 mm (Vorovenci 2024, P=0.002) but survival is technique-independent (~99%), so selection hinges on starting width (RS for the narrowest 2.5–3 mm crests, OD for low-density D3–D4), staging (one-stage favoured ~SMD 0.89 but low-certainty, Khanum 2024; two-stage for the dense mandible where greenstick fracture is uncontrollable), grafting (≥3 mm → expansion alone, <3 mm → selective graft, Abdallaoui 2025), and device (piezo/USG bigger gain, MRE enables immediate placement, Manekar 2022).

## Summary

This overview maps the decision space for **alveolar ridge split / expansion (RS)** — a family of techniques that longitudinally section a horizontally deficient alveolar crest and separate the buccal and lingual/palatal cortical plates to create width for an implant, without harvesting a donor block. The lineage runs from the classic chisel **split-crest** with deliberate greenstick fracture (Simion 1992) and its de-risked **staged mandibular** variant (Enislidis 2006), through modern **piezoelectric/motorized device** execution (Manekar 2022), to **osseodensification-assisted** expansion (Guner 2025, Chen 2022). Four quantitative syntheses anchor the numbers: a pooled estimate of RS itself (Lin 2023), a head-to-head ranking against GBR and OD (Vorovenci 2024), a narrow-ridge pooling of expansion/compaction/densification (López-Valverde 2025), and a graft-vs-no-graft threshold review (Abdallaoui 2025). One SR+MA addresses the staging question directly (Khanum 2024).

Five clinical questions structure the page:

1. **Is ridge split a reliable horizontal-augmentation technique at all?** — yes, pooled width gain and survival.
2. **Split vs GBR vs OD — which modality, when?** — bone-gain hierarchy vs survival equivalence.
3. **One-stage vs two-stage — when to place the implant?** — staging SR+MA + the mandible problem.
4. **Graft or no graft, and which device?** — width threshold + device-to-biology mapping.
5. **Maxilla vs mandible, and complication management.** — site-specific risk and how to mitigate it.

The unifying takeaway: across every modality, **implant survival is essentially technique-independent (~98–99%)**, so the decision is driven by *how much width you need, how narrow and how dense the crest is, which jaw, and operator skill* — not by survival.

---

## 1. Is ridge split reliable? — pooled outcomes

The single best estimate for RS in isolation is the Lin 2023 SR+MA (25 studies — 24 observational + 1 RCT): **pooled gained horizontal width 3.348 mm** (95% CI 2.533–4.163) and **pooled implant survival 98.1%** (95% CI 96.9–98.9), a survival comparable to implants in native unreconstructed bone. Lin 2023 also benchmarks RS (~3.63 mm) as yielding slightly less width than bone-block grafting (~4.25 mm) but with shorter treatment, lower morbidity, and the option of immediate implant placement. The Manekar 2022 device SR+MA independently corroborates the survival figure: **98.07%** across 1287 implants in 634 patients.

These numbers establish RS as a legitimate, donor-site-free horizontal augmentation method for narrow crests — the foundation the rest of the page builds decisions on.

| Outcome | Value | Source | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pooled horizontal width gain (RS) | **3.348 mm** (CI 2.533–4.163) | Lin 2023 SR+MA | individual range 1.6–5.3 mm |
| Pooled implant survival (RS) | **98.1%** (CI 96.9–98.9) | Lin 2023 SR+MA | range 93.9–100% |
| Overall survival (modern devices) | **98.07%** (1287 implants) | Manekar 2022 SR+MA | corroborates Lin |
| TRE width gain (mixed graft/no-graft) | 3.2–6.1 mm | Abdallaoui 2025 SR | grafted 4.5–6.1, non-grafted 3.2–5.0 |

## 2. Split vs GBR vs OD — the modality decision

The decisive head-to-head is Vorovenci 2024, the first SR+MA to pool osseodensification ridge expansion alongside GBR and RS (17 meta-analyzed studies → 21 groups; 336 patients / 665 implants). The bone-gain ranking is unambiguous, but its interpretation depends entirely on the *starting ridge width each modality is applied to*.

| Technique | Mean horizontal gain (95% CI) | Initial ridge width | Survival | Min-width indication |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **GBR** | **4.04 mm** (3.35–4.77) | 3.87 mm (intermediate) | 99.7% | ≥3 mm |
| **RS (ridge split)** | **3.66 mm** (2.99–4.40) | 3.43 mm (**narrowest**) | 98.7% | 2.5–3 mm |
| **OD (osseodensification)** | **2.15 mm** (1.33–2.98) | 4.37 mm (**widest**) | 98% | low-density D3–D4, not narrow per se |

Key reads (Vorovenci 2024):

- Bone-gain ANOVA **P=0.002**. GBR vs OD P=0.001; RS vs OD P=0.004; but **GBR vs RS P=0.09 (NS, they overlap)**.
- **Survival is technique-independent (~99% all three)** — so the decision moves onto gain need, width, complications, and skill, *not* survival.
- OD's lower gain (2.15 mm) is an artifact of indication: OD is applied to the *widest* crests (4.37 mm) because its real indication is **low-density bone**, not the truly narrow ridge. RS is the modality for the **thinnest** crests (3.43 mm).
- Graft moderator (P<0.001): autologous/mix 4.98 mm > synthetic 3.69 > xenogeneic 3.18 > allogeneic 2.73 > no-graft 2.47 mm.

López-Valverde 2025 pools expansion + compaction + densification together for the narrow (≤2.5 mm) crest and finds all three favour the experimental group for **bone density** (SMD −0.71, p=0.002, homogeneous/trustworthy), **crestal expansion** (SMD −1.12, p=0.04, but I²≥75%), and **ISQ** (SMD −8.88, p=0.0005, but I²=96%) — with the honest caveat that only the bone-density signal is solid; CE and ISQ are fragile (high heterogeneity + publication bias). It also flags the **clinical-vs-preclinical contradiction**: clinical pooling favours densification, yet animal models show no BIC/osseointegration gain, and Rizk 2024 (cited) attributed 3 implant failures to *over*-densifying a narrow crest (reduced blood supply, heat) — positioning these techniques behind GBR/blocks/crestal split when those are feasible.

**Decision rule (modality):**

```
Narrow ridge (need horizontal width)
├─ ridge ≥3 mm, want max bone / volume stability ──► GBR (4.04 mm)   [Vorovenci 2024]
├─ ridge 2.5–3 mm, narrowest crest ──────────────► RIDGE SPLIT (3.66 mm)
└─ ridge wider but LOW DENSITY (D3–D4) ──────────► OD expansion (2.15 mm; stability not width)
        (GBR ≈ RS on gain; choose by morbidity, donor avoidance, immediacy)
```

## 3. One-stage vs two-stage — the staging decision

Khanum 2024 is the first SR+MA to directly pool one-stage (split + implant in the same session) vs two-stage (expand first, place implant at a second surgery) ridge split. The pooled **standardized mean difference favours one-stage by ~0.89** (random-effects), so the authors conclude one-stage is superior on comparative analysis. **But the certainty is low**: all 11 included studies were moderate-to-high risk of bias, only 3 were meta-analysable, heterogeneity was poor, and funnel-plot asymmetry suggests publication bias.

The mandible is the principal exception that pulls toward **two-stage**. Lin 2023 spells out the difficulty gradient: the maxilla gains more width (thicker, more viscoelastic buccal wall; D2/D3/D4) and is more predictable, whereas the dense mandible (D1/D2) makes the greenstick fracture poorly controllable — so a delayed/two-stage approach is recommended in the mandible and whenever ridge width <3 mm. Enislidis 2006 is the canonical two-stage solution: a **first-stage buccal corticotomy predetermines the fracture line**, and after 40 days the ridge is split with the buccal segment kept **periosteally pedicled** (perfusion shifting internal→external) — 100% planned fractures and 100% 6-month implant stability/loading in 5 patients (17 implants), albeit case-report-level evidence.

| Factor | Favours ONE-STAGE | Favours TWO-STAGE |
|---|---|---|
| Pooled effect | one-stage superior, SMD ~0.89 (Khanum 2024, low-certainty) | — |
| Jaw / bone density | maxilla, D2–D4 (viscoelastic, predictable split — Lin 2023) | **dense mandible D1–D2** (greenstick uncontrollable, malfracture risk — Lin 2023, Enislidis 2006) |
| Ridge width | adequate residual width, sufficient primary stability achievable | **<3 mm**, thin/dense crest (Lin 2023) |
| Fracture control | thin maxillary cortex spreads as greenstick | corticotomy predetermines fracture line, periosteal pedicle preserves perfusion (Enislidis 2006) |
| Treatment time / surgeries | fewer (one surgery, immediate implant) | longer (two surgeries, ~40-day interval) |

## 4. Graft-or-not & device selection

**Graft threshold (Abdallaoui 2025, qualitative SR of 15 studies, baseline 2.5–4.5 mm).** The graft-or-not decision converts from clinician preference to a **width threshold**: ridges **≥3 mm respond reliably to expansion alone**; grafting is a **selective adjunct for ridges <3 mm or where long-term volume preservation matters**. Both branches widen the ridge predictably (3.2–6.1 mm) with survival 92–100% and *no significant between-group difference*; grafting buys a numerically larger gain (grafted 4.5–6.1 mm vs non-grafted 3.2–5.0 mm) and better volume stability, but the increment is modest and clinically non-significant. Within grafting, xenografts/alloplasts (Bio-Oss) are more dimensionally stable than autograft (which partly resorbs); this aligns with Vorovenci 2024's graft moderator (autologous gives the *largest* gain but at the cost of resorption).

**Device selection (Manekar 2022 SR+MA, 24 studies, 1287 implants).** Device choice follows the intended split biology:

| | Ultrasonic / Piezo (USG) | Motorized Ridge Expander (MRE) |
|---|---|---|
| Mean ridge-width gain | **3.40 mm** | **2.83 mm** (USG vs MRE p<0.0001) |
| Mean initial → final width | 3.76 → 7.13 mm | 3.78 → 5.92 mm |
| Implant survival | ~98.42% | ~98.97% |
| Split biology | osteo-mobilization (OM) — vertical+horizontal corticotomies | crestal-osteotomy + osteo-condensation (CO-OC), non-cutting drills |
| Implant timing | delayed | **immediate** (fewer stages) |
| Trade-off | larger gain, technique-sensitive, more buccal-fracture risk | minimally invasive, condenses bone, enables immediate placement |

GRADE certainty is **very low** (observational-dominant, 2 small RCTs, no direct modern-vs-conventional comparison) and the USG-vs-MRE gap is confounded by technique and implant timing — so read the 3.40-vs-2.83 mm difference as a tendency, not a hard rule.

**OD as the expansion driver.** Guner 2025 (retrospective, n=268 implants / 65 patients) is the first series of OD *in a ridge-split context*: OD significantly raised ISQ in the maxilla and mandibular posterior (all OD implants ≥65, the loading threshold; conventional dipped to 61–62) while producing **equivalent horizontal bone gain** — OD adds stability in low-density bone but no extra width, and adds *no* ISQ benefit in the dense mandibular anterior (D1–D2). Chen 2022 (in-vitro sawbone bench) isolates the expansion mechanism of reverse/counter-clockwise Densah drilling and shows the gain is **width-dependent — present only in narrow (6.75 mm) bone**, vanishing in wider bone, and at the cost of **shallower implant seating** from elastic rebound. Together they bound the OD-expansion claim: real but conditional (narrow + low-density), and not a universal width gain.

## 5. Maxilla vs mandible & complication management

**Site (Lin 2023, Manekar 2022):** the maxilla is more predictable — thicker, more viscoelastic buccal wall and lower cortical density (D2/D3/D4) allow a controlled greenstick spread, whereas the dense mandible (D1/D2) resists controlled fracture (favour two-stage / corticotomy / delayed placement). Abdallaoui 2025 agrees: TRE is more predictable in the maxilla than the mandible.

**Complication catalogue & rates:**

| Complication | Rate / signal | Source | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Buccal-plate fracture / "bad split" | 4–10%; **escalates sharply when width <3 mm** | Abdallaoui 2025, Lin 2023 | piezosurgery lowers complications & improves control; corticotomy in mandible; two-stage for <3 mm |
| Soft-tissue dehiscence | 3–8% (TRE); RS site dehiscence 12.3% | Abdallaoui 2025, Vorovenci 2024 | tension-free flap, membrane coverage |
| Paresthesia | 7.7% (RS) | Vorovenci 2024 | corticotomy planning, avoid nerve |
| Horizontal relapse (non-grafted) | minor <1 mm | Abdallaoui 2025 | graft if long-term volume preservation is the goal |
| Implant failure from over-densification | 3 failures attributed to over-densifying narrow crest (reduced blood supply, heat) | Rizk 2024 via López-Valverde 2025 | do not over-compact a thin crest; respect blood supply/heat |
| Membrane dehiscence/exposure (GBR comparator) | 20% (commonest GBR complication) | Vorovenci 2024 | (favours RS/OD when flap closure is risky) |

Piezosurgery is the recurring complication-reducer (Abdallaoui 2025 ranks it as lowering complications and improving expansion control vs rotary burs/chisels; Manekar 2022 maps it to the osteo-mobilization family). The dominant intraoperative risk across every synthesis is **buccal-plate fracture**, and the dominant risk modifier is **ridge width <3 mm** — the same threshold that triggers grafting and two-stage staging.

## 6. 원장 메모 체크리스트

- **첫 분기 = 골폭.** ≥3 mm면 확장 단독으로 충분(Abdallaoui 2025); <3 mm면 이식 + 2단계 고려(buccal fracture 위험 급증, Lin 2023).
- **술식 선택은 생존율로 하지 말 것** — 세 술식 모두 ~98–99%. 필요 증대량으로: 최대 골량 GBR, 가장 좁은 crest는 RS, 저밀도골은 OD(stability 보강·폭은 아님)(Vorovenci 2024, Guner 2025).
- **하악 치밀골(D1–D2)은 1단계 무리** — corticotomy 예비절단 + 2단계(Enislidis 2006), 또는 지연식립.
- **기구**: 즉시식립 원하면 MRE(골응축), 최대 폭·정밀 절골 원하면 피에조(USG) — 단 피에조는 협측골절 위험·지연식립 경향(Manekar 2022).
- **OD 과압축 금지** — 좁은 crest 과도 압축은 혈류·발열로 임플란트 실패 유발(Rizk 2024). OD는 저밀도골 1차 안정성용, narrow-ridge 폭 증대 술식 아님(Chen 2022은 좁은 골에서만·식립깊이 얕아짐).
- **근거 수준 전반 낮음**(비무작위 위주, GRADE very low~low, 출판편향 신호) — 환자 동의서에 명시 권장.
- OD 자체 의사결정은 [[overviews/osseodensification-clinical-applications]], 골증대 사다리는 [[overviews/bone-regeneration-protocol-ladder]] 참조.
