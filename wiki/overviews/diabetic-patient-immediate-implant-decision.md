> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - **바텀라인**: 당뇨(Diabetes Mellitus, DM)는 즉시식립(Immediate Implant Placement, IIP)의 절대 금기가 **아니다**. 그러나 "그냥 해도 됨"도 아니며, 결정은 **당화혈색소(HbA1c) 수준 × 부위(상악 vs 하악)** 두 축으로 갈린다.
> - **HbA1c 문턱값** (와그너 Wagner 2022, SR): **<8% = 정상군 수준으로 진행 / 8–9% = 강화 예방조치(enhanced precautions)로 진행 / ≥9% = 혈당 개선 후로 연기**. HbA1c는 필수 술전 선별검사.
> - **즉시부하(immediate loading) 생존율은 조절불량 당뇨에서도 비당뇨와 차이 없음** (안드라데 Andrade 2021, SR+MA): 상대위험도(Relative Risk, RR) 1.00, 조절불량군도 RR 1.08(비유의), 변연골소실(Marginal Bone Loss, MBL)도 통상부하와 차이 없음(MD −0.08mm).
> - **그러나 최대 규모 SR+MA가 이 낙관을 한정한다** (알-안사리 Al-Ansari 2022, 89편): 당뇨 임플란트 실패 교차비(Odds Ratio, OR) **1.777(p<0.001)**, 그리고 이 실패 신호는 **상악에서만 유의·하악은 비유의**. MBL은 +0.776mm이며 **시간이 갈수록 벌어진다**.
> - **핵심 충돌**: 두 SR+MA는 단기·하악 생존에서는 일치하지만, **상악·장기 위험에서 충돌**한다. Andrade는 부위·기간이 얕고(pooled당 ≤3편, 대개 ≤24개월, 조절불량군 I²=70%), Al-Ansari는 89편으로 두껍다 → **상악·장기 관점에선 Al-Ansari에 무게**.
> - **당뇨 유형**: 제1형(T1DM)이 제2형(T2DM)보다 훨씬 위험(OR 4.477). 대부분의 성인 케이스는 T2DM이라 상대적으로 저위험 쪽.
> - **즉시식립 술식 자체는 안전**: 구치부 IIP 생존율 ~97–98%로 지연식립과 동등 (라구치 Ragucci 2020, SR+MA). 단 **격중격(inter-radicular septum) 앵커·1차 안정성 확보가 전제**.
> - **부위가 이 결정의 진짜 리스크 축**: 상악 구치부(#16·#26 등) IIP는 (a) 당뇨 실패 신호가 몰린 상악 + (b) 저골질·상악동 인접이라 이중으로 취약 → 가장 방어적 조합은 **혈당 최적화 → 즉시식립 + 지연부하 + 강화 SPT**.
> - **하악 구치부**라면 당뇨 실패 신호가 비유의하므로 문턱이 낮아짐 — 8–9% gray-zone에서도 상대적으로 관대하게 진행 가능.
> - **임상 프로토콜**: 술전 HbA1c 확인 → 8–9%면 아목시실린(Amoxicillin) 2g 술전 + 클로르헥시딘(Chlorhexidine, CHX) 0.12% 2주 + osseointegration 시간 연장 + 지연부하; 매 리콜 MBL 추적(장기적으로 gap 확대); 주위염 지표(BOP/PD) 집중 관리.
> - **면책**: 의사결정 지원용 — 최종 임상 판단·책임은 술자에게 있다.

## One-line Summary
Cross-paper synthesis of when a diabetic patient can receive an immediate implant: DM is not an absolute contraindication, but the go/no-go is gated by **HbA1c band (<8% / 8–9% / ≥9%)** and **site (maxilla carries the significant DM failure signal; mandible does not)** — the two governing SR+MAs agree on short-term/mandibular survival but genuinely conflict on maxillary/long-term risk.

## 한줄요약
당뇨 환자 즉시식립 가부를 여러 논문으로 종합한 페이지: DM은 절대 금기가 아니며, 결정은 **HbA1c 밴드(<8%/8–9%/≥9%)** 와 **부위(상악은 당뇨 실패 유의, 하악은 비유의)** 로 갈린다 — 두 SR+MA는 단기·하악 생존에선 일치하나 상악·장기 위험에선 충돌한다.

## Summary

A diabetic patient presenting for immediate implant placement is not a single decision but the intersection of three questions the wiki answers separately: (1) *is diabetes a contraindication at all?*, (2) *does immediate loading make it worse?*, and (3) *does the immediate-placement technique itself hold up?* Reading them together produces a decision that is neither the reflexive "control glucose first, delay everything" nor the over-optimistic "HbA1c doesn't matter."

**The contradiction that shapes the answer.** Andrade 2021 (SR+MA, 5 quantitative studies) found **no survival penalty** for immediately *loaded* implants in type 2 DM — even in uncontrolled (high-HbA1c) patients (RR 1.08, 95% CI 0.87–1.33). Taken alone, this reads as "HbA1c is not a gate." But Al-Ansari 2022 — the largest meta-analysis (89 studies, 68,290 implants) — reports a real diabetic failure signal (OR 1.777, p<0.001) that is **significant in the maxilla and not in the mandible**, with MBL diverging over time (+0.776 mm, widening +0.032 mm/month). The two do not cleanly agree: Andrade's optimism is short-term (≤24 mo), immediate-loading-specific, and thin per estimate; Al-Ansari's caution is site-resolved and long-term. For a **maxillary** molar in a gray-zone patient, Al-Ansari's signal is the one that binds. This is why `al-ansari` now carries a `refines` edge onto `andrade` in the typed-relation graph.

**The HbA1c bands.** Wagner 2022 (SR) supplies the operational thresholds that neither SR+MA states as a rule: **<8%** proceed as normal, **8–9%** proceed with enhanced precautions (perioperative amoxicillin + CHX, delayed loading, extended osseointegration), **≥9%** defer until control improves. HbA1c correlates with peri-implant pathology (MBL/BOP/PD) more than with outright survival — so the risk shows up as bone loss and peri-implantitis over years, not as early failure.

**The technique is not the problem.** Ragucci 2020 (SR+MA) shows molar-socket IIP survives ~97–98%, equal to delayed placement, provided inter-radicular septum anchorage and primary stability are achieved. So the diabetic-patient decision is driven by the *host* (HbA1c) and the *site* (maxilla vs mandible), not by immediacy per se.

## Decision Ladder

```
Diabetic patient, considering immediate implant
│
├─ 1. HbA1c band? (Wagner 2022)
│     <8%  → proceed as standard
│     8–9% → proceed WITH enhanced precautions (AMX 2g + CHX 0.12% 2wk,
│            extended osseointegration, DELAYED loading)
│     ≥9%  → defer; optimize glycemia first
│
├─ 2. Site? (Al-Ansari 2022 — DM failure significant in MAXILLA only)
│     Mandibular posterior → DM signal not significant → lower threshold
│     Maxillary posterior  → DM signal significant + low bone quality
│                            + sinus proximity → most vulnerable combo
│
├─ 3. DM type? (Al-Ansari 2022)
│     T2DM → relatively lower risk
│     T1DM → OR 4.477 vs T2DM → more conservative
│
├─ 4. Immediate placement feasible? (Ragucci 2020)
│     Septum anchorage + primary stability achievable → IIP survival ~97–98%
│     If primary stability not achievable → convert to early/delayed
│
└─ 5. Loading (Andrade 2021 / Wagner 2022)
      Immediate loading NOT contraindicated by DM per se, BUT in the
      8–9% gray zone the safer default is DELAYED loading.
      Long-term: MBL widens in DM (Al-Ansari) → intensify SPT, track MBL each recall.
```

## Worked case (from the consult that produced this page)

68 y/o male, uncontrolled DM (HbA1c 8.2), #26 (maxillary first molar) immediate placement. HbA1c 8.2 sits in Wagner's **8–9% enhanced-precautions** band; #26 is **maxillary**, where Al-Ansari's DM failure signal is concentrated. Recommendation: **not contraindicated, but proceed conditionally** — optimize glycemia toward <8% (or run the enhanced-precautions protocol), place immediately if septum anchorage/primary stability allow, use **delayed loading**, and intensify SPT/MBL surveillance. Note the reversal: Andrade alone would say "just proceed"; the larger Al-Ansari SR+MA qualified that for the maxilla/long-term.

## Gray Zone / Open Questions

- **Andrade ↔ Al-Ansari** conflict is unresolved for the maxilla long-term: short follow-up (≤24 mo) in most immediate-loading data means the DM failure signal (which manifests late, via MBL) may be under-captured. Weight Al-Ansari for maxillary/long-term decisions.
- No wiki paper isolates **immediate placement + immediate loading + uncontrolled DM + maxilla** as a single stratum — the recommendation composes across pages rather than resting on one head-to-head trial.
- Whether tightening HbA1c *before* surgery changes hard outcomes (vs concurrent management) is not directly answered by these pages.
