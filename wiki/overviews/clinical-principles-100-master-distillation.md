> - 100개 원칙을 **19개 임상 도메인** — 임플란트 (식립·안정성·디자인·보철·주위조직), 골재생 (Bone Regeneration), 상악동거상술 (Sinus Lift), 근관치료 (Endodontics), 우식·수복·접착 (Caries/Restorative/Adhesion), 고정성보철·재료 (Fixed Prosth/Materials), 교합·가철성 (Occlusion/Removable), 치주 (Periodontics), 턱관절장애·이갈이·구강안면통증 (TMD/Bruxism/Orofacial Pain), 구강외과 (Oral Surgery), 약물·전신·마취 (Pharmacology/Systemic/Anesthesia), 구강내과 (Oral Medicine), 방사선 (Radiology), 교정 (Orthodontics), 행동·경영·근거 (Behavioral/Practice/Evidence) — 로 묶었다.
> - **가장 자주 쓰는 상위 규칙 10개**: ① 임플란트 식립 시기 (Type 1–4) 는 생존율 동등, 심미·변연골 (MBL) trade-off로 결정 [🟢]. ② 부하 결정은 **ISQ ≥70 (또는 4–6주 후 ≥65) + 삽입토크 ≥35 Ncm**, ISQ 단독 금지 [🟢]. ③ 생활치수요법 (VPT) 1차 재료는 바이오덴틴/MTA (Ca(OH)₂ 아님), 성공률 >93% [🟢]. ④ 술후 통증 1차 = 이부프로펜 400 + 아세트아미노펜 (APAP) 1000, NNT ~1.5, 오피오이드 회피 [🟢]. ⑤ 항응고제는 원칙적으로 중단 안 함 — INR ≤3.5면 와파린 유지, 단순발치는 DOAC 유지, bridging 금지 [🟢]. ⑥ 감염성심내막염 (IE) 예방적 항생제는 최고위험 심장군 4개만 [🟢]. ⑦ 조절된 당뇨 (HbA1c<7%) 는 임플란트 금기 아님 [🟢]. ⑧ 각화점막 (Keratinized Mucosa, KM) ≥2mm가 임플란트주위 건강 임계값 [🟢]. ⑨ 발치와는 미보존 시 6개월에 폭 ~3.79mm 소실 → 치조제보존술 (ARP) 은 감소시키되 제거하진 못함 [🟢]. ⑩ TMD는 보존적·비약물 우선, 비가역적 교합치료 최후 (CBT+biofeedback·도수치료가 최상위 근거) [🟢].
> - **근거등급 표기**: 🟢 강함 (SR+MA/일관 RCT) · 🟡 중간 (제한적 RCT/코호트) · 🟠 낮음 (case-dominated/이질적) · ⚪ 기전·합의 (mechanism/consensus, 임상종점 미확립). 등급은 종합 시점 기준이며, 새 근거로 갱신되는 living-document다.
> - **"덜 하는 게 낫다" 계열의 반직관 원칙**: 발치와 봉합·판막·미세술기는 대개 결과 동등 (저긴장 socket은 sutureless도 무방); 즉시 vs 다회 근관치료 차이 없음; 짧은·좁은 임플란트가 골증대+긴 임플란트와 동등하며 이환율 낮음; 교합지 (articulating paper) 는 힘·타이밍을 못 재므로 cusp-tip 마크 불신; 임플란트 보철 부적합 (misfit) 은 나사합병증은 유발하나 골소실 연관은 약함.
> - **자주 틀리는 진단·수치 게이트**: 치수진단은 reference standard가 없어 혈류 (vitality) 검사 > 감각 (sensibility) 검사; MB2는 "있다 가정하고" 적극 탐색 (상악 1대구치 47–79%); 근관장은 근단공 (AF) 이 아니라 근단협착부 (AC) 가 종점 (AC–AF 전치 0.85 / 구치 0.70mm) 이라 −0.5mm 규칙은 과소보정; 하악 사랑니 IAN 위험은 설측 관위치 (OR 4.96) 가 최고.
> - **재료 3대 접착 체인**: 지르코니아 = APC (air-abrade → MDP 프라이머 → 시멘트), 타액오염은 Ivoclean/재샌드블라스팅; 규산계 세라믹 = HF ≥3% → 실란+접착 → try-in 오염 시 Monobond E&P/HF 재부식 → 예열 복합레진; 상아질접착 내구성은 즉시 결합강도가 아니라 hybridization 품질·MMP 억제 (CHX) 가 좌우.
> - **과잉진료를 막는 원칙**: 무증상 NCCL·경도 대합치정출·항문동 슈도시스트·수복된 CTS 초기는 관찰 우선; 흑색착색 (black stain) 은 오히려 우식 보호적; 생체모방수복 (biomimetic) 프로토콜 중 근거 있는 건 심부변연거상 (DME) 뿐; 치주 보조제 (프로바이오틱스·aPDT·영양제) 는 모두 ~0.3mm CAL로 미미.
> - **영역별 "1차 선택" 빠른 참조**: 후방구치부 위축 상악 = 짧은 임플란트 ≥ 상악동거상; 미성숙 실활치 = 재생근관치료 (REP) > 근단형성술; 후방 부분피개 = 리튬디실리케이트 (e.max, 10년 ≥95%); 마모·침식 수복 = 초박형 교합면비니어 (table-top); 즉시식립 심미부 = 구개측 3D 포지셔닝 + gap graft + 얇은 biotype엔 CTG.

## Summary

The wiki holds 176 `wiki/overviews/` synthesis pages. No clinician re-reads 176 pages before a decision. This page is the **compression layer above the decision-ladder overviews** — it lifts each overview's load-bearing clinical thesis into a single actionable rule, then indexes it back to the source so the full conditions, exceptions, and numbers remain one click away.

**Scope and method.** These 100 principles are selected for *chairside decision value* — the rules you actually apply while treating a patient — not for corpus completeness. They are drawn verbatim in substance from the linked overviews (Rule #1: every claim is grounded in a paper we hold, surfaced through its overview). Domains are weighted by how often they drive a clinical decision, which is why implants, endodontics, restorative, and pharmacology carry the most principles. Meta/mechanism/economics overviews are cited where they change a chairside choice and otherwise left as background in their own pages.

**Evidence grades.** 🟢 strong (SR+MA / consistent RCT), 🟡 moderate (limited RCT / cohort), 🟠 limited (case-dominated / heterogeneous / in-vitro-only), ⚪ mechanism-or-consensus (biologically anchored or expert-agreed, clinical endpoint not established). Grades are as-of this synthesis and update as a living document — when a principle's linked overview is superseded, the grade moves with it.

**How to use.** Read the numbered rule to act; open the `→` linked overview when the case sits on a decision branch (thresholds, phenotype, comorbidity, timing). The rule is the compression; the overview is the expansion.

## A. Implant — Site, Timing & Immediate Placement

1. **Implant survival is equivalent across the ITI Type 1–4 timing spectrum (~97.4% immediate vs 97.5% delayed, RR 0.99)** — the decision turns on esthetic/MBL/complication/time trade-offs, not survival. Immediate preserves marginal bone better (molar MD −0.41 mm) but causes more early complications. 🟢 → [[immediate-vs-delayed-ridge-preservation-timing-overview]] · [[implant-placement-timing-immediate-early-delayed]]

2. **Immediate (Type 1) placement carries the highest esthetic risk**; three stacking risk factors predict midfacial recession — thin buccal wall (≤1 mm), buccal/mesial shoulder position, and thin biotype. 🟢 → [[immediate-implant-conditions]]

3. **Type 1A (immediate placement + immediate loading) demands the full selection bundle** — facial gap ≥2 mm, no endodontic infection, thick biotype, intact buccal plate (BBT ≥1 mm), primary stability ≥35 Ncm / ISQ ≥70, atraumatic extraction — under which survival ≈97%. 🟢 → [[type-1a-immediate-placement-immediate-loading-decision]]

4. **CBCT is mandatory before immediate placement** — most immediate-implant failure comes from skipped anatomic assessment (nerve encroachment, cortical perforation, buccal-plate loss), not poor regeneration. 🟢 → [[immediate-implant-anatomic-safety-overview]]

5. **The four immediate-implant surgical add-ons each independently improve tissue/esthetic outcomes** — flapless, immediate provisionalization, connective tissue graft (CTG), socket grafting — so stack them to the phenotype rather than pick one. 🟢 → [[cosyn-immediate-implant-4-bundle-decision]]

6. **At immediate implants, hard-tissue gap grafting and soft-tissue augmentation are separate decisions** — bone-substitute gap grafting cuts buccal resorption (~MD −0.5 mm); autogenous CTG is the strongest performer for buccal thickness/esthetics in thin phenotypes. 🟢 → [[immediate-implant-soft-tissue-gap-grafting-material-choice]] · [[immediate-implant-esthetic-soft-tissue-overview]]

7. **For a maxillary molar immediate implant with an inadequate inter-radicular septum, anchor in the palatal root socket** — it gives low-strain, PDL-derived osseointegration and avoids All-on-X cantilevers. 🟡 → [[maxillary-molar-palatal-root-socket-iip]]

8. **Socket shield preserves buccal bone and pink esthetics (BBPR ~0.32 vs 1.05 mm, PES +1.3) but is case-report-dominated with a 4–17% shield complication rate** — restrict to experienced operators. 🟠 → [[socket-shield-technique-overview]] · [[immediate-implant-decision-ladder]]

## B. Implant — Stability & Loading (ISQ)

9. **ISQ ≥70 at placement, or ≥65 after 4–6 weeks of healing, is the loadable threshold; <70 is risky — and ISQ is never a standalone metric** (pair it with insertion torque, bone type, protocol). 🟢 → [[implants-isq-stability-ladder]] · [[isq-loading-threshold]]

10. **Immediate loading requires ISQ ≥70 AND insertion torque ≥35 Ncm together.** 🟢 → [[isq-loading-threshold]]

11. **Crestal cortical thickness — not cancellous volume — is the dominant driver of primary stability and ISQ.** 🟢 → [[bone-quality-implant-risk-modification-overview]]

12. **When primary stability is adequate, the loading-protocol label (immediate / early / conventional) does not determine MBL or medium-term survival** — across single crowns, overdentures, and full-arch. 🟢 → [[implant-loading-protocol-prosthesis-type-overview]]

13. **Post-sinus-lift loading timing: two-stage lateral window reaches loadable ISQ (~70) at 3–6 months; transcrestal simultaneous placement permits 12-week early loading when insertion ISQ ≥70.** 🟡 → [[sinus-lift-isq-loading-timing]]

## C. Implant — Bone Quality, Design, Surface & Size

14. **Type IV bone fails ~1.5–1.9× more** (ISQ 58–65 vs 72–80 for type I); bone quality is a *risk axis*, modified by drilling protocol, macro-design, surface, and loading — not a procedure. 🟢 → [[bone-quality-implant-risk-modification-overview]]

15. **Peri-implant stress always concentrates at crestal cortical bone and rises as density falls (worst in D3/D4)** — the most optimizable lever is thread depth (deeper threads cut D3/D4 crestal stress ~40%). ⚪ → [[implant-design-bone-quality-stress-fea]] · [[implant-macrogeometry-clinical-outcomes-overview]]

16. **Surgical technique outweighs implant shape for early stability** — tapered raises insertion torque but not 6-week ISQ; neck/thread geometry matters most only in low-density bone. 🟡 → [[implant-macrogeometry-clinical-outcomes-overview]]

17. **SLA/SA is the surface standard (8-yr survival 94.8%); hydrophilic/bioactive surfaces (SLActive/CA) remove the D3/D4 stability dip rather than raising absolute ISQ.** 🟢 → [[implant-surface-comparison]] · [[osseointegration-foundations-implant-evidence-quality-overview]]

18. **UV photofunctionalization is a chairside, topography-preserving surface *re-activation*** — it reverses titanium aging (hydrocarbon removal → superhydrophilicity) for an early stability benefit. 🟡 → [[uv-photofunctionalization-dental-implants]]

19. **Cold/non-thermal plasma re-activates implant surfaces for two jobs — osseointegration boost and decontamination — with a small but partly significant early stability benefit** in the first human RCTs. 🟠 → [[plasma-surface-treatment-dental-implants]]

20. **Short implants (≤8 mm) equal longer implants on survival** — the real question is when short *stops* being better, governed by residual bone height, crown-to-implant ratio/occlusal load, and primary stability in poor bone, not by length. 🟢 → [[implant-length-selection-why-not-always-short]]

21. **Narrow-diameter implants (<3.75 mm) match regular-diameter on survival and MBL** — choose them to avoid bone augmentation, sometimes with better patient-reported/esthetic outcomes. 🟢 → [[narrow-diameter-implants-clinical-outcomes-overview]]

22. **Osseodensification's consistent benefit is raised insertion torque (plus autografting)** — use CCW Densah drilling in sub-antral augmentation, narrow ridge, D3/D4 bone, and immediate placement; vendor protocol cards are non-peer-reviewed, so anchor each to its evidence. 🟡 → [[osseodensification-clinical-applications]] · [[versah-protocol-selection-map]]

23. **Tissue-level implants lose 0.25–0.66 mm less marginal bone than bone-level in controlled studies** — advantage largest for short implants in posterior maxilla and thin-tissue sites. 🟡 → [[tissue-level-vs-bone-level-implant-outcomes]]

24. **Zirconia implants now show 10-yr CSR ~95% with low MBL** — residual failures concentrate in two-piece designs, narrow diameters, and discontinued products (execution/design, not the ceramic). 🟡 → [[zirconia-implant-clinical-outcomes]]

25. **Guided/robotic implant surgery (static/dynamic/robotic CAIS) beats freehand on accuracy — largest in immediate placement** (the empty socket pushes freehand drills off-axis); robotics reduce angular deviation, but the linear advantage is non-significant once the optical tracker is held constant. 🟡 → [[guided-robotic-accuracy-immediate-implant-overview]] · [[robotic-vs-navigation-implant-accuracy]]

26. **Flapless implant surgery is outcome-dependent, not a universal winner** — a 75% higher failure signal (RR 1.75) vanishes in low-risk-of-bias studies; weigh against soft-tissue/thermal factors. 🟡 → [[flapless-vs-flapped-implant-surgery-overview]]

## D. Implant — Prosthetics & Screw Joint

27. **Early implant failure (~1–4%, before loading) is multifactorial** (smoking, diabetes, radiation; posterior maxilla/type IV; immediate loading) — routine preoperative antibiotic prophylaxis is the modifiable lever with the clearest benefit. 🟡 → [[early-implant-failure-risk-prevention-overview]] · [[implants-clinical-decision-ladder]]

28. **Screw loosening — the most common mechanical complication (~10% at 5 yr, ~21% at 10 yr) — is driven by preload loss** (settling + cyclic fatigue); friction/thread roughness is the master lever, so retighten after settling. 🟡 → [[abutment-screw-preload-joint-stability-overview]]

29. **Emergence-profile shape and angle is the dominant driver of peri-implant mucosal stability** — a convex profile carries ~13× the recession risk of a concave one anteriorly; keep the buccal angle low (~32° halves molar recession). 🟢 → [[abutment-emergence-profile-peri-implant-tissue-overview]] · [[peri-implant-emergence-profile-soft-tissue-conditioning-overview]]

30. **Prosthesis marginal misfit reliably drives screw complications, but its link to bone loss is weak and biofilm-mediated** (each 0.1 mm gap → +0.08 mm bone loss; 150 µm tolerated ~19 yr). 🟡 → [[implant-prosthesis-misfit-connection-mbl-overview]]

31. **Retention choice: screw = retrievable, no residual cement, but needs an access hole; cement = esthetic + passive-fit tolerance, but residual-cement risk; SCRP hybrid is anatomically feasible only ~52–79% of maxillary anterior sites.** 🟡 → [[implant-prosthesis-retention-screw-vs-cement-overview]]

32. **Protect interproximal crestal bone with spacing: inter-implant distance ≥3 mm and implant-to-tooth ≥1.5 mm.** 🟡 → [[implant-spacing-proximity-crestal-bone-overview]]

33. **An osseointegrated implant has no PDL, so occlusal force is neither cushioned nor sensed — design to minimize and distribute load** (light occlusion; expect contacts to drift toward infraocclusion within 6–12 months). ⚪ → [[implant-occlusion-loading-biomechanics-overview]]

34. **Intentionally tilted implants (e.g. All-on-4) are clinically equivalent to axial in survival and short-term MBL** despite FEA showing off-axis stress rise — only a modest long-term MBL penalty. 🟡 → [[tilted-axial-implant-angled-abutment-overview]]

35. **Proximal contact loss between an implant crown and the adjacent tooth is frequent, time-progressive, and mesial-predominant** — surveillance and re-contouring (not prevention alone) is the realistic posture; tell the patient upfront. 🟡 → [[food-impaction-proximal-contact-loss-overview]]

## E. Peri-Implant Soft Tissue & Peri-Implantitis

36. **~2 mm keratinized mucosa width is the practical threshold** — inadequate KM is strongly associated with plaque, inflammation, and recession (OR 2.78–5.34); augment (FGG/CTG) below it. 🟢 → [[keratinized-mucosa-peri-implant-health-overview]]

37. **Peri-implant soft-tissue dehiscence averages 46% even in disease-free sites — it is expected progression, not an exceptional complication** — manage in three stages: pre-placement risk assessment → surgical technique → maintenance. 🟢 → [[peri-implant-soft-tissue-dehiscence-prevention]]

38. **Peri-implantitis prevalence ~19.5% patient-level; peri-mucositis management is primary prevention; no single decontamination protocol is superior.** 🟢 → [[peri-implantitis-management-overview]]

39. **Prosthetic design measurably lowers MBL/peri-implantitis** — platform switching (MBL −0.26 to −0.49 mm), conical connection, abutment height ≥2 mm, one-abutment-one-time; conversely emergence angle >30° + convex profile raises risk. 🟢 → [[peri-implantitis-management-overview]] · [[implants-clinical-decision-ladder]]

40. **Supportive peri-implant therapy works only as individualized risk-based recall (3–12 mo, computed from probing/%BoP/radiographs) — not a fixed annual schedule** — attenuating disease (PD −1.0 to −1.5 mm, 40–70% lower peri-implantitis). 🟡 → [[supportive-peri-implant-therapy-maintenance-overview]]

## F. Bone Regeneration — ARP / GBR / Ridge

41. **Untreated sockets lose ≈3.79 mm width (29–63%) and ≈1.2–1.7 mm buccal height at 6 months** — the baseline that justifies alveolar ridge preservation (ARP). 🟢 → [[bone-regeneration-protocol-ladder]] · [[socket-preservation-arp-overview]]

42. **ARP reduces but never eliminates ridge loss, and it is scenario-dependent, not universal** — an intact-wall socket with adequate volume may not need grafting. 🟢 → [[bone-regeneration-socket-biology-and-arp-critique]]

43. **In ARP the graft + open-healing protocol and site anatomy drive bone dimensions; micro-technique is largely equivalent — but flap elevation costs ~3.2 mm keratinized width vs flapless.** 🟡 → [[open-healing-arp-technique-variables-overview]]

44. **GBR membrane exposure is the dominant failure mode, governed more by flap/soft-tissue management than membrane brand** — non-cross-linked resorbables tolerate exposure better than cross-linked. 🟡 → [[gbr-barrier-membrane-exposure-axis]] · [[gbr-barrier-membrane-overview]]

45. **DBBM is an indispensable osteoconductive scaffold with a slow-resorption trade-off — BCP hybrids and PRF adjuncts rescue the new-bone gap.** 🟡 → [[dbbm-bone-substitute-overview]]

46. **Vertical ridge augmentation fails through barrier exposure / soft-tissue dehiscence, not bone regeneration per se** — mesh exposure is the dominant failure mode; and ridge split delivers ~3.3–3.7 mm width gain at ~98–99% survival without a donor site. 🟡 → [[vertical-ridge-augmentation-overview]] · [[ridge-split-expansion-technique-selection-overview]]

47. **Wound closure splits by tension: low-tension sockets are outcome-agnostic ("less is more," sutureless acceptable); high-tension GBR sites — the closure *is* the result** (coronal advancement / periosteal release for tension-free primary closure). 🟢 → [[suture-wound-closure-decision-ladder]]

## G. Sinus Lift

48. **Choose sinus technique by residual bone height (RBH): ≥5 mm → transcrestal first; <5 mm → lateral window (extendable to ~4 mm with osseodensification).** 🟡 → [[sinus-lift-technique-selection]]

49. **Within transcrestal approaches, osseodensification beats osteotome on ISQ and complications.** 🟡 → [[sinus-lift-technique-selection]]

50. **Short implants (4–8 mm, no lift) match sinus augmentation on survival with less MBL and lower morbidity** — decide on RBH, crown-to-implant ratio, and patient experience, not survival. 🟢 → [[short-implant-vs-sinus-augmentation-decision]]

51. **A competently repaired Schneiderian membrane perforation is not a contraindication to implant success.** 🟡 → [[sinus-lift-lateral-2026-synthesis]]

52. **An antral pseudocyst / mucous retention cyst is not an absolute contraindication to sinus elevation** — no proven clinically significant risk; work a 4-step decision tree (diagnosis → risk → treatment → placement). 🟠 → [[sinus-lift-pseudocyst-mrc-overview]]

## H. Endodontics

53. **Vital pulp therapy first-line materials are Biodentine or MTA (OR 4.41 and 3.02 vs Ca(OH)₂), not calcium hydroxide; overall success >93% — the critical gate is diagnosing reversible vs irreversible pulpitis.** 🟢 → [[vital-pulp-therapy-decision-ladder]] · [[endodontics-comprehensive-ladder]]

54. **Pulp diagnosis has no reference standard — blood-flow (vitality) tests outperform sensibility tests, in adults and children;** undiagnosed necrosis ends as a periapical lesion. 🟡 → [[endodontic-pulp-periapical-diagnosis-overview]]

55. **For immature non-vital permanent teeth, regenerative endodontics (REP) is preferred over apexification** — equivalent survival (~97%) but superior root elongation and dentin-wall thickening; do not use an LPRF scaffold. 🟢 → [[regenerative-endodontics-rep-overview]]

56. **The EAL "0" reading marks the apical foramen, but the obturation endpoint is the apical constriction — subtract (AC–AF ≈0.85 mm anterior / 0.70 mm posterior), so the common −0.5 mm rule under-corrects.** 🟡 → [[eal-working-length-protocol]]

57. **Normal anatomy → traditional straight-line access is the standard; minimally invasive access lacks clinical RCT support** (more residual debris, missed canals, perforation) and cannot yet be recommended as default. 🟡 → [[endodontic-access-cavity-decision-tree]]

58. **Assume the MB2 canal is present and actively hunt it** — maxillary first-molar prevalence ~47–79%; detection sensitivity rises stepwise from periapical radiograph → magnification → CBCT/troughing. 🟢 → [[mb2-detection-strategy]] · [[root-canal-morphology-prevalence-overview]]

59. **C-shaped canals follow a steep tooth-type gradient (mandibular 2nd molar 17.3% highest), are Asian-predominant, and show female predominance** — anticipate them in the East-Asian mandibular second molar. 🟢 → [[c-shaped-canal-anatomy-prevalence-overview]]

60. **Instruments leave 10–80% of canal walls untouched — that is why irrigant activation matters at all; every activation method beats needle irrigation, and PUI carries the strongest clinical evidence.** 🟢 → [[irrigation-activation-comparison]] · [[endodontic-endotoxin-lps-lta-overview]]

61. **Post-endodontic pain is dominated by preoperative symptomatic status — not instrument kinematics or working-length method** — it peaks 6–24 h and resolves by 7–14 d across techniques. 🟢 → [[endodontic-postoperative-pain-overview]]

62. **Single- vs multiple-visit root canal treatment gives no clinically significant difference in healing or pain — visit count is a logistics/case decision.** 🟢 → [[single-vs-multivisit-endodontic-outcomes-overview]]

63. **Rotary pathfinders beat manual K-files on glide-path transportation; low-taper files (TruNatomy) best preserve curvature; autoclaving does not meaningfully harm NiTi fatigue.** 🟠 → [[canal-shaping-glidepath-niti-fatigue-overview]]

64. **A premixed calcium-silicate bioceramic sealer (Ceraseal) matches the AH Plus epoxy gold standard on healing/survival, with less post-obturation pain and full retrievability.** 🟡 → [[ceraseal-bioceramic-sealer-clinical-material-synthesis]]

## I. Caries, Restorative & Adhesion

65. **Caries is a reversible biofilm–acid disease — arrest active non-cavitated lesions chemically without drilling** (SDF and sealant+fluoride rank most effective; SDF outperforms fluoride varnish for early childhood caries). 🟢 → [[non-restorative-caries-management-topical-fluoride-synthesis]]

66. **Detection hierarchy: DIAGNOdent Pen most sensitive (0.81–0.89), occlusal radiography near-useless (0–0.29), ICDAS the clinical standard; resin infiltration arrests white-spot lesions.** 🟢 → [[caries-detection-remineralization-overview]]

67. **Bond durability is governed mostly by hybridization quality, not immediate bond strength — when enamel margins exist, selective enamel etching or 3-step etch-and-rinse beats universal self-etch.** 🟢 → [[adhesive-bonding-strategies-overview]] · [[direct-resin-restoration-adhesion-placement-overview]]

68. **Resin-dentin bonds degrade via hydrolysis + MMP/cathepsin collagenolysis — durability levers are ethanol-wet bonding, MMP inhibition (CHX), MDP nanolayering, and biomimetic remineralization.** 🟡 → [[resin-dentin-bond-durability-degradation-overview]]

69. **Polymerization shrinkage stress is the root cause of marginal failure — attack it via monomer chemistry, cure strategy, and fiber reinforcement** (bulk-fill is acceptable within its depth limits). ⚪ → [[resin-polymerization-shrinkage-stress-overview]]

70. **Immediate dentin sealing has strong, consistent in-vitro bond benefit but contested clinical outcomes (postoperative sensitivity/survival)** — reasonable but not proven. 🟡 → [[immediate-dentin-sealing-evidence-synthesis]]

71. **NCCLs are multifactorial (stress + friction + biocorrosion) and abfraction-as-sole-cause is unproven — monitor asymptomatic lesions, and when restoring, retention depends on the etch/adhesive step (skipping adhesive = failure).** 🟡 → [[nccl-etiology-diagnosis-management-overview]]

72. **Of the biomimetic-restorative protocols, only deep margin elevation is evidence-supported** — caries-detector dyes, aluminum-oxide air abrasion, posterior enamel beveling, and "elected best" adhesives lack demonstrated superiority. 🟡 → [[reis-2024-biomimetic-restorative-myths-narrative]]

73. **Glass ionomer earns its place in specific restorative/preventive/bioactive indications (real fluoride release and bioactivity) but its bulk mechanical limits cap load-bearing use.** 🟡 → [[glass-ionomer-clinical-decision-overview]]

74. **Dentin hypersensitivity follows the Brännström hydrodynamic mechanism — ladder from at-home desensitizing toothpaste to in-office occluding agents/laser;** dental erosion is acid-driven (titratable acidity/contact time/Ca-PO₄-F saturation, not pH alone), so prevention precedes restoration. 🟡 → [[dentin-hypersensitivity-overview]] · [[dental-erosion-etiology-prevention-synthesis]]

## J. Fixed Prosthodontics & Materials

75. **Monolithic lithium disilicate gives the highest 5-yr single-crown survival (~98.5%) and ≥95% 10-yr inlay/onlay survival — first-line for single crowns and posterior partial coverage.** 🟢 → [[prosthetic-materials-decision-ladder]] · [[lithium-disilicate-inlay-onlay-evidence]]

76. **Zirconia bonding = APC (air-abrade → MDP primer → cement); clean saliva contamination with Ivoclean or re-air-abrasion — never HF/silane alone.** 🟡 → [[dental-materials-decision-ladder]] · [[zirconia-material-clinical-overview]]

77. **Zirconia grade selection is a strength–translucency trade-off: higher yttria = more translucent but weaker (3Y ~1200 MPa for posterior FPDs → 5Y/UHTZ ~300–500 MPa for anterior/monolithic esthetics).** 🟡 → [[zirconia-types-clinical-selection]]

78. **Crown preparation: target total occlusal convergence ~10–20° (clinical preps run higher), and it is resistance form — not retention alone — that governs fracture resistance.** 🟠 → [[crown-preparation-geometry-convergence-resistance-retention]]

79. **Ceramic (feldspathic/lithium-disilicate) veneers show high survival — design incisal coverage, use a minimally invasive mock-up/APT-guided "preparation by design," and cement with HF + silane.** 🟢 → [[veneer-material-survival-protocol-overview]] · [[veneer-preparation-design-minimally-invasive-overview]]

80. **Silica-based ceramic bonding is a chain: HF ≥3% (≥10% feldspathic) → prehydrolyzed silane + adhesive → Monobond Etch&Prime or HF re-etch after try-in contamination → preheated composite as the strongest luting agent.** 🟠 → [[ceramic-bonding-hf-silane-contamination-overview]]

81. **Post-and-core: a post improves survival vs no-post only when coronal tissue is lost, and a circumferential ferrule is the strongest modifiable predictor (RR 1.28) — outweighing post material (glass-fiber ≈ metal).** 🟢 → [[post-and-core-fiber-post-decision-overview]]

82. **Restorative margins must respect the ~2 mm supracrestal attachment (biologic width); supragingival is safest, subgingival tolerable only under excellent hygiene — misfit/overhang drives periodontal breakdown.** 🟡 → [[restorative-margin-periodontal-interface-overview]]

## K. Occlusion & Removable Prosthodontics

83. **No single method reliably sets complete-denture OVD — cross-check several (rest position + freeway space + phonetics + esthetics); digital ≈ conventional fabrication, and Gothic-arch tracing is more reproducible/accurate than nick-and-notch for centric relation.** 🟡 → [[complete-denture-ovd-determination-overview]] · [[complete-denture-digital-overdenture-overview]] · [[gothic-arch-jaw-relation-recording-overview]]

84. **CR/CO vs MIP: restore full-mouth cases in a reproducible CR/CO but accept a healthy existing MIP for limited work — the CR–MIP slide is normally <2 mm.** 🟡 → [[cr-co-micp-reference-position-debate]]

85. **Articulating paper is only moderately valid (~81% true positive, ~15% false positive) and cannot quantify force or timing — improve accuracy with 200 µm paper, the mandibular arch, both-arch coincidence, and distrust of cusp-tip marks.** 🟡 → [[occlusal-contact-detection-articulating-paper-overview]]

86. **Unopposed posterior teeth over-erupt in ~83% of cases (mean ~0.43 mm over ~9 months, mostly <1 mm) independent of pulp vitality — restore the opposing space or monitor, but ~18% never move.** 🟡 → [[unopposed-tooth-overeruption-overview]]

## L. Periodontics

87. **Every periodontal diagnosis starts from 2017 World Workshop Staging I–IV × Grading A–C, then treats: SRP ± adjunct → surgery/regeneration (GTR/EMD) → risk-based supportive periodontal therapy.** 🟢 → [[periodontics-treatment-ladder]]

88. **Mouthrinses are adjuncts — never substitutes — to mechanical hygiene: they reduce plaque/gingivitis but not pocket depth or attachment, and essential oils rank at least as high as chlorhexidine for gingivitis.** 🟢 → [[antiseptic-mouthrinse-chlorhexidine-essential-oil-overview]]

89. **Interdental cleaning has no universally superior device — adherence matters more than device choice; the interdental brush has the strongest single-device evidence where embrasures allow it.** 🟢 → [[interdental-cleaning-devices-synthesis]] · [[watanabe-toothpick-method-toothbrushing-synthesis]]

90. **All periodontal adjuncts (probiotics, aPDT, host-modulation/nutraceuticals) add only ~0.3 mm CAL — sub-millimeter, low-certainty, none dominant** — set expectations accordingly. 🟡 → [[periodontal-adjunctive-therapy-probiotics-pdt-overview]] · [[periodontal-host-modulation-nutraceutical-adjuncts-overview]]

91. **Guided biofilm therapy / air polishing delivers more comfort and least hard-tissue abrasion but is clinically equivalent — not superior — to SRP/PMPR on hard endpoints;** and occlusal trauma does not initiate periodontitis (it only modifies an already-inflamed periodontium — splinting won't save mobile teeth). 🟡 → [[professional-biofilm-management-gbt-air-polishing-overview]] · [[occlusal-trauma-periodontitis-overview]]

## M. TMD, Bruxism & Orofacial Pain

92. **TMD: conservative, non-pharmacological care first and irreversible occlusal therapy last — highest-certainty treatments are CBT+biofeedback, therapist-assisted jaw mobilization, and manual trigger-point therapy (Yao 2023 BMJ NMA, 233 RCTs); splints equal or trail exercise.** 🟢 → [[tmd-management-evidence-ladder]] · [[conservative-physical-therapy-myofascial-tmd-ladder]]

93. **Bruxism is a shared upstream driver — excess masticatory-muscle force discharges into whichever structure is weakest (TMJ, tooth/restoration, or implant); a splint buffers force downstream while occlusal adjustment is weak/unsupported for TMD.** ⚪ → [[bruxism-muscle-overload-axis]]

94. **BoNT-A efficacy varies sharply by indication — strong for masseter hypertrophy/myogenous pain, weaker/uncertain elsewhere — so it is not a blanket "safe cosmetic procedure."** 🟡 → [[btx-orofacial-applications-overview]]

95. **Primary BMS and trigeminal neuropathic pain are disinhibition (NKCC1/KCC2) + small-fiber neuropathy phenomena, not psychogenic-only — trigeminal neuralgia is first-line carbamazepine/oxcarbazepine (MVD surgery), and endodontics accounts for ~8% of iatrogenic trigeminal nerve injuries.** ⚪ → [[bms-orofacial-pain-neuropathic-mechanism-overview]] · [[trigeminal-neuralgia-neuropathy-overview]]

96. **PDRN's analgesic/anti-inflammatory effect has the strongest human support, while its regenerative effect remains animal/in-vitro — and spray-and-stretch gives immediate but short-lived masticatory relief on thin evidence.** 🟠 → [[pdrn-dentistry-evidence-synthesis]] · [[spray-and-stretch-tmd-myofascial-synthesis]]

## N. Oral Surgery & Third Molar

97. **Extract mandibular third molars for absolute indications (recurrent pericoronitis, cyst, adjacent resorption) — impaction carries a real pathology burden (pericoronitis 82%, distal caries of the 2nd molar 19%), while asymptomatic teeth may be retained.** 🟡 → [[oral-surgery-decision-ladder]] · [[mandibular-third-molar-management-overview]]

98. **Lower-third-molar IAN protocol: any positive Rood & Shehab sign → CBCT; a lingual canal position carries the highest injury risk (OR 4.96) → coronectomy for high-risk anatomy plus risk factors.** 🟡 → [[third-molar-ian-risk-protocol]]

99. **Odontogenic maxillary sinusitis is a treat-the-source disease — under-recognized (30–40%+ of chronic/unilateral maxillary sinusitis), increasingly iatrogenic (implants/extractions ≈ half), resolving 90–100% only when the dental source is controlled (dual confirmation: dental exam + CBCT).** 🟡 → [[odontogenic-maxillary-sinusitis-overview]]

100. **Baseline blood pressure dominates intraoperative hemodynamics — hypertensives run ~30 mmHg higher SBP and a 7× hemorrhage rate — yet stable hypertension does not raise long-term implant failure (OR 1.10, NS).** 🟡 → [[perioperative-hypertension-hemodynamic-management-overview]]

## Appendix — High-frequency principles beyond the core 100

The 100 above are weighted toward implant/endo/restorative decisions. These pharmacology, oral-medicine, radiology, orthodontic, and practice principles are used just as often and belong on the same shelf — indexed here rather than dropped, each still one click from its overview.

**Pharmacology / systemic / anesthesia**
- Postoperative pain first-line = **ibuprofen 400 mg + acetaminophen 1000 mg (NNT ~1.5); opioids are not superior — avoid; alternate dosing beats concurrent.** 🟢 → [[drug-analgesics-postop-pain-overview]]
- **Restrictive antibiotic prescribing** — IE prophylaxis limited to the 4 highest-risk cardiac groups; simple extractions in intact dentition need none; single perioperative doses suffice where indicated. 🟢 → [[drug-antibiotic-stewardship-overview]]
- **Do not routinely interrupt anticoagulants/antiplatelets** — continue VKA when INR ≤3.5, continue DOACs for simple extractions, no bridging (thromboembolic risk > dental bleeding risk). 🟢 → [[drug-anticoagulant-antiplatelet-perioperative-overview]]
- **MRONJ prevention starts with dental clearance BEFORE antiresorptive therapy;** once started, avoid extractions, use minimally invasive technique + prophylaxis + follow-up (denosumab risk is cumulative-dose driven). 🟡 → [[drug-mronj-antiresorptive-overview]]
- **Controlled diabetes (HbA1c <7%) is not an implant contraindication; 1:200,000 epinephrine matches 1:100,000** with less cardiovascular load — route drug decisions by the chair decision point, not drug class. 🟢 → [[drug-systemic-disease-dental-management-overview]] · [[drug-clinical-decision-ladder]]
- **4% articaine buccal infiltration ≈ lidocaine IANB for mandibular molars; supplementary/combined injections each roughly double success after IANB failure; buffering speeds onset and raises efficacy in pulpitis.** 🟢 → [[mandibular-anesthesia-efficacy-ladder]] · [[buffered-modified-local-anesthetics-overview]]
- **Topical anesthetic reduces needle pain vs placebo (agent differences small — technique/contact matter more); computerized/needle-free delivery gives no injection-pain advantage but reduces fear/anxiety and aids pediatric cooperation.** 🟡 → [[topical-anesthetic-injection-pain-overview]] · [[computerized-needle-free-anesthesia-delivery-overview]]
- **Autoimmune patients can be treated successfully when disease is controlled** (implant survival ≈ general population); Korea has a moderate-sedation CPG but no standardized local-anesthesia guideline yet. 🟡 → [[autoimmune-disease-dental-treatment-overview]] · [[drug-anesthesia-sedation-korean-guidelines-overview]]

**Oral medicine / mucosa**
- **Stratify oral-medicine lesions (OPMD monitoring, OLP, BMS, RAS, OMS) rather than treat generically.** 🟡 → [[oral-medicine-decision-ladder]]
- **Cancer-therapy oral mucositis: topical honey is the only meta-supported agent (LLLT has no pooled pediatric benefit) — match agent to the target outcome.** 🟡 → [[oral-mucositis-cancer-therapy-overview]]
- **Halitosis is ~32% prevalent and 80–90% intra-oral (coated tongue, periodontitis); probiotics/PDT give short-term benefit but never replace mechanical hygiene.** 🟡 → [[halitosis-etiology-measurement-management-overview]]
- **Caries and periodontitis are community dysbiosis (EPS matrix, keystone P. gingivalis, PSD model), not single-pathogen infections; black stain is inversely associated with caries — reassure, don't over-treat.** ⚪ → [[oral-microbiome-biofilm-dysbiosis-synthesis]] · [[black-stain-caries-protection-overview]]

**Radiology**
- **CBCT changes the endodontic plan in 24–56% of cases (78%/79% sensitivity/specificity for root resorption) — indication-specific FOV/voxel selection is the optimization lever.** 🟡 → [[cbct-diagnostic-performance-overview]]
- **Dose hierarchy is fixed (intraoral ~1.3 ≪ panoramic ~18 ≪ CBCT ~121 µSv) with four operator levers — FOV/collimation, kVp/mAs, voxel size, beam height; contextualize for consent (panoramic ≈ 2 days background, CBCT 3.6–46 days).** 🟢 → [[dental-imaging-radiation-dose-optimization-overview]] · [[dental-imaging-effective-dose-background-equivalent-overview]]

**Orthodontics**
- **TAD/miniscrew failure is ~13.5% (1 in 6–7); smoking and gingival tissue type are the only consistent risks, and site dominates (midpalatal 1.3% vs zygomatic buttress 16.4%).** 🟢 → [[miniscrew-stability-overview]]
- **Clear aligners equal fixed appliances for mild–moderate cases but are inferior/unpredictable for torque/rotation/extrusion and complex/extraction cases; auxiliaries (attachments, TADs, IPR) extend the envelope.** 🟢 → [[clear-aligner-indications-limitations]]
- **Myofunctional/elastodontic appliances work interceptively in growing/mixed dentition (Class II, transverse, open-bite, modest airway); orthodontic tooth movement needs light/intermittent force to avoid hyalinization/root resorption (NSAIDs and bisphosphonates impair it).** 🟡 → [[myofunctional-elastodontic-orthodontics-overview]] · [[orthodontic-tooth-movement-biology]]

**Behavioral, practice & evidence**
- **Behavioral factors decide whether clinical work lands — motivational interviewing drives behavior change and anxiety gates attendance; structure every consultation as open → gather → explain → decide → align expectations → close.** 🟡 → [[behavioral-dentistry-overview]] · [[behavioral-dentistry-decision-ladder]] · [[patient-consultation-communication-protocol]]
- **No-shows are predictable (42–52%, driven by forgetfulness/scheduling, not cost) — use risk-flagging + reminders; manage complaints as a pipeline (classify → expectation → non-defensive response → staff training).** 🟡 → [[dental-appointment-no-show-overview]] · [[complaint-management-pipeline-classification-expectation-response-education]]
- **Read SR+MA critically (effect measures, test selection, common errors) — most of the wiki's 800+ papers are SR+MA/RCT/cohort, so appraisal is the operational backbone.** 🟢 → [[evidence-appraisal-toolkit]] · [[statistical-abbreviations-cheatsheet]]
- **Oral frailty is a measurable, reversible geriatric syndrome — SDF 38% arrests root caries and professionally applied fluoride is the key lever; and cracked-tooth is the incomplete-fracture entry in the 5-type longitudinal-fracture spectrum (ESE: abandon the "CTS" label).** 🟡 → [[oral-frailty-geriatric-synthesis]] · [[cracked-tooth-syndrome-overview]]
- **Dental trauma: mouthguards prevent; store avulsed teeth in HBSS/propolis/milk (never saline/tap water); flexible short-term splinting; pulpal prognosis tracks apex maturity.** 🟢 → [[dental-trauma-management-overview]]
- **Digital-workflow reality: IOS is the standard for single crowns/short spans but degrades on full-arch; CAIS beats freehand for immediate/esthetic; quality-appraise AI (AMSTAR-2/QUADAS-2) and teledentistry (adequate for screening, patient acceptability is the binding constraint) before trusting the claim.** 🟡 → [[digital-workflow-decision-ladder]] · [[ai-dentistry-reviews-2024-2025-synthesis]] · [[teledentistry-overview]]
