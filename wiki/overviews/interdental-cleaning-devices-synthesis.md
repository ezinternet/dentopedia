## 한국어 핵심요약

> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - 핵심: 칫솔질만으로 닿지 않는 치간(interdental) 청소도구 4종 — 치실(Dental Floss, DF)·치간칫솔(Interdental Brush, IDB)·구강세정기/워터픽(Water Flosser/Oral Irrigator, WF/OI)·토스픽법(Watanabe Toothpick Method) — 을 위키 8편 + 토스픽법 overview로 종합.
> - **단일 보편 우승 도구는 없다** — 가장 큰 변수는 도구 자체가 아니라 **순응도(adherence)**. 환자가 실제로 매일 쓰는 도구가 최선.
> - 치간칫솔(IDB): 공간이 허용하면 **1순위**. 임신 치은염서 BOP 56%→12%(−79.6%, 출혈소실 최강 예측인자 OR 3.14, Carrouel 2026)로 가장 강한 단일-도구 근거.
> - 치실(DF): 치간칫솔이 **안 들어가는 좁은/정상 접촉**에만 한정 권장. 정유 에어플로스와 동등(Thomassen 2025), 구강세정기와도 차이 없음(Yiamwattana 2025) — DF 특유의 우월 근거는 약함, 순응도 낮음.
> - **올바른 술식조차 치실 효능을 살리지 못한다(Jung 2025, 전향 n=37)**: 동영상 교육으로 치실 술식(Flossing Performance Score, FPS 2.0→2.83, p<.001)은 향상됐으나 치태 제거(PSPI 차이 0.17 vs 0.21, p=.112)는 개선되지 않았고 술식과 무관 — "치실은 기술만 가르치면 된다"는 통념을 반박, DF를 좁은 접촉 한정으로 두는 결정을 보강.
> - **순응도(adherence)가 효능보다 중요하다는 직접 근거(Yilmaz 2025, RCT n=54, 24주)**: 고무 치간 픽(rubber interdental pick)을 규칙적으로 쓴 주수 12.61주 vs 치실 4.96주(p=0.003); 양군 모두 규칙적 사용 주수와 플라크·출혈이 음의 상관 — 가장 오래 쓰는 도구가 이긴다. 고무/실리콘 치간 픽이라는 도구 계열 추가.
> - **환경영향(sustainability)이라는 새 결정 축(Abed 2022, 전과정평가 LCA)**: 일회용 플로스픽(floss pick)이 16개 환경범주 중 13개에서 최악(플라스틱 손잡이가 탄소발자국의 49%), 대나무 치간칫솔·교체헤드 IDB·재사용 치실이 가장 친환경 — 임상적 필요·비용과 함께 환경발자국도 따져 권고.
> - **물치실 vs 치실(Mohapatra 2024, SR 7편 RCT)**: 다수(4/7)가 물치실 우위(특히 접근 어려운 인접면), 3/7은 차이 없음 — 정량 메타분석은 I²=97%로 미시행(질적 종합). 교정군 NS 결과(Yiamwattana 2025)와 긴장 관계 → 일반 성인 한정 신호.
> - **슈퍼플로스(Super Floss) — 교정·브릿지·임플란트용 도구(Sawan 2022, split-mouth RCT n=34)**: 교정환자서 슈퍼플로스와 물치실이 즉시 치태 제거 동등(둘 다 ~0.6→0.13, 군간 p=0.951), 대구치 원심 인접면에서만 물치실 근소 우세(p=0.033). 슈퍼플로스(스레더+스폰지+일반 치실 3분절)는 고정장치·보철물에 특화된 치실 변형.
> - 구강세정기/워터픽(WF/OI): 맥락 의존 — 변연치은 출혈은 치간칫솔보다 우세(Mancinelli-Lyle 2024), 치주염 NSPT 보조로 효과적(Liu 2025)이나, **교정 단독 부가는 이점 없음**(Tyler 2023 워터픽 NS; Yiamwattana NS), **임플란트 주위서도 칫솔질 단독 대비 NS**(Bishti 2025). 손재주 제약·교정장치·치주염 유지관리에 강점.
> - **워터플로서(WF)는 치면세균막보다 출혈·치은염 개선에 강하다**: 18 RCT SR+MA(Badahdah 2025, n≈1005)에서 출혈지수(BI, 중등도 근거)·치은지수(GI)는 약간 개선하나 플라크 우위는 없음 — 이득은 치실보다 '칫솔질 단독' 대비, 일반환자보다 교정환자에서 더 뚜렷. 스코핑리뷰(Sarkisova 2024, 275편)도 "염증은 일관되게 감소, 플라크는 엇갈림"으로 동일 결론 → 이 둘이 Mohapatra 2024(WF 우위) vs Yiamwattana 2025(교정서 NS)의 갈등을 **"WF는 플라크보다 출혈·치은염에 더 효과적"으로 해소**.
> - **일반 인구에서 칫솔질 보조로 효과적·안전(Ren 2023, RCT n=90, 12주)**: WaterPik 추가가 칫솔질 단독보다 치은염(4주부터)·치태(8주부터) 유의 개선, 압력-출혈 용량반응, 중대 이상반응·통증·상아질과민증·치은퇴축 없음 — 일반인구 효능+안전 공백을 메움.
> - **미생물군 호전(Kim 2023, 예비 RCT n=29, 3일 무칫솔 모델)**: 다채널 구강세정기가 BOP를 낮추고 대조군의 Prevotella +114%·Bacteroidetes 증가를 차단, 선구 공생 연쇄상구균을 늘림 — WF가 치은연하 생태를 유리하게 전환한다는 기전 근거(Liu 2025 16S와 일치).
> - **★안전 주의(Slekovec 2026, 증례)**: 비멸균 수돗물을 채운 워터플로서 저수조가 레지오넬라(Lp1) 생물막(300 CFU/L)을 품었고, 그 균주가 면역저하(골수종·자가조혈모세포이식) 환자의 병원내 레지오넬라병 균주와 전장유전체 동일(ST42). 가압 분사 = 흡입 위험 → **저수조 정기 세척·소독, 면역저하자·병원 환경서 주의**.
> - 토스픽법(칫솔질 기법): 인접면 플라그 + 치은 치유 자극(이중기전), 단 전문가 시술 기준 — 별도 overview [[overviews/watanabe-toothpick-method-toothbrushing-synthesis]] 참조.
> - 나무 이쑤시개(치간청소): **권장 안 함** — 습관적·수직 사용이 치간유두 소실·블랙트라이앵글 유발(El Haddad 2026).
> - 임플란트 주위 점막염: 어떤 기구도 우월성 미입증(Bishti 2025 SR+MA) — 도구 선택보다 규칙적 자가위생·정기 유지관리가 핵심.
> - 근거의 한계: 대부분 단기(2–8주)·소표본 RCT, 산업후원·이질성 큼(교정 SR I²=98%), 장기·경성 종점(치아상실·임플란트 생존) 근거 부재.
> - 임상 결정 트리: 열린 치간공간→치간칫솔 / 좁은 접촉→치실 / 손재주·교정·임플란트·비순응→워터픽 / 치주염 유지→워터플로싱 보조 / 나무 이쑤시개는 회피.

## One-line Summary

Synthesis of 18 interdental-device studies (plus the toothpick-method overview) comparing dental floss (DF), interdental brushes (IDB), rubber/silicone interdental picks, water flossers / oral irrigators (WF/OI), super floss, and the wooden toothpick: there is **no universally superior device — adherence matters more than device choice**; the IDB has the strongest single-device evidence where embrasures admit it (Carrouel 2026, pregnancy gingivitis BOP 56%→12%, OR 3.14), floss is best reserved for tight contacts where an IDB won't fit (equivalent to air-floss and to irrigators: Thomassen 2025, Yiamwattana 2025) and its weak efficacy is not fixable by better technique (Jung 2025, n=37, technique improved but plaque removal did not), rubber interdental picks were used far more consistently than floss over 6 months (Yilmaz 2025, 12.61 vs 4.96/24 weeks, p=0.003 — direct adherence-over-efficacy evidence), **water flossers help bleeding/gingivitis more than plaque** — the broadest evidence now (Badahdah 2025 SR+MA, 18 RCTs, n≈1005: slight bleeding/gingival benefit, moderate certainty for bleeding, no plaque superiority; Sarkisova 2024 scoping review of 275 sources: inflammation consistently down, plaque mixed) reconciles the earlier Mohapatra-2024-vs-Yiamwattana-2025 tension; they help with marginal bleeding (Mancinelli-Lyle 2024), are an effective and safe general-population toothbrushing adjunct (Ren 2023, RCT n=90, no pain/recession/hypersensitivity), favorably shift the subgingival microbiome (Kim 2023), and are a periodontitis adjunct (Liu 2025), yet add nothing over toothbrushing in fixed-orthodontic (Tyler 2023) or peri-implant contexts (Bishti 2025); super floss equals a water flosser for immediate plaque removal in orthodontic patients (Sawan 2022); a new safety caveat is reservoir hygiene — a nonsterile-tap-water flosser harbored Legionella biofilm and infected an immunocompromised patient (Slekovec 2026); environmental footprint is a further decision axis, single-use floss picks being the worst choice (Abed 2022, LCA); and habitual wooden-toothpick use harms the papilla (El Haddad 2026).

## Summary

Toothbrushing alone does not clean the interdental surfaces where most caries and gingival inflammation begin. Five families of interdental tools compete for that job: **dental floss (DF)** (including **super floss** for appliances/prostheses), **interdental brushes (IDB)** and **rubber/silicone interdental picks**, **water flossers / oral irrigators (WF/OI, "Waterpik")**, and the **Watanabe toothpick toothbrushing method**. This page synthesizes the 18 device-comparison studies the wiki holds (and cross-links the dedicated toothpick-method overview) to answer: *which tool, for which patient?*

The dominant finding across the evidence is that **no single device is universally best, and the largest determinant of outcome is whether the patient actually uses it** — adherence beats efficacy. The strongest direct evidence for this is now Yilmaz 2025 (RCT, n=54, 24 weeks): rubber interdental picks were used regularly for 12.61 of 24 weeks vs only 4.96 for floss (p=0.003), and within both arms more weeks of regular use tracked with lower plaque and bleeding — the easiest tool to keep using wins. Reinforcing the other side of the same point, Jung 2025 (prospective, n=37) showed that even *teaching correct technique* does not rescue floss: instruction raised the Flossing Performance Score (2.0→2.83, p<.001) but plaque removal did not improve (0.17 vs 0.21, p=.112) and was uncorrelated with technique — floss's weak record is not merely a matter of poor application. Within this frame, each tool has a niche.

**Water flossers help bleeding and gingivitis more than they remove plaque** — and that framing now resolves a long-standing conflict in the wiki. The broadest map of the category, Sarkisova 2024 (scoping review, 275 sources, 185 clinical trials), separates the two outcome domains cleanly: oral irrigators *consistently* reduce periodontal/peri-implant inflammation but their plaque-removal evidence is *mixed*, with the proposed mechanism being microbiome modulation rather than mechanical biofilm stripping. The strongest quantitative synthesis, Badahdah 2025 (SR+MA, 18 RCTs, 15 meta-analyzed, n≈1005, spanning regular and orthodontic patients), confirms this: a dental water jet gives a *slight* benefit on bleeding (moderate GRADE certainty) and gingival indices but *no* plaque superiority, with benefits larger versus brushing-alone than versus floss and larger in orthodontic patients. Together these reconcile the earlier tension between Mohapatra 2024 (which favored the water flosser over floss for plaque) and Yiamwattana 2025 (no significant superiority in orthodontics): the water flosser's real edge is on bleeding/gingivitis, not plaque — Badahdah explicitly attributes Mohapatra's plaque result to before/after-cleaning assessments lacking ≥2-week follow-up, and finds plaque parity with floss once follow-up is required. Two general-population RCTs anchor the efficacy and the mechanism: Ren 2023 (n=90 gingivitis patients) showed the irrigator as a toothbrushing adjunct beat brushing alone on gingival/bleeding indices (p<0.001 by 8–12 weeks) and on plaque from 8 weeks, with a pressure-BOP dose-response and — importantly, as the first irrigator trial to make safety a co-primary endpoint — *no* increase in serious adverse events, pain, dentin hypersensitivity, recession, or trauma; and Kim 2023 (pilot RCT n=29, 3-day no-brush model) showed a multichannel irrigator reduced BOP and blocked the control group's dysbiotic shift (Prevotella +114%, Bacteroidetes rise), instead enriching pioneer commensal streptococci — direct mechanistic support that water flossing favorably reshapes the subgingival ecology (mirroring Liu 2025's 16S findings).

**Toothbrushing is the baseline these tools supplement.** Interdental aids add to — not replace — daily toothbrushing, and the brushing tier has its own evidence: among electric toothbrushes, oscillating-rotating ranks first for plaque and gingivitis (Luo 2026 NMA), and powered beats manual in orthodontic patients (Liu 2026 SR+MA, GRADE-low). Optimize the brush first, then layer the right interdental tool on top.

**Peri-implant caveat.** Around implant-supported restorations, the Bishti 2025 SR+MA found *all* interproximal devices give some benefit but **none is proven superior**, and toothbrushing + a water irrigator was not significantly better than toothbrushing alone — so device choice matters less than regular self-care plus professional maintenance.

**Environmental-impact axis.** Beyond efficacy, adherence, and cost, a fourth selection axis is now on the table: environmental footprint. Abed 2022 — a comparative life-cycle assessment (LCA, not a clinical trial) of 8 aids used daily for 5 years across 16 impact categories — found **single-use floss picks are the worst environmental choice** (largest footprint in 13/16 categories; the polypropylene handle alone is 49% of the floss pick's carbon footprint), while **bamboo IDBs, replaceable-head IDBs, and reusable floss are the greenest**. There is no single "best" product, but the actionable rule is to **prefer reusable/refillable aids over single-use picks**, and to weigh environmental impact alongside clinical need and cost when recommending an aid. (Every aid's footprint is still far smaller than that of established periodontal disease, so preventing disease dominates the planetary calculus.)

**Safety considerations.** Interdental aids are overwhelmingly safe — Ren 2023 made safety a co-primary endpoint for the water flosser and found no excess pain, dentin hypersensitivity, recession, or soft/hard-tissue trauma over 12 weeks, and Sarkisova 2024 maps a quantified pressure envelope (70–90 psi for attached gingiva, 50–70 psi for ulcerated/non-keratinized tissue) plus a transient-bacteremia caution for infective-endocarditis-risk patients. The one serious new signal is **reservoir hygiene for water flossers**: Slekovec 2026 (case report) genomically linked a water flosser filled with nonsterile home tap water — its reservoir harboring a *Legionella pneumophila* serogroup-1 biofilm (300 CFU/L) — to fatal-risk nosocomial Legionnaires' disease in an immunocompromised myeloma/auto-HSCT patient (identical whole genome, ST42; all hospital water sources negative). The pressurized jet aerosolizes reservoir water, creating an inhalation route. Incidence is very low (first reported case), but the practical rules follow directly: **regularly clean and disinfect the water-flosser reservoir, avoid leaving stagnant water in it, and exercise particular caution in immunocompromised patients and hospital settings** (where patient-owned aerosol-generating devices should be captured at admission). The wooden toothpick carries its own (mechanical, not infectious) harm — habitual/vertical use drives interdental papilla loss and black triangles (El Haddad 2026).

## Decision Tree — which interdental tool?

1. **Open embrasure / lost papilla / wide interdental space** → **interdental brush (IDB)**, sized to the space (first-line; strongest evidence). A **rubber/silicone interdental pick** is a strong adherence-friendly alternative where the space admits it (used regularly far longer than floss — Yilmaz 2025).
2. **Tight or intact contacts where an IDB won't pass** → **dental floss** (its main remaining indication) — but counsel realistically: even correct technique yields little extra plaque removal (Jung 2025), so prioritize a tool the patient will actually use.
3. **Fixed orthodontic appliances, bridge pontics, or implant prostheses** → **super floss** (threader + spongy + regular segments) or a **water flosser** — equivalent for immediate plaque removal in ortho patients (Sawan 2022), with the WF holding a small edge at the hard-to-reach distal molar.
4. **Poor manual dexterity, fixed orthodontic appliances, implant-supported prostheses, or a patient who won't floss** → **water flosser / oral irrigator** as an easier-to-adhere alternative; set expectations correctly — its real benefit is on bleeding/gingivitis rather than plaque (Badahdah 2025 SR+MA; Sarkisova 2024), and it is a safe, effective general-population brushing adjunct (Ren 2023). The plaque edge over floss seen in some general-adult RCTs (Mohapatra 2024) is qualitative and not seen in the orthodontic SR (Yiamwattana 2025) — compliance, not a settled efficacy edge, is the main reason. **Counsel reservoir hygiene**: instruct regular cleaning/disinfection of the reservoir and no stagnant water, and **avoid (or take extra precautions with) water flossers in immunocompromised patients and hospital settings** because of the aerosolized-Legionella biofilm risk (Slekovec 2026).
5. **Active periodontitis maintenance after NSPT** → **water flossing** is an effective adjunct (Liu 2025); adding an herbal/antimicrobial irrigant gives little extra clinical benefit.
6. **Where two aids are otherwise comparable** → favor the lower environmental footprint: prefer **reusable/refillable aids over single-use floss/IDB picks** (Abed 2022).
7. **Always** → the best tool is the one the patient will use daily; reinforce technique and instruction frequency.
8. **Avoid** routine **wooden toothpicks** — counsel patients off habitual/vertical use (papilla loss).

## Limitations of the Evidence Base

- Mostly **short-term (2–8 week) small RCTs**, several measuring only **single-visit immediate plaque** (Sawan 2022); the irrigator-vs-floss SRs had extreme heterogeneity (I²=97–98%) and **disagree by population** — Mohapatra 2024 favored water flossers in general adults while Yiamwattana 2025 found no significant superiority in orthodontic patients, so the "water flosser > floss" signal is population-dependent, not settled.
- Several trials are **industry-sponsored** (e.g., Mancinelli-Lyle 2024, Water Pik) or single-IDB-size.
- **No hard-endpoint data** (tooth loss, implant survival, caries increment) — outcomes are plaque/bleeding indices over weeks.
- Peri-implant evidence is **limited** (Bishti 2025: only 2 of 6 studies meta-analysable).
- The toothpick *method* evidence is largely **professional-delivery**, not self-performed (see its overview).
