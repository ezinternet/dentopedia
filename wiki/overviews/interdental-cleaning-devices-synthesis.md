## 한국어 핵심요약

> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - 핵심: 칫솔질만으로 닿지 않는 치간(interdental) 청소도구 4종 — 치실(Dental Floss, DF)·치간칫솔(Interdental Brush, IDB)·구강세정기/워터픽(Water Flosser/Oral Irrigator, WF/OI)·토스픽법(Watanabe Toothpick Method) — 을 위키 8편 + 토스픽법 overview로 종합.
> - **단일 보편 우승 도구는 없다** — 가장 큰 변수는 도구 자체가 아니라 **순응도(adherence)**. 환자가 실제로 매일 쓰는 도구가 최선.
> - 치간칫솔(IDB): 공간이 허용하면 **1순위**. 임신 치은염서 BOP 56%→12%(−79.6%, 출혈소실 최강 예측인자 OR 3.14, Carrouel 2026)로 가장 강한 단일-도구 근거.
> - 치실(DF): 치간칫솔이 **안 들어가는 좁은/정상 접촉**에만 한정 권장. 정유 에어플로스와 동등(Thomassen 2025), 구강세정기와도 차이 없음(Yiamwattana 2025) — DF 특유의 우월 근거는 약함, 순응도 낮음.
> - 구강세정기/워터픽(WF/OI): 맥락 의존 — 변연치은 출혈은 치간칫솔보다 우세(Mancinelli-Lyle 2024), 치주염 NSPT 보조로 효과적(Liu 2025)이나, **교정 단독 부가는 이점 없음**(Tyler 2023 워터픽 NS; Yiamwattana NS), **임플란트 주위서도 칫솔질 단독 대비 NS**(Bishti 2025). 손재주 제약·교정장치·치주염 유지관리에 강점.
> - 토스픽법(칫솔질 기법): 인접면 플라그 + 치은 치유 자극(이중기전), 단 전문가 시술 기준 — 별도 overview [[overviews/watanabe-toothpick-method-toothbrushing-synthesis]] 참조.
> - 나무 이쑤시개(치간청소): **권장 안 함** — 습관적·수직 사용이 치간유두 소실·블랙트라이앵글 유발(El Haddad 2026).
> - 임플란트 주위 점막염: 어떤 기구도 우월성 미입증(Bishti 2025 SR+MA) — 도구 선택보다 규칙적 자가위생·정기 유지관리가 핵심.
> - 근거의 한계: 대부분 단기(2–8주)·소표본 RCT, 산업후원·이질성 큼(교정 SR I²=98%), 장기·경성 종점(치아상실·임플란트 생존) 근거 부재.
> - 임상 결정 트리: 열린 치간공간→치간칫솔 / 좁은 접촉→치실 / 손재주·교정·임플란트·비순응→워터픽 / 치주염 유지→워터플로싱 보조 / 나무 이쑤시개는 회피.

## One-line Summary

Synthesis of 8 interdental-device studies (plus the toothpick-method overview) comparing dental floss (DF), interdental brushes (IDB), water flossers / oral irrigators (WF/OI), and the wooden toothpick: there is **no universally superior device — adherence matters more than device choice**; the IDB has the strongest single-device evidence where embrasures admit it (Carrouel 2026, pregnancy gingivitis BOP 56%→12%, OR 3.14), floss is best reserved for tight contacts where an IDB won't fit (equivalent to air-floss and to irrigators: Thomassen 2025, Yiamwattana 2025), water flossers help with marginal bleeding (Mancinelli-Lyle 2024) and as a periodontitis adjunct (Liu 2025) but add nothing over toothbrushing in fixed-orthodontic (Tyler 2023) or peri-implant contexts (Bishti 2025), and habitual wooden-toothpick use harms the papilla (El Haddad 2026).

## Summary

Toothbrushing alone does not clean the interdental surfaces where most caries and gingival inflammation begin. Four families of interdental tools compete for that job: **dental floss (DF)**, **interdental brushes (IDB)**, **water flossers / oral irrigators (WF/OI, "Waterpik")**, and the **Watanabe toothpick toothbrushing method**. This page synthesizes the 8 device-comparison studies the wiki holds (and cross-links the dedicated toothpick-method overview) to answer: *which tool, for which patient?*

The dominant finding across the evidence is that **no single device is universally best, and the largest determinant of outcome is whether the patient actually uses it** — adherence beats efficacy. Within that frame, each tool has a niche.

**Evidence by device.**

| Device | Key wiki evidence | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| **Interdental brush (IDB)** | Carrouel 2026 (RCT n=323): daily calibrated IDB cut pregnancy-gingivitis BOP 56%→12% (−79.6%), strongest independent predictor of bleeding resolution (OR=3.14) | **First-line where the embrasure admits a brush** — strongest single-device benefit |
| **Dental floss (DF)** | Thomassen 2025 (RCT n=82): air-floss + essential oils ≡ waxed floss (NS); Yiamwattana 2025 (SR+MA): oral irrigator NOT superior to floss (NS, I²=98%) | **Reserve for tight/intact contacts** where an IDB won't fit; no proven superiority, poor adherence |
| **Water flosser / oral irrigator (WF/OI)** | Mancinelli-Lyle 2024 (RCT): WF > IDB for marginal bleeding (BOMP p=0.003); Liu 2025 (RCT): effective NSPT adjunct in periodontitis; Tyler 2023 (RCT): Waterpik adds nothing over manual brushing in fixed ortho (NS); Bishti 2025 (SR+MA): around implants, irrigator NOT superior to brushing alone | **Context-dependent** — good for marginal bleeding, periodontitis maintenance, poor dexterity, fixed appliances; NOT a proven upgrade in ortho or peri-implant |
| **Toothpick method (toothbrushing)** | see [[overviews/watanabe-toothpick-method-toothbrushing-synthesis]] | Proximal plaque + gingival healing, but benefit shown mostly with professional delivery |
| **Wooden toothpick (interdental tool)** | El Haddad 2026 (cross-sectional n=69): habitual/vertical use → interdental papilla loss / black triangles | **Avoid for routine cleaning** — a harm, not a recommended aid |

**Peri-implant caveat.** Around implant-supported restorations, the Bishti 2025 SR+MA found *all* interproximal devices give some benefit but **none is proven superior**, and toothbrushing + a water irrigator was not significantly better than toothbrushing alone — so device choice matters less than regular self-care plus professional maintenance.

## Decision Tree — which interdental tool?

1. **Open embrasure / lost papilla / wide interdental space** → **interdental brush (IDB)**, sized to the space (first-line; strongest evidence).
2. **Tight or intact contacts where an IDB won't pass** → **dental floss** (its main remaining indication).
3. **Poor manual dexterity, fixed orthodontic appliances, implant-supported prostheses, or a patient who won't floss** → **water flosser / oral irrigator** as an easier-to-adhere alternative (compliance, not superior efficacy, is the reason).
4. **Active periodontitis maintenance after NSPT** → **water flossing** is an effective adjunct (Liu 2025); adding an herbal/antimicrobial irrigant gives little extra clinical benefit.
5. **Always** → the best tool is the one the patient will use daily; reinforce technique and instruction frequency.
6. **Avoid** routine **wooden toothpicks** — counsel patients off habitual/vertical use (papilla loss).

## Limitations of the Evidence Base

- Mostly **short-term (2–8 week) small RCTs**; the orthodontic irrigator SR had extreme heterogeneity (I²=98%, Yiamwattana 2025).
- Several trials are **industry-sponsored** (e.g., Mancinelli-Lyle 2024, Water Pik) or single-IDB-size.
- **No hard-endpoint data** (tooth loss, implant survival, caries increment) — outcomes are plaque/bleeding indices over weeks.
- Peri-implant evidence is **limited** (Bishti 2025: only 2 of 6 studies meta-analysable).
- The toothpick *method* evidence is largely **professional-delivery**, not self-performed (see its overview).
