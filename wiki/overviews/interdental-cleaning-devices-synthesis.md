## 한국어 핵심요약

> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - 핵심: 칫솔질만으로 닿지 않는 치간(interdental) 청소도구 4종 — 치실(Dental Floss, DF)·치간칫솔(Interdental Brush, IDB)·구강세정기/워터픽(Water Flosser/Oral Irrigator, WF/OI)·토스픽법(Watanabe Toothpick Method) — 을 위키 8편 + 토스픽법 overview로 종합.
> - **단일 보편 우승 도구는 없다** — 가장 큰 변수는 도구 자체가 아니라 **순응도(adherence)**. 환자가 실제로 매일 쓰는 도구가 최선.
> - 치간칫솔(IDB): 공간이 허용하면 **1순위**. 임신 치은염서 BOP 56%→12%(−79.6%, 출혈소실 최강 예측인자 OR 3.14, Carrouel 2026)로 가장 강한 단일-도구 근거.
> - 치실(DF): 치간칫솔이 **안 들어가는 좁은/정상 접촉**에만 한정 권장. 정유 에어플로스와 동등(Thomassen 2025), 구강세정기와도 차이 없음(Yiamwattana 2025) — DF 특유의 우월 근거는 약함, 순응도 낮음.
> - **올바른 술식조차 치실 효능을 살리지 못한다(Jung 2025, 전향 n=37)**: 동영상 교육으로 치실 술식(Flossing Performance Score, FPS 2.0→2.83, p<.001)은 향상됐으나 치태 제거(PSPI 차이 0.17 vs 0.21, p=.112)는 개선되지 않았고 술식과 무관 — "치실은 기술만 가르치면 된다"는 통념을 반박, DF를 좁은 접촉 한정으로 두는 결정을 보강.
> - **순응도(adherence)가 효능보다 중요하다는 직접 근거(Yilmaz 2025, RCT n=54, 24주)**: 고무 치간 픽(rubber interdental pick)을 규칙적으로 쓴 주수 12.61주 vs 치실 4.96주(p=0.003); 양군 모두 규칙적 사용 주수와 플라크·출혈이 음의 상관 — 가장 오래 쓰는 도구가 이긴다. 고무/실리콘 치간 픽이라는 도구 계열 추가.
> - **환경영향(sustainability)이라는 새 결정 축(Abed 2022, 전과정평가 LCA)**: 일회용 플로스픽(floss pick)이 16개 환경범주 중 13개에서 최악(플라스틱 손잡이가 탄소발자국의 49%), 대나무 치간칫솔·교체헤드 IDB·재사용 치실이 가장 친환경 — 임상적 필요·비용과 함께 환경발자국도 따져 권고.
> - **물치실 vs 치실(Mohapatra 2024, SR 7편 RCT)**: 다수(4/7)가 물치실 우위(특히 접근 어려운 인접면), 3/7은 차이 없음 — 정량 메타분석은 I²=97%로 미시행(질적 종합). 교정군 NS 결과(Yiamwattana 2025)와 긴장 관계 → 일반 성인 한정 신호.
> - **슈퍼플로스(Super Floss) — 교정·브릿지·임플란트용 도구(Sawan 2022, split-mouth RCT n=34)**: 교정환자서 슈퍼플로스와 물치실이 즉시 치태 제거 동등(둘 다 ~0.6→0.13, 군간 p=0.951), 대구치 원심 인접면에서만 물치실 근소 우세(p=0.033). 슈퍼플로스(스레더+스폰지+일반 치실 3분절)는 고정장치·보철물에 특화된 치실 변형.
> - 구강세정기/워터픽(WF/OI): 맥락 의존 — 변연치은 출혈은 치간칫솔보다 우세(Mancinelli-Lyle 2024), 치주염 NSPT 보조로 효과적(Liu 2025)이나, **교정 단독 부가는 이점 없음**(Tyler 2023 워터픽 NS; Yiamwattana NS), **임플란트 주위서도 칫솔질 단독 대비 NS**(Bishti 2025). 손재주 제약·교정장치·치주염 유지관리에 강점.
> - 토스픽법(칫솔질 기법): 인접면 플라그 + 치은 치유 자극(이중기전), 단 전문가 시술 기준 — 별도 overview [[overviews/watanabe-toothpick-method-toothbrushing-synthesis]] 참조.
> - 나무 이쑤시개(치간청소): **권장 안 함** — 습관적·수직 사용이 치간유두 소실·블랙트라이앵글 유발(El Haddad 2026).
> - 임플란트 주위 점막염: 어떤 기구도 우월성 미입증(Bishti 2025 SR+MA) — 도구 선택보다 규칙적 자가위생·정기 유지관리가 핵심.
> - 근거의 한계: 대부분 단기(2–8주)·소표본 RCT, 산업후원·이질성 큼(교정 SR I²=98%), 장기·경성 종점(치아상실·임플란트 생존) 근거 부재.
> - 임상 결정 트리: 열린 치간공간→치간칫솔 / 좁은 접촉→치실 / 손재주·교정·임플란트·비순응→워터픽 / 치주염 유지→워터플로싱 보조 / 나무 이쑤시개는 회피.

## One-line Summary

Synthesis of 13 interdental-device studies (plus the toothpick-method overview) comparing dental floss (DF), interdental brushes (IDB), rubber/silicone interdental picks, water flossers / oral irrigators (WF/OI), super floss, and the wooden toothpick: there is **no universally superior device — adherence matters more than device choice**; the IDB has the strongest single-device evidence where embrasures admit it (Carrouel 2026, pregnancy gingivitis BOP 56%→12%, OR 3.14), floss is best reserved for tight contacts where an IDB won't fit (equivalent to air-floss and to irrigators: Thomassen 2025, Yiamwattana 2025) and its weak efficacy is not fixable by better technique (Jung 2025, n=37, technique improved but plaque removal did not), rubber interdental picks were used far more consistently than floss over 6 months (Yilmaz 2025, 12.61 vs 4.96/24 weeks, p=0.003 — direct adherence-over-efficacy evidence), water flossers help with marginal bleeding (Mancinelli-Lyle 2024), as a periodontitis adjunct (Liu 2025), and were favored over floss for plaque in most general-adult RCTs (Mohapatra 2024, 4/7) yet add nothing over toothbrushing in fixed-orthodontic (Tyler 2023) or peri-implant contexts (Bishti 2025); super floss equals a water flosser for immediate plaque removal in orthodontic patients (Sawan 2022); environmental footprint is a further decision axis, single-use floss picks being the worst choice (Abed 2022, LCA); and habitual wooden-toothpick use harms the papilla (El Haddad 2026).

치간 청소도구 8편 종합(+토스픽법 overview) — 치실·치간칫솔·구강세정기/워터픽·나무이쑤시개 비교: **보편적 우승 도구는 없고 순응도가 도구보다 중요**. 치간칫솔이 들어가는 공간이면 1순위(Carrouel 2026 임신치은염 BOP 56%→12%, OR 3.14), 치실은 좁은 접촉 한정(에어플로스·세정기와 동등), 워터픽은 변연출혈·치주염 보조엔 강하나 교정·임플란트 부가는 이점 없음, 나무 이쑤시개는 치간유두 위해로 회피.

## Summary

Toothbrushing alone does not clean the interdental surfaces where most caries and gingival inflammation begin. Five families of interdental tools compete for that job: **dental floss (DF)** (including **super floss** for appliances/prostheses), **interdental brushes (IDB)** and **rubber/silicone interdental picks**, **water flossers / oral irrigators (WF/OI, "Waterpik")**, and the **Watanabe toothpick toothbrushing method**. This page synthesizes the 13 device-comparison studies the wiki holds (and cross-links the dedicated toothpick-method overview) to answer: *which tool, for which patient?*

The dominant finding across the evidence is that **no single device is universally best, and the largest determinant of outcome is whether the patient actually uses it** — adherence beats efficacy. The strongest direct evidence for this is now Yilmaz 2025 (RCT, n=54, 24 weeks): rubber interdental picks were used regularly for 12.61 of 24 weeks vs only 4.96 for floss (p=0.003), and within both arms more weeks of regular use tracked with lower plaque and bleeding — the easiest tool to keep using wins. Reinforcing the other side of the same point, Jung 2025 (prospective, n=37) showed that even *teaching correct technique* does not rescue floss: instruction raised the Flossing Performance Score (2.0→2.83, p<.001) but plaque removal did not improve (0.17 vs 0.21, p=.112) and was uncorrelated with technique — floss's weak record is not merely a matter of poor application. Within this frame, each tool has a niche.

**Toothbrushing is the baseline these tools supplement.** Interdental aids add to — not replace — daily toothbrushing, and the brushing tier has its own evidence: among electric toothbrushes, oscillating-rotating ranks first for plaque and gingivitis (Luo 2026 NMA), and powered beats manual in orthodontic patients (Liu 2026 SR+MA, GRADE-low). Optimize the brush first, then layer the right interdental tool on top.

**Evidence by device.**

| Device | Key wiki evidence | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| **Interdental brush (IDB)** | Carrouel 2026 (RCT n=323): daily calibrated IDB cut pregnancy-gingivitis BOP 56%→12% (−79.6%), strongest independent predictor of bleeding resolution (OR=3.14) | **First-line where the embrasure admits a brush** — strongest single-device benefit |
| **Rubber / silicone interdental pick** | Yilmaz 2025 (RCT n=54, 24 wk): used regularly far longer than floss (12.61 vs 4.96/24 wk, p=0.003); plaque/bleeding negatively correlated with weeks of regular use | **Best adherence where the space admits it** — the practical adherence-over-efficacy choice vs floss |
| **Dental floss (DF)** | Thomassen 2025 (RCT n=82): air-floss + essential oils ≡ waxed floss (NS); Yiamwattana 2025 (SR+MA): oral irrigator NOT superior to floss (NS, I²=98%); Jung 2025 (prospective n=37): better technique (FPS 2.0→2.83) did NOT improve plaque removal (p=.112) and was uncorrelated | **Reserve for tight/intact contacts** where an IDB won't fit; no proven superiority, poor adherence, and weak efficacy even when done correctly |
| **Super floss (threader + spongy + regular)** | Sawan 2022 (split-mouth RCT n=34, ortho): ≡ water flosser for immediate plaque removal (both ~0.6→0.13, between-group p=0.951); WF marginally better only at distal molar (p=0.033) | **For fixed appliances, bridge pontics, implant prostheses** — equals a water flosser overall in orthodontic patients |
| **Water flosser / oral irrigator (WF/OI)** | Mancinelli-Lyle 2024 (RCT): WF > IDB for marginal bleeding (BOMP p=0.003); Mohapatra 2024 (SR, 7 RCTs): most trials (4/7) favored WF over floss for plaque, esp. interproximal (qualitative, no MA, I²=97%); Liu 2025 (RCT): effective NSPT adjunct in periodontitis; Tyler 2023 (RCT): Waterpik adds nothing over manual brushing in fixed ortho (NS); Bishti 2025 (SR+MA): around implants, irrigator NOT superior to brushing alone | **Context-dependent** — good for marginal bleeding, periodontitis maintenance, poor dexterity, fixed appliances; favored over floss for plaque in general adults but NOT a proven upgrade in ortho or peri-implant |
| **Toothpick method (toothbrushing)** | see [[overviews/watanabe-toothpick-method-toothbrushing-synthesis]] | Proximal plaque + gingival healing, but benefit shown mostly with professional delivery |
| **Wooden toothpick (interdental tool)** | El Haddad 2026 (cross-sectional n=69): habitual/vertical use → interdental papilla loss / black triangles | **Avoid for routine cleaning** — a harm, not a recommended aid |

**Peri-implant caveat.** Around implant-supported restorations, the Bishti 2025 SR+MA found *all* interproximal devices give some benefit but **none is proven superior**, and toothbrushing + a water irrigator was not significantly better than toothbrushing alone — so device choice matters less than regular self-care plus professional maintenance.

**Environmental-impact axis.** Beyond efficacy, adherence, and cost, a fourth selection axis is now on the table: environmental footprint. Abed 2022 — a comparative life-cycle assessment (LCA, not a clinical trial) of 8 aids used daily for 5 years across 16 impact categories — found **single-use floss picks are the worst environmental choice** (largest footprint in 13/16 categories; the polypropylene handle alone is 49% of the floss pick's carbon footprint), while **bamboo IDBs, replaceable-head IDBs, and reusable floss are the greenest**. There is no single "best" product, but the actionable rule is to **prefer reusable/refillable aids over single-use picks**, and to weigh environmental impact alongside clinical need and cost when recommending an aid. (Every aid's footprint is still far smaller than that of established periodontal disease, so preventing disease dominates the planetary calculus.)

## Decision Tree — which interdental tool?

1. **Open embrasure / lost papilla / wide interdental space** → **interdental brush (IDB)**, sized to the space (first-line; strongest evidence). A **rubber/silicone interdental pick** is a strong adherence-friendly alternative where the space admits it (used regularly far longer than floss — Yilmaz 2025).
2. **Tight or intact contacts where an IDB won't pass** → **dental floss** (its main remaining indication) — but counsel realistically: even correct technique yields little extra plaque removal (Jung 2025), so prioritize a tool the patient will actually use.
3. **Fixed orthodontic appliances, bridge pontics, or implant prostheses** → **super floss** (threader + spongy + regular segments) or a **water flosser** — equivalent for immediate plaque removal in ortho patients (Sawan 2022), with the WF holding a small edge at the hard-to-reach distal molar.
4. **Poor manual dexterity, fixed orthodontic appliances, implant-supported prostheses, or a patient who won't floss** → **water flosser / oral irrigator** as an easier-to-adhere alternative; in general adults it was favored over floss for plaque in most RCTs (Mohapatra 2024), though this is qualitative and not seen in the orthodontic SR (Yiamwattana 2025) — compliance, not a settled efficacy edge, is the main reason.
5. **Active periodontitis maintenance after NSPT** → **water flossing** is an effective adjunct (Liu 2025); adding an herbal/antimicrobial irrigant gives little extra clinical benefit.
6. **Where two aids are otherwise comparable** → favor the lower environmental footprint: prefer **reusable/refillable aids over single-use floss/IDB picks** (Abed 2022).
7. **Always** → the best tool is the one the patient will use daily; reinforce technique and instruction frequency.
8. **Avoid** routine **wooden toothpicks** — counsel patients off habitual/vertical use (papilla loss).

## Limitations of the Evidence Base

- Mostly **short-term (2–8 week) small RCTs**, several measuring only **single-visit immediate plaque** (Sawan 2022); the irrigator-vs-floss SRs had extreme heterogeneity (I²=97–98%) and **disagree by population** — Mohapatra 2024 favored water flossers in general adults while Yiamwattana 2025 found no significant superiority in orthodontic patients, so the "water flosser > floss" signal is population-dependent, not settled.
- Several trials are **industry-sponsored** (e.g., Mancinelli-Lyle 2024, Water Pik) or single-IDB-size.
- **No hard-endpoint data** (tooth loss, implant survival, caries increment) — outcomes are plaque/bleeding indices over weeks.
- Peri-implant evidence is **limited** (Bishti 2025: only 2 of 6 studies meta-analysable).
- The toothpick *method* evidence is largely **professional-delivery**, not self-performed (see its overview).
