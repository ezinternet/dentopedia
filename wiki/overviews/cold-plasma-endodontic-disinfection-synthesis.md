> - 핵심 명제: 냉대기압 플라즈마 (Cold Atmospheric Plasma, CAP) / 비열 플라즈마 (Non-Thermal Plasma)는 40°C 이하에서 활성산소·질소종 (Reactive Oxygen/Nitrogen Species, ROS/RNS)을 생성해 근관 내 세균을 죽이는 화학약품 비의존·열손상 비유발 소독법이다. wiki/endodontics/cold-plasma 7편 (in-vitro 5 · narrative-review 1 · RCT 1)을 기전-효능-비교-안전-임상 축으로 통합.
> - 기전: 플라즈마가 만드는 ·OH (수산화라디칼)·H₂O₂·O₃ (오존)·¹O₂ (단일항산소) 등 ROS와 NO·NO₂·ONOO⁻ (퍼옥시나이트라이트) 등 RNS가 세균 막·핵산·단백을 손상 — 항생제와 달리 내성을 유발하지 않는 것이 핵심 장점 (Muniz 2023). [근거: 리뷰 합의]
> - 표적 병원체: 지속성 근관감염의 ~80%를 차지하는 E. faecalis (Enterococcus faecalis) 바이오필름이 모든 연구의 주표적이며, C. albicans (칸디다)에도 유효. 바이오필름은 부유균보다 저항성이 크고, 두껍고 오래된(3주) 막일수록 더 긴 노출이 필요 (Muniz 2023, Li 2015).
> - 효능 임계시간 (직접 노출): 최대 항균효과는 ≥8분에서 도달 (Muniz 2023). Li 2015는 AC Ar/O₂ 12분에 3주 성숙 바이오필름 완전 멸균(0 CFU), Armand 2019는 He/O₂ 8분에 ~5.2 log 감소(log 5.39→0.2)·SEM상 완전 소실. [근거: in-vitro 일관]
> - 대 NaOCl: NaOCl이 여전히 더 빠르고 강하다 — 5.25% NaOCl은 5분에 0 CFU인 반면 CAP 제트는 5분에 유의한 감소를 내되 10분에도 완전 멸균엔 못 미침 (Kumar 2023). CAP는 NaOCl 대체가 아니라 보조 또는 NaOCl 위험(첨공 압출·세포독성)이 문제되는 상황의 대안.
> - 대 약물 정체: 효능 위계 TAP > CAP > Ca(OH)₂ — He/O₂ CAP 10분이 Ca(OH)₂ 12일보다 우수, 단 삼중항생제 페이스트 (Triple Antibiotic Paste, TAP)에는 미치지 못함 (Asnaashari 2022). Ca(OH)₂는 성숙 E. faecalis 바이오필름에 무력하다는 기존 근거를 CAP·Li 2015이 재확인.
> - 병용 시너지: CAP + NaOCl/CHX는 단독보다 우수하며, 시너지로 플라즈마 노출시간을 줄일 수 있어 임상적으로 매력적 (Muniz 2023). [근거: 리뷰 합의]
> - 안전성: Li 2015 — 플라즈마 12분 후 상아질 미세경도(Vickers)·조도(Ra) 유의 변화 없음(기계적 안전). Muniz 2023 — 단시간은 치주·치근단 세포에 생체적합, >10–15분은 세포독성 우려(안전 노출창 미확정). [근거: in-vitro·리뷰]
> - 소아 적용: 유치 근관에서 NTPP가 CHX·다이오드 레이저·프로폴리스를 제치고 최고 감소(4.06 log, 98.79%) — 유치는 개방 첨공으로 NaOCl이 후속 영구치배에 독성 위험이 있어 비열·비독성 플라즈마가 특히 매력적 (El Shishiny 2025).
> - 최고 임상근거 & 한계: Lyu 2025 — 수중방전 플라즈마 (Underwater Discharge Plasma, UDP) vs 6% NaOCl 최초 사람 RCT 파일럿(n=28, 4개월). 통증(VAS)·치근단 치유(PAI) 동등, 부작용 0, 임피던스(>5000 Ω) 자동차단 안전장치 검증. 단 치유율 71.4% vs 92.9%(NS, 검정력 0.65로 과소검정)·기관-기기 이해상충·미생물 종결점 부재 — 임상 도입 전 대규모·장기 RCT 필수. ## One-line Summary

Synthesis of 7 wiki pages (5 in-vitro, 1 narrative review, 1 RCT) on cold atmospheric plasma (CAP) / non-thermal plasma for root canal disinfection. Plasma generates ROS/RNS at <40 °C to kill E. faecalis biofilm without thermal or chemical toxicity and without inducing resistance; ≥8–12 min direct exposure can fully eradicate mature biofilm in vitro (Li 2015 12 min → 0 CFU; Armand 2019 ~5.2 log). It beats Ca(OH)₂ but trails NaOCl on speed/completeness (Kumar 2023) and trails TAP (Asnaashari 2022); synergistic with NaOCl/CHX (Muniz 2023). The single human RCT pilot (Lyu 2025, UDP vs 6% NaOCl, n=28) shows comparable 4-month pain/PAI healing and zero adverse effects — promising but underpowered.

## Summary

This page integrates the 7 pages in `wiki/endodontics/cold-plasma/` along five axes: **mechanism → efficacy against E. faecalis biofilm → head-to-head vs conventional agents → safety → clinical translation**. The clinical problem CAP addresses is the residual-infection failure mode of endodontics: persistent *Enterococcus faecalis* (≈80% of post-treatment infections) survives in dentinal tubules where NaOCl penetration is uneven, Ca(OH)₂ is ineffective, and high-concentration NaOCl carries apical-extrusion injury risk. Cold plasma offers a different attack vector — gas/aqueous-phase reactive species delivered at near-room temperature.

The evidence base is steeply pyramidal: **1 narrative review** (Muniz 2023, 17 studies), **5 in-vitro studies** (Armand 2019, Li 2015, Kumar 2023, Asnaashari 2022, El Shishiny 2025), and **1 human RCT pilot** (Lyu 2025). Five core propositions:

1. **Mechanism is ROS/RNS, not heat or fixed chemistry** — plasma ionizes feed gas (He/Ar/O₂) or water to generate ·OH, H₂O₂, O₃, ¹O₂ (ROS) and NO, NO₂, ONOO⁻ (RNS) that disrupt microbial membranes, nucleic acids, and proteins without inducing antimicrobial resistance. [review consensus] (Muniz 2023, Lyu 2025)
2. **≥8–12 min direct exposure fully eradicates mature biofilm in vitro** — Li 2015 (AC Ar/O₂, 12 min → 0 CFU on 3-week biofilm); Armand 2019 (He/O₂, 8 min → ~5.2 log, SEM-confirmed elimination). Dose-response is progressive below threshold. [in-vitro, consistent]
3. **CAP < NaOCl on speed/completeness, but CAP > Ca(OH)₂** — Kumar 2023 (NaOCl 0 CFU at 5 min vs CAP substantial-but-incomplete even at 10 min); Asnaashari 2022 (hierarchy TAP > CAP > Ca(OH)₂). [in-vitro]
4. **Synergy with chemical irrigants** — CAP + NaOCl/CHX exceeds either alone and shortens required plasma time. [review consensus] (Muniz 2023)
5. **Mechanically safe; clinical evidence still a single pilot** — no dentin microhardness/roughness change (Li 2015); first human RCT (Lyu 2025) shows non-inferior 4-month pain/healing vs 6% NaOCl with zero adverse events, but is underpowered.

## Mechanism — ROS/RNS at near-room temperature

Cold atmospheric / non-thermal plasma is a partially ionized gas generated by electrical discharge that stays below ~40 °C, so it disinfects without the thermal tissue damage that limits laser or thermal-plasma approaches. The bactericidal payload is a cocktail of reactive species (Muniz 2023):

- **ROS**: hydroxyl radical (·OH), hydrogen peroxide (H₂O₂), ozone (O₃), singlet oxygen (¹O₂)
- **RNS**: nitric oxide (NO), nitrogen dioxide (NO₂), peroxynitrite (ONOO⁻)
- Plus UV photons, charged particles, and electric-field effects (El Shishiny 2025)

These oxidize and nitrosylate microbial membranes, nucleic acids, and proteins. The decisive advantage over antibiotics is that this multi-target oxidative damage **does not select for resistance** (Muniz 2023). Li 2015 attributes complete biofilm kill specifically to plasma-generated ROS. Lyu 2025's underwater-discharge variant generates the *same* species but in the aqueous phase — ionizing water directly into ·OH, H₂O₂, and O₃ — which is mechanistically why it can be delivered as an irrigant rather than a gas plume.

**Device taxonomy** (Muniz 2023): two families — **dielectric barrier discharge (DBD)** (large-area, diffuse) and **plasma jets** (focused flowing plume, kINPen/OAUGDP types) better suited to canal geometry. Helium and argon are the commonest feed gases, often with O₂ admixture. **Exposure modes**: *direct* (plume into canal, 15/17 studies) vs *indirect* plasma-activated liquid (PAL, only 2/17) — the latter sidesteps the hard problem of delivering a plasma plume down a narrow canal.

## Efficacy against E. faecalis biofilm

E. faecalis biofilm is the universal target (its tubule penetration and acid/alkaline stress resistance make it the benchmark persister). Biofilm maturity is the key efficacy modifier — older, thicker biofilm needs longer exposure (Muniz 2023).

| Study | Plasma type | Biofilm age | Exposure | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Li 2015 | AC Ar/O₂ jet | 3 weeks (mature) | 12 min | **0 CFU** — complete sterilization (SEM+CLSM) |
| Armand 2019 | He/O₂ jet | 1 week | 8 min | **~5.2 log** (log 5.39→0.2); SEM complete elimination + tubule obliteration |
| Asnaashari 2022 | He/O₂ jet | 21 days | 10 min | Significant CFU reduction (p<0.05); SEM disrupted matrix |
| Kumar 2023 | CAP/DBD jet (He) | 7 days | 5–10 min | Substantial reduction, **not** zero even at 10 min |
| El Shishiny 2025 | NTPP jet (primary teeth) | — | — | **4.06 log / 98.79%** reduction — best of 4 modalities |

The threshold pattern is consistent: **maximum effect at ≥8 min** of direct exposure (Muniz 2023), with full eradication achievable at 12 min on even 3-week biofilm (Li 2015). Two studies confirm structural kill morphologically (SEM/CLSM), strengthening the CFU data. Armand 2019's added finding — He/O₂ obliterates *dentinal tubule openings*, implying deeper reactive-species penetration than PDT — partly answers the recurring concern that surface plasma may not reach intratubular persisters, though Muniz 2023 still flags tubule-depth penetration as inadequately studied.

## Head-to-head vs conventional agents

The seven papers collectively position CAP within the existing disinfection armamentarium:

- **vs NaOCl (gold standard)** — NaOCl wins on speed and completeness. Kumar 2023: 5.25% NaOCl reaches 0 CFU at ≥5 min while CAP needs ≥5 min for *substantial* (not complete) reduction and fails to sterilize even at 10 min (p<0.001 NaOCl > CAP at all time points). CAP's role is therefore **adjunct or alternative**, not replacement — relevant where NaOCl's cytotoxicity / apical-extrusion risk is the limiting concern (Lyu 2025, El Shishiny 2025).
- **vs PDT** — Armand 2019 ranks **He/O₂ plasma > PDT > He plasma**; plasma's gas/aqueous phase reaches complex anatomy where liquid photosensitizer distributes unevenly.
- **vs Ca(OH)₂** — CAP clearly superior. Li 2015 (12-min plasma > 1-week Ca(OH)₂/CHX/combination); Asnaashari 2022 (Ca(OH)₂ showed *no* significant reduction). This reconfirms the well-established failure of Ca(OH)₂ against mature E. faecalis (its alkaline mechanism is defeated by E. faecalis proton-pump/biofilm survival).
- **vs TAP** — CAP is *inferior* to triple antibiotic paste: Asnaashari 2022 hierarchy **TAP > CAP > Ca(OH)₂** (TAP achieved complete elimination). But CAP's non-chemical, non-cytotoxic mechanism may favor it over TAP for regenerative endodontics (TAP's stem-cell cytotoxicity is its limiting factor).
- **vs CHX / diode laser / propolis (primary teeth)** — El Shishiny 2025 ranks **NTPP > CHX ≈ diode laser > propolis** (propolis weakest, 81.99%).
- **Combination** — Muniz 2023: CAP + NaOCl/CHX > either alone, and synergy lets you shorten plasma time (clinically attractive given the ≥8-min monotherapy threshold).

**Net positioning**: CAP sits *below NaOCl and TAP* but *above Ca(OH)₂, PDT, propolis, and diode laser* as a monotherapy — and its best practical niche is as a resistance-free, low-cytotoxicity **adjunct** (synergy with NaOCl/CHX) or an **alternative where chemical/thermal toxicity is the binding constraint** (pediatric open apices, regenerative cases).

## Safety

- **Dentin substrate**: Li 2015 — no significant Vickers microhardness or 3D-profilometry roughness change after 12-min plasma. Mechanical integrity of the root is preserved. [in-vitro]
- **Periapical/periodontal cells**: Muniz 2023 — short exposures generally biocompatible; **prolonged exposure (>10–15 min, device-dependent) may be cytotoxic**; the precise safe-exposure window for canal use is *not yet defined*. This is the central unresolved safety tension given that efficacy wants ≥8–12 min.
- **Thermal injury**: Lyu 2025's UDP device operates <40 °C and adds a real-time **impedance feedback cutoff (>5000 Ω auto-stop)** that prevented thermal injury across all treated cases — the first clinical validation of an engineered safety interlock.
- **No resistance induction** — a class-level safety/stewardship advantage over antibiotic-based protocols (TAP) (Muniz 2023).

## Clinical translation — the Lyu 2025 RCT as the evidence ceiling

Six of seven papers are bench studies; **Lyu 2025 is the only human trial and thus the top of this evidence pyramid**. It is a prospective, single-operator, parallel-group **RCT pilot** (Hallym University, Korea) comparing **underwater discharge plasma (UDP, PLAZEN RCT®)** against **6% NaOCl** as the root-canal irrigant; 30 randomized → 28 completed (n=14/group), 4-month follow-up.

| Outcome (4 mo) | UDP | 6% NaOCl | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| VAS pain (mean) | 0.07±0.27 | 0.14±0.54 | NS (both ~0) |
| PAI (mean) | 1.66±0.79 | 1.43±0.62 | 0.401 |
| Success (PAI ≤2) | 71.4% | 92.9% | 0.326 (NS) |
| Adverse effects | 0 | 0 | — |

UDP was **non-inferior** to NaOCl on short-term pain and radiographic healing with zero adverse events (blinded PAI, κ=0.721). **But the caveats are decisive for interpretation**: (1) the numerically lower UDP success (71.4% vs 92.9%) most likely reflects **underpowering** (power=0.65, n=14/group) rather than true inferiority — yet it cannot be dismissed; (2) **no microbiological endpoint** (CFU/biofilm), so the trial measures clinical proxies, not the disinfection the in-vitro work documents; (3) follow-up only 4 months; (4) **institutional conflict of interest** — the study site is affiliated with the device developer (Dentory). [claude해석]

**Bottom line for practice**: cold plasma is a mechanistically sound, resistance-free, dentin-safe disinfection modality with strong in-vitro eradication of mature E. faecalis biofilm, but it does **not yet displace NaOCl**. Its evidence-supported roles today are (a) **adjunct** to NaOCl/CHX for synergy, and (b) candidate **alternative** where NaOCl's chemical/thermal toxicity is the limiting factor (pediatric open apices, regenerative endodontics). Routine clinical adoption awaits larger, longer (≥1 year), microbiologically-endpointed, independently-funded RCTs — the Lyu 2025 pilot defines the frontier, not the verdict.
