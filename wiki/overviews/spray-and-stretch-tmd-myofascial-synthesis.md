## 한국어 핵심요약

> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - 분사신장(Spray-and-Stretch, vapocoolant + 수동신장)은 Travell-Simons의 고전 트리거포인트(Myofascial Trigger Point, MTrP) 비활성화 기법으로, 마스티케이션 근막통(Masticatory Myofascial Pain)·턱관절장애(Temporomandibular Disorder, TMD) 관리에서 보조 모달리티로 쓰인다. 위키 5편 종합.
> - **핵심 결론**: 분사신장은 효과가 **실재하지만 단시간(<6시간)·증상 특이적**이다 — 즉시 개구량(Maximal Mouth Opening, MMO)↑과 통증↓은 일관되나 지속적 조직 변화나 압통역치(Pressure Pain Threshold, PPT) 개선 근거는 약하다.
> - **마스티케이션 직접 근거는 단 1편** — Koole 2019(전향 임상, n=121: 구강안면통증 60 + 대조 61): vapocoolant 양 볼 분사 후 MMO 즉시 증가, 통증군>대조군(P<.001), 여성>남성. 단 즉시효과만 측정, 무작위배정·sham 없음.
> - **무작위 대조군(RCT) 근거는 부위가 승모근(목)** — 마스티케이션 분사신장 RCT가 존재하지 않아, 전이가능(transferable) 기법 근거로 분류.
> - Ustun 2024 RCT(n=60, 상부 승모근): 분사신장 vs 건침(Dry Needling) 3주 — 두 군 모두 통증·기능·sEMG·초음파 호전, 분사신장이 압통역치·경부장애지수(Neck Disability Index, NDI)에서 건침보다 **우수**(P<.008, P<.028). → 분사신장은 건침에 비열등.
> - Martín-Pintado 2014 RCT(n=70 건강인, 잠복 트리거포인트): 분사신장이 건침후통증(postneedling soreness)을 줄였으나 **즉시 효과뿐, 6시간 후 소실**, PPT 무변화 — 효과의 시간적 천장을 설정.
> - **두 리뷰가 임상 위치를 정의**: Pal 2014(악안면, PMC 전문)는 분사신장을 교합스플린트·물리치료(초음파·TENS·LLLT)·트리거포인트 주사·근이완제·삼환계 항우울제·보툴리눔과 함께 2단계 알고리즘 안에 배치; Vázquez-Delgado 2010(구강안면통증 유닛)은 트리거포인트 치료 중 분사신장+직접주사를 **최선**으로 명시.
> - **임상 적용**: 분사신장은 ① 통증스러운 수동신장 없이 개구량을 즉시 늘리는 워밍업/보조, ② 다른 침습 처치(건침)의 후통증 완화 보조로 적합. 단독 근치요법이 아니라 운동치료·자가신장·행동/교합 치료와 병용해야 한다.
> - **예후·환자선택**: 만성 사례는 기분장애·불안 동반이 흔하고 예후가 나빠(Vázquez-Delgado 2010) 조기 진단·치료가 유리하며, 심리적 디스트레스가 처치후 통증 인식과 상관(Martín-Pintado 2014) → 다학제·심리사회 평가 병행.
> - **근거 한계 정직하게**: 마스티케이션 RCT 0편, 대부분 초록 기반, sham-통제 부재, 효과 단시간. 분사신장은 "저비용·저위험 보조기법"으로 권장 가능하나 고근거 단독요법으로 과대평가 금지. 더 강한 모달리티(운동치료 SR, 보툴리눔 SR+MA)가 위키에 별도 존재.

## One-line Summary

Synthesis of 5 wiki pages on spray-and-stretch (vapocoolant) for masticatory/myofascial trigger-point pain: the technique reliably produces an *immediate* increase in mouth opening and short-term pain/soreness relief, but masticatory-specific evidence is a single non-randomized study (Koole 2019), the only RCTs are in the trapezius (Ustun 2024 non-inferior to dry needling; Martín-Pintado 2014 effect <6 h), and orofacial reviews (Pal 2014, Vázquez-Delgado 2010) place it as a low-cost adjunct within a multimodal ladder — not a standalone cure.

## Scope

This page synthesizes the wiki's spray-and-stretch (분사신장) holdings as of 2026-06-21 — a deliberately small, sharply-bounded evidence base. Spray-and-stretch applies a **vapocoolant** (ethyl chloride / fluoromethane) over a muscle and its referral zone to reflexively lower muscle tone, allowing **passive stretch** and inactivation of a myofascial trigger point (Travell & Simons). The clinical question for this dental wiki: *what does spray-and-stretch actually do for masticatory myofascial pain / TMD, and where does it sit in management?*

## What the evidence supports

1. **An immediate, real effect on range of motion.** Koole 2019 showed vapocoolant spray to both cheeks increased maximal mouth opening in most subjects, with a larger relative gain in orofacial-pain patients than asymptomatic controls (P<.001). The clinical value: achieve the stretch benefit **without the discomfort of passive stretching**.

2. **At least non-inferiority to dry needling.** In the only modern head-to-head RCT (Ustun 2024, trapezius), cold-spray-stretching matched dry needling on within-group improvement and scored *higher* on pressure pain threshold (P<.008) and disability (P<.028) — credible efficacy for the technique, even if the muscle is not masticatory.

3. **A useful adjunct role.** Martín-Pintado 2014 shows spray-and-stretch can blunt the soreness *caused by* another trigger-point treatment (dry needling) — extending its utility beyond primary trigger-point therapy.

4. **Expert endorsement in the orofacial context.** Both reviews place spray-and-stretch among first-line physical modalities; Vázquez-Delgado 2010 goes furthest, naming spray-and-stretch + injection the *most effective* trigger-point options.

## What the evidence does NOT support (the honest boundary)

- **No masticatory RCT exists.** The strongest masticatory evidence (Koole 2019) is non-randomized, sham-free, and measures only the *immediate* effect. All RCTs are in the trapezius.
- **The effect is short-lived.** Martín-Pintado 2014 found the between-group benefit gone by 6 hours and **no change in pressure pain threshold** — consistent with a transient cutaneous-cooling/reflex mechanism, not durable tissue change.
- **Reviews are narrative, not graded.** Pal 2014 explicitly flags spray-and-stretch (and ultrasound) as resting on empirical rather than high-grade evidence; the "most effective" claim in Vázquez-Delgado 2010 is expert opinion, not pooled data.
- **Stronger modalities exist in the wiki.** Exercise/manual-therapy SRs ([[tmj/gonzalez-sanchez-2023-tmj-physiotherapy-treatment-sr]], [[tmj/zhu-2024-exercise-therapy-tmj-derangement-sr]]) and botulinum-toxin SR+MAs ([[tmj/saini-2024-botulinum-toxin-tmd-sr-ma]], [[tmj/zhu-2020-btx-painful-tmd-sr-ma]]) carry higher evidence weight for TMD.

## Clinical bottom line

Spray-and-stretch is best framed as a **low-cost, low-risk adjunct**, not a standalone cure for masticatory myofascial pain:

- **Use it** as (a) a comfortable warm-up to gain mouth opening before/around active stretching, and (b) an adjunct to reduce soreness after invasive trigger-point procedures.
- **Pair it** with exercise therapy, home self-stretching, and behavioral/occlusal management — the durable arms of TMD care.
- **Select patients** with attention to chronicity and psychosocial load: chronic cases with mood/anxiety carry poorer prognosis (Vázquez-Delgado 2010) and psychological distress correlates with post-treatment pain (Martín-Pintado 2014), so combine with multidisciplinary/psychosocial assessment.
- **Set expectations**: the relief is immediate but short-lived; repeat application and a multimodal plan are required for lasting benefit.

## Evidence gaps / future work

- A randomized, sham-controlled trial of spray-and-stretch on **masseter/temporalis** in DC/TMD-classified myofascial pain, with durable (weeks) outcomes — currently absent.
- Head-to-head vs exercise therapy and vs botulinum toxin in the masticatory system.
- Standardization of vapocoolant agent, spray distance, and stretch protocol for jaw muscles.
