> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - **핵심 명제**: 치간 치조정골 (interproximal crestal bone) 유지는 수직 위치가 아니라 **수평 간격 (horizontal spacing)** 이 지배한다. 인접 임플란트 사이 거리든 임플란트-자연치 거리든, 간격이 부족하면 측방 골소실 (lateral bone loss) 영역이 겹치면서 치조정골이 무너진다.
> - **3 mm 룰의 기원 (Tarnow 2000)**: 각 임플란트에서 약 1.4 mm의 측방 골소실이 발생하므로 두 영역이 겹치지 않으려면 임플란트간 거리 (inter-implant distance, IID) ≥3 mm 필요. IID ≤3 mm → 치조정 골소실 1.04 mm vs >3 mm → 0.45 mm (약 2.3배 차이).
> - **메커니즘 → 치간유두 (interdental papilla)**: 치조정골 높이가 유두 높이를 결정 → 간격 부족 시 골소실 → 유두 소실 → 심미 실패 (black triangle).
> - **3 mm 룰의 한계 (Morales Schwarz 2025)**: Tarnow의 데이터는 **외부 헥스 (external hex)·비-플랫폼스위칭 (non-platform-switching)** 구식 임플란트 기반. 현대 임플란트(내부 원추형 연결부, 플랫폼 스위칭, 오목형 지대주, 치조정하 식립)에서는 2 mm IID도 동등, 심지어 **1 mm IID에서 10년 골 유지** 케이스 보고.
> - **단, n=1**: Morales Schwarz는 단일 케이스(흡연자) + 동물실험 2편 — 가이드라인 변경 근거로는 불충분. **증거의 무게는 여전히 ≥3 mm** 권고를 지지.
> - **임플란트-자연치 측 (Joshi 2025 SR+MA)**: 11편·1,727명·임플란트 2,000개+. <1.5 mm 근접 시 **인접치 치수/치근단 병변 20–25%** (접촉 케이스), 치간 골소실 최대 4%. 그러나 **임플란트 생존율은 >95%로 일관**.
> - **위험의 비대칭성**: 임플란트-자연치 근접은 임플란트가 아니라 **옆 치아를 위협**한다 — 임플란트는 살아남지만 인접치 치수가 죽거나 우식이 생긴다.
> - **임플란트 유형 변수 (Ng 2018)**: <1 mm 근접의 해악은 **골수준 임플란트 (bone-level, BL)** 에서만 유의 (BOP↑, 치간 골소실↑); **조직수준 임플란트 (tissue-level, TL)** 는 근접 영향 없음 — IAJ(임플란트-지대주 연결부) 미세간극이 골 가까이 있느냐가 관건.
> - **임상 임계값**: 임플란트-임플란트 ≥3 mm (현대 디자인이면 2 mm까지 방어 가능), 임플란트-치근 표면 ≥1.5 mm. 중심-치근 거리로 환산 시 ≥1.5 mm + 임플란트 반경.
> - **임상 takeaway**: 간격이 부족하면 (1) 임플란트 디자인을 BL→TL 또는 플랫폼스위칭+내부원추형으로 전환하고, (2) 더 좁은 직경 임플란트를 선택하며, (3) 임플란트-자연치 근접 시 인접치 치수 생활력을 사전·사후 모니터링한다.

## One-line Summary

Synthesis of 4 papers establishing that horizontal spacing governs interproximal crestal bone: inter-implant distance ≥3 mm (Tarnow 2000; modern designs may tolerate down to 1–2 mm — Morales Schwarz 2025, n=1) and implant-to-tooth distance ≥1.5 mm (Joshi 2025 SR+MA; Ng 2018) minimize crestal bone loss, peri-implant inflammation, and adjacent-tooth pulpal damage.

## 한줄요약

4편 종합: 치간 치조정골은 수평 간격이 지배 — 임플란트간 거리 ≥3 mm (Tarnow 2000; 현대 디자인은 1–2 mm까지 가능 — Morales Schwarz 2025, n=1)와 임플란트-치근 거리 ≥1.5 mm (Joshi 2025 SR+MA; Ng 2018)가 치조정 골소실·주위염·인접치 치수손상을 최소화한다.

## Summary

**Thesis — horizontal spacing, not vertical position, governs interproximal crestal bone.** Across these four papers a single mechanism recurs: each fixture (implant or natural tooth root) carries its own zone of *lateral* (horizontal) crestal bone loss around the implant-abutment junction. When two such zones sit too close, they overlap and the interproximal bone between them collapses. This is why spacing — not insertion depth or implant length — is the dominant determinant of papilla support and esthetic outcome at the interproximal site.

**Implant-to-implant side.** Tarnow et al. (2000) first quantified the lateral component: ~1.34 mm (mesial) and ~1.40 mm (distal) of horizontal bone loss per implant. With each implant losing ~1.4 mm laterally, two implants must sit ≥2.8 mm apart to keep the zones from overlapping — hence the clinical **≥3 mm inter-implant distance (IID) rule**. Their data show interimplant crestal bone loss of 1.04 mm at IID ≤3 mm versus 0.45 mm at IID >3 mm (~2.3× difference), and because papilla height tracks bone crest height, IID <3 mm predicts papilla loss. **Caveat:** Tarnow's implants were external-hex, non-platform-switched, bone-level fixtures — a pre-modern design. Morales Schwarz et al. (2025) report a single case where two modern implants (internal conical 24° connection, 0.25 mm platform-switching, etched-to-collar surface, ~1.5 mm subcrestal placement, concave abutments) placed at a CBCT-confirmed **1.02 mm IID** maintained bone 1.40 mm coronal to the implant-abutment connection at 10 years, with a recovered papilla. Two supporting animal studies (rabbit, dog) found 1 mm IID equal or superior to wider distances. But this is n=1 in a smoker with imperfect hygiene; the authors themselves state the ≥3 mm guideline cannot be revised on one case. The honest reading: modern connection/platform design *can* defend narrow gaps, but the **weight of evidence still favors ≥3 mm**, with ~2 mm a reasonable modern floor when design factors are optimized and space is constrained.

**Implant-to-tooth side.** Joshi et al. (2025), a PRISMA SR+MA of 11 studies (1,727 patients, >2,000 implants), establishes the analogous tooth-side margin. The defining finding is an **asymmetry of risk**: implant survival stays consistently >95%, but the *adjacent tooth* is what suffers — pulpal/periapical pathology in 20–25% of contact cases (e.g., Kucukkurt 2020: 20.5% RCT, 2.3% extraction, yet 96.4% implant survival) and marginal bone loss/peri-implantitis in up to 4% of closely placed implants (pooled effect size 0.11, 95% CI 0.03–0.22, I²=95%). The recommended safety margin is **≥1.5 mm implant-surface-to-root-surface**. Ng et al. (2018), a key included study (98 patients, 195 implants, 37.8 months), adds the critical implant-type modifier: horizontal distance <1 mm increased BOP and interproximal bone loss **only for bone-level (BL) implants** — tissue-level (TL) implants showed no significant proximity effect. The mechanism mirrors the implant-implant case: in BL implants the IAJ microgap sits at the bone crest, so proximity compounds inflammation; in TL implants the polished collar holds the microgap away from bone, neutralizing the proximity penalty.

### Spacing thresholds

| Relationship | Minimum horizontal distance | Source | Mechanistic / clinical basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| Implant ↔ implant (classic rule) | **≥3 mm** (platform-to-platform) | Tarnow 2000 | ~1.4 mm lateral loss × 2 ≈ 2.8 mm → ≥3 mm prevents overlap; preserves interimplant papilla |
| Implant ↔ implant (modern designs) | ~2 mm acceptable; 1 mm maintainable in select cases | Morales Schwarz 2025 (n=1) + 2 animal studies | Internal conical + platform-switching + concave abutment + subcrestal placement defend the gap; n=1 → does not overturn ≥3 mm |
| Implant surface ↔ tooth root surface | **≥1.5 mm** | Joshi 2025 SR+MA | <1.5 mm → adjacent-tooth pulpal damage 20–25% (contact cases), MBL up to 4%; implant survival unaffected (>95%) |
| Implant center ↔ tooth root surface | **≥1.5 mm + implant radius** (e.g., Ø4.0 → ≥3.5 mm) | Joshi 2025 SR+MA | Convert surface margin to center for planning on a Ø-specified fixture |
| BL implant ↔ tooth root (type-specific floor) | ≥1 mm critical threshold (still target ≥1.5 mm) | Ng 2018 | <1 mm in BL implants → ↑BOP, ↑interproximal bone loss; TL implants not significantly affected |

### Clinical decision / planning framework

| Planning scenario | Constraint | Recommended action |
|---|---|---|
| Two adjacent implants, ample M–D space | Space ≥ 2× Ø + 6 mm | Place at **≥3 mm IID** (Tarnow); standard, safest for papilla |
| Two adjacent implants, restricted M–D space | Cannot achieve 3 mm | Select narrow-diameter implants + modern connection (internal conical + platform-switching + concave abutment + subcrestal). Down to ~2 mm IID is evidence-supported; 1 mm only as a documented exception (Morales Schwarz), not a default |
| Implant beside a natural tooth | Need ≥1.5 mm to root surface | Plan implant center ≥ (1.5 mm + radius) from root; verify on CBCT; angulate away from convergent roots |
| Implant beside a tooth, BL fixture chosen | <1.5 mm unavoidable | Prefer switching to a **TL implant** (Ng 2018: proximity penalty neutralized) or accept higher peri-implant inflammation risk |
| Any close implant-tooth case | Adjacent tooth at risk, not the implant | **Test and document pulp vitality pre-op and at follow-up**; warn patient of 20–25% pulpal-complication risk in true contact cases (Joshi) |
| Esthetic zone, papilla critical | Bone crest height = papilla height | Maximize IID toward 3 mm; bone crest preservation is the lever for papilla, per Tarnow's bone–papilla coupling |

**Bottom line:** interproximal crestal bone — and therefore papilla and esthetics — is a *horizontal spacing* problem. Honor ≥3 mm between implants and ≥1.5 mm to a tooth as the default; modern BL connection/platform design can buy back some space (down to ~2 mm IID, exceptionally 1 mm) but does not abolish the rule, and on the tooth side the principal casualty of crowding is the neighboring tooth's pulp, not the implant.
