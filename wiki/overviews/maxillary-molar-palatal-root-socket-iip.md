## 한국어 핵심요약

> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - **핵심 명제**: 상악 대구치 즉시식립(Immediate Implant Placement, IIP)에서 1차 선택은 치근간 격벽(Inter-Radicular Septum, IRS) 정착이지만, 격벽이 불충분할 때(Smith-Tarnow Type B/C) **구개 치근와(Palatal Root Socket)가 가장 신뢰할 수 있는 대안 정착부**다.
> - **왜 구개 치근인가**: 세 치근 중 구개 치근이 가장 굵고 깊어 1차 안정성(primary stability) 확보에 유리하고, 식립축이 자연스럽게 협측 치조정에서 ≥2 mm 구개측으로 향해 협측 골판 보존 원칙과 일치한다.
> - **빈도 근거**: CBCT상 직경 ≥5 mm 임플란트를 완전히 담을 격벽은 상악 1대구치 61.7%·2대구치 34%뿐(Deporter 2021) — 즉 다수 증례에서 격벽 단독 정착이 불가해 구개 치근와가 현실적 해법.
> - **생역학적 근거**: 쥐 구개치근와 모델에서 **저변형률(low strain) + 치주인대(Periodontal Ligament, PDL) 유래 Wnt 반응세포**가 골유착을 구동하고, 고변형률은 골세포사·섬유성 캡슐화를 유발(Yuan 2018) — 구개 치근와의 안정적 정착이 골유착에 유리한 이유.
> - **생존율**: 대구치 즉시식립 전반의 통합 생존율은 ~97%로 지연식립과 동등(Ragucci 2020 SR+MA); 즉시 vs 지연 식립 생존 차이 없음(Patel 2023 SR+MA, RR 0.99).
> - **안정성 거동**: 가이드 즉시 대구치 임플란트는 상·하악 모두 1차 ISQ >73이나, **상악은 2–4주에 stability dip**이 있고 24주 ISQ가 하악보다 낮다(78.2 vs 81.5, Witoonkitvanich 2025) → 상악 구치 조기부하는 보수적으로.
> - **최대 제약 = 상악동**: 구개 치근 첨부가 세 치근 중 **상악동에 가장 깊이 침범**(−2.2 mm, Matsuda 2016; 1대구치 구개근 동 침범률 28.85%, Deporter 2021) → 경치조골 상악동거상술(transcrestal sinus elevation) 동반이 흔히 필요.
> - **연조직/개방창**: 대구치 발치와는 임플란트 주위 잔존 gap이 커 개방창 치유가 되며, 반응성 연조직(Reactive Soft Tissue, RST)과 흡수성 콜라겐 스폰지(Absorbable Collagen Sponge, ACS)가 ~6개월 치유에 동등(Deng 2024).
> - **보철 응용**: All-on-X 전악 보철에서 구개 치근와 식립은 **후방 cantilever를 제거**하는 원위 지대주 확보 전략으로 활용(Yaghmai 2025).
> - **결정 틀**: ① 술전 CBCT 필수 → ② 격벽 충분(Type A)이면 격벽 정착 → ③ 불충분하면 구개 치근와 평가(직경·길이·동 거리) → ④ 동 근접 시 경치조골 거상 병행 → ⑤ 보철축·1차 안정성(≥35 Ncm) 확인 후 부하 결정. 구개치근와 단독 정착의 직접 근거는 아직 **증례 수준**(Liu 2024, Yaghmai 2025)임을 명시.

## One-line Summary

Cross-paper synthesis on using the palatal root socket as the primary anchorage for maxillary molar immediate implants when the inter-radicular septum is inadequate: anatomy/eligibility (CBCT), biomechanical rationale (low-strain + PDL-derived osseointegration), stability behavior, the sinus-proximity constraint, open-wound soft-tissue healing, and the All-on-X cantilever-avoidance application — survival ~97% (delayed-equivalent) but palatal-root single-anchorage direct evidence remains case-level.

## Summary

Maxillary molar extraction sockets rarely allow a "drop-in" implant: the three diverging roots leave a tri-radiate socket whose only solid pre-existing bone is the **inter-radicular septum (IRS)**. The standard plan (Smith & Tarnow 2013) is to engage the IRS for primary stability (Type A). But CBCT shows the IRS is frequently too small — only **61.7 % of first molars and 34 % of second molars** can fully contain a ≥5 mm implant in the septum (Deporter 2021). When the septum fails (Smith Type B/C), the **palatal root socket** becomes the most reliable alternative anchorage because it is the widest and deepest of the three roots, and engaging it naturally places the implant body palatally — consistent with the ≥2 mm-palatal buccal-bone-preservation principle used across immediate placement.

This page synthesizes the anatomy, biomechanics, stability, sinus constraint, soft-tissue healing, and prosthetic application of the palatal-root-socket approach.

## 1. Anatomy & eligibility (CBCT-driven)

| Parameter | First molar | Second molar | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| IRS able to contain ≥5 mm implant | 61.7 % | 34 % | Deporter 2021 |
| Furcation-to-sinus-floor distance (FSD) | 9.69 mm | 8.84 mm | Deporter 2021 |
| Palatal-root apex into sinus | deepest of 3 roots (−2.2 mm) | — | Matsuda 2016 |
| Palatal-root sinus intrusion rate | 28.85 % | — | Deporter 2021 |
| Buccal / palatal plate thickness | 1.58 / 1.34 mm | — | Matsuda 2016 |

Matsuda 2016 proposes a 3-tier (favorable / intermediate / contraindicated) framework, classifying ~32 % of first-molar sites as contraindicated for straightforward IIP — these are exactly the sites where palatal-root anchorage (± sinus elevation) is the salvage path. Milenkovic 2022 and Smith 2013 provide the septum-shape and A/B/C classifications that decide whether septal or palatal-root anchorage is chosen.

## 2. Biomechanical rationale (why the palatal socket integrates)

Yuan 2018 (murine first-molar palatal-root-socket model) is the mechanistic anchor: osteotomy in the palatal socket that preserves the buccal PDL shows **low interfacial strain + PDL-derived Wnt-responsive cells driving osseointegration**, whereas high strain causes osteocyte death and fibrous encapsulation. This explains clinically why the bulky palatal root socket — which lets the implant achieve solid apical/palatal engagement at controlled micromotion — favors bone rather than fibrous integration.

## 3. Stability behavior & survival

- **Survival**: molar IIP pooled survival ~97–98 %, delayed-equivalent, provided primary stability is achieved (Ragucci 2020 SR+MA). Immediate vs delayed placement overall: no survival difference (Patel 2023 SR+MA, RR 0.99, I²=0 %).
- **ISQ trajectory**: guided immediate molar implants reach primary ISQ >73 in both arches, but the **maxilla shows a 2–4 week stability dip** and a lower 24-week ISQ than the mandible (78.2 vs 81.5, P=0.018; Witoonkitvanich 2025). → counsel conservative loading for maxillary molars.
- Insertion torque ≥35 Ncm remains the better immediate-loading gate than ISQ alone in fresh sockets (see [[implants/isq/bavetta-2019-insertion-torque-isq-immediate-loading-extraction-socket]]).

## 4. The dominant constraint — sinus proximity

The palatal root apex is the closest of the three roots to the sinus floor (Matsuda 2016; Deporter 2021). Driving the osteotomy deep into the palatal socket for length frequently engages or perforates the sinus floor, so **transcrestal (osteotome / osseodensification) sinus elevation is commonly required** when FSD <10 mm. See [[sinus-lift/transcrestal/wu-2023-dynamic-navigation-transcrestal-sinus-floor]] and the [[overviews/short-implant-vs-sinus-augmentation-decision]] page.

## 5. Soft-tissue / open-wound healing

Molar sockets leave a residual peri-implant gap that heals as an open wound. Deng 2024 (retrospective, 40 posterior immediate implants) found **reactive soft tissue (RST) and absorbable collagen sponge (ACS) equivalent** for soft- and hard-tissue healing at ~6 months (ACS better color, RST better fibrous repair) — either is acceptable for sealing the posterior open wound.

## 6. Prosthetic application — All-on-X cantilever avoidance

Yaghmai 2025 reframes the palatal-root socket not just as a single-tooth salvage but as a **full-arch distal-support strategy**: placing an implant into the maxillary molar palatal socket provides posterior support that eliminates a distal cantilever in All-on-X rehabilitations.

## Decision tree

```
Maxillary molar to be extracted → pre-op CBCT (mandatory)
│
├─ IRS adequate (Smith Type A, ≥5 mm containable) ──► septal anchorage (standard IIP)
│
└─ IRS inadequate (Smith Type B/C) ──► evaluate PALATAL ROOT SOCKET
       │  measure: palatal-root width/length, FSD, apex-to-sinus
       │
       ├─ FSD ≥10 mm & adequate palatal bone ──► palatal-root anchorage, check primary stability (≥35 Ncm)
       │
       └─ FSD <10 mm / sinus intrusion ──► palatal-root anchorage + transcrestal sinus elevation
                                              │
                                              ▼
                 primary stability ≥35 Ncm & favorable prosthetic axis?
                   ├─ yes → consider immediate provisional (out of occlusion); maxilla = conservative
                   └─ no  → submerge / conventional loading + open-wound seal (RST or ACS)
```

## Bottom line

The palatal root socket is the **default fallback anchorage** for maxillary molar IIP when the inter-radicular septum is inadequate — anatomically the widest/deepest root, biomechanically favorable (low-strain, PDL-driven osseointegration), and consistent with palatal positioning. Overall molar-IIP survival is ~97 % (delayed-equivalent), but **direct evidence for palatal-root single-anchorage specifically remains case-level** (Liu 2024, Yaghmai 2025). The make-or-break variables are primary stability (≥35 Ncm), the near-universal need to assess/elevate the sinus, the maxillary 2–4 week stability dip (conservative loading), and prosthetic axis control.
