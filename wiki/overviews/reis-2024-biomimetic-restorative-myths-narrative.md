## Summary
Critical narrative review (J Appl Oral Sci 2024) from Reis/Loguercio group challenging 9 "biomimetic restorative dentistry" (BRD) protocols. Authors performed RCT/SR-focused literature search on PubMed/Embase/Cochrane CENTRAL for each protocol and concluded most lack high-level evidence of superiority over standard adhesive dentistry techniques. Names BRD proponents (David Alleman, Pascal Magne, Bio-Emulation Group™, Academy of Biomimetic Dentistry) and identifies cognitive biases (novelty, authority) driving adoption.

## Key Contributions
- Rare evidence-skeptical voice in BRD literature dominated by case reports and laboratory studies
- Provides RCT/SR-anchored verdict for each of 9 BRD protocols
- Identifies cognitive biases driving BRD adoption
- Useful as counterweight reference for clinical decision-making

## Methodology
- **Design**: Critical narrative review with literature search
- **Strategy**: RCT and SR search on PubMed/Embase/Cochrane CENTRAL per BRD protocol
- **Structure**: Origins of BRD → cognitive biases → protocol-by-protocol evidence

## Results — 9 BRD Protocols Evaluated

| # | Protocol | Reis et al. verdict | Counter-evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Caries-detector dyes | Excessively removes carious dentin | — |
| 2 | Aluminum oxide air abrasion | Overtreatment; occupational health risk | — |
| 3 | Enamel beveling (posterior) | Technically difficult; no advantage over butt-joint | — |
| 4 | Chlorhexidine MMP inactivation | No clinical evidence of improved longevity | Lab data supports MMP inhibition; HL stability gains in vitro |
| 5 | "Elected" gold-standard adhesives | No better than other good systems | — |
| 6 | Immediate dentin sealing (IDS) | Similar post-op sensitivity and longevity to DDS clinically | In vitro bond strength superior (Hardan 2022) |
| 7 | Deep margin elevation (DME) | Viable alternative for subgingival margins (SUPPORTED) | — |
| 8 | "Decoupling" with time | Lacks scientific evidence | — |
| 9 | Fiber inserts on pulpal floor/axial wall | No benefits over alternatives | — |

## Clinical Implications
- Consider BRD claims with skepticism unless RCT-level evidence exists for the specific outcome
- Validated by this paper: deep margin elevation for subgingival margins
- Challenged: caries dye use, posterior enamel beveling, fiber inserts, "decoupling", "elected" adhesive worship
- Unsettled (in vitro support, clinical doubt): IDS and CHX MMP inactivation
- Standard adhesive dentistry techniques (well-executed) likely match BRD outcomes in clinical practice

## Bias Disclosure
The Reis/Loguercio group has a long-standing position critical of biomimetic claims. Reading this alongside in vitro evidence (Hardan 2021/2022, Breschi 2025) and biomimetic-positive narrative (Samartzi 2021) gives a balanced view.

## Steelman of BRD position
The strongest version of BRD argument: (1) traditional restorative dentistry has high failure rates from poor adhesion and excess stress; (2) integrating bond strength, marginal seal, pulp vitality, polymerization stress reduction as paradigms is sound; (3) some protocols (DME, MMP inhibition) have plausible mechanistic basis even if RCT data lag. Reis et al. concede DME and acknowledge the underlying paradigms align with contemporary adhesive dentistry — the dispute is over specific protocol claims, not the framework.
