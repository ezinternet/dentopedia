> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - **핵심 결론**: 원격치과(Teledentistry)는 스크리닝·의뢰·모니터링 단계에서는 진단정확도가 충분히 높지만, 근거의 질(質)이 일관되게 낮고 진짜 병목은 정확도가 아니라 **환자 수용성(patient uptake)**이다 — "정확함 ≠ 채택됨".
> - **의뢰·진단치료계획 정확도**(Gurgel-Juarez 2022, 6개 SR, >7,000명): 민감도(sensitivity) 80–88% / 특이도(specificity) 73–95%로 대면검사에 필적 — 단, 원격·대면 평가자의 교육·경력이 비슷할 때만.
> - **우식 스크리닝 정확도**(Lee 2024, 호주 SR+MA, 18편): 평균 민감도 69.7% / 특이도 97.4% — 특이도는 높지만(위양성 적음) 민감도가 중간 수준이라 병소의 ~30%를 놓칠 수 있어 **확진 대면검사를 대체하지 못한다**.
> - **비용**: 4편 모두 비용절감을 시사하지만 Scheerman 2024(10개 SR)는 경제성 근거가 단 **5개 원논문**에만 의존하고 cost-benefit·cost-utility·증분분석(incremental analysis)이 전무 — 비용효과성은 **발견이 아니라 가정(assumption)**.
> - **근거의 질**: Gurgel-Juarez의 6개 SR은 AMSTAR-2 low(1)/critically-low(5), Scheerman의 10개 SR은 전부 low/critically-low — 전 분야가 낮은 방법론 품질 위에 서 있다.
> - **수용성 분열**: 환자는 대체로 긍정적, 임상의(clinician)는 의견이 갈림(mixed) — Lee 2024에서 명확.
> - **García 역설**(N=91 농촌 라티노): 가용성이 있어도 **57%가 무관심**, 가구소득 >$24k와 **치과보험 미보유**(uninsured가 오히려 더 수용적)만 유의 — 자각된 구강건강 필요는 무관.
> - **형평성 위험(equity risk)**: 단순히 서비스를 깔아도 언어·문화적응(acculturation) 장벽이 있는 농촌 소수집단은 배제될 수 있어, 설계·홍보·운영에 보건형평성을 **명시적으로** 통합해야 한다.
> - **적합 적응증**: 트리아지(triage)·스크리닝·의뢰·원격 모니터링 — 비동기(asynchronous, store-and-forward) + 스마트폰 촬영이 가장 실현 가능하고 저렴; 확진 진단·치료는 대면.
> - **활용 시나리오**: 지방·농어촌·오지(rural/remote) 접근성 향상 + COVID-19로 가속 — 단 사용자가 채택하지 않으면 접근격차는 그대로다.
> - **임상 시사점**: teledentistry는 대면치과의 **보조 게이트키퍼**로 도입하되, 진단정확도 수치만 믿지 말고 환자(특히 취약계층)의 채택·언어·신뢰 장벽을 함께 설계할 것.

## One-line Summary

Across 4 reviews/surveys (2 umbrella reviews, 1 SR+MA, 1 cross-sectional), teledentistry is diagnostically adequate for screening/referral/monitoring (referral/treatment-planning sensitivity 80–88%, specificity 73–95%; caries-screening sensitivity 69.7%, specificity 97.4%) and widely assumed cost-saving, but every supporting review is AMSTAR-2 low/critically-low and the binding constraint is patient uptake, not accuracy — 57% of rural Latina/o/x adults declined teledentistry even when available.

## 한줄요약

리뷰·설문 4편(우산형 리뷰 2, SR+MA 1, 단면조사 1) 종합: 원격치과는 스크리닝·의뢰·모니터링에 진단적으로 충분하고(의뢰·치료계획 민감도 80–88%·특이도 73–95%, 우식 스크리닝 민감도 69.7%·특이도 97.4%) 비용절감으로 널리 가정되지만, 뒷받침 리뷰가 전부 AMSTAR-2 low/critically-low 품질이고 진짜 제약은 정확도가 아니라 환자 채택률이다 — 농촌 라티노 성인의 57%는 가용해도 거부했다.

## Thesis

Teledentistry's case rests on two empirical pillars and one recurring caveat:

1. **Diagnostic accuracy is good enough for the upstream care pathway** (triage, screening, referral, treatment planning, monitoring) — but not for confirmatory diagnosis. Gurgel-Juarez (2022) reports referral/diagnostic-treatment-planning sensitivity 80–88% and specificity 73–95%; Lee (2024) pools caries-screening at sensitivity 69.7% / specificity 97.4%. The high specificity / moderate-or-variable sensitivity pattern is consistent: teledentistry rules cases *in* reliably but misses a meaningful fraction of true lesions, so it screens and refers rather than replaces the in-person exam.

2. **Cost-saving is plausible but unproven.** All four sources point toward cost savings, yet Scheerman (2024) shows the economic evidence is an *assumption*, not a finding: across 10 reviews only 5 distinct economic-evaluation primary studies exist, and none is a cost-benefit, cost-utility, or incremental analysis.

3. **The evidence quality is uniformly weak, and accuracy ≠ adoption.** Every supporting systematic review is AMSTAR-2 low or critically low (Gurgel-Juarez: 1 low + 5 critically low; Scheerman: all 10 low/critically low). And the demand side is the real bottleneck — García (2023) found 57% of rural Latina/o/x adults declined teledentistry even when offered, with cost-related enabling factors (income, lacking insurance) — not perceived oral-health need — predicting acceptability. A diagnostically capable, possibly cheaper modality still fails if the target population will not use it.

**Bottom line**: deploy teledentistry as an adjunct gatekeeper (triage/screening/referral/monitoring) layered on top of in-person dentistry, treat the published accuracy/cost numbers as provisional given low review quality, and design explicitly for patient uptake and health equity — otherwise the access promise is not realized.

## Clinical Decision Points

**When teledentistry fits (use it):**
- **Triage / screening / referral** — the evidence-supported core use case. High specificity means few false positives, so a positive remote screen reliably warrants in-person follow-up; moderate sensitivity (caries 69.7%) means a negative remote screen does not exclude disease.
- **Treatment monitoring / supervision** — supported (e.g. remote orthodontic monitoring comparable to in-person in Gurgel-Juarez), suitable for follow-up of an already-diagnosed, already-planned case.
- **Rural / regional / remote access** — the strongest real-world rationale (Lee 2024); teledentistry extends reach where in-person dental supply is thin.

**When teledentistry does NOT fit (route to in-person):**
- **Confirmatory diagnosis and definitive treatment** — accuracy is screening-grade, not diagnosis-grade; ~30% of caries lesions may be missed remotely. Confirm and treat in person.
- **No treatment-efficacy evidence** exists for teledentistry itself; do not substitute it for hands-on therapeutic procedures.

**Synchronous vs asynchronous:**
- **Asynchronous (store-and-forward) with smartphone image capture** is the most feasible, flexible, and least-costly configuration (Gurgel-Juarez; Scheerman) and dominated screening/diagnostic studies (~86%). Default to this for screening/referral.
- **Real-time (synchronous)** is used more for treatment/consultation and reported as better on some cost outcomes in one review (Joshi 2020) — reserve for interactive consultation where dialogue matters.

**Accuracy moderator to control:** remote-assessor education/experience should match the in-person standard; accuracy degrades with mid-level or mismatched assessors (Gurgel-Juarez).

**Equity integration (do not skip):** availability alone does not produce uptake. García shows majority non-interest in a rural Latino population, with language/acculturation barriers concentrated among the non-receptive. Build in bilingual/bicultural workforce, equity-centered design, community partnerships, and targeted marketing — otherwise teledentistry can widen, not narrow, access inequity.

## Gaps & Future Research

- **Methodological quality is the central limitation.** Every supporting systematic review is AMSTAR-2 low or critically low; teledentistry trails telemedicine in economic evidence (Scheerman). Conclusions are provisional.
- **RCTs and high-quality experimental designs are needed**, especially for long-term clinical effectiveness — neither umbrella review found conclusive long-term outcome data, and treatment-efficacy outcomes are essentially absent (Gurgel-Juarez).
- **Real economic evaluation is missing.** Cost-saving claims rest on ~5 primary studies and no cost-benefit/cost-utility/incremental analysis exists (Scheerman); proper health-economic studies are required before cost-effectiveness can be asserted.
- **Acceptability / demand-side research is underdeveloped.** García is a small (N=91) descriptive convenience sample with no multivariable modeling; larger, predictive, multi-population acceptability studies are needed, particularly in underserved and special-needs groups (no experimental effectiveness study exists for disability / mental illness / institutionalized / older populations despite high disease burden).
- **Terminology is inconsistent** (m-health / tele-oral-medicine / telehealth); standardized definitions (e.g. the IADR e-oral-health-network consensus) would make future synthesis comparable.
