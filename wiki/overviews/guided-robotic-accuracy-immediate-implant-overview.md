## 한국어 핵심요약

> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - 즉시 식립(Immediate Implant Placement, IIP)에서의 가이드·로봇 정확도 종합 — 정적 컴퓨터보조수술 (static CAIS, sCAIS), 동적 컴퓨터보조수술 (dynamic CAIS, dCAIS), 로봇 컴퓨터보조수술 (robotic CAIS, rCAIS)을 freehand와 비교한 7편(NMA 2편 + RCT/prospective/retrospective 5편).
> - 한 줄 결론: IIP에서 "guided > freehand"는 **근거강함** — 발치와(socket)의 빈 공간이 드릴을 최소저항 경로(구개측 사면→순측 편위)로 밀어 freehand 편차가 특히 커지므로 IIP일수록 guided 이득이 크다.
> - 프로토콜 순위(Nava 2026 NMA, 18연구·780 IIP): 각도·첨부 기준 rCAIS > dCAIS > 완전가이드 정적(FG-sCAIS) > 반가이드 정적(HG-sCAIS) > freehand.
> - 절대 수치(freehand 대비 감소, Nava 2026): rCAIS 각도 −3.36°·apex −1.43 mm, dCAIS 각도 −2.66°·apex −1.32 mm — 즉 로봇·동적이 각도를 약 3° 가까이 줄인다.
> - 고품질 근거에서 차이 축소: RCT-only 민감도분석에서는 guided 프로토콜 간 차이가 불명확해지고 rCAIS arm 자체가 빠진다 → robotic 우위는 가설생성(hypothesis-generating) 수준, CINeMA 전반 certainty는 **LOW**.
> - 추적장치 통제의 함정: 로봇의 재현 가능한 이점은 동일 추적장치로 통제하면 **각도(angular)**에 국한되고, 선형(플랫폼·첨부) 편차는 dCAIS와 통계적으로 동등해진다(Wei 2025 RCT).
> - dCAIS vs rCAIS: Nava NMA에서 플랫폼·첨부 편차는 두 군 간 유의차 없음 — 로봇의 추가 이득은 주로 각도 안정성.
> - 임상 절대 기준점: 전상악 가이드 IIP의 현실적 편차는 각도 ~4.5°·플랫폼 ~0.9 mm·apex ~1.5 mm 수준(Fettouh 2026, 단일군 26증례) — 이는 guided-surgery SR+MA 벤치마크(entry ~1 mm, apex ~1.6 mm, axis 5–6°)와 부합.
> - immediate vs healed 뉘앙스: healed-ridge NMA(robotic-vs-navigation overview)와 IIP-전용 NMA(Nava 2026)가 같은 결론 — "guided가 FH보다 낫고, robotic이 dynamic보다 낫다는 결론은 잠정적". IIP는 freehand 편위 위험이 더 커서 guided 우선순위가 한 단계 올라간다.
> - 임상 takeaway: 초보 술자, 전치부/심미존, 순측 골판이 얇거나 open-ended socket처럼 편위 위험이 큰 IIP일수록 dCAIS 이상을 우선. 단순 후방 단일치·숙련 술자라면 FG-sCAIS로 충분할 수 있고, rCAIS의 비용·학습곡선·시술시간 증가를 정당화할 hard-outcome(생존·변연골소실·보철적합) 근거는 아직 없음.

## One-line Summary

Across two immediate-implant-specific network meta-analyses plus robot-vs-navigation trials, every guided protocol (static/dynamic/robotic CAIS) significantly beats freehand on angular, platform, and apex deviation in immediate implant placement — the guided advantage is largest precisely because the empty extraction socket pushes freehand drills off-axis — while robotic vs dynamic superiority remains hypothesis-generating (CINeMA LOW; the robot's reproducible edge narrows to angular control once the optical tracker is held constant).

## 한줄요약

즉시식립 전용 NMA 2편 + 로봇/내비 임상시험 종합: IIP에서 모든 guided(static/dynamic/robotic CAIS)가 freehand 대비 각도·플랫폼·첨부 편차를 유의하게 줄이며(발치와 빈 공간이 freehand 드릴을 편위시키므로 IIP에서 이득이 가장 큼), robotic > dynamic 우위는 잠정적(CINeMA LOW; 동일 추적장치로 통제하면 로봇 이점은 각도에 국한).

## Thesis

Immediate implant placement (IIP) is the worst-case scenario for freehand drilling: the residual extraction socket presents no apical bone wall on the palatal/lingual slope, so the drill follows the path of least resistance and slips facially (anterior maxilla) or into the septum/furcation (molars). Guided surgery exists precisely to remove this degree of freedom, which is why the IIP-specific evidence shows a *larger* freehand-vs-guided gap than healed-ridge studies.

The two IIP-specific NMAs agree on the robust claim and disagree only on the speculative one. **Schiavon 2025** (RCT-only, 7 trials, 338 implants) establishes that CAIS — partial-static, full-static, and dynamic — consistently beats freehand on angular, coronal, and apical deviation, but is too small to separate the guided subtypes and never tested robots. **Nava 2026** (18 studies, 780 IIP, admitting non-RCT designs) reproduces "guided ≫ freehand" and additionally ranks the protocols — rCAIS > dCAIS > FG-sCAIS > HG-sCAIS > FH on angular/apex — but at the cost of CINeMA LOW certainty, and its own RCT-only sensitivity analysis blurs the guided-vs-guided distinctions and drops rCAIS entirely.

The robot-vs-navigation trials (`yu-2025`, `wei-2025`, `chen-2025`, `wu-2024`, mostly healed ridges) explain *why* the robotic ranking is fragile: in uncontrolled comparisons the robot halves all three deviation metrics, but the one RCT equalising the optical tracker across arms (Wei 2025) finds the robot's reproducible benefit is **angular control only** — coronal/apical deviation become equivalent to dynamic navigation. This is consistent with Nava's finding that rCAIS and dCAIS do not differ on platform/apex deviation.

[claude해석] The defensible synthesis: in IIP, *get off freehand* — that is the high-certainty, high-impact decision. The escalation from full-static to dynamic to robotic buys progressively more **angular** stability (useful when prosthetically-driven axis is tight or the socket actively deflects the drill), but the marginal linear-accuracy gain shrinks and is not yet linked to any hard clinical outcome. **Fettouh 2026** supplies an absolute anchor for what good guided IIP actually achieves in the anterior maxilla (≈4.5° / 0.9 mm / 1.5 mm), and shows that the dominant residual problem in IIP is angular/facial deflection — exactly the component robotic/dynamic systems target.

## Clinical Decision Points

**When is freehand acceptable in IIP?**
- Essentially never as the *default* for esthetic-zone or thin-buccal-plate IIP: the socket actively deflects the drill facially, and Nava/Schiavon both show freehand carries the largest deviation. [근거강함]
- A skilled operator placing a posterior IIP into a well-contained socket with abundant apical/inter-radicular bone (e.g. a 4-wall premolar socket with a long apical/septal landing zone) may achieve acceptable freehand accuracy — but this is a case-selection exception, not a protocol recommendation. [claude해석]

**Static vs dynamic vs robotic — trade-offs:**
- **Full-static (FG-sCAIS)**: largest accuracy jump over freehand for the least infrastructure; sleeve/drill-key adds vertical clearance and irrigation concerns; cannot re-plan intra-operatively. Reasonable default for single-site IIP when a stable, well-fitting guide is achievable. The empty socket can compromise guide seating/stability — verify support on adjacent teeth. [합의수준]
- **Dynamic (dCAIS)**: matches static/robotic on *linear* accuracy, allows real-time axis correction and open irrigation/visualization (valuable in IIP where you must inspect the socket walls), but carries a learning curve and line-of-sight/registration overhead. Strong fit for anterior IIP where the plan must adapt to socket morphology. [근거중]
- **Robotic (rCAIS)**: the clearest, most reproducible **angular** stability — its niche is prosthetically-driven angulation, multi-implant parallelism, and sockets that strongly deflect the drill. But: highest cost, steepest learning curve, longest chair time, and once the tracker is equalised the *linear* advantage over dynamic disappears (Wei 2025). No IIP-specific rCAIS RCT exists; the NMA ranking rests on non-randomised data (CINeMA LOW). [claude해석]

**Decision heuristic [claude해석]:** Risk of facial/axial deflection is the deciding axis. Higher deflection risk (anterior maxilla, thin/absent buccal plate, palatal socket slope, novice operator) → escalate to dCAIS or rCAIS for angular control. Lower risk (contained posterior socket, experienced operator) → FG-sCAIS is usually sufficient; robotic premium is hard to justify without hard-outcome evidence.

## Gaps & Future Research

- **No hard-outcome linkage.** All evidence is surrogate (planned-vs-actual deviation). Whether sub-millimeter/sub-degree gains translate into survival, marginal bone loss, prosthetic fit, or esthetics (PES) in IIP is untested.
- **rCAIS in IIP is hypothesis-generating only.** Nava 2026 ranked it #1 but had no robotic RCT; the RCT-only sensitivity analysis dropped rCAIS entirely. An IIP-specific robotic RCT is the key missing study.
- **Guide stability in the empty socket.** Static guides depend on adjacent-tooth support; the IIP-specific failure modes of guide seating are under-reported.
- **Heterogeneity.** Software/implant-system/socket-morphology heterogeneity, no cost-effectiveness analysis, learning-curve not isolated, predominance of anterior single-tooth cases (molar and full-arch IIP under-studied).
- **Confounding of robot vs tracker.** Only one RCT (Wei 2025) equalised the optical tracker; most robot-vs-navigation gaps conflate the robot with its tracking pipeline.
