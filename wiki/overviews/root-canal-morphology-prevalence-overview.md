## 한국어 핵심요약

> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - 핵심 명제: 놓친 해부(missed anatomy)는 근관치료 실패의 주요 원인이므로, 각 변이의 인구별 사전확률(population prior)을 알아야 술전 콘빔CT(Cone-Beam Computed Tomography, CBCT) 처방과 접근와동(access cavity) 수정 시점을 결정할 수 있다. 이 페이지는 치아·변이별 유병률 근거를 묶는 허브(hub)이며, C형 근관(C-shaped Canal)과 제2근심협측 근관(second mesiobuccal canal, MB2)은 각각 별도 심화 페이지로 위임한다.
> - 하악 제1대구치 3근(distolingual root = 원심설측근, Radix Entomolaris, RE): 전세계 치아 8.85%·환자 10.3%, 양측성 약 46% (Aung 2022 SR+MA, CBCT 72편·37,994치). [근거강함]
> - RE 인종 구배: 동아시아 24% → 유럽 2% → 아프리카 0.9% — 약 25배 차이. 동아시아 환자에선 하악 제1대구치 4개 중 1개가 여분의 설측 치근·근관을 가진다고 가정해야 함(예외가 아니라 기본값). [근거강함]
> - 치내치(Dens Invaginatus, DI): CBCT 통합 유병률 9.0% (Gonzalez-Mancilla 2022 SR+MA, CBCT 2,009건)로 2D 방사선 추정(0.25–7.7%)의 약 2배. 상악 측절치(maxillary lateral incisor) 최다 호발, Oehlers Type I(치관 국한) 최다. [근거강함이나 study 4편으로 근거폭 좁음]
> - 상악 견치(maxillary canine, MxC): Vertucci Type I(단일 근관) 75.4–100% 압도적 우세이나 최대 약 25%에서 변이(제2근관·연결관·부근관); 남성에서 Vertucci III(OR 2.07)·V(OR 2.93) 유의하게 많음 (Wolf 2024 SR+MA, 22편). [근거강함]
> - 하악 제1대구치 내부해부(micro-CT gold standard): 근심근(mesial root) Vertucci Type IV(분리된 2근관) 58.7% 우세, 원심근(distal root) Type I(단일) 83.0%; 중간근심관(middle mesial canal, MMC) 최대 46%, 근심근 isthmus 10–100% (Al-Rammahi 2023 SR+MA, micro-CT 30편). 단 이 리뷰는 3근(RE)·taurodont을 설계상 제외. [근거강함이나 이질성 I²=86–87%]
> - 비비구개관(nasopalatine canal, NPC): 근관 유병률이 아닌 전상악 임플란트 랜드마크 — 평균 길이 9.64 mm, 남성에서 더 길고 구개개구 전후경 큼, 내외측경은 노화로 증가; 원통형(cylindrical) 25.5%·깔때기형(funnel) 23.5% 형태 우세 (Yadav 2024 CBCT 단면연구 n=102). [근거약함, 단일집단·cross-sectional]
> - 근거 계층(evidence tiers): micro-CT(9.9–50 µm, ex-vivo gold standard) > CBCT(in-vivo 실용 표준, 2D 대비 변이 검출 약 2배) > 2D 방사선(변이 대량 과소평가). DI 9.0% vs 2D 0.25–7.7%, MB2 CBCT ~63% vs 방사선 15%가 이 계층을 정량적으로 보여줌.
> - 공통 임상 규칙: ①동아시아 하악 제1대구치·상악 측절치 등 고사전확률(high-prior) 상황은 CBCT 처방 임계값을 낮추고, ②변이 발견 시 접근와동을 수정(RE는 원심설측 orifice, MMC는 근심 developmental groove 홈형성)하며, ③양측성이 흔하므로(RE 46%) 반대측(contralateral) 동명치를 함께 확인한다.
> - 한계: 모두 유병률/해부 연구 — 변이치의 장기 치아생존(tooth-survival) outcome 데이터는 이 컬렉션에 없다. NPC는 근관 변이가 아닌 수술 랜드마크로 성격이 달라 별개 축으로 다룬다.

## One-line Summary

Hub synthesis of 5 anatomical-variant prevalence papers (3 CBCT SR+MAs, 1 micro-CT SR+MA, 1 CBCT cross-section) mapping how often each root/canal variant occurs and by what evidence tier: a third root (Radix Entomolaris) in 8.85% of mandibular first molars globally but 24% in East Asians (Aung 2022); dens invaginatus at 9.0% on CBCT — double 2D estimates — mostly in maxillary lateral incisors, Oehlers Type I (Gonzalez-Mancilla 2022); maxillary canines Vertucci Type I in 75.4–100% with ~25% variation and male-predominant Type III/V (Wolf 2024); the double-rooted mandibular first molar's internal system on micro-CT (mesial Type IV 58.7%, distal Type I 83.0%, MMC up to 46%, isthmus 10–100%; Al-Rammahi 2023); and the nasopalatine canal as an anterior-maxilla surgical landmark (Yadav 2024) — with C-shaped and MB2 anatomy delegated to their dedicated deep-dive overviews.

## Summary

Missed anatomy is a leading cause of endodontic failure, so the practical value of a variant's prevalence is that it sets the **pre-treatment index of suspicion** — the population prior that decides when to order CBCT and when to modify the access cavity. This page is the broad *morphological-variant prevalence hub*; it collects the per-tooth / per-variant prevalence evidence and points to two dedicated deep-dives — the C-shaped canal overview and the MB2 detection-strategy overview — rather than duplicating them.

Key propositions:

1. **A third root (Radix Entomolaris) occurs in 8.85% of mandibular first molars (teeth) and 10.3% of patients globally, with ~46% bilateral symmetry, along a steep ethnic gradient (East Asia 24% → Europe 2% → Africa 0.9%)** (Aung 2022) [sr+ma, 72 in-vivo CBCT studies / 37,994 teeth]. Radix Paramolaris (buccal accessory root) is rare (0.1%). In an East-Asian / Mongoloid patient, roughly one in four mandibular first molars carries an extra distolingual root and canal — treat it as expected, not exceptional.

2. **CBCT-assessed dens invaginatus prevalence is 9.0% (95% CI 7.2–10.8%), about double the 0.25–7.7% range historically reported from 2D radiographs, concentrated in the maxillary lateral incisor and predominantly Oehlers Type I (crown-confined)** (Gonzalez-Mancilla 2022) [sr+ma, 4 retrospective studies / 2,009 CBCTs]. A single head-to-head study showed the imaging gap directly (10.7% CBCT vs 3% panoramic in the same patients). Type II/III (extending to the amelocemental line or through the root) drive prognosis and often need CBCT-guided complex or surgical management; the 4-study base makes the pooled figure precise but narrow in evidence breadth.

3. **The maxillary canine is a single-canal tooth in the overwhelming majority — Vertucci Type I in 75.4–100% — but up to ~25% carry anatomical variation (second, connecting, or accessory canals), and men show significantly more Type III (OR 2.07) and Type V (OR 2.93)** (Wolf 2024) [sr+ma, 22 studies]. The variation is often mechanically non-instrumentable, so chemical irrigation and careful obturation, not extra filing, carry the outcome.

4. **On micro-CT (the ex-vivo gold standard), the double-rooted mandibular first molar's mesial root is dominated by Vertucci Type IV (two separate canals; 58.7%) and its distal root by Type I (single canal; 83.0%), with a middle mesial canal in up to 46% and a mesial-root isthmus spanning 10–100%** (Al-Rammahi 2023) [sr+ma, 30 micro-CT studies; I²=86–87%]. This review **excludes three-rooted (RE) and taurodont teeth by design**, so it complements — does not overlap — the Aung 2022 external-root prevalence stream. Mesial anatomy is consistently more complex than distal and drives the tooth's high vertical-root-fracture rate.

5. **The nasopalatine canal is not a canal-morphology variant but an anterior-maxilla surgical landmark whose dimensions and shape vary by sex and age** (Yadav 2024) [cross-sectional CBCT, n=102, single Indian population]: mean length 9.64 mm (longer and AP-wider in males), ML diameter enlarging with age, and seven sagittal shape variants (cylindrical 25.5% and funnel 23.5% most common). It is included as the "landmark, not lumen" arm — relevant to implant axis planning and anesthesia, distinct from the endodontic-variant axis.

**Evidence-tier principle:** the same three imaging tiers recur across every variant — micro-CT (9.9–50 µm, ex-vivo, resolves isthmus/MMC/accessory canals) > in-vivo CBCT (practical clinical standard, ~2× the variant detection of 2D) > 2D radiography (systematically underestimates variants). The dens invaginatus 9.0%-vs-2D-0.25–7.7% gap and the MB2 CBCT-vs-radiograph gap (see MB2 deep-dive) both quantify this ladder. Read every prevalence number against the modality that produced it.

## Clinical Decision Points

1. **Lower the CBCT threshold when the population prior is high.** An East-Asian / Mongoloid patient's mandibular first molar (RE ~24%) and a suspected maxillary-lateral-incisor dens invaginatus are both high-prior situations where pre-treatment CBCT is justified under ALARA; a European mandibular first molar (RE ~2%) is not. The prior — not a single global number — should drive the imaging decision (Aung 2022; Gonzalez-Mancilla 2022).

2. **Modify the access cavity to the expected variant.** For a suspected RE, extend to a trapezoidal pulp-floor and search for the distolingual orifice ~3 mm lingual to the distobuccal orifice, anticipating ~30° buccolingual root curvature (file-separation risk) (Aung 2022). For the mandibular first molar's mesial root, expect two canals (Type IV) as baseline and actively hunt the MMC (up to 46%) in the mesiobuccal–mesiolingual developmental groove — most orifices sit at the CEJ but some need deep troughing, and MMC working length cannot be transferred from MB/ML (Al-Rammahi 2023).

3. **Do not "instrument out" a variant that irrigation should clean.** Maxillary-canine variation (~25%) and the mesial-root isthmus (10–100%) are largely non-instrumentable; plan adjunctive irrigant activation and careful three-dimensional obturation rather than aggressive filing (Wolf 2024; Al-Rammahi 2023).

4. **Classify dens invaginatus by Oehlers type before treating.** Type I (crown-confined) may need only sealing/monitoring, whereas Type III (through-root, PDL communication) often requires complex endodontic or surgical management — CBCT is decisive for this staging (Gonzalez-Mancilla 2022).

5. **Check the contralateral tooth.** RE bilateral symmetry is ~46% (60% in East Asians), so finding a variant on one side should prompt inspection of the contralateral mandibular first molar (Aung 2022).

6. **For anterior-maxilla implants, respect the nasopalatine canal as a landmark, not a lumen.** Plan implant axis and length against its length (~9.64 mm), obliquity, and the age-related ML-diameter enlargement to avoid nerve/vessel injury and NPC-cyst formation (Yadav 2024).
