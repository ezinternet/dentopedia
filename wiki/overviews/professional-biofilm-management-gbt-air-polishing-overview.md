>
> - **핵심 메시지(bottom line)**: 전문가 치면세균막관리에서 가이드 바이오필름 치료(Guided Biofilm Therapy, GBT)·에어폴리싱(Air Polishing)은 **환자 중심 결과(편안함·시술시간·치질 보존)에서 이득**이 있으나, 치주낭깊이(Probing Pocket Depth, PPD)·임상부착수준(Clinical Attachment Level, CAL)·탐침시출혈(Bleeding on Probing, BoP) 같은 **단단한 임상지표에서는 전통적 스케일링·치근활택술(Scaling and Root Planing, SRP)보다 우월하지 않다**.
> - **GBT 임상효능 종합근거**: PRISMA 체계적고찰(Yein 2026, RCT 5편·n=231, GRADE 매우 낮음)은 GBT가 만족도·통증·시술시간은 우수하나 PPD/CAL/BoP는 일관된 우위가 없다고 결론 — "GBT의 임상적 우월성은 현재 근거로 지지되지 않음".
> - **개별 RCT도 일치**: 분악 RCT(Cyris 2024, n=60, stage III/IV)·유지치주치료 RCT(Stähli 2024, n=41, 1년) 모두 GBT(에리스리톨 에어폴리싱+초음파)와 SRP가 **임상적으로 동등**, GBT는 시술시간만 단축(30.3 vs 34.6분/사분악).
> - **단, 연조직·바이오마커 신호는 GBT 우위 가능성**: GBT vs 큐렛+초음파 RCT(Dönertaş 2026, n=80, stage 1–2)에서 GBT가 치은지수(Gingival Index, GI)·치태지수(Plaque Index, PI)·치은열구액(Gingival Crevicular Fluid, GCF) **인터루킨-1β(IL-1β)를 유의하게 더 감소**(p<0.05); TNF-α·IL-10·MMP-8는 차이 없음.
> - **치질 보존(tooth substance)이 GBT의 진짜 강점**: 기구별 경조직 영향 고찰(Lodigkeit 2026)·in-vitro 체계적고찰(Dvorská 2026)에서 **에어폴리싱이 법랑질·백악질을 가장 적게 마모**; 손기구(curette)는 스트로크당 손실 최대(평균 6.0 µm vs 초음파 4.89 µm). 단, 저연마 분말도 **초(秒)당 손실은 에어폴리싱이 가장 큼(평균 8.65 µm)** — 한 부위에 오래 머무르면 안 됨.
> - **분말 선택은 에리스리톨(Erythritol, 14 µm)**: 탄산수소나트륨(Sodium Bicarbonate, 40 µm)과 바이오필름 제거력 동등(Pujarern 2024, OD 0.130 vs 0.129)하면서 표면 손상이 적어 임플란트·치근면 모두에서 선호; SRP+EDTA는 smear layer를 제거하지 못하고(p=0.950) 에리스리톨 에어폴리싱이 기구흔을 줄인다(Ifrim 2026 SEM).
> - **임플란트(implant) 영역도 동일 결론**: 점막염 비외과치료 체계적고찰(Brunello 2026, 21편·n=1068)·5군 RCT(Eraydın-Tüfek 2026, 임플란트 80개)·서술적고찰(Mauriello 2026, 9 RCT)이 모두 **기계적 청결관리(Professional Mechanical Plaque Removal, PMPR)/debridement가 핵심**이고, 글리신(Glycine) 에어아브레이전 등 보조요법은 6개월 시점에 **추가 이득이 미미**하다고 일치.
> - **안전(safety) 경고 — 일반화 금지**: 5 mm 치주낭에 글리신 치은연하(subgingival) 에어폴리싱 후 **안면 피하기종(Subcutaneous Facial Emphysema, SFE)** 발생·3일 입원 사례(Hussein 2026). 출혈·배농이 없는 유지치료 부위에서도 발생 가능 → 기기·프로토콜별 안전성은 각각 검증, 치은연하 전용 노즐·증례 선별·부종/염발음 조기 인지 필수.
> - **의사결정 틀(decision frame)**: ① 치은연상·유지치주치료(Supportive Periodontal Care, SPC) → 에어폴리싱/GBT가 최적(편안함·시간·치질 보존·공시제 동기부여) ② 활동성 NSPT(stage III/IV) → SRP와 동등하니 술자·환자 선호와 효율로 선택하되 **치석은 여전히 초음파/손기구로 제거** ③ 치은연하 심부낭 → 바이오필름은 에어폴리싱, 치석은 기계적, 기종 위험 관리 ④ 임플란트 → PMPR 우선, 에어아브레이전은 합리적 보조이나 우월성 근거 없음.
> - **연구 공백(gap)**: 전 영역에서 표준화된 결과지표(outcome set)·환자보고결과(Patient-Reported Outcome, PRO) 부재, GRADE 낮음, 장기 데이터 부족, 네트워크 메타분석(Network Meta-Analysis, NMA) 수행 불가 — "더 잘 설계된 장기 RCT 필요"가 공통 결론.

## One-line Summary

Across 12 papers (2 SRs, 4 RCTs, 1 in-vitro SR + narrative review, 2 in-vitro, 1 case report; teeth and implants), professional dental biofilm management by Guided Biofilm Therapy / air polishing delivers patient-centered benefits (comfort, treatment time, and the least hard-tissue abrasion) but is **clinically equivalent — not superior — to conventional mechanical debridement (SRP/PMPR) on hard endpoints (PPD, CAL, BoP)**; erythritol is the preferred low-damage powder, adjuncts add little on implants, and subgingival air polishing carries a real (if rare) emphysema risk.

## 한줄요약

치아·임플란트 12편(SR 2·RCT 4·in-vitro SR+서술고찰·in-vitro 2·증례 1)을 종합하면, GBT·에어폴리싱은 편안함·시술시간·최소 치질 마모라는 환자 중심 이득은 있으나 PPD·CAL·BoP 같은 단단한 임상지표에서는 전통적 기계적 청결관리(SRP/PMPR)와 **동등할 뿐 우월하지 않다**; 분말은 에리스리톨이 표면 손상이 적어 선호되고, 임플란트에서 보조요법은 추가 이득이 미미하며, 치은연하 에어폴리싱은 드물지만 실재하는 피하기종 위험이 있다.

## Summary

"Professional dental biofilm management" spans the office procedures used to disrupt and remove supra- and subgingival biofilm: hand instrumentation (curettes), (ultra)sonic scaling, rubber-cup polishing, and — increasingly — **air polishing** with low-abrasive powders, often packaged as the commercial **Guided Biofilm Therapy (GBT)** protocol (biofilm disclosure → erythritol air-polishing → ultrasonic calculus removal). This overview synthesizes the current evidence across **natural teeth (NSPT and supportive care)** and **dental implants (peri-implant mucositis and early peri-implantitis)**.

The dominant, consistent finding is one of **clinical equivalence, not superiority**. For natural teeth, a 2026 PRISMA systematic review (Yein, 5 RCTs, n=231) concludes the evidence does **not** support clinical superiority of GBT over scaling-and-root-debridement: GBT's reproducible advantages are patient-centered (satisfaction, less pain, shorter chair time), while its effects on PPD, CAL and BoP are heterogeneous and inconsistent. Two head-to-head RCTs reach the same verdict — Cyris 2024 (split-mouth, stage III/IV NSPT, n=60: equivalent pocket closure, GBT ~4 min/quadrant faster) and Stähli 2024 (supportive care, n=41, 1 year: clinically equivalent, SRP marginally better on pain, GBT a possible furcation signal). The one nuance favoring GBT comes at the **soft-tissue / biomarker level**: Dönertaş 2026 (RCT, n=80) found GBT lowered GI, PI and GCF IL-1β significantly more than curette+ultrasonic, without differences in TNF-α, IL-10 or MMP-8.

Where GBT/air polishing genuinely wins is **tooth-substance preservation**. A narrative review (Lodigkeit 2026) and an in-vitro systematic review (Dvorská 2026) agree that hand curettes remove the most cementum per stroke and create the most surface irregularity, ultrasonics are intermediate and more uniform, and **air polishing is the least abrasive — particularly for biofilm management**. The important caveat from Dvorská: low-abrasive powders have the **highest substance loss per second**, so dwell time matters. Powder choice converges on **erythritol (14 µm)**: equal biofilm removal to sodium bicarbonate (Pujarern 2024, in-vitro implants) with far less surface damage, and on root surfaces erythritol air polishing reduced instrumentation marks while SRP+EDTA failed to eliminate the smear layer (Ifrim 2026, SEM).

For **implants**, the same hierarchy holds: mechanical debridement / PMPR is the backbone and adjuncts add little. Brunello 2026 (SR, 21 studies, n=1068) found all non-surgical protocols — including mechanical debridement alone — improved peri-implant mucositis, with adjuncts yielding only minor 6-month gains and an NMA impossible due to heterogeneity. Eraydın-Tüfek 2026 (5-arm RCT, 80 implants) showed glycine powder air abrasion (alone or with instrumentation) gave the greatest numerical reductions but **no statistically significant superiority** over mechanical instrumentation. Mauriello 2026 (narrative review, 9 RCTs) likewise keeps PMPR as the gold standard with inconsistent adjunctive benefit.

Finally, **safety is not automatic**: Hussein 2026 reports subcutaneous facial emphysema after glycine subgingival air polishing in a 5 mm pocket — even at a site without bleeding or suppuration during supportive therapy — requiring hospitalization. Air-polishing safety cannot be generalized across devices and protocols; subgingival nozzles, case selection, and early recognition of swelling/crepitus are essential.

## The Decision Framework

| Clinical scenario | Preferred biofilm-management approach | Evidence | Caveat |
|---|---|---|---|
| Supragingival prophylaxis & **supportive periodontal care (SPC)** | **Air polishing / GBT** (erythritol) | Stähli 2024, Yein 2026, Lodigkeit/Dvorská 2026 | Best for comfort, time, tooth-substance preservation, disclosing-driven motivation |
| Active **NSPT, stage III/IV** | SRP **or** GBT — clinician/patient choice | Cyris 2024, Yein 2026 | Clinically equivalent; **calculus still needs ultrasonic/hand instruments** |
| **Subgingival deep pockets** | Air polishing for biofilm **+** mechanical for calculus | Ifrim 2026, Hussein 2026 | Emphysema risk → subgingival-specific nozzle, careful case selection, limit dwell time |
| **Peri-implant mucositis / early peri-implantitis** | **PMPR / mechanical debridement** first-line; erythritol/glycine air abrasion as reasonable adjunct | Brunello 2026, Eraydın-Tüfek 2026, Mauriello 2026, Pujarern 2024 | Adjuncts add little at 6 mo; erythritol preferred for implant-surface safety |

## Clinical Bottom Line

1. **Choose air polishing / GBT for what it actually delivers** — comfort, efficiency, and the least damage to enamel and cementum — not for a clinical-outcome edge that the evidence does not show.
2. **Mechanical instrumentation remains the backbone** of both periodontal and peri-implant biofilm management; air polishing complements but does not replace ultrasonic/hand calculus removal.
3. **Use erythritol (14 µm)** as the default low-abrasive powder for teeth, roots, and implant surfaces.
4. **Mind the dwell time** — low-abrasive powder still removes substance fastest per second.
5. **Respect subgingival air-polishing safety** — emphysema can occur even at quiet maintenance sites; device-specific protocols and case selection are non-negotiable.
6. **Evidence quality is low across the board** — standardized outcome sets, routine PROs, and long-term RCTs are the field's shared need.
