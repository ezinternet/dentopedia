## One-line Summary

Across 9 papers spanning animal models, prospective series, and SR+MAs, DBBM emerges as an indispensable osteoconductive scaffold with a well-documented slow-resorption trade-off: BCP hybrids and PRF adjuncts consistently rescue the new bone formation gap, while collagen modifications and BMP2 functionalization extend the engineering frontier.

## Summary

DBBM (Deproteinized Bovine Bone Mineral, 탈단백 우골 광물질) has been the clinical gold standard for bone augmentation procedures for decades, valued for its predictable space maintenance, osteoconductive architecture, and extreme volume stability. However, a consistent body of preclinical and clinical evidence now characterizes a core biological trade-off: DBBM's very slow resorption — the property that confers volume stability — also limits the rate and total volume of new bone formation at early time points. The nine papers synthesized here define this trade-off with quantitative precision, and map four intervention vectors that address it: (1) blending DBBM with resorbable synthetics (BCP/β-TCP), (2) modifying the collagen carrier or block form, (3) adding PRF growth-factor concentrates, and (4) functionalizing calcium phosphate with recombinant BMP2.

### Core Propositions

1. **DBBM alone may paradoxically suppress early bone formation.** In a 4-week rat calvarial critical-size defect model, DBBM alone produced significantly less new bone than the empty (no-graft) control (p=0.025). All synthetic-additive combinations rescued new bone to natural healing levels (Fujioka-Kobayashi 2022). This counterintuitive finding reframes DBBM's role: it is a volumetric scaffold, not an osteogenic material, and it may occupy space at the expense of ingrowth at short time windows.

2. **BCP outperforms DBBM on new bone formation and resorption across models.** In a 6-week rabbit femoral critical-size defect, BCP (60% HA / 40% β-TCP) produced 70% more new bone and had 60% less residual material than DBBM (both p<0.001; Chakar 2014). In the clinical sinus augmentation context, the RCT SR+MA (10 RCTs, 328 patients) confirmed BCP yields +3.48% more new bone (p=0.01) and −8.41% less residual graft (p<0.001) vs DBBM (Alkandari 2025). The advantage is consistent across species and procedures.

3. **L-PRF addition to DBBM is the highest-evidence adjunct for sinus augmentation histomorphometry.** The SR+MA of 5 RCTs (n=140) showed L-PRF+DBBM vs DBBM alone: new bone +7.07% (95% CI 2.20–11.93; p<0.001; I²=37%) and residual graft −7.93% (95% CI −11.20 to −4.66; p<0.001; I²=0%). Zero heterogeneity for residual graft is exceptional and signals a mechanistically consistent PRF effect on DBBM incorporation (Almutairi 2025).

4. **A-PRF shows a larger raw effect in a single RCT (Motimath 2026).** Split-mouth RCT (n=30, 60 sinuses): A-PRF+DBBM at 9 months yielded 42.8% new bone vs 31.2% for DBBM alone (p=0.001; +11.6 percentage points) and 28.4% vs 41.6% residual graft (p=0.001; −13.2 percentage points). The A-PRF low-speed protocol (2700 rpm/12 min) is hypothesized to preserve more viable platelets and growth factors than classical L-PRF centrifugation.

5. **PRF benefit is PRF-subtype-specific and procedure-specific.** The 2023 SR (7 RCTs; Idiri 2023) — which pooled all PRF subtypes without meta-analysis — found no significant histomorphometric benefit of PRF+DBBM in sinus lift. The subsequent Almutairi 2025 SR+MA, restricted to L-PRF only with quantitative pooling, demonstrated significant benefit. The discrepancy resolves methodologically: PRF subtypes (L-PRF vs A-PRF vs solid PRF) and centrifugation protocols are not interchangeable in the sinus environment.

6. **Platelet lysate (APL) provides no measurable benefit to either DBBM or BCP.** Despite confirmed in vitro mitogenic activity (100–200% cell proliferation enhancement), APL addition to DBBM or BCP in a 6-week rabbit model yielded no significant difference in new bone or residual material (Chakar 2014). This negative finding emphasizes that growth factor release alone, without the fibrin scaffold architecture of modern PRF, does not drive in vivo bone formation.

7. **Collagen modification of DBBM expands clinical utility without sacrificing scaffold function.** The species-specific collagen-enriched DBBM block (C-DBBM: 95% DBBM + 5% bovine collagen) in ARP achieved 0/10 patients requiring GBR at implant placement and histological evidence of appositional membranous osteogenesis across multiple concurrent stages (van Orten 2024). The integrated collagen may address the DBBM-alone bone formation gap by providing cellular attachment sites and growth factor binding capacity. The all-bovine material chain also addresses porcine-free religious/ethical requirements.

8. **L-serine incorporation into collagen scaffolds provides tunable non-crosslinked membrane persistence.** Subcutaneous mouse model: 40 wt% L-serine collagen showed 2.70 mm² residual area vs 0.37 mm² unmodified collagen (7× more persistent, p<0.05), while Bio-Gide remained most stable at 5.64 mm². Structural mechanism: β-sheet enrichment and crystalline domain formation via hydrogen bonding without cytotoxic chemical crosslinkers (Kim 2025). This creates a design pathway for DBBM-carrying membrane scaffolds with programmable resorption timing.

9. **BMP2-functionalized calcium phosphate (BioCaP) outperforms DBBM in the challenging context of simultaneous orthodontic tooth movement.** Beagle dog study (n=9, 8 weeks): BioCaP produced 1.61× more new bone vs empty control and 1.25× more than DBB; bone mineral density 1.92× higher vs control and 1.36× higher vs DBB; root resorption 1.39× less than DBB. The BioCaP BMP2 dose (140 μg at 1 μg/mL co-precipitation) is 10-fold below conventional rhBMP2, reducing ectopic bone and seroma risk. DBB's slow-resorbing rigid particles are mechanically incompatible with OTM, causing 1.87× more root resorption than the empty control (Jiang 2020). This is the most clinically actionable negative signal for DBBM — it is contraindicated as a solo graft in sites requiring simultaneous tooth movement.

## Results

### Axis 1 — DBBM Core Properties: Slow Resorption, Volume Stability, Osteoconductive Scaffold

DBBM's defining characteristic is near-permanent residual material persistence. At 6 weeks in rabbit femoral defects, DBBM remained largely non-resorbed while BCP (β-TCP component) actively degraded (Chakar 2014). At 4 weeks in rat calvarial defects, DBBM showed no significant material density change versus the synthetic additives that resorbed measurably (Fujioka-Kobayashi 2022). In the clinical sinus augmentation context, residual graft % for DBBM alone ranged 38–44% at 6–9 month biopsies (Almutairi 2025; Motimath 2026), confirming the same kinetics in humans.

The scaffold function is genuine: new bone forms around DBBM granules in all models — the issue is rate and total volume relative to resorbable materials. DBBM's value is therefore primarily volumetric/mechanical (space maintenance, prevention of membrane collapse), not biologically stimulatory. This dichotomy is clinically important: choose DBBM for sites where long-term volume stability is the dominant need; choose resorbable alternatives or DBBM hybrids when early bone ingrowth is the priority.

### Axis 2 — DBBM + Synthetic Additive Hybrids (BCP / β-TCP)

**Fujioka-Kobayashi 2022** (rat calvarial, 4 weeks, n=30 defects, 5 groups): α-TCP + DBBM (1:1) and BBCP (3% or 6% HA / 97% or 94% α-TCP) + DBBM (1:1) all restored new bone to empty-defect levels. DBBM alone was significantly inferior to all combinations and to the empty defect (p=0.025). The synthetic components resorbed (mineral density decrease) while maintaining volume — preventing premature scaffold collapse. HA coating degree (3% vs 6%) had no significant effect at 4 weeks.

**Chakar 2014** (rabbit femoral, 6 weeks, n=12): BCP (60% HA / 40% β-TCP) vs DBBM — new bone +70% (p<0.001), residual material −60% (p<0.001). BCP's active resorption through the β-TCP component is the mechanism: as TCP dissolves, Ca²⁺ and PO₄³⁻ ions are released into the local environment, creating a mineral-saturated milieu that drives osteoblast activity and matrix mineralization.

**Alkandari 2025** (10 RCTs, 328 patients, sinus elevation): BCP vs DBBM histomorphometry — new bone MD +3.48% (95% CI 0.89–5.97; p=0.01; I²=44%), residual graft MD −8.41% (95% CI −13.12 to −3.72; p<0.001; I²=86%), soft tissue MD +6.01% (95% CI 2.39–9.63; p<0.001; I²=72%). The higher soft tissue fraction with BCP reflects active graft resorption creating space occupied transiently by soft tissue before bone fills in. High I² for residual graft (86%) reflects BCP formulation heterogeneity across the 10 included RCTs — BCP is not a single material.

**Synthesis**: The hybrid graft strategy (DBBM for space maintenance + resorbable synthetic for bone formation drive) addresses the core DBBM trade-off without abandoning its volume stability advantage. Clinically applicable ratio: 1:1 DBBM:synthetic showed benefit in both rat (Fujioka-Kobayashi 2022) and clinical (Alkandari 2025 pooled) contexts.

### Axis 3 — Collagen-Modified DBBM

**Van Orten 2024** (prospective case series, n=10, ARP, 130–319 days): Species-specific C-DBBM block (95% DBBM + 5% bovine collagen, same species as DBBM) with bovine cross-linked membrane using open-healing technique. Histological results: lattice-pattern cancellous bone trabeculae by appositional membranous osteogenesis surrounding residual C-DBBM granules. GBR requirement at implant placement: 0/10 patients (0%). No complications over 2.5–4.5 year follow-up. The collagen component likely provides integrin-binding RGD sites for osteoprogenitor cell attachment — addressing the cell-recruitment gap of mineral-only DBBM. The species-specific (all-bovine) chain is the first in literature to report histological confirmation.

Methodological note: the 0% GBR rate vs Fischer et al.'s SR+MA 6–19% augmentation-free rate gap reflects design differences — private practice case selection, longer healing periods (>6 months vs 3–4 months in RCTs), and block vs particulate form. No control group prevents causal attribution.

**Kim 2025** (mouse subcutaneous model + in vitro): L-serine incorporation into bovine collagen scaffolds at 40 wt% achieved 7× greater persistence than unmodified collagen (2.70 ± 1.45 vs 0.37 ± 0.22 mm², p<0.05). Bio-Gide® remained most persistent (5.64 ± 2.76 mm²). The stabilization mechanism is non-crosslinking: L-serine's hydroxyl group forms hydrogen bonds with collagen backbone amide/carbonyl groups and increases crystallinity (2θ=22–23° XRD peak), while low concentrations (10–30 wt%) preserve the native triple helix. No cytotoxicity is expected (no aldehyde or NHS-ester chemistry). Osteogenic performance in defect models: not yet tested — this is a degradation-tuning proof-of-concept.

**Synthesis**: Collagen modification occupies the middle ground between mineral-only DBBM and complex growth factor delivery. Integrated collagen (Van Orten 2024) appears to enable cellular colonization without requiring synthetic additives; amino acid-modified scaffolds (Kim 2025) offer programmable resorption timing for barrier membrane applications. Both eliminate porcine collagen — relevant for practice contexts with halal/ethical material requirements.

### Axis 4 — PRF Adjuncts: L-PRF, A-PRF, and All-PRF Evidence

| Paper | Design | PRF Type | New bone effect | Residual graft effect |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Idiri 2023 | SR (7 RCTs, no pooling) | Mixed (all PRF) | No significant difference | No significant difference (1 GBR study: significant reduction) |
| Almutairi 2025 | SR+MA (5 RCTs, pooled) | L-PRF only | +7.07% MD (p<0.001, I²=37%) | −7.93% MD (p<0.001, I²=0%) |
| Motimath 2026 | Split-mouth RCT (n=30) | A-PRF (2700 rpm) | +11.6% (42.8 vs 31.2%, p=0.001) | −13.2% (28.4 vs 41.6%, p=0.001) |

The hierarchical interpretation: Idiri 2023's null result was a PRF-type pooling artifact and a meta-analysis power limitation (7 RCTs, no pooling). When restricted to L-PRF and quantitatively pooled, Almutairi 2025 demonstrates significance with acceptable heterogeneity (I²=37% for new bone; 0% for residual graft). The zero I² for residual graft is the most reliable signal in this axis — it means L-PRF consistently reduces DBBM persistence across all 5 included RCTs regardless of protocol variation.

Almutairi 2025 also showed ISQ at loading was not significantly different (MD −2.25; p=0.56) despite better histomorphometry. This dissociation between tissue-level (histomorphometry) and implant-level (ISQ/osseointegration) benefit has clinical implications: better histomorphometric appearance does not necessarily justify earlier loading decisions on ISQ grounds alone.

A-PRF vs L-PRF: Motimath 2026 is the only head-to-head proxy available (no direct comparison RCT). A-PRF's lower centrifugation speed (2700 rpm vs L-PRF's 2700–3000 rpm in Choukroun protocols, and 3200–3300 rpm in Dohan) is hypothesized to retain more viable cells and platelets within the fibrin matrix, potentially explaining its numerically larger raw effect (+11.6% vs +7.07%). This remains a hypothesis without a direct comparison RCT.

Chakar 2014's APL (liquid platelet lysate, no fibrin scaffold) added no benefit — underscoring that the fibrin architecture of PRF membranes, not growth factor content alone, is critical for the in vivo effect.

### Axis 5 — BCP vs DBBM in Sinus Elevation (Alkandari 2025)

The Alkandari 2025 SR+MA provides the first RCT-level synthesis dedicated to this head-to-head comparison in sinus augmentation. Across 10 RCTs (328 patients, 389 implants):

- New bone formation: BCP +3.48% (p=0.01, I²=44%) — significant but moderate heterogeneity
- Residual graft: BCP −8.41% (p<0.001, I²=86%) — highly significant but very high heterogeneity; reflects BCP formulation diversity (60:40 vs 70:30 HA:TCP ratios, particle sizes)
- Soft tissue fraction: BCP +6.01% (p<0.001, I²=72%) — reflects turnover space before bone fills in

Critically, no implant survival data were available in this SR+MA. DBBM's long-term implant survival track record (10+ year data from Mordenfeld series) is more established than BCP's. Until survival-level equivalence is demonstrated in sinus augmentation, BCP's histomorphometric superiority should not be interpreted as clinical superiority for the final outcome of functioning implants.

The high I² for BCP outcomes (44–86%) is a material-heterogeneity problem: BCP is not one material. Clinicians should note that the pooled "BCP advantage" likely underestimates the effect of the best-performing BCP formulations and overestimates it for others.

### Axis 6 — BMP2-Functionalized Calcium Phosphate (BioCaP)

Jiang 2020 is the only paper in this set addressing growth factor functionalization. The BioCaP approach — BMP2 co-precipitated into a biomimetic CaP coating at 1 μg/mL (total 140 μg per graft) — achieves sustained release at 10× lower dose than conventional rhBMP2 delivery systems, reducing ectopic bone and seroma risks.

In the unique clinical scenario of alveolar defect grafting during concurrent orthodontic tooth movement (OTM), BioCaP achieved:
- New bone: 1.61× empty control, 1.25× DBB
- Bone mineral density: 1.92× empty control, 1.36× DBB
- Root resorption: 1.39× less than DBB
- OTM rate: no significant difference vs control
- GCF IL-1β: significantly lower (anti-inflammatory)
- DBB root resorption: 1.87× the empty control — a significant safety concern unique to the OTM context

The DBB root resorption signal is the most clinically actionable finding: rigid, non-resorbing DBB particles under orthodontic compressive forces create localized stress concentration at the root-PDL-graft interface, driving external root resorption. Fast-resorbing BioCaP particles are progressively replaced by bone, eliminating this stress concentrator.

This axis currently has one animal study (n=9 beagles, 3 groups); human data do not yet exist for BioCaP.

## Clinical Decision Threads [claude해석]

**Thread 1 — Routine extraction socket ARP: choosing between DBBM and C-DBBM**

Standard particulate DBBM provides volume stability but limited early bone formation (Fujioka-Kobayashi 2022). The collagen-enriched block form (C-DBBM, Van Orten 2024) appears to enable more complete bone colonization — possibly by providing cell-adhesion substrate — with 0% GBR requirement at implant placement in 10 ARP cases over 2.5–4.5 years. For patients with porcine-free requirements (halal practice), C-DBBM + bovine xCM is now the only species-consistent evidence-based ARP protocol. For standard patients, C-DBBM block + open-healing membrane is a reasonable protocol if the operator is comfortable without primary closure; particulate DBBM + primary closure membrane remains the RCT-validated standard.

**Thread 2 — Lateral sinus augmentation: DBBM alone vs DBBM+PRF**

The default choice (DBBM alone) is well-supported by long-term implant survival data. Adding L-PRF is now supported by the highest-evidence synthesis available for this combination: SR+MA of 5 RCTs shows +7.07% new bone and −7.93% residual graft, with zero I² on the residual graft finding. The L-PRF effect does not translate to better ISQ at loading (Almutairi 2025) — meaning it improves tissue quality at biopsy without proven functional acceleration of osseointegration. Clinical value: potentially faster bone maturation at histological level, possibly reducing the 6-month waiting period to implant placement, but this hypothesis requires prospective RCT confirmation. PRF cost is minimal (patient's own blood); the benefit/cost ratio favors routine L-PRF addition.

**Thread 3 — Lateral sinus augmentation: DBBM vs BCP**

BCP yields more new bone and less residual graft than DBBM in sinus augmentation RCTs (Alkandari 2025). However: (a) no implant survival SR+MA exists yet for BCP-augmented sinuses, (b) BCP heterogeneity is high (I²=86% for residual graft), and (c) DBBM has decades of implant survival data. The recommendation is: DBBM remains the preferred default for routine sinus augmentation. BCP or DBBM+BCP hybrids are appropriate in cases where maximum new bone formation speed is the priority (medically limited healing time, need for earlier loading) and the operator can select a well-characterized BCP formulation. The hybrid approach (DBBM+BCP 1:1) informed by Fujioka-Kobayashi 2022 and the Alkandari 2025 data may represent the optimal balance.

**Thread 4 — BMP2 use in grafted sites with simultaneous orthodontics**

Pure DBBM/DBB should not be used as the solo graft in alveolar defects receiving concurrent orthodontic tooth movement — root resorption risk is 1.87× the ungrafted control (Jiang 2020). BioCaP (BMP2-functionalized CaP) appears to be the only graft tested in this specific scenario with favorable root resorption profile. Until human RCT data exist, clinical use of BMP2-functionalized CaP in OTM sites should be considered experimental. Standard protocol: avoid non-resorbable graft particles in any site subject to orthodontic force.

**Thread 5 — Interpreting PRF generation heterogeneity in the literature**

Clinicians reading PRF studies should discriminate: (a) solid PRF vs liquid PRF (i-PRF) — different growth factor concentrations; (b) L-PRF centrifugation protocol (Dohan high-speed vs Choukroun protocols) — different fibrin architecture; (c) A-PRF low-speed (2700 rpm) — different platelet/cell distribution; (d) PRF mixed into graft vs used as membrane layer. Idiri 2023's null result and Almutairi 2025's positive result for the same general procedure (sinus lift + DBBM + PRF) are both methodologically valid and non-contradictory: they measure different subsets of PRF practice. Future meta-analyses should stratify by PRF generation and centrifugation protocol.
