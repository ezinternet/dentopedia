> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - **핵심 명제**: 수복 변연(restorative margin)이 치주에 끼치는 영향은 단일 변수가 아니라 4개 축의 곱이다 — (1) 생물학적 폭경(Biologic Width, BW) / 치조상부 조직부착(Supracrestal Tissue Attachment, SCTA) 존중, (2) 변연 위치(margin location), (3) 변연 적합·외형(marginal fit/contour), (4) finish line 디자인.
> - **생물학적 폭경 수치**: 교과서 표준은 Gargiulo 2.04 mm(접합상피 + 결합조직부착)이지만, Hamasni (2017) 생체 bone-sounding 측정값은 평균 **1.13 mm**(p<0.05로 유의하게 작음)이고 치은열구깊이(sulcus depth)는 1.96 mm로 오히려 큼 → "마법의 숫자"가 아니라 **부위별(site-specific) 변연-치조정 거리** 계획이 옳다.
> - **위반 식별 임계값**: Alam (2024)은 변연-치조정 거리 **<2 mm**(bone sounding) + 치조정 골소실 방사선 소견을 SCTA 위반 진단 기준으로 제시; 교정은 치은절제술 / 외과적 치관연장술(crown lengthening) / 정출(extrusion).
> - **변연 위치 결정 분기**: 치은연상(supragingival)이 항상 가장 안전(위생 가능, Srimaneepong 2022). 치은연하(subgingival)는 **위생이 탁월할 때만** 허용 — 다만 Schätzle 26년 데이터는 실제 환경에서 장기 염증·부착소실 위험이 현실임을 보여줌.
> - **Ercoli ↔ Schätzle 외관상 충돌의 해소**: Ercoli (2021)는 "intracrevicular 변연 자체는 치주염 원인이 아니다(위생 유지 전제)"; Schätzle (2001)는 26년 추적에서 치은연하 변연이 **plaque·치은지수(Gingival Index, GI, 7회 조사 모두 p<0.001)·부착소실**을 증가시켰다고 보고. → 둘은 모순이 아니라 **이상적 통제 위생 vs 실제 장기 위생**의 차이. 임상 결론: 위생을 보증할 수 없으면 변연을 올려라.
> - **적합·외형이 핵심 구동력**: 변연 부적합(marginal misfit)·overhang이 biofilm·치은열구액(Gingival Crevicular Fluid, GCF) 증가·이차우식·골소실의 failure cascade를 일으킨다(Srimaneepong 2022). 합의된 최적 gap은 없으나 인용 임계는 <120 µm, <100 µm, "적절" 20–75 µm.
> - **재료**: CAD/CAM 지르코니아가 더 나은 변연 적합·낮은 염증·쉬운 유지로 기존 합금 주조 수복물보다 치주 건강에 유리(Srimaneepong 2022). 표면 거칠기는 biofilm 유발 → 연마(polish)가 보호적.
> - **Finish line 디자인**: Hajaj (2025, 112편)은 수평(chamfer/shoulder) vs 수직 BOPT(Biologically Oriented Preparation Technique) 비교 — BOPT는 치은 두께 증가·안정 probing depth(<0.2 mm 변동)·낮은 BOP(<10%)·10년 변연 안정성과 연관되나 **우월성은 미입증**. Ercoli는 디자인 간 임상적 치주 반응 차이 없다고 봄.
> - **대비/예외 (counter-intuitive null)**: Lamichhane (2025, n=95 split-mouth)은 지대치가 비지대치보다 PI·GI·PD·CAL 모두 유의하게 나쁨(p<0.001)을 확인했으나, **변연 위치(supra/equi/sub)는 지대치 치주지표에 유의한 영향 없음** → 단면연구의 한계(인과 아님)와 위생·적합·기간·재료가 위치보다 더 지배적일 수 있음을 시사.
> - **임상 결론(통합)**: ① BW/SCTA를 침범하지 말 것(변연-치조정 ≥2 mm 확보, 부위별 측정), ② 가능하면 치은연상, 심미상 불가피하면 위생 가능한 얕은 치은내(intracrevicular)까지만, ③ 변연 적합을 µm 단위로 관리하고 overhang 제거, ④ 디자인은 개별화(전치·얇은 biotype → BOPT 고려, 구치·고하중 → 수평), ⑤ 모든 보철 환자는 정기 치주 모니터링·유지관리 필수.

## One-line Summary

A 7-paper synthesis arguing the restorative-margin/periodontium relationship is the product of four interacting axes — respecting the biologic width / supracrestal tissue attachment (~2 mm; Hamasni's clinical 1.13 mm shows real site variation), margin location (supragingival safest; subgingival tolerable only under excellent hygiene), marginal adaptation/fit and contour (misfit and overhangs drive the biofilm-to-bone-loss cascade), and finish-line design (horizontal vs vertical/BOPT) — and reconciling the apparent Ercoli-vs-Schätzle tension as a contrast between idealized controlled hygiene and real-world long-term maintenance.

## 한줄요약

7편 종합 — 수복 변연과 치주의 관계는 ① 생물학적 폭경/SCTA 존중(~2 mm, Hamasni 임상값 1.13 mm로 부위 편차 큼), ② 변연 위치(치은연상 최선, 치은연하는 탁월한 위생 시에만), ③ 변연 적합·외형(부적합·overhang이 biofilm→골소실 cascade 유발), ④ finish line 디자인(수평 vs 수직 BOPT) 4축의 상호작용 결과이며, Ercoli(통제 위생) vs Schätzle(실제 장기 위생)의 외관상 충돌은 위생 조건 차이로 해소된다.

## Summary

The seven papers converge on a single clinical object — the line where a restoration meets the marginal periodontium — but each illuminates a different axis. Read together they support a multiplicative model: periodontal outcome at a margin is not governed by any one factor but by the joint state of (1) the supracrestal tissue attachment (SCTA, the renamed biologic width), (2) where the margin sits relative to the gingiva, (3) how well the margin fits and how its emergence contour is shaped, and (4) the finish-line geometry. A failure in any one axis can dominate the others.

**Axis 1 — Biologic width / SCTA.** Hamasni (2017) measured biologic width directly in 42 living patients (504 sites, bone-sounding under anesthesia) and found a mean clinical biologic width of **1.13 mm** — significantly *smaller* than Gargiulo's classic 2.04 mm histologic standard — with sulcus depth of 1.96 mm (significantly *larger* than the histologic 0.69 mm). The lesson is not a new universal number but the opposite: the dimension varies by site, so a fixed "magic number" is the wrong planning tool and a patient/site-specific margin-to-crest distance should be established. Alam (2024) operationalizes this for diagnosis and correction: SCTA (junctional epithelium + connective tissue attachment, ideal ~1+1+1 mm) is violated when bone sounding shows a **margin-to-crest distance <2 mm** alongside crestal bone loss, and correction follows a worked pathway — gingivectomy, surgical crown lengthening (apically positioned flap ± osteotomy), or orthodontic/surgical extrusion, chosen against keratinized-tissue amount and bone-to-CEJ relationship.

**Axis 2 — Margin location, and the Ercoli-vs-Schätzle tension.** Srimaneepong (2022) states the default cleanly: supragingival (and equigingival) margins permit hygiene and do not provoke caries or periodontal disease, whereas subgingival margins impede hygiene and risk biologic-width violation. The apparent conflict is between two reviews/cohorts. **Ercoli (2021)** — a systematically-searched narrative review — concludes that *intracrevicular margins per se do not cause periodontal disease* and that contemporary restorations are compatible with periodontal health, with the harm attributable instead to large overhangs and connective-tissue-attachment invasion. **Schätzle (2001)** — a 26-year prospective cohort (160 Norwegian males; 98 test vs 615 control mesial sites) — found subgingival filling margins associated with higher plaque, higher gingival inflammation (GI elevated at all 7 surveys, p<0.001), and greater attachment loss, detectable 1–3 years after placement with a later "burn-out" plateau. These are not contradictory: Ercoli's "compatible" verdict is explicitly conditioned on maintained self-performed hygiene, while Schätzle documents what happens to subgingival margins over decades in a real (even relatively well-maintained) population. The reconciliation is the clinical rule itself — a subgingival margin is acceptable *only* to the extent excellent hygiene can be guaranteed; where it cannot, the long-term cohort data say the margin should be supragingival.

**Axis 3 — Marginal fit and contour.** Srimaneepong (2022) frames marginal discrepancy as the central failure pathway: a poor margin exposes a thick cement layer that dissolves, seeds biofilm and microleakage, and produces discoloration, increased GCF flow, recurrent caries, and ultimately periodontal lesions and bone loss. No optimum gap is agreed (cited thresholds <120 µm, <100 µm, "adequate" 20–75 µm), but larger gaps worsen sequelae, and surface roughness drives colonization so polish is protective. On materials, CAD/CAM zirconia is favored over conventional cast alloys for better fit, lower inflammation, and easier maintenance. Lamichhane (2025) supplies in-patient empirical support: in a 95-patient split-mouth design, FDP abutment teeth were significantly worse than contralateral matched non-abutment teeth across **all four parameters — PI, GI, PD, CAL (all p<0.001)**, with prosthesis duration and material modifying PI/GI/CAL.

**Axis 4 — Finish-line design.** Hajaj (2025, 112 studies) contrasts horizontal designs (chamfer, shoulder) with the vertical Biologically Oriented Preparation Technique (BOPT). Subgingival horizontal margins are linked to BOP >25% and papilla loss anteriorly, while BOPT is associated with increased gingival thickness, stable probing depths (mean variation <0.2 mm), BOP <10%, and up to 10-year margin stability — but the evidence is heterogeneous and **BOPT's superiority is not established**. This sits beside Ercoli's view that finish-line design produces no clinically different periodontal response, and Lamichhane's counter-intuitive null: **margin location showed no significant effect on abutment periodontal parameters** in that cohort. The honest reading is that design and exact margin position are secondary to the dominant drivers (hygiene, fit, attachment respect), and design choice should be individualized — BOPT for anterior/thin-biotype esthetic cases, horizontal for posterior/high-biomechanical-demand teeth.

**Synthesis.** The strongest, most consistent signals are: do not invade the attachment (keep margin-to-crest ≥2 mm, measured site-specifically), prefer supragingival placement and reserve subgingival/intracrevicular margins for cases where hygiene is assured, and obsess over marginal fit and overhang removal because misfit is the mechanistically central driver of breakdown. Finish-line geometry and the precise supra/equi/sub gradation are real but second-order. Every FDP patient needs ongoing periodontal monitoring because abutment teeth are measurably disadvantaged over time.
