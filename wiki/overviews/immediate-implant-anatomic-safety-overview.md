## 한국어 핵심요약

> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - 이 페이지는 즉시식립(Immediate Implant Placement, IIP)을 **부위별 해부 안전역**으로 묶은 spine이다. 핵심 관점: IIP 실패의 상당 부분은 골재생이 아니라 **수술 전 해부 평가 누락**(신경 침범·피질판 천공·협측판 흡수) — 그래서 술전 CBCT가 선택이 아니라 필수.
> - **상악 전치부 — 절치관(Incisive Canal, IC)**: CBCT 정량(Al-Amery 2015, n=90)에서 IC 평균 길이 16.33 mm·폭 3.85 mm, 전방골 두께 평균 7.63 mm. 구개측 경사식립 시 IC 침범이 식립 실패·감각이상 원인. [근거강함]
> - 절치관은 **민족차** 존재 — 몽골로이드는 서구인보다 길고 더 경사·만곡 → 서구 norm으로 계획하면 IC 침범 위험 과소평가. 한국인 환자 계획 시 주의. [claude해석]
> - **하악 구치부 — 하치조관(Inferior Alveolar Canal, IAC) + 설측 천공(Lingual Plate Perforation, LPP)**: SR+MA(Behfarnia 2025, 12편 CBCT, 1,834명)에서 치근첨–IAC 거리 제2소구치 5.14 / 제1대구치 6.32 / **제2대구치 4.65 mm**(가장 짧음). [근거강함]
> - 제2대구치는 **이중 위험 부위** — IAC에 가장 가깝고(4.65 mm) 동시에 언더컷(U형) 치조제가 73%로 설측판 천공 위험 최고. ≥2 mm IAC 안전역 + 단면 형태 CBCT 평가 필수. [근거강함]
> - **협측 갭·협측판(Greenstein 2013)**: jumping distance <2 mm는 대개 자발 골충전(개방 또는 contour용 DBBM 선택), **≥2 mm는 DBBM 이식 권고**. 협측판 두께 >2 mm는 수직흡수 적으나 <1–2 mm는 증대 권고. IIP 후 약 1/3에서 ~1 mm 협측 퇴축. [합의수준]
> - **1차 안정성은 골이식이 아니라 잔존(치근첨 너머) 천연골이 결정**: 파일럿 RCT(Askin Ekinci 2026, n=28)에서 갭 골이식은 초기 안정성 개선 안 함, 치유부가 식립토크·ISQ 유의 우위, 가상 골-임플란트 접촉(virtual BIC)은 **비이식 즉시식립군에서만** 초기 ISQ와 상관 → 갭이식은 dimensional/심미 목적이지 안정성 목적 아님. [근거강함]
> - 즉 해부 평가의 임상 함의가 한 줄로 연결됨: **소켓 너머 천연골 engagement가 안정성을 만들고**, 그 천연골 영역에 IC·IAC·설측 언더컷이 놓여 있으므로 해부 안전역 = 안정성 확보역. [claude해석]
> - **결과 비교의 방법론 경고(Pannuti 2026)**: 무치악 상악 로딩 타이밍 연구는 PRO/ClinRO 측정도구가 표준화 안 됨(VAS가 80%, ClinRO 12종 난립, 5편 중 1편만 평가자 보정) → IIP 부위·프로토콜 간 환자중심 결과 비교는 현재 신뢰도 제한. [근거강함]
> - **갭**: 한국인 IC normative CBCT 데이터 부재, 협측판 두께 임계의 인체 장기 RCT 부족, virtual BIC–안정성 상관의 대규모 검증 미흡, 로딩 타이밍 PROM 표준 미정립.

## One-line Summary

Synthesis of 5 immediate-implant (IIP) papers organized as a site-specific anatomic-safety map, premised on the idea that much IIP failure stems from skipped preoperative anatomic assessment (nerve encroachment, cortical perforation, buccal-plate loss) rather than poor regeneration — making preoperative CBCT mandatory. Anterior maxilla: incisive canal mean length 16.33 mm / width 3.85 mm with ethnic variation (Al-Amery 2015). Posterior mandible: pooled root-apex-to-IAC distance is shortest at the second molar (4.65 mm), which is also 73% undercut, making it a double-risk site (Behfarnia 2025). Buccal gap ≥2 mm warrants DBBM grafting and thin buccal plate warrants augmentation (Greenstein 2013). Grafting the gap does not improve primary stability — native bone beyond the socket drives it, and virtual bone-implant contact predicts early stability only when ungrafted (Askin Ekinci 2026). Outcome-measure heterogeneity currently limits cross-protocol comparison (Pannuti 2026).

## 한줄요약

즉시식립 5편을 부위별 해부 안전역으로 종합 — 상악 전치부 절치관(IC, 길이 16.33 mm·민족차), 하악 구치부 하치조관(제2대구치 4.65 mm로 가장 가깝고 73% 언더컷, 이중 위험), 협측 갭 ≥2 mm DBBM·협측판 <2 mm 증대. 1차 안정성은 갭 골이식이 아닌 소켓 너머 천연골이 결정(virtual BIC는 비이식군에서만 ISQ 상관). 술전 CBCT 필수. 로딩 타이밍 결과지표는 비표준화로 비교 신뢰도 제한.

## Summary

Immediate implant placement (IIP) puts an implant into a fresh extraction socket, where primary stability and final position depend on the bone *beyond and around* the socket — exactly where critical anatomy sits. This overview maps the site-specific anatomic constraints and ties them to the mechanical reality of how immediate implants gain stability.

**Anterior maxilla — the incisive canal (IC).** Palatally angulated anterior implants risk encroaching on the incisive canal, causing failed osseointegration or neurosensory disturbance. Al-Amery 2015 (CBCT, n=90) provides normative dimensions: IC mean length 16.33 mm, mid-canal width 3.85 mm, incisive foramen 2.80 × 3.49 mm, and anterior maxillary bone thickness 7.63 mm (the labial-to-IC safety corridor for palatally angled implants). Two planning-relevant modifiers: males have significantly larger canals and thicker anterior bone, and anterior bone thins with age even in dentate patients. Critically, the Mongoloid sample showed longer, more slanted-curved canals than European reports, so Western-derived planning norms may underestimate IC encroachment risk in Asian patients — directly relevant to Korean practice.

**Posterior mandible — IAC proximity and lingual plate perforation (LPP).** Behfarnia 2025 (SR+MA, 12 CBCT studies, 1,834 patients) quantifies the two governing risks. Pooled root-apex-to-IAC distance is 5.14 mm (2nd premolar), 6.32 mm (1st molar), and — counterintuitively shortest — 4.65 mm (2nd molar). The second molar is therefore the highest-risk site for inferior alveolar nerve injury, and it is simultaneously the most undercut (U-type ridge in 73%), making it most prone to lingual cortical perforation. The same site is both closest to the nerve and most likely to perforate lingually. The clinical rule is a ≥2 mm IAC safety margin plus mandatory cross-sectional CBCT assessment of ridge morphology before posterior mandibular IIP.

**The buccal wall — gap and plate.** Greenstein 2013 (narrative review, 14 guidelines) governs the buccal aspect, the most failure-prone wall because the thin buccal plate resorbs and drives recession. Decision thresholds: a "jumping distance" gap <2 mm usually heals spontaneously (leave open or add DBBM for contour), a gap ≥2 mm warrants DBBM grafting, and gaps >3–4 mm can fill but less predictably. Buccal plate >2 mm rarely resorbs vertically; <1–2 mm warrants hard-tissue augmentation. About one-third of patients show ~1 mm buccal recession after IIP. (Note: immediate vs healed-site implant *survival* is equivalent at 97.3–99%.)

**Why anatomy = stability, not just safety.** Askin Ekinci 2026 (pilot RCT, n=28; immediate+graft vs immediate no-graft vs healed) shows the mechanical link. Healed sites achieved significantly greater primary stability than immediate sites (insertion torque p=0.007, intraoperative ISQ p=0.014), and grafting the peri-implant gap conferred *no* primary-stability advantage. Most importantly, virtual bone-implant contact (BIC) predicted early stability (torque, intraoperative ISQ, ISQ-60) only in the ungrafted immediate group — meaning native bone engagement beyond the socket, not graft material, drives early stability. This closes the loop with the anatomic sections: the apical/lateral native bone that provides immediate-implant stability is the very bone that contains the incisive canal, the IAC, and the lingual undercut. Anatomic safety margins and stability-providing bone are the same territory.

**A methodological caution on comparing outcomes.** Pannuti 2026 (SR of 5 prospective studies on maxillary loading timing) found patient- and clinician-reported outcome measurement is not standardized: VAS dominated (80% of studies), 12 distinct clinician-reported outcomes were used with no consensus, and only 1 of 5 studies reported assessor calibration. So claims comparing immediate vs early vs delayed loading on patient-centered grounds — and by extension cross-protocol IIP comparisons — are currently methodologically unreliable. Interpret site/protocol outcome comparisons with this limitation in mind.

## Pre-Operative Checklist (CBCT-driven)

1. **Anterior maxilla** → measure incisive canal length/width and labial-to-IC bone thickness on cross-section; plan angulation to keep the apex labial to the canal; do not rely on Western norms for Asian patients.
2. **Posterior mandible** → measure root-apex-to-IAC distance (expect the 2nd molar to be closest, ~4.65 mm); keep ≥2 mm margin; read the cross-sectional ridge for U-type undercut (highest at 2nd molar, 73%) to avoid lingual perforation.
3. **Buccal wall** → assess buccal plate thickness and anticipated gap; <2 mm plate or ≥2 mm gap → plan DBBM/augmentation; expect ~1 mm recession in ~1/3 of cases.
4. **Stability planning** → rely on native apical/lateral bone for primary stability, not gap grafting; graft is for dimensional/esthetic goals. Where native bone is insufficient (immediate sites generally < healed), consider staging.
5. **Outcome interpretation** → treat cross-study/loading-timing patient-centered comparisons cautiously given non-standardized PROM/CROM (Pannuti 2026).

## Evidence Gaps

- No Korean/Korean-specific incisive-canal normative CBCT dataset in our holdings (Al-Amery is Malaysian Chinese/Malay).
- Buccal-plate-thickness thresholds rest on narrative-review synthesis (Greenstein), not long-term human RCT.
- Virtual BIC–stability correlation is a single n=28 pilot RCT needing larger validation.
- Loading-timing PROM/CROM standardization is explicitly unmet (Pannuti 2026).
