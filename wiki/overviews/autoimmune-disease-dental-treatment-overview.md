## 한국어 핵심요약

> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - 핵심 명제: 자가면역질환(Autoimmune Disease)은 치과치료에 **3개 축**으로 영향 — ① 임플란트/외과 성적, ② 치주-전신 양방향 연관, ③ 구강점막 병변·구강건조. 질환이 **조절되면 대부분 치료 가능**하나 위생·추적·약물관리가 관건. 위키 8편 + 기존 페이지로 합성.
> - **임플란트 생존 — 대체로 일반인과 동등**: 9개 자가면역질환 55편 종합(Esimekara 2022)에서 생존율 일반인 수준; 단 **2차 Sjögren·미란성 구강편평태선(erosive OLP)** 에서 peri-mucositis·변연골소실(MBL)↑.
> - **전신질환 일반 원칙(ITI)**: 조절된 당뇨·골다공증·류마티스관절염(RA)·HIV는 골유착(osseointegration) 저하 근거 없음; SSRI/PPI는 예비적 부정 신호; **항흡수제→약물관련 악골괴사(MRONJ)** 주의 (Aghaloo 2019).
> - **Sjögren 증후군**: 임플란트 생존 ~94%·삶의질 개선(Almeida 2017); 그러나 **구강건조(xerostomia)가 재발우식·수복물 실패 2.6–2.9배**↑(Poudel 2026) → 예방(불소·타액관리)이 성패 좌우.
> - **전신경화증(Systemic Sclerosis, scleroderma)**: 임플란트 생존 89–100%, 질환 자체 영향 없음; **소구증(microstomia)** 으로 접근·위생 곤란이 핵심 난제(Mosaddad 2023).
> - **류마티스관절염(RA) ↔ 치주염**: 치주염 환자에서 RA 위험 **69%↑**(OR 1.69, >100만명; Qiao 2020) — 공유 염증·시트룰린화(citrullination, *P. gingivalis*) 기전; 치주치료가 RA 활성도에도 영향 가능.
> - **전신홍반루푸스(SLE)**: 구강궤양이 가장 흔한 구강증상(경구개 호발), 타액분비저하·Sjögren 동반 흔함(García-Ríos 2022).
> - **자가면역 수포성질환**: 천포창(Pemphigus Vulgaris, PV)·점막유천포창(Mucous Membrane Pemphigoid, MMP)은 **구강이 주병소**(PV→협점막·MMP→박리성 치은염), 진단은 **직접면역형광(DIF)** (IgG/C3); PV 전신 스테로이드·MMP 국소 치료(Morais 2024).
> - **약물 고려**: 장기 코르티코스테로이드(부신억제→스트레스 용량), 면역억제제(감염위험), 항흡수제(MRONJ), NSAID(심혈관·소화기) — 협진·병력 청취 필수.
> - **확신도**: 임플란트 생존 동등성 = 중등도(다수 SR, 단 증례군·후향 위주); RA-치주 = 중등도(SR+MA); 구강점막·구강건조 결과 = 낮음~중등도(서술적 SR, RoB 높음). 전반적으로 **"조절된 질환 + 엄격한 위생/추적/예방"** 이 공통 성공 조건.

## One-line Summary

Synthesis of 8 wiki papers (+ existing Sjögren/OLP/corticosteroid pages) on dental care in autoimmune disease: across implant/surgical outcomes, the periodontal–systemic link, and oral mucosal/xerostomia management, autoimmune patients can generally be treated successfully when disease is controlled — implant survival approximates the general population, periodontitis raises rheumatoid-arthritis risk (~1.7×), and the dominant clinical burdens are xerostomia-driven caries/restoration failure, microstomia access (scleroderma), oral mucosal lesions (SLE, pemphigus/MMP), and medication management.

## 한줄요약

위키 8편 종합: 자가면역질환 환자는 질환이 조절되면 임플란트 생존이 일반인과 비슷하고 치과치료가 대체로 가능하나, 치주염이 RA 위험을 ~1.7배 높이고 구강건조(우식·수복실패)·소구증·구강점막병변·약물(스테로이드/항흡수제)이 주된 관리 부담이다.

## Synthesis

According to the 8 autoimmune-dental papers held in this wiki (plus existing Sjögren-classification, OLP, and corticosteroid pages), the clinical bottom line is: **autoimmune disease is rarely an absolute contraindication to dental treatment — with disease control, strict hygiene, prevention, and follow-up, implant and restorative outcomes approximate those of healthy patients — but each disease imposes a characteristic burden** (xerostomia, microstomia, mucosal fragility, bone/medication risk). Three axes organize the evidence.

### 1. Implant & surgical outcomes — generally comparable, disease-modified

The comprehensive critical-review anchor is [[implants/esimekara-2022-dental-implants-autoimmune-diseases-sr]] (55 studies, 9 autoimmune diseases): implant **survival rates are comparable to the general population**, but patients with **secondary Sjögren's syndrome or erosive oral lichen planus** show more peri-mucositis and marginal bone loss. The systemic-risk framework comes from [[implants/aghaloo-2019-systemic-diseases-medications-implant-osseointegration-sr]] (ITI consensus): controlled diabetes, osteoporosis, RA, and HIV show **no evidence of reduced osseointegration**, while antiresorptive-related osteonecrosis (MRONJ) and preliminary SSRI/PPI signals warrant caution. Disease-specific evidence: **Sjögren's** implants survive ~94% with improved quality of life ([[implants/almeida-2017-dental-implants-sjogren-syndrome-sr]]); **systemic sclerosis** implants survive 89–100% with no discernible disease effect, the central challenge being **microstomia** limiting access and hygiene ([[implants/mosaddad-2023-dental-implants-systemic-sclerosis-sr]]).

### 2. The periodontal–systemic (autoimmune) link

Periodontitis is bidirectionally tied to rheumatoid arthritis: [[periodontics/qiao-2020-rheumatoid-arthritis-risk-periodontitis-sr-ma]] (13 studies, >1 million subjects) found periodontitis patients carry a **69% higher RA risk (OR 1.69)**, strongest for incident RA and disease duration >5 years — consistent with shared inflammatory pathways and *Porphyromonas gingivalis*–driven protein citrullination generating anti-citrullinated protein antibodies (ACPA). Clinically this makes periodontal screening/treatment part of the medical co-management of RA patients.

### 3. Oral mucosal manifestations & xerostomia

Autoimmune disease frequently presents in the mouth, sometimes first: **SLE** — oral ulcers are the most common oral feature (hard-palate predilection), with frequent hyposalivation/secondary Sjögren ([[oral-medicine/garcia-rios-2022-oral-manifestations-systemic-lupus-erythematosus-sr]]); **autoimmune blistering disease** — pemphigus vulgaris (PV) and mucous membrane pemphigoid (MMP) are mouth-dominant (PV → buccal mucosa erosions, MMP → desquamative gingivitis), diagnosed by **direct immunofluorescence** (intercellular IgG in PV, basement-membrane IgG/C3 in MMP), treated with systemic corticosteroids (PV) vs more localized therapy (MMP) ([[oral-medicine/morais-2024-pemphigus-vulgaris-mucous-membrane-pemphigoid-oral-sr]]). Cutting across diseases, **xerostomia** (Sjögren, post-radiation, drug-induced) is a major outcome modifier: it raises restoration failure ~2.6–2.9× through recurrent caries and modestly lowers implant survival in radiation-induced cases ([[oral-medicine/poudel-2026-xerostomia-dental-treatment-outcomes-sr]]) — making aggressive caries prevention (fluoride, saliva management) decisive.

### 4. Medication & cross-cutting management

Autoimmune patients are usually on immunomodulators that reshape dental risk: long-term **corticosteroids** (adrenal suppression — consider stress-dose; candidiasis), **immunosuppressants/biologics** (infection risk, possible antibiotic prophylaxis), **antiresorptives** (MRONJ risk before extractions/implants), and **NSAIDs** (GI/cardiovascular). These are detailed in the existing [[overviews/drug-systemic-disease-dental-management-overview]] and corticosteroid/MRONJ pages.

### Clinical bottom line

Treat the **controlled** autoimmune patient much like a healthy one, but: (1) confirm disease control + coordinate with the rheumatologist/physician; (2) front-load **caries prevention** wherever xerostomia exists; (3) plan around **microstomia** in scleroderma; (4) maintain **meticulous peri-implant hygiene + close recall** (erosive OLP / secondary Sjögren raise peri-mucositis); (5) **biopsy + DIF** any desquamative gingivitis / chronic erosion; (6) manage **medication risks** (steroid cover, MRONJ, infection). Evidence is mostly low-to-moderate certainty (case series / descriptive SRs), so individualized risk assessment remains essential.
