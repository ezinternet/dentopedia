## One-line Summary
Synthesis of 6 REP papers (3 SR+MA, 1 prospective-microbiology, 1 narrative-review): REP/revitalization and MAP have equivalent survival (~97%) and success (~93%), but REP is significantly superior for root elongation and dentin wall thickening; LPRF scaffold is NOT recommended.

## Summary

Regenerative endodontic procedures (재생근관치료, REP) — also called revitalization or pulp revascularization — are the preferred approach for necrotic immature permanent teeth with open apices, where preserving root development potential is the primary clinical goal. The evidence base (2017–2024) consistently shows that REP and mineral trioxide aggregate (MTA) apical plug (MAP) achieve equivalent clinical and radiographic success, but only REP allows continued root elongation and dentin wall thickening — a critical structural advantage in children at risk for cervical root fracture.

---

## The Core Distinction: Apexification vs REP

| Goal | Procedure | Root development |
|---|---|---|
| Apical barrier formation only | Ca(OH)₂ apexification, MTA apical plug (MAP) | Arrested — walls remain thin |
| Resumed root elongation + wall thickening | REP / revitalization | Continued — fracture risk ↓ |

In traumatized immature permanent teeth (IPT), thin dentinal walls and the large open apex create a high fracture risk if root development is permanently arrested (Zhou 2022). This is the structural argument for REP over apexification in young patients.

---

## Survival and Success: REP ≈ MAP

**Torabinejad 2017 (SR+MA):**

| Outcome | MAP | REP | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pooled survival | 97.1% | 97.8% | NS |
| Pooled success | 94.6% | 91.3% | NS |

Evidence level: LOW (predominantly case series/retrospective). [근거강함 for the finding itself; design limitation acknowledged]

---

## Root Development: REP > MAP

Both Panda 2022 and Stefanidou 2024 report the same conclusion:

- **Root length increase**: revitalization significantly > apexification (p statistically significant, both SR+MA)
- **Dentin wall thickness increase**: revitalization significantly > apexification (p statistically significant, both SR+MA)
- Survival/success rates: no significant difference

Clinical implication: for a child with an immature traumatized or carious necrotic permanent tooth, REP is the biologically superior choice when the primary goal is structural root completion. [합의수준]

---

## Apical Periodontitis: REP Still Effective

Meschi 2023 (IEJ SR+MA, pulp necrosis + apical periodontitis):
- Clinical symptom resolution: high in revitalization
- Periapical radiographic healing: favorable
- Application in mature teeth: possible but limited evidence [합의수준]

Apical periodontitis alone does not disqualify a case from REP — adequate disinfection is the critical factor.

---

## Disinfection Protocol and LPRF Warning

**Sellami 2023 (prospective microbiology, n=14, qPCR 4 time points):**

Universal flora in 100% of REP cases:
- Fusobacterium nucleatum
- Treponema denticola
- Enterococcus faecalis

Session 1 (1.5% NaOCl): significant total bacterial load reduction maintained through session 2.
Session 2 (17% EDTA): further maintained disinfection + smear layer removal.

**LPRF (leukocyte and platelet-rich fibrin) as scaffold: NOT recommended.**
2/14 LPRF cases required retreatment; 2 additional had persistent apical periodontitis at 3-year recall. The control (no LPRF) group showed full clinical and radiographic healing. [합의수준]

Standard protocol:
- Disinfection: 1.5% NaOCl + 17% EDTA across two sessions
- Scaffold: blood clot (blood clot induction still standard); avoid LPRF
- Coronal barrier: MTA or Biodentine (biocompatible, seals coronal access)

---

## Histological Reality

Regenerated tissue in REP is often cementum- or bone-like, not true pulp-dentin complex (Zhou 2022). This has two implications:
1. Vitality tests may remain negative even after successful REP
2. The tissue provides structural benefit (wall thickening) without necessarily restoring pulp sensibility — manage patient expectations accordingly

## Decision Summary

```
Immature permanent tooth, pulp necrosis
    ↓
Some vital pulp remaining?
    → Yes → Partial pulpotomy (VPT) ← see vital-pulp-therapy-decision-ladder
    → No (complete necrosis) ↓
Age / Root development priority?
    → Young patient, root development goal → REP / revitalization
    → Older patient, rapid closure needed → MAP (MTA apical plug)
Both achieve equivalent survival; REP preferred when root elongation is the goal
```
