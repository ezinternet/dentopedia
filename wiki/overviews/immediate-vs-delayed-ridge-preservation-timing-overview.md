> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - 이 페이지는 발치 후 식립 **시점(timing)** 결정을 두 축으로 통합한다: ① 즉시(Type 1) vs 조기(Type 2/3) vs 지연(Type 4) 시점 스펙트럼(ITI Chen & Buser 분류), ② **즉시식립(Immediate Implant Placement, IIP) vs 치조제보존 후 지연식립(Alveolar Ridge Preservation, ARP)** 의 직접 비교.
> - **핵심 명제**: 선택된 증례에서 임플란트 **생존율은 시점에 무관**하다 — Patel 2023 SR+MA(10편, 700 임플란트)에서 즉시 97.4% vs 지연 97.5%, 위험비(Risk Ratio, RR) 0.99 (95% CI 0.96–1.02, I²=0%). 따라서 결정은 생존이 아니라 **변연골소실(Marginal Bone Loss, MBL)·심미·합병증·치료시간 trade-off**로 내린다.
> - **IIP vs ARP head-to-head**(Fan 2024 SR+MA, 11 RCT, n=701): IIP가 MBL을 더 잘 보존(비대구치 MD −0.36 mm, 대구치 MD −0.41 mm)하지만 **술후 합병증은 IIP에서 더 많다**(p<0.05). 분홍심미점수(Pink Esthetic Score, PES)·임플란트 실패·치조폭(Horizontal Width, HW)·치은변연(Free Gingival Margin, FGM) 변화·만족도는 양 군 동등.
> - **위험 집중점은 즉시(Type 1) 식립**: Tonetti 2017 RCT(n=124) — 즉시 vs 12주 지연에서 골증대 필요 72% vs 44%, 창상실패 26% vs 5%, 부적절 PES 42% vs 19%.
> - **조기(Type 2/3) 식립은 위험 균형 hedge**: Bassir 2019 SR+MA(12편) — 조기는 즉시·지연과 실패율 동등하면서 즉시보다 MBL이 적음(가중평균차 −0.14 mm).
> - **ITI Type 1–4 시점 프레임**(Thoma 2026): Type 1 즉시(당일, 생존 95–98%) → Type 2 조기/연조직치유(4–8주, 96–99%) → Type 3 조기/부분골치유(12–16주, 97–99%) → Type 4 지연(>6개월, >95%). Type 1 전제조건 = 활성감염 없음 + 4벽 온전 + 식립토크(Insertion Torque, IT) ≥35 Ncm + gap graft.
> - **결정 동인**: 협측골판(buccal wall) 무결성, 치주표현형(periodontal phenotype, 얇음/두꺼움), 근단감염, 심미 요구도. 협측골판 손상·얇은 표현형·근단병소·고소선(high smile line) → 시점을 늦춤(Type 2/3 또는 ARP).
> - **ARP가 즉시보다 선호되는 경우**: 협측골판이 광범위 결손·감염 소켓·심미부에서 연조직 부피 확보가 필요하고 즉시 1차안정성 확보가 불확실할 때. ARP는 결손 복잡도를 낮추고 식립 위치를 최적화하나 **추가 골유도재생(Guided Bone Regeneration, GBR) 필요를 제거하지는 못한다**(연계 페이지 [[overviews/socket-preservation-arp-overview]]).
> - **즉시가 ARP보다 합리적인 경우**: 협측골판 온전 + 근단감염 없음 + 두꺼운 표현형 + IT ≥35 Ncm 단일치 심미증례 — 치료시간·내원횟수 단축, MBL 보존 우위, gap graft ± 결합조직이식(Connective Tissue Graft, CTG) 병행. 단 큰 골결손(≥5 mm)이라도 술자 숙련 + GBR + 지연 임시보철이면 10년 지연-동등 가능(Meijer 2024, Nassani 2025) — default 아님.
> - **최신 근거 추가(2024–2025, 초록수준)**: Ickroth 2024(즉시 vs 조기 단일치 SR+MA), Riachi 2024(즉시/조기/지연 SR+MA), Gareb 2024(이식부위 임플란트 SR+MA), Jamil 2025(SR+MA) — 시점 간 생존 동등 명제를 보강하나 전문 미확인이라 수치는 본문 인용 보류.
> - **임상 takeaway**: 시점 선택은 "더 빠른가"가 아니라 "이 환자·이 소켓·이 조건에서 안전한 최조기 시점은 언제인가"(Thoma 2026)다. 생존은 시점 무관 → 협측골·표현형·감염·심미요구로 결정하라.

## One-line Summary

Across the ITI Type 1–4 timing spectrum, implant survival is broadly equivalent (Patel 2023 SR+MA: 97.4% immediate vs 97.5% delayed, RR 0.99, I²=0%), so the decision turns on MBL, esthetic, complication, and treatment-time trade-offs: a direct IIP-vs-ARP SR+MA (Fan 2024, 11 RCTs, n=701) shows immediate placement preserves marginal bone better (molar MD −0.41 mm) but causes more postoperative complications, with PES/failure/width/satisfaction equivalent; immediate (Type 1) concentrates the esthetic/biological risk (Tonetti 2017 RCT), while early (Type 2/3) placement matches immediate/delayed for survival with less marginal bone loss than immediate — making the buccal wall, phenotype, infection, and esthetic demand (not survival) the real decision drivers.

## 한줄요약

ITI Type 1–4 시점 전반에서 임플란트 생존율은 대체로 동등해(Patel 2023 SR+MA: 즉시 97.4% vs 지연 97.5%, RR 0.99, I²=0%) 결정은 MBL·심미·합병증·치료시간 trade-off로 내린다: IIP vs ARP 직접 비교 SR+MA(Fan 2024, 11 RCT, n=701)에서 즉시식립은 변연골을 더 잘 보존(대구치 MD −0.41 mm)하나 술후 합병증이 많고 PES·실패·폭·만족도는 동등; 즉시(Type 1)는 심미·생물학적 위험을 집중시키고(Tonetti 2017), 조기(Type 2/3)는 생존 동등에 MBL이 적어 — 생존이 아니라 협측골판·표현형·감염·심미요구가 실제 결정 동인이다.

## Thesis

Two intersecting timing axes govern post-extraction implant planning:

1. **The placement-timing spectrum** (ITI Chen & Buser classification, reviewed by Thoma 2026): **Type 1 immediate** (day of extraction), **Type 2 early/soft-tissue healing** (4–8 wk), **Type 3 early/partial bone healing** (12–16 wk), **Type 4 delayed** (≥6 mo).
2. **The IIP-vs-ARP fork**: place immediately into the fresh socket (IIP) **or** graft the socket at extraction (ARP) and place into a healed/regenerated ridge later.

The unifying finding is that **survival is timing-agnostic** in selected cases. Patel 2023's SR+MA (10 studies, 700 implants) found no survival difference (RR 0.99, 95% CI 0.96–1.02, I²=0%; 97.4% immediate vs 97.5% delayed). Because survival does not discriminate, the decision is made on the *trade-offs that live elsewhere*:

- **Immediate placement preserves marginal bone better than ARP** but carries more complications. Fan 2024's direct IIP-vs-ARP SR+MA (11 RCTs, n=701) found IIP significantly reduced MBL (non-molar MD −0.36 mm; molar MD −0.41 mm, 95% CI −0.49 to −0.32) but produced significantly more postoperative complications (p<0.05), with PES, implant failure, horizontal width change, FGM change, and patient satisfaction all equivalent.
- **Immediate (Type 1) concentrates esthetic and biological risk.** Tonetti 2017's RCT (n=124) showed immediate vs 12-week delayed needed more augmentation (72% vs 44%), caused more wound failures (26% vs 5%), and yielded worse pink esthetics (42% vs 19% inadequate).
- **Early (Type 2/3) placement is the risk-balanced hedge.** Bassir 2019's SR+MA (12 studies) found early placement matches immediate and delayed for failure while losing less marginal bone than immediate into a fresh socket (WMD −0.14 mm).
- **ARP does not eliminate the augmentation requirement** — it reduces defect complexity and optimizes implant positioning (see the ARP deep-dive [[overviews/socket-preservation-arp-overview]]). So choosing ARP-then-delayed over immediate is justified by *risk reduction in an unfavorable socket*, not by a survival or esthetic advantage.

The decision drivers, then, are anatomical and biological — **buccal-wall integrity, periodontal phenotype, periapical infection, and esthetic demand** — not survival. This page is the timing-and-ARP-comparison synthesis; the within-timing site/graft/loading detail lives in [[overviews/immediate-implant-decision-ladder]], and the within-ARP material/classification detail lives in [[overviews/socket-preservation-arp-overview]].

## Clinical Decision Points

### A. Choosing the timing tier (immediate / early / delayed)

1. **Esthetic-zone single tooth, intact buccal plate, no apical infection, thick phenotype, IT ≥35 Ncm achievable** → **immediate (Type 1)** is reasonable and time-efficient; pair with gap graft ± CTG (Thoma 2026 prerequisites; Tonetti 2017 cautionary anchor). [근거강함, selected cases]
2. **Esthetic zone but thin/compromised plate, soft-tissue deficiency, or any apical lesion** → favor **early (Type 2/3)**: equivalent survival, less marginal bone loss than immediate, lower wound-failure risk (Bassir 2019; "Type 2 often safer than Type 1" — Thoma 2026). [근거강함]
3. **Periapical lesion > 5 mm / active infection** → dominant real-world contraindication to immediate; debride and place early/delayed (Bineviciute 2024; Pranckeviciene 2024). [합의수준]
4. **Extensive bone loss / major augmentation needed / low esthetic demand** → **delayed (Type 4)** is the conservative default (Thoma 2026). But a large buccal defect (≥5 mm) is *not* an absolute bar to immediate when expertise + GBR + **deferred** provisionalization are available — 10-yr outcomes can equal delayed (Meijer 2024; Nassani 2025). Operator-dependent, not a default. [근거강함, conditional]

### B. IIP vs ARP-then-delayed (when both are technically possible)

5. **When the socket favors immediate (intact walls, primary stability)** → IIP wins on **MBL preservation** (molar MD −0.41 mm vs ARP) and treatment time, at the cost of **more postoperative complications** (Fan 2024). PES, failure, width, and satisfaction are a wash — so trade time/complication tolerance against marginal-bone benefit.
6. **When the socket is unfavorable** (extensive buccal defect, infection, or esthetic-zone soft-tissue volume needed and primary stability uncertain) → **ARP-then-delayed** reduces defect complexity and optimizes implant positioning, lowering the chance the site is unplaceable — but it does **not** eliminate the need for GBR at placement ([[overviews/socket-preservation-arp-overview]]). Choose ARP for *risk reduction in a bad socket*, not for a survival/esthetic edge.
7. **Grafted/regenerated sites (the ARP-delayed arm)** → outcomes of implants in grafted sites are addressed by Gareb 2024 (abstract-only) — to be quantified once full text is ingested.

### C. Loading-timing note

8. Loading timing (immediate vs early vs delayed loading) is a **separate axis** from placement timing and is not resolved by survival data either; in the edentulous maxilla, outcome reporting across loading-timing studies is non-standardized, so cross-study patient-centered comparison is currently unreliable (Pannuti 2026). Decide loading on primary stability (IT/ISQ) and prosthesis type, not on placement timing alone. See [[overviews/implant-loading-protocol-prosthesis-type-overview]].

## Gaps & Future Research

- **Few head-to-head RCTs** isolate early vs immediate vs delayed with standardized esthetic endpoints; "early" (Type 2 vs Type 3) is inconsistently defined across studies.
- **IIP-vs-ARP direct comparisons** remain limited (Fan 2024 pooled only 11 RCTs, n=701) and heterogeneous on graft material and follow-up; long-term (≥10 y) IIP-vs-ARP esthetic/biological stability data are thin.
- **Four recent SR+MAs (Ickroth 2024, Riachi 2024, Gareb 2024, Jamil 2025) are abstract-only** in the wiki — ingesting their full texts will let this page move from directional reinforcement to quantified synthesis.
- **Patient-reported outcomes rarely differ by timing** despite clinical/esthetic differences (Tonetti 2017); PROM/CROM reporting is not standardized (Pannuti 2026) — better instruments are needed.
- **AI + CBCT risk stratification** for personalized "safe earliest placement" (Thoma 2026) is an emerging but unvalidated direction.
