## 한국어 핵심요약

> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - 핵심 명제: 레진-상아질 결합은 시간이 지나며 **열화**한다. 두 기전 — ① 친수성 수지의 **가수분해(hydrolysis)**, ② 물이 많고 수지가 부족한 콜라겐 기질의 **효소분해(MMP·cysteine cathepsin)**. **공통 원흉은 잔류 물(water)** 이며 형태학적으로 nanoleakage·water-tree로 나타난다. 위키 15편을 6축으로 합성.
> - **혼성층(hybrid layer)의 양면성**: 결합의 핵심이자 가장 약한 고리. 노화 시 콜라겐 노출·수지 용출로 붕괴 (Breschi 2018, Tjäderhane 2015).
> - **Water-tree 개념**: Tay & Pashley가 전기케이블 절연체 열화 개념을 차용 — 혼성층 위/내부에 수지상(dendritic) 수분채널 형성, 노화 시 nanoleakage가 reticular→water-tree로 이행 (Tay 2003 ×2); 세관 폐쇄 시 소실 = **경상아질 수분 flux가 원인** (Tay 2005).
> - **5대 내구성 전략(랜드마크 통합)**: ①전환율↑·esterase 저항 ②MMP 억제제 ③콜라겐 가교 ④에탄올 습윤접착 ⑤생체모방 재광화 (Liu 2011, J Dent Res).
> - **MMP 억제 — 임상 권고 가능**: 클로르헥시딘(CHX)이 즉시 결합강도엔 무효하나 6/12/24개월 노화 결합강도를 유의 보존(SR+MA, Kiuru 2021) → CHX 적용 권고.
> - **MDP 화학결합 기전**: 10-MDP가 hydroxyapatite와 이온결합해 **자기조립 nanolayer(MDP-Ca)** 형성 + 산-염기 저항층 (Yoshihara 2011, Carrilho 2019). **단 반전**: 상용 접착제는 nanolayering이 희소(15% 실험용서만, Tian 2015)하고, in-vitro 우월성(Fehrenbach 2021)에도 **NCCL RCT선 임상 이점 없음(p=0.05, de Oliveira 2021)**.
> - **습윤접착 도그마 재검토**: 에탄올 습윤+CHX는 in-vitro 내구성↑(Talungchit 2014)이나, etch-and-rinse 건조 vs 습윤 NCCL RCT 5편서 **유지율·과민 차이 없음**(Forville 2024) → "촉촉한 상아질" 절대원칙 약화.
> - **보조 전략**: 산성 상아질 conditioner가 장기 결합 개선·선택적 extrafibrillar 탈회로 건조접착 보조 (Zheng 2024).
> - **임상 한 줄**: 결합 수명을 늘리려면 **물을 줄이고(용매 충분 휘발·습윤 과하지 않게)·효소를 막고(CHX)·콜라겐을 보존**하라. MDP/nanolayer·습윤접착의 in-vitro 우월성은 임상서 희석되므로 술식 규율(전환율·오염차단·CHX)이 더 결정적.
> - **확신도**: 열화 기전·water-tree·MDP nanolayering = in-vitro 강함; MMP-CHX = SR+MA 중등도; 습윤접착·MDP 임상효과 = RCT/SR이 in-vitro와 괴리(낮음~중등도).

## One-line Summary

Synthesis of 15 wiki papers on why resin-dentin bonds fail over time and how to prevent it: bonds degrade through hydrolysis of hydrophilic resin plus MMP/cathepsin collagenolysis of a water-rich matrix (manifest as nanoleakage/water-trees), and the durability strategies — ethanol-wet bonding, MMP inhibition (CHX), collagen crosslinking, MDP nanolayering, biomimetic remineralization, dentin conditioners — all converge on removing water, blocking enzymes, and preserving collagen; notably MDP nanolayering and wet-bonding show in-vitro superiority that thins out in clinical RCTs.

## 한줄요약

위키 15편 종합: 레진-상아질 결합은 친수성수지 가수분해 + MMP/cathepsin 콜라겐분해(잔류 물이 매개, nanoleakage/water-tree)로 열화하며, 대응 전략(에탄올습윤·MMP억제·가교·MDP nanolayer·재광화·conditioner)은 모두 "물 제거·효소 차단·콜라겐 보존"으로 수렴 — MDP·습윤접착의 in-vitro 우월성은 임상서 약화.

## Synthesis

According to the 15 resin-dentin papers held in this wiki (all in `resin-bonding`), the clinical bottom line is: **the resin-dentin bond is intrinsically time-limited, and every durability strategy is ultimately a way to remove water, inhibit collagenolytic enzymes, or preserve/replace the collagen matrix.** Six axes organize the evidence.

### 1. The hybrid layer: essential yet the weak link

The hybrid layer (resin-infiltrated demineralized collagen) is both the bonding mechanism and the primary failure site. Reviews establish that it degrades over time via collagen exposure and resin leaching ([[resin-bonding/breschi-2018-dentin-bonding-collagen-structure-bond-preservation-review]], [[resin-bonding/tjaderhane-2015-dentin-bonding-can-we-make-it-last-review]]).

### 2. Two degradation mechanisms + the role of water

The landmark unifying review [[resin-bonding/liu-2011-limitations-bonding-dentin-bond-degradation-review]] (J Dent Res) frames it: bonds fail through (1) **hydrolysis** of suboptimally polymerized hydrophilic resin and (2) **collagenolysis** of the water-rich, resin-sparse matrix by matrix metalloproteinases (MMPs) and cysteine cathepsins — and proposes five experimental strategies (below). Water is the shared culprit, made visible by silver-tracer nanoleakage.

### 3. Water-treeing & nanoleakage (the Tay–Pashley axis)

Borrowing from high-voltage cable-insulation science, [[resin-bonding/tay-2003-water-treeing-degradation-dentin-adhesives]] showed dendritic water-filled channels ("water trees") forming within/above the hybrid layer; [[resin-bonding/tay-2003-aging-two-modes-nanoleakage-bonded-dentin]] defined two nanoleakage modes (reticular within the hybrid layer vs water-trees in the adhesive) and showed aging shifts expression toward water-trees; [[resin-bonding/tay-2005-tubular-occlusion-prevents-water-treeing-self-etch]] proved transdentinal water flux (not just residual solvent) drives it — occluding tubules eliminates water-treeing.

### 4. MMP inhibition — the one clinically supportable adjunct

Chlorhexidine (CHX) is the most-studied MMP inhibitor: [[resin-bonding/kiuru-2021-mmp-inhibitors-dentin-bonding-sr-ma]] (SR+MA, 740→43 studies) found CHX has **no effect on immediate** bond strength but **significantly preserves aged** bond strength at 6, 12, and 24 months, with the advantage growing over time — supporting routine CHX application.

### 5. MDP chemical bonding & nanolayering — strong in vitro, diluted clinically

10-MDP chemically bonds to hydroxyapatite via ionic interaction, self-assembling into a "nanolayer" (the adhesion-decalcification concept): [[resin-bonding/yoshihara-2011-nanolayering-mdp-enamel-dentin]] (greater on dentin than enamel, concentration- and scrubbing-dependent) and [[resin-bonding/carrilho-2019-mdp-adhesive-interface-stability-sr]] (nanolayer + acid-base resistant zone + MDP-Ca salt). **But the clinical caveats are real**: commercial adhesives show *sparse* nanolayering (only the 15% experimental primer was profuse — [[resin-bonding/tian-2015-paucity-nanolayering-mdp-resin-dentin]]); MDP self-etch adhesives are superior in vitro ([[resin-bonding/fehrenbach-2021-10-mdp-bonding-performance-sr-ma]]) yet show **no clinical NCCL advantage** vs non-MDP (p=0.05, [[resin-bonding/de-oliveira-2021-10-mdp-self-etch-adhesive-dentin-sr]]).

### 6. Wet bonding, conditioners, and the moisture dogma re-examined

Ethanol-wet bonding (replacing water with ethanol before hydrophobic resin) plus CHX improves in-vitro durability ([[resin-bonding/talungchit-2014-ethanol-wet-bonding-chlorhexidine-resin-dentin-durability]]). However, the "moist dentin" dogma is weakening clinically: across 5 split-mouth NCCL RCTs, **dry vs wet etch-and-rinse bonding showed no difference** in retention or sensitivity ([[resin-bonding/forville-2024-moist-dentin-adhesive-systems-reevaluation]], moderate GRADE). Acid-based dentin conditioners can improve long-term durability and aid dry bonding via selective extrafibrillar demineralization ([[resin-bonding/zheng-2024-dentin-conditioners-bond-strength-sr]]).

### Clinical bottom line

To extend bond longevity: (1) **maximize degree of conversion** and evaporate solvents fully; (2) avoid both over-wet and over-dry extremes (the moisture window is wider than the dogma implied); (3) **apply chlorhexidine** (or other MMP inhibitor) to slow collagenolysis; (4) use **MDP-containing** adhesives for their chemical bond and acid-base-resistant zone — but don't expect nanolayering alone to guarantee longevity; (5) preserve collagen (crosslinkers/conditioners) and consider biomimetic remineralization as it matures. In-vitro superiority of MDP nanolayering and ethanol-wet bonding consistently exceeds their measured clinical benefit, so **technique discipline (conversion, contamination control, CHX) matters more than any single monomer**.
