> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - **핵심 질문**: 교합을 재건할 때 기준이 되는 상하악 관계위(reference position)를 무엇으로 잡을 것인가 — 과두 기준의 중심위(Centric Relation, CR)/중심교합(Centric Occlusion, CO)인가, 치아 기준의 최대교두감합위(Maximum Intercuspation Position, MIP=교두감합위 Intercuspal Position, ICP)인가.
> - **용어 정리**: CR=과두가 관절와에 안정적으로 앉은 하악위(치아 무관). CO=그 CR 상태에서의 교합접촉위. MIP/MICP/ICP=치아가 최대로 맞물리는 습관적 위치(과두 위치 무관). 셋 다 "centric"이라 혼용되지만 같지 않다.
> - **사실 1 — CO≠MIP가 정상**: Best Evidence Consensus(Kattadiyil 2021)는 유치악·부분유치악자 **대다수에서 CO와 MIP가 불일치**한다고 결론. 이 둘 사이의 미끄러짐이 **중심활주(centric slide, CR-MIP/CR-ICP discrepancy)**.
> - **사실 2 — 정상 활주의 한계치**: Pullinger 기준 무증상자에서 시상 활주 **>2mm는 거의 없음**(Fornai 2022 인용). 즉 작은 활주는 정상, 큰 활주는 비정상 신호일 수 있음.
> - **논쟁 A (CR 폐기파)**: Zonnenberg/Türp/Greene(2021) — "중심위"는 의미·개념·실용적으로 결함이라 **용어 폐기** 주장; 건강 유치악자는 MIP가 생물학적으로 수용 가능하니 과두위 일상 평가 불필요.
> - **논쟁 B (CR 유지파)**: Goldstein(2022) — CR은 **재현 가능·근거 있는 기준위**(특히 전악재건); 문제는 개념이 아니라 정의·기록법의 비일관성; 다른 위치를 쓰려면 다른 이름을 붙이라.
> - **논쟁 C (절충/모니터링파)**: Fornai(2022) — 용어 "CR"은 폐기하되 임상적으로는 **과두위를 능동 감시**해야(생물학적≠임상적 정상); 대체어로 **기준위(Reference Position, RP)** 제안.
> - **활주와 악관절장애(TMD)**: Jiménez-Silva(2017) SR — CR-ICP 불일치가 다수 연구서 근육통·관절잡음·디스크변위와 연관되나 **근거 질이 낮아(NOS 평균 3.36) 인과 확정 불가**. radej(2023)는 **과두발산형(hyperdivergent) 골격**이 CR→MIP 과두 후하방 변위의 최대 위험인자라고 정리.
> - **MIP의 "정상"도 모호**: Qadeer & Türp(2023) — MIP 교합접촉 수가 **11~70개로 광범위**, 검출지시재(교합지·shimstock·디지털)에 좌우 → 단일 정상치 없음. 즉 "MIP를 받아들이라"는 주장도 측정 표준이 없으면 검증 곤란.
> - **임상 합의점(실무 결론)**: ① **전악수복**처럼 기존 MIP를 잃거나 바꿔야 하면 → **재현 가능한 CR/CO를 기준위로** 채택(Kattadiyil·Goldstein). ② **건강하고 안정적인 유치악 + 소량 활주(<2mm)·무증상**이면 → 기존 **MIP를 수용**(Zonnenberg). ③ **과두 변형/불안정·TMD 징후**면 → 과두 안착을 신뢰 말고 **근육 유도 안정위/조기접촉 없는 ICP**로(Fukushima), 그리고 과두위 변화를 추적(Fornai). 변형 골격(hyperdivergent)·큰 활주는 경계.
> - **공통 한계**: 정의 불일치, 저질·이질적 근거, 측정법 표준 부재. CR↔MIP 우월성을 가리는 고품질 RCT는 없고, 건강인에서 어느 위치로 회복하든 보철 부작용의 결정적 차이는 보고되지 않음.

## One-line Summary

Synthesis of 9 papers on which maxillomandibular reference position should guide occlusal reconstruction — condyle-based centric relation/centric occlusion (CR/CO) vs tooth-based maximum intercuspation (MIP/ICP): CO and MIP are usually non-coincident, the CR–MIP slide is normally <2 mm, and consensus favours restoring full-mouth cases in a reproducible CR/CO while accepting an existing healthy MIP in stable dentate patients with small, asymptomatic slides.

## 한줄요약

교합 재건의 기준위(중심위 CR/중심교합 CO vs 최대교두감합위 MIP/ICP)를 다룬 9편 종합 — CO와 MIP는 대개 불일치하고 정상 중심활주는 <2mm이며, 전악수복은 재현 가능한 CR/CO로 회복하되 안정적·무증상 소량활주 유치악자는 기존 MIP를 수용하는 것이 현재 합의.

## Thesis

The clinical question is not "which single position is universally correct" but **"for this patient, in this clinical situation, which reference position is both reproducible and biologically safe?"** Three facts anchor the answer:

1. **CO and MIP are normally non-coincident** (Kattadiyil 2021): in most dentate/partially dentate people the teeth-defined intercuspal position (MIP/ICP) differs from the condyle-seated occlusal contact position (CO). The difference is the **centric slide (CR–MIP / CR–ICP discrepancy)**.
2. **A small slide is normal; a large one is a flag** — no asymptomatic subject in Pullinger's data had a sagittal slide >2 mm (cited by Fornai 2022).
3. **Neither "normal" is well-measured** — the MIP contact count itself ranges 11–70 depending on the detection method (Qadeer & Türp 2023), and the CR–ICP↔TMD association rests on low-quality evidence (Jiménez-Silva 2017).

The literature then splits on **what to do about condylar position**: abandon the concern in healthy dentate patients (Zonnenberg/Türp/Greene), defend CR as the reproducible restorative reference (Goldstein), or keep monitoring condylar position under a renamed "Reference Position" (Fornai). For unstable/deformed condyles the condyle cannot be trusted as a landmark at all, and a muscle-guided, premature-contact-free ICP is the safer target (Fukushima).

## Evidence Map

| Paper | Design | n / scope | Position favoured | Key finding |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Kattadiyil 2021 | Best Evidence Consensus (BECS) | 313 SR/RCT/MA screened | **CO** for full-mouth rehab | CO≠MIP in most patients; restore complete-mouth rehab in CO; CO–MIP discrepancy ↔ occlusal instability + TMD; no conclusive harm restoring in either in healthy patients |
| Zonnenberg/Türp/Greene 2021 | Narrative review | 70 yr of literature | **MIP** (abandon CR) | "Centric relation" semantically/conceptually/practically flawed → abandon term; in healthy dentate patients MIP is biologically acceptable; no routine condylar assessment |
| Goldstein 2022 | Narrative review | — | **CR** (keep) | CR is a reproducible, evidence-supported reference for diagnosis & full-arch reconstruction; problem is definition/recording inconsistency, not the concept |
| Fornai 2022 | Commentary / rebuttal | — | **RP** (monitor condyle) | Biological ≠ clinically normal; occlusal change alters condyle-fossa relation → monitor condylar position; abandon term "CR", adopt "Reference Position"; Pullinger slide cut-off ≈2 mm; GPT-9 CR definition only 29% consensus |
| Jiménez-Silva 2017 | Systematic review | 20 observational studies | (neutral) | CR–ICP discrepancy associated with muscle/joint disorders in most studies, but low quality (NOS 3.36) + heterogeneity → no definite causal link to TMD |
| Qadeer & Türp 2023 | Systematised review | 18 studies / 4 decades | (neutral) | MIP occlusal contacts range 11–70, method-dependent → no single "normal" count; standardise the indicator |
| Fukushima 2016 | Narrative review (25-yr case) | 1 case | **muscle-guided ICP** | In deformed/unstable condyles CR is unreliable; target a stable ICP where habitual closing terminates without premature contact; HCM endpoint scatters only ≈0.38 mm A-P |
| Radej 2023 | Scoping review | 10 studies | (risk stratification) | Hyperdivergent skeletal pattern is main risk factor for posteroinferior condylar displacement CR→MIP; clinically significant CD ≥2 mm (vert/horiz), ≥0.5 mm (transverse) |
| Kwak 2025 | Prospective case series | 16, ~10 yr | **function-defined CR** | Therapeutic position set at apex of normalized chewing pattern (CM-H); CM-H↔MIP concordance 63% stable over a decade — chewing-position-centered occlusion is durable |

## Clinical Decision Points

**Which reference position do I build to?** — a four-node decision path distilled from the evidence:

1. **Is full-mouth / large reconstruction needed (existing MIP lost or must change)?**
   - **No** → patient keeps a usable MIP. Go to node 2.
   - **Yes** → use a **reproducible CR/CO** as the build reference (Kattadiyil 2021, Goldstein 2022). For a function-led variant, set it at the normalized chewing-position apex (Kwak 2025).

2. **Are the condyles healthy and stable, with no TMD signs?**
   - **No** (deformed/unstable condyle, disc displacement, TMD signs) → do **not** trust condylar seating; build to a **muscle-guided, premature-contact-free ICP** and monitor condylar position over time (Fukushima 2016, Fornai 2022).
   - **Yes** → go to node 3.

3. **Is the CR–MIP slide small (<2 mm sagittal) and asymptomatic?**
   - **Yes** → **accept the existing MIP** as biologically acceptable; no routine condylar repositioning (Zonnenberg 2021). Small slides are normal.
   - **No** (slide >2 mm, hyperdivergent skeletal pattern, or symptomatic) → treat as a flag: full functional/condylar assessment before altering occlusion (Fornai 2022, Radej 2023).

4. **Whatever position is chosen — verify with a standardized contact method.**
   - MIP "normal" contact count is method-dependent (11–70); pick and document one indicator (articulating paper / shimstock / digital) and re-inspect at recall (Qadeer & Türp 2023).

| Clinical scenario | Recommended reference | Primary support |
|---|---|---|
| Complete/extensive full-mouth rehabilitation | CR / CO (reproducible, condyle-seated) | Kattadiyil 2021, Goldstein 2022 |
| Healthy dentate, slide <2 mm, asymptomatic | Existing MIP (accept) | Zonnenberg 2021 |
| Deformed / unstable condyle, TMD signs | Muscle-guided stable ICP + condyle monitoring | Fukushima 2016, Fornai 2022 |
| Hyperdivergent skeleton / slide >2 mm | Flag → functional + condylar assessment first | Radej 2023, Fornai 2022 |

## Gaps & Future Research

- **No head-to-head high-quality RCT** comparing CR/CO-based vs MIP-based reconstruction on long-term prosthodontic and TMD outcomes — the central question is decided by consensus and low-grade evidence.
- **Definition still unresolved**: the GPT-9 definition of centric relation reached only 29% expert consensus (Fornai 2022); competing terms (CR, Reference Position, adapted centric posture, muscular position) are not interoperable.
- **Measurement not standardized**: MIP contact counts (11–70) and CR–MIP slide measurement vary by instrument; digital occlusal analysis vs articulating paper concordance is poor (see [[occlusion/didier-2026-occlusal-contacts-ios-vs-carbon-paper-concordance]]).
- **Causality CR–ICP discrepancy → TMD** remains unestablished; longitudinal studies with validated diagnostic criteria are needed (Jiménez-Silva 2017).
