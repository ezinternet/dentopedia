## 한국어 핵심요약

> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - 핵심 명제: 투명교정(Clear Aligner Therapy, CAT)은 **경도~중등도 비발치 부정교합**에서 고정성장치(Fixed Appliance, FA)와 **교합결과가 동등**하고 치주·편의성에서 우위지만, **복잡 이동(torque·rotation·extrusion·bodily movement)·중증/골격 증례·발치 공간폐쇄**에서는 예측성이 낮거나 열등하다. 보조장치(attachment·TADs·IPR·trimline 설계)가 적응증 범위를 넓힌다. 임상 위키 보유 20편을 9축으로 합성.
> - **효율(교합결과)**: RCT 21편 메타분석(Baneshi 2024, n=970) — 단순 비발치서 ABO/LII/PAR 교합결과 CAT=FA **차이 없음**. 우산고찰(Yassir 2022, 18 SR)도 경중등도엔 효과적이나 **중증·특정 이동엔 열등** 결론.
> - **특정 이동 한계**: 성인 전치 이동(Ubuzima 2025, 12편) — 수직이동 비일관, **FA가 회전·토크 조절 우수**. 발치/복잡(Jaber 2023, 6편) — 결과는 동등하나 FA가 협설경사·교합접촉을 **단기간**에 달성, CAT는 예측-실제 괴리 큼. 치료기간(Alhamwi 2024)은 경중등도서 CAT≈FA.
> - **Class II 전략 — 원심이동(distalization)**: 상악 대구치 ~2 mm 가능(Bhate 2025), 단 **전치 anchorage loss 가능성 높음**; TADs+IPR 병용 시 예측성·안정성 향상(Inchingolo 2023).
> - **Class II 전략 — 하악전방유도(Mandibular Advancement, MA)**: MA aligner vs 기능성장치 골격효과 동등(Yu 2023 n=283 / Huang 2026 n=465), CAT가 **하악전치 순측경사를 더 억제**하나 하악지 성장(Co-Go)은 다소 적음 — 골격보다 치성/postural 경향.
> - **생체역학(설계)**: composite attachment이 전치 토크·회전·근원심이동·후방 anchorage를 대체로 개선(Nucera 2022); trimline 설계(straight/extended margin)가 힘 전달·유지력 좌우(Nakornnoi 2024). → CAT 효율은 "장치 설계 의존적".
> - **상악확장**: 성장기서 CAT 확장은 가능하나 conventional expander 대비 유의 적음(Fonseca-Planells 2026), 확장은 주로 **치조성(dentoalveolar)**, 골격엔 conventional 우위.
> - **안전성(치근흡수)**: CBCT 9편 메타(Butsabul 2024) — **최소** 외흡수(절치 치근길이 −0.56 mm), **상악 중절치 최대**.
> - **치주·구강위생**: CAT가 치태·치은·출혈지수 유리(Baneshi)하나 SR+MA(Crego-Ruiz 2023, I²=99%)·GRADE SR(Alhuwaizi 2026, very low~low)은 **근거 불충분·compliance 의존**으로 신중. 보조로 프로바이오틱이 aligner biofilm을 단기 감소(Thakur 2026, 예비적).
> - **저작근·TMJ**: 저작근 활성/불편은 착용 초기 일시 변화 후 정상화(Almalki 2024), sEMG pooled 차이는 무의(Lekavičiūtė 2024); 턱관절(TMJ) 형태변화는 성인 Class I/II 비발치서 미미(Montasser 2026, serious RoB). → 대체로 중립, 단 근거 약.
> - **가속(adjunct)**: 진동·저출력레이저(LLLT) 등 물리적 보조중재는 CAT 결과를 **일관되게 개선하지 못함**(Sirri 2026, GRADE low~very low).
> - **확신도 종합**: 효율 동등성·치근흡수 = 근거 중상(SR+MA); 치주·저작/TMJ·가속 = 근거 약(high RoB, low certainty); 복잡 이동 열등·설계 의존성 = 일관된 신호.

## One-line Summary

Synthesis of 20 wiki papers on clear aligner therapy (CAT): aligners equal fixed appliances for occlusal outcome in mild-to-moderate non-extraction cases with a periodontal/comfort edge, but are inferior or unpredictable for torque/rotation/extrusion, complex/extraction and severe/skeletal cases; auxiliaries (attachments, TADs, IPR, trimline design) extend the envelope; root resorption is minimal, and masticatory/TMJ effects are reassuring but low-certainty.

## 한줄요약

위키 20편 종합: 투명교정은 경중등도 비발치서 고정장치와 교합결과 동등 + 치주·편의 우위지만, 토크·회전·정출·복잡/발치·중증 골격 증례엔 열등·비예측적; attachment·TADs·IPR·trimline로 적응증 확장; 치근흡수 최소, 저작/TMJ 영향은 안심되나 근거 약.

## Synthesis

According to the 20 clear-aligner papers held in this wiki (`orthodontics/clear-aligner`), the clinical bottom line is an **indication gradient**: clear aligner therapy (CAT) performs at parity with fixed appliances (FA) at the simple end of the malocclusion spectrum and degrades toward the complex/skeletal end, where movement *type* and *appliance design* — not the aligner concept itself — govern success. The nine axes below organize the evidence from efficacy through safety to ancillary effects.

### 1. Efficacy vs fixed appliances (the core equivalence + its boundary)

The quantitative anchor is [[orthodontics/clear-aligner/baneshi-2024-clear-aligners-malocclusion-sr-ma]] — an RCT-only SR+MA (21 RCTs, n=970) finding **no significant CAT-vs-FA difference in occlusal outcome** (ABO-OGS, Little Irregularity Index, PAR) for simple non-extraction malocclusions, with CAT favored on plaque/gingival/bleeding indices and 6-month quality of life. The umbrella review [[orthodontics/clear-aligner/yassir-2022-cat-vs-fat-overview-systematic-reviews]] (18 SRs) states the same direction narratively while drawing the boundary: CAT is **effective for mild-to-moderate malocclusion but inferior for severe cases and specific movements (torque, extrusion, rotation)**, with greater relapse. Treatment duration in mild-to-moderate crowding is comparable ([[orthodontics/clear-aligner/alhamwi-2024-clear-aligner-vs-fixed-treatment-duration-crowding-sr]], GRADE low). The boundary is sharpened by two movement-specific reviews: adult anterior tooth movement ([[orthodontics/clear-aligner/ubuzima-2025-clear-aligner-fixed-anterior-teeth-movement-adults-sr]], 12 studies) found vertical movement inconsistent and **FA superior for rotation and torque control**; complex/extraction cases ([[orthodontics/clear-aligner/jaber-2023-clear-aligners-complex-extraction-vs-fixed-sr]], 6 trials, n=283) reached equivalent ABO-OGS/PAR endpoints but FA achieved better buccolingual inclination and occlusal contacts in less time, with notable predicted-vs-achieved discrepancies for CAT in extraction cases.

### 2. Class II correction — two aligner strategies

**Molar distalization**: achievable with aligners (up to ~2 mm, [[orthodontics/clear-aligner/bhate-2025-cat-maxillary-molar-distalization-class-ii-sr]], 11 Invisalign studies, achieved/predicted 36–78%) but **anterior anchorage loss is highly probable**; predictability and stability improve markedly with skeletal anchorage (TADs) and IPR — a hybrid approach is best, especially with large overjet ([[orthodontics/clear-aligner/inchingolo-2023-clear-aligner-distalization-predictability-sr]], 19 studies). **Mandibular advancement (MA)**: two SR+MAs converge — [[orthodontics/clear-aligner/yu-2023-mandibular-advancement-aligner-vs-functional-class-ii-sr-ma]] (9 controlled trials, n=283) and the larger/newer [[orthodontics/clear-aligner/huang-2026-clear-aligner-mandibular-advancement-vs-functional-class-ii-sr-ma]] (9 studies, n=465) both find MA aligners give skeletal Class II correction **comparable to traditional functional appliances** (Twin Block/Herbst; SNA/SNB/ANB n.s.) while **better controlling lower-incisor proclination** (Huang IMPA MD −0.90°; Yu 1.94° less) — i.e. superior sagittal dental control but a more dental/postural than skeletal-growth effect (Yu: ramus growth Co-Go 1.10 mm less).

### 3. Biomechanics — the success is in the design

Aligner efficacy is **appliance-design-dependent**. Composite attachments mostly increase effectiveness — improving anterior root torque, rotation, mesiodistal movement and posterior anchorage — though evidence is weaker for intrusion and lacking for extrusion/posterior expansion ([[orthodontics/clear-aligner/nucera-2022-composite-attachments-clear-aligners-sr]], 5 trials). Trimline (gingival margin) design drives force delivery and retention: straight/extended margins generate higher forces/moments and greater displacement than scalloped/short designs, with the effect varying by movement type ([[orthodontics/clear-aligner/nakornnoi-2024-aligner-trimline-biomechanics-tooth-movement-sr]], 12 studies). These explain *why* the movement-specific limitations in axis 1 exist and *how* they are partly mitigated.

### 4. Maxillary expansion

In growing patients, CAT can expand the maxilla but **significantly less than conventional expanders** ([[orthodontics/clear-aligner/fonseca-planells-2026-clear-aligner-maxillary-expansion-growing]], 15 studies/7 pooled: intermolar width −1.77 mm, palatal volume −460.6 mm³, arch perimeter −1.75 mm favoring conventional), and the expansion is **mainly dentoalveolar** (tipping of anterior/deciduous teeth) rather than skeletal — a clear limitation for true transverse skeletal deficiency.

### 5. Safety — root resorption

Reassuring: CBCT meta-analysis ([[orthodontics/clear-aligner/butsabul-2024-clear-aligner-root-resorption-cbct-sr-ma]], 9 studies) shows **minimal** external apical root resorption after CAT (overall incisor root length MD −0.56 mm; upper-incisor volume −13.34 mm³), with the **maxillary central incisor** most affected — consistent with the umbrella finding of lower root-resorption risk than FA.

### 6. Periodontal & oral hygiene — real but compliance-dependent

CAT's periodontal advantage is consistently *signalled* but **not firmly proven**. Baneshi's RCT-only MA gives favorable pooled effect sizes (plaque/gingival/bleeding), yet the dedicated periodontal SR+MA ([[orthodontics/clear-aligner/crego-ruiz-2023-periodontal-gingival-recession-aligner-vs-fixed-sr-ma]], 12 studies; only plaque mid-term MD −0.99 and PPD long-term −0.93 mm significant, both I²=99%) and the GRADE SR ([[orthodontics/clear-aligner/alhuwaizi-2026-clear-aligner-fixed-oral-hygiene-periodontal-grade-sr]], 6 RCTs, certainty very-low to low) conclude the **advantage is highly compliance-dependent** and evidence is insufficient for a firm claim. As an adjunct, consumer probiotics reduced aligner biofilm (CFU + EPS) short-term ([[orthodontics/clear-aligner/thakur-2026-probiotics-clear-aligner-biofilm-rct]], crossover RCT n=20), but this is preliminary (label-only verification, no mechanical-cleaning comparator).

### 7. Masticatory muscles & TMJ — broadly neutral, low certainty

Masticatory effects appear **transient and self-limiting**: muscle activity/discomfort change in the first days then normalize, with a noted bite-force reduction ([[orthodontics/clear-aligner/almalki-2024-clear-aligner-masticatory-stomatognathic-sr]], 6 studies), and surface-EMG pooling shows **no significant net change** in masseter/temporalis activity during CAT ([[orthodontics/clear-aligner/lekaviciute-2024-clear-aligner-masticatory-muscle-emg-sr-ma]], 6 studies). TMJ morphology (condylar position, joint spaces) is largely unchanged in adult Class I/II non-extraction cases, with forward condylar repositioning only in Class II div 2 ([[orthodontics/clear-aligner/montasser-2026-tmj-changes-clear-aligner-scoping-review]], 4 retrospective CBCT studies, serious RoB) — exploratory, not confirmatory.

### 8. Accelerated orthodontics — adjuncts do not help

Physical adjunctive interventions (vibration devices, low-level laser therapy) marketed to accelerate movement / improve tracking / reduce pain do **not** produce consistent benefit in CAT — no improvement in Little Irregularity Index, only transient day-1–3 pain reduction, vibration helping tracking only under a 7-day change cadence, GRADE low to very low ([[orthodontics/clear-aligner/sirri-2026-physical-adjunctive-interventions-clear-aligner-rct-sr]], 7 RCTs, n=266). The change cadence matters more than the device.

### Clinical bottom line

Choose aligners confidently for **mild-to-moderate, non-extraction, predominantly tipping/alignment cases** in compliant patients who value esthetics, comfort and easier hygiene. Escalate caution — and plan auxiliaries (attachments, TADs, IPR) or consider fixed appliances — for **torque/rotation/extrusion-heavy, extraction, bodily-movement, severe or transverse-skeletal cases**. Root resorption and TMJ/masticatory effects are not a deterrent on current evidence, the periodontal edge is real but compliance-dependent, and acceleration gadgets are not worth their cost.
