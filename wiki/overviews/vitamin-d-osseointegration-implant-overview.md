## 한국어 핵심요약

> [!summary] 한국어 핵심요약
> - 핵심 명제: 비타민 D(Vitamin D, 25(OH)D)와 골유착(Osseointegration)의 관계는 "기전·동물에서는 강하지만 사람 임상에서는 갈린다" — 결핍을 *위험 축*으로 보고 **중증 결핍만 교정**하는 것이 방어 가능한 임상 입장이고, 충분군에 대한 *루틴 보충*은 입증되지 않았다.
> - 근거 사다리: 우산형 종설 1편(Tallon 2024) ▸ 체계적 고찰 3편(Miron 2025·43편, Werny 2022·18편, Buzatu 2024·7편 human) ▸ RCT 1편(Ayyad 2025, 국소도포) ▸ 전향 코호트 2편(Mohsen 2024, Francis 2024) ▸ 후향 1편(Dulinska 2025).
> - 동물·기전 근거는 일관되게 양성: 결핍은 신생골·골-임플란트 접촉(Bone-to-Implant Contact, BIC)을 낮추고 보충은 골유착을 높임 — 특히 당뇨·골다공증·만성신장질환(CKD) 동반 시(Werny 2022).
> - 사람 근거가 갈리는 지점 = **결핍의 중증도**. 양성 신호는 거의 전부 **중증 결핍(<10~20 ng/mL)** + 동반위험(흡연·치주염·당뇨)에 몰려 있다.
> - 위험 쪽 정량: Mohsen 2024(전향, 143부위) — 조기실패 전체 7%이나 **<10 ng/mL에서 46.2%** vs >30 ng/mL 4.5%·10~30 ng/mL 2.3%; Miron 2025 — 결핍 시 조기실패 **최대 4배**; Dulinska 2025 — 결핍군 임플란트 주위 골소실 유의 증가.
> - 무효 쪽: Francis 2024(전향, 174 임플란트) — **상관 없음**, 실패한 8건이 오히려 비타민 D가 더 높음(42.5 vs 31.9 ng/mL); Buzatu 2024(7편 human·4450 임플란트) — 전반적 유의 연관 없음(평균 소실 8.37%).
> - 충돌 해석: 무효 연구는 대체로 **비타민 D 충분한 집단**이라 결핍 신호가 희석됨 — "결핍이 위험"과 "충분군에서 무차이"는 모순이 아니라 *용량-반응의 양 끝*.
> - 국소도포라는 다른 경로: Ayyad 2025 RCT(즉시식립 24개, 국소 칼시트리올 겔) — 6개월 탐침깊이·출혈지수·통증·ISQ(80 vs 75)·골밀도(+18.1% vs +6.4%) 모두 유의 우수 (소규모·단일센터, 전신 vs 국소는 별개 질문).
> - 근거 한계(Tallon 2024 우산형): 메타분석 부재, 선택적 보고, 기준치·용량·결과지표 불일치 — 그래서 "보충하면 좋아진다"는 인과는 아직 약함.
> - 임상 takeaway: ① 고위험(흡연·당뇨·치주염·재발 실패·중증 골다공증) 환자는 **술전 25(OH)D 선별** ② **중증 결핍(<10~20 ng/mL) 교정 후 식립** ③ 충분군에 루틴 보충은 근거 없음 ④ 비타민 D는 골질·1차 안정성을 대체하지 못함 — [[overviews/bone-quality-implant-risk-modification-overview]]의 위험 축에 *부가* 레버로 본다.

## One-line Summary
Synthesis of 8 papers (1 umbrella review, 3 SRs, 1 RCT, 2 prospective, 1 retrospective): vitamin D's pro-osseointegration effect is consistent in animals/mechanism but split in humans — the positive signal concentrates in severe deficiency (<10–20 ng/mL) plus co-risks, while studies in replete populations find no association; pre-surgical screening and correction of *severe* deficiency is defensible, routine supplementation in replete patients is unproven.

## 한줄요약
8편 종합(우산형 1·SR 3·RCT 1·전향 2·후향 1): 비타민 D의 골유착 촉진 효과는 동물·기전에서는 일관되나 사람에서는 갈린다 — 양성 신호는 중증 결핍(<10~20 ng/mL)+동반위험에 몰리고 충분군에서는 무차이; 고위험군 술전 선별 + *중증 결핍만* 교정이 방어 가능, 충분군 루틴 보충은 미입증.

## Summary

Vitamin D is biologically tied to bone metabolism (calcium homeostasis, osteoblast differentiation, immunomodulation), so a pro-osseointegration role is mechanistically plausible. The wiki holds 8 papers spanning the full evidence ladder, and they do **not** all agree. This page resolves the apparent contradiction.

**The signal is real but conditional.** Across the human studies, the association between low vitamin D and implant failure appears almost exclusively at the **severe-deficiency end** and is amplified by co-morbid risk (smoking, periodontitis, diabetes). Where populations are vitamin-D-replete, no association is detectable. The two "negative" studies and the "positive" studies are therefore best read as opposite ends of a dose–response curve, not as a true conflict.

### Why the studies disagree — reconciliation

- **Severity stratification is everything.** Mohsen 2024 shows a clean dose–response (46.2% → 4.5% → 2.3% as 25(OH)D rises). The null studies (Francis 2024, Buzatu 2024) sampled largely replete cohorts, so the high-risk <10 ng/mL stratum was thin — the curve's steep end was simply under-sampled.
- **Outcome timing.** The risk signal is strongest for *early* implant failure / osseointegration phase (Miron 2025, Mohsen 2024), where bone formation is rate-limiting — consistent with the animal BIC data (Werny 2022).
- **Co-risk amplification.** The worst loss rates (Buzatu 11.1%) require deficiency *plus* smoking/periodontitis — vitamin D is an effect modifier, not an isolated lever.
- **Local ≠ systemic.** Ayyad 2025 (topical calcitriol at the osteotomy) is a different mechanism question than serum status; promising but small/single-centre and not generalisable to systemic supplementation.
- **Evidence quality caps causality.** Tallon 2024 (umbrella) and Werny 2022 flag no meta-analysis, selective reporting, and inconsistent reference levels — so "correct deficiency and outcomes improve" remains association-level for systemic vitamin D in humans.

### Clinical decision

1. **Screen selectively, not universally.** Order serum 25(OH)D pre-operatively in high-risk patients: smokers, diabetics, periodontitis, prior/recurrent early implant failure, severe osteoporosis, malabsorption.
2. **Correct severe deficiency before placement.** The defensible threshold for action is severe deficiency (**<10–20 ng/mL**); this is where the failure curve is steep and supplementation is biologically rational.
3. **Do not routinely supplement replete patients** (>30 ng/mL) for implant outcomes — no human evidence of benefit, and failures have occurred at normal/high levels (Francis 2024).
4. **Vitamin D is additive, not substitutive.** It does not replace primary stability, bone quality management, or co-risk control — layer it onto, not instead of, the [[overviews/bone-quality-implant-risk-modification-overview]] risk axis.
5. **Topical vitamin D** is an emerging adjunct (Ayyad 2025) — interesting but not yet a standard-of-care recommendation.
