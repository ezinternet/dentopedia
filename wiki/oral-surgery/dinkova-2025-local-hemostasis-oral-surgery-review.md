## 한줄요약
서술적 고찰 (51편): 산화셀룰로오스·젤라틴 스폰지 표준 85% 이상 지혈; 트라넥삼산 구강세정 50–60% 출혈 감소; 피브린 실란트 고위험 환자 70–90% 효과.

## Summary
Narrative review of 51 studies (1990–2023) evaluating 국소지혈제 (local hemostatic agents) for oral surgery. Postoperative bleeding occurs in 4–6% of general cases and rises to 9–12% in patients on 항응고제 (anticoagulant) or 항혈소판제 (antiplatelet) therapy. Key findings: traditional agents (oxidized cellulose, gelatin sponges) control bleeding in >85% of standard cases; 자가혈소판농축물 (Autologous Platelet Concentrates, APCs) — PRP and L-PRF — reduce bleeding time 30–50%; 트라넥삼산 (tranexamic acid, TXA) mouthwash reduces bleeding 50–60%; fibrin sealants achieve 70–90% reduction in high-risk patients without increasing thromboembolic risk.

## Key Contributions
- Comprehensive comparative review of available local hemostatic options with quantified efficacy
- Highlights TXA as most effective pharmacological agent for anticoagulated patients
- Identifies gaps: no standardized protocols; limited data on nanomaterials and medically compromised populations

## Methodology
- Narrative review; 51 studies; databases: PubMed-MEDLINE, ScienceDirect, EBSCOhost, Medscape

## Results
| Agent | Efficacy |
|---|---|
| Oxidized cellulose / gelatin sponge | >85% standard cases |
| PRP / L-PRF | 30–50% bleeding time reduction |
| TXA mouthwash | 50–60% bleeding reduction |
| Fibrin sealants | 70–90% in high-risk patients |
| Topical TXA | 80% ↓ hemorrhagic events in anticoagulated patients |
