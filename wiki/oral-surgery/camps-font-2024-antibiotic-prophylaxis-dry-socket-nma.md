## One-line Summary
Network meta-analysis (16 RCTs, 2158 patients, 2428 lower third molars) of antibiotic prophylaxis (amoxicillin ± clavulanate, metronidazole, azithromycin, clindamycin) after lower third molar extraction: prophylaxis statistically reduces dry socket (NNT=25) and surgical site infection (NNT=18), but the high NNT plus adverse-effect/resistance concerns argue against routine use in healthy, low-risk patients.

## Summary
Camps-Font et al. (2024) conducted a systematic review and network meta-analysis of 16 RCTs (2158 patients, 2428 lower third molars) comparing amoxicillin (±clavulanic acid), metronidazole, azithromycin, and clindamycin for prevention of dry socket and SSI after lower third molar extraction. Pooled results confirmed that antibiotic prophylaxis statistically reduces both dry socket (NNT=25) and SSI (NNT=18). However, the high NNT values mean clinicians should weigh individual risk factors (systemic status, infection risk) rather than prescribing routinely. Antibiotic-related adverse effects (GI, allergic, microbiota disruption, resistance) further argue against indiscriminate use.

## Key Contributions
- First network meta-analysis providing direct and indirect comparisons of multiple antibiotic regimens for both dry socket and SSI prevention
- Quantifies NNT: 25 for dry socket, 18 for SSI — framing the cost-benefit of prophylaxis
- Supports antimicrobial stewardship: routine prophylaxis not justified in healthy, low-risk patients
- Confirms amoxicillin (±clavulanic acid) as most studied and commonly used first-line agent

## Methodology
- 16 RCTs; 2158 patients; 2428 lower third molars
- Antibiotics compared: amoxicillin, amoxicillin-clavulanate, metronidazole, azithromycin, clindamycin
- Pairwise meta-analysis (direct comparisons) + network meta-analysis (indirect comparisons)
- Primary outcomes: dry socket (alveolar osteitis), SSI
- Secondary: antibiotic-related adverse events
- Published online: August 21, 2023; print 2024

## Results
| Outcome | NNT | Background Incidence |
|---|---|---|
| Dry socket (alveolar osteitis) | 25 | ~0.5–5% of all extractions |
| Surgical site infection (SSI) | 18 | 1–13% of all third molar extractions |

- Pooled results statistically favor antibiotics for both outcomes
- High NNT argues against routine prophylaxis in healthy patients
- Antibiotic adverse effects: GI (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea), allergic reactions, microbiota disruption (candidiasis, C. difficile risk), rare hematological complications (thrombocytopenia, neutropenia, hemolysis), antimicrobial resistance

**Clinical recommendation**: Evaluate individual patient's systemic status and personal risk of developing postoperative infection before prescribing antibiotic prophylaxis.
