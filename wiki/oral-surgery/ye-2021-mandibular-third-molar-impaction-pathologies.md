## One-line Summary

Retrospective study (262 patients, 432 mandibular third molars, Shanghai): soft-tissue impaction and vertical angulation drove pericoronitis (82.4%), while mesio-angular shallow impaction predicted M2M distal caries, and deep mesio-angular/horizontal impaction predicted M2M distal periodontal pathology — all associations significant at p<0.0001.

## Summary

This retrospective single-center study from Shanghai (January 2019 – January 2020) enrolled 262 patients (432 mandibular third molars, M3Ms) referred to an oral and maxillofacial surgery department. It is the first study to simultaneously analyze all three major M3M-associated pathologies — pericoronitis (치관주위염), M2M distal caries (제2대구치 원심 우식), and M2M distal periodontal pathology (제2대구치 원심 치주병변) — using consistent classification: impaction depth (soft tissue vs. bony), Pell & Gregory position (A/B/C), and Winter's angulation (vertical/mesio-angular/horizontal).

The key clinical finding is that different impaction features predict different pathology types. Soft-tissue and vertically angulated impactions are strongly associated with pericoronitis, the dominant pathology across all age groups. Mesio-angular, shallow (position A) impactions are the primary driver of M2M distal caries. Deep (position B/C) mesio-angular and horizontal impactions are most strongly associated with M2M distal periodontal pathology, including external root resorption (ERR). Age matters: caries and periodontal lesions increase significantly in patients ≥30, while pericoronitis dominates in younger patients.

The worst outcomes — M2M extraction due to uncurable root caries or degree III mobility — clustered in soft-tissue, position A/B, mesio-angular/horizontal impacted teeth with a mean patient age of 44 years, highlighting the risk of long-term retention without surveillance.

The authors conclude that soft-tissue impacted teeth in vertical angulations should be considered for early extraction (pericoronitis risk), while removal of mesially and horizontally angulated or bony-impacted teeth can be delayed but should not be indefinitely deferred (cumulative caries and periodontal risk).

## Key Contributions

1. **Comprehensive three-pathology analysis**: First study to simultaneously assess pericoronitis, M2M distal caries, and M2M distal periodontal pathology in the same cohort using a unified classification approach.

2. **Pathology-specific impaction profiles** (clinically actionable):
   - Pericoronitis: soft-tissue impacted (p=0.0097) + vertical angulation (p<0.0001)
   - M2M distal caries: mesio-angular angulation + position A (shallower, p<0.0001)
   - M2M distal periodontal pathology: mesio-angular/horizontal angulation + deeper positions (p<0.0001)

3. **Age stratification**: Caries and periodontal lesions are significantly more common in patients ≥30 (p<0.0001), supporting continued monitoring of retained M3Ms in middle-aged patients.

4. **Prognosis data**: 10 M2Ms required extraction (5 for degree III mobility, 5 for uncurable root caries), underscoring the permanent cost of delayed extraction in high-risk impaction patterns.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Retrospective chart review
- **Setting**: Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery, Shanghai Xuhui District Dental Disease Prevention and Control Institute
- **Period**: January 2019 – January 2020
- **Sample**: 262 patients (129F/133M), ages 18–63 (mean 31); 432 M3M teeth
- **Classification**: Impaction depth (soft tissue / bony); Pell & Gregory (A/B/C); Winter's angulation (vertical/mesio-angular/horizontal)
- **Pathology diagnosis**: Pericoronitis — clinical symptoms (pain + swelling); M2M distal caries — caries in contact with M3M on OPG ± pulp/periapical involvement; M2M distal periodontal pathology — distal bony absorption and/or ERR confirmed by probing depth and OPG
- **Outcome metrics**: Extraction success; mucosa healing (1 week, 1 month, 3 months); M2M mobility (Miller classification); M2M endodontic complications
- **Statistics**: χ² and Fisher exact test (SAS 8.2); p<0.05 threshold

### Pathology prevalence
- Pericoronitis: **82.41%** of all M3Ms (356/432)
- M2M distal caries: **18.75%** (81/432)
- M2M distal periodontal pathology: **14.81%** (64/432)
- 64 teeth had two or three simultaneous pathologies

### Significant associations (Table 2)

**Pericoronitis**
- Soft tissue vs. bony: 332 vs. 64 positive; p=**0.0097** — soft-tissue more likely
- Winter angulation: vertical 219+ / mesio-angular 72+ / horizontal 65+; p=**<0.0001** — vertical highest rate
- Pell & Gregory: p=0.7366 (not significant)

**M2M distal caries**
- Pell & Gregory position A highest; p=**0.0206** — shallower impaction more likely
- Winter angulation: mesio-angular highest (40+/66−); p=**<0.0001**
- Impaction depth: p=0.9113 (not significant)

**M2M distal periodontal pathology**
- Pell & Gregory: deeper positions (B/C) higher; p=**<0.0001**
- Winter: mesio-angular 29+/77− and horizontal 27+/59−; p=**<0.0001**
- Impaction depth: p=0.0727 (trend only)

### Surgical outcomes
- Extraction success rate: **100%**
- All pericoronitis resolved post-surgery
- M2Ms requiring extraction: 5 (degree III mobility) + 5 (uncurable root caries) = 10 total
- Worst-outcome patients: mean age 44 years, predominantly soft-tissue impacted, position A/B, mesio-angular or horizontal angulation
