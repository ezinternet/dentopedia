## Summary
Proceeding paper (published at 5th Int'l Electronic Conference on Applied Sciences, December 2024) reviewing current clinical and surgical indications and guidelines for third molar removal. Reviews NICE (UK) and AAOMS (USA) guideline frameworks. Addresses clinical and radiographic evaluation criteria including angulation, depth of impaction, and presence of symptoms.

Clinical decision to extract third molars is based on a risk-benefit analysis: extraction reduces future pathology risk (pericoronitis, cysts, adjacent tooth resorption) while carrying risk of complications (IAN injury, dry socket, trismus). Coronectomy is indicated when IAC is in intimate contact with roots (high IAN injury risk on OPG/CBCT).

## Key Contributions
- Summarizes current multi-guideline consensus on third molar management
- Absolute extraction indications: recurrent pericoronitis, abscess/cellulitis, cyst formation, adjacent root resorption, orthodontic necessity
- Relative indications: prophylactic extraction in younger patients (before root completion, age <25) when impaction pattern predicts future problems
- Coronectomy: increasingly accepted when IAC-root contact is high risk; intentional retention of roots to preserve nerve
- General anesthesia/sedation: indicated for deeply impacted, anxious patients, or complex surgical cases
