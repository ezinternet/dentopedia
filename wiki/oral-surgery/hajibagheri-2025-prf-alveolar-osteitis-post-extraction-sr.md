## One-line Summary

Systematic review (BMC Oral Health 2025) of RCTs/controlled trials: platelet-rich fibrin (PRF) placed in extraction sockets significantly reduces alveolar osteitis (dry socket) incidence, postoperative pain (VAS at 24h–7d), facial edema, and trismus versus controls, with accelerated wound healing and no adverse events.

## Summary
This systematic review (BMC Oral Health 2025;25:869) synthesizes evidence on platelet-rich fibrin (PRF) use in tooth extraction sockets for preventing or reducing postoperative complications: alveolar osteitis (dry socket), edema, trismus, and pain. Multiple RCTs are included with outcomes measured at 24h, 48h, 72h, and 7 days. PRF is an autologous second-generation platelet concentrate derived from the patient's own blood by centrifugation; it releases growth factors (PDGF, TGF-β, VEGF) within a fibrin scaffold that supports healing.

## Key Contributions
- PRF significantly reduces incidence of alveolar osteitis compared to untreated sockets
- Postoperative pain (VAS) lower in PRF groups at all measured time points
- Facial swelling measurements significantly reduced with PRF
- Mouth opening restriction (trismus) less severe and resolves faster
- Wound healing and soft tissue closure accelerated
- No adverse events associated with autologous PRF application

## Methodology
- Systematic review following PRISMA guidelines
- Databases: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, Cochrane, and others
- Included: RCTs and controlled trials comparing PRF socket placement vs. control
- Outcomes: dry socket incidence, VAS pain scores, facial edema measurements, interincisal opening distance, wound healing assessment
- Risk of bias: Cochrane RoB tool

## Results
- **Alveolar osteitis**: Statistically significant reduction in dry socket incidence with PRF
- **Pain**: Reduced VAS scores at 24h, 48h, 72h, 7 days post-extraction
- **Edema**: Facial swelling measurably and significantly lower in PRF groups
- **Trismus**: Better mouth opening recovery with PRF
- **Wound healing**: Faster soft tissue closure observed
- Most benefit seen in high-risk extractions (impacted third molars, multiple extractions)
- Heterogeneity across studies in PRF preparation protocols (centrifugation speed and time)
