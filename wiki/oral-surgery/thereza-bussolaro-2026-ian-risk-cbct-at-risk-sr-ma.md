## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 11 CBCT + objective-neurosensory-test cohorts: overall IAN injury risk 9.52%; high-risk cases 14.76%; cortical bone absence 15.65% vs 0.39% with intact cortex — a 40-fold difference guiding surgical decision-making.

## 한줄요약

CBCT + 객관적 신경검사 코호트 11개 SR+MA — 전체 IAN 손상률 9.52%; 고위험군 14.76%; 피질골 소실 15.65% vs 피질골 존재 0.39% (40배 차이).

## Summary

This SR+MA by Thereza-Bussolaro et al. (JOMS 2026) is a companion to their JADA 2026 paper and shifts focus from Odds Ratios to absolute IAN injury risk percentages stratified by CBCT tomographic features. Of 1,039 screened records, only 11 met strict inclusion criteria (preoperative CBCT + objective neurosensory testing). The overall IAN injury risk is 9.52%, rising to 14.76% in high-risk cases. The single most powerful discriminator is mandibular canal (MC) cortical bone status: 15.65% risk without cortex vs 0.39% with intact cortex. MC position risk ranges 1.34–27.47% by subclassification type. GRADE certainty is low to very low, highlighting the urgent need for standardized prospective studies. These numbers directly support patient counseling and individualized surgical planning.

## Key Contributions
- Absolute risk figures (%) by CBCT feature — directly usable in patient counseling ("your risk is ~15% due to absent cortical bone")
- Cortical bone status is the most powerful discriminator: 40× difference in IAN injury risk
- Filters to only objective neurosensory testing — higher validity than studies using subjective patient report
- Only 1.05% of published studies met quality criteria for inclusion — reveals evidence gap in this field
- Motivates a more rigorous CBCT-based stratification system for mandibular third molar extraction

## Methodology

- **Design**: SR+MA; PRISMA; PROSPERO registered; no date/language restriction
- **Databases**: MEDLINE, EMBASE, Web of Science, Scopus, Cochrane, Google Scholar
- **Key inclusion criterion**: Preoperative CBCT + objective neurosensory testing post-operatively
- **Included**: 11 studies (prospective + retrospective cohorts)
- **Bias**: JBI tool
- **Statistics**: Random-effects model; I²/Tau²; GRADE

## Results

| CBCT Feature | IAN Injury Risk |
|---|---|
| Overall (all) | 9.52% (95% CI 7.18–12.53%) |
| High-risk CBCT cases | 14.76% (95% CI 11.37–18.94%) |
| Absent MC cortical bone | 15.65% |
| Intact MC cortical bone | 0.39% |
| MC position (by subtype) | 1.34–27.47% |
| MC shape variation | 6.56–18.36% |

- GRADE certainty: low to very low
- Substantial heterogeneity across studies; results should be interpreted with caution
