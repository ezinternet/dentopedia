## One-line Summary

Chinese multi-center expert consensus (Int J Oral Sci, 2026) covering the full clinical lifecycle of third molar management — classification, extraction indications, anesthesia, surgical techniques (coronectomy, root-removal-first, digital navigation), socket regeneration, complication rescue, and functional utilization via autotransplantation (~95% success) or orthodontic mesialization.

## Summary

Third molars exhibit the highest impaction rate of any permanent tooth, with mandibular impaction found in 70.3% of 13,168 Chinese individuals aged 17–22 (mesioangular predominant at 58.5%). Because developmental timing, eruption patterns, and cultural contexts vary substantially, clinical management decisions have historically been inconsistent. This Chinese multi-center expert consensus (Sun et al., *Int J Oral Sci*, 2026) provides evidence-based recommendations across the full third-molar clinical lifecycle to standardize decision-making.

The document is structured across eight major domains: (1) developmental trajectory and impaction characteristics; (2) clinical and radiographic examination; (3) classification systems; (4) adverse effects on oral health; (5) extraction indications; (6) preoperative preparation, anesthesia, and surgical protocols; (7) socket management; and (8) functional utilization of impacted third molars.

Key epidemiological signals: pericoronitis occurs at 82.41% mandibular incidence among pericoronitis cases, making it the dominant pathology indication; impacted third molars account for 50–70% of fascial space infections; ~19.5% of patients with impacted third molars develop distal second-molar caries; ~5% develop odontogenic cysts or tumors.

The consensus introduces two classification refinements: a Category D modification to Pell & Gregory (teeth below the apical one-third of the second molar or with linear crown-to-second-molar distance exceeding tooth length) and a four-level Archer system for maxillary impacted third molars with buccopalatal sub-classification guiding surgical approach.

Extraction indications are organized into three tiers: (1) definite pathologic indicators (recurrent pericoronitis/space infections, caries causing or adjacent-tooth caries, periodontal damage to second molars, cyst/tumor, moderate/severe root resorption); (2) therapeutic extraction (orthodontic, orthognathic, TMD, pre-radiotherapy/chemotherapy/immunotherapy, pre-pregnancy with deep periodontal involvement); and (3) prophylactic removal of potentially risky but currently asymptomatic teeth (partially erupted mesial/horizontal, soft-tissue-impacted unerupted, complete bony-impacted with distal alveolar bone deficient up to the crown). The third tier emphasizes shared decision-making and observational management for patients over 35 with complete bony impaction and high surgical risk.

Surgical technique highlights include a "Root Removal First" strategy for deeply embedded level C/D mesioangular or horizontal mandibular third molars (distal bone removed, 60° long-axis section, root elevated before crown displaced distally), a submaxillary sinus membrane space approach for level C/D maxillary third molars with sinus protrusion (10×10 mm ultrasonic trapezoidal bone window above second-molar apex), orthodontic traction before extraction when roots contact the mandibular canal, and coronectomy (crown section at or below pulp chamber floor, 3–5 mm below crestal bone) for roots in high-risk proximity to the inferior alveolar nerve. Digital technologies (guided bone window preparation, surgical navigation, robots, endoscopy) are endorsed for high-risk or less-experienced operator scenarios.

Socket management is graded by bleeding severity: mild bleeding (20–30 s) — no material required; moderate (fills socket in 10–15 s) — collagen sponge or absorbable hemostatic gauze; arterial — bone wax or iodoform gauze. For sockets with preoperative distal alveolar bone deficiency, CGF alone or CGF + bone substitute is recommended, with a bone lid technique (harvested cortical-cancellous window reimplanted after extraction) reserved for deep extractions requiring extensive buccal/distal bone removal. Bone substitutes should ideally resorb within 3–6 months. PTFE sutures outperform silk for biofilm reduction.

Severe-complication management is addressed with stepwise protocols for six scenarios: nerve injury (contused/crushed: glucocorticoids + vitamin B₁₂/mecobalamin + low-level laser for 3–6 months; transected: prompt anastomosis); root displacement into the mandibular canal (<3 mm / <1/3 canal diameter with no neurological deficit → observe; ≥3 mm or ≥1/2 canal or neurological deficit → remove with piezoelectric ultrasonic + microforceps under endoscopic visualization); root/tooth into lingual space; tooth into maxillary sinus (CBCT confirmation, anterolateral window creation, suction retrieval); alveolar vascular hemorrhage; and dry socket (debridement + iodoform/minocycline dressing every 3–5 days, CGF when granulation appears, photobiomodulation therapy adjunct).

Functional utilization: impacted third molars without symptoms adjacent to teeth requiring extraction may be preserved via autotransplantation (root ≥3/4 complete, extracorporeal time ≤15 min, digitized 3D guide for recipient socket preparation; Chinese success rate ~95% at 12 months) or orthodontic mesialization (vital pulp preserved, but technically demanding and extended treatment duration).

## Key Contributions

- **Category D modification** of Pell & Gregory for mandibular third molars below the apical one-third of the adjacent second molar — improves surgical difficulty prediction
- **Archer classification** for maxillary impacted third molars with buccal/central/palatal sub-classification guiding approach selection
- **Three-tier extraction indication framework** distinguishing definite pathologic, therapeutic, and prophylactic indications with explicit patient-preference weighting for the prophylactic tier
- **Root Removal First strategy** for deep mesioangular/horizontal mandibular impactions abutting the second molar root
- **Submaxillary sinus membrane space approach** for deep maxillary impactions with sinus protrusion
- **Graded socket management protocol** (bleeding severity → material selection; CGF ± bone substitute ± bone lid based on preoperative bone deficit)
- **Functional utilization criteria** for autotransplantation and orthodontic mesialization with explicit success-rate data (~95%)
- **Severe-complication rescue protocols** for 6 major intraoperative/postoperative scenarios with quantitative decision thresholds (root fragment size, canal penetration depth, neurological deficit presence)

## Methodology

Expert consensus developed by a Chinese multi-center panel, published in *International Journal of Oral Science* (Nature/Springer). The consensus synthesizes clinical evidence and expert opinion without formal GRADE-based evidence grading. Epidemiological figures are drawn from Chinese population data (notably n = 13,168 aged 17–22 for impaction prevalence). No formal SR/MA methodology is stated; the document functions as a practice position paper endorsed by the panel.
