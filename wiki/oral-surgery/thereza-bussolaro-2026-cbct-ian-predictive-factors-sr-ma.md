## Summary

This PROSPERO-registered SR+MA synthesized 18 CBCT-based studies to identify tomographic predictors of inferior alveolar nerve (IAN) injury during mandibular third molar extraction. Of 8 predictors identified, three reached statistical significance with OR >3: absent mandibular canal (MC) cortical bone (OR 9.87), dumbbell-shaped MC (OR 8.25), and lingual MC position (OR 3.82). When all three features coexist, the combined OR is 5.06 (95% CI 2.23–11.39). Evidence certainty was moderate to high per GRADE. The authors recommend oral/maxillofacial radiologists routinely report these features in pre-surgical imaging reports to allow risk-stratified surgical planning.

## Key Contributions
- Quantified 8 CBCT-based predictors of IAN injury as OR values — first SR+MA specifically focused on tomographic features
- Cortical bone absence around the mandibular canal is the single strongest predictor (OR ~10)
- Combined 3-feature risk (absent cortex + dumbbell + lingual) yields >5× odds of IAN injury
- Moderate-high GRADE certainty supports clinical adoption of these criteria
- Advances beyond panoramic-based classification (Rood & Shehab signs) to CT-based quantitative risk assessment

## Methodology

- **Design**: SR+MA; PROSPERO CRD42024582042
- **Included studies**: 18 (retrospective/prospective cohorts, RCTs, nested case-controls)
- **Intervention**: Mandibular third molar extraction with preoperative CBCT
- **Outcome**: IAN injury confirmed by objective neurosensory testing post-operatively
- **Risk of bias**: 16/18 studies — low risk
- **Analysis**: Random-effects OR with 95% CI; GRADE certainty evaluation

## Results

| CBCT Feature | OR | 95% CI | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Absent MC cortical bone | 9.87 | 4.10–23.83 | <0.001 |
| Dumbbell-shaped MC | 8.25 | 3.19–21.35 | <0.001 |
| Lingual MC position | 3.82 | 1.95–7.39 | <0.001 |
| All 3 features present | 5.06 | 2.23–11.39 | <0.001 |

- 8 total tomographic features identified across 18 studies
- GRADE: moderate to high certainty for top 3 predictors
