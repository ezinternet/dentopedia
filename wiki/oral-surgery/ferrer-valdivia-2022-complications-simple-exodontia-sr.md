## 한줄요약
체계적 문헌고찰(포함 연구 3편): 단순 발치 (Simple Exodontia) 후 합병증 중 개구제한 (Trismus), 치조염 (Alveolitis), 잔존치근 (Retained Root)이 가장 흔하며, 술자 경험·수술 시간·조직 손상과 관련.

## Summary
This systematic review (Ferrer-Valdivia et al., 2022) focuses specifically on complications after simple (non-surgical) exodontia — a topic distinct from the extensively studied third molar extraction complications. Despite a broad initial search of 1,446 articles across PubMed, Scopus, and ScienceDirect, only 3 studies met the inclusion criteria, highlighting the paucity of dedicated research in this area. The most prevalent complications were trismus, alveolitis, and retained roots, all linked to surgeon experience, surgery duration, and degree of tissue trauma.

## Key Contributions
- Distinguishes simple exodontia complications from surgical/third molar literature
- Identifies the three most prevalent CPES: trismus, alveolitis, retained roots
- Highlights that modifiable factors (surgeon experience, surgery duration, tissue trauma) drive complication rates
- Reveals a significant research gap — only 3 qualifying studies found despite broad search

## Methodology
- Databases: PubMed (725), Scopus (96), ScienceDirect (631); total 1,446 articles
- After deduplication: 948 → 9 full-text → 3 included
- Methods: Adapted from Cochrane Handbook and PRISMA statement
- Search period: start of coverage to January 31, 2020
- Exclusion of third molar and surgical exodontia literature

## Results
Most common complications after simple exodontia:
- **Most prevalent**: Trismus, alveolitis (dry socket), retained roots
- **Also reported**: Pain, dehiscence, infections, hemorrhage, paresthesia, sinus communication, TMD, tissue emphysema

Complication categories:
- Intraoperative (during surgery)
- Postoperative infectious: wound infection, abscess, necrotizing fasciitis
- Postoperative non-infectious: pain, edema, alveolitis, trismus, paresthesia, sinus communication, TMD

Key risk factors: surgeon inexperience, prolonged surgery duration, excessive tissue trauma
