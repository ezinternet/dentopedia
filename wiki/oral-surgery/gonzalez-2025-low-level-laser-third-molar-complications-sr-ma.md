## 한줄요약
체계적 문헌고찰 및 메타분석(JADA 2025): 저출력 다이오드 레이저(LLLT)는 하악 제3대구치 발치 후 통증, 부종, 개구제한을 대조군 대비 유의하게 감소시키며, 중등도 이상의 효과 크기를 보임.

## Summary
This systematic review and meta-analysis (JADA 2025;156(3):211-224.E11) evaluates the clinical effectiveness of low-level diode laser therapy (LLLT) as an adjunct to reduce pain, edema, and trismus following mandibular third molar surgical extraction. LLLT (photobiomodulation, PBM) uses low-power light (630–1000 nm wavelength) to stimulate cellular metabolism via cytochrome c oxidase, increasing ATP production, reducing inflammatory mediators, and promoting healing — without thermal effects. Diode lasers (typically 800–940 nm) are the most widely used clinical LLLT devices.

## Key Contributions
- LLLT significantly reduces postoperative pain at 24h, 48h, and 72h after third molar extraction
- Facial edema significantly lower in LLLT groups at 48–72h and 7-day follow-up
- Mouth opening (trismus) significantly better preserved and recovered with LLLT
- Effect size moderate to large for all three outcomes
- Diode wavelengths 800–940 nm most effective based on subgroup analysis
- No adverse events from LLLT reported across included studies
- Benefits observed with intraoral, extraoral, or combined application sites

## Methodology
- PRISMA systematic review; random-effects meta-analysis
- Databases: PubMed, Embase, Cochrane, Scopus, Web of Science
- Inclusion: RCTs comparing LLLT vs. placebo/sham laser following mandibular third molar extraction
- Outcomes: pain (VAS), facial swelling (cm measurements), mouth opening (mm)
- Subgroup analyses: laser wavelength, power density, application timing, site of application
- Risk of bias: Cochrane RoB 2.0

## Results
- **Pain**: Statistically significant VAS reduction at all postoperative time points (pooled SMD favoring LLLT)
- **Swelling**: Significantly lower facial measurements at 48–72h and 7 days
- **Trismus**: Significantly greater interincisal opening in LLLT groups
- **Wavelength**: 800–940 nm diode showed best outcomes in subgroup analysis
- **No adverse events**: Safe profile consistently across all RCTs
- High heterogeneity in laser parameters (wavelength, energy density, session number) limits precise protocol recommendation
