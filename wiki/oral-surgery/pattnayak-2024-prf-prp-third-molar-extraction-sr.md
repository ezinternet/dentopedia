## 한줄요약
체계적 문헌고찰(6편): 하악 제3대구치 발치 후 혈소판풍부섬유소 (PRF)와 혈소판풍부혈장 (PRP, Platelet-Rich Plasma) 비교 시, 4/6 연구에서 PRF가 연조직 치유 및 골밀도 개선에 유리하며, 준비 용이성·완전 자가 유래 측면에서 PRF가 우수.

## Summary
Pattnayak et al. (2024) conducted a systematic review directly comparing PRF and PRP after mandibular third molar extraction, covering January 2014–June 2024. Six studies met inclusion criteria (1 split-mouth prospective, 3 split-mouth comparative, 2 unilateral randomized comparative prospective). Four of six studies showed significant improvement in soft tissue healing and bone density at the PRF site compared to PRP; two studies showed no significant difference. Both materials positively influenced healing, but PRF offered practical advantages: no additives required and fully autologous preparation.

## Key Contributions
- Provides head-to-head PRF vs. PRP comparison specifically in mandibular third molar extraction context
- Establishes PRF superiority in soft tissue healing and bone density in majority of qualifying studies (4/6)
- Highlights PRF's practical advantage: simpler preparation (centrifugation without thrombin/calcium additives)
- Applies NHLBI quality assessment — all included studies rated "good"

## Methodology
- Databases: Scopus, PubMed, Google Scholar, Cochrane Central Register of Controlled Trials
- Period: January 2014 – June 2024; PRISMA guidelines
- PICO: mandibular third molar impaction (P) / PRF (I) / PRP (C) / pain, swelling, trismus, soft tissue healing, bone regeneration (O)
- NHLBI quality assessment tool applied
- Included study designs: split-mouth and unilateral randomized comparative prospective studies

## Results
| Outcome | Finding |
|---|---|
| Soft tissue healing + bone density | PRF superior in 4/6 studies |
| Pain, trismus, swelling, bone formation | No significant PRF vs. PRP difference in 2/6 studies |
| Overall | Both PRF and PRP positively influence healing |
| PRF practical advantage | Easier preparation; no additives; fully autologous |

Clinical context: Mandibular third molars have denser cortical bone, leading to higher postoperative complication risk. Successful surgical extraction typically age 24–30; outcomes vary by operator experience and patient factors.
