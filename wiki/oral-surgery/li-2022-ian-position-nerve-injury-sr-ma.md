## Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis comparing IAN injury rates between buccal and lingual positions of the inferior alveolar canal (IAC) relative to lower third molars (LM3). Searched PubMed, Cochrane CENTRAL, Web of Science, Journals@Ovid (2009–2021). 1,063 articles initially searched; full texts of 53 read; 11 studies included. Meta-analysis via Review Manager (Cochrane).

Lingual IAC position (canal on tongue side of roots) is significantly associated with higher IAN injury rate compared to buccal position. Provides quantitative justification for CBCT-based pre-surgical nerve position assessment.

## Key Contributions
- Lingual IAC: highest IAN injury risk — the nerve has less bone buffer between root apex and nerve when canal is lingual
- Buccal IAC position: comparatively lower risk, though not risk-free
- Supports CBCT use when OPG suggests IAC-root proximity — lingual position determination requires cross-sectional imaging
- Meta-analysis provides the strongest current evidence for this specific anatomical risk factor

## Methodology
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane CENTRAL, Web of Science, Journals@Ovid
- **Period**: 2009–2021
- **Included**: 11 studies comparing IAN injury rate by IAC position (buccal vs lingual)
- **Outcome**: IAN injury rate (paresthesia, anesthesia, neurosensory disturbance)
- **Meta-analysis**: Review Manager (Cochrane Collaboration)

## Results

| IAC Position | IAN Injury Risk |
|---|---|
| Lingual (tongue-side) | Significantly higher — highest risk |
| Buccal (cheek-side) | Lower |
| Meta-analysis result | Lingual position: significantly increased IAN injury (statistically significant) |
