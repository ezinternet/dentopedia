## One-line Summary

SR + network meta-analysis (9 studies, 7 RCTs, n=519 OACs) established buccal fat pad as the superior closure method over buccal advancement flap (OR 15; p=0.0002) and palatal rotational flap (OR 15.29; p=0.002); PRF comparisons inconclusive.

## 한줄요약

SR+네트워크 메타분석(9건, 7 RCT, n=519): 구강상악동교통(OAC)·누공(OAF) 폐쇄에서 협측지방패드(BFP)가 협측전진피판(OR 15, p=0.0002) 및 구개회전피판(OR 15.29, p=0.002) 대비 유의하게 우수; PRF 비교는 근거 불충분.

## Summary

This 2024 systematic review and network meta-analysis (PRISMA-compliant) searched PubMed, Scopus, and Google Scholar, screening 268 articles and including 9 studies (7 RCTs, 2 retrospective) with a combined 519 oroantral communications/fistulas. The four surgical modalities compared were buccal advancement flap (BAF, n=227, 44%), buccal fat pad (BFP, n=156, 30%), palatal rotational flap (PRF-palatal, n=115, 22%), and platelet-rich fibrin membrane (PRF-membrane, n=21, 4%). Network analysis used a fixed-effects model with CINeMA confidence assessment.

**Key conclusion**: BFP demonstrated statistically significant superiority over both BAF and palatal flap. BAF vs palatal flap showed no significant difference. PRF comparisons were inconsistent and underpowered.

**Timing principle**: Treatment within 24–48 h achieves 90–95% success. After 48 h, OAF (epithelialization) develops, reducing secondary closure success to ~67% and allowing sinusitis development in 50% within 3 days and 90% within 2 weeks if untreated.

## Key Contributions

1. **Network meta-analysis framework** enabling simultaneous comparison of 4 surgical modalities beyond pairwise RCT data
2. **BFP superiority quantified**: OR 15 vs BAF, OR 15.29 vs palatal flap — both statistically significant
3. **OAC etiology mapped**: Dental extraction accounts for 76.3% of cases; sinus lift procedures account for 2.2%
4. **Size thresholds clarified**: <3 mm = potential spontaneous closure; >3–5 mm with infection = surgery required
5. **PRF as promising alternative**: 100% success in single RCT (Bilginaylar 2019, n=21) with better patient-reported pain (VAS 6.0 vs BAF VAS 1.4) — but inadequate evidence for network positioning

## Methodology

- **Study selection**: PRISMA workflow; excluded secondary surgeries, studies <20 patients, no comparison group
- **Network model**: CINeMA (University of Bern); fixed-effects design-by-treatment interaction; global inconsistency test χ²=1.694, df=4, p=0.792
- **Risk of bias**: OHAT tool; 6/9 studies high risk (lack of allocation concealment, no blinding), 3/9 low risk
- **Heterogeneity**: I²=43% for BFP vs BAF comparison; I²=0% for BFP vs palatal flap; I²=50% for BAF vs palatal flap

## Results

### Pairwise Network Results

| Comparison | OR (95% CI) | p-value | Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| BFP vs Buccal Flap | 15 (3.52–63.83) | 0.0002 | BFP superior |
| BFP vs Palatal Flap | 15.29 (2.63–88.93) | 0.002 | BFP superior |
| Buccal Flap vs Palatal Flap | 0.77 (0.29–2.04) | 0.60 | No difference |

### OAC Etiology (n=519 total)
- Dental extraction: 76.3%
- Cystectomy: 12%
- Failed implants: 6%
- Sinus lift: 2.2%
- Tumor resection: 2.2%
- Endodontic therapy: 0.7%
- Osteonecrosis: 0.3%

### BFP Surgical Notes
- Incision: 2–3 cm, ≥2 cm below Stensen's duct
- Buccinator and zygomaticus major incised; blunt dissection for fat herniation
- Exposed adipose tissue epithelializes within 4–6 weeks
- Main complication risk: restricted mouth opening (trismus)
