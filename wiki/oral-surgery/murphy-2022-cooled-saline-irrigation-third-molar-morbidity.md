## One-line Summary

Evidence-Based Dentistry summary review of a double-blind, split-mouth RCT (48 patients): irrigating with cooled saline (4°C or 10°C) during mandibular third molar surgery significantly reduced post-operative pain, analgesic use, trismus and swelling versus 25°C saline, with the 4°C group performing best.

## Summary

This is an **Evidence-Based Dentistry summary review** (critical commentary) appraising a primary **double-blind, single-centre, split-mouth prospective RCT** of cooled saline irrigation during mandibular third molar surgery. In the primary trial, 48 medically fit adults (mean age 24.6 ± 3.8 y) with bilateral asymptomatic mandibular third molars had each side removed in separate operations ≥21 days apart by the same surgeon. The **control side used 25°C saline**; the **test side was randomized to 10°C (n=24) or 4°C (n=24)**. Anaesthetic, flap design, burr, and sutures were held constant, and patients were blinded to group and operation order. Pain (VAS) and analgesic use were self-recorded daily for 7 days; a second surgeon measured swelling and trismus on days 1, 3, 7.

Cooled irrigation significantly reduced morbidity: test patients had **lower pain and less analgesic use** (p=0.001), with the lowest values in the **4°C group** (p<0.001). **Trismus** decreased more on days 3 and 7 in test groups (p<0.001) than control (p=0.07), and **swelling** was greatest in the control group with the **4°C group lowest at all time points** (p<0.001). Operation durations were similar (p=0.051). The conclusion: intraoperative cooled saline (4°C and 10°C) is more effective than 25°C saline at reducing early pain, trismus, and swelling — a simple, low-cost intraoperative measure, with 4°C giving the greatest benefit.

(Note: this page is a critical summary of one single-centre RCT; the primary trial's generalizability is limited by its young, healthy, asymptomatic cohort and single surgeon.)

## Key Contributions

- Surfaces a low-cost intraoperative lever — **cooling the irrigant** — that meaningfully reduces the three classic early complications (pain, trismus, swelling) of third molar surgery.
- Suggests a dose-response: 4°C outperformed 10°C, which outperformed 25°C.

## Methodology

- Type: EBD summary review of a double-blind, single-centre, split-mouth RCT.
- Primary trial: 48 patients, bilateral mandibular third molars, two operations ≥21 days apart, same surgeon.
- Control 25°C vs test 10°C (n=24) or 4°C (n=24); constant anaesthetic/flap/burr/sutures; patient-blinded.
- Outcomes: 7-day VAS pain + analgesic use; swelling and trismus on days 1/3/7.

## Results

- Pain/analgesic use: lower in test vs control (p=0.001); lowest at 4°C (p<0.001).
- Trismus: greater decrease days 3 and 7 in test groups (p<0.001).
- Swelling: greatest in control (p<0.001); lowest at all time points in 4°C group (p<0.001).
- Operation duration: similar between groups (p=0.051).
