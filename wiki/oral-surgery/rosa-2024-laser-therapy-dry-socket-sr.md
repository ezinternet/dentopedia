Systematic review and meta-analysis (3 of 50 identified studies) evaluating laser therapy (primarily low-level laser therapy, LLLT) for treating established dry socket (alveolar osteitis); laser was effective versus Alvogyl though the correlation was not highly significant, offering single-session, minimally invasive treatment with less pain and faster healing, on a very limited and non-standardized evidence base.

## Summary
Rosa et al. (2024) conducted a systematic review and meta-analysis evaluating laser therapy (primarily LLLT) for the treatment of dry socket (alveolar osteitis, AO) post-tooth extraction. Searches across PubMed, Embase, Scopus, Google Scholar, and Web of Science (1998–January 2024) identified 50 studies; only 3 met inclusion criteria. Meta-analysis showed laser treatment is effective compared to Alvogyl (eugenol-based dressing), though the correlation was not highly significant. LLLT offers advantages of single-session treatment, reduced pain, faster healing, and fewer side effects vs. antiseptic dressings. The review also notes acemannan hydrogel (SaliCept) as another emerging treatment alternative.

## Key Contributions
- Evaluates LLLT specifically as a *treatment* for established dry socket (alveolar osteitis), not prevention
- Meta-analysis comparison against Alvogyl (conventional standard dressing)
- Characterizes LLLT mechanism: anti-inflammatory, wound healing promotion, antimicrobial effects
- Identifies SaliCept patch (acemannan/Aloe vera) as an additional non-laser alternative

## Methodology
- Databases: PubMed, Embase, Scopus, Google Scholar, Web of Science
- Search: "laser" AND "dry socket"; period 1998–January 31, 2024
- PECO framework: patients with AO post-extraction; laser therapy; various other therapies; AO treatment efficacy
- Inclusion: English clinical trials and RCTs with laser treatment
- 50 identified → 3 included in systematic review and meta-analysis

## Results
- **Meta-analysis**: Laser effective vs. Alvogyl for AO treatment; correlation not highly significant
- **60-patient RCT cited**: Laser group had significantly less pain, faster healing, fewer side effects vs. antiseptic dressing group
- **LLLT properties**: Anti-inflammatory, wound healing, antimicrobial effects on oral mucosa; single-session; minimally invasive
- **Conventional treatment**: Antiseptic dressings (chlorhexidine, eugenol-containing); some disagreement on ideal protocol
- **Alternative**: SaliCept patch (freeze-dried acemannan hydrogel from Aloe vera) — known antimicrobial properties

Limitation: Only 3 qualifying studies; very limited evidence base; laser parameters not standardized across studies.
