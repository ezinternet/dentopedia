## Summary
This Cochrane systematic review (Bailey et al., 2020, 3rd edition) comprehensively evaluates surgical technique variables for mandibular wisdom tooth removal, drawing exclusively on randomized controlled trials. Technique comparisons include flap design, lingual nerve protection, bone removal method (bur vs. chisel), and tooth sectioning approaches. Primary outcomes include alveolar osteitis (dry socket) and wound infection at 7 days, permanent altered sensation (nerve injury) at >6 months, pain at 24 hours, and swelling and trismus at 1 week. The review provides forest plots and summary of findings tables across all comparison groups.

## Key Contributions
- Gold-standard Cochrane methodology for comparing mandibular wisdom tooth surgical techniques
- Covers full range of intraoperative technique decisions: flap design, lingual nerve protection, bone removal, tooth sectioning
- Provides direct comparative data for alveolar osteitis, infection, nerve injury, pain, swelling, and trismus
- Highlights low certainty of evidence for most technique comparisons due to trial quality

## Methodology
- Cochrane systematic review; RCTs only
- Structured comparisons:
  - Comparison 1: Surgical flap type A vs. B (alveolar osteitis, wound infection, nerve injury, dehiscence, pain, swelling, trismus)
  - Comparison 2: Lingual nerve protection vs. no protection (permanent altered tongue sensation)
  - Additional technique comparisons for bone removal and sectioning
- Outcome timepoints: 7 days (infection/AO), 24 hours (pain), 1 week (swelling/trismus), >6 months (permanent nerve injury)
- Risk of bias assessed for all included trials

## Results
Key outcome measures across technique comparisons:
- **Alveolar osteitis** (7 days): Assessed across flap type comparisons
- **Wound infection** (7 days): Assessed across flap type comparisons
- **Permanent altered tongue sensation** (>6 months): Lingual nerve protection comparison
- **Pain** (24 hours, VAS 0–10): Flap type comparisons
- **Swelling** (1 week): Flap type comparisons
- **Trismus / Maximum mouth opening** (1 week): Multiple metric outcomes (SMD and MD)
- **Wound dehiscence** (up to 30 days): Flap type comparisons

Evidence certainty generally low; technique differences for most outcomes not definitively established. Lingual nerve protection data available but interpretation limited by trial heterogeneity.
