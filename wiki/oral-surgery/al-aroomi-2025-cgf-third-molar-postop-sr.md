## One-line Summary

Systematic review of 11 RCTs (PROSPERO CRD420251031885): CGF after mandibular third molar extraction consistently reduces pain (75% of studies) and analgesic consumption (100%), improves radiographic bone fill (80%), and periodontal parameters distal to the second molar (100%); effects on swelling and alveolar osteitis are uncertain.

## Summary

This PROSPERO-registered systematic review (Al-Aroomi et al., JOMS 2025) evaluated CGF — a distinct autologous platelet concentrate obtained via the Medifuge centrifuge — after mandibular third molar extraction. Unlike L-PRF and A-PRF previously reviewed, CGF produces a denser fibrin matrix. From 380 studies screened, 11 RCTs were included. Meta-analysis was not possible due to high heterogeneity in CGF preparation and outcome measurement. Results show consistent benefit for pain reduction and analgesic consumption, moderate evidence for bone fill improvement, and promising but uncertain effects on soft tissue and periodontal outcomes. The impact on swelling, trismus, and alveolar osteitis remains inconclusive.

## Key Contributions
- First SR exclusively evaluating CGF (not PRP/PRF/L-PRF) after third molar extraction
- Pain improvement in 75% of studies; analgesic consumption reduced in 100% of studies evaluating it
- Radiographic bone fill improved in 4/5 studies (80%) — supports socket regeneration potential
- Periodontal probing depth and attachment distal to M2 improved in all 4 studies — prevents second molar periodontal complications
- Soft tissue healing improved in both studies assessing it
- Heterogeneous evidence prevents definitive meta-analytic conclusions

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review; PRISMA; PROSPERO CRD420251031885
- **Search**: 11 databases, through April 2025
- **Population**: Healthy adults with mandibular third molar extraction
- **Intervention**: CGF application in extraction socket
- **Comparator**: No CGF (blood clot only) or placebo
- **Included**: 11 RCTs
- **Excluded**: Non-RCT, non-adult, non-third molar
- **Risk of bias**: 5 unclear / 6 low risk (modified Cochrane tool)
- **No meta-analysis**: High heterogeneity in CGF protocols and outcomes
