## Summary
This systematic review (Int J Oral Maxillofac Surg 2017;46(1):46-53) analyzes 124 published cases of late mandibular fracture occurring >24 hours after mandibular third molar extraction. Late mandibular fracture is a rare but serious complication occurring almost exclusively at the mandibular angle. Most cases are triggered by normal masticatory function rather than significant trauma, reflecting underlying structural weakness created by deep impaction removal. Management is predominantly surgical (ORIF) with generally favorable outcomes.

## Key Contributions
- Late mandibular fracture most commonly occurs 1–3 weeks postoperatively (peak risk window)
- Mandibular angle is the almost exclusive fracture location
- Deep impaction (horizontal/mesioangular, Pell-Gregory Class III/C) is the strongest risk factor
- Male predominance due to greater bite force and more frequent deep impactions
- Aggressive bone removal, thin lingual plate, large follicular cysts, and osteoporosis increase risk
- ORIF provides reliable fracture management with good outcomes

## Methodology
- Systematic review of case reports and case series
- Databases: PubMed, Medline, Cochrane, and others
- 124 cases compiled from literature
- Data extracted: demographics, impaction classification, time to fracture, precipitating event, treatment, outcome
- Qualitative analysis (no meta-analysis due to case-level data)

## Results
- **Timing**: Peak at 1–3 weeks postoperatively; nearly all within 4 weeks
- **Location**: Mandibular angle (virtually all cases)
- **Gender**: Male predominance
- **Impaction type**: Horizontal and mesioangular, Class III/C most at risk
- **Precipitating events**: Normal chewing or minimal trauma in most cases; fracture occurs during routine daily activity
- **Risk factors**: Deep impaction, large follicular cyst, thin lingual plate, aggressive ostectomy, osteoporosis/osteopenia, prior radiation
- **Treatment**: ORIF (plates and screws) most common; outcomes generally favorable
- **Prevention**: Conservative bone removal, avoidance of aggressive levering on thin lingual plate, preoperative CBCT in high-risk cases
