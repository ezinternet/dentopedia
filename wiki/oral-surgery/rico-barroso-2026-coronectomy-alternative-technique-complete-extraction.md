## One-line Summary

Systematic review (16 studies, 2551 patients; 2280 coronectomies vs 841 extractions): IAN injury 0.56% with coronectomy vs 6.53% with extraction; 6.22% of coronectomies converted to extraction; root migration the dominant coronectomy-specific complication.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰 (16편·2551명, 치관절제 2280건 vs 발치 841건): 하치조신경 손상 치관절제 0.56% vs 발치 6.53%, 치관절제 6.22% 완전발치 전환, 치관절제 특유 합병증은 치근 이동.

## Summary

This 2026 systematic review refreshes the coronectomy-versus-complete-extraction comparison for high-risk mandibular third molars (radiographic signs of IAN proximity). Across 16 primary studies and 2551 patients, coronectomy reduced IAN injury roughly tenfold (0.56% vs 6.53%) and permanent IAN injury nearly tenfold (0.13% vs 1.18%), while postoperative pain was comparable between techniques. The trade-off is coronectomy-specific: a 6.22% intra-operative failure (conversion to extraction, mostly from root mobilization) and root migration concentrated in the first 6 months. Methodologically it is a PRISMA systematic review with JBI appraisal, not a narrative review, though no meta-analytic pooling was performed.

## Key Contributions

- Up-to-date group-stratified nerve-injury figures for clinical counseling.
- Quantifies coronectomy intra-op failure/conversion (6.22%).
- Shows pain equivalence between coronectomy and extraction.

## Methodology

- PRISMA; PubMed + Cochrane (Oct 2024); 2005–2024, ≥10 coronectomy cases, ≥6-month follow-up.
- 211 → 16 included (10 prospective cohorts, 3 RCTs, 2 case-control, 1 retrospective cohort).
- JBI prevalence-study appraisal; most studies very low risk of bias.

## Results

- IAN injury: coronectomy **0.56%** vs extraction **6.53%**; permanent **0.13%** vs **1.18%**.
- Lingual nerve injury: 0.26% vs 0.12% (transient).
- Postoperative pain: 14.21% vs 15.69%; mean VAS 1.31 vs 1.18; duration 2.61 vs 3.40 days.
- Coronectomy failure/conversion: **6.22%** (144/2280).
- Root migration: leading coronectomy complication, mainly first 6 months.
