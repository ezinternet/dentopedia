## One-line Summary

Prospective cohort (n=132 oral cavity SCC) showing normal saline irrigation reduces wound contamination from exfoliated epithelial cells from 55% to 7.6% (p<0.001) — cytological evidence supporting the standard saline wound wash protocol.

## 한줄요약

구강 편평세포암 (Squamous Cell Carcinoma, SCC) 절제 수술 132명 전향적 코호트 — 생리식염수 (Normal Saline) 세정이 박리 상피세포 오염률을 55%에서 7.6%로 유의하게 감소 (p<0.001), 기계적 세정만으로 상당한 오염 제거 효과 입증.

## Summary

This prospective study from a head and neck oncology unit in India provides cytological evidence for the effectiveness of routine normal saline wound irrigation during oral cavity squamous cell carcinoma surgery.

After tumour resection and neck dissection, 55% of wounds showed exfoliated epithelial or abnormal cells in the wash effluent (cell block cytology). Following saline irrigation (500–1500 ml), smear cytology from the same wound sites showed only 7.6% contamination — a highly significant reduction (p<0.001). Normal saline acts through purely mechanical cleansing (isotonic solution, no cytotoxic effect).

Only perineural invasion was significantly associated with lavage positivity (p=0.037). T-stage, N-stage, tumour site, grade, LVI, margin distance — all were not significant predictors.

## Key Contributions

1. **Quantifies wound contamination**: 55% of oral cancer surgical wounds harbour exfoliated epithelial/abnormal cells after resection, raising concern for implantation metastasis risk.
2. **Establishes saline efficacy**: Isotonic normal saline reduces contamination by ~47 percentage points through mechanical action alone — comparable results to published head and neck cancer series.
3. **PNI as risk marker**: Perineural invasion is the only clinico-pathological feature predicting higher lavage positivity.
4. **Supports current surgical practice** of wound wash + glove/instrument change after tumour extirpation.

## Methodology

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Design | Prospective consecutive cohort |
| n | 132 enrolled; 104 analysed (28 excluded for incomplete data) |
| Population | Biopsy-proven treatment-naïve oral cavity SCC, Mahavir Cancer Sansthan, Patna |
| Procedure | Tumour resection + neck dissection → 500–1500 ml NS irrigation |
| Cytology 1 | Cell block (wash effluent): H&E stain, centrifugation + plasma/Thrombin clot |
| Cytology 2 | Smears (tumour bed + neck incision post-wash): Giemsa stain |
| Classification | Positive = epithelial/abnormal cells; Suspicious = cellular debris; Negative = acellular/blood |
| Statistics | Chi-square, Mann-Whitney; Jamovi software |
| Ethics | RMRI/EC/53/2022 |

**Limitation**: No immunohistochemical staining; cell block cytology accuracy ~85–90%.

## Results

**Contamination before vs after saline wash:**

| Measure | Pre-wash (cell block) | Post-wash (smear) |
|---|---|---|
| Epithelial/abnormal cells | 24% (25/104) | 0% |
| Cellular debris | 21% (22/104) | 7.6% (8/104) |
| **Combined positivity** | **55% (57/104)** | **7.6% (8/104)** |
| Reduction | — | 47.4 pp; chi² = 29.8, p<0.001 |

**Clinico-pathological predictors of lavage positivity:**
- Perineural invasion: p = 0.037 (significant)
- Site, morphology, T-stage, N-stage, grade, LVI, WPOI, margin, DOI: all NS

**Comparison with published data**: Other head/neck cancer series reported 7.5–26% malignant cells and 13–19% suspicious in wound washings — present results consistent (chi² = 3.35, p = 0.764).
