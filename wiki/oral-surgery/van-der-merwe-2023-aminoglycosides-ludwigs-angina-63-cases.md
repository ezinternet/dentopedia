## Summary
This retrospective study (J Stomatol Oral Maxillofac Surg 2023) analyzed 63 consecutive Ludwig's angina cases at the University of Pretoria / Steve Biko Academic Hospital, South Africa. The study challenges the empirical use of aminoglycosides in Ludwig's angina treatment protocols. Culture data showed predominantly Gram-positive streptococci — organisms intrinsically resistant to aminoglycosides — as the primary infecting organisms. The study has particular relevance to antibiotic stewardship in a population with high prevalence of diabetes mellitus and HIV.

## Key Contributions
- Culture data from 63 Ludwig's angina cases reveals streptococci as dominant organisms (~aminoglycoside-resistant)
- Aminoglycoside empirical use not supported by microbiological evidence in this population
- β-lactam + metronidazole or ampicillin-sulbactam recommended as empirical regimen
- High comorbidity burden (DM, HIV) documented in South African context
- Odontogenic origin confirmed as primary source in most cases
- Data support evidence-based antibiotic stewardship guidelines for deep neck space infections

## Methodology
- Retrospective chart and culture review
- Setting: Steve Biko Academic Hospital, University of Pretoria
- n = 63 consecutive Ludwig's angina cases
- Data: demographics, comorbidities, infection source, pus/blood cultures, antimicrobial sensitivities, antibiotic regimen, surgical management, outcomes
- Statistical analysis: frequency distributions, correlation of organism type with antibiotic appropriateness

## Results
- **Source**: Odontogenic (mandibular molars) predominant
- **Organisms**: Gram-positive streptococci most common — intrinsically resistant to aminoglycosides; mixed flora with anaerobes frequent; Gram-negative bacilli in minority (more common in DM/HIV patients)
- **Aminoglycoside gap**: Most isolated organisms are not covered by aminoglycosides as monotherapy
- **Comorbidities**: Diabetes mellitus and HIV highly prevalent
- **Treatment**: Surgical I&D + IV antibiotics; airway management critical; tracheostomy in cases with severe airway compromise
- **Conclusion**: Empirical aminoglycoside regimens common in practice but not justified by culture data; β-lactam-based regimens are microbiologically more appropriate
