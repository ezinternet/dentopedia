SR+MA of 9 observational studies (n=2176) found a ~4-fold increased odds of halitosis in periodontitis patients using both organoleptic (OR=4.05) and VSC-based (OR=4.52) assessment methods.

## Summary

This 2024 systematic review and meta-analysis in Clinical Oral Investigations examined the association between halitosis and periodontitis across 9 observational studies involving 585 cases and 1591 controls. The meta-analysis used Stata 17 to calculate pooled odds ratios with 95% confidence intervals, with subgroup analyses stratified by halitosis assessment method (organoleptic test vs volatile sulfur compound measurement). Both methods revealed a significant positive association with OR values approximately 4-fold, supporting a clinically meaningful link between periodontal disease and oral malodor. However, high statistical heterogeneity across studies means conclusions must be interpreted cautiously.

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies the halitosis–periodontitis association with pooled OR for two distinct measurement methods
- Demonstrates similar effect sizes for organoleptic (OR=4.05) and VSC-based (OR=4.52) assessments — suggesting the association holds regardless of measurement approach
- Extends and updates the earlier 2017 SR+MA by Silva et al. with more recent studies and subgroup methodology
- Highlights critical gap: high heterogeneity signals need for standardized halitosis measurement and periodontal classification in future studies

## Methodology

- **Study design**: Systematic review + meta-analysis of observational studies
- **Search**: PubMed, Web of Science, Embase, Scopus, Cochrane Library through August 18, 2023
- **Included studies**: 9 observational studies
- **Population**: 585 halitosis cases, 1591 controls (total n=2176)
- **Effect measure**: Odds ratios (OR) with 95% CI
- **Analysis software**: Stata 17
- **Subgroup analyses**: By halitosis measurement method — organoleptic test (OLT) vs volatile sulfur compound (VSC) instrument reading
- **Heterogeneity**: I² reported; significant heterogeneity noted across studies

## Results

| Assessment Method | OR | 95% CI | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Organoleptic test | 4.05 | 1.76–9.30 | < 0.01 |
| VSC measurement | 4.52 | 1.89–10.83 | < 0.01 |

- Both subgroups showed statistically significant positive associations
- Effect magnitude (~4x) is clinically meaningful — periodontitis patients have approximately 4-fold increased odds of halitosis
- Wide confidence intervals reflect heterogeneity and study variability
- Overall conclusion: positive association between halitosis and periodontitis confirmed
