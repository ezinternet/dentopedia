## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 5 population-based studies: periodontitis is associated with 3.16× higher odds of halitosis (OR 3.16; 95% CI 1.12–8.95); measurement criteria for both conditions explain the majority of between-study heterogeneity.

## Summary

This 2017 systematic review and meta-regression analysis by Silva et al., published in the Journal of Clinical Periodontology, is the first pooled quantitative synthesis of the association between periodontitis and halitosis in population-based studies. Searching PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, and SciELO, 1,107 articles were identified and 5 cross-sectional population-based observational studies were included. Meta-analysis yielded a pooled OR of 3.16 (95% CI: 1.12–8.95), indicating that individuals diagnosed with periodontitis have approximately 3× the odds of having halitosis compared to those without periodontitis. Meta-regression revealed that the diagnostic criteria used for halitosis assessment (organoleptic vs. instrumental/VSC measurement) explained approximately 45% of between-study heterogeneity, while the periodontitis case definition criteria explained approximately 24%. The authors conclude that a positive association exists but note that the evidence base is entirely cross-sectional, precluding causal inference.

## Key Contributions

1. **First pooled OR estimate**: Quantified the periodontitis–halitosis association at OR 3.16 (95% CI: 1.12–8.95) across 5 population-based studies.
2. **Meta-regression on heterogeneity**: Demonstrated that diagnostic methodology — not merely clinical variation — is the dominant source of between-study variance, with halitosis criteria explaining ~45% and periodontitis criteria ~24%.
3. **Methodological call to action**: Identified that lack of standardized diagnostic criteria for both halitosis and periodontitis is the principal obstacle to reliable pooled estimates.
4. **Cross-sectional limitation framing**: Explicitly delineated the evidence as association-only; prospective designs needed to establish temporal precedence and biological plausibility.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review + meta-analysis + meta-regression
- **Databases**: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, SciELO
- **Inclusion criteria**: Population-based observational studies testing the periodontitis–halitosis association
- **Studies included**: 5 (all cross-sectional)
- **Total identified**: 1,107 articles
- **Statistical methods**:
  - Random-effects meta-analysis (DerSimonian–Laird)
  - Meta-regression to identify covariates explaining heterogeneity
  - Subgroup analyses by halitosis measurement method and periodontitis diagnostic criteria
- **Reporting standard**: PRISMA

## Results

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Studies included | 5 |
| Pooled OR (periodontitis → halitosis) | 3.16 |
| 95% CI | 1.12–8.95 |
| Heterogeneity explained by halitosis criteria | ~45% |
| Heterogeneity explained by periodontitis criteria | ~24% |

- Positive and statistically significant association between periodontitis and halitosis confirmed in pooled analysis.
- Substantial heterogeneity present, predominantly attributable to differences in how both conditions are diagnosed across studies.
- Subgroup analyses showed that organoleptic vs. instrumental halitosis measurement and varying periodontitis case definitions (CDC/AAP criteria vs. pocket depth cutoffs) yielded different effect estimates.
- All included studies are cross-sectional; no longitudinal data available at time of publication.
