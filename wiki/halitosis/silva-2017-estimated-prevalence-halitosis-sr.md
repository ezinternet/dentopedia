## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 13 population-based studies estimates global halitosis prevalence at 31.8% (95% CI 24.6–39.0%), with year of publication and country socioeconomic status as significant heterogeneity drivers.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

This systematic review and meta-regression analysis (Silva et al., 2017) is the first to quantify global halitosis prevalence from population-based data. Searching four databases without restrictions, 584 articles were screened and 13 population-based observational studies met inclusion criteria. The pooled prevalence of halitosis was **31.8%** (95% CI 24.6–39.0%), indicating approximately one in three adolescents and adults is affected. High heterogeneity was noted across studies. Meta-regression identified **year of publication** and **country socioeconomic status** as significant predictors of prevalence variation. The authors conclude there is a worldwide rising trend in halitosis prevalence and call on dental professionals to take a more active role in prevention and treatment.

## Key Contributions

1. **First SR+MA** estimating global population-level halitosis prevalence.
2. Pooled estimate of **31.8%** — a clinically important benchmark for disease burden awareness.
3. **Meta-regression** reveals that more recent studies and studies from different socioeconomic contexts produce different prevalence values — methodological evolution and population differences both contribute.
4. Rising trend signal: prevalence increasing over time globally.
5. Underscores lack of standardized measurement protocols across studies (organoleptic, Halimeter, GC, self-report) as key limitation and research gap.

## Methodology

- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis + meta-regression
- **Databases**: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, SciELO (no restrictions)
- **Initial results**: 584 articles
- **Included**: 13 population-based observational studies (adolescents + adults)
- **Meta-analysis method**: Random-effects (DerSimonian & Laird)
- **Heterogeneity**: High; meta-regression used to explore sources
- **Meta-regression covariates**: Year of publication, country socioeconomic status
- **Additional analyses**: Sensitivity analyses, publication bias assessment (Egger's test)
- **PRISMA**: Followed

## Results

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Studies included | 13 |
| Pooled prevalence | **31.8%** (95% CI 24.6–39.0%) |
| Heterogeneity | High |
| Key meta-regression predictor 1 | Year of publication (significant) |
| Key meta-regression predictor 2 | Country socioeconomic status (significant) |
| Trend | Rising globally over time |

- Prevalence varied considerably across studies — different halitosis definitions (genuine vs. self-reported), measurement tools (organoleptic vs. VSC monitor vs. self-report), and population characteristics all contributed.
- The 31.8% pooled estimate represents both genuine (clinically measured) and self-reported halitosis combined across included studies.
- Socioeconomic context influences prevalence likely through diet, oral hygiene access, and healthcare-seeking behavior.
