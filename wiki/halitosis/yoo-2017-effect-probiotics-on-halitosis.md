## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 3 RCTs (Yoo 2017): probiotics (mainly Lactobacillus strains, ~2-week regimens) significantly reduce organoleptic halitosis scores (SMD −1.93, p<0.0001) but fail to significantly reduce volatile sulfur compound concentrations (SMD −0.02, p=0.98), leaving the evidence base quantitatively insufficient for definitive clinical recommendations.

## Summary

This 2017 systematic review and meta-analysis is one of the earliest quantitative syntheses of randomized controlled evidence for probiotic interventions in halitosis management. Only 3 RCTs (from 153 screened articles) met inclusion criteria — requiring any probiotic type with at least 2 weeks of treatment and a quantitative halitosis outcome (OLT or VSC).

The meta-analysis found a statistically significant and moderate reduction in organoleptic test (OLT) scores in probiotic vs placebo groups (SMD = −1.93, 95% CI −2.85 to −1.02, p<0.0001). However, no significant difference was found for volatile sulfur compound (VSC) concentrations (SMD = −0.02, 95% CI −2.12 to 2.07, p=0.98). This discordance between the two outcome measures suggests that probiotics may improve the panelist-perceived breath quality without necessarily reducing the key chemical mediators of halitosis.

The authors concluded that current evidence supports recommending probiotics (particularly Lactobacillus strains) for halitosis management, but the evidence base remains quantitatively and qualitatively insufficient for specific administration guidelines.

## Key Contributions

1. First SR+MA pooling RCT evidence for probiotics in halitosis with dual outcomes (OLT + VSC).
2. Quantified a moderate, significant OLT-score benefit (SMD −1.93) while revealing no significant VSC benefit (SMD −0.02) — pointing to a potential disconnect between sensory and chemical halitosis measurement.
3. Identified critical evidence gaps: only 3 RCTs eligible; average treatment duration ~2 weeks; no long-term data.
4. Provided actionable direction for future RCT design: longer follow-up, standardized probiotic protocols, and pretreatment considerations.

## Methodology

- **Study type**: SR+MA of RCTs
- **Databases searched**: PubMed, EMBASE, Web of Science, Cochrane CENTRAL (1946 – January 17, 2017)
- **Inclusion**: RCT, any probiotic, ≥2 weeks, quantitative halitosis outcome
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane Collaboration tool
- **Statistical approach**: SMD with 95% CI; random-effects model; I² for heterogeneity
- **Included RCTs** (n=3):
  - Penala et al. (2016): probiotic adjunct to scaling/root planing in chronic periodontitis + halitosis
  - Jamali et al. (2016): CHX pretreatment + Streptococcus salivarius K12 in children
  - Suzuki et al. (2014): Lactobacillus salivarius WB21 tablets (double-blind crossover)
- **Primary outcomes**: OLT (organoleptic test) score; VSC (volatile sulfur compound) concentration

## Results

| Outcome | SMD | 95% CI | P-value | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| OLT score | −1.93 | −2.85 to −1.02 | < 0.0001 | Significant moderate reduction |
| VSC concentration | −0.02 | −2.12 to 2.07 | 0.98 | No significant difference |

- Average treatment duration: ~2 weeks
- Primary probiotic strains: Lactobacillus salivarius WB21, Streptococcus salivarius K12
- Conclusion: Transient (~2-week) probiotic dosing has a moderate effect on panelist-perceived halitosis (OLT) but cannot be confirmed to reduce VSC levels
- Evidence graded as insufficient for specific administration strategy recommendations
