## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 4 RCTs (n=283): probiotics (Streptococcus salivarius K12/M18, Weissella cibaria) did not significantly reduce oral halitosis overall (p=0.53), but individual trials suggest possible benefit, warranting further research.

## Summary

This systematic review and meta-analysis evaluated the efficacy of probiotics for genuine oral halitosis. Searching PubMed, EMBASE, and Web of Science (2006–July 2021), the authors identified 4 eligible RCTs from 14 candidates (total n=283). Probiotic strains studied were Streptococcus salivarius K12 (SS K12), Streptococcus salivarius M18 (SS M18), and Weissella cibaria (WC), with follow-up ranging from 4 to 12 weeks. A fixed-effects meta-analysis found no statistically significant reduction in halitosis (p=0.53). However, individual trials showed varying results: SS M18 reduced halitosis in orthodontic patients, SS K12 after chlorhexidine disinfection showed benefit over 12 weeks, while another SS K12 trial found no effect. The review concludes that despite promising preliminary findings, the limited number and heterogeneity of available trials preclude definitive conclusions.

## Key Contributions

- First SR+MA dedicated to evaluating probiotics specifically for genuine oral halitosis
- Identified Streptococcus salivarius (K12, M18) as the primary studied oral probiotic strains for halitosis
- Established Weissella cibaria as an emerging candidate for anti-halitosis therapy
- Demonstrated that current evidence is insufficient to recommend probiotics as standard halitosis treatment
- Highlighted critical gaps: lack of dose standardization, short follow-up durations, and heterogeneous outcome measures

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review + fixed-effects meta-analysis (PRISMA; INPLASY20211900009)
- **Databases**: PubMed, EMBASE, Web of Science; 15-year window to July 2021
- **Inclusion**: English RCTs on probiotics for oral halitosis, ≥2-week follow-up
- **Studies included**: 4 RCTs (from 14 identified)
  - Jamali et al. (n=99, 12 wk, SS K12, post-chlorhexidine)
  - Benic et al. (n=64, 12 wk, SS M18, orthodontic patients, triple-blind)
  - He et al. (n=28, 4 wk, SS K12)
  - Lee et al. (n=92, Weissella cibaria)
- **Outcomes**: Volatile Sulfur Compound (VSC) levels, Organoleptic Test (OLT) scores, Bad Breath (BB) improvement
- **Statistical tool**: RevMan v5.4.1; mean difference (MD) ± SD; 95% CI
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane Collaboration tool

## Results

- **Overall meta-analysis**: p=0.53 — no statistically significant effect of probiotics on oral halitosis measures
- **Jamali et al.**: SS K12 following chlorhexidine disinfection may reduce halitosis severity over longer periods
- **Benic et al.**: SS M18 significantly reduced VSC levels in orthodontic patients
- **He et al.**: SS K12 alone did NOT significantly reduce halitosis
- **Lee et al.**: Weissella cibaria showed measurable Bad Breath improvement
- **Conclusion**: Some probiotics appear to have beneficial effects on halitosis, but pooled evidence is non-significant; more RCTs with standardized protocols are needed
