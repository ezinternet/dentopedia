## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 21 studies: H. pylori infection significantly associated with halitosis (OR 2.85), and successful eradication reduced halitosis by ~83% (RR 0.17), supporting eradication therapy for refractory halitosis.

## 한줄요약

21개 연구 메타분석: H. pylori 감염이 구취와 유의하게 연관(OR 2.85)되며, 성공적 제균 치료 시 구취가 약 83% 감소(RR 0.17)하여 난치성 구취에 제균 치료를 고려할 수 있다.

## Summary

This 2016 meta-analysis by Dou et al. pooled 21 studies (from English and Chinese databases, searched through December 2015) to evaluate the relationship between Helicobacter pylori (H. pylori) infection and halitosis. Four subgroup analyses were conducted:

1. **H. pylori prevalence in halitosis patients vs. controls**: OR 4.03 (95% CI 1.41–11.50; P=0.009) — halitosis patients are 4× more likely to have H. pylori infection.
2. **Halitosis prevalence in H. pylori-infected vs. non-infected patients**: OR 2.85 (95% CI 1.40–5.83; P=0.004) — infected patients are nearly 3× more likely to have halitosis.
3. **Halitosis after successful vs. unsuccessful H. pylori eradication**: RR 0.17 (95% CI 0.08–0.39; P<0.0001) — successful eradication reduced halitosis risk by 83%.
4. **Halitosis before vs. after successful eradication**: RR 4.78 (95% CI 1.45–15.80; P=0.01) — halitosis was nearly 5× more prevalent before eradication than after.

The authors concluded that H. pylori infection is a real contributor to halitosis pathophysiology, and eradication therapy should be considered in patients with refractory halitosis not explained by oral causes.

## Key Contributions

- First meta-analysis pooling English + Chinese evidence on H. pylori–halitosis link
- Quantified bidirectional association with OR and RR across 4 distinct clinical scenarios
- Demonstrated clinically meaningful halitosis reduction after successful H. pylori eradication
- Proposed biochemical mechanism: H. pylori produces VSCs (H₂S, methyl mercaptan) via CBS/CSE enzymes, and urease-generated ammonia contributes to malodor
- Supports a gastric/extra-oral etiology for a subset (~10–20%) of halitosis cases

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review and meta-analysis (MOOSE guidelines)
- **Search**: MEDLINE, PubMed, Cochrane Library, EMBASE, Web of Science, Wanfangdata; inception–December 2015; English + Chinese
- **Included study types**: Case reports, case-control, cross-sectional, RCTs, quasi-RCTs, comparative clinical trials
- **Total screened**: 115 citations → 21 included
- **Analysis**: Random-effects model when I²>50%; ORs for observational studies, RRs for interventional studies; Review Manager 5.2
- **Heterogeneity tool**: Cochrane Q + I² statistic; publication bias not assessed (n<10 per subgroup)

## Results

| Comparison | n studies | Effect | 95% CI | P |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| H. pylori in halitosis(+) vs. halitosis(-) | — | OR 4.03 | 1.41–11.50 | 0.009 |
| Halitosis in H. pylori(+) vs. H. pylori(-) | — | OR 2.85 | 1.40–5.83 | 0.004 |
| Halitosis: successful vs. unsuccessful eradication | — | RR 0.17 | 0.08–0.39 | <0.0001 |
| Halitosis: before vs. after successful eradication | — | RR 4.78 | 1.45–15.80 | 0.01 |

**Limitations**: High heterogeneity from mixed study designs and variable halitosis assessment methods; confounders (periodontal disease, oral hygiene) not uniformly controlled; causality not established; funnel plot not possible.
