## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 7 RCTs showing probiotics significantly reduce OLP scores and VSC levels in the short term (≤4 weeks), but only OLP scores remain significantly improved in the long term (>4 weeks).

## 한줄요약

7개 RCT를 포함한 SR+MA로, 프로바이오틱스가 단기(≤4주)에 관능검사 점수(OLP)와 VSC 농도를 유의하게 감소시키나, 장기(>4주)에는 OLP 점수만 유의한 개선이 지속됨.

## Summary

This systematic review and meta-analysis (BMJ Open, 2022) evaluated the efficacy of probiotics in managing halitosis using a time-stratified approach. Seven RCTs were included (n=23–68 per study; follow-up 2–12 weeks). Probiotic strains studied included *Lactobacillus salivarius*, *L. reuteri*, *Streptococcus salivarius* K12, and *Weissella cibaria*, delivered via chewing gum, tablets, capsule rinses, or powders.

In the short term (≤4 weeks), probiotics significantly reduced both organoleptic (OLP) scores (SMD=−0.58, 95% CI −0.87 to −0.30, p<0.0001) and total VSC levels (SMD=−0.26, 95% CI −0.51 to −0.01, p=0.04). H₂S subgroup reduction was also significant (SMD=−0.73, p=0.02) though with high heterogeneity (I²=75%). In the long term (>4 weeks), only OLP scores showed significant improvement (SMD=−0.45, 95% CI −0.85 to −0.04, p=0.03); VSC reductions were not sustained. Secondary outcomes (tongue coating score, plaque index) showed no significant difference at either timepoint. No publication bias was detected, and sensitivity analyses confirmed stable estimates.

## Key Contributions

1. First quantitative meta-analysis to stratify probiotic effects on halitosis by follow-up duration (short vs long term)
2. Demonstrated that probiotics provide short-term improvement in both subjective (OLP) and objective (VSC) halitosis measures
3. Showed VSC reduction does not persist beyond 4 weeks, while subjective OLP improvement may be sustained
4. Identified key strains with halitosis evidence: *L. salivarius*, *L. reuteri*, *S. salivarius* K12, *W. cibaria*
5. Confirmed no effect on tongue coating or plaque index — mechanism appears targeted to VSC metabolism rather than general oral hygiene

## Methodology

- **Design**: SR+MA; PRISMA guidelines; PROSPERO registered (CRD42021227504)
- **Search**: PubMed, EMBASE, Web of Science, Cochrane CENTRAL (up to Feb 2021); grey literature; no language restriction
- **Inclusion**: RCTs comparing probiotics vs placebo in patients with diagnosed halitosis (OLP and/or VSC criteria)
- **Primary outcomes**: OLP scores (Rosenberg 0–5 scale), VSC concentrations (Halimeter or OralChroma)
- **Secondary outcomes**: Tongue coating scores (TCS), plaque index (PI)
- **Time stratification**: Short-term ≤4 weeks; long-term >4 weeks
- **Statistics**: SMD + 95% CI, random-effects model; Q test + I² for heterogeneity; Egger's test for publication bias; leave-one-out sensitivity analysis
- **Risk of bias**: RoB2 tool — 1 low risk, 5 some concerns, 1 high risk

**Included studies**:

## Results

**Short-term (≤4 weeks)**:
- OLP: SMD=−0.58 (95% CI −0.87 to −0.30), p<0.0001; low heterogeneity (I²<50%)
- VSC total: SMD=−0.26 (95% CI −0.51 to −0.01), p=0.04; low heterogeneity
- H₂S: SMD=−0.73 (95% CI −1.36 to −0.10), p=0.02; high heterogeneity (I²=75%)
- CH₃SH, C₂H₆S, TCS, PI: not significant

**Long-term (>4 weeks)**:
- OLP: SMD=−0.45 (95% CI −0.85 to −0.04), p=0.03
- VSC: not significant; I²=58% (substantial heterogeneity)

**Publication bias**: No evidence (Egger's test p>0.05); symmetric funnel plots

**Sensitivity**: Leave-one-out analysis confirmed stable pooled estimates
