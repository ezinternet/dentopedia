## Summary
Systematic review addressing survival and complication rates of complete-coverage fixed lithium disilicate (LDS) prostheses compared to non-LDS (predominantly PFM). Search: PubMed/MEDLINE, EMBASE, Google Scholar, ClinicalTrials.gov; January 2006–August 2022. Inclusion: prospective clinical studies; LDS fixed crowns and bridges; natural teeth as abutments.

LDS single crowns demonstrate favorable survival rates generally comparable to or exceeding PFM at 5-year follow-up. Long-span LDS FPDs show significantly higher fracture risk than metal-ceramic FPDs. PFM remains the reference standard for multi-unit prostheses; LDS preferred for esthetic single-unit restorations. No meta-analysis performed due to study heterogeneity.

## Key Contributions
- LDS single crowns: 5-year survival comparable to PFM (generally >90%)
- LDS FPDs >3 units: fracture risk significantly higher than metal-ceramic alternatives — not recommended for long-span
- LDS primary advantages: superior esthetics, metal-free; no galvanic sensitivity
- Main complications: chipping/fracture of LDS material; marginal secondary caries

## Methodology
- **Databases**: PubMed/MEDLINE, EMBASE, Google Scholar, ClinicalTrials.gov
- **Search period**: January 2006–August 2022
- **Inclusion**: Prospective clinical studies; complete-coverage LDS crowns/FPDs; natural teeth abutments; English
- **Outcomes**: Survival rate, fracture/chipping, secondary caries, debonding

## Results
- LDS single crowns: ≥90% 5-year survival (qualitative synthesis)
- LDS FPDs: higher fracture risk for >3-unit spans
- Debonding and secondary caries: comparable to PFM
- No meta-analysis; 14–16 studies included (qualitative)
