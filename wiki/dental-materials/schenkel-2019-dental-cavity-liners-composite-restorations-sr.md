## One-line Summary

Cochrane SR + meta-analysis (8 RCTs, >700 participants) found inconsistent, low-quality evidence that a cavity liner under Class I/II posterior composite reduces postoperative hypersensitivity, and no evidence it improves restoration longevity.

## 한줄요약

코크란 SR+MA(8 RCT, 700명 초과): Class I/II 구치부 컴포지트 밑에 cavity liner를 깔아도 술후 과민증 감소 근거는 일관성 없는 저질이고, 수복물 수명 향상 근거는 없음 → 라이너 통상 사용의 적응증 약함.

## Summary

This is the evidence ceiling for the question *"should a cavity liner be placed under a posterior composite?"* — the indication anchor for liner-base products such as Ionosit-Baseliner (DMG, an RMGI/compomer liner-base). Liners have been used since the 19th century on the rationale of pulp protection and thermal insulation. This updated Cochrane review tested whether that rationale produces a measurable clinical benefit under Class I/II resin-based composite (RBC) in permanent teeth.

It does not, on current evidence. Across 8 RCTs (>700 participants), liners showed only inconsistent reductions in postoperative hypersensitivity (benefit at some time points, not others; **low-quality** GRADE) and **no** difference in restoration longevity at 1–2 years. No adverse events occurred. Note: this page is built from the PMC abstract + plain-language summary (full text not retrieved — `full_text: false`); quantitative detail is limited to the Cochrane structured abstract.

**Clinical bottom line:** routinely lining beneath a posterior composite is not supported by high-certainty evidence for either reduced sensitivity or improved longevity. An Ionosit-type RMGI liner-base is therefore justified by *case-specific* reasons (deep cavity / dentin sealing / blocking out undercuts / clinician protocol), not by a general expectation that lining lowers postoperative sensitivity or makes restorations last longer.

## Key Contributions

- Highest-tier (Cochrane) appraisal of the **cavity-liner-under-composite indication**.
- Postoperative hypersensitivity: 7 trials, inconsistent benefit, low-quality.
- Restoration longevity: 4 trials @1 yr + 3 @2 yr — no difference (near-zero failures both arms; one trial RR 1.00, 95% CI 0.07–15.00).
- No adverse events; all included trials at unclear/high risk of bias.

## Methodology

- Cochrane SR (2019 update of 2016); standard Cochrane RoB + duplicate extraction.
- RCTs (parallel + split-mouth), liner vs no liner under Class I/II posterior RBC in permanent teeth; all participants >15 years.
- Databases: Cochrane Oral Health Register, CENTRAL, MEDLINE, Embase, LILACS — to 12 Nov 2018, no language/date limit.
- 8 trials, >700 participants (USA ×2, Thailand ×2, Germany ×2, Saudi Arabia ×1, Turkey ×1).

## Results

| Outcome | Trials | Finding | Certainty |
|---|---|---|---|
| Postoperative hypersensitivity | 7 | Inconsistent — benefit at some but not all time points | Low |
| Restoration failure @ 1 yr | 4 | No difference (3/4 zero failures both arms; 4th RR 1.00, 95% CI 0.07–15.00) | Low |
| Restoration failure @ 2 yr | 3 | No failures either group | Low |
| Adverse events | 8 | None | — |

Authors' conclusion (verbatim): "There is inconsistent, low-quality evidence regarding the difference in postoperative hypersensitivity… Furthermore, no evidence was found to demonstrate a difference in the longevity of restorations placed with or without dental cavity liners."
