## Summary
In vitro study on micro-shear bond strength of TheraCem (dual-cure self-adhesive resin cement) to 3D-printed hybrid ceramic (Saremc Print Crowntec) after non-thermal atmospheric plasma (NTAP) ± sandblasting surface treatment. All bond strength values relatively low (2.12–2.70 MPa), suggesting 3D-printed hybrid ceramics present challenges for resin adhesion compared to milled materials.

## Key Contributions
- NTAP plasma: alternative to sandblasting for 3D-printed hybrid ceramics
- Combination SP50 (50µm SB + NTAP): highest µSBS — synergistic mechanical + chemical activation
- Low absolute bond values: 3D-printed polymers/hybrids bond differently from ceramics
- Clinical implication: surface treatment protocol for 3D-printed ceramics still evolving; current values may be insufficient for high-stress applications

## Results
| Group | µSBS (MPa) |
|-------|-----------|
| SP50 (SB50+NTAP) | 2.70 ± 0.49 |
| SP110 (SB110+NTAP) | 2.37 ± 0.38 |
| NTAP alone | 2.35 ± 0.57 |
| SB110 | 2.18 ± 0.31 |
| SB50 | 2.12 ± 0.75 |
