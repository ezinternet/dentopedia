## One-line Summary

5-year prospective follow-up of Koenig 2021 cohort (n=101): LTD significantly increases from baseline to 5 years in 3Y-TZP monolithic zirconia; grain pull-out raises 3Y-TZP nanoparticle release concern; fracture rate 4.5%; glazing still non-protective.

## Summary

Koenig et al. (2024) reports the 5-year results of the same cohort first described at 2 years (Koenig 2021). The study followed 101 posterior monolithic 3Y-TZP prostheses using ex vivo Raman spectroscopy and SEM (including FIB-SEM for the first time in this cohort). LTD, which was confirmed to initiate at 6 months in the prior report, was found to progress slowly but significantly through 5 years. The t-m transformation remained confined to the extreme surface in localized non-uniform clusters. A new concern raised is that tribological stress-induced grain pull-out may release sub-micron 3Y-TZP nanoparticles into the body — a question requiring dedicated toxicological follow-up. The prosthesis fracture rate was 4.5% at 5 years, though whether LTD contributed to these fractures is unresolved. Glazing remained ineffective as a protective measure.

## Key Contributions

- **LTD confirmed at 5 years**: Statistically significant increase in monoclinic phase from baseline to 5 years
- **Slow kinetics**: LTD remains at extreme surface, non-uniform — ISO 25% Vm threshold apparently not reached
- **Grain pull-out + nanoparticles**: Key new finding — tribological grain pull-out may release 3Y-TZP nanoparticles into the body; biocompatibility unknown
- **Fracture rate**: 4.5% at 5 years (whether LTD-related is uncertain)
- **Glazing**: Non-protective (consistent with 2-year finding)
- **FIB-SEM**: First use in this cohort to characterize grain-scale surface damage

## Methodology

- 5-year prospective follow-up; same cohort as Koenig 2021 (NCT02150226)
- Timepoints: baseline, 6m, 1y, 2y, 3y, 5y
- Raman spectroscopy (n=2,640 points/timepoint) + SEM + FIB-SEM (new)
- 4 surface types per element: occlusal, axial, glazed, unglazed
- Strobe compliant; CHU Liège ethics; partial 3M funding (independent study design)

## Results

| Finding | Detail |
|---|---|
| LTD progression | Significant increase baseline → 5 years |
| LTD location | Extreme surface; non-uniform clusters |
| LTD kinetics | Slow; ISO 25% Vm not exceeded |
| Grain pull-out | Present; potential nanoparticle release concern |

| Glaze protection | None |
