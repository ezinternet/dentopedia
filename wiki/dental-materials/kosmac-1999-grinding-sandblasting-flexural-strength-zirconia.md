## One-line Summary
In vitro (Dent Mater 1999): fine- and coarse-grained Y-TZP subjected to grinding/sandblasting — sandblasting significantly increases flexural strength; grinding reduces Weibull modulus; sandblasting produces maximum monoclinic phase transformation and transformed zone depth; foundational paper on zirconia strengthening.

## Summary
Foundational study demonstrating that sandblasting strengthens Y-TZP through compressive stress and phase transformation, while grinding increases surface defect size and reduces reliability (Weibull modulus). Cited >700 times.

## Key Contributions
- Sandblasting: significantly increases flexural strength in both fine and coarse grained Y-TZP
- Grinding: significantly different from control for fine-grained only; reduces Weibull modulus
- All surface treatments: reduce Weibull modulus (reliability decreases)
- Sandblasting: largest monoclinic phase content + deepest transformed zone depth (TZD)
- Grinding: moderate monoclinic phase; largest critical defect size

## Mechanism
- Sandblasting → compressive residual stress → crack propagation inhibited → strength ↑
- But Weibull ↓ = more variability in strength → clinical reliability concern
- Clinically: sandblasting strengthens but creates surface damage layer
