## Summary
In vitro study comparing silica-based glass-ceramic liners (P-containing and P-free) with MDP-containing primers (Monobond Plus, Z-Prime Plus) for tensile bond strength between zirconia abutments and crowns. P-containing liner achieves bond strength equivalent to MDP primers, suggesting dual (micromechanical + chemical) bonding mechanism via phosphate and silicate groups.

## Key Contributions
- Glass-ceramic liner (phosphate-containing) achieves TBS comparable to dedicated MDP primers
- Dual bonding mechanism: silica interlocking + phosphate-Zr chemical bonding
- P-free liner: lower than P-containing, confirming phosphate groups critical for chemical adhesion
- Clinical alternative to MDP primers when liner already applied in laboratory
