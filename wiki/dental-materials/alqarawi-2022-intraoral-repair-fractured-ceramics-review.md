## One-line Summary

Narrative review (21 studies, 2017–2022): intraoral ceramic repair is a viable minimally invasive alternative to crown replacement; for veneered zirconia, HF etching of veneering ceramic + silane + composite resin is optimal; bond strength is the critical success determinant.

## Summary

Alqarawi et al. (2022) reviewed 21 studies (17 in vitro, 3 case reports, 1 prospective clinical study) published 2017–2022 examining intraoral repair techniques for ceramic fractures in fixed dental prostheses. Ceramic chipping is the most frequent technical complication in fixed prosthodontics (12.7% at 3 years per prior SR). Intraoral repair avoids the risks and costs of crown removal and replacement. The review synthesizes surface conditioning and bonding protocols by ceramic type, identifying HF acid etching + silane + composite as the gold standard for glass-containing ceramics. For veneered zirconia specifically, when the fracture exposes the zirconia core, the core must be conditioned separately (sandblasting or MDP adhesive) while the remaining veneering ceramic receives HF + silane. Repair success depends primarily on achieving adequate bond strength between substrate and repair composite.

## Key Contributions

- Synthesizes repair protocols for all major ceramic types: feldspathic, leucite, lithium disilicate, zirconia (veneered and monolithic)
- **Veneered zirconia protocol**: HF etching of veneering ceramic + silane applied to exposed zirconia core + composite resin
- Surface conditioning hierarchy: HF + silane > universal adhesive alone; laser ± HF is an alternative for specific ceramics
- Identifies main failure mode: inadequate bond strength due to technique deviation or contamination
- Supports intraoral repair as temporary but effective; avoids abutment damage and excessive tooth reduction from crown removal

## Methodology

- Narrative review; PubMed search January 2017 – August 2022 (English only)
- Keywords: intraoral repair, ceramics, porcelain, porcelain fused to metal, efficacy
- 21 studies included: 17 in vitro, 3 case reports, 1 prospective clinical study
- No statistical analysis (narrative synthesis)

## Results by Ceramic Type

| Ceramic Type | Recommended Surface Treatment | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Feldspathic / Leucite | HF + silane + conventional adhesive | HF + silane > HF + universal adhesive (SBS) |
| Lithium disilicate | HF + silane; grit blasting + rubber dam | MEP primer as alternative |
| Zirconia-reinforced lithium silicate | HF ± laser (Nd:YAG, Er:YAG) | Laser combinations may be superior |
| Veneered zirconia (core exposed) | HF on veneer + silane + MDP adhesive on zirconia core | Two-substrate protocol required |
| Monolithic zirconia | Sandblasting + MDP adhesive | No HF (no glass phase); silane alone insufficient |

## Key Protocol (Veneered Zirconia)

1. Airborne abrasion (50 µm Al₂O₃) of exposed zirconia core
2. HF acid etching (9.6%) of remaining veneering ceramic for 60 seconds
3. Rinse + dry
4. Silane application to entire repair area; dry/heat activate
5. Adhesive resin (MDP-containing for zirconia) application
6. Composite resin application in increments; light cure
