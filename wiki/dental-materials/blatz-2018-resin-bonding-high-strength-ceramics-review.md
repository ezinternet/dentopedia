## Summary
Critical review in JDR discussing clinical evidence for resin bonding protocols for high-strength ceramic restorations (alumina, zirconia). Covers surface pretreatment methods (Al₂O₃ air-particle abrasion, silica/silane coating via Rocatec/CoJet), cementation strategies by restoration type, and clinical survival data for zirconia-based FDPs and RBFDPs.

## Key Contributions
- Resin bonding mandatory for adhesion-dependent restorations: veneers, RBFDPs, thin ceramics
- Conventional cementation acceptable for retentive full-coverage crowns
- Al₂O₃ air-particle abrasion: durable bond to zirconia
- Silica/silane coating (Rocatec, CoJet): recommended for zirconia surface activation
- Single-retainer cantilever RBFDP: 5yr 92.3%, 10yr 94.4% survival
- Monolithic full-contour zirconia (FCZ): limited long-term data available (2018)

## Methodology
Critical narrative review in Journal of Dental Research (2018). Reviewed clinical evidence on resin bonding for high-strength ceramics including alumina (In-Ceram, Procera) and zirconia. Surface pretreatment protocols: tribochemical silica coating (Rocatec, CoJet), Al₂O₃ sandblasting, laser irradiation, selective infiltration etching. Clinical outcome data for zirconia FDPs, RBFDPs, cantilever prostheses extracted from published studies.

## Results
- Resin bonding: mandatory for adhesion-dependent restorations
- Conventional cementation: acceptable for structurally retentive preparations
- Rocatec/CoJet: superior bond strength for zirconia vs sandblasting alone
- Zirconia-based 3-unit FDP: 100% survival at 5yr with proper design
- Cantilever RBFDP 5yr: 92.3%; 10yr: 94.4%
- Monolithic FCZ crowns: insufficient long-term clinical data in 2018
