## Summary
In vitro wear study (UAB, Alabama wear device) establishing that polished zirconia is minimally abrasive to opposing enamel, while glazed zirconia causes significant enamel wear. Foundational paper for the "polish rather than glaze" recommendation.

## Key Contributions
- Surface roughness order (smooth→rough): polished Zr < glazed Zr < polished+reglazed Zr < veneering porcelain < enamel
- **Polished Zr**: zero ceramic loss; least enamel antagonist wear
- **Glazed Zr**: significant ceramic AND enamel wear
- **Veneering porcelain**: most enamel wear of all groups
- Surface roughness directly predicts antagonist enamel wear

## Wear Results
| Group | Ceramic wear | Enamel antagonist wear |
|-------|-------------|----------------------|
| Polished Zr | None | Least |
| Glazed Zr | Significant | Moderate |
| Polished + reglazed Zr | Significant | Moderate |
| Veneering porcelain | Most | Most |
| Enamel (control) | Moderate | Moderate |
