## Summary
In vitro study establishing the relationship between abutment height (2 vs 4 mm) and pull-out retention force of monolithic zirconia crowns cemented with TheraCem (calcium silicate self-adhesive resin) vs glass ionomer (GI). Strong positive correlation between abutment height and retention. TheraCem significantly outperforms GI cement at all heights, supporting its use for short clinical crown scenarios.

## Key Contributions
- Abutment height: primary retention determinant (r=0.95) — preparation design critical
- TheraCem: significantly superior to glass ionomer at both 2mm and 4mm heights
- Short clinical crown management: TheraCem + monolithic zirconia = reliable option
- Conservative preparation guidance: even 2mm abutment with TheraCem achieves acceptable retention
