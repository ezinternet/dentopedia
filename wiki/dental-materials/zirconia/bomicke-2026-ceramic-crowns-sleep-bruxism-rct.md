## One-line Summary

3-year RCT (n=109; 4 groups by EMG-confirmed sleep bruxism × randomized monolithic lithium disilicate vs zirconia molar crown, no occlusal guards permitted): sleep bruxism showed no statistically detectable impact on technical complications, survival, or success of either ceramic.

## Summary

Sleep bruxism is widely treated as a relative contraindication or risk modifier for all-ceramic restorations because of presumed occlusal overload, yet direct RCT evidence with objective bruxism diagnosis is scarce. Bömicke et al. (2026) randomized 109 patients needing a single molar crown to monolithic lithium disilicate (LiDi) or zirconia (Zir), stratified by EMG-confirmed sleep-bruxism status (BruxOff portable device plus questionnaire and clinical exam), and deliberately did NOT provide protective occlusal guards. Over 3 years, no technical complications occurred in any of the four groups, and neither survival nor success differed significantly between bruxers and non-bruxers for either material. The numerically lower success in the LiDi-SB group (81.5% vs 95.2%) hints at a possible underpowered trend, but no comparison reached significance.

## Key Contributions

- First (per abstract) RCT to isolate sleep bruxism as a stratifying factor for monolithic LiDi vs Zir molar single-crown performance, using an objective EMG diagnosis rather than self-report alone.
- Removes the confounding of night guards (none permitted), testing the unmitigated effect of bruxism.
- Zero technical complications across all four groups over 3 years supports the durability of monolithic all-ceramic molar crowns even in untreated bruxers — at least within this horizon and sample size.

## Methodology

- **Design**: prospective RCT; crown material randomly allocated, sleep-bruxism (SB) status used as stratifying factor → four groups: LiDi-SB (n=27), LiDi-no SB (n=21), Zir-SB (n=21), Zir-no SB (n=24).
- **SB diagnosis**: structured questionnaire + clinical examination + portable EMG (BruxOff).
- **Restorations**: monolithic LiDi or Zir single molar crowns; protective occlusal guards not permitted.
- **Follow-up**: 1 week, 6 months, 1, 2, and 3 years post-cementation.
- **Outcomes**: technical complications, survival, success.
- **Statistics**: Fisher's exact test, α = 0.05. Registered NCT03039985.

## Results

| Outcome | Material | SB | no-SB | 95% CI of difference | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Survival | LiDi | 96.3% | 100% | −0.1828 to 0.1481 | >0.999 |
| Survival | Zir | 95.2% | 95.8% | −0.2190 to 0.1940 | >0.999 |
| Success | LiDi | 81.5% | 95.2% | −0.3590 to 0.1450 | 0.211 |
| Success | Zir | 85.7% | 95.8% | −0.3390 to 0.1530 | 0.326 |

- No technical complications in any group.
- No statistically significant SB effect on complications, survival, or success for either material over 3 years.
- Numerically lower success in SB groups (notably LiDi-SB) suggests a non-significant trend, plausibly underpowered given small per-group n and wide confidence intervals.
