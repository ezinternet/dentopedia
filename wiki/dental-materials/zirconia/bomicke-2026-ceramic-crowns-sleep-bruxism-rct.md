## One-line Summary

3-year RCT (n=109; 4 groups by EMG-confirmed sleep bruxism × randomized monolithic lithium disilicate vs zirconia molar crown, no occlusal guards permitted): sleep bruxism showed no statistically detectable impact on technical complications, survival, or success of either ceramic.

## 한줄요약

3년 RCT (환자 109명; EMG(BruxOff)로 확인한 수면 이갈이(Sleep Bruxism, SB) 유무 × 무작위 배정 모놀리식 리튬디실리케이트(Lithium Disilicate, LiDi) vs 지르코니아(Zirconia, Zir) 대구치 단일 크라운, 교합안정장치 미허용): 수면 이갈이는 두 재료 모두에서 기술적 합병증·생존율·성공률에 통계적으로 유의한 영향을 주지 않았다.

## Summary

Sleep bruxism (SB) is widely presumed to increase occlusal loads and risk ceramic crown fracture, yet direct RCT evidence using objective SB diagnosis was absent. Bömicke et al. (2026) randomized 109 patients requiring a single molar crown to monolithic lithium disilicate (LiDi) or zirconia (Zir), stratified by three-way EMG-confirmed SB status (BruxOff portable device + questionnaire + clinical signs). Protective occlusal guards were deliberately withheld to isolate the bruxism effect. Over 3 years, no technical complications (ceramic fractures or chipping) occurred in any of the four groups. Neither survival (LiDi: SB 96.3% vs no-SB 100%, p>0.999; Zir: SB 95.2% vs no-SB 95.8%, p>0.999) nor success rates differed significantly by SB status. Complications were exclusively biological (recurrent periodontitis, pulpitis, vertical root fractures — both VRFs in bruxers, though not statistically significant). The study is underpowered for rare fracture events and the 3-year horizon limits conclusions about long-term fatigue failure. Nevertheless, this represents the highest-quality current evidence that monolithic all-ceramic molar crowns perform safely in definitively diagnosed SB patients without splint protection.

## Key Contributions

- **First RCT with definitive SB diagnosis**: previous bruxism–ceramic studies used "possible" or "probable" SB based on self-report/clinical signs only. This trial required all three diagnostic methods to agree (EMG as objective arbiter), providing the most rigorous bruxism classification in this literature.
- **No occlusal guards permitted**: isolates the unmitigated mechanical effect of SB on ceramic restorations — directly applicable to patients who are non-compliant with night guards.
- **Zero technical complications across 109 crowns over 3 years**: the primary concern driving clinical caution about placing all-ceramics in bruxers did not materialize, for either material.
- **Both LiDi (pressed) and Zir (milled, 3 mol% Y-TZP)** evaluated in the same controlled trial under SB stratification — enables direct material comparison in a bruxism-exposed population.
- **Crown thickness verified pre-insertion** by independent examiners (mean 1.3–1.4 mm occlusal), establishing protocol-compliant material dimensions as a quality control baseline.

## Methodology

- **Design**: prospective, 2-arm, single-center RCT (parallel 1:1); Heidelberg University Prosthodontics, 2015–2020. Ethics S-312/2014; NCT03039985.
- **Participants**: 109 enrolled (16 excluded before 3-year analysis); adults ≥18 with ≥1 molar requiring a single crown, vital or root-treated abutment, natural antagonists, periodontally stable. Guards excluded by design.
- **SB assessment** (three-pronged, American Academy of Sleep Medicine criteria): questionnaire (Paesani) + structured interview (Raphael) + BruxOff EMG/ECG ≥5 nights (SB positive if >2 episodes/h in ≥1 night). All three must agree; otherwise excluded.
- **Groups (3-year analysis)**: LiDi-SB n=27 (moderate 14, heavy 13), LiDi-no SB n=21, Zir-SB n=21 (moderate 7, heavy 14), Zir-no SB n=24.
- **LiDi crowns**: IPS e.max Press (pressed glass-ceramic); HF 5%/20 s etch + Monobond Plus silane; cemented with RelyX Unicem.
- **Zir crowns**: Prettau Zirconia (3Y-TZP), milled and sintered at 1600°C; air-abraded 50 µm alumina; cemented with RelyX Unicem.
- **Preparation**: ≥1.2 mm occlusal, ≥1.0 mm axial; chamfer finish line; 6–12° total occlusal convergence. Composite core buildups as needed.
- **Follow-up**: 1 week, 6 months, 1, 2, 3 years. Probing depths, mobility, and vitality at all annual visits.
- **Statistics**: Fisher's exact test, α=0.05; 95% CI by Newcombe Wilson-based method. Survival = in situ, no replacement. Success = in situ, no complication of any kind.

## Results

**Technical complications**: 0 in all groups (no ceramic fractures, no chipping).

**Survival at 3 years**:

| Material | SB | no-SB | 95% CI (diff) | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| LiDi | 96.3% (26/27) | 100% (21/21) | −0.183 to 0.148 | >0.999 |
| Zir | 95.2% (20/21) | 95.8% (23/24) | −0.219 to 0.194 | >0.999 |

**Success at 3 years**:

| Material | SB | no-SB | 95% CI (diff) | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| LiDi | 81.5% (22/27) | 95.2% (20/21) | −0.359 to 0.145 | 0.211 |
| Zir | 85.7% (18/21) | 95.8% (23/24) | −0.339 to 0.153 | 0.326 |

**Biological complications** (only complication type observed): recurrent periodontitis (4 total), symptomatic irreversible pulpitis → root canal treatment (3 total), vertical root fracture → extraction or hemisection (2 total, both in SB groups), loss of proximal contact (2 total). Three crowns counted as failures (2 VRF-related in SB groups, 1 lost for FPD due to adjacent tooth loss in Zir-no SB).

**Numerically lower success in SB groups** (LiDi-SB 81.5% vs 95.2%; Zir-SB 85.7% vs 95.8%) did not reach significance. Both VRFs occurred in bruxers, suggesting possible subclinical risk increase in bruxism patients, but underpowered for definitive conclusion.

**Baseline imbalances**: Zir-SB group disproportionately male (71.4% vs ~33% others), expected to have higher bite forces; no-SB groups were older (higher age → lower occlusal force), potentially confounding between-group comparisons.
