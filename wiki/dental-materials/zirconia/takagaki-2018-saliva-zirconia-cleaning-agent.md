## One-line Summary
In vitro XPS + bond strength study (Dent Mater J 2018; saliva-contaminated Y-PSZ high-translucency zirconia): air abrasion achieved the highest resin cement bond strength and phosphoric-acid cleaning left phosphorous residues that chemically interfered with MDP bonding, warning against phosphoric acid for zirconia cleaning.

## 한줄요약
in-vitro(Dent Mater J 2018): Y-PSZ 고투명 지르코니아 — 인산세정 후 인산 잔류물이 MDP 화학결합 방해; 에어어브레이전 > Ivoclean > 인산; XPS로 표면 원소 확인.

## Summary
In vitro XPS analysis + bond strength study on Y-PSZ (high-translucency yttria-partially stabilized zirconia) after saliva contamination and cleaning. Key finding: phosphoric acid cleaning leaves phosphorous residues that chemically interfere with MDP bonding — a mechanism-level warning against using phosphoric acid for Y-PSZ cleaning.

## Key Contributions
- XPS evidence that phosphoric acid leaves P residues on zirconia → blocks MDP chemical adhesion
- Y-PSZ (more yttria = higher translucency, but same surface chemistry) — findings applicable to all translucency grades
- Air abrasion: most thorough mechanical decontamination
- Clinical implication: avoid phosphoric acid for zirconia cleaning; prefer air abrasion or Ivoclean

## Results
| Cleaning method | XPS contamination | Bond strength |
|----------------|------------------|--------------|
| Air abrasion | Lowest organic | Highest |
| Ivoclean | Low, no P residue | High |
| Phosphoric acid | P residue present | Reduced (MDP blocked) |
| Control (no clean) | High organic | Lowest |
