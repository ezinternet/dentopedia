## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 9 studies (n=1,657 monolithic zirconia CAD/CAM single crowns): survival rates 91–100% across studies; short follow-up (mean 1.07 yrs), high heterogeneity (I²=93.74%), bruxism subgroup shows markedly lower marginal integrity.

## Summary

Leitão et al. (2022) is the first SR+MA specifically focused on monolithic zirconia (MZ) CAD/CAM tooth-supported single crowns. It fills the gap explicitly noted by Pjetursson 2018, which excluded MZ due to insufficient follow-up data available in 2016. Nine studies comprising 594 patients and 1,657 restorations were included. All studies were rated moderate quality (NOS). Survival rates ranged widely (91–100%), with three studies reporting fractures requiring replacement. High heterogeneity (I²=93.74%) precludes a precise pooled estimate. The very short mean follow-up (1.07 years) means long-term durability remains unproven.

## Key Contributions

- First SR+MA exclusively on MZ (not veneered zirconia) tooth-supported SCs
- Demonstrates clinical viability of MZ (SR 91–100%) but highlights data scarcity
- Shows no chipping (by definition — monolithic, no veneer layer)
- BOP mean 29.12% — acceptable biological response
- Identifies bruxism as a key risk factor (marginal integrity SR 31.60% in one bruxism cohort)
- No significant correlation between SR and cementation type or follow-up duration (p>0.05)

## Methodology

- PRISMA-compliant SR+MA; PROSPERO CRD42020166112
- Databases: Medline/PubMed, Embase, Cochrane Library (through May 2020)
- Inclusion: adult patients, tooth-supported MZ CAD/CAM SCs, minimum 3-month follow-up
- Quality: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS) — moderate for all studies
- Statistics: random effects model; I² and Cochran Q for heterogeneity; linear regression for SR vs cementation type and follow-up
