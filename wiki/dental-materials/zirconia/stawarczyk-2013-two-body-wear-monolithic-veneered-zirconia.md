## 한줄요약
in-vitro(Acta Odontol Scand 2013): 1.2M사이클 저작시뮬레이터 — 연마 단일체 Zr(27μm): 최소 대합치 마모; 유약도재(GZC 118μm): 최대; 연마 Zr군: 대합치 에나멜 균열 100%; 표면재료가 결정인자.

## Summary
Long-term chewing simulation (1.2 million cycles) comparing 6 surface conditions of zirconia. Polished monolithic zirconia shows least enamel wear but paradoxically causes 100% enamel crack formation — raising concerns about contact stress concentration.

## Key Contributions
- 6 groups: VZ (veneered), GZC (glaze ceramic), GZS (glaze spray), MAZ (manual polish), MEZ (mechanical polish), MA (alloy control)
- Polished Zr (MAZ/MEZ): least enamel volume loss (27–28μm)
- Glazed with ceramic (GZC): most enamel volume loss (118μm)
- **Critical finding: 100% enamel cracks in polished Zr groups** — despite low volume loss
- Materials/cycles both significant (p<0.001)
- Surface material (not base) determines antagonist wear behavior

## Wear vs Crack Paradox
| Group | Enamel wear (μm) | Enamel cracks |
|-------|-----------------|---------------|
| MAZ/MEZ (polished Zr) | 27–28 (least) | 100% |
| GZC (glaze ceramic) | 118 (most) | 50% |
| Alloy/Veneered | Intermediate | 50% |
