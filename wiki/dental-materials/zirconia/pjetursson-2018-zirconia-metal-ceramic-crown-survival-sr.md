## One-line Summary

SR of 35 studies (n=5,275 implant-supported crowns): zirconia-ceramic 5-year survival 97.6% vs metal-ceramic 98.3% — similar overall, but zirconia shows significantly more material fractures (2.1% vs 0.2%) and fewer aesthetic complications.

## Summary

This 2018 ITI Consensus Conference SR (Pjetursson et al.) systematically reviewed 35 clinical studies to compare 5-year outcomes of veneered zirconia-ceramic vs gold-alloy metal-ceramic implant-supported single crowns (SCs). Using robust Poisson regression meta-analysis, the review found that zirconia and metal-ceramic SCs exhibit comparable overall survival and biological complication rates. However, zirconia carries a significantly higher risk of catastrophic material fracture, while metal-ceramic exhibits more aesthetic complications. Importantly, no monolithic zirconia studies met the ≥3-year inclusion threshold, making this review specific to veneered zirconia frameworks.

## Key Contributions

- **5-year survival**: Zirconia 97.6% (95% CI 94.3–99.0) vs Metal-ceramic 98.3% (95% CI 96.8–99.1) — not significantly different
- **Material fracture**: Zirconia 2.1% vs Metal-ceramic 0.2% (p=0.001) — significantly more zirconia crown fractures
- **Chipping**: Nearly identical — 2.8% (zirconia) vs 2.9% (metal-ceramic) at 5 years
- **Aesthetic complications**: Fewer in zirconia group
- **Biological outcomes**: Similar between groups
- **Complication-free rate**: Zirconia 83.8% vs Metal-ceramic 86.7% (wide CI for zirconia due to small n)

## Methodology

- PICO-based SR, PROSPERO registered (CRD42017079002)
- PubMed electronic search through November 2016; manual reference screening
- Inclusion: human, ≥10 patients, ≥3 year follow-up, implant-supported SCs, gold-alloy MC or zirconia-based
- 5,263 titles → 240 full texts → 35 included studies
- Robust Poisson regression for 5-year proportion estimates
- Dual independent reviewer selection

## Results

| Outcome | Zirconia-ceramic | Metal-ceramic |
|---|---|---|
| 5-year survival | 97.6% (n=912) | 98.3% (n=4,363) |
| Complication-free | 83.8% (n=76) | 86.7% (n=1,300) |
| Chipping rate | 2.8% | 2.9% |
| Material fracture | **2.1%** | **0.2%** (p=0.001) |
| Aesthetic complications | Fewer | More |
| Biological complications | Similar | Similar |

Note: Monolithic zirconia excluded — no studies with ≥3 year follow-up available at time of search.
