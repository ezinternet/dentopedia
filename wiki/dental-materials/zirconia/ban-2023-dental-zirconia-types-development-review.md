## Summary
Narrative review by Ban S (Aichi Gakuin University) tracing the development of dental zirconia from 1998 to 2021. Explains crystalline phases (monoclinic, tetragonal, cubic), yttria stabilization, and stress-induced phase transformation toughening. Summarizes the translucency-strength trade-off as yttria content increases from 3Y-TZP to 6Y/UHTZ.

## Key Contributions
- 3Y-TZP (1998): highest flexural strength and fracture toughness; opaque → core material
- 5Y-PSZ (2014+): improved translucency; cubic+tetragonal phases; lower strength
- Multilayer (M3Y, M5Y, 2015+): gradient translucency for esthetic anterior zones
- 6Y/UHTZ (2016–2021): ultra-high translucency; mostly cubic phase; lowest strength
- Core trade-off: yttria content ↑ → translucency ↑, flexural strength ↓
- Phase transformation toughening: tetragonal-to-monoclinic at crack tip (T-M transformation)

## Methodology
Narrative review from Dental Materials Journal 2023. Author: Ban S, Aichi Gakuin University. Covers commercial dental zirconia evolution 1998–2021. Categorizes by yttria content (3Y, 4Y, 5Y, 6Y) and commercial names (3Y-HA, 3Y-TZP, 5Y-PSZ, M3Y/M5Y, 6Y-TZP/UHTZ). Discusses crystallographic phases, sintering conditions, and mechanical properties (flexural strength, fracture toughness, translucency).

## Results
- 3Y-HA (1998): flexural strength ~1200 MPa; fracture toughness ~5 MPa√m
- 5Y-PSZ: flexural strength ~600–800 MPa; translucency improved
- M3Y/M5Y multilayer: gradient yttria distribution achieving cervical opacity + incisal translucency
- 6Y/UHTZ: flexural strength ~300–500 MPa; highest translucency
- Phase transformation toughening: tetragonal → monoclinic at crack front absorbs energy
- Clinical implication: 3Y-TZP for posterior FPDs; 5Y/6Y for monolithic anterior crowns
