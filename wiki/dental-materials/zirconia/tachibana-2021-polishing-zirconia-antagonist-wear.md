## Summary
Kyushu University study evaluating antagonist enamel wear of polished vs ground Y-TZP under three contact conditions (contact-slide, back-and-forth slide, repetitive contact). Confirms polishing after grinding is mandatory; intraoral polishing kits are essential clinical tools.

## Key Contributions
- 3 contact conditions tested to simulate varying intraoral situations
- Polished Y-TZP: consistently lowest antagonist enamel wear across ALL contact conditions
- Ground Y-TZP: higher roughness → higher antagonist wear regardless of contact type
- Polishing is the dominant factor over contact condition
- Clinical mandate: always polish zirconia after any grinding/occlusal adjustment
