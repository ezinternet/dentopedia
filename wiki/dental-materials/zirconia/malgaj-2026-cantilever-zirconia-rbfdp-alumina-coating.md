## One-line Summary

8-year RCT (57 patients, 64 cantilever zirconia RBFDPs, mean 61.4 mo) — non-invasive nanostructured alumina coating (NAC) achieved retention (88.4%) statistically equivalent to airborne-particle abrasion (89.1%, p=0.99), with 100% prosthesis survival.

## Summary

This prospective, randomized, controlled, double-blind clinical trial (CONSORT; NCT04693949) compared **nanostructured alumina coating (NAC)** against conventional **airborne-particle abrasion (APA)** as the bonding pretreatment for cantilever zirconia resin-bonded fixed dental prostheses (RBFDPs), conducted at the University of Ljubljana over December 2017–August 2025.

Fifty-seven patients (age 13–71 years; 28 women, 29 men) requiring replacement of a missing maxillary or mandibular incisor received 64 single-retainer cantilever RBFDPs fabricated from 3Y-TZP (Ceramill Zolid) with feldspathic porcelain veneer. APA frameworks (n=32) were air-abraded with 50-μm alumina after try-in; NAC frameworks (n=32) were immersed in an alumina precursor sol and calcination-fired at 900°C before veneering, then acid-conditioned post-try-in. All restorations were cemented under rubber dam with MDP-containing resin cement (Panavia 21 TC).

Over a mean follow-up of 61.4 ± 22.1 months (range 8.3–97.4 months), six debondings occurred (3 per group). Overall prosthesis survival was 100% for both groups. Retention was statistically indistinguishable (NAC 88.4%, APA 89.1%; log-rank p=0.99). Retention was highest for mandibular incisors (100%) and lowest for maxillary central incisors (79%), though pontic-position differences were not significant (p=0.18). SEM of debonded retainer wings revealed NAC failures concentrated at the NAC–zirconia interface (adhesive delamination), distinguishing them from the mixed cohesive–adhesive pattern in APA failures.

Clinical bottom line: NAC provides a minimally invasive pretreatment with mid- to long-term retention equivalent to APA, with particular relevance for damage-prone translucent (high-yttria) zirconia where APA-induced surface flaws carry unknown fracture risk.

## Key Contributions

- **Largest RCT of an alternative zirconia pretreatment** to date; extends prior short-term NAC data (same trial at 3 years, Ref. 32) to a mean of 5 years and a maximum of ~8 years.
- Establishes **clinical equivalence of NAC and APA** retention (88.4% vs 89.1%, p=0.99) — null hypothesis accepted.
- Demonstrates **100% prosthesis survival** in both groups; no framework fractures in 3Y-TZP.
- SEM failure analysis: NAC debondings show adhesive failure at NAC–zirconia interface, identifying coating–substrate adhesion as the primary optimization target.
- Contextualizes NAC as the only clinically-tested alternative to APA in an RCT; strengthens NAC's candidacy for translucent zirconia RBFDPs where APA creates surface-integrity concerns.
- Supports the historical finding that maxillary central incisors are the highest-risk pontic position (occlusal and traumatic stress exposure).

## Methodology

- **Design**: Prospective, randomized, controlled, double-blind RCT; CONSORT-reported.
- **Setting**: Academic center, Dept. of Prosthodontics, University of Ljubljana.
- **Participants**: 57 patients; 64 cantilever zirconia RBFDPs (6 patients with split-mouth allocation for two restorations). Dropout: 0%.
- **Inclusion**: No bruxism; healthy abutment (no periodontal/periapical disease); enamel bonding surface ≥30 mm²; adequate interocclusal space (retainer wing ≥0.7 mm; proximal connector ≥2 mm × 3 mm); incisor-canine guidance without pontic contact; adequate edentulous space.
- **Intervention groups**:
  - APA (n=32): 50-μm alumina abrasion (0.1 MPa, 10 mm distance) after final try-in → ultrasonic cleaning 96% ethanol 3 min.
  - NAC (n=32): VALLBOND precursor immersion → calcination 900°C/30 min (pre-veneering, SEM-verified) → 37% phosphoric acid conditioning after try-in.
- **Material**: 3Y-TZP (Ceramill Zolid) CAD/CAM frameworks; VITAVM9 feldspathic porcelain veneering; Panavia 21 TC (MDP) resin cement; rubber dam isolation.
- **Pontic sub-classification**: Group 1 = maxillary central incisors; Group 2 = maxillary lateral incisors; Group 3 = mandibular incisors.
- **Outcomes**: Primary = retention (debonding-free); Secondary = survival. Kaplan-Meier + log-rank (α=0.05). SEM for failure mode; MeshLab for bonding surface area.
- **Follow-up schedule**: 2 weeks, 6 months, then annually.

## Results

| Outcome | NAC (n=32) | APA (n=32) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Retention rate | 88.4% | 89.1% | 0.99 (NS) |
| Survival rate | 100% | 100% | — |
| Debondings | 3 | 3 | — |
| Mean follow-up (mo) | 61.4 ± 22.1 | 61.4 ± 22.1 | — |

**Retention by pontic position** (log-rank p=0.18, NS):
- Mandibular incisors (Group 3): 100%
- Maxillary lateral incisors (Group 2): 88.8%
- Maxillary central incisors (Group 1): 79%

**Debonding events**:
- APA: 1 post-trauma (6.3 mo), 2 spontaneous (15.6 and 36.1 mo).
- NAC: 2 post-trauma (2.1 and 22.9 mo), 1 spontaneous (30.6 mo).
- All debonded restorations were rebonded after APA reconditioning and remained functional.

**Complications**:
- 2 biological (gingivitis; tooth rotation — resolved at 12 mo).
- 1 occlusal trauma (resolved after adjustment at 6 mo).
- 1 veneer chip at 4-year follow-up (polished; restoration retained).
- No framework fractures.

**SEM failure mode**:
- APA: mixed cohesive/adhesive; thin cement layers with delamination toward zirconia surface.
- NAC: predominantly adhesive at NAC–zirconia interface; exposed NAC residues and zirconia grains.

**Literature benchmark**: Kern 2025 (15-year cohort, n=310): 92.4% at 10 years → 82.6% at 15 years. The present 8-year RCT (88.8% overall at mean 5 years) sits within this trajectory and confirms mid-term stability once early debonding risk is overcome.
