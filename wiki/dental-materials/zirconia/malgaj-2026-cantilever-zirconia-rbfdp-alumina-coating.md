## Summary

This prospective randomized controlled clinical trial evaluated whether a **nanostructured alumina coating (NAC)** — a non-invasive zirconia surface modification — can replace conventional **airborne-particle abrasion (APA)** as the bonding pretreatment for cantilever zirconia resin-bonded fixed dental prostheses (RBFDPs). Fifty-seven patients needing replacement of a maxillary or mandibular incisor received 64 cantilever RBFDPs, randomized to APA (50-μm alumina air abrasion) or NAC. Over a mean of 61.4 months (up to 97.4 months), retention was statistically indistinguishable between groups (NAC 88.4% vs APA 89.1%, p=0.99) and overall prosthesis survival was 100%. The clinical bottom line: NAC offers a minimally invasive pretreatment that matches APA durability, which is particularly attractive for damage-prone translucent zirconia where abrasion-induced surface flaws are a liability.

## Key Contributions

- Rare **long-term clinical RCT** evidence (not in-vitro) for zirconia surface conditioning, with a mean follow-up beyond 5 years.
- Establishes **clinical equivalence of NAC and APA** for cantilever RBFDP retention (88.4% vs 89.1%, p=0.99).
- Demonstrates a **minimally invasive alternative to sandblasting** — relevant when APA's mechanical aggressiveness threatens translucent zirconia integrity.
- SEM analysis localizes NAC failures to the NAC–zirconia interface, identifying the coating's adhesion as the optimization target.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Prospective, randomized, controlled clinical trial.
- **Participants**: 57 patients requiring replacement of a maxillary or mandibular incisor; 64 cantilever zirconia RBFDPs placed.
- **Randomization**: APA (n=32) — air-abraded with 50-μm alumina; NAC (n=32) — nanostructured alumina coating. Further classified by pontic position.
- **Analysis**: Retention and survival via Kaplan-Meier and log-rank tests; patient-specific factors via Mann-Whitney U and Fisher's exact tests (α=0.05). Debonded surfaces examined under SEM.

## Results

- Mean observation period: 61.4 ± 22.1 months (range 8.3–97.4 mo).
