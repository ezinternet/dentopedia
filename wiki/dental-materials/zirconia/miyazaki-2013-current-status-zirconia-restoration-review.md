## Summary
Comprehensive narrative review of zirconia restorations from five Japanese universities. Covers material science (Y-TZP vs Ce-TZP), CAD/CAM workflow, veneer bonding failure mechanisms, resin cement bonding (MDP critical), surface finish and antagonist enamel wear, and clinical performance of zirconia FDPs.

## Key Contributions
- Y-TZP: best mechanical properties; Ce-TZP/Al2O3: highest fracture toughness
- Main complication: veneering porcelain chipping — no clear chemical bond mechanism identified
- Zirconia-resin bond: MDP primer + sandblasting required; aging degrades bond
- Surface finish: polished < glazed for antagonist enamel wear
- Monolithic zirconia: eliminates chipping risk; introduced as alternative to veneered
- Marginal adaptation: clinically acceptable for FDPs
