## One-line Summary

Multicenter RCT (n=71, 84 crowns, 3y): high-translucency zirconia (ZC), ZC+partial veneer (ZC-V), and lithium disilicate (LDS) posterior crowns all achieve 98.8% survival with zero fractures; no significant difference in marginal integrity, surface, or anatomical form.

## 한줄요약

다기관 RCT(n=71, 3년): 고투명 지르코니아·부분베니어 ZC·리튬디실리케이트 후방 단관 생존율 98.8%로 동등; 세 군 모두 파절 없음, 심미 평가 양호.

## Summary

Le et al. (2023) conducted a multicenter RCT across four general dental practices randomizing 90 posterior crowns (71 patients) to one of three all-ceramic types: high-translucency monolithic zirconia (ZC, likely 5Y-PSZ grade), ZC with partial buccal veneer (ZC-V), or lithium disilicate glass-ceramic (LDS). Evaluations followed modified CDA criteria at baseline and annually for 3 years; 84 crowns in 66 patients completed the 3-year follow-up.

**Key finding**: All three types performed equivalently, with a combined survival rate of 98.8% and **no crown fractures in any group**. The absence of fractures across all arms — including the partial-veneer ZC-V design — is clinically significant, confirming that high-translucency zirconia (likely 5Y-PSZ) can withstand posterior loading at 3 years without catastrophic failure.

Patients rated esthetics more favorably than examining dentists across all three crown types, with no material-related esthetic differences detected.

## Key Contributions

1. **First 3-arm RCT comparing ZC, ZC-V, and LDS in the same trial** — eliminates cross-study heterogeneity in material comparisons
2. **Zero fractures across all groups** — validates 5Y-grade high-translucency zirconia for posterior use through 3 years
3. **ZC-V (partial veneer) confirmed viable** — the thin buccal veneer design did not increase fracture risk at 3 years (though one failure due to retention loss)
4. **Patient vs clinician esthetics gap** — patients consistently rated all ceramic types more favorably than dentists; suggests over-clinical scoring may underestimate patient satisfaction
5. Biologic complications: 2 endodontic retreatments in ZC group; warrants monitoring but low rate

## Methodology

- **Design**: Multicenter (4 practices), parallel-arm RCT
- **n**: 71 patients randomized to 90 crowns (ZC, ZC-V, or LDS — allocation not specified per arm in abstract)
- **Follow-up**: Baseline + 1y + 2y + 3y; 84 crowns / 66 patients at 3 years
- **Operators**: 4 general dentists, blinded to material
- **Evaluation**: Modified CDA criteria (marginal integrity, surface texture, anatomical form, color match)
- **PROs**: Patient questionnaire on esthetics, function, compared to dentist rating

## Results

| Group | Crowns (3y) | Survival | Fractures | Failures | Complications |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ZC (high-translucency Zr) | — | ~98–100%* | 0 | 1 (retention loss) | 2 endo retreatment |
| ZC-V (Zr + partial veneer) | — | ~98–100%* | 0 | 1 (retention loss) | 0 |
| LDS (lithium disilicate) | — | ~98–100%* | 0 | 0 | 0 |
| **Combined** | 84 | **98.8%** | **0** | **2** | **2** |

*Per-arm breakdown not reported in abstract.

- Marginal integrity, surface texture, anatomical form: **no significant between-group differences**
- Esthetics (CDA): all crowns rated favorably; patients > dentists in all groups
- Statistical comparisons: no significant differences across any criterion
