## Summary
Five-year retrospective cohort study comparing clinical performance and survival of zirconia (ZrO₂) single crowns vs zirconia FDPs. Conducted at Romanian university dental centers (Timișoara and Arad). Real-world clinical data assessing: survival rate, technical complications (chipping, fracture), biological complications, and marginal adaptation.

Both ZrO₂ single crowns and FDPs showed high 5-year survival rates. FDPs had a higher rate of veneering ceramic chipping compared to single crowns. Marginal adaptation remained acceptable for both restoration types over 5 years. Biological complications (secondary caries, peri-implant/periodontal) were low.

## Key Contributions
- Confirms high 5-year survival for ZrO₂ restorations in real-world clinical settings
- FDPs: higher chipping incidence than single crowns — consistent with bench data on stress distribution
- Marginal adaptation: clinically acceptable at 5 years for both types
- Supports zirconia as a viable metal-free alternative for both single and multi-unit prosthodontics

## Methodology
- **Design**: Retrospective cohort
- **Follow-up**: 5 years
- **Groups**: ZrO₂ single crowns vs ZrO₂ FDPs
- **Outcomes**: Survival, technical complications (chipping/fracture), biological complications, marginal adaptation
- **Setting**: University of Medicine and Pharmacy Timișoara + Vasile Goldiș University of Arad, Romania

## Results
- ZrO₂ single crowns: high 5-year survival (>90%)
- ZrO₂ FDPs: high survival; chipping more frequent than single crowns
- Marginal adaptation: acceptable at 5 years in both groups
- Biological complications: low in both groups
