## One-line Summary
Prospective (J Esthet Restor Dent 2025): 20 zirconia endocrowns (glazed vs polished) on mandibular first molars at 2 years — enamel wear 0.422 mm (polished) vs 0.491 mm (glazed), NS (p=0.108); 100% survival; both finishing methods clinically acceptable.

## Summary
2-year prospective clinical study comparing glazed vs polished monolithic zirconia endocrowns for antagonist enamel wear. Intraoral scanner + Geomagic software used for digital wear measurement. Both finishing methods produce clinically acceptable enamel wear with no significant difference.

## Key Contributions
- 20 endocrowns; CAD/CAM zirconia; endodontically treated lower first molars
- ZG (glazed, n=10) vs ZP (adjusted+polished, n=10)
- 100% survival at 2 years — no failures
- Enamel wear ZP: 0.422±0.130mm vs ZG: 0.491±0.105mm (NS, p=0.108)
- Digital measurement with intraoral scanner at 6, 12, 18, 24 months
- Clinical significance: glazing or chairside polishing both acceptable for endocrowns

## Note on In Vitro Discrepancy
In vitro studies (Janyavula, Park, Lawson) consistently show polished < glazed. This 2yr clinical study shows NO significant difference — possibly due to in vivo glaze wear smoothing out over time.
