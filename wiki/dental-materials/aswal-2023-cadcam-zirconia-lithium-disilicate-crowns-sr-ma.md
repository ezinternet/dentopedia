## One-line Summary
SR+MA of CAD/CAM (zirconia and lithium disilicate) vs conventional PFM crowns and FDPs (Cureus 2023, 13 studies, 1,598 restorations): esthetic complications OR 16.88 significantly higher for CAD/CAM; biological and technical complications not significantly different.

## 한줄요약
CAD/CAM(지르코니아·리튬 디실리케이트) vs 전통 도재-금속관(Porcelain-Fused-to-Metal, PFM) SR+MA (Cureus 2023): 13편 1,598수복물; 심미합병증 OR 16.88로 CAD/CAM 유의하게 높음; 생물학적·기술적 합병증은 비유의.

## Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis (13 studies, 1,598 restorations, 1,161 patients; mean follow-up 3.6 years) comparing CAD/CAM (zirconia and lithium disilicate) to conventional metal-ceramic (PFM) full crowns and FPDs. Primary outcome: biological, technical, and esthetic complications. Meta-analysis using RevMan5 random-effects model.

Biological complications (OR 1.17, 95% CI 0.64–2.17) and technical complications (OR 1.14, 95% CI 0.86–1.52) were not significantly different. Esthetic complications (color mismatch, surface roughness) were significantly higher in CAD/CAM restorations: OR 16.88 (95% CI 7.59–37.56, p<0.00001). Survival and success rates were comparable between CAD/CAM and conventional.

## Key Contributions
- Esthetic complications are the principal disadvantage of CAD/CAM restorations vs PFM: color mismatch and surface roughness after adjustment are predominant
- Biological and technical complication rates: statistically equivalent between CAD/CAM and PFM
- Survival rates: comparable at mean 3.6 years follow-up
- Esthetic complication definition varied across studies (patient-reported vs clinician-rated) — limits interpretation

## Methodology
- **Design**: SR+MA (PICOS); Cochrane RoB for RCTs; Newcastle-Ottawa for cohorts
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane, Embase, Wiley Online Library
- **n**: 13 studies; 1,598 restorations; 1,161 patients; mean follow-up 3.6 years (range 1–9.3)
- **Meta-analysis**: RevMan5; random-effects model
