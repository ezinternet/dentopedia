## Summary
Systematic review without meta-analysis comparing longevity of amalgam and composite resin restorations in permanent posterior teeth. Databases: PubMed, Cochrane, Google Scholar (1990–2024). Inclusion: clinical trials and observational studies with ≥12 months follow-up; posterior permanent teeth; both materials compared. Quality assessment by CASP. No meta-analysis performed due to study heterogeneity.

Amalgam demonstrates median survival time >16 years vs composite ~11 years in historical literature. Recent studies (post-2015) show a narrowing gap as composite technique and materials have improved. Secondary caries and marginal discoloration are primary failure modes for composite; fracture is primary for amalgam. High caries-risk patients show significantly higher composite failure risk.

## Key Contributions
- Amalgam median survival >16 years; composite ~11 years historically — amalgam still has longevity advantage in aggregate
- Post-2015 composite data shows improvement; narrowing of the gap with better bonding and materials
- High caries-risk patients: composite significantly worse — patient risk stratification is essential in material selection
- Single-surface restorations: survival rates comparable between materials

## Methodology
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane, Google Scholar (1990–2024)
- **Inclusion**: Clinical trials + observational studies; ≥12 months follow-up; permanent posterior teeth; both materials
- **Quality**: CASP; no meta-analysis due to heterogeneity
- **Study type**: Qualitative systematic review
