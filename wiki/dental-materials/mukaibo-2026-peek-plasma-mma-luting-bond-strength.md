## One-line Summary

In-vitro shear-bond-strength study (CAD-CAM PEEK, 244 specimens, n=10/group, two MMA-based luting systems incl. Super-Bond): alumina airborne-particle abrasion + non-thermal air plasma gives superior or comparable SBS to single treatments and markedly improves durability after 20,000 thermocycles; abrasion drives durability for Super-Bond, plasma adds wettability/durability for Beautylink (untreated fully debonded after aging).

## 한줄요약

In-vitro 전단결합강도 연구(CAD-CAM PEEK 244개, 군당 n=10, MMA계 luting 2종 — Super-Bond 포함): 알루미나 샌드블라스팅(AB) + 비열 공기 플라즈마(PT) 병용이 단일 처리보다 우월/동등한 결합강도와 2만회 열순환 후 향상된 내구성을 보임 — Super-Bond는 AB가 내구성의 주역, Beautylink는 플라즈마가 젖음성·내구성에 기여(미처리군은 노화 후 완전 탈락).

## Summary

PEEK is chemically inert and lacks reactive functional groups, so bonding luting cements to it is a recurring clinical failure mode (debonding of PEEK crowns/bridges/frameworks). This in-vitro study tested whether stacking two surface treatments — **alumina airborne-particle abrasion (AB, mechanical)** then **air-atmosphere non-thermal plasma (PT, chemical)** — improves bonding of **MMA-based luting systems** to dental CAD-CAM PEEK, evaluated both fresh and after aggressive thermocycling aging (20,000 cycles).

Two MMA-based luting systems were used: **Super-Bond (SB)**, a self-cure MMA-based resin cement (the classic 4-META/MMA-TBB chemistry) used with an MMA-containing primer, and **Beautylink (BL)**, a dual-cure composite-based resin cement with an MMA-containing primer. **Super-Bond is therefore explicitly one of the two MMA luting agents tested.** The combined AB + PT treatment produced the highest roughness and lowest contact angles where measurable, and gave superior or comparable shear bond strength with markedly better durability — supporting "sandblast then plasma, then MMA cement" as a chairside protocol for PEEK.

## Key Contributions

- **Super-Bond confirmed as an MMA-based luting agent for PEEK.** SB (self-cure, 4-META/MMA-TBB) + MMA-containing primer is one of the two systems; MMA-based cements are framed as outperforming composite-based resin cements on PEEK.
- **AB + PT is complementary, not redundant.** Abrasion supplies micromechanical interlocking (roughness ↑, residual alumina); plasma supplies surface energy / polar groups (–C=O, –COOH, –OH) — roughness unchanged by plasma, wettability improved.
- **Durability is where the combination pays off.** After 20,000 thermocycles, AB + PT outperformed single treatments; the untreated BL group fully debonded.
- **System-specific mechanism**: SB durability is AB-driven (plasma alone fails after aging); BL benefits more from plasma (wettability + post-aging strength).

## Methodology

- **244 CAD-CAM PEEK plates** (2 mm, polished #600), 4 groups n=61: **UT / PT / AB / AB+PT**.
- **Characterization**: SEM–EDX (15 kV), profilometry (Ra, n=10), contact angle of primers (sessile drop, n=10).
- **SBS**: Teflon-tube (ID 5 mm) bonding area; BL light-cured, SB self-cured; **initial** = 24 h/37 °C water; **aged** = 20,000 thermocycles 5–55 °C (60 s dwell). Universal testing machine, 1.0 mm/min, n=10/group. Failure modes ×30 (adhesive / cohesive / mixed).
- **Stats**: R 4.5.2; one-way ANOVA + Tukey (roughness, contact angle); two-way ANOVA treatment × aging + Tukey (SBS); p < 0.05.

## Results

> Per-group MPa values are in the article's figures/tables (not captured in the JATS full text); statistical findings below are verbatim.

**Roughness (Ra)**: PT ≈ UT (no significant change); AB significantly ↑; AB+PT significantly ↑ vs UT but not different from AB. Plasma does not alter abraded roughness.

**Wettability**: *BL primer* — PT, AB each lowered contact angle vs UT; **AB+PT lower than either alone**. *SB primer* — contact angle unmeasurable (immediate spreading, no stable droplet).

**SBS — BL** (treatment p<0.001 ηp²=0.400; aging p=0.003; interaction p=0.022): initial — no group differences; **aged — AB+PT significantly higher than PT and AB; UT completely debonded**.

**SBS — SB (Super-Bond)** (treatment p<0.001 ηp²=0.595; aging p<0.001 ηp²=0.466; interaction p=0.012): initial — AB+PT > UT; **aged — AB and AB+PT > UT; PT did not match AB**. → AB is the dominant durability driver for SB.

**Failure modes**: predominantly adhesive in all groups; a few mixed failures only in AB+PT (1 BL initial, 1 SB initial, 2 BL aged), indicating local approach to cohesive strength. Interface remains the "weakest link."

**Clinical note**: both treatments are simple, fast, device-available, and safer than acid etching; because plasma-induced surface energy decays with time, plasma should be done chairside immediately before luting.
