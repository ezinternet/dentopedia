## 한줄요약

## Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis of clinical performance of CAD/CAM all-ceramic tooth-supported fixed dental prostheses (FDPs). Databases: PubMed, Cochrane, Embase, Wiley Online Library. Included RCTs and cohort studies. Assessed survival rate, biological complications, and technical complications for CAD/CAM ceramics (zirconia, lithium disilicate, feldspathic) vs metal-ceramic (PFM) FDPs.

Five-year survival rates for all-ceramic FDPs generally >90% for single crowns. Zirconia framework fracture is rare; chipping of veneering ceramic remains the predominant technical complication. Lithium disilicate shows higher fracture risk for long-span FPDs. Biological complications comparable to metal-ceramic. Technical complications higher in all-ceramic FDPs (mainly chipping) than metal-ceramic.

## Key Contributions
- Zirconia FDP framework: very high strength — fracture risk low; veneering porcelain chipping remains unresolved
- Lithium disilicate: preferred material for single crowns; not recommended for extended FPDs (>3 units)
- CAD/CAM all-ceramic achieves survival rates comparable to PFM for short-span FDPs
- Provides evidence basis for material selection in fixed prosthodontics

## Methodology
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane, Embase, Wiley Online Library
- **Study types**: RCTs + cohort studies
- **Quality**: Cochrane RoB (RCTs); Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (cohorts)
- **Meta-analysis**: Random-effects; outcomes: survival, biological complications, technical complications

## Results
- 5-year crown survival: generally >90%
- Zirconia FPD veneering chipping: most frequent technical complication
- Lithium disilicate long-span FPDs: fracture risk significantly higher vs single crowns
- Biological complications: comparable to PFM controls
