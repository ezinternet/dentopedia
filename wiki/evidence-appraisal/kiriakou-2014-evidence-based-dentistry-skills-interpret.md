## One-line Summary
Dentistry-specific evidence-based dentistry (EBD) primer (Progress in Orthodontics 2014) anchored on the three-domain ADA definition (research evidence + clinician expertise + patient values), laying out the 5-step EBD workflow (focused question → identify/synthesize → appraise → apply → audit) and chairside critical-appraisal checklists for RCTs and systematic reviews.

## Summary
Of the nine evidence-appraisal papers in this wiki, this is the only one written specifically for dentists. The others (Shin IH 2009, Shin WJ 2015, Flechner 2011, Barraclough 2011, Monaghan 2021, Nam 2012, D'Arrigo 2024, anonymous SR worksheet) cover the same statistical machinery from a general-medicine angle. Kiriakou is what you read first if you are anchoring the toolkit to a dental practice.

## Key Contributions
- Anchors EBD on the three-domain definition (research evidence + clinician expertise + patient values), which is the framing most consistent with how individual treatment decisions actually get made in a private clinic.
- Provides the 5-step EBD workflow as an operational sequence rather than an abstract hierarchy: focused question → identify/synthesize evidence → appraise validity & applicability → apply → audit.
- Lays out the appraisal questions for both RCTs and SRs in the form a clinician can use at chairside or before a journal-club meeting.

## Methodology
- Narrative review. Builds on ADA's EBD definition.
- Walks through the standard validity criteria for RCTs (randomization, allocation concealment, blinding, ITT analysis, follow-up completeness) and SRs (a priori PICO, search comprehensiveness, inclusion/exclusion, quality assessment, heterogeneity, publication bias).

## Results
No empirical results; this is an educational synthesis.

## Clinical Applicability
- The "appraise before apply" step is the practical bottleneck — most clinicians skip from "search" to "apply" without reading the methods section. Kiriakou's appraisal questions are the minimum filter.
- For our wiki: every SR+MA in `implants/`, `bone-regeneration/`, `endodontics/`, etc., should be cross-checked against the SR appraisal checklist here before its claims drive a clinical decision.

## Caveats
- Pre-PRISMA 2020 / pre-GRADE-mainstream version. Modern SR appraisal also requires GRADE certainty assessment (low/moderate/high) which this paper does not yet emphasize.
- Pre-network-meta-analysis era — does not cover ranking probabilities, SUCRA, or transitivity assumption.
