## One-line Summary
Critical methodological appraisal (Korea University × Mayo Clinic, J Korean Dent Assoc 2015) of the four retrievable Korean clinical trials (1982, 1988, 1991, 1994) cited as evidence for Insadol (titrated unsaponifiable Zea mays L. fraction) in periodontal disease: none provide compelling evidence of effectiveness due to randomization failure, absent sample-size rationale, inappropriate statistical tests, and unclear blinding, with no new trial since 1994 despite the drug's 66.8% Korean market share.

## Summary
This 2015 critical appraisal — co-authored by Korea University Institute of Clinical Dental Research (Choi, Lee, Shin) and Mayo Clinic (Steven E. Eckert) — audits the four retrievable Korean clinical trials (1982, 1988, 1991, 1994) that the Insadol manufacturer cites as evidence of clinical effectiveness for periodontal disease. Despite Insadol holding **66.8% market share** of the Korean periodontal-drug market in 2011, the authors conclude that **none of the four trials provide compelling evidence** of clinical effectiveness for the titrated unsaponifiable Zea mays L. fraction, whether as monotherapy or as adjunct to standard periodontal care. The flaws span the entire methodology pipeline: undefined target population (age ranges 20–66 across trials), no rationale for sample size, no pathophysiological rationale for follow-up duration (1–4 months), randomization failure in 1 of 4 trials and unchecked balance in 3 of 4, unclear blinding in 2 of 4, and inappropriate statistical tests (wrong choice of t-test type, ANOVA on n = 10 per arm without normality verification, missing multiple-comparison correction) in 3 of 4 trials. The paper also flags that no new Insadol trial has been performed since 1994 (a 20-year evidence gap at time of writing).

## Key Contributions
- **First independent (Korea Univ × Mayo Clinic) methodological audit** of Insadol's clinical evidence base — published in the official Korean Dental Association journal, intended for the Korean general dentist audience.
- Provides a **per-trial tabulation** of methodological failures, making the critique concrete and reproducible.
- Identifies the **20-year clinical-trial gap** (no new Insadol trial since 1994 as of 2015) — a damning fact in context of the drug's market dominance.
- Frames the critique constructively: not "drug is ineffective" but "evidence base is insufficient" — and articulates what a proper future RCT would require (sample-size calculation, balance check, blinding, appropriate tests).
- Educational value: illustrates concrete biostatistical principles (paired vs unpaired t-test, parametric vs non-parametric for small n, multiple-comparison correction) using a real Korean clinical-pharmacology example.

## Methodology
- **Design**: Critical appraisal / methodological audit of published Korean clinical trials of Insadol (옥수수 불검화 정량추출물 = titrated unsaponifiable Zea mays L. fraction)
- **Source identification**: manufacturer's website (www.insadol.co.kr, accessed 1 March 2014) listed 5 trials; 4 were retrievable as full text in Korean or English (1982, 1988, 1991, 1994); 1 (Polish-language) was excluded
- **Extracted variables** (9):
  1. Year of publication
  2. Subject age range
  3. Sample size
  4. Follow-up period
  5. Co-administration with periodontal treatment
  6. Randomization performed (yes/no)
  7. Post-randomization balance check (yes/no/result)
  8. Blinded outcome measurement (yes/no/unclear)
  9. Type of statistical test
- **No quantitative re-analysis**; narrative critical appraisal only

## Results

### Per-trial methodological audit

| Year | n / Design | Follow-up | Co-treatment | Randomization | Balance check | Blinding | Stat test | Major flaw(s) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1982** | Test 20, Control 10 (placebo) | 3 mo | None | Performed but **failed** | Done — imbalance found (test had more severe disease) | Not stated | Inadequate | Baseline imbalance → invalid between-group comparison |
| **1988** | 4 arms × 10 (Insadol, Insadol+scaling, OHI+scaling, placebo) | 1 mo | Variable across arms | Performed | **Not checked** | Not stated | ANOVA (F-stat) on n=10/arm | No normality verification (Kruskal-Wallis would be more appropriate); no multiple-comparison correction; can't isolate which co-treatment drives any effect |
| **1991** | 87 patients, 2,056 surfaces, 6 arms (Insadol/placebo × OHI / OHI+scaling / +flap surgery) | 1 mo | Multiple combinations | Unclear | Not addressed | Done | t-test (paired vs unpaired unspecified) | Wrong unit of analysis (surface vs patient); randomization to combination arms unclear; no between-combo comparison |
| **1994** | Test + control, periodontal flap surgery patients | 2 mo | Periodontal flap surgery | Unclear | **Not checked** | Not stated | Student t-test (should be paired for within-group) | Only within-group analysis; no direct between-group comparison of change scores |

### Aggregate methodological findings
- **Target population**: age ranges 20–66 across trials — no consistent population, generalization unclear
- **Sample-size rationale**: **absent in all 4 trials**
- **Follow-up duration**: 1–4 months, no pathophysiological justification (periodontal healing has known latent windows; arbitrary choice biases ability to detect effect)
- **Randomization performed**: 4/4
- **Balance check performed**: 1/4 (and that one failed)
- **Blinded outcome measurement**: clear in 2/4, unclear in 2/4
- **Appropriate statistical test**: 1/4 (inappropriate in 3/4)
- **New Insadol trials since 1994**: 0 (20-year gap)

### Bottom-line conclusion
The four available clinical trials have **not demonstrated compelling evidence of therapeutic effectiveness** of systemic titrated extract of Zea mays L. unsaponifiable fraction for periodontal disease, with or without contemporary periodontal treatment.

## Clinical Implications
1. **Evidence-based prescribing**: Insadol's strong market position in Korea is not matched by strong clinical evidence. When patients ask whether they should take Insadol, this is the source to cite for the honest answer: "the existing trials have important methodological flaws and the most recent one is from 1994."
2. **Patient counseling**: Avoid implying clinical effectiveness; frame as a low-cost, low-risk supplement with **mechanistic plausibility** (see [[drug/kim-2018-magnoliae-cortex-zea-mays-modulate|Kim 2018 in vitro]] for the active-ingredient mechanism) but **weak clinical evidence**.
3. **Note on combination products**: subsequent research (Kim 2018 in vitro, Kim 2024 canine) has tested **Magnoliae cortex + Zea mays L. combination**, not the registered Insadol monotherapy. Therefore that newer animal/mechanistic literature **does not retroactively validate** the existing Insadol formulation; it suggests that a different combination might be more potent.
4. **Methodological lessons**:
   - Randomization without balance check is incomplete — always verify with a table-1 comparison
   - Sample-size rationale (power calculation) must be pre-specified — its absence is a CONSORT violation
   - For small n (~10/arm), use rank-based tests (Wilcoxon, Mann-Whitney, Kruskal-Wallis), not parametric tests
   - For pre/post measurements in the same subjects, use paired tests
   - For multiple comparisons across ≥3 arms, apply Bonferroni or Tukey-HSD correction after a significant omnibus test
5. **Pragmatic clinical stance**: Insadol is widely tolerated and inexpensive. Patients who insist on taking it can do so without harm, but it should not displace scaling, root planing, and home plaque control. Do not promise periodontal benefit beyond mild symptom relief.
6. **Comparison to other Korean periodontal supplement evidence**: a parallel drug class — CELC (vitamin C + vitamin E + lysozyme + carbazochrome, brand IGATAN F) — was tested in a properly designed multicenter RCT ([[drug/hong-2019-randomized-double-blind-placebo-controlled-multicenter|Hong 2019]]) and showed a small but statistically significant effect on gingival index only. So even with rigorous methodology, the achievable effect of these adjunct supplements appears small and confined to gingivitis indices.
