## 한줄요약
2009 대한간학회 추계학술대회 초청강의 (한국어): 메타분석의 정의·필요성·전제조건·효과크기 산정·이질성 검정·출판편향까지 임상의 비판적 해석을 위한 입문 핸드아웃.

## Summary
A Korean-language tutorial that grounds meta-analysis (MA) in the prior step of a well-conducted systematic review (SR), then walks through how clinicians should read a forest plot. It is conceptually a precursor to Shin WJ 2015 in this wiki — same language, similar coverage, earlier and more lecture-style.

## Key Contributions
- States the precondition: MA is only meaningful when the upstream SR has identified high-quality, comparable studies via PICO and reproducible search.
- Distinguishes the two main effect-size pathways: continuous outcomes (mean, SD, n → standardized mean difference) vs. categorical outcomes (OR / RR / rate difference).
- Names the three diagnostics every clinician should look for: homogeneity (Cochran Q), publication bias (funnel plot, plus language/database bias), and effect-size direction consistency across studies.

## Methodology
- Educational, not empirical. Uses 17-trial hypothetical with mixed direction and significance to motivate why naive vote-counting fails and pooled estimates are needed.
- Demonstrates 5-study pooling table (n, mean, SD, effect-size direction, d, SD of d).

## Results
- No original results. The take-home claim is that p-value pooling alone is insufficient — effect size pooling with weights is the correct frame.

## Clinical Applicability
- For a Korean-speaking clinician new to MA, this is a low-cost orientation before opening any Cochrane review or PROSPERO-registered SR.
- Apply with caution: predates PRISMA 2020, GRADE, network meta-analysis, and the now-standard I² statistic conventions — these are covered better in Shin WJ 2015 and D'Arrigo 2024 in this wiki.
