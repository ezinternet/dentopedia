## One-line Summary
Korean-language systematic review / meta-analysis (SR/MA) methodology primer (Hanyang Med Rev 2015) covering the full pipeline: search/selection/quality assessment (Cochrane Risk of Bias), effect-size pooling, heterogeneity (I² 25/50/75 heuristic, Cochran Q), fixed- vs random-effects model choice, and publication bias (funnel plot, Egger's test).

## Summary
The most complete Korean-language SR/MA methodology paper in this wiki. Shin IH 2009 is the lecture-style precursor; this is the journal-paper-level treatment. If you read only one Korean methodology source before tackling our Cochrane and PROSPERO-registered SRs, this is it.

## Key Contributions
- SR vs. MA distinction is sharp and operational: SR can exist without MA when heterogeneity is too high to pool.
- I² heuristic (25/50/75 low/moderate/high) gives a defensible model-choice rule between fixed and random effects.
- Funnel plot + Egger's test as the standard publication bias diagnostic; flags small-study effects.

## Methodology
- Narrative methodological review.
- Maps the full SR/MA pipeline including quality assessment via Cochrane Risk of Bias tool.
- Explains pooling weights as inverse of within-study variance (fixed effects) vs. within + between-study variance (random effects).

## Clinical Applicability
- When reading an SR+MA in our `implants/`, `bone-regeneration/`, `sinus-lift/`, etc. folders: first check the I². If I² > 75%, the pooled effect estimate is borderline meaningless — read the subgroup analyses instead.
- Funnel plot inspection is a 30-second check that catches publication bias. Look for asymmetry, especially missing small studies on the "negative effect" side.

## Caveats
- Pre-PRISMA 2020 — uses 2009 reporting items.
- Does not cover network meta-analysis (NMA), which now appears in our dental SR+NMA pages (e.g., Azadi 2025, Camps-Font 2024).
- Does not cover GRADE certainty assessment, which D'Arrigo 2024 implicitly assumes.
