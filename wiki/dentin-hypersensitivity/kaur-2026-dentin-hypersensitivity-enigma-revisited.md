## Summary

This 2026 narrative review from the Journal of Conservative Dentistry and Endodontics integrates current understanding of dentin hypersensitivity (상아질 과민증, DH) from structural biology through emerging biomaterial science. The review covers: (1) epidemiology and etiology, (2) four mechanistic theories, (3) a systematic diagnostic exclusion process, (4) tiered management from home care through surgical intervention, and (5) emerging biomimetic and nanotechnology approaches. The Triple-C recall framework (Continue/Change/Cease) is proposed for long-term maintenance. No single therapy provides permanent relief; a stepwise, evidence-based approach is emphasized.

**Key clinical bottom line**: DH is a diagnosis of exclusion. Management should follow a step-up strategy — initiate with desensitizing toothpastes/rinses, escalate to professional in-office agents (fluoride varnish, oxalates, Gluma), then restorative or periodontal surgery if needed. Endodontic therapy is the last resort. Neuroplastic sensitization explains persistent cases despite adequate tubule occlusion.

## Key Contributions

1. **Four-theory mechanistic framework** integrating hydrodynamic (primary), direct innervation, neuroplasticity/peripheral sensitization, and odontoblast mechanosensory concepts — essential for understanding why tubule-occlusion alone sometimes fails.
2. **Neuroplasticity emphasis**: Upregulation of TRP receptors (TRPV1), Nav channels, substance P, CGRP, and leukotriene B4 in persistent DH — justifies neural-modulating agents (potassium nitrate) alongside tubule-occluding strategies.
3. **Odontoblast as sensory cell**: TRP family ion channels, voltage-gated Na channels, and mechanosensitive K/Ca channels expressed on odontoblasts — emerging mechanosensory role.
4. **Diagnostic exclusion checklist**: Caries, cracked tooth syndrome, reversible/irreversible pulpitis, defective restorations, postoperative sensitivity must all be excluded.
5. **Emerging biomimetic landscape**: Nano-hydroxyapatite, CPP-ACP, self-assembling peptides (P11-4 / SAPM), mesoporous silica nanoparticles, potassium-doped bioactive glass, and experimental CalBots summarized with evidence levels.
6. **Triple-C recall framework** for systematic post-treatment reassessment and protocol adjustment.

- **Type**: Narrative review (not systematic)
- **Search**: Electronic search of major biomedical databases + dental journals + hand-searching reference lists.

## Results

### Epidemiology
- Prevalence: 1%–98% across populations; peaks 3rd–4th decade; higher in women.
- Most affected: canines > first premolars > incisors; buccal cervical surfaces dominant; more prevalent on left side (brushing pattern effect).

### Etiology — Two Preconditions Required
1. Dentin exposure (gingival recession, tooth wear — erosion/abrasion/attrition, enamel defects)
2. Dentinal tubule patency (disrupted smear layer allowing fluid movement)

- Gingival recession (periodontal disease, mechanical trauma) is the leading cause.
- Bleaching-related sensitivity: peroxide diffusion → transient pulpal inflammation + reversible neural sensitization (distinct from classical DH — not sustained tubule patency).

### Mechanism: Four Theories

| Theory | Key Concept | Nerve Type Involved |
|---|---|---|
| 수역학설 (Hydrodynamic) | Fluid displacement in tubules → mechanosensitive nerve activation | Aδ fibers (pulpal) |
| 직접 신경지배설 (Direct Innervation) | Aδ fibers extend toward DEJ; activated by stimuli | Aδ (fast, sharp); C fibers (dull, deep) |
| 신경가소성 (Neuroplasticity) | Lowered nociceptor threshold; TRP/Nav upregulation; CGRP, substance P release | Aδ + C fibers |
| 상아모세포 기계감각 (Odontoblast Mechanosensory) | Odontoblasts as primary sensory cells via TRP/Nav/K/Ca channels | Indirect (transducer role) |

### Diagnosis (Exclusion Process)
1. Careful history taking (stimulus characterization, onset, localization)
2. Controlled clinical stimulation: thermal (cold water/air), evaporative (air-blast), tactile (probe), osmotic (hypertonic sucrose)
3. Pain assessment (VAS/NRS)
4. Exclusion of: pulpal pathology (pulp testing), periodontal pathology, defective restorations, structural defects (cracks), caries

**Hypersensitive dentin characteristics on microscopy**: tubule density ~2× non-sensitive sites; increased tubule diameter; thin/disrupted smear layer.

### Management — Tiered Algorithm

**Home (at-home) Management**
- Desensitizing toothpastes: two mechanisms:
  - Tubule occlusion: stannous fluoride, strontium salts, calcium phosphates, bioactive glass (NovaMin/CSP), arginine
  - Neural modulation: potassium nitrate
- Evidence-superior agents: potassium nitrate, stannous fluoride, NovaMin (CSP), arginine
- RDA ≤ 250 (critical to prevent additional dentin wear)
- Mouth rinses (KNO₃, aluminum lactate, arginine, CSP): comparable to toothpastes; effect within 4–6 weeks
- Herbal agents (propolis, rhubarb, spinach, Hekla lava): supplementary only, not evidence-based substitutes

**In-Office Management**
- **Fluoride varnish** (5% NaF, e.g., Duraphat): fluorapatite formation + partial tubule sealing
- **Oxalate salts**: insoluble calcium oxalate crystals in tubules; 1.5%–3% potassium oxalate strips — rapid sustained relief
- **Glutaraldehyde + HEMA (Gluma)**: protein coagulation → multilayer protein plugs — immediate + long-lasting
- **Ion precipitation method**: Ca²⁺, PO₄³⁻, F⁻, oxalate ions form insoluble intratubular precipitates → reduce dentin permeability
- **Resin sealants / dentin bonding systems**: hybrid layer / micromechanical lock; GIC/RMGIC for NCCL with structural component
- **Laser therapy**:
  - High-power (Nd:YAG 1064 nm, Er,Cr:YSGG 2780 nm): coagulate proteins / melt dentinal walls — superior immediate reduction
  - Low-power diode (685–980 nm): modulate C-fiber neural transmission, promote secondary dentin — more gradual
  - Combination laser + topical: superior short-term; long-term superiority not conclusively established
  - Parameters: Er,Cr:YSGG (0.25–0.5 W, 20–30 s), Nd:YAG (0.1–1 W, 20–40 s), diode (0.25–0.5 W, 30–60 s)
- **Iontophoresis**: low-intensity current enhances fluoride/K⁺ penetration; limited contemporary use

**Restorative / Surgical**
- Resin composites, RMGIC: durable seal when significant hard-tissue loss present
- Connective tissue grafts / coronally advanced flaps: re-establish soft-tissue coverage for recession-associated DH
- Full-coverage restorations / veneers: tooth wear-related DH
- **Endodontic therapy**: last resort only

### Emerging Technologies

| Technology | Mechanism | Evidence Stage |
|---|---|---|
| Nano-hydroxyapatite (nHAp) | Biomimetic mineral deposition in/over tubules | Early clinical RCTs; promising |
| CPP-ACP | Ca²⁺/PO₄³⁻ release; tubule occlusion + remineralization | Established in vitro; some clinical |
| Bioactive glass (NovaMin/CSP) | Ca²⁺/PO₄³⁻ ion release; forms apatite layer | Good clinical evidence |
| Self-assembling peptides (P11-4 / SAPM) | Fibrillar scaffold → HAp nucleation → intrafibrillar remineralization | RCT initiated; limited long-term data |
| Potassium-doped bioactive glass | Dual: tubule occlusion (Ca/PO₄) + neural desensitization (K⁺) | Investigational |
| Mesoporous silica nanoparticles (CCMS, Ca-DMSN-L, P-DMSN-S) | Controlled Ca/PO₄ release; intratubular mineral deposition | Primarily in vitro |
| CalBots (magnetic calcium silicate nanobots, 387 nm) | Magnetically guided; self-assemble into tubule plugs (~500 µm depth) | Experimental |

### Recall / Maintenance — Triple-C Framework
- **Continue**: partial improvement → reinforce etiological factor control + continue regimen
- **Change**: persistent symptoms despite compliance → switch agent or escalate to professional intervention
- **Cease**: complete resolution → transition to routine preventive care with behavioral reinforcement
