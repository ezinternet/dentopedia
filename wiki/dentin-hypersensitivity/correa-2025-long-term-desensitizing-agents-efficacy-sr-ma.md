## Summary

Most dentin-hypersensitivity (DH) trials report short-term relief, but patients want durable results. This SR+MA (PRISMA; PROSPERO CRD42020210693) is the first to focus specifically on **long-term efficacy — randomized trials with ≥6-month follow-up** — and to group agents by **mechanism of action**: tubular occlusion, neural blocking, or both.

From databases searched to January 2025 (studies 1991–2024), **22 RCTs were included in the systematic review and 15 in the meta-analysis**, with pain (evaporative/cold/tactile stimuli) pooled as bias-corrected **standardized mean change (SMC)**. Several agents gave significant long-term pain reduction, but the standouts were **glutaraldehyde** (a tubule-occluding protein-coagulant, the Gluma family) and **low-level laser therapy (LLLT)**, which achieved the **largest effect sizes**. By contrast, **adhesive/resin systems and calcium-phosphate–based agents (e.g., CPP-ACP) did NOT show statistically significant long-term benefit** — a caution that promising short-term or in-vitro performance does not guarantee durability.

The clinical takeaway: when long-lasting relief is the priority, glutaraldehyde-based in-office agents and LLLT are the best-supported options; adhesives and calcium-phosphate agents should be chosen cautiously pending stronger long-term evidence.

## Key Contributions

- **First long-term–specific (≥6 mo) DH treatment SR+MA**, organized by mechanism (tubular occlusion / neural / both).
- Produces an evidence ranking: **glutaraldehyde and LLLT = durable winners**; adhesives and calcium-phosphate = unproven long-term.
- Bridges the wiki's mechanism/diagnosis reviews and laser-only SR+MAs into an **all-agent long-term efficacy benchmark** for treatment selection.

## Methodology

- **Design**: SR + meta-analysis, PRISMA, PROSPERO CRD42020210693.
- **Databases**: PubMed/MEDLINE, Cochrane CENTRAL, Embase, Scopus, Web of Science, Lilacs/BBO; last search January 2025.
- **Inclusion**: adults with DH; **RCTs**; desensitizing-agent treatment; **≥6-month follow-up**. Studies 1991–2024.
- **Counts**: 22 in SR, **15 in meta-analysis**.
- **Outcome/analysis**: pain from evaporative, cold, or tactile stimuli; pooled as **bias-corrected standardized mean change (SMC)** with 95% CI.

## Results

- Significant long-term pain reduction with several desensitizing agents.
- **Largest effect sizes: glutaraldehyde and low-level laser therapy (LLLT).**
- **No statistically significant long-term benefit: adhesive systems and calcium-phosphate–based agents.**
- Conclusion: glutaraldehyde and LLLT are effective for long-term DH pain reduction; adhesives and calcium-phosphate agents have limited long-term evidence → cautious therapeutic selection.

*(Abstract-only ingest — per-agent SMC point estimates, CIs, and heterogeneity were not available beyond the abstract.)*
