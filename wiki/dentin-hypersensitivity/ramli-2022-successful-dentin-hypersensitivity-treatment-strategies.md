## Summary
This narrative review synthesizes the contemporary understanding of dentin hypersensitivity — built on the hydrodynamic theory of fluid movement within exposed tubules — together with diagnostic considerations and the spectrum of at-home and in-office management options. It stresses that DH affects at least one in ten adults with meaningful quality-of-life impact, and that successful, lasting relief is less about any single agent than about a concerted effort between a motivated patient and a knowledgeable clinician selecting from tubule-occluding and nerve-modulating strategies.

## Key Contributions
- Up-to-date consolidation of DH mechanism, diagnosis, and management.
- Patient–clinician partnership framed as the determinant of treatment success.

## Methodology
Narrative (non-systematic) review; thematic synthesis of mechanism, diagnosis, and treatment literature.

## Results
DH prevalence ≥1 in 10 adults with OHRQoL impact; stepwise management from at-home desensitizers to in-office occlusion/nerve-modulating agents; success contingent on adherence and accurate diagnosis.
