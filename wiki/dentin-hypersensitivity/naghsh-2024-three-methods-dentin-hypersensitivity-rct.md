## One-line Summary
RCT (20 patients / 60 teeth, 4 groups) showing 980-nm diode laser alone gave the lowest VAS at 1 month for cold and air stimuli, but between-group differences were not significant at 3 months.

## 한줄요약
RCT(20명·60치): 980 nm 다이오드 레이저 단독이 1개월 VAS 최저였으나 3개월에는 군간 유의차 없음.

## Summary
This randomised clinical trial compared a 980-nm diode laser, Gluma, their combination, and an untreated control for cervical dentin hypersensitivity, assessing VAS responses to cold and air-blast stimuli through three months. The diode laser arm produced the greatest short-term relief (lowest VAS at one month), but all active groups converged by three months with no significant difference, indicating early benefit from photothermal treatment that is not clearly sustained over the chemical comparator.

## Key Contributions
- Direct comparison of diode laser, Gluma, and combination with a true untreated control.
- Two stimulus modalities (cold spray, air syringe) across six timepoints.

## Methodology
RCT; 20 patients, 60 teeth; 4 groups (laser ×2 sessions; Gluma ×2; laser+Gluma; control). VAS to cold and air blast at baseline, 15 min, 1 week, 2 week, 1 month, 3 month. Non-parametric tests, SPSS v22, P<.05.

## Results
Significant between-group difference at 1 month (P<.05) with laser lowest; no significant between-group difference at 3 months (P>.05).
