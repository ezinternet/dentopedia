## Summary

This randomized controlled trial (Çanakkale Onsekiz Mart University, Turkey; ClinicalTrials.gov NCT06216262) compared five home-use desensitizing agents head-to-head against a standard fluoride toothpaste control for dentin hypersensitivity (DH), over an 8-week period, using three measurement instruments in parallel: DHEQ-15 (oral-health-related quality of life), the Schiff Sensitivity Scale (SSS, evaporative/air-stimulus response), and the Visual Analog Scale (VAS, subjective pain).

180 individuals (ages 18–70, DH in ≥2 non-adjacent teeth) were assessed; 164 met inclusion criteria and were randomized into six groups, and 150 (25 per group) completed the final analysis. All five test agents produced statistically significant DH improvement at weeks 4 and 8 across DHEQ-15, SSS, and VAS (p<0.005), whereas the standard fluoride control improved significantly only on SSS and VAS by week 8 (not on DHEQ-15 quality of life). The CPP-ACP group (GC Tooth Mousse, brushing plus nightly finger inunction) demonstrated the greatest reduction in scores by week 8 — DHEQ-15 56.68±17.87, VAS 6.52±1.48, SSS 2.32±0.56 — and was the most effective agent overall.

## Key Contributions

- **First head-to-head comparison of five distinct home-use desensitizing agents** (Arginine, NovaMin, Propolis, Potassium nitrate, CPP-ACP) against a standard fluoride control, rather than each agent in isolation.
- **Triple-instrument triangulation** (DHEQ-15 + SSS + VAS) at baseline, week 4, and week 8 — separating quality-of-life impact from evaporative-stimulus response and subjective pain.
- **CPP-ACP ranked most effective** by week 8 across all three scales (brushing + finger inunction protocol).
- **Quality-of-life vs raw sensitivity dissociation**: the fluoride control significantly reduced SSS/VAS but not DHEQ-15 — minimal QoL benefit from fluoride alone.
- **Fluoride is not the sole driver of efficacy**: Potassium nitrate (used with fluoride) underperformed, while fluoride-free Propolis matched it on DHEQ-15/VAS — pointing to agent mechanism over fluoride co-use.

## Methodology

- **Design**: six-arm parallel-group RCT, 8-week intervention, single independent outcome examiner (CONSORT; NCT06216262). Sample size 144 by G*Power (alpha 0.05, power 0.85, effect size 0.3287).
- **Participants**: 180 assessed → 26 excluded → 164 randomized → 150 analyzed (25 per group). 62% female, mean age 40.9±15.1. DH most common in premolars.
- **Inclusion**: ages 18–70, DH in ≥2 non-adjacent teeth. **Exclusions**: pregnancy/lactation, advanced periodontal disease, relevant chronic disease, major oral pathology, certain neuro/psychotropic medications, desensitizing toothpaste in past 3 months, periodontal treatment in past 12 months, pulpitis/caries/cracked enamel/RPD abutments.
- **Groups (active ingredient / product)**:
  - **CPP-ACP** — GC Tooth Mousse (finger inunction 3 min nightly after fluoride brushing)
  - **Arginine 8%** — Colgate Sensitive Pro-Relief (1450 ppm MFP)
  - **NovaMin** — calcium sodium phosphosilicate 5% (Sensodyne Repair & Protect)
  - **Propolis** — Eyup Sabri Tuncer Natural Propolis Extract (fluoride-free)
  - **Potassium nitrate 3%** — Sensodyne Cool Mint Mouthwash (rinse twice daily after fluoride brushing)
  - **Control** — standard fluoride toothpaste 1450 ppm (Colgate Total)
- **Outcomes**: DHEQ-15 (15–105), SSS (0–3; air 1 cm/1 s, mean of 2 most-sensitive teeth), VAS (0–10), at baseline / week 4 / week 8.
- **Statistics**: Cronbach's alpha; ANOVA/Kruskal-Wallis (between agents); Repeated Measures ANOVA (Greenhouse-Geisser)/Friedman (over time); Bonferroni post-hoc; SPSS 25.0; p<0.05.

## Results

**DHEQ-15** (baseline → 8wk, mean±SD):

| Agent | Baseline | Week 8 | Over-time |
|---|---|---|---|
| CPP-ACP | 77.84±16.39 | **21.16±10.71** | p<0.001 |
| Arginine | 77.84±13.84 | 37.52±21.85 | p<0.001 |
| Potassium nitrate | 74.6±15.44 | 36.36±21.99 | p<0.001 |
| Novamin | 72.68±19.56 | 39.16±24.59 | p<0.001 |
| Propolis | 77.68±15.11 | 41.56±31.46 | p<0.001 |
| Control | 82.36±17.99 | 68.72±25.5 | p=0.068 (NS) |

All test agents significantly lower than control at week 8 (CPP-ACP p<0.001; KNO3 p=0.003; Arginine p=0.008; Novamin p=0.009; Propolis p=0.021). Control showed **no significant DHEQ-15 change** over time.

**SSS** (baseline → 8wk): CPP-ACP 2.5 → **0.18**; Arginine 2.28 → 0.56; Potassium nitrate 2.36 → 0.62; Novamin 2.12 → 0.68; Propolis 2.22 → 0.88; Control 2.38 → 1.9. All test agents lower than control at weeks 4 & 8; CPP-ACP < Novamin at week 4.

**VAS** (baseline → 8wk): CPP-ACP 7.44 → **0.92**; Arginine 7.36 → 2.52; Novamin 6.84 → 2.52; Potassium nitrate 6.84 → 2.76; Propolis 7.2 → 3.2; Control 7.4 → 4.96 (highest). CPP-ACP change significantly greater than Potassium nitrate, Novamin, and Propolis.

**Change baseline → week 8 (greatest = CPP-ACP)**: DHEQ-15 56.68±17.87, VAS 6.52±1.48, SSS 2.32±0.56. CPP-ACP DHEQ-15 change significant vs Novamin (p=0.030); SSS change vs Novamin (p=0.008) and Propolis (p=0.002).

**Overall ranking (week-8 reduction)**: CPP-ACP > Arginine ≈ Potassium nitrate > Novamin ≈ Propolis > Control.

**Bottom line**: every home-use desensitizing agent beat standard fluoride toothpaste for DH; CPP-ACP (brushing + finger inunction) was the most effective by 8 weeks. Fluoride co-use did not by itself explain efficacy. Limitations: only 8-week follow-up, no oral-hygiene control, asymmetric two-product protocols (CPP-ACP/KNO3 used with fluoride), and underdeveloped standardized DH product-testing procedures.
