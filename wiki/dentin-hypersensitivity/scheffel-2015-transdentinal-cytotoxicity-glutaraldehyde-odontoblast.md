## One-line Summary
In vitro transdentinal model (MDPC-23 odontoblast-like cells beneath dentin discs) showing glutaraldehyde-containing desensitizing solutions reduce pulp-side cell viability in a concentration-dependent manner.

## Summary
Using artificial pulp chambers with dentin discs, this study measured whether glutaraldehyde — the active in Gluma-type desensitizers — diffuses across dentin to harm odontoblast-like cells. Glutaraldehyde-containing treatments reduced MDPC-23 viability in a concentration-dependent fashion, confirming transdentinal cytotoxicity. The findings provide the mechanistic safety counterpoint to glutaraldehyde's clinical desensitizing efficacy and inform concentration/exposure caution near vital pulp.

## Key Contributions
- Demonstrates transdentinal diffusion and pulpal cytotoxicity of glutaraldehyde.
- Concentration-dependent viability data relevant to Gluma-type agents.

## Methodology
Artificial pulp chamber, dentin discs, MDPC-23 odontoblast-like cells on pulpal side; occlusal application of glutaraldehyde solutions/materials; metabolic/viability assays.

## Results
Glutaraldehyde-containing treatments produced concentration-dependent reduction in odontoblast-like cell viability across the dentin barrier.
