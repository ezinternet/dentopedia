## One-line Summary
Scoping review of 138 RCTs (368 toothpaste formulations) mapping active ingredients, fluoride type, and abrasives in desensitizing toothpastes — potassium compounds and CSP the most common actives, MFP and NaF the dominant fluorides.

## 한줄요약

## Summary
This scoping review catalogued the formulation landscape of desensitizing toothpastes from 138 randomized trials, classifying 368 formulations by active ingredient, fluoride form, and abrasive base. Potassium compounds (19%) and calcium sodium phosphosilicate (10%) were the most frequent active ingredients; sodium monofluorophosphate (29%) and sodium fluoride (22%) dominated the fluoride forms; silica (23%) and calcium (17%) the abrasives. Formulation numbers rose sharply after 2010. The review maps options rather than ranking efficacy, supporting informed product selection.

## Key Contributions
- Comprehensive inventory of desensitizing-toothpaste actives, fluorides, abrasives.
- Prevalence figures for each ingredient class.

## Methodology
Scoping review (PROSPERO CRD42018086815), 138 RCTs, 368 formulations classified; search to Aug 2021.

## Results
Actives: potassium 19%, CSP 10%, strontium 8%, arginine 8%, SnF2 6%, hydroxyapatite 2%. Fluoride: MFP 29%, NaF 22%. Abrasive: silica 23%, calcium 17%. 64% of formulations contained active DH ingredients.
