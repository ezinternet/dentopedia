## One-line Summary

8-week examiner-blind parallel RCT (217 screened / 215 completed): anhydrous 5% CSPS+NaF toothpaste reduced examiner-assessed DH (Schiff + Yeaple tactile threshold) significantly at every timepoint from Day 3 to Day 56 and significantly more than conventional NaF-silica toothpaste (all between-arm p<0.001), with relief growing over 8 weeks; DHEQ-48 OHRQoL improved progressively but the between-arm difference was not significant. Well-tolerated. (abstract-only)

## Summary

Anhydrous toothpastes containing 5% calcium-sodium phosphosilicate (CSPS — bioactive glass / NovaMin, the active in Sensodyne Repair & Protect) have well-established **long-term** efficacy against dentine hypersensitivity (DH), but their **short-term onset** has not been well characterised. This single-centre, 8-week, randomized, controlled, examiner-blind, 2-arm, stratified, parallel-group RCT (Haleon-sponsored) measured the **time-course** of DH reduction from Day 3 to Day 56 of twice-daily brushing, alongside patient-reported oral-health-related quality of life (OHRQoL) via the DHEQ-48 questionnaire.

In an orally-healthy DH population, two non-adjacent DH teeth were selected per participant. The **Test** arm used a 5% w/w CSPS toothpaste containing NaF; the **Reference** arm used a conventional NaF-silica toothpaste. The headline finding: the CSPS toothpaste produced statistically significant DH relief **as early as Day 3** by two independent clinical measures, was significantly superior to the conventional fluoride toothpaste, and the benefit **grew progressively across the 8 weeks**.

## Methodology

- **Design**: single-centre, 8-week, randomized, controlled, examiner-blind, 2-arm, stratified, parallel-group RCT.
- **Population**: orally-healthy adults with dentine hypersensitivity; two non-adjacent DH teeth per participant.
- **Arms** (twice-daily brushing):
  - **Test** — 5% w/w CSPS toothpaste containing NaF.
  - **Reference** — conventional NaF-silica toothpaste.
- **Endpoints**:
  - **Schiff** sensitivity score (evaporative/air stimulus) — Baseline, 3d, 7d, 14d, 28d, 56d.
  - **Tactile threshold (Yeaple probe)** — Baseline, 3d, 7d, 14d, 28d, 56d.
  - **DHEQ-48** (patient-reported OHRQoL) — 28d, 56d.
- **Sample**: 217 screened, 215 completed.
- **Registration**: ClinicalTrials.gov NCT06378008.

## Results

| Endpoint | Finding |
|---|---|
| Schiff + Yeaple, Test within-arm | Significant DH reduction at **all** post-baseline timepoints (3/7/14/28/56d), both measures, **p<0.0001** |
| Test vs Reference (between-arm) | Test **significantly greater** than Reference (all **p<0.001**) |
| Onset | Significant superiority **after 3 days** |
| Time-course | DH reduction **markedly increased** over the study, growing to 8 weeks |
| DHEQ-48 (OHRQoL) | Decreased progressively (improving) but **between-arm difference NOT significant** |
| Tolerability | Well-tolerated |

**Bottom line**: a 5% CSPS toothpaste reduced DH versus a marketed NaF-silica toothpaste by two clinical measures, with the effect significant after 3 days and increasing over 8 weeks. Examiner-assessed clinical superiority did not translate into a significant patient-reported OHRQoL difference between arms over this horizon.

*Abstract-only ingest (no PMC open access) — exact effect sizes, confidence intervals, and per-timepoint means were not retrieved.*
