## One-line Summary
SR+MA (34 studies; 11 in meta-analysis) showing laser therapy — high- or low-power — significantly reduces dentin hypersensitivity pain at 3 months, with no significant superiority of high- over low-power and no derivable standard protocol.

## Summary
This systematic review with meta-analysis pooled clinical trials of laser therapy for dentin hypersensitivity, covering both high-power (ablative/photothermal) and low-power (photobiomodulation) lasers. Meta-analysis of pre/post pain showed significant reduction at three months for both laser categories. Indirect comparison suggested high-power lasers trended toward greater relief but without statistical superiority over low-power. Marked heterogeneity in assessment methods prevented the authors from defining a standard treatment protocol, so laser is endorsed as effective but not yet protocol-standardized.

## Key Contributions
- Pools high- and low-power laser DH trials in one analysis.
- Indirect high- vs low-power comparison.
- Identifies methodological heterogeneity as the barrier to protocol standardization.

## Methodology
562 records (to April 2020), 160 full-text assessed, 34 included, 11 in meta-analysis. Independent data extraction and risk-of-bias; pre/post pain meta-analysis at 3 months.

## Results
Significant pain reduction at 3 months for both laser types; high-power non-significantly favoured over low-power; majority of studies followed ≤6 months.
