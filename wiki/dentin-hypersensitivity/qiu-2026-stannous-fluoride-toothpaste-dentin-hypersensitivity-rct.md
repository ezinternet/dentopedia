## One-line Summary

Two examiner-blind parallel RCTs (Study 1 n=104, Study 2 n=113; 18–65 y; healthy adults with dentin hypersensitivity): twice-daily anhydrous 0.454% stannous fluoride (SnF₂) toothpaste (Test) gave significantly greater Schiff reduction, tactile-threshold increase, and LMS pain improvement than standard fluoride toothpaste (Reference) at Day 28 and Day 56 (all p<0.05); Study 1 also showed superior DHEQ-48 quality-of-life at Day 56.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

Two independent, single-center, 56-day, randomized, parallel-group, examiner-blind RCTs tested a **new** anhydrous, glycerine-based 0.454% w/w SnF₂ toothpaste (reformulated for tooth-stain removal and organoleptic improvement) against a standard fluoride toothpaste in healthy adults with dentin hypersensitivity (DH). The design paired conventional clinician-assessed endpoints (Schiff evaporative score and Yeaple tactile threshold) with validated patient-reported outcomes (Labeled Magnitude Scales for pain; the DHEQ-48 quality-of-life questionnaire). Across both trials the Test toothpaste produced statistically significant within-group improvement at Day 28 and Day 56 and was significantly superior to the Reference for Schiff, tactile threshold, and all four LMS domains. DHEQ-48 quality-of-life superiority over Reference was demonstrated in Study 1 (Day 56) but not Study 2 (where Reference also improved, attributed to a placebo effect). Both products were well tolerated.

## Key Contributions

- Confirms the new anhydrous 0.454% SnF₂ formulation retains DH efficacy after the cosmetic/organoleptic reformulation, with **superiority** over a standard fluoride toothpaste on both objective and subjective measures.
- First demonstration that the anhydrous SnF₂ family yields a **superior LMS pain reduction versus a Reference** treatment; Schiff–LMS alignment links the clinical indicator to perceived pain.
- ADA clinical-meaningfulness threshold (≥20% between-treatment difference) reached by Day 28 and widening by Day 56.

## Methodology

- **Design**: two single-center, 56-day, randomized, controlled, parallel, examiner-blind trials with opaque product overwrapping. Study 1 = Salus Research, Fort Wayne, IN (ID 300103). Study 2 = All Sum Research Center, Melbourne, FL (ID 300104). Veritas IRB-approved.
- **Participants**: healthy adults 18–65 y; ≥20 natural teeth; self-reported DH 6 months–10 years; ≥2 non-adjacent sensitive teeth (incisors/canines/premolars) in different quadrants with tactile threshold ≤20 g AND Schiff ≥2 at screening and baseline; erosion/abrasion/recession (EAR); MGI 0; no mobility.
- **Acclimatization**: 14–28 days on a regular fluoride toothpaste (1000 ppm F) before randomization.
- **Intervention**: Test = 0.454% w/w SnF₂ (1100 ppm F ion); Reference = standard fluoride toothpaste (1100 ppm F as NaF). Brush ≥1 min twice daily ×56 days. Test group additionally instructed to brush the sensitive test teeth first ("focused brushing," per US label); Reference whole-mouth only.
- **Outcomes**: Schiff 0–3 (1-s air blast) and Yeaple tactile threshold in g (higher = lower sensitivity; max 80 g at follow-up) by blinded examiners; LMS (intensity, duration, tolerability, description on four 100-mm scales) and DHEQ-48 (Section 1 everyday impact; Section 2 restrictions/adaptation/social/emotional/identity) by participants.
- **Analysis**: mITT; primary/secondary = change-from-baseline Schiff and tactile threshold at Days 28/56, Test vs Reference; MMRM (unstructured covariance, Kenward-Roger df), LSM, 95% CI, two-sided p at 5%. 50/arm gave ≥90% power to detect a 0.4-unit Schiff difference (SD 0.6).

## Results

**Enrollment** — Study 1 N=103 (Test 52 / Reference 51); Study 2 N=112 (Test 56 / Reference 56); well-balanced baseline.

**Clinician-assessed (all Test vs Reference p<0.0001):**

| Endpoint | Study | Day 28 Test vs Ref (diff) | Day 56 Test vs Ref (diff) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Schiff Δ | 1 | −1.75 vs −0.10 (−1.65) | −2.25 vs −0.31 (−1.94) |
| Schiff Δ | 2 | −0.79 vs −0.17 (−0.62) | −1.40 vs −0.33 (−1.07) |
| Tactile Δ (g) | 1 | +36.59 vs +1.23 (+35.37) | +60.07 vs +2.70 (+57.37) |
| Tactile Δ (g) | 2 | +16.75 vs +2.45 (+14.31) | +41.13 vs +4.41 (+36.72) |

Reference within-group tactile changes were non-significant; Reference Schiff was significant only at later timepoints but far smaller than Test.

**Patient-reported LMS** — significant within-Test reduction in intensity, duration, tolerability, description at both timepoints (p<0.0001); Test vs Reference significant in all four domains at Day 28 and 56 (p<0.05). Example (Intensity Day 56): Study 1 Test −41.36 vs Reference −8.18 (diff −33.22); Study 2 Test −26.66 vs −5.99 (diff −20.67).

**DHEQ-48 quality of life** — Study 1: Test superior to Reference at Day 56 across Section 1, total score, all five domains, effect-on-life, and global oral health (total-score difference −24.49, p=0.0002); no between-group difference at Day 28. Study 2: within-Test improvements at Day 56, but **no** significant between-treatment difference at Day 28 or 56 (Reference also improved → attributed to placebo/Hawthorne effect in a multi-visit short trial).

**Safety** — well tolerated, no serious adverse events. Study 1: 9 treatment-related TEAEs (8 Test in 4 participants, 1 Reference); one Test participant with 3 severe TEAEs withdrew; remaining mild; all resolved. Study 2: no treatment-related TEAEs.

**Limitation** — Test group used focused brushing of sensitive areas (per US label) while Reference brushed whole-mouth only; this mirrors real-world label use but is not a pure formulation-only comparison.
