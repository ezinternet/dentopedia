## One-line Summary

Open-access review: primary afferent (DRG) nociceptors maintain high intracellular chloride via NKCC1/KCC2, so opening their many chloride channels (GABA-A, glycine, anoctamin/TMEM16, bestrophin, CLC, CFTR, VRAC) can paradoxically excite rather than inhibit — a critical, underappreciated driver of nociceptor sensitization in inflammatory and neuropathic pain.

## Summary

In the spinal dorsal horn the role of chloride conductances is well established, but chloride homeostasis in primary afferent neurons has been comparatively neglected. This review shows that dorsal root ganglion (DRG) sensory neurons keep an unusually high intracellular chloride concentration through the balanced activity of NKCC1 and KCC2. As a consequence, activation of chloride channels — which hyperpolarizes most central neurons — can produce inhibitory or even excitatory depolarization in primary afferents depending on the chloride reversal potential and the magnitude of chloride efflux. DRG neurons express a wide repertoire of chloride channels: ligand-gated GABA-A and glycine receptors, Ca²⁺-activated anoctamin/TMEM16, bestrophin and tweety-homolog channels, CLC channels/transporters, CFTR, and volume-regulated anion channels (VRAC). These conductances contribute to peripheral terminal transduction and amplification, action-potential generation, and dorsal-horn synaptic transmission, and are modulated by inflammatory mediators directly or via signaling cascades. Because both the channels and their modulators are regulated in pain disorders, chloride is reframed as a legitimate peripheral analgesic target.

## Key Contributions

- Establishes that DRG/primary afferent chloride homeostasis — not just spinal chloride — is central to nociception.
- Catalogs the full DRG chloride-channel repertoire (GABA-A, glycine, anoctamin/TMEM16, bestrophin, tweety, CLC, CFTR, VRAC).
- Explains the excitation paradox: high intracellular chloride means chloride-channel opening can depolarize sensory neurons.
- Highlights anoctamin 1 (TMEM16A) as a key Ca²⁺-activated chloride channel in nociceptor excitation.
- Reviews inflammatory-mediator modulation of chloride flux as a sensitization mechanism.

## Methodology

Narrative mechanistic review organized by chloride-channel family → DRG expression → contribution to nociception/sensitization → modulation by inflammatory mediators. No quantitative synthesis.

## Results

Qualitative. Specific chloride conductances shape peripheral transduction, excitability, and spinal transmission; their regulation in pain states drives nociceptor excitation and sensitization, particularly in neuropathic pain.
