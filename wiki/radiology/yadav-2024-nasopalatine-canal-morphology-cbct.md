## One-line Summary

Retrospective CBCT cross-sectional study (n=102, Indian population) documenting nasopalatine canal morphology, dimensions (length, AP/ML diameters, angle), shape variants, and opening counts stratified by sex and age — NPC longer and AP-wider in males, ML diameter enlarges with age.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

The nasopalatine canal (NPC) / 비비구개관 is a critical anatomical landmark in the anterior maxilla that dentists must understand before implant placement, local anesthesia delivery, or craniofacial surgery. This retrospective CBCT study from Manipal College of Dental Sciences (India) analyzed 102 scans (54 female, 48 male) across six age groups (≤20 to ≥61 years).

**Key morphometric findings:**
- Mean NPC length: 9.64 mm (range ~7–12 mm); significantly longer in males (10.19 vs 9.16 mm; P=0.012)
- Anteroposterior (AP) diameter at palatal opening: significantly greater in males (3.53 vs 2.92 mm; P<0.001)
- Nasopalatine angle 비구개각: significantly greater in females (116.3° vs lower in males; P=0.012); mean 114.4°
- ML lower diameter increases significantly with age (≥61 yr: 4.2 mm vs ≤20 yr: 2.8 mm; P=0.027)
- Canal length shortest in ≥61 yr group (7.15 mm)

**Morphological shape distribution (sagittal section):**

| Shape | Prevalence |
|---|---|
| Cylindrical | 25.5% (most common) |
| Hourglass | 18.6% |
| Banana | 17.6% |
| Funnel | 23.5% |
| Cone | 7.8% |
| Tree branch | 3.9% |
| Spindle | 2.9% (least common) |

Cylindrical shape predominant in females; funnel predominant in males (P=0.012). In coronal sections, single canal most common; Y-shape least common. Slanted orientation most prevalent; vertically curved canals rare.

**Number of canal openings (Stensen's foramina / 스텐센공 at nasal fossa):** 2 openings most common; 4 openings least common; variation correlated with age.

## Key Contributions

1. First systematic sex- and age-stratified CBCT morphometric dataset for NPC in a southern Indian population
2. Confirms male NPC is longer and wider at palatal opening — clinically relevant for implant axis planning
3. Documents 7 distinct sagittal shape variants and their sex distribution
4. NPC angle data supports understanding of canal obliquity relative to palatal plane
5. Highlights complication risks: nerve injury (sensory loss), vessel injury (bleeding), NPC cyst formation from surgical trauma

## Methodology

- Retrospective CBCT analysis; Planmeca unit, FOV 8×8 cm, 0.16 mm resolution
- 150 scans reviewed → 102 included (48 excluded: poor quality n=17, suspected pathology n=9, missing central incisors n=15, prior implant/bone graft n=7)
- Measurements: NPC length (IF midpoint to SF midpoint), AP and ML diameters at nasal/middle/palatal levels in axial sections, nasopalatine angle in sagittal section
- Reference protocol: Bornstein et al. standardized NPC CBCT measurement
- Statistics: Student's t-test, χ², Pearson correlation; SPSS 17.0

## Results

- NPC length: males significantly longer (P=0.012); ≤20 yr group also longer (P=0.002)
- AP lower diameter: males significantly larger (P<0.001); trend toward increase with age (NS for most age comparisons)
- ML lower diameter: significantly increases with age (P=0.027)
- Shapes not significantly different between age groups overall, but cylindrical vs funnel sex difference significant (P=0.012)
- Canal orientation: slanted most common overall; sex correlation significant
