## One-line Summary

Phantom dosimetry (7 collimator systems, adult + child): best FMS effective dose = 54 µSv (Focus-RC adult) vs 86 µSv circular; rectangular collimation reduces dose more than thyroid shielding; validates NCRP 2019 recommendations.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

Johnson & Ludlow (2020) performed a systematic phantom dosimetry study comparing 6 rectangular collimation (RC) modalities against standard circular collimation for adult and child full-mouth series. Using OSL dosimeters and an intraoral X-ray unit at 70 kVp, they found that all RC modalities significantly reduced effective dose versus circular — with the best performer (Focus-RC) achieving 54 µSv in adults, a ~37% reduction from circular's 86 µSv. Thyroid shielding with circular collimation provided less thyroid-dose reduction than RC alone, underscoring that field limitation (collimation) is the primary dose-reduction tool. This study validates and updates Ludlow et al. (2008) under current NCRP Report No. 177 guidelines.

## Key Contributions

- **Updated dose benchmarks** for modern RC systems — fills gap since Ludlow 2008 used older collimator technology.
- **RC > thyroid shield**: rectangular collimation reduces thyroid dose more effectively than thyroid shield + circular.
- Validates **NCRP Report 177 (2019)** recommendation: RC should be standard of care for intraoral imaging.
- Demonstrates **collimator size matters**: smaller rectangular field (Focus-RC exposure area 51% reduction) yields lowest E.

## Results

| Collimator | Adult E (µSv) | Reduction vs circular | Child E (µSv) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Focus-RC** | **54** | **37%** | **44** |
| JadRad-RC | 55 | 36% | 44 |
| Durr-RC | 58 | 33% | 45 |
| Rinn-RC | 62 | 28% | 48 |
| DEXshield-RC | 70 | 19% | 53 |
| TruAlign-RC | 85 | 1% | 85 |
| **Circular** | **86** | — | **89** |

**Background context:**
- Best FMS (54 µSv) ≈ **6.6 days** of natural background (US ~8.2 µSv/day)
- Per periapical image (18-image FMS): ~3 µSv ≈ **~8.7 hours** background equivalent
- Compared to transatlantic flight (~50 µSv): a single PA is ~6% of one transatlantic flight
