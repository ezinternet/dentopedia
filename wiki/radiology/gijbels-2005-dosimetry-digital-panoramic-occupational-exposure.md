## One-line Summary

Phantom dosimetry across 5 digital panoramic units: scatter dose at 1 m from unit is ≤0.60 μGy per exposure; 500 panoramics/year yields only 5–40 μSv annual operator organ dose.

## 한줄요약

5종 디지털 파노라마 장치 팬텀 실험: 1 m 거리 산란선량 ≤0.60 μGy/촬영, 연간 500회 시 술자 갑상선 5–15 μSv, 생식선 5–40 μSv — 연간 직업한도의 0.3% 이하.

## Summary

Gijbels et al. measured occupational scatter radiation at 1 m distance from five different digital panoramic X-ray units using an ionization chamber positioned at thyroid and gonadal levels at five positions around each unit. The maximum organ equivalent dose per exposure was 0.60 μGy. Extrapolating to 500 panoramic exposures per year — a high-workload estimate — the operator's annual additional organ effective dose ranged from 5–15 μSv for the thyroid gland and 5–40 μSv for the gonads, depending on machine type and position. These doses are far below the 20 mSv/year occupational limit and even below the 1 mSv/year public dose limit, supporting the position that operators at ≥1 m from a panoramic unit during exposure face negligible radiation risk without additional personal shielding.

## Key Contributions

- Provides the first systematic comparison of occupational scatter dose across 5 digital panoramic units
- Demonstrates that scatter dose at 1 m is ≤0.60 μGy per exposure — 3 orders of magnitude below patient-level dose
- Annual operator dose at 1 m (500 panoramics/year) is 5–40 μSv — far below both occupational (20 mSv) and public (1 mSv) limits
- Machine-to-machine variation is up to 10-fold (0.04–0.53 μGy), implying unit selection affects operator dose

## Methodology

In vitro phantom dosimetry. Anthropomorphic phantom used as patient surrogate. Five digital panoramic units tested (4 CCD direct-digital, 1 storage phosphor). Ionization chamber recorded scatter at 1 m from phantom center at 5 positions (anterior, posterior, left, right, additional) at two heights (thyroid level, gonadal level). Tube settings followed manufacturer recommendations: 64–74 kVp, 4–7 mA, 8.2–19.0 s exposure time. Organ equivalent dose (μGy) and effective dose (μSv) calculated per exposure and annually (500 exposures assumed).

## Results

| Parameter | Range |
|---|---|
| Max organ equivalent dose per exposure (1 m) | 0.60 μGy |
| Max organ effective dose per exposure (1 m) | 0.10 μSv |
| Average organ equivalent dose (all units, positions) | 0.18–0.30 μGy |
| Average organ effective dose | 0.01–0.05 μSv |
| Between-machine range | 0.04–0.53 μGy (equivalent); 0.01–0.08 μSv (effective) |
| **Annual thyroid dose @ 500 panoramics/year, 1 m** | **5–15 μSv** |
| **Annual gonadal dose @ 500 panoramics/year, 1 m** | **5–40 μSv** |

The occupational dose at 1 m is negligibly small. Even at 500 panoramics/year (high workload), cumulative annual dose is at most 0.04 mSv — well within public dose limits, let alone the 20 mSv occupational limit.
