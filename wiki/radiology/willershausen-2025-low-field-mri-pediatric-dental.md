## One-line Summary

Prospective 16-child study: radiation-free 0.55 T MRI matched ULD-CT image quality for tooth axis/root/resorption/cysts but not all structures.

## 한줄요약

전향적 소아 16명: 무피폭 0.55 T MRI가 치축·치근·치근흡수·낭종에서 초저선량 CT와 동등 화질, 단 모든 구조엔 미흡.

## Summary

Positions low-field MRI as an emerging radiation-free alternative for selected paediatric dental indications, with current spatial-resolution limits — the conceptual counterpoint to the dose-reduction cluster.

## Key Contributions

- 0.55 T MRI is feasible and radiation-free for delineating ectopic/supernumerary teeth in children.
- IQ similar to ULD-CT (CTDI 0.43 mGy) for tooth axis, root, root resorption, and cysts; motion artifacts not significantly different.
- Not yet optimal for all tooth/jaw structures; high-res CT/CBCT still needed for advanced indications. MRI acquisition ~9:45 min.

## Methodology

Prospective single-center: 16 paediatric patients (mean 12.4 y; 49 ectopic/supernumerary teeth) underwent same-day ULD-CT (reference) and 0.55 T MRI (0.6 mm isotropic 3D T1w FLASH); IQ for 7 structures scored twice by 3 senior readers (5-point Likert), dichotomized valid/non-valid.

## Results

Positions low-field MRI as an emerging radiation-free alternative for selected paediatric dental indications, with current spatial-resolution limits — the conceptual counterpoint to the dose-reduction cluster.
