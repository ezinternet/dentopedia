## Summary

Hedesiu et al. (2018) conducted a retrospective dosimetry analysis of 7,150 children and adolescents who underwent 12,252 dental radiological examinations at a Romanian center over 2 years. By correcting effective doses for age and device parameters, they found that conventional 2D imaging (intraoral, panoramic, cephalometric) remains at a low-dose level with median <20 µSv — equivalent to just a few days of natural background. CBCT, however, produced a median of 127.2 µSv (cumulative 156.5 µSv per CBCT patient), and the highest individual CBCT dose was equivalent to **34.1 days of natural background radiation** — found predominantly in 11–15 year olds undergoing orthodontic CBCT. CBCT contributed 70% of the collective dose while representing only 11% of exams, highlighting CBCT as the dominant dosimetric concern in pediatric dental practice.

## Key Contributions

- **Largest pediatric cohort** dental dosimetry study with simultaneous 2D + CBCT comparison.
- **"Days of background" anchor**: highest CBCT = 34.1 days background — independently validates Stervik 2024's 5–10 days for conventional orthodontic imaging.
- **70/30 rule**: CBCT drives 70% of collective dose; 2D exams are dose-negligible by comparison.
- **Age-vulnerability**: CBCT exposure peaks 11–15 years (orthodontic planning), when radiosensitivity is still elevated.

## Methodology

- n=7,150 children (0–18 y), 2-year retrospective, one Romanian dental center.
- 12,252 exams: 4,220 intraoral + 1,324 ceph + 5,284 panoramic + 1,424 CBCT.
- Age-corrected effective doses using logarithmic interpolation of mA/FOV parameters.
- LAR and RRL calculated; groups compared by chi-square and t-tests.

## Results

| Modality | Median E (µSv) | Background equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| 2D exams (all types) | <20 | <3 days |
| CBCT (median) | 127.2 | ~19 days |
| CBCT (highest, ages 11–15) | — | **34.1 days** |
| Cumulative (CBCT group) | 156.5 | ~24 days |

- CBCT = 11% of exams but 70% of collective dose (per-caput 184 µSv for CBCT group).
- LAR and RRL significantly higher under 18 for CBCT; age-specific risk factor applies.
- Overall CBCT dose still in "low" tier of medical imaging risk spectrum.

**Natural background context:**
- Global average ~2,400 µSv/year (~6.6 µSv/day)
- US average ~3,100 µSv/year (~8.5 µSv/day)
- High-altitude cities (Denver, Bogotá): +300–600 µSv/year extra cosmic component
- Transatlantic flight (~8 h): ~50 µSv (~1 week dental bitewings equivalent)
