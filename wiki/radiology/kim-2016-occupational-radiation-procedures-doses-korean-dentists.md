## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional survey of 658 South Korean dentists linked to National Dose Registry: mean annual effective dose 0.18 mSv (male) / 0.13 mSv (female), representing <1% of the 20 mSv occupational limit, with panoramic radiography the most frequent radiation procedure.

## Summary

Kim et al. surveyed 658 South Korean dentists (April 2012–May 2013) and linked survey data to the National Dose Registry. Panoramic radiography was the most frequently performed radiation procedure, followed by intraoral and portable dental units. Male dentists performed more procedures and wore lead aprons more often, and received slightly higher annual doses (0.18 vs 0.13 mSv). All doses were far below the 20 mSv/year occupational limit. Female dentists in provincial areas had significantly higher cumulative doses than metropolitan counterparts, possibly reflecting less optimal structural shielding or procedure patterns. Cumulative dose was greater for older and longer-employed dentists. The study establishes a Korea-specific occupational dose baseline, confirming that dental radiation work — when conducted under standard shielded-room conditions — poses minimal cumulative risk.

## Key Contributions

- First large-scale Korean cohort linking dentist survey to National Dose Registry
- Confirms panoramic radiography as the dominant radiation procedure in Korean dental practice
- Establishes Korean baseline: 0.13–0.18 mSv/y, substantially lower than the 20 mSv limit
- Identifies geographic disparity: provincial female dentists receive higher doses
- Male dentists more frequently use lead aprons, reflecting self-protection behavior differences

## Methodology

Cross-sectional survey + registry linkage. 658 dentists recruited at two time points (April 2012 – May 2013). Questionnaire covered demographic data, weekly procedure volume, and radiation protection behaviors. Linked to National Dose Registry (MFDS, Korea) for TLD-based annual dose data. Multiple linear regression assessed associations between demographic/occupational factors and annual dose.
