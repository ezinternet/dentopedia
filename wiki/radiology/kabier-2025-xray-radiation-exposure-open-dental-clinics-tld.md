## Summary

Kabier et al. measured annual radiation exposure of dentists and assistants in open dental clinics (no dedicated shielded X-ray room) in Basrah, Iraq using TLD-200 dosimeters placed at chest level over the lead apron. Mean annual effective doses were 5.623 mSv for dentists and 5.279 mSv for assistants — both well below the ICRP occupational limit of 20 mSv/year, but approximately 30-fold higher than Korean shielded-room data (Kim et al. 2016: 0.18 mSv). Assistants in general hospitals received higher doses than clinic-based dental staff, attributed to greater procedure frequency and patient throughput. The study confirms that personal protective equipment (lead apron) combined with proper protocols keeps doses within limits even without structural shielding, but underscores the critical role of dedicated shielded X-ray rooms in minimizing occupational exposure.

## Key Contributions

- Provides occupational dose data for open dental clinics without shielded X-ray rooms — the worst-case clinical scenario
- TLD placed above the lead apron captures residual scatter dose after personal protection
- Demonstrates that even in open clinics, doses remain below 20 mSv/year occupational limit
- Reveals 30× dose difference between open-clinic (5.6 mSv) and shielded-room (0.18 mSv) settings — quantifying the protective value of structural shielding
- Identifies general hospital dental departments as higher-risk than standalone dental clinics

## Methodology

Prospective TLD dosimetry. Setting: open dental clinics in Basrah City, Iraq (no dedicated shielded radiography room). Participants: dentists and dental assistants. Dosimeter: TLD-200 (CaF₂:Dy) worn at chest level **above the lead apron**. Measurement: annual effective dose integrated over the study period. Comparison groups: dentists vs assistants; dental clinics vs general hospital dental departments.

## Results

| Group | Mean Annual Effective Dose | vs ICRP limit |
|---|---|---|
| Dentists | 5.623 mSv/year | 28% of 20 mSv limit |
| Assistants | 5.279 mSv/year | 26% of 20 mSv limit |
| Korean dentists (shielded room) | 0.18 mSv/year | 0.9% of limit |

- All doses were within the ICRP 20 mSv/year occupational limit
- Assistants in general hospitals > clinic-based dentists (higher patient volume)
- The dose measured is **above** the apron: actual body dose from scatter reaching the torso beneath the apron would be further attenuated by the apron's ~0.25–0.5 mm Pb equivalent
