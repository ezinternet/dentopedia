## One-line Summary

Phantom + Monte Carlo (DIMITRA): 70 kVp/16 mAs/180 µm voxel cut paediatric CBCT effective dose ~45% with maintained image quality.

## 한줄요약

팬텀+몬테카를로(DIMITRA): 70 kVp·16 mAs·180 µm 복셀 조합으로 소아 CBCT 유효선량 ~45% 감소하면서 화질 유지.

## Summary

Counterintuitively, keeping a fine voxel while lowering exposure beats coarse-voxel ultra-low-dose; quantitative dose–quality trade-off operationalizes the ALADAIP principle for children.

## Key Contributions

- Smaller voxel (180 µm) preserved image-quality scores and sharpness even with reduced kVp/mAs.
- ~45% average effective-dose reduction with 70 kVp + 16 mAs + 180 µm voxel.
- Both 400 µm/7.4 mAs 'ultra-low-dose' protocols scored lowest — unsuitable for anatomical assessment despite high observer confidence.

## Methodology

Custom anthropomorphic paediatric phantoms scanned in 6 CBCT protocols; blinded randomized 4-point quality scoring by 3 observers; air/PMMA-edge sharpness; effective dose via validated paediatric-voxel Monte Carlo.

## Results

Counterintuitively, keeping a fine voxel while lowering exposure beats coarse-voxel ultra-low-dose; quantitative dose–quality trade-off operationalizes the ALADAIP principle for children.
