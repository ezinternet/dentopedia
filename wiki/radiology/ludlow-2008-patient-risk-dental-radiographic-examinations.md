## One-line Summary

Phantom dosimetry recalculation under ICRP 2007: FMX + PSP + rectangular collimation = 34.9 µSv; 4 bitewings = 5.0 µSv; round-collimation FMX = 171 µSv — values 32–422% higher than prior ICRP 1990 estimates.

## Summary

Ludlow, Davies-Ludlow & White (2008) is the foundational benchmark paper for dental radiograph effective dose. Using a tissue-equivalent head phantom with TLDs at 28 organ sites, they recalculated effective doses under both ICRP 1990 and the updated ICRP 2007 tissue-weighting scheme, which newly included salivary glands, extrathoracic region, and oral mucosa — organs directly in the dental X-ray beam. The result: ICRP 2007 values are substantially higher than 1990 values across all modalities. Key message: clinicians can reduce patient dose by ~91% on FMX simply by switching from D-speed film + round collimation to PSP/F-speed + rectangular collimation.

## Key Contributions

- **Standard dose table** for all common dental radiographic modalities under ICRP 2007 — cited in virtually all subsequent dental dosimetry literature.
- Quantifies collimation impact: FMX dose drops from 388 µSv (D-speed + round) → 170.7 µSv (PSP + round) → **34.9 µSv (PSP + rectangular)**.
- Provides the cancer-risk framework context: FMX at 34.9 µSv ≈ 4.3 days of natural background radiation (USA ~3,000 µSv/year).
- Establishes panoramic dose range: 14.2–24.3 µSv depending on unit — roughly equivalent to 2–3 days of background radiation.

## Methodology

- Alderson-Rando tissue-equivalent head phantom.
- TLD chips at 28 radiosensitive organ sites (thyroid, salivary glands, bone marrow, brain, skin, eyes, gonads, etc.).
- Compared multiple receptor/collimation combinations across all common dental radiograph types.
- Effective dose calculated per ICRP 1990 and ICRP 2007 weighting factors.

## Results — Dose Table (ICRP 2007)

| Exam | Receptor | Collimation | E(µSv) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **FMX** | PSP / F-speed | **Rectangular** | **34.9** |
| **4 Bitewings** | PSP / F-speed | **Rectangular** | **5.0** |
| FMX | PSP / F-speed | Round | 170.7 |
| FMX | D-speed film | Round | 388 |
| Panoramic Orthophos XG | CCD | — | 14.2 |
| Panoramic ProMax | CCD | — | 24.3 |
| PA cephalogram | PSP | — | 5.1 |
| Lateral cephalogram | PSP | — | 5.6 |

**Context comparisons (US figures):**
- Annual natural background: ~3,000 µSv/year (~8.2 µSv/day)
- FMX (best technique): 34.9 µSv ≈ **~4.3 days** of natural background
- 4 bitewings (best): 5.0 µSv ≈ **~14.6 hours** of natural background
- Single panoramic: 14–24 µSv ≈ **1.7–3.0 days** of background
