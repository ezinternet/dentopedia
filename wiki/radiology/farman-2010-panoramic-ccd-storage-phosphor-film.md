## One-line Summary

Receptor comparison: CCD (OP100D wide trough/higher dose; DigiPan narrow trough/lower dose), storage phosphor (high-res, no dose saving, slower) vs film.

## 한줄요약

수용체 비교: CCD(OP100D 넓은 초점골·고선량; DigiPan 좁은 초점골·저선량), 저장형광판(고해상도·선량절감 없음·느림) vs 필름.

## Summary

Establishes the receptor-level trade-off between focal-trough width (positioning tolerance), dose, and workflow speed during the film-to-digital transition.

## Key Contributions

- OP100D CCD: instant image, wide focal trough (positioning-error tolerant) but higher dose than film.
- DigiPan CCD: significant dose reduction + instant image, but narrower trough (positioning-error prone).
- DenOptix storage phosphor: high resolution, reasonable trough width, but slower and no dose saving vs film.

## Methodology

Comparative evaluation of image-layer characteristics, dose requirements, and convenience across 4 receptors (film, storage phosphor, two CCD sensors) for panoramic radiography.

## Results

Establishes the receptor-level trade-off between focal-trough width (positioning tolerance), dose, and workflow speed during the film-to-digital transition.
