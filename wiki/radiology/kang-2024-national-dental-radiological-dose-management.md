## 한줄요약
J Korean Dent Assoc 2024 (SNU OMFR): national-level dental radiation dose management based on diagnostic reference levels (DRLs); Korea established DRLs for intraoral/panoramic radiography in 2009 and for dental CBCT in 2018; ICRP recommends DRL revision every 3-5 years, more frequently after technological change.

## Summary
Narrative review of national-level dental radiation dose management in South Korea, structured around the framework of diagnostic reference levels (DRLs). It maps DRL evolution from ICRP publications 60 and 73 to the more recent ICRP 135 (2017), aligns it with the EU 97/43/Euratom directive and IAEA Basic Safety Standards Requirement 34, and tracks the Korean adoption timeline (intraoral/panoramic 2009, dental CBCT 2018). The author argues for regular 3-5-year DRL revision cycles, with shorter cycles when imaging technology evolves substantially.

## Key Contributions
- Maps DRL framework evolution (ICRP 60 → 73 → 135) to dental imaging
- Documents Korean dental DRL adoption timeline
- Argues two-pillar dose management: justification + optimization (ALARA)
- Recommends 3-5-year DRL revision cycle with shorter intervals after technology change

## Methodology
- Narrative review
- International standards vs Korean status comparison
- Application context for patient-level dose management

## Results (qualitative)
- DRL framework: ICRP 60·73 (older) → 135 (current)
- Korean dental DRL milestones: intraoral/panoramic 2009; CBCT 2018
- Justification (is the exam needed?) + Optimization (minimum diagnostic dose) as the two pillars
- DRL revision recommended every 3-5 years; more often when technology shifts

## Limitations
- Narrative review — no systematic synthesis
- Actual Korean dental dose data aggregation system still maturing
- Pediatric-specific DRL recommendations underdeveloped
