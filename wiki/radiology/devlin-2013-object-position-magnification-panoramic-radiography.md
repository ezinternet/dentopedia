## One-line Summary

Theoretical + ball-bearing study: panoramic magnification ~1.29 horizontal/1.26 vertical; only certain focal-trough positions give zero distortion; 6 mm sphere best for calibration.

## 한줄요약

이론+볼베어링 실험: 파노라마 확대율 수평 ~1.29·수직 ~1.26. 초점골 내 특정 위치에서만 왜곡 0. 보정용은 6 mm 구체가 우수.

## Summary

Refutes the assumption that placing teeth in the focal trough avoids distortion — magnification is not simply constant at the plane of focus; positioning precision matters for any metric use of panoramic.

## Key Contributions

- Mean magnification: 2.5 mm sphere 1.292 (H)/1.257 (V); 6 mm sphere 1.286 (H)/1.255 (V).
- Zero distortion occurs only at certain focal-trough positions; horizontal position errors cause distortion even within the trough.
- 6 mm ball bearing gives more reliable magnification/position estimation than 2.5 mm (preferred calibration object).

## Methodology

Two ball bearings (2.5, 6 mm) placed between teeth of a plastic skull, radiographed 21× (skull re-seated each time); automated edge-detection/ellipse-fit measurement; magnification equations derived linking object size, position, and machine parameters.

## Results

Refutes the assumption that placing teeth in the focal trough avoids distortion — magnification is not simply constant at the plane of focus; positioning precision matters for any metric use of panoramic.
