## One-line Summary

Retrospective record review (n=1,790 Swedish children/adolescents, two clinics) — a complete orthodontic imaging course (≈7 radiographs: 1 panoramic + 1 lateral ceph + ~3 intraorals) equals about 5–10 days of natural background radiation, with estimated fatal-cancer risk well under 1 per 100,000.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

Stervik et al. retrospectively reviewed the patient records of 1,790 children and adolescents (mean age 14.8 y) who completed orthodontic treatment at two specialist Swedish clinics, tallying the number and type of radiographs taken across treatment planning, treatment, and follow-up. Using effective-dose values and age-stratified risk factors (15%/Sv for <10 y, 10%/Sv for 10–20 y) under the linear non-threshold model, they estimated the stochastic fatal-cancer risk of the orthodontic imaging course. Each patient received about seven radiographs; the typical planning workup was one panoramic, one lateral cephalogram, and roughly three intraoral periapicals. The cumulative dose corresponded to **about 5–10 days of natural background radiation** (Swedish baseline 1–2 mSv/year), and the estimated fatal-cancer risk was low (under 1 per 100,000). The authors stress that, although per-patient risk is small, dental imaging is a high-volume procedure, accumulated exposure should be considered in young patients, and every exposure must be justified (ALARA) — yet justification was recorded for only 5% of planning-stage exams.

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies the **cumulative radiation burden of a full orthodontic imaging course** (≈7 radiographs/patient) in a large pediatric cohort.
- Provides a **second independent "days-of-background" anchor**: about 5–10 days of natural background radiation for the whole course, against a 1–2 mSv/year reference.
- Reports the **lateral cephalogram effective dose (5.6 µSv)** — a modality often absent from background-equivalent comparisons; notes a panoramic is ≈6× a lateral ceph and ≈4–5× an intraoral.
- Confirms **age-dependent risk** (higher in <10 y) and flags adult-derived risk factors as likely under-estimates for children.
- Documents a **justification-recording gap** (5% planning, 60% treatment) as an ALARA quality issue.

## Methodology

- **Design**: retrospective patient-record review, two Swedish specialist orthodontic clinics (A 2004–2005, n=1,125; B 2011–2012, n=665); total n=1,790.
- **Captured**: radiograph count/type by treatment stage, recorded justifications, supplementary referred tomography.
- **Dose source**: Granlund et al. 2016, Ludlow et al. 2008, EC Radiation Protection No. 172.
- **Risk model**: fatal-cancer risk = effective dose × age factor (15%/Sv <10 y; 10%/Sv 10–20 y), LNT model for the <100 mGy low-dose range.
- **Statistics**: between-clinic t-test, p < 0.05.

## Results

- **~7 radiographs per patient** across the course (total 14,306); only 1% had no radiographs.
- **Typical planning workup**: 1 panoramic + 1 lateral cephalogram + ~3 intraoral periapicals.
- **Per-image effective dose**: panoramic 14.2–36 µSv; lateral cephalogram 5.6 µSv; single intraoral 0.8 µSv; CBCT dento-alveolar 32–43 µSv.
- **Background-equivalent (key figure)**: the cumulative orthodontic dose corresponds to **about 5–10 days of natural background radiation** (Swedish natural background 1–2 mSv/year). A panoramic delivers ≈6× the dose of a lateral cephalogram and ≈4–5× that of an intraoral image.
- **Fatal-cancer risk** (induced per 100,000): one panoramic = 0.54 (<10 y) / 0.36 (10–20 y); lateral 0.10 / 0.06; intraoral 0.01; CBCT 0.65 / 0.32 — overall judged low.
- **Tomography**: 8.2% referred; CBCT small FOV (4×4 cm) in 6.1%; 85% of tomographic cases used small FOV, consistent with EC recommendations.
- **Justification recorded**: 5% (planning), 60% (treatment), 15% (follow-up).
