## Summary

This in-vitro study used an anthropomorphic RANDO head phantom and calibrated PTW dosimetry equipment (NOMEX Multimeter, Farmer Ionization Chamber Type 30010) to measure the occupational radiation profile of a handheld/portable intraoral X-ray unit, the Aribex NOMAD Pro 2. It addresses the recurring occupational-safety question raised by hand-supported, in-room X-ray sources: does operating the device expose the clinician to meaningful scatter? The authors quantified dose–area products, mapped the spatial extent of the regulatory control area, and characterized the scatter geometry. Their bottom line: scatter concentrates between the phantom and the tube head, is undetectable on the operator side of a vertical plane through the focal spot, and the control area is significantly smaller than European and other international radiation-protection standards specify — so the device does not increase operator risk when used per manufacturer specifications. This is the operator-side counterpart to patient-shielding evidence in dentomaxillofacial radiography.

## Key Contributions

- Direct instrumented measurement of operator-side exposure for a handheld intraoral X-ray device, rather than assumption-based occupational risk.
- Empirical mapping of the control-area (CA) extent vs. regulatory specification — finding the real CA substantially smaller than standards require.
- Scatter-pattern characterization localizing scatter to the phantom–device region, with none at the operator position behind the focal-spot plane.

## Methodology

- **Design**: in-vitro phantom dosimetry / radiation-protection measurement.
- **Device under test**: Aribex NOMAD Pro 2 handheld/portable intraoral X-ray unit (integrated backscatter shield).
- **Phantom & instruments**: RANDO male head phantom; consistency-testing body; PTW NOMEX Multimeter; PTW Farmer Ionization Chamber Type 30010.
- **Measured quantities**: (1) dose–area products across exposure times; (2) control-area extent (up to 4000 simulated exposures); (3) scatter pattern; (4) operator risk.

## Results

- **Dose–area products**: linear with exposure time, correlation factor > 0.9.
- **Control area** (4000 simulated exposures): maximum 42 cm; mean 16.7 cm; SD 10.8 cm — significantly smaller than European/international radiation-protection standards.
- **Scatter**: highest between phantom and X-ray device; **no scatter** at the dorsal phantom region or on the operator side of a vertical plane through the focal spot.
- **Conclusion**: no increased operator risk when the device is controlled per manufacturer specifications.
