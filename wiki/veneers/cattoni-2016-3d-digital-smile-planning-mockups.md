## One-line Summary

Prospective clinical study (n=28 patients, 108 IPS e.max porcelain laminate veneers, 2-year follow-up) of a fully digital 3D Digital Smile Planning (3D-DSP) workflow that fuses 2D facial photos with an intraoral STL scan, mills a PMMA mockup by CAD-CAM, and uses that mockup as a position guide for enamel-only minimally invasive preparation; complications were minimal (1 fracture, 2 sensitivities, 1 recession; no debonding/chipping/microleakage) with high patient satisfaction (85.7% rated the mockup test very effective).

## Summary

Cattoni et al. (2016) report a prospective clinical study of a "total" digital smile planning technique (3D-DSP) for fabricating esthetic crowns and veneers. Between September 2012 and July 2015, 28 adult patients (mean age 36) received 108 porcelain laminate veneers (PLVs). The novelty is the elimination of the analog laboratory chain — stone model, manual diagnostic wax-up, and silicone-key mockup transfer — in favor of an end-to-end digital flow. For each patient, three calibrated intra-/extraoral facial photographs were processed in 2D-Digital Smile System software, and an intraoral-scanner STL file was fused into the 3D-Digital Smile System to generate a virtual previsualization of the planned smile. After the patient approved the 3D design, a PMMA mockup was milled by CAD-CAM, tried in, then cemented with a spot-etch technique and used directly as a **position guide for minimally invasive enamel preparation** with margins kept on enamel. Definitive IPS e.max veneers were then milled and adhesively cemented onto vital teeth. At 2 years, complications were rare (1 total fracture, 2 sensitive teeth, 1 gingival recession; zero debonding, chipping, microleakage, secondary caries, or root canal therapy), and VAS satisfaction was high (64.3% found the digital previsualization very effective; 85.7% the mockup test). The authors frame the all-digital data transfer as faster, more predictable, and less error-prone than the analog wax-up route, while noting the small sample as a limitation.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates a fully digital alternative to the analog wax-up → silicone-key → printed-mockup chain by fusing 2D facial smile design with a 3D intraoral STL scan
- Uses a **CAD-CAM-milled PMMA mockup as the direct preparation guide**, operationalizing the APT (aesthetic pre-evaluative temporary) concept in a digital workflow to keep reduction within enamel
- Provides 2-year clinical outcome data for 108 IPS e.max PLVs on vital teeth with very low complication rates
- Adds patient-reported (VAS) evidence that both the virtual previsualization and the physical mockup test improve communication and esthetic consent
- Positions digital data transfer as a way to reduce operator error and chairside/lab time versus traditional prosthodontic manual steps

## Methodology

- **Design**: Prospective single-arm clinical study (case series), 28 patients, 108 PLVs, 2-year follow-up; no concurrent control arm
- **Planning**: 3 calibrated intra-/extraoral photos (Digital Smile System eyewear) → 2D-Digital Smile System; intraoral scan (Scanner 3D Progress, MHT) → STL; both fused in 3D-Digital Smile System (EGS) for virtual design
- **Mockup**: PMMA (Bredent) milled on a CAM system (Zirkonzahn) after patient approval of the 3D design; tried in for consent
- **Preparation**: mockup cemented with spot-etch technique, used as position guide for minimally invasive enamel-confined preparation with margins on enamel
- **Definitive restoration**: double-cord intraoral-scan impression; IPS e.max PLVs (Ivoclar Vivadent) milled by CAD/CAM (Zirkonzahn)
- **Cementation**: 78 Variolink Veneer (Ivoclar Vivadent), 30 Clearfil Esthetic Cement (Kuraray); all on vital teeth
- **Outcomes**: 2-year clinical complication tally + VAS satisfaction questionnaire (very effective / effective / ineffective) for previsualization and mockup test

## Results

**Veneer distribution (n=108):** maxilla 84 (anterior 54, posterior 30), mandible 24 (anterior 10, posterior 14).

| 2-year complication | n (of 108 veneers) | % |
|---|---|---|
| Total fracture | 1 | 0.9 |
| Sensitivity | 2 | 1.8 |
| Gingival recession | 1 | 0.9 |
| Chipping | 0 | 0 |
| Debonding | 0 | 0 |
| Microleakage | 0 | 0 |
| Secondary caries | 0 | 0 |
| Discoloration | 0 | 0 |
| Root canal therapy | 0 | 0 |

| VAS rating | Smile-design previsualization | Mockup test |
|---|---|---|
| Very effective | 18 (64.3%) | 24 (85.7%) |
| Effective | 10 (35.7%) | 4 (14.3%) |
| Ineffective | 0 | 0 |

- Preoperative indications included discoloration, bruxism, abrasion, crowding, caries, trauma, and diastema.
- The all-digital workflow is reported to reduce clinic/lab time, increase predictability of data matching for the CAD/CAM mockup, and reduce trauma to hard dental tissue.
- **Limitations**: small sample (n=28), no control group, 2-year follow-up only, proprietary equipment-dependent workflow, no quantitative enamel-preservation/depth metric.
