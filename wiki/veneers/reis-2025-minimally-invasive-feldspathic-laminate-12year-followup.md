## One-line Summary

Case report (J Esthet Restor Dent 2025) of maxillary anterior diastema closure and morphology correction with thin (<0.5 mm) feldspathic laminates on enamel-limited (~0.2 mm cervical, knife-edge) minimally invasive preparations — HF + phosphoric-acid clean + silane on ceramic, 3-step adhesive + light-cured veneer cement under cotton-roll isolation — surviving 12+ years with no fracture, caries, pulpal change, sensitivity, or repair.

## 한줄요약

상악 전치 치간이개 폐쇄·형태 수정을 thin (<0.5 mm) feldspathic 라미네이트로 시행한 case report (J Esthet Restor Dent 2025). 법랑질 한정 최소삭제(치경부 ~0.2 mm, knife-edge) + 세라믹 HF/인산 세척·실란, 3-step 접착제·광중합 비니어 시멘트, 코튼롤 격리로 접착하여 **12년+ 추적 동안 파절·우식·치수변화·과민·재수복 없이 생존**.

## Summary

Reis et al. (2025) document a single maxillary anterior rehabilitation (23-yr-old male; well-aligned, short, rounded teeth with diastemas) treated with thin feldspathic laminates over **minimally invasive, enamel-confined preparation** — and followed for **more than 12 years**. The workflow is a textbook minimally invasive sequence: additive diagnostic wax-up → PVS index → bis-acrylic mock-up (patient-approved) → preparation limited to ~0.2 mm cervical enamel with a knife-edge finish line and only minor incisal/interproximal smoothing → feldspathic porcelain layered on refractory dies → adhesive cementation. Bonding followed a meticulous protocol: ceramic etched with 10% HF (90 s) then cleaned with 37% phosphoric acid (removing hexafluorosilicate residue) and silanated; tooth enamel etched with phosphoric acid, a 3-step adhesive (Scotchbond Multi-Purpose) and light-cured veneer cement (RelyX Veneer) applied under **cotton-roll isolation** (cited as noninferior to rubber dam for longevity). At 12+ years the only observation was subtle marginal staining from age-related gingival recession; no fracture, secondary caries, pulpitis/necrosis, or postoperative sensitivity occurred, and no repair was needed. The case operationalizes the adhesion rule that ideal bonding requires ≥50% enamel substrate and ≥70% of the margin in enamel — achieved here by minimizing reduction.

## Key Contributions

- **12+ year documented survival** of enamel-limited minimally invasive feldspathic laminates with zero biological/mechanical complication.
- **Reproducible step-by-step protocol** (wax-up → mock-up → ~0.2 mm enamel prep → refractory-die feldspathic → HF/phosphoric/silane + 3-step adhesive + light-cured cement).
- Real-world demonstration that **cotton-roll isolation** can support long-term veneer success.
- Restates and clinically anchors the **≥50% enamel substrate / ≥70% enamel margin** adhesion threshold.

## Methodology

Single illustrative clinical case with serial photographic follow-up at baseline, 3 years, and 12+ years. Minimally invasive enamel-confined preparation; thin (<0.5 mm) feldspathic laminates on refractory dies (Creation CC, shade A1). No control or comparison group — evidence weight is **case-report** (illustrative, not generalizable).

## Results

- **>12 years** in function, no repairs.
- Only finding: subtle marginal staining attributed to age-related gingival apical migration; no esthetic/functional impact.
- Cervical prep ~**0.2 mm** enamel; laminate **<0.5 mm**.
- Ceramic etch HF 90 s + 37% phosphoric 30 s clean + silane 60 s; cement light-cured 1000 mW/cm² × 40 s/surface.
- No secondary caries, pulpitis/necrosis, or postoperative sensitivity at any timepoint.
