## One-line Summary

In-vitro study (n=42 maxillary molars, 6 groups) of CAD/CAM IPS e.max CAD lithium disilicate occlusal veneers: 1 mm + Monobond etch & prime achieved highest fracture load (1644.7 N); 0.5 mm veneers surpass posterior masticatory forces (400–800 N) with HF and Monobond surface treatments; Monobond etch & prime recommended over HF for biological safety.

## 한줄요약

In-vitro 연구 (n=42 대구치): CAD/CAM 리튬 디실리케이트 (Lithium Disilicate, LD) 교합면 비니어 두께(0.5 mm vs 1 mm) × 표면처리[불산 (Hydrofluoric Acid, HF), 인산불화물 (Acidulated Phosphate Fluoride, APF), 자가산화 프라이머 (Monobond etch & prime)] — 1 mm + Monobond etch & prime 최고 파절 하중 (Fracture Load) 1644.7 N; 0.5 mm도 교합력 수준(400–800 N) 상회; APF+0.5 mm군(962 N)은 이갈이 환자에 비권장; HF 생물학적 위험성 근거로 Monobond etch & prime 권장.

## Summary

This in-vitro study investigated the combined effects of restoration thickness (0.5 mm vs 1 mm) and surface conditioning protocol (HF acid, APF acid, Monobond etch & prime) on the fracture resistance of CAD/CAM-fabricated lithium disilicate (IPS e.max CAD) occlusal veneers bonded to maxillary molars.

Forty-two extracted maxillary molars were divided into 6 groups (n=7 each) and subjected to artificial aging (75-day water storage + 240,000 cyclic loading cycles at 49 N / 1.7 Hz, simulating ~1 year in vivo) before fracture testing under a universal testing machine.

Both thickness (p<0.0001) and surface treatment (p=0.002) significantly affected fracture load, while their interaction did not (p=0.785). The 1 mm groups consistently outperformed 0.5 mm groups. Among surface treatments, Monobond etch & prime > HF > APF at both thicknesses.

Key clinical finding: 0.5 mm lithium disilicate occlusal veneers treated with HF or Monobond etch & prime achieve fracture loads well above molar masticatory forces (400–800 N), supporting their use as minimally invasive restorations. APF-treated 0.5 mm veneers (962 N mean) are marginally above parafunctional forces and not recommended for bruxists.

Monobond etch & prime is recommended as the preferred surface treatment due to equivalent/superior fracture resistance compared to HF plus elimination of HF's biological hazards.

## Key Contributions

- **Validates 0.5 mm lithium disilicate occlusal veneers** for most clinical scenarios (excluding parafunctional patients with APF surface treatment)
- **Establishes surface treatment hierarchy** for fracture resistance: Monobond etch & prime ≥ HF >> APF at 0.5 mm
- **Safety recommendation**: Monobond etch & prime as HF substitute — equivalent fracture performance, lower biological risk (ammonium polyfluoride more biocompatible than HF)
- **APF-0.5 mm contraindicated** for bruxism/clenching patients — mean FL 962 N approaches bruxism load limit (800 N)
- **Failure mode analysis**: HF/MON groups showed favorable Mode I (crack without debonding, bond intact); APF groups showed Mode II (debonding, bond compromised) + SEM confirmation

## Methodology

- **Material**: IPS e.max CAD (lithium disilicate), milled by 5-axis CAD/CAM (CERAMILL MOTION 2); cemented with Multilink N dual-cure resin cement
- **Occlusal preparation**: 0.5 mm or 1 mm reduction using silicon index verification
- **Surface treatments**:
  - HF: 5% HF acid 20 s + Monobond N universal primer 60 s
  - APF: APF acid 2 min + Monobond N universal primer 60 s
  - MON: Monobond etch & prime (self-etching, agitated 20 s + react 40 s)
- **Tooth surface**: Selective etch (37% phosphoric acid) + Multilink N Primer A+B
- **Aging**: 75-day water storage at 37°C + 240,000 cyclic loading cycles (49 N, 1.7 Hz)
- **Fracture test**: Instron 3345 universal testing machine, 5 mm metal sphere on central fossa, 0.5 mm/min crosshead speed
- **Statistics**: Two-way ANOVA + Post Hoc Tukey HSD (SPSS v25)

## Results

**Fracture load (Mean ± SD, N)**:

| Group | Fracture Load (N) |
|---|---|
| MON-1 mm | 1644.7 ± 155.3 |
| HF-1 mm | 1514.6 ± 212.5 |
| APF-1 mm | 1405.8 ± 172.7 |
| MON-0.5 mm | 1277.0 ± 114.0 |
| HF-0.5 mm | 1166.2 ± 211.0 |
| APF-0.5 mm | 962.0 ± 249.6 |

- Thickness effect (1 mm > 0.5 mm) significant for all surface treatments: HF p=0.018, MON p=0.011, APF p=0.001
- Surface treatment effect at same thickness: only MON-0.5 vs APF-0.5 significant (p=0.042)
- No significant difference between MON-0.5 and HF-0.5 (p>0.05)
- All groups exceed posterior masticatory force range (400–800 N) except APF-0.5 mm is borderline for bruxists

**Failure modes**: Mode I (crack without debonding) dominant in HF/MON groups; Mode II (debonding) in APF groups — confirming APF compromises ceramic-cement-tooth bond integrity.

**Clinical reference thresholds**: Normal posterior mastication 400–800 N; bruxism can approach 800 N.
