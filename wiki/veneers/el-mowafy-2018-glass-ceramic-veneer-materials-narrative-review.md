## Summary

El-Mowafy et al. (2018) provide a concise 5-page narrative update on porcelain veneers for the Dental and Medical Problems journal. The review traces the history of ceramic veneers from Calamia's introduction in the early 1980s through the current era of CAD/CAM milled glass-ceramic. It covers the full clinical workflow: indications/contraindications, preparation design, impression/temporization, material selection, surface treatment (HF etching + silane), cementation, and occlusal adjustment. Longevity data from multiple studies are synthesized narratively, with the most comprehensive figure from Morimoto et al.'s SR reporting 89% survival at 9 years across 13 studies.

A key practical finding is that preparation margin location is the single most powerful failure predictor: margins on dentin showed a 10-fold higher failure rate than margins remaining on enamel. The review advocates maintenance of enamel margins and enamel preparation depth (typically 0.3 mm) as the cornerstone of technique.

## Key Contributions

1. Synthesizes survival data through 2018; identifies 89% (9-year SR) as the evidence benchmark.
2. Quantifies the dentin-margin penalty: 10× higher failure rate vs. enamel margin.
3. Discusses emergence of CAD/CAM (e.max) glass-ceramic veneers and notes their superior strength over feldspathic.
4. Recommends sandblasting + HF etching + silane as optimal intaglio surface treatment.
5. Introduces ultrasonic seating as a technique to minimize film thickness and future leakage.

## Methodology

Narrative review; no systematic search or quality appraisal. Covers clinical literature on:
- History, materials, indications, contraindications
- Preparation (feather-edge finish line, enamel confinement)
- Impression, temporization, try-in
- Surface treatment and cementation
- Gingival response
- Longevity studies (5, 7, 10, 12, 15-year data)

|  — glass-ceramic | | 9 yr | 94% |
|  — feldspathic | | 9 yr | 87% |

**Failure modes (Morimoto SR):** debonding 2%, fracture 4%, caries 1%, discoloration 2%.

**Risk stratification:**
- Dentin margin → 10× higher failure vs. enamel margin (Gurel et al.)
- Bruxism → survival ≈ 60% (Magne et al.; comparable to PFM in same patients)

**Material strengths:**
- Feldspathic: best esthetics, layering flexibility; technique-sensitive, void-prone
- Pressed ceramic: higher accuracy, fewer internal defects
- CAD/CAM milled (lithium disilicate): strongest; single-opacity limitation decreasing with multi-opacity blocks
