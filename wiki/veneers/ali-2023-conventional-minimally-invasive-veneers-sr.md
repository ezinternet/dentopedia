## One-line Summary

Systematic review of 4 comparative studies on conventional veneers (CVs) vs minimal/no-preparation veneers (MPVs): MPVs demonstrated superior or equivalent survival rates and success periods; microleakage, marginal fit, and color stability varied by preparation technique and material, with feldspathic porcelain at 0.2–0.5 mm performing well as an MPV substrate.

## Summary

Ali (2023) conducted a PRISMA-compliant systematic review using 8 databases (PubMed, Embase, Scopus, Web of Science, Cochrane Library, CINAHL, ProQuest, Google Scholar) and the PECOS protocol to compare conventional veneers (CVs, 0.3–1.0 mm) with minimal/no-preparation veneers (MPVs, 0.2–0.5 mm). Four comparative studies met inclusion criteria (minimum 1-year follow-up). Against the stated hypothesis that CVs would outperform MPVs, findings showed MPVs demonstrated enhanced survival rates and longer mean success periods. MPVs, particularly ultrathin contact-lens type fieldspathic porcelain veneers (0.2–0.3 mm), offered advantages including elimination of anesthesia, no need for temporaries, and preservation of natural dental architecture. Microleakage and marginal fit outcomes varied depending on preparation technique. Color stability was influenced by the interplay between material properties and fabrication methods. The review concludes that MPVs represent viable long-term restorative options when patient management and preparation technique are optimized, while acknowledging the diversity of study methodologies limits definitive conclusions.

## Key Contributions

- Synthesized comparative evidence for CV vs MPV across 6 clinical outcome dimensions
- Refuted the prior assumption of CV superiority: MPVs showed equal or better outcomes in survival and longevity
- Defined thickness criteria: MPV 0.2–0.5 mm (feldspathic) vs CV 0.3–1.0 mm; ultra-thin MPV 0.2–0.3 mm
- Highlighted no-anesthesia and no-temporary advantages of genuine MPVs
- Identified feldspathic porcelain as the substrate of choice for true ultra-thin MPVs
- Called out the dearth of clinical trials specifically on no-prep veneers (vs minimal-prep)

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review (no meta-analysis); PRISMA-compliant; PECOS-structured research question
- **Databases**: PubMed, Embase, Scopus, Web of Science, Cochrane Library, CINAHL, ProQuest, Google Scholar (8 databases)
- **Search approach**: Boolean MeSH term strategy with "AND/OR" operators
- **Studies included**: 4 comparative assessments
- **Minimum follow-up**: 1 year (clinical and in vitro studies both eligible)
- **Outcomes**: esthetics, longevity, periodontal health, patient satisfaction, microleakage, marginal fit, cement thickness, color stability
- **Hypothesis**: CVs have better clinical outcomes than MPVs (rejected by findings)

## Results

| Outcome Domain | Conventional Veneer | Minimal/No-Prep Veneer |
|---|---|---|
| Survival rate | Baseline | Higher (MPV advantage) |
| Mean success period | Baseline | Extended (MPV advantage) |
| Microleakage | Varies by technique | Favorable in some MPV studies |
| Marginal fit | Depends on preparation | Acceptable for ultra-thin types |
| Color stability | Material-dependent | Feldspathic MPV maintains integrity |
| Patient satisfaction | Standard | MPV: no anesthesia, no temporaries |
| Periodontal health | Standard | MPV: low plaque accumulation |

- **Preparation thickness**: MPV 0.2–0.5 mm; CV 0.3–1.0 mm; contact-lens MPV 0.2–0.3 mm
- **Substrate of choice for MPV**: feldspathic porcelain (allows ultrathin fabrication, HF-etchable)
- **Limitations**: 4 studies only; heterogeneous methodologies; genuine no-prep evidence scarce
