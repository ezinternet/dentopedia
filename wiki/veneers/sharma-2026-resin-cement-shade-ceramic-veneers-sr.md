## One-line Summary

In-vitro systematic review (10 studies, 2011–2021; no meta-analysis) showing resin cement shade and translucency materially shift the final color of ceramic laminate veneers — white-opaque/high-chroma cements drive the largest ΔE (often above the 3.3 acceptability threshold), worsened by thin (0.3–0.5 mm) and high-translucency veneers, while try-in pastes poorly predict the cured cement.

## 한줄요약

In-vitro 계통고찰 (10편, 2011–2021; 메타분석 없음) — 레진 시멘트의 색조·투명도가 세라믹 라미네이트 비니어의 최종 색을 유의하게 바꾼다. 백색-불투명·고채도 시멘트가 ΔE를 가장 크게 (흔히 임상 허용역 3.3 초과) 올리고, 얇은(0.3–0.5 mm)·고투명도 비니어에서 효과가 증폭되며, try-in 페이스트는 경화된 시멘트 색을 잘 예측하지 못한다.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.3389/fdmed.2026.1789416](https://doi.org/10.3389/fdmed.2026.1789416)), this PRISMA 2020 systematic review (PROSPERO CRD42023450540) screened 229 records and included 10 in-vitro studies (2011–2021) that measured, with objective CIE L*a*b* spectrophotometry, how resin luting cement shade and translucency change the final color of ceramic laminate veneers. Substantial heterogeneity (cement systems, ceramics, veneer thickness, aging, ΔE method) precluded a meta-analysis, so the evidence is synthesized qualitatively. The consistent finding is that the cement is an **active optical component**, not a passive luting medium: white-opaque (WO) and high-chroma/high-value cements produce the greatest color change (ΔE often exceeding the ~3.3 clinical acceptability threshold), while translucent/low-chroma cements stay esthetically favorable (ΔE < 3.3). The effect is amplified by thin veneers (0.3–0.5 mm) and high-translucency ceramics, which transmit more light from the cement and substrate, and opaque/high-value cements raise ΔE most over light backgrounds. Two practical hazards stand out: aging causes marked discoloration (VarioLink ΔE = 10.4 after aging vs ceramic-alone ΔE = 0.9), and try-in pastes poorly predict the cured cement (ΔE 0.32–11.49 mismatch) because curing alters refractive index and polymer matrix. Risk of bias was low in 3 studies and moderate in the rest; almost all relied on ΔE*ab rather than the perceptually weighted ΔE00. Clinically: choose the luting cement based on the *actual cured cement* (not the try-in paste alone), accounting for veneer thickness/translucency and substrate shade — favor translucent/low-chroma cements for thin high-translucency anterior veneers and reserve opaque/high-value cements for masking dark substrates where some color shift is acceptable.

## Key Contributions

- Establishes the resin cement as an active optical determinant of cemented ceramic veneer color, not a passive adhesive.
- Ranks the drivers of cement-induced color change: cement opacity/value/chroma > veneer thickness > ceramic translucency > substrate shade.
- Quantifies the try-in paste ↔ cured cement mismatch (ΔE 0.32–11.49), challenging paste-based shade selection.
- Flags long-term color instability under aging as a distinct esthetic-survival risk.
- Surfaces a methodological gap — near-universal ΔE*ab use, no consistent ΔE00, frequent missing sample-size justification/blinding.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review (PRISMA 2020 + Cochrane Handbook); PROSPERO CRD42023450540.
- **Search**: PubMed, Web of Science, ProQuest, Google Scholar to March 2023.
- **Inclusion**: in-vitro studies of resin-based luting agents on ceramic laminate veneers with objective color measurement (CIE L*a*b*, spectrophotometry).
- **Exclusion**: full-coverage restorations; subjective/visual shade assessment.
- **Risk of bias**: QUIN tool (in-vitro-specific) — 3 low, rest moderate.
- **Selection**: 229 records → 10 studies (sample sizes 10–392 specimens).
- **Synthesis**: qualitative — no meta-analysis due to heterogeneity.
- **Materials covered**: ceramics feldspathic / IPS Empress / e.max HT & LT / ZLS / LDS; cements RelyX, VarioLink, Panavia, Maxcem, eCement, Choice 2.

## Results

- **Cement opacity/chroma is the dominant driver**: WO and high-chroma/high-value cements → greatest ΔE, often > 3.3 (acceptability threshold); translucent/low-chroma cements → ΔE typically < 3.3.
- **Veneer thickness modulates**: thinner veneers (0.3–0.5 mm) amplify cement-shade influence vs ≥1.0 mm; high-translucency ceramics accentuate it.
- **Cement–substrate interaction**: opaque/high-value cements raise ΔE most over light backgrounds (Jankar, Chen, Yildirim).
- **Aging discoloration**: VarioLink ΔE = 10.4 after aging vs ceramic-alone ΔE = 0.9 (Hoorizad) → long-term color-stability concern.
- **Try-in pastes unreliable**: ΔE 0.32–11.49 between try-in paste and cured cement (Aldahlawi).
- **Per-study ΔE ranges**: ~3.0 (Alqahtani); 1.37–2.11 (Lee); <3.3 to >4.0 (Xing); 1.93–3.04 (Hernandes); 0.9–10.4 (Hoorizad, aging); 1.6–3.4 (Turgut); up to 7.16 (Chen).
- **Evidence quality**: in-vitro, moderate; most used ΔE*ab, none consistently ΔE00.
