## One-line Summary

Narrative review (Bioinformation 2026) comparing no-preparation vs conventional porcelain veneers: both reach high survival (91–100%; no-prep mean ~97% vs conventional ~92%, no-prep success up to 97.4% when enamel-bonded), but no-prep is contraindicated for intrinsic discoloration, misalignment, or major morphological/contour correction — supporting a patient-specific, enamel-centered approach where the two techniques are complementary rather than competing.

## Summary

Chandode et al. (2026) review current clinical evidence on no-preparation / minimal-preparation veneers (MPVs) versus conventional veneers (CVs). The central message is that enamel preservation has become the organizing principle of veneer dentistry: ultra-thin no-prep restorations bond primarily to **sound enamel**, which improves adhesive reliability and lowers the need for anesthesia and provisionals, while conventional veneers require 0.3–1.0 mm reduction (occasionally into dentin) that is irreversible and can cause postoperative sensitivity. Both techniques achieve high survival (91–100%), with no-prep showing a slightly higher mean (~97% vs ~92%) and fewer catastrophic failures — but the authors stress these figures come from heterogeneous studies with differing survival/failure definitions (some count trauma as failure), so cross-study comparison is fragile. The decisive variable is **case selection**: MPVs suit well-aligned teeth with minimal esthetic demand, whereas CVs remain first-choice for difficult discolorations, morphological alterations, and alignment deviations where controlled reduction enables masking and contour accuracy. The review reframes MPV and CV as complementary, not opposing, approaches.

## Key Contributions

- **Indication map** separating MPV (well-aligned, minimal demand) from CV (intrinsic discoloration, misalignment, morphological correction needing controlled reduction).
- **Survival benchmark**: 91–100% both techniques; no-prep mean ~97% vs conventional ~92%, no-prep success up to 97.4% when enamel-bonded.
- **Preparation design as the deciding factor** for MPV outcome; additive, enamel-only approach preserves contour and avoids over-contouring of the emergence profile.
- Explicit warning on **methodological heterogeneity** behind reported survival ranges.

## Methodology

Narrative/clinical-considerations review of peer-reviewed studies; representative studies tabulated for clinical longevity, esthetic outcome, and tooth preservation (Table 1, source PDF). No formal search protocol or pooled meta-analysis — evidence weight is interpretive (narrative-review).

## Results

- No-prep success **up to 97.4%** when bonded to enamel in well-selected cases.
- Survival **91–100%** both; no-prep ~**97%** vs conventional ~**92%**, fewer catastrophic failures with no-prep.
- Conventional reduction **0.3–1.0 mm** for masking/contour control.
- No-prep advantages: enamel preservation, reduced postoperative sensitivity, low biological cost.
- No-prep contraindications: intrinsic discoloration, misalignment, significant morphological discrepancy.
