## Summary

Sorensen et al. (1992) conducted one of the earliest controlled in-vitro comparisons of porcelain veneer fabrication techniques with respect to marginal accuracy and sealing performance. Maxillary incisors with matched enamel quantity received uniform 0.5 mm intraenamel labial preparations. Veneers fabricated by either the platinum foil or refractory die technique were etched, silanated, and cemented with composite resin; margins were finished and polished. After thermocycling (1000×, 37°C), specimens were stained with silver nitrate, sectioned buccolingually and mesiodistally, and examined at ×250 magnification.

The platinum foil technique produced significantly better vertical marginal fidelity but significantly more overcontouring at the margin. Both techniques exhibited microleakage universally at the tooth–composite resin interface, whereas the porcelain–composite resin interface remained essentially sealed. Critically, no significant correlation was found between the size of the marginal gap and the extent of microleakage — suggesting that microleakage at the tooth–cement interface is a material/bond property, not simply a gap-size problem.

## Key Contributions

1. Established a measurable marginal advantage for platinum foil over refractory die in terms of vertical gap accuracy.
2. Demonstrated that overcontouring is a trade-off with the platinum foil technique.
3. Identified the tooth–composite resin interface (not the porcelain–cement interface) as the primary site of microleakage in bonded ceramic veneers.
4. Showed that marginal gap size and microleakage magnitude are not correlated — implications for how marginal fitness should be clinically interpreted.

## Methodology

- **Specimens**: Matched maxillary incisors (size and enamel quantity); 0.5 mm uniform intraenamel labial reduction.
- **Groups**: Platinum foil veneers vs. refractory die veneers.
- **Luting**: HF etching + silane + composite resin cement; margins finished and polished.
- **Aging**: 37°C water storage + 1000 thermocycles.
- **Microleakage**: Silver nitrate staining; sections imaged at ×250.
- **Marginal measurement**: Vertical marginal gap and overcontour quantified at multiple sites per section.

## Results

| Variable | Platinum Foil | Refractory Die |
|---|---|---|
| Vertical marginal fidelity | Significantly better | Inferior |
| Overcontouring | Significantly more | Less |
| Microleakage — tooth/composite interface | Universal | Universal |
| Microleakage — porcelain/composite interface | Negligible | Negligible |
| Gap size vs. leakage correlation | Not significant | Not significant |
