## One-line Summary

Narrative review (55 clinical studies, 2000–2023): silica-based ceramic LVs achieve 72–100% survival across follow-up periods; dentin exposure, bruxism, and adhesive protocol quality are the primary modifiable failure determinants; no ≥3-year clinical data exist for zirconia LVs.

## Summary

This narrative review from Nihon University (Tokyo, Japan) surveyed 55 clinical studies on laminate veneer (LV) survival and success rates published between 2000 and April 2023. Key findings include that silica-based ceramics — feldspathic porcelain, leucite-reinforced glass ceramic (LRG), and lithium disilicate ceramic (LDS) — remain the materials of choice based on available clinical evidence. Survival rates decline with observation time (95.1–100% at ≤5y; 82.5–100% at ≤10y; 72.6–95.0% at >10y). Dentin exposure is a critical prognostic factor, and immediate dentin sealing (IDS) significantly improves outcomes. Bruxism substantially reduces survival unless managed with occlusal splints (89.1% with splint vs 63.9% without at 8y). HF etching plus silane priming is the established gold standard for ceramic surface treatment. No adequately powered clinical trial (≥3y) for zirconia LVs was identified.

## Key Contributions

- **Material evidence hierarchy**: Feldspathic (64–100%), LRG (76–100%), LDS (87–100%) across clinical studies — silica-based ceramics recommended; indirect composites inferior long-term (75% at 10y)
- **IDS protocol quantification**: Gresnigt 11y data — with IDS 96.4% survival vs 81.8% without; dentin exposure >50% is a risk category
- **Bruxism management**: Occlusal device mandatory for bruxism patients; 25-point survival difference at 8y without device
- **Preparation design evidence**: No-prep variants in feldspathic porcelain show competitive outcomes; overlap designs theoretically protect incisal edge
- **Failure taxonomy**: Debonding/fracture dominant <5y (adhesive errors); caries/marginal breakdown dominant >5y (marginal integrity issues)
- **Evidence gap flagged**: Zero qualifying studies for zirconia LVs and insufficient data for resin-matrix hybrid materials

## Methodology

- **Type**: Narrative review; not a systematic review or meta-analysis
- **Databases**: MEDLINE/PubMed, Web of Science, Cochrane Library, Google Scholar
- **Search window**: 2000 to April 2023
- **Inclusion**: Clinical trials with ≥3 year observation period
- **Exclusion**: Case reports, case series, reviews, abstracts, in vitro studies, observational studies
- **Reviewers**: 5 independent researchers; disagreements resolved by discussion
- **Studies included**: 55 articles
- **Subgroups analyzed**: Observation period, material, preparation design, dentin exposure, tooth vitality, jaw arch (maxillary/mandibular), tooth type (anterior/premolar), bruxism status

## Results

### Survival by Time Period
| Follow-up | Survival range |
|---|---|
| ≤5 years | 95.1–100% |
| ≤10 years | 82.5–100% |
| >10 years | 72.6–95.0% |

Annual intervention rate (Rinke): rose from 0.6 to 1.78 between 3y and 10y.

### Survival by Material
| Material | Range | Articles |
|---|---|---|
| Feldspathic porcelain | 64–100% | 21 |
| LRG (e.g. IPS Empress) | 76–100% | 16 |
| LDS (e.g. IPS e.max) | 87–100% | 10 |
| Indirect composite resin | 75% (10y) | 2 |

### Key Modifiers
- **IDS**: 96.4% (with IDS) vs 81.8% (without IDS) at 11y — Gresnigt
- **Bruxism + splint**: 89.1% vs 63.9% (without splint) at 8y — Faus-Matoses (n=364)
- **Vitality**: Nonvital teeth have higher failure risk per Beier; conflicting results in other studies
- **Location**: Premolars may carry higher failure risk (narrower occlusal contact area, higher stress)

### Surface Treatment
- HF etching (5–10%) + silane: gold standard for all silica-based ceramics
- Additional adhesive application to LV intaglio: inconsistent evidence — may improve wettability but laboratory SR shows no bond-strength benefit after HF+silane
- Abutment: phosphoric acid etching (37–40%) × 15–30 sec; IDS for dentin-exposed teeth

### Failure Modes
- **<1 year**: Debonding at 1 week–3 months, fracture at 1 month — attributed to adhesive errors
- **1–5 years**: Fracture, chipping, endodontic complications
- **>5 years**: Caries, marginal disintegration, retention loss, shade mismatch, gingival recession

### Complications in Service (sub-survival)
- **<5 years**: Chipping, marginal discoloration
- **>5 years**: Marginal disintegration, marginal defects, gingival recession
- Postoperative sensitivity: observed but typically resolves within 3 weeks
