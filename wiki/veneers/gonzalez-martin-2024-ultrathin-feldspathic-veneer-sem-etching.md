## One-line Summary

Pilot SEM study (Int J Periodontics Restorative Dent 2024; DOI 10.11607/prd.6574) evaluating HF etching time (0/90/120/150 s; 9.6% HF) on intaglio surface microroughness of ultrathin feldspathic veneers fabricated by platinum foil vs. refractory die techniques; 120 s optimal for platinum foil, 90 s for refractory die, and 150 s confers no additional benefit.

## Summary

Gonzalez-Martin et al. (2024) conducted a pilot in-vitro SEM investigation to determine whether optimal hydrofluoric acid (HF) etching time for the intaglio (fitting) surface of ultrathin feldspathic ceramic veneers differs by fabrication technique. Replicas of a maxillary central incisor ultrathin veneer were fabricated using either the platinum foil technique or the refractory die technique. Specimens from each fabrication group were subjected to one of four intaglio surface-etching protocols (9.6% HF): no etching, 90 seconds, 120 seconds, or 150 seconds. Intaglio surfaces were then evaluated by SEM for microroughness pattern.

The 120-second protocol produced a favorable microroughness surface pattern in the platinum foil group, comparable to what was achieved with 90 seconds in the refractory die group. Extending etching time to 150 seconds did not improve the roughness pattern in either group. The unetched controls showed smooth surfaces inadequate for bonding.

This is the first study to demonstrate that the fabrication technique creates structurally different intaglio surfaces, requiring different etching durations to achieve optimal bonding substrate morphology — a clinically actionable distinction for laboratory and chairside protocols.

## Key Contributions

1. Established that platinum foil and refractory die fabrication techniques produce distinct intaglio surface characteristics requiring different optimal HF etching times.
2. Identified 120 s (platinum foil) and 90 s (refractory die) as technique-specific optimal protocols with 9.6% HF.
3. Confirmed that 150 s etching is not beneficial — guideline against over-etching ultrathin feldspathic ceramics.
4. Provided SEM documentation of no-etch control surfaces as the comparator for bonding inadequacy.

## Methodology

- **Model**: Replicas of an ultrathin feldspathic veneer for a maxillary central incisor.
- **Fabrication groups**: Platinum foil technique vs. refractory die technique.
- **Etching groups**: 0 s, 90 s, 120 s, 150 s (all with 9.6% HF).
- **Assessment**: Scanning electron microscopy (SEM) — qualitative/descriptive evaluation of intaglio surface microroughness pattern.
- **Design**: Pilot study — exploratory, no statistical analysis.

## Results

| HF Etching Time | Platinum Foil Intaglio | Refractory Die Intaglio |
|---|---|---|
| 0 s (control) | Smooth — insufficient for bonding | Smooth — insufficient for bonding |
| 90 s | Suboptimal roughness | Optimal roughness pattern |
| 120 s | Optimal roughness pattern | Comparable to 90 s |
| 150 s | No additional improvement | No additional improvement |

- Platinum foil surfaces required a longer etching duration (120 s vs. 90 s) to reach comparable microroughness to refractory die surfaces.
- Over-etching (150 s) does not augment roughness and may risk surface degradation.
