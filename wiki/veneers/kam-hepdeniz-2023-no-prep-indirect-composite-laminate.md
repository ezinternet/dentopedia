## One-line Summary

Prospective case series (n=80 veneers, 35 patients, 84 months): no-prep indirect microhybrid composite laminate veneers achieved 91.3% survival (Kaplan-Meier) with zero tooth fractures, zero secondary caries, and zero antagonist wear; failures limited to 4 debondings and 3 fractures.

## Summary

Kam Hepdeniz and Temel (BMC Oral Health 2023) conducted a 7-year (84-month) prospective case series evaluating 80 no-prep indirect composite laminate veneers in 35 patients at Suleyman Demirel University, Turkey. All veneers were fabricated from microhybrid indirect composite (Gradia, GC) by a single technician and placed by one experienced clinician without any enamel preparation. Indications were diastema closure (80%), wedge tooth anomalies (11.3%), and re-shaping (8.8%). Veneers were cemented with light-cured resin cement (Choice 2, Bisco) using All-Bond 3 adhesive (Bisco). Clinical evaluation used the modified USPHS criteria at baseline, 24 months, and 84 months. The overall survival rate was 91.3% (Kaplan-Meier), with a mean retention time of 78.3 months. Seven absolute failures were recorded: 4 debondings (in 2 patients) and 3 fractures (each following patient-reported hard food biting). Critically, zero tooth fractures, secondary caries, antagonist wear, or postoperative sensitivity were observed at any recall. Surface roughness (41/73 veneers scored 1 at 84 months) and marginal discoloration (15/73) were the most common minor complications; both were polishable and acceptable. Compared to published composite veneer survival with preparation (87% at 3 years, 75% at 10 years), the no-prep enamel-only bonding approach outperformed, attributable to optimal enamel bond strength.

## Key Contributions

- **Longest no-prep indirect composite veneer cohort**: 84-month prospective data fills a gap previously identified in Lim et al. SR+MA (2023), which had no long-term no-prep specific data
- **91.3% survival exceeds prepared composite historical data**: supports the enamel-bonding hypothesis that preserving enamel improves adhesive durability and reduces failure
- **Safety profile**: absolute absence of tooth fracture, caries, and antagonist enamel wear over 7 years — a favorable risk profile compared to ceramic veneers requiring enamel reduction
- **Minor complications are manageable**: surface roughness and marginal discoloration were treatable with polishing, not requiring replacement
- **Failure mode characterization**: debonding linked to planning errors and early cementation issues; fractures associated exclusively with patient non-compliance (hard food)

## Methodology

- **Design**: Single-center prospective case series (ethics 2014/420)
- **n**: 80 veneers, 35 patients; 73 veneers in 33 patients at 84 months
- **Population**: 19–45 year-olds with diastema, wedge anomalies, or re-shaping needs; excluded bruxism, severe malocclusion, crossbite, heavy discoloration, large defects
- **Material**: Indirect microhybrid composite Gradia (GC); lab-fabricated polychromatic layering
- **Cementation**: 32% phosphoric acid etch on enamel → All-Bond 3 (A+B) → Choice 2 light-cure resin cement → rubber dam isolation; 40 s light-cure from 4 aspects
- **No preparation**: zero enamel reduction; all margins on intact enamel
- **Evaluation**: Two calibrated clinicians; modified USPHS (7 categories); disagreements resolved by consensus
- **Statistics**: Kaplan-Meier survival analysis; Wilcoxon Signed Ranks test (α=0.05)

## Results

|---|---|

| Mean retention time | 78.3 months |
| Absolute failures | 7/80 (4 debonding, 3 fracture) |
| Tooth fracture | 0/73 (100% score-0) |
| Secondary caries | 0/73 (100% score-0) |

**Significant deterioration vs baseline**: marginal adaptation (p=0.008), color match (p<0.001), marginal discoloration (p<0.001), surface roughness (p<0.001), fracture of restoration (p=0.001).
**Marginal adaptation 24→84 months**: NOT significant (p=0.165) — late marginal failure did not progress.
