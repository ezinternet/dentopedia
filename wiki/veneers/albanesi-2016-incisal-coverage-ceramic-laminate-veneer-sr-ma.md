## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 8 clinical studies (1,145 screened): incisal coverage vs non-coverage yielded 88% vs 91% veneer survival; OR 1.25 (non-significant) — both preparation designs are safe and effective.

## Summary

Albanesi et al. (2016) conducted the first SR+MA focused exclusively on whether incisal coverage in ceramic laminate veneer preparations affects clinical survival. Searching MEDLINE and Cochrane from 1977 to June 2016 (1,145 initial records), 8 clinical studies met inclusion criteria. The estimated survival rate was 88% for preparations with incisal coverage versus 91% for those without. The odds ratio for incisal coverage was 1.25 — not statistically significant. No randomized controlled trials directly comparing both designs were identified. No studies on crystalline ceramic (fully crystalline) veneers were found meeting inclusion criteria. The authors concluded that both preparation designs produce high survival rates and are effective conservative treatments, while calling for standardized future RCTs.

## Key Contributions

- Answered a specific clinical question: does incisal coverage design matter for ceramic veneer survival? Answer: no significant difference
- Only 8 studies qualified due to requiring explicit preparation-design description and failure data — highlighting a critical gap in literature reporting standards
- No RCTs comparing incisal vs non-incisal coverage existed as of June 2016
- Ceramic veneers remove only 3%–30% of coronal tooth structure by weight — the most conservative indirect restoration option
- Three preparation design types described: feathered/window (incisal edge preserved), butt joint (incisal edge reduced), palatal chamfer (extends palatal)

## Methodology

- **Search**: MEDLINE (PubMed) + Cochrane Central; 1977 to June 5, 2016; no language restriction; PRISMA-compliant; PROSPERO CRD42015016606
- **Records screened**: 1,145 → 181 full-text review → 8 included
- **Study types**: RCTs and prospective/retrospective cohort studies in humans
- **Risk of bias**: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS)
- **Statistics**: Cochran Q + I² for heterogeneity; metaregression; meta-analysis for survival rates by preparation design
- **Exclusion note**: No crystalline ceramic veneer studies identified

## Results

|---|---|---|
| With incisal coverage | 88% | 1.25 (NS) |
| Without incisal coverage | 91% | Reference |

- Incisal coverage OR 1.25: 95% CI crosses 1.0 → not statistically significant
- Both designs demonstrated "high survival rates"
- Most variation between studies arose from differences in follow-up duration and patient selection, not preparation design
- The absence of RCTs means all included evidence is from observational studies with inherent confounding

**Clinical bottom line**: Clinicians may choose incisal coverage or not based on esthetic needs, occlusal factors, and patient anatomy rather than survival prognosis — evidence does not favor either design. However, the evidence base is weak (observational only, heterogeneous reporting).
