## One-line Summary

In vitro study (n=24, 3 ceramic groups) comparing Er;Cr:YSGG laser debonding of 0.5 mm ultrathin CAD/CAM occlusal veneers: debonding time equivalent across Emax, LiSi, and Katana STML zirconia (~87 s, p=0.995), but 60% of GC Initial LiSi samples were damaged (fractured/cracked) vs. 0% for the other two materials.

## 한줄요약

In vitro 연구(n=24): Er;Cr:YSGG 레이저를 이용한 0.5 mm 초박형 교합면 비니어(Occlusal Veneer) 탈접(Laser Debonding) 시간은 리튬 디실리케이트(LD), 초고밀도 LiSi, 반투명 지르코니아(STML) 간 차이 없음(~87초, p=0.995); 그러나 LiSi 표본 60%가 균열·파절 손상 — Emax·지르코니아는 손상 0%.

## Summary

This in vitro study (BMC Oral Health 2024) evaluated whether Er;Cr:YSGG laser can efficiently debond 0.5 mm ultrathin CAD/CAM occlusal veneers made from three different ceramics, and whether any material is damaged in the process. Twenty-four extracted upper first molars received standardized flat occlusal preparations and were restored with veneers milled from IPS Emax CAD, GC Initial LiSi (highly condensed lithium disilicate), or Katana STML zirconia (n=8 each). All veneers were cemented with dual-cure resin cement (Breeze) after material-specific surface treatment, then debonded using a scanning Er;Cr:YSGG protocol (6 W, 20 Hz, 2 mm distance). Debonding time and stereomicroscopic damage were recorded.

Key findings: debonding time was statistically equivalent across all three materials (~87 seconds; p=0.995), attributable to their matched translucency parameters and thin (0.5 mm) restoration profile. However, material integrity post-debonding differed dramatically: Emax and zirconia were completely undamaged, while 60% of LiSi samples showed cracks or catastrophic fractures. The proposed mechanism is that HDM (High-Density Micronization) technology — microsized, uniformly dispersed crystals — makes LiSi more susceptible to laser-induced crystal disintegration than the larger, randomly dispersed crystals of conventional lithium disilicate.

## Key Contributions

- Establishes that Er;Cr:YSGG laser (scanning method, 6 W) is material-agnostic in debonding time for translucency-matched 0.5 mm veneers
- Identifies GC Initial LiSi as high-risk for laser debonding: 60% damage rate (vs. 0% for Emax and Katana STML)
- Proposes HDM crystal architecture as the structural mechanism for LiSi fragility during laser irradiation
- Supports retrieval and reuse potential of Emax and zirconia veneers via laser debonding; questions same for LiSi
- Confirms scanning method superiority over point-contact for minimizing heat conduction

## Methodology

- **Design**: In vitro, comparative, n=8 per group (total n=24); one-way ANOVA + Tukey
- **Substrate**: Freshly extracted upper first molars, standardized flat occlusal preparation (dentin centrally, enamel peripherally)
- **Materials**: IPS Emax CAD (Group A), GC Initial LiSi (Group B), Katana STML zirconia (Group C)
- **Fabrication**: 5-axis milling (350i PRO, imes-icore), 0.5 mm standardized thickness, Exocad design
- **Surface treatment**: HF acid + silane (Emax, LiSi); Al2O3 sandblast + MDP primer (zirconia)
- **Cementation**: Dual-cure resin cement (Breeze, Pentron), 250 g load, selective enamel etching
- **Laser**: Er;Cr:YSGG Biolase Waterlase iPlus 2.0, 2780 nm, 6 W, 20 Hz, 80% water/60% air, MGG6 Saffire tip 600 μm, non-contact scanning mode, 2 mm distance
- **Outcomes**: Debonding time (s); stereomicroscopic damage classification (intact/cracked/fractured); failure mode (adhesive/mixed/cohesive)

## Results

| Material | Debonding time (s, mean±SD) | Intact (%) | Cracked (%) | Fractured (%) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| IPS Emax CAD | 86.94 ± 20.63 | 100% | 0% | 0% |
| GC Initial LiSi | 86.14 ± 25.16 | 40% | 20% | 40% |
| Katana STML | 87.52 ± 20.45 | 100% | 0% | 0% |

- **Group difference in time**: p = 0.995 (no significant difference)
- **Failure mode**: No cohesive failures in any group; mixed failure predominated (Emax 6/8, LiSi 7/8); adhesive failure more common in zirconia (5/8)
- **Clinical interpretation**: Er;Cr:YSGG laser safe for Emax and zirconia retrieval/reuse; LiSi requires further investigation
