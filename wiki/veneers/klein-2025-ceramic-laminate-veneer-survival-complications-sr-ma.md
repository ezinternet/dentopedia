## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 29 studies (7,753 veneers): LDS ceramic laminate veneers show best long-term profile (96.81% survival, technical complication 6.1% at 10.4 years); all materials comparable in survival; zirconia 100% at 2.6 years but no long-term data.

## Summary

Klein et al. (2025) performed the most comprehensive SR+MA to date on ceramic laminate veneers, covering four material types — feldspathic, leucite-reinforced glass-ceramic (LRGC), lithium disilicate (LDS), and zirconia — across 29 studies (41 study arms), involving 7,753 veneers in 986 patients observed for 1.0–20.7 years. The pooled survival rates at approximately 10.4 years were: feldspathic 96.13%, LRGC 93.70%, LDS 96.81%, and zirconia 100% (at 2.6 years only). No statistically significant difference in survival was found between materials. However, complication rates differed markedly: feldspathic had the highest technical complication rate (41.48%), followed by LRGC (29.87%), while LDS showed dramatically lower technical complications (6.1%). Biological complications were lowest for LDS (0.45%). Zirconia showed no complications at 2.6 years but long-term evidence is absent. Digital workflow studies were too few for separate analysis.

## Key Contributions

- First SR+MA to simultaneously compare all four major ceramic laminate veneer materials at three time horizons (short, mid, long-term)
- LDS emerges as preferred material for long-term success despite similar survival to feldspathic — distinction lies in complication burden
- Zirconia is promising but requires long-term clinical validation
- Separated technical, esthetic, and biological complication categories for clinical granularity
- All materials showed high survival (>93%), making material choice a complication-prevention decision rather than a survival decision
- Published as open access (CC BY-NC-ND)

## Methodology

- **Search**: MEDLINE, Cochrane, Scopus, WHO ICTRP, ClinicalTrials.gov; inception to February 19, 2024; hand-search of reference lists
- **Records**: 12,592 titles (+ 14 hand-searched) → 7,913 after deduplication → 98 full-text → 29 included
- **Study types**: 2 RCTs, 13 prospective, 14 retrospective; 41 study arms total
- **Sample**: 7,753 veneers, 986 patients, age 17–87, follow-up 1.0–20.7 years
- **Countries**: 12 countries including Belgium, Brazil, China, Germany, Italy, Spain, Switzerland, Turkey, USA
- **Risk of bias**: RoB 2.0 (RCTs); MINOR score (observational studies)
- **Statistics**: Random-effects model (DerSimonian-Laird); arcsine transformation for proportions; JASP v0.19.0; Egger's test (if ≥10 studies); metaregression (Wald test, p < 0.05)
- **Preparation designs recorded**: No-prep, window/feather, butt joint, palatal chamfer; 5 total designs across studies; 4 study arms used no-prep
- **Fabrication**: Conventional (35 arms), digital (5 arms), hybrid (1 arm)
- **PROSPERO**: CRD42024568719

## Results

### Survival Rates (~10.4 years observation)

| Material | Pooled Survival |
|---|---|
| Feldspathic | 96.13% |
| LRGC | 93.70% |
| LDS | 96.81% |
| Zirconia | 100% (at 2.6 yr) |

No statistically significant difference between feldspathic, LRGC, and LDS (metaregression p > 0.05).

### Complication Rates at 10.4 Years

| Material | Technical | Esthetic | Biological |
|---|---|---|---|
| Feldspathic | 41.48% | 19.64% | 6.51% |
| LRGC | 29.87% | 17.89% | 4.4% |
| LDS | 6.1% | 1.9% | 0.45% |
| Zirconia | 0% | 0% | 0% (2.6 yr) |

**LDS clinical advantage is in complications, not survival.** The near-7-fold difference in technical complication rate (feldspathic 41.48% vs LDS 6.1%) at 10+ years is the key clinical finding. Marginal discoloration (esthetic) and biological events both favor LDS decisively.

**Clinical bottom line**: For long-term predictability, LDS is the preferred ceramic material for laminate veneers. When esthetics demand feldspathic or LRGC, patients should be counseled on higher complication likelihood requiring maintenance. Zirconia requires more evidence before routine use in long-term treatment planning.
