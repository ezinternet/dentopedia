## One-line Summary

In-vitro four-way comparison (Int J Prosthodont 1993; PMID 8507328) of marginal fit of porcelain veneers: platinum foil (best) > refractory die > cast ceramic 0.5 mm > cast ceramic 1.0 mm; both fabrication technique (p < .001) and measurement site (p < .05) significantly affected fit.

## Summary

Sim and Ibbetson (1993) performed the first controlled in-vitro study comparing four distinct porcelain veneer fabrication techniques in terms of marginal fit. Ceramic veneers were fabricated for the labial surface of a maxillary central incisor model using: (1) feldspathic porcelain / platinum foil, (2) feldspathic porcelain / refractory die, (3) cast glass ceramic at 0.5 mm thickness, and (4) cast glass ceramic at 1.0 mm thickness. Marginal openings were measured before and after sectioning specimens to distinguish elastic spring-back from permanent marginal discrepancy.

A clear hierarchy emerged: the platinum foil technique produced the smallest marginal openings, followed by the refractory die, then cast ceramic at 0.5 mm, and finally cast ceramic at 1.0 mm. This hierarchy was consistent both before and after sectioning. Fabrication technique was the dominant factor (p < .001), and measurement site was also significant (p < .05).

## Key Contributions

1. Four-way comparison providing the most comprehensive technique ranking for marginal fit as of 1993.
2. Measurement before and after sectioning: separates elastic spring-back from actual marginal gap, adding methodological rigor.
3. Demonstrated that cast glass ceramic veneers — then the newest material — had the worst marginal fit.
4. Showed that thicker cast ceramic (1.0 mm) performed worse than thinner (0.5 mm), suggesting that material thickness and processing introduce additional distortion.

## Methodology

- **Model tooth**: Maxillary central incisor, standard labial surface preparation.
- **Groups**: 4 fabrication method/material combinations (platinum foil, refractory die, cast ceramic 0.5 mm, cast ceramic 1.0 mm).
- **Measurement**: Marginal opening measured pre-sectioning (intact) and post-sectioning (spring-back released).
- **Statistics**: Effects of fabrication technique and measurement site tested; p-values reported.
