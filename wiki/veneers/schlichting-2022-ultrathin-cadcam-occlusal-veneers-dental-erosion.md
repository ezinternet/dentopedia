## One-line Summary

Prospective RCT (n=11, 60 restorations, up to 3 years): CAD-CAM ceramic (e.max CAD) occlusal veneers achieved 100% survival vs 84.7% for composite resin (Lava Ultimate) in severe dental erosion — survival difference non-significant (P=.124), but composite resin showed significantly greater surface degradation (P=.003).

## 한줄요약

전향적 무작위대조시험(RCT) (n=11명, 60개 수복물, 최대 3년): 중증 치아 침식증(dental erosion)에 CAD-CAM 초박형 교합면 비니어(occlusal veneer) — 세라믹(lithium disilicate, e.max CAD) 생존율 100% vs 복합레진(composite resin, Lava Ultimate) 84.7% (P=.124, 유의하지 않음), 레진군 표면 조도(surface roughness) 유의하게 증가 (P=.003).

## Summary

This prospective randomized clinical trial evaluated CAD-CAM ultrathin occlusal veneers — either lithium disilicate ceramic (e.max CAD) or composite resin (Lava Ultimate) — for restoring posterior teeth severely worn by dental erosion. Eleven participants (mean age 30.4 years) received 60 total restorations (24 ceramic, 36 composite resin), with material randomized per tooth. All restorations used immediate dentin sealing (IDS with OptiBond FL) and were adhesively luted with preheated composite resin (Filtek Z100). Outcomes were assessed using modified USPHS criteria at baseline and annually for up to 3 years. Kaplan-Meier survival was 100% for ceramic and 84.7% for composite resin, with the difference not reaching statistical significance (P=.124). All 5 failures were partial chipping events in the composite resin group, all restorable. Surface roughness was significantly greater in the composite resin group (P=.003). The authors conclude both materials present statistically comparable clinical performance, though composite resin showed concerning surface degradation.

## Key Contributions

- Provides the first medium-term RCT data (up to 3 years) directly comparing CAD-CAM ceramic vs composite resin ultrathin occlusal veneers in dental erosion patients.
- Demonstrates that the ultrathin occlusal veneer approach is clinically viable: zero complete failures across both groups at 3 years.
- Highlights a clinically meaningful but statistically non-significant 15-percentage-point survival gap favoring ceramic — study was likely underpowered.
- Quantifies significantly greater surface degradation in CAD-CAM composite resin (Lava Ultimate) vs ceramic (e.max CAD), raising long-term durability concerns.
- Validates immediate dentin sealing (IDS) as the bonding protocol for ultra-minimal posterior occlusal preparations.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Prospective, parallel-group randomized clinical trial
- **Participants**: 11 patients with severe dental erosion (mean age 30.4 years)
- **Restorations**: 60 total; 24 ceramic (e.max CAD), 36 composite resin (Lava Ultimate); randomized per tooth
- **Preparation**: Trial restoration-driven, minimal/no preparation; immediate dentin sealing (IDS) with OptiBond FL
- **Ceramic surface treatment**: Hydrofluoric acid etching + silanization
- **Composite resin surface treatment**: Airborne-particle abrasion + silanization
- **Luting**: Preheated composite resin (Filtek Z100) for all restorations
- **Evaluation**: Modified USPHS criteria (scores 1–5); baseline + annual for up to 3 years
- **Primary outcome**: Time to failure (scores 4 or 5)
- **Analysis**: Kaplan-Meier survival curves; log-rank test; Wilcoxon/Mann-Whitney for secondary outcomes

## Results

| Parameter | Ceramic (e.max CAD) | Composite Resin (Lava Ultimate) | P-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| n restorations | 24 | 36 | — |
| Complete failures | 0 | 0 | — |
| Partial failures (chipping, score 4) | 0 | 5 | — |
| Kaplan-Meier 3-year survival | 100% | 84.7% (SE 0.065%) | .124 (NS) |
| Surface roughness degradation | Minimal | Significant increase | .003 |

- No restorations were lost during the observation period in either group.
- All 5 partial failures (chipping) occurred in the composite resin group, all restorable.
- Composite resin (Lava Ultimate) exhibited significantly greater surface roughness over time, suggesting material aging in the oral environment.
- The 15-percentage-point survival advantage for ceramic was not statistically significant — likely due to small sample size.
