## One-line Summary

Consensus statement (SSRD/SEPES/PROSEC; built on 2 SR+MA covering 35 studies) endorsing feldspathic/LRGC/LDS for ceramic laminate veneers (≈96% 10-year survival) and LDS/RMC/LRGC for posterior partial coverage restorations, with enamel bonding and butt-joint incisal preparation as the key success drivers.

## Summary

This is a joint consensus statement from the SSRD, SEPES, and PROSEC conference (Working Group 1) on minimally invasive restorations. It rests on two pre-conference systematic reviews with meta-analysis (35 publications screened by PRISMA): one on posterior ceramic partial coverage restorations (PCRs), one on anterior ceramic laminate veneers (the Klein et al. 2024 SR+MA). For laminate veneers, pooled survival was excellent across all materials (97.76% at 2.6y, 96.05% at 10.4y), with lithium disilicate (LDS) showing significantly fewer technical and biological complications than feldspathic or leucite-reinforced glass ceramic (LRGC). The panel's actionable recommendations: bond veneers to enamel (dentin/existing-restoration bonding raises long-term complication risk), and when preparation is required, prefer a flat incisal butt-joint over non-overlapping or palatal-chamfer designs. For posterior PCRs, LDS and resin matrix ceramic (RMC) showed comparable 3-year survival (93.7% vs 89.3%), and all materials were judged appropriate mid-term. The overarching ethic is preservation-first: transfer risk to the prosthetic restoration, prioritize tooth longevity, and favor repair over replacement.

## Key Contributions

- Elevates the laminate-veneer SR+MA pooled numbers into expert-ratified clinical statements — the "what to do" layer above the meta-analysis.
- Material verdict: feldspathic, LRGC, and LDS all recommended for veneers; LDS preferred for its significantly lower technical+biological complication rate.
- Preparation guidance: enamel bonding mandatory where possible; flat incisal butt-joint over non-overlap or palatal chamfer.
- Extends synthesis to posterior PCRs (onlays/occlusal veneers/partial crowns) — LDS/RMC/LRGC all appropriate mid-term.
- Flags the still-open questions (CAD/CAM vs conventional, definitive bonding protocol, zirconia veneer longevity, prep vs non-prep) as research priorities rather than settled practice.

## Methodology

Consensus methodology: PRISMA-based systematic screening identified 35 publications, condensed into two SR+MA, from which Working Group 1 drafted statements refined by plenary expert discussion. SR Paper 1 (posterior PCRs): 1–3y follow-up, 252 restorations across 4 RCTs (loss of retention from 3 RCTs, 212 restorations, 2–3y). SR Paper 2 (laminate veneers): Klein et al. 2024, 29 studies, materials feldspathic/LRGC/LDS/zirconia. Confidence label `consensus` — evidence weight derives from the underlying SR+MA, not from new primary data.

## Results

**Ceramic laminate veneers** — pooled survival 97.76% (2.6Y), 97.12% (5.0Y), 96.05% (10.4Y). Long-term by material: feldspathic 96.13%, LRGC 93.7%, LDS 96.81%. LDS significantly fewer technical+biological complications vs feldspathic (p=0.044) and vs LRGC (p=0.033). Esthetic complications: no significant between-material difference (feldspathic vs LRGC p=0.972; feldspathic vs LDS p=0.117; LRGC vs LDS p=0.087).

**Posterior PCRs (3-year)** — LDS 93.7% vs RMC 89.3% survival (NS). Failures/100 restoration-years: LDS 2.13, RMC 3.69; chipping 1.89 vs 2.29; loss of retention 0.98 vs 2.76; endodontic 0.53 vs 0.92; bulk fracture 1.07 vs 0.32.

**Clinical recommendations (veneers):** recommend feldspathic/LRGC/LDS; bond to enamel; if prepared, flat incisal butt-joint preferred. Zirconia veneer mid/long-term performance remains uncertain (insufficient data).
