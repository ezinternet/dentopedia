## One-line Summary

Seminal clinical-technique / narrative paper establishing the Aesthetic Pre-evaluative Temporary (APT) workflow (mock-up → wax-up → APT → silicone index) so porcelain laminate veneer teeth are prepared *through* the patient-approved provisional — referencing reduction to the final restorative contour rather than the existing tooth, to preserve enamel and align dentist, patient, and ceramist on one outcome.

## Summary

> Source scope: **abstract + section snippets only — full text not retrieved (paywalled ScienceDirect export).** The body below is built only from the published abstract and the section-snippet previews; no quantitative outcome data is available.

Gürel (2007) is the canonical exposition of *minimally invasive porcelain laminate veneer (PLV) preparation by design*. Its core insight is that conservative reduction is not achieved by free-handing on the existing — often worn, dark, or malpositioned — tooth, but by first **designing the final outcome** and then preparing relative to it. The workflow proceeds: analyze the smile in three dimensions → evaluate function (can the teeth bear the proposed restorations?) → create a composite **mock-up** → refine into a **wax model** → fabricate an **Aesthetic Pre-evaluative Temporary (APT)** → capture it in a **silicone index**. The APT does double duty: the patient (and their family/friends) evaluates esthetics, phonetics, and function before committing, *and* the clinician then prepares the tooth structure **through the APT**, because the APT carries the exact final contours (incisal edge position, facial volume). Preparing through this provisional means reduction is referenced to where the porcelain will ultimately sit, avoiding over-reduction and maximizing residual enamel for adhesion. The same silicone index is handed to the ceramist so the laboratory reproduces the patient-approved contours (in the illustrated case, a pressable porcelain with a layering technique). The paper frames esthetics as an objective subject demanding structured communication among dentist, patient, and ceramist, and stresses that PLVs are conservative *only if* case selection, planning, and this communication chain are followed.

## Key Contributions

- Coins and operationalizes **"minimal tooth preparation by design"**: minimize reduction by designing the endpoint first, then preparing relative to the planned restorative contour rather than the existing tooth.
- Establishes the **APT (Aesthetic Pre-evaluative Temporary)** as both an evaluation device and a **preparation guide** — prepare *through* the APT.
- Defines the analog communication chain **mock-up → wax-up → APT → silicone index**, with the same index transferred to the ceramist for outcome fidelity.
- Embeds **functional evaluation** (occlusal scheme decision, e.g. canine guidance vs splinting) as a prerequisite before any reduction.
- Provides the conceptual foundation later digitized by Digital Smile Design and CAD-CAM mock-up techniques (3D-DSP), and the basis for "APT-technique" clinical-performance follow-ups.

## Methodology

- **Design**: single-author clinical technique / narrative exposition with at least one illustrative case (not a comparative, survival, or in-vitro study); no statistics or numeric outcomes.
- **Confidence**: `narrative-review`.
- **Described workflow**: (1) 3D smile analysis; (2) functional evaluation of supporting teeth and intended occlusal scheme; (3) treatment planning via composite mock-up; (4) freehand composite mock-up conveying tooth length, facial bulkiness, lip/phonetic/occlusal effects; (5) **tooth preparation through the APT**, treating complex cases as if teeth were already in final position; (6) laboratory fabrication using the *same* silicone index that produced the patient-approved APT.

## Results

No numeric outcomes are reported in the retrieved abstract + snippets. Qualitative conclusions:

- The mock-up → wax-up → APT → silicone-index sequence yields the **best esthetic, phonetic, and functional outcome** and superior dentist–patient–laboratory communication.
- **Permanent diagnostic provisionals** can further reinforce this communication.
- Patients can pre-evaluate the result **themselves and with their social circle** before the definitive restorations are placed.
- PLVs are among the most conservative options, **but only when** case selection, planning, and the communication chain are respected.
