SR+MA of 7 studies (3 RCTs + 4 cohorts, follow-up 24–97 months): resin composite laminate veneers showed 88% pooled survival rate overall, with direct veneers (91%) outperforming indirect (84%); surface roughness and color mismatch were the most common complications.

## Summary

This SR+MA by Lim et al. (2023), registered in PROSPERO (CRD42022336857), evaluated the survival and complication rates of resin composite laminate veneers. From 827 articles screened, 7 studies (3 RCTs, 4 cohort studies) met inclusion criteria requiring ≥2-year follow-up on permanent dentition. The pooled survival rate from RCTs was 88% (95% CI: 81%–94%), with the mean follow-up ranging 24 to 97 months. The direct approach demonstrated a 91% survival rate while the indirect approach achieved 84%. The most reported complications were surface roughness, color mismatch, and marginal discoloration. Most complications were considered clinically acceptable with or without reintervention. The authors highlighted a striking scarcity of published evidence comparing direct versus indirect resin composite veneers — a gap that has persisted since these restorations were first studied in the 1990s.

## Key Contributions

- Established the first SR+MA dedicated to resin composite (not ceramic) laminate veneer survival
- Quantified the direct vs indirect survival gap: direct 91% vs indirect 84%
- Identified surface roughness as the most frequently reported complication
- Demonstrated that complications, while common, are largely manageable without replacement
- Revealed a critical evidence gap: high-quality comparative RCTs for indirect resin composite veneers are nearly absent despite decades of clinical use

## Methodology

- **Design**: SR+MA; PRISMA-compliant; PROSPERO registered
- **Databases**: MEDLINE (PubMed), Scopus, Cochrane Central Register; hand search of cited reviews
- **Eligibility**: RCTs and cohort studies; ≥2-year follow-up; permanent dentition; years 1998–May 2022
- **Studies included**: 7 (3 RCTs + 4 cohort)
- **Heterogeneity (RCTs)**: I² = 50.5% (P = .108) — moderate
- **Bias assessment**: 3 published quality scales applied
- **Inter-rater agreement**: Cohen's κ = 0.81

## Results

| Parameter | Result |
|---|---|
| Overall pooled survival rate | 88% (95% CI: 81%–94%) |
| Direct laminate veneer survival | 91% |
| Indirect laminate veneer survival | 84% |
| Mean follow-up range | 24–97 months |
| RCT heterogeneity (I²) | 50.5% |
| Most common complication | Surface roughness |
| 2nd/3rd complications | Color mismatch, marginal discoloration |

The most common complications (surface roughness, color mismatch, marginal discoloration) were clinically acceptable with or without reintervention. Clinical maintenance needs are high for resin composite veneers.
