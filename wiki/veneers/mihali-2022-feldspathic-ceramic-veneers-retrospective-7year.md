## One-line Summary

Retrospective cohort (30 patients, 170 feldspathic veneers, 7 years) found 91.77% survival; zero fractures at 0.5 mm thickness; no significant survival difference between vertical-prep and no-prep methods.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

This retrospective study from a Romanian private practice evaluated 170 feldspathic ceramic laminate veneers placed in 30 patients (2013–2014) and followed for up to 7 years. Two minimally invasive preparation techniques were compared — vertical/biologically oriented preparation (n=108 veneers) and no-preparation (n=62 veneers) — across five maximum thickness groups (0.5, 1, 1.5, 2, and 2.5 mm). Clinical evaluation used FDI World Dental Federation criteria (C1–C11, scored 1–5). Fourteen veneers failed due to core fracture (failure rate 8.23%) and were replaced with lithium disilicate; 10 additional cases of minor chipping were managed by polishing. The key clinical finding is that 0.5 mm feldspathic veneers showed zero fractures over the observation period, while paradoxically thicker veneers (>1.5–2 mm) were more susceptible to fracture due to unsupported ceramic mass. No secondary caries or pulpal necrosis were observed in either group.

## Key Contributions

1. **Thickness paradox**: 0.5 mm veneers outperformed 1–2.5 mm veneers for fracture outcomes; thicker ceramic without adequate support is more, not less, fracture-prone.
2. **No-prep advantages**: No-prep veneers scored significantly better at 7 years for esthetic anatomical form (C3, p=0.038), occlusal contour/wear (C6, p<0.001), recurrent caries (C8, p=0.001), and periodontal response (C10, p<0.001).
3. **Prep vs. no-prep equivalence for survival**: Log Rank test showed no significant difference in survival curves (p=0.247), validating minimally invasive vertical preparation as an alternative.
4. **Excellent esthetic stability**: Color match/translucency (C2) and marginal adaptation (C5) remained score 1 (excellent) for all veneers in both groups over 7 years.
5. **Biologic safety**: No secondary caries, no pulpal necrosis; demonstrates biologic compatibility of feldspathic ceramic with adhesive cementation on vital teeth.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Retrospective cohort; single clinician, single technician
- **Population**: 30 patients (18–72 years, mean 36.7 ± 12); vital teeth, no endodontic treatment, no bruxism
- **Veneers**: 170 feldspathic ceramic (70 maxillary, 100 mandibular)
- **Groups**: Vertical prep (n=108) vs. no-prep (n=62); 5 thickness subgroups (0.5–2.5 mm)
- **Fabrication**: Refractory die technique; flexural strength 62–90 MPa; modulus of elasticity 69 GPa
- **Cementation**: HF etching (3–7%, 60 s) + orthophosphoric acid cleaning + silanization (Monobond Plus) + resin composite cement; rubber dam isolation
- **Evaluation tools**: FDI World Dental Federation criteria (C1–C11); Mann–Whitney U, Kruskal–Wallis H, Wilcoxon Signed Ranks, Log Rank (Mantel-Cox) tests
- **Follow-up recall**: All patients recalled February–June 2021 (7-year mark)

## Results

| Parameter | Result |
|---|---|
| Overall survival (7 years) | 91.77% (156/170) |
| Total failures (core fracture) | 14 veneers (8.23%) |
| Minor chipping (polished, not replaced) | 10 veneers |
| Survival: vertical-prep vs. no-prep | p=0.247 (NS) |
| Survival: five thickness groups | p=0.065 (NS) |
| Fractures at 0.5 mm | Zero |
| Color match/translucency (C2) | Score 1 (excellent) — all veneers, all time points |
| Marginal adaptation (C5) | Score 1 (excellent) — both groups |
| Secondary caries | None observed |
| Pulpal necrosis | None observed |

**FDI score comparisons at 7 years**:
- No-prep vs. prep: significantly better C3 (p=0.038), C6 (p<0.001), C8 (p=0.001), C10 (p<0.001)
- 0.5 mm vs. ≥1 mm: 0.5 mm significantly better for C1, C4, C6, C8, C10 (all p<0.05)
- Most failures occurred in the first 6 months post-cementation

**Thickness paradox** (key clinical takeaway): Veneers with thickness >1.5–2 mm were more susceptible to fracture due to unsupported ceramic mass, whereas 0.5 mm ultrathin veneers were most fracture-resistant over the study period.
