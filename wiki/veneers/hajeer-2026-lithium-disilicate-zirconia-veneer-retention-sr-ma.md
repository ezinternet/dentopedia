## Summary

Hajeer et al. (2026) conducted a PRISMA 2020-compliant SR+MA examining bond strength of lithium disilicate (LDS) versus translucent zirconia (TZ) veneers bonded with light-cured resin cements. From 1,936 screened records, 6 in vitro studies met full inclusion criteria. The overall pooled mean bond strength was 15.9 MPa. LDS veneers showed a substantially higher pooled bond strength (25.4 MPa) compared to TZ veneers (12.1 MPa). Enamel substrates yielded significantly higher bond strength (21.1 MPa) than composite cores (11.3 MPa). An exploratory meta-regression identified an inverse trend between veneer thickness and bond strength, but the small dataset limits its interpretation. Considerable heterogeneity (I²=98.2%) across studies reflects methodological diversity in surface treatment protocols, bond strength test methods, and aging procedures. The authors applied an adapted GRADE framework for in vitro evidence and concluded findings should be considered exploratory, not directly extrapolated to clinical performance.

## Key Contributions

- First quantitative SR+MA specifically targeting LDS vs translucent zirconia veneers under light-cured cementation
- Demonstrated a 2:1 bond strength advantage for LDS over TZ under identical light-cure protocols
- Quantified substrate effect: enamel bondable substrate (~2× bond strength compared to composite core)
- Identified inverse veneer thickness–bond strength relationship (exploratory)
- Applied REML + Hartung–Knapp adjustment — methodologically appropriate for small meta-analyses
- Highlighted the mechanistic basis: LDS benefits from HF etching + silanization; TZ relies on MDP phosphate monomer without silica network

## Methodology

- **Design**: SR+MA of in vitro studies; PRISMA 2020-compliant; no PROSPERO registration (in vitro design)
- **Databases**: PubMed, Google Scholar, ScienceDirect, Wiley, SpringerLink (Jan 2010–Mar 2025)
- **Studies screened**: 1,936 records → 94 full-texts → 7 qualitative → 6 quantitative (meta-analysis)
- **Inclusion**: LDS or TZ veneers; light-cured resin cement only; MPa bond strength reported; enamel or composite substrate
- **Exclusion**: crowns/inlays, dual-cure/self-adhesive cements without separate light-cure data, missing SD, FEA studies, case reports
- **Statistical model**: Random-effects (REML), Hartung–Knapp–Sidik–Jonkman adjustment
- **Subgroup analyses**: ceramic material type; substrate type
- **Meta-regression**: veneer thickness vs bond strength (exploratory)
- **Bias tool**: adapted 5-domain framework for in vitro dental studies

## Results

| Parameter | Bond Strength |
|---|---|
| All studies pooled | 15.9 MPa |
| Lithium disilicate subgroup | 25.4 MPa |
| Translucent zirconia subgroup | 12.1 MPa |
| Enamel substrate subgroup | 21.1 MPa |
| Composite core substrate subgroup | 11.3 MPa |
| Heterogeneity (I²) | 98.2% |

- Meta-regression: thicker veneer → lower bond strength (inverse trend; exploratory only)
- Evidence certainty: low (in vitro, high heterogeneity, small n = 64 specimens total)
- LDS + enamel: highest bond strength combination under light-cured protocol
