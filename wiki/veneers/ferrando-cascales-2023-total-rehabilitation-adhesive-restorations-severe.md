## One-line Summary

5-year retrospective case series (8 patients, 212 restorations) of adhesive full-mouth rehabilitation in severe tooth wear (TWES 2–4) reporting 90.1% overall survival; occlusal veneers 79.1%, vestibular veneers 94.1%, palatal veneers 100%; posterior location is the dominant risk predictor (p = 0.008).

## 한줄요약

후향적 증례군 연구 (8명, 212개 수복물, 60개월): 중증 치아마모(Tooth Wear, TWES 2–4) 환자의 접착성 전악 수복(Total Adhesive Rehabilitation) 생존율 90.1%; 교합면 비니어(Occlusal Veneer) 79.1%, 순측 비니어(Vestibular Veneer) 94.1%, 구개측 비니어(Palatal Veneer) 100%; 후방 위치(Posterior Location)가 유일한 통계적 위험인자(HR 0.143, p=0.008).

## Summary

Eight patients with severe generalized tooth wear (TWES 2.0 grade 2–4) were treated via full-mouth adhesive rehabilitation at a private Spanish clinic by a single expert operator. A total of 212 restorations were placed: 66 occlusal veneers (CAD-CAM indirect composite resin, Cerasmart), 26 palatal veneers (nanoceramic resin), and 120 vestibular veneers (feldspathic ceramic or lithium disilicate E.max Press). All restorations were adhesively cemented with a light-cured resin cement (Calibra Veneer Bleach). Vertical dimension of occlusion (VDO) was increased using a semi-additive approach guided by DSD and Kois dentofacial analyzer.

At 60 months, overall survival was 90.1% (mean survival time 57.6 months). Of 21 complications, 18 were minor chipping repaired with direct composite, 1 was debonding (recemented), 1 was tooth fracture (requiring extraction and implant), and 2 were secondary caries. No marginal microleakage or postoperative sensitivity was observed throughout follow-up. Cox regression identified posterior tooth location as the only statistically significant risk predictor (HR 0.143, p = 0.008); material type (composite 86.3% vs ceramic 93.1%, p = 0.107) was not significant.

## Key Contributions

- First medium-term (5-year) clinical dataset reporting survival for all three adhesive veneer types (occlusal, vestibular, palatal) within a single full-mouth rehabilitation protocol for severe tooth wear
- Documents that 18 of 21 complications were repaired without replacing the restoration — validates the reparability advantage of the additive/adhesive philosophy
- Confirms that composite resin and ceramic materials show comparable 5-year survival (non-significant), giving clinical flexibility based on patient economics
- Identifies posterior veneer location (not material, not bruxism, not TWES grade) as the dominant survival predictor
- Illustrates a stepwise semi-additive VDO-raising protocol (DSD → Kois analyzer → posterior VDO → anterior guidance → esthetic veneers)

## Methodology

- **Design**: Retrospective case series; single operator; private clinic, Spain
- **n**: 8 patients (6M, 2F; mean age 46.6 years); 212 restorations; 60 months follow-up
- **Inclusion**: TWES 2.0 grade 2–4, esthetic complaint as chief concern, ≥5 years post-treatment records
- **Exclusion**: Grade 1 wear, periodontitis, poor hygiene compliance
- **Materials**: Occlusal veneers — Cerasmart (GC) CAD-CAM indirect composite; vestibular veneers — feldspathic (Creation) or lithium disilicate (E.max Press); palatal veneers — nanoceramic resin
- **Outcome**: Modified USPHS criteria; Kaplan–Meier + log-rank; Cox regression (SPSS 24.0)
- **Limitations**: Small n; single operator; retrospective; no control group; mixed materials

## Results

| Outcome | Value |
|---|---|
| Overall survival (212 restorations, 60 months) | 90.1% |
| Mean survival time | 57.6 months |
| Occlusal veneer survival | 79.1% |
| Vestibular veneer survival | 94.1% |
| Palatal veneer survival | 100% |
| Composite resin survival | 86.3% |
| Ceramic survival | 93.1% (p=0.107 NS) |
| Total complications | 21/212 |
| Minor chipping (repaired) | 18 (85.7% of complications) |
| Debonding | 1 |
| Tooth fracture (extraction) | 1 |
| Secondary caries | 2 |
| Marginal microleakage | 0 |
| Postoperative sensitivity | 0 |

**Significant predictors of failure**:
- Restoration type (occlusal vs anterior): p < 0.000
- Posterior veneer location: HR 0.143 (CI 0.034–0.601), p = 0.008
- Material (NS, p = 0.401); TWES grade (NS); parafunction (NS); bite guard (NS, trend positive)

**Complication timing**: Peak failures at year 2 (5%, n=10) and year 5 (6%, n=6); minor at year 3 (2%) and year 4 (1%).
