## One-line Summary

In-vitro study (n=72 molars) of IPS e.max CAD full-coverage occlusal veneers: only the 0.7/1.0 mm (fissure/cusp) group survived 600,000 masticatory cycles undamaged; thinner groups showed higher fracture with enamel-only bonding; minimum 0.7–1.0 mm recommended.

## 한줄요약

In-vitro 연구 (n=72): 리튬 디실리케이트 세라믹 (Lithium Disilicate Ceramic, LDS) 교합면 비니어 (Occlusal Veneer)에서 협착부 0.7 mm/교두 1.0 mm군만 교합 시뮬레이터 무손상 완주; 두께가 파절 저항성 (Fracture Resistance)에 가장 유의한 영향을 줌, 최소 0.7–1.0 mm 권고.

## Summary

This in-vitro study investigated the combined effects of ceramic thickness and dental bonding substrate on the fracture resistance of full-coverage adhesively retained occlusal veneers fabricated from IPS e.max CAD (lithium disilicate glass-ceramic). Seventy-two extracted molars were assigned to three preparation groups: enamel-only, enamel-plus-dentin, and enamel-plus-composite. Each group was further subdivided by veneer thickness (thin: 0.3/0.6 mm; medium: 0.5/0.8 mm; thick: 0.7/1.0 mm fissure/cusp). All restorations were luted with Multilink Automix using Multilink Primer A/B after HF etching and silanation, then subjected to thermomechanical aging (7,500 thermal cycles + 600,000 masticatory cycles at 10 kg). Only the thickest subgroup achieved 100% survival without any damage. Fracture resistance medians ranged from 610 N (thin, enamel-only) to 3390 N (thick groups). Both ceramic thickness and bonding substrate significantly affected outcomes.

## Key Contributions

1. **Minimum thickness recommendation**: 0.7 mm at the fissure and 1.0 mm at the cusp is the practical minimum for non-retentive full-coverage LDS occlusal veneers using a self-etching primer protocol.
2. **Substrate compensation for thin veneers**: When ceramic thickness is reduced, bonding to dentin or composite resin provides significantly higher fracture resistance than bonding to enamel alone — a clinically relevant finding for cases with existing restorations.
3. **Thickness as primary predictor**: Thickness was the statistically dominant factor; substrate type differences became less relevant in the thickest group.
4. **Masticatory simulation protocol**: 600,000 cycles at 10 kg represents a rigorous in-vitro aging protocol, adding clinical validity to the results.

## Methodology

- **Design**: In-vitro laboratory study
- **Specimens**: 72 extracted human molars (no caries/fillings); N=24 per main group, N=8 per subgroup
- **Material**: IPS e.max CAD (CAD/CAM lithium disilicate; Ivoclar Vivadent)
- **Preparation groups**: (1) enamel only, (2) enamel + dentin, (3) enamel + composite resin filling
- **Thickness subgroups**: Thin (0.3/0.6 mm), Medium (0.5/0.8 mm), Thick (0.7/1.0 mm fissure/cusp)
- **Bonding**: 5% HF etching + silanation; Multilink Primer A/B (self-etching) + Multilink Automix (composite resin cement)
- **Aging**: 37°C water storage 3 days; 7,500 thermal cycles (5–55°C); 600,000 chewing simulator cycles at 10 kg
- **Fracture test**: Universal testing machine on surviving specimens
- **Statistics**: Kruskal-Wallis + Wilcoxon with Bonferroni-Holm correction (α=0.05)

## Results

| Group | Thickness (fissure/cusp) | Bonding | Survival (no damage) | Median Fracture Load |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Thick | 0.7/1.0 mm | All substrates | 100% | ~3200–3390 N |
| Medium | 0.5/0.8 mm | Enamel only | ~50% | ~610 N |
| Medium | 0.5/0.8 mm | Dentin / Composite | ~75–87.5% | ~1800–2200 N |
| Thin | 0.3/0.6 mm | Enamel only | 12.5% | ~610 N |
| Thin | 0.3/0.6 mm | Dentin / Composite | ~50% | ~1200–1700 N |

- Ceramic thickness had a statistically significant influence on fracture resistance (p ≤ 0.05)
- In thin and medium groups, dentin/composite bonding was significantly superior to enamel-only (p ≤ 0.05)
- No significant difference between dentin and composite bonding surfaces
- Survival with some damage ranged 50–100% across non-thick subgroups
