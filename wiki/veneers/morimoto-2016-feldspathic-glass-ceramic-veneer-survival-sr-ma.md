## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 13 clinical studies: ceramic laminate veneers show 89% overall cumulative survival at median 9 years; glass-ceramic (94%) outperforms feldspathic (87%); fracture/chipping is the most frequent complication at 4%.

## Summary

Morimoto et al. (2016) performed a systematic review and meta-analysis of 13 clinical trials evaluating feldspathic porcelain and glass-ceramic laminate veneers. Across a median follow-up of 9 years, the pooled cumulative survival rate was 89% (95% CI: 84%–94%). Glass-ceramic veneers showed higher survival (94%; 95% CI: 87%–100%) than feldspathic porcelain (87%; 95% CI: 82%–93%), though metaregression found that ceramic type and follow-up period did not significantly influence failure rate. Fracture and chipping was the most frequent complication (4%), followed by debonding and endodontic problems (both 2%). Incisal coverage showed an OR of 1.25 (95% CI: 0.33–4.73), which was not statistically significant, indicating it was neither a clear risk nor protective factor for veneer survival.

## Key Contributions

- Established the first comprehensive pooled survival estimate for feldspathic and glass-ceramic laminate veneers at long-term follow-up
- Fracture/chipping identified as the dominant complication mode (4%), informing clinical risk counseling
- Incisal coverage does not significantly alter survival probability (OR 1.25, p NS)
- Metaregression: neither ceramic type nor follow-up duration drove failure rate — both materials are clinically viable
- Could not meta-analyze enamel vs dentin preparation effect due to heterogeneous reporting

## Methodology

- **Search**: Cochrane + PubMed; 899 articles screened → 13 included
- **Follow-up**: Median 9 years
- **Materials**: Feldspathic porcelain + glass-ceramic (leucite/LDS not differentiated as today)
- **Outcomes**: Survival, debonding, fracture/chipping, secondary caries, marginal discoloration, endodontic problems, incisal coverage effect
- **Statistics**: Cochran Q + I² for heterogeneity; metaregression for moderator analysis (ceramic type, follow-up period)

## Results

| Outcome | Estimate | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|
| Overall survival | 89% | 84%–94% |
| Glass-ceramic survival | 94% | 87%–100% |
| Feldspathic survival | 87% | 82%–93% |
| Debonding | 2% | 1%–4% |
| Fracture/chipping | 4% | 3%–6% |
| Secondary caries | 1% | 0%–3% |
| Severe marginal discoloration | 2% | 1%–10% |
| Endodontic problems | 2% | 1%–3% |
| Incisal coverage OR | 1.25 | 0.33–4.73 (NS) |

**Clinical bottom line**: Ceramic laminate veneers (both feldspathic and glass-ceramic) demonstrate high long-term survival. Fracture/chipping is the most clinically relevant complication to counsel patients about. Incisal coverage and ceramic type selection do not significantly alter survival probability by this evidence.
