## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1111/ipd.70084](https://doi.org/10.1111/ipd.70084)), this umbrella review (overview of systematic reviews) synthesizes SR-level evidence on artificial intelligence in pediatric dentistry — caries detection, risk prediction, anomaly identification, and treatment planning. Seven systematic reviews (109 primary studies) were appraised with AMSTAR-2, and primary-study overlap was quantified with the GROOVE tool (corrected covered area, CCA). Convolutional neural networks (CNNs) applied to imaging delivered pooled sensitivity/specificity of 80–83% and AUC 0.87–0.91, but most included reviews were of low or critically-low quality (no protocol registration, limited bias assessment), and overlap was moderate (CCA = 8.27%). This page is **abstract-only** — full text was not retrieved, so per-SR breakdowns and appraisal tables are unavailable.

Clinically: AI image-based diagnostics (e.g., CNN caries detection on radiographs) are promising enough to watch and pilot, but the evidence base is still preliminary and built on weak underlying reviews — do not treat current AI tools as a validated standard of care in pediatric dentistry; interpret vendor accuracy claims with caution.

## Key Contributions

- First umbrella review consolidating systematic-review evidence on AI across pediatric dentistry.
- Adds a meta-epidemiological layer: AMSTAR-2 quality grading and GROOVE/CCA overlap quantification rather than a fresh pooled estimate.
- Establishes the field's headline caveat — strong diagnostic metrics rest on low/critically-low-quality reviews.

## Methodology

- **Design:** Umbrella review (overview of systematic reviews); PROSPERO CRD420251142904.
- **Sources:** PubMed/MEDLINE and the Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews, searched to 31 August 2025.
- **Quality:** AMSTAR-2 for each included SR.
- **Overlap:** corrected covered area (CCA) via the GROOVE tool.
- **Note:** Abstract-only — PRISMA flow and per-review detail not retrievable.

## Results

- **7 systematic reviews** included, covering **109 primary studies**.
- Topic split: **4** early childhood caries; **1 each** dental anomalies, cleft lip and palate, caries risk prediction.
- **CNN imaging** — pooled **sensitivity/specificity 80–83%**, **AUC 0.87–0.91**.
- **Quality** — most reviews **low or critically low** (no protocol registration; limited bias assessment).
- **Overlap** — **moderate, CCA = 8.27%** (GROOVE).
- **Bottom line** — AI is promising for image-based tasks, but evidence is preliminary; interpret with caution.
