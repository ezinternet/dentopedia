## One-line Summary

Overview of systematic reviews (umbrella review; 12 SRs, ROBIS-appraised) finding recent AI models on dental panoramic radiographs reach high accuracy across caries (91.5%), osteoporosis (89.29%), maxillary sinusitis (87.5%), periodontal bone loss (93.09%), and teeth ID/numbering (93.67%), with periapical-lesion detection at 99.95% sensitivity / 92% specificity — interpreted cautiously given few, heterogeneous source studies.

## Summary

This is an **overview of systematic reviews** (umbrella review) — its unit of evidence is systematic reviews, not primary studies — establishing the state of AI applied to dental panoramic radiographs (DPRs) and how it evolved over 1988–2023. Twelve systematic reviews qualified for qualitative synthesis after searches across ACM, BASE, Google Scholar, and PubMed, with risk of bias appraised using the ROBIS tool (PROSPERO CRD42023416048). It aggregates the latest reported per-task accuracies into one comparative landscape spanning caries, osteoporosis, maxillary sinusitis, periodontal bone loss, teeth identification/numbering, and periapical lesions. The authors conclude AI can meaningfully support dentists in DPR analysis but that the underlying evidence is small and heterogeneous, and that SRs on AI date quickly and need regular updating.

**Note: abstract-only — full text not retrieved** (PMC full-text body was empty). All content is grounded strictly in the structured abstract; per-review ROBIS ratings, task-level review counts, and inclusion-criteria detail are not captured.

## Key Contributions

- Umbrella-level positioning: sits above the many task-specific AI-on-DPR systematic reviews, giving a single cross-task view.
- Traces the 1988–2023 development trajectory of information-technology / AI methods for DPR analysis.
- ROBIS-based risk-of-bias appraisal of the 12 included systematic reviews.
- Cross-task accuracy anchors for six distinct DPR analysis tasks.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Overview of systematic reviews (umbrella review); qualitative synthesis; PROSPERO-registered (CRD42023416048).
- **Databases**: ACM, BASE (Bielefeld Academic Search Engine), Google Scholar, PubMed.
- **Risk of bias**: ROBIS tool.
- **Included**: 12 systematic reviews.
- **Reporting**: results visualized with timelines, tables, and charts.

## Results

Latest analyzed AI models on dental panoramic radiographs:

| Task | Reported performance |
|---|---|
| Caries detection | 91.5% accuracy |
| Osteoporosis detection | 89.29% accuracy |
| Maxillary sinusitis detection | 87.5% accuracy |
| Periodontal bone loss detection | 93.09% accuracy |
| Teeth identification and numbering | 93.67% accuracy |
| Periapical lesion detection | 99.95% sensitivity / 92% specificity |

Overall, significant development of DPR-analysis IT/AI methods was observed across 1988–2023. **Caveat**: because the systematic reviews synthesized here draw on a small number of heterogeneous source studies, the pooled results should be interpreted with caution, and the authors stress that AI-focused SRs become outdated quickly and require regular updating.
