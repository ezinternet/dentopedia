## One-line Summary
Narrative pathophysiology review placing NCCLs within a unified demineralization-disease framework governed by environmental acid, biomechanics, and intercrystalline fluid percolation.

## 한줄요약
NCCL을 acid·biomechanics·percolation이 지배하는 통합 demineralization 질환 틀 안에 위치시킨 병태생리 review.

## Summary
Roberts, Mangum, and Schneider argue that osteoporosis, dental caries, and NCCLs share a common demineralization physiology rather than being unrelated diseases. All mineralized tissues depend on cell-mediated apposition, a protein matrix, fluid logistics, intercrystalline ion percolation, cyclic demineralization/remineralization, and are vulnerable to acid-based degradation. Within this frame, NCCLs arise from a dual etiology of acidic demineralization and mechanical flexure, with cervical loss resembling clastic-cell catabolic modeling seen in bone. The authors highlight fluoride percolation and intercrystalline fluid flow as key regulators of demineralization resistance and stress that mineralized tissues differ chiefly in turnover (only bone is continuously remodeled; enamel is nonvital). The review supplies the biomechanical and physicochemical substrate underlying the clinical NCCL guidelines that co-author Roberts develops in Goodacre 2022.

## Key Contributions
- Unified demineralization model spanning skeletal and dental tissues.
- NCCL recast as acid + mechanical-flexure phenomenon with bone-analogous catabolic modeling.
- Foregrounds percolation and physiologic fluoride as resistance regulators.

## Methodology
Narrative review (Craniofacial Skeleton topical collection); integrates osteology, biomechanics, and cariology literature without systematic search or pooling.

## Results
No pooled clinical outcomes. Notable quantitative points: mandibular remodeling ~44%/yr (3x femur); dental demineralization lifetime prevalence 85-100%; subsurface caries <400 µm clinically undetectable. Attrition, abrasion, erosion, and abfraction are diagnosed visually and frequently share acidic + mechanical etiology.
