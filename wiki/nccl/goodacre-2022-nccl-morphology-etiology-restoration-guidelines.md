## Summary
This review synthesizes the NCCL literature across morphology, progression, prevalence, etiology, and pathophysiology, then derives clinical restoration guidelines. NCCLs occur either as saucer-like depressions progressing mainly in height or V-shaped notches progressing in both height and depth, with progression accelerating as the depth/height ratio rises. Prevalence ranges from under 10% to over 90% depending on population and criteria, increasing with age; premolar facial surfaces are most affected. Etiology is multifactorial (toothbrush abrasion, dietary acid, GERD, salivary hypofunction), with the abfraction/occlusal-stress contribution still controversial. Management should be conservative-first; when restoration is indicated, the authors recommend surface texturing of sclerotic dentin, enamel beveling, extended 30-second dentin etching, and adhesive application with a light 20-second scrubbing motion.

## Key Contributions
- Two-type morphologic framework (saucer vs V-shaped) with distinct progression behavior; D/H ratio as progression predictor.
- Quantitative progression benchmarks: >=50 µm/yr height progression in low-D/H lesions; volume loss 0.36-1.50 mm3/yr.
- Balanced reading of the abfraction controversy (supportive and opposing literature both cited).
- Step-by-step restorative protocol addressing sclerotic dentin's etch resistance; SDF as non-restorative option when caries coexists.

## Methodology
PubMed narrative search covering NCCL morphology, progression, prevalence, etiology, pathophysiology, and restoration. No systematic review protocol, risk-of-bias assessment, or meta-analysis; evidence integrated qualitatively with clinical guidelines proposed by the authors.

## Results
Prevalence reports span 9.1-93% (e.g., 13.1-17.1% in lower-range studies vs 61.7-63% in higher-range studies); per-tooth involvement 5.7-17.2%. Severe lesions: 29.9% of ages 26-30 vs 42.6% of ages 46-50. Progression risk: low salivary buffering, age, dietary acid. Restorative guidance: delay until clinically necessary; composite resin most common material; texture sclerotic dentin with fine diamond; 30-s etching of sclerotic dentin; light scrubbing adhesive application 20 s; flowable + sculptable composite layering acceptable.
