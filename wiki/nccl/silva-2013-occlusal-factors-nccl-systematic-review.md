## One-line Summary
Systematic review of 9 studies in which the majority found no significant association between NCCLs and occlusal risk factors; only 3 found associations with specific variables (p<0.05), under high heterogeneity.

## Summary
This systematic review evaluated the evidence linking NCCLs to occlusal risk factors (ORF: excursive interferences, occlusal force, premature contacts, guidance type, and skid of centric occlusion to maximum intercuspation) in adults. From 1082 potentially eligible records, 106 underwent full-text review and 9 were included (6 cross-sectional, 2 case-control, 1 clinical trial), with strong reviewer agreement (Kappa 0.8). The majority of studies found no significant association between NCCLs and ORF; only three reported associations with specific variables (occlusal contact area, right canine guidance, premature contacts in centric relation, working side; p<0.05). Methodological quality varied and heterogeneity was high, with no standardized units for occlusal-factor analysis, so pooling was not performed. The review reinforces the conclusion that clinical evidence does not robustly support occlusion as an NCCL cause, contrasting with the laboratory-weighted Duangthip 2017 SR.

## Key Contributions
- Clarifies that the predominant clinical finding is a null NCCL-occlusion association.
- Pinpoints the specific occlusal variables that did reach significance in the minority of studies.
- Documents the lack of standardized occlusal measurement as a barrier to synthesis.

## Methodology
SR across PubMed, Web of Science, Cochrane, Lilacs, Clinical Trials, NRR, NIH; 1082 → 106 full text → 9 included; two independent reviewers (Kappa 0.8); standardized quality scales; no meta-analysis. (Abstract-only ingest.)

## Results
6 cross-sectional, 2 case-control, 1 clinical trial. Majority null for NCCL-ORF association; 3 positive for specific variables (p<0.05). High heterogeneity, variable quality.
