## Summary
This widely cited KU Leuven review addresses the practical decision of when, why, and how to treat NCCLs. It reiterates that NCCLs are multifactorial (biocorrosion, abrasion, possible abfraction) and that diagnosis requires a structured etiologic checklist. The decision to intervene rather than monitor should rest on lesion progression and the degree to which the lesion compromises tooth vitality, function, and esthetics. Adhesive restoration is presented as the last treatment option, sometimes combined with a root-coverage surgical procedure. The review argues that while composite resins are generally indicated for their esthetics and performance, the type of composite does not significantly affect clinical outcome — the adhesive system and correct operative technique do — and that yearly maintenance with margin repolishing prolongs restoration lifespan. This entry is abstract-derived; the full text (Int J Esthet Dent 2020;15(1):16-42) sits behind a Quintessence paywall.

## Key Contributions
- A when/why/how decision framework distinguishing monitoring from intervention.
- Claim that adhesive choice and operator technique, not composite material type, drive NCCL restoration success.
- Emphasis on maintenance (margin repolishing) as a longevity factor.

No quantitative results in the abstract. Qualitative guidance: monitor low-risk lesions; restore for progression, hypersensitivity, likely pulp exposure, esthetics, or to support a partial denture; expect marginal degradation with aging and counter it with annual maintenance.
