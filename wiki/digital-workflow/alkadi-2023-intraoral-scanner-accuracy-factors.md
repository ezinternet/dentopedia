## Summary
Narrative review analyzing factors that influence intraoral scanner (IOS) accuracy, drawing from a PubMed keyword search. Published October 2023 in Diagnostics. Reviews structured light, confocal microscopy, active wavefront sampling, and photogrammetry technologies. No PRISMA or systematic protocol.

Key finding: full-arch scanning accumulates greater error than quadrant scanning — critical implication for full-arch implant prosthetics. Edentulous arches show the greatest IOS error due to reduced anatomical landmarks.

## Key Contributions
- Four IOS optical technologies compared: structured light, confocal microscopy, active wavefront sampling, photogrammetry — no single superior technology identified across all scenarios
- **Arch size effect**: quadrant > half-arch > full-arch accuracy gradient; cumulative stitching error increases with scan extent
- **Edentulous arches**: featureless mucosa → poor feature matching → significantly greater error than dentate arches
- **Operator experience**: one of the most significant modifiable factors; consistent scanning path reduces error
- **Powder**: reduces some reflective surface error; alters surface texture; modern powder-free IOS systems generally equivalent
- **Temperature/moisture**: can affect accuracy in some systems; stabilization period recommended

## Methodology
- Database: PubMed; keyword search
- Type: Narrative review — no systematic search protocol, no PRISMA, no risk of bias assessment
- Factors evaluated: scanner technology, scanning strategy, arch size/type, tooth presence, operator experience, environmental conditions

## Results
| Factor | Direction of Effect |
|---|---|
| Arch size (full vs quadrant) | Full arch: greater error accumulation |
| Tooth presence | Edentulous: greater error vs dentate |
| Operator experience | Less experience → more error |
| Powder use | Marginal benefit; modern powder-free IOS comparable |
| Temperature/moisture | Some system-specific sensitivity |
| Scanner technology | No single superior across all scenarios |
