## One-line Summary
Open-label RCT (40 single-tooth patients) — with the same optical tracker in both arms, the autonomous robot lowered angular deviation (1.01° vs 1.78°, p<0.01) but coronal/apical deviation were comparable; robotic surgery took longer.

## 한줄요약
공개라벨 RCT(환자 40명·단일치): 동일 추적장치 통제 시 자율로봇은 각도 편차만 유의 개선(1.01° vs 1.78°), 관상·첨부는 동등, 시술시간은 더 김.

## Summary
This two-arm RCT is the highest-evidence comparison of an autonomous robot versus dynamic navigation, and it deliberately gave both arms the same optical tracking system. Under that control, the robot's advantage narrowed to angular deviation only; coronal and apical deviation were statistically equivalent, and robotic surgery was significantly slower. Postoperative morbidity and early success were comparable. This refines the larger deviation gaps reported in non-randomized comparisons (e.g. Yu 2025), suggesting part of the robot's apparent edge there reflects tracking/operator differences rather than the robot per se.

## Key Contributions
- Isolates the robot-vs-hand-guided variable by holding the tracker constant.
- Shows the robot's reliable benefit is angular control, not linear accuracy, in straightforward single-tooth cases.

## Methodology
Prospective open-label RCT, AR n=20 vs DN n=20, single missing tooth, identical optical tracking. Primary outcomes angular/coronal/apical deviation; secondary surgical time, pain, edema, complications, early success.

## Results
- Angular: 1.01° ± 0.65° (AR) vs 1.78° ± 1.15° (DN), p < 0.01.
- Coronal & apical: NS.
- Surgical time: 21.52 ± 9.81 vs 14.27 ± 3.57 min, p < 0.01.
- Pain/edema/complications/early success: comparable.
