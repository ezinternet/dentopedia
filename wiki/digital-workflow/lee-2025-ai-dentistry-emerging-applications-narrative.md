## Summary
Narrative review (Journal of Dentistry April 2025) organising AI applications around three pillars — dental education, patient care, and practice management. 120 articles divided among 4 reviewers; English-language. Frames AI deployment as bridging theoretical knowledge and clinical practice across specialties.

## Key Contributions
- **Three-pillar framework**: education / patient care / practice management
- Adaptive learning systems and VR simulation as emerging educational deployments
- Practice-management AI explicitly addressed (claims processing, scheduling, communication) — underrepresented in other reviews

## Methodology
- Narrative review (4 reviewers, divided topics)
- Electronic search across multiple databases
- 120 articles included; English-only
- No PRISMA, no formal quality grading

## Results
**Education pillar**
- Adaptive learning systems
- Simulation and VR for dental training
- Administrative efficiency in academic settings

**Patient care pillar**
- Improved diagnosis, disease prediction, prognosis classification
- Clinical decision-making support
- Treatment execution across specialties (restorative, prosthodontics, periodontics, endodontics, oral surgery, oral pathology, oral medicine, implant dentistry)

**Practice management pillar**
- AI-driven operational tools
- Workflow optimisation
- Patient-administrative interface improvements

## Clinical Implications [claude해석]
- The practice-management pillar is the most underleveraged in Korean clinics — most AI conversation focuses on diagnostic AI
- VR/simulation deployment maturity hinges on hardware cost curves and integrated curriculum design
- Data privacy under Korean PIPA (개인정보 보호법) requires explicit consent flows for AI training data — implementation gap
