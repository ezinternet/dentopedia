## One-line Summary
Retrospective clinical study (57 implants) — semi-autonomous robot (sa-RASS) gave lower platform (0.91 vs 1.26 mm) and apex (1.06 vs 1.51 mm) deviation than dynamic navigation; angular deviation comparable (3.07° vs 3.71°, NS).

## 한줄요약

## Summary
This retrospective study compared a novel semi-autonomous robotic-assisted surgery system (sa-RASS) with a dynamic navigation system in 57 single-tooth implant placements. sa-RASS produced significantly smaller platform and apex deviations, but angular deviation did not differ significantly between systems — contrasting with autonomous-robot studies that show a clear angular advantage. The semi-autonomous design keeps the operator partly in the loop, which may explain the smaller angular gain.

## Key Contributions
- Introduces a semi-autonomous (haptic-constrained) robot to the comparison, distinct from fully autonomous robots.
- Linear (platform/apex) accuracy favored the robot; angular accuracy did not.

## Methodology
Retrospective, single center, Jan–Dec 2023. 57 patients/57 implants (sa-RASS 29, DNS 28), partial edentulism. CBCT-measured platform, apex, angular deviation. KS test then t-test/Mann–Whitney.

## Results
- Platform: 0.91 ± 0.46 vs 1.26 ± 0.51 mm, p < 0.001.
- Apex: 1.06 ± 0.60 vs 1.51 ± 0.56 mm, p < 0.001.
- Angular: 3.07° ± 1.66° vs 3.71° ± 1.64°, p > 0.05.
