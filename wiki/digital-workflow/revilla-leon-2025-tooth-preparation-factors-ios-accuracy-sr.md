## One-line Summary

Systematic review (39 studies) showing that simpler preparation geometry, greater total occlusal convergence (TOC), anatomic occlusal reduction, supragingival/chamfer finish lines, and wider interdental space improve intraoral scanner (IOS) accuracy for tooth-supported restorations, while existing foundation restorations and apical finish-line locations reduce it.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(39편): 형성 형태가 단순하고 축면 경사(TOC)가 크며 교합면을 해부학적으로 삭제하고 finish line이 치은연 위·chamfer일수록, 인접치 간격이 넓을수록 구강스캐너(IOS) 정확도가 좋아지며, 기존 코어 수복물과 치은연하 finish line은 정확도를 떨어뜨린다.

## Summary

This systematic review (JBI critical appraisal; 5 databases + manual search; 39 included articles, predominantly in vitro) isolates **tooth preparation** as a determinant of IOS accuracy — distinct from the better-studied scanner, operator, and patient factors. Articles were classified into 5 preparation-related factor groups: preparation geometry, foundation/existing restorations, surface finishing, finish line location/design, and interdental space.

The clinical bottom line: scanability and accuracy improve with **simpler geometry, greater TOC, anatomic occlusal reduction, supragingival finish lines, chamfer margin design, and wider interdental space.** Existing foundation restorations and more apical (intracrevicular) finish lines degrade accuracy; gingival displacement (displacement cords) dramatically recovers accuracy at intracrevicular margins.

## Key Contributions

- First SR dedicated to preparation-related IOS accuracy factors for tooth-supported restorations.
- Provides a 5-category framework clinicians can act on at the chairside (geometry, foundation, finishing, finish line, interdental space).
- Quantifies several effects: ≤5 µm discrepancy among composite foundation materials; 19-µm trueness / 5-µm precision spread across finishing procedures.

## Methodology

- Design: Systematic review without meta-analysis (heterogeneity in scanners/materials/measurement).
- Databases: PubMed/Medline, Scopus, Embase, Web of Science, Cochrane + manual search.
- Appraisal: Joanna Briggs Institute, 2 independent reviewers + third for consensus.
- Outcome: IOS accuracy = trueness and precision (µm).

## Results

- **Geometry**: Simpler geometry + greater TOC + anatomic occlusal reduction → better scanability/accuracy.
- **Foundation restorations**: Reduce accuracy; ≤5 µm spread among composite resin foundation materials.
- **Surface finishing**: 19-µm trueness / 5-µm precision discrepancy across finishing procedures.
- **Finish line location**: More apical → lower accuracy; displacement-cord gingival displacement strongly improves accuracy at intracrevicular margins.
- **Finish line design**: Chamfer = best accuracy.
- **Interdental space**: Wider space → better accuracy; optimal threshold undeterminable (limited data).
