## Summary
In vitro head-to-head comparison of 4 intraoral scanner (IOS) devices, evaluating trueness (deviation from reference scan) and precision (scan-to-scan reproducibility) in µm. Standardized dental model scanned under identical conditions. Published July 2024, Diagnostics 14:1453. Carol Davila University of Medicine and Pharmacy, Bucharest, Romania.

Statistically significant differences found between scanners for both trueness and precision. Performance ranking varies by arch region (anterior vs posterior). All devices within clinically acceptable range for single-unit prosthetics; variation increases for full-arch scanning.

## Key Contributions
- Direct in vitro comparison under identical conditions eliminates operator and environment variability
- Provides clinically relevant accuracy benchmarks across the 4 tested devices
- Regional performance difference: anterior vs posterior ranking not consistent across all scanners — important for selecting device by intended clinical use
- Full-arch scanning shows greater divergence between devices than single-unit assessment
- All 4 devices acceptable for single-unit restorations; differentiation matters more for full-arch implant cases

## Methodology
- Design: In vitro; standardized dental model
- Metrics: Trueness (µm; deviation from laboratory scanner or CT reference) and precision (µm; scan-to-scan repeatability)
- Reference: Laboratory scanner or CT scan as gold standard
- Setting: Carol Davila University, Bucharest, Romania

## Results
- Statistically significant inter-scanner differences in trueness and precision (p < 0.05)
- Performance ranking is arch-region dependent (anterior vs posterior)
- All devices within clinically acceptable range for single-unit prosthetics
- Full-arch divergence increases — consistent with cumulative stitching error documented in Alkadi 2023
