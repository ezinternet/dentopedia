## One-line Summary

Short communication (Monaco 2016) introducing the Fully Digital Technique (FDT): two IOS scans — scanbody + provisional restoration (in situ then subgingival) — are superimposed via best-fit algorithm to create a merged STL encoding implant position and peri-implant mucosal architecture, enabling fully digital customized CAD/CAM abutment fabrication without conventional impressions.

## Summary

This short communication from the University of Bologna proposes the **Fully Digital Technique (FDT)** for capturing peri-implant soft tissue contours and emergence profile using an intraoral scanner (IOS), eliminating conventional impression-taking in the esthetic zone.

The workflow consists of three STL files:
- **STL1**: Scanbody placed on the implant; IOS records the exact 3D implant platform position and angulation.
- **STL2**: Two-part scan with the provisional retained restoration. First, the vestibular and palatal aspects are scanned with the provisional in situ alongside adjacent teeth. The provisional is then unscrewed and immediately scanned out of the mouth to capture the subgingival emergence shape before soft tissue collapses.
- **STL3**: STL1 and STL2 are imported into imaging software and superimposed using a "best fit" algorithm, producing a single merged file containing (a) implant platform position, (b) the full peri-implant mucosal architecture, and (c) the emergence profile as conditioned by the provisional.

STL3 serves as the digital input for CAD/CAM abutment design and can also generate a 3D-printed (SLA) physical working model.

## Key Contributions

1. First description of the FDT two-scan + STL superimposition workflow for soft tissue capture.
2. Novel subgingival scan step — scanning the just-removed provisional out of mouth captures the full emergence contour that would be invisible with the scanbody alone.
3. Fully digital pathway from IOS impression → CAD/CAM abutment → SLA model, with no conventional polyvinylsiloxane impression.
4. Concept applicable to any esthetic-zone single-tooth implant case where a provisional has conditioned the peri-implant mucosa.

## Methodology

- **Study type**: Technique description / short communication (no controlled clinical trial)
- **Setting**: Anterior maxilla, single implant, esthetic zone
- **Scanner**: Intraoral scanner (IOS); specific device not stated
- **Software**: Imaging software with STL superimposition and best-fit algorithm
- **Outcome**: Proof-of-concept; no quantitative accuracy data, no Pink Esthetic Score, no MBL data reported

## Results

- FDT successfully produced a merged STL3 file capturing the complete peri-implant soft tissue shape from the provisional.
- Customized CAD/CAM abutment designed from STL3 reproduced the conditioned emergence profile in the definitive restoration.
- SLA stereolithographic model allowed conventional lab steps to proceed if needed.
- Authors state FDT is a "rapid digital approach" that replaces conventional implant impressions for soft tissue information.
- No comparative accuracy data vs conventional impression or open-tray technique provided.
