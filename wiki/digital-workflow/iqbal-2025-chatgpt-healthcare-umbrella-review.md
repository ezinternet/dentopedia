## Summary
PRISMA-compliant umbrella review (Journal of Biomedical Science 2025) synthesising 17 ChatGPT-in-healthcare reviews (15 SRs + 2 MAs). AMSTAR-2 quality assessment exposes pervasive methodological weakness in the underlying evidence base. Notably acknowledges the umbrella review's own constraint: heterogeneity prevented quantitative synthesis.

## Key Contributions
- **Evidence-quality audit**: 5 moderate / 12 low AMSTAR-2 — most current ChatGPT-in-healthcare SRs are methodologically weak
- **Thematic distribution mapping**: 82.4% general usage; 17.6% niche (med exams, conducting SRs); 52.9% general healthcare; 41.2% specialty (incl. public health dentistry)
- **Manuscript review/writing application**: 17.6% of reviews touch this use case — emerging concern

## Methodology
- PRISMA-compliant umbrella review
- Databases: Cochrane Library, PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, Google Scholar
- Search: inception to February 2024
- Two independent reviewers for title/abstract/full-text
- AMSTAR-2 checklist for quality assessment
- Narrative synthesis (no quantitative pooling due to heterogeneity)

## Results
| Dimension | Finding |
|---|---|
| Reviews included | 17 (15 SRs + 2 MAs) |
| AMSTAR-2 moderate | 5 |
| AMSTAR-2 low | 12 |
| Most common theme | Disease diagnosis / clinical decision-making |
| General usage focus | 82.4% |
| Specialty-specific focus | 41.2% |
| Manuscript review/writing | 17.6% |

**Common AMSTAR-2 deficiencies**: study design justification, funding source reporting

**Specialty coverage**: radiology, neurosurgery, gastroenterology, **public health dentistry**, ophthalmology

## Clinical Implications [claude해석]
- The 12/17 low-quality AMSTAR-2 rating is a strong caution against citing single ChatGPT-in-healthcare SRs without inspecting methodology
- For dental practice: public health dentistry coverage exists but is thin — direct chairside LLM evidence is patient-question-focused (see [[digital-workflow/zhang-2025-llm-patient-instructions-dentistry-sr-ma]])
- Ethical/legal/accuracy concerns frequently raised but rarely operationalised in included SRs — policy gap
