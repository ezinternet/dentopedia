## One-line Summary
Retrospective CBCT analysis (64 patients, 128 maxillary sinuses): 3D volumetric measurement using implant-planning software (SMOP) yielded mean bony sinus volume 17.1 cm³, sinus obliteration in 42.2% (mean obliterated volume 5.1 cm³) associated with sinus pathology, and significantly larger bony sinus volume in males.

## 한줄요약
콘빔 컴퓨터 단층촬영 (Cone-Beam Computed Tomography, CBCT) 후향 분석 (64명, 128 상악동): 임플란트 계획 소프트웨어(SMOP)로 3D 부피·표면적을 산출했고, 골성 상악동 평균 부피 17.1 cm³, 폐색 상악동 비율 42.2% (평균 폐색 부피 5.1 cm³), 폐색 부피는 상악동 병변과 연관, 남성에서 골성 상악동 부피가 유의하게 컸다.

## Summary
This 2018 University of Zurich CBCT study used the SMOP implant-planning software to perform 3D volumetric measurement of the maxillary sinus in 128 sinuses from 64 patients. The authors quantified both the **osseous-bordered volume** (the bony envelope of the sinus) and the **mucosa-bordered (remaining pneumatized) volume**, treating the difference as the **obliteration volume** caused by mucosal thickening or pathology. The work establishes population reference values and demonstrates that CBCT combined with curve-based segmentation is feasible for routine 3D sinus assessment in implant planning.

## Key Contributions
- Validates CBCT + implant-planning software (SMOP) for volumetric maxillary sinus assessment in routine clinical workflow.
- Provides numeric reference ranges (mean osseous volume ~17 cm³; mean residual pneumatized volume ~15 cm³).
- Operationalizes "sinus obliteration" as the arithmetic difference between osseous and mucosa-bounded volumes — a reproducible metric.
- Tests, and largely rules out, several intuitive associations (age effects, dentition state effects, season effects on opacification).

## Methodology
- Retrospective cross-sectional review of 64 CBCT scans (128 sinuses) from a single tertiary center (2013).
- Device: KaVo 3D eXam CBCT (120 kV, 5.0 mA; voxel 0.125–0.4 mm; FOV 16 cm × 10–13.3 cm).
- Segmentation: parallel closed curves drawn every 2 mm in the coronal plane (15–25 curves per sinus); volume and surface computed by SMOP's interpolation tool.
- Two volumes per sinus measured: osseous-bordered and mucosal-bordered. Obliteration = osseous − mucosal.
- Covariates recorded: age, sex, dentition state, sinus pathology, apical lesions, communicating tooth roots, season of scan.

## Results
- Osseous-bordered sinus: mean surface 39.7 cm², mean volume **17.1 cm³**.
- Mucosa-bordered (residual pneumatized) sinus: mean surface 36.4 cm², mean volume **15 cm³**.
- Obliteration in **42.2%** of sinuses; mean obliterated volume **5.1 cm³**, associated with sinus pathology.
- Males had significantly larger mean osseous volume than females.
- No significant association between sinus volume and patient age or dentition state.
- No significant association between communicating tooth roots and sinus pathologies / unilateral opacity / apical radiolucency.
- No significant seasonality (autumn-winter vs spring-summer) for bilateral obliteration.
