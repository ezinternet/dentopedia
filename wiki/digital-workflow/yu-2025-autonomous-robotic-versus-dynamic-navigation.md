## One-line Summary
Prospective comparative study (83 patients, 135 implants) — autonomous robotic CAIS ~halved angular (1.62° vs 3.61°), platform (0.50 vs 1.12 mm) and apex (0.58 vs 1.36 mm) deviation vs dynamic navigation, and was faster.

## 한줄요약

## Summary
This prospective clinical study compared an autonomous dental implant robot (r-CAIS) against a dynamic navigation system (d-CAIS) in 83 partially edentulous patients receiving 135 implants. Using pre/post CBCT superimposition, the robot achieved significantly lower angular, platform and apical deviations — roughly half those of dynamic navigation — while also shortening combined drilling and placement time. Allocation was not randomized, so results should be read alongside the parallel RCT by Wei et al.

## Key Contributions
- One of the first clinical head-to-head comparisons of an autonomous implant robot vs dynamic navigation.
- Robot roughly halved all three accuracy metrics.
- Demonstrated time efficiency, countering the assumption that robotic setup is slower intra-operatively.

## Methodology
Prospective comparative, single center, Dec 2022–Mar 2024. r-CAIS 43 patients/71 implants; d-CAIS 40 patients/64 implants. Linear and angular deviation between planned and final positions via CBCT in 2D/3D; phased surgical timing. Non-randomized allocation.

## Results
- Angular deviation: 1.62° ± 0.93° (r-CAIS) vs 3.61° ± 1.65° (d-CAIS), p < 0.001.
- Platform deviation: 0.50 ± 0.19 vs 1.12 ± 0.51 mm, p < 0.001.
- Apex deviation: 0.58 ± 0.21 vs 1.36 ± 0.57 mm, p < 0.001.
- Drilling + placement time: 8.3 ± 3.4 vs 10.6 ± 3.8 min, p < 0.01; preparation time NS.
