## Summary
Narrative literature review (European Journal of Medical Research 2025) on AI practice in healthcare. Not dental-specific. Distinguished from other AI healthcare reviews by explicit emphasis on cross-disciplinary integration (AR/VR/IoT/wearables) and rehabilitation as deployment domain.

## Key Contributions
- **Robotic surgery breakdown** across pre/intra/post-operative phases
- **AI + rehabilitation** as deployment domain (less covered in dental-focused reviews)
- **Cross-tech integration**: AR, VR, IoT, wearable analytics as adjacent AI surfaces
- Calls for governance to overcome regulatory/ethical/trust barriers

## Methodology
- Narrative literature review; no formal protocol disclosed
- Thematic organisation by healthcare deployment domain
- No PRISMA, no quality grading

## Results
**Domain coverage**:
- Robot-assisted surgery: AI enhances pre/intra/post-operative phases
- Rehabilitation: personalised programs, robotic PT, real-time monitoring
- Medical imaging and diagnostics
- Virtual patient care
- Medical research and drug discovery
- Patient engagement and adherence
- Administrative applications

**Integration directions**:
- AI + IoT → real-time patient monitoring, early diagnosis, chronic illness management
- AI + AR/VR → medical education, procedure rehearsal
- AI + wearables → personalised health insights, proactive interventions

**Adoption barriers**:
- Data quality and bias
- Ethical and privacy concerns
- Regulatory and legal considerations
- Cost and accessibility

## Clinical Implications [claude해석]
- Most relevant to dental practice: wearable + AI analytics for chronic-disease patients (diabetes, cardiovascular) feeding into pre-treatment risk stratification — Korean clinics rarely receive structured wearable data feeds yet
- Robot-assisted surgery findings don't transfer directly to dental robotics (Yomi, etc.) — different scale, regulatory class
- IoT + AI patient monitoring is not yet operationalised in private dental clinics; large hospital ENT/OMFS departments more relevant adopters
