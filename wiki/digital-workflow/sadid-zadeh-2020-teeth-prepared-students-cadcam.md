## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional rubric-based evaluation of 334 typodont preparations made by senior dental students for CAD/CAM restorations, finding finish line quality the most frequent error (136/223 prostheses) — the criterion most critical for well-fitting CAD/CAM restorations.

## 한줄요약

단면연구(루브릭, 타이포돈트 형성치 334개): 본과 4학년 학생의 CAD/CAM 형성치에서 finish line(변연선) 품질 오류가 가장 빈번(223건 중 136건)했으며, 이는 CAD/CAM 수복물 적합도에 가장 결정적인 항목이다.

## Summary

A dental-education study in which four calibrated faculty applied a newly developed rubric to score senior students' tooth preparations for CAD/CAM restorations on four factors: retention/resistance form; marginal/internal adaptation (manufacturing); biology/esthetics/durability of the restorative material; and intraoral scanner impression. Across 111 lithium disilicate crown preparations (#4) and 223 monolithic zirconia partial FDP preparations (#3, #5) on typodonts, **finish line quality was by far the most common error (136 of 223 prostheses)**.

Because finish line geometry is exactly the preparation factor [[digital-workflow/revilla-leon-2025-tooth-preparation-factors-ios-accuracy-sr]] identifies as a primary driver of IOS accuracy (apical/intracrevicular and non-chamfer margins scan worse), this study supplies the educational/clinical-frequency counterpart: the factor most likely to be done poorly is the one that most degrades digital-workflow fit.

## Key Contributions

- Provides a 4-factor rubric (incl. an explicit intraoral-scanner-impression criterion) for assessing CAD/CAM preparations.
- Identifies finish line quality as the dominant, most-frequent preparation error in a predoctoral cohort.

## Methodology

- Design: Cross-sectional, rubric-based faculty assessment (AY 2018–19).
- Raters: 4 calibrated faculty members.
- Sample: 111 lithium disilicate crown preps (#4); 223 monolithic zirconia partial FDP preps (#3, #5); typodont teeth.

## Results

- Finish line quality: most common error, 136 errors / 223 prostheses.
- Recommendation: explicit rationale + adherence to detailed rubric criteria to prevent poor-fitting CAD/CAM restorations.
