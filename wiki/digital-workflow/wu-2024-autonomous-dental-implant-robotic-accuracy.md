Single-arm clinical study (74 patients, 86 implants) — autonomous implant robot (ADIRS) achieved coronal 0.61 ± 0.20 mm, apical 0.79 ± 0.32 mm, angular 2.56° ± 1.10° deviation, unaffected by region/length/surgeon.

## Summary
This single-arm clinical study quantified the placement accuracy of an autonomous dental implant robot system (ADIRS) in 74 partially edentulous patients (86 implants). With CBCT-based planning and a postoperative CBCT comparison, the robot achieved sub-millimeter coronal and apical deviation and ~2.6° angular deviation, with no intra-operative complications. Accuracy was independent of implant region, length, and operating surgeon — a key argument for de-skilling precision. There is no comparator arm, so it complements rather than replaces the robot-vs-navigation trials.

## Key Contributions
- Establishes absolute accuracy benchmark for a fully autonomous robot.
- Accuracy invariant to anatomical region, implant length, and surgeon.

## Methodology
Single-arm prospective; 74 patients/86 implants. Preoperative CBCT planning, robotic osteotomy and placement under navigation, postoperative CBCT deviation analysis; GLMM (α=.05).

## Results
- Coronal: 0.61 ± 0.20 mm (95% CI 0.23–1.0).
- Apical: 0.79 ± 0.32 mm (95% CI 0.17–1.41).
- Angular: 2.56° ± 1.10° (95% CI 0.42–4.70).
