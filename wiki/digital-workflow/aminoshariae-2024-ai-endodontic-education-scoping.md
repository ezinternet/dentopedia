## Summary
Scoping review (Journal of Endodontics May 2024) mapping AI use cases for endodontic education curriculum. Search to December 2023; 251 records screened, 35 relevant. Identifies 10 distinct domains where AI augments dental students' didactic and clinical endodontic learning. Aligned with Schwendicke et al. 2023 dentistry AI core curriculum.

## Key Contributions
- **10-domain framework** for AI in endodontic education
- Bridges AI capability to curriculum redesign explicitly
- Recommends individualised feedback, automated detection/diagnosis, AI-based student progress evaluation

## Methodology
- PRISMA-ScR scoping review style
- Databases: MEDLINE, Web of Science, Cochrane Library (to Dec 2023)
- Manual reference search; grey literature + ClinicalTrials.gov
- 251 records → 35 included after relevance screening

## Results
The 10 AI domains identified:

1. Radiographic interpretation
2. Differential diagnosis
3. Treatment planning and decision-making
4. Case difficulty assessment
5. Preclinical training
6. Advanced clinical simulation and case-based training
7. Real-time clinical guidance
8. Autonomous systems and robotics
9. Progress evaluation and personalised education
10. Calibration and standardisation

## Clinical/Educational Implications [claude해석]
- For Korean dental education: AI-augmented preclinical phantom training is the most actionable near-term application — minimal regulatory friction
- AI-based calibration would address inter-instructor variability in endodontic teaching, but requires institutional buy-in for standard ground truth
- Real-time clinical guidance use in students raises supervision and liability questions in Korean dental practice acts — pending regulatory clarity
