## One-line Summary
Comprehensive narrative review of S. mutans GtfB, GtfC, and GtfD — their distinct glucan products, differential adsorption to pellicle vs. bacterial surfaces, 3–4x activity enhancement upon adsorption, and coordinated roles in assembling the cariogenic biofilm EPS matrix.

## Summary
Bowen and Koo comprehensively review the biology of S. mutans glucosyltransferases. Three genetically distinct enzymes synthesise structurally different glucans from sucrose: GtfB produces primarily insoluble α-1,3-linked glucan and binds avidly to bacterial surfaces; GtfC produces both soluble and insoluble glucan and is the primary pellicle enzyme (detected within 1 min of tooth surface placement); GtfD forms predominantly soluble glucan acting as a primer for GtfB. Upon adsorption to saliva-coated hydroxyapatite (sHA), Gtf activity is enhanced 3–4x and Km values decrease 2–8-fold relative to solution-phase enzyme. GtfC affinity for sHA is highest by Scatchard analysis. GtfB adsorbs to non-Gtf producers (A. viscosus, L. casei) converting them to de facto glucan producers. Simultaneous GtfB/GtfC surface activity generates insoluble glucan that drives microcolony formation. Glucan constitutes 10–20% of dental plaque dry weight; fructan ~1–2%. Glucan-degrading enzymes (mutanase, dextranase) are plaque control targets, but solution-phase inhibitors often fail against adsorbed Gtf.

## Key Contributions
- Three-Gtf model (GtfB/C/D) with distinct substrate specificities, surface affinities, and roles.
- Pellicle-adsorbed GtfC: 3–4x enhanced activity; active within 1 min of oral placement.
- GtfB converts non-Gtf producers to glucan-forming organisms; drives interspecies EPS matrix assembly.
- Glucan 10–20% dry weight of dental plaque; fructan ~1–2%.
- Surface-adsorbed Gtf produces glucan with more α-1,3-linkages and branching than solution-phase enzyme.

## Methodology
Comprehensive narrative review. Evidence from in vitro biochemical Gtf purification, sHA pellicle binding assays (Scatchard analysis), in situ HA disc oral experiments, glucan structural analyses (dextranase/mutanase digestion), and gene deletion/cloning studies.

## Results
GtfC greatest sHA affinity (highest binding sites, Scatchard); GtfD fewest binding sites. Km values 2–8-fold lower after sHA adsorption. Active Gtf on tooth within 1 min. GtfB/GtfC simultaneous activity drives tight bacterial cell clustering and microcolony formation. Surface-formed glucan: increased α-1,3-linkages, 3,4-linked glucose major branch point. Glucan: 10–20% plaque dry weight; fructan: ~1–2%.
