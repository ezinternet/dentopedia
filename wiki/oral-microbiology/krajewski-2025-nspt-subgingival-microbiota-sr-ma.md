## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 115 studies across four microbiological technologies shows NSPT significantly reduces mean counts of disease-associated subgingival species (Pg, Td, Tf, Aa, Pi, En, Cr), with SPC providing further reductions; 16S rRNA diversity findings remain heterogeneous due to methodological variability.

## Summary

Krajewski et al. (2025) conducted the first SR+MA comprehensively comparing the effect of NSPT on subgingival microbiota across all four dominant microbiological detection technologies. A literature search in Medline, EMBASE, LILACS, and Cochrane (updated June 2024) identified 115 eligible studies (checkerboard DNA–DNA hybridisation n=41, qPCR n=30, bacterial culture n=36, 16S rRNA gene sequencing n=8) from 31 countries, spanning 1983–2024.

Meta-analyses (longitudinal random-effects model, Hedges' g) showed significant decreases in mean counts of *Aggregatibacter actinomycetemcomitans* (Aa), *Treponema denticola* (Td), *Porphyromonas gingivalis* (Pg), *Tannerella forsythia* (Tf), *Eubacterium nodatum* (En), *Prevotella intermedia* (Pi), and *Campylobacter rectus* (Cr) after NSPT. Supportive periodontal care (SPC) provided a further significant decrease in mean counts of Pg and Td at 6 months after NSPT.

Qualitative analysis of 16S rRNA studies showed consistent decrease of *P. gingivalis* at species level and decrease of genus *Treponema* with increases in health-associated *Streptococcus* and *Rothia*. However, alpha diversity (Shannon index) and beta diversity findings were heterogeneous across studies, attributed to small sample sizes, variable region choice, and pooled vs. site-specific sampling strategies.

Adjunctive therapies (CHX, PDT, laser) did not show significant additional effects on mean microbial counts versus NSPT alone. Only ~5% of included studies calculated sample sizes based on microbiological outcomes, indicating widespread underpowering for microbiological endpoints.

## Key Contributions

- Confirms NSPT-driven significant reduction of seven disease-associated species (Aa, Td, Pg, Tf, En, Pi, Cr) via meta-analysis across multiple detection technologies
- Demonstrates SPC provision at 6 months further reduces Pg and Td mean counts beyond NSPT alone
- Qualitative Socransky complex analysis (12 checkerboard studies): red and orange complex decrease; blue (*Actinomyces*) and white complex increase — sustained to 12 months
- 16S rRNA data: heterogeneous alpha/beta diversity results; only decrease of *P. gingivalis* species consistent across studies
- Identifies split-mouth design as a source of smaller effect sizes (potential carry-across effect) — parallel-arm studies recommended for future research
- Proposes standardized microbiological reporting framework: site-specific sampling, sample size on Shannon index, parallel-arm design, V1–V2 variable region for 16S

## Methodology

- **Design**: SR+MA; PROSPERO CRD42020167170; PRISMA compliant
- **Databases**: Medline (OVID), EMBASE, LILACS, Cochrane; search updated 1 June 2024
- **Studies**: 115 included from 196 meeting criteria (3,067 retrieved; kappa 0.68/0.77 for title and full-text screening)
- **Technologies analysed**: checkerboard (n=41), qPCR (n=30), bacterial culture (n=36), 16S rRNA (n=8)
- **Statistical method**: DerSimonian-Laird longitudinal random-effects model; Hedges' g; minimum ≥5 studies per species/technology/timepoint for pooling
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane RoB2 (RCTs); ROBINS-I (non-RCTs)
- **Population**: systemically healthy adults ≥18 years with periodontitis; antibiotics within 3 months = exclusion
- **Follow-up**: minimum 6 weeks; most common 3 months (61%); also 1.5, 6, 9, 12, 24 months

## Results

**Significant reductions post-NSPT (meta-analysis)**:

| Species | Technologies | Key timepoints |
|---|---|---|
| *P. gingivalis* (Pg) | Checkerboard, qPCR, culture | 3 and 6 months |
| *T. denticola* (Td) | qPCR | 1.5, 3, and 6 months |
| *T. forsythia* (Tf) | qPCR | 1.5, 3, and 6 months |
| *A. actinomycetemcomitans* (Aa) | qPCR, culture | 3 and 6 months |
| *P. intermedia* (Pi) | Checkerboard, qPCR | 3 months |
| *E. nodatum* (En) | Checkerboard | 3 months |
| *C. rectus* (Cr) | Checkerboard | 3 months |

**SPC effect**: Checkerboard subgroup meta-analysis at 6 months — significantly higher effect sizes for Pg and Td in studies providing SPC during the trial.

**CHX adjunct**: No significant additional effect on Pg mean counts vs. NSPT alone (checkerboard cohort subgroup analysis).

**Adjunctive therapies overall**: similar microbiological profile of changes with or without adjuncts at 3 months; no significant subgroup differences.

**Smokers**: no statistically significant changes in mean counts at 3 months (but limited to few studies).

**Split-mouth vs. whole-mouth design**: smaller effect sizes for Pg and Tf in qPCR split-mouth studies; significant difference for Aa (p=0.03), suggesting carry-across effect.

**16S rRNA (qualitative)**:
- Species level: consistent significant decrease of *P. gingivalis* only; other species heterogeneous
- Genus level: decrease in *Treponema* (4/7 studies); increase in *Streptococcus* (4/7) and *Rothia* (3/7)
- Alpha diversity: mostly no significant changes to Shannon index; two studies showed transient decreases
- Beta diversity: heterogeneous; only one study showed significant Bray-Curtis change at 3 months

**Socransky complex shift** (qualitative, 12 checkerboard studies): red and orange complex bacteria proportions decreased; blue (*Actinomyces*) and white complex species increased; sustained to 12 months.

**Clinical secondary outcomes**: reduction in FM-PPD, FM-CAL, FMBS, and FMPS at 3 months across all technologies; FMPS heterogeneous (range 10%–64% at 3 months).

**Sample size limitation**: only ~5% of studies used microbiological outcomes for sample size calculation; 41% used clinical outcomes (CAL/PPD); 50% provided no information — most studies underpowered for microbiological endpoints.
