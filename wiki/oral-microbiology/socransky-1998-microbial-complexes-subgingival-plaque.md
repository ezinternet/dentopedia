## One-line Summary

Landmark cross-sectional study (185 subjects, 13,261 subgingival plaque samples, 40 taxa by checkerboard DNA-DNA hybridization) that defined five reproducible microbial complexes, establishing the "red complex" (P. gingivalis, T. forsythia, T. denticola) as the group most tightly associated with pocket depth and bleeding on probing.

## Summary

Socransky and colleagues analyzed 13,261 subgingival plaque samples from 185 adults (160 with periodontitis, 25 without), quantifying 40 bacterial taxa by checkerboard DNA-DNA hybridization with whole-genomic probes. Using phi-coefficient clustering plus principal components and correspondence analysis, they found **five recurrent microbial complexes** that emerged consistently regardless of the analytical method. These were assigned color codes that have organized periodontal microbiology ever since: **red** (the disease-associated triad), **orange** (a disease-associated "bridge" community), and the more health-/early-colonization-associated **yellow** (oral streptococci), **green**, and **purple** complexes, with a handful of outlier species.

The central finding is that the **red complex** — *Porphyromonas gingivalis*, *Tannerella forsythia* (then *Bacteroides forsythus*), and *Treponema denticola* — is a tightly co-occurring group whose presence relates **strikingly** to clinical disease severity, particularly **pocket depth and bleeding on probing**. The orange complex (notably *Fusobacterium nucleatum*, *Prevotella intermedia/nigrescens*, *Peptostreptococcus micros*) typically precedes and accompanies red-complex colonization, suggesting an ecological succession from early colonizers toward a late, disease-associated community.

This paper crystallized the **"specific pathogen / red-complex" paradigm** of periodontitis. Later community-ecology frameworks (keystone-pathogen, polymicrobial synergy and dysbiosis) reinterpret these same organisms not as independent causal agents but as low-abundance community remodelers — making this study the indispensable historical anchor those models build "beyond."

## Key Contributions

- Defined **five reproducible subgingival microbial complexes** stable across multiple multivariate methods.
- Introduced the enduring **color-coded complex nomenclature** (red, orange, yellow, green, purple) used in periodontal microbiology teaching and research.
- Established the **red complex** (*P. gingivalis*, *T. forsythia*, *T. denticola*) as the group most strongly correlated with pocket depth and bleeding on probing.
- Described an implicit **ecological succession**: early colonizers (yellow/purple/green) → orange "bridge" → red complex in deep, inflamed pockets.
- Demonstrated the power of **checkerboard DNA-DNA hybridization** for large-scale community-level plaque microbiology.

## Methodology

- **Design**: cross-sectional microbiological survey.
- **Subjects**: 185 adults (mean age 51 ± 16 y); 160 periodontitis, 25 periodontally healthy.
- **Sampling**: subgingival plaque from the mesial aspect of every tooth → 13,261 samples.
- **Microbiology**: 40 taxa detected/quantified by checkerboard DNA-DNA hybridization using whole-genomic DNA probes.
- **Clinical**: 6 sites/tooth (pocket depth, bleeding on probing, etc.).
- **Analysis**: phi-coefficient species similarity → averaged unweighted linkage clustering; community ordination by PCA and correspondence analysis.

## Results

- **Five major complexes** recurred under any analytical method.
- **Red complex**: *T. forsythia*, *P. gingivalis*, *T. denticola* — tightly related; most strongly associated with deep pockets and bleeding on probing.
- **Orange complex**: *F. nucleatum/periodonticum* subspecies, *P. intermedia*, *P. nigrescens*, *P. micros* (core), plus *Eubacterium nodatum*, *Campylobacter rectus/showae/gracilis*, *S. constellatus* (associated) — the disease-associated bridge.
- **Yellow complex**: *S. sanguis*, *S. oralis*, *S. mitis*, *S. gordonii*, *S. intermedius* (early colonizers).
- **Green complex**: *Capnocytophaga* spp., *C. concisus*, *E. corrodens*, *A. actinomycetemcomitans* serotype a.
- **Purple complex**: *Veillonella parvula*, *Actinomyces odontolyticus*.
- **Outliers**: *A. actinomycetemcomitans* serotype b, *Selenomonas noxia*, *A. naeslundii* genospecies 2.
