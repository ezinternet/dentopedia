## One-line Summary
Perspective review coining and defining 'matrixome' — the full inventory of EPS components (polysaccharides, proteins, eDNA/eRNA, lipids, biogenic minerals) across model mono- and polymicrobial biofilms — and advocating matrixome-targeted combinatorial antibiofilm strategies.

## Summary
Karygianni et al. introduce the term 'matrixome' — adapted from the eukaryotic 'matrisome' — to denote the entire inventory of extracellular polymeric substances (EPS) associated with biofilm assembly, physico-chemical properties, and virulence. The review systematically catalogues EPS components across major model organisms: S. mutans glucans/fructans and Gtf/Ftf enzymes; P. aeruginosa Psl/Pel/alginate; V. cholerae VPS; C. albicans mannans/β-glucans; biogenic minerals (CaCO3 in B. subtilis, Mycobacterium). A pivotal finding highlighted is that S. mutans Gtfs bind non-Gtf producers (A. viscosus, L. casei, C. albicans), converting them to glucan producers — a key mechanism of cross-species and cross-kingdom cariogenic biofilm assembly. A three-phase matrix assembly model (surface EPS → 3D expansion → microcolony scaffold) is conserved across diverse biofilm formers. The authors emphasise that polymicrobial biofilm EPS is critically under-studied and call for in-depth mechanistic and in vivo research.

## Key Contributions
- Coins and defines 'matrixome' as the full EPS inventory of a biofilm community.
- Comprehensive EPS taxonomy table (Table 1) across B. subtilis, S. aureus, S. mutans, P. aeruginosa, V. cholerae, C. albicans.
- Biogenic minerals (CaCO3, calcite) recognised as matrixome components.
- S. mutans Gtf binding to heterologous microbial surfaces — cross-species/kingdom glucan production.
- Three-phase matrix assembly model conserved across diverse biofilm-forming organisms.

## Methodology
Perspective/narrative review (Trends in Microbiology). Synthesises CLSM/FISH imaging studies, single-cell live imaging, in vitro multi-species biofilm experiments, and comparative EPS biochemistry. Model organisms: B. subtilis, S. aureus, S. mutans, P. aeruginosa, V. cholerae, C. albicans, E. coli.

## Results
Six-species oral biofilm (S. mutans, S. oralis, A. oris, F. nucleatum, V. dispar, C. albicans) visualised by CLSM with component-specific staining. Gtfs bind A. viscosus, L. casei, and C. albicans surfaces converting non-producers to glucan formers. CaCO3 in B. subtilis and M. smegmatis biofilm matrix. Proteus spp. cause catheter encrustation via calcium/magnesium minerals. Flagella and type IV pili act as mechanosensors triggering EPS production. Three-phase matrix assembly model conserved across P. aeruginosa, B. subtilis, V. cholerae, S. mutans.
