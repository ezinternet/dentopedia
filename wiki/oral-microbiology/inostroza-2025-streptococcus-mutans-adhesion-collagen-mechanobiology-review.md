Narrative review (Crit Rev Microbiol 2025) of *S. mutans* collagen-binding proteins (SpaP, WapA, Cnm, Cbm) and their mechanobiology — characterized by AFM single-molecule (SMFS) and single-cell (SCFS) force spectroscopy — as drivers of dentin/ECM adhesion, coaggregation, and virulence, and as targets for anti-biofilm caries prevention.

## Summary

*Streptococcus mutans* is the keystone caries-associated bacterium: it adheres to oral tissue surfaces and promotes biofilm formation. Beyond its well-known glucosyltransferase/EPS-matrix pathway, it expresses specialized **surface adhesins**, among which **collagen-binding proteins (CBPs)** mediate attachment to **dentin**, **bacterial coaggregation**, and **extracellular matrix (ECM) invasion**. This narrative review reframes CBP–collagen attachment as a **mechanobiological** process — how the bonds behave under physiological mechanical force — and argues that understanding this force-dependent behavior is crucial for preventing biofilm formation in both oral and remote (systemic) tissues.

## Key Contributions

- Reviews the mechanical properties of the four relevant *S. mutans* CBPs — **SpaP, WapA, Cnm, Cbm** — and how their adhesive/mechanobiological characteristics contribute to virulence toward the host.
- Establishes **atomic force microscopy (AFM)**-based force spectroscopy as the key interdisciplinary toolset for this question:
  - **Single-molecule force spectroscopy (SMFS)** — probes individual CBP–collagen bond mechanics.
  - **Single-cell force spectroscopy (SCFS)** — probes whole-cell *S. mutans* attachment to collagen substrates in real time under physiological conditions.
- Proposes SMFS/SCFS as a platform to discover **novel anti-biofilm molecules** against *S. mutans*, i.e. a route toward caries-preventive treatments.

## Methodology

Narrative review of recent evidence. The mechanistic backbone is AFM force spectroscopy: SMFS for single CBP–collagen bonds and SCFS for whole-cell attachment to collagen, both measured in real time under physiological conditions. ## Results

Abstract-level findings:

- CBPs (SpaP, WapA, Cnm, Cbm) mediate *S. mutans* adhesion to dentin, coaggregation, and ECM invasion.
- The mechanobiological and adhesive properties of these adhesins drive host virulence.
- Force-spectroscopy characterization of CBP–collagen attachment is positioned as the basis for anti-biofilm, caries-preventive strategies — including in remote tissues where *S. mutans* may disseminate.
