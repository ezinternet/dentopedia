## One-line Summary
Narrative critical review synthesizing evidence that F. nucleatum is enriched in OSCC and OPMDs and promotes carcinogenesis via STAT3/cyclin D1/MMP-9 and Fap2/Gal-GalNAc, with paradoxical survival benefit in OSCC (Neuzillet n=151, 82.1% Fn+ → better OS/RFS/MFS; Chen n=68, 55.8% Fn-high → better CSS/RFS).

## Summary
McIlvanna et al. critically synthesize the evidence for F. nucleatum involvement in oral carcinogenesis. F. nucleatum constitutes ~25% of dental plaque as a core species in both health and disease but benefits from a 3-log increase in total microbial load in periodontal inflammation. It serves as a bridging organism facilitating progression from early (streptococcal) to late (P. gingivalis) colonizers, and is enriched in OSCC tumors and OPMDs (supporting early malignant transformation rather than mere opportunistic colonization). Murine OSCC models show F. nucleatum-infected tumors are 2.5× larger and more invasive, with STAT3/cyclin D1/MMP-9 as key oncogenic mediators. Paradoxically, two prognostic cohort studies (Neuzillet n=151, 82.1% Fn+; Chen n=68, 55.8% Fn-high) find F. nucleatum positivity associated with better survival in OSCC — the opposite of CRC — possibly reflecting distinct patient biology (HPV-negative, older, non-smokers/non-drinkers).

## Key Contributions
- Confirmed F. nucleatum enrichment in OSCC and OPMDs, suggesting a role in early malignant transformation.
- Identified STAT3 as the central oncogenic signaling hub (controls proliferation, angiogenesis, invasion, metastasis) in F. nucleatum-associated OSCC.
- Reported paradoxical favorable OSCC prognosis with F. nucleatum positivity (opposite of CRC).
- Described Fap2/Gal-GalNAc axis: PNA-staining intensity weak→intermediate→strong along normal→dysplasia→malignancy, matching F. nucleatum abundance.
- Established F. nucleatum as a bridging organism (early/late colonizer link) with ammonia production creating alkaline niche for P. gingivalis.

## Methodology
- Narrative critical review (non-systematic). Two prospective prognostic studies reviewed:
  - Neuzillet et al. (2021): fresh-frozen OSCC; qPCR; n=151; 82.1% (124/151) Fn-positive.
  - Chen et al. (2020): fresh-frozen HNSCC; qPCR; n=68; 55.8% (38/68) Fn-high.
- Murine OSCC model (Binder Gallimindi et al.): chemically-induced oral tumors infected with F. nucleatum → 2.5× larger tumors, significantly more invasive.
- Harrandah et al.: separate murine oral tumor model; upregulated MMP1/9, IL-8, MYC, JAK1, STAT3, ZEB1, TGF-β; more and larger lesions in Fn-infected animals.

## Results
- Neuzillet (n=151): 82.1% Fn+ → better OS, RFS, MFS; associated with older >56 years, non-drinkers, low pN stage.
- Chen (n=68): 55.8% Fn-high → better cancer-specific survival + RFS; non-smokers, lower pT/pN stage; LXN/SMARCA2 hypermethylation.
- Murine OSCC: Fn-infected tumors 2.5× larger, significantly more invasive; cyclin D1 + MMP-9 key drivers; STAT3 central mediator.
- OSCC bacteriome enrichment: F. nucleatum most abundant (Al-Hebshi NGS); LPS biosynthesis, flagellar assembly, chemotaxis gene pathways enriched.
- Gal-GalNAc–Fap2 gradient: PNA staining normal (weak) → dysplasia (intermediate) → malignant SCC (strong).
- F. nucleatum core species: ~25% of dental plaque in health and disease; 3-log increase in total load during periodontal inflammation.
