## One-line Summary
Narrative review formalizing the PSD model with mechanistic depth on P. gingivalis–S. gordonii Ptk1/Ltp1/CdhR interspecies signaling, driver–passenger pathobiont dynamics, and cooperative GEC invasion to explain chronic polymicrobial inflammatory disease.

## 한줄요약

## Summary
Lamont and Hajishengallis formalize the PSD framework introduced in 2012, adding molecular granularity. Keystone pathogen P. gingivalis communicates with accessory pathogen S. gordonii via a tightly regulated Ptk1/Ltp1/CdhR tyrosine phosphorylation cascade that governs community formation and constrains overgrowth. Pathobionts (Filifactor alocis, Peptostreptococcus stomatis, Prevotella spp.) emerge as main drivers of tissue destruction in advanced disease, analogous to the colorectal cancer driver–passenger model. GEC invasion by P. gingivalis + F. nucleatum is cooperative and higher than either species alone. SerB-mediated CXCL8/CXCL9/10/11 suppression shifts the immune balance toward Th17-driven bone loss while suppressing Th1 cell-mediated immunity. Complement components at up to 80% of serum concentration in GCF are evaded by gingipain-class proteases of P. gingivalis, T. forsythia, and P. intermedia.

## Key Contributions
- Mechanistic Ptk1/Ltp1/CdhR signal cascade governing P. gingivalis–S. gordonii community formation.
- Driver–passenger pathobiont model applied to periodontitis (Filifactor alocis, Peptostreptococcus stomatis, Prevotella spp., Megasphaera, Selenomonas, Desulfobulbus).
- Cooperative GEC invasion: P. gingivalis + F. nucleatum invade in higher numbers than either alone.
- T. denticola suppresses F. nucleatum-driven IL-8 in GECs by blocking lysosomal fusion.
- A. actinomycetemcomitans spatial cross-feeding: optimal >4 µm from S. gordonii for H2O2 balance.
- Black Queen Hypothesis applied: keystone pathogens maintain costly community-essential functions.

## Methodology
- Narrative synthesis of mouse dual-infection models, in-vitro co-culture/invasion assays, metatranscriptomic human data, and biochemical pathway studies.
- Mouse co-infection: P. gingivalis + S. gordonii → greater alveolar bone loss than monoinfection.
- GCF complement: components present at up to 80% of serum concentration.
- A. actinomycetemcomitans spatial positioning: >4 µm from S. gordonii required for optimal cross-feeding.

## Results
- P. gingivalis + S. gordonii: more bone loss in mice than either alone (S. gordonii = accessory pathogen).
- Ptk1/Ltp1 regulates FimA/Mfa1 adhesin expression and constrains P. gingivalis community development over time.
- Metatranscriptomic data: majority of virulence upregulation in periodontitis derived from underappreciated species, not red complex.
- T. denticola + P. gingivalis: metabolic cross-feeding and mutually upregulated virulence factors (dentilisin, RgpA/Kgp/HagA) in co-culture.
- SerB suppresses CXCL8, CXCL9, CXCL10, CXCL11 via NF-κB p65 and IRF-1/Stat1 → promotes Th17 over Th1.
