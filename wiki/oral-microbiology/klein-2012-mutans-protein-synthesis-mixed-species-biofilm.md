## One-line Summary
In-vitro MudPIT proteomics of S. mutans in 3-species biofilms on saliva-coated hydroxyapatite reveals that mixed-species context significantly up-regulates glucan-synthesis (GtfB/C, DexA, GbpB) and acid-tolerance proteins (F1F0-ATPase, FabM, GroEL) vs. single-species biofilms (P<0.05), detecting up to 60% of the UA159 proteome.

## Summary
Klein et al. applied MudPIT shotgun proteomics to characterize S. mutans UA159 protein expression during formation of 3-species biofilms (with A. naeslundii and S. oralis) on saliva-coated hydroxyapatite discs following sucrose-induced ecological challenge. The method detected up to 60% of the S. mutans proteome within biofilms. Mixed-species conditions significantly elevated glucan-synthesis (gtfB, gtfC, dexA) and glucan-binding (gbpB) proteins, as well as acid-stress adaptations (F1F0-ATPase, FabM, GroEL), compared with single-species controls. After 1% sucrose introduction at 29 h, S. oralis predominance reversed and S. mutans became dominant by 115 h. Findings were corroborated by RT-qPCR across 4 time points (n=12). This study mechanistically explains how interspecies context amplifies S. mutans cariogenic virulence.

## Key Contributions
- First quantitative MudPIT proteomics dataset for S. mutans in an ecologically relevant 3-species biofilm model.
- Demonstrated that up to 60% of the S. mutans UA159 proteome is detectable in biofilms.
- Showed mixed-species context amplifies GtfB/C-driven glucan matrix assembly and acid-tolerance mechanisms.
- Linked sucrose introduction (1%) to ecological dominance shift from S. oralis to S. mutans by 115 h.
- Provided proteomics-transcriptomics concordance validating spectral count as an abundance proxy.

## Methodology
- Design: in-vitro, batch culture, saliva-coated hydroxyapatite (sHA) disc biofilm model.
- Organisms: S. mutans UA159 (10² CFU/mL) + A. naeslundii ATCC 12104 (10⁶ CFU/mL) + S. oralis ATCC 35037 (10⁷ CFU/mL); comparison with S. mutans single-species biofilms.
- Sucrose challenge: 0.1% sucrose for 0–29 h, then 1% sucrose at 29 h; biofilms harvested at 67 h and 115 h.
- Proteomics: MudPIT (12-step SCX/C18 LC-MS/MS on LTQ-Orbitrap); DTASelect 2.0 at 0.1% FPR; abundance by spectral count normalized to S. mutans CFU.
- RT-qPCR: 43, 67, 91, 115 h time points; 12 genes monitored; n=12 (6 experiments × duplicate).
- Stats: ANOVA + Tukey; significance P<0.05.

## Results
- Up to 60% of S. mutans UA159 encoded proteins detected by MudPIT in biofilm conditions.
- Glucan-synthesis proteins GtfB, GtfC, DexA and glucan-binding protein GbpB: significantly higher spectral counts in mixed-species vs. single-species biofilms at both 67 h and 115 h (P<0.05).
- Acid-tolerance cluster (F1F0-ATPase/AtpD, FabM, branched-chain AA metabolism, GroEL): augmented in mixed-species context.
- Ecological shift: S. oralis dominant at 29 h; S. mutans dominant at 115 h post-sucrose challenge.
- RT-qPCR expression data for gtfB/C, dexA, gbpB concordant with protein abundance trends.
