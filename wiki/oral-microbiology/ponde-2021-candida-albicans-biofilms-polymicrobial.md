## Summary
Ponde et al. comprehensively review C. albicans biofilm development through four stages: adhesion (mediated by Als, Hwp, and Iff/Hyr adhesin families), microcolony formation, maturation (ECM: proteins 55%, carbohydrates 25%, lipids 15%, nucleic acids 5%; predominant polysaccharide: mannan–β-1,6 glucan complex), and dispersal of virulence-enhanced cells. A core transcriptional network of 6 regulators (Bcr1p, Tec1p, Efg1p, Ndt80p, Rob1p, Brg1p) controls ~1,000 genes governing hypha formation, adhesion, ECM production, and drug resistance. Biofilm maturation occurs within 24–48 h on PVC catheters. Dispersed cells display enhanced virulence, filamentation capacity, and antifungal resistance. Cross-kingdom interactions with S. mutans (Gtf-mediated glucan coating of fungal surfaces) and R. dentocariosa (inhibition of dispersal) highlight the polymicrobial context. Candidaemia affects >400,000 patients/year with ~50% mortality.

## Key Contributions
- ECM quantified: proteins 55%, carbohydrates 25%, lipids 15%, nucleic acids 5%.
- Six-factor core transcriptional network governing ~1,000 biofilm-development genes.
- eDNA as structural scaffold; DNase + acetazolamide enhances amphotericin B efficacy.
- Dispersed cells are pre-conditioned for maximum virulence.
- R. dentocariosa inhibits C. albicans dispersal; oral bacteria shape fungal dispersal ecology.

## Methodology
Narrative (non-systematic) review. Draws on C. albicans gene-deletion studies, rat catheter/denture stomatitis models, in vitro multispecies biofilm experiments, and clinical epidemiological data.

## Results
Candidaemia: >400,000 cases/year; ~50% mortality. Biofilm maturation: 24–48 h (PVC), 38–72 h (PMMA). 51 total transcription factors identified; 6-factor core network essential for in vivo biofilm. Zap1p negatively regulates β-1,3 glucan via GCA1/GCA2; Rlm1p positively regulates ECM. DNase + acetazolamide improves amphotericin B efficacy against C. albicans biofilm in vitro.
