## One-line Summary
Narrative review proposing the PSD (Polymicrobial Synergy and Dysbiosis) model, in which P. gingivalis acting as a keystone pathogen at <0.01% of the microbiota remodels a symbiotic community into a bone-destructive dysbiotic state via complement subversion and chemokine paralysis.

## 한줄요약
Narrative review: P. gingivalis가 전체 미생물의 <0.01%에 불과하지만 keystone pathogen으로 작용해 공생 군집을 dysbiotic 상태로 전환·치조골 파괴를 유도하는 PSD 모델 제안.

## Summary
Hajishengallis and Lamont propose the PSD model to replace the reductionist "red complex" paradigm of periodontitis. The core argument is that periodontitis results from a synergistic dysbiotic community rather than specific exogenous pathogens. P. gingivalis, even at <0.01% of total microbiota, acts as a keystone pathogen by subverting host immunity (CXCL8/IL-8 chemokine paralysis via SerB; C5aR–TLR2 crosstalk to disable neutrophil killing) and facilitating the overgrowth of other organisms. Mouse model data show P. gingivalis cannot cause disease in germ-free animals, establishing community-dependence. The model defines keystone and accessory pathogen roles and proposes that the community-sustained inflammatory milieu itself serves as a nutrient reservoir for inflammophilic bacteria.

## Key Contributions
- Introduced the PSD model and the term "keystone pathogen" for periodontitis research.
- Established community-dependence of P. gingivalis pathogenicity (fails in germ-free mice).
- Identified C5aR as required for P. gingivalis-driven dysbiosis in mice.
- Defined "local chemokine paralysis" (SerB suppressing CXCL8) as an early evasion mechanism.
- Recognized >700 oral species with ~50 present per site — far beyond the red complex triad.

## Methodology
- Narrative synthesis of mouse oral gavage model data, non-human primate studies, human 16S microbiome surveys, and in-vitro mechanistic experiments.
- Key quantitative anchor: P. gingivalis at <0.01% of total microbiota caused alveolar bone loss and shifted microbiota composition in SPF mice; no disease in germ-free mice.
- Microbiome context: oral cavity harbors >700 species; ~200 per individual; ~50 per site.

## Results
- P. gingivalis at <0.01% of total microbiota sufficient to remodel symbiotic → dysbiotic community and trigger bone loss in mice.
- C5aR-deficient mice resist P. gingivalis colonization, dysbiosis, and periodontitis.
- SerB-mediated CXCL8 suppression delays neutrophil recruitment, enabling initial colonization.
- ~500 proteins differentially expressed in P. gingivalis when in community with S. gordonii + F. nucleatum.
- Del-1 knockout mice develop bone loss without P. gingivalis — alternative dysbiosis pathway exists.
