## One-line Summary
Narrative review detailing how P. gingivalis serially subverts gingival epithelial cell innate immunity (FimA/SerB invasion, chemokine paralysis, miRNA reprogramming), complement (gingipain-mediated C3 degradation, C5aR–TLR2 crosstalk), and adaptive immunity (IL-12/IFN-γ suppression via ERK1/2-IRF-1) to function as a keystone pathogen orchestrating periodontal dysbiosis.

## Summary
This review systematically details the molecular arsenal by which P. gingivalis manipulates host immunity at three levels. At the epithelial interface, FimA fimbriae engage β1-integrin while SerB dephosphorylates NF-κBp65 (S536), suppressing CXCL8 production and neutrophil recruitment (chemokine paralysis); Ndk blocks P2X7 apoptosis. In the GCF/immune-cell compartment, gingipains degrade C3 (blocking all complement pathways) while independently generating C5a via C5-convertase-like activity, activating C5aR–TLR2 crosstalk in macrophages to suppress iNOS killing and IL-12/IFN-γ immunity while upregulating bone-resorptive cytokines (TNF, IL-1β, IL-6, IL-17A). P. gingivalis also differentially regulates ~30% of its proteome in community contexts, and interspecies interactions (Mfa1–SspA/B with S. gordonii) are tightly regulated via tyrosine kinase/phosphatase signaling. Collectively these mechanisms allow a quantitatively minor organism to sustain a nutrient-rich inflammatory dysbiosis benefiting the entire community.

## Key Contributions
- Detailed FimA–β1-integrin invasion route and SerB-mediated cofilin/NF-κBp65 dephosphorylation.
- Revealed C5aR–TLR2 crosstalk as the core macrophage subversion axis (cAMP-PKA → GSK3β inhibition → iNOS suppression).
- Showed gingipains degrade C3 across all initiation pathways and generate C5a independently.
- Demonstrated P. gingivalis regulates ~30% of expressed proteome by community/planktonic/epithelial context.
- Identified miR-105 (suppresses TLR2) and miR-203 (inhibits SOCS3/6) as P. gingivalis-induced GEC reprogramming.

## Methodology
- Narrative review integrating in-vitro cell biology (GEC invasion assays, macrophage killing assays), mouse oral gavage model (C5aR-KO, TLR2-KO, CR3-KO), and proteome/transcriptome studies.
- SerB-deficient mutant data: less bone loss + enhanced neutrophil recruitment vs. WT P. gingivalis.
- P. gingivalis proteome context-dependence: ~30% of expressed proteome regulated by niche.

## Results
- SerB dephosphorylates NF-κBp65-S536 → blocks CXCL8/IL-8 transcription; also suppresses CXCL9/10/11 via IRF-1/Stat1.
- C5aR–TLR2 crosstalk: synergistic cAMP-PKA → GSK3β inhibition → iNOS downregulation; ERK1/2-IRF-1 axis suppresses IL-12p35/p40.
- Gingipains degrade C3 (all three complement pathways blocked); generate C5a via HRgpA/RgpB (C5-convertase-like); degrade C5b to prevent MAC.
- C5aR-KO mice: P. gingivalis fails to colonize, no dysbiosis, no bone loss.
- CR3-KO macrophages: shorter intracellular P. gingivalis persistence vs. WT.
- Bone-resorptive cytokines (TNF, IL-1β, IL-6, IL-17A) upregulated via C5aR; IL-12 selectively downregulated.
