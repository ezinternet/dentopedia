## One-line Summary
Narrative review systematically cataloguing the extracellular matrix of dental plaque — carbohydrates (~20% dry weight), proteins, eDNA, and lipids — and their structural and virulence roles in supragingival caries and subgingival periodontal disease.

## Summary
Jakubovics et al. comprehensively review the extracellular matrix of dental plaque biofilms, an undercharacterised component compared to the microbial cells themselves. The matrix is composed of carbohydrates (glucans 2–10% dry weight, fructans at lower amounts, capsular polysaccharides, poly-N-acetyl-D-glucosamine), proteins (adhesins, glucosyltransferases, DNABII proteins), extracellular DNA, and lipids including lipoteichoic acids. The review emphasises that the subgingival matrix supporting periodontal disease progression is chemically distinct and far less understood than the sucrose-derived supragingival matrix driving caries. Key findings include the structural scaffolding role of eDNA (vulnerable to DNase), the dual matrix/virulence role of DNABII proteins, and the immune-modulating function of P. gingivalis capsular polysaccharides. The matrix is proposed as an underexploited therapeutic target.

## Key Contributions
- Establishes the major EPS classes: carbohydrates (~20% dry wt supragingival), proteins (~40% dry wt), eDNA, lipids.
- Glucans (2–10% dry weight) and fructans as principal sucrose-derived cariogenic matrix polymers.
- DNABII proteins as structural matrix components and therapeutic dispersal targets.
- Poly-N-acetyl-D-glucosamine as key A. actinomycetemcomitans adhesion/positioning mediator.
- Proposes subgingival EPS matrix as critical but understudied target for periodontal disease control.

## Methodology
Narrative (non-systematic) review. No defined population/comparator; synthesises in vitro biofilm experiments, electron microscopy, proteomics, and murine model data across published literature.

## Results
Carbohydrates ~20% dry weight (supragingival plaque); glucans 2–10%; ~80% water; ~40% dry-weight protein. Lipoteichoic acids associate with glucan polymers and influence S. gordonii biofilm structure. Anti-DNABII immunotherapy disperses biofilms in vivo. DNase treatment disrupts eDNA-dependent matrix structural integrity. P. gingivalis capsule reduces phagocytosis but is required for F. nucleatum coaggregation and synergistic murine periodontitis virulence.
