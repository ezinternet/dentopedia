## Summary
This is the JEBDP-commissioned commentary/critical appraisal of Moro et al. 2022 (the dentist burnout SR+MA). Afrashtehfar and Jurado restate the underlying study methodology, endorse the MBI-only inclusion criterion, and reinforce the emotional-exhaustion-dominant pattern in the findings. No new data, no re-analysis — its value is as a published peer-reviewer perspective demonstrating that the field accepted the Moro SR+MA's conclusions.

## Key Contributions
- Independent endorsement of the MBI-only methodology used in Moro 2022
- Reinforces the operational message that emotional exhaustion (EE) is the dominant subscale signal in dental burnout
- Provides the "commentary" voice that lets you cite Moro 2022 with reduced single-paper risk

## Methodology
Standard JEBDP commentary structure — restates the original Article Title and Bibliographic Information, Source of Funding, Type of Study/Design, Selection Criteria, then offers Commentary and Analysis. No primary research conducted.

## Results
Restates Moro 2022 headline numbers; reviewers concur that:
- MBI is the gold-standard tool
- The 3-subscale architecture (EE/DP/PA) is the appropriate diagnostic frame
- EE prevalence ~28% is the most clinically meaningful single number to remember

## Why It Matters for Clinic Operations
Mostly a citation-hygiene paper. When you cite Moro 2022's burnout prevalence numbers internally (e.g., in a wellbeing program rationale doc), pairing it with this commentary signals that the original finding survived editorial peer review at the same journal. Practically negligible incremental insight.
