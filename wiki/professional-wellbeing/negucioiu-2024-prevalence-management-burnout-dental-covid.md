## 한줄요약
COVID 전·중·후 전 구간을 담은 첫 dentist 번아웃 SR — 모든 phase에서 EE/DP/PA 저하 확인, pandemic-related stress가 증폭 인자. 핵심 모디파이어블 레버는 **workplace dynamics & professional relationships**. 개인 코핑보다 occupational health strategy 권고.

## Summary
Negucioiu et al. (PROSPERO-registered, Newcastle-Ottawa-quality-assessed) provide the first SR that brackets dentist burnout across the entire COVID arc: pre-pandemic, intra-pandemic, and post-pandemic. The pooled picture isn't a meta-analysis — they conducted narrative synthesis — but the directional finding is clear: high MBI subscale scores (EE, DP, reduced PA) persist across all three phases, with pandemic-era stressors amplifying impact.

The operationally important contribution is the identification of **workplace dynamics and professional relationships** as the leading modifiable factor. This converges with Plessas 2022's critique that the literature focuses too much on individual coping and not enough on organisational/relational interventions.

The authors explicitly call for **occupational health strategies** rather than additional individual-level mindfulness programs.

## Key Contributions
- First dental burnout SR covering the full pre/during/post-COVID arc
- Confirms high prevalence persists across all three phases (no pandemic-driven regression to baseline)
- Identifies workplace dynamics and professional relationships as the highest-leverage modifiable factor
- Documents differential effects by educational level and age
- Frames the intervention argument at the **occupational health** level, not individual level

## Methodology
PRISMA-compliant SR registered on PROSPERO (CRD42024586616). Search Jan 2014 → Apr 2024 across PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science. Eligibility broad enough to include prevalence studies, intervention studies, and impact studies on dental professionals. Newcastle-Ottawa Scale quality assessment. Narrative synthesis only.

## Results
- High EE, DP, and reduced PA confirmed across all pandemic phases
- Pandemic-era stressors amplified impact (fear of infection, financial pressure, PPE burden)
- Workplace factors and professional relationships emerged as the strongest modifiable predictor
- Differential effects by:
  - **Educational level** — direction implied but not quantified in the narrative
  - **Age** — younger/early-career professionals appear more affected
- Intervention literature heavily skews toward individual-level (psychoeducation) over organizational

## Why It Matters for Clinic Operations
- For a 20-person hygienist clinic post-COVID: this is the most current SR-level evidence specific to dental practice
- The **modifiable factor** finding (workplace dynamics & relationships) is directly actionable — peer support structures, conflict mediation, predictable scheduling, transparent expectations beat handing out mindfulness apps
- The age gradient (younger more affected) suggests onboarding and early-career support are high-leverage
- Calls for occupational health strategies match South Korean industrial health legislation framework (산업안전보건법 directionally aligned, though Korean dental clinics fall under different regulatory regime depending on size)

## Open Questions
- Does the post-COVID "after" phase truly show recovery, or just adaptation? Negucioiu's search ends Apr 2024 — too early to tell.
- The differential effect by education level needs quantification — current narrative is qualitative.
- Workplace dynamics is named as the key lever but the SR doesn't specify which dynamics — peer support? Hierarchical autonomy? Conflict resolution? Needs primary research.
- MDPI Healthcare publication context — PROSPERO registration improves credibility, but the field's reproducibility track record warrants caution.
