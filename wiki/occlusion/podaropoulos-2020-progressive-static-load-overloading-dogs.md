## One-line Summary

Beagle dog RCT (n=40 implants): progressive orthodontic pre-loading before overloading improved implant success rate to 87.5% vs 67.5% with overloading alone, though BIC was not further increased; overloading raised BIC vs no-load controls regardless of prior conditioning.

## Summary

This 2020 experimental study by Podaropoulos, Trisi, et al. (Int J Oral Maxillofac Implants 35:25-38) investigated whether controlled progressive orthodontic static loading, applied before overloading, could condition peri-implant bone to better resist the damaging effects of excessive occlusal forces in a beagle dog model.

Five beagle dogs received 40 implants in surgically created edentulous maxillary ridges. Three groups were tested: unloaded controls (UL, n=8), overloading only (OL, n=16) with supraocclusal crowns for 16 weeks, and progressive loading followed by overloading (PL+OL, n=16) using ascending Ni-Ti spring forces (100 g → 200 g → 300 g, 3 weeks each) before identical overloading. Histologic and histomorphometric analysis assessed BIC, peri-implant bone density, and crestal bone resorption.

Both loaded groups showed significantly higher BIC than unloaded controls. However, prior progressive loading did not increase BIC beyond overloading alone (P=.225). Critically, implant success rate in the PL+OL group (87.5%) matched the unloaded controls and significantly exceeded the OL-only group (67.5%), suggesting the progressive loading protocol confers protective adaptation against implant loss without measurable histomorphometric BIC enhancement. Crestal bone resorption was lower in both loaded groups compared with UL controls.

## Key Contributions

1. **Progressive pre-loading improves success rate under overload**: PL+OL achieved 87.5% success vs 67.5% in OL-only — a clinically meaningful difference suggesting bone conditioning reduces overload-induced failure risk.
2. **Overloading per se is not uniformly harmful**: Both OL and PL+OL groups had higher BIC and less crestal bone resorption than unloaded controls, challenging the assumption that any overloading degrades osseointegration.
3. **BIC ceiling effect**: Progressive pre-loading did not further increase BIC over overloading alone, implying that the bone-contact response may plateau once functional loading is applied.
4. **Progressive loading protocol defined**: 100 g / 200 g / 300 g at 3-week intervals via Ni-Ti orthodontic springs is the first operationally specified progressive static loading protocol tested against subsequent overloading in an animal model.

## Methodology

- **Model**: Beagle dogs (n=5); bilateral edentulous posterior maxilla created surgically; 8-week healing.
- **Groups**: UL (n=8, unloaded 32 weeks); OL (n=16, 16-week osseointegration + 16-week overloading with supraocclusal crowns); PL+OL (n=16, 8-week osseointegration + 9-week progressive loading at 100/200/300 g via Ni-Ti springs + 16-week overloading).
- **Outcomes**: BIC%; peri-implant bone density at 1 mm and 2 mm; crestal bone resorption; implant success rate.
- **Analysis**: Histomorphometry with statistical testing (significance P<.05).
- **Limitation**: Published PDF is an abstract-only page; full histomorphometric tables are inaccessible.
