## Summary
Leone and colleagues update the occlusion-periodontitis question with a 2026 scoping review that adds a mechanistic layer to the older clinical debate. Excessive occlusal loading acting on already-inflamed tissue may accelerate periodontal breakdown through inflammatory and proteolytic pathways, including extracellular-matrix degradation. Their synthesis lands on the now-consensus position: occlusal trauma is a modifying factor that can worsen progression in susceptible individuals, not an initiating cause of periodontitis. They propose pairing occlusal assessment with the active MMP-8 (aMMP-8) biomarker to identify active destruction objectively, and — because measured clinical effect sizes are modest — argue for selective rather than routine occlusal intervention. This reinforces Dommisch's outcome finding from the mechanistic side.

## Key Contributions
- Mechanistic integration (inflammatory/proteolytic pathways, ECM degradation) of occlusal overload in periodontitis.
- Reaffirms occlusal trauma as modifying, not initiating.
- Introduces aMMP-8 biomarker-guided occlusal assessment as an emerging direction.
- Argues for selective occlusal intervention given modest effect sizes.

## Methodology
Scoping review synthesizing clinical, animal, and in vitro studies on the mechanistic and clinical interplay of excessive occlusal loading and periodontal breakdown.

## Results
Occlusal overload on inflamed tissue may accelerate breakdown via inflammatory/proteolytic pathways; occlusal trauma exacerbates rather than initiates periodontitis in susceptible patients; aMMP-8/ECM markers proposed as objective adjuncts; modest clinical effect sizes favor selective intervention.

## Clinical Application
- Confirms the management stance: address occlusion as a co-factor in an already-inflamed, susceptible periodontium — not as a primary cause to "fix" prophylactically.
