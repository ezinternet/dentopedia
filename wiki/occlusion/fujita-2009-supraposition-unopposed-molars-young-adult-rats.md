## One-line Summary

Animal micro-CT study (40 Wistar rats): unopposed maxillary molars in young (4-wk) rats reached 262 ± 135 µm supraposition vs only 125 ± 108 µm in adults (26-wk) after 4 weeks (P < 0.01); alveolar bone followed the tooth only in the young — overeruption potential is age-dependent.

## Summary

This controlled rat experiment is the primary animal evidence that **unopposed-tooth overeruption (supraposition) potential is greater in young/growing than in adult individuals** — a clinically suspected age effect that prior human cross-sectional data (Craddock & Youngson; Kiliaridis et al.) raised but never tested within a single controlled design. Right maxillary molar crowns were ground out of occlusion in 4-week-old (young) and 26-week-old (adult) Wistar rats; after 4 weeks, micro-CT showed young unopposed molars supraposed roughly twice as much as adult ones. Importantly, in the young animals the alveolar bone level was higher on the supraposed side (bone followed the tooth, i.e., dento-alveolar growth), whereas in adults no such alveolar difference appeared — the two age groups differ not only in magnitude but in the nature of the response.

## Key Contributions

- Direct within-study **young-vs-adult contrast** of unopposed-molar supraposition under identical grinding/occlusal-loss conditions.
- Quantifies the age effect: **262 ± 135 µm (young) vs 125 ± 108 µm (adult)**, P < 0.01 — young ≈ 2× adult over 4 weeks.
- Separates **tooth movement from dento-alveolar growth**: alveolar bone followed the tooth only in young rats; adults showed supraposition without matching alveolar bone change.
- Provides the mechanistic/age substrate that clinical overeruption pages reference when explaining why younger patients (or earlier antagonist loss) carry higher overeruption risk.

## Methodology

- **Design**: controlled animal experiment; 40 male Wistar rats (20 young 4-wk, 20 adult 26-wk) + 40 age-matched controls.
- **Groups**: Young Unopposed (YU), Young Control (YC), Adult Unopposed (AU), Adult Control (AC).
- **Intervention**: right maxillary molar crowns reduced ~0.8 mm occlusally by hand-piece grinding under anaesthesia, eliminating antagonist contact.
- **Conditions**: soft diet + water ad libitum, 4-week observation.
- **Outcome**: micro-CT — vertical tooth position (supraposition vs contralateral opposed molar) and alveolar bone level.
- *Source detail*: abstract/section-snippet level (ScienceDirect landing print); headline figures captured, deeper per-group statistics abstract-level only.

## Results

| Group | Supraposition vs opposed side | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Young unopposed (YU) | **262 ± 135 µm** | vs young control −23 ± 63 µm, P < 0.01 |
| Adult unopposed (AU) | **125 ± 108 µm** | significantly < young, P < 0.01 |

- Overeruption potential of unopposed molars is **higher in young than adult rats**.
- **Alveolar bone**: higher on the supraposed side in young animals (bone followed tooth); **no** alveolar difference in adults.
- No significant body-weight differences between unopposed and control groups in either age (no diet/growth confounder by weight).
- All four experimental first molars remained out of occlusion at 4 weeks (antagonist contact not re-established).
