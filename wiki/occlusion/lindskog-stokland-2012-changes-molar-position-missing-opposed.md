## One-line Summary

Prospective 12-year panoramic study of 292 women: unopposed molars carried a 4.9× higher risk of ≥2 mm overeruption (95% CI 1.5–15.3) than opposed molars, averaging ~4.5% (≈0.9 mm) and increasing with reduced alveolar bone support; mesial tipping was small (mean 0.8°) and unrelated to the edentulous space, but overeruption and tipping interacted significantly.

## Summary

This Gothenburg population cohort provides the longest-interval human evidence on what happens to a molar after it loses its antagonist. Using paired panoramic radiographs taken **12 years apart** in **292 women**, the investigators measured changes in overeruption, mesial tipping, and alveolar bone height. The headline finding is a **4.9-fold higher risk of clinically meaningful (≥2 mm) overeruption** in unopposed versus opposed molars (95% CI 1.5–15.3). The *average* overeruption was modest — about **4.5% (≈0.9 mm)** — so most of the clinical risk sits in the tail of the distribution rather than in the mean.

Two secondary findings sharpen the clinical picture. First, **overeruption increased as alveolar bone support decreased**, identifying reduced periodontal support as a modifier that pushes a tooth toward the dangerous end of the distribution. Second, **mesial tipping into a bounded edentulous space was small (mean 0.8°) and was not predicted** by the presence of the space or by bone level/bone-level change — yet **overeruption and tipping interacted significantly**, meaning the two movements are not independent processes. Site asymmetry was also noted: unopposed molars were more common in the **upper** jaw, while molars facing a mesial gap were more prevalent in the **lower** jaw.

## Key Contributions

- First long-interval (12-year) **prospective human population** quantification of unopposed-molar overeruption risk: **RR 4.9 for ≥2 mm** (95% CI 1.5–15.3).
- Identifies **reduced alveolar bone support** as a dose-response modifier increasing overeruption magnitude.
- Demonstrates that **mesial tipping is typically minor** and not driven by the edentulous space, while still **interacting** with overeruption.
- Provides a real-patient incidence/risk anchor that complements cross-sectional CBCT morphometry.

## Methodology

- **Design**: prospective population-based longitudinal radiographic study; subsample of the Study of Women in Gothenburg.
- **Sample**: 292 women with **panoramic radiographs at a 12-year interval**.
- **Measurements**: scanned panoramic films analysed for change in overeruption, mesial tipping, and alveolar bone height of molars and control teeth.
- **Analysis**: relative risk of ≥2 mm overeruption (unopposed vs opposed) with 95% CI; tests of edentulous-space and bone-level effects on tipping; overeruption×tipping interaction. Cited measurement-reproducibility and panoramic-distortion references address 2-D angular/linear error; multilevel-modelling reference supports clustered (tooth-within-subject) analysis.
