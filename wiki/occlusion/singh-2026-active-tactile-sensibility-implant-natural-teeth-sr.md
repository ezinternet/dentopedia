## One-line Summary

PRISMA systematic review of 6 clinical studies (4 RCTs, 1 prospective cohort, 1 cross-sectional): single-implant active tactile sensibility (ATS) thresholds (10–100 µm) are consistently coarser than natural-tooth thresholds (<10–50 µm), but osseoperception improves over time with functional loading; immediate loading and occlusion against a natural antagonist accelerate sensory recovery — the physiological rationale for lighter implant occlusal contacts.

## Summary

After tooth extraction, loss of periodontal ligament (PDL)–mediated mechanoreception degrades fine tactile sensibility and occlusal control. Implant-supported prostheses recover this only partially, via **osseoperception** — sensory feedback from periosteal, mucosal, muscular, and central receptors rather than a PDL. This review asked how large the residual sensory gap is for a single implant opposing a natural tooth (compared contralaterally with natural tooth-to-tooth contacts), and which clinical factors modify implant-mediated sensory rehabilitation.

Across 6 included studies, implant ATS thresholds (10–100 µm) were consistently higher (coarser discrimination) than natural-tooth thresholds (<10–50 µm). Sensibility was not fixed: it improved progressively with functional loading (neuroplasticity), was greater under immediate than delayed loading, and was better when the implant opposed a natural tooth than another implant. Implant location (anterior vs posterior) showed inconsistent effects, and no consistent age or sex effect was found.

Clinically, the coarser force discrimination of implants — even after osseoperceptive adaptation — supports the convention of establishing **lighter occlusal contacts** on implant restorations and favoring natural antagonists where the occlusal scheme allows.

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies the implant-vs-natural-tooth ATS gap: implants 10–100 µm vs natural teeth <10–50 µm.
- Establishes osseoperception as **time-dependent and load-dependent** rather than static — ATS improves longitudinally with function.
- Identifies two modifiable accelerators of sensory recovery: **immediate loading** and **occlusion against a natural antagonist**.
- Supplies the mechanoreceptive rationale for the clinical practice of lighter occlusal loading on implants.

## Methodology

- PRISMA 2020 systematic review; PROSPERO CRD420251104655; no meta-analysis.
- PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science + manual search; literature through June 2025.
- Inclusion: human studies reporting quantifiable ATS thresholds for a single implant opposing a natural tooth vs contralateral natural tooth pairs, by psychophysical testing (e.g., foil/thickness detection).
- 6 studies: 4 RCTs, 1 prospective cohort, 1 cross-sectional.
