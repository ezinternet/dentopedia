## One-line Summary

Prospective ~10.6-year follow-up of 22 unopposed maxillary molars in 12 healthy adults showing slow three-dimensional displacement (vertical overeruption 0.8 mm, mesial drift, palatal movement 0.9 mm), clinically insignificant in periodontally healthy patients but accelerated by periodontal involvement.

## 한줄요약

건강한 성인 12명의 대합치 없는 상악 대구치 22개를 평균 약 10.6년 추적한 전향 연구로, 무대합 치아가 수직 정출(0.8 mm)·근심·구개측(0.9 mm)의 3차원으로 천천히 이동했으며, 치주적으로 건강한 환자에서는 임상적으로 미미하나 치주 침범 시 더 크게 정출함을 보여줌.

## Summary

This prospective longitudinal study followed 12 healthy mature adults (mean age 45 years 11 months) whose maxillary molars had been unopposed for more than 10 years, over a mean follow-up of 10 years 7 months. Plaster casts were scanned in three dimensions and the centroid of each occlusal surface was tracked. The 22 unopposed molars displaced in all three planes — vertically (overeruption), mesially (when no mesial neighbor), and palatally — whereas 14 antagonist-bearing control teeth remained comparatively stable. The displacement was statistically detectable but clinically insignificant in periodontally healthy adults; periodontally affected molars overerupted significantly more. The authors attribute the movement to either late growth remodelling or altered dental equilibrium after antagonist loss.

This is the longitudinal human anchor for the claim that unopposed-tooth movement is a genuinely three-dimensional, slow process rather than purely vertical overeruption.

## Key Contributions

- Longitudinal (not cross-sectional) human evidence that unopposed adult molars move in three planes of space, not vertically alone.
- Quantified displacement vectors over ~10.6 years: vertical 0.8 mm (SD 0.65), palatal 0.9 mm (SD 0.6), plus significant mesial drift when no mesial neighbor.
- Periodontal status is an effect modifier: affected molars overerupt significantly more than healthy ones (P ≤ 0.01).
- Reassuring clinical bottom line for periodontally healthy adults: the long-term change is clinically insignificant.

## Methodology

- Design: prospective longitudinal cohort, plaster-cast follow-up.
- Subjects: 12 healthy mature adults (6 M, 6 F; mean age 45y 11m) with maxillary molars unopposed > 10 years.
- Follow-up: mean 10 years 7 months.
- Units: 22 unopposed maxillary molars present at both first and last examination; 14 antagonist-bearing posterior teeth (≥1 per subject) as internal controls.
- Measurement: plaster casts from first and last visit scanned in 3D; change in the centroid of the occlusal surface measured in three dimensions.
- Statistics: t-test comparing changes between unopposed teeth and controls.

## Results

| Direction | Unopposed molars | Controls | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Vertical (overeruption) | 0.8 mm (SD 0.65) | 0.4 mm (SD 0.2) | P ≤ 0.06 |
| Palatal | 0.9 mm (SD 0.6) | 0.5 mm (SD 0.3) | P ≤ 0.02 |
| Mesial (no mesial neighbor) | significant vs control | — | P ≤ 0.04 |

- Periodontally affected molars erupted more than periodontally healthy molars (P ≤ 0.01).
- Overall: three-dimensional displacement of unopposed teeth occurs in the long term but is clinically insignificant in periodontally healthy adults.
- Mechanism: late growth remodelling and/or altered dental equilibrium after antagonist loss.
