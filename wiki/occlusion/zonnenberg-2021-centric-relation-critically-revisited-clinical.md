## One-line Summary

Narrative critical review of ~70 years of literature concluding that the term "centric relation" (CR) is semantically, conceptually and practically flawed and should be abandoned; in healthy dentate patients, maximum intercuspation (MIP) determines the TMJ relationship and is biologically acceptable.

## Summary

This is the provocative "abandon CR" paper in the centric-relation terminology debate. Surveying roughly 70 years of dental literature, Zonnenberg, Türp and Greene argue that the meaning of *centric relation* has been repeatedly redefined (from a rearmost/retruded condylar position to an anterosuperior one), and that these shifting definitions have produced persistent confusion among clinicians and academics alike. They contend the term's instability has driven a range of **unnecessary diagnostic and therapeutic procedures** — CR registration, condylar-position assessment, and occlusal interventions aimed at relocating the mandible.

Their bottom line is normative and deliberately strong: on semantic, conceptual and practical grounds the term CR is flawed and **should be abandoned**. In its place they propose that every individual has a unique temporomandibular joint relationship that no single term can capture; in healthy dentate patients this relationship is **determined by maximum intercuspation (MIP)** of the teeth and should be regarded as biologically acceptable — implying routine condylar-position assessment is not needed in such patients.

This page represents one pole of an ongoing argument. It is directly contested by Fornai (2022) and Goldstein (2022), and sits alongside the Kattadiyil (2021) consensus and the CR–IP discrepancy literature.

## Key Contributions

- Documents a ~70-year pattern of changing CR definitions and consequent clinical disagreement.
- Argues the term's instability causes unnecessary CR registrations, condylar-position assessments and occlusal therapies.
- Concludes the term CR is flawed (semantic / conceptual / practical) and should be **abandoned**.
- Proposes MIP as the biologically acceptable, individual-specific reference in healthy dentate patients.

## Methodology

- Narrative critical review (not systematic; no PRISMA flow or quantitative synthesis).
- PubMed search terms: 'centric relation', 'masticatory muscles', 'maxillomandibular relationship', 'condylar position'.
- Literature from the past ~70 years to present appraised.
- *Abstract-only ingest — full text not retrieved; argumentation steps and cited evidence not captured.*

## Results

- Confirms a "problematic pattern of changing definitions and clinical disagreements" with significant impact on practice.
- No quantitative outcomes — a terminology/conceptual argument rather than an empirical study.
- Clinical implication asserted: in healthy dentate patients, MIP is the acceptable reference and CR-based relocation procedures are unnecessary.
