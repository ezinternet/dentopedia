## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional survey of 228 Bulgarian dentists: articulating paper is by far the most-used occlusal indicator (52.4%), followed by articulation foil (26.2%) and silk (16.8%); shim stock foil (2.45%), T-Scan (1.4%), and OccluSense (0.7%) are rarely used, while 58.7% expressed willingness to adopt quantitative/digital occlusion methods.

## Summary

This nationwide questionnaire (228 of 2014 invited Bulgarian dentists; 14.87% response) mapped which occlusal indicators clinicians actually use and their appetite for digital alternatives. Qualitative indicators dominate: articulating paper (52.4%), articulation foil (26.2%), and silk (16.8%) account for the overwhelming majority, because they are cheap and easy and reveal contact location/number. Shim stock foil is rarely used (2.45%) — the authors attribute this to its limited output: it only confirms whether an antagonist contact holds the foil (presence/absence), without staining or visualizing the contact on the tooth surface. Quantitative/digital systems that capture force and timing — T-Scan (1.4%) and OccluSense (0.7%) — are barely used in practice, yet 58.7% of respondents said they would adopt digital occlusion analysis, and attitude toward quantitative methods was associated with holding a specialty (χ²=14.73, p=0.005).

## Key Contributions

- Real-world usage ranking of occlusal indicators: articulating paper ≫ foil > silk ≫ shim stock ≈ T-Scan > OccluSense.
- Documents and explains the marginal clinical role of shim stock foil (contact presence/absence only; no surface marking).
- Quantifies a large gap between current digital-occlusion usage (~2% combined) and stated willingness to adopt (58.7%).
- Associates specialist training with more favorable attitudes toward quantitative occlusion methods.

## Methodology

- Design: cross-sectional anonymous electronic questionnaire; descriptive statistics + nonparametric tests (SPSS 25.0; p<0.05).
- Sample: 2014 randomly selected dentists nationwide (Bulgaria); 228 returned (response rate 14.87%); 131 F / 97 M; mean age 50.35±9.85; 31.1% with a specialty.
- Instrument: self-developed; sociodemographic/professional items + opinions on indicator positives/negatives/effectiveness. Ethics approval MU–Plovdiv no. 4 (16.10.2014).

## Results

- Indicator usage: articulating paper 52.45±2.95% · articulation foil 26.22±2.6% · silk 16.78±2.21% · shim stock foil 2.45±0.91% · T-Scan 1.40±0.69% · OccluSense 0.70±0.49%.
- Willingness to use quantitative indicators: 58.74±2.11%.
- Specialty × opinion on quantitative methods: χ²=14.73, df=4, p=0.005 (significant).
