## One-line Summary

Rat experimental study (n=24, 4 groups): occlusal hypofunction reduced PDL thickness and alveolar bone BMD; low-level laser (904–910 nm) irradiation restored both to control levels and raised bFGF/VEGF-positive cell counts (P<0.01).

## 한줄요약

랫드 실험(n=24, 4군): 교합 무자극(hypofunction)은 치주인대(PDL) 두께와 치조골 골밀도(BMD)를 떨어뜨렸고, 저출력 레이저(904–910 nm) 조사가 이를 대조군 수준으로 회복시키며 bFGF·VEGF 양성세포를 증가시켰다(P<0.01).

## Summary

This in vivo rat study addresses the *underloading* end of the occlusion–periodontium axis: teeth deprived of occlusal stimuli (hypofunctional teeth, as seen clinically in open-bite or high-canine malocclusions) undergo atrophic periodontal changes. Using a bite-raising appliance to eliminate molar occlusal force for 2 weeks, the authors confirmed that occlusal hypofunction during the growth period thins the periodontal ligament (PDL stricture) and lowers alveolar-bone mineral density (BMD). Critically, applying low-level laser (LLL) irradiation through the gingival sulcus restored both PDL thickness and alveolar BMD to levels statistically indistinguishable from normal-occlusion controls, and normalized the otherwise-reduced number of bFGF- and VEGF-immunopositive periodontal cells. The proposed mechanism is LLL-driven upregulation of these angiogenic/remodeling growth factors.

## Key Contributions

- Documents that occlusal hypofunction (loss of occlusal load), not just overload, damages periodontal tissue — PDL thinning + reduced alveolar crest BMD.
- Shows LLL (904–910 nm diode, 48.6 J, 3 min every other day × 2 wk) reverses these changes toward normal.
- Links the recovery to restored bFGF/VEGF-positive cell counts in PDL fibroblasts, cementum, and alveolar bone-lining cells.
- Suggests a pre-orthodontic LLL strategy to recondition hypofunctional periodontium and potentially reduce root resorption during subsequent tooth movement.

## Methodology

- 24 male Wistar rats (5 wk), randomized; four groups: control, control+LLL, hypofunctional, hypofunctional+LLL.
- Hypofunction induced by anterior metal cap + bite plate (composite-bonded) raising the bite off the molars for 2 weeks; confirmed by soft X-ray.
- LLL: Lumix2 HEPL diode, 904–910 nm, peak 45 W, ≤30 kHz, 200 ns pulse; 48.6 J at maxillary first molar via gingival sulcus, 3 min every other day for 2 weeks.
- Outcomes: Micro-CT (SkyScan1176) PDL thickness and alveolar BMD at 5 and 7 weeks; H-E histology; bFGF/VEGF immunohistochemistry (blinded cell counts in 300×400 mm area).
- Statistics: repeated one-way ANOVA + Tukey-Kramer, p<0.05.

## Results

| Outcome (7 wk) | Hypofunctional vs control | Hypofunctional+LLL vs control |
|---|---|---|
| PDL thickness | Significantly thinner | No significant difference |
| Alveolar bone BMD | Significantly lower | No significant difference |
| bFGF+ cells | Significantly fewer (P<0.01) | Similar (restored) |
| VEGF+ cells | Significantly fewer (P<0.01) | Similar (restored) |

- No significant inter-group differences at 5 weeks; effects emerged by 7 weeks.
- Body weight/growth unaffected across groups.
- Histology: hypofunctional PDL showed stricture and thinning; hypofunctional+LLL resembled controls.
- Conclusion: occlusal hypofunction during growth weakens periodontal tissue; LLL irradiation restores a near-normal periodontal condition, mediated at least partly by enhanced bFGF/VEGF expression.
