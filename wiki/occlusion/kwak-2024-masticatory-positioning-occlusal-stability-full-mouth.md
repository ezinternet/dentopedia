## One-line Summary

Prospective 10-year follow-up case series (n=16) using an intraoral Gothic arch tracer to set the normalized hard-food chewing position (CM-H) as the therapeutic position for full-mouth rehabilitation; CM-H↔MICP concordance was 63% at both timepoints and overall four-movement stomatognathic recovery improved 19%, supporting long-term stability of chewing-position-centered occlusion.

## 한줄요약

전악 수복 환자 16명을 약 10년 추적한 전향적 증례군 — 구내 고딕아치 트레이서로 정상화한 단단한 음식 저작위치(CM-H)를 치료위로 설정. CM-H↔최대교두감합위(MICP) 일치율이 1·2차 모두 63%로 유지되고 4운동 전체 회복률은 19% 개선되어, 저작위 중심 교합 재건의 장기 안정성을 뒷받침.

## Summary

In extensive occlusal reconstruction, the therapeutic position (centric relation, CR) is the starting point of mastication and a determinant of occlusal-surface morphology. This study departs from Gysi's classical definition — CR as the most-posterior, ligament-guided border-movement position — and instead defines the therapeutic position as the **apex of a normalized chewing pattern (CM-H, hard-food chewing)**, a physiologic position harmonized with the neuromuscular system and TMJ. Sixteen full-mouth-rehabilitation patients were retraced with the same intraoral Gothic arch tracer (N-GAT) after a mean of 10 years 3 months to test whether occlusion built around this chewing position stays stable and whether the stomatognathic system recovers. The final occlusion was a mutually protected scheme (point-centric fossa + canine guidance) resembling natural dentition.

## Key Contributions

- **Function-defined therapeutic position**: CR set at the apex of a *normalized chewing pattern* (CM-H) rather than the most-posterior condylar/border position — reframing the long-standing point-centric vs long-centric debate around physiologic chewing function.
- **Rare decade-long human longitudinal data**: same 16 patients, same device, two recordings ~10 years apart.
- **Movement concordance as a stomatognathic-health panel**: CM-H↔CM-S (muscle health), CM-H↔MICP (chewing-position/occlusion stability), CM-H↔BM (TMJ-complex health), all-four (overall recovery).

## Methodology

- **Design**: prospective long-term follow-up case series, n=16 (6 M / 10 F; mean age 39.75 y). IRB PO1-201605-21-002.
- **Device**: N-GAT (BIOCR, Seoul) intraoral Gothic arch tracer — mandibular recording pin (fixed/spring stylus) + maxillary recording plate; sequential Arti-spray layering records all movements on one plate.
- **Groups (4×4)**: G1 normal TMJ + prosthetic + occlusal adjustment; G2 TMD + prosthetic + occlusal adjustment; G3 TMD + orthodontic (no occlusal adjustment); G4 TMD + orthodontic + occlusal adjustment. TMD patients with joint noise had 3–6 mo splint therapy before CP recording.
- **Movements**: CM-S, CM-H, BM, MICP (recorded twice ~10 y apart). Tapping movements (T-U, T-S) recorded but excluded for high variability.
- **Quantification**: CM-H apex = origin (0,0); discrepancies in 0.1 mm units along X (lateral) and Y (anteroposterior); each movement classified as progression / maintenance / decline between recordings.
- **CP rule**: when CM-H and CM-S apices differ, the CM-H apex is the therapeutic position.

## Results

| Concordance pair | System indicator | 1st recording | 2nd recording | Change |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| CM-H ↔ MICP | chewing-position & occlusion stability | 10/16 (63%) | 10/16 (63%) | stable (0%) |
| CM-H ↔ CM-S | masticatory-muscle health | 11/16 (69%) | 13/16 (81%) | +13% |
| CM-H ↔ BM | TMJ-complex health | 1/16 (6%) | 5/16 (31%) | +25% |
| All four movements | overall stomatognathic recovery | 1/16 (6%) complete | 4/16 (25%) complete | +19% |

- **Overall recovery rate**: 6/16 maintained (37%) + 7/16 progressed (44%) = **13/16 (81%)**; 3/16 declined (19%).
- **Group ranking**: G1 (normal TMJ, prosthetic + occlusal adjustment) generally best, reaching 100% on several metrics; for CM-H↔MICP stability G1 > G4 > G2 > G3, with G3 (orthodontic, no occlusal adjustment) weakest (steady 25%).
- **Takeaway**: occlusion reconstructed around the normalized chewing position remains stable over a decade and supports recovery of masticatory-muscle and TMJ function, compensating for age-related chewing decline. The strongest recovery occurs when occlusal adjustment accompanies reconstruction (G1, G4 vs G3).
