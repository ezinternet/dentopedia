Per PubMed, short clinical report (3 groups × 20 = 60 mandibular 2-implant overdenture patients) found balanced occlusion gave the highest 6-month bite force (200 N), masticatory efficiency (80%), and satisfaction (9.0), ranking balanced > lingualized > monoplane on every outcome.

## Summary

According to PubMed, this brief clinical report (Kuriakose et al., 2024; [DOI](https://doi.org/10.4103/jpbs.jpbs_795_24)) compared three occlusal schemes — **balanced, lingualized, and monoplane** — in **mandibular 2-implant overdentures** (two implants in the canine regions), 20 patients per scheme. Across bite force, masticatory efficiency, and OHIP-EDENT–based patient satisfaction at 6 months, **balanced occlusion ranked first on every outcome**, followed by lingualized, then monoplane. The authors recommend balanced occlusion as the preferred scheme for implant-supported overdentures.

Although the abstract labels the study an RCT, the published version is a 3-page Supplement note with no CONSORT reporting, no inferential statistics beyond a blanket "p < 0.05," and conspicuously round group means — so the findings are best treated as **directional support** for balanced occlusion rather than high-quality trial evidence (graded `narrative-review` here).

## Key Contributions

- First wiki entry directly comparing **balanced vs lingualized vs monoplane** occlusion specifically in the **implant-supported overdenture** context (prior wiki coverage was conventional complete dentures or general edentulous implant design).
- Quantifies a consistent rank order across an objective measure (bite force), a semi-objective measure (color-mixing masticatory efficiency), and a PRO (satisfaction): **balanced > lingualized > monoplane**.

## Methodology

- 60 completely edentulous patients, mandibular **2-implant (canine-region) overdentures**, allocated to 3 groups of 20.
- Schemes: A = balanced (simultaneous bilateral centric + eccentric contact), B = lingualized (maxillary palatal cusps into mandibular fossae), C = monoplane (flat/cuspless surfaces).
- Outcomes at baseline, 3 mo, 6 mo: bite force (GM10 transducer, first molar bilaterally), masticatory efficiency (two-color chewing-gum colorimetry), satisfaction (OHIP-EDENT 19-item, 1–10 at 6 mo). SPSS v25.0.

## Results

| Outcome (6 mo) | Balanced (A) | Lingualized (B) | Monoplane (C) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bite force (N) | **200 ± 20** | 180 ± 15 | 160 ± 10 |
| Masticatory efficiency (%) | **80 ± 5** | 75 ± 4 | 70 ± 3 |
| Satisfaction (1–10) | **9.0 ± 0.5** | 8.5 ± 0.4 | 8.0 ± 0.3 |

- All within-group gains from baseline and all between-group differences reported significant (p < 0.05).
- **Favored scheme: balanced occlusion** for implant-supported overdentures; lingualized intermediate; monoplane lowest.

**Caveats**: single-center, n=20/group, 6-month follow-up, no implant survival/MBL/maintenance endpoints, minimal statistical reporting. Read as directional, not confirmatory.
