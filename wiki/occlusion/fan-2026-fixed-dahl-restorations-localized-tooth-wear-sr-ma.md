## One-line Summary

First SR+MA (11 studies; 8 low / 3 moderate RoB) on fixed Dahl restorations for localized tooth wear: indirect restorations showed much lower annual failure rates (0–0.7%) than direct (0–8.7%, P<.05), and complete posterior occlusal re-establishment occurred in a pooled 86.9% of patients (low–moderate GRADE certainty).

## Summary

The **Dahl concept** treats localized (typically anterior) tooth wear by placing a restoration in supra-occlusion; over weeks to months, relative axial movement (intrusion of the loaded teeth plus over-eruption of the temporarily separated posterior teeth) re-establishes full posterior occlusal contact — gaining restorative space without removing sound tooth structure. Despite decades of clinical use, no SR+MA existed. This review (search to June 2025; PubMed, Embase, Cochrane CENTRAL, Web of Science, Scopus, ClinicalTrials.gov) pooled 11 studies (RCTs, cohorts, case series; 8 low and 3 moderate risk of bias) and concluded that fixed Dahl restorations are a **suitable option** for localized tooth wear with high patient satisfaction, but with a wide therapy-time range and a high propensity for failures and complications. Certainty was low to moderate (GRADE).

## Key Contributions

- First synthesized evidence base (SR+MA) for fixed Dahl restorations in localized tooth wear.
- Direct vs. indirect material comparison via Poisson regression — indirect materials had significantly lower annual failure rates.
- Meta-analysis + meta-regression quantifying **occlusal re-establishment**, the defining outcome of the Dahl mechanism, with age as a tested moderator (non-significant).
- GRADE certainty rating for pooled outcomes.

## Methodology

- **Designs included**: RCTs, cohort studies, case series (n = 11 studies).
- **Risk of bias**: revised Cochrane RoB tool (RCTs), Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (cohorts), Joanna Briggs Institute critical appraisal (case series) → 8 low, 3 moderate.
- **Certainty**: GRADE (low to moderate).
- **Analysis**: Poisson regression comparing direct vs. indirect material outcomes (α=.05); meta-analysis and meta-regression for occlusal re-establishment.

## Results

| Material | Annual failure rate (AFR) | Annual complication rate (ACR) | Follow-up |
|---|---|---|---|
| Direct | 0%–8.7% | 5.2%–30.2% | 1.3–7 y |
| Indirect | 0–0.7% | 5.6%–7.2% | 1.7–2.2 y |

- Indirect AFR significantly lower than direct (**P<.05**) — but note the much shorter indirect follow-up (confounds the contrast).
- Predominant failures/complications: restoration **fracture, wear, and loss**.
- **Occlusal re-establishment** (reported 1–25.4 months): pooled proportions — no contact **0.6%**, partial **10.0%**, complete **86.9%**; not significantly influenced by patient age.
- Patients were largely satisfied; GRADE certainty low–moderate.
