## One-line Summary

Prospective in-vivo study (n=18 analyzed of 20 enrolled): after a single posterior implant single-crown restoration, occlusal force redistributes across the whole arch — anterior and posterior contact/force decrease (posterior drop > anterior), while opposing-arch and total contact/force increase; the Medit i700 IOS (contact area) and OccluSense (contact area + force) correlate strongly (Kendall's Tau-B, p<0.001).

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1186/s12903-025-06205-w](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12903-025-06205-w)), this prospective in-vivo study from Necmettin Erbakan University asked two questions about single posterior implant single crowns: (1) does placing one implant crown change whole-arch occlusal force distribution, and (2) do two digital occlusal analysers — the **Medit i700 intraoral scanner** (contact-area only) and **OccluSense** (sensor-integrated articulating paper giving contact area *and* force amplitude) — give parallel readings?

Measuring 18 patients (20 enrolled, 2 excluded) before and after the prosthesis, the answer to both is yes. Force distribution changed significantly throughout the arch — not just at the restored tooth — and the two devices correlated strongly. Clinically: a single implant crown reshapes occlusion arch-wide (relevant to TMJ load and adjacent-tooth biomechanics), and OccluSense's force-amplitude data is proposed as the more practical aid during chairside occlusal adjustment, while the IOS better localizes contact points.

## Key Contributions

1. First in-vivo head-to-head comparison of Medit i700 IOS vs OccluSense for single posterior implant restorations.
2. Quantifies pre→post whole-arch force redistribution (anterior, posterior, opposing arch, total), confirming the effect is systemic, not local.
3. Demonstrates strong method correlation (p<0.001) while clarifying each device's complementary strength: IOS = contact-point location, OccluSense = force magnitude/dynamics.

## Methodology

- **Design**: prospective comparative/observational in-vivo study; ClinicalTrials.gov NCT06862973.
- **Sample**: G*Power → min 19; 20 enrolled, 18 analyzed.
- **Recording**: OccluSense at 100 Hz × 6 s, max-force bite (max intercuspation); Medit i700 full-arch STL in parallel, same position. Same-morning pre/post to avoid diurnal variation; post recorded after final occlusal adjustment.
- **Processing**: Medit STL → CloudCompare v2.13.1 (thresholded to OccluSense's 60 µm sensor thickness); OccluSense → ImageJ 1.54f pixel-based area. Outcome = % change in contact area / force across 4 regions; absolute area not assessed.
- **Stats**: Kruskal–Wallis + DSCF/Bonferroni for region differences; Kendall's Tau-B for method agreement (p<0.05).

## Results

Pre→post % change (region differences p<0.001):

| Region | Medit i700 (area) | OccluSense (area) | OccluSense (force) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Anterior | ↓ 47.8 ± 1.02% | ↓ 16.36 ± 2.2% | ↓ 18.54 ± 1.58% |
| Posterior | ↓ 65.5 ± 1.39% | ↓ 34.39 ± 0.8% | ↓ 30.77 ± 2.66% |
| Opposing arch | ↑ 30.04 ± 1.82% | ↑ 110.02 ± 3.76% | ↑ 30.29 ± 4.82% |
| Total | ↑ 17.66 ± 1.09% | ↑ 36.71 ± 1.92% | ↑ 7.3 ± 3.36% |

- Consistent direction across methods: anterior + posterior down, opposing arch + total up; **posterior reduction > anterior** (p<0.001).
- Strong Medit i700–OccluSense correlation (p<0.001) → H2 accepted; H1 rejected (force distribution does change).
- IOS shows contact locations but no intensity; OccluSense shows force intensity but less-distinct locations.
- Consistent with Zhou 2021 (anterior force drop), Kim 2021 (opposing-arch force rise), Roque 2017 (over-time, patient-variable redistribution).
- **Limitations**: small n; single posterior implants only; chewing-habit/avoidance not controlled; the two devices measure different parameters; articulating paper deliberately excluded; controlled-clinic (not real-life) mastication.
