## One-line Summary

Interventional RCT (n=30, Class I 4-premolar-extraction cases) — CR-guided occlusal adjustment one month after debonding produced NO statistically significant difference vs no-adjustment control in T-Scan III occlusal contact intensity or number at 1 or 6 months (all P>0.05); only a non-significant trend toward improved posterior force distribution.

## 한줄요약

중재 RCT (n=30, Class I 소구치 4개 발치 케이스) — 디본딩 1개월 후 중심위(CR) 기반 교합조정을 해도 비조정 대조군 대비 T-Scan III 교합접촉 강도·개수에서 1·6개월 모두 통계적 유의차가 없었고(전부 P>0.05), 후방 힘 분포 개선의 비유의 경향만 나타남.

## Summary

This single-center interventional RCT (Mashhad, Iran; IRCT20230310057665N1) asked a practical orthodontic-finishing question: after debonding, does a deliberate centric-relation occlusal adjustment improve the objectively recorded occlusion? Thirty Class I patients (crowding >8 mm, all four first premolars extracted, MBT 0.022) were randomized to occlusal adjustment one month after debonding (n=15) or no adjustment (n=15). Occlusion was recorded with **T-Scan III** in centric relation at debonding (T0), one month (T1), and six months (T2). Across every comparison of occlusal **contact intensity** and **number** (anterior and posterior), there was **no statistically significant between-group difference**. The adjustment briefly intensified posterior contacts, but continuing physiologic tooth settling over six months washed out any difference, leaving only a non-significant trend toward better posterior force balance. The clinical takeaway: when a finished case already shows acceptable static intercuspation, a one-time CR adjustment does not produce a measurable T-Scan advantage within six months — though digital monitoring still adds value for detecting subtle imbalances that conventional alignment indices miss.

## Key Contributions

- An RCT-level test (rare in this niche) of occlusal adjustment **after** orthodontic treatment, measured with a quantitative dynamic tool (T-Scan III) rather than static alignment indices alone.
- Demonstrates that occlusal contacts keep **settling** post-debonding, so an early adjustment's effect is transient.
- Negative/trend-only result that counters the assumption that routine post-orthodontic occlusal equilibration measurably improves recorded occlusion in already-acceptable Class I extraction cases.

## Methodology

- **Design**: two-arm interventional RCT, n=30 (15 intervention / 15 control); ages 17–28 (mean 20.20±3.20); groups balanced for age (P=0.624) and sex (P=0.713).
- **Population**: Class I malocclusion, crowding >8 mm, four-first-premolar extraction, MBT 0.022. Excluded TMJ disorders, parafunction, periodontitis, bone-active drugs.
- **Intervention** (intervention arm only, at T1): Dawson bimanual manipulation to CR after cotton-roll deprogramming; premature contacts marked with 8-µm Arti-Fol; "heavy contact" defined as T-Scan force **>20% of total occlusal load**; 50–100 µm enamel reduction (833F bur); eccentric interferences removed to achieve **canine guidance**.
- **Both arms**: upper/lower 1.5 mm PETG Essix retainers (full-time 4 months, then nights); no fixed retainers.
- **Recording**: T-Scan III (85-µm, 2,500-cell sensor), Frankfort plane parallel, CR via physiological swallowing, 3 closures averaged, single operator.
- **Outcomes**: occlusal contact intensity & number, anterior/posterior, at T0/T1/T2. **Note** — the published tables report intensity and contact-number changes; explicit numeric occlusion-time/disclusion-time/asymmetry-index values are not tabulated in the full text (the system records timing, but only contact intensity/number deltas are reported).
- **Stats**: SPSS 25; Shapiro-Wilk (normal); t-tests, Mann-Whitney, Chi-square, Wilcoxon; α=0.05.

## Results

- **No significant between-group difference in any comparison** (all P>0.05).
- **Contact intensity** (intervention vs control): T2–T0 anterior −2.17±6.34 vs 0.23±4.15 (P=0.230); T2–T0 posterior 2.17±6.34 vs −0.23±4.15 (P=0.230); T1–T0 anterior P=0.329, posterior P=0.440; T2–T1 anterior P=0.815, posterior P=0.659.
- **Contact number**: T1–T0 anterior P=0.164, posterior P=0.243; T2–T0 anterior P=0.806, posterior P=0.902; T2–T1 anterior P=0.250, posterior P=0.285.
- **Pattern**: posterior contacts intensified immediately after adjustment, then equalized between groups by six months as teeth continued to settle. A non-significant trend favored improved posterior force distribution in the intervention arm.
- **Demographics**: 17 F (56.7%) / 13 M (43.3%).
- Underpowered (n=15/group from a 5-patient pilot) and only 6-month follow-up — authors recommend larger cohorts and ≥12-month follow-up.
