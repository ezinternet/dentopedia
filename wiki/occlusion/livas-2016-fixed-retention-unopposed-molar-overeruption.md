## One-line Summary

Retrospective panoramic study (65 Class II div 1 cases): unopposed mandibular second molars retained with bonded sectional wires showed no statistically significant overeruption vs partial-contact non-retention controls — both moved only ~0.1 mm (clinically insignificant); partial occlusal contact restrained eruption as well as fixed retention.

## 한줄요약

후향적 파노라마 연구(Class II 1류 65명): 고정 sectional wire로 잡아둔 대합치 없는 하악 2대구치는 부분접촉 비고정 대조군과 비교해 통계적으로 유의한 정출이 없었고, 양쪽 모두 약 0.1mm(임상적으로 무의미)만 이동 — 부분 교합접촉도 고정 retainer만큼 정출을 억제했다.

## Summary

This study tests the **intervention** side of the overeruption question: if an unopposed molar tends to over-erupt, does splinting it with a bonded retainer actually stop it? In 65 orthodontic Class II cases where the maxillary first molars had been extracted, the now-unopposed mandibular second molars were either bonded to a multistranded sectional wire (retention group) or left with partial antagonist contact (control). Over the follow-up:

- **Neither group over-erupted in any clinically meaningful way** — movement was ~0.1 mm (0.5–1.2% of crown width), statistically nonsignificant.
- The retention group moved slightly less, but **not significantly** — i.e. fixed retention could not be shown to beat simply leaving partial occlusal contact.

The wider value of the paper is the **overeruption epidemiology it consolidates** from prior work:
- A 12-year cohort: **4.9× higher risk** of ≥2 mm overeruption in unopposed molars.
- **18% of long-term unopposed teeth never over-erupt** at all.
- **Maxillary unopposed teeth migrate more than mandibular**; eruption is **fastest in the first years**.
- **Younger age amplifies** overeruption; periodontal effect is inconsistent (positive in humans, not in some animal models).

### Clinical reading (대합치 없을 때 — 막아야 하나?)

Two practical takeaways for the unopposed-tooth scenario:
1. **Most overeruption is small and slow-after-the-first-years**, and a meaningful fraction of teeth never move — so reflexive "must prosthetically fill every opposing gap" thinking is not supported (85% of dentists believe overeruption is inevitable; the data are softer).
2. **Splinting/retention to prevent overeruption has weak evidence** — here it was no better than partial natural contact, and the authors say it "cannot be fully warranted." Monitoring is a defensible default for the low-risk tooth; reserve active measures for the high-risk profile (young patient, maxillary tooth, fully unopposed, early after antagonist loss, periodontally involved).

This dovetails with [[occlusion/wang-2022-unopposed-molar-overeruption-implant-cbct]]: Wang shows overeruption *does* occur (0.43 mm / ~9 months) but is mostly < 1 mm; Livas shows that trying to *prevent* it with a retainer adds little over natural partial contact.

### On endo vs vital (carry-over note)

Like every paper in this cluster, Livas **does not stratify by pulp vitality or restoration type**. Nothing here changes the mechanistic conclusion that overeruption is PDL/alveolar-bone–driven and therefore **independent of whether the unopposed tooth is endodontically treated or vital**.

## Key Contributions

- First clinical test of bonded sectional retention wires for preventing overeruption of unopposed molars — found non-significant vs partial-contact controls.
- Reframes "fixed retention prevents overeruption" as **caution-warranted, not established**.
- Consolidates quantitative overeruption epidemiology (4.9× ≥2 mm risk over 12 y; 18% non-eruptors; maxilla > mandible; fastest early; younger faster).

## Methodology

- Retrospective; panoramic radiographs T1 (post-treatment) vs T2 (follow-up); 65 Class II div 1 cases (retention 30 / control 35).
- Second-molar centroid from 77 outline points; Procrustes + best-fit superimposition; vertical movement normalized to mesiodistal crown width (1% ≈ 0.11 mm).
- Inclination vs mandibular plane; parametric/nonparametric tests; reproducibility on 25 re-traced pairs.

## Results

| Outcome | Retention | Non-retention (control) |
|---|---|---|
| Overeruption (% crown width) | 0.5–1.0% (~0.1 mm) | 1.1–1.2% (~0.1 mm) |
| T1–T2 inclination / vertical change | not significant | not significant |
| Between-group difference | **not significant (p > 0.05)** | — |

## Limitations

- Retrospective, no a-priori sample size; panoramic distortion limits absolute measurement.
- Assumes stable adjacent teeth (may explain negative vertical values); young-subject root growth; possible retention-wire deformation.
- Orthodontic post-extraction population (not general restorative); molars only; no pulp-vitality/restoration stratification.
