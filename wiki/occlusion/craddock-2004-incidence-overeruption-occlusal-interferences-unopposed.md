## One-line Summary

Single-centre retrospective clinical study (120 adults, 155 unopposed posterior sites): 83.9% of unopposed sites over-erupted (0–5.39 mm; 95% CI 78–90), 51.6% were involved in an occlusal interference (RCP/protrusive/excursive), and the correlation between over-eruption extent and interference presence was weak and non-significant.

## Summary

Craddock and Youngson measured a Combined Care Clinic population of 120 adults (>18 yr, mean age 50.9) presenting with 155 unopposed posterior sites (121 upper, 34 lower). "Unopposed" was defined as ≤20% of the occlusal surface contacting an opponent in intercuspal position. Over-eruption was measured on 3D-scanned study models in QuickCAD as the vertical distance from each tooth's cusp tip to the curve of Spee, with the curve extrapolated by Orthlieb's progressive-differential-angle method for terminal teeth lacking a distal reference. Occlusal interferences (RCP, protrusive, working-side, non-working-side) were recorded clinically by a single examiner using 8-µm articulating foils.

The headline findings: **83.9% of unopposed posterior sites over-erupted** (95% CI 78–90), with magnitudes ranging from 0 to 5.39 mm in a right-skewed distribution most commonly in the 1.0–2.5 mm range. Separately, **51.6% of unopposed sites were involved in an occlusal interference** (95% CI 44–60), ICP/RCP being the most frequent type. Crucially, the Spearman correlation between the *extent* of over-eruption and the *presence* of an interference was weak and statistically non-significant — interference cannot be predicted from how far a tooth has over-erupted. Reliability was good (Bland–Altman for over-eruption; Kappa 0.56–0.87 for interference types). This is the primary source for the widely-cited "~83%" over-eruption figure.

## Key Contributions

- **Canonical over-eruption prevalence:** 83.9% of unopposed posterior sites over-erupt — the most-cited primary figure for this phenomenon.
- **Magnitude distribution:** 0–5.39 mm, skewed, modal bands 1.0–1.5 / 1.5–2.0 / 2.0–2.5 mm; severe cases (up to 5.39 mm) may preclude conventional restoration without extraction.
- **Occlusal-interference layer:** 51.6% of unopposed sites carry an interference (upper 48.8% vs lower 61.8%), a high figure for a single occluding site (vs 75–89% whole-mouth in Agerberg & Sandström).
- **Decoupling of magnitude and interference:** weak, non-significant correlation — clinicians cannot infer interference presence from over-eruption extent.
- **Methodological:** validated 3D-scanner + curve-of-Spee (Orthlieb extrapolation) workflow with good intra-examiner reliability.

## Methodology

- **Design:** single-centre clinical retrospective/cross-sectional analysis (Leeds Dental Institute, year 2000 patients).
- **Sample:** 120 adults, 155 unopposed posterior sites (121 upper, 34 lower; 81 male, 74 female sites); mean age 50.9 yr (SD 13.94); no BPE >3; max 2 unopposed sites per jaw.
- **Definitions:** posterior = premolar or molar; unopposed = ≤20% occlusal contact in ICP; over-eruption present = any cusp-tip-to-curve-of-Spee distance >0.
- **Over-eruption:** alginate → stone models, 7-sided trim, Black Widow 9636 3D scanner with in-image mm scale, QuickCAD measurement; Orthlieb extrapolation for terminal teeth.
- **Interference:** single examiner, 8-µm Hänel foils, RCP/protrusive/working/non-working recorded, repeated to confirm.
- **Reliability:** 10% re-examined at 3–6 months; Bland–Altman (over-eruption, all within 95% CI); Kappa (ICP/RCP 0.87, protrusive 0.70, working 0.56, non-working 0.63).
- **Stats:** Spearman correlation for the over-eruption-vs-interference association; sample size pre-calculated.

## Results

| Outcome | Value | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|
| Over-eruption incidence | 130/155 = **83.9%** | 78–90 |
| Over-eruption range | **0 – 5.39 mm** (skewed; modal 1.0–2.5 mm) | — |
| Any occlusal interference | 80/155 = **51.6%** | 44–60 |
| Upper-site interference | 59/121 = 48.8% | 39.9–57.7 |
| Lower-site interference | 21/34 = 61.8% | 45.4–78.1 |

- **Interferences per site:** 0:74, 1:47, 2:28, 3:4, 4:0 (32 sites had >1; none had all four).
- **Interference type counts:** ICP/RCP 42 (most common) > protrusive 32 > working-side 23 > non-working-side 21. Initial RCP contact in 27% of interference cases; protrusive in 21%.
- **Association:** over-eruption extent vs interference presence — **weak, non-significant** (Spearman).
- **Clinical implication:** position of unopposed teeth should be monitored; extreme over-eruption may be impractical/impossible to correct; preventive measures (etched-metal splint, adhesive cantilever, Dahl-type appliance) may preserve the space; interferences (RCP, protrusive) complicate restoration of the unopposed tooth, its opponent, and the bounding teeth.
