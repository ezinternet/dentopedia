## One-line Summary

Narrative review (single 25-yr-follow-up case) arguing that centric relation is unreliable in patients with deformed/unstable condyles, and that the proper occlusal reference is a stable intercuspal position where habitual closing movements terminate **without premature tooth contact**, located by muscle-driven jaw position rather than condylar seating.

## Summary

This conceptual review addresses a long-standing controversy: which maxillomandibular reference should guide occlusal reconstruction. In physiologically normal joints, centric relation (CR) remains a valid criterion. However, advances in TMJ imaging reveal many patients with **deformed condyles**, and Fukushima argues CR — even Dawson's "adapted centric posture" — fails when the condyle is **not braced by bone** and sits unstably on the articular eminence slope. The clinically decisive question is therefore not *deformed vs not* but *stable vs unstable* condylar seating. As a substitute reference independent of condylar position, the author advocates **muscle-induced mandibular positions** — the muscular position (MP) and the terminal points of **habitual closing movements (HCM)** — and defines the practical goal as a **stable ICP at which HCMs terminate without premature tooth contact**, to be continually inspected. A 37-year-old patient with bilateral deformed condyles and dislocated discs was treated by equilibrating an occlusal splint to a stable HCM, transferring that position to definitive prostheses; the ICP remained stable for 25 years even though the condyle stayed anteriorly positioned without bony support.

## Key Contributions

- Reframes the occlusion debate around **condylar stability** (stable vs unstable in the fossa) rather than condylar deformity, and limits CR's applicability accordingly.
- Positions **premature-contact-free HCM termination** as the operational definition of a sound, stable ICP — connecting the abstract CR debate to a checkable chairside criterion.
- Offers **muscular position (MP)** and **HCM terminal points** as CR substitutes when condylar position cannot be trusted; cites that HCMs scatter only ≈0.38 mm A-P / 0.27 mm lateral and the condyle moves only 0.3–0.8 mm over the last 5 mm of closure — i.e., the muscle-guided endpoint is tightly constrained.
- Gives a chairside **instability screen**: backward guidance of the mandible >~1.5–2.0 mm behind ICP (excess posterior condylar space) flags an unstable condyle.
- Demonstrates with a 25-year case that **a stable ICP can outweigh an unstable condyle** — function is preserved as long as the ICP holds, even on a bone-unsupported anterior condyle.

## Methodology

Single-author **narrative/conceptual review** (Jpn Dent Sci Rev 2016;52(3):49–53); no systematic search, no pooled data. It synthesizes classic jaw-tracking and prosthodontic literature (Dawson's ACP; Posselt, Kawaguchi, Shigeta et al., Klineberg & Jagger; the author's own HCM measurements) and the evolving GPT definitions of CR (1987 shift from most-posterior to antero-superior condylar position). The argument is illustrated by **one clinical case** (37-yr-old woman, bilateral missing mandibular molars, bilateral deformed condyles with dislocated discs) managed via a splint equilibrated to stable HCM, then transferred to two maxillary 3-unit FPDs plus a mandibular crown and removable partial denture, followed 25 years. Evidence weight is correspondingly low (narrative review + single case).

## Results

- **Stability dominates outcome**: with a stable ICP, the system generally tolerates an unstable/deformed condyle; disorders (anterior open bite, progressive condylar resorption, PCR) cluster where **both** ICP and condyle are unstable.
- **Quantitative anchors** (from cited measurement studies): HCM terminal scatter ≈ 0.38 mm A-P / 0.27 mm lateral; condylar travel 0.3–0.8 mm over the final 5 mm of HCM; instability threshold of >~1.5–2.0 mm posterior guidance behind ICP.
- **Imaging caveat**: panoramic TMJ views, tomography, CT, MRI detect condylar bone change but cannot alone confirm stability; presence/absence of TMD symptoms is likewise non-diagnostic of deformity or stability.
- **Case**: splint-derived, premature-contact-free ICP transferred to prostheses remained positionally and functionally stable for **25 years** on an anteriorly positioned, bone-unsupported condyle.
