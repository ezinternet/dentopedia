## Summary

Production of an appropriate occlusal curve is essential when rehabilitating a dentition deranged by posterior tooth loss — overeruption, tipping, and drifting all distort the occlusal plane and challenge the dental technician. An earlier paper by the same group (Craddock et al. 2005, J Oral Rehabil 32:895–900) showed the **Broadrick flag method** reproduces the occlusal curve relatively accurately for most intact arches. This study extends that work to **deranged arches following posterior tooth loss**.

Using the Broadrick flag method on study casts/radiographs, the authors show that when a posterior tooth has remained **unopposed for 5 years or more**, positional changes occur that cause **deviation from the ideal Broadrick curve**. The extent of deviation **may be extreme**, potentially making it difficult to restore a harmonious occlusal scheme. Importantly, the study also shows the Broadrick curve can still be **accurately reproduced even when the posterior determinant tooth is tipped** — a **moderate degree of tipping** has little effect on the radius of the constructed curve.

## Key Contributions

- Documents that **≥5 years unopposed** is the threshold at which posterior-tooth positional change produces clinically meaningful **deviation from the Broadrick occlusal curve**.
- Quantifies the **prosthetic cost** of overeruption: extreme curve deviation complicates restoration of a harmonious occlusal scheme.
- Establishes the **robustness** of the Broadrick flag method — a **moderate degree of tipping** of the posterior determinant tooth has little effect on the curve radius, so the method stays usable in mildly deranged arches.
- Forms the consequence half of the Craddock series: the 2004 paper measured *incidence* of overeruption/interference; this paper measures its *effect on the occlusal curve*.

## Methodology

- Retrospective analysis applying the **Broadrick flag method** (geometric compass/flag construction of the ideal anteroposterior occlusal curve / curve of Spee) to casts of dentitions deranged by posterior tooth loss.
- Compares the **existing** occlusal curve against the constructed **ideal Broadrick curve** in teeth unopposed for varying durations.
- Tests the influence of **tipping of the posterior determinant tooth** on the radius/position of the Broadrick curve.

## Results

- **Unopposed ≥5 years → deviation**: posterior teeth left unopposed for 5+ years show positional changes (overeruption, tipping, drifting) that deviate the occlusal curve from the ideal Broadrick curve; deviation can be **extreme**.
- **Tipping tolerance**: **moderate tipping** of the posterior determinant tooth has **little effect** on the Broadrick curve radius — the method remains an accurate reproduction of the occlusal curve under moderate tipping.
- **Clinical implication**: extreme deviation challenges the technician and complicates restoring a harmonious occlusal scheme, arguing for earlier intervention before the 5-year window.
