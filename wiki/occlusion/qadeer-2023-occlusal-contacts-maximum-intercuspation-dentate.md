## One-line Summary

Systematised review of 18 studies over four decades (1980s–2022): the number of occlusal contacts at maximum intercuspation (MICP) in dentate adults ranges widely from 11 to 70, driven mainly by the occlusal indicator/technique and population differences — so there is no single "normal" count.

## Summary

Counting occlusal contacts at maximum intercuspation (MICP) is a long-standing prerequisite for guiding clinicians during occlusal corrections, yet "how many is normal" has never had a stable answer. This systematised review of dentate adults across four decades (1980s–2022) shows why: across the 18 included studies the reported number of occlusal contacts at MICP spans **11 to 70**. The authors attribute this nearly seven-fold spread chiefly to the **type and technique of the occlusal indicator** (articulating paper, wax sheets, shimstock foil, impression material, gnathodynamometers, digital occlusal analysis systems), together with population diversity and differing data-collection procedures. The practical takeaway is that a contact count is meaningless without specifying how it was measured, and that occlusal-contact norms must be method-standardised before being used clinically or compared across studies.

## Key Contributions

- Establishes the literature-wide range of occlusal contacts at MICP in dentate adults: **11–70**.
- Frames occlusal-contact count as **method-dependent**, not an intrinsic anatomical constant — reinforcing that detection method governs what is recorded.
- Provides a four-decade catalogue of the occlusal indicators used and identifies indicator heterogeneity as the dominant driver of between-study variation.

## Methodology

- Systematised review (abstract-only here; full text not retrieved) of literature from the 1980s to 2022.
- Searches in **three databases** — PubMed, Livivo, Ovid — with combined search terms.
- Screening: **651 (PubMed) + 37 (Livivo/Ovid)** records → **168 screened** after removing duplicates/irrelevant → **61 full-text** → **18 included** (studies reporting occlusal contacts as a quantifiable value).
- Indicators across included studies: articulating paper, wax sheets, shimstock foil, impression material, gnathodynamometers, digital occlusal analysis systems.

## Results

- **Number of occlusal contacts at MICP ranged from 11 to 70.**
- Variation driven by: (1) occlusal indicator type/technique, (2) population diversity, (3) differing data-collection procedures.
- No defensible single "normal" value can be extracted; method-specific, standardised norms are required for clinical interpretation and cross-study comparison.
