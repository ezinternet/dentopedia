## One-line Summary

Retrospective CBCT cohort (59 patients, 68 unopposed molars, all periodontitis): unopposed molars over-erupted a mean 0.43 mm (max 0.75 mm) and tipped 1.7° buccally within ~9 months between antagonist extraction and implant restoration — overeruption is a true 3D movement (vertical + tipping + rotation) and is fastest in the early period.

## 한줄요약

후향적 CBCT 코호트(환자 59명·대합치 없는 대구치 68개, 전원 치주염): 대합치 발치부터 임플란트 보철까지 약 9개월 동안 대합치 없는 대구치가 평균 0.43 mm(최대 0.75 mm) 정출하고 협측으로 1.7° 경사 — 정출은 수직 이동·경사·회전이 섞인 3차원 움직임이며 초기에 가장 빠르다.

## Summary

When a tooth loses its opposing (antagonist) contact, it tends to **over-erupt (정출, supraeruption)** toward the empty space. This CBCT superimposition study measured that movement directly during the clinically critical window — between extraction of the antagonist and the final implant restoration.

Key clinical numbers over ~9 months:
- **Mean overeruption 0.43 mm, maximum 0.75 mm** (statistically significant).
- **72% of teeth moved < 1 mm**, but a minority moved fast — one upper second molar over-erupted **2.56 mm in just 9 months**.
- Movement is **not purely vertical**: average **1.7° buccal tipping**, with the maximum eruption usually at the lingual/palatal cusps.

The practical message: even within the ~9–12-month implant treatment timeline, an unopposed tooth keeps moving, so **space management (monitoring, a provisional/retention contact, or timely restoration) should be planned**, not assumed away.

### How this answers the clinical question (대합치 없을 때 정출 — endo치 vs 생활치 차이)

Two separate questions are easy to conflate:

1. **Does an unopposed tooth over-erupt?** — Yes (this paper + Craddock 2004: 83% of unopposed posterior teeth over-erupt). The driver is **periodontal-ligament / alveolar-bone-mediated eruption**, the same biology that erupts a tooth into the mouth. Loss of the opposing occlusal force releases the tooth to keep migrating occlusally, and the alveolar bone follows.

2. **Does it matter whether the tooth is endodontically treated (실활치) vs vital (생활치), or whether it carries a crown?** — **No paper we hold (including this one) stratifies overeruption by pulp vitality, endodontic status, or restoration type.** Mechanistically there is no reason vitality would change overeruption: eruption/extrusion is a **PDL phenomenon, not a pulp phenomenon** — the periodontal ligament and alveolar bone move the whole root, and they remain present and functional in an endodontically treated tooth. So an endo-treated crowned molar and a vital crowned molar, both unopposed and both with healthy/comparable periodontium, are expected to over-erupt similarly. A **crown does not anchor the tooth** — the entire tooth (crown + root) migrates as a unit.

What *does* modulate overeruption in our evidence is **periodontal status** (all subjects here had periodontitis; Fujita 2009 rat work showed periodontitis amplifies overeruption) and **age** (Denes 2020 rat: younger faster). Pulp vitality is not a documented variable.

> Honest scope note (Rule #4): I do **not** have a paper that directly compares endo-treated vs vital tooth overeruption. The "no difference" conclusion above is a mechanistic inference from how eruption works (PDL-driven) plus the absence of any vitality stratification in the overeruption literature we hold — not a measured head-to-head result. If you have a paper specifically on that comparison, give me the PDF and I will ingest it.

## Key Contributions

- First to quantify unopposed-molar overeruption **within the implant-restoration window (~9 months)** using 3D CBCT superimposition (prior data came from 6.9–12-year follow-ups).
- Shows overeruption is a **compound movement**: vertical migration + buccal tipping (1.7°) + rotation — measuring only crown cusp height (2D) under-describes it.
- Reinforces that overeruption is **fastest early** (0.43 mm in ~9 months here vs ~0.8–0.9 mm over a decade elsewhere).

## Methodology

- Retrospective cohort, tooth-level analysis; 59 patients / 68 unopposed molars; all periodontitis (69.5% stage 3 grade C).
- Pre-op CBCT (within 3 months of antagonist extraction, T0) vs pre-restoration CBCT (T1), mean 9.1 months apart.
- Mimics 20.0 multi-threshold segmentation (crown/root/alveolar bone) → STL; iterative-closest-point best-fit superimposition (Geomagic) on stable crowns + stable alveolar bone.
- Outcomes: mean & max overeruption of cusps (z-axis), bucco-lingual / mesio-distal tipping change.
- Reliability ICC 0.965–0.973; Dahlberg error 0.074–0.092 mm; STROBE-compliant.

## Results

| Parameter | Value (mean, ~9.2 mo) |
|---|---|
| Mean overeruption of cusps | **0.432 mm** (p < 0.001) |
| Max overeruption of cusps | **0.753 mm** |
| Buccal tipping | **1.717°** (69.1% tipped buccally) |
| Teeth with OEmax < 1 mm | 72.1% |
| Largest single OEmax | 2.555 mm (upper 2nd molar, 9 mo) |
| Among molar types | no significant difference (ANOVA) |

## Limitations

- All subjects had periodontitis → likely overstates overeruption vs periodontally healthy patients.
- Small sample, descriptive only (no multivariate prediction; age/periodontal parameters not modeled).
- Molars only, ≤ ~1-year follow-up.
- **No stratification by pulp vitality, endodontic status, or restoration type** — cannot answer the endo-vs-vital question.
