## One-line Summary

Randomized crossover trial (n=5 edentulous, 2 mandibular implants each): a novel "centralized occlusal scheme" — maxillary canines reshaped to add centric contacts over the canine pillar — improved occlusal contact area by 21.22% and masticatory efficiency by 21.87% vs conventional bilateral balanced occlusion, restoring chewing to 55.13% of natural dentition, with comparable satisfaction/speech/aesthetics (abstract-only).

## Summary

According to PubMed, this Chinese-language RCT from Peking University ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.19723/j.issn.1671-167X.2026.01.017)) addresses a structural problem in implant-supported overdentures: the **support center and the occlusal load center do not coincide**, which limits function. The authors propose a **centralized occlusal scheme (집중교합형)** that reshapes the maxillary canines (referencing premolar morphology) so that the lingual fossa carries **centric occlusal contacts** in the canine region, recruiting the support of the anterior mandibular implants and the maxillary **canine pillar/buttress** while preserving labial contour and occlusal balance.

In a within-patient randomized crossover (each patient wore both schemes), the centralized scheme outperformed conventional bilateral balanced occlusion on function while matching it on patient-reported outcomes. PMC full text was not retrievable, so this page reflects the English abstract only (`full_text: false`).

## Key Contributions

- A **new occlusal design** for mandibular two-implant overdentures that deliberately moves a centric stop into the canine region to better align bite load with available implant/bone support.
- Demonstrates **functional gains (contact area, chewing efficiency)** without sacrificing stability, balance, aesthetics, speech, or satisfaction.
- **FEA** confirms the mechanistic rationale: the support-bearing area extends to the maxillary canine region.

## Methodology

- **Design**: randomized crossover RCT; both schemes delivered to each patient in randomized sequence.
- **Population**: 5 completely edentulous patients, each with 2 mandibular implants.
- **Comparator**: conventional bilateral balanced occlusion vs novel centralized occlusal scheme.
- **Outcomes**: masticatory efficiency (two-color chewing-gum method); occlusal contact area (digital "virtual articulating paper" method); patient satisfaction (validated questionnaires); 3D finite element stress analysis.

## Results

- **Occlusal contact area**: +**21.22%** (95% range 17.48%, 26.01%) for centralized vs conventional.
- **Masticatory efficiency**: +**21.87%** (17.51%, 24.18%); centralized reached **55.13%** (43.73%, 59.09%) of complete natural-dentition efficiency.
- **Patient-reported outcomes**: comparable on overall satisfaction, speech, and aesthetics.
- **FEA**: centralized scheme expanded the denture support area to the maxillary canine region.
- **Caveat**: n=5, surrogate short-term endpoints; preliminary feasibility evidence only.
