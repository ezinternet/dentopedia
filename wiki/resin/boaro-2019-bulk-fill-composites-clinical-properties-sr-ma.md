## Summary
This SR+MA included 103 articles (laboratory studies + RCTs) sourced from PubMed and Scopus, analyzed using the Peto method in RevMan software. The study compared bulk-fill and conventional composite resins across nine outcomes: polymerization shrinkage, polymerization stress, cusp deflection, marginal quality, degree of conversion, microhardness, flexural strength, fracture strength, and clinical performance. Bulk-fill composites showed significantly less shrinkage, polymerization stress, and cusp deflection compared to conventional composites. Flowable bulk-fill composites showed DC comparable to conventional composites at ≤2 mm depth but superior DC at >2 mm depth. Microhardness was the sole property where bulk-fill was inferior (at ≤2 mm thickness). Clinical performance in RCTs over 1–10 years was equivalent between bulk-fill and conventional composites.

## Key Contributions
- Dual synthesis covering both in vitro physicochemical properties and clinical RCT outcomes in a single SR+MA
- Established that the key in vitro advantages of bulk-fill (less shrinkage, less stress, less cusp deflection) do not translate into measurable clinical superiority in RCTs
- Identified microhardness at shallow depths (≤2 mm) as the sole clinically relevant potential weakness of bulk-fill composites
- Flowable bulk-fill DC advantage at >2 mm depth: clinically significant for deep posterior cavity restoration

## Methodology
- **Design**: SR+MA; PubMed + Scopus search
- **Study types**: Laboratory studies and RCTs
- **Comparator**: Bulk-fill vs. conventional resin composite
- **Outcomes**: Polymerization shrinkage, polymerization stress, cusp deflection, marginal quality, DC, microhardness, flexural strength, fracture strength, clinical performance
- **Statistical method**: Peto method (RevMan software)
- **Studies**: 103 articles included

## Results
| Property | Bulk-fill vs. Conventional | Direction |
|---|---|---|
| Polymerization shrinkage | Less | Bulk-fill advantage |
| Polymerization stress | Less | Bulk-fill advantage |
| Cusp deflection | Less | Bulk-fill advantage |
| Microhardness (≤2 mm) | Lower | Bulk-fill disadvantage |
| Marginal quality | Similar | Equivalent |
| Flexural strength | Similar | Equivalent |
| Fracture strength | Similar | Equivalent |
| DC (flowable, ≤2 mm) | Similar to conventional | Equivalent |
| DC (flowable, >2 mm) | Greater than conventional | Bulk-fill advantage |
| Clinical performance (RCT, 1–10 yr) | Similar | Equivalent |

Note: An internal discrepancy exists in the publication — the abstract states regular-viscosity bulk-fill shows higher shrinkage; the body text states similar shrinkage.
