## 한줄요약
53개 in vitro 연구 체계적 문헌고찰: 유동성 벌크필 레진(flowable bulk-fill RBC)의 전환율(DC) 19–94%, 중합수축 1.3–10.5%, 수축응력 2–3 MPa, 굴곡강도 대부분 80 MPa 초과로 재료 간 편차가 크며 임상연구 부재.

## Summary
This systematic review (PRISMA, QUIN risk-of-bias) included 53 in vitro studies from PubMed, Scopus, and Web of Science to evaluate the physico-chemical properties of flowable (low-viscosity) bulk-fill resin-based composites. The review highlighted extreme heterogeneity: degree of conversion (DC) ranged 19.41–93.71%, polymerization shrinkage 1.26–10.45%, shrinkage stress predominantly 2–3 MPa across most studies, and flexural strength above 80 MPa for most materials. Moderate risk of bias was observed in most included studies. The authors concluded that flowable BF-RBCs meet requirements for bulk-fill technique but warned that inter-product variability precludes generalizing results across different commercial materials. Clinical studies are urgently needed.

## Key Contributions
- Clarified the definitional confusion: "bulk-fill" is a technique requirement (adequate DC throughout + ability to compensate/dissipate shrinkage stress), not a material category — manufacturers apply the label inconsistently
- Identified two technical requirements for true BF-RBC classification: (1) adequate DC at depth; (2) compensation/dissipation of polymerization shrinkage stress at cavity margins
- Documented that nanofillers in this generation of flowable BF-RBCs have enhanced mechanical properties while maintaining low viscosity and self-leveling capability
- DC is achieved mainly through: increased translucency; alternative photoinitiators (more efficient than camphorquinone); modified monomer chemistry + filler particle interaction for stress reduction

## Methodology
- **Design**: SR; PRISMA guidelines; QUIN RoB tool
- **Databases**: Medline (PubMed), Scopus, Web of Science
- **Inclusion**: In vitro studies; English only; ≤10 years old; specified light-curing time; light-cured only (no dual-cure)
- **Outcomes**: DC, polymerization shrinkage, polymerization shrinkage stress, flexural strength
- **Initial screen**: 684 articles → 53 included
- **Risk of bias**: QUIN — moderate in most studies

## Results
| Property | Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Degree of conversion (DC) | 19.41–93.71% | Extremely wide; product-dependent |
| Polymerization shrinkage | 1.26–10.45% | Wide variation |
| Polymerization shrinkage stress | Predominantly 2–3 MPa | Most studies in this range |
| Flexural strength | >80 MPa for most materials | Meets mechanical requirements |

Key strategies for DC: increased translucency, efficient photoinitiators beyond camphorquinone.
Key strategies for stress reduction: modified monomer chemistry, improved monomer-filler interaction.
