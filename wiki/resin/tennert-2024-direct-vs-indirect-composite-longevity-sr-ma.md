## 한줄요약
구치부 직접 vs 간접 복합레진 5 RCT 627 수복 SR+MA — 직접 수복 실패 위험 유의하게 낮음(RR=0.61), 모든 연구 high risk of bias.

## Summary
This 2024 SR+MA from the University of Bern (Dental Materials) compared longevity of direct and indirect composite restorations in posterior teeth. Five RCTs were included (627 restorations: 323 direct, 304 indirect; 279 patients, age 28–81 years). Meta-analysis showed significantly lower failure risk for direct restorations (RR=0.61 [0.47; 0.79]), contrasting with most prior reviews that found no significant difference. However, all included studies carried high risk of bias and evidence certainty was very low. Indirect restoration AFR ranged 0–15.5%; direct AFR ranged 0–5.4%. Most frequent failures: chipping/fracture, then caries.

## Key Contributions
- **Direct composite significantly lower failure risk**: RR=0.61 [0.47; 0.79] (very low evidence)
- Indirect AFR range 0–15.5%, direct AFR range 0–5.4%
- All 5 RCTs rated high risk of bias
- Largest meta-analysis for this PICO at time of publication
- PubMed/CENTRAL/Embase; search updated June 2024

## Methodology
- PICOS: adult posterior permanent teeth; indirect adhesive-luted composite vs direct composite; ≥3-year RCTs
- Risk of Bias 2.0 + GRADE Profiler 3.6
- Exclusion: case reports, class I restorations, <3-year follow-up

## Results
- **Meta-analysis**: RR=0.61 [0.47; 0.79] — direct restorations fail less (very low evidence)
- Indirect: highest AFR up to 15.5%; direct: up to 5.4%
- Primary failure: chipping and fracture (indirect), secondary caries
- No differential recommendation possible for premolars vs molars due to evidence gaps
