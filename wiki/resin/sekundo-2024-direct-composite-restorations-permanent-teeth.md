## One-line Summary

German S3 consensus-based clinical practice guideline (Part 2; 6 PICO questions, panel from 20 societies) issuing 7 recommendations + 2 statements on composite processing: prefer one-stage selective caries removal near pulp, accept relative or absolute isolation (rubber dam may aid longevity), use anatomically preformed sectional matrix + ring/wedge for Class II, etch enamel with phosphoric acid, prefer 2-step self-etch / 3-step etch-and-rinse / universal adhesives, and polish all restorations.

## Summary

Part 2 of the German S3 (AWMF 083-028) clinical practice guideline on direct composite restorations covers the **manufacturing process** rather than indications (Part 1). A systematic literature search (MEDLINE + Cochrane via OVID, to December 2021) addressed six PICO questions — caries excavation, work field isolation, matrix technique, adhesive technique, light polymerization, finishing/polishing — with outcomes of restoration survival and quality. Because systematic evidence grading was applied only to Part 1 (PICO 1–5) for feasibility, the Part 2 recommendations (PICO 6–11) are **consensus-based**, formulated in a NIH Type 1 structured consensus conference by a panel from 20 national societies. All resulting recommendations and statements were reached by strong consensus.

## Key Contributions

- A step-by-step, voted consensus protocol for placing a direct composite restoration, spanning caries removal through polishing.
- Seven consensus-based recommendations plus two (three counting bulk-fill) consensus statements, each passed by strong consensus (16/0/0 or 17/0/0).
- Explicitly grounds each procedural choice in the dual outcomes of restoration survival and restoration quality.

## Methodology

- Two methodologists; MEDLINE + Cochrane Library via OVID, studies to December 2021; manual reference-list screening.
- Inclusion: ≥12-month follow-up, ≥15 restorations, published since 1990, languages German/English/French/Russian; designs varied by PICO (SR/MA, CCT/RCT, prospective/retrospective cohort).
- Identified evidence by topic: caries excavation 8 SRs; isolation 5 SRs + 6 trials; matrix 4 in-vivo studies; adhesives 13 SRs; light curing 1 SR + 8 trials; polishing 2 studies.
- Recommendations drafted by working groups per AWMF, then presented, amended, and voted in a NIH Type 1 structured consensus conference. PRISMA flow diagrams document selection.

## Results

- **Caries excavation**: both selective and non-selective acceptable; for dentin lesions close to the pulp, **one-stage selective removal preferred** over stepwise/non-selective (Rec 1).
- **Isolation**: both relative and absolute techniques usable; **rubber dam may benefit long-term longevity** (Rec 2). Most reviews found no rubber-dam vs cotton-roll difference; some favored rubber dam.
- **Matrix**: metal and acrylic both give adequate proximal contact (Rec 3); for Class II, **anatomically preformed sectional matrix + wedge + ring preferred** (Rec 4), supported by in-vitro contact-strength and marginal-ridge data.
- **Adhesive**: **etch enamel with phosphoric acid** to reduce marginal discoloration (Rec 5); **prefer 2-step self-etch, 3-step etch-and-rinse, or universal adhesives** (Rec 6).
- **Light polymerization**: curing is decisive — handling, applied energy (power × time), opacity/shade matter (Statement 1); **bulk-fill safe to 4 mm depth with adequate-power units** (Statement 2).
- **Finishing/polishing**: restorations **should be polished** to improve surface and reduce plaque (Statement 3).
- Evidence on light curing and polishing was poor, so those statements rely on standardized protocols rather than clinical trials.
