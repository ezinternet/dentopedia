## One-line Summary

German S3 (GRADE) consensus guideline (20 societies, MEDLINE/Cochrane to Dec 2021, 6 PICO questions): direct composite is recommended for cavity Classes I–V, cusp replacement, and tooth-shape corrections, and should be preferred over indirect composite inlays in the posterior region.

## Summary

This is Part 1 of a German S3 clinical practice guideline (AWMF 083-028) developed under GRADE methodology by a panel drawn from 20 national dental societies, based on a MEDLINE/Cochrane systematic search through December 2021 structured by six PICO questions. It is the first comprehensive evidence-based guideline on the indications for direct composite in permanent teeth. The core recommendations: direct composite is a viable option across cavity Classes I–V, can be used for cusp-replacement restorations and for tooth shape/position/shade corrections, and — notably — should be preferred over indirect composite inlays in the posterior region. Class V restorations are endorsed provided contamination control and adhesive technique are adequate. The guideline frames composite as the workhorse of minimally invasive restorative dentistry (≈47 million direct restorations placed in Germany in 2021).

## Key Contributions

- First evidence-based, multi-society guideline formalizing indications for direct composite in permanent teeth.
- Expands recognized indications beyond standard Classes I–V to cusp replacement and esthetic-functional corrections.
- Issues a directional posterior preference for direct composite over indirect composite inlays.

## Methodology

Developed per AWMF Standing Guideline Commission standards and GRADE. Two methodologists conducted the MEDLINE + Cochrane Library (OVID) search to December 2021; six PICO questions guided retrieval. Outcomes assessed: survival, restoration quality, process quality. Recommendations reached by structured consensus across the 20-society panel. Part 1 scope = indications only.

## Results

- Direct composite viable for Classes I–V.
- Suitable for cusp-replacement restorations and tooth-shape corrections.
- Posterior: direct composite preferred over indirect composite inlays.
- Class V: usable with adequate contamination control + adhesive technique.
- Authors flag need for long-term studies vs (modified) glass-ionomer comparators.
