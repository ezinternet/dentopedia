## Summary
This Cochrane systematic review (2021 update) compared direct composite resin with amalgam fillings for permanent posterior teeth. It found comparable 5-year survival rates between materials, though composite restorations showed a slightly higher trend toward secondary caries. Study heterogeneity was high and blinding was inherently difficult. The amalgam decline trend continues due to environmental/health concerns (mercury), while composite materials and techniques have significantly improved.

## Key Contributions
- **5-year survival**: composite vs amalgam — no statistically significant difference
- Secondary caries risk: composite somewhat higher but non-inferiority margin uncertain
- High research heterogeneity; blinding not possible in clinical trials
- Environmental/health pressure driving amalgam phase-out
- Cochrane Collaboration standard; 2021 update (pub3)

## Methodology
- Cochrane systematic review methodology; RCTs only
- Outcome: survival, secondary caries, fracture, patient preference
- No language restriction

## Results
- Survival at 5 years: similar between composite and amalgam
- Secondary caries: composite slightly higher trend (uncertain non-inferiority)
- Fracture risk: similar between materials
- Amalgam declining: mercury concerns, cost, durability advantages diminishing
- Included RCTs generally small and short-term
