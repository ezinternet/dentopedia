## 한줄요약
21개 RCT(최장 60개월) 메타분석: 실로란(silorane)·오르모서(ormocer)·벌크필 포함 저수축 레진은 전통 메타크릴레이트 레진과 임상적으로 동등; 12개월 변연적합도만 메타크릴레이트 레진이 유의하게 우수하며, "저수축=임상 우월"은 성립하지 않음.

## Summary
This SR+MA (PROSPERO CRD42015023940) searched PubMed, Web of Science, Scopus, LILACS, and EMBASE, screening 14,217 studies and including 21 RCTs with ≥6 months follow-up (longest: 60 months). Target composites were defined by modified monomers designed to reduce polymerization shrinkage — silorane, ormocer, and bulk-fill composites — compared to conventional Bis-GMA/methacrylate-based composites. The meta-analysis found that only marginal adaptation at 12 months showed a statistically significant difference, with conventional methacrylate composites performing better. All other outcomes (marginal discoloration, recurrent caries, retention, postoperative sensitivity) showed no statistically significant differences. Excluding studies <24 months did not change results. The study concluded that low polymerization shrinkage is not the principal factor determining clinical resin superiority.

## Key Contributions
- Pooled three distinct modified-monomer composite families (silorane, ormocer, bulk-fill) under one inclusion framework — allows cross-class comparison
- Maximum 60-month follow-up included — longest available at time of publication
- Sensitivity analysis (≥24-month subset): identical conclusion to full analysis
- The only statistically significant finding (marginal adaptation at 12 months favoring methacrylate) is a notable exception warranting further study
- Defined target composites strictly: NOT Bis-GMA or traditional di-/methacrylates as main monomer; modified monomers enabling reduced shrinkage, modified placement, or deeper cure

## Methodology
- **Design**: SR+MA; PROSPERO CRD42015023940; PLOS ONE (open access)
- **Databases**: PubMed, Web of Science, Scopus, LILACS, EMBASE
- **Inclusion**: RCTs ≥6 months; Class I or II posterior permanent teeth; modified-monomer composites (silorane, ormocer, bulk-fill) vs. conventional methacrylates
- **Initial results**: 14,217 → 21 included
- **Outcomes**: Marginal integrity/adaptation, marginal discoloration, recurrent caries, retention, postoperative sensitivity
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane Collaboration; 6/11 low, 5/11 unclear
- **Follow-up**: 6–60 months (longest: 60 months)
- **Sensitivity analysis**: Restricted to ≥24 months

## Results
| Outcome | Result | Significant? |
|---|---|---|
| Marginal adaptation at 12 months | Methacrylate better | YES (only significant finding) |
| Marginal discoloration | No difference | No |
| Recurrent caries | No difference | No |
| Retention | No difference | No |
| Postoperative sensitivity | No difference | No |
| Sensitivity analysis (≥24 mo) | Same conclusion | — |

I² (heterogeneity): Not significant across all parameters — internally consistent.
