## One-line Summary

In-vitro two/three-body ACTA wear study of 4 bulk-fill composites vs 1 nanohybrid (Grandio): uncovered bulk-fills wore 59-118 µm/200k cycles vs 31 µm for the nanohybrid and had much lower Vickers hardness, but a 0.5-1.0 mm conventional cover layer wore the same as the nanohybrid alone (bulk-fill substrate stiff enough to act as a monoblock).

## 한줄요약

In-vitro ACTA 마모시험 — 벌크필 레진 4종 vs 나노하이브리드(Grandio) 비교: 커버 없는 벌크필은 59-118 µm/200k cycle로 나노하이브리드(31 µm)보다 마모가 크고 비커스 경도도 낮았으나, 0.5-1.0 mm 일반 컴포지트로 덮으면 나노하이브리드 단독과 동일한 마모를 보여 벌크필이 단단한 받침(monoblock) 역할을 함. 단독 응력 부담 부위에는 부적합.

## Summary

This in-vitro laboratory study evaluated the wear resistance and surface microhardness of four commercial bulk-fill resin composites (SDR, X-tra base, Filtek Bulk Fill, Dual-Curing Bulk Composite) against a highly filled conventional nanohybrid composite (Grandio, GDO). Using the ACTA three-body wear machine (ISO/TS 14569), the authors tested three configurations — bulk-fill covered by 0.5 mm GDO, 1.0 mm GDO, or left uncovered — at 3 days and 1 month. The key clinical question was whether a low-modulus bulk-fill substrate beneath a conventional hybrid cover layer would weaken the layered restoration's wear resistance. It does not: the covered configurations wore essentially the same as the conventional composite alone, indicating the bulk-fill substrate is stiff enough to distribute surface stress and behave as a monoblock. However, uncovered bulk-fills wore 2-4× faster and were substantially softer, with SDR approaching the wear level of temporary filling materials. The authors conclude bulk-fills have higher wear and lower hardness than conventional nanohybrids, making them unsuitable as the occlusal/stress-bearing surface unless covered by a conventional composite layer.

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies intrinsic wear rate and Vickers hardness of four bulk-fill composites vs a nanohybrid control under a standardized ACTA protocol.
- Shows a conventional 0.5-1.0 mm cover layer over a bulk-fill substrate wears like the conventional composite alone (monoblock behavior) — the underlying low-modulus bulk-fill does not compromise it.
- Identifies vol% filler content as the strongest predictor of wear rate (r=0.844), ahead of wt% (r=0.645) and Vickers hardness (r=0.758).
- Provides a mechanistic rationale (low E-modulus → more deformation and fatigue wear) for restricting uncovered bulk-fills from stress-bearing posterior restorations and bruxers.

## Methodology

- **Materials**: SDR, X-tra base (XBA), Filtek Bulk Fill (FUP), Dual-Curing Bulk Composite (FBFL); control = Grandio nanohybrid (GDO).
- **Wear**: ACTA three-body machine (ISO/TS 14569), Ø48 mm specimen wheel vs Ø19 mm stainless-steel antagonist, 15% speed difference, 15 N force, rice/millet-shell slurry (pH 7). One run = 200,000 cycles (~55.5 h, 1 Hz). Profilometry, 10 tracings/wheel, n=20.
- **Configurations**: (1) 0.5 mm GDO cover, (2) 1.0 mm GDO cover, (3) uncovered bulk-fill. Time points 3 days and 1 month; stored in water at room temperature. SEM for surface alterations.
- **Hardness**: Vickers, n=5, 5 indentations/specimen, 20 N, 20 s dwell.
- **Statistics**: two-way ANOVA (material × configuration), one-way ANOVA + Tukey (P<0.05), independent t-tests across time, linear correlations.

## Results

- **Covered (0.5 / 1.0 mm GDO)** at 3 days: ~29.3–32.8 µm/200k rev — not clinically different from GDO alone (any significant differences within ~2 µm).
- **Uncovered bulk-fills** at 3 days: 59.3–118.4 µm/200k rev, all significantly > GDO (31.4 µm). Highest = SDR (118.4); FUP 59.3, XBA 60.5, FBFL 69.7 µm.
- **At 1 month**: rates dropped (SDR 96.3, FUP 49.0, FBFL 59.3, GDO No-Cover 22.4 µm); all 3-day rates significantly higher than 1-month.
- **Vickers hardness**: GDO 124 > XBA 70 > FUP 62 > SDR 45 > FBFL 40.
- **Correlations (day 3)**: vol% filler r=0.844 (strongest), Vickers hardness r=0.758, wt% filler r=0.645.
- SDR's ~120 µm wear approaches temporary materials (Protemp 4 ~100 µm) → unsuitable for stress-bearing restorations.
