## One-line Summary
Systematic review and network meta-analysis (12 RCTs) of Class II posterior restorations found no statistically significant difference between any bulk-fill subtype and incremental layering for marginal adaptation, fractures, or retention at 6, 12, or 24 months; risk ratios clustered around unity, with ormocer-based and overlay bulk-fill composites ranking highest (non-significantly) at 12–24 months.

## Summary
This SR+NMA (PROSPERO CRD420251170292; PRISMA 2020) included 12 RCTs and performed network meta-analyses at 6, 12, and 24 months for Class II posterior restorations. Primary outcomes were marginal adaptation, fractures, and retention. No statistically significant differences were observed between any bulk-fill subtype and incremental layering at any timepoint. Risk ratios clustered around unity. P-scores showed minimal ranking variability, confirming comparable outcomes among bulk-fill subtypes regardless of viscosity or curing mode. Ormocer-based and overlay bulk-fill composites showed the highest (non-statistically superior) ranking probabilities at 12 and 24 months. CINeMA evaluation found predominantly low concerns for indirectness and risk of bias, supporting network robustness and transitivity.

## Key Contributions
- First NMA specifically for Class II posterior restorations comparing multiple bulk-fill subtypes simultaneously (not just bulk-fill vs. incremental as a binary comparison)
- P-score ranking at 6, 12, and 24 months: ormocer-based and overlay bulk-fill occupy highest ranks at 12–24 months (non-significantly)
- Confirms that viscosity and curing mode of bulk-fill do not significantly affect comparative outcomes vs. incremental layering
- CINeMA quality assessment: predominantly low concerns for indirectness and risk of bias — highest reported evidence confidence among recent bulk-fill SR+MAs

## Methodology
- **Design**: SR+NMA; PRISMA 2020; PROSPERO CRD420251170292
- **Databases**: 5 databases searched
- **Inclusion**: RCTs; Class II posterior restorations; bulk-fill vs. incremental layering
- **Studies**: 12 RCTs
- **Risk of bias**: RoB2 (Cochrane); most studies low risk
- **Evidence quality**: CINeMA framework
- **Timepoints**: 6, 12, 24 months
- **Outcomes**: Marginal adaptation, fractures, retention (relative risks)
- **Ranking**: P-scores

## Results
| Timepoint | Outcome | Significant difference? | Highest P-score |
|---|---|---|---|
| 6 months | Marginal adaptation, fractures, retention | No | — |
| 12 months | Marginal adaptation, fractures, retention | No | Ormocer-based, overlay BF |
| 24 months | Marginal adaptation, fractures, retention | No | Ormocer-based, overlay BF |

- Risk ratios: clustered around 1.0 across all comparisons
- P-scores: minimal separation — no subtype statistically superior to incremental
- Viscosity (flowable vs. sculptable) and curing mode: no significant effect on comparative outcomes
- CINeMA: low concerns for indirectness and risk of bias; transitivity assumptions met
