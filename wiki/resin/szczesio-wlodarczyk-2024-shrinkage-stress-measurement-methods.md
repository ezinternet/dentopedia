In-vitro methodological comparison (10 commercial composites) showing photoelastic shrinkage-stress values (6.4-13.4 MPa) are several-fold higher than contraction-force values (1.2-4.8 MPa) for the same materials, yet the two methods correlate strongly (r=0.8).

## Summary
This Dental Materials Journal (2024) study from Turku and Lodz directly compared two laboratory methods for measuring polymerization shrinkage stress — the photoelastic method and the contraction-forces method — on the same ten commercial resin composites (including EverX Posterior/Flow, several G-ænial products, Filtek One Bulk Fill, SDR flow+, Aura Bulk Fill). The practical message is that absolute shrinkage-stress numbers are highly method-dependent: photoelastic readings (6.4-13.4 MPa) were several times higher than contraction-force readings (1.2-4.8 MPa) for identical materials. However, the two methods correlated strongly (r=0.8), so relative rankings of materials are largely preserved across methods. This is a key caveat when comparing shrinkage-stress values reported across different studies and rigs.

## Key Contributions
- Demonstrates shrinkage-stress magnitude is method-dependent (photoelastic ≫ contraction-force) for the same composites
- Strong inter-method correlation (r=0.8) means material rankings transfer even when absolute values do not
- Provides explicit value ranges for 10 contemporary commercial composites under both methods
- Cautions against cross-study comparison of raw MPa values without matching methodology

## Methodology
Ten commercial resin composites tested by (1) the photoelastic method and (2) the contraction-forces method. The slope of the linear trendline and C-factor were calculated for each specimen, and value ranges plus inter-method correlation were reported.

## Results
- Photoelastic stress range: 6.4-13.4 MPa
- Contraction-force stress range: 1.2-4.8 MPa
- Inter-method correlation: r = 0.8 (strong positive linear)
