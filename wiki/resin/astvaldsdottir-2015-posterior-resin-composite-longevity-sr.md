## Summary
A Swedish systematic review (Journal of Dentistry 2015) examining the longevity of posterior resin composite restorations in adults. Of 4,275 abstracts screened, 18 articles were full-text reviewed and 8 met inclusion criteria (prospective trials, ≥4 years follow-up, ≥40 restorations/group, <5% annual attrition). The overall incidence rate was 1.55 lost restorations per 100 restoration-years across 62,030 restoration-months. Secondary caries was the most common biological failure reason (31/80 failures). Quality of evidence was rated low.

## Key Contributions
- **Overall failure rate: 1.55/100 restoration-years** (from 8 eligible prospective trials)
- 80 total failures across 62,030 restoration-months of follow-up
- Secondary caries = most common failure cause (often appearing after 3 years)
- High survival in efficacy settings; results cannot be directly generalized to effectiveness settings
- SBU (Swedish Health Technology Assessment) quality methodology used

## Methodology
- Inclusion: prospective RCTs/trials; ≥4 years follow-up; ≥40 restorations/experimental group; <5% annual attrition
- 4275 abstracts → 93 full texts → 18 quality assessed → 8 included
- SBU standard checklist for methodological quality
- Adult patients only (≥18 years)

## Results
- Overall failure incidence: **1.55/100 restoration-years**
- Primary biological failure: secondary caries (31/80 restorations)
- Secondary caries often occurs after 3 years → adequate follow-up critical
- Fracture also important failure mode
- Low quality evidence overall
