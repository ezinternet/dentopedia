## One-line Summary

In vitro lab study (5 flowable composites, n=10 each): the conventional flowable (Filtek Ultimate Flowable) had the highest microhardness, while bacterial adhesion was composition-dependent and not explained by surface roughness alone — resin matrix chemistry and filler composition drive both physical properties and biofilm affinity.

## Summary

This in vitro study compared four self-adhesive flowable composite resins (Vertise Flow, Fusio Liquid Dentin, Constic, Nova Compo SF) against a conventional flowable composite (Filtek Ultimate Flowable, FU) across mechanical, optical, and microbiological properties, with explicit emphasis on how **material composition** governs each property. The conventional flowable retained the highest surface microhardness, confirming that the self-adhesive chemistry (acidic functional monomers, typically lower/altered filler loading) trades mechanical performance for one-step application. The central message is microbiological: bacterial adhesion of *S. mutans* and *L. casei* was strongly **material-dependent and could not be predicted from surface roughness alone**, implicating resin matrix chemistry and filler composition as the dominant drivers of biofilm affinity. Clinically, the authors map the rankings to material selection — higher microhardness for stress-bearing posterior sites, lower bacterial adhesion for high-caries-risk patients, and better color stability for esthetic anterior restorations.

## Key Contributions

- Same-protocol head-to-head of **4 self-adhesive flowables vs 1 conventional flowable** spanning microhardness, roughness, color stability, and dual-species bacterial adhesion.
- Demonstrates that **bacterial adhesion is dissociated from surface roughness** — the roughest material (Fusio Liquid Dentin) was also the most colonized, but adhesion ranking overall tracked composition, not Ra.
- Identifies **Nova Compo SF** as the self-adhesive standout: lowest color change (ΔE) and among the lowest bacterial adhesion, despite lower microhardness than the conventional control.
- Provides a material-science framing (matrix chemistry + filler composition → property profile) usable as a selection heuristic rather than a single-endpoint bench test.

## Methodology

- **Design**: in vitro comparative laboratory study; n = 10 per material, 5 materials.
- **Groups**: Vertise Flow (VF), Fusio Liquid Dentin (FL), Constic (Co), Nova Compo SF (NC) — self-adhesive flowable; **Filtek Ultimate Flowable (FU)** — conventional flowable control.
- **Outcomes**: surface microhardness (Vickers, VHN); surface roughness (Ra); color change after 24 h (ΔE1) and 7 days (ΔE2) in coffee solution; bacterial adhesion / growth inhibition of *Streptococcus mutans* and *Lactobacillus casei* (crystal violet assay + SEM).
- **Statistics**: one-way ANOVA + Tukey post hoc, α = 0.05.

## Results

- **Microhardness (VHN)**: conventional **FU highest** (p < 0.05); self-adhesive flowables lower.
- **Surface roughness (Ra)**: significant differences among self-adhesive materials; **FL highest Ra** (p < 0.05).
- **Color stability (ΔE)**: composition-dependent; **NC lowest ΔE** after prolonged staining (p < 0.05) = most color-stable.
- **Bacterial adhesion**: material-dependent; **FU and NC lowest** for both species, **FL highest** (p < 0.05); **not explained by surface roughness alone**.
- Absolute VHN/Ra/ΔE and biofilm-quantity values are not in the source abstract (Elsevier, no PMC full text) — directions/rankings only.
