## 한줄요약
11개 RCT 메타분석: 실로란(silorane) 기반 복합레진(SBC)은 구치부 수복에서 메타크릴레이트 기반 복합레진(MBC)과 임상 성적 동등; 모든 지표에서 I² 비유의, 24개월 이상 하위분석에서도 동일 결론 — "저중합수축 = 임상 우월"은 성립하지 않음.

## Summary
This SR+MA (J Adhes Dent 2016; PMID 27695714) searched 7 databases (PubMed, Scopus, Bireme, Science Direct, Web of Science, ClinicalTrials.gov, OpenGrey) with no language or date restrictions, identifying 544 studies and including 11 RCTs. Two meta-analyses were conducted: one with all 11 studies and one restricted to studies with >24 months follow-up. Both analyses found statistically similar clinical performance between silorane-based composites (SBC) and methacrylate-based composites (MBC). Heterogeneity (I²) was not significant across all parameters. Six of 11 studies were low risk of bias; five were unclear. The conclusion emphasized that reduced polymerization shrinkage alone does not confer clinical superiority.

## Key Contributions
- Silorane-specific SR+MA (most detailed focused analysis on this material class)
- Two meta-analyses (all studies + ≥24 months subset) confirm robustness of null finding
- I² non-significant across all parameters — high internal consistency of included evidence
- Clinical verdict: SBC (primarily Filtek Silorane, 3M ESPE) performs equivalently to MBC in posterior restorations
- Key message with broad implications: material-level shrinkage reduction does not automatically translate to clinical benefit

## Methodology
- **Design**: SR+MA; PICOS framework; RevMan software
- **Databases**: PubMed, Scopus, Bireme, Science Direct, Web of Science, ClinicalTrials.gov, OpenGrey (7 databases)
- **Language/date restrictions**: None
- **Study type**: RCTs only
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane Collaboration scheme
- **Studies**: 544 identified → 17 full text → 11 included
- **Meta-analyses**: (1) All 11 studies; (2) Studies >24 months follow-up
- **Parameters analyzed**: Marginal integrity, marginal discoloration, retention, secondary caries, postoperative sensitivity

## Results
| Comparison | Outcome | Significant? |
|---|---|---|
| SBC vs. MBC (all studies) | All parameters | No |
| SBC vs. MBC (>24 months only) | All parameters | No |
| Heterogeneity (I²) | All parameters | Not significant |
| Risk of bias | 6/11 low; 5/11 unclear | — |

Key conclusion: "Low polymerization shrinkage is not the principal factor that determines the superiority of a resin."

Note: Silorane composites (Filtek Silorane) have largely been discontinued by manufacturers subsequent to this publication. The finding is primarily of theoretical/mechanistic importance.
