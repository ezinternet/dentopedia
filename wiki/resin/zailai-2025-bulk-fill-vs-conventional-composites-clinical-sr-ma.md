## 한줄요약
9개 RCT(632개 수복물) 메타분석: 구치부 영구치에서 벌크필 레진(bulk-fill resin)과 적층충전 레진(incremental resin)의 수복물 실패율 차이 없음 (RR = 0.82; p = 0.67), 단기~장기 추적 모두 동등한 임상 성적.

## Summary
This SR+MA (PROSPERO CRD420251173044) evaluated 9 RCTs involving 632 restorations in posterior permanent teeth, with a minimum 12-month follow-up, to compare failure rates and restoration quality between bulk-fill and incrementally placed resin composites. Using a random-effects model with the Cochrane RoB 2 tool, the primary outcome (restoration failure) showed no statistically significant difference: RR = 0.82 (95% CI: 0.33–2.01; p = 0.67). Subgroup analysis by follow-up (<24 months vs. ≥24 months) yielded no significant between-group variation. Secondary outcomes (marginal adaptation, discoloration, postoperative sensitivity) were comparable across most studies.

## Key Contributions
- Updated SR+MA through November 2025, capturing modern bulk-fill formulations including self-adhesive variants
- Established RR = 0.82 (non-significant) as the primary point estimate for bulk-fill vs. incremental failure comparison
- Subgroup analysis confirmed equivalence at both short-term (<24 months) and longer-term (≥24 months) follow-up
- Categorized bulk-fill composites into three clinically relevant types: (1) flowable/low-viscosity (require capping layer), (2) full-body/high-viscosity (standalone), (3) self-adhesive (no separate bonding step)

## Methodology
- **Design**: SR+MA; PRISMA guidelines; PROSPERO registration
- **PICO**: Posterior permanent teeth (P) / bulk-fill composite (I) / incremental composite (C) / failure rate + quality at ≥12 months (O)
- **Studies included**: 9 RCTs, 632 restorations; Class I and II direct restorations
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane RoB 2
- **Analysis**: Random-effects model; risk ratios (RR) with 95% CI
- **Subgroups**: Follow-up duration <24 months vs. ≥24 months
