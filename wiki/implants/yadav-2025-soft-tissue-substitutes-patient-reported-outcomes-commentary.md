## One-line Summary

EBD commentary on a SR+MA (29 studies, 19 RCTs) showing that soft-tissue substitutes significantly reduce postoperative pain, analgesic use, and surgery time vs autogenous grafts in peri-implant soft tissue augmentation, while achieving equivalent patient satisfaction and esthetic perception.

## Summary

This EBD commentary critically appraises a systematic review and meta-analysis (SR+MA) that evaluated patient-reported outcome measures (PROMs) associated with soft-tissue substitutes versus autogenous grafts in peri-implant soft tissue augmentation. The appraised SR searched five databases through November 2021 and included 29 clinical studies (19 RCTs, 7 non-randomized studies, 3 case series). Eleven subgroup meta-analyses using random-effects models and weighted mean differences (WMD) were conducted.

The central finding is that soft-tissue substitutes confer a meaningful patient-experience advantage over autogenous grafts (primarily SCTG): statistically significant reductions in postoperative pain on both 0–100 and 0–10 VAS scales, approximately 1.56 fewer analgesic tablets consumed, and approximately 10.9 fewer minutes of surgery time. These benefits are achieved without sacrificing patient-reported satisfaction, esthetic perception, or quality of life, all of which were statistically equivalent between substitutes and autogenous grafts.

The commentary notes the SR's quality assessment via AMSTAR 2, certainty of evidence via GRADE, and appropriate use of prediction intervals — reinforcing the reliability of the conclusions within the bounds of the included evidence.

## Key Contributions

1. **PROM superiority for substitutes (pain)**: Both mucosal thickness gain and keratinized tissue gain indications showed significantly reduced pain VAS scores with substitutes vs SCTG, across two VAS scale formats (0–100 and 0–10).
2. **Analgesic consumption**: Substitutes required ~1.56 fewer tablets (WMD −1.56; 95% CI −1.91 to −1.22; P < 0.00001; n=6 studies).
3. **Surgery time**: Substitutes reduced operative time by ~10.9 minutes (WMD −10.9 min; 95% CI −17.19 to −4.60; P < 0.0001; n=5 studies).
4. **Equivalence for satisfaction and esthetics**: Patient satisfaction, aesthetic perception, and quality of life did not differ significantly — substitutes achieve parity with autogenous grafts on these dimensions.
5. **Clinical implication**: The PROM evidence supports substitutes as a patient-experience-friendly alternative to SCTG when clinically appropriate, avoiding donor-site morbidity while maintaining comparable subjective outcomes.

## Methodology

- **Article type**: EBD commentary (secondary critical appraisal) — not a primary study or independent meta-analysis.
- **Appraised SR scope**: PROMs in peri-implant soft tissue augmentation; substitutes vs autogenous grafts.
- **Databases**: PubMed, Embase, CENTRAL, Web of Science, Epistemonikos + grey literature (search cutoff November 22, 2021).
- **Included studies**: 29 (19 RCTs, 7 non-randomized, 3 case series).
- **Statistical approach**: WMD with 95% CI; 11 subgroup meta-analyses; random-effects models; prediction intervals reported.
- **Quality tools**: AMSTAR 2 (SR quality), GRADE (evidence certainty).

| Patient satisfaction | — | NS | — | NS |
| Aesthetic perception | — | NS | — | NS |
| Quality of life | — | NS | — | NS |

Negative WMD = substitute favored (less pain, less analgesic use, shorter surgery). NS = no significant difference (equivalence, not inferiority).

**Limitations**: Search cutoff November 2021 means recent RCTs are excluded. Small subgroup sizes (2–6 studies each) limit precision. Heterogeneous substitute materials are pooled. This commentary is a secondary appraisal — it cannot add new evidence beyond the appraised SR.
