## One-line Summary

Two-arm RCT, 24 immediate anterior-mandible implants (12 control vs 12 + topical vitamin D3/calcitriol emulgel applied to the implant surface and osteotomy, no graft): the vitamin D arm showed significantly better 6-month probing depth (1.17 vs 1.83 mm) and bleeding index (0.58 vs 1.42), lower day-7 pain (1.00 vs 2.50 VAS, 81.2% reduction), higher ISQ (80 vs 75), and higher bone density (+18.1% vs +6.4%).

## Summary

This single-center, examiner-blinded RCT (Suez Canal University, Egypt; NCT06806423) tested whether **locally delivered** vitamin D3 (calcitriol) improves immediate implant outcomes — a question prior work had answered mostly in animal/in-vitro models or with a bone-graft confound. Twenty-four ASA I patients (21–40 y) needing single-rooted mandibular anterior extractions received an immediate ROOTT R-line implant; 12 also received a characterized calcitriol emulgel painted onto the implant surface and osteotomy walls immediately before placement. Crucially, **no bone graft was placed at the jumping gap in either arm**, isolating the vitamin D effect.

Across all four outcome domains — peri-implant soft tissue (probing depth, bleeding index), postoperative pain (VAS), implant stability (ISQ/RFA), and radiographic bone density (GSV) — the calcitriol arm was significantly better at the final 6-month time point, with well-balanced baselines (no significant baseline difference in ISQ or bone density). All 24 implants survived without failure.

## Key Contributions

- **Isolates the topical vitamin D effect**: by omitting grafting at the gap in both arms, the design separates calcitriol's contribution from grafting (unlike Amr 2019, which combined vitamin D with xenograft).
- **Four-domain convergence**: soft tissue, pain, stability, and bone density all favor calcitriol — a coherent osseointegration signal rather than a single isolated endpoint.
- **Delivery vehicle characterized**: emulgel pH 7.28, viscosity 3912.7 cps, ~98% drug content, biphasic (rapid-then-sustained) in vitro release — reproducible for replication.
- Adds RCT-level human evidence to the largely systemic-deficiency literature on vitamin D and implants.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Two-arm parallel RCT, examiner-blinded, CONSORT-compliant; n=24 implants (12/group), G*Power-justified.
- **Population**: ASA I, 21–40 y, thick phenotype, KTW ≥2 mm; non-restorable single-rooted mandibular anterior teeth. Smokers, bruxers, severe periodontitis, and periapical infection excluded.
- **Intervention**: Atraumatic extraction → immediate implant (osteotomy 950–1100 rpm, 35 Ncm). Study arm: sterile calcitriol emulgel on implant surface + osteotomy before placement. No graft at gap. Healing abutment + 3-0 Vicryl.
- **Outcomes/timing**: PD, BI at 3 & 6 mo; VAS pain days 1, 3, 7; ISQ (Osstell) immediate, 3, 6 mo; bone density (GSV via IDRISI Kilimanjaro on standardized Rinn-XCP periapical radiographs) baseline, 3, 6 mo.
- **Stats**: Independent t-tests per time point (SAS JMP 17.2), p<0.05.

## Results

| Outcome | Time | Control | Vitamin D | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Probing depth (mm) | 6 mo | 1.83 ± 0.72 | 1.17 ± 0.72 | 0.033* |
| Bleeding index | 3 mo | 2.50 ± 0.52 | 1.75 ± 0.45 | 0.0011* |
| Bleeding index | 6 mo | 1.42 ± 0.51 | 0.58 ± 0.51 | 0.0007* |
| Pain VAS | Day 3 | 3.83 ± 0.94 | 2.58 ± 0.67 | 0.0012* |
| Pain VAS | Day 7 | 2.50 ± 0.67 | 1.00 ± 0.74 | <0.0001* |
| ISQ | Immediate | 50.00 ± 5.59 | 53.92 ± 5.93 | 0.1103 (NS) |
| ISQ | 3 mo | 60.58 ± 2.27 | 64.25 ± 3.39 | 0.0057* |
| ISQ | 6 mo | 75.00 ± 2.13 | 80.00 ± 3.86 | 0.0011* |
| Bone density (GSV) | 6 mo | 121.01 ± 13.03 | 139.01 ± 13.76 | 0.0257* |
| Bone density Δ overall (0–6 mo) | — | +6.44% | +18.07% | <0.0001* |

- **Soft tissue**: 6-mo PD reduction 41.5% (Vit D) vs 24.4% (control); BI reduction 66.9% vs 43.2%.
- **Pain**: day-7 reduction 81.2% (Vit D) vs 59.5% (control); not significant on day 1 (early postoperative pain comparable).
- **Stability**: baseline ISQ comparable; divergence emerges by 3 mo and widens to a clinically meaningful 80 vs 75 at 6 mo.
- **Bone density**: both arms dipped during the 0–3 mo remodeling phase (Vit D dipped more, −7.27% vs −2.33%), then the Vit D arm surged in 3–6 mo (+27.60% vs +9.26%), yielding the higher net gain.
- **Survival**: 24/24 implants survived 6 months, no major complications.

Mechanistic interpretation offered: calcitriol's anti-inflammatory action and role in collagen synthesis explain soft-tissue/pain gains, while VDR-mediated osteoblast activity and calcium deposition explain the bone-density/stability gains.
