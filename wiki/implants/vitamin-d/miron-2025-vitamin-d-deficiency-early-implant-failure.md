## One-line Summary

Systematic review (43 studies = 16 animal + 27 human, search to May 2025) finding that vitamin D deficiency is associated with up to a fourfold increase in early implant failures, while pre-surgical supplementation improved bone-implant contact (BIC) and osseointegration even in high-risk diabetic patients.

## Summary

This systematic review (Periodontology 2000, Miron et al. 2025) examined the relationship between pre-operative vitamin D levels and implant osseointegration, and assessed studies of vitamin D supplementation before implant placement. From 151 screened publications, 43 met inclusion criteria — 16 animal and 27 human studies, with a literature search cutoff of May 15, 2025. The central finding is that vitamin D deficiency is associated with up to a fourfold increase in early implant failures, and that pre-surgical supplementation improves osseointegration outcomes, including in high-risk systemic conditions such as diabetes. The authors recommend pre-operative screening and correction of vitamin D deficiency to optimize implant success. (abstract-only — full text not retrieved from PMC; all claims grounded in the published abstract.)

## Key Contributions

- Largest synthesis to date in the vitamin D–osseointegration subdomain (43 studies from 151 screened).
- Quantifies the deficiency risk: up to fourfold increase in early implant failures.
- Establishes supplementation as a modifiable pre-operative intervention that improves osseointegration, BIC, and peri-implant bone preservation — even in diabetics.
- Concordant signal across both animal (13/16 positive) and human (22/27 supportive) evidence.
- Clinical recommendation: pre-operative screening and correction of vitamin D deficiency.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review (no meta-analysis) of in vivo experimental and clinical studies published up to May 15, 2025.
- **Screening**: 151 publications identified → 43 included.
- **Animal studies (16)**: 9 on supplementation before placement, 6 on vitamin D implant-surface coatings; models included osteoporosis, diabetes mellitus, UV-light deficiency, chronic kidney disease-induced uremia, and orchiectomy.
- **Human studies (27)**: 3 case reports, 10 retrospective, 3 prospective case series, 8 prospective controlled trials (2–4 cohorts), 3 RCTs.

## Results

- **Animal**: positive effect of vitamin D on osseointegration in 13 of 16 studies.
- **Human**: 22 of 27 studies supported a beneficial association between adequate vitamin D levels and improved osseointegration or reduced early implant failure.
- **Effect signal**: vitamin D deficiency associated with up to a fourfold increase in early implant failures.
- **Supplementation**: enhanced osseointegration, improved BIC, promoted peri-implant bone preservation, and reduced early failures, even among diabetics.
- **Implant parameters**: pocket depths, radiographic marginal bone levels, and implant stability were consistently poorer in vitamin D-deficient groups.
- **Mechanism**: deficiency impairs both new bone formation and BIC.
