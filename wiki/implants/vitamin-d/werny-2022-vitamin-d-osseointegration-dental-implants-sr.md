## One-line Summary

Systematic review of 18 studies (13 animal experimental + 2 clinical + 3 case reports; titanium implants 24–1740): vitamin D deficiency negatively affects new bone formation/BIC and supplementation enhances osseointegration in animals — especially in systemic disease (diabetes, osteoporosis, CKD) — while human evidence is only slight and at high risk of bias.

## Summary

This systematic review (PRISMA, two PICO questions) asks whether vitamin D deficiency worsens — and vitamin D supplementation improves — osseointegration of dental implants, pooling both animal and human subjects. After screening 60 records, 18 studies were included: 13 animal experimental studies, 2 clinical studies, and 3 case reports, with titanium implant counts spanning 24–1740. No meta-analysis was performed; the evidence was synthesized qualitatively.

The animal evidence is consistent and directional: all three studies comparing vitamin D deficient vs. replete animals found reduced BIC and/or new bone formation, and eight studies found that supplementation enhanced BIC and/or new bone formation — roughly 75% of systemic-supplementation studies. The benefit concentrates in systemic-disease models (nephrectomized mice, ovariectomized rats, diabetic rats), whereas the one study in healthy replete animals (Pimentel) found no significant effect, supporting the interpretation that supplementation rescues the deficient rather than boosting the already-replete.

Human evidence is thin and weaker. The key clinical signal is Mangano's finding that low serum 25(OH)D correlates with higher early dental implant failure (EDIF, within 4 months before abutment connection); Kwiatek showed supplementation improved peri-implant bone-level change (with ISQ); and case reports describe deficient/ESRD patients re-treated successfully after supplementation. The authors conclude the human evidence is "slight" and call for longitudinal RCTs.

## Key Contributions

- Maps the vitamin D–osseointegration field as predominantly preclinical (13/18 animal), correcting any impression that human implant guidance on vitamin D rests on strong clinical data.
- Separates two distinct effects: a robust **deficiency penalty** (animal) and a **supplementation benefit that is conditional on deficiency/systemic disease** (not a universal boost).
- Highlights the human early-implant-failure association (low 25(OH)D → higher EDIF) as the actionable clinical takeaway pending RCTs.
- Flags coated/local vitamin D implants as still controversial (only 50% of coating studies positive; dose-dependent opposing cellular effects).

## Methodology

- **Guideline / design**: PRISMA-structured systematic review, qualitative synthesis (no meta-analysis), two PICO questions (deficiency arm + supplementation arm).
- **Databases / window**: PubMed/Medline, Cochrane Library, Google Scholar; 1969 → March 2021.
- **Search terms**: "implant", "bone to implant contact", "vitamin D", "osseointegration".
- **Eligibility**: original studies / case reports with a control group; English and German only; letters, historic reviews, commentaries excluded.
- **Flow**: 60 records → 42 excluded → 18 included.
- **Subjects**: mice, rats (8), rabbits (3), dogs (1) for animal arm; humans (2 clinical + 3 case reports).
- **Outcomes & assessment**: BIC, new bone formation, implant resistance (biomechanical), BV/TV; histology (gold standard), histomorphometry, micro-CT, 2D radiography, SEM, wound-fluid analysis, ISQ (one clinical).

## Results

- **Deficiency (animal)**: 3/3 studies → significantly reduced BIC and/or new bone formation.
- **Supplementation (animal)**: 8 studies → enhanced BIC/NBF; ~75% of systemic-supplementation studies positive.
- **Conditionality**: Pimentel (only healthy replete animals) → no significant effect; healthy/replete subjects benefit less, and high-dose supplementation in healthy subjects can even lower bone mineral density.
- **Systemic disease**: nephrectomized mice → significant gain; ovariectomized rats → improved implant resistance + bone area density with vitamin D + alendronate; diabetic rats → no effect from vitamin D alone, but insulin + vitamin D matched healthy controls.
- **Coated/local implants**: only 50% positive; effect controversial (dose-dependent ALP/osteocalcin vs. LDH responses).
- **Human**: Mangano → low 25(OH)D associated with higher EDIF; Kwiatek → supplementation improved peri-implant bone-level change (ISQ used); Fretwurst/Flanagan case reports → successful re-treatment of deficient/ESRD patients after supplementation.
- **Limits**: animal follow-up ≤12 weeks (no long-term survival data); high methodological heterogeneity; human evidence weak / high risk of bias; no consensus on supplementation timing/dose or 25(OH)D measurement.
