## One-line Summary

## Summary

This prospective cohort assessed whether serum vitamin D levels measured on the day of implant placement relate to early dental implant failure. Across 174 implants in 109 patients followed to restoration (~3–6 months), 8 patients experienced an early failure (defined as ≥50% bone loss or implant mobility). Contrary to the prevailing hypothesis, the failed cases had a *higher* mean serum vitamin D (42.54 ng/mL) than the successful patients (31.92 ng/mL), and the difference was not statistically significant. The authors concluded there was no correlation between low serum vitamin D and early implant failure.

This is a negative-result paper and functions as a counterweight to the more positive vitamin D → osseointegration literature. The small number of failure events (n = 8) limits statistical power, and the directional finding may reflect chance rather than a protective absence of effect. ## Key Contributions

- A null / negative finding in a subdomain otherwise weighted toward a positive vitamin D association.
- Counterintuitive observation: early-failure cases had higher mean serum vitamin D (42.54 vs 31.92 ng/mL) than successes.
- Day-of-placement serum measurement tied to a defined failure endpoint, followed to restoration.

## Methodology

- Design: prospective cohort.
- Sample: 174 implants in 109 patients.
- Exposure: serum vitamin D measured on the day of implant placement.
- Follow-up: to restoration, ~3–6 months.
- Outcome: early implant failure = ≥50% bone loss or implant mobility.
- Analysis: comparison of mean serum vitamin D between failed and successful patients; correlation with low serum vitamin D assessed (difference not statistically significant).
- abstract-only — full text not retrieved; covariate adjustment and statistical detail unavailable.

## Results

- 8 patients had an early implant failure.
- Mean serum vitamin D — failed cases: 42.54 ng/mL.
- Mean serum vitamin D — successful patients: 31.92 ng/mL.
- Difference: not statistically significant.
- Conclusion: no correlation between low serum vitamin D and early implant failure.
