## One-line Summary

Retrospective observational study (72 patients / 115 implants); vitamin D deficiency was associated with significantly greater peri-implant bone loss, and gingival (soft-tissue) thickness independently affected alveolar bone preservation around the implant platform regardless of vitamin D3 status.

## 한줄요약

후향적 관찰연구(환자 72명 / 임플란트 115개): 비타민 D 결핍군에서 임플란트 주위 골소실이 유의하게 많았고, 연조직 두께(soft tissue/gingival thickness)는 비타민 D3 상태와 무관하게 변연골 소실(marginal bone loss, MBL) 보존에 독립적으로 영향을 주었다 — 25-하이드록시콜레칼시페롤(25-hydroxycholecalciferol, 25(OH)D) 모니터링과 치은 두께 평가의 병행을 제안.

## Summary

This single-center retrospective chart review assessed how two preoperative factors — serum vitamin D3 level and soft-tissue (gingival) thickness over the implant site — relate to alveolar bone preservation around the implant platform. Across 72 patients (115 implants) treated between January 2022 and February 2023, patients with vitamin D deficiency showed significantly higher peri-implant bone loss than those with normal vitamin D levels. Crucially, gingival thickness had a significant, **independent** effect on bone preservation regardless of vitamin D3 status, meaning the two factors act as separate determinants. The authors propose combining serum 25-hydroxycholecalciferol (25(OH)D) monitoring with radiological assessment of gingival thickness as a simple, reliable preoperative workflow for predictable implant outcomes.

This page is *abstract-only — full text not retrieved from PMC*; quantitative effect sizes, deficiency thresholds, and measurement methods are not available.

## Key Contributions

- Confirms, in a clinical human sample, that **preoperative vitamin D deficiency is associated with greater peri-implant marginal bone loss**.
- Establishes **gingival (soft-tissue) thickness as an independent factor** for bone preservation — effect held regardless of vitamin D3 status.
- Suggests a combined predictive workflow: serum 25(OH)D monitoring + radiological gingival-thickness assessment.

## Methodology

- Design: retrospective observational study (chart review), single private facility.
- Sample: 72 patients, 115 dental implants, January 2022 – February 2023.
- Each implant had simultaneous preoperative determination of serum vitamin D3 level and soft-tissue thickness over the implant site.
- Comparison: vitamin D deficiency vs normal levels; gingival thickness evaluated as a separate factor.
- Outcome: preservation of alveolar bone near the implant platform.
- abstract-only: statistical methods, deficiency cut-offs, follow-up duration, and bone-loss measurement technique are not reported in the abstract.

## Results

- **Vitamin D deficiency → significantly higher peri-implant bone loss** vs normal vitamin D levels.
- **Gingival thickness significantly affected bone preservation, independent of vitamin D3 status.**
- Conclusion: monitoring 25(OH)D alongside radiological gingival-thickness assessment may be a simple, reliable method for predictable implant outcomes.
- Numerical effect sizes not available (abstract-only).
