## One-line Summary

Prospective study (143 implant sites / 53 patients, RFA-measured stability): overall early implant failure 7% (10/143), but 46.2% at serum vitamin D <10 ng/mL vs 4.5% (>30 ng/mL) and 2.3% (10–30 ng/mL); deficiency also tracked lower CBCT bone density, lower ISQ, and a higher immature/mature collagen ratio.

## 한줄요약

전향 연구(임플란트 143부위/환자 53명, 공명주파수분석(Resonance Frequency Analysis, RFA) 안정성 측정): 전체 조기 임플란트 실패(Early Dental Implant Failure, EDIF) 7%(10/143)이나 혈청 비타민 D <10 ng/mL군에서 46.2%로 급증(>30 ng/mL: 4.5%, 10–30 ng/mL: 2.3%); 결핍군은 CBCT 골밀도와 임플란트 안정성 지수(Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ)가 낮고 미성숙/성숙 콜라겐 비율이 높았다.

## Summary

A single-center prospective observational study (Egypt, 2020–2023) testing whether serum vitamin D deficiency (VDD) predicts early dental implant failure (EDIF). In 53 patients receiving 143 implants of a single system, pre-operative serum 25(OH)D was categorized as sufficient (>30 ng/mL), insufficient (10–30 ng/mL), or deficient (<10 ng/mL). Outcomes were captured to 12 weeks: EDIF, CBCT bone density, resonance frequency analysis (RFA) implant stability quotient (ISQ) at placement (T1) and 12 weeks (T2), and Masson-trichrome bone-biopsy histomorphometry. The deficient group showed a markedly higher EDIF rate (46.2%), lower bone density, lower ISQ, and more unmineralized (immature) collagen — while age, gender, smoking, and controlled diabetes showed no significant association. The findings give patient-level support to the systematic-review conclusion that VDD elevates early implant-failure risk.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates a steep, statistically significant dose-response between serum 25(OH)D category and EDIF (46.2% vs 2.3% vs 4.5%; p=0.0007), with the deficient group differing significantly from both other groups (p<0.001).
- Connects VDD to implant outcomes through three measured intermediates in the same cohort: reduced CBCT bone density, reduced RFA/ISQ stability, and elevated immature/mature collagen ratio on bone biopsy.
- Isolates VDD as the dominant risk signal — no significant effect of age, gender, smoking, or diabetes (HbA1c <6.5).

## Methodology

- Prospective observational cross-sectional design; STROBE-reported, Helsinki-compliant; powered for ≥132 implants (80% power, α=0.05).
- 53 adults, 143 sites, one implant system (controls for surface/design); ≥3 months post-extraction. Excluded augmentation/sinus-lift/immediate placement, bisphosphonates, head/neck radiation, infection, pregnancy, immunocompromise, uncontrolled diabetes.
- Serum 25(OH)D by enzyme immunoassay pre-op; CBCT bone density as grey-scale at 1 mm around virtual implant (PLANMECA); trephine bone biopsy with Masson-trichrome histomorphometry (IM/M); ISQ by Osstell RFA at T1 and T2 (12 weeks).
- EDIF (≤12 weeks): ≥1 of loss of osseointegration/mobility, peri-implant infection, bleeding pocket >6 mm, or marginal bone loss (MBL) >2.5 mm.
- Stats: t-test, one-way ANOVA (Tamhane's T2/Bonferroni post-hoc), Chi-square/Fisher's exact.

## Results

- **Overall EDIF**: 10/143 = 6.9%.
- **EDIF by group**: deficient 6/13 (46.2%), insufficient 2/86 (2.3%), sufficient 2/44 (4.5%); p=0.0007.
- **CBCT bone density (grey scale)**: sufficient 660.3, insufficient 643.22, deficient 370.66; p=0.003 (deficient significantly lower).
- **ISQ T1 (placement)**: 77.52 / 75.33 / 71.54; p≈0.000003.
- **ISQ T2 (12 weeks)**: 78.02 / 76.09 / 73.50; p≈0.000002.
- **Immature/mature collagen ratio** — mandible: 0.68% / 6.39% / 30.1%; maxilla: 2.36% / 17.92% / 48.7%; both p<0.001.
- **No association** with age (p=0.968), gender (p=0.955), smoking (p=0.131; 0/25 smoker failures), or diabetes (p=0.051).
- Caveat: the 46.2% figure rests on only 6 failures within a 13-implant deficient subgroup — the point estimate is striking but the precision is limited.
