## One-line Summary

SR+MA (15 studies qualitative, 14 quantitative) finding short implants (≤8 mm) show no significant influence of length, diameter, or crown-to-implant ratio on implant loss or peri-implant bone loss.

## 한줄요약

체계적 고찰 15편(정량 14편) 메타분석. 짧은 임플란트(≤8mm)에서 길이·직경·크라운-임플란트비(C/I ratio)는 임플란트 상실·주위 골소실에 유의한 영향이 없었다 — C/I ratio 우려 완화.

## Summary

This SR+MA evaluated whether the three parameters most often blamed for short-implant failure — length, diameter, and crown-to-implant (C/I) ratio — actually predict poorer outcomes. Across 14 quantitatively pooled studies, none of the three showed a significant association with implant loss or peri-implant bone loss. The practical implication is that an unfavorable C/I ratio (>1:1), historically a reason to avoid short implants, is not by itself a contraindication, supporting short implants as a biologically sound alternative to bone grafting in reduced-bone-height sites. The length association (p=0.06) was borderline, leaving a residual trend worth noting.

## Key Contributions

- Stratifies short-implant outcomes by length, diameter, and C/I ratio simultaneously.
- Provides quantitative reassurance that C/I >1:1 does not significantly raise loss or MBL.
- Supports short implants as a graft-avoidance strategy.

## Methodology

PRISMA SR+MA (PROSPERO CRD42020191093). PubMed, Embase, Cochrane, Scopus, Web of Science. Inclusion: partially/totally edentulous posterior sites, short ≤8 mm vs >8 mm, ≥10 patients, ≥1-year follow-up, RCT or prospective case-control. 15 articles qualitative, 14 quantitative.

## Results

- Implant loss vs length / diameter / C-I ratio: no significant difference (p = 0.06 / 0.10 / 0.9).
- Peri-implant bone loss vs length / diameter / C-I ratio: no significant difference (p = 0.71 / 0.72 / 0.36).
