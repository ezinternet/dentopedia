## One-line Summary

SR+MA (36 trials, 2741 NDIs) showing narrow-diameter implants (<3.75 mm) achieve high pooled survival (maxilla 97.0%, mandible 96.5%) for fixed restorations in posterior premolar/molar sites, consistent across jaw location and implant material.

## Summary

The largest synthesis to date on narrow-diameter implants in load-bearing posterior sites (36 trials, 2741 NDIs) directly challenges the traditional restriction of NDIs to the anterior region. Pooled survival was high and indistinguishable between maxilla (97.0%) and mandible (96.5%), and between premolar (97.5%) and molar (98.6%) sites, regardless of implant material. The dominant problems were technical (screw loosening, fracture, restoration detachment, 0–23.5%) rather than biological — though biological complication data were too sparse and heterogeneous to pool, which is the key evidence gap. For posterior fixed prostheses in limited bone, this supports NDIs as a viable graft-avoidance option, with attention to occlusal/technical management.

## Key Contributions

- Largest NDI posterior-FDP dataset (2741 NDIs) — extends NDI indication into load-bearing posterior sites.
- Demonstrates survival consistency across jaw location, site, and material.
- Identifies technical (not biological) complications as the main concern; flags the biological-data gap.

## Methodology

PRISMA SR+MA (PROSPERO CRD42023428880). MEDLINE, Scopus, Cochrane + manual. NDI (<3.75 mm) + fixed dental prostheses in posterior region. RoB: Cochrane (RCT) + MINORS (non-randomized); random-effects, subgroup by jaw/material/follow-up. 36 trials in SR, 29 in meta-analysis.

## Results

- Survival range 92.73–100% (follow-up 10.8 months–12 years).
- Pooled SR: maxilla 97.0% (95% CI 96.2–97.8), mandible 96.5% (95% CI 95.7–97.3); p=0.688.
- Premolar 97.5% (95% CI 95.9–99.1), molar 98.6% (95% CI 96.5–99.6).
- Technical complications 0–23.5%; biological comparisons not feasible.
