## One-line Summary

Technique report (n = 3) demonstrating that titanium pins — repurposed from GBR fixation — can reliably stabilize free gingival grafts (FGGs) and apically repositioned flaps (ARFs) during vestibuloplasty, achieving successful KM augmentation with minimal shrinkage in sites where conventional suturing was impractical.

## Summary

Adequate 각화점막 (Keratinized Mucosa, KM) width around implants and natural teeth is essential for peri-implant/periodontal health and patient comfort during oral hygiene. Classical vestibuloplasty using 유리치은이식편 (Free Gingival Graft, FGG) or 근단이동판막 (Apically Repositioned Flap, ARF) relies on suturing to hold the graft against the periosteum during the early revascularization window. However, heavy muscle pull and anatomically compromised recipient beds render suturing unreliable in certain patients.

Zwanzig et al. (2025) repurposed titanium fixation pins — the same hardware used to tack Ti-mesh or membranes to bone in 골유도재생술 (Guided Bone Regeneration, GBR) — to mechanically stabilize FGGs and ARFs during vestibuloplasty. In three patients with insufficient KM width, peri-implant/periodontal inflammation, and oral hygiene discomfort, the technique yielded successful graft integration, increased KM width and vestibular depth, and minimal shrinkage without reported complications. The authors attribute the favourable outcome to the pins' ability to ensure intimate graft-to-periosteum contact across the entire recipient surface, facilitating 재혈관화 (Revascularization) and reducing ischemic shrinkage.

## Key Contributions

- **Tool repurposing**: Demonstrates that standard GBR titanium fixation pins can be applied to soft tissue surgery without modification, expanding the utility of hardware already in most implant surgical kits.
- **Suturing limitation overcome**: Pin fixation is described as effective in sites with extensive muscle pull and an unstable recipient bed where traditional sutures lose purchase or tear soft tissue.
- **Graft physiology rationale**: Pin-secured intimate graft-periosteum contact promotes capillary ingrowth, minimizes dead space, reduces shrinkage, and lowers risk of graft necrosis — the same logic as membrane tacking in GBR.
- **Procedure simplification**: Authors claim shorter operative time and reduced patient morbidity versus multi-suture approaches (not quantified in the abstract).
- **Scope**: Applicable to both peri-implant and peri-dental KM augmentation.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Technique report / prospective case series
- **Sample**: 3 patients
- **Indication**: Insufficient KM width with peri-implant or periodontal inflammation and oral hygiene discomfort
- **Procedure**: Vestibuloplasty (FGG or ARF) with titanium pin fixation replacing or supplementing sutures
- **Mechanism**: Pins press graft flat against periosteum, preventing graft mobility during the critical first revascularization phase
- **Follow-up**: Postoperative (duration unspecified in abstract)
- **Outcome measures**: Graft integration, KM width, vestibular depth, graft shrinkage, complications

## Results

All three patients showed successful graft integration, increased KM width and vestibular depth, and minimal graft shrinkage. No complications were reported. Pin fixation performed reliably in sites where conventional suturing was described as impractical. Specific mm values for KM width change and shrinkage percentage are available only in the full text (pp. 395–405).

Authors recommend randomized clinical trials for formal efficacy evaluation.
