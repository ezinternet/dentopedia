## 한줄요약
Systematic review (16 in vivo studies, 1 clinical + 15 animal across 5 species): substantial heterogeneity in dual-biofunctional (antibacterial + osseointegration) implant coatings; 6 studies use peri-implantitis challenge models; only one short-term clinical trial; calls for protocol standardization.

## Summary
Systematic review of in vivo evaluations of implant coatings engineered to simultaneously deliver antibacterial activity and osseointegration. Sixteen studies were included (one 15-patient 4-month clinical trial and 15 animal studies across rats, rabbits, dogs, pigs, and goats), covering coatings based on inorganic compounds, peptides, metal-based osteogenic agents, and antimicrobial metals/metal oxides/polymers. Protocol heterogeneity is high, peri-implantitis challenge models are used in 6 studies, and clinical evidence remains thin.

## Key Contributions
- First in-vivo-focused SR of dual-biofunctional coatings
- Maps coating chemistries to animal model and outcome heterogeneity
- Highlights peri-implantitis challenge models as a critical missing standard

## Methodology
- Search: Medline, Scopus, Web of Science, English, up to December 2024
- Eligibility: in vivo studies involving intraoral implantation
- 16 studies (1 clinical, 15 animal)
- Narrative synthesis (heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis)

## Results
- Coating components: inorganic (e.g., calcium phosphate), peptides, metal-based osteogenic agents, antibacterial metals/metal oxides/polymers
- Animal models: 5 species (rat, rabbit, dog, pig, goat) — large variance
- 6 studies use peri-implantitis models to evaluate osseointegration under disease challenge
- Clinical trial: 15 patients, 4-month outcomes only

## Limitations
- No quantitative pooling possible
- Animal protocol heterogeneity
- Single short-term clinical trial — far from clinical evidence base
- Recommends new standardized in vitro and in vivo methods
