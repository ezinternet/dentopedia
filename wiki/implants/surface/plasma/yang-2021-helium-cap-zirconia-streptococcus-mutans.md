## One-line Summary

In vitro zirconia study: helium cold atmospheric plasma (CAP) increased O/C ratio and hydrophilicity (good biocompatibility) and inhibited early S. mutans adhesion/biofilm via reactive oxygen species — but the antibacterial effect lasted only ~24 h, attenuated at 48 h, and disappeared by 72 h.

## 한줄요약

지르코니아 in vitro: 헬륨 CAP가 O/C 비·친수성↑(생체적합성 양호), 반응성 산소종으로 S. mutans 초기 부착·바이오필름 억제 — 단 항균효과는 약 24h만 지속, 48h 약화, 72h 소실.

## Summary

Quantifies the time-decay of plasma's antibacterial effect on zirconia. The benefit is real but short-lived (~24 h), which has a direct clinical implication: any decontamination/placement should follow treatment promptly. Pairs with Lee 2022's "longer is worse" to define a narrow effective window.

## Key Contributions

- Time-decay curve of plasma antibacterial effect (24 h → gone by 72 h).
- Effect attributed to reactive oxygen species; O/C↑, biocompatible.

## Methodology

- Helium CAP on zirconia; surface chemistry/microstructure, cytotoxicity; S. mutans at intervals post-CAP.

## Results

- ↑ O/C ratio, hydrophilicity; good biocompatibility.
- Inhibited early S. mutans adhesion/biofilm; effect ~24 h, attenuated 48 h, gone 72 h.

**Boundary (living-document):** in vitro; antibacterial benefit transient (timing matters); single species; no in vivo/clinical data.
