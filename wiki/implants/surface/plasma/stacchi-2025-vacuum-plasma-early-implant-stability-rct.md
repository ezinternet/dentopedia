## One-line Summary

Single-blind split-mouth RCT (24 patients, 48 implants): vacuum plasma activation immediately before insertion gave significantly higher ISQ than untreated controls at 60 and 90 days (P=0.04, P=0.03), with limited absolute difference; insertion torque unchanged (P=0.86), all 48 osseointegrated.

## 한줄요약

단일맹검 split-mouth RCT(환자 24명, 임플란트 48개): 식립 직전 진공 플라즈마가 60·90일에 대조군보다 유의하게 높은 ISQ(P=0.04, P=0.03), 절대차는 작음. 식입토크 차이 없음(P=0.86), 48개 모두 골유착.

## Summary

The strongest human evidence in the cluster. A proper controlled split-mouth RCT shows vacuum plasma yields a more favourable early ISQ recovery curve, reaching significance at 60 and 90 days — but the authors are explicit that the absolute ISQ gap is small and both arms osseointegrated predictably. So: real, controlled, but modest.

## Key Contributions

- Controlled human RCT (split-mouth) of pre-insertion plasma — fills the clinical gap.
- Full early ISQ trajectory; significance at 60/90 d.

## Methodology

- 24 patients, contralateral plasma vs control implants. Plasma X Motion (MegaGen). ISQ at placement, 7/14/21/28/42/60/90 d.

## Results

- All 48 osseointegrated; no adverse events. Insertion torque NS (P=0.86).
- Both arms dipped, lowest ISQ at 21 d.
- Plasma significantly higher ISQ at 60 d (P=0.04) and 90 d (P=0.03); absolute difference limited.

**Boundary (living-document):** small n (24); significance only at later timepoints; absolute effect small; single device.
