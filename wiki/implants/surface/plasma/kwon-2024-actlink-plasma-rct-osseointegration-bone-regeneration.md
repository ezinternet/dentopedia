## One-line Summary

Prospective RCT (28 patients, SLA vs SLA+plasma before placement): no significant difference in buccal bone defects or implant stability, but the plasma group showed significantly greater mesial/distal marginal bone changes — authors read this as potential bone-regeneration enhancement.

## 한줄요약

전향적 RCT(28명, SLA vs 식립 전 SLA+플라즈마): 협측 골결손·임플란트 안정성은 차이 없고, 플라즈마군이 근심·원심 변연골 변화가 유의하게 컸다 — 저자는 골재생 촉진 가능성으로 해석.

## Summary

The human RCT that complicates the optimistic story. Its primary stability endpoint was negative (no difference vs control), with only a secondary marginal-bone-change signal. Read alongside [[implants/surface/plasma/stacchi-2025-vacuum-plasma-early-implant-stability-rct]] (which found a late ISQ benefit), it shows the human evidence is genuinely mixed.

## Key Contributions

- Controlled human RCT; transparent negative primary (stability) result.

## Methodology

- 28 patients randomized SLA vs SLA/plasma (ACTLINK). Recall 1/3 months; second-stage at 4 months. Buccal defects, stability, marginal bone change.

## Results

- No significant difference in buccal bone defects or implant stability.
- Plasma group: significantly greater marginal bone changes mesial/distal.

**Boundary (living-document):** small n (28), short follow-up; primary stability endpoint negative; marginal-bone interpretation needs longer follow-up.
