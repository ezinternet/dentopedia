## One-line Summary

In vitro study (machined and rough Ti): plasma significantly reduced carbon (p<0.001) and increased hydrophilicity (p<0.0001) without architecture change, promoted greater early fibroblast/osteoblast attachment (differences gone after 12 h), and upregulated angiogenesis/proliferation genes (Apln, Crabp2 at 6 h).

## Summary

Adds molecular (RNA-seq) depth to the decontamination/attachment mechanism on both surface roughnesses. The honest nuance: the early-attachment advantage diminishes by 12 h — consistent with the cluster's pattern that plasma accelerates early events rather than raising the endpoint.

## Key Contributions

- Gene-expression evidence (Apln, Crabp2) for plasma's early biological effect.
- Both machined and rough surfaces; fibroblasts + osteoblasts.

## Methodology

- Plasma on machined/rough Ti; SEM, XPS, hydrophilicity; viability, IHC; RNA-seq.

## Results

- ↓ carbon (p<0.001), ↑ hydrophilicity (p<0.0001); no architecture change.
- Greater early attachment (diminished after 12 h); Apln/Crabp2 up at 6 h.

**Boundary (living-document):** in vitro; early-attachment advantage transient; clinical significance for peri-implant disease undetermined.
