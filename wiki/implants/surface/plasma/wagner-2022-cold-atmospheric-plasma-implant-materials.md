## One-line Summary

In vitro (Ti + zirconia discs): CAP did not change elemental composition but regulated proliferation/ECM/adhesion markers in a treatment-time-dependent way in MG-63 osteoblasts and HGF-1 gingival fibroblasts — beneficial for faster cell proliferation and adhesion on both materials.

## Summary

Provides the cell-level mechanism behind the animal osseointegration results, and notably covers both bone-facing (osteoblast) and soft-tissue-facing (fibroblast) cells on both titanium and zirconia. The key nuance: the benefit is time-dependent and chemistry-neutral — CAP re-activates the surface for cells without changing its elemental makeup, so application time matters.

## Key Contributions

- Dual material (Ti + zirconia) and dual cell (osteoblast + fibroblast) in vitro.
- Effect is time-dependent and independent of elemental composition.

## Methodology

- Ti/zirconia discs, CAP at various durations; MG-63 and HGF-1.
- EDS (composition); real-time PCR (adhesion/proliferation/ECM); SEM/fluorescence.

## Results

- No change in elemental composition (EDS).
- Proliferation/ECM/adhesion markers differently regulated by CAP application time.
- Faster cell proliferation and adhesion on both Ti and zirconia.

**Boundary (living-document):** in-vitro only; no single optimal time (material/cell-dependent); no in vivo/clinical outcome here.
