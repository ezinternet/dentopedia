## One-line Summary

Systematic review (MEDLINE/PubMed + EMBASE; 15 studies): CAP effectively reduced bacterial loads on dental implant surfaces (incl. S. mitis, S. aureus) and combated biofilm/plaque relevant to peri-implantitis — promising decontamination, mostly pre-clinical evidence.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(15편): CAP는 임플란트 표면 세균(S. mitis, S. aureus 등)을 효과적으로 감소, 임플란트주위염 관련 바이오필름·플라크 억제 — 제염 옵션으로 유망하나 대체로 전임상.

## Summary

Narrows the antimicrobial question to the implant surface — the peri-implantitis target. Across 15 control-group studies CAP reliably reduced bacterial load and biofilm. It is a credible decontamination adjunct candidate, but the body of evidence is pre-clinical and unpooled.

## Key Contributions

- Implant-surface-specific bactericidal synthesis.
- Frames CAP as peri-implantitis decontamination candidate.

## Methodology

- MEDLINE/PubMed, EMBASE to May 2023; CAP-vs-control bactericidal studies; no-control/no-bactericidal excluded.

## Results

- 15 studies included.
- CAP ↓ bacterial loads on implant surfaces (S. mitis, S. aureus); ↓ biofilm/plaque.
- Conclusion: significant bactericidal activity; promising.

**Boundary (living-document):** mostly in-vitro/pre-clinical; no pooled estimate; no clinical peri-implantitis outcomes.
