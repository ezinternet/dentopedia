## One-line Summary

In vitro study of a cylindrical dielectric barrier discharge (DBD) plasma source (implant as electrode, packaging as dielectric, kept sterile): reduced surface carbon ~60% (XPS), produced a super-hydrophilic surface with increased calcium elution, and significantly improved cell adhesion, proliferation, and differentiation on SLA implants.

원통형 유전체 장벽 방전(DBD) 플라즈마 장치(임플란트=전극, 포장재=유전체, 무균 유지) in vitro: 표면 탄소 약 60%↓(XPS), 초친수성·칼슘 용출↑, SLA에서 세포 부착·증식·분화 유의 향상.

## Summary

A device-engineering contribution: DBD lets you treat the implant inside its sterile packaging — practical for chairside use. Biologically it hits the cluster's standard targets (carbon down, super-hydrophilic, better cell response). In vitro only.

## Key Contributions

- DBD source treats the implant while keeping it sterile (chairside-ready).
- ~60% carbon reduction; super-hydrophilic.

## Methodology

- Implant as electrode, packaging as dielectric → DBD plasma; discharge optimization; XPS; in vitro cells on SLA.

## Results

- ~60% surface carbon reduction (XPS); super-hydrophilic; ↑ calcium elution.
- Significantly improved cell adhesion, proliferation, differentiation.

**Boundary (living-document):** in vitro only; no in vivo/clinical data.
