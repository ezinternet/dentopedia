## One-line Summary

In vitro study comparing a novel magnetic-field "vortex plasma" source vs conventional direct plasma discharge on titanium: vortex plasma improved hydrophilicity, reduced carbon, and enhanced osteoblast adhesion/activity comparably to direct plasma, while keeping discharge independent of implant size/shape and preserving morphology.

## Summary

Addresses a real engineering limitation: direct-discharge plasma (implant as electrode) is non-uniform across implant sizes/shapes. The magnetic-field vortex design delivers uniform, target-independent treatment with equivalent biological outcome — relevant to consistent chairside use across a product range.

## Key Contributions

- Target-independent uniform plasma (vortex) equivalent to direct discharge.

## Methodology

- Vortex vs direct plasma on Ti; hydrophilicity, surface chemistry, morphology; osteoblast activity.

## Results

- Vortex: ↑ hydrophilicity, ↓ carbon, ↑ osteoblast adhesion/activity — comparable to direct plasma; morphology preserved.

**Boundary (living-document):** in vitro; equivalence not superiority; no in vivo/clinical data.
