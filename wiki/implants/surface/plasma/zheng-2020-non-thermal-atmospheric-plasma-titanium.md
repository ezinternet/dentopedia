## One-line Summary

In vitro + in vivo (rat): novel mixed-gas non-thermal atmospheric plasma (NTAP) on Ti dropped water contact angle ~100% and carbon ~80% (oxygen +50%), accelerated MC3T3-E1 preosteoblast attachment/proliferation/differentiation, and in vivo raised BIC 25–40% while suppressing osteoclasts/bone resorption ~50%.

## Summary

The cluster's tightest single-paper causal chain: surface re-activation (hydrophilic, decontaminated) → preosteoblast response → in vivo bone, with the extra finding that plasma not only increased bone formation (BIC) but suppressed osteoclastic resorption. The novel mixed-gas device addresses single-gas plasma's slow-time/recontamination weakness.

## Key Contributions

- Mixed-gas NTAP device (faster, recontamination-resistant).
- Adds osteoclast/resorption suppression to the usual BIC-up finding.

## Methodology

- Ti + mixed-gas NTAP; surface (contact angle, carbon, oxygen).
- In vitro MC3T3-E1 (attachment→mineralization); in vivo rat (BIC, osteoclasts, resorption).

## Results

- Contact angle ↓ ~100%; carbon ↓ ~80%; oxygen ↑ ~50%.
- ↑ MC3T3-E1 attachment/spread/proliferation/osteogenic differentiation/mineralization.
- In vivo: BIC ↑ 25–40%; osteoclasts & bone resorption ↓ ~50%.

**Boundary (living-document):** rat + in vitro; no human/clinical/loading data.
