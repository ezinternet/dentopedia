## One-line Summary

In vivo mouse safety study (180 mice, 12 groups; buccal mucosa treated with two CAP sources for 10 s or 1 min vs UV and untreated controls; sacrificed 1 day or 1 week): a pre-clinical screen for short-term mucosal tissue damage before in-human CAP use for peri-implant disease.

## 한줄요약

쥐 in vivo 안전성 연구(180마리, 12군; 협측 점막에 2종 CAP 10초·1분 vs UV·무처리, 1일·1주 후 희생): 사람 임플란트주위염 CAP 적용 전 단기 점막 손상 여부를 보는 전임상 안전성 검증.

## Summary

The cluster's safety anchor. Before clinicians point a plasma jet at peri-implant/mucosal tissue, someone has to check it doesn't damage mucosa — this study is that check, comparing devices and exposure times against UV and untreated controls with blinded histopathology.

## Key Contributions

- Pre-clinical mucosal-safety basis for clinical CAP use in peri-implant disease.
- Device/dose comparison (two sources; 10 s vs 1 min).

## Methodology

- 180 mice, 12 groups (n=15); buccal mucosa CAP 10 s (two sources) or 1 min (kINPen 09); UV and untreated controls; sacrifice 1 day or 1 week; blinded histology, weights.
