## One-line Summary

In vivo rabbit study (18 SLA implants in 6 rabbits, femur, 4 wk): vacuum-plasma (VP) implants had higher bone area ratio, bone volume, and ISQ than controls, but no difference was statistically significant; both groups osseointegrated well.

## 한줄요약

토끼 in vivo(SLA 18개, 6마리, 대퇴골, 4주): 진공 플라즈마(VP)군이 골면적비·골량·ISQ 높았으나 통계적 유의차 없음. 두 군 모두 양호한 골유착.

## Summary

The cluster's null-but-honest data point. Direction favors plasma (higher BA, bone volume, ISQ) — consistent with the other animal studies — but nothing reached significance, almost certainly because the study is small and short (18 implants, 6 rabbits, 4 weeks). It cautions against over-reading the positive animal signal as uniformly robust.

## Key Contributions

- Vacuum-plasma chairside device tested with ISQ + histomorphometry + micro-CT.
- Transparent non-significant result that tempers the cluster's positive trend.

## Methodology

- 18 SLA implants, untreated vs VP, in femurs of 6 rabbits; 4-week sacrifice.
- Endpoints: BIC, bone area ratio (BA), bone volume (micro-CT), ISQ.

## Results

- VP higher BA, bone volume, ISQ than control.
- No statistically significant between-group differences.
- Both groups achieved good osseointegration.

**Boundary (living-document):** underpowered (18 implants/6 rabbits, single 4-wk timepoint); industry-affiliated; no human/clinical/loading data.
