## One-line Summary

In vivo miniature pig study (16 SLA implants in 4 pigs, 8-week healing; cold argon plasma 240 s before insertion): plasma group had higher BIC (90.4% vs 86.5%) and interthread bone density (72.5% vs 63.4%), peri-implant bone density similar (60.5% vs 61.1%); authors call the gains slight, with no fluorescence sign of significantly faster mineralization.

## 한줄요약

미니돼지 in vivo(SLA 16개, 4마리, 8주; 식립 전 cold argon plasma 240초): 플라즈마군 BIC 90.4 vs 86.5%, 나사산간 골밀도 72.5 vs 63.4%로 높았으나 임플란트주위 골밀도는 유사(60.5 vs 61.1%). 증가폭은 "약간" 수준, 광물화 가속 근거 없음.

## Summary

A balanced data point: pre-insertion cold argon plasma raised BIC and interthread bone density in pigs, but the authors themselves frame the improvement as slight, peri-implant bone density was unchanged, and fluorescence labeling did not show significantly faster or stronger mineralization. CAP did not alter surface morphology. It supports a real-but-modest in vivo benefit rather than a dramatic one.

## Key Contributions

- Early in vivo test of chairside pre-insertion CAP on SLA implants.
- Documents morphology-neutral surface conditioning with modest osseointegration gains.

## Methodology

- 16 SLA implants in frontal bone of 4 miniature pigs; test group CAP 240 s (60 s/quadrant) before insertion.
- Sequential fluorescence labeling; 8-week histomorphometry/radiology.

## Results

| Metric | Plasma | Control |
|---|---|---|
| BIC | 90.4% | 86.5% |
| Interthread bone density | 72.5% | 63.4% |
| Peri-implant bone density | 60.5% | 61.1% |

- Surface morphology unchanged (SEM). No adverse peri-implant effects.
- Fluorescence: no sign of significantly faster/stronger mineralization.

**Boundary (living-document):** small (16 implants/4 pigs), single 8-wk timepoint; gains modest and not framed as strongly significant; abstract/discussion-level source (no full statistics). Animal only.
