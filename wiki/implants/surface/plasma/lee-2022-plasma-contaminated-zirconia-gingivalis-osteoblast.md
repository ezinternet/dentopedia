## One-line Summary

In vitro contaminated zirconia: at 60 s non-thermal plasma (NTP), P. gingivalis adhesion = control (p=0.91), but adhesion significantly increased at 300/600 s (p<0.05); osteoblast (MC3T3-E1) activity increased at 72/96 h after 60/120 s NTP (p<0.017). Conclusion: 60 s NTP inhibits P. gingivalis and increases osseointegration on contaminated zirconia.

## 한줄요약

오염 지르코니아 in vitro: NTP 60초는 P. gingivalis 부착 대조와 동일(p=0.91), 300·600초는 부착 유의 증가(p<0.05); 골모세포 활성은 60/120초 후 72·96h 증가(p<0.017). 결론: 60초가 적정.

## Summary

The cluster's clearest "dose matters, and more is not better" lesson. Short NTP (60 s) keeps bacterial adhesion at control level while boosting osteoblast activity on contaminated zirconia — but extending plasma to 300–600 s significantly *increased* P. gingivalis adhesion. The clinical implication: a short, specific protocol is essential; over-treatment backfires.

## Key Contributions

- Non-monotonic, time-sensitive plasma effect on bacterial adhesion.
- Decontamination + osteoblast response on zirconia in one study.

## Methodology

- Zirconia NTP 60/300/600 s → P. gingivalis 48 h (adhesion).
- MC3T3-E1 osteoblasts on contaminated zirconia after NTP 60/120 s, 48/72/96 h (activity).

## Results

- P. gingivalis adhesion: 60 s = control (p=0.91); ↑ significantly at 300/600 s (p<0.05).
- Osteoblast activity: ↑ at 72/96 h for 60/120 s (p<0.017).

**Boundary (living-document):** in-vitro; strongly time-dependent — benefit only at short (60 s) exposure, longer is counterproductive; no in vivo/clinical outcomes.
