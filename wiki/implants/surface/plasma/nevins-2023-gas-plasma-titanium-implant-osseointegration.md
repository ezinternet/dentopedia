## One-line Summary

Preclinical in vivo: 36 implants in 6 foxhounds (chairside gas-plasma test vs control, sacrificed 2/4/6 wk): BIC higher for plasma at all timepoints, significantly higher at 4 wk (p=0.046); radiographic bone level significantly better for plasma at 6 wk (p=0.016).

## 한줄요약

전임상 in vivo: foxhound 6마리 임플란트 36개(식립 직전 gas plasma vs 대조, 2·4·6주): BIC 모든 시점 플라즈마군 높음, 4주 유의(p=0.046); 방사선 골수준 6주 유의(p=0.016).

## Summary

Validates the chairside, immediately-before-placement plasma protocol in a large animal with a proper randomized split design and time-course. The benefit is real but timepoint-specific: BIC trended higher throughout and hit significance at 4 weeks, while the radiographic bone-level advantage emerged at 6 weeks. Per-timepoint samples are small.

## Key Contributions

- Randomized large-animal validation of chairside pre-placement gas plasma.
- Time-course histomorphometry + radiography.

## Methodology

- 6 male foxhounds; mandibular premolar/first-molar extraction, 42-day socket healing.
- 36 implants, mandibles randomized plasma vs control; sacrifice 2/4/6 wk (2 animals each). SLA surface.

## Results

- BIC higher for plasma at all timepoints; significant at 4 wk (p=0.046).
- Radiographic bone level: NS at 2 & 4 wk; significant at 6 wk (p=0.016, more control bone loss).

**Boundary (living-document):** small per-timepoint n (2 animals); significance is timepoint-specific, not uniform; no RTQ/clinical/loading data; animal only.
