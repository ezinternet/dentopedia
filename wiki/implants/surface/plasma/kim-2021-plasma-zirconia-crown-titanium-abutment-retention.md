## One-line Summary

In vitro (SFE on 24 zirconia blocks; retentive strength on 120 zirconia-crown/Ti-abutment assemblies, self-adhesive cement): NTP raised zirconia surface free energy and improved retention without thermocycling, but after 5000-cycle thermocycling the NTP benefit disappeared and NTP+silane/primer was significantly weaker than silane/primer alone.

## 한줄요약

in vitro: 비열 플라즈마(NTP)는 지르코니아 표면자유에너지↑·비열순환 유지력↑(대조 대비), 그러나 5000회 열순환 후 NTP 이득 소실, NTP+실란/프라이머는 실란/프라이머 단독보다 유의하게 약함.

## Summary

A cautionary, conditional result in the prosthetic axis. NTP alone improves zirconia surface energy and short-term retention, but the gain does not survive aging (thermocycling), and — importantly — combining NTP with a primer/silane is actively detrimental after aging. So plasma is not a free add-on to existing zirconia bonding protocols.

## Key Contributions

- Tests plasma for zirconia cement retention with aging and primer interaction.
- Surfaces a negative interaction (NTP + primer/silane worse after thermocycling).

## Methodology

- SFE (Owens–Wendt) on 24 zirconia blocks; RS on 120 crown/abutment assemblies (G-CEM LinkAce).
- Groups: Control, NTP, Si, NTP+Si, Pr, NTP+Pr; half thermocycled 5000× (5–55 °C). ANOVA/Tukey, t-test.

## Results

- SFE: NTP > Control (p<0.05).
- RS non-thermocycling: NTP > Control (p<0.05).
- RS thermocycling: NTP = Control (NS).
- Thermocycling: NTP+Si and NTP+Pr significantly < Si and Pr alone (p<0.05).

**Boundary (living-document):** in-vitro; NTP-alone benefit lost after aging; NTP+primer/silane detrimental after aging — do not assume additive benefit clinically.
