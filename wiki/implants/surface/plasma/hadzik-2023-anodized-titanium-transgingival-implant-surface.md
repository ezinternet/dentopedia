## One-line Summary

In vitro preliminary report: anodized Ti-6Al-4V discs (28/67/78/98 V) with half additionally treated by low-pressure RF oxygen plasma ("S"); plasma left morphology unchanged but raised surface oxygen ~1% and produced significantly higher human-fibroblast proliferation and adhesion than anodized-only "A", with no cytotoxicity (best corrosion resistance at 78 V).

## Summary

The cluster's soft-tissue axis. Most plasma papers here target the bone interface; this one targets the transgingival surface (healing abutment/collar). Oxygen plasma added on top of anodization improved fibroblast proliferation and adhesion — relevant to the soft-tissue seal — without altering morphology or causing cytotoxicity. Preliminary and in vitro.

## Key Contributions

- Soft-tissue (transgingival) surface application of plasma.
- Isolates oxygen-plasma's incremental fibroblast benefit over anodization alone.

## Methodology

- Grade-23 Ti-6Al-4V anodized at 28/67/78/98 V ("A"); half + low-pressure RF oxygen plasma ("S").
- SEM, XPS, Raman, corrosion; CHO + primary human fibroblasts (MTT, adhesion).

## Results

- "S" morphology = "A"; surface oxygen ↑ ~1%.
- Best corrosion resistance: A-78V and S-78V.
- No cytotoxicity; "S" (plasma) significantly higher fibroblast proliferation and adhesion than "A".

**Boundary (living-document):** preliminary, in vitro; no in vivo/clinical soft-tissue sealing data; protocol not finalized.
