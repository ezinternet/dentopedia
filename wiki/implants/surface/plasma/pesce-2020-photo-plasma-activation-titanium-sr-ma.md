## One-line Summary

PRISMA SR + meta-analysis of pre-clinical animal studies (518 identified → 10 included; MA pooled 4 UV-in-rabbit studies): UV and non-thermal argon plasma activation of Ti surfaces may improve osseointegration in vivo; BIC significantly increased in bio-activated groups when follow-up was homogeneous, but heterogeneity was high (I²>75%).

## 한줄요약

전임상 PRISMA SR+메타분석(518→10편 포함; 메타분석은 토끼 UV 4편): 티타늄 표면 UV·비열 플라즈마 활성화가 동물에서 골유착 향상 가능, BIC 유의 증가(추적기간 동질 시). 이질성 높음(I²>75%).

## Summary

The cluster's most rigorous (meta-analytic) signal — but with two important caveats the abstract is explicit about: the quantitative pooling was carried out on UV photofunctionalization studies in rabbits (plasma stayed qualitative), and heterogeneity exceeded 75%. So this supports "surface re-activation helps osseointegration in animals" more strongly for UV than for plasma specifically.

## Key Contributions

- Pre-clinical pooling of surface bio-activation (UV/plasma) on BIC.
- Frames UV and plasma as parallel chairside re-activation methods.

## Methodology

- PubMed/Medline, Scopus, Cochrane to May 2020. 518 identified → 10 included.
- Meta-analysis on 4 UV-rabbit studies; DerSimonian–Laird random effects. Outcome BIC.

## Results

- Qualitative: both UV and non-thermal argon plasma may improve in vivo osseointegration.
- MA (4 UV-rabbit studies): BIC significantly increased in bio-activated groups (homogeneous follow-up).
- High heterogeneity (I²>75%) in all models.

**Boundary (living-document):** animal-only; quantitative effect rests on UV (not plasma); no plasma-specific pooled estimate; I²>75%. No human/clinical outcomes.
