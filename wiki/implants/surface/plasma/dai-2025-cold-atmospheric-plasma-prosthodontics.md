## One-line Summary

Review (J Dent 2025; 4-database search, 154 publications) of CAP in prosthodontics across three applications: dentin bonding (resin monomer penetration/polymerization), antibacterial action (ROS/RNS), and implant surface modification (osseointegration, lower infection, soft-tissue healing).

## Summary

The most recent and broadest umbrella over the cluster, extending CAP beyond implants to dentin bonding and general antibacterial use. Its implant-relevant claim — CAP improves surface properties, osseointegration, infection resistance, and soft-tissue healing — is the conceptual through-line connecting the cluster's osseointegration and decontamination arms.

## Key Contributions

- Tri-application (bonding/antibacterial/implant) conceptual map, 2025.
- Mechanistic framing (ROS/RNS, UV photons).

## Methodology

- Four-database search; two-reviewer screening; 154 publications (original research).

## Results

- Dentin bonding: ↑ resin monomer penetration/polymerization.
- Antibacterial: ROS/RNS inactivate bacteria/biofilm; lower drug-resistance likelihood.
- Implant surface: ↑ surface properties/osseointegration, ↓ infection, ↑ soft-tissue healing.

**Boundary (living-document):** narrative/scoping synthesis (no meta-analysis); clinical protocols unsettled.
