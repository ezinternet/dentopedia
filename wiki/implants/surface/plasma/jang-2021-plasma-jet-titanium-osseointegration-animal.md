## One-line Summary

In vivo dog study (6 mongrel dogs, 41 implants: 21 NTAPPJ, 20 control, maxilla+mandible, 4 or 8 wk): non-thermal atmospheric pressure plasma jet significantly increased bone volume and BIC at 4 weeks (p<0.05), but the advantage diminished by 8 weeks as controls caught up.

## 한줄요약

개 in vivo(mongrel 6마리, 임플란트 41개: NTAPPJ 21·대조 20, 상·하악, 4/8주): 비열 대기압 플라즈마 제트가 4주 골량·BIC 유의 증가(p<0.05), 8주엔 대조군이 따라잡아 차이 감소.

## Summary

A clean illustration of the cluster's recurring time pattern: plasma accelerates early bone response (4 wk) but the untreated control converges by 8 wk. The benefit is about *speed* of early healing/stability, not a higher final plateau.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates early-only (4 wk) BV/BIC advantage with 8-wk convergence.

## Methodology

- 41 implants (21 NTAPPJ, 20 control) in maxilla/mandible of 6 mongrel dogs; 4 or 8 wk. BV, BIC (ROI); Wilcoxon.

## Results

- 4 wk: NTAPPJ significantly higher BV and BIC (p<0.05).
- 8 wk: differences less significant (control caught up).

**Boundary (living-document):** transient (early-only) benefit; animal model; long-term predictability inferred.
