## One-line Summary

SR of in-vitro studies (55 included, quality 31–92%): CAP is antimicrobial against dental pathogens with reduction factors that cluster by pathogen, working gas, and treatment time; a time-dependent increase of effect is consistent, but heterogeneity precludes a recommended protocol.

## 한줄요약

in vitro SR(55편, 품질 31–92%): CAP는 구강 병원균에 항균적이며 감소폭이 병원균·작동가스·처리시간별로 군집, 시간의존적 증가가 일관됨. 이질성으로 프로토콜 권고는 불가.

## Summary

The broad antimicrobial backbone of the cluster. CAP reliably kills oral pathogens in vitro, and the effect grows with treatment time — but the magnitude depends heavily on pathogen, gas, and exposure, so no standard dose can yet be recommended. Most evidence is short of mature multi-species biofilm, which is what matters clinically.

## Key Contributions

- 55-study synthesis + a standardized in-vitro assessment tool.
- Pinpoints the three determinants of effect (pathogen, gas, time).

## Methodology

- PubMed, Embase, Scopus. Device parameters/setups/cultivation extracted; quality-scored.

## Results

- 55 studies (quality 31–92%).
- Reduction factors varied strongly; clustered by pathogen/gas/time.
- Consistent time-dependent increase of antimicrobial effect.

**Boundary (living-document):** in-vitro only; high heterogeneity → no protocol/device recommendation; multi-species biofilm models needed before RCTs.
