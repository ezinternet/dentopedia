## Summary

Ballo et al. (2011) provide a comprehensive narrative review of dental implant surface science, spanning from first-generation machined surfaces to emerging nanostructured and bioactive coatings. The chapter synthesizes in vitro, animal (rat tibia), and clinical evidence to explain how surface topography and chemistry drive osseointegration.

**Surface roughness classification** (Albrektsson & Wennerberg 2004):
- Smooth: Sa < 0.5 µm
- Minimally rough: Sa 0.5–1.0 µm (machined)
- Moderately rough: Sa 1.0–2.0 µm (SLA, anodized) — optimal for bone response
- Rough: Sa > 2.0 µm

**Surface modification progression**:
1. Machined — first generation; 56–85% BIC long-term clinically; high fibrinous acute response
2. Sandblasted — Sa 0.5–2.0 µm; optimal bone response at Sa ≈1.5 µm
3. Acid-etched — creates micropits; combined SLA shows superior response
4. SLA (SLActive hydrophilic variant) — most clinically documented moderately rough surface
5. Anodically oxidized (e.g., TiUnite) — thick TiO₂ layer; dramatically different inflammatory profile
6. Laser-modified — precise micro/nano control; limited clinical evidence (2011)
7. HA / fluoride-HA / strontium-HA coatings — enhanced early BIC; delamination risk
8. Bioactive glass (BAG) — equivalent to HA at 1 yr in pilot RCT
9. Nanostructured surfaces (DCD, TiO₂ nanotubes) — accelerate osteoblast adhesion; no RCT evidence at time of writing

## Key Contributions

**Molecular biology of osseointegration**: Using rat tibia qPCR + immunohistochemistry, the group showed that anodically oxidized surfaces vs machined surfaces differ fundamentally in early inflammatory response:
- Machined implants: higher TNF-α (3 h, 1 day), IL-1β (1–6 days), MCP-1 — more fibrinous material, more pro-inflammatory
- Oxidized implants: 11-fold higher CXCR4 expression at 12 h — recruits mesenchymal stem cells (MSCs) via SDF-1α axis → faster bone formation

**Surface chemistry confounding**: Altering roughness changes surface chemistry — so roughness vs composition effects on bone response are not separable with current methods (as of 2011).

**Bioactive coating hierarchy**: HA > uncoated titanium for early BIC, but BAG ≥ HA at ≥4 weeks in animal models; clinical evidence still preliminary.

## Methodology

- Narrative review of literature through ~2010
- In vivo data primarily from rat tibia model (qPCR, SEM, immunohistochemistry, removal torque)
- Clinical data from long-term cohort studies of machined and SLA surfaces
- No formal systematic search or PRISMA methodology

## Results

Key quantitative benchmarks:
- Machined surface Sa: 0.3–1.0 µm; TiO₂ oxide layer 2–10 nm thick (amorphous)
- Optimal sandblasted roughness: Sa ≈1.5 µm (removal torque + BIC peak)
- SLA clinical evidence: well-documented multicenter RCTs (Cochran 1998 onward)
- Oxidized surface CXCR4 upregulation: 11× vs machined at 12 h post-implantation
- BAG vs HA: equivalent success at 1 yr in 1 clinical pilot study (Mistry 2011)
- Nanostructured surfaces: improved in vitro osteoblast adhesion, enhanced in vivo bone contact in animal models — no RCT evidence as of 2011
