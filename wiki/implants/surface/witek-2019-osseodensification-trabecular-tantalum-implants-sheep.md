## Summary
Witek et al. paired osseodensification drilling with a porous trabecular tantalum (TM) implant body in a sheep iliac-crest low-density-bone model. Three osteotomy modes — Regular, OD clockwise (cutting), OD counter-clockwise (densifying) — were prepared in each ilium of three female sheep at 1100 rpm under saline irrigation. After 3 weeks of healing, ilia were harvested for non-decalcified histology and ImageJ-based histomorphometry.

The chief positive finding is that OD-CCW raised BAFO% vs Regular. The proposed mechanism: counter-clockwise densifying packs bone chips into the TM implant's porous network, where they serve as nucleation sites for new bone. Other histomorphometric outcomes (BIC%, OD-CW vs Regular) did not reach significance. The authors interpret this as showing that the porous TM architecture itself did not strongly drive osseointegration — the autograft-into-porosity effect of OD-CCW did.

This is a small, short-duration, single-laboratory study (NYU Coelho group), and its findings should be cited as proof-of-concept for OD × porous-implant interaction rather than as clinical evidence.

## Key Contributions
- First in vivo paper combining OD drilling with a porous-body trabecular tantalum implant.
- Identifies a mechanistic interaction: OD-CCW bone chips → trapped in TM porous network → BAFO gain.
- Demonstrates that, in this model, the porous TM body alone did not outperform conventional drilling — the OD protocol was the driver.
- Standardized protocol (1100 rpm, saline) lets the study isolate the drilling-mode variable.

## Methodology
Animal model: 3 female sheep, both ilia. Within each ilium, 3 osteotomies (Regular, OD-CW, OD-CCW) at 1100 rpm with saline irrigation. Implant: trabecular metal Zimmer 3.7 × 10 mm. Healing: 3 weeks, then euthanasia, ilium harvest, non-decalcified histology, ImageJ histomorphometry. Outcomes: BIC% and BAFO%.

## Results
- BAFO%: OD-CCW > Regular, statistically significant — attributed to bone chips trapped in TM porous network.
- Other pairwise comparisons (BIC% across modes, OD-CW vs Regular for BAFO): no statistically significant differences reported.
- All implants showed successful bone formation in peri-implant environment and within the TM porous spaces.
- Exact BIC%, BAFO% values, SDs, p-values: Numerical detail not extractable from available text — see PDF tables.

## Limitations
- n = 3 sheep, 18 implants — power is limited.
- 3-week healing — early osseointegration only, no loading or long-term remodeling data.
- Findings on the porous TM body do not generalize to standard solid-screw implants without further study.
- Single-laboratory (NYU Coelho group) with Zimmer-supplied hardware — independence not external.
- BIC% non-significance may reflect underpowering rather than a true null.

## Living Document Notes
- Cite as **mechanistic support** for the "OD packs bone chips into the implant surface region" hypothesis when discussing porous-bodied implants.
- Do not generalize to standard turned/SLA solid-body implants — the autograft-into-porosity effect is specific to porous architecture.
- The non-significant BIC%/OD-CW results should be reported as such, not waved away with "OD is generally better."
