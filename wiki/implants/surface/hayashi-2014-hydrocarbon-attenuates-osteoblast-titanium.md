## One-line Summary
In vitro study (J Dent Res 2014, MC3T3-E1 osteoblasts): first direct dose-response evidence that hydrocarbon deposition on titanium concentration-dependently suppresses osteoblast attachment, spreading, ALP activity, and calcium mineralization (>40% reduction at C/Ti=1.0) — mechanistically explaining the clinical Bone-to-Implant Contact (BIC) plateau of 45–65% on commercial implants (17.9–76.5% surface carbon) and providing the rationale for UV-PF.

## 한줄요약
인비트로(J Dent Res 2014): 탄소/티타늄 (C/Ti) 비율이 높을수록 골아세포 (Osteoblast) 부착·증식·분화 농도 의존적 감소; C/Ti=1.0에서 알칼리인산분해효소 (ALP)·칼슘 무기화 >40% 감소 — 탄화수소 오염이 골-임플란트 접촉률 (BIC) 정체의 직접 원인임을 최초 실증.

## Summary
In vitro study (MC3T3-E1 cells) providing the first direct dose-response evidence that hydrocarbon deposition on titanium attenuates osteoblast activity. C/Ti ratios (0.3, 0.7, 1.0) were experimentally controlled on mirror-polished cpTi. Cell attachment and spreading were concentration-dependently suppressed. At C/Ti=1.0, ALP activity and calcium mineralization were reduced by >40%. Contextualises the BIC plateau (45-65%) observed clinically as a direct consequence of unavoidable hydrocarbon accumulation on commercial implant surfaces (17.9-76.5% C detected across 34 commercial implants).

## Key Contributions
- First study directly testing hydrocarbon dose-response on osteoblast activity
- Mechanistic explanation for BIC plateau at 45-65% — long-standing mystery resolved
- Commercial implants carry 17.9-76.5% surface carbon → all have compromised osteoconductivity
- Rational basis for UV-PF (removes hydrocarbons) as countermeasure
