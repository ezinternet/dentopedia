## One-line Summary
In vitro study (Biomaterials 2015): UV photofunctionalization (UV-PF) renders machined titanium superhydrophilic and significantly reduces oral polymicrobial bacterial attachment and biofilm formation under saliva/blood conditions via an anti-adhesion (not bactericidal) mechanism — bacterial viability is unchanged, function persists after liquid storage, but microbial communities converge as biofilm matures (delay, not permanent prevention).

## Summary
In vitro study demonstrating that UV photofunctionalization (UV-PF) of machined titanium creates superhydrophilic surfaces that significantly reduce oral polymicrobial bacterial attachment and biofilm formation in the presence of salivary and blood components. The mechanism is anti-adhesion (not bactericidal) — bacterial viability is unchanged. Liquid storage (PBS) post-UV maintains anti-adhesion function. Microbial community profiles differ initially but converge as biofilm matures, suggesting UV-PF delays rather than permanently prevents biofilm.

## Key Contributions
- First study on UV-PF effect on polymicrobial oral biofilm with physiologically relevant conditions (saliva + blood)
- Confirms anti-adhesion (not bactericidal) mechanism
- Liquid-storage maintenance of function: clinically relevant for pre-soaked storage strategies
- Community convergence limitation: UV-PF does not provide permanent peri-implantitis protection
