## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 7 RCTs (722 implants) shows sandblasted implants have ~80% lower failure rate at 1 year and ~74% at 5 years vs machined implants, with no significant difference in marginal bone loss.

## Summary

Czumbel et al. (2019) conducted the first meta-analysis restricted exclusively to randomized controlled trials (RCTs) directly comparing sandblasted vs machined (turned) dental implants in healthy, systemically uncompromised patients. Seven RCTs met strict inclusion criteria (362 sandblasted, 360 machined implants). Sandblasting produced a dramatic reduction in implant failure risk at both 1-year (RR=0.20; 95% CI 0.06–0.67) and 5-year (RR=0.26; 95% CI 0.09–0.74) follow-up, with zero heterogeneity (I²=0%) for both outcomes. However, marginal bone level (MBL) change was not significantly different between surface types at 1 year (WMD: −0.10 mm) or 5 years (WMD: −0.01 mm). The authors concluded that sandblasting is superior to machined surfaces for preventing implant failure but does not confer measurable advantage in preserving crestal bone.

## Key Contributions

- Quantified ~80% reduction in 1-year implant failure risk for sandblasted vs machined surfaces (RR=0.20)
- Demonstrated survival benefit persists at 5 years with ~74% risk reduction (RR=0.26)
- Low heterogeneity (I²=0%) for failure outcomes — robust and consistent signal
- Established that MBL preservation is equivalent between the two surface types
- First SR+MA limited to RCTs on this topic — previous reviews mixed RCT and non-RCT data

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review + meta-analysis; PRISMA compliant; PROSPERO CRD42018084190
- **Databases**: Cochrane Central Library, Embase, PubMed (to August 2018)
- **Inclusion**: RCTs only; healthy patients; endosteal implants; sandblasted vs machined (no surface modification) comparison; similar implant designs
- **Exclusion**: Non-RCTs, bone augmentation, growth factors, neck-only modification, systemic disease
- **Records screened**: 130 (post-deduplication) → 7 included
- **Statistics**: Pooled RR (dichotomous, random effects DerSimonian-Laird), WMD (continuous); I² and chi² for heterogeneity
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane Handbook 8-domain assessment per study

## Results

### Implant Failure Rate

| Timepoint | Sandblasted | Machined | RR | 95% CI | I² |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 year | — | — | 0.20 | 0.06–0.67 | 0.0% |
| 5 years | — | — | 0.26 | 0.09–0.74 | 0.0% |

Sandblasted implants had significantly lower failure rates at both 1 and 5 years. Both outcomes showed no heterogeneity across included RCTs.

### Marginal Bone Level Change

| Timepoint | WMD (mm) | 95% CI | I² | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 year | −0.10 | −0.20, 0.01 | 0.0% | NS |
| 5 years | −0.01 | −0.12, 0.09 | 26.2% | NS |

No significant difference in marginal bone loss between surface types. Slight heterogeneity at 5-year MBL not investigated in subgroup analyses.

### Context
- Surface roughness (Sa) of sandblasted implants: 1.2–2.2 µm (moderately rough to rough)
- Machined surface Sa: 0.5–1 µm (minimally rough)
- Sandblasting particles: Al₂O₃, TiO₂, or SiO₂; particle size 25–250 µm
