## Summary
Comprehensive narrative review of established and experimental dental implant surface modifications and their impact on osseointegration. Covers the biological cascade from initial blood contact to mature bone formation, and how surface properties (topography, hydrophilicity, chemistry) modulate each phase. Identifies SLA and hydrophilic SLActive as clinical gold standards, while presenting emerging technologies for compromised bone scenarios.

## Key Contributions
- Establishes hierarchy of surface modification approaches from clinical gold standards to experimental
- Quantifies hydrophilicity advantage: SLActive → faster early BIC and clinical osseointegration vs SLA
- Addresses challenging patient populations (diabetes, osteoporosis, bisphosphonates, radiotherapy)
- Reviews growth factor coatings (BMP-2, BMP-7, PDGF) for future compromised bone applications

## Surface Modification Taxonomy
| Type | Mechanism | Clinical status |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| SLA | Sandblast + acid etch; Ra 1–2µm | Gold standard |
| SLActive | SLA + N₂ storage; super-hydrophilic | Clinical use; faster early osseointegration |
| DCD | Bioresorbable CaP particles | Clinical use (Biomet 3i) |
| Laser ablation | Controlled micro/nanotopography | Research/emerging |
| Protein coatings | Fibronectin, RGD | Experimental |
| Growth factors | BMP-2, rhBMP-7, PDGF | Experimental |
