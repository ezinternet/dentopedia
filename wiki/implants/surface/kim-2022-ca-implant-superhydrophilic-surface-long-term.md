## One-line Summary
Retrospective study (258 Osstem CA super-hydrophilic calcium-modified SLA implants, 120 patients, mean 62-month follow-up) reporting 97.3% survival, 94.2% success, and mean MBL of 0.074mm — excellent long-term outcomes consistent with the hydrophilic surface advantage.

## 한줄요약
후향적 연구(258개 Osstem CA 임플란트, 120명, 평균 62개월): 생존율 97.3%, 성공률 94.2%, 평균 MBL 0.074mm — 초친수성 칼슘 처리 표면의 우수한 장기 예후

## Summary
Retrospective study evaluating long-term outcomes of Osstem CA (calcium-modified, super-hydrophilic SLA) implants. Over an average 62-month follow-up: **97.3% survival, 94.2% success, MBL 0.074 mm** — excellent outcomes consistent with the hydrophilic surface advantage.

## Results

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Implants / Patients | 258 / 120 |
| Mean follow-up | 62 months (≥60 months minimum) |
| Survival rate | **97.3%** |
| Success rate (Albrektsson) | **94.2%** |
| Mean final MBL | **0.074 mm** |
| Mean diameter | 4.63 mm (3.5–5.5) |
| Mean length | 9.94 mm (7.0–13.0) |

## CA Surface Technology
- SLA base (sandblasted + acid-etched) → calcium ion modification
- Contact angle ≈ 0° (super-hydrophilic) → enhanced protein adsorption → faster BIC
- Compared to standard SA surface: accelerated osseointegration expected

## Clinical Takeaway
- CA surface performs at or above standard implant long-term benchmarks (97.3% survival)
- MBL of 0.074 mm over 5+ years: minimal crestal bone loss — favorable for long-term soft tissue stability
- No contraindications identified for bone graft cases (graft use allowed in study)
