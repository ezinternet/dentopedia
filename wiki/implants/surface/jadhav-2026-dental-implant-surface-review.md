## Summary
This review updates the implant surface field, organizing failure causes against the surface-modification strategies that address them. It argues that surface modification is critical for long-term success, with bio-functionalized surfaces improving osseointegration and reducing peri-implantitis in both healthy and compromised patients. It maps the frontier — stimuli-responsive coatings, 'omics-informed design, and AI translation — while cautioning that immune response and in vivo validation are routinely under-studied and that most strategies still need clinical confirmation. As a narrative review it sets context rather than ranking interventions.

## Key Contributions
- Structured map of failure causes ↔ surface strategies.
- Emphasis on bio-functionalized surfaces and emerging responsive/'omics/AI directions.

## Methodology
Narrative review of last-decade literature on implant materials, failure, and surface modification.

## Results
- Surface modification deemed critical; targets antibacterial activity, texture, geometry.
- Bio-functionalized surfaces favored; immune/in vivo factors under-addressed.
