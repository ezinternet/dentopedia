## One-line Summary

Split-mouth double-blind RCT (BMC Oral Health 2025; 21 patients, 42 implants, bilateral posterior mandible): UV-photofunctionalized (test) sites showed a statistically significant pre-to-post-placement rise in peri-implant trabecular fractal dimension (FD 1.075 → 1.103, p=0.044) while non-PF controls did not (1.113 → 1.118, p=0.794); 100% early survival in both arms, ISQ not measured — first human study to evaluate PF effect on peri-implant bone via fractal analysis.

## 한줄요약

분할입(split-mouth) 이중맹검 RCT (BMC Oral Health 2025, 환자 21명·임플란트 42개, 양측 하악 구치부): UV 광기능화 (Photofunctionalization, PF) 처리군은 식립 전→후 임플란트 주위 해면골 프랙탈 차원 (Fractal Dimension, FD)이 유의하게 상승(1.075→1.103, p=0.044), 비처리 대조군은 무변화(1.113→1.118, p=0.794); 양군 조기 생존율 100%, 임플란트 안정성 지수 (ISQ)는 미측정 — 프랙탈 분석으로 PF의 임플란트 주위 골 영향을 평가한 최초의 인체 연구.

## Summary

This randomized, split-mouth, double-blind clinical trial tested whether UV photofunctionalization (PF) of dental implants improves peri-implant trabecular bone quality, measured non-invasively by **fractal dimension (FD)** on panoramic radiographs. Twenty-one patients with bilateral edentulous posterior mandibles each received a PF-treated (test) implant on one side and a non-treated (control) implant on the contralateral side, so each patient served as their own control. FD was computed (ImageJ, White & Rudolph box-counting) in a standardized 25 × 25-pixel trabecular ROI apical to the implant, on a pre-implant radiograph and again ~12–16 weeks after placement.

PF sites began with significantly lower FD than control sites (1.075 vs 1.113, p=0.021) but showed a **statistically significant within-patient FD increase** after placement (→1.103, p=0.044), whereas control sites did not change significantly (→1.118, p=0.794). By the post-placement timepoint the inter-group FD difference was no longer significant (p=0.428) — the PF sites had effectively caught up. All 42 implants survived (100% early survival, no bone loss). The authors position this as the first human study to use fractal analysis to demonstrate a positive PF effect on peri-implant bone, independent of clinical stability measurement.

## Key Contributions

- **First human fractal-analysis evidence** that UV-PF enhances peri-implant trabecular bone complexity (prior human PF evidence was ISQ- or CBCT-based; mechanistic evidence was cellular/animal).
- Supplies a **non-invasive, ISQ-independent imaging surrogate** (panoramic FD) for osseointegration / bone-quality gain — potentially useful for early-loading or compromised-case decisions.
- **Split-mouth, double-blind RCT** design removes patient-level confounders (systemic bone-metabolic factors, age) — strongest internal validity tier for this question to date.
- Within-patient FD rose significantly only at PF sites; controls did not — a clean differential signal.

## Methodology

- **Design**: randomized, split-mouth, double-blind RCT (CONSORT-adherent, ClinicalTrials.gov registered); Ankara University Faculty of Dentistry.
- **Sample**: 21 patients (12 F, 9 M; mean age 50.4 ± 9.2 y), 42 implants (21 test PF, 21 control). G*Power: paired t-test, d=0.5, α=0.05, power=0.80 → 21/group.
- **Implants**: Gravis (STARK, Turkey), packaged ≥6 months; standard dimensions only; single operator (H.F.); mandibular premolar/molar.
- **PF**: UV irradiation **10 s** (Dentis Co. LTD, South Korea), removed from packaging first, inserted immediately.
- **Randomization/blinding**: coin flip; allocation by 2 independent investigators (F.K., Z.D.G.); analyst (E.P.B.) and implanting clinician both blinded.
- **Imaging**: Planmeca ProMax panoramic (64 kV, 10 mA, 10 s), TIFF 300 dpi; pre-implant and ~12–16-week follow-up.
- **ROI/FDA**: 25 × 25 px trabecular ROI apical to implant (MicroDicom landmark-localized); ImageJ 1.49x White & Rudolph box-counting (crop → 400% upsample → Gaussian σ=35 → subtract → +128/threshold 128 → binarize → erode/dilate → invert → skeletonize; box sizes 2–64 px).
- **Statistics**: non-normal (Shapiro-Wilk) → Wilcoxon (intra-group), Mann-Whitney U (inter-group), Kruskal-Wallis (group×gender); Spearman intra-observer (20 images, 1 month apart).
- **ISQ / mechanical stability NOT measured** (acknowledged limitation).

## Results

| Side | Pre (FD ± SD, median) | Post (FD ± SD, median) | Δ p (Wilcoxon) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Control (non-PF) | 1.113 ± 0.064 (1.102) | 1.118 ± 0.056 (1.105) | **0.794 (NS)** |
| Test (PF) | 1.075 ± 0.067 (1.053) | 1.103 ± 0.047 (1.092) | **0.044 \*** |

- **Inter-group (Mann-Whitney):** pre 1.113 vs 1.075, **p=0.021** (control higher); post 1.118 vs 1.103, **p=0.428 (NS)** — gap closed after PF.
- **Region-specific:** significant FD rise in the #45 PF site (p=0.046).
- **Gender:** no significant subgroup differences (all p>0.05).
- **Survival:** **100% in both arms**; no bone/implant loss; healing abutments on all by month 3–4. No MBL or ISQ reported.
- **Reliability:** intra-observer agreement satisfactory.

**Interpretation:** PF sites started biologically "behind" (lower FD) yet caught up via a significant post-placement FD gain absent in controls — read as PF-accelerated trabecular organization / osseointegration.

**Caveats:** pilot-scale single-center (n=42), 2D panoramic rather than CBCT, and no clinical stability anchor (no ISQ) limit functional interpretation.
