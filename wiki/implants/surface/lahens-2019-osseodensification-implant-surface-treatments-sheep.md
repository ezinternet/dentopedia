## 한줄요약

## Summary
This sheep ilium study tested whether osseodensification (OD) drilling alters primary stability and early osseointegration of endosteal implants in low-density trabecular bone, and whether OD modifies the historical surface-treatment advantage of acid-etched over as-machined implants. Twelve sheep received 72 implants (36 machined, 36 acid-etched) split across three drilling protocols: subtractive regular drilling (R), OD clockwise (CW), and OD counterclockwise (CCW). Endpoints were insertion torque, BIC, and bone area fraction occupancy (BAFO) at 3 and 12 weeks.

Insertion torque was significantly higher with CW and CCW drilling than with R drilling (p < 0.001). BIC was higher for CW (p = 0.024) and CCW (p = 0.006) versus R. At 3 weeks the histology showed resorbed bone and osteoblast precursors; at 12 weeks new bone extended into the trabecular region in all groups. Notably, for CCW OD there was no statistically detectable difference in BIC or BAFO between machined and acid-etched implants at either time point — suggesting OD drilling can partially compensate for a less bioactive surface in low-density bone. Authors conclude OD in soft bone improves primary stability without impairing osseointegration regardless of surface.

## Key Contributions
- Animal evidence that OD raises insertion torque and BIC versus standard subtractive drilling in low-density (ilium) bone.
- Demonstrates surface-treatment effect (machined vs acid-etched) can be neutralized at the BIC/BAFO level when CCW OD is used.
- Confirms no impairment of secondary osseointegration at 12 weeks despite higher initial compression.
- Provides a histologic timepoint (3 wk) showing OD does not arrest osteoclast/precursor activity.

## Methodology
Twelve adult sheep had six implants per animal placed bilaterally in the ilium. Each animal received three of each surface (machined, acid-etched) distributed across the three drilling protocols (R: 2.0 → 3.2 → 3.8 mm twist drills; CW OD: Densah 2.0 → 2.8 → 3.8 mm clockwise; CCW OD: same Densah sequence reversed). Implants were 3.75 mm-equivalent endosteal. Outcomes: insertion torque (Ncm) at placement, histomorphometric BIC and BAFO at 3 and 12 weeks. Statistical comparison reported as p-values for the contrast of OD vs R drilling and machined vs acid-etched within each drilling protocol.

## Results
- Insertion torque: CW and CCW > R, p < 0.001.
- BIC vs R: CW p = 0.024; CCW p = 0.006 (both higher with OD).
- Machined vs acid-etched within CCW OD: no statistically significant difference in BIC or BAFO at 3 or 12 weeks.
- 3-week histology: resorbed bone fragments with preosteoblasts present across groups (no OD-specific necrosis flag).
- 12-week histology: new bone formation extending into trabecular bone in all groups.

## Limitations
- Sheep ilium is not a 1:1 analogue of human posterior maxilla; load conditions absent.
- Exact mean BIC/BAFO percentages and SDs not extractable from the abstract page; see PDF for numerical tables.
- Short follow-up (12 weeks); no MBL or long-term stability data.
