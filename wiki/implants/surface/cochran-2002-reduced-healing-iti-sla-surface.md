## One-line Summary

Prospective multicenter trial (6 centers, 4 countries; 326 SLA ITI implants, 110 patients) showing 4.1 mm SLA solid-screw implants restored at ~6 weeks (class IV bone at ~12 weeks) achieved 99.3% abutment-placement success (avg healing 49 days) and 99.1% life-table implant success at 1 and 2 years.

## 한줄요약

전향적 다기관 임상연구(6개 센터·4개국, 110명·326개 SLA ITI 임플란트): 4.1 mm SLA 솔리드 스크류 임플란트를 약 6주(class IV 골질은 약 12주)에 보철 수복 시 보철 연결 성공률 99.3%(평균 치유 49일), 1·2년 생명표 성공률 99.1%로 짧아진 치유기간의 높은 예측성을 입증.

## Summary

ITI dental implants were available with two bone-anchoring surfaces — titanium plasma-sprayed (TPS) and the then-newly introduced sandblasted and acid-etched (SLA) surface. Preclinical cell-culture and animal data showed the SLA surface stimulates 골세포 분화 (bone cell differentiation) and protein production, produces large 골-임플란트 접촉 (Bone-to-Implant Contact, BIC), and yields high removal-torque values. Building on this, the authors ran a prospective multicenter human trial to test whether 4.1 mm SLA ITI solid-screw implants could be **predictably and safely restored as early as ~6 weeks** after placement — a markedly **reduced healing time (RHT)** relative to longer conventional intervals.

The reduced ~6-week protocol was restricted to (a) healthy patients with sufficient bone volume and (b) good bone quality (Lekholm–Zarb classes I–III). Sites with poor bone quality (class IV) were not restored until ~12 weeks. Across 6 centers in 4 countries with planned 5-year follow-up, the SLA surface supported restoration at an average of 49 days with >99% success at 2 years — the landmark early evidence base for SLA accelerated loading.

## Key Contributions

- First prospective human-trial evidence that the **SLA surface permits a reduced ~6-week healing time** before restoration.
- Defined a **bone-quality–stratified loading protocol**: classes I–III → ~6 weeks; class IV → ~12 weeks.
- Set the historical efficacy benchmark (>99% success at 2 years) for SLA early/accelerated loading.

## Methodology

- **Design**: prospective, ongoing multicenter clinical trial — 6 centers, 4 countries, planned 5-year follow-up.
- **Implants**: 4.1 mm diameter ITI® solid-screw implants with an SLA endosseous surface (one-stage, non-submerged).
- **Reduced-healing inclusion**: healthy + sufficient bone volume + bone quality classes I–III → restored ~6 weeks; class IV → restored ~12 weeks.
- **Primary outcome**: successful abutment placement under **35 Ncm tightening force with no countertorque**, no pain, no implant rotation.
- **Secondary outcome (implant success)**: no mobility, no persistent pain/infection, no peri-implant radiolucency.
- **Analysis**: life-table (Cutler–Ederer) actuarial analysis.

## Results

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Centers / countries | 6 / 4 |
| Patients (1-yr recall) / implants | 110 / 326 |
| Patients (2-yr recall) / implants | 47 / 138 |
| Implants lost before abutment connection | 3 |
| Implants restored after shortened healing | 307 |
| Average healing time | 49 days (~7 weeks) |
| Abutment-placement success | 99.3% |
| Life-table implant success (1 yr, n=329) | 99.1% |
| Life-table implant success (2 yr, n=138) | 99.1% |
| Implant losses in 24 mo after restoration (n=138) | 0 |

**Conclusion**: under defined conditions, solid-screw ITI implants with an SLA endosseous surface can be restored after ~6 weeks of healing with high predictability — abutment placement at 35 Ncm without countertorque — and subsequent implant success >99% at 2 years post-restoration.

**Caveats**: interim/early results of an ongoing trial; single implant type/diameter (4.1 mm); restricted to healthy patients with class I–III bone for the 6-week arm; surrogate torque-based primary endpoint rather than long-term marginal bone level.
