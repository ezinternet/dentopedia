## 한줄요약
후향적 연구(96개 Osstem SA 임플란트, 45명, 8년 이상): 생존율 94.8%; 합병증 발생 및 보철 완료 1년 내 MBL >1mm 모두 유의한 실패 예측인자

## Summary
Retrospective long-term study of Osstem SA (SLA) implants. **94.8% survival** over ≥8 years. Key finding: early complications and MBL >1 mm within the first year after prosthetic completion are both significant independent failure predictors.

## Results

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Implants / Patients | 96 / 45 |
| Follow-up | ≥8 years (2008–2010 placement) |
| Survival rate | **94.8%** (5 failures) |
| Total complications | 14 (6 early, 8 delayed) |
| All 5 failures | Occurred in early complication group |

## Failure Predictors

| Factor | Significance |
|---|---|
| Occurrence of complications | **p<0.05** — significant |
| MBL >1mm within 1 year post-prosthesis | **p<0.05** — significant |

→ **MBL >1mm in year 1 = early warning sign** for long-term failure — intensify monitoring

## Clinical Takeaway
- SA surface standard benchmark: 94.8% long-term survival (slightly lower than CA/hydrophilic surfaces)
- Monitor MBL at 1-year recall: if >1mm → escalate care
- Early complications (within healing period) predict most failures — address them aggressively
