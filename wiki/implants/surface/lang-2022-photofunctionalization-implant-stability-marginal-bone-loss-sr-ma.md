## One-line Summary
SR+MA (7 clinical studies, 5 meta-analyzed; 430 patients / 734 implants): UV photofunctionalization significantly raised the osseointegration speed index (OSI; MD = 2.13, p = 0.007) and 2-month implant stability (MD = 3.48, p = 0.04), but pooled effects on stability at other timepoints and on marginal bone loss were non-significant amid high heterogeneity and high risk of bias.

## 한줄요약
SR+MA (임상연구 7편, 메타분석 5편; 환자 430명 / 임플란트 734개): UV 광기능화는 골유착 속도지수(OSI, MD=2.13, p=0.007)와 2개월 임플란트 안정성(MD=3.48, p=0.04)을 유의하게 높였으나, 다른 시점 안정성과 변연골소실은 비유의 — 높은 이질성·비뚤림 위험이 한계.

## Summary
This is the first evidence-based synthesis of the **clinical** effects of UV photofunctionalization on dental implants (prior reviews were animal-based). From 1788 records, 7 clinical studies (5 RCTs, 1 CCT, 1 case-control; 430 patients / 734 implants across Japan, Lithuania, Pakistan, Korea, India) were qualitatively reviewed and 5 meta-analyzed. The strongest, most consistent signal is on the **rate** of establishing stability: the osseointegration speed index (OSI) was significantly higher with photofunctionalization. Absolute implant stability reached significance only at the 2-month timepoint, and marginal bone loss (MBL) showed no significant pooled difference (though point estimates favored photofunctionalization). High heterogeneity (I² up to 99%), few pooled studies, and high/serious risk of bias temper all conclusions. Qualitatively, benefit is greatest in poor-quality bone and complex/staged cases.

## Key Contributions
- First clinical (non-animal) SR+MA isolating UV photofunctionalization on dental implant stability and MBL.
- Separates and pools two endpoints: ISQ-based stability and MBL (mesial/distal, 2 and 6 months).
- Pools the **OSI** rate metric = [(ISQ at loading) − (ISQ at placement)] / healing time (months), normalizing across different healing periods.
- Establishes that the rate of stability development (OSI) is the most reliable clinical benefit, not absolute stability or MBL.
- Maps heterogeneity drivers: bone quality, implant characteristics, and non-standardized UV parameters (250–382 nm, 10–20 min, varied devices).

## Methodology
- **PICOS**: adults ≥18 y with dental implants; photofunctionalization vs non-photofunctionalization; outcomes = implant stability + OSI (primary), MBL (secondary). PRISMA; PROSPERO CRD42022352186.
- **Designs included**: RCTs, CCTs, cohort/case-control. Excluded animal, in vitro, case reports/series, reviews, cross-sectional.
- **Search**: PubMed, Cochrane, EMBASE, Web of Science (4 Sep 2022) + hand-search; no language/date limit.
- **Selection**: 1788 → 292 duplicates removed → 1484 excluded on title/abstract → 12 included; +185 manual (3 full-text). After 15 full-texts, 8 excluded → **7 qualitative, 5 meta-analyzed**.
- **Risk of bias**: RoB 2.0 (RCTs), ROBINS-I (CCT/case-control).
- **Analysis**: RevMan 5.4; MD with 95% CI; p = 0.05 (two-tailed z); I²/Q-test; fixed-effects if I² < 50%, else random-effects.

## Results

### Risk of bias
- RCTs (n=5): 1 (20%) some concerns, 3 (60%) high risk (driver: selection of reported result).
- CCT and case-control: both **serious risk of bias** (ROBINS-I).

### Implant stability (ISQ, mean difference, test − control)
| Timepoint | Studies | MD | 95% CI | p | I² |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Immediately after placement | 2 | −5.73 | −18.47 to 7.01 | 0.38 | 86% |
| 2 weeks | 2 (52 vs 53 implants) | 0.24 | −0.68 to 1.16 | 0.62 | 0% |
| 4 weeks | 2 | 0.97 | −0.88 to 2.82 | 0.31 | 69% |
| **2 months** | 2 | **3.48** | −0.23 to 6.73 | **0.04** | 88% |
| 4 months | 2 | 6.22 | −4.19 to 16.63 | 0.24 | 99% |

→ Only the 2-month pool reached significance favoring photofunctionalization.

### OSI (rate of stability development)
- **MD = 2.13, 95% CI 0.57 to 3.68, p = 0.007, I² = 81%** (2 studies) — significant increase with photofunctionalization.

### Marginal bone loss (MBL, mean difference, test − control)
| Timepoint / site | MD | 95% CI | p | I² |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2 months — mesial | −0.05 | −0.40 to 0.29 | 0.76 | 89% |
| 2 months — distal | −0.22 | −0.64 to 0.19 | 0.29 | 89% |
| 6 months — mesial | −0.27 | −0.67 to 0.13 | 0.18 | 93% |
| 6 months — distal | −0.48 | −1.13 to 0.17 | 0.14 | 97% |

→ All NS; point estimates favor lower MBL with photofunctionalization but heterogeneity is high and only 2 of 4 MBL-reporting studies were poolable.

### Bottom line
Photofunctionalization significantly accelerates the *rate* of establishing implant stability (OSI) and improves absolute stability at 2 months; effects at other timepoints and on MBL are non-significant. Qualitatively, the benefit is largest in poor-quality bone and complex/staged cases.
