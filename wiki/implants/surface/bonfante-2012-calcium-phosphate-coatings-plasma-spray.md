## One-line Summary

Animal study (6 beagles, 36 plateau implants, 6 and 12 weeks) showing that plasma-sprayed amorphous CaP (ACP) and partially crystalline hydroxyapatite (PSHA) coatings produce equivalent BIC and BAFO despite differing significantly in roughness, crystalline phase content, and carbonate group presence.

## Summary

This animal study compared two plasma-spray calcium phosphate (CaP) coatings on Ti-6Al-4V plateau root form implants: plasma-sprayed hydroxyapatite (PSHA, partially crystalline) and plasma-sprayed amorphous calcium phosphate (ACP). Four characterization methods (SEM, interferometry, XRD/Rietveld, FT-IR) revealed significant physicochemical differences between the coatings. The ACP surface was markedly rougher (Sa 5.18 vs 3.22 μm; P<0.03) and contained carbonate groups (CO3) absent from PSHA, while PSHA held far greater crystalline HA content (~71% vs <1%). Despite these substantial physicochemical differences, histomorphometric evaluation at 6 and 12 weeks in a beagle dog radius epiphysis model found no significant differences in bone-to-implant contact (BIC; P>0.86) or bone area fraction occupancy (BAFO; P>0.75). Both surfaces supported lamellar bone formation in close contact with the implant and within healing chambers by 12 weeks, demonstrating equivalent osseoconductive potential.

## Key Contributions

- **Phase quantification:** Rietveld refinement confirmed PSHA is 71% HA with 23% calcium pyrophosphate; ACP is effectively amorphous (<1% HA), a rarely quantified distinction for clinically deployed coatings.
- **Carbonate chemistry:** FT-IR identified CO3 groups exclusively in ACP — biochemically relevant because carbonate substitution increases CaP solubility and resorption rate, potentially accelerating early bone bonding.
- **Roughness divergence:** ACP's higher Sa and Sq (both in the "rough" range per Albrektsson/Wennerberg) did not translate into histomorphometric advantage at 6 or 12 weeks.
- **Healing mode confirmation:** Both surfaces showed intramembranous-like healing within plateau healing chambers — woven bone at 6 weeks transitioning to lamellar bone at 12 weeks — consistent with the known plateau root form implant biology.
- **Equivalence with long-term retrieval data:** Mean BIC ~65% at 12 weeks matches human retrieval findings for PSHA-coated plateau implants at 2 months to 13 years in function.

## Methodology

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Model | 6 adult male beagles, ~1.5 years old |
| Implant type | Plateau root form, Ti-6Al-4V, 4.5 mm × 8.0 mm (Bicon LLC) |
| Surfaces | PSHA vs ACP (n=18 each; 6 per group for characterization, 12 per group for in vivo) |
| Sites | Radius epiphysis (right limb = 6 wk; left limb = 12 wk) |
| Characterization | SEM, interferometry (Sa/Sq), XRD + Rietveld (MAUD), FT-IR |
| Histology | Toluidine blue, undecalcified sections ~50 μm (Donath & Breuner technique) |
| Outcomes | BIC (50×–200×), BAFO (100×) |
| Statistics | BIC/BAFO: Friedman test; IFM/Rietveld: ANOVA and t-test (P<0.05) |

## Results

**Surface characterization:**
- ACP rougher: Sa 5.18 ± 0.95 μm vs PSHA 3.22 ± 0.27 μm (P<0.03); Sq 6.35 vs 3.88 μm (P<0.02)
- PSHA crystalline composition: HA 71.3%, calcium pyrophosphate 23.0%, calcium phosphate 5.6%, CaO 0.1%
- ACP crystalline composition: HA 0.83%, calcium pyrophosphate 7.5%, calcium phosphate 1.3%, CaO 0.3% (essentially amorphous)
- FT-IR: PO4 peaks shared by both; CO3 groups (850–800 cm⁻¹) present only in ACP

**In vivo histomorphometry:**
- 6 weeks: intimate bone contact and woven bone in healing chambers — both surfaces
- 12 weeks: lamellar bone replacing woven bone in healing chambers — both surfaces
- BIC: no difference between PSHA and ACP at 6 or 12 weeks (P>0.86)
- BAFO: no difference between PSHA and ACP at 6 or 12 weeks (P>0.75)

**Key inference:** Significantly higher crystalline HA content (PSHA) and significantly higher roughness with carbonate chemistry (ACP) were both insufficient to produce detectable histomorphometric differences at static time points measured. Potential biomechanical differences (torque-to-fracture) were not tested and remain an open question.
