## One-line Summary

Split-mouth RCT in controlled diabetic patients (J Oral Implantol 2025): 20-s pre-placement UV photofunctionalization (UVP) gave significantly higher 3-month ISQ gain (4.40 vs 2.60, P<.029), higher osseointegration index (OSI 1.42 vs 0.84, P=.032), and less 9-month distal crestal bone loss (0.35 vs 0.64 mm, P=.003) than non-UV implants.

## 한줄요약

조절된 당뇨 환자 대상 split-mouth RCT (J Oral Implantol 2025): 식립 전 20초 자외선 광기능화 (UV Photofunctionalization, UVP) 시 비처리군 대비 3개월 임플란트 안정성 지수 (Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ) 상승폭(4.40 vs 2.60, P<.029)·골유착지수 (Osseointegration Index, OSI 1.42 vs 0.84, P=.032)가 유의하게 높고 9개월 치조정 골소실 (0.35 vs 0.64 mm, P=.003)이 유의하게 적었다.

## Summary

**abstract-only — full text not retrieved.** Split-mouth randomized clinical trial comparing UV-photofunctionalized (UVP) versus non-UV-photofunctionalized (NUVP) titanium implants in controlled diabetic patients, a compromised-healing host group. UVP implants were activated in a UV device for 20 seconds immediately before placement. Implant stability (ISQ) and an osseointegration index (OSI) were measured at placement and 3 months; crestal bone change at 3 and 9 months. Across all three outcomes the UVP group performed significantly better: larger 3-month secondary-stability gain, higher OSI, and less distal crestal bone loss at 9 months. The trial extends UVP benefit from general/anatomically-complex cases into the metabolically compromised diabetic population.

## Key Contributions

- RCT-level evidence that UV photofunctionalization improves early stability and bone preservation specifically in **controlled diabetic** patients (compromised healing).
- Split-mouth design controls for patient-level confounders — each patient serves as own control.
- Demonstrates simultaneous benefit on secondary stability (ISQ), osseointegration speed (OSI), and crestal bone preservation.
- Complements Hirota 2020's finding that UVP overcomes anatomical compromise — here it also aids a *metabolic* compromise.

## Methodology

- **Design**: split-mouth RCT in controlled diabetics; sites randomly allocated UVP vs NUVP.
- **Sampling**: stratified random sampling (age, gender, subgroups proportionally represented).
- **Intervention**: UVP = 20-second UV activation immediately before placement; NUVP = conventional.
- **Outcomes**: ISQ + OSI at placement and 3 months; crestal bone change at 3 and 9 months.
- **Statistics**: descriptive statistics + paired t-tests (intragroup + intergroup).
- Exact sample size, implant system, and glycemic-control thresholds not available (abstract-only).

## Results

| Outcome | UVP | NUVP | P-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| ISQ change (baseline → 3 mo) | **4.40 ± 1.89** | 2.60 ± 1.17 | **<.029** |
| Osseointegration index (OSI) | **1.42 ± 0.62** | 0.84 ± 0.39 | **.032** |
| Distal crestal bone loss at 9 mo (mm) | **0.35 ± 0.08** | 0.64 ± 0.18 | **.003** |

UVP yielded greater secondary stability gain, higher OSI, and ~45% less distal crestal bone loss than NUVP in controlled diabetics. All three effects favor UVP; crestal bone preservation had the strongest significance (P=.003).
