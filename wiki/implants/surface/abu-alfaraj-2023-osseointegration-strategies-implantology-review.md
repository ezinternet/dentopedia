## Summary
Cross-disciplinary narrative review of current and emerging strategies for optimising osseointegration in dental implantology. Covers implant macrogeometry and microgeometry, established surface modifications (SLA, hydrophilic surfaces, nanotopography), systemic and local biological factors, and biologic adjuncts including PRF, PRP, BMP-2, PDGF. Also addresses digital workflow integration for precision implant placement.

## Key Topics
- Implant design: tapered > parallel-walled for primary stability in soft bone
- Surface modifications: SLA/SLActive gold standard; nano-texturing emerging
- Systemic factors: diabetes, smoking, osteoporosis as negative modulators
- Biologics: PRF membrane over implant site; BMP-2 for compromised bone
- Digital workflow: CBCT-guided surgery → predictable bone utilisation
