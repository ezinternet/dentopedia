## 한줄요약
서술적 고찰: 골유착 3차원 이론 — 재료(D1)·표면형상(D2)·시간/노화(D3)의 상호의존성; UV 광기능화로 티타늄 생물학적 노화 완전 역전.

## Summary
Narrative review introducing the 3D Theory of Osseointegration, which reframes implant integration as governed by three interdependent dimensions: material composition (Dimension 1), surface topography/roughness (Dimension 2), and time — specifically biological aging via hydrocarbon accumulation and loss of hydrophilicity (Dimension 3). UV photofunctionalization resolves Dimension 3, making topography (Dimension 2) the primary remaining frontier for innovation.

## Key Contributions
- Systematic framework unifying material, topography, and aging effects on osseointegration
- Explains why BIC never reaches 100% and why failure rates plateaued at ~8%
- UV photofunctionalization = reversible, predictable restoration of D3
- Advocates mechanism-driven (not empirical) topography engineering

## The 3D Framework
| Dimension | Factor | Status |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| D1 | Material composition (cp Ti, Ti alloys) | Mature — titanium established as gold standard |
| D2 | Surface topography/roughness | Underdeveloped — next frontier |
| D3 | Time/biological aging (hydrocarbons, hydrophilicity loss) | Solved by UV photofunctionalization |
