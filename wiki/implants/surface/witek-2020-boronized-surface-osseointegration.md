## One-line Summary

Animal study (5 sheep, ovine ilium, 3 and 6 weeks) showed boronized CP-titanium implants produce significantly lower BIC and declining BAFO over time vs. acid-etched controls, indicating boronization inhibits rather than enhances osseointegration.

## Summary

This in vivo animal study by Witek et al. (NYU College of Dentistry, Med Oral 2020) evaluated whether boronization — a solid-state diffusion thermo-chemical surface treatment that creates a TiB/TiB2 hard layer on titanium — improves osseointegration. Four implant groups were tested bilaterally in the ilia of 5 adult sheep at 3 and 6 weeks: boronized machined (BM), boronized acid-etched (BAA), control machined (CM), and control acid-etched (CAA).

The central finding was unexpected and contrary to in vitro predictions: both boronized groups showed declining BIC and BAFO from 3 to 6 weeks, while both control groups showed significant increases. At 6 weeks, CAA controls demonstrated the highest osseointegration, while BAA implants showed the sharpest decline in BIC (21.73% at 3 wk → 5.93% at 6 wk). Non-decalcified histology confirmed the absence of normal intramembranous-like bone remodeling in boronized groups and revealed focal demineralized regions (osteoid) adjacent to BAA surfaces.

The authors hypothesize that the TiB2/TiB surface layer releases boron locally at concentrations that are suprathreshold for bone cells — potentially disrupting osteoblast/osteoclast precursors and macrophages despite systemic boron being well-tolerated. The acid-etched boronized group performed worst, attributed to greater surface area and therefore greater local boron exposure.

## Key Contributions

- First large-animal (ovine) in vivo study of boronized titanium implant osseointegration
- Demonstrated time-dependent inhibition of osseointegration by boronization (BIC/BAFO declining 3→6 wk vs. increasing controls)
- Showed histological evidence of focal bone demineralization adjacent to boronized surfaces — bone reverting toward osteoid state
- Established that increased surface roughness (acid-etching) worsens the inhibitory boron effect by exposing more surface area
- Highlighted the translational gap: in vitro osteogenic boron findings do not predict in vivo bone-implant interface behavior

## Methodology

- **Animal model**: 5 adult male sheep (~55 kg); bilateral ovine iliac crest (hip); low-density cancellous bone
- **Total implants**: 40 (n=10 per group); 4 implants per hip per animal
- **Time points**: 3 weeks (right hip) and 6 weeks (left hip); staggered bilateral surgery
- **Implant specs**: Type II CP titanium; 4 mm diameter × 10 mm length
- **Boronization**: solid-state pack diffusion — 97% amorphous boron + 2% B4C + 1% KBF4; 1100°C × 8 h argon atmosphere; coating thickness 10–15 µm; sterilized by gamma irradiation (25 kGy)
- **Drilling**: 1100 rpm under saline irrigation; final drill diameter 3.8 mm
- **Histology**: non-decalcified MMA embedding; SVG stain; Aperio slide scanner; ImageJ measurement; single blinded examiner
- **Outcomes**: BIC (% implant perimeter in bone contact) and BAFO (% bone area within implant threads)
- **Statistics**: linear mixed model (IBM SPSS v23); fixed factors = surface texture, boronization, time point

## Results

**BIC at 3 weeks**: Only CAA was significantly higher than the other three groups (p<0.02); BM (16.44% ± 7.9), BAA (21.73% ± 7.9), and CM showed no significant differences among themselves.

**BIC change 3→6 weeks**: BAA decreased significantly (21.73% → 5.93%, p<0.01); BM showed non-significant decrease (16.44% → 8.9%); CM and CAA both showed significant increases.

**BAFO at 3 weeks**: CM was the lowest (19.92% ± 6.6, p<0.02 vs. BM, BAA, CAA); the three remaining groups did not differ significantly (BAA 25.82%, BM 27.29%, CAA 30.76%).

**BAFO change 3→6 weeks**: Both boronized groups declined significantly (p<0.03); both control groups increased significantly — a clear bidirectional divergence.

**Histology**: Controls showed progressive intramembranous-like healing and lamellar remodeling at 6 weeks. Boronized groups lacked this dynamic; BAA showed focal demineralized woven bone (osteoid regions) near the implant surface at 6 weeks. No inflammation or infection was observed clinically in any group; all implants were clinically stable at necropsy.

**No removal torque data** was reported (histomorphometry only).
