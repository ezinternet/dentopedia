## One-line Summary

In-vitro sawbone bench study (27 blocks, 3 ridge widths × 3 drilling protocols): reverse (counter-clockwise) Densah osseodensification drilling expanded narrow ridges (6.75 mm) significantly more than standard forward drilling, but only in narrow bone — at the cost of higher induced bone stress and significantly shallower implant insertion depth from elastic rebound.

## 한줄요약

인공골(sawbone) 벤치 실험(27블록, 골폭 3종 × 드릴링 3종): 반시계(역회전, reverse) Densah 골밀도화(Osseodensification, OD) 드릴은 좁은 치조제(6.75 mm)에서만 표준 정회전보다 골폭을 유의하게 확장했지만, 더 큰 골 응력과 탄성 반발 때문에 임플란트 식립 깊이는 유의하게 더 얕았다.

## Summary

This bench experiment tested whether the **reverse (counter-clockwise) drilling technique** can split/expand a narrow alveolar ridge, using synthetic sawbone blocks to remove natural bone-quality variability. Three osteotomy protocols were compared on three ridge widths (6.75, 7.25, 7.75 mm): (1) osseodensification (OD) with a Densah bur at 1500 rpm reverse, (2) modified OD with a triple straight-blade bur at 200 rpm reverse, and (3) standard drilling with the same triple-bladed bur at 1600 rpm forward. A φ5.0 × 8.0 mm implant was driven into each prepared site until 5 mm depth or fracture.

The reverse-drilling expansion benefit was **real but conditional**: only in narrow (6.75 mm) bone did the groups differ, and only the Densah/OD bur expanded the ridge significantly more than standard forward drilling. The gain vanished at 7.25 and 7.75 mm. Critically, the OD group's implants reached a **significantly shallower insertion depth** — the bench analog of the well-known OD trade-off: counter-clockwise compaction raises bone stress, and the bone's elastic rebound narrows the prepared site, resisting full-depth seating.

## Key Contributions

- Isolates the **ridge-expansion** component of reverse/counter-clockwise drilling on standardized synthetic bone, separate from the usual primary-stability framing.
- Shows the expansion benefit of OD over standard drilling is **width-dependent — present only in narrow ridges**, not a universal gain.
- Documents a quantified trade-off: higher narrow-ridge expansion comes with **shallower implant insertion depth** and higher induced bone stress (elastic rebound of the prepared site).
- Provides a direct head-to-head of OD (Densah) vs a low-speed "modified OD" reverse triple-blade drill — the two converged in wider bone.

## Methodology

- **Design**: in-vitro bench experiment on sawbones block 35 PCF (rigid synthetic bone), plates 5 mm thick × 40.5 mm long, three widths 6.75 / 7.25 / 7.75 mm (±0.05 mm).
- **n**: 27 samples (3 protocols × 3 widths × 3 replicates).
- **Osteotomy**: Lindemann pilot drill 1600 rpm forward, then one of three protocols; final drill diameter standardized at 4.5 mm.
  - OD group: Densah bur (Versah), **1500 rpm reverse** torque.
  - Modified OD group: triple straight-blade bur, **200 rpm reverse** torque.
  - Standard group: same triple-bladed drill, **1600 rpm forward** torque.
- **Implant**: IDEOSS (Taipei), φ5.0 × 8.0 mm, driven to 5 mm depth or until fracture.
- **Outcomes**: bone-block width change (expansion, digital caliper), implant insertion depth, bone fracture (success).
- **Statistics**: ANOVA + Tukey post-hoc (depth, width change); chi-square (fracture/success).
- **Note**: co-author is a shareholder of the implant manufacturer (IDEOSS) — declared conflict of interest.

## Results

- **Ridge expansion**: significant between-group difference **only at 6.75 mm** (p < 0.05). Both reverse-torque groups expanded more, but in pairwise comparison **only the Densah/OD bur beat the standard forward drill**. No difference at 7.25 / 7.75 mm, and no significant difference between OD and modified-OD.
- **Implant insertion depth**: OD group significantly **shallower** than the other two protocols (p ≈ 0.005); standard and modified-OD allowed deeper seating before fracture.
- **Bone fracture / success**: fewest fractures with standard forward drilling, but **no significant difference** among groups (chi-square).
- **Mechanism (authors)**: counter-clockwise rotation expands the ridge but raises bone stress; elastic rebound of the sawbone shrinks the prepared site, so OD implants seat shallower. When expansion is not needed (wider ridges), conventional forward drilling minimizes fracture risk and seats implants deeper.
