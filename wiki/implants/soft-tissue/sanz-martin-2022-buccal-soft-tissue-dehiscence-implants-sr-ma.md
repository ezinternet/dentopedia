## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 22 investigations on buccal peri-implant soft tissue dehiscence (BSTD): thin biotype (OR 2.85) and buccal malposition (OR 14.37) are significant risk factors; CTG absence raises odds 9-fold; buccal bone plate thickness and implant timing are not significant; BSTD frequency ranges 0%–61% across studies.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

This systematic review and meta-analysis (Clin Oral Implants Res 2022;33 Suppl 23:109–124) systematically analyzed factors associated with buccal soft tissue dehiscence (BSTD) at dental implants and their frequency of occurrence. BSTD was defined as apical migration ≥1 mm of the peri-implant mucosal margin from baseline (final restoration) or vs. the adjacent/contralateral tooth. Twenty-four articles from 22 clinical investigations (7 case series, 8 cohort studies, 1 CCT, 6 RCTs) were included.

Key findings: (1) thin soft tissue biotype (OR 2.85) and buccal implant malpositioning (OR 14.37) were statistically significant BSTD risk factors; (2) absence of connective tissue grafting (CTG) conferred a 9-fold increased odds of BSTD (OR 9.00), confirming CTG as a strong protective measure; (3) buccal bone plate thickness <1 mm (OR 1.29, p = .704) and immediate vs. delayed implant placement (OR 1.56, p = .477) were not significantly associated with BSTD. BSTD frequency ranged widely from 0% to 61%, with high heterogeneity precluding a pooled mean frequency estimate.

## Key Contributions

- First comprehensive SR+MA on BSTD at implants—simultaneously estimating frequency range and risk-factor ORs.
- Identified buccal implant malposition as the dominant modifiable risk factor (OR 14.37), reinforcing three-dimensional implant positioning within the alveolar envelope as the most critical prevention strategy.
- Confirmed CTG as the strongest protective intervention (9-fold OR reduction).
- Showed that thin buccal bone plate and immediate placement do not independently predict BSTD—challenging assumptions that bone volume alone drives soft tissue outcomes.
- BSTD frequency varies widely (0%–61%) depending on study population, surgical technique, and augmentation procedure—baseline comparison across studies is precluded without risk-factor stratification.

## Methodology

- **Design**: PRISMA-guided SR+MA; PROSPERO CRD42020203155
- **Databases**: MEDLINE/PubMed, Embase, Cochrane (searched to September 1, 2020) plus hand-search of 7 implant/perio journals
- **Inclusion**: RCTs, CCTs, prospective/retrospective cohort studies, prospective case series; ≥6 months post-prosthesis follow-up; ≥10 patients; English only; no cross-sectional designs
- **BSTD threshold**: ≥1 mm apical migration of mucosal margin
- **Statistics**: Random-effects meta-analysis; OR with 95% CI; diagnostic plots for publication bias; R + `metafor` package
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane tool (RCTs/CCTs); modified Newcastle–Ottawa Scale (observational)
- **Screening kappa**: 0.93 (title/abstract), 0.90 (full text)

## Results

### Meta-analysis risk factors

| Factor | OR [95% CI] | Studies (n) | p |
|--------|-------------|-------------|---|
| Thin biotype | 2.85 [1.40, 5.80] | 5 | .003 |
| Buccal implant malposition | 14.37 [4.58, 45.14] | 3 | <.001 |
| No CTG (vs CTG) | 9.00 [3.11, 26.02] | 5 | <.001 |
| Buccal bone plate <1 mm | 1.29 [0.35, 4.77] | 2 | .704 (NS) |
| Immediate vs. delayed placement | 1.56 [0.46, 5.26] | 4 | .477 (NS) |

### BSTD frequency across studies (0%–61%)

- **0%**: Tian 2019 (flapless IIP + DBBM, anterior maxilla)
- **3.4%**: Bianchi 2004 (CTG used); Raes 2018 (flapless IIP/DIP)
- **4%**: Lindeboom 2006 (flap + autogenous bone + CM)
- **12.6%**: Cairo 2020 (all lateral GBR, mixed jaw)
- **13.3%**: Arora 2017 (flapless IIP + DBBM)
- **20–35%**: Multiple cohort/RCT studies (Cosyn 2012: 35%; van Nimwegen 2018: 20%; Seyssens 2020: 33%)
- **40–57%**: Bittner 2019 (57%), Cordaro 2009 (53%), Evans & Chen 2008 (40%)
- **61.3%**: Oates 2002 (highest; flap elevation, no bone graft, anterior maxilla and mandible)

No significant association between BSTD frequency and follow-up duration or study type was detected.

### CTG sub-analysis (5 studies)

| Study | BSTD with CTG | BSTD without CTG |
|-------|--------------|-----------------|
| Bianchi 2004 | 0/96 | 4/20 |
| Migliorati 2015 | 1/24 | 7/23 |
| van Nimwegen 2018 | 2/25 | 8/25 |
| Seyssens 2020 | 0/6 | 6/12 |
| Cosyn 2016 | included | included |

### Buccal position sub-analysis (3 studies)

- Cosyn 2012: buccal shoulder position OR 17.2 for BSTD
- Evans & Chen 2008: 9/32 well-positioned vs. 8/10 buccally placed implants with BSTD
- Seyssens 2020: 0/12 well-positioned vs. 3/6 buccally placed implants with BSTD
