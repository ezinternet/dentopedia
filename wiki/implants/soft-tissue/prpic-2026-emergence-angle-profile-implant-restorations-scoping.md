## One-line Summary

PRISMA-ScR scoping review (17 studies, incl. 2 RCT + 1 SR+MA + cross-sectional/retrospective) of implant-restoration emergence angle/profile: an emergence angle < 30° may reduce marginal bone loss (evidence inconclusive), while a concave emergence profile is consistently associated with greater peri-implant soft tissue margin stability and reduced mid-facial recession.

## 한줄요약

임플란트 보철 emergence angle/profile에 관한 17편 scoping review — emergence angle < 30°가 변연골 흡수를 줄일 수 있으나(근거 불확실), concave(오목) emergence profile은 일관되게 peri-implant 연조직 안정·중안면 퇴축 감소와 연관된다.

## Summary

This scoping review (PRISMA-ScR, OSF-registered) synthesizes 17 studies on the prosthodontic **emergence angle (출현각)** and **emergence profile (출현윤곽)** of implant-supported restorations and their effect on peri-implant hard and soft tissue. The two design parameters were extracted to seed a parametric / generative-design workflow for hybrid (zirconia + titanium) implant abutments.

Two converging signals emerge: (1) an **emergence angle < 30°** is *plausibly* protective against marginal bone loss and peri-implantitis, but the evidence is inconsistent — several cohorts found no association, so the authors call it inconclusive and pending RCTs; (2) a **concave emergence profile** is *consistently* associated with peri-implant soft tissue margin stability and reduced recession, supported by two RCTs. Critically, the angle threshold and the profile interact: the angle's biological risk becomes meaningful mainly when it exceeds 30° **and** the profile is convex, while a concave profile (and a mechanically stable abutment interface, and abutment height > 2 mm) appears to buffer the risk of a larger angle.

## Key Contributions

- Consolidates the **< 30° emergence angle** rule and the **concave profile** recommendation into one source intended as design input for hybrid-abutment generative design.
- Frames the subgingival contour with the **EBC zone scheme**: E (esthetic, 1 mm below free gingival margin) / B (bounded, 1–2 mm apical) / C (crestal, 1–1.5 mm at platform).
- Distinguishes profile contours: **convex** (mechanical support, biofilm-retention risk, pairs with larger angles), **concave** (anterior zone, vascularization, soft-tissue creeping, pairs with smaller angles), **straight** (posterior, cleanability).
- Explicitly excludes platform-switching, connection type, and esthetic-vs-posterior site from scope.

## Methodology

- PRISMA-ScR scoping review; OSF registration 10.17605/OSF.IO/Q7VDS. No risk-of-bias assessment, no meta-analysis (by design).
- PubMed + Scopus, up to 1 Dec 2025, 5-year window. Inclusion: full-text, English, human. Exclusion: case series, editorials, interviews.
- Flow: 791 retrieved → 650 removed pre-screen (640 automation, 10 duplicates) → 141 screened → 124 excluded → **17 included** (10 on angle, 7 on profile; Neckel/Lin/Izzetti counted in both).
- Two primary + two supplementary reviewers.

## Results

**Emergence angle (10 studies):**
- Pro-<30° (5): Neckel (cross-sectional, n=462 — <30° + concave → ↓annual bone loss); Lin (SR+MA — <30° + concave/straight → ↓peri-implantitis risk); Misch (retrospective, n=192 — >=30° risk significant only when abutment height < 2 mm); Majzoub (retrospective, n=83 — >30° → early ≤2 y MBL in peri-implantitis); Yu (digital, n=100 — cantilever molar restorations have smaller angles, fewer >30°).
- No association / no consensus (5): Tang (n=57, angle 36.7±13.7° — no link to buccal bone loss); Maghsoudi (no diff <30° vs >=30°, well-maintained patients); Lops ×2 (stability preserved if concave profile + stable interface, even 30–50°); Izzetti review (no consensus).

**Emergence profile (7 studies):**
- Pro-concave (4): Neckel (profile matters most when angle >30°; negligible <30°); Lin (SR+MA — concave/straight → ↓risk); **Siegenthaler RCT (n=47, anterior maxilla)** — concave provisional → greater mucosal margin stability vs convex at 1 y; **Endres RCT (n=47, esthetic zone, 3 y)** — concave → superior soft tissue stability, fewer mid-facial recessions, coronal creeping.
- Narrative/comprehensive reviews (3): Gervyte, Hamilton, Izzetti — emphasize careful profile management (implant positioning, thin phenotype).
- Pattern: convex ↔ larger angle; straight/concave ↔ smaller angle.

**Threshold takeaway**: angle **< 30°** = inconclusive-but-favorable design target; **concave profile** = consistent soft-tissue stability benefit, especially in the esthetic zone.

**Limitations**: heterogeneous designs; English-only / 2-database; no risk-of-bias appraisal.
