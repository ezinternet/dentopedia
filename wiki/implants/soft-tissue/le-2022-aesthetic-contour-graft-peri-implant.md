## One-line Summary

Clinical technique article introducing the "Aesthetic Contour Graft" — GBR with particulate allograft + collagen membrane to augment labial bone contour and enhance peri-implant soft tissue thickness and emergence profiles, including pontic sites, with cases showing stability to 8 years.

## Summary

Le & Hayashi (2022) introduce the concept of the **Aesthetic Contour Graft** — a GBR-based strategy specifically aimed at enhancing the labial bone volume beneath peri-implant soft tissue in order to achieve ideal emergence profiles, rather than relying primarily on soft tissue grafts.

The central thesis is that **most peri-implant soft tissue deficiencies have an underlying osseous etiology**. When the labial bone contour is deficient, the overlying mucosa will be thin, recession-prone, and unable to support natural emergence. Correcting the bone via GBR inherently corrects the soft tissue — and in many cases eliminates the need for a connective tissue graft (CTG) as a separate procedure.

## Key Contributions

**The "bone drives soft tissue" principle:**  
A strong correlation between labial bone thickness and labial soft tissue thickness around maxillary anterior implants is cited — augmenting bone directly thickens the overlying mucosa. Minimum targets: ≥2–3 mm vertical soft tissue, ≥2–3 mm horizontal soft tissue, and ≥2 mm labial bone labial to the implant platform after remodeling.

**Two clinical approaches:**
1. **Staged GBR**: GBR performed before implant placement. Allows maximum correction of large defects; implant placed into augmented ridge at second stage. Overcorrection is intentional to account for expected resorption.
2. **Simultaneous single-stage**: Implant + GBR in one procedure for mild-to-moderate defects. Non-submerged healing with a healing abutment to tent soft tissue and reduce apical graft migration. Referenced evidence shows 100% and 79.3% bone regeneration for small and medium buccal dehiscences; large defects only partially corrected.

**Pontic site development:**  
GBR to build ridge volume prior to bridge delivery supports a full ovate pontic emergence contour, again without necessarily needing separate soft tissue surgery.

**Tenting screws:**  
Used to maintain the space under the membrane at the labial crest, preventing graft collapse and ensuring bone forms at the crest level where soft tissue support is most critical.

## Methodology

- Clinical article with technique description and illustrative case series (no prospective control group).
- Material: particulate mineralized allograft + resorbable collagen membrane.
- Cases include congenitally missing maxillary laterals, post-extraction sites, and edentulous spans requiring pontic site development.
- Longest follow-up reported: 8 years (stable soft tissue contours).

## Results

- Peri-implant soft tissue contours maintained at 8 years in simultaneous-placement cases with GBR.
- Implant angulation effect: cingulum angulation → 2.98 mm crestal soft tissue; labial angulation → 1.71 mm — highlighting that position planning affects tissue volume as much as augmentation.
- No systematic quantitative outcomes table; evidence is case illustration + literature synthesis.
