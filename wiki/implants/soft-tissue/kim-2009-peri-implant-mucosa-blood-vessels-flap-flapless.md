## One-line Summary

Canine RCT (n=6 dogs, 12 implant pairs) showed flapless implant surgery produces significantly richer peri-implant mucosal vascularity than conventional flap surgery — vessel fraction 1.7% vs 1.2% (p<0.05) — suggesting flapless technique enhances soft-tissue defense capacity.

## Summary

This Korean animal study is the first to experimentally compare peri-implant mucosal blood vessel distribution between conventional flap and flapless implant surgery protocols. In 6 mongrel dogs, bilateral edentulous ridges were created, and after 3 months of healing, 2 implants per side were placed using either a full-thickness mucoperiosteal flap or a 3 mm soft-tissue punch (flapless) technique. After 3 further months of healing, histomorphometric analysis of buccal/lingual sections quantified vessel number and vessel fraction (cross-sectional area %) in a standardized zone from the junctional epithelium base to the alveolar crest, 500 µm lateral to the implant surface.

Flapless sites showed significantly higher vascularity on both measures (vessel fraction 1.7 ± 0.7% vs 1.2 ± 0.3%; vessel number 51.4 ± 9.2 vs 38.2 ± 8.1; both p<0.05, Wilcoxon signed-rank test). Clinically, flapless sites showed no mucosal inflammation signs, while 5/12 flap-site implants had redness and bleeding on probing. Flapless mucosa was also thinner — interpreted as less scar tissue formation due to the smaller, cleaner, well-approximated wound created by the punch technique.

The authors propose two mechanisms for higher vascularity with flapless surgery: (1) preservation of supraperiosteal vessels (which Berglundh et al. 1994 identified as the primary blood supply to the supracrestal connective tissue), and (2) reduced wound size leading to less scar formation (scar tissue being inherently hypovascular).

## Key Contributions

- First animal study to quantify peri-implant mucosal vascularity comparing flap vs flapless surgery
- Establishes that flapless approach preserves supraperiosteal vessel network, measurably increasing peri-implant soft tissue blood supply
- Provides biological rationale for why flapless procedures may reduce peri-implant inflammation risk: richer vascularity → better immune defense

## Methodology

**Design**: Animal experiment, split-mouth design, randomized side allocation  
**Animals**: 6 mongrel dogs, 18–24 months, 15–20 kg  
**Implants**: Osstem GS II Φ4×10.0 mm; healing abutments Φ4.5×5 mm  
**Groups**: Flap (full mucoperiosteal flap + suture) vs Flapless (3 mm punch, no suture)  
**Healing**: 3 months post-implant before sacrifice  
**Histomorphometry**: H-E stained 5 µm sections; measurement zone = junctional epithelium base → alveolar crest height, 0–500 µm lateral from implant surface; IBAS image analysis  
**Statistics**: Wilcoxon signed-rank test, SPSS (α=0.05)

## Results

| Outcome | Flapless | Flap | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Vessel fraction (%) | 1.7 ± 0.7 | 1.2 ± 0.3 | <0.05 |
| Vessel number | 51.4 ± 9.2 | 38.2 ± 8.1 | <0.05 |

- Flapless: no mucosal inflammation; thinner mucosa; some vessels >40 µm diameter
- Flap: 5/12 sites with redness/BOP; thicker mucosa; all vessels ≤30 µm diameter
