## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 17 RCTs: non-crosslinked XCM is inferior to CTG for mucosal thickness (MD −0.27 mm, P=0.01) but crosslinked XCM (VCMX) equals CTG; FGG is superior to XCM* for keratinized mucosa width gain (MD 1.47 mm, P=0.0006) at implant sites.

## 한줄요약

17편 RCT SR+MA: 임플란트 부위 연조직 증대에서 비가교 이종 콜라겐 매트릭스 (XCM, Xenogeneic Collagen Matrix)는 결합조직이식 (CTG, Connective Tissue Graft) 대비 점막두께 (MT, Mucosal Thickness) 열위(MD −0.27 mm, P=0.01)이나 가교형 VCMX는 CTG와 동등; 유리치은이식 (FGG, Free Gingival Graft)은 XCM 대비 각화점막폭 (KMW, Keratinized Mucosa Width) 1.47 mm 우위.

## Summary

This 2025 SR+MA (PROSPERO CRD42023495454) is the largest to date comparing xenogeneic collagen matrices (XCMs) vs autogenous grafts at implant sites. Seventeen RCTs met inclusion criteria (search: inception to August 2024; databases: PubMed, Web of Science, Scopus); 14 were included in the meta-analysis.

**Key clinical finding**: The type of XCM matters.
- **Non-crosslinked XCM (XCM*)** — e.g., Geistlich Mucograft — is significantly inferior to CTG for mucosal thickness increase (MD −0.27 mm, 95% CI −0.49 to −0.05, P=0.01).
- **Crosslinked XCM (VCMX)** — e.g., Geistlich Fibro-Gide — shows no significant difference from CTG for MT augmentation (MD −0.02 mm, 95% CI −0.17 to +0.22, P=0.83), making it a potentially viable morbidity-sparing alternative.
- **FGG remains superior** to XCM* for keratinized mucosa width (MD +1.47 mm, 95% CI 0.63 to 2.30, P=0.0006); autologous graft is still the gold standard for KMW augmentation.

Risk of bias was moderate for XCM vs CTG studies and low for XCM* vs FGG studies.

## Key Contributions

- First SR+MA to separately analyse crosslinked (VCMX) vs non-crosslinked (XCM*) porcine collagen matrices against CTG, resolving previous contradictions from pooled analyses.
- Largest RCT pool for this clinical question (17 RCTs; prior best: 9).
- Establishes that the crosslinked collagen matrix architecture (VCMX) achieves volumetric stability comparable to CTG, while the non-crosslinked variant does not.
- FGG superiority for KMW confirmed with low-RoB studies; ~1.5 mm gap is clinically meaningful.

## Methodology

- **Design**: SR+MA of RCTs; random-effects model; pooled mean difference.
- **PICO**: Patients with MT <2 mm and/or KMW <2 mm; XCM vs CTG (MT); XCM* vs FGG (KMW); follow-up ≥3 months.
- **Subgroups**: XCM* vs CTG; VCMX vs CTG; XCM* vs FGG.
- **RoB tool**: Cochrane RoB 2.
- **n**: 17 RCTs qualitative (12 XCM vs CTG; 5 XCM vs FGG); 14 quantitative (9 XCM vs CTG; 5 XCM vs FGG).

## Results

| Comparison | n RCTs (MA) | Pooled MD (mm) | 95% CI | P | Conclusion |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| XCM* vs CTG (MT) | 9 | −0.27 | −0.49 to −0.05 | 0.01 | CTG superior |
| VCMX vs CTG (MT) | subset | −0.02 | −0.17 to +0.22 | 0.83 | Equivalent |
| XCM* vs FGG (KMW) | 5 | −1.47 | −2.30 to −0.63 | 0.0006 | FGG superior |

- Aesthetic and patient-reported outcomes (PROs) were generally comparable between XCM and autogenous grafts across studies, with XCM showing advantages in operative time, donor-site morbidity, and postoperative pain.
- Moderate heterogeneity across XCM vs CTG studies due to varied surgical approaches and XCM products; low heterogeneity in FGG comparison.
