## One-line Summary

SR + network meta-analysis (52 studies, 23 RCTs) comparing five soft tissue phenotype modification techniques around implants: CTG/ADM bilaminar ranks highest for mucosal thickness gain, APF+FGG ranks highest for keratinized mucosa width gain, and both pathways improve clinical peri-implant health markers.

## Summary

Tavelli et al. (J Periodontol 2021) conducted the first network meta-analysis (NMA) comparing soft tissue augmentation techniques aimed at modifying the peri-implant soft tissue phenotype (PSP). PSP is defined as the composite of keratinized mucosa width (KMW), mucosal thickness (MT), and supracrestal tissue height (STH). Fifty-two studies were included in qualitative synthesis; 23 RCTs entered the NMA. The evidence was organized along two surgical pathways — bilaminar techniques (for MT) and apically positioned flap (APF) techniques (for KMW) — with separate networks for each. The study established a clear technique hierarchy: for MT gain, connective tissue graft (CTG) and acellular dermal matrix (ADM) bilaminar outperform collagen matrix (CM); for KMW gain, APF with free gingival graft (FGG) is the only technique to significantly exceed APF alone. Clinically, APF-based approaches reduced probing depth, soft tissue dehiscence, and plaque index regardless of the graft used, while bilaminar CTG or CM protected marginal bone levels.

## Key Contributions

- **Established PSP (Peri-implant Soft tissue Phenotype)** as a unified three-component construct (KMW + MT + STH), giving the field a common language
- **First NMA on peri-implant soft tissue augmentation**: Enables indirect treatment comparisons across a network of 23 RCTs where head-to-head data do not exist
- **Pathway separation**: Bilaminar (MT-focused) vs APF (KMW-focused) as distinct clinical decision trees
- **Quantified FGG's unique advantage**: FGG is the only add-on to APF that significantly increases KMW beyond APF alone — CTG, CM, and ADM added to APF do not provide incremental KMW benefit
- **MBL protection via bilaminar CTG/CM**: Provides bone-level justification for connective tissue grafting even when soft tissue esthetics is not the primary concern

## Methodology

- **Type**: Systematic review + network meta-analysis (NMA)
- **Qualitative synthesis**: 52 articles
- **NMA**: 23 RCTs only, divided into:
  - Bilaminar network: 16 RCTs (CTG, CM, ADM vs non-augmented)
  - APF network: 7 RCTs (APF+FGG, APF+CTG, APF+CM, APF+ADM vs APF alone)
- **PSP outcomes**: KMW, MT, STH
- **Health outcomes**: MBL change, probing depth (PD), soft tissue dehiscence, plaque index (PI)
- **Publication**: J Periodontol Vol 92, Issue 1, January 2021 (online first 25 July 2020)

## Results

### Bilaminar techniques — Mucosal Thickness (MT) ranking

1. CTG ≈ ADM (both significantly superior to non-augmented and CM)
2. CM (significant MT gain vs non-augmented; inferior to CTG and ADM)
3. No significant KMW differences across bilaminar techniques
4. CTG and CM bilaminar → significant MBL stability benefit

### APF-based techniques — KMW ranking

1. APF + FGG (only technique significantly superior to APF alone for KMW)
2. APF + CTG / APF + CM / APF + ADM (no significant KMW gain vs APF alone)
3. APF with any graft → significant reduction in PD, dehiscence, PI vs non-augmented
4. APF-based approaches on MBL: inconclusive evidence

### Clinical decision framework

| Goal | Best technique | Second choice |
|---|---|---|
| Increase mucosal thickness (MT) | CTG bilaminar | ADM bilaminar |
| Increase keratinized mucosa width (KMW) | APF + FGG | APF + CTG/CM (marginal) |
| Protect marginal bone (MBL) | Bilaminar CTG or CM | — |
| Reduce PD / plaque / dehiscence | APF with any graft | — |
