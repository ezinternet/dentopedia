## One-line Summary

Retrospective radiographic analysis (192 bone-level implants / 119 patients, mean 7.4-yr follow-up) crossing trans-mucosal abutment height (TmAH) × restoration emergence angle (REA) into 4 groups: Short/Wide-Angle (TmAH <2 mm + REA ≥30°) had the greatest MBL (+0.48 mm vs Long/Narrow, p<0.001) and ~4× higher peri-implantitis (OR 4.19 vs Long/Wide, p=0.013); REA was a significant MBL factor ONLY when TmAH <2 mm, and every 1 mm TmAH increase cut odds of any MBL by 37% (OR 0.63, p=0.003).

## Summary

This University of Michigan retrospective radiographic study (n=192 bone-level implants, 119 partially edentulous patients, mean total follow-up 7.4 years) asks how the **trans-mucosal abutment height (TmAH)** and the **restoration emergence angle (REA)** act *together* on marginal bone loss (MBL) and peri-implantitis (PI). Sites (mesial + distal, 384 total) were cross-classified into four groups by TmAH (cutoff 2 mm) × REA (cutoff 30°): LN (Long/Narrow), LW (Long/Wide), SN (Short/Narrow), SW (Short/Wide).

The central finding is that **emergence angle is a conditional, not an independent, risk factor**: REA ≥30° drove significant MBL only when TmAH was below 2 mm. With a tall abutment (>2 mm), a wide angle (LW) was statistically indistinguishable from a narrow angle (LN). The Short/Wide group concentrated nearly all the harm — the most MBL, ~4× the peri-implantitis, and 9 of 10 failed sites. Abutment height itself was the dominant protective lever: each 1 mm of TmAH cut the odds of any MBL by 37%, while REA alone was non-significant. Mechanistically, a short abutment leaves insufficient vertical space for supracrestal tissue adhesion (STAd) to form, allowing the rough implant surface/threads to be exposed and plaque to accumulate near the crest.

## Key Contributions

- First cross-classification of TmAH × REA on bone-level implants; reframes REA ≥30° as significant for MBL **only** under short (<2 mm) abutments.
- Quantifies TmAH as the dominant protective factor: OR 0.63 per mm for any MBL (p=0.003); REA non-significant alone (OR 0.99, p=0.991).
- Ties Short/Wide configuration to ~4× peri-implantitis and the majority of failures, extending Galindo-Moreno's abutment-height work with a prosthetic-angle dimension.

## Methodology

- Retrospective single-center radiographic cohort; implants placed/restored 2012-2020; bone-level fixtures only (tissue-level, full-arch, peri-implantitis-treated excluded).
- MBL measured T0 (12-18 mo post-crown) → T1 (>1 yr later) on mesial & distal aspects by one calibrated examiner; calibrated to charted implant length; ICC 0.95.
- PI per 2017 World Workshop (progressive bone loss >0.5 mm + deepening PD + inflammation).
- GEE regression (accounts for multiple sites/implants per patient); multivariate adjustment for sector, arch, implant length, connection type. Post hoc power 86.2%.

## Results

| Group | TmAH | REA | Mean MBL | MBL>0 prevalence | Intra-group PI rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| LN (Long/Narrow) | >2 mm | <30° | 0.26 mm | 35.1% | 13.3% |
| LW (Long/Wide) | >2 mm | ≥30° | 0.27 mm | 47.5% | 9.1% |
| SN (Short/Narrow) | <2 mm | <30° | 0.46 mm | 69.9% | 13.2% |
| SW (Short/Wide) | <2 mm | ≥30° | **0.79 mm** | 67.7% | **25.6%** |

- Adjusted pairwise MBL: SW vs LN +0.48 mm (p<0.001); SW vs LW +0.43 mm (p=0.001); SW vs SN +0.25 mm (p=0.013); SN vs LN +0.29 mm (p=0.004); **LN vs LW not significant** (+0.08 mm, p=0.444).
- Probability of any MBL: **TmAH OR 0.63/mm** (95% CI 0.46-0.85, p=0.003); REA OR 0.99 (p=0.991, NS); interaction REA×TmAH NS (p=0.276).
- Peri-implantitis: prevalence 18.8% implant-level; SW = 65.7% of all PI cases; **SW vs LW OR 4.19** (p=0.013), SW vs LN OR 4.04 (p=0.091). Other PI predictors: periodontitis history (OR 4.81, p=0.018), maintenance visits/yr (OR 2.64, p=0.002); internal hex protective vs external hex (OR 0.17, p=0.024).
- Failures: 5 total, all in SW sites; severe PI main cause.
- Model interpretation of interaction: +1 mm TmAH reduces pMBL>0 by 41% at REA 20°, 30% at 40°, 16% at 60° — the protective benefit of a tall abutment shrinks as the angle widens.

**Clinical bottom line**: choose an abutment > 2 mm (deeper apico-coronal platform placement / longer trans-mucosal abutment) whenever possible — it largely neutralizes the bone-loss penalty of a ≥30° emergence angle. A wide emergence angle is most dangerous when stacked on a short abutment.
