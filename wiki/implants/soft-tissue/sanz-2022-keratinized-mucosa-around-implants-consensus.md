## One-line Summary

DGI/SEPA/Osteology 2022 consensus (34 statements, 2 SRs, n=22+10 studies): keratinized peri-implant mucosa (KPIM) < 2 mm is associated with higher peri-implantitis prevalence, plaque, mucosal recession, and MBL; FGG is standard of care for augmentation, with xenogeneic substitutes non-inferior to CTG and offering lower morbidity.

## Summary

This consensus report from the DGI/SEPA/Osteology Workshop (Group 1, 2022) synthesizes two commissioned systematic reviews and expert panel opinion into 34 consensus statements, 8 clinical practice implications, and 13 future research implications on the importance of keratinized peri-implant mucosa (KPIM) around dental implants.

The report addresses two core questions: (1) what is the clinical significance of a reduced width of KPIM for peri-implant health, and (2) what surgical interventions and grafting materials are most effective for augmenting KPIM when it is deficient.

The consensus establishes **KT < 2 mm** as the operative threshold for "inadequate" KPIM, associated with increased clinical signs of disease. However, it also clarifies that wider bands of KT (2–11 mm) do not provide additional protection against mucosal inflammation. **Free gingival autogenous grafts (FGG)** are confirmed as the standard of care for KPIM augmentation, while **xenogeneic soft-tissue substitutes** emerge as a viable alternative offering comparable width gain to CTG with significantly reduced surgical time and post-operative pain.

## Key Contributions

- **KT threshold operationalized**: KT < 2 mm is the consensus-agreed threshold associated with higher biofilm accumulation, BOP, mucosal recession, MBL, and patient discomfort; KT ≥ 2 mm is the target.
- **Mucositis incidence inconclusive**: No consistent evidence that KT width affects peri-implant mucositis incidence; prevalence data are heterogeneous.
- **Peri-implantitis prevalence difference confirmed**: 10.5%–44% at KT < 2 mm sites vs 5.1%–17% at KT ≥ 2 mm sites across included studies.
- **FGG standard of care**: Apically positioned flap + autogenous FGG yields 1.5–6.5 mm KPIM gain; graft shrinkage 42%–60% over 12 months.
- **Xenografts non-inferior to CTG**: No significant difference in KPIM width gain between xenogeneic substitutes and autogenous grafts (WMD = 0.8 mm; p = .062); xenografts gain 3.5 mm on average and reduce surgical time by ~18.5 min.
- **Allogeneic substitutes show higher shrinkage**: 19.5% greater shrinkage vs autogenous grafts (p = .009) — a distinguishing finding from xenografts.
- **Routine monitoring mandated**: KPIM width should be measured at every follow-up visit at buccal and lingual aspects using a mm-scaled probe.

## Methodology

**SR #1 — Influence of KT on peri-implant health** (Ramanauskaite et al., 2022):
- PECO: implant patients (P), KT < 2 mm (E) vs KT ≥ 2 mm (C), peri-implant disease prevalence + soft/hard tissue stability (O)
- 22 articles / 21 studies: 15 cross-sectional, 5 longitudinal, 1 pre-post series
- Search: through September 2020 + updated through January 2022
- Risk of bias: high to low

**SR #2 — Soft-tissue substitutes vs autogenous grafts** (Montero et al., 2022):
- PICOS #1: RCTs/CCTs ≥6 months comparing substitutes vs autogenous grafts for KPIM width
- PICOS #2: All study designs including prospective/retrospective series
- 11 articles / 10 investigations: 5 RCTs, 1 CCT, 4 case series
- Risk of bias: unclear to high (Newcastle-Ottawa mean 3.0)

**Consensus process**: Group discussion → plenary session → unanimous approval → formal publication in Clin Oral Implants Res Suppl 23.

| Parameter | KT < 2 mm | KT ≥ 2 mm | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Peri-implantitis prevalence | 10.5%–44% | 5.1%–17% | 5 studies |
| Peri-implant mucositis prevalence | 20.8%–46.6% | 20.5%–53% | 5 studies (inconclusive) |
| Plaque scores | Significantly higher | Reference | 3 longitudinal + 6 cross-sectional |
| Mucosal recession | Significantly more (cross-sectional) | Reference | 6 studies |

### Key clinical consensus statements (selected)

1. Measure KPIM with mm-scaled probe at buccal and lingual aspects; remove prosthesis if margin is obscured.
2. Measure KPIM at every follow-up appointment; width changes over time.
3. KT < 2 mm threshold is associated with higher disease risk; KT > 2 mm does not provide additional protection beyond 2 mm.
4. Surgical augmentation is indicated when KPIM < 2 mm is associated with pathological changes.
5. Aim for non-mobile (attached) mucosa, not just adequate width.
6. In shallow vestibule with absent KPIM, combine vestibule deepening with autogenous graft.
7. Xenogeneic substitutes are a valid alternative to FGG/CTG, with lower donor-site morbidity and shorter surgical time.
8. A minimum amount of residual KT is required as a "cell migration border" for keratinization of substitutes.
