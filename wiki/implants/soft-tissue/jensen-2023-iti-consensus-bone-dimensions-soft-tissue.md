## One-line Summary

7th ITI Consensus (Group 1; built on 2 systematic reviews): in healed implant sites a buccal bone wall <1.5 mm predicts greater vertical bone loss and worse clinical/radiographic outcomes; simultaneous GBR over buccal dehiscence defects and connective-tissue-graft soft tissue augmentation deliver stable peri-implant outcomes from 1 to 5 years.

## 한줄요약

7차 ITI 합의문(Group 1, 2개 SR 기반): 치유된 식립부에서 협측 골벽(BBW) <1.5 mm는 더 큰 수직 골소실·불리한 임상/방사선 결과를 예측하며, 협측 열개 결손에 대한 동시 GBR과 결합조직이식(CTG) 연조직증대(STA)는 1–5년간 안정적인 임플란트 주위 결과를 준다.

## Summary

This is the Group 1 consensus report of the 7th ITI Consensus Conference, synthesizing two systematic reviews into clinical statements on (i) the role of buccal bone wall thickness (BBW) on peri-implant hard/soft tissue changes and (ii) whether soft tissue augmentation (STA) produces stable medium/long-term peri-implant conditions.

The consensus defines a **thin BBW as <1.5 mm** and **initial healing as ≤6 months** after implant placement in healed (non-fresh-socket) sites. After implant placement in healed sites, the alveolar ridge undergoes buccolingual reduction, with BBW reductions of **0.3–1.8 mm** (up to 72 months). Sites with a thin BBW exhibit vertical bone loss during initial healing and less favorable clinical/radiographic outcomes. Where buccal dehiscence defects undergo **simultaneous GBR**, they show less vertical bone loss than non-augmented defects.

For soft tissue: single-implant peri-implant bone and clinical parameters (probing depth, bleeding, plaque) measured at 1 year remain stable to 5 years with or without STA. Sites augmented with **connective tissue grafts (CTG)** keep a stable soft tissue margin and stable/increased soft tissue thickness and keratinized mucosa width 1→5 years, whereas non-augmented sites are more prone to apical margin migration. Esthetic outcomes (PES, VAS) after CTG/substitute are maintained or improved 1→5 years.

## Key Contributions

- Sets the clinically actionable **BBW <1.5 mm = "thin"** threshold for healed-site implant placement.
- Patient-facing rule of thumb: ~1.5 mm buccal + ~1 mm palatal bone → a 4 mm implant needs a ridge ~6.5–7 mm wide.
- Endorses **simultaneous GBR** for buccal dehiscence defects (less vertical bone loss vs non-augmented).
- Endorses **CTG augmentation** at esthetically demanding single-implant sites to keep the soft tissue margin stable and prevent apical migration.
- Names alternative esthetic-risk mitigations: reduced implant diameter, deeper crestal placement, posterior ridge flattening.

## Methodology

- Two systematic reviews prepared in advance: BBW review (16 studies — 12 clinical, 4 preclinical, out of 1700 records) and STA review (15 clinical studies — 4 RCT, 5 non-randomized, 6 case series; 447 patients / 461 implants; 3–10 y follow-up, mean 8 y).
- Structured group discussion to consensus, accepted by the full plenum. Outputs: consensus statements, clinical recommendations, future-research recommendations, patient perspectives.
- The STA review could not pool data (no meta-analysis) — descriptive synthesis only.

## Results

- BBW reduction after healed-site placement: 0.3–1.8 mm (up to 72 months; 11 prospective studies).
- Simultaneous GBR over buccal dehiscence: less vertical bone loss than non-augmented (supported by 1 RCT, 22 patients / 28 implants).
- Single-implant interproximal bone stable 1→5 y ± STA (12 studies; 3 RCT, 9 prospective).
- CTG sites: stable soft tissue margin to 5 y (10 studies); non-augmented sites prone to apical migration (5 studies).
- Esthetics (PES/VAS) maintained or improved after CTG/substitute 1→5 y; non-STA sites show more discoloration.
