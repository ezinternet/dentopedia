## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 14 RCTs (475 patients, 538 implants): soft-tissue augmentation at implant sites improves keratinized tissue height and thickness; CTG is superior to XCM for soft-tissue thickness gain (WMD −0.30 mm, 95% CI −0.43 to −0.17, P < .00001).

## Summary

Cairo et al. (2019) conducted an SR+MA restricted exclusively to randomized controlled trials (RCTs) to evaluate (a) whether soft-tissue augmentation at implant sites provides clinical benefit, and (b) which surgical technique produces the best outcomes. Fourteen RCTs (475 patients; 538 implants) were included. Primary outcomes were change in keratinized tissue height (KT) and soft-tissue thickness (STT).

Single-study comparisons consistently showed that any augmentation procedure outperformed no augmentation for both KT and STT. The only comparison with sufficient RCT data for meta-analysis was **CTG vs xenogeneic collagen matrix (XCM)**: CTG produced significantly greater STT gain (WMD −0.30 mm, 95% CI −0.43 to −0.17; P < .00001). Only 5 of 14 RCTs were at low risk of bias, limiting the certainty of the pooled estimate.

The authors concluded that soft-tissue augmentation improves peri-implant soft-tissue quantity and quality, but further high-quality RCTs are needed before procedural hierarchies can be definitively established.

**Important depth note**: The source PDF is a Wiley HTML abstract print — full methodology, individual-study data, and forest plots are not available. All quantitative data are from the abstract.

## Key Contributions

- Establishes that augmentation (vs none) consistently improves KT and STT across 14 RCTs
- Only pooled meta-analytic result: CTG > XCM for STT gain (WMD −0.30 mm, P < .00001)
- Highlights sparse high-quality RCT evidence: only 5/14 studies at low risk of bias
- Provides the foundational RCT-only evidence base that later SRs (Valles 2022, Stefanini 2023, Montero 2022) built upon

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review + random-effects meta-analysis
- **Inclusion**: RCTs only; implant sites; primary outcomes KT height and STT
- **Studies**: 14 RCTs; n = 475 patients; 538 implants
- **Statistical method**: Weighted mean differences (WMD), 95% CI, random-effects model
- **Risk of bias**: 5/14 low risk; 9/14 unclear/high risk
- **Techniques represented**: CTG, XCM (mucograft/collagen matrix), FGG (free gingival graft), ADM (acellular dermal matrix), VSCM (volume-stable collagen matrix), L-PRF, vestibuloplasty

## Results

| Comparison | Outcome | WMD (mm) | 95% CI | P |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| CTG vs XCM | STT (favors CTG) | −0.30 | −0.43 to −0.17 | < .00001 |

- All 14 included RCTs: augmentation > no augmentation for KT and STT (single-study level)
- KT gain meta-analysis: data insufficient for pooling across techniques; single-study comparisons favor augmentation
- Evidence certainty: low (risk of bias, heterogeneity of procedures and follow-up)
