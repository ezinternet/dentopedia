## One-line Summary
Beagle dog split-mouth preclinical study (n=5 dogs, 30 implants, 30° narrow vs 60° wide emergence angle, splinted prostheses, 6-month function): wide emergence angle produced greater crestal bone remodeling (24-wk T-splint 1.4 vs 0.57 mm), larger infiltrated connective tissue, shorter connective tissue attachment (~0.7 vs ~1.1 mm), and reduced peri-implant epithelial collagen — failing to form an adequate soft tissue seal.

## Summary
This is the first preclinical animal study to test how the **restorative prosthetic emergence angle** — not merely abutment geometry — affects peri-implant soft and hard tissues. In a split-mouth design across 5 beagle dogs (30 implants placed equicrestally as three-implant splints per hemi-mandible), each side received custom titanium abutments with either a **narrow (NE, 30°)** or **wide (WE, 60°)** emergence angle, restored with splinted resin crowns and followed for 6 months of function. Radiographs (0/4/12/24 wk), undecalcified histomorphometry, and circularly polarized light collagen quantification were stratified by splint position (T-end, T-splint, M-splint).

The wide emergence angle consistently worsened outcomes: more crestal bone remodeling, larger infiltrated connective tissue, an apically shifted/lengthened junctional epithelium, a shorter connective tissue attachment, and lower collagen fiber fraction in the peri-implant epithelium. The narrow angle formed an adequate biologic width (epithelial ~2.1 mm, connective tissue ~1.1 mm) with firm tissue contact, whereas the wide angle failed to (epithelial ~2.7 mm, connective tissue ~0.7 mm) and showed plaque and inflammatory infiltrate in the implant-tissue gap. The mechanistic chain: wide prosthetic contour → compromised connective tissue seal → bacterial infiltration → bone remodeling.

## Key Contributions
- First animal evidence linking **restorative emergence angle** (30° vs 60°) to histologic soft tissue seal quality and crestal bone remodeling using real splinted prostheses.
- Demonstrated biologic-width failure under wide EA: shorter connective tissue attachment (~0.7 vs ~1.1 mm) and longer junctional epithelium (~2.7 vs ~2.1 mm).
- Quantified greater infiltrated connective tissue and reduced peri-implant epithelial collagen under wide EA, especially at splinted/middle positions.
- Provides a biological mechanism for clinical reports that emergence angles >30° associate with peri-implant bone loss, and that splinted/middle implants are at higher risk.

## Methodology
- **Design**: split-mouth, 5 beagle dogs, 30 implants (3.1 × 7 mm, NR line Dentium), 3 splinted implants per side at equicrestal level, ≥3 mm inter-implant distance, non-submerged.
- **Intervention**: custom grade V titanium abutments, narrow 30° vs wide 60° emergence angle, randomized per hemi-mandible; splinted resin crowns (FujiCEM 2), 30 Ncm, kept out of occlusion; 6 months function.
- **Outcomes**: primary = histologic implant shoulder-to-bone crest distance; radiographic crestal bone remodeling (ImageJ, high vs low at >0.5 mm), histomorphometry (PM/aJE/IS/BC/fBIC, ICT area), circularly polarized light collagen-fiber area fraction (Picrosirius red).
- **Positions**: T-end, T-splint, M-splint. **Statistics**: animal as unit (n=5), nonparametric (Friedman/Dunn, Wilcoxon, Cochran's Q/McNemar-Bonferroni).

## Results
- **Bone remodeling at 24 wk (WE vs NE)**: T-end 0.90 vs 0.12 mm; T-splint 1.4 vs 0.57 mm; M-splint 1.03 vs 0.61 mm — WE greater at every site. All WE splinted zones classified as high (>0.5 mm) remodeling (overall Cochran Q p=0.013; no significant pairwise post hoc).
- **Histomorphometry**: IS-BC distance larger in WE at all sites; ICT area significantly larger in WE at T-splint (2.76 vs 1.34 mm²) and M-splint (3.79 vs 1.56 mm²); connective tissue attachment (aJE-fBIC) significantly longer in NE at T-end (1.10 vs 0.55 mm) and T-splint (1.23 vs 0.83 mm).
- **Biologic width**: NE adequate (epithelial ~2.1 mm, CT ~1.1 mm); WE inadequate (epithelial ~2.7 mm, CT ~0.7 mm).
- **Collagen**: PIE collagen fraction lower in WE (significant at M-splint, 29.2% vs 42.7%); OE collagen declined toward the middle implant in WE (T-end 85.5% > T-splint 54.1% > M-splint 33.8%).
- **Histology**: NE = thin junctional epithelium, firm CT attachment; WE = plaque in the gap, JE down-growth to threads, extensive infiltrated CT.
