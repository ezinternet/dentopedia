## One-line Summary

SR+MA (5 studies, 2 RCTs + 3 prospective, 87 patients): soft tissue augmentation (CAF + CTG/substitutes) for buccal peri-implant soft tissue dehiscence achieves professional esthetic score 7.7/10, 71% complete coverage, and 2.2 mm PSTD depth reduction.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

This 2025 systematic review and meta-analysis from Nart et al. (Periodontology 2000) is the first to specifically pool professional esthetic assessment and patient-reported outcomes (PROs) for soft tissue augmentation (STA) targeting buccal peri-implant soft tissue dehiscence (PSTD). Eight publications across five studies (two RCTs, three prospective) published 2013–2024, totaling 87 patients with ≥6 months follow-up, were included. All studies applied a coronally advanced flap (CAF) with connective tissue graft (CTG) or xenogeneic substitutes; one RCT arm used a tunnel procedure; two studies also modified the prosthetic component. Three of five studies were at low risk of bias. Ten meta-analyses demonstrated that CAF-based STA achieves a professional esthetic score of 7.7/10 (95% CI: 6.63–8.83) and patient-reported esthetics of 60.8/100 VAS (95% CI: 46.56–75.01), with moderate-to-high heterogeneity. PSTD depth was reduced by 2.2 mm (95% CI: 1.76–2.69) and complete coverage was achieved in 71% of cases (95% CI: 59–82%). The authors conclude that STA positively influences esthetic outcomes, but the evidence base remains limited and further large-scale RCTs are needed.

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies professional esthetic outcomes of PSTD treatment: 7.7/10 (CAF-based STA)
- Quantifies patient-reported esthetic satisfaction: 60.8/100 VAS
- Complete PSTD coverage rate: 71% (95% CI: 59–82%)
- PSTD depth reduction: 2.2 mm — clinically meaningful improvement
- Identifies prosthetic modification as adjunct factor (2/5 studies) that may contribute to esthetic outcomes independently of surgery
- Highlights moderate-to-high heterogeneity as a key limitation, signaling need for standardized measurement protocols

**Clinical interpretation**: A professional esthetic score of ~7.7/10 indicates generally good but not perfect esthetic outcomes. Complete coverage in ~7 of 10 treated sites suggests substantial but incomplete predictability. The VAS of ~61/100 for patient satisfaction may reflect residual dissatisfaction in some patients. Moderate-to-high heterogeneity across all esthetic outcomes indicates that patient/site selection and technique variation substantially influence results.
