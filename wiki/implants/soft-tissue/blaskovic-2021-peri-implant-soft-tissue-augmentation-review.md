## Summary

Blašković & Blašković (2021) authored this open-access book chapter as a procedural compendium for peri-implant soft tissue (PIS) augmentation. The chapter synthesizes clinical evidence and surgical principles underlying two primary objectives: (1) restoring adequate keratinized mucosa (KM) width (≥2 mm threshold) and (2) augmenting peri-implant soft tissue volume (≥2 mm thickness). It covers the full graft taxonomy — autogenous (FGG, CTG, sCTG, pedicle), local tissue rearrangement (APF, roll technique, tunneling), and commercial substitutes (xenogeneic collagen matrix) — with step-by-step procedural rationale and graft biology (plasmatic imbibition, transvascularization).

Key thresholds established: KM <2 mm is associated with higher plaque accumulation, peri-implant mucositis, mucosal recession, marginal bone loss, and increased peri-implantitis risk in erratic maintenance-compliers. PIS <2 mm causes visible titanium abutment color through mucosa and triggers biologic-width-driven marginal bone resorption (biologic width = 2.5–4 mm). Graft survival depends on three conditions: rigid immobilization, intimate graft-bed contact (no intervening hematoma), and adequate recipient-site vascularity.

## Key Contributions

- **KM ≥2 mm**: mandatory for oral hygiene access, plaque control, and hard/soft tissue stability around implants; <2 mm in non-compliant patients is associated with increased peri-implantitis risk
- **PIS ≥2 mm**: required to prevent titanium color show-through, and to avoid biologic-width-driven marginal bone resorption
- **Graft biology**: three-phase survival (plasmatic imbibition → early revascularization → transvascularization at day 3–4); uniform graft thickness and periosteal recipient bed are critical
- **CTG gold standard**: subepithelial CTG (sCTG) is gold standard for volume augmentation and esthetic soft tissue coverage due to superior color match vs FGG
- **FGG primary indication**: predictable keratinization (KM width increase); esthetic compromise in visible zones due to palatal color imprint
- **Pedicle grafts**: reduced postoperative shrinkage when blood supply from donor site is preserved; suitable for single-site augmentation
- **APF**: increases KM width via periosteal keratinization without palatal harvest; often combined with grafts
- **Xenogeneic collagen matrices**: eliminate donor-site morbidity; evidence suggests comparable KM width gain but potentially inferior volume gain vs autogenous CTG

## Methodology

Narrative review / educational book chapter (IntechOpen, open access). No formal systematic search protocol, PICO framework, or risk-of-bias assessment. Authors synthesize published RCTs, case series, systematic reviews, and expert technique descriptions. Clinical photographs accompany procedural descriptions.

## Results

| Technique | Primary Goal | Donor Site | Key Trade-off |
|---|---|---|---|
| FGG | KM width | Palate / tuberosity | Esthetic compromise (palatal color) |
| CTG / sCTG | PIS volume, recession | Palate (subepithelial) | Palatal harvest morbidity |
| De-epithelized FGG | KM + volume | Palate FGG → de-epithelized | Larger volume; extra step |
| Pedicle graft | Single-site volume | Adjacent tissue | Less shrinkage; limited by local supply |
| APF | KM width | Local mucosa | Periosteum must be present and vascular |
| Roll technique | Ridge volume | Local palatal CT | No second wound; volume limited |
| Tunneling | Recession/volume | CTG via tunnel | Minimally invasive; technique-sensitive |
| Xenogeneic collagen matrix | KM width / volume | Commercial | No harvest; may have inferior volume vs CTG |
