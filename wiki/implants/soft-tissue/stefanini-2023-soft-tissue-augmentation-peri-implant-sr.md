## One-line Summary

Systematic review of 15 prospective studies (≥36-month follow-up, up to 10 yr) finds that soft tissue augmentation at implants yields survival rates of 90.9–100%, stable soft tissue margins (CTG: −0.06 mm apical shift / 4.8 yr), maintained KMW and MT gains, and stable MBL (≤0.6 mm loss), while non-augmented controls exhibit progressive soft tissue recession averaging 0.96 mm over 6.2 years.

## Summary

This systematic review (Clin Oral Implants Res 2023;34 Suppl 26:28–42) addresses the focused question: do soft tissue augmentation (STA) techniques provide stable and favorable peri-implant conditions in the medium (≥36 months) and long (≥60 months) term? Fifteen prospective studies were included in qualitative analysis. Due to heterogeneity across study designs and outcome measures, no meta-analysis was possible.

STA was performed with either an apically positioned flap (APF) combined with a free gingival graft (FGG), or bilaminar techniques combined with connective tissue graft (CTG), acellular dermal matrix (ADM), or xenogeneic cross-linked collagen matrix (CCM). The overall message is clear: STA gains achieved early are durable. CTG-based bilaminar procedures show the strongest evidence base for medium-to-long term stability, with some sites showing further KMW increase (creeping attachment analogue) and progressive MT gain over time. Non-augmented sites, by contrast, show measurable soft tissue recession that accumulates with time.

## Key Contributions

1. **Medium/long-term STA stability confirmed**: The first SR to focus exclusively on ≥36-month outcomes of peri-implant STA — early gains in KMW and MT are maintained at 3–10 years.
2. **CTG bilaminar superiority for margin stability**: Weighted mean soft tissue margin apical shift of only −0.06 mm over 4.8 years (vs. 0.96 mm recession in non-augmented controls over 6.2 years).
3. **10-year evidence (Seyssens 2020)**: 100% of non-augmented immediately placed implants developed ≥1 mm midfacial recession by 10 years — none of the CTG-augmented sites did.
4. **MBL stability**: Most augmented sites showed marginal bone loss ≤0.6 mm; APF + FGG (Oh 2020, 4 yr) showed significantly better MBL vs. non-augmented.
5. **KMW creeping attachment**: Several CTG-augmented cohorts showed progressive KMW increase beyond 1 year — indicating biological maturation of the grafted tissue.
6. **Graft substitutes (ADM, CCM)**: Early outcomes maintained at 3–5 years, but data limited to 2 cohorts; CTG evidence base is substantially stronger.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review (PRISMA; PROSPERO registered)
- **Search**: MEDLINE, EMBASE, Cochrane CENTRAL, LILACS, Web of Science, Scopus, ClinicalTrials.gov, OpenGrey; search cutoff September 1, 2022
- **Eligibility**: Prospective interventional human studies; ≥10 participants at first follow-up ≥36 months; healthy implants at baseline; STA with FGG, CTG, ADM, or CCM using APF or bilaminar approach
- **Exclusion**: Retrospective, animal, case reports; pre-existing peri-implant disease; simultaneous hard + soft tissue augmentation; smokers-only populations
- **Studies included**: 15 (4 RCTs, 5 non-RCTs, 6 case series)
- **Risk of bias**: Mixed — majority low to moderate; 1 RCT (Oh 2020) low risk
- **Analysis**: Qualitative only — heterogeneity precluded quantitative pooling

## Results

| Outcome | CTG bilaminar | CCM bilaminar | APF + FGG | Non-augmented |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Soft tissue margin shift (wt. mean) | −0.06 mm / 4.8 yr | +0.58 mm / 3–5 yr | +0.54 mm coronal / 4 yr | −0.96 mm / 6.2 yr |
| MBL (wt. mean) | ≤0.63 mm / ~5 yr | ~0.18 mm / ~4 yr | Significantly better than control | Higher loss |
| KMW | Stable or increased | Maintained | Increased | Progressive loss |
| MT / volumetric | Stable or gain to 5 yr | Maintained | N/A | Volume loss at 3 and 5 yr (Hosseini 2020) |
| Survival rate | 90.9%–100% | — | — | — |
| Peri-implantitis | 0%–7.14% | — | — | — |
| Peri-implant mucositis | 0%–50% | — | — | — |

**Key individual study findings**:
- Seyssens 2020 (10 yr, 18 pts): 100% of non-augmented implants ≥1 mm recession; 0% of CTG-augmented
- Roccuzzo 2016 (10 yr, 98 pts): FGG → less brushing discomfort and better MBL vs. no KM or KM without augmentation
- Oh 2020 (4 yr, RCT): APF + FGG → coronal soft tissue creep +0.54 mm + significantly better MBL vs. controls
- Thoma 2020 / 2022 (RCT, 3–5 yr): CTG and CCM both maintained early KMW/MT outcomes; CCM non-inferior at these time points
- Hosseini 2020 (5 yr, 3D scanning): CTG sites volume gain; non-augmented sites volume loss

**Plaque/BOP/PD**: No significant change from early to late time points in any included study. Patient esthetics satisfaction and PES maintained throughout follow-up.
