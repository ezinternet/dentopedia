## One-line Summary

Systematic review (10 prospective studies/case series, all high bias) of second-stage soft-tissue augmentation: APPTF/VP + FGG or XCM best for KM width (up to +9.35 mm); REF or APPTF + SCTG best for volume gain (2.41–3.10 mm); no meta-analysis possible due to heterogeneity.

## Summary

This systematic review by Bassetti et al. (Clin Oral Investig 2016) is the first SR specifically scoped to **second-stage (implant uncovering) surgery** as the timing window for peri-implant soft-tissue augmentation. The review searched MEDLINE and EMBASE through July 2015, identifying 10 studies (8 prospective, 2 case series) — all rated high risk of bias by the Cochrane tool. Heterogeneity in design, graft materials, and outcome measurement precluded meta-analysis.

The review evaluated two distinct goals: (1) increasing **keratinized mucosa (KM) width** and (2) gaining **soft-tissue volume**. Technique selection and outcomes differed by goal and by jaw location.

**Key clinical guidance from this SR:**
- For KM width: APPTF in the maxilla; APPTF/VP + FGG or XCM in both jaws
- For volume: REF in the maxilla; APPTF + SCTG in both jaws
- Jaw location and individual clinical situation are primary determinants of technique selection

## Key Contributions

1. **Timing-specific scope**: First SR exclusively addressing second-stage surgery as the intervention window — distinct from pre-implant or post-loading augmentation.
2. **Dual-outcome framework**: Clearly separated KM width gain vs. soft-tissue volume gain as distinct clinical objectives requiring different surgical approaches.
3. **Jaw-specific recommendations**: Differentiated technique selection by maxilla vs. mandible, reflecting anatomical differences in keratinized tissue availability and flap design options.
4. **Evidence gap signal**: All 10 included studies had high risk of bias; absence of low-bias RCTs at this time point motivated subsequent RCT-level research (cf. Montero 2022, Zhang 2025 consensus).
5. **XCM as FGG alternative**: Documented early evidence that xenogeneic collagen matrix (e.g., Mucograft) at vestibuloplasty could provide acceptable KM gain vs. FGG — a finding later confirmed with better-quality evidence.

## Methodology

- **Databases**: MEDLINE (PubMed) + EMBASE (OVID); hand search
- **Horizon**: Inception to July 31, 2015
- **Population**: Humans; two-stage (submerged) implants at uncovering (second-stage) surgery
- **Interventions**: Any soft-tissue augmentation/correction technique at uncovering
- **Outcomes**: KM width change (mm) and/or soft-tissue volume gain (mm)
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane Collaboration's tool
- **Included**: 8 prospective studies + 2 case series (n = 10 total; all high risk of bias)
- **Meta-analysis**: Not performed — excessive design and measurement heterogeneity

**Techniques in included studies:**
- Apically positioned partial-thickness flap (APPTF) ± vestibuloplasty (VP)
- APPTF/VP + free gingival graft (FGG)
- APPTF/VP + xenogeneic collagen matrix (XCM; porcine)
- Roll envelope flap (REF) / palatal roll technique
- APPTF + subepithelial connective tissue graft (SCTG)
- Rotated palatal flap

## Results

| Technique | Goal | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| APPTF/VP + FGG | KM width | −0.20 to +9.35 mm (widest range; best gains) |
| APPTF/VP + XCM | KM width | Acceptable; comparable to FGG |
| APPTF (maxilla) | KM width | Acceptable in maxilla |
| REF (palatal roll) | Volume | Mean +2.41 mm |
| APPTF + SCTG | Volume | Mean +3.10 mm |

**Jaw-specific conclusions:**
- **KM width, maxilla**: APPTF alone provides acceptable results
- **KM width, both jaws**: APPTF/VP + FGG or XCM provides the most reliable gain
- **Volume, maxilla**: REF is a reliable option
- **Volume, both jaws**: APPTF + SCTG is reliable

**Evidence quality**: All studies high risk of bias; no RCT with low bias included. No quantitative pooling possible.
