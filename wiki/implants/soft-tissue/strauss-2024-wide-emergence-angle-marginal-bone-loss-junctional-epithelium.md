## One-line Summary

Preclinical RCT (6 mongrel dogs, 48 implant sites, 4 emergence-angle groups 20/40/60/80°, 24 wk): wider restorative emergence angle progressively increased marginal bone loss (24-wk MBL ~4× higher at 80° vs 20°) and ≥60° angles impaired junctional-epithelium continuity, supporting keeping the angle <40°.

## 한줄요약

전임상 RCT (개 6마리, 임플란트 48부위, emergence angle 20/40/60/80° 4군, 24주): 보철 emergence angle이 넓을수록 변연골소실(MBL)이 단계적으로 증가했고(80°군 24주 MBL이 20°군의 약 4배), 60° 이상 광각은 접합상피(junctional epithelium) 연속성을 손상시켜 — 각도를 40° 미만으로 유지할 것을 뒷받침.

## Summary

This dog-model study isolates a variable that clinical cross-sectional data could only correlate: the **restorative emergence angle** of the implant prosthesis. Four customized abutments (20°, 40°, 60°, 80°) were placed on bone-level implants and followed 24 weeks with radiography, micro-CT, undecalcified histology, and synchrotron imaging. The result is a clean **dose-response**: marginal bone loss (MBL) rose monotonically with angle, and angles ≥60° degraded the supracrestal soft-tissue seal. It provides a histological mechanism for the long-standing clinical advice to keep the emergence profile narrow, and supports the **<40°** threshold previously chosen on weaker (cross-sectional/cohort) grounds.

## Key Contributions

- First longitudinal preclinical study to test emergence angle on peri-implant **hard AND soft tissue**, including **buccal** sites, with four controlled angles.
- Establishes a dose-dependent angle→MBL relationship, removing the confounding of prior observational data.
- Histological demonstration that wide angles disrupt **junctional-epithelium integrity** and disorganize supracrestal connective tissue — a candidate mechanism for peri-implant disease/recession.
- Multimodal validation (periapical radiograph + micro-CT + histology + synchrotron).

## Methodology

- 6 male mongrel dogs; ARRIVE/3Rs; IACUC-approved (Yonsei, IRB 2022-0174).
- mandibular P1-P4 + M1 extracted, 3-month healing; 4 bone-level implants per hemimandible (Conelog, 7×3.8 mm, ≥15 Ncm), each randomly allocated to a 20°/40°/60°/80° customized abutment placed immediately.
- 24-week follow-up: periapical radiographs at insertion + 6/9/16/24 wk (MBL = shoulder to first BIC; 0.7 mm pitch calibration; ImageJ; ICC 0.99); micro-CT fBIC (mesial/distal/buccal); 24-wk BOP + plaque (PCR); undecalcified histology (48 sites, Toluidine blue) + distance from thickest inflammatory infiltrate to fBIC; synchrotron 3D.
- Statistics: GEE adjusted for baseline soft-tissue thickness (Bonferroni); Kruskal-Wallis + Dunn for histomorphometry.

## Results

- All implants osseointegrated; no failures or complications over 24 wk.
- **MBL increased over time** (p<0.001) with a **significant angle effect** (p<0.001) and time×angle interaction (p<0.001); 80° greatest. Baseline soft-tissue thickness had no significant influence (p=0.915).
- **24-wk mean MBL** — 20°: 0.07 ± 0.20 mm · 40°: 0.11 ± 0.27 mm · **60°: 0.23 ± 0.31 mm (>2×)** · **80°: 0.38 ± 0.27 mm (~4× the 20° value)**.
- **Micro-CT fBIC (mesial/distal)**: 0.10 / 0.13 / 0.20 / 0.40 mm (p<0.0001). Buccal fBIC numerically higher with angle but NS (p=0.637).
- **Plaque** rose with angle (66.6 / 66.6 / 91.7 / 100%); BOP 14 / 25 / 31 / 25% (60° highest; wide 80° likely under-probed).
- **Histology**: 20°/40° → continuous, intact junctional epithelium + organized connective tissue; **60°/80° → disorganized connective tissue, more inflammatory infiltrate, partial/absent junctional epithelium**, biofilm against abutment. Distance from infiltrate to buccal fBIC **shortened** with wider angle (1.69 / 1.67 / 1.09 / 1.00 mm; 20° vs 80° p=0.065) — fits Waerhaug's ~1 mm plaque radius-of-action.
- **Threshold**: keep the restorative angle **as narrow as clinically possible (<40°)**.
- **Caveats**: preclinical, unloaded (no zirconia crown), small/short, per-hemimandible randomization imbalance (40° outliers at narrow P2), possible histologic artefact in junctional-epithelium impairment.
