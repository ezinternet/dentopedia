## One-line Summary

RCT 15–17-year follow-up (n=60, 151 implants; Zurich) comparing one-piece Straumann SLA vs two-piece Brånemark TiUnite implants: cumulative survival 95%, one-piece superior marginal bone preservation (MBL 0.08 mm vs 1.53 mm) but 6× higher technical complication rate (35.4% vs 5.8% implant-level).

## Summary

This study reports the 15–17-year outcomes of a randomized clinical trial (RCT) conducted at the University of Zürich comparing one-piece Straumann SLA implants (STM) with two-piece Brånemark TiUnite implants (BRA) in 60 patients (151 implants). After a mean follow-up of 16.4 years, the overall cumulative survival rate was 95% (STM 91.84%; BRA 98.04%). The one-piece system showed markedly better marginal bone preservation (MBL 0.08 ± 1.15 mm vs 1.53 ± 0.81 mm), but significantly more technical complications at the implant level (35.4% vs 5.8%). Peri-implantitis prevalence was 13.25% overall, slightly higher in the STM group. The results reveal a biological vs. technical trade-off between implant designs, and caution that the BRA system studied is an older-generation two-piece design, not reflective of modern internal-connection/platform-switching implants.

## Key Contributions

- Longest published RCT follow-up comparing one-piece vs two-piece dental implants (15–17 years)
- Demonstrates a clear trade-off: one-piece = superior marginal bone preservation; two-piece = fewer technical complications
- Cumulative survival 95%: STM 91.84% vs BRA 98.04% (5 total losses, all from peri-implant disease)
- MBL at 15–17y: STM 0.08 mm vs BRA 1.53 mm — elimination of implant-abutment interface proposed as mechanism
- Technical complications: STM 35.4% vs BRA 5.8% at implant level — screw loosening and matrix renewals predominate in STM
- Peri-implant mucositis cumulative rate: STM 70.77% vs BRA 33.72% (many resolved); peri-implantitis patient-level: STM 23.33% vs BRA 16.67%
- BOP higher in STM group throughout; PPD and PCR comparable
- Cautions against extrapolating BRA findings to modern two-piece systems (internal connection, platform-switching)

## Methodology

- **Study design:** 15–17-year follow-up of a previously published RCT
- **Setting:** Clinic for Reconstructive Dentistry, University of Zürich, Switzerland
- **Sample:** 60 patients randomized to STM (one-piece Straumann SLA, n=30, 65 implants) or BRA (two-piece Brånemark TiUnite, n=30, 86 implants)
- **Randomization:** Computer-generated list
- **Implant placement:** Crestal position per system guidelines; bone grafting if needed; transmucosal or submerged healing per surgeon preference
- **Restorations:** 93 fixed + 6 removable; 103 screw-retained + 31 cement-retained
- **Follow-up timepoints:** Baseline (final restoration), 1y, 5y, 10y, 12y, 15y, 17y
- **Final cohort:** 39 patients / 95 implants; mean FU 16.43 years (±1.06)
- **Attrition:** 21 patients (56 implants) — no significant baseline differences vs completers
- **Outcomes:** PPD, PCR, BOP (6-site per implant); MBL via paralleling radiograph + Image J; all technical complications; esthetic parameters
- **Statistics:** Primarily descriptive at final timepoint; t-tests and chi-square for attrition analysis

## Results

### Survival

| Group | Implants placed | Lost | Survival Rate |
|-------|----------------|------|---------------|
| STM (one-piece Straumann) | 65 | 4 | 91.84% |
| BRA (two-piece Brånemark) | 86 | 1 | 98.04% |
| Overall | 151 | 5 | 95.0% |

All losses: peri-implant disease with progressive bone loss.

### Marginal Bone Levels (positive = bone loss, relative to reference point)

| Timepoint | STM Mean (SD) | BRA Mean (SD) |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|
| Baseline | 0.61 mm (0.82) | 1.07 mm (0.51) |
| 5 years | 0.62 mm (0.77) | 1.39 mm (0.44) |
| 10 years | 0.03 mm (1.08) | 1.38 mm (0.52) |
| 15–17 years | **0.08 mm (1.15)** | **1.53 mm (0.81)** |

Note: STM reference point = rough-smooth border (at bone crest); BRA reference = shoulder (at crest). Patient-level MBL change BL→FU5: STM +0.14 mm, BRA +0.32 mm.

### Biological Parameters (implant-level)

| Parameter | STM 15/17y | BRA 15/17y |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| PPD | 3.79 mm (±1.01) | 3.51 mm (±1.06) |
| PCR | 14.34% (±24.82) | 13.18% (±19.77) |
| BOP | 45.63% (±30.81) | 38.33% (±25.37) |

- **Peri-implant mucositis** (cumulative, entire observation): 75 implants / 34 patients; STM implant-level 70.77%, BRA 33.72%; after treatment/resolution, remaining rate 25.83% of all implants
- **Peri-implantitis** (cumulative): 20 implants / 12 patients; overall 13.25% implant-level; patient-level STM 23.33% (7/30), BRA 16.67% (5/30)

### Technical Complications (entire observation period)

| Metric | STM | BRA |
|--------|-----|-----|
| Total events | 34 | 6 |
| Implants affected | 23/65 | 5/86 |
| Implant-level rate | **35.38%** | **5.81%** |
| Patients affected | 9/30 | 4/30 |
| Patient-level rate | 30% | 13.33% |

STM complication types: 11 matrix renewals, 10 screw loosenings, 5 minor chippings, 2 major chippings, 4 screw access hole restoration losses, 1 debonding, 1 ball attachment renewal.
BRA complication types: 4 minor chippings, 1 major chipping, 1 screw access hole restoration loss.
**No implant fractures, screw fractures, abutment fractures, or framework fractures** in either group.

### Esthetic Observations

- Mucosal shimmering: 54 implants (STM 22, BRA 32)
- Crown margin visible: 62 implants (STM 31, BRA 32)
- Peri-implant mucosal margin more apical than contralateral: 92 implants total
