## One-line Summary

12-month RCT (n=36) comparing triangular vs round implant neck design in immediately placed anterior maxillary implants: no significant differences in MBL, probing depth, buccal bone width, or soft tissue thickness; 88.9% survival in both groups.

## Summary

This 12-month RCT enrolled 36 patients requiring tooth extraction in the anterior maxilla and immediately placed implants with immediate provisionalization. Patients were randomized to receive either a triangular neck (TN) implant (test) or a round neck (RN) implant (control). The triangular neck was hypothesized to provide better distribution of occlusal forces and potentially enhanced preservation of crestal bone and soft tissues compared to the conventional round collar design.

Outcome measures at surgery included insertion torque, ISQ, crestal width, and soft tissue thickness at 3 mm and 5 mm from the gingival margin. CBCT assessed buccal bone width at baseline and 4 months (time of definitive crown placement). Clinical parameters (MBL, PD, BOP, CW, STT) were recorded at 4 and 12 months postoperatively.

Two implants per group failed to integrate, yielding 88.9% survival in both the TN and RN groups at 12 months. No statistically significant between-group differences were detected for any outcome at any time point. The authors note that the study was not designed or powered for equivalence testing, and that results are consistent with small undetected differences that cannot be ruled out.

## Key Contributions

- RCT evidence for a novel triangular neck implant design in the immediate placement context
- Null result at 12 months: TN did not demonstrate superiority over RN for any hard or soft tissue parameter
- Establishes that both designs support similar short-term survival (88.9%) in anterior maxillary immediate placement with provisionalization
- Highlights the need for adequately powered equivalence/non-inferiority trials to make definitive claims about neck geometry

## Methodology

- **Design**: Parallel-group RCT
- **n**: 36 patients (18 TN, 18 RN)
- **Site**: Anterior maxilla; immediate placement + immediate provisionalization
- **Imaging**: CBCT for buccal bone width (day 0 and 4 months)
- **Clinical parameters**: IT, ISQ, CW, STT (day 0); MBL, PD, BOP, CW, STT (4 months, 12 months)
- **Registration**: NCT05944419
