## One-line Summary

SR+MA (24 studies, n=2,637 patients, 5,594 tilted + 5,611 axial implants, 3–18yr follow-up): implant survival 93.91%, prosthesis survival 99.31%; marginal bone loss significantly LOWER around axial vs tilted implants (P<.0001); arch (maxilla vs mandible) did not affect MBL.

## Summary

This is the most comprehensive SR+MA (as of 2022) evaluating fixed full-arch prostheses supported by tilted and axially placed implants in edentulous patients, requiring ≥3 years of follow-up.

**Population**: 2,637 patients, 2,735 full prostheses (1,464 maxillary, 1,271 mandibular), supported by 5,594 tilted + 5,611 axial implants across 24 studies.

**Survival outcomes:**
- Cumulative implant survival rate: **93.91%**
- Prosthesis survival rate: **99.31%**
- 274 implants failed over 3–18 years

**Marginal bone loss (key finding):**
- Mean MBL was moderate; exceeded 2mm in only 2 studies
- **Axial implants showed significantly LOWER marginal bone loss than tilted implants (P<.0001)**
- This contrasts with Del Fabbro & Ceresoli 2014 (which found NS difference at 1yr)
- The maxilla vs mandible comparison showed NO significant difference in MBL (P=.17)

**Clinical implication**: Fixed full-arch rehabilitation on mixed tilted + axial implants is a predictable, long-term viable option. However, tilted implants may carry a marginal bone loss penalty compared to axial implants over longer follow-up periods. This should factor into placement strategy when anatomy permits axial placement.

## Key Contributions

- Largest SR+MA with longest follow-up (≥3yr, range to 18yr) on this topic
- First to show P<.0001 significant MBL difference: axial < tilted
- Resolves the 2014 NS finding with larger dataset and longer follow-up

## Methodology

- Electronic + hand search through April 2021; PRISMA guidelines
- 824 articles retrieved → 24 included
- Main outcomes: cumulative implant survival, marginal bone level changes, complications at ≥3yr
- Meta-analysis + Mantel-Cox test for tilted vs axial and maxilla vs mandible
