## One-line Summary

SR+MA (5 studies; 282 NDIs, 100 RDIs) finding narrow-diameter implants yield implant survival, MBL, and PPD comparable to regular-diameter implants with bone augmentation in the anterior maxilla over 36 months.

## 한줄요약

5편(NDI 282개, RDI 100개) 체계적 고찰·메타분석. 전치부 상악에서 좁은 직경 임플란트(NDI)는 골증대 동반 정규 직경(RDI)과 36개월 생존율·MBL·PPD가 동등했고 치료기간·심미 회복이 빠른 이점.

## Summary

This SR+MA addresses the esthetic-zone trade-off: in the narrow anterior maxilla, clinicians can either place a regular-diameter implant after bone augmentation or place a narrow-diameter implant directly. Pooling 5 studies (282 NDIs, 100 RDIs), implant survival, marginal bone loss, and pocket probing depth were equivalent at 36 months, and — notably — soft tissue dehiscence, the esthetic-zone complication clinicians most fear, was actually most common in the RDI (augmentation) group. The authors position NDIs as preferable in the esthetic zone given equivalent outcomes plus shorter treatment time and faster esthetic improvement, with the caveat that follow-up is short and the RDI arm small.

## Key Contributions

- Restricts NDI vs RDI to the esthetic anterior maxilla with augmentation as comparator.
- Shows soft tissue dehiscence concentrated in RDIs, not NDIs.
- Frames NDI as a graft-avoidance, faster-esthetics option in the esthetic zone.

## Methodology

SR+MA (RCTs + cohort studies, ≥12-month follow-up), search to February 2024. NDI vs RDI+bone augmentation in anterior maxilla. 288 records → 5 studies (282 NDIs, 100 RDIs). Random-effects when ≥2 comparable studies.

## Results

- ISR at 36 months: NDI 93.8–100%, RDI 100%; no difference (RR 0.989; 95% CI 0.839–1.165; p=0.896).
- MBL, PPD: no significant difference.
- Soft tissue dehiscence: most common complication, in the RDI group.
