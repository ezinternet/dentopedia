## One-line Summary

FEA (All-on-4 edentulous mandible, 17°/30°/45° × screw/cement): increasing posterior implant angulation raises bone/implant stress proportionally, but all values remain safe (<550 MPa); cement-retained frameworks carry >3.5× less stress than screw-retained counterparts.

## 한줄요약

유한요소분석(무치악 하악 All-on-4, 경사 17°/30°/45° × 나사/시멘트): 경사 증가 → 골·임플란트 응력 비례 증가하나 모두 안전 범위(<550 MPa); 시멘트 유지 프레임워크가 나사 유지 대비 3.5배 이상 응력 낮음.

## Summary

This finite element analysis evaluated stress distribution in peri-implant bone, implants, and frameworks across six All-on-4 models using single-piece bendable (Mode Provo) implants — varying posterior inclination (17°, 30°, 45°) and prosthesis retention type (screw vs cement) in an edentulous mandible model.

**Key findings:**
1. **Angulation effect**: Increasing posterior implant inclination raises both tensile (Pmax) and compressive (Pmin) stresses in peri-implant bone, and von Mises stress in implants — proportionally and consistently across models.
2. **Safe range confirmed**: The highest recorded implant stress was 108.6 MPa (45°, vertical load), far below the titanium yield strength of 550 MPa. No failure risk at any angulation tested.
3. **Screw vs cement**: Screw-retained frameworks generated >3.5× higher framework stress than cement-retained (e.g., 75.3 MPa vs ~20 MPa). This difference is clinically relevant for mechanical complication risk.
4. **Loading direction**: Posterior implants experience higher stress under vertical loading; anterior implants experience higher stress under oblique loading.
5. **Stress concentration**: In posterior implants, stress concentrates at the mesial neck region; in anterior implants, at the buccal crestal region.

**Clinical implication**: For edentulous full-arch cases requiring high-angulation (especially 30–45°) posterior implants, cement-retained prosthesis may reduce biomechanical stress on the framework and peri-implant bone — though screw-retained prostheses retain the advantage of retrievability.

## Key Contributions

- First FEA specifically evaluating BENDABLE single-piece implants at three angulations in All-on-4 configuration
- Demonstrates that all inclinations (17°–45°) are biomechanically safe in bendable one-piece systems
- Quantifies the stress penalty of screw retention vs cement retention at each angulation level

## Methodology

- 3D mandible model from CT (Visible Human Project), 2mm cortical + trabecular core
- Six FEA models: 17°/30°/45° distal inclination × screw/cement retention
- Two anterior vertical implants (3.5mm × 12mm) + two posterior tilted (3.5mm × 15mm)
- CoCr framework with 14mm standardized distal cantilever
- Loading: 150N vertical + 150N oblique (75°) to premolar–molar region
- Outcome: Pmax, Pmin (bone); von Mises stress (implants, framework)

## Results

| Inclination | Retention | Posterior Pmin oblique (MPa) | Posterior VM implant (MPa) | Framework VM (MPa) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 17° | Screw | — | — | 75.3 |
| 17° | Cement | −32.6 | 72.8 | ~20 |
| 45° | Screw | −75.1 | 108.6 | ~72 |
