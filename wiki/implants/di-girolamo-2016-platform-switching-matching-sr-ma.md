## One-line Summary
SR+MA (15 publications, 1439 implants, 642 patients) comparing platform switching (PS) vs platform matching (PM): most studies showed significantly less marginal bone loss (MBL) around PS implants with no difference in implant failure rates, confirming PS as effective for limiting crestal bone resorption.

## Summary
SR+MA comparing platform switching (PS) vs platform matching (PM) implants. 15 publications, 1439 implants, 642 patients eligible. More studies showed significantly less marginal bone loss (MBL) around PS implants. No differences in implant failure rates between groups. Supports platform switching as effective for preventing marginal bone resorption.

## Key Contributions
- 15 RCTs included; 1439 implants, 642 patients
- PS implants: consistently lower MBL vs PM across most studies
- No significant differences in implant survival/failure rates
- Platform switching effective technique to prevent marginal bone resorption
- Heterogeneity of included studies requires cautious interpretation

## Methodology
Systematic review + meta-analysis. English-language RCTs only. Inclusion: ≥1 PS vs PM group, ≥12 months follow-up post-loading, ≥10 implants, quantitative MBL data provided. Outcomes: implant failure rates and marginal bone loss.

## Results
- 15 publications met inclusion criteria (1439 implants, 642 patients)
- Most studies: significantly less MBL around PS implants
- No studies showed significantly worse outcomes for PS vs PM
- Implant failure rates: no statistically significant differences
- Conclusion: PS technique effectively limits crestal bone resorption
