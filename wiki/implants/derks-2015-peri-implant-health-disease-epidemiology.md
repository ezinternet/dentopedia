## One-line Summary
SR+MA (11 studies): weighted mean population prevalence of peri-implant mucositis 43% (95% CI 32–54%) and peri-implantitis 22% (95% CI 14–30%), with extreme heterogeneity (I²=96–98%); prevalence rises with longer implant function time and varies strongly with the case definition (bone-loss threshold) used.

## 한줄요약
SR+MA (11개 연구): 임플란트 주위 점막염 유병률 43%, 임플란트 주위염 22%; 기능 기간이 길수록 유병률 증가, 케이스 정의에 따라 큰 차이.

## Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis of 11 studies (15 articles) estimating population-level prevalence of peri-implant diseases. Weighted mean prevalence: peri-implant mucositis 43% (CI 32–54%), peri-implantitis 22% (CI 14–30%). Both estimates show extreme heterogeneity (I²=96–98%) attributed to inconsistent case definitions. Meta-regression confirms longer function time and higher bone-loss threshold both predict higher apparent peri-implantitis prevalence.

## Key Contributions
- Established benchmark prevalence figures: ~43% mucositis, ~22% peri-implantitis
- Quantified effect of case definition choice on reported prevalence
- Demonstrated prevalence of peri-implantitis increases with implant function time
- Called for standardised case definitions — addressed by Berglundh 2018 consensus

## Methodology
- PRISMA-compliant SR; search March 2014 (MEDLINE, EMBASE, Cochrane)
- Inclusion: ≥100 subjects, subject-level prevalence data
- Binary random-effects meta-analyses; I² for heterogeneity
- Meta-regression: function time and bone-loss threshold as moderators

## Results
| Outcome | Range across studies | Weighted mean (95% CI) | I² |
|---------|---------------------|----------------------|-----|
| Peri-implant mucositis | 19–65% | 43% (32–54%) | 96% |
| Peri-implantitis | 1–47% | 22% (14–30%) | 98% |

- Peri-implantitis prevalence increases with function time
- Higher bone loss threshold used in definition → lower apparent prevalence
