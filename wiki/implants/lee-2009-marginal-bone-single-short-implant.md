## One-line Summary

Retrospective radiographic study of 40 single Brånemark short implants (<10 mm) at 1-year follow-up found marginal bone loss comparable to standard implants, with crown-to-implant ratio (C:I) exerting no statistically significant effect on bone loss.

## 한줄요약

단근 단기 임플란트(10 mm 미만) 40개의 1년 방사선 추적 결과, 변연골 소실은 표준 임플란트와 유의한 차이가 없었고 치관-임플란트 비율(C:I ratio)도 골 소실에 유의한 영향을 미치지 않았다.

## Summary

This retrospective Korean study from Yonsei University (Kangnam Severance Hospital) evaluated marginal bone level changes in 40 single short implant cases (<10 mm, Brånemark machined-surface system) selected from 389 short implants placed at Yongdong Severance Dental Hospital from 2003. Radiographic marginal bone loss was measured at 1 year using UTHSCSA ImageTool and compared against standard implants (≥10 mm). Additionally, the crown-to-implant (C:I) ratio was calculated and its influence on marginal bone loss was assessed.

Key finding: short implants demonstrated marginal bone changes not significantly different from standard implants at 1 year. C:I ratio subgroup analysis (C:I ≤1 vs. >1) likewise showed no statistically significant differences in bone loss, suggesting that an elevated C:I ratio inherent to short implants does not independently accelerate marginal bone resorption within the first year of loading.

## Key Contributions

- Early Korean institutional data directly comparing short vs. standard single implant marginal bone behavior at 1 year.
- C:I ratio analysis on single short implants — clinically important because short implants inherently produce higher C:I ratios in areas of reduced bone height.
- Provides historical reference for machined-surface Brånemark short implants, enabling comparison with modern roughened-surface short implant outcomes.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Retrospective case selection from 389 short implants placed at single Korean academic center (2003 onwards)
- **n**: 40 single short implant cases (<10 mm); compared to standard implant controls (≥10 mm)
- **Implant system**: Brånemark system, machined surface, hexed/headed fixture
- **Measurement tool**: UTHSCSA ImageTool (0.1 mm calibration); periapical radiographs
- **C:I ratio groups**: ≤1 vs. >1 (crown length from occlusal surface to bone crest ÷ intrabony implant length)
- **Statistics**: SAS v8; p<0.05 significance threshold
- **Convention**: positive values = bone loss; negative values = bone apposition

## Results

- **Marginal bone loss**: Short implant group showed marginal bone loss not statistically different from standard implant group at 1 year (p > 0.05).
- **C:I ratio effect**: Neither C:I ≤1 nor C:I >1 subgroups showed significant differences in marginal bone loss (all groups NS).
- **Bone apposition**: Observed in some cases in both groups (negative values).
- **Limitation of design**: Single-unit restorations only; splinting effects not evaluable.
