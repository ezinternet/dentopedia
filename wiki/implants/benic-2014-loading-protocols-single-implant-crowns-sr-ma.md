## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 11 RCTs finding immediately and conventionally loaded single-implant crowns equally successful for implant survival (1-yr OR 0.75, 95% CI 0.32–1.76) and marginal bone loss (SMD −0.05 mm) through 5 years, valid for implants placed at ≥20–45 Ncm torque or ISQ ≥60–65 without simultaneous augmentation.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

This meta-analysis of 11 RCTs established that immediately and conventionally loaded single-implant crowns achieve equivalent implant survival and marginal bone loss from 1 through 5 years, with no difference in papilla level. Critically, this equivalence holds for implants placed with adequate primary stability — insertion torque ≥20–45 Ncm or ISQ ≥60–65 — and without simultaneous bone augmentation, defining the clinical envelope for safe immediate loading of single crowns. Buccal mucosa recession evidence was contradictory and inconclusive.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates immediate ≈ conventional loading for single crowns on survival and MBL.
- Specifies the primary-stability threshold (torque ≥20–45 Ncm or ISQ ≥60–65) governing the conclusion.

## Methodology

- SR+MA of RCTs (≥1-yr follow-up); Medline/Embase to 2012; OR and SMD with 95% CI.

## Results

- 1-yr survival OR 0.75 (0.32–1.76); 1-yr MBL SMD −0.05 mm (−0.41 to 0.31).
- No survival/MBL differences at 2/3/5 yr; no papilla difference.
