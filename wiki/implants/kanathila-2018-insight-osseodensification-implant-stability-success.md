## 한줄요약
서술적 종설 (narrative review) — 골밀도화 (Osseodensification, OD)의 개념을 정리: 다중날 (multi-fluted) Densah 버 (bur)를 반시계방향으로 회전시켜 골 (bone)을 절삭하지 않고 압축·자가이식 (autograft)하며, 저밀도 골에서 삽입 토크 (Insertion Torque, IT)가 약 49 Ncm로 일반 드릴링의 약 25 Ncm 대비 상승했다고 보고. 임상 적용 한계: 발명자(Huwais) 측 연구·인용에 크게 의존하고 정형 systematic review가 아니므로 정량적 효과 크기는 후속 메타분석 (Meta-Analysis)으로 보완 필요.

## Summary
Kanathila and Pangi's 2018 review in JCDR is an accessible conceptual introduction to osseodensification (OD), the non-extraction osteotomy technique developed by Salah Huwais. The article explains how specially designed tapered, multi-fluted Densah burs rotate counterclockwise to compact rather than excavate bone, autografting the displaced bone debris into the walls of the osteotomy. The mechanistic claim is that this dense, autografted peri-osteotomy zone provides both immediate mechanical interlock (primary stability) and a richer biological matrix for subsequent osseointegration (secondary stability).

The review situates OD within the broader low-density-bone toolkit: underpreparation (10% undersized last drill), stepped osteotomy, and Summers osteotome bone condensation. It cites two key preclinical anchors — Trisi et al.'s sheep iliac-crest study (30% increase in bone volume percentage, ridge expansion, trabecular thickening coronally) and Lahens et al.'s in-vivo histometry showing improved 6-week BIC — plus Huwais's own oft-quoted torque comparison (~49 Ncm with OD vs ~25 Ncm with conventional drilling in low-density bone).

As a 2018 narrative review, this paper is best read as a primer on the *concept and rationale* of OD. For evidence synthesis, the SR+MAs of Gaikwad (2022), Gaspar (2022), Lima Monteiro (2024), and Kalra (2025) — all in this wiki — are now the preferred references. The Kanathila review remains useful for the clinical "why" but should not be cited as the primary evidence base.

## Key Contributions
- Frames OD as a non-extraction, compaction-and-autograft osteotomy technique distinct from conventional cutting drills, underpreparation, and Summers osteotomes.
- Explains the bur kinematics: counterclockwise = densifying; clockwise = extraction; tapered geometry allows lift-away for irrigation.
- Cites Trisi sheep iliac-crest data: 30% bone volume gain, trabeculae thickened coronally.
- Cites Lahens et al.: improved primary stability and 6-week BIC with OD, direction-independent within the OD bur geometry.
- Quotes the inventor's 49 Ncm (OD) vs 25 Ncm (conventional) torque comparison in low-density bone — the dominant numerical claim in the early OD literature.
- Notes the spring-back / elastic-recovery mechanism producing an osteotomy diameter smaller than the bur, contributing to interference fit.

## Methodology
Narrative review with no formal search strategy, eligibility criteria, or quality appraisal. The authors summarise the Huwais OD concept (US Patent 2013), describe Densah bur geometry and counterclockwise kinematics, and present supporting evidence from a small number of preclinical and bench studies (Huwais 2017, Trisi et al., Lahens et al.). No quantitative pooling is performed.

## Results
- **Insertion torque (cited from Huwais)**: OD ≈ 49 Ncm vs conventional ≈ 25 Ncm in low-density bone.
- **Trisi sheep iliac-crest (cited)**: 30% increase in bone volume %; ridge width expanded; coronal trabeculae thickened.
- **Lahens et al. (cited)**: OD raised IT and 6-week histometric BIC; effect was direction-independent within the OD bur geometry — implicating bur geometry, not just rotation direction.
- **Underpreparation alternative**: 10% undersized last drill sufficient; further undersizing offers no additional gain.
- ISQ, MBL, and implant survival numbers not provided in this review.
