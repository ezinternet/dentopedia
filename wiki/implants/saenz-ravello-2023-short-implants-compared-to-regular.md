## One-line Summary
Umbrella review + meta-analysis (15 primary studies, 281 patients, 595 implants) in the atrophic posterior mandible: short implants (<10 mm) in native bone may reduce implant failure, MBL, and biological complications vs regular implants placed after bone augmentation, with patient preference favouring short implants — though GRADE certainty is very low to low.

## Summary
Umbrella review + meta-analysis comparing **short implants (<10 mm)** placed in native bone vs. **regular implants (≥10 mm)** placed after bone augmentation (BA) in the atrophic posterior mandible. Based on 595 implants and 281 patients from 15 primary studies (all Italian RCTs/cohort), short implants may offer comparable or superior outcomes with less morbidity.

## Key Contributions
- Short implants **may reduce implant failure** at 1-year vs. regular+BA
- Short implants **may reduce MBL** at 3, 5, and 8 years
- Short implants **likely reduce biological complications** at all follow-up timepoints
- Bone height correlates with MBL and complication rate — lower native bone = worse outcomes for both groups
- Patients tend to **prefer short implants** (less surgery, faster recovery, lower cost)
- Overall evidence certainty: **very low to low** (GRADE) — conclusions are provisional

## Methodology
- **Design**: Umbrella review (phase 1: 21 SR/MAs via AMSTAR-2) + meta-analysis of 15 primary studies (phase 2)
- **Short implant definition**: <10 mm in this review (literature varies: <4 to <8 mm in other studies)
- **BA techniques compared**: Distraction osteogenesis, inlay/onlay bone block, guided bone regeneration (GBR)
- **Follow-up range**: 1–8 years
- **Risk of bias**: Most SRs critically low/low (AMSTAR-2); primary studies high risk (RoB 2.0)

## Results Summary

| Outcome | Short vs. Regular+BA | Certainty |
|---|---|---|
| Implant failure (1 yr) | Favors short | Very low |
| MBL (3, 5, 8 yr) | Favors short | Low |
| Biological complications (1–8 yr) | Favors short | Low |
| Patient preference | Favors short | Low |
| Prosthetic complications | No difference | Very low |

## Clinical Takeaways
- In atrophic posterior mandible, **short implants are a valid first-line alternative** to BA + regular implants
- Avoids BA morbidity (complication rate for GBR: 5.8–27.3%; bone blocks: 2.5–100%)
- Lower bone height → worse prognosis for both groups; consider patient selection carefully
- Short implant definition inconsistency (4–10 mm) makes direct comparisons across studies difficult
