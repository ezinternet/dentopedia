## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1007/s00784-020-03210-x](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00784-020-03210-x)), this systematic review with meta-analysis (abstract-only — full text not retrieved) evaluated whether **titanium healing-abutment surface modifications** (machined vs anodized/laser/other treatments) affect peri-implant soft-tissue healing, inflammation, and maintenance. A database search through 30 April 2019 yielded 10 studies; 6 short-follow-up studies (4 RCT, 2 CCT; 118 patients, 182 implants) were poolable via random-effects (DerSimonian–Laird) meta-analysis. Modified surfaces showed **no significant short-term difference** vs controls in plaque index (P = 0.091), bleeding on probing (P = 0.099), or probing depth (P = 0.488), with no significant moderator effects. The 4 long-follow-up studies (5–6 y) reported **contradictory** results depending on the surface-modification technique.

**Clinically:** abutment **surface treatment** is not a reliable lever on peri-implant soft-tissue inflammation in the short term — do not expect a modified titanium surface to reduce plaque, bleeding, or pocket depth over routine timeframes. Long-term evidence is too conflicting to support a recommendation; the choice should rest on other factors until validated by long-term studies.

## Key Contributions

- Isolates abutment **surface** (vs shape/customization) as the determinant tested for peri-implant soft-tissue behavior.
- Provides a random-effects meta-analytic answer: null short-term effect on PI, BoP, and PD.
- Documents that long-term (5–6 y) evidence is contradictory and technique-dependent — a caution flag rather than a green light.

## Methodology

- Systematic review + random-effects meta-analysis (DerSimonian–Laird estimator), search through **30 April 2019**.
- **10 studies** reviewed; **6 pooled** (4 RCT + 2 CCT), **118 patients**, **182 implants**, short follow-up, modified healing-abutment surface.
- **4 studies** with 5–6 y follow-up too heterogeneous/contradictory to pool.
- Mixed-effects moderator analysis: study type, study design, number of implants, follow-up length.

## Results

- **Plaque index (PI):** no significant difference between surfaces — **P = 0.091**.
- **Bleeding on probing (BoP):** no significant difference — **P = 0.099**.
- **Probing depth (PD):** no significant difference — **P = 0.488**.
- **Moderators:** no significant effect for any mixed-effects moderator (study type, design, implant number, follow-up length).
- **Long-term (4 studies, 5–6 y):** contradictory results depending on the surface-modification technique.
- **Conclusion:** peri-implant soft tissue may not be affected by titanium abutment surface treatment short-term; long-term findings need validation.
