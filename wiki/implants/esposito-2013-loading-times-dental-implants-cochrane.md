## Summary

This 5th update of the Cochrane review on loading times for dental implants synthesizes evidence from 26 RCTs (1217 participants, 2120 implants) comparing immediate loading (within 1 week), early loading (1 week–2 months), and conventional loading (after 2 months). The review found no convincing evidence of clinically important differences in prosthesis failure, implant failure, or marginal bone loss between the loading protocols. The mean implant failure rate across all included trials was low at 2.5%. Evidence quality was rated very low (GRADE) due to high or unclear risk of bias in most primary studies and evidence of reporting bias. The authors identify insertion torque ≥35 Ncm as a key prerequisite for successful immediate/early loading.

## Key Contributions

- Highest-quality (Cochrane) synthesis of RCTs on implant loading timing through June 2012
- Standardized definitions: immediate (≤1 week), early (1 week–2 months), conventional (>2 months)
- No significant difference in prosthesis failure (RR 1.90; 95% CI 0.67–5.34) or implant failure (RR 1.50; 95% CI 0.60–3.77) for immediate vs conventional loading
- Statistically significant but clinically negligible bone level advantage for immediate loading (MD −0.10 mm; P=0.03)
- Insertion torque ≥35 Ncm identified as prerequisite for immediate/early loading
- Overall implant failure rate: 2.5% across all 26 trials

## Methodology

- **Design**: Cochrane systematic review with meta-analysis (pub5 — 5th update)
- **Search**: CENTRAL, MEDLINE, EMBASE through June 2012; no language/date restrictions
- **Inclusion**: RCTs of root-form osseointegrated implants; follow-up 4 months–1 year; comparing immediate/early/conventional or occlusal/non-occlusal/progressive loading
- **Outcomes**: Prosthesis failure, implant failure, radiographic marginal bone level change
- **Analysis**: Fixed-effect model; RR for dichotomous; MD for continuous; 95% CI; GRADE quality assessment
- **Bias**: 2/26 trials low risk; 12 high risk; 12 unclear

## Results

| Comparison | Outcome | Effect | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Immediate vs Conventional (15 RCTs) | Prosthesis failure | RR 1.90 (0.67–5.34) | NS |
| Immediate vs Conventional (15 RCTs) | Implant failure | RR 1.50 (0.60–3.77) | NS |
| Immediate vs Conventional (9 RCTs) | MBL change | MD −0.10 mm (−0.20 to −0.01) | P=0.03; I²=44% |
| Early vs Conventional (3 RCTs) | All outcomes | Insufficient evidence | — |
| Immediate vs Early (6 RCTs) | All outcomes | Insufficient evidence | — |
| Occlusal vs Non-occlusal (2 RCTs) | All outcomes | Insufficient evidence | — |

- Overall implant failure rate: 2.5% (mean across 26 trials)
- GRADE evidence quality: very low for all comparisons
- No progressive loading trials identified
