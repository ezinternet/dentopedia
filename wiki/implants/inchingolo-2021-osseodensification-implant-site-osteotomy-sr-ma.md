## 한줄요약
체계적 문헌고찰 + 메타분석 (Systematic Review + Meta-Analysis, SR+MA), n=16편 (그 중 임상연구 11편: 동물 8 + 인간 3) — 골밀도화 (Osseodensification, OD)는 통상 드릴링 대비 삽입 토크와 골-임플란트 접촉률 (Bone-to-Implant Contact, BIC)을 유의하게 높이고, 시계방향(절삭) vs 반시계방향(다짐) OD 간에는 BIC·토크는 차이 있으나 골면적분율 (Bone Area Fraction Occupancy, BAFO)은 차이 없음. 메타분석이 동물 데이터에 한정되어 인간 임상 외삽은 여전히 RCT가 필요.

## Summary
This 2021 SR+MA updates the OD evidence base. Of 16 papers screened in PubMed (Medline), 11 were clinical (8 animal, 3 human), 4 were narrative reviews, and 1 was a case report. Meta-analytic pooling was restricted to the animal studies (only design homogeneous enough), comparing clockwise OD, counter-clockwise OD, and conventional drilling for BIC %, BAFO %, and insertion torque.

The headline qualitative finding is that OD raises insertion torque and BIC vs conventional drilling, and that clockwise vs counter-clockwise modes themselves differ on BIC and torque but not on BAFO. The authors are explicit that human clinical evidence remains thin and that randomized controlled trials in humans are required before strong recommendations can be made.

This is currently the most quantitative synthesis in the OD literature, but it inherits the weaknesses of its source base — heavy author/affiliation overlap across included papers (Coelho/Neiva/Witek/Tovar group), animal-skewed evidence, and proprietary device exposure (Densah bur).

## Key Contributions
- First meta-analytic pooling in the OD literature (animal subset).
- Establishes a significant clockwise vs counter-clockwise effect on BIC and torque — relevant to the actual surgical protocol step.
- Reports no BAFO difference between clockwise and counter-clockwise OD — challenges the assumption that the densifying step itself drives bone-area gains.
- Reaffirms OD > conventional drilling on insertion torque in vivo.

## Methodology
PubMed-Medline-only systematic search per PRISMA. 16 papers included. The meta-analytic component pooled animal studies on BIC %, BAFO %, and insertion torque, comparing clockwise OD, counter-clockwise OD, and conventional drilling. Human clinical data was synthesized narratively (3 studies only).

## Results
- Included: 16 articles (11 clinical: 8 animal + 3 human; 4 literature reviews; 1 case report).
- Pooled (animal): OD significantly raises insertion torque vs conventional drilling.
- Pooled (animal): clockwise vs counter-clockwise OD — significant difference in BIC and insertion torque; **no significant difference in BAFO**.
- Specific pooled effect sizes, 95% CIs, and I² values: Numerical detail not extractable from available front/back text — see PDF tables.

## Limitations
- Meta-analysis limited to animal studies — clinical magnitude of OD effect in humans remains unquantified.
- Search restricted to PubMed-Medline (cf. Padhye 2020 which also queried Embase and Google Scholar).
- Heavy authorship/affiliation overlap in the OD literature pool (NYU/Coelho group dominates) — included studies may not be independent.
- Proprietary device exposure across nearly all included papers — reporting bias risk not formally adjudicated.
- Pooled effect magnitudes and heterogeneity statistics are not surfaceable from the abstract/references — must consult PDF body.
