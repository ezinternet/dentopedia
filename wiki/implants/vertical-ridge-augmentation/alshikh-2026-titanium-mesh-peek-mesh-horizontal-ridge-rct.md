## One-line Summary

RCT (14 patients / 28 maxillary sites) comparing prebent titanium mesh (60:40 graft) vs customized PEEK mesh (70:30 graft) for predominantly horizontal ridge augmentation: no significant difference in linear horizontal (3.02 vs 2.42 mm, p=0.065) or vertical gain, but PEEK gained more absolute bone volume (499 vs 371 mm³, p=0.004) and lost more graft (175 vs 128 mm³, p=0.040) with near-identical proportional resorption (~34–35%); exposure 7.1% (PEEK) vs 14.3% (Ti), NS; 100% implant placement both arms.

## Summary

This single-center two-arm RCT enrolled 14 partially edentulous patients (28 maxillary sites, 7 per group / 14 sites per group) with predominantly horizontal ridge deficiencies (2–5 mm width) and compared two CAD/CAM-driven GBR protocols over 6 months: a **prebent titanium mesh** (adapted over a 3D-printed virtually augmented STL model) with a 60:40 autogenous:xenograft mixture, versus a **patient-customized milled PEEK mesh** (medical-grade Juvora, 0.5–0.7 mm, perforated) with a 70:30 mixture. The higher autograft fraction in the PEEK arm was a deliberate compensation for PEEK's lower porosity/permeability — which the authors stress makes this a **comparison of two treatment protocols, not of mesh materials in isolation**.

The two protocols were statistically indistinguishable on the linear primary outcome (horizontal gain) and on vertical gain, but diverged on the 3D volumetric secondary outcomes: PEEK produced significantly greater absolute bone volume yet also greater absolute graft loss. Crucially, when resorption was normalized to baseline augmented volume, both protocols resorbed ~34–35% — so the larger PEEK graft loss appears proportional to its larger initial volume rather than indicating inferior stability. Complications were low and comparable; all 28 sites accepted implants.

## Methodology

- **Design:** RCT, patient-level randomization (n=14), site-level measurement (28 sites); SNOSE allocation concealment; linear mixed-effects model (treatment fixed, patient random intercept) to handle site-within-patient clustering.
- **Control (Ti):** STL model from virtually augmented CBCT, 3D-printed; readymade Ti mesh prebent over model; 60:40 autogenous (ramus, ACM bur):xenograft; 3–4 microscrews.
- **Study (PEEK):** 5-axis-milled 0.5–0.7 mm perforated PEEK mesh; 70:30 autogenous:xenograft; 3–4 microscrews.
- Both: full-thickness flap, decortication, periosteal releasing incision, tension-free Prolene 4-0 closure; AMX/CLV prophylaxis; CHX rinse.
- **Imaging:** Scanora3D CBCT (133 μm voxel); OnDemand 3D semi-automatic threshold segmentation; voxel-based registration; ICC > 0.90.
- Follow-up CBCT preop / immediate postop / 6 months; mesh removed and implants placed at 6 months.

## Results

| Outcome | Titanium | PEEK | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Horizontal bone gain (mm) | 3.02 ± 0.68 | 2.42 ± 0.38 | 0.065 (NS) |
| Vertical bone gain (mm) | 1.12 ± 0.10 | 1.09 ± 0.25 | 0.738 (NS) |
| Gained bone volume (mm³) | 370.82 ± 51.69 | 499.47 ± 80.46 | 0.004 |
| Graft loss volume (mm³) | 127.73 ± 35.55 | 174.83 ± 40.78 | 0.040 |
| Graft loss (% of baseline) | ~34% | ~35% | ~equal |
| Mesh exposure | 2/7 (14.3%) | 1/7 (7.1%) | NS |
| Exposure timing | 4–5 months | 3 months | — |
| Implant placement | 7/7 (100%) | 7/7 (100%) | — |

- All edema resolved ≤1 week; no donor-site infection, no neurosensory disturbance; 1 severe bleed (Ti only, NS).
- Exposures in both groups managed conservatively without compromising regeneration; no PEEK fracture at 0.5–0.7 mm.
- Linear gains in this trial (Ti 3.02 mm horizontal) were lower than the ~5 mm horizontal / up to 7 mm vertical reported elsewhere — consistent with predominantly horizontal, modest-defect inclusion.
- **Bottom line:** both protocols predictably enabled implant placement; volumetric differences cannot be attributed to mesh material alone because graft composition differed. No superiority conclusion is warranted; the study is underpowered for complications.
