## One-line Summary

Technical note / stepwise protocol (no patient outcome dataset): the TACOS technique (Titanium Alveolar Customized Osteogenic Scaffold) — a ~14-step procedure for horizontal+vertical ridge augmentation with CAD/CAM/SLM customized titanium mesh, autogenous bone + DBBM (≥60:40), cortical perforations for RAP, CGF, and a resorbable membrane over the mesh.

## Summary

A technical note that codifies a reproducible surgical protocol — the **TACOS technique** — for horizontal and vertical alveolar ridge augmentation using a patient-specific (CAD/CAM, selective laser melting) titanium mesh. The deliberate focus is *execution*: each surgical step is described as a clinician's guide rather than reported as outcomes. The workflow couples digital pre-planning of graft volume with intraoral autologous bone harvesting (piezoelectric, nerve-aware), an autologous:DBBM graft mix (≥60:40), biological accelerators (cortical perforations to trigger the regional acceleratory phenomenon; concentrated growth factors), and a resorbable membrane laid over the mesh. The mesh has a designed central "weak track" both for compacting graft and for easier second-stage removal.

Because this is a protocol paper, it does **not** provide a quantitative cohort, augmentation millimeters, complication rates, or survival data — it is procedural knowledge, not efficacy evidence. (Labeled `narrative-review` confidence as the closest descriptive/non-comparative category in the wiki vocabulary.)

## Key Contributions

- Names and systematizes the TACOS protocol for customized-Ti-mesh GBR.
- Operationalizes flap design, tension-free closure, and soft-tissue management as integral to avoiding mesh exposure.
- Integrates RAP-inducing cortical perforations and CGF into a standard mesh-GBR sequence.

## Methodology

Indications: implant candidates with severe atrophic edentulous crest (upper/lower jaw); CBCT planning. Exclusions: surgical contraindications, smokers, pregnant/lactating women. Conscious sedation + antibiotic/analgesic regimen.

Core surgical steps (abbreviated):
1. Mid-crestal (lower) or paracrestal buccal (upper) incision; mucogingival split-thickness papillae anticipating coronally advanced flap → full-thickness flap.
2. Crest debridement.
3. Trial-fit CAD/CAM mesh (YXoss CBR, ReOss); pre-plan bone volume (PBV).
4. 0.8 mm landmark drilling for screw registration.
5. Autologous bone harvest from ramus–corpus (safe-scraper / calibrated ultrasonic block osteotomies, IAN-aware).
6. Fill mesh extraorally with autologous bone + DBBM (Bio-Oss), ≥60:40 (illustrated case ~80:20).
7. Cortical perforations (0.8 mm, dry, 100–150 rpm) to activate RAP; dusty bone kept in situ.
8. Fix filled mesh with Ø1.3 × 6 mm osteosynthesis screws at landmarks.
9. Compact additional graft via central tracks.
10. First CGF layer over graft.
11. Resorbable membrane (Bio-Gide) over mesh, buccally fixed; followed by further CGF layering and tension-free primary closure.

## Results

No quantitative outcomes are reported. The abstract asserts "predictable results in clinical and radiographic evaluations," but the extracted text contains no cohort size, augmentation measurements, exposure/complication rates, or implant survival. Clinical value is procedural reproducibility, not demonstrated efficacy.
