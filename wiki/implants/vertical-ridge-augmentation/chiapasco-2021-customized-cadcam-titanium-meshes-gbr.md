## One-line Summary

Retrospective human study (41 patients, 53 atrophic sites, 106 implants): customized CAD/CAM titanium mesh GBR with autogenous bone + bovine bone mineral achieved mean vertical gain 4.78 mm and horizontal gain 6.35 mm, 11/53 mesh exposures, 100% implant survival (mean 10.6 mo post-loading).

## Summary

This retrospective case series (41 patients, 53 severely atrophic edentulous sites, 2018–2019) reports the preliminary clinical outcomes of guided bone regeneration (GBR) using **customized CAD/CAM titanium meshes** filled with a mix of autogenous bone chips and bovine bone mineral (BBM). After a mean healing of 7 months the meshes were removed and 106 implants placed, then restored after a mean of 3.5 months. The technique delivered substantial three-dimensional augmentation (vertical ~4.8 mm, horizontal ~6.4 mm) with 100% short-term implant survival. The dominant complication was mesh exposure (11/53 sites, ~21%), and exposed sites lost significantly more graft volume — making soft-tissue management over the mesh the key determinant of outcome, a theme echoed across the sibling soft-tissue studies in this category.

## Key Contributions

- Human clinical evidence base for **patient-specific (CAD/CAM) titanium mesh** GBR in severe atrophy, complementing the procedural TACOS protocol note.
- Quantifies both vertical and horizontal gain plus interim graft resorption between augmentation and implant placement.
- Demonstrates that mesh exposure significantly increases graft volume loss (p < .001 in both dimensions), localizing the principal risk to soft-tissue dehiscence.

## Methodology

- Design: retrospective, single-center, no control group.
- Population: 41 patients, 53 atrophic sites.
- Augmentation: titanium mesh + autogenous bone chips + bovine bone mineral (BBM).
- Healing before mesh removal: mean 7 months (range 5–12).
- 106 implants placed at mesh removal; uncovered after mean 3.5 months (range 2–5).
- Outcomes: vertical/horizontal bone gain, interim resorption, mesh exposure, implant survival; mean implant follow-up after loading 10.6 ± 6.5 months.

## Results

| Outcome | Value |
|---|---|
| Mean vertical bone gain | 4.78 ± 1.88 mm (1.00–8.90) |
| Mean horizontal bone gain | 6.35 ± 2.10 mm (2.14–11.48) |
| Interim vertical change (to implant placement) | −0.39 ± 0.64 mm |
| Interim horizontal change | −0.49 ± 0.83 mm |
| Mesh exposure | 11 / 53 sites (~21%); 8 uneventful, 3 with partial bone loss |
| Volume loss in exposed vs non-exposed | Significantly greater in exposed (p < .001, both dimensions) |
| Implant survival | 100% (mean follow-up 10.6 ± 6.5 mo) |

Clinical bottom line: customized Ti-mesh is a reliable tool for 3D reconstruction of severely atrophic ridges, but mesh exposure is the main risk and directly costs graft volume — so flap/soft-tissue management governs the result.
