## One-line Summary
SR+MA (22 studies; 608 patients, 1,318 implants) comparing customized CAD/CAM titanium mesh (CTM) vs conventional titanium mesh (TM); comparable vertical bone gain (5.14 vs 6.24 mm, NS), significantly greater horizontal gain with CTM (6.38 vs 3.85 mm, P=0.004), and a non-significant trend to less exposure with CTM (20.3% vs 30.9%).

## 한줄요약
맞춤형 CAD/CAM 티타늄메쉬(CTM) vs 기성 메쉬(TM) SR+MA(22편, 608명·임플란트 1,318개). 수직골증대 유사(5.14 vs 6.24 mm, NS), 수평골증대는 CTM 유의 우수(6.38 vs 3.85 mm, P=0.004), 막노출 CTM 낮은 경향(20.3% vs 30.9%, NS).

## Summary
Customization buys precision of fit, not more vertical height: pooled vertical bone gain was statistically equivalent between CTM and TM. Where CTM wins is horizontal gain (significant, +2.5 mm) and a consistent — though underpowered — trend toward fewer exposures and complications. The practical read is that CTM's advantage is reduced intraoperative adaptation/surgical time and better contour control, while the core vertical-augmentation result is mesh-agnostic. Evidence is mostly retrospective (12/22 studies), so the exposure/complication trends should not yet be treated as established.

## Key Contributions
- First meta-analysis directly pooling CTM vs TM ridge-augmentation outcomes.
- Dissociates vertical vs horizontal effect, localizing where customization helps.

## Methodology
- 22 studies (3 RCT, 6 prospective, 12 retrospective, 1 cohort), 4-database search to Jan 2025.
- Random-effects pooling of vertical/horizontal bone gain, exposure, complications.

## Results
- Vertical gain: TM 6.24 vs CTM 5.14 mm (P=0.628, NS).
- Horizontal gain: CTM 6.38 vs TM 3.85 mm (P=0.004, favors CTM).
- Exposure: TM 30.9% vs CTM 20.3% (P=0.721, NS); infections more frequent in TM (NS).
