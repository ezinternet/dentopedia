## One-line Summary

Case series (n=10, esthetic zone): cross-linked collagen membrane + tenting screws (no Ti mesh) → VBG 6.2 ± 1.61 mm (80% defect resolution); low complications; 9.3-month healing.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

Wang HL group (University of Michigan) describes a novel VRA technique for the esthetic zone: **tenting screws + cross-linked resorbable collagen membrane**, without titanium mesh. The approach achieves 6.2 mm vertical bone gain with 80% defect resolution.

**Core concept — tenting screws**: Titanium osteosynthesis mini-screws are placed into the residual bone at the planned augmentation height, creating a structural framework. The cross-linked collagen membrane is then draped over these screws and the graft is placed underneath. The screws maintain the space (function of Ti mesh), while the collagen acts as barrier (function of GBR membrane). This eliminates the need for a rigid titanium mesh.

**Why cross-linked collagen (not native)**: Native collagen resorbs in 4–6 weeks — too fast for the 9.3-month healing needed for large VRA. Cross-linked collagen (ribose or glutaraldehyde) maintains barrier function 6–9+ months. Resorbable = no 2nd surgery for removal.

**VBG comparison context**: This 6.2 mm exceeds the Sabri 2024 SR+MA pooled Ti mesh VBG (3.36 mm vertical) — though direct comparison is invalid (different defects, case series vs SR).

## Key Contributions

- Proof-of-concept: Ti mesh is replaceable for esthetic-zone VRA using tenting screws + XL collagen
- 6.2 ± 1.61 mm VBG — highest reported with resorbable barriers alone in VRA
- 80% defect resolution — competitive with non-resorbable approaches
- No second surgery for membrane removal (resorbable)
- Avoids Ti mesh exposure risk and neurological injury risk during mesh adaptation
- Wang HL group — high credibility author in GBR/VRA literature

## Methodology

- **Design**: Case series (no control group)
- **N**: 10 patients (5M/5F), esthetic zone severe vertical defects
- **Technique**: 
  1. Tenting screws (Ti osteosynthesis mini-screws) placed into bone at planned augmentation height
  2. Cross-linked collagen membrane draped over screws
  3. Bone graft placed under tented membrane
  4. No Ti mesh used
- **Healing**: mean 9.3 months before re-entry
- **Outcomes**: VBG (mm); defect resolution (%)
- **Limitation**: abstract only; no complication breakdown available

## Results

| Outcome | Value |
|---|---|
| N | 10 patients |
| Mean healing time | 9.3 months |
| Mean defect resolution | 80% |
| Mean VBG | **6.2 ± 1.61 mm** |
| Minimum case VBG | 6 mm (even in the 50%-resolution outlier) |
| Complication rate | Low (exact figures not in abstract) |

Comparison reference: Sabri 2024 SR+MA (n=22 studies) Ti mesh pooled vertical gain = 3.36 mm (95% CI 2.44–4.64).
