## One-line Summary

First RCT (n=30) on CAD/CAM Ti mesh ± cross-linked collagen membrane for GBR: healing complications 33% (mesh alone) vs 13% (mesh+membrane), regeneration rate 74.3% vs 82.3% — all numerically favor membrane, but noninferiority analysis inconclusive for both.

## 한줄요약

첫 RCT(n=30): CAD/CAM 맞춤형 Ti 메시 단독(합병증 33%, RR 74.3%) vs +가교화 콜라겐막(13%, 82.3%) — 막 추가군 수치상 우수, 비열등성·우월성 검정 모두 inconclusive(유의차 없음).

## Summary

This RCT is the **first study comparing CAD/CAM customized Ti mesh with vs without a covering resorbable membrane** for GBR. The clinical question is directly relevant: should you always add a cross-linked collagen membrane (Cytoplast RTM) over your titanium mesh, or is the mesh alone sufficient?

**Study setup**: 30 patients (maxilla + mandible; mostly posterior vertical defects) randomized to:
- **Mesh− (n=15)**: CAD/CAM Ti mesh alone, fixed with osteosynthesis screws
- **Mesh+ (n=15)**: CAD/CAM Ti mesh + cross-linked collagen membrane over it

Both groups used the same 50:50 autologous bone + xenograft (Zcore).

**Answer**: Membrane addition is **not proven necessary** (noninferiority inconclusive), but **numerically superior** across all outcomes — roughly halving complications (33% → 13%), reducing severe pseudo-periosteum (Type 3: 26.7% → 6.7%), improving regeneration rate (74.3% → 82.3%), and increasing VBG (4.7 → 6.4 mm). The study was **underpowered** (n=15/group) — these trends are real but the trial cannot confirm them statistically. Clinical interpretation: adding a membrane is probably beneficial and should be considered the default.

## Key Contributions

1. **First comparative RCT**: No prior study had directly randomized Ti mesh ± resorbable membrane
2. **Exposure rate halved**: 26.7% (Mesh−) vs 6.7% (Mesh+) — most clinically important safety signal
3. **Pseudo-periosteum Type 3 reduced**: 26.7% → 6.7% — severe fibrous tissue under mesh nearly eliminated
4. **Regeneration rate trend**: 74% → 82% (NS) — membrane likely creates better regenerative environment
5. **Inconclusive design** appropriate: noninferiority margin 10% was reasonable given absence of prior data

## Methodology

- **Design**: Parallel-group RCT, noninferiority primary endpoint (Δ=10%), Bologna/Rome centers
- **Group A (Mesh−)**: CAD/CAM SLM Ti mesh (BTK 3D-Mesh) filled with 50:50 autologous bone + Zcore xenograft, stabilized with titanium mini-screws, primary closure
- **Group B (Mesh+)**: Same + cross-linked collagen membrane (Cytoplast RTM) applied over mesh surface
- **Re-entry at 6 months**: CBCT, pseudo-periosteum thickness, bone density, implant placement
- **Outcomes**: Healing complications (primary), RR/RBV/VBG (secondary), pseudo-periosteum type, implant survival

## Results

| Outcome | Mesh− | Mesh+ | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Healing complications | 33.3% | 13.3% | 0.39 (NS) |
| Exposure rate | 26.7% | 6.7% | 0.33 (NS) |
| Pseudo-periosteum Type 3 | 4/15 (26.7%) | 1/15 (6.7%) | NS |
| Regeneration rate | 74.3% | 82.3% | 0.44 (NS) |
| VBG (mm) | 4.7 | 6.4 | 0.08 (NS) |
| Implant survival | 94.0% | 97.0% | NS |

**Noninferiority**: inconclusive for healing complications (CI: −0.44 to 0.17) and RR (CI includes Δ and 0).  
**Superiority**: no significant differences, but all trends favor Mesh+.
