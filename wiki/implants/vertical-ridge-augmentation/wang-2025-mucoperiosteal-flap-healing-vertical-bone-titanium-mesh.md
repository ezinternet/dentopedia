## One-line Summary

Randomized-block animal study (6 beagle dogs, 4 protocols, ~12 sites/group): a mucoperiosteal flap left open without periosteal releasing incision (PRI) healed over a clean titanium-membrane surface with the lowest dehiscence (group C 8.3% vs PRI groups 41.67–50%) and a +1.77 mm gain in keratinized gingiva (vs −4.37 mm with PRI + closure, p<.001).

## Summary

This beagle-dog study challenges a near-universal habit in titanium-mesh GBR: cutting the periosteum (periosteal releasing incision, PRI) to obtain tension-free primary closure. PRI works but narrows keratinized gingiva, shallows the vestibule, and interrupts periosteal blood supply — and Ti-mesh exposure rates are still reported up to ~51%. The authors test four protocols, including **group C**, where the flap is deliberately left **open without PRI** over a clean titanium membrane and allowed to heal by secondary intention.

The open, non-PRI approach won on both counts that matter for exposure: it had the lowest dehiscence (8.3%, one site) versus 41.67–50% in the PRI + closure groups, and it *widened* keratinized gingiva by +1.77 mm instead of the −4.37 mm loss seen with PRI + closure. New gingiva in group C also looked histologically better. The practical message — that a mucoperiosteal flap can heal over a clean Ti-membrane surface and even gain keratinized tissue — reframes flap management for Ti-mesh vertical augmentation and pairs with Kaner 2017 (soft-tissue conditioning) as the soft-tissue half of this category. Note the extracted data center on soft-tissue/KG and membrane-surface outcomes; vertical-bone-gain millimeters are not the headline result.

## Key Contributions

- Direct in-vivo comparison of PRI + closure vs open healing without PRI over Ti-membrane.
- Open, non-PRI healing minimizes dehiscence AND widens keratinized gingiva — the opposite of PRI's KG loss.
- Histological + membrane-surface evidence that gingiva heals on a clean Ti-membrane.

## Methodology

- 6 male beagle dogs; mandibular P2–M1 extracted bilaterally, 3-month healing.
- 4 sites/side (8 mm, randomized block) into 4 groups (~12 sites/group per sample-size calc):
  - A: Bio-Oss + Ti-membrane + Bio-Gide + PRI, closed.
  - B: Bio-Oss + Ti-membrane + Bio-Oss(over) + Bio-Gide + PRI, closed.
  - C: Bio-Oss + Ti-membrane + Bio-Oss(over) + Bio-Gide + **no PRI, open** (fissure covered with gelatin sponge).
  - D: Ti-membrane + Bio-Gide + no PRI, closed (no graft).
- Porous Ti-membrane (CTi-mem, Neobiotech) 7×5 mm; Bio-Gide collagen membrane fixed by mattress suture.
- Outcomes: dehiscence rate, KG width change, gingival histology, Ti-membrane surface analysis.

## Results

| Group | Protocol | Dehiscence | KG width change |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | Bio-Oss + Ti-mem + Bio-Gide + PRI, closed | 50% | — |
| B | A + Bio-Oss over mesh, PRI, closed | 41.67% | −4.37 ± 0.45 mm |
| C | B but **no PRI, open healing** | **8.3% (1 site)** | **+1.77 ± 0.37 mm** |
| D | Ti-mem + Bio-Gide, no PRI, closed | 8.3% | — |

A vs B not significantly different (p=1.000). C vs B KG difference highly significant (p<.001); C gingiva histologically superior. Clinical bottom line: leaving a clean Ti-membrane to heal open, without PRI, can lower exposure and *gain* keratinized gingiva — but this is short-term beagle data and needs human confirmation.
