## Summary

Soares et al. (2025) evaluated 9 consecutive GBR cases at the University of Florida using customized 3D-printed Ti mesh, introducing CBCT subtraction volumetric analysis as an outcome measure. The study provides the most comprehensive volumetric data for 3D-printed Ti mesh GBR, but also documents a high mesh exposure rate (77.8%).

**Defect types:**
- Combined large (n=5), vertical large (n=2), combined medium (n=2)

**Bone gain:**
- Vertical (large defects): 5.7 ± 2.3 mm
- Vertical (medium defects): 2.7 ± 0.5 mm
- Vertical (overall): **4.8 ± 2.4 mm**
- Volumetric gain: **0.49 ± 0.20 cm³**
- Graft volume stability: **87.9 ± 19.5%** (12% loss is considered acceptable)
- Augmentation efficacy: **0.037 ± 0.012 cm³/mm**

**Complications:**
- No complications: 2/9 (22.2%)
- Mesh exposure: 7/9 (77.8%)
  - Complete early exposure: 2/9 (22.2%) → **total graft loss** in both cases
  - Managed partial/late exposure: 5/9

**Catastrophic vs manageable exposure:**
Complete early exposure is an all-or-nothing event — it leads to total graft loss and requires implant plan revision. Partial or late exposure can often be managed conservatively.

**Factors associated with success:**
1. Thorough preoperative planning (digital workflow precision)
2. Precise surgical execution (mesh seating, tension-free closure)
3. Effective post-op exposure management

**Factors associated with failure:**
1. Systemic compromise (medical history)
2. Improper mesh adaptation or seating
3. Complete early mesh exposure

**Methodological contribution — CBCT subtraction analysis:**
The study introduces "augmentation efficacy" (0.037 cm³/mm) as a standardized metric linking volumetric bone gain to planned augmentation height. This allows cross-study comparison independent of defect size.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces volumetric (cm³) and efficacy (cm³/mm) metrics to Ti mesh GBR literature
- Documents that 3D-printed Ti mesh exposure risk remains high (77.8%) without specific design optimization
- Distinguishes complete early exposure (catastrophic) from partial/late exposure (manageable)
- Directly contrasts with Keller 2026 (0% exposure) — same technology (3D-printed Ti), different outcome due to design/technique differences

## Methodology

- Retrospective, consecutive, single institution (University of Florida Periodontology)
- 9 cases; CBCT pre- and post-op; spatial registration + semi-automatic segmentation
- 3D digital models for subtraction; 3 operators
- Metrics: linear vertical gain (middle plane), volumetric gain (cm³), graft stability (%), augmentation efficacy (cm³/mm)
