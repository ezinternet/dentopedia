## One-line Summary

Pilot RCT (n=40, posterior mandible): d-PTFE Ti-reinforced membrane vs Ti mesh + cross-linked collagen → equivalent VBG (4.2 vs 4.1 mm, NS) and complications (15.0 vs 21.1%, NS); d-PTFE had 3× fewer surgical complications and simpler handling.

## Summary

This pilot RCT compared two non-resorbable GBR barrier approaches in 40 patients with atrophic posterior mandibles requiring simultaneous implant placement:

- **Group A (d-PTFE, n=20)**: Cytoplast Ti-250XL titanium-reinforced dense-PTFE membrane — a single non-resorbable space-maintaining barrier
- **Group B (Ti mesh + XL collagen, n=20)**: Trinon titanium mesh for space maintenance, covered with Osseoguard cross-linked collagen membrane as GBR barrier

Both groups used 50:50 autogenous bone + allograft, decortication, and 9-month submerged healing.

**Results were equivalent across all primary endpoints** — VBG (4.2 vs 4.1 mm, p=0.58), healing complications (15.0 vs 21.1%, p=0.69), and major complications (10.0 vs 15.8%). Surgical complications (intraoperative) were numerically lower with d-PTFE (5.0 vs 15.8%, all temporary paresthesias), attributable to easier shaping and adaptation of the d-PTFE membrane versus Ti mesh.

## Key Contributions

- First RCT directly comparing d-PTFE Ti-reinforced membrane vs Ti mesh + cross-linked collagen for posterior mandible VRA
- Confirms clinical equivalence: d-PTFE achieves same VBG and complication rate as Ti mesh + XL collagen
- **Ti mesh handling cost**: 3× higher surgical complication rate (all neurological), more surgical time and dexterity required
- **16.3% VBG deficit per cm²** of Ti mesh exposed — quantifies the bone loss penalty per area of exposure
- Late exposure (>4 weeks) — in EITHER group — did not reduce VBG, consistent with Abu-Mostafa 2022 4-week threshold SR
- Ti mesh: when removed due to late exposure + infection, implants retained → revisability advantage
- d-PTFE: requires removal at reopening, but simpler second surgery

## Methodology

- **Design**: Pilot, parallel, double-blind RCT; Bologna (Ethics 30/2013/O/Disp)
- **N**: 40 patients, 108 implants placed simultaneously with GBR
- **Follow-up**: T0 (surgery) → T1 (reopening, 9 mo) → T2 (loading)
- **VBG**: UNC-15 probe at 4 sites (mesial/distal/buccal/lingual) per implant
  - VBG = initial bone defect (IBD) − final bone defect (FBD)
- **Complication classification**: Fontana 2011 (surgical + healing; major vs minor)
- **Graft**: 50:50 autogenous bone (bone scraper) + allograft (EnCore)
- **Decortication**: yes; **fixation**: mini-screws

## Results

| Parameter | Group A (d-PTFE) | Group B (Ti mesh + XL collagen) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Surgical complications | 5.0% | 15.8% | 0.34 NS |
| Healing complications | 15.0% | 21.1% | 0.69 NS |
| Major complications | 10.0% | 15.8% | NS |
| VBG (mean ± SD) | 4.2 ± 1.0 mm | 4.1 ± 1.0 mm | 0.58 NS |
| ISQ at implant placement (T0) | 87.0 | 84.5 | NS |
| ISQ at reopening (T1) | 71.0 | 66.5 | NS |
| Osseointegration at T1 | 100% | 100% | NS |

VBG by site (mm): mesial 3.6 vs 3.3, lingual 4.2 vs 3.8, distal 4.1 vs 4.0, buccal 5.0 vs 5.1 — all NS.

Ti mesh exposure impact: **16.3% deficit in new bone volume per cm² of mesh exposed**.
