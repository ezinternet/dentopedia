## One-line Summary

Retrospective case series (15 patients, 33 implants, 2005–2010, SNU Bundang Hospital): simultaneous maxillary sinus bone graft + vertical ridge augmentation (Ti-mesh/membrane in nearly all) gave 81.8% implant survival and success, low grafted-bone resorption (sinus 0.37 mm, ridge 0.20 mm at final), but elevated complications (4 Ti-mesh/membrane exposures) — authors recommend delayed implant placement.

## 한줄요약

후향적 증례 시리즈 (환자 15명, 임플란트 33개, 2005–2010, 분당서울대병원): 상악동 골이식 + 수직 치조제 증대술 동시 시행(거의 전 증례 티타늄 메시/막)에서 임플란트 생존·성공률 81.8%, 이식골 흡수는 낮았으나(최종 상악동 0.37 mm, 치조제 0.20 mm) 합병증(메시/막 노출 4건)이 많아 지연 식립 권고.

## Summary

This single-center Korean retrospective case series reports the hardest version of vertical ridge augmentation: doing it **simultaneously with a maxillary sinus bone graft** in the severely atrophied posterior maxilla. Fifteen patients (33 implants, 2005–2010) had vertical augmentation (particulate grafts, Ti-mesh/membrane barriers in all but one case) plus sinus grafting; nine implants were placed at the same time, 24 were delayed (~4.3-month healing).

Two findings sit in tension. The grafted bone was *stable* — sinus and ridge resorption stayed below half a millimeter at final observation. But the procedure was *risky*: implant survival/success was only 81.8% (6/33 failed, 3 before loading and 3 late), with a heavy complication list including four titanium mesh/membrane exposures, peri-implantitis, sinusitis, and fixture fractures. The authors attribute this to the difficulty of tension-free primary closure and limited dual blood supply when both procedures are combined, and conclude that a sufficient healing period with **delayed implant placement** gives a better prognosis. (Despite the PDF header reading "REVIEW," this is a retrospective chart-review case series.)

## Key Contributions

- Outcome data for the combined sinus-graft + vertical-augmentation scenario, where Ti-mesh/membrane is standard and soft-tissue closure is hardest.
- Quantifies that grafted-bone resorption can be low while implant survival is still depressed (81.8%) because of wound/biological complications.
- Supports a staged, delayed-placement strategy over simultaneous placement in this setting.

## Methodology

- Retrospective, single-center, single-surgeon; 15 patients (11 M / 4 F), 33 implants, mean age 54.2 y.
- Severe posterior-maxilla atrophy; simultaneous sinus bone graft + vertical ridge augmentation.
- Grafts mostly particulate, mixed (autogenous bone, autoBT, allograft, xenograft, alloplast); block in 2 cases. Barriers: Goretex / collagen / titanium mesh (all but one case); fibrin glue in 19; Surgicel for 2 sinus perforations.
- 9 implants placed simultaneously, 24 delayed (mean healing 4.3 mo).
- Success by Albrektsson–Zarb (1986); ISQ (Osstell); radiographic resorption at 1/2/3 y + final; marginal bone loss.

## Results

| Outcome | Value |
|---|---|
| Implant survival / success | 81.8% (6/33 failed: 3 early, 3 late) |
| Sinus graft resorption | 0.23 ± 0.40 mm (1 y) → 0.37 ± 0.61 mm (final) |
| Vertical ridge graft resorption | 0.12 ± 0.29 mm (1 y) → 0.20 ± 0.37 mm (final) |
| Marginal bone loss | 0.12 mm (1 y) → 0.20 mm (final) |
| Ti-mesh/membrane exposure | 4 cases |
| Other complications | 8 ecchymosis, 3 peri-implantitis, 3 hematoma, 2 sinusitis, 2 fixture fracture, 1 each bleeding/numbness/trismus/fixture loss |
| Prosthetic complications | 2 screw loosening, 1 abutment fracture, 1 food impaction |

Clinical bottom line: combining sinus graft + vertical augmentation keeps the graft stable but carries a notably higher complication and failure load than routine implant therapy; staged delayed placement is recommended.
