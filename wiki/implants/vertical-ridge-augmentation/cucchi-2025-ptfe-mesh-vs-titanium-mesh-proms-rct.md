## One-line Summary

RCT secondary analysis (n=48): CAD/CAM Ti mesh non-inferior to PTFE mesh in complications (8.3% vs 12.5%); PROMs, pain, and costs are equivalent; Ti mesh requires 3× longer pre-op planning (84 vs 27 min).

## 한줄요약

RCT 2차 분석 (n=48): CAD/CAM Ti 메쉬가 PTFE 메쉬 대비 합병증 비열등(8.3% vs 12.5%); 통증·NSAID·비용 동등; 단, 수술 전 계획 시간은 Ti 메쉬가 3배 소요(84 vs 27분).

## Summary

This secondary analysis of Cucchi et al.'s double-blind non-inferiority RCT (primary paper: DOI 10.1111/clr.14350) fills a critical evidence gap by reporting patient-reported outcomes (PROMs), clinician-reported outcomes (ClinROs), operative times, and material costs for two digital vertical ridge augmentation (VRA) approaches.

**Groups (n=24 each, 48 completing per-protocol):**
- PTFE group: Customised Ti-reinforced PTFE mesh (RPM, Osteogenics) + collagen membrane
- Ti mesh group: CAD/CAM Ti mesh (YxossCBR, Reoss) + collagen membrane
- Graft: 50:50 autogenous bone + DBBM + peripheral venous blood
- Healing: 6 months (mandible) / 9 months (maxilla)

### Healing Complications (Primary Outcome)

| | PTFE | Ti mesh | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Complications | 12.5% (3/24) | 8.3% (2/24) | 0.645 (NS) |
| Non-inferiority | Confirmed (CI -0.22 to 0.13; Δ=0.1 respected) | | |

Ti mesh is **non-inferior** to PTFE mesh. Although not statistically superior, the trend favours Ti mesh.

### Operative Times

| Phase | PTFE | Ti mesh | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-op planning | 26.6 ± 11.9 min | 84.4 ± 15.5 min | <0.0001 |
| Intra-op surgery | 121.7 ± 37.2 min | 118.2 ± 31.3 min | NS |
| Total (pre+intra) | 148.3 ± 41.0 min | 221.1 ± 30.0 min | <0.0001 |

The Ti mesh total time is longer due to CAD upload and engineer coordination, not surgical time. PTFE requires only a brief pre-op modelling (~10 min chairside) with the 3D bone replica.

### Costs

| | PTFE | Ti mesh | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Total material cost | €1,200 ± 177 | €1,272 ± 148 | 0.132 (NS) |

Both devices are expensive. Compared to conventional PTFE membranes (non-digital), Ti mesh may actually save ~€400.

### PROMs (All Equivalent Between Groups)

| PROM | PTFE | Ti mesh | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-op anxiety (VAS) | 5.3 | 3.9 | NS |
| Post-op anxiety (VAS) | 1.9 | 0.3 | NS |
| Pain day 1 (VAS) | 2.7 | 2.2 | 0.565 |
| NSAIDs day 1 (doses) | 2.3 | 2.2 | 0.599 |
| OHIP-14 | ~12 | ~12 | 0.845 |
| PoSSe discomfort | 35.7 | 33.2 | 0.343 |
| Willingness to repeat | ~66% "no problem" | ~66% "no problem" | NS |

Key takeaway: ~90% of patients in both groups were symptom-free by 2 weeks.

### ClinROs

- Pre-op clinician anxiety: ~6.3–6.4 / 10 (high) — GBR is stressful even for experienced operators
- Post-op clinician satisfaction: ~8.6–8.7 / 10 (high)

## Key Contributions

- **Only RCT-based PROMs data** for digital VRA procedures (PROMs were primary outcome in only 2.2% of prior VRA studies per recent SR)
- Confirms both digital approaches have equivalent patient experience
- Quantifies the hidden cost of Ti mesh: 3× longer pre-op planning time (engineer interface, CAD upload)
- Validates sedation protocol: anxiety halved even by day-of-surgery
- Benchmark: mean VBG >5.5 mm (severe defect cohort)

## Methodology

- Non-inferiority RCT (margin Δ=0.1 for complications); double-blind; per-protocol analysis
- Both surgeons blinded (first surgeon revealed allocation just before graft placement)
- PROMs collected via daily patient diary × 14 days
- ClinROs via VAS scales

## Results Summary

Both CAD/CAM digital approaches (Ti mesh and PTFE mesh) yield:
- Equally low complication rates
- Equivalent patient experience (pain, anxiety, function, OHIP-14)
- Similar material costs (~€1200–1300)
- Similar intra-operative time (~120 min)

Key difference: Ti mesh requires 3× more pre-operative planning time (84 vs 27 min) — a hidden workflow cost relevant to practice efficiency.
