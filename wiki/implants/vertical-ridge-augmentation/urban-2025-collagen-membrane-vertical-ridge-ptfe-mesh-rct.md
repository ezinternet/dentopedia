## One-line Summary

Non-inferiority RCT (n=30): perforated Ti-reinforced d-PTFE mesh alone vs PTFE + collagen membrane (CM) for VRA — non-inferiority of PTFE alone NOT demonstrated for absolute vertical bone gain (4.5±2.1 vs 4.1±2.7 mm), but complications (6.7% both) and bone density were comparable; PTFE+CM had more type-1 pseudo-periosteum, suggesting CM helps exclude soft-tissue ingrowth.

## Summary

This parallel-group non-inferiority RCT addressed a focused clinical question: does a perforated Ti-reinforced dense-PTFE mesh need an overlying collagen membrane (CM) in vertical ridge augmentation? The mesh's perforations enhance graft revascularization but compromise the cell-occlusion principle, prompting the recommendation to add a CM. Thirty patients were randomized to PTFE alone (test) or PTFE + CM (control), with mesh removal and implant placement at 9 months. The primary outcome was absolute vertical bone gain (VBG).

## Key Contributions

- First RCT isolating the effect of an overlying CM on a perforated Ti-reinforced d-PTFE mesh in VRA.
- Uses ID-COSM core outcomes; CBCT-based VBG, regeneration rate, pseudo-periosteum type, bone density.
- Provides mechanistic signal: CM may compensate for the cell-occlusion loss caused by mesh perforations.

## Methodology

- 30 partially edentulous patients, vertical (>1 mm) + horizontal defects; single experienced operator (I.U.), Budapest, April 2021–May 2023.
- Two phases: VRA at T0; mesh removal + implant placement at 9 months (T1).
- Test: perforated Ti-reinforced d-PTFE mesh alone. Control: same mesh + collagen membrane. CBCT assessment.
- Non-inferiority design; H1 = PTFE alone non-inferior to PTFE+CM for absolute VBG.

## Results

- Absolute VBG: PTFE+CM 4.5 ± 2.1 mm vs PTFE alone 4.1 ± 2.7 mm — non-inferiority of PTFE alone NOT demonstrated.
- Relative VBG: 85.8% ± 10.6% (PTFE+CM) vs 79.2% ± 16.6% (PTFE alone).
- Effective regeneration rate: 72.3% ± 16.4% (PTFE+CM) vs 69.3% ± 17.9% (PTFE alone).
- Complication rate: 6.7% in both groups.
- Type-1 pseudo-periosteum more frequent in PTFE+CM.
- Conclusion: non-inferiority for absolute VBG not established; both techniques nonetheless gave comparable VBG, complications, and bone density. The higher type-1 pseudo-periosteum and lacking bone volume in PTFE+CM suggest a CM may help prevent soft-tissue ingrowth.
