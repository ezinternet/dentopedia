## One-line Summary

SR/MA of 42 studies (11 RCTs): peri-implant bone loss (PBL) as the primary outcome across VRA techniques pooled to 1.38 mm (95% CI 1.10–1.66) at mean 41.0 ± 27.8 months, varying by technique (SBB 0.66, GBR 1.06, Onlay 1.31, Inlay 1.72, osteodistraction 1.81 mm).

## Summary

This is the first systematic review and meta-analysis to take peri-implant bone loss (PBL) after at least 12 months of functional loading as the *primary* outcome across vertical ridge augmentation (VRA) techniques — prior reviews used vertical bone gain or complication rates as primary endpoints. Forty-two studies (11 RCTs) were pooled. RCT-level evidence existed only for GBR, onlay, and inlay techniques. The overall weighted mean PBL was 1.38 mm, and the magnitude differed by intervention type, though heterogeneity and variable follow-up limit firm ranking.

## Key Contributions

- First VRA SR/MA with PBL as the primary outcome.
- Per-technique PBL pooling across GBR, inlay, onlay, osteodistraction, and split bone block (SBB).
- Secondary synthesis of VBG, healing/surgical complication rates, implant survival and success.

## Methodology

- PRISMA-compliant, PROSPERO-registered; included all VRA studies reporting PBL with ≥12-month follow-up.
- 42 studies, 11 RCTs (RCTs only for GBR, onlay, inlay).
- Three pairwise meta-analyses; weighted mean estimate (WME) for PBL overall and per technique.

## Results

- Overall PBL: 1.38 mm (95% CI 1.10–1.66) at mean follow-up 41.0 ± 27.8 months.
- Technique weight: GBR 32.9%, Inlay 30.6%, Onlay 25.0%, osteodistraction 7.6%, SBB 3.9%.
- Per-technique PBL (WME, 95% CI): GBR 1.06 (0.87–1.26), Onlay 1.31 (0.87–1.75), Inlay 1.72 (1.00–2.43), osteodistraction 1.81, SBB 0.66 (0.55–0.77) mm.
- Conclusion: PBL can be influenced by VRA technique, but long-term RCTs of peri-implant bone level behaviour are needed for all techniques.
