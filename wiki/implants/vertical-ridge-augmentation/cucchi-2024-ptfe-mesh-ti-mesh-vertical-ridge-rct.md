## One-line Summary

Non-inferiority RCT (n=48 treated of 50 randomized): customized CAD/CAM Ti-mesh was non-inferior to Ti-reinforced d-PTFE mesh for surgical/healing complications in VRA; PTFE gave numerically higher vertical bone gain (5.79 vs 5.18 mm, p=.233) and a significantly higher regeneration rate (99.5% vs 87.0%, p=.013), with ~98% osseointegration in both.

## 한줄요약

비열등성 RCT (50명 무작위, 48명 수술): 맞춤형 CAD/CAM Ti-mesh가 Ti-보강 d-PTFE mesh 대비 수술/치유 합병증에서 비열등 — PTFE가 수직골증가(5.79 vs 5.18 mm, p=.233)와 재생률(99.5% vs 87.0%, p=.013)에서 더 높았고(VBG는 비유의), 양군 골유착률 약 98%.

## Summary

This double-blind parallel-group non-inferiority RCT compared two customized digital barrier devices for vertical ridge augmentation: a customized Ti-reinforced dense-PTFE mesh (RPM, Osteogenics; control "PTFE group") versus a customized CAD/CAM titanium mesh (Yxoss CBR, Reoss; test "Ti-mesh group"). Both groups received a 50:50 mix of autogenous bone chips and deproteinized bovine bone matrix (DBBM, Bio-Oss), and both devices were covered with a collagen membrane (pericardium Vitala for PTFE, native Bio-Gide for Ti-mesh). The primary outcome was the healing complication rate, tested against a 10% non-inferiority margin. CAD/CAM Ti-mesh was non-inferior to reinforced PTFE mesh for complications.

## Key Contributions

- First randomized head-to-head of customized CAD/CAM Ti-mesh vs customized reinforced d-PTFE mesh for VRA.
- Formal non-inferiority design (margin 10%, power 80%, α 5%; ≥17/group required, inflated to 25/group).
- Reports complications, VBG, regenerated bone volume, regeneration rates, pseudo-periosteum, bone density, implant stability, and osseointegration in one trial.

## Methodology

- 50 patients with vertical bone defects >3 mm, partial edentulism (≤5 teeth), maxilla or mandible; randomized 25/25; 48 treated.
- Control: Ti-reinforced d-PTFE mesh + pericardium collagen membrane. Test: CAD/CAM Ti-mesh + native collagen membrane. Graft: 50:50 autogenous + DBBM in both.
- Digital workflow: CBCT/DICOM → virtual implant placement → virtual customized device (STL). Blinded patients/clinicians/statistician.
- α = 0.05.

## Results

- 48/50 patients underwent augmentation surgery.
- Surgical complication rate: PTFE 4% vs Ti-mesh 12%.
- Healing complication rate: PTFE 12.5% vs Ti-mesh 8.3%.
- Vertical bone gain: PTFE 5.79 ± 1.71 mm (3.2–8.8) vs Ti-mesh 5.18 ± 1.61 mm (3.1–8.0); p = .233 (NS).
- Regenerated bone volume: PTFE 1.46 ± 0.48 cc vs Ti-mesh 1.26 ± 0.55 cc.
- Regeneration rate: PTFE 99.5% vs Ti-mesh 87.0%; p = .013 (significant, favors PTFE).
- Pseudo-periosteum, bone density, implant stability: similar between groups.
- Osseointegration: PTFE 98.2% vs Ti-mesh 98.3%.
- Bottom line: Ti-mesh non-inferior for complications; PTFE higher on regeneration rate (significant) but not VBG.
