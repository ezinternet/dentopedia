## One-line Summary

Retrospective study (n=40, 5-year follow-up) comparing CAD/CAM customized Ti mesh vs tenting screws + resorbable collagen membrane for GBR: comparable bone gain at 6 months, significantly shorter operative time with Ti mesh (62.4 vs 72.7 min), 100% implant survival.

## Summary

This retrospective study (University of Genoa, treatments 2020, 5-year follow-up) compared two GBR approaches for severe horizontal and/or vertical alveolar ridge defects:

- **Group A (Tent-pole)**: DBBM + 50% autologous bone (ramus scraper), covered with **resorbable collagen membrane supported by tenting screws**; primary closure
- **Group B (CAD/CAM Ti mesh)**: Same graft, covered with **patient-specific CAD/CAM titanium mesh** + resorbable collagen overlay

Both groups achieved comparable vertical and horizontal bone gain at 6 months (p>0.05 all), allowing predictable implant placement without additional augmentation. The Ti-mesh group had significantly shorter operative time (62.4 vs 72.7 min, p<0.05), attributed to passive fit eliminating intraoperative trimming. At 5 years, all 60 implants survived with stable marginal bone levels (mean MBL ~1.3–1.8 mm). No major complications in either group.

## Key Contributions

1. **Bone gain equivalence confirmed**: Mean height gain ~3.5–4.0 mm, width gain ~2.5–3.3 mm in both groups — no statistically significant intergroup difference
2. **Operative time advantage for Ti mesh**: 62.4 min vs 72.7 min (p<0.05) — passive fit and elimination of intraoperative membrane/screw adaptation
3. **5-year implant survival 100%**: No failures; marginal bone levels stable from loading to 5 years
4. **Pseudo-periosteum**: Observed in both groups (tent-pole more frequent, NS); benign tissue
5. Patient satisfaction similar between groups; patients slightly favored Ti mesh for surgical duration comfort

## Methodology

- Retrospective observational; 40 patients (24M, 16F); mean age 40.7 (tent-pole) vs 48.6 (Ti-mesh)
- CBCT at baseline and 6 months; peri-implant radiographs at prosthetic loading and 5 years
- Both groups: same graft composition (DBBM + 50% autologous); ANCOVA adjusted for baseline age
- Zimmer Biomet Certain implants (DCD/Osseotite surface, internal hex)
- Statistical: Welch's t-test, ANCOVA, Fisher's Exact Test; α=0.05

## Results

**6-month bone gain (mean ± SD):**

| Site | Tent-pole H | Ti-mesh H | Tent-pole W | Ti-mesh W |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Maxilla | 8.70±2.60 mm | 8.93±2.54 mm | 4.70±1.45 mm | 5.27±1.54 mm |
| Mandible | 9.35±2.32 mm | 9.25±2.26 mm | 4.90±1.34 mm | 5.30±1.42 mm |

All comparisons p>0.05.

**Marginal bone loss at 5 years:**
- Maxilla: 1.80 mm (all); mandible: 1.35 mm (all); posterior > anterior (p<0.0001)

**Operative time**: Ti-mesh 62.4 min vs tent-pole 72.7 min, p<0.05
