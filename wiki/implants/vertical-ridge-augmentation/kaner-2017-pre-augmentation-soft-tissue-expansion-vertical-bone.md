## One-line Summary

Randomized split-mouth animal study (10 beagle dogs, supra-alveolar critical-size mandibular defects): pre-augmentation soft-tissue expansion (STE) before alloplastic-scaffold (BCP/PEG) vertical GBR eliminated flap dehiscence (0/STE vs 8 control, p=0.002), preserved flap microcirculation, and significantly increased new bone (median NB 3.81 vs 1.06 mm², p=0.023).

## Summary

This randomized, split-mouth **dog** study tests whether conditioning the soft tissue *before* vertical augmentation improves both wound healing and bone regeneration. In ten beagles, supra-alveolar critical-size mandibular defects were created bilaterally; test sites received a 5-week soft-tissue expansion (STE) before grafting, controls did not. Both sides were then augmented with an alloplastic scaffold (biphasic calcium phosphate + resorbable PEG membrane) of comparable volume.

The result is a clean mechanistic chain: STE preserved flap microcirculation (perfusion stable vs a significant post-surgical drop in controls), which eliminated dehiscence (0 vs 8 exposures), which in turn yielded significantly more new bone and less connective-tissue fill — with two control sites forming no bone at all. New bone correlated with augmented area only in the well-healed STE sites. This is the biological argument behind the clinical observation (Chiapasco 2021) that exposed sites lose significantly more graft volume: soft-tissue integrity is upstream of bone gain in vertical augmentation.

## Key Contributions

- First demonstration of STE preconditioning improving **alloplastic-scaffold-only** (no autogenous graft) vertical GBR.
- Quantitatively links flap microcirculation → absence of dehiscence → increased new bone formation.
- Provides the mechanistic rationale for the flap-management and tension-free-closure emphasis across the Ti-mesh literature.

## Methodology

- 10 male beagle dogs; bilateral supra-alveolar critical-size mandibular defects (premolars extracted + bone reduced).
- Split-mouth: STE (test) via self-filling tissue expander (0.7 ml, 5-week expansion) vs no STE (control).
- Vertical augmentation: BCP + blood + resorbable PEG membrane; scaffold volume measured. Test closed without extra flap advancement; controls required periosteal/submucosal release.
- Outcomes: primary = soft-tissue dehiscence at 2 weeks; flap microcirculation by laser Doppler flowmetry; histomorphometry + immunohistochemistry (osteocalcin, transglutaminase-II) at 8 weeks. Blinded analysis; α=0.05.

## Results

| Outcome | STE (test) | Control | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Scaffold volume (median) | 141 mm³ | 130 mm³ | 0.529 (n.s.) |
| Flap dehiscence | 0 | 8 | 0.002 |
| Post-augmentation perfusion | stable (p=0.627 vs baseline) | significant drop (p<0.001) | 0.012 (better in STE) |
| Total augmented area (median) | 9.49 mm² | 5.26 mm² | — |
| New bone (median) | 3.81 mm² | 1.06 mm² | 0.023 |
| Connective tissue (% of TA) | 19.80% | 42.27% | 0.009 (less CT in STE) |
| TA↔NB correlation | r=0.794, p=0.006 | r=0.614, n.s. | — |
| TG-II (matrix maturation) | stronger | weaker | 0.036 |

Two of ten control sites formed no bone at all. Clinical bottom line: preconditioning the soft tissue before vertical GBR converts dehiscence-prone closures into uneventful ones and meaningfully raises new-bone yield.
