## One-line Summary

SR (8 trials, 2011–2021): VABA with Ti mesh yields 2.56–4.78 mm vertical gain; exposure rate 7.69–66.66%; early exposure (<4 weeks) leads to inadequate regeneration; late exposure causes only minor bone loss.

## Summary

Abu-Mostafa et al. (2022) systematically reviewed 8 studies (from 574 screened) on vertical alveolar bone augmentation (VABA) using titanium mesh (TM) from 2011 to 2021. This review is notable for classifying TM into three modalities and establishing the critical 4-week exposure threshold.

**Bone gain (across 8 studies):**
- 2 RCTs: 20.8% bone gain (relative measure)
- Other studies: means of 2.56–4.78 mm

**Mesh exposure (the defining complication):**
All studies reported TM exposure, ranging from **7.69% to 66.66%**. This extreme range (nearly 9× difference) reflects technique dependence more than material properties.

**Critical threshold — timing of exposure:**
- **Early exposure (≤4 postoperative weeks):** → inadequate bone regeneration. Early exposure allows soft tissue and connective tissue cells to invade the regeneration space before osteogenic cells can establish a scaffold.
- **Late exposure (>4 weeks):** → no effect or only slight bone resorption. Once the blood clot has organized and early mineralization has begun, exposure is largely manageable.

**Early TM removal:**
Indicated in only 2.4–11.1% of cases (1 case each in 2 studies) despite high exposure rates — most exposures can be managed conservatively.

**Infection:**
3 studies, 1 case each: 5%, 11.1%, 25% — infection rates are low but unpredictable.

**Three TM modalities reviewed:**
1. Conventional (direct bone adaptation) — highest variability in exposure rates
2. Pre-bent on 3D models preoperatively — intermediate
3. 3D-printed custom mesh — emerging; early results promising

## Key Contributions

- Establishes the 4-week exposure threshold as the key prognostic indicator
- Shows all Ti mesh types have exposure — no mesh type is immune
- Distinguishes manageable (late) from catastrophic (early) exposure
- First SR to include all three TM modalities in one analysis

## Methodology

- Databases: PubMed, SCOPUS, Science Direct, Cochrane Library (2011–2021)
- 574 retrieved → 8 included (3 RCT, 2 prospective, 3 retrospective)
- Risk of bias: CASP checklist
- Qualitative synthesis only (too few studies for meta-analysis)
