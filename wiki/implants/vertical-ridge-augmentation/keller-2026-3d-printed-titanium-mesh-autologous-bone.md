## One-line Summary

Retrospective (n=16): DMLS micro-perforated grade-2 Ti mesh + pure autologous bone → 0% mesh exposure in 16 consecutive cases; planned and real CBCT bone augmentation statistically equivalent.

## Summary

Keller et al. (2026) report 16 consecutive GBR cases using a specific 3D-printed Ti mesh design that achieved **zero mesh exposure** — a remarkable result given the literature reports 10.8% pooled complication rates (Sabri 2024) and some series report 77.8% exposure (Soares 2025).

**The technique:**
- Mesh: CAD/CAM, DMLS (Direct Metal Laser Sintering), **micro-perforated**, **grade-2 titanium**
- Fixation: only **2 vestibular screws** (minimal hardware)
- Graft: **pure autologous bone** only (no xenograft mixture)
- Two-step protocol: mesh placement → ≥4 months → removal + implant + secondary slight GBR

**Results:**
- Mesh exposure: **0/16 (0%)**
- All 16 patients had sufficient bone for implant placement after 122–160 days
- Planned vs real augmentation: no significant difference (linear mixed model, 102 measurements, 3 operators)

**Why zero exposure? Proposed mechanisms:**

| Factor | Reasoning |
|---|---|
| Micro-perforation design | Allows mucosal tissue compliance, reduces tension at wound margins |
| Grade-2 titanium | Higher biocompatibility, lower stiffness than grade-4/5; reduces friction/pressure on mucosa |
| Pure autologous bone | Faster biological integration, lower inflammatory response vs xenograft |
| 2-screw minimal fixation | Less mesh bulk, lower mucosal lifting force |

**Critical caveat:** This is a composite technique — the zero-exposure result cannot be attributed to a single factor. Future controlled studies isolating each variable are needed.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates that mesh exposure is not inevitable — design and technique matter
- Introduces DMLS micro-perforated grade-2 Ti as a specific design approach
- CBCT outcome validation: planned = achieved (predictability)
- Directly contradicts the assumption that 3D-printed Ti mesh = high exposure (cf. Soares 2025)

## Methodology

- Retrospective, single center, 16 patients
- CBCT: pre- and post-surgical; spatial registration + semi-automatic segmentation
- 102 measurements by 3 independent operators
- Statistical analysis: linear mixed model (accounts for repeated measures per patient)
- Augmentation planned digitally, achieved clinically compared
