## One-line Summary

Prospective case series (10 patients / 12 implants) using a customized 3D preformed titanium mesh (SMARTbuilder; Osstem) over a 1:1 intraoral autograft + FDBA mixture for peri-implant dehiscence/fenestration defects: 4-month core biopsy showed ~80% vital bone, mesh exposure in 3/10 (30%), and all implants functional with stable marginal bone at 12 months.

## Summary

This preliminary prospective study evaluated a **customized, three-dimensional, preformed titanium mesh** (SMARTbuilder; Osstem, Busan, Korea) as a ready-to-use GBR barrier for peri-implant alveolar defects appearing immediately after implant placement. Ten partially edentulous patients (5 M / 5 F, aged 17–87), all presenting fenestration or dehiscence around the fixture, received 12 implants (TS III CA; Osstem) with simultaneous GBR. The defect was grafted with a **1:1 volume mixture of intraoral autologous bone and freeze-dried bone allograft (FDBA)** and covered by the preformed mesh, which was anchored to the implant via a purpose-designed "height" and stabilized by a cover cap (staged) or healing abutment (transmucosal). A core biopsy was taken at ~4-month re-entry and patients were followed 12 months after definitive prosthesis.

Histology showed satisfactory vital bone formation with limited fibrous tissue and FDBA particles incorporated into newly formed bone; the representative core (patient #2) was reported as ~80% vital bone, ~5% fibrous marrow, ~15% remaining allograft. Mesh exposure / flap dehiscence occurred in 3/10 (30%) but did not prevent acceptable regeneration or implant survival, and marginal bone stayed stable at 12 months. The authors position the preformed, round/blunt-edged design (no intraoperative cutting/bending) as both faster and lower-exposure-risk — a conceptual precursor to later CAD/CAM customized titanium mesh GBR.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates clinical + histologic feasibility of a **preformed, customized (1-/2-/3-wall, types I–III) titanium mesh** as a ready-to-use peri-implant GBR barrier.
- Pairs the mesh with an under-reported graft combination — **1:1 autograft + FDBA** — and provides histomorphometric evidence of FDBA incorporation into vital bone.
- Frames the preformed round/blunt-edge design as a mitigation of titanium mesh's chief liability (mucosal irritation → exposure), foreshadowing CAD/CAM custom-mesh GBR.

## Methodology

- **Design**: prospective preliminary case series, n=10 patients / 12 implants, single center (Hanyang University Hospital; IRB-approved).
- **Indication**: fenestration or dehiscence around the implant at placement; GBR done simultaneously.
- **Mesh**: SMARTbuilder (Osstem) preformed titanium mesh selected by defect configuration; types I (1-wall), II (2-wall), III (3-wall). Anchored via implant-connected "height"; stabilized by cover cap (staged) or healing abutment (transmucosal).
- **Graft**: intraoral autologous bone (mandibular ramus / adjacent site, Autobone Collector) + FDBA (Sure-Oss; HansBiomed) in 1:1 volume ratio; tension-free primary closure; antibiotics/analgesics ×10 days + 0.12% chlorhexidine.
- **Re-entry & biopsy**: ~4 months, minimal-flap; mesh removed; 2.2 mm trephine core biopsy; MMA-embedded, ~40 μm sections, H&E, light-microscopy histomorphometry.
- **Follow-up**: osseointegration check 2 months post re-entry; complications + success tracked 12 months after definitive prosthesis; periapical radiographs at insertion / removal / 12 months.

## Results

- **Histology**: satisfactory vital bone with limited fibrous tissue; "pseudoperiosteum" connective layer at re-entry (left undebrided); FDBA particles embedded/incorporated in regenerated bone. Representative core (patient #2): ~**80% vital bone**, ~**5% fibrous marrow**, ~**15% remaining allograft**.
- **Complications**: **3/10 (30%)** — mesh exposure in 2 patients + flap dehiscence over cover cap in 1 (one maxilla, two mandible); managed by chlorhexidine irrigation; no infection/suppuration; graft resorption only at exposed sites. Authors note no edge exposures (round/blunt preformed edges).
- **Implant outcome**: all implants functional; stable marginal bone on radiographs at 12 months; no osseointegration or GBR failures.
- **Comparative exposure context**: 30% here vs Corinaldesi 14.8% (mandible) and Louis 52% (43–55%).
