## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 7 RCTs (393 patients, 474 implants) showing short implants (<8 mm) reduce marginal bone loss (MD −0.26 mm) and biological complications versus long implants with sinus floor elevation in the atrophic posterior maxilla, with equivalent survival.

## 한줄요약

RCT 7편(환자 393명, 임플란트 474개) 체계적 고찰·메타분석. 위축 구치부 상악에서 짧은 임플란트(<8mm)는 상악동거상술 동반 긴 임플란트보다 변연골소실(MBL −0.26mm)·생물학적 합병증이 적었고 생존율은 동등.

## Summary

This 2025 RCT-only SR+MA is the most current synthesis on the short-vs-sinus-lift question, searching to June 2025. Across 7 RCTs, short implants (<8 mm) significantly reduced marginal bone loss and biological complications relative to long implants placed with sinus floor elevation, while implant survival and prosthetic complications did not differ. The direction reproduces earlier syntheses (Mester 2023, Vetromilla 2021): short implants are a less invasive, bone-preserving alternative in the atrophic posterior maxilla, with the standing caveat that follow-up remains too short to settle long-term survival.

## Key Contributions

- Updates the short-vs-long+SFE evidence to 2025 with RCT-only inclusion.
- Sets MBL (not survival) as the primary outcome — a bone-preservation framing.
- Quantifies a biological-complication advantage for short implants (OR 0.39).

## Methodology

PRISMA + Cochrane Handbook; PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, Cochrane (inception–June 2025). PICO: atrophic posterior maxilla, short (<8 mm) vs long (8–15 mm)+SFE, RCTs only, intention-to-treat. RoB-2; random-effects (STATA MP 18). 7 RCTs, 393 patients, 474 implants.

## Results

- MBL: short lower (MD = −0.26 mm; 95% CI −0.43 to −0.09; p<0.001; I²=56.3%).
- Biological complications: short lower (OR 0.39; 95% CI 0.18–0.85; p=0.02; I²=0%).
- Survival: no difference (OR 0.96; 95% CI 0.74–1.25; p=0.76; I²=0%).
- Prosthetic complications: no difference (OR 2.18; 95% CI 0.82–5.82; p=0.12).
