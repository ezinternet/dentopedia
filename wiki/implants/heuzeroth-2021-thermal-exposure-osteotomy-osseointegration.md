## One-line Summary
Preclinical in vivo minipig study (n=36 implants, 4 drilling protocols): optimized novel test drills with a shortened sequence lowered peak intraosseous temperature (35.4 vs 46.5°C, p=.0021) and drill time (4.5 vs 10.3 s) and raised bone-to-implant contact (BIC) at 2 weeks (44.9 vs 31.3%, p<.0001), giving direct evidence that reduced drill heat improves osseointegration.

## Summary
The first preclinical in vivo study to directly link osteotomy thermal exposure to histomorphometric osseointegration outcomes. In a minipig mandibular model, 36 tapered implants were placed using one of four drilling protocols. Novel test drills with optimized design and surface treatment, run with a shortened sequence, produced ~11°C lower peak temperatures and ~5× shorter drilling times than standard drills with conservative sequence. These thermal improvements translated to significantly higher BIC at both 2 and 8 weeks, demonstrating that drill thermal profile is an independent contributor to osseointegration quality — not merely a surrogate.

## Key Contributions
Bridges the in vitro thermal literature (which has long shown drills can exceed 47°C/1 min necrosis threshold) to a histologic outcome (BIC), confirming that the temperature signal is biologically meaningful at the bone-implant interface.

## Methodology
- 36 tapered implants in minipig mandible, guided drilling.
- 4 groups (n≈9 each):
  - (1) Control drills + conservative sequential drilling
  - (2) Control drills + shortened drill sequence (PF)
  - (3) Novel test drill (optimized design + surface treatment) + PF
  - (4) Aged test drill + PF
- Intraosseous temperature probe; histomorphometric BIC, fBIC, and tissue reaction at 2 and 8 weeks.

## Results
| Outcome | Test drill (3) | Control + PF (2) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Peak T (°C, 95% CI) | 35.4 (30.2–40.5) | 46.5 (41.0–52.0) | 0.0021 |
| Drill time (sec) | 4.5 (1.6–7.3) | 10.3 (7.3–13.4) | — |
| BIC 2 wk (%) | 44.9 (34.1–55.7) | 31.3 (20.5–42.2) | <0.0001 |
| BIC 8 wk (%) | 73.7 (64.2–83.2) | 66.2 (57.0–75.4) | 0.0455 |

- Control group CI exceeded 47°C necrosis threshold.
- Aged test drill (4) included to assess wear effect within the optimized design.

## Clinical Implications
- Drill thermal management은 단순 안전 마진이 아니라 골유착 정량적 결정인자 — 최고온도 ~11°C 감소가 BIC 2주에서 13.6%p 상승으로 이어짐.
- 제조사 권장 축약 시퀀스 + 신형 drill 사용이 보수적 다단계 + 구형 drill 사용보다 결과 우수 가능성.
