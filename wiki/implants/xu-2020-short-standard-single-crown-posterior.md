## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 5 RCTs finding short implants (≤6 mm) supporting single crowns in the posterior region have survival comparable to standard implants short-term but significantly poorer long-term (RR 0.94), with no MBL difference.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

This SR+MA restricted to RCTs isolated the single-crown (nonsplinted) posterior scenario, where short implants cannot borrow stability from adjacent splinted units. Five trials were pooled. Short implants (≤6 mm) matched standard implants for survival in the short term but showed significantly poorer survival on long-term follow-up, while marginal bone loss did not differ despite the higher crown-to-implant ratio of short implants. The clinical message is conditional: short single crowns are acceptable but carry a time-dependent survival penalty that splinting may mitigate — a refinement of the broader "short ≈ standard" narrative.

## Key Contributions

- Isolates the nonsplinted single-crown posterior scenario from multi-unit/splinted designs.
- Separates short- vs long-term survival, surfacing a time-dependent gap.
- Confirms higher C/I ratio does not worsen MBL.

## Methodology

PRISMA SR+MA (PROSPERO CRD42018112978). PubMed, EMBASE, Cochrane (before August 2019); RCTs only; Cochrane RoB. 1954 references screened → 5 trials in quantitative synthesis. Short ≤6 mm vs standard >6 mm supporting single crowns in posterior alveolar bone.

## Results

- Short-term survival: no difference (RR 0.99; 95% CI 0.97–1.02; P=.72).
- Long-term survival: short poorer (RR 0.94; 95% CI 0.90–0.99; P=.01).
- MBL: no difference (MD 0.00; 95% CI −0.10 to 0.11; P=.94).
