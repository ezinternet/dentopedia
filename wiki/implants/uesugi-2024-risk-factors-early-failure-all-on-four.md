## One-line Summary

Retrospective cohort (561 patients / 2364 implants, all-on-four immediate loading): 1-year implant-level survival 98.9% (maxilla) vs 99.6% (mandible); multivariate early-failure risk factors were maxilla (OR 3.12) and smoking (OR 2.92), while photofunctionalisation showed only a non-significant protective trend (OR 0.51, p=0.25).

## 한줄요약

## Summary

In full-arch immediate-loading rehabilitation by the all-on-four concept, early failure of even a single implant can jeopardize the whole prosthesis, so this large single-clinic retrospective cohort (Malo Dental Tokyo) asked which factors drive **early failure within 1 year** and whether **photofunctionalisation** (chairside UV surface conditioning) helps. One-year implant-level survival was high (98.9% maxilla, 99.6% mandible) but significantly lower in the maxilla. On multivariate logistic regression, the **maxilla (OR 3.12)** and **smoking (OR 2.92)** were the only significant predictors; implant length, diameter, angulation, and primary-stability band were not. Photofunctionalisation reduced failure numerically at every level but never reached significance (OR 0.51, p=0.25), limited to anodic-oxidation implants only.

## Key Contributions

- First clinical evaluation of **photofunctionalisation** specifically within all-on-four full-arch immediate loading; protective trend (OR 0.51) but underpowered/NS.
- Confirms **maxilla** and **smoking** as early-failure drivers in the highest-risk loading scenario (immediate full-arch), echoing general-placement cohorts ([[implants/yari-2023-risk-factors-early-implant-failure]]).
- Shows implant geometry (length/diameter/angulation) and insertion-torque band do **not** independently predict early failure here — reorienting attention to site (maxilla) and host (smoking).
- Provides 1-year jaw-stratified survival benchmarks for all-on-four and contextualizes them against population data (Lin: early failures = 48.1% of all lost implants).

## Methodology

- Design: retrospective cohort, single private clinic; placements Sep 2005-Oct 2020; five surgeons (>10 y experience each).
- Sample: abstract 561 patients / 2364 implants (Results report 617 patients / 2453 fixtures); mean age 56.8 ± 10.8 y.
- Protocol: all-on-four, anodic-oxidation implants (Nobel Speedy Groovy), posterior tilted ≥4.0 mm + anterior ≥3.3 mm, 17°/30° angled abutments, same-day Co-Cr-reinforced provisional, final prosthesis at 6 months.
- Photofunctionalisation: implants placed after Aug 2016 (172 nm excimer UV, 20 mW/cm², 1 min, chairside).
- Outcome: early failure = implant removal within 1 year (mobility/pain/swelling/abscess). Exclusions: zygomatic implants, immunosuppression, active malignancy, IV bisphosphonates, uncontrolled systemic disease.
- Analysis: chi-square univariate → multivariate logistic regression; SPSS 20; p<0.05.

## Results

- 1-year survival (early-failure rate): maxilla patient 97.1% (2.9%), maxilla implant 98.9% (1.1%); mandible patient 98.5% (1.5%), mandible implant 99.6% (0.4%). Maxilla lower at implant level (p=0.043).
- Univariate significant: male 3.4% vs female 1.0% (p=0.043); smokers 4.1% vs non-smokers 1.3% (p=0.023); osteoporosis trend (p=0.051).
- Photofunctionalisation: overall implant level Yes 0.5% vs No 0.9% (p=0.25); maxilla implant level 0.6% (3/486) vs 1.3% (12/890).

Multivariate logistic regression:

| Factor | OR | P | Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| Maxilla (treatment area) | 3.12 | 0.0444 | implant |
| Smoking | 2.92 | 0.0296 | patient |
| Male sex | 1.25 | 0.649 (NS) | patient |
| Photofunctionalisation | 0.51 | 0.237 (NS, protective trend) | implant |

- Not significant: implant angulation (straight 0.6% vs tilted 1.0%, p=0.24), length, diameter, primary stability.
