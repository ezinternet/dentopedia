## One-line Summary

Animal study (minipig, n=48 implants, 12 wk, moderate bone): novel fully tapered self-cutting tissue-level implant (Straumann TLX) achieved non-inferior BIC (45–61% vs 52–55%) and superior crestal bone maintenance (fBIC) vs Straumann Standard Plus across three diameters, with 4–5× higher insertion torques.

## 한줄요약

동물연구(미니피그, n=48, 12주, 중등 골질): 신규 완전 테이퍼형 자가절삭 조직 수준 임플란트(Straumann TLX)가 기존 Standard Plus 대비 BIC 비열등(45–61% vs 52–55%), 치조정 골 유지(fBIC) 우위, 삽입 토크 4–5배 높음.

## Summary

This non-randomized controlled pre-clinical pilot study evaluated Straumann's novel fully tapered self-cutting tissue-level implant (TLX, test) against the long-established Straumann Standard Plus tissue-level implant (control) in an edentulous mandibular minipig model (n=8 animals, 48 implants, 12-week healing, moderate type 2/3 bone quality). Three diameter groups (small/medium/large) were compared using a split-mouth design.

Primary outcome BIC was statistically non-inferior for the TLX in all three diameter groups. Secondary outcome fBIC — reflecting crestal bone level relative to the rough-smooth surface junction — was consistently higher (better bone preservation) for TLX vs Standard Plus, reaching statistical significance in the small-diameter group (p = 0.047). Bench-top insertion torque measurements in polyurethane plates confirmed markedly superior primary stability for the tapered TLX design (23–54 Ncm vs 5–11 Ncm for control). No fibrous encapsulation was observed histologically in any specimen, and no peri-implant inflammation was recorded at termination.

The authors conclude that the TLX achieves osseointegration and crestal bone maintenance equivalent to or better than a well-established tissue-level benchmark, with significantly higher primary stability, qualifying it as a candidate for immediate placement in bone of limited quality.

## Key Contributions

- Confirmed non-inferiority of TLX BIC vs established tissue-level implant across small, medium, and large diameter groups in moderate bone at 12 weeks.
- Demonstrated consistently superior crestal bone maintenance (fBIC) for TLX; statistically significant for the small-diameter group; non-inferiority accepted for all groups.
- Quantified the primary stability advantage of the fully tapered self-cutting design: insertion torques 4–5× higher than control across all diameter groups.
- Established that microgap relocation (tissue-level design) combined with tapered self-cutting geometry is compatible with high levels of osseointegration in moderate bone quality.
- Provided pre-clinical evidence supporting the TLX's clinical indication for immediate implant placement in compromised bone quality situations.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Non-randomized controlled in vivo pilot study; non-inferiority statistical framework
- **Model**: Sinclair Minipig mandible (n=8 female, 20–24 months, 40–60 kg); teeth extracted 12 weeks prior; fully healed edentulous sites; moderate (type 2/3) bone quality
- **Implants**: Test = Straumann TLX (fully tapered, slim core, WN shoulder, VeloDrills osteotomy); Control = Straumann Standard Plus (parallel-walled, RN/WN shoulder, Twist drill PRO osteotomy)
- **Groups**: 3 diameter subgroups; split-mouth; n=8 per group (control small-diameter n=7)
- **Healing**: 12 weeks submerged (titanium cover screws)
- **Histology**: Non-decalcified MMA embedding; 30–50 μm central bucco-lingual sections; paragon staining
- **Primary outcomes**: BIC (%), fBIC (μm)
- **Bench outcome**: Maximum insertion torque in polyurethane plates (Ncm)
- **Statistics**: Wilcoxon signed-rank test; mixed linear regression (animal as random effect, side and position as fixed effects); Dunnett-Hsu adjustment; 90% two-tailed CI for non-inferiority

## Results

**BIC (mean ± SD)**:

| Diameter | TLX (test) | Standard Plus (control) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Small | 61.30 ± 10.63% | 54.46 ± 18.31% | 0.99 |
| Medium | 60.91 ± 14.42% | 54.68 ± 9.16% | 0.55 |
| Large | 45.60 ± 14.67% | 52.52 ± 14.76% | 0.31 |

All groups: non-inferiority confirmed (null hypothesis rejected).

**fBIC (mean ± SD, μm; negative = apical bone loss from reference)**:

| Diameter | TLX (test) | Standard Plus (control) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Small | −19.01 ± 475.93 | −1026.23 ± 1603.23 | 0.047* |
| Medium | −136.85 ± 355.98 | −376.35 ± 478.01 | 0.25 |
| Large | −328.49 ± 379.04 | −764.94 ± 363.48 | 0.078 |

All groups: non-inferiority confirmed (lower 90% CI bound above tolerance). TLX showed 1007 μm better median crestal bone retention than control in small-diameter group.

**Insertion torque (Ncm, bench test)**:

| Diameter | TLX (test) | Standard Plus (control) |
|---|---|---|
| Small | 23.0 ± 1.3 | 5.3 ± 3.1 |
| Medium | 53.8 ± 2.4 | 10.8 ± 5.9 |
| Large | 40.2 ± 1.3 | 8.7 ± 4.6 |

No fibrous encapsulation; no peri-implant inflammation at any site.
