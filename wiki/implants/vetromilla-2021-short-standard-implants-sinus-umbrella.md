## One-line Summary

Umbrella review (7 SRs, 66 studies) finding short implants perform better-than-or-equal to standard implants with sinus floor elevation across survival, MBL, and biologic outcomes — on a base of critically low / low quality reviews.

## Summary

This umbrella review synthesized 7 systematic reviews (66 primary studies) comparing short implants with standard implants placed in conjunction with sinus floor elevation in the atrophic posterior maxilla. Across survival, marginal bone loss, complications, patient satisfaction, and cost, short implants performed equal-to or better-than standard implants with sinus augmentation. Implant survival showed no difference (RR=1.08), short implants had lower MBL (MD=−0.22 mm) and fewer biologic complications, while standard implants had fewer prosthetic complications in the quantitative (but not qualitative) analysis. The dominant caveat is methodological: 57% of included reviews were AMSTAR-2 "critically low" and the rest "low," so GRADE certainty was high only for survival, moderate elsewhere. The page therefore functions as an evidence-quality ceiling for the short-vs-sinus-lift question, not a fresh effect estimate.

## Key Contributions

- Top-of-hierarchy synthesis (umbrella review) for short vs standard+SFE in posterior maxilla.
- Quantifies that the favorable short-implant signal rests on low/critically-low quality reviews.
- Exposes a quantitative–qualitative discordance for prosthetic complications.

## Methodology

Umbrella review of SR/MAs (Medline, Scopus, Cochrane). AMSTAR-2 methodological quality and GRADE certainty by 2 independent reviewers. From 2011 records, 7 SRs (66 studies) met eligibility. Outcomes: implant survival, MBL, prosthetic/biologic complications, patient satisfaction, cost, operative time.

## Results

- Implant survival: no significant difference (RR=1.08; P=.79); GRADE high certainty.
- MBL: short lower (0.98 ±0.12 mm; MD=−0.22; P<.01); GRADE moderate.
- Biologic complications: favor short (RR=0.16; P<.01).
- Prosthetic complications: standard fewer in quantitative analysis (RR=3.27; P<.01); no difference qualitatively.
- Satisfaction similar; cost and time favor short.
- Review quality: 57.1% critically low, remainder low.
