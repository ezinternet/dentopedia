## One-line Summary
In vitro study (BioMed Res Int 2018, Ø 2.0 mm drills from 5 manufacturers): white-light laser interferometry plus multivariate discriminant analysis showed microstructural surface roughness statistically distinguishes every manufacturer, implying drill micromorphology — not just macroscopic geometry — predicts clinical heat generation and wear.

## 한줄요약
인비트로(BioMed Res Int 2018, n=5 제조사 2.0 mm drill): 백색광 간섭계로 측정한 미세표면 거칠기가 제조사별로 유의하게 다르며 — 발열·마모·내구성 예측 가능. 즉, "같은 직경 drill이라도 표면 미세구조가 임상 성능을 가른다."

## Summary
Implant drills (Ø 2.0 mm) from five manufacturers were characterized using confocal white-light laser interferometry. Multivariate discriminant analysis showed that microstructural surface roughness parameters significantly distinguish manufacturers at every cut-off length tested. The authors argue these differences predict different clinical durability and performance — particularly heat generation and wear onset — independently of macrostructural design (cutting lips, flute, helix angle).

## Key Contributions
Demonstrates that drill quality cannot be inferred from macroscopic geometry alone; manufacturer-specific micromorphology is a measurable, statistically separable variable that maps to clinical tribology.

## Methodology
- Five manufacturers, Ø 2.0 mm implant drills.
- Confocal microscope + white-light laser interferometry.
- Roughness parameters extracted at multiple cut-off filter lengths.
- Multivariate discriminant analysis identifies parameters separating manufacturers per cut-off.

## Results
- All five manufacturers statistically separable on microstructural parameters at each cut-off.
- Surface differences large enough to plausibly drive different heat/wear behavior in clinical use.
- No two manufacturers share an identical surface signature.

## Clinical Implications
- 같은 직경·디자인 drill이라도 제조사 간 미세거칠기 차이가 발열·마모에 직결될 수 있음 — 제조사 변경 시 발열 패턴이 달라질 수 있다고 가정해야 함.
