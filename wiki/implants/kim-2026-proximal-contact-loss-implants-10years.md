## One-line Summary
Cross-sectional study (JPIS 2026, n=142 implants, 101 patients, all >10 years in function): proximal contact loss (PCL) occurred in 53.7% of long-term implants, predominantly mesial (65.6% vs 28.3% distal), with molar location and poor periodontal condition of adjacent teeth as significant risk factors (P<0.05).

## Summary
Cross-sectional study (Pusan National University Dental Hospital) evaluating proximal contact loss (PCL) in implants functioning >10 years. PCL occurred in more than half of long-term implants, predominantly on the mesial side, with molar location and poor periodontal condition of adjacent teeth as significant risk factors.

## Key Contributions
- **n=142 implants, 188 adjacent teeth, 101 patients**; all >10 years in function
- **Overall PCL rate: 53.7%** — majority of long-term implants develop PCL
- Mesial PCL: **65.6%** >> Distal PCL: **28.3%**
- **Significant risk factors** (P<0.05):
  - Molar region implant placement
  - Adjacent tooth: plaque accumulation, mobility, BOP, pocket depth ≥4mm
- No implant-related factors independently significant after multivariable analysis

## Clinical Implications
- PCL is a **highly prevalent long-term complication** — systematic monitoring required
- Periodontal health of adjacent teeth is the modifiable key risk factor
- **Molar implants** need heightened vigilance for PCL at recall appointments
- Mesial contacts more vulnerable than distal — likely due to mesial drift of adjacent teeth
- PCL can lead to food impaction, secondary caries, periodontal deterioration
