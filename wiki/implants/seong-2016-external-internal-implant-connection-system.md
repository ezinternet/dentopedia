## Summary

This KDA narrative review by Seong et al. (Ajou University, 2016) surveys the biomechanical and clinical differences between external hexagon (EH) and internal connection (IC) implant-abutment designs. The Brånemark external hexagon was the original standard, but internal connections — particularly conical Morse taper designs — have progressively gained preference for their superior screw-joint stability, reduced microgap exposure at the crestal bone, and better lateral load distribution. The review covers fixture design, anti-rotation mechanisms, impression techniques, abutment options, and platform switching concepts, providing a practical clinician-oriented guide for selecting the appropriate connection system.

## Key Contributions

1. **Comparative framework**: Structured comparison of EH vs. IC across biomechanics, microgap biology, laboratory procedures, and prosthetic component costs
2. **Morse taper emphasis**: Identifies conical internal connection (Morse taper) as biomechanically superior — cold-weld effect via tapered interference fit eliminates micromotion
3. **Microgap repositioning**: IC places the microgap internal to the implant body and away from crestal bone, reducing bacterial contamination of the peri-implant sulcus
4. **Platform switching rationale**: Explains how a narrower prosthetic platform diameter shifts the biologic width establishment medially, protecting crestal bone
5. **Clinical protocols**: Differentiates transfer vs. pick-up impression techniques for EH vs. IC systems in multiple-unit scenarios

## Results

### External Hexagon (EH)

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| Anti-rotation | External hex projecting above implant platform (~0.7 mm) |
| Stress path | Lateral force transmitted to crestal bone via abutment neck |
| Microgap | At implant shoulder, exposed to oral environment |
| Screw loosening | Higher risk under lateral/bending loads |
| Prosthetics | Wide component library; easier multiple-unit impressions (transfer type) |
| Lab advantage | Simpler abutment modification; more casting abutment options |

### Internal Connection (IC) — Morse Taper Subtype

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| Engagement depth | ~3 mm (4–5 mm diameter implants); vs ~1.7 mm for EH |
| Cold-weld effect | Tapered interference fit eliminates abutment micromotion |
| Sinking-down | Apical seating under load; tightens seal (positive effect) |
| Microgap position | Internal; displaced away from crestal bone |
| Stress distribution | More axially directed; reduced crestal bone strain |
| Screw stability | Superior to EH and spline-type IC |
| Platform switching | Readily accommodated; protects biologic width zone |

### Prosthetic Decision Points

- **Screw-retained** is preferred over cement-retained to avoid residual cement complications and maintain retrievability
- **Gold screw + screw abutment**: allows re-entry; reduces cast post use
- **Impression technique**: Transfer type for EH multiple units; pick-up type advantageous for IC in angled abutment scenarios
- **Porcelain chipping**: Risk similar between types; managed through occlusal scheme design
