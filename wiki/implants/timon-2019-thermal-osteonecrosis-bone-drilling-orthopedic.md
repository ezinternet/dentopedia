## 한줄요약
서술적 고찰(Cureus 2019, 정형외과): bone drilling thermal osteonecrosis 50°C critical value; 발열은 multifactorial이지만 **external irrigation이 단일 가장 중요한 mitigation 변수**. 임플란트 분야의 47°C 기준보다 보수적.

## Summary
A literature review from an orthopedic perspective on thermal osteonecrosis during bone drilling. Critical temperature: 50°C (slightly higher than the classical dental 47°C). Heat generation factors are multifactorial and interact, but the authors single out **external irrigation as the most important variable** for keeping bone temperature below the critical threshold. Level V evidence; calls for more in vivo human studies.

## Key Contributions
Cross-specialty validation of the same physical problem treated in dental implantology — provides an outside-domain check on the dental literature's mitigation hierarchy.

## Methodology
- PubMed, ScienceDirect, Cochrane through December 2017.
- Boolean + thesaurus searches; reference tracking.
- Narrative review.

## Results
| Factor | Effect | Authors' weight |
|---|---|---|
| External irrigation | Single most important mitigation | Highest |
| Drill parameters (speed, feed, intermittence) | Multifactorial | Moderate |
| Drill characteristics (geometry, sharp, material) | Multifactorial | Moderate |
| Critical temperature threshold | 50°C | — |

## Clinical Implications
- 임플란트 임상에서도 동일 — irrigation이 발열 통제의 1순위. drill 디자인·재질 선택은 그 다음 변수.
- 47°C(dental) vs 50°C(orthopedic) — 안전마진 고려 시 dental 기준이 더 보수적; 임상에선 47°C/1 min 기준 채택 권장.
- in vivo 인체 데이터 부재 — 모든 임상 권고는 in vitro·동물 모델 외삽이라는 점 명시.
