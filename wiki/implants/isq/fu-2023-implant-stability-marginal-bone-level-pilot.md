## One-line Summary

2-year prospective pilot study (n=90 patients, 156 MIS Seven implants, Taiwan) found that bone density and insertion torque — not primary ISQ — are the dominant predictors of marginal bone loss, with secondary ISQ stabilizing after 6 months and converging across all subgroups by 24 months (mean ~74–75 ISQ).

## Summary

Fu et al. (2023) conducted a 2-year prospective pilot study at Kaohsiung Medical University Hospital enrolling 90 patients who received 156 single-crown-supporting implants (MIS Seven internal hex, delayed loading at 4–5 months). ISQ was measured by Osstell Mentor at 10 time points from surgery through 24 months; MBL was evaluated radiographically at 7 time points. The study sought to determine which patient- and implant-level variables influence primary ISQ, insertion torque (IT), and MBL.

The central finding is a dissociation between primary ISQ and MBL: while primary ISQ and IT are both substantially driven by bone density (Misch D1–D4) and implant diameter, only bone density and IT show high positive correlations with MBL (r=0.826 and r=0.794). Primary ISQ itself shows no significant independent effect on MBL. Secondary ISQ does correlate negatively with MBL (r=−0.581), consistent with the interpretation that ongoing bone loss erodes later stability — but this is a consequence, not a predictor, relationship.

Secondary ISQ recovery follows a predictable trajectory: fluctuation during 2–6 months (healing/remodeling dip), then plateau after 6 months. By 9–12 months all subgroups (age, gender, bone density, length, diameter) converge to within clinically narrow bands. The 100% 2-year survival rate in this strictly selected (no augmentation) cohort provides a baseline reference for non-compromised implant sites.

## Key Contributions

- **IT and primary ISQ drivers**: Bone density is the strongest predictor of IT (r=0.836); implant diameter is the strongest predictor of primary ISQ (r=0.772). Implant length has no significant effect on either.
- **Primary ISQ mean**: 65.06±6.28 (range 53–85). By bone type: D1=78.5, D2=68.5, D3=64.0, D4=56.7 ISQ.
- **IT mean**: 33.75±6.82 Ncm (range 20–50). By bone type: D1=47.3, D2=40.8, D3=30.8, D4=23.2 Ncm.
- **Secondary ISQ stabilization**: No significant changes from 6 months onward; 24-month mean ~74–75 ISQ across subgroups.
- **MBL predictors**: Bone density (r=0.826) and IT (r=0.794) dominate; primary ISQ, age, gender, diameter, and length are non-significant.
- **Secondary ISQ–MBL link**: Moderate negative correlation (r=−0.581); more MBL → lower secondary ISQ (directional, not predictive).
- **Survival**: 100% at 24 months (no mobility or hardware failure).

## Methodology

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Study type | Prospective pilot cohort |
| n | 90 patients, 156 implants |
| Implant system | MIS Seven (internal hex) |
| Diameters | 3.3, 3.75, 4.2, 5.0 mm |
| Lengths | 10, 11.5, 13 mm |
| ISQ device | Osstell Mentor + Smartpeg (4-direction mean) |
| ISQ time points | Surgery, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 9, 12, 18, 24 months |
| MBL time points | Baseline, 3, 6, 9, 12, 18, 24 months |
| Bone density | Misch D1–D4 via CBCT (Implametric) + intraoperative tactile |
| Loading | Delayed (crowns at 4–5 months) |
| Exclusions | Bone augmentation needed, bruxism, active infection, poor hygiene |
| Statistics | SPSS 22.0; t-test, ANOVA, Pearson/Spearman correlation |

## Results

**ISQ Time Course (overall means)**

| Time point | Secondary ISQ (approx.) |
|---|---|
| Surgery (primary) | 65.1±6.3 |
| 2 months | ~66–67 |
| 3 months | ~68–69 |
| 4 months | ~69–71 |
| 5 months | ~71–73 |
| 6 months | ~71–74 |
| 9 months | ~72–75 |
| 12 months | ~73–75 |
| 18 months | ~73–75 |
| 24 months | ~73–76 |

Convergence: by 9 months, diameter-group ISQ differences narrow to within 4 ISQ units; bone density, age, and gender differences are small from 6 months onward.

**Key Correlations**

| Pair | r | Strength |
|---|---|---|
| IT / bone density | 0.836 | High positive |
| Primary ISQ / diameter | 0.772 | High positive |
| IT / diameter | 0.684 | Moderate positive |
| IT / primary ISQ | 0.602 | Moderate positive |
| Primary ISQ / bone density | 0.529 | Moderate positive |
| Bone density / MBL | 0.826 | High positive |
| IT / MBL | 0.794 | High positive |
| Secondary ISQ / MBL | −0.581 | Moderate negative |

**MBL Pattern**: Statistically significant change detected between 3 and 6 months but deemed not clinically significant by the authors; no significant MBL differences post-12 months.
