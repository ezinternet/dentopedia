## One-line Summary

Retrospective clinical study (300 Brånemark Mk III TiUnite implants, 111 patients) showing strong correlations between CT bone density (HU), insertion torque, and ISQ (r = 0.76–0.88, all p < 0.001); failed implants had markedly lower HU/torque/ISQ (267 HU, 21.8 Ncm, 46.5 ISQ) than successful ones (645 HU, 37.2 Ncm, 67.1 ISQ).

## Summary

Turkyilmaz & McGlumphy retrospectively analyzed 300 implants in 111 patients to test whether local bone density (measured preoperatively by CT in Hounsfield Units) predicts primary stability and implant success. Bone density correlated strongly with both insertion torque and ISQ, and all three parameters cleanly separated the 280 successful from the 20 failed implants. The study positions CT as an objective preoperative tool to anticipate poor bone quality and adjust treatment planning (implant location, longer healing).

## Key Contributions

- Establishes, in a large clinical sample, that **CT bone density (HU) correlates strongly with both insertion torque and ISQ** (r = 0.768 and 0.882 respectively).
- Shows failed implants are characterized by **low bone density, low insertion torque, and low ISQ** — providing a discriminating ISQ value (~46.5 failed vs ~67 successful).
- Documents **site-specific bone density** (highest anterior mandible, lowest posterior maxilla) and **sex differences** (lower HU/torque/ISQ in females).
- Supports preoperative CT for predicting primary stability and modifying treatment plans where bone quality is poor.

## Methodology

- **Design**: retrospective; 300 Brånemark Mk III TiUnite implants (NobelBiocare) in 111 patients (2003–2005), 3-year follow-up (avg 3.7 ± 0.7 yr).
- **Bone density**: spiral CT (Siemens Somatom AR-SP 40), acrylic templates with 1 mm metal indicator rods; recipient-area density in HU. Misch D1–D5 bands referenced.
- **Stability**: insertion torque via OsseoCare motor (final values 20/32/45 Ncm); ISQ via Osstell RFA at placement, 6 mo, 12 mo.
- **Success criteria**: modified Albrektsson & Zarb; only pre-prosthesis failures counted (to exclude loading-protocol effects).
- **Statistics**: SPSS; non-parametric; Mann-Whitney U (group differences), Spearman's rho (correlations); p < 0.05.

## Results

- **Survival**: 93.3% over 3 years (20/300 lost in 15 patients; no failure after prosthesis delivery).
- **All 300 at placement**: 620 ± 251 HU, 36.1 ± 8 Ncm, 65.7 ± 9 ISQ.
- **Correlations (p < 0.001)**: HU↔torque r = 0.768; HU↔ISQ r = 0.882; torque↔ISQ r = 0.764.
- **Successful (280) vs failed (20), p < 0.001**: 645 ± 240 HU / 37.2 ± 7 Ncm / 67.1 ± 7 ISQ vs 267 ± 47 HU / 21.8 ± 4 Ncm / 46.5 ± 4 ISQ.
- **ISQ trajectory (successful)**: 67.1 (surgery) → 66.9 (6 mo, p > 0.05) → 68.6 (12 mo, p < 0.001 vs 6 mo) — slight dip then recovery.
- **Site density**: anterior mandible 846 HU > anterior maxilla 591 HU > posterior mandible 526 HU > posterior maxilla 403 HU (range 199–1231 HU).
- **Sex (p < 0.001)**: females 542 HU / 34.5 Ncm / 64 ISQ; males 692 HU / 37.6 Ncm / 67.3 ISQ.
- **Smoking**: smokers 9.1% failure vs non-smokers 5.02%.
