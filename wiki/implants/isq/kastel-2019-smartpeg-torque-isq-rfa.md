## One-line Summary
In vitro PU-foam study (4 implant types, 3 SmartPeg types): SmartPeg tightening torque had no significant effect on ISQ between hand tightening (2–11 Ncm) and machine tightening (2–6 Ncm) (p=0.343 mesial / p=0.890 buccal) — hand-tightening SmartPegs is clinically reliable for RFA measurement.

## 한줄요약
In vitro PU foam: 수동 스마트팩 조임(2–11 Ncm)과 기계 조임(2–6 Ncm) 간 ISQ 차이 없음(p=0.343/0.890); 임상에서 수동 조임 신뢰 가능

## Summary
In vitro study evaluating whether SmartPeg tightening torque (hand vs machine) affects ISQ readings. Result: **no significant difference** — clinicians can hand-tighten SmartPegs without compromising measurement accuracy.

## Key Contributions
- Validated manual SmartPeg placement for clinical RFA measurement
- Showed ISQ is robust to torque variation within the clinical range (2–11 Ncm hand; 2–6 Ncm machine)
- Tested across 4 implant types and 3 SmartPeg types

## Results

| Measurement | Hand tightening | Machine tightening | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mesial ISQ | 79.76 ± 2.11 | ~similar | 0.343 (NS) |
| Buccal ISQ | 77.98 ± 2.xx | ~similar | 0.890 (NS) |

- No significant effect of tightening method on ISQ
- Consistent across all 4 implant types tested

## Clinical Takeaway
Hand-tightening the SmartPeg is clinically acceptable. No need for a torque-controlled device. ISQ readings are reproducible regardless of tightening method within the physiological torque range.
