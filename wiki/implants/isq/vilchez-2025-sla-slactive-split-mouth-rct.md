## One-line Summary

Split-mouth RCT (n=68, 136 implants, 12 months): modified SLActive and conventional SLA Bone Level Tapered Roxolid implants showed equivalent ISQ trajectories and no significant difference in the primary outcome (MBL change loading→12 months: 0.04 vs 0.07 mm, p=0.658), with 100% implant survival after loading in both groups.

## Summary

This dual-center, split-mouth RCT compared Straumann Bone Level Tapered Roxolid implants with modified SLActive (test, hydrophilic) vs. conventional SLA (control) surfaces in 68 patients (136 implants) followed for 12 months after loading. Each patient received one implant of each surface in separate quadrants, eliminating inter-patient confounding. The study was designed as a non-inferiority trial with a primary endpoint of MBL change from loading to 12 months.

The primary outcome showed no statistically significant difference: MBL change was −0.04 mm (SD=0.39) for modified SLActive and −0.07 mm (SD=0.22) for SLA (p=0.658). ISQ values were high and equivalent at placement (73.0 vs 72.3, p=0.636) and at 8 weeks (75.5 vs 75.9, p=0.805). Implant survival was 100% after loading in both groups.

A small but statistically significant difference in MBL changes from placement to 12 months was observed (MD=0.15 mm, p=0.024), driven entirely by events during the pre-loading healing phase; the post-loading phase showed no significant difference. The absolute magnitude of bone change was clinically negligible in both groups, with no implant exceeding 1.5 mm bone loss. No peri-implantitis occurred.

The key clinical takeaway: surface chemistry (SLA vs. SLActive) does not meaningfully differentiate ISQ trajectories or peri-implant bone stability at 12 months when implant macrodesign is identical. Implant placement factors (3D position, subcrestal depth, abutment height, soft tissue height) appear more influential than surface hydrophilicity for early bone remodeling outcomes.

## Key Contributions

1. **Primary outcome equivalence**: MBL change loading→12 months: 0.04 vs 0.07 mm (p=0.658) — non-inferiority of SLActive confirmed.
2. **ISQ equivalence**: No significant differences in ISQ at placement, 8 weeks, or change over time — high stability in both groups (baseline ~72-73, 8 weeks ~75-76).
3. **Healing phase drives MBL differences**: Greater MBL change during placement→loading interval than during the loading period; surface chemistry did not influence post-loading bone behavior.
4. **Bone type subgroup signal**: Modified SLActive showed slightly less MBL change (placement→12 months) in soft bone (types III-IV) and in maxillary implants — aligns with prior Marković and Ganeles reports.
5. **100% post-loading survival**: Both surfaces; favorable peri-implant health (0% peri-implantitis, 17.2% mucositis at implant level).
6. **Rigorous split-mouth RCT design**: Eliminates between-patient confounding; blinded examiners; CONSORT-compliant reporting.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Split-mouth RCT, two centers (UCM Madrid + USC Santiago de Compostela, Spain)
- **Sample**: 68 patients (136 implants, 68 per group); 61 completers
- **Implants**: Bone Level Tapered Roxolid (Straumann AG); lengths 8/10/12 mm; diameters 3.3/4.1 mm; ~1 mm subcrestal placement
- **Surfaces**: Test = modified SLActive (hydrophilic, 55°C post-production holding step); Control = conventional SLA
- **Abutment**: Platform-switching screw-retained, 2.5 mm height, placed at surgery (one abutment-one time)
- **Loading**: Standard protocol ~8-10 weeks; 14 patients delayed due to COVID-19
- **ISQ**: Osstell Beacon (perpendicular + parallel average) at implant placement and 8 weeks
- **MBL**: AutoCAD 2023 on standardized periapical radiographs, calibrated to 0.8 mm thread distance; blinded expert; measurements at placement, loading, 6 months, 12 months post-loading
- **Statistics**: Paired t-test, mixed ANOVA with Bonferroni correction; non-inferiority margin 0.10 mm
- **Enrollment**: October 2019 – July 2022

## Results

### ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)

| Timepoint | Modified SLActive | Conventional SLA | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| At placement | 73.0 ± 10.3 | 72.3 ± 9.8 | 0.636 |
| At 8 weeks | 75.5 ± 8.5 | 75.9 ± 9.6 | 0.805 |
| Change placement→8w | +3.4 ± 9.3 | +4.1 ± 11.7 | 0.715 |

ISQ was stable and high throughout healing for both surfaces. No dip detected between timepoints. Both surfaces showed modest ISQ increase from placement to 8 weeks.

### Marginal Bone Level (MBL)

| MBL Change | Modified SLActive | Conventional SLA | MD (95% CI) | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Placement→loading | +0.24 ± 0.51 mm | +0.14 ± 0.32 mm | 0.09 (−0.06, 0.25) | 0.219 |
| Loading→12 months (PRIMARY) | −0.04 ± 0.39 mm | −0.07 ± 0.22 mm | 0.03 (−0.09, 0.14) | **0.658** |
| Placement→12 months | +0.20 ± 0.39 mm | +0.06 ± 0.20 mm | 0.15 (0.02, 0.27) | 0.024* |

*Significant but clinically negligible. No implant exceeded 1.5 mm bone loss (range 0.01–1.27 mm).

### Survival Rates

| Period | Modified SLActive | Conventional SLA |
|---|---|---|
| To loading (early losses) | 95.5% (3 losses) | 97.0% (2 losses) |
| Loading→12 months | 100% | 100% |

### Peri-implant Health at 12 Months

| Parameter | SLActive | SLA | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| BOP | 52.5% | 47.5% | 0.648 |
| PPD | 2.61 ± 0.82 mm | 2.58 ± 0.78 mm | 0.658 |
| Mucositis (implant level) | 10.6% | 6.6% | ns |
| Peri-implantitis | 0% | 0% | — |

### Patient-Reported Outcomes

OHIP-14 improvement: mean −6.59 points (SD=9.0) from baseline to 12 months. Greatest gains in physical pain and psychological discomfort. (No between-surface comparison — patient-level PRO only.)
