## One-line Summary
Cross-sectional reliability study of the Osstell Mentor ISQ device (85 implants, 23 patients): ICC = 0.97 (excellent reliability, mean ISQ 72.4), a single measurement is statistically sufficient, and SmartPeg types I and II produce equivalent readings.

## 한줄요약
횡단면 연구(85 임플란트, 23명): Osstell ISQ ICC=0.97(탁월); 단회 측정으로 충분; SmartPeg I·II 동등; 평균 ISQ 72.4

## Summary
Cross-sectional reliability study of the Osstell Mentor ISQ device in 85 implants across 23 patients. Result: **ICC = 0.97** — excellent reliability. One measurement is statistically sufficient. SmartPeg types I and II produce equivalent readings.

## Results

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| ICC (intraclass correlation) | **0.97** (excellent) |
| Mean ISQ | 72.4 |
| SmartPeg I vs II | No significant difference |
| Measurement direction | Clinically non-significant variation |
| Repeat measurement need | Single measurement sufficient |

## Clinical Takeaway
- **One ISQ measurement is reliable** — no need to repeat and average
- **SmartPeg type (I vs II)** doesn't matter — use whichever fits the implant
- Osstell Mentor readings are highly reproducible between operators and sessions
- ICC of 0.97 qualifies the device as clinically excellent for longitudinal monitoring
