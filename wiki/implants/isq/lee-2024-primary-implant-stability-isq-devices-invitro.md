## One-line Summary

In vitro study (n=60, polyurethane bone, Seoul National University) comparing Osstell Beacon+ (ISQ) vs Anycheck (IST): both devices showed equivalent trends across bone densities (15 vs 30 PCF) and implant lengths (7.3/10/13 mm), with Anycheck demonstrating superior operator-independence due to its contact-based measurement method.

## 한줄요약

서울대치과 체외 연구(n=60, 인공골블록)에서 Osstell Beacon+(ISQ)과 Anycheck(IST)는 골밀도·임플란트 길이에 따른 동등한 경향을 보였으며, Anycheck의 직접접촉법이 측정자 숙련도 무관하게 더 일관된 결과를 제공했다.

## Summary

Lee et al. (Seoul National University, 2024) conducted a controlled in vitro experiment placing 60 implants (IS-III Active, Neobiotech; 4.0 mm diameter; 7.3/10/13 mm lengths) into polyurethane bone blocks at two densities (15 PCF = low, 30 PCF = medium) to compare resonance frequency analysis (RFA, Osstell Beacon+, ISQ) and damping capacity analysis (DCA, Anycheck, IST).

Key findings: (1) Both devices showed strongly positive correlations between bone density and primary stability at all implant lengths (R up to 1.000 for ISQ, 0.999 for IST). (2) In low-density bone, increasing implant length significantly improved primary stability for both devices. (3) In medium-density bone, primary stability plateaued or declined above 10 mm, indicating diminishing returns of length in denser bone. (4) Both devices tracked the same directional trends but ISQ values were more variable between expert and non-expert operators, whereas IST values remained consistent regardless of operator experience. The study concludes Anycheck is a viable, simpler alternative to Osstell for primary stability assessment.

## Key Contributions

- Validates IST (Anycheck/DCA) as equivalent in trend to ISQ (Osstell/RFA) for assessing primary stability — the first in vitro comparison under fully controlled polyurethane bone conditions
- Demonstrates density-modulated length effect: longer implants are beneficial in D3–D4 bone but add no benefit at medium density beyond 10 mm
- Identifies ISQ as more operator-sensitive (contactless SmartPeg torque variability) vs IST as operator-independent (contact method, standardized by healing abutment torque)
- Practical guidance: Anycheck measurements should be taken by the same observer across follow-up appointments for valid longitudinal comparison

## Methodology

- **Model**: 60 implants × 2 groups (15 PCF, 30 PCF) × 3 lengths (7.3, 10, 13 mm); n=10 per subgroup; no cortical bone layer (pure cancellous simulation)
- **Placement**: machine-controlled drilling, standardized depth/angle; insertion torque ~18 Ncm (Group I) and 35–40 Ncm (Group II)
- **ISQ measurement**: Osstell Beacon+, SmartPeg type 5 at 4–6 Ncm; 4 directions × 3 = 12 readings, averaged; 5 replicates per implant
- **IST measurement**: Anycheck; healing abutment at 10 Ncm; 10° jig for angle standardization; 2 directions × 5 replicates
- **Observers**: expert (5-year experienced dental hygienist) vs non-expert (naïve)
- **Statistics**: paired t-test, one-way ANOVA (Scheffe/Duncan post hoc), simple linear regression

## Results

**Bone density effect** (both devices, all lengths significant, P <0.001):
- 7.3 mm implant: ISQ 60.5 (low) → 75.3 (medium); IST 58.6 → 71.3 (expert values)
- 10 mm implant: ISQ 66.4 → 75.8; IST 63.2 → 72.8
- 13 mm implant: ISQ 69.3 → 77.4; IST 64.9 → 73.7

**Implant length effect**:
- Low density (15 PCF): ISQ and IST increased with length; R² for ISQ = 0.956 (expert), 0.573 (non-expert); R² for IST = 0.885 (expert)
- Medium density (30 PCF): no further benefit beyond 10 mm; ISQ R² = 0.900 (expert), 0.066 (non-expert)

**Device concordance**: Both ISQ and IST showed parallel trends; inter-examiner gap was larger for Osstell (SmartPeg hand-tightening variability) than for Anycheck (standardized torque + contact method)

**Clinical implication**: In D3–D4 low-density bone (e.g., posterior maxilla), longer implants (>10 mm) meaningfully compensate for poor primary stability. In medium-density bone, 10 mm is sufficient.
