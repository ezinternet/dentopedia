## One-line Summary

Prospective clinical study (28 patients, 45 implants across 3 systems — Astra Osseospeed/Camlog/Replace, single-stage, unloaded) tracking ISQ over 24 weeks: ISQ change patterns over time differed by bone type, not by implant system (time × bone-type interaction P=.0022/.017/.0018); stability rose sharply weeks 0–6 (biggest gain in soft Type 4 bone, ~48–56%) then plateaued to 6 months; 45/45 survived.

## 한줄요약

전향적 임상연구(환자 28명, 임플란트 45개, 3개 시스템 — Astra/Camlog/Replace, 단일 단계, 무하중)에서 24주간 임플란트 안정성 지수 (Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ)를 추적: ISQ의 시간 변화 패턴은 임플란트 시스템이 아니라 골질 유형 (bone type)에 따라 갈렸고(시간×골질 상호작용 P=.0022/.017/.0018), 안정성은 0~6주에 급증(연약한 4형 골에서 증가폭 최대 ~48–56%) 후 6개월까지 정체했으며, 45개 모두 생존했다.

## Summary

This unloaded, single-stage prospective study followed 45 implants from three commercially distinct systems (Group A = Astra Osseospeed, Group C = Camlog, Group R = Replace/Nobel Biocare) in 28 partially edentulous patients, measuring ISQ with magnetic resonance frequency analysis (Osstell) at 0, 3, 6, 8, 10, 12, and 24 weeks. The central finding is that the **trajectory of implant stability over early healing is governed by bone quality, not by which implant system is used**: in every system a two-factor mixed-model ANOVA showed a significant time × bone-type interaction (A P=.0022, C P=.017, R P=.0018).

At placement, primary stability tracked bone density (mean ISQ >70 in Type 1, 65–70 in Type 2 & 3, 48–50 in Type 4). During weeks 0–6 stability rose steeply, and the *size* of that rise was inversely related to bone density — softest Type 4 bone gained ~48–56% while dense Type 1 gained ~0.5–7%. After week 6 all curves flattened (plateau effect) toward 6 months. Anatomical location (maxilla vs mandible) had no significant effect on ISQ change by t-test. No implant failed.

## Key Contributions

- **System-agnostic, bone-driven healing curve**: the shape of the ISQ-vs-time curve is set by Lekholm & Zarb bone type, not by implant macrodesign — significant time × bone-type interaction in all three systems.
- **Baseline ISQ gradient quantified**: Type 4 < Type 2&3 < Type 1 (≈48–50 / 65–70 / >70), reflecting primary (mechanical) stability scaling with cortical bone stiffness.
- **Inverse early-gain pattern**: soft bone shows the *largest* relative ISQ recovery in weeks 0–6 (fast cancellous/woven-bone healing near vascular marrow), dense bone the smallest.
- **Plateau effect** weeks 6→24 consistent with woven-to-lamellar bone maturation.
- **Location not a significant driver** of the ISQ change pattern in this sample.
- Confirms Osstell RFA gives reproducible, painless clinical ISQ across different implant/transducer systems.

## Methodology

- **Design**: prospective clinical time-series; no control group, no randomization; one operator.
- **Subjects**: 28 patients (25 M / 3 F; mean age 58.6 ± 9.23); strict inclusion (good hygiene, ≥6 mo post-extraction sites) and exclusion (heavy smokers, bruxism, uncontrolled HTN/DM, malignancy) criteria.
- **Implants (n=45)**: Group A Astra Osseospeed, Group C Camlog, Group R Replace; placed single-stage per manufacturer protocol with manual torque wrench, healing abutment, no loading. Maxilla 13 / mandible 32.
- **Bone quality**: Lekholm & Zarb Type 1–4 by drilling resistance; Types 2 & 3 pooled (n: Type 1 = 11, Type 2&3 = 25, Type 4 = 9).
- **ISQ**: Osstell magnetic RFA; system-specific SmartPeg (4–5 N·cm); buccolingual + mesiodistal, 3 reps each; recorded at 0, 3, 6, 8, 10, 12, 24 weeks.
- **Statistics**: two-factor mixed-model ANOVA (time × type; time × location), contrast comparisons, ANOVA + Tukey across bone types (P=.05), two-sample t-test for maxilla vs mandible.

## Results

- **Survival**: 45/45 implants, no failures or surgical complications over 24 weeks; high reproducibility.
- **Baseline ISQ**: Type 1 >70, Type 2&3 65–70, Type 4 48–50 (all distinct, P<.05).
- **Time × bone-type interaction significant** in all systems (A P=.0022; C P=.017; R P=.0018).
- **% ISQ increase week 0→6**:
  - Group A: Type 1 +4.78%, Type 2&3 +8.73%, Type 4 +56.47%
  - Group C: Type 1 +0.52%, Type 2&3 +6.38%, Type 4 +49.11%
  - Group R: Type 1 +7.37%, Type 2&3 +11.87%, Type 4 +47.81%
  - → early gain ordered Type 1 < Type 2&3 < Type 4.
- **Weeks 6→24**: plateau (slow rise); slow-phase significance varied by system/bone type (e.g., Group C Type 1 & 2&3 significant, Type 4 not; Group A/R dense Type 1 mostly non-significant).
- **Location**: no significant maxilla-vs-mandible difference by two-sample t-test in any group; only Group R showed a mixed-model location effect (P=.0238), attributed to a small/uneven maxilla sample (5/13 maxilla cases were Type 4).
