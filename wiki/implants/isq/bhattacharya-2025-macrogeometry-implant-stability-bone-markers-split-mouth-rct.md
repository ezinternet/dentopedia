## One-line Summary

Split-mouth RCT (n=18) — modified macrogeometry implants with healing chambers showed equivalent ISQ-measured primary stability to conventional implants despite significantly lower insertion torque, and reduced peri-implant TNF-α at early healing time points.

## Summary

This prospective double-blind split-mouth RCT compared implants with modified macrogeometry (healing chambers) versus conventional macrogeometry implants placed in bilateral posterior edentulous sites in 18 patients. The central finding is a dissociation between insertion torque and ISQ stability: despite significantly lower insertion torque in the modified-geometry group, ISQ values at placement and through 90 days were statistically equivalent between groups. Additionally, peri-implant crevicular fluid analysis revealed lower TNF-α in the modified group, suggesting a reduced osteoclastic signaling environment during early healing. Other bone markers showed no between-group differences.

The study supports the concept that healing-chamber macrogeometry does not compromise primary stability (as measured by RFA/ISQ) even when frictional seating torque is reduced — which may be clinically relevant when high-torque placement is not achievable (low-density bone, wider osteotomy).

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates ISQ equivalence between modified and conventional macrogeometry implants at placement, 45 d, and 90 d — despite lower IT in the test group
- Provides peri-implant crevicular fluid cytokine time-course (7, 14, 30, 90 days): TNF-α selectively reduced in healing-chamber group
- Split-mouth design controls for patient-level bone quality confounders — one of the stronger causal designs available in clinical implantology

## Methodology

- **Design**: Prospective, controlled, randomized, double-blind, split-mouth RCT
- **n**: 18 patients (bilateral posterior edentulism)
- **Test**: modified macrogeometry implant with healing chambers
- **Control**: conventional macrogeometry implant
- **Primary outcomes**: Insertion torque (IT) at surgery; ISQ by RFA immediately post-placement, at 45 d and 90 d
- **Secondary outcomes**: Peri-implant crevicular fluid bone markers (immunoenzymatic) at 7, 14, 30, 90 d — including TNF-α
- **Statistics**: Paired t test (IT); generalized linear mixed models with repeated measures (ISQ, immunoenzymatic variables); α = .05
- **Institution**: Universidade Paulista (UNIP), São Paulo, Brazil

## Results

| Outcome | Modified macrogeometry | Conventional | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Insertion torque | Lower | — | P < .05 (test < control) |
| ISQ (0, 45, 90 d) | — | — | P > .05 (no difference) |
| TNF-α | Lower | — | P < .05 (test < control) |
| Other bone markers | — | — | P > .05 (no difference) |

Key interpretation: lower IT did not translate to inferior stability (ISQ); healing chambers may partially decouple torque-driven friction from RFA-measured stiffness. Reduced TNF-α may reflect lower osteoclastic activity — potentially a more favorable healing milieu.
