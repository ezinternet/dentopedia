## One-line Summary
Single-blinded RCT (n=54, 3 arms) in fully guided immediate placement: deeper thread depth raised insertion torque (more sites ≥40 Ncm) but did NOT improve longitudinal ISQ/ISV or 12-month MBL, and significantly increased angular deviation (matched MST vs MDT p=0.029), worst in mandibular molars.

## 한줄요약
완전가이드 즉시식립 RCT(54개, 3군): thread depth를 깊게 하면 식립토크는 올라가도(≥40 Ncm 비율↑) 시간에 따른 ISQ·ISV와 12개월 변연골은 동등했고, thread depth만 다른 두 군 비교에서 각도편차는 오히려 유의하게 커졌다(p=0.029, 특히 하악 대구치).

## Summary
This RCT isolates **thread depth** as a macro-design variable in the clinically hardest scenario — fully guided, flapless immediate placement into extraction sockets — where prior thread-depth evidence (healed ridges, FEA, synthetic bone) does not cleanly extrapolate. The matched MST (standard thread) vs MDT (deep thread) contrast shows that deeper threads buy higher insertion torque but no durable ISQ/ISV advantage and no MBL benefit at 12 months, while costing angular accuracy. The practical message: in immediate sockets, **primary stability is governed by local anatomy (septum, residual walls, apical engagement) more than by thread depth**, and aggressive thread engagement against residual walls drives off-axis drift.

## Key Contributions
- First clinical RCT testing thread depth on **both** primary stability and positional accuracy under immediate + fully guided placement.
- Clean within-design contrast (MST vs MDT, identical except thread depth) separated from a system-level reference arm (BLT).
- Adds an implant-design axis to the "what determines immediate ISQ" question alongside osteotomy protocol.

## Methodology
- Single-blinded 3-arm RCT, n=54 (18/arm), Yonsei Univ Dental Hospital, Aug 2021–May 2025, CONSORT 2025; reg KCT0009402 (retrospective).
- All self-tapping tapered SLA. Fully guided flapless (CBCT+IOS merge, R2GATE/Megagen, non-metal tripod guide); 3 freehand conversions excluded from accuracy, kept in ITT for stability.
- Predefined socket management: spontaneous (gap ≤2 mm) / gap-defect graft (gap >2 mm) / GBR (dehiscence ≥3 mm, collagen membrane); balanced across arms.
- Stability: insertion torque + ISQ (Osstell) + ISV (Any-Check damping) at placement and 1/3/6/12 mo; linear mixed-effects (no group×time interaction). Accuracy: plan↔postop IOS superimposition (Geomagic Control X). MBL: 12-mo PA radiograph, absolute level.

## Results
- **Insertion torque** (Ncm): BLT 35.28 ± 11.18 / MST 40.00 ± 11.88 / MDT 43.89 ± 10.92 (p=0.062). ≥40 Ncm achieved in 14/18 MDT vs 12/18 MST vs 8/18 BLT — deeper thread → more high-torque sites.
- **ISQ/ISV**: no significant group difference at any timepoint (all p>0.30); steady rise over time (largest gains 1→3 mo, 3→12 mo); 12-mo ISQ ~81–85 across all arms. Thread depth did not change the stability trajectory once early healing began.
- **Positional accuracy**: angular deviation BLT 4.73° / MST 3.58° / MDT 5.57° (overall p=0.062; **matched MST vs MDT p=0.029**). The 4 largest angular deviations (9.2–10.4°) were ALL in MDT mandibular molar sites. Horizontal/vertical/platform/apex: no significant difference.
- **MBL** at 12 mo: BLT −0.40 / MST −0.68 / MDT −0.53 mm (p=0.65), all coronal to platform — stable regardless of thread depth or simultaneous augmentation.
- 3 early failures (MST 1, MDT 2), attributed to local socket anatomy rather than thread design.

### Clinical takeaway
Deeper-thread designs are a reasonable choice **when extra primary stability is the priority** (e.g., compromised socket aiming at a torque threshold), but in immediate + guided molar cases they demand **deliberate angulation control** — the torque gain comes with a real angular-drift penalty and no longitudinal ISQ or marginal-bone payoff. Interpret BLT comparisons as system-level, not thread-depth, effects. Note MegaGen (implant manufacturer) part-funded the trial.
