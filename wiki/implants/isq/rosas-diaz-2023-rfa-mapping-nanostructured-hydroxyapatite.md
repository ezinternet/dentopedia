## One-line Summary

Prospective case series (n=21 analyzed, 8 patients; SIN-UNITITE nano-HA surface) mapping ISQ weekly over 20 weeks: universal stability dip at weeks 2–3, full recovery to or above baseline by weeks 8–10; larger diameter (4.3 mm) and higher torque (≥40 N-cm) confer the highest ISQ values throughout.

## Summary

This prospective observational case series performed weekly resonance frequency analysis (RFA) mapping on 23 SIN-UNITITE implants (nanostructured hydroxyapatite surface; S.I.N. Implant System, Brazil) placed in 8 patients (5 female, 3 male; age 20–79 years) in Lima, Peru (2017–2019). Two implants failed, leaving 21 for ISQ analysis across 20 weeks. Measurements used the Osstell device with SmartPegs in buccal-lingual and mesio-distal directions, averaged per implant.

The central finding is that all nano-HA implants showed a stability dip in the first 3 weeks post-placement — mirroring the pattern seen with conventional surfaces — followed by progressive ISQ recovery that reached or exceeded baseline by weeks 8–10. At week 20, all subgroups converged to ISQ values of 68–74. No subgroup escaped the early dip, confirming that the primary-to-secondary stability transition is a universal biologic phenomenon not abolished by nano-HA coating.

Subgroup analysis revealed that larger diameter (4.3 mm), longer implants (>10 mm), and higher insertion torque (≥40 N-cm) were associated with higher ISQ throughout. The maxilla showed an earlier and steeper dip (week 1) relative to the mandible (weeks 3–4), likely reflecting lower cortical bone density. Final ISQ values at week 20 were comparable between jaws.

## Key Contributions

- First systematic RFA time-course mapping for nanostructured hydroxyapatite (nano-HA) coated implants, providing a surface-specific ISQ trajectory reference
- Confirms the universal stability dip at weeks 2–3 for nano-HA implants, validating the primary→secondary stability model for this novel surface class
- Documents subgroup-specific dip timing: 4.3 mm diameter and ≥40 N-cm torque dip earlier (week 2) and recover faster; 3.5 mm and <40 N-cm dip at week 3 and recover more gradually
- Maxilla dips at week 1 (earlier, steeper) vs mandible at weeks 3–4; both reach similar ISQ by week 20
- Survival rate 91.3% (21/23) at 20 weeks without prosthetic loading

## Methodology

- **Design**: Prospective observational case series
- **Sample**: 8 patients, 23 implants placed; 21 analyzed (2 failed)
- **Implant**: SIN-UNITITE with nanostructured hydroxyapatite surface; lengths 8–15 mm; diameters 3.5 mm (n=12) and 4.3 mm (n=11)
- **Bone quality**: predominantly Type II (n=10) and Type III (n=6) by Lekholm/Zarb
- **Jaw distribution**: 17 mandible (73.9%), 6 maxilla (26.1%); 22/23 healed sites
- **Insertion torque**: balanced split — 11 implants <40 N-cm, 12 implants ≥40 N-cm
- **RFA device**: Osstell; measurements at day 0, suture removal, then weekly through week 20
- **ISQ calculation**: average of B-L and M-D direction readings; no statistical testing — purely descriptive mapping
- **Grouping**: by length (≤10 mm vs >10 mm), diameter (3.5 vs 4.3 mm), torque (<40 vs ≥40 N-cm), jaw (maxilla vs mandible)

## Results

### ISQ Time Course by Subgroup

| Subgroup | Baseline ISQ | Dip ISQ (week) | Week 20 ISQ |
|---|---|---|---|
| Length ≤10 mm | 58.1 | 41.6–41.9 (wk 3–4) | 68.8 |
| Length >10 mm | 62.5 | 49.4 (wk 2) | 71.8 |
| Diameter 3.5 mm | 54.3 | 35.3 (wk 3) | 68.1 |
| Diameter 4.3 mm | 69.0 | 57.2 (wk 2) | 73.7 |
| Torque <40 N-cm | 52.6 | 39.7 (wk 3) | 68.6 |
| Torque ≥40 N-cm | 67.9 | 50.8 (wk 2) | 72.2 |
| Maxilla | 60.5 | 42.7 (wk 1) | 68.1 |
| Mandible | 60.7 | 45.7–46.1 (wk 3–4) | 71.1 |

**Dip pattern rule**: Higher initial ISQ (larger diameter, higher torque) → dip occurs earlier (week 2) and at higher absolute ISQ. Lower initial ISQ (smaller diameter, lower torque) → dip later (weeks 3–4) and at lower absolute ISQ. Both groups converge by week 20.

**Maxilla vs Mandible**: Maxilla dips at week 1, earlier than mandible (weeks 3–4). Both recover to 68–71 ISQ by week 20.

**Directional mapping**: B-L and M-D readings were averaged per implant; no anisotropy analysis reported.

### Survival
- 2 implants failed to osseointegrate (excluded from ISQ analysis)
- 21/23 (91.3%) achieved osseointegration over 20 weeks
