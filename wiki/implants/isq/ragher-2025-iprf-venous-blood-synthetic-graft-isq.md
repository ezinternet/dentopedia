## One-line Summary

RCT (n=64) comparing no graft, venous blood, i-PRF, and NovaBone synthetic graft for indirect crestal sinus augmentation: i-PRF and NovaBone achieved ISQ ~70–76 at 6 months vs. ~55–62 for no graft/venous blood, with no significant difference between i-PRF and NovaBone.

## 한줄요약

간접 경치조골 상악동거상술 후 i-PRF군과 NovaBone 합성골군이 무이식·정맥혈군 대비 6개월 ISQ를 유의하게 높였으며(~70–76 vs ~55–62), 두 생물학적 강화재 간 차이는 없었다 (RCT, n=64).

## Summary

This prospective, parallel-group, double-blinded RCT compared four augmentation strategies for indirect crestal sinus augmentation in 64 patients with residual bone height (잔존골 높이, RBH) of 4–6 mm. All participants received 4.2 × 8 mm Noris Tuffimplants at 30 Ncm insertion torque. Implant stability quotient (임플란트 안정성 지수, ISQ) was measured by resonance frequency analysis (공명주파수분석, RFA) via Osstell SmartPeg at baseline and 6 months from buccal and mesial aspects.

At baseline all groups were equivalent (~ISQ 50–53). At 6 months, i-PRF and synthetic graft groups both surpassed ISQ 70 — the threshold associated with favorable stability and early loading readiness — while no-graft and venous blood groups remained at ISQ 55–62. The i-PRF and NovaBone groups showed no statistically significant difference from each other (p = 0.546 buccal; p = 0.749 mesial), confirming equivalent stability outcomes between an autologous biologic and a synthetic osteoconductive scaffold.

## Key Contributions

1. **First 4-arm RCT** directly comparing no graft, venous blood, i-PRF, and synthetic graft under the same indirect sinus augmentation protocol.
2. **ISQ 70 threshold crossed** by both i-PRF and NovaBone groups at 6 months — clinically actionable criterion for loading decisions.
3. **Venous blood clarified**: no significant advantage over no graft for ISQ outcomes; lacks structured fibrin matrix and concentrated growth factors.
4. **i-PRF equivalence to synthetic graft**: autologous, minimally invasive, chairside-efficient, avoids foreign biomaterial.
5. **Lower ISQ variability** in Groups 3 and 4 (CV 0.034–0.090 vs 0.099–0.250), indicating more predictable outcomes.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Parallel-group RCT, double-blinded (outcome assessor + statistician blinded)
- **Registration**: CTRI/2025/03/081640
- **n**: 64 (16 per group), aged 18–60, RBH 4–6 mm
- **Surgical system**: CAS Kit (Osstem); sequential drills 2.8→3.1→3.3 mm; hydraulic membrane elevation ~5 mm with 0.5 mL saline
- **Implant**: Noris Tuffimplant 4.2 × 8 mm (roughened, tapered), 30 Ncm
- **i-PRF protocol**: 10 mL venous blood, 60 g (700 rpm), 3 min; yellow upper layer aspirated
- **Groups**: (1) no graft, (2) 0.5 mL venous blood, (3) 0.5 mL i-PRF, (4) 0.5 cc NovaBone alloplastic putty
- **ISQ measurement**: Osstell, SmartPeg, buccal + mesial directions
- **Statistics**: Shapiro-Wilk normality check, one-way ANOVA, Tukey's post hoc, p < 0.05

## Results

**Baseline ISQ** (groups comparable, p > 0.05):
- Buccal: ~50.1–50.4; Mesial: ~53.1 across all groups

**6-month ISQ**:

| Group | Buccal Mean ± SD | Mesial Mean ± SD |
|---|---|---|
| 1 No graft | 54.81 ± 6.18 | 55.75 ± 8.10 |
| 2 Venous blood | 56.75 ± 5.63 | 61.50 ± 9.30 |
| 3 i-PRF | 70.06 ± 6.32 | 74.75 ± 2.52 |
| 4 NovaBone | 75.88 ± 2.87 | 76.38 ± 2.94 |

- ANOVA: p < 0.001 (both directions)
- Groups 3 & 4 vs Groups 1 & 2: p = 0.001 to p < 0.001
- Group 3 vs Group 4: p = 0.546 (buccal), p = 0.749 (mesial) — not significant
- Group 1 vs Group 2: p = 0.732 (buccal), p = 0.535 (mesial) — not significant
- No implant failures, membrane perforations, or postoperative infections

**Interpretation**: i-PRF's growth factors (PDGF, TGF-β1, VEGF) in a sustained-release fibrin matrix accelerate primary-to-secondary stability transition. NovaBone contributes via mechanical space maintenance and osteoconductive scaffold. Venous blood lacks structured architecture — insufficient to support osseointegration under functional loading in borderline RBH situations.
