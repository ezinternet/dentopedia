## One-line Summary

In vitro polyurethane-block study (36 implants, 3 macro-geometries × bone types 3 and 4): implant geometry and bone density were the key determinants of primary stability — triple-thread Zimmer TSV gave the highest insertion/removal torque, while double-thread, deeper-thread Dentium Superline gave the highest ISQ, in both low-density bone types.

## Summary

This in vitro study tested whether implant **macro-geometry** (thread design and body shape) drives primary stability in low-density bone. Thirty-six tapered implants (12 each: Straumann BLT, Zimmer TSV, Dentium Superline; all 10 mm, 4.0–4.1 mm diameter) were placed under controlled drilling into rigid polyurethane blocks simulating bone type 3 (20 PCF, 0.35 g/cm³) and type 4 (15 PCF, 0.24 g/cm³). Primary stability was captured by three metrics — ISQ via RFA (Osseo 100+), insertion torque (IT), and removal torque (RT).

The null hypothesis was rejected: all three metrics differed significantly across geometries (p<0.001), and the "best" implant depended on which metric was used. The triple-thread, small-pitch (0.6 mm) **Zimmer TSV** produced the highest IT and RT, while the double-thread, deep-thread (0.40 mm) **Dentium Superline** produced the highest ISQ — both consistent across bone types 3 and 4. Stability correlated strongly and positively with bone density for every design (R = 0.83–0.97). The authors stress that these are bench results that cannot be generalized clinically without trials, but they support choosing tapered implants with deeper, more aggressive, multi-thread designs for compromised bone such as the posterior maxilla.

## Key Contributions

- Isolates implant macro-geometry as the variable by holding body shape (tapered), length (10 mm), diameter (~4 mm), and drilling protocol constant across three commercial systems in standardized synthetic bone.
- Demonstrates a **metric-dependent ranking**: torque (IT/RT) → ZTSV > SBLT > DSL; stiffness (ISQ) → DSL > SBLT > ZTSV. High IT does not imply high ISQ — they reward different geometric features.
- Links specific design features to outcomes: small pitch + triple thread → high torque; deep double thread → high ISQ; half-apical self-cutting blade → lower RT.
- Confirms bone density is itself a dominant determinant (strong positive correlation in all designs), complementing the geometry effect.

## Methodology

- **Design**: in vitro, 3 groups × 2 bone densities, n=12 per group (36 implants).
- **Implants**: Straumann BLT (single thread, reverse-buttress, pitch 0.8 / depth 0.3 mm, self-cutting); Dentium Superline (double thread, reverse-buttress, pitch 1.8 / depth 0.40 mm, half-apical self-cutting blade); Zimmer TSV (triple thread, V-shape, pitch 0.6 / depth 0.36 mm, micro-thread, not self-cutting). All tapered, 10 mm, 4.0–4.1 mm.
- **Synthetic bone**: rigid polyurethane (SYNBONE), ASTM F-1839-08, 5×5×5 cm blocks; 20 PCF = type 3, 15 PCF = type 4.
- **Measurements**: controlled drilling/testing machine; IT and RT on a Hung Ta machine; ISQ by RFA with MultiPeg (Osseo 100+, NSK), measured buccal + mesial and averaged.
- **Statistics**: sample size via G*Power (effect size f=0.57, α=0.05, power 80%); IT/RT by one-way ANOVA, ISQ by Kruskal-Wallis with post hoc; SPSS 22; p<0.05.

## Results

**Bone type 3 (20 PCF):**
| Metric | SBLT | ZTSV | DSL |
|---|---|---|---|
| IT (Ncm) | 26.50 ± 2.36 | **30.21 ± 1.38** | 22.90 ± 1.60 |
| RT (Ncm) | 21.43 ± 1.59 | **23.25 ± 2.30** | 14.23 ± 2.33 |
| ISQ | 61.00 ± 0.52 | 60.88 ± 0.38 | **63.29 ± 0.54** |

**Bone type 4 (15 PCF):**
| Metric | SBLT | ZTSV | DSL |
|---|---|---|---|
| IT (Ncm) | 10.93 ± 0.34 | **18.07 ± 1.71** | 10.37 ± 1.15 |
| RT (Ncm) | 8.86 ± 1.02 | **14.48 ± 1.81** | 6.34 ± 0.74 |
| ISQ | 57.33 ± 1.05 | 55.70 ± 0.58 | **58.46 ± 0.78** |

- All metrics significantly different across groups (p<0.001).
- **IT/RT ranking**: ZTSV > SBLT > DSL. **ISQ ranking**: DSL > SBLT > ZTSV (both bone types).
- Non-significant post-hoc pairs: IT SBLT vs DSL (type 4, p=0.337); RT SBLT vs ZTSV (type 3, p=0.126); ISQ SBLT vs ZTSV (type 3, p=0.270).
- **Stability–density correlation (R)**: IT 0.943–0.959, RT 0.830–0.960, ISQ 0.842–0.968 — strong positive for all designs.
- Authors note optimal IT in low-density bone is ~30–35 Ncm; cite deeper threads → higher ISQ and smaller pitch / larger surface area → higher IT.
- **Limitations**: polyurethane ≠ biological bone; clinical variables (cortical thickness, anatomy, surgical technique, systemic factors) not captured; only primary stability assessed; not generalizable without clinical trials.
