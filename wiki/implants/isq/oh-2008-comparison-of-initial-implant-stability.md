# Comparison of Initial Implant Stability Across Systems (RFA Analysis)

**Source**: [[implants/isq/oh-2008-comparison-of-initial-implant-stability]]  
**Year**: 2008 | **Method**: Resonance Frequency Analysis (RFA) | **Key Measure**: ISQ Values

## Clinical Decision Framework

### Primary Stability Assessment Tool
This study established **Resonance Frequency Analysis (RFA)** as the non-invasive gold standard for measuring **Implant Stability Quotient (ISQ)** at insertion.

**ISQ Threshold Values for Clinical Decision-Making**:
- **ISQ < 60**: Extended healing period required; avoid immediate loading
- **ISQ 60-70**: Allows early loading with modified protocol; monitor closely
- **ISQ > 70**: Immediate or early loading candidate; loading can proceed with standard protocol

### Clinical Application
When choosing implant systems for specific bone conditions:
1. Select system based on bone quality and anticipated ISQ outcomes
2. Measure ISQ at insertion using Osstell device
3. Use measurement to determine loading protocol
4. Document baseline for stability monitoring over time

## Implant Selection by Bone Quality

### Different implant designs produce varying ISQ values
- **Implant design features** influence initial stability (thread pitch, surface, diameter, length)
- **Bone quality interaction** critical—same implant different in D1 vs. D4 bone
- Testing shows **cross-system variation**—not all implants equivalent
- **Clinical choice** should anticipate probable ISQ in specific patient bone

## Integration with Treatment Planning

**Modified Early Loading Decision Tree**:
```
Implant placed → Measure ISQ
├─ ISQ < 60: Delayed loading (4-6 months)
├─ ISQ 60-70: Early loading with occlusal adjustment (8-12 weeks)
└─ ISQ > 70: Immediate/early loading possible
```

## Monitoring Strategy

- **Baseline measurement** at insertion establishes stability baseline
- **Follow-up RFA** at 2-3 weeks and 6-8 weeks detects stability loss early
- **Increasing or stable ISQ** indicates osseointegration proceeding
- **Decreasing ISQ** flags potential issue—extends healing before loading
- **Non-invasive monitoring** eliminates removal torque testing risks

## Evidence Grade
**Prospective Clinical/In Vivo** — RFA validation in implantology accepted standard, though long-term predictive value continues to be studied
