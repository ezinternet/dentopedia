## One-line Summary
Prospective Korean cohort (54 patients, 104 implants, 3 systems) found significant baseline ISQ differences (Type A 76.2 > Type B 72.7 > Type C 67.6) that converged to non-significance by 2 months, with Type B/C increasing and Type A stable — a "catch-up" effect driven by implant surface and design.

## 한줄요약
전향적 코호트(54명, 104개 임플란트): 3종 임플란트(3i Osseotite, Replace Select, ITI SLA) 기저 ISQ 유의차(76.2 vs 72.7 vs 67.6) 있으나 2개월 시점에 수렴(catch-up 효과); 표면처리·형태가 ISQ 변화 궤적 결정.

## Summary

This 2008 single-centre prospective study compared 공명주파수분석 (Resonance Frequency Analysis, RFA) — measured as 임플란트 안정성 지수 (Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ) — across three implant systems with distinct surfaces and macro-designs, tracked over 4 months at Chonbuk National University.

**Key finding**: At surgery, ISQ differed significantly across systems (p<0.05). By 2 months, all three converged to near-equivalent values (~76–78), and the difference was no longer significant. The authors ascribe this to a **catch-up effect**: low-ISQ implants rise (lamellar bone replacing woven bone), while high-ISQ implants plateau or slightly decline.

## Key Contributions

1. **Catch-up convergence**: Baseline ISQ differences across three systems (3i Osseotite acid-etched, Replace Select TiUnite tapered, ITI SLA) disappear by 2 months — timing mirrors histological lamellar bone deposition (Barewal; Roberts).
2. **Surface and design affect trajectory, not just magnitude**: Type B (tapered + TiUnite) and Type C (SLA) increase; Type A (acid-etched) remains flat. Consistent with O'Sullivan et al. — tapered design and rough surface → higher bone-implant contact and ISQ gain.
3. **ITI supracrestal shoulder explains low baseline ISQ**: The 2.8 mm shoulder above the crest effectively extends the transducer height, reducing ISQ reading — not an inferior implant but an artefact of geometry (corroborates Meredith; Ersanli).
4. **System-specific normative ISQ ranges needed**: The paper argues against a single universal ISQ loading threshold; loading decisions should use system-specific reference values.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Prospective in vivo clinical cohort
- **n**: 54 patients (36M/18F), mean age 49 yr; 104 implants
- **Systems**:
  - Type A — 3i Osseotite® (Osseotite™ acid-etched surface), 36 implants
  - Type B — Replace® Select Tapered (Nobel Biocare; TiUnite™ electrochemical oxide, tapered), 48 implants
  - Type C — ITI (Straumann SLA® surface, 2.8 mm supracrestal shoulder), 20 implants
- **RFA device**: Osstell™ Mentor (magnetic smartpeg; ISQ ~8–12 units higher than older electronic Osstell)
- **Timepoints**: Baseline (surgery), 2 months, 4 months (one-stage); surgery + abutment connection (two-stage)
- **GBR concomitant**: 35 implants (33.7%) with autogenous bone / Bio-Oss® / mixture
- **Statistics**: Mann-Whitney U (between groups); paired t-test (within-implant change)

## Results

| Timepoint | Type A (3i) | Type B (Replace) | Type C (ITI) | p (between) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Baseline** | 76.2 (48.5–87.0) | 72.7 (45.0–84.0) | 67.6 (41.0–84.0) | **<0.05** |
| **2 months** | 76.8 (55.5–86.0) | 77.8 (67.0–85.5) | 76.4 (64.0–82.5) | NS |

**Within-implant ISQ change (matched subset, baseline → 2 months)**:
- Type B: significant increase (p<0.05) — TiUnite surface + tapered design promotes lamellar bone gain
- Type C: significant increase (p<0.05) — SLA surface promotes bone apposition
- Type A: slight decrease (NS) — acid-etched surface / parallel wall; high initial mechanical stability but less active bone gain

**4-month trend** (36 implants matched): Type B and C continue increasing; Type A remains stable.

**No failures** during the observation period → RFA as failure predictor could not be evaluated.
