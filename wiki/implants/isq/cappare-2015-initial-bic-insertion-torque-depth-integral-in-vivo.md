## One-line Summary
In vivo study testing whether the insertion torque/depth integral from an instantaneous torque-measuring motor correlates with initial bone-to-implant contact, supporting intra-operative bone-quality and primary-stability quantification.

## Summary
This clinical study evaluates an intra-operative micromotor that records bone density and insertion parameters (instantaneous, average, and peak torque plus the torque/depth integral) during implant placement, asking whether the torque/depth integral correlates with initial bone-to-implant contact. It moves the concept from the bench (later formalized in bovine ribs by the same group) into the patient, framing two practical levers: a real-time bone-quality reading and an objective primary-stability assessment at placement. As a publisher-page capture, the directional finding is reliable but pooled coefficients should be verified against the full article.

## Key Contributions
- In vivo validation of the torque/depth integral as a BIC correlate.
- Demonstrates a chairside device for objective bone-quality and primary-stability reads.
- Bridges bench torque-integral work to clinical practice.

## Methodology
In vivo clinical study; implants placed with an instantaneous torque-measuring micromotor; recorded bone density, insertion torque (instantaneous/average/peak), torque/depth integral; correlated with initial BIC.

## Results
- Torque/depth integral examined as a correlate of initial BIC.
- Intra-operative bone-quality + primary-stability quantification supported. (Pooled coefficients to verify in full text.) [미검증]
