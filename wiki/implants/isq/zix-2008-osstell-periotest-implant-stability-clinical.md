## Summary
Two non-invasive techniques dominate clinical implant stability measurement: **resonance frequency analysis (RFA, Osstell, ISQ scale)** and **damping capacity assessment (DCA, Periotest, PTV scale)**. Although both have been validated independently, head-to-head comparisons in the same implants and patients are uncommon. This 2008 University of Berne clinical study measured 213 one-stage implants in 65 patients in triplicate with both devices.

The two techniques correlated moderately to well (Pearson -0.64, Spearman -0.65; the negative sign reflects the inverse direction of the two scales). However, single-measure intraclass correlation revealed a clear precision gap: **ISQ ICC 0.99 vs Periotest ICC 0.88**. The Periotest distribution also deviated from a normal curve more than the ISQ. Implant **diameter** correlated significantly with both measurements; implant **length** did not. The authors conclude that resonance frequency analysis is the more precise technique and less susceptible to clinical measurement variables.

## Key Contributions
- Quantitative head-to-head comparison in 213 clinically stable implants.
- Establishes **ISQ as more precise and reproducible than Periotest** under matched clinical conditions.
- Confirms implant **diameter** (not length) as a determinant of both stability measurements — an early hint at what later ISQ literature (Stoilov 2023, Shiffler 2016) would refine.
- Provides population reference values for ISQ (~58) and PTV (~-5) in routine maintenance.

## Methodology
- 213 one-stage implants in 65 patients, stable (loaded + unloaded).
- Each implant measured in triplicate with Osstell (ISQ) and Periotest (PTV).
- Statistics: Pearson, Spearman, ICC; SPSS 11.0.2.

## Results
| Metric | ISQ (Osstell) | PTV (Periotest) |
|---|---|---|
| Mean ± SD | 57.66 ± 8.19 | −5.08 ± 2.02 |
| Single-measure ICC (95% CI) | **0.99** | 0.88 |
| Correlation to other technique | −0.64 (Pearson) / −0.65 (Spearman) | same |
| Correlation with implant length | NS | NS |
| Correlation with implant diameter | **Significant (p<.005)** | **Significant (p<.005)** |
| Distribution normality | Gaussian-like | Diverges from Gaussian |

Clinical implication: when a single, reproducible stability number per implant is needed (decision threshold for loading), **Osstell ISQ should be preferred**. Periotest remains useful as a complementary tool, but expect more measurement variance.
