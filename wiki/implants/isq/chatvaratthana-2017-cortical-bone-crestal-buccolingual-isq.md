## One-line Summary

Prospective study (n=19, Conelog, posterior jaws) showing crestal cortical bone thickness correlates strongly with ISQ at placement (r=0.885, p<0.001), with only 3 mm depth bone parameters (buccolingual cortical and cortical/cancellous ratio) being significant — 6 and 9 mm depths have no ISQ correlation.

## 한줄요약

전향적 연구(n=19, Conelog, 구치부): 치조정 피질골 두께가 ISQ와 강한 상관(r=0.885, p<0.001)이며, 3 mm 깊이의 협설측 피질골과 피질/해면골 비율만 유의—6·9 mm 깊이 파라미터는 ISQ와 무관.

## Summary

This prospective clinical study from Mahidol University investigated which bone parameters measured on CBCT correlate with ISQ at the time of implant placement. Nineteen Conelog implants (5×9 mm) were placed in 16 patients in posterior mandible (n=17) and maxilla (n=2). CBCT-derived measurements included crestal cortical bone thickness, buccolingual cortical and cancellous bone thickness at 3, 6, and 9 mm depths, and the cortical-to-cancellous ratio at each depth. ISQ was measured with Osstell at 4 orientations immediately after placement.

The key finding is that ISQ is determined almost entirely by bone characteristics within 3 mm of the crestal level. Crestal cortical bone thickness shows the strongest correlation (r=0.885, p<0.001), followed by buccolingual cortical bone at 3 mm (r=0.535, p=0.018) and the cortical/cancellous ratio at 3 mm (r=0.488, p=0.034). No significant correlations exist at 6 or 9 mm depths. Cancellous bone thickness at any depth shows no correlation with ISQ. These findings support the concept that RFA primarily measures marginal bone stiffness rather than overall bone quantity.

## Key Contributions

1. **Depth-specific ISQ determinants**: Only the 3 mm crestal zone correlates with ISQ; deeper bone is irrelevant to RFA readings.
2. **Strong crestal cortical correlation**: r=0.885 is among the highest reported for a single CBCT parameter vs. ISQ.
3. **Objective bone quality classification**: Cortical/cancellous ratio at 3 mm (simplified Leckholm & Zarb) stratifies ISQ — Group 1 (ratio >0.75) mean ISQ 80.5 vs Group 3 (ratio 0.25–0.50) mean ISQ 72.1.
4. **Cancellous bone irrelevance**: No correlation at any depth, challenging assumptions that trabecular density drives primary stability.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Non-randomized prospective clinical study; 16 patients, 19 implants (Conelog 5×9 mm, posterior)
- **CBCT**: 3D Accuitomo 170; 90 kVp/5 mA/17.5 s; voxel 0.125 mm; slice 0.5 mm; radiographic stent with gutta-percha markers
- **Bone parameters measured**: Crestal cortical (buccal+lingual); buccolingual cortical at 3/6/9 mm; cancellous at 3/6/9 mm; cortical/cancellous ratio at 3/6/9 mm (4 groups)
- **ISQ**: Osstell ISQ device; 4 measurement orientations; recorded at implant placement
- **Statistics**: Spearman's rank correlation; intra-observer ICC >0.986; p<0.05 significance threshold
