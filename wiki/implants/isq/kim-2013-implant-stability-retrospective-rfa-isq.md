## One-line Summary

Retrospective RFA study (90 Osstem implants, 72 patients) demonstrating that wider diameter (5 mm > 4 mm) and mandibular placement significantly increase ISQ, while implant length (10–13 mm) has no significant effect; all groups showed ISQ increase from surgery to impression taking.

## 한줄요약

Osstem 임플란트 90개 후향적 RFA 연구: 직경 5 mm가 4 mm보다, 하악이 상악보다 ISQ 유의하게 높음; 길이(10–13 mm)는 ISQ에 유의한 영향 없음; 전 군에서 식립 후 인상채득 시 ISQ 증가.

## Summary

This single-center retrospective study analyzed 90 Osstem US II plus implants (72 patients, 2007–2011) placed by a single operator in maxilla and mandible, anterior and posterior regions. RFA measurements using Osstell Mentor were taken at implant placement (T1) and immediately before impression taking (T2). Implants were grouped into six subgroups by diameter (4 mm, 5 mm) and length (10, 11.5, 13 mm), n=15 per group.

**Key findings**:
- **Diameter**: ø5 mm implants had significantly higher ISQ than ø4 mm at both time points (surgery: 79.26 vs 74.33; impression: 82.33 vs 77.43; P<.05).
- **Position**: Mandibular implants showed significantly higher ISQ than maxillary implants (surgery: 80.58 vs 75.82; impression: 83.29 vs 79.25; P<.05).
- **Length**: Increasing length from 10 to 13 mm showed a trend toward higher ISQ but no statistically significant difference for either diameter group (P>.05).
- **Gender**: No significant ISQ difference between male and female patients.
- **Temporal change**: ISQ was higher at T2 than T1 across all subgroups, confirming ongoing osseointegration during the healing period.

Most implants had ISQ ≥65 at placement, enabling early loading (mandible at ~8 weeks, maxilla at ~12 weeks — 1–2 months ahead of the conventional protocol).

## Key Contributions

1. Provides normative ISQ ranges for Osstem US II plus (RBM surface) at placement and loading: mean ISQ at surgery ranged 74–84 depending on diameter, with all groups exceeding ISQ 65 for early loading eligibility.
2. Confirms **diameter effect on ISQ** in a Korean clinical population with a domestic implant system.
3. Confirms **jaw position effect**: mandibular cortical bone density confers meaningfully higher ISQ (~5 ISQ units) over maxillary bone.
4. Establishes that in the 10–13 mm range, **length does not significantly alter primary or secondary stability** as measured by RFA.
5. Validates RFA (Osstell Mentor) as a clinically effective tool for tracking the change in stability through osseointegration at two time points.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Retrospective chart review, single private clinic, 2007–2011
- **Sample**: 72 patients (41 M / 31 F; mean age 45.3 yrs), 90 implants; 6 groups (n=15)
- **Implant**: Osstem US II plus, external hex, RBM surface; ø4/5 mm × 10/11.5/13 mm
- **Exclusion**: Uncontrolled systemic disease, heavy smokers, augmentation, immediate implants, bruxism, full edentulism
- **RFA**: Smartpeg + Osstell Mentor; buccal + palatal/lingual, 2 readings each → mean ISQ; measured at T1 (placement) and T2 (impression)
- **Loading criterion**: ISQ ≥65 at T1 → early loading; mandible ~8 wks, maxilla ~12 wks
- **Statistics**: SPSS 21.0; Independent t-test, one-way ANOVA, Tukey HSD, paired t-test; α=0.05

## Results

| Subgroup | ISQ at Surgery (mean ±SD) | ISQ at Impression (mean ±SD) |
|---|---|---|
| ø4 mm × 10 mm | 74.33 ±7.13 | 77.43 ±4.56 |
| ø4 mm × 11.5 mm | 76.03 ±6.84 | 79.33 ±5.21 |
| ø4 mm × 13 mm | 77.47 ±6.61 | 80.07 ±4.87 |
| ø5 mm × 10 mm | 79.27 ±5.51 | 82.33 ±3.46 |
| ø5 mm × 11.5 mm | 81.07 ±7.17 | 84.97 ±3.02 |
| ø5 mm × 13 mm | 83.90 ±5.27 | 85.90 ±4.34 |
| Maxilla (all) | 75.82 ±7.52 | 79.25 ±5.68 |
| Mandible (all) | 80.58 ±6.07 | 83.29 ±4.14 |

**Significant effects** (P<.05): diameter, jaw position, T1 vs T2 increase.  
**Non-significant** (P>.05): implant length, gender.
