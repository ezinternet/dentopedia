## One-line Summary

RCT (85 implants, 66 patients, 6-week dense ISQ/IST tracking) comparing Straumann BLX (tapered) vs BL (cylindrical): BLX had higher insertion torque (42.4 vs 30.6 N·cm) but BL achieved higher ISQ at 6 weeks (75.2 vs 72.2, p=0.02); both maintained high stability throughout, supporting either design for early loading protocols.

## Summary

This RCT from Chulalongkorn University (Bangkok) compared stability trajectories of two Straumann implant designs — tapered BLX and cylindrical BL (both SLA/Roxolid) — over 6 weeks of early healing using dense serial measurements (8 time points). Eighty-five implants were placed in 66 patients in healed edentulous sites (extraction ≥2 months prior).

BLX implants showed a significantly higher maximum insertion torque (MIT) at placement (42.35 vs 30.63 N·cm, p<0.001), attributable to tapered geometry compressing bone at the apical end. However, by Week 6 (T8), cylindrical BL implants reversed the advantage and showed higher ISQ (75.21 vs 72.23) and IST values (79.69 vs 76.06, p=0.02). Intermediate time points (T2–T7) showed no statistically significant differences between designs, suggesting the divergence emerges in the late biological stabilization phase.

A moderate positive correlation between ISQ and IST was confirmed (r=0.56, p<0.001). Both designs remained in the high-stability zone throughout, and the authors conclude both are suitable for various prosthetic loading protocols, including early loading.

## Key Contributions

1. **Dense ISQ/IST time series for two macro-designs**: 8 measurement points over 6 weeks isolates when stability differences emerge — only in the final week (T8), not during the dip/recovery phase.
2. **Counter-intuitive torque–ISQ dissociation**: Higher MIT for tapered (BLX) does not predict higher final ISQ; cylindrical (BL) converges and exceeds by 6 weeks.
3. **IST validated as ISQ surrogate**: r=0.56 confirms IST can substitute for ISQ for interim monitoring when RFA device unavailable.
4. **Clinical loading guidance**: Both designs support early-loading protocols — neither falls below stability thresholds at any monitored time point.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Parallel-arm RCT
- **Setting**: Chulalongkorn University Faculty of Dentistry, Bangkok
- **Inclusion**: Adult patients, healed edentulous ridge (extraction ≥2 months), single implant placement
- **Allocation**: Random assignment to BLX (tapered, n=45) or BL (cylindrical, n=40)
- **Implants**: Straumann SLA surface, Roxolid alloy (Ti-Zr); standard BLX vs BL geometry
- **Measurements**:
  - MIT at placement (T1)
  - ISQ (Osstell/RFA) at T1 and T8
  - IST at T1, T2 (Day 2), T3–T8 (weekly)
- **Primary outcome**: Stability change over 6 weeks
- **Trial registration**: TCTR20210628006; TCTR20230826014

## Results

| Parameter | BLX (Tapered) n=45 | BL (Cylindrical) n=40 | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| MIT (N·cm) | 42.35 ± 13.43 | 30.63 ± 6.01 | <0.001 |
| ISQ at T8 | 72.23 ± 0.88 | 75.21 ± 0.93 | 0.02 |
| IST at T8 | 76.06 ± 1.04 | 79.69 ± 1.11 | 0.02 |
| ISQ–IST correlation | r = 0.56 | — | <0.001 |

- T2–T7 differences: not statistically significant (both designs track similarly through early healing dip/recovery)
- Both groups maintained high stability zone (ISQ >70) at all measured time points
- ISQ at T1 (abstract-only — group breakdown not reported in abstract)

**Clinical interpretation**: Both designs are viable for early/immediate loading protocols. The tapered BLX gains biomechanical primary stability via compression (higher MIT), while cylindrical BL achieves equivalent or superior secondary (biological) stability by 6 weeks, consistent with lamellar bone remodeling around a larger bone-implant contact interface.
