## 한줄요약
시험관(in vitro) 연구, n=49 임플란트 (3개 부분실험) — 임플란트 안정성 지수 (Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ)와 Periotest 값 (Periotest Value, PTV)이 직접·연접 인터페이스 차이 (P<0.001)와 0/2/4/6 mm 수평노출 단계를 모두 검출했으나, 공명주파수분석 (Resonance Frequency Analysis, RFA) 기반 Osstell이 표준편차가 작고 경화 인터페이스의 강성 변화까지 추적해 더 신뢰성이 높았음. 임상 적용 한계: 아크릴 큐브·실리콘·자가중합 레진 인터페이스는 골 (bone) 실제 조건과 다르므로 ISQ 값의 절대 수치는 in vivo로 외삽할 수 없음.

## Summary
This in-vitro acrylic-cube study by Al-Jetaily and Al-Dosari directly compared the two leading implant-stability quantification systems — Osstell (resonance frequency analysis, ISQ) and Periotest (electronic percussion, PTV) — across three controlled scenarios designed to mimic the principal clinical failure modes: fibrous encapsulation, progressive crestal bone loss, and bone-interface stiffening over time. Forty-nine fixtures were embedded in acrylic cubes and tested in (1) hard vs soft interface, (2) four levels of horizontal fixture exposure (0, 2, 4, 6 mm), and (3) a hardening autopolymerizing resin interface.

Both systems were sensitive to interface changes and to bone-loss simulation (P < 0.001), but Osstell stood out on two fronts. First, its within-group standard deviations in hard and soft interfaces were smaller than Periotest's (Osstell SD 0.0 / 0.24 vs Periotest SD 0.24 / 0.28). Second, only Osstell tracked the time-dependent stiffening of the resin interface with a near-perfect correlation (r = 0.986) — an analogue of secondary stability development. Importantly, the soft-interface mean ISQ of 53.95 ± 0.24 sat just below Sennerby's published ISQ < 55 failure-risk threshold, supporting the clinical decision rule.

For everyday clinical use, this paper underwrites the standard ISQ interpretation: values around 70 reflect a well-integrated implant, values dropping toward and below 55 signal failure risk, and progressive crestal bone loss produces a roughly monotonic ISQ decline (here, 70 → 52 across 0 → 6 mm exposure). It does not, however, substitute for in-vivo validation, and the absolute ISQ values produced in a homogeneous acrylic block cannot be extrapolated to heterogeneous trabecular bone.

## Key Contributions
- Quantified ISQ–PTV correspondence in a controlled model: ISQ 70.0 at hard contact, ISQ 53.95 at soft interface — bridging the two scales clinically.
- Showed monotonic ISQ decline (70.4 → 64.1 → 57.5 → 52.1) with each 2 mm increment of horizontal fixture exposure; PTV mirrored this (−3.52 → +4.33).
- Osstell uniquely tracked progressive interface stiffening over 28 min (r = 0.986) — a mechanistic analogue of secondary stability development.
- Confirmed ISQ < 55 as a meaningful risk zone (matches Sennerby's published threshold and Osstell manufacturer's ISQ < 50 cutoff).
- Osstell was more reliable overall in hard and soft interfaces (lower SDs); Periotest's reliability paradoxically improved at low stability — a likely sensor-saturation artifact.

## Methodology
Forty-nine implant fixtures were placed in acrylic cubes. Part I tested 14 fixtures (7 hard, 7 soft polyvinyl siloxane). Part II divided 28 fixtures into four exposure groups (0, 2, 4, 6 mm horizontal exposure, n = 7 each). Part III placed 7 fixtures in autopolymerizing acrylic resin and measured ISQ across 28 minutes as it cured. Each fixture was measured with both Osstell (Smartpeg, ISQ 1–100) and Periotest (PTV scale). Group means, SDs, and Pearson correlations (exposure–PTV, exposure–ISQ, hardening time–stiffness) were computed. Significance threshold P < 0.001 for the main comparisons.

## Results
- **Hard vs soft interface (Part I)**: ISQ 70.0 ± 0.0 vs 53.95 ± 0.24; PTV −3.52 ± 0.39 vs significantly higher; both P < 0.001.
- **Horizontal exposure (Part II, 0/2/4/6 mm)**:
  - Mean ISQ: 70.43 / 64.14 / 57.47 / 52.14 (r = −0.946 with exposure)
  - Mean PTV: −3.52 / −1.14 / +1.28 / +4.33 (r = +0.967 with exposure)
- **Hardening interface (Part III, 28 min)**: ISQ rose monotonically; r = 0.986 between Osstell readings and resin stiffness change.
- **Reliability (SD across Part II groups, 0/2/4/6 mm)**:
  - Osstell: 0.00 / 0.24 / 0.40 / 0.32
  - Periotest: 0.39 / 0.00 / 0.00 / 0.14
