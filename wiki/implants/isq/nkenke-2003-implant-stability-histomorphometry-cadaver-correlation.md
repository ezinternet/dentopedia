## One-line Summary
Human cadaver correlation study (48 stepped-cylinder implants in 4 jaws) relating implant primary stability to CT bone mineral density and histomorphometric parameters of the implant site.

## 한줄요약

## Summary
This foundational ex vivo study establishes the biological substrate of primary stability. Using 48 stepped-cylinder implants placed in four unfixed human maxillae and mandibles, it correlated measured primary stability with CT-derived bone mineral density and histomorphometric parameters of each site. The result grounds the now-standard claim that primary stability is a read-out of local bone quality — the mechanistic anchor beneath modern insertion-torque and ISQ correlation studies. Limitations are the small donor count and absence of any healing phase.

## Key Contributions
- Direct correlation of primary stability with both BMD and histomorphometry in human bone.
- CT-based objective site characterization.
- Historical/mechanical anchor for stability–bone-quality reasoning.

## Methodology
Ex vivo human cadaver study; 4 unfixed maxillae/mandibles; 48 stepped cylinder screw implants; BMD by CT; histomorphometric parameters; correlation with primary stability.
