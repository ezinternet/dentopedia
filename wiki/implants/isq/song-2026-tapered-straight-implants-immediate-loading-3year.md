## One-line Summary

3-year RCT follow-up (n=28, 61 implants, posterior jaws): tapered self-tapping implants had numerically higher patient-level survival (95.8% vs 78.3%, NS) and markedly fewer composite immediate-loading protocol failures (4 vs 13) than straight implants; MBL was minimal (<1 mm) and statistically equivalent in both groups.

## Summary

This paper reports the 3-year follow-up of a two-arm parallel-group RCT comparing immediately loaded tapered implants (TI; Yuhan Evertis SI, self-tapping) versus straight implants (SI; Straumann Bone Level) in the posterior maxilla and mandible. Of the 41 patients who completed the original 1-year trial, 28 patients with 61 implants were available for 3-year analysis. All implant failures had occurred during the first year — zero late failures (years 1–3) were recorded in either group. Tapered implants demonstrated numerically superior patient-level survival (95.8% vs 78.3%, p=0.109) and far fewer composite protocol failures (4/36 vs 13/43 implants). MBL was low and equivalent between groups at 3 years (~0.23 vs 0.50 mm, NS). The main conclusion: tapered macro-design offers more predictable primary stability for immediate loading, but once osseointegration is established, design does not influence long-term outcomes.

## Key Contributions

- First RCT directly comparing tapered vs straight implants under immediate loading in posterior dentition with 3-year follow-up
- Zero late failures (after year 1) in both groups — immediate loading risk is concentrated in the osseointegration phase
- ITV ≥30 Ncm threshold: 2 SI implants failed to qualify for immediate loading (insufficient primary stability); zero TI implants excluded
- Composite protocol failure rate: SI 30.2% vs TI 11.1% — more than double in straight implants, driven mainly by early osseointegration failure and high annual MBL
- SLA surface on both implants: secondary stability (osseointegration rate) was equivalent — the tapered advantage was purely about primary stability
- Peri-implantitis case in TI group linked to convex emergence angle >30° in limited interproximal space, not implant macro-design
- Prosthetic complications (mechanical) occurred only in SI group (attributed to parafunction in 2 patients)

## Methodology

- **Design**: RCT follow-up; CONSORT-compliant
- **Setting**: Yonsei University College of Dentistry (periodontists + prosthodontists)
- **Original enrollment**: 48 patients (24 TI, 24 SI); 41 completed 1-year trial
- **3-year cohort**: 28 patients, 61 implants (TI: 15 patients/32 implants; SI: 13 patients/29 implants)
- **Implant systems**: TI = Yuhan Evertis SI (tapered, self-tapping); SI = Straumann Bone Level (straight, no cutting edge); both SLA surface
- **Loading**: Provisional splinted PMMA within 7 days; definitive prosthesis at 1 year
- **MBL assessment**: Standardized periapical radiographs (Rinn technique); mesial + distal average; calibrated to fixture length
- **Statistics**: Kaplan-Meier + Cox regression for survival; LMM with patient random intercept for MBL; SPSS v26 + R v4.4.0

## Results

**3-year survival rates** (Kaplan-Meier):

| Level | TI | SI | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Implant | 96.2% (90.1–99.5%) | 90.0% (81.4–99.5%) | 0.351 |
| Patient | 95.8% (88.2–100%) | 78.3% (63.1–97.1%) | 0.109 |

**Immediate loading protocol failures**:

| Criterion failed | TI | SI |
|---|---|---|
| ITV <30 Ncm (cannot load) | 0 | 2 |
| Early osseointegration failure | 2 | 5 |
| Annual MBL ≥0.2 mm | 2 | 6 |
| **Total** | **4** | **13** |

**MBL (LMM estimated means)**:

| Time | TI | SI | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 year | 0.13 ± 0.18 mm | 0.21 ± 0.20 mm | 0.543 |
| 3 years | 0.23 ± 0.42 mm | 0.50 ± 0.64 mm | 0.729 |
| Annual rate | 0.13 ± 0.13 mm/yr | 0.11 ± 0.14 mm/yr | 0.868 |

**Complications**:
- Biologic (peri-implantitis): 2 TI implants (1 patient) — MBL progressed to ~7 mm at 3 years; conservatively managed
- Mechanical: 2 SI patients — bridge exfoliation, partial crown fracture; immediately restored
