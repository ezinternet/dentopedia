## One-line Summary

Korean in-vitro study (n=8 SmartPegs/experiment, 2008) demonstrating ISQ stability through 400 repeated measurements, 400 attach-detach cycles, and six clinical sterilization methods, with magnetic loss (non-functional SmartPeg) occurring only at ≥150°C — above all standard clinical sterilization temperatures.

## 한줄요약

한국 체외 연구(2008, SmartPeg 8개/실험): SmartPeg은 400회 측정·탈부착 및 6종 임상 소독법 후 ISQ 유의 변화 없으나, ≥150°C 에서 자성 소실로 측정 불가(임상 소독 최고온도 132°C/15분 이하이므로 임상적으로 안전).

## Summary

Won et al. (2008, Dankook University) conducted a bench-top in-vitro study to determine whether SmartPeg™ transducers — the magnetic resonance pegs used with the Osstell™ Mentor RFA device — remain reliable under conditions of repeated use and clinical sterilization. The manufacturer recommended single use, but the study tested five experimental conditions across 8 SmartPegs each:

1. **400 fixed repeated measurements**: ISQ constant (mean=75 all units; p>0.01, no change)
2. **400 attach-detach cycles**: ISQ range 73.2–74.1; no significant drift (p>0.05)
3. **Six sterilization methods (single application)**: Autoclave, UV, hot-oil (Dentistar), boiling water, H₂O₂+alcohol, vacuum dry heat (138°C/5 min) — all produced zero ISQ change (p>0.01)
4. **10 repeated autoclave cycles**: ISQ 73.4–74.8; no change (p>0.05)
5. **Curie temperature test (magnetic loss threshold)**:
   - 138°C/10 min → no loss
   - 150°C/5 min → 2/8 SmartPegs lost magnetism (ISQ=0)
   - 150°C/10 min → 8/8 SmartPegs lost magnetism (ISQ=0)

The study concluded that SmartPegs can be safely reused and resterilized by any standard clinical method without ISQ measurement error, provided thread integrity is maintained and temperature does not exceed ~138–150°C.

## Key Contributions

- **First systematic SmartPeg reuse/sterilization study** from a Korean academic center (published 2008 in 대한치과보철학회지)
- Empirically challenged the manufacturer single-use recommendation — no ISQ drift found up to 400 uses
- Identified the magnetic loss (Curie point) boundary: safe below 138°C, loss begins at 150°C
- Established that all standard clinical autoclave protocols (132°C/15 min) fall safely below this threshold
- Provided paired t-test evidence that SmartPeg ISQ is robust to mechanical reuse (attach-detach) and chemical/thermal sterilization

## Methodology

- **Device**: Osstell™ Mentor + SmartPeg™ Type IV; Osstem fixture Ø4.1×10 mm placed in D3 artificial bone block (Osstem)
- **Setup**: Osstell™ Mentor and bone block rigidly fixed on plastic board to eliminate positional variability
- **SmartPegs**: 8 per sub-experiment, color-coded to track individual units
- **Sterilization equipment tested**:
  - Autoclave 1321 (Hanshin Medical, Seoul) — 132°C/15 min standard protocol
  - UV sterilizer (Bubang Techron, Seoul)
  - High-temperature oil sterilizer (Dentistar, BIG LINE, Japan)
  - Boiling water (10 min)
  - 35% H₂O₂ + 83% Ethanol solution
  - Vacuum dry-heat unit (Sejong Dental, Seoul) — tested at 138°C/5, 138°C/10, 150°C/5, 150°C/10 min
- **Statistics**: Paired t-test; α=0.01 (experiments 1, 3, 5) or α=0.05 (experiments 2, 4)
- **Limitation**: Single implant system, D3 artificial bone only; no in-vivo validation; thread wear not imaged

## Results

| Condition | n | Result | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fixed repeated measurement (400×) | 8 | ISQ mean=75, stable | >0.01 |
| Attach-detach cycles (400×) | 8 | ISQ 73.2–74.1, stable | >0.05 |
| Autoclave (1×) | 8 | No change | >0.01 |
| UV sterilization (1×) | 8 | No change | >0.01 |
| Hot-oil sterilization (1×) | 8 | No change | >0.01 |
| Boiling water 10 min (1×) | 8 | No change | >0.01 |
| H₂O₂ + Alcohol (1×) | 8 | No change | >0.01 |
| Vacuum dry heat 138°C/5 min (1×) | 8 | No change | >0.01 |
| Autoclave repeated (10×) | 8 | ISQ 73.4–74.8, stable | >0.05 |
| Vacuum dry heat 138°C/10 min | 8 | No change | >0.05 |
| Vacuum dry heat 150°C/5 min | 8 | **2/8 non-functional** | — |
| Vacuum dry heat 150°C/10 min | 8 | **8/8 non-functional** | — |

**ISQ context** (from study): maxilla 50–60, mandible 60–80; ISQ<45 = stability warning; successful osseointegrated implants ≈60–70.
