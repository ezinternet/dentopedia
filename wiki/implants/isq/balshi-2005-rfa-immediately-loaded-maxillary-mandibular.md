## Summary

Balshi et al. (2005) prospectively measured resonance frequency analysis (RFA) stability in 51 patients (22 male, 29 female) receiving 344 Brånemark System implants under the Teeth-in-a-Day immediate functional loading protocol. RFA was performed at placement (T0) and at 30, 60, and 90 days postoperatively. Of the 344 implants, 276 had complete four-timepoint data and formed the analysis set.

The key finding was a characteristic ISQ dip at one month — mean ISQ fell from 70.35 ± 0.50 at placement to 66.38 ± 0.50 at 30 days, then recovered to 68.01 ± 0.50 at 60 days and 68.82 ± 0.49 at 90 days. The authors interpreted this trajectory as adaptive bone remodeling around the immediately loaded implant. Clinical implant survival was 98.5% (5 failures).

Stratified analyses showed consistently lower ISQ values in softer bone (Lekholm-Zarb type IV), posterior jaw locations, and female patients. The authors defined "predictor" ISQ stability levels for high-risk clinical scenarios — particularly soft bone combined with long inter-implant spans — and concluded that an undisturbed healing period of at least 2 months is warranted to allow the remodeling dip to resolve before modifying loading protocols.

## Key Contributions

1. **ISQ dip trajectory**: First large prospective documentation of the ISQ decrease at ~30 days post-immediate loading followed by spontaneous recovery, establishing the concept of an osseointegration stability dip.
2. **Predictor ISQ thresholds**: Identified that soft bone + posterior site + long inter-implant span = highest-risk ISQ profile, requiring clinical vigilance even when initial ISQ appears adequate.
3. **2-month undisturbed healing recommendation**: Derived from the ISQ recovery curve; loading protocol modification before 60 days risks intervening during the stability nadir.
4. **Bone type / position / sex modifiers**: All three variables independently associated with lower ISQ, consistent with reduced bone density effects on implant primary stability.

## Results

**ISQ trajectory over 90 days:**

| Timepoint | Mean ISQ ± SE |
|---|---|
| Placement (T0) | 70.35 ± 0.50 |
| 30 days (T1) | 66.38 ± 0.50 |
| 60 days (T2) | 68.01 ± 0.50 |
| 90 days (T3) | 68.82 ± 0.49 |

**Stratified findings:**
- **Bone type**: Type I (dense) > Type II > Type III > Type IV (soft) ISQ at all timepoints; greatest dip magnitude in type III–IV
- **Jaw position**: Posterior ISQ consistently lower than anterior; this applied to both maxilla and mandible
- **Sex**: Female ISQ lower than male, most pronounced in soft bone; sex × bone type interaction not formally tested
- **Survival**: 98.5% implant survival at 90 days (5 failures/344); failures clustered in soft bone and posterior regions

**Clinical predictor framework:**
- Soft bone (type III/IV) + posterior location + long inter-implant span = highest-risk profile
- These conditions justify more conservative ISQ targets before immediate loading and closer monitoring during the 30-day dip window
