## One-line Summary
Retrospective study (41 tapered immediately-loaded implants, 22 fresh sockets vs 19 healed bone): fresh-socket implants started with significantly lower ISQ (57.6 vs 72.9, p<0.001) but converged by 4 months, with 100% survival — showing insertion torque (ITV ≥35 Ncm), not ISQ alone, is the reliable immediate-loading criterion in fresh sockets.

## Summary
Retrospective study of 41 tapered implants placed in fresh extraction sockets (n=22) vs healed bone (n=19) with immediate loading. Fresh socket implants start with significantly lower ISQ (57.6) but converge with healed-bone ISQ by 4 months. Insertion torque value (ITV ≥35 Ncm) was the primary immediate loading criterion — ISQ alone was insufficient for fresh socket decisions.

## Key Finding

| Group | T0 ISQ | T1 (4mo) ISQ | p (T0) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fresh extraction socket | 57.55 ± 1.93 | 68.68 | <0.001 |
| Healed bone | 72.86 ± 5.25 | 74.54 | — |
| p (T1) | — | NS | — |

- Both groups: 100% survival, all loaded immediately
- Loading criterion: ITV ≥35 Ncm (not ISQ threshold)

## Clinical Takeaway
- **Fresh socket ISQ is unreliable as an immediate loading criterion** — 57 is below the conventional ≥70 threshold yet all implants succeeded with IT ≥35 Ncm
- ITV is the practical loading criterion in fresh sockets; ISQ is useful for monitoring trajectory
- Both groups converge by 4 months → ISQ meaningful for follow-up, not for placement decision in fresh sockets
