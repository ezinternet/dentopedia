## One-line Summary
Prospective 1-year study (48 Neoss ProActive Tapered single implants): a simplified protocol loading at 6–8 weeks when insertion torque (IT) >40 Ncm (met by 96%) showed no stability dip, stable ISQ, marginal bone loss of −0.47 mm at 1 year, and 100% survival.

## Summary
Prospective 1-year study of a simplified loading protocol for Neoss ProActive Tapered implants. Criterion: IT >40 Ncm → loading at 6–8 weeks. Result: 96% of implants met the criterion, ISQ was stable (no dip observed), MBL was acceptable at 1 year, and survival was 100%.

## Results

| Timepoint | Observation |
|---|---|
| T0 (placement) | IT>40 Ncm in 46/48 (96%); ISQ measured |
| T1 (6 weeks) | Provisional loading; ISQ stable |
| T2 (3 months) | ISQ stable or slightly increased |
| T3 (6 months) | ISQ maintained |
| T4 (1 year) | MBL −0.47 ± 0.38 mm; 100% survival |

- **No stability dip observed** — tapered ProActive design maintains primary stability during early healing
- MBL within acceptable range (<1 mm at 1 year)
- No peri-implantitis, no prosthetic complications

## Clinical Takeaway
- IT >40 Ncm = reliable simplified loading criterion for tapered implants
- 6–8 week loading is safe in this protocol without waiting for full ISQ maturation
- Absence of stability dip suggests tapered ProActive design preserves mechanical stability during the remodeling phase
