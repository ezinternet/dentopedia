## One-line Summary

Prospective RFA study (36 patients, 106 ITI SLA implants: 63 immediately loaded + 43 delayed) showing Osstell could not reliably detect mobile implants, but all delayed-loaded implants with ISQ-at-placement ≥49 and all immediately-loaded implants with ISQ-at-placement ≥54 achieved and maintained osseointegration at 1 year — with healing-phase stability trajectories differing by ISQ-at-placement band.

## Summary

Nedir et al. (2004) is one of the early prospective clinical resonance-frequency analysis (RFA) studies that tried to convert primary stability into a clinical loading-decision rule. Across 36 patients and 106 ITI implants with a sandblasted, large-grit, acid-etched (SLA) surface — 63 immediately loaded (IL) and 43 delayed-loaded (DL, loaded at 3 months) — implant stability quotient (ISQ) was tracked with the Osstell device at placement and at 1, 2, 4, 6, 8, 10 and 12 weeks.

Two messages stand out. First, RFA is **not** a reliable chairside test for detecting a mobile implant (a negative finding). Second, despite that, the **ISQ at placement (ISQi)** carries protocol-specific predictive value: after 1 year, every DL implant with ISQi ≥49 and every IL implant with ISQi ≥54 was osseointegrated. The two failures had ISQi of 48 (DL) and 53 (IL) — each just below its protocol's threshold. The immediate-loading protocol thus demands a **higher** placement stability than delayed loading.

The paper also described the now-familiar **ISQi-band-dependent stability curve** during healing: low-stability implants gain stability, mid-band implants dip transiently then recover, and the stiffest implants dip early then plateau — a pattern the authors used to argue for selective monitoring.

## Key Contributions

- **Protocol-specific ISQi cut-offs**: DL ≥49 and IL ≥54 for predicted/maintained osseointegration at 1 year — IL threshold is higher than DL.
- **RFA negative finding**: Osstell RFA was not a reliable diagnostic tool to identify mobile implants.
- **Reliability floor**: stability could be reliably determined for implants displaying ISQ ≥47.
- **ISQi-band stability trajectories** during the first 3 months of healing (rise vs transient dip vs early dip-then-plateau).

## Methodology

- **Design**: prospective clinical RFA study, two loading protocols.
- **Sample**: 36 patients, 106 ITI SLA implants — IL group 18 patients/63 implants; DL group 18 patients/43 implants (loaded after 3 months).
- **Measurement**: ISQ via Osstell at placement (ISQi) and at weeks 1, 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12.
- **Aims**: (1) Osstell as a stable-vs-mobile discriminator; (2) a placement-time ISQ cut-off (ISQitv) predictive of osseointegration; (3) compare the predictive cut-off between IL and DL implants.
- **Endpoint**: 1-year loading control / osseointegration status.

## Results

- All implants passed the 1-year loading control; **2 failures**: 1 DL (ISQi 48) and 1 IL (ISQi 53).
- RFA **not reliable** to identify mobile implants.
- Stability **reliably determinable for ISQ ≥47**.
- Osseointegration maintained at 1 year for **all DL implants with ISQi ≥49** and **all IL implants with ISQi ≥54**.
- Healing-phase stability by ISQi band (by 3 months):
  - **ISQi < 60** → stability increased.
  - **ISQi 60–69** → decreased over ~8 weeks, then returned to initial values.
  - **ISQi > 69** → decreased over the first ~4 weeks, then remained stable.
- Clinical implication: the data may orient the practitioner in choosing loading protocols and in selectively monitoring implants during healing.
