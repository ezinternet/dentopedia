## One-line Summary

Retrospective case series (30 ARP sites, 28 patients) showing that extending implant osteotomy 3 mm beyond ARP graft boundary increases insertion torque (33.33 vs. 17.08 Ncm) but yields equivalent ISQ at 10 weeks (80.66 vs. 76.44), with a moderate positive torque–ISQ correlation (r=0.415).

## 한줄요약

후향적 증례연구(30부위, 28명): ARP 경계 초과 임플란트는 삽입 토크가 높았으나(33.33 대 17.08 Ncm), 10주 임플란트 안정성 지수 (ISQ)는 양 군 간 유의 차이 없음(80.66 대 76.44); 삽입 토크-ISQ 중등도 양의 상관(r=0.415).

## Summary

This retrospective case series from a single private clinic in Seoul compared ISQ (임플란트 안정성 지수, ISQ) outcomes between implants placed entirely within an alveolar ridge preservation (치조제보존술, ARP) graft boundary vs. those extending up to 3 mm beyond the boundary into pristine bone.

ARP was performed with DBBM-C (Bio-Oss Collagen) and porcine collagen membrane (Bio-Gide) using the open membrane technique in periodontally compromised extraction sockets. Implant placement followed a minimum 16-week healing period. All implants were placed 0.5–1 mm subcrestally with a 1-stage protocol; healing abutments were connected at placement.

The key finding: vertical implant positioning within vs. beyond the ARP boundary significantly affected initial insertion torque (삽입 토크) — the beyond-ARP group had nearly double the torque (33.33 vs. 17.08 Ncm, p=0.018) — but this advantage did not translate into superior ISQ (공명주파수분석, RFA) at 10 weeks. Both groups exceeded the ISQ ≥70 threshold used for loading decisions, and no significant difference was detected in 10-week ISQ.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates that ARP grafted sites (DBBM-C + collagen membrane) can support implants with ISQ ≥70 even when the implant remains entirely within the graft — pristine bone engagement is not required for adequate 10-week stability
- Distinguishes primary stability (삽입 토크 반영) from secondary stability (10주 ISQ 반영): initial torque advantage of beyond-ARP disappears by 10 weeks
- Provides moderate positive correlation between insertion torque and 10-week ISQ (r=0.415, p=0.023), suggesting low initial torque implants may still osseointegrate adequately

## Methodology

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Design | Retrospective case series (STROBE) |
| Sites | 30 ARP sites, 28 patients (Nov 2015–Jul 2019) |
| Within-ARP | 12 sites (12 patients) |
| Beyond-ARP | 18 sites (16 patients) |
| ARP material | DBBM-C (Bio-Oss Collagen) + Bio-Gide membrane, open membrane technique |
| Healing before implant | 16–24 weeks |
| Implant brands | Astra Osseospeed, Dentium Superline |
| Diameter | Mostly ≥5 mm |
| Length | Mostly 8–10 mm |
| Placement depth | 0.5–1 mm subcrestal |
| Protocol | 1-stage (healing abutment at placement) |
| ISQ measurement | 10 weeks post-placement (Osstell RFA) |
| Loading threshold | ISQ ≥70 → impression for prosthesis |
| Statistics | Multiple linear regression, Pearson correlation |

## Results

| Parameter | Within-ARP | Beyond-ARP |
|---|---|---|
| Insertion torque (Ncm) | 17.08 ± 11.17 | 33.33 ± 13.39 |
| ISQ at 10 weeks | 76.44 ± 6.62 | 80.66 ± 6.27 |
| Bone quality type 3 | 75% | 72.2% |
| Bone quality type 4 | 25% | 27.8% |

- **Insertion torque**: beyond-ARP group significantly higher (β=14.14, p=0.018 at implant level)
- **ISQ**: no significant difference between groups at 10 weeks
- **Torque–ISQ correlation**: r=0.415, R²=0.172, 95%CI 0.028–0.345, p=0.023
- **Implant failure**: 1/12 in within-ARP group (failure at 2 years after loading; insertion torque 10 Ncm, ISQ 72.5 at 10w)
- **Survival**: 29/30 implants survived through prosthetic delivery (96.7%)
- No type 1 or 2 bone quality found in either group (all type 3 or 4 — soft bone at ARP sites)
