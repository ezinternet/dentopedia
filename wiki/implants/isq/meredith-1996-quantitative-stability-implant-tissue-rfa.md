## One-line Summary

The landmark 1996 method paper that introduced **resonance frequency analysis (RFA)** to implant dentistry: an in-vitro + in-vivo study showing a transducer's resonance frequency correlates strongly (r=0.94, p<0.01) with exposed fixture height and rises with surrounding-material stiffness — the conceptual origin of the modern ISQ scale.

## 한줄요약

공명주파수분석 (Resonance Frequency Analysis, RFA)을 임플란트학에 처음 도입한 1996년 기념비적 방법 논문 — in-vitro·in-vivo 연구에서 트랜스듀서 공명주파수가 노출된 고정체 높이와 강한 상관(r=0.94, p<0.01)을 보이고 주변 재료 강성(stiffness)이 커지면 상승함을 입증했으며, 현대 임플란트 안정성 지수 (Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ) 척도의 개념적 기원이다.

## Summary

Before this paper, clinicians judged implant stability with crude, poorly reproducible methods — percussing the fixture with a blunt instrument and reading non-standardised radiographs. Meredith, Alleyne and Cawley (Bristol) proposed a **non-invasive, quantitative** alternative: attach a small transducer to the implant fixture and measure its **resonance frequency**, which tracks the stiffness/stability of the implant-tissue interface.

In an aluminium-block model the resonance frequency correlated strongly with the **height of fixture exposed** (r=0.94, p<0.01), showing RFA is sensitive to effective implant length / marginal support level. To mimic bone maturation during healing, implants were embedded in self-curing **PMMA** and measured during polymerisation; resonance frequency **increased as interfacial stiffness increased**. In-vivo measurements correlated well with the in-vitro results.

This is the **primary-source origin of RFA** and, downstream, of the **Implant Stability Quotient (ISQ)** scale that pervades modern stability/loading decisions. The 1996 paper itself reports raw resonance frequency (kHz), not the later normalised 1–100 ISQ value, and does not set clinical loading thresholds.

## Key Contributions

- **First introduction of RFA** as a non-invasive, quantitative implant-stability test, replacing percussion/radiograph-based crude assessment.
- Established that **transducer resonance frequency reflects interface stability** — the founding principle of RFA/ISQ.
- Demonstrated sensitivity to **exposed fixture height** (r=0.94, p<0.01) — RFA detects effective implant length and marginal bone level.
- Modelled **healing stiffness** with PMMA polymerisation: resonance frequency rises as the surrounding material stiffens, paralleling osseointegration.
- **In-vivo validation** consistent with in-vitro findings, supporting clinical translation.

## Methodology

Three linked experiments using transducer resonance frequency as the single output:

1. **Exposed-height model (in-vitro)** — fixtures embedded at varying heights in an aluminium block; resonance frequency vs exposed height measured → r=0.94, p<0.01.
2. **Stiffness/healing model (in-vitro)** — implants embedded in self-curing PMMA; resonance frequency tracked during polymerisation as a proxy for bone maturation.
3. **In-vivo measurement** — RFA performed on implants in vivo; results compared against in-vitro data.

## Results

- **Resonance frequency ↔ exposed fixture height**: strong correlation, **r = 0.94, p < 0.01**.
- **PMMA polymerisation**: significant **increase in resonance frequency** with rising surrounding-material stiffness.
- **In-vivo**: measurements **correlated well** with in-vitro findings.

Absolute frequency values, sample sizes, and per-experiment statistics are not present in the abstract-only PDF and are therefore not reported here.
