## One-line Summary
Systematic review (PubMed/Scopus/EMBASE/Cochrane/EBSCO, Jan 2013–Dec 2022): most included studies showed osseodensification (OD) drilling yielded significantly higher primary implant stability (ISQ/RFA) and bone density than conventional drilling, but the included RCTs carried HIGH overall risk of bias (RoB 2), limiting confidence and signaling the need for well-designed low-RoB RCTs.

## Summary
Systematic review comparing osseodensification (OD) vs conventional drilling for primary implant stability, as measured by ISQ/RFA and bone density. Searched PubMed, Scopus, EMBASE, Cochrane, and EBSCO (Dentistry and Oral Science Source) through December 2022. Included RCTs and NRSIs reporting ISQ with ≥3 months follow-up. Majority of studies showed significantly higher bone density and ISQ in the OD group. Quality assessment: NRSIs showed low overall RoB (ROBINS-I); RCTs showed HIGH overall risk of bias (RoB 2) — limiting confidence in pooled conclusions. Osseodensification appears to improve primary stability, but high-quality confirmatory evidence remains lacking.

## Key Contributions
- Most studies support OD → higher ISQ and bone density vs conventional drilling
- NRSIs (observational evidence): low RoB — more trustworthy within their design limits
- RCTs: HIGH overall RoB — major limitation that undermines the review's conclusions
- Identifies gap: need for well-designed, low-RoB RCTs (Konuklu 2026 partially addresses this)
- OD principle: clockwise compaction preserves bone volume, densifies trabecular bone at osteotomy walls

## Methodology
PRISMA SR. Databases: PubMed, Scopus, EMBASE, Cochrane Oral Health Group, EBSCO. Search: January 2013–December 2022. Inclusion: RCTs and NRSIs, OD vs conventional, ISQ reported, ≥3 months follow-up. Risk of bias: RoB 2 (RCTs), ROBINS-I (NRSIs). 2 independent investigators.

## Results
- ISQ: majority of studies → OD significantly higher
- Bone density: majority of studies → OD significantly higher
- NRSI overall RoB: LOW
- RCT overall RoB: HIGH (limits conclusions)
