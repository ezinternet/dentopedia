## One-line Summary
Prospective 5-month clinical study: ISQ (Osstell) correlates significantly with insertion torque and bone density (D1/D2 > D3/D4), and ISQ ≥65 at placement supports early/immediate loading; ISQ shows a typical stability dip at 3–4 weeks recovering by 8–12 weeks.

## 한줄요약

## Summary
Prospective 5-month clinical study investigating correlations between ISQ values (Osstell) and implant insertion variables (torque, bone density) as predictors for loading protocol selection. Demonstrates significant correlations between insertion torque and ISQ, and between bone density (type) and ISQ. ISQ ≥65 at placement emerges as a threshold for early/immediate loading protocols.

## Key Findings
- Significant positive correlation between insertion torque and ISQ at placement
- Bone density type significantly affects ISQ: D1/D2 > D3/D4
- ISQ ≥65 at placement supports early/immediate loading
- ISQ trajectory monitored monthly: typical stability dip at 3–4 weeks, recovery by 8–12 weeks
