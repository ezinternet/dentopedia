## One-line Summary
Prospective controlled clinical trial (n=36 patients, 60 implants in D3/D4 bone): nano-superhydrophilic bioactive surface maintained ISQ stability with no stability dip, whereas traditional SLA-type surface implants showed significant ISQ decrease at 30 and 45 days; bioactive group required fewer delayed-loading cases (0 vs 6 at 180 days).

## 한줄요약
전향적 임상시험(n=36, D3/D4, 60개 임플란트): 생체활성 나노표면 = ISQ 안정 유지(딥 없음) vs 전통표면 ISQ 30·45일 유의하게 감소; 생체활성군 조기 부하 가능성 증가.

## Summary
Controlled clinical trial comparing nano-superhydrophilic bioactive implant surface vs traditional SLA-type surface in 36 patients with D3/D4 bone quality (30 implants/group). The bioactive surface prevents the stability dip phenomenon, maintaining ISQ stability throughout the primary-to-secondary stability transition. Traditional surface implants show significant ISQ decrease at 30 and 45 days, requiring delayed loading in 6 cases.

## Key Contributions
- Demonstrates that bioactive surface eliminates ISQ stability dip in poor bone quality
- Provides loading protocol advantage: fewer implants requiring delayed loading (4 vs 7)
- Torque density (force rate) — not IT or ISQ at baseline — predicts early ISQ in D3 bone
- Clinically relevant for the challenging D3/D4 posterior maxilla scenario

## Results
| Timepoint | Bioactive (test) ISQ | Traditional (control) ISQ |
|-----------|---------------------|--------------------------|
| T0 (placement) | 74.57 ± 7.85 | 77.12 ± 5.83 |
| T30 (30 days) | 74.78 ± 7.31 | 73.33 ± 6.13* |
| T45 (45 days) | 74.97 ± 6.34 | 73.44 ± 7.89* |

*Significant decrease vs T0 (p<0.05)

Loading outcomes (ISQ ≥70 threshold):
- 45-day loading: 26 bioactive vs 23 control
- 180-day delayed: 0 bioactive vs 6 control
