## One-line Summary

Prospective cohort (15 mandibular SLA-coated ITI implants, 10 patients) with weekly Osstell mentor RFA over 11 weeks: mean ISQ fell from 77.2 at placement to a 72.13 nadir at week 4, then recovered significantly (P<0.05) to 75.6 by week 11 without regaining baseline — implying loading before week 4 may be disadvantageous.

## 한줄요약

전향적 코호트 (Shokri 2013) — 하악 SLA ITI 임플란트 15개를 11주간 매주 공명주파수분석 (Resonance Frequency Analysis, RFA)으로 추적. 평균 임플란트 안정성 지수 (Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ)가 식립일 77.2 → 4주째 72.13(최저, stability dip) → 11주째 75.6으로 유의 회복(p<0.05)했으나 초기값 미회복. 4주 이전 하중은 불리할 수 있음.

## Summary

This small prospective clinical study traced the **early healing stability trajectory** of dental implants in the mandible using resonance frequency analysis (RFA). Fifteen threaded SLA-coated ITI (Straumann) implants in 10 healthy non-smoker patients (>40 yr, D2/D3 bone) were placed one-stage at 35 N/cm insertion torque and measured by an Osstell mentor device at surgery and at weeks 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 7, and 11 — all kept unloaded throughout.

The mean ISQ described a characteristic **stability dip**: a continuous decline from 77.2 at placement to a nadir of 72.13 at week 4, then a statistically significant rebound to 75.6 by week 11, never quite reaching the baseline. The week-4 nadir held across all bone types studied. The authors interpret this as a period in which **loading might be disadvantageous prior to the 4th week** after placement, where mechanical (primary) stability has decayed but biological (secondary) stability has not yet fully compensated.

## Key Contributions

- Captures the **primary→secondary stability transition** at fine weekly resolution in human mandibular implants, localizing the ISQ nadir to **week 4**.
- Frames a clinical caution: avoid loading before week 4 post-placement, when stability is lowest.
- Adds RFA-tracked longitudinal evidence in D2/D3 mandibular bone with a single implant system and torque (controlled but narrow).

## Methodology

- **Design**: prospective cohort, sequential sampling, single center, single surgeon.
- **Sample**: 10 patients (4 M, 6 F; 42–65 yr), 15 implants; ≥6 mo edentulous; bone height ≥12 mm, crest width ≥6 mm, density D2/D3 (Friberg).
- **Implants/protocol**: threaded SLA-coated ITI (Straumann), one-stage, 35 N/cm insertion torque, 400–600 rpm drilling under saline, CBCT (NewTom VGI) + panoramic planning.
- **Measurement**: Osstell mentor RFA at surgery day and weeks 1–5, 7, 11; first two equal values accepted; primary ISQ ≤47 = questionable stability. No provisional load during 11-week follow-up.
- **Statistics**: paired *t*-test, P<0.05 (Excel 2007).

## Results

| Time | Mean ISQ | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|
| Surgery day | 77.20 | 2.49 |
| Week 1 | 77.00 | 1.78 |
| Week 2 | 74.20 | 1.93 |
| Week 3 | 73.20 | 2.62 |
| **Week 4 (nadir)** | **72.13** | 2.88 |
| Week 5 | 72.33 | 2.34 |
| Week 7 | 73.55 | 1.79 |
| Week 11 | 75.60 | 1.88 |

- Lowest mean stability at **week 4** in all bone types.
- Significant recovery (P<0.05) by week 11, but baseline (77.2) not regained.
- Clinical inference: loading prior to week 4 may be disadvantageous.
