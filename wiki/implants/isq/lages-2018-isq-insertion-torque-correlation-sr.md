## 한줄요약
SR (12편, PRISMA): 삽입토크와 ISQ 간 유의한 상관관계 없음(r=0.366, p=0.079); 두 측정법은 독립적·비교 불가 — 임상 결정 시 한 가지 방법만 사용할 것 권고; 근거 확실도 낮음 (CIDRE 2018).

## Summary
PRISMA-based systematic review (12 studies, from 2,017 identified) examining the correlation between insertion torque (IT) and resonance frequency analysis (ISQ) for measuring dental implant primary stability. Pooled Pearson r=0.366 (p=0.079) — not statistically significant. Certainty of evidence: LOW (downgraded for RoB and indirectness). IT and RFA measure different biomechanical aspects of implant-bone interaction and should be considered independent, incomparable methods. Clinical recommendation: select one method per implant/patient and do not treat them as interchangeable. Note: superseded by Tisci 2026 (n=48 studies, pooled r=0.44, p<0.001) which shows a moderate significant correlation with more evidence — the discrepancy likely reflects the smaller sample in this SR and study selection differences.

## Key Contributions
- Establishes early evidence that IT and ISQ are NOT interchangeable stability metrics
- Low-certainty conclusion: no significant correlation between IT and RFA/ISQ
- Clinical recommendation: one method per implant, not both combined
- Later evidence (Tisci 2026) shows moderate correlation — interpretation still limited by high heterogeneity

## Methodology
SR, PRISMA. Databases: PubMed, Web of Science, SCOPUS, Cochrane, OVID, SciELO + manual search. No language/year restrictions. 3 independent reviewers. 12 studies (clinical trials reporting ISQ and IT). Pearson r pooled.

## Results
- Pooled Pearson r: 0.366
- p = 0.079 (not significant)
- Certainty: LOW (risk of bias + indirectness)
