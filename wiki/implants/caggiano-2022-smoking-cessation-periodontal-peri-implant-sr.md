## One-line Summary
Systematic review (PRISMA) of smoking cessation effects on periodontal and peri-implant health: cessation improves periodontal clinical indices and peri-implant outcomes versus continued smoking, with magnitude and time-course of recovery varying — the cessation-side companion to smoking–implant-failure SRs (Naseri 2020, Mustapha 2022, Fan 2024).

## Summary
Systematic review examining whether smoking cessation translates to improved periodontal and peri-implant outcomes (versus continued smoking). The evidence supports that cessation improves periodontal indices and peri-implant health, but the magnitude and time-course of recovery varies.

This is the cessation-side companion to the smoking-implant-failure SRs (Naseri 2020, Mustapha 2022, Fan 2024).

## Key Contributions
- Quantifies and characterizes the benefit of cessation specifically (not just smoking vs non-smoking).
- Provides the rationale for pre- and peri-implant cessation counseling.

## Methodology
PRISMA SR of cessation-outcome studies in periodontal and peri-implant settings.

## Results
[합의수준] Cessation → improved periodontal indices and peri-implant outcomes vs continued smoking.

## Limitations
- Heterogeneous cessation interventions
- Self-report cessation status
- Time-to-effect not standardized

## Clinical Implication
For Korean implantology counseling: cessation before and around implant placement is supported by evidence. Brief motivational intervention (5 As: ask, advise, assess, assist, arrange) is reasonable standard practice. Even partial reduction may matter (Naseri 2020 dose-response).
