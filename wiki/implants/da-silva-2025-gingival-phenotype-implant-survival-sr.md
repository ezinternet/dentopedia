## One-line Summary

Systematic review (8 studies): implant success >91% at up to 5 years for BOTH thin and thick gingival phenotypes; phenotype does not directly determine survival, but the thin phenotype is a main risk factor for additional marginal bone loss — so its effect on survival is indirect, via peri-implantitis/MBL.

## Summary

Does gingival phenotype (thin vs thick) decide whether an implant survives? This PRISMA-guided systematic review of 8 studies says: not directly. Implant success exceeded 91% at up to 5 years regardless of phenotype, and the evidence that tissue thickness directly changes marginal bone level is inconclusive. The clinically important nuance is the *indirect* pathway — the thin phenotype is one of the principal risk factors for additional bone loss and is associated with higher peri-implantitis risk, which is the route by which a thin phenotype can ultimately erode survival. A practical secondary finding: a flapless protocol gives better short-term esthetics. The takeaway is to preserve/augment a thin phenotype not to "save" the implant per se, but to reduce downstream peri-implantitis/MBL risk.

## Key Contributions

- Phenotype–survival relationship is indirect (mediated by peri-implantitis/MBL), not direct.
- High implant success (>91% to 5 y) in both thin and thick phenotypes.
- Thin phenotype = main risk factor for additional bone loss; flapless protocol → better short-term esthetics.

## Methodology

- PRISMA SR; PICO on phenotype influence on MBL around implants.
- PubMed/MEDLINE, PMC, B-On; searched Apr–Sep 2023; last-13-years filter; ≥1-year follow-up with MBL assessment required.
- 2 independent reviewers + 3rd adjudicator; JBI Critical Appraisal for risk of bias.
- 62 records → 8 included studies.

## Results

- Implant success >91% for both phenotypes at up to 5-year follow-up.
- Direct effect of tissue thickness on marginal bone level: unclear/inconclusive.
- Thin phenotype: principal risk factor for additional bone loss; higher suggested peri-implantitis risk.
- Flapless protocol: better short-term esthetic outcome.
