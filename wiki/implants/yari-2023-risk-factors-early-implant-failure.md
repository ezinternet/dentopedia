## One-line Summary

Retrospective cohort (1323 implants / 738 patients; 53 early failures before final prosthetic loading): independent multivariate predictors of early implant failure were immediate provisional prosthesis (OR 3.42), posterior maxilla (OR 2.96), type IV bone (OR 2.88), periodontal-extraction site (OR 2.53), previously augmented site (OR 2.24), and smoking (OR 1.84).

## Summary

This single-center retrospective cohort analysed 1323 implants placed in 738 patients over four years to isolate predictors of **early** implant failure, defined as failure occurring **before final prosthetic loading** (the osseointegration window). Fifty-three implants in 52 patients failed early (≈4.0% implant-level; ≈7.0% patient-level). Rather than testing one factor at a time, the authors entered patient-, local-, surgical-, and implant-level variables into a single multivariate logistic model. Six factors remained independently significant, led by **immediate provisional prosthesis (OR 3.42)** and **posterior maxilla (OR 2.96)**. A univariate signal for narrow implants did not survive adjustment, illustrating why early-failure risk should be judged in a multivariable frame.

## Key Contributions

- Anchors a multivariable (not single-factor) picture of early failure: anatomy (posterior maxilla, type IV bone), site history (periodontal extraction, prior augmentation), protocol (immediate provisional), and behavior (smoking) each contribute independently.
- Quantifies **immediate provisional prosthesis** as the single strongest predictor (OR 3.42) in a general placement population — complementary to the all-on-four immediate-loading cohort ([[implants/uesugi-2024-risk-factors-early-failure-all-on-four]]).
- Reproduces the smoking signal (OR 1.84) seen in pooled data ([[implants/fan-2024-smoking-early-implant-failure-sr-ma]]), but with a smaller effect once anatomy/protocol are held constant.
- Demonstrates a vanishing narrow-implant effect after adjustment (OR 2.32, P=0.068) — a methodological caution.

## Methodology

- Design: retrospective cohort, consecutive implant patients over a 4-year timeframe.
- Sample: 1323 implants / 738 patients (mean 1.8 implants/patient); 53 early failures in 52 patients.
- Predictors: age, sex, smoking, systemic disease; region, reason for extraction, bone quality; bone augmentation, placement timing, staging, antibiotic prophylaxis; implant brand, length, diameter.
- Outcome: early implant failure (before final prosthetic loading).
- Analysis: bivariate analyses + multivariate logistic regression (odds ratios).

## Results

Multivariate significant predictors (OR, P):

| Predictor | OR | P |
|---|---|---|
| Immediate provisional prosthesis | 3.42 | 0.019 |
| Posterior maxillary region | 2.96 | 0.006 |
| Type IV bone | 2.88 | 0.008 |
| Periodontal reason for extraction | 2.53 | 0.004 |
| Previously augmented site | 2.24 | 0.014 |
| Smoking | 1.84 | 0.031 |
| Narrow implant | 2.32 | 0.068 (NS in MV; sig in bivariate P=0.012) |

No 95% CIs are reported in the abstract. The authors conclude early failure is more expected in smokers, posterior maxilla, history of periodontal problems, type IV bone, augmented bone, and immediately loaded cases.
