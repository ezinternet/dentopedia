## One-line Summary

Dog histomorphometric study (6 dogs, 24 implants, 6-month unloaded) comparing Straumann SP (tissue-level) vs BL (bone-level) implants placed in fresh extraction sockets: neck design had no significant effect on vertical bone resorption, but bone-level implants produced significantly wider lingual biologic width at 1 mm subcrestal placement (3.16 vs 2.43 mm, P=0.009).

## Summary

This dog model histomorphometric study compared peri-implant tissue remodeling between Straumann Standard Plus (SP, tissue-level) and Bone Level (BL, bone-level) implants placed immediately in mandibular premolar extraction sockets. Six Labrador dogs received 24 implants across 4 extraction sites each, with both implant types placed at two insertion depths (flush and 1 mm subcrestal). Animals healed unloaded for 6 months before sacrifice. Undecalcified sections stained with Van Gieson were analyzed for vertical bone resorption and biologic width components on both buccal and lingual aspects.

The principal finding was that the two neck designs — SP's divergent smooth machined collar vs BL's micro-thread with 0.265 mm platform switching — produced statistically equivalent vertical crestal bone resorption at both insertion depths. The only significant difference was a wider lingual biologic width in BL implants when placed 1 mm subcrestal. Buccal bone resorption exceeded lingual resorption for both designs, consistent with prior immediate implant dog studies documenting the vulnerability of the thin buccal plate (0.53–0.71 mm at crest tip).

## Key Contributions

- Null result for bone resorption: tissue-level vs bone-level neck design does not significantly alter crestal bone remodeling in fresh extraction sockets at 6 months
- BL implants yield longer biologic width, especially at the lingual aspect when placed 1 mm subcrestal — potentially advantageous for esthetic and immunologic outcomes (greater extracellular matrix, collagen volume, vascularity)
- 1 mm subcrestal placement does not increase bone loss for either implant type vs crestal placement
- Thin buccal plate (~0.53 mm at crest) documented in canine extraction sockets; scattered BIC zones between threads at coronal implant level suggest secondary bone contact after primary buccal bone loss
- The small platform switching offset in BL implants (0.265 mm) is suggested as a reason bone preservation effect was absent (contrast with studies using 0.4–0.65 mm offset)

## Methodology

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Model | 6 adult male Labrador dogs (~21 kg, ~1 yr) |
| Sites | Mandibular premolars, bilateral extraction |
| Implants | Straumann SP (TL, 3.3×10 mm, 1.8 mm machined collar) and BL (3.3×10 mm, 0.265 mm platform switch, micro-thread neck); both SLA surface |
| Groups | SP-0 mm, SP-1 mm subcrestal, BL-0 mm, BL-1 mm subcrestal (n=6 each) |
| Healing | 6 months, non-submerged, no functional loading, no socket grafting |
| Histology | Van Gieson stain, ~70 µm sections, Olympus BX51, Image-Pro Plus |
| Measurements | Vertical bone resorption (IS–BIC minus insertion depth); biologic width (PM–aBE + aBE–BIC); buccal and lingual sides separately |
| Statistics | Two-sample t-test or Wilcoxon sign-rank test; α=0.05 |

## Results

**Vertical bone resorption (SP vs BL)**

| Side | Depth | SP mm | BL mm | P |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Buccal | 0 mm | 3.15 ± 0.52 | 2.70 ± 0.52 | 0.165 |
| Buccal | −1 mm | 2.55 ± 0.41 | 2.99 ± 0.21 | 0.091 |
| Lingual | 0 mm | 1.68 ± 0.38 | 1.68 ± 0.37 | 1.000 |
| Lingual | −1 mm | 1.47 ± 0.18 | 1.56 ± 0.19 | 0.419 |

No significant differences between implant types at any depth or side.

**Biologic width (SP vs BL)**

| Side | Depth | SP mm | BL mm | P |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Buccal | 0 mm | 3.34 ± 0.66 | 3.44 ± 1.11 | 0.853 |
| Buccal | −1 mm | 3.35 ± 0.42 | 3.57 ± 0.57 | 0.464 |
| Lingual | 0 mm | 2.43 ± 0.25 | 3.00 ± 0.58 | 0.052 |
| Lingual | −1 mm | 2.43 ± 0.42 | 3.16 ± 0.36 | **0.009*** |

Sole statistically significant difference: wider lingual biologic width in BL implants at −1 mm subcrestal placement.

**Within-type depth comparison**: No significant difference in bone resorption or biologic width between 0 mm and −1 mm for either SP or BL implants.

**Buccal vs lingual bone resorption**: Buccal significantly greater than lingual for both implant types at both depths (P≤0.010), confirming asymmetric remodeling in immediate implant placement.

**BIC position on histology**: SP crestal BIC at or below machined-SLA junction; BL crestal BIC at or below platform-abutment junction.
