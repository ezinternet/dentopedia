## One-line Summary
SR+MA (≥5-year follow-up) finding short posterior implants survive similarly to long implants in the mandible but possibly worse in the maxilla, with fewer biological complications overall.

## 한줄요약
≥5년 SR+MA — short implant는 하악에서 long과 생존 유사, 상악에서는 더 낮을 수 있음; 생물학적 합병증은 적음.

## Summary
This long-term meta-analysis adds an arch-specific nuance to the short-implant story. Pooling studies with at least five years of follow-up, it finds short implants in the mandible match long implants for survival, but short implants in the maxilla may survive at a lower rate. Across sites, short implants showed fewer biological complications. The authors caution that participant and implant numbers are limited, so the maxillary disadvantage is a signal to weigh rather than a settled fact — but it usefully qualifies blanket "short equals long" conclusions when the site is the maxilla.

## Key Contributions
- Arch-stratified ≥5y survival comparison (maxilla vs mandible).
- Identifies a possible maxillary survival disadvantage for short implants.
- Confirms the lower biological-complication profile of short implants.

## Methodology
SR+MA; ≥5-year observation; short vs longer implants in posterior alveolar region; subgroups by arch; outcomes survival and biological complications. Limited sample.

## Results
- Mandible: short ≈ long survival.
- Maxilla: short possibly < long survival.
- Biological complications lower with short implants.
