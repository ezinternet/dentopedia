## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 17 RCTs comparing single (1-IOD) vs two (2-IOD) implant-supported mandibular overdentures: implant survival is equivalent through 5 years, but denture fracture (5Y RR 2.10), denture remake (5Y RR 2.57), and metal-housing reattachment (5Y RR 2.31) are ~2× more frequent with 1-IOD; relining and O-ring replacement do not differ.

## Summary

The McGill (2002) and York (2009) consensus statements established **two implants (2-IOD)** as the first-choice mandibular overdenture standard, but a **single midline implant (1-IOD)** has been proposed as a less invasive, lower-cost alternative. This meta-analysis of **17 RCTs** (follow-up 12–60 months) directly compares the two on implant survival and prosthetic complications.

The headline result is a clean dissociation: **implant survival is identical** between 1- and 2-IOD at 1, 3, and 5 years (and across loading-protocol subgroups), but **1-IOD carries a roughly 2× higher prosthetic-complication burden** — specifically denture fracture, the need for a new denture, and metal-housing reattachment. Crucially, **relining and O-ring replacement do NOT differ**, which the authors read as evidence that the extra fractures stem from reduced **structural strength** under the single-implant fulcrum rather than from greater denture mobility. They therefore recommend a **metal framework** to reinforce the 1-IOD (a cited FEA found framework insertion cut tensile stress around the implant housing by 61.8%).

This updates and partly overturns an earlier SR (Ahmed et al.) that had reported *better* implant survival for 1-IOD; with post-2017 RCTs added, that survival advantage disappears.

## Key Contributions

- **Implant survival equivalence**: no significant 1- vs 2-IOD difference at 1Y / 3Y / 5Y, including loading-protocol subgroups.
- **Quantified complication penalty for 1-IOD** — denture fracture, denture remake, metal-housing reattachment all ~2× more frequent.
- **Maintenance parity** on relining and O-ring replacement → points to a structural (not mobility) cause → **metal-framework reinforcement** recommendation.
- **Updates the literature**: contradicts the earlier higher-survival-for-1-IOD finding once recent RCTs are included.

## Methodology

- PRISMA-reported SR+MA; PROSPERO CRD420250644169. RCTs only. Databases: PubMed, Google Scholar, CENTRAL, Web of Science (final search April 2025).
- Selection: 1,083 → 17 RCTs included (15 of 32 full-texts excluded: 2 no full text, 6 duplicate data, 6 follow-up < 1 year, 1 insufficient data).
- Included: ball/Locator attachments; immediate/2W/21D/6W/3M loading; Straumann, Dentis, ADIN, Neodent, Brånemark, Conexão, Neobiotech, Sterngold, Implantium systems.
- Random-effects model; RR (95% CI) for dichotomous, MD for continuous; heterogeneity via chi-square + I²; RevMan 5.4.1; funnel plots. Cochrane RoB 2 (mostly low; "some concerns" for randomization and for missing data in 5Y studies). Symmetrical funnel → low publication-bias risk.

## Results

**Implant survival (1Y / 3Y / 5Y)**: no statistically significant difference; loading-protocol subgroups also NS.

| Outcome (RR > 1 favors 2-IOD) | 1 year | 3 years | 5 years |
|---|---|---|---|
| Denture fracture | RR 2.28 (1.01–5.14), p=0.05 | RR 2.08 (0.82–5.28), NS | RR 2.10 (1.21–3.64), p=0.008 |
| New denture fabrication | no events | RR 2.29 (0.76–6.90), NS | RR 2.57 (1.11–5.94), p=0.03 |
| Metal-housing reattachment | NS | RR 2.29 (1.04–5.04), p=0.04 | RR 2.31 (1.03–5.18), p=0.04 (I²=84%) |
| Relining | NS | NS | NS |
| O-ring replacement | NS | NS | NS |

- Denture fracture in 1-IOD ≈ twice that of 2-IOD at 5Y.
- **Satisfaction / function** (not pooled): 1-IOD ≫ conventional complete denture; vs 2-IOD, OHRQoL/OHIP mixed (some favor 2-IOD, some NS). Mixing-ability (two-colored gum): both improve vs CD, no 1-vs-2 difference; almond test: 2-IOD greater improvement than 1-IOD.
- **Mechanism (cited FEA, El-Zawahry et al.)**: 1-IOD total deformation ~50% greater; higher Von Mises stress; metal framework cuts implant-housing tensile stress by 61.8%.
- **Cost** (cited): 1-IOD ≈ 75% of 2-IOD; long-term complications + framework reinforcement narrow the gap.
- **Clinical steer**: 1-IOD suits patients prioritizing lower initial cost / lower invasiveness who can attend frequent maintenance, or where ridge asymmetry hinders 2-IOD; 2-IOD for those needing maximal retention/support or unable to attend regular maintenance.
