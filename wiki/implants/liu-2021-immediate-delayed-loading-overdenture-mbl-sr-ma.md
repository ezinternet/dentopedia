## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 5 RCTs + 2 cohorts (191 participants, 400 implants) finding no significant difference in peri-implant MBL between immediate and delayed loading of unsplinted mandibular overdentures (WMD 0.04 mm, 95% CI −0.13 to 0.21), though immediate loading carried a numerically higher implant failure rate (5.03% vs 1.00%).

## 한줄요약

5 RCT + 2 코호트(191명·400 임플란트) SR+MA — 무치악 하악 비연결형 오버덴처에서 즉시 로딩과 지연 로딩 간 변연골소실(MBL) 차이는 없었으나(WMD 0.04 mm), 즉시 로딩군 임플란트 실패율이 수치상 더 높았다(5.03% vs 1.00%).

## Summary

This SR+MA compared marginal bone loss between immediate and delayed loading of **unsplinted mandibular implant-retained overdentures** across 5 RCTs and 2 cohort studies (191 participants, 400 implants). Pooled MBL was statistically equivalent between protocols, and the result held in subgroups using Locator attachments or ball anchors. However, immediate loading was associated with a roughly 5-fold higher (but not formally pooled) implant failure rate, signalling a survival trade-off when loading edentulous mandibular implants immediately.

## Key Contributions

- First MBL meta-analysis dedicated to unsplinted mandibular overdentures.
- Attachment-type subgroup analysis (Locator vs ball anchor).
- Highlights the immediate-loading early-failure trade-off despite equivalent bone loss.

## Methodology

- SR+MA of RCTs/CCTs/cohorts; PubMed, EMBASE, CENTRAL + grey literature (to Dec 2020).
- Outcome: pooled MBL (WMD, 95% CI); ROB via Cochrane tool, Newcastle–Ottawa, Egger's test.

## Results

- 5 RCTs + 2 cohorts; 191 participants, 400 implants.
- MBL: WMD 0.04 mm (95% CI −0.13 to 0.21, P > .05) — no difference.
- Subgroup (Locator vs ball anchor): no difference.
- Implant failure: immediate 5.03% (10/199) vs delayed 1.00% (2/201).
