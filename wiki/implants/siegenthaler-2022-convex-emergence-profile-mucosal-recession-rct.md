## One-line Summary

3-arm RCT (n=47 implants, anterior; convex vs concave emergence vs healing-abutment control): at 12 months, mid-facial mucosal recession occurred in 64.3% of convex, 14.3% of concave, and 31.4% of control sites; convex carried ~13× higher odds of recession vs concave (OR 12.69, 95% CI 1.89–88.48, p=.01).

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1111/jcpe.13696](https://doi.org/10.1111/jcpe.13696)), this University of Zurich parallel-group RCT randomized 47 anterior single implants (OsseoSpeed EV) to three transmucosal conditioning modalities — an implant provisional with a **convex** emergence profile, a provisional with a **concave** profile, or a **healing-abutment-only control** (no provisional) — and tracked mid-facial mucosal margin stability for 12 months after final crown loading. The shape of the emergence profile, not merely the presence of a provisional, governed recession: by 12 months recession occurred in 64.3% of convex sites versus only 14.3% of concave sites, with convex carrying roughly 13-fold higher adjusted odds of recession than concave (OR 12.69, 95% CI 1.89–88.48, p=.01). Aesthetic scores (PES/WES) did not differ between groups, while providing any provisional added ~2.5× more appointments and ~869 CHF (>50% of the total restoration cost). The convex group also trended thinner in baseline soft tissue, a plausible mechanism for its instability. Implant and restoration survival were 100%.

**Clinically:** in the anterior esthetic zone, **favor a concave (or at least flat/under-contoured) emergence profile over a convex one** to keep the mid-facial mucosal margin stable and avoid recession; reserve a convex contour for the specific case where excess overgrown soft tissue must be deliberately pushed apically. Because aesthetics were equivalent with or without a provisional, the routine use of a provisional purely for soft-tissue conditioning should be weighed against its substantial added time and cost.

## Key Contributions

- First RCT to directly compare convex vs concave emergence profiles (plus a no-provisional control) for anterior peri-implant mucosal-margin stability in prosthetically ideal-position implants.
- Establishes emergence-profile **shape** as the driver of recession risk, quantified by adjusted odds ratios.
- Shows aesthetic equivalence (PES/WES) across all three modalities, questioning the routine need for a conditioning provisional given its added time/cost.

## Methodology

- Prospective, parallel-group RCT, 3 arms; 47 patients / 47 anterior implants (40 maxilla, 7 mandible; incisors, canines, premolars); mean age 60.3 y.
- After abutment connection, sealed-envelope randomization to **CONVEX**, **CONCAVE**, or **CONTROL** (healing abutment, no provisional).
- Test groups: undercontoured screw-retained provisional filled stepwise with flowable composite to a convex or concave contour (shaped ≈1–2 mm above abutment neck; up to 3 conditioning visits); final monolithic zirconia crown on an individualized zirconia abutment replicating the same profile (25 Ncm).
- Blinded calibrated examiner at baseline, 6m, 12m post-loading. **Primary outcome**: presence/absence of mid-facial mucosal recession (probe-based change in clinical crown length). Secondary: PES/WES, KT, soft-tissue thickness, profilometric/linear contour (STL superimposition at 1 mm/3 mm below margin), time/cost, MBL.
- Multivariable logistic regression (reference = CONCAVE; adjusted for soft-tissue thickness and KT width) and GEE linear models (Wald χ²), Bonferroni post hoc, α=5%.

## Results

- **Recession frequency at 6m**: CONVEX 53.8% · CONCAVE 7.7% · CONTROL 21.4%.
- **Recession frequency at 12m**: CONVEX **64.3%** · CONCAVE **14.3%** · CONTROL 31.4%.
- **Adjusted odds of recession (ref = CONCAVE)**: 6m — CONVEX OR 13.38 (95% CI 1.29–138.51, p=.030); 12m — CONVEX OR 12.69 (95% CI 1.89–88.48, p=.010). CONTROL not significant at either time (6m OR 3.30, p=.335; 12m OR 2.43, p=.350). With CONTROL as reference, CONVEX trended worse (OR 5.2, p=.05).
- **Mean recession magnitude at 12m**: CONVEX 0.72 ± 0.60 mm · CONCAVE 1.00 ± 0.00 mm · CONTROL 0.90 ± 0.65 mm (small absolute values near probe error range).
- **Aesthetics (PES/WES)**: no between-group difference at any time point (PES p=.735; WES p=.842); PES tended to improve over time in all groups (p=.061).
- **Soft-tissue thickness**: decreased over time in all groups (p=.003), magnitude similar between groups; CONVEX trended thinner at baseline (3.20 vs 4.04 vs 4.56 mm; NS) — plausible mechanism for higher recession.
- **Peri-implant tissue width / profilometric contour (1 mm & 3 mm)**: significant reduction over time, similar magnitude across groups (ROI-3 between-group trend p=.067).
- **MBL**: bone gain over time (p<.001), similar across groups (p=.599).
- **Survival**: implant + restoration 100% at 12m. **Cost/time**: provisional added ~2.5× visits and ~869 CHF (>50% of restoration cost); aesthetics unchanged.
