## Summary
PRISMA-compliant systematic review + meta-analysis of **49 studies, 12,056 patients, 29,905 implants**. Bone types I, II, and III all show significantly higher implant survival than type IV. Types I and II are statistically equivalent; type III is intermediate. The gap is explained by primary stability: type IV (low-density, thin cortical) yields ISQ 58–65 vs. 72–80 for type I.

## Lekholm-Zarb Bone Classification

| Type | Description | ISQ (mean) | Clinical location |
|---|---|---|---|
| I | Homogeneous compact bone | 72–80 | Anterior mandible |
| II | Dense spongy + thick cortical | 72–75 | Anterior jaw |
| III | Dense spongy + thin cortical | 70–75 | Anterior maxilla |
| IV | Low-density spongy + thin cortical | 58–65 | **Posterior maxilla** |

## Key Contributions
- Meta-analysis confirms type IV bone is a significant independent risk factor for implant failure
- Types I and II: statistically equivalent survival (no significant difference)
- Surface treatment (SLA, TiUnite oxidized) partially compensates for type IV bone deficiency
- Posterior maxilla = highest prevalence of type IV bone (sinus pneumatization + resorption)

## Meta-analysis Results

| Comparison | RR | 95% CI | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Type I vs. IV | **1.92** | 1.52–2.43 | <0.00001 |
| Type II vs. IV | **1.89** | 1.59–2.24 | <0.00001 |
| Type III vs. IV | **1.50** | 1.27–1.76 | <0.00001 |
| Type I vs. II | NS | — | NS |

> RR = relative risk of failure avoidance; higher = fewer failures vs. type IV

**Historical benchmark**: Jaffin & Berman (1991) — 35% failure in type IV vs. 3% in types I–III  
**10-year survival baseline**: >90% overall (Alghamdi, 2018)

## Clinical Implications
- Preoperative bone quality assessment is mandatory for prognosis and treatment planning
- In type IV (posterior maxilla): consider sinus lift, bone condensation, or surface-enhanced implants
- ISQ < 65 at placement → anticipate lower primary stability → extended healing before loading
- Type IV + machined surface: highest risk combination; prefer SLA or oxidized surface
