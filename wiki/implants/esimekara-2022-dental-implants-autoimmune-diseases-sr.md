## One-line Summary

PRISMA systematic critical review (55 studies, 9 autoimmune diseases) finding dental implant survival comparable to the general population, though secondary Sjögren's and erosive oral lichen planus raise peri-mucositis and marginal bone loss risk.

## 한줄요약

PRISMA 체계적 비판적 고찰 (55편, 자가면역질환 9종): 치과 임플란트 생존율은 일반 인구와 동등하나, 이차 쇼그렌증후군과 미란성 구강편평태선에서 임플란트주위점막염(peri-mucositis)과 변연골소실(MBL) 위험 증가.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1016/j.jormas.2022.01.005](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jormas.2022.01.005)), this PRISMA systematic critical review evaluated complications and survival rates of dental implants in patients with autoimmune diseases, analyzing 55 studies that spanned 9 distinct autoimmune conditions (search of Google Scholar + PubMed, stop date September 2021). Despite marked heterogeneity and methodological limitations among the included studies, implant survival rates were comparable to those reported in the general population; the review singled out secondary Sjögren's syndrome and erosive oral lichen planus as subgroups more susceptible to peri-mucositis and increased marginal bone loss. (Abstract-only — full text not retrieved; per-disease survival percentages and follow-up durations were not extractable.)

Clinically: implants are generally a safe, viable option across the autoimmune-disease spectrum, but only under strict oral-hygiene maintenance and long-term recall — and with heightened peri-implant monitoring for secondary Sjögren's and erosive OLP patients, who carry the highest peri-mucositis and marginal-bone-loss risk.

## Key Contributions

- Broadest single synthesis of dental implant outcomes across the autoimmune-disease spectrum — 9 diseases in one review, versus prior disease-specific reviews.
- Demonstrates implant survival comparable to the general population despite the autoimmune burden and study heterogeneity.
- Identifies secondary Sjögren's syndrome and erosive OLP as the specific higher-risk subgroups for peri-mucositis and marginal bone loss.
- Positions strict oral-hygiene maintenance + long-term follow-up as the universal determinants of uneventful treatment.

## Methodology

- PRISMA-guided systematic critical review (no meta-analysis).
- Databases: Google Scholar + PubMed; search stop date September 2021.
- Inclusion: full-text, human, English-language studies of autoimmune-disease patients treated with dental implants.
- Outcomes: implant complications and survival rates.

## Results

- **55 studies** across **9 autoimmune diseases**:
  - Sjögren's syndrome (SS): **17**
  - Oral lichen planus (OLP): **11**
  - Type 1 diabetes mellitus (T1DM): **8**
  - Rheumatoid arthritis (RA): **6**
  - Systemic sclerosis / scleroderma (SSc): **4**
  - Crohn's disease (CD): **3**
  - Systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE): **3**
  - Mucous membrane pemphigoid (MMP): **2**
  - Pemphigus vulgaris (PV): **1**
- Implant **survival comparable to the general population** across the disease spectrum.
- **Secondary SS** and **erosive OLP**: more susceptible to **peri-mucositis** and **increased marginal bone loss**.
- Conclusion: implants are safe and viable for edentulous autoimmune-disease patients; strict hygiene + long-term follow-up are common determinants of success.
