## Summary

This PRISMA-registered systematic review with meta-analyses (PROSPERO IRD42020179528) investigated whether the timing of implant placement and loading affects biological outcomes and implant survival for implant-supported multiple-unit fixed dental prostheses (FDPs supported by ≥2 implants) in partially edentulous patients.

After screening 7,002 titles and 360 full texts, 14 studies comprising 21 cohort groups were included — covering 6 RCTs, 5 cohort studies, and 3 case series with a minimum 3-year follow-up. The studies represented only 5 of 9 theoretically possible placement/loading protocol combinations: DPIL (delayed placement + immediate loading), DPDL (delayed placement + delayed loading), DPEL (delayed placement + early loading), IPIL (immediate placement + immediate loading), and IPEL (immediate placement + early loading). Early placement (EP) protocols were entirely absent from the multiple-unit FDP evidence base.

All protocol groups except IPIL (90% in one cohort) demonstrated implant survival rates above 90%. Only one quantitative meta-analysis was feasible — comparing DPIL vs. DPDL from 3 RCTs — and found no significant difference in survival (p = .227). GRADE evidence certainty was very low for all comparisons. Biological complications (peri-implantitis, mucositis) were poorly reported; pooled MBL analysis was precluded by heterogeneity.

## Key Contributions

- Restricts scope to multiple-unit FDPs (≥2 implants), distinguishing this evidence base from the extensive single-crown literature
- Maps all 9 theoretical protocol combinations; demonstrates that only 5 are covered by existing clinical data
- Provides the only meta-analysis for DPIL vs. DPDL survival in multiple-unit FDP context (3 RCTs, p = .227, NS)
- Constructs four GRADE Summary of Findings tables enabling direct evidence-certainty comparison across protocol pairs
- Identifies critical underreporting of mucositis as a primary outcome in all 14 included studies

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review with meta-analyses; PRISMA-compliant; PROSPERO registered
- **Search**: PubMed/Medline, Embase, Cochrane CENTRAL; up to April 29, 2020; English language; year 2000–2020
- **Inclusion**: RCTs, prospective/retrospective cohorts, case series; ≥10 patients per arm; ≥3-year follow-up; FPDs on ≥2 root-form implants; diameter 3–6 mm; permanently retained FPDs; identifiable placement + loading timing
- **Exclusion**: Full-arch dentures, removable prostheses, irradiated bone, alveolar clefts, bone-compromising medications; inability to separate single-unit from multiple-unit outcomes
- **Placement definitions**: Immediate (IP) = same day as extraction; Early (EP) = 1–4 months post-extraction; Delayed (DP) = >6 months post-extraction
- **Loading definitions**: Immediate (IL) = prosthesis within 1 week; Early (EL) = 1 week–2 months; Delayed (DL) = >2 months post-placement
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane RoB 2.0 (RCTs); Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (cohorts); Joanna Briggs Institute checklist (case series)
- **Statistics**: Random-effects meta-analysis for survival rate (DPIL vs. DPDL only); GRADE certainty assessed for 4 comparisons

## Results

### Included studies summary

| Protocol | Cohort groups | Implants | Weighted survival |
|---|---|---|---|
| DPIL | 11 | 502 | 97.2% (mean FU 60.1 mo) |
| DPDL | 7 | 476 | 98.1% |
| DPEL | 1 | 107 | 100% (55 mo FU) |
| IPIL | 1 | 20 | 90% (47.6 mo FU) |
| IPEL | 1 | 67 | 100% (55 mo FU) |

### Meta-analysis: DPIL vs. DPDL (3 RCTs)

- No significant difference in implant survival (p = .227)
- DPIL: 74 patients / 188 implants; DPDL: 72 patients / 182 implants
- GRADE certainty: VERY LOW (all outcomes)

### MBL changes (between-study, not pooled)

| Protocol | Mean MBL change | Implants |
|---|---|---|
| DPIL | 0.71 ± 0.66 mm | 378 |
| DPDL | 1.68 ± 0.97 mm | 298 |
| DPEL | 0.28 ± 0.88 mm | 107 |
| IPIL | 1.57 ± 0.91 mm | — |
| IPEL | 0.99 ± 1.35 mm | 67 |

Note: MBL values are descriptive between-study comparisons; heterogeneity precluded pooled analysis. No statistically grounded conclusion on MBL timing effects is possible.

### Biological complications

- DPIL: peri-implantitis 0.9%, peri-implant mucositis 0% (not reported)
- DPDL: peri-implantitis 3.5%, mucositis 0% (not reported)
- Mean probing depth: DPIL 2.83 ± 0.92 mm; DPDL 3.12 ± 1.08 mm (1 RCT data)
- Mucositis not reported in any of the 14 included studies

### Key clinical finding

When delayed implant placement is chosen, immediate loading and delayed loading produce statistically equivalent survival rates for multiple-unit FDPs over 3–15 years of follow-up. Evidence on early placement protocols (EP) is completely absent for this prosthetic category. The IPIL combination (immediate placement + immediate loading for FDPs) was represented by only one small cohort (n = 20 implants, 90% survival) — insufficient to draw conclusions.
