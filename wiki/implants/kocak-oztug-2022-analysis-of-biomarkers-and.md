## Summary

A patient-blinded, split-mouth randomized controlled trial that placed 94 implants in 27 subjects and randomized each implant to a platform-switched (PS, 0.3 mm inset abutment) or nonplatform-switched (NPS) configuration. Over 12 months, PS implants lost significantly less marginal bone and showed peri-implant crevicular fluid (PICF) biomarker profiles consistent with reduced osteoclastogenesis. This supplies primary RCT-level evidence and a candidate mechanism for the platform-switching MBL benefit established at the SR+MA level (Strietzel 2015), reinforcing rather than altering that bottom line.

## Key Contributions

- Randomized confirmation of the platform-switching MBL advantage: 0.51 mm (PS) vs 0.75 mm (NPS), p<0.001 at 12 months.
- Mechanistic biomarker link: PS group showed lower RANKL/OPG ratio (T1), lower MCP-1 (T2), and smaller MCP-1 rise (T1→T2), all p<0.05.
- Clinical peri-implant health parameters (BOP, mPI, PPD) were similar between groups — the difference is subclinical/radiographic and molecular.

## Methodology

Balanced permuted-block randomization (randomizer.org) in a split-mouth design; identical Camlog implants placed one-stage at crestal level (≥30 N·cm). PS abutment inset 0.3 mm. PICF collected with PerioPaper, RANKL/OPG/IL-1β/MCP-1 by ELISA. MBL measured on standardized periapical radiographs (IAI-to-IBC, ImageJ, intra-examiner R=0.937) at restoration (T1) and 12 months (T2). Powered to 80% at α=0.05.

## Results

- MBL: PS 0.51 ± 0.31 mm vs NPS 0.75 ± 0.29 mm, p<0.001.
- Both groups: IL-1β decreased; RANKL, OPG, MCP-1 increased T1→T2 (p<0.05).
- PS favorable on RANKL/OPG ratio (T1), MCP-1 (T2), and MCP-1 change (p<0.05).
- Peri-implant clinical health: negligible between-group difference.
