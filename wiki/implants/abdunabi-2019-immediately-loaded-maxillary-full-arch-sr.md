## One-line Summary

Systematic review (4 controlled clinical trials; meta-analysis not possible due to heterogeneity) finding only weak evidence of any difference between immediate loading and early/conventional loading of fully implant-supported maxillary full-arch fixed prostheses, for patient satisfaction or maintenance complications.

## Summary

This systematic review evaluated immediate loading of fully implant-supported maxillary full-arch fixed prostheses against early and conventional loading. Only 4 controlled clinical trials qualified, and heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis. The qualitative synthesis found weak, inconsistent evidence: a short-lived satisfaction advantage for immediate loading and no significant differences in implant success or prosthetic complications. The authors stress that immediate loading shows promise in the edentulous maxilla but that robust RCTs are required before firm protocol recommendations.

## Key Contributions

- Dedicated evidence summary for the higher-risk maxillary full-arch immediate-loading indication.
- Documents the scarcity and heterogeneity of controlled trials.

## Methodology

- SR of CCTs; 8 databases searched to Jan 2019; duplicate screening/extraction; Cochrane RoB tool; qualitative synthesis (no pooling).

## Results

- 4 CCTs from 1,052 screened.
- Satisfaction: transient advantage for immediate loading (lost by 12 months).
- Implant success / prosthetic complications: no significant differences in 3 trials.
