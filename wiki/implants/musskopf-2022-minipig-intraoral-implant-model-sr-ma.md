## Summary

This SR+MA (PRISMA) synthesized 55 original minipig intraoral dental implant studies (from 279 identified, databases through June 2021) to establish normative benchmarks for osseointegration (BIC) and crestal bone remodeling in this preclinical model. The minipig has emerged as a preferred large-animal intraoral model due to anatomical and physiological similarities with humans: comparable bone formation/remodeling rates (~1.2–1.5 mm/day gap fill vs 1.0–1.5 in humans), similar collagen organization, osteocyte density, and alveolar bone mineral density.

The standard model protocol involved mandibular premolar/molar extraction, 12±4 weeks post-extraction healing, 3 narrow (3.5mm diameter, 8mm length) implants per jaw quadrant, submerged healing, and 8 weeks of osseointegration before histological evaluation. The Gottingen minipig was the most common strain (30.9%); most studies used females only. Quality of reporting was generally low across studies, with frequent omissions in animal characteristics, examiner masking, and sample size calculations.

Pooled mean BIC was 59.88% (95% CI: 57.43–62.33), with significant temporal progression (p<0.001): 40.93% at 2 weeks, 58.37% at 4 weeks, and 66.33% beyond 4 weeks. Heterogeneity was high (I²>90%), mainly explained by post-extraction and post-implant observation intervals and implant surface type. These values are comparable to canine and human histological benchmarks, supporting the minipig intraoral model's translational validity.

## Key Contributions

- Establishes normative BIC benchmarks for the minipig intraoral dental implant model
- Provides time-stratified BIC reference values (2 wk / 4 wk / >4 wk)
- Identifies principal sources of heterogeneity: observation interval and implant surface
- Documents the standard minipig model protocol and strain characteristics
- Highlights systematic quality-of-reporting deficiencies in preclinical implant animal studies
- Validates minipig model translational relevance vs canine and human data

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review + random-effects meta-analysis (PRISMA)
- **Databases**: PubMed/MEDLINE + EMBASE through June 2021
- **Studies included**: 55 original studies (125 full-text reviewed from 279 identified)
- **Inclusion**: Minipigs, intraoral titanium implants, native alveolar bone, histological BIC data with central tendency + variability
- **Outcomes**: BIC (primary), first BIC (distance platform → first bone contact), crestal bone level
- **Statistics**: Random-effects models (Stata 17), meta-regression with REML; I² heterogeneity assessment; funnel plots + Egger's test for publication bias
- **Moderators tested**: Animal strain/age, post-extraction healing duration, implant placement healing, staging, loading type, implant surface
- **Quality**: ARRIVE checklist (scored 0/1/2 per item)

## Results

| Timepoint | Mean BIC (%) | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|
| Overall | 59.88 | 57.43 – 62.33 |
| 2 weeks | 40.93 | 34.95 – 46.90 |
| 4 weeks | 58.37 | 54.38 – 62.36 |
| >4 weeks | 66.33 | 63.45 – 69.21 |

- Temporal increase statistically significant (p<0.001)
- Heterogeneity: I²>90% for all analyses
- Main heterogeneity moderators: post-extraction interval, post-implant interval, implant surface
- Quality of reporting: generally low; common omissions — animal characteristics, examiner masking/calibration, sample size calculation
- ~90% of studies used undecalcified incandescent light microscopy for histometrics
- Most studies focused on implant surface technologies (47%), implant material (11%), implant design (7%)

**Standard minipig protocol**:
- Strain: Gottingen (most common, 30.9%); Lanyu small-ear pig (7.3%)
- Extraction: mandibular premolars + first molar; 12 wks healing (most common)
- Implants: 3 per quadrant, 3.5mm × 8mm, delayed placement (80%), submerged healing (64%)
- Average animals per study: 10.1±5.6 (range 3–30)
- Average implants per animal: 6.5±3.6 (range 2–20)
