## One-line Summary

6th EAO consensus report (group 2; based on 3 systematic reviews + 1 critical review) concluding that soft tissue augmentation at immediate or delayed implant placement reduces mid-buccal recession and (at immediate placement) increases mucosal thickness in esthetic sites, with autologous grafts showing a consistent but non-significant tendency to outperform substitutes, and insufficient evidence on optimal timing.

## Summary

This EAO consensus report (Working Group 2, 6th Consensus Conference) synthesized commissioned systematic reviews on soft tissue augmentation (STA) at dental implants. For esthetic sites, STA reduced mid-buccal recession at both immediate and delayed placement and increased mucosal thickness at immediate placement. Autologous grafts showed a consistent but statistically non-significant tendency to outperform soft-tissue substitutes, and there was insufficient evidence to define an optimal augmentation timepoint. Clinically, STA simultaneous with immediate placement is recommended in high-esthetic-priority cases to limit recession and thicken the mucosa.

## Key Contributions

- Translates STA evidence into EAO consensus statements and clinical recommendations.
- Stratifies effects by placement timing and graft type.

## Methodology

- Consensus over 3 systematic reviews + 1 critical review assessing clinical, radiographic, and patient-reported STA outcomes.

## Results

- Immediate + STA: less mid-buccal recession (4 RCTs, 2 CCTs), thicker mucosa (2 RCTs).
- Delayed + STA: less recession (1 RCT, 5 CCTs); no significant thickness gain.
- Autologous vs substitute: no significant difference, tendency favoring autologous.
- Timing: insufficient evidence.
