## One-line Summary

PRISMA SR+MA (792 records → 6 RCTs, only 2 meta-analysed for I²=68–98% heterogeneity): oral irrigators and interdental brushes gave higher but NOT significant improvement in peri-implant mucositis signs vs dental floss, and toothbrushing + water oral irrigator was NOT significantly different from toothbrushing alone for BOP/PI — all devices show some benefit but self-performed interproximal-hygiene evidence around implants remains limited.

## 한줄요약

PRISMA 체계적문헌고찰+메타분석(792편→6 RCT, 이질성 I²=68–98%로 2편만 메타분석): 구강세정기(water flosser)와 치간칫솔이 치실보다 임플란트 주위 점막염 개선이 더 컸으나 통계적으로 유의하지 않았고, 칫솔질+물 구강세정기가 칫솔질 단독 대비 BOP/PI에서 유의차가 없었다 — 모든 기구가 일부 효과는 있으나 임플란트 주위 자가 치간위생 근거는 여전히 제한적.

## Summary

Self-performed interproximal hygiene around implant-supported restorations has no well-defined protocol, and recommendations have largely been extrapolated from natural-tooth periodontal studies. This PRISMA systematic review + meta-analysis (Bishti, Wolfart, Tuna; RWTH Aachen) restricts inclusion to **RCTs and prospective trials** of home-care interproximal aids used **adjunctively to toothbrushing** in patients with **peri-implant mucositis** (peri-implantitis patients excluded), comparing against **toothbrushing alone**.

From 792 records, **6 RCTs** (2013–2024, 248 participants) qualified, but extreme heterogeneity (I²=68–98%) and frequent absence of control arms (4 of 6 studies) limited pooling to **only 2 studies**. Devices: **dental floss, interproximal/interdental brush, oral irrigator with water (water flosser), oral irrigator with 0.06% CHX**, measured on PI, GI, BOP, IL-6.

Descriptively, **oral irrigators and interdental brushes showed higher — but statistically non-significant — improvement** in mucositis signs versus dental floss. In the pooled meta-analysis, **toothbrushing + water oral irrigator was NOT significantly different from toothbrushing alone** for either modified plaque index (mean diff 0.2, I²=68%) or BOP (mean diff 0.24, I²=98%). The bottom line: every device offers *some* biofilm-control benefit, but no device is proven superior, and the evidence base for self-performed interproximal hygiene around implants is thin and inconsistent. Authors stress that **accessibility, manual dexterity, restoration design, and patient compliance** likely matter more than the specific device, and caution that **retained dental-floss remnants** in the peri-implant sulcus have been implicated (case reports) in localized inflammation and marginal bone resorption where rough implant surfaces are exposed.

## Key Contributions

- First SR+MA confined to **RCTs/prospective trials of self-administered interproximal aids vs toothbrushing alone** around implants — prior reviews mixed device categories or pooled teeth + implants.
- Head-to-head map of the four home-care devices (floss / interdental brush / water flosser / CHX irrigator) against PI, GI, BOP, IL-6.
- Quantifies that the implant home-care evidence is **too heterogeneous and control-poor** to establish device superiority — a deliberate "we don't know yet" result with a future-trial blueprint (≥6-month follow-up, toothbrushing-alone control, standardized mucositis definition).

## Methodology

- **PRISMA + Cochrane Handbook v6.2.** PubMed MEDLINE, Cochrane CENTRAL, Google Scholar gray literature, up to Feb 2024; manual reference screening; no language/date limit.
- **PICO** — implant restorations with mucositis (P) / homecare interproximal device (I) / toothbrush alone (C) / PI, PD, BOP, interleukin (O).
- **Inclusion**: RCTs + prospective, ≥10 patients, fixed single/multi-unit implant restorations with mucositis. **Exclusion**: retrospective/case report/animal/in-vitro, removable restorations, peri-implantitis patients, teeth+implant-pooled data.
- **Bias**: Cochrane Collaboration tool (RCTs) + ROBINS-I. **Certainty**: GRADE.
- **Pooling**: random-effects, DerSimonian–Laird τ², I², 95% CI, forest plots, RevMan 5.4.

## Results

**Selection.** 792 records (781 electronic + 11 manual) → 674 excluded on title → 118 abstracts → 24 full-text → **6 RCTs** extracted; **only 2** meta-analysable (heterogeneity).

**Included RCTs** (all parallel-design, 248 participants, ages 23–89, single implant/patient): Almoharib (14 d), Bevilacqua (2 wk, titanium SLA), Bunk (12 wk, has control), Magnuson (30 d, has control), Nwachukwu (3/6 mo), Tütüncüoğlu (12 wk, has control). Device counts: water flosser n=4, interproximal brush n=4, dental floss n=4, oral irrigator + 0.06% CHX n=1.

**Descriptive findings.**

| Device | Signal | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| Dental floss | Bevilacqua: PI ↓ p<0.01, GI ↓ p<0.0001 at 2 wk; Magnuson: 33.3% BOP reduction at 30 d; IL-6 non-significant | Mixed; GI tied to manual dexterity |
| Interdental brush | PI/GI ↓ on interproximal surfaces at 14 d; Tütüncüoğlu vs control ↓ PI/BOP/PPD at 12 wk | Non-significant vs control |
| Water flosser (oral irrigator, water) | Magnuson: 81.8% BOP reduction (2.45×, p=0.0018); Bunk: ↓ BOP-positive sites vs control p=0.004 | Significant in individual studies |
| Oral irrigator + 0.06% CHX | Bunk: severity ↓ vs toothbrushing alone p=0.001; CHX vs water NS p=0.16 | CHX vs water NS |

**Meta-analysis (2 studies — toothbrushing alone vs toothbrushing + water flosser):**
- **Modified plaque index**: no significant difference; mean difference **0.2** favoring control (toothbrushing alone); **I²=68%**.
- **BOP**: no significant difference; mean difference **0.24** favoring control; **I²=98%**.

**Bias / GRADE.** Every study ≥1 high/unclear domain; performance bias unclear in all (home-care intervention cannot be blinded); ≥3 high attrition; all low selection bias. Modified PI = moderate certainty; BOP = low (I²=98%); short-term (≤3 mo) low, >6 mo moderate–high.

**Clinical bottom line.** All interproximal devices provide *some* biofilm control and reduce peri-implant inflammation adjunctive to toothbrushing, but **none is proven superior** and **adding a water irrigator did not significantly beat toothbrushing alone** in the pooled analysis. Device choice should be **individualized** to dexterity, prosthetic design, and accessibility; floss is discouraged where rough implant surfaces are exposed (snag/retention risk). Evidence remains limited — larger ≥6-month RCTs with controls are needed.
