## Summary

Peri-implant mucositis and peri-implantitis are increasingly prevalent, so evidence-based maintenance matters. This review (JADA 2025) evaluated whether **professionally delivered supportive peri-implant therapy (SPiT)** prevents or slows disease progression, looking at radiographic bone loss, clinical parameters, and patient-reported outcomes. Across 25 studies it found a consistent signal: **individualized, risk-based recall intervals outperform fixed-interval maintenance or no maintenance.** Because of high clinical and methodological heterogeneity, results were synthesized narratively rather than pooled.

## Key Contributions

- Covers the full disease spectrum (mucositis + peri-implantitis) under one maintenance lens.
- Establishes risk-based recall (vs fixed-calendar) as the better-supported maintenance strategy for PD/BoP and implant longevity.
- Documents the evidence base's heterogeneity as the key barrier to firmer guidance.

## Methodology

- Search of MEDLINE, Embase, Cochrane Library, Web of Science through 2025-04-30.
- Included RCTs (9), cohort (13), and case-control (3) studies of professionally delivered SPiT.
- Risk of bias: Cochrane ROB 2.0 (RCTs) + Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (observational).
- Narrative synthesis — no meta-analysis (heterogeneity).

## Results

- **Risk-based, individualized recall** consistently improved probing depth and bleeding on probing vs standard or no maintenance.
- Substantial variability in adjunctive therapies, patient compliance, and systemic-comorbidity reporting.
- Clinical recommendation: integrate risk-based SPiT into routine implant maintenance; tailor recall interval + adjuncts to patient-specific risk (periodontitis history, smoking, systemic health).
- Limitations: heterogeneity and inconsistent reporting limit definitive/generalizable conclusions; no pooled effect estimates.
