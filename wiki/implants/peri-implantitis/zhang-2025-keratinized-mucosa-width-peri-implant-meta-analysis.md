## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 30 studies (1,773 subjects, 5,241 implants, follow-up 6 months–25 years) demonstrating that adequate keratinized mucosa width (KMW ≥2 mm) significantly reduces plaque index (MD −0.30), gingival index (MD −0.26), bleeding index (MD −0.20), and marginal bone loss (MD −0.27 mm) around dental implants compared to KMW <2 mm, while probing depth and CAL differences were non-significant.

## Summary

This systematic review and meta-analysis (PRISMA) addresses the contested question of whether keratinized mucosa width (KMW) around dental implants is a clinically relevant risk factor for peri-implant disease. The key threshold examined is KMW ≥2 mm (adequate) versus <2 mm (inadequate), derived from the Lang & Loe 1972 periodontal standard.

The authors searched PubMed, Web of Science, CNKI, and VIP Data (inception–May 2024), ultimately including 30 original studies — a major expansion from the prior leading meta-analysis (Ravida 2022, 9 studies). Studies spanned 1994–2024, included 1,773 patients and 5,241 implants, with follow-up from 6 months to 25 years. Study types: 15 prospective cohorts, 13 cross-sectional, 2 retrospective. Mean NOS quality score 6.43 (14/30 low risk of bias; 16/30 high risk).

Six outcomes were meta-analyzed using random-effects models (given high heterogeneity throughout):
- **PI**: MD −0.30 (95% CI −0.42 to −0.17), I²=95.5%, significant — adequate KMW associated with less plaque
- **GI**: MD −0.26 (95% CI −0.38 to −0.13), I²=91.5%, significant — new finding not reported in Ravida 2022
- **BI**: MD −0.20 (95% CI −0.33 to −0.07), I²=97.0%, significant — expanded from prior MA's 7 studies to 15
- **PD**: MD −0.03 (95% CI −0.16 to +0.10), I²=83.1%, non-significant
- **CAL**: MD −0.09 (95% CI −0.22 to +0.04), non-significant
- **BL**: MD −0.27 (95% CI −0.42 to −0.12), I²=80.8%, significant — bone loss significantly lower with adequate KMW

Sensitivity analyses confirmed stability for all significant outcomes. Trim-and-fill for BL found 0 missing studies (Egger's P=0.01 but no correction needed). Subgroup analyses showed results held across study design and systemic disease status but not in retrospective studies for PI/GI, nor in follow-up >3 years for GI/BI.

Clinical implication: clinicians should consider soft-tissue augmentation (free gingival graft or connective tissue graft) to achieve KMW ≥2 mm when it is deficient, as a measure to reduce plaque, inflammation, and marginal bone loss around implants.

## Key Contributions

1. Largest meta-analysis on KMW and peri-implant outcomes to date (30 studies vs. 9 in Ravida 2022)
2. First MA to include GI as a pooled outcome for KMW comparison — adds gingival inflammation dimension absent from prior synthesis
3. BI synthesis expanded to 15/30 studies, overcoming prior MA's limited power (7 studies, borderline BOP result)
4. Confirmed significant BL reduction (MD −0.27 mm) with KMW ≥2 mm — prior MA (Ravida 2022) found no significant BL difference
5. Trim-and-fill and sensitivity analyses support robustness despite very high I² (>80%) across all outcomes
6. Subgroup data by study design, systemic disease, implant position, follow-up, and NOS quality

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review + meta-analysis; PRISMA-compliant
- **PECO**: patients with implants; exposure = KMW <2 mm; comparison = KMW ≥2 mm; outcomes = PI, GI, BI, PD, CAL, BL
- **Databases**: PubMed, Web of Science, CNKI, VIP Data; inception–May 2024
- **Inclusion**: human studies, ≥6-month follow-up, KMW ≥2 vs. <2 mm grouping, ≥1 peri-implant clinical index
- **Quality tool**: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS 0–9); mean 6.43; 14 low-risk (≥7), 16 high-risk (4–6)
- **Statistical model**: random-effects; MD with 95% CI; Q-test and I² for heterogeneity; Begg's + Egger's for bias; trim-and-fill for correction
- **Software**: Stata v12.0

## Results

### Effect sizes — adequate KMW (≥2 mm) vs. inadequate (<2 mm)

| Outcome | k | MD | 95% CI | I² | Significant? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Plaque Index | 28 | −0.30 | −0.42, −0.17 | 95.5% | Yes |
| Gingival Index | 18 | −0.26 | −0.38, −0.13 | 91.5% | Yes |
| Bleeding Index | 19 | −0.20 | −0.33, −0.07 | 97.0% | Yes |
| Probing Depth | 29 | −0.03 | −0.16, +0.10 | 83.1% | No |
| CAL/AL | 5 | −0.09 | −0.22, +0.04 | — | No |
| Bone Loss | 14 | −0.27 | −0.42, −0.12 | 80.8% | Yes |

All four significant outcomes stable on sensitivity analysis. Trim-and-fill: BL unchanged (0 studies trimmed); BI adjusted to MD −0.31 (4 studies trimmed, still significant); PD shifted to MD −0.14 (4 studies trimmed, borderline non-significant).

### Subgroup findings

- Prospective cohort and cross-sectional designs both showed significant PI, GI, BI, BL differences favoring adequate KMW
- Retrospective cohort subgroup: PI and GI non-significant (likely underpowered: only 2 retrospective studies)
- Follow-up >3 years: GI and BI non-significant — unclear if true long-term attenuation or selection effect
- NOS ≥7 (low risk of bias): significant PD and BL associations with KMW
- Systemic disease status (present vs. absent): no significant moderation of outcomes
