## One-line Summary

Single-center cross-sectional study (70 subjects, 227 implants; 35 T2DM vs 35 non-DM) found no statistically significant difference in peri-implant disease (80% vs 77%, p=0.99), mucositis (51% vs 63%, p=0.47), or peri-implantitis (51% vs 43%, p=0.63) — but the study was underpowered and a 83–94% history-of-periodontitis baseline likely masked any T2DM effect.

## Summary

This Italian single-center, cross-sectional, observational study (University of Padua / Castelfranco Veneto Hospital) compared peri-implant disease prevalence between 35 patients with well-controlled, pharmacologically treated type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM, HbA1c-confirmed) and 35 non-diabetic patients, all carrying dental implants in function for at least one year. Using the EFP S3 diagnostic definitions (Herrera et al.), the authors found **no statistically significant association** between T2DM and peri-implant mucositis or peri-implantitis at either the subject or implant level.

This contrasts with the conventional framing of diabetes as a major peri-implantitis risk factor, and the authors argue that the **quality of metabolic control** — not the diagnosis itself — is the key determinant. Two important caveats temper the negative finding: the study was **underpowered** (observed peri-implantitis prevalence ~50% in both groups, far above the 8% planning assumption), and a very high history-of-periodontitis rate in both arms (83% diabetic, 94% non-DM) likely acted as a confounder masking any T2DM-specific effect. Plaque control, longer functional loading, and cement-retention (cement remnants) emerged as the operative correlates of disease in this cohort.

## Key Contributions

- Direct T2DM-vs-non-DM head-to-head prevalence comparison using current EFP S3 definitions — a data point that **contrasts older systematic reviews and cross-sectional studies** that report diabetes as a peri-implantitis risk factor.
- No significant difference at subject level (disease 80% vs 77%; mucositis 51% vs 63%; peri-implantitis 51% vs 43%) or implant level (disease 62% vs 66%; mucositis 28% vs 31%; peri-implantitis 34% vs 35%).
- Reframes the clinical message toward **metabolic control quality** rather than diabetes diagnosis, while honestly flagging underpowering and periodontitis-history confounding.

## Methodology

- **Design**: single-center, cross-sectional, observational (Mar–Aug 2024; ethics CET-ANV 0005306/24).
- **Participants**: ≥30 y, ≥1 implant in function ≥1 year. Exposed = T2DM (HbA1c-confirmed, all on pharmacotherapy); unexposed = non-diabetic. Excluded prediabetes, metabolic syndrome, gestational diabetes, pregnancy/breastfeeding.
- **Measures**: six-site charting (PCP-UNC 15), PPD, FMPS plaque index, FMBS bleeding on probing, suppuration, mucosal hyperplasia, mobility; periapical radiograph with ImageJ-calibrated marginal bone loss (implant shoulder reference, no baseline radiographs). Single operator.
- **Diagnosis**: EFP S3 (Herrera et al.); peri-implantitis = BoP/suppuration + PPD ≥6 mm + bone ≥3 mm apical of intraosseous portion.
- **Analysis**: a priori n=70 (35/35) for 80% power (8%→35% peri-implantitis); subject-level Mann-Whitney / Chi-square / Fisher; implant-level logistic regression with cluster-robust SE.

## Results

- **Sample**: 70 subjects (32 M, 38 F; median age 73), 227 implants analyzed (8 excluded). Diabetics older (median 77 vs 71 y, p=0.004).
- **Subject-level (T2DM vs non-DM)**: peri-implant disease 28/35 (80%) vs 27/35 (77%), p=0.99; mucositis 51% vs 63%, p=0.47; peri-implantitis 51% vs 43%, p=0.63.
- **Implant-level (104 vs 123 implants)**: disease 62% vs 66%, p=0.67; mucositis 28% vs 31%, p=0.73; peri-implantitis 34% vs 35%, p=0.91.
- **Plaque index**: higher in diseased subjects (median 27% vs 14%, p=0.003).
- **Loading period**: associated with peri-implant disease (13 vs 10 y, p=0.02) and mucositis (14 vs 11 y, p=0.04); not peri-implantitis (p=0.72).
- **Prosthesis** (screw n=61 vs cement n=166): peri-implantitis 21% vs 39% (p=0.08); mucositis 39% vs 26% (p=0.08) — trends, not significant.
- **Confounder**: history of periodontitis 83% (diabetic) / 94% (non-DM); all on supportive peri-implant therapy.
- **Limitations**: cross-sectional (no causality), small/underpowered sample, no examiner calibration or controlled probing force, no baseline radiographs.
