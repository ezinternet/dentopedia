## One-line Summary

Retrospective study (77 patients, 202 platform-switched bone-level implants): a restoration-margin-to-alveolar-crest (RM-AC) distance ≤1.5 mm gave 3.42× higher marginal bone loss risk than >1.5 mm; risk also rose with periodontitis stage (Stage 4 vs 2 = 26.31×) and with greater implant diameter (6.10×) and length (5.02×).

## Summary

The IDRA (Implant Disease Risk Assessment) diagram lists eight peri-implant-disease risk factors, one being a restoration-margin-to-alveolar-bone-crest (RM-AC) distance below 1.5 mm. This Turkish retrospective study clinically tested that single threshold against radiographic marginal bone loss in 202 platform-switched, cement-retained, bone-level implants. The null hypothesis was rejected: RM-AC ≤1.5 mm carried a 3.42-fold higher MBL incidence than >1.5 mm, confirming the IDRA cutoff as a real, measurable risk factor rather than an arbitrary line. Two co-drivers dominated even more strongly — a history of severe periodontitis (Stage 4 vs Stage 2 → 26.31× MBL) and implant macro-geometry (wider and longer implants showed higher MBL). Clinically: aim for an RM-AC > 1.5 mm (adequate supracrestal tissue height / abutment height) and weigh implant diameter/length and periodontal history when assessing peri-implant risk.

## Key Contributions

- Clinical validation of the IDRA RM-AC <1.5 mm threshold as a genuine MBL risk factor (3.42×).
- Quantifies dominant co-factors: periodontitis stage (26.31× Stage 4 vs 2), implant diameter (6.10×), length (5.02×).
- Supports post-loading radiographic prosthetic risk assessment as a complement to surgical-phase IDRA.

## Methodology

- 77 partially edentulous patients (22–76 y); 202 platform-switched conical-connection, cement-retained, bone-level implants (2016–2021).
- Follow-up 6–36 months post functional loading.
- Group A RM-AC ≤1.5 mm (n=69) vs Group B >1.5 mm (n=133); multinomial logistic regression.
- MBL: implant shoulder → alveolar bone; RM-AC: restoration margin → alveolar crest (radiographic).

## Results

- RM-AC ≤1.5 mm: 3.42× MBL incidence vs >1.5 mm (significant).
- Periodontitis Stage 4: 26.31× MBL vs Stage 2.
- Larger diameter: 6.097× MBL; greater length: 5.02× MBL.
