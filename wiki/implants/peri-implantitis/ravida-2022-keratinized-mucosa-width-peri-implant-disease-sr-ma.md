## One-line Summary

SR + meta-analysis + TSA (9 studies, 685 implants, interventional designs only): KMW <2 mm vs ≥2 mm showed significantly higher plaque accumulation (MD 0.37, p=0.002; TSA-confirmed), but MBL, PD, and soft-tissue recession differences were underpowered and inconclusive by TSA — overall risk of peri-implant disease attributable to KMW quantity remains low (GRADE: very low to low).

## Summary

This 2022 SR+MA+TSA by Ravidà et al. addresses a contested clinical question: is having less than 2 mm of keratinized mucosa width (KMW) around implants a true risk factor for peri-implant disease? The review is methodologically distinguished from prior reviews by: (1) restricting inclusion to interventional study designs (RCT, non-RCT controlled trials, prospective cohorts with comparison arms), thereby excluding cross-sectional and retrospective studies that confound cause and effect; and (2) applying Trial Sequential Analysis (TSA) to each outcome, directly testing whether the pooled evidence meets the statistical power needed for a conclusive result.

Nine studies were included in qualitative analysis (1 RCT, 3 non-RCTs, 5 prospective cohorts; follow-up 1–5 years). Four studies entered the quantitative meta-analysis, contributing 685 implants. GRADE ratings ranged from very low (soft-tissue recession, PROMs) to low (PD, MBL, mPI).

The key TSA finding is that **plaque index is the only outcome with confirmed statistical power**: 430 implants exceeded the required information size (RIS) of 310, and the z-curve crossed both the conventional and sequential boundaries (MD=0.37, p=0.002). For all other outcomes — MBL (conventionally significant at p=0.03 but TSA-reversed as underpowered; RIS 424 vs n=257), PD (RIS 2171 vs n=430), and soft-tissue recession (RIS 2525 vs n=430) — TSA classified results as inconclusive regardless of conventional p-values.

Brushing discomfort was higher for KMW<2 mm (VAS 12.28 vs 4.25 for ≥2 mm), but only one study assessed this outcome. Peri-implantitis incidence, implant survival, CAL, and gingival index could not be meta-analyzed due to insufficient comparable studies.

The authors conclude that "the impact of amount of KMW as a risk factor for developing peri-implant disease remains low" given current evidence, and that future RCTs with adequate sample sizes (≥424 for MBL; ≥2171 for PD) and longer follow-up are required.

## Key Contributions

- **TSA application**: First SR+MA to apply trial sequential analysis to the KMW-peri-implant disease question, revealing that most findings are underpowered — including a nominally significant MBL result (p=0.03) that TSA reclassifies as inconclusive
- **Plaque index as the only TSA-confirmed finding**: KMW ≥2 mm was associated with statistically and sequentially confirmed lower plaque accumulation (MD 0.37 [0.16, 0.58], p=0.002; RIS 310 met by 430 implants)
- **MBL signal but not conclusive**: MD=0.17 mm MBL favoring ≥2 mm KMW (p=0.03, I²=0%) — a clinically plausible finding, but TSA requires 424 implants and only 257 were available
- **Design integrity**: Excluding cross-sectional studies eliminates the reverse-causation confound (peri-implantitis destroying KM rather than lack of KM causing disease)
- **GRADE transparency**: All outcomes rated low or very low quality; indirectness consistently flagged as serious across outcomes

## Methodology

- **Study types included**: RCT (n=1), non-RCT controlled (n=3), prospective cohort with comparison arm (n=5); ≥1 year follow-up; KMW threshold dichotomized at 2 mm
- **Excluded**: Cross-sectional, retrospective cohort, case series — to avoid confounding directionality of KMW–disease relationship
- **Search**: PubMed/MEDLINE, Scopus, Web of Science, Grey Literature; manual search of 12 dental journals; PROSPERO CRD42021233756
- **Risk of bias**: ROBINS-I (non-RCTs); Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (prospective cohorts); inter-rater kappa 0.85–0.87
- **Meta-analysis**: Fixed-effect model (I²<50%); random-effect model (I²>50%); pooled MD with 95% CI; inverse-variance weighting
- **TSA**: Computed RIS per outcome at α=5%, power=80%; conclusiveness judged by z-curve vs trial sequential monitoring boundary
- **GRADE**: Applied per outcome; downgraded for inconsistency (high I²), imprecision (wide CI), and serious indirectness

## Results

**Peri-implantitis incidence**: Cannot be meta-analyzed — insufficient comparable studies across all 9 included papers.

**Implant survival**: Not estimable — insufficient comparable studies.
