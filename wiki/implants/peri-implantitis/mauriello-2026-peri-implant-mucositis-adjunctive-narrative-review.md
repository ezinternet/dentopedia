## Summary
Narrative review of nine RCTs (414 patients) testing chemical and pharmaceutical adjuncts to PMPR for peri-implant mucositis. PMPR alone produced significant reductions in bleeding on probing and probing depth, but complete disease resolution was inconsistent. Adjunctive agents (chlorhexidine, local antibiotics, sodium hypochlorite, probiotics, bioactive agents) yielded within-group improvements but did not reliably outperform PMPR alone. Heterogeneity in diagnostic criteria, outcomes, and protocols limits head-to-head comparison.

## Key Contributions
- Synthesizes the current adjunctive-therapy evidence base for PiM
- Reinforces PMPR as the foundation of PiM management
- Identifies methodological heterogeneity as the rate-limiting step for stronger conclusions

## Methodology
- Design: narrative review (PubMed + Scopus, up to March 2025)
- Inclusion: adult RCTs of PiM treatment, BOP outcome, ≥3-month follow-up
- 9 RCTs (414 patients), qualitative analysis only

## Results
- PMPR alone: significant BOP and PD reductions, but complete resolution inconsistent
- Adjunctive therapies (CHX / local antibiotics / NaOCl / probiotics / bioactive): within-group improvements but added benefit over PMPR limited and often non-significant
- Heterogeneity in criteria and protocols limits comparability

## Limitations
- Narrative — no quantitative pooling
- Adjunctive-agent subgroup pooling not feasible
- Recommends standardized RCTs with longer follow-up and unified outcomes
