PRISMA systematic review (11 studies, n=15–85, mean age 60–68; none at low risk of bias) on supportive peri-implant care frequency for implant-supported full-arch prostheses, recommending professional oral hygiene every 6 months and prosthesis removal at least once per year, tailored to patient risk.

## Summary

Full-arch fixed implant prostheses are the hardest reconstruction for a patient to keep clean, so the maintenance question becomes: how often should the clinician remove the prosthesis for professional supportive care? This PRISMA review screened 915 PubMed records and included 11 studies (RCTs/CCTs/cohorts, ≥1-year follow-up). The evidence was too sparse and biased to set a definitive interval, but the authors offer a pragmatic default — **professional oral hygiene every 6 months and prosthesis removal at least once per year**, individualized to each patient's risk profile.

## Key Contributions

- First SR addressing the *frequency* of supportive care specifically for full-arch prostheses.
- Converts thin evidence into an actionable clinical default (6-month OH; ≥annual prosthesis removal).
- Transparently flags low certainty — none of the 11 studies were at low risk of bias.

## Methodology

- PRISMA statement + PICO tool; PubMed search.
- Inclusion: RCTs, controlled clinical trials, cohort studies; ≥1-year follow-up; supportive peri-implant care for full-arch prostheses.
- Blinded selection (100% agreement); Cochrane Handbook quality assessment.
- 915 screened → 11 included (8 reported frequency; 3 detailed procedures).

## Results

- Patients per study 15–85; mean age 60.4–68.4 years.
- **None** of the included studies judged low risk of bias.
- No specific optimal frequency derivable; pragmatic recommendation: **professional OH every 6 months + remove prosthesis ≥ once/year**, tailored to patient risk.
