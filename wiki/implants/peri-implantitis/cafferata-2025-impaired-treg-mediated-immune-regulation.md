## One-line Summary

Retrospective histological/molecular study of human peri-implantitis granulation tissue (PI surgical n=23, explantation PI-X n=23, healthy n=20): PI lesions show FOXP3↑ and IL-35↑ but NRP-1↓ and TGF-β1↓ — Tregs are present but functionally impaired, and these alterations correlate with probing depth and vertical defect depth.

## Summary

This retrospective study from the Frankfurt Goethe University group examined the **granulation tissue** curetted during peri-implantitis (PI) surgery, asking why that tissue remains a chronic, non-resolving inflammatory lesion. It profiled the **T regulatory cell (Treg)** axis across three groups: PI lesions treated by reconstructive surgery (PI, n=23), more severe PI lesions indicated for explantation (PI-X, n=23), and healthy peri-implant tissue harvested at second-stage surgery (H, n=20).

The central finding is a **dissociated, functionally impaired Treg signature**. The Treg master transcription factor FOXP3 and the immunosuppressive cytokine IL-35 were both up-regulated in disease, yet neuropilin-1 (NRP-1) — which stabilises Treg suppressive function — and TGF-β1 were down-regulated. In other words, FOXP3⁺ Tregs accumulate in the lesion, but the machinery that makes them actually suppressive is lost. Under a pro-inflammatory microenvironment (rich in IL-6), Tregs are thought to become unstable and even acquire an osteolytic profile, so FOXP3 up-regulation reflects a compensatory rather than an effective regulatory response. The signature tracked clinical severity: NRP-1 and TGF-β1 fell as probing depth (PD) and vertical defect depth (VDD) rose, while FOXP3 and IL-35 climbed with PD and bleeding on probing (BOP).

Clinically, this supplies a **host-side explanation** for why mechanical and antimicrobial PI therapy alone often fails to fully resolve disease, and it nominates NRP-1 and IL-35 as candidate progression/risk markers and Treg-restoration (e.g., NRP-1 agonism, TGF-β1/IL-35 modulation, M2 resolution) as an adjunctive immunomodulatory direction.

## Key Contributions

- **First Treg characterization of human PI granulation tissue**, combining H&E histology, RT-qPCR, immunohistochemistry (FOXP3, NRP-1), and crevicular-fluid ELISA (IL-10, TGF-β1, IL-35).
- Defines an **impaired/dissociated Treg phenotype**: FOXP3↑ + IL-35↑ but NRP-1↓ + TGF-β1↓ — regulation present but non-functional.
- **First measurement of IL-35 and NRP-1** in human PI lesions and peri-implant crevicular fluid.
- Molecular markers correlate with clinical severity (PD, BOP, VDD), suggesting diagnostic and immunotherapeutic value.

## Methodology

- **Design**: retrospective, cross-sectional analysis of archived tissue/fluid (Goethe University Frankfurt, 2022-2024; ethics N:2022-652).
- **Groups**: PI (surgery, n=23 implants), PI-X (explantation, n=23), H (healthy second-stage biopsy, n=20). Inclusion required >2 mm keratinised mucosa, plaque index <1; standard EFP PI thresholds (BOP/SUPP, PD ≥6 mm, MBL ≥3 mm); PI-X = bone loss ≥⅔ implant length and/or mobility.
- **Tissue**: granulation tissue curetted directly from implant surface/defect (PI, PI-X); punch biopsy over cover screw (H). PI granulation tissue was fragmented and lacked epithelial landmarks, so it could not be oriented histologically.
- **Assays**: H&E; RT-qPCR (FOXP3, HELIOS, NRP1, IL10, TGFβ1, IL35B; 18S control); IHC (FOXP3 % nuclei, NRP-1 % area); crevicular-fluid ELISA (paper strips, 30 s).
- **Statistics**: Kruskal-Wallis / Mann-Whitney; Spearman correlation; multiple linear + logistic regression adjusted for age and smoking; n=20/group → 80% power (effect size 0.65); p<0.05.

## Results

- **Histology**: healthy = intact epithelium, dense collagen, minimal immune cells; PI/PI-X = fragmented collagen, abundant capillaries, dense lymphocyte + macrophage-like infiltrate. No PI vs PI-X morphological difference.
- **Transcription factors**: FOXP3 ↑ in PI (3.8-fold, p<0.001) and PI-X vs H, but PI-X < PI (p=0.02); HELIOS ↑ in PI/PI-X (p<0.001); **NRP1 ↓** in PI/PI-X (p=0.04).
- **IHC**: FOXP3⁺ cells ↑ in PI/PI-X vs H (p<0.001; PI vs PI-X ns); **NRP-1⁺ area ↓** in PI/PI-X vs H (p<0.001). Healthy NRP-1 at epithelial membrane; disease NRP-1 sparse, cytoplasmic in macrophage-like cells.
- **Cytokine mRNA**: IL10 ns (p=0.052); TGFβ1 ns (p=0.120); **IL35 ↑ 2.3-fold** (p=0.026).
- **Crevicular-fluid protein**: IL-10 unchanged (p=0.11); **TGF-β1 ↓** in PI/PI-X (p<0.001); **IL-35 ↑** in PI/PI-X (p=0.004).
- **Clinical correlation**: FOXP3 ↔ PD (rho=0.59), BOP (OR 1.17), VDD (p<0.05). NRP-1 inversely ↔ PD (rho=-0.61), BOP (OR 0.20), VDD (-0.40). TGF-β1 inversely ↔ PD (rho=-0.70). IL-35 directly ↔ PD (rho=0.53), BOP (OR 1.86). IL-10 no associations.
- **Limitations**: retrospective/cross-sectional (no causality); no flow cytometry/single-cell (no functional Treg proof); modest n; second-stage healthy controls (unloaded, earlier).
