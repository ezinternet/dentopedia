## One-line Summary

Double-blind RCT (n=60: CB-TPM 21 / chlorhexidine-TPM 20 / saline-TPM 19), all delivered by the toothpick method (TPM) — only CB-TPM reduced salivary occult blood (Cohen's d = 1.148) and uniquely produced large-effect (d ≥ 0.8) reductions in *P. micra*, *T. forsythia*, and *P. intermedia*, positioning natural Cibotium barometz extract + TPM as a side-effect-free non-surgical option for peri-implant mucositis.

## 한줄요약

이중맹검 RCT(n=60: CB-TPM 21 / 클로르헥시딘-TPM 20 / 생리식염수-TPM 19) — 세 군 모두 토스픽 방법(Toothpick Method, TPM)으로 전달했을 때, CB-TPM만 타액 잠혈을 감소(Cohen's d=1.148)시켰고 P. micra·T. forsythia·P. intermedia에서 단독으로 큰 효과(d≥0.8)를 보여, 천연 Cibotium barometz 추출물+TPM이 부작용 없는 비수술적 임플란트주위 점막염 옵션임을 제시.

## Summary

Peri-implant mucositis (PIM) is reversible soft-tissue inflammation around an implant — the precursor to peri-implantitis — and its management hinges on mechanical plaque removal. This Korean double-blind RCT (Busan, n=60) keeps the **mechanical method constant** and varies only the solution: all three arms were treated with the **toothpick method (TPM)** — expert-performed Watanabe toothbrushing using a double-row medium-bristle toothbrush — applied with either a natural **Cibotium barometz (CB) extract**, 0.12% chlorhexidine, or saline. The design isolates the chemical agent's contribution on top of an identical mechanical technique.

Across salivary inflammation biomarkers and 12 PIM-related bacteria, CB-applied TPM matched or beat chlorhexidine-applied TPM and was the only arm to reduce salivary occult blood (an early bleeding/inflammation marker). The authors position natural CB extract + TPM as a non-surgical PIM option that avoids chlorhexidine's known downsides (staining, taste change, mucosal epitheliolysis, rare hypersensitivity/parotitis).

## Key Contributions

- **First study combining TPM with a natural anti-inflammatory agent** (CB extract) against PIM-related bacteria — extends the toothpick method from natural-tooth plaque control into peri-implant maintenance.
- Three-arm head-to-head with the **delivery method held constant** (all TPM), isolating CB vs chlorhexidine vs saline as the variable.
- Shows a side-effect-free natural agent reaching large effect sizes on inflammatory bleeding and key periodontopathogens.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Randomized, double-blind, controlled trial; computer-generated randomization; double-blinded. Single clinic (M Dental Clinic, Busan), Jan–Jul 2023. ICTRP KCT0008626.
- **Power**: G*Power (α=0.05, power=0.95, d=0.5) → 54 required; 72 recruited → 60 analyzed.
- **Three arms — ALL delivered by the toothpick method (TPM)** (Watanabe toothbrushing, double-row medium-bristle brush):
  - **CB-TPM (CBG)**: 10 mg/mL Cibotium barometz extract — n=21
  - **Chlorhexidine-TPM (CG)**: 0.12% chlorhexidine — n=20
  - **Saline-TPM (SG)**: saline, negative control — n=19
- **Population**: ≥20 y, ≥1 implant, PIM (soft-tissue inflammation, no bone loss) diagnosed within past year. Smokers, severe periodontitis (PD>4 mm), xerostomia, chronic disease excluded; scaling 1 week pre-baseline to equalize plaque.
- **Outcomes (before vs after TPM)**: salivary occult blood / leukocytes / proteins / total (Sill-Ha ST-4910); 12 PIM-related bacteria by real-time PCR from subgingival paper-point samples; PSR, BOP, satisfaction.
- **Stats**: ANOVA + chi-square, paired t-test, Cohen's d (0.2/0.5/0.8), eta-squared, Duncan post-hoc.

## Results

**Baseline / homogeneity**: No significant between-group differences in demographics, oral health, BOP (SG 6.00 / CG 5.50 / CBG 5.90, p=0.967), or baseline bacteria. Patient satisfaction with the solution: **CBG 4.43/5** vs CG 1.40 vs SG 1.58.

**Salivary inflammation (Cohen's d, pre→post):**

| Marker | Saline-TPM (SG) | CHX-TPM (CG) | CB-TPM (CBG) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Occult blood | ↑ 53.6→77.3 (d=-2.21, p<0.001) | NS (d=-0.02) | **↓ 55.2→37.2 (d=1.15, p<0.001)** |
| Leukocytes | NS (d=0.38) | ↓ d=1.25 | ↓ d=1.46 |
| Proteins | NS | ↓ d=0.60 | **↓ 54.4→9.1 (d=1.97)** |
| Total | ↑ (NS) | ↓ d=0.64 | **↓ d=2.28** |

Only CB-TPM reduced salivary occult blood; saline-TPM actually increased it. Only CBG reached large effect (d≥0.8) on occult blood, proteins, and total.

**PIM-related bacteria (12 species, Cohen's d):**
- **Saline-TPM significantly INCREASED 5/12** (P. micra, E. nodatum, T. forsythia, C. rectus, S. aureus) — mechanical-only TPM with no antibacterial agent allowed regrowth.
- **Chlorhexidine-TPM increased 3/12** (P. micra, E. nodatum, T. denticola).
- **Large effect (d ≥ 0.8) ONLY in CB-TPM**: *Parvimonas micra* (d=1.088), *Tannerella forsythia* (d=0.631, the strongest CBG decrease while SG rose), *Prevotella intermedia* (d=0.912) — three species the paper flags as significant only in CBG.
- **Large effect in BOTH CB-TPM and CHX-TPM**: *Fusobacterium nucleatum* (CBG d=2.007 / CG d=1.832), *Staphylococcus aureus* (CBG d=1.122 / CG d=0.907); *Prevotella nigrescens* (CBG d=4.055 / CG d=0.563).
- *Eikenella corrodens* and *A. actinomycetemcomitans* fell in all three groups.

**Interpretation**: CB extract's flavonoids/tannins/terpenoids act through multiple antibacterial pathways (cell-wall/membrane disruption, enzyme inhibition), unlike chlorhexidine's single mechanism — proposed to explain its broader and side-effect-free suppression. CB-applied TPM is presented as a synergistic mechanical-plus-chemical non-surgical PIM option.

**Limitations**: small single-session arms (~20 each), single clinic, no long-term follow-up; some bacteria still rose post-TPM in SG/CG. Large RCTs and in-vitro verification called for.
