## One-line Summary

Single-blind RCT (n=52 patients, 63 implants, 1 year) comparing reconstructive peri-implantitis surgery (autogenous bone + xenograft + collagen membrane) vs open-flap debridement in 3- and 4-wall defects: reconstruction improved radiographic marginal bone levels (β = −1.65 mm at 12 months; p<0.001) and reduced mid-buccal recession (β = −1.68 mm; p<0.001) but showed no difference in PPD, BoP, SoP, or composite disease resolution (≈44% in both groups).

## 한줄요약

단일맹검 RCT (n=52명, 1년): 3·4벽 임플란트주위염 골결손에서 재건적 수술 (자가골 + 이종골 + 콜라겐막)은 개방소파술 대비 변연골 수준 (Marginal Bone Level, MBL) 개선 (β = −1.65 mm; p<0.001)과 협측 연조직 퇴축 감소 (β = −1.68 mm; p<0.001)에서 유의한 이점을 보였으나, 치주낭 깊이 (Probing Pocket Depth, PPD)·출혈 (Bleeding on Probing, BoP)·화농 (Suppuration on Probing, SoP)·복합 질환 해소율(양군 모두 약 44%)에는 차이가 없었다.

## Summary

This single-blind RCT from the University Medical Center Groningen (Netherlands) enrolled 52 patients with peri-implantitis that failed non-surgical instrumentation and had confirmed 3- or 4-wall bone defects intraoperatively. Patients were randomized 1:1 to reconstructive surgery (autogenous bone from SafeScraper + Bio-Oss xenograft in 1:1 mixture + Bio-Gide membrane) or open-flap debridement only. The study ran from January 2020 to January 2025 with 1-year follow-up.

The key finding is a consistent but paradoxical result: reconstruction improved radiographic bone levels and soft tissue contour throughout the year, yet failed to translate into superior composite disease resolution or reduced clinical inflammatory signs (PPD, BoP, SoP). This pattern—better structural anatomy without better clinical outcomes—raises fundamental questions about what radiographic defect fill means biologically and whether it reliably indicates re-osseointegration, especially when radiopaque xenograft is present.

Subgroup analyses (exploratory) showed that 3-walled defects appeared to benefit from reconstruction earlier (T3–T6), while 4-walled defects showed benefit only at T12, consistent with the known biological principle that more walled defects are more favorable for guided bone regeneration.

Reconstruction carried a significantly higher adverse event burden (pain, mouth dryness, metal taste, headache), which should factor into clinical decision-making given equivalent disease resolution rates.

## Key Contributions

1. **Defect morphology stratification**: First RCT to provide defect-specific (3-wall vs 4-wall) subgroup data — 3-wall defects show earlier and more pronounced MBL benefit from reconstruction
2. **Reconstruction-recession relationship confirmed**: −1.68 mm less mid-buccal recession at 1 year — clinically meaningful soft tissue benefit, consistent with Tomasi et al. SR
3. **Radiographic-clinical paradox documented**: Sustained MBL benefit without improved disease resolution or inflammatory markers — challenges reliance on radiographic endpoints as surrogates for biological success
4. **Adverse events quantified**: Significantly more patient-reported adverse events with reconstruction — pain, dryness, metal taste, headache (all p<0.05)
5. **Contemporary methodology**: Multilevel mixed-effects models, ITT analysis, LOCF — methodologically rigorous for clustered dental data

## Methodology

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Design | Single-blind parallel-group RCT, 1:1 allocation |
| Setting | University Medical Center Groningen, Netherlands |
| Period | Jan 2020 – Jan 2025 (last follow-up) |
| N (enrolled) | 52 patients (27 test, 25 control) |
| N (implants) | 63 (34 test, 29 control) |
| Defect types | 3-wall: 16 test / 19 control; 4-wall: 18 test / 10 control |
| Peri-implantitis criteria | 2017 World Workshop criteria |
| Prerequisite | Failed NSI (non-surgical submarginal instrumentation) |
| Pre-op NSI protocol | Air-polishing (Airflow Master Piezon) + erythritol powder + 0.3% CHX |
| Surgical decontamination | Air-polishing until macroscopically clean + 35% phosphoric acid gel ×1 min |
| Test intervention | Autogenous bone (SafeScraper TWIST) + Bio-Oss (1:1 mix) + Bio-Gide membrane |
| Control intervention | Open-flap debridement (changed from registered resective protocol) |
| Post-op | CHX+CPC mouthwash ×2 wks + amoxicillin 500 mg TID ×1 wk |
| Randomisation | Sealed envelope, fixed block size 4, intraoperative |
| Blinding | Assessor-blind (J.H.) |
| Primary outcomes | Mean PPD, MBL |
| Statistics | Multilevel mixed-effects models (lme4), ITT, LOCF |
| Follow-up timepoints | T0, T3, T6, T9, T12 (months) |

## Results

### Primary Outcomes — Between-group difference (mixed-effects model)

**Marginal Bone Level (MBL) — Reconstruction Superior**

| Timepoint | β (mm) | 95% CI | p |
|-----------|--------|--------|---|
| T3 | −0.85 | −1.66 to −0.05 | 0.04 |
| T6 | −1.12 | −1.93 to −0.31 | 0.01 |
| T9 | −1.39 | −2.23 to −0.55 | <0.001 |
| T12 | −1.65 | −2.54 to −0.77 | <0.001 |

*Negative β = reconstruction group had higher (better) bone level. Benefit increases over time.*

**PPD — No Significant Difference**

| Timepoint | β (mm) | 95% CI | p |
|-----------|--------|--------|---|
| All timepoints | −0.27 | −0.83 to 0.28 | 0.32 |

### Secondary Outcomes — Between-group difference

**Mid-buccal Recession — Reconstruction Superior**

| Timepoint | β (mm) | 95% CI | p |
|-----------|--------|--------|---|
| T3 | −1.06 | −1.87 to −0.24 | 0.01 |
| T6 | −1.26 | −2.08 to −0.45 | <0.001 |
| T9 | −1.47 | −2.31 to −0.64 | <0.001 |
| T12 | −1.68 | −2.57 to −0.80 | <0.001 |

**Other Secondary Outcomes — No Significant Differences**

| Parameter | β | p |
|-----------|---|---|
| BoP (%) | −7.49 | 0.21 |
| Disease resolution (OR) | −0.14 | 0.96 |
| SoP (%) | −4.16 | 0.15 |
| Midbuc KM (mm) | −0.55 | 0.27 |

### Disease Resolution at T12

| Group | Rate |
|-------|------|
| Test (reconstruction) | 43.8% |
| Control (open-flap) | 44.4% |

*Composite criterion: PPD ≤5 mm + no BoP >1/6 sites + no SoP.*

### Descriptive Clinical Trajectory

| Parameter | Test T0 → T12 | Control T0 → T12 |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| Mean PPD (mm) | 5.7 → 3.3 | 6.0 → 3.5 |
| MBL (mm, from reference) | −4.3 → −2.3 | −4.0 → −3.2 |
| BoP implant-level | 100% → 87.9% | 96.6% → 88.9% |
| SoP implant-level | 58.8% → 15.2% | 72.4% → 18.5% |

### Defect-type Subgroup Analyses (Exploratory, Post Hoc)

**3-walled defects — Reconstruction Advantage (early)**:
- MBL at T3: test 2.2 (±1.2) vs control 4.6 (±2.2) mm; p=0.03
- MBL at T6: test 2.4 (±1.4) vs control 4.3 (±2.3) mm; p=0.01
- Disease resolution at T6: test 58.3% vs control 16.7%; p=0.045

**4-walled defects — Reconstruction Advantage (later)**:
- MBL at T12: test 2.5 (±1.8) vs control 3.0 (±1.0) mm; p=0.04

### Adverse Events (Test > Control, Significant)

- Pain: p=0.03
- Mouth dryness: p=0.01
- Metal taste: p=0.03
- Headache: p=0.04

No membrane- or graft-related complications reported.
