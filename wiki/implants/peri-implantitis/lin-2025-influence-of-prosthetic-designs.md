## Summary

A joint Academy of Osseointegration / American Academy of Periodontology systematic review and meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD42023484513) appraising how implant prosthetic design influences peri-implant marginal bone loss (MBL) and peri-implantitis risk. From a 1980–2023 search, 93 comparative studies (RCT + non-RCT, ≥12 mo follow-up, ≥10 implants/group) were included; 85 entered meta-analyses. The paper consolidates many previously single-factor reviews into one evidence-weighted hierarchy of bone-protective prosthetic choices, extending the implant-type MBL literature (tissue- vs bone-level) into the prosthetic domain.

## Key Contributions

- A single, current map of which prosthetic levers move MBL and which do not.
- Strong, consistent MBL reductions for platform switching, conical internal connections, abutment height ≥2 mm, and one-abutment-one-time (all p<0.0001).
- Nonsplinting modestly favorable (p=0.04); emergence angle <30° and concave/straight profile lower peri-implantitis risk (p=0.05 and p=0.03).
- Clears two common debates: retention mode (screw vs cement, p=0.51) and crown-to-implant ratio (p=0.32) are not significant MBL drivers.

## Methodology

PECOS-framed question; pooled weighted mean difference via inverse-variance meta-analysis (RevMan), implant as unit, forest plots per factor. Subgroup analyses for crown-to-implant ratio and connection type (straight vs conical). Emergence factors analyzed as risk ratios for peri-implantitis prevalence. Random-effects when I²>50%. RoB 2 (RCTs) + ROBINS-I (non-RCTs); funnel plots for bias.
