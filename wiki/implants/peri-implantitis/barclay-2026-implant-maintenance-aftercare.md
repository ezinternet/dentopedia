## Summary

A British Dental Journal clinical article on implant maintenance and aftercare. Its argument: implants were historically placed mostly in the early-edentulous population, but placement has shifted to the wider general population — so a **patient-focused** peri-implant maintenance approach is now essential to manage biological complications and protect implant survival. The piece situates this within the **2018 periodontal classification**, the first to formally include peri-implant diseases and conditions.

## Key Contributions

- Calls for patient-tailored (not uniform) peri-implant maintenance as the recipient population broadens.
- Anchors maintenance to the 2018 classification's inclusion of peri-implant diseases.
- General-practice perspective on aftercare, biological complications, and implant survival.

## Methodology

- Narrative/clinical commentary; no systematic methodology or quantitative data.

## Results

- No quantitative outcomes. Central message: maintenance protocols must adapt to a more varied patient population to limit biological complications and preserve implant survival.
