## Summary

This randomized, assessor-blinded, multi-arm clinical trial compared five nonsurgical decontamination strategies for early peri-implantitis across 80 implants in 26 patients, with clinical outcomes (probing pocket depth, bleeding on probing, modified plaque index) at baseline, 3 months, and 6 months. Over 6 months all five modalities produced significant clinical improvement. The combination of mechanical instrumentation + glycine powder air abrasion achieved the largest numerical reductions in PPD, BOP, and plaque, yet no statistically significant differences emerged between any of the groups. The authors conclude that nonsurgical mechanical instrumentation, with or without adjuncts, yields meaningful short-term improvement in early peri-implantitis, and that adjunctive glycine powder air abrasion may add benefit without clear overall superiority. ## Key Contributions

- First head-to-head RCT in this set comparing mechanical instrumentation alone vs. three adjuncts (chlorhexidine irrigation, ozone, glycine powder air abrasion) plus glycine air abrasion monotherapy.
- Adds 6-month clinical evidence to the largely in-vitro/observational base for air-powder abrasion in peri-implant biofilm control.
- Suggests adjunct choice matters less than performing thorough nonsurgical debridement in early disease.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Randomized, assessor-blinded, multi-arm controlled clinical trial.
- **Sample**: 80 implants / 26 patients.
- **Arms (5)**: (1) mechanical instrumentation alone; (2) mechanical + chlorhexidine irrigation; (3) mechanical + ozone; (4) mechanical + glycine powder air abrasion; (5) glycine powder air abrasion monotherapy.
- **Outcomes**: PPD, BOP, modified plaque index at baseline, 3 mo, 6 mo.
- **Analysis**: Linear mixed-effects models with patient-level clustering.

## Results

(abstract-only — full text not retrieved; no point estimates beyond the abstract)

- All modalities → significant clinical improvement over 6 months.
- Greatest numerical PPD/BOP/plaque reductions with mechanical instrumentation + glycine powder air abrasion.
- No statistically significant between-group differences.
- Bottom line: nonsurgical debridement ± adjuncts helps early peri-implantitis short-term; glycine air abrasion may add benefit but does not clearly outperform the alternatives. The modest sample (26 patients, 5 arms) limits power, so the null between-group result should not be read as proven equivalence.
