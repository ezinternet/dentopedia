## Summary
MDPI Dentistry 2026 paper (Croatian universities) combining a narrative review of peri-implantitis treatments with three clinical case reports illustrating a novel biodegradable magnesium membrane for GBR in peri-implantitis defects.

## Key Contributions
- **Narrative review**: comprehensive overview of current peri-implantitis treatment options
- **Novel approach**: biodegradable **magnesium (Mg) membrane** for GBR in peri-implantitis defects
- Magnesium membrane properties: biocompatible, resorbable, osteoconductive
- **3 case reports** illustrate clinical application — results described as favorable
- Evidence level: case reports only — no comparative data, no control group
- Preliminary clinical exploration; no RCT or controlled trial data available

## Why Magnesium Membrane?
- Traditional titanium mesh (non-resorbable) requires removal → second surgery
- Collagen membranes (resorbable) may lack mechanical rigidity
- Mg membrane: resorbable with controlled degradation + structural support
- Biocompatible degradation products (magnesium ions) may have mild osteogenic effects
