## One-line Summary

Immunohistochemical study of granulation tissue biopsies from 20 human peri-implantitis lesions: macrophages occupy ~14% of the inflammatory connective tissue, with M1 (CD80) significantly exceeding M2 (CD206) at advanced sites; CD68 and M1 expression correlate with probing depth.

## Summary

This Frankfurt Goethe University study characterized the **macrophage compartment of peri-implantitis (PI) granulation tissue** — the inflamed, vascular connective tissue curetted from the intrabony defect during surgery. Twenty patients with PI (BOP ± suppuration, PD ≥6 mm, MBL ≥3 mm) had all granulation tissue circumferentially harvested and stained immunohistochemically for CD68 (pan-macrophage), CD80 (M1, pro-inflammatory) and CD206 (M2, reparative), with CD68 used as a control to confirm M1/M2 attribution.

Macrophages were a substantial fraction of the infiltrate (**14.36% of inflammatory connective tissue**), higher than earlier reports (5-11%), which the authors attribute to lesion severity and to sampling the intrabony-defect granulation tissue (where macrophages drive granulation-tissue formation) rather than supracrestal soft tissue. Overall M1 and M2 did not differ, but at **advanced** sites M1 significantly exceeded M2 (mean **M1/M2 ratio 1.56**), and both CD68 and M1 expression correlated with probing depth. The interpretation is that an **M1-skewed, "destructive" macrophage response** accompanies deeper pockets and more advanced osteolysis, and that shifting M1→M2 may be key to resolution and repair.

This is the innate-immunity counterpart to the adaptive Treg-dysfunction picture of the same tissue described by [[implants/peri-implantitis/cafferata-2025-impaired-treg-mediated-immune-regulation]].

## Key Contributions

- **First study correlating macrophage M1/M2 polarization with PI disease severity.**
- Samples **intrabony-defect granulation tissue** specifically, distinguishing it from prior supracrestal soft-tissue biopsies.
- Establishes CD68/CD80/CD206 histomorphometry with CD68 as an internal attribution control.

## Methodology

- **Design**: cross-sectional immunohistochemical study, 20 patients / 20 implants (Goethe University Frankfurt, May-Nov 2019; ethics 92/19).
- **Diagnosis / severity**: BOP ± suppuration + PD ≥6 mm + MBL ≥3 mm; severity by MBL/implant length (slight <25%, moderate 25-50%, advanced >50%).
- **Inclusion**: screw-type titanium implant with PI; plaque index <1; non-smokers; no untreated periodontitis / autoimmune disease; controlled diabetes (HbA1c ≤7).
- **Tissue**: all granulation tissue circumferentially curetted from the intrabony defect; 4% formalin; 4 μm serial sections.
- **IHC**: CD68 (1:200), CD80 (1:200), CD206 (1:300); EnVision detection; % positive area of infiltrated connective tissue (ICT).
- **Statistics**: paired t-test (M1 vs M2 within severity), one-way ANOVA + Tukey (among groups), linear regression vs PD/BOP%; n=6 per severity group; α=0.05; proof-of-principle (no a priori power).

## Results

- **Cohort**: 12 F / 8 M; 35% history of periodontitis; mean implant function 9.15 ± 6 y.
- **Biopsy**: mean surface 7.61 ± 1.4 mm²; ICT 2.5 ± 1.9 mm² (33% of biopsy).
- **Macrophage load**: CD68 = **14.36%** (95% CI 11.4-17.2) of ICT.
- **Polarization**: M1 (CD80) 7.07% (5.9-9.4); M2 (CD206) 5.22% (3.8-6.6); overall M1 vs M2 ns; **M1/M2 ratio 1.56** (1.12-1.9).
- **Severity**: M1 significantly > M2 at **advanced** cases (p=0.01); slight/moderate ns; no M1/M2 difference among groups.
- **Correlations**: CD68% ↔ PD (Coef 3.18, R=0.77, p=2.7E-07); M1% ↔ PD (Coef 1.65, R=0.57, p=0.0001); M2% and M1/M2 not associated with PD/BOP.
- **Limitations**: no healthy comparator (ethical); supracrestal soft tissue not analyzed; marker cross-reactivity; small proof-of-principle sample.
