## Summary

This umbrella systematic review and quantitative meta-meta-analysis synthesises evidence from 10 systematic reviews published between 2012 and 2023 on the significance of peri-implant keratinized mucosa (KM) width for implant health. Using Hedges' g pooled into equivalent odds ratios (eOR), the study demonstrates that narrow KM (< 2 mm) is significantly associated with mucosal recession (eOR 4.05), gingival inflammation (eOR 3.131), elevated plaque scores (eOR 5.34), and marginal bone loss (eOR 1.852). No significant associations were found for bleeding on probing, pocket depth, implant survival, or peri-implant disease prevalence. Follow-up duration did not significantly modify outcomes. Methodological quality of included SRs was assessed using AMSTAR-2; evidence certainty was graded via GRADE.

## Key Contributions

- Provides the highest-level evidence synthesis (umbrella review + meta-meta-analysis) on peri-implant KM significance, pooling 10 SRs spanning 2012–2023.
- Quantifies effect sizes using Hedges' g converted to eOR, enabling direct clinical interpretation across heterogeneous SRs.
- Identifies four outcomes significantly worsened by KM < 2 mm: mucosal recession, Gingival Index/mGI, modified Plaque Index, and marginal bone loss.
- Establishes that BOP, pocket depth, survival, and disease prevalence lack sufficient evidence for a KM-linked association.
- Demonstrates that follow-up time is not a significant moderator, suggesting KM-related risk is persistent rather than time-dependent.
- Evidence mapping component visualizes consistency and heterogeneity across prior SRs for rapid synthesis appraisal.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Umbrella systematic review with evidence mapping + quantitative meta-meta-analysis
- **Included SRs**: 10 systematic reviews, published 2012–2023
- **Comparison**: Case = KM < 2 mm (inadequate); Control = KM ≥ 2 mm (adequate)
- **Effect size**: Hedges' g → converted to equivalent odds ratios (eOR)
- **Quality appraisal**: AMSTAR-2 for each included SR
- **Evidence certainty**: GRADE framework per outcome
- **Outcomes**: Mucosal recession, Gingival Index (GI)/modified GI (mGI), modified Plaque Index (mPI), marginal bone loss (MBL), bleeding on probing (BOP), pocket depth

## Results

| Outcome | eOR | P-value |
|---|---|---|
| Mucosal recession | 4.05 | 0.03 |
| Gingival Index / mGI | 3.131 | 0.001 |
| Modified Plaque Index | 5.34 | 0.005 |
| Marginal bone loss | 1.852 | 0.0007 |
| Bleeding on probing | NS | — |
| Pocket depth | NS | — |

- KM < 2 mm threshold consistently associated with worse soft- and hard-tissue outcomes across all four significant domains.
- Follow-up time: not a significant moderator for any outcome.
- Implant survival and peri-implant disease prevalence: insufficient evidence / no significant association identified.
