## One-line Summary

Systematic review (21 studies — 19 RCTs, 2 NRCTs; 1068 patients) of nonsurgical peri-implant mucositis therapy: all protocols including mechanical debridement alone reduced BOP and improved clinical parameters, with mucositis resolution ranging 9%–100%; adjuncts gave only minor additional benefit at 6 months, and a planned network meta-analysis could not be performed due to data scarcity and heterogeneity.

## Summary

This systematic review asked how clinical parameters change in implants affected by peri-implant mucositis after different nonsurgical treatments (or no treatment), monitored for at least 6 months. After searching MEDLINE-PubMed, Cochrane CENTRAL, and Web of Science (May 2015–April 2025) for RCTs and prospective controlled studies, 21 studies (19 RCTs, 2 NRCTs; 1068 patients) were included. A network meta-analysis was planned but could not be performed because of limited data and heterogeneity. All treatments — curettes, (ultra)sonic scalers, air-polishing, laser, antimicrobials, probiotics, antibiotics, alone or combined — reduced bleeding on probing (BOP) to varying degrees. The clinical message: nonsurgical protocols, including mechanical debridement alone, improve clinical parameters, while adjuncts deliver only minor additional improvements at 6 months.

## Key Contributions

- Provides a systematic, risk-of-bias-appraised synthesis of nonsurgical peri-implant mucositis therapy (21 controlled studies; 1068 patients).
- Bottom line: mechanical debridement alone suffices to improve clinical parameters; adjuncts add little at 6 months.
- Highlights wide variability in mucositis resolution (9%–100%; only one study group reached 100%).
- Flags sparse, heterogeneous reporting of microbiological/immunological outcomes and PROs; calls for standardized outcome sets and routine PRO collection.

## Methodology

- **Question**: change in clinical parameters after nonsurgical treatment (or no treatment) of peri-implant mucositis, with >=6 months follow-up.
- **Eligible designs**: RCTs and prospective controlled studies (NRCTs).
- **Databases**: MEDLINE-PubMed, Cochrane CENTRAL, Web of Science.
- **Search window**: May 2015 – April 2025.
- **Treatments**: curettes, (ultra)sonic scalers, air-polishing, laser, antimicrobials, probiotics, antibiotics — alone or combined.
- **Planned synthesis**: network meta-analysis (NMA) — not feasible (limited data + heterogeneity); narrative synthesis instead.
- **Quality**: only one-third of studies at low risk of bias.

## Results

- 21 studies included (19 RCTs, 2 NRCTs), 1068 patients.
- All treatments reduced BOP to various extents.
- Peri-implant mucositis resolution ranged 9%–100% across study groups (only one group reached 100%).
- Microbiological/immunological data and PROs sparsely and heterogeneously reported.
- One-third of studies had low risk of bias.
- NMA not performed.
- Conclusion: all nonsurgical protocols including mechanical debridement alone improved clinical parameters; adjuncts yielded only minor improvements at 6 months.
