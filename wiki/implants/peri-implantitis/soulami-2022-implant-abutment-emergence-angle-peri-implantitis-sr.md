## One-line Summary

Systematic review (3 studies, 168–349 implants): a larger implant-abutment emergence angle (>30°) and a convex emergence profile are associated with higher peri-implantitis prevalence / marginal bone loss vs EA <30°; with only 3 eligible studies, causality is unproven and findings need cautious reading.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

Prosthetic design is an increasingly recognised but under-quantified peri-implantitis risk axis. This ACTA systematic review isolated two restorative parameters — the implant-abutment emergence angle (EA) and the emergence profile (EP) — and asked whether they relate to peri-implant disease. From 1116 titles only 3 studies qualified, so no meta-analysis was possible. The convergent signal: in bone-level implants, an EA >30° carries significantly higher peri-implantitis prevalence than EA <30°, and a convex EP is associated with higher prevalence (especially when combined with EA >30°). Because the evidence is thin and observational, the review frames these as associations, not causes. Clinically it supports a restorative bias toward smaller emergence angles and concave/straight profiles, particularly for bone-level implants, to ease hygiene access and limit MBL.

## Key Contributions

- First focused SR on emergence angle/profile as peri-implantitis risk variables.
- Consistent direction of effect: EA >30° and convex EP → higher peri-implantitis/MBL.
- Identifies bone-level implants as the subgroup where the EA effect is clearest.

## Methodology

- PICO: implants with large EA / convex EP vs small EA / concave-straight EP; outcome peri-implant disease.
- PubMed + Cochrane, inception–April 2022; 2 independent reviewers + 3rd adjudicator; reference-list hand-search.
- Eligible: (R)CTs, cohort, cross-sectional, case-control in healthy adults ≥18 y. Meta-analysis planned only if homogeneous — not performed.

## Results

- 3 included studies, 168–349 implants each.
- Peri-implantitis prevalence 16.7% and 24.8% (two studies); both significant for EA >30° vs <30° in bone-level implants.
- Third study: smaller MBL when EA ~20°–40°.
- Convex EP → significantly higher peri-implantitis (one study; another only when EA >30°).
