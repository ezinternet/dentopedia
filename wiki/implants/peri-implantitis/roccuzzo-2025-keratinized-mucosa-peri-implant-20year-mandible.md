## One-line Summary

20-year prospective cohort (n=64, posterior mandible SLA tissue-level implants) shows peri-implantitis in 25% of implants lacking keratinized mucosa vs. 4.2% in keratinized-tissue implants (OR 6.67), and 100% soft-tissue dehiscence rate in the non-keratinized group; FGG during supportive care restored outcomes equivalent to native keratinized tissue.

## 한줄요약

20년 전향적 코호트(n=64, 하악 구치부 조직수준 SLA 임플란트)에서 각화점막 없는 임플란트의 임플란트주위염 발생률은 25%(각화점막 있는 군 4.2%, OR 6.67)이고 연조직 열개 발생률은 100%였으며, 유지치료 중 시행한 유리치은이식(FGG)은 각화점막이 처음부터 있던 군에 상응하는 보호 효과를 20년간 유지했다.

## Summary

This 20-year prospective cohort study (J Periodontal Res 2025;60:1212–1221) is the longest published follow-up assessing the impact of keratinized mucosa (KM) status on peri-implant health. Starting with 128 patients enrolled between 1998 and 2002, 64 patients (64 Straumann SLA tissue-level implants) attended both 10- and 20-year follow-up visits. Patients were stratified by peri-implant soft-tissue character at implant placement: keratinized tissue (KT, n=42) or alveolar mucosa (AM, n=16), with a third group of AM patients who received a free gingival graft (FGG) during supportive care in years 1–10 (AM+FGG, n=6). All patients participated in an individualized Supportive Periodontal and Peri-implant Care (SPC) program throughout follow-up.

At 20 years, implants in the AM group showed significantly greater marginal bone loss, bleeding on probing, soft-tissue recession, and peri-implant disease compared to KT implants. Free gingival grafting (applied to AM patients who had difficulty maintaining plaque control) provided durable protection: AM+FGG outcomes at 20 years were statistically indistinguishable from KT outcomes for MBL, BOP, and peri-implantitis rate. All AM implants developed soft-tissue dehiscence (REC ≥1 mm) by 20 years, versus 35.4% of KT/AM+FGG implants.

No implants were lost between 10 and 20 years, confirming long-term osseointegration stability independent of soft-tissue character, while highlighting that peri-implant health and soft-tissue integrity are significantly compromised in the absence of keratinized mucosa.

## Key Contributions

- Establishes 20-year peri-implantitis prevalence: 4.2% (KT/AM+FGG) vs. 25% (AM); OR 6.67 (95% CI 1.09–40.9, p=0.041).
- Documents 100% soft-tissue dehiscence rate in AM group vs. 35.4% in KT/AM+FGG group; OR 81.6 (95% CI 8.45–785.9, p<0.001).
- First long-term evidence that FGG applied during SPC (years 1–10) sustains protective peri-implant outcomes for at least 10 additional years (to 20 years).
- Shows AM patients required additional treatment (antibiotics and/or surgery — CIST C/D) at 5× the rate of KT patients (50% vs. 5%, p<0.01) between 10 and 20 years.
- Cumulative MBL at 20 years: KT 0.66 mm vs. AM 1.29 mm (p=0.006); MBL progression 10→20 years: KT 0.32 mm vs. AM 0.84 mm (p<0.001).

## Methodology

- **Design**: Single-center prospective cohort study; STROBE compliant.
- **Implants**: Straumann SLA tissue-level implants, posterior mandible (premolar/molar), placed in pristine bone (no concomitant GBR).
- **Groups**: KT (keratinized tissue present at placement), AM (absent, no graft), AM+FGG (absent at placement, FGG applied during SPC years 1–10 for poor plaque control with self-reported soreness).
- **KM assessment**: Visual, binary (present/absent), mid-buccal at baseline; no quantitative width measurement reported.
- **SPC**: Individualized recall by same experienced dental hygienist throughout follow-up; standardized with oral hygiene, supra-marginal instrumentation.
- **Outcomes**: PPD (4 sites), BOP, plaque index (PII), REC (Castroviejo caliper), MBL (periapical radiographs, ImageJ; κ=0.906), peri-implant health status by 2018 Case Definitions.
- **Soft-tissue dehiscence**: Defined as REC ≥1 mm.
- **Statistics**: Non-parametric tests (Mann-Whitney, Kruskal-Wallis, Chi², Fisher's exact); logistic regression for OR estimation; STATA BE v17.1.

## Results

**Peri-implant health at 20 years**:

| Group | PH | PM | PI |
|---|---|---|---|
| KT (n=42) | 73.8% | 21.4% | 4.8% |
| AM (n=16) | 43.8% | 31.2% | 25.0% |
| AM+FGG (n=6) | 66.7% | 33.3% | 0% |
| KT+AM+FGG (n=48) | 72.9% | 22.9% | 4.2% |

**Peri-implantitis OR** (KT/AM+FGG vs. AM): OR 6.67, 95% CI 1.09–40.9, p=0.041.

**Soft-tissue dehiscence (REC ≥1 mm)**:
- AM: 16/16 (100%)
- KT/AM+FGG: 17/48 (35.4%)
- OR 81.6, 95% CI 8.45–785.9, p<0.001

**20-year cumulative MBL**: KT 0.66 mm, AM 1.29 mm (p=0.006), AM+FGG 0.90 mm (NS vs. KT).

**BOP**: KT 30.9%, AM 51.6% (p=0.015), AM+FGG 25.0% (NS vs. KT).

**REC at 20 years**: KT 0.43 mm, AM 2.69 mm (p<0.001), AM+FGG 1.50 mm (p=0.048 vs. KT, better than AM).

**Additional treatment (10–20 years)**: AM 50% vs. KT 5%, p<0.01.

**PPD and PII**: No significant differences among groups at 20 years.

**No implant loss** between 10 and 20 years across all groups.
