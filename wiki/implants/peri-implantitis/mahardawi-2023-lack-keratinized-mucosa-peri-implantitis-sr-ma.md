## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 22 studies (4,044 patients; 13,265 implants) established lack of keratinized mucosa as an independent risk factor for peri-implantitis (overall OR=2.78, 95% CI 2.07–3.74), confirmed across five subgroup analyses controlling for case definition, prosthesis type, maintenance scheme, and confounders.

## Summary

This SR+MA from Chulalongkorn University (Scientific Reports 2023;13:3778) addressed a longstanding controversy: whether inadequate keratinized mucosa (KM) width independently increases peri-implantitis risk. Previous reviews were inconclusive because they pooled heterogeneous clinical scenarios without subgroup stratification.

The authors included 22 studies (2006–2021; 4,044 patients; 13,265 implants) and meta-analyzed 16 cross-sectional studies. The overall pooled OR was 2.78 (95% CI 2.07–3.74, I²=52%, random-effects). Critically, the risk persisted and heterogeneity vanished (I²=0%) when restricting to studies using a uniform peri-implantitis definition of MBL ≥2 mm (OR=1.96, 95% CI 1.41–2.73). Confounder-adjusted studies yielded the highest OR (3.68, 95% CI 2.32–5.82), confirming that KM insufficiency is not merely a proxy for other risk factors.

GRADE evidence certainty: **moderate**, limited by the cross-sectional design of most included studies.

## Key Contributions

- Established lack of KM as an **independent** peri-implantitis risk factor via subgroup analysis controlling for peri-implantitis case definition, prosthesis type (fixed only), maintenance regime, and multilevel statistical adjustment for other variables.
- Showed OR stability across different KM cutoff definitions: 2 mm (OR=2.73), 1 mm (OR=3.37), and presence/absence (OR=3.24).
- Demonstrated that standardizing peri-implantitis case definitions eliminates inter-study heterogeneity (I²=52% overall → 0% in same-definition subgroup), providing a methodological argument for future study design.
- Underscored that even patients under regular implant maintenance with inadequate KM carry elevated peri-implantitis risk (OR=2.08), contradicting the view that maintenance compensates for tissue deficiency.

## Methodology

- **Design**: PRISMA-compliant SR+MA; PROSPERO CRD42022319868
- **Databases**: PubMed (Medline) and Scopus; no date restrictions
- **Screening**: 1,248 initial records → 968 (deduplicated) → 117 full-text → 22 included (κ=0.80 inter-examiner)
- **Statistical model**: Random-effects (DerSimonian-Laird) for heterogeneous subgroups; fixed-effects when I²=0%
- **KM thresholds**: <2 mm (13 studies), <1 mm (2 studies), presence/absence (5 studies)
- **Quality tool**: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale adapted for cross-sectional studies (scores 5–8/9)
- **Sensitivity**: Sequential leave-one-out; OR range 2.53–2.96 — result robust

## Results

| Subgroup analysis | n studies | OR (95% CI) | I² |
|---|---|---|---|
| Overall | 16 | 2.78 (2.07–3.74)* | 52% |
| MBL ≥2 mm case definition | 7 | 1.96 (1.41–2.73)* | 0% |
| Fixed prostheses only | 10 | 2.82 (1.85–4.28)* | 59% |
| Regular maintenance patients | 4 | 2.08 (1.41–3.08)* | 0% |
| Confounder-adjusted studies | 9 | 3.68 (2.32–5.82)* | 62% |

*p<0.001 or p<0.01 across all analyses

- Peri-implantitis prevalence range: 6.68–62.3% (patient-level), 4.5–58.1% (implant-level) — reflecting heterogeneous case definitions
- Individual study ORs ranged 0.50–14.94; the single inverse result (OR=0.50) was not significant
- One overdenture study found no significant KM effect — insufficient data to generalize to removable prostheses
