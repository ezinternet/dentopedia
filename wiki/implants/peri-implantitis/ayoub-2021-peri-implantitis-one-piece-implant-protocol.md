## Summary
Case series presenting a management protocol for peri-implantitis specifically in patients treated with one-piece implants. Protocol combines diode laser debridement of the infected crestal bone area, albumin-coated allograft, sticky bone, and platelet-rich fibrin (PRF). Clinical and radiographic assessment documented. One-piece implants pose unique challenges due to integrated abutment design.

## Key Contributions
- Peri-implantitis protocol specific to one-piece implants
- Diode laser: decontamination of infected bone and soft tissue
- Albumin-coated allograft + sticky bone + PRF: combined regenerative approach
- Clinical and radiographic outcomes assessed
- Addresses gap in literature: one-piece implant peri-implantitis management

## Methodology
Case series. Patients with peri-implantitis around one-piece implants. Protocol: surgical debridement, diode laser decontamination, albumin allograft + sticky bone graft, PRF membrane placement. Clinical parameters (pocket depth, bleeding on probing) and radiographic bone levels assessed pre- and post-treatment.

## Results
- Protocol successfully managed peri-implantitis in one-piece implant cases
- Diode laser effective for decontamination without thermal damage
- Sticky bone + PRF: supported bone regeneration
- Clinical and radiographic improvement demonstrated
- Protocol reproducible across case series
