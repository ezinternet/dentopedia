## Summary
This systematic review examined whether implant surface characteristics influence long-term outcomes of surgically treated peri-implantitis, restricting to ≥3-year follow-up. Across 17 studies, rough (modified) surfaces were associated with higher recurrence and implant loss than smooth (machined/turned) surfaces, and reconstructive approaches using bone grafts and membranes outperformed non-reconstructive ones. The certainty of evidence is low due to heterogeneity, small samples, and crude smooth-vs-rough classification. Clinically, it operationalizes the known double edge of surface roughness — better osseointegration but greater vulnerability once peri-implantitis is established and treated.

## Key Contributions
- Connects surface roughness to long-term surgical treatment prognosis, not just to osseointegration.
- Supports reconstructive over non-reconstructive surgical strategies.

## Methodology
SR of clinical studies with ≥3-year follow-up, stratified by surface type; outcomes recurrence, survival, clinical/radiographic parameters, surgical approach. 17 studies; no meta-analysis; GRADE certainty low.

## Results
- Rough surfaces → higher recurrence and implant loss.
- Reconstructive (graft + membrane) → more favorable outcomes.
- Evidence certainty: low.
