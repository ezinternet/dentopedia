## Summary

Maintenance is only as good as the measurements that trigger it. This Periodontology 2000 review surveys the diagnostic toolkit for the lifelong post-treatment phase of periodontal and peri-implant care: clinical probing, bleeding on probing, intraoral radiography, biomarker analysis, and microbiological testing, complemented by emerging technologies — **resonance frequency analysis (RFA/ISQ)** and **ultrasound imaging**. Combined with patient risk factors, these measures let clinicians detect recurrence or progression early and **adjust supportive-therapy intervals** accordingly.

## Key Contributions

- Positions diagnostic monitoring as the mechanism that drives recall-interval adjustment.
- Catalogs traditional and emerging peri-implant diagnostic measures in one framework.
- Couples objective measurement with patient risk assessment for holistic peri-implant health evaluation.

## Methodology

- Single-author narrative review (expert synthesis), not systematic.
- Diagnostics organized by their role across the lifelong maintenance phase.

## Results

- Maintenance goal: control disease activity and stabilize tissues via supportive care that embeds diagnostics at each follow-up.
- Early detection enables timely intervention, including **shortening/adjusting supportive-therapy intervals**.
- RFA and ultrasound imaging extend the picture beyond probing and radiography.
