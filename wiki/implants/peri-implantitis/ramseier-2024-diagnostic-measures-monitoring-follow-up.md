## Summary

This 27-page review (Periodontology 2000, 2024) by Ramseier (University of Bern) synthesizes the full diagnostic toolkit for the lifelong post-treatment maintenance phase of periodontal and peri-implant care. It is structured sequentially by diagnostic modality — each first described for natural teeth, then adapted for implants — and culminates in patient compliance data, clinical stability criteria, individualized recall-interval computation, risk stratification tools (PRA, IDRA), and future AI perspectives.

The central argument: maintenance is only as effective as the measurement system that triggers and calibrates it. Diagnostic measures should not merely flag disease but actively drive the recall interval, the risk tier, and the decision to escalate treatment. The review provides clinicians with an integrated framework linking every measurement back to a clinical decision.

## Key Contributions

1. **Comprehensive modality catalog**: One framework covers oral hygiene indices (O'Leary plaque record, Silness–Löe plaque index, disclosing agents), periodontal and peri-implant probing, BOP indices, furcation monitoring, intraoral radiography (periapical/bitewing, CBCT), GCF/PICF biomarkers (IL-1β, PGE2, MMP-8, RANKL), salivary biomarkers, microbial sampling, RFA (ISQ/Osstell), and ultrasound.

2. **SPT interval algorithm** (Ramseier et al., perio-tools.com/spt): Developed from 11,842 SPT visits across 883 patients. Residual PPD distribution is used to compute an individualized 3–12 month recall interval. Input variables: cumulative %PPD ≥4/5/6/7 mm, %BOP, furcation status, smoking, diabetes. Empirical no-change thresholds: %PPD ≥4 mm (−5% to 25%), ≥5 mm (−2% to 16%), ≥6 mm (−1% to 7%), ≥7 mm (−1% to 9%).

3. **Smoking-adjusted %BOP thresholds**: Non-smokers/former smokers <20–23%; smokers <16%. These thresholds reflect that smokers suppress gingival bleeding despite active disease (masked inflammation).

4. **20-year tooth loss analysis**: Patients attending >50% of SPT visits earlier than scheduled: mean tooth loss 0.60 vs. 1.45 for later attenders (p<0.0001 over 20 years). Early attendance effect diminishes beyond 10 years in smokers but later attenders show progressive divergence.

5. **Risk tools reviewed and validated**:
   - PRA (Lang & Tonetti 2004): 6-factor spider chart; dose-dependent prediction of tooth loss and periodontitis progression; included in 12 publications (systematic review by Lang et al.).
   - IDRA (Heitz-Mayfield et al. 2020): 8 parameters; De Ry et al. cohort (239 patients, ≥5y SPT): peri-implantitis 12% intermediate-risk vs. 27% high-risk (OR 2.727, NS); Mo et al. (110 patients, 10y): significant correlation with implant failure; overall survival 90.9%.

6. **Peri-implant probing safety**: Non-metallic probes recommended to prevent titanium microabrasion. Force ≤0.15 N recommended around implants (vs. 0.25 N around teeth) to avoid false-positive BOP. Increasing force from 0.15 to 0.25 N raises BOP by +13.7% around implants vs. +6.6% around teeth.

7. **Emerging modalities**: RFA/ISQ (Osstell) quantifies osseointegration but does not replace bone-level radiography; ultrasound (A/B/C-scan) provides buccal/lingual bone imaging without ionizing radiation but is operator-dependent. AI algorithms show early promise for automating periapical/CBCT analysis and home monitoring via patient-uploaded photographs.

## Methodology

Single-author narrative review. Literature organized by diagnostic category. No systematic search protocol, pooling, or evidence grading. Key original data are from Ramseier et al. longitudinal cohort studies (Bern University / Medi School of Dental Hygiene, 1985–2011). Sections cover 21 subsections from oral hygiene through AI, with 27 pages and ~140 references.

## Results

### Clinical Stability Criteria

**Periodontal stability (patient level)**:
- Plaque index <20% (<15% for regenerative surgery patients)
- %BOP <20% (non-smokers), <23% (former smokers), <16% (smokers)
- Minimum number of PPDs >3 mm
- Optimal risk factor reduction

**Periodontal stability (site level)**: PPD ≤4 mm without BOP; furcation involvement ≤Grade 1 (horizontal probing depth <3 mm)

**Peri-implant stability**: PPD <5 mm, absence of BOP/suppuration, <2 mm bone loss from baseline; initial bone remodeling up to 1.5 mm may be physiological

### Diagnostic Thresholds Summary

| Parameter | Threshold | Clinical Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| %BOP (non-smoker/former smoker) | <20–23% | Periodontal stability |
| %BOP (smoker) | <16% | Periodontal stability |
| PPD (site) | ≤4 mm without BOP | Stable site |
| PPD (peri-implant stability) | <5 mm | Stable implant |
| Marginal bone loss | <0.5–1 mm change | No progression |
| Probing force (teeth) | 0.25 N | Standard |
| Probing force (implants) | ≤0.15 N | Avoids false-positive BOP |
| Plaque index | <20% | Acceptable oral hygiene |

### Patient Compliance Data (n=1,336, Medi School Bern 1985–2011)

- Overall mean compliance: 69.8% (non-smokers 71.7%, former smokers 69.7%, smokers 67.0%; p<0.05 qualitative difference).
- Factors confounding compliance more than smoking: older age, female sex, longer SPT intervals, greater disease severity.
- Less than 50% of patients compliant with recommended SPT visits at 5 years (Checchi et al.); only 26% fully compliant (Jansson & Hagstrom).

### Biomarker Highlights

- Chair-side aMMP-8 (PerioSafe® PRO DRS / ImplantSafe® DR): validated for peri-implant disease screening; non-invasive, rapid, quantitative.
- PICF markers for peri-implantitis: IL-1β, MMP-8 elevated (distinguishes from healthy implants); PGE2, sST2, RANKL, IL-23 also studied.
- Salivary MIP-1α, IL-1β, IL-6, MMP-8: high sensitivity/specificity for periodontitis; fewer peri-implantitis validation studies.
