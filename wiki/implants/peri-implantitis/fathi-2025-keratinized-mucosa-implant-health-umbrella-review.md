## One-line Summary

Umbrella review of 10 SRs/MAs (7,139 participants) confirms that keratinized mucosa width ≥2 mm around dental implants is consistently associated with reduced peri-implant inflammation, plaque accumulation, mucosal recession, and peri-implantitis risk (OR 2.78 when absent); FGG remains superior for KM augmentation, while XCM is a viable esthetic-site alternative.

## Summary

This 2025 umbrella review (Journal of Oral Implantology) synthesizes 10 systematic reviews and meta-analyses — spanning 7,139 participants and 132 primary studies — on the significance of keratinized mucosa (KM) for dental implant health. Searching five major databases through August 2024 and applying AMSTAR 2 quality assessment, the authors found a consistent positive association between keratinized mucosa width (KMW) ≥2 mm and peri-implant tissue health across all included reviews. Reduced KMW was linked to greater plaque accumulation, peri-implant inflammation, mucosal recession, marginal bone loss (MBL), and elevated peri-implantitis risk. For soft tissue augmentation, free gingival grafts (FGG) outperform xenogeneic collagen matrix (XCM) in KM width gain and bleeding indices, while connective tissue grafts (CTG) are superior to XCM for gingival thickness. The overall conclusion supports maintaining ≥2 mm of KMW around implants as a clinically meaningful protective measure, though long-term interventional data remain insufficient.

## Key Contributions

- **First umbrella review** aggregating all SR/MA evidence on KM–implant health across multiple peri-implant outcomes
- Establishes OR = 2.78 (Mahardawi et al 2023) as the key quantitative signal linking KM deficiency to peri-implantitis
- Synthesizes augmentation strategy evidence: FGG > XCM for KMW gain; CTG > XCM for gingival thickness; XCM comparable to CTG for PAS
- Confirms the 2 mm KMW threshold as clinically critical for preventing bone loss and soft-tissue recession
- Highlights persistent evidence gaps: no causative proof, insufficient long-term survival data

## Methodology

**Design**: Umbrella review of systematic reviews and meta-analyses. PRISMA-compliant; search through August 2024.

**PICO**: KMW <2 mm vs. ≥2 mm at implant placement in adult patients; outcomes: probing depth (PD), soft-tissue recession, mean gingival index (mGI), peri-implantitis incidence, marginal bone loss (MBL).

**Study selection**: 47 articles identified → 26 after deduplication → 10 included for data extraction. Eligibility required comparative analyses of KM on implant health published in English; excluded narrative reviews, editorials, protocols.

**Quality appraisal**: AMSTAR 2 (16 items). Inter-rater kappa = 1.0 (selection), 0.9 (quality). Low ROB: ≥8 affirmative responses; moderate: 4–7; high: <3.

**Included reviews**: Thoma et al 2014; Moraschini et al 2017; Longoni et al 2019 (n=1,031); Moraschini et al 2020 (n=304); Montero et al 2022; Ravidà et al 2022 (n=220); Ramanauskaite et al 2022 (n=1,076); Mahardawi et al 2023 (n=4,044); Atieh et al 2024 (n=174); Oh et al 2024 (n=290).

High heterogeneity precluded pooled meta-analysis at the umbrella level; narrative synthesis used.

## Results

**Peri-implantitis risk**:
- KM deficiency significantly increases peri-implantitis risk: OR = 2.78 (Mahardawi et al 2023)
- KT prevalence at 0 mm: 20.5%–53% peri-implantitis vs. 5.1%–8% at KT ≥2 mm (Montero et al 2022)
- All 10 included reviews showed a positive association between KMW ≥2 mm and peri-implant health

**Inflammation and plaque**:
- KMW ≥2 mm significantly reduces mean plaque index (P = .002; Ravidà et al 2022)
- Adequate KT linked to less peri-implant inflammation (mGI/GI); trend favors adequate KT for bleeding but not statistically significant

**Mucosal recession and MBL**: Reduced KMW consistently linked to increased mucosal recession and marginal bone loss across multiple included reviews

**Augmentation outcomes**:
| Method | KMW gain | Gingival thickness | PAS | Preference |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| FGG | Best | Good | Good | First-line for KMW + bleeding indices |
| CTG | Good | Best (P=.001) | Similar to XCM | First-line when GT priority |
| XCM | Comparable to CTG | Less than CTG | Similar to CTG | Viable at esthetic/low-morbidity sites |

**Clinical recommendation**: ≥2 mm KMW around dental implants is critical to prevent bone loss, soft-tissue recession, and facilitate effective oral hygiene.
