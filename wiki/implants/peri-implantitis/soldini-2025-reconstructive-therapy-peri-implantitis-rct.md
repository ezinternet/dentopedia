## One-line Summary
RCT (34 patients) — after standardized non-surgical therapy, reconstructive surgery vs supportive care alone produced equal disease resolution (~44% both), but ~3× greater radiographic bone fill (1.21 vs 0.36 mm).

## 한줄요약

## Summary
This RCT asks whether reconstructive surgery adds value over supportive care after non-surgical peri-implantitis therapy. Non-responders to standardized non-surgical treatment were randomized to a reconstructive group or to supportive care alone. At 12 months, disease resolution was essentially identical (~44% in both arms), but radiographic bone fill was roughly three times greater with reconstruction. The clinical message is nuanced: reconstruction does not improve the binary resolution endpoint short-term, yet it does rebuild bone — so its value depends on whether one weights radiographic/defect morphology gains over a resolution-only definition. Pairs with Monje 2025, which compares decontamination methods within reconstructive therapy.

## Key Contributions
- Tests the incremental benefit of reconstruction vs supportive care after non-surgical therapy.
- Dissociates disease resolution (no difference) from bone fill (clear benefit).

## Methodology
RCT, 36 enrolled/34 completed. Standardized non-surgical therapy first; non-responders randomized to reconstructive (RG) vs non-reconstructive supportive (NRG). Defects ≥3 mm. Primary: 12-mo mean radiographic bone change (Mann–Whitney); PROs collected. NCT05168891 (registered post-recruitment).

## Results
- Disease resolution: NRG 44.4% vs RG 43.8% (NS).
- Bone fill: RG 1.21 ± 0.92 mm vs NRG 0.36 ± 0.59 mm (significant).
- Clinical variables: no significant difference.
