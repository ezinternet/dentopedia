## One-line Summary

Narrative review (Zurich 2026, n=clinical cases + literature synthesis) showing that concave emergence profile with tall titanium base abutment is the critical modifiable prosthetic variable for preventing and reversing peri-implant inflammation and bone loss.

## Summary

This narrative review from the University of Zurich synthesizes histological, cross-sectional, and randomized evidence on how prosthetic emergence geometry influences peri-implant tissue health. It frames the transmucosal restoration zone using four integrated nomenclatures (STH, ISC, Supraplatform Complex, EBC) and establishes restorative angle and emergence profile contour as modifiable risk factors for peri-implantitis — independent of implant position or patient compliance.

A clinical case demonstrates resolution of a 4-year angular bone defect by replacing a convex, short-abutment lithium disilicate crown with a concave, tall-abutment monolithic zirconia crown. Radiographic bone defect resolution was observed at 4 months.

## Key Contributions

- **Unified conceptual framework**: integrates EBC, critical/subcritical contour, and tripartite zone models into a single biological map of the transmucosal zone.
- **Concave EP as standard**: most evidence favors concave subcritical (B-zone) contour + slightly convex critical (E-zone) for hygiene access and mucosal stability.
- **Mucosal tunnel rule**: tunnels > 3 mm (from over-deep implant placement) → elevated plaque accumulation, delayed inflammation resolution, higher bleeding scores even in compliant patients.
- **Abutment height**: ≥ 2 mm Ti-base repositions restorative interface coronally, protecting the connective tissue zone.
- **Surface finish**: CAD/CAM polishing to < 0.2 μm Ra reduces bacterial adhesion.
- **Provisionalization**: "space, volume, time" triad; milled PMMA preferred; contour shapes future soft-tissue architecture.

## Methodology

- Narrative review; no systematic search or GRADE.
- Synthesizes histological studies, cross-sectional analyses, and small RCTs on abutment morphology.
- Two illustrative clinical cases: (1) peri-implantitis bone defect resolved by prosthetic redesign; (2) soft tissue reestablishment via provisional reconditioning.

## Results

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Supracrestal complex height | ~3.5–4 mm (2 mm epithelial + 1.5–2 mm connective tissue) |
| Optimal implant depth | 3–5 mm apical to restorative zenith |
| Critical mucosal tunnel depth | > 3 mm → hygiene compromise |
| Restorative angle threshold | > 30° linked to peri-implant inflammation (RCT evidence) |
| Ti-base height recommendation | ≥ 2 mm for subcrestal implants |
| Surface roughness target | < 0.2 μm Ra |

Biological rationale: collagen fibers in peri-implant connective tissue run *parallel* (not perpendicular as in periodontal ligament) → weaker barrier → more susceptible to inflammation if prosthetic contour promotes biofilm.
