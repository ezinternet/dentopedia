## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 57 studies: pooled peri-implantitis prevalence 19.53% (95% CI 12.87–26.19) patient-level and 12.53% (95% CI 11.67–13.39) implant-level; estimate stays highly definition-dependent and rises when probing depth is a diagnostic criterion.

## 한줄요약

57편 SR+MA: 임플란트주위염 유병률 환자 단위 19.53% (95% CI 12.87–26.19), 임플란트 단위 12.53%; 진단 정의에 크게 좌우되며 탐침 깊이 기준 사용 시 더 높음.

## Summary

A PRISMA-guided systematic review and meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD42022313472) covering literature from Dec 2005 to Dec 2021. Fifty-seven studies (18 cross-sectional, 18 longitudinal, 1 case–control, 17 cohort, 3 RCTs; all with ≥10 subjects and ≥5-year function) were pooled. The headline pooled prevalence is 19.53% at the patient level and 12.53% at the implant level, but the authors emphasize that the figure remains highly variable even after restricting to a single 2017-World-Workshop-style clinical case definition. This refines the older Derks & Tomasi epidemiology anchor by adding a more current, larger-evidence estimate while confirming the core problem persists: the number is an artifact of how peri-implantitis is defined.

## Key Contributions

- Current pooled prevalence: 19.53% (95% CI 12.87–26.19) patient-level; 12.53% (95% CI 11.67–13.39) implant-level.
- Meta-analysis of the dominant definition subgroup (BOP + PD ≥6 mm + BL ≥2 mm, 31 articles): 20.0% (95% CI 16.6–23.7) random-effects.
- Quantifies the probing-depth artifact: prevalence 24.69% (with PD) vs 17.56% (without) at patient level — diagnostic criteria, not biology, drive much of the spread.

## Methodology

Studies binned into four definition groups; Group 2 (largest weight, 53.45%) meta-analyzed in MetaXL. Heterogeneity by I²; publication bias by Egger regression + Doi plot; leave-one-out sensitivity analyses. Function-time subanalysis (5–9 y vs longer) and probing-depth subanalysis performed.

## Results

- Patient-level 19.53%; implant-level 12.53%.
- Function time non-significant: patient 17.1% vs 18.63% (p = 0.82); implant 10.98% vs 9.76% (p = 0.8).
- Probing-depth inclusion raised prevalence (patient 24.69% vs 17.56%; implant 15.21% vs 11.99%) but not significantly (p = 0.27 / 0.31).
