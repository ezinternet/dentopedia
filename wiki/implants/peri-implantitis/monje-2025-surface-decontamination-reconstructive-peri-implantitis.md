## One-line Summary
Multicenter RCT (58 patients) — electrolytic cleaning (GalvoSurge) vs hydrogen peroxide as adjunct for surface decontamination in reconstructive peri-implantitis: both produced significant PPD reduction (~3.2 mm) and comparable disease resolution, with no significant superiority of either.

## 한줄요약
다기관 RCT(환자 58명): 재건적 임플란트주위염 치료의 보조 표면오염제거에서 전해법(GalvoSurge)과 과산화수소가 둘 다 유의한 PPD 감소(~3.2mm)와 동등한 질환 해소를 보여 우열 없음.

## Summary
This powered multicenter RCT compared two adjunctive surface-decontamination strategies — electrolytic cleaning (GalvoSurge, GS) and hydrogen peroxide (HP) — within reconstructive therapy of peri-implantitis intrabony defects. Across 58 completed patients with reconstructive surgery and supportive therapy, both adjuncts achieved significant and clinically similar probing-depth reduction and disease resolution at 12 months, with no statistically significant superiority of the newer electrolytic device. It directly addresses the unresolved question from earlier decontamination reviews: single-method superiority remains unproven, so device choice can rest on access, cost, and titanium-release considerations rather than demonstrated efficacy gaps.

## Key Contributions
- First powered multicenter RCT of electrolytic vs peroxide adjunct in reconstructive peri-implantitis.
- Comparable outcomes — no efficacy advantage for the electrolytic device at 12 months.

## Methodology
Multicenter prospective two-arm RCT. 58 completers (HP 31 implants, GS 32 implants), crater-like/circumferential intrabony defects, reconstructive therapy + SPT. Outcomes at T0/6mo/12mo (radiographic T0/T2); primary disease resolution; GEE models with post-hoc power.

## Results
- PPD reduction T0→T2: GS 3.15 mm, HP 3.31 mm (both significant).
- Baseline-balanced; mean defect depth 3.98 ± 0.91 mm.
- No significant between-group difference in disease resolution.
