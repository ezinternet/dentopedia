## One-line Summary
In vitro CNC-controlled study (PLOS ONE 2025; two drill protocols, 150 holes each without irrigation): drill-bit wear (tracked by SEM TiN-coating loss) correlated directly and progressively with peak intraosseous drilling temperature, while hole-geometry deviation was only weakly affected — supporting strict adherence to drill-replacement protocols since heat rise begins well before the manufacturer's ~25–30-use limit.

## Summary
A CNC-controlled in vitro experiment (PLOS ONE 2025) quantified the relationship between drill bit wear and intraosseous temperature rise in implant osteotomy. Two proprietary drill protocols (AT, PT) were each used for 150 holes — 5–6× the manufacturer-recommended life — with no irrigation, to isolate wear as the independent variable. Temperature was recorded with three thermocouples (1.5/7/12 mm depths). Wear was tracked by SEM with TiN-coating loss as the marker. Tool wear correlated directly with peak temperature rise during drilling; geometric deviation of the hole was much less responsive to wear. The implantation phase itself stayed within acceptable temperature/torque ranges.

## Key Contributions
Provides clean quantitative evidence that drill wear, isolated from coolant and operator factors, drives temperature rise — supporting strict adherence to drill replacement protocols.

## Methodology
- Synthetic bone block (human bone characteristics).
- CNC drilling, 3200 rpm, peck-feed, intermittent strategy, **no irrigation by design**.
- 150 holes per set (vs. manufacturer's ~25–30 limit).
- Three Type-K thermocouples at 1.5, 7, 12 mm depth.
- SEM (BSED) wear assessment every 10 holes; TiN coating loss = wear marker.
- CMM verification of hole geometry.
- Implantation phase: torque + temperature recorded.

## Results
| Variable | Correlation with wear | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Peak drilling temperature | Strong, direct | Increases progressively with TiN loss |
| Hole geometry deviation | Weak | Geometry tolerant of wear |
| Implantation temperature | Stayed in acceptable range | Wear primarily affects drilling phase |
| Implantation torque | Acceptable range | — |

## Clinical Implications
- 제조사 권장 사용 횟수(보통 20–30회) 준수가 — 절대 안전 마진이 아닌 — 점진적 발열 위험 통제 수단. 권장치 직전부터도 마모는 진행 중.
- 본 연구는 의도적으로 irrigation 미사용 — 임상 절대온도는 직접 적용 불가; 그러나 마모-발열 인과관계는 유효.
- High-volume 임상에서 drill 교체 로그 유지 권장 — 사용 횟수 추적이 thermal safety 인자.
