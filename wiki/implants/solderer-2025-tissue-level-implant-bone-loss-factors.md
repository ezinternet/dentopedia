## One-line Summary
Retrospective cohort (University of Zurich, tissue-level implants; n=106 implants at 1yr, 59 at 3yr): mean marginal bone loss (MBL) was 0.93 mm at 1 year and 1.04 mm at 3 years, with smoking and jaw of treatment significantly associated with greater bone loss, while age, sex, and periodontitis history were not significant predictors (elastic net regression).

## Summary
Retrospective cohort (University of Zurich, 2006–2020) assessing biological and host factors affecting radiographic bone loss around tissue-level implants. 1479 patients screened; 106 implants at T1 (1yr), 59 at T2 (3yr). Mean MBL 0.93 mm at 1yr and 1.04 mm at 3yr. Smoking status and jaw of treatment were significantly associated with bone loss.

## Key Contributions
- Large institutional retrospective: 1479 patients with tissue-level implants
- MBL at 1yr: 0.93±0.83 mm; at 3yr: 1.04±0.97 mm
- Smoking and jaw of treatment: significant risk factors for MBL
- Elastic net regression used for multivariate analysis
- Strong Spearman correlation between mesial and distal bone loss

## Methodology
Retrospective chart review at University of Zurich. Inclusion: tissue-level implants 2006–2020, screw-retained posterior rehabilitations without simultaneous augmentation, ≥12 months follow-up. Radiographic MBL at periapical x-rays. Variables: age, sex, smoking, history of periodontitis, jaw, reconstruction type, prosthetic emergence angle. Elastic net regression.

## Results
- T1 (1yr): n=106 implants, mean MBL 0.93 mm (SD 0.83)
- T2 (3yr): n=59 implants, mean MBL 1.04 mm (SD 0.97)
- Smoking: significantly associated with greater MBL
- Jaw of treatment: significantly associated with MBL
- Strong mesial-distal MBL correlation (Spearman)
- Age, sex, periodontitis history: not significant predictors
