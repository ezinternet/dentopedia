## Summary
Consensus report from Workgroup 4 of the 2017 AAP/EFP World Workshop on the Classification of Periodontal and Peri-Implant Diseases and Conditions. Establishes standardised case definitions for peri-implant health, peri-implant mucositis, peri-implantitis, and soft/hard tissue deficiencies. These definitions are now the universal standard referenced in all subsequent clinical guidelines and epidemiological studies.

## Key Contributions
- **Peri-implant health**: absence of BOP, erythema, swelling, suppuration; no absolute probing depth defines "health"
- **Peri-implant mucositis**: BOP on gentle probing as primary criterion; plaque is etiological factor; reversible
- **Peri-implantitis**: progressive bone loss + inflammation + BOP/suppuration + increased PD; plaque-associated
- Recommends baseline radiographs and probing depths at time of prosthesis delivery
- Evidence for keratinized mucosa benefit is equivocal for long-term health

## Methodology
- Five position papers pre-commissioned for Workgroup 4
- Joint AAP/EFP workshop, 2017
- Expert consensus with structured deliberation; published simultaneously in J Periodontol and J Clin Periodontol

## Key Case Definitions
| Condition | Main criteria |
|-----------|--------------|
| Health | No BOP, no erythema/suppuration; reduced bone support compatible with health |
| Mucositis | BOP on gentle probing; ± erythema/swelling; no bone loss progression |
| Peri-implantitis | BOP ± suppuration + progressive bone loss + increased PD |
