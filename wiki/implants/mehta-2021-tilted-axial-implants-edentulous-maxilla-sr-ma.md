## One-line Summary

SR+MA (11 studies survival, 7 MBL) finding no significant difference between tilted and axial implants in immediately loaded atrophic edentulous maxillae at 3 years for implant survival (RR 1.00, 95% CI 0.98–1.01) or marginal bone loss (MD −0.02 mm, 95% CI −0.09 to 0.06).

## 한줄요약

## Summary

This systematic review and meta-analysis pooled 11 studies (7 for marginal bone loss) on tilted versus axial implants in immediately loaded atrophic edentulous maxillae. At 3 years, implant survival and marginal bone loss were statistically indistinguishable between tilted and axial placement. The findings support tilting implants — as in All-on-4-type full-arch designs — to exploit available bone and improve anteroposterior spread without a survival or bone-loss penalty over conventional axial placement.

## Key Contributions

- Quantitative survival and MBL comparison of tilted vs axial implants in the immediately loaded maxilla.
- Supports the biomechanical legitimacy of tilted full-arch designs at 3 years.

## Methodology

- PRISMA SR+MA; multiple databases + hand search; Evidence Project ROB; RevMan 5.4; GRADE.
- Outcomes: 3-year implant survival and MBL.

## Results

- Survival: RR 1.00 (0.98–1.01), P = 0.59 — no difference.
- MBL: MD −0.02 mm (−0.09 to 0.06), P = 0.69 — no difference.
