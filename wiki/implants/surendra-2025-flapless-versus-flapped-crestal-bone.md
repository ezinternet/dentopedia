## One-line Summary

Prospective RCT (n=40, posterior mandible, healed ridges) finding flapless placement preserved significantly more crestal bone than flapped at 3 months (0.32 vs 0.56 mm) and 6 months (0.48 vs 0.82 mm, both p<0.001), with 100% survival in both arms.

## Summary

This single-center randomized controlled trial tested whether avoiding flap elevation preserves crestal bone in delayed, single-tooth posterior mandibular implant placement. Forty systemically healthy patients were randomized 1:1 to flapless (soft-tissue punch) or flapped (full-thickness mucoperiosteal) surgery using identical 4.0 × 10 mm implants. On standardized periapical radiographs the flapless arm showed roughly 40% less crestal bone loss at both 3 and 6 months, a statistically significant difference, while implant survival was 100% in both groups. The authors attribute the advantage to preservation of the periosteal blood supply and reduced surgical trauma, while cautioning that flapless is technique-sensitive and unsuitable when augmentation or direct visualization is required.

## Key Contributions

- Isolates flapless vs flapped in **healed posterior-mandibular ridges**, distinct from the fresh-socket immediate-implant flap literature.
- Demonstrates a **significant early crestal-bone advantage** for flapless at both 3 and 6 months in an RCT design.
- Confirms the difference is in bone level, not survival (100% both arms short-term).
- Provides a clean RCT counterpoint to cohort data showing no crestal difference.

## Methodology

- Design: prospective RCT, computer-generated allocation, 20 per arm.
- Population: 40 healthy adults (25–55 y), single posterior mandibular tooth, CBCT ≥6 mm width / ≥10 mm height, plaque index <20%, non-/light smokers; augmentation cases excluded.
- Intervention: Group A flapless (soft-tissue punch) vs Group B full-thickness mucoperiosteal flap; standardized 4.0 × 10 mm implants; immediate healing abutments; sutures only in flap group.
- Outcome: crestal bone loss (implant-abutment junction → first bone-implant contact, mesial + distal) on standardized periapical radiographs with customized film holder, at baseline / 3 / 6 months.
- Statistics: SPSS v26; paired (intragroup) and unpaired (intergroup) t-tests; α=0.05.

## Results

- Crestal bone loss at 3 months: flapless 0.32±0.10 mm vs flapped 0.56±0.14 mm (p<0.001).
- Crestal bone loss at 6 months: flapless 0.48±0.16 mm vs flapped 0.82±0.21 mm (p<0.001).
- Significant intragroup progression 3→6 months in both arms (flapless p=0.012, flapped p=0.005).
- Implant survival 100% in both groups; no complications.
- Limitations: 6-month follow-up only, 2D radiography, single center/surgeon.
