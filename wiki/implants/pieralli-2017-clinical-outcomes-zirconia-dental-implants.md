## Summary
This JDR systematic review provided early pooled estimates for zirconia implants restored with single crowns or fixed prostheses. Across 9 trials (398 implants, 12–60 month follow-up), 12-month survival was 95.6% with most loss occurring during the healing period; thereafter survival curves were nearly flat. Mean 12-month MBL was 0.79 mm and was not significantly influenced by implant material, design, restoration type, minor augmentation, or loading mode. It remains a useful historical anchor; Mohseni 2024 later extended the evidence to 25 studies and a 10-year survival estimate.

## Key Contributions
- First high-quality pooled zirconia implant survival and MBL estimates.
- Showed failures cluster in the first year (healing period).
- No significant covariate effect on MBL.

## Methodology
SR to Nov 2015; 4196 titles → 2 RCT + 7 prospective (326 patients, 398 implants). Separate meta-analyses for first vs subsequent years; 12-mo MBL meta-analysis with covariate assessment. PRISMA.

## Results
- 12-mo survival 95.6% (95% CI 93.3–97.9); ~0.05%/yr decrease thereafter.
- 12-mo MBL 0.79 mm (95% CI 0.73–0.86).
- Design/material/restoration/loading: no significant MBL effect.
