## One-line Summary

RCT (n=21, single mandibular molars, supracrestal mucosa ≥2 mm): a buccal emergence profile designed from the mucosal width/height (W/H) ratio cut 12-month gingival recession to 0.13 ± 0.32 mm vs 0.63 ± 0.38 mm for the healing-abutment-derived profile (p=0.006), with a lower initial emergence angle (31.4° vs 40.0°, p=0.025).

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1111/cid.13088](https://doi.org/10.1111/cid.13088)), this randomized controlled trial compared two buccal emergence profile designs for single implant-supported molar restorations and tested whether the prosthetic emergence profile is a modifiable factor in postsurgical buccal mucosal recession. Twenty-one patients with supracrestal mucosal thickness ≥2 mm were randomized to a profile designed from the buccal mucosal width/height (W/H) ratio (test) or to one maintaining the original emergence profile of the healing abutment (control). At 12 months after loading, the test group showed significantly less gingival recession (0.13 ± 0.32 mm vs 0.63 ± 0.38 mm, p=0.006) and a lower initial emergence angle (31.4° vs 40.0°, p=0.025).

Clinically: when supracrestal soft tissue is ≥2 mm at a mandibular molar, design the definitive restoration's buccal emergence profile from the mucosal W/H ratio rather than simply copying the healing-abutment contour, and keep the initial emergence angle near ~32° rather than 40° to better preserve the buccal gingival margin over the first year.

## Key Contributions

- Frames the prosthetic buccal **emergence profile** as a modifiable driver of postsurgical mucosal recession, not a fixed consequence of the healing abutment.
- Demonstrates a **W/H-ratio-based design** significantly outperforms the healing-abutment-derived profile for gingival margin preservation at 12 months.
- Supplies a working **emergence-angle threshold** (~32° better than 40°) for molar sites with thick (≥2 mm) supracrestal mucosa.

## Methodology

- **Design:** Parallel-group RCT (ChiCTR registration).
- **Participants:** 21 patients, each needing a single mandibular molar implant restoration, supracrestal mucosal thickness ≥2 mm.
- **Groups:** Test = buccal emergence profile designed from buccal mucosal W/H ratio; Control = original emergence profile of the healing abutment retained.
- **Time points:** T0 (pre-definitive-restoration), T1 (prosthesis placement), T2 (1 month post-loading), T3 (12 months post-loading).
- **Outcomes:** gingival margin level change (△GM), initial emergence angle, buccal mucosal W/H ratio, marginal bone loss, implant failure, complications.

## Results

- **Gingival recession at T3 (12 mo):** test 0.13 ± 0.32 mm vs control 0.63 ± 0.38 mm — significantly lower in test (p = 0.006).
- **Initial emergence angle:** test 31.4 ± 7.22° vs control 40.0 ± 7.60° — significantly lower in test (p = 0.025).
- **Buccal mucosal W/H ratio:** test group higher at T2 than T0, then stable; control group showed a continued rising trend.
- **Authors' conclusion:** with supracrestal soft tissue ≥2 mm, a W/H-ratio-based emergence profile significantly reduced gingival margin recession; an emergence angle of ~32.4° maintained the gingival margin better than 40°.
