## One-line Summary

Human retrieval study (n=30 plateau root form implants, 0.3–24 years) demonstrating that peri-implant cortical bone elastic modulus and hardness increase significantly during the first 5 years of function (E: r=0.84; H: r=0.78; p<0.001 after 5 years), then stabilize regardless of arch location or surface treatment.

## 한줄요약

인간 회수 연구(n=30, 0.3–24년 기능)에서 플래토 루트폼 임플란트 주위 피질골의 탄성계수(Elastic Modulus)와 경도(Hardness)가 기능 후 5년까지 유의하게 증가(r=0.84, r=0.78, p<0.001)하고 이후 안정화됨을 나노인덴테이션(Nanoindentation)으로 확인.

## Summary

This human retrieval study examined the long-term evolution of cortical bone mechanical properties around plateau root form (PRF) implants. Thirty implants-in-bone specimens with no clinical failure were retrieved from patients across an in-vivo time range of 0.3 to ~24 years due to retreatment needs. Using nanoindentation (Oliver–Pharr method) under wet conditions on non-decalcified bone sections (~50 μm), the authors measured elastic modulus (E, GPa) and hardness (H, GPa) within the first three plateau contact zones (120 indentations per implant).

The central finding is that both E and H increased progressively with time in vivo, with the largest gains occurring within the first 5 years (E: r=0.84; H: r=0.78). After 5 years, values were significantly higher than earlier time points (p<0.001) and remained stable thereafter. Neither arch location (maxilla vs. mandible) nor implant surface treatment affected the long-term mechanical outcome. This suggests that peri-implant bone undergoes progressive mechanical maturation — transitioning from less mineralized woven/immature bone to mechanically stiffer lamellar bone — over approximately 5 years, after which the interface has reached a stable equilibrium.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrated time-dependent mechanical maturation of cortical bone around PRF implants across a 0.3–24 year retrieval series
- Identified ~5 years as the threshold beyond which E and H reach a stable plateau
- Showed no effect of implant surface treatment on long-term bone mechanical properties
- Provided nanoindentation-level quantification in human specimens, complementing histomorphometric data from parallel retrieval studies

## Methodology

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Design | Observational human retrieval (retrospective) |
| n | 30 PRF implants (no clinical failure) |
| Time in vivo | 0.3 to ~24 years |
| Retrieval reason | Retreatment (not failure) |
| Specimen prep | Dehydrated; methacrylate resin-embedded; non-decalcified; ~50 μm bucco-lingual sections |
| Test zones | First 3 plateau contact areas per implant |
| Indentations | 120 per implant, wet conditions |
| Max load | 300 μN; loading rate 60 μN/s; hold 10 s; unload 2 s |
| Analysis method | Oliver–Pharr for E (GPa) and H (GPa) |
| Statistics | Pearson r; group comparisons for arch/surface effects |

## Results

| Outcome | Finding |
|---|---|
| E correlation with time | r = 0.84 (strong positive) |
| H correlation with time | r = 0.78 (strong positive) |
| E and H after 5 years | Significantly higher vs. earlier (p<0.001); stable thereafter |
| Effect of arch (maxilla/mandible) | Not significant |
| Effect of surface treatment | Not significant (E: p≥0.09; H: p≥0.3) |

Both E and H increased during the first ~5 years and plateaued, indicating mechanical maturation of the bone-implant interface that reaches equilibrium by approximately 5 years of function.
