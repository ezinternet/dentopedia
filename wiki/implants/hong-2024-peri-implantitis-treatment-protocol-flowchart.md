## One-line Summary
Narrative review (Yonsei/SNU/Sanz-Martín) proposing a phased peri-implantitis treatment protocol and flowchart modeled on EFP periodontitis step-care: classify failed (extract) vs failing implants, then nonsurgical debridement ± antibiotics, then defect-morphology-guided surgery (resective/access/reconstructive) with combined surface decontamination, followed by risk-based supportive peri-implant therapy (SPT).

## Summary
Narrative review (Yonsei University/SNU/Sanz-Martín) proposing a comprehensive peri-implantitis treatment protocol and flowchart based on current scientific evidence. Adopts a phased approach analogous to the EFP periodontitis treatment protocol, with re-evaluation built into each decision point.

## Key Contributions
- Phased protocol framework analogous to EFP periodontitis step-care
- **Classify first**: failed implant (extract) vs failing implant (treat)
- **Nonsurgical treatment** (initial): mechanical debridement ± adjunctive antibiotics; antibiotics shown to improve success
- **Surgical options** (by defect morphology):
  - Resective surgery: horizontal/supracrestal defects
  - Access surgery: limited surgical access
  - Reconstructive surgery: intrabony defects with regenerative potential
- **Surface decontamination**: mechanical + chemical + pharmacological — combination required
- **Success criteria**: PD ≤5mm + no BOP + no suppuration + no progressive bone loss
- **SPT**: mandatory; interval by individual risk assessment

## Treatment Flowchart Logic
```
Peri-implantitis diagnosed
→ Failed? → Extract
→ Failing? → Nonsurgical first → Re-evaluate
  → Success? → SPT
  → Insufficient? → Surgical (defect morphology determines type)
    → Success? → SPT
    → Persistent? → Consider extraction
```
