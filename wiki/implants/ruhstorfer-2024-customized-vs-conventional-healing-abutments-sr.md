## One-line Summary

Systematic review (5 studies — 2 RCTs, 2 prospective, 1 retrospective; 190 implants, 100% survival, 6–36 mo) finding customized healing abutments trend toward better peri-implant soft/hard tissue and esthetics with less treatment pain and no biological or esthetic disadvantage vs conventional titanium stock abutments.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(5편 — RCT 2·전향 2·후향 1; 임플란트 190개, 생존율 100%, 6–36개월)로, 맞춤형 치유지대주(customized healing abutment)가 기성 티타늄 치유지대주 대비 임플란트주위 연·경조직과 심미가 더 좋아지는 경향을 보이고 치료 통증은 줄이며 생물학적·심미적 불리함은 없다고 결론.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1186/s40729-024-00581-8](https://doi.org/10.1186/s40729-024-00581-8)), this systematic review (PROSPERO CRD42024532449) screened 1,396 titles and ultimately included 5 studies — 2 randomized controlled trials, 2 prospective cohorts, and 1 retrospective investigation — comparing customized versus conventional (prefabricated titanium stock) healing abutments in 190 implants (91 customized, 99 conventional), all with 100% survival over 6–36 months. All 5 studies were judged low risk of bias (RoB2 for RCTs, JBI for cohorts). A meta-analysis was not performed because the data were too heterogeneous, so synthesis was qualitative. Across soft tissue, hard tissue, esthetics, and patient-reported pain, the review found a trend toward equal-or-better outcomes with customized abutments and, importantly, no biological or esthetic disadvantage — leading the authors to reject all four null hypotheses.

Clinically: reach for a customized (anatomically shaped) healing abutment when you want the emergence profile contoured during the healing phase itself — especially in immediate placement of esthetic/anterior sites or large posterior sockets — to spare the patient the painful stepwise compressive soft-tissue shaping at prosthesis delivery and to streamline the (digital) workflow. CAD/CAM-fabricated, sterilizable abutments can cut chairside resin fabrication time. There is no measured downside versus conventional abutments, but the evidence is short-term and thin, so this is a reasonable preference rather than a mandate.

## Key Contributions

- First systematic review to compare customized vs conventional healing abutments while holding the healing method constant (non-submerged in both arms), correcting a confounder in the earlier Lenz 2023 review.
- Consolidates a sparse literature (1,396 → 5 studies), all rated low risk of bias.
- Rejects all four null hypotheses (soft tissue, hard tissue, esthetics, PROMs) — advantage or parity for customized abutments, never a disadvantage.
- Documents customized-abutment fabrication routes actually used clinically: hand-made resin (4 studies) and CAD/CAM PEEK (1 study).

## Methodology

- **PICO**: In implant patients, do customized healing abutments yield beneficial peri-implant conditions vs conventional ones?
- **Search**: PubMed, PubMed Central, Cochrane, Web of Science to 2024-04-20; MeSH + free-text synonyms; gray literature excluded; reference hand search.
- **Dual independent screening/extraction** (TG, MR), consensus resolution, authors emailed for missing data.
- **Risk of bias**: RoB2 (RCTs) + JBI 11-item Critical Appraisal (cohorts); STROBE for observational reporting → all low risk.
- **Synthesis**: qualitative only (heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis).

## Results

- **Funnel**: 1,396 titles → 259 dup/ineligible removed → 991 excluded by title → 146 abstracts → 131 excluded → 15 + 1 hand-search full texts → **5 included**.
- **Cohort**: 2 RCTs + 2 prospective + 1 retrospective; 190 implants (91 customized / 99 conventional); follow-up 6–36 mo; **100% survival** all groups. 4 studies = immediate placement (± grafting); 1 = late placement in healed sites.
- **Soft tissue**: 2/5 favored customized — Amato significantly lower bucco-palatal volumetric reduction of keratinized tissue (p<0.001); Perez significantly better papilla indices (p<0.001). Remaining studies = stable, similar keratinized tissue both arms. Hu: customized abutments aid closure of large posterior immediate sockets.
- **Hard tissue**: Chokaree, Hu, Perez — no significant vertical bone difference (6–12 mo). Amato — horizontal ridge nearly stable at 6 mo. Crespi — significant alveolar-width gain baseline→3 mo in both, greater with customized (+2.5±0.5 mm vs +1.0±0.9 mm conventional, p<0.001); stable 3 mo→3 yr.
- **Peri-implant health**: Crespi — no significant difference in probing depth or bleeding on probing.
- **Esthetics (PES)**: mixed — Chokaree significantly better PES with customized at 6 mo (p=0.022); Perez no significant PES difference at 12 mo.
- **PROMs (pain)**: only Chokaree — NRS pain significantly higher with conventional at insertion (p=0.003) and 2 h (p=0.013); no difference at 24 h.
- **Net**: trend toward better/equal peri-implant soft & hard tissue and esthetics and less early pain with customized abutments; no measured disadvantage.
