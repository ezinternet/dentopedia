## One-line Summary
AO/AAP SR+MA (102 studies, 13,030 patients) of peri-implant disease epidemiology and risk indicators: prevalence of peri-implant mucositis 46% (CI 41–51%) and peri-implantitis 21% (CI 17–24%); periodontitis and smoking are consistent risk indicators for both, with obesity (mucositis) and diabetes/alcohol (peri-implantitis) also significant.

## 한줄요약

## Summary
AO/AAP Best-Evidence Consensus systematic review and meta-analysis of 102 studies involving 13,030 patients, providing the most comprehensive and up-to-date epidemiological data on peri-implant diseases. Prevalence: mucositis 46% (CI 41–51%), peri-implantitis 21% (CI 17–24%). Over 20 years, cumulative incidence: mucositis 53%, peri-implantitis 22%. Identifies periodontitis and smoking as consistent risk indicators for both conditions.

## Key Contributions
- Largest and most comprehensive SR+MA on peri-implant disease epidemiology to date
- First to concurrently assess systemic (diabetes, obesity, CVD), behavioral (smoking, alcohol), and patient characteristics as risk indicators
- Confirms periodontitis as the strongest risk indicator for both mucositis and peri-implantitis
- Identifies obesity as risk indicator for mucositis; diabetes and alcohol for peri-implantitis

## Methodology
- PRISMA-compliant; PROSPERO CRD4202346657800
- PECOS framework
- Databases: MEDLINE-PubMed, EMBASE, Cochrane, ClinicalTrials.gov
- Random-effects pooled analyses
- Limitation: observational designs → risk indicators only, not confirmed causal risk factors

## Results
| Outcome | Prevalence (95% CI) | 20-yr Incidence |
|---------|---------------------|----------------|
| Peri-implant mucositis | 46% (41–51%) | 53% |
| Peri-implantitis | 21% (17–24%) | 22% |

**Risk indicators for peri-implant mucositis**: periodontitis, obesity, smoking habits
**Risk indicators for peri-implantitis**: periodontitis, diabetes mellitus, smoking habits, alcohol consumption
