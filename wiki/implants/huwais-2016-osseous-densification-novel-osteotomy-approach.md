## Summary
The foundational in-vitro study introducing osseodensification (OD) as a novel osteotomy preparation technique. OD uses reversed-rotation (counterclockwise) multi-fluted burs (Densah burs) that compact and autograft trabecular bone along the osteotomy walls rather than excavating it. Compared to standard drilling and extraction drilling in porcine tibial plateau bone: OD significantly increases insertion/removal torques, creates a compacted bone layer (confirmed by µCT and SEM), and triples BIC.

## Key Contributions
- **Original Densah bur paper** — all subsequent OD clinical studies cite this as the technique foundation
- Mechanical rationale: viscoelastic bone spring-back creates smaller osteotomy → more bone contact
- Biological rationale: preserved bone matrix/cells/biochemicals in situ → accelerated healing hypothesis
- ISQ not significantly different from standard drilling — OD advantage is in torque/BIC, not initial ISQ

## Results
