## Summary
Nine studies (RCT/non-RCT/in vitro) appraised under RoB 2.0, ROBINS-I, and QUIN tools were synthesized. The consistent signal: piezosurgery significantly reduces postoperative pain (typically VAS), but generates higher intraoperative bone temperatures during osteotomy compared with conventional rotary drilling. This trade-off — patient comfort post-op vs. thermal risk intra-op — is the central clinical finding.

## Key Contributions
First SR explicitly framing piezo vs rotary as a pain-vs-heat trade-off, with appropriate bias-tool stratification.

## Methodology
- PubMed/MEDLINE, EBSCO, Google Scholar.
- 2,279 → 9 included (RCTs, non-randomized, in vitro).
- Bias appraisal: RoB 2.0, ROBINS-I, QUIN.

## Results
| Outcome | Piezo vs conventional | Direction |
|---|---|---|
| Postoperative pain (VAS) | Significant reduction | Favors piezo |
| Intraoperative temperature | Higher with piezo | Favors conventional |

## Clinical Implications
- 환자 술후 편안함이 우선이고 thermal protocol(차가운 saline, 적절한 load, quarter-turn 회전) 준수 가능한 시술자 → piezo 고려.
- D1 dense bone, deep cortical drilling 등 thermal load 큰 상황에선 회전 drill이 더 안전 가능성.
- aquilanti-2023의 in vitro 결과와 일치(piezo 발열 ↑) — 단 aquilanti는 과부하·비회전 protocol이라 절대 차이는 과대평가 가능.
