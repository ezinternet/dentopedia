## Summary

This invited review provides a structured overview of dental implant science from historical, design, and materials perspectives. Published in 2014 by biomedical engineers at UT San Antonio, it serves as a comprehensive reference for the state of implant science at that time.

### Historical timeline
- **600 AD** — Mayan mandible fragment: three carved shell pieces placed in incisor sockets; compact bone formation observed around two implants at archaeological excavation (Honduras, 1930s)
- **Middle Ages** — allografts and xenografts; abandoned due to infectious disease risk
- **1948** — Goldberg & Gershkoff: first successful subperiosteal implant
- **1957** — Per-Ingvar Brånemark (Swedish orthopedic surgeon) discovers 골유착 (osseointegration): bone grows in proximity with 티타늄 (titanium, Ti) without rejection
- **1965** — First Ti dental implants placed in human patient (34-year-old, mandible, 4 fixtures); served >40 years
- **1978–1981** — Brånemark cofounds implant company; 7 million+ Brånemark System implants placed to date
- **1982** — FDA approves Ti dental implants; Toronto Conference on Osseointegration
- **1983** — Matts Andersson develops Procera CAD/CAM crown manufacturing
- **1992** — Modern ceramic surface development begins

### Implant types
Four historical designs:
1. **골막하 임플란트 (Subperiosteal implant)** — rests on bone, under periosteum; largely historical
2. **블레이드 임플란트 (Blade implant)** — flat, knife-shaped; historical
3. **하악지 프레임 (Ramus frame)** — historical
4. **골내 임플란트 (Endosseous implant)** — screw-shaped, placed within bone; dominant modern design

Endosseous implants: typically Grade 4 commercially pure Ti (corrosion-resistant); Ti6Al4V alloy also used (stronger, more fatigue-resistant).

### Macro design parameters

**Body shape**
- Tapered vs cylindrical: canine study (Kim et al.) showed conical implants have higher primary stability but cylindrical implants had higher success rates (NS difference); conical implants may cause periosteal over-compression
- Threaded vs smooth: threaded implants — 95.5% survival; smooth implants — 75.4% survival (animal study)

**Thread design**: pitch, depth, and helix angle determine stress distribution to surrounding bone; optimal design distributes stress uniformly to minimize hypertrophy/atrophy

**Implant diameter**
- Narrow: <3.5 mm
- Standard: 3.75–4.0 mm
- Wide: >4.5 mm
- Wider diameter → greater bone contact area → better initial stability in softer bone

**Implant length**: longer implants preferred in D3/D4 (low-density) bone; affects primary mechanical stability

**임플란트-어버트먼트 연결부 (Implant-abutment connection)**
- External hex: original design; vulnerable to higher occlusal forces, micromovement
- Internal hex: developed to increase stability, reduce micromovement at the interface; now predominant

### Surface modification strategies

Surface characteristics profoundly affect osseointegration speed and quality. Three main categories:

**Physical modification**
- 샌드블라스팅 (Sandblasting/grit-blasting): Al2O3 or TiO2 particles; creates Ra 1–2 µm surface; increases surface area and mechanical interlocking

**Chemical modification**
- 산 에칭 (Acid-etching): HF, HCl/H2SO4 creates micropits; promotes osteoblast attachment
- **SLA (Sandblasted Large-grit Acid-etched)**: combined blasting + acid-etching; current gold standard for surface roughness
- 양극산화 (Anodization): electrochemical TiO2 layer formation; improves corrosion resistance; can create porous nanotubular structure

**Biochemical/biological modification**
- 하이드록시아파타이트 코팅 (Hydroxyapatite coating, HA): plasma spray (25–50 µm); chemically similar to bone mineral; enhances early osseointegration; concerns about long-term coating delamination
- 성장인자 (Growth factors): BMP-2, BMP-7 (bone morphogenetic proteins); VEGF (angiogenesis); FGF — loaded onto implant surfaces to promote osteogenesis
- **RGD 펩타이드 (RGD peptide)**: Arg-Gly-Asp sequence; cell-adhesion motif for osteoblast attachment
- 항균 코팅 (Antibacterial coating): Ag nanoparticles, chlorhexidine — emerging approach to reduce peri-implantitis risk

### Clinical benchmarks (2014 data)
- ~450,000 osseointegrated implants placed/year globally
- ~95% success rate for single-tooth implant-supported crown
- 69% of adults 35–44 have lost ≥1 permanent tooth
- 26% of adults >74 have lost all permanent teeth

### Emerging trends
- Drug-eluting implants with controlled release kinetics
- Biomimetic hierarchical surfaces (nano + micro combined topography)
- Zirconia 임플란트 (ceramic implants) — metal-free alternative
- CAD/CAM-guided surgery integration
- Antimicrobial surface functionalization for peri-implantitis prevention

## Key Contributions

1. First comprehensive English-language synthesis of implant history published in Korean oral surgery journal (accessible JKAOMS readership)
2. Organized framework linking biomaterial science to clinical design parameters
3. Bridge between historical development and then-emerging surface functionalization concepts (BMP, RGD, VEGF)

## Methodology

Invited narrative review; no systematic search protocol or PRISMA flow. Incorporates basic science, animal, and limited clinical data without formal evidence grading. Authored by biomedical engineers rather than clinicians — reflects engineering/materials science perspective.

## Results

See design parameters and surface modification sections above. No primary data; synthesizes published literature through ~2013.
