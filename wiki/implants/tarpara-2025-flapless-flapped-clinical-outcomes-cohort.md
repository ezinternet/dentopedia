## One-line Summary

Prospective non-randomized cohort (n=20, single posterior implants, 12-month post-load) where flapless gave significantly less postoperative pain and lower 6-month probing depth, but **no crestal bone height difference** vs flapped at any time point.

## 한줄요약

단일 구치부 임플란트 비무작위 코호트 (n=20, 부하 후 12개월)에서 무피판(flapless)이 술후 통증·6개월 탐침깊이는 유의하게 낮았으나, 치조정 골높이(CBH)는 피판군과 어느 시점에도 차이 없음.

## Summary

This pragmatic prospective cohort compared flapless and flapped placement of single posterior implants in healed ridges, with allocation by biotype/ridge and surgeon judgment rather than randomization. Over 12 months post functional loading it measured three domains: VAS pain, four-site probing depth, and ImageJ-quantified crestal bone height. Flapless roughly halved early postoperative pain and produced shallower probing depths at 6 months, but those probing-depth differences disappeared by 12 months and crestal bone height never differed between groups. The authors conclude flapless offers early comfort and soft-tissue benefits without changing long-term hard-tissue outcomes — the small, non-randomized design being the main caveat.

## Key Contributions

- Separates **early** (pain, soft-tissue) flapless benefits from **long-term** crestal outcomes, showing only the former.
- Multi-domain, real-world-allocated comparison (pain + PD + CBH) over 12 months post-load.
- Provides the cohort counterpoint to RCT data claiming a crestal-bone advantage for flapless.

## Methodology

- Design: prospective cohort, **non-randomized** (allocation by biotype/ridge/surgeon judgment + patient preference).
- Population: 20 adults (18–60 y), single posterior implant, ≥6 mm width / ≥10 mm height, ≥2 mm keratinized mucosa; standard medical exclusions; non-smokers. G*Power → 10/group.
- Protocol: delayed placement (≥3 mo post-extraction) and delayed loading; Adin implants; flapless via tissue punch + 2 mm healing collar vs full-thickness flap with cover screw and 3-0 silk sutures (removed day 7); amoxicillin-clavulanate prophylaxis.
- Outcomes: VAS pain days 3 & 7; probing depth (4 sites, plastic UNC-15) at 6 & 12 mo; crestal bone height on standardized IOPA (Rinn XCP, ImageJ) at baseline / 6 / 12 mo post-load. Single blinded examiner, ICC 0.85.
- Statistics: Stata 18; t-tests (VAS, PD), Mann-Whitney U + Friedman (non-normal CBH); α=0.05.

## Results

- Pain (VAS): day 3 flapless 3.1±0.74 vs flap 5.7±0.95 (p=0.001); day 7 flapless 0.5±0.71 vs flap 2.4±1.17 (p=0.001).
- Probing depth at 6 months significantly lower for flapless at all 4 sites (e.g., MB 3.43±0.42 vs 3.96±0.57, p=0.030); at 12 months no significant difference (p>0.05).
- Crestal bone height: no significant intergroup difference at baseline/6/12 mo (all p>0.05); significant intragroup loss over time in both arms (Friedman p=0.001).
- The 6-month PD difference, though significant, stayed within normal physiological range (limited clinical relevance).
- Limitations: n=20, non-randomized (selection bias), 12-month follow-up, 2D radiography, single center.
