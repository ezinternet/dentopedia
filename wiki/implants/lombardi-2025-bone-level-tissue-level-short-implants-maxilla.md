## One-line Summary

Prospective case–control study (n=58 patients, 71 short implants, 1-year follow-up) in posterior maxilla found tissue-level implants had significantly less physiological bone remodeling than bone-level implants (PBR 0.30 vs. 0.55 mm at 1 year, p=0.003), with 100% survival in both groups.

## Summary

Lombardi et al. (2025) conducted a multicenter prospective case–control study across three Italian centers to compare clinical outcomes of tissue-level (TL) and bone-level (BL) short implants in the posterior maxilla after 1 year of functional loading. All implants were from the same system (M2, Zeros; 4 mm diameter, conical hybrid connection, moderately rough surface Sa 1–2 μm), sharing identical macro-morphology — the only variable being implant-abutment junction position. TL implants (5 or 6.5 mm) had a 2 mm machined transgingival neck placed at mucosal level; BL implants (7 mm) were placed 1 mm subcrestally. Prosthetic loading occurred at 5 months with screwed metal-ceramic crowns or bridges.

The primary outcome was physiological bone remodeling (PBR) measured radiographically at placement (T0), prosthesis delivery (T1, 5 months), and 12 months loading (T2). TL implants consistently demonstrated less bone remodeling than BL implants at both timepoints. The advantage of tissue-level design is attributed to positioning the microgap away from the bone crest, reducing microbial-driven crestal inflammation. All 71 implants survived 1 year without biological or mechanical complications.

## Key Contributions

1. First comparative study of BL vs. TL short implants in posterior maxilla using identical implant macro-morphology — confirming that junction position, not implant geometry, drives bone remodeling differences.
2. Tissue-level design confers a statistically significant and clinically relevant marginal bone preservation advantage (mean 0.25 mm less PBR at 1 year, p=0.003).
3. Thin mucosa (≤2 mm) is the single strongest predictor of early bone remodeling (T0–T1) in both implant types, outweighing smoking, periodontitis, insertion torque, ISQ, and bicortical engagement.
4. Short prosthetic abutments (≤2 mm) significantly increase bone remodeling during the loading phase (T1–T2) in both types; emergence angle >30° additionally predicts increased remodeling in BL implants only.
5. Bicortical engagement of the implant apex in the sinus floor significantly improves primary stability (IT and ISQ).
6. Both implant types achieve 100% survival and zero complications at 1 year in posterior maxilla with 4.5–8.0 mm residual bone height — confirming short implants as a reliable option avoiding sinus augmentation.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Multicenter prospective case–control; STROBE guidelines; ClinicalTrials.gov NCT05975138
- **Enrollment**: June 2020–May 2023; 3 Italian centers (Catanzaro, Trieste, Padova)
- **Sample**: 59 enrolled → 58 analyzed (1 dropout); 36 TL implants (5 mm: n=18; 6.5 mm: n=18) + 35 BL implants (7 mm); 13 patients received one TL and one BL implant
- **Patient profile**: mean age 63.3 ± 11.3 years; 25M/33F; 20.3% smokers; 30.5% history of periodontitis
- **Residual bone height**: 4.5–8.0 mm (mean 6.2 ± 2.3 mm) between crest and sinus floor
- **Radiography**: Periapical long-cone paralleling; Rinn-type customized holder; ImageJ measurements; ICC intra-observer 0.99, inter-observer 0.87
- **Stability**: IT recorded by surgical motor (Implantmed, W&H); ISQ by Osstell (Smartpeg #27)
- **Statistics**: t-test / Wilcoxon rank-sum; multiple linear regression (Enter method); α=0.05

## Results

**Implant survival**: 100% (71/71) at 1 year; no biological or mechanical complications.

**Primary stability (T0)**: No significant difference between TL and BL — IT 41.9 ± 14.0 vs. 40.8 ± 11.3 N·cm (p=0.56); ISQ 66.8 ± 9.3 vs. 64.7 ± 10.3 (p=0.42). At T1, ISQ also similar: 69.5 ± 7.0 vs. 71.4 ± 6.5 (p=0.10).

**Physiological bone remodeling**:

| Period | TL (mm) | BL (mm) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| T0–T1 (healing) | 0.11 ± 0.27 | 0.34 ± 0.35 | 0.004 |
| T0–T2 (1 yr) | 0.30 ± 0.23 | 0.55 ± 0.42 | 0.003 |

**Predictors of PBR (T0–T1) — both groups**: Thin vertical mucosa ≤2 mm (TL: B=−0.410, p<0.001; BL: B=−0.480, p<0.001). Age, sex, smoking, periodontitis history, IT, ISQ, bicortical engagement all non-significant.

**Predictors of PBR (T1–T2)**:
- Both groups: short abutment ≤2 mm (significant; negative coefficient = more bone loss with shorter abutment)
- BL only: emergence angle >30° (mesial B=0.014, p=0.019; distal B=0.015, p=0.003)
- TL: emergence angle not significant

**Bicortical engagement** at T0: significantly correlated with higher IT and ISQ (positive correlation in multivariate analysis).
