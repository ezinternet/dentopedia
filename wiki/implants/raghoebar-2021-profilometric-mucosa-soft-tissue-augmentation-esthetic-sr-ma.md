## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 18 studies (2,185 screened) showing soft-tissue grafting at esthetic-zone implants significantly reduces mid-buccal recession (MD +0.34 mm, p=.002) and increases mucosa thickness (MD +0.66 mm, p<.001) for immediate placement, with stable medium-term outcomes; delayed placement shows recession benefit only.

## Summary

This EAO 2021 Consensus Conference-supported SR+MA addresses a specific clinical question: does soft-tissue augmentation (grafting) change measurable mucosal dimensions — both vertically (level/recession) and horizontally (thickness/profilometry) — at implant sites in the esthetic zone compared with no grafting?

Across 18 included studies (screened from 2,185), the meta-analysis stratified outcomes by implant placement timing:

**Immediate placement** (graft vs no graft): Grafting produced significantly less mid-buccal recession (MD +0.34 mm, 95% CI 0.13–0.56, p=.002) and significantly greater mucosa thickness (MD +0.66 mm, 95% CI 0.35–0.97, p<.001). Both linear and profilometric gains remained stable over the medium term.

**Delayed placement** (graft vs no graft): Grafting produced significantly less recession (MD +0.17 mm, 95% CI 0.01–0.34, p=.042), but the thickness advantage was not statistically significant (MD +0.22 mm, 95% CI −0.04–0.47, p=.095).

The bottom line: soft-tissue augmentation in the esthetic zone is most impactful — and statistically justified — for **immediate implant placement**, where both recession protection and tissue volume benefits are achievable. For delayed placement, the main benefit is recession reduction.

## Key Contributions

1. Provides quantitative thresholds for counseling patients on expected mucosal gain with grafting (0.34 mm less recession, 0.66 mm more thickness at immediate sites)
2. Separates immediate vs delayed placement subgroups — a clinically critical distinction that prior reviews had pooled
3. Confirms medium-term stability of augmentation outcomes — addressing the durability concern
4. Commissioned for EAO 2021 Consensus, giving findings high clinical translation weight
5. Adds profilometric framing: the "linear and profilometric" title signals movement toward 3D outcome assessment even when most included studies used linear caliper measures

## Methodology

- **Search**: MEDLINE, EMBASE, Cochrane CENTRAL; last search 1 June 2020
- **Screening**: 2,185 records → 18 included studies
- **Inclusion**: Soft-tissue augmentation at implant sites in the esthetic zone; ≥ 1-year follow-up; reporting mid-buccal mucosa level and/or thickness
- **Comparator**: Graft vs no graft; immediate vs delayed implant placement subgroups
- **Statistical model**: Random-effects meta-analysis; mean difference (MD) with 95% CI
- **Graft types included**: Connective tissue graft (CTG), xenogeneic collagen matrix (XCM), and other soft-tissue substitutes
- **Primary outcomes**: Change in mid-buccal mucosa level (mm); change in mid-buccal mucosa thickness (mm)
- **Secondary**: Implant survival, peri-implant health, patient satisfaction

## Results

### Quantitative Summary

| Placement | Outcome | MD (mm) | 95% CI | p | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Immediate | Vertical mucosa change (recession) | +0.34 | 0.13–0.56 | .002 | Significant — favor graft |
| Immediate | Mid-buccal mucosa thickness | +0.66 | 0.35–0.97 | <.001 | Significant — favor graft |
| Delayed | Mid-buccal mucosa level (recession) | +0.17 | 0.01–0.34 | .042 | Significant — favor graft |
| Delayed | Mid-buccal mucosa thickness | +0.22 | −0.04–0.47 | .095 | NS |

- Medium-term stability of outcomes confirmed across included studies
- Implant survival, peri-implant health, patient satisfaction reported (detailed data in full text)

### Clinical Interpretation

- For **immediate implant placement in the esthetic zone**: grafting provides both recession protection and genuine tissue volume gain — both clinically and statistically meaningful
- For **delayed placement**: grafting offers recession protection but the tissue-thickening benefit, while in the right direction (0.22 mm), did not reach significance in this meta-analysis — possibly due to fewer included studies or less pronounced effect after healing
- The 0.66 mm thickness gain at immediate sites is clinically relevant — a threshold of ~0.5 mm has been associated with biological protection in some studies
- Results support the principle that connective tissue grafting (or equivalent) should be considered standard of care for immediate implant placement in the esthetic zone
