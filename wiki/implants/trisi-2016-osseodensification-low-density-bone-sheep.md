## Summary
Trisi et al. published one of the foundational in vivo OD studies. In 2 sheep, the left iliac crests received 10 conventional-drilled 3.8 × 10 mm Dynamix implants and the right iliac crests received 10 OD-drilled 5 × 10 mm Dynamix implants, both healed for 2 months. The OD group showed ~30% higher bone volume percentage, significantly better removal torque, less micromotion under lateral loading, and greater ridge width than the conventional group. No implant failures occurred in either arm.

The biggest methodological caveat is that the two arms used **different implant diameters** (5 mm OD vs 3.8 mm conventional). This was likely a deliberate test of OD's purported ridge-expansion claim — you can place a wider implant in the same starting bone — but it also means the comparison conflates "drilling protocol" with "implant size," and the apparent OD advantage cannot be cleanly attributed to the bur alone.

The paper is widely cited as proof that OD raises bone volume in low-density bone, and it informs subsequent SR/MA pools (Padhye 2020, Inchingolo 2021).

## Key Contributions
- Foundational in vivo demonstration of OD's bone-volume effect (~30% higher %BV vs conventional drilling).
- First showing of OD's biomechanical advantage on **removal torque** and **micromotion under lateral load**.
- Demonstrates the **ridge-expansion** claim — OD enabled a larger implant diameter in the same starting bone.
- 0/20 implant failures at 2 months — no safety signal in this short-term sheep model.

## Methodology
2 sheep, both iliac crests exposed. Left ilium: 10 × 3.8 × 10 mm Dynamix (Cortex) implants with conventional drilling (control). Right ilium: 10 × 5 × 10 mm Dynamix implants with OD (Versah Densah). 2-month healing. Outcomes: bone volume % (histomorphometry), removal torque (biomechanical), micromotion under lateral force, ridge width. Hardware donated by Cortex and Versah.

## Results
- Implant survival: 20/20 at 2 months.
- %BV ~30% higher in OD vs conventional (sign and magnitude reported in abstract).
- Removal torque significantly higher in OD vs conventional.
- Micromotion under lateral loading significantly lower in OD vs conventional.
- Ridge width significantly greater in OD sites.
- Specific N·cm, μm, %BV values, SDs and p-values: Numerical detail not extractable from available text — see PDF tables.

## Limitations
- n = 2 sheep; 20 implants — small sample, low power.
- Implants of different diameters (5 mm OD vs 3.8 mm control) — confounds drilling-mode effect with implant-size effect.
- Single laboratory, industry-sponsored (Cortex + Versah disclosed).
- Only 2 months of healing — no long-term or loaded outcomes.
- Iliac-crest model is a proxy for low-density jawbone, not anatomically equivalent.

## Living Document Notes
- When citing the "OD gives 30% higher bone volume" finding, **always disclose the diameter mismatch** between arms — the effect size cannot be cleanly attributed to OD alone.
- Use as in vivo proof of concept for ridge expansion + biomechanical anchorage in low-density bone, not as evidence of equivalence in matched-diameter human clinical conditions.
- Subsequent SR/MA (Padhye 2020, Inchingolo 2021) pool this study but do not adjust for the diameter confound.
