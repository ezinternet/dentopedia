## One-line Summary

Meta-analysis of 23 studies found flapless implant surgery carries significantly higher implant failure risk (RR 1.75, 95% CI 1.07–2.86, P=0.03) versus open flap, though this significance disappears in low-risk-of-bias subgroup analysis; no significant difference in postoperative infection or marginal bone loss.

## Summary

Chrcanovic et al. (2014) conducted a PRISMA-compliant meta-analysis comparing flapless versus conventional open flap implant surgery across 23 studies (10 RCTs, 7 CCTs, 6 retrospective). The primary finding was a statistically significant 75% higher relative risk of implant failure with flapless surgery (RR 1.75, 95% CI 1.07–2.86). However, a sensitivity analysis restricted to the five low-risk-of-bias studies showed this significance did not hold, suggesting that much of the signal was driven by the 16 high-risk-of-bias studies. Neither postoperative infection (RR 0.96, P=0.96) nor marginal bone loss (MD −0.07 mm, P=0.16) differed significantly between techniques. Funnel plots indicated no publication bias. The authors concluded results must be interpreted cautiously given the predominantly high-risk-of-bias literature.

## Key Contributions

- Quantified 75% higher relative implant failure risk for flapless surgery (RR 1.75)
- Demonstrated no significant difference in infection rate or marginal bone loss between approaches
- Highlighted that the failure rate advantage of open flap surgery is not supported by low-risk-of-bias evidence alone
- Established a benchmark meta-analysis for surgical approach selection prior to widespread adoption of digital/CBCT-guided flapless protocols

## Methodology

- **Search**: PubMed, Web of Science, Cochrane OHGTR, hand search of 17 journals (through March 2014)
- **Inclusion**: Clinical human studies (RCT/CCT/retrospective) comparing flapless vs. open flap implant surgery
- **Exclusion**: Case reports, animal studies, in vitro, reviews
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane criteria (sequence generation, allocation concealment, incomplete outcomes, blinding) — 16 high, 2 moderate, 5 low risk
- **Statistics**: I² heterogeneity; random-effects or fixed-effects inverse variance; RR for dichotomous, MD for continuous; RevMan 5.2.8

## Results

| Outcome | RR or MD | 95% CI | P-value | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Implant failure rate | RR 1.75 | 1.07–2.86 | 0.03 | Significant (flapless worse) |
| Postoperative infection | RR 0.96 | 0.23–4.03 | 0.96 | Not significant |
| Marginal bone loss | MD −0.07 mm | −0.16–0.03 | 0.16 | Not significant |

**Sensitivity analysis**: When pooling only low-risk-of-bias studies, the failure rate difference was no longer statistically significant, diminishing the strength of the main finding.

**Quality**: 16/23 studies at high risk of bias; only 5 low risk.
