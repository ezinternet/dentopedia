## 한줄요약
다기관 후향 연구 (retrospective), n=21 환자/28 임플란트 — 골밀도화 (Osseodensification, OD)로 좁은 능선(3–4 mm)에서 평균 2.83±0.66 mm의 능선 폭 확장이 가능했고 평균 삽입토크 61.2±13.9 Ncm, 임플란트 안정성 지수 (Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ) 77±3.74를 보였으나 임플란트 생존율 92.8% (28개 중 2개 실패)로 표준 임플란트 시리즈 대비 낮고 비교군 없는 후향 디자인이 한계.

## Summary
Koutouzis et al. retrospectively pooled cases from multiple centers where OD (Densah burs in counterclockwise mode) was used to expand healed alveolar ridges and simultaneously place implants. Ridge widths were measured at the crest and 10 mm apical to the crest before and after instrumentation. Patients were stratified by starting coronal width into three groups: 3–4 mm (n = 9), 5–6 mm (n = 12), and 7–8 mm (n = 7).

Coronal expansion was largest in the narrowest ridges (2.83 ± 0.66 mm) and decreased with starting width (1.50 ± 0.97 mm and 1.14 ± 0.89 mm), with between-group p < 0.05. Mean insertion torque and ISQ at placement were high (61.2 ± 13.9 Ncm; ISQ 77 ± 3.74). Of 28 implants, 26 integrated → 92.8% survival; two failed during the healing period. Authors propose two mechanisms: (1) plastic expansion with elastic spring-back of bone against the implant surface, and (2) compaction autografting where displaced trabeculae act as a vital autograft nucleating new bone. They caution against OD in dense cortical (type I) ridges or thin-base resorbed ridges where the technique can over-strain bone and risk microfracture; in those cases they recommend GBR before attempting OD expansion.

## Key Contributions
- Quantifies OD-mediated coronal ridge expansion by starting width (greatest gain in 3–4 mm ridges).
- Mean insertion torque 61.2 ± 13.9 Ncm and ISQ 77 ± 3.74 at placement post-expansion.
- Implant survival 92.8% (26/28) — clinically usable but below typical implant survival benchmarks.
- Articulates the "do NOT undersize with OD in expansion cases" clinical rule; recommends oversizing the osteotomy to avoid bone over-strain.
- Defines the clinical envelope where OD expansion is contraindicated (type I cortical, thin-base resorbed ridges).

## Methodology
Retrospective chart review across multiple centers. Inclusion: patients who underwent implant placement via osseodensification with documented pre/post ridge-width measurements at the crest and 10 mm apical. Outcome variables: change in ridge width, insertion torque (Ncm), ISQ at placement, implant integration/survival at follow-up. Three subgroups by starting coronal width were compared (one-way contrast, p < 0.05). No comparator group, no blinded outcome assessor.

## Results
- Coronal expansion (mean ± SD): 3–4 mm group 2.83 ± 0.66 mm; 5–6 mm group 1.50 ± 0.97 mm; 7–8 mm group 1.14 ± 0.89 mm. Between-group p < 0.05.
- Mean insertion torque overall: 61.2 ± 13.9 Ncm.
- Mean ISQ at placement overall: 77 ± 3.74.
- Implant survival: 26/28 = 92.8%; 2 failures during integration.
- Authors cite a separate 16-case series showing stable ISQ through the 6-week healing window (interpreted as continuous primary-to-secondary stability via compacted autograft).

## Limitations
- Retrospective, multicenter, no comparator group, no blinding.
- Small n (21 patients / 28 implants), unbalanced subgroups.
- Apical-level expansion data not foregrounded statistically — only coronal expansion analyzed.
- 92.8% survival is notably below typical immediate-/healed-ridge benchmarks; two failures meaningfully shift the rate in this small sample.
- Authors are inventors / licensors of OD — conflict of interest.
- Living-document note: authors' enthusiastic framing of "predictable" expansion should be balanced against this survival rate and the absence of randomization.
