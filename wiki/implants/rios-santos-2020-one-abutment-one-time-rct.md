## One-line Summary

Multicenter double-blind RCT (147 patients, 231 platform-switched subcrestal implants) finding no significant 12-month difference in peri-implant marginal bone loss or ISQ between the one-abutment-one-time protocol (0.36 ± 0.79 mm) and repeated healing-abutment disconnection (0.48 ± 0.71 mm); abutment height did matter (2 mm < 1 mm bone loss, p = 0.002).

## 한줄요약

다기관 이중맹검 RCT(환자 147명, 플랫폼 스위칭·치조골하 임플란트 231개)에서 원-어버트먼트-원-타임(0.36 ± 0.79 mm)과 반복적 치유 어버트먼트 분리(0.48 ± 0.71 mm) 간 12개월 변연골소실·ISQ 유의차 없음; 단, 어버트먼트 높이는 유의(2 mm < 1 mm 골소실, p = 0.002).

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.3390/ijerph17249453](https://doi.org/10.3390/ijerph17249453)), this 20-center, double-blind RCT in Spain and Portugal randomized 147 patients (231 Klockner VEGA platform-switched, subcrestal, bone-level implants) to either the "One Abutment—One Time" (OAOT) protocol — definitive abutment placed on surgery day and never removed — or a conventional control in which the healing screw was disconnected and replaced up to three times during the prosthetic phase. At 12 months, cumulative peri-implant marginal bone loss did not differ significantly between groups (OAOT/DEF 0.36 ± 0.79 mm vs control/HEA 0.48 ± 0.71 mm), nor did implant stability (ISQ, p = 0.731) or plaque/bleeding indices. The only significant marginal-bone factor was **abutment height**: pooled across groups, 2 mm abutments lost less bone than 1 mm abutments (p = 0.002). Bone loss in both groups occurred mainly in the first 6 months and was stable thereafter.

Clinically: in healed-ridge, platform-switched, subcrestal implants, placing the definitive abutment at surgery (OAOT, avoiding repeated healing-abutment disconnection) did **not** yield a statistically significant crestal-bone benefit over conventional repeated disconnection at 1 year — so the theoretical advantage of disconnection avoidance was not confirmed here. The DEF arm was numerically (not significantly) more favorable. The actionable lever was abutment height: prefer ≥2 mm transmucosal abutments to minimize crestal bone loss, and because OAOT showed no bone-level penalty either way, height/abutment selection can be safely deferred until tissues heal (e.g., for esthetic CAD/CAM workflows).

## Key Contributions

- One of the larger multicenter RCTs testing OAOT, uniquely in **healed-ridge, subcrestal, platform-switched** implants (vs prior mostly-crestal/immediate trials).
- Negative primary result: OAOT did not significantly reduce 12-month marginal bone loss vs repeated disconnection.
- Replicates the abutment-height effect (taller = less bone loss) independent of disconnection protocol.
- High, stable ISQ in both arms with no between-group difference.

## Methodology

- **Test (DEF / OAOT)**: definitive Permanent abutment (1 or 2 mm) placed surgery day, never removed (n = 71 patients / 103 implants).
- **Control (HEA)**: 5 mm healing abutment; healing screw removed/replaced up to 3 times (impression 12 wk, framework try-in 14 wk, prosthesis 16 wk) (n = 76 patients / 128 implants).
- Randomization by sealed envelope **after** insertion + primary-stability check; implants <60 ISQ excluded.
- Klockner VEGA ContacTi implants, internal conical connection, platform switching (0.35/0.60 mm), ~1 mm subcrestal; screw-retained definitive prosthesis at 16 weeks (25 Ncm abutment torque both groups).
- Outcomes: mesial–distal marginal bone level on standardized long-cone radiographs (baseline/2/6/12 mo, ImageJ, 2 blinded calibrated operators, ICC 0.947); ISQ (Osstell RFA); modified plaque/bleeding indices.
- Repeated-measures GLM (group × time), Bonferroni post-hoc, SPSS 26; powered at 70/group.

## Results

- **Cumulative 1-year marginal bone loss**: DEF (OAOT) 0.36 ± 0.79 mm vs HEA (control) 0.48 ± 0.71 mm — **not significant**.
- **Marginal bone level day-0 → 12 mo**: HEA −0.70 → −0.21 mm; DEF −0.79 → −0.43 mm.
- **Significant change only over time** (0–6 and 0–12 mo, p < 0.001), regardless of group; most loss in first 6 months, stable 6–12 mo.
- **Abutment height (pooled)**: 1 mm 0.45 ± 0.78 mm vs 2 mm 0.41 ± 0.70 mm cumulative loss — **significant (p < 0.002), taller = less loss**.
- **ISQ**: surgery HEA 77.18 ± 9.30 / DEF 78.36 ± 8.13; 6 mo 79.67 / 76.54; 12 mo 80.07 / 76.51 — **no significant difference (p = 0.731)**.
- **Soft tissue**: modified plaque and bleeding indices — no significant HEA-vs-DEF difference at 6 or 12 mo.
- **Survival**: 98.42% (4 implant failures, all in first 2 months); final analyzed 147 patients / 231 implants.
