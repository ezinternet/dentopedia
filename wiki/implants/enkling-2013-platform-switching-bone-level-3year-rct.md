## One-line Summary
Three-year RCT in two-piece implants comparing platform switching (PS) vs platform matching (PM): PS produced significantly less crestal bone loss, maintained through 36 months with no difference in implant survival — extending the typically 12-month PS evidence to 3 years.

## Summary
Three-year RCT testing the hypothesis that platform switching reduces peri-implant crestal bone loss compared to platform matching in two-piece dental implants. Most published data at the time were limited to 12 months; this trial extended to 36 months. Results supported platform switching as beneficial for preserving crestal bone levels.

## Key Contributions
- 3-year follow-up extending beyond the typical 12-month studies
- Randomized controlled trial design with PS vs PM groups
- Platform switching: significantly less crestal bone loss
- Multi-center university collaboration (Berne, Witten, Bonn)
- Addresses controversy around platform switching clinical evidence

## Methodology
Randomized clinical trial. Two-piece dental implants randomized to PS or PM abutment configurations. Follow-up at 1, 2, and 3 years post-loading. Radiographic bone level changes measured on standardized periapical radiographs.

## Results
- PS group: significantly less crestal bone loss over 3 years vs PM
- Benefit of platform switching maintained through 36-month follow-up
- No significant difference in implant survival between groups
- Results extend and confirm short-term PS advantages to 3 years
