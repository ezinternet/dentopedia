## One-line Summary
Systematic review (15 studies, no meta-analysis) finding that undersized, osteotome, Piezosurgery, and osseodensification drilling all raise primary stability in low-density bone, but evidence for improved long-term survival vs conventional drilling is weak.

## Summary
This systematic review consolidates the four surgical drilling strategies proposed for type IV (low-density) bone — undersized preparation, osteotome condensation, Piezosurgery, and osseodensification — and asks whether any improves implant success beyond conventional drilling. Across 15 heterogeneous studies, all techniques consistently increased primary stability (insertion torque), but long-term integration and survival were comparable to conventional protocols. The authors grade the supporting evidence as weak, cautioning against treating primary-stability gains as a proxy for long-term success.

## Key Contributions
- One comparative frame for the four low-density-bone drilling alternatives.
- Decouples primary-stability gain (well supported) from long-term survival benefit (weak evidence).
- Documents heterogeneity that blocks meta-analysis — a methodological gap signal.

## Methodology
Systematic review; MEDLINE/PubMed to April 2018; animal + clinical studies on drilling technique vs integration/survival in low-density bone. 15 included (7 experimental, 8 clinical). Narrative synthesis (no meta-analysis due to heterogeneity).

## Results
- All four protocols raise primary stability.
- Long-term outcomes comparable to conventional drilling.
- Weak overall evidence for enhanced osseointegration/survival in low-density bone.
