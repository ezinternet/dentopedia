## One-line Summary

Systematic review (6 studies, 1997–2016; 357 implants in SS patients) finding high mean implant survival (93.7–94.6% over mean ~4 y), low marginal bone loss, low biological complications, and quality-of-life improvement in all studies.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1371/journal.pone.0189507](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0189507)), this systematic review (PROSPERO CRD42016053277) pooled 6 observational studies published between 1997 and 2016 (1 case series, 1 prospective cohort, 4 retrospective cohorts; 357 implants) to assess dental implant outcomes in Sjögren's syndrome (SS) patients — a population at elevated a priori failure concern because SS-driven xerostomia, caries, and periodontal disease degrade the oral environment. Mean implant survival was 94.6 ± 5.6% over a mean follow-up of 3.97 years (a 93.7% figure also appears in the dataset). Marginal bone loss (MBL) was generally low and comparable to non-SS subjects, biological complications were predominantly low (mucositis most common, in one study up to 94% of carriers), and every study that assessed quality of life reported improvement after implant rehabilitation. No study found a difference between primary and secondary SS, and no correlation emerged between peri-implant parameters and SS duration or immunosuppressive drug use.

Clinically: dental implants are a favorable rehabilitation option for SS patients — high survival, low MBL, and improved quality of life are expected — but the ~94.6% survival over only ~4 years (vs ~95.3–96.5% over 10 years in healthy patients) hints at higher early failure risk, so SS patients warrant careful case selection, rough-surface implants, and continuous hygiene/maintenance support. Evidence is weak (no RCTs, mostly retrospective, 2/6 studies high risk of bias), so these conclusions are provisional.

## Key Contributions

- First systematic review (per authors, 2017) on implant survival/success specifically in SS patients.
- SS-pooled survival benchmark (94.6 ± 5.6% over 3.97 y) positioned against healthy-patient 10-year survival (~95.3–96.5%), flagging possible early-failure risk.
- Documents low MBL and low biological complications, with mucositis the dominant complication.
- Confirms quality-of-life improvement across all assessing studies.
- SS-specific deep-dive that reinforces the broader autoimmune-disease implant SR ([[implants/esimekara-2022-dental-implants-autoimmune-diseases-sr]]).

## Methodology

- **Design**: systematic review, descriptive synthesis (no meta-analysis); PRISMA-P / Cochrane Handbook / PRISMA / AMSTAR; PICOS framework. Registered PROSPERO CRD42016053277.
- **Databases**: PubMed/MEDLINE, Cochrane CENTRAL, Web of Science, EMBASE (to June 2017); grey literature (Grey Literature Report, OpenGrey); ClinicalTrials.gov; journal hand-search + cross-referencing. No date/language restriction.
- **Population**: adults (≥18 y) with SS rehabilitated with dental implants. **Outcomes**: survival (primary); MBL, biological complications, quality of life (secondary).
- **Eligible designs**: cohort (prospective/retrospective), case series, controlled clinical trial, RCT. Excluded animal/in-vitro studies, case reports, reviews, untreated periodontal disease, <18 y, other metabolic disease.
- **Screening**: two independent reviewers; Cohen's kappa 0.79 / 0.85.
- **Risk of bias**: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS; ≥6/9 = high quality).

## Results

- **Search → inclusion**: 151 records (115 PubMed + 3 Cochrane + 13 Web of Science + 20 EMBASE) → 11 full texts → 5 excluded (3 case reports, 1 duplicate, 1 review) → **6 studies included**.
- **Subjects**: 3–205 per study (mean 45.3); **357 implants** in primary/secondary SS.
- **Pooled survival: 94.6 ± 5.6%** over mean **3.97 years**. Per study: Payne 1997 88.4% (3 fail/26), Isidor 1999 87% (7/54), Weinländer 2010 100% (0/21), Öczakir 2015 100% (0/12), Korfage 2016 97% (4/140), Albrecht 2016 95.2% (5/104).
- **Surface**: the 2 highest-failure studies (Payne, Isidor) were the only machined-surface ones; early/late failure data missing, so surface effect not conclusive.
- **MBL** (3 studies, mean 4.2 y): Isidor 0.65 ± 0.07 mm; Korfage 0.89 ± 0.9 mm; Weinländer 3.1 ± 0.7 mm — generally low/stable, no difference vs non-SS (Misch threshold <2 mm).
- **Biological complications** (2 studies): one found higher probing depth/bleeding in SS; another more mucositis (up to 94% of carriers) and peri-implantitis vs non-SS. No primary-vs-secondary SS difference; no correlation with SS duration or immunosuppressive use.
- **Quality of life**: improved (mastication, comfort, satisfaction) in all 3 assessing studies.
- **Risk of bias (NOS)**: Korfage 8/9, Albrecht 7/9, Isidor 6/9, Weinländer 6/9 (high quality); Payne 5/9, Öczakir 5/9 (potential bias).
