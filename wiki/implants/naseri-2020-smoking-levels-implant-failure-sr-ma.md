## 한줄요약
JCP 2020 SR+MA (Naseri, 23편): 흡연 dose (cigarettes/day) 증가 → 임플란트 실패 위험 dose-dependent 증가; heavy smoker가 non-smoker 대비 유의하게 높은 위험 — 흡연 양 자체가 risk modifier.

## Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis specifically addressing whether the *amount* of smoking (cigarettes per day) affects implant failure risk. Studies that did not report smoking quantity were excluded, leaving 23 articles for analysis. Pooled RR shows a dose-dependent relationship — more cigarettes per day = higher implant failure risk.

This builds beyond binary smoker/non-smoker analyses (which obscure dose-response) and informs patient counseling: even partial smoking reduction may meaningfully lower implant failure risk.

## Key Contributions
- Quantifies the smoking dose-implant failure dose-response curve.
- Excludes studies without quantitative smoking data (methodologically stricter than prior reviews).

## Methodology
4 databases (PubMed, Embase, WoS, Scopus) through Jan 2019; required cigarettes/day reporting; fixed/random effects MA.

## Results
[근거강함] Implant failure RR rises with cigarettes/day. Heavy smokers significantly higher risk vs non-smokers.

## Limitations
- Observational base
- Smoking quantification heterogeneity
- Cannot distinguish current vs lifetime cumulative exposure
