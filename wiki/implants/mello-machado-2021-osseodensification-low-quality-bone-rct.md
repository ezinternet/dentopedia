## One-line Summary
Double-blind RCT (n=16 patients / 55 implants) in Misch D3/D4 low-quality bone: osseodensification (OD, healing-chamber preparation) vs standard undersized drilling — OD significantly raised insertion torque (39.0±6.4 vs 32.0±3.4 Ncm, p<0.001) but ISQ was equivalent at placement (67.1 vs 65.5) and 6 months (74.0 vs 73.3, p>0.05 both), with equivalent survival.

## 한줄요약
무작위대조시험 (Randomized Controlled Trial, RCT), 이중맹검, n=16 환자/55 임플란트 — Misch D3/D4 저밀도골에서 골밀도화 (Osseodensification, OD) 군이 표준 언더사이즈 드릴링 대비 삽입토크는 유의하게 높았으나(39.0±6.4 vs 32.0±3.4 Ncm, p<0.001) 임플란트 안정성 지수 (Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ)는 placement(67.1 vs 65.5)·6개월(74.0 vs 73.3) 모두 동등(p>0.05), 생존율도 동등. 환자 수가 16명에 불과해 군집효과로 정밀도 과대평가 가능.

## Summary
This double-blind RCT compared osseodensification (OD, Densah burs CCW, osteotomy wider than implant core — the "healing chamber" preparation) against standard undersized drilling in 16 patients with Misch D3 or D4 bone quality. Across 55 implants (G1 OD n = 29; G2 standard n = 26), insertion torque, ISQ at placement, ISQ at 6 months, and survival were assessed.

Insertion torque was significantly higher with OD (39.0 ± 6.4 vs 32.0 ± 3.4 Ncm, p < 0.001). ISQ values were statistically indistinguishable between groups at both timepoints (placement 67.1 vs 65.5; 6 months 74.0 vs 73.3; p > 0.05 both). Within both groups, ISQ rose significantly from placement to 6 months (p < 0.001), consistent with successful secondary stability. Survival at 6 months was equivalent. Authors conclude OD enables the healing-chamber concept in low-quality bone "without reduction in implant stability." Practically, the data support equivalence on ISQ/survival with a modest IT advantage — not a decisive superiority claim.

## Key Contributions
- First double-blind RCT comparing OD vs standard undersized drilling specifically in D3/D4 bone.
- OD raises insertion torque (~7 Ncm) but does not change ISQ at placement or 6 months.
- Both groups achieve similar successful osseointegration (significant within-group ISQ gain) and equivalent 6-month survival.
- Supports OD as a viable alternative — not a clear winner — for low-quality bone implant placement.

## Methodology
Sixteen patients with D3 or D4 bone (Misch classification) were randomized; outcome assessor and patient blinded (surgeon unblinded by necessity). G1: Densah burs in CCW, osteotomy intentionally wider than implant core (healing-chamber). G2: standard undersized drilling (final drill narrower than implant thread). Outcomes: insertion torque (Ncm) and ISQ at placement, ISQ + survival at 6 months. Comparisons by independent-sample t-test between groups; paired test within groups; significance at p < 0.05.

## Results
- Insertion torque: G1 39.0 ± 6.4 Ncm vs G2 32.0 ± 3.4 Ncm, p < 0.001.
- ISQ at placement: G1 67.1 ± 3.2 vs G2 65.5 ± 2.7, p > 0.05.
- ISQ at 6 months: G1 74.0 ± 3.6 vs G2 73.3 ± 3.2, p > 0.05.
- Within-group ISQ gain placement → 6 months: significant in both groups (p < 0.001).
- 6-month implant survival: equivalent between groups (p > 0.05).

## Limitations
- Only 16 patients despite 55 implants — multiple implants per patient introduces clustering; reported precision is optimistic.
- Short follow-up (6 months); no MBL or long-term survival.
- Surgeon unblinded by necessity; outcome bias possible at IT recording.
- "Healing chamber" gap inferred from drilling geometry, not directly measured radiographically or histologically.
- IT difference (~7 Ncm) is statistically significant but the clinically relevant ISQ — more correlated with osseointegration outcome — was identical between groups.
- Living-document note: the abstract's headline ("OD enables the healing chamber without reduction in stability") is supported by ISQ equivalence; the simultaneous IT increase is interesting but somewhat at odds with the "healing chamber = gap = lower IT" rationale and deserves cautious interpretation.
