## One-line Summary

Prospective cohort (n=80, 18 months, Iraq) demonstrating that bite force and implant stability (ISQ) are significantly positively associated and jointly modulated by time, jaw location (posterior > anterior), and sex (males > females).

## 한줄요약

전향적 코호트 (n=80, 18개월): 교합력 (Bite Force, BF)과 임플란트 안정성 지수 (ISQ)는 유의한 양의 상관관계를 보이며, 시간·해부학적 위치 (구치부 > 전치부)·성별 (남 > 여)이 두 지표 모두에 복합적으로 영향을 미친다.

## Summary

This prospective cohort study enrolled 80 patients (ages 25–40, both sexes) at Al-Ameed University, Iraq, who received 235 immediately-loaded Straumann® and Medintika® implants in anterior and posterior jaw positions. Bite force (BF) was measured with the Loadstar™ sensor and implant stability with the Osstell ISQ Mentor at three time points: immediately at crown placement, 6 months, and 18 months post-insertion. The study found that males had significantly higher posterior BF and higher posterior ISQ than females at all three visits. Two-way and three-way ANOVA confirmed that BF and ISQ values are significantly influenced by the interaction of time, implant location, and sex. Pearson correlation and mixed-effects regression (ISQ ~ BF + time + location + gender) confirmed a significant BF–ISQ association that varies dynamically as the bone–implant interface matures.

## Key Contributions

- First dedicated prospective study measuring concurrent BF and ISQ to directly quantify their association under immediate loading.
- Establishes that posterior implants achieve higher ISQ than anterior implants, consistent with higher posterior BF providing beneficial mechanical stimulation to bone.
- Documents sex-related difference: males show significantly higher posterior BF and posterior ISQ across all time points.
- Time-dependent BF–ISQ dynamics: the correlation coefficient changes as osseointegration progresses from immediate loading to 18 months.
- Mixed-effects regression model provides a framework for predicting ISQ from BF combined with patient/site variables.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Prospective cohort; STROBE guidelines; Ethics #52017 (Al-Ameed University, Iraq); n=80 from 128 screened.
- **Groups**: 4 (anterior male / posterior male / anterior female / posterior female), 20 patients each.
- **Implants**: 235 total; Straumann® and Medintika®; diameters 3.3–4.0 mm; lengths 9–11 mm; immediate loading protocol throughout.
- **BF measurement**: Loadstar™ sensor (Fremont, CA); maximum bite force (MBF) in Newtons at the first molar region; 3 trials/side, averaged; 100–400 N measurement range.
- **ISQ measurement**: Osstell ISQ Mentor (RFA); Smartpeg per implant; 3-directional readings; ISQ 0–100 scale.
- **Timepoints**: Visit 1 (crown placement day), Visit 2 (6 months), Visit 3 (18 months).
- **Statistics**: One-way repeated-measures ANOVA, two-way/three-way ANOVA, Bonferroni post-hoc, Pearson correlation, mixed-effects regression, Shapiro-Wilk normality, Greenhouse-Geisser correction for sphericity.
- **Exclusions**: Systemic bone disease, periodontitis, smoking, alcohol, parafunctional habits, delayed loading.

## Results

- Males exhibited significantly higher posterior BF than females at all three visits (p < 0.05).
- Males exhibited significantly higher posterior ISQ than females at all three visits.
- Two-way ANOVA: both BF and ISQ significantly influenced by interaction of time × location × gender (p < 0.05).
- Pearson correlation confirmed significant positive BF–ISQ relationship; regression coefficient (b) quantified the effect with 95% CIs modulated by time, location, and sex.
- Posterior implants consistently showed greater stability than anterior implants, mirroring the bite force gradient.
- BF–ISQ relationship is dynamic: changes over 18 months as the bone–implant interface matures, suggesting osseointegration quality progressively mediates the force-stability coupling.
