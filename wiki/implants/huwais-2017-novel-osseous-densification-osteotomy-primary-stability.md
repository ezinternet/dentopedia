## One-line Summary
Foundational in vitro bench study (72 porcine tibial osteotomies, 2 implant diameters): osseodensification (OD) using the same multi-fluted bur run counterclockwise significantly raised insertion/removal torque and roughly tripled bone-to-implant contact (BIC) vs standard drilling and formed a peri-osteotomy layer of increased bone mineral density (BMD), while implant stability quotient (ISQ) and temperature did not differ between groups.

## 한줄요약
시험관 벤치 연구 (in vitro), 돼지 경골 (porcine tibia) 72 골삭제 (osteotomy) — 같은 다중날 (multi-fluted) 버 (bur)를 반시계방향으로 회전시키는 골밀도화 (Osseodensification, OD)가 표준 드릴링 대비 삽입·제거 토크 (Insertion/Removal Torque)와 골-임플란트 접촉률 (Bone-to-Implant Contact, BIC, 약 3배)을 유의하게 상승시키고 골삭제 둘레에 골밀도 (Bone Mineral Density, BMD) 증가층을 형성했으나, 임플란트 안정성 지수 (Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ)와 온도 상승은 군간 차이 없음. 임상 적용 한계: 생체 외 모델로 치유반응 미반영이며 발명자 주도 연구이므로 후속 동물·임상 메타분석 (Meta-Analysis)으로 보완 필요.

## Summary
This is the foundational publication that introduced osseodensification (OD) to the implant literature. Huwais (the inventor and patent holder) and Meyer designed an elegant three-arm bench comparison in porcine tibial bone to isolate the effect of bur rotation direction from bur geometry: the same tapered multi-fluted bur was used both as an extractor (clockwise) and as a densifier (counterclockwise), with conventional standard drills as a separate cutting control. Across 72 osteotomies and two implant diameters (4.1 mm, 6.0 mm), OD significantly raised insertion and removal torque, tripled bone-to-implant contact (BIC) versus standard drilling, and produced a measurable peri-osteotomy layer of increased bone mineral density visible on SEM and micro-CT.

Two findings deserve careful clinical interpretation. First, **ISQ did not differ significantly among the three groups** even though insertion torque did — a dissociation that recurs in subsequent OD literature and reflects the fact that ISQ measures lateral stiffness while torque measures axial resistance; OD's effect appears axial-dominant in this model. Second, **temperatures did not rise with OD**, contradicting the intuition that compaction would generate more heat than cutting. The bur's tapered geometry allows lift-away for irrigation, which likely contributes.

This paper is the mechanistic anchor for every downstream OD study — animal histomorphometry (Gaikwad 2022, Lima Monteiro 2024), human ISQ (Gaspar 2022, Kalra 2025), and clinical sinus lift (Huwais 2018). It should be read with awareness that the 3× BIC ratio is from immediate post-insertion bench measurement, not from biologically integrated implants, and the inventor's involvement creates inherent conflict-of-interest. Subsequent independent SR+MAs have replicated direction and significance but not always the magnitude.

## Key Contributions
- Introduces OD as a counterclockwise compaction-drilling paradigm with the same tapered multi-fluted bur used clockwise for extraction.
- Significantly higher insertion and removal torque with OD vs both standard and extraction drilling.
- ~3× increase in BIC with OD vs standard drilling (immediate post-insertion bench measurement).
- Discrete peri-osteotomy layer of increased BMD visible on SEM and micro-CT.
- Spring-back / elastic-strain effect: OD osteotomy diameter < bur diameter, producing interference fit.
- **ISQ unchanged** across groups — torque–ISQ dissociation flagged early.
- **Temperature unchanged** — counters the intuition that compaction generates more heat than cutting.

## Methodology
Seventy-two osteotomies in porcine tibial plateau bone, randomised across three preparation techniques: (1) standard cutting drilling, (2) osseous extraction drilling with the tapered multi-fluted bur clockwise, (3) osseous densification with the same bur counterclockwise. Two implant diameters (4.1 mm, 6.0 mm). Outcome measures: process variables (temperature, force, torque during drilling), mechanical stability (insertion torque, removal torque, ISQ), and bone imaging (SEM of walls, micro-CT for peri-osteotomy BMD, histomorphometry for %BIC). Bench setting permits direct mechanical/imaging measurement but excludes biological healing.

## Results
- Insertion torque: significantly higher with OD vs standard drilling and extraction drilling.
- Removal torque: significantly higher with OD vs both controls.
- BIC: ~3× higher with OD vs standard drilling.
- Peri-osteotomy BMD: discrete layer of increased density on SEM/micro-CT in OD.
- Osteotomy diameter: OD < extraction < standard (spring-back).
- ISQ: no significant difference among groups.
- Temperature: no significant difference among groups.
- Exact mean ± SD and p-values are in the paywalled body of the article.
