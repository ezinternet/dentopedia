## Summary
This open-access systematic review and meta-analysis (Marzorati et al., Int J Oral Maxillofac Surg 2026) is currently the cleanest evidence on counterclockwise OD drilling vs conventional subtractive osteotomy in human clinical implant placement. It deliberately excludes Summers osteotomes and magnetic-mallet densification, which prior SRs (Gaspar 2021, Yu 2022, Gaikwad 2022, Lima Monteiro 2024, Kalra 2025) had bundled together — addressing a long-standing critique that the OD evidence base had been muddied by heterogeneous "densification" comparators.

The pooled analysis covers 555 patients and 685 implants (343 OD / 342 standard) across in vivo human studies published up to September 2025. OD produced a mean insertion torque of 45.75 ± 6.55 N·cm vs 38.00 ± 7.01 N·cm for standard osteotomy (P < 0.001). The ISQ random-effects mean difference was 3.24 (95% CI 0.72–5.95, P = 0.024) — statistically significant but modest. Longitudinal ISQ data were too sparse to support trend analysis, so we still don't know whether OD's primary-stability advantage carries through the 2–4 week stability dip.

The clinical takeaway: in healthy adults, OD reliably increases insertion torque by ~7–8 N·cm and immediate-post-op ISQ by ~3 units. Whether this translates to fewer failures, less MBL, or earlier loading remains unestablished by this SR — the meta-analysis was scoped to biomechanical surrogates, not survival/MBL/clinical outcomes.

## Key Contributions
- Tightest inclusion criteria to date — OD-only vs subtractive drills, in vivo human trials only.
- Pooled IT: OD 45.75 ± 6.55 N·cm vs 38.00 ± 7.01 N·cm; P < 0.001.
- Pooled ISQ: MD 3.24 (95% CI 0.72–5.95), P = 0.024 — random-effects model.
- Maps the prior SR landscape and notes the contamination of earlier reviews with non-OD densification methods.
- Open access — usable as a reference standard for clinic-internal decision documents.
- Explicitly flags the longitudinal ISQ gap — invites future RCTs to track ISQ through the dip period and beyond loading.

## Methodology
PRISMA 2020 with English-language search to 11 September 2025 across the standard databases (PubMed, Scopus, etc.). Inclusion: in vivo human clinical trials, healthy adults, single/multiple implants in partially/fully edentulous segments, comparing counterclockwise OD vs subtractive drilling. Outcomes: insertion torque (N·cm), ISQ at placement. Quality assessed with RoB 2, Newcastle–Ottawa, and GRADE. ISQ synthesized with random-effects meta-analysis.

The methodological discipline of excluding Summers osteotome and magnetic-mallet studies is its main improvement over earlier reviews. As a result the effect sizes are *smaller* than Yu 2022 (which pooled OD with osteotome and under-drilling, finding ISQ MD 10.25); the smaller, more precise estimate here is likely closer to the true OD-only effect.

## Results
- Insertion torque: OD 45.75 ± 6.55 N·cm | standard 38.00 ± 7.01 N·cm (P < 0.001, ~7.75 N·cm difference).
- ISQ: random-effects mean difference 3.24 (95% CI 0.72–5.95, P = 0.024).
- Total: 555 patients, 685 implants (343 OD / 342 standard); 59.2% female where sex reported.
- Longitudinal ISQ pooling was not feasible due to insufficient comparable data.
- Heterogeneity (I²) and individual-study weights are presented in the full paper but not retrievable from the available extract.

## Critical Reading / Living-document Notes
- The ISQ 95% CI lower bound (0.72) is uncomfortably close to zero — a single excluded outlier or a different subgroup definition could move the conclusion. This is a *statistically* significant but *clinically* small effect.
- Insertion torque difference of ~7.75 N·cm pushes the OD group from the "borderline acceptable" (≥30 N·cm) range further into the "comfortable for immediate provisionalization" range (≥45 N·cm) — *that* is where the clinical relevance lives, more than the ISQ delta itself.
- The exclusion of Summers osteotome from the comparator pool is a real methodological strength but means this SR cannot answer: "Is OD better than the technique I currently use if I currently use osteotome?" — that question still rests on lower-grade direct comparisons (e.g., Slete 2018, Tian 2019).
- No survival, MBL, or soft-tissue endpoints — clinicians should not infer that the IT/ISQ gain translates 1:1 to outcomes that matter to patients.
- Industry-linked authorship in many included trials is a recurring confounder across the OD literature. This SR's authors are not (apparently) industry-linked, which is a positive.
- For Korean clinical decision-making: this SR justifies OD as a reasonable choice in soft (D3/D4) bone and immediate-load candidates, but does NOT yet justify routine replacement of conventional drilling in normal-density bone.
