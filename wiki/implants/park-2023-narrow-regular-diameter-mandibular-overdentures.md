## One-line Summary

SR+MA (12 publications, 8 independent studies) finding narrow-diameter implants for mandibular overdentures match regular-diameter implants on survival and MBL while delivering significantly better patient satisfaction and oral health-related quality of life.

## 한줄요약

12편 논문(독립 연구 8편) 체계적 고찰·메타분석. 하악 임플란트 피개의치에서 NDI는 RDI와 생존율·MBL이 동등했고 환자 만족도(VAS)·구강건강 삶의질(OHRQoL)은 유의하게 더 좋았다.

## Summary

For mandibular implant overdentures, this SR+MA (Korean group) found narrow-diameter implants equivalent to regular-diameter implants on implant survival and marginal bone loss, while outperforming them on patient-reported outcomes — VAS satisfaction and OHIP-measured quality of life were significantly better with NDIs. Because mandibular overdentures are frequently placed in elderly or medically compromised patients with atrophic ridges, the ability to avoid bone augmentation while improving PROMs makes NDIs an attractive first-line option here. The evidence base is modest (8 independent studies) and the NDI/mini-implant boundary is somewhat blurred across included trials.

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies a PROM advantage (satisfaction, OHRQoL) for NDIs in mandibular overdentures.
- Supports NDIs as a graft-avoidance option in atrophic edentulous mandibles.
- Equivalent survival/MBL + superior PROMs strengthens the minimally invasive argument.

## Methodology

Cochrane-based SR+MA. Medline, Embase, CENTRAL, Web of Science, Scopus (to July 22, 2022). 782 non-duplicate records + 83 registrations → 26 full-text → 12 publications / 8 independent studies. Meta-analysis: ISR, MBL, VAS satisfaction, OHIP.

## Results

- Implant survival rate: no significant difference (NDI vs RDI).
- Marginal bone loss: no significant difference.
- Patient satisfaction (VAS) and OHRQoL (OHIP): NDI significantly better than RDI.
