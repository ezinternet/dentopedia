## One-line Summary
SR+MA (9 studies, 6 in meta-analysis, 2013-2023): porcine xenogeneic collagen matrices (XCM) vs autogenous grafts (CTG/FGG) for peri-implant soft tissue augmentation without bone grafts; autografts superior for KMW and STT (pooled KMW MD -0.96 mm), aesthetics comparable.

## 한줄요약
bone graft 없는 임플란트 연조직 증대에서 XCM은 자가이식보다 KMW·STT 열위(KMW 통합 MD -0.96 mm, p<0.05), 심미는 동등하고 XCM은 술시간·통증·진통제에서 우위.

## Summary
This systematic review and meta-analysis compared porcine XCM with autogenous soft tissue grafts (CTG/FGG) for increasing keratinized mucosa width (KMW), soft tissue thickness (STT), and soft tissue volume (STV) around dental implants, restricted to cases without concurrent bone grafting. Nine studies (8 RCTs, 1 CCT) met inclusion; six entered meta-analysis. Pooled estimates consistently favored autogenous grafts for both KMW and STT, while aesthetic outcomes were comparable and XCM offered lower morbidity, shorter surgical time, and reduced analgesic use. The autograft type modulated the effect: CTG achieved KMW parity with XCM (Cairo), whereas FGG outperformed XCM for KMW (Huang, Ramanauskaite). Evidence is limited by Mucograft predominance and mostly short follow-up.

## Key Contributions
- First SR+MA isolating soft tissue augmentation without bone grafts when comparing XCM to autogenous grafts (authors' claim).
- Quantifies autograft superiority separately for KMW (6 and 12 months) and STT (6 months).
- Demonstrates that the choice of autograft (CTG vs FGG) changes the comparison outcome versus XCM.

## Methodology
SR+MA (PRISMA; PROSPERO CRD42023455643). PubMed, Scopus, Cochrane CENTRAL + manual search, 2013-2023. Inclusion: human RCT/CCT, follow-up > 6 months, > 10 cases, porcine XCM vs autogenous graft, no bone graft, English. 9 studies included, 6 in meta-analysis. Risk of bias: Cochrane RoB (7 low, 1 high) and ROBINS-I (1 moderate). Random- or fixed-effects models selected by I2; sensitivity analysis excluded the high-RoB study.

## Results
- KMW 6mo: pooled MD -0.96 (95% CI -1.71 to -0.21), random-effects, I2 82%, favoring autograft (p<0.05).
- KMW 6mo sensitivity (excluding high-RoB Ramanauskaite): MD -0.61 (-1.11 to -0.10), I2 51% (p<0.05).
- STT 6mo: MD -0.35 (-0.51 to -0.19), fixed-effects, I2 0% (p<0.05), favoring autograft.
- KMW 12mo: MD -1.16 (-1.78 to -0.54), fixed-effects, I2 0% (p<0.05).
- Aesthetics comparable; XCM lower morbidity, analgesic need, and surgical time. CMX/XCM resorption (shrinkage) is a recurring caveat.
