## One-line Summary

SR+MA (19 studies, n=670 patients, 1,494 axial + 1,338 tilted implants, ≥1yr): crestal bone loss around tilted vs axial implants NOT significantly different at 1yr (P=.09) or ≥3yr (P=.30); tilted implants are a predictable technique.

## Summary

This foundational SR+MA (Del Fabbro & Ceresoli, *EJOI* 2014 Suppl) compared crestal bone level change around axial vs tilted implants supporting fixed prosthetic reconstructions in edentulous jaws, with at least 1 year of function.

**Population**: 670 patients, 716 prostheses (415 maxillary, 301 mandibular), 1,494 axial + 1,338 tilted implants across 19 studies.

**Key findings:**
- Crestal bone loss at 1yr: axial 0.43–1.13 mm; tilted 0.34–1.14 mm → **NS (P=.09)**
- Crestal bone loss at ≥3yr: trend toward lower loss in axial, still **NS (P=.30)**
- 46 implants failed within first year (18 axial, 28 tilted); most in maxilla
- Maxillary survival (97.4%) significantly lower than mandibular (99.6%)
- Location, loading mode, prosthesis type, study design → no significant effect on MBL
- No prosthesis failures reported

**Clinical significance**: Established that tilted implant placement is a predictable technique with excellent short-to-mid-term prognosis, forming the evidence base for the All-on-4 concept.

**Note**: The 2022 update (Del Fabbro et al., IJOMI) with longer follow-up (≥3yr) found statistically significant lower MBL for axial vs tilted — this 2014 NS result reflects shorter follow-up and smaller sample.

## Key Contributions

- First meta-analysis on MBL: axial vs tilted implants (2014 baseline)
- Identifies maxilla as higher-risk arch for tilted implant failure
- Establishes evidence base for All-on-4 and full-arch tilted concepts

## Methodology

- Electronic + hand search through January 2014
- 758 articles → 62 eligible → 19 included (14 prospective, 5 retrospective)
- Outcomes: marginal bone loss, implant survival
- Meta-analysis comparing crestal bone level change
