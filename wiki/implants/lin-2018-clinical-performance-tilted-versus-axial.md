## One-line Summary

SR (42 studies, edentulous jaws): intentionally tilted implants show NO significant difference versus axial implants in survival, marginal bone loss, prosthesis survival, or patient-reported outcomes.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

This systematic review (Lin & Eckert, *Clin Oral Implants Res* 2018 Suppl) evaluated the clinical performance of intentionally tilted dental implants compared to axially positioned implants in edentulous patients.

**Search**: 5 electronic databases, PICO-based strategy, 811 articles identified → 42 included (2 level I, 20 level II evidence).

**Key finding**: Descriptive analysis of the included data revealed **no differences** in:
- Implant survival rate
- Marginal bone loss
- Prosthesis survival
- Patient-reported outcome measures (PROMs)

**Important caveat**: Only ONE level II study directly compared tilted vs axial implants head-to-head. The evidence base is largely indirect, with heterogeneity preventing meta-analysis.

**Tilting direction**: All tilted implants were inclined toward the **distal aspect** of edentulous jaws — not buccolingual or random inclination.

**Clinical implication**: Intentional distal tilting of posterior implants is a clinically acceptable alternative to axial placement, allowing avoidance of anatomical structures (maxillary sinus, mental foramen) without compromising outcomes.

## Key Contributions

- Landmark SR establishing the equivalence of tilted vs axial implants
- Codifies the evidence basis for the All-on-4 and similar full-arch concepts
- Identifies gap: need for direct head-to-head RCTs

## Methodology

- PICO question + 5-database search
- Oxford Centre for Evidence-based Medicine levels (March 2009)
- Data extracted only from level I and II studies
- 811 retrieved → 46 full-text review → 42 included

## Results

| Outcome | Tilted vs Axial |
|---|---|
| Implant survival | No significant difference |
| Marginal bone loss | No significant difference |
| Prosthesis survival | No significant difference |
| PROMs | No significant difference |

Heterogeneity of included studies → descriptive analysis only (no meta-analysis possible).
