## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 10 clinical studies on implant-supported overdentures (IODs) retained by CAD-CAM milled bars: ~100% implant survival across titanium/PEEK/BioHPP/zirconia bars; milled titanium bars had the fewest complications (pooled bar-level 7.4%, biological 2.8%, MBL 0.324 mm) and best OHIP/satisfaction, while polymer (PEEK/BioHPP) bars carried a peri-implantitis signal.

## Summary

This systematic review and meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD42021284190; PRISMA, Cochrane Handbook v6.2) evaluated the clinical performance of implant-supported overdentures (IODs) retained by **CAD-CAM milled bars**, separating outcomes by bar material — milled titanium, PEEK, BioHPP, and zirconia. From 127 references, 10 clinical studies were included (1 RCT, 1 pseudorandomized, 6 prospective, 2 retrospective), with follow-up from 3 months to 4 years.

The bottom line: CAD-CAM **milled titanium bars** show optimal daily clinical performance — universal implant survival, minimal mechanical and biological complications, and significantly improved oral health-related quality of life. Milled titanium bars were statistically indistinguishable from conventional soldered gold bars on complications but avoid the soldering-related fracture mode. **PEEK, BioHPP, and zirconia** bars are promising short-term but under-evidenced, and the **polymer bars (PEEK/BioHPP) carried a peri-implantitis signal** that titanium and zirconia did not.

## Key Contributions

- First SR+MA focused on CAD-CAM **milled bars** (not attachments) for IODs, stratified by bar material.
- Pooled event rates for milled titanium: bar-level complications **7.4%**, biological complications **2.8%**, MBL **0.324 mm**.
- Head-to-head meta-analysis: milled titanium vs soldered gold bars, **RR 0.638 (95% CI 0.108–3.755; p=0.619)** — no significant difference.
- Identifies a **material-dependent biological risk**: peri-implantitis with PEEK/BioHPP bars, none with titanium/zirconia.

## Methodology

- **Design**: SR+MA, random-effects models, Comprehensive Meta-Analysis v3.0; heterogeneity by Q and I²; significance p<0.05.
- **PICO**: edentulous IOD patients; CAD-CAM printed/milled bars; conventional-method IODs / CAD-CAM prostheses with baseline; outcomes = survival, MBL, biological/prosthodontic complications, OHIP, plaque/bleeding, satisfaction.
- **Search**: MEDLINE, PubMed, Cochrane Library, SciELO, Web of Science to May 2023; selection kappa k=1.0.
- **Quality/bias**: NOS (scores 6–9), ROBINS-I, RoB tool, GRADE (overall very low to moderate; technical complications and MBL rated low).
- **Flow**: 127 → 91 de-duplicated → 13 full-text → **10 included**.

## Results

**Implant survival**: nine studies reported **100% implant survival** across all bar materials (titanium, PEEK, BioHPP, zirconia), follow-up up to 4 years.

**Mechanical / prosthetic complications (milled titanium)**
- vs conventional gold bar (113 vs 128): 9 vs 13 complications; **RR 0.638 (95% CI 0.108–3.755; p=0.619)**; Q=1.804 (p=0.179), I²=44.6 — no significant difference.
- Pooled bar-level complication event rate: 4 studies, 171 IODs, 11 events; range 4.2–12.6%; **pooled 7.4% (95% CI 4.2–12.6)**; I²=0.0.
- Maxillary IODs had fewer complications than mandibular; CAD-CAM bars avoided the soldered-extension fracture mode.

**Biological complications**
- Pooled rate (milled titanium): 3 studies, 159 IODs, 4 events; range 1.1–6.9%; **pooled 2.8% (95% CI 1.1–6.9)**; I²=0.0.
- **Peri-implantitis reported with PEEK and BioHPP bars** (polymer), **none with titanium or zirconia** — possibly from bar-design space limiting hygiene and greater microbial accumulation on polymer surfaces.

**Marginal bone loss (MBL)**
- 4 studies, **324 implants** (Ti or zirconia bars), follow-up 12–24 months; **pooled 0.324 mm (95% CI 0.061–0.587)**, but **very high heterogeneity (Q=120.3, p=0.0, I²=97.5)** — interpret cautiously.
- Zirconia ≈ titanium ≈ conventional; bone loss independent of bar metal. Per-study: Pozzi 2016 Ti 0.64±0.21 mm; Toia 2019 Ti 0.27±0.35 mm; Zuercher 2022 zirconia 0.20±0.67 mm; Srinivasan 2020 Ti+gold clip 0.137±0.671 mm.

**Hygiene, OHIP, satisfaction**
- Plaque/bleeding (5 studies): four favored milled titanium; one (Cordaro) favored Locator (smaller, easier cleaning); zirconia showed no plaque/bleeding in Zuercher.
- OHIP (4 studies): all significant OHRQoL improvement with milled titanium; improvement seen as early as week 2; implant-fixed prosthesis scored even lower (better) in Katsoulis 2011.
- Satisfaction (5 studies): high patient and prosthodontist satisfaction with milled titanium; zirconia favored over Co-Cr for appearance/comfort/hygiene; Locator favored for hygiene ease.

**Conclusion**: milled **titanium** CAD-CAM bars are the best-evidenced choice (maxilla and mandible) up to 4 years; PEEK, BioHPP, and zirconia are promising but need long-term RCTs, with polymer bars warranting peri-implantitis vigilance.
