## Summary
This 2023 systematic review and meta-analysis is the largest synthesis of zirconia dental implant outcomes, pooling 25 clinical studies (4017 implants). The 10-year cumulative survival rate was 95.1%, with most failures (and all fractures, concentrated in narrow-diameter implants) occurring early. Survival was significantly lower for two-piece designs, implants whose coronal part was prepared by drilling, and discontinued products — practical signals that material and design execution, not zirconia per se, drive failure. Marginal bone loss was modest and increased only ~0.005 mm/month. It updates and extends Pieralli 2017, which was limited to ≤60-month follow-up.

## Key Contributions
- Largest, most recent zirconia implant survival/MBL synthesis with 10-year CSR.
- Pinpoints modifiable risk factors: two-piece design, coronal drilling, narrow diameter, discontinued systems.

## Methodology
Systematic review + meta-analysis, 3 databases (search to June 2023) + hand search. CSR estimation, MBL meta-analysis by follow-up window, meta-regression MBL vs time. PROSPERO CRD42022342055.

## Results
- 172 failures (mean 12.0 ± 16.1 mo); 47 early, 26 fractures (mostly narrow-diameter).
- 10-year CSR 95.1%.
- Lower survival: drill-prepared coronal (p<0.001), two-piece vs one-piece (p=0.017), discontinued products (p<0.001). Maxilla vs mandible NS.
- MBL 0.632–2.060 mm to 132 mo; +0.005 mm/month.
