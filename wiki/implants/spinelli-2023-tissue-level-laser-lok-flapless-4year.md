## One-line Summary

Prospective 4-year cohort (41 tissue-level Laser-Lok implants, flapless, n=36 patients) showed 100% survival, mean MBL −1.10 ± 0.89 mm at 48 months, stable BoP and plaque scores, and no peri-implantitis; implant diameter (4.6 > 3.8 mm) was the only significant predictor of crestal bone preservation.

## Summary

Spinelli et al. (2023) performed a 4-year prospective single-center cohort study at the University of Bologna Dental School evaluating tapered tissue-level implants with a Laser-Lok (laser-microtextured, 8 µm cell-sized microchannels) collar placed via a flapless (transmucosal) technique. Forty-one implants were placed in 36 consecutive patients (mean age 60 ± 9 years), enrolled September 2017–November 2018, with a minimum 48-month follow-up. Three placement timing protocols were used: immediate (fresh socket, n=12), early (8–12 weeks, n=12), and delayed (≥10–12 months, n=17). All patients received metal-ceramic crowns cemented on customized abutments at 4 months post-insertion. Periapical radiographs assessed MBL at 1, 3, 6, 12, 36, and 48 months.

The survival rate was 100% with zero peri-implantitis, mucositis, bone necrosis, or prosthetic complications. Total MBL at 48 months was −1.10 ± 0.89 mm, with the bulk of bone loss occurring between T12 and T36 (loading phase). Multilevel regression analysis identified implant diameter as the sole significant predictor of MBL at T48 (p = 0.027): 4.6 mm implants lost −0.64 mm vs −1.43 mm for 3.8 mm implants. Early timing placement showed the most favorable MBL (−0.63 mm at T48). Soft-tissue parameters (BoP ≈ 4.4%, PS ≈ 8%) were stable throughout 4 years with no sign of inflammation.

## Key Contributions

- First 4-year prospective dataset on tissue-level Laser-Lok implants placed with a strictly flapless approach
- 100% implant survival and zero peri-implant disease events over 48 months in a mixed immediate/early/delayed cohort
- Quantifies MBL trajectory: gradual loss through year 1 (−0.53 mm), then a second phase between T12–T36 (+0.53 mm additional), stabilizing by T48
- Implant diameter (4.6 mm vs 3.8 mm) is the only statistically significant multivariable predictor of MBL (p = 0.027)
- Early placement timing (2–3 months post-extraction) most protective against bone loss, but significant only from T36 onward
- Pre-loading gingival thickness (thick phenotype) conferred early MBL protection that disappeared by T48
- Laser-Lok connective tissue attachment hypothesis supported clinically: no crestal bone resorption beyond expected remodeling, despite flapless periosteal disruption being absent

## Methodology

- **Design**: Prospective single-center cohort; STROBE-reported
- **n**: 41 implants in 36 patients (16 M / 20 F; mean age 60 ± 9 y)
- **Implant**: Tapered Tissue-Level Laser-Lok (Biohorizons); diameters 3.8 mm (n=14) and 4.6 mm (n=27); 2.0 mm transmucosal collar (1.8 mm Laser-Lok + 0.2 mm smooth)
- **Surgery**: Flapless for early and delayed; atraumatic flapless extraction for immediate; 1.2 mm pilot drill + graduated drills at 225 rpm; no surgical guide
- **Prosthetics**: Metal-ceramic crowns, cemented (polycarboxylate) on customized abutments, loaded at 4 months
- **Radiography**: Periapical, paralleling technique, analog film, 968 dpi scan at ×20; calibrated single-blind operator
- **MBL**: Distance from implant shoulder to crestal bone-to-implant contact (mesial + distal, 0.1 mm increments)
- **Soft tissue**: PS and BoP at T3, T6, T12, T48 (4-site dichotomous); gingival thickness at surgery (K-file #20, thin ≤2 mm / thick >2 mm)
- **Statistics**: Stata 17.1; GEE linear regression; multilevel mixed-effects logistic regression for T48 predictors

## Results

### MBL Over Time (Total Cohort)
| Time | MBL (mm, mean ± SD) |
|---|---|
| T1 | −0.15 ± 0.18 |
| T3 | −0.29 ± 0.29 |
| T6 | −0.45 ± 0.37 |
| T12 | −0.53 ± 0.45 |
| T36 | −1.06 ± 1.13 |
| T48 | **−1.10 ± 0.89** |

### MBL by Subgroup at T48
| Variable | Subgroup | MBL (mm) |
|---|---|---|
| Diameter | 3.8 mm | −1.43 ± 1.01 * |
| Diameter | 4.6 mm | −0.64 ± 1.12 * |
| Timing | Immediate | −1.01 ± 1.01 |
| Timing | Early | −0.63 ± 0.89 * |
| Timing | Delayed | −1.45 ± 1.10 |
| Gingival | Thin | −1.27 ± 1.11 |
| Gingival | Thick | −1.01 ± 1.02 (NS at T48) |

\* p < 0.05 versus comparator within variable.

### Survival and Disease
- Survival rate: **100%** (41/41)
- Peri-implantitis: **0 cases**
- Mucositis: **0 cases**
- Dropouts: **0**

### Soft-Tissue Health at T48
- BoP mean: 4.42% (range 0–6.5%); stable from T3 baseline
- PS mean: ~8.1% at T3, similar at T48; oral site uniformly 0% at all timepoints
- Mesial site most affected by both BoP and PS throughout follow-up

### Multivariable Predictor of T48 MBL
Only implant diameter was significant (coeff −0.786, p = 0.027); gender, location, timing, and gingival thickness were all NS.
