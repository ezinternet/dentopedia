## One-line Summary

SR+MA (8 studies qualitative, 7 quantitative; 256 Ti-Zr NDIs) showing titanium-zirconium narrow-diameter implants for single crowns achieve 97.5% survival and 97.2% success at up to 36 months, with no difference from commercially pure titanium.

## Summary

The mechanical-strength concern is the classic objection to narrow-diameter implants. This SR+MA tests whether the titanium-zirconium (Ti-Zr / Roxolid) alloy resolves it in the single-crown scenario. Across 7 pooled studies (256 Ti-Zr NDIs), cumulative survival (97.5%) and success (97.2%) at up to 36 months were equivalent to commercially pure titanium, and 1-year marginal bone loss (0.44 mm) did not differ. The result supports Ti-Zr NDIs as a sound single-crown option where bone width is limited, while the authors stress that maximum follow-up of 36 months and only 7 quantitative studies leave long-term performance unproven.

## Key Contributions

- First synthesis crossing Ti-Zr alloy × NDI × single-crown specifically.
- Provides direct evidence that Ti-Zr NDIs match cpTi on survival, success, and MBL.
- Isolates single crowns, removing prosthesis-type confounding.

## Methodology

PRISMA SR+MA (PROSPERO CRD42022324205). PubMed/MEDLINE, Scopus, Embase, Cochrane (to April 2022). Ti-Zr NDI 3–3.5 mm, ≥10 patients, ≥12-month follow-up, single crowns. 779 results → 8 qualitative, 7 quantitative (256 Ti-Zr NDIs).

## Results

- Cumulative survival 97.5% (95% CI 94.5–98.9), success 97.2% (95% CI 94.2–98.7) up to 36 months.
- vs cpTi: no difference in survival/success.
- MBL at 1 year 0.44 (0.04) mm (95% CI 0.36–0.52); MD vs cpTi 0.02 mm (95% CI −0.23 to 0.10).
