## Summary
Short communication and narrative review of the platform switching concept in implant dentistry. Covers the mechanism by which using a smaller-diameter abutment on a larger-diameter implant collar displaces the microgap away from the crestal bone, reduces stress concentration, and preserves marginal bone. Provides conceptual overview without original clinical data.

## Key Contributions
- Accessible overview of platform switching mechanism and rationale
- Explains microgap displacement as the primary bone preservation mechanism
- Reviews circumferential bone loss reduction through biomechanical stress redistribution
- Summarizes early clinical evidence supporting platform switching
- Concise reference for understanding PS concept basis

## Methodology
Narrative short communication review. Literature overview of platform switching mechanism and clinical evidence available up to 2013.

## Results
- Platform switching: use of smaller-diameter abutment on larger implant collar
- Mechanism: displaces microgap away from crestal bone
- Effect: reduces circumferential bone resorption
- Clinical evidence to 2013: consistently supports PS for bone preservation
- Simple, effective technique for long-term peri-implant bone maintenance
