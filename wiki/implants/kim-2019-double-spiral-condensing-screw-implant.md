## One-line Summary

Korean patent application (KR 10-2021-0014513 A; applicant HaeNaem Co., inventor Kim Seong-ju) for a dental condensing screw with a double-spiral structure (compression thread + bone-powder guide grooves + lower compression dome) that expands the osteotomy and densifies bone both laterally and apically in one pass, enabling transcrestal sinus elevation — the engineering basis of the HaeNaem bur.

## 한줄요약

(주)해냄 특허출원(공개 10-2021-0014513, 발명자 김성주): 이중 스파이럴(압착나사산부 + 본파우더안내홈 + 하부압착돔) 콘덴싱 스크류. 골삭제·측방 골치밀화를 동시에 하면서 분쇄골을 하부압착돔으로 보내 근단(내측)까지 치밀화 → 종래 버의 측방-편중 한계를 보완하고 단일 기구로 상악동 거상까지 수행. 해냄버의 설계 근거 문서.

## Summary

This is the **(주)해냄 (HaeNaem Co.) patent** behind the HaeNaem 골밀도화 (Osseodensification, OD) bur. The only prospective clinical study of this bur, [[sinus-lift/transcrestal/changrani-2024-haenaem-zero-bone-loss-indirect-sinus-lift]], was **subsequently RETRACTED — do not cite it as clinical evidence.** This patent itself is a disclosure, not a clinical study — it documents *how the instrument is built and why*, not patient outcomes, and remains valid as an engineering reference independent of the retracted clinical paper.

The core problem it addresses: a conventional OD bur forms its thread **opposite to the rotation direction**, so on rotation the released bone powder cannot escape and is pressed against the osteotomy wall — densifying without cutting bone away (the OD principle). But this gives strong *lateral* densification (side-wall zone thickness d1) and weak *apical/inner* densification (floor zone d2 ≪ d1), which is insufficient for transcrestal sinus elevation; it also applies continuous lateral pressure that hurts the patient.

The invention's solution is a **double-spiral geometry**: a compression thread (압착나사산부, 111) — whose peaks carry both a densification face (치밀화면, surface contact) and a cutting edge (절삭날, line contact) — interleaved with bone-powder guide grooves (본파우더안내홈, 115). The grooves channel detached bone powder and irrigation water down to a convex **lower compression dome (하부압착돔, 117)** at the tip, which densifies the osteotomy floor/apical region. This lets a single instrument (1) expand the osteotomy, (2) densify both wall and floor, and (3) elevate the Schneiderian membrane under a densified bone column — collapsing the conventional pilot → sequential drills → separate densification → final drill → implant sequence into initial drill → condensing screw → implant. The patent claims reduced patient pain by dispersing lateral pressure through the powder-channeling design.

**Direction-mechanism note (ties to prior conversation):** the patent describes thread geometry tuned to a single working rotation direction (thread formed opposite to that direction = compaction). It does NOT describe a reversible dual-direction tool the way the Densah/Versah system does — where the *same* bur cuts clockwise (extraction) and densifies counterclockwise ([[implants/huwais-2017-novel-osseous-densification-osteotomy-primary-stability]]). So the HaeNaem device is an alternative engineering route to OD, not a mirror of Densah's reversible-direction concept.

## Key Contributions

- **Double-spiral structure**: compression thread + overlapping bone-powder guide grooves, both opposite to rotation direction.
- **Apical densification via lower compression dome** — the stated advance over prior OD burs that densify mainly the side wall.
- **Single-pass osteotomy expansion + densification + sinus elevation**, shortening the implant workflow.
- **Membrane-sparing transcrestal sinus lift** by hydraulic elevation under a densified bone column.
- Documents the design lineage of the HaeNaem ("Zero Bone Loss") bur system.

## Results

No clinical or bench measurements (patent document). Asserted effects only: greater inner/apical densification (raising d2 toward d1); shortened workflow; reduced patient pain via lateral-pressure dispersion; membrane-sparing sinus elevation. None quantified or independently validated.
