## 한줄요약
MA (21 RCTs, 2,576임플란트): 초단임플란트(≤6mm) 1–3년 생존 동등; 골증대 없이 생물학적 합병증 68% 감소

## Summary
Meta-analysis of 21 RCTs (2576 implants, 1387 patients) comparing extra-short implants (≤6 mm) vs. longer implants (≥8 mm). **Survival is equivalent at 1–3 years.** At 5 years, longer implants are better in native bone — but equal when bone augmentation was performed. Extra-short implants have significantly **less MBL and 68% fewer biological complications**.

## Key Contributions
- Survival equivalent at 1 yr (RR 1.002) and 3 yr (RR 0.996)
- 5-year advantage of longer implants **disappears when bone augmentation is used**
- MBL from placement: extra-short 0.22 mm less (p < 0.01)
- Biological complications: RR = 0.321 — extra-short implants have 68% lower rate
- Prosthetic complications: no difference

## Survival Results by Follow-up

| Follow-up | RR | p | Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 year | 1.002 (0.981–1.024) | 0.856 | Equal |
| 3 years | 0.996 (0.968–1.025) | 0.772 | Equal |
| 5 years (all) | 0.970 (0.944–0.997) | <0.05 | Longer better |
| 5 years + BA | 0.977 (0.945–1.010) | 0.171 | **Equal** |
| 5 years, native | 0.955 (0.912–0.999) | <0.05 | Longer better |

## Clinical Takeaways
- Extra-short implants are safe and predictable for **1–3 year horizon**
- At 5+ years in **native bone**: longer implants perform better — consider this in younger patients
- At 5+ years with **bone augmentation**: equivalent — BA compensates for implant length
- Low biological complication rate makes extra-short implants favorable for high-risk patients
- **Smoking and loading method**: did not significantly affect outcomes in meta-regression
