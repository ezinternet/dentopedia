## 한줄요약
3D 유한요소분석(8개 모델): 나사형·원통형 임플란트 모두 치조정 부위에 최대 응력 집중; 원통형이 D3·D4 저밀도 골에서 응력분산 유리.

## Summary
Three-dimensional finite element analysis comparing stress distribution around threaded versus cylindrical implants in four Misch bone density types (D1–D4). Maximum Von Mises stress consistently located at the crestal/neck region in all models. Threaded implants generate higher stress than cylindrical in all bone densities, with cylindrical design showing relatively more favourable distribution in low-density (D3/D4) bone.

## Key Contributions
- Confirms crestal stress concentration as universal phenomenon regardless of bone density or implant macrodesign
- Supports cylindrical (parallel-walled) design in poor bone quality
- Quantifies biomechanical rationale for implant selection based on bone density

## Methodology
- ANSYS v10; 8 3D models (D1–D4 × threaded/cylindrical)
- 400 N vertical load on mandibular first molar crown
- Von Mises stress criterion; homogeneous isotropic material assumption

## Results
- Highest stress: threaded > cylindrical across all bone densities
- Peak stress location: neck/crestal region (all models)
- D3/D4 bone: cylindrical design substantially more favourable
