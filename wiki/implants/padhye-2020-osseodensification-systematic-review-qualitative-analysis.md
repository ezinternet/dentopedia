## 한줄요약
체계적 문헌고찰 (Systematic Review, SR), n=12편 (2010~2018.11) — 골밀도화 (Osseodensification, OD)는 통상적 드릴링 대비 삽입 토크, 골-임플란트 접촉률 (Bone-to-Implant Contact, BIC), 골면적분율 (Bone Area Fraction Occupancy, BAFO) 모두 평균적으로 증가시킴. 다만 12편 중 8편이 동물 조직학 연구, 인간 임상은 2편뿐이라 장기 임상 결과로의 외삽은 어렵다.

## Summary
Padhye et al. published one of the first systematic reviews focused exclusively on osseodensification (OD), the Densah-bur technique introduced by Huwais in 2015. They searched PubMed-Medline, Embase, and Google Scholar (2010 through Nov 2018) with no language restriction. From 132 hits they included 12 articles: 8 animal histologic studies, 2 human clinical studies, 1 case series, and 1 case report.

The synthesis is qualitative — no pooled effect sizes or meta-analytic estimates are reported, only direction of effect. Across the included studies, OD raised insertion torque, BIC, and BAF compared with conventional drilling. ARRIVE-based quality assessment showed 6/8 animal studies scored as "high quality". The authors are explicit that the evidence base is largely animal/histologic and that long-term clinical success of OD "cannot be ascertained" from this body of literature.

This is a useful **landscape map** of the OD field circa 2018, but should not be cited as quantitative evidence of clinical superiority. For pooled estimates, see [[implants/inchingolo-2021-osseodensification-implant-site-osteotomy-sr-ma]].

## Key Contributions
- First dedicated SR on OD — establishes the consensus that OD trends toward higher torque, BIC, and BAFO vs conventional drilling.
- Inventories the OD evidence base by study type: animal histologic dominant (8/12), human clinical scarce (2/12).
- Documents the Densah bur mechanism (4+ lands, negative rake, counterclockwise densifying mode) for downstream clinical readers.
- Uses ARRIVE to formally quality-rate the animal studies (6/8 high score).

## Methodology
PRISMA-guided two-reviewer screen of PubMed-Medline, Embase, Google Scholar (Jan 2010 – Nov 2018), no language restriction. PICO: effect of OD on primary stability of implants in humans by clinical and histologic evaluation. Outcomes harvested: insertion torque (10/12), BIC (7/12), BAFO (6/12). Quality of 8 animal studies assessed by ARRIVE. No meta-analysis performed because of design heterogeneity.

## Results
- Included studies: 12 (8 animal histologic, 2 human clinical, 1 case series, 1 case report).
- Outcome reporting: insertion torque 10/12, BIC 7/12, BAFO 6/12.
- Direction of effect: OD > conventional drilling for all three outcomes.
- Quality: 6/8 animal studies rated high on ARRIVE.
- Pooled effect sizes, 95% CI, I²: **not reported** (qualitative review only).

## Limitations
- Evidence dominated by animal histology; only 2 human clinical studies.
- No meta-analytic pooling — cannot give a magnitude of effect for clinical decisions.
- Risk-of-bias for the human studies is not explicitly summarized in the extracted text.
- Commercial/proprietary nature of Densah burs creates a potential reporting bias not formally adjudicated.
