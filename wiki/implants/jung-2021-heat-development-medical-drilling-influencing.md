## Summary
A narrative mini-review categorizing the heat-generating factors of medical and dental bone drilling into internal (drill-side) and external (environment-side) families, comparing temperature measurement methods (thermocouple, IR thermography, fiber optic), and presenting preliminary inflammatory tissue-reaction data from inadequate drilling. Argues for standardized ex vivo measurement protocols to improve reproducibility and reduce animal experimentation.

## Key Contributions
Clean internal/external factor framework that other SRs implicitly use. Methodological discussion of measurement-tool comparability — often the hidden source of heterogeneity in the field.

## Methodology
- Narrative literature review.
- Factor categorization (internal vs external).
- Comparison of thermocouple vs IR thermography vs fiber optic.
- Preliminary inflammatory tissue reaction data.

## Results — Factor Framework
| Family | Variables |
|---|---|
| Internal | Drill diameter, shape, material, speed, feed, intermittence, depth |
| External | Cooling method, irrigation flow/temperature, bone density, anatomy |
| Measurement | Thermocouple, IR thermography, fiber optic — not interchangeable |

47°C threshold cited; clinical consequences enumerated (implant loss, fractures, repeat surgery, healthcare cost).

## Clinical Implications
- 발열 통제는 internal·external 양쪽 동시 — drill 선택만으로 또는 irrigation만으로 부족.
- 문헌 비교 시 측정도구가 다르면 절대값 직접 비교 불가 — 본인 임상 도입 시 도구 동질성 확인.
- 표준 ex vivo 측정 protocol 도입이 향후 메타분석 가능성 결정.
