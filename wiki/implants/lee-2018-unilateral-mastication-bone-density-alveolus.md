## 한줄요약

## Summary
This cross-sectional CBCT study measured interradicular alveolar bone density (Hounsfield units) in 25 adults with self-reported unilateral mastication habits, comparing chewing versus non-chewing sides across four regions per arch. Bone density was significantly higher on the chewing side at canine, premolar, and molar regions (p<0.05) but not at the incisor region, where occlusal forces are smallest. The posterior-to-anterior gradient of bone density was consistent regardless of chewing side. The mandibular molar region showed the greatest asymmetry, with chewing-side bone ~100 HU denser than the opposite side.

## Key Contributions
- Demonstrated functional loading asymmetry translates to measurable alveolar bone density differences (HU) detectable by CBCT
- Posterior regions most affected: mandibular molar showed largest chewing/non-chewing difference
- Anterior (incisor) region unaffected by chewing preference (low occlusal forces)
- Clinical implication: in unilateral mastication patients, implant site bone density may differ substantially between sides

## Methodology
- **Design**: Cross-sectional; 25 adults (6M/19F; mean 24y5mo) with unilateral mastication by questionnaire
- **Exclusions**: <18y, craniofacial anomaly, TMD, missing teeth, severe crowding
- **CBCT**: Alphard Vega; 80kV, 5mA, 0.39 mm voxel; EzImplant software for MPR reconstruction
- **Measurement**: Interradicular bone density at 5 mm below alveolar crest; 4 sites per arch (incisor, canine, premolar, molar interradicular midpoint)
- **Method error**: Dahlberg formula; 4.8–11.3 HU (acceptable)
- **Statistics**: Paired t-test (chewing vs opposite side per region)
