## Summary
Scoping review covering the implant failure spectrum (definition, classification, mechanism) and — more practically — the retrieval techniques for failed implants. Compares the major removal approaches: counter-torque/reverse-torque devices, trephine bur, piezo-surgery, and combined techniques. Modern preference: minimally invasive techniques (piezo, reverse-torque) preserve crestal bone better, enabling earlier subsequent replacement in many cases.

## Key Contributions
- Bridges failure classification and retrieval techniques in one review.
- Updates retrieval technique landscape with piezo-surgery and modern counter-torque devices.
- Bone-preservation-oriented protocol framing.

## Methodology
Scoping review per PRISMA-ScR; broad inclusion across failure and retrieval literature.

## Results
[합의수준] Technique selection by:
- Failure type (early vs late, peri-implantitis vs fracture vs infection)
- Implant location (anterior aesthetic vs posterior functional)
- Bone quality and crestal architecture
Modern minimally invasive techniques preferred for bone preservation.

## Limitations
- Scoping not systematic
- Case series dominant evidence base
- Outcome reporting heterogeneity
