## One-line Summary

5-year retrospective study (288 patients, 506 implants) using CAD-derived 3D (mesiodistal + buccolingual) angulation measurement: nonaxial implants had significantly greater marginal bone loss (0.22±0.48 vs 0.10±0.39 mm, P<.05), maxilla > mandible (P<.001), with a significant angulation×antagonist interaction (nonaxial opposing an implant-supported FDP worst, Δ0.373 mm).

## Summary

This Kyung Hee University doctoral retrospective study asks whether implant angulation drives peri-implant marginal bone loss (MBL) once microbial confounding is controlled (only patients with plaque score ≤5% included; routine occlusal adjustment removing premature contacts). Its methodological advance is measuring angulation in three dimensions from CAD prosthetic data — capturing the buccolingual tilt that prior studies ignored — rather than the mesiodistal-only 2D angle used by Koutouzis & Wennström (2007) and Lee et al., whose 2D approach had found no association. Across 506 implants followed a mean 5.1 years, nonaxial implants lost significantly more bone than axial implants, and maxillary implants more than mandibular. Notably, no single clinical factor (prosthetic unit, attrition, antagonist type) reached significance on its own; the clinically interesting finding is an interaction — a nonaxial implant opposing another implant-supported fixed prosthesis loses the most bone (Δ0.373 mm vs axial), consistent with the absence of periodontal-ligament shock absorption at both arches concentrating stress at the crestal cortex. The result reframes the older "nonaxial loading is benign" literature as an artifact of incomplete (2D) angle measurement. Caveats: absolute losses are small (0.1–0.22 mm), MBL itself was scored on 2D periapical films (so buccolingual bone change was not captured in the outcome despite 3D angle input), and emergence/subgingival profile was not modeled — all single-center, single-rater.

## Key Contributions

- Replaces mesiodistal-only 2D angle assessment with CAD-derived 3D (mesiodistal + buccolingual) angulation, correcting a recurring measurement limitation in the off-axial-loading literature.
- Demonstrates a significant nonaxial→MBL association in clinical data, contrasting multiple prior negative/weak-correlation studies.
- Isolates an angulation × antagonist-type interaction: implant-supported-FDP antagonists amplify the nonaxial penalty, whereas natural-tooth and removable antagonists do not.
- Confirms a maxilla > mandible MBL gradient under matched plaque control, attributing it to lower maxillary density / thinner cortex.

## Methodology

- Design: single-center retrospective cohort. 288 patients, 506 implants; mean follow-up 5.1±0.3 yr after definitive prosthesis.
- Variables: angulation (axial/nonaxial, CAD 3D), arch (maxilla/mandible), position (anterior/premolar/molar), prosthetic unit (single/multi), attrition (normal/localized/generalized), antagonist (natural tooth / implant-FDP / removable denture).
- Outcome: MBL on standardized 2D periapical radiographs; all measurement/classification by one prosthodontist.
- Confounding control: only plaque score ≤5% patients; routine occlusal adjustment removing premature contacts.
- Statistics: Shapiro-Wilk → nonparametric (Mann-Whitney U, Kruskal-Wallis); interactions via Two-way ANOVA with Levene's test; Bonferroni correction across 5 categories (α=0.01); base P<.05.

## Results

- Angulation (primary): nonaxial 0.22±0.48 mm vs axial 0.10±0.39 mm, P<.05 (H0 rejected).
- Arch: maxilla > mandible, P<.001.
- Non-significant alone: prosthetic unit (single vs multi); attrition (P=0.121); antagonist type (natural 0.15±0.44 / implant-FDP 0.18±0.47 / removable 0.22±0.28 mm, P=0.493); position (P>.05).
- Interaction angulation × antagonist: significant (P=0.007 < Bonferroni α=0.01). Nonaxial−axial difference = 0.373 mm when opposing implant-FDP (vs 0.005 mm natural tooth, −0.158 mm removable).
