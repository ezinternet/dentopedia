## Summary
Yu et al. published a four-arm SR+MA comparing alternative implant-site preparations (osseodensification, osteotome, piezosurgery, under-drilling) against conventional drilling, with primary implant stability quotient (ISQ) as the outcome. Across 17 studies (12 RCTs), they found that OD, osteotome, and under-drilling all significantly improved ISQ over conventional drilling, while piezosurgery did not.

The headline result for OD is a mean difference of +10.25 ISQ (95% CI 4.97–15.52, P < 0.001) — strikingly large. However the OD subgroup contains only 3 studies, the CI is wide (~10 ISQ units), and a subsequent tighter SR (Marzorati 2026, with 343 OD implants) found a much smaller ISQ MD of 3.24. The Yu estimate is best read as *directionally correct but magnitude-inflated* — likely because the 3 OD studies were small and concentrated in extreme low-density-bone scenarios where OD shines hardest.

The under-drilling result (MD +11.43) is informative for a different reason: it reminds us that simply *under-preparing* the osteotomy yields a similarly large ISQ gain — OD's clinical case rests not on raw ISQ but on the *biological* mechanism (compaction autografting + spring-back without microfracture trauma) which under-drilling does not provide.

Funded by the Chinese National Natural Science Foundation — non-industry funding source, a methodological positive.

## Key Contributions
- Four-arm comparison of alternative site preparations vs conventional drilling in one SR.
- OD: MD +10.25 ISQ (95% CI 4.97–15.52, P < 0.001) — 3 studies.
- OT: MD +6.34 ISQ (95% CI 2.26–10.42, P = 0.002) — 8 studies.
- UD: MD +11.43 ISQ (95% CI 5.17–17.68, P < 0.001) — 4 studies.
- PS: MD +1.50 ISQ (95% CI −2.54 to 5.54, P = 0.47) — non-significant; 5 studies.
- Included Chinese-language (Wanfang) sources — broader than English-only SRs.

## Methodology
PRISMA-based SR; searches across MEDLINE/PubMed, Embase, Scopus, Wanfang (Chinese), up to 31 March 2022. Inclusion: clinical studies comparing one of OD/OT/PS/UD to conventional drilling with ISQ as outcome. 12 of 17 included were RCTs. Random-effects pooling; subgroup analyses by technique; GRADE for overall body of evidence. Supporting appendices cover PRISMA checklist, excluded studies, quality, subgroup analysis, GRADE.

## Results
- OD vs conventional drilling: +10.25 ISQ (95% CI 4.97–15.52, P < 0.001).
- OT vs conventional: +6.34 ISQ (95% CI 2.26–10.42, P = 0.002).
- UD vs conventional: +11.43 ISQ (95% CI 5.17–17.68, P < 0.001).
- PS vs conventional: +1.50 ISQ (95% CI −2.54 to 5.54, P = 0.47, ns).
- I² values and study-level forest plots not retrievable from the available extract.

## Critical Reading / Living-document Notes
- The OD subgroup is small (n=3 studies) and the CI is wide; this is the single biggest reason to interpret the +10.25 ISQ headline cautiously.
- The Marzorati 2026 SR — with stricter OD-only inclusion (no Summers, no magnetic mallet) and 343 OD implants — reports OD ISQ MD of only +3.24. The two SRs differ by ~3x in their OD point estimate, which is large. Reasonable synthesis: OD genuinely improves primary ISQ in low-density bone, but the *typical* effect is closer to Marzorati's 3 ISQ units than Yu's 10.
- UD's effect being similar in magnitude to OD is a reminder that "primary stability" is mostly a story about the *radial bone-to-implant mismatch* — both UD and OD increase that mismatch by different means. Whether the resulting ISQ rise reflects biology or just mechanics is not addressed by this SR.
- Piezosurgery's null finding aligns with mechanism — ultrasonic vibration does not compact bone, so the ISQ-vs-conventional comparison should not differ. This is a coherence check on the SR.
- This SR is the best-known *cross-technique* comparison in the literature and is widely cited. Its inflated OD estimate should be remembered when reading downstream citations.
