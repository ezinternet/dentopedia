## 한줄요약
동물 실험 (Sheep, n=12, 양측 paired, 경추 C2·C3·C4, 0/3/6주 시점) — 골밀도화 (Osseodensification, OD)가 통상 드릴링 대비 척추 hardware의 발탈강도(pullout strength)를 유의하게 높이고 (p<0.01), 골-임플란트 접촉률 (Bone-to-Implant Contact, BIC) p<0.01·골면적분율 (Bone Area Fraction Occupancy, BAFO) p=0.024로 모두 우수. 다만 정형외과/척추 hardware 모델이라 치과 임플란트로 외삽 시 별도 추론 단계가 필요.

## Summary
Lopez et al. transferred the OD principle out of dentistry into spinal hardware fixation. In 12 sheep, paired 4 × 10 mm fixation devices were placed bilaterally in C2, C3, and C4 vertebral bodies — left side with regular drilling, right side with OD. C2 and C4 served as the t=0 time point; C3 was harvested at 3 and 6 weeks. Outcomes were pullout strength (biomechanical) and BIC% / BAFO% (histomorphometry).

OD significantly outperformed regular drilling on all three outcomes: pullout strength p<0.01 collapsed over time (Wilcoxon paired at C2 and C4 each p=0.031; C3 at 3+6 weeks collapsed p=0.027), BIC p<0.01, BAFO p=0.024. A characteristic time pattern was seen for BAFO: dip between 0 and 3 weeks followed by recovery to baseline by 6 weeks, consistent with early resorptive remodeling followed by woven-bone regain. Histology shows residual autologous bone chips in the OD osteotomies acting as osteogenic nucleation sites — the same mechanistic story reported in dental OD studies.

**Important context flag**: this is an **orthopaedic / spinal** application. The OD principle is plausibly transferable to dental osteotomy, but cervical vertebrae are biomechanically and compositionally different from edentulous alveolar ridge. Cite the mechanism (bone chips as nucleation sites; densified wall) freely, but do not cite the pullout-strength numbers as if they applied to a dental implant in maxillary D3/D4 bone.

## Key Contributions
- First published application of OD outside dentistry — establishes the principle as transferable to general hardware fixation.
- Paired within-subject sheep design (left R vs right OD across three vertebrae) reduces between-animal variance.
- Three time points (0, 3, 6 weeks) capture the early-remodeling BAFO dip-and-recover pattern.
- Numerical OD advantage: pullout p<0.01, BIC p<0.01, BAFO p=0.024 — three independent outcomes, consistent direction.
- Histological confirmation of bone-chip nucleation mechanism (Fig. 9 white arrows).

## Methodology
12 sheep ~65 kg. 4 × 10 mm fixation devices placed bilaterally in C2, C3, C4. Left = regular drilling (R); right = OD. C2 and C4 = t=0; C3 = t=3 and t=6 weeks. Biomechanical pullout strength + histomorphometry (%BIC, %BAFO). Paired Wilcoxon signed-rank tests for statistical inference.

## Results
- Pullout strength: OD > R collapsed over time, **p < 0.01**.
- Wilcoxon paired pullout: C2 p=0.031, C4 p=0.031, C3 (3+6 wk collapsed) p=0.027.
- %BIC: OD > R, **p < 0.01**.
- %BAFO: OD > R, **p = 0.024**.
- BAFO temporal pattern: decrease 0→3 weeks (early resorptive remodeling), recovery 3→6 weeks back to roughly t=0 level.
- Histology: bone chips visible in OD samples, remodeling sites in both groups.

## Limitations
- Orthopaedic / cervical spine context — not a dental implant model.
- Cervical vertebral bone composition and loading differ from human edentulous alveolar ridge — generalizability requires careful argument.
- 6 weeks max follow-up; no fatigue/loaded data.
- 12 sheep, paired design — moderate power.
- NYU Coelho group authorship cluster (shared with Witek 2019 and other OD literature) — chain of evidence not independently replicated.
- DOI inconsistency between citation (10.1016/j.jmbbm.2016.12.020) and manuscript metadata (10.1016/j.jmbbm.2017.01.020).
