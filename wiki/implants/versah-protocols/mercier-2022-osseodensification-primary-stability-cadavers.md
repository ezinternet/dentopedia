## Summary

In 21 formalin-fixed human cadaver mandibles, OD using Densah profile drills (Versah) was compared to standard drilling (Axiom, Anthogyr) in 58 matched osteotomies. OD produced significantly higher insertion torque (34.9 vs 23.6 Ncm, p=0.036) and a moderate positive IT-ISQ correlation (ρ=0.527), absent in the SD group. CBCT analysis confirmed significant peri-implant bone density increase with OD (p=0.026).

The OD IT value (34.9 Ncm) aligns closely with Rittipakorn 2025 clockwise OD cadaveric data (34.0 Ncm), suggesting consistent primary stability outcomes from OD across cadaveric designs, independent of bone origin (mandible vs tibia) or OD variant (standard vs clockwise).

## Key Contributions

- OD significantly increases IT in cadaveric mandibular bone (+48% over SD)
- IT-ISQ correlation stronger with OD (ρ=0.527) vs SD — greater predictability
- CBCT bone density increase with OD: morphological confirmation at peri-implant level
- Cross-cadaver IT replication with Rittipakorn 2025 (OD IT ~34–35 Ncm in both studies)

## Methodology

- 21 formalin-fixed human cadaver mandibles
- 29 OD (Densah burs, Versah) + 29 SD (Axiom, Anthogyr); Axiom implants placed in all 58 sites
- 16 additional osteotomies without implant placement → CBCT bone tissue analysis
- Measurements: IT (Ncm), ISQ; CBCT peri-implant bone density
- Statistics: Mann-Whitney U; Spearman ρ; α=0.05
