## One-line Summary

Retrospective study (n=268 implants, 65 patients) of OD vs conventional Esset kit in ridge-split: OD significantly improved ISQ in maxilla and mandibular posterior (all ISQ ≥65), with equivalent horizontal bone gain.

## 한줄요약

후향적 연구(268개 임플란트, 65명): 상악 및 하악 구치부에서 OD가 유의하게 높은 ISQ를 보였으며(모두 ≥65 달성), 수평 골폭 획득은 동등했다.

## Summary

In 65 patients undergoing simultaneous implant placement with alveolar ridge split, OD (Densah burs, n=133) versus conventional ridge-split (Osstem Esset, n=135) was compared. Primary (T0) and secondary stability (T1 at 4 months) were measured by Osstell RFA; bone width by CBCT.

**Stability findings:** OD yielded significantly higher ISQ in maxillary anterior and posterior at both T0 and T1 (p<0.01). In the mandibular posterior, OD showed significantly higher secondary stability (p=0.001). No difference was found in the mandibular anterior. Critically, all OD implants maintained ISQ ≥65 (prosthetic loading threshold); conventional minimum was 62 at T0 and 61 at T1.

**Bone gain:** Both techniques produced equivalent horizontal bone gain (coronal ~1.1–1.6 mm range across regions; NS throughout). Greater coronal vs apical gain was universal.

**Interpretation:** OD's mechanical advantage from bone compaction appears most meaningful in low-density (maxillary) bone. In dense mandibular anterior (D1–D2), OD adds no ISQ benefit. This region-specificity aligns with the biomechanical rationale for OD.

## Key Contributions

- First retrospective series of OD in a ridge-split context (not standard osteotomy) — clinically distinct setting
- ISQ ≥65 threshold universally maintained with OD; conventional group had outliers below threshold
- OD technique did not impair augmentation: bone gain equivalent to conventional ridge-split
- Minimum meaningful ISQ floor: OD 68 (T0), 71 (T1); conventional 62 (T0), 61 (T1)

## Methodology

- 65 patients (ASA I–II, horizontal ridge deficiency; mean age 51.8y; range 31–69)
- 268 Osstem implants across 4 anatomical regions
- OD protocol: 800 rpm clockwise pilot → Densah burs counterclockwise 1200 rpm (2.3–3.3 mm)
- Conventional: Osstem Esset twist + expansion burs (1.6–4.1 mm, 800–1200 rpm)
- All sites: CGF membrane placed over expanded ridge; 3-0/4-0 silk sutures
- RFA (Osstell) at placement and 4-month abutment connection; CBCT at preop and 4 months
