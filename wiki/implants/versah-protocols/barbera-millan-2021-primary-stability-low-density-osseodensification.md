## One-line Summary

In vitro pig-tibia study (Lekholm-Zarb type IV bone, n=55 implants/group): Osseodensification produced significantly higher insertion torque (21.7 vs 8.9 Ncm, p<0.001) and ISQ (69.8 vs 65.2, p=0.001) than conventional under-drilling.

## 한줄요약

저밀도 돼지 경골(Lekholm-Zarb type IV) in vitro 연구(군당 임플란트 55개): 골밀도화(OD)가 기존 언더드릴링(UD)보다 식립 토크(21.7 vs 8.9 Ncm, p<0.001)와 ISQ(69.8 vs 65.2, p=0.001) 모두 유의하게 높았다.

## Summary

This bench study placed 110 conical internal-connection implants (4×10 mm) into low-density porcine tibia sections chosen to mimic Lekholm-Zarb type IV (D4) human maxilla, comparing **Osseodensification (OD, Densah burs)** against **conventional under-drilling (UD)** — the standard soft-bone preparation. The choice of UD (not plain hole-drilling) as the comparator is what makes this a stringent test: OD still won on both primary-stability metrics. The cortical layer was removed so that primary stability reflected the trabecular preparation alone, and bone sections were randomized between groups.

## Key Contributions

- Tests OD against **under-drilling**, the established under-sizing technique for soft bone — not just against hole-drilling — and OD still produces significantly higher primary stability.
- Large, randomized sample (55/group) in a tissue model preserving real trabecular mechanics.
- Maps the torque–ISQ relationship with sigmoid regression, showing an ISQ plateau (~75.6) beyond which added torque buys no further stability, and a tighter torque–ISQ fit with OD.

## Methodology

- **Implants**: 110 Klockner Vega internal-connection bone-level implants, 4 mm × 10 mm.
- **Bone**: 30 coronal sections of frozen fresh pig tibia (type D4 analogue), cortex removed in the coronal zone.
- **Groups (randomized, 55 each)**: UD (control) vs OD (Densah burs, counterclockwise, 1200 rpm, copious irrigation). 3 implants per section.
- **Outcomes**: insertion torque (Implantmed motor, 5 Ncm steps; >50 Ncm via ratchet, logged as ">50") and ISQ (Penguin RFA, 4 sites averaged).
- **Stats**: R; Student t-test between groups; sigmoid regression for torque–ISQ.

## Results

| Parameter | UD (control) | OD (Densah) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Insertion torque (Ncm) | 8.87 ± 6.17 | 21.72 ± 17.14 | 0.000 |
| ISQ (RFA) | 65.16 ± 7.45 | 69.75 ± 6.79 | 0.001 |

- OD ≈ 2.4× the mean insertion torque of UD and ~4.6 ISQ points higher.
- Torque–ISQ association tighter for OD (pseudo r²=0.420 vs UD 0.272); ISQ plateaus near 75.6 regardless of further torque.
- Authors judge both groups' stability compatible with immediate loading per the literature; OD ISQ could not be benchmarked against prior work (novel technique).
- In-vitro only: no osseointegration/healing data; cortex removed; single implant system — clinical confirmation called for.
