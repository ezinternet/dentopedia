## One-line Summary

In vitro study (48 polyurethane foam blocks, 0.160 g/cm³ Type IV bone analog, CNC DMU machine): osseodensification drilling (OD, Densah) vs conventional drilling (CD, BLT tapered) — OD gave higher insertion/removal torque (IT 11.73 vs 7.77 N·m, RT 9.28 vs 6.65 N·m, both p<0.001) and fewer site-wall defects despite comparable ISQ (47.1 vs 46.7, NS); recommended OD parameters: 1500 rpm, 0.04 mm/z feed, with 4°C saline irrigation.

## 한줄요약

In vitro 연구 (폴리우레탄 폼 블록 48개, 0.160 g/cm³ Type IV 골 모사, CNC DMU 장비): 골밀도화 드릴링 (Osseodensification Drilling, OD, Densah) vs 기존 드릴링 (Conventional Drilling, CD, BLT tapered) — OD가 식립·제거 토크를 더 높이고 (IT 11.73 vs 7.77 N·m, RT 9.28 vs 6.65 N·m, 모두 p<0.001) 골벽 결함이 적었으나 임플란트 안정성 지수 (Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ)는 동등 (47.1 vs 46.7, NS); 권장 OD 파라미터는 1500 rpm, 0.04 mm/z, 4°C 식염수 관주.

## Summary

This in vitro bench study set out to answer a parameter-selection question that most osseodensification (OD) literature sidesteps: OD-vs-conventional comparisons are abundant, but Huwais and subsequent authors never specified *which* drilling parameters are optimal for OD. Tao et al. drilled 48 polyurethane foam blocks (0.160 g/cm³, a standardized Type IV / low-density bone analog) on a CNC machine (DMU) — eliminating surgeon hand-pressure variability — while varying spin speed (300/800/1500 rpm per Versah protocol), feed per tooth, and irrigation (with/without 4°C saline). Densah burs (Versah) in densifying mode (OD) were compared against Straumann BLT tapered drills (CD), and the osteotomies were characterized for thrust force, intra-hole temperature, hole-wall morphology, insertion torque (IT), removal torque (RT), and implant stability quotient (ISQ) using Straumann BLT 4.1 mm implants.

OD produced substantially higher IT and RT than CD (both p<0.001) and left fewer iatrogenic defects (tears/potholes) on the osteotomy walls, yet ISQ was statistically indistinguishable between the two techniques — reproducing the now-common OD finding that torque metrics and resonance-frequency stability dissociate. Temperature elevation was lower with OD than CD under irrigation, and irrigation was essential in both to avoid thermal damage. The authors recommend OD at **1500 rpm and 0.04 mm/z feed with irrigation** for Type IV alveolar bone, concluding OD enhances primary stability and bone density while protecting the osteotomy morphology. The key caveat is that this is synthetic-bone bench data drilled by a CNC machine — the recommended parameters need in vivo / clinical validation.

## Key Contributions

- **Parameter optimization, not just technique comparison**: first OD study to systematically vary spin speed × feed per tooth × irrigation to recommend a concrete OD setting for Type IV bone, filling the gap left by the original OD descriptions.
- **CNC-controlled drilling** removes operator hand-pressure noise, giving clean force/temperature/torque measurements.
- **Recommended OD settings**: 1500 rpm, 0.04 mm/z, with 4°C saline irrigation.
- Confirms the **IT/RT–ISQ dissociation** in a controlled in vitro setting: OD raises torque markedly but not ISQ.
- Documents **lower thermal load and fewer wall defects** for OD vs CD, supporting OD's bone-preserving rationale.

## Methodology

- **Design**: In vitro CNC osteotomy experiment; OD (Densah) vs CD (BLT tapered) on synthetic bone; independent-samples t-test (Shapiro–Wilk normality), p<0.05; SPSS 26.0.
- **Bone analog**: Polyurethane foam blocks (Sawbones) 0.160 g/cm³ (PCF 10), ASTM-standardized Type IV bone simulant; 80 × 40 × 20 mm.
- **Implants**: Straumann BLT, 4.1 mm diameter, 10 and 16 mm.
- **Drills**: CD = BLT tapered (2.2 pilot, 2.8, 3.5 mm); OD = Densah burs (1.6 pilot, 1828, 2838) in densifying mode per Versah protocol.
- **Variables**: 3 spin speeds (300/800/1500 rpm), 2 feeds per tooth (Fz), 2 irrigation conditions (±4°C saline), on a DMU CNC machine.
- **Instrumentation**: Kistler 9272 dynamometer (thrust force); K-type thermocouples at 5/10/16 mm + NI LabVIEW (intra-hole temperature); FLIR A615 IR imager (surface temp); Keyence VK-X3000 confocal + SEM (hole-wall morphology); Tohnichi torque tester (IT/RT); Osstell RFA (ISQ).
- **Sample size**: 48 (G*Power; effect size 0.92, α 0.05, power 0.8).

## Results

| Parameter | OD (Densah) | CD (BLT) | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Insertion torque IT (N·m) | 11.73 ± 0.45 | 7.77 ± 0.21 | OD higher, **p<0.001** |
| Removal torque RT (N·m) | 9.28 ± 0.45 | 6.65 ± 0.19 | OD higher, **p<0.001** |
| ISQ | 47.08 ± 5.95 | 46.71 ± 8.56 | NS (p=0.86) |
| Max thrust force (N, at 300 rpm) | 2.99 ± 0.22 | 2.77 ± 0.17 | OD higher, **p<0.01** |
| Temp elevation under irrigation (°C) | 3.35 | 4.67 | OD lower |

- **Torque**: OD substantially higher IT and RT — the main between-group differences.
- **ISQ**: no significant difference — torque and ISQ dissociate.
- **Thrust force**: OD higher, peaking at low speed (300 rpm).
- **Temperature**: OD generates less heat under irrigation; irrigation essential in both techniques to prevent thermal damage.
- **Morphology**: OD leaves fewer hole-wall defects (tears/potholes) — less iatrogenic damage.
- **Recommendation**: OD at **1500 rpm + 0.04 mm/z + irrigation** for Type IV bone.
- **Limitations**: synthetic bone (no biology/healing/osseointegration); single density (0.160 g/cm³); CNC (not freehand) drilling; no loading/survival endpoint; ISQ–torque dissociation mechanism unexplained.
