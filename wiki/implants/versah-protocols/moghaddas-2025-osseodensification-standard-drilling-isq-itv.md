Prospective clinical trial (21 patients, 39 implants, posterior maxilla): osseodensification raised insertion torque ~37% over standard drilling (50.3 vs 36.1 Ncm, p<0.001) but produced no significant ISQ difference at placement or 3 months — IT and ISQ dissociate, both groups staying above the ISQ 68 threshold.

## 한줄요약

전향적 임상시험 (환자 21명, 임플란트 39개, 상악 구치부): 골밀도화 (Osseodensification, OD)가 표준 드릴링 (Standard Drilling, SD) 대비 식립 토크 (Insertion Torque, IT)를 ~37% 높였으나 (50.3 vs 36.1 Ncm, p<0.001), 임플란트 안정성 지수 (Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ)는 식립 시점·3개월 모두 유의차 없음 — 두 군 모두 ISQ 68 임계값 이상 유지, IT와 ISQ가 해리됨.

## Summary

This prospective clinical trial compared osseodensification (OD) against standard drilling (SD) on two primary-stability surrogates — insertion torque (IT) and implant stability quotient (ISQ) — in real patients receiving implants in the posterior maxilla, a low-density site where OD is most promoted. Across 39 implants in 21 patients, OD elevated IT by roughly 37% over SD (statistically significant), but produced no significant ISQ difference either immediately at placement or at 3 months. Both groups remained above the ISQ 68 threshold considered favorable for early loading throughout. The result reproduces the increasingly common finding that OD raises insertion torque without a matching rise in ISQ, i.e. the two metrics dissociate, and adds clinical-trial weight (rather than cadaver or animal data) to that observation. The authors conclude OD allowed passive, low-stress placement while maintaining high stability, but note SD already achieved adequate ISQ in this ridge-restricted cohort.

## Key Contributions

- Real-patient (not cadaver/animal) head-to-head of OD vs SD on **both** IT and ISQ in the posterior maxilla.
- Reproduces the **IT–ISQ dissociation**: OD significantly raised insertion torque but not ISQ, at placement and at 3 months.
- Both groups stayed **above the ISQ 68 favorable-loading threshold** at every timepoint — SD already reached adequate stability in this ridge-height ≥9 mm / width ≥6 mm cohort.
- IT correlated positively with ISQ at baseline, yet the IT gain from OD did not translate into a measurable ISQ gain.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Prospective two-technique comparison (OD = test, SD = control), CONSORT-styled, operator reading IT/ISQ blinded to the drilling protocol. Two-way repeated-measures ANOVA, significance set at 0.001.
- **Sample**: 21 patients (10 F / 11 M, mean age 40); 39 implants in the posterior maxilla. Group counts are reported inconsistently — abstract says 16 OD / 23 SD, body says 18 OD / 20 SD, with "20 implants in each group statistically analyzed" after two non-cooperating patients (two implants) were excluded at follow-up.
- **Implant**: MEGAGEN ANYONE (internal conical connection, tapered, sandblasted), uniform 4 × 10 mm, placed 1 mm subcrestal.
- **Burs**: Original Versah (USA) Densah burs for OD per the densifying reference guide; conventional burs for SD per company protocol. Both at 1100 rpm, sequential similar-diameter burs, saline irrigation, max torque 50 Ncm during osteotomy.
- **Outcomes**: IT = maximum Ncm at insertion termination (manual surgical torque wrench); ISQ via MEGA ISQ resonance frequency analysis (Smartpeg) at placement and at 3 months.
- **Inclusion**: ≥18 y, ridge height ≥9 mm and width ≥6 mm, healed sites ≥4 months post-extraction, no augmentation needed. **Exclusion**: smoking, diabetes, bisphosphonates/steroids, immunocompromise, prior augmentation, etc. Pre-op periapical + CBCT; amoxicillin 500 mg q8h × 7 days.

## Results

| Parameter | OD (test) | SD (control) | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Insertion torque (Ncm) | 50.31 ± 5.90 | 36.09 ± 11.07 | OD ~37% higher, **p<0.001** |
| ISQ at surgery | 76.13 ± 6.07 | 71.74 ± 2.42 | NS (p≈0.134–0.176) |
| ISQ at 3 months | 75.13 ± 6.44 | 73.43 ± 1.67 | NS (p≈0.471–0.488) |

- **Insertion torque**: OD ~37% higher than SD, the only significant between-group difference. IT correlated positively with ISQ at baseline (zero time).
- **ISQ**: no significant OD-vs-SD difference at placement or at 3 months; values stable in both groups over time.
- **ISQ 68 threshold**: all OD and SD parameters remained above 68 at both timepoints.
- **Safety**: no inflammation, infection, or implant mobility at second-stage surgery.
- **Limitations**: posterior maxilla only; small sample with inconsistent per-group counts; short (3-month) follow-up with no loading/survival endpoint; single implant size (4 × 10) and one implant system.
