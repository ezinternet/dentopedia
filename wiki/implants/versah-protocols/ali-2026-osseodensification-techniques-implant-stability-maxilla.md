## One-line Summary

Split-mouth RCT (n=7 patients, 14 implant sites, maxillary anterior zone): Densah bur osseodensification yielded significantly higher primary ISQ (70.1 vs 49.0) and labial plate thickness vs Magnetic Mallet, with comparable bone density and crestal width at 6 months.

## 한줄요약

분할구 RCT (7명, 14부위, 상악 전치부): Densah bur 골밀화가 Magnetic Mallet 대비 1차 ISQ(70.1 vs 49.0) 및 순측 골판 두께에서 유의하게 우수했고, 골밀도와 치조정 폭경은 6개월 시점에 동등했다.

## Summary

This split-mouth RCT compared two osseodensification (OD) approaches — rotary Densah bur (DB) and pulse-based Magnetic Mallet (MM) — for implant placement in the maxillary anterior esthetic zone. Seven partially edentulous patients received bilateral implants (IS-III-CMI, Neobiotech), with each side randomly assigned to one OD technique. ISQ was measured via Osstell at placement and 6 months; CBCT assessed bone density, crestal width, and labial plate thickness immediately and at 6 months. Wound healing (EWH score) and pain (VAS) were tracked at days 7, 14, and 21.

The Densah bur group consistently exceeded the 70-ISQ stability threshold at both time points (primary 70.1, secondary 74.7), while the Magnetic Mallet group had substantially lower primary stability (ISQ 49.0) but improved significantly by 6 months (59.8). Labial plate thickness was significantly greater in the DB group at both timepoints (~1.8 vs ~1.2 mm). Both groups showed equivalent peri-implant bone density (both decreased over time, consistent with woven-to-lamellar remodeling) and comparable crestal width at 6 months. The Magnetic Mallet group had two intraoperative complications (1 labial plate fracture causing implant failure, 1 palatal crack), both in early cases, suggesting a steep learning curve.

## Key Contributions

1. First split-mouth RCT directly comparing Densah bur vs Magnetic Mallet OD in the maxillary anterior esthetic zone
2. Densah bur achieved ≥70 ISQ at placement — clinically above the early loading threshold; MM did not
3. Densah bur preserved significantly more labial bone thickness at both immediate and 6-month timepoints
4. Both techniques delivered equivalent bone density and crestal width at 6 months
5. MM associated with intraoperative complications (labial plate fracture / palatal crack) in thin-ridge anterior sites
6. Rigorous 2-level LMM statistical model to handle split-mouth clustering

## Methodology

- **Design**: Split-mouth RCT, CONSORT-compliant, IRB approved, blinded assessors
- **n**: 7 patients, 14 sites (7 DB / 7 MM; 1 MM failure → 6 completed)
- **Implant**: IS-III-CMI (Neobiotech), 1 mm sub-crestal, maxillary anterior zone
- **DB protocol**: Pilot drill clockwise 800 RPM → Densah Burs progressively larger in reverse counter-clockwise densifying mode
- **MM protocol**: Pilot osteotome Mode 1 (75 daN, 80 µs) → 100P-Osteotome Mode 2 (90 daN, 80 µs)
- **Primary outcome**: ISQ (Osstell 3rd gen) at placement and 6 months
- **Secondary**: EWH score (days 7/14/21), VAS pain, CBCT bone density (Hu), crestal width (mm), labial plate thickness (mm)
- **Statistics**: 2-level hierarchical LMM (REML, Bonferroni, Kenward-Roger df, AIC/BIC model selection)

## Results

| Outcome | DB | MM | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary ISQ | 70.14 ± 14.99 | 49.00 ± 7.13 | <0.001 |
| Secondary ISQ (6 mo) | 74.71 ± 8.10 | 59.83 ± 4.07 | 0.003 |
| Bone density immediate (Hu) | 901.86 ± 131.65 | 835.67 ± 102.14 | 0.216 NS |
| Bone density 6 mo (Hu) | 749.00 ± 134.76 | 700.17 ± 106.19 | 0.342 NS |
| Crestal width 6 mo (mm) | 5.30 ± 0.31 | 5.42 ± 0.40 | 0.625 NS |
| Labial plate immediate (mm) | 1.85 ± 0.36 | 1.17 ± 0.23 | 0.002 |
| Labial plate 6 mo (mm) | 1.68 ± 0.54 | 0.98 ± 0.32 | 0.002 |
| EWH score day 14 | 9.14 ± 1.46 | 6.83 ± 2.23 | 0.009 |

**LMM group effect on ISQ**: p < 0.001; time effect p = 0.019; group×time NS (p = 0.308)
**LMM group effect on labial plate thickness**: p < 0.001; time and G×T NS
**LMM on bone density**: group NS (p = 0.131); time significant (p = 0.002)
**Intraoperative complications**: 0 in DB; 2 in MM (1 implant failure due to labial plate fracture; 1 palatal crack)

Key mechanistic note: DB relies on rolling-sliding plastic deformation and autografting; MM relies on fracture-and-impact compaction with high-energy impulses. Both activate mechanotransduction (osteocyte network → osteoblast recruitment). The MM group's late ISQ gain (secondary stability improvement, p=0.024) suggests adequate biological healing despite low initial primary stability.
