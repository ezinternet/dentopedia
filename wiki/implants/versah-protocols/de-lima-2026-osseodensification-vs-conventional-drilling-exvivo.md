## One-line Summary

Paired ex vivo study (n=16 bovine rib blocks) found both Densah®/Versah and Bone Reamer Drills®/WF osseodensification kits significantly improved insertion and removal torque vs conventional drilling in type IV bone, but produced no significant ISQ or temperature differences.

## Summary

De Lima et al. (2026) conducted a paired comparative ex vivo study on 16 standardized bovine rib bone blocks to compare three osteotomy techniques: (G1) conventional drilling, (G2) osseodensification with Densah®/Versah burs, and (G3) osseodensification with Bone Reamer Drills®/WF — a Brazilian-made kit. Each block received one osteotomy per technique (48 total). Outcomes measured: insertion torque (IT), removal torque (RT), ISQ via Osstell® RFA, maximum temperature via thermocouple, bur mass loss, and SEM of bur surfaces and bone beds.

**Key finding**: Both OD systems significantly outperformed conventional drilling on torque metrics. Notably, the WF kit produced higher torque than Versah (IT: 95.25 vs 77.62 Ncm; RT: 89.62 vs 69.62 Ncm). However, ISQ values (~79–82) and temperature (~28–31°C) did not differ significantly across groups. SEM confirmed compacted bone at the OD implant interfaces. Bur integrity was maintained for both OD systems (no significant mass loss), though WF burs showed greater surface wear than Densah® burs.

## Key Contributions

1. **Torque vs ISQ dissociation**: In type IV bovine bone, osseodensification improves torque-based primary stability without improving RFA-derived ISQ — suggests OD's mechanism acts primarily via mechanical interlocking (compaction) rather than stiffness changes detectable by RFA at this density.
2. **WF kit competitive on torque**: The Brazilian-made Bone Reamer Drills®/WF outperformed Densah®/Versah on both IT and RT; positions WF as a potentially cost-effective alternative for the Brazilian/emerging market.
3. **Thermal safety confirmed**: Neither OD kit exceeded safe bone temperature thresholds (all groups ~28–31°C, far below the 47°C necrosis threshold) when irrigation used.
4. **SEM compaction evidence**: Bone microparticle compaction at the OD implant interface confirmed histomorphologically, supporting the proposed osseointegration mechanism.
5. **Bur durability**: No significant mass loss in either OD kit; Densah® burs showed less wear than WF burs per SEM.

## Methodology

- **Model**: 16 bovine rib bone blocks (50×12×30 mm); type IV bone analog; 3 osteotomies/block (paired design).
- **Implants**: Biomorse® 4×10 mm (uniform surface/shape across groups).
- **G1 Protocol**: Clockwise, sequential burs 1.8→2.2→2.8→3.2→3.6 mm.
- **G2 Protocol**: Counterclockwise + oscillating pumping; VPLTT→VT1828→VT2838; final diameter ~0.5–0.7 mm undersized vs implant (per manufacturer type IV bone recommendation).
- **G3 Protocol**: Counterclockwise + oscillating bombardment; helical lance 1.8→BRD 2.2→2.6→3.0→3.4 mm.
- All groups: 1000 rpm, 0.9% saline irrigation at 4–6°C.
- **Statistics**: Repeated-measures ANOVA + Bonferroni post-hoc; α = 0.05; n=16 per group (power=0.80).

## Results

| Outcome | Conventional | Versah (VS) | WF | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Insertion Torque (Ncm) | 61.25 ± 29.6 | 77.62 ± 31.6 | **95.25 ± 45.8** | 0.007 |
| Removal Torque (Ncm) | 58.18 ± 32.7 | 69.62 ± 35.6 | **89.62 ± 48.4** | 0.008 |
| ISQ | 79.42 ± 6.81 | 82.40 ± 4.03 | 80.68 ± 5.00 | 0.157 (NS) |
| Max Temp (°C) | 27.81 ± 2.94 | 30.62 ± 4.61 | 28.37 ± 2.68 | 0.087 (NS) |

All pairwise torque comparisons significant (p<0.05). ISQ and temperature: no significant group differences. No bur mass loss detected. SEM: bone compaction at OD interfaces; G2 showed more crack propagation in bone bed; WF burs exhibited more surface wear.
