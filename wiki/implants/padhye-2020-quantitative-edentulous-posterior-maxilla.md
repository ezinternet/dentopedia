## One-line Summary
Retrospective CBCT study (349 edentulous posterior-maxilla sites, 250 patients): over half of sites had inadequate horizontal width (<6 mm), most molar sites had inadequate vertical height (<8 mm), and 54.14% had an oblique sinus floor — so augmentation (sinus elevation/GBR) is frequently required before standard implant placement, supporting routine pre-implant CBCT.

## Summary
Retrospective CBCT analysis quantifying the residual alveolar ridge in 349 edentulous posterior maxillary sites from 250 patients. More than half of all sites — molar and premolar — had inadequate horizontal width (< 6 mm) for a standard-diameter implant, and the majority of molar sites had inadequate vertical bone (< 8 mm) for a standard-length implant without sinus elevation. Over half of all sites had an oblique sinus floor, an additional anatomic factor that elevates membrane perforation risk during lateral-window sinus floor augmentation.

## Key Contributions
- Population-level prevalence data on **the unmet "standard implant" envelope** in the edentulous posterior maxilla.
- Reports **sinus-floor obliqueness** as a third anatomic dimension beyond width and height — clinically meaningful for choosing transcrestal vs lateral approaches and for perforation risk counseling.
- Supports a routine **3D imaging (CBCT) requirement** prior to posterior maxillary implant planning.

## Methodology
- Design: retrospective cross-sectional CBCT analysis.
- Sample: 349 edentulous sites from 250 CBCT scans (Indian referral cohort).
- Stratification: by tooth position (premolar vs molar).
- Measurements:
  - Apico-coronal bone height (sagittal slice, crest to sinus floor).
  - Bucco-palatal ridge width (coronal slice, at crest).
  - Sinus-floor obliqueness (flat vs oblique morphology, coronal).
- Clinical cutoffs: < 6 mm horizontal width → can't place standard-diameter implant without augmentation; < 8 mm vertical → can't place standard-length implant without sinus floor elevation.

## Results
- **Horizontal ridge < 6 mm**:
  - Molar sites: 112/202 (**55.45%**).
  - Premolar sites: 74/136 (**54.42%**).
- **Apico-coronal height < 8 mm**:
  - Molar sites: 137/202 (**67.83%**) — i.e., 2/3 of molar sites need sinus elevation.
  - Premolar sites: 61/136 (**44.86%**).
- **Oblique sinus floor**: 183/338 (**54.14%**) of evaluated sites — relevant to SM perforation risk.
- **Conclusion**: a high fraction of edentulous posterior maxillary sites require augmentative procedures (sinus elevation, GBR, or both) for standard implant placement. Pre-implant CBCT should be considered the default, not optional.

## Clinical Implications (Author Memo)
- Default expectation for posterior maxillary edentulous patients should be: **at least one augmentative procedure will likely be required** (sinus elevation more often than GBR).
- **Order CBCT pre-implant routinely** for this region, not just when clinical suspicion is high — half of patients fall outside the standard-implant envelope.
- Note sinus-floor **obliqueness** on CBCT — it increases lateral-approach perforation risk and may shift the choice toward transcrestal or osseodensification approaches when residual bone permits.
- Single-center Indian cohort — proportions may differ by population, but the *direction* (high augmentation need) is consistent with global literature.
