## One-line Summary

Retrospective study (n=120 implants, 53 patients, 2 years) finding tissue-level implants with convergent neck had 0.31 mm MBL vs 0.97 mm for bone-level implants (p<0.001), independent of prosthesis type or arch location.

## Summary

This retrospective clinical study from the University of Valencia compared peri-implant marginal bone loss (MBL) between tissue-level (TL) implants with a convergent transmucosal neck (PRAMA RF, Sweden & Martina) and bone-level (BL) implants with a convergent neck (KOHNO, Sweden & Martina) after 2 years of functional loading. A total of 120 implants were placed in 53 patients (mean age 54.2 years) in posterior zones only (molars, premolars). Each implant type was subdivided into screw-retained single crowns (n=30) and 3-unit screw-retained fixed partial prostheses on 2 implants (n=30). MBL was measured on parallelized periapical radiographs at surgery and at 24 months using Rhinoceros® software scaled to the known implant platform width.

Key conclusion: tissue-level implants with a convergent neck exhibit significantly less peri-implant bone loss than bone-level implants regardless of the prosthesis type, positioning this implant design as favorable for long-term peri-implant tissue stability.

## Key Contributions

1. **Tissue-level convergent neck design preserves bone**: MBL of 0.31 ± 0.48 mm (TL) vs 0.97 ± 0.91 mm (BL) — a 3-fold difference, p < 0.001. Both values are below the historical 1.5 mm threshold for physiological bone loss, but TL is substantially lower.

2. **Prosthesis type (crown vs FPP) does not significantly influence MBL**: Overall MBL was 0.55 mm (crowns) vs 0.73 mm (FPP), p = 0.338. This held true within both implant groups. Crowns showed a non-significant tendency toward lower MBL, possibly from better emergence profiles and hygiene.

3. **Arch location (maxilla vs mandible) does not affect MBL**: 0.46 mm vs 0.75 mm, p = 0.079 — non-significant despite the numerical difference.

4. **Anterior position in FPP is a risk factor only for bone-level implants**: Bone-level anterior FPP implants lost 1.44 ± 1.32 mm vs 0.74 ± 0.47 mm posteriorly (p = 0.001). Tissue-level FPP showed no positional effect (0.40 vs 0.35 mm, p = 0.606). The convergent neck design appears to buffer positional biomechanical stress.

5. **Biological rationale**: The convergent transmucosal neck displaces the microgap supragingivally away from the bone crest, preventing bacterial filtration to crestal bone. Additionally, the hyperbolic profile creates more coronal space for peri-implant collagen fibers (following BOPT philosophy), promoting tissue stability.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Retrospective comparative study; single surgeon (15 years experience); single institution (University of Valencia Dental Clinic)
- **Sample**: 53 patients; 120 implants; 4 subgroups (n=30 each): TSSC, TFPP, BSSC, BFPP
- **Implants**: PRAMA RF (tissue-level, 2.8 mm machined convergent neck, internal hex 3.4 mm) vs KOHNO (bone-level, internal hex 3.8 mm, juxta-osseous peripheral ring)
- **Prosthetics**: Screw-retained only; single crowns (1 implant) or 3-unit FPP (2 implants)
- **Follow-up**: 24 months minimum post-loading; radiographs at surgery, loading, 1 year, 2 years
- **Radiographic method**: Rinn paralleling positioners; Carestream RVG digital; Rhinoceros® measurement software; reference = platform edge (BL) or machined neck end (TL); mesial + distal MBL measured by single observer
- **Statistics**: Wald chi-square test; GEE for interactions; Bonferroni for multiple comparisons; α = 0.05
