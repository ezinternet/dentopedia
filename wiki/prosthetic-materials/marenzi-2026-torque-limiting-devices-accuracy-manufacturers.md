## One-line Summary

In vitro study comparing 3 manufacturers' mechanical torque wrenches found all under-deliver torque by 11–29.3%, but all achieve zero microgap at the implant-abutment interface when set to 25 Ncm.

## 한줄요약

기계식 토크 렌치(Mechanical Torque Wrench) 3종 비교 in vitro 연구: 모든 장치 설정값 대비 11–29.3% 낮은 토크 전달, 그러나 25 Ncm 설정 시 전 제조사에서 미세간극(Microgap) 無.

## Summary

This in vitro study systematically evaluated the torque accuracy of three commercial mechanical torque-limiting devices (Biotech Dental A, Biosafin B, Global D C) and their effect on the implant-abutment microgap. Using a universal testing machine with standardized 3D-printed supports, 5 wrenches per manufacturer were tested at 15, 20, and 25 Ncm (20 measurements each). All wrenches delivered less torque than the set value — the error range spanned 11% (A at 20 Ncm) to 29.3% (B at 15 Ncm). Wrench A was consistently and significantly more accurate than B and C. Despite under-delivery, when set to 25 Ncm, all groups demonstrated zero microgap by SEM analysis. At 20 Ncm, Biotech Dental implants (group A) showed smaller gaps than Biosafin (B) and Global D (C), suggesting that both wrench accuracy and implant connection geometry (taper angle) interact to determine interface quality.

## Key Contributions

1. **Systematic under-delivery confirmed**: All 3 mechanical torque-limiting wrenches deliver less than set torque across all tested levels (15, 20, 25 Ncm), with errors of 11–29.3%.
2. **25 Ncm is a safe clinical threshold**: Despite under-delivery, setting to 25 Ncm abolished microgap in all manufacturer groups (zero gap confirmed by SEM).
3. **Inter-manufacturer accuracy difference**: Wrench A (Biotech Dental) was significantly more accurate than B and C; B and C did not differ.
4. **Connection geometry matters at borderline torque**: At 20 Ncm where gaps appeared, the implant with the smallest taper angle (3° — Biosafin B) and largest taper angle (8° — Global D C) performed worse than the 5° group (A), suggesting taper design interacts with delivered torque.

## Methodology

- **Wrench accuracy**: Universal Testing Machine (Sun 500, Galdabini), 3D-printed PLA support, 1 mm/min displacement speed, torque = lever arm × maximum force at click.
- **Microgap**: 20 implants per group (10 at 20 Ncm, 10 at 25 Ncm); implants embedded in epoxy resin, longitudinally sectioned, SEM (FEI Quanta 250 + Zeiss Gemini 300, 600×), ImageJ measurement (3–5 points per image).
- **Implant connections**: Conical Morse taper; Biotech Dental 5°, Biosafin 3°, Global D 8°.
- **Statistics**: Paired t-test (actual vs. nominal); one-way ANOVA + Bonferroni post-hoc.

## Results

| Torque Setting | Wrench A | Wrench B | Wrench C |
|---------------|----------|----------|----------|
| 15 Ncm | 14.6 ± 0.56 Ncm | 14.1 ± 0.74 Ncm | 14.1 ± 0.72 Ncm |
| 20 Ncm | 19.6 ± 0.48 Ncm | 18.9 ± 0.64 Ncm | 19.0 ± 0.71 Ncm |
| 25 Ncm | 24.0 ± 0.77 Ncm | 23.7 ± 0.83 Ncm | 23.7 ± 0.94 Ncm |

- All deviations from nominal were statistically significant (p<0.0001).
- Wrench A significantly more accurate than B and C at all levels (p<0.001–0.047).
- **Microgap at 25 Ncm**: Zero gap in all groups (all manufacturers).
- **Microgap at 20 Ncm**: Group A < Group B and C (p<0.05).
