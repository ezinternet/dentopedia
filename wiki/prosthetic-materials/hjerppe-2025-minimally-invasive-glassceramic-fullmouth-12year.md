## 한줄요약
전악 최소침습 유리세라믹 수복 전향적코호트(n=20, 439개 수복물, 최대 12.4년): 5년 생존율 96.6%(수복물)/86.4%(환자); 탈락 및 파절 3%; 환자만족도 심미 9.5/기능 9.3

## Summary
Prospective cohort study (n=20 patients, 439 restorations, up to 12.4-year follow-up) of minimally invasive glass-ceramic full-mouth rehabilitation. 5-year restoration survival 96.6% and patient-level survival 86.4% (Kaplan-Meier). Lithium disilicate comprised 79.7% of restorations. Technical complications 3%; biological complications 4.3%. High patient satisfaction (VAS esthetics 9.5, function 9.3).

## Key Contributions
- Longest prospective cohort for minimally invasive glass-ceramic full-mouth rehabilitation in this wiki
- 5-year restoration survival: 96.6%; patient-level: 86.4% (KM)
- 439 restorations: 79.7% lithium disilicate, 17.5% feldspathic, 2.7% leucite
- Design distribution: 35.3% partial coverage, 31.2% full-contour, 27.8% veneers
- Technical complications 3%: chipping (n=5), retention loss (n=7)
- Biological complications 4.3%
- Patient VAS: esthetics 9.5/10, function 9.3/10

## Methodology
Prospective cohort. n=20 patients requiring full-mouth rehabilitation. Minimally invasive glass-ceramic restorations (lithium disilicate, feldspathic porcelain, leucite). Partial and full-coverage designs, veneers, onlays, inlays. Follow-up: mean 4.5 years, max 12.4 years. Outcomes: survival, complications (technical/biological), patient-reported outcomes (VAS). Kaplan-Meier for time-to-event; FDI/USPHS criteria.

## Results
- 5-year restoration survival: 96.6%; patient survival: 86.4%
- Chipping (n=5): main technical complication
- Retention loss (n=7): second most common complication
- Biological complications: caries, periodontal issues (4.3%)
- VAS esthetics: 9.5/10; function: 9.3/10
- Lithium disilicate: best-performing subgroup within cohort
