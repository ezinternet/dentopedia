Systematic review of 24 clinical studies on zirconia bonding to tooth/implant abutments: APA or TBS APA surface pretreatment + MDP primer + MDP/phosphoric-ester resin cement is the most durable bonding protocol; resin cement type is a secondary factor.

## Summary

Alammar and Att (2022) conducted a systematic review of all clinical studies on zirconia restorations bonded to tooth or implant abutments, published up to September 2020. From 2,856 initially identified studies, 24 met inclusion criteria. The heterogeneity of study designs, surface pretreatment methods, primers, resin cements, abutment types, and restoration designs precluded meta-analysis.

The key clinical finding is that airborne-particle abrasion (APA) and tribochemical silica coating APA (TBS APA) consistently yielded positive clinical bonding outcomes across all tooth surface types. Combining either pretreatment method with an MDP monomer-based primer and an MDP or phosphoric ester monomer-based resin cement produced durable, long-term bonding in clinical settings. Notably, skipping the surface pretreatment step did not negatively affect clinical outcomes for certain zirconia restoration types on most tooth or implant abutment surfaces, suggesting that restoration design and geometry may partially compensate for reduced chemical adhesion in some scenarios. Resin cement type was identified as a less influential variable than surface pretreatment and primer selection.

## Key Contributions

- First systematic review focused specifically on **clinical** (not laboratory) bonding durability of zirconia restorations to tooth and implant abutments (Part II companion to a laboratory SR)
- Establishes **APA + MDP primer + MDP/phosphoric-ester cement** as the evidence-based bonding protocol for all tooth surface types
- Identifies **resin cement type** as less critical than surface pretreatment method and primer chemistry
- Clarifies that surface pretreatment omission may be clinically acceptable for **certain restoration types** on most abutment surfaces — providing a nuanced, clinically actionable conclusion
- Offers practical clinical guidelines stratified by abutment type (tooth vs. implant) and zirconia restoration design

## Methodology

- **Study type**: Systematic review (PRISMA-guided search, no meta-analysis)
- **Search**: English-language dental journals + MeSH terms + free text; up to September 2020
- **Studies identified**: 2,856 → 24 included after screening
- **Variables extracted**: surface pretreatment method, primer type, resin cement type, abutment type (tooth vs. implant), zirconia restoration type and design, clinical outcomes
- **Analysis**: Narrative synthesis due to high data heterogeneity

## Results

| Bonding variable | Clinical finding |
|---|---|
| Surface pretreatment | APA and TBS APA both yield positive outcomes on all tooth surface types |
| Primer | MDP monomer-based primer chemically activates APA/TBS APA surfaces; recommended |
| Resin cement | MDP or phosphoric ester monomer-based cement; **cement type less influential** than pretreatment + primer |
| No surface pretreatment | Did not compromise outcomes for **certain** zirconia restoration types on most abutment surfaces |
| Abutment type | Clinical data covers both tooth abutments and implant abutments; protocol remains consistent |

**Key clinical guideline**: APA or TBS APA → MDP primer → MDP-based or phosphoric-ester-based resin cement = durable zirconia bonding protocol for all tooth abutment types.

**Note on omitting pretreatment**: This finding applies specifically to certain zirconia restoration designs (likely full-coverage crowns with macromechanical retention geometry) and should not be generalized to all situations.
