## One-line Summary

In vitro study (n=90, external hex, ±ISO cyclic loading): PTFE tape consistently lowers preload and reduces RTV under static conditions, but uniquely raises RTV above control and GapSeal (31.50 vs 26.00 Ncm) after 300,000-cycle oral simulation — GapSeal is superior without loading, PTFE superior after.

## 한줄요약

체외 연구(n=90, 외부 헥스, ISO 사이클 하중 유무): PTFE 테이프는 모든 조건에서 예압을 감소시키고 비하중 시 제거토크(RTV)도 낮았으나, 30만 사이클 ISO 하중 후에는 PTFE의 RTV(31.50 Ncm)가 대조군(26.00)과 GapSeal(27.44)을 역전 초과함 — 하중 없을 땐 GapSeal, 구강 환경 모사 후엔 PTFE가 우세.

## Summary

This in vitro study (Coelho 2025) is the direct follow-up to Coelho 2024, adding ISO 14801-compliant cyclic loading (CL: 300,000 cycles, 20–200 N, 2 Hz, 37 °C distilled water) to the same PTFE vs GapSeal® screw coating comparison. Ninety external hex implant-abutment complexes (DIUIMPLANT) were randomized across 6 subgroups (3 coatings × 2 loading conditions, n=15 each). All screws tightened to 30 Ncm and retightened at 10 min. Preload and RTV were measured by dynamometer; micro-CT confirmed absence of microgaps; SEM documented coating status post-loosening.

Key finding: **Loading condition reverses PTFE's effect on RTV.** Without CL, PTFE tape reduced RTV to 15.30 Ncm versus 27.98 Ncm (control) and 28.48 Ncm (GapSeal). After CL, PTFE yielded the highest RTV at 31.50 Ncm versus 26.00 (control) and 27.44 (GapSeal). PTFE consistently lowered preload in both conditions. GapSeal showed no significant preload effect in either condition and maintained superior RTV without CL.

The null hypothesis (coatings do not affect preload or RTV) was rejected for PTFE in all conditions and for GapSeal in RTV comparisons (nCL only). Micro-CT confirmed no microgap creation from either coating. SEM showed PTFE fragments accumulate in thread valleys post-CL — the proposed mechanism for mechanical interlocking that increases RTV after loading.

## Key Contributions

- **RTV reversal effect**: PTFE's clinically problematic low RTV (nCL) flips to the highest RTV under oral-environment cyclic loading — a clinically important and previously unreported phenomenon.
- **GapSeal stability**: Maintains high RTV without CL and no microgap creation; appropriate for non-parafunctional or early-loading scenarios.
- **Preload caveat**: Both coatings leave preload ≤ control; PTFE significantly reduces preload in all conditions (30.29–29.92 Ncm vs 31.72–30.95 Ncm for control).
- **Micro-CT safety**: Neither coating introduces microgaps at the implant–abutment interface.
- **SEM insight**: Fragmented PTFE debris redistributes into thread inner surfaces post-CL, potentially acting as a mechanical "lock."

## Methodology

- **Implants**: External hex, 11.5 mm × 4.0 mm (DIUIMPLANT, Busan, Korea)
- **Abutments**: Straight hex, sleeve 1 mm, length 7 mm; screw: Torx 1.7 titanium
- **Coatings**: (1) No coating (control); (2) GapSeal® injected into implant internal compartment; (3) PTFE tape wrapped twice around screw (0.075 mm × 12 mm, MIARCO)
- **CL protocol**: ISO 14801:2016 — 30° inclination, 20–200 N sinusoidal, 2 Hz, 300,000 cycles, 37 °C distilled water
- **Outcomes**: Screw preload (dynamometer), RTV (dynamometer post-nCL or post-CL), microgap presence (Micro-CT, n=2/group, nCL), coating status (SEM, n=2/group/condition)
- **Stats**: One-way ANOVA, Tukey HSD, η² effect size; significance α=0.05

## Results

### Non-Cyclic Loading (nCL)

| | Control | GapSeal | PTFE |
|---|---|---|---|
| Preload (Ncm) | 30.95±1.00 | **31.19±1.22** | 29.92±0.76* |
| RTV (Ncm) | 27.98±1.20 | **28.48±1.47** | 15.30±1.21*** |

*p<0.05 vs GapSeal/Control; ***p<0.001 vs both. Effect size: preload η²=0.24, RTV η²=0.94 (large)

### Cyclic Loading (CL)

| | Control | GapSeal | PTFE |
|---|---|---|---|
| Preload (Ncm) | **31.72±1.16** | 31.42±0.98 | 30.29±0.83* |
| RTV (Ncm) | 26.00±2.00 | 27.44±3.76 | **31.50±0.80*** |

*p=0.009; ***p<0.001 vs both. Effect size: preload η²=0.29, RTV η²=0.48 (large)

**Micro-CT**: No microgaps in any group (nCL, n=6 samples).  
**SEM**: PTFE heavily fragmented post-CL; fragments in thread valleys. GapSeal present post-CL but less uniform than nCL.
