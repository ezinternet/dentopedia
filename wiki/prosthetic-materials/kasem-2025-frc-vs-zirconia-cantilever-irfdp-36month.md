## One-line Summary
RCT (n=40 IR-FDPs, 36-month follow-up) comparing monolithic zirconia vs fiber-reinforced composite (FRC) cantilever inlay-retained FDPs for missing mandibular premolar: overall survival 97.5% and success 96.6%, with no significant difference between materials (p>0.05) — both viable for minimally invasive posterior single-tooth replacement.

## Summary
RCT (n=40 IR-FDPs, 36-month follow-up) comparing monolithic zirconia vs fiber-reinforced composite (FRC) cantilever inlay-retained FDPs. Overall survival 97.5%, success 96.6%. No statistically significant difference between materials (p>0.05). Both lingual and occlusal coverage designs showed satisfactory results. Provides evidence for conservative treatment of single mandibular posterior tooth loss.

## Key Contributions
- Overall survival: 97.5%; success: 96.6% at 36 months
- No significant difference: zirconia vs FRC (p>0.05)
- One zirconia OC fracture at connector (30mo); one Zr debond (10mo, re-bonded)
- One FRC endo complication (12mo)
- Both lingual (LC) and occlusal (OC) coverage designs: satisfactory
- Conservative, minimally invasive alternative for mandibular premolar replacement

## Methodology
RCT (n=32 patients, 40 cantilever IR-FDPs). Two ceramic materials: monolithic zirconia and FRC. Two retainer coverage designs: lingual (LC) and occlusal (OC). Replacement of missing mandibular second premolar. 36-month follow-up. Modified FDI criteria (5 categories). Kaplan-Meier and log-rank test.

## Results
- Survival: 97.5%; success: 96.6% at 36 months
- Material comparison: no significant difference (p>0.05)
- One Zr-OC connector fracture (month 30)
- One Zr-LC debond (month 10, successfully re-bonded)
- One FRC-LC endo complication (month 12)
- Coverage design: no significant impact on outcomes
