## One-line Summary
Systematic review (12 studies) comparing screw- vs cement-retained implant-supported reconstructions: no significant difference in survival rate, but cement-retained shows more biological complications (peri-implantitis, cement remnants) while screw-retained shows more mechanical complications (screw loosening/fracture) — selection should be driven by complication profile, with screw retention favored for retrievability.

## Summary
Systematic review of 12 studies comparing screw-retained (SR) vs cement-retained (CR) implant-supported reconstructions. **No significant overall difference in survival rate.** The key clinical differentiator is the **type** of complication: CR = more biological complications; SR = more mechanical complications. Retrievability strongly favors SR.

## Key Findings

| Domain | Winner | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| Survival rate | **Equal** | No significant difference |
| Biological complications | **SR better** | CR: peri-implantitis, cement remnants, peri-implant mucositis |
| Mechanical complications | **CR better** | SR: screw loosening, screw fracture |
| Retrievability | **SR clearly better** | Easier re-intervention, repair |
| Marginal bone loss | **SR trend** | Lower MBL trend in SR (not always significant) |

## Complication Profile

**Cement-retained biological complications:**
- Excess cement remnants → subgingival inflammation
- Peri-implant mucositis and peri-implantitis rates higher
- Difficult to detect excess cement in deep implants

**Screw-retained mechanical complications:**
- Screw loosening — most common; manageable with re-torquing
- Screw fracture — rare but more complex to manage
- Access hole affects esthetic zone options

## Clinical Takeaway
- **Overall survival equivalence** — both are valid clinical options
- Choose based on **which complication type is riskier for this patient/site**:
  - High peri-implant disease risk → favor SR
  - Esthetic zone with access hole issue → CR with retrievable cement protocol
- Retrievability of SR is underrated — crucial for complex prosthetics and long-term maintenance
- **Subgingival CR placement** dramatically increases biological complication risk — avoid
