## Summary
The German S3 clinical guideline (highest evidence level in the German AWMF system) on implant-supported all-ceramic restorations, developed by the German Association of Oral Implantology (DGI) with DGZMK. Based on systematic literature review + formal expert consensus using AGREE II and GRADE. The guideline covers three indication categories: single crowns, short-span FDPs (3–4 units), and full-arch restorations. For **single crowns**, lithium disilicate, silicate ceramics, and all zirconia generations show acceptable ~96–97% 3-year survival. For **FDPs and full-arch**, only 3Y-TZP zirconia has sufficient clinical evidence. Polymer-infiltrated ceramics are explicitly not recommended. Translucent (high-Y) zirconia generations, while more esthetic, lack long-term implant validation.

## Key Contributions
- Highest-level German guideline (S3) specifically for all-ceramic implant-supported restorations
- Three-part structure matching clinical indications: single crown, FDP, full-arch
- Explicit "shall/should/can" recommendation strengths based on GRADE evidence quality
- First guideline to explicitly flag polymer-infiltrated ceramics as unsuitable for implant crowns
- Addresses design features (monolithic, micro-veneering) to reduce chipping risk
- Emphasizes patient education where evidence is limited (newer materials, full-arch cases)

## Methodology
- **Guideline type**: S3 (evidence + formal consensus); AWMF regulatory framework
- **Methodology instrument**: AGREE II
- **Evidence synthesis**: Systematic literature review with predefined inclusion criteria + GRADE evidence rating
- **Consensus**: Strong consensus on every recommendation; group discussion
- **Recommendation grades**: A ⇑⇑ ("shall"), B ⇑ ("should"), 0 ⇔ ("can")
- **Three sections**: Part A (single crowns), Part B (short-span FDPs), Part C (full-arch)

## Results
**Part A — Single crowns:**
| Material | 3-Year Survival | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Lithium disilicate | ~96–97% | Recommended |
| Silicate ceramics | ~96–97% | Recommended |
| All zirconia generations (incl. translucent) | ~96–97% | Recommended (with caveats for translucent) |
| Polymer-infiltrated ceramics | Inferior | NOT recommended |

**Part B — Short-span FDPs (3–4 units):**
- 3Y-TZP zirconia only — sufficient clinical evidence
- Micro-veneering and monolithic designs preferred (reduce chipping)

**Part C — Full-arch restorations:**
- 3Y-TZP zirconia only — adequate evidence
- Patient education critical; limited evidence for newer materials
- Interdisciplinary collaboration required

**Design principles applicable to all indications:**
- Adhere to bonding protocols for ceramic-to-implant-abutment interface
- Monolithic or micro-veneered designs over full-veneered to minimize chipping
- Highly translucent zirconia (4Y, 5Y-TZP): increased esthetics but reduced strength; long-term implant data lacking — use with caution
