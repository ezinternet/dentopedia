## One-line Summary

In vitro synchrotron microtomography of 4 IAC designs under static loading (30–200 N) found pre-load microgaps in all systems (0.15–9 μm) expanding to 40.5 μm under load; small-angle conical friction-fit (<12°) caused inward abutment displacement deforming the outer implant wall, while large-angle butt-joint (~45°) showed different force distribution without outer-wall deformation.

## 한줄요약

4종 임플란트 연결부(3종 소원추각 friction-fit <12°, 1종 대원추각 butt-joint ~45°)의 방사광 마이크로단층촬영 정하중 시험에서 하중 전부터 모든 시스템에 마이크로갭(0.15–9 μm)이 존재했고 하중 시 최대 40.5 μm로 증가했으며, 소원추각 연결부는 임플란트 외벽 변형을 유발하는 반면 대원추각 butt-joint는 다른 힘 분산 패턴을 보였다.

## Summary

Angermair et al. (2024) used synchrotron-based phase-contrast radioscopy (방사광 위상차 방사선투시, SRX) at the European Synchrotron Radiation Facility (ESRF), Grenoble to examine microgap formation and abutment displacement mode in four commercial implant systems under static off-axis loading. Three systems featured conical friction-fit connections with cone angles <12° (Medentika, Medentis, NobelActive); one featured an angulated butt-joint connection at approximately 45° (BEGO Semados). The imaging resolution was 0.1 μm — far exceeding clinical X-ray (~20 μm detectability limit).

Key finding: all four systems exhibited microgaps before any loading was applied (0.15–9 μm). Under increasing load (30 N, 100 N at 90°; 200 N at 30°), microgaps expanded — reaching up to 40.5 μm at 100 N/90°. The critical mechanical distinction was in the mode of abutment displacement: small-angle conical systems showed inward axial seating of the abutment that caused measurable deformation of the outer implant shoulder wall. The large-angle butt-joint distributed forces differently without this outer-wall deformation. Authors conclude that IAC design — specifically cone angle — critically determines force distribution on the implant wall, with potential implications for peri-implant bone stability.

## Key Contributions

1. **Pre-load microgap ubiquity**: No system was microgap-free even before any mechanical force was applied, challenging assumptions about sealed conical interfaces.
2. **Cone-angle-dependent displacement mode**: Small-angle (<12°) conical connections seat deeper under load (inward displacement) → outer implant wall deformation. Large-angle (~45°) butt-joint shows different stress pathway.
3. **First SRX comparison including butt-joint**: Prior SRX literature focused exclusively on conical friction-fit; this is the first study to include a large-angle butt-joint connection at μm-resolution.
4. **Implant-wall deformation implication**: Outer implant shoulder deformation under occlusal load may transmit stress to crestal bone, potentially contributing to marginal bone loss.

## Methodology

- **Study type**: In vitro mechanical + imaging (synchrotron)
- **Systems**: Medentika Microcone, Medentis ICX-Templant, NobelActive Internal RP (all conical <12°); BEGO Semados (angulated butt-joint ~45°)
- **Specimen count**: n=2 per system
- **Loading**: ISO 14801:2008 — 30 N/90°, 100 N/90°, 200 N/30° (static, off-axis)
- **Imaging method**: Synchrotron phase-contrast radioscopy (PCR) + microtomography, ESRF Grenoble; 0.1 μm accuracy via Fresnel propagation phase contrast
- **Screw torque**: 30 Ncm (MA/MS/BE), 35 Ncm (NO) per manufacturer specs
- **Embedding**: methylmethacrylate in 15 mm brass cylinder

## Results

| Condition | Microgap range |
|---|---|
| Pre-load (all systems) | 0.15–9 μm |
| 30 N / 90° | Moderate increase |
| 100 N / 90° | Up to 40.5 μm |
| 200 N / 30° | Off-axis expansion |

- Conical <12° systems: abutment settles axially inward under load → outward bulging of implant wall at shoulder level.
- Butt-joint ~45° system: different force distribution; no comparable outer-wall deformation observed.
- Microgap magnitude correlates with both load magnitude and loading angle.
- Intra-system variation was present (n=2 per group limits statistical analysis).
