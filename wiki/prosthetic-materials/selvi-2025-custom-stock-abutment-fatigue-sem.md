## One-line Summary

In-vitro fatigue study (n=44, ISO-14801, 2 million cycles) of internal conical-hex assemblies: CAD-CAM custom abutments had lower baseline removal torque than stock abutments but showed equivalent preload maintenance at 2 million cycles; greatest preload loss occurs before 1 million cycles, recommending early screw retightening.

## 한줄요약

체외 피로 시험(n=44, ISO-14801, 200만 사이클)에서 원추형-헥스 연결 CAD-CAM 맞춤 어버트먼트(custom abutment)는 기성 어버트먼트(stock abutment)보다 기저 제거 토크값(Removal Torque Value, RTV)이 낮았으나 200만 사이클 후 예압(preload) 유지는 동등하였으며, 예압 소실의 대부분은 50만 사이클 이전에 발생하므로 초기 어버트먼트 나사 재조임이 권장된다.

## Summary

Selvi et al. (2025) compared the fatigue performance of CAD-CAM custom abutments (CA) and stock abutments (SA) using internal conical-hex connections over 2 million cycles per ISO-14801. Key findings:

- **Baseline**: Stock abutments had higher RTVs than custom abutments at initial tightening.
- **Progressive loss**: Both groups showed progressive RTV decline up to 1 million cycles, with CA values significantly lower.
- **Recovery plateau**: From 1–2 million cycles, a partial RTV recovery occurred with no significant between-group difference, confirming mechanical equivalence at clinical loading durations.
- **Critical window**: The greatest preload loss occurs between 50,000 and 1 million cycles — the early post-insertion period is the highest-risk window.
- **SEM**: Stock abutments showed more extensive surface wear; custom abutments showed localized adaptations only. Thread integrity was maintained in all specimens.
- **Re-tightening**: Post-fatigue retightening restored RTVs in both groups.

**Clinical implication**: Both abutment types are mechanically suitable for functional loading. Early screw retightening (within the first weeks post-insertion) is warranted to compensate for initial preload loss.

## Key Contributions

1. First study to compare custom vs. stock conical-hex abutments across 5 cycle checkpoints up to 2 million cycles with concurrent SEM analysis.
2. Established that the 50,000–1,000,000 cycle interval is the critical preload-loss window regardless of abutment type.
3. SEM evidence that custom abutments undergo localized surface adaptation (not generalized wear) while thread integrity is preserved.
4. Supported post-fatigue retightening as an effective rescue strategy for preload restoration.

## Methodology

- **Design**: In-vitro comparative, n=44 (SA group n=22, CA group n=22)
- **Connection**: Internal conical-hex implant-abutment
- **Abutments**: CA = CAD-CAM milled; SA = stock/prefabricated
- **Fatigue**: ISO-14801 cyclic loading
- **RTV measurements**: Baseline, 50,000; 1×10⁶; 1.5×10⁶; 2×10⁶ cycles; post-fatigue retightening
- **SEM**: Baseline, 1×10⁶, 2×10⁶ cycles

## Results

| Time point | SA vs CA |
|---|---|
| Baseline | SA significantly higher RTV |
| 50,000 – 1×10⁶ cycles | Both decrease; CA significantly lower |
| 1.5×10⁶ – 2×10⁶ cycles | Partial recovery; no significant difference |
| Post re-tightening | RTV restored in both groups |
| SEM – SA | Extensive surface wear |
| SEM – CA | Localized adaptation; threads intact |
