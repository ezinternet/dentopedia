## 한줄요약
Full-ceramic 레진본드 보철물(RBFDP) 14편 SR: 3–10년 생존율 76–100%, cantilever (단일 abutment) 디자인이 fixed-fixed보다 우세, IPS e.max ZirCAD·In-Ceram zirconia·CAD/CAM 지르코니아가 주류 framework, debonding이 최다 실패 양상.

## Summary
SR of 14 studies (2010–2020 English) on full-ceramic resin-bonded fixed dental prostheses (RBFDPs). Survival rate 76–100% over 3–10 years follow-up. Cantilever single-abutment design outperformed two-abutment fixed designs (less debonding from differential abutment movement). Dominant framework materials: IPS e.max ZirCAD, In-Ceram alumina, zirconia CAD/CAM. In-Ceram alumina showed lower survival (85.3–94.8%) than zirconia variants. Retentive features (proximal box, groove, pinhole) improved survival. Standard surface treatment: air abrasion + silanization or HF etching. Adhesive resin cements dominant. Debonding > framework fracture in failure modes.

## Key Contributions
- Synthesizes design and material decisions for full-ceramic RBFDP
- Quantifies survival range and identifies best-performing materials
- Confirms cantilever single-abutment as preferred design
- Provides actionable preparation guidance: include retentive aids

## Methodology
- **Design**: SR
- **Databases**: WoS, MEDLINE, Scopus, Embase, Cochrane, Google Scholar
- **Period**: English studies 2010–2020
- **Included**: 14 studies
- **Variables**: design (cantilever vs 2-abutment), preparation features, framework material, surface treatment, cement type, survival, failure mode

## Results
**Survival (3–10y follow-up)**:
- All-ceramic RBFDPs overall: 76–100%
- In-Ceram alumina: 85.3–94.8% (lower)
- In-Ceram zirconia, IPS e.max ZirCAD: higher

**Design**: cantilever single-abutment > 2-abutment fixed-fixed
**Preparation aids improving survival**: proximal box, groove, pinhole
**Surface treatment**: air abrasion + silane, or HF etching (glass ceramics)
**Cement**: adhesive resin cement (dominant)
**Failure modes**: debonding (most common) > framework fracture

## Clinical Implications
- For anterior single-tooth replacement in non-implant candidates, cantilever full-ceramic RBFDP is a defensible conservative option
- Use zirconia framework (IPS e.max ZirCAD or In-Ceram zirconia) over In-Ceram alumina
- Include retentive features (box/groove/pinhole) in abutment preparation
- Cantilever design preferred — avoids differential abutment movement that drives debonding
- Adhesive resin cement with appropriate surface treatment (air abrasion + silane)
