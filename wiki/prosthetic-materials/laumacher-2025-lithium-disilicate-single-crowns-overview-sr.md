## One-line Summary
Umbrella review of systematic reviews on tooth- and implant-supported lithium disilicate (LD) single crowns: 5-year survival 95–98% for tooth-supported LD; monolithic LD eliminates veneering-related chipping, and implant-supported LD evidence is growing with comparable outcomes to conventional ceramics.

## Summary
Umbrella review synthesizing systematic reviews on clinical outcomes of tooth- and implant-supported lithium disilicate single crowns.

## Key Contributions
- Tooth-supported LD single crowns: 5-year survival 95–98% across studies
- Implant-supported LD crowns: lower chipping than veneered options; evidence growing
- Monolithic LD: mechanically superior to feldspathic veneered ceramics

## Methodology
Systematic search of existing systematic reviews.

## Results
LD crowns perform excellently at 5 years. Monolithic single-layer LD eliminates veneering-related chipping. Implant-supported LD: comparable outcomes to conventional ceramics.
