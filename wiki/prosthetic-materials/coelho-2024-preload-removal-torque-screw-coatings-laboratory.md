## One-line Summary

In vitro study (n=45, external hex implants) showed PTFE tape significantly reduced screw preload (28.88 vs 30.41 N·cm control, p<0.05), while GapSeal silicone gel maintained preload and increased removal torque (28.67 vs 26.59 N·cm, p<0.001); GapSeal recommended, PTFE contraindicated for preventing screw loosening.

## Summary

This laboratory study compared two chairside screw coating materials — GapSeal silicone sealing gel and PTFE (polytetrafluoroethylene) tape — against an uncoated control for their effect on implant-abutment screw preload and removal torque value (RTV). Forty-five external hexagonal implant-abutment-screw complexes (DIUIMPLANT, Korea) were equally divided into three groups (n=15). Screws were torqued to 30 N·cm (manufacturer recommendation) and preload was recorded with a high-precision force gauge (1000 Hz); RTV was measured immediately after tightening.

Key finding: PTFE tape acts as a lubricant, reducing preload by ~5% (p=0.004, η²=0.23) — a clinically significant reduction since preload is the primary mechanism preventing screw loosening. In contrast, GapSeal did not reduce preload (NS vs control) but substantially increased RTV by 2.1 N·cm above control (p<0.001, η²=0.83), likely through viscous resistance to rotational displacement.

ANCOVA adjusting RTV for baseline preload confirmed group differences remained significant (η²p=0.90), with GapSeal maintaining the highest adjusted RTV (28.70 N·cm) and PTFE the lowest (17.20 N·cm).

Clinical implication: at chairside, GapSeal can be applied to implant internal spaces before seating the abutment screw to reduce loosening risk without compromising the preload. PTFE tape should be avoided despite its common use, as it reduces the very clamping force it is meant to protect.

## Key Contributions

1. **PTFE lubricates, it does not lock**: PTFE tape reduces preload (p=0.004) — directly undermining screw stability. This challenges the clinical practice of wrapping PTFE around abutment screws.
2. **GapSeal dual benefit**: maintains preload (no significant loss) AND increases RTV (p<0.001) — the only tested material to improve both parameters simultaneously.
3. **Strong preload–RTV correlation**: positive association confirmed (ANCOVA preload effect p=0.068, moderate η²p=0.08), consistent with Siamos et al.'s foundational work.
4. **Large effect sizes** (η²=0.83 for RTV model; R²=88.3%) indicate coating choice is the dominant determinant of RTV, not chance variation.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Bench/laboratory comparative study
- **Sample**: 45 external hexagonal implants (4.0×11.5 mm), abutments (1mm cuff, 7mm), Ti screws 1.7 Torx (DIUIMPLANT, Korea)
- **Groups** (n=15 each):
  - Control (GC): no coating
  - GapSeal (GG): silicone sealing gel (Hager & Werken, Germany) injected into implant well
  - PTFE (GP): Teflon tape wound ×2 around screw shaft
- **Tightening**: 30 N·cm via calibrated digital torque meter (iSD900, NSK); recalibrated after each measurement
- **Preload**: Centor Touch Star TH force gauge, ISO 9001:2015 certified, 1000 Hz
- **Statistics**: ANOVA → Tukey post-hoc; ANCOVA for RTV adjusted to preload; η² effect sizes; Shapiro-Wilk + Levene tests; R v4.3.0

## Results

| Parameter | Control | GapSeal | PTFE |
|---|---|---|---|
| Preload (N·cm) | 30.41 ± 1.04 | ~30.2 (NS) | 28.88 ± 1.07* |
| RTV (N·cm) | 26.59 ± 1.25 | 28.67 ± 1.47* | 16.15 ± 2.89* |
| Adj. RTV (ANCOVA) | 26.40 | 28.70* | 17.20* |

*p<0.05 vs control; all pairwise RTV comparisons also significant (p<0.05)

- 프리로드 (Preload, PL): PTFE만 유의하게 감소 (p=0.004); GapSeal은 대조군과 차이 없음
- 제거 토크 (Removal Torque Value, RTV): 세 군 모두 유의하게 다름 — GapSeal > Control > PTFE
- PTFE RTV는 대조군보다 10.4 N·cm 낮음 (39% 감소) — 임상적으로 매우 유의미
