## Summary

Goodacre, Campagni, and Aquilino review ~250 years of complete-coverage tooth preparation, emphasizing the prior 50 years of scientific data, and condense the literature into nine principles spanning mechanical, biologic, and esthetic success. The central numeric guidelines — 10-20° TOC, minimal axial height (4 mm molars / 3 mm others), and an occlusocervical-to-faciolingual ratio ≥0.4 — became the standard contemporary teaching benchmarks, displacing the historically taught (but clinically unachievable) low single-digit taper.

## Key Contributions

- **Nine scientific principles** for complete-crown preparation, organized by mechanical/biologic/esthetic success.
- **10-20° TOC** established as the clinically realistic convergence target.
- **Resistance form as a system**: prep height, base width, and taper together — captured by the occlusocervical-to-faciolingual ratio ≥0.4 — not taper alone.
- **Auxiliary resistance features** (axial grooves/boxes, preferentially proximal) prescribed when dimensions are deficient (short / over-tapered teeth).
- **Finish line**: selection by crown type/esthetics/operator; enhanced marginal fit from a specific finish-line type was **not validated**; supragingival location preferred.

## Methodology

- Narrative/critical literature review (not systematic; no meta-analysis).
- MEDLINE search plus extensive manual search; English-language articles; emphasis on scientific data of the last 50 years within a 250-year scope.

## Results

| Principle | Guideline |
|---|---|
| Total occlusal convergence (TOC) | 10-20° |
| Minimal occlusocervical (axial) height | 4 mm molars / 3 mm other teeth |
| Occlusocervical-to-faciolingual ratio | ≥0.4 |
| Line angles | Preserve facioproximal/linguoproximal; round all |
| Auxiliary resistance | Grooves/boxes (proximal preferred) when geometry deficient |
| Finish line type | Choose by crown/esthetics/ease; no proven marginal-fit advantage |
| Finish line location | Supragingival more acceptable |
| Surface | Reasonable smoothness |

## Clinical Implications [근거강함]

- The "6° taper" remains a teaching ideal; **10-20° TOC** is the achievable, defensible clinical band — consistent with Tiu 2015's documented historic drift.
- When a molar is short (<4 mm axial) or the O-C:F-L ratio falls below 0.4, do not rely on reducing taper (impractical intraorally); instead add **proximal grooves/boxes** to restore resistance.
- Finish-line choice should be driven by the restorative material, esthetics, and operator skill rather than an expectation of superior marginal fit from one geometry.
