## One-line Summary

In-vitro bench study of Bicon-type pure Morse taper (1.5°) implant-abutment specimens (≥10 per group) showing abutment subsidence ("sinking-down") rises with seating load (r=0.645) but is reduced most by greater locking depth (r=−0.807), larger locking-post diameter, and thicker implant wall, while pull-apart locking force rises with load (r=0.963), depth (r=0.607), and post diameter (r=0.372) — supporting ≥5 seating loadings and delayed final occlusal adjustment to prevent late occlusal change.

## Summary

Pure Morse taper (taper-lock) implant-abutment connections — exemplified by Bicon — hold the abutment by friction/cold welding with **no retention screw**, so the abutment can keep "sinking down" (subsidence) into the implant under occlusal load after delivery, lowering occlusal contacts over time. This bench study fabricated Bicon-mimicking specimens with a fixed 1.5° taper and varied four factors — locking depth (1/2/3 mm), locking-post diameter (2.5/3.0/3.5 mm), implant outer-wall thickness (0.15/0.30 mm), and seating load (200/300/400 N; finger pressure + 5× machine loading) — measuring both how far the abutment sank and the axial pull-apart "locking force." Subsidence was governed most by **locking depth** (deeper = less sinking; r=−0.807) and increased with seating load (r=0.645); locking force was governed most by **seating load** (r=0.963), with depth and post diameter also contributing. The clinical translation: load the abutment **≥5 times** at seating, restore provisionally with only preliminary occlusal adjustment, and defer final restoration and occlusal adjustment until the joint has settled — directly addressing the late occlusal drift characteristic of taper-lock systems.

## Key Contributions

- First quantitative bench mapping (for a pure Morse taper / taper-lock joint) of **both** retention ("locking force") and **abutment subsidence** against design + loading variables.
- **Locking depth is the dominant determinant of subsidence** (r=−0.807) and also raises retention (r=0.607) — a design lever favorable on both axes.
- **Seating load is the dominant determinant of locking force** (r=0.963) but simultaneously increases subsidence (r=0.645) — the trade-off that necessitates a clinical seating/occlusion protocol rather than a one-shot torque.
- **Implant outer-wall thickness** is uncoupled from locking force yet inversely related to subsidence (thicker wall resists elastic deformation → less sinking).
- Provides the mechanistic rationale for the taper-lock seating protocol: **≥5 seating loadings + provisional + delayed final occlusal adjustment**.

## Methodology

- Design: in-vitro mechanical (bench) experiment; custom-machined specimens modeled on the **Bicon** pure Morse taper design. Confidence: `in-vitro`.
- Fixed: locking taper **1.5°**. Variables: locking depth **1.0/2.0/3.0 mm**, locking-post diameter **2.5/3.0/3.5 mm**, implant outer-wall thickness **0.15/0.30 mm**, loading force **200/300/400 N**.
- ≥10 specimens per group, universal testing machine; identical loading = **finger pressure + specified load × 5**.
- Subsidence = change in total implant-abutment height across before-finger-pressure / after-finger-pressure / after-5×-loading states.
- Locking force = axial separation (subluxation) force on pull-apart.
- Analysis: correlation coefficients per variable vs each outcome.

## Results

**Locking force (pull-apart) — positive correlations:**
- Loading force **r=0.963** (strongest) · Locking depth **r=0.607** · Locking-post diameter **r=0.372** · Outer-wall thickness — **no significant correlation**.

**Abutment subsidence:**
- ↑ with loading force **r=0.645**
- ↓ with locking depth **r=−0.807** (strongest determinant)
- ↓ with locking-post diameter **r=−0.280**
- ↓ with thicker implant outer wall (inverse; r not reported in abstract).

**Clinical protocol (authors' conclusion):** increasing locking depth, post diameter, and the magnitude/number of seating loadings raises locking force and reduces loosening/detachment; to prevent subsidence-driven late occlusal change, **load the abutment ≥5 times at seating**, use a provisional with only preliminary occlusal adjustment early, and perform final restoration + occlusal adjustment after a period of in-mouth use.
