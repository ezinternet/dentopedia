## 한줄요약
SR+MA(RCT/크로스오버 6편, 모두 저비뚤림): 하악 임플란트 오버덴쳐에서 **볼이 로케이터·자석보다 보철 합병증 유의하게 적음(RR 0.55, p=0.03)**; 변연골소실·탐침깊이는 어태치먼트별 차이 없음.

## Summary
For mandibular implant-supported overdentures (OD), the attachment system is one of the most clinically actionable choices. This 2021 PRISMA systematic review and meta-analysis included only RCTs and crossover trials from 2010–2020 with >1-year follow-up; six studies met inclusion and all were low risk of bias. The meta-analysis showed that **ball attachments incurred significantly fewer prosthodontic complications and maintenance events than locator attachments** (RR 0.55, 95% CI, p=0.03), and a similar pattern against magnets.

Peri-implant tissue outcomes (probing depth, marginal bone loss) were equivalent across ball, magnet, and locator — meaning the attachment choice influences how often the prosthetic side needs service, not how the implant–bone interface holds up. Retention and patient satisfaction were reviewed narratively due to sparse data: magnet retention was the lowest, while ball and locator offered acceptable retention and comparable satisfaction.

## Key Contributions
- Meta-analytical confirmation that **ball < locator in complication and maintenance frequency** in mandibular OD.
- Demonstrates **peri-implant tissue is attachment-agnostic** under standard care.
- Highlights that "best attachment" is a maintenance question, not an implant survival question.

## Methodology
- Databases: PubMed, Cochrane Central, ScienceDirect; manual + reference search.
- Inclusion: RCT or crossover clinical trial, 2010–2020, >1-year follow-up.
- 6 studies retained (all low RoB by Cochrane tool).
- Meta-analysis: RevMan 5.4.

## Results
| Comparison | Outcome | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Ball vs Locator | Prosthodontic complications / maintenance | Ball **fewer** (RR 0.55, p=0.03) |
| Ball vs Magnet | Prosthodontic complications | Ball **fewer** (significant) |
| Ball vs Locator vs Magnet | Probing depth | NS |
| Ball vs Locator vs Magnet | MBL | NS |
| All vs All | Retention | Magnet lowest; ball/locator acceptable |
| All vs All | Patient satisfaction | Narrative; ball ≈ locator |

Clinical implication: in mandibular OD, **ball-and-O-ring is a low-maintenance first choice**; locator is interchangeable for divergent implants or limited interarch space, accepting more matrix changes; magnets are reserved for special indications (low manual dexterity, severe ridge atrophy).
