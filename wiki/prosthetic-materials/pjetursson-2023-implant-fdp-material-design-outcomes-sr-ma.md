## Summary

Pjetursson et al. (2023) conducted a SR+MA (32 studies, 42 arms) evaluating how prosthetic material and design influence outcomes for **implant-supported multi-unit fixed dental prostheses (iFDPs) in the posterior area**. Materials compared: porcelain-fused-to-metal (PFM), veneered zirconia, monolithic/micro-veneered zirconia, and lithium disilicate. Designs compared: FDPs with pontic vs implant splinted crowns (iSC).

Using robust Poisson regression (the Pjetursson group's established model), **3-year survival rates were statistically equivalent** across all material groups. However, **veneered restorations showed significantly more ceramic fractures and chipping** (p<0.01), with veneered zirconia FDPs exhibiting the highest fracture rate among all groups — paradoxically worse than the core material's strength advantage would suggest.

Design comparison: No significant difference in survival or complication rates between PFM FDPs with pontic and PFM iSCs.

## Key Contributions

1. **Survival equivalence across all materials at 3 years** — confirms PFM, Zr, and LDS are interchangeable for short-term posterior multi-unit implant prosthetics
2. **Monolithic Zr outperforms veneered Zr for fracture/chipping** (p<0.01) — the highest fracture rate was in veneered Zr FDPs; monolithic design eliminates the ceramic layer most prone to failure
3. **Veneered Zr FDP chipping risk identified as clinically meaningful** — this is the key warning for prescribers choosing layered zirconia for multi-unit implant FDPs
4. **Design effect (pontic vs splinted): negligible** — allows flexibility in design choice without survival penalty
5. LDS iFDPs showed wide CI (84.8–99.9%), indicating limited evidence base; clinical adoption for multi-unit implant FDPs remains under-studied

## Methodology

- **Search**: Electronic + manual, studies ≥12 months follow-up, posterior area
- **n**: 32 studies, 42 study arms included
- **Statistical model**: Robust Poisson regression → pooled 3-year survival proportions
- **Materials evaluated**: PFM, veneered zirconia FDP, monolithic/micro-veneered zirconia FDP, LDS FDP
- **Designs**: FDP with pontic vs implant splinted crowns
- **Abstract-only** — detailed heterogeneity data not available

## Results

**3-Year Survival Rates (Pooled):**

| Material | 3-year Survival | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|
| PFM iFDP | 98.3% | 95.6–99.3% |
| Veneered zirconia iFDP | 97.5% | 95.5–98.7% |
| Monolithic/micro-veneered Zr iFDP | **98.9%** | 96.8–99.6% |
| Lithium disilicate iFDP | 97.0% | 84.8–99.9% |

- All pairwise comparisons: **no statistically significant differences**
- **Chipping/fracture**: veneered restorations >> monolithic (p<0.01)
- **Highest ceramic fracture rate**: veneered zirconia FDPs
- **Design (pontic vs splinted)**: no significant difference in survival (98.3% vs 99.1%) or overall complications
