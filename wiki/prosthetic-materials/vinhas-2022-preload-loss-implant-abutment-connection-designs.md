## One-line Summary

In vitro removal-torque study of 180 Klockner implants across 4 connection designs (external SK2/KL, internal Vega/Essential) finding connection design did NOT influence preload maintenance, cyclic loading (500 N × 1000 cycles) significantly reduced removal torque for all connections, and multiple re-tightening gave higher RTV than single tightening (except Vega) — hence retighten retaining screws a few minutes after insertion.

## 한줄요약

180개 Klockner 임플란트(외부연결 SK2/KL·내부연결 Vega/Essential 4종)의 제거토크값(RTV) 실험. 연결방식(connection design)은 예압(preload) 유지에 영향이 없었고, 반복하중(500 N×1000회)은 모든 연결의 제거토크를 유의하게 낮췄으며, 다회 재조임이 단회 조임보다 높은 RTV를 보임(Vega 제외) → 보철나사는 식립 수 분 뒤 재조임 권장.

## Summary

Screw loosening is the most frequent mechanical complication of implant-supported restorations (estimated 10.4% at 5 years, 20.8% at 10 years), and its primary driver is loss of preload — the clamping force generated when the abutment screw is tightened. This blinded in vitro study tested whether the implant-abutment connection design (external hexagon vs internal) governs how well preload is maintained, and how much it degrades under repeated tightening and cyclic loading. Across all four Klockner connection designs, removal torque (the indirect surrogate for residual preload) was always lower than insertion torque, fell further after cyclic loading, and was best preserved by re-tightening the screw multiple times. Critically, connection design itself did not determine preload retention. The clinical takeaway is procedural rather than product-based: retighten the retaining screw a few minutes (3–10 min) after the initial torque, before masticatory loading, to recover the preload lost to the sedimentation/embedment-relaxation effect.

## Key Contributions

- Head-to-head comparison of 4 connection designs (2 external, 2 internal) for preload maintenance under single tightening, multiple tightening, and multiple tightening + cyclic loading.
- Shows connection design (external vs internal) does NOT significantly influence preload maintenance — challenges the assumption that internal connections inherently resist loosening better.
- Quantifies the sedimentation effect and provides a mechanistic, evidence-based rationale for the re-tightening protocol.

## Methodology

- **180 Klockner implants** (SOADCO, Andorra), all 4 mm × 12 mm, in 4 groups (45 each, split into 3 subgroups of 15 per phase):
  - **SK2** — external connection, 25 Ncm insertion torque
  - **KL** — external connection, 30 Ncm insertion torque
  - **Vega** — internal connection, 25 Ncm insertion torque
  - **Essential** — internal connection, 30 Ncm insertion torque
- Removal torque value (RTV) measured with a digital torque meter (Andilog Centor Touch Star TH); screws tightened with a calibrated cordless screwdriver (NSK iSD900).
- **Three phases:**
  1. **Single tightening** — RTV measured 1 min after one tightening.
  2. **Multiple tightening** — 10 tightenings at 15 s intervals; RTV measured after 1 min.
  3. **Multiple tightening + cyclic loading** — 10 tightenings, then 500 N × 1000 cycles (CS Dental fatigue machine), RTV measured afterward.
- Statistics: SPSS v27, Shapiro-Wilk + ANOVA, p < 0.05.

## Results

- **Connection design did NOT significantly influence preload maintenance.** At equal theoretical insertion torque, internal and external connections behaved comparably; no connection geometry showed superior fatigue/preload behavior.
- **Cyclic loading significantly reduced RTV for all connections** — Phase III produced the lowest removal torque means of all phases (significantly below insertion torque, ANOVA F(3;56)=20.122, p<0.05).
- **Multiple re-tightening > single tightening:** multiple tightening gave higher RTV than single tightening for all implants EXCEPT Vega (multiple 25.40 Ncm vs single 25.56 Ncm).
- **Phase I detail:** RTV < insertion torque for all, statistically significant only for the internal connections Essential (30.497→27.692, p<0.001) and Vega (26.496→25.562, p<0.016).
- SK2 and Vega (both 25 Ncm) differed significantly from each other across all 3 phases; Essential and KL (both 30 Ncm) behaved similarly.
- **Clinical recommendation:** retighten the retaining screw a few minutes (3–10 min) after insertion, before masticatory loading, to restore preload lost to the sedimentation/embedment effect.
