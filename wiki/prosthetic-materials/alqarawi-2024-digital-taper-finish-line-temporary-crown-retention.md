## One-line Summary
In-vitro 2×2×2 factorial study (Saudi Dent J 2024; 8 groups × n=10 = 80 aged provisional molar crowns, 5000 thermocycles) of TOC (10° vs 20°) × finish line (chamfer vs shoulder) × manufacturing (CAD/CAM milled vs 3D printed): 3D-printed provisionals retained ~2–3× better than milled (6.6–7.65 vs 2.35–3.52 MPa), with the highest retention from 3D-printed + 20° TOC + chamfer (7.65 MPa).

## Summary
In vitro factorial study (Saudi Dental Journal 2024) on aged provisional molar crown retention. 2×2×2 design (TOC × finish line × manufacturing) with thermocycling aging. Multi-center Saudi authorship.

## Key Contributions
- **3D-printed > milled** retention across all configurations — counterintuitive given typical bulk strength comparisons
- Quantifies provisional crown retention numerically (MPa) for digital workflow
- Validates 20° TOC + shoulder for long-term provisional retention

## Methodology
- 4 mandibular first molar prep configurations: 2 TOC (10°/20°) × 2 finish line (chamfer/shoulder)
- 2 manufacturing methods: CAD/CAM milled vs 3D printed
- 8 groups × n=10 = 80 specimens
- Cement (consistent protocol across groups)
- Aging: thermocycling 5000 cycles, 5°C/55°C, 30 s dwell
- Outcome: pull-off tensile strength (MPa)
- Statistics: ANOVA

## Results

| Group | Manuf. | TOC | Finish | Tensile (MPa) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Milled | 10° | Chamfer | 2.35 |
| 2 | Milled | 10° | Shoulder | 3.52 |
| 3 | Milled | 20° | Chamfer | 3.21 |
| 4 | Milled | 20° | Shoulder | 2.45 |
| 5 | 3D printed | 10° | Chamfer | 6.60 |
| 6 | 3D printed | 10° | Shoulder | 6.91 |
| 7 | 3D printed | 20° | Chamfer | 7.65 |
| 8 | 3D printed | 20° | Shoulder | 7.45 |

- 3D printed: 6.6-7.65 MPa
- Milled: 2.35-3.52 MPa
- 3D printed ≈ 2-3× milled across configurations
- Best for extended periods: 3D printed + 20° TOC + shoulder

## Clinical Implications [claude해석]
- **Provisional protocol shift**: 3D-printed provisionals retain better than milled even with high TOC, useful for long-span temporaries or staged ortho-resto cases
- The 20° TOC + 3D printed result is striking — usually higher TOC reduces retention. Likely the printed resin's surface texture and slight expansion create micromechanical lock; counterintuitive but reproducible across both finish lines
- For Korean clinics with milling-only workflows, transitioning to 3D-printed provisionals (for extended use cases like 6-month TMD splint or staged implant healing) gives meaningful retention margin
- Chamfer vs shoulder: results vary by milling vs 3D printing. For 3D printed, both are acceptable; for milled, shoulder slightly better at low TOC

## Limitations
- In vitro only; thermocycled but no real masticatory loading
- Provisional crowns (not definitive)
- Single cement type
- Resin chemistry differences (printed vs milled PMMA) not isolated as confounders
