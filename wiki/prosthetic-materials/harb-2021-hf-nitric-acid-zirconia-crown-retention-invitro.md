## 한줄요약
in-vitro (Materials 2021;14:960, Mansoura/Zurich, n=40 maxillary premolar, 2×2 factorial): 지르코니아 크라운 air-abrasion ± 핫애시드 에칭 (48% HF + 69% HNO₃) × Panavia SA Cement vs GC LinkForce — 핫애시드 에칭 유지력 유의 향상 (p=0.001), 시멘트 종류 단독 NS (p=0.882); 최강 조합: air-abrasion + 핫애시드 + Panavia SA (AHP); 실패 양상: Panavia SA mixed, LinkForce adhesive.

## Summary
In vitro factorial study (Materials MDPI 2021) on zirconia crown retention with two surface treatment + two adhesive cement combinations. Multi-institution: Mansoura U. (Egypt) + University of Zurich. Cited in zirconia bonding literature.

## Key Contributions
- Hot acid etching (48% HF + 69% HNO₃) significantly improves zirconia retention beyond air-abrasion alone
- Demonstrates **cement-effect dominance reversal**: cement type alone NS, but cement × surface treatment interaction matters
- Documents mode-of-failure differences as adjunct outcome (Panavia SA → mixed; GC LinkForce → adhesive)

## Methodology
- 40 maxillary premolars prepared with proximal extensions
- CAD/CAM zirconia crowns
- 4 groups × n=10 (2-way factorial):

| Group | Surface | Cement |
|---|---|---|
| AP | Air-abrasion | Panavia SA Cement |
| AL | Air-abrasion | GC LinkForce |
| AHP | Air-abrasion + hot acid | Panavia SA Cement |
| AHL | Air-abrasion + hot acid | GC LinkForce |

- Hot acid: 48% HF + 69% HNO₃ mixture
- Outcome: dislodgement force (N) → retention strength (MPa)
- Statistics: 2-way ANOVA + interaction

## Results
- **Hot acid etching effect**: significant (p = 0.001)
- **Cement type effect**: NS (p = 0.882)
- Group ranking (highest → lowest retention):
  - AHP (air-abrasion + hot acid + Panavia SA) — highest
  - AP (air-abrasion + Panavia SA)
  - AHL (air-abrasion + hot acid + GC LinkForce)
  - AL (air-abrasion + GC LinkForce) — lowest
- Mode of failure:
  - Panavia SA Cement: mixed (cohesive + adhesive)
  - GC LinkForce: adhesive (interface failure dominant)

## Clinical Implications [claude해석]
- **Hot acid handling caution**: 48% HF + 69% HNO₃ requires fume hood, full PPE, NaF treatment plan for skin contact, and disposal protocol — significant occupational risk for in-clinic use
- For practical clinics: air-abrasion + MDP-containing primer + Panavia SA Cement (or equivalent) is the achievable baseline; hot acid is lab-only realistically
- Mixed-mode failure of Panavia SA indicates true adhesion to zirconia surface; pure adhesive failure of LinkForce suggests interface dominance
- 한국 임상 적용: 일상 진료에서는 핫애시드 사용 권장 안 함. 알루미나 air-abrasion (50 μm, 0.2 MPa) + MDP-containing primer가 안전한 baseline. 연구실 셋팅이나 specialized indications에서만 핫애시드 고려

## Limitations
- In vitro; no thermocycling/fatigue cycling
- Limited to 2 cement types
- Hot acid handling not clinically practical
- No comparison with tribochemical silica coating + silane (CoJet) alternative
