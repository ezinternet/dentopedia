## Summary
Edentulism is common in older adults, and implant-supported overdentures (OD) improve retention and patient satisfaction over conventional dentures. The choice of attachment system, however, materially affects peri-implant tissue response, prosthodontic maintenance, and patient-reported outcomes. This 2025 systematic review and meta-analysis pooled 14 randomized or crossover trials (2010–2020) on mandibular implant overdentures with ≥1-year follow-up, all rated low risk of bias, to compare attachment systems head-to-head.

Bar attachments produced the highest retention values, while telescopic attachments delivered the highest patient satisfaction. Locator attachments were advantageous when interarch space was limited or implants were angulated. Magnet attachments performed worst on both retention and soft-tissue response. Critically, implant survival did **not** differ significantly across attachment types once basic prosthetic principles were observed — meaning attachment choice should be driven by anatomy, hygiene access, and patient expectation rather than a fear of implant failure.

## Key Contributions
- Direct comparison of bar, locator, ball, telescopic, and magnet attachments on retention, tissue response, satisfaction, and survival.
- Establishes that **implant survival is attachment-type independent** for low-risk-of-bias studies.
- Provides a decision framework: bar (retention), telescopic (satisfaction), locator (constrained anatomy), avoid magnets.

## Methodology
- Databases: PubMed, Cochrane Central, ScienceDirect + manual search.
- Inclusion: RCTs and crossover clinical trials 2010–2020, ≥1-year follow-up.
- Risk-of-bias: Cochrane Collaboration tool; all 14 included studies = low risk.
- Statistical analysis: RevMan 5.4 (random-effects where appropriate).

## Results
| Attachment | Retention | Patient satisfaction | Tissue response | Implant survival |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Bar | **Highest** | High | Plaque-prone (cleansability ↓) | No diff |
| Telescopic | High | **Highest** | Good | No diff |
| Locator | Moderate–high | Good | Good; flexible for angulation up to ~40° | No diff |
| Ball | Moderate | Moderate | Moderate; matrix wear → maintenance | No diff |
| Magnet | **Lowest** | Lower | **Worst** (more soft-tissue change) | No diff |

Take-aways:
- Bar for maximum retention or stress distribution across implants.
- Telescopic for patient-reported outcomes (perceived denture quality).
- Locator for limited interarch space (<10 mm) or divergent implants.
- Magnets are rarely first-line under current evidence.
