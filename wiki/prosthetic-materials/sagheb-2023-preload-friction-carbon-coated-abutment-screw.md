## One-line Summary

In vitro study (n=25, Nobel Biocare) found that repeated tightening of carbon-coated abutment screws to 25 Ncm significantly reduced preload (329.9 N → 253.7 N over 10 cycles) and increased the coefficient of friction (0.33 → 0.44), indicating carbon-coated screws should not be reused.

## Summary

Sagheb et al. (2023) designed a custom load frame to simultaneously measure preload force and the thread-friction torque component in a carbon-coated abutment screw system. Twenty-five Nobel Biocare implant-abutment-screw complexes were torqued ten times to 25 Ncm under dry conditions. Preload decreased monotonically with each repetition — significantly from the 1st to 2nd tightening (Δ17.4 N, p<0.0001) and from the 2nd to 3rd (Δ13.8 N, p=0.001). The coefficient of friction (COF) increased from 0.33 to 0.44. Critically, thread-friction showed no consistent trend; the preload loss is attributed primarily to increasing screw head friction — consistent with the mechanical engineering model that ~50% of tightening torque is consumed by screw head friction and only ~10% generates preload. The conclusion is that carbon coating wears or changes with repeated tightening, increasing head friction and thereby reducing preload below optimal levels.

## Key Contributions

- Quantified COF of a carbon-coated dental abutment screw system using VDI 2230 standard (previously unknown for this interface)
- Isolated the site of friction increase: screw head friction, not thread friction
- Provided the first evidence that repeated use of carbon-coated screws degrades preload — clinical implication: single use only
- Established a methodological template (custom load frame + dual load cell + COF calculation) for future screw coating studies

## Methodology

- **Design**: In vitro mechanical test, custom load assembly with two load cells (750 N / 150 N)
- **Sample**: n=25 Nobel Biocare Replace Select Tapered implant + Temporary Abutment Non-engaging + carbon-coated Abutment Screw Nobel Replace
- **Protocol**: 10 tightening cycles at 25 Ncm; 60 s hold; dry conditions; 0.10 mm abutment-implant gap maintained
- **COF calculation**: VDI 2230 Part 1; geometry by SEM (50×)
- **Statistics**: Linear mixed model; Tukey–Kramer post-hoc; α = 0.05

## Results

| Cycle | Preload (N) | COF |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | 329.9 ± 33.3 | 0.33 ± 0.04 |
| 10th | 253.7 ± 36.8 | 0.44 ± 0.07 |

- Monotone decreasing preload across all 10 cycles (p < 0.0001)
- Largest drops: 1st→2nd (Δ17.4 N) and 2nd→3rd (Δ13.8 N)
- Thread-friction: no significant trend — screw head friction drives the loss
- Range at 10th cycle: 200.1–332.5 N (wide individual variation)
