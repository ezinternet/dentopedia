## One-line Summary

Systematic review of 27 in vitro studies: rounded-shoulder finish line favored marginal fit, chamfer favored internal adaptation, and increased taper plus planar occlusal reduction improved fit of digitally fabricated zirconia prostheses (all within the ~60–120 µm clinically acceptable range).

## Summary

This PRISMA/PROSPERO-registered systematic review pooled 27 in vitro studies to isolate how tooth-preparation design — finish line, taper, and occlusal reduction — affects marginal discrepancy and internal gap in fully digitally fabricated (scan → CAD → mill) complete-coverage zirconia prostheses. The headline is that the optimal design differs by outcome: a rounded-shoulder finish line minimizes the marginal gap, whereas a chamfer minimizes the internal gap. Increasing taper helped both, and a planar (flat) occlusal reduction paired with a chamfer improved marginal fit — consistent with milling-bur geometry favoring rounded, simplified surfaces. Across studies, fit values stayed within the clinically acceptable window (marginal 60–120 µm; internal 50–100 µm), so the design choice is an optimization within an already-acceptable range rather than a pass/fail gate.

## Key Contributions

- Translates generic preparation-geometry knowledge into outcome-specific (marginal vs internal) design guidance for the digital zirconia workflow.
- Documents that finish-line choice optimizing marginal fit (rounded shoulder) and internal fit (chamfer) diverge.
- Confirms taper and planar occlusal reduction as fit-favorable in milled zirconia.

## Methodology

Searched 6 databases + manual reference screening (130 → 93 de-duplicated → 39 full-text → 27 included). Study composition: 17 finish line, 9 taper, 1 occlusal reduction, 1 occlusal reduction + finish line, 2 FPD (rest single crowns). Quality scored with the QUIN tool (12 parameters). Holmes et al. misfit terminology adopted; clinical acceptability anchored at marginal 60–120 µm and internal 50–100 µm. Qualitative + quantitative synthesis.

## Results

- Marginal fit best with rounded-shoulder finish line.
- Internal adaptation best with chamfer finish line.
- Planar occlusal reduction + chamfer → better marginal fit.
- Increased taper → positive for both marginal fit and internal adaptation.
- All reported values generally within clinically acceptable thresholds.
