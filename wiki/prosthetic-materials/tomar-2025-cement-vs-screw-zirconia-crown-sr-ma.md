## One-line Summary
SR+MA (7 studies, 334 zirconia implant crowns, 3–10 years) of screw- vs cement-retained zirconia implant crowns: screw-retained had significantly lower marginal bone loss at 6 months (SMD -0.63, p=0.002) and 46% lower complication risk (RR 0.54, p=0.02), with long-term (1–5 year) MBL comparable between retention types.

## Summary
First SR+MA specifically for zirconia implant crowns (monolithic/layered). Key finding: **screw-retained (SR) crowns show significantly less marginal bone loss at 6 months** (SMD -0.63, p=0.002), but **long-term MBL (1–5 years) is comparable** between SR and cement-retained (CR). SR also reduces overall complication risk by 46% (RR 0.54, p=0.02).

## Study Characteristics
- 7 studies included (RCTs + prospective cohorts)
- 168 CR + 166 SR zirconia crowns
- Follow-up range: 3–10 years
- Mix of monolithic and veneered zirconia

## Clinical Interpretation

**6-month MBL advantage (SR):**
- Clinically relevant for implants in augmented bone or compromised sites
- Early crestal bone preservation may reduce long-term peri-implant disease risk
- Likely mechanism: no cement contamination → cleaner peri-implant environment early on

**Long-term equivalence:**
- After 1 year, MBL trajectories converge — both retention types perform similarly
- Confirms Hamed 2020 SR finding: **survival equivalence** with different complication profiles

**46% complication reduction (SR):**
- Includes all complication types; SR's mechanical complications are less frequent than CR's biological ones in this zirconia-specific cohort

## Clinical Takeaway
- For zirconia crowns: **SR is preferred** for early bone preservation and lower overall complication risk
- Particularly important in **posterior sectors, GBR sites, or patients with peri-implantitis history**
- Long-term prognosis equivalent — patient preference and esthetic zone concerns can guide final choice
