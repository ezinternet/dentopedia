## Summary
Implant-supported overdentures (OD) are the prosthodontic backbone for edentulous patients, yet failures still occur and the underlying drivers are multifactorial. This 2024 PRISMA systematic review pooled 20 studies (13 RCTs, 1 prospective CT, 3 prospective, 2 retrospective, 1 cross-sectional) published 2018–2023 to identify variables associated with OD failure.

Failures cluster around four axes: (1) the **patient** — systemic disease (diabetes, head/neck irradiation), smoking, and certain medications; (2) the **arch** — maxillary OD consistently outperforms mandibular OD in difficulty, demanding more implants and often splinting; (3) the **number of implants** — two-implant mandibular OD remains robust, but maxillary OD generally requires four or more; (4) the **loading protocol** — immediate/early loading is riskier where primary stability is borderline. Beyond these, the operator's surgical and prosthetic aptitude and a disciplined recall program were repeatedly highlighted as decisive.

## Key Contributions
- Synthesises **patient-, anatomy-, surgical-, prosthetic-, and operator-side risk factors** in one place.
- Anchors current evidence around the McGill/York consensus for mandibular 2-implant OD, and clarifies why the maxilla is a different problem.
- Frames OD failure as a maintenance-sensitive disease — recall intensity is a modifiable risk factor.

## Methodology
- Databases: MEDLINE/PubMed, Cochrane Library, LILACS + hand search.
- Inclusion: CT, RCT, observational study, 2018–2023.
- Cochrane Collaboration RoB tool; two independent screeners.
- Excluded: case reports, in vitro studies, narrative reviews, paediatric populations.

## Results
**Patient factors increasing failure risk:**
- Uncontrolled diabetes, head/neck irradiation, smoking, bisphosphonate use.
- Severely resorbed ridges with limited bone height/width.

**Arch effects:**
- Mandibular OD: 2 implants sufficient as default (per McGill / York).
- Maxillary OD: usually ≥4 implants; splinted bar increases predictability in soft bone.

**Loading protocol:**
- Immediate loading viable in mandible with high primary stability (ITV ≥35 Ncm) and bar splinting; otherwise conventional loading preferred.
- Maxilla — conventional loading is the safer default unless bone quality and stability are excellent.

**Prosthodontic complications (frequent but rarely catastrophic):**
- Matrix/insert change, reline, framework or denture base fracture — well-managed by recall.

**Operator / follow-up:**
- Aptitude of the dentist and structured recall reduce failure rate independently of attachment choice.
