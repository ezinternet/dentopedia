## One-line Summary

In vitro study (n=80, internal vs external hexagon, 35 Ncm): retightening at 2 minutes post-initial torque minimized preload loss significantly better than 5-minute, 10-minute, or no-retightening controls.

## 한줄요약

체외 연구(내·외부 육각 각 40개, 35 Ncm): 초기 조임 후 2분 재조임이 전조임 소실을 가장 효과적으로 감소시켰으며, 5분·10분·비재조임 대비 유의한 차이(P<0.05).

## Summary

Abutment-screw loosening is among the most common implant-prosthetic complications, driven primarily by the **settling effect** — flattening of microscopic surface asperities at the implant-abutment interface that reduces preload by 2–15% within minutes of initial tightening.

Varvara et al. compared 40 internal hexagon (IG) and 40 external hexagon (EG) implants (WAY Milan, Geass S.r.l.) embedded in acrylic resin at 35 Ncm initial torque. Each design was split into four groups: no retightening (control), retightening at 2, 5, or 10 minutes, with all implants detorqued at 30 minutes.

**Key finding**: Retightening at **2 minutes** produced the lowest preload loss for both IG and EG. Internal hexagon connections consistently retained more preload than external hexagon at matched intervals. The prior standard recommendation of 10-minute retightening (Dixon 1995, Bakaeen 2002) was found to be suboptimal — by 10 minutes, additional settling had already occurred, leaving less preload to recover.

## Key Contributions

1. **Optimal retightening window is 2 minutes**, not 5 or 10 minutes (contradicts most prior in vitro guidance).
2. Internal hexagon design is mechanically superior for preload retention vs external hexagon under identical conditions.
3. Provides time-resolved torque-loss data across both connection types — first study to directly compare IG vs EG across multiple retightening intervals.
4. Recommends RCTs to validate clinical translation of the 2-minute protocol.

## Methodology

- **Implants**: 3.8 × 15 mm titanium implants (WAY Milan, Geass S.r.l., Italy) — 40 IG + 40 EG; titanium abutment screws
- **Fixture**: Embedded in acrylic resin (Orthojet) in PVC cylinders (bone analog, static)
- **Torque**: 35 Ncm initial tightening (digital torque meter, 0.1 Ncm precision, Imada HTG2-5 N)
- **Groups** (n=10 each): Control (no retighten), 2-min, 5-min, 10-min retightening
- **Endpoint**: Detorque at 30 minutes post-initial tightening
- **Statistics**: Shapiro-Wilk → Student t-test (group vs control) + one-way ANOVA; P < 0.05; 5 outliers removed by Chauvenet's/Pierce's criteria

## Results

| Connection | Group | Preload loss outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Internal hexagon | IG0 (control) | Highest loss |
| Internal hexagon | IG2 (2 min) | Lowest — P<0.05 vs control |
| Internal hexagon | IG5 (5 min) | Intermediate — P<0.05 vs control |
| Internal hexagon | IG10 (10 min) | Worse than IG2/IG5 — P<0.05 vs control |
| External hexagon | EG0 (control) | Highest loss (greater than IG0) |
| External hexagon | EG2 (2 min) | Lowest in EG — P<0.05 vs control |
| External hexagon | EG5 (5 min) | Intermediate |
| External hexagon | EG10 (10 min) | Worse than EG2/EG5 |

- Internal hexagon < external hexagon in absolute preload loss at every matched time point.
- Settling effect accounts for 2–15% initial preload loss (consistent with literature).
- 2-minute retightening outperformed the previously recommended 10-minute protocol.
