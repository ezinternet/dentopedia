## One-line Summary

In vitro study (n=32) across four internal implant–abutment connection types found that octagon connections produced the least settling (axial displacement) under repeated torque (20–30 Ncm), while internal hexagon connections produced the most, with the plateau reached after the second torque application.

## Summary

Repeated torque tightening causes axial settling in all internal implant–abutment connections; the magnitude depends significantly on connection geometry. This in vitro study by Saleh Saber et al. (2017) quantified settling across four common internal designs at each clinical/laboratory stage of prosthetic fabrication. The octagon connection consistently displaced the least; the internal hexagon the most. Settling accumulates progressively with torque magnitude and through successive prosthetic stages (impression coping → resin pattern → metal framework → final abutment seating), potentially accumulating enough axial discrepancy to compromise passive fit.

## Key Contributions

1. **Connection geometry dominates settling**: Internal hexagon shows 2–3× more settling than internal octagon at equivalent torque across all stages.
2. **Torque plateau at 2nd application**: For repeated 30 Ncm torque on abutments, the 2nd application produced significant additional settling vs the 1st, but applications 3–5 showed no further significant displacement. This informs clinical retightening protocols.
3. **Stage-additive misfit risk**: Each laboratory/clinical stage adds incremental settling; cumulative axial discrepancy from impression through final delivery can reach >30 µm (hexagon) vs ~15 µm (octagon) at 30 Ncm.
4. **Morse taper intermediate**: Morse hex 6° and 11° connections settle intermediate amounts — less than hexagon, more than octagon — with 6° slightly less consistent across stages.

## Methodology

- **Design**: In vitro bench study
- **System**: Periosave implant analogs (4 mm diameter); narrow abutments (4.5 mm); 8 samples per connection type; 32 total
- **Connection types**: Internal hexagon, internal octagon, Morse hex 6°, Morse hex 11°
- **Stages**: Impression coping, abutment, resin pattern, Ni-Cr metal framework (uncemented)
- **Torques**: 20, 25, 30 Ncm (digital torque controller Lutron TQ-8800); 30 Ncm repeated ×5 for abutment stage
- **Measurement**: Digital indicator (Mitutoyo, 0.001 mm resolution); 3 readings per sample, mean reported
- **Statistics**: One-way ANOVA + Tukey post hoc; repeated-measures ANOVA for torque repetition; p<0.05

## Results

| Stage / Torque | Hexagon (µm) | Octagon (µm) | Morse 6° (µm) | Morse 11° (µm) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Impression coping 20 Ncm | 12.5 | 4.1 | 6.0 | 8.0 |
| Impression coping 25 Ncm | 18.5 | 7.5 | 19.9 | 15.6 |
| Impression coping 30 Ncm | 24.1 | 12.3 | 24.5 | 24.1 |
| Resin abutment 30 Ncm | 29.5 | 15.6 | 20.8 | 27.1 |
| Final frame 30 Ncm | 30.3 | 14.5 | 20.5 | 29.0 |
| Abutment 30 Ncm (1st) | 31.5 | 16.1 | 22.5 | 24.5 |
| Abutment 30 Ncm (2nd–5th) | 34.7 | 18.1 | 25.4 | 24.5 |

All between-group differences: p<0.001. Repeated 30 Ncm: significant change 1st→2nd; no significant change 2nd→5th (plateau).
