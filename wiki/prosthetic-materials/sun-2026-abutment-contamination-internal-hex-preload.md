## One-line Summary
In vitro study (n=90) demonstrating that abutment contamination within internal hexagonal connections reduces initial preload even at partial contamination, while extensive contamination additionally accelerates cyclic-loading-induced preload loss via two mechanistically distinct stages.

## 한줄요약
체외 연구 (n=90): 내부 육각형 연결부 어버트먼트 오염 시 부분 오염만으로도 초기 역토크값 (Reverse Torque Value, RTV) 감소; 완전 오염 (CT6)은 반복하중 (Cyclic Loading) 후 예비하중 손실 (Preload Loss)을 추가 가속화.

## Summary
This in vitro study investigated how abutment surface contamination within internal hexagonal implant-abutment connections affects (1) initial preload formation and (2) preload loss after cyclic loading. Ninety titanium implant-abutment assemblies were divided into three groups: no contamination (CT0), contamination on 3 of 6 hexagonal surfaces (CT3), and contamination on all 6 surfaces (CT6). Contamination was simulated using dental stone applied to designated internal surfaces. Abutment screws were tightened to 30 N·cm, and reverse torque values (RTV) were measured before and after ISO 14801:2016 cyclic loading (90 N, 2 Hz, 240,000 cycles).

Key finding: two mechanistically distinct failure stages exist. First, any degree of contamination impairs initial preload formation (pre-RTV CT0 > CT3 ≈ CT6). Second, extensive contamination amplifies cyclic-loading-induced preload loss (ΔRTV CT6 > CT0 ≈ CT3). SEM imaging revealed localized surface wear at line angles in heavily contaminated specimens, while clean specimens showed minimal surface change. Preceding clinical audit of 265 custom abutments at a university hospital confirmed that visible contamination of hexagonal connection surfaces is a real-world occurrence.

## Key Contributions
- Quantified two-stage contamination effect: initial preload compromise (all contamination) vs. accelerated fatigue-induced preload loss (extensive contamination only)
- Established that partial contamination (3/6 surfaces) is mechanically equivalent to extensive contamination (6/6) for initial preload formation — threshold effect on preload formation
- SEM evidence of physical surface damage mechanism (line-angle wear) in extensively contaminated specimens
- Real-world clinical prevalence data from 265 custom abutments confirming contamination is not hypothetical

## Methodology
- **Implants**: Titanium (Ø4.5 × 11.5 mm; Neo Biotech), embedded in acrylic resin per ISO 14801:2016
- **Groups (n=30 each)**: CT0 (clean), CT3 (3/6 hex surfaces contaminated), CT6 (6/6 surfaces contaminated)
- **Contamination agent**: Type III dental stone + distilled water, applied by single operator
- **Tightening torque**: 30 N·cm; settle 10 min; re-tighten; wait 5 min → measure pre-RTV
- **Cyclic loading**: ISO 14801:2016 (90 N, 2 Hz, 240,000 cycles) → measure post-RTV
- **ΔRTV** = pre-RTV − post-RTV (absolute torque loss)
- **SEM**: CT0 and CT6 subsets, ×150/×500/×2000
- **Statistics**: Shapiro-Wilk; paired t-test with Bonferroni; one-way ANOVA + Tukey HSD (α=.05)

## Results
| Outcome | CT0 (clean) | CT3 (partial) | CT6 (extensive) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-RTV | Highest | ↓ vs CT0* | ↓ vs CT0* (≈CT3) |
| Post-RTV | Highest | Intermediate | Lowest* |
| ΔRTV | Lowest (≈CT3) | Lowest (≈CT0) | Highest* |

*P < .001 vs indicated group (Tukey HSD)

- Pre-RTV: Both CT3 and CT6 significantly lower than CT0; CT3 vs CT6 not significant
- Post-RTV: Progressive decrease CT0 > CT3 > CT6 (all pairwise P < .001)
- ΔRTV: CT6 significantly greater than CT0 and CT3; CT0 vs CT3 not significant
- SEM: Line-angle wear on hexagonal surfaces in CT6; minimal alteration in CT0
