## One-line Summary
Prospective 12-month comparison (n=100; 50 screw-retained vs 50 cement-retained single implant crowns): opposing complication profiles — screw-retained have more screw loosening (18% vs 4%), cement-retained have more marginal gap (20% vs 8%) and cement washout (14% vs 2%); peri-implantitis trended higher in cement-retained but was non-significant at 12 months.

## 한줄요약

## Summary
Prospective 12-month comparison of single implant crowns: 50 screw-retained (SR) vs 50 cement-retained (CR). Key finding: **SR has more mechanical complications** (screw loosening 18% vs 4%); **CR has more biological/marginal complications** (marginal gap 20% vs 8%; cement washout 14% vs 2%). Peri-implantitis trended higher in CR but did not reach significance at 12 months.

## Complication Rates

| Complication | SR | CR | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Screw loosening | **18%** | 4% | 0.032 |
| Marginal gap | 8% | **20%** | <0.05 |
| Cement washout | 2% | **14%** | <0.05 |
| Peri-implantitis | 4% | 10% | 0.146 (NS) |
| Crown fracture | NS | NS | NS |

## Clinical Takeaway
- No one-size-fits-all: choose retention type based on **which complication is less acceptable**
- SR: budget for **screw maintenance** — torque check at recalls; access hole management
- CR: use **retrievable cement** (temp or low-perm); verify seating/margins; risk of subgingival cement
- 12-month data: peri-implantitis difference is trending but not yet significant — longer follow-up needed

## Decision Framework

| Prefer SR when | Prefer CR when |
|---|---|
| Angulation allows access hole | Access hole placement is esthetic problem |
| Patient returns for recalls reliably | Operator/lab setup favors cementation |
| GBR/augmented site (protect crestal bone) | High parafunctional load (crown fracture risk) |
| Deep peri-implant pocket risk | Standard anatomy, no deep pockets |
