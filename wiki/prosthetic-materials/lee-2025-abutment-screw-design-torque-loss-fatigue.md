## Summary

Abutment screw loosening (ASL) is the most frequent mechanical complication in implant prosthodontics (5–15% incidence), driven by loss of preload — the clamping force generated at tightening, of which only ~8–10% of applied torque actually converts to preload. This in vitro study compared two Osstem systems with different abutment-screw designs: **TSIII** (conventional screw) versus **KSIII** (modified screw: reduced head diameter, narrower thread profile, longer nominal length). Across tightening torques of 20/25/30/35/40 Ncm and identical ISO 14801 cyclic loading (150 N, 14 Hz, 100,000 cycles, 30° off-axis), KSIII retained significantly more preload at every level. KSIII held torque loss **below 25% regardless of torque** (torque-insensitive, no significant inter-group difference), whereas TSIII was strongly torque-dependent, losing **51.8% at 20 Ncm down to 36.5% at 35 Ncm**, then rising again to 38.2% at 40 Ncm. TSIII torque loss averaged ~2.5–3.0× that of KSIII. The data support a tightening torque of **~35 Ncm** as giving the most stable preload, and indicate that screw geometry itself — not just torque magnitude — is a dominant determinant of loosening resistance. Clinically relevant to Korean practice where both systems are in routine use.

## Key Contributions

- Direct TSIII-vs-KSIII head-to-head under one controlled fatigue protocol — both are widely used Osstem systems in Korea.
- Shows the KSIII modified screw geometry confers torque-insensitive preload stability (<25% loss at all levels).
- Separates torque-magnitude effect from screw-design effect: TSIII is torque-dependent, KSIII is not.
- Reinforces ~30–35 Ncm as the practical tightening optimum and shows 40 Ncm is not protective (excessive torque risks microdamage without preload benefit).

## Methodology

- **Specimens**: 50 implant–abutment assemblies, **n=5 per group**, 10 groups (2 systems × 5 torque levels). Ø4.0 × 10 mm cylindrical implants, straight abutments (Ø5.0 platform, 2.0 mm gingival height); Grade-4 cp-Ti fixtures, Ti-6Al-4V abutments/screws.
- **Systems**: Osstem **TSIII** (conventional screw) vs **KSIII** (reduced head diameter, narrower thread profile, longer nominal length); differ in implant–abutment connection geometry.
- **Tightening torques**: 20, 25, 30, 35, 40 Ncm via calibrated digital torque gauge (MTT03-100, MARK-10) at ~1 rpm, single operator, 22 ± 1 °C / 50 ± 5% RH.
- **T1 (initial removal torque)** measured 5 min after tightening (settling-effect capture). Specimens then retightened to assigned torque before loading.
- **Cyclic loading**: ElectroPuls E3000 (Instron), per ISO 14801 — **150 N, 14 Hz, 100,000 cycles, 30° off-axis** (≈1–2 months of mastication).
- **T2 (post-fatigue removal torque)** measured immediately after; **torque loss rate (%) = (T1 − T2)/T1 × 100**.
- **Statistics**: Shapiro–Wilk; independent t-tests (TSIII vs KSIII per torque); one-way ANOVA + Tukey within each system; α = 0.05.

## Results

KSIII showed **significantly higher T1 and T2 than TSIII at every torque level (p < 0.05)**.

**Torque loss rate (%):**

| Tightening | TSIII | KSIII |
|---|---|---|
| 20 Ncm | 51.8% | 23.1% |
| 25 Ncm | 47.7% | 16.3% |
| 30 Ncm | 39.4% | 20.7% |
| 35 Ncm | 36.5% | 18.7% |
| 40 Ncm | 38.2% | 23.3% |

- **TSIII**: torque-dependent; highest loss at 20 Ncm (51.8%), lowest at 35 Ncm (36.5%); 40 Ncm rose to 38.2%. Significant among-torque differences (ANOVA p < 0.05; 20 vs 35 Ncm Tukey p = 0.008).
- **KSIII**: all values **<25%**; no significant difference between torque groups (ANOVA p > 0.05).
- **Overall**: TSIII loss ~**2.5–3.0× higher** than KSIII (p < 0.001 at every level).
- **Bottom line**: **~35 Ncm** gave the most stable preload; KSIII's modified screw geometry inherently reduces loosening risk and may lessen the need for frequent retightening.
