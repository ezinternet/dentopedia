## One-line Summary

Systematic review (24 studies; 1 pilot RCT, 2 cross-sectional, 4 cross-over, 12 case reports, 5 dental techniques) concluding indirect emergence-profile scanning (extraoral scan of the provisional, superimposed) most accurately captures the peri-implant emergence profile, whereas direct scanning after provisional removal shows considerable soft-tissue collapse (~200–500 μm immediate, up to ~1 mm by 20 min).

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(24편; 파일럿 RCT 1·단면 2·교차 4·증례 12·술식 5)로, 임플란트 주위 잇몸형태(emergence profile, EP)를 디지털로 캡처할 때 간접 스캐닝(임시보철 구외 스캔 중첩)이 가장 정확하며, 임시보철 제거 직후 직접 스캐닝은 연조직 붕괴(즉시 약 200~500 μm, 20분에 최대 약 1 mm)로 정확도가 낮다.

## Summary

For implant-supported single crowns in the esthetic zone, the peri-implant **emergence profile (EP)** sculpted by the provisional must be transferred faithfully to the definitive restoration. This first-of-kind systematic review compares three digital impression workflows against the conventional elastomeric method (CM):

- **Direct scanning** — intraoral scan of the soft tissue *immediately after* removing the implant-supported provisional restoration (ISPR). Every clinical study found significant soft-tissue collapse once the ISPR is out, because the mucosa loses its support.
- **Indirect scanning** — an extraoral scan of the removed ISPR superimposed onto the in-situ scan, recovering the submucosal contour without the time pressure of collapse.
- **Conventional method (CM)** — elastomeric open-tray impression with a customized coping; the gold-standard comparator.

The qualitative verdict: **indirect EP scanning is the most promising digital method and approaches the conventional method**, while **direct scanning is less reliable** for the submucosal EP.

## Methodology

- PICO comparing direct/indirect digital impression accuracy vs conventional for peri-implant EP in single implant-supported crowns.
- PRISMA 2020 / Cochrane Handbook; PROSPERO CRD42023459484; 4 databases (MEDLINE, CENTRAL, Embase, Web of Science), searched 7 Aug 2024.
- Risk of bias: RoB 2, ROBINS-I, JBI — most studies moderate-to-high RoB.
- **24 studies**: 5 dental techniques, 12 case reports, 1 pilot RCT, 2 cross-sectional, 4 cross-over. Mostly maxillary esthetic zone, Straumann implants, phenotype unreported. Scanners: 3Shape TRIOS (most common), iTero, Carestream, Cerec Omnicam, Planmeca, 3M ESPE.
- Planned quantitative meta-analysis was **not interpretable** owing to heterogeneity → qualitative synthesis only.

## Results

**Direct-scan soft-tissue collapse (most accurate method = least collapse):**

| Study | Collapse / deviation | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| da Silva Marques | ~244 μm (NS, n=6, <1 mm threshold) | vs CM with CIIC |
| Xiong | direct 414.7 ± 116.0 μm; **indirect 230.6 ± 85.5 μm**; ~500 μm within 20 s | vs conventional |
| Li | ~200 μm immediate → ~1 mm at 20 min; 0.08–0.18 mm sig. | vs indirect |
| Galibourg | −5% (30 s), −10% (2 min), −14% (5 min) volume | vs immediate direct |
| Joda | −5.5% (5 min), −21.7% (10 min) | vs immediate direct |
| Duran | 0.51–1.35 mm (whole cross-section; not comparable) | vs conventional |
| Ling (RCT) | direct 1.87 mm vs **indirect 0.75 mm** labial; 1.66 vs 0.65 palatal | vs provisional EP |

- **Indirect scanning** replicated the EP as accurately as the conventional method (Xiong) and acceptably reproduced the provisional submucosal contour (Ling RCT); 3-year outcomes comparable to conventional (Canullo, PES analog 8 / digital 9). Pink esthetic score in Ling: direct 7.89 ± 1.52 vs indirect 8.09 ± 2.27.
- ISPR is crucial for preserving peri-implant soft-tissue architecture; removing it triggers rapid, time-dependent collapse.
- Indirect-scan caveat: multiple superimposed scan files; scanner software can struggle with large/complex files. Coded scannable healing abutments are promising but data are limited. Multilayered monolithic zirconia (Tanaka) may enable fully digital workflows without a physical veneering cast.
- Limitations: few well-designed studies, high heterogeneity, moderate-to-high RoB; no agreed clinically acceptable EP-discrepancy threshold. Future homogeneous RCTs needed comparing indirect scanning to the conventional gold standard.
