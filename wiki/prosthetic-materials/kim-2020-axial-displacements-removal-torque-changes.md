## One-line Summary

In-vitro static-loading study (5 implant-abutment connection designs, n=10 each, 700 N vertical load) showing settling (axial displacement) increases with tapered interfaces (external butt-joint lowest at 0.8 µm, internal 8° Morse-taper octagon highest at 25.6 µm), and that two-piece abutments lose removal torque (preload) from settling while one-piece abutments gain it through friction.

## 한줄요약

In-vitro 정적하중 연구(5종 임플란트-지대주 연결, 군당 n=10, 700 N 수직하중): settling(축방향 변위)은 테이퍼 계면일수록 커지고(외부 버트조인트 0.8 µm 최소 → 내부 8° Morse-taper 옥타곤 25.6 µm 최대), 2-piece 지대주는 settling으로 제거토크(preload)가 감소하나 1-piece 지대주는 마찰로 오히려 증가.

## Summary

This study isolated the **settling effect** under a single clinically realistic static load to explain why abutment screws loosen in posterior implants. Fifty Osstem implant-abutment assemblies (5 connection geometries, n=10 each) were tightened to 30 Ncm and loaded once vertically to 700 N (maximum posterior bite force) in a universal testing machine. Settling was measured as the change in total assembly length (1 µm resolution), and preload retention was inferred from removal torque before vs after loading. Settling scaled with interface taper — flat external butt-joints barely moved (0.8 µm), while steeper internal Morse-taper octagons settled most (25.6 µm). Critically, the same settling produced **opposite preload outcomes by abutment type**: two-piece (screw-joint/tension) abutments lost removal torque — one nearly fell out (Int-O2, 1.14 Ncm) — whereas one-piece (friction-retained) abutments gained removal torque. Clinically, settling-driven preload loss in molar regions risks both screw loosening and a measurable lowering of occlusion after prosthesis delivery.

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies the settling effect as **axial displacement (µm)** under a single 700 N static load, isolating static from cyclic loading.
- Links settling magnitude directly to **post-loading removal torque** across 5 macro-geometries from one manufacturer (controlling fabrication source).
- Establishes a **geometry gradient**: flat external < internal hexagon (11°) < internal octagon (8° Morse taper) for settling.
- Reveals an **abutment-type divergence**: two-piece abutments LOSE preload to settling; one-piece abutments GAIN removal torque via friction — the same phenomenon, opposite clinical signs.
- Flags a practical consequence beyond loosening: settling can **lower the occlusion** after delivery in load-heavy molar sites.

## Methodology

- **Design**: in-vitro, 5 groups × n=10 = 50 Osstem implant-abutment assemblies.
  - Ext = external butt-joint + cemented two-piece abutment
  - Int-H2 = internal hexagon (11°) + transfer two-piece abutment
  - Int-H1 = internal hexagon (11°) + rigid one-piece abutment
  - Int-O2 = internal octagon (8° Morse taper) + Comocta two-piece abutment
  - Int-O1 = internal octagon (8° Morse taper) + solid one-piece abutment
- **Tightening**: 30 Ncm twice at 10-min interval; initial removal torque measured 10 min after second tightening; re-secured to 30 Ncm.
- **Loading**: single 700 N vertical static load (Instron 8841, 1 mm/min).
- **Settling**: total assembly length change before vs after loading (digital micrometer, 1 µm resolution).
- **Preload proxy**: post-loading vs initial removal torque (digital torque gauge).
- **Statistics**: one-way ANOVA + Tukey HSD (between groups); paired t-test (within-group initial vs post-loading removal torque); α = 0.05.

## Results

**Settling (axial displacement) after 700 N, mean ± SD:**

| Group | Connection | Abutment | Settling (µm) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ext | external butt-joint | two-piece (cemented) | 0.8 ± 0.45 |
| Int-H1 | internal hexagon (11°) | one-piece (rigid) | 10.2 ± 0.84 |
| Int-H2 | internal hexagon (11°) | two-piece (transfer) | 11.2 ± 0.84 |
| Int-O2 | internal octagon (8° taper) | two-piece (Comocta) | 19.2 ± 4.21 |
| Int-O1 | internal octagon (8° taper) | one-piece (solid) | 25.6 ± 2.97 |

Order: **Ext < Int-H1, Int-H2 < Int-O2 < Int-O1** (α = 0.05).

**Removal torque after 700 N (initial 30 Ncm), mean ± SD:**

| Group | Post-load removal torque (Ncm) | Direction vs initial |
|---|---|---|
| Int-O1 (one-piece) | 39.64 ± 4.28 | ↑ increased |
| Int-H1 (one-piece) | 36.38 ± 6.25 | ↑ increased |
| Ext | 22.78 ± 0.40 | ↓ small but significant ↓ |
| Int-H2 (two-piece) | 11.62 ± 0.56 | ↓ decreased |
| Int-O2 (two-piece) | 1.14 ± 0.40 | ↓ near-total loss (screw nearly loose) |

Order: **Int-O2 < Int-H2 < Ext < Int-H1, Int-O1**.

**Mechanism**: one-piece internal abutments → friction-dominant retention → removal torque rises after settling; two-piece internal abutments → tension/screw-joint retention → preload lost to settling, removal torque falls. The steeper 8° Morse-taper octagon settles most.

**Limitations**: static (not cyclic) loading; single manufacturer; friction-seated metal cap (no cement) and absent vertical stop in the octagon design may overestimate settling there; removal torque is an indirect preload proxy (no direct screw-elongation measurement); small n.
