## Summary

This cross-sectional study investigated whether caregivers understand midlevel providers and whether there is theoretical acceptance of integrating a midlevel dental therapist (DT) into the dental workforce. Validated DT-integration surveys were distributed to caregivers of pediatric medical patients at an urban teaching hospital. Respondents reported any previous care from a midlevel medical provider (ML) and answered acceptance items about a midlevel dental provider (DT). Of 202 evaluable questionnaires, 71.2% recalled previous midlevel medical care (ML) and 28.7% were unaware (UML). Caregivers were broadly receptive to DTs performing dental procedures, but both groups strongly agreed those procedures should be carried out under dentist supervision, and the authors emphasize a need to raise public awareness of DT education, scope of practice, and workforce integration.

This is an abstract-only ingest (full text not retrieved); only the reported summary statistics below are available.

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies caregiver acceptance of a midlevel dental therapist (DT) using validated DT-integration surveys (n=202).
- Shows prior exposure to a midlevel medical provider (ML) is associated with greater agreement toward DT-delivered procedures.
- Documents a near-universal expectation that DT procedures occur under dentist supervision.
- Surfaces a public-awareness gap on DT education and scope of practice.

## Methodology

- Design: cross-sectional questionnaire survey.
- Setting/population: caregivers of pediatric medical patients at an urban teaching hospital.
- Instrument: validated DT-integration surveys; respondents reported previous midlevel medical care, then rated acceptance of a midlevel dental provider (DT).
- Stratification: ML (recalled previous midlevel medical care) vs UML (unaware).
- n = 202 evaluable questionnaires.

## Results

- 71.2% recalled previous midlevel (ML) medical care; 28.7% were unaware (UML).
- ML respondents significantly agreed with DTs performing:
  - primary tooth restorations (p<0.001)
  - permanent tooth restorations (p=0.001)
  - primary tooth extractions (p=0.022)
  - emergency treatment (p<0.001)
- Both UML and ML significantly agreed procedures should be completed under dentist supervision (p<0.001).
- Conclusion: caregivers are receptive to DTs and largely agree with them performing dental procedures, but information dissemination is needed to increase public awareness of DT education, scope of practice, and workforce integration.
