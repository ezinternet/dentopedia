## One-line Summary

Retrospective review of 1,364 missed dental appointments (Riyadh military medical city, Jan–May 2024): nonattendees were mostly single (56.2%), female (60.7%), ≤35y (67.3%), with morning slots and new-patient visits most missed — but no significant day/time-by-characteristic association.

## 한줄요약

사우디 리야드 군병원 치과 미내원(no-show) 1,364건 후향 분석 — 미혼·여성·35세 이하·오전·초진 예약에서 결석이 많았으나, 요일·연령과 시간/날짜 간 통계적 유의 연관은 없었다. 저녁 진료·워크인 슬롯이 권고 대책.

## Summary

Single-center retrospective secondary analysis of appointment records at the Dental Center, Prince Sultan Military Medical City (Riyadh, Saudi Arabia) over a 4-month window (1 Jan – 1 May 2024), profiling **1,364 nonattended (no-show) appointments**. The study characterizes *who* misses dental appointments and *when*, to guide schedule and resource management. The dominant no-show profile is **single, female, young (≤35y), new/initial-visit, morning-slot** patients. Despite this descriptive skew, formal association tests (ANOVA, chi-square, correlation) found **no statistically significant** relationship between patient characteristics and the day, date, or time of missed appointments. The authors recommend **evening appointments** and **walk-in slots** as operational levers to recover lost chair time. This is the first operational no-show / appointment-attendance page in the wiki's practice-management category.

## Key Contributions

- First retrospective secondary analysis of dental-appointment nonattendance among Riyadh City dental centers (per authors).
- Concrete demographic + timing profile of no-shows usable for targeted reminder/scheduling interventions.
- Two actionable operational recommendations: evening appointments and walk-in slots.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Retrospective secondary analysis of existing appointment medical records.
- **Setting/period**: Dental Center, Prince Sultan Military Medical City, Riyadh; 1 Jan – 1 May 2024.
- **Sample**: 1,364 missed-appointment records (all ages/genders; including patients with prior nonattendance). Records with missing/incomplete data excluded.
- **Variables**: demographics (age, gender, marital status), health status (fit/unfit), prior attendance history, appointment time, day/date, visit type (new vs follow-up).
- **Statistics**: descriptive statistics + correlation analysis, ANOVA, and chi-square tests (IBM SPSS v25.0).
- **Ethics**: IRB-approved; de-identified data, consent waived; Declaration of Helsinki.

## Results

**No-show profile (N=1,364):**
- Marital status: **Single 56.2%** (767) > Married 43.3% (591); Divorced 0.1%, Widow 0.3%.
- Gender: **Female 60.7%** (828) > Male 39.3% (536).
- Health status: Fit 59.4% > Unfit 40.6%.
- Age: **≤25y 36.7%** + **26–35y 30.6%** = 67.3% of no-shows; ≥56y only **3.8%**.

**Timing:**
- **Morning slots** (8:00 AM–3:00 PM) most missed; fewer 4:00–8:00 PM.
- Day-of-week: **Tuesday highest (331)**, then Mon 274 / Wed 273 / Thu 251; **Sunday lowest (234)**.
- **New (initial) patients** dominated misses (Sunday 86.0% new vs 14.0% follow-up; Monday 83.2% new) — consistent with fear/anxiety at first visit.

**Association tests — all non-significant:**
- Age vs appointment time: F=0.224, **p=0.925**; age vs date: F=0.840, **p=0.500**.
- Day-of-week vs marital status p=0.919, gender p=0.908, health p=0.971, age p=0.954, visit type p=0.137.
- Correlations weak: age–date r=0.029; age–time r=−0.011.

**Interpretation:** strong *descriptive* demographic skew, but no significant interaction between patient characteristics and scheduling dimensions in this sample. Operational recommendations: evening appointments and walk-in slots.

**Caveats:** single-center military population, 4-month window, no attended-appointment comparator (so no true no-show rate or relative risk), and missing predictors (distance, insurance, reminders) flagged by prior systematic reviews.
