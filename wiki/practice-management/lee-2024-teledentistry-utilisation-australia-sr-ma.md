## Summary

This systematic review and meta-analysis evaluated the utilisation of teledentistry in Australia — the remote delivery of dental care via information-based technologies, used in regional/rural/remote regions and accelerated during COVID-19. Searching PubMed, Google Scholar, EMBASE, and Web of Science to June 2024 and appraising quality/bias with the JBI Critical Appraisal Tools, the authors included **18 articles**: six diagnostic tests, six cross-sectional studies, four economic evaluations, one qualitative study, and one expert opinion. The synthesis spans three dimensions — diagnostic accuracy, stakeholder acceptance, and economics — and concludes that teledentistry increases healthcare access (especially in regional/rural/remote areas) and is an effective caries-screening tool, while clinician opinions remain mixed and implementation barriers persist.

## Key Contributions

- Country-specific (Australia) evidence synthesis of teledentistry utilisation across diagnostic accuracy, acceptance, and cost.
- Pooled caries-screening accuracy: average sensitivity **69.7%**, average specificity **97.4%**.
- Separates stakeholder acceptance — patients **positive**, clinicians **mixed**.
- Economic evaluations indicate **cost savings** for both patients and healthcare providers.

## Methodology

- Systematic search of four databases (PubMed, Google Scholar, EMBASE, Web of Science) to June 2024.
- Risk-of-bias / quality appraisal with JBI Critical Appraisal Tools.
- 18 included articles across mixed study designs (6 diagnostic tests, 6 cross-sectional, 4 economic evaluations, 1 qualitative, 1 expert opinion).
- Meta-analysis of caries-screening diagnostic accuracy (pooled sensitivity/specificity).

## Results

- **Caries screening accuracy**: average sensitivity = 69.7%; average specificity = 97.4% — high specificity (few false positives) with moderate sensitivity (~30% of lesions potentially missed), so teledentistry screens well but does not replace confirmatory in-person examination.
- Non-significant negative correlation between specificity and sensitivity (r = 0.432).
- **Acceptance**: patient opinions positive; clinician opinions mixed.
- **Economics**: potential savings for patients and healthcare providers.
- **Access**: increases healthcare access, especially in regional/rural/remote areas; implementation barriers identified.
