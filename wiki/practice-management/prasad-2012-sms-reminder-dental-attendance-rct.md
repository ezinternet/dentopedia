## One-line Summary

Two-group controlled trial in 4 outpatient dental departments (India, n=206) — SMS appointment reminders raised on-time attendance to 79.2% vs 35.5% in the no-reminder control group.

## Summary

Nonattendance for dental outpatient appointments is a major operational and financial burden, with patients most often citing forgetting the appointment or confusion over its date and time. This trial tested whether a short message service (SMS) text reminder — a low-cost channel — improves attendance at the ITS Centre for Dental Studies and Research (ITS-CDSR), Muradnagar, Ghaziabad, India, across the Departments of Prosthodontics, Endodontics, Orthodontics and Paedodontics.

Patients seen over a 4-month period who had provided a mobile number were allocated to a test group (received an SMS reminder) or a control group (no reminder). On-time attendance was the outcome. The SMS group attended on time at more than double the rate of the control group. The authors conclude that SMS appointment reminders are an effective, inexpensive strategy for reducing nonattendance.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates a large attendance gain from a low-cost SMS reminder in a real dental-clinic setting spanning four specialties.
- Directly targets the two leading nonattendance causes (forgetting; date/time confusion) with a single intervention.
- Provides controlled-trial efficacy evidence to pair with descriptive no-show studies that documented patient demand for reminders.

## Methodology

- **Design**: two-group controlled trial — test (SMS reminder) vs control (no reminder). Allocation method, blinding, and the exact statistical test were not recoverable from the abstract; the design is best characterized as a **quasi-RCT / non-randomized controlled trial**, so unmeasured selection differences between groups cannot be excluded.
- **Setting**: ITS-CDSR, Muradnagar, Ghaziabad, Uttar Pradesh, India; Prosthodontics, Endodontics, Orthodontics, Paedodontics, over 4 months.
- **Participants**: **n=206** (male 124, female 82); test group **96** (male 57, female 39), control group **110** (male 67, female 43). Eligibility required a contact mobile number.
- **Outcome**: rate of on-time attendance at the scheduled appointment.

## Results

- On-time attendance: **test group 79.2% vs control group 35.5%** — reported as significantly higher in the SMS group.
- The roughly 2.2-fold attendance gain is consistent with the wider reminder literature (an active reminder substantially outperforms relying on patient memory).

**Limitations**: abstract-only ingest (no full text); apparent non-randomized allocation with unstated balance; single academic center in one country; no cost or patient-acceptability outcomes; no confidence intervals reported.
