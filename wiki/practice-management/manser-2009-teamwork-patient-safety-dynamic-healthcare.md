## One-line Summary

Narrative literature review (general healthcare, acute/dynamic domains) synthesizing three evidence streams — critical-incident analyses, provider-perception studies, and observational performance studies — that together establish teamwork as a determinant of quality and patient safety.

## Summary

This is **general-healthcare evidence (not dental-specific)**, drawn from highly dynamic / acute domains — operating rooms, intensive care, emergency medicine, and trauma/resuscitation. It is ingested here to anchor the dental team-communication axis: the dental clinic is a smaller, lower-acuity team environment, so the OR/ICU teamwork findings transfer to dentistry only as an extrapolation, but they supply the canonical rationale for *why* team communication, coordination, and leadership matter for safe care.

Manser argues teamwork is a measurable determinant of quality and patient safety rather than a soft adjunct, and consolidates the supporting literature into three convergent streams. The review also links teamwork and leadership style to staff well-being, and well-being to the capacity to deliver safe care — a chain directly relevant to dental-practice staffing, delegation, and burnout.

## Key Contributions

- Establishes teamwork as a determinant of quality and patient safety in acute care.
- Organizes the field into three convergent evidence streams (see Results).
- Links teamwork/leadership style → staff well-being → ability to provide safe care.
- Sets the research agenda: validated team-performance assessment instruments and integrative theoretical models to drive team training and work-system design.

## Methodology

Narrative review of the literature (no systematic search or meta-analysis reported). Single author. Evidence grouped into three analytic categories spanning OR, ICU, ED, and trauma/resuscitation settings. General-healthcare scope; dental application is by extrapolation.

## Results

Three evidence streams support the teamwork → patient-safety relationship:

1. **Critical incidents / adverse events** — teamwork plays an important role in both the **causation and prevention** of critical incidents and adverse events.
2. **Provider perceptions** — staff perceptions of teamwork and attitudes toward safety-relevant team behavior relate to quality and safety of care; perceptions of teamwork and leadership style **associate with staff well-being**, which may in turn affect the ability to provide safe care.
3. **Observational teamwork behaviors** — studies of high clinical performance identify recurring patterns of **communication, coordination, and leadership** that underpin effective teamwork.

No pooled effect sizes are reported (narrative synthesis; abstract-only). The stated future challenge is to develop and validate team-performance assessment instruments and integrative theoretical models, to improve team training and design clinical work systems that support effective teamwork and safe care.
