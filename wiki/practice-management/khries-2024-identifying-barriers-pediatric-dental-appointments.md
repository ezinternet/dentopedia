## Summary

This cross-sectional, expert-validated questionnaire study assessed why children miss pediatric dental appointments at a hospital clinic in Al-Rusaifeh, Jordan. Among 265 children under 12 (mean age 6.85 yr) who each had >3 prior appointments, 44% had missed at least one appointment — even though 85% carried dental insurance, showing that cost is not the dominant driver of non-attendance. Barriers were organized into four domains (parent/caregiver, patient, administrative, nonspecific). Missed appointments correlated significantly with the child's age, gender, and reliance on public transportation, but not with parental occupation or education. The authors frame the findings as targets for scheduling and retention interventions: reminders, clearer no-show policy, shorter follow-up gaps, and anxiety-reduction measures.

## Key Contributions

- Documents a high pediatric **missed-appointment rate of 44%** despite 85% insurance coverage — financial access alone does not explain non-attendance.
- Provides a **four-domain barrier taxonomy** (parent/caregiver, patient, administrative, nonspecific) directly usable for scheduling/retention SOP design.
- Establishes demographic correlates of non-attendance (age, gender, transport mode) and the null findings (parental occupation and education not significant).
- Translates barriers into concrete practice-management levers: automated reminders, clear cancellation/no-show policy, reduced wait times, shortened follow-up gaps, oral health literacy and anxiety-reduction interventions.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Cross-sectional descriptive/analytical questionnaire study.
- **Setting/Sample**: Pediatric dentistry clinic, Prince Faisal bin Al-Hussein Hospital, Jordan; n = 265 children <12 yr (each with >3 prior appointments; every third eligible follow-up patient selected). Age 2.5–12 yr, mean 6.85 ± 1.7.
- **Instrument**: Pre-tested, expert-validated structured questionnaire completed by parents/guardians via interview (Cronbach's alpha 0.95).
- **Window**: Nov 2023 – Jan 2024 (spanning school vacation).
- **Analysis**: SPSS v26; descriptive statistics + chi-square for associations with gender, parental occupation, education, transport type; P ≤ 0.05.

## Results

- **Missed-appointment rate**: 44% (117/265).
- **Sample profile**: 57% male; 76% school-age; 85% insured; 92% living with both parents; 79% using public transport; majority below-high-school parental education.
- **Significant correlates** (P < 0.05): child age (P = 0.0041), transportation type (P = 0.002); gender reported significant in text (Table 3 chi-square P = 0.091).
- **Non-significant**: father/mother occupation (P = 0.561 / 0.134); father/mother education (P = 0.132 / 0.129).
- **Top barriers** (Table 4):
  - Parent/caregiver: social & family commitments 15.5%, forgetfulness 11.2%, illness 8.6%, work 6%, dental issue resolved 4.3%.
  - Patient: illness 12.1%, school commitment 6.9%, fear of dental treatment 3.5%.
  - Administrative: staff miscommunication 10.3%, long wait time 5.2%, long follow-up gaps 5.2%.
  - Nonspecific: 11.2% (insurance-card renewal; expense for uninsured child).
- **Limitations**: single-region Jordanian sample; self-report response bias; cross-sectional (no causal/longitudinal inference).
