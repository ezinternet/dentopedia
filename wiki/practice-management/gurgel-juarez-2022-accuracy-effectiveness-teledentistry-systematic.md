## Summary

This is a **review of systematic reviews** (umbrella review) addressing a single practice-relevant question: how accurate and effective is teledentistry compared with in-person consultation for screening, diagnosis, and therapeutic management in children and adults? Motivated by COVID-19's push toward remote care, it screened 817 citations and included **6 SRs** (2013-2020) covering **61 primary studies and >7,000 participants**, with **30 primary studies** directly relevant.

The bottom line: teledentistry is an **effective means for dental referrals, treatment planning, and treatment monitoring**. Accuracy was good-to-very-good for diagnostic-treatment planning and referrals, and virtual assessments were comparable to in-person exams **when remote and in-person assessors had similar education/experience**. The most feasible real-world configuration is **asynchronous (store-and-forward) communication with smartphone image capture**, which is more flexible and cheaper than real-time setups or dedicated intraoral cameras.

The major caveat is evidence quality: all 6 SRs were low (1) or critically low (5) on AMSTAR-2, built on primary studies with high bias risk, and **no treatment-efficacy data** existed (treatment outcomes were limited to compliance and supervision). It opens the teledentistry sub-topic in practice-management as the service-delivery (remote care) counterpart to the category's existing in-clinic operational and legal focus.

## Key Contributions

- First umbrella-level synthesis quantifying teledentistry **accuracy and effectiveness** across the full screening → diagnosis → treatment pathway.
- Endorses teledentistry for **referrals, treatment planning, and monitoring** as the evidence-supported service-delivery use cases.
- Identifies the **asynchronous + smartphone** configuration as the feasible, scalable implementation pathway.
- Names the practical accuracy moderator: assessor **education/experience parity** between remote and in-person clinicians.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Review of systematic reviews, PRISMA-reported, pre-registered protocol; narrative synthesis (heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis).
- **Search**: Medline, Embase, CINAHL, Web of Science (inception–June 2020) + OpenGrey, Brazilian MoH Virtual Library, IADR archives (2001+); reiterative reference/citation chasing into Sept 2020.
- **Eligibility**: SRs on virtual screening, diagnosis, or therapeutic intervention via remote communication; no population/setting/language limits.
- **Selection & appraisal**: dual independent reviewers across 3 stages; AMSTAR-2 (with diagnostic-accuracy extension) for quality.

## Results

- **Yield**: 817 → 547 (deduped) → 34 full-text → **6 SRs** included.
- **Scope**: 6 SRs, 61 primary studies, >7,000 participants; 30 primary studies of interest; 4 RCTs + 17 other designs. Concentrated in **paediatrics and oral medicine**; mostly clinical settings (57%), rural 20%; >70% from Europe/USA/Brazil.
- **Dominant modality**: store-and-forward (asynchronous), 86% of screening/diagnostic studies; real-time used for treatment.
- **Diagnostic accuracy** (6 primary studies): DFS detection sensitivity 94-100% / specificity 52-100%; diagnostic-treatment planning sensitivity 81-88% / specificity 82-95%. Abstract summary: referrals + diagnostic-treatment planning **80-88% / 73-95%**, exceeding other index/reference tests.
- **Screening accuracy** (3 studies): sensitivity 48-100% / specificity 81-98%; DFS best; caries and DMFT high specificity but low sensitivity.
- **Treatment** (2 studies): interceptive orthodontics under remote supervision (PAR improvement 36% remote vs 44% in-person — comparable) and paediatric compliance 56-100%. **No treatment-efficacy outcomes.**
- **Quality**: 1 low + 5 critically low (AMSTAR-2). Accuracy degraded for mid-level/inexperienced assessors or assessor mismatch.
