## Summary
Elias and colleagues describe CODE (Compassion, Operational Support, De-escalation, Empowerment), a training program for complaint-management staff built on two parallel tracks: procedural training on operational systems and resources, and interpersonal communication training for empathy and de-escalation. By pairing operational competence with compassionate communication, CODE targets exactly the two breakdowns the rest of the literature documents -- bureaucratic mishandling and relational failure. It is a descriptive program paper, providing a transferable curriculum rather than controlled effectiveness data.

## Key Contributions
- Names a structured, dual-track complaint-handling curriculum (CODE).
- Track 1: procedural training on operational systems/resources; Track 2: interpersonal communication for empathy and human connection.
- Explicitly bridges operational efficiency and compassionate communication -- the two failure modes seen elsewhere.
- Provides a transferable training framework for patient-experience/complaint staff.

## Methodology
Descriptive account of the development and implementation of the CODE training program for patient-experience complaint-management staff. Not a controlled outcome study; outlines program structure, dual-track framework, and rationale.

## Results
CODE comprises two core tracks -- procedural/operational training and interpersonal communication training -- so that staff can both access resources for complainants and communicate with empathy. The dual-track structure is presented as the program's key strength for managing complaints successfully; formal effectiveness/outcome data are not the focus of this descriptive paper.
