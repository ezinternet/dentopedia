## One-line Summary

Retrospective 10-year (2012-2022) analysis of Brazilian facial cosmetic surgery litigation (992 identified → 122 included) found that appropriate written informed consent yields favorable defendant outcomes and significantly reduces litigation losses and mean financial damage ($59,536).

## Summary

This retrospective analysis examined facial cosmetic surgery litigation in Brazil over a 10-year window (2012-2022) to assess the medico-legal role of written informed consent. An online search identified 992 litigations, of which 122 met inclusion criteria. Legal disputes increased over the study period, and female patients were more prone to litigation. The central finding is that lawsuits accompanied by appropriate written consent frequently resulted in a favorable outcome for the defendant, and that written consent significantly reduced both litigation losses and financial damage. (abstract-only — full text not retrieved.)

## Key Contributions

- Provides litigation-based evidence that appropriate written informed consent is associated with favorable defendant outcomes and reduced financial exposure.
- Documents an increasing trend of cosmetic-surgery legal disputes over a decade.
- Reports the specialty distribution of defendants: plastic surgery 67%, maxillofacial surgery 9%.

## Methodology

- Design: retrospective analysis of online litigation records, Brazil, 2012-2022 (10 years).
- Screening: 992 litigations identified; 122 met inclusion criteria and were analyzed.
- abstract-only — full text not retrieved; effect sizes, confidence intervals, and confounder adjustment are not available.

## Results

- 992 litigations identified → 122 included.
- Trend: number of legal disputes increasing over the years.
- Demographics: female patients more prone to litigation.
- Financial damage: range $75 to $500,000; mean $59,536.
- Consent effect: many lawsuits with appropriate written consent had a favorable outcome for the defendant; written consent significantly reduces both litigation losses and financial damage.
- Specialty distribution: plastic surgery 67%, maxillofacial surgery 9%.
