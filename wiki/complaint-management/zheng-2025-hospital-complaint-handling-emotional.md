## Summary
Zheng and colleagues report a case study at a Chinese tertiary hospital's service centre that applies an emotional-language analysis model to patient complaints. Rather than treating complaints as liabilities, they mine the affective content -- patients' perceptions, opinions, and emotional responses -- to guide 'high-quality hospital development'. The work illustrates an analytics-forward direction for the response axis, where complaint sentiment data feed directly into management strategy, though it remains a single-site, language-specific case study.

## Key Contributions
- Applies an emotional/sentiment language-analysis model to complaint narratives.
- Treats patient complaints as a driver of 'high-quality hospital development', not just risk.
- Demonstrates systematic analysis of patients' perceptions, opinions, and emotional responses.
- Bridges complaint analytics and management strategy at an organisational level.

## Methodology
Single-site case study at a tertiary-hospital service centre. Systematic analysis of patient perceptions, opinions, and emotional responses within the complaint-handling process using an emotional-language (sentiment) analysis model. (Mixed quantitative text analysis + organisational case interpretation.)

## Results
The emotional-language model characterised patients' affective responses across the complaint-handling process and linked complaint-handling quality to indicators of high-quality hospital development, supporting use of complaint sentiment data to target service improvement.
