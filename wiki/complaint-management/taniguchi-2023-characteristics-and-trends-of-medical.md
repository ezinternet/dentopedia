## One-line Summary
Descriptive study of all 13,340 closed medical malpractice claims from Japan's Supreme Court (2006–2021): 52.9% settled, 35.3% judged (75.8% favoring physicians), claim volume declining — sharply in OB/GYN after the 2009 no-fault compensation system.

## Summary
Taniguchi and colleagues described every closed medical malpractice claim recorded by Japan's Supreme Court over 16 years (n=13,340), the first national-prevalence picture rather than a high-risk subset. More than half (52.9%) ended in settlement and 35.3% reached judgment, where physicians prevailed in 75.8% of cases. Plastic surgery and OB/GYN tended toward settlement, while psychiatry settled less and produced more patient-unfavorable outcomes. Claim volume fell over the period, with OB/GYN declining most steeply — temporally tied to Japan's 2009 no-fault Obstetrics/Gynecology Medical Compensation System — illustrating how a system-level policy can reshape litigation epidemiology.

## Key Contributions
- First national-prevalence dataset of all closed malpractice claims in Japan.
- Specialty-level breakdown of settlement vs judgment and outcome direction.
- Documents a falling claim trend, with the OB/GYN drop linked to a 2009 no-fault policy.
- Quantifies the 75.8% physician-favoring rate among judged cases.

## Methodology
Descriptive analysis of all closed malpractice claims from the Supreme Court of Japan, 2006–2021. Trends and characteristics extracted by medical specialty and outcome (settlement, judgment, dismissal/withdrawal), with counts normalized per 1,000 physicians for cross-specialty comparison.

## Results
n=13,340 closed claims. Settled 7,062 (52.9%); judged 4,734 (35.3%) with physicians favored in 3,589 (75.8%). Plastic surgery and OB/GYN skewed to settlement; psychiatry skewed away from settlement with more patient-unfavorable outcomes. Overall and OB/GYN-specific claim counts (incl. per-1,000-physicians) declined significantly vs 2006, coinciding with the 2009 OB/GYN no-fault compensation system.

## Clinical Application (Dentistry)
- Macro context for the Korean micro-analyses: in East Asian jurisdictions most claims settle, and judged cases lean physician-favorable — but that tilt does not negate the specific liability grounds documented at case level.
- Demonstrates that a system-level no-fault scheme can durably reduce litigation volume — relevant when weighing dental malpractice insurance and policy.
