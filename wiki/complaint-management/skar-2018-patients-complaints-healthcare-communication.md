## Summary
Skar and Soderberg analysed written patient complaints about healthcare encounters using qualitative content analysis. Dissatisfaction centred on communication: patients reported insufficient information, not being listened to or believed, and a lack of respect and individualised attention, often aggravated by time pressure. The findings give concrete encounter-level texture to the relational domain of complaint taxonomies and underline that how patients are treated interpersonally drives complaints as much as clinical outcomes.

## Key Contributions
- Centres the *encounter* and communication as the locus of complaint.
- Identifies themes: insufficient information, not being listened to/believed, and lack of respect.
- Reinforces that relational failures (not only clinical error) generate complaints.
- Links to person-centred care: meaningful relationship = individualised attention + involvement in decisions.

## Methodology
Qualitative content analysis of written patient complaints concerning healthcare encounters and communication submitted to a Swedish patients' advisory committee.

## Results
Complaints clustered around communication failures -- patients not receiving adequate information, not feeling listened to or believed, and experiencing a lack of respect and individualised attention. Time pressure in encounters was a recurring obstacle to a caring relationship.
