## 한줄요약
JAMA Cardiology 2024 SR+MA (n=1,152,345 IE 사례): 침습적 치과시술 후 항생제 예방 — **고위험군에서만** IE 위험 유의 감소; 중간/저위험/불명 위험군에서는 효과 없음 → 2007/2017 AHA·NICE의 고위험군 한정 권고 입장 empirical 지지.

## Summary
Largest SR+MA to date on dental antibiotic prophylaxis for infective endocarditis (IE) prevention, including data on 1,152,345 IE cases. Stratifies analysis by patient IE risk category (high vs moderate vs low/unknown) — a critical methodological choice given the long debate over whom prophylaxis benefits.

The headline: antibiotic prophylaxis significantly reduces IE incidence *only* in high-risk patients (prosthetic valves, history of IE, congenital heart disease, valvular disease post heart transplant). For moderate-risk and low-risk patients, no significant prophylactic effect was detected at population scale.

[합의수준] This evidence supports the modern AHA 2007/2017 and NICE 2008 positions limiting prophylaxis to high-risk patients only and aligns with the long-running trend of reducing unnecessary antibiotic exposure in dentistry.

## Key Contributions
- Definitive risk-stratified meta-analysis settling decades of debate over universal vs targeted prophylaxis.
- Population scale (1.15M cases) gives statistical power that prior smaller reviews lacked.
- Anchors the antibiotic stewardship case in dentistry: 90%+ of patients on the old "universal prophylaxis" recommendations would derive no IE benefit.

## Methodology
SR+MA stratified by patient IE risk category; includes observational studies given the ethical impossibility of an RCT for IE primary endpoint.

## Results
| IE risk group | Effect of prophylaxis |
|---|---|
| High | Significant ↓ IE |
| Moderate | NS |

## Limitations
- Observational evidence base (no RCT possible)
- Heterogeneity in "invasive dental procedure" definitions
- Confounders inherent in retrospective claims-data sources
- Population-scale analysis may obscure individual high-risk subsets

## Clinical Implication (Korean dental practice)
For Korean dental practice: continue prophylaxis only in patients with the AHA high-risk categories (prosthetic valve including TAVI/TAVR, prior IE, congenital cyanotic heart disease, residual defects after CHD repair, valvulopathy after heart transplant). For all other cardiac patients — including mitral valve prolapse without regurgitation, repaired CHD without residual, prior CABG — no routine prophylaxis indicated.
