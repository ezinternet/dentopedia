## Summary
Narrative review covering bisphosphonate pharmacology, BRONJ risk stratification by route/type, dental management protocols. Notable finding: topical bisphosphonate application to implant surfaces **promotes osseointegration** — a beneficial effect. Systemic IV administration carries the highest BRONJ risk.

## BRONJ Risk by Drug Type

| Route | Drug Examples | Indication | BRONJ Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| IV | **Zoledronic acid**, pamidronate | Malignancy, myeloma, hypercalcemia | **Very High** |
| Oral long-term (>3yr) | Alendronate, risedronate | Osteoporosis | Moderate |
| Oral short-term (<3yr) | Same | Osteoporosis | Low |
| Topical | Experimental coatings | Implant surface | **Beneficial** (osseointegration ↑) |

## BRONJ Staging (AAOMS)

| Stage | Description | Management |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | No bone exposure; pain, imaging changes only | Conservative; CHX rinse |
| 1 | Exposed bone; asymptomatic; no infection | Conservative; CHX; monitor |
| 2 | Exposed bone + infection (pain, pus) | Antibiotics + CHX + debridement |
| 3 | Stage 2 + fracture / fistula / extensive osteolysis | Surgical resection; specialist |

## Dental Management Protocol

```
Before starting BP therapy:
  └─ Dental exam + OPG
  └─ Extract hopeless teeth (wait ≥3 weeks healing before first dose)
  └─ Treat caries, periodontal disease
  └─ Remove ill-fitting dentures

During oral BP therapy <3 years:
  └─ Routine dental care OK
  └─ Caution with surgical procedures

During oral BP therapy >3 years OR any IV BP:
  └─ Drug holiday 2 months before + after invasive procedure (oral BP)
  └─ IV BP: consult oncologist; extreme caution; no consensus on holiday
  └─ Prefer endodontic > extraction where possible
  └─ Antibiotic prophylaxis for surgical procedures

Active BRONJ:
  └─ No elective surgery
  └─ Conservative management + antibiotics + CHX
  └─ Stage 2–3: specialist referral
```

## Implant Considerations
- IV bisphosphonates: implant placement generally **contraindicated**
- Oral BP <3yr, no risk factors: implants possible with informed consent
- Oral BP >3yr: drug holiday protocol + informed consent + careful monitoring
