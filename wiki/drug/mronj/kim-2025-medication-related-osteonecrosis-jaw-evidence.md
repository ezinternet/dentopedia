## One-line Summary

Korean 5-society multidisciplinary consensus (2025) providing drug-class-specific prophylactic drug holiday timings (oral BP 2 mo; IV zoledronate 6-12 mo; denosumab 3-4 mo post-last-dose) and recommending early surgical intervention for all MRONJ stages, supported by Korean nationwide cohort data.

## Summary

Kim et al. (2025), representing five Korean academic societies, provides the first Korean-specific evidence-based consensus for MRONJ prevention, diagnosis, and management. Using a systematic literature review combined with three rounds of formal expert survey, the committee adapted international evidence (AAOMS, JBMR, MASCC) to the Korean clinical environment. Key innovations over AAOMS 2022 include: drug-class-dependent prophylactic drug holiday timings grounded in Korean cohort data; a diagnostic addendum allowing MRONJ diagnosis without the 8-week criterion if necrotic bone is radiographically confirmed; retention of Stage 0; and explicit advocacy for early surgical intervention at all stages including Stage 1, where 91.3% of conservatively managed lesions persisted. CTX is affirmed non-useful for risk stratification. Teriparatide holds the strongest adjunctive evidence (RCT-level).

## Key Contributions

- **Drug-class-specific drug holiday timings** (Table 3): Oral BPs — 2 months pre-procedure if prolonged use/risk factors; IV zoledronate — 6-12 months (Korean IV BP cohort: risk not significantly reduced until ≥12 months after last infusion); Denosumab — schedule invasive procedure 3-4 months after last dose (bone turnover declining, before the 6-9 month rebound fracture risk window)
- **SERM bridging**: Consider interim raloxifene for high-fracture-risk patients requiring extended Dmab holiday — novel over AAOMS 2022
- **Diagnostic 8-week addendum**: If necrotic bone confirmed clinically or radiographically, MRONJ can be diagnosed without the standard 8-week exposure/persistence requirement
- **Stage 1 surgery**: Among 92 conservatively managed Stage 1 lesions, 91.3% had persistent exposed bone and many progressed to higher stages
- **Pre-treatment dental evaluation**: Meta-analysis cited — reduces MRONJ incidence by 77.3% in cancer patients on high-dose antiresorptive

## Methodology

Multidisciplinary consensus; systematic literature review (PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science) + 3-round formal expert survey (Delphi-type) by representatives of 5 Korean societies: Korean Society for Bone and Mineral Research, Korean Association of Oral and Maxillofacial Surgeons, Korean Association of Maxillofacial Plastic and Reconstructive Surgeons, Korean Society of Osteoporosis, Korean Endocrine Society. Korean-specific datasets (600,000-patient nationwide BP cohort; IV BP timing study) incorporated to localize international evidence.

## Results

**Korean epidemiology**:
- Nationwide BP cohort (n≈600,000): 254 MRONJ cases = 0.04%
- Oral BPs: prevalence 0.10%; incidence 36.6/100,000 person-years
- Denosumab 60 mg (osteoporosis): 0-0.18%; FREEDOM-extension 10yr = 0.3% (5.2/10,000 patient-years)
- Denosumab 120 mg (cancer): 0.8-2.0%; real-world with biologics 5-12.6%
- BP→Dmab switch: significantly higher risk vs monotherapy

**Drug holiday evidence base**: SR (2022): RR 0.73 (95% CI 0.51-1.06) — not statistically significant. Recommendation is precautionary + mechanism-based.

**Therapeutic drug holiday**: Meta-analysis (13 studies) — complete cure 85% with holiday vs 56.4% without. Korean retrospective (n=54): longer holiday (median 9 months) → better post-surgical prognosis. Counter-evidence from Japanese large retrospectives (no benefit at 60-180 days).

**Teriparatide**: RCT — 6-month teriparatide 20 µg/day + surgery vs surgery alone → significantly promoted MRONJ resolution. PDRN: preliminary promising data cited.

**CTX**: Explicitly NOT recommended (large-scale studies + meta-analyses: not a reliable predictor).
