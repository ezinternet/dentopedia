Comprehensive review (2025): 4-level MRONJ prevention framework (primary = pre-medication dental treatment, secondary = intraoperative, tertiary = established MRONJ management, quaternary = preventing over-medicalization); primary prevention most effective; IV bisphosphonates and denosumab are highest risk.

## Summary
Comprehensive narrative review of MRONJ prevention across all dental specialties using a **4-level prevention framework**. Primary prevention (pre-medication dental clearance) is the most effective strategy. Covers all causative medications: bisphosphonates (oral/IV), denosumab, antiangiogenics, mTOR inhibitors.

## Causative Medications

| Drug Class | Examples | MRONJ Risk |
|---|---|---|
| IV bisphosphonate | Zoledronic acid, pamidronate | **Highest** |
| Oral bisphosphonate >3yr | Alendronate, risedronate | Moderate |
| Oral bisphosphonate <3yr | Same | Low |
| Denosumab (RANK-L inhibitor) | Prolia (60mg/6mo), Xgeva (120mg/mo) | High (Xgeva) / Moderate (Prolia) |
| Antiangiogenic | Bevacizumab, sunitinib | High (especially with BP) |
| mTOR inhibitors | Everolimus, temsirolimus | Moderate |

## 4-Level Prevention Framework

### 🟢 Primary Prevention — MOST EFFECTIVE
Before starting medication:
- Full dental exam + panoramic radiograph
- Extract all hopeless teeth (wait ≥3 weeks healing before first drug dose)
- Complete active caries and periodontal treatment
- Optimize oral hygiene
- Remove ill-fitting dentures / orthodontic appliances

### 🟡 Secondary Prevention — During Medication
- Avoid elective invasive procedures where possible
- Prefer endodontic treatment over extraction
- If surgery unavoidable:
  - Oral BP: drug holiday 2 months before + 2 months after
  - IV BP / denosumab: oncologist consult; no proven holiday benefit but extreme caution
- CHX 0.12–0.2% mouthwash pre/post procedure
- Antibiotic prophylaxis (amoxicillin) for all surgical procedures

### 🔴 Tertiary Prevention — Established MRONJ
| Stage | Management |
|---|---|
| Stage 0–1 | CHX rinse, antibiotics, pain management; no surgery |
| Stage 2 | Antibiotics (amoxicillin ± metronidazole) + CHX + conservative debridement |
| Stage 3 | Surgical resection + primary closure; HBO therapy adjunct |

### ⚪ Quaternary Prevention
- Reduce over-prescription of antiresorptive medications
- Promote non-pharmacologic osteoporosis prevention (Ca/Vit D, weight-bearing exercise)
- Research into alternative medications with lower MRONJ risk

## Implant-Specific Guidance
- IV bisphosphonate / high-dose denosumab: **implant placement contraindicated**
- Oral BP <3yr, no risk factors: implants possible with informed consent + close monitoring
- Oral BP >3yr: drug holiday + specialist evaluation required before implant placement
