## Summary
2024 narrative review on antibiotic prophylaxis in dentistry. Guidelines have been **significantly narrowed** due to antibiotic resistance concerns. Prosthetic joint patients no longer qualify for routine AP. Only 4 specific patient groups still indicated.

## Current AP Indications

| Patient Group | AP Required? | Protocol |
|---|---|---|
| IE high-risk (4 AHA categories) | ✅ Yes | Amoxicillin 2g PO 30–60 min before |
| Prosthetic joint patients | ❌ No (AAOS/ADA 2012) | Not routinely required |
| Immunocompromised (HIV, chemo, transplant) | ✅ Case-by-case | Amoxicillin 2g; consult physician |
| IV bisphosphonate / antiangiogenic | ✅ For surgical procedures | Amoxicillin 2g |
| Head/neck irradiation (>50 Gy) | ✅ For surgical procedures | Amoxicillin 2g |
| Healthy patients — routine extractions | ❌ No | — |
| Poorly controlled DM alone | ⚠️ Not standard | Case-by-case only |

## Drug Protocol

| Situation | 1st Choice | Penicillin Allergy | Route |
|---|---|---|---|
| Standard | **Amoxicillin 2g** | Clindamycin 600mg | PO 30–60 min before |
| Cannot take oral | Ampicillin 2g | Cefazolin 1g | IM/IV 30 min before |

- Single pre-procedure dose only; post-procedure antibiotics NOT indicated
- Duration of prophylaxis effect: limited to several hours of transient bacteremia

## Why AP Indications Were Narrowed
1. **Antibiotic resistance**: major public health crisis; unnecessary prescriptions accelerate resistance
2. **Evidence gap**: no RCT evidence that AP reduces IE in moderate-risk patients
3. **Daily bacteremia**: brushing, chewing cause similar bacteremia as dental procedures in healthy patients
4. **Risk-benefit**: anaphylaxis risk from antibiotics may outweigh theoretical IE benefit in low-risk patients

## Prosthetic Joints — Key Point
- AAOS/ADA 2012 joint statement: **routine AP NOT recommended**
- Exception: immunocompromised + invasive dental procedure → individual decision
- Common misconception: many clinicians still prescribe AP for prosthetic joints without evidence basis
