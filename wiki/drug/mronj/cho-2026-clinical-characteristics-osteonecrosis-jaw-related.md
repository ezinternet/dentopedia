## One-line Summary

5-year retrospective cohort (n=178 DRONJ, KNUDH 2019-2024) demonstrating high-dose Dmab (Xgeva) achieves only 53.8% treatment success vs 85.0% for low-dose (Prolia), with Dmab dose as the sole significant predictor (OR 5.13); BP-to-Dmab switch accelerates onset but does not worsen prognosis.

## Summary

Cho et al. (2026, JKAOMS) conducted a 5-year retrospective cohort study at a single Korean tertiary center (KNUDH) in 178 patients with denosumab-related osteonecrosis of the jaw (DRONJ), divided into low-dose Dmab (Prolia 60mg, G1), BP-to-Prolia switch (G2), and high-dose Dmab (Xgeva 120mg, G3) groups. The study's primary finding is a statistically significant dose-dependent difference in treatment success: Xgeva patients achieved only 53.8% success vs 85.0% for Prolia patients (P=0.027). Multivariate logistic regression identified Dmab dose as the only significant predictor of treatment success (OR 5.13), while MRONJ stage, age, treatment duration, and lesion location were non-significant. The BP-to-Dmab switch group developed DRONJ significantly earlier after starting Dmab (16.0 vs 23.2 months, P<0.001), explained by cumulative antiresorptive duration, but showed no difference in severity or outcomes from the Prolia-only group.

## Key Contributions

- First Korean study directly comparing three denosumab-dose groups (Prolia vs BP→Prolia vs Xgeva) for DRONJ treatment outcomes in a single cohort
- Establishes Dmab dose as the sole independent predictor — MRONJ stage does not predict success, upending stage-guided treatment decisions
- BP→Dmab switch accelerates DRONJ onset (16.0 vs 23.2 months) but does NOT affect severity or outcome — cumulative antiresorptive burden explains earlier onset
- Peri-implantitis as the third-most-common precipitating factor (12.4%), after extraction (45.5%) and periodontal disease (14.0%) — critical for implant planning in antiresorptive-exposed patients
- Recurrence: G3 (Xgeva) 23.1% vs G1 (Prolia) 11.7% — reflects worse disease biology in oncology patients

## Methodology

Retrospective cohort; single institution (KNUDH, Daegu, Korea); August 2019 – August 2024; n=178 (193 eligible minus 15 excluded). Groups defined by drug and dose: G1 = Prolia (n=60); G2 = BP→Prolia switch (n=105); G3 = Xgeva (n=13). Staging per 2022 AAOMS criteria. Minimum follow-up 3 months. Treatment success = complete + partial healing. Statistics: ANOVA + Tukey, Fisher's exact/chi-square, multiple logistic regression (SPSS v29).

## Results

**Dmab duration to MRONJ onset**: G1 23.2±15.3 mo; G2 16.0±11.3 mo (P<0.001); G3 27.9±18.8 mo

**Precipitating events**: Extraction 45.5%; Periodontal disease 14.0%; Peri-implantitis 12.4%; Denture trauma 9.6%; Implant surgery 6.2%; Idiopathic 9.0%

**MRONJ staging**: Stage 1 18.0% / Stage 2 60.7% / Stage 3 21.3% — no intergroup difference (P=0.552)

**Surgical vs conservative**: 78.8% vs 71.9% complete healing (P=0.657, NS) — both approaches comparably effective, but surgery more commonly used (81.6%)

**Logistic regression**: Dmab dose OR 5.13 (95% CI 1.19-22.14; P=0.028) — all other variables NS
