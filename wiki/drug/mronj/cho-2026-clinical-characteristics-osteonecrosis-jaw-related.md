## One-line Summary

5-year retrospective cohort (n=178 DRONJ, KNUDH 2019-2024) demonstrating high-dose Dmab (Xgeva 120mg) achieves only 53.8% treatment success vs 85.0% for low-dose (Prolia 60mg), with Dmab dose as the sole significant predictor (OR 5.13, P=0.028); BP-to-Dmab switch accelerates onset by ~7 months but does not worsen prognosis.

## Summary

Cho et al. (2026, JKAOMS) conducted a 5-year retrospective cohort study at Kyungpook National University Dental Hospital (KNUDH, Daegu, Korea) in 178 patients with denosumab-related osteonecrosis of the jaw (DRONJ), divided into three groups: low-dose Dmab only (G1: Prolia 60mg/6mo, n=60), BP-to-Prolia switch (G2, n=105), and high-dose Dmab (G3: Xgeva 120mg/4wk, n=13). The study's primary finding is a statistically significant dose-dependent difference in treatment success: Xgeva patients achieved only 53.8% success vs 85.0% for Prolia patients (P=0.027). Multivariate logistic regression identified Dmab dose as the only significant predictor of treatment success (OR 5.13, 95% CI 1.19–22.14, P=0.028), while MRONJ stage, age, treatment duration, lesion location, and treatment modality were all non-significant.

The BP-to-Dmab switch group developed DRONJ significantly earlier after starting Dmab (G2: 16.0±11.3 vs G1: 23.2±15.3 months, P<0.001), explained by the cumulative antiresorptive burden (mean prior BP duration 56.7±46.4 months), but showed no difference in severity or outcomes from the Prolia-only group. The cohort is predominantly female (86%) and older (mean age 75.9±8.0yr), with osteoporosis as the primary indication in 92.7% — a distribution distinct from earlier DRONJ literature dominated by oncology/high-dose cases, reflecting Korea's 2016 national health insurance expansion for denosumab in osteoporosis.

## Key Contributions

- **First Korean 3-group comparison**: Prolia vs BP→Prolia vs Xgeva — all within one cohort, enabling direct comparison free of between-study confounders
- **Dose is decisive, stage is not**: Dmab dose (OR 5.13) is the only significant predictor; MRONJ stage, age, treatment duration, location, and treatment modality all fail to reach significance — challenges stage-guided prognostication
- **BP→Dmab switch paradox resolved**: Onset is earlier (~7 months) in G2 due to cumulative antiresorptive exposure, but severity and outcomes are identical to Prolia-only (G1); prior BP use is not a reason to expect worse surgical outcomes
- **Peri-implantitis as third trigger** (12.4%): Surpasses implant surgery (6.2%) alone; total implant-related precipitants (peri-implantitis + implant surgery) = 18.6%; critical for patient selection and counseling in antiresorptive-exposed patients considering implants
- **Surgery vs conservative equivalence**: 78.8% vs 71.9% complete healing (P=0.657); surgical management favored numerically but not significantly — supports current guidelines recommending surgical debridement while acknowledging conservative approaches for poor surgical candidates
- **High-dose Dmab recurrence**: G3 23.1% vs G1 11.7% — nearly double the recurrence risk for oncology patients

## Methodology

Retrospective cohort at a single Korean tertiary referral center (KNUDH, Daegu). Inclusion: DRONJ diagnosed August 2019–August 2024, per 2022 AAOMS criteria, minimum 3-month follow-up. Exclusion: prior jaw radiation, inaccessible medication history, refused treatment or lost to follow-up. Groups defined by drug/dose (G1: Prolia; G2: prior BP then Prolia; G3: Xgeva). Primary outcome: treatment success (complete + partial healing) at ≥3 months. Recurrence defined as re-appearance of exposed bone for >8 weeks after a previously complete-healing classification. Statistics: ANOVA+Tukey (continuous), Fisher's exact/chi-square (categorical), multiple logistic regression; SPSS v29 (IBM).

## Results

**Dmab duration to MRONJ onset**:
- G1 (Prolia only): 23.2±15.3 months
- G2 (BP→Prolia): 16.0±11.3 months (P<0.001 vs G1)
- G3 (Xgeva): 27.9±18.8 months
- G2 prior BP duration: 56.7±46.4 months

**Precipitating events** (P=0.656, no intergroup difference):
- Tooth extraction 45.5%; Periodontal disease 14.0%; Peri-implantitis 12.4%; Denture trauma 9.6%; Implant surgery 6.2%; Idiopathic 9.0%

**MRONJ staging** (P=0.552): Stage 1: 18.0% / Stage 2: 60.7% / Stage 3: 21.3%

**Location**: Mandible 60.1% (posterior 49.5%); Maxilla 34.8%; Both 5.1%

**Treatment outcomes**:

| Group | n | Treatment Success | Complete Healing | Recurrence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| G1 (Prolia 60mg) | 60 | **85.0%** | 80.0% | 11.7% |
| G2 (BP→Prolia) | 105 | **82.8%** | 79.0% | 12.4% |
| G3 (Xgeva 120mg) | 13 | **53.8%** | 53.8% | 23.1% |
| Overall | 178 | **81.5%** | 77.5% | 12.9% |
| P-value | — | **0.027** | 0.102 (NS) | — |

**Logistic regression** (predicting treatment success):

| Variable | OR | 95% CI | P |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Dmab dosage** | **5.13** | **1.19–22.14** | **0.028** |
| Age | 1.02 | 0.96–1.07 | 0.564 |
| Duration of Dmab | 1.00 | 0.98–1.03 | 0.761 |
| Location | 1.21 | 0.59–2.49 | 0.612 |
| MRONJ stage | 1.51 | 0.79–2.87 | 0.212 |
| Treatment modality | 1.02 | 0.34–3.05 | 0.976 |

Surgical vs conservative complete healing: 78.8% vs 71.9% (P=0.657, NS). Mean surgical interventions: G3 1.45 vs G1/G2 ~1.20 (P=0.231, NS).
