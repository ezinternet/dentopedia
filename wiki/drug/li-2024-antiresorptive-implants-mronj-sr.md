## One-line Summary
SR of 8 studies (445 implants) on osseointegrated implants after antiresorptive drug initiation: overall failure rate 23%, 83% of failures attributed to MRONJ, mean ARD-to-MRONJ latency 34 months, mandibular posterior 65%.

## Summary
Systematic review (PRISMA 2020) evaluating the impact of antiresorptive drug (ARD) therapy initiated after implant osseointegration. Eight studies including 445 implants in 135 subjects were analyzed. Overall implant failure rate was 23% (101/445), with 83% of documented failures attributed to MRONJ. The mean latency from ARD initiation to MRONJ diagnosis was 34 months (range 3–192 months). Mandibular posterior region was the predominant location. Peri-implantitis was identified as a likely MRONJ trigger in some cases. Most MRONJ sites healed with sequestrectomy and antibiotics; 27% of failed implants could be preserved.

## Key Contributions
- Quantifies MRONJ as the primary cause of implant failure under ARD: 83% of failures
- Mean ARD-to-MRONJ latency: **34 months** — supports active monitoring within first 3 years
- Mandibular posterior dominant: 65% mandible, 85% posterior within mandible
- Peri-implantitis implicated as a MRONJ trigger (parallels extraction-site MRONJ pathophysiology)
- Most MRONJ responds to treatment: 75% complete healing

## Methodology
- **PICO**: Patients with osseointegrated implants who started ARD after osseointegration
- **Databases**: PubMed + Embase (inception – Oct 2023) + manual search (IJOMS, JOMS, J Craniofac Surg, JOMI)
- **Inclusion**: ≥5 implants; ARD after osseointegration; ≥6 months follow-up post-MRONJ
- **Study quality**: 7/8 case series (moderate risk of bias); 1 retrospective cohort
- **No meta-analysis**: heterogeneity prevented quantitative pooling

## Results

### Demographics
- 135 subjects, 445 implants, 8 studies
- Gender: 91% female (typical of osteoporosis/breast cancer populations)
- Median age: 65 years (range 42–83)

### Drug Distribution (n=75 with ARD type reported)
| Drug | % | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Bisphosphonate total | 88% | Alendronate 44%, zoledronate 24%, risedronate |
| Denosumab | 12% | SC administration |
| Indication: osteoporosis | 93% | Low-dose ARD predominant |
| Route: oral | 61% | 39% IV or SC |

### Implant Outcomes
| Outcome | n | % |
|---|---|---|
| Survival | 344/445 | 77% |
| **Failure** | **101/445** | **23%** |
| — MRONJ cause | 76/92 | 83% |
| — Peri-implantitis cause | 16/92 | 17% |

### MRONJ Latency
| Timeframe | n (Kim 2020) |
|---|---|
| ≤12 months | 11 |
| 13–35 months | 10 |
| ≥36 months | 13 |
| **Mean (all studies)** | **34 months (3–192 months)** |

### Location
- Mandible: 65% (20/31)
- Maxilla: 36% (11/31)
- Posterior mandible: 85% of mandibular cases

### Severity (2 studies, n=15)
- Stage 2: 12/15 (majority)
- Stage 3: 3/15

### Treatment
- Sequestrectomy + antibiotics (amoxicillin/clavulanic acid) standard approach
- 75% complete healing; remaining sites stable
- ~36% of failed implants preservable in select series

## Clinical Implications
- Patients starting ARD who have osseointegrated implants require heightened monitoring for first 3 years
- Peri-implantitis should trigger immediate consideration of MRONJ risk assessment
- Oncology-dose ARD (not well represented here) likely carries substantially higher risk
- Dental implants contraindicated during high-risk ARD therapy per AAOMS 2022
