## One-line Summary
Cochrane overview of Cochrane SRs (Moore 2015): single-dose oral analgesics for acute postoperative pain — ibuprofen 200/400mg + APAP 500/1000mg combination NNT ~1.5 (lowest); ibuprofen 400mg alone ~2.5; APAP+codeine ~2.2; codeine 60mg alone NNT ~12 (essentially placebo); tramadol 75mg ~9 — NSAID+APAP combination is the standard first-line.

## Summary
Cochrane overview synthesizing multiple single-drug Cochrane SRs of single-dose oral analgesics for acute postoperative pain (mostly dental extraction populations). Uses the NNT framework — number of patients who must be treated for one to achieve ≥50% pain relief at 4-6 hours vs placebo.

The clinical bottom line is now well-established: **NSAID + APAP combination is the most effective single-dose strategy**. Ibuprofen + APAP (200/500 or 400/1000) reaches NNT ~1.5 — meaning nearly every patient gets meaningful relief. Ibuprofen 400mg alone NNT ~2.5. APAP 1000mg alone NNT ~3.5-4. Weak opioids as monotherapy are remarkably ineffective: codeine 60mg NNT ~12 (essentially indistinguishable from placebo in many patients); tramadol 75mg NNT ~9.

This is the anchoring evidence behind the 2022 ADA acute dental pain guidelines and the broader shift away from opioids in dental pain management.

## Key Contributions
- Establishes NNT framework as the standard for comparing acute analgesics.
- Quantifies ibuprofen + APAP combination superiority.
- Demonstrates that opioid monotherapy is poor pain control — they look good only because they're often combined with APAP.

## Methodology
Overview of multiple Cochrane SRs; primary outcome ≥50% pain relief over 4-6 h vs placebo, expressed as NNT.

## Results
| Drug | NNT (≥50% relief 4-6h) |
|---|---|
| Ibuprofen 200mg + APAP 500mg | ~1.5 |
| Ibuprofen 400mg + APAP 1000mg | ~1.5 |
| Ibuprofen 400mg alone | ~2.5 |
| APAP 1000mg + codeine 60mg | ~2.2 |
| APAP 1000mg alone | ~3.5-4 |
| Codeine 60mg alone | ~12 |
| Tramadol 75mg alone | ~9 |

## Limitations
- Mostly third molar / postoperative dental pain populations
- Single-dose only — does not address sustained 24-72h dosing
- 2015 evidence base; minor refinements since

## Clinical Implication (Korean Practice)
First-line acute dental pain (post-extraction, post-endodontic, post-implant): **ibuprofen 400mg + APAP 1g** PO at the time of anesthesia wear-off or pre-emptively, then ibuprofen 400mg q6h alternating with APAP 1g q6h (in healthy adults without contraindications). Avoid codeine/tramadol monotherapy. Reserve combination opioid (APAP + oxycodone 10mg) for the small subset where NSAID-APAP is inadequate and the bleeding/CV risk profile permits.
