## One-line Summary
Narrative review (Exp Ther Med 2022, Kunming Medical Univ.) — HbA1c interference systematized across 3 axes (① method-specific variants/derivatives, ② biochemical glycation rate, ③ RBC lifespan changes); catalogs false-high/false-low direction by condition (anemia, CKD, HbS/C/D/E, pregnancy, aspirin, vitamin C, dapsone) — basis for limitations of HbA1c alone in pre-extraction/pre-implant assessment.

## Summary
The Chen 2022 review proposes a **three-axis interference framework** for HbA1c interpretation: (1) **methodological** — Hb variants and HbF/derivative interference specific to each detection method (IEC, BAC, CE, IA, enzymatic); (2) **biochemical** — variable glycation rates of Hb variants vs HbA producing bias particularly in affinity chromatography; (3) **hematological** — RBC lifespan changes from hemolysis, transfusion, iron deficiency, splenectomy, CKD/uremia, and erythropoietin therapy. The paper systematically catalogs the **direction of bias** (falsely low vs falsely high HbA1c) for each interfering condition and emphasizes that clinical HbA1c must be reconciled with fingerstick capillary glucose or alternative biomarkers (fructosamine, glycated albumin, 1,5-anhydroglucitol, CGM-derived TIR) when discordance with clinical picture occurs.

## Key Contributions
- **Three-axis interference framework** as conceptual structure for HbA1c interpretation in clinical practice.
- **Method × variant cross-tabulation**: each Hb variant (S, C, D, E, HbF, HbA2 elevation) mapped to interference direction on each detection method.
- **RBC lifespan effect quantified by direction**:
  - Shortened RBC lifespan → falsely LOW HbA1c: hemolytic anemia, hereditary spherocytosis, G6PD deficiency, recent blood loss, transfusion <3 months ago, pregnancy (last trimester), erythropoietin therapy, advanced liver disease.
  - Prolonged RBC lifespan → falsely HIGH HbA1c: iron deficiency anemia, vitamin B12/folate deficiency, splenectomy.
- **Drug interference catalog**: aspirin (HbCarb-like elevation), vitamin C and E (variable by method), dapsone/ribavirin (hemolysis → falsely low), iron therapy initiation (paradoxical drop from RBC turnover normalization), HIV antiretrovirals.
- **Alternative biomarker guidance**: when HbA1c unreliable, use fructosamine (2–3 wk), glycated albumin (2–4 wk), 1,5-anhydroglucitol (1–2 wk), or CGM-derived time-in-range.

## Results
**Diagnostic and reference standards confirmed**:
- Reference range: 4–6% (20–42 mmol/mol)
- Diabetes diagnosis: ≥6.5% (≥48 mmol/mol)
- High risk: 5.7–6.4% (39–46 mmol/mol)
- NGSP-IFCC conversion: NGSP (%) = 0.09148 × IFCC (mmol/mol) + 2.152

**Conditions that falsely LOWER HbA1c (consider alternative biomarker)**:
- Hemolytic anemia, hereditary spherocytosis, G6PD deficiency
- Recent blood loss or transfusion (<3 months)
- Pregnancy (last trimester — increased erythropoiesis)
- Erythropoietin therapy
- Chronic liver disease
- Certain Hb variants on specific methods

**Conditions that falsely RAISE HbA1c**:
- Iron deficiency anemia (prolonged RBC lifespan + glycation buildup)
- Vitamin B12 or folate deficiency
- Splenectomy
- Uremia/CKD (HbCarb formation from urea-derived cyanate)
- Chronic high-dose aspirin (acetylated Hb on certain methods)
- Hyperbilirubinemia, hypertriglyceridemia (assay interference)

**Method-specific resilience**:
- **Enzymatic method**: largely variant-independent → preferred in mixed-variant populations.
- **BAC**: variant-blind but cannot distinguish HbA1c from total glycated Hb.
- **IEC/IA**: most affected by HbF and variants.

## Clinical Implications for Dentistry
- **Before relying on HbA1c for diabetic surgical risk stratification**, screen for conditions that invalidate the test:
  - CKD patient on dialysis — HbA1c falsely high from HbCarb; use glycated albumin instead.
  - Iron-deficient elderly — HbA1c falsely high; verify with fingerstick or fructosamine.
  - Patient post-transfusion (<3 months) — HbA1c falsely low; defer interpretation.
  - Pregnancy — HbA1c falsely low in 3rd trimester; use 1,5-AG or self-monitoring.
- **Population context**: for Southeast Asian patients (HbE common), African descent (HbS/C), Mediterranean/Indian (HbS) — note that IEC and IA methods may produce variant-induced bias; if testing chairside, prefer enzymatic POC device.
- **Discordance protocol**: when HbA1c contradicts fingerstick or clinical impression, treat HbA1c as suspect; do not delay urgent extraction in clearly symptomatic infection based on isolated high HbA1c.
- **Compounds with diabetic patients receiving anticoagulants/antiplatelets**: chronic high-dose aspirin may elevate HbA1c on some assays — see [[overviews/drug-anticoagulant-antiplatelet-perioperative-overview]] for parallel medication context.
