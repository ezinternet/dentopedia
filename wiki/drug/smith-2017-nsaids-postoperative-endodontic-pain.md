## One-line Summary
SR+MA (15 RCTs) in endodontic patients with preoperative pain: ibuprofen 600mg effective vs placebo at 6 hours; ibuprofen+acetaminophen combination superior to placebo but not significantly better than ibuprofen alone at 6 hours.

## 한줄요약

## Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis of 15 RCTs evaluating NSAIDs vs placebo or non-narcotic analgesics for postoperative endodontic pain in patients presenting with preoperative pain. Ibuprofen 600 mg was effective at 6 hours vs placebo (ES=10.50, p=0.037). The combination of ibuprofen 600 mg + acetaminophen 1000 mg was superior to placebo (ES=34.89, p<0.001) but not significantly better than ibuprofen alone (ES=13.94, p=0.317). All 15 studies had moderate-to-high risk of bias. Insufficient data exist to recommend the optimal NSAID, dose, or interval for pain beyond 6 hours.

## Key Contributions
- Confirms ibuprofen 600 mg as an evidence-based first-line choice at 6 h
- Combination (ibuprofen+APAP) adds no statistically significant benefit over ibuprofen alone at 6 h — though trend favors combination
- First SR+MA specifically focused on patients with preoperative pain (higher-risk subgroup)
- Adverse events: drowsiness, dizziness, nausea, emesis (5 studies); 2 studies reported none

## Methodology
PICO: preoperative-pain patients undergoing initial orthograde endodontic treatment. Databases: Ovid MEDLINE (1946–Dec 2015), Cochrane CDSR, CCTR. Two independent reviewers; Cochrane risk-of-bias tool; L'Abbé plots for qualitative review; random-effects meta-analysis for pooled effect size (ES).

## Results
| Comparison | Timepoint | ES | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ibuprofen 600 mg vs placebo | 6 h | 10.50 | 0.037 |
| Ibuprofen+APAP vs placebo | 6 h | 34.89 | <0.001 |
| Ibuprofen+APAP vs ibuprofen | 6 h | 13.94 | 0.317 (ns) |

15 studies included; all moderate-to-high risk of bias. Adverse events mild and transient.
