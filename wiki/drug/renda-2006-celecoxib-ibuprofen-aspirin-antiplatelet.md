## One-line Summary
RCT in OA + stable IHD patients (n=24): ibuprofen 600mg TID significantly blocked aspirin 100mg's platelet COX-1 inhibition (TXB2 rose to 19 ng/mL); celecoxib 200mg BID achieved equivalent COX-2 inhibition without interfering with aspirin's antiplatelet effect.

## Summary
This placebo-controlled RCT extends the ibuprofen–aspirin interaction from healthy volunteers (Catella-Lawson 2001) to patients with the exact clinical scenario where the interaction matters most: individuals with osteoarthritis who need analgesics and stable ischemic heart disease who depend on aspirin for cardiovascular protection. Twenty-four patients already on aspirin 100 mg daily were randomized to ibuprofen 600 mg TID, celecoxib 200 mg BID, or placebo for 7 days. Ibuprofen significantly elevated serum TXB2 (median 19 ng/mL pre-dose on day 7 vs. baseline 1.65 ng/mL, p < .001) and impaired platelet function on multiple measures. Celecoxib achieved equivalent COX-2 suppression (≥70% PGE2 inhibition) but left aspirin's platelet COX-1 inhibition intact.

## Key Contributions
- Confirmed the clinical relevance of ibuprofen–aspirin interaction in a real high-risk patient population
- Demonstrated that COX-2 selectivity is protective: celecoxib does not compete for the platelet COX-1 active site
- Provided quantitative pharmacodynamic data: TXB2 levels, platelet aggregation (AA- and ADP-induced), and PFA-100 occlusion time
- Supports selective COX-2 inhibitors or non-interfering alternatives (e.g., acetaminophen, diclofenac) as preferred analgesics in aspirin-dependent cardiovascular patients

## Methodology
- **Population**: 24 OA patients on chronic aspirin 100 mg/day for stable IHD
- **Design**: Placebo-controlled, randomized 3-arm crossover, 7 days per arm
- **Arms**: Celecoxib 200 mg BID / ibuprofen 600 mg TID / placebo
- **Endpoints**: Serum TXB2, AA-induced and ADP-induced platelet aggregation, PFA-100 closure time, urinary 11-dehydro-TXB2, LPS-stimulated PGE2 (COX-2 marker)

## Results
- **Ibuprofen**: Serum TXB2 rose to median 19.13 ng/mL (baseline 1.65 ng/mL; p < .001); AA-induced aggregation ↑ (p < .01); ADP-induced aggregation ↑ (p < .05); PFA-100 occlusion time ↓ (p < .01) — aspirin effect abolished
- **Celecoxib**: TXB2 and aggregation unchanged from placebo — aspirin effect fully preserved; COX-2 inhibition ≥70%
- **Ibuprofen COX-2 inhibition**: ≥80% — comparable to celecoxib, yet platelet COX-1 interference occurred
- **Urinary 11-dehydro-TXB2**: Not significantly changed by either drug (systemic thromboxane biosynthesis unaffected)
- The COX-2/COX-1 selectivity ratio, not the degree of COX-2 inhibition, determines whether aspirin's platelet protection is compromised
