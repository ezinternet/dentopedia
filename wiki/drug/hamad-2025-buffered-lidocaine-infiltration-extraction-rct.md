## One-line Summary

Double-blind prospective RCT (n=100, 50/group, ASA I/II, 18–60y), maxillary posterior extraction via supraperiosteal infiltration: buffered 2% lidocaine/1:100,000 (measured pH 7.3) beat conventional (pH 4.2) on injection pain (VAS 2.8±0.7 vs 4.2±0.9), onset (2.3 vs 4.7 min) and duration (45.8±7.6 vs 36.4±8.2 min), all p<0.01; reinjection rate NS (4% vs 8%, p=0.678).

## Summary

Most buffered-LA trials test nerve blocks (IANB). This Iraqi double-blind RCT instead tests **supraperiosteal infiltration** — the standard route for maxillary teeth — extending the buffering evidence to a different delivery method. One hundred ASA I/II adults having maxillary posterior extraction for chronic periapical lesions received either conventional 2% lidocaine/1:100,000 (verified pH 4.2) or the same buffered with 8.4% NaHCO3 at 1:10 (verified pH 7.3).

Buffering improved all three pharmacodynamic endpoints: ~33% lower injection-pain VAS, ~51% faster onset (−2.4 min), and ~26% longer duration (+9.4 min), every difference at p<0.01. The one efficacy-adjacent endpoint — need for reinjection — did not separate the groups (4% vs 8%, p=0.678), so the "better efficacy" claim rests on onset/duration rather than a hard success outcome.

The notable methodological strength is **measuring the pH** with a calibrated meter rather than assuming the alkalinization worked — confirming the mechanism (raising pH toward physiological 7.4 increases the uncharged base fraction that crosses the nerve membrane). With n=100 it is the largest single trial in this buffered-LA cluster.

## Key Contributions

1. **Infiltration-route evidence** — buffering benefit holds for maxillary supraperiosteal infiltration, not only nerve blocks.
2. **Largest cluster sample (n=100)**, G*Power-sized (effect size 0.8, power 90%).
3. **Measured pH confirmation** (4.2±0.1 vs 7.3±0.1) — mechanistic verification, not assumption.
4. Demonstrated the buffering **triad** (less injection pain, faster onset, longer duration) in a clean chronic-periapical extraction model.

## Methodology

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Design | Double-blind prospective RCT, 2 arms, Sep 2024–Apr 2025 |
| n | 100 (50/group), 18–60 y, ASA I/II, maxillary posterior + chronic periapical lesion |
| Group A (control) | Conventional 2% lidocaine/1:100,000 adrenaline (pH 4.2±0.1) |
| Group B (buffered) | 0.18 mL 8.4% NaHCO3 + 1.8 mL 2% lidocaine/1:100,000 (1:10), fresh, pH 7.3±0.1 |
| Dose / route | 1.8 mL/tooth, supraperiosteal infiltration, 27-G needle, 1 mL/min |
| Blinding | Surgeon blinded to LA; second blinded investigator assessed outcomes |
| Outcomes | Injection pain (VAS), onset (first numbness on probing), duration (to first analgesic), reinjection |
| Statistics | Independent t-test / Mann–Whitney U; Chi-square; p<0.05; SPSS |

## Results

[근거강함] Baseline comparable (age 35.2 vs 34.8 y; baseline pain VAS 6.5 vs 6.7, p=0.53).

| Parameter | Conventional (A) | Buffered (B) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Injection pain (VAS) | 4.2 ± 0.9 | 2.8 ± 0.7 | < 0.01 |
| Onset (min) | 4.7 | 2.3 | < 0.01 |
| Duration (min) | 36.4 ± 8.2 | 45.8 ± 7.6 | < 0.01 |
| Reinjection | 8% (4/50) | 4% (2/50) | 0.678 (NS) |

[근거강함] Effect sizes: ~33% less injection pain, ~51% faster onset, ~26% longer duration with buffering.

[claude해석] The only success-adjacent endpoint (reinjection) was NS — efficacy is supported by faster onset and longer duration, not by a direct success-rate measure. Buffering here improves comfort and pharmacodynamics rather than rescuing failures (unlike the SIP setting, where the failure baseline is high).

## Clinical Implications for Korean Practice

[합의수준] For 상악 구치 발치 (maxillary posterior extraction) by infiltration, buffering the standard 2% lidocaine/1:100,000 cartridge measurably reduces injection sting, speeds onset by ~2 min, and extends working time by ~9 min — directly relevant to single-tooth extraction comfort and chair efficiency.

[claude해석] The mechanism is route-agnostic (pH normalization), so the maxillary-infiltration result reasonably generalizes to other infiltration sites; nerve-block confirmation comes from the sibling IANB trials.

[추정] Prepare buffered solution immediately before injection (precipitation risk on standing). No dose adjustment was made for weight/age in this ASA I/II adult cohort.

[wiki-living-document] Healthy adult, non-inflamed (chronic periapical, not acute SIP) population; reinjection was the only efficacy proxy and was NS. Apply as "buffering improves comfort/onset/duration in routine maxillary infiltration," not as evidence for rescuing failed anesthesia — for the high-failure SIP scenario see [[drug/karunakar-2024-buffered-precooled-lidocaine-ianb-pulpitis]] and [[drug/gandhi-2022-preheated-buffered-lignocaine-ianb-pulpitis-rct]].
