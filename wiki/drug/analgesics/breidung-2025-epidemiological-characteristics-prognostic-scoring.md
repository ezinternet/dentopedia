## One-line Summary

17-year single-center retrospective (n=68 histopathology-confirmed SJS/TEN) in which metamizole — a pyrazolone analgesic — was the single most frequent culprit drug (8 cases, ahead of allopurinol's 7); overall mortality 51%, and a CHAID classification tree (AUC 0.88) outperformed SCORTEN/Re-SCORTEN/ABCD-10 for mortality prediction.

## 한줄요약

17년 단일기관 후향연구(조직검사 확진 SJS/TEN 68명)에서 **피린계 진통제 메타미졸(metamizole)이 단일 최다 원인약물(8건 > 알로푸리놀 7건)**, 전체 사망률 51%, 분류트리 모델(AUC 0.88)이 기존 SCORTEN 계열 점수보다 사망예측 우수.

## Summary

This German burn-center cohort frames the most severe expression of pyrazolone (피린계) drug allergy. Across 68 biopsy-confirmed Stevens–Johnson syndrome / toxic epidermal necrolysis (SJS/TEN) patients treated 2006–2023, **metamizole (dipyrone) was the most frequently implicated culprit drug (8/68), ranking ahead of allopurinol (7)** — a finding the authors stress contrasts with international literature where metamizole is rarely reported, and which they attribute to Germany's heavy analgesic use of the drug. Overall mortality was 51%. Higher body surface area (BSA), lower platelet count, and elevated creatinine/BUN distinguished non-survivors; among legacy scores only Re-SCORTEN separated outcomes (3.0 vs 4.2, p=0.01). A CHAID classification tree built from COPD, gender, hemoglobin, and base excess reached AUC 0.88, exceeding SCORTEN (0.61), Re-SCORTEN (0.66), and ABCD-10 (0.56), though the authors caution it is not yet ready for bedside use (single center, overlapping confidence intervals).

For dental drug-history taking, the clinical message is that a reported "pyrazolone/피린계 allergy" is not trivial: this class sits at the top of the culprit list for life-threatening SCAR in populations where it is commonly prescribed, reinforcing strict avoidance and substitution (e.g., acetaminophen) when such a history is given.

## Key Contributions

- **Metamizole = #1 SJS/TEN trigger (8/68)** in a histopathology-confirmed cohort — concrete severity evidence behind a "피린계 알러지" history.
- Quantifies the geography caveat: metamizole's high rank reflects regional (German) prescribing, not a universal hierarchy; ~20% of SJS/TEN were idiopathic (no identifiable drug).
- Admission predictors of mortality: higher BSA, lower platelets, higher creatinine and BUN (renal dysfunction / AKI).
- Re-SCORTEN was the only legacy score discriminating survivors vs non-survivors (p=0.01); SCORTEN and ABCD-10 did not.
- A CHAID classification tree (COPD, gender, hemoglobin, base excess) reached AUC 0.88 — hypothesis-generating, not validated.

## Methodology

Retrospective single-center cohort, Burn Unit, Klinikum Nuremberg (Germany), Jan 2006–Jul 2023. n=68 (33 survivors, 35 non-survivors), all **histopathologically confirmed** SJS/TEN (ICD L51.20 <30% BSA, L51.21 ≥30% BSA). Severity bands: SJS <10% BSA, SJS/TEN overlap 10–30%, TEN >30%. Admission-day demographics, comorbidities, presumed culprit drugs, clinical/laboratory parameters, and prognostic scores (SCORTEN, Re-SCORTEN, ABCD-10) collected. Statistics: Fisher's exact/Pearson, t-tests, Monte Carlo randomization, bootstrap logistic regression (OR, 95% CI), CHAID classification tree with 10-fold cross-validation, ROC/AUC comparison; p<0.05.

## Results

- **Mortality 51%** (35/68).
- BSA at admission: survivors 43.4%±29.7% vs non-survivors 56.8%±26.2% (p<0.05).
- Severity: SJS 6% in both groups; overlap 36% (surv) vs 11% (non-surv, p=0.02); TEN 58% (surv) vs 83% (non-surv, p=0.02).
- Labs: platelets 160.2 vs 221.4; creatinine 1.3±1.0 vs 1.9±1.8 mg/dL (p=0.05); BUN 30.9±19.7 vs 45.2±30.6 mmol/L (p=0.02). Hb, RDW, RDW/Hb, AST/ALT, CRP, procalcitonin, glucose, lactate, base excess, bicarbonate NS.
- Scores: Re-SCORTEN 3.0 vs 4.2 (p=0.01); SCORTEN p=0.20; ABCD-10 p=0.65.
- **Culprit drugs** (26 across 68): metamizole 8, allopurinol 7, piperacillin/tazobactam 4, ciprofloxacin 4; ~20% (14) no definite drug.
- Classification tree: accuracy 89%, NPV 81%, PPV 95%, sensitivity 93%, specificity 86%; **AUC 0.88** vs Re-SCORTEN 0.66, SCORTEN 0.61, ABCD-10 0.56.
