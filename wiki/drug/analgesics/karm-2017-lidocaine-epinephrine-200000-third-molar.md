## One-line Summary
Multicenter RCT crossover (NCT02696369, 6 university hospitals, n=65 enrolled/51 analyzed, KDA Journal 56(1) 2017): 2% lidocaine with 1:80,000 vs 1:200,000 epinephrine for impacted mandibular third-molar extraction — analgesic efficacy equivalent (VAS, duration, bleeding, satisfaction all NS), but systolic blood pressure and pulse changes significantly smaller with 1:200,000 (p≤0.01); 1:200,000 recommended for cardiovascularly vulnerable patients.

## Summary

Korean-language KDA Journal introduction article (vol 56, no. 1, Dec 2017) summarizing a multicenter, randomized, double-blind, crossover, phase IV trial registered as **NCT02696369**. The trial compared 2% lidocaine HCl with two different epinephrine concentrations — **1:80,000 (L80)** vs **1:200,000 (L200)** — for surgical extraction of bilateral impacted mandibular third molars, with each participant serving as own control across two visits 1–4 weeks apart.

The clinical question is practical: vasoconstrictor (epinephrine) prolongs anesthesia and reduces bleeding, but higher catecholamine load increases blood pressure and heart rate. Can the epinephrine concentration be reduced from the conventional 1:80,000 to 1:200,000 without losing analgesic efficacy?

The answer: **yes — efficacy is preserved and hemodynamic load is reduced**. All efficacy endpoints (immediate-post-extraction VAS, 2/4/6 h VAS, onset and duration of analgesia, intraoperative bleeding, operator and participant satisfaction, drug dose, adverse event rate) showed no statistically significant difference between groups (p > 0.05). The only significant difference favored L200: changes in systolic blood pressure and heart rate after anesthetic injection were significantly smaller (p ≤ 0.01).

Clinical implication: for hemodynamically vulnerable patients — uncontrolled hypertension, ischemic heart disease, arrhythmia — switching from 1:80,000 to 1:200,000 epinephrine in 2% lidocaine gives the same analgesia with less cardiovascular load. The trial population was ASA I–II healthy adults, so direct extrapolation to the highest-risk groups is limited by design.

## Key Contributions

1. **Multicenter RCT-level evidence for equivalent efficacy at half the epinephrine dose**: prior literature (Aggarwal 2014 in symptomatic irreversible pulpitis) had shown this for IAN block; this trial extends the finding to surgical extraction of bilateral impacted mandibular third molars — a more painful, more vasoconstriction-dependent indication.
2. **Quantified hemodynamic safety margin**: ΔSBP and ΔHR significantly smaller with 1:200,000 (p ≤ 0.01). Continuous monitoring protocol (q1 min × 5 min → q3 min × 20 min → q5 min × 70 min) gives a robust hemodynamic curve.
3. **Crossover design controls inter-patient variability**: each participant received both formulations contralaterally, eliminating pain-threshold confounding between groups.
4. **Practical recommendation for high-risk cohorts**: in patients where the cardiovascular safety margin matters more than the marginal vasoconstriction benefit, 1:200,000 is the preferred choice.

## Methodology

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Design | Multicenter, RCT, double-blind, crossover (within-subject), phase IV |
| Registration | NCT02696369 |
| Sites | 6 university dental hospitals (Seoul NU, Pusan NU, Kyung Hee, Dankook, Yonsei, Wonkwang) |
| n | 65 enrolled (L80 first n=34; L200 first n=31); 51 analyzed; 78.5% completion |
| Inclusion | ≥19 y; ASA I–II; bilateral impacted mandibular 3rd molars on panoramic; informed consent |
| Exclusion | Lidocaine hypersensitivity; active infection/abscess; bleeding disorder; hyperthyroidism; atherosclerosis; heart failure; seizure; uncontrolled HTN or DM; current vasoconstrictor/antidepressant/sedative/analgesic; anticoagulant/antiplatelet/steroid within 1 wk; concurrent surgery; pregnancy/lactation; prior oral surgery |
| Intervention | Each subject received L80 at one side and L200 at contralateral side across two visits, 1–4 wk apart |
| Primary outcome | VAS pain immediately post-extraction |
| Secondary outcomes | Onset/duration of analgesia; VAS at 2/4/6 h; time to first pain; intraoperative bleeding; operator and participant satisfaction; drug dose; adverse events; vital signs (BP, HR) for 70 min |
| Vital-sign sampling | q1 min × first 5 min → q3 min to 20 min → q5 min to 70 min |
| Statistics | Paired t-test / Wilcoxon signed rank / unpaired t-test as appropriate; α = 0.05; SPSS v21 |
| Power | α = 0.025, β = 0.1, power 90%; prior VAS difference 15 mm → 23/group needed; with 20% dropout, ≥29/group enrolled |

## Clinical Implications for Korean Practice

[합의수준] For 매복 하악 제3대구치 발치 (impacted mandibular third molar extraction):

- **Healthy ASA I–II patients**: either L80 or L200 acceptable — no efficacy difference.
- **Hypertension (controlled), prior cardiovascular event, arrhythmia, elderly**: **L200 preferred** based on the hemodynamic safety margin demonstrated.
- **Uncontrolled hypertension, recent MI, unstable angina**: this trial excluded these patients; L200 is the safer extrapolation but procedural decision should follow Korean cardiology consult and the broader local anesthesia guidelines.
- **Hemostatic concern only (anticoagulant/antiplatelet patients)**: this trial excluded them; intraoperative bleeding showed NS between L80 and L200, supporting that the vasoconstriction at 1:200,000 is clinically adequate for routine hemostasis.

[claude해석] The Korean dental market historically defaulted to 2% lidocaine + 1:100,000 epinephrine (Yuhan / Huons preparations) and 1:80,000 (often in 2.2 mL Septanest-equivalent cartridges). The 1:200,000 formulation maps to the lower-vasoconstrictor option used in dental anesthesiology departments for cardiovascular-risk patients. This paper provides the RCT-level evidence base for that choice in third molar surgery.

## Limitations (Critical Reading)

[claude해석]
1. **Healthy population only** — ASA I–II enrollment is the standard for phase IV efficacy trials but means the patients who most need the lower-epinephrine formulation (CVD, uncontrolled HTN) were excluded by design. The authors acknowledge this and flag the ethical complexity of conducting the follow-up trial in cardiovascular patients.
2. **Crossover period effect** — 1–4 wk washout assumes no residual effect on the second-side measurement. Acute surgical recovery (swelling, jaw guarding, central sensitization) could plausibly bias second-visit outcomes. The randomization of first-side assignment partially mitigates.
3. **No long-term safety / post-op course comparison** — focus is on intra- and immediate post-op only.
4. **Single ethnic population** — Korean adults; pharmacogenomic variation in lidocaine/epinephrine metabolism (e.g., CYP1A2/CYP3A4) is not addressed.
5. **KDA Journal format** — Korean-language summary by the trial PI; the full numeric data and figures are in the corresponding English publication (J Dent Anesth Pain Med 2017). For research use, cite both.

[wiki-living-document] If the 1:200,000 formulation availability in Korea changes (manufacturing/import status), this recommendation needs revisiting — the conclusion assumes the formulation is procurable.
