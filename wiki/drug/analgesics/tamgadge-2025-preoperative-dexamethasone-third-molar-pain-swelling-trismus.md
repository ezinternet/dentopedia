## One-line Summary

Split-mouth single-blind placebo-controlled trial (n=60 bilateral impacted mandibular third molars): a single preoperative 4 mg IM dexamethasone significantly lowered postoperative VAS pain (day 2: 1.2 vs 2.3; day 7: 0.4 vs 1.6, both p<0.001), improved mouth opening (3.5 vs 2.7 cm, p<0.001), and reduced facial swelling by day 7 (2.1 vs 2.8 cm, p=0.04), with no adverse reactions.

## Summary

This split-mouth, single-blind, placebo-controlled clinical trial evaluated whether a single preoperative intramuscular injection of 4 mg dexamethasone reduces the three classic sequelae of impacted mandibular third molar surgery — postoperative pain, swelling, and trismus. Each of 60 patients (aged 18–30) received the active drug on the right (test) side and a sterile-water placebo on the left (control) side, with the two surgeries separated by at least three weeks. Dexamethasone significantly outperformed placebo on pain at both day 2 and day 7, on mouth opening at both timepoints, and on swelling by day 7, with no adverse reactions reported. The findings support routine preoperative corticosteroid use for third molar extractions.

## Key Contributions

- Intra-patient (split-mouth) confirmation that a single low-dose preoperative IM dexamethasone benefits all three classic third-molar sequelae — pain, trismus, swelling — simultaneously.
- A simple, cheap, single-dose preoperative protocol (4 mg IM) with no observed adverse reactions.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Split-mouth, single-blind, placebo-controlled (each patient own control).
- **n**: 60 patients, 18–30 years, all needing bilateral surgical removal of impacted mandibular third molars.
- **Intervention**: Right side (test) = 4 mg dexamethasone IM preoperatively; left side (control) = 1 cc sterile-water placebo. Surgeries ≥3 weeks apart.
- **Exclusions**: medical comorbidities, pregnancy, symptomatic third molars.
- **Outcomes** (preop, day 2, day 7): pain (VAS), mouth opening (interincisal, Vernier caliper), facial swelling (fixed anatomical landmarks).
- Impaction types: mesioangular 41.66%, vertical 30%, horizontal 21.66%, distoangular 6.66%.

## Results

| Outcome | Test (dexamethasone) | Control (placebo) | P |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pain VAS, day 2 | 1.2 ± 0.5 | 2.3 ± 0.7 | <0.001 |
| Pain VAS, day 7 | 0.4 ± 0.2 | 1.6 ± 0.6 | <0.001 |
| Mouth opening (cm) | 3.5 ± 0.3 | 2.7 ± 0.4 | <0.001 |
| Swelling (cm) | 2.1 ± 0.3 | 2.8 ± 0.5 | 0.04 (day 7) |

- Pain significantly lower in the dexamethasone arm at both day 2 and day 7.
- Mouth opening significantly better with dexamethasone at both timepoints.
- Swelling significantly reduced by day 7; no significant difference at day 2.
- No adverse reactions in either arm.
- **Limitations**: moderate single-center sample, single-blind only, subjective VAS pain measure, no standardized dosing protocol established.
