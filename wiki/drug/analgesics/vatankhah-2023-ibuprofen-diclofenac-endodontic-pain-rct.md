## 한줄요약
RCT (Eur Endod J 2023): 비가역적 치수염 1대구치 endodontic treatment 후 — diclofenac potassium 50mg이 ibuprofen 400mg보다 pain-free 비율 + 평균 VAS 점수 유의하게 우수. Ibuprofen 무반응 환자의 대안.

## Summary
RCT comparing ibuprofen 400mg vs diclofenac potassium 50mg single doses for management of postoperative endodontic pain (PEP) in maxillary and mandibular first molars with irreversible pulpitis. Diclofenac potassium showed:
- Significantly more patients fully pain-free
- Significantly lower mean pain scores at several follow-up timepoints
Conclusion: diclofenac potassium is a viable better-tolerated alternative for established irreversible pulpitis PEP management.

## Key Contributions
- Head-to-head NSAID comparison in endodontic PEP indication.
- Demonstrates diclofenac potassium superiority over ibuprofen monotherapy.
- Provides Korean dentists with an alternative when ibuprofen response is inadequate.

## Methodology
RCT in irreversible pulpitis patients undergoing first molar endodontic treatment; primary outcome PEP; VAS pain at multiple timepoints; pain-free rate.

## Results
[근거강함]
- Pain-free rate: diclofenac K > ibuprofen (significant)
- Mean VAS: diclofenac K < ibuprofen (significant at multiple timepoints)

## Limitations
- Single-center
- Single dose tested (no titration arm)
- No ibuprofen + APAP combination arm (which is the current gold standard from Moore 2015 NNT data)
- Iranian population — generalizability acceptable but to be noted

## Clinical Implication
For Korean endodontic practice: as alternative NSAID for ibuprofen-inadequate response in irreversible pulpitis PEP, diclofenac potassium 50mg is supported. The combination ibuprofen + APAP remains the best-evidence first-line, but this RCT enriches the second-line option set.
