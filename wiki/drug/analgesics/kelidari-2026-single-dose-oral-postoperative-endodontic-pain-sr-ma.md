## One-line Summary

Frequentist network meta-analysis (10 RCTs qualitative, 5 quantitative, n=347): for single-dose oral analgesia after non-surgical RCT, Diclofenac+Acetaminophen is best for immediate relief (6–8 h, MD −6.28), while Novafen and Naproxen sustain efficacy at 12–24 h; Diclofenac+Acetaminophen reverses toward placebo by 24 h.

## 한줄요약

네트워크 메타분석 (RCT 10편 정성·5편 정량, n=347): 비수술 근관치료 후 단회투여 경구 진통제 — 즉각(6–8h) 진통은 디클로페낙+아세트아미노펜(MD −6.28)이 최고, 12–24h 지속 효과는 노바펜·나프록센이 우수, 디클로페낙+아세트아미노펜은 24h엔 위약 수준으로 소실.

## Summary

Post-endodontic pain after non-surgical root canal treatment (RCT) is common and erodes patient trust. Single-dose postoperative analgesics are routinely prescribed, but the relative efficacy of the available regimens — and how that ranking shifts over the first 24 hours — was unclear. This is the **first SR/NMA limited to single-dose postoperative medication** (deliberately excluding pre- and intra-operative dosing, and multi-dose regimens, to reduce adverse effects, cost, and adherence problems).

The clinical bottom line is **time-dependent**:

- **Immediate (6–8 h)**: Diclofenac + Acetaminophen gives the largest reduction, then Novafen, then Ibuprofen + Acetaminophen.
- **Sustained (12–24 h)**: Novafen (ibuprofen + acetaminophen + caffeine) and Naproxen are the standouts; Tramadol and Ibuprofen give moderate sustained relief.
- **Fading combinations**: Diclofenac + Acetaminophen and Ibuprofen + Acetaminophen lose significance by 12 h, and Diclofenac + Acetaminophen even trends toward placebo by 24 h — so it needs redosing or a longer-acting alternative for prolonged pain.

Practically: pick **Diclofenac + Acetaminophen or Novafen for fast onset**, and **Naproxen (or Novafen) for 24-h coverage**. NSAIDs (especially naproxen and ibuprofen) remain the dependable first line. Tramadol works but carries opioid side effects; alprazolam adds little true analgesia (its benefit is anxiolytic/psychogenic) and is not advised as a routine analgesic adjunct.

## Key Contributions

- Isolates single-dose **postoperative** analgesia after RCT — the actual prescribing decision a clinician faces at the end of the appointment.
- Time-resolved P-score rankings at 6–8 h, 12 h, 24 h rather than one pooled estimate.
- Surfaces the fast-but-short (diclofenac+acetaminophen) vs sustained (naproxen, Novafen) pharmacokinetic divide, and the caffeine-driven persistence of Novafen over plain ibuprofen+acetaminophen.

## Methodology

- PRISMA 2020 SR + frequentist NMA (R v4.4.3, netmeta). PROSPERO CRD420251001005.
- PICO: endodontic-treatment patients (moderate-to-severe preop pain, mostly irreversible pulpitis) → single-dose oral med after RCT vs placebo → VAS pain.
- Search: PubMed, Scopus, Embase, Cochrane CENTRAL to Feb 19, 2025; no limits.
- RoB 2 risk of bias (most studies moderate-to-good quality).
- Outcomes: mean differences (MD, 95% CI) vs placebo at 6–8 / 12 / 24 h; random-effects when I² > 50%; P-score ranking; design-by-treatment global inconsistency; sensitivity analysis excluding Islam et al.; GRADE-for-NMA.
- 10 RCTs (1999–2021) qualitative; **5 RCTs quantitative, combined n=347** (Menhinick 2004, Islam 2018, Mehrvarzfar 2012, Baradaran 2014, Makkar 2012). Five others excluded for missing SD.

## Results

| Drug (vs placebo) | 6–8 h MD (95% CI) | 12 h MD (95% CI) | 24 h MD (95% CI) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Diclofenac + Acetaminophen | **−6.28** (−11.99, −0.56) | NS (wide) | +0.50 (p=0.93, → placebo) |
| Novafen (ibu+acet+caffeine) | −3.46 (−5.27, −1.65) | **−2.70** (−3.87, −1.52) | **−2.89** (−4.14, −1.63) |
| Naproxen | −2.62 (−3.97, −1.27) | −2.58 (−3.44, −1.73) | −2.48 (−3.42, −1.54) |
| Ibuprofen + Acetaminophen | −3.05 (−4.80, −1.30) | NS | NS |
| Ibuprofen | (favorable, smaller) | −1.64 (−2.57, −0.71) | −1.32 (−2.28, −0.37) |
| Tramadol | (sig, smaller) | −1.46 (−2.35, −0.57) | −1.39 (−2.41, −0.38) |
| Ibuprofen + Alprazolam | −1.75 (−3.56, 0.07) NS | −2.39 (−3.75, −1.04) sig | NS |

- **6–8 h P-score top 3**: Diclofenac+Acetaminophen, Novafen, Ibuprofen+Acetaminophen.
- **24 h P-score top 3**: Novafen, Naproxen, Tramadol.
- High heterogeneity I² > 70% at 6–8 h and 24 h. Excluding Islam et al. resolved global inconsistency and left effects unchanged (robust). Funnel plots symmetrical; most GRADE comparisons moderate-to-high certainty.
- Mechanism: NSAIDs inhibit COX/prostaglandins (plus central opioid/NO modulation); acetaminophen acts centrally; Novafen's caffeine prolongs action; alprazolam is anxiolytic, not analgesic.

**Best single-dose drug for post-endodontic pain**: **Diclofenac + Acetaminophen for immediate (6–8 h) relief**, and **Novafen or Naproxen for sustained (12–24 h) coverage**; Naproxen and Novafen are the most consistent across the full 24 h.
