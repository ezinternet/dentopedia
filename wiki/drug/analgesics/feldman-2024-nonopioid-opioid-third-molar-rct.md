## One-line Summary
Multisite RCT (n=1,815, JADA 2025): after impacted mandibular third-molar extraction, non-opioid (ibuprofen 400 mg + APAP 500 mg) produced significantly less pain on days 1–2 (MD −0.70, p<0.001) than opioid (hydrocodone 5 mg + APAP 300 mg) and was noninferior over the full period; opioids showed no advantage at any time point.

## Summary
The largest multisite RCT (n=1,815 adults, 6+ academic dental centers) comparing nonopioid (ibuprofen 400mg+APAP 500mg) versus opioid (hydrocodone 5mg+APAP 300mg) combination analgesics taken q4–6h PRN after bony mandibular third-molar extraction. Nonopioid group had significantly less pain on days 1–2 and 2–3 post-operatively; was noninferior to opioid over the full postoperative period; and achieved higher overall patient satisfaction (85.3% vs 78.9%, p=0.006). Opioid regimen did not outperform nonopioid at any measured timepoint. Results directly confirm the ADA recommendation that ibuprofen+APAP should be first-line pharmacotherapy for acute dental surgical pain; routine opioid prescribing after dental extractions is not supported.

## Key Contributions
- Largest RCT to directly compare opioid vs nonopioid combination for acute dental surgical pain
- Nonopioid superior on primary endpoint (days 1–2 pain NRS) and on satisfaction
- Noninferiority confirmed over entire postoperative period — no clinical rationale for opioid prescribing
- ADA 2022 CPG recommendation on first-line ibuprofen+APAP confirmed by powered pragmatic trial

## Methodology
Double-blind, randomized, stratified noninferiority trial. Two arms: (A) hydrocodone 5mg+APAP 300mg; (B) ibuprofen 400mg+APAP 500mg. Both taken q4–6h PRN starting after extraction. Outcomes: NRS pain score (day/night averages), pain interference, sleep quality, overall satisfaction. Stratified by sex. Multisite, IRB: Rutgers, DSMB appointed by NIDCR. NCT04452344.

## Results
| Timepoint | MD (nonopioid minus opioid) | 95% CI | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Day 1–2 pain | −0.70 | −0.94 to −0.45 | <0.001 |
| Day 2–3 pain | −0.28 | −0.52 to −0.04 | 0.015 |
| Full period (noninferiority) | −0.20 | −0.45 to 0.05 | 0.172 (noninferior) |
| Overall satisfaction (nonopioid) | 85.3% vs 78.9% | OR 1.21–1.98 | 0.006 |
