## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 6 RCTs (n=262) in periodontal surgery: NSAIDs outperform corticosteroids (SAIDs) for early pain control (0–6 h, MD 1.00–2.33 on 100-pt VAS), SAIDs outperform NSAIDs at 48 h (MD −0.23), swelling and rescue medication comparable — moderate-to-low GRADE certainty; clinically insignificant differences overall.

## Summary

This PRISMA-guided SR+MA (Abusamak et al., JDR Clinical & Translational Research, 2025, PROSPERO CRD42024568891) is the first systematic review to directly compare perioperative steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (SAIDs, i.e., corticosteroids such as dexamethasone) versus nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs, i.e., ibuprofen, etoricoxib, celecoxib) for postoperative recovery in adults undergoing periodontal surgery. Of 2,354 screened articles, 6 RCTs with 262 participants met inclusion criteria (direct SAID vs NSAID comparison in periodontal surgery patients). A random-effects model was used; evidence quality was assessed with GRADE.

**Key finding — time-dependent reversal of drug efficacy:** NSAIDs are statistically superior for pain reduction in the first 6 postoperative hours (MD 1.00–2.33 VAS points), while SAIDs provide better pain control at 48 hours (MD 0.23 VAS points). Swelling and rescue analgesic consumption were comparable between drug classes. Both differences, while statistically significant, are of uncertain clinical importance given the small absolute VAS differences and moderate-to-low GRADE certainty.

**Pharmacokinetic explanation:** NSAIDs (e.g., ibuprofen: half-life 1–2 h, plasma peak 30–60 min) have faster onset than SAIDs (e.g., dexamethasone: half-life 6–12 h, plasma peak 3–4 h). For SAIDs to provide perioperative benefits, authors recommend starting the night before and 1–2 hours preoperatively, with a dose at 6 hours postoperatively.

**Clinical decision by surgery type:** SAIDs may be preferred for complex/plastic periodontal procedures (GBR, soft-tissue augmentation, connective tissue grafting) where swelling control is critical to prevent wound dehiscence. NSAIDs are a reasonable choice for simple periodontal procedures (open flap debridement) where tension-free wound closure is less critical.

## Key Contributions

1. **Time-dependent reversal**: NSAIDs > SAIDs early (0–6 h); SAIDs > NSAIDs late (48 h) — first direct head-to-head comparison in periodontal surgery context.
2. **Swelling and rescue medication parity**: Neither drug class is superior on secondary outcomes, simplifying selection criteria for these endpoints.
3. **Pharmacokinetic-based dosing rationale**: Onset kinetics explain why single preoperative SAID dosing is suboptimal — pre-night + pre-morning loading recommended.
4. **Surgery-type stratification**: Complex wound-healing scenarios favor SAIDs; simpler procedures tolerate either agent.
5. **GRADE audit**: All outcomes rated moderate-to-low certainty — robust clinical guidelines cannot yet be written without further multicentric RCTs.

## Methodology

- **Study type**: SR+MA (PRISMA) — PROSPERO CRD42024568891
- **Search**: EMBASE, MEDLINE, Scopus, and additional databases; up to April 2025
- **PICOS**: Adults undergoing periodontal surgery (P); perioperative SAIDs (I) vs NSAIDs (C); pain, swelling, rescue medication (O); RCTs (S)
- **Included studies**: 6 RCTs (from 14 identified); n=262 participants total
- **Excluded**: Studies comparing only to placebo, or third-molar extraction studies
- **Statistical model**: Random-effects (Knapp-Hartung variance estimator)
- **Heterogeneity**: Cochrane Q + I²
- **Risk of bias**: RoB 2 tool
- **Evidence quality**: GRADE per outcome

## Results

### Postoperative Pain (VAS 0–100) — head-to-head SAIDs vs NSAIDs

| Timepoint | Favors | MD (95% CI) | I² | GRADE |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1–6 h | NSAIDs | MD 1.00–2.33 (0.21, 3.77) | 0–19% | Moderate |
| 7–8 h | Neither | MD ~0 | — | Low |
| 24 h | Neither | MD −0.39 (−2.09, 1.31) | >50% | Very low |
| 48 h | SAIDs | MD −0.23 (−0.31, −0.14) | 0% | Moderate |
| 72 h | Neither | MD 0.00 (−0.04, 0.03) | — | Low |

### Secondary Outcomes

| Outcome | Timepoint | Result | GRADE |
|---|---|---|---|
| Swelling (VAS) | 24 h | No sig. diff. (MD −3.38; CI −126.81 to 120.04) | Very low |
| Rescue medication | Various | No sig. diff. (MD 0.01; CI −0.33 to 0.36) | Low |

**Bottom line**: Statistically significant differences exist at 0–6 h (NSAIDs) and 48 h (SAIDs) for pain, but absolute VAS differences (≤2.33 points on 100-point scale) are likely below minimal clinically important difference. All swelling and rescue medication results are non-significant with wide CIs.

### Adverse Events

Only 3 of 6 included RCTs reported adverse events. No major adverse events attributed to short-term, low-dose perioperative SAIDs or NSAIDs. Safety conclusions remain limited by underreporting.
