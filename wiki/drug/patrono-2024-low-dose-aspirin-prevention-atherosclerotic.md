## One-line Summary

State-of-the-art narrative review of 30 years of low-dose aspirin (75–100 mg/day) evidence: irreversible platelet COX-1 acetylation suppresses thromboxane A2, reducing atherothrombosis (e.g., RR 0.52, NNT 10 in one secondary-prevention dataset) while increasing GI bleeding and lowering colorectal adenoma recurrence.

## Summary

This European Heart Journal state-of-the-art review by Carlo Patrono revisits the role of low-dose aspirin across the cardiovascular risk continuum after 30 years of antiplatelet developments. It explains aspirin's mechanism (selective, irreversible acetylation of Ser-529 on platelet COX-1, suppressing thromboxane A2 biosynthesis), pharmacokinetics, and pharmacodynamics, then discusses clinical efficacy and safety. Repeated daily dosing produces a cumulative eight-fold potency shift (ID50 from 26 mg single-dose to ~3 mg). Low-dose aspirin reduces coronary atherothrombosis and recurrence, increases gastrointestinal bleeding from pre-existing mucosal lesions, and decreases sporadic colorectal adenoma recurrence. The piece also covers the efficacy-to-safety emphasis shift, aspirin-free regimens after PCI, and P2Y12-inhibitor monotherapy comparisons.

## Key Contributions

- Consolidates mechanism, pharmacology, cardiovascular efficacy/safety, and chemoprevention into a single clinical perspective.
- Clarifies why low doses spare COX-2 (short half-life + nucleated-cell resynthesis) while permanently blocking platelet COX-1.
- Useful reference for weighing aspirin continuation vs interruption around dental surgery (bleeding vs thrombotic risk).

## Methodology

- Expert narrative / state-of-the-art review (no systematic search). Draws on IPD overviews (Antiplatelet Trialists' Collaboration, 145 trials), RCTs, and pharmacology literature.
- Organised mechanism → pharmacokinetics → pharmacodynamics → efficacy/safety.

## Results

- Ser-529 COX-1 acetylation → near-complete TXA2 suppression at low daily doses.
- ID50 shift: 26 mg (single) → ~3 mg (repeated daily) — eight-fold cumulative shift.
- Pharmacokinetics: ~50% oral bioavailability, 15–20 min half-life.
- Efficacy (graphical-abstract dataset): aspirin 75 mg vs placebo RR=0.52, p=0.0001, NNT=10.
- Trade-offs: ↓atherothrombosis, ↑GI bleeding, ↓colorectal adenoma recurrence.
- Antiplatelet Trialists' Collaboration: medium-dose (75–325 mg) not bettered by higher doses for vascular-event prevention.
