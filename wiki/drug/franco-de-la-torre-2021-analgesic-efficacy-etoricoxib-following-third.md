## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 8 high-quality trials (6 pooled) found etoricoxib (120 mg dominant dose) significantly reduced the NUMBER of patients needing rescue analgesia after third molar surgery vs nonselective NSAIDs (p=0.0004; vs ibuprofen 400 mg p=0.00001), with no significant difference in adverse effects.

## Summary

This is the first meta-analysis to isolate the analgesic effectiveness of etoricoxib alone — rather than coxibs as a class — against nonselective NSAIDs after surgical removal of mandibular third molars. From 149 records, 11 met PICO criteria, 8 were high-quality (low risk of bias), and 6 entered the quantitative analysis. Etoricoxib 120 mg was the dose used in all quality studies, mostly as a single postoperative dose. The key finding was a significantly lower number of patients requiring rescue analgesia in the etoricoxib group versus NSAIDs (p=0.0004), and versus ibuprofen 400 mg specifically (p=0.00001). Global treatment assessment trended in favor of etoricoxib without reaching significance, and adverse-effect rates did not differ significantly from nonselective NSAIDs.

## Key Contributions

- Isolates etoricoxib's individual efficacy, addressing the gap left by class-level coxib analyses.
- Confirms a clinically meaningful reduction in rescue-analgesia need.
- Reports no excess adverse effects vs nonselective NSAIDs in this short postoperative window.

## Methodology

- Search: PubMed + Google Scholar; English/Spanish; trials to July 2020.
- PICO: third molar removal patients; etoricoxib vs nonselective COX-2 NSAIDs; outcomes = total rescue analgesic consumption, number using rescue analgesics, VAS pain, global assessment.
- Quality: Cochrane risk-of-bias tool; only low-RoB trials extracted.
- Statistics: inverse variance + SMD (continuous); Mantel-Haenszel + OR (dichotomous); Review Manager 5.3; p≤0.05.

## Results

- 149 → 11 PICO → 8 high-quality → 6 meta-analysis.
- Etoricoxib dose 120 mg in all quality studies (range 60–240 mg).
- Qualitative: etoricoxib superior to ibuprofen (2 trials) and diclofenac (1 trial); similar in 3/8 (2 vs ibuprofen, 1 vs naproxen sodium).
- Fewer patients needing rescue analgesia with etoricoxib vs NSAIDs (p=0.0004); vs ibuprofen 400 mg p=0.00001.
- Total rescue consumption (single study, Calvo): MD −0.44 (95% CI −1.38 to 0.5; p=0.36, NS).
- Adverse effects (6 trials): no significant difference.
- Mechanism: etoricoxib COX-2 selectivity ratio ≈106; ED50 3.27 mg/kg (mice).
