## Summary
Narrative review of prosthodontic treatment modifications for 9 systemic disease categories commonly affecting aged patients. Unique focus: restorative/prosthetic decision-making (fixed vs removable, implant eligibility) under medical compromise.

## Quick-Reference: Prosthodontic Modifications by Disease

| Disease | Key Prosthetic Concern | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Osteoarthritis (TMJ)** | Limited mouth opening | Short appointments; special trays; delay definitive FDP in active phase |
| **Rheumatoid Arthritis** | ↓ Manual dexterity; prosthetic joints | Fixed > removable; antibiotic prophylaxis for implants; delay active flare |
| **Hypertension / IHD** | Stress-induced events | Short midday appointments; profound LA; keep nitroglycerin accessible |
| **Post-MI** | Unstable phase | Delay definitive prosthetics ≥6 months; emergency only in first month |
| **Diabetes (uncontrolled)** | Delayed healing; infection | HbA1c check; delay implants if HbA1c ≥9%; morning appointments |
| **Bisphosphonates** | MRONJ / BRONJ risk | Oral BP <3yr: proceed; oral BP >3yr or IV BP: drug holiday; no implants in irradiated fields |
| **Head/Neck Radiation** | Osteoradionecrosis | No implants in radiation field without HBO evaluation; XRT dose >50Gy = contraindication |
| **Chemotherapy (active)** | Mucositis; ↓ healing | Defer elective; check WBC/platelets before proceeding |
| **CKD** | Platelet dysfunction; drug clearance | Avoid NSAIDs; adjust drug doses; check INR if on dialysis-related anticoagulants |
| **Cirrhosis** | ↓ Clotting factors; ↓ drug metabolism | Check INR; reduce LA/acetaminophen doses; avoid hepatotoxic drugs |
| **COPD** | Orthopnea | Semi-supine position; avoid rubber dam; avoid respiratory depressants |
| **Parkinson's** | Tremor; drooling | Fixed > removable; shorter appointments; swallowing assessment |

## Key Drug Alerts

| Drug | Condition | Concern |
|---|---|---|
| Epinephrine | Hyperthyroidism | Arrhythmia risk → use plain LA or reduce epi |
| NSAIDs | COPD with aspirin-sensitivity (AERD) | Bronchospasm → use acetaminophen |
| Methacrylate (denture) | RA patients on methotrexate | Theoretical interaction → monitor for mucosal reaction |
| Acetaminophen | Cirrhosis | Hepatotoxic even at therapeutic doses → limit to <2g/day |

## Bisphosphonate / MRONJ Protocol Summary
- Oral BP <3 years, no risk factors: routine treatment OK
- Oral BP >3 years OR systemic risk factors: drug holiday 2 months before/after invasive procedure
- IV bisphosphonate (oncologic): implants and surgical extractions contraindicated without specialist evaluation
