## Summary
Narrative review covering NSAIDs in all dental specialties. NSAIDs are the **WHO first-line drugs** for orofacial pain and inflammation. Used across all dental fields. Additional anti-inflammatory mechanisms beyond COX inhibition identified. Topical NSAIDs (benzydamine, diclofenac) represent a promising direction to avoid systemic side effects.

## NSAID Mechanisms

```
Primary:    COX-1/COX-2 inhibition → ↓ Prostaglandins → ↓ Pain, ↓ Inflammation
Secondary:  NF-kB inhibition → ↓ TNF-α, IL-6, IL-1β
            iNOS inhibition → ↓ Nitric oxide production
            Leukocyte migration blockade → ↓ Local inflammation
            ↓ Oxidative stress
```

## Clinical Applications by Specialty

| Dental Field | Indication | Preferred NSAID |
|---|---|---|
| Conservative / Endodontics | Pulpitis, post-instrumentation pain | Ibuprofen 400–600mg q6h |
| Oral Surgery | Post-extraction pain (1st line) | Ibuprofen 400–600mg; ketorolac IM for severe |
| Periodontics | PGE2-mediated bone resorption (experimental) | Experimental — mostly preclinical |
| Orthodontics | Post-activation bracket pain | Ibuprofen 400mg; timing 1h pre-activation |
| Oral Medicine | Aphthous ulcers, mucositis | Topical benzydamine; triamcinolone |
| Oncology/Orofacial Pain | Cancer pain, facial neuralgia | Part of WHO analgesic ladder (Step 1) |

## Common Dental NSAIDs

| Drug | Class | Dose | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ibuprofen | Non-selective COX-1/2 | 400–600mg q6–8h | Most evidence; OTC available |
| Diclofenac | Preferential COX-2 | 50mg q8h | Less GI effect |
| Naproxen | Non-selective | 250–500mg q12h | Longer half-life |
| Ketorolac | Non-selective | 10mg PO / 30mg IM | Short-term only; severe acute pain |
| Celecoxib | Selective COX-2 | 200mg q12–24h | ↓ GI risk; ↑ cardiovascular risk |
| Benzydamine | Topical | Rinse/spray | Mucositis; anti-inflammatory + local anesthetic |

## Contraindications / Cautions

| Condition | NSAID Risk | Alternative |
|---|---|---|
| AERD (Aspirin-exacerbated asthma) | Bronchospasm | Acetaminophen; consider COX-2 selective |
| Active GI ulcer | GI bleed ↑ | Add PPI; or COX-2 selective |
| CKD (GFR <30) | ↓ Renal perfusion (prostaglandin-dependent) | Acetaminophen |
| Anticoagulant therapy | ↑ Bleeding (↓ platelet COX-1 + GI irritation) | Acetaminophen; short course only |
| CVD / Hypertension | ↓ Antihypertensive effect (ACE-I, diuretics) | Acetaminophen; monitor BP |
| Pregnancy (3rd trimester) | Premature ductus arteriosus closure | Avoid; acetaminophen |

## Topical NSAIDs — Future Direction
- **Benzydamine (Tantum Verde)**: mucositis, post-extraction pain, aphthous ulcers — well-established
- **Diclofenac gel/rinse**: experimental for periodontal pocket application
- Advantage: avoids systemic GI, renal, cardiovascular, antiplatelet side effects
