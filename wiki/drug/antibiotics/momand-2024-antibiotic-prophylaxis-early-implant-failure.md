## One-line Summary

Systematic review + meta-analysis (7 placebo-controlled double-blind RCTs, 1859 patients / 3014 implants): preoperative antibiotic prophylaxis did NOT significantly reduce early implant failure (RR 0.66, 95% CI 0.30-1.47; risk difference -0.007; NNT 143), GRADE moderate certainty — routine prophylaxis not supported for uncomplicated implant surgery.

## Summary

This SR+MA isolates the highest-quality evidence on a long-contested question: does preoperative antibiotic prophylaxis prevent early implant failure in healthy patients? By restricting inclusion to **placebo-controlled, double-blinded RCTs with low/moderate risk of bias** (7 trials, 1859 patients, 3014 implants) and adding a formal **GRADE** rating, it finds **no statistically significant** benefit (RR 0.66, 95% CI 0.30-1.47) and a tiny absolute effect (risk difference -0.007), yielding an **NNT of 143** with a CI extending to infinity. Certainty that the effect is small is moderate. The authors conclude routine prophylaxis is not warranted for uncomplicated implant surgery, especially given antibiotic-resistance concerns. The one residual signal: the two RCTs including immediate post-extraction implants had higher placebo-group failure — the scenario where prophylaxis (better framed as treatment of a potentially infected socket) may still matter.

## Key Contributions

- First implant-prophylaxis SR+MA limited to **placebo-controlled, double-blinded, low/moderate-RoB RCTs** plus a GRADE certainty rating — addresses why prior reviews disagreed (they pooled high-RoB/non-blinded trials).
- Translates statistics into clinical relevance: **NNT 143** (95% CI 29-∞) → routine prophylaxis would dose very many patients to prevent one early failure.
- Subgroup excluding immediate-socket studies reverses direction (RR 1.10), localizing any benefit to **immediate post-extraction** placement rather than routine surgery.
- Reframes the decision around **antibiotic stewardship**: in healthy patients, prophylaxis should be exceptional, not routine.

## Methodology

- Design: PRISMA SR + random-effects (Hedges) meta-analysis; PROSPERO CRD42021292610.
- PICO: antibiotic prophylaxis vs placebo/no antibiotics in overall healthy implant patients; primary outcome early implant failure (loss before loading).
- Search: PubMed, Cochrane, Web of Science to 1 Oct 2023 + hand search + clinicaltrials.gov.
- Inclusion: RCTs, ≥20 patients, ≥2 months follow-up. Exclusion: mini-implants; immediate placement at apical-pathology sites; patients medically needing prophylaxis.
- Screening: 1267 records → 12 eligible for RoB → 7 in meta-analysis (5 high-RoB excluded). RoB-2; Stata 16 SE; GRADE.
- Regimens: 5 RCTs amoxicillin 2 g 1 h preop; 1 amoxicillin 2 g preop + 500 mg ×3 days 1-3 postop; 1 clindamycin 600 mg 1 h preop. All double-blinded.

## Results

| Analysis | Studies | RR (95% CI) | Risk difference (95% CI) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Overall | 7 | 0.66 (0.30-1.47) | -0.007 (-0.035-0.020); NNT 143 (29-∞) |
| Amoxicillin only | 6 | 0.60 (0.27-1.31) | -0.011 (-0.029-0.006) |
| Excluding immediate-socket | 5 | 1.10 (0.35-3.45) | 0.002 (-0.027-0.030) |

- Early failure: 20/929 (2.2%) antibiotic vs 31/930 (3.3%) placebo; overall 51/1859 patients (2.7%). No individual RCT significant.
- GRADE certainty that the effect is small: **moderate**.
- Immediate-post-extraction RCTs had higher placebo-group failure (5.1%, 4.7%) — only signal favoring prophylaxis.
