## 한줄요약
2021 AHA 과학성명: 2007년 IE 예방 가이드라인 변경 없이 유지; 4개 최고위험 심장 상태에만 예방적 항생제 권고; 2007년 이후 VGS-IE 빈도·사망률 증가 없음이 확인됨

## Summary
2021 AHA Scientific Statement reviewing and **reaffirming the 2007 IE prophylaxis guidelines unchanged.** After 14 years of follow-up data, there is no evidence that relaxing prophylaxis increased VGS-IE frequency or mortality. Antibiotic prophylaxis (AP) recommended only for 4 highest-risk cardiac categories.

## The 4 Highest-Risk Conditions — AP Required

| Category | Specifics |
|---|---|
| **Prosthetic cardiac valve** | Including transcatheter-implanted prostheses |
| **Prior infective endocarditis** | Any history of IE |
| **Congenital heart disease (CHD)** | Unrepaired cyanotic CHD; repaired with residual defect; repaired within 6 months |
| **Cardiac transplant** | Recipients with valvulopathy |

> All other cardiac conditions (MVP, RHD, bicuspid aortic valve, hypertrophic CM, repaired CHD with no residual defect) → **NO AP required**

## Antibiotic Protocol

| Situation | Drug | Dose | Route | Timing |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Standard | **Amoxicillin** | 2g (adult) / 50mg/kg (child) | PO | 30–60 min before |
| Penicillin allergic | Clindamycin | 600mg | PO | 30–60 min before |
| Penicillin allergic alternative | Cephalexin | 2g | PO | 30–60 min before |
| Cannot take oral | Ampicillin | 2g | IM/IV | 30 min before |
| Cannot take oral + PCN allergic | Cefazolin | 1g | IM/IV | 30 min before |

## Dental Procedures Requiring AP (if high-risk cardiac condition)
- Manipulation of gingival tissue
- Periapical region of teeth
- Perforation of oral mucosa
- NOT required for: routine anesthesia injections through non-infected tissue, dental X-rays, orthodontic bracket placement, shedding of primary teeth

## Key Evidence Review (2007→2021)
- VGS-IE incidence: **not increased** since 2007 guideline change
- VGS-IE morbidity/mortality: **not increased**
- General compliance with 2007 guidelines: good, but variable
- Conclusion: 2007 recommendations remain valid

## Clinical Takeaway
- **Know the 4 criteria** — do not prescribe AP for any other cardiac condition
- Amoxicillin 2g is the universal first choice (single dose)
- **Good oral health maintenance** emphasized as more protective than AP for low/moderate-risk patients
