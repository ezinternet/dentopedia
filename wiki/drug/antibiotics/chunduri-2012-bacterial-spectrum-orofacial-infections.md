## One-line Summary
Susceptibility study (n=68 orofacial infections, India): isolates highly sensitive to amoxicillin-clavulanate (Gram-positive cocci 95%, Gram-negative rods 97%, anaerobes ~100%) and clindamycin; erythromycin of questionable benefit.

## Summary
Culture and susceptibility study of 68 orofacial infection patients. Streptococcus viridans (64%), Prevotella (43%), and Peptostreptococcus (26%) predominated among 151 isolates, with anaerobic Gram-negative bacilli the most common group (40%). Amoxicillin-clavulanate showed the broadest, highest susceptibility (95-100% across aerobic and anaerobic groups), as did clindamycin among anaerobes, while erythromycin showed questionable activity. The authors advocate amox-clav and clindamycin as useful options for severe orofacial/dentoalveolar infection, with surgery remaining primary.

## Key Contributions
- Early independent (India, 2012) susceptibility dataset supporting amox-clav as broad, effective empirical option.
- Documents high amox-clav susceptibility across aerobes and anaerobes (95-100%).
- Casts doubt on erythromycin for severe orofacial infections.

## Methodology
68 patients (18-50y), aseptic pus aspiration, aerobic + anaerobic culture (Kirby-Bauer disc diffusion) before antibiotics; descriptive statistics. Single-center pilot.

## Results
- 151 isolates (64 aerobic + 87 anaerobic); S. viridans 64%, Prevotella 43%, Peptostreptococcus 26%.
- Susceptibility: Gram+ cocci amox-clav 95% / amoxicillin 90% / levofloxacin 83%; Gram- rods amox-clav 97%; anaerobes amox-clav 100% / clindamycin 100%.
- Erythromycin higher resistance; levofloxacin active but high MIC.
