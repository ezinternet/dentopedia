## One-line Summary
Susceptibility study (n=68 orofacial infections, India, 151 isolates): amoxicillin-clavulanate covers all tested species at 92-100%; erythromycin shows poor coverage of Prevotella (62%) and Fusobacterium (66%) and is of questionable empirical benefit.

## Summary
Single-center descriptive microbiology study of 68 orofacial infection patients at a tertiary oral and maxillofacial surgery department in Hyderabad, India. Pus was collected by aseptic aspiration before antibiotic initiation; organisms were cultured aerobically and anaerobically and susceptibility tested by Kirby-Bauer disc diffusion against five antibiotics. A total of 151 isolates were recovered (64 aerobic, 87 anaerobic; 2.2/patient). Anaerobic Gram-negative bacilli predominated (40% of isolates) over aerobic Gram-positive cocci (34%). Amoxicillin-clavulanate achieved the highest susceptibility rates across all tested organisms (92-100%), followed closely by clindamycin (85-100%). Erythromycin showed insufficient coverage, especially for Prevotella (62%) and Fusobacterium (66%). Levofloxacin disc susceptibility appeared high, but MIC values were elevated, limiting its practical benefit. The submandibular space was most frequently involved (34%).

## Key Contributions
- Regional (South India) susceptibility dataset establishing amoxicillin-clavulanate as the broadest-spectrum empirical agent for orofacial infections.
- Quantifies per-species susceptibility rates for five antibiotics across 151 isolates from 68 patients — precise data usable for antibiogram benchmarking.
- Documents erythromycin inadequacy (59-66% susceptibility in key anaerobes), undermining its use as a penicillin-allergy substitute.
- Confirms anaerobic predominance when specimens are collected by aspiration rather than swab — methodological note with clinical implications for culture technique.
- Notes Staphylococcus as 13% of aerobic isolates, possibly reflecting skin contamination from aspiration site.

## Methodology
- **Design**: Descriptive/evaluative clinical microbiology; no control arm; IRB exemption.
- **Patients**: 68 consecutive patients (37M/31F; age 18-50y, mean 32y) with orofacial infections; exclusion: ongoing antibiotics, systemic immunocompromise.
- **Specimen**: Aseptic needle aspiration of abscess; anaerobic transport in Robertson cooked meat broth; plated on blood agar + MacConkey agar; 37°C / 48h incubation (aerobic + anaerobic).
- **Organism ID**: Conventional microbiological methods.
- **Susceptibility**: Kirby-Bauer disc diffusion for amoxicillin, amoxicillin-clavulanate, erythromycin, clindamycin, levofloxacin.
- **Space involvement**: Submandibular 34%, buccal 28% (deviation from Western literature: fewer lateral pharyngeal cases).

## Results
**Isolate distribution**:
- Aerobic (64 strains): S. viridans 41 (64%), Staphylococcus 8 (13%), Corynebacterium 5 (8%), Candida albicans 4 (6%), Enterobacter 4 (6%), Haemophilus 2 (3%).
- Anaerobic (87 strains): Prevotella spp. 38 (43%), Peptostreptococcus 23 (26%), Fusobacterium 12 (14%), Porphyromonas 6 (7%), Bacteroides 4 (5%), Eubacterium 3 (3%), Veillonella 2 (2%).

**Susceptibility rates (Kirby-Bauer disc diffusion)**:

| Organism | Amoxicillin | Amox-Clav | Erythromycin | Clindamycin | Levofloxacin |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| S. viridans | 90% | 95% | 59% | 85% | 83% |
| Prevotella | 78% | 92% | 62% | 92% | 84% |
| Peptostreptococcus | 91% | 100% | 78% | 100% | 87% |
| Porphyromonas | 83% | 100% | 83% | 100% | 83% |
| Fusobacterium | 75% | 100% | 66% | 92% | 75% |

**Key findings**:
- Amox-clav: highest susceptibility across all five organisms (92-100%); addition of clavulanate to cover beta-lactamase-producing Prevotella key advantage over amoxicillin alone.
- Clindamycin: 85-100% susceptibility; 100% for obligate anaerobes (Peptostreptococcus, Porphyromonas).
- Erythromycin: poor against Prevotella (62%) and Fusobacterium (66%), S. viridans (59%); inadequate for empirical coverage.
- Levofloxacin: disc susceptibility 75-87%, but MIC values high — authors caution fluoroquinolone benefit may be small in practice.
- Amoxicillin alone: 75-91% susceptibility; reasonable for non-beta-lactamase-producing organisms.
