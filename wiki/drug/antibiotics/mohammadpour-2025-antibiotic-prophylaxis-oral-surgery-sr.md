## One-line Summary

Scoping review of 125 studies (25 RCTs, 60 cohort, 40 SRs) mapping antibiotic prophylaxis across oral and maxillofacial surgery: perioperative prophylaxis (≤24 h) is supported for third molar extraction, mandibular fractures, TMJ replacement, complex implants, and oncological resections; extended postoperative courses beyond 24 h do not reduce SSI and increase antimicrobial resistance risk.

## 한줄요약

구강악안면외과 125편(RCT 25·코호트 60·SR 40) 스코핑 리뷰: 제3대구치 발치·하악골절·TMJ 치환·복잡 임플란트·두경부종양에서 수술전후 항생제 예방투여 (Antibiotic Prophylaxis, AP) 지지; 술후 24시간 초과 연장 투여는 수술부위감염 (Surgical Site Infection, SSI) 감소 효과 없이 항균제 내성 (Antimicrobial Resistance, AMR) 위험만 증가.

## Summary

This large-scope scoping review (PRISMA-ScR 2020) searched five major databases (2000–2025) and ultimately included 125 studies — 25 RCTs, 60 cohort studies, and 40 systematic reviews — covering the full spectrum of oral and maxillofacial surgical procedures. Infection rates ranged from 0% to 62% depending on surgery type and wound contamination class. The review proposes a four-tier contamination-risk classification: Type I (clean, 1–4% SSI), Type II (clean-contaminated, 5–15%), Type III (contaminated/oncological, 16–25%), and Type IV (infected/dirty, >25%), with procedure-specific antibiotic recommendations mapped to each class.

The central finding across procedure types is that perioperative antibiotic prophylaxis (administered within 2 h preoperatively and not extending beyond 24 h postoperatively) is adequate for most indications. Multiple RCTs and SRs found no significant advantage of extending postoperative antibiotic prophylaxis beyond 24 h for mandibular fractures, and meta-analytic evidence showed that adding prolonged postoperative courses to standard peri-/perioperative regimens produced no notable reduction in SSI risk. Furthermore, extended prophylaxis >72 h was associated with paradoxically increased SSI incidence (Delaplain et al.) and >7 days with markedly higher late-onset infections (Zosa et al.).

First-line agents across procedures are amoxicillin (2 g single preoperative dose) and co-amoxiclav (amoxicillin/clavulanic acid), which provide broad coverage against oral flora including Gram-positive cocci and anaerobes. Clindamycin is consistently identified as the appropriate alternative only for penicillin-allergic patients — it is not recommended as routine first-line due to its resistance profile. For contaminated/oncological surgery, broader coverage (ampicillin/sulbactam, piperacillin/tazobactam, cefuroxime + metronidazole) is required to address Gram-negative bacteria as well. The review underscores antimicrobial stewardship (AMS) as an ethical and clinical imperative: excessive antibiotic prescribing is the primary driver of AMR, and maxillofacial surgeons must align practice with evidence-based guidelines rather than habit.

## Key Contributions

1. First broad-scope evidence map of antibiotic prophylaxis across the entire oral and maxillofacial surgery spectrum in a single PRISMA-ScR review (2000–2025)
2. Four-tier contamination-risk classification (Type I–IV) with SSI rates and targeted antibiotic recommendations for each level
3. Strong, cross-procedural evidence that postoperative antibiotic courses beyond 24 h do not reduce SSI and increase AMR risk
4. Amoxicillin/co-amoxiclav established as first-line across most procedures; clindamycin reserved for penicillin allergy
5. Identification of evidence gaps: cleft lip/palate surgery and immunocompromised patients lack RCT-level guidance
6. Survey data showing that real-world practice significantly deviates from guidelines — ~55% of surgeons still prescribe ≥1 week postoperative antibiotics for open mandibular fractures

## Methodology

- **Study design**: Scoping review (PRISMA-ScR 2020)
- **Databases**: Ovid MEDLINE, PubMed, Embase, Google Scholar, Web of Science
- **Search period**: 2000–2025
- **Screening**: 542 identified → 261 excluded → 281 screened → 125 included
- **Included study types**: RCTs (n=25), cohort studies (n=60), systematic reviews (n=40)
- **Excluded**: Case reports/series, non-English, conference abstracts, letters, non-human studies
- **Population**: All ages/genders undergoing oral or maxillofacial surgical procedures
- **Evidence grading**: GRADE (Level I/IIa majority → strong; Level IIb majority → weak recommendation)

## Results

### Contamination-Risk Classification and SSI Rates

| Type | Description | SSI Rate | Prophylaxis |
|---|---|---|---|
| I (Clean) | No mucosal disruption | 1–4% | None or ≤24 h co-amoxiclav |
| II (Clean-contaminated) | Mucosal incision | 5–15% | Co-amoxiclav 2 g ± repeat 1 g q4h; clindamycin+gentamicin if PCN-allergic |
| III (Contaminated) | Oncological oral/neck | 16–25% | Ampicillin/sulbactam or piperacillin/tazobactam |
| IV (Infected/dirty) | Active infection | >25% | Therapeutic (not prophylactic) antibiotics |

### Procedure-Specific Evidence Summary

| Procedure | SSI Rate | Pre-op | Peri-op | Post-op (>24 h) | First-line |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Third molar removal | 1–11% | Yes (Grade I, single dose) | Evidence unclear | No | Amoxicillin 2 g PO |
| Implant (simple, healthy) | 4–10% | No | No | No | Amoxicillin 2 g if needed |
| Implant (complex/graft) | 4–10% | Yes | Yes | No | Amoxicillin 2 g |
| Mandibular fracture | 3.3–43.9% | Strongly Yes | Strongly Yes | 24 h IV only | Penicillin/co-amoxiclav |
| Maxillofacial fractures | 1–33% (pooled 5.6%) | Yes | Yes | No | Amoxicillin 2 g or penicillin V |
| Orthognathic surgery | 1.4–33.4% | Yes | Yes | Weak (5-day may reduce morbidity) | Co-amoxiclav, cefazolin |
| TMJ replacement | 1.5–4.5% | Weakly Yes | Strongly Yes | Limited evidence | Cefazolin IV, clindamycin if allergic |
| Head/neck oncology | 22.7–46% | Yes | Yes | Until drainage ceases | Cefuroxime + metronidazole |
| Cleft lip/palate | 1.1–4.4% | Insufficient | Cefuroxime IV | Amoxicillin 5 d (fistula ↓) | — |
| Parotidectomy | 2–11% | No (routine) | Yes (extensive only) | No | Cefazolin (clindamycin if allergic) |

### Penicillin-Allergic Protocol
- **Standard alternative**: Clindamycin across all procedure types
- **For Gram-negative coverage**: Clindamycin + gentamicin (Types II–III)
- NOT recommended as first-line for non-allergic patients

### Key Quantitative Findings
- Third molar infection risk reduction with AP: **60–70%**
- Implant failure reduction with preoperative amoxicillin: **~2%**
- Mandibular fractures: 24-h postoperative course equivalent to multi-day (multiple RCTs)
- Extended prophylaxis >7 days: significantly higher late SSI rate (Zosa et al.)
- Extended prophylaxis >72 h: increased SSI incidence (Delaplain et al.)
