## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional questionnaire (n=103 general dentists/residents, Shiraz, Iran): moderate-to-good AAE knowledge (70.9% knew first-line) but low-to-moderate clinical performance — 69.9% inappropriately prescribed antibiotics for immunocompromised endodontic patients and 51.5% for localized acute apical abscess; residents outperformed GPs (p=0.019); 88.3% wanted more training.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

Nazari et al. surveyed 103 general dentists and residents in Shiraz, Iran (73.6% response) with a validated questionnaire (CVI=0.89, α=0.82) built on AAE guidelines, to measure the gap between knowledge and real-case performance in endodontic antibiotic prescribing. Knowledge was moderate-to-good but clinical performance was only low-to-moderate, and the two did not correlate. The standout finding is a marked over-prescription tendency for cases that do not warrant antibiotics under AAE guidance, plus widespread use of the non-guideline first-line combination amoxicillin + metronidazole. The authors frame this as an antimicrobial-stewardship education gap.

## Key Contributions

- First Shiraz (2024) measurement of AAE endodontic-antibiotic guideline adherence among general dentists.
- Documents a knowledge↔performance dissociation (no significant correlation between the two scores).
- Quantifies indication-specific over-prescription and the amoxicillin+metronidazole first-line habit.
- Identifies academic supervision (resident status) as the only factor associated with better performance.

## Methodology

- Cross-sectional validated questionnaire; simple random sampling, 140 invited → 103 completed (response 73.6%).
- Inclusion: general dentists/residents in Shiraz with ≥2-5 yr endodontic experience; specialists/non-practicing excluded.
- Domains: demographics, self-assessment, knowledge of prescribing principles, clinical case-based performance. Score 0-14.
- Analysis: SPSS v22→v27, Spearman, Kruskal-Wallis, chi-square, binary logistic regression; significance <0.05. Self-report bias acknowledged as a key limitation.

## Results

- Guideline awareness: 84.5% had NO awareness of current guidelines (only 15.5% familiar); 36% used no specific reference source.
- First-line (non-penicillin-allergic): amoxicillin+metronidazole combination chosen by 70.9% (a deviation from AAE); amoxicillin 35.9% and penicillin V 10.7% (the recommended first-line) chosen less often. Penicillin-allergic: clindamycin by majority (50.5%); only 5.8% recognized azithromycin, none moxifloxacin.
- Duration: 50.5% chose 7-10 days, 43.7% 3-7 days; almost none chose 10-20 days or "until pain subsides."
- Indication table (% who would prescribe): irreversible symptomatic pulpitis 1%, pulpal necrosis 9.7%, symptomatic apical periodontitis 7.8%, **localized acute apical abscess 51.5% (unnecessary, non-compliant)**, diffuse facial swelling 70.9%, abscess with fever/trismus 77.7%, immunocompromised localized abscess 70.9%, after all RCT 0%, pain 2-3 d post-RCT only 17.5% (an AAE indication, under-recognized).
- Prophylaxis (adults): amoxicillin 2 g 1 h preop 90.3% (guideline-concordant); penicillin-allergic → clindamycin 66%.
- Performance cases: diffuse facial swelling — 41.7% prescribed antibiotics before RCT despite immediate treatment being feasible; irreversible pulpitis — 84.5% correctly chose RCT without antibiotics; **immunocompromised patient — only 30.1% guideline-compliant, 69.9% inappropriately prescribed antibiotics.**
- Residents > GPs on performance (p=0.019); no association with gender, experience, or workplace. 88.3% requested further training. Self-reported data may overstate true adherence.
