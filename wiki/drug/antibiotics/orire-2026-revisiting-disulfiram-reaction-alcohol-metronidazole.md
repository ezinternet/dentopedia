## One-line Summary

Focused literature review (4 databases, 1970–2024, 11 studies): evidence does **not** strongly support a clinically significant disulfiram-like reaction between alcohol and oral metronidazole — case reports say yes, but controlled trials/chart review/animal studies say no — questioning strict alcohol abstinence. ## Summary

This focused review pooled 11 studies (PubMed, EMBASE, Cochrane CENTRAL; Jan 1970–Nov 2024) on the alleged alcohol–metronidazole disulfiram-like reaction. The proposed mechanism is aldehyde dehydrogenase (ALDH) inhibition causing acetaldehyde accumulation (flushing, nausea, vomiting, tachycardia). Evidence split cleanly by design: **all 4 case reports plus 1 older clinical trial supported a link, but 3 clinical trials, 1 cross-sectional chart review, and 2 animal studies did not.** The authors conclude the available evidence does not strongly support a clinically significant interaction, calling into question the routine "no alcohol with metronidazole" counseling rule. **Dental relevance**: metronidazole is a common adjunct for odontogenic/periodontal anaerobic infections, and the strict-abstinence warning is a standard counseling point — this review suggests that warning rests on weak evidence. Note this page is built from the abstract only (full text paywalled).

## Key Contributions

1. Consolidates 50+ years of heterogeneous evidence into a single design-stratified verdict.
2. Shows the "positive" signal comes almost entirely from uncontrolled case reports, while controlled/experimental data are negative.
3. Directly questions the strict-abstinence counseling rule embedded in pharmacy labels and clinical practice.

## Methodology

- Literature review across PubMed, EMBASE, Cochrane CENTRAL.
- Date range Jan 1, 1970 – Nov 22, 2024; broad search term "disulfiram-like reaction."
- 11 studies met inclusion; no meta-analysis or formal risk-of-bias reported in the abstract → classified `narrative-review` (despite a structured search) pending full-text confirmation.

## Results

| Evidence type | n | Verdict on interaction |
|---|---|---|
| Case reports | 4 | Supports a link |
| Older clinical trial | 1 | Supports a link |
| Clinical trials (controlled) | 3 | No link |
| Cross-sectional chart review | 1 | No link |
| Animal experimental | 2 | No link |

**Bottom line**: weak/unsupported for a clinically significant reaction; strict alcohol abstinence during metronidazole therapy may be unnecessary.
