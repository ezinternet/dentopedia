## One-line Summary

Narrative review (Zahedan Univ, Iran): odontogenic pain/infection affects >43% of pregnant women; endodontic treatment with appropriate drugs is safe and should not be delayed — amoxicillin/penicillin/cephalexin first-line antibiotics (erythromycin/clindamycin if penicillin-allergic), acetaminophen first-line analgesic, 1:100,000 epinephrine LA, 2nd trimester safest for elective care.

## 한줄요약

내러티브 리뷰 (이란 Zahedan 의대): 임산부 >43%가 치성 통증·감염을 겪으며, 적절한 약물과 함께라면 근관치료 (Root Canal Therapy, RCT)는 안전하고 미뤄선 안 됨 — 1차 항생제 아목시실린·페니실린·세팔렉신(페니실린 알러지 시 에리스로마이신·클린다마이신), 1차 진통제 아세트아미노펜 (Acetaminophen), 국소마취 1:100,000 에피네프린, 선택 시술은 2분기가 가장 안전.

## Summary

This narrative review addresses a common but under-guided scenario: pregnant patients with odontogenic infection/pain needing endodontic care. It reaffirms the ADA (2012/2019) + ACOG position that timely oral care is integral to a healthy pregnancy and that untreated odontogenic infection — which can progress rapidly to deep-space/airway-threatening infection — poses greater maternal-fetal risk than appropriate treatment. The review summarizes safe pharmacotherapy: first-line antibiotics (amoxicillin, penicillin, cephalexin; erythromycin or clindamycin for penicillin allergy; IV clindamycin/cephalosporin for severe cellulitis), acetaminophen as first-line analgesic (controlled ibuprofen/oxycodone), local anesthesia with 1:100,000 epinephrine plus aspiration at minimum effective dose, and ALARA-protected intraoral radiography. A trimester framework places elective care in the 2nd trimester, emergency-only in the 1st (obstetrician consult if organogenesis incomplete) and 3rd. **Bottom line: antibiotics and endodontic treatment can be used safely in pregnancy; prompt treatment of odontogenic infection is mandatory.** As a narrative review it lacks systematic rigor — pair with primary/SR evidence for specifics.

## Key Contributions

1. Pregnancy-specific endodontic pharmacotherapy in one place (antibiotics, analgesics, LA, intracanal agents, radiography).
2. Trimester-by-trimester treatment-timing framework.
3. Reinforces that untreated odontogenic infection is the larger risk vs appropriate drug use.

## Methodology

- Narrative (non-systematic) review; no search protocol, inclusion criteria, or risk-of-bias reported → low evidence weight.

## Results

| Domain | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Antibiotics (mild) | Amoxicillin / penicillin / cephalexin first-line |
| Penicillin allergy | Erythromycin or clindamycin |
| Severe cellulitis | Hospital IV clindamycin / cephalosporin |
| Analgesia | Acetaminophen first-line; controlled ibuprofen/oxycodone |
| Local anesthesia | 1:100,000 epinephrine + aspiration, min dose |
| Radiography | Intraoral safe with ALARA, lead apron, collimation |
| Timing | 2nd trimester safest; 1st/3rd emergency-only |
