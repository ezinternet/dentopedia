## One-line Summary
Overview of SRs for post-operative endodontic pain: NSAIDs (ibuprofen) as first choice, effective adjunct corticosteroid combination; preemptive administration superior; technical factors (instrumentation extent, irrigation, occlusal reduction) also influence pain (Healthcare 2022).

## Summary
Overview of systematic reviews covering oral medications for post-operative endodontic pain, combined with a narrative review of technical procedural factors. Post-operative pain affects 2.5–60% of patients, peaking at 6–12 hours and falling to ~11% at 1 week. NSAIDs — particularly ibuprofen — represent first-line pharmacotherapy. Corticosteroids (dexamethasone) provide additive benefit when combined with NSAIDs. Preemptive (pre-operative) analgesic administration is superior to post-operative administration alone. Technical factors including instrument extrusion beyond the apex, irrigant volume/concentration, and single- vs. multi-visit treatment also influence post-operative pain outcomes.

## Key Contributions
- Integrates pharmacological (overview of SRs) and technical (narrative review) evidence into unified clinical recommendations
- Establishes preemptive analgesia as preferred strategy over reactive post-operative dosing
- Confirms NSAIDs + corticosteroids combination as evidence-supported adjunct regimen
- Reviews occlusal reduction evidence: some support, but universal recommendation premature

## Methodology
Primary: overview of systematic reviews of RCTs on orally administered post-operative medications. Secondary: narrative review of technical procedural factors. Databases searched; SRs of RCTs as primary evidence source. Evidence-based clinical recommendation synthesis.

## Results — Pharmacological
| Drug class | Evidence level | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Ibuprofen (±APAP) | Highest | First-line |
| Corticosteroids (dexamethasone) | Moderate | Adjunct to NSAIDs |
| Preemptive vs reactive | Moderate | Preemptive preferred |
| Antibiotics | Low | Not indicated for pain |

## Results — Technical
- Instrument extrusion beyond apex → pain increase
- NaOCl: concentration and volume should be controlled
- Single-visit vs multi-visit: mixed evidence for pain difference
- Occlusal reduction: some benefit in symptomatic cases
