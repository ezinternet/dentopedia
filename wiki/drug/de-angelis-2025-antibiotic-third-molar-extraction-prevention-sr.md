## One-line Summary
Univ. Genoa SR (Dent J 2025): antibiotic prophylaxis for third-molar extraction — marginal benefit for simple extractions, limited indication for impacted/surgical extractions; consistent with Camps-Font 2024 NMA conclusion of NNT=25.

## Summary
SR on antibiotic prophylaxis in third-molar extraction. Confirms that prophylaxis offers only marginal benefit for routine simple extractions, with somewhat clearer benefit (still modest) for surgically impacted extractions where soft/hard tissue disruption is greater.

## Key Contributions
- Updated 2025 SR after the NMA-level work (Camps-Font 2024).
- Discriminates simple vs surgical extraction context — important nuance for routine practice decisions.

## Methodology
SR of antibiotic prophylaxis RCTs/cohorts in third molar extraction.

## Results
[합의수준] Prophylaxis marginal in simple extractions; modest in surgical/impacted extractions; NNT remains high (25 for dry socket per Camps-Font NMA), arguing against routine prescription in healthy patients.

## Limitations
- SR without NMA (Camps-Font 2024 provides the NMA)
- Subgroup data by impaction class limited

## Clinical Implication
For Korean dental practice: routine antibiotic prophylaxis for simple third molar extraction in healthy adults is not justified. For complex impacted cases (Pell-Gregory III/C, anatomically high-risk), consider preoperative single-dose amoxicillin 2g — but the NNT remains high and the AMR argument increasingly weighs against it.
