## 한줄요약
서술적 고찰(KDA 2026 Special Issue): 치과 DDI 유병률 40.7%, 처방의 인식률 42.7% — 항생제·NSAIDs·혈관수축제별 고령환자 처방 안전 전략과 GFR/LFT 기반 용량조절 표 제시.

## Summary
A Korean Dental Association special-issue narrative review (2026, vol. 64 no. 4) addressing the polypharmacy challenge in geriatric dentistry. Synthesizes real-world DDI epidemiology (40.7% prevalence; 42.7% prescriber recognition; ADR risk approaches 100% at ≥8 concurrent drugs) and proposes drug-class–specific prescribing rules (clindamycin 15.7× more ADRs than amoxicillin; epinephrine cap in beta-blocker/TCA users; GFR-driven dose-interval extension).

## Key Contributions
- Quantifies the prescriber–recognition gap in dental DDIs (mean 42.7% correct, warfarin–cimetidine 18.2%)
- Tabulates polypharmacy ADR risk: 2 drugs 6% → 5 drugs 50% → ≥8 drugs ~100%
- Ranks dental antibiotic safety by ADR rate (amoxicillin 21.5 vs. clindamycin 337.3 per million)
- Formalizes epinephrine restriction in non-selective beta-blocker and TCA users (≤2 cartridges of 1:200,000 or substitute mepivacaine)
- Provides a single GFR/LFT-based prescribing table for elderly dental patients

## Methodology
Narrative review without systematic search or risk-of-bias appraisal. Synthesizes Korean and international real-world dental-DDI epidemiology, US prescriber-knowledge surveys (Ko et al.), and pharmacology guidelines. Organized by epidemiology → age-related pharmacokinetics → prescriber awareness → drug-class management.

## Results

### DDI epidemiology
| Source | Finding |
|---|---|
| de Oliveira 2022 (320 records) | 359 pDIs; analgesics 50.4%, local anesthetics 25.9%, antibiotics 23.7%; "monitor closely" 68.8%, "serious" 8.6%, "contraindicated" 0.83% |
| Real-world cohort (105 pt / 1,332 drug pairs) | 40.7% DDI prevalence; CVD patients median 18 vs. non-CVD 10 |
| Ko et al. US prescriber survey | 42.7% correct mean; warfarin–cimetidine 18.2% |

### Polypharmacy → ADR
| Concurrent drugs | ADR risk |
|---|---|
| 2 | 6% |
| 5 | 50% |
| ≥8 | ~100% |

### Antibiotic safety (ADR per million Rx)
| Drug | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Amoxicillin | 21.5 | Safest dental option |
| Clindamycin | 337.3 | 15.7× higher; *C. difficile* risk |
| Metronidazole, erythromycin | — | CYP2C9/3A4 inhibition → warfarin INR ↑ |

### Vasoconstrictor cautions
- Non-selective β-blocker + epinephrine → unopposed α-vasoconstriction → HTN crisis + reflex bradycardia
- TCA + epinephrine → catecholamine sensitivity → arrhythmia/HTN crisis risk
- Rule: 1:200,000 epinephrine, ≤2 cartridges, or substitute mepivacaine

### Geriatric dosing rules
- Age ≥85 or weight <45 kg: 50% starting dose ("Start low, go slow")
- GFR <10 mL/min: amoxicillin/cephalosporin q24h
- LFT >4× elevated: avoid acetaminophen/lidocaine or minimize dose
- ≥8 drugs: mandatory DDI-screening tool before prescribing

## Clinical Implications
- 진료 전 약물력 점검(약학정보원 등 신뢰도 높은 DB 활용)을 표준화해야 prescriber-recognition gap 줄어듦
- 페니실린 알레르기 환자에서 clindamycin 대신 다른 대체(예: cephalexin, azithromycin 단기) 우선 고려 — ADR/CDI 위험 15× 감소
- 비선택적 베타차단제·TCA 복용 환자에 epinephrine 1:200,000 ≤2 cartridges 캡 강제, 필요 시 mepivacaine 전환
- GFR <30 mL/min 또는 LFT >4× 이상인 환자에서 amoxicillin q24h, NSAID 회피 → 신독성·간독성 예방
- 8개 이상 복용 환자는 처방 전 DDI 스크리닝 도구 사용을 진료 SOP에 박아둘 것
