## Summary
Systematic review (56 publications) examining whether diabetes increases implant complication rates. **Controlled DM = similar implant success to healthy controls.** Poorly controlled DM (HbA1c ≥9%) significantly increases peri-implantitis and long-term implant loss. Perioperative antibiotics + CHX are standard and improve outcomes.

## Key Results

| DM Status | Peri-implantitis | Long-term Implant Loss |
|---|---|---|
| Poorly controlled (HbA1c ≥9%) | ↑ especially early | ↑ |
| Controlled (HbA1c <8%) | Similar to control | Similar to control |
| Prediabetes | Possible ↑ peri-implant disease | No clear effect on survival |

## Clinical Protocol for DM Patients

```
Pre-treatment:
  └─ Check HbA1c
     ├─ <8%:  proceed with standard implant protocol
     ├─ 8–9%: proceed with enhanced precautions
     └─ ≥9%:  defer until glycemic control improved

Perioperative:
  └─ Antibiotics (amoxicillin 2g PO preoperative + 5 days)
  └─ CHX 0.12% mouthwash × 2 weeks post-op

Loading:
  └─ Delayed loading protocol preferred
  └─ Extended osseointegration time expected
```

## Clinical Takeaway
- DM is **NOT a contraindication** for implants if HbA1c <8%
- HbA1c is the mandatory pre-implant screening test
- Perioperative anti-infective protocol is essential in all DM patients
