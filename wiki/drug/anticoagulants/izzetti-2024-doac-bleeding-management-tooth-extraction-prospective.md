## One-line Summary

Prospective cohort (n=49 DOAC patients, 136 extractions): bleeding occurred in ~50% intra-/early peri-op and peaked at 46.9% on post-op day 1, yet all episodes were mild and controlled by local hemostasis (gauze ± tranexamic acid); DOAC suspension did NOT reduce post-operative bleeding, while >3 teeth, flap elevation, and osteotomy were the significant drivers.

## 한줄요약

전향코호트(DOAC 49명·발치 136개): 술중·초기 술후 출혈이 약 50%, 술후 1일째 46.9%로 최고였으나 전부 경미·국소지혈(거즈±트라넥삼산)로 조절. DOAC 중단은 술후 출혈을 줄이지 못했고, 3개 초과 발치·판막거상·골삭제가 유의한 출혈 인자였다.

## Summary

This single-center prospective cohort from Pisa followed 49 adults on direct oral anticoagulants (DOACs) through 136 tooth extractions, recording bleeding intra-operatively, peri-operatively (every 20 min to 80 min), and daily for the first post-operative week. Two-thirds (33/49) suspended their DOAC pre-operatively (mean 28.5 h), the rest continued. The central clinical message: bleeding was common but uniformly mild — every episode was managed with local measures (gauze compression, tranexamic-acid gauze, oxidized cellulose, additional sutures, occasionally diathermocoagulation), and no reversal agent was needed. Crucially, suspending the DOAC did not reduce post-operative bleeding, supporting a non-interruption + local-hemostasis approach for routine extractions and reserving suspension for high bleeding-risk cases.

## Key Contributions

- Time-resolved bleeding curve (intra → 20/40/60/80 min → days 1–7) rather than a single binary endpoint.
- Head-to-head of suspension vs maintenance under one protocol: no post-operative benefit from suspension.
- Procedural factors (>3 teeth, flap, osteotomy, posterior/complex) identified as the dominant bleeding drivers over the suspension decision.
- Drug-level signal: rivaroxaban and apixaban bled more often; rivaroxaban dominated delayed (day 4–7) bleeding.

## Methodology

- **Design**: single-center non-randomized prospective trial, 1-week follow-up, STROBE-adherent (Pisa IRB 17796/2020).
- **Patients**: n=49 (21 F; mean age 72.2 ± 8.3 y), 136 extractions. DOACs: rivaroxaban 17, apixaban 16, edoxaban 8, dabigatran 8. Excluded antiplatelet/VKA users and coagulopathies.
- **Suspension**: 33/49 (67%) interrupted pre-op (mean 28.5 ± 13.0 h); all stopped ≥24 h after surgery.
- **Surgery**: standardized minimally invasive; mepivacaine 2% + epi 1:100,000; collagen sponge + interrupted absorbable sutures; **no prophylactic extra hemostatic placed** — local rescue measures only if bleeding occurred. Stratified by ≤3 vs >3 teeth, flap, osteotomy.
- **Bleeding definitions**: intra-op = field obscured; peri-op = oozing/active bleeding within 80 min uncontrolled by routine measures; post-op = bleeding >12 h forming hematoma needing intervention.

## Results

- **Intra-operative**: 27/49 (55.1%). >3 extractions bled in 69.2% vs 50% for ≤3 (p<0.001). No suspended-vs-maintained difference.
- **Peri-operative**: 57.1% (20 min) → 38.8% (40 min) → 28.6% (60 min) → 16.3% (80 min). Apixaban differed from rivaroxaban at 20 min. More frequent posterior vs anterior (81% vs 64%) and complex vs simple (26% vs 13%). No suspended-vs-maintained difference at any timepoint.
- **Post-operative**: peaked day 1 — 46.9% (23/49); day 2 22.4%; day 3 8.2%; days 4–7 only rivaroxaban. Rivaroxaban vs dabigatran significant at day 1 (p=0.016) and day 2 (p=0.023). Non-significant trend: early bleeding (days 1–2) in non-discontinuers, delayed bleeding (days 3–7) in those who suspended.
- **Severity**: all episodes mild, controlled with gauze compression ± tranexamic acid; no reversal agents.
- **Significant drivers (p<0.05)**: >3 teeth, flap elevation, osteotomy. Rivaroxaban + apixaban = highest bleeding risk.
- **Limitations**: small unbalanced sample, heterogeneous suspension protocols, no anti-Xa assays, dose-timing not standardized.
