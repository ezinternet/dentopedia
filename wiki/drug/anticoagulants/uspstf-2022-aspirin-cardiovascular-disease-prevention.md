## One-line Summary
2022 USPSTF recommendation statement narrowing low-dose aspirin for primary cardiovascular disease (CVD) prevention: adults 40–59 y with ≥10% 10-year CVD risk = Grade C (individualize), adults ≥60 y = Grade D (recommend against initiation), reversing the 2016 statement because age-related bleeding harms now outweigh the small CVD benefit; applies only to primary prevention, not established secondary prevention.

## Summary
2022 US Preventive Services Task Force update revising the population eligible for aspirin primary CVD prevention. The recommendation narrows substantially compared with the 2016 statement:

- **Adults 40-59 y with ≥10% 10-year CVD risk** — Grade C: small net benefit; individualize (informed shared decision).
- **Adults ≥60 y** — Grade D: recommend against initiating aspirin primary prevention.

Driven by new evidence (ASPREE, ARRIVE, ASCEND trials, 2018) showing bleeding harms (GI, intracranial) increase with age and now outweigh the small CVD risk reduction at and above 60. The prior CRC chemoprevention argument is downgraded based on more recent data.

[claude해석] Dental implications: any new aspirin started after 2022 in adults ≥60 should be questioned (likely already in current practice if discontinuation indicated). However, patients already established on aspirin for secondary prevention (post-MI, post-stroke, post-stent) are *not* affected by this — the recommendation only addresses *primary* prevention initiation. For dental management of patients on long-standing aspirin, continue to follow non-interruption protocol (see related papers).

## Key Contributions
- Major reversal of the 2016 USPSTF aspirin recommendation.
- Sharp age cutoff at 60 for the strong against-initiation recommendation.
- Anchors current US primary-care discussion of aspirin de-prescribing.

## Methodology
USPSTF systematic review covering: CVD event effects, CV/all-cause mortality, CRC incidence/mortality, and bleeding harms in primary prevention populations.

## Results
| Age | Old (2016) | New (2022) |
|---|---|---|
| 50-59 y, ≥10% CVD risk | B (recommend) | merged → 40-59 C |
| 60-69 y, ≥10% CVD risk | C | D (recommend against initiation) |
| 40-59 y, ≥10% CVD risk | I (insufficient) | C (small benefit, individualize) |
| ≥60 y | not specified | D |

## Limitations
- Primary prevention only — does not address aspirin for established CVD (secondary prevention).
- Does not address dental procedure management; relevant only as background for the patient's medication landscape.
