## One-line Summary

Prospective study (n=100, patients within 1 year of PCI/coronary stenting) — uninterrupted single (aspirin) vs dual (aspirin + clopidogrel) antiplatelet therapy showed no significant difference in post-extraction bleeding (complete hemostasis 78% vs 64%, p=0.08); all persistent/delayed bleeding controlled with local measures.

## Summary

This prospective two-arm study (n=100) addresses one of the highest-anxiety scenarios in dental surgery for medically compromised patients: tooth extraction in patients who underwent percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) with stent placement within the past year, and who remain on antiplatelet therapy. Interrupting antiplatelet therapy in this window carries a meaningful stent-thrombosis risk, so the clinically relevant question is whether extraction can be done safely *without* stopping the drugs.

Patients were split into a single antiplatelet therapy (SAPT) arm (aspirin 100 mg, n=50) and a dual antiplatelet therapy (DAPT) arm (aspirin 100 mg + clopidogrel 75 mg, n=50), both uninterrupted. Bleeding was classified as complete hemostasis, persistent bleeding, or delayed bleeding. There was **no statistically significant difference** in bleeding status between SAPT and DAPT (p=0.08), and bleeding did not correlate with sex, age, underlying disease, number of teeth/roots extracted, or whether the extraction was simple or surgical. Critically, **every persistent or delayed bleeding episode was easily controlled with local hemostatic measures.** The authors conclude that simple or complicated multiple-tooth extraction can be performed safely during the first post-PCI year on uninterrupted single or dual antiplatelet therapy.

## Key Contributions

- Direct clinical evidence in a **recent-PCI (≤1 year post-stent)** population — the subgroup most at risk if antiplatelet therapy is interrupted.
- SAPT-vs-DAPT head-to-head for post-extraction bleeding, both arms uninterrupted.
- Reinforces the **continue-antiplatelet-therapy** recommendation: local hemostasis suffices even on DAPT.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Prospective, two-group comparative clinical study (single center, Iran).
- **Population**: 100 patients with coronary stent placement within the past year requiring tooth extraction.
- **Arms**: Monotherapy (aspirin 100 mg, n=50) vs dual therapy (aspirin 100 mg + clopidogrel 75 mg, n=50); both uninterrupted through extraction.
- **Outcomes**: Complete hemostasis / persistent bleeding / delayed bleeding.
- **Covariates**: Sex, age, underlying diseases, number of teeth and roots extracted, simple vs complicated/surgical procedure.

## Results

| Outcome | Monotherapy (aspirin, n=50) | Dual (aspirin+clopidogrel, n=50) |
|---|---|---|
| Complete hemostasis | 39/50 (78%) | 32/50 (64%) |
| Persistent bleeding | 11/50 (22%) | 14/50 (28%) |
| Delayed bleeding | 0/50 (0%) | 4/50 (8%) |

- Between-group difference in bleeding status: **not significant (p = 0.08)**.
- No significant association of bleeding with sex, age, underlying diseases, number of teeth/roots extracted, or procedure type (all p > 0.05).
- **All** persistent and delayed bleeding was easily controlled by local measures.
- **Bottom line**: Multiple-tooth extraction (simple or complicated) is safe within the first post-PCI year without interrupting single or dual antiplatelet therapy.
