## One-line Summary

SR+MA (24 studies, n=8663; 4 dental) — continuous DOAC reduced major bleeding (OR 0.57, 95% CI 0.37-0.87, I²=0%) and thrombotic events (OR 0.54, 0.33-0.91) overall, but ALL differences vanished in high-quality RCT data (very low/low GRADE); both continue and interrupt are acceptable for minimal bleeding-risk procedures including minor dental.

## Summary

This systematic review and meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD42024621729) asked whether **continuing** a direct oral anticoagulant (DOAC) through a **minimal bleeding-risk procedure** is safe versus **interrupting** it. Twenty-four studies (n=8663; 8 retrospective, 8 prospective, 8 RCTs) were pooled. Cardiac ablation (13 studies) dominated, with pacemaker insertion (4) and **dental procedures (4)** also represented; minor dental work (extractions, restorations, prosthetics, endodontics, cleanings, fillings) is explicitly inside the minimal-risk category. Most patients (n=8448) were on a DOAC for atrial fibrillation.

The headline pooled estimates favored **continuous** DOAC, but the authors' central message is that this benefit is an artifact of low-quality observational data: once the analysis is restricted to the 8 RCTs, **every difference — thrombotic, major bleeding, minor bleeding — loses significance**. A significant design-based subgroup difference for total bleeding (RCT OR 0.96 vs nonrandomized OR 0.56, p=.03) underlines the selection bias: in observational practice, lower-risk patients were preferentially continued and comorbid patients interrupted. Certainty was **very low to low** (GRADE). Bottom line: for minimal bleeding-risk procedures including minor dental work, **both strategies appear acceptable**, with no demonstrated harm from continuing.

## Key Contributions

- **Pooled bleeding effect (continuous vs interrupted):** major bleeding **OR 0.57 (95% CI 0.37-0.87, I²=0%)** significant overall; overall bleeding OR 0.79 (0.62-1.01, NS); minor bleeding OR 0.93 (0.69-1.24, NS).
- **Pooled thrombotic effect:** OR 0.54 (0.33-0.91; abstract) / 0.33-0.87 (text), I²=52-60%, favoring continuous overall; NS at 30 days.
- **RCT-only subgroup nullifies everything:** major bleeding OR 0.82 (0.42-1.61), thrombotic OR 0.53 (0.17-1.64), minor bleeding OR 1.02 (0.71-1.45) — all NS.
- Largest such synthesis (11 more trials than prior reviews); pre-specified subgroups by design, procedure, and interruption timing.

## Methodology

- **Design:** PRISMA 2020 SR+MA; DerSimonian-Laird random-effects; OR + risk difference; I² heterogeneity; ICEMAN subgroup credibility.
- **Search:** Ovid Medline, CENTRAL, EMBASE, CINAHL, registries — inception to Sep 11, 2025.
- **Comparison:** continuous (DOAC taken morning of procedure) vs interrupted (≥1 dose held: day-of / 24h / 48h / 72h).
- **DOACs:** apixaban (n=2991), rivaroxaban (n=2479), dabigatran (n=2042), edoxaban (n=786) — not separated in analysis.
- **Quality:** RoB 2.0 (RCTs: 4 high, 2 some concerns, 2 low), ROBINS-I v2 (nonrandomized: 15/16 high RoB), GRADE (very low → low).

## Results

| Outcome | Continuous vs interrupted | OR (95% CI) | I² | Significant? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Overall bleeding (20 studies) | 2.4% vs 3.2% | 0.79 (0.62-1.01) | 0% | No |
| **Major bleeding** (19 studies) | 1.0% vs 2.2% | **0.57 (0.37-0.87)** | 0% | **Yes (favors continuous)** |
| Minor bleeding (12 studies) | 4.6% vs 4.8% | 0.93 (0.69-1.24) | 0% | No |
| Thrombotic (19 studies) | 5.7% vs 6.3% | 0.54 (0.33-0.87) | 52% | Yes (overall) |
| Thrombotic @30d | 4.9% vs 4.5% | 0.57 (0.28-1.16) | 34% | No |
| Mortality (4 studies) | 0.3% vs 0.8% | 0.33 (0.08-1.41) | 0% | No |
| Total complications (9 studies) | 6.1% vs 5.9% | 1.05 (0.71-1.54) | 0% | No |

**RCT subgroup (8 RCTs):** thrombotic OR 0.53 (0.17-1.64), major bleeding OR 0.82 (0.42-1.61), minor bleeding OR 1.02 (0.71-1.45) — all NS.
**Dental subgroup (underpowered):** major bleeding OR 1.02 (0.08-12.70); minor bleeding OR 1.44 (0.11-18.47) — no signal.
**Interruption timing:** thrombotic benefit favoring continuation appeared with morning-of (OR 0.51) and 48h (OR 0.41) interruption, not 24h (OR 0.68, NS).

## Clinical Bottom Line (dental relevance)

Minor dental procedures — extractions, restorations, prosthetics, endodontics, cleanings, fillings — sit squarely in the minimal bleeding-risk category. This review supports the prevailing dental guidance to **not routinely interrupt DOACs** for these procedures: continuation did not increase major or minor bleeding, and in observational data was associated with fewer thrombotic events. The honest caveat is that the dental-specific subgroup was too small (very wide CIs) and the overall continuation advantage disappeared under randomized conditions — so the defensible position is that **both continuing and briefly interrupting are acceptable**, decided per patient, with local hemostatic measures rather than a blanket interrupt-everyone policy.
