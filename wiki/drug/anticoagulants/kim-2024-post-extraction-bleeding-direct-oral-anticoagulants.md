## One-line Summary

Korean single-center retrospective cohort (NHIS Ilsan Hospital, 2015–2021; n=293 patients, 414 teeth) — continuing DOAC therapy during tooth extraction did not significantly increase post-extraction bleeding vs discontinuing (12/246 = 4.9% continued vs 0/47 discontinued, P=0.122).

## Summary

This retrospective cohort from National Health Insurance Service Ilsan Hospital (Korea) addresses the domestic evidence gap on whether direct oral anticoagulants (DOACs) need short-term discontinuation before tooth extraction. Among 293 DOAC-treated patients (414 teeth extracted, 2015–2021), 246 were extracted while continuing DOAC therapy and 47 after discontinuation (only on the prescriber's recommendation). Post-extraction bleeding (active bleeding/seepage judged by an oral surgeon, controlled by suture, pressure, or biting) occurred in 12 patients — all in the continued group (4.9%) and none in the discontinued group — with no statistically significant difference (P=0.122). INR did not differ between groups (1.15 vs 1.13, P=0.866) and showed no correlation with bleeding, consistent with INR being an unreliable monitor for DOACs. The authors conclude that continuing DOAC therapy during extraction does not increase bleeding tendency and that local hemostatic management (atraumatic technique, sutures, gelatin sponge, gauze pressure, confirming hemostasis before discharge, ± tranexamic acid mouthwash) is decisive.

## Key Contributions

- First Korean cohort to quantify post-extraction bleeding on DOACs (dabigatran, rivaroxaban, apixaban, edoxaban) and to directly compare continuation vs discontinuation.
- Real-world evidence that uninterrupted DOAC extraction is not associated with higher bleeding, reinforcing the international "continue therapy" consensus in a domestic NHIS dataset.
- Per-drug, per-technique, and per-arch bleeding breakdowns.

## Methodology

- **Design**: single-center retrospective cohort; IRB-approved (NHIMC 2022-03-004), consent waived.
- **N**: 293 patients (133 M / 160 F), 414 teeth (231 maxillary / 183 mandibular).
- **Groups**: 246 continued DOAC vs 47 discontinued (prescriber-directed).
- **DOACs**: dabigatran (direct thrombin/factor IIa inhibitor); rivaroxaban, apixaban, edoxaban (factor Xa inhibitors).
- **Bleeding outcome**: surgeon-judged active bleeding/seepage requiring suture, pressure, or biting; PT/INR not routinely tested (recent INR used when available).
- **Hemostasis**: pre-op antibiotic prophylaxis, 2% lidocaine/1:80,000 epi, 3-0 silk sutures, gauze pressure, absorbable gelatin sponge for persistence; no socket grafting.
- **Statistics**: chi-square between groups; per-drug bleeding frequency.

## Results

- **Continue vs discontinue**: 12/246 (4.9%) vs 0/47; **P=0.122 (NS)**. (Abstract reports 6.9% overall; body computes 4.1% of 293 / 4.9% of 246; 12/414 = 2.9% of sites.)
- **INR**: 1.15±0.50 vs 1.13±0.55, P=0.866; no INR–bleeding correlation.
- **Age**: 77.4±8.9 vs 74.5±10.6 years, P=0.087.
- **Per DOAC**: dabigatran 3/75, rivaroxaban 3/53, apixaban 7/216, edoxaban 0 — differences NS.
- **Technique**: 5/87 osteotomy/surgical vs 7/327 simple extractions.
- **Arch**: 4 maxillary vs 8 mandibular bleeding sites (mandible-predominant pattern).
- **Limitations**: unequal groups (246 vs 47, zero events in the small arm), retrospective single-center, small per-DOAC subgroups, inconsistent percentage denominators, no graded bleeding severity. Authors call for larger multicenter studies.
