## One-line Summary
Crossover trial (healthy volunteers): ibuprofen taken BEFORE aspirin blocks platelet COX-1 acetylation → antiplatelet effect lost; taken after has no impact; rofecoxib, diclofenac, and acetaminophen do not interfere.

## 한줄요약

## Summary
This pharmacodynamic crossover study by FitzGerald's group at the University of Pennsylvania is the foundational human trial establishing the ibuprofen–aspirin interaction. Healthy volunteers received aspirin 81 mg QAM with various co-drugs over 6-day arms. The critical finding is that **timing matters**: ibuprofen taken 2 hours before aspirin blocks aspirin's irreversible acetylation of platelet COX-1, whereas aspirin taken 2 hours before ibuprofen preserves full antiplatelet effect. Rofecoxib (COX-2 selective), diclofenac (delayed-release 75 mg BID), and acetaminophen (1000 mg) produced no interference in either order.

## Key Contributions
- Established order-dependent pharmacodynamic interaction between ibuprofen and low-dose aspirin at platelet COX-1
- Demonstrated COX-2 selectivity (rofecoxib) eliminates the interaction: COX-2 inhibitors do not occupy the COX-1 active site
- Confirmed clinical safety of aspirin-before-ibuprofen dosing as a strategy to preserve cardioprotection
- Extended findings to diclofenac and acetaminophen (no interaction), providing guidance for alternative analgesic choices in aspirin-dependent patients

## Methodology
- **Design**: Multiple crossover arms, 6 days per arm, healthy human volunteers
- **Aspirin dose**: 81 mg every morning (low-dose/cardioprotective dose)
- **Comparators**: ibuprofen 400 mg QD or TID, rofecoxib 25 mg QD, acetaminophen 1000 mg QD, delayed-release diclofenac 75 mg BID
- **Primary endpoints**: Serum thromboxane B2 (TXB2; COX-1 activity index) and platelet aggregation, measured 24 h after day-6 dose
- **Mechanism**: NSAIDs and aspirin share a narrow hydrophobic channel in COX-1. Ibuprofen occupying this channel before aspirin arrives sterically prevents acetylation of serine-529/530.

## Results
| Regimen | COX-1 Inhibition (TXB2) | Platelet Aggregation |
|---|---|---|
| Aspirin → ibuprofen 400 mg QD | Maximal inhibition | Suppressed |
| Ibuprofen 400 mg QD → aspirin | **Blocked** | **Not suppressed** |
| Ibuprofen 400 mg TID + EC-aspirin | **Blocked** | **Not suppressed** |
| Aspirin → rofecoxib 25 mg | Maximal inhibition | Suppressed |
| Rofecoxib 25 mg → aspirin | Maximal inhibition | Suppressed |
| Aspirin → acetaminophen 1000 mg | Maximal inhibition | Suppressed |
| Acetaminophen → aspirin | Maximal inhibition | Suppressed |
| EC-aspirin + diclofenac 75 mg BID | Maximal inhibition | Suppressed |

Key conclusion: ibuprofen in any multi-dose regimen taken before aspirin reliably blocks aspirin's antiplatelet effect.
