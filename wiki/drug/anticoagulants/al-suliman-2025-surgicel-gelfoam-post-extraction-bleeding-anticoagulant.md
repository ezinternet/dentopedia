## One-line Summary

Prospective single-blinded split-mouth clinical trial (40 patients, 80 sockets; 20 aspirin + 20 warfarin) — Surgicel (oxidized cellulose) beat Gelfoam (gelatin sponge) on the same patient's contralateral socket for hemostasis (VIBe), 24-h delayed bleeding, gingival healing, and pain (all p<0.05); warfarin patients fared worse than aspirin patients.

## 한줄요약

전향적 단일맹검 분악(split-mouth) 임상시험 (환자 40명, 발치와 80곳; 아스피린 20 + 와파린 20) — Surgicel(산화셀룰로오스)이 같은 환자 반대쪽 발치와에서 Gelfoam(젤라틴 스폰지)보다 지혈·24시간 지연출혈·치은치유·통증 모두 우수(전부 p<0.05); 와파린군이 아스피린군보다 결과 나쁨.

## Summary

In anticoagulated patients (aspirin 80 mg/day or warfarin at INR 2–3.5) undergoing simple extraction of paired teeth, a split-mouth comparison applied Gelfoam to one socket and Surgicel to the contralateral socket of the same patient. Surgicel — oxidized regenerated cellulose — was superior to the gelatin sponge Gelfoam on every endpoint: less intraoperative and reactionary bleeding (VIBe), fewer 24-h delayed bleeding events, better gingival healing at day 7, and lower pain. Across both dressings, warfarin patients bled more, healed slower, and reported more pain than aspirin patients. The clinical thrust: hemostatic socket dressings let anticoagulation continue uninterrupted, and when choosing a dressing, oxidized cellulose is the better default in this population.

Note the design caveat — side allocation was **fixed** (Gelfoam right / Surgicel left), not randomized, and there was no suture-only control arm, so this is graded `prospective` rather than `rct`. No DOAC patients were included.

## Key Contributions

- First split-mouth intra-patient head-to-head of Surgicel vs Gelfoam specifically in anticoagulated extraction patients, isolating dressing effect from patient-level coagulation variability.
- Translates the SR-level "use a local hemostat, don't stop the drug" guidance into a concrete agent preference (oxidized cellulose > gelatin sponge).
- Quantifies the aspirin-vs-warfarin gradient in bleeding, healing, and pain burden.

## Methodology

- **n = 40** (20 aspirin, 20 warfarin); **80 sockets** (paired teeth), 60% molars / 25% premolars / 15% anterior.
- Mean age 58.1 ± 6.89 y; 55% female; mean INR 2.58 ± 0.32 (range 2.1–3.1). Anticoagulant doses unchanged throughout.
- Split-mouth, **fixed side**: Gelfoam → right, Surgicel → left. Atraumatic periotome extraction; figure-of-8 3.0 silk suture over dressing.
- Outcomes: VIBe (FDA-validated bleeding scale) at T0 (immediate) and T1 (2 h); 24-h delayed bleeding (clinic + phone); Gingival Healing Index at D3/D7; VAS pain at D1/D3/D7. G*Power sizing (f=0.47, α=0.05, 95% power); GraphPad Prism (t-tests, ANOVA, Chi-square).

## Results

- **Hemostasis (VIBe, lower better)**: Surgicel < Gelfoam in both drug groups at T0 and T1 (paired t-test p<0.0001). Warfarin > aspirin bleeding at all time points (p<0.0001).
- **24-h delayed bleeding**: Aspirin Gelfoam 10% vs Surgicel 5%; Warfarin Gelfoam 15% vs Surgicel 10%. Surgicel significantly better (Chi-square p<0.05); warfarin > aspirin (p<0.05).
- **Gingival healing (D7, higher better)**: Surgicel > Gelfoam within group (p<0.05); warfarin lower than aspirin at D7 (p<0.05).
- **Pain (VAS)**: Surgicel < Gelfoam at all days (p<0.0001); warfarin > aspirin (p<0.0001).
- INR subgroup (2.1–2.5 vs 2.6–3.1): non-significant trend to more bleeding at higher INR (p>0.05).
- **Caveats**: fixed (non-randomized) side allocation; no suture-only control; split-mouth single session limits pain localization; rare bleeding events overall; single-center Syria; no DOACs.
