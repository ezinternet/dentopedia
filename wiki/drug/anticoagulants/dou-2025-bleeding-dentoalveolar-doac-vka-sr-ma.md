## One-line Summary
SR+MA (29 studies, n=29,212): uninterrupted DOACs carry a 21% lower post-dentoalveolar-surgery bleeding risk than VKAs (RR 0.79, 95% CI 0.68–0.92), driven mainly by dabigatran (RR 0.40); both DOACs (RR 3.23) and VKAs (RR 3.35) raise bleeding ~3-fold vs no-anticoagulant controls, but severe bleeding is rare. GRADE very low.

## Summary
Meta-analysis of 29 observational studies (29,212 patients) comparing post-operative bleeding risk after 치조골 수술 (Dentoalveolar Surgery — extraction, implant placement, alveolar bone reconstruction) in patients on **uninterrupted** 직접경구항응고제 (Direct Oral Anticoagulant, DOAC) vs 비타민 K 길항제 (Vitamin K Antagonist, VKA). Both drug classes roughly triple bleeding risk versus non-anticoagulated controls, but clinically severe bleeding (hospitalization/irreversible harm) was rarely reported. DOACs carry a **21% lower** bleeding risk than VKAs — an advantage driven almost entirely by **dabigatran**. The paper supports continuing anticoagulation with local hemostasis rather than interrupting it.

## Key Contributions
- First MA to **quantify** DOAC-vs-VKA bleeding effect size in dentoalveolar surgery: **RR 0.79 (0.68–0.92)**, I²=0%, P=0.002.
- Drug-level resolution: the benefit is **dabigatran-specific** (RR 0.40, 95% CI 0.23–0.67); rivaroxaban, apixaban, edoxaban show **no** significant difference vs VKAs.
- DOAC vs control RR 3.23 (2.18–4.78); VKA vs control RR 3.35 (2.31–4.85) — both ~3× baseline.
- Concomitant 항혈소판제 (antiplatelet) therapy did not significantly increase bleeding.
- Pharmacologic rationale: dabigatran's short half-life, local thrombin inhibition, and reversibility (idarucizumab) ease hemostatic control vs hard-to-reverse factor Xa inhibitors.

## Methodology
- PubMed, Embase, Web of Science, CENTRAL through 28 Sep 2024; PRISMA / PICOS; PROSPERO CRD42024608201.
- 29 studies (13 cohort, 14 case-control, 2 cross-sectional). No RCTs (ethical constraints).
- Quality: ROBINS-I; certainty: GRADE → **very low**.
- Pooled RR via **fixed-effects** model (RevMan 5.4); heterogeneity Q + I²; subgroup, sensitivity, Begg/Egger publication-bias tests (all non-significant).

## Results
| Comparison | Studies | n | RR (95% CI) | I² | P |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **DOACs vs VKAs** | 23 | 13,825 | **0.79 (0.68–0.92)** | 0% | 0.002 |
| Dabigatran vs VKAs | — | — | **0.40 (0.23–0.67)** | 0% | 0.0006 |
| Rivaroxaban vs VKAs | — | — | 1.08 (0.84–1.39) | 38% | 0.54 (NS) |
| Apixaban vs VKAs | — | — | 0.88 (0.64–1.3) | 0% | 0.46 (NS) |
| Edoxaban vs VKAs | — | — | 0.70 (0.45–1.11) | 60% | 0.13 (NS) |
| DOACs vs Control | 10 | 4,091 | 3.23 (2.18–4.78) | 24% | <0.0001 |
| VKAs vs Control | 7 | 4,565 | 3.35 (2.31–4.85) | 0% | <0.0001 |

- Bleeding incidence across studies ranged 0%–46%.
- Hemostatic adjuncts cited as effective: sutures, pressure, gelatin sponge, oxidized cellulose, chitosan agents, tranexamic acid sponges.
- **Bottom line**: continue (do not interrupt) anticoagulation for dentoalveolar surgery with local hemostasis; if a switch/choice exists, dabigatran has the most favorable bleeding profile vs VKAs. Evidence very low → interpret cautiously.
