## 한줄요약
3번째 대구치 발치 후 통증에 대한 SR+MA (20 RCT): 이부프로펜 400mg 단독 TOTPAR6 SMD=3.009(대효과); 아세트아미노펜+코데인 SMD=0.796(중간효과); 이부프로펜이 명확하게 우위 (Pain Medicine 2022).

## Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis of 20 placebo-controlled RCTs comparing single-dose ibuprofen (400mg) or acetaminophen 600mg+codeine 60mg (A/C) for pain after third-molar extraction. Ibuprofen showed a large standardized mean difference for TOTPAR6 (3.009, p=0.001) and significant PR at 6h (SMD=0.854, p<0.001). A/C showed a moderate TOTPAR6 effect (SMD=0.796, p<0.001) but non-significant PR at 6h (SMD=0.019, p=0.059). Ibuprofen is clearly the more effective single-dose analgesic for third-molar extraction pain.

## Key Contributions
- Focused direct comparison of ibuprofen vs codeine-containing combination in 3rd molar model
- Ibuprofen TOTPAR6 SMD=3.009 = large effect (threshold ≥0.8)
- A/C codeine combination has moderate TOTPAR6 effect but fails on PR at 6h
- Supports removal of codeine from dental analgesic protocols in favor of ibuprofen
- Provides meta-analytic complement to NMA studies that include combination ibuprofen+APAP

## Methodology
PubMed + ClinicalTrials.gov search using "molar/molars," "tooth/teeth," "extraction," "pain." Inclusion: (1) randomized, blinded, placebo-controlled; (2) single dose A/C (APAP 600mg+codeine 60mg) or ibuprofen 400mg; (3) TOTPAR6 or PR at 6h recorded. 2,949 identified → 79 full-text → 20 included. Weighted SMD calculated vs placebo.

## Results
| Drug | TOTPAR6 SMD | 95% CI | p | PR at 6h SMD | 95% CI | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
