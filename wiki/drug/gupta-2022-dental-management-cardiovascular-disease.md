## Summary
Narrative review covering dental management protocols for 7 cardiovascular disease categories. Key update: **no elective dental care within 30 days of MI** (revised from prior 6-month rule). Drug interactions between CVD medications and commonly used dental drugs are tabulated.

## BP-Based Treatment Decision

| BP (mmHg) | Action |
|---|---|
| <140/90 | Proceed normally |
| 140–159/90–99 | Monitor; proceed with caution |
| 160–179/100–109 | Defer elective; emergency only with precaution |
| ≥180/120 (Crisis) | **Defer all treatment; refer immediately** |

- White coat effect affects 15–30%; verify with out-of-office readings before deferral

## Ischemic Heart Disease / MI

| Scenario | Dental Management |
|---|---|
| Stable angina | Elective OK; short appointments; keep nitroglycerin available |
| Unstable angina | Refer to physician; no elective |
| Post-MI <30 days | **No elective dental care** |
| Post-MI 1–6 months, symptom-free | Elective with caution |
| Post-MI >6 months | Routine care |

- Avoid early morning and late afternoon (peak cardiac event times)
- Profound LA + anxiolysis → minimize stress-induced angina

## Infective Endocarditis Prophylaxis (AHA 2007)
High-risk only:
- Prosthetic cardiac valve
- Prior IE history
- Congenital cyanotic heart disease (unrepaired or recent repair)
→ **Amoxicillin 2g PO 30–60 min before procedure**

## Drug Interactions

| Dental Drug | CVD Interaction | Clinical Implication |
|---|---|---|
| NSAIDs | ↓ antihypertensive effect; ↑ bleeding on anticoagulants | Use acetaminophen instead in hypertensive patients |
| Epinephrine (1:100,000) | Transient BP ↑; arrhythmia risk | Safe in controlled CVD; limit 2 carpules (0.036 mg) |
| Amide LA | ↓ metabolism with certain antihypertensives | Use standard doses; observe |
| Macrolide antibiotics | QT prolongation with cardiac drugs | Avoid clarithromycin + some antiarrhythmics |

## Anticoagulants
- Do NOT routinely stop for simple extractions
- Use local hemostatic measures (tranexamic acid, gelfoam, pressure)
