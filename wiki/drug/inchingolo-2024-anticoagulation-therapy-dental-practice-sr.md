## One-line Summary
Systematic review (25 studies, 1989–2024): VKA and DOAC discontinuation unnecessary for routine dental procedures; local hemostatic measures (tranexamic acid mouthwash) are first-line; thrombotic rebound risk from discontinuation exceeds bleeding risk.

## 한줄요약
체계적 고찰(25편, 1989–2024): 항응고제 복용 환자에서 루틴 치과 처치 시 VKA·DOAC 중단 불필요; 국소 지혈 처치(트라넥사민산 양치액)가 1차 대응; 중단 시 혈전 리바운드 위험이 출혈 위험 초과

## Summary
Systematic review of 25 clinical studies on anticoagulated patients undergoing dental procedures. **Consensus: do not routinely stop anticoagulants.** Local hemostatic measures are sufficient for most procedures. Tranexamic acid mouthwash is the best-evidenced adjunct.

## Core Principle
> **항응고제 임의 중단 금지** — 중단에 따른 혈전색전증 리스크 > 치과 처치 출혈 리스크

## Management by Drug Class

### VKA (Warfarin)
| INR | Action |
|---|---|
| ≤3.5 | Proceed; add local hemostasis |
| 3.5–4.0 | Defer elective; consult prescriber |
| >4.0 | Defer all; urgent INR normalization |

- Check INR within 24–72 hours of procedure
- Do NOT bridge-stop for simple extractions

### DOACs (Dabigatran, Rivaroxaban, Apixaban, Edoxaban)
- Simple procedures: **continue DOACs unchanged**
- Major surgery (jaw resection, multiple extractions, implant placement):
  - Skip morning dose on procedure day
  - Resume evening if hemostasis achieved
  - Dabigatran: extra care in CKD (renal clearance 80%)
- No INR monitoring needed; no reversal required for routine procedures

### Antiplatelet (Aspirin / Clopidogrel / Dual antiplatelet)
- **Do NOT stop** — thrombosis rebound (stent thrombosis risk)
- Local pressure + oxidized cellulose sufficient in most cases
- Dual antiplatelet therapy: consult cardiologist before any surgery; usually continue both

## Local Hemostatic Measures

| Agent | Evidence | Protocol |
|---|---|---|
| **Tranexamic acid 4.8% mouthwash** | ✅ Strongest (1993–2024) | Rinse 10 mL × 2 min, 4×/day × 7 days; swallow prohibited |
| Oxidized cellulose (Surgicel) | ✅ Moderate | Pack socket; primary closure |
| L-PRF (leukocyte-platelet fibrin) | ⚠️ Emerging | Socket fill; reduces complications |
| N-butyl-2-cyanoacrylate glue | ✅ Alternative | Comparable hemostasis to conventional |
| Sutures + pressure pack | ✅ Always | Primary closure when possible |

## Clinical Takeaway
1. **Never stop anticoagulants without prescribing physician approval**
2. **INR ≤3.5**: proceed with tranexamic acid mouthwash + local hemostasis
3. **DOACs**: continue for simple extractions; time-adjust for major surgery
4. **Antiplatelet**: always continue; thrombosis risk of stopping >> bleeding risk
5. **Tranexamic acid 4.8% mouthwash**: order every time for anticoagulated extractions
