## Summary
Prospective randomized double-blind RCT in 71 emergency endodontic patients with high pre-op pain (necrosis + apical radiolucency + moderate-to-severe pain). After emergency debridement, randomized to ibuprofen 600mg q6h alone vs ibuprofen 600mg + APAP 1000mg q6h. Combination superior in pain reduction and reduced rescue analgesic use.

## Key Contributions
- Real-world emergency endodontic patient cohort (high baseline pain).
- Confirms ibuprofen + APAP > ibuprofen alone in this indication.
- Adds clinical-context endorsement to the NNT-based Moore 2015 framework.

## Methodology
Prospective double-blind RCT; n=71; ibuprofen 600mg q6h vs ibuprofen 600mg + APAP 1000mg q6h post emergency debridement.

## Results
[근거강함] Combination → significantly less pain + less rescue medication use.

## Limitations
- Small (n=71), single-center
- No combo with codeine/opioid arm
- Single year of testing

## Clinical Implication
For Korean endodontic emergency practice: combination ibuprofen + APAP is the protocol of first choice for symptomatic necrosis cases. Single-NSAID monotherapy is inferior.
