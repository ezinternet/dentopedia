## One-line Summary
10-year retrospective (n=744 orofacial infections, Bratislava): amoxicillin-clavulanate had the lowest resistance (4.9% per isolate; 7.7% per patient) vs clindamycin 25.8%, metronidazole 25.5%, penicillin 15.3%, supporting amox-clav as first-line empirical therapy.

## 한줄요약
구강안면감염 744명 10년 후향: amox-clav 내성 4.9%로 최저(clindamycin 25.8%·metronidazole 25.5%·penicillin 15.3%) → 경험적 1차로 amox-clav 권고, 단 외과적 배농이 우선.

## Summary
Single-center 10-year retrospective study of 744 hospitalized orofacial infection patients. Aetiology was predominantly odontogenic (83.3%), most often with the causal tooth still in the socket (62.9%). Among 698 isolates, Streptococci, Prevotella, and Parvimonas were most frequent. Resistance was highest for clindamycin (25.8%) and metronidazole (25.5%) and lowest for amoxicillin-clavulanate (4.9% per isolate), leading the authors to recommend amox-clav as the empirical first choice when antibiotics are indicated, while emphasizing that surgical source control and drainage outweigh antibiotic selection.

## Key Contributions
- Large (n=744) local resistance dataset informing empirical antibiotic choice for orofacial infections.
- Quantifies amox-clav as lowest-resistance option (4.9% isolate / 7.7% patient).
- Flags high clindamycin/metronidazole resistance, limiting penicillin-allergic alternatives.

## Methodology
Retrospective chart review, Comenius University Bratislava, 2014-2023, n=744. Resistance assessed for penicillin, amoxicillin-clavulanate, clindamycin, metronidazole. Treatment protocol: first-line IV amox-clav 1.2 g q8h + metronidazole; penicillin-allergic, clindamycin + metronidazole. Kruskal-Wallis, Bonferroni, regression (AIC) analyses.

## Results
- Aetiology odontogenic 83.3% (tooth-in-socket 62.9%, post-extraction 20.4%).
- Resistance per isolate: clindamycin 25.8% > metronidazole 25.5% > penicillin 15.3% > amox-clav 4.9%.
- Per patient: metronidazole 41.4%, clindamycin 39%, penicillin 24.6%, amox-clav 7.7%.
- CRP increased ~24.75 mg/l per additional pathogen (p<0.001); resistant-strain carriers had higher CRP (158.4 vs 114.4 mg/l, p<0.001).
