## Summary
The 2022 AAOMS position paper (fourth edition; prior: 2007, 2009, 2014) updates MRONJ management guidance. The case definition and staging system are unchanged from 2014. New additions include romosozumab as an at-risk medication, removal of bone turnover markers (CTX) from clinical recommendations, specific denosumab holiday timing guidance, and an explicit statement that no AAOMS consensus was reached on drug holidays. The paper reinforces pre-treatment dental optimization as the most evidence-supported prevention strategy.

## Key Contributions
- **Romosozumab (Evenity®)** added to MRONJ-associated medication list
- **Drug holiday**: no consensus; AAOMS working group evenly split; CTX no longer useful
- **Denosumab-specific holiday protocol** (if pursued): surgery 3–4 months after last dose → restart 6–8 weeks post-surgery
- **Rebound fracture risk** emphasized for denosumab discontinuation: multiple-level vertebral fractures reported
- Reinforces pre-ARD dental screening as the strongest evidence-based preventive measure

## MRONJ Case Definition
Requires ALL three:
1. Current/previous antiresorptive therapy (±immune modulators/antiangiogenics)
2. Exposed bone or bone probeable through intraoral/extraoral fistula in maxillofacial region, persisting **>8 weeks**
3. No history of jaw radiation; no jaw metastatic disease

## Staging System

| Stage | Description |
|---|---|
| At-risk | Asymptomatic; on ARD; no necrotic bone |
| 0 | No exposed bone; nonspecific symptoms (odontalgia, jaw pain, paresthesia); radiographic changes (sclerosis, PDL widening) |
| 1 | Exposed/necrotic bone; **asymptomatic**; no infection |
| 2 | Exposed/necrotic bone; **symptomatic**; with infection |
| 3 | Stage 2 + necrosis beyond alveolar bone, pathologic fracture, extraoral fistula, oro-antral/nasal communication, or osteolysis to inferior border/sinus floor |

Stage 0 → Stage 1 progression in up to 50% of cases.

## Drug Holiday (Controversy)
- No RCT data sufficient to support or refute
- AAOMS 2022 working group: **evenly split** (case-by-case holiday vs. never recommend)
- If denosumab holiday elected: surgery at **3–4 months** after last dose; reinstitute **6–8 weeks** postoperatively
- Concern: discontinuing denosumab risks rebound increase in bone resorption → multiple vertebral fractures

## Bone Turnover Markers
- CTX and other biomarkers: **not validated** for clinical MRONJ risk assessment
- No biomarker currently recommended for decision-making

## Prevention by Patient Group
**Pre-therapy, non-malignant disease (osteoporosis)**: Risk is low; educate about MRONJ; dental optimization can occur concurrently with ARD initiation; no urgency for pre-treatment clearance.

**Pre-therapy, malignant disease**: Extract non-restorable/poor-prognosis teeth before ARD start if systemic status allows; delay ARD until mucosal healing (2.5× relative risk reduction with regimented surveillance).

**During ARD, non-malignant**: No routine operative plan change; weigh drug schedule, comorbidities, surgery extent.

**During ARD, malignant**: Avoid dentoalveolar surgery; consider root retention; **dental implants contraindicated**.
