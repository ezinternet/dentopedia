## One-line Summary
Cochrane SR (CD003811.pub3, 3rd update): perioperative antibiotic prophylaxis significantly reduces infectious complications, pain, dry socket, swelling, and trismus after tooth extraction, but routine use in healthy patients must be weighed against adverse effects and resistance risk.

## Summary
This Cochrane systematic review (third update, 2021) synthesizes randomized evidence on whether perioperative antibiotic prophylaxis prevents complications after tooth extraction in otherwise healthy patients. The review evaluates eight outcomes at day 6–7 post-extraction: infectious complications, pain (dichotomous and VAS), fever, swelling, trismus, dry socket, and adverse events. Meta-analyses (Analyses 1.1–1.8) assess each. The review provides the highest level of evidence informing antibiotic stewardship in oral surgery: while antibiotics reduce several post-extraction complications, the absolute benefit must be weighed against antibiotic-related adverse events and the broader public health imperative of minimizing antibiotic resistance.

## Key Contributions
- Highest-quality evidence (Cochrane) on antibiotic prophylaxis for tooth extraction complications
- Covers all key post-extraction outcomes: infection, pain, dry socket, fever, swelling, trismus, adverse events
- Informs international clinical guidelines on when antibiotic prophylaxis is and is not justified
- Quantifies benefit (RR reduction for each outcome) vs. harm (adverse event NNH) to enable shared decision-making
- Especially relevant for third-molar (wisdom tooth) extractions where dry socket risk is highest

## Methodology
- **Type**: Cochrane SR, 3rd update of CD003811
- **Databases**: Cochrane Oral Health Group Register, CENTRAL, MEDLINE, EMBASE; no language restriction
- **Included studies**: RCTs comparing antibiotic vs. placebo/no treatment perioperatively for tooth extraction
- **Population**: Generally healthy (immunocompetent) adults
- **Antibiotic**: Any type, dose, timing
- **Primary outcome**: Postsurgical infectious complications at day 6–7
- **Secondary outcomes**: Pain (dichotomous; VAS), fever, swelling, trismus, dry socket, adverse events (all at day 6–7)

## Results
Eight meta-analyses (Analyses 1.1–1.8) cover:

| Outcome | Direction of Effect |
|---|---|
| Infectious complications (1.1) | Antibiotics reduce infection |
| Pain — dichotomous (1.2) | Antibiotics reduce pain prevalence |
| Pain — VAS score (1.3) | Antibiotics reduce pain severity |
| Fever (1.4) | Antibiotics reduce fever |
| Swelling (1.5) | Antibiotics reduce swelling |
| Trismus (1.6) | Antibiotics reduce trismus |
| Dry socket (1.7) | Antibiotics reduce dry socket |
| Adverse events (1.8) | Antibiotics increase GI adverse events |

Key clinical balance: antibiotics prevent dry socket and infectious complications, but adverse events (primarily GI) occur in a meaningful proportion of patients; for healthy patients with routine extractions, the NNT vs. NNH comparison typically does not favor routine prophylaxis; high-risk extractions (impacted third molars, immunocompromised patients, complex surgery) may still benefit.
