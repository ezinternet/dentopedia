## One-line Summary

Single-center retrospective case-control study (n=2,059 extractions in 1,314 hypertensive patients under intraoperative ECG monitoring): 8.01% required intraoperative antihypertensive drugs and 37.20% developed transient new-onset ECG abnormalities (all non-lethal, only 2 procedures terminated); preoperative SBP ≥159 mmHg (AUC 0.91, sens 84%/spec 89%) and operative time (OR 3.62) predicted BP intervention, while older age, female sex, cerebrovascular disease, more teeth extracted, and longer operative time predicted ECG abnormalities.

## Summary

A retrospective case-control study of **2,059 tooth-extraction visits** in **1,314 hypertensive patients** (mean age 74.6 y, 70% with ≥1 other comorbidity) seen under continuous intraoperative ECG + serial BP monitoring at Peking University School of Stomatology. The study separately models two intraoperative outcomes — need for acute antihypertensive intervention, and new-onset ECG abnormality — and derives an actionable **preoperative SBP cutoff of ≥159 mmHg** (AUC 0.91) for triaging who is likely to need intraoperative BP control. Overall the procedure was safe: all arrhythmias were non-lethal and only 2/2,059 procedures were terminated, but a notable 37.2% rate of transient new arrhythmias supports routine ECG monitoring in this elderly hypertensive population.

## Key Contributions

- Derives the first extraction-specific **preoperative SBP threshold (≥159 mmHg, AUC 0.91, sensitivity 84.24%, specificity 88.75%)** for predicting intraoperative antihypertensive need — filling a gap where ACC/AHA only generically labels dental procedures "low-risk."
- Demonstrates the two risk outcomes have **distinct predictor sets** (hemodynamic/procedural for BP intervention; patient + procedural for ECG abnormality).
- Provides reassurance on overall safety while quantifying the high background rate (37.2%) of benign transient arrhythmias.

## Methodology

- Design: single-center retrospective case-control; year 2022 records, 3,312 → 2,059 extraction visits after exclusions.
- Monitoring: continuous ECG (NIHON KOHDEN BSM-2351 C); BP every 5–10 min at three timepoints (preop, operation start, intraoperative max); cardiologist co-management.
- **Intervention trigger**: intraoperative SBP ≥180 mmHg, or SBP >170 mmHg on ≥2 readings → temporary antihypertensive (nitroglycerin in 86%).
- **ECG abnormality**: any new finding absent on preoperative ECG.
- Multivariate logistic regression (enter method) per outcome; ROC for cutoffs; SPSS v27, P<0.05.
- Confidence: **retrospective** (single-center, no causality; repeat visits treated as independent events).

## Results

**Outcome 1 — Intraoperative antihypertensive intervention: 165/2,059 = 8.01%** (167 flagged high-risk, 2 terminated).
- Independent predictors: preoperative SBP OR 1.138 (P<0.001); SBP at operation start OR 1.019 (P=0.028); HR at start OR 1.064 (P=0.004); estimated operative time OR **3.620** (P<0.001).
- ROC: preoperative SBP **AUC 0.91**; cutoff ~159 mmHg → sensitivity 84.24%, specificity 88.75%.
- Drug: nitroglycerin 0.3–0.5 mg in 86%; rest captopril/metoprolol/combos.
- NOT significant: age, sex, coronary heart disease, diabetes, cerebrovascular disease, arrhythmia, number of teeth, handpiece, surgical incision (P=0.068 trend), periodontitis.

**Outcome 2 — New-onset intraoperative ECG abnormality: 766/2,059 = 37.20%** (all non-lethal).
- Most common: supraventricular premature atrial contraction (44.4%) + ventricular premature contraction (42.3%) = 86.7% combined.
- Independent predictors: age OR 1.024 (P<0.001); female sex OR 0.796 (P=0.019, females higher risk); cerebrovascular disease OR 1.301 (P=0.020); SBP at start OR 1.011 (P=0.006); number of teeth extracted OR 1.182 (P<0.001); estimated operative time OR 1.236 (P=0.017).
- ROC: age AUC only 0.57 (cutoff 79.5 y) — weak discrimination.
- NOT significant: coronary heart disease, diabetes, preoperative SBP/HR, handpiece, incision, periodontitis.

**Baseline hemodynamics**: females higher SBP/HR & lower DBP (P<0.001); age ≥76 higher SBP, lower DBP, slower HR.

**Clinical takeaway**: Screen preoperative SBP — at ≥159 mmHg expect likely intraoperative BP intervention. For elderly, female, cerebrovascular-disease, or multi-tooth/long cases, use continuous ECG monitoring and consider fewer extractions per visit / shorter sessions to reduce intraoperative cardiovascular stress.
