## One-line Summary

SR+MA (7 studies qualitative / 5 quantitative) finds immediately loaded dental implants in type 2 diabetics show no survival difference vs nondiabetics (RR 1.00, 95% CI 0.96–1.04; I²=0%), even with poor glycemic control (RR 1.08, 95% CI 0.87–1.33), and no marginal bone loss difference vs conventional loading (MD −0.08 mm).

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (정성 7편 / 정량 5편): 제2형 당뇨 환자의 즉시부하(immediate loading) 임플란트 생존율이 비당뇨군과 차이 없고(RR 1.00), 혈당 조절 불량 환자에서도 차이 없었으며(RR 1.08), 변연골소실(MBL)도 통상부하 대비 차이 없었다 — 프로토콜·구강위생 준수 시 DM2는 즉시부하의 위험인자가 아니다.

## Summary

This is the first systematic review and meta-analysis to focus specifically on **immediately loaded (IL)** dental implants — prosthesis within 72 h of placement — in type 2 diabetes mellitus (DM2), a question earlier diabetes-implant reviews left to conventional loading. Pooling 5 low-risk-of-bias studies, it finds **no survival penalty** for IL in DM2 relative to nondiabetics, holding **even in uncontrolled (high HbA1c) patients**, and no difference in 12-month marginal bone loss between immediate and conventional loading within DM2. The clinical message: with strict surgical/prosthetic protocol adherence and good oral hygiene, IL appears safe in DM2 — but the evidence base is thin (≤3 studies per pooled estimate, mostly ≤24 mo follow-up, high heterogeneity in the uncontrolled-DM2 and MBL analyses), so the conclusion is provisional.

## Key Contributions

- First dedicated SR+MA on immediate loading in DM2 (PROSPERO CRD42021223736, PRISMA).
- Three meta-analyses: IL survival in DM2 vs nondiabetic; IL survival in **uncontrolled** DM2 vs nondiabetic; MBL of IL vs conventional loading (CL) in DM2 at 12 months.
- Reframes DM2 from presumed IL contraindication toward conditional safety, with glycemic control and oral hygiene as the gating factors.

## Methodology

- Searched 04 Jan 2021: MEDLINE/PubMed, Cochrane, BVS, Web of Science, Scopus, LIVIVO + gray literature. 3566 records → 2210 de-duped → 49 full-text → **7 qualitative / 5 quantitative**. Two blinded reviewers, Cohen's κ = 0.87.
- Eligible: human clinical trials/cohort/case-control/cross-sectional/case-series reporting survival, success, or peri-implant measures (BOP, PD, MBL) of IL in DM2. Reviews, animal/in-vitro, case reports excluded.
- Included studies (2008–2020) from Portugal, Lebanon, Spain, Saudi Arabia, Egypt, Romania; n = 4–108 patients, 16–352 implants; follow-up mostly 6–24 mo (one 5-yr, one 12-yr); single/partial/overdenture/All-on-Four prostheses.
- RevMan 5.3; RR for survival counts, MD for MBL; 95% CI; I² for heterogeneity. Risk of bias by JBI Critical Appraisal Tools (all 5 quantitative studies = low risk).

## Results

| Comparison | Effect | 95% CI | p | I² |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| IL survival: DM2 vs nondiabetic | RR 1.00 | 0.96–1.04 | 0.91 | 0% |
| IL survival: uncontrolled DM2 vs nondiabetic | RR 1.08 | 0.87–1.33 | 0.48 | 70% |
| MBL: IL vs conventional loading in DM2 (12 mo) | MD −0.08 mm | −0.25 to 0.08 | 0.33 | 83% |

- Survival was 100% in 5/7 studies; 86.3–100% in the other two (the 86.3% from an uncontrolled DM2 group). Success rate reported in 4 studies — all 100%.
- Survival analyses drew on studies with ≥24-month follow-up.
- The uncontrolled-DM2 and MBL analyses carry **high heterogeneity** and rest on very few studies (the MBL pool is only 2 studies), so these two estimates are the least robust.
- HbA1c level correlated with peri-implant pathology; uncontrolled DM2 tended toward higher MBL/BOP/PD descriptively even where pooled survival did not differ.
