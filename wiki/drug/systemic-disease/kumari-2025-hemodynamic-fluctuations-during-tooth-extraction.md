## One-line Summary

Prospective observational study (n=50 normotensive adults) showing SBP, DBP and HR rise significantly (P=0.0001) during injection and extraction under 2% lidocaine + 1:100,000 adrenaline, peaking at the extraction step and recovering below baseline by 5 min postop; authors urge anxiolysis/sedation caution in anxious and hypertensive patients.

## 한줄요약

전향적 관찰연구 (정상혈압 성인 50명): 2% 리도카인 + 1:100,000 아드레날린 하 발치 시 수축기·이완기 혈압과 심박수가 마취주사·발치 단계에서 유의하게 상승(P=0.0001), 발치 시점 최고치 후 술후 5분에 기저치 이하로 회복 — 불안·고혈압 환자에서 진정/항불안제 사용 권고.

## Summary

This single-center prospective observational study measured intraoperative hemodynamic fluctuations during routine tooth extraction performed under 2% lidocaine with 1:100,000 adrenaline. Fifty healthy, **normotensive** adults (equal male/female, mean age 45.8 y) had SBP, DBP, HR and SpO2 recorded at four timepoints: 5 min preoperative (T1), injection (T2), extraction (T3), and 5 min postoperative (T4). All four parameters varied significantly across time (P=0.0001), with blood pressure and heart rate rising during injection/extraction, peaking at the extraction step, then falling below baseline postoperatively. The absolute magnitude was modest in this normotensive cohort (overall SBP swing ~6 mmHg). The authors extrapolate that anxious and hypertensive patients should be managed with care, including distraction, anxiolytics, or conscious sedation — though hypertensives were excluded from the sample, so this recommendation is inferred rather than directly tested.

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies the **timing** of the hemodynamic response to extraction: BP and HR peak at the extraction step (T3), not at needle insertion, and recover below baseline by 5 min postop.
- Provides a **normotensive reference curve** against which the steeper expected response of true hypertensives can be framed.
- Documents a gender pattern: females show larger BP rises; males have higher baseline SBP and greater pain tolerance (mean VAS 2/10).
- Reinforces the supportive-care rationale for anxiolysis/conscious sedation in anxious and hypertensive extraction patients.

## Methodology

- **Design**: prospective observational, single institution Dental OPD.
- **Sample**: 50 by convenience, equal male/female, mean age 45.8 y (18–65); no a priori power calculation.
- **Inclusion**: healthy, not on medication, requiring extraction.
- **Exclusion**: hypertension, diabetes, pregnancy, systemic illness, any medication → cohort is normotensive (central caveat).
- **Intervention**: lidocaine 2 mL, 2% with 1:100,000 adrenaline.
- **Outcomes**: SBP, DBP, HR, SpO2 at T1 (preop), T2 (injection), T3 (extraction), T4 (postop). Mean operative time 22 min; mean VAS pain 2/10.
- **Analysis**: SPSS v20.0.

## Results

Overall means (all parameters P=0.0001):

| Parameter | Preop (T1) | Injection (T2) | Extraction (T3) | Postop (T4) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| SBP (mmHg) | 126.6±5.41 | 127.5±4.09 | **128.0±3.06** | 122.0±3.51 |
| DBP (mmHg) | 72.7±1.74 | 73.3±2.29 | **74.6±1.95** | 73.5±1.33 |
| HR (bpm) | 80.9±4.18 | 81.1±1.38 | **84.6±4.73** | 76.5±3.76 |
| SpO2 (%) | 97.5±0.67 | 97.9±0.48 | 98.0±0.53 | 98.1±1.08 |

- **SBP/DBP**: rise through injection and extraction, fall below baseline postop; swing larger in females.
- **HR**: peaks at extraction (84.6 bpm), drops below baseline postop (76.5 bpm); consistent across genders.
- **SpO2**: clinically stable (~97–98%) throughout.
- Authors note the small magnitude reflects the normotensive cohort and "low statistical power."
