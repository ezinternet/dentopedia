## One-line Summary

Retrospective cohort (n=728, single Beijing center) — perioperative hypertension occurred in 26.77% of implant + bone-augmentation surgeries; male (RR 1.74), age ≥60 (RR 2.35), and surgical time >60 min (RR 1.65) were risk factors, with the active factors varying by augmentation approach (all significant in GBR; only age in transalveolar sinus lift; none significant in lateral-window sinus lift).

## Summary

This single-center retrospective cohort from Peking University School and Hospital of Stomatology asked how often blood pressure spikes during outpatient implant placement performed *simultaneously with bone augmentation*, and which patients/procedures drive it. Across 728 cases, **perioperative hypertension occurred in 26.77%**, with systolic blood-pressure variability averaging ~10% (max ~50%). Three factors raised risk overall — being **male (RR 1.74)**, **age ≥60 years (RR 2.35)**, and **surgical time >60 min (RR 1.65)**. Importantly, while overall incidence did not differ across guided bone regeneration (GBR), transalveolar sinus lift, and lateral-window sinus lift, the *which-factor-matters* profile changed by approach. Clinically this argues for proactive intraoperative blood-pressure monitoring and patient screening in implant-plus-grafting cases, especially older male patients and procedures expected to run long.

## Key Contributions

- First wiki data point on **perioperative blood-pressure surge during implant + bone-augmentation surgery**: incidence 26.77%.

- Shows the **bone-augmentation approach modulates the risk-factor profile** even when overall incidence is similar.

## Methodology

- **Design**: single-center retrospective cohort, Sept 2021–Aug 2022.
- **n = 728** implant + simultaneous bone-augmentation patients.
- **Exposures**: gender, age, surgical time, augmentation approach (GBR, transalveolar/transcrestal sinus floor elevation, lateral-window sinus floor elevation).
- **Outcome**: perioperative hypertension from systolic/diastolic/mean-arterial-pressure variability.
- **Analysis**: relative risk (95% CI) for each factor; subgroup analysis by approach.

## Results

- **BP variability**: SBP 9.93% ± 6.63% (max 50.41%); DBP 12.45% ± 8.79% (max 68.75%); MAP 10.02% ± 6.61% (max 49.48%).
- **Overall perioperative hypertension incidence: 26.77%.**
- **Whole-cohort risk factors (P < 0.05):**
  | Factor | RR (95% CI) |
  |---|---|
  | Male sex | 1.74 (1.21–2.50) |
  | Age ≥60 years | 2.35 (1.54–3.58) |
  | Surgical time >60 min | 1.65 (1.15–2.38) |
- **By augmentation approach** (incidence not significantly different across the three, P > 0.05):
  - **GBR** → male, age ≥60 y, surgical time >60 min all significant (P < 0.05).
  - **Transalveolar sinus lift** → only age ≥60 y significant (P < 0.05).
  - **Lateral-window sinus lift** → male, age ≥60 y, surgical time >60 min all non-significant (P > 0.05).
- **Clinical takeaway**: monitor BP intraoperatively and pre-screen cardiovascular risk in implant-plus-grafting cases — highest concern in older male patients and longer (>60 min) GBR procedures.
