## One-line Summary

Retrospective analysis of prospectively collected observational data (n=39: 21 normotensive vs 18 treated hypertensive) showing baseline hypertensive status is the dominant perioperative hemodynamic determinant (eta-squared=0.33) and a 7-fold higher hemorrhagic-complication rate (66.7% vs 9.5%, p=0.008) during oral surgery; proposes the unvalidated 3-phase Perozo perioperative BP-management protocol.

## Summary

Arterial hypertension is the most common systemic disease in oral surgery patients, yet perioperative cardiovascular management is poorly standardized — AHA-ADA and WHO-ISH give thresholds but no integrated chairside workflow. This pilot study (n=39: 21 normotensive, 18 controlled/treated hypertensive at two Venezuelan public hospitals) recorded SBP/DBP/MAP/HR at four perioperative time points and found that **baseline hypertensive status is the dominant determinant of hemodynamic variability** (eta-squared = 0.33), eclipsing anesthetic choice, surgical timing, and anxiety. Hypertensives ran higher SBP throughout (preop 159 vs 128; intraop peak 170 vs 136 mmHg) and had a markedly higher **hemorrhagic-complication rate (66.7% vs 9.5%, p=0.008, Cramer's V=0.55)**. Epinephrine-containing lidocaine raised BP/HR more than plain mepivacaine (chiefly in hypertensives); morning procedures peaked higher than afternoon (circadian surge); anxiety drove SBP rises in hypertensives but HR rises in normotensives. The authors packaged these observations into the **Perozo protocol**, a hypothesis-generating 3-phase framework. It is explicitly **unvalidated** and awaits multicenter prospective testing.

## Key Contributions

- Effect-size evidence that **baseline BP status dominates** perioperative hemodynamics in oral surgery (eta-squared = 0.33).
- Large hemorrhagic-risk signal in controlled hypertensives (66.7% vs 9.5%).
- The **Perozo protocol** — a structured 3-phase, 20-step chairside perioperative BP-management framework.
- **Differential anxiety physiology**: anxiety → SBP in hypertensives, → HR in normotensives.

## Methodology

- **Design**: retrospective secondary analysis of prospectively collected, de-identified observational data; pilot cohort; not a clinical trial (no research-assigned interventions).
- **n = 39** — normotensive 21, treated/controlled hypertensive 18. Excluded: uncontrolled HTN (SBP ≥180/DBP ≥110 at baseline), acute CV event ≤3 months, pregnancy, anesthetic contraindications.
- **Time points (4)**: preop baseline → during LA injection → active intraoperative → immediate postoperative.
- **Anxiety**: STAI (low ≤37 / moderate 38–44 / high ≥45).
- **Anesthetics (observed)**: 2% lidocaine + 1:100,000 epinephrine vs 3% plain mepivacaine; surgeon-selected.
- **Timing**: morning (08:00–12:00) vs afternoon (13:00–17:00).
- **Stats**: repeated-measures ANOVA, Pearson Chi-squared (Cramer's V), Pearson correlation; α = 0.05.

## Results

| Variable | Normotensive (n=21) | Hypertensive (n=18) | Stat | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Preop SBP (mmHg) | 128 ± 9 | 159 ± 12 | F=8.41 | 0.006 |
| Intraop SBP (mmHg) | 136 ± 10 | 170 ± 15 | F=9.02 | 0.004 |
| Intraop DBP (mmHg) | 82 ± 7 | 97 ± 9 | F=6.75 | 0.013 |
| Peak HR (bpm) | 86 ± 8 | 85 ± 9 | F=0.14 | 0.71 (NS) |
| Hemorrhagic complications | 9.5% | 66.7% | χ²=7.02 | 0.008 |
| Anxiety vs ΔSBP | — | — | r=0.58 | 0.01 |

- **Anesthetic**: lidocaine+epinephrine > plain mepivacaine for BP/HR rise (mainly hypertensives).
- **Circadian**: morning peak SBP HTN >250 vs NT 196 mmHg (+34%); afternoon HTN 187 vs NT 168 mmHg (+11%).
- **Anxiety** — HTN ΔSBP: 1.5 → 14.7 → 24.9 mmHg (low/mod/high). NT ΔHR: 1.0 → 6.5 → 9.6 bpm.

### The Perozo protocol (3 phases)

- **Phase I — Preoperative risk stratification**: **Low** (SBP <140, DBP <90) → proceed routine; **Moderate** (140–159 / 90–99) → minor procedures only, reduce anxiety, monitor BP; **High** (160–179 / 100–109) → short procedures, strict BP monitoring; **Very high** (≥180 / ≥110) → defer + urgent medical consultation.
- **Phase II — Intraoperative / emergency medications**: captopril 25 mg sublingual (hypertensive crisis); nifedipine bitten capsule (acute surge); nitroglycerin sublingual (coronary symptoms); clonidine (refractory HTN). **Epinephrine capped at 0.04 mg = two cartridges of 1:100,000** in controlled hypertensives.
- **Phase III — Postoperative care**: vital-sign monitoring in recovery; **avoid NSAIDs** (cardiovascular risk); antibiotics if indicated; written discharge instructions; warning-sign education; structured follow-up.

**Caveat**: pilot, single-country two-center, n=39, unvalidated; do not adopt as a validated standard. Multicenter prospective validation required.
