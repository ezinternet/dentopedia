## One-line Summary

Case-control study (n=86: 45 healthy + 41 T2DM) of 8 salivary markers (OPG, RANKL, HGF, TNF-α, IL-18, MMP-9, TOS, TAC) and HbA1c at baseline and 3 months post-extraction; T2DM showed higher inflammatory/oxidative/bone-turnover markers, baseline RANKL/TNF-α/IL-18/MMP-9/TOS/OPG were strong ROC predictors of HbA1c >6.5% (RANKL AUC 0.999), and post-extraction TOS correlated positively (r=+0.777) and TAC negatively (r=−0.475) with HbA1c.

## 한줄요약

건강군 45명·2형 당뇨군 41명에서 타액 8종 마커와 HbA1c를 발치 전·발치 3개월 후 측정한 case-control 연구로, 당뇨군은 염증·산화·골대사 마커가 더 높았고 baseline RANKL·TNF-α·IL-18·MMP-9·TOS·OPG가 HbA1c>6.5%의 우수한 예측인자(RANKL AUC 0.999)였으며, 발치 후 TOS는 HbA1c와 양의 상관(r=+0.777)·TAC는 음의 상관(r=−0.475)을 보여 비침습 타액 검사가 당뇨 환자 발치 후 치유·혈당 상태의 지표가 될 수 있음을 시사.

## Summary

Type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM) is a major risk factor for oral pathology and impaired post-extraction bone/soft-tissue healing. This Romanian case-control study tested whether a panel of salivary immuno-biochemical markers — bone-metabolism (osteoprotegerin OPG, RANKL), growth (hepatocyte growth factor HGF), inflammatory (TNF-α, interleukin-18 IL-18, matrix metalloproteinase-9 MMP-9) and oxidative-stress (total oxidant status TOS, total antioxidant capacity TAC) — change after tooth extraction and correlate with glycated hemoglobin (HbA1c). Unstimulated whole saliva and venous blood were collected at baseline (T0) and 3 months post-extraction (T1) in 45 systemically healthy controls and 41 T2DM patients (HbA1c 6.5–11%, diagnosed ≥3 yr).

T2DM patients had significantly higher OPG, RANKL, TNF-α, MMP-9, IL-18 and TOS than controls at both time points, while OPG/RANKL ratio and HGF were higher in controls. Three months after extraction, OPG, OPG/RANKL ratio and TAC rose while RANKL, MMP-9, IL-18 and TOS fell in both groups; TNF-α decreased significantly only in the diabetes group, accompanied by a significant fall in mean HbA1c (7.98% → 7.62%, p=0.05) and a drop in uncontrolled DM from 22.0% to 2.4%. At follow-up in the DM group, HbA1c correlated positively and strongly with TOS (r=+0.777) and negatively/moderately with TAC (r=−0.475). On ROC analysis, baseline RANKL (AUC 0.999), TNF-α (0.907), IL-18 (0.901), MMP-9 (0.899), TOS (0.876) and OPG (0.833) were good predictors of HbA1c >6.5%. The authors conclude salivary oxidative and proinflammatory biomarkers track glycemic control and may serve as non-invasive indicators of post-extraction healing status in diabetic patients.

## Key Contributions

- Links a **salivary biomarker panel to HbA1c**, supporting saliva as a non-invasive surrogate for monitoring glycemic control and post-extraction healing in T2DM.
- Provides **ROC cut-offs** for dysglycemia (HbA1c >6.5%): RANKL 15, OPG 2.77 ng/mL, TNF-α 6.02 pg/mL, MMP-9 120 ng/mL, IL-18 198 pg/mL, TOS 17.5 µmol H2O2 Equiv/L.
- Characterizes the **3-month post-extraction trajectory** of bone, inflammatory and oxidative markers in diabetic vs. healthy patients.
- Reports oxidative-stress markers (TOS↑ positive, TAC↓ negative) as the markers most tightly correlated with HbA1c after extraction.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Case-control study, paired T0 (pre-treatment) and T1 (3-month post-extraction) sampling.
- **Subjects**: 86 completers — control (n=45, no systemic disease) and T2DM (n=41, ADA diagnosis ≥3 yr, HbA1c 6.5–11%). Smokers, BMI>30, periodontitis stage 3–4, recent antibiotics/anti-inflammatories, and other bone-affecting systemic diseases excluded.
- **Samples**: ~3 mL unstimulated whole saliva (passive drooling, fasting, 8–10 am) + same-morning venous blood for HbA1c; stored −80 °C.
- **Assays**: ELISA (OPG, RANKL, HGF, TNF-α, MMP-9, IL-18); spectrophotometry (TAC, TOS by Erel 2005 method).
- **Statistics**: SPSS v18; unpaired t-test (between-group), paired t-test (intragroup), chi-square/Fisher, ROC (AUC/Se/Sp/cut-off), linear regression for HbA1c determinism. G*power sized on TNF-α (38/group min).

## Results

| Marker | Control T0→T1 | DM T0→T1 | DM vs C | ROC AUC (HbA1c>6.5%) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| OPG | 2.08 → 6.73 ↑ | 3.79 → 7.58 ↑ | DM higher | 0.833 |
| RANKL | 8.02 → 3.02 ↓ | 36.70 → 31.70 ↓ | DM higher | 0.999 |
| OPG/RANKL | 0.29 → 5.47 ↑ | 0.12 → 0.29 ↑ | C higher | 0.204 |
| HGF | 211.8 → 874.9 ↑ | 13.3 → 26.4 (ns) | C higher | low |
| TNF-α | 4.81 → 4.57 (ns) | 9.69 → 8.28 ↓* | DM higher | 0.907 |
| MMP-9 | 91.4 → 70.8 ↓ | 160.9 → 137.6 ↓ | DM higher | 0.899 |
| IL-18 | 223 → 147 ↓ | 480 → 367 ↓ | DM higher | 0.901 |
| TOS | 15.4 → 13.5 ↓ | 18.9 → 17.4 ↓ | DM higher | 0.876 |
| TAC | 0.83 → 1.12 ↑ | 0.73 → 1.12 (ns) | C higher (T0) | 0.339 |
| HbA1c | 5.01 → 5.00 (ns) | 7.98 → 7.62 ↓* | DM higher | — |

\* statistically significant only in DM group.

- **Post-extraction HbA1c correlations (DM, T1)**: TOS r=+0.777 (p=0.001), TAC r=−0.475 (p=0.002); OPG/HGF/TNF-α weak ns; RANKL/MMP-9/IL-18 independent.
- **HbA1c fell significantly** in DM after extraction; uncontrolled DM 22.0% → 2.4%.
- Linear regression: all studied salivary markers contributed to HbA1c determinism in considerable proportion.

**Clinical takeaway**: In T2DM patients, non-invasive saliva sampling can flag dysglycemia (RANKL, TNF-α, IL-18, MMP-9, TOS, OPG as predictors) and track oxidative/inflammatory burden during post-extraction healing. Causality is not established — the post-extraction HbA1c decline is associative and confounded by improved oral care during follow-up.
