## One-line Summary
SR+MA (89 studies, 5,510 diabetic vs 62,780 non-diabetic implants): diabetic implant failure OR 1.777 (p<0.001); T1DM > T2DM (OR 4.477); MBL difference +0.776 mm; significant difference in the maxilla only.

## Summary
Largest meta-analysis on DM and dental implants (89 publications, 68,290 implants total). Diabetic patients have **77.7% higher implant failure risk** (OR 1.777, p<0.001). Type 1 DM carries a significantly greater risk than Type 2 (OR 4.477). MBL is 0.776 mm greater in DM patients and widens over time.

## Meta-Analysis Results

| Outcome | DM vs Non-DM | p-value |
|---|---|---|
| Implant failure (OR) | **1.777** | <0.001 |
| T1DM vs T2DM failure | OR **4.477** | 0.032 |
| MBL difference | **+0.776 mm** | 0.027 |
| MBL gap per additional month | +0.032 mm/mo | <0.001 |
| Maxilla failure difference | Significant | — |
| Mandible failure difference | **Not significant** | — |

## Key Interpretation
- **Mandible**: DM patients do NOT show significantly higher failure → posterior mandible implants relatively safe even in DM
- **Maxilla**: DM significantly increases failure → extra caution in upper jaw
- MBL diverges over time → long-term monitoring more critical in DM patients
- OR decreases slightly with longer follow-up → surviving implants in DM eventually become stable

## Clinical Takeaway
- Maxillary implants in DM: higher risk → stricter HbA1c control, longer monitoring
- MBL monitoring mandatory at every recall in DM patients (gap widens over time)
- T1DM: significantly more risky than T2DM → more conservative approach warranted
