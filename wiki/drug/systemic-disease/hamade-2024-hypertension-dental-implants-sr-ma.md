## One-line Summary

SR+MA (24 studies, 21,066 implants) finding that hypertensive patients show no higher dental-implant failure odds than normotensive patients (OR 1.100, 95% CI 0.709-1.707, p=0.671).

## 한줄요약

24편·임플란트 21,066개 SR+MA — 고혈압 환자의 임플란트 실패 위험은 정상혈압 환자와 차이 없음 (OR 1.100, 95% CI 0.709-1.707, p=0.671).

## Summary

This systematic review and meta-analysis (Hamadé, El-Disoki & Chrcanovic, Malmö University, 2024) tested whether hypertension raises the dental-implant failure rate. Searching four databases plus manual journal/reference checking, 24 clinical studies (published 2005-2021) were included. Despite known mechanistic concerns — hypertension elevates PTH and RANKL (driving osteoclastogenesis/bone resorption) and causes microvascular rarefaction that could impair osseointegration — the pooled data showed **no significant difference**. Hypertensive patients had 257 failures among 4874 implants vs 809 failures among 16,192 implants in normotensive patients, giving a non-significant pooled OR of 1.100 (95% CI 0.709-1.707, p=0.671). The effect was stable across follow-up time (meta-regression p=0.824) and a funnel plot showed no clear publication bias. The authors caution that the result is limited by poor reporting of hypertension control status (antihypertensive medication use), confounders (diabetes, smoking, bruxism, bisphosphonates, etc.), and predominantly retrospective designs.

## Key Contributions

- Largest direct SR+MA on hypertension and implant failure, expanding beyond a prior 3-study SR restricted to medication-type reporting.
- Clinically reassuring null result: controlled-or-not, hypertension by itself does not appear to increase implant failure odds.
- Pooled across 21,066 implants (4874 hypertensive / 16,192 normotensive) from 24 "good"-quality studies.

## Methodology

- PRISMA 2020; PROSPERO CRD42023487489. PICO: hypertensive vs normotensive implant patients; outcome = implant failure (unit = implant).
- Databases: PubMed/Medline, Web of Science, Scopus, Ebsco + manual search; no time restriction (Aug 2022, updated Oct 2023).
- Hypertension defined as BP >= 130/80 mm Hg; normotension < 120/80 mm Hg. Failure = removal (early/late) or fracture. Only modern cylindrical screw-type Ti implants.
- Quality: NIH tool, all 24 studies "good" (>= 7 pts).
- Random-effects inverse-variance model (I2 = 72.96%, p<0.001); OR; meta-regression on follow-up; funnel plot. Software: OpenMeta[Analyst], OpenMEE.

## Results

| Outcome | Value |
|---|---|
| Studies | 24 (8 prospective, 16 retrospective) |
| Implants — hypertensive | 4874 (257 failures) |
| Implants — normotensive | 16,192 (809 failures) |
| Pooled failure OR | 1.100 (95% CI 0.709-1.707), p=0.671 — not significant |
| Heterogeneity | I2 = 72.96%, p<0.001 |
| Follow-up effect (meta-reg) | p=0.824 (none) |
| Mean follow-up (16 studies) | 30.0 ± 21.6 months |
| Publication bias | none apparent (funnel plot) |

Limitations: poor characterization of hypertension control / antihypertensive medication, many confounders (diabetes, smoking, bruxism, RT/CT, SSRIs, PPIs, bisphosphonates), retrospective designs, small samples, short follow-up.
