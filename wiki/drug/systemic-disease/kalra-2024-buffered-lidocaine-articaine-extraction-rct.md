## One-line Summary

Prospective 4-arm RCT (n=28, 7/group), mandibular extraction IANB: chair-side 8.4% NaHCO3 buffering significantly sped onset of every milestone for both 2% lidocaine and 4% articaine (buffered articaine fastest — objective onset 84 s vs 181 s non-buffered lidocaine, p<0.001), but extraction VAS did not differ (χ²=4.107, p=0.250).

## 한줄요약

전향적 4-군 RCT (n=28, 군당 7명), 하악 발치 IANB: chair-side 8.4% 중탄산나트륨 buffering이 2% 리도카인·4% 아티카인 모두에서 마취 발현을 유의하게 단축(buffered articaine 최속 — 객관적 발현 84초 vs non-buffered lidocaine 181초, p<0.001), 단 발치 중 VAS는 차이 없음(χ²=4.107, p=0.250).

## Summary

This Indian single-center prospective RCT is the first to compare all four combinations of buffering state (buffered vs non-buffered) and drug (2% lidocaine vs 4% articaine), each with 1:100,000 adrenaline, for inferior alveolar nerve block (IANB) in mandibular extraction. Buffering was done chair-side by replacing 0.2 mL of a 2 mL cartridge with 0.2 mL 8.4% sodium bicarbonate. The clinical question: does alkalinizing the cartridge to ~physiological pH accelerate onset, and how does that interact with choosing the faster-diffusing articaine?

The answer is a clean directional ordering: for the **same drug**, buffered onsets faster than non-buffered; for the **same buffering state**, articaine onsets faster than lidocaine. Buffered articaine was fastest on all four milestones; non-buffered lidocaine slowest. However, this onset advantage did **not** translate into less pain during the extraction itself — VAS during the procedure was statistically indistinguishable across the four groups. So the takeaway is "faster to numb," not "less painful surgery."

The trial is small (7 per arm), so many pairwise post-hoc comparisons were non-significant; the abstract's blanket claim that buffered is "significantly better" overstates the per-pair evidence. The robust signal is the onset acceleration of buffered articaine versus non-buffered lidocaine.

## Key Contributions

1. First simultaneous **4-arm** comparison of buffered/non-buffered × lidocaine/articaine for mandibular IANB extraction.
2. Quantified **onset across four sequential milestones** (first symptom → lip numbness → subjective → objective) in seconds.
3. Showed a **drug × buffering interaction**: both levers shorten onset and stack additively (buffered articaine fastest).
4. Separated the **onset benefit from intraoperative-pain benefit** — VAS during extraction was NS, so faster onset ≠ less surgical pain here.

## Methodology

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Design | Prospective RCT, single OMFS department, 4 parallel arms |
| n | 28 (7/group) |
| Groups | (1) non-buffered 2% lido/1:100k; (2) non-buffered 4% articaine/1:100k; (3) buffered 2% lido; (4) buffered 4% articaine |
| Buffering | 0.2 mL of 2 mL cartridge replaced with 0.2 mL 8.4% NaHCO3, inverted 5× |
| Technique | Single operator, IANB, 27-G 1.5-inch needle, aspirating syringe |
| Outcomes | Onset (s) of first symptom / lip numbness / subjective / objective; VAS during extraction |
| Exclusions | <18 y, significant medical history, pregnancy, LA/sulfite allergy, interfering meds |
| Statistics | One-way ANOVA + Tukey (onset); Chi-square (VAS); α=0.05; SPSS v16 |

## Results

[근거강함] Mean onset (seconds):

| Group | First symptom | Lip numbness | Subjective | Objective |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Non-buffered lidocaine | 58.71 | 78.71 | 128.00 | 181.00 |
| Non-buffered articaine | 36.14 | 70.00 | 97.00 | 136.28 |
| Buffered lidocaine | 30.57 | 51.85 | 82.72 | 100.00 |
| Buffered articaine | 16.85 | 38.57 | 67.42 | 84.14 |

[근거강함] Omnibus p-values: first symptom p<0.001; lip numbness p=0.003; subjective p=0.014; objective p=0.009. Direction consistent: buffered < non-buffered (same drug); articaine < lidocaine (same buffering).

[근거강함] **VAS during extraction**: χ²=4.107, **p=0.250 — no difference across the four groups.**

[claude해석] Most individual Tukey pairwise contrasts were NOT significant (only ~4 pairs per milestone reached p<0.05), consistent with n=7/arm underpowering. The reliable conclusion is the extreme contrast (buffered articaine vs non-buffered lidocaine), not every pairwise step.

[claude해석] Because 2% lidocaine and 4% articaine differ in both concentration and molecule, "articaine fastest" confounds drug and concentration; only the within-drug buffering effect is cleanly attributable.

## Clinical Implications for Korean Practice

[claude해석] For routine 하악 발치 IANB, chair-side buffering of the Korean-standard 2% lidocaine/1:100,000 cartridge is a low-cost way to shorten time-to-block (objective onset ~100 s buffered vs ~181 s non-buffered). The benefit is "start sooner," not "less pain during surgery" — this trial found no extraction-VAS advantage.

[추정] If using 4% articaine, buffering further trims onset, but buffered solutions precipitate if held too long, so prepare immediately before use.
