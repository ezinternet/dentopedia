## 한줄요약
비외과적 근관치료 후 NSAIDs·코르티코스테로이드가 8~72시간 전 시점에서 통증 유의 감소; 효과는 시간 경과에 따라 감소 (16 RCT, n=2,021, network MA, IJPPP 2025).

## Summary
Network meta-analysis of 16 RCTs (n=2,021) comparing medications for post-operative endodontic pain (PEP) after non-surgical root canal treatment. NSAIDs and corticosteroids showed statistically significant pain reduction at all assessed timepoints (8, 12, 24, 48, 72 hours). Effect sizes diminished over time, with the largest effect at 8 hours (pooled effect −3.10, I²=100%) and smallest at 72 hours (−0.64, I²=73.1%). Funnel plot was symmetrical (low publication bias). High heterogeneity limits direct clinical translation of pooled estimates.

## Key Contributions
- First network MA to systematically compare medications at 5 timepoints across 8–72 h post-NSRCT
- Confirms NSAIDs + corticosteroids as statistically effective across all timepoints
- Effect magnitude declines predictably: 8h > 12h > 24h > 48h > 72h
- Symmetrical funnel plot → publication bias unlikely
- High I² (up to 100%) at early timepoints: pooled estimates must be interpreted cautiously

## Methodology
Network meta-analysis. Databases: Scopus, PubMed, Google Scholar (searched to February 2024). Inclusion: RCTs on NSRCT with oral medication interventions. 16 RCTs, 2,021 participants. Random-effects model; I² heterogeneity; funnel plot; sensitivity analysis (72 h: 1 article excluded).

## Results
| Timepoint | Pooled Effect | I² (%) |
|---|---|---|
| 8 h | −3.10 | 100 |
| 12 h | −1.69 | 99.2 |
| 24 h | −1.48 | 99.9 |
| 48 h | −1.42 | 99.4 |
| 72 h | −0.64 | 73.1 |
