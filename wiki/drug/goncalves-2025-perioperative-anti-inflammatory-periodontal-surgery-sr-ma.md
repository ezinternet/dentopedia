## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 6 RCTs (n=262) in periodontal surgery: NSAIDs outperform corticosteroids (SAIDs) for early pain control (0–6 h, MD 1.00–2.23), SAIDs outperform NSAIDs at 48 h (MD −0.23), swelling and rescue medication comparable — moderate-to-low GRADE certainty.

## Summary

This 2025 PRISMA-guided SR+MA (Goncalves et al., JDR Clinical & Translational Research) evaluated whether perioperative steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (SAIDs, i.e., corticosteroids) or nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) provide better postoperative recovery after periodontal surgery. Of 2,354 screened articles, 6 RCTs with 262 adult participants were included. Random-effects meta-analysis with GRADE evidence quality assessment was used.

Key finding: drug efficacy is **time-dependent**. NSAIDs are superior for early pain relief (0–6 h postoperatively), while SAIDs (corticosteroids) provide superior pain control at 48 h. For swelling reduction and rescue analgesic consumption, both drug classes perform comparably. The authors conclude that evidence quality is moderate-to-low and call for individualized perioperative protocols based on surgical complexity and wound-healing requirements.

## Key Contributions

1. **Time-dependent pain reversal**: NSAIDs > SAIDs at 0–6 h; SAIDs > NSAIDs at 48 h — a clinically actionable distinction for perioperative planning.
2. **Swelling and rescue medication**: No significant difference between drug classes — neither drug type has a clear advantage on these outcomes.
3. **First periodontal-surgery-specific SR+MA** on this comparison (prior MA literature focused primarily on third-molar extractions).
4. GRADE assessment reveals moderate-to-low certainty — limits definitive protocol recommendations.
5. Highlights substantial **heterogeneity** in drug agents and dosing regimens, complicating pooled inferences.

## Methodology

- **Study type**: SR+MA (PRISMA)
- **Search**: Multiple databases, up to April 2025
- **Included studies**: 6 RCTs; 262 participants total
- **Population**: Adults undergoing periodontal surgeries
- **Interventions**: Perioperative NSAIDs vs perioperative SAIDs (corticosteroids)
- **Primary outcome**: Postoperative pain (VAS)
- **Secondary outcomes**: Swelling, rescue medication consumption
- **Statistical model**: Random-effects model
- **Evidence quality**: GRADE system

## Results

| Outcome | Timepoint | Favors | MD (95% CI) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pain (VAS) | 0–6 h | NSAIDs | MD 1.00–2.23 (95% CI: 0.21, 3.77) |
| Pain (VAS) | 48 h | SAIDs | MD −0.23 (95% CI: −0.31, −0.14) |
| Swelling | Various | Neither | No significant difference |
| Rescue medication | Various | Neither | No significant difference |

**GRADE certainty**: Moderate-to-low for all outcomes.

**Clinical note**: The statistically significant 48-h pain advantage of SAIDs (MD −0.23 on VAS) is of uncertain **clinical** significance given the small absolute difference and low certainty evidence.
