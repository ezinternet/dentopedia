## 한줄요약
다기관 RCT(n=100, 8주): SRP에 비타민C/E + 라이소자임 + 카르바조크롬(CELC, IGATAN F) 보조투여 시 위약 대비 치은지수(GI) 4주 시점 0.18 vs +0.01 (p=0.015), GEE OR=2.46 (p=0.022); PD·CAL·PI 유의차 없음 — 임상적 효능 한계 명확.

## Summary
This multicenter, double-blind, placebo-controlled randomized clinical trial (RCT) evaluated whether an 8-week fixed-dose combination of vitamin C (150 mg) + vitamin E (10 mg) + lysozyme (30 mg) + carbazochrome (2 mg) — abbreviated CELC and marketed in Korea as IGATAN F (Myung-In Pharmaceutical) — provides additional benefit beyond scaling and root planing (SRP) in chronic periodontitis. 100 patients across Kyung Hee, Yonsei, and Dankook University dental hospitals were enrolled (2014–2015), with 93 completing the study. The primary outcome was reduction of gingival index (GI) at 4 weeks; secondary outcomes were plaque index (PI), probing depth (PD), clinical attachment level (CAL), and 100 mm VAS at 4 and 8 weeks. CELC reduced GI significantly more than placebo at 4 weeks (Δ −0.18 ± 0.33 vs +0.01 ± 0.38, p = 0.015) and the GEE-adjusted odds ratio for GI improvement was 2.46 (p = 0.022). However, no significant between-group differences were observed for PI, PD, or CAL.

## Key Contributions
- First published RCT (and only multicenter trial) of the CELC fixed-dose combination as an adjunct to SRP in chronic periodontitis.
- Establishes a small but statistically significant effect on gingival inflammation (a surface-level outcome) — supports the host-modulation/antioxidant hypothesis.
- Provides safety data: no specific treatment-emergent adverse events (TEAEs) during 8 weeks.
- Failed to show clinical effect on harder endpoints (PD, CAL), tempering inflated marketing claims about disease-modifying activity.

## Methodology
- Design: 3-center, double-blind, parallel RCT with 1:1 randomization stratified by center (block randomization via SPSS 12.0, off-site)
- Pre-randomization run-in: 4-week SRP + oral hygiene instruction for all subjects
- Intervention: CELC (test) for 8 weeks vs placebo (anhydrous dibasic calcium phosphate, matched shape/color) for first 4 weeks then crossover to CELC for last 4 weeks (control)
- Outcomes measured on Ramfjord teeth (#16, #21, #24, #36, #41, #44):
  - Primary: ΔGI baseline → 4 weeks (Löe & Silness 1963)
  - Secondary: GI 8 wk, PI, PD, CAL, 100 mm VAS at 4 & 8 wk
- n = 100 enrolled (test 50 / control 50); FAS n = 97; PP n = 93 (test 48, control 45)
- Statistics: repeated-measures ANOVA between groups; Wilcoxon signed-rank within-group; Mann-Whitney U between-group at single time points; GEE with age/gender/visit/group as covariates; α = 0.05
- Funding: Myung-In Pharmaceutical Co. Ltd. (manufacturer of IGATAN F); registered KCT0001366 (retrospective, 29 Jan 2015)

## Results
| Parameter | Control Δbaseline–4wk | Test Δbaseline–4wk | Between-group p |
|---|---|---|---|
| GI | +0.01 ± 0.38 | −0.18 ± 0.33 | **0.015** |
| PI | −0.05 ± 0.40 | −0.06 ± 0.48 | 0.138 (8wk RM-ANOVA) |
| PD (mm) | −0.02 ± 0.25 | −0.11 ± 0.29 | 0.381 |
| CAL (mm) | +0.01 ± 0.31 | −0.07 ± 0.42 | 0.571 |
| 100 mm VAS | −3.96 ± 21.45 | −7.29 ± 17.55 | 0.059 |

- GEE odds ratio for GI improvement (test vs control, adjusted for age, gender, visit) = 2.457 (95% CI not explicitly tabulated; p = 0.022)
- Within-group: test group GI decreased significantly at 4 wk (p < 0.001) and 8 wk (p < 0.001); control group GI did not change significantly at either time point
- Test group PD within-group decrease was statistically significant at 4 wk (p = 0.022) and 8 wk (p = 0.018) but absolute reduction was only ~0.12 mm and not significant between groups
- 100 mm VAS at 8 wk: control 12.64 vs test 4.94 mm (p = 0.027 by Mann-Whitney U) — i.e. patient-reported discomfort lower in CELC; not robust in GEE

## Clinical Implications
1. **Effect size is modest and largely confined to soft-tissue inflammation.** CELC adjunct produces a real but small reduction in gingival index (~0.2 points on 0–3 scale, vs no change on placebo) over 4 weeks beyond SRP. This is consistent with a mild antioxidant / anti-inflammatory effect.
2. **No demonstrated change in PD or CAL.** The drug does not modify the disease at the level of attachment loss or pocket depth within 8 weeks. Patients/clinicians expecting bone regeneration or pocket closure from CELC have no basis in this trial.
3. **Run-in design means CELC is being tested as add-on to optimal SRP**, not as a replacement. The clinical question is "does CELC add anything to SRP + OHI?" and the answer is: a small improvement on gingivitis indices, but not on the harder periodontal endpoints.
4. **Safety profile appears favorable** over 8 weeks with no specific TEAEs at this fixed-dose combination — useful for patients who specifically ask about over-the-counter Korean periodontal "잇몸약."
5. **Generalizability**: Korean adults, incipient-to-moderate periodontitis (PD 4–6 mm in at least one site). Severe periodontitis, smokers (excluded ≥1 cigarette/day in spirit; 0% current smokers in either arm), and anticoagulated patients were excluded.
6. **Patient counseling**: framing IGATAN F as a mild adjunct that can reduce gingival bleeding/redness symptoms — not as a substitute for SRP and not as a cure — aligns with the evidence.
7. **Cost-benefit conversation**: given small effect on the surrogate marker (GI) and no effect on PD/CAL, patients may legitimately question whether the cost is justified. The trial does not answer this question.
