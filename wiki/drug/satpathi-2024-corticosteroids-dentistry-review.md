## Summary
Narrative review of corticosteroid use across dental specialties. Key applications: dexamethasone for post-surgical edema, topical triamcinolone for aphthous ulcers and OLP, systemic prednisolone for severe OLP/pemphigus. Steroid-dependent patients require adrenal crisis prevention protocol.

## Corticosteroid Quick Reference

| Drug | Route | Potency | Primary Dental Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Dexamethasone** | PO/IV/IM | 25× | Post-surgical edema (4–8mg before/after extraction) |
| **Triamcinolone acetonide** | Topical paste | 5× | Aphthous ulcers, OLP (Kenalog in Orabase) |
| **Clobetasol propionate** | Topical | 500× | Severe/erosive OLP; recalcitrant lesions |
| Prednisolone | PO | 4× | Systemic OLP, pemphigus, severe allergic reaction |
| Methylprednisolone | PO/IV | 5× | Post-surgical protocol |
| Hydrocortisone | IV | 1× (reference) | Adrenal crisis emergency |

## Clinical Applications by Indication

| Indication | Protocol |
|---|---|
| **Post-extraction edema** | Dexamethasone 4–8mg PO/IM 1h before + 8h after extraction |
| **Aphthous ulcers** | Triamcinolone acetonide 0.1% paste (Kenalog) qid until healing |
| **Oral Lichen Planus** | Topical clobetasol 0.05% gel qid; systemic prednisolone 40–60mg/day tapering for severe |
| **Allergic reaction (mild)** | Prednisolone 40mg PO; antihistamine |
| **Anaphylaxis** | Epinephrine 1st line → IV hydrocortisone 200mg |
| **TMJ injection** | Triamcinolone 10–20mg intra-articular |
| **Pericoronitis** | Dexamethasone 4mg + antibiotics (short course) |

## Steroid-Dependent Patient Protocol
Patients on long-term systemic corticosteroids (≥5mg prednisolone/day >2 weeks):
- HPA axis suppression → cannot mount cortisol response to surgical stress
- **Stress protocol for major dental surgery**:
  - Hydrocortisone 25–50mg IV/IM immediately before procedure
  - OR double normal steroid dose on day of procedure
- Minor procedures (scaling, simple restoration): no supplementation typically needed
- Signs of adrenal crisis: hypotension, tachycardia, confusion → IV hydrocortisone 100mg immediately

## Cautions
- Short single-dose use (dexamethasone 8mg): minimal systemic risk
- Long-term topical steroids: oral candidiasis risk → concurrent antifungal (nystatin/miconazole)
- Overcautious prescribing concern: do NOT use systemic corticosteroids for minor extractions without indication
