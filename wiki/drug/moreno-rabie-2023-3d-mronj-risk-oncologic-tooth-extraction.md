## One-line Summary

Retrospective case–control (n = 47 oncologic ARD patients / 87 extractions vs 50 controls / 106 extractions): pre-operative CBCT showed ARD sites had more thickened lamina dura, widened PDL, osteosclerosis, osteolysis and sequestrum; MRONJ (32% of patients, 38% of sites) was driven by multiple extractions, smoking, shorter drug holiday and younger age — periosteal reaction and sequestrum proposed as pre-clinical MRONJ markers.

## 한줄요약

후향적 환자–대조군 연구(항흡수제 투여 암환자 47명·발치 87건 vs 대조 50명·발치 106건): 발치 전 CBCT에서 항흡수제군은 치조백선 비후·치주인대강 확장·골경화·골용해·격리골이 더 많았고, MRONJ(환자 32%·부위 38%)는 다수치 동시발치·흡연·짧은 휴약기간·젊은 나이와 연관 — 골막반응과 격리골은 임상적 골노출 전 단계(pre-clinical MRONJ) 표지자로 제안됨.

## Summary

This single-center retrospective, longitudinal case–control study (UZ/KU Leuven, 2010–2020) asked whether **pre-operative CBCT** can flag oncologic patients at risk of MRONJ before extraction. From 525 ARD-treated patients screened, 47 oncologic patients (87 extractions) with a pre-extraction CBCT formed the study group, matched against 50 controls (106 extractions, no ARD). Three blinded calibrated oral radiologists scored each socket for bony and dental features. The central message: ARD use leaves a detectable **3D radiographic fingerprint before clinical bone exposure**, and two features — **periosteal reaction and sequestrum formation** — are candidate **pre-clinical (latent) MRONJ lesions**. This shifts MRONJ imaging from a reactive (after bone is exposed) to a potentially predictive role, but the evidence remains univariable and unconfirmed histologically.

## Key Contributions

- Moves MRONJ risk imaging from panoramic 2D to **3D CBCT**, capturing buccolingual detail and avoiding overlap/magnification.
- Defines a CBCT signature separating ARD vs non-ARD sockets: thickened lamina dura, widened PDL space, osteosclerosis, osteolysis, sequestrum (the last **exclusive to the ARD group**).
- Names **periosteal reaction (p = 0.051, borderline) and sequestrum** as suspected pre-clinical MRONJ markers.
- Shows a **drug-type radiographic divergence**: bisphosphonate → significantly more osteosclerosis (extensive 74% vs 45%, p = 0.003); denosumab → no early radiographic signal, implying risk of late detection.

## Methodology

- **Design**: retrospective longitudinal case–control; STROBE-reported; ethics S63934.
- **Groups**: study = 47 oncologic patients / 87 extractions (oncologic-dose zoledronic acid 4 mg, denosumab 120 mg, pamidronate 90 mg, alendronate, ibandronate); control = 50 patients / 106 extractions, age/gender/tooth matched, no ARD. BP-only 21, Dmab-only 24, both 2.
- **Imaging**: 3D Accuitomo 170 / Newtom VGi evo; voxel 80–300 µm; read in IMPAX by 3 blinded calibrated readers (outcome = mode of ≥2).
- **Scored per site**: alveolar bone loss, furcation, lamina dura, PDL space, endodontic-treatment quality, periapical lesion (size/cortical involvement), root remnant, trabecular pattern (osteosclerosis / osteolysis / periosteal reaction / sequestrum), mandibular cortical width.
- **Stats**: χ²/Fisher's exact + Mann–Whitney U; Fleiss'/Cohen's κ for agreement. p ≤ 0.05.

## Results

- **MRONJ**: 15/47 patients (32%); 33/87 sites (38%). Site stage: 1 = 63.6%, 2 = 30.3%, 3 = 6.1%.
- **ARD vs control sites** (significantly more in ARD): thickened lamina dura, widened PDL, osteosclerosis, osteolysis, sequestrum (sequestrum only in ARD group).
- **Clinical predictors of MRONJ** (study group):
  - Multiple simultaneous extractions — mean 3 (MRONJ+) vs 1 (MRONJ−), p = 0.006
  - Current smoking — 5/5 current smokers developed MRONJ, p = 0.004
  - Shorter drug holiday — 7.8 vs 27.5 mo, p = 0.021 (denosumab subgroup 1.9 vs 11 mo, p = 0.041; BP subgroup p = 0.094 NS)
  - Younger age — 64 vs 71 yr, p = 0.025
  - Cancer type, ARD type, treatment length, anti-angiogenics, hormone therapy: NOT associated.
- **Radiographic predictors of bone exposure**: periosteal reaction p = 0.051 (borderline); all periosteal-reaction & sequestrum sites also had caries/periodontitis/periapical pathology with symptomatic teeth.
- **BP vs Dmab (42 vs 42 extractions)**: only osteosclerosis differed — BP extensive 74% vs Dmab 45% (p = 0.003); lamina dura, PDL, osteolysis, periosteal reaction, sequestrum all NS.
- **Healing**: control 2.4 wk vs study 14.2 wk to mucosal closure (p < 0.001); MRONJ+ sites 42.8 wk.
- **Agreement**: interobserver κ = 0.69 (substantial), intraobserver κ = 0.87.

**Clinical takeaway**: In oncologic ARD patients facing extraction, a pre-operative CBCT can reveal ARD-induced bone change and possible latent lesions (periosteal reaction, sequestrum). Limit simultaneous extractions, address smoking, and weigh a longer drug holiday — especially for denosumab, whose short half-life makes a very recent dose (mean 1.9 mo before extraction in MRONJ+ cases) the dominant risk, and which gives no early radiographic warning.
