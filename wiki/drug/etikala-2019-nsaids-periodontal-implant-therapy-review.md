## One-line Summary
Narrative review (9 periodontal studies, 6 implant studies): NSAIDs produce conflicting results for periodontal wound healing; selective COX-2 inhibitors may inhibit peri-implant bone formation; very limited human clinical evidence — clinicians prescribing NSAIDs after dental surgery should be aware of potential impact on osseous healing (Compend 2019).

## Summary
Narrative literature review addressing two clinical questions: (1) how do NSAIDs affect periodontal wound healing? (2) do NSAIDs affect osseointegration of dental implants? 9 clinical studies on periodontal healing showed conflicting results — no clear conclusion. For dental implant osseointegration, 4 animal studies and 2 human clinical studies were reviewed; selective COX-2 inhibitors specifically inhibited bone formation around orthopedic implants in animals. Conclusion (within limitations): NSAIDs negatively affect titanium implant osseointegration, but human evidence quality is poor and results conflicting. Dental clinicians must be aware of potential NSAID effects on osseous healing following periodontal and implant procedures.

## Key Contributions
- COX-2 selective inhibitors are specifically implicated in bone formation inhibition — not all NSAIDs equally
- Periodontal healing: NSAIDs produce conflicting results; not established as harmful or helpful
- Implant osseointegration: the concern is real in animal models; human data insufficient
- Raises key clinical question: should ibuprofen (non-selective COX inhibitor) be the default post-implant analgesic?
- Practical implication: consider APAP-first approach or minimize NSAID duration in fresh implant sites

## Methodology
Narrative review, PubMed MEDLINE search. 9 clinical studies (periodontal) + 4 animal + 2 human clinical studies (implant). No systematic protocol or quality appraisal tool reported.

## Results
| Context | Evidence | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Periodontal wound healing | 9 clinical studies | Conflicting — no conclusion |
| Implant osseointegration (animal) | 4 studies (COX-2 inhibitors) | Inhibit bone formation |
| Implant osseointegration (human) | 2 clinical studies | Negative effect; low evidence quality |
