## One-line Summary
International multicenter prospective cohort (n=507, T1DM 268/T2DM 159/normal 80): established the linear regression eAG (mg/dL) = 28.7 × A1C − 46.7 (R²=0.84), independent of age, sex, race, diabetes type, or smoking — enables conversion from A1C % to eAG for patient communication.

## Summary
The A1C-Derived Average Glucose (ADAG) study enrolled 507 adults across 10 international centers (US, Europe, Africa, Asia) and combined ~2,700 glucose values per subject from continuous glucose monitoring (CGM every 5 min), eight-point fingerstick calibration days, and seven-point self-monitoring (≥3 days/week) over 3 months. After excluding hemoglobinopathies, anemia, CKD, transfusion, and other RBC-altering conditions, linear regression yielded **eAG (mg/dL) = 28.7 × A1C − 46.7** with R² = 0.84 (p<0.0001). The slope and intercept did not differ significantly across age, sex, diabetes type, race/ethnicity, or smoking status, supporting universal application of the equation outside the excluded populations.

## Key Contributions
- **eAG equation as ADA/EASD reporting standard**: HbA1c can be reported alongside an average glucose value in the same mg/dL or mmol/L units that patients see on their glucometer, reducing the historical confusion of two unit systems.
- **Subgroup robustness**: equation holds across T1DM/T2DM, sex, race, and smoking — single equation applies broadly outside excluded RBC-confounder populations.
- **eAG reference values**: A1C 6%→126, 7%→154, 8%→183, 9%→212, 10%→240 mg/dL — direct patient education tool.

## Methodology
- **Subjects**: 507 adults (18–70y), stable A1C (two values within 1% in prior 6 months); 268 T1DM, 159 T2DM, 80 non-diabetic.
- **Centers**: 10 international (1 Asian center excluded due to sample storage failure).
- **Glycemia measurement**: 3 modalities combined and weighted:
  1. CGM (Medtronic Minimed CGMS) — 2+ days at baseline, every 4 weeks for 12 weeks.
  2. Eight-point HemoCue 201 Plus capillary calibration during CGM days.
  3. Seven-point OneTouch Ultra fingerstick ≥3 days/week throughout.
- **A1C measurement**: central lab, four DCCT-aligned assays (Tosoh G7 HPLC, Roche A1C, Roche Tina-quant, Primus Ultra-2 affinity); mean of four used; intra/inter-assay CV <2.5%.
- **Statistics**: linear regression (quadratic offered no improvement, p=0.82); heteroschedasticity correction; 90% prediction intervals.
- **Exclusions**: hemoglobinopathies, anemia (Hct <39% M / <36% F), high reticulocytosis, transfusion, CKD, liver disease, high-dose vitamin C, erythropoietin — all conditions altering RBC lifespan or A1C measurement.

## Results
- **Primary regression**: eAG (mg/dL) = 28.7 × A1C − 46.7; R² = 0.84; p<0.0001.
- **mmol/L equivalent**: eAG (mmol/L) = 1.59 × A1C − 2.59.
- **eAG reference table**:

  | A1C (%) | eAG (mg/dL) | eAG (mmol/L) | 95% CI (mg/dL) |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | 5 | 97 | 5.4 | 76–120 |
  | 6 | 126 | 7.0 | 100–152 |
  | 7 | 154 | 8.6 | 123–185 |
  | 8 | 183 | 10.2 | 147–217 |
  | 9 | 212 | 11.8 | 170–249 |
  | 10 | 240 | 13.4 | 193–282 |
  | 11 | 269 | 14.9 | 217–314 |
  | 12 | 298 | 16.5 | 240–347 |

- **Subgroup consistency**: no significant slope/intercept difference by age (<50 vs ≥50), sex, diabetes type, race/ethnicity, or smoking — supports universal application.

## Clinical Implications for Dentistry
- **Patient communication**: For diabetic patients before extraction/implant, eAG translation (e.g., "HbA1c 8% means your average blood sugar has been around 183 mg/dL for the past 2–3 months") communicates risk more concretely than the % unit.
- **Surgical risk stratification**: HbA1c-based thresholds — <7% (eAG <154) routine; 7–9% (eAG 154–212) heightened monitoring; ≥9% (eAG ≥212) defer elective surgery — link to peri-implant disease risk (see [[galarraga-vinueza-2025-peri-implant-disease-risk-factors]]) and implant failure (see [[al-ansari-2022-diabetes-mellitus-dental-implants-sr-ma]], [[wagner-2022-diabetes-mellitus-dental-implants-sr]]).
- **Caveat in special populations**: eAG equation not validated in patients excluded from ADAG (anemia, CKD, hemoglobinopathy, recent transfusion) — see [[chen-2022-interpretation-hba1c-analytical-methodology-hematology]] for alternative biomarker guidance.
