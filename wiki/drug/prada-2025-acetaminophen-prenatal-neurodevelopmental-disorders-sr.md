## Summary

Acetaminophen is the first-line analgesic/antipyretic recommended in pregnancy — including for dental pain ([[drug/zhou-2023-pregnancy-physiology-oral-local-anesthesia-review]], [[drug/aliabadi-2022-antibiotic-use-endodontic-treatment-pregnancy]]). This systematic review applied the **Navigation Guide** environmental-health methodology to 46 studies of prenatal acetaminophen exposure and offspring neurodevelopmental disorders (NDDs). **27 reported positive associations, 9 null, 4 negative (protective), and higher-quality studies were more likely positive.** The authors conclude the evidence is consistent with an association and recommend precautionary limitation of acetaminophen in pregnancy. **Clinical caveat (important): this is observational, association-not-causation evidence with major confounding by indication** (fever, infection, and pain are themselves linked to NDDs), a qualitative (vote-counting) synthesis, and an author group associated with this hypothesis; large sibling-controlled cohorts have found the association attenuates to null. **ACOG and FDA continue to consider acetaminophen the preferred agent in pregnancy.** For dental prescribing: acetaminophen remains first-line for the pregnant patient, but use the lowest effective dose for the shortest duration — consistent with general analgesic stewardship.

## Key Contributions

1. Comprehensive Navigation-Guide grading of the prenatal-acetaminophen–NDD literature (46 studies).
2. Quality-stratified direction-of-association tally showing a predominance of positive studies.
3. A precautionary "limit use" recommendation that reframes acetaminophen's blanket safe-in-pregnancy status.

## Methodology

- Systematic PubMed search through Feb 25, 2025; predefined inclusion criteria.
- Risk-of-bias and strength-of-evidence rating per Navigation Guide.
- Qualitative synthesis (no meta-analysis) due to heterogeneity → `sr` (no MA).

## Results

| Direction of association | Studies (of 46) |
|---|---|
| Positive (link to NDD) | 27 |
| Null (no link) | 9 |
| Negative (protective) | 4 |

Higher-quality studies → more often positive. Conclusion: evidence consistent with an association; advise limiting acetaminophen in pregnancy.
