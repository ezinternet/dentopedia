## 한줄요약
대규모 단일기관 후향 코호트 (Kaohsiung Chang Gung, Taiwan, n=1,331/1,816 extractions, 2018): warfarin 지속군 (INR<4.0) 발치 후 출혈률 9.1%; warfarin 중단+heparin bridging, 단일/이중 항혈소판 (DAPT) 지속 모두 국소 지혈로 통제 가능 — 발치 위해 항혈전제 중단 불필요라는 현대적 입장의 대만 임상 근거.

## Summary
Single-center retrospective cohort at Kaohsiung Chang Gung Memorial Hospital examining dental extraction outcomes in 1,331 Taiwanese patients across 1,816 extraction occasions stratified by antithrombotic regimen: 60 on warfarin (28 continued at INR<4.0 vs 32 switched to in-hospital heparin bridging), 183 on antiplatelet therapy (aspirin 125, clopidogrel 42, DAPT 16), and 1,088 controls without antithrombotic therapy.

The headline finding: post-extraction bleeding incidence in the warfarin-continued group was **9.1%**, all controllable with local hemostasis. The dataset supports the contemporary position — do not interrupt warfarin or antiplatelet therapy for routine dental extractions; manage bleeding locally.

## Key Contributions
- Real-world Asian-population data complementing Western SR-level evidence (Inchingolo 2024, Moldovan 2023).
- All five common antithrombotic regimens compared head-to-head in one institution: warfarin continued, warfarin stopped + heparin bridging, aspirin, clopidogrel, DAPT.
- Confirms that bridging anticoagulation (the higher-risk option for thromboembolism) is not necessary for dental extractions.

## Methodology
Retrospective chart review; outcomes: bleeding rate, INR levels, regimen continuation/discontinuation, number/type of extractions, demographic factors.

## Results
| Group (n patients / occasions) | Postoperative bleeding |
|---|---|
| Warfarin continued, INR<4.0 (28/33) | 9.1% |
| Warfarin stopped + heparin bridging (32/37) | data in paper |
| Aspirin (125/185) | low, locally controllable |
| Clopidogrel (42/65) | low, locally controllable |
| DAPT (16/24) | higher than monotherapy but controllable |
| Control no antithrombotic (1,088/1,472) | baseline |

[합의수준] Authors recommend continuation of warfarin (INR<4.0) and antiplatelet therapy through dental extractions.

## Limitations
- Single center, retrospective, no randomization
- 2018 Taiwan: predominantly warfarin era; no DOAC subgroup
- INR>4.0 not addressed — high-INR cases should not be extrapolated from this cohort
- Heparin bridging arm small; statistical comparison constrained
