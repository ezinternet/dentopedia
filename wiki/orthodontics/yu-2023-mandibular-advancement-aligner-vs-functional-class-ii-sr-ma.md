## One-line Summary

SR+MA (9 controlled trials, n=283): mandibular advancement (MA) clear aligners and traditional functional appliances give comparable skeletal/dental Class II correction (SNA/SNB/ANB/Go-Pog/U1-SN/overjet n.s.); aligners control lower-incisor proclination better (1.94° less) but produce less mandibular ramus growth (Co-Go 1.10 mm less) — a genuine limitation for growth-dependent correction.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.7518/hxkq.2023.2022453](https://doi.org/10.7518/hxkq.2023.2022453)), this Chinese-language SR+MA (West China J Stomatology) pools 9 clinical controlled trials (n=283) comparing **mandibular advancement (MA) clear aligners** against **traditional functional appliances** for skeletal Class II malocclusion, appraised with ROBINS-I (RevMan 5.4 / Stata 17.0). It sits in the clear-aligner *limited-indication* evidence base as the Class II growth-modification axis — complementing the distalization route covered by [[orthodontics/bhate-2025-cat-maxillary-molar-distalization-class-ii-sr]] and [[orthodontics/inchingolo-2023-clear-aligner-distalization-predictability-sr]].

On the core sagittal and dental outcomes — **SNA, SNB, ANB, mandibular body length (Go-Pog), upper-incisor inclination (U1-SN), and overjet — there was no statistically significant difference** between MA aligners and functional appliances. The two appliance types diverge on two measures that define the aligner trade-off: aligners **better controlled lower-incisor labial inclination (1.94° less proclination)** — a desirable anchorage effect — but the functional-appliance group achieved **1.10 mm more mandibular ramus growth (Co-Go)**, with only a 0.90° between-group difference in mandibular plane angle (MP-SN essentially preserved by aligners).

Clinically: for a growing skeletal Class II patient, an MA aligner can deliver overjet/ANB correction comparable to a conventional functional appliance while keeping lower incisors more upright and the mandibular plane stable — but it is **weaker at stimulating true ramus/condylar growth**, so the correction leans more dental/postural than skeletal. The authors recommend auxiliary measures to augment ramus growth when skeletal change is the goal. Evidence is limited by controlled (not all randomized) designs and modest sample size; this page is ingested abstract-only (PMC carries only the bilingual abstract, body not available).

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies the MA-aligner vs functional-appliance comparison for skeletal Class II with pooled cephalometric estimates.
- Isolates the aligner trade-off: superior lower-incisor inclination control vs inferior ramus growth.
- Adds the growth-modification axis to the clear-aligner limited-indication evidence base.

## Methodology

SR+MA. PubMed/WoS/Embase/Cochrane + four Chinese databases; PICOS selection by two reviewer groups; ROBINS-I quality appraisal; RevMan 5.4 / Stata 17.0. 9 clinical controlled trials, n=283. Confidence `sr+ma`; ingested abstract-only (Chinese article, PMC body empty).

## Results

- SNA, SNB, ANB, Go-Pog (mandibular body length), U1-SN, overjet: no significant CA-vs-functional difference.
- Mandibular plane angle (MP-SN): 0.90° between-group difference (aligners keep it essentially unchanged).
- Ramus growth (Co-Go): functional group +1.10 mm vs aligner group (favors functional).
- Lower-incisor labial inclination: aligner group 1.94° less proclined (favors aligner anchorage control).
