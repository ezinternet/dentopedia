## Summary
A systematic review and meta-analysis of 18 studies evaluating whether orthodontic miniscrews can be retrieved from patients, sterilized, and successfully reused. In vitro mechanical testing showed **no significant difference** in insertion torque, removal torque, or fracture torque between retrieved-and-sterilized vs new miniscrews. However, meta-analysis of clinical studies revealed that **reused miniscrews have a significantly higher failure rate** (RR 0.46, 95% CI 0.24–0.69). Sterilization alone increased insertion torque but did not compromise fracture resistance. Outcomes are influenced by sterilization method, insertion technique, and patient-specific anatomy.

## Key Contributions
- First SR+MA directly addressing clinical and mechanical outcomes of reused orthodontic miniscrews
- Separates the mechanical (in vitro) question from the clinical (failure rate) question
- Identifies that while mechanics are preserved, real-world clinical reuse carries higher failure risk
- Isolates sterilization-only effect (unused + sterilized screws) to distinguish sterilization effects from cumulative prior-use effects

## Methodology
- **Design**: SR + MA
- **Search**: MEDLINE, Web of Science, EMBASE, Scopus, Cochrane CENTRAL up to February 2024; grey literature; manual search orthodontic journals 2010–2023
- **Inclusion**: Studies on retrieved + sterilized miniscrews; in vitro and clinical trials; unused + sterilized included to isolate sterilization effects
- **Studies**: 18 total (13 retrieved+sterilized; 5 unused+sterilized)
- **Outcomes**: Insertion torque, removal torque, fracture torque (in vitro); clinical failure rate (clinical)
- **Statistics**: Random-effects meta-analysis; risk ratio for clinical data; mean difference for mechanical data

## Results
**In vitro (mechanical):**
| Outcome | Retrieved vs Unretrieved | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Insertion torque | No significant difference | — |
| Removal torque | No significant difference | — |
| Fracture torque | No significant difference | — |
| Insertion torque (sterilized unused) | Significant increase | p<0.05 |
| Fracture resistance (sterilized unused) | Not affected | — |

**Clinical:**
- Reused miniscrews: **Risk Ratio = 0.46 (95% CI 0.24–0.69)** — significantly higher failure rate on reuse
- Modifying factors: sterilization method, insertion technique, patient anatomy
