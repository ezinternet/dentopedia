## One-line Summary
Experimental study showing cortical (but not trabecular or total) bone mineral density correlates with insertion torque and primary stability of orthodontic miniscrews, with CBCT and DEXA BMD strongly correlated (r=0.866).

## Summary
This study pinpoints which bone compartment governs orthodontic miniscrew primary stability. Measuring bone mineral density by both CBCT and DEXA, it found the two imaging methods strongly agreed (r=0.866), and that cortical BMD — not trabecular or total BMD — drove insertion torque (r=0.518) and primary stability (r=0.713, P=.001). The practical message is that cortical bone quality is the dominant site factor for miniscrew anchorage, mirroring the cortical-thickness dependence seen for dental implants. As a bone-block model it lacks in vivo loading.

## Key Contributions
- Identifies cortical BMD as the key determinant of miniscrew primary stability.
- Cross-validates CBCT against DEXA for BMD (r=0.866).
- Separates cortical from trabecular/total contributions.

## Methodology
Experimental bone-block study; miniscrews inserted; BMD by CBCT and DEXA (total/cortical/trabecular ROIs); outcomes insertion torque and primary stability; correlation analysis.

## Results
- Total BMD CBCT vs DEXA: r=0.866, P<.001.
- Cortical BMD vs IT r=0.518; vs PS r=0.713, P=.001.
- Total/trabecular BMD: weak, non-significant with PS.
