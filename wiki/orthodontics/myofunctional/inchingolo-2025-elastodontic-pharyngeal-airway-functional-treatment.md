## One-line Summary

Retrospective lateral-cephalometric comparison (n=50 growing Class II children, 6–12 y) of pharyngeal airway change after Twin Block vs RPE vs AMCOP elastodontic therapy: all three significantly enlarged SPAS/MAS/PAS, but Twin Block produced the largest gains (SPAS +2.1 mm, large effect d=1.35) and AMCOP the smallest (SPAS +0.85 mm, small-moderate d=0.55).

## 한줄요약

후향적 측방두부방사선 비교(성장기 II급 아동 50명, 6–12세)로 Twin Block·RPE·AMCOP 탄성교정장치(elastodontic)의 인두기도(pharyngeal airway, SPAS/MAS/PAS) 변화를 비교: 세 장치 모두 유의하게 기도를 넓혔으나 Twin Block이 가장 컸고(SPAS +2.1mm, d=1.35) AMCOP이 가장 작음(SPAS +0.85mm, d=0.55). 모든 변화는 2D 형태학적 변화이며 기능(airflow/AHI) 개선은 입증 안 됨.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.3390/life15121939](https://doi.org/10.3390/life15121939)), this single-center retrospective cephalometric study traced lateral teleradiographs (T0 pre / T1 post) of 50 growing Class II children (6–12 y, mean 9.2 ± 1.8 y) treated with one of three appliances — Twin Block (TB, n=18; true sagittal mandibular advancement), Rapid Palatal Expander (RPE, n=16; transverse maxillary expansion), or AMCOP SC elastodontic device (n=16; neuromuscular re-education with light elastic forces) — and compared changes in three pharyngeal airway widths: SPAS (retropalatal), MAS (oropharyngeal), and PAS (retroglossal). All three appliances produced statistically significant linear airway enlargement, scaling with the magnitude of mandibular advancement: TB largest (SPAS +2.14 mm, large effects d=1.11–1.35), RPE intermediate (moderate, d=0.59–0.84), AMCOP smallest (small-to-moderate, d=0.46–0.55). ANOVA/Tukey confirmed TB > RPE ≈ AMCOP for airway gains (TB vs others p<0.01; RPE vs AMCOP NS).

Clinically: early functional/orthopedic treatment of mandibular retrusion is *associated with* more favorable upper-airway morphology in growing Class II children, and AMCOP elastodontic therapy does measurably (if modestly) widen the airway — but these are 2D linear morphological gains only. With no AHI, oxygen-saturation, polysomnography, CBCT volumetry, or untreated control group, the study does NOT establish improved airflow or reduced pediatric OSA; treat airway widening as a hypothesis-generating signal, not a functional outcome.

## Key Contributions

- First within-cohort, three-arm comparison of mechanistically distinct growing-patient appliances on the same pharyngeal airway parameters.
- Supplies AMCOP-specific, effect-size–stratified airway benchmarks the elastodontic literature previously lacked.
- Demonstrates a dose-response pattern: greater mandibular advancement (TB) → greater linear airway change.
- Explicitly bounds the claim — morphological, not functional; positions 2D cephalometry as a first-level step toward CBCT + sleep studies.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Retrospective, observational, single-center (Univ. Hospital "Aldo Moro", Bari), T0 vs T1, three parallel appliance groups.
- **Sample**: 50 consecutive children (27 F, 23 M; 6–12 y), Class II skeletal (ANB > 4°), systemically healthy, no airway-affecting conditions. Groups baseline-comparable (age, sex, ANB/SNB, duration; p>0.05); no matching.
- **Appliances**: TB (5–7 mm advancement bite, 20–22 h/day, ~11 mo); RPE Hyrax (1/4-turn/day to diastema, ≥6 mo passive, ~9 mo); AMCOP SC silicone (8–10 h overnight + 1–2 h day, ~11 mo).
- **Imaging/tracing**: Digital lateral teleradiographs (natural head position, max intercuspation, nasal breathing); Delta Dent v2.2.1; single **non-blinded** examiner. Intra-examiner ICC 0.86–0.94 (skeletal), 0.83–0.91 (airway).
- **Statistics**: SPSS 27; paired t-tests (intra-group), one-way ANOVA + Tukey (inter-group), Cohen's d; α<0.05.

## Results

- **Skeletal (TB greatest)**: TB ΔSNB +1.7° (p<0.001), ΔANB −1.5° (p<0.001), ΔCo–Gn +3.3 mm; RPE & AMCOP modest but significant (p<0.05).
- **Dentoalveolar**: Overjet — TB −3.6 mm (p<0.001), RPE −1.6 mm (p=0.012), AMCOP −1.7 mm (p=0.009). Overbite — TB −1.4 mm (p=0.014), RPE −0.6 mm (NS), AMCOP −0.8 mm (NS). IMPA — TB +2.9° (p=0.019), RPE +1.5° (p=0.046), AMCOP +1.5° (NS).
- **Twin Block airway** (Δ mm [95% CI], d): SPAS +2.14 [1.92; 2.36] d=1.35; MAS +1.96 [1.81; 2.12] d=1.24; PAS +1.42 [1.27; 1.57] d=1.11 — all p<0.001, large effects.
- **RPE airway**: SPAS +1.41 [1.15; 1.67] d=0.84; MAS +0.89 [0.67; 1.10] d=0.76; PAS +0.75 [0.47; 1.04] d=0.59 — moderate-to-large.
- **AMCOP airway**: SPAS +0.85 [0.60; 1.10] d=0.55; MAS +0.91 [0.73; 1.09] d=0.48; PAS +0.58 [0.36; 0.81] d=0.46 — small-to-moderate, all CIs above zero.
- **Intergroup**: ANOVA significant for SNB, ANB, Co–Gn, all airway parameters (p<0.05); Tukey — TB > RPE & AMCOP for SNB and SPAS/MAS/PAS (p<0.01); RPE vs AMCOP NS (p>0.05).
- **Caveat (authors')**: 2D linear projection only — no volume/cross-section; no AHI/SpO₂/polysomnography; no untreated control; single non-blinded tracer; no long-term follow-up. Gains are morphological, not functional.
