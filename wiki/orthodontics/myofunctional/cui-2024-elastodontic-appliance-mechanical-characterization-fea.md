## One-line Summary

In-vitro mechanical + structural bench study (46 elastodontic appliances artificial-aged at salivary pH 6/7/8 over 1–3 mo + 5 clinically retrieved appliances worn 2–12 mo) showing EAs are PDMS + silica-filler viscoelastic elastomers whose stress-softening (Mullins) ability and peak stress fall while compression set rises with aging — via side-chain (–CH₃) scission and Si–O–Si main-chain crosslinking, worst under alkaline (OH⁻) saliva, with structural integrity still acceptable to ~6 months of clinical service.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1186/s12903-024-05101-z](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12903-024-05101-z)), this in-vitro study characterized the mechanical force-delivery and aging mechanism of elastodontic appliances (EAs) — the silicone myofunctional devices behind the EF-Line family — using 46 standard-shape EAs artificially aged in pH 6/7/8 artificial saliva (37 °C, 1/2/3 months, where 3 mo of 24-h aging ≈ 6 mo of 12-h/day clinical service) plus 5 clinically retrieved appliances worn 2, 3, 6, 9, and 12 months. FTIR/XRD identified the material as polydimethylsiloxane (PDMS) with silica-based fillers — a two-part highly viscoelastic liquid silicone rubber (LSR) whose stress-softening (Mullins effect) is *what lets it exert light, biocompatible orthodontic force* when the patient bites the occlusal plate. Cyclic compression (T4: 4 cycles 0.5–2 mm; T10: 10 cycles at 1.5 mm) showed that with aging the Mullins index and peak stress decline while compression set rises — the material stiffens. The driving chemistry is side-chain (–CH₃) scission plus Si–O–Si main-chain crosslinking (no change in overall molecular composition), forming a denser, less mobile polymer network; this is accelerated under alkaline (OH⁻, periodontitis-range) saliva, which also produced the worst surface delamination and filler debonding. Detached silica filler became a calcification nucleus for dental calculus (Fukalite, Ca-silicate-carbonate-hydroxide). Structural integrity stayed clinically acceptable to ~6 months; by 12 months the appliance fractured and was no longer serviceable. Intraoral wear degraded the surface more than salivary immersion alone (compression load + bacterial adhesion dominate).

**Clinically:** EAs deliver force through viscoelastic stress-softening, which decays predictably — supporting the routine **6–9-month replacement window** (structurally fine to ~6 mo). For **periodontitis patients with alkaline saliva (pH up to 8)**, expect faster degradation/stiffening and consider earlier replacement or surface-modified appliances; acidic vs neutral pH made little difference, but alkaline did. A stiffened (aged) EA delivers higher unwanted force/torque and worse stress absorption — a theoretical root-resorption / discomfort risk — so a smooth, intact surface and timely replacement matter for both efficacy and safety.

## Key Contributions

- Establishes the **material chemistry** of elastodontic appliances: PDMS polymer matrix + silica-based filler particles (3–5 μm) — i.e., filled liquid silicone rubber.
- Quantifies the **force-delivery mechanism** (viscoelastic Mullins stress-softening, residual strain, compression set) that produces light biocompatible force on biting.
- First analysis of **EA aging under defined salivary pH** (6/7/8), not just neutral — relevant to caries-prone (acidic) and periodontitis (alkaline) patients.
- Pairs controlled **artificial aging** (n=46) with **clinically retrieved** EAs (n=5, 2–12 mo) to anchor bench mechanics to real intraoral wear.
- Yields a **clinical timeline** (acceptable ≤6 mo, fractured by 12 mo) + the **alkaline-saliva degradation warning** and a surface/filler-modification recommendation.

## Methodology

- **Samples**: 46 standard-shape EAs → group A (1 mo, n=15), B (2 mo, n=15), C (3 mo, n=15), + 1 unaged REF; each timepoint split across artificial-saliva pH 6/7/8 (X6/X7/X8). Plus 5 clinically retrieved EAs (worn 2/3/6/9/12 mo; patient salivary pH 6.6–7.4). Specimens ~7 × 3 × 2 mm from buccal flange (BF) and occlusal plate (OP).
- **Artificial aging**: pH-adjusted artificial saliva (CMC, Leagene), 37 °C water bath, foil-sealed, 3× sample volume submersion, fluid changed every 3 days; 3 mo of 24-h aging modeled 6 mo of 12-h/day clinical service.
- **Compression** (MTS E43.104, 23 °C, ~5 mm/min, pre-force 1 N, dental-model fixture, ×5 repeats): **T4** = 4 cycles increasing amplitude 0.5/1/1.5/2 mm → Mullins index (MI); **T10** = 10 cycles at 1.5 mm → compression set (CS) + peak stress (at 1.0 mm).
- **Structural**: SEM-EDS (15.0 kV; C/O/Ca/Si mapping), XRD (CuKα, 40 kV/200 mA, 5–100° 2θ), ATR-FTIR (400–4000 cm⁻¹).
- **Statistics**: SPSS 26; two-way ANOVA (peak stress); three-way ANOVA + LSD post-hoc (MI, CS); p < 0.05.

## Results

- **Material ID**: FTIR peaks Si–O–Si 1100–1000 cm⁻¹, Si–CH₃ 1257 cm⁻¹, Si–(CH₃) 792 cm⁻¹, C–H 2958/2917 cm⁻¹ → PDMS. XRD broad peaks at 2θ 10–15° (crosslinked PDMS) and ~22° (SiO₂ filler), amorphous.
- **REF mechanics**: MI(1–2) 1.021 ± 0.006, MI(2–3) 1.048 ± 0.007; CS1 0.335, CS2 0.346, CS10 0.406; peak stress **14.818 ± 1.627**.
- **MI ↓ with aging time**; REF largest except A8/B8. **Alkaline (pH 8) showed largest MI** (p < 0.01 at 1 & 2 mo; p < 0.05 at 3 mo); neutral vs acidic NS (p = 0.696 / 0.938).
- **Peak stress ↓** after 1 month and with immersion time (p < 0.01); **neutral pH 7 retained highest stress potential** (p < 0.01); fell to ~9.8–10.5 by 3 mo (C6 9.878, C7 10.416, C8 9.752).
- **Compression set**: lower in aged-in-saliva vs REF (p < 0.01); ↑ with pH at 1 mo (p < 0.01); non-monotonic at 2 mo (B8↑/B7↓, p < 0.01); acidic vs neutral NS at 3 mo (p = 0.380); ↑ with cycles (p < 0.01). Instant CS ~0.3–0.45.
- **Aging time predominates**: cycle-number × pH interaction NS (MI p = 0.32, CS p = 0.75, peak stress p = 0.29).
- **Filler–polymer interface damage** signs: T4 slant drop (cycle 3/4) + T10 shoulder ~3 N, more prominent in aged samples.
- **SEM**: filler particles 3–5 μm; alkaline C8 worst (surface delamination + outer-film cracking). Intraoral timeline — 2–3 mo minor; **6 mo** scale-like BF, microcracks ~50 μm, OP ridges ~70 μm gaps (still acceptable); **9 mo** ridge gaps 20–40 μm + calculus <200 μm; **12 mo** OP fractures, cross-section voids, calculus plaques 50–300 μm (high Ca) → no longer serviceable.
- **Aging chemistry**: no new bands / molecular composition unchanged; with aging Si–O–Si ↑ (more crosslinking), –CH₃ / Si–(CH₃) ↓ (side-chain scission), lowest –CH₃ in alkaline. XRD aged ≈ REF amorphous + sharp **Fukalite** peaks (ICDD 01-075-5080 / 01-075-2979, Ca-silicate-carbonate-hydroxide) → calculus nucleation on detached filler.
- **Intraoral > in-vitro degradation** → compression loading + bacterial adhesion dominate over salivary immersion.
