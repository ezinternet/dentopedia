## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.3390/jcm15020804](https://doi.org/10.3390/jcm15020804)), this retrospective cohort from the University of Pavia compared an elastodontic bioactivator (ED) against high-pull headgear (HPHD) in 40 hyperdivergent skeletal Class II growing patients (20 per group, ages 7–11), assessing lateral cephalometric change over an 18-month interval (T0→T1). Both appliances produced a statistically significant intragroup increase in SNB (mandibular sagittal position) with moderate-to-large effect sizes, despite their opposite biomechanical rationales — ED works through neuromuscular stimulation and mandibular advancement, HPHD through orthopaedic restraint of maxillary forward/downward growth. SNA, ANB, the vertical mandibular plane angles (SN^Go-Gn, AnsPns^Go-Gn, N-Go-Gn), and dentoalveolar/soft-tissue parameters all stayed stable, with no clinically meaningful between-group differences. Notably, the hyperdivergent pattern was not worsened by either device, but neither reproduced the ~1–2° mandibular plane reduction that some earlier HPHD studies report.

Clinically: in growing hyperdivergent Class II children, a removable elastodontic bioactivator achieved short-term mandibular positional improvement (SNB) comparable to high-pull headgear while preserving the vertical dimension and avoiding unwanted dentoalveolar/soft-tissue effects — making it a less visible, lower-compliance-burden alternative when the goal is mandibular positional benefit with vertical maintenance (not active vertical reduction). Evidence is limited: small, non-randomized, 2D, 18-month, with unequal prescribed wear time and unmeasured adherence.

## Key Contributions

- One of the few studies offering **direct head-to-head** cephalometric data for an elastodontic device against a conventional, established Class II interceptive appliance (HPHD).
- Shows **comparable mandibular positional benefit (SNB)** between two mechanistically opposite appliances.
- Documents **vertical stability without worsening** of the hyperdivergent pattern for both devices.
- Reports **Cohen's d effect sizes** alongside p-values for clinically interpretable magnitudes.

## Methodology

- **Design**: observational retrospective cohort (IRB 2024-0110; ClinicalTrials.gov registered); two parallel groups, T0 → T1 at 18 months.
- **Sample**: 40 patients, 20 ED vs 20 HPHD; ages 7–11; skeletal Class II hyperdivergent; no dropouts. Mann–Whitney U confirmed no significant baseline demographic/maturational differences.
- **Sample size**: ClinCalc.com, α=0.05, power=80%, primary outcome SN^Go-Gn (expected mean 33.9, difference 3.9, SD 4.39) → 40 (20/group).
- **Interventions**: ED = removable preformed silicone elastomer bioactivator, worn night-time + 1 h/day; HPHD = Bio-High-Pull Headgear (FORESTADENT) on maxillary first-molar bands, worn ~14 h/day.
- **Imaging**: same Soredex Cranex-D unit; Delta-Dent tracing per Giannì analysis; CVM staging; single operator, intra-rater ICC = 0.99.
- **Statistics**: R v3.1.3; Kolmogorov–Smirnov normality; ANOVA + Tukey (repeated-measures correction intragroup); Cohen's d; Bonferroni correction; linear regression for age/CVM; α=0.05.

## Results

- **SNB**: significant intragroup increase T0→T1 in **both** groups (p<0.05), moderate-to-large effect sizes — the principal positive outcome. (Literature ED SNB increments ~0.8°–1.5°.)
- **SNA**: no significant change in either group — no significant maxillary displacement/restraint.
- **ANB**: unchanged in both groups (SNB rose but SNA did not fall, so the discrepancy held); sagittal correction modest and mandible-driven.
- **Vertical mandibular plane (SN^Go-Gn, AnsPns^Go-Gn, N-Go-Gn)**: stable; only flagged comparison was control-T0 vs trial-T1 (p<0.05); no other intra/intergroup vertical differences → H2 (no between-group sagittal difference) accepted.
- **Dentoalveolar & soft tissue**: stable within groups (small/negligible effect sizes) → H3 accepted.
- **Regression**: cephalometric scores not significantly influenced by age or CVM stage (p>0.05; possibly underpowered).
- **Hypotheses**: H1 (vertical skeletal) partially rejected; H2 (sagittal skeletal) accepted; H3 (dentoalveolar/soft tissue) accepted.
