## One-line Summary

Prospective controlled clinical study (n=60 children aged 7–15, Class II div 1/2; 30 EQ Series II [Eptamed] vs 30 Occlus-o-Guide) where both elastodontic devices reduced overjet and overbite over 12 months, with the EQ Series II achieving significantly lower OJ and OB at T1 than Occlus-o-Guide (p=0.0019), measured by iTero digital-model evaluation.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1186/s12903-021-01628-7](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12903-021-01628-7)), this prospective controlled clinical study at the University of L'Aquila compared two commercial elastodontic (myofunctional) appliances for overjet (OJ) and overbite (OB) correction in 60 growing Class II patients (divisions 1 and 2), aged 7–15. Thirty children were treated with the EQ Series II (Eptamed; test) and 30 with the Occlus-o-Guide (Sweden & Martina; control), each worn overnight for 12 months. OJ and OB were measured before treatment (T0) and after 1 year (T1) on digital 3D models obtained by converting alginate-impression plaster casts via iTero intraoral scanning. Both devices reduced OJ and OB from T0 to T1, but at T1 the EQ Series II produced significantly lower OJ and OB than the Occlus-o-Guide (p = 0.0019). Exact mm reductions are reported only in the paper's figure, not the running text.

Clinically: in mixed-dentition Class II children, removable elastodontic appliances worn overnight can intercept overjet and overbite over 12 months, but device choice matters — here the Eptamed EQ Series II outperformed the Occlus-o-Guide on both measures. These devices are easy to wear, allow good home oral hygiene, and disturb oral microbiota less than fixed appliances, but the evidence base is small-sample, single-center, and short-term.

## Key Contributions

- First head-to-head clinical comparison of EQ Series II (Eptamed) vs Occlus-o-Guide (Sweden & Martina) for OJ/OB reduction in growing Class II patients.
- Outcome assessment by **digital 3D model superimposition** (iTero scan of plaster casts) rather than manual caliper measurement.
- Shows elastodontic device **brand/design affects correction magnitude** — the EQ Series II achieved significantly greater OJ and OB reduction at 12 months.
- Reinforces elastodontic appliances as low-burden interceptive tools (overnight wear, fewer check-ups, lower microbiota impact than fixed appliances) acting on the neuromuscular/stomatognathic system.

## Methodology

- **Design**: prospective controlled clinical study, two parallel groups (test vs control), within-group T0→T1 + between-group comparison at T1.
- **Screening**: 115 patients aged 6–16 examined (panoramic radiographs, photos, alginate impressions); treatment need per Brook & Shaw IOTN index.
- **Exclusion**: IOTN > 4; epilepsy, systemic disease, TMD, periodontal disease; no written informed consent.
- **Inclusion**: skeletal + dental Class II malocclusion (div 1 and 2); presence of OJ and OB.
- **Final sample (n=60, 30/group)**:
  - Test — EQ Series II (Eptamed): 30 patients (14 M, 16 F; mean age 10.66 ± 2.12 y); medium-hardness orange Class II device, mouthguard-like covering both arches to last molars, sized by inter-first-premolar palatal-cusp distance, muscle-activated by biting, worn overnight.
  - Control — Occlus-o-Guide (Sweden & Martina): 30 patients (15 M, 15 F; mean age 10.76 ± 2.52 y); G-type elastomeric EGA for mixed dentition, sized by inter-upper-lateral-incisor distance, worn overnight, checked every 30 days.
- **Timepoints**: T0 (baseline) and T1 (12 months).
- **Measurement**: OJ and OB on iTero-derived 3D virtual models (same orthodontist took all impressions; DTW lab scanning).
- **Statistics**: Wilcoxon test (within-group), significance p < 0.05; between-group difference at T1 p = 0.0019.

## Results

- T0: OJ and OB were similar across both groups (matched baseline orthodontic features).
- T1 (12 months): both OJ and OB were significantly lower for the EQ Series II (Eptamed) than for the Occlus-o-Guide — **p = 0.0019**.
- Both devices improved OJ and OB from T0 to T1; the EQ Series II (test) group showed a statistically significant improvement in values.
- Exact mm OJ/OB means (SD) per group are presented only in the paper's figure, not the running text; the transcribed full text reports direction of effect and the p = 0.0019 between-group result.
- Secondary: removable design preserved home oral hygiene; microbiota alterations registered ~1 month after start, but lower oral-bacteria impact than fixed appliances.
- Limitations: small sample (n=60), short 12-month follow-up, single-center/single-clinician; authors call for larger, more homogeneous replication.
