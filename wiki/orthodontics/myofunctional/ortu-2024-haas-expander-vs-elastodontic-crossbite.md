## One-line Summary

Single-centre prospective comparative study (n=64, ages 7–8; 32 elastodontic Equilibrator vs 32 Haas expander) finding **no statistically significant difference** in intermolar (IMW) or intercanine (ICW) width gain at 12 months — the two devices were equally effective.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.3390/reports7020041](https://doi.org/10.3390/reports7020041)), this single-centre prospective comparative study (self-described observational pilot) randomized 64 growing children aged 7–8 with Class I crossbite (IOTN grade 3) 1:1 to a removable **elastodontic device** (Eptamed Equilibrator, EQ Series 00, worn overnight) or a rigid banded **Haas rapid palatal expander**, and compared transverse maxillary width on intraoral scans (iTero → OrthoCAD) at baseline and 12 months. Both groups gained intermolar (IMW) and intercanine (ICW) width with **no statistically significant between-device difference** — the two appliances were equally effective, and IMW increased more than ICW in both (attributed to posterior anchorage). This is the first reported head-to-head of an elastodontic (myofunctional) appliance against a traditional orthopaedic expander.

Clinically: in **mild maxillary transverse constriction** in growing children, a removable elastodontic device can match the dentoalveolar transverse gain of a rigid Haas expander while offering greater comfort, no painful sutural distraction, no parental screw-activation burden, easier oral hygiene, and (hypothesized) lower relapse via functional re-education — making it a reasonable alternative when true skeletal expansion is not required. The Haas remains the choice when greater, rapid skeletal expansion is the goal.

## Key Contributions

- First direct head-to-head of an **elastodontic device vs a rigid orthopaedic palatal expander** (Haas) for transverse deficiency in growing patients — a gap the authors explicitly flag.
- Establishes **equivalence** on dentoalveolar transverse outcomes (IMW, ICW) at 12 months.
- Frames elastodontic appliances as a comfort/compliance-favourable, hardware-free option for mild constriction, contrasting **functional (neuromyofascial) expansion** against **skeletal sutural distraction**.

## Methodology

- **Design**: single-centre prospective comparative study; computer-generated 1:1 randomized allocation (Sealed Envelope power calculator), stratified by centre, random block sizes 4/6/8. IRB 57/2021–22 (University of L'Aquila).
- **Sample**: 120 screened → 64 enrolled (27 declined, 29 ineligible). Test (elastodontic) n=32, 16F/16M, mean age 7.08 ± 0.44 (10 unilateral / 22 bilateral crossbite); Control (Haas) n=32, 16F/16M, mean age 7.32 ± 0.50 (14 unilateral crossbite). No dropouts.
- **Inclusion**: skeletal & molar Class I, complete upper first-molar eruption, uni-/bilateral crossbite (IOTN grade 3). **Exclusion**: IOTN > 4, caries, TMD, epilepsy, systemic/periodontal disease, no consent.
- **Test protocol**: Eptamed Equilibrator (00 series, medium hardness), bite-activated removable functional appliance worn overnight; monthly review.
- **Control protocol**: Haas banded on upper first permanent/deciduous molars, cemented, chair-activated 2 quarter-turns (0.40 mm; Lamparski 2003); home activation 0.40 mm/day × 15 days, then blocked, left in situ 3 months.
- **Outcomes**: IMW (upper first molars) and ICW (canines) on iTero Element scans at T0 and T1 (12 mo), measured in OrthoCAD 5.9.1.50 by a single operator; method error via Dahlberg's formula (30 casts re-measured at 1 week).
- **Statistics**: t-test / chi-square; non-parametric Wilcoxon rank-sum adopted for conservativeness; p < 0.05.

## Results

- **No statistically significant difference** between Haas and Eptamed groups for IMW or ICW at T0 or T1 — devices equally effective.
- Baseline groups comparable: sex p = 1, age p = 0.143; IMW and ICW means/SD not different between groups at T0 or T1.
- After Wilcoxon control for sex and physiological growth, between-group differences depended only on device type.
- **Intermolar width (IMW) increased more than intercanine width (ICW)** in both groups, attributed to posterior band/anchorage-driven enlargement (concordant with Boysen et al., Petren et al.; opposite to Bjerklin et al.).
- 100% retention — no dropouts; all patients collaborative.
- (Exact mm gains and group means are reported only in the source's figures/tables, not transcribed in the available full text; the load-bearing finding is the non-significant between-device difference.)
