## One-line Summary

Scoping review (PRISMA-ScR; 16 studies, n=345 treated, mean age 8.48 y) finding elastodontic appliances produce statistically significant sagittal correction in mixed-dentition Class II — SNB ↑ (13 studies, +0.61° to +2.82°), ANB ↓ (11–12 studies, −0.38° to −2.42°), OVJ ↓ (10 studies, −2.1 to −4.58 mm) — effective vs no-treatment but weaker than twin-block.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.3390/dj12080247](https://doi.org/10.3390/dj12080247)), this is the first scoping review (PRISMA-ScR) to evaluate elastodontic-device outcomes by cephalometric measurement on the sagittal plane in growing mixed-dentition patients. From 1662 records (search 13 May 2024), 16 studies were included (12 retrospective, 4 prospective; 14 with control), totaling 345 treated patients (mean age 8.48 y); 15 studies were Class II and 1 was Class III. Elastodontic appliances (EAs) — preformed or custom removable silicone/elastic devices activated by the patient's muscle function — produced statistically significant sagittal correction: SNB increased in 13 Class II studies (+0.61° to +2.82°), ANB decreased in 11–12 studies (−0.38° to −2.42°, indicating mandibular advancement or forward repositioning), and overjet decreased in 10 studies (−2.1 to −4.58 mm). Overbite decreased in 4 studies; mandibular length increased in 8. Vertical/divergence parameters (FMA, SN-Go-Gn) were largely unchanged. Head-to-head, EAs beat no-treatment but were weaker than rigid functional appliances (twin-block superior in Johnson; greater OVJ/OVB reduction with twin-block vs Occlus-o-Guide in Galluccio), plausibly because high appliance elasticity makes a sustained protrusive mandibular posture hard to maintain. No methodological-quality appraisal was performed and compliance was poorly documented. Clinically: in growing mixed-dentition patients with mild-to-moderate Class II and altered oral function, EAs are a simpler, less-invasive, cost-effective adjunct that meaningfully reduce overjet and advance the mandible — but they do not replace validated functional/orthopedic appliances, so case selection and compliance (typically 1–4 h/day plus overnight) are decisive.

## Key Contributions

- First cephalometric-outcome scoping review of elastodontic devices for sagittal malocclusion in mixed dentition.
- Quantifies the effect ceiling: SNB +0.61° to +2.82°, ANB −0.38° to −2.42°, OVJ −2.1 to −4.58 mm.
- Establishes the consensus: EAs > no treatment, but EAs < twin-block / rigid functional appliances.

## Methodology

- **Design**: PRISMA-ScR scoping review (not registered).
- **Databases**: Scopus, PubMed, Embase, Web of Science (search 13 May 2024); no year/language limit.
- **PICO**: growing mixed-dentition humans / elastodontic treatment / vs other functional appliances or untreated / teleradiographic (cephalometric) dental and skeletal outcomes.
- **Selection**: 1662 → 726 (de-dup) → 83 full-text → 16 included; 2 independent reviewers + 3rd for disputes.
- **No quality (risk-of-bias) appraisal** of included studies — an explicit limitation.

## Results

- **Corpus**: 16 studies — Italy 8, China 3, Turkey 3, India 1, Egypt 1; years 2021 (6), 2022 (5), 2023 (3), 2004 (1). 12 retrospective, 4 prospective; 14 controlled.
- **Sample**: 345 treated (142 M / 148 F / 55 unreported); mean age 8.48 y; sample-size calc in 6 studies. 15 Class II (6 Class II Div 1; 2 also Class I), 1 Class III.
- **SNB**: ↑ significant in 13 Class II studies, +0.61° to +2.82°; ↓ in the Class III study.
- **ANB**: ↓ in 12 studies (Discussion: 11 significant, −0.38° to −2.42°) → mandibular advancement / forward repositioning; 2 non-significant.
- **SNA**: significant ↓ in only 2 studies.
- **OVJ**: ↓ significant in 10 studies, −2.1 to −4.58 mm; ↑ in 1 Class III study.
- **OVB**: ↓ significant in 4 studies.
- **Mandibular length**: ↑ in 8 studies (low heterogeneity); not evaluated in 7; 1 non-significant.
- **Vertical pattern**: SN-Go-Gn divergence ↓ in 3 studies (non-sig in 7); FMA unchanged (no significant improvement in 6; unmentioned in 10).
- **Head-to-head**: Coban & Madian — no myobrace-vs-twin-block difference; Johnson — twin-block superior (Ar-Gn, OVB, SN-Go-Gn); Galluccio — twin-block > Occlus-o-Guide for OVJ/OVB; Yang — preformed myofunctional > conventional for SNB; Zhang — T4K > Hyrax (SNA, SNB, Go-Me).
- **vs Controls**: Ciavarella Co-Gn p=0.0173 (but Patano Co-Me p=0.102 ns); Galluccio Ar-Pg p=0.004, SNA p=0.017; Johnson Go-Me p=0.005, Ar-Gn p<0.001, SNB p=0.017; OVJ/OVB reductions significantly greater than control in several studies.
- **Compliance**: typical regimen 1–4 h/day plus overnight; adherence poorly documented but directly tied to outcome.
