## One-line Summary

Retrospective controlled cohort (n=39; TG=25, CG=14, mean age 7y) — AMCOP elastodontic activator fully corrected functional posterior cross-bite and increased palatal symmetry (surface-matching 81.1%→92.3%) plus intermolar/intercanine widths over 12 months, while untreated controls showed no change.

## 한줄요약

후향적 대조 코호트 (n=39; 치료군 25, 대조군 14, 평균 7세) — AMCOP 탄성교정장치(Elastodontic Appliance, EA)가 기능성 후방 교차교합(Functional Posterior Cross-Bite, FPXB)을 완전 교정하고 12개월간 구개 대칭성(표면 일치율 81.1%→92.3%)과 대구치·견치 폭경을 증가시켰으나, 미치료 대조군은 변화가 없었다.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1186/s12903-023-02731-7](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12903-023-02731-7)), this is the first study to evaluate maxillary-arch and palatal-morphology changes after treatment of functional posterior cross-bite (FPXB) with an elastodontic appliance (EA) in growing children, using a 3D digital-model imaging workflow (intra-oral scan superimposition + mirrored-model surface-to-surface matching + Euclidean deviation analysis). In a retrospective controlled cohort of 39 early-mixed-dentition subjects (mean age 7y), 25 treated with the AMCOP Integral/Basic activator were compared with 14 untreated controls (treatment delayed one year due to COVID-19 restrictions). After 12 months, all treated subjects achieved complete FPXB correction with coincident midlines in centric occlusion and centric relation, with significant increases in intermolar width (IMW) and intercanine width (ICW), greater expansion on the cross-bite side, and a rise in palatal surface symmetry from 81.1% to 92.3%. Controls showed no spontaneous correction and no significant change. The baseline asymmetry was localized to the dentoalveolar process (lower palate), sparing the basal bone, consistent with adaptive alveolar bending in FPXB.

Clinically: an elastodontic activator (AMCOP) can correct functional posterior cross-bite with mild bilateral maxillary contraction in early mixed dentition, improving both transverse arch width and palatal symmetry within ~12 months — a myofunctional alternative to conventional dentoalveolar/skeletal expanders for selected mild cases; long-term stability remains unproven and the evidence is retrospective with a small control group.

## Key Contributions

- First reported evidence that elastodontic appliances can treat FPXB and mild maxillary contraction — a previously unproven EA indication.
- Objective 3D quantification of palatal symmetry (mirrored-model surface matching + deviation analysis) rather than linear arch widths alone.
- Localizes FPXB asymmetry to the dentoalveolar process (lower palate), sparing basal bone — supporting an adaptive-alveolar-bending mechanism.
- Uses a COVID-delayed untreated control group to separate treatment effects from growth.

## Methodology

- **Design**: retrospective controlled cohort, early mixed dentition, University of Catania (Sept 2017–Dec 2020); IRB-approved, parental consent.
- **Sample**: n=39, mean age 7 ± 0.8 y. TG = 25 (7.3 ± 0.9 y) received AMCOP Integral/Basic activator (Ortho Protec, Italy); CG = 14 (6.8 ± 0.7 y) postponed treatment one year (COVID-19).
- **Inclusion**: intra-oral scans at T0 and T1; posterior cross-bite of ≥2 maxillary posterior teeth; mandibular functional shift ≥2 mm toward cross-bite; class I or edge-to-edge molars. **Exclusion**: missing teeth, anterior cross-bite, TMD, prior orthodontics, caries, mobile deciduous posteriors, craniofacial deformity.
- **Wear**: night + 2 h/day during correction (biting with lips in contact); retention 2 h/day after correction; ± slight occlusal grinding of maxillary deciduous canines.
- **Imaging**: Carestream 3600 intra-oral scans at T0 and T1 = 12 months. Linear IMW/ICW and emi-lateral eIMW/eICW (Ortho Analyzer, 3Shape). 3D mirrored-model surface-to-surface matching across the median palatal plane (MPP), best-fit alignment, Euclidean deviation across 100% of surface points, color maps at 0.5 mm tolerance (Geomagic Control X 2017).
- **Reliability**: intra- (A.L.G., 4-week repeat) and inter-operator (V.R.) ICC 0.887–0.936 (excellent).
- **Statistics**: parametric (normal distribution); paired/unpaired Student's t-tests, chi-square; p < 0.05; SPSS v24. Power calc: 13/group for a 1.52 mm eIMW difference at 80% power.

## Results

- **Arch widths**: significant increase in IMW and ICW T0→T1 in TG (p < 0.05); slight non-significant decrease in CG (p > 0.05).
- **Baseline asymmetry**: at T0 the cross-bite side was narrower than the non-cross-bite side in both groups — ~1 mm for eICW, ~1.5 mm for eIMW.
- **Asymmetry reduction**: eICW/eIMW side-to-side differences significantly reduced T0→T1 in TG (p < 0.05; expansion greater on cross-bite side) but unchanged in CG (p > 0.05); TG improvement significant vs CG (p < 0.05).
- **Palatal surface symmetry (% agreement, original vs mirrored model)**: T0 — TG 81.12% ± 3.03, CG 79.36% ± 2.95; T1 — TG **92.32% ± 4.05** (significant increase, p < 0.05), CG 77.47% ± 3.14 (non-significant decrease, p > 0.05).
- **Deviation map**: vault predominantly green (symmetric); intense blue/red on opposite alveolar-process sides — asymmetry confined to the lower palate at the alveolar process, basal bone spared.
- **Cross-bite correction**: 100% of TG achieved complete FPXB correction with coincident upper/lower midlines in centric occlusion and centric relation; 0% spontaneous correction in CG (FPXB not self-correcting).
- **Reliability**: ICC 0.887–0.936 across linear and 3D measures (excellent).
