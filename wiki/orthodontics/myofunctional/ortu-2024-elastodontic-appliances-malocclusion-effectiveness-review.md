## One-line Summary

Narrative literature review (8 articles, 2020–June 2023; abstract-only) finding that elastodontic devices treat malocclusion in growing patients safely, simply, and comfortably, while noting a lack of knowledge/evidence about the technique.

## 한줄요약

문헌 고찰(8편, 2020–2023년 6월; 초록 전용)로, 탄성교정장치(elastodontic device)가 성장기 환자의 부정교합을 안전하고 간단하며 편안하게 치료할 수 있으나 해당 기술에 대한 근거·지식이 부족하다고 결론.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.23804/ejpd.2024.2030](https://doi.org/10.23804/ejpd.2024.2030)), this review of the literature set out to verify the effectiveness of elastodontic devices in treating malocclusions in growing patients. An English-language search of PubMed, Web of Science, and Scopus covering 2020 to June 2023 yielded eight articles meeting the inclusion/exclusion criteria. The included articles highlight the ability of elastodontic devices to manage malocclusion in a safe, simple, and more comfortable way for the young patient, but the authors emphasize a lack of knowledge about the technique and frame the review as supplying the scientific community with updated evidence.

This page is **abstract-only** — full text was not retrieved (no open-access copy located), so effect sizes, per-malocclusion-type results, and risk-of-bias appraisal are not available.

Clinically: elastodontic (prefabricated elastomeric myofunctional) appliances are positioned as a safe, simple, patient-comfortable interceptive option in growing patients, but the supporting evidence base is thin (8 articles) and explicitly described as under-developed — treat as a low-burden adjunct pending stronger primary trials, not as an established first-line standard.

## Key Contributions

- Confirms, from recent literature, that elastodontic devices can act on malocclusion in growing patients.
- Frames the appliances as safe, simple, and more comfortable for young patients.
- Names an evidence/knowledge gap as the central limitation of the field.

## Methodology

- Narrative review of the literature (English-language).
- Databases searched: PubMed, Web of Science, Scopus.
- Search window: 2020 → June 2023.
- Eight articles included after inclusion/exclusion screening.
- Abstract-only: screening criteria, individual study designs, and quality appraisal are not recoverable.

## Results

1. Eight articles met the inclusion/exclusion criteria.
2. Included articles highlight the capability of elastodontic devices to act in cases of malocclusion.
3. Treatment described as **safe** for the young patient.
4. Treatment described as **simple** to deliver.
5. Treatment described as **more comfortable** for the growing patient.
6. A **lack of knowledge** about the technique is noted, motivating the review and limiting confidence.
