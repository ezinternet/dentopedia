RCT (n=70 children, mean age 11±1 y) comparing the Eptamed Equilibrator elastodontic device (series 00) vs the Schwartz plate for palatal expansion of posterior crossbite; the elastodontic device achieved expansion comparable to the conventional removable plate, with greater comfort and compliance.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.23804/ejpd.2025.2230](https://doi.org/10.23804/ejpd.2025.2230)), this randomised open-label clinical trial compared two removable devices for maxillary palatal expansion in children with posterior crossbite — the **Eptamed Equilibrator elastodontic device (series 00)** against the conventional **Schwartz plate**. Posterior crossbite, a very frequent malocclusion in the Caucasian population usually associated with maxillary hypoplasia or retrusion, was treated in 70 children (mean age 11 ± 1 y) randomised to a test (elastodontic) or control (Schwartz plate) arm, with transverse width measured at the first-upper-premolar cusps (or second-deciduous-molar width) at 6 and 12 months. The elastodontic device produced palatal expansion **comparable** to the traditional expander, but with greater patient comfort and compliance.

This page is **abstract-only** — the full text was not retrieved (no open-access copy), so numeric expansion magnitudes (mm gained), between-group p-values, and per-timepoint data are not available; only the directional conclusion is citable.

**Clinically:** in growing children with posterior crossbite, a prefabricated elastodontic appliance can be considered a comfort/compliance-friendly alternative to a screw-activated Schwartz plate when the goal is mild transverse/palatal expansion — expecting comparable expansion rather than superior expansion. Reserve fixed/screw expanders (Haas/RME) when larger or skeletally driven transverse correction is required, since this trial only establishes parity with another removable device, not with fixed expansion.

## Key Contributions

- RCT-level evidence that an elastodontic (prefabricated elastomeric myofunctional) device can match a conventional removable expander on a transverse-expansion endpoint.
- Demonstrates a patient-experience advantage (comfort, compliance) for the elastodontic device over the Schwartz plate.
- Positions elastodontic appliances within the standard posterior-crossbite expansion toolkit alongside fixed and screw-activated removable devices.

## Methodology

- **Design:** randomised, open-label, two-arm clinical trial.
- **Participants:** 70 children (30 boys, 40 girls); mean age 11 ± 1 years.
- **Test arm:** palatal expansion with the Eptamed Equilibrator elastodontic device (series 00).
- **Control arm:** palatal expansion with the Schwartz plate (traditional removable expander).
- **Outcome measure:** distance between cusps of the first upper premolars (or width between second deciduous molars as reference).
- **Follow-up:** assessments at 6 months and 12 months from treatment start.
- **Statistics:** t-test / Wilcoxon signed-rank (continuous); chi-square / Kruskal-Wallis (categorical).

## Results

- **n = 70** children (30 boys, 40 girls), mean age **11 ± 1 years**, randomised to **2 groups**: elastodontic (Eptamed Equilibrator series 00) vs Schwartz plate.
- The **elastodontic device achieved palatal expansion comparable to the traditional (Schwartz plate) expander**.
- The elastodontic device delivered this with **greater comfort and greater patient compliance**.
- Transverse outcome assessed at **6 and 12 months** via inter-first-premolar cusp distance (or second-deciduous-molar width).
- **Abstract-only:** numeric expansion magnitudes (mm), between-group significance, and per-timepoint values are not reported in the abstract and were not retrievable.
