## One-line Summary

Case series (4 of 38 developing-dentition children, ages 7–10) + literature review showing AMCOP elastodontic Bio-activators corrected Class I/II/III, deep-bite, open-bite and atypical-swallowing malocclusions over ~12–20-month active+retention protocols with no follow-up fixed appliances.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.3390/ijerph19020988](https://doi.org/10.3390/ijerph19020988)), this Inchingolo (2022) paper is a retrospective case series of 4 illustrative cases (drawn from 38 children treated in developmental age) combined with a narrative literature review of elastodontics, describing the AMCOP (Cranium-Occluded-Postural Multifunctional Harmonizers) Bio-activator family — Micerium-patented preformed silicone-elastomer appliances with vestibular + lingual flanges, an indentation-free free central area, and a lingual ramp/tongue button. The central conceptual claim is the **"elastodontic space"**: because AMCOP devices have no indentations, teeth align as a *result of restored neuromuscular balance* (lips, tongue, mandibular elevators/depressors), not from device-induced tooth movement — which the authors argue yields more stable results. All 4 cases (Class I deep-bite/atypical swallowing, Class III, Class II deep-bite, skeletal open bite) were corrected with no subsequent fixed appliances; cases #1 and #4 had 3-year follow-up photos.

Clinically: in deciduous/mixed dentition, AMCOP Bio-activators are a low-compliance-burden single-phase interceptive option — select the device by malocclusion (INTEGRAL/BASIC for Class I & open bite, SC for Class II, TC for Class III, OPEN for skeletal open bite, D/DC for pacifier-related deficits, ELASTO-OSAS for snoring/OSA), run an active phase (night + 1 h/day × ~6 mo, then night only) plus a retention phase, and add speech therapy for atypical swallowing. Evidence weight is low (uncontrolled case series; authors themselves note the absence of RCTs precludes meta-analysis); they cannot correct tooth rotations or severe skeletal dysmorphosis.

## Key Contributions

1. First consolidated catalog of the **AMCOP Bio-activator family** with device-by-device indications, as an elastodontic interceptive protocol.
2. Defines the **"elastodontic space"** — indentation-free design so alignment follows restored function, not induced movement.
3. Reports **single-phase resolution** of Class I/II/III, deep-bite and open-bite cases without subsequent fixed appliances.
4. Notes AMCOP's **TC device extends elastodontics to Class III**, which prior elastomeric literature excluded a priori.
5. Frames AMCOP within the historical lineage of functional appliances (Andresen activator → Kesling positioner → Bergersen EGA/Occlus-O-Guide → AMCOP).

## Methodology

- **Design**: retrospective case series (4 illustrative cases of 38 treated) + narrative literature review; single center (University of Bari, Italy); ethics-exempt, informed consent obtained.
- **Device family** (free central area, no indentations; lingual ramp + tongue button; thermoactive superelastic, chairside-adjustable; 4 arch shapes):
  - **D / DC** — deciduous dentition, very young children (DC = "pacifier" grip); transverse deficit & pacifier-related open bite.
  - **BASIC** — Class I; anteriorly thick occlusal plane to raise vertical dimension in deep bite.
  - **INTEGRAL** — Class I; flat occlusal plane; open-bite correction & arch balancing.
  - **SC (Second Class)** — mandibular anterior sliding plane; advances mandible, corrects overjet (upper-incisor retroinclination + lower-incisor proinclination), improves TMJ dysfunction.
  - **TC (Third Class)** — upper anterior sliding plane + posterior pressure on lower arch to brake mandibular growth.
  - **INTEGRAL PLUS** — internal groove for combined use with multibracket appliances.
  - **OPEN** — posteriorly raised occlusal plane; skeletal open bite.
  - **ELASTO-OSAS** — front sliding plane + accentuated lingual shield; snoring/sleep apnea.
- **Wear protocol**: active phase — night + 1 h/day × ~6 mo, then night only × 6–12 mo; followed by retention/restraint (e.g. every other night). Pre/post cephalometric analysis (DeltaDent/Delta software); speech therapy adjunct for atypical swallowing.

## Results

- **Case 1** (10-y F, atypical swallowing, deep bite, skeletal Class I; SNA 82°, SNB 78.6°, ANB 3.4°, Wits 1.5 mm) — INTEGRAL S + speech therapy → dentoskeletal Class I, corrected overjet/overbite, tongue reeducation; 3-y follow-up photos.
- **Case 2** (7-y F, skeletal Class III, anterior crossbite; SNA 82.4°, SNB 83.2°, ANB −0.8°, Wits −8.5 mm) — AMCOP TC → molar+canine Class I, proper overjet/overbite, improved skeletal class (ongoing growth control advised).
- **Case 3** (7-y M, skeletal Class II deep bite; ANB 7.7°, Wits 4.2, IS^II 103°, IS^NS 119°) — INTEGRAL OS × 8 mo + SC × 12 mo → **ANB 7.7°→3.2°, Wits 4.2→−0.8 mm, IS^NS 119°→103.4°, IS^II 103°→122°**.
- **Case 4** (8-y F, skeletal open bite + mild Class II; AnsPns^GoGn 37°, SN^GoGn 42.1°, ANB 4.2°) — speech therapy + AMCOP OPEN → **open bite corrected (AnsPns^GoGn 37°→23.3°, SN^GoGn 42.1°→36.1°)**, upper-arch expansion enabling permanent-tooth eruption; 3-y follow-up photos.
- **Compliance/comparators (review)**: Twin Block failure up to **34%**; high AMCOP compliance reported (despite augmented salivation). Occlus-O-Guide series (167 children): overjet 3.1→1.9 mm, overbite 3.2→2.1 mm, incisor alignment in **98%**.
- **Interception yield (review)**: corrects ~15% and improves ~49% of developing mixed-dentition problems. Mandibular growth peak = CVM stages 2–3.
- **Limits**: 4/38 cases, retrospective, single-center; full long-term follow-up only for cases #1 & #4; no RCTs → no meta-analysis; cannot correct rotations or severe skeletal dysmorphosis.
