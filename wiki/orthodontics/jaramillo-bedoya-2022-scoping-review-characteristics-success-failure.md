## Summary

This scoping review charts how temporary anchorage devices (TADs) are actually used in orthodontics — the descriptive context that a pooled failure-rate meta-analysis cannot give. Across 103 studies (2010–2020), TADs were most often maxillary and inter-radicular (between the second premolar and first molar), with AbsoAnchor the most-used brand and a typical configuration of 1.6 mm diameter × 8 mm length, self-drilled, closed-technique, immediately loaded, at forces of 40–800 g. Just under half of studies (47.6%) reported success rates ≥90%. As a scoping review it deliberately omits quality appraisal and effect pooling, so it complements rather than competes with the SR+MA anchors — it supplies the practice-pattern map behind their numbers.

## Key Contributions

- Descriptive landscape of TAD device parameters, sites, loading, and force ranges.
- Aggregates reported success-failure rates across a large body (103 studies).
- Flags design heterogeneity and geographic publication concentration.

## Methodology

- PRISMA-ScR + JBI scoping methodology; PubMed-MEDLINE, Scopus, LILACS, EMBASE; 2010–2020; EN/ES/PT.
- Phase A (umbrella-review records) + Phase B (new search) → 103 included.
- Sample-size-weighted mean success rate; no risk-of-bias appraisal (scoping by design).

## Results

- 103 studies; mostly maxillary, inter-radicular (2PM–1M).
- Dominant brand AbsoAnchor; typical 1.6 mm × 8 mm, self-drilled, closed, immediate loading, 40–800 g.
- 47.6% of studies reported success ≥90%.
- 47 journals (AJODO most frequent, n=26).
