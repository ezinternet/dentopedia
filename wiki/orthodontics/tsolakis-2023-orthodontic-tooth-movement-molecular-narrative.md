## Summary
Narrative review (Cochrane, PubMed, Scopus; until June 2023) examining biomedical parameters influencing orthodontic tooth movement (OTM). Published November 2023, Bioengineering 10:1275. Aristotle University Thessaloniki + NKUA Athens + Case Western Reserve.

Core molecular focus: OPG/RANK/RANKL triad governing the balance between osteoclastogenesis and osteogenesis during OTM. Reviews surgical (corticotomy, piezoelectric decortication), pharmacological, physical (LIPUS, vibration), gene therapy, and stem cell approaches to accelerate or control OTM. Conclusion: biomedical approach offers multiple application possibilities but scarcity of human studies and high cost/complexity limit clinical translation.

## Key Contributions
- **OPG/RANK/RANKL triad**: RANKL stimulates osteoclastogenesis → bone resorption; OPG is decoy receptor inhibiting RANKL → protects bone; RANK is the receptor on osteoclast precursors
- **Osteoblast products**: OCN, ON, OPN, ALP, BSP, COL1A1/COL1A2; signaling cascade driven by hormones, cytokines, prostaglandins, growth factors
- **Corticotomy/decortication**: Regional acceleratory phenomenon (RAP) → accelerates OTM by intensifying local bone remodeling
- **LIPUS (Low-Intensity Pulsed Ultrasound)**: promotes fracture healing; mixed evidence on OTM acceleration
- **Gene therapy / stem cells**: preclinical evidence only; no accepted clinical application
- No universally accepted biomarkers for measuring or controlling OTM

## Results
| Intervention | OTM Effect |
|---|---|
| Corticotomy/decortication | Accelerates OTM (RAP) |
| RANKL upregulation | ↑ osteoclastogenesis → faster resorption |
| OPG administration | ↓ bone resorption → slows OTM |
| LIPUS/vibration | Promotes healing; equivocal OTM acceleration data |
| Gene therapy | Preclinical only |
| Stem cell approaches | Theoretically enhanced regeneration; minimal human data |
