## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1186/s12903-024-05274-7](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12903-024-05274-7)), this SR isolates the third predictability determinant — the **trimline** (gingival margin geometry and extension) — completing the force-delivery triad with attachments and anchorage. Twelve studies (low-to-moderate RoB) were synthesized.

Trimline design influences efficacy through **two mechanisms**: enhanced **force/moment delivery** and increased **aligner retention**. **Straight, extended margins** generate higher forces and moments and grip better than **scalloped or shorter** designs, translating into greater tooth displacement for **intrusion, translation, tipping, and root torquing** — though the magnitude is movement-dependent. Extended trimlines also retain better, which the authors link to fewer revisions, shorter overall treatment, and higher satisfaction. Evidence is largely biomechanical/in-vitro, so optimal trimline-by-movement combinations are not yet defined.

Clinically: trimline is a modifiable design lever (often under operator/lab control) that meaningfully affects expression of the harder movements; specifying straight/extended margins where torque or intrusion is planned is mechanistically justified. Because torque and intrusion forces also bear on root-resorption risk, this links to the adverse-effects axis.

## Key Contributions

- Trimline geometry as an independent, modifiable predictability lever.
- Mechanisms: enhanced force delivery + increased retention.
- Straight/extended margins favor intrusion, translation, tipping, torque.

## Methodology

SR (no MA). 5 databases Jan 2000-Aug 2024; ROBINS-I; 12 studies, low-moderate RoB; qualitative synthesis. Largely biomechanical/in-vitro. Confidence `sr`; PMC OA full text.

## Results

- Straight/extended > scalloped/short for force, moment, retention.
- Greater displacement for intrusion, translation, tipping, root torque.
- Effects movement-dependent; optimal combinations undefined.
