## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1016/j.ejwf.2025.12.001](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ejwf.2025.12.001)), this is a randomized four-period single-blind crossover trial testing whether consumer probiotic products can suppress the biofilm that accumulates on clear aligners — the hygiene burden that the aligner-vs-fixed periodontal literature flags as the residual concern even where aligners show a periodontal edge. Twenty Invisalign users each cycled through four 7-day interventions (probiotic gummy, rinse, capsule, and drink) separated by 14-day washouts, with biofilm sampled from standardized aligner discs.

All four delivery forms produced statistically significant reductions in total viable counts and in biofilm matrix (EPS) fluorescence versus pre-intervention controls (P<0.001). CFU reductions spanned **0.62–1.06 log10**, and the **probiotic drink (Yakult; *L. paracasei* Shirota) gave the largest effect** (LS-mean difference vs baseline 1.055 log10 CFU; 95% CI 0.93–1.18). CLSM fluorescence fell by 93–145 AU across forms (all P<0.001).

The trial is **abstract-only** here (no open-access full text) and the authors themselves frame it as **preliminary**: the intervention periods were short (7 days), probiotic content was verified by label only (no strain identification or viability assay), and there was no comparison against routine mechanical cleaning — the actual standard of care. So the data show probiotics *can* lower aligner biofilm in the short term, but not that they add value beyond, or could replace, brushing/soaking.

Clinically: consumer probiotics — a drink in particular — measurably reduced aligner biofilm load and EPS over a week, but this is hypothesis-generating only. Do not present probiotics as a substitute for mechanical aligner hygiene; reserve any recommendation until species-resolved trials with longer follow-up and a mechanical-cleaning comparator exist.

## Key Contributions

- First crossover RCT to compare four delivery forms of consumer probiotics for clear aligner biofilm control.
- Dual readout — viable load (CFU/mL, log10) and biofilm matrix (EPS) by CLSM/Concanavalin A.
- Ranks forms: probiotic drink delivered the greatest CFU reduction.

## Methodology

- Randomized 4-period single-blind crossover; n=20 Invisalign users; four 7-day periods with 14-day washouts.
- Interventions (as labelled; no independent strain/viability verification): gummy (*B. coagulans* 1.5×10^9 CFU), rinse (multi-strain 1.5×10^9 CFU/10 mL), capsule (multi-strain 3.0×10^10 CFU), drink (Yakult; *L. paracasei* Shirota ~6.5×10^9 CFU/65 mL).
- Outcome: 5-mm discs from standardized aligner sites → total viable counts (CFU/mL) + EPS/viability by CLSM (Concanavalin A).
- Linear mixed-effects models (treatment/period/sequence fixed, subject random); LS-means (95% CI), Bonferroni-adjusted. Confidence `rct`; abstract-only ingest.

## Results

- All four forms reduced total viable counts and EPS fluorescence vs pre-intervention (P<0.001).
- CFU reductions 0.62–1.06 log10 (mean 3.8–4.3 ×10^3 CFU/mL).
- Probiotic drink largest: LS-mean diff vs baseline 1.055 log10 CFU (95% CI 0.93–1.18; P<0.001).
- CLSM fluorescence reductions 93–145 AU (all P<0.001).
- Preliminary: 7-day periods, label-only content verification, no mechanical-cleaning comparator.
