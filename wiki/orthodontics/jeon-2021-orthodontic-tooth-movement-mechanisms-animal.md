## Summary
Critical review of transgenic animal model studies for orthodontic tooth movement (OTM) mechanisms. Published April 2021, JCM 10:1733, University of Pennsylvania. Summarizes cellular and molecular events established via cause-and-effect from knockout/overexpression animal models.

OTM on compression side: (1) PDL compression 4–7 days, (2) hyalinization 7–14+ days, (3) secondary direct resorption. Tension side: PDL stretch → osteoblastic proliferation → osteoid deposition. Key intercellular signals: RANKL, TNF-α, DKK1, sclerostin, TGF-β, BMPs. Mechanosensors: focal adhesion kinase (FAK), cilia, ion channels.

## Key Contributions
- **Sterile inflammation**: TNF, IL-1, IL-6, PGE₂, MMPs released within hours of force — pathogen-free; required for osteoclastogenesis
- **RANKL**: upregulated by compression; binds RANK on osteoclast precursors → multinucleated osteoclast formation → bone resorption
- **DKK1 (Dickkopf-1)**: Wnt inhibitor expressed on compression side; suppresses osteoblast activity → net resorption
- **Sclerostin (SOST)**: osteocyte-derived Wnt inhibitor; modulates bone formation during OTM
- **TGF-β**: couples resorption to formation; PDL homeostasis
- **BMPs + Runx2**: tension side; osteocalcin and osteopontin upregulated
- **FAK (focal adhesion kinase)**: mechanosensor in PDL cells → prostaglandin secretion upon mechanical deformation
- Transgenic models (conditional knockouts) provide cause-and-effect data not possible from human GCF or in vitro studies

## Methodology
- Critical review of transgenic animal model studies
- Focus on cell types: PDL fibroblasts, MSCs, osteoblasts, osteocytes, osteoclasts
- Cause-and-effect established via conditional knockout and overexpression models

## Results
| Signal | Location | Role |
|---|---|---|
| RANKL | Compression PDL cells | Osteoclast differentiation |
| TNF-α | Compression side | Pro-inflammatory; synergistic RANKL induction |
| DKK1 | Compression side | Wnt inhibition → anti-osteogenic |
| Sclerostin | Osteocytes | Wnt inhibition; bone formation modulation |
| TGF-β | Both sides | Resorption-formation coupling |
| BMP / Runx2 | Tension side | Osteogenesis; matrix protein upregulation |
| FAK | PDL cells | Primary mechanosensor |
