## One-line Summary

SR (10 studies, 6 RCTs; GRADE low): in mild-to-moderate crowding, treatment duration with clear aligners is similar to fixed appliances; no meta-analysis (high inconsistency); duration also driven by patient/treatment factors.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1007/s00784-024-05629-y](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00784-024-05629-y)), this SR resolves part of the umbrella's "conflicting duration" statement by stratifying on case complexity. Across 10 studies (6 RCTs; 9 non-extraction), screened from 3537, the **low-certainty** conclusion is that in **mild-to-moderate crowding** there is **no significant difference** in treatment duration between clear aligners and fixed appliances. High inconsistency precluded meta-analysis, and the authors emphasize that duration is shaped by patient- and treatment-related factors beyond the appliance itself.

Read alongside Jaber (where fixed appliances were shorter in **extraction** cases), the picture is **case-class dependent**: duration parity in simple crowding, FA advantage as complexity/extraction enters. This is the practical answer to the common "are aligners slower?" question — not inherently, for mild-moderate crowding. Ingested abstract-only (paywalled).

## Key Contributions

- Duration parity CA vs FA in mild-to-moderate crowding (low certainty).
- Contrasts with FA duration advantage in extraction cases (case-class dependence).

## Methodology

SR (no MA). 9 databases to Jun 2023; RCTs + matched non-randomized; RoB 2 / ROBINS-I; GRADE. 3537 -> 10 (6 RCTs). Confidence `sr`; abstract-only ingest.

## Results

- No significant CA vs FA duration difference in mild-moderate crowding (GRADE low).
- Duration also driven by patient/treatment factors. MA not feasible (inconsistency).
