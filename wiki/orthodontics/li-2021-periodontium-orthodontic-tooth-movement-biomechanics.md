## Summary
Structured literature review (6,808 → 170 studies after quality/relevance filtering) updating OTM mechanisms for the 2020s decade. IJOS 2021. West China Hospital of Stomatology, Sichuan University.

Proposes new hypothesis: PDL cells (PDLCs) and osteocytes serve as primary mechanosensors in OTM — not previously foregrounded at this level. Organizes OTM into 4 sequential biomechanical/biological steps. Provides clinical implications for optimal orthodontic force (OOF), OTM acceleration, and root resorption prevention.

## Key Contributions
- **4-step sequential OTM model**:
  1. Extracellular mechanobiology: ECM remodeling; collagen fiber compression/tension; PDL fluid dynamics
  2. Cell strain: PDLCs and osteocytes as primary mechanosensors; cilia, integrins, ion channels transduce force
  3. Cell activation/differentiation: RANKL↑ (compression) → osteoclastogenesis; Runx2↑ (tension) → osteogenesis; sterile inflammation (TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-6, PGE₂)
  4. Tissue remodeling: compression side bone resorption + PDL compression; tension side new bone + PDL widening; root resorption risk at excess force
- **Osteocyte role**: newly emphasized as mechanosensors transmitting canalicular signals to osteoclasts/osteoblasts
- **Optimal orthodontic force (OOF)**: sufficient to activate remodeling without sustained hyalinization or root resorption
- **Root resorption prevention**: avoid heavy/continuous forces; CBCT monitoring for high-risk cases
- Research models: in vivo rat/mouse (most common), in vitro PDL cell culture, FEM analytical models

## Methodology
- Initial search 6,808 papers; 2,863 after deduplication; 170 final (excluded low quality/irrelevance)
- Focus on 2010–2021 publications (new decade update)
- Multi-model synthesis across in vivo, in vitro, and FEM analytical models

## Results
| Step | Key Elements |
|---|---|
| Extracellular mechanobiology | ECM/collagen remodeling; PDL fluid shifts |
| Cell strain (sensing) | PDLCs + osteocytes; FAK, cilia, integrins, piezo channels |
| Cell activation | RANKL↑ compression; Runx2↑ tension; TNF-α, IL-1β, PGE₂ sterile inflammation |
| Tissue remodeling | Bone resorption/formation; PDL width dynamics; root resorption |
