## Summary
A systematic review and meta-analysis of 16 RCTs and 30 prospective cohort studies (~3,250 miniscrews from 41 studies in the pooled analysis) estimating the overall failure rate of orthodontic miniscrews at **13.5% (95% CI 11.5–15.9%)**. The review found that **gingival tissue type** (keratinized vs mobile mucosa) and **smoking** were the only statistically significant risk factors. Diameter, length, design, patient age, and jaw of insertion had minimal effect. High heterogeneity across studies warrants cautious interpretation.

## Key Contributions
- Establishes a pooled baseline failure rate of 13.5% for mechanically-retained orthodontic miniscrews
- Identifies keratinized gingival tissue and smoking as the two modifiable/predictive risk factors for failure
- Clears confusion in the literature about the role of diameter and patient age — neither significantly predicts failure
- Provides a methodologically rigorous reference (Cochrane Risk of Bias + Newcastle-Ottawa Scale) for future orthodontic TAD studies

## Methodology
- **Design**: SR + MA; PRISMA guidelines
- **Search**: MEDLINE, Cochrane, Scopus, Ovid up to July 2017; grey literature; hand search
- **Inclusion**: RCTs and prospective cohort studies; English; miniscrew anchorage; diameter <2 mm
- **Studies**: 16 RCTs + 30 PCSs = 46 included; 41 in meta-analysis (5 excluded for insufficient statistics)
- **Sample**: ~3,250 miniscrews
- **Statistics**: Random-effects model; I² and Chi² for heterogeneity; subgroup and sensitivity analyses

## Results
| Risk Factor | Effect on Failure |
|---|---|
| Gingival tissue type | Significant |
| Smoking | Significant |
| Miniscrew diameter | Non-significant |
| Miniscrew length | Non-significant |
| Miniscrew design | Non-significant |
| Patient age | Non-significant |
| Jaw (maxilla vs mandible) | Non-significant |

- **Overall failure rate**: 13.5% (95% CI 11.5–15.9%)
- High I² heterogeneity across included studies
- Conclusion: miniscrews have "acceptably low" failure rate; high-quality large RCTs still needed
