## One-line Summary

SR (19 studies): molar distalization is achievable with clear aligners alone, but anterior anchorage loss is highly probable; predictability and stability improve markedly with skeletal anchorage (TADs) and IPR — a hybrid approach gives the best success, especially with large overjet.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.3390/bioengineering10121390](https://doi.org/10.3390/bioengineering10121390)), this SR addresses one of the movements where the Yassir umbrella flagged aligners as weakest: distalization. Screening 146 studies to 19 included, it reframes predictability as an **anchorage** problem rather than an intrinsic appliance limit.

Distalization **can** be achieved with aligners alone, but reciprocal **anterior anchorage loss is highly probable** — the planned posterior movement is partly spent proclining/advancing the anterior segment, widening the predicted-vs-achieved gap. The fix is biomechanical reinforcement: **temporary anchorage devices (TADs)** for direct/indirect skeletal anchorage plus **interproximal reduction (IPR)** to manage overjet, with the **hybrid aligner+TAD** approach producing the greatest success. Patients with large overjet are explicitly high-risk for needing midcourse corrections if distalization (especially of maxillary molars) is planned without auxiliaries.

Clinically this is a case-selection and mechanics rule: aligner distalization is predictable when staged with TAD anchorage and IPR, unpredictable when relying on the aligner and Class II elastics alone in demanding (large-overjet) cases. The reliance on TADs links this directly to miniscrew-stability evidence already in the wiki.

## Key Contributions

- Recasts distalization predictability as an anchorage-management problem.
- TADs + IPR identified as predictability multipliers; hybrid approach best.
- Large overjet flagged as high-risk for midcourse correction.

## Methodology

SR (no MA). Scopus/WoS/PubMed, last 10 years; 146 -> 19 studies. Qualitative synthesis of distalization, anchorage, auxiliaries. Confidence `sr`; PMC OA full text.

## Results

- Aligner-only distalization possible but anterior anchorage loss probable.
- TADs (direct/indirect) + IPR improve stability and reduce side effects.
- Hybrid aligner+TAD = greatest success; large overjet needs auxiliaries.
