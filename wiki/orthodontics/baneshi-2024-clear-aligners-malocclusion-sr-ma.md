## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 21 RCTs (n=970): no significant difference between clear aligners and fixed appliances in occlusal outcome (ABO/LII/PAR) for simple non-extraction malocclusions; CA significantly better on plaque/gingival/bleeding indices and 6-month QoL; evidence quality low-to-moderate.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1016/j.jebdp.2024.102081](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jebdp.2024.102081)), this RCT-only SR+MA is the quantitative backbone for the efficacy claim that the Yassir umbrella states narratively. Restricting inclusion to 21 randomized trials (970 participants) and grading with RoB 2 raises the evidence bar above mixed-design reviews.

On the primary outcome — occlusal correction quality measured by ABO objective grading, Little Irregularity Index, and PAR — there was **no significant difference** between clear aligners and fixed appliances in simple, non-extraction malocclusions. Meta-analysis was feasible only for secondary outcomes, where aligners showed a consistent periodontal advantage: plaque index **MD -0.76 (95% CI -1.14, -0.38)**, gingival index **-0.61 (-0.78, -0.44)**, bleeding index **-0.71 (-0.92, -0.49)** — all favoring CA — plus better **6-month quality of life (MD -4.37; -6.93, -1.80)** and favorable trends for chairside time, pain, and root resorption. The authors caution the evidence is **low** quality for periodontal outcomes and **moderate** for QoL, with most trials at unclear risk of bias and complex malocclusions under-represented.

Clinically: for the mild-to-moderate non-extraction case, aligners deliver occlusal results equivalent to braces while reducing plaque/gingival/bleeding burden and improving early QoL — the periodontal/comfort edge, not occlusal superiority, is the differentiator. Complex cases remain an evidence gap. Ingested abstract-only (paywalled).

## Key Contributions

- RCT-only, RoB 2: equivalence of occlusal outcome CA vs FA in simple non-extraction cases.
- Pooled periodontal effect sizes favoring CA (plaque/gingival/bleeding).
- 6-month QoL advantage quantified.

## Methodology

SR+MA, RCTs only. Six databases to Oct 2024; RoB 2; RevMan 5.4. 600 records -> 21 RCTs, n=970. MA limited to secondary outcomes. Confidence `sr+ma`; abstract-only ingest.

## Results

- Occlusal (ABO/LII/PAR): no significant CA vs FA difference.
- Plaque MD -0.76 (-1.14,-0.38); gingival -0.61 (-0.78,-0.44); bleeding -0.71 (-0.92,-0.49) favor CA.
- 6-mo QoL MD -4.37 (-6.93,-1.80) favor CA; pain/chairside/root resorption favor CA.
- Evidence low (perio) to moderate (QoL).
