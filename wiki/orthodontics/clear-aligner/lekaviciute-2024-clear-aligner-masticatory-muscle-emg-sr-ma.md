## One-line Summary

SR+MA (6 studies, 108 adults, NOS low–moderate RoB; PROSPERO CRD42024522231): clear aligner treatment transiently raised masseter sEMG at maximal clench then it fell, with mixed temporalis/resting responses, but the pooled meta-analysis found a NON-significant difference in masseter and temporalis activity (effect size 0.20 and 0.24, both CIs cross 0) — evidence too thin for firm conclusions.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1093/ejo/cjae030](https://doi.org/10.1093/ejo/cjae030)), this systematic review and meta-analysis (European Journal of Orthodontics 2024;46(4); PROSPERO CRD42024522231) pooled six studies (2 prospective cohort, 3 observational longitudinal, 1 observational longitudinal case–control; 108 adults, mean age 22–35) to ask whether clear aligner treatment (CAT) alters masseter and anterior-temporalis activity measured by surface electromyography (sEMG). Risk of bias was low–moderate (Newcastle-Ottawa Scale); all six entered the meta-analysis.

Narratively the muscle response was task- and time-dependent. During maximal voluntary clenching (MVC), masseter sEMG showed a significant initial increase (P < .05) that then decayed to a non-significant decrease, while temporalis was mixed (two studies significant increase, one no change). The single study measuring submaximal voluntary clenching (SMVC) found a consistent significant masseter reduction (RMS ~−20%, P < .01) even as temporalis rose. At the mandibular resting position results split (one significant masseter decrease, one significant increase, one no change). Crucially, the pooled random-effects meta-analysis found a NON-significant difference for both muscles (effect size masseter 0.20, P = .078; temporalis 0.24, P = .128; both 95% CIs contain 0), with low heterogeneity and no detectable publication bias.

Clinically: clear aligners produce only transient, task-specific neuromuscular adaptations of the masseter/temporalis that do not amount to a statistically reliable net change, and (per the one comparative study) parameters return to normal range within ~3 months after treatment — reassuring against a sustained masticatory-muscle penalty, but the evidence base is too small and heterogeneous for a firm verdict, so larger standardized trials are needed before counselling patients definitively.

## Key Contributions

- First SR+MA to quantitatively POOL masseter/temporalis sEMG across CAT, upgrading prior qualitative reviews to a meta-analytic effect size.
- Bottom line: pooled difference NON-significant for both masseter (ES 0.20, P = .078) and temporalis (ES 0.24, P = .128).
- Disaggregates response by clenching task (MVC, SMVC, resting position), exposing that CAT effects are direction-variable depending on the test condition.
- Robustness: low heterogeneity (masseter I² = 14.99%; temporalis I² = 45.53%), no small-study/publication bias (Egger P = .237 / P = .949), and no moderating effect of mean age, study quality, or study design.

## Methodology

- **Design**: PRISMA 2020 SR+MA; PROSPERO CRD42024522231; PICO = adults under CAT, masseter/temporalis sEMG outcomes.
- **Search**: PubMed, Web of Science, SCOPUS, Cochrane, Google Scholar to March 2024; English only; aligner thickness 0.7–0.8 mm inclusion. 2418 → 6 included (3 independent reviewers + arbiter).
- **RoB**: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (5 cohort + 1 case–control); low–moderate.
- **Analysis**: random-effects model (RStudio); Tau²/I²/Q heterogeneity, forest + funnel plots, Egger's test, leave-one-out sensitivity, moderator/confounder tests.

## Results

- **MVC — masseter**: significant initial increase (P < .05) → non-significant decrease over time (Lou peak at passive/first active aligner then drop; Paes-Souza ~20% net rise at 32 wk); 2 studies no change.
- **MVC — temporalis**: mixed; significant increase in 2 (Paes-Souza ~70%; Liu at 3 mo), no change in 1.
- **SMVC** (1 study, Paes-Souza): masseter significant reduction (RMS −20%; right 108.6→88.7, left 87.8→68.1); temporalis instead +90% (P = .000).
- **Resting position**: masseter mixed — Nota significant decrease at 1 mo, Paes-Souza significant increase ~30% at 8 mo, Liu no change; temporalis 2 increases + 1 no change.
- **POC / indices**: significant POC changes (Dellavia, Tepedino); TC and Asym generally stable; values normalized 3 mo post-treatment.
- **CAT vs fixed appliances** (Dellavia): significant masseter POC change only in fixed group, not CAT; both normalized by 3 mo.
- **Pooled MA**: masseter ES 0.20 (P = .078), temporalis ES 0.24 (P = .128) — NON-significant (CIs cross 0). Sensitivity: Lou influential (masseter), Liu influential (temporalis).
- Clinically: transient, reversible neuromuscular adaptation; no statistically reliable net masseter/temporalis change attributable to clear aligners on current evidence.
