## One-line Summary

First RCT-only SR (7 RCTs, n=266) of physical adjunctive interventions (vibration, LLLT) in clear aligner treatment: no consistent benefit on alignment (Little's Irregularity Index MD 0.08 maxilla p=0.869, 0.44 mandible p=0.487), case completion (p=0.999), compliance (~77%, p=0.390) or QoL; only transient day-1–3 pain reduction; GRADE low to very low.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1371/journal.pone.0346566](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0346566)), this is the first systematic review to focus exclusively on physical adjunctive interventions (PAIs) — vibration devices and low-level laser therapy (LLLT) — used to accelerate or improve clear aligner treatment (CAT), restricting the evidence base to human RCTs and applying outcome-level RoB 2 plus GRADE. Seven RCTs (n=266; six vibration, one LLLT), all at the leveling/alignment stage, were synthesized narratively because clinical and methodological heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis.

Across six outcome tracks the signal is consistently null. Vibration produced **no improvement in alignment** (Little's Irregularity Index MD 0.08 maxilla, p=0.869; 0.44 mandible, p=0.487) and **no difference in case completion** under a 7-day change schedule (p=0.999). Where benefits appeared — better tracking under a 7-day schedule (Alansari p=0.003), shorter total time (Pescheret), maintained fit with LLLT (Caccianiga) — they tracked the **accelerated aligner-change cadence**, not the device itself; the authors repeatedly note cadence and adherence are stronger determinants than the adjunct. **Compliance** showed no active-vs-sham difference (~77% mean, p=0.390) and declined over time. **Pain** was lower only transiently on days 1–3 (p<0.05), with no durable analgesic effect and no QoL (OHIP-14) benefit. **Periodontal indices** (PI/GI/BOP) were unchanged and **biomarkers** (GCF, RANKL/OPG) inconsistent. GRADE certainty was low (periodontal) to very low (all other domains), downgraded for risk of bias, inconsistency, indirectness, and imprecision.

Clinically: do not expect a vibration device or LLLT to shorten clear aligner treatment, improve final alignment, boost compliance, or meaningfully relieve discomfort beyond the first few days — current randomized evidence does not support routine use of PAIs in CAT, and protocol discipline (change interval, wear-time adherence) matters more than any adjunct.

## Key Contributions

- First RCT-only SR isolating PAIs (vibration + LLLT) in CAT, with **outcome-level** RoB 2 rather than study-level (avoids masking outcome-specific bias).
- Demonstrates that apparent acceleration/tracking gains are confounded by aligner-change cadence and adherence, not the device.
- Provides GRADE certainty (low to very low) across six predefined outcome tracks; PROSPERO-registered (CRD420251132229).

## Methodology

- RCTs only (parallel or split-mouth), healthy CAT patients; PAIs vs no-adjunct or sham. Six outcome tracks: objective tooth movement, process/completion, compliance, PROs, biomarkers, periodontal indices.
- Search inception→2025-06-30 (run 2025-08-25): PubMed, Embase, Scopus, WoS, CENTRAL + TRIP, Google Scholar, grey literature, four core ortho journals; no language/date limits.
- 357 records → 217 deduped → 14 full-text → **7 RCTs, n=266**.
- Synthesis narrative (SWiM); no meta-analysis (heterogeneity). RoB 2 outcome-level; GRADE per domain.

## Results

- **Alignment (GRADE very low)**: Little's Irregularity Index — no AcceleDent effect; MD 0.08 maxilla (p=0.869), 0.44 mandible (p=0.487).
- **Efficiency vs accuracy (Bragassa)**: 4-day schedule ↑PCPDI efficiency (18.9%→23.8%→29.1% with vibration, p=0.003) but ↓accuracy (incisor alignment 48%→36%/37%, p=0.023/0.047).
- **Tracking (Alansari)**: 7-day + vibration > 7-day sham (p=0.003), matched 14-day control; 7-day sham underperformed.
- **Completion (Katchooi)**: weekly-regimen completion identical active vs sham (p=0.999).
- **Time**: Pescheret shorter with vibration (0–12 mo p=0.010) but confounded by 7-day cadence + grey-literature thesis; Caccianiga LLLT completed 40±2 wk (correctly-fitted aligners 22.1±1.0 vs 3.6±0.8, p=0.001) vs control discontinued.
- **Compliance**: no active–sham difference (p=0.390); mean ~77%, declines over time.
- **Pain/QoL**: transient day-1–3 reduction only (p<0.05); no durable analgesia; no OHIP-14 benefit (p>0.05).
- **Periodontal (GRADE low)**: PI/GI/BOP unchanged (p>0.05). **Biomarkers (very low)**: inconsistent RANKL/OPG and GCF signals.
- **Overall**: GRADE low to very low; no consistent, clinically meaningful adjunct-specific benefit.
