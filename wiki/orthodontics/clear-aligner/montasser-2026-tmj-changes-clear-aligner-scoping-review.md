## One-line Summary

Scoping review (PRISMA-ScR) of 4 retrospective CBCT studies: after clear-aligner treatment, condylar position and joint spaces stay largely unchanged in adult Class I (extraction + non-extraction) and Class II non-extraction; Class II div 2 non-extraction shows forward condylar repositioning; adolescent Class II div 1 functional aligners show growth-driven changes. All 4 studies serious RoB (ROBINS-I) — evidence exploratory, not confirmatory.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1186/s12903-026-08414-3](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12903-026-08414-3)), this scoping review maps the CBCT evidence on temporomandibular joint (TMJ) morphological and positional change after clear-aligner (CA) orthodontic treatment — the structural counterpart to the masticatory/stomatognathic question covered in companion aligner reviews. Using PRISMA-ScR and a PCC framework, it screened 320 records down to **4 retrospective comparative studies** (all from China, July 2023–Jan 2025), assessed risk of bias with ROBINS-I, and synthesized narratively because heterogeneity was too high for meta-analysis.

The primary outcomes were condylar position and joint spaces; secondary outcomes were condylar dimensions/inclination and mandibular fossa dimensions, all measured on CBCT (T0 vs T1). In adults, CA produced **no significant change in condylar position or joint spaces** in skeletal Class I (both non-extraction and extraction) and in Class II non-extraction treated by molar distalization — and where fixed appliances (FA) did show intra-group changes, the CA-vs-FA intergroup differences were mostly non-significant. The exception was **Class II division 2**, where initially posteriorly seated condyles moved forward toward the fossa centre in both CA and FA (Pullinger posterior fraction falling from ~61% to ~14% with CA), with the CA group additionally showing increased superior joint space and glenoid-fossa depth (interpreted as adaptive remodeling). In adolescent Class II division 1, functional clear aligners and Twin-Block both produced significant condylar-dimension and fossa changes — a growth-modification effect rather than an appliance-specific one. The authors stress all 4 studies carry **serious risk of bias** and institutional clustering, and none included clinical TMD outcomes (pain, sounds, function).

Clinically: current CBCT evidence is reassuring but weak — for routine adult Class I and Class II non-extraction aligner cases there is no signal of pathological condylar displacement, while Class II div 2 and adolescent growth cases see meaningful (largely adaptive/growth-driven) condylar change that is not unique to aligners. Treat these findings as exploratory; do not infer TMD risk or protection from them.

## Key Contributions

- First scoping review dedicated to TMJ change after clear-aligner treatment, defining the CBCT outcome domains (position, joint spaces, condylar dimensions/inclination, fossa).
- Exposes the thin, clustered evidence base: 4 retrospective studies, all serious RoB, no RCTs, no clinical TMD measures.
- Separates adult (limited growth) from adolescent (growth modification) effects and contrasts Class I / Class II non-extraction (stable) vs Class II div 2 (forward repositioning).

## Methodology

PRISMA-ScR scoping review; PCC eligibility (CA orthodontic patients; TMJ change; RCT/CCT/retrospective comparative designs). Six sources searched (MEDLINE, Scopus, WoS, ProQuest, ClinicalTrials.gov, WHO ICTRP), 2025-05-27, updated 2025-11-17. 320 → 287 (de-dup) → 6 (title/abstract) → 4 included. ROBINS-I for risk of bias; descriptive synthesis only (high heterogeneity); publication-bias test not run (<10 studies). Confidence `sr`; full text via PMC.

## Results

- **Included (n=4, all retrospective, CBCT, China):** Al-Somairi 2025 (adult Class I, FA 30 vs CA 30, extraction + non-extraction); Al-Worafi 2024 (adult Class II, FA 35 vs CA 35, molar distalization); Zheng 2023 (adult Class II div 2, FA 25 vs CA 22); Zhang 2024 (adolescent Class II div 1, Twin-Block 24 vs functional CA 25).
- **Class I adult (non-extraction + extraction):** CA — no significant intra-group change in condylar position or joint spaces; CA-vs-FA intergroup differences mostly non-significant (extraction subgroup: APCP, APCJP differed between groups).
- **Class II adult non-extraction:** no significant condylar-position or joint-space change in either CA or FA.
- **Class II division 2 adult:** posterior-condyle fraction fell ~61%→14% (CA) and ~68%→24% (FA) → forward repositioning in both; CA alone increased superior joint space + glenoid-fossa depth (adaptive remodeling).
- **Adolescent Class II div 1 (functional CA vs Twin-Block):** condyles posterior→neutral in both; significant increases in condylar dimensions and fossa width/depth in both; greater CH and APDC change in Twin-Block — growth modification, not appliance-specific.
- **Condylar inclination:** negligible/non-significant between CA and comparators across all types.
- **Risk of bias:** all 4 serious (ROBINS-I); institutional clustering; no clinical TMD outcomes; CBCT changes <1 mm may be measurement error.
