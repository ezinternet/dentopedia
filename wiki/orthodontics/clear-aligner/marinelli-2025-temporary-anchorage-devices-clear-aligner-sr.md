## One-line Summary

Systematic review (PRISMA, 14 studies — mostly case reports/small series; ROBINS-I: 1 low / 9 some-concerns / 4 high risk of bias) concluding that combining clear aligners with temporary anchorage devices (TADs/miniscrews) improves precision and control of anchorage-demanding movements (distalization, intrusion, extrusion, vertical control), but the evidence is low-certainty with no controlled comparisons and few RCTs.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰 (PRISMA, 14편 — 대부분 증례보고/소규모 시리즈; ROBINS-I 비뚤림: 1편 낮음 / 9편 일부우려 / 4편 높음): 투명교정장치 (Clear Aligner)에 임시고정원장치 (Temporary Anchorage Device, TAD; 미니스크류)를 병용하면 후방이동·압하·정출·수직조절 등 고정원이 많이 필요한 치아이동의 정밀도·조절력이 향상되나, 대조군이 없고 무작위배정연구가 거의 없어 근거 수준은 낮다.

## Summary

This PRISMA systematic review (PROSPERO CRD42024576712) addresses a gap left by prior reviews that examined clear aligners or skeletal anchorage **separately**: it synthesizes the literature on the **combined aligner + TAD modality**. From 458 records across PubMed/Scopus/Web of Science (2004–2024), 14 studies met eligibility — overwhelmingly case reports and small case series, reflecting how recently the two techniques have been integrated clinically.

The clinical thesis: clear aligners alone are biomechanically weak for movements that demand strong, tooth-independent anchorage. TADs (titanium miniscrews placed in palatal/alveolar bone under local anesthetic) supply that anchorage and, applied via coil springs, elastics, or direct attachment, let aligners deliver controlled forces for **molar distalization, intrusion, extrusion, and vertical control** while reducing reciprocal/anchorage-loss movements and aligner stages. The review maps a broad indication set — Class III lower-molar distalization toward functional Class I, Class II non-extraction correction, anterior open-bite, alveolar protrusion, hyperdivergent vertical control, impacted-canine traction, dental asymmetry, and surgery-first skeletal-discrepancy cases.

The crucial caveat is methodological. A ROBINS-I appraisal rated only 1 of 14 studies at low risk of bias (9 some-concerns, 4 high), with bias driven by confounding and selective reporting. No meta-analysis was possible (heterogeneous designs/protocols/outcomes). The conclusion is therefore directional, not confirmatory: the modality is **promising** but rests on weak evidence pending large, standardized, controlled prospective studies.

## Key Contributions

- Dedicated synthesis of the **aligner + TAD combination** (not aligners or TADs alone) across the full range of indications.
- Indication map of where TADs rescue aligner-weak movements: distalization, intrusion/vertical control, extrusion, open-bite, asymmetry, surgery-first cases.
- Transparent ROBINS-I risk-of-bias appraisal making the weakness of the current evidence base explicit (1 low / 9 some-concerns / 4 high).
- Research agenda: comparative effectiveness, patient-reported outcomes, aligner material behavior under load with TADs, long-term stability, and standardized case-selection/placement protocols.

## Methodology

- **Design**: PRISMA systematic review, qualitative synthesis only (no meta-analysis). PROSPERO CRD42024576712.
- **Search**: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science; English; 1 Jan 2004 – 17 Jul 2024. Boolean (aligners/clear aligners/transparent aligners/Invisalign) AND (TADs/miniscrews/skeletal anchorage/micro-implants/anchorage systems).
- **Eligibility (PICOS)**: clinical studies (RCT, retrospective, case series, case reports) with **both** aligners and TADs; full text English. Excluded: animal, in vitro, off-topic, reviews/letters/comments, non-English.
- **Flow**: 458 records → 369 de-duplicated → 355 excluded (348 off-topic, 7 reviews) → **14 included**.
- **Process**: three independent reviewers (Zotero v6.0.15), senior-reviewer adjudication; quality via **ROBINS-I** (7 domains), two reviewers + third-reviewer adjudication.

## Results

- **14 studies** synthesized qualitatively; predominantly case reports / small series.
- **Risk of bias (ROBINS-I)**: 1 low, 9 some concerns, 4 high (remainder unclear). High bias from confounding and selective reporting; measurement and participant-selection domains mostly low risk.
- **Clinical signal**: aligner + TAD improves precision/control for distalization, intrusion, extrusion, and vertical control; reduces aligner stages and reciprocal movements; high patient satisfaction from aligner aesthetics/comfort. Challenges: precise miniscrew placement, insertion discomfort.
- **Representative cases**: Greco (G-Block posterior skeletal anchorage post-distalization; impacted canines), Lu (preset intrusion bulbs, hyperdivergent Class II, 4-yr stability), Auladell (mini-implant molar distalization), Kottemann (dental asymmetry), Ojima (non-extraction Class II), Lin (3D mandibular-molar distalization), Iodice (gummy smile / mandibular retrognathia, surgery-first), De Almeida (aligner-with-TSAD forces; PETG/polyurethane thermoplastics).
- **Bottom line**: directionally favorable but **low-certainty**; no controlled comparisons, few RCTs; conclusions cautious and non-generalizable.
