## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 9 studies (1 RCT + 8 NRSI, n=465): clear aligner mandibular advancement (CAMA) achieves skeletal effects comparable to Twin Block and Herbst (SNA/SNB/ANB, vertical all NS); CAMA gives marginally greater overjet reduction (MD -0.46 mm, clinically minimal) and significantly less lower-incisor proclination (IMPA MD -0.90°, P=0.0002); certainty low (mostly retrospective).

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1186/s12903-026-08175-z](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12903-026-08175-z)), this SR+MA pooled 9 studies (1 RCT + 8 non-randomized studies of interventions, n=465 growing skeletal Class II patients, mean age 11.2-13.0 y) to test whether clear aligner mandibular advancement (CAMA — aligners with precision-wing/MA modules) matches traditional functional appliances. Across both major comparators, Twin Block and Herbst, the meta-analysis found **no statistically significant difference in any sagittal skeletal parameter** (SNA, SNB, ANB, Wits) or in vertical dimensions, indicating CAMA delivers an equivalent orthopedic mandibular response. Against untreated controls, CAMA produced genuine skeletal change (SNB MD +1.00°, P=0.003; ANB MD -1.55° after removing an outlier, P<0.00001), confirming it functions as a true functional appliance rather than a dental-alignment-only device.

The differentiator is dental control. CAMA produced a marginally greater overjet reduction than Twin Block (MD -0.46 mm, P=0.03) — statistically significant but the authors stress it is clinically minimal — and, more meaningfully, **significantly less lower-incisor proclination** (IMPA MD -0.90°, P=0.0002). The proposed mechanism is full-crown encapsulation moving the force closer to the center of resistance and generating a counter-moment that resists the labial tipping that occlusal inclined planes provoke. The certainty of evidence is **low**: only one RCT (high RoB), most studies retrospective (5 of 8 NRSI high RoB by ROBINS-I), high heterogeneity in some skeletal parameters, and physiologic pubertal growth confounding appliance effect.

Clinically: in a growing Class II patient with mandibular retrognathism, CAMA is a reasonable functional-appliance alternative — it achieves comparable skeletal correction to Twin Block/Herbst while better protecting the lower incisors from compensatory proclination (relevant to dehiscence/recession risk and to preserving the skeletal-correction signal). The overjet edge is not a real clinical advantage; treat the choice as driven by incisor torque control, comfort/esthetics, and concurrent alignment, not by skeletal superiority. Decisions should be cautious until larger RCTs confirm.

## Key Contributions

- First MA to compare CAMA head-to-head against BOTH Twin Block and Herbst for growing skeletal Class II.
- Establishes skeletal-orthopedic equivalence across all sagittal and vertical parameters.
- Quantifies CAMA's lower-incisor torque-control advantage (IMPA MD -0.90°).
- Confirms CAMA produces true skeletal change vs untreated controls (SNB, ANB).

## Methodology

- SR+MA, PRISMA 2020, PROSPERO CRD420251050592.
- PubMed/Embase/Web of Science/Cochrane CENTRAL to Dec 31 2025; PICOS framework.
- RoB 2 (RCT) + ROBINS-I (NRSI); RevMan 5.4, random-effects WMD/SMD; leave-one-out + high-RoB-exclusion sensitivity analyses.
- 1589 records -> 60 full text -> 9 included (1 RCT + 8 NRSI), n=465. CAMA: Invisalign MA, Angelalign A6, Clear Twin Block; comparators: Twin Block, Herbst, Vanbeek Activator, untreated.

## Results

- **CAMA vs Twin Block (skeletal):** all NS — SNA MD 0.01 (-0.66, 0.68); SNB MD -0.09 (-0.93, 0.74); ANB MD -0.10 (-0.36, 0.15, I²=0%); Wits MD -0.15 mm (I²=98%).
- **CAMA vs Herbst (skeletal):** all NS, I²=0% — SNA MD 0.13; SNB MD -0.50; ANB MD 0.53.
- **CAMA vs untreated control:** SNB MD +1.00° (P=0.003); ANB MD -1.55° (P<0.00001, post leave-one-out); Wits MD -2.51 mm (P=0.05); SNA NS.
- **Overjet:** vs TB MD -0.46 mm (P=0.03, I²=0%) favoring CAMA but clinically minimal; vs control MD -2.16 mm, NS (P=0.06).
- **Lower-incisor inclination (IMPA):** vs TB MD -0.90° (-1.38, -0.42; P=0.0002, I²=43%) — CAMA less proclination.
- **Vertical:** all NS — overbite vs TB MD -1.04 mm; ANS-Me vs TB MD -0.20 mm; overbite vs control MD -0.68 mm.
- **Certainty:** low — 1 RCT (high RoB), 5/8 NRSI high RoB, growth confounding.
