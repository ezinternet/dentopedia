## One-line Summary

Systematic review (7 in-vitro/in-vivo studies, PRISMA/QUIN) concluding clear aligners are generally safe but with residual concern over thermoplastic chemical leaching (BPA below toxic limits), thermoforming-increased cytotoxicity, and moderate cytotoxicity in some materials (notably Biolon, 64.6% viability; 5 of 7 studies judged safe).

## Summary

This is the first systematic review to isolate **clear aligners alone** (excluding retainers, expanders, and other multi-material orthodontic devices that previous reviews bundled in) to answer one question: do the thermoplastic materials release toxic chemicals that harm the oral cavity? Following PRISMA, the authors screened 413 records down to **7 included in-vitro/in-vivo studies**, appraised with the QUIN Tool.

The overall verdict is reassuring but qualified: **5 of the 7 studies concluded clear aligners are safe for clinical use, while 2 reported some degree of cytotoxicity.** BPA is detectable from some aligners but generally within regulatory safety limits; the open questions are long-term cumulative exposure (especially adolescents and pregnant women) and BPA-free substitutes (BPS, BPF) that may carry similar endocrine-disruptor risk. Cytotoxicity is driven by leaching of residual monomers/additives, surface degradation, oxidative stress (ROS), and inflammatory responses — all amplified by **thermoforming** and by an **acidic/hot intraoral environment**, motivating the clinical advice to avoid hot or acidic drinks while aligners are worn.

## Key Contributions

- Aligner-only scope: removes the confounding of metal/acrylic devices present in prior toxicity reviews.
- Ranks commercial aligner materials by cell viability (Duran best, Biolon worst).
- Identifies **thermoforming as a cytotoxicity amplifier** (notably TPU and Zendura).
- Synthesizes the BPA / bisphenol-substitute leaching evidence and the pH/temperature modifiers that worsen it.
- Connects material toxicity to the broader oral-health picture (xerostomia from reduced saliva flow, biofilm accumulation, gingival irritation).

## Methodology

- **Design**: systematic review, PRISMA-guided, no meta-analysis (heterogeneous lab studies).
- **Databases**: PubMed/Medline (367), Scopus (31), Cochrane (15) — searched 21–22 Sept 2024.
- **Search**: (clear aligners OR Invisalign) AND (biocompatibility OR toxicity OR biological effects); human, English/Portuguese, 2014–2024.
- **Screening**: Rayyan; two reviewers (PL, MF) + adjudicator (AG); Cohen's Kappa.
- **Quality**: QUIN Tool (12-item in-vitro quality assessment) — all but two studies (Yu 2022, Yan 2024) met most criteria.
- **Flow**: 413 → 19 duplicates → 360 excluded on title/abstract → 34 full-text → 27 excluded → **7 included**.

## Results

**Cell viability by material (lower = more cytotoxic; Martina 2019 unless noted):**

| Material | Cell viability | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Duran | 84.6% | Highest viability, lowest cytotoxicity |
| SmartTrack (Invisalign) | 78.8% | |
| Zendura | 74.4% | |
| Biolon | 64.6% | **Most cytotoxic — clinical caution** |
| CA1 / CA2 thermoplastics (Dinu 2024) | >82% | Safe per EpiDerm model |

- **Alhendi 2022**: dose-dependent drop in human gingival fibroblast viability across commercial aligners (Invisalign, SureSmile) → slight-to-moderate, dose-responsive toxicity.
- **Nemec 2023**: saliva from Invisalign patients did not impair hGF/oral-epithelial viability or proliferation and did not raise inflammatory mediators (contrast: bracket patients); minimal before/after differences.
- **Yu 2022 / Yan 2024**: low cytotoxicity, preserved morphology (TPU; fluoride-coated antibacterial/remineralizing aligner).
- **Thermoforming** significantly increases cytotoxicity (esp. TPU, Zendura); **acidic pH** lowers keratinocyte confluence (Dinu 2024).
- **BPA** generally within regulatory limits; substitutes BPS/BPF may pose similar risk.
- **Bottom line**: generally safe; residual concerns = thermoplastic chemical leaching, bacterial accumulation from reduced saliva flow, mild inflammatory responses. Clinical advice: avoid hot/acidic beverages while wearing aligners, clean with non-abrasive agents, follow replacement schedule, maintain meticulous hygiene; consider material composition for sensitive/periodontal patients.
