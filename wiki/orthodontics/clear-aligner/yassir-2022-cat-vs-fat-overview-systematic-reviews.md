## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1007/s00784-021-04361-1](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00784-021-04361-1)), this is the highest-level synthesis available on whether clear aligner treatment (CAT) matches fixed appliance treatment (FAT): an overview of systematic reviews (umbrella review) pooling 18 SRs after screening 361. It functions as the anchor that bounds the three sweep subtopics — efficacy, complex/extraction, and adverse effects.

Its central message is a severity boundary: aligners are clinically effective for **mild-to-moderate** malocclusion treated largely non-extraction, but produce **inferior** outcomes in severe cases and for specific tooth movements that aligners struggle to express — anterior torque, extrusion, and rotation. Across secondary domains the trade-offs are consistent with mechanism: **periodontal health is better** with CAT (removable, better hygiene access), **root-resorption risk tends to be lower**, but **relapse is greater** and treatment-duration evidence is conflicting (possibly shorter in mild-moderate). Pain data are unclear, though patients report CAT as more comfortable with less impact on eating/chewing. The authors rate the overall evidence as **moderate** and call for high-quality RCTs, especially in severe cases.

Clinically this sets the case-selection rule the rest of the aligner literature elaborates: aligners as a legitimate first-line option in mild-to-moderate crowding/spacing, with caution (auxiliaries, attachments, possibly fixed appliances) where torque, extrusion, rotation, or severe discrepancy dominate. Ingested abstract-only (paywalled, no PMC OA).

## Key Contributions

- SR-of-SRs: bounds CAT effectiveness vs FAT at the severity/movement level.
- Aligners effective mild-to-moderate; inferior for severe cases and torque/extrusion/rotation.
- Periodontal health better and root resorption lower with CAT; relapse greater.

## Methodology

Overview of systematic reviews (PROSPERO CRD42021246855). Search to 15 Jul 2021; 361 reviews -> 18 included after excluding non-relevant/low-quality; two-author screening/quality/extraction; narrative synthesis of moderate/high-quality reviews. Confidence `sr`; abstract-only ingest.

## Results

- Effective for mild-to-moderate malocclusion; inferior for severe and for torque/extrusion/rotation.
- Periodontal health better with CAT; root-resorption risk tended lower.
- Relapse greater with CAT; duration conflicting (maybe shorter in mild-moderate).
- CAT more comfortable; pain evidence unclear. Overall evidence moderate.
