## One-line Summary

SR+MA (9 CBCT studies) — clear aligners cause minimal but measurable EARR: overall incisor root length −0.56 mm and upper-incisor root volume −13.34 mm³, greatest at maxillary central incisors (−0.74 mm).

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (CBCT 9편) — 투명교정(clear aligner)은 경미하지만 측정 가능한 치근 흡수(EARR)를 유발: 전체 절치 치근길이 −0.56 mm, 상악 절치 치근부피 −13.34 mm³, 상악 중절치(−0.74 mm)에서 최대.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1016/j.identj.2024.04.008](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.identj.2024.04.008)), this systematic review and meta-analysis pooled 9 CBCT studies (from 16 qualitatively reviewed; 638 patients, 6524 teeth) to quantify the **absolute** amount of external apical root resorption (EARR) caused by comprehensive clear aligner treatment using pre- versus post-treatment values. This is the key methodological advance: earlier reviews only compared aligners against fixed appliances — a confounded contrast, since the two techniques differ in indication, duration, and force mechanism — so the true magnitude of aligner-induced resorption had never been isolated.

The pooled overall incisor root length decreased significantly by **−0.56 mm** (95% CI −0.73, −0.38; P < .00001), with the **maxillary central incisor** the worst-affected tooth at −0.74 mm. Significant reductions were confined to maxillary central incisors, maxillary lateral incisors, and mandibular central incisors; canines and mandibular lateral incisors trended toward loss but were non-significant amid high heterogeneity (I² 62–80%). A 3D root-volume sub-analysis (3 studies) showed upper-incisor volume loss of **−13.34 mm³** (95% CI −16.57, −10.10; P < .00001), confirming the central > lateral incisor pattern. Notably every patient had at least one tooth with detectable resorption, but severe resorption (>20% root loss) affected only ~3.7–6.3% of teeth.

Clinically: aligners do resorb roots, but the 0.56 mm mean is not clinically meaningful — reassuring for routine cases. The actionable signal is to **monitor maxillary central and lateral incisors most closely**, especially when planning intrusion/extrusion, extraction-space closure, severe-crowding correction, or movements that bring the root apex toward the palatal cortical plate, all flagged as resorption amplifiers. Use CBCT, not 2D panoramic/periapical, when an accurate amount of resorption must be quantified.

## Key Contributions

- First SR+MA to report the **absolute** amount of clear-aligner EARR from pre/post CBCT (not an aligner-vs-fixed relative comparison).
- Quantified overall incisor root-length loss at **−0.56 mm** and upper-incisor root-volume loss at **−13.34 mm³**.
- Localized the highest resorption to the **maxillary central incisor** (−0.74 mm; sensitivity-tested to −0.64 mm), confirmed by 3D volume data.
- Demonstrated CBCT superiority over 2D radiography (2D ~0.2 mm overestimate; panoramic RCR data too heterogeneous to pool).
- Catalogued risk-factor evidence: vertical movement, extraction, severe crowding, and root-apex proximity to palatal cortex increase EARR; age/sex/duration inconsistent.

## Methodology

- **Design**: SR+MA; PROSPERO CRD42022372249; PRISMA + Cochrane Handbook.
- **Search**: PubMed, SCOPUS, EBSCO, Cochrane Library, LILACS (to May 2023) + manual/reference search; no language/date limit.
- **Screening funnel**: 435 → 355 (de-dup) → 34 (title/abstract) → 16 qualitative → 9 quantitative.
- **Pool**: 1 RCT + 1 prospective + 14 retrospective; 2013–2023; 638 patients / 6524 teeth; duration 15–30 mo.
- **Endpoints pooled**: root length change (mm) + root volume loss (mm³), CBCT only. 4 panoramic RCR studies excluded from pooling.
- **Stats**: MD + 95% CI; I² > 40% → random-effects; subgroup + sensitivity (remove Chen et al.) + funnel-plot bias check; RevMan 5.4.
- **RoB**: RCT moderate (Cochrane RoB); non-RCTs 11 moderate / 4 high (ROBINS-I) — outcome measurement + retrospective design weakest domains.

## Results

- **Overall incisor root length**: −0.56 mm (95% CI −0.73, −0.38; P < .00001).
- **Maxillary central incisor** (7 studies): −0.74 mm (95% CI −0.98, −0.49; P < .01) — greatest; sensitivity-test −0.64 mm.
- **Maxillary lateral incisor** (6 studies): −0.56 mm (95% CI −0.92, −0.21; P = .002); I² = 33%.
- **Mandibular central incisor** (3 studies): −0.47 mm (95% CI −0.78, −0.17; P = .002) — significant.
- **Mandibular lateral incisor** (3): −0.50 mm (95% CI −1.24, −0.21; P = .16) — NS; I² = 80%.
- **Maxillary canine** (3): −0.76 mm (P = .14, I² = 70%); **mandibular canine** (3): −0.51 mm (P = .18, I² = 62%) — both NS.
- **Root volume — overall incisors** (3 studies): −13.34 mm³ (95% CI −16.57, −10.10; P < .00001); maxillary central −19.91 mm³, maxillary lateral −12.06 mm³.
- **Incidence/severity (2D + qualitative)**: every patient had ≥1 resorbed tooth (incidence 41.81–100%); mostly mild (<10%); severe (>20%) only ~3.69–6.31% of teeth, mainly maxillary incisors.
- **Risk factors**: extraction ↑ (Liu), severe crowding > mild (Aman), intrusion/extrusion ↑, root apex near palatal cortex strongly ↑; age/sex/duration inconsistent.
- **Bottom line**: aligners cause minimal, not clinically meaningful root resorption; greatest at maxillary central incisors.
