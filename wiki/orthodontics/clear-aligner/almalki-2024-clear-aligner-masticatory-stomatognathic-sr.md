## One-line Summary

Systematic review (no meta-analysis; 6 clinical studies, n=10–31, ROB-2) finding clear aligner therapy causes a transient early rise in masticatory muscle activity/discomfort that normalizes on follow-up, plus a significant bite-force reduction; all 6 studies of good methodological quality (overall low risk of bias), with CAT broadly TMD-neutral.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(메타분석 없음; 임상연구 6편, n=10–31, ROB-2): 투명교정(Clear Aligner Therapy, CAT)은 초기 저작근 활성도·불편감을 일시적으로 증가시키나 추적 시 정상화되며 교합력(bite force)은 유의하게 감소함. 6편 모두 질 양호(전체 비뚤림 낮음), CAT는 측두하악장애(Temporomandibular Disorder, TMD)에 대체로 중립적.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1186/s12903-024-04029-8](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12903-024-04029-8)), this PRISMA/Cochrane-conformant systematic review (PROSPERO 509370) screened 437 records down to **six clinical studies** to ask whether clear aligner therapy (CAT) measurably affects the masticatory musculature and stomatognathic system. Because of marked heterogeneity in study design, sample size (n=10–31), follow-up (9 days to 8 months) and outcome measures, only a qualitative synthesis was possible — no meta-analysis was performed despite the title's "Cochrane handbook" framing.

The synthesized signal is a **time-limited disturbance, not a chronic one**. Surface-EMG studies (Liu, Nota, Paes) showed a significant change in masticatory muscle activity from baseline (T0) to the first measurement point (T1) that reverted toward baseline on later follow-up, an effect the authors attribute to muscle plasticity and adaptation to the new occlusal load. Discomfort/pain rose early (e.g. Nota: palpation pain 12.5%→25% at T1; Tran: mild tooth pain and muscle soreness, pain correlated with trait anxiety r=0.423, p=0.008), and a **significant bite-force reduction** was consistently noted (Paes: bite force ↓ <20%, p<0.05) as aligners interpose between the arches. One outlier — Manfredini's invisible-retainer trial — found no significant change in sleep-time masticatory muscle activity. Overall risk of bias was **low** (all studies "good methodological quality"), with only minor flags (Liu's all-female selection; Paes's n=10).

Clinically: counsel CAT patients to expect mild, self-limiting masticatory muscle soreness/discomfort and reduced biting efficiency in the first days-to-weeks, which typically normalizes — and reassure that current evidence does not link CAT to TMD onset (orthodontics is broadly TMD-neutral), while screening for pre-existing TMD signs during planning.

## Key Contributions

- First focused SR isolating **clear aligner** (not fixed-appliance) effects on the masticatory/stomatognathic system. (extends fixed-appliance literature)
- Establishes the **transient temporal pattern**: early ↑ muscle activity/discomfort (T0→T1) → reversion to baseline on follow-up.
- Flags **bite-force reduction** as a consistent secondary effect of aligner interposition.
- Reaffirms CAT as **TMD-neutral** — no causal link to TMD onset on current evidence.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review, qualitative synthesis only (PRISMA + Cochrane handbook; PROSPERO 509370).
- **PEOS**: P = humans any age/sex; E = clear aligner therapy; C = none; O = masticatory muscle activity change / TMD; S = clinical studies.
- **Databases**: PubMed, Embase, Web of Science, Scopus, Cochrane Library, CINAHL, PsycINFO, Google Scholar (English-only).
- **Flow**: 437 → 276 screened → 134 eligibility → **6 included**.
- **Bias**: ROB-2, two independent reviewers (+third for disputes).

## Results

| Study | Region/Year | n | Follow-up | Key finding |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Liu | China 2017 | 23 | 6 mo | TA activity ↑ T0→T1 (P<0.05); OBC ↓; insignificant after 3 mo (plasticity) |
| Manfredini | Italy 2018 | 19 | n/s | No significant change in sleep masticatory muscle activity |
| Nota | Italy 2021 | 16 | 3 mo | Masseter rest sEMG ↓ at T1, back to baseline T2; palpation pain 12.5%→25% |
| Paes | Brazil 2023 | 10 | 8 mo | sEMG RMS ↑ ~30% (p=0.001); **bite force ↓ <20% (p<0.05)** |
| Pittar | New Zealand 2023 | 31 | 9 days | Contraction amplitude ↓ (p=0.003); occlusal discomfort ↑ (p=0.048) |
| Tran | Canada 2020 | 27 | 4 wk | Mild tooth pain + muscle soreness; pain ~trait anxiety (r=0.423, p=0.008) |

- **Pattern**: short-term T0→T1 change in muscle activity reverting to baseline; pain/discomfort ↑ early; bite force significantly ↓.
- **Risk of bias**: overall low; minor flags (Liu selection "unclear"; Paes small n).
- **Note**: the paper's limitations text says "eight studies," but its abstract, PRISMA flow, and tables consistently report **six** — six is correct.
