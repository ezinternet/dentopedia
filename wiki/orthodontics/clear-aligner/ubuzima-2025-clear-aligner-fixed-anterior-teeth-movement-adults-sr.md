## One-line Summary

Qualitative SR (GRADE, no meta) of 12 studies (2 low-RoB RCTs, 10 moderate-RoB non-RCTs) on anterior tooth movement in adults: vertical movement (extrusion/intrusion) inconsistent between CA and FA, while FAs appear superior for rotation and torque control; limited evidence from few studies.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1016/j.ortho.2025.101084](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ortho.2025.101084)), this systematic review compares clear aligners (CAs) and fixed appliances (FAs) for orthodontic movement of the anterior teeth (OMAT) in adults, broken down by movement type rather than as a single efficacy verdict. It was ingested abstract-only (no open-access full text), so figures below are the qualitative study tallies reported in the abstract; no meta-analysis was performed.

Twelve studies were included (2 RCTs at low risk of bias, 10 non-RCTs at moderate risk of bias), with evidence quality graded via GRADE. The picture is mixed for translation and vertical movement: buccolingual crown translation split 2 FA / 2 CA / 2 no-difference; mesiodistal translation was equal (2 studies); extrusion was inconsistent (3 favor CA, 2 favor FA); intrusion was equally effective (1 each); tipping was mixed (1 each plus 2 no-difference). The discriminating finding is **torque control, better with FAs in 1 study (1 no difference)**, and the authors' conclusion that **FAs appear to offer superior rotation and torque control** while vertical OMAT efficacy is inconsistent — all on limited evidence from a small number of studies.

Clinically: for adult anterior cases, CAs are broadly comparable for bodily translation and ambiguous for vertical (extrusion/intrusion) movement, but **rotation and torque (third-order root) control remain the weak points of aligners versus braces** — the same limitation flagged at the umbrella level. Plan auxiliaries (attachments, power ridges, overcorrection, or hybrid/finishing with fixed appliances) when correcting rotated teeth or precise incisor torque in adults.

## Key Contributions

- Decomposes CA-vs-FA anterior tooth movement by movement type (translation / extrusion / intrusion / tipping / rotation / torque).
- Substantiates the umbrella-level claim of CA inferiority for rotation and torque control with a per-study tally.
- Flags vertical movement (extrusion/intrusion) as the inconsistent, unresolved domain.

## Methodology

Qualitative SR (no meta-analysis). PubMed, Cochrane, Science Direct, Web of Science, Scopus, Google Scholar to Sept 2024. Randomized + non-randomized clinical trials eligible; RoB via Cochrane RCT and non-RCT tools; GRADE for evidence quality. 12 studies (2 low-RoB RCTs, 10 moderate-RoB non-RCTs). Confidence `sr`; abstract-only ingest.

## Results

- Buccolingual crown translation: 2 favor FA, 2 favor CA, 2 no difference.
- Mesiodistal translation: equal (2 studies).
- Extrusion: inconsistent — 3 favor CA, 2 favor FA.
- Intrusion: equally effective — 1 study each.
- Tipping: mixed — 1 each + 2 no difference.
- Torque control: better with FA in 1 study; 1 study no difference.
- Bottom line: vertical OMAT inconsistent; FAs appear superior for rotation and torque control; limited evidence.
