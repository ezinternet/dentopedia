## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.7759/cureus.38311](https://doi.org/10.7759/cureus.38311)), this SR probes the severe end of the severity boundary that the Yassir umbrella draws: premolar-extraction and complex cases. Six trials (3 RCTs, 2 retrospective cohorts, 1 CCT; n=283) were synthesized with RoB 2 / ROBINS-I.

The nuance is between **outcome** and **process**. On final occlusal grading (**ABO-OGS, PAR**), three studies found **no difference** between aligners and fixed appliances — aligners can reach an acceptable endpoint even in extraction cases. But the **process** favored fixed appliances: better **buccolingual inclination** and **occlusal contacts**, achieved in **shorter** treatment time, while aligners showed meaningful **predicted-vs-achieved discrepancies** in the bodily movement and space closure that extraction cases demand. In other words, aligners can get there, but with more under-expression, weaker root/contact control, and longer duration in this case class.

Clinically: extraction/complex cases are feasible with aligners in experienced hands but carry a predictability penalty — expect mid-course corrections/refinements, plan robust anchorage and attachments, and counsel on longer timelines; for demanding torque/inclination control, fixed appliances retain an edge. This operationalizes the umbrella's "inferior for severe cases" statement at the extraction-case level.

## Key Contributions

- Tests CAT in its weakest domain (premolar-extraction/complex).
- Final grading equivalent, but process inferior: inclination, contacts, duration, predictability.

## Methodology

SR (no MA). 6 databases to Feb 2023; RCT/CCT/cohort/cross-sectional; RoB 2 + ROBINS-I. 6 trials, n=283. Confidence `sr`; PMC OA full text.

## Results

- 3 studies: no CA vs FA difference on ABO-OGS/PAR.
- CA predicted-vs-achieved discrepancies in extraction cases.
- FA: shorter duration, better buccolingual inclination and occlusal contacts.
