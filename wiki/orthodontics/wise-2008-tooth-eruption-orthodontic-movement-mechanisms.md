## Summary
Comparative narrative review of molecular mechanisms of tooth eruption and orthodontic tooth movement (OTM). J Dent Res 2008;87(5):414–434. NIHMS49084. LSU Veterinary Medicine (Wise) + University of Washington Orthodontics (King).

Both processes rely on osteoclastogenesis and osteogenesis, sharing core molecular machinery (RANKL, OPG, CSF-1). Key distinction: eruption requires the dental follicle as its essential soft tissue; OTM requires the PDL. Eruption is primarily physiological/developmental; OTM combines pathological (reversible injury) and physiological (mechanoadaptation) responses.

## Key Contributions
- **Dental follicle requirement for eruption**: surgical removal prevents eruption; inert object substitution with intact follicle → eruption proceeds (Marks & Cahill experimental series)
- **Eruption vs OTM**: share osteoclastogenic pathway but differ in force role and tissue type
- **CSF-1**: critical for molar eruption (limited eruption teeth); does not drive incisor eruption (continuous eruption teeth) — distinct regulatory logic
- **OTM three clinical phases**: (1) immediate displacement; (2) delay; (3) linear movement — reflects hyalinization and secondary resorption sequence
- **Biologically active soft tissue requirement**: eruption = dental follicle; OTM = PDL — in both cases, tissue is mandatory mediator
- Focus on teeth of limited eruption (human dentition, rodent molars); continuous eruption (rodent incisors) governed by different molecules

## Methodology
- Narrative review; surgical, transgenic, pharmacological data synthesis
- Emphasis on molecular and cellular events over historical theories
- Focus on translational implications for dental treatment development

## Results
| Feature | Tooth Eruption | Orthodontic Tooth Movement |
|---|---|---|
| Required soft tissue | Dental follicle | Periodontal ligament (PDL) |
| Force role | Secondary | Primary driver |
| Process character | Physiological/developmental | Pathological + physiological |
| RANKL/OPG | Both | Both |
| CSF-1 | Critical (limited eruption) | Less central |
| Sterile inflammation | Not required | Required |
