## 한줄요약

## Summary
This Yonsei University pilot RCT compared BoNT/A (n=14) to saline placebo (n=7) in 21 patients with myogenous TMD presenting with masticatory muscle pain (MMP) and concurrent headache. Injections were tailored to palpated tender sites across 16 muscle areas (masseter, temporalis, splenius capitis, sternocleidomastoid, trapezius). Over 12 weeks, BoNT/A produced significant within-group reductions in orofacial VAS, tender point count, headache VAS, and headache frequency (all p ≤ 0.005), while none of these changed in the saline group. The decline in tender point count significantly differed between groups at all follow-up timepoints. No adverse events were reported.

## Key Contributions
- Novel design pairing MMP and headache as co-primary outcomes — previously evaluated separately.
- Tailored injection protocol based on tender-point palpation rather than fixed anatomical sites.
- Includes cervical and shoulder muscles (splenius capitis, SCM, trapezius) in addition to masticatory muscles — reflects clinical practice for headache-comorbid TMD.
- Reports objective surrogate (TP count) with strong between-group signal.
- Demonstrates feasibility and safety for a properly powered confirmatory RCT.

## Methodology
Pilot RCT, double-blind, single-center. 21 patients (19F/2M, age 21-53) clinically diagnosed with MMP. Random 2:1 to BoNT/A (n=14) or saline (n=7). Injections placed at tenderness sites among 16 muscle areas. Outcomes assessed at pre-injection, 4, 8, and 12 weeks: OVAS, HVAS, TPs, MMO, HF. Friedman test for within-group temporal change, Mann-Whitney for between-group comparison, Wilcoxon signed-rank for post-hoc paired contrasts.

## Results
| Outcome | BoNT/A (median pre → 12 wk) | Friedman p (BoNT/A) | Saline (median pre → 12 wk) | Friedman p (Saline) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| OVAS | 5.00 → 2.50 | 0.003 | 6.00 → 4.00 | 0.256 (ns) |
| TPs | 9.50 → 4.50 | <0.001 | 5.00 → ~5.00 | 0.588 (ns) |
| HVAS | 4.50 → 0.00 | 0.005 | — | ns |
| HF | 2.00 → 0.00 | 0.002 | — | ns |
| MMO | no significant change | ns | no significant change | ns |

- TPs differed between groups at all timepoints (between-group significance most pronounced at 4 and 12 weeks).
- No adverse events reported in either group through 12 weeks.

## Clinical Implications
- For myogenous TMD patients with concurrent headache, tender-point-guided BoNT/A injections produced clinically meaningful within-group pain and headache reductions over 12 weeks in this small RCT.
- Tender point count is a feasible objective response marker for BoNT/A response.
- MMO did not improve — manage patient expectations: BoNT/A in this regimen targets pain, not jaw mobility.
- Pilot-level evidence; do not extrapolate to broader TMD populations without confirmatory trials. Combined SR/MA evidence (e.g., Saini 2024, Coelho 2025) tempers enthusiasm.
