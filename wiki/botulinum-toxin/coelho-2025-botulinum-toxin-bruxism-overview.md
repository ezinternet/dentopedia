## One-line Summary
Overview of 14 systematic reviews of BoNT-A for bruxism: although most SRs report reduced pain (10/14), bruxism event frequency (7/14), and maximum bite force (5/14), 13/14 SRs score AMSTAR-2 "critically low" with high primary-study overlap (CCA = 10.11%), so evidence-based recommendations cannot be supported.

## Summary
This Brazilian UFSC-led overview of 14 systematic reviews on BoNT-A for bruxism finds a methodological-quality crisis: 13 of 14 SRs score "critically low" on AMSTAR-2 and only one scores "low" (none reach moderate or high). Despite the majority of SRs reporting BoNT-A reduces pain (10/14), bruxism event frequency (7/14), and maximum bite force (5/14), the authors caution that the underlying SR methodology is too weak — with high overlap (CCA = 10.11%) of the same primary studies — to support evidence-based recommendations. None of the SRs effectively assessed BoNT-A's effect on functional movements.

## Key Contributions
- Disaggregates "bruxism" from broader "TMD" literature explicitly — earlier reviews routinely conflated the two.
- AMSTAR-2 + CCA analytical pairing exposes both quality and dependency-overlap problems in the SR base.
- Surfaces an evidence-base reality check: SR count is high, SR quality is low.
- Identifies the specific AMSTAR-2 domains failing across SRs: methods statement, search strategy, exclusion justification, RoB assessment, statistical combination, RoB interpretation.
- Lists outcome reporting frequency across SRs (pain, events, bite force, function).

## Methodology
Two-phase independent screening of 461 database + 196 grey-literature references (16 June 2024). 14 SRs included. AMSTAR-2 used for SR-level quality. CCA computed across SRs to quantify primary-study overlap. Outcomes catalogued descriptively: pain, bruxism event frequency, maximum bite force, functional movements. No new meta-analysis performed (overview design).

## Results
| Domain | Finding |
|---|---|
| SRs included | 14 (published 2012–2023, 13 journals, 10 countries) |
| AMSTAR-2 "critically low" | 13/14 |
| AMSTAR-2 "low" | 1/14 (the highest in the pool) |
| CCA (overlap) | 10.11% — "high" |
| SRs reporting pain reduction with BoNT-A | 10/14 |
| SRs reporting reduction in bruxism event frequency | 7/14 |
| SRs reporting reduction in maximum bite force | 5/14 |
| SRs evaluating functional movements | 0/14 effectively |
| SRs using GRADE successfully | 0/14 |

## Clinical Implications
- Do not over-interpret "BoNT-A works for bruxism" SRs in clinical decisions — the evidence base is dominated by critically-low-quality reviews.
- Treat "reduction in maximum bite force" as a double-edged effect: a positive surrogate for muscle quieting, but also a documented adverse effect on masticatory performance.
- For sleep bruxism, the absence of any SR that effectively evaluated functional movements means functional outcome claims are unsupported.
- Counsel patients that systematic-review-level certainty for BoNT-A in bruxism is currently absent — discuss alongside splints, behavioral therapy, and pharmacological options.
