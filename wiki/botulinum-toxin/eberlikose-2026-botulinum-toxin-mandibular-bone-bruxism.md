## One-line Summary

Retrospective case-control (n=60, 3 groups: BoNT-A bruxism / untreated bruxism / healthy controls) using panoramic radiography over 6 months showed BoNT-A group had significantly increased cortical thickness and reduced G2/G3 pathological mandibular morphology vs untreated bruxism; antegonial notch depth unchanged. Abstract-only.

## Summary

This is the first study providing radiographic evidence that BoNT-A treatment for bruxism induces measurable changes in mandibular bone morphology. Most BoNT-A bruxism literature focuses on soft-tissue outcomes (pain, muscle thickness, sleep quality); this study uniquely evaluates the hard-tissue consequences of reducing masticatory muscle hyperactivity.

The three-group design is the key methodological strength: by including both treated bruxism and untreated bruxism groups against healthy controls, it separates (1) baseline bruxism-induced bone changes from (2) BoNT-A treatment effects. The untreated bruxism group shows the expected pathological bone morphology (higher G2/G3, lower cortical thickness); the BoNT-A group moves toward the control group morphology over 6 months.

The proposed mechanism is indirect: BoNT-A reduces masticatory muscle activity → decreased occlusal load → reduced pathological bone stress → cortical remodeling toward healthier morphology. This is consistent with known bone mechanobiology (Wolff's law applied in reverse).

## Key Contributions

- First panoramic radiographic evidence of BoNT-A effect on mandibular bone morphology in bruxism
- Cortical thickness increase and G2/G3 reduction in BoNT-A group (p<0.05 vs untreated bruxism)
- Antegonial notch depth (AND): no significant change — suggests AND is a less sensitive index than CT for tracking BoNT-A effects
- Validates panoramic radiography as monitoring tool for BoNT-A bruxism treatment (beyond just clinical pain assessment)
- Supports the mechanical unloading hypothesis: BoNT-A → occlusal force ↓ → bone remodeling normalization

## Methodology

- **Design**: Retrospective case-control; 3 groups (n=20 each)
- **Groups**: (1) Bruxism + BoNT-A; (2) Bruxism only (no treatment); (3) Healthy controls
- **Imaging**: Panoramic radiography at baseline and 6-month follow-up
- **Measurements**: G0–G3 morphological classification, AMD, AND, cortical thickness (CT)
- **Statistics**: Between-group comparisons at 6 months (p<0.05 threshold)

## Results

- **Cortical thickness (CT)**: BoNT-A > untreated bruxism (p<0.05)
- **G2/G3 categories (pathological)**: BoNT-A < untreated bruxism (p<0.05)
- **AND (antegonial notch depth)**: No significant difference between groups
- **AMD**: Not specified in abstract
- Interpretation: BoNT-A reverses/prevents pathological mandibular bone apposition via occlusal stress reduction
