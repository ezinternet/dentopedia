## 한줄요약
Prospective n=15: Yonsei triangle BTX-A 4U/side로 잇몸노출 3.5±1 mm 감소, 1-2개월차 정점(-4mm), 4개월 효과 유지, 6개월 완전 반전. 만족도 94% (very/satisfied).

## Summary
Prospective single-arm clinical study of 15 patients with excessive gingival display treated with BTX-A (Botox, Allergan; 4 U at each Yonsei point + 2 U at philtrum) and followed for 6 months. Mean gingival display dropped from 7.07 ± 1.28 mm pre-op to 3.07 ± 1.03 mm at months 1-2 (a 4 mm reduction, p<0.001), with effect waning from month 4 (5.00 mm) and returning to baseline by month 6 (6.93 mm, NS). No adverse events; 67% very satisfied, 27% satisfied at 1 month follow-up.

## Key Contributions
- Operationalizes the Yonsei triangle protocol (Hwang 2009) in a 15-patient clinical cohort
- Provides monthly granular data through 6 months showing onset, peak (month 1-2), plateau (month 3), wane (month 4), and full reversal (months 5-6)
- Tailors injection sites by gummy smile subtype (anterior, posterior, mixed)
- Demonstrates safety with zero adverse events in this small cohort
- Confirms patient acceptance: all 15 patients willing to repeat treatment

## Methodology
- Design: prospective single-arm, single-center (Chennai, India), Dec 2018-Oct 2019
- Inclusion: 18-40 y, >2 mm gingival exposure, hyperactive lip elevator; exclusions for neuromuscular disease, pregnancy, calcium channel blockers, aminoglycosides
- Drug: Botox (Allergan) 100 U + 2.5 mL 0.9% saline → 4 U / 0.1 mL
- Injection: Yonsei triangle (Point 1 = lateral ala; Point 2 = nasolabial midpoint; Point 3 = 1/4 distance ala-to-tragus). 4 U each side at Yonsei point + 2 U philtrum (anterior/mixed); 1 cm lateral to Yonsei triangle for posterior subtype
- Measurement: standardized photos at day 3, 7, 15, then monthly to month 6; gingival crest to lower lip border on maximum smile
- Stats: paired Student's t-test

### Patient satisfaction (1 month)
- Very satisfied: 67%
- Satisfied: 27%
- Disappointed: 6%
- 100% willing to repeat

### Subtype distribution
Anterior 53%, mixed 40%, posterior 7%.

### Safety
No injection site pain, edema, ecchymosis, dry mouth, ptosis, or other complications were reported in this cohort.

## Clinical Implications
- BTX-A at Yonsei triangle (4 U/side) reliably reduces gingival display by ~4 mm at peak, sustained for 3-4 months — re-injection cycle should be planned at ~16-week intervals
- Effect is fully reversible — patients must understand this is not a permanent solution
- Subtype classification (anterior/posterior/mixed) drives injection site selection; posterior subtype requires injection lateral to the Yonsei triangle (zygomaticus major target)
- Conservative dosing (4 U/side) appears effective and safe; no need for higher initial doses for first-time patients with <8 mm display
