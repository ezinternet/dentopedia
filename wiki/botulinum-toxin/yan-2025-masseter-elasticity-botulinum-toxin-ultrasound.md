## One-line Summary

Prospective self-controlled study (n=14, 28 masseter muscles, ultrasound elastography) showed BoNT-A caused 28.9–31.5% masseter thickness decrease at 1–3 months (47.4% partial volume recovery at 6mo) and decreased shear wave velocity (elasticity) at 1–3 months that **fully returned to baseline by 6 months**; thickness and elasticity recovery were uncorrelated. Abstract-only.

## 한줄요약

초음파 탄성영상(USE) 전향 자기대조 연구(n=14, 28개 교근)에서 BoNT-A 후 교근 두께가 1–3개월에 28.9–31.5% 감소(6개월에 47.4% 부분 회복)하고, 전단파 속도(탄성도)는 1–3개월 감소 후 6개월에 기저선으로 **완전 회복**; 두께와 탄성 회복은 비상관. 초록만.

## Summary

This prospective self-controlled study is the first to use **ultrasound elastography (USE)** — specifically shear wave velocity measurement — to track BoNT-A-induced microstructural changes in human masseter muscles longitudinally. Prior knowledge of BoNT-A masseter effects came from thickness measurements (B-mode ultrasound) and animal histology; USE adds the critical dimension of tissue stiffness tracking.

The central clinical finding: **elasticity (shear wave velocity) returns fully to baseline by 6 months**, even though muscle volume (thickness) has only partially recovered (47.4%). This dissociation reveals two distinct recovery mechanisms operating on different timescales:
1. **Stiffness/fibrosis pathway**: rapidly reversible — normalized by 6 months
2. **Volume/atrophy pathway**: slower — only 47.4% recovered at 6 months

The **absence of elevated stiffness at 6 months** is clinically reassuring: it argues against progressive fibrosis with repeated BoNT-A injections (at least within the injection intervals studied).

**Practical injection timing implication**: If elasticity returning to baseline (~6 months) reflects true functional recovery, this may support a ≥6-month re-injection interval from a structural standpoint.

## Key Contributions

- First USE-based longitudinal tracking of BoNT-A masseter microstructure in humans
- **Elasticity (shear wave velocity): fully reversible by 6 months** → no fibrosis signal
- Thickness: only 47.4% recovered at 6 months — volume recovery is slower than elasticity recovery
- Uncorrelated thickness and elasticity changes → distinct biological mechanisms
- USE as an objective re-injection readiness marker (elasticity back to baseline = structural recovery)
- Safety implication: reversible elasticity changes argue against progressive tissue damage with repeated injections

## Methodology

- **Design**: Prospective self-controlled (paired pre-post); each subject = own control
- **Sample**: n=14 subjects; ages 21–27 years; 28 masseter muscles
- **Intervention**: BoNT-A injection into masseter (dose not specified in abstract)
- **Timepoints**: Baseline, 1 month, 3 months, 6 months
- **Measurements**:
  - Masseter thickness (B-mode ultrasound, mm)
  - V median (shear wave velocity, m/s; proxy for elastic modulus)
- **Statistics**: Paired t-tests, Spearman correlation (thickness vs elasticity)

## Results

| Parameter | Baseline | 1–3 months | 6 months |
|---|---|---|---|
| Masseter thickness | Reference | ↓ 28.9–31.5% (p≤0.05) | 47.4% partial recovery |
| V median (m/s) | 2.53 ± 0.46 | 2.10 ± 0.45 (↓, p≤0.05) | **2.70 ± 0.68 (p>0.05 vs baseline — FULL RECOVERY)** |

- Thickness vs elasticity recovery: **Spearman correlation non-significant** → uncorrelated
