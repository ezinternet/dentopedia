## One-line Summary

Prospective observational study (n=40 women with awake + sleep bruxism, standardized BTX-A 30U masseter + 20U temporalis) showed PSQI-defined good sleep quality rising from 17.5% to 60% post-treatment; all PSQI subscales significantly improved (all p<0.05). Abstract-only.

## Summary

This study uniquely targets patients with **comorbid awake bruxism (AB) and sleep bruxism (SB)** simultaneously — a population underrepresented in BoNT-A bruxism literature, which typically studies one bruxism type. Both AB and SB contribute to masticatory muscle hyperactivity, and patients with both types may have greater burden and thus more measurable sleep improvement following muscle paralysis.

The standardized injection protocol is clearly reported: 30U bilateral masseter + 20U bilateral temporalis — a moderate-dose regimen, lower than the ~200U used in Angelo et al. (2026) which also included SCM. This allows dose comparison across studies.

The primary outcome — PSQI ≤5 (good sleep quality) — nearly tripled in prevalence (17.5% → 60%). All seven PSQI subscales improved significantly, including daytime function (p=0.002). This broad improvement across all PSQI domains (not just subjective quality) suggests BTX-A-mediated reduction in nocturnal muscle activity translates to comprehensive sleep architecture improvement.

The lack of a control group is the major limitation; placebo responses in bruxism studies can be substantial, especially for sleep-related outcomes.

## Key Contributions

- Rare study with **both AB + SB** in the same cohort (not just one bruxism type)
- Clear standardized protocol: **30U masseter + 20U temporalis** (bilateral)
- PSQI ≤5 (good sleep) rate: **17.5% → 60%** post-treatment (p<0.001)
- **All 7 PSQI subscales** improved: subjective quality, latency, duration, efficiency, disturbance, medication use, daytime function
- Daytime function improvement significant (p=0.002) — affects waking hours, not just nighttime
- Pairs with Hira (2026) to build "BoNT-A → sustained PSQI improvement" evidence axis

## Methodology

- **Design**: Prospective observational study
- **Sample**: n=40 female patients (all-female); probable sleep bruxism + awake bruxism diagnoses
- **Intervention**: BTX-A bilateral: 30U masseter + 20U temporalis
- **Outcome**: PSQI pre- and post-treatment
- **Subgroups**: PSQI subscale analysis (7 domains)
- **Statistics**: Pre-post comparison with p-values per subscale
