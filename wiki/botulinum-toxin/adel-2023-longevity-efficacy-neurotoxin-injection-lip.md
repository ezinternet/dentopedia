## 한줄요약
Experimental n=10 female: IncoBotulinumTox A 사전 주입 → 입술 재배치 수술 → 2/4/8개월 반복 BTX-A; 잇몸노출 5.12 → 0 mm (14일~8개월 유지), 12개월 0.78 mm minimal relapse — BTX-A 단독·수술 단독보다 안정적이라 주장한 1년 follow-up 단일군 연구.

## Summary
Experimental single-arm study (n=10 female, age 25-37) of a 5-stage combined protocol: Stage 1 — IncoBotulinumTox A (Xeomin) injection; Stage 2 — lip repositioning surgery 2 weeks later; Stages 3-5 — repeat Xeomin at 2, 4, 8 months. Dose individualized to gingival display (1 U per mm at Yonsei point). Result: gingival display 5.12 → 0 mm sustained from 14 days through 8 months, only 0.78 mm relapse at 12 months; interlabial distance 17.99 → 6.84 mm at 14 days, gradually 12.21 mm at 12 months. No serious complications. Authors propose this combined approach produces longer-lasting outcomes than either BTX-A alone or surgery alone.

## Key Contributions
- First protocol combining pre-surgery BTX-A + lip repositioning + 3 maintenance BTX-A injections at 2/4/8 months
- Individualized dose: 1 U per mm gingival display at the Yonsei point
- Achieves complete elimination (0 mm) of gingival display sustained through 8 months
- Minimal relapse at 12 months (0.78 mm)
- Hypothesis: muscle relaxation enables fibrous tissue maturation in the surgically reduced vestibular depth, mechanically stabilizing the surgery
- Avoids myotomy/myectomy (regarded as overly aggressive)

## Methodology
- Design: experimental single-arm case series with structured 5-stage protocol
- Population: 10 White female patients, 25-37 y, upper lip hypermobility or short upper lip; vertical maxillary excess excluded
- Drug: IncoBotulinumTox A (Xeomin, Merz) 100 U + 2 mL preservative-free saline
- Surgery: full-thickness flap with inferior incision at mucogingival junction + superior at mucosa, lateral connection at premolars / first molar, 5-0 Vicryl closure
- Injection sites by subtype:
  - Anterior: Yonsei point only (1 cm from ala)
  - Mixed: 3 bilateral points (Yonsei + deepest nasolabial contraction point + midway)
  - Vermilion point added when orbicularis oris hyperactivity present (1 U at Cupid's bow tip)
- Dose rule: Yonsei = mm display; half-dose at other 2 sites for mixed cases
- Measurements: Adobe Photoshop CS6 on standardized photos; gingival display + interlabial distance during spontaneous smile
- Follow-up: 14 days, 4 mo, 8 mo, 12 mo

### Cosmetic adjuncts
- Patients reported increased vermilion height — combined effect of lip repositioning + orbicularis BTX-A
- No paresthesia, no serious complications
- Post-operative edema in all patients, resolved within 1 week
- High patient satisfaction at study end

## Clinical Implications
- For patients motivated to undergo combined surgical + pharmacologic protocol, this approach offers near-complete suppression of gingival display through at least 8 months — appropriate for severe muscular-etiology cases
- Repeated BTX-A maintenance (q2-4 months initially, then q4 mo) is essential — without it, relapse expected
- NOT appropriate for vertical maxillary excess (explicit exclusion)
- Individualized dose by mm display is a pragmatic guide; aligns with the SR+MA finding that flat dose-response curves apply within typical ranges ([[botulinum-toxin/rojo-sanchis-2023-non-surgical-management-gingival-smile]])
- Cost and antibody formation from repeated incobotulinumtoxinA injections deserve patient counseling
- Lip repositioning alone has high relapse rate (cited surgical SRs); combined approach addresses elevator muscle pull mechanically + pharmacologically
- Strong female cohort bias — male/different ethnic-group generalizability unclear
