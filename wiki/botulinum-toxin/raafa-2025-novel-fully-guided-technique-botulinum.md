## Summary
Lateral pterygoid muscle (LPM) is a deep, hard-to-target masticatory muscle implicated in DDWR — anterior disc displacement that "reduces" on opening, producing clicking and pain. Blind LPM injection risks diffusion to pharyngeal muscles (dysphagia, nasal regurgitation); the current standard guidance is electromyography (EMG), which relies on operator skill and provides only functional, not visual, confirmation.

This single-center prospective double-blinded RCT (Alexandria University, n=20, registered NCT06633445) compared a novel **CT-segmentation–based 3D-printed needle guide** (group I, n=10) against EMG-guided injection (group II, n=10). All patients had DDWR confirmed by DC/TMD and MRI; all received 25 U onabotulinumtoxinA (Botox) into the symptomatic LPM.

The fully guided technique used Mimics to segment the LPM itself (not just bone landmarks), Blue Sky Plan to plan needle trajectory from the buccal of upper molars to the muscle center, and 3D printing of a tooth-supported needle guide. Both techniques achieved 100% clicking elimination at 1 month and equivalent improvements in MIO, disc position (TD/TP method), and TMJ tenderness. The fully guided arm was **superior in LPM tenderness reduction at 3 and 6 months** (p=0.009 and 0.015, respectively).

The clinical message: muscle-segmentation 3D guidance is a viable, reproducible alternative to EMG that reduces operator-skill dependence and may give cleaner symptomatic outcomes — at the cost of CT radiation and a 2-day fabrication lead time.

## Key Contributions
- First RCT to compare **muscle-segmentation 3D-printed guide** vs **EMG** for LPM BoNT-A injection.
- Demonstrates **non-inferiority on MIO, disc position, clicking, TMJ tenderness** and **superiority on LPM tenderness** at 3 and 6 mo.
- Establishes a digital workflow blueprint: intraoral scan + CT → Mimics LPM segmentation → Blue Sky Plan trajectory → 3D-printed tooth-supported guide → 22-G cannula injection.
- Reduces reliance on operator EMG-interpretation skill while providing visual 3D confidence in needle position.

## Methodology
- Design: prospective, double-blinded (outcome assessor + data analyst), parallel-arm RCT; 1:1 allocation; CONSORT-compliant.
- Population: 20 DDWR patients (DC/TMD + MRI), 20–45 y, Angle Class I. Group I: 9 F + 1 M, age 27.30 ± 5.36 y. Group II: 10 F, age 23.10 ± 2.85 y. No dropouts.
- Exclusions: degenerative joint disease, neuromuscular/bleeding disorders, pregnancy, prior TMJ surgery, BoNT hypersensitivity, posterior edentulous saddles, anterior open bite, MRI-contraindicated.
- Group I (fully guided):
  - CEREC Omnicam AC intraoral scan (STL).
  - CT with soft-tissue window, 0.5 mm slices, patient biting on bite block.
  - Mimics: skull bone segmentation, then bilateral LPM segmentation via axial lasso; merged into single 3D model.
  - Blue Sky Plan v4.7: needle trajectory from buccal upper molar to LPM center; guide tube 1.5 mm × 15–20 mm height, zero offset.
  - 3D-printed in standard resin (Esun).
  - Injection: 25 U onabotulinumtoxinA (Botox; Allergan), 22-G cannula, negative aspiration.
- Group II (EMG): 27-G monopolar cannulated electrode, intraoral approach above upper molar lateral to maxillary tuberosity, Neuro-EMG-Micro (Neurosoft); 25 U Botox after EMG confirmation.
- Post-op: open/close several times, upright 4 h.
- Outcomes:
  - MIO (mm) at baseline, 1, 3, 6 mo.
  - TMJ clicking (present/absent).
  - LPM tenderness (intraoral palpation behind tuberosity, VAS 0–10).
  - TMJ tenderness (palpation, VAS 0–10).
  - Disc position (MRI, Kruita TD/TP method) at 3 mo only.
- Statistics: SPSS 20; Shapiro-Wilk; Student t / Mann-Whitney; RM-ANOVA + Bonferroni / Friedman + Dunn; Chi-square / Fisher; α=0.05.

## Results
### MIO
- Group I: dropped insignificantly at 1 mo (p=0.163); ↑ significantly at 3 and 6 mo (p<0.001).
- Group II: dropped significantly at 1 mo (p<0.001); ↑ significantly at 3 and 6 mo (p<0.001).
- No inter-group difference at any timepoint (p=0.201, 0.122, 0.080).

### Disc position (TD/TP at 3 mo)
- Group I: 0.40 ± 0.04 → 0.48 ± 0.04.
- Group II: 0.43 ± 0.03 → 0.51 ± 0.05.
- Both improved; no inter-group difference (p=0.291).

### Clicking
- 100% disappearance in both groups at 1 mo.
- 1/10 (group I) and 2/10 (group II) regained clicking at 3 mo; stable through 6 mo.

### LPM tenderness (median VAS 0–10)
| | Pre-op | 1 mo | 3 mo | 6 mo |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Group I  | 10 | 0   | 0   | 0   |
| Group II | 10 | 1   | 4   | 4   |
| p (inter-group) | 0.579 | 0.052 | **0.009** | **0.015** |
- Intra-group reduction significant in both (p<0.001). Group I better at 3 and 6 mo.

### TMJ tenderness
- Both groups significantly reduced at all follow-ups; no inter-group difference (p=1, 0.739, 0.739).

## Clinical Implications
- For clinics already running a digital workflow (intraoral scan + CBCT/CT + 3D printer), muscle-segmentation guided LPM BoNT-A is a reasonable alternative to EMG — equivalent symptom outcomes and visually confirmed needle trajectory.
- The superiority on LPM tenderness at 3 and 6 mo may reflect **more centered intramuscular delivery** vs EMG (which confirms muscle entry but not depth/center).
- Trade-off: CT (or CBCT) radiation + 2-day fabrication lead time + per-case 3D print cost vs disposable EMG electrodes.
- Indication anchoring: **DDWR with persistent clicking and TMJ/LPM tenderness despite conservative care** — consistent with Egyptian/MENA practice patterns and prior EMG literature (Emara, Taema, Altaweel).
- The needle-guide design is tooth-supported (buccal of upper molars) — caution if patient has posterior edentulous saddles or anterior open bite (excluded in this study).
- Single 25 U dose was effective at 6 months; the durability question (when, or whether, to re-inject) is unanswered by this trial.
