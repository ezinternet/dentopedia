## One-line Summary
SR+MA (15 prospective + 1 retrospective studies, 11 in meta-analysis): BTX-A reduces gingival exposure by -3.22 mm at 2 weeks (95% CI -4.43 to -2.01) and -2.70 mm at 3 months — no significant difference between timepoints; dose 1.25–7.5 U/side was not associated with effect (meta-regression p=0.78).

## Summary
PRISMA-compliant systematic review and meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD42019115783) screening 1250 references → 15 included studies (14 prospective, 1 retrospective) → 11 in quantitative meta-analysis. Pooled random-effects estimate: BTX-A reduces gingival exposure by -3.22 mm at 2 weeks (95% CI -4.43, -2.01) and -2.70 mm at 3 months (95% CI -4.52, -0.88). Critically, the 2 weeks vs 3 months difference is not significant (Q=0.221, p=0.638) and dose (1.25-7.5 U/side) shows no significant influence on outcome (meta-regression p=0.781 at 2 wk; p=0.357 at 3 mo). Bias: 12/13 studies moderate ROBINS-I risk, only 1 low risk; I² >95% (high heterogeneity). The strongest evidence base to date for BTX-A in gummy smile.

## Key Contributions
- Highest-evidence synthesis (SR+MA) on BTX-A for gummy smile to date
- Meta-regression demonstrates dose-effect is NOT linear within the 1.25-7.5 U/side range
- Subgroup analysis confirms effect is preserved between 2 weeks and 3 months
- Publication bias minimal (fail-safe N = 1156 at 2 wk, 530 at 3 mo; no imputed studies in trim-and-fill)
- Proposes treatment algorithm by anterior gingival exposure (≤4 mm vs >4 mm) and anatomic factors
- ROBINS-I risk-of-bias appraisal for non-randomized studies

## Methodology
- Databases: PubMed, Scopus, Embase, Web of Science, Cochrane + OpenGrey grey literature + hand search
- Last update: June 2021
- Inclusion: ≥10 adult patients, gingival exposure >2 mm, BTX-A treatment; prospective/retrospective controlled trials
- Exclusion: pure altered passive eruption, gingival thickening, overeruption; case reports, narratives, n<10
- Selection: 1250 → 742 (de-duplicated) → 49 full-text → 15 included (κ=0.74)
- Bias: ROBINS-I tool
- Pooling: random effects, inverse variance
- Heterogeneity: Q test, I²
- Publication bias: funnel plot, Duval-Tweedie trim-and-fill, fail-safe N
- Software: Comprehensive Meta-Analysis v3.0

## Results

### Pooled meta-analytic estimates

| Endpoint | n studies | n patients | Pooled Δ (mm) | 95% CI | I² | Heterogeneity |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2 weeks | 9 | 213 | -3.22 | -4.43, -2.01 | 95.3% | high |
| 3 months | 5 | 182 | -2.70 | -4.52, -0.88 | 97.9% | high |

### Meta-regression (BTX-A units → reduction)
| Endpoint | Slope p-value | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| 2 weeks | 0.781 | No dose-response |
| 3 months | 0.357 | No dose-response |

### Subgroup analysis (time)
- 2 weeks vs 3 months: Q=0.221, p=0.638 → no significant decay through 3 months

### Per-study reductions at 2 weeks
Range -1.22 mm (Hexsel) to -5.11 mm; significant in 7/9 (NS for Hexsel et al., Sucupira & Abramovitz).

### Per-study reductions at 3 months
Range -1.00 mm (Somaiah) to -5.95 mm (Al Wayli); significant in 4/5.

### Pre-treatment exposure
3.5 to 7.2 mm across studies.

### Target muscles and doses
- LLSAN, LLS, zygomaticus minor (Yonsei point) most common
- Dose range: 1.25-7.5 U per side
- Follow-up: most studies at 2 weeks, 1 month, 3 months, 6 months

### Risk of bias (ROBINS-I)
- Low: 1 (7.7%)
- Moderate: 12 (92.3%)
- Serious/critical: 0

### Publication bias
- Trim-and-fill: no imputed studies
- Fail-safe N: 1156 (2 wk), 530 (3 mo) — robust against publication bias

## Clinical Implications
- Best-available evidence: expect ~3.2 mm reduction at 2 weeks and ~2.7 mm at 3 months — but expect wide between-patient variability (high I²)
- Within the 1.25-7.5 U/side range, dose does NOT predict response — implies that higher dosing for additional effect is unsupported
- Effect is well-preserved at 3 months — re-injection cycle can be planned around 3-4 months
- Patient counseling: short-term recurrence in first 6-8 weeks is uncommon (unlike lip repositioning surgery)
- BTX-A is the higher-evidence non-surgical option vs hyaluronic acid (HA fillers give only ~1.37 mm reduction in cited comparison)
- Treatment algorithm: anterior exposure ≤4 mm vs >4 mm + anatomic factors guides BTX-A as standalone or combined therapy
