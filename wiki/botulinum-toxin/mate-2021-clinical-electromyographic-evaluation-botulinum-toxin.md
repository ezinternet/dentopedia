## Summary
Prospective single-arm clinical study (n=10) that pairs clinical (photographic gingival display) and electromyographic (compound muscle action potential, C-MAP) outcomes for BTX-A treatment of gummy smile across 4 subtypes (anterior, posterior, mixed, asymmetric). Dose 1.25 U per site (lower than typical Yonsei protocols). Gingival display dropped 6.20 → 3.30 mm at 2 weeks (significant, p<0.001) with C-MAP dropping 254.7 → 114.8 µV (directional but not statistically significant due to wide SD); both gradually returned toward baseline by 6 months. First study to use EMG to objectively quantify upper lip elevator blockade by BTX-A.

## Key Contributions
- First reported use of surface EMG (C-MAP) to quantify BTX-A effect on upper lip elevator muscles
- Subtype-tailored injection sites (anterior, posterior, mixed, asymmetric)
- Lower-dose protocol (1.25 U per site; 8 mL dilution per 100 U)
- Demonstrates statistical significance between 2-week and 3-month C-MAP values (p=0.015) — confirms wane of blockade
- Includes patient satisfaction and discomfort assessment

## Methodology
- Design: prospective single-arm, IRB-approved (KIMSDU, Karad, India, Nov 2016 - Aug 2018)
- Inclusion: 18-35 y, gingival display >3 mm, no neuromuscular drugs (aminoglycosides, anticholinesterase, etc.), not pregnant/lactating
- Drug: BTX-A (SIAX, Aakar Pharmaceuticals, India) diluted 8 mL/100 U → 1.25 U / 0.1 mL
- Injection sites by subtype:
  - **Anterior**: LLSAN (3-5 mm lateral to ala) + LLS (upper 1/3-lower 2/3 of nasolabial fold)
  - **Posterior**: ZM-ZMn origin (cheek prominence) + insertion (most lateral nasolabial fold while smiling)
  - **Mixed**: all four points
  - **Asymmetric**: bilateral on affected side at origin + insertion; only insertion on unaffected side
- C-MAP: facial nerve stimulated at tragus; surface electrode on target muscle; peak-to-peak amplitude (5 sub-units mean)
- Photographic measurement: gingival margin (GP1) to lower lip border (GP2) on Adobe Photoshop 7.0, normalized to incisor crown height
- Follow-up: pre-op, 2 weeks, 3 months, 6 months
- Stats: repeated measures ANOVA + post hoc Bonferroni

## Results

### Subtype distribution
Anterior 3, posterior 2, mixed 3, asymmetric 2.

### Gingival display (mm)
| Time | Mean ± SD | Δ from baseline | p (Bonferroni) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-op | 6.20 ± 1.75 | — | — |
| 2 weeks | 3.30 ± 0.95 | -2.90 | <0.001 |
| 3 months | 4.40 ± 1.17 | -1.80 | 0.004 |
| 6 months | 5.60 ± 1.51 | -0.60 | 0.143 (NS) |

### C-MAP (µV)
| Time | Mean ± SD | Δ from baseline | p (vs baseline) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-op | 254.7 ± 236.7 | — | — |
| 2 weeks | 114.8 ± 94.1 | -139.9 | 0.080 (NS) |
| 3 months | 145.1 ± 115.7 | -109.6 | 0.121 (NS) |
| 6 months | 229.5 ± 204.7 | -25.3 | 0.343 (NS) |
- 2 weeks vs 3 months C-MAP: p=0.015 (significant)

### Pairwise gingival display
- 2 weeks vs 3 months: significant (p=0.001)
- 2 weeks vs 6 months: significant (p<0.001)
- 3 months vs 6 months: significant (p=0.006)

### Satisfaction (6-month questionnaire)
- 70% fully satisfied, 30% satisfied
- 70% would repeat
- 50% no discomfort / 50% slight pain
- One patient (mixed, 10 mm baseline) required re-injection 1.25 U at 2 weeks
- No adverse reactions

## Clinical Implications
- C-MAP correlates with clinical effect — could potentially guide dose titration or detect early wane
- Conservative dosing (1.25 U per site, with 4 sites for mixed) yields ~3 mm reduction at peak; may be appropriate for first-time patients or smaller-stature/female patients
- Subtype classification clarifies which muscle to target — posterior gummy smile needs ZM/ZMn rather than just LLSAN/LLS
- C-MAP variability (wide SD) limits its diagnostic utility for individual patients in clinical practice; useful as a research/group-level metric
- Re-injection planning around 3-month mark when statistical effect on display fades
