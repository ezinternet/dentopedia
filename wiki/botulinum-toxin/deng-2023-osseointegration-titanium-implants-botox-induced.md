## One-line Summary
Rat femur animal study (botox-induced muscle-paralysis/mechanical-unloading model) showing botox degrades trabecular and cortical bone and impairs osseointegration of smooth (PT) titanium implants (removal torque ↓56% in rough Ti), while biomimetic SLAnano surfaces preserve BIC and local semaphorin 3A (sema3A) hydrogel further enhances BIC for SLAnano (no effect on smooth implants).

## Summary
This VCU animal study used a rat botox-paralysis model (botulinum toxin A injection into hindlimb) as an analogue of mechanically unloaded bone, then placed customized titanium implants in the distal femur with either smooth (PT) or biomimetic multiscale (SLAnano) surfaces, with or without local semaphorin 3A (sema3A) delivered in a Cu-free click hydrogel. Botox dramatically reduced trabecular and cortical bone (BV/TV, thickness, porosity) and impaired osseointegration. Biomimetic SLAnano surfaces preserved BIC under mechanical unloading; adding sema3A via hydrogel further enhanced BIC for SLAnano implants but had no effect on smooth implants. Mechanical strength of bone integrated with rough implants dropped 56% under botox, regardless of sema3A.

## Key Contributions
- First rat implant study comparing smooth vs. biomimetic Ti surfaces in a botox-induced mechanical-unloading model with biologic (sema3A) co-delivery.
- Cleanly separates whole-bone phenotype effect (botox compromises trabecular and cortical bone everywhere) from osseointegration effect (surface and biologic dependent).
- Shows that surface topography matters more than biologic when bone is mechanically unloaded.
- Provides mechanical-strength data (removal torque, 3-point bending) — most BIC studies stop at imaging.
- Translational warning for clinical contexts of repeated BTX masseter/temporalis injection: adjacent maxillary/mandibular bone may experience analogous unloading.

## Methodology
12-week-old male Sprague-Dawley rats. Botox injected IM 21 days before implant insertion → mechanically unloaded femur. Custom grade-4 Ti implants (2.5 × 3.5 mm) with PT (smooth machined) or SLAnano (grit-blasted, acid-etched, aged hydrophobic) surfaces. Six groups: control+PT, control+SLAnano, BTX+PT, BTX+PT+Sema3A, BTX+SLAnano, BTX+SLAnano+Sema3A. Sema3A delivered in Cu-free click hydrogel (PEG-dithiol + DBCO-maleimide + PEG-N3, in situ crosslinking, ~14-day degradation) into cortical defect and above implant. Endpoints 28 days post-insertion: microCT (trabecular and cortical morphometry, BIC total/marrow/cortical), 3-point bending, removal torque. Statistics: one-way ANOVA + Tukey, two-way ANOVA for interactions, Wilcoxon paired for contralateral comparisons; α = 0.05.

## Results
| Domain | Healthy | BTX (mechanical unload) | BTX + SLAnano | BTX + SLAnano + Sema3A |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Distal trabecular BV/TV | baseline | ↓↓ | — | unchanged (sema3A no rescue at this site) |
| Cortical thickness (distal) | baseline | ↓ | — | unchanged |
| Trochanter cortical porosity | baseline | ↑ | — | restored by local sema3A |
| BIC, smooth PT implants | normal | dramatic ↓ | (not applicable) | not rescued by sema3A |
| BIC, SLAnano implants | superior to PT | preserved relative to BTX+PT | ↑ over BTX+PT | further ↑ |
| Removal torque (rough Ti) | baseline | ↓ 56% (max load, stiffness, yield) | unchanged | unchanged by sema3A |
| Removal torque (smooth Ti) | already low | no further drop | — | — |

- Botox effect on bone phenotype: trabecular BV/TV ↓, total porosity ↑, trabecular thickness/number ↓; cortical BV/TV ↓, porosity ↑, thickness ↓ at distal metaphysis.
- Local sema3A in hydrogel rescues bone formation specifically at SLAnano implant sites — surface-biologic synergy.
- Sema3A injection alone (no hydrogel) did NOT rescue bone phenotype.

## Clinical Implications
- Reinforces the biological plausibility of De la Torre Canales 2024's reported mandibular bone-volume loss after BoNT-A in M-TMD patients: muscle paralysis → mechanical unloading → bone loss.
- For implant therapy in patients with neuromuscular pathology, long-term BTX exposure, or other mechanical-unloading conditions, biomimetic (SLA / SLAnano / equivalent) surfaces are strongly preferred over smooth machined surfaces.
- Suggests a future biologic-augmented implant strategy: surface roughness + local osteogenic factor in degradable hydrogel.
- For clinical dental implant placement following BTX therapy in masseter/temporalis (e.g., implant overdenture bruxer treated per Ali 2021), a buffer period may be prudent and a biomimetic-surface implant is preferable on mechanistic grounds.
- Animal-to-human translation requires caution: jaw bone is not femoral bone, but the mechanical-unloading principle is shared.
