## 한줄요약
Narrative review (28편): 검토 결과 muscular-etiology gummy smile에서 BTX-A 2.0-2.5 IU/region, 효과 onset 1-2주, 지속 4-6개월; Yonsei point + 부위별 4-point 기법이 표준; ptosis·smile 비대칭은 dose-dependent 합병증.

## Summary
Narrative review of 28 studies on BTX-A for gummy smile, covering subtype classification (Mazzuco-Hexsel: anterior/posterior/asymmetric/mixed), dosing (2.0-5.0 IU per muscle region, gender-adjusted), Yonsei point injection technique, FDA-approved formulations (onabotulinum, abobotulinum, incobotulinum toxin A), and complications. Confirms BTX-A as effective non-surgical alternative for muscular-etiology gummy smile with effect onset at 1-2 weeks and duration of 4-6 months; standard dose 2.0-2.5 U per muscle region, with adjustments based on gender and severity.

## Key Contributions
- Tabulates 9 clinical trials with dose/n/technique/outcome
- Frames BTX-A indication as 3-fold: corrective (muscular cause), adjuvant (combined with ortho/lip aug), palliative (pre-surgical bridge)
- Highlights adjuncts: zinc supplementation before BTX-A may enhance efficacy (Shemais 2021)
- Documents subtype-specific injection sites: anterior (lateral to nasal ala), posterior (lateral to ala + 2 cm further at tragus line with half dose), mixed (combined)
- Lists contraindications: pregnancy/lactation, calcium channel blockers, aminoglycosides/cyclosporine, neuromuscular disease, BTX/saline hypersensitivity
- Compares with surgery: BTX-A safer and equal/superior outcomes to lip repositioning (Makkeiah)

## Methodology
- Databases: PubMed, Google Scholar; no time restriction
- Keyword set: "botulinum toxin", "Botox", "gummy smile" (Boolean)
- 62 papers screened → 28 included
- No PRISMA, no risk-of-bias appraisal, no meta-analysis (narrative format)
- Single author, no independent second reviewer

## Results

### Subtype classification (Mazzuco-Hexsel)
| Subtype | Definition | Target muscles |
|---|---|---|
| Anterior | >3 mm between canines | LLSAN |
| Posterior | normal anterior, >3 mm distal to canines | Zygomaticus |
| Asymmetric | severe one-sided exposure | Asymmetric ZM or LLSAN |
| Mixed | severe in both regions | ZM + LLSAN |

### Vertical maxillary excess severity
| Degree | Exposure | Suggested treatment |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 2-4 mm | Orthodontic / perio / prostho |
| 2 | 4-8 mm | Mixed approach incl. BTX-A |
| 3 | >8 mm | Orthognathic surgery |

### Selected clinical-trial data (from cited tables)
| Author | Year | Dose | n | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Polo | 2008 | 2.5 IU | 30 | 5.2 mm mean reduction; transitory |
| Suber | 2014 | 5 IU | 14 | LLSAN + LLS effective |
| Mazzuco-Hexsel | 2010 | 2.5-5 IU | 16 | 75.1% mean improvement |
| Aldhaher-Bede | 2022 | 2.5 & 5 IU | 40 | 4-point > 2-point technique |
| Shemais | 2021 | 3 IU | 25 | Zinc supp enhances effect |
| Skaria | 2020 | 2.5 IU | 20 | 4.93 → 3.71 mm |
| Gong | 2021 | 2 IU | 29 | Gender + severity drive dose |
| Cengiz | 2020 | 5 IU | 28 | Relapse at month 4 |
| Duruel | 2019 | 5 IU | 3 | Yonsei point safe ≤5 IU |

### Onset and duration
- Onset: 1-2 weeks
- Peak: ~2 weeks
- Duration: 4-6 months (most studies); some report 3 months

### Complications
Localized: inflammation, nerve palsy, pain, hematoma, infection, bruising, edema, muscle weakness. Technique-dependent: speech difficulty, asymmetric smile, drinking/chewing problems. Overdose: ptosis / lip drooping that covers teeth on smiling.

## Clinical Implications
- For first-time gummy smile patients with muscular hyperactivity: start with 2.0-2.5 U per region; dose-adjust upward for males/larger muscle volume
- Re-injection should not be done before complete clearance of prior dose (avoid antibody formation)
- Confirm gingival display is not from short clinical crowns (refer to periodontist) before BTX-A
- Patient counseling: effect lasts 4-6 months, re-injection cycle expected
- Subtype-driven planning: anterior → LLSAN; posterior → ZM at tragus line; mixed → both with reduced dose at nasal ala point
