## One-line Summary
Single-center head-to-head RCT (n=52) in refractory chronic localized masticatory myalgia: both bilateral BTA (100 U) and single-session percutaneous needle electrolysis (PNE, 0.5 mA × 3 s × 3) reduced VAS pain (≈6.5 → ≈2.6) and improved MIO (+4 mm), sustained through 90 days, with no between-group difference; only PNE caused mild adverse events (4 cases of bruising/pain).

## Summary
This Seville-based RCT randomized 52 adults with refractory chronic localized masticatory myalgia (>12 months) and active trigger points to either bilateral BTA injection (100 U total) or single-session percutaneous needle electrolysis (PNE, 0.5 mA × 3 s × 3 applications). Both treatments produced statistically significant within-group improvements in pain (VAS ≈6.5 → ≈2.6), maximum interincisal opening (+4 mm), lateral movements, protrusion, and quality of life from day 28 and sustained through day 90 (p < 0.001 within-group). No between-group difference emerged on any variable; only PNE produced mild adverse events (4 cases of bruising/pain at the puncture site).

## Key Contributions
- Head-to-head comparison of two minimally invasive therapies (BTA vs. PNE) in carefully phenotyped refractory localized masticatory myalgia.
- Demonstrates equivalence: clinician choice can be informed by AE profile and patient preference rather than expected efficacy.
- 90-day follow-up sufficient to show sustained improvement after a single session of either intervention.
- DC/TMD-aligned diagnostic restriction (localized myalgia, not referred or myofascial-referred pain) sharpens applicability.
- EMG + MRI baseline rules out internal joint pathology — strengthens phenotype consistency.

## Methodology
RCT, single center, 52 adults (26 BTA, 26 PNE) with chronic localized MM > 12 months and active TrPs. BTA arm: 100 U distributed bilaterally in masseter and temporalis, single session. PNE arm: 0.5 mA / 3 s / 3 consecutive applications via puncture needle, single session. Outcomes (baseline, days 28/60/90): pain (VAS), MIO (mm), lateral right/left (mm), protrusion (mm), 100-point QoL questionnaire, patient/observer efficacy & tolerance (0-4). Stats: Mann-Whitney intergroup, Friedman intragroup, Wilcoxon paired between consecutive checkpoints.

## Results
| Variable | BTA (Day 0 → Day 90) | PNE (Day 0 → Day 90) | Intragroup p (both) | Intergroup p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| VAS pain | 6.50 → 2.58 | 6.42 → 2.46 | <0.001 | ns (0.872 at D90) |
| MIO (mm) | 37.81 → 42.73 | 40.15 → 44.69 | <0.001 | ns (0.104 at D90) |
| Lateral right (mm) | 5.08 → 7.19 | 6.08 → 7.12 | <0.001 | ns |
| Lateral left (mm) | 5.54 → 7.23 | 6.15 → 7.08 | <0.001 (BTA) | ns |
| Protrusion (mm) | 5.19 → 7.04 | 5.46 → 6.54 | <0.001 | ns |
| 100-point QoL | 70.83 → 86.65 | 67.63 → 85.73 | <0.001 | ns |
| Adverse events | 0 | 4 mild (pain/bruising) | — | — |

- Most improvement occurred between day 0 and day 28; values stable thereafter.

## Clinical Implications
- For refractory chronic localized masticatory myalgia, PNE is a credible non-pharmacological alternative to BTA with equivalent efficacy at 90 days.
- A single session of either intervention provides a 3-month durability of benefit in this population.
- AE profile favors BTA in this trial (no AEs vs. 4 mild PNE cases), but PNE avoids drug exposure and muscle atrophy risk.
- Reasonable to prefer PNE in patients reluctant to receive BTX, in younger patients (cumulative atrophy concern), or where masticatory performance preservation is critical.
- Use only after conservative therapy failure and confirmed localized (non-referred) myalgia on DC/TMD.
