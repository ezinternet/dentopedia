## One-line Summary

Prospective study (n=200 completed/320 enrolled, 2-year follow-up, Iraq) of BTX injection into masseter + temporalis tender points for myofascial pain showed sustained reduction (VAS 7.99→3.87 at 2yr); early pain response moderated by age/socioeconomic factors; long-term outcomes equalized. Abstract-only.

## Summary

This prospective study from Basrah, Iraq is currently the longest published follow-up (2 years) for botulinum toxin injection into masticatory muscles for myofascial pain. With 320 enrolled patients and 200 completing the full 2-year protocol, it provides the most temporally extended evidence that early, large pain reductions from BTX injections are maintained — rather than fading — at the 2-year mark.

The study also contributes a rarely reported clinical finding: while demographic and socioeconomic factors (younger age, higher income, higher education) predict better **early** pain response (at 8 weeks), these advantages disappear by the 2-year assessment — long-term outcomes appear demographically equitable. This has clinical implications for counseling patients who initially respond less well.

The high dropout rate (37.5% did not complete) is a limitation that could introduce selection bias toward patients with better outcomes.

## Key Contributions

- Longest prospective follow-up for BTX in masticatory myofascial pain: **2 years**
- Large real-world sample: 320 enrolled, 200 completed
- **Sustained pain reduction maintained** to 2-year endpoint (not a transient effect)
- Identifies clinical moderators: age 26–35 yr, higher income/education → better **early** response only
- **Long-term results converge** regardless of demographic factors — important for patient counseling
- Targets tender points (not fixed anatomical sites) — reflects real-world injection practice

## Methodology

- **Design**: Prospective clinical study; Basrah, Iraq; January 2019 – November 2024
- **Sample**: 320 enrolled; 200 completed 2-year follow-up
- **Intervention**: BTX injections at tender points of masseter and temporalis
- **Outcome**: Pain intensity scale (baseline → serial follow-ups → 2yr)
- **Subgroups**: Age, gender, marital status, income, education level

## Results

- Baseline pain: 7.99 (SD 0.79)
- 2-year pain: 3.87 (SD 1.53)
- Largest reduction: early follow-up phase; sustained through 2 years
- Age 26–35 yr: lowest pain at 8 weeks
- Income/education advantages: present early → converge by 2 years
- Gender difference: small
