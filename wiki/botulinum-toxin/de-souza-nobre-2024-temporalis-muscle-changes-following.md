## One-line Summary
Triple-blinded, placebo-controlled RCT (n=26 women with masseter hypertrophy) showing repeated masseter abobotulinumtoxinA injection induces dose-cumulative compensatory temporalis hypertrophy (greater thickness, p<0.02, and EMG, p<0.01, at 6 mo) plus emergent temporalis-localized pain (7/12 in the multiple-injection group at 6 mo, p<0.02).

## Summary
This level-I, triple-blinded, placebo-controlled RCT enrolled 26 Brazilian women (25–50 y, Merz II–V) with masseter hypertrophy (MH). All received 75 U abobotulinumtoxinA (Dysport) bilaterally in the masseter at baseline. At 3 months they were randomized 1:1 to a second injection of either saline (S-BoNT-A, n=11) or BoNT-A (M-BoNT-A, n=12). Temporalis thickness (ultrasound at MVC), EMG (RMS), masseter prominence (Merz), and VAS pain were tracked at 1, 3, and 6 months.

The temporalis muscle, which works in functional concert with the masseter, showed a measurable **compensatory increase in thickness and EMG activity**. The increase was transient (3 mo only) after a single injection but **persisted and amplified** after a second injection. At 6 months, the multiple-injection group additionally exhibited a clinically meaningful shift in pain location: 7/12 reported pain exclusively in the temporalis, despite no overall change in VAS intensity. The asymmetric pattern (right > left for both thickness and EMG) was not explained by the authors.

This is the first trial to capture temporalis structure, function, and pain together — establishing repeated masseter BoNT-A as carrying a non-trivial, time-cumulative adverse profile on the neighboring masticatory muscle.

## Key Contributions
- First trial to assess **temporalis thickness, EMG, and pain together** after masseter BoNT-A.
- Quantifies **dose-cumulative compensatory hypertrophy** of temporalis: significant inter-group difference in thickness (p<0.02) and EMG (p<0.01) at 6 mo favoring M-BoNT-A.
- Identifies **temporalis pain emergence** as a delayed adverse event of repeated masseter injection (p<0.02 inter-group at 6 mo for painful-region prevalence).
- Supports a cautious "single low-dose session" strategy for aesthetic MH treatment when pure aesthetics is the goal.

## Methodology
- Design: longitudinal, randomized, triple-blinded, placebo-controlled trial; CONSORT-compliant; secondary analysis of a larger MH trial (RBR-7tdjcn5).
- Population: 26 women, mean age 32.5 ± 8.04 y; Merz II–V; completers n=22 (S=10, M=12).
- Intervention (baseline, all): 75 U abobotulinumtoxinA per masseter, 3 points × 25 U bilaterally (total 150 U). Injection zone: tragus-to-mouth-corner line, 0.5 cm above mandibular border, between anterior/posterior masseter belly markings; 26G needles.
- Randomization (3 mo): saline (0.3 mL/side) vs second identical BoNT-A dose; blocks of 4; sealed envelopes opened by independent researcher.
- Outcomes (baseline, 1, 3, 6 mo):
  - Ultrasound (SonoScape A6, L745 11.0–5.0 MHz, 0.01 mm accuracy; intra-observer Kappa 0.86) — anterior temporalis thickness at MVC, mean of 3 measurements.
  - Surface EMG (Miotool NG USB, 10–700 Hz, 3000 Hz sampling, RMS of 5 s MVC × 3, anterior temporalis electrodes 1 cm lateral to eyebrow, sternal reference).
  - Merz photonumeric scale (10-point, rest & contracted).
  - VAS 0–10 + pain-region drawing.
- Statistics: Log10(x+1) transformation for thickness; two-way RM ANOVA + Sidak; Friedman + Mann-Whitney for Merz; α=0.05.

## Results
### Temporalis thickness (US, MVC)
- Right: both groups ↑ at 3 mo (p<0.02); persistence to 6 mo only in M-BoNT-A (p<0.01). Inter-group: M > S at 6 mo (p<0.02).
- Left: both groups ↑ at 3 mo (p<0.01); only M-BoNT-A maintained at 6 mo (p<0.001). No inter-group difference at any timepoint.

### Temporalis EMG (RMS at MVC)
- Right: only M-BoNT-A showed significant ↑ at 6 mo (p<0.03). Inter-group: M > S at 6 mo (p<0.01).
- Left: no significant intra- or inter-group changes.

### Masseter prominence (Merz)
- S-BoNT-A: improved in rest position at 3 and 6 mo (p<0.03).
- M-BoNT-A: improved in rest (p<0.02) and contracted (p<0.0001) at all follow-ups.
- Inter-group: M superior to S only at 1 mo (rest p<0.02; contracted p<0.03). No difference at 6 mo.

### Pain
- VAS intensity: baseline 3.4 ± 3.1 (S) and 2.6 ± 2.2 (M); no significant change over time or between groups.
- Pain location: at 6 mo, **7/12 M-BoNT-A patients reported pain exclusively in temporalis** (inter-group p<0.02); not seen in S-BoNT-A.

## Clinical Implications
- For pure aesthetic MH treatment in women, **a single low-dose session** captures most of the subjective masseter-prominence benefit (S and M both improved at rest); repeated injections add little visible benefit beyond 1 mo but add structural and symptomatic burden on the temporalis.
- Counsel patients about **temporal hollowing reversal / oval-face shift** and possible temporalis pain when planning a maintenance regimen.
- For patients already showing temporal fullness or temporalis tenderness on palpation, **defer or down-titrate** the second injection.
- Pre-injection palpation of temporalis pain, US baseline of temporalis thickness, and follow-up EMG-if-symptomatic add objective tracking — these are reasonable additions in a clinic doing repeat aesthetic BoNT.
- The masseter–temporalis functional couple should be assessed together, not in isolation, when MH treatment is offered.
