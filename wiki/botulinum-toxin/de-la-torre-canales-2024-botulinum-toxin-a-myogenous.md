## Summary
This Karolinska-led umbrella review aggregates 18 high-quality systematic reviews of BoNT-A for myogenous temporomandibular disorders (M-TMD), the highest-evidence synthesis to date. BoNT-A reduces pain more than placebo but does not outperform standard treatments (splints, physiotherapy, pharmacotherapy), and provides no MMO benefit over either. The authors highlight a substantive adverse-event profile — muscle atrophy, fatty infiltration, bite-force reduction, and mandibular/condylar bone-volume loss — and explicitly recommend BoNT-A as a last-line therapy for M-TMD.

## Key Contributions
- Sets the current ceiling of evidence on BoNT-A in M-TMD by restricting inclusion to high-quality SRs only (Aromataris methodology).
- Separates the "vs. placebo" benefit from the "vs. standard care" non-superiority — clarifying clinical misinterpretation.
- Provides a structured AE inventory across muscle and bone compartments, not just safety counts.
- Issues a directive clinical recommendation: BoNT-A is not first-line for M-TMD.
- PROSPERO-registered (CRD42023468160), multi-language search, 8 databases through Dec 2023.

## Methodology
Umbrella review per Aromataris methodology. PICOTS: adult M-TMD patients (P); intramuscular BoNT-A any dose (I); no treatment, placebo, conservative or invasive comparators (C); pain (VAS/NRS), MMO, adverse events (O); short/intermediate/long follow-up (T); SRs only (S). Eight databases including MEDLINE, EMBASE, CINAHL, Cochrane CENTRAL, Web of Science, Epistemonikos, ClinicalTrials.gov, ICTRP through 6 Dec 2023. Two reviewers screened via Rayyan. Only SRs scored high-quality on the umbrella-review-methodology checklist were retained (n=18).

## Results
| Comparison | Outcome | Finding |
|---|---|---|
| BoNT-A vs. placebo | Pain (VAS/NRS) | Significant reduction in favor of BoNT-A |
| BoNT-A vs. standard treatments | Pain | No superiority |
| BoNT-A vs. placebo or standard | MMO | No superiority |
| BoNT-A vs. controls | Adverse events on muscle | Higher risk (atrophy, fatty replacement, weakness, reduced bite force, decreased masticatory performance) |
| BoNT-A vs. controls | Adverse events on bone | Higher risk (mandibular bone volume loss, condylar and alveolar bone changes) |

Authors conclude evidence is moderate-quality for efficacy and that the AE signal is clinically meaningful enough to justify last-line positioning.

## Clinical Implications
- BoNT-A is not first-line for M-TMD; reserve for refractory cases after splint, physiotherapy, behavioral, and pharmacological trials.
- Informed consent must include muscle atrophy/weakness and bony changes (mandibular, condylar, alveolar) — not merely transient injection-site effects.
- Repeated dosing should be carefully weighed given cumulative AE risk on bony tissue.
- "Positive vs. placebo" published findings should not be interpreted as superiority over existing M-TMD standard care.
