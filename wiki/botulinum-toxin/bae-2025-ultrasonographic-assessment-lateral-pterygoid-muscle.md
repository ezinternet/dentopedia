## One-line Summary
Anatomical/ultrasonographic cross-sectional study (60 healthy young Korean women, 120 hemifaces) quantifying lateral pterygoid muscle (LPM) skin-to-muscle depth (30.9 ± 1.9 mm) and a mandibular-notch approach landmark (39.6 mm anterior, 7.8 mm inferior to the tragus), with 100% visualization of masseter/temporalis/LPM — operationalizing radiation-free US-guided BoNT-A LPM injection.

## Summary
The lateral pterygoid muscle (LPM) lies deep in the infratemporal fossa, with its upper head fibers continuous with the TMJ articular disc and capsule. Because the muscle is hard to reach safely without imaging, BoNT-A injections in the LPM have relied on CT or MRI guidance — accurate but complex, time-consuming, and costly. This Yonsei University communication establishes a practical anatomical foundation for **ultrasound-guided LPM injection via the mandibular-notch (sigmoid-notch) window**.

Using a 15 MHz linear transducer on 120 hemifaces from 60 healthy young Korean female volunteers (mean age 36.7 ± 3.5 y) with mouths opened 2 cm, the masseter, temporalis, and LPM were visualized in **all 60 subjects (100%)**, layered superficial-to-deep in that order. The midpoint (MP) of the mandibular-notch window was located 39.6 ± 3.3 mm anterior and 7.8 ± 1.6 mm inferior to the tragus. From skin to LPM at the MP, the path traverses 9.7 ± 1.0 mm of skin+subcutaneous tissue, 10.3 ± 1.3 mm of masseter, and 10.9 ± 1.6 mm of temporalis, with **LPM depth at 30.9 ± 1.9 mm**.

These numbers operationalize a real-time, radiation-free, low-cost alternative to CT/MRI guidance for repeated BoNT-A LPM injection — particularly relevant for TMD patients who may need maintenance dosing.

## Key Contributions
- Defines reproducible surface-landmark coordinates (CorP, MP, ConP) relative to the ala-tragus line for US-based LPM targeting.
- Quantifies the **depth budget** for needle planning (skin to LPM ≈ 31 mm).
- Confirms 100% feasibility of visualizing all three masticatory muscles (masseter, temporalis, LPM) via the mandibular-notch window in healthy volunteers.
- Provides a non-ionizing, real-time methodology complement to CT-based muscle-segmentation approaches.

## Methodology
- 60 healthy Korean female volunteers, 120 hemifaces, mean age 36.7 ± 3.5 y.
- Exclusions: TMJ disorder, obvious facial asymmetry, prior plastic surgery, BoNT injection ≤6 mo prior.
- Device: ALPINION E-CUBE 15 PLATINUM with 15 MHz linear transducer.
- Procedure:
  - Skin landmarks pre-marked with waterproof eyeliner.
  - Thick US gel layer to avoid tissue compression.
  - Subject seated upright; mouth opened 2 cm.
  - Transducer aligned/rotated until both coronoid and condylar processes were simultaneously visible on the same plane.
  - Side-view photograph captured to record transducer position.
- Landmarks (relative to ala-tragus line):
  - CorP — coronoid process.
  - ConP — condylar process (lateral margin).
  - MP — midpoint between CorP and ConP (used for thickness/depth measurements).
- Measurements at MP (ImageJ): skin+subcutaneous, masseter, temporalis thicknesses; depth from skin to LPM.

## Results
### Layer thicknesses / LPM depth at MP (mean ± SD, mm)
| Layer | Value |
|---|---|
| Skin + subcutaneous | 9.7 ± 1.0 |
| Masseter | 10.3 ± 1.3 |
| Temporalis | 10.9 ± 1.6 |
| LPM depth from skin | **30.9 ± 1.9** |

### Landmark coordinates relative to ala-tragus line (mean ± SD, mm)
| Landmark | Anterior to tragus | Inferior to ala-tragus line |
|---|---|---|
| CorP | 54.0 ± 3.9 | 10.7 ± 2.8 |
| MP   | 39.6 ± 3.3 | 7.8 ± 1.6 |
| ConP | 25.2 ± 3.0 | 4.9 ± 1.9 |

### Visualization yield
Masseter, temporalis, and LPM visualized in all 60 subjects (100%) via the mandibular-notch window.

## Clinical Implications
- For US-guided extraoral LPM BoNT-A injection in a healthy Korean female chair-patient, plan needle entry approximately **40 mm anterior, 8 mm inferior to the tragus**, with **30–32 mm penetration depth** to reach the LPM center.
- Use a 22-G needle of ≥35 mm length; advance under live US visualization rather than blind depth marking.
- Switch on Doppler to identify and avoid the maxillary artery and pterygoid venous plexus before injecting — the paper notes Doppler capability but does not map vessels here, so this is a manual clinical step.
- The numbers are derived from healthy young Korean females; **scale expectations** for males, older patients, or higher BMI (skin+subcutaneous likely thicker), and adjust for unilateral hypertrophy or atrophy.
- US guidance complements (and may replace, in many indications) CT or MRI for repeated BoNT-A regimens because of zero ionizing radiation and immediate dynamic confirmation.
- The mandibular-notch "window" requires 2 cm mouth opening — confirm passive opening capacity before planning the approach in DDWoR or trismus patients.
- For TMD-specific applications, complement with the CT-segmentation 3D guide approach (Raafa 2025) when the LPM is unusually deep, asymmetric, or when the operator wants pre-planned trajectory documentation.
