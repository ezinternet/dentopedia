## One-line Summary
Triple-blinded RCT (n=26 women) on masseter hypertrophy: a single 75 U abobotulinumtoxinA injection per masseter caused transient thinning and EMG suppression that recovered by 6 months, whereas a second injection produced persistent masseter thinning and EMG suppression (p<0.05) without additional aesthetic (Merz) benefit, while masticatory performance was impaired in both groups — multiple injections add functional cost without incremental cosmetic gain.

## Summary
Companion paper to [[botulinum-toxin/de-souza-nobre-2024-temporalis-muscle-changes-following]] — same 26-woman cohort, same protocol, same randomization. This paper reports the masseter-side primary outcomes (thickness, EMG, masticatory performance, prominence).

Twenty-six Brazilian women (25–50 y, Merz II–V) received 75 U abobotulinumtoxinA per masseter at baseline; at 3 months they were randomized to either saline (G1, n=11) or a second identical BoNT-A dose (G2, n=12). Masseter thickness, EMG, masticatory performance (bicolor-gum color-mixing test), and Merz prominence were tracked at 1, 3, and 6 months.

A single injection caused **transient** masseter thinning and EMG suppression that largely recovered by 6 months. A second injection produced **persistent** thinning (left masseter G2 < G1 at 6 mo, p<0.02), persistently suppressed EMG (G1 > G2 at 6 mo bilaterally, p<0.005 right, p<0.044 left), and identical masticatory-performance loss to G1 across all follow-ups. Subjective masseter-prominence improvement (Merz) was achieved equally by both groups by 3–6 months, so the second injection delivered no incremental aesthetic gain beyond month 1.

The headline trade-off: **multiple injections produce functional adverse effects in the masseter without delivering a clear additional aesthetic benefit**.

## Key Contributions
- Demonstrates **decoupling of subjective aesthetic benefit from objective muscle changes**: Merz scores improve in both groups but only G2 shows lasting structural/functional cost.
- Quantifies **masticatory-performance loss** with an objective, validated colorimetric method (ViewGum VOH); both groups remained significantly impaired at 6 mo (p<0.03).
- Provides masseter-side numbers (thickness in mm, EMG in μV) that are directly usable for clinical counselling.
- Pairs with the temporalis companion paper to show that the *masticatory-system whole* — not just the injected muscle — must be considered.

## Methodology
- Identical design to the temporalis companion paper. Brief recap:
  - Triple-blinded, placebo-controlled RCT, CONSORT-compliant.
  - 26 women, Merz II–V, ages 25–50, mean 32.5 ± 8.04 y.
  - Baseline injection: 75 U abobotulinumtoxinA (Dysport, 25 U/0.1 mL) per masseter, 3 sites × 25 U bilaterally, 26G needle.
  - 3-month randomization (blocks of 4): G1 saline 0.3 mL/side, G2 second identical BoNT-A.
  - Visits: screen, baseline (Visit 2), 1 mo, 3 mo (randomization), 6 mo.
- Outcomes (this paper):
  - Masseter US thickness at MVC (SonoScape A6, L745 11.0–5.0 MHz, 0.01 mm accuracy, Kappa 0.86) — **primary**.
  - Masseter surface EMG (Miotool NG USB, 10–700 Hz, 3000 Hz sampling, RMS over 5 s MVC × 3).
  - Masticatory performance: 20-cycle bicolor chewing-gum (Vivident Fruitswing Karpuz) → 1 mm flattening → 300 dpi scan → ViewGum colorimetric Variance of Hue (VOH); higher = worse mixing.
  - Merz prominence scale (rest and contracted).
- Statistics: Log10(x+1) transformation for thickness; two-way RM ANOVA + Sidak post-hoc; Friedman + Mann-Whitney for Merz; SPSS 28, α=0.05.
- Completers: G1 n=10, G2 n=12.

## Results
### Masseter thickness (US, MVC, mm)
| | Baseline | 1 mo | 3 mo | 6 mo |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Right G1 | 14.29 ± 1.73 | 12.92 ± 2.61 | 13.81 ± 1.74 | 14.10 ± 1.85 |
| Right G2 | 14.73 ± 2.14 | 13.68 ± 2.31 | 14.47 ± 2.10 | 13.94 ± 1.61 |
| Left G1  | 13.95 ± 1.67 | 13.05 ± 2.30 | 14.56 ± 2.19 | 15.20 ± 1.45 |
| Left G2  | 14.68 ± 2.23 | 13.24 ± 2.81 | 15.00 ± 1.14 | 13.90 ± 2.45 |
- Both groups ↓ at 1 mo (p<0.05). Right masseter: no inter-group difference. **Left masseter: G2 < G1 at 6 mo (p<0.02)**.

### Masseter EMG (RMS μV, MVC)
| | Baseline | 1 mo | 3 mo | 6 mo |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Right G1 | 273.72 ± 174.64 | 48.63 ± 20.29 | 88.39 ± 48.89 | 178.83 ± 93.15 |
| Right G2 | 226.40 ± 103.09 | 35.89 ± 15.69 | 83.99 ± 42.39 | 81.77 ± 48.69 |
| Left G1  | 243.23 ± 145.11 | 49.44 ± 51.85 | 78.23 ± 58.86 | 163.12 ± 118.70 |
| Left G2  | 267.54 ± 117.01 | 40.38 ± 15.98 | 91.21 ± 34.16 | 74.49 ± 48.29 |
- **G1 > G2 at 6 mo: right p<0.005, left p<0.044** — single-injection EMG recovers, double-injection EMG does not.

### Masticatory performance (VOH, higher = worse)
- G1: 0.06 ± 0.02 → 0.27 ± 0.17 → 0.18 ± 0.12 → 0.34 ± 0.06.
- G2: 0.05 ± 0.01 → 0.16 ± 0.13 → 0.24 ± 0.17 → 0.32 ± 0.13.
- Both groups significantly worse than baseline at every follow-up (p<0.03). No inter-group difference.

### Masseter prominence (Merz)
- G1: significant improvement at 3 mo and 6 mo in rest (p<0.02 and p<0.01); no change contracted.
- G2: significant improvement at all follow-ups in both rest (p<0.02) and contracted (p<0.0001).
- Inter-group: G2 superior only at 1 mo (rest p<0.020; contracted p<0.036).

## Clinical Implications
- **Single low-dose session is the rational default for aesthetic MH** if the goal is appearance; subjective Merz benefit at 3–6 mo is equivalent to the multiple-injection regimen at much lower functional cost.
- **Repeated injections** carry persistent masseter thinning, sustained EMG suppression, and equally impaired masticatory mixing — discuss explicitly with the patient before any "maintenance" injection.
- **Masticatory performance was reduced in both groups** even after a single injection — patients should be warned about reduced chewing efficiency for several months regardless of dose strategy.
- Pair the masseter outcomes here with the temporalis findings (compensatory hypertrophy + temporalis pain in repeated injections) when counselling: the whole masticatory system, not just the target muscle, changes.
- For MH patients who also have TMD/myofascial pain or bruxism, BoNT-A may still be defensible as therapeutic; for pure aesthetics in healthy chewers, the risk-benefit is less favorable than commonly assumed.
