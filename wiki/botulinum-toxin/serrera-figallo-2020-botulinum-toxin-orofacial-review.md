## Summary
This narrative review from the University of Sevilla provides a comprehensive clinical reference for 보툴리눔독소 (Botulinum Toxin, BTX) applications across orofacial dental practice. Coverage includes 악관절증 (TMD) myofascial pain, 수면이갈이 (Sleep Bruxism), trigeminal neuralgia, sialorrhea, gummy smile correction, and facial aesthetics. The review details anatomical injection sites, dosing recommendations by muscle, and product equivalence between BTX formulations. It serves as a practical clinical guide rather than an evidence synthesis, providing actionable protocol information that complements the evidence-based SR+MA literature.

## Key Contributions
- Comprehensive orofacial BTX indication map: TMD, bruxism, neuralgia, sialorrhea, cosmetic
- Practical dosing reference: masseter 25–75 U, temporalis 15–50 U (OnabotulinumtoxinA equivalents)
- Describes injection landmarks and needle placement techniques
- Discusses BTX product comparison (Botox/Dysport/Xeomin) and dose conversion
- Highlights long-term effects: muscle volume reduction (masseter 20–40% volume loss with repeated injections)

## Methodology
- Narrative review; no systematic search
- Based on published literature and expert clinical experience
- Organized by clinical indication
- Includes anatomical considerations per injection site

## Results
- **Masseter injection** (for bruxism/myofascial TMD): 25–75 U OnabotulinumtoxinA per side; 2–5 injection points
- **Temporalis injection**: 15–50 U per side; temporal fossa approach
- **Lateral pterygoid** (선택적): 10–25 U; complex anatomy requires imaging guidance
- Effect onset: 3–5 days; peak: 2–4 weeks; duration: 3–6 months
- Long-term repeated use: significant masseter atrophy (20–40% volume reduction)
- **Gummy smile**: 2.5–5 U per injection point (upper lip levators, LLSAN)
- Adverse effects: injection site pain, asymmetric smile, temporary chewing weakness
