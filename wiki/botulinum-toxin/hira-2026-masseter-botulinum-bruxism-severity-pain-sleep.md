## One-line Summary

Prospective study (n=80, 6-month follow-up) of masseter BoNT-A injection for bruxism showed rapid BDI reduction at 1 month (20.8→4.3) with partial rebound by 6 months, sustained PSQI improvement (10.0→2.8 at 6mo), and significant VAS pain reduction; sleep quality was the most durable outcome. Abstract-only.

## Summary

This prospective study from a single center (n=80, 6-month follow-up) is notable for using PSQI as a systematic sleep quality outcome — a measure rarely employed as a primary endpoint in bruxism BoNT-A studies, which more commonly rely on VAS pain alone.

The key finding is a **temporal dissociation** between outcomes:
- **BDI** (bruxism severity / psychological burden proxy): large early gain at 1 month → **partial rebound** by 6 months
- **PSQI** (sleep quality): improvement initiated early → **sustained through 6 months** without rebound

This differential durability has practical significance: if a patient asks "what will still be better at 6 months?", the answer appears to be sleep quality rather than bruxism severity scores. This may support re-injection intervals tailored to bruxism severity rebound (shorter) vs sleep-focused patients (potentially longer).

## Key Contributions

- PSQI as systematic sleep quality endpoint in bruxism BoNT-A research (rare in literature)
- **Differential durability**: sleep quality (PSQI) sustained at 6 months; bruxism severity (BDI) shows partial rebound
- Temporal profile across 4 timepoints: baseline, 1mo, 3mo, 6mo — captures kinetics
- Provides patient counseling data: "sleep quality benefits outlast bruxism severity suppression"
- Significant VAS pain reduction throughout (p=0.004)

## Methodology

- **Design**: Prospective clinical study
- **Sample**: n=80 bruxism patients
- **Intervention**: Masseter BoNT-A injection (dose not specified in abstract)
- **Timepoints**: Baseline, 1 month, 3 months, 6 months
- **Outcomes**: BDI (Beck Depression Inventory), PSQI (Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index), VAS pain
- **Statistics**: Repeated measures ANOVA

## Results

| Outcome | Baseline | 1 month | 6 months | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| BDI | 20.8 ± 4.3 | 4.3 ± 1.5 | Partial rebound | <0.001 |
| PSQI | 10.0 ± 2.3 | — | 2.8 ± 1.0 (sustained) | <0.001 |
| VAS pain | — | — | Significant | 0.004 |

- PSQI threshold ≤5 (good sleep): direction favored (specific rate not in abstract)
- **Key pattern**: BDI rebounds, PSQI does not → distinct mechanisms or differential persistence of BoNT-A effect
