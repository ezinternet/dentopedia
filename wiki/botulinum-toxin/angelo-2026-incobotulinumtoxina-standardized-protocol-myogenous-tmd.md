## One-line Summary

Retrospective cohort (n=98, DC/TMD-confirmed myogenous TMD, 12-month follow-up) of a standardized incobotulinumtoxinA (Xeomin) injection protocol (~200U bilateral; masseter + temporalis + SCM) showed large reduction in myalgia (r=0.86), VAS pain (2.44→0.37), and preserved MMO; 79.6% favorable outcome rate.

## 한줄요약

DC/TMD로 진단 확정된 근육성 악관절장애 환자(n=98)에게 표준화된 incobotulinumtoxinA(Xeomin) 프로토콜(~200U, 교근·측두근·흉쇄유돌근 양측) 적용 12개월 후향 코호트 — 근통 대폭 감소(r=0.86), VAS 통증(2.44→0.37), 최대 개구량 유지, 79.6% 호전율.

## Summary

This 12-month retrospective cohort is notable for two methodological strengths absent from most BoNT-A TMD studies: (1) **DC/TMD diagnostic criteria** applied strictly (arthrogenous TMD excluded), and (2) a **fully described standardized injection protocol** (Ângelo Protocol) using incobotulinumtoxinA (Xeomin) rather than the more commonly studied onabotulinumtoxinA (Botox).

The Ângelo Protocol distributes ~200U bilaterally across three muscle groups: masseter (extra-oral 25U + intra-oral 15U per side), temporalis (35U per side), and sternocleidomastoid (25U per side). The sternocleidomastoid is included based on cervical-mandibular functional interaction and referred pain patterns — an unusual but rationally argued inclusion.

The key pharmacological argument for Xeomin: it is the only pure 150 kDa neurotoxin (no complexing proteins), conferring lower immunogenicity than Botox or Dysport. The authors advocate early adoption of incobotulinumtoxinA to prevent neutralizing antibody development, as antibodies may emerge before clinical resistance becomes apparent.

## Key Contributions

- Standardized, reproducible injection protocol with specific dose distribution per muscle reported
- DC/TMD diagnostic framework enforced — addresses long-standing comparability limitation
- 12-month follow-up data; one of the longest real-world BoNT-A TMD evaluations published
- 79.6% favorable composite outcome (myalgia 0–1 AND VAS ≤2 AND MMO ≥35mm)
- MMO preserved (negligible effect size, d=0.10) — functional safety demonstrated
- Introduces PUB-TT concept: BoNT-A as preoperative unloading before TMJ surgery
- IncobotulinumtoxinA: zero reported cases of immune resistance

## Methodology

- **Design**: Single-center retrospective cohort; Portugal; June 2022 – June 2025
- **Final n**: 98 (from 930 screened, 252 treated, 154 excluded for incomplete records/follow-up)
- **Demographics**: Mean age 45.55 ± 16.27; 85.71% female
- **Inclusion**: DC/TMD myogenous only, ≥3 months conservative failure, ≥12 months follow-up
- **Protocol**: ~200U incobotulinumtoxinA bilateral; 30G needles; SCM included
- **Adjunctive**: 5 physiotherapy sessions (10–15 days post-injection) + behavioral counseling
- **Statistics**: Wilcoxon (myalgia ordinal), paired t-test (VAS, MMO), Cohen's d/r

## Results

| Outcome | Baseline | 12-month | p | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Myalgia (0–3, median) | 3.0 | 0.0 | <0.001 | r=0.86 (large) |
| VAS pain (0–10, mean) | 2.44 ± 2.54 | 0.37 ± 1.15 | <0.001 | d=0.76 (mod-large) |
| MMO (mm, mean) | 42.55 ± 16.72 | 43.10 | 0.35 | d=0.10 (negligible) |

- Favorable outcome: 79.6%
- Treatment failures: 20 patients (12 reintervention — 7 repeat BoNT-A, 5 arthrocentesis)
- Arthrogenous progression: 5 patients (5.1%)
- Adverse events: transient local discomfort/mild muscle fatigue only
