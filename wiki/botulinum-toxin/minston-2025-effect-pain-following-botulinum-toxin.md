## One-line Summary
Swedish double-blind multicentre pilot RCT (n=45; BTX-A n=23 vs saline n=22) in DC/TMD-defined jaw myalgia: a single session of 100 U BTX-A across 14 masseter/temporalis sites did not significantly reduce diary-recorded days with functional jaw pain at 2 months (10.5 vs 14, p=0.585); adverse events were mild and transient in both arms.

## Summary
This Swedish multicentre pilot RCT randomized 45 adults with DC/TMD-defined jaw myalgia to a single session of 100 U Botox (BTX-A) or saline placebo across 14 sites in masseter and temporalis. The novel primary outcome — diary-recorded days with functional jaw pain at 2 months — did not differ significantly between groups (p = 0.585). Adverse events were mild and transient in both arms. Authors conclude that a single BTX-A session is not effective for jaw myalgia in this pilot, while leaving open the possibility that a properly powered study might detect efficacy.

## Key Contributions
- Methodologically rigorous pilot: double-blind, multicentre (6 specialist clinics), DC/TMD-defined population, calibrated examiners, independent randomisation.
- Innovates outcome measurement: "days with functional jaw pain" rather than instantaneous VAS — captures temporal pain burden.
- Reinforces national-level guidance (Swedish National Board of Health and Welfare) against BTX-A for myofascial pain.
- Reports per-site total dose (100 U / 14 sites) clearly, enabling protocol reproducibility.
- Honest reporting of underpowering — does not claim efficacy from a null pilot.

## Methodology
Double-blind, parallel-group, multicentre RCT in adults with DC/TMD jaw myalgia (≥3 months, ≥18 y). Intervention: 100 U BTX-A in 2.0 mL saline distributed across 14 IM injection sites in masseter and temporalis bilaterally. Control: identical-volume saline placebo. Three visits + 1 telephone call; 2-month follow-up. Diary-based outcome (days with functional jaw pain) collected for 14 days pre-intervention and at follow-up. Statistical comparison: between-group p-value on change in median days with pain.

## Results
| Parameter | BTX-A (n=23) | Saline (n=22) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Days with jaw pain at function, baseline median (IQR) | 14 (4) | 14.0 (1.5) | — |
| Days with jaw pain at function, 2 mo median (IQR) | 10.5 (6) | 14 (5) | 0.585 |
| Adverse events | mild, transient | mild, transient | similar |

- No significant difference in primary outcome.
- Both groups well-tolerated; no serious adverse events.
- Point estimate favors BTX-A but CI/IQR overlap is large and p-value clearly non-significant.

## Clinical Implications
- A single session of 100 U BTX-A distributed across masseter and temporalis should not be expected to meaningfully reduce days with functional jaw pain at 2 months.
- Avoid framing BTX-A as a default therapy for jaw myalgia — Sweden's national guidance against this use is supported by this trial.
- For clinical decisions, this pilot's null result should be considered alongside the SR+MA (Saini 2024) and umbrella review (De la Torre Canales 2024) reaching consistent conclusions.
- If BTX-A is still chosen, expect modest median-day improvement (≈3-4 days less) but no statistically distinguishable benefit over placebo at this dose and follow-up.
