## One-line Summary

SR+MA (36 studies, adults with sleep bruxism) comparing occlusal splints (OS) vs BTX-A: no significant difference in pain reduction at intermediate follow-up; BTX-A faster short-term relief; OS preferred for mechanical protection; moderate certainty of evidence. Abstract-only.

## Summary

This SR+MA directly answers the most common clinical comparison question in bruxism management: "occlusal splint or botulinum toxin?" With 36 included studies covering adults with sleep bruxism and a meta-analysis for pain outcomes, it provides the highest-level evidence available for this specific comparison.

**Bottom line**: The two interventions are **statistically equivalent for pain** at intermediate follow-up — neither is superior for analgesia in the medium term. However, they differ meaningfully in mechanism and clinical role:
- **BTX-A**: faster short-term onset; also reduces jaw muscle activity and bite force (temporarily)
- **OS**: mechanical protection of tooth surfaces; function maintenance; reversible; no muscle volume effects

The clinical decision algorithm that emerges: choose OS when tooth wear/mechanical protection is the primary concern; choose BTX-A when rapid pain relief is the priority or OS is not tolerated. The moderate certainty of evidence means this guidance is robust but not definitive.

## Key Contributions

- Highest-level evidence (SR+MA, n=36 studies) for OS vs BTX-A comparison in adult sleep bruxism
- **Pain equivalence at intermediate follow-up**: pivotal negative finding supporting OS-first strategy in most patients
- **BTX-A advantage**: faster short-term symptom relief
- **OS advantage**: mechanical tooth/restoration protection (irreplaceable function)
- Moderate certainty for pain; lower certainty for other outcomes (muscle activity, bite force)
- Temporomandibular joint–relevant: BTX-A associations with reduced jaw muscle activity and bite force are BTX-specific effects

## Methodology

- **Database search**: PubMed, Embase, Scopus, Web of Science, ScienceDirect, Google Scholar (1998–Feb 2025)
- **Included studies**: 36 studies (qualitative synthesis); meta-analysis for pain outcomes
- **Population**: Adults with sleep bruxism
- **Interventions**: OS vs BTX-A (various types and protocols)
- **Primary outcome**: Pain-related clinical sequelae
- **Secondary**: Jaw muscle activity, bite force, mandibular mobility, QoL, adverse events
- **Evidence certainty**: GRADE framework (moderate for pain; lower for others)

## Results

- **Pain (meta-analysis, intermediate follow-up)**: No statistically significant difference between OS and BTX-A
- **Short-term relief**: BTX-A faster in some studies
- **BTX-A confirmed effects**: Temporary jaw muscle activity ↓, bite force ↓
- **OS role**: Mechanical protection; functional maintenance
- **Evidence certainty**: Moderate (pain), low (other outcomes)
- **Guideline implication**: OS preferred as conservative first-line when mechanical sequelae prevention is primary goal
