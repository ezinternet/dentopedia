## One-line Summary
4-arm RCT (n=80, 12 weeks, NCT06583551) — BoNT-A, dry needling, pharmacological therapy, and manual therapy showed no between-group differences on VAS/MMO/OHIP-14; only the BoNT-A group showed a worsening trend in OHIP-14.

## Summary
This 4-arm parallel RCT (Hamidiye, Istanbul) randomized 80 bruxism patients with masticatory myalgia to BoNT-A, dry needling (DN), pharmacological therapy (PT), or manual therapy (MT) (20 each), measuring VAS, MMO, and OHIP-14 at baseline, 2, 4, and 12 weeks. All four treatments produced significant within-group VAS pain reduction (p<0.001 for BoNT-A/DN/MT; p=0.002 for PT), but no between-group differences at any timepoint and no MMO change in any group. The null hypothesis of equivalence was supported. OHIP-14 worsened over time only in the BoNT-A group (within-group p=0.020), suggesting non-pain QoL trade-offs.

## Key Contributions
- Only head-to-head RCT directly comparing BoNT-A with DN, PT, and MT in bruxism-myalgia.
- Provides a baseline for relative cost-effectiveness reasoning — three cheaper/less-invasive options match BoNT-A on the primary pain outcome.
- Distinct OHIP-14 signal in the BoNT-A arm is hypothesis-generating regarding QoL trade-offs (e.g., chewing function).
- CONSORT-aligned, NCT-registered, single-surgeon design controls operator variability.
- Mild and transient AE profile across all arms over 12 weeks.

## Methodology
Prospective single-center RCT, 80 adults (44F/36M) with bruxism (Pintado AB + AASM SB criteria), ASA I-II, moderate-to-severe muscle pain, no prior bruxism treatment. Random 1:1:1:1 to BoNT-A, DN, PT, MT (n=20 each). Same maxillofacial surgeon delivered all therapies. Outcomes: VAS (0-10), MMO via TheraBite ruler (mm), OHIP-14-TR. Timepoints: baseline, 2, 4, 12 weeks. Mixed-design repeated-measures two-way ANOVA + Tukey.

## Results
| Outcome | Baseline → 12 wk (BoNT-A) | Baseline → 12 wk (DN) | Baseline → 12 wk (PT) | Baseline → 12 wk (MT) | Between-group p |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| VAS | 6.60 → 3.25 | 6.15 → 4.15 | 5.15 → 3.75 | 6.05 → 3.50 | 0.807 (ns) |
| MMO (mm) | 39.55 → 42.95 | 43.05 → 44.40 | 37.65 → 40.80 | 45.00 → 44.25 | 0.530 (ns) |
| OHIP-14 | 12.05 → 16.95 | 12.75 → 13.95 | 13.30 → 13.95 | 14.70 → 15.30 | 0.816 (ns) |

- Within-group VAS reductions all significant (p≤0.002).
- Within-group MMO changes: not significant in any group.
- Within-group OHIP-14: significant change only in BoNT-A group (p=0.020), trending worse.
- Adverse events: bruising at BoNT-A injection sites; one DN patient with itching; no exclusions.

## Clinical Implications
- For bruxism-induced myalgia in the first 3 months, BoNT-A offers no superiority over DN, PT, or MT on pain or function — choose the least invasive option consistent with patient preference and cost.
- Watch for QoL trade-offs after BoNT-A despite pain relief — counsel patients on potential chewing/quality-of-life impact.
- DN and MT, with no drug exposure and lower cost, are viable first-line conservative interventions.
- Single-session BoNT-A here used masseter+temporalis bilateral injections per the De la Torre Canales 2022 protocol — same protocol used in many comparator RCTs.
