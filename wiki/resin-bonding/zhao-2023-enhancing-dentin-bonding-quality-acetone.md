## One-line Summary

In vitro microtensile study (60 third molars, 4 solvent groups): acetone wet-bonding preserved bond strength after thermocycling/collagenase aging (p<0.05) without affecting immediate strength, with less nanoleakage and suppressed MMP collagenolytic activity vs water wet-bonding.

## Summary

Zhao et al. tested whether acetone — a low-viscosity "water-chaser" solvent — could serve as a sole dentin preconditioning agent ("acetone wet-bonding", AWB) to improve adhesive durability. Using 60 third molars split into water (WWB), ethanol (EWB), 50% acetone, and 100% acetone (AWB) groups with Singlebond Universal, they measured microtensile bond strength immediately, after 10,000 thermocycles, and after one month of collagenase aging, plus nanoleakage, in situ zymography, and contact angle. Acetone did not boost immediate strength but it preserved strength after aging — the durability metric that matters clinically. Mechanistically, acetone lowered the dentin contact angle (better wettability), reduced nanoleakage, and suppressed hybrid-layer MMP activity, addressing the main collagen-degradation pathway. This refines the ethanol-centric wet-bonding literature by positioning acetone as a comparably effective, lower-viscosity alternative — though the evidence is bench-level only.

## Key Contributions

- Establishes acetone as a standalone wet-bonding preconditioner with a full durability/mechanism panel.
- Shows the benefit is durability (post-aging), not immediate strength.
- Links acetone to improved wettability and MMP suppression as the mechanistic basis.

## Methodology

4 groups × 15 teeth (WWB, EWB, 50%AWB, AWB). Mid-coronal dentin, 600-grit smear layer, 15 s 35% H3PO4 etch, rinse/blot, 60 s solvent primer, Singlebond Universal, light-cure, Charisma composite buildup. 12 beams/tooth → MTBS at 24 h, after 10,000 thermocycles (5/55 °C, 15 s dwell), and after 1-month 0.1 mg/mL collagenase aging. Plus failure-mode, nanoleakage (silver), in situ zymography, contact angle.

## Results

- Acetone preserved MTBS after thermocycling/collagenase aging (p<0.05).
- No difference in immediate 24 h MTBS (p>0.05).
- AWB < WWB nanoleakage.
- Significantly reduced dentin contact angle (improved wettability) with acetone.
- Suppressed hybrid-layer collagenolytic (MMP) activity in AWB.
