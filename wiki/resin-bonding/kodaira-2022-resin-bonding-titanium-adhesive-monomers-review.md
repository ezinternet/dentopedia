## One-line Summary

Narrative literature review (abstract-only) concluding that adhesive functional monomers (MDP, MEPS, 6-MHPA, 4-META) in primers/luting agents bond resin to titanium and titanium alloy as effectively as silanization plus tribochemical silica coating.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.2334/josnusd.22-0036](https://doi.org/10.2334/josnusd.22-0036)), this narrative review surveys the literature on titanium bonding systems, focusing on the role of the adhesive (functional) monomer in the resin luting agent, with the clinical aim of expanding the application of resin-bonded fixed prostheses made of titanium or titanium alloys. The motivation is Japan-specific: as palladium and gold prices rose, cast titanium restorations were added to the Japanese national health insurance (NHI) system, increasing the need for reliable resin-to-titanium luting. The review finds that adhesive monomers are effective for bonding to titanium and provide results equal to the procedures of silanization plus tribochemical silica coating; a primer or luting agent containing MDP, MEPS, 6-MHPA, or 4-META promotes the bonding characteristics between titanium and the resin material.

This page is **abstract-only** — the full text was not retrieved, so quantitative bond-strength values, aging/thermocycling data, and per-monomer ranking are not available.

Clinically: for resin cementation to a titanium or titanium-alloy framework, an MDP/MEPS/6-MHPA/4-META-containing primer or self-adhesive luting agent is a chemically driven alternative that performs on par with the more equipment-intensive silanization + tribochemical silica coating workflow — useful when chairside air-abrasion silica coating is impractical.

## Key Contributions

- Establishes that **functional adhesive monomers chemically bond resin to titanium**, not only to tooth structure and zirconia.
- Reports functional-monomer bonding as **equal to silanization + tribochemical silica coating** — a chemical route that can replace or complement the mechanical/silica-coating route.
- Names four effective monomers: **MDP, MEPS, 6-MHPA, 4-META** (phosphate / thiophosphate / phosphonate / carboxylate-anhydride chemistries).

## Methodology

- Narrative literature review (not systematic; no meta-analysis). No search protocol, evidence grading, or risk-of-bias appraisal reported in the abstract.
- Comparison axis: functional-monomer chemistry vs. silanization + tribochemical silica coating, on titanium and titanium-alloy substrates.

## Results

(abstract-only — no pooled bond-strength figures available)

1. Adhesive functional monomers are **effective for bonding** resin luting materials to titanium.
2. Functional-monomer performance is reported **equal to** silanization + tribochemical silica coating.
3. A primer/luting agent containing **MDP, MEPS, 6-MHPA, or 4-META** promotes titanium–resin bonding.
4. Clinical driver: cast **titanium restorations entered the Japanese NHI system**, motivating wider resin-bonded titanium prosthesis use.
