## One-line Summary

Narrative literature review of 11 in vitro studies (1995–2017) finding that adhesive functional monomers — especially MDP (phosphate), MEPS (thiophosphate), 6-MHPA (phosphonoacetate), and 4-META (carboxylate anhydride) — bond resin luting agents to titanium and titanium alloys as effectively as silanization plus tribochemical silica coating, with 10 wt% MDP-based primer and 4-META/MMA-TBB resin showing the best thermocycling durability.

## Summary

This 4-page narrative review (Journal of Oral Science 2022;64(3):181–184) surveys 11 in vitro studies on resin bonding to titanium and titanium alloys, specifically examining how the chemical identity of the adhesive (functional) monomer in primers and luting agents drives bonding durability. The clinical impetus is Japan-specific: rising palladium and gold prices led cast titanium restorations to be added to the Japanese national health insurance (NHI) system, creating demand for reliable resin-to-titanium cementation protocols.

The authors searched PubMed (August 2020 – March 2021; keywords: *titanium AND (adhesive OR bond) AND (primer OR monomer) AND dental*), screened for adhesive-monomer focus, and selected 11 studies. Outcome comparisons are based on shear or tensile bond strength after in vitro thermocycling aging (most commonly 10,000–20,000 cycles), summarized in four tables covering commercial primers, surface modification systems, commercial luting agents, and study-by-study data.

Key conclusion: adhesive functional monomers that contain **carboxyl or phosphate groups** — particularly MDP, MEPS, 6-MHPA, and 4-META — chemically interact with the TiO₂ passive oxide film on titanium's surface, providing bonding durability equal to the established tribochemical silica coating + silanization workflow (Rocatec system) but without requiring specialized equipment. This conclusion makes functional-monomer-based priming a practical chairside strategy for titanium prosthesis cementation.

The bonding mechanism is attributed to chemical interaction between the acidic functional monomer and the TiO₂ oxide film — likely via hydrogen bonding / ionic forces — analogous to the MDP–ZrO₂ bond established spectroscopically by Nagaoka et al. (2017) for zirconia (see [[resin-bonding/nagaoka-2017-mdp-zirconia-chemical-interaction-nmr]]). Molecular-level spectroscopic confirmation on titanium is noted as still lacking.

Clinically relevant negative finding: **sulfur-containing monomers** (VTD, MTU-6), which are effective for noble metal alloys, are **not effective** for titanium — substrate-specificity must be respected when selecting primer chemistry.

## Key Contributions

1. **Functional monomers = silanization + tribochemical silica coating** in bond durability to titanium — but require no dedicated equipment (Rocatec/Cojet not needed).
2. **MDP-dose response**: 10 wt% MDP-based primer > 1 wt% and 0.1 wt% > 4-META-based primers at comparable concentrations (Tsuchimoto et al. [31]).
3. **4-META/MMA-TBB (Super-Bond C&B, bulk-mix)** is the highest-performing luting agent class across thermocycling aging studies; outperforms composite-based luting agents without adhesive monomers and PMMA-based cements with MAC-10.
4. **VTD (sulfur monomer) is NOT effective for titanium** — a clinically important exception vs. noble metal bonding systems (Koizumi [37]).
5. **Phosphoric acid surface pre-treatment inhibits monomer adsorption** onto titanium; hydrochloric acid (1 N) is superior for surface decontamination (Tsuchimoto [32]).
6. Provides four structured market tables listing 22+ commercial primers and luting agents with their active monomer identities — a practical reference for chairside selection.
7. Contextualizes titanium bonding within the broader Japanese NHI policy shift prompted by precious-metal price increases.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Narrative literature review / scoping survey. Not a formal systematic review or meta-analysis.
- **Search**: PubMed, August 2020 – March 2021. Primary keywords + PubMed "similar articles" snowballing. Two-step screening: (1) exclude veneering / denture-repair material papers; (2) retain adhesive-monomer–focused articles → **11 studies selected** (spanning 1995–2017).
- **Outcome**: Bond strength (shear or tensile) after in vitro thermocycling aging (up to 20,000 cycles). Studies sorted in Table 4 by duration of longest thermocycler test.
- **Substrates**: CP titanium (Grades II/III), Ti-6Al-7Nb alloy, Ti-Ni superelastic alloy, high-purity titanium.
- **Surface treatments evaluated**: Alumina airborne-particle abrasion (50–70 µm), heat oxidation, HCl/H₃PO₄ acid etching, Rocatec tribochemical silica coating, no surface treatment.
- **Primer and luting systems**: >22 commercial primers (Table 1) and 10 commercial luting agents (Table 3); study-level comparison in Table 4 marks "*" for significantly superior performers.

## Results

### Primer ranking (thermocycling durability)

| Rank | Monomer class | Representative commercial products | Key finding |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1st | MDP (phosphate) | Alloy Primer, Cesead II Opaque Primer, Estenia Opaque Primer, Targis Link | Best across CP Ti and Ti-6Al-7Nb; 10 wt% > 1 wt% > 0.1 wt% |
| 2nd | 4-META (carboxylate) | Super-Bond Liquid, Meta Base M | Effective; MMA-TBB resin pairing critical |
| 3rd | MEPS (thiophosphate) | Metal Primer, Metal Primer II (GC) | Effective with MMA-TBB resin |
| 3rd | 6-MHPA (phosphonoacetate) | M.L. Primer (Shofu), AZ Primer | Effective, similar tier to MEPS/4-META |
| Inferior | MAC-10, 4-AET, BPDM | MR. Bond, Acryl Bond, All Bond II Primer B | Lower bond promotion for Ti |
| Not effective | VTD, MTU-6 (sulfur monomers) | V-Primer, Metaltite | Noble-metal-specific; ineffective for TiO₂ |

### Luting agent ranking

| Rank | Monomer | Product | Key finding |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1st | 4-META/MMA-TBB | Super-Bond C&B (bulk-mix) | Highest durability; brush technique inferior to bulk-mix |
| 2nd | MDP | Panavia F 2.0, Panavia Fluoro Cement | Significantly better than non-adhesive composites when paired with MDP primer |
| 3rd | MDP | RelyX Unicem (self-adhesive) | Effective without separate primer in Di Francescantonio [42] |
| Low | None (composite base) | RelyX ARC, LinkMax (no adhesive monomer) | Poor durability without primer or adhesive monomer in cement |

### Surface treatment findings

- **Alumina blasting (50–70 µm)**: recommended for all protocols — removes contamination, increases surface roughness for mechanical interlocking.
- **Hydrochloric acid (1 N)**: preferred over H₃PO₄ for surface decontamination; phosphoric acid inhibits functional-monomer adsorption onto TiO₂.
- **Rocatec tribochemical silica coating + silane**: effective, comparable to MDP-primer protocols — but requires dedicated silica-blasting unit.
- **Heat oxidation**: thick oxide layer is detrimental to bonding; moderate oxidation acceptable; alumina blasting restores bonding by mechanically disrupting thick oxide.

### Bonding mechanism

- Titanium's passive TiO₂ surface film is the bonding target.
- Carboxyl- and phosphate-group monomers interact chemically (likely hydrogen bonding / ionic) with TiO₂.
- Sulfur monomers (VTD) work on noble metal surfaces via S–Au coordination but cannot interact with TiO₂ — substrate specificity.
- Degree of polymerization and mechanical properties of the resin system (initiator: MMA-TBB vs. light/dual cure) also influence final bond strength alongside monomer chemistry.
