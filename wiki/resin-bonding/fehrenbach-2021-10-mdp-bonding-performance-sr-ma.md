## One-line Summary

In vitro SR+MA (206 studies, 64 SE adhesive systems): 10-MDP-based self-etch adhesives gave significantly higher bond strength than most other acidic-monomer adhesives at both dentin (effect 6.98; 95% CI 5.61–8.36) and enamel (2.79; 95% CI 1.62–3.96), p<0.00001 — but the benefit was conditional on test method, substrate, comonomer, and application category.

## 한줄요약

In vitro 체계적 문헌고찰+메타분석 (논문 206편, 자가산부식 접착제 64종): 10-MDP 함유 접착제가 대부분의 다른 산성 단량체 접착제보다 상아질(효과크기 6.98; 95% CI 5.61–8.36)·법랑질(2.79; 95% CI 1.62–3.96) 모두에서 유의하게 높은 결합강도를 보였으나(p<0.00001), 그 이점은 시험법·기질·공단량체·적용 방식에 따라 달라졌다.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1016/j.dental.2021.08.014](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.dental.2021.08.014)), this systematic review + meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD42020175715; abstract-only — full text not retrieved) asked whether the mere presence of the functional monomer 10-MDP raises self-etch (SE) adhesive bonding performance versus adhesives built on other acidic monomers. From 740 full-text-screened studies, 210 entered the SR and 206 the MA, covering 64 adhesive systems; microtensile testing dominated (73.4%) and most studies carried medium risk of bias (56.7%). The 10-MDP group was favored at both dentin (overall effect 6.98; 95% CI 5.61–8.36; p<0.00001) and enamel (2.79; 95% CI 1.62–3.96; p<0.00001). The advantage was not absolute: 10-MDP beat PENTA, 6-MHP, 4-META, 4-MET, pyrophosphate esters, mixed compositions, and sulfonic-acid monomers (p≤0.01), but tied PEM-F, acrylamide phosphates, 4-AET, MAC-10, and polyacrylic/phosphonic-acid monomers (p≥0.05), and was itself surpassed only by GPDM (p=0.03).

Clinically: when choosing a self-etch (or universal-in-SE-mode) adhesive, a 10-MDP-containing formulation is a sound default for higher bond strength to dentin and enamel — but the gain depends on the comonomer it is compared against (no advantage over GPDM, PEM-F, MAC-10, 4-AET, etc.) and on application category, with **2-step** SE applied to dentin giving the best dentin bonds. Note this is an in vitro bond-strength synthesis, not a clinical longevity outcome.

## Key Contributions

- Largest synthesis isolating the **functional-monomer variable** (10-MDP vs other acidic monomers) on SE adhesive bond strength.
- Quantifies a significant **10-MDP advantage on both dentin and enamel**, dentin larger.
- Delivers a **monomer-by-monomer ranking** (which monomers 10-MDP beats, ties, and is beaten by).
- Adds **Bayesian network meta-analysis** across SE application categories (1-step / 2-step / universal).

## Methodology

- **Design**: SR + MA + Bayesian NMA, PRISMA, PROSPERO CRD42020175715.
- **Databases**: PubMed, Web of Science, SciELO, Scopus, LILACS, IBECS, BBO; through 30 June 2021.
- **Eligibility**: in vitro bond-strength studies (microtensile, microshear, tensile, shear) to sound dentin/enamel comparing ≥2 SE systems, ≥1 with 10-MDP and ≥1 with a distinct acidic composition.
- **Analysis**: RevMan 5.3.5, random-effects, p<0.05; Bayesian NMA via MetaInsight V3.
- **Yield**: 740 screened → 210 SR / 206 MA; 64 adhesive systems; microtensile 73.4%.
- **Risk of bias**: medium 56.7%, low 35.2%, high 8.1%.

## Results

- **Dentin** — 10-MDP favored: overall effect **6.98** (95% CI 5.61–8.36; p<0.00001).
- **Enamel** — 10-MDP favored: overall effect **2.79** (95% CI 1.62–3.96; p<0.00001).
- **10-MDP > these** (p≤0.01): PENTA, 6-MHP, 4-META, 4-MET, pyrophosphate esters, mixed composition, sulfonic-acid-derived monomers.
- **10-MDP ≈ these** (p≥0.05, no difference): PEM-F, acrylamide phosphates, 4-AET, MAC-10, polyacrylic-/phosphonic-acid-derived monomers.
- **GPDM > 10-MDP** (p=0.03) — the only monomer to exceed 10-MDP.
- **Application category**: dentin bonds favored **2-step 10-MDP-based** adhesives; enamel bonds favored **2-step versions regardless of 10-MDP**.
- **NMA indirect evidence**: 1-step 10-MDP-free worst in dentin; universal 10-MDP-free worst in enamel.
