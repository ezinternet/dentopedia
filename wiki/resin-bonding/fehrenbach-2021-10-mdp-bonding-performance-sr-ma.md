## One-line Summary

In vitro SR+MA (206 studies, 64 SE adhesive systems): 10-MDP-based self-etch adhesives gave significantly higher bond strength than most other acidic-monomer adhesives at both dentin (effect 6.98 MPa; 95% CI 5.61–8.36) and enamel (2.79 MPa; 95% CI 1.62–3.96), p<0.00001 — but the benefit was conditional on test method, substrate, comonomer, and application category.

## Summary

This PRISMA-registered systematic review + meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD42020175715; Dental Materials, October 2021) is the first to isolate the **functional-monomer variable** and directly compare bonding performance of self-etch (SE) adhesives containing 10-MDP against those formulated with other acidic monomers. From 7,355 titles, 210 in vitro bond-strength studies entered the SR and 206 the MA, covering 64 adhesive systems; microtensile testing dominated (73.4%) and most studies carried medium risk of bias (56.7%).

The 10-MDP group was significantly favored at both dentin (overall effect **6.98 MPa**; 95% CI 5.61–8.36; p<0.00001) and enamel (**2.79 MPa**; 95% CI 1.62–3.96; p<0.00001). The advantage was not absolute: 10-MDP outperformed PENTA, 6-MHP, 4-META, 4-MET, pyrophosphate esters, mixed compositions, and sulfonic-acid monomers (p≤0.01), but was equivalent to PEM-F, acrylamide phosphates, 4-AET, MAC-10, and polyacrylic/phosphonic-acid monomers (p≥0.05). The single exception was GPDM, the only monomer to outperform 10-MDP (p=0.03). For application category: 2-step 10-MDP-based adhesives gave the best dentin bonds; 2-step SE systems gave the best enamel bonds regardless of 10-MDP presence. Bayesian NMA indirect evidence showed 1-step 10-MDP-free adhesives as worst in dentin and universal 10-MDP-free adhesives as worst in enamel.

Clinically, a 10-MDP-containing SE adhesive is a sound default for higher bond strength to dentin and enamel, especially when applied as a 2-step system to dentin — but the advantage does not hold against GPDM, PEM-F, MAC-10, 4-AET, or acrylamide phosphate-based competitors. This is an in vitro bond-strength synthesis; clinical longevity data (restoration survival) is required for definitive clinical guidance.

## Key Contributions

- Largest synthesis isolating the **functional-monomer variable** on SE adhesive bond strength across 206 studies and 64 systems.
- First MA to deliver a **ranked monomer-by-monomer comparison**: beats, ties, or is beaten by each specific acidic comonomer.
- Quantifies the dentin vs enamel asymmetry: dentin effect (6.98) is 2.5× the enamel effect (2.79).
- Adds **Bayesian NMA** across SE application categories (1-step / 2-step / universal), enabling indirect comparison where direct head-to-heads are missing.
- Identifies GPDM as the only acidic monomer outperforming 10-MDP — a finding with implications for universal adhesive formulation.

## Methodology

- **Design**: SR + MA + Bayesian NMA; PRISMA; PROSPERO CRD42020175715.
- **Databases**: PubMed, Web of Science, SciELO, Scopus, LILACS, IBECS, BBO; through 30 June 2021.
- **Eligibility**: in vitro bond-strength studies (microtensile, microshear, tensile, shear) to sound dentin/enamel comparing ≥2 SE systems, ≥1 with 10-MDP and ≥1 with a distinct acidic composition.
- **Statistical analysis**: RevMan 5.3.5, random-effects models, p<0.05; Bayesian NMA via MetaInsight V3.
- **Yield**: 7,355 titles → 770 full-text screened → 210 SR / 206 MA; 64 adhesive systems; most-studied: Clearfil SE Bond (Kuraray).
- **Test method distribution**: microtensile 73.4%, microshear, tensile, shear (minority).
- **Risk of bias**: medium 56.7%, low 35.2%, high 8.1%.

## Results

| Substrate | Overall effect (MPa) | 95% CI | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dentin | 6.98 | 5.61–8.36 | <0.00001 |
| Enamel | 2.79 | 1.62–3.96 | <0.00001 |

**10-MDP outperformed** (p≤0.01): PENTA, 6-MHP, 4-META, 4-MET, pyrophosphate esters, mixed composition, sulfonic-acid-derived monomers.

**10-MDP equivalent** (p≥0.05): PEM-F, acrylamide phosphates, 4-AET, MAC-10, polyacrylic-/phosphonic-acid-derived monomers.

**GPDM > 10-MDP** (p=0.03) — sole exception.

**Application category effects**:
- Dentin: 2-step 10-MDP-based adhesives best.
- Enamel: 2-step SE adhesives best, regardless of 10-MDP.

**NMA indirect evidence**:
- 1-step 10-MDP-free: worst dentin performance.
- Universal 10-MDP-free: worst enamel performance.

**Heterogeneity**: moderate-to-high across studies; effect magnitude depends on test type, substrate, comonomer composition, and SE application category.
