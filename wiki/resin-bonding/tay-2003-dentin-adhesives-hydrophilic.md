## Summary
Foundational narrative review by Tay & Pashley establishing the "too hydrophilic" concept for simplified dentin adhesives. As manufacturers simplified from 3-step TE → 2-step single bottle → 1-step SE, each simplification increased hydrophilicity and water permeability, leading to bond degradation and dual-cure incompatibility.

## Hydrophilicity Spectrum

| System | Steps | Hydrophilicity | Durability |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3-step TE (e.g. OptiBond FL) | Etch + Primer + Adhesive | Lowest | Best |
| 2-step TE (1-bottle) | Etch + Primer/Adhesive | ↑ | ↓ |
| 2-step SE (e.g. Clearfil SE Bond) | SE Primer + Adhesive | ↑↑ | ↓↓ |
| 1-step SE | All-in-one | Highest | Worst |

## Core Problem: Water Blister Formation
- Dentin continuously weeps fluid (hydraulic conductance)
- Hydrophilic adhesive → water permeates through polymerized film
- Water blisters trapped at interface → pumping effect → postoperative sensitivity
- Blisters accelerate hydrolytic degradation of hybrid layer

## Dual-Cure Incompatibility
- Simplified acidic adhesives inhibit tertiary amine activators in dual-cure composites
- Result: inadequate polymerization at interface when dual-cure or chemical-cure composite is used over 1-step SE adhesive
- Solution: interpose a separate hydrophobic adhesive layer before dual-cure composite
