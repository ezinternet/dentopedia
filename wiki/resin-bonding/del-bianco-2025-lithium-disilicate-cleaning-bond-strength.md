## One-line Summary

In vitro (n=70, 7 groups): after saliva + try-in silicone contamination of IPS e.max CAD, self-etching ceramic primer (Monobond Etch&Prime) equaled re-etching with 5% HF in bond strength, while water, phosphoric acid, ethanol, and cleaning paste (Ivoclean) were all insufficient.

## Summary

This Bologna University in vitro study (Breschi/Mazzoni group) investigated the most effective method for decontaminating lithium disilicate (IPS e.max CAD) after try-in contamination with fresh human saliva and silicone try-in paste. Seven cleaning approaches were compared after standardized contamination, with shear bond strength to resin cement (Variolink DC) as the outcome. The self-etching ceramic primer Monobond Etch&Prime (MEP) restored bond strength to levels matching 5% HF re-etching regardless of whether initial HF pre-etching had been performed. None of the cleaning methods completely eliminated contaminants by SEM analysis, yet MEP's combined etching and priming action was sufficient to achieve clinical-grade bond strength. Commercial cleaning paste (Ivoclean) was particularly ineffective despite marketing claims.

## Key Contributions

- Monobond Etch&Prime (self-etching primer) = clinical alternative to HF re-etching after try-in contamination
- Self-etching primer effective with or without prior HF etching (SEP ≈ HFSEP ≈ HF re-etch)
- Water rinse, phosphoric acid, 70% ethanol, and Ivoclean cleaning paste all insufficient after combined saliva + try-in paste contamination
- SEM confirms no method achieves complete contaminant removal — yet MEP achieves acceptable bond through aggressive surface chemistry
- Ivoclean (cleaning paste) showed residual contaminants under SEM — poorest outcome among active cleaning methods

## Methodology

- Material: IPS e.max CAD CAD/CAM LiSi blocks (14×7×3 mm, n=10); sintered in Programat P500/G2
- Pre-etching: 50 specimens with 5% HF (IPS ceramic gel) 20 s; 20 specimens without
- Contamination: Saliva (cotton pellet, 60 s) + try-in silicone paste (Fit Checker, GC; finger-pressed 2 min)
- Cleaning groups: C (water), PA (37% H₃PO₄), E (70% ethanol), CP (Ivoclean paste), HFSEP (MEP with prior HF), HF (re-etch 5% HF, no prior HF), SEP (MEP only, no prior HF)
- Post-cleaning: Monobond Plus silane (groups C/PA/E/CP/HF) or not (HFSEP/SEP — MEP contains silane), Adhese Universal adhesive, Variolink DC cement
- Storage: 24h at 37°C; SBS test; SEM fractography

## Results

| Cleaning Method | Prior HF | Outcome |
|----------------|---------|---------|
| Water rinse (C) | Yes | Lowest SBS |
| H₃PO₄ (PA) | Yes | Low SBS |
| 70% Ethanol (E) | Yes | Low SBS |
| Ivoclean paste (CP) | Yes | Low SBS + residual contaminants by SEM |
| Monobond Etch&Prime (HFSEP) | Yes | **High SBS** |
| 5% HF re-etch (HF) | No | **High SBS** |
| Monobond Etch&Prime (SEP) | No | **High SBS** |

HFSEP ≈ SEP ≈ HF (no significant difference, p>0.05). All inferior groups significantly lower (p<0.001).

**Clinical implication**: At try-in, if re-etching with HF is inconvenient or risky, Monobond Etch&Prime (20 s rubbing, rinse, dry) is a clinically viable and equally effective decontamination protocol.
