## One-line Summary

In vitro (n=360 total, 12 groups): preheated composite resin (68°C) achieved the highest microshear bond strength to both feldspathic ceramic and PICN at baseline and after thermocycling, outperforming conventional resin cement and flowable composite; all groups declined significantly after 5,000 thermal cycles.

## Summary

This Brazilian in vitro study from UNESP São José dos Campos compared three luting strategies (resin cement, flowable composite, preheated regular composite at 68°C) for bonding to two silica-based ceramic materials: Vita Mark II feldspathic ceramic and Vita Enamic PICN. Using a standard HF etching + Monobond N silane protocol, microshear bond strength was measured at baseline (24h) and after simulated aging (5,000 thermal cycles). Preheated composite consistently produced the highest bond values across materials and time points, with reduced viscosity at 68°C enabling better flow into HF-etched surface microporosity. Flowable composite consistently performed worst, raising concerns about its use as a luting agent. These findings support preheated composite as an effective alternative to conventional resin cement for silica-based ceramic adhesive cementation.

## Key Contributions

- Preheated composite (68°C) superior to both conventional resin cement and flowable composite for µSBS to feldspathic ceramic and PICN
- Flowable composite consistently inferior — concerns for ceramic fracture risk with low-modulus luting agents confirmed
- Thermocycling significantly reduces bond values across all groups; long-term durability is a universal challenge
- PICN slightly higher bond values than feldspathic ceramic across luting agents — polymer network of Vita Enamic may enhance bonding substrate

## Methodology

- Materials: Vita Enamic PICN (86% ceramic + 14% polymer) and Vita Mark II feldspathic ceramic
- Surface treatment: 5% HF 60 s + ultrasonic 5 min + Monobond N silane 10 s active + 60 s
- Luting agents: Multilink N (RC), Tetric N Ceram flowable (FC), Filtek P60 preheated 68°C/10 min (PH)
- Specimen geometry: 10×8×2 mm plates; Tygon tubes Ø 1.6 mm × 2 mm
- Groups: 12 (2 materials × 3 luting agents × 2 time points), n=15 per group
- Aging: 5,000 thermal cycles (5°C–55°C, 30 s dwell)
- Test: µSBS; failure analysis by stereomicroscope + SEM
- Statistics: Two-way ANOVA

## Results

**Feldspathic (FEL)**:
- Baseline: PH > RC ≈ FC (significantly different)
- After aging: PH > RC ≈ FC (rank maintained, all reduced)
- Most common failure: adhesive and cohesive in ceramic

**PICN**:
- Baseline: PH > RC > FC (all significantly different)
- After aging: PH > RC > FC (rank maintained, all reduced)
- Thermocycling reduced all groups significantly

**Key mechanism**: Preheating to 68°C reduces viscosity, allowing composite to flow into HF-created microporosity before cure, mimicking the advantage of low-viscosity resin cement without sacrificing filler content.
