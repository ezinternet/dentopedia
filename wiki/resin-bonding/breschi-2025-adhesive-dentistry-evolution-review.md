## Summary
Authoritative narrative review (Dental Materials 2025) from Breschi's University of Bologna group synthesizing 70 years of adhesive dentistry: classification (EAR vs SE per smear-layer interaction — rejecting "generation" terminology), evolution to universal adhesives, and detailed treatment of hybrid layer (HL) degradation mechanisms (hydrolysis, endogenous MMP/cysteine cathepsins). Reviews 10+ strategies to stabilize HL: MMP inhibitors (chlorhexidine, EDC), ethanol-wet bonding, biomodification with proanthocyanidin/riboflavin-UV, hydrophobic overlay, DMSO, bioactive monomers. Concludes universal adhesives are not truly universal; operator knowledge of protocol remains critical.

## Key Contributions
- Definitive contemporary classification: EAR (3-step, 2-step) vs SE (2-step, 1-step), with universal as multi-mode
- Detailed mechanistic synthesis of HL degradation: hydrolytic + enzymatic (MMP-2, MMP-9, cysteine cathepsins)
- Comprehensive review of biomodification strategies (cross-linkers, MMP inhibitors, hydrophobic overlay)
- Rejects "universal" terminology as speculative — emphasizes operator dependency
- Notes ongoing Bis-GMA replacement with bio-safer monomers

## Methodology
- **Design**: Narrative review (no PRISMA, no meta-analysis)
- **Databases**: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science
- **Scope**: Dental substrate structure, HL degradation, contrast strategies
- **Authors**: Breschi, Maravic, Mazzitelli, Mazzoni — among the most-cited adhesive dentistry researchers globally

## Key Points
- **EAR**: 32–37% phosphoric acid → removes smear layer, demineralizes superficial dentin/enamel; 3-step has primer/adhesive separate (gold standard for long-term performance)
- **SE**: Acidic primer simultaneously demineralizes + infiltrates; no rinse; less technique-sensitive but generally lower enamel bond
- **Universal**: Multi-mode (EAR / selective-etch / SE); contains 10-MDP; chemical bond to hydroxyapatite, zirconia, metal oxides
- **HL degradation drivers**:
  - Water sorption + hydrolysis of resin-collagen interface
  - Endogenous MMPs/cysteine cathepsins (activated during acid etch)
  - Incomplete monomer infiltration → exposed naked collagen
- **HL stabilization strategies**:
  - MMP inhibitors: chlorhexidine 2% rinse, EDC primer, benzalkonium chloride
  - Ethanol-wet bonding (improves resin infiltration)
  - Cross-linkers: proanthocyanidin, riboflavin/UV, glutaraldehyde
  - Hydrophobic overlay
  - DMSO pre-treatment
  - Bioactive monomers (10-MDP, MDPB)

## Clinical Takeaways
- Selective enamel etch + SE on dentin is widely supported
- 3-step EAR remains gold standard for long-term performance
- Chlorhexidine 2% rinse before bonding extends HL stability (clinically validated)
- "Universal" does not mean foolproof — read manufacturer instructions
