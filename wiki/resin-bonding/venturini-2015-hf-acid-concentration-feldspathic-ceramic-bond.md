## Summary

Venturini et al. (2015) evaluated the effect of four hydrofluoric (HF) acid concentrations (1%, 3%, 5%, 10%) on contact angle and resin bond strength durability to Vita Mark II feldspathic ceramic. All etched surfaces were silanized and bonded with resin cement. Key finding: in the immediate (non-aged) condition, all HF concentrations produced statistically similar microtensile bond strengths (14.2–15.7 MPa). However, after aggressive aging (230 days + 12,000 thermocycles), only the 1% HF group showed a significant bond strength decrease (14.5 → 10.2 MPa). Groups etched with 3%, 5%, or 10% HF maintained stable bond strength. Contact angle analysis confirmed better wettability with higher HF concentrations (UC: 61.4°; HF10: 17.5°).

**Clinical takeaway**: A minimum of 3% HF is required for durable resin bonding to feldspathic ceramic. Lower concentrations (1%) provide acceptable immediate strength but fail to maintain long-term bond integrity.

## Key Contributions

- Established minimum effective HF concentration (≥3%) for durable feldspathic ceramic bonding
- Demonstrated immediate bond strength equivalence across all HF concentrations — aging is necessary to differentiate
- Quantified wettability improvement with increasing HF concentration
- Supported use of 5% HF (common clinical formulation) as reliable and equivalent to 10%
- AFM and SEM confirmed surface morphological changes correlating with bond performance

## Methodology

- **Material**: VitaBlocks Mark II (feldspathic CAD/CAM ceramic), 12×10 mm specimens
- **Groups**: Unconditioned control + HF1, HF3, HF5, HF10 (60 s etching each)
- **After etching**: Silanization → resin cement application
- **Aging subgroups**: Non-aged (immediate) vs. aged (230 days + 12,000 thermocycles at 5°C/55°C)
- **Test**: Microtensile bond strength (μTBS)
- **Surface analysis**: Atomic force microscopy (AFM) + scanning electron microscopy (SEM)
- **Statistics**: One-way ANOVA, Tukey's post-hoc

## Results

| Group | Contact Angle | Immediate μTBS (MPa) | Aged μTBS (MPa) | Bond Degradation |
|-------|-------------|----------------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| UC | 61.4° | — | — | — |
| HF1 | — | 14.5 | 10.2 | Significant ↓ |
| HF3 | — | 14.2 | ~14 | Stable |
| HF5 | — | 15.7 | ~15 | Stable |
| HF10 | 17.5° | ~15 | ~14 | Stable |

- Immediate: no significant difference between any HF group (p > 0.05)
- After aging: HF1 only group with significant bond loss (p < 0.05)
- Higher HF concentration → lower contact angle → improved ceramic wettability
