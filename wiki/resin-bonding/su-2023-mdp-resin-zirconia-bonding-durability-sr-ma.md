## One-line Summary

Systematic review + meta-analysis (72 studies SR / 68 in MA): 10-MDP-pretreated resin-to-zirconia bond strength falls significantly after aging (MD 5.58 MPa), is unaffected by the MDP application vehicle, and is significantly stronger when 10-MDP is combined with additional surface treatments (MD 10.17 MPa) than when used alone.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.3760/cma.j.cn112144-20230915-00158](https://doi.org/10.3760/cma.j.cn112144-20230915-00158)), this systematic review + meta-analysis assessed the durability of 10-methacryloyloxydecyl dihydrogen phosphate (10-MDP)-pretreated resin-to-zirconia bonding to guide clinical zirconia bonding strategies. A search across PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, CNKI and Wanfang yielded 72 studies for the systematic review, 68 of which were eligible for meta-analysis (RevMan 5.4). Studies were stratified by aging method (water storage, thermocycling, or both), 10-MDP application method (within primer, adhesive, resin cement, or combinations), and additional surface treatment (alumina sandblasting, tribochemical silica coating, acid/laser etching, plasma). This page is **abstract-only** (full text not retrieved) and the full article is **Chinese-language** — only the PubMed English abstract was available, so methods detail, heterogeneity, and risk-of-bias appraisal could not be extracted.

Clinically: 10-MDP alone (whatever the delivery vehicle) is not enough to maintain a durable resin-to-zirconia bond — bond strength drops significantly after aging. To preserve bond durability, pair 10-MDP with an additional surface treatment (sandblasting / tribochemical silica / etching / plasma); the additional treatment adds roughly twice the protective effect (MD 10.17 MPa) of the aging loss it counteracts (MD 5.58 MPa). Don't agonize over whether the 10-MDP comes in the primer, the adhesive, or the resin cement — that choice did not change post-aging strength.

## Key Contributions

- Largest 10-MDP resin-to-zirconia bonding-durability synthesis we hold: 72 SR / 68 MA studies.
- Quantifies significant aging-induced degradation of 10-MDP-pretreated zirconia bonds.
- Shows the 10-MDP application **vehicle** (primer vs adhesive vs resin cement) does not affect post-aging strength.
- Shows **combining** 10-MDP with additional surface treatments significantly improves post-aging strength over 10-MDP alone.

## Methodology

- **Design**: systematic review + meta-analysis (RevMan 5.4).
- **Databases**: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, CNKI, Wanfang.
- **Population**: resin-to-zirconia bonding after 10-MDP surface pretreatment.
- **Outcome**: zirconia bonding strength after pretreatment.
- **Subgroups**: aging method (water storage / thermocycling / both); 10-MDP application method (primer / adhesive / resin cement / combinations); additional surface treatment (alumina sandblasting, tribochemical silica coating, acid etching, laser etching, plasma).

## Results

- **Size**: 72 studies in systematic review; 68 eligible for meta-analysis.
- **Aging**: zirconia bond strength decreased significantly after aging — MD 5.58, 95% CI 5.11–6.05, p<0.001.
- **MDP application method**: no significant difference among primer / adhesive / resin cement after aging (all p>0.05).
- **MDP + additional surface treatment vs MDP alone**: significantly higher post-aging bond strength — MD 10.17, 95% CI 8.20–12.14, p<0.001.
