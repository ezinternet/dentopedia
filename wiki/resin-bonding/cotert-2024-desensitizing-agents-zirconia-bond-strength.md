## One-line Summary
In vitro shear-bond-strength study (80 molars, 8 groups n=10, 2500 thermocycles) finding Admira, Smart Protect, and Gluma increased zirconia-to-dentin SBS with a self-adhesive resin cement, while oxalate/fluoride agents performed worst.

## 한줄요약
In vitro SBS(80치·8군·열순환 2500): Admira·Smart Protect·Gluma가 self-adhesive cement에서 지르코니아-상아질 SBS를 증가시킴; 옥살산/불소계가 최저.

## Summary
This bench study asked whether applying a desensitizer to prepared dentin before luting a zirconia restoration with a self-adhesive resin cement (Rely-X U200) compromises or improves bond strength. Across eight groups, resin-based (Admira), glutaraldehyde-based (Smart Protect), and HEMA/glutaraldehyde (Gluma) agents increased shear bond strength (highest arms ≈10.7, 9.6, 8.9 MPa), whereas oxalate (BisBlock) and some fluoride agents gave the lowest values (down to ≈1.6 MPa). The authors recommend the HEMA/resin/glutaraldehyde-type agents for pre-cementation use to manage post-operative sensitivity without sacrificing bonding.

## Key Contributions
- Ranks 7 desensitizers by effect on zirconia-to-dentin SBS with a self-adhesive cement.
- Identifies which agents help (Admira, Smart Protect, Gluma) vs hurt (oxalate/fluoride) bonding.

## Methodology
80 molars, dentin exposed, 8 groups (n=10) incl. control; Rely-X U200 luting; 24 h water (37°C); 2500 thermocycles (5–55°C); SBS at 0.5 mm/min; ANOVA with post hoc; failure-mode analysis.

## Results
Highest SBS for Admira/Smart Protect/Gluma (~8.9–10.7 MPa); lowest for oxalate/fluoride agents (~1.6 MPa). Conclusion: resin-, glutaraldehyde-, and HEMA-based agents increased SBS and are recommended pre-cementation.
