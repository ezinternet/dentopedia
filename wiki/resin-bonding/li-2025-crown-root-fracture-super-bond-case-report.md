## One-line Summary

Case report (n=1, 55-month follow-up): a complicated oblique crown-root fracture of the maxillary left central incisor was treated by immediate fragment reattachment with the Super-Bond C&B (4-META/MMA-TBB) adhesive plus a #1.4 fiber post under a M-MIST labial flap, achieving stable restoration, probing depth <3 mm with negative BoP, no root/bone resorption, and complete periapical cyst healing at 55 months.

## Summary

A 54-year-old woman presented within 1 hour of an oblique crown-root fracture of the maxillary left central incisor (tooth 21), with the fragment fallen out and stored in water. The fracture ran palatal→labial, ~3 mm from the gingival margin, with the mesial end ~1 mm subgingival (below the alveolar crest). The tooth had prior root canal treatment and apical cyst removal (1 year earlier), no crown protection, and was discolored.

Implant, crown lengthening, and orthodontic extrusion were all judged unsuitable (insufficient apical bone after cyst resection → no implant primary stability; central-incisor gingival-margin discrepancy risk; extrusion too slow), and the patient strongly wished to preserve the natural tooth. The authors therefore performed **immediate fragment reattachment** using the **Super-Bond C&B (4-META/MMA-TBB)** self-curing adhesive, reinforced with a **#1.4 glass fiber post**, under a **M-MIST** small labial mucoperiosteal flap with strict moisture/contaminant control. A definitive all-ceramic crown was placed at 12 months once periodontal health was confirmed. At **55 months** the result remained stable: sealed crown margin, healthy periodontium (PD <3 mm, BoP negative), no root or alveolar bone resorption, and complete periapical cyst healing.

The mechanistic argument is that Super-Bond's tri-n-butylborane (TBB) initiator makes polymerization *enhanced* by moisture and air, so the bond resists the blood/saliva contamination inherent to a subgingival fracture line — where conventional/hydrophilic adhesives degrade. This positions Super-Bond as the moisture-tolerant adhesive of choice for subgingival crown-root fragment reattachment, the long-follow-up clinical anchor for this indication in the wiki.

## Key Contributions

- **55-month clinical anchor** for crown-root fragment reattachment — durability evidence beyond the usual short-term reports, supporting reattachment as a conservative alternative to crown lengthening / extrusion / extraction + implant.
- **Super-Bond (4-META/MMA-TBB) as moisture-tolerant adhesive** — TBB-initiated polymerization is enhanced by moisture/air, resisting the blood/saliva contamination of a subgingival fracture line.
- **M-MIST flap + fiber-post protocol** — small labial full-thickness flap (lingual papilla preserved) for direct visualization and dry field, with a #1.4 fiber post mechanically reinforcing the fragment-to-root bond.

## Methodology

- **Tooth/defect**: maxillary left central incisor, oblique crown-root fracture; mesial end ~1 mm subgingival. Endodontically treated, post–apical-cyst-surgery, discolored. Fragment intact, presented within 1 h.
- **Fragment prep**: 2% chlorhexidine disinfection → 0.9% saline storage.
- **Post space**: lingual access with diamond burs + Peeso reamers for passive #1.4 fiber post seating.
- **Flap**: M-MIST small full-thickness labial mucoperiosteal flap; lingual flap unreflected (papilla preserved).
- **Moisture control**: two palatal saliva ejectors + ferric-sulfate hemostatic gel (ViscoStat Clear).
- **Adhesion**: 37% phosphoric acid etch (Scotchbond Universal Etchant) on fragment; Super-Bond C&B dentin conditioner on root; rinse/dry.
- **Bonding**: fragment + #1.4 fiber post (Matchpost RTD) + root coated with Super-Bond C&B; fragment visually aligned, fiber post reinforcing the bond; excess cement removed; root planing; flap sutured (4-0 Dafilon).
- **Definitive restoration**: all-ceramic crown at 12 months (RelyX Ultimate resin cement).
- **Follow-up**: 1 wk, 1 mo, quarterly to 12 mo; 6-monthly clinical+radiographic to 55 mo; lifelong biannual protocol instituted.

## Results

- **12 months**: proper fragment adaptation, healthy periodontium, no root/bone resorption (gated the definitive crown).
- **55 months**: crown margin well-sealed; periodontal tissues healthy with satisfactory gingival color/texture; **PD <3 mm** labial and palatal; **BoP negative**; radiograph shows **no alveolar bone or root resorption** and **complete periapical cyst healing**. No microleakage, debonding, or periodontal complications. Patient satisfied with esthetics and function.
- **Conditions for success**: prompt, intact, well-fitting fragment; minimal (~1 mm) subgingival extension; adequate residual root; strict moisture/contaminant isolation; good plaque control allowing biological-width re-establishment.
- **Stated limit**: not suitable for teeth with severely damaged biological width; single case, lowest evidence tier.
