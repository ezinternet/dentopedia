## One-line Summary
In-vitro study (Front Bioeng Biotechnol 2025) comparing 6 dual-cure resin cements × light-curing vs self-curing × 3 time points (24h/3mo/6mo) for µTBS to dentin: curing mode was the dominant variable (F=1237.5), with light-curing significantly improving both µTBS and long-term durability; Nexus 3 (N3) performed best overall and all dual-cure cements should be light-activated.

## Summary
In vitro study comparing six dual-cure resin cements under two curing modes (light-curing vs self-curing alone) at three time points (24h, 3 months, 6 months) for µTBS to dentin. Key finding: curing mode is the most significant variable — all dual-cure cements should be light-activated for optimal bond strength and durability.

## Key Contributions
- Curing mode: dominant factor (explains most variance in µTBS, F=1237.5)
- Light-curing: significantly higher µTBS AND slower degradation at 6 months
- N3 (Nexus 3 Universal): best overall — highest µTBS light-cured and best durability
- TheraCem (calcium silicate): benefits from light-curing like other cements
- Clinical mandate: always light-cure dual-cure cements when access allows
