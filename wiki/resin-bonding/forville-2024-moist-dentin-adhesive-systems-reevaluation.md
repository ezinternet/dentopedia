## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1016/j.jdent.2024.105495](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jdent.2024.105495)), this systematic review and meta-analysis re-examined whether dentin moisture state changes the clinical performance of composite restorations bonded with the etch-and-rinse (ER) strategy. Five split-mouth randomized clinical trials from Brazil (2005–2023; 195 adult patients with non-carious cervical lesions, NCCLs; follow-up up to 5 years) were pooled. No significant difference between dry- and wet-bonding was found at any recall for retention or postoperative sensitivity, with moderate certainty of evidence by GRADE. (Abstract-only — full text not retrieved.)

Clinically: the long-taught requirement to keep ER-etched dentin visibly moist is not supported by pooled clinical retention data in NCCLs — dry vs moist bonding gave equivalent retention and postoperative sensitivity out to 5 years, suggesting ER bonding tolerates a wider moisture window than the classic wet-bonding dogma implies.

## Key Contributions

- Pools the **clinical** (not in-vitro) evidence on dentin moisture state for the ER strategy.
- Answers a focused question: do retention and secondary outcomes of NCCL composite restorations differ between dry- and wet-bonding?
- Reports a **null result** (no dry-vs-wet difference) at moderate GRADE certainty, challenging the textbook insistence on visibly moist ER dentin.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review + random-effects meta-analysis of randomized clinical trials.
- **Population/intervention**: NCCLs restored with ER or universal adhesives applied to dry vs moist dentin.
- **Databases**: MEDLINE/PubMed, Web of Science, Cochrane Library, Brazilian Library in Dentistry, LILACS, Scopus, Embase, grey literature; no language/date limits; updated May 2024.
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane RoB 2.0 (3 low, 1 some concerns, 1 high).
- **Certainty**: GRADE.
- **Included**: 5 split-mouth RCTs, all Brazil, 2005–2023; 195 patients; follow-up up to 5 years.
- **Registration**: PROSPERO 2023 CRD42023427861.

## Results

- Retention rate, risk ratio (95% CI) — no significant difference at any recall:
  - 18–24 months: **RR 0.91 (0.45–1.84)**
  - 36 months: **RR 0.82 (0.38–1.78)**
  - 60 months: **RR 1.00 (0.42–2.38)**
- Postoperative sensitivity, risk difference (95% CI): **0.00 (−0.03 to 0.03)** at all follow-ups.
- Certainty of evidence (GRADE): **moderate**.
- Conclusion: retention and secondary outcomes were **not affected by dentin moisture** under the ER strategy.
