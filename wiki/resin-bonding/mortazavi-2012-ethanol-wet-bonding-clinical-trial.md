## 한줄요약
RCT (NCCL 36개, 12개월): 에탄올 습윤접착법·OptiBond FL·Clearfil SE Bond 3군 모두 6개월 100% 유지율; 12개월 에탄올군 91.67%; 군간 유의차 없음(p=0.358)

## Summary
1-year RCT (36 NCCLs, 12 patients, Isfahan, Iran) comparing three adhesive strategies in cervical restorations: OptiBond FL (3-step E&R), Clearfil SE Bond (2-step SE + enamel etch), and ethanol wet bonding (EWB) with OptiBond FL adhesive component. No significant difference between groups at 12 months.

## Retention Results

| Group | 6-month | 9-month | 12-month |
|---|---|---|---|
| OptiBond FL (E&R) | 100% | 100% | **100%** |
| Clearfil SE Bond | 100% | 100% | **100%** |
| Ethanol wet bonding | 100% | 100% | **91.67%** |

- p=0.358 at 12 months — no significant difference
- No marginal defects, discoloration, or caries in any group
- All teeth remained vital

## Ethanol Wet Bonding Concept
- Dentin progressively dehydrated with ascending ethanol concentrations
- Collagen network maintained in expanded (non-collapsed) state without water
- Adhesive penetrates hydrophobic dentin → theoretically more durable hybrid layer
- Clinical evidence here: **numerically lower** retention at 12mo vs E&R/SE Bond (NS)

## Limitation Context
- 36 NCCLs is small; NS finding may be underpowered
- 12 months too short for EWB advantage to manifest (degradation is gradual)
- EWB is technique-sensitive; operator variability
