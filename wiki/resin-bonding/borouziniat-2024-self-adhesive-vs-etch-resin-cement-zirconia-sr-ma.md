## One-line Summary

Systematic review + meta-analysis (26 in-vitro studies, 4 comparison groups) finding no significant difference in bond strength of self-adhesive vs self-etch resin cement to zirconia (immediate p=0.055; delayed p=0.143), while total-etch resin cement bonds significantly LOWER than self-adhesive (p=0.000); both self-adhesive and self-etch are suitable for zirconia luting per manufacturer instructions.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.4103/JCDE.JCDE_225_23](https://doi.org/10.4103/JCDE.JCDE_225_23)), this PRISMA systematic review and meta-analysis pooled 26 in-vitro studies to compare the bond strength of three resin cement classes — self-adhesive, self-etch, and total-etch — to yttria-stabilized tetragonal zirconia polycrystal (Y-TZP). Studies were grouped into four comparisons (self-etch vs self-adhesive and total-etch vs self-adhesive, each at immediate and delayed/>48 h storage) and analyzed with random-effects models. Self-adhesive and self-etch cements showed no significant difference in bond strength to zirconia at either time point, whereas total-etch resin cement bonded significantly weaker than self-adhesive. The advantage of self-adhesive/self-etch cements is mechanistic: they carry 10-MDP phosphate functional monomer, which chemically bonds zirconia oxides and forms hydrolysis-resistant nanolayers, while total-etch cements lack this monomer. A descriptive analysis further confirmed self-adhesive cement outperforms RMGI, glass ionomer, and zinc phosphate (p<0.001).

Clinically: for luting zirconia restorations, use a 10-MDP-containing self-adhesive OR self-etch resin cement applied per manufacturer instructions (typically with airborne-particle/sandblast surface treatment) — the two classes are interchangeable on bond strength; avoid total-etch resin cement and conventional GI/RMGI/zinc phosphate as they bond significantly weaker to zirconia.

## Key Contributions

- First meta-analytic head-to-head ranking of self-adhesive vs self-etch vs total-etch resin cement classes specifically for zirconia bonding.
- Self-adhesive ≈ self-etch (no significant difference, immediate and delayed); total-etch significantly inferior at both time points.
- Attributes the class hierarchy to the presence/absence of 10-MDP phosphate functional monomer.
- Confirms self-adhesive resin cement is significantly superior to RMGI, GI, and zinc phosphate cements.

## Methodology

- **Design**: PRISMA systematic review + meta-analysis of in-vitro bond-strength studies; PICOS-structured search.
- **Search**: PubMed, Scopus, Cochrane, ISI/Web of Science (and allied indices) up to November 1, 2021; no language/time restriction.
- **Selection**: 858 records → 376 after de-duplication → 109 (title/abstract) → 43 full-text → **26 included** (17 excluded for non-adherence to manufacturer instructions or incomplete statistics); all English.
- **Inclusion criteria**: shear or tensile bond-strength test; reported sample size, mean, SD; cement applied per manufacturer instructions. No clinical studies qualified.
- **Risk of bias**: 6 criteria → of 33 articles assessed, 1 low / 16 medium / 16 high.
- **Analysis**: random-effects models (high heterogeneity, I² >90% in self-etch comparisons), Cochran Q, Comprehensive Meta-Analysis v2; conventional cements analyzed descriptively. Predominant self-adhesive cement studied: RelyX Unicem (10-MDP).

## Results

- Search: 858 records → **376 after de-duplication** → 109 → 43 full-text → **26 studies selected**.
- **Group 1 (immediate, self-etch vs self-adhesive)**: 15 articles / 32 categories; I²=93.50; **no significant difference, p=0.055**.
- **Group 2 (delayed, self-etch vs self-adhesive, >48 h)**: 14 articles / 29 categories; I²=92.68; **no significant difference, p=0.143**.
- **Group 3 (immediate, total-etch vs self-adhesive)**: 9 articles / 19 categories; total-etch **significantly LOWER, p=0.000**.
- **Group 4 (delayed, total-etch vs self-adhesive, >48 h)**: 9 articles / 16 categories; total-etch **significantly LOWER, p=0.000**.
- **Conventional cements (descriptive)**: self-adhesive significantly higher bond strength than RMGI, GI, and zinc phosphate (**p<0.001**).
- Conclusion: self-etch and self-adhesive achieve the highest immediate and delayed bond strength to zirconia; both are suitable when used per manufacturer instructions.
