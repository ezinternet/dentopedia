## One-line Summary

Bayesian network meta-analysis (77 in-vitro studies): for zirconia-resin bonding, SIE/hot-etching rank highest by SUCRA but air abrasion remains the practical standard; fine air-abrasion particles (25–53 µm) beat coarse (110–150 µm) immediately, Rocatec > CoJet, and 10-MDP-containing primers/cements are significantly superior to all other acidic monomers (MD 12.15 MPa), while silane/HEMA alone and gas plasma confer no benefit.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1016/j.jdsr.2024.05.004](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jdsr.2024.05.004)), Li et al. (2024) performed the first systematic review and Bayesian network meta-analysis (NMA) to simultaneously rank both mechanical surface treatments and chemical adhesive-monomer combinations for zirconia (Y-TZP)–resin bonding, pooling 77 in-vitro studies (2000–2023). Four Bayesian NMAs (immediate and aged, surface and monomer) plus standard pairwise meta-analyses (SPMA) for three clinically practical sub-questions converged on a clear hierarchy: selective infiltration etching (SIE) and hot etching achieve the highest SUCRA rankings, but only SIE was statistically superior to air abrasion, and both are technique-sensitive/hazardous, leaving air abrasion the practical standard. Chemically, 10-MDP is the dominant determinant — formulations without it are ineffective, and the four 10-MDP-containing formulations do not differ from one another.

Clinically: condition zirconia with **air abrasion using fine alumina (25–53 µm)** and cement/prime with a **10-MDP-containing** primer or resin cement; do not rely on silane or HEMA alone (they add no benefit and may even diminish the 10-MDP effect), and skip gas plasma (no benefit over no treatment). Tribochemical silica coating (Rocatec > CoJet) is a reasonable alternative — both air abrasion and silica coating meaningfully slow the aging-related bond decay that weakens 91.2% of bonds.

## Key Contributions

- First NMA to rank mechanical surface treatments AND adhesive-monomer combinations for zirconia bonding in one framework (prior MAs were descriptive/pairwise).
- Identifies 10-MDP as the single most important chemical factor and quantifies its advantage over other acidic monomers (MD 12.15 MPa).
- Provides clinically actionable SPMA answers on air-abrasion particle size, silica-coating system choice, and 10-MDP use.
- Demonstrates that air abrasion and silica coating protect against aging-related bond degradation.

## Methodology

- **Design**: PRISMA-NMA + Cochrane Handbook; question "which surface treatment and adhesive monomer are most advantageous for bonding to zirconia?"
- **Search**: PubMed, Embase, Web of Science, Scopus, Cochrane Library; Jan 2000 – May 30, 2023; two independent reviewers.
- **Selection**: 8878 records → 4518 screened → 181 full-text (+2 manual) → **77 in-vitro studies** included.
- **Bias**: modified CONSORT (15 items) — 94.8% medium risk, 5.2% high risk.
- **Analysis**: 4 Bayesian NMAs (gemtc 0.9–8, BUGSNET 1.0.3, MetaInsight V4.0.0; SUCRA ranking; MCMC 5000 burn-in + 20,000 iterations × 4 chains) split into surface vs monomer and immediate vs aged; SPMA in RevMan 5.4 (random effects) for particle size, silica system, and 10-MDP. Aging: ISO 10477–2020 basis, ≥5000 thermocycles.
- **Surface treatment arms** (n studies): air abrasion 54, silica coating 25, laser 16, SIE 8, gas plasma 5, hot etching 4. **Monomer NMA**: 27 studies, 12 component arms.

## Results

- **Surface SUCRA — immediate**: SIE 91.30% > hot etching 86.76% > silica coating 68.77% > air abrasion 49.68% > laser 33.90% > gas plasma 17.98%.
- **Surface SUCRA — aged**: SIE 90.49% > hot etching 85.16% > laser 56.93% > silica coating 53.65% > air abrasion 46.84% > gas plasma 9.44%.
- **vs air abrasion**: SIE significantly superior (immediate MD 5.73, 95% CI 0.93–10.53; aged MD 6.15, 95% CI 0.72–11.54); no other treatment differed significantly from air abrasion, except gas plasma significantly worse when aged (MD −9.78, 95% CI −16.74 to −2.76).
- **Gas plasma**: only treatment NOT better than control (immediate ES 2.82, 95% CI −1.47 to 7.16; aged ES 0.440, 95% CI −6.27 to 7.16) — ineffective.
- **Air-abrasion particle size**: fine 25–53 µm > coarse 110–150 µm for immediate bond strength (p<0.00001); no significant difference after aging (p=0.52).
- **Silica coating system**: Rocatec Soft and Rocatec Plus both > CoJet; Rocatec Soft vs Rocatec Plus not different (immediate p=0.64, aged p=0.14).
- **10-MDP vs other acidic monomers**: 10-MDP superior, MD 12.15 MPa (95% CI 8.91–15.39, p<0.00001); consistent across aging (p=0.80).
- **Monomer NMA**: only 10-MDP-containing formulations effective; silane+HEMA+10-MDP highest, HEMA-only lowest; the four 10-MDP formulations did not differ — adding silane or HEMA gave no benefit (HEMA/BPDM/silane may even impair MDP–Zr durability).
- **Durability**: 91.2% of bonds weakened after aging; air abrasion (slope 0.78±0.10) and silica coating (0.72±0.13) mitigate decline vs control (0.27±0.11).
