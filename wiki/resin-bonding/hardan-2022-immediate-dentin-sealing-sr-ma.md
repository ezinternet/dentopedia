## 한줄요약
IDS의 결합강도 효과를 정량화한 in vitro SR/MA (21편): IDS가 즉시·노화 모두에서 DDS 대비 결합강도 우세, 가장 큰 효과는 3-step EAR 또는 (접착제 + flowable resin) 조합에서.

## Summary
SR+MA of 21 in vitro studies (3,717 initial hits) on bond strength of IDS vs DDS for indirect restoration cementation. Overall, IDS improved bond strength for both immediate (p<0.001) and aged (p=0.001) testing. Subgroup analysis revealed advantage was driven mainly by 3-step etch-and-rinse adhesives and by adhesive + flowable resin combinations; 2-step EAR and 1-step SE subgroups showed no significant advantage. Authors recommend 3-step EAR + flowable resin overlay as the most reliable IDS protocol.

## Key Contributions
- Quantitative confirmation of IDS bond strength advantage over DDS (immediate and aged)
- Subgroup analysis identifies WHICH adhesives benefit most: 3-step EAR > 2-step SE / universal; 2-step EAR and 1-step SE: no benefit
- Endorses adding flowable resin overlay for aged bond strength
- Complements [[resin-bonding/samartzi-2021-immediate-dentin-sealing-review]] (narrative)

## Methodology
- **Design**: SR + MA (PRISMA)
- **PICOS**: P=indirect restorations; I=IDS; C=DDS; O=bond strength; S=in vitro
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane, ISI Web of Science, Scielo, Scopus, Embase (through Jan 2022)
- **Analysis**: RevMan v5.4.1; SMD with 95% CI
- **Subgroups**: 2-step EAR, 3-step EAR, 1-step SE, 2-step SE, universal adhesive
- **Aging**: thermocycling, water storage

## Results
**Overall**: IDS > DDS for both immediate (p<0.001) and aged (p=0.001) bond strength.

**By adhesive subgroup**:
| Adhesive | Immediate BS | Aged BS |
|---|---|---|
| 2-step EAR | NS (p=0.07) | NS |
| 3-step EAR | IDS superior | IDS superior (p<0.001) |
| 1-step SE | NS (p=0.15) | NS |
| 2-step SE | IDS superior | IDS superior |
| Universal | IDS superior | IDS superior |
| Adhesive + flowable resin layer | IDS superior | IDS superior (p=0.01) |

## Clinical Implications
- 3-step EAR is preferred adhesive for IDS
- Adding a thin flowable resin layer over the adhesive significantly enhances aged bond strength
- 2-step EAR and 1-step SE do not show clear IDS advantage — consider 3-step EAR if IDS is planned

## Counterevidence
[[overviews/reis-2024-biomimetic-restorative-myths-narrative]] argues clinical outcomes (post-op sensitivity, longevity) do not show IDS superiority. This MA is in vitro only — clinical RCT translation remains needed.
