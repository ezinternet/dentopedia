JERD 2005 single-author narrative review (Pascal Magne, 30+ articles): the foundational paper that named and codified the immediate dentin sealing (IDS) protocol — apply and light-cure the dentin bonding agent immediately after preparation (before impression), optionally with a filled adhesive resin liner — claiming improved bond strength, fewer gaps, less leakage, and reduced sensitivity; the primary citation for all subsequent IDS reviews.

## Summary

This is the paper that named and codified the "Immediate Dentin Sealing" protocol. Magne synthesizes 30+ articles plus his own biomimetic clinical practice (Magne & Belser 2002 textbook) to argue that the traditional delayed sealing (DDS at cementation) leaves dentin in suboptimal condition for bonding. The recommended IDS sequence: apply and light-cure DBA immediately after preparation, before impression; optionally overlay with a filled adhesive resin (low elastic modulus liner) for stress absorption and clinical handling.

Claimed benefits (each supported by prior in-vitro citations): improved bond strength, fewer gap formations, decreased bacterial leakage, reduced dentin sensitivity. The paper is the conceptual basis for all subsequent IDS literature, including Magne's own follow-up µTBS studies (2005, 2007) and later SR+MAs.

## Key Contributions

1. Named and defined the IDS protocol as a distinct procedure from DDS.
2. Operationalized the technique: DBA application + cure + filled resin liner, before impression.
3. Framed IDS within biomimetic dentistry (intact tooth restoration philosophy).
4. Listed four clinical benefits that became the IDS marketing template.

## Methodology

Narrative review of >30 articles on dentin bonding for indirect restorations; integration with Magne & Belser (2002) biomimetic textbook clinical experience. No quantitative synthesis.

## Results

- Conceptual: IDS = DBA applied to freshly cut dentin, before impression, then light-cured.
- Clinical recommendation: pair DBA with filled adhesive resin liner (low elastic modulus).
- Cited benefits: bond strength ↑, gap formation ↓, bacterial leakage ↓, dentin sensitivity ↓.
