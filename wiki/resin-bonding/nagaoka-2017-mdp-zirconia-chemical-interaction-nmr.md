## One-line Summary

In-vitro NMR mechanistic study (¹H/³¹P MAS NMR + 2D ¹H→³¹P HETCOR) showing 10-MDP bonds to zirconia via both ionic interaction (deprotonated P-O⁻ ↔ partial-positive Zr) and hydrogen bonding (P=O / non-deprotonated P-OH ↔ Zr-OH), with concentration-dependent shear bond strength; the prior "ionic-only" hypothesis was rejected.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1038/srep45563](https://doi.org/10.1038/srep45563)), Nagaoka and colleagues used solid-state ¹H and ³¹P magic-angle-spinning (MAS) NMR together with two-dimensional ¹H→³¹P heteronuclear correlation (HETCOR) NMR — the first time these methods were applied to the 10-MDP–zirconia interface — to resolve, at the atomic level, *how* the acidic functional monomer 10-MDP chemically interacts with yttria-stabilized zirconia. Earlier characterizations (TOF-SIMS, FTIR, XPS) could detect 10-MDP's phosphate functionality on zirconia but could not identify the *type* of bond. The NMR data show that 10-MDP is chemisorbed onto zirconia through two mechanisms simultaneously: ionic bonding between deprotonated phosphate (P-O⁻) and partially positive surface Zr, and hydrogen bonding involving the P=O oxo group and one non-deprotonated P-OH with surface Zr-OH (and with neighboring phosphate groups). Because 10-MDP has two phosphate pKa values (2.2 and 7.0) and primers are near-neutral overall, typically only one P-OH interacts with zirconia while the other can hydrogen-bond to an adjacent 10-MDP molecule. A parallel shear-bond-strength (SBS) experiment showed a clear concentration dependency, with no benefit at ≤1 ppb 10-MDP. The study's tested hypothesis — that bonding relies *solely* on ionic interaction — was rejected.

Clinically: this explains why 10-MDP-containing primers and self-adhesive/MDP cements achieve durable bonds to inert zirconia where micromechanics alone (e.g., air abrasion) fail after aging — the bond is a real chemical (ionic + hydrogen) interaction with the oxide surface, and it is concentration-dependent, supporting the use of adequately concentrated (commercial ≥1 wt%) 10-MDP primers/cements rather than dilute or 10-MDP-free formulations for zirconia.

## Key Contributions

- First atomic-level resolution of the 10-MDP–zirconia interaction using ¹H/³¹P MAS NMR + 2D ¹H→³¹P HETCOR — direct evidence of bond *type*, unlike TOF-SIMS/FTIR/XPS.
- Rejected the "ionic-only" hypothesis: bonding is both ionic (P-O⁻ ↔ partial-positive Zr) AND hydrogen bonding (P=O / non-deprotonated P-OH ↔ Zr-OH).
- Quantified adsorbed-species fractions by ³¹P deconvolution: ~68.6% chemisorbed monomer vs physisorbed monomer/dimer.
- Established a concentration-dependent SBS effect with a practical lower threshold (no benefit at ≤1 ppb 10-MDP).
- First report of inter-monomer hydrogen bonding between adsorbed 10-MDP phosphate groups, and the finding that only one P-OH of each 10-MDP typically engages zirconia (consistent with pKa 2.2 / 7.0).

## Methodology

- **Study type:** In-vitro laboratory study — solid-state NMR spectroscopy + shear bond strength testing on 3 mol% yttria-stabilized tetragonal zirconia (Y-TZP).
- **SBS arm:** Polished (15-μm diamond, non-sandblasted) Y-TZP plates, washed and UV-cleaned (Xe₂ excimer, 172 nm), dipped 1 h in 10-MDP/acetone from 0.1 ppb to 1 wt% (n=10/group) plus untreated controls; excess monomer washed off. Sandblasted zirconia bars luted with Clearfil Esthetic Cement (~25-μm film), stored 24 h/37 °C, tested on Instron at 0.5 mm/min; failure mode classified under 40× microscope. ANOVA + Tukey (α=0.05).
- **NMR arm:** Zirconia nanopowder (3 mol% yttria, grain ≤100 nm, 10–25 m²/g) coated with 25 wt% 10-MDP/acetone, centrifuged, thoroughly acetone-washed to remove unbound monomer; untreated powder as comparison.
- **NMR setup:** Agilent DD2 500 MHz, 3.2-mm rotor, 15-kHz MAS. ³¹P direct-polarization MAS at 202.3 MHz (recycle 90 s, ~790 scans); ¹H MAS at 499.8 MHz (recycle 120 s); 2D ¹H→³¹P HETCOR by cross-polarization (3-ms contact, 64 t₁ slices). Each analysis run twice.

## Results

- **SBS — concentration dependency:** No significant difference between control, 0.1 ppb, and 1 ppb 10-MDP; significantly higher SBS at higher concentrations with clear concentration dependency (practical lower threshold above ~1 ppb).
- **Failure mode shift:** 100% adhesive failure at 10-MDP ≤1 ppm; at ≥10 ppm, 50% adhesive / 50% mixed (cohesive+adhesive) — interfacial bond stronger at higher 10-MDP.
- **¹H MAS NMR:** Zr-OH + adsorbed water at 5.0 ppm (broad); 10-MDP methacryloyloxy sharp peaks at 1.4/1.6/1.9/4.1/5.5/6.0 ppm; broad ~6 ppm = mixed surface + P-OH hydroxyls.
- **2D ¹H→³¹P HETCOR:** Slices at −2.8 and −7.1 ppm (³¹P) correlate to ¹H of P-OH (8–11 ppm), Zr-OH (6.8 ppm), and CH₂-O-P (4.0 ppm) → P-OH sits close to surface Zr-OH.
- **³¹P deconvolution of adsorbed 10-MDP (4 peaks):** 0.3 ppm = physisorbed/H-bonded monomer, no deprotonation (10.0%); −13.7 ppm = physisorbed dimer, no deprotonation (21.4%); −7.7 ppm = chemisorbed monomer (48.4%); −2.6 ppm = chemisorbed monomer (20.2%) → ~68.6% chemisorbed monomer total.
- **Free-monomer reference (³¹P, acetone):** monomer 0.93 ppm, dimer −13.7 ppm.
- **Mechanism:** P=O oxo group and one non-deprotonated P-OH hydrogen-bond to zirconia or neighboring phosphates; the other P-OH can deprotonate (P-O⁻) and ionically bond to partial-positive Zr; two distinct ³¹P shifts (−7.7, −2.6 ppm) = two P-OH chemical states. → bonding is ionic AND hydrogen bonding (ionic-only hypothesis rejected).
- **pKa logic:** 10-MDP pKa₁ 2.2 / pKa₂ 7.0; near-neutral primer pH → typically one P-OH binds zirconia, the other H-bonds a neighboring 10-MDP (inter-monomer interaction, reported here for the first time).
