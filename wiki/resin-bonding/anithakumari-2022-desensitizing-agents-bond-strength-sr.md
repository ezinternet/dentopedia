## One-line Summary
Systematic review (23 in vitro human-tooth studies, 2010–2020) of how desensitizing agents affect dentin-bonding-agent bond strength — effect is controversial and depends on the specific agent and application protocol.

## 한줄요약
SR(in vitro 23편): 탈감작제가 dentin bonding agent 결합강도에 미치는 영향은 제제·프로토콜 의존적이며 결과 상충.

## Summary
This PICO-structured systematic review addressed a long-standing controversy: does applying a desensitizing agent before a bonded restoration weaken the dentin adhesive bond? Screening 146 records to 23 included in vitro studies on human teeth (2010–2020) with a modified Cochrane risk-of-bias tool, the review found no uniform effect — some desensitizers reduced bond strength while others preserved or increased it, depending on the agent chemistry and application timing. The net clinical message is that desensitizer-adhesive compatibility must be considered case by case rather than assumed.

## Key Contributions
- Synthesizes the controversial desensitizer-on-bond-strength evidence.
- Agent- and protocol-dependent effect direction made explicit.

## Methodology
PubMed/Cochrane/Google Scholar (2010–2020); in vitro human-tooth bond-strength studies after desensitizer use; 146 → 23 included; modified Cochrane Collaboration RoB.

## Results
No uniform direction: effect on bond strength varies by desensitizer and protocol; some reduce, some preserve/increase.
