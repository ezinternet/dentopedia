## One-line Summary

In vitro (n=40, 4 groups): for intraoral feldspathic porcelain chipping repair, using a separate silane primer + adhesive (BISCO) dramatically outperformed a universal adhesive regardless of surface treatment; HF etching and diamond disc roughening produced equivalent bond strength within the same bonding method.

## Summary

This Norwegian in vitro study from NIOM and the University of Oslo investigated which combination of surface treatment and bonding method best supports intraoral repair of chipped feldspathic porcelain restorations. Testing four protocols (2 surface treatments × 2 bonding methods), it found that the choice of bonding method dominated outcomes: a separate silane primer (BISCO Intraoral Repair Kit) followed by a dedicated adhesive produced significantly higher shear bond strength than Scotchbond Universal adhesive, which caused 50–70% specimen debonding during thermal cycling. Critically, both roughening with a diamond disc and HF acid etching produced statistically equivalent bond strength when the same bonding agent was used — suggesting HF's toxicity risk may not justify its use for intraoral chipping repair.

## Key Contributions

- Bonding method is the dominant variable for porcelain repair bond strength, not surface treatment
- Separate silane primer (TMSPM in acetone) + adhesive significantly outperforms universal adhesive containing silane
- HF etching (9.5%, 90 s) ≈ diamond disc roughening (P220 grit) — HF provides no meaningful bond advantage for repair
- Universal adhesive failure rate: 7/10 (R-UA) and 5/10 (E-UA) debonded during 5,000 thermal cycles
- Inferiority of universal adhesive attributed to silane monomer hydrolysis and instability at acidic all-in-one pH

## Methodology

- Material: Initial Zr-FS feldspathic porcelain (GC Europe), 5×5×2 mm, n=10 per group
- Groups:
  - R-UA: Diamond disc roughening (P220) + Scotchbond Universal (3M)
  - R-SA: Diamond disc roughening + BISCO silane primer + adhesive
  - E-UA: 9.5% HF etching 90 s + Scotchbond Universal
  - E-SA: 9.5% HF etching 90 s + BISCO silane primer + adhesive
- Composite: Filtek Supreme XTE A3 (3M), Ø 2.38 mm cylinders per ISO 29022:2013
- Aging: 24h at 37°C + 5,000 thermal cycles (5–55°C)
- Shear bond strength (1 mm/min); fracture mode by light microscopy
- Statistics: Wilcoxon rank-sum test (non-normal distribution)

## Results

- R-UA / E-UA: 50–70% debonded during thermal cycling (SBS = 0 MPa); very low mean SBS
- R-SA / E-SA: High survival through thermal cycling; significantly higher SBS (p<0.05)
- R-SA ≈ E-SA (ns, p>0.05): Surface treatment type does not differentiate outcomes
- R-UA ≈ E-UA (ns, p>0.05): Same poor outcome regardless of surface treatment with universal adhesive
- Fracture mode: survivors showed cohesive/mixed fractures; failures = adhesive at composite-porcelain interface
