## Summary
Sodium hypochlorite, a standard root-canal irrigant, oxidizes dentin collagen and degrades the resin-dentin bond — a problem at the endodontic-restorative interface. This meta-analysis of 14 in vitro studies (most at low risk of bias) found that applying collagen crosslinkers (EDTA, phytic acid/IP6, proanthocyanidin, rosmarinic acid, sodium ascorbate) after NaOCl exposure generally restored bond strength. The benefit was not uniform: it depended on the NaOCl concentration and exposure time, so crosslinker reconditioning is most relevant after higher-concentration or longer irrigation.

## Key Contributions
- Quantifies crosslinker-mediated recovery of NaOCl-compromised resin-dentin bonds.
- Subgroup signals by crosslinker type and NaOCl concentration/duration.

## Methodology
Six databases; in vitro studies of crosslinker effect on NaOCl-treated dentin bond strength; 1,538 → 14 included; 11/14 low RoB; bond-strength meta-analysis with subgroup comparisons.

## Results
Overall advantage of crosslinkers for bond strength on NaOCl-treated dentin, influenced by NaOCl concentration and exposure duration.
