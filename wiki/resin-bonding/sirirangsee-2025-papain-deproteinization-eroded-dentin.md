## One-line Summary
In-vitro study (Sci Rep 2025) showing papain-enzyme deproteinization of eroded dentin improves and stabilizes the microtensile bond strength of self-etch adhesives, especially Clearfil Universal Bond Quick.

## 한줄요약
in-vitro — papain 효소 탈단백 전처리가 침식상아질에서 self-etch 접착제 µTBS를 향상·안정화(특히 UBQ).

## Summary
Artificially eroded dentin was pretreated with papain enzyme (vs untreated control) and bonded with Clearfil SE Bond 2 (SEB) and Clearfil Universal Bond Quick (UBQ). Microtensile bond strength (µTBS) was measured at 24 h and after 10,000 thermocycles; deproteinization was quantified by the Raman amide-to-phosphate ratio, with SEM of dentin and resin-dentin interfaces. Papain slightly increased initial µTBS of SEB and significantly increased that of UBQ (p<0.05), and stabilized µTBS of both after thermocycling (p>0.05). It significantly reduced the amide-to-phosphate ratio, dissolving surface organic components, improving resin infiltration and hybrid-layer thickness, and enhancing bond durability to eroded dentin.

## Key Contributions
- Demonstrates papain deproteinization as a pretreatment to overcome the collagen-rich barrier of eroded dentin.
- Links Raman-measured deproteinization to improved hybrid layer and durable µTBS.

## Methodology
In-vitro. Artificially eroded dentin ± papain pretreatment; bonded with SEB and UBQ; µTBS at 24 h and after 10,000 thermocycles; Raman amide/phosphate ratio; SEM. Three-way ANOVA + Tukey, t-tests (p<0.05).

## Results
Papain: ↑initial µTBS (UBQ significant; SEB slight), stabilized µTBS post-thermocycling for both (p>0.05), ↓amide-to-phosphate ratio (p<0.05), thicker hybrid layer / improved interface.
