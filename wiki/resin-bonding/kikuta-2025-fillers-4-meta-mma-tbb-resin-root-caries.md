## One-line Summary

In-vitro study (DSC curing time + ISO-4049 3-point flexural + shear bond to root dentin, n=3–15/group) of 3 wt% fillers in 4-META/MMA-TBB resin (Super-Bond chemistry): organic composite filler shortened curing time and raised post-thermocycling dentin bond strength while preserving flexural strength, whereas 45S5 bioactive glass and aluminosilicate glass prolonged curing and lowered flexural/bond strength — organic composite filler is the most suitable additive for root-caries restoration.

## 한줄요약

In-vitro 연구(DSC 경화시간 + ISO-4049 3점 굴곡강도 + 근면 상아질 전단접착, n=3~15/군): 4-META/MMA-TBB(슈퍼본드 화학) 수지에 3 wt% filler 첨가 시, 유기 복합 filler는 경화시간을 단축하고 열순환 후 상아질 접착강도를 높이며 굴곡강도를 유지한 반면, 45S5 생활성유리·알루미노실리케이트 유리는 경화를 지연시키고 굴곡·접착강도를 낮춰 — 노인 root caries 수복에는 유기 복합 filler가 가장 적합.

## Summary

4-META/MMA-TBB resin (the chemically-cured **Super-Bond** chemistry; 4-META = adhesive functional monomer, TBB = tri-n-butylborane initiator) is being repositioned from pure adhesive to a **restorative material for root caries in older adults**, where moisture control is hard (gingival recession, bleeding, cognitive impairment). This in-vitro study asks which reinforcing/bioactive filler best supports that role. Three filler classes were added at a fixed **3 wt%** to base 4-META/MMA-TBB resin and benchmarked against the commercial organic-filler product **Bondfill SB Plus (BSP, Sun Medical)**: organic composite filler (OC), 45S5 bioactive glass (BG), and aluminosilicate glass (AS). Outcomes: curing time, flexural strength, shear bond to root dentin (with thermocycling), and mineralization.

Bottom line: **organic composite filler wins** — it shortens curing (better wet-field handling), keeps flexural strength near BSP, and is the only group whose dentin bond strength *rose* after 10,000-cycle thermocycling. **Bioactive and aluminosilicate glass are detrimental** at this load (slower cure for BG, lower flexural strength, weaker post-thermocycling bond), and 3 wt% BG produced **no detectable hydroxyapatite** in SBF.

## Key Contributions

- Direct material characterization of **Super-Bond (4-META/MMA-TBB) chemistry as a root-caries restorative**, not merely as an adhesive — relevant to the geriatric / moisture-compromised indication.
- Head-to-head of three filler classes (organic composite vs bioactive 45S5 glass vs aluminosilicate glass) at matched 3 wt% against commercial Bondfill SB Plus.
- Establishes that **filler choice trades handling/bond against (failed) bioactivity** — and that low-load (3 wt%) bioactive glass does not deliver mineralization while it does cost cure time and bond strength.

## Methodology

- **Resin**: 4-META/MMA-TBB, chemically cured (not photocurable), set at 37 °C.
- **Fillers (3 wt%)**: OC (polymer-coated radiopaque, 15 µm, 7.0 g/cm³, 6.84 vol%); BG (45S5, 5 µm, 2.8 g/cm³, 1.61 vol%); AS (1 µm, 2.4 g/cm³, 1.87 vol%). Variants OC/BG_multi vs _opacious differ only in <1 wt% pigment. Control: **BSP (Bondfill SB Plus)**.
- **Curing time** — isothermal DSC at 37 °C, exothermic-peak time (n=3).
- **Flexural strength** — 2×2×25 mm rods, ISO 4049, 37 °C/100% RH ×1 wk, 3-point bend 20 mm span, 1 mm/min (n=15); Weibull MLE analysis.
- **Shear bond to root dentin** — extracted human teeth, sound root dentin, caries fully removed; Teeth Primer 20 s; cured 30 min RT; arms = **24 h water** vs **10,000-cycle thermocycling 5↔55 °C** (n=15); linear mixed-effects model.
- **Mineralization** — 10×2 mm disks in SBF (ISO 23317) 4/8 wk; SEM/EDS/GIXRD for hydroxyapatite (BSP + BG groups, n=10).

## Results

- **Curing time**: OC_multi, OC_opacious, AS **significantly shorter** than BSP/BG (P<0.001); BG ≈ BSP. BG_opacious longer than BG_multi (P=0.00808). 45S5 likely raises pH → suppresses acidic-monomer/TBB polymerization → slower cure.
- **Flexural strength**: OC_opacious, BG_multi, BG_opacious, AS **significantly lower than BSP**; **OC_multi lower but NOT significant** (preserves strength). 
- **Shear bond to root dentin**: 24 h — no group differences. After thermocycling — **OC_multi & OC_opacious significantly highest** (P<0.05). Intragroup 24 h→TC: BSP ↓ (P=0.0041), BG_multi ↓ (P=0.0050); BG_opacious & AS unchanged; **OC_multi ↑ (P=0.0376)**, **OC_opacious ↑ (P=0.0075)**. No cohesive failures; failure shifted adhesive→mixed after thermocycling. Organic filler's gain attributed to reduced polymerization-shrinkage stress and better interfacial stress distribution (cf. Saeki et al. on Bondfill SB).
- **Mineralization**: EDS Ca/P extremely weak (strong C, O, Yb); needle-like crystals on some BG specimens but **GIXRD found no hydroxyapatite** at 4/8 wk. 3 wt% BG too low (Kohda et al. needed 30–50 wt% for measurable enamel hardness effect).
- **Clinical relevance (authors)**: organic composite filler favorable for root-caries restoration in older adults under poor moisture control; bioactive/aluminosilicate glass should be used with caution.
