## One-line Summary

Systematic review + meta-analysis (740→43 studies; MA on 21 chlorhexidine articles) showing chlorhexidine (an MMP inhibitor) has no effect on immediate resin-dentin bond strength but significantly preserves aged bond strength at 6/12/24 months, with the advantage growing over time.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1155/2021/9949699](https://doi.org/10.1155/2021/9949699)), resin-dentin bond strength decreases over time because host-derived collagenolytic enzymes — matrix metalloproteinases (MMPs) and cysteine cathepsins — degrade the collagen matrix of the hybrid layer. This systematic review with meta-analysis screened 740 records down to 43 studies (240 experimental groups, 21 different enzyme inhibitors) and pooled the subset of 21 chlorhexidine (CHX 0.2–2%) articles by aging time and adhesive strategy. CHX showed no significant effect on immediate (baseline) bond strength but a significant protective effect at 6, 12, and 24 months, with the benefit increasing as aging lengthened.

Clinically: applying chlorhexidine as a dentin pretreatment (MMP inhibition) does not boost the initial resin-dentin bond, but it meaningfully slows hybrid-layer degradation and preserves bond strength over the long term — so CHX can be recommended as an adjunct to increase the longevity of bonded restorations, while recognizing the underlying evidence is largely medium/high risk of bias.

## Key Contributions

- First meta-analysis to quantitatively pool an MMP inhibitor's (CHX) effect on resin-dentin bond strength across aging time and adhesive strategy.
- Establishes the key clinical distinction: **immediate bond strength is unaffected; aged bond strength is preserved**, and the effect grows with longer aging.
- Catalogues the MMP-inhibitor landscape (21 inhibitors across 240 groups) and identifies CHX as dominant (32/43 studies), BAC second (7).
- Exposes systematic methodological weakness in the field (no sample-size calculations; near-universal lack of blinding; zero low-risk-of-bias studies).

## Methodology

- **Databases**: PubMed + Scopus, searched to 5 July 2018.
- **Rejection criteria**: aging <6 months, thermocycling-only aging, no bond-strength data, no MMP inhibitor, no control, reviews, carious teeth.
- **Screening**: 3-stage; 740 initial → 43 accepted for full review (240 groups; 21 different inhibitors).
- **Meta-analysis subset**: only 0.2–2% CHX articles pooled (other inhibitors too heterogeneous) → 21 articles, grouped by aging (6/12/24 mo) and adhesive type (etch-and-rinse vs self-etch).
- **Risk of bias**: 6-item scale (randomization, intact teeth, per-instructions, same operator, sample-size calc, blinding) → high (1–2), medium (3–4), low (5–6).
- **Software**: MedCalc v19.2.1.

## Results

- 35/43 studies showed lower bond-strength loss with MMP inhibitors; 13 inhibitors gave ≥50% lower percentage loss vs control.
- Most common inhibitor: CHX (32 studies); next BAC (7 studies).
- Baseline (immediate) CHX vs control: **no significant difference** (p=0.308; I²=21.7%).
- 6 months: CHX significantly higher bond strength (**p<0.001**; I²=82.1%).
- 12 months: CHX significantly higher (**p<0.001**; I²=75.0%).
- 24 months: CHX significantly higher (**p<0.001**; I²=0%).
- Adhesive-interface fracture increased with aging regardless of inhibition.
- Risk of bias: 5/21 high, 16/21 medium, 0 low; no sample-size calculation in any study; all but one lacked operator blinding.
