## One-line Summary

In vitro 3-way factorial study (n=80, 8 groups, 90-day water storage): only HF concentration was significant — 10% HF yielded higher shear bond strength to Vita Mark II than 5% HF; etching time (60 vs 120 s) and phosphoric acid + sodium bicarbonate neutralization were both non-significant.

## Summary

This Brazilian in vitro study from UFRN used a 3-way factorial design to evaluate three variables in feldspathic ceramic surface treatment: HF acid concentration (5% vs 10%), etching time (60 vs 120 s), and use of phosphoric acid post-treatment followed by sodium bicarbonate neutralization. Vita Mark II ceramic blocks were cemented with AllCem Dual resin cement and stored for 90 days in water at 37°C before shear bond strength testing. The only significant variable was HF concentration (p=0.02), with 10% HF producing higher bond strength. Etching time and the phosphoric acid neutralization protocol had no significant effect, providing reassurance that clinicians using PA + NaHCO₃ to manage HF residues and toxicity are not compromising bond strength.

## Key Contributions

- 10% HF concentration > 5% HF for Vita Mark II shear bond strength (only significant factor)
- Increasing etching time from 60 s to 120 s does not improve (and may slightly harm) bond strength
- Phosphoric acid post-etching + sodium bicarbonate neutralization does not impair bond strength — safe for HF waste management
- 90-day water storage protocol provides durability data beyond the common 24-hour testing
- Cohesive ceramic and mixed failures dominant (82.5%) — indicates bond exceeded ceramic fatigue resistance

## Methodology

- Material: Vita Mark II (feldspathic ceramic), 80 blocks (10×12×2 mm), embedded in acrylic resin
- 3 factors, 2 levels each: HF% (5 vs 10%), time (60 s vs 120 s), PA (with vs without)
- HF product: Condac (FGM, Brazil), 5% or 10%
- PA post-treatment: 37% H₃PO₄ (Condac, FGM) 60 s → Na bicarbonate 1 min → ultrasonic 5 min (distilled water)
- Silane: Prosil (γ-MPS, FGM); Adhesive: Ambar (UDMA+HEMA, FGM)
- Cement: AllCem Dual (dual-cure, FGM), cylinders Ø 2 mm × 2 mm
- Storage: 37°C distilled water, 90 days
- Shear bond test (50 kgF load cell, 1 mm/min); SEM + stereomicroscope failure analysis
- Statistics: 3-way ANOVA + Tukey's test (α=0.05)

## Results

- HF concentration p=0.02 (significant): 10% > 5%
- Etching time p>0.05 (not significant): 60 s ≈ 120 s
- Phosphoric acid treatment p>0.05 (not significant): with PA ≈ without PA
- Failure modes: cohesive in ceramic 40%, mixed 42.5%, adhesive 17.5%
- Predominantly cohesive failures indicate sufficient bond strength exceeded ceramic material strength under 90-day aging conditions
