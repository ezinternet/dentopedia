## One-line Summary

In vitro nano-DMA + micro-permeability study (5 groups, 1-year SBF aging): rinse-out AB-type proanthocyanidin (PAC-DESIGNER) primers raised the complex modulus of the hybrid layer and underlying dentin and reduced interfacial micropermeability vs unprimed control, with trimeric (DP3) PACs outperforming tetrameric (DP4) — a layer-specific mechanism for durable resin-dentin bonding.

## Summary

According to PubMed, this NIH-funded study from the Bedran-Russo dentin-biomodulation group ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jdent.2026.106594)) dissects *how* **proanthocyanidin (PAC)** collagen cross-linking stabilizes the resin-dentin interface, rather than reporting a single bulk bond-strength figure. Using **nano-dynamic mechanical analysis (nano-DMA)** and rhodamine **micro-permeability** at 24 h, 6 months, and 1 year of simulated-body-fluid aging, it resolves the effect separately at the three interface layers — adhesive layer (AD), hybrid layer (HL), and underlying dentin (UD).

The PAC primers were made with the **DESIGNER** standardization concept (reproducible, scalable enrichment of specific oligomers from *Pinus massoniana* and *Cinnamomum verum* plant material), enabling controlled comparison of **trimeric (DP3)** vs **tetrameric (DP4)** AB-type PACs and of **interflavan-linkage (IFL)** configuration. The headline finding: PAC primers raise the **complex modulus (E\*)** of the HL and UD and **reduce interfacial micropermeability**, with **trimeric PACs (especially PM-AB) the most effective and most stable over the full year** — while the native viscoelastic (damping) behavior of each layer is preserved.

## Key Contributions

- **Layer-resolved** mechanism: PAC biomodulation acts strongest on the hybrid layer and the underlying (mineralized) dentin, not the adhesive layer.
- Demonstrates PAC diffusion into **mineralized underlying dentin** (not just demineralized matrix), with reinforcement persisting at 1 year.
- Maps **structure–activity relationships**: DP3 > DP4; terminal monomer (catechin > epicatechin); IFL configuration (A-type favors HL durability, B-type favors AD).
- Couples mechanical reinforcement with **reduced micropermeability** (sealing), proposing a unified durability mechanism (collagen cross-linking → resists enzymatic cleavage, displaces unbound water).

## Methodology

- 25 sound human molars; mid-coronal dentin, standardized smear layer. 5 groups (n=5): unprimed control, CV-AB, PM-AB (trimeric), CV-ABB, PM-ABA (tetrameric).
- Etch-and-rinse with **35% glycolic acid** (mild α-hydroxyl acid) → rinse-out neutral PAC primer (6.5% w/v, HEPES pH ~7, 1 min) → model methacrylate adhesive (Bis-GMA/TEGDMA/HEMA) → composite.
- Aging in simulated body fluid (pH 7.4, 37°C) to 24 h / 6 months / 1 year.
- **Nano-DMA** (TI980 triboindenter, Berkovich, 50 Hz, hydrated): E\* and damping (tan δ) at AD, HL, UD (10 μm below HL).
- **Micro-permeability**: rhodamine-B fluorescence emission intensity at HL and UD. ANOVA + post-hoc (α = 0.05).

## Results

| Interface layer | Effect of AB-type PAC primers (1-year) |
|---|---|
| Hybrid layer (HL) | All PAC > control E\*; CV-AB & PM-AB highest; **PM-AB stable across full year** |
| Underlying dentin (UD) | All PAC > control; **PM-AB highest**; trimeric > tetrameric |
| Adhesive layer (AD) | No difference at immediate/6 mo; CV-ABB & PM-AB higher at 1 year |
| Micro-permeability (HL & UD) | All PAC **less** rhodamine infiltration vs control (p<0.001) |

- **Trimeric (DP3) > tetrameric (DP4)** — better fit into collagen interfibrillar spaces.
- Damping capacity preserved within native dentin range (0.02–0.3) → viscoelastic nature intact.
- Control's UD micropermeability **increased** after 1 year, whereas PAC groups stayed sealed.
