## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1016/j.dental.2017.11.005](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.dental.2017.11.005)), this critical narrative review traces resin-dentin bonding from dentin collagen structure through hybrid-layer (HL) formation, the mechanisms of HL degradation, and the strategies developed to preserve the bond over time. The review frames decades of work around three pillars: understanding HL-degradation mechanisms, manufacturing improved adhesive systems, and developing HL-preservation strategies.

Note: this page is **abstract-only** — full text was not retrieved (no open-access copy), so section-level detail and numeric benchmarks are not available; the content reflects the PubMed abstract.

The review's core argument is that HL degradation occurs through two main mechanisms — enzymatic degradation of collagen fibrils and leaching of resin from the HL — both enabled by residual unbound water trapped between denuded collagen fibrils at the bottom of the HL. That water activates endogenous dentinal enzymes (matrix metalloproteinases, MMPs, and cysteine cathepsins), which degrade the denuded collagen matrix. Preservation strategies therefore fall into two families: removing the unbound water from inter-fibrillar gaps, and silencing endogenous enzymatic activity.

Clinically: durable resin-dentin bonds depend on managing the demineralized collagen interface — minimizing residual water in the bonded zone and inhibiting endogenous MMP/cathepsin activity (e.g., MMP-inhibitor pretreatments) are the levers that extend hybrid-layer longevity.

## Key Contributions

- Establishes a unifying framework: residual **unbound water** at the base of the hybrid layer is the common enabler of both degradation mechanisms.
- Distinguishes **two HL-degradation mechanisms**: endogenous enzymatic collagenolysis and resin leaching.
- Identifies the responsible **endogenous enzymes** (MMPs and cysteine cathepsins) activated within the HL.
- Organizes **preservation strategies** into water removal and enzyme silencing — a structure that maps directly onto clinical adhesive protocols and adjuncts.

## Methodology

Critical narrative review of peer-reviewed literature on hybrid-layer formation, degradation, and preservation, organized conceptually from dentin collagen structure → HL formation → degradation → preservation → clinical applications. Not a systematic review or meta-analysis; no pooled estimates. Detailed search methodology is not recoverable from the abstract.

## Results

- HL degradation proceeds via **two mechanisms**: (1) enzymatic degradation of collagen fibrils; (2) resin leaching from the HL.
- Both mechanisms are **enabled by residual unbound water** between denuded collagen fibrils at the bottom of the HL.
- **Endogenous MMPs and cysteine cathepsins** are activated and degrade the denuded collagen matrix.
- **Preservation strategies** entail (a) removal of unbound water from inter-fibrillar gaps and (b) silencing endogenous enzymatic activity by various modes.
- Authors conclude that **impressive progress** has been achieved despite remaining hurdles, with the large research volume reflecting the drive toward greater quality and longevity of resin-dentin bonds.
