## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 11 RCTs in noncarious cervical lesions found no significant difference in clinical performance between self-etch adhesives with vs without the 10-MDP functional monomer (p=0.05; I²=0–53%; moderate-to-high GRADE certainty).

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.2341/20-053-LIT](https://doi.org/10.2341/20-053-LIT)), this systematic review and meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD42016050538) asked whether self-etch adhesives containing the 10-MDP (10-methacryloyloxydecyl dihydrogen phosphate) functional monomer deliver better clinical performance in noncarious cervical lesion (NCCL) restorations than self-etch systems without it. The rationale is that 10-MDP's acidic phosphate group chemically interacts with hydroxyapatite to form a stable bond — an attractive in-vitro property. After duplicate removal, 4208 records were screened and 11 RCTs were included (9 low risk of bias, 2 unclear; GRADE moderate-to-high), all carried into the meta-analysis. The pooled result showed **no statistical difference** between "with 10-MDP" and "without 10-MDP" adhesives across all evaluated clinical criteria (p=0.05), with heterogeneity ranging from 0% to 53%. (This page is abstract-only — full text was not retrieved, so per-criterion effect sizes and confidence intervals are not captured.)

Clinically: choosing a self-etch adhesive *because* it contains 10-MDP does not, by itself, buy you better NCCL restoration outcomes — the molecule's favorable hydroxyapatite chemistry does not translate into a measurable clinical retention/integrity advantage at the body-of-evidence level. Select adhesives on overall track record and technique fit, not on 10-MDP presence alone.

## Key Contributions

- Synthesizes **clinical** RCT evidence (not bench bond-strength) on the value of 10-MDP in self-etch adhesives for NCCL.
- Applies Cochrane risk-of-bias + GRADE, producing a high-certainty negative answer.
- Counterbalances in-vitro enthusiasm for 10-MDP: stable HAp chemistry ≠ automatic clinical superiority.

## Methodology

- **Comparison**: self-etch adhesives **with** vs **without** the 10-MDP functional monomer.
- **Outcome**: clinical performance of restorations in noncarious cervical lesions (NCCL).
- **Databases**: MEDLINE/PubMed, Cochrane Library, Scopus, Web of Science, OpenGrey, Clinical Trials, Current Controlled Trials, EU Clinical Trials Register, plus reference hand-search.
- **Inclusion**: randomized controlled clinical trials only.
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane Collaboration tool. **Certainty**: GRADE. **Heterogeneity**: I².

## Results

- Records retrieved after duplicate removal: **4208**.
- Studies included (qualitative and quantitative): **11 RCTs** (all 11 pooled in the meta-analysis).
- Risk of bias: **9 low**, **2 unclear**.
- GRADE certainty of evidence: **moderate-to-high**.
- Clinical performance, with vs without 10-MDP: **no statistical difference for all evaluated criteria (p=0.05)**.
- Heterogeneity: **I² = 0% to 53%**.
- Conclusion: 10-MDP presence **did not influence** clinical performance of NCCL restorations.
