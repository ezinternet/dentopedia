## Summary

Dimitriadi et al. (2019) investigated why silane-containing universal adhesives and primers may underperform compared to dedicated prehydrolyzed silane primers when bonding to glass-ceramics. Using 13C-NMR spectroscopy on 5 commercially available products, the authors showed that only Calibra Silane Coupling Agent (CS) contained the desired active silanol monomers; other products contained silanol-siloxane adducts (Monobond Plus, Scotchbond Universal) or siloxane polymers (Kerr Silane Primer) or methoxylated-siloxane adducts (G-Multi Primer). These condensed/polymerized forms have reduced reactivity with ceramic surfaces, as confirmed by ATR-FTIR analysis showing chemical bonding evidence (Si-O peaks) only for CS on the ceramic substrate.

Shear bond strength (SBS) Weibull analysis showed CS ranked highest in characteristic life for both polished (Group A) and HF-etched (Group B) lithium disilicate surfaces. Critically, HF etching increased bond strength across all products, effectively leveling the playing field between products with active silanols and those with degraded silane forms — meaning that in HF-etched conditions, the silane chemistry advantage of CS is partially neutralized by micromechanical retention.

**Clinical implication**: The premise that silane-containing universal adhesives are equivalent to dedicated silane primers is chemically unfounded for most products. For optimal ceramic bonding, especially on polished surfaces (repairs, indirect bonding without surface roughening), a prehydrolyzed silane primer should be preferred. HF etching partially compensates for silane chemistry deficiencies through micromechanical retention.

## Key Contributions

- First study to use 13C-NMR to characterize silane chemical status in commercial dental silane primers and universal adhesives
- Established that most universal adhesives/multicomponent primers contain silane in degraded (siloxane) forms
- ATR-FTIR confirmed chemical bonding to ceramic substrate only for silanol monomer-containing product
- Weibull analysis provided statistically robust comparison of bond reliability (not just mean strength)
- Demonstrated that HF etching compensates for poor silane chemistry via micromechanical retention — explains why clinical outcomes may be acceptable even with degraded silane products when HF etching is used
- Addresses conflicting literature on whether surface condition (polished vs. HF-etched) or silane type is the dominant factor

## Methodology

- **Ceramic**: Lithium disilicate glass-ceramic (IPS e.max CAD/Press implied by context)
- **Surface conditions**: Group A (polished) vs. Group B (HF acid-etched)
- **Products**: CS (Calibra Silane), GM (G-Multi Primer), KS (Kerr Silane), MB (Monobond Plus), SB (Scotchbond Universal)
- **Characterization**:
  - 13C-NMR: Silane chemical status in vials (silanol, silanol-siloxane, siloxane polymer, methoxylated-siloxane)
  - ATR-FTIR: Chemical reactivity with ceramic surface
  - Optical profilometry: Surface roughness (Sa, Sz, Sdr, Sc, Sv), n=5/group
- **Bond testing**: SBS of flowable composite, n=20/group/product; Weibull statistical analysis
- **Failure mode**: Adhesive predominant; partial cohesive in Group B

## Results

**NMR Silane Status** (key mechanistic finding):
| Product | Silane Form | Reactivity |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| CS (Calibra Silane) | Silanol monomers | Highest |
| MB (Monobond Plus) | Silanol-siloxane adducts | Intermediate |
| SB (Scotchbond Universal) | Silanol-siloxane adducts | Intermediate |
| GM (G-Multi Primer) | Methoxylated-siloxane | Lower |
| KS (Kerr Silane) | Siloxane polymers | Lowest |

**SBS Weibull Characteristic Life Rankings**:
- Polished (Group A): CS > SB, KS, MB > GM > NS (no silane)
- HF-etched (Group B): CS > GM > SB, KS, MB, NS

**HF etching effect**: Group B significantly higher SBS than Group A for same silane (p < 0.05); for silanol-siloxane/siloxane products, the Group B elevation partially neutralizes the advantage of CS, bringing values to comparable levels.

**ATR-FTIR**: Si-O chemical bonding peaks detected only for CS on ceramic substrate; carboxylate salts formed for SB adhesive (more pronounced on polished surfaces).
