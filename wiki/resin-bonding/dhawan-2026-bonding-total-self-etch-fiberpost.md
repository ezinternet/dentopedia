## Summary

This in vitro study from Guru Nanak Dev Dental College (India, 2026) compared the push-out bond strength of glass fiber posts luted to root dentin using three adhesive system classes: total-etch (TE), self-etch (SE), and self-adhesive (SA). Sixty single-rooted mandibular premolars were prepared with standardized root canal treatment, post-space preparation, and randomly allocated to three groups (n=20 each). The middle root-third 5 mm slice was tested on a universal testing machine. SE demonstrated significantly superior bond strength (131.03 N), followed by TE (115.25 N), with SA showing the lowest performance (75.04 N). One-way ANOVA confirmed highly significant differences (p=0.001). The finding supports SE adhesives as the clinical choice for fiber post cementation, owing to reliable smear-layer penetration without the over-drying/over-wetting risk inherent to TE systems.

## Key Contributions

- Establishes SE system as superior to both TE and SA for glass fiber post push-out bond strength under standardized conditions
- Clarifies failure mechanisms: TE suffers from operator-sensitive moisture control; SA provides insufficient micromechanical interlocking
- 5 mm middle-segment slice methodology preferred over thinner slices to better replicate in vivo stresses
- Clinical implication: SE system (e.g., ParaCore with non-rinse conditioner) recommended as optimal luting strategy for GFP in endodontically treated teeth

## Methodology

- **Sample**: 60 freshly extracted mandibular premolars; decoronated to 15 mm root length; single canal verified radiographically
- **RCT protocol**: Chemo-mechanical prep (K-files → Neo Endo Flex 35/4%), NaOCl + EDTA irrigation, sectional obturation with gutta-percha + Endoseal sealer
- **Post space**: Fiber post drills to size 3; 24h setting time before post placement
- **Groups** (n=20 each):
  - Group A (TE): 37% phosphoric acid 15 s → rinse → ParaBond adhesive → ParaCore cement
  - Group B (SE): Non-rinse conditioner → ParaBond A+B → ParaCore cement
  - Group C (SA): Solocem self-adhesive cement only
- **Testing**: Push-out on universal testing machine (0.5 mm/min); 1 mm plunger; apical-to-coronal force direction; middle 5 mm segment
- **Statistics**: Shapiro-Wilk normality, one-way ANOVA, Tukey's HSD post hoc; Stata 18; p<0.05

## Results

| Group | System | Mean Bond Strength | Post-hoc |
|---|---|---|---|
| B (SE) | Self-etch | **131.03 N** | Highest; p=0.001 vs A and C |
| A (TE) | Total-etch | 115.25 N | Intermediate |
| C (SA) | Self-adhesive | 75.04 N | Lowest |

- All pairwise comparisons significant by Tukey's HSD
- Null hypothesis (no difference between groups) rejected

**Mechanistic explanation**:
- **SE superiority**: Acidic primer simultaneously conditions smear layer and penetrates dentin tubules, forming reliable hybrid layer; eliminates rinsing/drying steps that introduce moisture-control errors
- **TE inferiority relative to SE**: Despite maximum demineralization, risk of over-wet (monomer dissolution by hydrophilic monomers) or over-dry (collapsed collagen fibrils → incomplete hybrid layer) compromises deep-canal bond in confined space
- **SA lowest**: Only mild demineralization with chemical hydroxyapatite bond; insufficient micromechanical interlocking in root dentin
