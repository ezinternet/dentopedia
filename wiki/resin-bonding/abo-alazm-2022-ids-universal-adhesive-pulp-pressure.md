## One-line Summary

Eur J Dent 2022 in-vitro (50 human 3rd molars; iBOND SE vs GLUMA Bond Universal under 1-week simulated pulp pressure + CAD/CAM composite block): immediate dentin sealing (IDS) µTBS exceeded delayed dentin sealing (DDS) at 24h and 6mo (P<.05), MDP-containing GLUMA outperformed MDP-free iBOND, both adhesives showed 6-month Weibull strength decay, and both significantly reduced dentin permeability.

## Summary

This Egyptian in-vitro study extends Magne-era IDS evidence (OptiBond FL, SE Bond) to the current universal-adhesive era under more clinically relevant conditions: simulated pulp pressure for 1 week before CAD/CAM block cementation, plus 6-month aging. IDS preserves its µTBS advantage; the MDP-containing GLUMA Bond Universal outperforms the MDP-free iBOND at both 24h and 6mo. Both adhesives show Weibull strength decay over 6 months — IDS does not eliminate aging effects.

The dentin permeability result is mechanistically relevant: significant reduction in fluid filtration after IDS, supporting the (in-vitro) basis for POS reduction claims — though the clinical translation remains disputed ([[resin-bonding/josic-2022-ids-postoperative-sensitivity-sr-ma]] GRADE low).

## Key Contributions

1. Confirmed IDS µTBS advantage with universal adhesive (extending Magne lab's OptiBond FL/SE Bond data).
2. Demonstrated MDP-containing universal > MDP-free universal for IDS bonding.
3. Showed 6-month Weibull degradation even with IDS — long-term aging matters.
4. Quantified dentin permeability reduction by adhesive type.

## Methodology

50 caries-free human 3rd molars; 40 for µTBS, 10 for permeability discs. Simulated pulp pressure × 1 week before CAD/CAM resin composite block cementation. µTBS at 24h and 6mo water storage. Permeability: fluid filtration after etching, smear layer, adhesive. Stats: Shapiro-Wilk + Weibull.

Adhesives:
- iBOND Self-Etch (Heraeus Kulzer): UDMA, 4-META, glutaraldehyde — no MDP.
- GLUMA Bond Universal (Heraeus Kulzer): UDMA, MDP, 4-META, HEMA.

## Results

- IDS µTBS > DDS µTBS at 24h and 6mo (P<.05).
- GLUMA Bond Universal > iBOND Self-Etch for both IDS and DDS modes.
- Both adhesives: significant Weibull characteristic strength reduction at 6mo.
- Both adhesives: significant dentin permeability reduction, no inter-adhesive difference.
