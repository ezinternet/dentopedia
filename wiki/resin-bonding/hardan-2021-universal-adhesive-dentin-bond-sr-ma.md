## One-line Summary
In-vitro SR+MA of 61 studies on strategies to improve universal adhesive bond strength to dentin: ten strategies significantly increased bond strength (prior MMP inhibitor, prolonged application, scrubbing, selective dentin etching, non-atmospheric plasma, ethanol-wet bonding, prolonged air-blowing, multiple layers, prolonged curing, hydrophobic overlay), while shortened application time and pre-bonding dentin desensitizers significantly reduced it.

## Summary
SR+MA of 61 in vitro studies (5,671 initial hits) on strategies to improve universal adhesive bond strength to dentin. Ten strategies SIGNIFICANTLY IMPROVED bond strength: prior MMP inhibitor application (p<0.001), prolonged application time (p=0.007), scrubbing technique (p<0.001), selective dentin etching (p<0.001), non-atmospheric plasma (p=0.01), ethanol-wet bonding (p<0.01), prolonged air-blowing (p=0.02), multiple layer application (p=0.005), prolonged curing time (p=0.006), hydrophobic layer coating (p<0.001). Two strategies IMPAIRED bond strength: shortened application time (p=0.006) and dentin desensitizers (p=0.01). High heterogeneity in most analyses.

## Key Contributions
- Most comprehensive quantitative synthesis of universal adhesive bond strength modifiers
- Provides "do this" and "avoid this" clinical checklist
- Confirms MMP inhibition consistently improves bond — mechanistic support for biomodification
- Confirms scrubbing (active application) improves bond — supports manufacturer instructions
- Identifies dentin desensitizers as bond-impairing — important clinical caveat

## Methodology
- **Design**: SR + MA
- **Databases**: PubMed, ISI Web of Science, Scopus, EMBASE (through Sep 2020)
- **Inclusion**: In vitro studies on universal adhesive dentin bond strength with additional strategies
- **Software**: RevMan v5.3.5
- **Quality assessment**: Modified parameters from prior SR

## Results — Strategy Effects on Universal Adhesive Bond Strength

| Strategy | Effect | p-value |
|---|---|---|
| Prior MMP inhibitor (CHX, EDC) | ↑ | <0.001 |
| Prolonged application time | ↑ | 0.007 |
| Scrubbing (active) application | ↑ | <0.001 |
| Selective dentin etching | ↑ | <0.001 |
| Non-atmospheric (cold) plasma | ↑ | 0.01 |
| Ethanol-wet bonding | ↑ | <0.01 |
| Prolonged air-blowing | ↑ | 0.02 |
| Multiple layer application | ↑ | 0.005 |
| Prolonged light-curing | ↑ | 0.006 |
| Hydrophobic layer overlay | ↑ | <0.001 |
| Shortened application time | ↓ | 0.006 |
| Dentin desensitizers (pre-bonding) | ↓ | 0.01 |

## Clinical Implications
- Apply 2% chlorhexidine rinse before bonding (MMP inhibition)
- Use scrubbing motion during application
- Apply for full manufacturer-recommended time (don't shortcut)
- Use selective enamel etching with phosphoric acid
- Adequate air-blowing and light-curing time
- Consider hydrophobic overlay for additional layer
- Avoid dentin desensitizers immediately before bonding

## Caveats
- All in vitro — clinical translation needed
- High heterogeneity in most subgroups (different products, substrates)
- "Universal adhesive" lumps different manufacturers and formulations
