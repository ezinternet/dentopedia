## One-line Summary

Systematic review (PRISMA-P; 72 of 1383 references) finding that 10-MDP self-etch/universal adhesives form a nano-layered, acid-base-resistant interface with low-solubility MDP-Ca salts, giving the most hydrolytically stable, durable resin-dentin bond among functional monomers — when applied by scrubbing with adequate infiltration time.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.3390/ma12050790](https://doi.org/10.3390/ma12050790)), this PRISMA-P systematic review screened 1383 references (PubMed, Cochrane Library, Web of Science, Embase) and included 72 to characterize what physically distinguishes the 10-MDP (10-methacryloyloxydecyl dihydrogen phosphate) adhesive interface and to compare its bond strength and stability against other functional monomers. The recurring finding across all three evaluated axes — molecular structure/nanolayering (7 studies), acid-base-resistant zone (6 studies), and adhesive stability (22 studies) — is that 10-MDP establishes an unusually intense and durable chemical interaction with hydroxyapatite. Its phosphate group ionically bonds calcium to form water-insoluble MDP-Ca salts (lower solubility than salts of 4-META, phenyl-P, etc.), which protect collagen fibrils and keep the interface stable after one year of water storage. 10-MDP also self-assembles into nano-layered structures at enamel and dentin interfaces, producing a water-stable interface — though the authors caution nanolayering is not seen across all commercial 10-MDP adhesives, so it enhances immediate performance and biodegradation resistance rather than being the sole durability mechanism. Bond quality also depends on concentration/purity of 10-MDP and on co-formulants (ethanol limits phosphate dissociation; MDP-HEMA aggregates leave collagen unprotected; zinc competes for calcium). **Clinically:** apply 10-MDP adhesives with an active scrubbing technique and give the solution adequate dwell/infiltration time so it can hybridize and form the stable MDP-Ca salt that protects collagen; use selective enamel etching with higher-pH one-bottle systems.

## Key Contributions

- Synthesizes 72 studies showing 10-MDP's interface superiority rests on three linked features: self-assembled **nanolayering**, an **acid-base-resistant zone (ABRZ)**, and low-solubility **MDP-Ca salts**.
- Frames the mechanism via the adhesion-decalcification concept: a stable (low-solubility) calcium-monomer complex means the bond persists instead of re-demineralizing.
- Distills the evidence into application rules — scrubbing technique, infiltration dwell time, selective enamel etching, optional extra hydrophobic layer for one-step systems.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review (no meta-analysis; methodological disparity precluded pooling), PRISMA-P protocol.
- **PICO**: resin-bonded restorations (P); 10-MDP adhesives (I); other adhesives/functional monomers (C); interface characteristics, bond strength, adhesive stability (O).
- **Databases/languages/cutoff**: PubMed + Cochrane + Web of Science + Embase; English/Spanish/Portuguese; through January 2019.
- **Selection**: 1383 → 212 (title/abstract) → 72 (full text), dual independent full-text review.
- **Outcome buckets**: 7 nanolayering/structure, 6 ABRZ, 22 stability.

## Results

1. **Nanolayering**: self-assembled nano-layered structures (signature of 10-MDP) found on enamel and dentin produce a water-stable interface; NOT universal across all 10-MDP adhesives, so they boost immediate performance and biodegradation resistance rather than solely driving durability.
2. **ABRZ**: 10-MDP forms an acid-base-resistant zone adjacent to the hybrid layer, outperforming other common functional monomers.
3. **MDP-Ca salts**: water-insoluble, lower solubility than competing monomers' salts; protect collagen; interface stable after 1 year of water storage.
4. **Stability vs others (22 studies)**: long hydrophobic spacer chain resists hydrolytic degradation; 4-META and phenyl-P bond at substantially lower potential or give hydrolytically unstable bonds.
5. **Concentration/purity**: under-concentrated 10-MDP universal adhesives gave poor interfaces → optimal concentration/purity likely exists; impurities/dimers reduce adhesive force.
6. **Co-formulant effects**: MDP-Ca formation depends more on other constituents than on MDP/water concentration; ethanol limits MDP phosphate dissociation; MDP-HEMA aggregates compromise MDP-collagen interaction; zinc competes for calcium; Ca(OH)₂ raises degree of conversion without harming bond/nanoleakage; HEMA-free preferred.
7. **Application technique**: active scrubbing improves solvent evaporation, monomer impregnation, water-chasing and smear-layer dissolution (especially mild self-etch); selective enamel etching advised for higher-pH one-bottle systems; extra hydrophobic layer aids one-step durability.
8. **Cautions**: minimally toxic 10-MDP concentrations promoted pulp-cell inflammation and suppressed odontoblastic differentiation; MDP hydrolysis acidifies the adhesive during storage.
