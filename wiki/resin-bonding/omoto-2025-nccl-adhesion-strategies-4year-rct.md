## One-line Summary
4-year split-mouth RCT (50 patients, 200 NCCLs) comparing universal adhesive +/- selective enamel etching and RMGIC +/- EDTA: selective enamel etching cut retention loss, RMGIC lost more luster, and survival curves did not differ (p=0.315).

## Summary
This UNESP randomized, double-blind, split-mouth trial restored 200 NCCLs in 50 patients (mean age 61) using four adhesion strategies: Scotchbond Universal without etching (SBU), with selective enamel etching (E-SBU), resin-modified glass-ionomer (Vitremer; RMGIC), and RMGIC with EDTA pretreatment (E-RMGIC). Over four years (40 patients returning), retention survival curves did not differ significantly among the four groups (p=0.315), but selective enamel etching reduced retention loss for the universal adhesive (SBU alone dropped below its baseline retention while E-SBU did not). Ionomer restorations lost more surface luster/texture than composite. EDTA pretreatment of RMGIC produced no clear clinical benefit. Initial marginal defects and marginal discoloration increased across all strategies by four years. The trial extends the universal-adhesive mode literature to a four-year horizon and supports selective enamel etching as a practical retention-preserving step.

## Key Contributions
- 4-year head-to-head of composite (universal adhesive) vs RMGIC strategies, each with/without an enamel/dentin pre-conditioning step.
- Clinical evidence that selective enamel etching reduces universal-adhesive retention loss over time.
- Shows EDTA pretreatment of RMGIC offers no clear clinical advantage and RMGIC degrades in surface luster faster than composite.

## Methodology
Prospective, randomized, double-blind, split-mouth RCT; 200 restorations / 50 patients; modified USPHS criteria at baseline and 1-4 years; Kruskal-Wallis, Friedman, and Wilcoxon tests (p<0.05). Elderly-leaning cohort (mean 61, range 38-92); 40/50 patients at 4-year recall; no intention-to-treat.

## Results
Retention survival: no significant difference among groups (p=0.315). SBU retention at 4 yr < baseline; E-SBU mitigated loss. All groups showed reduced alpha scores for marginal integrity and discoloration vs baseline; initial marginal defects increased universally. RMGIC and E-RMGIC degraded more in surface texture/luster than SBU/E-SBU.
