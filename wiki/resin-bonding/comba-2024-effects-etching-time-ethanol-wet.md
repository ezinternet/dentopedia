## One-line Summary

In vitro study (60 single-root teeth, 4 groups): ethanol pre-treatment significantly increased push-out bond strength of a multi-mode adhesive on radicular dentin (p<0.01) and reduced hybrid-layer MMP activity (p<0.01), while etching time (15 vs 30 s) did not affect bond strength.

## 한줄요약

60개 단근치 in vitro 실험(4군): 에탄올 전처리가 다목적 접착제의 근관내 상아질 push-out 접착강도를 유의하게 증가(p<0.01)시키고 혼성층 MMP 활성을 감소(p<0.01)시켰으며, 산부식 시간(15 vs 30초)은 접착강도에 영향 없음.

## Summary

Comba et al. crossed two variables — phosphoric-acid etching time (15 vs 30 s) and ethanol pre-treatment — on radicular dentin of 60 single-root teeth restored with fiber posts and a multi-mode universal adhesive (Clearfil Universal Bond Quick). Using push-out testing and in situ zymography, they found ethanol pre-treatment was the dominant beneficial factor: it raised bond strength (p<0.01) and lowered hybrid-layer MMP gelatinolytic activity (p<0.01). Etching time did not affect bond strength, but the longer 30 s etch significantly increased MMP activity — an argument against over-etching. By demonstrating the ethanol wet-bonding benefit at the root, with a modern universal adhesive and a direct degradation-enzyme readout, this study reinforces the coronal-dentin ethanol wet-bonding anchor and pushes its applicability into post-endodontic fiber-post cementation.

## Key Contributions

- Confirms ethanol wet-bonding benefit on radicular dentin (not just coronal) in a fiber-post model.
- Separates etching time (neutral for strength, harmful for MMP at 30 s) from ethanol pre-treatment (beneficial for both).
- Provides direct MMP-activity evidence linking solvent choice to the collagen-degradation pathway.

## Methodology

4 groups (n=15): G1 15 s etch + UBQ; G2 30 s + UBQ; G3 15 s + ethanol + UBQ; G4 30 s + ethanol + UBQ. Teeth endodontically treated (WaveOne Gold, 5% NaOCl/10% EDTA), obturated, 8 mm post space, Rebilda #15 fiber post, DC Core dual-cure cement (40 s cure). After 24 h artificial saliva, 1 mm slices for push-out + nanoleakage (coronal/apical) and in situ zymography. ANOVA + Tukey, α=0.05.

## Results

- Ethanol pre-treatment → significantly higher push-out bond strength (p<0.01).
- Etching time → no significant effect on bond strength.
- MMP activity present in all groups; significantly increased at 30 s etch.
- Ethanol pre-treatment → significantly reduced hybrid-layer MMP activity (p<0.01).
