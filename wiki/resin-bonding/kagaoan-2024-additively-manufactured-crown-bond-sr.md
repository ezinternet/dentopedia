## 한줄요약
3D 프린팅(주로 광경화 레진) 크라운 6개 in vitro 연구 SR: AM 크라운의 결합강도는 밀링 크라운과 동등하며, 알루미나 샌드블라스팅이 가장 권장됨. 임상 적용 전 임상 연구 필요.

## Summary
SR of 6 in vitro studies on bond strength of additively manufactured (AM) crown materials (5 photopolymerized resin, 1 zirconia 3D-printed). AM crowns made from photopolymers showed bond strength comparable to milled materials. No definitive superior cement type identified across studies. Airborne-particle abrasion with Al₂O₃ generally recommended for surface pretreatment. All 6 studies had low risk of bias. Major literature gap — no clinical data exists for cementation performance.

## Key Contributions
- First systematic review specifically on AM crown bond strength
- Establishes parity of photopolymer AM crowns with milled crowns in bond performance (in vitro)
- Identifies alumina airborne-particle abrasion as consistent pretreatment recommendation
- Highlights critical research gap: no clinical studies on cementation of AM crowns
- Suggests caution before adopting AM crowns for permanent restorations until clinical RCT data emerges

## Methodology
- **Design**: SR following PRISMA-P
- **PICO(S)**: P=AM crown specimens; I=cementation/post-processing; C=untreated or milled; O=bond strength; S=quantitative in vitro
- **Databases**: Ovid MEDLINE, Scopus, Web of Science, Google Scholar (through Jan 2023)
- **Risk of bias**: Modified CONSORT — all included low risk
- **Bond strength tests**: tensile, micro-tensile, micro-shear, shear, pull-off

## Results
- 6 studies included (5 photopolymer, 1 zirconia 3D-printed)
- AM photopolymer crown bond strength ≈ milled crowns (3 direct comparison studies)
- Surface pretreatment: alumina airborne-particle abrasion most consistently recommended
- No definitive superior cement type
- Variables analyzed: surface pretreatments, airborne abrasion pressure, cement type, crown taper, artificial aging

## Clinical Implications
- AM crowns are mechanically promising but lack clinical validation
- For provisional/short-term restorations, AM crowns are reasonable
- Surface preparation with alumina airborne abrasion + appropriate primer/cement
- Avoid skipping pretreatment — bond is not "automatic" with AM materials
