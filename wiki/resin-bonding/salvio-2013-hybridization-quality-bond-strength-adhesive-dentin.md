## One-line Summary

In vitro study (10 molars, 5 adhesives, µ-TBS + SEM nanoleakage) showing two-step self-etch adhesives — especially Clearfil SE Bond — achieve superior bond strength and hybridization quality, with hybridization quality directly predicting immediate bond strength.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

This in vitro study compared 5 adhesive systems on 40 caries-free human third molars: two single-step self-etch (ADP, XE), two two-step self-etch (SE = Clearfil SE Bond, ADSE = Adhe SE), and one single-step etch-and-rinse (SB = Adper Single Bond). Microtensile bond strength (µ-TBS) and SEM nanoleakage were evaluated. Clearfil SE Bond (mild pH 1.9 primer) achieved the highest µ-TBS, followed by ADSE and XE at intermediate levels, with SB and ADP (strong SE) performing worst. SEM showed that SE and ADSE produced well-sealed hybrid layers with minimal silver uptake, while SB showed the most intense nanoleakage (porous bottom zone from the E&R concentration gradient) and ADP/XE showed intermediate-to-high silver deposits. The study concludes that hybridization quality — not merely interaction depth or acidity category — directly governs immediate bond strength.

## Key Contributions

- **Hybridization quality predicts immediate µ-TBS**: demonstrates direct correlation between hybrid layer sealing integrity (nanoleakage) and same-session bond strength, not just long-term durability
- **Two-step SE superiority explained mechanistically**: mild-pH two-step SE (Clearfil SE Bond) leaves hydroxyapatite partially intact to support collagen and minimizes residual water that would inhibit polymerization — best of both worlds
- **Strong single-step SE paradox**: strong pH produces deep demineralization resembling E&R but with ionization-medium water causing incomplete monomer polymerization → inferior results despite deeper hybridization
- **E&R nanoleakage mechanism**: natural resin concentration gradient in phosphoric-acid-etched dentin creates water-rich, resin-poor zone at hybrid layer base → highest permeability despite full collagen exposure

## Methodology

- **Specimens**: 40 caries-free human third molars; occlusal enamel ground to expose mid-coronal dentin; smear layer standardized with #600-grit SiC (60 s)
- **Adhesive groups** (5): ADP (single-step SE, strong), XE (single-step SE, strong), SE (two-step SE, mild), ADSE (two-step SE, mild), SB (single-step E&R)
- **µ-TBS**: beams ~0.8 mm² cut from 10 teeth (8 quarters/group, 25 sticks/group); Filtek Z250 composite; 24 h water storage at 37°C; 0.5 mm/min; ANOVA + Tukey (α = 0.05)
- **Nanoleakage SEM**: 30 additional teeth; silver methenamine + ammoniacal silver nitrate labeling; backscattered SEM

## Results

| Adhesive | Type | µ-TBS rank | Nanoleakage |
|---|---|---|---|
| SE (Clearfil SE Bond) | 2-step SE, pH 1.9 | 1st (highest) | Minimal |
| ADSE (Adhe SE) | 2-step SE, pH 1.7 | 2nd–3rd (= XE) | Low |
| XE (Xeno III) | 1-step SE, pH ~1 | 2nd–3rd (= ADSE) | Moderate |
| SB (Adper Single Bond) | 1-step E&R | 4th–5th (= ADP) | High (most intense) |
| ADP (Adper Prompt) | 1-step SE, strong | 4th–5th (= SB) | High |

- SE significantly higher than all others (P < 0.05)
- ADSE = XE > SB = ADP
- SB showed most intense silver uptake at resin-dentin interface; SE/ADSE showed least
