## One-line Summary

Narrative review (PubMed/Scopus/LILACS, 2005–2025) of acidic functional monomers in contemporary dental adhesives: 10-MDP dominates (~50% of marketed products) and beats 4-META and GPDM on bonding, hydrolytic stability and collagen interaction; 4-META is a carboxylic monomer (two -COOH groups) that hydrolyzes to 4-MET (pKa ≈ 2.9, vs 10-MDP 2.2) and is structurally weaker but still widely used.

## Summary

This literature-based review from Bart Van Meerbeek's group critically maps the physico-chemical properties of the acidic functional monomers that drive chemical adhesion in modern self-etch (SE) and universal adhesives, plus self-adhesive materials. The three "key players" are 10-MDP, GPDM and 4-META. The unifying argument: a monomer's chemical structure — the type of acidic moiety (phosphate vs carboxylic), the spacer-chain length, and the hydrophilic/hydrophobic balance — determines its etching efficacy, its bond strength, the stability of the monomer-calcium salt it forms, and its long-term hydrolytic durability. By this logic **10-MDP** (long 10-carbon spacer, phosphate group, very hydrophobic Log P 4.09) self-assembles into ~1.9-nm nano-layered 10-MDP_Ca salts, stably complexes collagen, and resists hydrolysis — making it the most reliable monomer and the one present in nearly half of marketed adhesives.

For this wiki the page's value is as the **canonical 4-META composition reference** (the monomer behind the Super-Bond / 4-META-MMA-TBB resin family). 4-META is 4-methacryloxyethyl trimellitic **anhydride**, a **carboxylic** acidic monomer presenting **two carboxylic acid groups**; in aqueous solution it **hydrolyzes to 4-MET** (the di-acid form). It is a weaker acid than 10-MDP (4-MET pKa ≈ 2.9 vs 10-MDP 2.2 vs orthophosphoric acid 2.0), bonds HAp ionically through its carboxyls but forms a comparatively **soluble, less hydrolytically stable Ca salt**, and — unlike 10-MDP — does **not** stably complex dentin collagen. It nonetheless persists in many formulations and, at high concentration (40 wt%), can maintain 1-year bond stability.

## Key Contributions

- Common-basis comparison of contemporary acidic monomers (MW, Log P, Log S, pKa) so 10-MDP, GPDM and 4-META can be ranked on chemistry, not marketing.
- Canonical positioning of **4-META** (structure, hydrolysis to 4-MET, acidity, HAp/collagen behaviour) — the reference this wiki previously lacked.
- Mechanistic account of **why 10-MDP wins**: spacer length, hydrophobicity, nano-layering, stable collagen complexation, hydrolytic resistance.
- Context for the **MMA-TBB** chemistry (Super-Bond family) and how acidic monomers interfere with CQ/amine vs phosphine-oxide/borate initiators.

## Methodology

- Qualitative literature synthesis (narrative review, NOT a meta-analysis), drawing on both peer-reviewed studies and manufacturer technical data.
- Searches in PubMed, Scopus and LILACS; 20-year window (2005–2025); no language restriction.
- Data domains: chemical structure/composition, adhesive performance, pKa, etching efficacy, polymerization, mechanical properties, toxicity, hydrolytic stability/degradation.
- Framing: the adhesion-decalcification (AD/AE) model and the etch-and-rinse (E&R) vs self-etch (SE) dichotomy. Key tables — commercial-adhesive monomer composition; MW/Log P/Log S; pKa vs orthophosphoric acid.

## Results

### 4-META / 4-MET (focus — Super-Bond composition reference)

- **Structure**: 4-methacryloxyethyl trimellitic **anhydride**; **carboxylic-acid** monomer with **two -COOH groups** as the active moiety (phosphate-free, unlike 10-MDP/GPDM/phenyl-P).
- **Hydrolysis**: in aqueous solution 4-META → **4-MET** (4-methacryloxyethyl trimellitic acid). Commercial tables list the pair as "4-META/4-MET".
- **Physico-chemical**: 4-META MW 304.2, Log P 2.00, Log S −3.04; 4-MET MW 322.3, Log P 1.71, Log S −2.74. Markedly **less hydrophobic than 10-MDP** (Log P 4.09).
- **Acidity (pKa)**: orthophosphoric acid 2.0 · 10-MDP 2.2 · **4-MET 2.9** · MMEP 3.1 · MAC-10 4.7. Higher pKa → **weaker mineral-dissolution/etching** than 10-MDP.
- **Etching/application**: ~20 s active application optimal (same as GPDM); <10 s gives inferior bond strength.
- **HAp salt**: 4-META_Ca is **more soluble / less hydrolytically stable** than the insoluble, nano-layering 10-MDP_Ca.
- **Collagen**: 10-MDP **stably interacts with collagen, as opposed to 4-META** (4-META lacks the stable phosphate-collagen complexation).
- **Concentration effect (Amaral 2016)**: 12 wt% lowest degree of conversion; only **40 wt% 4-META maintained bonding after 1 y** (all maintained 6-mo stability).
- **Degradation**: 4-META (like 10-MDP) undergoes **ester cleavage** to non-functional by-products; 10-MDP outperforms 4-META/GPDM/phenyl-P on hydrolytic stability across thermocycling (30k–100k).
- **Cytotoxicity**: 4-META IC50 is 56× higher than UDMA, 170× higher than Bis-GMA (0.03 mmol/L), ~2× higher than HEMA (i.e., less toxic than UDMA/Bis-GMA); usable in experimental pulp-capping with good cell viability.

### MMA / TBB (Super-Bond / 4-META-MMA-TBB resin chemistry)

- **MMA** = bulk/diluent monomer; **TBB (tri-n-butylborane)** = self-cure initiator of MMA-TBB self-polymerizing resins.
- Acidic monomers inactivate CQ/amine co-initiators via acid-base reaction; **borate (TBB) initiators and phosphine-oxides (TPO/BAPO) are less inhibited** by acidic monomers than CQ/amine — relevant to why 4-META-MMA-TBB cures well.
- Hiraba 2023: 10-MDP/MMA-TBB luting resin to enamel, 1.7–2.0 mol% 10-MDP optimal (non-etched, after aging).

### 10-MDP / GPDM benchmark

- **10-MDP**: ~50% of marketed adhesives; long 10-C spacer + phosphate → ~1.9-nm nano-layering, stable collagen complexation, best hydrolytic stability. Meta-analytic data (64 adhesives): 10-MDP **significantly > 4-META, 4-MET, 6-MHP, PENTA, Pyro-EMA (P<0.01)**.
- **GPDM**: short spacer, hydrophilic, tends to **etch rather than adhere**; lower bonding efficacy.
