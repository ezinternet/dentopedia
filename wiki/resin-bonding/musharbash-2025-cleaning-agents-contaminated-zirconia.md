## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1111/jerd.13512](https://doi.org/10.1111/jerd.13512)), this in-vitro study compared four methods of decontaminating saliva-contaminated zirconia before resin bonding: air-particle abrasion (APC) versus three commercial cleaners — Katana Cleaner (KC), Ivoclean (IC), and Zirclean (ZC). Across 100 KATANA STML specimens bonded with Panavia V5 and tested with and without 10,000 thermal cycles, air-particle abrasion produced significantly higher shear bond strength than all chemical cleaners (p<0.05), but the chemical cleaners were judged viable alternatives. Thermocycling significantly reduced bond strength in every group. This page is abstract-only — exact SBS values, the ranking among the three chemical cleaners, and failure-mode distribution were not retrieved.

Clinically: when a zirconia restoration is contaminated by saliva at try-in, re-roughening by air-particle abrasion gives the strongest re-bond, but where re-abrasion is impractical (e.g., chairside on a finished intaglio), a dedicated cleaner (Katana Cleaner / Ivoclean / Zirclean) is an acceptable substitute. Expect some bond-strength loss with thermal aging regardless of cleaning method.

## Key Contributions

- Single-bench head-to-head of mechanical (APC) versus three commercial chemical cleaners (KC, IC, ZC) on saliva-contaminated zirconia.
- Used a clinically relevant MDP-containing dual-cure cement (Panavia V5) and 10,000-cycle thermal aging.
- Establishes air abrasion as the highest-performing decontamination while validating chemical cleaners as practical alternatives.

## Methodology

- **Specimens**: 100 KATANA STML (Noritake) zirconia specimens; 4 experimental arms + 1 control, n=20 each.
- **Contamination**: human saliva.
- **Cleaning arms**: air-particle abrasion (APC), Katana Cleaner (KC), Ivoclean (IC), Zirclean (ZC).
- **Bonding/aging**: composite cylinders cemented with Panavia V5; 48 h water storage at 37°C, then half thermocycled ×10,000.
- **Testing**: shear bond strength at 0.5 mm/min; failure mode by stereomicroscope.
- **Statistics**: one-way ANOVA + Bonferroni (α=0.05).

## Results

- Air-particle abrasion (APC): significantly higher SBS than all other cleaning methods (p<0.05).
- Katana Cleaner, Ivoclean, Zirclean: viable chemical alternatives to air abrasion.
- Thermocycling: significant SBS reduction in all groups (p<0.05).
- Numeric SBS means/SD and per-group failure-mode data: not available (abstract-only).
