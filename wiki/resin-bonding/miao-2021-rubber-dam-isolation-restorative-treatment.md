## One-line Summary

Cochrane SR+MA (6 RCTs, 1342 participants, all high risk of bias): rubber dam isolation may increase survival of direct composite restorations of NCCLs at 6 months (OR 2.29, 95% CI 1.05–4.99; low-certainty), with very uncertain evidence at later time points.

## Summary

This Cochrane review (update of the 2016 version, `.pub3`) assesses whether rubber dam isolation improves restorative treatment outcomes compared with other isolation methods. Effective moisture and microbial control is a prerequisite for successful adhesive restoration. Six RCTs (2010–2015, 1342 participants, mostly children) were included; all were at high risk of bias. Five compared rubber dam with cotton rolls and one with the Isolite system. The only outcome reaching low-certainty evidence is restoration survival of non-carious cervical lesion (NCCL) composites at 6 months, favouring rubber dam. At all other time points the evidence is very uncertain. No study reported adverse effects or direct treatment cost.

## Key Contributions

- Highest-tier (Cochrane SR+MA) evidence on isolation method as a determinant of restoration survival.
- Quantifies a 6-month survival benefit of rubber dam over cotton rolls for direct composite NCCL restorations (OR 2.29).
- Highlights the weak/very-uncertain evidence base (all studies high risk of bias) and the absence of adverse-effect and cost data.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review with meta-analysis; GRADE certainty assessment.
- **Eligibility**: RCTs (incl. split-mouth) >1 month comparing rubber dam vs alternative isolation methods for restorative treatments.
- **Search**: Cochrane Oral Health Trials Register, CENTRAL, MEDLINE, Embase, LILACS, SciELO, Chinese databases (CBM, VIP, CNKI), and trial registries; searched 13 January 2021; no language/date limits.
- **Process**: Two authors independently screened, extracted, and assessed risk of bias.
- **Comparisons**: (1) rubber dam vs cotton rolls (5 studies); (2) rubber dam vs Isolite system (1 study, chair-time outcome).

## Results

- **Included**: 6 RCTs, 1342 participants (233 lost to follow-up); all high risk of bias. One cotton-roll study excluded from analysis (data inconsistencies).
- **6 months — NCCL direct composite** (pooled, 2 studies, n=192): rubber dam may increase survival, OR 2.29 (95% CI 1.05–4.99); **low-certainty**.
- **12 months** (1 study, n=30): little/no effect, OR 1.38 (95% CI 0.45–4.28); very low-certainty.
- **18 months** (1 study, n=30): OR 1.00 (95% CI 0.45–2.25); very low-certainty.
- **24 months — proximal ART, primary molars** (1 study, n=559): rubber dam may reduce failure, HR 0.80 (95% CI 0.66–0.97); very low-certainty.
- **Adverse effects / cost**: not reported by any study.
- **Authors' conclusion**: low-certainty evidence that rubber dam may lower restoration failure vs cotton rolls at 6 months; evidence very uncertain at other time points; further high-quality research needed.
