In vitro (n=70 feldspathic porcelain disks, 7 groups, SBS after 5,000 thermocycles): Separate silane application significantly increased repair bond strength with Single Bond Universal and All Bond Universal, but not with FuturaBond Universal — silane remains necessary even with silane-containing universal adhesives for most products.

## Summary

Valian et al. (2021) addressed a critical clinical question: do silane-containing universal adhesives eliminate the need for separate silane application when repairing fractured feldspathic porcelain? In a 7-group in vitro design, 70 feldspathic porcelain disks (Ceramco 3) were HF-etched (9.5%, 2 min) and then bonded with Filtek Z250 composite using various adhesive protocols, with or without an additional separate Bis-Silane step. After 24 h water storage and 5,000 thermocycles, shear bond strength (SBS) was tested.

Key finding: for Single Bond Universal and All Bond Universal, adding a separate silane step **significantly** increased SBS (p < 0.001 for both). However, FuturaBond Universal showed no significant difference regardless of additional silane. This suggests that FuturaBond's silane formulation achieves more effective incorporation/retention than the other two universal adhesives.

**Clinical takeaway**: Even when using silane-containing universal adhesives (e.g., Single Bond Universal, All Bond Universal) for ceramic repair, a dedicated separate silane application step following HF etching remains beneficial for most products. Clinicians should not assume universal adhesive silane content is equivalent to dedicated silane priming.

## Key Contributions

- Challenged the assumption that universal adhesive silane content eliminates need for separate silane priming
- Demonstrated product-specific behavior: FuturaBond behaves differently from Single Bond Universal and All Bond Universal
- All groups baseline-treated with 9.5% HF (2 min) + thermocycled for clinically relevant aging
- Mixed failure mode predominant across groups — bonding was clinically adequate in all groups; differences were quantitative, not qualitative
- Open-access paper (CC BY 3.0) from Shahid Beheshti University of Medical Sciences

## Methodology

- **Ceramic**: Feldspathic porcelain disks (Ceramco 3, Dentsply), 6 mm × 2 mm, n=10/group
- **Surface prep**: All specimens polished (400, 600 grit SiC) → cleaned with H3PO4 → HF 9.5% (Bisco), 2 min
- **Groups**:
  - C: HF + Bis-Silane (Bisco) + Porcelain Bond — *conventional control*
  - HSB: HF + Single Bond Universal (3M ESPE)
  - HSSB: HF + Bis-Silane + Single Bond Universal
  - HAB: HF + All Bond Universal (Bisco)
  - HSAB: HF + Bis-Silane + All Bond Universal
  - HFB: HF + FuturaBond Universal (Voco)
  - HSFB: HF + Bis-Silane + FuturaBond Universal
- **Composite**: Filtek Z250, 2 mm in Tygon tubes (3 × 4 mm), light-cured 40 s
- **Aging**: 37°C water, 24 h + 5,000 thermocycles (5–55°C, 30 s/10 s)
- **Test**: SBS (ZwickRoell Z050), 1 mm/min crosshead speed
- **Failure analysis**: Stereomicroscope (×20), SEM for representative specimens
- **Statistics**: One-way ANOVA, Tukey's post-hoc (SPSS v.21)

## Results

- Overall group differences significant (p < 0.0001)
- **HSSB > HSB** (p < 0.001): Separate silane boosted SB Universal performance
- **HSAB > HAB** (p < 0.001): Separate silane boosted All Bond Universal performance
- **HSFB = HFB** (p > 0.05): No benefit from separate silane with FuturaBond
- Dominant failure mode: mixed (adhesive + cohesive) in all groups
- Product-specific silane integration efficacy is the key variable
