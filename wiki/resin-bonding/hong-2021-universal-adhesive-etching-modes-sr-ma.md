## 한줄요약
유니버설 접착제(Universal Adhesive)를 임상에서 사용 시 산부식-세척법(Etch-and-Rinse)이 자가산부식법(Self-Etch)보다 유지율, 변연적합도, 변연착색 모두에서 통계적으로 유의하게 우수함을 13개 RCT 메타분석으로 확인.

## Summary
This systematic review and meta-analysis analyzed 13 randomized clinical trials to compare etch-and-rinse (E&R) vs self-etch (SE) application modes of universal adhesives. E&R mode demonstrated significantly better retention (OR 0.35, p=0.003), marginal adaptation (OR 0.49, p<0.001), and marginal staining (OR 0.49, p<0.001). No significant differences existed in secondary caries or postoperative sensitivity between modes. Literature searched from January 2000 to March 2020 across PubMed, Cochrane Library, and Embase.

## Key Contributions
- Confirms clinical superiority of E&R mode for retention, marginal adaptation, and marginal staining
- No significant difference in secondary caries or postoperative sensitivity between E&R and SE
- Synthesizes in vitro literature with clinical evidence, bridging lab findings to patient outcomes
- Provides clinician guidance: E&R mode is preferred when optimal retention and marginal quality are priorities

## Methodology
- **Design**: SR + meta-analysis (PRISMA)
- **Search**: PubMed, Cochrane Library, Embase; January 2000 – March 2020; initial 2,516 records
- **Included studies**: 13 RCTs evaluating universal adhesive clinical performance
- **Analysis tool**: RevMan 5.3.5
- **Outcomes**: Retention rate, marginal adaptation, marginal staining, secondary caries, postoperative sensitivity
- **Statistics**: Odds ratio (OR) with 95% CI

## Results
| Outcome | Favors | OR | 95% CI | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Retention | E&R | 0.35 | 0.18–0.71 | 0.003 |
| Marginal adaptation | E&R | 0.49 | 0.36–0.67 | <0.001 |
| Marginal staining | E&R | 0.49 | 0.36–0.66 | <0.001 |
| Secondary caries | — | NS | — | — |
| Postoperative sensitivity | — | NS | — | — |

Both groups showed very low incidence of secondary caries and postoperative sensitivity.
