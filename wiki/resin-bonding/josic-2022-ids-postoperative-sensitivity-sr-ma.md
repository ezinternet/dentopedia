## One-line Summary

JERD 2022 SR+MA (4 clinical trials qualitative, 2 quantitative): immediate dentin sealing (IDS) vs delayed dentin sealing (DDS) showed no significant difference in postoperative sensitivity (POS) at baseline or 2 years (P>.05; GRADE very-low to low), concluding no clinical evidence favors IDS over DDS for POS — directly conflicting with the Magne narrative and the Alghauli 2025 SR+MA.

## Summary

Breschi lab's rigorously conducted SR+MA challenges the Magne-era narrative that IDS reduces postoperative sensitivity. After PRISMA + PICOS screening, only 4 clinical trials met inclusion; only 2 were poolable. RoB-2 rated 2 of these as "some concerns" for POS outcome. GRADE certainty was very-low at baseline and low at 2 years.

The authors are careful to separate (a) in-vitro literature showing higher bond strengths with IDS (which they accept) from (b) clinical POS reduction (which they reject as unsupported). Their clinical-significance statement is direct: "no clinical evidence to favor IDS over DDS" — at least for POS.

The conflict with [[resin-bonding/alghauli-2025-clinical-benefits-immediate-dentin-sealing]] (P<.05 in favor of IDS) is real and unresolved. Alghauli pooled 11 studies through Dec 2023 — Josic pooled 2 through ~2021. The expanded evidence base may explain the divergence; alternatively, outcome metrics and study quality weighting may differ. Both reviews are valid; clinicians should not cite POS reduction as a settled IDS benefit.

## Key Contributions

1. First rigorous GRADE-evaluated SR+MA of IDS POS outcome.
2. Surfaced the gap between in-vitro IDS claims and clinical evidence quality.
3. Anchored the conservative reading of IDS clinical literature pre-2023.

## Methodology

- PRISMA + PICOS framework.
- Inclusion: adult patients, ≥1 indirect restoration each with IDS vs DDS.
- Primary outcome: POS. Secondary: survival, retention, marginal adaptation/discoloration, surface roughness.
- 4 studies qualitative synthesis; 2 studies quantitative meta-analysis.
- Tools: RoB-2, GRADE.

## Results

- POS at baseline: IDS vs DDS, P>.05, GRADE very-low.
- POS at 2-year follow-up: IDS vs DDS, P>.05, GRADE low.
- RoB-2: 2 studies "some concerns" for POS.
- Quantitative pooling under-powered (2 studies).
