## One-line Summary

JPD 2025 SR+MA (11 clinical studies): immediate dentin sealing (IDS) for indirect restorations reduced complications versus non-IDS, with survival 96.4–100% vs 81.8–96.7% and significantly lower postoperative sensitivity (POS) intensity and incidence (P<.05) — though this POS benefit directly conflicts with the Josic 2022 SR+MA no-difference conclusion.

## Summary

This SR+MA shifts the IDS evidence base from in-vitro µTBS (Hardan 2022, Magne 2005) to clinical outcomes. Eleven clinical trials show consistent benefit of IDS on complication, survival, and postoperative sensitivity. Survival gap narrows with longer follow-up (negative correlation with observation time), suggesting that early differences may attenuate over years.

The IDS-favorable POS result directly contradicts Josic 2022 SR+MA (4 studies, GRADE low: no difference). The two reviews likely differ in inclusion period (through 2021 vs through Dec 2023), study count (4 vs 11), and outcome definition. Clinical decisions should weigh: more recent and larger evidence base (Alghauli) vs more rigorous GRADE assessment (Josic).

## Key Contributions

1. First SR+MA focused specifically on clinical (not in-vitro) outcomes of IDS for indirect restorations.
2. Quantifies survival gap: IDS 96.4–100% vs non-IDS 81.8–96.7%, with narrowing over time.
3. Statistically confirms POS reduction (P<.05) — directly opposing Josic 2022 conclusion.

## Methodology

- PRISMA-style SR; search through December 2023, no language/time limits.
- Inclusion: clinical trials comparing IDS-protocol indirect restorations vs non-IDS (DDS/conventional).
- Meta-analysis: Review Manager 5.4, α=.05.
- 11 studies included.

## Results

- Complication rate: IDS < non-IDS (qualitative direction; quantitative pooling not extracted from accessible text).
- Survival: IDS 96.4–100% vs non-IDS 81.8–96.7%; negatively correlated with observation time.
- POS intensity and incidence: IDS < non-IDS·conventional (P<.05).
- Authors recommend long-term RCTs to confirm.
