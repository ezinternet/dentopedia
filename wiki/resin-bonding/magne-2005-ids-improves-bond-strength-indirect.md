## One-line Summary

JPD 2005 in-vitro µTBS study (15 human molars, OptiBond FL 3-step E&R): immediate dentin sealing (IDS) reached 58.25 MPa, statistically equivalent to direct control (55.06 MPa, P>.05) and ~5× higher than delayed dentin sealing (DDS) at 11.58 MPa (P=.0081); SEM showed well-organized 3–5 µm hybrid layers for IDS/control vs marked disruption for DDS.

## 한줄요약

JPD 94(6), 2005 in-vitro µTBS (15 human molars, OptiBond FL 3-step E&R): IDS 58.25 MPa ≈ direct control 55.06 MPa (P>.05) >> DDS 11.58 MPa (P=.0081). SEM에서 IDS·C는 잘 조직된 hybrid layer 3–5 µm; DDS는 marked disruption.

## Summary

Magne lab's foundational in-vitro evidence that IDS recovers the dentin bonding quality of fresh dentin. With OptiBond FL (3-step etch-and-rinse, filled adhesive), IDS specimens reached µTBS statistically equivalent to direct immediate bonding and 5x higher than DDS. SEM shows DDS hybrid layers are markedly disrupted at the resin interface — likely from provisional contamination and water/temperature insults during the provisional phase.

This 5x bond strength gap is the headline number cited across the IDS literature. Limitations: small n (15 molars, 5/group), one DBA only, 24h storage (no aging), in-vitro only.

## Key Contributions

1. First Magne lab µTBS proof of IDS bond strength advantage vs DDS.
2. Demonstrated IDS = direct immediate bonding equivalence (substrate quality recovery).
3. SEM visualization of hybrid layer integrity differences.
4. Closed the film-thickness concern (IDS sealant does not impede seating).

## Methodology

15 freshly extracted human molars, 3 groups × 5 teeth:
- C (control): direct immediate bonding.
- DDS: indirect approach, sealing at cementation.
- IDS: indirect approach, sealing immediately after preparation.

OptiBond FL (3-step E&R, Kerr). µTBS: 11 beams (0.9×0.9×11 mm) per tooth, 24h water storage. Stats: Kruskal-Wallis + Mann-Whitney U (α=.05). SEM analysis of failure modes.

## Results

| Group | µTBS (MPa) | SEM hybrid layer | Failure mode |
|---|---|---|---|
| C (direct) | 55.06 | 3–5 µm, well-organized | mixed cohesive/interfacial |
| IDS | 58.25 | 3–5 µm, well-organized | mixed cohesive/interfacial |
| DDS | 11.58 | marked disruption | interfacial, top of hybrid layer |

- C vs IDS: not significantly different (P>.05).
- DDS vs others: P=.0081.
