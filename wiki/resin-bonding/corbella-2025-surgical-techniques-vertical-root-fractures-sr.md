## One-line Summary

Systematic review (8 articles / 6 case series, ~64 teeth) of surgical management of confirmed vertical root fractures (VRF): 4 of 6 studies bonded fracture fragments with 4-META/MMA-TBB resin (Super-Bond), with highly heterogeneous survival (e.g. intentional-replantation 6/6 to resin-ionomer 0/5); evidence is sparse, low-quality (all case series), and insufficient to recommend any technique as standard.

## 한줄요약

확진된 수직치근파절(VRF) 외과적 관리 체계적 문헌고찰(논문 8편/연구 6건, 전부 case series): 6개 연구 중 4개가 4-META/MMA-TBB 레진(Super-Bond)으로 파절 단편을 접착했고, 의도적 재식립 6/6 성공부터 resin-ionomer 0/5 실패까지 생존율이 매우 이질적이며, 근거가 빈약·저질이라 표준 술식 권고 불가.

## Summary

Vertical root fractures (VRFs) — axial fractures through cementum, dentin and the root canal system — are usually treated as an absolute indication for extraction, especially in single-rooted teeth ("hopeless"). This is the first systematic review of **surgical** techniques that attempt to preserve such roots. Corbella et al. screened MEDLINE/EMBASE/Cochrane Central plus grey literature and journal hand-search (last search Jan 2024, PROSPERO CRD42024524356), including only case series with ≥5 patients (case reports excluded). They retrieved **8 articles representing 6 studies**, all observational case series.

The reviewed approaches fall into two families: **(1) extraction + extraoral bonding of the fragments with 4-META/MMA-TBB resin (Super-Bond) followed by (intentional) replantation**, and **(2) in-situ repair via flap elevation, sealing the fracture with a bioceramic (MTA) while the tooth stays attached**. Four of the six studies used 4-META/MMA-TBB resin (one of them combined 4-META with silver glass-ionomer). Survival was strongly heterogeneous — intentional-replantation series (Okaguchi 6/6, Sugaya 9/12) looked favourable, whereas resin-ionomer (Anderegg 0/5) and silver-glass-ionomer (Selden) repairs failed. Because all evidence is low-quality case series with high heterogeneity, no meta-analysis was possible and **no technique can be recommended as a standard approach**; the authors call for randomised comparative trials.

## Key Contributions

- **First SR of surgical VRF management** — fills a documented gap (no prior SR/MA).
- **Super-Bond is the dominant repair material in this literature**: 4 of 6 studies bonded fragments with **4-META/MMA-TBB resin**; the most favourable signals all come from extraoral 4-META bonding + replantation.
- **Resin-ionomer / glass-ionomer cements are NOT supported** for VRF repair (Anderegg all-failed; Selden silver-GIC all-failed long-term).
- **Evidence-quality caveat front and centre**: all case series, small n, high heterogeneity, JBI weaknesses (demographics, symptom reporting), no quantitative synthesis.

## Methodology

- **Design**: PRISMA-compliant systematic review, qualitative synthesis (meta-analysis not feasible). PROSPERO CRD42024524356.
- **Inclusion**: case series with **≥5 treated patients**; case reports and series < 5 patients **excluded** (a key reason much of the VRF-repair literature is left out).
- **Sources**: MEDLINE (OVID), EMBASE, Cochrane Central; Greylit/OpenGrey; trial registers; manual journal search (since 2000); no language restriction; last search 5 Jan 2024.
- **Selection**: dual independent screening (ST, SC), third reviewer (ER) for disputes, Cohen's kappa.
- **Quality**: JBI Critical Appraisal Tool for case series.

## Results

**Included**: 8 articles = 6 studies (Japan ×2, USA ×2, Italy ×1, Taiwan ×1); all case series.

**Material split**: 4-META/MMA-TBB resin in **4 studies**, MTA in 1, resin-ionomer cement in 1. Four studies repaired the VRF **without extraction**.

**4-META/MMA-TBB (Super-Bond) studies — verbatim:**

| Study | Technique | n teeth | Outcome | Follow-up |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Hayashi 2002/2004 | 4-META/MMA-TBB, extraoral (extract+replant) & intraoral | 26 | **8 failed**; 6 success, 12 functional w/ periapical lesion; worse for posterior teeth & fractures > 2/3 from cervical | mean 34.9 mo |
| Sugaya 2001 | 4-META/MMA-TBB extraoral bonding + replant (group B) | 12 | **9/12 good prognosis**; 3 extracted. PD 7.4→4.6 mm; bleeding 91%→8.3%; bone level 18.8%→29.2%; no ankylosis/resorption | > 22 mo |
| Okaguchi 2019 | 4-META/MMA-TBB **intentional replantation** + fragment bonding | 6 | **6/6 successful** (4 complete + 1 near-complete radiographic healing, 1 not assessed) | 33–74 mo (~50 ± 15) |
| Selden 1996 | **4-META** + silver glass-ionomer + e-PTFE GBR, in-situ | 6 | **5 failed 2–11 mo; 1 split at 12 mo** → all failed | 2–12 mo |

**Non-Super-Bond:**
- **Taschieri 2009/2010** (MTA seal + calcium sulfate, incomplete maxillary anterior VRF): **5/7 successful at 33 mo**, 2 extracted; excluded teeth with PD > 4 mm.
- **Anderegg 2018** (resin-ionomer): **0/5 — all failed within 6 months**; authors warn against resin-ionomer for VRF.

**Synthesis**: extraoral 4-META/MMA-TBB bonding + (intentional) replantation gives the best case-series signals, but heterogeneity and low evidence quality prevent any standard recommendation. Repair is feasible only for **incomplete** VRF, correctly diagnosed (CBCT insufficient; deep, narrow, isolated single-sided probing defect is the clinical clue). Presumed failure mode = progression to complete fracture / split tooth. Adverse events were close to nil. **Conclusion: insufficient evidence; RCTs needed.**
