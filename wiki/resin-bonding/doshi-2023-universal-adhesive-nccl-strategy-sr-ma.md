## 한줄요약
비우식성 치경부 병소(NCCL)에서 유니버설 접착제의 전부산부식(Total-Etch)과 자가산부식(Self-Etch) 전략 간 유지율·변연적합도·변연착색 등 5가지 임상 지표 모두에서 유의한 차이 없음을 17개 RCT 메타분석(I²=0%)으로 확인.

## Summary
This updated SR+MA included 17 RCTs (13 in meta-analysis) comparing total-etch and self-etch strategies for universal adhesives in NCCLs. Five clinical outcomes (retention, marginal adaptation, marginal discoloration, secondary caries, postoperative sensitivity) were assessed at 18, 24, and 36 months using both USPHS and FDI criteria. No statistically significant differences were found for any outcome at any time point (p > 0.05, I² = 0%). A strongly suspected publication bias was noted in the retention domain at 18-month follow-up under FDI criteria.

## Key Contributions
- No significant difference between total-etch and self-etch strategies across all five NCCL outcomes
- I² = 0% indicating high consistency across studies — reliable estimate
- Suggests clinicians can choose either strategy for NCCLs based on preference or substrate characteristics
- Caution: publication bias suspected at 18-month FDI retention data

## Methodology
- **Design**: SR + meta-analysis (PRISMA)
- **Search**: PubMed, Scopus, Cochrane, Web of Science, Open Gray, Clinical Registries
- **Included studies**: 17 SR / 13 meta-analysis
- **Follow-up**: 18, 24, 36 months
- **Outcomes**: Retention, marginal adaptation, marginal discoloration, secondary caries, postoperative sensitivity
- **Criteria**: USPHS and FDI criteria analyzed separately
- **Heterogeneity**: I² = 0% across all comparisons

## Results
- No statistically significant difference between TE and SE for any of five outcomes at any time point
- Both strategies show acceptable clinical performance in NCCLs
- Strongly suspected publication bias in retention domain at 18-month FDI follow-up
- Universal adhesives perform well regardless of etching strategy in the NCCL context
