## Summary

Leyva del Rio et al. (2021) investigated whether heating HF acid to 70°C prior to ceramic etching improves surface roughness and microtensile bond strength (μTBS) compared to room-temperature HF, across two clinically relevant ceramic types. A 9% HF acid was used with ceramic-specific etching times (60 s for feldspathic, 20 s for lithium disilicate). All etched groups were silanized and bonded with dual-cure composite cement. Heated HF significantly increased surface roughness in both ceramics, but the bond strength benefit was ceramic-type specific: lithium disilicate (L70) achieved 29.11 ± 8.26 MPa — significantly superior to all other groups — while feldspathic ceramic showed no significant difference between room-temperature and heated HF (FC: 19.94 vs. F70: 18.24 MPa). The differential response is attributed to the distinct microstructures: lithium disilicate has crystalline phases whose dissolution pattern is more dramatically enhanced by heat.

**Clinical takeaway**: Heated HF (70°C) offers a meaningful bond strength advantage for lithium disilicate glass-ceramics, but provides no added benefit over room-temperature HF for feldspathic ceramics.

## Key Contributions

- First in-class comparison of heated vs. room-temperature HF on two major ceramic types simultaneously
- Heated HF increases surface roughness (Ra, Rq) significantly in both ceramics
- Bond strength advantage of heated HF is material-specific: exclusive to lithium disilicate
- L70 achieved highest μTBS across all groups (29.11 ± 8.26 MPa)
- Room-temperature HF groups across both ceramics clustered in 17–20 MPa range
- SEM confirmed differential microstructural etching patterns between ceramics and temperature conditions

## Methodology

- **Materials**: Feldspathic ceramic + lithium disilicate glass-ceramic disks
- **Etching agent**: 9% HF acid, room temperature (RT) or heated to 70°C
- **Etching times**: 60 s for feldspathic; 20 s for lithium disilicate
- **Groups**: FP (polished), FC (RT), F70 (heated); LP (polished), LC (RT), L70 (heated)
- **Bond procedure**: Silanization → dual-cure composite cement → 24 h → μTBS specimens
- **Surface roughness**: Contact stylus profilometer (Ra, Rq)
- **SEM**: Surface morphology analysis
- **Statistics**: Welch's ANOVA + Games-Howell post-hoc

## Results

**Surface roughness**: Heated HF > RT HF > polished for both ceramics (all comparisons p < 0.001).

**Microtensile bond strength**:

| Group | Ceramic | μTBS (MPa ± SD) | Significance |
|-------|---------|-----------------|-------------|
| L70 | Lithium disilicate, 70°C HF | 29.11 ± 8.26 | Highest (p < 0.001 vs all) |
| FC | Feldspathic, RT HF | 19.94 ± 4.14 | } No significant |
| F70 | Feldspathic, 70°C HF | 18.24 ± 5.29 | } difference |
| LC | Lithium disilicate, RT HF | 17.87 ± 6.96 | } (p > 0.05) |

- FC, F70, LC: statistically equivalent
- L70 uniquely superior — heated HF unlocks additional bond potential in lithium disilicate crystalline microstructure
