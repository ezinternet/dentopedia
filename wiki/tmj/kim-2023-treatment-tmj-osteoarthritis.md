## One-line Summary
Korean narrative clinical review (2023) on TMJ osteoarthritis (TMJ OA) treatment: a conservative-first ladder — self-care → NSAIDs (celecoxib if GI risk) → hard full-arch occlusal stabilization splint (upper/lower equivalent) → arthrocentesis with hyaluronic acid (HA) + steroid → surgery as last resort — with hands-on arthrocentesis technique detail; disease-modifying agents remain experimental.

## Summary
TMJ osteoarthritis (TMJ OA) treatment aims at pain reduction, halting condylar bone destruction, and restoring function. This Korean clinician special-feature review lays out a conservative-first ladder and gives hands-on arthrocentesis technique. Disease-modifying (cytokine-based) agents remain experimental.

## Key Contributions
- Conservative-first treatment ladder framed for the Korean general dentist
- Practical arthrocentesis technique: entry point, anesthesia trade-offs, two-needle lavage
- Drug selection logic (NSAID first-line, celecoxib for GI risk, muscle relaxant for myalgia)

## Methodology
- Narrative review (no systematic search, no meta-analysis).

## Results
- **Self-care first**: reassurance + parafunction avoidance is the initial intervention.
- **NSAIDs** (ibuprofen/naproxen/aceclofenac): first-line; relief ~1 week; suppress IL-6/TNF-α. Celecoxib if GI disturbance history. Muscle relaxant when masticatory myalgia present.
- **Occlusal splint**: hard > soft; full-arch with accurate opposing contacts; upper vs lower efficacy equivalent (upper = stable for severe bruxism/masseter hypertrophy; lower = esthetic/comfortable for long daytime wear); reported to induce favorable condylar remodeling.
- **Arthrocentesis**: intra-articular infiltration or auriculotemporal nerve block — block can co-anesthetize the facial nerve temporal branch (eyelid won't close) → deep insertion + pre-inform patient. Entry ~10 mm anterior, 2 mm inferior to tragus (anatomy-dependent). Two-needle bidirectional lavage; then HA (lubrication) + steroid (anti-inflammatory/analgesic).
- **Surgery**: last resort for severe, intractable cases; incomplete recovery and uncertain prognosis.
- Disease-modifying OA drugs: not yet clinically applicable.

## Limitations
- Narrative review — no graded evidence/effect sizes.
- Korean-clinic device conventions may not generalize.
