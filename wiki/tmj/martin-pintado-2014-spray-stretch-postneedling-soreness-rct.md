> [!note] Extra-masticatory (transferable) evidence
> Conducted on a **latent trigger point in the upper trapezius of healthy volunteers** — not symptomatic masticatory pain. Filed under `tmj` to keep the spray-and-stretch cluster together; read it as technique-level evidence with limited direct transfer to jaw muscles.

## One-line Summary

Single-blind RCT (n=70 healthy, latent upper-trapezius MTrP) — spray-and-stretch after dry needling reduced postneedling soreness but only short-term (<6 h); no effect on pressure pain threshold; psychological distress correlated with postneedling pain.

## 한줄요약

단일맹검 RCT (n=70 건강인, 상부 승모근 잠복 트리거포인트) — 건침 후 분사신장이 건침통증을 줄였으나 효과는 단시간(<6시간)에 그쳤고 압통역치는 변화 없음; 심리적 디스트레스가 건침후 통증과 상관.

## Summary

This RCT documents a **different clinical role** for spray-and-stretch than the others in the cluster: not as a primary trigger-point treatment, but as an **adjunct to blunt the soreness that follows dry needling**. Seventy healthy volunteers with a latent upper-trapezius trigger point all received dry needling, then were randomized to spray-and-stretch (ethyl chloride) vs no intervention.

The honest finding is bounded: spray-and-stretch significantly reduced postneedling soreness **only immediately after application**, with the between-group difference gone by 6 hours, and it did **not** change the pressure pain threshold. Psychological distress (somatization, anxiety, interpersonal sensitivity, hostility) correlated with how much postneedling pain patients reported. For the synthesis, this is the page that keeps the enthusiasm honest — the vapocoolant effect is real but **short-lived and symptom-specific**, consistent with a transient neurophysiologic (cutaneous-cooling/reflex) mechanism rather than durable tissue change.

## Key Contributions

- Establishes spray-and-stretch as a **short-term adjunct** for postneedling soreness.
- Delimits the **time course** (<6 h) and shows **no PPT change** — a ceiling on what the technique does.
- Connects **psychological distress** to postneedling pain perception.

## Methodology

- **Design**: single-blind RCT, 72-h follow-up; intervention vs no-intervention control.
- **Sample**: 70 healthy volunteers (mean 21±4 y), latent MTrP in one upper trapezius.
- **Procedure**: all received dry needling → spray-and-stretch vs control.
- **Outcomes**: VAS soreness (to 72 h), pressure pain threshold (to 48 h), SCL-90-R distress.

## Results

| Outcome | Finding | Stat |
|---|---|---|
| Postneedling soreness (group×time) | Significant interaction | F=3.19; P<.05; ηp²=.04 |
| Between-group, immediately post | Spray < control | P=.002 |
| Between-group, after 6 h | No difference | P>.05 |
| Pressure pain threshold | No significant effect | P=.131 |
| Distress ↔ postneedling pain | Correlated | P<.05 |

Conclusion: short-term (<6 h) soreness reduction; PPT unchanged; psychology matters.
