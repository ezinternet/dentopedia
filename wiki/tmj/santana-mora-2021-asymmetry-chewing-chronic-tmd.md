## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional double-blind study (n=24, chronic unilateral TMD, untreated normal occlusion): the habitual chewing side coincided with the TMD-painful side in 15/17 (Kappa=0.767), and the affected side had a steeper condylar path (50.5° vs 45.5°, P=0.002) and flatter lateral guidance (38.0° vs 47.5°, P<0.001).

## Summary

In patients with chronic, substantial, **unilateral** TMD pain and **clinically normal occlusion that had never undergone occlusal therapy**, the painful side displays a consistent three-part dimorphism: it is the **habitual chewing side**, it has a **steeper condylar path (CP)** angle (TMJ remodeling), and it has a **flatter lateral guidance (LG)** angle (dental-incline remodeling). By restricting to untreated, naturally-matured occlusion, the study removes occlusal-therapy confounding — a long-standing reason the occlusion–TMD link has been controversial — and isolates these peripheral biodynamic factors as candidate etiological contributors. The authors frame a self-reinforcing loop: flatter LG on one side promotes preferential chewing there, driving TMJ overload and ipsilateral remodeling/symptoms. Importantly, neither CP nor LG recording is diagnostically sensitive or specific for TMD on its own; they are proposed as dynamic descriptors, not screening tests.

## Key Contributions

- Confirms, in an untreated-occlusion sample, that the **habitual chewing side = TMD-painful side** (Kappa=0.767, risk estimate 4.5).
- Quantifies sided remodeling: **CP steeper** on the affected side, **LG flatter** on the affected side (flatter in 100% of participants).
- Introduces **asymmetry indexes (AiCP, AiLG)** and a combined equation that discriminate the affected side and predict the habitual chewing side.
- Removes the occlusal-therapy confounder that has muddied prior occlusion–TMD studies, strengthening the case for peripheral biodynamic etiology of chronic unilateral TMD.

## Methodology

- **Design**: cross-sectional, double-blind diagnostic study (DC/TMD, Schiffman 2014); ethics CAEI 2009/017; data collected Aug 2014 – Oct 2018.
- **Sample**: 924 screened → 77 eligible → exclude 26 bilateral + 27 prior orthodontic/prosthetic → **n=24** (17 F / 7 M; mean age 37.3 ± 11.7 y). Inclusion: chronic (>6 mo) unilateral pain VAS 4–9/10, Angle Class I normal stable occlusion, complete dentition, no irreversible occlusal therapy.
- **Chewing side**: gum-chewing observation + kinesiography + interview (agreement = consistent side).
- **CP**: axiographic Gysi method, Gerber kinematic face-bow, ≥3 tracings/TMJ, two assessors (ICC=0.87).
- **LG**: K7 kinesiograph (Myotronics), 3 tracings, ImageJ, two clinicians (ICC=0.94).
- **Imaging**: MRI (two radiologists) for disc/condyle pathology.
- **Statistics**: paired/unpaired two-sided t-tests; Fisher exact, χ², Kappa for chewing/pain side; α=0.05.

## Results

| Outcome | Affected side | Non-affected side | P |
|---|---|---|---|
| Habitual chewing = pain side | 15 of 17 | — | Kappa 0.767; Fisher 0.002 |
| Condylar path (CP) angle | 50.52° (9.98°) | 45.50° (7.98°) | 0.002 |
| Lateral guidance (LG) angle | 38.04° (8.15°) | 47.48° (13.10°) | <0.001 |

- Risk estimate for chewing-on-affected-side = **4.5** (95% CI 1.326–15.277).
- Steeper CP on affected side in **17/22 (77%)** (binomial P=0.017).
- LG flatter on affected side in **all** participants.
- Right-vs-left of jaw (control comparison) NS for both CP (P=0.974) and LG (P=0.131) — the asymmetry tracks the *affected* side, not a fixed left/right bias.
- Asymmetry indexes (AiCP, AiLG) and combined equation discriminate affected side (all P≤0.002); no between-sex differences.
- Caveat: neither CP nor LG recording has sensitivity/specificity to detect TMD individually; cross-sectional design precludes causal direction.
