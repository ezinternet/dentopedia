## One-line Summary
SR+MA (20 RCTs qualitative, 6 pooled) showing botulinum toxin type A (BTX-A) injection to masticatory muscles is significantly superior to placebo for myofascial pain in painful temporomandibular disorder (TMD), but provides no significant benefit for maximum mouth opening (MMO) — indicating it acts on muscle-origin pain rather than joint mechanics.

## 한줄요약
20개 RCT SR+MA(6개 메타분석): 보툴리눔독소(BTX-A)는 근막통(Myofascial Pain)에서 위약 대비 유의한 통증 감소를 보이나, 하악 최대개구량(MMO) 개선은 없음.

## Summary
This systematic review and meta-analysis evaluated 20 RCTs on 보툴리눔독소 (Botulinum Toxin, BTX-A) for painful 악관절증 (TMD). Qualitative synthesis included all 20 studies; 6 provided poolable data for meta-analysis. BTX-A injection to masticatory muscles (masseter, temporalis) was statistically superior to placebo for myofascial pain reduction. However, no significant benefit was found for 하악 최대개구량 (Maximum Mouth Opening, MMO) compared to placebo, indicating BTX-A acts primarily on muscle-origin pain rather than joint mechanics. This was the largest synthesis of BTX evidence for TMD at the time of publication.

## Key Contributions
- Largest BTX-A TMD SR+MA at time of publication (20 RCTs)
- Demonstrates BTX-A superiority over placebo for myofascial pain — significant pooled effect
- Shows no MMO benefit — BTX-A does not improve jaw opening function
- Clarifies that BTX-A effect is on muscle activity/pain, not disc-condyle mechanics
- Highlights dose and injection protocol heterogeneity as critical research gap

## Methodology
- Databases: PubMed, Embase, Cochrane
- Inclusion: RCTs comparing BTX-A to placebo/saline for painful TMD
- 20 RCTs qualitative; 6 poolable for meta-analysis
- Primary: pain (VAS), MMO (mm)
- Subgroup: myofascial vs. arthrogenous TMD
- Random-effects model; Cochrane RoB

## Results
- **Pain**: BTX-A significantly > placebo for myofascial pain (pooled SMD p<0.05)
- **MMO**: No significant difference from placebo (pooled MD not significant)
- Myofascial subtype most consistently benefited
- Arthrogenous/disc displacement: insufficient evidence
- Adverse effects: mild, transient (injection site pain, temporary muscle weakness)
- Duration of effect: 3–6 months requiring re-injection
