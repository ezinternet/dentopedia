## 한줄요약
SR+MA: 악관절증(TMD) 전반적 유병률 약 31%; 통증성 TMD 약 11–13%; 여성이 남성보다 1.5–2배 높은 유병률; 20–45세 호발.

## Summary
This systematic review and meta-analysis published in Clinical Oral Investigations (2021) provides the largest epidemiological synthesis of 악관절증 (Temporomandibular Disorder, TMD) prevalence globally. Pooled data showed approximately 31% prevalence for any TMD sign or symptom in the general population. Painful TMD (myofascial pain and/or arthralgia) affected approximately 11–13%. Disc displacement prevalence was approximately 30%, though many cases are asymptomatic. Women showed 1.5–2× higher rates of painful TMD versus men. Peak prevalence was in the 20–45 year age group. High heterogeneity (I²>80%) across included studies reflects variation in diagnostic criteria and sampling methods.

## Key Contributions
- Largest prevalence SR+MA for TMD at time of publication
- Distinguishes painful TMD (clinically important) from disc displacement (often asymptomatic)
- Quantifies sex disparity: female predominance in painful TMD
- Establishes age distribution: predominantly 20–45 years
- Provides epidemiological foundation for TMD treatment research and resource planning

## Methodology
- Databases: PubMed, Embase, Cochrane, Lilacs
- Inclusion: population-based cross-sectional/cohort studies; standardized TMD diagnosis (RDC/TMD or DC/TMD preferred)
- Primary: prevalence of any TMD sign/symptom
- Secondary: subtype-specific prevalence, sex and age distribution
- Random-effects meta-analysis; inverse-variance weighting
- Meta-regression for moderators (age, sex, diagnostic criteria)

## Results
- **Any TMD sign/symptom**: ~31% pooled prevalence
- **Painful TMD** (myofascial + arthralgia): ~11–13%
- **Disc displacement** (with and without reduction): ~30%
- **Sex**: Women 1.5–2× more likely to have painful TMD
- **Age**: Peak 20–45 years; lower in children and elderly
- High heterogeneity (I²>80%) across all analyses
