## One-line Summary

First SR+MA (47 RCTs, n=3238) comparing patient education + self-management (ED/SM) to other non-surgical treatments for adult TMD: other interventions may modestly beat ED/SM-alone on short-term pain (SMD 0.67) and HRQoL (SMD 0.61), but no consistent superiority/inferiority overall and combining ED/SM with other tx rarely adds benefit — all very low to low certainty (0/47 RCTs at low risk of bias).

## Summary

This is the first systematic review with meta-analysis to quantitatively pit education + self-management (ED/SM) against other non-surgical treatments for adult temporomandibular disorders (TMD). Across 47 RCTs (n=3238, 77% female, mean age 34.4 y), ED/SM was usually delivered as a generic, standardized program — most often as the *control* arm. The headline: there is **no consistent evidence that ED/SM is either superior or inferior** to splints, manual therapy, electrotherapy, supervised/unsupervised exercise, or multimodal care. Two specific signals favour the comparator over ED/SM-alone — short-term pain (SMD 0.67) and short-term HRQoL (SMD 0.61) — but on **very low** certainty. Adding other interventions to ED/SM improved short-term HRQoL only (SMD 0.50), with no benefit for pain or function at any time point. Crucially, **0 of 47 trials were at low risk of bias**, and ED/SM arms were rarely individualized (6%) or grounded in best-practice frameworks, which the authors argue likely biased the evidence against showing ED/SM benefit. The clinical takeaway: ED/SM remains a reasonable low-cost first-line option offering broadly comparable effects, but the evidence base is too weak to rank it against active treatments.

## Key Contributions

- First quantitative head-to-head SR+MA of ED/SM vs other non-surgical TMD treatments (prior SRs were narrative or pooled ED/SM only against placebo/no-treatment).
- Coded ED/SM content against Durham (Delphi consensus) and van der Meer frameworks, documenting that most "control" ED/SM arms are under-specified and non-individualized (only 6% tailored).
- GRADE certainty rated uniformly very low → low across pain, function, HRQoL at short/medium/long term.

## Methodology

- 6 databases (MEDLINE, PubMed, Embase, Cochrane Central, CINAHL, PsycINFO) to March 2025; PRISMA; PROSPERO CRD42024529862; Covidence RCT classifier pre-screen; dual-reviewer.
- Inclusion: adults with RDC/TMD or DC/TMD diagnosis; ED/SM (education, jaw/joint guidance, posture, lifestyle/psychosocial, unsupervised exercise), alone or combined; pain/function/HRQoL outcome; RCT in English/French. Excluded psychologist-delivered CBT and dysphagia/tinnitus/migraine populations.
- Cochrane RoB 1 (7 domains). Random-effects MA (RevMan 5.4), SMD (negative favours ED/SM). Strata: short 1–6 wk, medium 7–26 wk, long 27–52 wk. SMD: <0.2 trivial, 0.2–0.49 small, 0.5–0.79 moderate, ≥0.8 large.

## Results

**Cohort**: 17,859 records → 47 RCTs (n=3238; 76.8% female; mean age 34.4±7.3 y; samples 19–186; 62% university clinics; 19 myofascial-only, 22 mixed, 5 disc displacement, 15 persistent ≥3 mo). Providers: dentists/hygienists (n=21), physiotherapists (n=12). Delivery: 91% brief 1:1 sessions, 57% leaflets, only 6% individualized.

**ED/SM vs other non-surgical interventions** (15 RCTs; 8 pooled):

| Outcome (follow-up) | SMD (95% CI) | Studies / pts | Certainty | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Short-term pain | 0.67 (0.13–1.20)* | 6 RCTs / 323 | very low | other tx superior |
| Medium-term pain | 0.43 (−0.25–1.10) | 4 / 144 | very low | NS |
| Medium-term function | 0.36 (−0.46–1.18) | 2 / 132 | very low | NS |
| Short-term HRQoL | 0.61 (0.20–1.01) | 2 / 124 | very low | other tx superior |

\* Pooled MA figure SMD 0.79 (0.27–1.32, I²=78%, 7 studies, 359 pts); GRADE summary 0.67/323/6 RCTs.

**ED/SM + another tx vs ED/SM alone** (34 high-RoB RCTs; 19 pooled): NS for all outcomes/follow-ups **except short-term HRQoL** (SMD 0.50, 0.18–0.82, I²=0%, 3 studies, 154 pts — combination superior). Short-term pain SMD 0.25 (−0.06–0.56, 15 RCTs, 816 pts) NS; medium-term pain 0.48 (−0.29–1.26) NS; long-term pain −0.10 (−0.44–0.25, low certainty) NS; short-term function 0.41 (−0.03–0.85) NS; long-term function 0.15 (−0.27–0.57, low) NS.

**ED/SM added to another tx vs that tx alone** (2 RCTs): short-term pain SMD −0.44 (−2.11–1.23, I²=89%, 61 pts) NS.

**Risk of bias**: 0/47 low, 3 unclear, 44 high. GRADE certainty very low for most outcomes; low only for long-term pain and long-term function.

## Clinical Bottom Line

ED/SM is a legitimate, low-cost, broadly deliverable first-line option for adult TMD with effects largely comparable to splints, manual therapy, electrotherapy, exercise, and multimodal care. Where signals exist, an active comparator may give a modest short-term edge on pain and HRQoL — but on very-low-certainty evidence. Layering other interventions onto ED/SM does not reliably help (only short-term HRQoL). The dominant message is methodological: the trials are weak (0/47 low RoB) and used generic, non-individualized ED/SM as controls, so the field cannot yet rank ED/SM against active care — patient-centered, theory-informed trials are needed.
