## One-line Summary
Single-author narrative clinical review (Korean Dental Association Journal, 2012) on TMD diagnosis and treatment: a seven-step bedside exam differentiating masticatory muscle vs intracapsular joint disorders (end-feel, opening-pathway deviation/deflection, loading test, diagnostic anesthesia) and a reversible-first treatment ladder (NSAIDs/physiotherapy/stabilization splint → arthrocentesis/lavage → arthroscopy → open surgery).

## Summary

Single-author narrative clinical review by an OMFS clinician in the 임상가 특집 (clinician's feature) section of the Korean Dental Association Journal, vol. 50 no. 5 (May 2012). Provides a clinically usable scaffold for diagnosing and treating TMD without specialized equipment.

The article is built around an etiologic inequality: **normal mandibular function + events (stimuli) > physiological tolerance → TMD symptoms**. Both sides matter — events (macrotrauma like MVA, microtrauma like bruxism/occlusal interference) and tolerance (gender, genetics, skeletal disharmony, systemic stress). Bruxism is reframed as predominantly CNS-mediated (~70%) and not curable; clinical goal is damage control with appliances and stress modification, not eradication.

The diagnostic core is a seven-step bedside exam designed to differentiate masticatory muscle disorders from intracapsular joint disorders before resorting to imaging:

| Step | Muscle disorder | Joint disorder |
|---|---|---|
| History | No clear trigger; fluctuating symptom cycle | Trauma history; persistent or worsening |
| Mandibular ROM (forced opening end-feel) | Soft end-feel (further opening possible with stretch) | Hard end-feel (opening blocked) |
| Lateral excursion | Generally unrestricted | Contralateral excursion limited or painful |
| Opening pathway (Fig 1) | Deviation possible; lateral excursion clean | Deviation (도중편향) → DDwR; Deflection (종점편향) → DDwoR / adhesion |
| Palpation (Fig 2) | Trigger points in masseter/temporalis/SCM/trapezius/splenius capitis | Joint capsule (1 cm anterior to tragus + posterior condyle palpation with open mouth) |
| Joint loading test (Fig 4, bilateral manipulation) | No pain | Pain on loading → intracapsular |
| Diagnostic anesthetic blockade (Fig 5–6) | Trigger point injection (lidocaine 2% w/o vasoconstrictor) is both diagnostic and therapeutic | Auriculotemporal nerve block or intra-articular injection |

Treatment is organized as **definitive treatment** (modifies cause: orthognathic surgery for asymmetry-driven TMD, posterior prosthetic restoration to restore bilateral mastication, trigger point injection + self-stretching for myofascial pain) vs **supportive therapy** (modifies symptoms only: NSAIDs, soft diet, physical therapy).

Reversible methods are first-line: medication, thermal therapy, habit modification, physical therapy, occlusal appliance therapy, arthrocentesis and lavage. Irreversible methods (arthroscopic lysis, open surgery, occlusal adjustment) are reserved for refractory cases.

## Key Contributions

1. **Pragmatic differential framework**: muscle vs joint TMD differentiation using *end-feel* on forced opening — a single bedside maneuver that re-orients the workup.
2. **Opening pathway as diagnostic shortcut**: deviation (returns to midline) vs deflection (progressive lateral drift) maps onto disc displacement with reduction vs without reduction or adhesion.
3. **Bilateral manipulation loading test** (Fig 4): thumbs on chin pushing down + four fingers under mandibular angle pushing up → pain on loading = intracapsular origin.
4. **Trigger point injection technique**: 2% lidocaine *without* vasoconstrictor (masseter/trapezius ~½ ampule; temporalis ~⅓), with fan-shaped needling to mechanically disrupt the taut band. Self-stretching protocol after injection: press trigger point firmly for ~30 s, then maximal opening + tongue elevation for 15–20 s, 10× repetitions × 2–3 cycles/day.
5. **Occlusal stabilization appliance default**: hard resin, maxillary, full-arch coverage, canine-guided occlusion, adjusted q2–7 days early, then q2–4 weeks. Night-only wear suffices in most; extend to daytime if insufficient.
6. **Arthrocentesis and lavage protocol**: two needles into superior joint space, saline irrigation to release vacuum/closed lock, optional terminal injection of steroid or sodium hyaluronate; landmark = mid-tragocanthal line.

## Methodology

Narrative clinical review — no systematic search, no quantitative synthesis. Anchored in the author's OMFS practice and standard TMD textbooks of the 2000s–early 2010s. Heavy use of clinical figures (7 figures, 2 tables) for hand-positioning and injection technique.

## Results

[claude해석] No primary outcomes — this is a clinical guide. The article positions itself as a practical bridge for dentists who recognize TMD as a major oral disease but lack equipment, prior knowledge, or confidence to manage it in-clinic. Most recommended interventions (NSAIDs, splint, trigger point injection, arthrocentesis) can be performed at outpatient level.

## Classification → Treatment Map

**Masticatory muscle disorders**:
- Protective cocontraction → most self-limited; remove cause (high restoration, intraoral pain); NSAIDs + soft diet as supportive.
- Local muscle soreness / non-inflammatory myalgia → NSAIDs + lidocaine ± steroid injection + light massage + opening stretch; consider night splint if bruxism/clenching.
- Myofascial pain (~50% of muscle-origin pain) → trigger point injection + self-stretching as definitive; occlusal splint and BTX-A for refractory.
- Chronic centrally mediated myalgia → avoid local injection (paradoxical worsening post-anesthetic); restrict mandibular motion within painless range; centrally acting medication; multidisciplinary referral (pain/neurology/psychiatry).

**TMJ joint disorders**:
- Disc displacement (DDwR/DDwoR), retrodiscitis, osteoarthritis → focus is *reducing intra-articular overload* rather than repositioning the disc; the goal is to give the joint surfaces time to remodel and to let retrodiscal tissue avascularly adapt.
- First-line: occlusal stabilization splint.
- Refractory: arthrocentesis and lavage ± intra-articular steroid or sodium hyaluronate.
- Last resort: arthroscopic lysis, open surgery.

## Limitations (Critical Reading)

[claude해석] As a 2012 narrative review by a single author, this paper precedes:
- **DC/TMD (2014)** — Diagnostic Criteria for TMD, the current research-grade case definition.
- **Singh 2024 Cochrane SR (occlusal interventions)** — strong recommendation *against* irreversible occlusal work; the splint protocol in this paper aligns, but the term "occlusal stabilization" should be understood as reversible appliance therapy, not occlusal adjustment.
- **BMJ 2023 chronic TMD pain guideline** — supports conservative first-line; conditional BTX-A in refractory myofascial pain.
- **Zhu 2020 / Saini 2024 BTX-A meta-analyses** — quantify BTX-A effect on myofascial pain (pain reduction yes, MMO improvement minimal).
- **IJOMS 2023 SR+MA on arthrocentesis** — quantifies arthrocentesis MMO improvement at +1.12 mm.

Where this paper is most useful today: the *bedside exam protocol* (end-feel, opening path, loading test, palpation of cervical referred pain sources) and the *trigger point injection technique*. Where it should be cross-checked: any specific treatment recommendation should be re-validated against the post-2014 evidence base (~~(미ingest) tmd-clinical-pathway-2025~~ if/when written).
