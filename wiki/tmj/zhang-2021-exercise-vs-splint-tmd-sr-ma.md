## 한줄요약
SR+MA: 운동치료는 교합안정장치(Occlusal Splint)와 동등한 통증 감소 효과를 보이며, 하악 최대개구량(MMO) 개선에서 일부 우월한 결과를 나타냄.

## Summary
This systematic review and meta-analysis from Shanghai Ninth People's Hospital directly compared exercise therapy to 교합안정장치 (Occlusal Splint) therapy for painful 악관절증 (Temporomandibular Disorder, TMD). Pooled analysis demonstrated that exercise therapy was non-inferior to splint therapy for pain reduction (pooled mean difference not significant), and numerically superior in some studies for 하악 최대개구량 (Maximum Mouth Opening, MMO) improvement. Patient adherence was consistently higher with exercise protocols. The results support exercise therapy as a viable, cost-efficient, non-appliance alternative for painful TMD management.

## Key Contributions
- Provides head-to-head meta-analytic comparison of two common TMD treatments
- Exercise therapy shown to be equivalent to splints for pain; potentially superior for function
- Higher patient adherence with exercise protocols — practical advantage
- Challenges assumption that appliance fabrication is necessary for most painful TMD patients

## Methodology
- Databases: PubMed, Cochrane, EMBASE, Web of Science
- Inclusion: RCTs with direct comparison arms (exercise vs. splint)
- Primary outcomes: pain (VAS/NRS), MMO (mm)
- Random-effects meta-analysis; I² for heterogeneity
- GRADE certainty assessment

## Results
- **Pain reduction**: No significant difference between exercise and splint (pooled MD ≈ 0, p>0.05)
- **MMO improvement**: Favored exercise in several pooled analyses
- **Patient adherence**: Higher with exercise in most included trials
- **Overall certainty**: Moderate (GRADE)
- No serious adverse events with either intervention
- Follow-up predominantly ≤6 months
