## One-line Summary
SR+MA of 7 RCTs (448 patients): arthrocentesis vs conservative management for painful TMD yields a statistically significant but clinically modest maximum mouth opening (MMO) gain of +1.12 mm (95% CI 0.46–1.78, p=0.001), with borderline pain benefit and high heterogeneity.

## Summary
This systematic review and meta-analysis in the International Journal of Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery (2023) pooled 7 RCTs (448 patients) comparing 관절천자 (Arthrocentesis) with conservative management (splints, physiotherapy, medications) for painful 악관절증 (TMD). The key finding was a statistically significant but clinically modest improvement in 하악 최대개구량 (Maximum Mouth Opening, MMO): weighted mean difference +1.12 mm (95% CI 0.46–1.78, p=0.001) favoring arthrocentesis. Pain reduction was statistically borderline with moderate-to-high heterogeneity. The clinical significance of a 1.12 mm MMO difference is debatable, suggesting arthrocentesis offers modest incremental benefit over conservative care.

## Key Contributions
- First SR+MA restricted to RCT designs for arthrocentesis vs. conservative management comparison
- Quantifies MMO benefit at +1.12 mm — statistically significant but clinically modest
- Pain benefit borderline; reinforces conservative-first approach for most patients
- Identifies high procedural heterogeneity as key methodological barrier

## Methodology
- Search: MEDLINE, Embase, Cochrane CENTRAL, Web of Science
- Inclusion: RCTs comparing arthrocentesis (±lavage) vs. conservative management for painful TMD
- 7 eligible RCTs; 448 total patients
- Primary outcomes: MMO (mm), pain (VAS/NRS)
- Random-effects meta-analysis
- Cochrane RoB 2.0; GRADE assessment

## Results
- **MMO**: WMD +1.12 mm (95% CI 0.46–1.78, p=0.001) favoring arthrocentesis
- **Pain**: Statistically significant but clinically borderline; high heterogeneity (I²>50%)
- Most studies: moderate risk of bias
- Arthrocentesis superior to conservative on MMO but clinically modest difference
- No serious adverse events reported with arthrocentesis in included trials
