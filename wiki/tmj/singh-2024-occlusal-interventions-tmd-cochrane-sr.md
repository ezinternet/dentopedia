## One-line Summary
Cochrane systematic review (2024) of occlusal interventions for TMD: very low to low certainty (GRADE) evidence overall, with stabilization splints showing minimal benefit vs no treatment and no advantage over non-occluding splints, and a strong recommendation against irreversible occlusal modifications (grinding, crowns for TMD).

## Summary
This Cochrane systematic review comprehensively evaluates all occlusal interventions for 악관절증 (Temporomandibular Disorder, TMD): 교합안정장치 (Occlusal Splint), occlusal adjustment/equilibration, and irreversible occlusal modifications. The overall certainty of evidence was rated very low to low (GRADE) across most comparisons. Stabilization splints showed minimal evidence of benefit compared to no treatment, and no advantage over placebo/non-occluding splints. Irreversible occlusal modifications (grinding, crowns for TMD) lacked evidence of benefit and carry risk of harm — a strong recommendation against their use was issued. This review serves as the authoritative evidence base underlying TMD clinical guidelines.

## Key Contributions
- Definitive Cochrane synthesis of all occlusal intervention types for TMD
- Explicit strong recommendation against irreversible occlusal modifications for TMD
- Very low to low certainty evidence for splint benefit — reinforces conservative-first principle
- Updates evidence base for the BMJ 2023 TMD guideline

## Methodology
- Cochrane methodology: CENTRAL, MEDLINE, Embase, trial registries
- Inclusion: RCTs and quasi-RCTs; any occlusal intervention; any TMD subtype (DC/TMD preferred)
- Primary outcomes: pain, jaw function, quality of life
- Two independent reviewers; Cochrane RoB tool
- GRADE for all comparisons

## Results
- **Stabilization splints vs. no treatment**: Very low certainty; small pain benefit
- **Stabilization vs. non-occluding splints**: No meaningful difference (very low certainty)
- **Occlusal adjustment**: No benefit; strong recommendation against
- **Irreversible modifications**: Insufficient/no evidence; strong recommendation against
- Adverse events: mild (muscle soreness, tooth discomfort)
- Overall: insufficient high-quality evidence to support any occlusal intervention as definitively effective
