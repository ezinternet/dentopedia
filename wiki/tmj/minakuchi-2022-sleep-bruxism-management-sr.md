## One-line Summary
Systematic review (Okayama University, 2022) of adult sleep bruxism management: occlusal splints protect teeth but do not reduce bruxism episode frequency, biofeedback has the strongest evidence for reducing episodes, botulinum toxin (BTX) lowers muscle activity/pain but is equivocal for episode frequency, and no current treatment permanently eliminates sleep bruxism.

## Summary
This systematic review from Okayama University in the Japanese Dental Science Review (2022) comprehensively evaluates management strategies for 수면이갈이 (Sleep Bruxism) in adults. A critical finding is that 교합안정장치 (Occlusal Splint) — the most commonly prescribed intervention — does not reduce bruxism episode frequency; it protects teeth but does not treat the underlying bruxism. Biofeedback devices showed the most consistent evidence for reducing bruxism episodes. 보툴리눔독소 (Botulinum Toxin, BTX) injection reduced muscle activity and pain but showed equivocal results for bruxism episode frequency. Pharmacological options (clonazepam, clonidine) had limited evidence and notable side effects. No currently available treatment permanently eliminates sleep bruxism.

## Key Contributions
- Clarifies the fundamental limitation of splints for bruxism: tooth protection, not bruxism treatment
- Identifies biofeedback as having the strongest evidence for reducing bruxism episode frequency
- Provides evidence hierarchy: biofeedback > BTX > pharmacotherapy for bruxism reduction
- Synthesizes PSG-validated vs. EMG-only studies — important methodological distinction
- Highlights that no current treatment cures sleep bruxism

## Methodology
- Databases: PubMed, Embase, Cochrane
- Inclusion: RCTs and clinical trials on sleep bruxism management in adults; preferred PSG or EMG-validated diagnosis
- Primary: bruxism episode frequency (PSG-based), EMG amplitude
- Secondary: pain, tooth wear, sleep quality, patient-reported outcomes
- Narrative synthesis with evidence grading
- ICSD-3 and AASM bruxism definitions applied

## Results
- **Occlusal splints** (교합안정장치): Reduces muscle activity and protects teeth; does NOT reduce bruxism episode frequency
- **Biofeedback devices**: Best evidence for reducing bruxism episodes; compliance challenge
- **BTX injection** (보툴리눔독소): Reduces masseter volume/pain; equivocal for bruxism frequency
- **Clonazepam**: Short-term bruxism reduction; limited by dependency, drowsiness
- **Clonidine**: Some evidence; limited by hypotensive side effects
- **CBT/relaxation**: Low evidence; favorable safety
- No treatment permanently eliminates sleep bruxism
