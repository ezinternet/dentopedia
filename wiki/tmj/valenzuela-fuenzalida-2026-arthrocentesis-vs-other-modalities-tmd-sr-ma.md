## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 32 RCTs (n=1247; 640 arthrocentesis vs 607 other modalities) finds arthrocentesis is NOT superior for pain (VAS MD −0.25, 95% CI −1.09–0.59, p=0.55, I²=96%); MMO (SMD −0.67, p=0.005) and MIO (SMD −2.93, p=0.0004) statistically favored comparators; only masticatory efficiency (2 studies) favored arthrocentesis — all low-to-very-low GRADE certainty.

## Summary

This 2026 SR+MA (PRISMA 2020; PROSPERO CRD420251272475) is the broadest direct head-to-head appraisal of TMJ arthrocentesis: **32 RCTs, 1247 patients** (640 arthrocentesis vs 607 comparator), published 1996–2023. Unlike prior reviews that compared arthrocentesis only against conservative care, here the comparator pool spans **conservative therapy** (occlusal splints, physiotherapy, pharmacology), **intra-articular injections** (hyaluronic acid, PRP, i-PRF), **arthroscopy**, and **alternative arthrocentesis protocols**, analyzed by within-trial pairwise comparison (no synthetic pooled comparator arm).

The bottom line: **arthrocentesis does not demonstrate superiority** for pain or most functional outcomes. Pain (VAS) showed no difference; maximum mouth opening (MMO) and maximum incisal opening (MIO) statistically *favored the comparators* (i.e., arthrocentesis gained less); only masticatory efficiency — based on just 2 studies — favored arthrocentesis. Heterogeneity was extreme (I² 85–100% in most pools) and GRADE certainty was low-to-very-low throughout, so all conclusions are cautious. Clinically, the authors frame arthrocentesis as an **adjunctive option within a stepwise strategy** (reasonable in conservative-refractory patients), not a definitive or superior intervention.

## Key Contributions

- Largest direct comparative SR+MA of arthrocentesis vs *all* therapeutic modalities (not conservative care alone).
- Pathology-stratified analysis: disc displacement, osteoarthritis, arthralgia, "other" (JIA, condylar fracture, TMJ dysfunction syndrome).
- Converts the prior "modest MMO benefit vs conservative" message into "no superiority once injections/arthroscopy/alternative protocols are the comparators."

## Methodology

- **Design**: PRISMA 2020 SR+MA; RCTs only (only RCTs met eligibility).
- **Search**: MEDLINE/PubMed, EMBASE, SCOPUS, Web of Science, CINAHL, Google Scholar (first 200); inception–Dec 2025; English/Spanish. 180 → 91 → 58 full-text → **32 included**.
- **Diagnosis**: per primary study; no uniform RDC/TMD or DC/TMD across trials.
- **Bias**: Cochrane RoB (7 domains), overall moderate, κ=0.91; high performance-bias risk (blinding) inherent to intervention.
- **Synthesis**: RevMan 5.4, inverse-variance random-effects; MD/SMD with 95% CI; GRADE certainty. Publication-bias funnel testing skipped where <10 studies/comparison.

## Results

| Outcome | Studies | Pooled effect (95% CI) | p | I² | Favors | GRADE |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Pain VAS (global)** | 26 | MD −0.25 (−1.09–0.59) | 0.55 NS | 96% | — (no difference) | very low |
| MMO (global) | 14 | SMD −0.67 (−1.14 to −0.20) | 0.005 | 85% | comparators | very low |
| MIO (global) | 11 | SMD −2.93 (−4.54 to −1.32) | 0.0004 | 96% | comparators | very low |
| Mandibular movement (global) | 22 | MD −0.40 (−0.93–0.14) | 0.14 NS | 100% | — | very low |
| Masticatory efficiency | 2 | MD +1.15 (0.22–2.08) | 0.02 | 98% | arthrocentesis | very low |

- **Pain subgroups** (disc displacement MD −0.79; osteoarthritis +0.39; arthralgia −0.22; other +0.60): all non-significant.
- **MMO/MIO subgroups**: where significant, they favored comparators (e.g., MIO disc-displacement SMD −5.07).
- **All mandibular-movement subgroups** (lateral, protrusive, ipsi-/contralateral): non-significant.
- **Adverse effects**: only 1/32 trials reported AEs — 4 patients with transient (<30 min), fully reversible facial nerve paresis from anesthetic diffusion / irrigation extravasation. Under-reporting elsewhere precludes conclusions.
- **Meta-regression**: age positively associated with lateral TMJ movement (β=0.16, p=0.0024).

## Clinical Takeaway

Arthrocentesis is **not first-line and not demonstrably superior** to other modalities. It remains a reasonable minimally-invasive step for conservative-refractory intra-articular TMD before surgery, but it should be presented as one option in a stepwise plan — not as outperforming injections, arthroscopy, or conservative care. Interpret cautiously given extreme heterogeneity and very-low-certainty evidence.
