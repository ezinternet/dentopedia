## One-line Summary
Open-label RCT (n=118, ADDwR) — a novel one-piece Mono-Block (nMB) anterior repositioning splint beat the Twin-Block (TB) on joint-noise reduction (T6 82.1% vs 54.4%, p=0.001) and patient comfort/stability, while pain relief, mouth opening, and CBCT disc-condyle position were equivalent; neither splint durably repositioned the disc.

## 한줄요약
공개표지 RCT (n=118, 정복성 전방관절원판변위 ADDwR) — 신규 일체형 단일블록 전방재위치장치(novel Mono-Block, nMB)가 트윈블록(Twin-Block, TB)보다 관절잡음 감소(6개월 82.1% vs 54.4%, p=0.001)와 편안함·안정성에서 우월; 통증·최대개구량(MMO)·CBCT 과두-와 위치는 동등하고 두 장치 모두 관절원판을 지속적으로 정복시키지는 못함.

## Summary

This single-centre, open-label RCT compared two anterior repositioning splint (ARS) designs for anterior disc displacement with reduction (ADDwR): a redesigned one-piece **novel Mono-Block (nMB)** splint with an anterior breathing space versus a modified **Twin-Block (TB)** splint (two separate occlusal-incline pieces). 118 DC/TMD- and MRI-diagnosed ADDwR patients were randomised 1:1 and worn the splints nighttime-only for 6 months. The nMB was superior at controlling the hallmark symptom — joint clicking — and was rated more comfortable and stable, while both splints relieved pain and improved mouth opening equally. Critically, CBCT showed neither splint achieved a durable change in condyle-fossa (disc) position, reinforcing the broader principle that ARS therapy is symptom-directed, not disc-repositioning, and that joint-noise disappearance may even mark progression toward displacement *without* reduction.

## Key Contributions

- First head-to-head RCT of an integrated one-piece ARS (nMB) versus the two-piece Twin-Block ARS specifically for ADDwR.
- Shows ARS *design* affects nocturnal stability → better joint-noise control and patient-reported comfort, even when pain/MMO outcomes converge.
- CBCT confirms no durable disc-condyle repositioning by either design.

## Methodology

- **Design:** open-label RCT, single centre (Guangzhou Medical University), Dec 2023 – Dec 2024. Statistician blinded; patients/clinicians/assessors not blinded (designs visibly differ).
- **n = 118**, randomised 1:1 (computer-generated, variable blocks 4/6, SNOSE concealment): nMB n=59, TB n=59. 118/118 completed, no dropouts, no adverse events.
- **Population:** DC/TMD + MRI-confirmed ADDwR; 27.1% M / 72.9% F; age 12–68 y. Excluded MMO < 20 mm, major tooth loss, prior TMD/orthognathic/trauma. Baseline-balanced (gender p=0.679, age p=0.937).
- **Interventions:** both ARS set to minimal protruded position eliminating joint noise; worn nighttime only.
- **Outcomes** at T0/T1(1mo)/T3(3mo)/T6(6mo): joint noise (palpation), joint pain (0–5 VAS), MMO (mm); CBCT ln(P/A) (Kamelchuk/Pullinger) at T0/T6; 5-domain satisfaction VAS at T6.

## Results

| Outcome | nMB | TB | Between-group |
|---|---|---|---|
| Joint noise improvement (T6) | 82.1% | 54.4% | **nMB > TB at T1 (p=0.040) & T6 (p=0.001)**; T3 p=0.055 ns |
| Joint pain VAS (T0→T6) | 2.69→0.27 | 2.71→0.41 | diff only at **T3 (p=0.009)**; ns T0/T1/T6 |
| MMO (T0→T6) | 35.83→42.38 mm | 35.56→41.99 mm | diff at T0 (0.022) & **T1 (0.035)**; ns T3/T6 |
| CBCT ln(P/A) (T0→T6) | -0.45→-0.38 (p=0.737) | -0.54→-0.56 (p=0.601) | ns at T0/T6 — no disc repositioning |
| Satisfaction (T6) | higher | lower | **nMB > TB: general (p<0.001), comfort (p=0.005), stability (p<0.001)**; cleaning/retention ns |

- Condyle-posterior positive rate (ln(P/A) < -0.25): nMB 75%→56% (p=0.264), TB 78%→78% (p=1.000) — no significant change either group.
- **Bottom line:** nMB wins on joint noise + comfort/stability; equivalent on pain and MMO; neither repositions the disc.
- **Caveats:** open-label (observer/performance bias), single centre, 6-month follow-up only, CBCT not MRI, no placebo/conservative-care control, no orthodontic monitoring of possible occlusal side effects.
