## One-line Summary
Narrative review (Oral Surgery 2020): TMD is biopsychosocial/multifactorial; DC/TMD covers 12 diagnoses; OPPERA — 4% annual incidence, 36% adult prevalence; conservative multidisciplinary treatment is standard of care.

## Summary
Comprehensive narrative review covering current understanding of TMD etiology, pathophysiology, epidemiology, and management. TMD is positioned as a biopsychosocial condition driven by three domains: psychological profile, pain amplification, and global health. Key epidemiological anchor: OPPERA study shows 4% annual incidence and 36% adult prevalence of painful TMD; TMJ clicking (30%) does not equal disc displacement diagnosis (8%); 51% remit within 8 months. DC/TMD provides reliable, valid diagnosis across 12 conditions. Evidence supports conservative multidisciplinary management; irreversible interventions discouraged.

## Key Contributions
- Biopsychosocial model for TMD with three actionable domains
- OPPERA epidemiology: incidence, prevalence, remission rates
- DC/TMD as gold standard classification (12 diagnoses)
- TMJ clicking ≠ pathology: high prevalence in asymptomatic population
- Poor correlation between intra-articular diagnoses (DD, DJD) and pain intensity/function
- Conservative management: all modalities effective; irreversible interventions contraindicated first-line

## Methodology
Narrative review; OPPERA cohort data (large US multisite prospective study); DC/TMD validation literature; biopsychosocial framework literature review.

## Epidemiology Summary (OPPERA)

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Annual incidence (18–44 y) | 4% |
| Prevalence painful TMD (20–49 y) | 36% |
| TMJ clicking prevalence | 30% |
| Disc displacement (DD) diagnosis | 8% |
| DJD prevalence | 17% |
| 8-month remission rate | 51% |
| 5-year remission | 49% |

## DC/TMD Classification
**Painful diagnoses**: myalgia, arthralgia, headache attributed to TMD  
**Non-painful diagnoses**: DD with/without reduction (with/without limited opening), DJD, subluxation

## Biopsychosocial Risk Domains
1. Psychological (anxiety, depression, somatization)
2. Pain amplification / central sensitization
3. Global health / comorbidities (IBS, insomnia, fibromyalgia)

## Management Evidence
| Modality | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Self-management + education | Effective; first-line |
| Physical therapy | Effective (see González-Sánchez 2023) |
| Pharmacotherapy | Effective by subtype (see Häggman-Henrikson 2017) |
| Behavioural therapy | Effective for chronic TMD |
| Irreversible occlusal treatment | **Contraindicated** as first-line |
