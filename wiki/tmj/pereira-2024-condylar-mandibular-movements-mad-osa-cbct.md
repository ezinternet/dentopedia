## One-line Summary
Prospective CBCT study (n=20, OSA): MAD increases VD and anterior condylar translation; forward condylar movement directly correlates with upper airway volume; eminence morphology modulates condylar movement.

## 한줄요약
전향적 CBCT 연구 (n=20, OSA): MAD는 VD 증가·과두 전방 이동; 전방 과두 이동은 상기도 용적과 직접 상관; 관절 융기 형태가 과두 이동량 조절.

## Summary
This prospective CBCT study evaluated TMJ, condylar, and mandibular movements in 20 OSA patients treated with a mandibular advancement device (MAD), correlating these with upper airway (UA) volume and polysomnographic outcomes. Key findings: anterior condylar translation and anterior mandibular displacement directly correlated with total UA volume; vertical mandibular translation inversely correlated with inferior oropharynx volume. MAD significantly increased vertical dimension and altered condylar position. Notably, articular eminence morphology (central/medial lengths inversely; lateral length/height directly) modulated the extent of condylar rotation and translation. No direct correlation was found between condylar movement and polysomnographic variables (e.g., AHI), suggesting the airway volumetric effect may not directly translate to AHI improvement.

## Key Contributions
- Anterior condylar translation → ↑ total UA volume (direct correlation)
- Vertical mandibular translation → ↓ inferior oropharynx volume (inverse)
- MAD: significant VD increase + condylar position change (rotation & translation)
- Articular eminence morphology modulates how much condyle moves with MAD
- Individualized TMJ anatomy evaluation important for MAD therapy planning

## Methodology
- n=20 OSA (polysomnography-confirmed); prospective; CBCT at baseline and therapeutic protrusion
- 3D airway, TMJ, mandible measurements; Pearson/Spearman correlation
- Polysomnographic variables: AHI, oxygen saturation, sleep stages

## Results
| Measurement | Correlation with UA volume |
|---|---|
| Condylar rotation | Direct ↑ (total UA) |
| Anterior condylar translation | Direct ↑ (total UA) |
| Anterior mandibular displacement | Direct ↑ (total UA) |
| Vertical mandibular translation | Inverse ↓ (inferior oropharynx) |
| Condylar movement vs AHI | No significant correlation |
