## 한줄요약
RCT 체계적 문헌고찰: 교합안정장치(Occlusal Splint)가 악관절증(TMD) 환자에서 통증을 유의하게 감소시키나, 플라시보 장치 대비 우월성 증거는 낮은 수준임.

## Summary
This systematic review from Fujian Medical University synthesizes RCT evidence on the efficacy of 교합안정장치 (Occlusal Splint) for 악관절증 (Temporomandibular Disorder, TMD). The review found moderate evidence that stabilization splints reduce pain intensity compared to no treatment. However, evidence comparing stabilization splints to non-occluding or flat-plane splints showed no clinically meaningful difference, raising questions about the mechanism of action (specific vs. nonspecific effect). Overall evidence certainty was rated low to moderate by GRADE assessment. Methodological limitations — particularly in blinding — affected most included trials.

## Key Contributions
- Synthesizes RCT evidence specifically for occlusal splints in TMD management
- Demonstrates modest pain benefit vs. no treatment; questions superiority over placebo devices
- Identifies need for standardized DC/TMD diagnostic criteria in future trials
- Highlights blinding as an inherent limitation of splint RCTs

## Methodology
- Databases: MEDLINE, Embase, Cochrane Central
- Inclusion: RCTs examining occlusal splints for TMD; DC/TMD or RDC/TMD diagnosis
- Outcomes: pain (VAS/NRS), 하악 최대개구량 (Maximum Mouth Opening, MMO), joint sounds, quality of life
- Cochrane risk of bias tool
- Narrative synthesis (meta-analysis not performed due to heterogeneity)

## Results
- Stabilization splints significantly reduced pain vs. no-treatment controls in most trials
- No statistically or clinically significant difference between stabilization and non-occluding splints
- MMO improvement inconsistent across studies
- GRADE certainty: low to moderate
- Most trials had high risk of bias (blinding, allocation concealment)
