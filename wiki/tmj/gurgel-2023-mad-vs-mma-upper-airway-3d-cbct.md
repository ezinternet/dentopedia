## One-line Summary
Retrospective CBCT cohort (n=17 MAD vs n=17 MMA, BMI-matched): MAD = clockwise rotation + superior oropharynx gain; MMA = counterclockwise rotation + greater gains in all UA regions.

## 한줄요약
후향적 CBCT 코호트 (MAD 17명 vs MMA 17명): MAD는 시계방향 회전으로 상부 구인두 확장; MMA는 반시계방향으로 전 상기도 영역에서 더 큰 확장.

## Summary
This BMI-matched retrospective CBCT cohort compared 3D upper airway (UA) changes after mandibular advancement device (MAD, n=17) versus maxillomandibular advancement surgery (MMA, n=17). Both treatments significantly increased superior oropharynx volume, but MMA showed greater gains. Only MMA significantly increased inferior oropharynx volume. A key finding: MAD produces clockwise mandibular rotation (chin down/back, ~−4°) while MMA produces counterclockwise rotation (chin forward/up, ~+3°). In the MAD group, greater mandibular advancement correlated inversely with superior oropharynx gain but directly with inferior gain, suggesting an optimal advancement range.

## Key Contributions
- MAD vs MMA: opposite mandibular rotation directions → different airway regions benefited
- Both: superior oropharynx significantly enlarged; MMA > MAD
- MMA only: inferior oropharynx significantly enlarged
- MAD clockwise rotation: VD increases, potentially relevant for TMJ loading
- Greater MAD advancement ≠ more superior oropharynx gain (inverse correlation)

## Methodology
- Retrospective cohort; BMI/weight/height matched; CBCT at T0 and T1 (6–12 mo)
- UA segmentation: ITK-SNAP + 3D Slicer; standardized head orientation

## Results
| Parameter | MAD | MMA | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mandibular rotation | −3.97° (clockwise) | +2.40° (counterclockwise) | <0.001 |
| Superior oropharynx ↑ | Significant | Significant, greater | 0.010 |
| Inferior oropharynx ↑ | Not significant | Significant | 0.010 |
