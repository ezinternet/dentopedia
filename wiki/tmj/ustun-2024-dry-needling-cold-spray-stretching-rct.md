> [!note] Extra-masticatory (transferable) evidence
> This RCT was conducted in the **upper trapezius**, not the masticatory muscles. It is filed under `tmj` to keep the spray-and-stretch evidence cluster together, but direct transfer of its effect sizes to masseter/temporalis is unproven. Read it as technique-level (efficacy/comparator) evidence, not as a TMD trial.

## One-line Summary

Prospective single-blind RCT (n=60, upper-trapezius MPS) — cold-spray-stretching vs dry needling over 3 weekly sessions; both improved pain, function, sEMG and ultrasound histogram (P<.05), with cold-spray-stretching scoring higher on algometer (pain threshold) and NDI than dry needling (P<.008 and P<.028).

## 한줄요약

전향 단일맹검 RCT (n=60, 상부 승모근 근막통) — 분사신장 vs 건침 3주(주 1회); 두 군 모두 통증·기능·sEMG·초음파 호전(P<.05), 분사신장군이 압통역치·NDI에서 건침보다 우수(P<.008, P<.028). 교근 외 전이가능 근거.

## Summary

This is the strongest **evidence-grade** support for spray-and-stretch the wiki holds: a randomized controlled trial putting cold-spray-stretching head-to-head against dry needling — currently a popular trigger-point intervention — in 60 patients with an active upper-trapezius trigger point. Both techniques produced significant within-group improvement across pain (NRS), pressure pain threshold (algometer), disability (NDI), and the instrumented trigger-point measures (sEMG, ultrasound histogram). Between groups, **cold-spray-stretching was at least non-inferior and scored higher** on algometer (P<.008) and NDI (P<.028).

For the masticatory question this wiki cares about, the caveat is anatomical: the trigger point was in the trapezius, so the magnitudes don't transfer directly to jaw muscles. But the technique mechanism (vapocoolant-assisted passive stretch inactivating an active trigger point) is the same one used on masseter/temporalis, so this trial anchors the efficacy claim that the orofacial reviews ([[tmj/pal-2014-trends-management-myofascial-pain]], [[tmj/vazquez-delgado-2010-myofascial-pain-differential-diagnosis-treatment]]) assert but do not test.

## Key Contributions

- Modern RCT comparing **spray-and-stretch vs dry needling** — the comparison absent from the masticatory literature.
- Adds objective **sEMG + ultrasound-histogram** trigger-point endpoints, not just subjective pain.
- Positions cold-spray-stretching as a credible alternative (favored on PPT and disability) to dry needling.

## Methodology

- **Design**: prospective, single-blind RCT; no sham arm.
- **Sample**: 60 MPS patients (51 female; mean 39.7±11.6 y) with active upper-trapezius trigger point.
- **Arms**: dry needling vs cold-spray-stretching; 1 session/week × 3 weeks.
- **Outcomes**: NRS, algometer (PPT), neck ROM, NDI, sEMG, US histogram; baseline/end + pre/post sessions 1 and 3.

## Results

| Comparison | Finding | P |
|---|---|---|
| Within group (both arms) | ↑ pain, function, sEMG, US histogram | <.05 |
| Cold-spray-stretch vs DN — algometer (PPT) | Spray favored | <.008 |
| Cold-spray-stretch vs DN — NDI | Spray favored | <.028 |

Conclusion: both options effective; cold-spray-stretching non-inferior / favored on pressure-pain-threshold and disability.
