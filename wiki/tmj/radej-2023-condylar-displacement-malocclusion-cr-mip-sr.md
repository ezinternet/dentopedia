## One-line Summary
Scoping review (10 studies, Feb 2022): hyperdivergent skeletal pattern is the main risk factor for posteroinferior condylar displacement (CR→MIP); other malocclusions lack conclusive evidence.

## 한줄요약
스코핑 리뷰 (10편): 과두발산형 골격이 CR→MIP 과두 후하방 변위의 주요 위험 요소; 기타 부정교합과의 관계는 근거 불충분.

## Summary
This scoping review (PRISMA-ScR; 10 studies from 2321) examines how maxillofacial morphology and malocclusion influence condylar displacement (CD) from centric relation (CR) to maximum intercuspal position (MIP) in orthodontic patients. The key finding: hyperdivergent facial skeletal structure is the strongest risk factor for increased CD, with condyles typically displaced in a posteroinferior direction. Skeletal Class II profiles may carry higher risk of TMJ disc displacement. The review emphasizes that CD is only one of many TMD risk factors and cannot diagnose TMD in isolation. No proven causal relationship exists between malocclusion and TMD. Routine TMD screening before orthodontic treatment is recommended.

## Key Contributions
- **Hyperdivergent pattern** → increased CD (posteroinferior), especially in vertical dimension
- Clinically significant CD thresholds: ≥2 mm (vertical/horizontal), ≥0.5 mm (transverse)
- Transverse crossbite may link to transverse CD and TMD
- No proven causal relationship: malocclusion → TMD
- CD assessment via MPI/CPI should complement, not replace, clinical TMD exam

## Methodology
- PRISMA-ScR; JBI appraisal; PubMed/Cochrane/WoS/Scopus to Feb 2022
- 10 studies; 5 analysis domains (vertical/sagittal skeletal pattern; vertical/horizontal/transverse malocclusion)

## Results
| Skeletal/Dental Factor | CD Risk |
|---|---|
| Hyperdivergent (vertical pattern) | ↑ CD, posteroinferior direction |
| Skeletal Class II | Possibly ↑ disc displacement/degeneration |
| Transverse crossbite | May ↑ transverse CD |
| Other malocclusions | Inconclusive |
