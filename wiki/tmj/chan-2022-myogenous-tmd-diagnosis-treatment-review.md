## Summary
Clinical update review focusing specifically on myogenous TMD (M-TMD), the most common TMD subtype (45.3% of all cases). Reviews the 2014 DC/TMD muscle classification, which distinguishes four muscle disorders (myalgia, tendonitis, myositis, spasm) and three myalgia subtypes (local myalgia, myofascial pain, myofascial pain with referral). Diagnosis relies on history, palpation (1 kg × 5 sec to allow referral patterns to emerge), and pain replication. Treatment ranges from conservative (PT, splint, pharmacotherapy) to minimally invasive (trigger point injection, dry needling, BTX, LLLT). Central sensitization and psychosocial comorbidities are emphasized.

## Key Contributions
- DC/TMD muscle subclassification operationalized clinically (palpation protocol)
- Central sensitization: possible mechanism for chronic M-TMD pain
- Bruxism–myofascial pain positive correlation (EMG evidence)
- BTX: effective for masseter/temporalis myalgia
- Multidisciplinary approach with psychological component for chronic cases
- COVID-19 stress → TMD exacerbation link

## DC/TMD Muscle Classification

| Diagnosis | Clinical Definition |
|---|---|
| Local myalgia | Familiar pain localized to palpation site |
| Myofascial pain | Familiar pain spreading within muscle on palpation |
| Myofascial pain with referral | Familiar pain spreading beyond muscle boundary on palpation |
| Tendonitis | Tendon origin |
| Myositis | Inflammatory muscle |
| Spasm | Acute involuntary muscle contraction |

## Diagnostic Protocol
- Palpation: 1 kg pressure × 5 sec (longer duration elicits referral patterns)
- Provocation: jaw opening, lateral excursion → must replicate pain
- VAS for monitoring
- Surface EMG / pressure pain threshold: adjunct quantification

## Treatment Summary

| Approach | Options |
|---|---|
| Conservative | PT + manual therapy, jaw exercises, heat/cold, CBT, splint |
| Pharmacological | NSAIDs, muscle relaxants, TCA (chronic), BZD (short-term) |
| Minimally invasive | Trigger point injection (LA ± corticosteroid), dry needling, BTX |
| Adjunct physical | LLLT, ultrasound, TENS |
