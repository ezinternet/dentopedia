## One-line Summary
Expert narrative (BDJ) on managing patient expectations in implant/aesthetic dentistry — social media inflates expectations beyond biological reality; transparent communication, informed consent, and empathy align them and protect both patient satisfaction and clinician wellbeing.

## Summary
This is the domain-specific application of the communication/SDM principles to the practice's highest-expectation procedures. Dawood argues that digital media and celebrity culture have decoupled patient expectations from biologically achievable outcomes, so **expectation management becomes a core clinical competency**, not a soft add-on. The levers are transparent communication, robust informed consent, and empathetic care; the failure mode is cognitive dissonance when results meet biology rather than the imagined outcome. He extends the duty of care to the **clinician's own resilience and team cohesion**, especially with complex histories or repeated prior failures. It is single-author opinion from a referral-heavy complex-implant practice — persuasive framework, no data.

## Key Contributions
- Recasts expectation management as core competency in implant/aesthetic care.
- Links social-media culture to the perceived-vs-achievable outcome gap.
- Extends care duty to clinician wellbeing and team cohesion under expectation pressure.

## Methodology
- Reflective expert essay; organized around psychological concepts, consent, and communication; no empirical data.

## Results (key points)
- Social media heightens and distorts expectations.
- Clear communication + informed consent align expectations and reduce dissatisfaction.
- Patients misunderstand implant complexity/limitations → clinicians must correct false assumptions.
- Unrealistic expectations + past failures stress patients and clinicians → resilience and tailored care needed.
