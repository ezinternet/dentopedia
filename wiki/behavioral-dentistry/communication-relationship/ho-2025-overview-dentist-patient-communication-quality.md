## Summary
If Choi 2024 establishes *that* communication is the top relationship determinant, Ho et al. supply the *how*. The overview organizes dentist–patient communication into four pillars. **Importance**: accurate information transfer, dentist efficiency/confidence, lower occupational stress, fewer complaints/litigation, reduced dental anxiety, trust, adherence, and satisfaction. **Barriers**: time constraints, rapport difficulty, patient oral-health illiteracy, dentist skill/perception, and language. **Strategies**: a patient-centered premise (understand the illness, share decisions, pace to the patient), jargon-free verbal language, and deliberate non-verbal behavior (posture, gestures). **Training**: a structured pedagogy of didactic instruction, role-play, patient interviewing, and ongoing assessment — with MI, open-ended questioning, affirmations, reflective listening, and summaries as the named skill set. It is descriptive guidance, not comparative evidence.

## Key Contributions
- One-source synthesis of importance/barriers/strategies/training for chairside communication.
- Operational definition of the patient-centered approach.
- A concrete training blueprint usable for hygienist/resident education.

## Methodology
- Narrative review anchored to FDI Vision 2030; structured importance → challenges → strategies → training.

## Results
- Benefits and barriers enumerated (above).
- Strategy core = patient-centered + jargon-free verbal + supportive non-verbal.
- Training core = didactic + role-play + interviewing + assessment; skills = MI, open-ended Qs, affirmations, reflective listening, summaries.
