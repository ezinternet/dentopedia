## One-line Summary

Concise narrative review (PubMed/MEDLINE) concluding that shared decision-making (SDM) is applied in dentistry but supported by limited published evidence, that informed consent is intertwined with the SDM process, and that patient decision aids (PDAs) need further development across more dental fields.

## 한줄요약

치과 분야의 공유의사결정(Shared Decision-Making, SDM)을 다룬 간결한 서술적 종설로, SDM이 치과에 적용되고 있으나 근거는 제한적이며, 충분한설명 후 동의(informed consent)가 SDM 과정과 얽혀 있고, 환자 의사결정 보조도구(Patient Decision Aids, PDA)가 더 많은 치과 영역에서 개발될 필요가 있음을 정리했다.

## Summary

This concise narrative review examines how shared decision-making (SDM) is implemented in dentistry and the roles of informed consent and patient decision aids (PDAs) within that process. Drawing on pertinent literature from PubMed and MEDLINE, the author finds that only a limited number of studies address SDM implementation in dental practice, and that the studies available show SDM to be influenced by many factors. Informed consent is framed not as a discrete administrative step but as a process that intertwines with SDM, beginning with the provision of high-quality information to patients and the use of PDAs. Some dental fields already have PDAs developed to facilitate the process, but the author concludes that PDAs need to be developed for more dental fields. (abstract-only — full text not retrieved.)

## Key Contributions

- Frames SDM as already present in dentistry but under-studied, with implementation shaped by multiple factors.
- Positions informed consent as integral to and intertwined with the SDM process, not separate from it.
- Identifies PDAs as the practical mechanism that facilitates SDM and flags their uneven availability across dental fields as a development gap.

## Methodology

Narrative (non-systematic) review of pertinent literature from PubMed and MEDLINE addressing the current application of SDM in dentistry and the influence of informed consent and PDAs. No systematic search protocol, risk-of-bias appraisal, or meta-analysis is reported. Methodological detail beyond the abstract is unavailable (abstract-only — full text not retrieved).

## Results

- A limited number of published studies on SDM implementation in dental practice is available.
- SDM in dentistry is influenced by many factors.
- Informed consent intertwines with the SDM process and begins with providing high-quality information and employing PDAs.
- PDAs have been developed for some dental fields to facilitate SDM.
- Conclusion: SDM is applied in dentistry; informed consent is an important part of the process; PDAs in more dental fields need to be further developed.
- (No quantitative outcomes reported — abstract-only.)
