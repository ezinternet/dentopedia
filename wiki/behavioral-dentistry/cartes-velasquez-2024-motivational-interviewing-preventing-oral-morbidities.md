## One-line Summary
SR+MA of MI for adult oral health (7 RCTs, all periodontal): only bleeding on probing reached significance (−6.41 pp); plaque and gingival indices did not — evidence too sparse to conclude MI prevents adult oral morbidities.

## 한줄요약
성인 동기면담(MI) SR+MA — 7편밖에 없고 전부 치주 outcome. BoP만 통계적으로 유의(−6.41pp), plaque/gingival index는 비유의. "성인 oral morbidity 예방에 MI 효과 있다"고 결론 내리기엔 증거 너무 빈약.

## Summary
This is the adult counterpart to Jahanshahi 2022's pediatric MI SR+MA — and the conclusion is more cautious. Of 602 candidate studies, only 7 met inclusion. All 7 reported periodontal outcomes; no studies on adult caries prevention, implant maintenance, or other oral morbidities qualified. Within periodontal outcomes:

- **Bleeding on probing (BoP)** reaches significance: −6.41pp favoring MI (95% CI −12.18 to −0.65)
- **Plaque index** does not: −3.59pp (CI crosses null: −11.44 to +4.25)
- **Gingival index** does not: −0.70 (CI crosses null: −1.87 to +0.48)

All point estimates directionally favor MI, but only BoP separates from zero. The authors' honest conclusion: "the current evidence available is limited to periodontal outcomes, and it is not possible to determine whether MI effectively prevents oral morbidities in adults."

This is a different evidence picture from pediatric MI (Jahanshahi 2022 found significant ECC reduction). The difference is consistent with adult behavior change being harder, follow-up shorter, and the wrong outcomes being measured for the wrong populations.

## Key Contributions
- First SR+MA of MI in adults for oral health
- Documents that the entire adult MI evidence base is **periodontal**, not caries
- Quantifies effect for the 3 main periodontal indices
- Honest about the inability to conclude beyond periodontal outcomes
- Provides open meta-analysis code (GitHub)

## Methodology
RCTs, cluster-RCTs, community-based randomized trials. Adults 18–60. MI vs. any oral health education or negative control. 602 studies screened → 7 included. Random-effects model. Follow-up 1 month to 1 year.

Population breakdown:
- 272 with moderate-to-severe periodontitis
- 112 pregnant women
- 60 with mental disorders / alcohol problems

## Results
| Outcome | Studies | n | Effect (95% CI) | Significant |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Plaque index | 4 | 267 | −3.59 pp (−11.44, +4.25) | No |
| Bleeding on probing | 2 | 177 | −6.41 pp (−12.18, −0.65) | **Yes** |
| Gingival index | 2 | 166 | −0.70 (−1.87, +0.48) | No |

All point estimates favor MI, but only BoP reaches statistical significance, and that's from only 2 studies.

## Why It Matters for Clinic Operations
For a clinic considering MI training for hygienists on adult periodontal patients:
- **Best-supported outcome**: bleeding on probing — gum inflammation responds modestly to MI counseling
- **Weakest evidence**: plaque index — the simplest hygiene proxy doesn't shift significantly
- **Inferential gap**: adult caries, implant maintenance, peri-implantitis — no MI evidence at all
- **Operational implication**: MI for adults is defensible for periodontal maintenance programs (especially with measurable BoP follow-up), but you cannot promise "MI prevents caries in adults" — the evidence isn't there
- Pair with [[behavioral-dentistry/jahanshahi-2022-motivational-interviewing-prevent-early-childhood]] for full-lifespan MI program design — pediatric evidence is stronger

## Open Questions
- Why is MI underperforming on plaque index but reaching significance on BoP? Possibly BoP is more sensitive to short-term hygiene improvement than PI; or studies happen to measure BoP in more responsive populations.
- The mental disorders / alcohol problems subgroup (n=60) likely behaves very differently — separate analysis would clarify.
- Caries-outcome MI trials in adults are essentially missing — primary research gap.
- Follow-up beyond 1 year is unstudied; behavior change literature suggests longer follow-up may show effect divergence.
