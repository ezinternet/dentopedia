## One-line Summary
SR+MA (12 RCTs + 1 non-RCT) of patient-reported outcomes in sinus floor elevation finding graftless and transcrestal approaches associated with less postoperative pain/discomfort than grafted lateral SFE, with sparse overall evidence.

## Summary
This meta-analysis shifts the sinus-lift comparison from survival and bone metrics to the patient's experience. Across 13 studies assessing patient-reported outcomes — primarily postoperative VAS pain in the first week — the graftless approach produced less pain at days 3 and 7 than grafted sinus floor elevation, and the transcrestal approach produced less discomfort than the lateral window. The authors stress a paucity of PROM evidence overall. Because this page is built from the publisher's preprint abstract rather than full text, the directional findings are reliable but the pooled magnitudes are not recorded here.

## Key Contributions
- Adds a patient-experience axis (pain/discomfort) to sinus-lift technique selection.
- Directional ranking: graftless < grafted, transcrestal < lateral for postoperative pain.
- Documents how thin the PROM evidence base is for SFE.

## Methodology
SR+MA; PubMed/Scopus/Cochrane CENTRAL to 2025-01-31; comparative studies with ≥1 SFE group (≥5 patients) assessing PROMs; 12 RCTs + 1 non-RCT included; pairwise meta-analysis on proportion reporting pain at days 1/3/7; primary outcome VAS pain. RoB: 10 moderate, 1 high.

## Results
- Graftless: less pain at days 3 and 7 vs grafted SFE.
- Transcrestal SFE: less discomfort than lateral SFE.
- Quantitative pooled estimates not captured (abstract-only).
