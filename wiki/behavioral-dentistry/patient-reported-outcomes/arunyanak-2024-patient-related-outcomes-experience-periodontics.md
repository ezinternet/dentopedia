Review (Periodontology 2000) of behavioral/psychosocial + PRO/PRE assessment across periodontitis, implant-candidate, and peri-implantitis patients — treatment improves QoL, but diverse expectations/perceptions undermine satisfaction and long-term implant success; pre-therapy behavioral assessment with remedial correction is essential.

## Summary
Arunyanak et al. carry the PRO/PRE framework into periodontal and, importantly, **implant** care — the domain where patient expectations most directly threaten outcomes. Periodontitis degrades quality of life and its treatment yields patient-experienced improvement; implant rehabilitation likewise improves QoL, **but** heterogeneous expectations and perceptions can interfere with satisfaction and even long-term success. The clinical mandate is a thorough pre-therapy **behavioral assessment** — perceptions, expectations, and risk for behavioral disorders — followed by remedial correction of misperceptions before proceeding. The paper also frames a peri-implantitis diagnosis as a source of distress, resentment, and loss of trust, tying biological complication to relationship breakdown. This is the PRO-side complement to Dawood's expectation-management essay.

## Key Contributions
- Extends PRO/PRE into implant candidacy and peri-implantitis.
- Centers structured pre-therapy behavioral assessment + remedial action.
- Connects peri-implantitis to psychological burden and trust loss.

## Methodology
- Narrative review of behavioral/psychosocial assessment across the three patient groups.

## Results
- Periodontitis harms QoL; treatment improves patient-experienced outcomes.
- Implant QoL gains are real but expectation-dependent; misperceptions must be corrected pre-therapy.
- Peri-implantitis carries significant psychological/trust burden affecting long-term outcomes.
