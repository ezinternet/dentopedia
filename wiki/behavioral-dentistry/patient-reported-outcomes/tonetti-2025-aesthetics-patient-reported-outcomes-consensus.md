## One-line Summary

EFP 2025 expert consensus (40 experts, 5 commissioned SRs) finds clinician-reported aesthetic scores and patient PROs are complementary but discordant across root coverage and implant soft-tissue procedures, and mandates integrated PRO+CRO reporting in future RCTs.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

This EFP-organized expert consensus report (Florence, January 2025) synthesizes findings from five commissioned systematic reviews to evaluate aesthetics and patient-reported outcomes in three clinical scenarios: (1) gingival recession defects (GRDs), (2) peri-implant buccal soft-tissue dehiscences (PSTDs), and (3) implant placement (immediate/early/delayed/late) with soft-tissue augmentation (STA).

The central finding is a systematic discordance between clinician-reported aesthetic scores (CROs) and patient-reported outcomes (PROs): even when CAF+CTG achieves superior root coverage and high clinician aesthetic scores, patients' own aesthetic perceptions do not correspondingly improve. Conversely, in the treatment of peri-implant soft-tissue dehiscences, both CRO and PRO aesthetics align in favor of STA (CTG under CAF) — the only scenario where the two perspectives converge. The consensus formally distinguishes PRO, PRE, CRO, and PROM, and calls for validated, disease-specific PROMs with established minimally important differences (MIDs) to be co-primary endpoints in future periodontal/implant RCTs.

## Key Contributions

1. **PRO–CRO discordance formalized**: Positive CRO aesthetic score improvement with %RC does not translate to corresponding patient-perceived aesthetic improvement in GRD therapy — a central methodological finding.
2. **Implant STA inadequately captured by current tools**: CTG at immediate implant placement reduces mid-facial mucosal apical shift, but neither CRO nor PRO tools capture this volumetric benefit adequately.
3. **PSTD: aligned outcome signal**: Soft-tissue augmentation (STA; CTG under CAF) for peri-implant dehiscences produces concordant improvement in both CRO and PRO aesthetics — the only intervention scenario where both metrics agree.
4. **Terminology framework**: Distinguishes PRO, PRE (experience/satisfaction), CRO, PROM, and MID in the periodontal/implant context.
5. **Research agenda**: Calls for future RCTs with co-primary CRO+PRO endpoints, validated PROMs, MID determination, and systematic harm reporting from the patient perspective.

## Methodology

- **Format**: Focused Workshop organized by EFP+SIdP+SEPA; 40 experts from 12+ countries.
- **Two working groups**:
  - WG1 (GRDs; chairs: Tonetti, Herrera): covered single GRDs (Cairo et al. 2025 SR) and multiple GRDs (Stefanini et al. 2025 SR).
  - WG2 (implants; chairs: Sanz, Chapple): covered immediate implant STA (Ramanauskaite et al. 2025 SR), early/delayed/late implant STA (Lambert et al. 2025 SR), and PSTD treatment (Nart et al. 2025 SR).
- **Evidence hierarchy**: Five commissioned SRs provided structured evidence; expert consensus filled remaining gaps.
- **PROM framework**: PROMs considered the methodological reference when validated; MID defined as the minimally important change detectable as relevant to the patient.

## Results

| Scenario | CRO finding | PRO finding | Concordance |
|----------|------------|-------------|-------------|
| Single GRD (CAF±CTG) | %RC correlates with aesthetic scores | Patient aesthetic perception not correlated with %RC | Discordant |
| Multiple GRD | Similar CRO improvement | PROs inconsistently reported | Discordant |
| Immediate implant + CTG | CTG reduces apical mucosal shift | CRO/PRO scores do not capture volumetric gain | Discordant |
| Early/delayed/late implant + STA | Limited data | Limited data | Inconclusive |
| Peri-implant dehiscence (STA/CTG+CAF) | Favorable CRO aesthetics | Favorable PRO aesthetics | **Concordant** |

**GRD epidemiology**: 88% of adults ≥65, 50% of adults 18–64 have ≥1 recession; only ~28% of GRDs are perceived by patients; 92% are asymptomatic or unnoticed by the patient.

**Patient concerns with GRDs** (Nieri et al. 2013, n=120): tooth loss fear, aesthetics, dentine hypersensitivity, brushing difficulty — but most patients were unaware of their recessions.

**PROM gaps**: No validated, periodontal/implant-specific PROMs with established MIDs currently exist for most clinical indications.
