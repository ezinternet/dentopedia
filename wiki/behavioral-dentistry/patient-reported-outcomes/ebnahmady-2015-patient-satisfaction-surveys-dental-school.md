## Summary

Health care is increasingly patient-centered, and patient satisfaction matters because it influences treatment cooperation, which in turn supports better long-term oral health — including in the dental school clinic setting. To equip clinic administrators and clinicians to deliver high-quality care, this review set out to identify the **dimensions** used to measure patient satisfaction with dental school clinic services. The authors searched PubMed for studies published between 1980 and March 2014. Through a multi-stage screening process, 41 of 730 articles were selected, and after critical appraisal **nine articles** were retained. From these, **five satisfaction dimensions** emerged: **quality, interaction, access, environment, and cost**. Knowing these dimensions can help dental institutions structure satisfaction measurement and target quality improvement. (This page is built from the abstract only — full text was not retrieved.)

## Key Contributions

- Reduces a heterogeneous literature into **five reusable satisfaction dimensions**: quality, interaction, access, environment, cost.
- Positions patient satisfaction as an operational lever (cooperation → long-term health), not merely a service courtesy.
- Documents how thin the appraised evidence base is for this niche (730 records → 9 retained articles).

## Methodology

- **Design**: narrative review / dimension comparison (no meta-analysis).
- **Source**: PubMed only.
- **Window**: 1980 – March 2014.
- **Selection funnel**: 730 records identified → 41 selected → **9 retained** after critical appraisal.
- **Synthesis**: satisfaction dimensions categorized across retained studies.

## Results

- **Five dimensions of patient satisfaction** with dental school clinic services:
  - **Quality** — perceived clinical/technical quality of care.
  - **Interaction** — patient–provider communication and relationship.
  - **Access** — availability, waiting time, ease of obtaining care.
  - **Environment** — physical clinic setting and facilities.
  - **Cost** — financial aspects of care.
- These constitute the construct set the authors recommend dental institutions measure to drive care quality.
- abstract-only — per-study results and instrument-level detail were not retrieved.
