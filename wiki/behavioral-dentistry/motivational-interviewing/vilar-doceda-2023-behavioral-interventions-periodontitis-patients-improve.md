## One-line Summary

Systematic review (no meta-analysis) of 21 studies (16 RCTs, 5 NRCTs; n 20–297) found social-cognitive-model interventions combining goal-setting, planning, self-monitoring and feedback may improve oral hygiene in periodontitis patients, and CBT combined with motivational interviewing may reduce plaque and bleeding scores.

## 한줄요약

21개 연구(RCT 16편, 비무작위 5편; n 20–297)를 종합한 체계적 문헌고찰(메타분석 없음)로, 목표설정·계획·자기점검·피드백을 결합한 사회인지모델 기반 중재가 치주염 환자의 구강위생을 개선할 수 있고, 인지행동치료(CBT)와 동기면담(MI) 병용이 치면세균막·출혈 점수를 낮출 수 있음을 시사했다.

## Summary

This systematic review evaluated psychological models, strategies, and methods for improving plaque control and gingival inflammation in periodontitis patients (gingivitis-only excluded). Of 5460 records, 21 studies (16 RCTs, 5 NRCTs) met inclusion criteria. The studies spanned six psychological models — Social Cognitive Theory, the Theory of Planned Behavior, the Health Action Process Approach, Leventhal's self-regulatory theory, Motivational Interviewing, and Cognitive Behavioral Therapy — plus audio-visual and SMS modalities. Heterogeneity in protocols, outcomes, and follow-up precluded meta-analysis. The authors concluded that social-cognitive-model interventions combining goal-setting, planning, self-monitoring, and feedback may improve oral hygiene and periodontal outcomes, and that CBT combined with MI may reduce plaque and bleeding scores.

## Key Contributions

- Broadens the behavior-change evidence base beyond MI to six psychological models in periodontitis.
- Highlights the goal-setting + planning + self-monitoring + feedback bundle as the most promising technique cluster.
- Provides a structured map placing MI within the wider behavioral-intervention landscape.

## Methodology

- PubMed/MEDLINE, Cochrane Library, Embase; 2 blinded reviewers; search to October 2022.
- PICOS: adults >18 y with periodontitis; behavioral/educational OH interventions vs no/regular OHI; plaque & bleeding indices; RCT/NRCT/cohort/case-control, ≥1-month follow-up.
- Quality: Cochrane RoB 2 (RCTs) + Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NRCTs).
- Narrative synthesis only (no pooling) due to high heterogeneity.

## Results

- 5460 records → 55 full-text → 21 included (16 RCT, 5 NRCT).
- Sample sizes 20–297; follow-up 1 month–3 years; ages 18–80.
- Williams 2018 example (4 weeks): plaque improved in both arms (test 68→79.8; control 65.8→76.5).
- Social-cognitive interventions with goal-setting/planning/self-monitoring/feedback may improve OH; CBT+MI may decrease plaque and bleeding.
- Carra et al. SR+MA cited: cognitive-construct + MI may reinforce OH but no significant pooled between-group difference.
