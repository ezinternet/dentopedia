## One-line Summary

Systematic review (Medline/EMBASE/Cochrane CENTRAL, 1966–Mar 2012) of RCTs on interventions to improve adherence in orthodontic patients aged 12–18; 381 articles screened → 4 RCTs, each a different method (rewards/awards, Hawthorne effect, written information, plaque-microbiology demonstration); all except reward/award improved adherence, with insufficient evidence to name a single best method.

## Summary

Adolescent orthodontic patients (12–18 y) frequently fail to maintain oral hygiene or attend appointments, undermining treatment. This systematic review asked which interventions actually improve adherence in this group. Searching three databases plus a hand search and screening 381 articles, the authors found only **4 randomized controlled trials**, each testing a **different** intervention method. Three of the four (the Hawthorne effect, written information, and demonstration of the microbiology of plaque) were associated with improved adherence; the one **reward/award** system was not. Because each method appeared in only a single trial with heterogeneous outcomes, no meta-analysis was possible and no single best method can be recommended. The recurring lesson is the value of **clinician time spent illustrating to the patient why adherence matters** — an attention/engagement mechanism more than an extrinsic-incentive one. This abstract-level page (full text not retrieved) sits beside the motivational-interviewing literature, which formalizes that "spend time engaging the patient" intuition into a structured counseling method.

## Key Contributions

- First SR focused on **adherence-improving interventions specifically in adolescent (12–18 y) orthodontic patients**.
- Adherence defined across four domains: information recall, appointment attendance, self-reported oral-hygiene behavior, and clinical oral-hygiene indexes.
- Exposed the thinness of the evidence base — only 4 RCTs — and catalogued the direction of effect for each intervention type.

## Methodology

- **Design**: systematic review of RCTs; narrative synthesis (no meta-analysis, due to intervention/outcome heterogeneity).
- **Sources**: Medline via OVID (1 Jan 1966 – 1 Mar 2012), EMBASE, Cochrane CENTRAL (inception – Mar 2012), plus hand search.
- **Screening**: 381 articles identified → 21 met inclusion at title/abstract screening (all authors) → full texts retrieved → **4 RCTs** finally included.
- **Interventions (one per trial)**: (1) rewards/awards system; (2) Hawthorne effect; (3) written information; (4) demonstration of the microbiology of plaque.

## Results

- **3 of 4 interventions improved adherence**: Hawthorne effect, written information, and plaque-microbiology demonstration.
- **Exception**: the **reward/award** intervention was **not** associated with improved adherence.
- **No single best method** could be identified — each intervention was tested in only one trial, and outcomes were heterogeneous.
- **Clinical bottom line**: the value lies in **spending time with patients to illustrate why adherence matters**; extrinsic rewards alone did not deliver. Authors recommend future trials use multiple adherence-assessment methods together (self-report + behavioral observation + clinical-index change) and compare intervention types head-to-head.
