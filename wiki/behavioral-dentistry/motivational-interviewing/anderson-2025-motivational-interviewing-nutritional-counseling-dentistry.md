## Summary
This narrative review extends the MI evidence into a concrete indication — **nutritional/dietary counseling** delivered by dental hygienists — and frames diet as a shared oral-and-systemic risk lever. From 122 screened articles, 6 were included and sorted into MI use by provider type/setting (4) and MI application for a population/condition (2). The practical output is the **NRACT (Nutritional Risk Assessment and Counseling Tool)**, positioned as a brief-MI instrument fitting time-limited dental visits. Because only the abstract is held, effect sizes and study-level detail are not extractable here.

## Key Contributions
- Specifies MI for the nutritional-counseling indication within oral health care.
- Surfaces NRACT as a structured brief-MI tool for dental teams.
- Links dietary behavior change to both oral and systemic health outcomes.

## Methodology
- PRISMA-structured narrative review; databases: Ovid Medline ALL, CINAHL, Scopus, Web of Science; screened in Rayyan by 2 reviewers.
- 122 articles → 6 included (4 provider-type/setting; 2 population/condition).

## Results
- MI training improved provider confidence/interest in nutritional counseling.
- Brief MI showed effectiveness in dietary behavior change (narrative; no pooled effect available from abstract).
