## One-line Summary
Feasibility study (Scotland) of ENGAGE, a <5-min chairside lifestyle-risk intervention (smoking/alcohol/diet) with NHS-helpline signposting — patients accept being asked (<10% offended); 17/18 GDPs found it deliverable in a routine check-up.

## 한줄요약
스코틀랜드 ENGAGE 실행가능성 연구 — 검진 중 5분 이내 생활습관(흡연·음주·식이) 위험 전달 + 무료 NHS 상담전화 연결. 환자 거부감 거의 없음(<10%), GDP 18명 중 17명이 실행 가능·현행 개선으로 평가.

## Summary
ENGAGE expands the dental visit from oral hygiene to **general lifestyle-risk counseling**, but keeps the burden low: communicate risk + hand a leaflet signposting to free NHS helplines, in under five minutes during a routine check-up. The study tests **acceptance, not yet effectiveness**. A central finding dismantles the common clinician assumption that patients resent such questions: fewer than 10% would be offended, over 70% would feel reassured by the professionalism. GDPs corroborated feasibility — 17 of 18 judged it deliverable and an improvement over current practice. Effectiveness for actual behavior change is deferred to a planned multi-centre UK trial.

## Key Contributions
- Provides an acceptance/feasibility base for opportunistic general-health promotion in dental primary care.
- Offers a sustainable low-burden model (risk-communication + signposting) rather than full counseling.
- Quantifies — and largely refutes — the perceived-offense barrier clinicians assume.

## Methodology
- Study 1 (patients): N=1000 invited from NHS database; 200 completed online survey (52% male; 90% visited dentist in prior 12 months).
- Study 2 (GDPs): 50 invited to training workshop (first 20 enrolled), implement with next 20 check-up patients, audit, then survey. 18 GDPs (9/14 health boards) delivered to 335 patients (~18 each).

## Results
- Patients: <15% recalled being asked about smoking/alcohol/diet last visit; <10% would be offended if asked; >70% reassured by professionalism; <4% offended by an NHS-helpline leaflet.
- GDPs: 17/18 agreed ENGAGE is deliverable in a check-up, an improvement on current practice, and may shift patient thinking/behavior.
- Conclusion: feasible in Scottish dental primary care; effectiveness to be tested in a UK multi-centre trial.
