## One-line Summary
Systematic review + meta-analysis of 18 studies (n=2,132) finding a pooled body dysmorphic disorder prevalence of 32.7% (95% CI 23.6–41.7) among rhinoplasty candidates, varying by diagnostic tool and region.

## Summary
Nabavizadeh and colleagues pooled 18 studies (n=2,132) to estimate how common body dysmorphic disorder (BDD) is among people seeking rhinoplasty — the esthetic procedure most frequently pursued by BDD patients. The pooled prevalence was 32.7% (95% CI 23.6–41.7), roughly an order of magnitude above general-population estimates, but with very high heterogeneity (I²≈96%). Prevalence depended strongly on the screening instrument: the structured BDD-YBOCS returned 47.3% versus lower questionnaire-based figures, a difference confirmed by meta-regression. The clinical message is patient-selection–oriented: a substantial minority of esthetic-procedure candidates carry undiagnosed BDD, for whom surgery tends to produce residual dissatisfaction and repeat-procedure seeking rather than resolution, so preoperative psychological screening is warranted.

## Key Contributions
- First meta-analytic pooled BDD prevalence in rhinoplasty candidates (32.7%).
- Demonstrates the diagnostic-instrument effect (BDD-YBOCS > questionnaire), so reported prevalence is partly tool-dependent.
- Maps heterogeneity by region, sample size, and study quality via subgroup and meta-regression analysis.
- Provides quantitative backing for routine preoperative psychological screening in esthetic care.

## Methodology
PRISMA + MOOSE systematic review. Seven databases searched to August 2022; 12,999 records screened to 18 included studies (n=2,132). Random-effects meta-analysis in STATA v16; heterogeneity via I². Subgroup analyses on study year, age, gender, WHO region, sociodemographic index, country, sample size, and diagnostic tool; meta-regression to locate heterogeneity sources.

## Results
Pooled BDD prevalence 32.7% (95% CI 23.6–41.7; I²=96.45%). Higher in European studies (42.8%), samples ≥100 (40.2%), BDD-YBOCS tool (47.3%), and quality ≥6 (34.2%). Meta-regression: BDD-YBOCS vs questionnaire β=0.23 (95% CI 0.04–0.43; p=0.022). Primary-study range 7%–53%.

## Clinical Application (Dentistry)
- Esthetic dentistry (veneers, smile design, anterior prosthetics) draws the same appearance-driven motivation as rhinoplasty; a meaningful subset of these patients may have BDD and will remain dissatisfied regardless of technical success.
- Watch for red flags: minor/imagined defect preoccupation, repeated requests for redo work, prior multiple esthetic procedures, disproportionate distress. Consider a brief screening question set and psychological referral before irreversible esthetic treatment.
- BDD-driven dissatisfaction is an upstream driver of the esthetic-procedure complaint/litigation pattern documented in the medico-legal cluster — patient selection is risk management.
- [미검증] No direct prevalence figure exists for esthetic *dental* candidates; the 32.7% is rhinoplasty-specific and transfers only by analogy.
