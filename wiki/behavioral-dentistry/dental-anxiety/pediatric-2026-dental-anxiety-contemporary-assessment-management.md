## Summary
This review turns the dental-anxiety subcategory from measurement into **management**. It pairs guidance on selecting validated pediatric anxiety instruments — whose applicability varies by age and cognitive development — with a catalog of non-pharmacological strategies: tell–show–do, modeling, parental guidance, audiovisual distraction, and VR-based immersive distraction, all reported as consistently effective for reducing anxiety and improving cooperation. Its forward-looking emphasis is on **emerging digital/immersive distraction** as a modern direction. The thesis is that structured assessment combined with a multimodal behavioral strategy improves both treatment outcomes and clinical efficiency. As a narrative review it gives no pooled effects, and the VR evidence base is still maturing.

## Key Contributions
- Couples assessment-tool selection with a non-pharmacological management catalog.
- Foregrounds VR/immersive distraction as an emerging modality.
- Frames structured assessment + multimodal strategy as an efficiency lever, not just comfort.

## Methodology
- Structured literature search (studies, SRs, guidelines); emphasis on validated scales, communication, environmental adaptation, digital interventions.

## Results
- Multiple validated instruments exist; age-appropriate selection required.
- Tell–show–do, modeling, parental guidance, AV distraction, VR consistently reduce anxiety/improve cooperation.
- Structured assessment + multimodal strategy enhances outcomes and chair efficiency.
