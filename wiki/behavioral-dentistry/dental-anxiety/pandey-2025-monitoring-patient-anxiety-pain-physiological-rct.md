## One-line Summary
Observational study (n=76 adults, single-visit root canal treatment) — MDAS anxiety + VAS pain with HR/SpO2 at 6 stages; HR rose significantly at local anesthesia (93.9±8.2) and drilling (94.7±8.07), esp. with moderate anxiety/severe pain (p<0.05); prior negative experience raised anxiety (p=0.018); moderate anxiety–HR correlation, SpO2 stable.

## 한줄요약

## Summary
Pandey et al. give the dental-anxiety subcategory an **adult, objective-physiology** anchor: rather than rating scales alone, they pair MDAS and VAS with heart rate and SpO2 monitored across six stages of single-visit root canal treatment. Heart rate rose significantly at the two highest-trigger moments — **local anesthesia** and **drilling** — and most in patients with moderate anxiety and severe pain (p<0.05), while SpO2 stayed stable. A moderate positive anxiety–HR correlation links the subjective and physiological, and **prior negative dental experience** predicted higher anxiety (p=0.018) — a concrete screening cue. The key interpretive caveat: HR during anesthesia is partly driven by the vasoconstrictor (epinephrine), so the physiological signal is not purely anxiety. Clinical message: screen for anxiety/prior bad experiences and monitor vitals, especially at injection and drilling.

## Key Contributions
- Couples self-reported anxiety with objective HR across the procedure timeline.
- Localizes peak stress to local anesthesia and drilling.
- Confirms prior negative experience as an anxiety amplifier (actionable at intake).

## Methodology
- Observational, n=76 (20–55y, mean 35.6±8.9). MDAS, VAS; HR + SpO2 at preoperative, local anesthesia, access cavity, working-length, biomechanical prep, 10-min post.
- Descriptive stats, Pearson's correlation, Mann–Whitney.

## Results
- Moderate pain (VAS 4–6) in 47.36%.
- Significant HR rise at local anesthesia (93.9±8.2) and drilling (94.7±8.07), esp. moderate-anxiety/severe-pain (p<0.05).
- SpO2 stable; moderate positive anxiety–HR correlation; prior negative experience → higher anxiety (p=0.018).
