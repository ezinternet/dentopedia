## One-line Summary
In-vitro contact-profilometry study (n = 90 bovine enamel, 6 groups, 7-day acid challenge) showing
citrus sweets cause 1.3–2.4 μm erosive enamel wear; Fini Diet (pH 3.3, lactic+citric, 2.4 μm) and
Fini Regaliz Acid Tubes (pH 3.1, maleic+citric, 2.2 μm) equalled 0.1% citric acid and exceeded
Coca-Cola (pH 2.6, 1.4 μm), refuting the assumption that pH alone predicts erosive rank.

## Summary
This in-vitro study (CRIS-compliant) assessed erosive enamel wear of five commercially available
citrus sweets compared with 0.1% citric acid (standard control) and Coca-Cola using contact
profilometry on bovine enamel samples. Sweets were dissolved (40 g/250 mL deionised water) and
samples subjected to 4 × 90 s immersions daily for 7 days with artificial-saliva remineralisation
between cycles. All sweets caused measurable enamel wear (1.3–2.4 μm). Importantly, Fini Diet
(pH 3.3, lactic+citric) and Fini Regaliz Acid Tubes (pH 3.1, maleic+citric) caused wear
statistically equivalent to the 0.1% citric acid control (2.3 μm) and significantly greater than
Coca-Cola (1.4 μm), despite having higher pH values. This challenges the clinical assumption
that lower pH equals greater erosive risk and highlights acid combination and type as key determinants.
The findings have major public health relevance given that children — the primary consumers of
such sweets — already show 30–50% erosion prevalence in permanent and deciduous dentition.

## Key Contributions
- Citrus sweets can be MORE erosive than phosphoric-acid cola despite higher pH values.
- Dual-acid combinations (citric+lactic or citric+maleic) produce greater wear than single acids.
- Citric acid contribution: both direct H+ release (at three pKa steps: 3.1, 4.74, 6.42) and
  calcium chelation by citrate ion (dominant at pH > 4).
- Erosive risk of sweets (OR 2.24) exceeds that of soft drinks (OR 1.61) for ETW in children.
- Sweet adhesion to tooth surfaces (not modelled) would further amplify erosive effect in vivo.

## Methodology
- Design: In-vitro controlled acid-challenge, CRIS-compliant.
- Specimens: n = 90 bovine incisor enamel samples, polished baseline, nail-varnish control areas.
- Groups (n = 15 each): 0.1% citric acid pH 2.5; Coca-Cola pH 2.6; Fini Diet pH 3.3 (lactic+
  citric); Fini Kisses pH 3.5 (citric+lactic); Fini Bubblegum pH 2.6 (maleic); Fini Regaliz pH 3.1
  (maleic+citric). Sweets dissolved 40 g/250 mL deionised water.
- Challenge: 4 × 90 s/day immersion (30 mL, 25°C) + 2 h artificial saliva remineralisation; 7 days.
- Profilometry: Mahr contact profilometer (Göttingen, Germany); 5 scans × 6 mm; wear =
  final – baseline step height (μm); detection limit 0.1 μm.
- Statistics: Kruskal-Wallis + Dunn post hoc; p < 0.0001.

## Results
Enamel wear medians [IQR] in μm:
- 0.1% citric acid: 2.3 [0.7] — reference
- Coca-Cola (phosphoric, pH 2.6): 1.4 [0.5]
- Fini Diet (lactic+citric, pH 3.3): 2.4 [1.2] — highest; ns vs citric acid; > Coca-Cola (p < 0.0001)
- Fini Kisses (citric+lactic, pH 3.5): 1.4 [0.9] — comparable to Coca-Cola
- Fini Bubblegum (maleic, pH 2.6): 1.3 [0.3] — lowest; comparable to Coca-Cola
- Fini Regaliz Acid Tubes (maleic+citric, pH 3.1): 2.2 [0.5] — ns vs citric acid; > Coca-Cola

Key: acid-type combination (citric+lactic or maleic+citric) drives erosive outcome more than pH.
