## Summary
This controlled in-vitro study assessed five commercially available soft drinks for their erosive
potential on human enamel using pH measurement, titratable acidity (TA) titration, and diamond
stylus profilometry (Mitutoyo SJ-301). Ninety-six enamel samples from 24 unerupted third molars
were divided into six groups and subjected to 30 immersions over 10 days at three exposure
durations (15, 30, 60 min). Coca-Cola (pH 2.67, phosphoric acid) showed the greatest roughness
increase at 15 min (Ra 1.49 μm vs. control 0.67 μm), but orange juice (pH 3.73, citric acid,
TA 5.70 mL NaOH) reached equivalent roughness at 30 and 60 min due to its high acid reservoir.
Cedevita and Guarana (both citric acid-based) showed similar, intermediate erosion despite
differing pH (3.49 vs. 2.52), illustrating that TA modifies erosive outcome. Strawberry yoghurt
did not erode enamel at any time-point, consistent with calcium/phosphate supersaturation.

## Key Contributions
- Demonstrated that both pH and titratable acidity (TA) must be considered to predict erosive potential.
- Quantified enamel roughness (Ra, Rq, Rz, Ry) at three clinically relevant exposure durations.
- Orange juice (citric acid, highest TA 5.70 mL NaOH) matched phosphoric-acid cola erosion at
  30–60 min, despite a significantly higher initial pH (3.73 vs. 2.67).
- Confirmed yoghurt's protective role via calcium/phosphate supersaturation despite sub-critical pH.
- Provided exposure-time dose-response data relevant to counselling patients on drinking habits.

## Methodology
- Design: In-vitro controlled acid-challenge experiment.
- Specimens: n = 96 human enamel samples (24 unerupted third molars, 4 cuts/tooth), embedded in resin.
- Groups (n = 18/drink, 6/time-point): Coca-Cola (pH 2.67), orange juice (pH 3.73), Cedevita
  (pH 3.49), Guarana (pH 2.52), strawberry yoghurt (pH 4.21), saliva control (n = 6).
- Protocol: 50 mL drink, 3×/day, 10 days = 30 immersions; between immersions stored in filtered saliva.
- Profilometer: Mitutoyo Surftest SJ-301, diamond stylus; parameters Ra, Rq, Rz, Ry.
- Stats: one-way ANOVA, Tukey post hoc, Student-Newman-Keuls; p < 0.05.

## Results
- All drinks had initial pH < 5.5 (critical dissolution threshold for hydroxyapatite).
- Coca-Cola: Ra 1.49 ± 0.08 μm at 15 min (highest), 1.82 ± 0.01 μm at 60 min.
- Orange juice: Ra 1.27 ± 0.01 μm at 15 min, 1.78 ± 0.07 μm at 60 min (ns vs Coca-Cola at 30 & 60 min).
- Guarana (pH 2.52, citric acid): Ra 1.07 ± 0.02 μm at 15 min — lower than Coca-Cola despite lower pH,
  likely due to tannins/flavonoids with potential protective effect.
- Cedevita ≈ Guarana at all time-points (similar TA despite pH 3.49 vs 2.52).
- Strawberry yoghurt: Ra 0.67 ± 0.02–0.68 ± 0.05 μm (not significantly different from control at any time).
- Roughness increased with time for all erosive drinks (Coca-Cola Ra: 1.49 → 1.63 → 1.82 μm).
