## One-line Summary

Prospective RCT (n=11, 60 restorations, ≤3 years) comparing CAD-CAM lithium disilicate vs composite resin ultrathin occlusal veneers for severe dental erosion: 100% vs 84.7% Kaplan-Meier survival (P=.124, NS), with significantly greater surface degradation in the composite resin group (P=.003).

## 한줄요약

전향적 무작위대조시험(Randomized Controlled Trial, RCT) (n=11명, 60개 수복물, 최대 3년 추적): 중증 치아 침식증(dental erosion)에 CAD-CAM 초박형 교합면 비니어(occlusal veneer) — 세라믹(e.max CAD) 생존율(survival rate) 100% vs 복합레진(composite resin, Lava Ultimate) 84.7% (P=.124, 유의 차이 없음), 레진군 표면 조도(surface roughness) 유의하게 악화 (P=.003).

## Summary

This prospective RCT by Schlichting et al. is the first medium-term randomized comparison of CAD-CAM ceramic (IPS e.max CAD, lithium disilicate) versus CAD-CAM composite resin (Lava Ultimate, resin nanoceramic) ultrathin occlusal veneers (≤1 mm) as a conservative treatment for severely eroded posterior teeth. Eleven participants (mean age 30.4 years) received 60 restorations (24 ceramic, 36 composite resin) with material assignment randomized per tooth. All restorations were placed using immediate dentin sealing (IDS, OptiBond FL) and adhesive luting with preheated composite resin (Filtek Z100). Participants were evaluated with modified USPHS criteria at baseline and annually for up to 3 years.

No complete failures occurred in either group. Five chipping events (partial failures, all restorable) occurred exclusively in the composite resin group. Kaplan-Meier survival was 100% for ceramic versus 84.7% for composite resin — a clinically meaningful but statistically non-significant difference (P=.124), likely due to underpowering at n=11. Surface roughness degradation was significantly greater in the composite resin group (P=.003), indicating faster material aging in the oral environment.

The authors conclude that both materials provide clinically comparable medium-term outcomes, with the caveat that composite resin shows more surface degradation and a trend toward more chipping.

## Key Contributions

1. **First medium-term RCT** comparing CAD-CAM ceramic vs composite resin ultrathin occlusal veneers in severe dental erosion.
2. **No complete losses** in either group at ≤3 years; confirms ultrathin occlusal veneers as a viable, conservative treatment modality.
3. **Survival gap**: 100% (ceramic) vs 84.7% (composite resin) — clinically relevant, though not statistically significant with n=11.
4. **Surface degradation**: composite resin veneers exhibited significantly greater surface roughness increase (P=.003), indicating concern for long-term material aging.
5. Validates **IDS + adhesive luting** protocol as a reliable bonding strategy for ultrathin posterior restorations.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Prospective parallel-group RCT; randomized per tooth (not per participant)
- **Sample**: 11 participants, mean age 30.4 years, severe dental erosion
- **Restorations**: 24 ceramic (e.max CAD, IvoclarVivadent) + 36 composite resin (Lava Ultimate, 3M ESPE)
- **Preparation**: Minimal/no preparation, trial-restoration driven; immediate dentin sealing (IDS) with OptiBond FL
- **Ceramic surface treatment**: Hydrofluoric acid etching + silane
- **Composite resin surface treatment**: Airborne-particle abrasion + silane
- **Cementation**: Preheated composite resin (Filtek Z100) for all restorations
- **Outcome criteria**: Modified USPHS (scores 1–5; 4 = partial failure, 5 = complete failure)
- **Primary outcome**: Time to failure; Kaplan-Meier survival analysis + log-rank test
- **Secondary**: Surface roughness (Wilcoxon/Mann-Whitney)

## Results

| Outcome | e.max CAD (ceramic) | Lava Ultimate (composite resin) | P-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| N restorations | 24 | 36 | — |
| Complete failures | 0 | 0 | — |
| Partial failures (chipping) | 0 | 5 (score 4, restorable) | — |
| Kaplan-Meier survival (3y) | 100% | 84.7% (SE 0.065%) | .124 (NS) |
| Surface roughness degradation | Minimal | Significant | .003 |

- All partial failures were chipping events in the composite resin group, all restorable (score 4)
- No restoration was completely lost
- Surface roughness increase was significantly higher in composite resin — consistent with material fatigue and aging in the wet oral environment
