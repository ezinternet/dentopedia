## One-line Summary

Retrospective cohort (206 patients, 395 implant-crown↔natural-tooth contact points, 2015–2025, Cox regression): PCL incidence 67.5% patient-level / 40.3% contact-level; independent predictors were food impaction (HR 2.75), residual ridge height discrepancy (HR 2.51), and severe adjacent alveolar bone loss >3/8 root length (HR 5.83). Demographic/behavioral/prosthetic factors were not significant.

## Summary

Liu et al. (2025) retrospectively followed 206 patients with single implant-supported crowns placed adjacent to natural teeth (395 interproximal contact points, June 2015–June 2025) and used time-to-event Cox proportional hazards modeling to identify independent risk factors for proximal contact loss (PCL). PCL was common — 67.5% of patients and 40.3% of contact points — confirming PCL as a frequent, progressive complication. Crucially, on multivariate adjustment only three **local anatomic/prosthetic** factors remained independent predictors: **food impaction (HR 2.75)**, **residual ridge height discrepancy (HR 2.51)**, and **advanced alveolar bone loss in the adjacent tooth (>3/8 root length, HR 5.83)**. All demographic, behavioral, occlusal, and prosthetic variables (sex, age, smoking, alcohol, diabetes, hypertension, bruxism, jaw arch, free-end span, restoration format, opposing dentition) were non-significant. Mesial contacts accounted for 87% of PCL cases but lost statistical significance after adjustment (likely collinearity with the anatomic factors).

## Key Contributions

- Large-cohort quantification of PCL: 67.5% patient-level, 40.3% contact-level incidence — consistent with the 30–58% literature range.
- Time-to-event Cox modeling isolates **independent** predictors beyond cross-sectional association.
- Identifies **residual ridge height discrepancy** (vertical crest difference >1 mm between implant platform and adjacent tooth) as an under-recognized anatomic risk factor (HR 2.51).
- Severe adjacent **periodontal bone loss** (>3/8 root) carries the largest effect (HR 5.83), linking periodontal support to interproximal contact stability.
- Reinforces that **local biomechanics dominate** — patient/systemic/prosthetic factors do not predict PCL, implying universal surveillance regardless of patient profile.

## Methodology

- Single-center retrospective cohort, contact-point as unit of analysis.
- Inclusion: single implant crown, osseointegration (insertion torque ≥35 N·cm), baseline intact contacts + periodontal health; excluded Stage III/IV periodontitis, adjacent mobility ≥Grade II, heavy smokers, bone-metabolism-affecting disease/meds.
- PCL diagnosis: floss-resistance test (25-µm floss / 8-µm shim-stock passes without resistance) and/or radiographic separation ≥0.2 mm. PCL gap quantified by 50 µm shim-stock count.
- Bone support = (CEJ→crest)/(CEJ→apex) ratio on standardized periapical radiographs.
- Univariate log-rank → variables P<0.05 → multivariate Cox proportional hazards (SPSS 26.0); 20% double-examiner reliability.

## Results

Multivariate independent predictors:

| Factor | HR | 95% CI | P |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Food impaction** (present) | 2.75 | 1.70–4.58 | <0.001 |
| **Residual ridge height discrepancy** (present) | 2.51 | 1.37–4.58 | 0.003 |
| **Alveolar bone loss >3/8 root** | 5.83 | 1.05–32.33 | 0.044 |
| Bone loss 2/8–3/8 (ns) | 2.48 | 0.57–12.12 | 0.244 |
| Distal contact (ns) | 1.46 | 0.73–2.95 | 0.288 |
| Edentulism ≥5 y (ns) | 0.67 | 0.41–1.11 | 0.115 |
| Plunger cusp (ns) | 1.75 | 0.92–3.32 | 0.087 |

- Incidence: 67.5% patient-level (139/206); 40.3% contact-level (159/395).
- 87% of PCL cases at mesial contacts (physiologic mesial drift vs implant immobility), significant in univariate (χ²=38.00, P<0.001) but not multivariate.
- Food impaction and PCL form a **bidirectional, self-perpetuating** loop (impaction is both a sequela and an aggravator of contact loss).
- Clinical recommendation: contact check at 3 months then semiannually; optimize adjacent periodontal status pre-prosthetically; consider ridge augmentation / prosthetic contour modification for vertical deficiency; enameloplasty, occlusal splints, or interproximal restorations for high-risk sites.
