## Summary
This PRISMA-compliant, OSF-registered systematic review with single-arm meta-analysis quantified the prevalence of interproximal contact loss (ICL) between implant restorations and adjacent natural teeth. Fifteen studies published 2014–2023, all in posterior regions, met eligibility; seven reported ICL above 20%. Pooled proportions showed mesial ICL of 44.2% (95% CI 30.6–58.6%) and distal ICL of 27.5% (95% CI 10.5–55.0%), confirming a clear mesial predominance. Between-study heterogeneity was very high (I²=87.8%, 95% CI 75.9–93.8%), reflecting differences in measurement method and follow-up. No significant differences were found by age, gender, or arch location. This is the most precise quantitative anchor in the category for the mesial-versus-distal ICL split.

## Key Contributions
- First single-arm meta-analysis to pool mesial vs distal ICL with 95% CIs.
- Quantifies mesial predominance (44.2% vs 27.5%).
- Confirms no age, gender, or arch effect at the meta-analytic level.
- All evidence confined to posterior regions, defining the clinical scope.

## Methodology
Systematic review + single-arm meta-analysis of proportions, PRISMA-compliant, registered on OSF (10.17605/OSF.IO/2S95Y). PICO: prevalence of ICL between implant restorations and adjacent teeth. Four main electronic databases plus manual search to February 2023. Qualitative risk-of-bias assessment; proportion meta-analysis for mesial vs distal and total ICL.

## Results
- 15 eligible studies (2014–2023); 7 with ICL >20%; all posterior.
- Mesial ICL: 44.2% (95% CI 30.6–58.6%).
- Distal ICL: 27.5% (95% CI 10.5–55.0%).
- Heterogeneity: I²=87.8% (95% CI 75.9–93.8%).
- No significant difference by age, gender, or arch location.
