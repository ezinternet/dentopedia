Systematic review and meta-analysis of PCL prevalence between implant-supported fixed prostheses and adjacent teeth and its associated factors, confirming PCL as a frequent postoperative complication with mesial predominance.

## Summary
This systematic review and meta-analysis evaluated the prevalence of proximal contact loss (PCL) between implant-supported fixed prostheses and adjacent teeth and its associated factors. A bibliographic search (June 2021, updated January 2022) with no date or language limits identified eligible clinical studies, which were pooled as proportions using Comprehensive Meta-Analysis software with JBI-MAStARI quality appraisal. Heterogeneity was assessed with Q-statistic and I², and a random-effects model was applied where heterogeneity was significant. PCL emerged as one of the most frequent postoperative complications of implant fixed prostheses, again showing a mesial-contact predominance. The exact pooled prevalence is reported in the article's forest plots and should be transcribed from the source figures (see note below).

## Key Contributions
- An independent SR+MA of PCL prevalence, separate in team and search from Sheba 2023.
- Formal quantitative pooling of proportions with heterogeneity statistics (Q, I²).
- Subgroup synthesis of associated factors.

## Methodology
Systematic review + meta-analysis of proportions. Search June 2021, updated January 2022, no date/language restriction. Quality appraisal with JBI-MAStARI; pooling in Comprehensive Meta-Analysis v2.2. Heterogeneity via Q-statistic and I²; fixed-effect model when heterogeneity non-significant (P≥0.05), otherwise random-effects. Funnel plots assessed publication bias.

## Results
- PCL synthesized as a frequent postoperative complication; mesial predominance consistent with other reviews.
- High between-study heterogeneity (random-effects context).
- [미검증] Exact pooled prevalence (%) is figure-based and was not machine-extractable from the text layer — verify against the original forest plot before citing a precise number.
