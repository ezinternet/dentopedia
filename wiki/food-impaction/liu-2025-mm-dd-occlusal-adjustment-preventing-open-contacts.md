## One-line Summary
Retrospective single-practice study (124 single-tooth posterior implants, 100 patients, 2–21.5 yr): the MM-DD occlusal adjustment — at crown delivery, selectively relieving the mesial occlusal surface of the mesial adjacent tooth and the distal occlusal surface of the distal adjacent tooth to let adjacent teeth drift mesially and keep the contact closed — reduced open-contact incidence from 46.7% (conventional control, n=49) to 2.7% (MM-DD, n=75; p<0.001), with Kaplan–Meier contact survival 99.1%/97.5%/96.2% at 5/10/15 yr.

## Summary
Open proximal contacts adjacent to posterior implants are common (literature 34–66%, mandible-predominant) and lead to food impaction, peri-implant inflammation, proximal bone loss, and lower satisfaction. The root cause is a biomechanical mismatch: PDL-supported natural teeth keep drifting mesially under function, while an osseointegrated implant is ankylosed and cannot follow — so a gap opens on the implant's adjacent surfaces. Existing management (splinting, periodic recontouring, over-contoured proximal surfaces) is reactive and inconsistent over the long term.

Liu's **MM-DD (mesial–mesial/distal–distal) technique** flips this to a preventive strategy applied **at crown delivery**: selectively relieve the **mesial occlusal surface of the mesial adjacent tooth** and the **distal occlusal surface of the distal adjacent tooth**, creating occlusal relief zones so the adjacent teeth are free to drift mesially toward the implant crown and keep the contact closed. The implant crown itself keeps a firm central-fossa contact with only light axial contact on adjacent teeth, minimizing lateral force on the implant.

In this single-operator retrospective series (124 single-tooth posterior implants in 100 patients, 2–21.5 yr; MM-DD n=75 vs conventional historical control n=49), open contacts occurred in only 2.7% of MM-DD restorations vs 46.7% of controls (p<0.001). Kaplan–Meier proximal-contact survival was 99.1%/97.5%/96.2% at 5/10/15 years for MM-DD vs 66.7%/53.3% at 5/10 years for controls. MM-DD cases also had far less food impaction, lower discomfort, higher satisfaction, and ~0.3 mm vs 0.9 mm 5-yr marginal bone change. No implants failed in either arm.

## Key Contributions
- **Preventive occlusal protocol, not a reactive repair**: the first long-term (≤21.5 yr) clinical evidence that adjusting *adjacent-tooth* occlusion at crown delivery prevents open contacts, rather than treating them after they form.
- **Defines MM-DD precisely**: relieve mesial-occlusal of mesial neighbor + distal-occlusal of distal neighbor; keep firm central-fossa contact on the implant crown, light axial contact on neighbors.
- **Large, durable effect**: 2.7% vs 46.7% open contacts (p<0.001); benefit sustained in the >10-yr subgroup (2.9% vs 60.0%).
- **Links open contact to measurable harm**: food impaction 86.7% vs 3.2%, +0.83 mm proximal bone loss, +1.1 mm probing depth at adjacent teeth (all p<0.001).
- **Risk targeting**: highest-yield in second molars, older patients, mandibular molars (stronger mesial drift).

## Methodology
- Retrospective, single private practice, single operator (CLL), 2000–2022; IRB Kaohsiung Medical University Hospital. Non-randomized **historical control** (conventional adjustment, 2000–2005).
- 100 patients / 124 single-unit posterior implant crowns (28 max premolar, 32 max molar, 18 mand premolar, 46 mand molar); 124 mesial + 110 distal contacts. MM-DD n=75, control n=49.
- Implants 3.5–6.0 mm Ø, 8.5–13 mm, moderately rough, screw/cement-retained, PFM/zirconia/lithium disilicate.
- Contact integrity graded tight/normal/open by floss resistance + 21 μm articulating paper; PROs (food impaction, VAS discomfort 0–10, 5-pt Likert satisfaction); annual standardized periapical radiographs; periodontal probing/BoP/mobility. Follow-up 2 wk → 3 mo → 6 mo → annual.
- Kaplan–Meier for contact survival; chi-square/Fisher, t-test/Mann–Whitney; multivariate regression. α=0.05; post-hoc power >80% for ≥20% difference.

## Results
| Outcome | MM-DD (n=75) | Conventional control (n=49) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Open-contact incidence | 2.7% (2 sites) | 46.7% (23 sites) | <0.001 |
| Open contacts, >10-yr subgroup | 2.9% | 60.0% | <0.001 |
| Mean time to open contact | 8.3 ± 3.4 yr | 3.6 ± 1.8 yr | — |
| KM contact survival 5 / 10 / 15 yr | 99.1% / 97.5% / 96.2% | 66.7% / 53.3% / — | — |
| 5-yr marginal bone change | <0.3 mm | 0.9 mm | <0.05 |
| Food impaction (group VAS) | 0.8 ± 0.6 | 4.7 ± 1.1 | <0.001 |
| Satisfaction (Likert) | 4.7 | 2.5 | <0.001 |

- Open vs closed contact (pooled): food impaction 86.7% vs 3.2%; discomfort VAS 4.7 vs 0.8; satisfaction 2.5 vs 4.7; +0.83 mm proximal bone loss; +1.1 mm probing depth (all p<0.001).
- Location: 1.9% maxillary vs 2.8% mandibular posterior. Second molars 3.4% > first molars 2.6% > premolars 2.0% (p=0.021). Younger (24–40 yr) 1.8% vs >60 yr 2.9% (p=0.042). No effect of sex or restorative material.
- **No implant failures** in either group.
- Salvage for the residual 2.7%: proximal recontouring, MM-DD re-adjustment, crown replacement with better contours, or watch-and-wait if mild/asymptomatic; early intervention better.
- **Caveats**: single-clinician retrospective with historical (non-randomized) control; single-tooth posterior only; no digital occlusal analysis/FEA. Authors call for prospective multi-center RCTs.
