## One-line Summary

Retrospective study (204 patients, 445 posterior implant prostheses, ≤10y; ICL = aluminum-strip interspace >50 μm): patient-level ICL 59.8%, mesial 40.0% > distal 24.1%; mesial independent risk factors = food impaction (OR 4.99), function duration, adjacent-tooth status (implant/crown), bruxism; distal = mechanical complications (OR 7.04), peri-implantitis, food impaction, bruxism.

## Summary

Single-center Chinese retrospective study quantifying interproximal contact loss (ICL) between posterior implant-supported prostheses and adjacent teeth. ICL was defined objectively as an aluminum-strip-measured interspace exceeding 50 μm (graded 20–100 μm strips, refining Pang's protocol). Among 204 patients (445 implants, 565 contact sites, up to 10 years function), patient-level ICL reached 59.8%, higher than the ~43.5% pooled estimate from prior meta-analyses — partly because this study reports both patient- and site-level prevalence in one cohort. ICL was markedly more common mesial (40.0%) than distal (24.1%), a ≈1.66-fold difference attributed to physiologic mesial drift of natural teeth against the ankylosed (non-drifting) implant. Prevalence rose with function duration. Critically, mesial and distal contacts had different risk-factor profiles, and no factor was significant at the patient level.

## Key Contributions

- Dual-level prevalence (patient 59.8%; mesial 40.0%, distal 24.1%) in a single cohort, resolving the patient-vs-site heterogeneity of earlier studies.
- Separate mesial vs distal logistic models — the risk-factor sets diverge.
- Objective graded-aluminum-strip measurement (>50 μm = ICL).
- Adds bruxism, mechanical complications, and peri-implantitis to the ICL risk-factor literature.

## Methodology

- Design: retrospective; 204 patients (111 M / 93 F), 445 implant prostheses placed 2011–2020 (204 maxilla / 241 mandible; 396 molar / 49 premolar; 289 single crowns / 69 bridges); 565 contact sites (345 mesial, 220 distal); ≥1 year function (≤10y).
- ICL = aluminum strips (20–100 μm) inserted to resistance; recorded interspace >50 μm.
- Exam: probing, peri-implantitis (BOP + bone loss >2 mm), Jemt Papilla Index, occlusion type (200 μm paper), Tooth Wear Index, adjacent-tooth/endodontic status, food impaction, mechanical complications; parallel-X-ray bone loss. Questionnaire for bruxism/mastication/smoking/diabetes/maintenance.
- Chi-square/U tests → logistic regression + multivariate linear regression. SPSS 24.0, α=0.05.

## Results

- **Prevalence:** patient 59.8% (122/204); mesial 40.0% (138/345) vs distal 24.1% (53/220); mesial interspace thicker (P<0.001); rises with function duration (mesial 37.1% by 4y, distal 25.3% by 2y).
- **Patient level:** NO factor significant (age, gender, diabetes, smoking, maintenance, bruxism, unilateral mastication all NS) — ICL is a site phenomenon, not a patient-type phenomenon.
- **Mesial independent risk factors (logistic):** food impaction (OR 4.991, P<0.001), function duration (OR 1.299/yr, P<0.001), adjacent-tooth status (implant/crown OR 4.062, P=0.042; implant/implant OR 0.314, protective), bruxism (OR 2.098, P=0.034), full-mouth wear (OR 1.875, P=0.04), keratinized-gingiva width (OR 1.131, P=0.015). Linear interspace-thickness: external connection (B=34.927, P=0.003) ↑, gingival papilla recession (B=26.532, P=0.001) ↑, implant/implant (B=−34.241) protective.
- **Distal independent risk factors (logistic):** mechanical complications (OR 7.041, P<0.001), peri-implantitis (OR 2.385, P=0.021), food impaction (OR 2.809, P=0.002), bruxism (OR 2.356, P=0.019). Group-function occlusion significant univariate (P<0.001). Linear: mechanical complications (B=39.434, P=0.006), gingival papilla recession (B=38.150, P<0.001).
- **Mechanisms proposed:** mesial drift vs implant ankylosis (mesial>distal); implant/crown hard-hard friction + cement microleakage (crown worse than implant neighbor); distal sites dissipate occlusal stress poorly (reduced adjacent support); bruxism's supraphysiologic, sensory-feedback-deficient loading; ICL↔food-impaction/papilla-recession synergistic loop (sensitivity analysis: model fit dropped when these excluded).

## Clinical Takeaway

Expect ICL in a majority of posterior implant patients over a decade, concentrated on the mesial of the implant. Recall and proximal-contact checks should weight the mesial site and lengthen with function time. Bruxers, peri-implantitis cases, and prostheses with mechanical complications warrant closer distal-contact surveillance; the protective implant/implant adjacency and the worse implant/crown adjacency can inform sequencing/material choice. Retrospective, single-center, measurement-error caveats apply.
