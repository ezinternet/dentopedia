## Summary
This Korean retrospective study used cast-model analysis to identify causal factors for proximal contact loss (PCL) between posterior fixed implant prostheses and adjacent natural teeth. From 653 implants, 293 in 240 patients were analyzed against sex, implant site, jaw position, Angle's classification, anterior overbite, a pre-existing interproximal gap between the adjacent (mesial) natural teeth, generalized full-arch gap, and mandibular anterior crowding. Ninety sites (30.7%) showed PCL, and Kaplan–Meier analysis showed a gradual time-dependent increase with half of cases occurring by 19.2 months. A pre-existing interproximal gap between adjacent natural teeth was the dominant factor, significant on chi-square (P=0.002), logistic regression (OR 2.684, P=0.007), and GEE (OR 3.255, P=0.003); a generalized arch gap was also significant on chi-square (P=0.027). The work extends the PCL factor list toward patient-specific pre-existing dentition geometry.

## Key Contributions
- Cast-model quantification of patient-specific anatomy (pre-existing interproximal gap, generalized arch gap) as PCL predictors.
- Time-to-event data: half of PCL by 19.2 months (Kaplan–Meier).
- Consistent odds ratios across logistic and GEE models, confirming robustness of the pre-existing-gap factor.

## Methodology
Retrospective cast-model analysis. 293 of 653 posterior implants (240 patients) analyzed. Factors: sex, implant site, jaw position, Angle's classification, anterior overbite, pre-existing interproximal gap between adjacent natural teeth, generalized full-arch gap, mandibular anterior crowding. Analysis by chi-square, multivariate logistic regression, and multivariate GEE; PCL timing by Kaplan–Meier.

## Results
- PCL in 90/293 sites (30.7%).
- Kaplan–Meier: half of PCL cases by 19.2 months; gradual increase over time.
- Chi-square significant: pre-existing interproximal gap (P=0.002), generalized arch gap (P=0.027).
- Logistic regression: pre-existing interproximal gap OR 2.684 (P=0.007).
- GEE: pre-existing interproximal gap OR 3.255 (P=0.003).
