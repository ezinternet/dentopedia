## One-line Summary
Retrospective study (317 patients, posterior implant FDPs, up to 18 years): mesial PCL (27%) far exceeded distal (5%); functional years >5, frequent interdental-brush use, and food impaction were independent factors on multivariate GEE.

## Summary
This retrospective study evaluated proximal contact loss (PCL) between posterior implant fixed dental prostheses (FDPs) and adjacent teeth in 317 patients up to 18 years after delivery, assessing 19 candidate factors including contact tightness, oral hygiene, periodontal condition, and food impaction. The mesial contact-loss rate (27%) was significantly higher than the distal rate (5%), and PCL increased with functional time at both sites. Six factors were associated with mesial PCL on chi-square/univariate GEE — patient age, implant functional years, frequent interdental-brush use, splinting vs single implant, plunger cusp, and food impaction — but only functional years >5, frequent interdental-brush use, and food impaction remained significant on multivariate GEE. The study highlights modifiable, occlusion- and hygiene-related factors alongside the consistent mesial predominance.

## Key Contributions
- Broad 19-factor retrospective with the longest follow-up window (up to 18 years).
- Identifies modifiable independent factors (interdental-brush use, food impaction) via multivariate GEE.
- Names plunger cusp and food impaction as occlusal/masticatory contributors.

## Methodology
Retrospective cohort. 317 patients with posterior implant FDPs, up to 18 years post-delivery. Nineteen factors assessed (contact tightness degrees, oral hygiene, periodontal conditions, food impaction, etc.). Analysis by chi-square, univariate GEE, and multivariate GEE; mesial and distal contacts evaluated separately.

## Results
- Mesial contact loss 27% vs distal 5% (significant).
- PCL increased over functional time at both mesial and distal sites.
- Six factors on chi-square/univariate GEE: age, implant functional years, frequent interdental-brush use, splinting/single implant, plunger cusp, food impaction.
- Independent on multivariate GEE: functional years >5, frequent interdental-brush use, food impaction.
