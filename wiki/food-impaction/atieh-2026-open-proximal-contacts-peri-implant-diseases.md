## One-line Summary

SR+MA (5 non-randomized observational studies, 4882 implants, 1050 with open proximal contact): open proximal contacts significantly raised peri-implant mucositis incidence (RR 1.74, 95% CI 1.06–2.86, p=0.03) but MBL change (MD 0.07), PPD change (MD 0.11), and peri-implantitis (RR 1.63, p=0.12) were all non-significant.

## Summary

This systematic review and meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD420251089277) asked the outcome question that most prior open-proximal-contact literature left open: does open proximal contact (OPC) adjacent to implant restorations actually harm peri-implant tissues, beyond being a prevalent prosthetic nuisance? From 276 records, five non-randomized observational studies (Alhakeem/Iran, Byun/Korea, French/Canada, Ko/Taiwan, Latimer/USA; follow-up 24–84 months) pooling 4882 implants (1050 OPC) were analyzed at the implant level using random-effects RevMan, ROBINS-I V2, and GRADE.

The signal is narrow but real: OPC was a **statistically significant risk indicator for peri-implant mucositis** (RR 1.74, single study, Latimer). Everything else — marginal bone level change, probing pocket depth change, and peri-implantitis incidence — trended worse with OPC but did not reach significance. Certainty was low to very low (4 of 5 studies at serious risk of bias; cross-sectional designs preclude causality). The authors attribute the muted hard-tissue effect partly to the included cohorts' good oral hygiene and supportive periodontal care, contrasting with earlier reports (Koori, Saber) that found significant deeper probing depths and bone loss with OPC.

## Key Contributions

- First SR+MA to directly meta-analyze **OPC → peri-implant disease outcomes** (MBL, PPD, mucositis, peri-implantitis), rather than prevalence/risk factors.
- Establishes OPC as a **risk indicator for peri-implant mucositis** (RR 1.74) — the only significant outcome.
- Confirms OPC trends toward worse MBL/PPD/peri-implantitis but **not significantly**, with low-to-very-low certainty.
- Reinforces the prevalence picture: OPC rises with follow-up (11.8% at 8 yr → 54.2% at >10 yr), mesial > distal, and food impaction is 2.2× more frequent at OPC sites yet itself not linked to peri-implant harm.

## Methodology

- **PICO**: adults ≥18 with implant-supported fixed prostheses; intervention = OPC (implant–tooth or two adjacent non-splinted implants); comparison = closed proximal contact (CPC); outcomes = MBL change (primary), PPD change, peri-implant mucositis/peri-implantitis incidence; non-randomized observational; implants in function ≥1 yr.
- Databases CENTRAL, MEDLINE, EMBASE, ClinicalTrials.gov to 8 July 2025; PRISMA + Cochrane; duplicate screening/extraction.
- Contact status assessed by **dental floss** (closed = resistance; open = none). RevMan 5.3 random-effects; MD (continuous) / RR (dichotomous); ROBINS-I V2; GRADE; leave-one-out sensitivity. Statistical unit = implant. Publication bias not formally assessed (<10 studies).
- French et al. contributed the largest sample (~5054 implants); Latimer drove the disease-incidence outcomes.

## Results

| Outcome | Studies | Effect (95% CI) | p | Heterogeneity | GRADE |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Marginal bone level change (mm) | 4 | MD 0.07 (−0.09 to 0.24) | 0.38 | I²=48% | very low |
| Probing pocket depth change (mm) | 3 | MD 0.11 (−0.29 to 0.51) | 0.59 | I²=68% | very low |
| **Peri-implant mucositis** | 1 (Latimer) | **RR 1.74 (1.06–2.86)** | **0.03** | — | low |
| Peri-implantitis | 2 (Alhakeem, Latimer) | RR 1.63 (0.88–3.02) | 0.12 | I²=0% | low |

- Peri-implant mucositis: 182 more cases per 1000 with OPC. Peri-implantitis: 58 more per 1000 (non-significant).
- OPC prevalence 11.8% (8 yr) → 54.2% (>10 yr); ~50% lost by 9 yr; strong positive correlation with follow-up duration.
- Distribution: mesial > distal (Sheba 2.1× mesial), mandible > maxilla (20% vs 15%), anterior > posterior (18% vs 14%).
- Food impaction 2.2× at OPC sites (Byun: 63% vs 39%) but NOT significantly associated with peri-implant outcomes.
- Leave-one-out (MBL): pooled MD 0.03–0.16, remained non-significant throughout.
- Clinical implications: prioritize oral hygiene/supportive care; consider splinting adjacent teeth, occlusal guards/Essix retainers; prefer screw-retained restorations for easy retrieval and correction.
