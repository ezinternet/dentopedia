## Summary
This umbrella review synthesized existing systematic and meta-analytic reviews on proximal contact loss (PCL) between implant-supported prostheses and adjacent natural teeth, a complication whose pooled prevalence had remained uncertain across heterogeneous primary studies. From 43 eligible articles, five systematic/meta-analytic reviews were included, three of which carried a low risk of bias and were treated as the clinical evidence base. Across these, open (rather than tight) contact loss predominated, and PCL was more frequent at mesial contacts, particularly in the mandibular arch. No significant differences were reported by sex or between anterior and posterior regions. It positions PCL as a frequent, well-documented implant-prosthetic complication and sets the top of the evidence hierarchy for this category.

## Key Contributions
- First umbrella-level synthesis of PCL evidence, sitting above individual SR/MAs.
- Confirms mesial predominance and mandibular predilection at the highest evidence tier.
- Establishes open-contact phenotype as the dominant PCL pattern.
- Reports no sex or anterior/posterior effect, narrowing the risk-factor list.

## Methodology
Umbrella review following PRISMA. Databases: MEDLINE/PubMed, Cochrane, Google Scholar through August 2021, no language restriction. Inclusion: systematic/meta-analytic reviews addressing contact loss between implant prostheses and adjacent teeth. Risk of bias judged by 2–3 researchers with a fourth as arbiter. Of 43 eligible articles, 5 reviews were selected; 3 were low risk of bias.

## Results
- 5 SR/MA included; 3 low-risk → clinical evidence.
- Open contact loss predominant over tight/closed loss.
- Mesial PCL > distal PCL, strongest in the mandibular arch.
- No significant sex difference; no anterior vs posterior difference.
- Synthesis remained largely narrative because of heterogeneity among primary studies.
