## One-line Summary

Narrative review (56 studies + a 148-case, 25-year private-practice retrospective) of open proximal contacts beside posterior implants; introduces the **MM-DD occlusal-adjustment** concept (remove the mesial-of-mesial-tooth and distal-of-distal-tooth occlusal contacts to steer adjacent teeth *toward* the implant), reporting open-contact incidence 11.5% vs the 34-66% conventional range.

## 한줄요약

후방 임플란트 인접 개방접촉(open contact, proximal contact loss, PCL)의 다인자성 원인·발생률·결과를 정리하고, 인접 자연치를 임플란트 쪽으로 유도하는 MM-DD 교합조정 개념(근심치의 근심 / 원심치의 원심 교합접촉 제거)을 제안한 서술적 리뷰 — MM-DD군 개방접촉 11.5% vs 통상 34-66%(후향·단일 술자, RCT 없음).

## Summary

Open proximal contacts between posterior implant restorations and adjacent natural teeth are a frequent, underappreciated complication, reported in **34-66%** of cases (a recent SR puts the range at 17-66%), most often in the mandibular molar/premolar region. The root cause is the **biomechanical disparity** between an ankylosed (rigidly osseointegrated) implant and PDL-mobile natural teeth: under functional load, adjacent teeth drift mesially while the implant stays fixed, so the original contact separates. Prosthetic contour, surgical positioning, occlusal force distribution, and opposing-dentition type modulate the effect.

Consequences are **food impaction** (reported up to 86.7%; Wang et al. found 78.6% where PCL is present), plaque accumulation, gingival inflammation, proximal/peri-implant bone loss, and reduced satisfaction. The review groups existing management into five domains and notes each has limits: prosthetic over-contouring impairs plaque control; splinting complicates hygiene and maintenance; occlusal equilibration and corrective composite/orthodontics are reactive.

The paper's contribution is the **MM-DD (mesial-mesial / distal-distal) occlusal adjustment technique**: at crown delivery, selectively remove the occlusal contact from the *mesial occlusal surface of the mesial adjacent tooth* and the *distal occlusal surface of the distal adjacent tooth*. This redirects each adjacent tooth's physiologic migration *toward* the implant crown rather than away from it, proactively preserving the proximal contact without adding prosthetic bulk, chair time, or lab steps.

## Key Contributions

- **Etiology synthesis** — eight categories of contributing factors centered on the ankylosed-implant-vs-mobile-tooth biomechanical disparity and mesial drift.
- **Risk factors** — mandibular arch, single-unit restoration, longer time since restoration (esp. >12 months), younger age, occlusal scheme, opposing-dentition type.
- **Five preventive/corrective domains** — prosthetic design (over-contouring, pressure-responsive impressions, digital workflow), surgical placement (3D positioning, bone quality, placement timing), occlusal management (equilibration, night guards), corrective (restoration replacement, composite, orthodontics), biomechanical (loading protocols, splinting).
- **MM-DD concept** — a minimally invasive, proactive occlusal-vector adjustment that leverages biologic mesial drift to maintain contact.

## Methodology

- Narrative/PRISMA-style review: PubMed + Scopus + Google Scholar, 2000-2024; 183 → 92 full-text → **56 studies** included.
- Embedded **retrospective clinical observations**: 25 years (1999-2024), **148 single-unit posterior implants** (92 mandibular, 56 maxillary), mean age 51.6 ± 10.9 yr, ≥12-mo follow-up; screw-retained n=87 / cement-retained n=61; zirconia 82 / PFM 39 / lithium disilicate 27. Plus a **matched conventional control (n=102)**.
- Contact integrity by floss + 21-50 µm shim stock; marginal bone by standardized periapicals; VAS (0-10) for food impaction / discomfort / satisfaction. MM-DD occlusion checked with 42 µm articulating paper (MIP + excursions); implant crown set to primary contact at central fossa, secondary contact >1 mm inside the marginal ridge.

## Results

- **Open-contact incidence**: MM-DD 11.5% (17/148) vs historical 34-66%; matched conventional control 38.2% (39/102).
- **Food impaction**: MM-DD 18.4%; in control's open-contact cases, 76.9%.
- **Discomfort (VAS)**: MM-DD 1.9 ± 1.4 vs control 4.2 ± 2.3 (p<0.01); food-impaction VAS 1.6 ± 1.2 (MM-DD overall).
- **Subgroup risk**: mandibular 15.2% vs maxillary 7.1%; single-unit 13.9% vs splinted 4.8%; <50 yr 16.7% vs older 9.3%; risk rises after 12 months.
- **Open-contact vs intact contacts**: VAS food impaction 4.9 ± 1.8 vs 1.5 ± 1.1; discomfort 4.2 ± 2.3 vs 1.6 ± 1.4 (p<0.01).
- **Material**: zirconia (higher fracture resistance / dimensional stability) may preserve contact better than metal-ceramic; resin-based materials more wear-prone.

**Caution**: MM-DD evidence is retrospective and single-operator with no RCT; the headline 11.5% figure is compared against a *historical* 34-66% range rather than the concurrent matched control. The authors explicitly call for prospective RCTs, calibrated examiners, long follow-up, and FEA modeling before adoption.
