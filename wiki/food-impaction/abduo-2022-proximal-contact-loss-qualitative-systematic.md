## One-line Summary
Qualitative systematic review (19 studies) showing PCL prevalence rises with service time — 11–30% (<2y), 13–65% (2–5y), 29–83.3% (>5y) — driven mainly by duration and mesial location, with food impaction and patient dissatisfaction as the chief implications.

## Summary
This qualitative systematic review pooled 19 clinical studies on proximal contact loss (PCL) between implant prostheses and adjacent natural teeth, stratifying prevalence by service duration. Short-term studies (<2 years) reported 11–30%, medium-term (2–5 years) 13–65%, and long-term (>5 years) 29–83.3%, establishing PCL as a time-dependent, progressive phenomenon. The most consistent influencing factors were service duration and the mesial location of the contact; occlusion, vitality of adjacent teeth, implant location, patient age and splinting had weaker or inconsistent associations. Food impaction and patient dissatisfaction were the clearest implications, whereas bone loss, peri-implant inflammation, bleeding on probing and pocket depth were less clearly linked. The authors conclude PCL is frequent, inevitable and progressive, and call for research on prosthesis design.

## Key Contributions
- Stratifies PCL prevalence into <2y / 2–5y / >5y bands, making the time dependence explicit.
- Ranks influencing factors into strong (duration, mesial location) vs weak/unclear (occlusion, vitality, location, age, splinting).
- Separates established implications (food impaction, dissatisfaction) from uncertain ones (bone loss, BoP, PD).
- Frames PCL as "frequent, inevitable and progressive."

## Methodology
Qualitative systematic review (no meta-analytic pooling). Eight databases (PubMed, Google Scholar, Cochrane, Scopus, EMBASE, Open Grey, ScienceDirect, Web of Science) searched to September 2021. Nineteen clinical studies on PCL around implant prostheses were eligible. Prevalence was tabulated by follow-up band; influencing factors and implications were synthesized narratively.

## Results
- Short-term (<2y): PCL 11–30%.
- Medium-term (2–5y): PCL 13–65%.
- Long-term (>5y): PCL 29–83.3%.
- Strong factors: service duration, mesial contact location.
- Weak/unclear factors: occlusion, adjacent-tooth vitality, implant location, age, splinting.
- Clear implications: food impaction, patient dissatisfaction; unclear: bone loss, peri-implant inflammation, BoP, pocket depth.
