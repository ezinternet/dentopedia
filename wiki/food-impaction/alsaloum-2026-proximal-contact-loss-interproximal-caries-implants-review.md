## One-line Summary
Narrative review reframing proximal contact loss (PCL) between implant restorations and adjacent natural teeth as a *direct biological risk factor for interproximal caries* — synthesizing PCL prevalence (18–66%), consistent mesial > distal predominance, time-dependent progression (~9–10%/year; ~50% of contacts lost by 3–9 yr), the implant-infraposition growth mechanism (lifelong ~0.1–0.2 mm/yr tooth eruption against a fixed implant), and prevention via tight contacts + Essix retainers (~15–50% PCL reduction).

## Summary
This narrative review consolidates the literature on proximal contact loss (PCL) between implant-supported restorations and adjacent natural teeth, and — its distinctive contribution — reframes PCL not as a mere prosthetic nuisance but as a **direct biological risk factor for interproximal caries on the adjacent natural tooth**. The proposed causal chain: the open embrasure created by PCL traps food and plaque → shifts the local microbiota toward a cariogenic biofilm that routine hygiene cannot clear → sustained acid production → demineralization of the vulnerable proximal surface, alongside gingival inflammation and local alveolar bone loss (food impaction associated with inflammatory peri-implant soft-tissue change in >50% of impacted cases).

The review's second pillar explains *why* PCL is essentially inevitable over time: implants are **ankylosed and do not erupt**, whereas natural teeth keep drifting mesially and erupting throughout life (~0.1–0.2 mm/yr in adults, 1.2–1.5 mm/yr during active growth). The static implant therefore falls into relative **infraposition** and loses contact with the drifting neighbor — most pronounced mesially. This mechanism is unpacked across age, sex, and maxillary growth (transverse/sagittal/vertical), with special sections on syndromic patients (ectodermal dysplasia/anodontia, acromegaly). Being a narrative review, it offers no pooled estimates and notably does **not** quantify a caries-incidence figure despite the title — the caries link is mechanistic/inferential.

## Key Contributions
- **PCL → interproximal caries reframing**: positions PCL as a direct caries risk factor for the adjacent natural tooth (food impaction → cariogenic biofilm → demineralization), the angle differentiating this page from the prevalence-focused pages in this category.
- Synthesizes PCL **prevalence**: 18% to >65%; ~3–33.5%/year, central tendency ~10%/year. Within 1 yr: 11% (n=4,325 implants), 12.5% (7-yr prospective), 24.3% (1-yr); two prospective cohorts 16.5%→25.7% and 3.3%→21.3% (yr1→yr2).
- Documents **consistent mesial > distal predominance**: Koori 51.8% vs 15.6%; Chao-Hua 27% vs 5% (5.4×); Saber 42.1% vs 14.5%; Byun 2.1× (P=0.048); schematic 52% mesial vs 16% distal. Molar > premolar (42.6% / 22.2% / 10%).
- Ties PCL to the **infraposition/growth mechanism** by age and sex; implant at age 7 ends ~10 mm more apical than neighbors after 9 yr (Ranly); >1 mm infraposition in a substantial fraction at 15–20 yr.
- **Prevention**: tight proximal contacts at delivery + regular follow-up/radiographs + Essix retainer (one study 30%→15%, a 50% reduction; another ~15% reduction, primarily mesial).

## Results
- **Arch**: inconsistent — mandible-higher in some (Shi 37.2% vs 9.1%; Koori), maxilla-higher in others (Varthis 57.9% maxilla); many report no significant arch difference (Saber, Jui-Ying, Mehanna).
- **Region**: most studies find posterior > anterior, though Saber found anterior higher.
- **Timing**: PCL detectable from 3 months; ~9%/yr rise; Kaplan-Meier ~50% of contacts lost within 3–9 yr; progression up to 12 yr.
- **Patient factors**: gender — no measurable effect (Bento 41% women vs 36% men; multiple NS). Age — steady rise in frequency/size (esp. mesial), though Saber found no age effect. Removable antagonist prostheses → fewer contact changes (reduced force transmission).
- **Infraposition magnitude**: adult eruption ~0.1–0.2 mm/yr; children with single implants ~age 12 → 5–7 mm relative infraocclusion after 4 yr; maxillary incisors shift ~2.5 mm buccal + 6 mm down between ages 9–25.
- **Caries angle**: framed as a direct risk factor; food impaction linked to inflammatory peri-implant soft-tissue change in >50% of impacted cases. No numeric caries-rate outcome is reported.
