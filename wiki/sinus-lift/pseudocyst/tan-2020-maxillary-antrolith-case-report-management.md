## One-line Summary

Case report of a 3.1×3.6mm asymptomatic maxillary antrolith (CBCT incidental finding, 67-year-old) managed with Caldwell-Luc excision; literature review confirms surveillance is appropriate for small/asymptomatic antroliths, surgical removal for complicated cases.

## 한줄요약

67세 여성 CBCT 우연 발견 3.1×3.6mm 무증상 상악동석 증례 — 소형 무증상 상악동석은 경과관찰, 합병증 동반 시 수술 제거(Caldwell-Luc/ESS).

## Summary

Antroliths (상악동석) are rare pathological calcifications (prevalence 0.15–3.2%) formed by mineral salt deposition around a central nidus in the maxillary sinus. First described in 1654, fewer than 30 cases appeared in literature until 2005. Female predisposition (55–60%). Most are incidental, asymptomatic findings on routine radiographs.

This case: 67-year-old pre-Denosumab dental clearance → panoramic radiograph showed rounded right maxillary sinus radiopacity → CBCT confirmed 3.1×3.6mm circumscribed dense radiopacity at sinus floor near #17 buccal roots → no sinus mucosal thickening or opacification. Patient elected removal due to breast cancer history concern → Caldwell-Luc procedure → histopathology: metaplastic bone and calcification = antrolith.

## Key Contributions

- Documents CBCT-to-surgical workflow for asymptomatic antrolith
- Defines management algorithm: observe vs. remove
- Differential diagnosis framework for sinus floor radiopacity (critical for pre-implant planning)

## Methodology

- Single case + PubMed literature review
- CBCT imaging for localization + surgical access planning
- Caldwell-Luc (5mm buccal window, local anesthesia)
- Histopathological confirmation

## Results

**Antrolith characteristics:**
- Composition: mineral salts (calcium phosphate, calcium carbonate) around a nidus
- Nidus types — *Endogenous*: tooth/bony fragments, blood, mucus, fungi; *Exogenous*: GP points, dental burs, silver points, cotton, foreign bodies
- CT: homogeneous high-density periphery with central lower density

**Symptoms (when symptomatic):**
- Epistaxis, facial pain, chronic sinusitis, nasal obstruction
- Purulent/foul-smelling discharge, halitosis, anosmia, otorrhea

**Differential diagnosis of sinus floor radiopacity (pre-implant CBCT):**
- Root fragments, impacted teeth
- Calcified MRC/polyp, osteomyelitis
- Ossifying fibroma, fungal ball (aspergilloma)
- Benign/malignant tumour

**Management:**
| Scenario | Management |
|---|---|
| Small, asymptomatic antrolith | Surveillance; prophylactic removal NOT necessary |
| Symptomatic (pain, sinusitis, fistula) | Surgical removal (ESS ± Caldwell-Luc) |
| Large (poorly accessible by ESS) | Caldwell-Luc for better exposure/drainage |
| All surgical cases | + Concurrent sinus infection treatment |

- Prognosis: excellent; no recurrences documented in literature

**Relevance to implant planning:**
- Incidental antrolith on pre-implant CBCT → consider histological evaluation (especially malignancy risk)
- Decision: observe (small/asymptomatic) vs. remove before sinus lift (large/symptomatic)
- Key message: an antrolith alone does NOT preclude implant placement
