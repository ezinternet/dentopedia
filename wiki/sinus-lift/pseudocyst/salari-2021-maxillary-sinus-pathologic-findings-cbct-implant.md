## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional CBCT study (n=140 implant candidates, northern Iran) found 63.5% had maxillary sinus pathologies; mucosal thickening (31.4%) was most prevalent, followed by retention cysts (17.1%), with no significant demographic predictors.

## 한줄요약

임플란트 후보 140명 CBCT 분석: 63.5%에서 상악동 병변, 점막비후(31.4%)가 가장 흔하고 연령·성별·치아상태와 유의한 연관 없음.

## Summary

This descriptive cross-sectional study evaluated 140 CBCT scans (280 maxillary sinuses) of dental implant candidates in northern Iran over a 6-month period. The study used a threshold of >5 mm for pathological mucosal thickening — higher than the current 2-3 mm consensus — which may have underestimated mild thickening prevalence.

**Pathology distribution:**
| Finding | Frequency (% of sinuses) |
|---|---|
| Mucosal thickening (>5mm) | 31.4% |
| Retention cyst | 17.1% |
| Partial/complete opacification | 9.3% |
| Polypoidal thickening | 5.7% |
| Healthy | 36.5% |

No statistically significant associations were found between pathology and age (<46 vs ≥46y), gender (male 65.5% vs female 63.5%), or dentition status (partial edentulism showed highest rate at 90.9% — approaching significance, p=0.061).

## Key Contributions

- Population-specific prevalence data for northern Iran cohort
- 5-category classification system including polypoidal subtype
- Confirms mucosal thickening as the most common sinus pathology across populations
- Seasonal collection (summer/autumn) may affect retention cyst rates

## Methodology

- Descriptive/cross-sectional; 6-month consecutive series from 2 radiology clinics
- CBCT: NewTom 3G, FOV=9", kVp=120, mA=150
- Single examiner (OMF radiologist), all three planes reviewed
- Threshold for mucosal thickening: >5mm (higher than other studies using >2-3mm)

## Results

- 63.5% overall pathology rate (vs 68% in Küçükkurt 2025 using >3mm threshold)
- Higher rate in partial edentulism patients: 90.9% (not significant, p=0.061)
- Mucosal thickening likely reflects odontogenic irritation — consistent with adjacent periapical/periodontal pathology
