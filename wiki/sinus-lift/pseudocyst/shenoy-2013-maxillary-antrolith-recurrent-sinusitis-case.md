## One-line Summary

Case report of a 2×1cm symptomatic maxillary antrolith causing recurrent sinusitis and oroantral fistula (prior Caldwell-Luc residual bone chips as nidus); managed with combined ESS + repeat Caldwell-Luc; recommends thorough sinus irrigation after ESS to prevent antrolith formation.

## 한줄요약

과거 Caldwell-Luc 수술 잔류 골편을 nidus로 한 2×1cm 대형 상악동석이 재발성 상악동염·구강상악동루 유발; ESS+Caldwell-Luc 복합 제거 후 완치 — ESS 후 충분한 세척으로 예방 권고.

## Summary

This Indian case report demonstrates the large/symptomatic end of the antrolith spectrum, in contrast to Tan 2020's asymptomatic small antrolith. The 47-year-old patient had a 1984 Caldwell-Luc surgery history for polypoid disease — residual bone chips left behind became the endogenous nidus for antrolith formation over decades.

**Key message**: Prior sinus surgery → potential endogenous nidus → antrolith risk. Thorough sinus irrigation after all ESS procedures is recommended to prevent this complication.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates large antrolith causing overt complications (sinusitis, fistula)
- Establishes prior sinus surgery residual debris as antrolith nidus
- Combined ESS + Caldwell-Luc approach for large antroliths
- Recommends thorough sinus irrigation after ESS as prevention

## Methodology

- Case report (India)
- CT osteomeatal complex + paranasal sinuses
- Diagnostic nasal endoscopy
- Wide antrostomy → too large for ESS removal → Caldwell-Luc
- Histopathology: decalcification + PAS staining; exclusion of osteoma, malignancy, rhinoscleroma, fungal concretions

## Results

**Pathogenesis sequence:**
1. Prior Caldwell-Luc → residual bony chips (endogenous nidus)
2. Long-standing infection + poor sinus drainage
3. Purulent fluid concentration
4. Calcium phosphate + calcium carbonate precipitation
5. Progressive encrustation → 2×1cm antrolith over ~30 years

**Clinical presentation (symptomatic large antrolith):**
- Heaviness left face ×2 months
- Oroantral fistula (mobile upper left 2nd molar)
- Foul-smelling purulent nasal discharge
- Thick postnasal drip

**Imaging mimics:**
- CT initially thought: osteoma vs. malignancy
- ESS revealed: brownish, hard, gritty, freely mobile mass

**Microbiology**: Pseudomonas spp. (resistant to ampicillin)

**Antrolith classification by nidus:**
| Type | Examples |
|---|---|
| Endogenous | Bone/tooth fragments, blood, pus, mucus, fungi |
| Exogenous | Gutta percha, dental burs, silver points, cotton, bullets, wood, grass |

**Historical perspective**: "Rhinolith" coined 1845; only ~30 world cases until 2005; maxillary sinus most common site; almost always unilateral.

**Prevention**: Thorough sinus cavity irrigation after ESS → removes residual bony/tissue fragments that could serve as nidus.
