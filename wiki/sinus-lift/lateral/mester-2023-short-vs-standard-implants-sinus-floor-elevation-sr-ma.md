## One-line Summary
SR+MA of 5 ≥5-year RCTs showing no significant survival difference between short implants and standard implants + sinus floor elevation, with significantly less marginal bone loss and fewer biological complications favoring short implants.

## 한줄요약
≥5년 RCT 5편 메타분석 — 생존율 유의차 없음(RR 0.97, p=0.07), 변연골소실·생물학적 합병증은 short implant가 유의하게 적음.

## Summary
By restricting inclusion to RCTs with at least five years of follow-up, this meta-analysis tests whether the short-implant equivalence holds long-term. Across five trials, implant survival did not differ significantly (RR 0.97, p=0.07), though standard implants with sinus floor elevation were numerically higher. Short implants showed significantly less marginal bone loss and fewer biological complications, while prosthetic complications did not differ. The conclusion is conditional: short implants are a viable long-term alternative, but the borderline survival signal and small trial count temper a strong equivalence claim.

## Key Contributions
- Long-term (≥5y) RCT-only restriction — addresses durability directly.
- Significant MBL (WMD −0.29) and biological-complication (RR 0.46) advantages for short implants.
- Flags a numerically higher survival for standard+SFE not reaching significance — honest caveat.

## Methodology
SR+MA; 1619 screened → 5 RCTs with ≥5-year follow-up; short implants vs standard implants + sinus floor elevation, atrophic posterior maxilla; outcomes ISR, MBL, biological/prosthetic complications.

## Results
- ISR: RR 0.97 (0.94–1.00), p=0.07.
- MBL: WMD −0.29 (−0.49 to −0.09), p=0.005 (favors short).
- Biological complications: RR 0.46 (0.23–0.91), p=0.03 (fewer with short).
- Prosthetic complications: RR 1.51 (0.64–3.55), p=0.34 (ns).
