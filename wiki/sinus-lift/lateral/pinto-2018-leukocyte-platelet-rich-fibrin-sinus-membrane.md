## 한줄요약
측방창 (Lateral window) 상악동거상술 중 발생한 대형 슈나이더리안 막 (Schneiderian membrane) 천공을 백혈구 혈소판풍부피브린 (Leukocyte Platelet-Rich Fibrin, L-PRF)과 콜라겐 막으로 봉합하고, 8개월 후 외육각형 (External hexagon) 임플란트 2개를 식립한 케이스 리포트.

## Summary
Pinto et al. describe the management of a large intraoperative Schneiderian membrane perforation during a lateral-window maxillary sinus floor elevation (SFE) in a partially edentulous patient missing teeth #16, #17, and #18. Instead of aborting the procedure, the team sealed the perforation with **L-PRF (leukocyte platelet-rich fibrin)** combined with collagen membranes. After 8 months of uneventful healing, two external-hexagon implants were placed at the #16 and #17 sites and restored with a screw-retained prosthesis. The case supports L-PRF as a salvage option that lets surgeons continue augmentation in the same session.

## Key Contributions
- Concrete clinical demonstration of L-PRF as a biological seal for a large sinus perforation during lateral-window SFE.
- Combines L-PRF with a collagen membrane for mechanical + biological repair.
- Avoids the conventional 6–8 week reentry interval after a perforation-driven abort.

## Methodology
- Lateral-window approach to the maxillary sinus.
- Large Schneiderian membrane perforation encountered intraoperatively.
- L-PRF membranes harvested chairside from the patient's blood; combined with collagen membrane to seal the perforation.
- Bone graft placed under the L-PRF/collagen seal.
- 8-month healing; second-stage implant placement at #16 and #17 (external hexagon connection); screw-retained prosthesis delivered.

## Results
- Perforation successfully sealed; surgery not aborted.
- Uneventful 8-month healing with sufficient bone formation.
- Two implants placed and restored; prosthesis delivered.
- Authors cite background literature perforation rate during SFE: 3.6–56%.
