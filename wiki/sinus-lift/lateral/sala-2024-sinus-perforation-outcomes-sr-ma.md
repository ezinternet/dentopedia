## One-line Summary
SR+MA (29 studies, 6,860 implants): sinus floor-penetrating implant failure rate 2.1%; membrane-perforated vs intact sinus augmentation OR 1.347 (p=0.197, NS) — perforation does not significantly increase implant failure risk.

## 한줄요약
SR+MA (29편, 6,860개 임플란트): 상악동 저부 천공 (Sinus Floor Perforation) 임플란트 실패율 2.1%; 슈나이더 막 천공 (Schneiderian Membrane Perforation, SMP) 상악동거상술 vs 비천공 간 임플란트 실패 오즈비 (Odds Ratio, OR) 1.347 (p=0.197) — 천공 자체는 유의한 실패 위험인자 아님.

## Summary

Sala 2024 is the first SR+MA to analyse two distinct perforation scenarios within a single study: (1) dental implants protruding through the sinus floor without sinus augmentation, and (2) Schneiderian membrane perforation during sinus lift procedures. 29 studies were included (PRISMA 2020, PROSPERO-registered). All 29 studies rated "good" by NIH quality assessment.

**Key finding**: Neither scenario significantly elevates implant failure risk when managed appropriately. Postoperative sinusitis/infection risk is low and may be driven by perforation dimensions or patient anatomical predisposition rather than perforation per se.

## Key Contributions

1. Dual-scenario synthesis: quantifies failure risk for sinus-penetrating implants (Group 1) and membrane-perforated augmented sinuses (Group 2) in the same meta-analytic framework.
2. Group 1 estimated failure rate 2.1%; drops to 1.0% when excluding the historical Brånemark machined-implant cohort (modern surface-modified implants).
3. Group 2 pairwise meta-analysis (n=6,860 implants, 17 studies): OR 1.347 (95% CI 0.857–2.117), p=0.197 — not significant.
4. Meta-regression: OR decreases non-significantly with longer follow-up (−0.004/month, p=0.500), suggesting no cumulative disadvantage of perforation over time.
5. Reassurance signal: funnel plot shows no clear asymmetry → low publication-bias concern.

## Methodology

- **Design**: SR+MA, PRISMA 2020, PROSPERO CRD42024497046
- **Databases**: PubMed/Medline, Web of Science, Scopus (no time restriction; last search Oct 2023)
- **29 studies**: 11 for Group 1 (sinus floor penetration), 18 for Group 2 (membrane perforation during SFE)
- **Stats**: Untransformed proportion for Group 1; random-effects pairwise meta-analysis (I²=36.1%) for Group 2; meta-regression with follow-up as covariate

## Results

### Group 1 — Implants penetrating the sinus floor (n=11 studies)

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Estimated implant failure rate | **2.1%** (95% CI 0.2–4.1%, SE 1.0%, p=0.035) |
| Excluding machined implants (Brånemark 1984) | **1.0%** (95% CI 0.0–1.9%, I²=0%) |
| Sinusitis reported | 1 case (transient) only |
| Most common radiological finding | Mucosal thickening around implant apex |
| Studies with 0 failures | 6 of 11 |

Key clinical note: mucosal thickening at the sinus floor is common even without perforation when implants are placed within 2 mm of the sinus cortex — this is a normal adaptive response, not a complication in itself.

### Group 2 — Membrane perforation during sinus augmentation (n=17–18 studies)

| Metric | Perforated | Non-perforated |
|---|---|---|
| Implants | 1,817 | 5,043 |
| Failures | 73 (4.0%) | 274 (5.4%) |
| OR (perforation vs no perforation) | **1.347 (95% CI 0.857–2.117, p=0.197)** | — |
| Heterogeneity | I²=36.1%, τ²=0.241 | — |
| Meta-regression (follow-up) | −0.004/month, p=0.500 | — |

Post-operative sinusitis/infection: reported in 8 studies; treated with antibiotics ± incision/drainage. Three studies reported zero post-operative symptoms. Possible association with graft displacement into sinus when membrane is perforated.

### Perforation size caveat
Two individual studies (not pooled) found that *larger* perforations were associated with higher failure rates. Perforation size data were too heterogeneous across studies to meta-analyse as a subgroup — this is the primary unresolved question and a signal to monitor repair technique for large tears.

## Clinical Implications

1. **Sinus floor penetration (no augmentation)**: With modern surface-modified implants, the failure risk is ~1% — comparable to non-penetrating implants. Transient sinusitis is rare; mucosal thickening is common but typically asymptomatic.
2. **Membrane perforation during sinus lift**: Does not significantly increase implant failure risk (OR 1.35, NS). Appropriate repair normalises outcomes — consistent with Díaz-Olivares 2021 and Soares 2024.
3. **Sinusitis/infection**: Low prevalence; may be more related to graft displacement and patient anatomy (turbinate deviation, septal position) than membrane perforation alone.
4. **Perforation size**: While not meta-analysable here, the individual-study signal that large tears carry higher risk supports the repair-protocol hierarchy (≤10 mm vs >10 mm) established in the clinic's lateral-window synthesis.

## Limitations

- Retrospective design predominant → data quality inconsistency
- Confounders (diabetes, smoking, bruxism, bisphosphonates) not controllable
- Operator variability across studies
- Perforation size not systematically reported — key unresolved subgroup
