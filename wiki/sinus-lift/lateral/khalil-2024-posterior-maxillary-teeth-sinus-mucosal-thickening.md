## One-line Summary

Retrospective CBCT study (n=124 patients, 992 teeth) found periapical lesions (AOR=32.7), first molars (AOR=3.97), and severe periodontal bone loss (AOR=2.75) as strongest independent predictors of maxillary sinus mucosal thickening.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

This retrospective cross-sectional study quantified the independent contributions of specific tooth characteristics to maxillary sinus mucosal thickening (MT) using multivariable logistic regression on 992 posterior maxillary teeth in 124 patients.

**MT prevalence**: 72.6% of patients (90/124); 33.9% bilateral — higher than many previous studies due to Grade 1 (0-2mm) inclusion.

**Grade distribution** (of 992 teeth):
| Grade | Range | % |
|---|---|---|
| Grade 1 (normal) | 0-2mm | 72.5% |
| Grade 2 (moderate) | 2-10mm | 19.2% |
| Grade 3 (severe) | >10mm | 8.4% |

**Root proximity (RPMT) classification** — Sharan et al.:
- Type 0: no contact (56.0% of teeth)
- Type 1: contact with cortical border, inferiorly curving floor (28.0%)
- Type 2: laterally adjacent, apex outside sinus (11.8%)
- Type 3: apex protruding into sinus (3.6%)
- Type 4: sinus floor enclosing root (0.5%)

**Adjusted odds ratios for MT:**

| Risk factor | AOR | 95% CI | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1st molar (vs 1st premolar) | 3.97 | 1.82-3.10 | <0.001 |
| 2nd molar | 2.30 | 1.29-2.79 | <0.001 |
| 2nd premolar | 1.57 | 1.27-2.84 | 0.03 |
| RPMT Type 2 (vs Type 0) | 2.27 | 1.38-3.34 | 0.001 |
| RPMT Type 1 (vs Type 0) | 2.24 | 1.54-3.26 | <0.001 |
| Severe periodontitis | 2.75 | 1.29-5.86 | 0.009 |
| Mild-moderate periodontitis | 1.68 | 1.19-4.93 | 0.03 |
| Periapical lesion present | 32.7 | 4.53-83.61 | <0.001 |
| No periapical lesion (vs extracted) | 19.8 | 8.18-74.23 | <0.001 |
| Gender | NS | — | 0.28 |

**Key insight**: Extracted teeth (reference category) showed lowest MT odds — i.e., tooth presence itself (regardless of pathology) is associated with higher MT risk. Even teeth without periapical lesions carry AOR=19.8 vs extracted sites.

**Pathophysiology**: Bacterial toxins and cytokines from periapical/periodontal lesions spread to sinus mucosa via porous bone, blood, and lymphatic routes → mucosal inflammation → thickening.

## Key Contributions

- Highest resolution AOR analysis for specific tooth positions and types (premolar vs molar)
- Confirms periapical lesion as the dominant predictor (AOR=32.7 — far exceeding other factors)
- Identifies first molar as highest-risk tooth position for sinus involvement
- RPMT Types 1-2 (root contact/proximity) associated with ~2× MT risk
- Recommends: pre-treatment CBCT for second molars with periodontal involvement; collaborate with ENT for significant MT

## Methodology

- Retrospective cross-sectional, Pharos University, Egypt (Feb 2023-Feb 2024)
- 124 patients (50M/50F), age 25-55y, 992 posterior maxillary teeth
- CBCT: Morita 3D, voxel 0.2mm
- MT grading: Grade 1=0-2mm, Grade 2=2-10mm, Grade 3=>10mm
- RPMT: Sharan Types 0-4
- Periodontal BL: Engebretson <25%/25-50%/>50%
- Inter-examiner κ=0.97; multivariable logistic regression
