## One-line Summary

Retrospective CBCT study (n=1957 sinuses) demonstrating that osteomeatal complex (OMC) anatomical variations — ostium width, infundibulum length, ethmoidal bulla diameter, and uncinate process superior attachment (UPSA) types 3/4/6 — are significantly associated with maxillary sinus mucosal thickening and ostium obstruction, expanding the CBCT risk-assessment framework before sinus floor elevation.

## Summary

Gulbey and colleagues (2026) retrospectively analyzed CBCT scans from 1957 maxillary sinuses to investigate whether anatomical variations of the osteomeatal complex (OMC) — the drainage pathway shared by the maxillary, anterior ethmoid, and frontal sinuses — are associated with Schneiderian membrane (mucosal) thickness and appearance. The study found that 54% of sinuses demonstrated some degree of mucosal thickening, with flat thickening being the most common pattern (23.5%). Critically, ostium obstruction was significantly associated with mucosal thickness >10 mm, and OMC structural dimensions (OW, IL, EBD) all showed significant positive correlations with MT. UPSA types 3, 4, and 6 were significantly linked to polypoid thickening and ostium obstruction. Concha bullosa had no significant relationship with MT. These findings establish that pre-surgical CBCT evaluation should extend beyond the sinus floor to include systematic assessment of the OMC drainage anatomy.

## Key Contributions

- **Large-scale OMC-focused analysis**: n=1957 sinuses — the largest CBCT study specifically linking OMC variations to Schneiderian membrane thickness
- **OMC drainage anatomy linked to mucosal pathology**: OW, IL, and EBD all significantly positively correlated with MT
- **UPSA classification prognostic value**: Types 3, 4, and 6 (orbital, middle turbinate, and skull base attachments) significantly associated with polypoid thickening and ostium obstruction
- **Sex differences quantified**: MT, IL, OW, and EBD all significantly higher in men
- **Haller cells (HC)**: significant positive correlation with MT
- **Concha bullosa (CB)**: no significant effect on MT (important negative finding)
- **Mucosal prevalence benchmark**: 54% overall thickening; flat (23.5%) > polypoid > obstruction

## Methodology

- **Design**: Retrospective cross-sectional CBCT analysis
- **Sample**: 1957 maxillary sinuses from a CBCT database (bilateral sampling; GEE used to account for correlation)
- **OMC measurements on CBCT**:
  - Ostium Width (OW) — narrowest dimension of the maxillary ostium
  - Infundibulum Length (IL) — length of the ethmoidal infundibulum
  - Ethmoidal Bulla Diameter (EBD)
- **Anatomical variants recorded**: Haller cells (HC), Concha Bullosa (CB), Uncinate Process Superior Attachment (UPSA) type 1–6
- **Mucosal Thickness (MT)**: measured at thickest point on CBCT
- **Mucosal Appearance (MA)**: 4-category classification — normal, flat thickening, polypoid thickening, ostium obstruction
- **Statistics**: Kruskal–Wallis, Mann–Whitney U, Chi-square; GEE models for bilateral/repeated measurements
- No histologic validation; no direct correlation with intraoperative perforation events reported

## Results

| Parameter | Key Finding |
|---|---|
| Overall mucosal thickening | 54% of 1957 sinuses |
| Flat thickening | 23.5% (most prevalent pattern) |
| Ostium obstruction ↔ MT >10 mm | Significant association |
| OW, IL, EBD ↔ MT | All significantly positively correlated |
| Sex (M > F) | MT, IL, OW, EBD all higher in men |
| Haller cells | Significant positive correlation with MT |
| Concha bullosa | NO significant relationship with MT |
| UPSA types 3, 4, 6 | Significantly associated with polypoid thickening + ostium obstruction |

**Clinical implication**: OMC drainage obstruction — driven by UPSA configuration and structural dimensions — amplifies sinus membrane thickening. Pre-SFE CBCT should include OMC mapping, not just sinus floor height and membrane thickness.

## Clinical Relevance for Sinus Floor Elevation

1. **Thin membrane risk**: Standard SFE perforation risk models focus on membrane thickness <2 mm (fragile) or >2 mm (thickened and adherent). This study adds an upstream cause: compromised OMC drainage independently drives membrane thickening.
2. **Pre-surgical CBCT checklist expansion**: Practitioners should assess OW, IL, EBD, UPSA type, and Haller cell presence — not only residual bone height and membrane thickness — before SFE planning.
3. **UPSA types 3/4/6 = red flag**: If UPSA is attached to the orbit floor, middle turbinate, or skull base, polypoid change and ostium obstruction are more likely; ENT consultation may be warranted before SFE.
4. **Sex as a covariate**: Male patients have anatomically larger OMC dimensions AND thicker membranes — potentially requiring modified osteotomy approach or pre-operative ENT clearance at higher frequency.
