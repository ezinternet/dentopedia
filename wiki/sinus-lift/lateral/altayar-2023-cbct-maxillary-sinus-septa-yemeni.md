## 한줄요약
예멘 인구 440명 880개 상악동의 콘빔CT (Cone-Beam CT, CBCT) 단면 연구. 환자 63.9%, 상악동 47%에서 격벽 (Septa)이 존재하며 평균 높이 5.2 mm, 대부분 바닥에서 기시 (54.5%)·중간부 (43%)·관상방향 (66%)·완전형 (58.2%)으로, 측방 상악동거상술 전 CBCT 평가의 필요성을 뒷받침.

## Summary
Altayar et al. analyzed 880 maxillary sinuses (440 Yemeni patients) on CBCT to characterize maxillary sinus septa anatomy and its implications for sinus floor elevation (SFE). The study reports one of the highest prevalence rates in the literature — septa in 63.9% of patients and 47% of sinuses, mean height 5.2 mm — and details origin wall, anteroposterior location, orientation, and configuration. The authors conclude that preoperative CBCT is essential before SFE in this population due to high septa prevalence, which increases Schneiderian membrane perforation risk (background literature cites 11–56% perforation rate in SFE).

## Key Contributions
- First CBCT-based maxillary sinus septa epidemiology in a Yemeni population.
- Provides quantitative 3D septa parameters useful for surgical risk stratification before lateral-window SFE.
- Tests demographic and dental-status covariates; finds none significantly associated with septa presence.

## Methodology
- Retrospective cross-sectional design, 880 sinuses (440 patients).
- CBCT analysis with Anatomage Invivo v6.
- Variables: prevalence, height, location, orientation, origin, configuration.
- Statistics: descriptive + analytical, p < 0.05 threshold.

## Results
- Septa prevalence: 63.9% (patients), 47% (sinuses).
- Mean septum height: 5.2 mm.
- Laterality: 15.7% right, 18% left, 30.2% bilateral.
- Origin: 54.5% floor; Location: 43% middle; Orientation: 66% coronal; Configuration: 58.2% complete.
- Gender, age, dental status: not significantly associated with septa presence.
- No significant association between septa and sinus membrane pathology.
