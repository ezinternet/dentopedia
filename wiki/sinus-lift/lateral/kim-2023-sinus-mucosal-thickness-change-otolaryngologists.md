## One-line Summary

Retrospective study (n=9) of ENT-assessed patients with preoperative maxillary sinus mucosal thickening found no significant MSMT change post-implantation across three treatment groups (sinus augmentation, bone grafting, no grafting; p > 0.05), with zero postoperative sinusitis.

## Summary

This retrospective study from The Catholic University of Korea (Seoul St. Mary's Hospital) enrolled nine patients with preoperative 상악동점막두께 (MSMT) referred from Periodontology to Otolaryngology before dental implant placement. Patients underwent CBCT at baseline and at ~7 months postoperatively. The study evaluated three groups (sinus augmentation, bone grafting, no grafting, n=3 each) and tracked MSMT, MOD (ostium-to-floor distance), and MSMT/MOD 비율 as candidate risk indicators for post-implantation sinusitis.

The key finding was that MSMT did not significantly change after treatment (4.3 → 4.5 mm overall, p > 0.05), regardless of procedure type. Importantly, no postoperative sinusitis occurred even among patients with anatomical nasal variations (Haller cells in 4/9, concha bullosa in 2/9, paradoxical middle turbinate in 4/9). The authors conclude that ENT-cleared patients with preoperative mucosal thickening can safely undergo implant-related maxillary sinus surgery.

## Key Contributions

- Longitudinal MSMT data confirming stability (~7 months) after implant surgery in ENT-screened patients
- Validates MSMT/MOD ratio as a monitoring metric (mean preop 0.13 ± 0.05; postop 0.15 ± 0.06, NS)
- Zero sinusitis despite multiple anatomical variants present in most patients
- Supports multidisciplinary 이비인후과-치과 협진 (ENT-dental collaboration) as the safe pathway for borderline sinus cases

## Methodology

- Single-center retrospective chart and CBCT review
- 9 patients, December 2019 – August 2022
- CBCT measured with InVivoDental v6.0.5; parameters: MSMT, MOD, MSMT/MOD ratio
- Groups: sinus augmentation lateral approach with Bio-Oss + BioGide (n=3); GBR with Bio-Oss (n=3); implant placement only (n=3)
- Statistics: Kruskal–Wallis (between groups), Wilcoxon signed-rank (pre vs. post); α = 0.05
- Exclusion criteria: diabetes, asthma, history of endoscopic sinus surgery, ostial obstruction

## Results

| Parameter | Preoperative | Postoperative | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| MSMT (overall) | 4.3 ± 2.0 mm | 4.5 ± 2.3 mm | NS (p > 0.05) |
| MOD (overall) | 31.4 ± 4.6 mm | — | — |
| MSMT/MOD (overall) | 0.13 ± 0.05 | 0.15 ± 0.06 | NS (p > 0.05) |

- Sinus augmentation group: mean preop MSMT 5.1 ± 0.6 mm, MOD 34.8 ± 0.8 mm, MSMT/MOD 0.15 ± 0.02
- Bone grafting group: mean preop MSMT 4.9 ± 3.3 mm, MOD 28.5 ± 5.1 mm, MSMT/MOD 0.16 ± 0.08
- No grafting group: mean preop MSMT 3.0 ± 0.8 mm, MOD 31.1 ± 5.3 mm, MSMT/MOD 0.10 ± 0.01
- No significant between-group differences for any parameter (p > 0.05)
- Alveolar bone height gain in sinus augmentation group: 6.0 ± 6.0 mm
- Postoperative sinusitis: 0/9 patients
