## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.4317/medoral.26545](https://doi.org/10.4317/medoral.26545)), this systematic review and meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD42023483303) pooled 11 CBCT-based observational studies (1841 lateral window sinus lift procedures, 2014–2023, all Newcastle-Ottawa Scale 7) to test whether Schneiderian membrane thickness predicts intraoperative perforation. Two analyses were run. On the continuous scale (7 studies), membrane thickness was significantly LOWER in perforation cases (MD −0.91 mm, 95% CI −1.48 to −0.33, I²=94%), with 6 of 7 studies agreeing and no outlier on leave-one-out. But dichotomizing by cutoff (2, 1.5, 1 mm) yielded only non-significant odds ratios, so no usable threshold emerged. The authors invoke a possible U-shaped relationship (both very thin and very thick membranes perforate more; lowest risk ~1–1.5 mm) to reconcile the paradox.

Clinically: a thinner Schneiderian membrane on preoperative CBCT signals somewhat higher perforation risk during lateral sinus lift, but there is no validated thickness cutoff to triage cases — do not over-rely on a single number; thickness is one continuous, confounded factor among many (window size, septae, instruments, operator experience), and very thick pathological membranes are not protective.

## Key Contributions

- Isolates membrane thickness as a single perforation risk factor for the lateral window technique only (excludes osteotome/transcrestal), unlike prior reviews that mixed techniques or cutoffs.
- Two separate meta-analyses: continuous thickness (perforation vs non-perforation) and subgroup pooling by cutoff (2 / 1.5 / 1 mm).
- Documents the paradox — thinner membranes in perforation cases continuously, yet no significant cutoff association — and frames the U-shaped curve as the likely explanation.

## Methodology

- Design: SR+MA of observational studies, PRISMA-reported, PROSPERO CRD42023483303.
- Search: Embase, PubMed, Web of Science to 4 Dec 2023; 1104 → 482 (dedup) → 27 relevant → 11 included (kappa=0.95).
- Inclusion: lateral window sinus lift; thick-vs-thin comparison reporting perforation, or thickness reported by perforation status. Exclusion: osteotome technique, missing thickness/perforation data, abstracts.
- Studies: 2 prospective + 9 retrospective; 8 countries; all measured thickness via preoperative CBCT; perforation rate 10–37%; all NOS=7.
- Analysis: random-effects (RevMan 5.3); MD for continuous, OR for binary, 95% CI; I² heterogeneity; leave-one-out + funnel plot.

## Results

- Continuous thickness (7 studies): membrane significantly thinner in perforation cases — MD −0.91 mm (95% CI −1.48, −0.33), I²=94%; 6/7 studies concordant; no leave-one-out outlier; funnel plot symmetric (no publication bias).
- Cutoff 2 mm (4 studies): OR 0.97 (95% CI 0.44, 2.17), I²=56% — non-significant.
- Cutoff 1.5 mm: OR 0.66 (95% CI 0.29, 1.48), I²=72% — non-significant.
- Cutoff 1 mm: OR 0.93 (95% CI 0.34, 2.56) — non-significant.
- Net: thinner membrane associates with perforation continuously, but no tested cutoff predicts it; few studies/cutoff, inconsistent thresholds, and a probable U-shaped (non-monotonic) relationship are the proposed reasons. More studies needed.
