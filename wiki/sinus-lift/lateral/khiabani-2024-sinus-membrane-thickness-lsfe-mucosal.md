Prospective controlled trial (n=40 sinuses, 20/group) showing lateral SFE in thickened sinus mucosa (>5 mm, <10 mm) resolves membrane swelling significantly (6.8→3.4 mm, p<.001) without rhinosinusitis — thickened mucosa is not a contraindication to LSFE.

## Summary

Khiabani and colleagues conducted a prospective controlled clinical trial to determine whether a thickened sinus membrane (SM >5 mm) affects sinus health or augmentation outcomes after lateral sinus floor elevation (LSFE). Forty patients undergoing unilateral LSFE were divided into Group A (<5 mm SMT, n=20) and Group B (>5 mm SMT, n=20), with CBCT evaluation at baseline and 6 months.

The pivotal finding is that the thickened membrane group (Group B) underwent a **significant and clinically favorable reduction** in SM thickness from 6.8 ± 1.0 mm preoperatively to 3.4 ± 1.7 mm at 6 months (p<.001), while Group A showed no significant change (1.4 → 1.3 mm). Critically, neither group showed clinical or radiographic rhinosinusitis at 6 months, and augmentation adequacy (bone height and length) was similar between groups.

The authors conclude that thickened sinus mucosa up to <10 mm **should not be considered a contraindication to LSFE**, challenging the historical clinical hesitation around mucosal hypertrophy in this context.

## Key Contributions

- First prospective controlled trial directly comparing <5 mm vs >5 mm SMT groups through LSFE with 6-month CBCT follow-up.
- Demonstrates significant membrane thickness normalization in the thickened group post-LSFE — the procedure itself appears to have a therapeutic effect on mucosal hypertrophy.
- Establishes that membrane perforation rates and augmentation adequacy are equivalent regardless of preoperative SMT.
- Confirms zero rhinosinusitis incidence in both groups — the key safety concern with operating on an inflamed/thickened sinus.
- Quantifies the surgical difficulty differential: thickened mucosa causes more intraoperative bleeding (p=.003) and resistance to elevation (p=.001), with a trend toward longer operation time (8.6 vs 12.1 min, p=.097).

## Methodology

- **Design**: Prospective controlled clinical trial
- **n**: 40 subjects, 40 unilateral sinuses; 72.5% male; mean age 48.8 ± 7.6 years
- **Group A**: SM thickness <5 mm (n=20) — mean 1.4 ± 0.9 mm
- **Group B**: SM thickness >5 mm (n=20) — mean 6.8 ± 1.0 mm
- **Procedure**: LSFE with simultaneous implant placement (52 implants total)
- **Follow-up**: 6 months
- **Imaging**: CBCT preoperative and 6-month postoperative
- **Primary outcome**: SM thickness change
- **Secondary outcomes**: Sinus health (rhinosinusitis), augmentation adequacy, membrane perforation rate, intraoperative bleeding, resistance to membrane elevation, operation time

## Results

**Sinus membrane thickness (primary outcome):**

| | Group A (<5 mm) | Group B (>5 mm) |
|---|---|---|
| Preoperative SMT | 1.4 ± 0.9 mm | 6.8 ± 1.0 mm |
| 6-month SMT | 1.3 ± 0.6 mm | 3.4 ± 1.7 mm |
| Change significance | Not significant | **p < .001** |
| Between-group difference | — | **p < .001** |

**Safety and augmentation:**
- **Rhinosinusitis** (clinical + radiographic): **0/40** — none in either group
- **Membrane perforation rate**: Similar in both groups (no significant difference)
- **Augmentation adequacy** (bone height + length): Similar in both groups (no significant difference)

**Surgical difficulty:**
- Bleeding during membrane separation: Group B significantly higher (**p = .003**)
- Resistance to membrane elevation: Group B significantly higher (**p = .001**)
- Operation time: Group B 12.08 ± 8.26 min vs Group A 8.64 ± 3.70 min (**p = .097**, non-significant)

**Clinical conclusion from authors:** LSFE in thickened mucosa (<10 mm) and thinner mucosa (<5 mm) does not cause abnormal changes in SM or sinus health. Thickened membrane is **not a contraindication to LSFE**.
