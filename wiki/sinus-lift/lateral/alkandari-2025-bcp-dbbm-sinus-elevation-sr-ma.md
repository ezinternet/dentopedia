## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 10 RCTs (328 patients, 389 implants) finding BCP superior to DBBM for new bone formation (MD +3.48%) and soft tissue percentage, with lower residual graft material, though high heterogeneity limits conclusions.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

Alkandari et al. (2025) conducted a PRISMA-compliant SR+MA of RCTs comparing biphasic calcium phosphate (BCP) vs. deproteinized bovine bone mineral (DBBM) for sinus floor elevation, focusing exclusively on histomorphometric outcomes from biopsy specimens.

**Clinical context:**
- Maxillary sinus pneumatization + posterior ridge resorption after tooth loss creates vertical bone deficiency limiting implant placement
- DBBM (Bio-Oss) is the historically dominant grafting material — highly osteoconductive, slow resorption, excellent volume stability
- BCP (typically 60% HA + 40% β-TCP) is a synthetic alternative combining scaffold stability (HA) with resorbable, osteostimulatory phase (TCP releases Ca²⁺/PO₄³⁻ ions)

**Search and inclusion:** PubMed, Scopus, WOS, CENTRAL; inception to May 2025; 442 citations → 10 RCTs included.

## Key Contributions

1. **Only RCT-based SR+MA** directly comparing BCP vs. DBBM for sinus augmentation histomorphometry
2. **BCP superiority in new bone formation** (MD +3.48%; p=0.01) — statistically significant with moderate heterogeneity (I²=44%)
3. **BCP leaves less residual graft** (MD −8.41%; p<0.001) — consistent with TCP's faster resorption kinetics vs. DBBM's near-permanent scaffold behavior
4. **Higher soft tissue fraction with BCP** (MD +6.01%; p<0.001) — reflects space turnover dynamics during graft remodeling
5. Establishes BCP as a clinically viable synthetic alternative to DBBM when histomorphometric new bone formation is the priority

| Parameter | Detail |

## Results

### Primary Outcomes (histomorphometry)

| Outcome | MD (BCP vs DBBM) | 95% CI | p | I² |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| New bone formation % | +3.48% | 0.89 – 5.97 | 0.01 | 44.03% |
| Residual graft % | −8.41% | −13.12 to −3.72 | <0.001 | 85.71% |

### Secondary Outcome

| Outcome | MD (BCP vs DBBM) | 95% CI | p | I² |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Soft tissue % | +6.01% | 2.39 – 9.63 | <0.001 | 71.96% |

**Interpretation:**
- BCP produces more new bone and resorbs more completely than DBBM — consistent with TCP component's osteoconductive + ion-release mechanism
- DBBM's slow resorption means more residual graft remains at biopsy time — historically valued for volume stability, but less favorable for new bone replacement
- High I² for residual graft (86%) reflects differences in BCP formulations (HA:TCP ratios, particle sizes) across studies — not all BCPs behave identically
- **No implant survival data**: this SR+MA addresses histomorphometry only; implant survival in BCP-augmented sinuses requires separate evidence

## Limitations

- High heterogeneity for residual graft (I²=86%) and soft tissue (I²=72%) outcomes
- Diverse BCP compositions (HA:TCP ratios vary: 60:40, 70:30, etc.) contribute to heterogeneity
- Lack of standardized histomorphometric protocols across RCTs
- No data on implant survival or prosthetic success in BCP vs. DBBM sites
- Short-term follow-up in some included studies
- Cureus is an open-access journal with broader publication criteria than specialty implant journals
