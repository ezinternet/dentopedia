## One-line Summary

Narrative review (Seoul St. Mary's Hospital ENT team, 2019) synthesizing preoperative maxillary sinus evaluation criteria and a CT-based management protocol for dental implant candidates, emphasizing natural ostium patency as the critical determinant.

## Summary

This narrative review from Korean ENT specialists addresses the preoperative assessment and joint management of maxillary sinus conditions before dental implant placement. The central argument is that ostium patency — not merely mucosal thickness — determines whether implant placement is safe, because any sinus-lift or graft material that obstructs the natural ostium will cause sinusitis.

The review presents a three-component preoperative workup: (1) thorough history (prior nasal surgery, chronic rhinosinusitis, systemic diseases); (2) CBCT extended to include the natural ostium (panoramic alone is inadequate for soft-tissue assessment); (3) nasal endoscopy to evaluate the ostiomeatal complex. A CT-based management flowchart (Fig. 3) categorizes findings into three arms: proceed with implant, require medical/surgical treatment first, or require further workup for suspected malignancy.

## Key Contributions

- Establishes a practical CT-based decision protocol integrating ENT expertise into the pre-implant sinus evaluation
- Identifies ostium plugging by dislodged bone graft as a key sinusitis mechanism distinct from Schneiderian membrane perforation
- Provides size thresholds for cysts/polyps relative to total sinus height for safe implant candidacy
- Clarifies FESS indications and its role in both preventive treatment (before high-risk implant) and remediation (extraction of dislocated implants)
- Summarizes antibiotic selection for acute and chronic rhinosinusitis in the dental implant context

## Methodology

Narrative review with expert opinion, CT image atlas (Fig. 1), endoscopic photographs (Fig. 2), and decision flowchart (Fig. 3). Single-institution perspective from Seoul St. Mary's Hospital ENT department. No systematic literature search or statistical analysis performed.

## Results

**Post-implant sinusitis rate:** ~5% of maxillary implant patients develop chronic sinusitis postoperatively; preoperative sinusitis is a significant risk factor.

**Incidental CT findings (before implant):**
- Mucosal thickening: 23.7–28.2%
- Cyst or solitary polyp: 8.9–19.4%
- Sinusitis: 3.6–6.5%

**Size threshold principle:** lesions < 1/3–1/2 of maxillary sinus height (not adjacent to natural ostium) are compatible with implant placement. Larger lesions risk ostium blockage when the Schneiderian membrane is elevated.

**Pathology-specific management:**

| Condition | Key Decision |
|---|---|
| Mucosal thickening, no ostium block | Proceed with implant; repeat CT after medication if generalized |
| Ostium-blocking mucosal edema | Treat before implant |
| Small cyst/polyp (10–15 mm, not near ostium) | Implant OK |
| Large cyst/polyp (> 1/2 sinus height) | FESS before implant |
| Acute rhinosinusitis | Resolve first; viral = symptom relief, bacterial = amoxicillin 5–10 days |
| Chronic rhinosinusitis (≥12 weeks) | Antibiotics ± intranasal steroids; FESS if drug-resistant |
| Fungal sinusitis (hyperdense calcification on CT) | FESS; resolves in ~1 month |
| Paranasal malignancy | Stage and treat; no implant |

**Antibiotic selection for acute bacterial rhinosinusitis:** amoxicillin or amoxicillin-clavulanate 5–10 days (first line); high-dose amoxicillin-clavulanate (2 g BID) in high-resistance areas; 3rd-generation cephalosporin or clindamycin for penicillin allergy.

**FESS role:** gold standard for restoring sinus clearance; also the preferred route for endoscopic retrieval of a dislocated implant through the widened natural ostium (Caldwell-Luc reserved for implants too large for endoscopic extraction).
