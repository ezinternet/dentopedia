## One-line Summary

Retrospective cohort (109 patients, 121 sinuses, 252 implants): preoperative chronic sinusitis was the strongest predictor of postoperative infection (p=0.007) and implant loss (p=0.007) after lateral sinus augmentation, making preoperative ENT evaluation and sinusitis treatment mandatory before surgery.

## 한줄요약

후향적 코호트 연구 (109명, 121개 상악동, 252개 임플란트): 술 전 만성 부비동염 (Chronic Sinusitis, CS)이 측방 상악동거상술 (Lateral Sinus Augmentation, LSA) 후 감염 (p=0.007) 및 임플란트 실패 (p=0.007)의 최강 예측인자로, 수술 전 이비인후과 평가·치료가 필수임을 다변량 분석으로 제시.

## Summary

This single-center retrospective cohort study from Kyushu University evaluated 109 patients (121 sinuses, 252 implants) undergoing lateral approach sinus augmentation (측방 접근 상악동거상술) to identify causes of postoperative infection and implant loss.

The headline finding: **preoperative chronic sinusitis was the dominant independent risk factor for both complications**, with stronger association than membrane perforation, diabetes, or timing of implant placement. Overall, 8/121 sinuses (6.6%) experienced postoperative infection and implant loss — within the published range of 1.6–8.5%.

The authors propose a structured prevention pathway: SNOT-20 + endoscopy + CT preoperatively; ENT referral for active chronic sinusitis; macrolide therapy (clarithromycin) ≥12 weeks or FESS to achieve sinus clearance; only then proceed to augmentation.

## Key Contributions

1. **Chronic sinusitis as #1 risk factor**: Multivariate logistic regression confirmed preoperative chronic sinusitis (p=0.007) surpassed all other variables for both infection and implant loss outcomes.
2. **Risk hierarchy established**:
   - *Infection axis*: chronic sinusitis > timing of implant insertion > membrane perforation (NS) > diabetes (NS)
   - *Implant loss axis*: chronic sinusitis > sex (male) > diabetes > postoperative denture use > membrane perforation
3. **Clinical protocol proposed**: Mandatory preop rhinologic workup (SNOT-20, endoscopy, CT) → treat CS before augmentation → confirm clearance → proceed.
4. **Membrane perforation is not the primary driver**: Contrary to common clinical emphasis, membrane perforation ranked below chronic sinusitis in both outcome models.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Retrospective cohort, single center (Kyushu University / Kyushu Central Hospital, Fukuoka, Japan)
- **Sample**: 109 patients, 121 sinuses, 252 implants; follow-up through osseointegration assessment
- **Outcome definitions**:
  - Postoperative infection: purulent discharge, fever, or pain requiring reoperation
  - Implant loss: any implant failure within augmented sinus
- **Chronic sinusitis diagnosis**: EPOS 2012 criteria (≥12 weeks symptoms + endoscopic or CT evidence); SNOT-20 scoring
- **Analysis**: Univariate + multivariate logistic regression across 8 clinical variables

## Results

### Complication Rate
- Postoperative infection + implant loss: **8/121 sinuses (6.6%)**
- All 8 complicated cases occurred in the chronic sinusitis-positive subset

### Postoperative Infection — Strongest Predictors (logistic regression)
| Rank | Predictor | p-value |
|------|-----------|---------|
| 1 | Preoperative chronic sinusitis | **0.007** |
| 2 | Timing of implant insertion | significant |
| 3+ | Membrane perforation, diabetes | NS |

### Implant Loss — Strongest Predictors (logistic regression)
| Rank | Predictor | p-value |
|------|-----------|---------|
| 1 | Preoperative chronic sinusitis | **0.007** |
| 2 | Sex (male) | significant |
| 3 | Diabetes | significant |
| 4 | Postoperative denture use | significant |
| 5 | Sinus membrane perforation | significant |

### Proposed Prevention Protocol
1. **Preoperative screening**: SNOT-20 questionnaire + nasal endoscopy + CT of paranasal sinuses
2. **ENT referral**: if chronic sinusitis diagnosed, initiate macrolide therapy (clarithromycin) ≥12 weeks OR refer for FESS
3. **Confirm clearance**: endoscopic/CT resolution of sinusitis before scheduling augmentation
4. **Intraoperative**: meticulous Schneiderian membrane management; consider staged approach in high-risk cases
5. **Postoperative**: avoid removable denture pressure over grafted sinus; diabetic glucose control
