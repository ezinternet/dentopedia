## One-line Summary

Korean narrative review of 7 PubMed studies (1998–2009, >436 implants) on Schneiderian membrane perforation repair in lateral sinus lift, stratified by perforation size (<5 mm, 5–10 mm, >10 mm, >15 mm), with implant success rates 69.6–98.9%.

## Summary

Sinus floor elevation is a predictable procedure for posterior maxillary implant placement, but membrane perforation is its most frequent complication (incidence 10–60%). This Korean narrative review searched PubMed 1990–2010 and selected 7 clinical studies documenting implant success by perforation repair technique. Key finding: outcome correlates strongly with perforation size. Small perforations (<5 mm) repaired with resorbable collagen membrane achieve 94–99% implant success. Larger perforations (>10 mm) achieve only 74.1%, requiring more complex reconstruction (lamellar bone, buccal fat pad, block graft). Very large (>15 mm) perforations are managed with slow-resorbing membranes ± PRP ± microsurgical suture; only small case series exist. Prevention (piezosurgery, CT planning, round window corners) is emphasized as primary strategy.

## Key Contributions

1. **Size-stratified repair protocol**: first practical summary in Korean literature organizing <5 mm / 5–10 mm / >10 mm / >15 mm decision branches with technique recommendations.
2. **Implant success by repair strategy**: aggregates outcomes from 7 studies, total >436 implants, success range 69.6–98.9%.
3. **Fugazzoto & Vlassis classification (Class I–V)**: higher class = more difficult repair + lower success.
4. **Risk factors quantified**: thin membrane (28%), septa (22%), adhesions (17%), prior surgery (17%), scar (11%), cyst (5%) (Becker et al. data).
5. **Prevention emphasis**: piezosurgery, pre-op CT, rounded window margins, allergy/rhinitis screening.

## Methodology

- Narrative review (article review format)
- PubMed search, 1990–2010; keywords: sinus lift, sinus augmentation, sinus floor elevation, sinus graft, sinus perforation, repair of sinus perforation, repair of sinus membrane
- Inclusion: lateral-approach clinical studies in humans; perforation repair documented; implant success rate reported per repair method
- 7 studies selected; data extracted: author, year, perforation size, repair method, implant system, bone graft material, patient/implant count, success rate

## Results

### Perforation incidence and classification
- Reported incidence: 10–60% across included studies
- Fugazzoto & Vlassis Class I–V: Class I (near osteotomy edge, mesiodistal) → Class V (perforation within prior tear); progresses from most manageable to least; success declines with class

### Size-stratified repair and implant success

| Perforation size | Recommended repair | Implant success |
|---|---|---|
| Very small (< ~2 mm) | No repair; membrane self-folds | Normal expected |
| < 5 mm | Resorbable collagen membrane (Bio-Gide or Bio-Mend) ± 6-0 Vicryl suture | 94.4–98.9% |
| 5–10 mm | Collagen membrane + lamellar bone from osteotomy | 91.9% |
| > 10 mm | Lamellar bone ± buccal fat pad; block bone graft | 74.1% |
| > 15 mm | Slow-resorbing collagen membrane (1–2 sheets, pin-fixed) ± PRP; microsurgical suture (8-0) + fibrin glue; collagen pouch technique | ~100% (case reports only) |

### Study-level data (Table 1)
- Proussaefs 2004 (>2 mm, collagen membrane, Bio-Oss ± DFDBA): 69.6% (n=21 implants)
- Shlomi 2004 (>5 mm, demineralized lamellar bone sheet): 89.7%
- Hernández-Alfaro 2006: <5 mm → 97.1%; 5–10 mm → 91.9%; >10 mm → 74.1% (lamellar bone ± buccal fat pad ± block graft)
- Ardekian 2006 (<10 mm, collagen membrane): 94.4% (n=35 patients); RBH ≤3 mm → 85% perforation rate; RBH 6 mm → 25%; no success difference between groups
- Becker 2008 (<5 mm Bio-Gide; >5 mm + suture): 98.9% (n=93 implants)
- Pikos 2008 (>15 mm, slow-resorbing membrane + PRP): 100% (n=10 implants)
- Kim 2008 (15 mm, buccal fat pad): 100% (n=2 implants; case report)

### Adjunct materials reviewed
- Fibrin glue: fibrinogen → fibrin net; useful adjunct for sealing large perforations
- Cyanoacrylate adhesive: tested in rabbit model by Choi et al.
- PRP: may support membrane healing as adjunct in large-perforation cases (Pikos 2008)

### Key cautions
- Suturing risk: thin membrane may enlarge with needle passage; extreme care required
- Covering sinus floor with collagen membrane may restrict blood supply to graft material (Pikos)
- Large perforations may require abandoning surgery and waiting ~4 months for re-entry
