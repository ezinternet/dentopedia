## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1016/j.oooo.2024.12.008](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.oooo.2024.12.008)), this systematic review with meta-analysis scrutinized the risk factors associated with Schneiderian membrane perforation during lateral-window preparation for maxillary sinus elevation. This page is built from the **abstract only (full text not retrieved)**. Across a predominantly retrospective evidence base, the authors identified a small set of factors that meaningfully raise perforation risk: a **thin membrane (<1.5 mm)**, the **presence of sinus septa (≈3× risk)**, and **smoking (1.6× risk, roughly one perforation per seven smokers treated)**. **Lateral wall thickness** and **residual ridge height** were also significant but rested on only 3 studies, and **piezosurgery** showed a marginally significant advantage over rotary instruments (P = .05). By contrast, **window size** and **number of missing teeth** did not affect perforation likelihood.

Clinically: preoperative CBCT should triage perforation risk by **membrane thickness, septa, and smoking status** above all — a thin membrane, an intervening septum, or a smoker each warrants slower, more careful window preparation (and consider piezosurgery), whereas window dimensions and the number of missing teeth do not by themselves justify a different protocol.

## Key Contributions

- Ranks Schneiderian membrane perforation risk factors specifically for the **lateral window** approach, identifying membrane thickness, septa, and smoking as the most prominent.
- Supplies usable effect sizes (smoking 1.6×, septa 3×, thin membrane <1.5 mm) for preoperative risk stratification.
- Separates evidence-backed risk factors from non-contributory ones (window size, missing teeth count).

## Methodology

- Search across multiple electronic databases; critical appraisal of studies meeting inclusion criteria; systematic review + meta-analysis.
- Included evidence is predominantly **retrospective** (authors' stated chief limitation).
- Abstract-only ingest — exact databases, search dates, pooled study counts, and full pooled statistics (CIs, heterogeneity, risk of bias) were not retrieved.

## Results

- **Thin membrane (<1.5 mm)** — significantly higher perforation risk than thicker membranes.
- **Smoking** — perforation likelihood increased **1.6×** (≈ 1 perforation per 7 smokers).
- **Sinus septa** — perforation risk **3×** higher vs absence of septa.
- **Piezosurgery vs rotary** — marginal benefit favoring piezosurgery (**P = .05**).
- **Lateral wall thickness** and **residual ridge height** — significant, but on **limited evidence (3 studies)**.
- **Window size** and **number of missing teeth** — **no effect** on perforation likelihood.
- **Most prominent factors**: membrane thickness, septa, smoking.
