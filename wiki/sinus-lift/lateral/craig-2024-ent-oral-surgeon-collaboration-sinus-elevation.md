Narrative review proposing a Felisati grading system (1-5) for ENT referral decisions before maxillary sinus elevation; only grade ≥3 (OMC obstruction, overt sinusitis) warrants otolaryngological evaluation — mucosal thickening and MRC do not.

## Summary

This multidisciplinary narrative review (otolaryngologists + implantologists) addresses a common clinical confusion: when should a maxillary sinus elevation (MSE) candidate be referred to ENT before surgery?

**Core problem identified**: Dental providers often misinterpret mucosal thickening (>2-6mm on CBCT) as "pathologic sinusitis" requiring ENT evaluation, leading to excessive referrals. Meanwhile, truly high-risk conditions (OMC obstruction, fungal sinusitis) may be overlooked.

**Core solution**: The Felisati grading system (1-5) combining OMC patency and sinus opacification assessment from CBCT.

## Key Contributions

- First comprehensive framework defining ENT referral criteria for MSE candidates
- Clarifies evidence: mucosal thickening and MRC generally safe for MSE (do not increase complications)
- Defines absolute contraindications: active infectious sinusitis, fungal sinusitis, severe OMC obstruction
- Describes ENT evaluation toolkit: nasal endoscopy, CBCT review, SNOT-22/CRS-PRO
- Proposes Felisati grading system for standardized decision-making

## Methodology

- Narrative review of existing literature
- Expert consensus (multidisciplinary team)
- Framework development based on 3 pillars: patient history, radiology (CBCT), nasal endoscopy

## Results

### Felisati Grading System

| Grade | CT/CBCT Finding | ENT Action |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Normal OMC, no pathology | No referral |
| 2 | Mild mucosal thickening, small MRC | Optional if symptomatic |
| 3 | Moderate OMC narrowing, mucosal disease | Referral recommended |
| 4 | Significant OMC obstruction, CRS | Referral mandatory |
| 5 | Complete obstruction, active/fungal infection | Referral + treat before MSE |

### Evidence Summary: What does NOT increase MSE risk

- Mucosal thickening alone (including >2-6mm): NOT a reliable MSE risk factor
- Asymptomatic MRC / antral pseudocyst: confirmed safe by Mardinger, Kim, Ritter
- Small/medium cysts without OMC obstruction: no increased perforation, sinusitis, or implant failure

### True Contraindications to MSE (require ENT first)

1. **Pus draining from middle meatus** (infectious sinusitis) → medical treatment
2. **Fungal sinusitis** → ESS first
3. **Severe OMC ostial scarring** → ESS for adequate drainage
4. **Large polyps obstructing OMC** → treatment before MSE
5. **Incurable chronic rhinosinusitis** → ESS + medical management

### ENT Evaluation Components

1. **Patient history/symptoms**: allergies, prior sinusitis, nasal medications, Eustachian tube history, SNOT-22
2. **CBCT review**: OMC patency, type of sinus opacification (thickening vs. cyst vs. polyp vs. pus)
3. **Nasal endoscopy**: gold standard — direct visualization of middle meatus for pus, edema, OMC patency

### Why OMC Patency Matters More Than Mucosal Thickness

- Maxillary sinus drains via superomedial ostium → "anti-gravity" mucociliary transport
- OMC obstruction (by polyp, scarring, edema) → impaired drainage → post-MSE sinusitis risk
- Mucosal thickening without OMC obstruction → usually safe

### Practical Referral Triggers

Refer to ENT when:
- Patient has symptoms suggesting sinusitis (+ OMC findings on CBCT)
- CBCT shows OMC obstruction or complete opacification (Grade ≥3)
- History of fungal sinusitis, severe CRS, or failed prior sinus surgery
- Known sinus polyps beyond MRC/pseudocyst

Do NOT refer for:
- Incidental mucosal thickening (<6mm, no infection signs)
- Asymptomatic MRC or pseudocyst
- Grade 1-2 CBCT findings in asymptomatic patient
