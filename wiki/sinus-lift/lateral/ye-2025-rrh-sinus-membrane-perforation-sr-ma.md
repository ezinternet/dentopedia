## One-line Summary

SR+MA (10 studies, 1,809 lateral-window sinus lifts): mean RRH significantly lower in perforated vs. non-perforated sinuses (MD −0.89 mm, 95% CI −1.47 to −0.31); small RRH (3–4 mm) OR 2.47 (NS overall; OR 4.68, p<0.05 after outlier removal), supporting RRH as a potential perforation risk factor.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

This SR+MA (Ye et al., BMC Oral Health 2025) is the first to pool evidence specifically examining whether **잔류 치조골 높이 (Residual Ridge Height, RRH)** influences risk of **슈나이더 막 천공 (Sinus Membrane Perforation, SMP)** during lateral-window sinus floor elevation (SFE). Ten studies (1,601 patients, 1,809 procedures) were included; all achieved NOS score 7/9. The overall pooled SMP rate was **19.2%**.

Two meta-analyses were conducted:
1. **Continuous RRH** comparison between perforation and non-perforation groups: MD −0.89 mm (95% CI −1.47 to −0.31, I² = 90%) — statistically significant, though small in magnitude.
2. **Categorical small vs. large RRH** (cut-off ~3–4 mm per each study): OR 2.47 (95% CI 0.49–12.21, I² = 88%) — not statistically significant, but after removing the outlier (Tukel 2018), OR 4.68 (p < 0.05).

## Clinical Interpretation

Small RRH (잔류 치조골 높이, **< 3–4 mm**) is a plausible risk factor for SMP in lateral-window SFE. The biological rationale is straightforward: cases with lower RRH require **greater membrane elevation** to achieve adequate implant length; since the Schneiderian membrane has limited extensibility (<1 mm thickness typical), more extensive lifting increases tearing probability.

**Practical implication**: When RRH is < 4 mm, surgeons should:
- Plan piezosurgical window preparation when possible (literature supports lower perforation rates vs. rotary bur).
- Have repair materials ready (collagen membrane, PRF, sutures) before the window is opened.
- Inform patients of elevated perforation risk preoperatively.

However, the clinical magnitude of the RRH effect is modest (MD only 0.89 mm), and other anatomical risk factors (sinus septa, lateral wall thickness, membrane thickness, smoking) likely interact. The absence of multivariate-adjusted data in the included studies limits confident attribution to RRH alone.

## Limitations

- High heterogeneity (I² 88–90%) — likely driven by differences in patient populations, surgical protocols, surgeon experience, and instrument types (rotary vs. piezo not segregated).
- No standardized "small RRH" threshold — studies used 3 mm, 4 mm, or other cut-offs.
- No multivariate-adjusted data controlling for confounders (septa, smoking, membrane thickness, surgeon experience).
- Predominantly retrospective studies.
- Small number of studies per meta-analysis limits precision.
