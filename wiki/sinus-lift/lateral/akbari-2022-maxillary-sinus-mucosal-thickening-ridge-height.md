## One-line Summary

CBCT study (n=240 sinuses, 141 implant candidates) found significant association between lower residual alveolar ridge height and greater maxillary sinus mucosal thickening, with >3mm as pathological threshold.

## Summary

This study evaluated whether residual alveolar bone height in the posterior maxilla correlates with the degree of Schneiderian membrane thickening. Using CBCT imaging in 141 implant candidates (240 sinuses — 99 bilateral, 42 unilateral), the study found a significant inverse relationship: lower bone height → greater mucosal thickening.

This finding has direct clinical implications for preoperative sinus lift planning:
- Patients with very low bone height (requiring extensive SFE) may also have thicker, more inflamed membranes
- Thicker membranes (>3mm) are paradoxically associated with higher perforation risk
- Optimal membrane thickness for lowest perforation risk: **1.5-2mm** (confirmed by multiple studies)

**Perforation risk curve:**
- <0.8mm thin: high perforation risk
- 1.5-2mm: lowest perforation risk
- >3mm: increased perforation risk (paradoxical — thicker ≠ safer)

## Key Contributions

- Establishes ridge height as a predictor of mucosal thickening in an Iranian cohort
- Contradicts Maska 2017 finding (no significant ridge height association) — population difference likely
- Reinforces >3mm threshold for pathological mucosal thickening
- Highlights that thin membranes (<0.8mm) and thick membranes (>3mm) both increase perforation risk

## Methodology

- Retrospective CBCT, implant candidates; 62.1% male, 37.9% female
- 99 bilateral + 42 unilateral = 240 sinus evaluations
- Maximum mucosal thickness measured on coronal sections
- Residual ridge height: alveolar crest to sinus floor
- Pathological threshold: >3mm
- Correlation and comparative statistics

## Results

- Significant inverse correlation: lower residual ridge height → greater mucosal thickening
- Majority showed some degree of mucosal thickening
- Male-predominant cohort (62.1%)
- Membrane thickness 1.5-2mm associated with lowest perforation risk during SFE
