## One-line Summary

Retrospective cross-sectional CBCT study (35 patients, 40 sinuses) of edentulous maxillary molar regions before lateral-window sinus floor augmentation: mean sinus mucosal thickness (SMT) was 1.09 ± 1.30 mm, with 25% above the 2 mm threshold and 50% below the 0.8 mm threshold; sex, time since extraction, reason for extraction, sinus septa, and nasal septum deviation were significant factors influencing SMT (all P < .05).

## Summary
Munakata and colleagues investigated which clinically observable factors govern Schneiderian membrane thickness (sinus mucosal thickness, SMT) in **edentulous** maxillary molar regions about to undergo lateral-window sinus floor augmentation — a population previously underrepresented in SMT literature, which is largely dentate-cohort. Across 40 sinuses (35 patients), the mean SMT was **1.09 ± 1.30 mm**, but the distribution was bimodal-leaning: 25% sat above the 2 mm "pathological swelling" threshold, while half were below the 0.8 mm "thin and fragile" threshold. Both thresholds have been independently linked to higher intraoperative membrane perforation risk.

## Key Contributions
- Provides edentulous-specific SMT statistics for preoperative risk stratification before lateral-window sinus floor augmentation.
- Identifies a five-factor model (sex, time since extraction, reason for extraction, sinus septa, nasal septum deviation) that significantly associates with SMT.
- Distinguishes the variable set predicting **thick** SMT (> 2 mm: sex, reason for extraction) from the set predicting **thin** SMT (< 0.8 mm: time since extraction, nasal septum deviation) — relevant because these two phenotypes confer different perforation mechanisms.
- Anchors CBCT as the working tool for preoperative SMT measurement.

## Methodology
- Retrospective single-center cohort: 35 patients (40 sinuses; 11 M / 24 F; age 41–79, mean 58.9 ± 9.0).
- CBCT measurements: SMT, residual alveolar bone height (RBH), sinus septa, nasal septum deviation.
- Tested predictors: age, sex, systemic disease, smoking, time since extraction, reason for extraction, RBH, sinus septa, nasal septum deviation.
- Statistical analysis: correlation (age, RBH) and group comparisons (categorical), significance threshold P < .05.

## Results
- Mean SMT: **1.09 ± 1.30 mm**; mean RBH: **2.14 ± 1.02 mm**.
- SMT > 2 mm: **25.0%**.
- SMT < 0.8 mm: **50.0%**.
- Significant factors for SMT:
  - Sex (P = .0078) — males trended thicker
  - Time since extraction (P = .0075) — < 6 months trended thicker
  - Reason for extraction (P = .020) — periapical lesions trended thicker
  - Sinus septa present (P = .0076) — thicker SMT
  - Nasal septum deviation (P = .038) — thicker SMT
- Specifically:
  - **SMT > 2 mm**: associated with sex and reason for extraction.
  - **SMT < 0.8 mm**: associated with time since extraction and nasal septum deviation.
