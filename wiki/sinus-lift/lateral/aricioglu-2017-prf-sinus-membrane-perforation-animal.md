## One-line Summary
Preclinical animal study (42 rabbits, 84 sinuses) comparing platelet-rich fibrin (PRF) membrane vs resorbable collagen membrane for repair of standardized 1 cm Schneiderian membrane perforations: at 1, 2, and 4 weeks histological cell counts showed no significant difference between PRF and collagen (both significantly better than untreated controls), establishing autologous PRF as a low-cost alternative for small/medium perforation closure.

## 한줄요약
가토 동물실험 (n=42, 84 부비동): 슈나이더 막 (Schneiderian membrane) 천공 시 혈소판풍부피브린 (Platelet-Rich Fibrin, PRF) 막과 콜라겐막 (Collagen Membrane, CM) 조직학적 치유 결과 동등 — PRF가 CM의 대안 가능.

## Summary
Rabbit animal study comparing PRF membrane vs resorbable collagen membrane for repair of standardized 1 cm Schneiderian membrane perforations. At 1, 2, and 4 weeks, histological cell counts (lymphocytes, fibroblasts, veins, collagen fibrils) showed no statistically significant difference between PRF and collagen membrane. Both materials significantly outperformed untreated contralateral controls.

## Key Contributions
- Direct histological PRF-vs-collagen comparison with contralateral within-animal controls.
- Establishes PRF as an autologous, low-cost alternative to commercial collagen membranes.
- Provides three-timepoint healing curve (1/2/4 weeks).

## Methodology
- 42 female New Zealand rabbits randomized into two groups (n=21 each).
- Bilateral 1 cm bony defect + 1 cm membrane perforation per sinus.
- Right side untreated (control); left side: collagen (group 1) or PRF (group 2).
- PRF from central ear artery, no anticoagulant, Choukroun centrifugation.
- Sacrifice at 1, 2, 4 weeks (n=7 per timepoint).

## Results
- **PRF vs collagen membrane**: no significant histological difference at any timepoint (p > .05).
- **Treated vs untreated control**: both PRF and collagen significantly improved healing parameters.
- No infection, dehiscence, or animal loss across the study.
- Authors conclude PRF is an acceptable alternative for closing sinus membrane perforations.

## Clinical Relevance
- Chair-side autologous option — no membrane material cost, no foreign body risk.
- Best evidence for small/medium perforations; >10 mm perforations not modeled.
- Human RCT evidence still needed before widespread clinical adoption.
