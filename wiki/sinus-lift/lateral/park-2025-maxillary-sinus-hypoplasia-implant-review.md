## Summary
Narrative review (University of Zurich/Hanyang/Kyung Hee) on maxillary sinus hypoplasia (MSH) — a reduction in sinus volume contrasting with the more clinically recognized sinus pneumatization. Three MSH variants are highlighted for their impact on implant therapy and sinus floor elevation surgery. CBCT diagnosis is essential.

## Key Contributions
- MSH = reduced sinus volume → sinus floor positioned more cranially than nasal floor
- Three clinically relevant MSH variants:
  1. **Chronic maxillary sinus atelectasis** (ostium blockage → implant-related infection risk)
  2. **Inferior meatus pneumatization** → lateral nasal expansion → nasal floor elevation needed
  3. **Ethmomaxillary sinus** → septum-like structure distally → two bone windows required for LSA
- MSH reduces available space for augmentation and alters anatomical relationships
- CBCT 3D imaging mandatory for accurate MSH diagnosis and surgical planning

## Why MSH Matters for Implant Surgery
- Altered anatomy changes access window location for lateral sinus augmentation
- Atelectasis increases post-surgical infection risk if implant complications arise
- Inferior meatus pneumatization requires nasal floor elevation as adjunct to implant placement
- Ethmomaxillary sinus necessitates two separate bone windows — increased surgical complexity
