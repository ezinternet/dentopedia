## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 5 RCTs (n=140): L-PRF+DBBM significantly improves new bone formation (MD +7.07%, p<0.001, I²=37%) and reduces residual graft (MD −7.93%, p<0.001, I²=0%) vs DBBM alone in lateral sinus augmentation; ISQ at loading not significantly different.

## Summary

This systematic review and meta-analysis (PRISMA; Cochrane Handbook) searched PubMed, Web of Science, Scopus, and Cochrane Central through May 2025, identifying 5 RCTs (140 patients; 76 L-PRF+DBBM, 64 DBBM alone) for lateral maxillary sinus augmentation. Included studies used either Dohan or Choukroun L-PRF centrifugation protocols with 6–8 month follow-up. Der-Simonian-Laird random-effects meta-analysis was performed in STATA MP v18.

The pooled analysis demonstrated that L-PRF significantly increased the percentage of new bone formation (MD +7.07%; 95% CI 2.20–11.93; p<0.001; I²=37%) and significantly reduced residual graft material (MD −7.93%; 95% CI −11.20 to −4.66; p<0.001; I²=0%). No significant differences were found for ISQ at implant loading (MD −2.25; p=0.56) or soft tissue percentage (MD −3.67; p=0.32; I²=89%). Leave-one-out sensitivity analysis confirmed robustness. Four of five studies had "some concerns" on ROB-2. Authors conclude that L-PRF adjunction to DBBM enhances histomorphometric outcomes and call for further standardized long-term RCTs.

## Key Contributions

- Highest-evidence synthesis specifically for L-PRF+DBBM in sinus augmentation
- New bone formation benefit: pooled MD +7.07% — clinically meaningful and statistically significant
- Residual graft reduction: zero heterogeneity (I²=0%) — most consistent finding; DBBM slow incorporation is reliably mitigated by L-PRF across all 5 RCTs
- ISQ equivalence: despite better histomorphometry, functional osseointegration (ISQ) is not different — clinical implication: earlier implant loading may not be justified on ISQ alone
- Resolves partial inconsistency vs Idiri 2023 SR (which found no significant effect): restricting to RCTs and using quantitative pooling changes the conclusion

## Methodology

- Design: SR+MA; PRISMA + Cochrane Handbook
- Databases: PubMed, Web of Science, Scopus, Cochrane Central (inception – May 2025)
- Included: 5 RCTs; L-PRF+DBBM vs DBBM alone; lateral sinus augmentation; histomorphometric outcomes
- Total N: 140 (mean age 50.4 ± 7.3 years; 62% female)
- Residual bone height: <5 mm (3 studies); <4 mm (2 studies)
- L-PRF protocols: Dohan (3 studies) and Choukroun (2 studies)
- Risk of bias: ROB-2; 1 low risk, 4 "some concerns"
- Statistics: Random-effects (DL); Cochrane Q + I²; leave-one-out sensitivity; STATA 18MP

## Results

**Primary (histomorphometry)**:

| Outcome | MD | 95% CI | p | I² |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| New bone formation (%) | +7.07 | 2.20 to 11.93 | <0.001 | 36.86% |
| Residual graft (%) | −7.93 | −11.20 to −4.66 | <0.001 | 0% |

**Secondary**:

| Outcome | MD | 95% CI | p | I² |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ISQ at loading | −2.25 | −9.76 to +5.27 | 0.56 | 48.67% |
| Soft tissue (%) | −3.67 | −10.94 to +3.60 | 0.32 | 89.21% |

- Residual graft: I²=0% — zero heterogeneity is exceptional; highly consistent benefit
- Soft tissue: very high heterogeneity (89%) — L-PRF preparation variability likely responsible
