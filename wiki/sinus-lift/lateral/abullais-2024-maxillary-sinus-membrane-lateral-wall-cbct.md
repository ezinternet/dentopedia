## One-line Summary

Retrospective CBCT study (n=75, 150 sinuses, Saudi Arabia) showing facial index type (euryprosopic/mesoprosopic/leptoprosopic) has no significant correlation with maxillary sinus membrane thickness (MT) or lateral wall thickness (LWT); females had significantly thicker LWT (p<0.05) and premolar MT was significantly greater than molar MT (p<0.001).

## Summary

Abullais and colleagues conducted a retrospective radiographic image analysis of 75 CBCT scans (150 maxillary sinuses) at King Khalid University, Abha, Saudi Arabia, to determine whether facial index type predicts maxillary sinus membrane thickness (MT) or lateral wall thickness (LWT). Facial index was calculated from panfacial CBCT landmarks (nasion, gnathion, zygion) using the Bannister/Williams formula; subjects were classified as euryprosopic (broad), mesoprosopic (round), or leptoprosopic (long). MT and LWT were measured at three points 3 mm apart from the sinus floor in both premolar and molar coronal sections.

The primary finding was **no significant correlation between facial index and either MT or LWT** (all p>0.05), refuting the hypothesis that facial morphotype could serve as a clinical surrogate for sinus anatomy. This means clinicians cannot use a patient's face shape to estimate surgical risk without a CBCT.

Key secondary findings with clinical relevance:

- **LWT sex difference**: Females had significantly thicker LWT than males in both premolar (1.83±0.56 vs 1.54±0.50 mm; p=0.018) and molar (1.89±0.74 vs 1.56±0.59 mm; p=0.032) regions. Thicker LWT prolongs osteotomy and increases heat generation — a sex-specific consideration for the lateral window approach.
- **MT regional difference**: Premolar MT (1.46±0.49 mm) was significantly thicker than molar MT (1.17±0.53 mm; p<0.001). Thicker membrane in the premolar zone may paradoxically offer more protection against perforation but is more likely to exhibit pathological thickening.
- **No MT sex difference**: MT did not differ between males and females (p>0.05), consistent with Munakata 2021 and Kalyvas 2018.
- **Age group 40–49**: The thinnest premolar MT (1.17±0.40 mm; p=0.021 vs other age groups) — a "thin-membrane" alert age band for sinus lift planning.
- **Side correlation**: LWT in the molar region correlated significantly with right vs left side (p=0.009), suggesting mild bilateral asymmetry that CBCT must document bilaterally before bilateral procedures.

## Key Contributions

1. Largest facial-index vs sinus anatomy study to date: confirmed no correlation, closing a gap in the anatomy literature.
2. Saudi Arabian (Abha, high-altitude) population reference values for MT and LWT: MT premolar ~1.46 mm, molar ~1.17 mm; LWT premolar ~1.68 mm, molar ~1.72 mm.
3. Identified female sex as a significant predictor of thicker LWT — a practical pre-surgical CBCT audit point.
4. Premolar-region thicker membrane finding adds nuance to lateral window site selection (molar vs premolar zone).
5. Reinforces that CBCT is mandatory regardless of gross facial morphology — no facial shortcut for risk stratification.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Retrospective CBCT image analysis
- **Sample**: 75 subjects (39 male, 36 female; mean age 32.62±14.22 yr, range 20–60), Saudi nationals, healthy second premolar and first molar present
- **Exclusions**: Sinus pathology, heavy smokers (>20 cig/day), prior sinus surgery or implant, unclear scans
- **Equipment**: Kavo 3D Pro CBCT (89 kVp, 9.0 mA, 16 sec); Cliniview v11.10.1 for analysis
- **Facial index**: (Morphological facial height N–Gn / Bizygomatic width Zyr–Zyl) × 100; Bannister/Williams categories
- **Measurements**: MT and LWT at 3 points × 3 mm intervals from sinus floor in premolar and molar coronal sections; 3 examiners, duplicate measurements 10 days apart
- **Statistics**: t-test, ANOVA, Pearson correlation; α=0.05
