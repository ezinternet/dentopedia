## One-line Summary

Retrospective CBCT study (n=72 sinuses, 59 patients): MSO diameter fell from 1.8 mm to 1.1 mm and infundibulum occlusion peaked at 19.4% one week after lateral-window SFE, fully recovering to 1.5 mm / 1.4% at 9 months with zero sinusitis cases.

## 한줄요약

후향적 CBCT 연구 (n=72 상악동, 59명): 측방창 상악동거상술(Lateral-Window Sinus Floor Elevation, LWSFE) 후 1주에 상악동 자연공(Maxillary Sinus Ostium, MSO) 직경이 1.8→1.1 mm로 감소하고 누두(Infundibulum) 폐쇄가 19.4%까지 증가하지만, 9개월에 1.5 mm/1.4%로 완전 회복되고 부비동염(Sinusitis) 발생 없음.

## Summary

Sakuma et al. performed serial CBCT assessments of the maxillary sinus ostium (MSO) before, 1 week after, and 9 months after lateral-window sinus floor augmentation (SFA) in 72 sinuses from 59 patients. The primary finding was that post-operative edema causes a transient but substantial reduction in MSO patency that resolves completely by 9 months without producing sinusitis.

**Key quantitative findings:**
- MSO diameter: 1.83 mm (T0) → 1.07 mm (T1w) → 1.55 mm (T9m), all differences p=0.0001
- Virtual sinus mucosa thickness: 2.74 mm → 7.70 mm → 1.65 mm (p=0.0001)
- Virtual sinus mucosa area (coronal): 37.2 → 184.5 → 34.0 mm² (p=0.0001)
- Infundibulum out of patency: 3 (4.2%) → 14 (19.4%) → 1 (1.4%)
- In 8 sinuses (11.1%), the palatal edema anatomically reached the MSO at 1 week

**The "virtual" thickness concept:** The paper introduces the term "virtual" to flag that post-op CBCT thickness measurements cannot separate membrane from clot/edema — clinically important because this confounds inter-study comparisons of post-op sinus mucosal change.

**No sinusitis:** Despite 19.4% transient infundibulum occlusion at 1 week, no infectious or inflammatory sinus complications occurred throughout the 9-month follow-up. The one residual infundibulum occlusion at 9 months (1.4%) was also asymptomatic without radiographic pathology.

**Perforations:** 6 membrane perforations occurred and were sealed with collagen membrane. None caused MSO obstruction or sinusitis.

**Accessory maxillary ostia (AMO):** 9.7% prevalence (7/72 sinuses); mean diameter 1.9 mm, positioned ~10.9 mm posteriorly from primary MSO and closer to the sinus floor — an anatomical variable relevant to drainage when the primary MSO is compromised.

## Key Contributions

1. **First serial CBCT quantification of MSO diameter and infundibulum patency** across baseline → 1 week → 9 months after lateral-window SFA.
2. **Resolves concern about sinusitis risk**: transient 19.4% occlusion rate at 1 week is self-limiting; no clinical sinusitis developed.
3. **Defined "virtual" sinus mucosa thickness** as CBCT-inclusive metric (membrane + edema + clot) to standardize inter-study comparisons.
4. **AMO characterization**: 9.7% prevalence; positioned lower and more posteriorly than primary MSO — may provide supplementary drainage when primary MSO is transiently obstructed.
5. **No predictors found** for post-op patency loss (gender, age, smoking, side, edentulism type, ostium position — all NS by logistic regression).

## Methodology

- **Design**: Retrospective observational; STROBE checklist; Ethics approval CURN-0002-CE282020
- **N**: 72 maxillary sinuses (40 right, 32 left), 59 patients (39F, 20M; mean age 53.3 ± 9.1 years)
- **CBCT**: 3D Accuitomo 170 (J. Morita); 80 kV, 8 mA; voxel 0.125 mm; FOV 77×77×74 mm; i-Dixel 2.0 software
- **Timepoints**: T0 (pre-op), T1w (1 week post-op), T9m (9 months post-op)
- **Grafts**: OsteoBiol Gen-Os (porcine cortico-cancellous, 250–1000 µm) or Cerabone (bovine cancellous, 1.0–2.0 mm)
- **Reference plane**: Nose floor (X-axis coronal; Z-axis lateral); anterior nasal spine–nasal septum as vertical reference
- **Statistics**: Friedman test + Nemenyi post-hoc for 3-timepoint data; Wilcoxon for paired T1w–T9m; logistic regression for patency predictors; p<0.05 threshold; κ >0.9 intra-examiner

## Results

### MSO Diameter Over Time

| Timepoint | Mean ± SD (mm) | Range (mm) |
|---|---|---|
| T0 (baseline) | 1.83 ± 0.54 | 0.94–4.6 |
| T1w (1 week) | 1.07 ± 0.65 | 0–2.64 |
| T9m (9 months) | 1.55 ± 0.77 | 0–6.42 |

- 18.1% of sinuses had MSO diameter = 0 at 1 week; 36.1% had diameter < 1 mm
- Mean Δ T1w–T0: −0.8 ± 0.7 mm (range −2.1 to +1.4 mm)

### Infundibulum Patency

| Timepoint | Out of patency (n) | % |
|---|---|---|
| T0 | 3 | 4.2% |
| T1w | 14 | 19.4% |
| T9m | 1 | 1.4% |

The 1.4% residual at T9m was asymptomatic; no accessory ostia present in that patient.

### Edema Extent and Mucosa Volume

- Virtual mucosa thickness: 2.74 → 7.70 → 1.65 mm (p=0.0001); net +5 mm increase at 1 week
- Virtual mucosa area (coronal): 37.2 → 184.5 → 34.0 mm² (Δ +147.3 mm² at 1 week)
- Virtual mucosa area (lateral): 61.8 → 289.5 → 59.2 mm²
- Vertical edema extension (lateral, mesial): 11.9 ± 9.7 mm above Z-axis at T1w → 2.2 ± 5.5 mm at T9m
- Distance palatal edema to MSO: 10.3 ± 9.5 mm (T1w) → 22.6 ± 7.1 mm (T9m); edema reached MSO in 8/72 sinuses at T1w

### Anatomical Data

- Floor-to-MSO distance: 33.3 ± 3.6 mm (min 25.2 mm)
- Infundibulum length: 8.8 ± 1.8 mm
- AMO found in 7 sinuses (9.7%); mean diameter 1.9 ± 0.5 mm; positioned 10.9 mm posteriorly and ~7.8 mm closer to X-axis than primary MSO
