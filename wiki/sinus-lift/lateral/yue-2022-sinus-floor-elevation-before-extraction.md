## One-line Summary
3-year case report of Modified Lateral Sinus Floor Elevation (MSFE) performed before extraction of a periodontally hopeless maxillary molar (#16), augmenting residual bone height from 1–2 mm to 12–14 mm; the tooth was retained 13 months, then extracted with immediate implant placement, with histology showing predominantly new bone and <2 mm bone resorption and no peri-implantitis after 3 years of loading.

## Summary
This first-of-its-kind 3-year case report describes Modified Lateral Sinus Floor Elevation (MSFE) performed *before* the extraction of a periodontally hopeless maxillary molar (#16). The tooth was retained for 13 months while the graft consolidated, then atraumatically extracted with simultaneous immediate implant placement (4.8 × 10 mm SLA Straumann at 35 N·cm). Residual bone height was augmented from 1–2 mm to 12–14 mm. Histology at extraction showed predominantly new bone, and after 3 years of loading the implant remained healthy with <2 mm bone resorption, no peri-implantitis, and no mobility. A contralateral #26 received conventional post-extraction LSFE for informal comparison.

## Key Contributions
- Introduces a novel sequencing strategy that shortens the edentulous interval for patients with hopeless periodontal teeth in the posterior maxilla, where conventional staged LSFE adds 6–9 months.
- Uses the doomed tooth as a biological "tent" / spacer that protects graft volume and preserves partial chewing function during graft maturation.
- Provides histologic and 3-year radiographic evidence supporting the concept.

## Methodology
- 33 y female, aggressive periodontitis, hopeless #16 and #26 (Class II–III mobility, PD 6–10 mm, RBH 1–2 mm).
- IPT + monthly SPT to achieve a remittent periodontal status before MSFE.
- MSFE on #16: 5 × 8 mm lateral antrostomy at root apex (≥3–5 mm above sinus floor), sinus membrane elevated ≥10 mm, Bio-Gide collagen membrane lining, 1.25 g Bio-Oss graft, Bio-Gide cover, Prolene 4-0 sutures.
- 13 months of supportive care; tooth pulp vitality preserved.
- Atraumatic extraction → 2-mm trephine biopsy → immediate implant (Straumann 4.8 × 10 mm SLA, 35 N·cm); gap <2 mm so no extra graft.
- 4-month unloaded healing → cement-retained zirconia crown.
- Contralateral #26 underwent conventional LSFE after extraction with same materials.

## Results
- Bone height: 1–2 mm → 12–14 mm before extraction.
- Histology (biopsy at extraction): predominance of new bone.
- 3-year post-loading: bone resorption <2 mm; PD 2–4 mm; no peri-implantitis or mobility.
- Edentulous interval substantially shortened vs the conventional #26 arm (>7 mo).
