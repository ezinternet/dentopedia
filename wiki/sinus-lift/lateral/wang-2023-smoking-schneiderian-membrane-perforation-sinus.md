## Summary

Wang et al. (2023) conducted the first systematic review and meta-analysis quantifying the association between smoking and Schneiderian membrane perforation (슈나이더 막 천공, SMP) during maxillary sinus floor augmentation (상악동거상술, SFE). Nine studies (8 retrospective + 1 clinical trial, n=1,424 patients) were included after searching PubMed, Web of Science, Embase, and the Cochrane Library. A random-effects meta-analysis of 8 studies yielded OR 1.58 (95% CI 1.10–2.25), confirming smoking as a significant modifiable risk factor for SMP. Heterogeneity was low (I²=5%), and there was no significant publication bias (Egger's test p=0.827).

## Key Contributions

1. **First SR+MA on smoking and SMP** — fills the gap in the risk-factor literature that previously focused on anatomical variables (sinus septa, membrane thickness, lateral wall thickness) but lacked pooled data on systemic/behavioral risk factors.
2. **OR 1.58 (95% CI 1.10–2.25)** — a ~58% increased odds of perforation in smokers vs non-smokers; statistically significant (p<0.05).
3. **Low heterogeneity (I²=5%)** — the risk signal is consistent across retrospective cohorts with differing techniques and patient populations.
4. **No publication bias detected** (Egger's test p=0.827) — result is unlikely an artifact of selective reporting.
5. **Clinical implication**: Smoking status should be assessed preoperatively; cessation counseling before SFE may reduce intraoperative complication risk.

## Methodology

- **PROSPERO registration**: CRD42022306570
- **Databases**: PubMed, Web of Science, Embase, Cochrane Library
- **Inclusion criteria**: Age >18; n >10; smoking and perforation accurately recorded
- **Studies included**: 9 (8 retrospective cohorts, 1 clinical trial); n=1,424 patients
- **Risk of bias tool**: Newcastle–Ottawa Scale (NOS); all 9 rated high quality
- **Statistical model**: Random-effects (due to methodological heterogeneity)
- **Effect measure**: Odds ratio (OR) with 95% CI
- **Heterogeneity**: I² statistic
- **Publication bias**: Funnel plot + Egger's test
- **Software**: RevMan 5.4.1, Stata 15.0
- **Reporting standard**: PRISMA 2020

## Results

|---|---|
| Studies in systematic review | 9 |
| Studies in meta-analysis | 8 |
| Total patients | 1,424 |
| Study quality (NOS) | All high quality |
| Pooled OR (smoker vs non-smoker) | **1.58** |

| Heterogeneity I² | 5% (low) |
| Heterogeneity chi-square p | 0.39 |
| Egger's test p-value | 0.827 (NS) |

**Interpretation**: Smokers undergoing maxillary sinus floor augmentation have 58% higher odds of sustaining Schneiderian membrane perforation compared to non-smokers. The consistency across studies (I²=5%) and absence of publication bias (p=0.827) strengthen the clinical applicability of this finding.

### Mechanism (discussed by authors)

Smoking impairs the mucociliary function and vascular supply of the sinus mucosa (상악동 점막, Schneiderian membrane), making it thinner, more fragile, and prone to tearing. Nicotine-induced vasoconstriction reduces mucosal perfusion, and carbon monoxide impairs oxygen delivery. The result is a structurally compromised membrane that tears more readily during elevation.

### Limitations

- **Dose–response unavailable**: Poor standardization of cigarettes-per-day reporting across included studies; the authors could not establish a threshold or dose–response curve.
- **Mostly retrospective evidence**: 8 of 9 studies are retrospective — selection and information biases cannot be fully excluded.
- **Technique heterogeneity**: Included studies used varied surgical approaches, instruments, and operator experience levels.
- **Definition inconsistency**: Definitions of "smoker" and "non-smoker" varied across studies.

## Clinical Decision Implications

| Scenario | Implication |
|---|---|
| Preoperative screening | Ask specifically about smoking status before scheduling SFE |
| Consent | Counsel smokers on ~58% increased membrane perforation risk |
| Preoperative cessation | Evidence supports recommending cessation before SFE to reduce complication risk |
| Intraoperative preparation | Higher index of suspicion; have membrane repair materials (PRF, collagen membrane) ready for smokers |
