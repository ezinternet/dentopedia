## One-line Summary

Prospective cohort (n=140) of lateral-window SFE identifies four independent perforation risk factors with quantified hazard ratios: sinus septa HR 8.07, mucous retention cyst HR 27.75, smoking HR 25, multi-tooth edentulous span HR 68.09.

## Summary

Nemati and colleagues (2023) conducted a prospective cohort study at Shahid Beheshti University of Medical Sciences (Iran) to quantify the independent risk contribution of anatomical, habitual, and pathological factors to intraoperative Schneiderian membrane perforation during the lateral-window sinus floor augmentation (SFE) approach. A total of 140 consecutive subjects (81 male, 59 female; mean age 54.66 ± 11.73 years) with residual bone height < 5 mm underwent pre-operative CBCT and lateral-window SFE surgery. Pre-operative CBCT assessed the presence of sinus septa, mucous retention cysts, lateral wall thickness, membrane thickness, and residual bone height; smoking status, age, and gender were also recorded.

Cox proportional hazards regression identified four independent perforation risk factors, all highly statistically significant (p < 0.001). The study is notable for providing quantified hazard ratios in a prospective design — a methodological step above most retrospective or cross-sectional literature on this topic. The results directly support chairside pre-operative risk stratification using CBCT and patient history.

## Key Contributions

- Provides prospective, multi-variable quantification of perforation risk using hazard ratios (not just prevalence comparisons).
- Identifies sinus septa as an 8× independent risk multiplier.
- Quantifies smoking as a 25× risk factor — the single most impactful modifiable factor.
- Identifies mucous retention cyst (MRC) as a 27.75× risk factor — the strongest anatomical predictor.
- Demonstrates that multi-tooth edentulous span carries an extreme HR of 68.09 vs single-tooth gaps, likely reflecting longer, technically more demanding membrane elevation.
- Supports a structured pre-operative CBCT checklist for risk communication with patients.

## Methodology

- **Study design**: Prospective cohort
- **Institution**: Dept. of Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery, Shahid Beheshti University, Iran
- **Period**: January 2019 – September 2021
- **n**: 140 subjects (81 M, 59 F; mean age 54.66 ± 11.73 years)
- **Inclusion**: Edentulous posterior maxilla, RBH < 5 mm, candidate for lateral-window SFE
- **Pre-operative imaging**: CBCT for all subjects
- **Outcome**: Intra-operative presence/absence of Schneiderian membrane perforation
- **Predictors assessed**:
  - Sinus septa (present/absent)
  - Mucous retention cyst (present/absent)
  - Lateral wall (buccal bone) thickness (continuous, mm)
  - Membrane thickness (continuous, mm)
  - Residual bone height (continuous, mm)
- **Covariates**: Age, gender, smoking status, edentulous span (single vs ≥2 teeth)
- **Statistics**: Cox proportional hazards regression; HR with 95% CI

## Results

### Quantified Hazard Ratios for Membrane Perforation

| Risk Factor | Hazard Ratio (HR) | 95% CI | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sinus septa present | **8.07** | 2.93–22.29 | < 0.001 |
| Mucous retention cyst present | **27.75** | 8.73–88.23 | < 0.001 |
| Smoking (vs non-smoking) | **25.00** | 7.58–82.51 | < 0.001 |
| Multi-tooth edentulous span (≥2 teeth vs 1 tooth) | **68.09** | 9.52–49.16* | not reported separately |

*CI bounds as published — lower bound 9.52 appears internally consistent; upper bound may be a typographic issue in the original abstract.

### Additional risk factors stated in conclusion (direction confirmed; magnitude not fully extracted from abstract):
- Increasing age → increased perforation risk
- Thinner lateral wall → increased perforation risk
- Lower residual bone height → increased perforation risk
- Thinner membrane → increased perforation risk (note: this is paradoxical with the standard hydrodynamic fragility model — thick membranes may be inflamed/friable; thin membranes may be fragile; the direction of the continuous HR requires full-text confirmation)

### Background context
- Published perforation rate for lateral-window SFE: 10%–60% across the literature.
- This cohort's mean membrane thickness (0.77 mm) is at the thin-fragile threshold (< 0.8 mm = risk per Munakata 2021 data).

## Clinical Implications

### Pre-operative CBCT checklist for perforation risk communication

Use CBCT to assess before lateral-window SFE:

| CBCT/Clinical Finding | Approximate Risk Multiplier | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Mucous retention cyst present | HR ~28× | Discuss cyst management options; consider pseudocyst overview protocol |
| Smoking history | HR ~25× (modifiable) | Counsel cessation; document refusal; consent for elevated perforation risk |
| Sinus septa present | HR ~8× | Modify osteotomy design; piezoelectric instrumentation preferred; plan for 2-window approach |
| Multi-tooth span (≥2 teeth) | HR ~68× vs single tooth | Acknowledge extreme risk; ensure repair materials (PRF membrane, collagen) on standby |
| Thin lateral wall | (magnitude TBD) | Heightened care with osteotomy; ensure no aggressive lateral wall removal |
| Low RBH (< 3 mm) | (magnitude TBD) | Steeper membrane elevation arc; risk of septal angulation |

### Repair preparedness
Given the extremely high HR for MRC (27.75) and smoking (25), these patients should have membrane repair materials (PRF, collagen membrane, suture) ready before surgery begins — not retrieved intra-operatively after perforation.
