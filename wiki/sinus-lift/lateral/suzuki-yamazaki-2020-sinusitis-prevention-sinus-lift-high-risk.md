## One-line Summary

Case report (n=1, 48M) in which preoperative topical fluticasone + pranlukast (LTRA) + perioperative antibiotics prevented post-sinus-lift maxillary sinusitis in a 4-factor high-risk patient (septal deviation, concha bullosa, Haller cell, allergic rhinitis); bilateral sinus lift uneventful at 7 months.

## Summary

This single case report (Ohu University, Japan) demonstrates a noninvasive medical prevention protocol for post-sinus-lift maxillary sinusitis (Post-Sinus-Lift Maxillary Sinusitis, PSLMS) in an otorhinolaryngologically high-risk patient. A 48-year-old male with a 1-year history of alternating nasal congestion and known nasal allergy was referred from periodontist to ENT prior to planned bilateral sinus lift. Preoperative CT and nasal endoscopy revealed four concurrent anatomic-functional impairments:

1. Left septal deviation (비중격만곡)
2. Left concha bullosa of the middle turbinate (함기화 중비갑개)
3. Left Haller cell (할러 세포) narrowing the maxillary ostium
4. Nasal mucosal swelling from allergic rhinitis — no active sinusitis

The patient was classified as a "reversible otorhinolaryngological contraindication" (Pignataro criteria). Surgical options (FESS + septoplasty, Kunihiro minimally invasive antrostomy) were offered but declined. Medical conservative therapy was then administered for 2 months preoperatively:

- **Topical nasal steroid**: fluticasone propionate aqueous nasal spray
- **LTRA**: pranlukast hydrate (for allergic-rhinitis-driven mucosal swelling)
- **Perioperative antibiotic prophylaxis**: amoxicillin (Sawacillin®)

After 2 months, nasal congestion resolved almost completely. Bilateral maxillary sinus floor elevation was performed with Cytrans Granules® (GC) bone graft. At 2-month postoperative panoramic radiography no sinusitis was detected. Clinical course remained uneventful through 7-month follow-up.

## Key Contributions

- Establishes a concrete **3-drug noninvasive prevention protocol** for sinus lift in ENT-contraindicated (reversible) patients
- First published use of LTRA (pranlukast) specifically as a component of pre-sinus-lift prophylaxis
- Demonstrates ENT–dentist interdisciplinary triage as a patient benefit (avoids FESS in unwilling patients)
- Clarifies why vasoconstrictors/nasal decongestants should be avoided: risk of further reducing already-low sinus oxygen tension

## Patient Profile and ENT Risk Stratification

| Risk Factor | Finding |
|---|---|
| Septal deviation (비중격만곡) | Left; impairs left sinus drainage |
| Concha bullosa (함기화 중비갑개) | Left middle turbinate; narrows ostiomeatal complex |
| Haller cell (할러 세포) | Left; reduces maxillary ostium cross-section |
| Nasal mucosal swelling | Allergic rhinitis; dynamic additional obstruction |
| Accessory ostium | Left maxillary sinus; no sinusitis at baseline |
| **Classification** | Reversible contraindication (Pignataro) = "high-risk case" |

## Medical Prevention Protocol

| Agent | Drug | Timing | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Topical nasal steroid (비강 국소 스테로이드) | Fluticasone propionate nasal spray | 2 months preoperative | Reduce mucosal inflammation, promote ostial patency |
| LTRA (류코트리엔 수용체 길항제) | Pranlukast hydrate | 2 months preoperative | Reduce eosinophilic mucosal swelling from nasal allergy (CysLT1 blockade) |
| Perioperative antibiotic (주술기 항생제) | Amoxicillin (Sawacillin®) | Perioperative | Standard sinus lift prophylaxis against infection |
| Vasoconstrictor | NOT used | — | Avoided: further reduces sinus O₂ tension |

## Pignataro Contraindication Classification

The ENT contraindication taxonomy underpins this case's clinical logic:

**Irreversible contraindications** (sinus lift absolutely contraindicated):
- Wegener's granulomatosis, sarcoidosis, inverted papilloma, malignant nasosinus neoplasms

**Reversible contraindications** (sinus lift conditionally contraindicated — addressable by ENT treatment):
- Anatomic-structural impairments of maxillary sinus drainage pathways (septal deviation, concha bullosa, Haller cell)
- Phlogistic-infective processes (allergic rhinitis, chronic rhinosinusitis)

**Management path for reversible cases**:
1. Preferred: surgical correction (FESS ± septoplasty ± Kunihiro minimally invasive antrostomy)
2. If patient declines surgery: medical conservative therapy (this case)

## Outcome

- Nasal congestion: resolved almost completely after 2 months of medical therapy
- Intraoperative: no complications during bilateral sinus lift
- Postoperative (2-month panoramic): no sinusitis; graft material bilaterally present
- Clinical (7-month): uneventful

## Limitations

- n=1; no comparative data or control group
- 7-month follow-up only
- Contribution of LTRA vs steroid alone cannot be disentangled
- Pranlukast dosing/duration rationale not detailed
- Authors explicitly call for clinical trials to validate this regimen
