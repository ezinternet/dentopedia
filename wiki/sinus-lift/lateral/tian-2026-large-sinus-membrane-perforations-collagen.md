## 한줄요약
대형 (>10 mm) 슈나이더리안 막 (Schneiderian membrane) 천공 시 콜라겐 막을 상악동 내측·외측벽에 택 (tack)으로 고정해 동시 골이식을 가능하게 하는 측방창 (Lateral window) 술기 노트.

## Summary
Large (>10 mm) Schneiderian membrane perforations during lateral-window maxillary sinus floor elevation (SFE) often force the surgeon to abandon grafting and adopt a delayed reentry approach. Tian et al. describe a reproducible technique using a resorbable collagen membrane anchored to the **mesial and lateral sinus walls with fixation tacks**, creating a stable subantral compartment that accepts particulate graft and, when primary stability permits, simultaneous implant placement. The aim is to convert an "abort" scenario into a one-stage augmentation.

## Key Contributions
- Defines a stepwise method for handling perforations >10 mm without aborting the SFE.
- Uses mechanical fixation (tacks) rather than relying on adhesion of the membrane to the residual mucosa, providing more predictable containment of the graft.
- Permits simultaneous implant placement when residual ridge height supports primary stability.

## Methodology
- Lateral-window osteotomy and elevation of the Schneiderian membrane.
- On encountering a large (>10 mm) perforation, a resorbable collagen membrane is trimmed to size and draped over the defect.
- The membrane is anchored with bone tacks into the mesial and lateral bony walls of the sinus cavity.
- Particulate bone graft is condensed into the tented compartment beneath the membrane.
- Implants are placed at the same session when feasible; otherwise staged.

## Results
Specific quantitative outcomes are not exposed in the publicly available abstract/preview. The authors report the technique as feasible and permissive of simultaneous grafting and implant placement in the cases described.
