## 한줄요약
9건의 대형 슈나이더리안 막 (Schneiderian Membrane, SM) 천공 사례에서 백혈구·혈소판풍부피브린 (Leukocyte- and Platelet-Rich Fibrin, L-PRF) 막 + 탈단백우골미네랄 (Deproteinized Bovine Bone Mineral, DBBM, Bio-Oss) + 콜라겐 막 조합으로 봉합 → 8개월 후 임플란트 13개 식립, 3–5년 추적에서 전부 골유착·감염 없음.

## Summary
Long-term case series of 9 patients with large Schneiderian membrane perforations during lateral-window sinus floor augmentation (SFA), repaired by interposing L-PRF membranes over the perforation, grafting the antrum with Bio-Oss, and covering the lateral window with a collagen membrane. At 8 months, 13 implants were placed with satisfactory primary stability. At 3–5 year follow-up, all 13 implants were osseointegrated radiographically and no signs of sinus infection or mucus accumulation were detected on CBCT/panoramic imaging.

## Key Contributions
- Demonstrates feasibility of **completing SFA in the same session as a large SM perforation** when L-PRF is available.
- Provides long-term (3–5 year) outcome data for an L-PRF-based perforation repair protocol — most prior reports were short-term.
- Adds clinical evidence supporting **autologous biologic membranes** as an alternative or adjunct to collagen patches for "large" perforations exceeding collagen-only repair capacity.

## Methodology
- Design: clinical case series, n = 9 patients with intraoperative SM large perforation during lateral-window SFA.
- Repair protocol:
  1. L-PRF membranes (Choukroun protocol) prepared from patient blood, no anticoagulant.
  2. L-PRF interposed over the perforated SM until rupture was no longer visualized — relies on fibrin's natural adhesive and elastic properties.
  3. Maxillary sinus cavity filled with DBBM (Bio-Oss, Geistlich).
  4. Lateral access window covered by resorbable collagen membrane.
- Re-entry: 8 months post-SFA, 13 implants placed.
- Follow-up: CBCT or panoramic radiography at 3–5 years post-implant for qualitative assessment.

## Results
- **Perforation closure**: 9/9 SM large perforations successfully sealed with L-PRF.
- **Implant placement**: 13/13 implants placed at 8 months with satisfactory primary stability.
- **Long-term outcomes (3–5 years)**:
  - 13/13 implants osseointegrated (radiographic).
  - No signs of postoperative sinusitis, mucus accumulation, or graft migration on imaging.
- **Complications**: none reported.

## Clinical Implications (Author Memo)
- **Have L-PRF capacity ready** before any lateral-window SFA; even "favorable" anatomy can produce large perforations.
- L-PRF + DBBM + collagen is a workable combined-repair strategy that lets the surgeon **complete grafting in the same session** rather than aborting.
- Caveat: no control arm, no quantitative perforation sizing, no histology — evidence level is case-series; controlled trials needed to compare L-PRF-only vs collagen-only vs combined repair.
