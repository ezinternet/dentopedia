## One-line Summary

Retrospective study (n=120, 11 years, Korean tertiary center) showing MSMT/MOD ratio — not MOD alone — significantly differentiates preoperative sinusitis-risk groups; osteomeatal complex status provides additional independent risk signal for sinus augmentation planning.

## 한줄요약

한국 3차병원 11년 후향적 연구(n=120)에서 상악동점막두께(MSMT)/자연공-동저 거리(MOD) 비율이 세 군 간 유의한 차이를 보인 반면 MOD 단독은 무의미; 자연공-골수계(OMC) 폐쇄가 독립적 위험인자임을 확인.

## Summary

Lee et al. (2020) performed an 11-year retrospective study at Seoul St. Mary's Hospital (Catholic University of Korea) on 120 patients referred from periodontology to otolaryngology before maxillary sinus augmentation. The study introduced and validated three CBCT-derived preoperative parameters: 상악동점막두께 (Maxillary Sinus Mucosal Thickness, MSMT), 자연공-동저 거리 (distance between ostium and sinus floor, MOD), and the MSMT/MOD 비율 (MSMT/MOD ratio) as a relative occupancy index.

Patients were stratified into three groups based on clinical management: Group A (no preoperative antibiotics; n=95), Group B (preoperative antibiotics; n=16), and Group C (implant placed after FESS; n=9). MSMT and MSMT/MOD ratio showed significant between-group differences at all pairings, while MOD showed none — demonstrating that the relative metric (ratio) captures pathological sinus burden more sensitively than either absolute measurement alone. The institution's low postoperative sinusitis rate (1.6% vs. literature mean 5.1%) was attributed to systematic dentist–ENT collaboration and CBCT-based preoperative screening.

Critically, one case with a low MSMT/MOD ratio (0.23) still developed postoperative sinusitis due to ostium obstruction, confirming that osteomeatal complex (OMC) status must be assessed independently of mucosal thickness metrics.

## Key Contributions

1. **MSMT/MOD ratio** as a novel preoperative risk metric: the proportion of sinus depth occupied by mucosal disease, providing functional context to absolute mucosal thickness
2. Demonstrates MOD itself is not a discriminator (p=NS across all group pairs) — only the ratio matters
3. Establishes a clinical FESS threshold in practice: mean MSMT/MOD 0.78 in the FESS-then-implant group
4. Confirms OMC obstruction as an independent sinusitis risk factor that can override a favorable MSMT/MOD ratio
5. Demonstrates 1.6% postoperative sinusitis rate achievable with strict dentist-ENT collaboration protocol

## Methodology

- **Design**: Retrospective chart review; 11-year period (October 2008–October 2019)
- **Center**: Seoul St. Mary's Hospital, Catholic University of Korea (tertiary referral center)
- **n**: 120 patients; mean age 60.1 ± 13.5 years; 47.6% male
- **Imaging**: CBCT (all patients) prior to implant placement
- **Groups**: A (n=95 no Ab), B (n=16 preop Ab), C (n=9 post-FESS)
- **Key variables**: MSMT, MOD, MSMT/MOD ratio, ostium obstruction (CBCT), anatomical variants (Haller cells, paradoxical middle turbinate, concha bullosa), postoperative sinusitis
- **Statistics**: Kruskal–Wallis + post-hoc Mann–Whitney with Bonferroni correction (SAS v9.4)

## Results

| Parameter | Group A | Group B | Group C |
|---|---|---|---|
| MSMT (mm) | 5.68 ± 5.74 | 11.84 ± 8.22 | 25.3 ± 11.1 |
| MOD (mm) | 30.33 ± 5.08 | 29.98 ± 7.31 | 31.35 ± 6.81 |
| MSMT/MOD ratio | 0.19 ± 0.19 | 0.39 ± 0.25 | 0.78 ± 0.24 |

- MSMT: significant A vs B (p=0.001), B vs C (p=0.003), C vs A (p<0.0001)
- MOD: NS at all pairings (p=0.804, 0.502, 0.637)
- MSMT/MOD ratio: significant A vs B (p=0.001), B vs C (p<0.0001), C vs A (p<0.0001)
- Postoperative sinusitis: 2/120 (1.6%)
- Anatomical variants: concha bullosa 25.0%; Haller cells 11.0%; paradoxical middle turbinate 0.8%
- Ostium obstruction on CBCT: 2/120 (1.7%) — both of the sinusitis cases had ostium-related findings
- One sinusitis case had MSMT/MOD ratio of only 0.23 but ostium obstruction → highlights OMC as independent predictor
