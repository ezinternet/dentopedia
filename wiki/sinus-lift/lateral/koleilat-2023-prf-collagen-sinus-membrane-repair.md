## One-line Summary

Case report (70-year-old male, non-restorable maxillary molar, lateral window approach with incidentally discovered antral pseudocyst): a Schneiderian membrane perforation created during pseudocyst removal was repaired with platelet-rich fibrin (PRF) plus a resorbable collagen membrane; re-entry showed a thickened intact fibrotic membrane, and sinus elevation with implant placement proceeded uneventfully.

## 한줄요약
증례 보고 (70세 남성, 비복원성 상악 대구치, 측방창 (Lateral Window) 접근, 우연히 발견된 상악동 가성낭종 (Antral Pseudocyst)): 가성낭종 제거 중 발생한 슈나이더 막 (Schneiderian Membrane) 천공을 혈소판 풍부 피브린 (Platelet-Rich Fibrin, PRF) + 흡수성 콜라겐 막 조합으로 봉합하여 재진입 시 두꺼워진 섬유성 막이 유지되었고, 상악동 거상술 (Sinus Lift) 및 임플란트 식립 성공.

## Summary
A 70-year-old healthy male presented for a non-restorable maxillary molar implant; preoperative CBCT showed insufficient residual ridge height and incidentally revealed an **antral pseudocyst** on the sinus floor. A staged plan was adopted: lateral-window approach was performed and the pseudocyst removed; this created a **Schneiderian membrane perforation**. Repair was done with **PRF membranes layered over the perforation and stabilized with a resorbable collagen membrane**. After a healing interval, re-entry showed a **thickened but intact fibrotic membrane**; standard sinus elevation and implant placement proceeded uneventfully. Histology of the original biopsy confirmed pseudocyst. The authors argue the PRF + collagen combination accelerates membrane healing through PRF-released growth factors (TGF-β, VEGF, PDGF), shortening total treatment time compared with conventional staged sinus grafting alone.

## Key Contributions
- Documents a **specific protocol** for the dual problem of sinus floor pathology (pseudocyst) plus iatrogenic membrane perforation.
- Adds a clinical data point supporting **PRF + collagen** (rather than collagen alone) for sinus membrane perforation repair.
- Highlights the value of **preoperative CBCT** to detect occult sinus pathology before lateral-window surgery.

## Methodology
- Single patient case report; staged surgical protocol.
- Stage 1: lateral window osteotomy, pseudocyst excision and histologic confirmation, PRF + collagen membrane repair of resulting perforation, healing interval.
- Stage 2: re-entry, sinus elevation with bone graft, implant placement.
- PRF prepared by centrifugation of autologous blood without anticoagulant.

## Results
- Histology: pseudostratified ciliated columnar epithelium overlying connective tissue → antral pseudocyst confirmed.
- Re-entry: thickened but intact fibrotic Schneiderian membrane.
- Sinus elevation and implant placement uneventful; no further perforation.
- Author claim: shorter overall timeline vs conventional staged approach.
