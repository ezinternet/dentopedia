## Summary

This Bayesian network meta-analysis is the first to simultaneously rank both lateral sinus floor elevation (LSFE) and transcrestal sinus floor elevation (TSFE) instrument subtypes by their Sinus Membrane Perforation (SMP) risk. Eighty-five studies comprising 5,551 SFE procedures were included. Within the LSFE group, rotary bur use produced the worst SMP ranking (SUCRA = 0.0745), suggesting the highest perforation risk. Within the TSFE group, reamer drilling showed the best ranking (SUCRA = 0.9444), i.e., the lowest SMP risk. Critically, the analysis quantified that each 10% increase in SMP rate is associated with a 55% reduction in implant survival odds (OR = 0.45, 95% CrI: 0.21–0.71, P < .001), establishing a significant dose-response link between technique-related perforation burden and implant outcomes. Vertical ridge gain was also examined. The authors concluded that technique selection within both LSFE and TSFE categories can meaningfully reduce SMP risk and downstream implant failure risk, while calling for standardized RCTs to enable direct LSFE-vs-TSFE comparisons.

## Key Contributions

1. **First NMA for SFE technique vs SMP**: enables simultaneous indirect comparison of multiple instruments without requiring head-to-head RCTs for every pair.
2. **SUCRA-based ranking**: provides clinician-friendly ordinal ranking of instrument-technique combinations for SMP prevention counseling.
3. **Survival dose-response**: OR = 0.45 per 10% SMP increase — first quantitative estimate of the SMP-to-survival damage function.
4. **Prevention perspective**: complements post-perforation repair literature by addressing upstream risk reduction.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review + Bayesian NMA
- **Literature period**: January 1960 – August 2021
- **Included studies**: 85 (prospective and retrospective designs)
- **Total procedures**: 5,551 SFE
- **Primary outcome**: SMP incidence by technique/instrument
- **Ranking**: SUCRA (0–1; higher = safer)
- **Secondary outcomes**: vertical ridge gain, implant survival rate stratified by SMP status
- **Survival analysis**: OR with 95% Bayesian credible intervals; significant = CrI excludes 1.0

## Results

### LSFE — Technique Ranking (SMP Risk, SUCRA)

| Instrument/Technique | SUCRA | SMP Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Rotary bur | 0.0745 | Highest |
| Piezoelectric / other | intermediate | Moderate |

> Rotary bur is the worst performer in the LSFE group for SMP prevention.

### TSFE — Technique Ranking (SMP Risk, SUCRA)

| Instrument/Technique | SUCRA | SMP Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Reamer | 0.9444 | **Lowest** |
| Osteotome / balloon | lower | Higher than reamer |

> Reamer-based TSFE is the safest technique class for SMP avoidance across all techniques assessed.

### SMP → Implant Survival

- OR = 0.45 (95% CrI: 0.21–0.71) per 10% increase in SMP prevalence
- P < .001; direction: higher perforation burden → significantly lower survival
- Implication: a center with 20% vs 10% SMP rate is predicted to have substantially worse implant survival odds

- SMP impact on vertical bone gain also evaluated; full data in article (abstract-only available).
