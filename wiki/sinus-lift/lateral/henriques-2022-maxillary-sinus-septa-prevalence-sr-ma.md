## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 62 studies (22,460 sinuses; 13,701 patients) establishing pooled maxillary sinus septa prevalence at 33.2% per sinus and 41.0% per patient, with most septa in the mid-sinus region and 86% transversely oriented.

## Summary

Henriques et al. conducted a PROSPERO-registered (CRD42019124933) SR+MA searching PubMed, Scopus, and Web of Science up to October 5, 2020. Of 62 included studies involving 13,701 patients and 22,460 maxillary sinuses assessed by CBCT or CT, the pooled **per-sinus prevalence of septa was 33.2%** (95% CI 27.8–38.5%; I² = 98.32%) based on 35 studies, and the **per-patient prevalence was 41.0%** (95% CI 36.0–46.0%; I² = 96.45%) based on 42 studies. The sex-stratified OR was 0.785 (95% CI 0.590–1.046; P = 0.098) — no significant sex difference. Septa were most frequent in the **middle third** of the sinus and with a **transverse orientation in 86.0%** of cases. Very high between-study heterogeneity (I² ≈ 96–98%) was partially attributed to differing septum height-thresholds and study populations by meta-regression.

## Key Contributions

- Provides the largest pooled **baseline prevalence estimate** for maxillary sinus septa (62 studies; supersedes Pommer 2012 at n=9 studies).
- Confirms **transverse** orientation as the dominant morphology (86%) — the orientation that bisects the lateral window osteotomy and mandates a two-window technique or piezoelectric septum removal.
- Demonstrates **no significant sex difference** (P = 0.098), ruling out sex as a clinical screening criterion.
- Identifies mid-sinus as the **most common location** — the region where the lateral window osteotomy is typically placed, maximizing conflict probability.
- Provides the **denominator** needed to translate Yang 2024's perforation OR (>4×) into absolute pre-operative risk.

## Methodology

- **Design:** PROSPERO-registered SR+MA; dual independent reviewers; Cohen's κ for inter-rater reliability.
- **Databases:** PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science; search cutoff October 5, 2020; bibliography hand-search; author contact.
- **Quality appraisal:** Joanna Briggs Institute (JBI) critical appraisal checklist for prevalence studies (10-item).
- **Statistical approach:** Freeman–Tukey double-arcsine transformation for proportion stabilization; random-effects model; Cochran Q and I² for heterogeneity; meta-regression for source-of-heterogeneity analysis; funnel plot for publication bias.
- **Inclusion:** CBCT or CT studies reporting maxillary sinus septa prevalence; ≥10 sinuses analyzed.

- **Sex difference:** OR 0.785, P = 0.098 — not significant.
- **Location:** Mid-sinus most frequent.
- **Orientation:** Transverse 86.0%; this is clinically the most consequential morphology.
- **Total literature:** 62 studies, 13,701 patients, 22,460 sinuses.

### Clinical translation: from prevalence to risk

Combining Henriques 2022 prevalence data with Yang 2024 risk data:
- ~33% of sinuses harbor septa (this paper).
- Presence of septa → OR >4× for Schneiderian membrane perforation during lateral SFE (Yang 2024).
- Septa in 86% transverse orientation → most will intersect the planned window osteotomy line.

**Bottom line:** roughly 1 in 3 sinuses requires a modified lateral-window approach. Preoperative CBCT for septa detection is warranted before every lateral SFE.
