## One-line Summary

Double-center retrospective case series on the reamer-drilling lateral window approach (LWA) for maxillary sinus augmentation: Schneiderian membrane perforation rate was 12.5% (lower than the historical 20–30% with conventional bur/piezo), with lateral wall thickness ≥1.25 mm as the threshold below which perforation risk rises — judged a safe, effective alternative.

## Summary
Two-center retrospective case series on the reamer-drilling lateral window approach. Sinus membrane perforation rate 12.5% — lower than historical 20–30% with conventional bur or piezo techniques. Lateral wall thickness ≥1.25 mm is the threshold below which perforation risk rises.

## Key Contributions
- First focused outcome report for the reamer-drilling LWA technique.
- Defines 1.25 mm lateral wall thickness cutoff for safe use.
- Compares favorably to historical bur/piezo perforation rates.

## Methodology
- Retrospective two-center case series.
- Lateral window opened with dedicated reamer drill.
- Outcome: intraoperative Schneiderian membrane perforation (yes/no, size).
- Co-variables: lateral wall thickness, membrane thickness, septa, residual bone height, surgeon experience.

## Results
- **Overall perforation rate**: 12.5%.
- **Lateral wall <1.25 mm**: slightly higher perforation rate.
- **Lateral wall ≥1.25 mm**: reamer drilling judged safe and effective.
- Septa noted as independent risk factor (consistent with literature).
- No significant effect of membrane thickness alone in this series.

## Clinical Relevance
- Pre-op CBCT: measure lateral wall thickness; ≥1.25 mm → reamer drill viable.
- Reamer drilling is a useful alternative when piezo not available — gentler than diamond bur on the membrane.
- Confirm septa presence on CBCT; if present, plan modified window or use piezo regardless of technique.
