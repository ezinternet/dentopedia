## One-line Summary

Pilot preclinical animal study (rabbit sinus model, n=6): re-entry sinus floor elevation performed 8 weeks after intentional complete sinus membrane perforation (≥10 mm) sealed with collagen matrix yielded significantly less new bone area than intact controls (5.8±2.7 vs 10.5±1.9 mm², P<0.05), indicating large perforation impairs bone formation in subsequent re-entry SFE.

## Summary
Pilot animal study (Kyung Hee University, n=6 rabbits) evaluating early bone formation outcomes when re-entry sinus floor elevation (SFE) is performed 8 weeks after intentional complete sinus membrane perforation (≥10mm) sealed with collagen matrix.

## Key Contributions
- **Complete perforation (>10mm)** followed by collagen matrix repair; re-entry SFE at 8 weeks
- **Micro-CT**: no significant differences in total augmented or mineralized tissue volume
- **Histomorphometry**: new bone area significantly smaller in SFE_ComPerf vs SFE control:
  - SFE_ComPerf: **5.8±2.7 mm²**
  - SFE intact: **10.5±1.9 mm²** (P<0.05)
- Histology: impaired new bone formation near sinus membrane in SFE_ComPerf group
- Conclusion: complete perforation may compromise bone formation in subsequent re-entry SFE

## Clinical Implications
- Large sinus membrane perforation (>10mm) impairs bone formation when SFE is re-attempted
- Collagen matrix alone may not fully restore membrane integrity for bone formation
- Staged re-entry after large perforation should allow extended healing > 8 weeks
- Further clinical studies needed to translate rabbit findings to humans
