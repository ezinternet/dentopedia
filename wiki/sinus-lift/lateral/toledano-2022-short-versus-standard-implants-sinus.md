## 한줄요약
MA (14 RCTs, 901임플란트): 단임플란트(≤6mm) = 표준+상악동거상 생존 동등; MBL ≤1년 0.11mm, >1년 0.23mm 더 적음

## Summary
Meta-analysis of 14 RCTs comparing short implants (≤6 mm, no sinus lift) vs. standard implants (≥8 mm, with sinus lift) in atrophic posterior maxilla. **Implant survival is equivalent**, but **short implants show significantly less MBL** at all time points. Short implants also offer less morbidity and shorter treatment time.

## Key Contributions
- Survival: **RR = 1.02** (p = 0.09) — no significant difference
- MBL ≤1 year: **0.11 mm less** with short implants (p = 0.03)
- MBL >1 year: **0.23 mm less** with short implants (p = 0.005)
- Supports short implants as **primary alternative** to sinus lift in posterior atrophic maxilla
- Sinus lift complication rate up to 38%; short implants largely avoid this risk

## Results Summary

| Outcome | RR / MD | p | Favors |
|---|---|---|---|
| Implant survival | RR 1.02 (1.00–1.05) | 0.09 | Equal |
| MBL ≤1 yr | MD 0.11 mm (0.01–0.20) | 0.03 | Short implants |
| MBL >1 yr | MD 0.23 mm (0.07–0.39) | 0.005 | Short implants |

## Clinical Takeaways
- In atrophic posterior maxilla with sufficient residual bone for ≤6 mm implant: **short implant first**
- Sinus lift reserved for cases requiring longer implants (bone height too low even for 6 mm)
- Less MBL with short implants = better long-term peri-implant bone maintenance
- Reduced surgical complexity, patient morbidity, and treatment cost
