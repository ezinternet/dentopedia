## One-line Summary
In vitro study (160 bovine-rib specimens, 4 residual bone heights × 4 Straumann implant macro-designs) on placement stability in sinus floor elevation: ISQ rises with residual bone height (RBH), and at RBH 6–9 mm the Tapered Effect (TE) design yields the highest ISQ vs SP/BL/BLT (p<0.01), attributed to apical engagement compressing low-density type-IV cancellous bone.

## Summary
In vitro bovine rib (type-IV bone mimic), 160 specimens, 4 RBH × 4 Straumann macro-designs. **ISQ rises with RBH**; at RBH 6–9 mm **Tapered Effect (TE) design yields highest ISQ** vs SP/BL/BLT (p<0.01). Joint effect of RBH + macro-design on placement stability.

## RBH × Macro-design Map

| RBH | Best macro-design (ISQ) |
|---|---|
| 3 mm | All low (limited anchorage) |
| 6 mm | **TE > SP, BL, BLT** (p<0.01) |
| 9 mm | **TE > SP, BL, BLT** (p<0.01) |
| 15 mm | All high (no design dominance reported) |

## Mechanism
- Tapered design: **apical engagement compresses cancellous bone** → higher peri-apical density → higher ISQ in low-density type-IV bone
- Parallel-walled (SP, BL) and BLT show less mechanical compression in atrophic sites

## Clinical Relevance
- For sinus lift cases with RBH 6–9 mm, **tapered/apically-condensing macro-design** preferred to maximize placement ISQ
- Below 4 mm RBH (LSA range): macro-design effect smaller; bone graft consolidation determines later ISQ
- Combine with [[sinus-lift/transcrestal/sirinirund-2025-minimally-invasive-sinus-augmentation-review]]: RBH ≥5 mm → VSA; <5 mm → LSA
