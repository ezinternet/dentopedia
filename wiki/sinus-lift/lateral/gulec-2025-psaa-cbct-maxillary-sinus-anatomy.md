## One-line Summary

Retrospective CBCT study (n=255 patients, 510 sinuses, Turkey 2025): posterior superior alveolar artery (PSAA) is bilateral in 70.2%, predominantly intraosseous (~63%), diameter <1 mm in ~55%, mean distance to alveolar crest ~16 mm — key preoperative landmarks that guide lateral window incision placement to minimize intraoperative hemorrhage.

## 한줄요약

후향 CBCT 연구 (255명, 510개 상악동): 후상치조동맥 (Posterior Superior Alveolar Artery, PSAA) 양측성 70.2%, 골내형 (intraosseous) 63%, 직경 <1 mm 약 55%, 치조정까지 평균 거리 ~16 mm — 측방 상악동 거상술 (Maxillary Sinus Floor Augmentation, MSFA) 혈관 합병증 예방을 위한 수술 전 콘빔 전산화 단층촬영 (Cone Beam Computed Tomography, CBCT) 계측 기준값.

## Summary

This retrospective CBCT study from Karaman, Turkey characterizes the anatomy of the posterior superior alveolar artery (PSAA, 후상치조동맥) in 255 patients (510 sinuses) by measuring its prevalence, position type, diameter, and distances to key surgical landmarks. The PSAA is the primary source of intraoperative hemorrhage in lateral-window sinus floor augmentation, and its CBCT visibility guides safe window placement. Key findings: PSAA is bilateral in 70.2%, unilateral in 15.3%, absent in 14.5%; intraosseous type predominates (~63%); diameter <1 mm in ~55%, with larger-diameter arteries showing significantly reduced clearance from both the sinus floor and alveolar crest. No significant relationship was found between PSAA presence and sinus septa or sinus pathologies. Sinus pathologies were more prevalent in females (p=0.002). Males showed greater PSAA-to-sinus-floor distance (p=0.049).

## Key Contributions

### 1. PSAA Prevalence and Bilaterality

- Bilateral: **70.2%** of patients
- Right only: 8.6%; Left only: 6.7%; Absent: **14.5%**
- Most common tooth level: **first molar** (right 28.6%, left 25.5%)
- Absence on CBCT does NOT exclude hemorrhage risk — superficially coursing PSAA may be radiologically invisible

### 2. PSAA Type Distribution

| Type | Right | Left |
|---|---|---|
| Intraosseous (골내형) | 62.7% | 63.9% |
| Intrasinus (동내형) | 13.3% | 9.8% |
| Superficial (표면형) | 3.1% | 3.1% |

Intraosseous type (PSAA fully enclosed by cortical bone within lateral antral wall) is highest surgical concern for inadvertent incision.

### 3. PSAA Diameter

| Diameter group | Right | Left |
|---|---|---|
| <1 mm | 54.9% | 56.1% |
| 1–2 mm | 22.4% | 20.0% |
| >2 mm | 0.8% | 1.2% |

Larger PSAA diameter (>1 mm) correlates with significantly *smaller* anatomical clearance distances — i.e., the artery sits closer to the sinus floor and alveolar crest, raising hemorrhage risk during osteotomy.

### 4. Key Anatomical Distances (mm, mean ± SD)

| Measurement | Right | Left |
|---|---|---|
| PSAA → sinus floor | 7.08 ± 2.89 | 8.53 ± 3.41 |
| PSAA → alveolar crest | 15.93 ± 3.45 | 17.24 ± 3.71 |
| PSAA → medial wall | 13.11 ± 3.45 | 13.31 ± 3.32 |

Clinical implication: vertical incisions for lateral window sinus lift should be planned with awareness that the PSAA is positioned ~16 mm above the alveolar crest on average; osteotomy height should stay safely below this.

### 5. Gender Effects

- Females: significantly higher sinus pathology prevalence (χ²=14.33; p=0.002; Cramér's V=0.237) — attributed to hormonal factors
- Males: greater PSAA-to-sinus-floor distance (p=0.049) — attributed to larger average sinus volume
- No gender effect on PSAA presence/absence

### 6. PSAA–Pathology Relationship

No statistically significant association between:
- PSAA presence vs. sinus septa presence
- PSAA presence vs. sinus pathology

This finding diverges from prior hypotheses suggesting the PSAA might mechanically contribute to mucosal pathology via blood supply effects.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Retrospective CBCT cross-sectional; n=255 patients aged 18–65; 510 sinuses
- **Device**: Kavo OP 3D Pro; 90 kVp, 8 mA, 4.5 s; 8×15 cm FOV; 0.35 mm voxel
- **Classification**: Danesh-Sani PSAA type system (superficial/intraosseous/intrasinus)
- **Assessment level**: Coronal CBCT sections at first molar
- **Pathology criterion**: Mucosal thickening >2 mm = pathology (non-specific)
- **Statistics**: t-test, ANOVA, Tukey, Chi-square, Cramér's V; α=0.05
