## One-line Summary
Prospective clinical pilot CBCT study (n=10, 4–7 mm residual bone) of graftless lateral sinus floor elevation (LSFE) with immediate implant placement as a space maintainer confirmed reliable new bone formation around all implants using only the blood clot, while quantifying ~35–43% bone height contraction and a mean blood-clot volume contraction of 16.52% ± 8.60% over 6 months.

## 한줄요약
인간 임상 파일럿 (n=10): 측방창 상악동거상술 (Lateral Sinus Floor Elevation, LSFE) + 즉시 임플란트 식립으로 무이식재 (graftless) 신생골 형성 확인. 6개월간 혈병 (Blood Clot) 부피 평균 16.52% ± 8.60% 수축, 골 높이 35–43% 수축.

## Summary
A controlled clinical pilot study in 10 patients tested whether the "tent-pole" effect of an immediately placed implant could maintain enough subantral space — using only the patient's blood clot, no graft material — to allow predictable new bone formation under an elevated Schneiderian membrane. Residual bone heights were 4–7 mm in posterior maxillary sites. CBCT scans at 15 days (T1) and 180 days (T2) tracked the contraction of the clot and the height of newly formed bone in three regions around each implant. Bone formed reliably around all implants, but clinicians should expect 35–43% height contraction and a mean blood-clot volume contraction of 16.5%.

## Key Contributions
- Human in-vivo CBCT confirmation that a graftless lateral sinus lift with immediate implant placement yields measurable bone neoformation.
- Quantitative contraction figures that allow surgeons to plan for clot shrinkage when no graft is used.
- A pragmatic protocol — lateral window + immediate implant as space maintainer + resorbable collagen membrane (Bio-Gide) to close the window.

## Methodology
- Ten patients with 4–7 mm residual crestal bone height in premolar/molar sites.
- Lateral-window LSFE; sinus membrane elevated; implant placed in same session.
- No bone substitute; window closed with resorbable bovine collagen membrane.
- CBCT performed at 15 days (T1) and 180 days (T2); measurements with OsirixMD in mesial, apical, and distal reference regions; paired Student's t-test on normally distributed data.

## Results
- Mesial: clot 4.77 mm → new bone 2.95 mm (height contraction 38%).
- Apical: clot 0.77 mm → new bone 0.44 mm (height contraction 43%).
- Distal: clot 5.30 mm → new bone 3.45 mm (height contraction 35%).
- All height changes T1→T2 statistically significant (p < 0.05).
- Volume T1: 0.90 ± 0.60 cm³; T2: 0.75 ± 0.62 cm³ (p < 0.005).
- Mean clot volume contraction: 16.52% ± 8.60%.
- All implants showed osseointegration with consistent bone formation.

## Clinical Implications
- Graftless lateral sinus lift with an immediate implant is feasible when residual bone height is sufficient for primary stability (4–7 mm in this cohort).
- Plan for ~35–43% vertical contraction over 6 months — overestimate the desired final bone envelope when selecting implant length and elevating membrane height.
- Sample is small; long-term prosthetic data not provided.
