## 한줄요약
체계적 문헌고찰·메타분석 (Systematic Review + Meta-Analysis, SR+MA, 130개 연구 / 1,972 부비동): 슈나이더 막 천공 (Sinus Membrane Perforation, SMP) 적절 수복 시 임플란트 소실률 4% (95% 신뢰구간 2–8%) — 무손상 대비 차이 없음; 천공 크기와 임플란트 소실 간 관련성 없음 (P=0.86).

## Summary
Largest SR+MA on SMP repair outcomes. Pooled implant loss in repaired perforation sites is 4%, statistically indistinguishable from non-perforated controls. Perforation size does not predict implant loss when proper repair is performed. Resorbable collagen membrane is the dominant repair material; sinusitis is the leading reported complication.

## Key Contributions
- 1,972 repaired sinuses synthesized — largest dataset on SMP outcomes.
- Quantifies pooled implant loss (4%) and confirms parity with non-perforated baseline.
- Meta-regression: perforation size NOT a predictor of implant loss.
- Documents real-world repair-material distribution and complication profile.

## Methodology
- PROSPERO-registered SR+MA, PRISMA-guided.
- Five databases searched through Feb 2021.
- Two reviewers, random-effects meta-analysis.
- Meta-regression on perforation size.

## Results
- 130 studies in qualitative synthesis; 20 in MA.
- **Pooled implant loss (repaired SMP)**: 4% (95% CI 2–8%).
- **No significant difference** vs non-perforated sinuses.
- **Perforation size vs implant loss**: P=0.86 (no association).
- **Most common repair material**: resorbable collagen membrane.
- **Most common complication**: sinusitis.
- Bone formation parameters: no difference between repaired and intact sinuses in majority.

## Clinical Relevance
- Encountering an SMP intraoperatively is not a reason to abandon the case if proper repair is feasible.
- Collagen membrane is the default repair material with strongest evidence base.
- Monitor for postoperative sinusitis — the leading complication; consider antibiotic coverage.
- Document perforation size for informed consent but reassure patient that survival is preserved with repair.
