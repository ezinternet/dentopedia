## 한줄요약
한국인 20대 60명 (남30/여30) 원뿔형 빔 컴퓨터 단층촬영 (Cone-Beam Computed Tomography, CBCT) 3차원 분석: 비강저 (Nasal Cavity Floor, NCF) 기준 상악동 하부 부피·높이·폭 모두 남성이 유의하게 크며, 협골 폭 (Bizygomatic Breadth)과 상관 — 임플란트 수술 전 해부학적 평가 시 성차 고려 필요.

## Summary
Korean retrospective 3-D CBCT study (n=60, ages 20s, healthy non-missing dentition). Used Mimics 22.0 to segment the inferior portion of the maxillary sinus defined relative to the NCF reference plane. All inferior sinus dimensions (height NCF-F, width CW/SW, volume) significantly larger in males than females; bizygomatic breadth correlates with inferior sinus size.

## Key Contributions
- Full 3-D volumetric measurements rather than 2-D slice approximations.
- Sex-based reference values for inferior sinus anatomy in young Korean adults.
- Bizygomatic breadth as a facial-width covariate predicting sinus dimensions.

## Methodology
- 60 subjects, 30 M / 30 F, 20s, malocclusion patients with no missing teeth, asymmetry, or systemic disease.
- Alphard 3030 CBCT (0.39 mm slice); Mimics 22.0 for 3-D reconstruction.
- NCF defined per Kawakami protocol; NCF-F, CW, SW, V, PNR angle measured.
- Stratification by bizygomatic breadth (≥127 vs <127 mm).
- Mann-Whitney U, linear regression (SPSS 23.0), α=0.05.

## Results
- **NCF-F height (male)**: 15.06 / 13.30 mm (L/R) vs **(female)**: 10.59 / 11.19 mm — significant.
- **CW**: ~24.5 mm (M) vs ~20.5 mm (F) — significant.
- **SW**: ~32.5 mm (M) vs ~26.1 mm (F) — significant.
- **Volume (V)**: significantly larger in males bilaterally.
- Bizygomatic breadth ≥127 mm → larger inferior sinus dimensions across both sexes.

## Clinical Relevance
- Pre-implant CBCT in posterior maxilla: expect larger sinus pneumatization in males and wider faces.
- 2-D CBCT slices underestimate sinus volume — use 3-D segmentation for sinus lift planning when feasible.
- PNR angle measurement helps anticipate medial wall geometry during lateral window approach.
