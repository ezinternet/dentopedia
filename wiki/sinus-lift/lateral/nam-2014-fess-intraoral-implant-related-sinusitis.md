## One-line Summary

Case report (n=1, 39F): implant protruding 5 mm into right maxillary sinus with ostium obstruction after osteotome sinus elevation was resolved by combined FESS + intraoral approach; full CT recovery at 10 months.

## 한줄요약

증례보고 (n=1, 39세 여): 골돌출형 임플란트(5 mm)로 인한 상악동 자연공 폐쇄성 상악동염에서 기능적 내시경 부비동 수술(FESS) + 구내접근법 병용으로 10개월 추적 CT 완전 회복.

## Summary

A 39-year-old woman presented 3 days after osteotome-technique implant placement at #16 with right zygomatic pain and temporal headache. Panoramic and CT imaging confirmed the implant protruded 5 mm into the right maxillary sinus, with a displaced sinus floor bone fragment, opacification of both the maxillary and ethmoidal sinuses, and obstruction of the natural ostium. Four weeks of medical therapy (antibiotics, NSAIDs, expectorants, antihistamines) produced no improvement.

Cooperative surgery under general anesthesia was performed by an oral-maxillofacial surgeon and an otolaryngologist. The FESS component included: middle turbinate excision, uncinate process removal, surgical enlargement of the natural ostium, evacuation of pus and infected graft particles, and saline irrigation — with deliberate preservation of sinus mucosa. The intraoral component included: loose implant removal and primary closure of the oro-antral fistula. Nasal packing was maintained for 1 week.

At 1-week follow-up, radiopacity decreased markedly though mucosal thickening persisted. By 10-month follow-up, transverse dental CT confirmed full sinus recovery with no complications.

## Key Contributions

- Establishes that FESS alone is insufficient when an intraoral approach is simultaneously needed (oro-antral fistula closure, removal of a loose sinus-intruding implant)
- Confirms the literature threshold: implant protrusion >4 mm from the sinus floor is associated with sinusitis/rhinosinusitis
- Documents ethmoidal sinus co-involvement when ostium obstruction occurs — highlighting the importance of CT-guided treatment planning beyond the maxillary sinus alone
- Supports cooperative ENT–OMFS team surgery under general anesthesia for complex implant-related sinusitis

## Methodology

- **Study design**: Single case report
- **Imaging workup**: Panoramic radiograph → coronal CT → Water's view → transverse dental CT (follow-up)
- **Medical management**: 4-week empirical course (penicillin, clindamycin, NSAIDs, expectorants, antihistamines) — failed
- **Surgical steps**:
  - FESS: epinephrine-soaked cotton to middle meatus → middle turbinate excision → uncinate process removal → natural ostium enlargement → mucosal enucleation → saline irrigation (mucosa preserved)
  - Intraoral: implant removal (loose, 5 mm exposure) → primary oro-antral fistula closure
- **Postoperative care**: Polyvinyl acetal nasal packing ×1 week
- **Follow-up intervals**: 1 week, 10 months

## Results

| Timepoint | Findings |
|---|---|
| Preoperative | 5 mm implant protrusion; displaced bone fragment; maxillary + ethmoidal opacification; ostium obstruction |
| 1 week post-op | Radiopacity markedly reduced; mucosal thickening still present |
| 10 months post-op | Full sinus recovery on CT; no complications |

- Protrusion threshold cited from literature: >4 mm → sinusitis/rhinosinusitis risk
- FESS preferred over Caldwell-Luc for: shorter hospital stay, lower morbidity, OMU preservation
- OMU alteration cannot be repaired by intraoral approaches alone; FESS is required for functional restoration
