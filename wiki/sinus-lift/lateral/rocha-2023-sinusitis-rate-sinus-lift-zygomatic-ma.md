## Summary

This PROSPERO-registered meta-analysis (Rocha 2023) is the first to directly compare postoperative sinusitis prevalence between sinus floor elevation (SFE) and zygomatic implant (ZI) placement across 27 studies. The central finding: zygomatic implants carry ~3.4× higher overall sinusitis prevalence than sinus lift procedures (3.76% vs 1.11%), but this aggregate hides dramatic technique-level variation — the sinus slot ZI technique produces 21.62% sinusitis, while the extrasinus ZI approach produces 0.00%, mirroring the 0.00% seen with transcrestal SFE.

## Key Contributions

1. **First direct MA comparison** of sinusitis rates between SFE (all techniques) and ZI (all techniques).
2. **Technique-level sinusitis rates** quantify that risk is not inherent to the procedure but to the path taken through (or around) the sinus.
3. The **extrasinus ZI technique** is validated as a low-sinusitis option comparable to transcrestal SFE — critical for severe maxillary atrophy case planning.
4. The **sinus slot technique** (original Stella-Warner ZI approach) carries unacceptably high sinusitis risk (21.62%) compared to modern alternatives.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review + meta-analysis; PROSPERO-registered
- **Databases**: Six (includes PubMed, Embase, Cochrane, Scopus)
- **Initial references**: 2,419; final inclusion: 27 studies (18 SFE, 9 ZI)
- **Risk of bias**: RoB 2.0 (RCTs) + ROBINS-I (non-randomized studies)
- **Statistical model**: Random-effects (DerSimonian-Laird); prevalence based on total patients
- **Sinusitis definition**: EPOS 2012 criteria
- **Subgroup analysis**: By technique within each procedure category

## Results

### Sinus Lift — Sinusitis Prevalence by Technique

| Technique | Prevalence | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|
| **Overall SFE** | **1.11%** | 0.30–2.28% |
| Lateral window | 1.35% | 0.34–2.80% |
| Transcrestal | 0.00% | 0.00–3.18% |
| SALSA | 1.20% | 0.00–5.10% |

### Zygomatic Implants — Sinusitis Prevalence by Technique

| Technique | Prevalence | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|
| **Overall ZI** | **3.76%** | 0.12–10.29% |
| Sinus slot | 21.62% | 9.62–36.52% |
| Intrasinus | 4.36% | 0.33–11.08% |
| Extrasinus | 0.00% | 0.00–1.22% |

### Clinical Interpretation

- **SFE is the lower-sinusitis procedure overall** — 1.11% vs 3.76% for ZI.
- **Extrasinus ZI = zero sinusitis** in pooled data; is now the preferred ZI route in contemporary practice.
- **Sinus slot ZI must be avoided** where extrasinus or intrasinus approaches are feasible given the 21.62% sinusitis prevalence.
- **Transcrestal SFE = zero sinusitis** in pooled data; its lower sinus exposure likely explains this.
- Wide CIs for zero-prevalence subgroups reflect small study numbers; absence of events ≠ proven safety but is directionally reassuring.
