## One-line Summary

Case report (n=2) showing that intentional intraoral surgical drainage (large lateral window + deliberate Schneiderian membrane incision) performed simultaneously with MSFA and implant placement resolved chronic maxillary sinusitis — including ostium blockage — without FESS, with 100% implant survival and sinus clearance at 1 year.

## 한줄요약

FESS를 거부한 만성 상악동염 환자 2명에서 측방창 MSFA 도중 의도적 슈나이더막 절개를 통한 구강내 배액을 동시 시행하여 1년 추적에서 임플란트 100% 생존율과 상악동 병변 소실을 보고한 증례 보고.

## Summary

Two male smokers (ages 55 and 59) with complete unilateral maxillary sinus opacification and confirmed 만성 상악동염 (Chronic Maxillary Sinusitis, CMS) underwent 2-week antibiotic therapy (amoxicillin/clavulanate) without resolution. 내시경 부비동 수술 (Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery, FESS) was recommended but refused. Both patients underwent 상악동거상술 (Maxillary Sinus Floor Augmentation, MSFA) via lateral window approach with simultaneous **intentional** 슈나이더막 (Schneiderian membrane) perforation using a #15 blade in the upper third of the exposed membrane to enable thorough sinus drainage and saline irrigation, followed by simultaneous xenograft placement (OsteonXeno) and SLA implant placement (Implantium).

At 1-year follow-up: complete resolution of sinus opacification, 100% implant survival, no peri-implant crestal bone loss, and maintained augmented sinus floor in both cases. No postoperative sinusitis. Case 1 also showed indirect reduction of the nasal polyp obstructing the 자연공 (natural ostium), restoring 점막섬모수송 (Mucociliary Clearance, MCC).

## Key Contributions

1. **Intentional long incision** (vs. prior small needle puncture technique) allows suction tip insertion in all directions — critical for complete drainage of CMS with full sinus opacification
2. **Perforation self-reduces** after drainage: tension release allows membrane folding and buccal relocation of the perforation, eliminating need for collagen barrier repair at perforation site
3. **Prichard elevator** used to block remaining perforation during graft packing — prevents graft particle displacement through the perforated site without membrane repair
4. **Simultaneous implant placement** is feasible even with intentional perforation when drainage is thorough and CMS symptoms are mild
5. Demonstrates ostium patency restoration (Case 1) as an indirect benefit of intraoral drainage reducing membrane thickness

## Methodology

**Patient criteria:**
- Complete unilateral sinus opacification on wide-range CBCT
- CMS diagnosis confirmed by otolaryngologist
- 2-week amoxicillin/clavulanate — minimal radiographic change
- FESS recommended but refused; mild CMS symptoms

**Surgical sequence:**
1. Full-thickness mucoperiosteal flap with vertical releasing incisions
2. Lateral bony window ~1.5 × 2.0 cm (bur, no Schneiderian damage)
3. Horizontal incision in upper third of exposed membrane (#15 Bard–Parker blade)
4. Suction drainage of purulent exudate (all directions, sufficient depth)
5. Thorough saline irrigation until clear
6. Membrane detachment + elevation at sinus floor (distal to perforation)
7. Xenograft (OsteonXeno, Genoss) + Prichard elevator covering perforation
8. SLA implant placement; primary closure; no lateral window membrane
9. Ciprofloxacin 500 mg + naproxen 275 mg × 2 weeks postop; no nose-blowing

**Case 1**: rhinogenous CMS (59M, smoker, edentulous maxilla); 3 implants (Ø3.8 × 10 mm); nasal polyp at middle meatus blocking ostium
**Case 2**: odontogenic CMS (55M, smoker, hypertension); 4-tooth extraction simultaneous; 3 implants + 1 additional at uncovering; peri-implant defects covered with resorbable collagen membrane

## Results

| Outcome | Case 1 | Case 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Sinus opacification | Resolved (mild residual thickening on graft floor) | Fully resolved; nasal polyps also disappeared |
| Implant survival (1 year) | 100% (3/3) | 100% (4/4) |
| Peri-implant bone loss | None | None |
| Augmented bone | Maintained | Maintained |
| Postoperative sinusitis | None | None |
| Graft displacement | None | None |
| Notable finding | Nasal polyp reduced; ostium patency restored | Odontogenic source eliminated; sinus fully cleared |

Both patients: uneventful healing by 6 months; prostheses delivered 2 months after uncovering.
