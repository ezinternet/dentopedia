## One-line Summary
Prospective (n=42 ITI/SLA implants, 20-week RFA): graftless OSFE achieves 95.2% osseointegration; ISQ dips weeks 2–6, recovers by week 16; RBH 4–8 mm and bone type do not predict stability.

## Summary

Lai et al. (Clin Oral Implants Res 2008) prospectively tracked 42 ITI implants (SLA surface) in 32 patients who underwent OSFE without bone grafting. RBH ranged 4–8 mm (mean 6.36 mm). RFA (Osstell ISQ) was measured at 8 timepoints over 20 weeks. Osseointegration was achieved in 40/42 implants (95.2%). ISQ followed a characteristic pattern: good primary stability at placement, a dip between weeks 2–6 during the secondary stability transition, recovery by week 16, and a stable plateau thereafter. Neither RBH (4–8 mm), implant length, nor bone type (Lekholm & Zarb type 3 vs 4) significantly affected the ISQ trajectory — a finding with practical implications for case selection in posterior maxillary OSFE.

This 2008 study established the ISQ dip template for graftless OSFE that later protocol papers (Zhao 2018) incorporated into their RFA measurement schedules.

## Key Contributions

The predictability of the ISQ dip pattern (weeks 2–6) and recovery (week 16) enables clinicians to interpret stability readings in context — a temporary drop does not indicate failure. The finding that bone type 3 vs 4 does not predict ISQ differences challenges the assumption that very soft bone (type 4) is a contraindication for graftless OSFE.

## Methodology

- n=42 implants, 32 patients; posterior maxilla; Shanghai Ninth People's Hospital
- OSFE without graft; RBH 4–8 mm (mean 6.36 mm)
- ITI implants (SLA surface); RFA at day 0, weeks 2, 4, 6, 8, 12, 16, 20
- Bone type classified by Lekholm & Zarb index

## Results

- **Osseointegration**: 40/42 (95.2%)
- **ISQ pattern**: primary stability → dip (wk 2–6) → recovery (wk 16) → stable
- **Bone type 3 vs 4**: NS difference in ISQ at placement or follow-up
- **RBH (4–8 mm)**: NS effect on ISQ
- **Implant length**: NS effect on ISQ
