## One-line Summary
Retrospective multicenter study (n=430 implants, RBH ≤5 mm, transcrestal sinus floor elevation): early implant failure 2.8%; bucco-palatal sinus width >12 mm (OR 8.5) and membrane perforation (OR 4.21) are the only independent predictors of early failure.

## Summary
This 7-center retrospective study examined risk factors for intraoperative complications and early implant failure in transcrestal sinus floor elevation (TSFE) performed in the challenging scenario of residual bone height (RBH) ≤5 mm. Across 430 single-implant cases, 418 implants (97.2%) were functional at one year of loading, with 12 early failures (2.8%). On multivariate logistic regression, only two variables independently predicted early failure: a wide bucco-palatal sinus cavity (>12 mm at 10 mm height) and intraoperative Schneiderian membrane perforation. Sinus membrane perforation occurred in 7.2% overall but was strongly width-dependent — 1.1% in narrow sinuses versus 16.1% in wide cavities. The study is the first to clinically demonstrate bucco-palatal sinus width as a discrete, CBCT-measurable risk factor for TSFE.

## Key Contributions
- Identifies **bucco-palatal sinus width >12 mm** as an independent CBCT-measurable risk factor for both membrane perforation and early implant failure — a predictor not previously isolated clinically.
- Quantifies the width effect on perforation: narrow (≤12 mm) 1.1% vs wide (>12 mm) 16.1%.
- Confirms membrane perforation independently predicts early failure (OR 4.21) even in the minimally invasive transcrestal approach.
- Provides early-failure benchmark (2.8%) and complication spectrum for TSFE restricted to RBH ≤5 mm.

## Methodology
Retrospective multicenter chart review across 7 Italian centers. Population: 430 patients receiving single-implant TSFE at sites with RBH ≤5 mm. Predictor variables spanned patient level (age, sex, smoking, periodontitis), sinus level (pre-op bone height, bucco-palatal sinus width at 10 mm height, floor inclination, Underwood septa) and surgical level (antrostomy technique [rotary/osteotome/ultrasonic], implant surface roughness, simultaneous vs staged placement, intraoperative membrane perforation). Sinus width measured on CBCT when available, dichotomized narrow ≤12 mm / wide >12 mm. Primary outcome: early implant failure (loss before loading or within first year of function). Univariate and multivariate stepwise logistic regression; χ² for complication associations.

## Results
- Survival: 418/430 functional at 1 yr; early failure 2.8% (12/430).
- Multivariate predictors of early failure — Sinus width wide: OR 8.50 (95% CI 1.02–70.42, p=.047); Membrane perforation: OR 4.21 (95% CI 1.10–16.05, p=.035). RBH, technique, septa, smoking, timing all non-significant.
- Membrane perforation 7.2% overall; sole univariate predictor = bucco-palatal width (OR 15.5, 95% CI 3.59–56.59, p=.000); narrow 1.1% vs wide 16.1%.
- Other adverse events: acute sinusitis 0.9%, implant displacement into sinus 0.7%, oro-antral fistula 0.2%, BPPV 0.5%.
