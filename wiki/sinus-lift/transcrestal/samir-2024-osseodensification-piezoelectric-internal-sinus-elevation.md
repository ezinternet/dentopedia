## One-line Summary
Randomized controlled trial (RCT, NCT06055127): for transcrestal sinus floor elevation with delayed implant placement, osseodensification (OD) outperformed piezoelectric internal sinus elevation (PISE) on bone gain, bone density, surgery time, and patient satisfaction, while PISE gave superior day-of-surgery primary stability — both effective, differing in strengths.

## Summary
Randomized controlled clinical trial comparing osseodensification vs piezoelectric internal sinus elevation (PISE) for transcrestal sinus floor elevation with delayed implant placement in the posterior maxilla.

## Key Contributions
Adds the OD-vs-piezoelectric axis to transcrestal technique selection: the two minimally invasive approaches trade off differently — OD for bone gain/density/morbidity, PISE for immediate primary stability.

## Methodology
RCT, posterior maxilla, transalveolar sinus elevation prior to delayed implant placement. Adjuncts: sticky bone, i-PRF. (Specific n / follow-up to confirm in full text.)

## Results
- OD: greater bone gain, improved bone density, shorter surgery, faster healing, higher patient satisfaction.
- PISE: superior implant primary stability on day of surgery.
- Both effective; choice depends on whether bone gain/density or immediate primary stability is prioritized.
