## One-line Summary
Prospective 3-arm study (n=126 implants) in RBH ≤5 mm: Bio-Oss Collagen + CGF achieves ISR 96%, superior bone gain and lower pain versus no-graft or Bio-Oss alone.

## Summary

Shahood et al. (BMC Oral Health 2024) conducted a prospective 3-arm study in 123 patients (126 implants) with RBH ≤5 mm undergoing OSFE with simultaneous placement. Group A received no graft, Group B Bio-Oss alone, and Group C Bio-Oss Collagen + CGF (concentrated growth factor). Overall ISR was 96% at 1-year functional loading, with a significant positive correlation between RBH and ISR (r=0.359, p<0.001). Both grafted groups showed significantly greater endo-sinus bone gain and lower total bone resorption (TBR) than the no-graft group. Graft volume (D) was an independent predictor of ISR at multiple timepoints. Notably, VAS pain scores were significantly lower in Group C than Group B (p<0.001), suggesting that CGF incorporation reduces postoperative morbidity while maintaining superior bone gain over Bio-Oss alone.

## Key Contributions

Graft size is an independent predictor of survival (OR 8.06 at D0), and CGF appears to confer both biological (bone gain, MBL) and patient-experience (lower pain) advantages over conventional Bio-Oss alone. This is the first 3-group comparison including CGF+Bio-Oss Collagen for very low RBH (≤5 mm) OSFE.

## Methodology

- 126 implants / 123 patients; single center
- Groups: A=no graft, B=Bio-Oss, C=Bio-Oss Collagen+CGF
- Radiographic follow-up at 4 timepoints; ISQ and bone density measured
- Endpoints: ISR (primary), ESBG, TBR1/TBR2, ISQ, VAS pain

## Results

| Outcome | Group A | Group B | Group C |
|---|---|---|---|
| ISR | included in 96% overall | — | — |
| ESBG | lowest | moderate | highest |
| TBR1/TBR2 | highest | lower (p=0.004/p<0.001) | lower |
| VAS pain | low | highest | low |

ISQ and bone density: no significant between-group differences.
