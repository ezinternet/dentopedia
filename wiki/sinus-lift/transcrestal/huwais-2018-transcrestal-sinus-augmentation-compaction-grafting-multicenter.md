## Summary
The Huwais 2018 multicentre retrospective study is the largest single clinical dataset on osseodensification (OD) transcrestal sinus augmentation and the most frequently cited clinical anchor for the technique. Across three centres and a five-year time window (May 2012 – September 2017), 222 patients received 261 implants placed with simultaneous OD transcrestal sinus floor elevation and compaction grafting. The baseline subsinus residual bone height (RBH) of 5.4 ± 1.9 mm placed many of these cases in the borderline indication zone for a lateral window in traditional protocols.

Two clinical findings dominate the paper. First, the mean vertical bone gain of 7.0 ± 2.49 mm is comparable to what is reported for lateral-window grafting, achieved here via a single crestal access — a significant morbidity reduction. Second, and more striking, the authors report **zero sinus membrane perforations across all 261 cases**, with **no late implant failures** between 6 and 64 months follow-up and a **97% cumulative implant survival rate**. These numbers underpin the safety case for OD in sinus surgery and reappear consistently in subsequent SR+MAs ([[sinus-lift/transcrestal/cobo-vazquez-2025-crestal-sinus-lift-osteotome-vs-osseodensification]], [[sinus-lift/transcrestal/starch-jensen-2025-transcrestal-sinus-osseodensification-meta-analysis]]).

The retrospective single-arm design and Huwais's role as inventor/patent holder warrant interpretive caution. The "zero perforations" figure is remarkable and should be read alongside the broader literature (osteotome perforation rates often 3–20% — see [[sinus-lift/lateral/diaz-olivares-2021-schneiderian-membrane-perforation-sinus-lift]]). Even allowing for some under-detection of subclinical perforations, the safety signal is substantial and has held up in subsequent prospective and meta-analytic work.

## Key Contributions
- Largest single OD transcrestal sinus dataset to 2018: 261 implants, mean 35 months follow-up.
- Demonstrates that transcrestal access can achieve ~7 mm vertical gain — territory historically held by lateral window techniques.
- Reports **zero membrane perforations** across 261 cases — the foundational safety claim for OD sinus lift.
- 97% cumulative implant survival, no late failures.
- Establishes the OD compaction-grafting technique as a credible alternative to both osteotome and lateral window approaches.

## Methodology
Retrospective chart review of consecutive patients treated at three centres between May 2012 and September 2017 with OD transcrestal sinus augmentation and simultaneous implant placement. Summary statistics: means for continuous variables, percentages for categorical. The OD protocol uses tapered multi-fluted Densah burs run counterclockwise to compact bone and hydraulically advance the autografted bone column through the sinus floor, gently elevating the Schneiderian membrane. Implants placed in the same surgical visit. Detailed inclusion/exclusion criteria, graft material breakdown, and implant brand/macrogeometry are paywalled and not extractable from the available abstract excerpt.

## Results
- **n** = 261 implants in 222 patients (3 centres)
- **Follow-up**: 6–64 months; mean 35 months
- **Baseline RBH**: 5.4 ± 1.9 mm
- **Vertical bone gain**: 7.0 ± 2.49 mm
- **Sinus membrane perforations**: 0 / 261
- **Late implant failures (6–64 months)**: 0
- **Cumulative implant survival**: 97%
