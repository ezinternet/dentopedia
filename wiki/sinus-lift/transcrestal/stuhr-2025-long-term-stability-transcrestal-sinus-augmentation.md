## Summary
Narrative review (Periodontology 2000) on TSFE long-term stability. Synthesizes 4+ decades of technique evolution from osteotome-mediated condensation onward. Examines treatment planning, surgical execution, postoperative care, and emerging technologies. **Proposes standardized clinical & outcome-reporting checklist** for TSFE.

## TSFE Indication Threshold
| Parameter | Value | Note |
|---|---|---|
| RBH (minimum) | **≥4 mm** | Subject to revision with osseodensification, endoscope |
| Bone width | **≥5 mm** | Implant accommodation |

## Primary Stability Mechanism (Osteotome-mediated)
1. Osteotomes of increasing diameter prepare implant bed
2. **In-fracture** of sinus floor (controlled green-stick)
3. **Trabecular bone condensation** at implant bed walls → density ↑
4. → Enhanced primary stability (higher placement ISQ)

## Long-Term Stability Drivers
- Patient selection (RBH, anatomy, systemic health, smoking)
- Surgical execution (membrane integrity, depth control)
- Postoperative management (sinus precautions, infection control)
- Graft material choice — or graft-less (cf. [[sinus-lift/transcrestal/guo-2020-non-grafted-platelet-concentrations-grafted-transcrestal-sinus]])

## Clinical Relevance
- Reference framework for **TSFE outcome reporting** in research/audit
- Standardized checklist promotes reproducibility across centers
- Bridges short-term ISQ data and long-term radiographic/survival outcomes

## Caveats
- Narrative review — no statistical synthesis
- Subjective curation of evidence
- Checklist proposal needs validation cohort
