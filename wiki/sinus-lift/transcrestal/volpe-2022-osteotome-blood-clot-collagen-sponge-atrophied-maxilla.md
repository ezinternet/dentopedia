## One-line Summary
Retrospective (n=36 Neoss implants, mean 8.5 yr): graftless OSFE with collagen sponge alone yields 97.2% survival, ISQ 73→76, VBH gain 3.8 mm retained at 2.4 mm net long-term.

## Summary

Volpe et al. (Int J Dentistry 2022) report a retrospective series of 36 single-tooth Neoss implants placed in 36 patients (2006–2014) using transcrestal osteotome SFE without any bone graft. The only adjunct material was a collagen sponge placed sub-membrane to support blood clot organization. RBH eligibility was ≥4 mm. At mean follow-up of 8.5±2.8 years (range 5–13 years), survival was 97.2% (35/36; one failure at year 4). ISQ improved from 73.2±6.2 at placement to 75.8±3.9 at abutment surgery (4–6 months). VBH progressed from 5.9±1.4 mm pre-op to 9.7±1.1 mm at second-stage, settling at 8.3±1.8 mm at long-term, representing a net retained gain of ~2.4 mm.

The study is notable as one of the longest follow-up datasets for graftless transcrestal SFE, demonstrating that osteotome-induced blood clot alone, stabilized by collagen sponge, can sustain clinically meaningful bone formation over a decade.

## Key Contributions

VBH dynamics show a predictable pattern: peak at 4–6 months (9.7 mm) then modest remodeling to a stable plateau (8.3 mm). Net gain of 2.4 mm over baseline at 8+ years suggests durable bone formation from blood clot without graft. This reinforces graftless protocol viability for RBH ≥4 mm single-tooth cases and provides long-term baseline for comparison with newer graft-augmented protocols.

## Methodology

- n=36 single-implant cases; 2006–2014; single operator (Volpe, Rome)
- Transcrestal osteotome SFE, no graft, collagen sponge sub-membrane
- RBH ≥4 mm; submerged healing 4–6 months; single crown restoration
- ISQ by Osstell at placement and second surgery
- VBH on periapical radiographs at pre-op, 4–6 mo, annual check-up
