## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 17 RCTs (10 meta-analyzed) on transcrestal maxillary sinus elevation showing a pooled 100% (95% CI 99–100%) implant survival independent of follow-up time, tempered by a hand-osteotome–specific risk of sinus-membrane perforation and patient vertigo.

## Summary

This systematic review and meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD42024504513) evaluated implant outcomes after transcrestal (closed) maxillary sinus floor elevation, restricting inclusion to randomised clinical trials. From 1807 records, 17 RCTs were included and 10 entered a random-effects meta-analysis of hand-osteotome lifts. Pooled implant survival was 100% (95% CI 99–100%) for both grafted and non-grafted hand-osteotome transcrestal lifts, and meta-regression found follow-up time did not significantly moderate survival. Subgroup analyses detected no significant difference between bone-level vs tissue-level implants or one-stage vs two-stage placement. The clinically important nuance is a technique-dependent safety signal: only hand-osteotome studies reported subsequent vertigo/dizziness, alongside a higher rate of sinus-lining perforation. The authors conclude closed sinus elevation is a relatively safe, high-survival technique, but hand osteotomes warrant caution.

## Key Contributions

- Provides an RCT-only pooled survival estimate (100%, 95% CI 99–100%) specific to transcrestal sinus elevation, reinforcing the high-survival consensus from OD-focused syntheses.
- Shows survival is robust to follow-up duration (non-significant meta-regression) and to implant type/loading protocol (bone- vs tissue-level, one- vs two-stage).
- Isolates a hand-osteotome–specific morbidity signal (perforation + vertigo/dizziness), giving a technique-selection caveat that survival alone hides.
- Complements perforation-rate (Huang 2026) and technique-comparison (Starch-Jensen 2025) syntheses with a broad survival anchor.

## Methodology

Databases: PubMed, Web of Science, Embase, Scopus to 21 Feb 2024. Inclusion: RCTs using transcrestal sinus lifts. Elevation methods across studies: hand osteotomes, piezoelectric + hand-osteotome combinations, drills, and hydraulic "smart lift" systems. 1807 records → 17 included → 10 meta-analyzed. Random-effects pooling of hand-osteotome transcrestal lift survival (grafted and non-grafted), with meta-regression on follow-up time and subgroup analyses (implant level, staging); publication bias assessed via funnel plots and Egger's regression; α = 0.05. (Abstract-only ingest — forest-plot weights, I² heterogeneity, and per-study data not extracted.)

## Results

- Pooled implant survival: **100% (95% CI 99–100%)**, grafted and non-grafted hand-osteotome transcrestal lifts.
- Meta-regression: follow-up time not a significant moderator of survival.
- Subgroups: bone-level vs tissue-level — no significant difference; one-stage vs two-stage — no significant difference.
- Safety: vertigo/dizziness reported only among hand-osteotome studies; higher sinus-lining perforation rate with hand osteotomes.
