## 한줄요약
RCT (29 tSFE vs 28 lSFE, RBH 3–6mm): 수술 당일 lSFE 통증 낮음; 이후 14일간 tSFE 부종·타박·비출혈 유의하게 적음

## Summary
RCT, 29 tSFE vs 28 lSFE, RBH 3-6 mm, same xenograft + simultaneous implant. **Day 0: lSFE less painful**; **days 1-14: tSFE less swelling, bruising, nasal discharge, less functional limitation**. tSFE = overall more tolerable.

## Morbidity Comparison

| Outcome | tSFE | lSFE | Winner |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pain (VAS, day 0) | Higher | Lower | **lSFE** |
| Pain (VAS, days 1-14) | Similar | Similar | tie |
| Swelling | Less | More | **tSFE** |
| Bruising | Less | More | **tSFE** |
| Nasal discharge/bleeding | Less | More | **tSFE** |
| Swallowing limitation | Less | More | **tSFE** |
| Eating, speaking, mouth opening | Less limited | More limited | **tSFE** |
| Work/school disruption | Less | More | **tSFE** |

## Why the Day-0 Reversal?
tSFE involves osteotome impacts transmitted to the sinus floor → acute mechanical discomfort during the procedure. lSFE uses a window approach with less impact transmission. But the **lateral window means larger tissue trauma**, hence more swelling/bruising in the days that follow.

## Clinical Relevance
- For patient morbidity: **tSFE preferred** when RBH 3-6 mm allows it
- Set patient expectation: tSFE = "sharper but briefer" day-of pain
- lSFE → counsel patients about 1-2 week recovery with visible swelling
- Both techniques achieve adequate vertical augmentation for implant placement (subject of other reviews)

## Caveats
- RBH 3-6 mm range — at the lower edge of tSFE indication
- Same xenograft + simultaneous protocol — does not isolate graft or staging variables
- 14-day morbidity window only
