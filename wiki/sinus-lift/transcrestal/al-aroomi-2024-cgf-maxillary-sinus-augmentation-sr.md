## One-line Summary
Systematic review (13 studies, transcrestal + lateral maxillary sinus elevation): CGF — alone or with graft — appears to enhance new bone formation, reduce postoperative sequelae and support high implant survival, but evidence is heterogeneous and RCT-poor.

## 한줄요약
체계적 문헌고찰(13편, 경치조골+측방창 상악동거상): CGF는 단독·이식재 병용 모두 신생골 형성·술후 후유증·임플란트 생존을 개선하는 경향이나, 근거가 이질적이고 RCT가 부족함.

## Summary
This PRISMA-guided systematic review evaluated concentrated growth factor (CGF), a later-generation autologous platelet concentrate, in maxillary sinus floor elevation across both transcrestal and lateral approaches. From 783 screened records, 13 studies were included. The majority reported that CGF — used alone or combined with grafting material — positively influenced clinical, radiographic and histological outcomes, promoting vascularization and regeneration, improving the quality and quantity of newly formed sinus bone, reducing postoperative sequelae, and achieving high implant survival. However, the authors stress substantial heterogeneity and a shortage of high-quality RCTs, so no quantitative pooling was performed and conclusions remain provisional. The review extends single-study CGF evidence and parallels the broader platelet-concentrate literature where biologic adjuncts show benefit on healing/PROMs but limited hard advantage on survival.

## Key Contributions
- First systematic review dedicated to CGF specifically within maxillary sinus augmentation.
- Aggregates clinical + radiographic + histological signals across transcrestal and lateral techniques.
- Frames CGF's plausible mechanism (vascularization, fibrin scaffold) while flagging the weak evidence base (heterogeneity, few RCTs).

## Methodology
Systematic review (qualitative, no meta-analysis) following PRISMA. Searches across PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, Google Scholar, Cochrane up to March 2024. Inclusion: transcrestal or lateral maxillary sinus floor elevation with CGF alone or combined with grafting materials. 783 records screened, 13 eligible. Outcomes assessed: postoperative complications, new bone formation (radiographic/histologic), implant survival. Designs were mixed (RCT and non-randomized).

## Results
- 13/783 studies included; heterogeneous designs and CGF protocols.
- Majority reported positive CGF effect: enhanced vascularization/regeneration, improved newly formed bone quality and quantity, reduced postoperative sequelae, high implant survival.
- No pooled effect size; data variability precluded definitive quantitative conclusions. Authors call for robust RCTs.
