## Summary
Cobo-Vázquez and colleagues (Complutense University of Madrid) conducted the first PRISMA-guided systematic review and meta-analysis to directly compare the two dominant crestal sinus lift schools: the classic Summers osteotome (OST) and Huwais osseodensification (OD). Across 13 studies and 519 treated sites (10 OST, 3 OD), the headline finding is a striking complication-rate gap — 14.32% with OST versus 2.78% with OD, roughly fivefold — despite clinically similar bone gain (3.37 mm OD vs 3.18 mm OST) and overlapping implant survival ranges (94.1–100%).

The clinical implication is that OD is a safer crestal technique for similar vertical augmentation. The mechanistic interpretation is plausible: OD's hydraulic membrane elevation via the autografted bone column avoids the mallet-driven percussive trauma associated with osteotome work, and the compacted bone wall provides additional buffering. However, the OD evidence base is still thin (only 3 studies pooled here), and OD cohorts started with slightly more native bone (5.94 vs 5.00 mm RBH at baseline) — a confounder that may exaggerate OD's apparent safety advantage.

For clinicians, this paper supports OD as a reasonable first-line crestal sinus lift technique when the indication is appropriate, but it should be read alongside the 5-year Huwais multicentre retrospective ([[sinus-lift/transcrestal/huwais-2018-transcrestal-sinus-augmentation-compaction-grafting-multicenter]]) and the Starch-Jensen 2025 SR+MA ([[sinus-lift/transcrestal/starch-jensen-2025-transcrestal-sinus-osseodensification-meta-analysis]]) before drawing strong conclusions.

## Key Contributions
- First PRISMA SR+MA pooling OST vs OD crestal sinus lift outcomes (13 studies, 519 sites).
- Bone gain equivalent: OD 3.37 mm vs OST 3.18 mm.
- Implant survival 94.1–100% across both techniques — no meaningful between-group difference.
- Complication rate ~5× lower with OD (2.78%) vs OST (14.32%) — the headline finding.
- Documents baseline asymmetry (OD RBH 5.94 mm vs OST 5.00 mm) — must inform interpretation.
- Identifies OD evidence as still sparse (n = 3 studies); larger RCTs needed.

## Methodology
PRISMA 2020 systematic review of MEDLINE/PubMed, SCOPUS, and Cochrane CENTRAL up to early 2025. Eligibility: crestal sinus lift via osteotome (Summers) or osseodensification (Huwais Densah burs) with simultaneous implant placement. Risk of bias via Newcastle–Ottawa (observational) and Cochrane RoB tool (RCTs). Random-effects meta-analysis on three outcomes: implant survival, residual bone height (RBH), and bone gain. Complications and follow-up duration synthesised narratively. Thirteen studies (10 OST, 3 OD) totalling 519 sites included. Modal implant: 4 × 10 mm.

## Results
- **RBH at baseline**: OD 5.94 mm vs OST 5.00 mm
- **Bone gain**: OD 3.37 mm vs OST 3.18 mm
- **Most-used implant size**: 4 mm × 10 mm
- **Implant survival**: 94.1–100% (both groups)
- **Complication rate**: OST 14.32% vs OD 2.78% (≈ 5× lower with OD)
- **Studies / sites**: 13 / 519 (10 OST + 3 OD)
