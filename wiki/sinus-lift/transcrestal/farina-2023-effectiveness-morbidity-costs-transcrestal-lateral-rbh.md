## One-line Summary

Re-analysis of a parallel-arm RCT (ITT 29 tSFE vs 28 lSFE) stratified by residual bone height: both reach median 100% implant-surface bone contact, but at RBH ≥4 mm tSFE wins on chair time, cost, and morbidity, while at RBH <4 mm tSFE causes more early pain and lSFE more bruising and higher cost.

## Summary

This secondary analysis re-examined a parallel-arm RCT comparing transcrestal (tSFE) and lateral (lSFE) sinus floor elevation, stratifying sites by residual bone height (RBH <4 mm vs ≥4 mm) to make technique selection RBH-conditional rather than averaged. Radiographic effectiveness was equivalent: both arms reached a median 100% implant-surface bone contact (totCON%) regardless of RBH, so the decision pivots on morbidity and cost. At RBH ≥4 mm, tSFE was clearly preferable — fewer postoperative signs/symptoms, less chair time, lower cost. At RBH <4 mm the picture is a genuine trade-off: tSFE produced significantly more day-0/1 pain (with the same analgesic dose), while lSFE produced more bruising and cost more. The practical takeaway is to default to tSFE at RBH ≥4 mm, and when choosing tSFE at RBH <4 mm to pre-plan early-phase analgesia.

## Key Contributions

- RBH-stratified comparison turns a global tSFE-vs-lSFE result into a site-conditional decision rule.
- Effectiveness (totCON% 100%) equivalent across techniques and RBH → bone contact does not discriminate; morbidity/cost do.
- At RBH ≥4 mm tSFE dominates on chair time, cost, and morbidity.
- At RBH <4 mm a trade-off emerges (tSFE early pain vs lSFE bruising/cost), with an actionable analgesia-planning recommendation.

## Methodology

Re-analysis of a parallel-arm RCT; ITT 29 tSFE and 28 lSFE patients. Within each RBH stratum (<4 mm, ≥4 mm), arms were compared for chair time, surgery-related cost, morbidity (pain severity, postoperative signs/symptoms, bruising, analgesic dose), and radiographic totCON% (proportion of implant surface in contact with the radiopaque area). (Abstract-only ingest — per-stratum n, effect sizes/CIs, and follow-up not extracted; subgroup comparisons are post hoc.)

## Results

- totCON%: median 100% irrespective of RBH and technique.
- RBH <4 mm: tSFE pain significantly higher at days 0–1 (analgesic dose equal); lSFE significantly more bruising and greater cost.
- RBH ≥4 mm: tSFE significantly fewer postoperative signs/symptoms, less chair time, lower cost.
