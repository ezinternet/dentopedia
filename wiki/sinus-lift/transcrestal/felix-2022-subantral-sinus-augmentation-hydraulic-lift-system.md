## 한줄요약
위축된 우측 상악 구치부 (Atrophic Posterior Maxilla)에서 유압식 거상 시스템 (Hydraulic Lift System)과 인산규소염 퍼티 (Phosphosilicate Putty) 골이식, 동시 임플란트 식립을 보고한 경치조 접근 (Transcrestal Approach) 케이스 리포트.

## Summary
Felix et al. report a single case of a 58-year-old partially edentulous female with limited residual bone height in the right posterior maxilla, treated with **transcrestal (indirect) maxillary sinus floor elevation** using a **hydraulic lift device** that uses controlled saline pressure to atraumatically elevate the Schneiderian membrane. Alloplastic phosphosilicate putty was used as the graft, and an implant was placed simultaneously. The authors position the technique as combining the controlled elevation of the lateral-window approach with the lower morbidity and reduced perforation rate of the crestal approach.

## Key Contributions
- Illustrates a hybrid hydraulic-pressure transcrestal technique with simultaneous implant placement in atrophic posterior maxilla.
- Describes step-by-step protocol from CBCT planning through prosthesis delivery.
- Argues for lower perforation risk vs. classical osteotome (Summers) technique.

## Methodology
- CBCT-based assessment of residual ridge height and sinus floor.
- Crestal osteotomy, careful breach of the sinus floor.
- Saline-pressure (hydraulic) elevation of the Schneiderian membrane through the osteotomy.
- Alloplastic phosphosilicate putty placed into the elevated space.
- Simultaneous implant placement with primary stability achieved.
- Standard healing, then screw-retained prosthesis.

## Results
- No intraoperative perforation reported.
- Implant integrated; prosthesis delivered; uneventful early healing.
- Quantitative bone-gain and ISQ values not provided in the abstract preview.
