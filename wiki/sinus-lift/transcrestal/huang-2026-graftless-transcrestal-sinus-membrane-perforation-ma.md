## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.2340/aos.v85.46021](https://doi.org/10.2340/aos.v85.46021)), this systematic review and meta-analysis (14 studies, 4 RCTs + 10 non-randomised cohorts, 2012–2024; 1370 patients, 2119 crestal sinus-lift sites) is the first to isolate the Schneiderian membrane perforation rate specific to **graftless** transcrestal maxillary sinus floor elevation. The pooled perforation incidence was **1% (95% CI 0–6%)** (raw 95/2119 = 4.48%), early implant failure ≤12 months pooled **1% (95% CI 0–6%)**, and **no** postoperative complications (acute sinusitis, epistaxis) were reported. Subgroup and meta-regression analyses identify surgical technique — not residual bone height (RBH) — as the dominant driver of perforation, with conventional OSFE pooling at 0% but motor-driven osseodensification (OD) at 34%. GRADE certainty was very low (high heterogeneity, imprecision, indirectness).

Clinically: graftless transcrestal sinus elevation is a low-perforation, high-early-survival approach with no reported sinusitis/epistaxis — but the safety margin is technique-dependent. Conventional osteotome (OSFE) and minimally invasive (MISE) techniques are the safest; motor-driven OD carries a markedly higher perforation risk and a steep learning curve, so adhere to manufacturer speed/torque limits and obtain training before use. RBH alone does not predict perforation — assess sinus width on CBCT (wide sinuses ~15× higher perforation per Stacchi) and consider a lateral window when anatomy is unfavourable.

## Key Contributions

- First meta-analysis isolating Schneiderian membrane perforation in **graftless** transcrestal sinus floor elevation (grafted and lateral-window approaches excluded).
- Pooled perforation incidence **1% (95% CI 0–6%)** across 14 studies / 2119 sites.
- Establishes **surgical technique** as the predominant moderator (explains 66.9% of heterogeneity), overshadowing RBH and publication year.
- Documents favourable safety: early implant failure ≤12 mo **1%** and **zero** reported postoperative sinusitis/epistaxis.

## Methodology

- **Design**: PRISMA 2020 systematic review + random-effects meta-analysis (R `meta`, Freeman–Tukey double-arcsine).
- **Search**: PubMed, Embase, Cochrane CENTRAL, Web of Science, Scopus to May 2025; English; reference hand-search.
- **Eligibility**: adults, graftless transcrestal techniques (OSFE, OD-TSFE, MISE); RCTs + prospective/retrospective cohorts; ≥10 graftless sinus-lift sites. Excluded: grafting, lateral window, animal/cadaveric, paediatric/mixed.
- **Outcomes**: primary = Schneiderian membrane perforation; secondary = early implant failure ≤12 mo, postoperative complications (acute sinusitis, epistaxis). Unit = site (bilateral = 2).
- **Analyses**: subgroup (design, technique, follow-up), meta-regression (RBH, technique, year), Egger test, trim-and-fill, leave-one-out sensitivity.
- **Quality**: RoB 2.0 + ROBINS-I (all 14 low risk); GRADE rated **very low** for all outcomes.

## Results

- **Included**: 14 studies (4 RCTs + 10 non-randomised), 2012–2024; 1370 patients, **2119 crestal sinus-lift sites**; OSFE 10/14, mean RBH 2.4–7.4 mm (28.5% ≤5 mm), median follow-up 24 mo.
- **Perforation (primary)**: pooled **1% (95% CI 0–6%)**; raw 95/2119 (4.48%); I²=80.6%; 95% prediction interval 0–6%.
- **Early implant failure ≤12 mo**: pooled **1% (95% CI 0–6%)**, 711 implants, I²=0% (highest individual: Si 7.3%, Alzoubi 10.3%).
- **Postoperative complications**: **none reported** (no acute sinusitis, no epistaxis).
- **By design**: prospective 4% (0–24%), retrospective 1% (0–17%), RCTs 0% (0–1%) — not significant (p=0.592).
- **By technique** (significant, p<0.001): conventional **OSFE 0%** (0–4%), **motor-driven OD 34%** (18–54%), OD-TSFE 7%, MISE 0%.
- **By follow-up**: ≤12 mo 1% vs >12 mo 1% — not significant (p=0.750).
- **Meta-regression**: RBH non-significant (β=−0.0064, p=0.760, R²=0%); technique model optimal (AIC −13.2, R²=66.9%); MISE and OSFE independently protective.
- **Publication bias**: none for perforation/implant failure (Egger p>0.05; trim-and-fill 0 imputed); complication funnel asymmetric (interpret with caution).
- **Certainty**: GRADE **very low** for all outcomes.
