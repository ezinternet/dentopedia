## Summary
Narrative review comprehensively covering osseodensification (OD) technique for crestal maxillary sinus floor elevation (CSFE). Reviews the Huwais-Meyer biomechanical mechanism (compaction vs excavation), available clinical evidence, and comparison with osteotome-mediated CSFE. Concludes that OD provides superior primary stability, reduced membrane perforation risk, and comparable or lower post-operative morbidity vs osteotome technique in RBH 3–8mm cases.

## Key Contributions
- Synthesises clinical evidence for OD in CSFE across multiple study types
- Mechanism explanation: counterclockwise bur rotation → lateral/apical bone compaction → spring-back effect
- OD advantages: higher primary ISQ, bone autografting at osteotomy walls, lower perforation rate
- Suitable RBH range: 3–8mm with simultaneous implant placement
