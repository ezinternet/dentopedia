## One-line Summary
RCT (n=11, 12 implants, RBH 5–8 mm, 6-month): osseodensification (OD, Densah burs) vs osteotome for transcrestal sinus floor elevation — OD yields significantly higher primary stability ISQ (66.17 vs 54.83, p=0.043) and significantly superior secondary stability, with shorter operating time (p=0.002) and equivalent postoperative pain/edema.

## 한줄요약

## Summary
Randomized clinical trial comparing osseodensification using Densah burs versus osteotome technique for transcrestal maxillary sinus floor elevation in cases with RBH 5–8mm. OD technique achieves significantly higher primary and secondary implant stability (ISQ) compared to osteotome, with shorter operating time. No difference in postoperative pain or edema.

## Key Contributions
- Direct RCT comparison: OD vs osteotome TSFE in RBH 5–8mm
- OD primary ISQ significantly higher (66.17 vs 54.83, p=0.043)
- OD secondary stability also significantly superior
- OD is faster technique (p=0.002) with equivalent patient comfort

## Results
| Outcome | OD (Densah) | Osteotome | p-value |
|---------|------------|-----------|---------|
| Primary ISQ | 66.17 ± 9.57 | 54.83 ± 7.19 | 0.043* |
| Secondary stability | Higher | Lower | Significant |
| Operating time | Shorter | Longer | 0.002* |
| Pain/edema | Similar | Similar | NS |
