## Summary
This systematic review and meta-analysis pooled 22 human clinical studies (864 implants) of graft-free sinus floor elevation (GFSFE) with simultaneous implant placement, using either the lateral window or transcrestal approach at sites with mean residual bone height 5.7 ± 1.7 mm. Mean implant survival was 97.9%, demonstrating that predictable osseointegration is achievable without any graft material — the implant itself maintains the sub-antral space and the blood clot mineralizes around the protruded apex. Mean vertical bone gain was 3.8 mm, with meta-regression identifying surgical approach, implant length, and immediate protruded implant length (PILi) as significant determinants; PILi alone explained 83.9% of VBG variance. The work provides the foundational SR+MA evidence for the "graft is often unnecessary" position in sinus elevation, later reinforced by RCTs such as Sansupakorn 2024 and the platelet-concentration meta-analysis of Guo 2020.

## Key Contributions
- Highest-tier (SR+MA) quantitative benchmark for graft-free sinus elevation: ISR 97.9%, VBG 3.8 mm, PILf 1.26 mm, MBL 0.91 mm.
- Meta-regression of VBG determinants: PILi (R² 83.9%) ≫ implant length (39.3%) > surgical approach (19.1%).
- Demonstrates the mineralization potential of the implant-tented blood clot without biomaterial, across both lateral and transcrestal approaches.

## Methodology
Systematic review + meta-analysis. Electronic and manual search of English human clinical studies (Jan 1976–Mar 2016) on simultaneous implant placement with graft-free sinus floor elevation via lateral window or transcrestal approach. 740 records screened → 22 studies, 864 implants, mean RBH 5.7 ± 1.7 mm. Random-effects model for weighted mean clinical parameters; mixed-effect meta-regression to evaluate factors influencing vertical bone gain. (Note: ingested copy is the publisher web-print containing abstract and references; full methods text not embedded.)

## Results
- Implant survival: 97.9% ± 0.02% (range 93.5%–100%).
- Vertical bone gain: 3.8 ± 0.34 mm; significantly influenced by surgical approach, implant length, and PILi (R² 19.1%, 39.3%, 83.9%).
- Protruded implant length at follow-up (PILf): 1.26 ± 0.33 mm (range 0.3–2.1 mm).
- Marginal bone loss: 0.91 ± 0.11 mm, significantly associated with follow-up period (R² 43.75%).
