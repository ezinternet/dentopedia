## One-line Summary

RCT (24 patients, 40 implants) of transcrestal sinus floor elevation with vs without enamel matrix derivative: EMD significantly increased endo-sinus bone gain at 3 months (3.72 vs 3.10 mm) but the difference disappeared by 12 months, with no inter-group ISQ difference.

## Summary

This randomized, parallel CBCT study tested enamel matrix derivative (EMD) as a biologic adjunct in transcrestal sinus floor elevation (TSFE) with simultaneous implant placement. Twenty-four patients (40 implants) were allocated to EMD+TSFE (13 patients, 20 implants) or TSFE alone (11 patients, 20 implants) and followed to 3 and 12 months. EMD significantly increased endo-sinus bone gain (ESBG) at 3 months (3.72 vs 3.10 mm), but the advantage was no longer significant at 12 months, and there was no inter-group implant-stability (ISQ) difference at placement or 3 months (only an intragroup ISQ increase within the EMD arm). The conclusion is that EMD accelerates early apical bone formation but confers no durable bone or stability advantage by one year — a limited-marginal-utility result that bears on whether the added biologic cost is justified.

## Key Contributions

- Adds a biologic-adjunct axis (EMD) to the transcrestal evidence base, alongside grafting and osteotome/osseodensification technique comparisons.
- Demonstrates an early (3-month) ESBG benefit from EMD that does not persist to 12 months.
- Shows no clinically meaningful inter-group ISQ benefit, arguing against EMD improving osseointegration endpoints.

## Methodology

Randomized, parallel-arm, CBCT-based study. 24 patients / 40 implants: EMD+TSFE (13 patients, 20 implants) vs TSFE alone (11 patients, 20 implants), recalled at 3 (T3) and 12 (T12) months. Outcomes: residual bone height (RBH), implant protrusion length (IPL), peri-implant sinus bone level (SBL), endo-sinus bone gain (ESBG), implant stability (ISQ); multivariate regression by group. (Abstract-only ingest — graft co-use, randomization detail, and covariates not extracted.)

## Results

- ESBG at T3: EMD+TSFE 3.72 ± 0.85 mm vs TSFE 3.10 ± 0.05 mm — significant (P < .05).
- ESBG at T12: no significant inter-group difference (P > .05).
- ISQ: no significant inter-group difference at T1/T3; significant intragroup increase within the EMD arm at T3.
