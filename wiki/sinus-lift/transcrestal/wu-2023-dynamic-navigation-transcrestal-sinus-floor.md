## One-line Summary
Retrospective pilot accuracy study (28 patients, 28 implants): dynamic navigation (dCAIS) plus piezoelectric instrumentation for transcrestal sinus floor elevation (TSFE) gave angular deviation 3.66 ± 1.67°, entry-point deviation 1.07 ± 0.69 mm, and apical deviation 1.09 ± 0.67 mm — comparable to non-TSFE dCAIS accuracy, with no obvious Schneiderian membrane perforation on immediate postoperative CBCT (premolar < molar angular deviation, p=0.004).

## 한줄요약
후향적 파일럿 연구 (28명, 28개 임플란트, 베이징대학교 구강병원, 후방 상악 경치조골 상악동거상술 (Transcrestal Sinus Floor Elevation, TSFE)): 동적 네비게이션 (Dynamic Computer-Aided Implant Surgery, dCAIS) + 압전 (Piezoelectric) 기구 사용 시 각도 편차 3.66 ± 1.67°, 진입점 수평 편차 1.07 ± 0.69 mm, 첨단 수평 편차 1.09 ± 0.67 mm로, 비-TSFE dCAIS 정확도와 동등하며 술 직후 콘빔 컴퓨터단층촬영 (Cone-Beam Computed Tomography, CBCT)에서 명백한 슈나이더 막 (Schneiderian Membrane) 천공은 없었음.

## Summary
This **retrospective pilot accuracy study** from Peking University Hospital of Stomatology evaluated **dynamic navigation (dCAIS)** combined with **piezoelectric instrumentation** for **transcrestal sinus floor elevation (TSFE)** — a procedure traditionally dependent on surgeon tactile experience because of limited visibility. Twenty-eight Straumann SP/BLT implants (4.1 or 4.8 mm diameter; 8 or 10 mm length) were placed in 28 patients via TSFE under real-time dynamic navigation; osteotomes plus piezoelectric devices completed the sinus elevation. Planned vs actual implant positions were compared on postoperative CBCT. Mean **angular deviation was 3.66 ± 1.67°**, **entry-point horizontal deviation 1.07 ± 0.69 mm**, and **apical-point horizontal deviation 1.09 ± 0.67 mm** — comparable to dynamic-navigation accuracy reported in non-TSFE implant placement. Premolar sites had smaller angular deviation than molar sites (p = 0.004), no surgeon effect was found, and no obvious sinus perforation was detected on immediate postoperative CBCT.

## Key Contributions
- First quantitative accuracy benchmark for **dCAIS in TSFE**.
- Demonstrates feasibility and safety of combining dynamic navigation with piezoelectric instrumentation for the sinus floor approach.
- Shows premolar > molar accuracy — relevant for case selection and operator expectations.

## Methodology
- 28 patients, 28 implants, posterior maxilla TSFE via dynamic navigation (Oct 2019–Jul 2021).
- Workflow: CBCT planning → dCAIS-guided drilling to within 1 mm of sinus floor → osteotome + piezoelectric sinus elevation → dCAIS-guided implant insertion.
- Outcome measures: AD (°), EPHD (mm), APHD (mm) from postop CBCT superimposition.
- Subgroup analyses by tooth type (premolar vs molar) and surgeon.

## Results
- AD 3.66 ± 1.67°, EPHD 1.07 ± 0.69 mm, APHD 1.09 ± 0.67 mm.
- Premolar < molar AD (p = 0.004).
- No surgeon-level differences.
- No obvious sinus perforation on immediate postop CBCT.
