## 한줄요약
SR+MA (18연구): TSFE 1년 생존율 — 비이식 97% vs 혈소판농축물 99%, 유의차 없음; 잔존골 충분하면 이식재 선택적

## Summary
SR+MA, 18 studies (13 non-grafted TSFE + 5 PC-grafted). **1-year ISR equivalent**: non-grafted **97%** (CI 0.96–0.99) vs PC-grafted **99%** (CI 0.97–1.00). Implies that for transcrestal sinus elevation with adequate RBH, **bone graft may be optional**.

## ISR Comparison Table

| Group | Studies | 1-yr ISR | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|---|
| Non-grafted TSFE | 13 | **97%** | 0.96–0.99 |
| PC-grafted TSFE (PRP/PRF/CGF/PRGF) | 5 | **99%** | 0.97–1.00 |

→ No statistically significant difference

## Why Non-Grafted Can Work in TSFE
- "Tenting effect" — elevated Schneiderian membrane + blood clot creates a closed compartment
- Blood clot → fibrin scaffold → endogenous bone formation (no exogenous graft needed)
- Implant body itself supports membrane displacement

## Clinical Relevance
- For TSFE with **sufficient RBH (typically ≥5 mm)**, grafting may not be essential to achieve 1-year survival
- Platelet concentrates (PRF, PRP, CGF, PRGF) **do not significantly raise** 1-year survival vs no graft
- Cost-benefit: skipping graft simplifies procedure without sacrificing 1-year ISR
- **Caveat**: this is 1-year data only; long-term endo-sinus bone stability less clear

## Caveats
- Heterogeneous PC types pooled
- Mostly non-randomized cohort studies
- 1-year endpoint — no long-term graft remodeling data
- RBH stratification incomplete
