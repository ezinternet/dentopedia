## One-line Summary
RCT of transcrestal (closed) sinus lift finding ozone gel adjunct did not significantly increase peri-implant bone width or labial plate thickness vs graftless control at 4 months, though both reached acceptable dimensions.

## 한줄요약
경치조골 상악동거상 RCT — ozone gel은 무이식 대조 대비 4개월 골 폭·협측판 두께를 유의하게 높이지 못함.

## Summary
This RCT evaluates whether an ozone gel adjunct improves peri-implant bone dimensions after transcrestal sinus floor elevation performed with osteotomes. At four months, neither bone width (8.96 vs 8.54 mm) nor labial plate thickness (1.89 vs 1.86 mm) differed significantly between the ozone and graftless groups, with both achieving clinically acceptable values. The result is a useful negative: in this dimensional, short-term endpoint, ozone added no measurable benefit over a graftless transcrestal approach.

## Key Contributions
- Direct RCT test of ozone gel as a transcrestal sinus-lift adjunct.
- Null result on bone width and buccal plate thickness — calibrates expectations for biological adjuncts.
- Supports adequacy of a graftless transcrestal approach for modest dimensional goals.

## Methodology
RCT; closed/transcrestal sinus elevation with osteotomes; study (ozone gel) vs control (graftless); radiographic bone width and labial plate thickness at 4 months. Registered NCT06604819 (retrospective).

## Results
- Bone width: 8.54 ± 1.46 mm (control) vs 8.96 ± 1.66 mm (ozone), ns.
- Labial plate thickness: 1.86 ± 0.63 mm vs 1.89 ± 0.51 mm, ns.
