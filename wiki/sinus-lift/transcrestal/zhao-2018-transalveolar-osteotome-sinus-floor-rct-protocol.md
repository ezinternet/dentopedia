## One-line Summary
RCT protocol (n=120, 4-arm, NCT03445039) for osteotome-TSFE vs modified drill-TSFE ± graft in posterior maxilla; RFA tracked at 6 timepoints over 26 weeks.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

Zhao et al. (Trials 2018) published the protocol for a 4-arm RCT at Peking University comparing two transcrestal approaches (classical osteotome-TSFE vs modified TSFE using crestal non-cutting drills) crossed with graft/no-graft, yielding four groups of 30 patients each (total n=120). The modified TSFE replaces malleting osteotomes with drill-based crestal preparation, hypothesized to reduce intraoperative discomfort and trauma while achieving equivalent implant stability and bone gain. RFA will be measured 6 times (baseline, weeks 6, 8, 12, 16, 26) enabling characterization of the ISQ stability dip pattern across technique groups. This is a protocol paper; efficacy results are pending.

## Key Contributions

The factorial 2×2 design simultaneously answers two clinically important questions: (1) osteotome vs modified drill for TSFE, and (2) graft necessity in transcrestal SFE. The 6-point RFA schedule is particularly informative for stability dip characterization. Registration: NCT03445039.

## Methodology

- 4 groups: osteotome±graft, modified TSFE (non-cutting drill)±graft
- n=120 (30/group); single center, Peking University
- Primary endpoints: survival, ISQ (RFA ×6 over 26 weeks), bone remodeling
- Secondary: complication rate, VAS pain, PRO
- Follow-up: 1 year post-loading

## Results

Protocol paper only — no efficacy data.
