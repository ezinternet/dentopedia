## One-line Summary
Case series (n=3, residual bone height [RBH] 0.4–1.5 mm): an osseodensification (OD)-based "crestal sinus window" (Densah Ø4.0→Ø5.3, 1 mm increments) achieved 10.3–13.6 mm vertical sinus bone gain with 0/3 membrane perforations, proposed as a transcrestal alternative to the lateral window in severely atrophic posterior maxilla — hypothesis-generating only given n=3, single operator, no comparator.

## Summary
Salgar proposes an "osseodensified crestal sinus window" technique that pushes the indication boundary of transcrestal sinus elevation from the traditional RBH ≥ 5 mm threshold down to RBH ≤ 1.5 mm — historically the exclusive zone of the lateral window approach. The protocol uses Densah burs in osseodensification (counter-clockwise) mode at 1100 rpm with copious irrigation, stepping from Ø 4.0 mm to Ø 5.3 mm and advancing past the sinus floor in 1-mm increments up to a maximum of 3 mm beyond the floor. The densified bone "window" then accommodates bone graft introduction.

Three patients (single hyperpneumatized molar site; large posterior sextants with and without septa, RBH 0.4–1.5 mm) achieved vertical sinus bone height gain of 10.3–13.6 mm with no Schneiderian membrane perforations and uneventful healing. The author argues this is comparable to lateral window outcomes, where the literature typical crestal-only gain is only 3–4 mm.

The clinical proposition is interesting but the evidence tier is the lowest possible — a 3-patient case series from a single private practice. A 0/3 perforation rate is not generalizable. There is no concurrent comparator. Long-term implant survival and post-op CBCT volumetric data are not extractable from the available text. Treat this as a hypothesis-generating technique description, not as evidence that OD-crestal can replace lateral window in extremely atrophic maxilla.

## Key Contributions
- Proposes extending OD-based transcrestal sinus elevation into a vertical-gain zone (10+ mm) previously requiring lateral window.
- Documents the Densah Ø4.0 → Ø5.3 stepwise protocol with 1 mm/step advancement past sinus floor, max 3 mm.
- Reports 0/3 membrane perforation despite RBH as low as 0.4 mm.
- Reports vertical gain 10.3–13.6 mm — within lateral-window magnitude.

## Methodology
Three patients selected for difficult sinus-lift indications (large posterior edentulous sextants with/without septa; single hyperpneumatized molar; RBH 0.4–1.5 mm). Full-thickness flap → Densah Ø 4.0 crestal osteotomy through floor → Densah Ø 5.3 in CCW (OD) mode at 1100 rpm with copious irrigation, 1-mm increments to max 3 mm past floor → bone graft → implant placement. Pre-op CBCT planning, post-op evaluation.

## Results
- 3/3 uneventful healing.
- 0/3 Schneiderian membrane perforations.
- Vertical sinus bone height gain: **10.3 to 13.6 mm**.
- Literature comparator (cited): typical mean crestal gain 3–4 mm.
- Implant survival, follow-up duration, post-op CBCT volumetric numbers: Numerical detail not extractable from available text — see PDF.

## Limitations
- **n = 3** — case series, lowest evidence tier; cannot generalize complication rate.
- Single operator, single private practice — no inter-operator variability data.
- No concurrent lateral-window control — "comparable to lateral window" claim is based on historical literature comparison.
- Long-term implant survival and graft remodeling not extractable from available text.
- 0/3 perforation has very wide CI given n — a single perforation in a future series would put the rate at 25%+.
- Technique depends on proprietary device (Densah/Versah); no conflict-of-interest declared but device dependence is structural.
