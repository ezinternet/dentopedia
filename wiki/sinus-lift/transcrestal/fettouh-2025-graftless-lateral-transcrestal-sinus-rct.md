## One-line Summary

RCT (n=26, RBH 4–6 mm, graftless) comparing lateral window vs transcrestal MSFE: no significant 12-month ISQ (80.92 vs 80.19) or marginal bone loss difference, but significantly greater intra-sinus vertical bone gain with the lateral window (3.82 vs 2.42 mm) at the cost of more postoperative complications and discomfort.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

This parallel-arm RCT compared graftless lateral window versus transcrestal (crestal implant) maxillary sinus floor elevation in 26 single posterior maxillary sites with residual ridge height 4–6 mm, all with delayed implant placement. With grafting removed from both arms, the approach variable is isolated. At 12 months, implant stability (ISQ) was statistically equivalent between approaches (lateral 80.92 ± 4.57 vs crestal 80.19 ± 7.28), and marginal bone loss did not differ (p = 0.31). The lateral window produced significantly greater intra-sinus vertical bone gain at both 6 and 12 months (3.82 vs 2.42 mm at 12 mo, p < 0.001), but at the expense of more postoperative complications and greater patient discomfort. For the 4–6 mm RBH window, the transcrestal approach delivers equivalent stability with less morbidity, while the lateral window buys more apical bone the clinical necessity of which is debatable when stability is already matched.

## Key Contributions

- Head-to-head RCT of **graftless** lateral vs transcrestal MSFE under matched RBH (4–6 mm) — controls for grafting, isolating the surgical approach.
- Demonstrates 12-month ISQ equivalence between the two approaches.
- Quantifies the trade-off: lateral window → significantly more vertical bone gain; transcrestal → fewer complications, less discomfort.
- Shows marginal bone loss equivalence at 12 months for both approaches.

## Methodology

Parallel-arm RCT; 26 single missing posterior maxillary teeth, RBH 4–6 mm, delayed placement; randomised to graftless lateral window (control) or crestal/transcrestal approach (test). Outcomes: implant stability by resonance frequency analysis (ISQ); intra-sinus vertical bone gain and marginal bone loss via CBCT at 6 and 12 months; clinical parameters and patient-reported outcomes. (Abstract-only ingest — allocation concealment, attrition, and PROM instruments not extracted.)

## Results

- ISQ at 12 mo: lateral 80.92 ± 4.57 vs crestal 80.19 ± 7.28 — not significant.
- Intra-sinus vertical bone gain: lateral significantly higher (p < 0.001) — 6 mo 3.64 ± 0.84 mm, 12 mo 3.82 ± 0.84 mm vs crestal 1.66 ± 1.27 mm and 2.42 ± 1.39 mm.
- Marginal bone loss: no intergroup difference (p = 0.31) at 12 mo.
- Postoperative complications and patient discomfort: more evident with the lateral window.
