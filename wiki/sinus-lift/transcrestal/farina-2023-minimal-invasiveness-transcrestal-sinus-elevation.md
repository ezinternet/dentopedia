## 한줄요약
체계적 문헌고찰 (19편): 경치조골 상악동거상술 (transcrestal Sinus Floor Elevation, tSFE)에서 회전 동력 기구 + 스톱 장치 + 표준화된 드릴 시퀀스(트레핀 포함)는 임상 효과를 유지하면서 침습성을 유의하게 낮추며, 잔존골 (Residual Bone Height, RBH) 4 mm 이상의 대구치 발치공 격벽이 있으면 즉시식립까지 함께 시행할 수 있다.

## Summary
Farina and coworkers systematically reviewed controlled clinical trials that addressed the invasiveness of transcrestal sinus floor elevation (tSFE). Across 19 articles representing 15 studies, the consistent finding is that tSFE — as a category — is a minimally invasive and clinically effective option for the atrophic posterior maxilla. Invasiveness can be reduced further by selecting **powered instruments with controlled pressure and excursion**, **standardized drill sequences** (often including a trephine to recover a bone core), and by **collapsing two procedures into one** when anatomy permits (immediate placement at molar extraction sites with sufficient interradicular septum).

## Key Contributions
- Operationalizes "invasiveness" of tSFE: morbidity, chair time, need for autologous harvesting, reconstructive devices, and cost.
- Shows that powered drill sequences and pressure-controlled osteotomes outperform manual mallet osteotomes on morbidity without losing effectiveness.
- Recommends standardized, RBH-adapted drill sequences with a trephine for bone-core harvest.
- Articulates a clinical decision rule: at molar extraction sites with **interradicular septum ≥ 4 mm**, immediate tSFE + implant placement is supported.

## Methodology
- Systematic literature search of controlled trials addressing one or more invasiveness determinants of tSFE.
- 19 articles / 15 studies included.
- Outcomes synthesized: intra- and postoperative morbidity, chair time, costs, oral health-related quality of life (OHRQoL), radiographic bone gain, and complications.
- No formal pooled meta-analysis due to heterogeneity; structured narrative synthesis with comparative tables.

## Results
- Postoperative pain after tSFE typically < 12 / 100 on a VAS — close to native-bone implant placement.
- A network meta-analysis (Al-Moraissi et al) cited shows tSFE superior to lateral SFE at RBH 4–8 mm.
- Farina et al's own RCT (RBH 3–6 mm) favored tSFE over lateral SFE on chair time, intra- and postoperative complications, patient-reported outcomes, analgesic dose, OHRQoL, and cost.
- Drill + trephine sequences offered both clinical efficiency and a usable autologous bone core.
- Immediate implant + tSFE at molars is viable when the interradicular septum height ≥ 4 mm.
