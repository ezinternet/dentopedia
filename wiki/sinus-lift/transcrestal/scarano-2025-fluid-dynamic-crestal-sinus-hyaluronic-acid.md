## Summary

Scarano et al. (Materials 2025) present a prospective series of 58 patients undergoing transcrestal sinus floor elevation using the Flusilift device (fluid-dynamic membrane elevation) followed by injection of 2% cross-linked hyaluronic acid as the sole sinus fill material, with simultaneous implant placement. CBCT at 12 months showed a mean bone gain of 7.5 mm. All 58 implants achieved primary stability; no failures or peri-implantitis were recorded. Wider sinuses correlated with greater bone gain, likely because a larger volume of HA can be injected and retained. The authors propose HA as a viable alternative to granular bone substitutes in transcrestal SFE, citing lower morbidity, no graft-handling variability, and the intrinsic bioactive properties of hyaluronic acid (anti-inflammatory, angiogenic scaffolding).

This study represents a distinct technical paradigm within transcrestal SFE: hydraulic pressure replaces osteotome malleting, and fluid HA replaces particulate graft — both departures from Summers' original protocol.

## Key Contributions

HA as sole sinus fill demonstrates bone regeneration (7.5 mm at 12 mo CBCT), suggesting that membrane elevation + blood clot scaffold does not strictly require particulate graft — consistent with Volpe 2022 and Sansupakorn 2024's graftless findings, but using HA to provide a bioactive volume-maintaining scaffold. The wider-sinus→more-gain relationship has planning implications for case selection.

## Methodology

- 58 patients / 58 implants; multi-center private practice; Italy
- Preop CBCT for RBH and sinus width
- Flusilift device for fluid-dynamic membrane elevation; 2% cross-linked HA injection; simultaneous implant placement
- Follow-up: CBCT at 12 months; ISQ at second-stage surgery
