## One-line Summary

RCT (n=60) comparing WANDSTA computer-controlled single tooth anesthesia (intra-ligamentary, 4% articaine) versus conventional IANB (4% articaine) for surgical removal of impacted mandibular third molars: STA had faster onset (2.2 ± 0.25 min difference, p<0.05) and lower postoperative pain/trismus, but required more supplemental long buccal nerve blocks (50% vs 23.3%) and showed higher intra-operative VAS during tooth elevation.

## Summary

This RCT randomized 60 patients undergoing surgical removal of impacted mandibular third molars into two equal groups: Group I received single tooth anesthesia (STA) via the WANDSTA computer-controlled intra-ligamentary injection system with 4% articaine, and Group II received conventional inferior alveolar nerve block (IANB), also with 4% articaine. STA achieved statistically significantly faster onset of action (2.2 ± 0.25 min difference, p<0.05) than IANB. Intra-operative VAS differed significantly only during tooth elevation, where the STA group reported higher pain than the IANB group. STA required more supplemental long buccal nerve blocks (50% vs 23.3% repeat blocks in the IANB group) and occasional supplemental lingual block (6.7%). Postoperative pain and trismus at 24 h were higher in the IANB group. The authors conclude WANDSTA STA is a viable alternative for third-molar surgical anesthesia, especially where nerve blocks are contraindicated, despite some limitation in the extent of anesthesia achieved.

## Key Contributions

- Head-to-head comparative RCT of computer-controlled intra-ligamentary STA vs conventional IANB specifically for a **surgical** (impacted third molar) indication, extending STA's traditionally pediatric/restorative use case.
- Quantifies faster onset for STA (2.2 ± 0.25 min, p<0.05) versus IANB.
- Documents the supplemental-injection cost of STA (50% needed long buccal block; 6.7% needed lingual block) versus IANB's 23.3% repeat long buccal block rate.
- Reports STA favors lower postoperative pain/trismus at 24 h despite higher intra-operative VAS during tooth elevation.

## Methodology

- Randomized comparative study, n=60, 30 per group.
- Group I (study): STA via WANDSTA system, intra-ligamentary injection, 4% articaine.
- Group II (control): conventional IANB, 4% articaine.
- Outcomes: onset of action, anesthesia failure, intra-operative VAS pain (including during tooth elevation specifically), need for supplemental lingual/long buccal blocks, lip numbness incidence, 24-h postoperative pain and trismus.
- Full details on randomization method, blinding, and statistical tests are not available (abstract-only).
