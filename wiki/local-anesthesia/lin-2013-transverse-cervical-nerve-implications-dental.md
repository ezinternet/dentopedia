## One-line Summary

Fresh cadaver study (n=2, Sihler's staining technique): the transverse cervical nerve (C2-C3, cervical plexus) was found innervating the posterior mandible in one of two specimens, supporting accessory (non-trigeminal) innervation as a cause of IANB failure in posterior mandibular teeth.

## Summary

The inferior alveolar nerve block (IANB) has the highest reported failure rate of any dental anesthetic technique, and mylohyoid/mental-foramen accessory innervation had already been proposed as one cause. This study asks whether the **cervical plexus** — via the **transverse cervical nerve (TCN)**, a branch of C2-C3 ventral rami classically taught as supplying only anterior neck skin — also reaches the posterior mandible. Using the Sihler's whole-mount nerve-staining technique on two fresh human cadavers (chosen because conventional dissection struggles with the small size of mandibular accessory foramina and nerve branches), the authors traced the TCN and found it innervating the posterior mandible in **one of the two specimens**. This is direct anatomical evidence — not just clinical inference — that cervical-plexus accessory innervation of the posterior mandible exists, at least in some individuals, and could explain IANB failures unrelated to injection technique.

## Key Contributions

- Direct anatomical demonstration (not inferred from clinical failure rates) that the transverse cervical nerve can supply the posterior mandible.
- Introduces the Sihler's staining technique to this anatomical question, resolving the visualization problem that had previously made small accessory branches hard to study.
- Provides a mechanistic anchor for supplemental-anesthesia decision-making when standard IANB fails in posterior mandibular teeth.

## Methodology

- Two fresh human cadaver specimens; skin dissected.
- Sihler's technique: formalin fixation → KOH maceration → acetic acid decalcification → Ehrlich's hematoxylin staining → acetic acid destaining → glycerin clearing, enabling three-dimensional nerve tracing.
- Abstract-only ingest — full text not retrieved.

## Results

- TCN (stained blue) innervated the posterior mandible in **1 of 2** cadaver specimens.
- Confirms cervical plexus **may** supply accessory innervation to the inferior border of the posterior mandible via the TCN — variable, not universal.
- Supports considering supplemental anesthetic techniques when IANB is inadequate in posterior mandibular teeth.
