## One-line Summary
SR+MA (6 RCTs from 976 screened): for SRP in adults, injected anesthetic gave lower pain intensity (P=.03) and required less rescue anesthetic (P<.0001) than intrapocket topical gel, but risk of developing pain and patient preference were similar.

## Summary
This systematic review with meta-analysis evaluated pain control during scaling and root planing (SRP) in adults, comparing intrapocket topical anesthetic gel against injectable local anesthetic. From 976 articles, 6 RCTs entered qualitative synthesis (4 low, 2 unclear risk of bias). Injected anesthetic produced lower pain intensity and less need for rescue anesthetic, but patients did not prefer one over the other and the overall risk of developing pain was comparable — meaning intrapocket topical gel remains a reasonable, less-invasive option for SRP despite injection's edge on pain intensity.

## Key Contributions
- First SR+MA directly comparing intrapocket topical vs injected anesthesia for SRP.
- Separated outcomes: pain intensity, rescue-anesthetic need, patient preference, risk of pain.
- Frames the efficacy-vs-invasiveness trade-off relevant to hygienist-led periodontal therapy.

## Methodology
SR+MA. 6 databases, RCTs only, comparing intrapocket vs injectable anesthetic during SRP. Primary outcome: risk/intensity of pain. Quality: Cochrane risk-of-bias tool; meta-analysis restricted to low/unclear-risk studies. 976 identified → 6 in qualitative synthesis.

## Results
Pain intensity: injected < topical gel (P=.03). Rescue anesthetic: injected < topical (P<.0001). Patient preference: no difference (P=.09). Risk of developing pain: similar between injected and topical. [근거강함]
