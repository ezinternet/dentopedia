## One-line Summary

Retrospective CBCT study (680 scans, 1,340 mandibular sides): retromolar canal (RMC) found in 16.12% of sides, carrying accessory nerve fibers alongside blood vessels — a documented anatomical cause of mandibular block anesthesia failure and a structure to spare or additionally anesthetize during retromolar surgery.

## Summary

This retrospective imaging study quantifies a third anatomical route for accessory mandibular innervation: the **retromolar canal (RMC)**, a variant branch of the mandibular canal running toward the retromolar region. Analyzing 680 CBCT scans (1,340 mandibular sides), the authors found an RMC in **16.12%** of sides — roughly one in six — most commonly with a vertical course (type A1, 39.82%) and least commonly horizontal (type C, 6.02%). Mean diameter was ~1 mm, mean height ~10 mm, and the canal sat ~15 mm from the second molar on average. Because the RMC carries **accessory nerve fibers** alongside blood vessels, its presence can account for mandibular block anesthesia failures localized to the retromolar/posterior region, and injury to its neurovascular bundle during surgery can cause hemorrhage or dysesthesia. The authors recommend sparing the canal surgically when present, or considering supplemental locoregional anesthesia in that area, and note CBCT is well suited for preoperative identification.

## Key Contributions

- Large-series (680 scans) CBCT quantification of RMC prevalence (16.12%) and morphologic subtype distribution.
- Confirms accessory nerve-fiber content of the RMC, tying its anatomy directly to anesthesia-failure and surgical-injury risk.
- Provides measurable benchmarks (diameter, height, distance to second molar) for preoperative planning.

## Methodology

- Retrospective review of 680 CBCT scans (1,340 mandibular sides).
- RMC identified and classified by course type; measured for diameter, height, and distance to second molar.
- Statistical testing of demographic/CBCT-resolution effects on frequency.
- Abstract-only ingest — full text not retrieved.

## Results

- RMC present in **216/1,340 sides (16.12%)**.
- Type A1 (vertical) most common (39.82%); type C (horizontal) least common (6.02%).
- Mean diameter 1.03 mm (SD 0.27), mean height 10.19 mm (SD 2.64), mean distance to 2nd molar 15.10 mm (SD 2.83).
- No significant demographic or CBCT-resolution effect on frequency.
- Clinical recommendation: spare the RMC intraoperatively or add locoregional anesthesia in the retromolar region when identified.
