## Summary

A short position paper from a Korean OMS academic, arguing for the development of formal clinical practice guidelines for dental local anesthesia. The author observes that dental local anesthesia is the most frequently performed clinical procedure in dentistry and a foundational element of dental-school curricula, yet Korea lacks an explicit, structured guideline. The article enumerates topics that a future guideline would need to address — sensory innervation and anesthetic selection across teeth, periodontal tissue, oral mucosa, TMJ, and the maxillofacial area.

## Key Contributions

- Documents the standardization gap in Korean dental local anesthesia practice.
- Articulates the scope a future guideline must cover.
