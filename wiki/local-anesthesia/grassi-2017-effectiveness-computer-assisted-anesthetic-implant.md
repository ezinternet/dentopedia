Prospective cohort study (n=45) found computer-assisted Single Tooth Anesthesia (STA) delivered markedly less pain (mean 1.6/10) than patients' prior experience with conventional anesthesia (mean 7.9/10) during dental implant surgery, using half the anesthetic volume.

## Summary

*Abstract-only — full text not retrieved from PMC (empty body); this page is built from the structured abstract.*

This prospective cohort study evaluated the effectiveness of a computer-assisted local anesthesia (CLA) delivery system — Single Tooth Anesthesia (STA) — in dental implant surgery. Forty-five consecutive patients whose treatment plan included immediate or delayed dental implants were enrolled; all had previous implant surgery under conventional anesthesia (CA) within the past 3 years and no prior pain-relief treatment, enabling a within-patient comparison of remembered CA pain versus new STA pain. STA was used for all patients with half the anesthetic quantity normally used for CA. Reported pain was dramatically lower with STA (mean 1.6, SD 0.7) than with CA (mean 7.9, SD 1.2; z=5.873, p<0.0001), and comfort during STA was rated high (mean 9.5, SD 0.79, range 7–10). A second (half-dose) injection was needed in only 3 of 45 cases. The authors conclude STA is effective at reducing pain, discomfort, and total anesthetic volume in implant surgery.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates a large, statistically significant reduction in patient-reported pain with computer-assisted STA versus conventional syringe anesthesia (CA) specifically in the dental implant surgery setting.
- Shows STA can halve the anesthetic volume typically needed for implant surgery while maintaining high patient comfort.
- Covers both immediate/post-extractive (27/45) and delayed/healed-site (18/45) implant placement, suggesting applicability across implant timing protocols.

## Methodology

- Prospective cohort, n=45 consecutive patients.
- Inclusion: prior implant surgery under CA within 3 years, no prior pain-relief treatment.
- All patients received STA (computer-controlled delivery) at half the usual CA anesthetic dose.
- Outcomes: 0–10 pain scale (recalled CA vs new STA), 0–10 comfort scale (STA), total anesthetic volume, need for second injection.
- Analysis: correlation analysis, regression models, z-test for pain comparison.

## Results

- Pain: STA 1.6 (SD 0.7) vs CA 7.9 (SD 1.2); z=5.873, p<0.0001.
- Comfort during STA: mean 9.5 (SD 0.79), range 7–10.
- Second injection (half initial dose) needed in 3/45 cases only.
- Anesthetic volume: half of conventional CA dose sufficient with STA.
- Subgroups: 27/45 post-extractive implants, 18/45 healed-site implants.
