## One-line Summary

Prospective cohort (Bologna master program), 89 root canal treatments at 24 months: Ceraseal premixed calcium-silicate sealer with warm carrier-based (Thermafil) obturation matched AH Plus on healing (91.1% vs 88.6%, NS) and survival (97.8% overall), with lower apical extrusion (13.3% vs 25%) and uniquely showed radiographic disappearance of 3 of 6 extrusions by 24 months.

## Summary

This is the first clinical study to pair a **premixed calcium-silicate (CaSi) bioceramic sealer — Ceraseal (MetaBiomed)** — with a **warm carrier-based** gutta-percha technique (Thermafil), and to report 2-year healing and survival. According to PubMed, the paper ([DOI 10.3390/jfb14030164](https://doi.org/10.3390/jfb14030164)) was conducted at the University of Bologna Endodontic master program as a prospective cohort.

Eighty-five patients (94 root canal treatments) were allocated to **Ceraseal-TF (n=47)** or epoxy-resin **AH Plus-TF (n=47)** based on operator training (12 master operators were randomly assigned to be trained on only one technique). At 24 months, 89 treatments in 82 patients were analyzed.

The bottom line: **Ceraseal with a warm carrier-based technique performed equivalently to AH Plus** on both healing and survival, while extruding less often and — uniquely — sometimes resorbing radiographically when extruded. Healing and survival were governed by case factors (preoperative PAI, primary vs secondary treatment), not by the sealer chosen.

## Key Contributions

- Establishes a **clinical (not bench) anchor** for Ceraseal: prior CaSi-sealer clinical evidence was limited to cold techniques and 12-month follow-up.
- Shows **non-inferiority to AH Plus** at 24 months — extends the carrier-based "gold-standard" obturation rationale to a bioactive sealer.
- Documents **radiographic disappearance of apically extruded Ceraseal** (3/6 by 24 mo), a CaSi-specific behavior tied to material solubility and calcium release, contrasting with dimensionally stable, inert epoxy-resin extrusions.
- Quantifies Ceraseal's **lower extrusion frequency** and links it to lower radiopacity (~6.5 mmAl vs AH Plus 10.0-11.8 mmAl) and physicochemical properties.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Prospective cohort, May 2019-March 2022, University of Bologna (ethics OUTENDOPROSP / CE 20079; STROBE-reported). 2-arm, operator-allocated (operators randomized to one technique each — therefore a cohort, not a tooth-level RCT).
- **Obturation**: Both arms used the **Thermafil warm carrier-based technique** (Thermaprep oven). Ceraseal-TF = premixed CaSi sealer (tricalcium silicate 20-30%, dicalcium silicate 1-10%, tricalcium aluminate 1-10%, ZrO₂ 45-50%); AH Plus-TF = epoxy-resin control.
- **Instrumentation**: NiTi rotary (Mtwo / Rotate) for primary, reciprocating (Reciproc Blue) for secondary RCT; 5% NaOCl + 10% EDTA + sterile-water final rinse.
- **Outcome**: Periapical radiographs at baseline, T6, T12, T24; **PAI** scored single-blind by two non-treating evaluators (weighted kappa 0.90). Sealer extrusion measured in ImageJ (bins 0 / 0.1-1.0 / 1.1-3.0 / >3 mm). Healed = PAI <3; survival = retained teeth.
- **Statistics**: Chi-squared / Fisher exact; multilevel mixed-effect logistic regression (person-level fixed effects, tooth-level random effects, robust SE for patient clustering).

## Results

| Outcome (24 mo) | Ceraseal-TF | AH Plus-TF | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Healed (PAI 1-2) | 91.1% | 88.6% | 0.624 (χ²); Fisher 0.714 — NS |
| Cumulative survival (both arms) | 97.8% (2/89 teeth lost) | — | — |
| Apical sealer extrusion | 6/44 = 13.3% | 11/44 = 25% | 0.040 (baseline) |
| Extrusion resorbed by 24 mo | 3 of 6 (3 stable) | 0 of 11 (all stable) | — |

- Both teeth lost were due to **horizontal root fracture** (one per arm); no extraction was for endodontic failure.
- **No new periapical lesions or re-exacerbations**; no pain/fistula/swelling during follow-up.
- **Predictors of non-healing** (multilevel regression, p<0.05): preoperative **initial PAI >3** and **previous (secondary) endodontic treatment**. The sealer/technique was *not* a predictor, and **sealer extrusion did not affect healing**.
- Overall extrusion across both arms: 17/89 = 19.0%.

**Limitations**: short 24-month follow-up (ESE recommends ≥4 years to declare healing); cohort (operator-randomized) rather than RCT; primary and secondary treatments not analyzed separately; lower Ceraseal radiopacity may under-detect small extrusions.
