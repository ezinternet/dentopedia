## Summary

This single-center pilot RCT (Pusan National University Dental Hospital, Nov 2020 – Oct 2021) compared **sealer-based (single-cone) root canal obturation** with four sealers: two epoxy-resin-based sealers (ERBS) — **AH Plus** (Dentsply Sirona, the gold standard) and **ADseal** (Meta Biomed) — and two calcium-silicate-based sealers (CSBS) — **CeraSeal** (Meta Biomed) and **EndoSeal TCS** (Maruchi). Eighty patients/teeth (asymptomatic irreversible pulpitis and/or pulp necrosis ± chronic apical periodontitis) were randomized 1:1:1:1 (n=20/arm); 71 teeth were analyzed (98.6% recall). Across radiographic voids, sealer extrusion, and 3-month postoperative pain, **no statistically significant difference** emerged between CSBS and ERBS. Differences in filling quality tracked individual product properties and canal anatomy rather than the resin-vs-bioceramic class. This is the wiki's clinical (in-vivo) anchor for the CeraSeal / bioceramic-sealer cluster, whose other members are largely in-vitro.

## Key Contributions

- Direct clinical RCT comparison of two CSBS (CeraSeal, EndoSeal TCS) against two ERBS (AH Plus, ADseal), all delivered by the same sealer-based obturation technique.
- Reports patient-level outcomes (voids, extrusion, VAS pain, percussion/palpation, periapical radiolucency regression), not bench proxies.
- Demonstrates that bioceramic sealers achieved **comparable short-term clinical efficiency** to the AH Plus gold standard.

## Methodology

- **Design**: prospective single-center, non-blinded, 4-arm RCT; G*Power sizing (α 0.05, power 0.8, ω 0.4 → 69; goal 83 with 20% dropout buffer). n=20/arm.
- **Inclusion**: asymptomatic irreversible pulpitis and/or pulp necrosis ± chronic apical periodontitis. **Exclusion**: mobility, crack, resorption, calcification, acute periapical periodontitis/abscess, severe systemic disease, intentional endo, C-shaped canals.
- **Treatment** (single endodontist, >20 yr, two visits): OneFlare flaring, PathFile #1 glide path, ProTaper Next instrumentation (apical X2–X5), 2.5% NaOCl with passive ultrasonic irrigation (PUI), Calcipex II Ca(OH)₂ between visits; visit 2 NaOCl + EDTA PUI then single-cone sealer-based obturation (CSBS via premixed syringe + master cone; ERBS via master cone).
- **Evaluation**: paralleling-technique radiographs for voids (two shifted views) and extrusion (per canal and per tooth); recall at 1 week, 1 month, 3 months with VAS pain, percussion, palpation, and apical-radiolucency assessment. Chi-square / Fisher exact, SPSS v15.0.

## Results

- **Recall / analysis**: 72/80 received intervention, 71 analyzed (98.6%).
- **Voids (per canal)**: EndoSeal TCS 60%, AH Plus 56.3% vs ADseal 31.6%, CeraSeal 29.4% — **NS (p > 0.05)**. Voids more frequent in molars (50%, 23/46) than premolars (38.5%, 5/13) or anteriors (30.7%, 4/13), i.e., anatomy-driven.
- **Sealer extrusion (per tooth / per canal)**: AH Plus 56.3% / 33.3% and CeraSeal 58.8% / 29.7% > EndoSeal TCS 35% / 17.8% and ADseal 21.1% / 12.2% — **NS (p > 0.05)**.
- **Postoperative pain (VAS)**: all arms 0–0.06 at 1 week, unchanged except AH Plus rising 0.06 → 0.13 (1 mo, 3 mo). One ADseal case with positive percussion; no other positive percussion/palpation.
- **3-month outcome**: all maintained except one ADseal case (recurring sinus tract at 1 month from a mesial-root cemental tear). Periapical radiolucency regression: ADseal 3/7, EndoSeal TCS 4/10, AH Plus 2/6, CeraSeal 2/8; pooled ERBS 38.5% (5/13) vs CSBS 33.3% (6/18) — similar.
- **Bottom line**: CSBS ≈ ERBS clinically at 3 months; filling-quality variation is product/anatomy-specific, not class-specific.
- **Caveats**: pilot scale, 3-month follow-up only, non-blinded; authors flag concern about hydraulic resorption of calcium-silicate sealers and call for long-term studies.
