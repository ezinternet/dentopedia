## One-line Summary

Systematic review (PROSPERO CRD420250655029, 2015–2025) of emerging endodontic irrigants (nanoparticle-based, herbal, photoactivated) and activation systems (ultrasonic, laser-activated, multisonic, negative-pressure): they enhance antimicrobial action, biofilm disruption, and smear-layer removal over conventional NaOCl/EDTA, but long-term clinical/healing benefit is unproven (e.g., no significant 12-month periapical-healing difference).

## 한줄요약

근관 세정제·활성화 시스템 최신 발전(2015–2025)을 정리한 체계적 고찰 — 나노입자·허브·광활성 세정제와 초음파·레이저·멀티소닉·음압 활성화가 기존 NaOCl/EDTA보다 항균력·바이오필름 제거·스미어층 제거를 향상시키나, 12개월 치유율 등 장기 임상 이득은 아직 입증되지 않았다.

## Summary

This systematic review maps two intersecting frontiers of root-canal disinfection: **next-generation irrigants** and **next-generation activation**. On the irrigant side it surveys nanoparticle solutions (silver, chitosan, copper oxide), herbal/natural agents (green tea polyphenols, neem, curcumin, *Punica granatum*, *Emblica officinalis*), photoactivated disinfection (methylene blue / indocyanine green + laser), and ozonated water / electrolyzed saline. On the activation side it covers passive ultrasonic (PUI), laser-activated irrigation (Er:YAG / Er,Cr:YSGG; SWEEPS), multisonic (GentleWave), apical negative-pressure (EndoVac), and manual dynamic activation. The recurring message: bench and short-term in-vitro data favor the new agents/systems for biofilm penetration, antimicrobial kill, and smear-layer removal, yet clinical and healing endpoints remain thin and underpowered.

## Key Contributions

- A single 2015–2025 snapshot organizing irrigation advances along the **irrigant vs activation** axes.
- Highlights agent-specific trade-offs: herbal irrigants preserve dentin microhardness and outperform NaOCl on smear-layer removal/penetration in vitro; nanoparticles give prolonged action and deeper biofilm penetration.
- Synthesizes activation hierarchy for penetration depth: **laser > ultrasonic > conventional syringe** (AgNP model).
- Flags that postoperative-pain and 12-month healing RCT signals do **not** reach statistical significance, tempering enthusiasm.

## Methodology

- Databases: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, Google Scholar; window 2015–2025; PROSPERO CRD420250655029.
- PICO: human endodontic patients; novel irrigants/activation; vs NaOCl/EDTA/syringe; outcomes = antimicrobial efficacy, biofilm removal, smear-layer removal, cytotoxicity.
- Inclusion: clinical trials + in vitro + in vivo. Excluded: non-English, reviews/opinion, insufficient data.
- Quality: Cochrane RoB tool for RCTs (6 studies tabulated). Narrative comparative synthesis; no meta-analysis.

## Results

- **Multisonic (GentleWave)** > PUI for E. faecalis biofilm removal (Choi 2019).
- **Activation penetration**: laser > ultrasonic > conventional (Kumari 2024, AgNP).
- **Herbal**: *P. granatum* least microhardness loss; herbal > NaOCl for smear-layer removal/penetration (Lakshmaiah 2023).
- **Negative pressure**: EndoVac caused less early postop pain than positive-pressure needle irrigation (Topçuoğlu 2018, RCT).
- **Healing**: LAI-SWEEPS and PUI showed numerically greater periapical healing but **no significant difference at 12 months** (Doğan 2024, RCT).
- Conventional NaOCl/CHX/ozonated water all reduced CFU significantly vs control, with no significant inter-agent difference (Pinheiro 2018).
