## One-line Summary

In-vitro dual-species (*E. faecalis* + GFP *E. coli*) biofilm model in 70 single-rooted premolars: PUI, XP-endo Finisher, and GentleWave all significantly reduced bacteria, LTA, and LPS, with GentleWave + minimally invasive technique most effective (XP-EF ≈ PUI); none fully eliminated LTA/LPS.

## 한줄요약

70개 단근 소구치 dual-species(*E. faecalis* + GFP *E. coli*) 바이오필름 in-vitro: PUI·XP-endo Finisher·GentleWave 모두 세균·LTA·LPS를 유의하게 감소시켰고 GentleWave+최소침습기법이 가장 효과적(XP-EF ≈ PUI)이었으나, 어느 것도 LTA/LPS를 완전 제거하지 못했다.

## Summary

Endodontic infections are polymicrobial, with LTA (from Gram-positive) and LPS (from Gram-negative) bacteria as the two principal virulence factors driving periapical inflammation and bone destruction. An optimal disinfection protocol should clear not only viable bacteria but also these residual endotoxins. This study built a novel dual-species "Live/Live" biofilm model (*E. faecalis* + GFP *E. coli*) to measure bacteria, LTA, and LPS simultaneously, then compared conventional PUI against two contemporary protocols — XP-endo Finisher and the GentleWave multisonic system.

All three protocols significantly reduced bacteria, LTA, and LPS after disinfection. XP-EF performed equivalently to PUI, while GentleWave + minimally invasive technique was the most effective overall and for intraradicular (cryogenically ground) residual infection. Critically, LTA and LPS were still detectable in 100% of samples after disinfection and after grinding — no protocol fully eliminated endotoxins, echoing the clinical limitation seen for calcium hydroxide.

## Key Contributions

- First simultaneous comparison of XP-EF, GentleWave, and PUI against bacteria + LTA + LPS.
- Novel dual-species GFP *E. coli* / *E. faecalis* biofilm model with cryogenic grinding for intraradicular residual quantification.
- GentleWave + MIT ranked most effective; XP-EF ≈ PUI.

## Methodology

- 70 single-rooted mandibular premolars (14 neg control, 56 infected with 3-week dual-species biofilm; 14 pos control, 42 experimental).
- Protocols (n=14): GWS+MIT, PUI+CIT, XP-EF+CIT.
- Sampling s1 (pre), s2 (post-disinfection), s3 (post-cryogenic grinding = intraradicular).
- CFU counts; LTA-ELISA; LPS by LAL assay.

## Results

- All protocols significantly reduced bacteria/LTA/LPS at s2 (p<0.05).
- XP-EF ≈ PUI (p>0.05).
- GWS+MIT most effective for bacteria, LTA, LPS and for s3 intraradicular disinfection (p<0.05).
- LTA/LPS detectable in 100% of samples at s2 and s3 — incomplete elimination.
