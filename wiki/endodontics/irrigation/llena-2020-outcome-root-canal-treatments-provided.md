## One-line Summary

Retrospective study (585 teeth, 1–6 yr follow-up) of primary RCT by endodontic postgraduates: preoperative periapical/periodontal status and especially the quality of the coronal restoration governed complete periapical healing (overall ~90.9% healing).

## Summary

This retrospective observational study (University of Valencia, 2013–2018) evaluated 585 teeth that received primary root canal treatment (RCT) by postgraduate endodontics students under specialist supervision, with 1–6 years of follow-up. Healing was defined per European Society of Endodontology (ESE) criteria as the absence of clinical (pain, swelling, sinus tract, mobility) and radiological signs of periapical pathology. Overall complete healing was approximately 90.9% (532/585). Significant predictors were screened by chi-squared test and then entered into separate preoperative and postoperative logistic regression models. The dominant message is that RCT prognosis is driven both by preoperative disease severity and by the **quality of the final coronal restoration** — the only postoperative factor retained in multivariable analysis.

## Key Contributions

- Identifies favorable **coronal restoration fit** as the single independent postoperative predictor of periapical healing — reinforcing the coupling of endodontic and restorative success (coronal seal).
- Quantifies the steep prognostic penalty of large preoperative lesions: healing 97.4% (no radiolucency) → 86.2% (1–4 mm) → 47.1% (>4 mm).
- Shows pulpal/systemic status matters: vital teeth healed 95.6% vs non-vital 82.3%; ASA I 92.5% vs ASA II 86%.
- Demonstrates that teeth serving as bridge/fixed-prosthesis abutments heal less well, a function/load effect on outcome.

## Methodology

- Design: retrospective observational study (ethics file H1445365577359); patients 18–81 yr, primary RCT ≥1 yr before exam, adequate preoperative radiograph.
- Exclusions: drugs altering bone metabolism, periodontal disease, previously treated teeth, non-endodontic extractions, missing data.
- Standardized ESE protocol: rubber dam, EAL working length (Root ZX), apical size ≈ #30, NaOCl (2.5–5.25%) irrigation with 17% EDTA and 2% chlorhexidine final rinse, ultrasonic activation, Ca(OH)₂ interappointment medicament when needed, gutta-percha + AH Plus (or BioRoot RCS) obturation, composite coronal restoration ± fiber post within 2 weeks.
- Analysis: chi-squared screening → logistic regression (separate preoperative and postoperative models).

## Results

- Overall complete healing ≈ 90.9% (532/585 teeth).
- Preoperative factors associated with healing (chi-squared): ASA status (p = 0.01), vitality (p < 0.01), lesion presence/size (p < 0.01), absence of preoperative pain (p = 0.04), positive pulp test, RCT cause (caries/pain/abscess/sinus tract), probing depth <4 mm, absence of mobility.
- Preoperative logistic regression retained: absence or <4 mm periapical lesion; probing depth <4 mm; RCT cause = caries/pain/abscess/sinus tract; tooth not a bridge abutment.
- Postoperative factors associated with healing: direct restoration, tooth not supporting a fixed prosthesis, favorable coronal restoration (all p < 0.01).
- Postoperative logistic regression retained **only** the status of the coronal restoration.
- Loss of tooth walls at presentation was not significantly associated with healing (p = 0.96).
- Conclusion: preoperative conditions and adequate fit of the coronal restoration influence RCT outcome.
