## One-line Summary
Systematic review (56 of 538 articles) comparing chelating agents across efficacy, erosion, cytotoxicity, and interactions: 17% EDTA is most effective for smear-layer removal and growth-factor release, while 9–18% HEDP (etidronic acid) offers NaOCl compatibility and avoids smear-layer formation.

## Summary
This systematic review updated the evidence on endodontic chelating agents (17% EDTA, 9/18% HEDP, citric acid, peracetic acid, 7% maleic acid) across seven axes: efficacy, erosive potential, cytotoxicity, interaction, antimicrobial effect, sealer adhesion, and growth-factor release. From 538 citations, 56 (moderate-to-low evidence) were included. EDTA at 17% remained the benchmark for smear-layer removal and for releasing growth factors relevant to regenerative endodontics, but also showed greater cytotoxicity. HEDP (etidronic acid) at 9–18% emerged as advantageous because it can be combined with sodium hypochlorite in a single irrigant and helps prevent smear-layer formation, making it an attractive contemporary alternative to sequential EDTA.

## Key Contributions
- Seven-axis comparison of the major chelators.
- Frames HEDP as a NaOCl-compatible, smear-layer-preventing alternative to EDTA.

## Methodology
MEDLINE/Cochrane/Scopus to Jan 2023; 538 → 56 included; modified CONSORT + Cochrane RoB; agents incl. EDTA, HEDP, citric acid, PAA, maleic acid.

## Results
17% EDTA best for smear-layer removal + growth-factor release (and most cytotoxic); 9/18% HEDP optimal for NaOCl compatibility and avoiding smear-layer formation.
