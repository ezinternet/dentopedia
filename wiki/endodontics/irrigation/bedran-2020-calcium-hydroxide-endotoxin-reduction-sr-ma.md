## One-line Summary

SR+MA (9 studies qualitative, 7 meta-analyzed): calcium hydroxide intracanal medication significantly reduced root-canal LPS before and after chemomechanical preparation (SMD ≈ −0.9 to −1.1, p<0.001) but did not eliminate endotoxin completely; certainty of evidence very low.

## Summary

LPS (endotoxin) from Gram-negative bacteria is a key driver of periapical inflammation, and complete endotoxin clearance is a goal of root canal disinfection. This SR+MA asked whether calcium hydroxide (Ca[OH]₂) intracanal medication (ICM) reduces LPS in infected root canals, pooling clinical/experimental pre-post studies up to June 2020.

Across 7 meta-analyzed studies, Ca(OH)₂ (with or without an added antimicrobial substance) significantly reduced LPS both before chemomechanical preparation (SMD = −1.087) and after (SMD = −0.919), and the effect held across irrigant subgroups. The incidence of LPS reduction was higher when an antimicrobial substance was combined with Ca(OH)₂ (98.9%) than Ca(OH)₂ alone (61.7%). However, Ca(OH)₂ **could not completely eliminate** LPS, and the GRADE certainty of evidence was **very low** — driven by predominantly pre-post designs, high heterogeneity, and few low-risk-of-bias studies. The practical message: Ca(OH)₂ dressing meaningfully lowers but does not abolish residual endotoxin.

## Key Contributions

- Quantitative pooled estimate of Ca(OH)₂'s LPS-reduction effect before and after CMP.
- Shows adding an antimicrobial substance raises the incidence of LPS reduction (98.9% vs 61.7%).
- Establishes that Ca(OH)₂ reduces but does not eliminate endotoxin, at very low certainty.

## Methodology

- Searches to June 2020; clinical + experimental studies comparing LPS before vs after Ca(OH)₂ ICM.
- 9 studies qualitative, 7 meta-analyzed; RoB via RoB 2 / ROBINS-I / NIH pre-post tool; GRADE.
- Random-effects SMD, subgrouped by formulation, antimicrobial substance, irrigant, and reduction incidence.

## Results

- Before CMP: SMD = −1.087 (CI −1.453 to −0.721), p<0.001, I²=58.7%.
- After CMP: SMD = −0.919 (CI −1.156 to −0.682), p<0.001, I²=24.7%.
- LPS-reduction incidence: 98.9% (Ca[OH]₂ + AS) vs 61.7% (Ca[OH]₂ alone).
- Endotoxin not fully eliminated regardless of irrigant; certainty of evidence very low.
