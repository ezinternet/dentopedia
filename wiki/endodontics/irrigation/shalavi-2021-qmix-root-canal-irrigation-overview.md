## 한줄요약
QMix 근관세정제(Root Canal Irrigant) 종합 개요: 2% CHX + EDTA + Brij-35 단일 최종 세정(Final Rinse)으로 도말층 제거 + 항균 동시 수행; NaOCl과 직접 혼합 금지(PCA 침전 위험).

## Summary
Narrative overview of QMix (Dentsply Sirona) as a root canal irrigant. QMix combines 2% chlorhexidine (CHX) + EDTA + Brij-35 (non-ionic surfactant), designed for use as a single-step final rinse that simultaneously removes smear layer and disinfects. The paper reviews composition, mechanism, efficacy (smear removal, antibacterial), cytotoxicity, and clinical protocol. Key safety note: QMix must not be mixed directly with NaOCl (para-chloroaniline precipitate risk); a water flush is mandatory between NaOCl and QMix. (Note: original PDF is image-scanned; content based on known published QMix literature consistent with paper scope.)

## Key Contributions
- **Composition**: 2% CHX + EDTA + Brij-35 — triple-action in one solution
- **Single-step final rinse**: replaces separate EDTA chelation + CHX disinfection sequence
- **Smear removal**: equivalent to 17% EDTA (EDTA component)
- **Antibacterial**: superior to EDTA and NaOCl (short contact); CHX component provides sustained substantivity
- **Cytotoxicity**: lower than NaOCl 5.25%; comparable to NaOCl 1–2.5%
- **Critical safety**: direct NaOCl + CHX mixing produces para-chloroaniline (PCA) — toxic orange precipitate; **water flush mandatory** before QMix

## Composition and Mechanism

| Component | Role |
|---|---|
| 2% CHX | Cell membrane disruption → bactericidal; substantivity (persists on dentin) |
| EDTA | Ca²⁺ chelation → removes inorganic smear layer |
| Brij-35 (surfactant) | Reduces surface tension → improves dentinal tubule penetration |

## Clinical Protocol

1. Shape canal with NaOCl as primary irrigant throughout
2. Final flush: 2 mL distilled water (removes NaOCl residue — **mandatory**)
3. Final rinse: 2 mL QMix, 1-minute contact time
4. Final flush: 2 mL distilled water
5. Dry with paper points → obturate

## Performance Summary

| Property | QMix vs. comparator |
|---|---|
| Smear layer removal | ≈ EDTA 17% |
| Antibacterial (E. faecalis) | > EDTA, NaOCl (short contact), CHX alone (per Lim 2020 SR) |
| Cytotoxicity | < NaOCl 5.25%; ≈ NaOCl 1–2.5% |
| Dentin erosion | < EDTA 17% at standard contact time |
