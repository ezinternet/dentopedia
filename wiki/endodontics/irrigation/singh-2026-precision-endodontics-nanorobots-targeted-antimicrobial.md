## Summary

This editorial frames the next frontier of endodontic disinfection as **precision endodontics with nanorobots**. Conventional irrigation (NaOCl, chlorhexidine) with sonic/ultrasonic activation fails to fully reach dentinal tubules, isthmuses, lateral canals, and apical ramifications — the very regions that drive treatment failure. Engineered nanoagents (AgNPs, ZnO, chitosan, bioactive glass, PLGA carriers) and actively or magnetically steered nanorobots are proposed as carriers and effectors for targeted antimicrobial delivery and biofilm disruption. The authors stress that the technology is **aspirational** at present, with biocompatibility, cytotoxicity, long-term safety, cost, and regulatory pathway all unresolved.

## Key Contributions

- Names the bottleneck explicitly: conventional irrigation cannot enter dentinal tubule depths or apical complex anatomy.
- Surveys candidate nanoagents and integration platforms (irrigants, intracanal medicaments, sealers, scaffolds).
- Separates **what is experimentally demonstrated** (in vitro antimicrobial efficacy of nanoparticles) from **what is aspirational** (controllable magnetically steered nanorobots in vivo).
