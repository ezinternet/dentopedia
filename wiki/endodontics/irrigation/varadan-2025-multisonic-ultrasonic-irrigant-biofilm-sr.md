## One-line Summary

Systematic review (3 in-vitro studies, multispecies biofilm in extracted teeth) finding GentleWave multisonic activation achieved bacterial reduction similar to passive ultrasonic irrigation — multisonic did not consistently outperform ultrasonic.

## 한줄요약

다균종 biofilm을 심은 발치치아 모델 3편(in vitro)을 모은 체계적 문헌고찰: GentleWave 멀티소닉 활성화는 초음파 활성화(PUI)와 세균 감소량이 비슷했고, 멀티소닉이 일관되게 더 우수하지는 않았다.

## Summary

This PRISMA systematic review (no meta-analysis) compared **multisonic** irrigant activation — the GentleWave® System (GWS), which combines broad-spectrum acoustic frequencies, negative apical pressure, and continuous fluid delivery — against **ultrasonic** activation (PUI/PiezoFlow) for removing **multispecies/polymicrobial biofilm** from extracted teeth. The review deliberately excluded monospecies E. faecalis models as not representative of in-vivo endodontic flora. Only 3 in-vitro studies qualified. The overall conclusion: multisonic activation showed **similar efficacy** to ultrasonic activation in reducing intraradicular biofilm — GWS outperformed ultrasonic in 2 of 3 studies but the difference was not consistent and the synthesis judged the two broadly comparable.

## Key Contributions

- First SR isolating the **multisonic vs ultrasonic** comparison on **multispecies biofilm** (the clinically relevant target).
- Reinforces the position that no activation system has a proven edge: at the bench, the expensive multisonic system does not consistently beat ultrasonic activation for biofilm reduction.

## Methodology

- PICOS: P = polymicrobial biofilm models; I = multisonic activation; C = ultrasonic activation; O = biofilm removal; S = in-vitro.
- Databases: SCOPUS, PubMed, Cochrane, EBSCO host, LILACS + Google Scholar (inception–Dec 2024); PROSPERO CRD42024583277.
- Risk of bias: modified CRIS + JBI criteria for in-vitro studies.
- 3 included studies; outcome by qPCR (16S rRNA/NGS, 2 studies) or CFU (1 study).

## Results

- **Coaguila-Llerena 2022** (10-taxon polymicrobial, molars): GWS ≈ PUI, no significant difference.
- **Zhang 2018** (E. faecalis + S. mutans): GWS 99.91% vs PiezoFlow 98.01% DNA reduction (GWS marginally higher).
- **Alquria 2023** (E. faecalis + E. coli, premolars): GWS + minimally invasive technique most effective; PUI and XP-Endo Finisher also highly effective.
- Confounder: ultrasonic arms prepared to 35/.04 while GWS used minimal apical preps (15–20/.04–.06), so the comparison is not strictly equal-prep.
- **Bottom line: multisonic ≈ ultrasonic for multispecies biofilm reduction.**
