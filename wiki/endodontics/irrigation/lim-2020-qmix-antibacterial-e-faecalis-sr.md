## 한줄요약
QMix 항균효과 SR(14개 in vitro 연구): 장구균(E. faecalis)에 대해 NaOCl·EDTA·CHX·MTAD·Cetrimide 모두 대비 우수; NaOCl 대비 우위는 접촉 시간 <10분 또는 용량 <3mL 조건에서만.

## Summary
Systematic review of 14 in vitro studies evaluating QMix's antibacterial efficacy against Enterococcus faecalis. QMix was consistently superior to all tested irrigants (NaOCl, EDTA, CHX, MTAD, Cetrimide) under most tested conditions. The advantage over NaOCl specifically applies when NaOCl contact time is under 10 minutes or volume under 3 mL; with prolonged high-volume NaOCl, the difference disappears. No meta-analysis was possible due to heterogeneous outcome measures. No clinical RCT data available.

## Key Contributions
- QMix superior to EDTA, CHX, MTAD, and Cetrimide across all conditions tested
- QMix vs. NaOCl: **condition-dependent** — advantage only at short contact or small volume
- Dual mechanism: CHX (membrane disruption) + EDTA (Ca²⁺ chelation from bacterial cell wall) synergy
- No clinical outcome data; all evidence from in vitro E. faecalis models

## Methodology
- **Study type**: Systematic review; 14 studies; no meta-analysis
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane, Google Scholar (inception–2019)
- **Inclusion**: In vitro; E. faecalis; QMix vs. ≥1 other irrigant; quantitative bacterial count or zone of inhibition
- **Quality**: Modified Newcastle-Ottawa Scale for in vitro studies

## Results

### QMix vs. comparators
| Comparator | QMix result | Condition |
|---|---|---|
| NaOCl | **Superior** | Contact <10 min or volume <3 mL |
| NaOCl | Comparable | Contact >10 min AND volume >3 mL |
| EDTA (any) | **Superior** | All conditions |
| CHX 0.2–2% | **Superior** | All conditions |
| MTAD | **Superior** | All conditions |
| Cetrimide | **Superior** | All conditions |

### Proposed mechanism
- CHX → disrupts E. faecalis cell membrane → bactericidal
- EDTA → chelates Ca²⁺ from Gram-positive cell wall → potentiates bactericidal effect
- Brij-35 surfactant → improves penetration into dentinal tubules
