## One-line Summary

Single-blind RCT (n=45, 15/group) of single-visit treatment in asymptomatic necrotic mandibular first molars: both passive ultrasonic irrigation (PUI) and side-vented needle irrigation (SNI) lowered postoperative pain, swelling, and analgesic use vs conventional needle irrigation (CNI) at 24–72 h, with no difference between PUI and SNI.

## 한줄요약

단일맹검 RCT (n=45, 군당 15명), 무증상 괴사 하악 제1대구치 단일내원 근관치료: 수동초음파세정 (Passive Ultrasonic Irrigation, PUI)과 측방배출침세정 (Side-vented Needle Irrigation, SNI) 모두 24~72시간 수술후 통증·부종·진통제 사용을 일반침세정 (Conventional Needle Irrigation, CNI)보다 낮췄으며, PUI와 SNI 사이에는 차이가 없었다.

## Summary

This single-blind, parallel-group RCT (Mashhad University, Iran) randomized 45 patients with asymptomatic necrotic mandibular first molars equally to three final-irrigation delivery methods — CNI, SNI, and PUI — all treated in a single visit with standardized ProTaper Gold instrumentation and 5.25% NaOCl + 17% EDTA. The clinically novel contribution is benchmarking ultrasonic activation (PUI) against the *modern safe needle standard* (side-vented), not only against outdated open-ended CNI. Result: PUI and SNI were clinically equivalent on every outcome and time point, and both significantly outperformed CNI from 24 h onward. The authors interpret this as evidence that minimizing apical extrusion (a side-vented needle's lateral flow) may be as clinically important as acoustic agitation in this subgroup — meaning SNI offers comparable short-term symptom control at lower cost/complexity than ultrasonic activation.

## Key Contributions

- Head-to-head clinical RCT of CNI vs SNI vs PUI in a homogeneous high-risk flare-up group (asymptomatic necrotic mandibular first molars).
- Tests whether ultrasonic activation adds value over the contemporary safe needle, not just over open-ended CNI — finding no added benefit.
- Treats postoperative swelling as a co-primary patient-centered outcome alongside pain, plus analgesic consumption.

## Methodology

- **Design**: single-blind (patient + assessor), parallel-group RCT; block randomization (blocks of 3), sealed opaque envelopes; single 3-year endodontic resident operator, single center.
- **Sample size**: a priori on 24 h VAS, Cohen d=0.8, alpha 0.05, power 80% → 15/group (45 total).
- **Inclusion**: mature mandibular first molars, pulp necrosis (no thermal/EPT response), asymptomatic, no acute apical periodontitis, periapical radiolucency < 5 mm.
- **Standardized protocol**: ProTaper Gold rotary, 5.25% NaOCl during prep. Final irrigation per canal = 5 mL 5.25% NaOCl + 5 mL 17% EDTA; tip 1–2 mm short of WL; flow 1–2 mL/min; room temp.
  - CNI: 30-G open-ended needle, each solution passive 3 min.
  - SNI: 30-G side-vented needle (Zenplus), each 3 min.
  - PUI: NaOCl activated 30–60 s/canal (Ultra X, Eighteeth, silver tip #20/.00), EDTA passive 3 min.
- **Obturation**: cold lateral condensation + AH-26 (warm techniques avoided). Single visit.
- **Outcomes**: VAS 0–10 pain + binary swelling at 6/24/48/72 h; binary analgesic (ibuprofen 400 mg PRN). Kruskal–Wallis (Dunn post-hoc), Fisher's exact, Cochran's Q; alpha 0.05.

## Results

| Outcome | 6 h | 24 h | 48 h | 72 h |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Pain** (median VAS) | PUI 2.0 / CNI 3.0 / SNI 2.0 (p=0.206, NS) | PUI 0.0 / CNI 2.0 / SNI 0.0 (p=0.003; CNI > both) | CNI higher (p=0.008) | CNI higher residual (p=0.001) |
| **Swelling** (% present) | 33.3 / 46.7 / 20 (p=0.30, NS) | PUI 6.7 / CNI 46.7 / SNI 0 (p=0.002; CNI > PUI p=0.035, > SNI p=0.003) | CNI 6.7 only (p>0.99) | all 0% |
| **Analgesic** (% yes) | 26.7 / 46.7 / 13.3 (p=0.162, NS) | PUI 0 / CNI 33.3 / SNI 0 (p=0.007; CNI > both p=0.042) | CNI 6.7 only (p>0.99) | all 0% |

- **PUI vs SNI**: no significant difference at any time point on any outcome (null hypothesis accepted).
- All groups: significant within-group decline in pain over time (p<0.001); PUI/SNI resolved faster and more completely.
- Peak pain in first 6 h across all groups; CNI penalty emerges at 24 h and persists to 72 h.
- **Bottom line**: improved irrigant delivery (lateral-flow needle OR ultrasonic activation) reduces short-term postoperative flare-ups; activation confers no measurable advantage over a side-vented needle here.
