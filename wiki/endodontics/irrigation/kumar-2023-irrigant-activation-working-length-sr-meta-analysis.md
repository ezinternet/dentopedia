## One-line Summary
SR+MA: irrigant delivery to working length ranked — ANP (EndoVac) > PUI > sonic > MDA > conventional needle irrigation; all activation techniques significantly superior to conventional needle irrigation.

## 한줄요약
SR+MA: 관개액 근첨부 도달 효율 비교 — ANP(EndoVac) > PUI > 소닉 > MDA > 일반 주사기; 모든 활성화 기법이 conventional needle보다 유의하게 우수

## Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis comparing irrigant activation techniques (IATs) for delivery of irrigant to working length (WL) in mature permanent teeth. Searched MEDLINE, Scopus, Cochrane. Conclusion: all IATs superior to conventional needle irrigation (CNI); ANP (apical negative pressure, EndoVac) ranked first, PUI second.

## Ranking: Irrigant Delivery to Working Length

| Rank | Method | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **ANP** (EndoVac) | Best for WL delivery, prevents extrusion |
| 2 | **PUI** | Reliable; broad evidence base |
| 3 | **SI** (Sonic) | Simpler setup, effective |
| 4 | **MDA** | Manual dynamic activation |
| 5 | CNI | Reference; inferior to all IATs |

- Consistent across both **straight** and **curved** canals
- All IATs significantly better than CNI (p<0.05)

## Clinical Implication
- ANP best for apical delivery but not universally available
- PUI: strong second-line option with broad availability
- Routine incorporation of IAT recommended over CNI alone

## Limitations
- High heterogeneity across included studies
- Majority of studies in vitro / ex vivo — limited direct clinical translation
- ANP system availability varies by practice setting
