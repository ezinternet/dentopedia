## Summary
This comprehensive narrative review compares the major endodontic irrigant classes — sodium hypochlorite, chlorhexidine, EDTA, TetraClean, calcium hydroxide, distilled water, and herbal agents — across their antimicrobial, tissue-dissolving, and smear-layer roles. Its central message is that no single irrigant satisfies all requirements, so clinicians should individualize selection per case and use irrigants in sequence. It highlights chlorhexidine's value as a final rinse for sustained (substantive) antimicrobial activity, while warning that mixing NaOCl with CHX produces an orange-brown precipitate (a chemical smear layer) that can occlude tubules and compromise sealing.

## Key Contributions
- Single-reference comparison of irrigant classes and clinical roles.
- Practical cautions: CHX final-rinse substantivity; NaOCl+CHX precipitate.

## Methodology
Narrative (non-systematic) invited review.

## Results
No ideal irrigant; individualize per case; CHX final rinse for substantivity; avoid direct NaOCl+CHX mixing.
