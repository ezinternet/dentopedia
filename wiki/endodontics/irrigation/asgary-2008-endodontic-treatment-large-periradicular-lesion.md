## One-line Summary
Case report (n=1, 29-y female): a ~25 mm cyst-like periradicular lesion involving two maxillary molars healed completely by 9 months after single-visit non-surgical orthograde root canal therapy (no intracanal medicament), with sinus tract resolution in 2 weeks.

## Summary
A 29-year-old healthy female presented with mild pain in the right maxillary sinus area, a firm vestibular swelling, and a buccal sinus tract discharging purulent exudate. The right maxillary first molar was non-vital (negative pulp/cavity tests) and the adjacent second molar had a poor prior root filling. A panoramic radiograph revealed a well-circumscribed radiolucency ~25 mm in diameter extending from the distal of the second premolar to the distal of the second molar, with profound root resorption of the first molar — clinically diagnosed as a large cyst-like periradicular lesion, most likely an infected radicular cyst of endodontic origin. Both molars received single-visit non-surgical orthograde root canal treatment with no intracanal medicament. The sinus tract and symptoms resolved within 2 weeks, and 9- and 12-month recalls showed complete bony regression of the lesion. The case supports non-surgical, single-visit endodontics as the first-line treatment for even large periradicular lesions when the canal system is properly disinfected.

## Key Contributions
- Documents complete healing of a large (~25 mm) cyst-like periradicular lesion without endodontic surgery.
- Reinforces correct diagnosis plus effective biomechanical cleaning/disinfection of the infected canal system as the critical factor for healing.
- Demonstrates a single-visit protocol with no intracanal medicament achieving full radiographic resolution.
- Emphasizes prompt permanent coronal restoration (within 2 weeks) as a contributor to endodontic success.

## Methodology
- Single case; healthy 29-year-old female. Two right maxillary molars implicated.
- Diagnosis: clinical/radiographic (nonvital teeth, lesion >1.6 mm with a radiopaque cystic border) — no histology.
- Sinus tract traced with a gutta-percha cone; panoramic radiograph used to capture the full occluso-apical lesion dimension.
- Single-visit RCT under rubber dam: access cavity, Flexo-File #15–40 step-back instrumentation, copious sterile normal saline irrigation, working length by radiographs, obturation with gutta-percha + Roth's 801 sealer (lateral condensation). Permanent amalgam restoration at 2 weeks.
- Recall schedule: 1 day, 2 weeks, 9 months, 12 months.

## Results
- Sinus tract and clinical signs/symptoms resolved within 2 weeks.
- 9- and 12-month recalls: asymptomatic; panoramic and periapical radiographs showed complete bony regression (increased density, trabecular regeneration); no percussion/palpation sensitivity.
- Contextual literature cited: ~74% bony healing of large periradicular lesions in one series; 2008 Cochrane review found no significant difference in radiologic success between single- and multiple-visit RCT.
