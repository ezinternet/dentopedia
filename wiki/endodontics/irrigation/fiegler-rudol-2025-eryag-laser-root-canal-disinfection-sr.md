## One-line Summary

Systematic review (15 studies, 2015–2025) finding Er:YAG laser-activated irrigation (PIPS/SWEEPS) consistently improved root canal disinfection, biofilm disruption, and smear-layer removal over conventional and ultrasonic methods, and enabled lower NaOCl concentrations — though heterogeneous protocols limit pooling and clinical generalization.

## Summary

This PRISMA 2020 systematic review (15 studies, 2015–2025; no meta-analysis) evaluated **Er:YAG laser-activated irrigation (LAI)** — principally via **PIPS** (Photon-Induced Photoacoustic Streaming) and **SWEEPS** (Shock Wave-Enhanced Emission Photoacoustic Streaming) — for root canal disinfection. The Er:YAG wavelength (2940 nm) is strongly absorbed by water, generating photoacoustic streaming and cavitation with lower thermal risk than diode/Nd:YAG lasers. **Every included study** found Er:YAG activation significantly improved disinfection over conventional or ultrasonic methods, with superior apical bacterial reduction and dentinal-tubule penetration. Two clinically attractive findings recur: laser activation permitted **lower NaOCl concentrations** without loss of efficacy, and some studies reported **reduced post-operative discomfort**. The major caveat is heterogeneity — laser parameters, irrigants, and outcomes varied widely, precluding meta-analysis and firm clinical guidelines.

## Key Contributions

- Up-to-date (through Jan 2025) synthesis of Er:YAG PIPS/SWEEPS evidence, extending earlier PIPS reviews with SWEEPS data.
- Documents two practice-relevant levers: NaOCl concentration reduction and possible post-op comfort benefit.
- Apical third and dentinal tubules are where Er:YAG most outperforms ultrasonic/conventional irrigation.

## Methodology

- PICO: P = patients undergoing RCT; I = Er:YAG laser; C = conventional disinfection; O = disinfection effectiveness.
- Databases: PubMed/MEDLINE, Embase, Scopus, Cochrane (English, 1 Jan 2015 – 3 Jan 2025); PROSPERO CRD42025638319.
- Risk of bias: 10-item checklist based on the Cochrane Handbook.
- Outcomes: antibacterial efficacy, biofilm disruption, smear-layer removal; laser settings and irrigants extracted.

## Results

- **Uniform direction**: all 15 studies favored Er:YAG activation over conventional/ultrasonic for disinfection and smear-layer removal.
- PIPS/SWEEPS gave superior bacterial reduction, especially apically.
- Lower NaOCl concentrations remained effective when laser-activated.
- Representative studies: Mandras 2020 (PIPS reduced bacterial load + post-op discomfort), Yavagal 2021 (complete E. faecalis elimination in primary teeth), Neelakantan 2015 (mature biofilm destruction, NaOCl+etidronic acid / NaOCl-EDTA-NaOCl best), Shan 2022 (Er:YAG ≈ ultrasonic, both > conventional; access invasiveness did not matter).
- **Caveat**: protocol/parameter heterogeneity → no pooling, limited clinical generalizability.
