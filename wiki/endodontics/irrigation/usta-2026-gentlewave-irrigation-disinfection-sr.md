## Summary

This PRISMA systematic review (12 in-vitro studies; narrative synthesis, no meta-analysis) asked whether the high-cost **GentleWave® (GW) multisonic system** delivers superior root canal disinfection over conventional and advanced activation techniques (PUI, XP-Endo Finisher, laser-assisted) — particularly in the **conservative (small apical prep)** scenario GW is marketed for. GW gave **significantly higher LPS and LTA reduction** than PUI, and most studies showed enhanced biofilm and debris/smear removal with GW. However, the advantage was **inconsistent**: several studies found PUI similar or superior when paired with adequate canal preparation and optimized protocols, and Er:YAG lasers achieved debris removal comparable to GW. The authors conclude GW is effective in conservative preps but its overall superiority remains unproven, with study heterogeneity and cost constraining clinical interpretation.

## Key Contributions

- Largest GentleWave disinfection SR (12 studies), covering both microbiological outcomes (biofilm, CFU/qPCR, LPS, LTA) and smear/debris removal.
- Reframes GW economically: is its premium cost justified over accessible PUI, especially where it claims its niche (conservative shaping)?
- LPS/LTA endotoxin reduction is the one outcome where GW shows a clear, significant edge over PUI.

## Methodology

- PICOS: P = extracted human teeth (mature apices)/ex-vivo; I = GentleWave®; C = PUI / XP-Endo Finisher / laser-assisted; O = biofilm/microbial reduction, LPS/LTA, smear/debris removal; S = in-vitro.
- Databases: PubMed, Web of Science, Scopus, ScienceDirect, Cochrane (9 July 2025); OSF registration 10.17605/OSF.IO/AZN3T.
- Risk of bias: QUIN Tool.
- Heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis → narrative synthesis.

## Results

- **LPS/LTA**: GW significantly > PUI.
- **Biofilm + smear/debris**: majority favored GW.
- **Inconsistency**: some studies found PUI similar/superior with optimized prep+protocol; Er:YAG comparable to GW for debris removal.
- **Bottom line**: GW effective in conservative preps, but no consistent superiority over conventional/advanced systems; cost and heterogeneity limit a definitive verdict.
