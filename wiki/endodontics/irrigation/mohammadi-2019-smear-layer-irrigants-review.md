## One-line Summary
Narrative review of smear layer removal: MTAD > EDTA (speed); QMix ≈ EDTA; maleic acid ≈ EDTA; HEBP weakest; NaOCl alone cannot remove the inorganic smear component.

## 한줄요약

## Summary
Narrative literature review comparing the smear layer-removing ability of root canal irrigants: EDTA, MTAD, QMix, maleic acid, citric acid, HEBP (etidronate), NaOCl, and CHX. MTAD showed the fastest and most complete smear layer removal, outperforming EDTA. QMix was equivalent to EDTA while adding antibacterial action. Maleic acid (7%) was comparable to EDTA 17%. HEBP demonstrated the weakest chelating action. NaOCl alone cannot remove the inorganic smear component; a chelating agent is always necessary.

## Key Contributions
- **NaOCl alone is insufficient**: removes only organic smear component; chelator always required
- **MTAD**: fastest smear removal; superior to EDTA in speed and completeness
- **QMix**: matches EDTA + adds CHX antibacterial + surfactant penetration (single-step advantage)
- **Maleic acid 7%**: EDTA equivalent; may offer lower surface tension for better tubule penetration
- **HEBP (etidronate)**: weakest chelator; not recommended as sole smear-removal agent
- **CHX**: zero chelating action; smear layer entirely unaffected

## Results — Smear Layer Removal Hierarchy

| Irrigant | Efficacy | Key Notes |
|---|---|---|
| MTAD | Best | Doxycycline + citric acid + Tween 80; fastest, most complete |
| EDTA 17% | Reference standard | 1–3 min contact; removes inorganic component |
| QMix | ≈ EDTA | Combines EDTA + CHX + Brij-35; single-step |
| Maleic acid 7% | ≈ EDTA | Lower surface tension; less studied |
| Citric acid 10% | ≈ EDTA | Adequate; less clinical use |
| HEBP (etidronate) | Inferior | Weakest chelation; requires high concentration |
| NaOCl (any %) | None (inorganic) | Dissolves organic smear only |
| CHX (any %) | None | No chelating action |

### Two-step vs. one-step considerations
- Standard: NaOCl throughout → final EDTA rinse (1 min) → distilled water flush
- Single-step alternative: NaOCl → water flush → QMix or MTAD (eliminates separate chelating step)
- **Do not mix NaOCl directly with CHX** (PCA precipitate); water rinse between mandatory
