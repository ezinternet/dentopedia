## 한줄요약
치근단 치주염(Apical Periodontitis) 임상 SR+MA: 단회 치료(Single-visit Treatment)가 Ca(OH)₂ 다회 방문보다 방사선학적 치유율 유의 우수(RR 1.10, 1.03–1.19); NaOCl 농도 차이·CHX vs NaOCl 간 7일 통증 유의차 없음.

## Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis of clinical RCTs evaluating irrigation and intracanal dressing protocols for apical periodontitis (AP). Single-visit root canal treatment achieved significantly higher radiographic healing rates compared to multi-visit treatment with Ca(OH)₂ dressing (RR 1.10, 95% CI 1.03–1.19). Neither NaOCl concentration comparisons nor CHX vs. NaOCl comparisons demonstrated significant differences for postoperative pain at 7 days. Published in International Endodontic Journal Supplement 3, 2023.

## Key Contributions
- **Single-visit superiority confirmed**: RR 1.10 (95% CI 1.03–1.19) for AP healing vs. multi-visit + Ca(OH)₂
- Ca(OH)₂ intracanal dressing provides no pooled healing benefit over direct obturation in single-visit treatment
- High vs. low NaOCl concentration: **no significant difference** for healing or postoperative pain
- CHX vs. NaOCl: **no significant difference** for 7-day pain scores
- Clinical message: Irrigation protocol choice matters less than technique quality; multi-visit with Ca(OH)₂ is not superior for healing

## Methodology
- **Study type**: SR+MA; clinical RCTs
- **Population**: Patients with radiographically confirmed AP; ≥12 months follow-up for healing outcomes
- **Outcomes**: Radiographic healing (primary); postoperative pain (secondary)
- **Statistical**: Random-effects; relative risk (RR) for healing; mean difference for pain

## Results

### Healing (radiographic, ≥12 months)
| Comparison | RR | 95% CI | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Single-visit vs. multi-visit + Ca(OH)₂ | **1.10** | **1.03–1.19** | Single-visit superior |
| High vs. low NaOCl concentration | NS | — | No difference |

### Postoperative pain (7 days)
| Comparison | Result |
|---|---|
| CHX vs. NaOCl | NS |
| High vs. low NaOCl | NS |

**Clinical takeaway**: The single-visit approach is both non-inferior and marginally superior for AP healing. Ca(OH)₂ dressing cannot be justified on healing grounds for routine AP cases based on current pooled evidence.
