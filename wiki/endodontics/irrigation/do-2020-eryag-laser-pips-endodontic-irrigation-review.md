## One-line Summary
Literature review (59 studies): Er:YAG laser-activated irrigation (LAI)/photon-induced photoacoustic streaming (PIPS) outperformed conventional methods in 33/59 studies — no in vivo studies, no standardized protocol.

## 한줄요약

## Summary
Literature review of 59 in vitro/ex vivo studies evaluating Er:YAG 레이저 활성 관개 (Laser Activated Irrigation, LAI) and 광유도 광음향 스트리밍 (Photon-Induced Photoacoustic Streaming, PIPS) for endodontic irrigation. PIPS uses sub-ablative energy (20 mJ, 15 Hz) with ultra-short 50 µs pulses to generate intracanal cavitation and shockwaves. Er:YAG LAI/PIPS outperformed other activation methods in 33 of 59 studies for 스미어층 제거 (smear layer removal), debris removal, and canal disinfection. No in vivo studies were found. Large variability in study methodology limits conclusions.

## Key Contributions
- Establishes PIPS as a distinct laser activation mode using photoacoustic/photomechanical effects
- Demonstrates superiority in smear layer removal and debris reduction (bench evidence)
- Highlights critical gap: absence of clinical in vivo data

## Methodology
- Narrative literature review; 59 PubMed-sourced studies
- Compared against: syringe irrigation, passive ultrasonic irrigation (PUI), sonic activation

## Results
- Superior to other methods in 33/59 studies
- Strong bench evidence for smear layer removal and antimicrobial effect
- No in vivo studies available; protocols highly variable across studies
