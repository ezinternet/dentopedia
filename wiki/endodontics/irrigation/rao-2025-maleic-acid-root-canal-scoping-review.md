## Summary
This scoping review mapped the evidence for maleic acid (MA), a mild low-pH organic chelator, as a root-canal conditioner. It concludes that 7% MA is a potent irrigant with consistent superiority over EDTA for removing the apical-third smear layer — the region where EDTA is weakest — and offers advantages in adhesion and regenerative contexts (including calcium-hydroxide removal). Because MA also demineralizes dentin, the authors recommend controlled, short conditioning times (1–3 min) to optimize smear-layer removal while minimizing erosive risk. It deepens the maleic-acid strand of the broader chelating-agent literature.

## Key Contributions
- Focused synthesis of maleic-acid efficacy, antimicrobial action, dentin effects, and interactions.
- Practical 1–3 min conditioning recommendation to balance efficacy vs erosion.

## Methodology
Scoping review (PRISMA-ScR 2018; Arksey & O'Malley) mapping MA's role across endodontic outcomes.

## Results
7% MA superior to EDTA for apical smear-layer removal, with adhesion/regeneration advantages; short application advised to limit demineralization.
