## One-line Summary

Overview of 5 systematic reviews (2 high-quality by AMSTAR2): endotoxin levels correlate with symptomatic teeth; chemomechanical preparation and calcium hydroxide ICM significantly reduce but cannot eliminate endotoxins; multi-session treatment outperforms single-session in symptomatic apical periodontitis.

## 한줄요약

5개 체계적 문헌고찰 종합 overview(AMSTAR2 고품질 2편): endotoxin 수치는 증상치와 상관하고, 화학기계적 형성과 수산화칼슘 첩약은 endotoxin을 유의하게 감소시키나 완전 제거는 못 하며, 증상성 근단치주염에서 다회 내원이 단회보다 우수하다.

## Summary

This umbrella review (overview of systematic reviews) sits at the top of the endodontic-endotoxin evidence pyramid, synthesizing 5 SRs (Medline/Scopus/Embase/Cochrane/Google Scholar, up to July 2021; AMSTAR2-appraised, 2 of high quality). It addresses two questions: the role of endotoxins in endodontic infection, and how effectively root canal therapy removes them.

Endotoxin levels were significantly higher in teeth with exudation, a prior pain episode, and pain on percussion — linking LPS load to clinical symptomatology. On the therapeutic side, chemomechanical preparation significantly reduced endotoxin, and calcium hydroxide intracanal medication in symptomatic teeth produced a significant reduction (SMD −1.051; very low certainty, I²=83.3%). Multiple-session treatment was significantly more effective than single-session for endotoxin/LPS removal in symptomatic apical periodontitis. The overarching conclusion mirrors the rest of this batch: conventional chemomechanical preparation and intracanal medication **reduce but cannot eliminate** endotoxins, so more effective disinfection therapies are warranted.

## Key Contributions

- Top-of-pyramid synthesis (overview of SRs) of the endotoxin–endodontics literature.
- Links endotoxin load to clinical symptoms (exudation, prior pain, percussion pain).
- Documents the evidence ceiling: no conventional procedure eliminates endotoxin; multi-session > single-session in symptomatic AP.

## Methodology

- Databases: Medline, Scopus, Embase, Cochrane, Google Scholar; reviews to July 2021.
- Inclusion: SRs on endotoxins from clinical/observational studies; AMSTAR2 quality appraisal.
- 5 SRs included, 2 high quality.

## Results

- Higher endotoxin in symptomatic teeth (exudation, prior pain, percussion pain).
- Chemomechanical preparation significantly reduces endotoxin.
- Ca(OH)₂ ICM in symptomatic teeth: SMD −1.051 (95% CI −2.039 to −0.063), p<0.05, I²=83.3%, very low certainty.
- Multi-session > single-session for endotoxin removal in symptomatic apical periodontitis.
- No conventional procedure eliminates endotoxin.
