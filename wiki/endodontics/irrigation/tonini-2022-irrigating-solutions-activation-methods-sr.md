## 한줄요약
SR (RCT 7편): 활성화 기법(PUI, XP-endo, EndoVac)이 기존 주사기 관개 대비 유의하게 우수; NaOCl+HEDP/MTAD 병용은 항균력 추가 이점 없음

## Summary
RCT-only systematic review (7 RCTs, search to Jan 2021) on irrigating solutions and activation methods in clinical endodontics. Activation methods consistently outperform conventional needle irrigation (CNI) for biofilm reduction. Combining NaOCl with HEDP or MTAD adds no antimicrobial advantage over NaOCl alone.

## Key Findings

| Comparison | Finding |
|---|---|
| NaOCl + HEDP vs NaOCl alone | **No** antimicrobial advantage |
| NaOCl + MTAD vs NaOCl alone | **No** antimicrobial advantage |
| NaOCl vs CHX | Broadly comparable antimicrobial efficacy |
| CNI vs PUI / XP-endo / EndoVac | **Activation significantly better** for biofilm reduction |

## Activation Methods Included
- **PUI** (Passive Ultrasonic Irrigation): strongest evidence
- **XP-endo Finisher**: effective, thermomechanical activation
- **F-file**: sonic-based
- **EndoVac** (ANP): superior apical delivery

## Design Strength
- RCT-only inclusion → higher internal validity vs mixed-design reviews
- Cochrane RoB2 tool for quality assessment
- Limitation: only 7 RCTs — small evidence base; heterogeneous protocols
