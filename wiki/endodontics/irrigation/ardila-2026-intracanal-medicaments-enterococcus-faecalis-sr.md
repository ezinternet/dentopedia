## One-line Summary

Systematic review (PRISMA 2020, PROSPERO CRD420261345958; 12 studies, in vitro/ex vivo/clinical) with domain-based interpretive synthesis + exploratory meta-analysis of intracanal medicaments vs Enterococcus faecalis: antibiotic-based pastes (esp. triple antibiotic paste) showed comparatively stronger antimicrobial performance, calcium hydroxide variable/weaker (esp. deep dentin), nanoparticle/bioactive agents promising but heterogeneous; pooling (only 2 studies) showed extreme heterogeneity, certainty low–moderate.

## Summary

Persistent post-treatment apical periodontitis is repeatedly linked to *Enterococcus faecalis*, a biofilm-forming, dentinal-tubule-penetrating, nutrient-deprivation-tolerant organism. This SR asks which **between-visit intracanal medicament** best controls it. Across 12 in-vitro/ex-vivo/clinical studies, the authors apply a **structured domain-based interpretive synthesis** (because the literature is too heterogeneous to pool) plus an exploratory meta-analysis. The interpretive verdict: antibiotic-based pastes — especially triple antibiotic paste — are comparatively the strongest, calcium hydroxide is variable and notably weaker in deep dentin (E. faecalis tolerates its alkalinity within tubules and biofilm) but improves in combination, and nanoparticle/bioactive systems are promising yet inconsistent. The quantitative side is weak: only 2 studies could be pooled, with extreme heterogeneity and imprecise estimates, leaving overall certainty low-to-moderate.

## Key Contributions

- Pathogen-specific synthesis isolating E. faecalis as the disinfection target, rather than mixed-flora endodontic infection.
- A **domain-based interpretive method** to rank medicament classes when standard meta-analysis is infeasible (heterogeneous CFU/log-reduction/MIC/MBC metrics).
- Comparative ranking: **antibiotic paste (TAP) > CH combinations > CH alone**; novel nanoparticle/bioactive agents flagged as heterogeneous.
- Honest reporting of meta-analytic fragility (n=2 poolable) and low–moderate GRADE certainty.

## Methodology

- PRISMA 2020; a priori protocol (PROSPERO CRD420261345958).
- Databases: PubMed/MEDLINE, Scopus, Embase (Ovid), to Feb 2026, no language limit, plus citation tracking.
- PICOS: E. faecalis primary organism (dentin/biofilm/clinical); CH, CHX-as-medicament, TAP/modified TAP, nanoparticle and other antimicrobial formulations; vs other medicaments/untreated/vehicle; outcomes CFU, log reduction, MIC, MBC, persistence/resistance/tolerance; in vitro/ex vivo/clinical with quantitative data.
- Excluded: irrigant/activation/sealer/PDT-only studies, reviews, case reports, non-quantitative.
- RoB: Cochrane RoB 2 (clinical) + adapted in-vitro/ex-vivo tool. Exploratory meta-analysis where feasible.

## Results

- **12 studies** included.
- **Triple antibiotic paste / antibiotic-based**: comparatively strongest, especially bacterial reduction and biofilm outcomes.
- **Calcium hydroxide**: variable, generally lower, especially deep dentin; combinations improve it.
- **Nanoparticle / bioactive agents**: promising but heterogeneous.
- **Meta-analysis**: only 2 studies poolable → extreme heterogeneity, imprecise estimates.
- **Certainty**: low to moderate.
