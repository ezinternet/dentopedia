## One-line Summary

Systematic review (GMS Hyg Infect Control 2025; PubMed/Scopus/Cochrane, 2020–2024 RCT/cohort/case-control; no meta-analysis due to heterogeneity) of intracanal medications for periapical-abscess infection control and healing: calcium hydroxide gives strong microbial reduction (~99.5%) and lowers IL-1β/TNF-α, triple antibiotic paste + diclofenac (TAPC) and nano-formulations (nano-Ag, nano-CH) give superior 48-h pain relief, Ledermix is fastest for acute apical periodontitis pain, and CBCT-monitored nonsurgical treatment of large lesions reaches ~76% healing.

## Summary

Post-treatment apical periodontitis and postoperative pain hinge on controlling persistent intracanal infection — frequently *Enterococcus faecalis* and biofilm-forming bacteria resistant to chemomechanical debridement. This review compares four between-visit medication strategies across three coupled endpoints (microbial control, inflammation/pain, radiographic healing). Calcium hydroxide remains the reliable workhorse for microbial reduction and cytokine suppression but is slow and weak on immediate pain. The newer formulations differentiate on speed of pain relief: TAP combined with diclofenac potassium (TAPC) and nano-formulations (nano-silver, nano-CH) cut pain meaningfully within 48–72 h, while Ledermix (corticosteroid + antibiotic) is the fastest analgesic for acute apical periodontitis. CBCT is positioned as the monitoring backbone, with ~76% healing reported for large lesions managed nonsurgically. The authors flag antimicrobial-stewardship limits on antibiotic-containing pastes.

## Key Contributions

- Aligns medication choice with the **clinical goal** (rapid pain relief vs durable disinfection vs lesion healing) rather than antimicrobial kill alone.
- Positions **Ledermix** as fastest for acute apical periodontitis pain and **TAPC/nano** for 48–72 h pain relief, with **CH** as the microbial-reduction and anti-inflammatory standard.
- Embeds **CBCT** as the diagnostic/monitoring standard for periapical healing (~76% in large nonsurgical lesions).
- Adds an explicit stewardship caveat against routine local antibiotics where an antiseptic of comparable efficacy exists.

## Methodology

- Databases: PubMed, Scopus, Cochrane Library; window Jan 2020 – Dec 2024.
- Inclusion: intracanal-medication-duration vs periapical-abscess healing; clinical/radiographic/microbiological endpoints; RCT, cohort, or case-control; English.
- Exclusion: incomplete data; non-comparative/reviews; animal or in-vitro.
- RoB: Cochrane RoB (RCTs) + Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (observational).
- Narrative synthesis; **no meta-analysis** (heterogeneous designs/outcomes).

## Results

- **Calcium hydroxide**: microbial reduction up to **99.5%**; lowered IL-1β / TNF-α; enhanced healing; slow, limited immediate pain relief.
- **TAPC (TAP + diclofenac potassium)**: superior pain relief vs CH, significant within **48 h** (dual antibacterial + anti-inflammatory).
- **Nano-formulations (nano-Ag, nano-CH)**: enhanced pain relief and effective microbial control, first **48–72 h**.
- **Ledermix paste**: most effective for **rapid pain reduction in acute apical periodontitis**.
- **CBCT-monitored nonsurgical** treatment of large lesions: ~**76%** healing.
