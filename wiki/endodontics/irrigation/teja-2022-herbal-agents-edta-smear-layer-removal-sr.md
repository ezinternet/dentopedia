## One-line Summary
Systematic review of in vitro studies comparing herbal agents with EDTA for smear-layer removal, finding quixabeira, Morinda citrifolia, oregano extract, and neem outperform other herbals while EDTA remains the standard comparator.

## Summary
This systematic review evaluated whether plant-derived (herbal) chelating agents can match EDTA for removing the smear layer from instrumented root canals — a question driven by interest in more biocompatible alternatives. Among herbals, quixabeira, Morinda citrifolia (noni), oregano extract, and neem performed better than other herbal agents, but EDTA generally remained the reference standard. The evidence is in vitro and heterogeneous, so herbal agents are promising adjuncts rather than established EDTA replacements.

## Key Contributions
- Ranks herbal chelating alternatives for smear-layer removal.
- Benchmarks them against EDTA.

## Methodology
PRISMA systematic review of in vitro smear-layer-removal studies comparing herbal agents with EDTA.

## Results
Quixabeira, Morinda citrifolia, oregano, and neem best among herbals; EDTA a strong comparator overall.
