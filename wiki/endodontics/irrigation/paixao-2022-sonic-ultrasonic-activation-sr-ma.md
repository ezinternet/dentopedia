## 한줄요약

## Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis (PRISMA 2020) of in vitro studies comparing sonic vs. ultrasonic irrigant activation. Sonic activation (EndoActivator-type) was significantly superior for apical-third smear layer removal (SMD −0.48, 95% CI −0.93 to −0.02, p = .03). Ultrasonic activation (passive ultrasonic irrigation, PUI) was significantly superior for push-out bond strength at both the middle (SMD −0.69) and apical thirds. No significant difference was found for coronal/middle smear layer removal or coronal push-out bond strength.

## Key Contributions
- Sonic activation outperforms ultrasonic specifically at the **apical third for smear layer removal**
- Ultrasonic activation (PUI) outperforms sonic for **push-out bond strength at middle and apical thirds**
- Neither method demonstrates a significant advantage in the coronal third for either outcome
- Results suggest the choice of activation method should depend on the primary clinical objective: clean apical smear vs. maximize sealer bonding

## Methodology
- **Study type**: SR+MA; PRISMA 2020; in vitro studies only
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane, Embase, Web of Science (inception–2021)
- **Inclusion**: Human single-canal teeth; final NaOCl/EDTA sequence; SEM-based smear layer scoring; sonic vs. ultrasonic comparison
- **Statistical**: Random-effects model; SMD (standardized mean difference)

## Results

| Outcome | Advantage | SMD | 95% CI | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Apical smear layer removal | Sonic | −0.48 | −0.93 to −0.02 | .03 |
| Middle smear layer | NS | — | — | NS |
| Coronal smear layer | NS | — | — | NS |
| Middle push-out bond strength | Ultrasonic | −0.69 | sig. | <.05 |
| Apical push-out bond strength | Ultrasonic | −0.78 | sig. | <.05 |
| Coronal push-out bond strength | NS | — | — | NS |

**Clinical implication**: For apical debris/smear clearance → favor sonic. For maximizing sealer-dentin bond strength → favor PUI. Both are superior to needle irrigation alone.
