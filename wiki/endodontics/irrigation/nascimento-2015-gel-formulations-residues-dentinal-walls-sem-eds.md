## One-line Summary
In vitro SEM/EDS study (48 single-rooted teeth) showing residues of 5.5% NaOCl gel, 2% CHX gel, and 24% EDTA gel persist on dentinal walls after canal preparation and a final saline + passive-ultrasonic rinse.

## 한줄요약
In vitro SEM/EDS(48 단근치): 최종 식염수·초음파 세척 후에도 NaOCl gel·CHX gel·EDTA gel 잔류물이 상아질벽에 남음.

## Summary
This study used scanning electron microscopy with energy-dispersive X-ray spectroscopy to determine whether gel-form irrigants leave detectable residues on canal walls after instrumentation and final irrigation. Three gels — 5.5% sodium hypochlorite, 2% chlorhexidine, and 24% EDTA — each left distinct morphological and elemental residue signatures on dentinal walls despite a final saline rinse with passive ultrasonic activation. The finding cautions that gel vehicles can persist on canal walls, potentially affecting sealer adhesion and obturation, reinforcing concerns about non-solution (paste/gel) carriers in the canal.

## Key Contributions
- Demonstrates persistence of gel-irrigant residues via SEM/EDS.
- Shows final ultrasonic irrigation does not fully clear gel residues.

## Methodology
48 single-rooted teeth; 4 groups (5.5% NaOCl gel, 2% CHX gel, 24% EDTA gel, control); final 6 mL saline + 1 min PUI; SEM/EDS residue and elemental analysis.

## Results
Each gel left persistent residue/elemental traces on dentinal walls after final irrigation/ultrasonics.
