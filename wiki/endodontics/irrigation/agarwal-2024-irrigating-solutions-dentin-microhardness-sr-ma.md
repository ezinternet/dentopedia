## One-line Summary
SR+MA (44 studies) showing endodontic irrigating solutions reduce root-canal dentin microhardness, with the measured magnitude strongly influenced by testing methodology (indentation load, dwell time, sectioning).

## Summary
This meta-analysis pooled in vitro studies measuring how root-canal irrigants affect dentin microhardness. Across 44 assessed studies of generally consistent quality, irrigating solutions — particularly sodium hypochlorite, EDTA, and acidic chelators — reduced dentin microhardness, reflecting demineralization and collagen disruption. A key methodological message is that absolute hardness values are highly sensitive to indentation method, load, dwell time, and how the tooth is sectioned, complicating cross-study comparison. The mechanical findings parallel the chemical changes documented by FTIR reviews.

## Key Contributions
- Quantitative synthesis of irrigant effects on dentin microhardness.
- Identifies testing-method moderators that confound comparisons.

## Methodology
PRISMA SR+MA; 44 studies assessed; Vickers/Knoop microhardness outcomes pooled with moderator analysis (load, dwell time, sectioning).

## Results
Irrigants reduce dentin microhardness; effect magnitude depends on solution concentration and on indentation methodology.
