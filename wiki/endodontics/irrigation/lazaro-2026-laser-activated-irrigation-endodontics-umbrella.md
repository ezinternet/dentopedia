## One-line Summary

Umbrella SR of 9 systematic reviews on laser-activated irrigation (LAI) in endodontics finds erbium lasers (Er:YAG, Er,Cr:YSGG) most consistently beneficial for canal decontamination, diode lasers for postoperative pain, Nd:YAG risky for microcrack formation, with overall evidence quality predominantly low to critically low.

## 한줄요약

레이저 활성화 세정(LAI)에 관한 9개 체계적 문헌고찰의 Umbrella SR: 에르븀 레이저가 근관 살균에 가장 일관적 효과, 다이오드 레이저는 술후 통증 감소, Nd:YAG는 미세균열 위험, 전체 근거 질은 낮음~매우 낮음.

## Summary

This umbrella review (PROSPERO CRD42025599352) synthesises 9 systematic reviews of clinical studies on laser use in endodontic treatment, identified from 319 initial records across 5 databases through December 2024. Quality assessment by AMSTAR 2 revealed that only 1 SR was high quality, 2 moderate, 2 low, and 4 critically low.

The central clinical finding is that laser-activated irrigation should be regarded as an adjunct with potential, but not yet definitive, clinical benefit. Among laser systems, erbium-based lasers (Er:YAG 2940 nm; Er,Cr:YSGG 2780 nm) showed the most consistent evidence for enhanced canal decontamination and biofilm disruption, driven by photoacoustic and cavitation mechanisms from strong water/hydroxyapatite absorption. However, erbium LAI was not consistently superior to passive ultrasonic irrigation (PUI), suggesting it is an alternative rather than a clearly better modality.

Diode lasers (810–980 nm) demonstrated clinically useful postoperative pain reduction (first 48–72 h) via photobiomodulation, but offered little benefit in terms of disinfection, smear layer removal, or biofilm disruption. Nd:YAG lasers (1064 nm) can penetrate dentinal tubules for deep disinfection, but carry a significant risk of dentinal microcrack formation at high-energy or continuous-emission settings, making their risk-benefit balance uncertain.

The underlying rationale for any activation modality is that approximately 60% of root canal surface remains uninstrumented by rotary NiTi files, leaving bacteria in accessory canals, lateral ramifications, and isthmuses. Laser-activated irrigation, like PUI and sonic activation, targets these anatomically inaccessible zones.

## Key Contributions

1. **First umbrella review for endodontic LAI** across all major laser types — establishes the current evidence ceiling and identifies the evidence deficit.
2. **Mechanistic differentiation** by laser class: erbium (photoacoustic/cavitation → irrigant penetration), diode (photobiomodulation → analgesia), Nd:YAG (photothermal penetration → tubule disinfection, microcrack risk).
3. **AMSTAR 2 quality mapping**: 8 of 9 included SRs below high quality; 4 critically low — flags rapid clinical adoption outpacing evidence base.
4. **Microcrack risk quantification**: Nd:YAG most implicated; effect is parameter-dependent; not consistently worse than ultrasonic instruments; baseline crack assessment absent in most primary studies (only 9/22 articles assessed baseline).
5. **Pediatric LAI data**: 1 SR on pulpotomy in primary teeth (n=7 studies; age 4.5–7.9 y) — no definitive conclusions due to heterogeneity.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Umbrella review, PRISMA 2020
- **Search**: 5 databases (PubMed-MEDLINE, Cochrane, SciELO, Web of Science, LILACS), inception–December 2024
- **Screening**: Dual-blinded via Rayyan; κ = 1.00 at full-text stage
- **Inclusion**: SRs with/without MA of clinical endodontic laser studies
- **Exclusion**: Exclusively in vitro SRs
- **Quality tool**: AMSTAR 2 (16 items); interexaminer κ = 0.78 (95% CI 0.487–1.000)
- **Final included**: 9 systematic reviews from 22 full texts screened (13 excluded with reasons)

## Results

| Laser type | Wavelength | Primary clinical role | Key safety concern |
|---|---|---|---|
| Er:YAG | 2940 nm | Canal decontamination, photoacoustic irrigant activation | Minimal at correct parameters |
| Er,Cr:YSGG | 2780 nm | Canal decontamination, photoacoustic activation | Minimal at correct parameters |
| Diode | 810–980 nm | Postoperative pain reduction (photobiomodulation) | Poor irrigant activation |
| Nd:YAG | 1064 nm | Deep dentinal tubule disinfection | Microcrack formation at high energy/continuous mode |

**Key numbers**:
- 9 included SRs / 319 initial records / 22 full-text screened
- AMSTAR 2: 1 high, 2 moderate, 2 low, 4 critically low
- ~60% of root canal surface uninstrumented by rotary files (rationale for activation)
- Postoperative pain benefit (diode): first 48–72 hours post-treatment
- Pediatric pulpotomy SR: 7 studies, age 4.5–7.9 y, no definitive conclusions

**Clinical guidance from authors**:
- Erbium lasers: best choice for irrigant activation in complex canal anatomy
- Diode lasers: consider when postoperative pain reduction is the priority
- Nd:YAG lasers: use only with strictly controlled parameters; routine use not recommended
- No laser system should replace conventional chemomechanical preparation

**Evidence verdict**: LAI is an adjunct with potential but unproven definitive benefit. Routine recommendation requires high-quality RCTs with standardised parameters, clinically relevant endpoints, and long-term follow-up.
