## One-line Summary

Parallel RCT (n=46 single-canalled symptomatic apical periodontitis teeth) — low-level laser therapy (LLLT, 976 nm diode, 30 s) vs Calcium Hydroxide intracanal medication: no significant intergroup difference in postoperative pain (6/12/24/48 h, all p>0.05), percussion pain (1 wk, p=0.708), or reduction of periapical IL-8 (p=0.123) and Substance P (p=0.385); both gave comparable acute symptom relief, supporting LLLT as a single-visit alternative — but CH's antimicrobial role was not assessed.

## Summary

This is the first RCT to directly compare **low-level laser therapy (LLLT)** against **Calcium Hydroxide (CH)** intracanal medication in patients with symptomatic apical periodontitis, pairing patient-reported pain with objective periapical inflammatory biomarkers. In 46 analyzed patients (23 LLLT / 23 CH), the two approaches were statistically indistinguishable on every primary and secondary endpoint: postoperative VAS pain at 6, 12, 24, 48 h, percussion pain at 1 week, and the periapical reduction of IL-8 and Substance P.

Both groups achieved significant reductions from baseline, and LLLT pain scores were numerically (not significantly) lower at all intervals. The clinical message the authors draw is that LLLT — applied to an **empty canal** in a single visit — can give acute symptom control comparable to a CH dressing, potentially enabling **single-visit endodontics** and avoiding the inter-appointment flare-up, coronal-leakage, and recontamination risks of multi-visit therapy. The important caveat: CH's well-established **antimicrobial/disinfecting** role was deliberately not evaluated, so LLLT is positioned as a symptom-management alternative, not a replacement for CH's long-term disinfection.

## Key Contributions

- First head-to-head LLLT vs CH for symptomatic apical periodontitis.
- Dual readout: VAS/percussion pain **plus** ELISA quantification of IL-8 and Substance P from periapical fluid (PS-1 post-instrumentation, PS-2 at 1 week).
- Frames LLLT as a single-visit option within the single-visit vs multi-visit endodontic debate.

## Methodology

- Parallel 1:1 RCT, CONSORT 2025; concealed allocation; molecular biologist + statistician blinded (participants not blinded). NCT04594317.
- n=48 enrolled (powered on postoperative pain), 46 analyzed.
- Single-canalled mature permanent teeth (anterior + single-rooted posterior), age 20–55, symptomatic AP with percussion pain.
- Standardized shaping (M-Pro rotary to WL), irrigation 2.5% NaOCl + saline + final 17% EDTA, obturation modified cold lateral compaction.
- **LLLT**: 976 ± 20 nm diode (Woodpecker LX16 plus), 0.5 W, 10 Hz, 200-µm tip, ~10 mm from buccal apex, 30 s/tooth (Arslan 2018 protocol), empty canal.
- **CH**: Metapaste 1–2 mm short of WL; removed at 1 wk with saline + ultrasonic (Ultra-X), radiograph-confirmed clear before PS-2.
- Stats: SPSS v25.0; non-parametric (Mann-Whitney U, Wilcoxon, Friedman + Dunn); alpha 0.05.

## Results

| Outcome | LLLT | CH | Intergroup p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pain 6 h (mean) | 3.0 | 3.2 | 0.773 |
| Pain 12 h | 2.4 | 2.8 | 0.751 |
| Pain 24 h | 1.5 | 1.7 | 0.885 |
| Pain 48 h | 0.6 | 1.1 | 0.644 |
| Percussion pain 1 wk | 2.9 | 2.8 | 0.708 |
| IL-8 reduction (ng/L) | 15.3 | 24.7 | 0.123 |
| Substance P reduction (ng/L) | 25.2 | 38.7 | 0.385 |

- All intergroup comparisons NS (p>0.05). LLLT numerically lower pain at every interval.
- Intragroup: percussion pain fell significantly in both (LLLT p<0.001, CH p=0.039). Substance P fell significantly in both (LLLT p=0.04, CH p=0.013). IL-8 fell significantly only in CH (p=0.044); LLLT NS (p=0.064).
- No significant correlation between IL-8/SP levels and pain at any time point.
- Confounders: posterior teeth more frequent in LLLT (p=0.049); higher baseline Substance P in LLLT (p<0.001).

## Clinical Bottom Line

For **acute postoperative symptom and inflammatory-mediator control** in symptomatic apical periodontitis, a single 30-s LLLT application is comparable to a 1-week Calcium Hydroxide dressing — making LLLT a candidate for single-visit treatment. However, because CH's antimicrobial action was not tested, LLLT does not displace CH where long-term canal disinfection is the goal. Findings are limited by the single-canalled-teeth restriction, unblinded participants, and biomarker analysis being underpowered.
