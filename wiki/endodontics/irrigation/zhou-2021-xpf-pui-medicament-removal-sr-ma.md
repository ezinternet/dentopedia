## 한줄요약
수산화칼슘(Ca(OH)₂) 잔류 세정 SR+MA(in vitro): 수동초음파세정(PUI) > XP-Endo Finisher 전반적 우세(OR 3.09), 단 NaOCl+EDTA 병용 시 유의차 소실(OR 1.51); PUI 우위는 2주 기간 calcium hydroxide에서 유의.

## Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis of in vitro studies comparing the XP-Endo Finisher (XPF) and passive ultrasonic irrigation (PUI) for removal of intracanal medicaments (primarily Ca(OH)₂). Overall, PUI was significantly superior (OR 3.09, 95% CI 1.96–4.86). However, when both methods utilized combined NaOCl + EDTA irrigation, the difference was no longer significant (OR 1.51, 95% CI 0.71–3.20). PUI showed a significant advantage specifically for 2-week Ca(OH)₂ dressing removal, but not for 1-week dressings.

## Key Contributions
- **PUI overall superior**: OR 3.09 (95% CI 1.96–4.86) vs. XPF for Ca(OH)₂ removal
- **Irrigant synergy eliminates the gap**: NaOCl + EDTA combination normalizes both techniques (OR 1.51, NS)
- **Dressing duration matters**: PUI advantage significant for 2-week Ca(OH)₂ but not 1-week
- Apical third remains the hardest-to-clean region regardless of technique
- Clinical implication: if using single-irrigant protocol, prefer PUI; if using NaOCl+EDTA, XPF is a viable alternative

## Methodology
- **Study type**: SR+MA; in vitro
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane, Embase, Web of Science, CNKI
- **Inclusion**: Human extracted teeth; Ca(OH)₂ (or other) medicament; quantitative removal measurement (microCT, SEM, weighing); XPF vs. PUI comparison
- **Statistical**: Random-effects model; OR with 95% CI; I² for heterogeneity

## Results

### Overall medicament removal
| Comparison | OR | 95% CI | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| PUI vs. XPF (all studies) | **3.09** | **1.96–4.86** | <.001 |
| PUI vs. XPF (NaOCl+EDTA subgroup) | 1.51 | 0.71–3.20 | NS |

### By Ca(OH)₂ dressing duration
| Duration | Result |
|---|---|
| 1-week Ca(OH)₂ | No significant difference (PUI vs. XPF) |
| 2-week Ca(OH)₂ | PUI significantly superior |

### By canal third
- Apical third: lowest removal rate for both techniques in all studies
- PUI advantage most pronounced at apical third
