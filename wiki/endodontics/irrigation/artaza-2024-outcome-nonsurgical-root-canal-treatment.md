## One-line Summary
Retrospective study (199 teeth/184 patients, single operator, NaOCl single-visit protocol, >1–8 y follow-up): nonsurgical RCT of large apical periodontitis lesions healed/healing in 89% (loose) / 67% (rigid) for initial treatment and 79% / 47% for retreatment; previous abscess, antibiotic use, lesions ≥10 mm, and sinus tract worsened outcome, and CBCT-evaluated cases showed lower healed rates.

## Summary
This single-operator retrospective study evaluated the outcome of nonsurgical root canal treatment and orthograde retreatment of teeth carrying **large** apical periodontitis lesions — a presentation for which clinicians often consider surgery. Across 199 teeth from 184 patients, mostly treated in a single visit with NaOCl irrigation and followed from more than one year up to eight years, the authors show that high favorable outcomes are achievable nonsurgically. Initial treatment reached 89% success under a loose criterion (67% under a rigid one), and retreatment reached 79% (47% rigid). The study reinforces a nonsurgical-first stance for large lesions while flagging the clinical features that lower the odds of healing.

## Key Contributions
- Provides direct outcome evidence that **large** apical periodontitis lesions (including very large, ≥10 mm) can heal with nonsurgical treatment/retreatment, reducing the default reliance on apical surgery.
- Quantifies success under both loose and rigid criteria for initial treatment (89% / 67%) and retreatment (79% / 47%).
- Identifies negative prognostic factors: previous acute abscess, prior antibiotic use, lesion ≥10 mm, and presence of a sinus tract.
- Documents an imaging-modality effect: CBCT-evaluated cases show lower healed rates than radiograph-evaluated cases, underscoring that outcome figures depend on assessment sensitivity.

## Methodology
- **Design**: retrospective outcome study, single operator, single center.
- **Sample**: 199 teeth / 184 patients, all with large apical periodontitis lesions; both initial treatment and retreatment cases.
- **Protocol**: predominantly single-visit treatment using NaOCl irrigation as the core disinfection step.
- **Follow-up**: periodic recall from >1 to 8 years; median ~31.5 months (treatment), ~32 months (retreatment).
- **Outcome**: clinical + radiographic/tomographic (periapical radiograph and/or CBCT) criteria categorized as healed, healing, or diseased; data dichotomized (healing = success under loose criterion, failure under rigid criterion).

## Results
- **Initial treatment**: 67% healed, 22.5% healing, 11% diseased → 89% (loose) / 67% (rigid) success.
- **Retreatment**: 47% healed, 32% healing, 21% diseased → 79% (loose) / 47% (rigid) success.
- **Worse outcome** associated with previous abscess, prior antibiotic use, very large lesions (≥10 mm), and a sinus tract.
- **CBCT-assessed cases had lower healed rates**, reflecting more sensitive detection of residual periapical disease than 2D radiographs.
- **Conclusion**: nonsurgical root canal treatment and retreatment can achieve high favorable outcomes even for teeth with large apical periodontitis lesions.
