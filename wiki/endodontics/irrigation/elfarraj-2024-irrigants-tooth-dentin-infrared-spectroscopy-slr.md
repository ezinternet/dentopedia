## One-line Summary
Systematic literature review (30 of 3452 studies) of FTIR-detected chemical changes to root dentin by irrigants: NaOCl mainly degrades the organic matrix (lower amide/phosphate ratio), EDTA mainly affects mineral, and glycolic acid acts more strongly than EDTA.

## Summary
This systematic review surveyed how common endodontic irrigants chemically alter root dentin as measured non-destructively by FTIR. From 3452 records, 30 in vitro studies (mostly ATR-FTIR) met inclusion. Sodium hypochlorite primarily degrades the organic (collagen) component — seen as a falling amide-to-phosphate ratio with increasing concentration — while EDTA and acids primarily remove mineral, with effects intensifying with time and concentration. Glycolic acid showed stronger demineralizing effects than EDTA. The review consolidates the spectroscopic basis for why irrigant sequence and concentration matter to dentin integrity and downstream bonding.

## Key Contributions
- First FTIR-focused systematic synthesis of irrigant-induced dentin chemistry.
- Organic (NaOCl) vs inorganic (EDTA/acids) effect separation with dose/time dependence.

## Methodology
1983–2023 search; OHAT risk of bias; in vitro extracted human/bovine teeth with FTIR spectral data; 3452 → 30 included; ATR most common (21).

## Results
NaOCl: organic loss (↓amide/phosphate) with concentration. EDTA: mineral effect ↑ with time/concentration. Glycolic acid > EDTA in effect.
