## Summary

*Enterococcus faecalis* is strongly associated with persistent endodontic infection and resists calcium hydroxide (CH) via proton secretion that neutralizes alkalinity; CH is also limited by application/removal difficulty and apical-extrusion risk. This study tested whether a **postbiotic** — lipoteichoic acid (LTA) purified from the probiotic *Lacticaseibacillus rhamnosus* GG (Lgg.LTA) — could serve as a safer alternative intracanal medicament. Interestingly, LTA is normally a Gram-positive virulence factor and a disinfection *target*; here probiotic-derived LTA is repurposed as a *therapeutic*.

In a human dentinal cylinder model with 2-week *E. faecalis* biofilm, Lgg.LTA (50 μg/ml, 24 h) inhibited intracanal biofilm formation as effectively as CH, whereas sterile distilled water and the NMP vehicle had little effect. Lgg.LTA also reduced both live and dead bacteria within dentinal tubules, suggesting lower re-infection potential.

## Key Contributions

- First test of *Lactobacillaceae*-derived LTA as an intracanal medicament in a human root canal model.
- Lgg.LTA matched calcium hydroxide for *E. faecalis* biofilm inhibition.
- Reduced intratubular bacterial load (live + dead), implying reduced reinfection.
- Proposes a postbiotic alternative avoiding live-probiotic bacteremia risk and CH extrusion toxicity.

## Methodology

- Ex-vivo: 28 single-rooted human dentinal cylinders, smear layer removed (NaOCl/EDTA), sterilized.
- *E. faecalis* KCTC 3206, 2-week biofilm; 4 groups (n=6): SDW, 20% NMP, CH (CleaniCal 30%), Lgg.LTA 50 μg/ml; 24 h.
- Readouts: SEM, CLSM live/dead, culture CFU including intratubular counts.

## Results

- Lgg.LTA ≈ CH for intracanal *E. faecalis* biofilm inhibition (both >> SDW, NMP).
- Lgg.LTA reduced live and dead intratubular bacteria.
- Lgg.LTA was the most potent of three *Lactobacillaceae* LTAs screened.
