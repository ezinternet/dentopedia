## One-line Summary
SR+MA of 72 studies: root canal treatment pain prevalence drops from 81% pre-treatment to 40% at 24 hours and 11% at 1 week; severity falls from 54 to 24 to 5 out of 100; supplemental anesthesia required in 60% of cases.

## 한줄요약
근관치료 전 통증 유병률 81%, 24시간 후 40%, 1주 후 11%로 급감; 통증 강도도 54→24→5점으로 감소; 보조마취 필요율 60% (72편 SR+MA).

## Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis of 72 studies quantifying pain prevalence and severity at three timepoints: pre-treatment, 24 hours post-treatment, and 1 week post-treatment. L'Abbé plots demonstrated that root canal treatment substantially reduces both pain prevalence and severity. Pretreatment pain prevalence was 81 (±28)%, dropping to 40 (±24)% at 24 hours and 11 (±14)% at 1 week. Severity on a 100-point scale fell from 54 to 24 to 5. Supplemental anesthesia was frequently required in 60 (±24)% of cases.

## Key Contributions
- First SR+MA to systematically quantify how RCT changes pain prevalence and severity across three timepoints
- Establishes that pretreatment pain is moderate-to-severe but resolves rapidly
- High supplemental anesthesia requirement (60%) highlights inadequacy of primary injection alone
- Provides normative data useful for patient counseling and research benchmarking

## Methodology
MEDLINE, Embase, Cochrane, PsycINFO searched. 5,517 articles identified → title/abstract/full-text screening → 72 studies included for meta-analysis. L'Abbé plots for treatment effect visualization. Pre-, intra-, and post-treatment data analyzed separately. Quality rating conducted.

## Results
| Timepoint | Pain Prevalence (%) | Pain Severity (/100) |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-treatment | 81 ± 28 | 54 ± 24 |
| 24 h post-treatment | 40 ± 24 | 24 ± 12 |
| 1 week post-treatment | 11 ± 14 | 5 ± 5 |

Supplemental injection requirement: 60 ± 24%.
