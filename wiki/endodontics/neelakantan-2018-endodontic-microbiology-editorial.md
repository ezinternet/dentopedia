## Summary

A short editorial introducing the "Endodontic Microbiology" special issue of Dentistry Journal. Its lasting value is conceptual framing, not data: it crystallises the now-standard premise that pulpal and periradicular infections are biofilm-mediated, and that the resulting antimicrobial resistance arises both from biofilm *structure* and from the *dynamics of interactions* among the biofilm's microbial components. The author argues that understanding biofilm biology is prerequisite to designing effective and efficient targeted therapy, and notes that despite major advances "much is still a mystery." Use this page to anchor the why behind intracanal disinfection strategy — cite it for framing, never as evidence of a treatment effect.

## Key Contributions

- Crystallises the biofilm-mediated paradigm for endodontic infection.
- Attributes resistance to biofilm structure + inter-microbial (polymicrobial) dynamics.
- Defines targeted, biofilm-aware disinfection as the therapeutic objective; surveys the field's open questions (EPS matrix, quorum sensing, persistent infection, irrigants, nanoparticles).

## Methodology

Not applicable — editorial / call for papers. No study design, no data.

## Results

No empirical results. Central claim: biofilm structure and dynamics drive the extraordinary resistance of endodontic microbial flora; much remains unknown, motivating basic-science and translational research.
