## Summary

Spinelli et al. (University of Bologna) report the first 36-month prospective clinical outcome of the **single-cone technique combined with Ceraseal**, a premixed calcium silicate-based bioceramic root canal sealer (Meta Biomed; ~20–30% tricalcium silicate, ~45–50% zirconium dioxide). Prior Ceraseal clinical evidence had been limited to ≤24 months. Across 58 root canal treatments in 52 ASA 1–2 patients, survival was **92.7%** and per-protocol healing (PAI ≤2) reached **92.1%** at 3 years (intention-to-treat 79.3%). Notably, **all pulpitis and all pulp-necrosis teeth healed (100%)** per protocol, while retreatment cases (exacerbated periapical lesion) healed at 80%. The study also characterizes Ceraseal sealer extrusion: 24% of cases extruded, and a subset (3/14) showed complete radiographic resorption over time, consistent with the bioactive/osteoconductive behaviour expected of calcium-silicate sealers. The authors conclude Ceraseal + single-cone is safe and effective, with a potential therapeutic indication in pulpal necrosis, while noting the absence of a control group and a follow-up shorter than the ESE-recommended 4 years. According to PubMed, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.14744/eej.2024.75537).

## Key Contributions

- **Longest clinical follow-up (36 months) for Ceraseal** single-cone obturation, extending evidence beyond the prior 24-month ceiling.
- Survival **92.7%**; per-protocol healing (PAI ≤2) **92.1%** (OR 16.04 vs baseline); ITT healing **79.3%** (OR 5.43).
- **100% healing of pulpitis and pulp-necrosis teeth** at 36 months (per protocol) — supports premixed bioceramic sealers where wet apices and large apical diameters impede hydrophobic epoxy-resin set.
- Documents **time-dependent radiographic resorption of extruded Ceraseal** (3/14 extrusions fully resorbed), aligning with calcium-silicate bioactivity.
- Extrusion-without-resorption and exacerbated periapical lesion were significant **negative** predictors of healing.

## Methodology

- **Design**: single-arm prospective cohort, Bologna University, May 2019–June 2023; STROBE-compliant; ethics OUTENDOPROSP CE 20079.
- **Sample**: 52 patients / 58 RCTs (14 incisors, 8 canines, 15 premolars, 21 molars); 58.6% with a baseline periapical lesion.
- **Protocol**: Root ZX WL; glide path to #15.02; rotary NiTi (Mtwo/Rotate) for primary, reciprocating NiTi (Reciproc Blue) for retreatment; 5% NaOCl + 10% EDTA irrigation.
- **Obturation**: **Ceraseal** single-cone with gutta-percha; sealer spread with K-file 3 mm short of WL.
- **Outcome**: PAI by two single-blind evaluators at 6/12/24/36 months; sealer extrusion measured (ImageJ). Linear + logistic regression with two-way clustering (teeth, patients); per-protocol and intention-to-treat (LOCF); Barnard CSM exact test for baseline associations.

## Results

| Metric | ITT (n=58) | Per protocol (n=38) |
|---|---|---|
| Mean PAI baseline → 36 mo | 2.5 → 1.5 (Δ −1.0) | 2.4 → 1.2 (Δ −1.3) |
| Healed (PAI ≤2) baseline → 36 mo | 41.4% → 79.3% | 42.1% → 92.1% |
| OR for healing (36 mo vs baseline) | 5.43 (2.74–10.76) | 16.04 (4.79–53.74) |

- **Survival**: 38/41 = **92.7%** (excluding 2 deaths, 15 unknown dropouts; 3 extractions for horizontal root fracture).
- **Healing by diagnosis (PP)**: pulpitis 100%, pulp necrosis 100%, retreatment 80% (ITT: 100% / 71% / 65%).
- **Sealer extrusion**: 14/58 (24%) — 11 without resorption, 3 with complete radiographic resorption.
- **Significant predictors of healing (ITT)**: pulpitis +46% (p=0.002), no extrusion +33% (p=0.028), composite restoration +28% (p=0.045); negative: extrusion-without-resorption −39% (p=0.003), exacerbated periapical lesion −34% (p=0.034), crown −30% (p=0.036).
- No post-operative pain, fistulas, or swelling; no iatrogenic complications.
- **Limitations**: no control group, small cohort, 34.5% loss to follow-up, no CBCT, follow-up below ESE-recommended 4 years.
