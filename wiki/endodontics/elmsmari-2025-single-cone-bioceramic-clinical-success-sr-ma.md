## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 3 RCTs (n=259) — single-cone bioceramic obturation showed numerically higher periapical success than conventional techniques at 6/12/18 mo (88.7/87.1/92.0% vs 76.4/76.4/90.7%) but no statistically significant difference (OR 1.12–2.09, all p>0.05); GRADE certainty low.

## Summary

This is the first systematic review and meta-analysis restricted to **randomized controlled trials** that compares single-cone obturation with bioceramic (calcium-silicate) sealers against conventional obturation (warm vertical compaction or single-cone with resin-based sealers) on **clinical and radiographic healing** — not laboratory surrogates such as sealing ability or push-out strength. Across three RCTs (259 participants), bioceramic single-cone consistently produced numerically higher periapical success at every follow-up, but the difference never reached statistical significance and converged to near-equivalence by 18 months. The honest bottom line is **equivalence with a non-significant trend toward bioceramic**, on **low-certainty** evidence.

The clinical message mirrors the single-paper signals already in this wiki: the simplified, technique-insensitive single-cone + bioceramic approach is at least as good as operator-dependent conventional techniques for healing — its advantages are workflow (simplicity, moist-field tolerance, reduced procedural error), not a proven healing superiority.

## Key Contributions

- **RCT-only, outcome-focused meta** — filters out the large in-vitro literature and isolates clinical/radiographic healing.
- Pooled success **favors bioceramic at all time points** (OR 1.12–2.09) but **NS**, with no publication bias and low GRADE certainty.
- Documents a thin evidence base: only **3 eligible RCTs** (one high risk of bias), short follow-up, no patient-reported outcomes.

## Methodology

- PRISMA 2020, PROSPERO-registered. PubMed + ScienceDirect + Cochrane to 31 Mar 2024 → 4,812 deduplicated records → 18 full texts → **3 RCTs** (Coşar, Kim, Bardini).
- Inclusion: permanent teeth, single-cone bioceramic vs conventional, ≥6-mo follow-up, ≥10/group, defined clinical + radiographic outcomes.
- Risk of bias: RoB 2.0 — Coşar low, Kim some concerns (randomization), Bardini high (protocol deviation, unclear concealment).
- Random-effects model; OR + 95% CI at 6/12/18 mo; I²; Egger's test; GRADE.

## Results

| Follow-up | Bioceramic | Control | OR (95% CI) | p | I² |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 6 mo | 88.7% | 76.4% | 2.09 (0.76–5.87) | 0.154 | 63.3% |
| 12 mo | 87.1% | 76.4% | 1.62 (0.50–5.31) | 0.424 | 57.5% |
| 18 mo | 92.0% | 90.7% | 1.12 (0.38–3.25) | 0.845 | 0% |

- No publication bias (Egger intercept −0.619, p=0.84).
- **GRADE: LOW** — downgraded for risk of bias, inconsistency, imprecision.
- Heterogeneity high early (mixed sealers, tooth types, primary vs retreatment), resolves to I²=0% by 18 mo.
- **Limitations**: 3 RCTs only (no subgroup/meta-regression); follow-up too short for full periapical bone healing (≥24 mo needed); single-cone–only scope; no PROs.
