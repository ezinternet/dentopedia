## One-line Summary

In-vitro L929 fibroblast MTT/microscopy study (n=36; CeraSeal, Bio-C, MTA-Fillapex, AH Plus at 0 h/24 h/7 d) where calcium-silicate sealers CeraSeal and Bio-C fall from severe cytotoxicity at 0 h to noncytotoxic (>90% viability) by 24 h, while AH Plus stays severely cytotoxic at every time point and MTA-Fillapex only reaches moderate.

## Summary

According to PubMed (PMC10003288, DOI 10.4103/jcd.jcd_320_22), this in-vitro study compared the cytotoxicity of two premixed calcium-silicate bioceramic sealers — **CeraSeal** (Meta Biomed, Korea) and **Bio-C** (Angelus, Brazil) — against the MTA/salicylate-resin sealer MTA-Fillapex and the epoxy-resin gold standard AH Plus, using the MTT assay on L929 mouse fibroblasts plus phase-contrast microscopy at 0 h, 24 h, and 7 days after mixing. The clinically important pattern: cytotoxicity is set-state dependent. All four sealers were severely cytotoxic when freshly mixed (0 h, <30% viability), but the calcium-silicate sealers recovered to noncytotoxic (>90% viability) once set, whereas AH Plus stayed severely cytotoxic throughout and MTA-Fillapex plateaued at moderate.

This is the wiki's first CeraSeal / premixed bioceramic-sealer biocompatibility anchor.

## Key Contributions

- Head-to-head cytotoxicity ranking of CeraSeal and Bio-C vs MTA-Fillapex vs AH Plus on a single L929 line under identical conditions.
- Demonstrates that fresh (unset) calcium-silicate sealers are transiently severely cytotoxic — attributed to the burst of Ca2+ and OH- (high pH) disrupting calcium homeostasis — but become biocompatible after setting.
- CeraSeal posted the single highest viability at 24 h (96.4%) and 7 d (97.2%); AH Plus never left the "severe" band even fully set.

## Methodology

- **Cells / assay:** L929 mouse fibroblasts (ATCC), 5 x 10^4 per well in 96-well plates; MTT assay (1 mg/mL PBS, absorbance 550 nm) for % viability; phase-contrast microscopy x20 graded per US Pharmacopeia.
- **Samples:** Teflon molds 4 mm x 2 mm, eluted per ISO-10993-12:2021 at 0.1 g/ml; three discs per material per time period (0 h / 24 h / 7 d), total n=36. 48 h exposure. Positive control 130 mg% phenol; negative control untreated.
- **Cytotoxicity bands (% viability vs control):** severe <30%, moderate 30-60%, slight 61-90%, noncytotoxic >90%.
- **Stats:** one-way ANOVA + Tukey's post hoc (between groups); repeated-measures ANOVA + Bonferroni (within group over time); P < 0.05.

## Results

Mean % cell viability (+/- SD), n=6 per cell:

| Sealer | 0 h | 24 h | 7 days |
|---|---|---|---|
| **CeraSeal** | 29.94 +/- 0.90 (severe) | 96.39 +/- 1.45 (noncytotoxic) | 97.19 +/- 1.51 (noncytotoxic) |
| **Bio-C** | 29.80 +/- 2.34 (severe) | 91.43 +/- 1.34 (noncytotoxic) | 93.58 +/- 2.60 (noncytotoxic) |
| **MTA-Fillapex** | 14.94 +/- 1.49 (severe) | 51.47 +/- 2.99 (moderate) | 59.65 +/- 2.19 (moderate) |
| **AH Plus** | 12.49 +/- 2.57 (severe) | 22.73 +/- 2.73 (severe) | 28.89 +/- 2.00 (severe) |

- Bio-C and CeraSeal showed higher viability than MTA-Fillapex and AH Plus at all time intervals (P < 0.001).
- CeraSeal vs Bio-C: not significant at 0 h (P=0.99); CeraSeal slightly higher at 24 h (+4.96, P=0.006) and 7 d (+3.62, P=0.04) — Bio-C's traces of aluminum/tungsten/iron oxide cited as the reason for its marginally lower viability.
- Microscopy matched MTT: CeraSeal/Bio-C normal subconfluent monolayers with no lysis at 24 h–7 d; AH Plus severe morphologic damage at every time point.
- Limitation: in-vitro only; no periapical blood washout modeled, single cell line, small per-cell n.
