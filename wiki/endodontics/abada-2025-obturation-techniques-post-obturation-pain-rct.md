## One-line Summary

RCT (n=150 mandibular first molars, single-visit, asymptomatic irreversible pulpitis) comparing CeraSeal vs AH Plus across cold lateral / continuous-wave / single-cone obturation: pain was low-to-moderate (VAS 0–1.4) in all groups; obturation technique alone did not affect pain (p=0.124) but AH Plus gave significantly higher pain than CeraSeal overall (p<0.001) and under continuous-wave compaction (p<0.001); sealer-extrusion incidence did not differ between groups (p=0.499) yet extrusion of either sealer raised pain (p<0.001).

## Summary

This prospective, CONSORT-registered randomized clinical trial enrolled 150 patients (30 per arm) with mandibular first molars diagnosed as asymptomatic irreversible pulpitis, treated in a single visit by one endodontist. Two sealers — **CeraSeal** (CS; premixed calcium-silicate / bioceramic sealer, META BIOMED) and **AH Plus** (AH; epoxy-resin sealer, Dentsply) — were crossed with three obturation techniques: cold lateral compaction (CLC), continuous-wave compaction (CWC), and single-cone (SC; CeraSeal only). The five arms were CLC-CS, CWC-CS, SC-CS, CLC-AH, and CWC-AH. Post-obturation pain (VAS 0–10) was recorded at 6, 12, 24, 48, and 72 h, and sealer extrusion was scored present/absent on standardized periapical radiographs by two blinded calibrated examiners. Overall pain was low (0–1.4). Obturation technique by itself did not drive pain, but AH Plus produced significantly more pain than CeraSeal — an effect concentrated under heated continuous-wave compaction. Sealer-extrusion incidence did not differ between arms, but when extrusion occurred it independently increased pain. This page seeds the wiki's first Ceraseal/bioceramic-sealer cluster.

## Key Contributions

- First clinical head-to-head of **CeraSeal vs AH Plus** in the wiki for post-obturation pain and sealer extrusion, with obturation technique crossed in.
- Shows **sealer chemistry, not obturation technique, drives the pain difference**: pooled technique p=0.124 (NS) vs pooled sealer p<0.001 (AH Plus higher).
- Localizes the AH Plus penalty to **continuous-wave (heated) compaction**, consistent with heat degrading AH Plus amine setting initiators.
- Demonstrates that **sealer extrusion raises pain regardless of sealer type** (p<0.001), while extrusion incidence itself is similar across sealers/techniques.

## Methodology

- **Design**: prospective RCT, 5 arms × 30 (n=150); sealed-envelope concealment; operator blinded to allocation until after biomechanical preparation. Power analysis: ≥27/group.
- **Patients**: ASA I–II, 18–60 y, mandibular first molar, asymptomatic irreversible pulpitis with three separate canals, PAI ≤ 2, periodontally healthy.
- **Treatment (standardized, single endodontist)**: 4% articaine IANB; ProTaper Next (mesial X2 25/.06, distal X3 30/.06); 5.25% NaOCl + 17% EDTA (60 s) + saline; AH Plus → canals dried, CeraSeal → canals kept slightly wet. CWC via System B (200 °C) + Obtura III (160 °C); Fuji IX GP temporary seal.
- **Outcomes**: VAS pain at 6/12/24/48/72 h (SMS reminders); sealer extrusion (beyond radiographic apex) by two blinded endodontists (Cohen kappa > 0.8); >2 mm extrusion excluded.
- **Stats**: Kruskal-Wallis + Dunn (intergroup), Friedman + Nemenyi (intragroup), chi-square (extrusion), FDR-adjusted, R 4.4.2.

## Results

- **Pain magnitude**: 0–1.4 (no pain to mild) in all arms; no emergency visits.
- **Intergroup pain**: NS at 6 h (p=0.068), 24 h (p=0.171), 72 h (p=0.068). Significant at **12 h** (p=0.018; CWC-CS lowest, CWC-AH highest) and **48 h** (p=0.015; CLC-CS lowest, CWC-AH highest).
- **Intragroup**: pain peaked at 6 h, fell significantly in all groups (p<0.001), ~0 by 72 h; decline began earliest for CWC-CS (after 6 h), latest for CLC-CS (after 24 h).
- **Obturation technique (pooled)**: NS for pain (CLC 0.56, CWC 0.65, SC 0.54; p=0.124).
- **Sealer type (pooled)**: AH Plus 0.69 vs CeraSeal 0.53, **p<0.001**.
- **Interaction**: with CeraSeal, technique irrelevant (p=0.890); with AH Plus, CWC > CLC (p=0.010). Under CWC, AH Plus > CeraSeal (p<0.001).
- **Sealer extrusion**: intergroup NS (p=0.499); SC-CS 60% (highest), CLC-CS 40% (lowest), CWC 50%. By technique p=0.186; by sealer p=0.548 (CeraSeal 50%, AH Plus 45%).
- **Extrusion → pain**: extrusion raised pain overall (p<0.001) and within each sealer (CeraSeal p=0.004, AH Plus p=0.030).
- **Gender**: females higher pain than males (0.75 vs 0.36; p<0.001).
- **Bottom line**: regardless of sealer, obturation technique did not affect pain; regardless of technique, AH Plus caused more pain than CeraSeal; sealer extrusion increased pain.
