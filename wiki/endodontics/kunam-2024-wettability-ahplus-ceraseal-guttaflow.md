## One-line Summary

In vitro contact-angle study (Ossila goniometer) on 60 NaOCl/EDTA/CHX-treated human mandibular premolar root-dentin halves (n=20/group): AH Plus wet dentin best (47.40°) < Ceraseal (61.64°) < Guttaflow bioseal (95.64°, nonwetting); all pairwise differences highly significant (P<0.001).

## Summary

This in vitro goniometry study measured how well three root canal sealers wet human radicular dentin, using contact angle as the index of wettability (lower angle = better wetting and adaptation). Sixty root-dentin halves from 30 single-rooted mandibular premolars were treated with a 3% NaOCl + 17% EDTA + 2% CHX irrigation protocol, then a 0.1 mL droplet of each sealer was dispensed and its contact angle read 5 min later on an Ossila goniometer.

AH Plus (epoxy resin) had the lowest contact angle (mean 47.40°) and thus the best wettability, followed by Ceraseal (calcium-silicate bioceramic, 61.64°), with Guttaflow bioseal (silicone, 95.64°) the worst. Because Guttaflow bioseal exceeded 90°, it was classified as nonwetting on the treated dentin, whereas AH Plus and Ceraseal were wetting (<90°). All three sealers differed from each other with high statistical significance (ANOVA F=537.74, P<0.001; all Tukey pairwise P<0.001). The authors attribute AH Plus's advantage to penetration of dentin microirregularities and covalent bonding to collagen amino groups, Ceraseal's intermediate behavior to small grain size and water affinity letting it creep into lateral micro-canals, and Guttaflow's poor wetting to high silicone surface tension and hydrophobic gutta-percha.

This page seeds the wiki's first Ceraseal / calcium-silicate-bioceramic sealer cluster, providing the bench interface-property data (wettability) that complements clinical Ceraseal outcome evidence such as [[endodontics/spinelli-2024-three-year-single-cone-ceraseal-cohort]].

## Key Contributions

- First reported wettability comparison of Ceraseal and Guttaflow bioseal vs AH Plus on human root dentin.
- Three-tier ranking by sealer chemistry: epoxy resin (AH Plus) > bioceramic (Ceraseal) > silicone (Guttaflow bioseal).
- Identifies Guttaflow bioseal as nonwetting (>90°) on irrigant-treated radicular dentin.

## Methodology

- 30 single-rooted human mandibular premolars (orthodontic extractions) decoronated, apically resected, split buccolingually → 60 root-dentin halves; inner dentin polished with Sof-Lex disks; mounted on cold-cure resin blocks.
- Uniform surface treatment for all specimens: 10 mL 3% NaOCl (5 min) + 10 mL 17% EDTA (5 min) + 2% CHX (10 min); blot-dried.
- Randomized n=20/group: AH Plus, Ceraseal, Guttaflow bioseal.
- 0.1 mL sealer dispensed by micropipette; contact angle read 5 min later on Ossila goniometer.
- One-way ANOVA + Tukey post hoc; P<0.05.

## Results

| Group | Sealer (type) | Mean contact angle (°) | SD |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | AH Plus (epoxy resin) | 47.40 | 5.73 |
| 2 | Ceraseal (Ca-silicate bioceramic) | 61.64 | 4.53 |
| 3 | Guttaflow bioseal (silicone) | 95.64 | 3.90 |

- ANOVA F=537.74, P<0.001.
- Tukey pairwise mean differences (all P<0.001): AH Plus vs Ceraseal −14.24°; AH Plus vs Guttaflow bioseal −48.24°; Ceraseal vs Guttaflow bioseal −34.01°.
- Wetting (<90°): AH Plus, Ceraseal. Nonwetting (>90°): Guttaflow bioseal.
- Conclusion: AH Plus had the lowest contact angle and best wettability on root canal dentin; Ceraseal intermediate; Guttaflow bioseal poorest.

Limitations: single 5-min time point, no load applied, irrigant-technique effects not isolated, bench interface property only (no in-vivo seal/bond/outcome).
