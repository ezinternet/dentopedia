## One-line Summary
Ex-vivo CBCT study (n=51 mandibular molar MB canals, 25–45° curvature): manual SS K-file produces significantly larger canal transportation at 3- and 9-mm levels than PathFile or Scout RaCe rotary systems; the two rotary systems are equivalent.

## 한줄요약
Ex-vivo extracted mandibular molar mesiobuccal canals (n=51, 25-45° curvature) CBCT: manual SS K-file produces significantly larger transportation at 3- and 9-mm levels than PathFile or Scout RaCe rotary systems; the two rotary systems are equivalent.

## Summary
Pre/post CBCT comparison of three glide-path techniques in moderately to severely curved mesiobuccal canals of mandibular molars. Manual stainless-steel K-files caused more apical and coronal transportation than either PathFile or Scout RaCe rotary systems, while the two rotary systems performed equivalently.

## Key Contributions
- Natural-dentin substrate (not resin) — generalizable to clinical canals
- Three-level (3-, 6-, 9-mm) transportation quantification
- Practical implication: rotary glide path preferable over manual for curved canals

## Methodology
- Design: in vitro CBCT pre/post
- Specimens: 51 extracted mandibular 1st/2nd molars with 25-45° MB root curvature
- Three groups (n=17): SS K-file manual, PathFile rotary, Scout RaCe rotary
- Statistics: Kruskal-Wallis between groups, Friedman within groups; α=0.05

## Results
- Manual SS K-file: significantly higher transportation at 3 mm and 9 mm from apex (p<0.05)
- PathFile vs Scout RaCe: not statistically different

## Limitations
- Modest sample (n=51 across 3 groups)
- CBCT voxel resolution lower than micro-CT
- Operator standardization not detailed
- Single root, single curvature range
