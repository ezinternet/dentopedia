## One-line Summary
In vitro micro-CT study comparing saline, 17% EDTA solution, and RC-Prep during rotary NiTi instrumentation: EDTA 17% did not increase canal transportation vs saline, RC-Prep produced significantly less transportation, and EDTA 17% increased canal volume the most.

## 한줄요약
In vitro micro-CT: 식염수·17% EDTA·RC-Prep 비교 — EDTA는 transportation 증가 없음, RC-Prep는 유의하게 적은 transportation, EDTA는 canal volume 최대 증가.

## Summary
Using x-ray micro-computed tomography and digital modeling, this study assessed how chelating-agent vehicles influence canal geometry during rotary NiTi instrumentation. A 17% EDTA solution did not cause more canal transportation than saline irrigation alone, while RC-Prep paste resulted in significantly less transportation than either saline or EDTA. Canal volume increased significantly more when EDTA 17% solution was used. The overall message is that chelating agents during instrumentation do not increase apical transportation — and a chelating paste may even reduce it — informing lubricant/chelator choice during shaping.

## Key Contributions
- Quantifies chelator-vehicle effect on transportation, working length, and canal volume.
- Demonstrates chelating agents do not worsen apical transportation.

## Methodology
In vitro micro-CT + digital modeling; rotary NiTi files with saline, 17% EDTA solution, or RC-Prep; outcomes: transportation, working length, canal volume.

## Results
EDTA 17% transportation = saline; RC-Prep significantly less transportation; EDTA 17% greatest canal-volume increase; chelators did not increase apical transportation.
