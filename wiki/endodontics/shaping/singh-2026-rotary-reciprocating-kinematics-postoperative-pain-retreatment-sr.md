## One-line Summary

Systematic review (PRISMA 2020; qualitative synthesis, no MA) of 5 RCTs (n=554) finding rotary and reciprocating NiTi systems produce comparable postoperative pain after nonsurgical endodontic retreatment; pain is mild, peaks at 24 h, and declines over time without consistent dependence on kinematics.

## Summary

This systematic review (registered PROSPERO CRD420261325110, conducted per PRISMA 2020) is the first to **exclusively** evaluate whether rotary vs reciprocating nickel–titanium kinematics influence postoperative pain in **nonsurgical endodontic retreatment** — a scenario distinct from primary treatment because of filling-material removal and increased apical debris-extrusion risk. Five RCTs (554 participants; 1 Brazil, 4 Türkiye) met inclusion. Because of heterogeneity in pain scales (VAS, NRS, verbal rating, 4-point verbal), follow-up timing, and reporting, only a qualitative synthesis was possible. The bottom line: rotary and reciprocating systems produce **comparable** postoperative pain. Pain peaks within the first 24 h and declines to minimal by 7–14 days regardless of technique, and analgesic (ibuprofen/NSAID) intake is low and comparable. GRADE certainty is **low to very low** (few trials, heterogeneity).

## Key Contributions

- First retreatment-specific SR on instrumentation kinematics and postoperative pain; explicitly argues primary-treatment evidence cannot be extrapolated to retreatment.
- Concludes neither rotary nor reciprocating is definitively superior for postoperative pain after retreatment.
- Reframes postoperative pain as multifactorial (preoperative status, debris extrusion, apical irritation, operator technique, working length, irrigation, tooth anatomy) — kinematics is only one component; in-vitro extrusion differences do not consistently translate to clinical pain.

## Methodology

- **PICO**: nonsurgical endodontic retreatment of permanent teeth; reciprocating vs rotary NiTi; incidence and intensity of postoperative pain.
- **Databases**: PubMed/MEDLINE, Scopus, Embase, Web of Science (Feb 2016 – Feb 2026). 194 records → 78 duplicates removed → 116 screened → 8 full texts → **5 RCTs**.
- **Inclusion**: human RCTs comparing reciprocating vs rotary NiTi in nonsurgical retreatment with clear pain assessment; excluded follow-up <48 h, non-English, lab/animal/case-report/review.
- **Risk of bias**: Modified Cochrane RoB-2 (2 low; 3 "some concerns," mainly infeasible participant/operator blinding). **GRADE**: low to very low certainty.
- Synthesis qualitative (no meta-analysis) because of clinical/methodological heterogeneity.

## Results

- **Systems compared** — Rotary: Mtwo Retreatment, ProTaper Universal Retreatment, Hyflex EDM, One Shape, Revo-S, One Curve Mini. Reciprocating: Reciproc, Reciproc Blue, WaveOne Gold, WaveOne, One RECI. One trial also included hand SS files.
- **Protocols**: apex locator + radiograph for working length; NaOCl (mostly 2.5%, one 5.25%) + 17% EDTA final; mostly single-visit (one two-visit with Ca(OH)₂).
- **Rotary vs reciprocating**: most trials → **no statistically significant difference** in pain incidence/intensity at 24, 48, 72 h. Eyüboğlu & Özcan → **higher early pain with reciprocating (WaveOne)** but **not significant (P>0.05) at later follow-up**. Hand-vs-rotary-vs-reciprocating RCT → **hand files significantly more painful at first 48 h (P<0.05)**; rotary vs reciprocating not significant (P>0.05).
- **Time course**: pain highest at 24 h, declines to minimal by 7–14 days, independent of technique.
- **Analgesics**: low and comparable across kinematics; no significant difference in ibuprofen consumption (Comparin et al.; Çanakçi et al.).
- **Clinical implication**: instrument selection should be driven by canal anatomy, filling-removal efficiency, operator familiarity, and treatment objectives rather than expected pain differences.
