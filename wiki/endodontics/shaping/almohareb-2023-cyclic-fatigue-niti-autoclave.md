## One-line Summary
In vitro fatigue trial (n=60, three heat-treated NiTi systems × four sterilization conditions): EdgeTaper Platinum showed the highest cyclic fatigue resistance overall; only EdgeTaper Platinum showed a significant increase in fatigue resistance after 1 and 10 autoclave cycles, while ProTaper Gold and TruNatomy were unaffected by sterilization.

## Summary
Three heat-treated NiTi rotary systems (EdgeTaper Platinum 25/.06, ProTaper Gold 25/.08, TruNatomy Prime 26/.04) were tested for cyclic fatigue across 0, 1, 5, and 10 autoclave cycles. EdgeTaper Platinum outperformed both ProTaper Gold and TruNatomy, and uniquely benefited from repeated sterilization. ProTaper Gold and TruNatomy were sterilization-neutral. Provides direct evidence for clinic-level reuse and sterilization SOPs.

## Key Contributions
- Direct comparison of three current heat-treated systems under identical sterilization protocols
- Quantifies sterilization-induced strengthening unique to EdgeTaper Platinum
- Practical reuse decision input: PTG and TruNatomy not weakened by 1-10 autoclave cycles

## Methodology
- Design: in vitro
- Sample: 60 files (20 per type) split into 4 subgroups (control, 1, 5, 10 autoclave cycles)
- Test: metal canal block, rotation per manufacturer instruction until fracture
- Outcomes: number of rotations to failure, fractured segment length, SEM of fracture surface
- Statistics: Kruskal-Wallis, Dunn-Bonferroni; α=0.05

## Results
- EdgeTaper Platinum > ProTaper Gold ≈ TruNatomy across all conditions; after 5 cycles, PTG-TN-ETP separation significant (p=0.014)
- EdgeTaper Platinum after 1 and 10 cycles: significantly more rotations to failure than non-sterilized (p=0.039 and 0.021)
- Fractured segment of ETP after sterilization also longer (p=0.010)
- ProTaper Gold and TruNatomy: sterilization-neutral (NS)

## Limitations
- Single canal geometry
- Cycles 0/1/5/10 only — 2-4 cycle behavior unobserved
- No correlation to in vivo fracture rate
