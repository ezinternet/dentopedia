## One-line Summary
In vitro fatigue trial (n=240, ProTaper Universal/Next/Gold × four sterilization protocols): ProTaper Gold significantly outperforms Universal and Next in baseline cyclic fatigue; 10 autoclave cycles before testing increase NCF for PTN and PTG, demonstrating sterilization-driven alloy strengthening.

## 한줄요약
In vitro fatigue trial (n=240, ProTaper Universal/Next/Gold × four sterilization protocols): ProTaper Gold significantly outperforms Universal and Next in baseline cyclic fatigue; 10 autoclave cycles before testing increase NCF for PTN and PTG, demonstrating sterilization-driven alloy strengthening.

## Summary
Head-to-head comparison of three ProTaper generations under four sterilization protocols. ProTaper Gold (gold-wire) was the most fatigue-resistant out of the box. Autoclaving (10 cycles) before testing significantly raised the number of cycles to failure for both PTN and PTG. When files were pre-fatigued and then autoclaved, PTN still gained NCF compared to its non-sterilized counterpart.

## Key Contributions
- Within-brand generational comparison (Universal → Next → Gold)
- Quantifies the strengthening effect of autoclaving on heat-treated NiTi
- Tests sterilization timing (before use vs after partial use)

## Methodology
- Design: in vitro
- Sample: 240 files (80 per type) × 4 subgroups × 20
- Subgroups: (1) as received, (2) 10× pre-test autoclave, (3) 25%/50%/75% NCF use + autoclave then fatigue test, (4) same use without autoclave
- Statistics: one-way ANOVA + Tukey post hoc; α=0.05

## Results
- Baseline (G1): PTG > PTU and PTN (p<0.05)
- 10× pre-autoclave (G2): NCF significantly increased for PTN and PTG (p<0.05)
- Partial use then autoclave (G3) vs no autoclave (G4): PTN G3 > G4 (p<0.05)
- PTG G2 > G3 and G4 (p<0.05): autoclaving a fresh file is better than autoclaving a partially used file

## Limitations
- Single canal geometry
- No correlation with clinical fracture rate
- ProTaper-only — other heat-treated systems out of scope
