## One-line Summary
In vitro S-shaped resin block trial (n=100, 5 groups × 20): manual K-file produces significantly more canal transportation than any rotary system in apical, middle, and coronal thirds; four rotary glide-path systems (Scout RaCe, One G, PathFile, GPS) are statistically equivalent.

## 한줄요약
In vitro S-shaped resin block trial (n=100, 5 groups × 20): manual K-file produces significantly more transportation than any rotary system in apical, middle, and coronal thirds; four rotary glide-path systems (Scout RaCe, One G, PathFile, GPS) are statistically equivalent.

## Summary
Five-arm comparison of glide-path systems in S-shaped blocks: four rotary NiTi systems behaved equivalently in producing canal transportation, while manual stainless-steel K-files caused significantly more deviation, particularly in the apical and middle thirds. Reinforces the rotary-over-manual conclusion of Aflaki 2020 with a broader system comparison.

## Key Contributions
- Five-system head-to-head transportation comparison
- Confirms manual K-file inferiority across all canal thirds
- Demonstrates homogeneity among four current rotary glide-path systems

## Methodology
- Design: in vitro
- Sample: 100 S-shaped resin blocks (5 × 20)
- Groups: Scout RaCe, One G, PathFile, GPS, K-file (control)
- Procedure: pre/post photo superimposition (Photoshop CC 2019); 10 cross-section transportation measurements (Digimizer)
- Statistics: one-way ANOVA, repeated-measures ANOVA; α=0.05

## Results
- Manual K-file: significantly higher transportation in apical and middle thirds (p<0.001)
- Coronal third: K-file > Scout RaCe (p<0.05)
- Four rotary systems: not statistically different from each other

## Limitations
- Resin substrate (not dentin)
- Single operator
- No biological or time-cost outcome
