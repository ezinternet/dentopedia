## 한줄요약

## Summary
Three nickel-titanium shaping systems were compared in 60 S-shaped resin canals. The minimally-tapered TruNatomy showed the lowest canal transportation in the apical region and the shortest preparation time, while ProTaper GOLD removed the most resin and produced the largest apical transportation. Findings support TruNatomy as a more conservative option for S-shaped or otherwise complex canals.

## Key Contributions
- Same-block, identical-protocol comparison of three NiTi systems
- Quantitative apical-curve transportation data favoring TruNatomy
- Shaping time and resin-removal data for chair-side workflow planning

## Methodology
- Design: in vitro experimental
- Sample: 60 S-shaped resin blocks, 3 groups × 20
- Procedure: pre/post-staining superimposition, transportation measured at 1-mm increments from apex up to 9 mm
- Statistics: paired t-test within group, one-way ANOVA and Kruskal-Wallis between groups, α=0.05

## Results
- **TruNatomy**: significantly less canal transportation at 3- and 5-mm levels (p<0.05)
- **ProTaper GOLD**: largest transportation in apical curve (2-3 mm levels, p<0.05)
- Resin removal: TruNatomy < WaveOne GOLD < ProTaper GOLD across all sections (p<0.05)
- Shaping time: TruNatomy < WaveOne GOLD < ProTaper GOLD (p<0.05)

## Limitations
- Resin blocks lack dentin hardness/microhardness
- Operator skill not reported
- Single canal geometry (S-shape)
- No clinical endpoints (success, pain, healing)
