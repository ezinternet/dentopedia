## One-line Summary
Ex-vivo study using E. faecalis: all three glide-path techniques (manual K-file, One G continuous rotary, WaveOne Gold Glider reciprocation) extrude measurable amounts of bacteria apically; provides biological-extrusion evidence complementing geometric-transportation studies.

## 한줄요약
Ex-vivo extracted-tooth study using E. faecalis: all three glide-path techniques (manual K-file, One G continuous rotary, WaveOne Gold Glider reciprocation) extrude measurable amounts of bacteria apically; biological-extrusion-side evidence complementing geometric-transportation studies.

## Summary
Comparative ex-vivo study quantifying apical bacterial extrusion after glide path preparation with three different kinematics: manual stainless-steel K-file, continuous-rotary One G, and reciprocating WaveOne Gold Glider. All techniques produced measurable apical extrusion of E. faecalis, with relevance to flare-up risk and post-operative pain.

## Key Contributions
- Quantifies the biological cost (apical bacterial extrusion) of the glide-path stage
- Compares three different file kinematics (manual vs continuous rotary vs reciprocation)
- Complements geometric transportation studies that ignore the biological dimension

## Methodology
- Design: ex-vivo experimental
- Specimens: single-rooted extracted teeth inoculated with E. faecalis
- Three glide-path groups (manual K, One G, WaveOne Gold Glider)
- Outcome: number of bacteria extruded beyond the apical foramen

## Results
- All instrumentation techniques produced measurable apical extrusion
- Specific between-group values and significance: see PDF tables

## Limitations
- Single bacterial species (E. faecalis) — multi-species biofilm not modeled
- Single-rooted teeth; multi-rooted molars not represented
- No in vivo flare-up endpoint
