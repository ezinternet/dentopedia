## Summary

The single-cone technique's recognized weakness is its reliance on a sealer-dominant fill: more sealer, more potential **voids/porosity**, especially apically. This meta-analysis of laboratory studies asks whether **ultrasonic activation (UA)** of the sealer — energizing it with an ultrasonic tip before/after cone placement — improves fill quality. The pooled answer is **yes for porosity and penetration, with caveats**: UA significantly lowers porosity on micro-CT and at the apical/coronal thirds by stereomicroscopy, and significantly increases sealer penetration into dentinal tubules at the apical and middle thirds (CLSM). However, **push-out bond strength and some penetration indicators are inconsistent**, so the benefit is real for density/adaptation but not yet shown to improve mechanical interface strength.

This sits one layer below the clinical papers in the single-cone cluster: it is a **technique-modifier** that could narrow the lab-quality gap between single-cone and warm/compaction techniques, but it carries no clinical-outcome evidence.

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies UA vs non-activation (NA) on single-cone **fill quality** across porosity, penetration, and bond strength.
- Consistent **porosity reduction** (micro-CT + apical/coronal stereomicroscopy) and **deeper sealer penetration** (CLSM apical/middle).
- Honest about **inconsistency** in push-out bond strength and other penetration metrics.

## Methodology

- 6 databases; 20 studies included, 11 meta-analyzed; quality assessment performed.
- Comparison: UA vs NA of root canal sealers (calcium-silicate + resin-based) in single-cone obturation.
- Endpoints: porosity (micro-CT, stereomicroscopy by canal third), sealer penetration % (CLSM), push-out bond strength.

## Results

| Endpoint | Effect (UA vs NA) | 95% CI | I² |
|---|---|---|---|
| Porosity — micro-CT | MD −1.21 | −1.69 to −0.74 | 28% |
| Porosity — stereo coronal | MD −0.05 | −0.82 to −0.18 | 0% |
| Porosity — stereo middle | MD −0.44 | −1.56 to 0.68 | 89% |
| Porosity — stereo apical | MD −0.50 | −0.78 to −0.21 | 0% |
| Penetration — CLSM apical | SMD 2.28 | 1.17 to 3.40 | 71% |
| Penetration — CLSM middle | SMD 2.69 | 1.16 to 4.22 | 87% |

- Other penetration indicators + push-out bond strength: **inconsistent**.
- **Limitations**: lab-only (no clinical outcomes); high heterogeneity at the middle third and CLSM endpoints; bond-strength variability unexplained.
