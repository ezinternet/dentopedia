## One-line Summary
ESE-commissioned systematic review (28 studies) concluding that the effectiveness of clinically diagnosing pulpitis is low because no valid reference standard exists; a multi-test "diagnostic package" is the best current approach.

## Summary
This European Society of Endodontology (ESE)-commissioned systematic review evaluated how well clinicians can diagnose the true inflammatory status of the pulp, the basis for the ESE S3-level guideline. Across 28 eligible studies covering sensibility tests, vitality tests, biomarkers, and pulp bleeding, the review found that diagnostic effectiveness is low — chiefly because there is no clinical reference standard for the "true" pulp condition, so no single test can be validated against ground truth. The authors conclude that the most defensible current strategy is a combination of clinical tests and symptoms (a "diagnostic package"), with molecular/biomarker diagnostics as a future direction. The practical implication is that preoperative prediction of vital-pulp-treatment success remains weak.

## Key Contributions
- Establishes the reference-standard gap as the core barrier to pulpitis diagnosis.
- Rates overall diagnostic effectiveness as low on current evidence.
- Recommends a combined multi-test "diagnostic package" over reliance on any single test.
- Serves as the evidence base for ESE S3 pulpitis-diagnosis guidelines.

## Methodology
Systematic review built on three a priori ESE-agreed PICO questions (sensibility testing, biomarkers, pulp bleeding). PubMed, Web of Science, Scopus, and Google Scholar searched. 28 studies (from 29 publications) included: 12 pulp-vitality accuracy, 10 pulpal-condition accuracy, 6 biomarker, 3 prognostic/therapeutic. PROSPERO CRD42021265366. Heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis.

## Results
Diagnostic effectiveness for pulpitis judged low. No reliable preoperative indicator of true pulp status or of vital-pulp-treatment prognosis was identified. Best-supported approach: combine clinical tests with symptom history; molecular diagnosis flagged as a future requirement.

## Clinical Application
- Treat a single test (cold/EPT) as a weak signal, not a verdict — corroborate with history, percussion, radiographs.
- Because preoperative pulp-status prediction is unreliable, vital pulp therapy decisions ([[endodontics/vpt]]) carry irreducible diagnostic uncertainty; consent and follow-up should reflect that.
