## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional study (n=40 retreatment teeth with apical periodontitis): LPS (mean 5.1 EU/mL) and LTA (mean 537.5 pg/mL) found in all canals; higher LPS correlated with larger periapical radiolucency and clinical signs/symptoms, and defective coronal restoration raised virulence-factor load.

## Summary

In persistent/secondary endodontic infection (the substrate of retreatment), the host response and lesion are driven not only by surviving microbes but by their virulence byproducts. This UNICAMP cross-sectional study quantified both the Gram-negative endotoxin LPS and the Gram-positive cell-wall LTA in 40 root-filled teeth with radiographic apical periodontitis, under rigorous endotoxin-free sampling. Both molecules were present in every sample. Critically, LPS load tracked with the *clinical phenotype*: larger periapical radiolucency and the presence of pain/symptoms. Restoration quality mattered — defective coronal restorations were associated with higher intracanal virulence-factor burden, reinforcing the coronal-seal principle for endodontic prognosis.

## Key Contributions

- Simultaneous LPS (LAL assay) + LTA (ELISA) quantification in the same secondary-infection canals.
- Endotoxin (LPS) load correlated with periapical bone-resorption area and clinical signs/symptoms.
- Coronal restoration defect → higher virulence-factor load (coronal-seal relevance).

## Methodology

- 40 retreatment patients, all with radiographic apical periodontitis + defective filling/restoration and/or persistent symptoms.
- Gamma-sterilised instruments, rubber dam, H2O2 + NaOCl crown disinfection (thiosulphate-inactivated), culture+PCR sterility gate, LPS-free water.
- LPS by Limulus amebocyte lysate (EU/mL); LTA by ELISA (pg/mL); periapical area by ImageJ (mean of 3 readings).
- STROBE-reported cross-sectional design.

## Results

- LPS + LTA in 100% of samples.
- Mean LPS 5.1 ± 2.83 EU/mL; mean LTA 537.53 ± 134.14 pg/mL.
- Higher LPS ↔ larger periapical radiolucency + presence of clinical signs/symptoms.
- Defective coronal restoration ↔ higher LPS/LTA load.
