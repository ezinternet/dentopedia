## One-line Summary

Retrospective cohort (53 RCTs, 48 patients, NeoSealer Flo) — single-cone vs carrier-based had equivalent 36-mo healing (93% vs 88%, p>0.05) but carrier-based showed significantly more apical sealer extrusion (75% vs 48.2%); extrusion did not compromise healing.

## Summary

The Bologna group (Prati/Zamparini/Spinelli) extends its single-cone Ceraseal work to a different premixed calcium-silicate sealer, **NeoSealer Flo**, and directly compares the **cold single-cone** technique against a **warm carrier-based (Thermafil)** technique at **36 months** — the first clinical data on NeoSealer Flo in a carrier-based workflow. The clinical headline is **technique-independence of healing**: 93% (single-cone) vs 88% (carrier-based), not significantly different, in line with the 85–91% benchmarks for premixed bioceramic single-cone fills.

The discriminating finding is **apical extrusion**: carrier-based extruded sealer in 75% of teeth vs 48.2% for single-cone, with significantly larger extrusion in carrier-based fills **and** in teeth with preoperative periapical lesions (PAI ≥3). Crucially, extrusion did **not** translate into worse healing — extruded calcium-silicate sealer was mostly radiographically stable and clinically silent, consistent with its bioactive, low-inflammatory behavior. Practical takeaway: both techniques are clinically acceptable with NeoSealer Flo; the warm carrier-based route buys denser fill at the cost of more (apparently benign) extrusion, and pre-existing apical lesions are the main extrusion driver regardless of technique.

## Key Contributions

- First clinical evaluation of **NeoSealer Flo with a carrier-based technique** (manufacturer indicates warm + cold single-cone only).
- **Same-sealer** head-to-head: healing equivalent, extrusion frequency/extent differs by technique.
- Longest NeoSealer Flo cohort (36 mo) with a **digital extrusion-measurement** methodology; postgraduate operators → no intraoperative complications (rapid-learning-curve techniques validated for teaching settings).

## Methodology

- Retrospective cohort (STROBE); 48 patients / 53 RCTs treated 2020–2022; 9 postgraduate master operators each trained on ONE technique, preference-allocated, tutor-supervised.
- Shaping: NiTi rotary (primary) / reciprocating (retreatment); irrigation 5% NaOCl + 10% EDTA, final sterile water.
- Obturation: NeoSealer Flo + Thermafil (24) **or** cold single-cone (29); sealer to WL−3 mm; restored with Clearfil SE Bond + flowable composite.
- Outcome: PAI ≤2 + no signs/symptoms = healthy; PAI ≥3 / symptoms / retreatment = lesion. Two blinded calibrated examiners (κ=0.90). Chi-square; extrusion measured digitally (BlueSkyPlan).

## Results

- **Healing**: single-cone 27/29 = 93% (95% CI 78.0–98.1); carrier-based 21/24 = 87.5% (95% CI 69.0–95.7); absolute difference 5.6% (95% CI −10.5 to 21.7); not significant.
- **Apical extrusion**: carrier-based 18/24 (75%) vs single-cone 14/29 (48.2%); mean size 1.49 mm. Significantly larger extrusion with **carrier-based technique** and **preoperative PAI >3**; larger apical diameters trended higher (NS).
- **Radiographic modification** of extruded sealer: ~9.4% of cases (5/53; 3 single-cone, 2 carrier-based); majority stable. Extrusion not associated with failure.
- Benchmarks: 88.6% (24 mo carrier-based + Ceraseal), 85.4% (3 y single-cone + Ceraseal, Spinelli), 90.9% (3 y single-cone + EndoSequence), 89.6% (4 y single-cone + BioRoot RCS).
- Thermal concern addressed: in-canal temperature rise with Thermafil <1 °C; 37–77 °C does not alter CaSi flow/setting.
- **Null hypotheses**: H1 (no healing difference) not rejected; H2 (technique no effect on extrusion) partially rejected.
- **Limitations**: retrospective, small n, few failures (no multivariable analysis), operator-preference allocation. Calls for prospective/RCT confirmation.
