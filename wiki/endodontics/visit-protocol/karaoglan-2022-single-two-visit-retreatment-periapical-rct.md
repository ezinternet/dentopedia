## One-line Summary

RCT (n=100, single-rooted asymptomatic retreatment teeth with periapical lesions, 24-mo follow-up): single-visit (CHX final irrigation) vs two-visit (Ca(OH)₂ dressing) root canal retreatment showed no significant difference in healing (88.6% vs 86.7% healed, p>.05); lesion size and pre-op filling length affected outcome.

## Summary

A randomized clinical trial comparing single-visit versus two-visit non-surgical root canal *retreatment* in 100 single-rooted, asymptomatic teeth with periapical lesions, all retreated by one operator and followed for 24 months. The single-visit arm used 2% chlorhexidine as the final irrigant before immediate obturation; the two-visit arm used a calcium hydroxide inter-appointment dressing. At 24 months (89% recall), healing was equivalent between groups with no significant difference, while periapical lesion size and pre-operative root-filling length significantly affected outcome. The study extends the single- vs multiple-visit equivalence finding from primary treatment into the retreatment setting.

## Key Contributions

- Provides RCT-level evidence that single-visit retreatment matches two-visit retreatment for 24-month healing in asymptomatic teeth with periapical lesions.
- Supports a single-visit CHX final-irrigation protocol as an alternative to two-visit Ca(OH)₂ dressing in retreatment.
- Identifies lesion size and pre-operative root-filling length as significant prognostic factors (independent of visit number).

## Methodology

- RCT; 100 root-canal-treated, single-rooted, asymptomatic teeth with periapical lesions; single operator; randomized 1:1 (single-visit n=50 / two-visit n=50).
- Both arms: existing filling removed, re-instrumented by step-back with hand files, irrigated with 2.5% NaOCl + 5% EDTA, obturated by lateral compaction.
- Single-visit: 5 mL 2% CHX final irrigation, immediate obturation. Two-visit: Ca(OH)₂ inter-appointment dressing, obturated at visit 2.
- 24-month follow-up; recall 89/100 (89%). Success = healed + healing. Chi-square / Fisher's exact test.

## Results

At 24 months:

| Outcome | Single-visit | Two-visit |
|---|---|---|
| Healed | 39 (88.6%) | 39 (86.7%) |
| Healing | 3 (6.8%) | 2 (4.4%) |
| Failed | 2 (4.5%) | 4 (8.9%) |

- No statistically significant difference in success between groups (p>.05).
- Periapical lesion size and pre-operative root-filling length significantly affected outcome (p<.05).
- Caveats: single-rooted asymptomatic teeth only; single operator/center; CHX-vs-Ca(OH)₂ is confounded with visit number.
