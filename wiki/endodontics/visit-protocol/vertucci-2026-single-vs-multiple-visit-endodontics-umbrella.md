## One-line Summary

Umbrella SR of 12 systematic reviews (search to Dec 2025): no consistent or clinically important difference in post-operative pain between single-visit and multiple-visit root canal treatment; visit-strategy choice should be driven by case complexity, infection control, and patient preference.

## Summary

This umbrella systematic review by Gupta et al. (2026, *J Dent*) synthesized 12 systematic reviews comparing post-operative pain after single-visit versus multiple-visit root canal treatment (RCT) in permanent teeth. Methodological quality was assessed with AMSTAR 2 and risk of bias with ROBIS; overlap of primary studies across SRs was also examined.

The higher-confidence reviews consistently found no clinically important difference in post-operative pain intensity (measured by VAS/NRS) or pain incidence between the two approaches. Flare-up definitions and reporting were inconsistent across reviews, limiting conclusions on that outcome. Overall confidence in the synthesized evidence is constrained by heterogeneity in outcome definitions, measurement tools, and follow-up periods, compounded by substantial primary-study overlap across the included SRs.

The clinical bottom line: adult patients requiring RCT on permanent teeth can be treated in either a single or multiple visit without expecting a meaningful pain difference. The choice should be guided by case complexity, infection control needs, patient preference, and logistical considerations.

## Key Contributions

- Highest-level synthesis on this question: 12 SRs pooled under one umbrella review
- Confirmed absence of clinically important post-operative pain difference (higher-confidence SRs)
- Identified inconsistent flare-up definitions as a major evidence gap
- Applied AMSTAR 2 + ROBIS dual appraisal framework and accounted for primary-study redundancy
- Calls for standardized pain outcomes and transparent overlap reporting in future syntheses

## Methodology

- **Design**: Umbrella systematic review (SRs ± meta-analysis eligible)
- **Search**: Major bibliographic databases + grey literature, inception to December 2025
- **Inclusion**: SRs comparing single-visit vs multiple-visit RCT reporting post-endodontic pain
- **Appraisal tools**: AMSTAR 2 (methodological quality), ROBIS (risk of bias)
- **Overlap**: Primary-study overlap across SRs examined
- **Outcomes**: Pain intensity (VAS/NRS), pain incidence, flare-up rate
- **Note**: abstract-only — full text not retrieved; detailed SR-level results and subgroup data not available

## Results

| Outcome | Finding |
|---|---|
| Post-operative pain intensity (VAS/NRS) | No consistent clinically important difference — single vs multiple visit |
| Pain incidence | No clinically important difference (less often reported) |
| Flare-up rate | Inconsistently reported; variable definitions; no firm conclusion |
| Confidence level | Limited by heterogeneity, outcome variability, and primary-study overlap |
| Included SRs | n = 12 |
| Search horizon | Inception to December 2025 |
