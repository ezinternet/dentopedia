## Summary

Wong et al. (2015) conducted a randomized clinical trial at the University of Hong Kong to compare primary non-surgical endodontic treatment completed in a single visit versus across multiple visits. A total of 220 teeth from 194 patients were followed up for a mean of 29.4 months (minimum 18 months). Both groups used identical materials (ProTaper NiTi rotary files, 5.25% NaOCl irrigation, Thermafil core-carrier obturation); the multiple-visit group received calcium hydroxide inter-appointment medication and IRM temporization.

The key finding is equivalence: success rates of 88.9% (single-visit) and 87.4% (multiple-visit) showed no statistically significant difference (p=0.729). Neither did postoperative pain rates at 7 days (21% vs 12%, p=0.055) or at final evaluation (0.9% vs 1.0%, p>0.999). The one significant difference favoring single-visit was chairside time — 62 minutes versus 93 minutes (p<0.001).

Logistic regression identified two independent prognostic factors: maxillary arch location (OR 3.16) and absence of preoperative apical periodontitis (OR 4.35). Visit protocol was not a significant predictor of success.

## Key Contributions

- Establishes RCT-level equivalence in success rate between single-visit and multiple-visit primary non-surgical endodontic treatment (~88% in both groups).
- Single-visit significantly shortens total chairside time (~31 min; p<0.001) without compromising outcomes.
- Identifies maxillary arch and absence of preoperative apical periodontitis as the key independent predictors of success — stronger than visit protocol choice.
- No significant difference in short- or long-term postoperative pain, contradicting the clinical belief that single-visit causes more postoperative discomfort.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Single-center parallel-group RCT; University Health Service Dental Clinic, University of Hong Kong.
- **Sample**: 256 teeth enrolled → 220 analyzed (14.1% dropout); mean age 46.4 years.
- **Randomization**: Calculator-based random-number allocation per tooth; blinded assignment.
- **Single-visit arm**: ProTaper NiTi shaping + 5.25% NaOCl → Thermafil obturation in one session.
- **Multiple-visit arm**: Same shaping/irrigation → UltraCal XS calcium hydroxide + IRM temporary restoration → obturation next week (2–3 visits).
- **Success criterion**: No clinical signs/symptoms AND no periapical radiolucency at ≥18 months follow-up (strict; incomplete healing = failure).
- **Statistics**: Multiple logistic regression (backward stepwise) for success; ANCOVA for chairside time; chi-square/Fisher's exact for pain.

## Results

| Outcome | Single-visit (n=117) | Multiple-visit (n=103) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Success rate | 88.9% | 87.4% | 0.729 |
| Postoperative pain ≤7 days | 21% | 12% | 0.055 (NS) |
| Postoperative pain ≥18 months | 0.9% | 1.0% | >0.999 (NS) |
| Mean chairside time (min) | 62.0 ± 23.5 | 92.9 ± 37.8 | <0.001 |

**Prognostic factors (logistic regression)**:
- Maxillary arch: OR 3.16 (95% CI 1.33–7.46), p=0.009
- No preoperative apical periodontitis: OR 4.35 (95% CI 1.43–13.24), p=0.010
- Visit protocol: NOT significant in final model

**Chairside time reduction** (ANCOVA): single-visit, magnifying loupe use, single-canal tooth, and non-vital tooth all independently reduced total chairside time.
