## One-line Summary

Prospective RCT (n=100): single-visit vs multiple-visit RCT showed no significant difference in post-operative pain (VAS at 24/48/72 h); single-visit group trended toward higher complete-healing rates at 6 and 12 months.

## Summary

A prospective RCT of 100 patients (18–65 y) needing root canal therapy for irreversible pulpitis or apical periodontitis, randomized 1:1 to single-visit or multiple-visit RCT. The primary outcome was post-operative pain measured by VAS at 24, 48, and 72 hours; secondary outcome was radiographic healing at 6 and 12 months. Post-operative pain was statistically comparable between groups at every time point, and the single-visit arm showed a trend toward higher complete-healing rates. The study supports single-visit RCT as providing equivalent pain relief with potentially favorable healing.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates no significant post-operative pain penalty for single-visit vs multiple-visit RCT at 24/48/72 h.
- Adds a 100-patient RCT to the visit-protocol evidence base, reinforcing the "no meaningful outcome difference by visit number" consensus.
- Suggests immediate obturation in single-visit treatment may reduce inter-appointment recontamination (proposed mechanism for the healing trend).

## Methodology

- Prospective RCT (2021–2022, tertiary care, IRB-approved); n=100, randomized 1:1 (single-visit n=50 / multiple-visit n=50) via computer-generated randomization.
- Single-visit arm completed in one session; multiple-visit arm over ≥2 appointments.
- Pain outcome: VAS at 24/48/72 h recorded by a blinded assessor.
- Healing outcome: periapical radiographs at 6 and 12 months read by two independent, allocation-blinded radiologists.

## Results

- Groups demographically comparable (mean age 42.5±8.3 vs 43.2±7.9 y; balanced sex ratio).

| Time (h) | Single-visit VAS | Multiple-visit VAS |
|---|---|---|
| 24 | 3.2±1.1 | 3.5±1.2 |
| 48 | 2.8±1.0 | 3.0±1.1 |
| 72 | 2.5±0.9 | 2.7±1.0 |

- No significant between-group difference in post-operative pain at any time point.
- Single-visit group trended toward higher complete-healing at 6 and 12 months (reported narratively; not framed as a significant effect).
- Limitations: 12-month follow-up only, single-center, healing data reported without explicit per-group P values.
