## One-line Summary

Single-blind RCT (n=30, single-rooted teeth with primary apical periodontitis): single-visit vs multiple-visit RCT showed no significant difference in PAI-based radiographic periapical healing at 6/12 months (93.3% vs 86.7% success at 12 mo, P>0.05).

## Summary

A prospective single-blind RCT comparing single-visit and multiple-visit root canal treatment in 30 patients with single-rooted teeth diagnosed with primary apical periodontitis (pre-operative PAI 3–5). Radiographic healing was scored by the Periapical Index (PAI) at 6 and 12 months, with success defined as PAI ≤2 at 12 months. Both protocols used identical, standardized chemo-mechanical preparation; the multiple-visit arm additionally received a calcium hydroxide inter-appointment dressing. No statistically significant difference in healing was found at either time point, supporting single-visit treatment as a viable, efficient alternative without compromising long-term prognosis.

## Key Contributions

- Primary RCT data confirming no radiographic healing advantage from a multiple-visit (Ca(OH)₂ dressing) protocol over single-visit treatment in single-rooted apical periodontitis.
- Implies thorough chemo-mechanical debridement, not the inter-appointment medicament, is the determinant of healing.
- Supports the single-visit trend (reduced chair time, fewer appointments, lower inter-appointment contamination risk).

## Methodology

- Prospective single-blind RCT, n=30, randomized 1:1 (single-visit n=15 / multiple-visit n=15); allocation by sealed opaque envelopes; blinded independent PAI evaluator.
- Inclusion: 18–60 y, single-rooted teeth, primary apical periodontitis, pre-op PAI 3/4/5, no prior RCT. Exclusion: acute abscess, complex anatomy, systemic disease, pregnancy/lactation.
- Single experienced operator; standardized preparation/irrigation. Multiple-visit arm received Ca(OH)₂ at visit 1, obturated ~1 week later.
- Outcome: PAI at 6 and 12 mo (success = PAI ≤2 at 12 mo). Chi-square and Mann–Whitney U, P<0.05.

## Results

| Outcome | Single-visit (n=15) | Multiple-visit (n=15) | P |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mean PAI 6 mo | 2.1 | 2.2 | 0.58 |
| Mean PAI 12 mo | 1.8 | 1.9 | 0.71 |
| Success (PAI ≤2) 6 mo | 80.0% (12/15) | 73.3% (11/15) | 0.68 |
| Success (PAI ≤2) 12 mo | 93.3% (14/15) | 86.7% (13/15) | 0.6 |

- No significant between-group difference at any time point (all P>0.05).
- Both protocols achieve comparable high success; single-visit deemed equally effective.
- Caveat: small sample (n=30), single-rooted teeth only, 12-month follow-up, single operator/center.
