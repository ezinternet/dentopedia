## One-line Summary

SR+MA+TSA of 29 RCTs (4,341 patients): single-visit and multiple-visit root canal treatment show equivalent long-term complications and postoperative pain, but single-visit carries a significantly higher flare-up risk (RR 2.13; 95% CI 1.16–3.89) on very weak, inconclusive evidence.

## Summary

This 2017 BMJ Open SR+MA+TSA by Schwendicke & Göstemeyer (Charité Berlin) is the first endodontic visit-protocol review to apply Trial Sequential Analysis to quantify the robustness of the accrued evidence. 29 controlled trials (4,341 patients) were included; 23/29 were at high risk of bias.

**Core finding**: There is insufficient evidence to determine whether clinically important differences exist between single-visit and multiple-visit root canal treatment for any of the three outcomes examined. TSA confirmed that the Diversity-Adjusted Required Information Size (DARIS) was not reached for any outcome — meaning no firm conclusion of benefit, harm, or futility is statistically supportable.

**Practical clinical signal**: Single-visit treatment appears safe with respect to long-term complications and pain, but the possibly elevated flare-up risk — driven largely by one outlier study — may favour multiple-visit treatment for teeth with periapical lesions or higher flare-up susceptibility.

## Key Contributions

1. **TSA applied to visit-protocol evidence**: TSA monitoring boundaries spuriously crossed for flare-up by conventional meta-analysis — TSA correctly identifies this as insufficient evidence, not firm harm.
2. **Long-term equivalence**: RR 1.00 (0.75–1.35) for complications ≥1 year; consistent across vitality, pain status, and periapical lesion subgroups.
3. **Pain equivalence**: RR 0.99 (0.76–1.30); moderate GRADE evidence — the most reliable finding in this review.
4. **Flare-up signal with important caveat**: RR 2.13 (1.16–3.89), but one study (flare-up rate 20% in single-visit arm) drives the estimate; excluding it gives RR 1.85 (0.89–3.86; non-significant).
5. **Null moderators**: Pulp vitality, preoperative pain, periapical lesion status, instrumentation type, and calcium hydroxide intracanal medication did not significantly moderate any outcome in meta-regression.

## Methodology

- **Study design**: PROSPERO-registered (CRD42016036386) SR+MA+TSA
- **Databases**: PubMed/MEDLINE, EMBASE, Cochrane Central, opengrey.eu; March 2016 search; no date/language limits
- **Eligibility**: RCTs or controlled trials (no selection bias); permanent teeth, closed apices; outcome ≥1 year complications and/or short-term pain and/or flare-up
- **Included trials**: 29 (4,341 patients); 23 high risk of bias, 6 low risk
- **Statistical model**: Random-effects (DerSimonian-Laird); RR + 95% CI; continuity correction (+1) for zero events
- **TSA**: DARIS computed per outcome; Z-curves plotted against sequential monitoring boundaries
- **GRADE**: Applied to each of the three outcomes separately

### Outcome Definitions

| Outcome | Timeframe | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| Long-term complications | ≥1 year | Pain, infection/swelling/sinus track, periapical lesion development/persistence/worsening, PDL widening |
| Postoperative pain | <1 year | Any pain after obturation (binary or continuous, at shortest post-obturation timepoint) |
| Flare-up (급성 악화) | <1 year, usually ≤7 days | Acute exacerbation requiring further intervention (reaccess, I&D, or retreatment) |

## Results

### Summary Table

| Outcome | Studies | Teeth | RR (95% CI) | Heterogeneity | GRADE |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Long-term complications | 10 | 1,257 | 1.00 (0.75–1.35) | Low | Weak |
| Postoperative pain | 20 | 3,008 | 0.99 (0.76–1.30) | Low | Moderate |
| Flare-up | 8 | 1,110 | 2.13 (1.16–3.89) | Low | Very weak |

### TSA Results

- **All three outcomes**: DARIS not reached; cumulative Z-curve did not cross superiority, inferiority, or futility boundaries.
- **Flare-up**: Conventional significance threshold was crossed — but TSA sequential monitoring boundary (TSMB) for harm was NOT crossed. This is the key correction TSA adds: the conventional p<0.05 is spurious.

### Flare-up Sensitivity

Removing the highest-incidence study (flare-up rate ~20% in single-visit): RR drops to 1.85 (95% CI 0.89–3.86) — non-significant. This single study contributes disproportionate weight.

### Meta-regression (Null Moderators)

None of the following significantly moderated any of the three outcomes:
- Pulp vitality (vital vs non-vital)
- Preoperative pain (painful vs pain-free)
- Periapical lesion status (present vs absent)
- Intracanal medication (calcium hydroxide vs none)

### Clinical Implication

Given the possible (but unconfirmed) flare-up elevation, the authors suggest that **multiple-visit treatment may be preferred for teeth with periapical lesions** while acknowledging this is not firmly supported by the evidence.
