## One-line Summary

Split-tooth in-vitro study (4 sealers × 3 irrigation sequences) — continuous chelation with HEDP caused less dentin erosion, a larger mineral zone at the hydraulic-cement/dentin interface, and improved sealer antimicrobial properties; all sealers lost antimicrobial efficacy after contact with irrigated dentin.

## Summary

Camilleri's group reframes single-cone + hydraulic-cement (calcium-silicate) obturation as a **matched system**: the irrigation chosen for canal preparation directly shapes the dentin–sealer interface and the sealer's antimicrobial behavior, so obturation quality cannot be optimized by sealer choice alone. Using a split-tooth model with SEM/EDS/FTIR and antimicrobial assays, they tested three irrigation sequences (NaOCl, EDTA, **HEDP/continuous chelation**) against four sealers (AH Plus, BioRoot RCS, BioRoot Flow, TotalFill).

Two findings matter for single-cone protocols. First, **HEDP continuous chelation** preserves dentin (less erosion) and produces a **larger mineral concentration zone** at the hydraulic-cement/dentin interface — the mineralizing interface that bioactive sealers depend on — and improves the antimicrobial properties of the hydraulic-cement sealers. Second, and more cautionary, **every sealer's antimicrobial effectiveness drops once it contacts irrigated dentin**, meaning the dentin substrate buffers/inactivates sealer antimicrobial action. The clinical implication: for single-cone bioceramic obturation, irrigation protocol selection (favoring continuous chelation) is part of optimizing the seal and bioactivity, not a separate upstream step.

## Key Contributions

- Treats irrigation × sealer as a **coupled system** for single-cone hydraulic-cement obturation.
- Identifies **HEDP continuous chelation** as the irrigation best preserving dentin and the mineralizing interface, while boosting sealer antimicrobial action.
- Flags a substrate effect: **irrigated dentin reduces antimicrobial efficacy of all sealers** tested.

## Methodology

- Split-tooth model; SEM, EDS, FTIR of dentin, sealers, and interface before/after obturation and irrigation contact.
- 3 irrigation sequences: NaOCl, EDTA, HEDP (water control); 4 sealers: AH Plus, BioRoot RCS, BioRoot Flow, TotalFill; single-cone obturation.
- Microbiology: direct contact test + minimum inhibitory concentration, with/without dentin contact. Two-way ANOVA + Tukey (p<0.05).
- *abstract-only ingest — quantitative interface/MIC values not extracted.*

## Results

- **HEDP**: less dentin erosion; larger mineral concentration zone at hydraulic-cement/dentin interface; improved antimicrobial properties of hydraulic-cement sealers via continuous chelation.
- **All sealers**: reduced antimicrobial effectiveness after contact with irrigated dentin.
- Conclusion: irrigation protocol affects interfacial characteristics and antimicrobial behavior → must be matched to the obturation system to optimize outcomes.
- **Limitations**: in-vitro split-tooth, no clinical outcomes; does not isolate single-cone fit quality from irrigation effects.
