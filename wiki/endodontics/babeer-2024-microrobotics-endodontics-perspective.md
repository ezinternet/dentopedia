## One-line Summary
Perspective review describing three magnetically controlled microrobot platforms (IONP microswarms, 3D helicoids ~700 μm × 1.5 mm, silica nanorobots ~3 μm) capable of simultaneous biofilm disruption, in situ ROS generation, on-demand antibiotic release, and diagnostic sampling within root canal intricacies unreachable by conventional methods.

## Summary
Babeer et al. present a perspective on magnetically controlled microrobotics as a solution to the documented failure of conventional endodontic disinfection to predictably access anatomical intricacies (isthmuses, lateral canals, dentinal tubules). Three platforms are reviewed: (1) aggregated IONP microswarms that configure dynamically to canal geometry, disrupt biofilm mechanically, generate ROS catalytically, and retrieve microbiome samples; (2) 3D-molded soft helicoids (~700 μm × 1.5 mm) driven by rotating magnetic fields with screw-like shear force and on-demand antibiotic release, CBCT-trackable; (3) helical silica nanorobots (~3 μm) penetrating dentinal tubules to ~1000 μm depth with magnetic hyperthermia killing E. faecalis. All platforms are in vitro/ex vivo proof-of-concept; clinical translation remains future work.

## Key Contributions
- Establishes the clinical rationale gap: existing activation methods (ultrasonic, sonic, laser) cannot predictably access isthmuses, fins, and dentinal tubules (citing Boutsioukis & Arias-Moliz 2022).
- Three-platform taxonomy: microswarms (configurable to any geometry), helicoids (screw-driven, drug-loaded), nanorobots (tubule-penetrating, hyperthermia-capable).
- IONP dual function: magnetic navigation + intrinsic peroxidase-like catalytic activity (ROS from H₂O₂ at acidic pH) — combining mechanical and chemical biofilm killing.
- Silica nanorobot dentinal tubule penetration: ~1000 μm — exceeds all current adjunctive endodontic disinfection methods.
- First demonstration of integrated multifunctional platform: disruption + killing + drug delivery + sample retrieval in a single system.
- Potential for regenerative endodontics: drug delivery without mechanical instrumentation in immature teeth with necrotic pulps.

## Methodology
Narrative perspective review (non-systematic). Evidence base: in vitro (canal wall biofilm disruption, microswarm killing vs 3% NaOCl) and ex vivo (helicoid CBCT tracking, nanorobot tubule penetration) proof-of-concept. Funded indirectly through Penn/KAU institutional affiliations; patent interests declared by Karabucak and Koo.

## Results
- IONP microswarms: biofilm disruption on canal walls and isthmus (in vitro); apical microbiome sample retrieval demonstrated; preliminary killing vs 3% NaOCl favours microswarms.
- 3D helicoids (~700 μm × 1.5 mm): actuated to apical region; CBCT-trackable; retrieved by field reversal; antibiotic release on rapid oscillating field.
- Silica nanorobots (~3 μm): ~1000 μm dentinal tubule penetration; E. faecalis killing by hyperthermia (in vitro); partial retrieval demonstrated, complete retrieval requires further development.
