## One-line Summary
Single-blind RCT (n=120, Shanghai) found no significant difference in 12-month DPC success among iRoot BP Plus (88.5%), MTA (93.1%), and Ca(OH)₂ (84.6%); proximal lesion location was the strongest failure predictor (OR=18.18, p=0.001), outweighing material choice.

## 한줄요약
성숙 영구치 가역성 치수염(Reversible Pulpitis)에서 직접치수복조(Direct Pulp Capping, DPC) 3종 재료(iRoot BP Plus 바이오세라믹, MTA, 수산화칼슘(Calcium Hydroxide, CH))를 비교한 단맹 무작위 대조 연구(RCT)(n=120): 12개월 성공률 세 군 동등(88.5% vs 93.1% vs 84.6%, p=0.6); 근심면 국한 우식(Proximal Lesion)이 실패의 압도적 예측인자(OR=18.18).

## Summary

This single-blind, parallel-group RCT (ChiCTR2100047588) enrolled 120 adults with caries-induced reversible pulpitis at Shanghai Ninth People's Hospital (July 2021–July 2024). Patients were randomized 1:1:1 to direct pulp capping (DPC) with iRoot BP Plus, ProRoot MTA, or Calxyl Ca(OH)₂. All procedures were performed by a single experienced endodontist under microscopic magnification with rubber dam isolation. Hemostasis was achieved with 3% NaOCl. The primary outcome was combined clinical+radiographic success at 12 months.

At 12 months, 81 of 120 patients (67.5%) were evaluable (32.5% dropout attributed mainly to COVID-19 disruptions). Overall success was 88.9%, with no statistically significant difference between groups (iRoot BP Plus 88.5%, MTA 93.1%, Ca(OH)₂ 84.6%; p=0.6). Kaplan–Meier overall survival was 89.3% (95% CI: 82.8%–96.4%); log-rank p=0.66 across materials.

Critically, case selection factors outweighed material choice: proximal lesion location was the dominant failure predictor (OR=18.18 at 12 months; all 6 failures at 6 months were proximal-only cases). Anterior tooth location and exposure size >1 mm were significant at 6 months. No adverse events were reported.

## Key Contributions

1. **First 3-way RCT** directly comparing iRoot BP Plus (bioceramic), MTA, and Ca(OH)₂ in mature permanent teeth with caries-related DPC — closing the evidence gap for bioceramics in this indication
2. **Material equivalence confirmed at 12 months**: iRoot BP Plus performs at the same level as MTA, supporting its use given superior handling (premixed, no discoloration)
3. **Proximal location dominates prognosis** (OR=18.18): this finding reframes DPC decision-making — case selection > material selection
4. **Comprehensive prognostic analysis**: identifies anterior tooth position and exposure size >1 mm as additional risk factors; establishes that age, sex, arch, and bleeding time are not significant predictors

## Methodology

- **Design:** Single-center, single-blind, parallel-group, superiority RCT
- **N:** 120 patients (40/group); mature permanent teeth; caries-induced reversible pulpitis; adults ≥18 years
- **Inclusion criteria key points:** Deep caries >2/3 dentin without spontaneous pain; reversible pulpitis (brief stimulus-induced pain); no periapical pathology; pulp exposure confirmed after caries removal; hemostasis achievable within 5 min
- **Materials:** Calxyl (Ca(OH)₂), ProRoot MTA (Dentsply Sirona), iRoot BP Plus (Innovative Bioceramix)
- **Protocol:** Microscopic magnification, rubber dam, 3% NaOCl hemostasis, ≥1.5 mm capping layer, GIC liner, temporary GIC restoration → permanent restoration at 1 month
- **Primary outcome:** Combined clinical + radiographic success at 12 months
- **Secondary outcomes:** Success at 3 and 6 months; exploratory Kaplan–Meier survival analysis
- **Blinding:** Patients and outcome assessors blinded; 2 independent endodontists assessed radiographs
- **Statistics:** Chi-square/Fisher's exact; Kaplan–Meier + log-rank; R 4.5.0; per-protocol with censoring of dropouts
- **Median follow-up:** 364 days; data cutoff November 30, 2025

## Results

### 12-Month Success by Material

| Material | n | Success | Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| iRoot BP Plus | 26 | 23 | 88.5% |
| MTA | 29 | 27 | 93.1% |
| Ca(OH)₂ | 26 | 22 | 84.6% |
| **Overall** | **81** | **72** | **88.9%** |

p=0.6 — no significant between-group difference

### Success Trajectory (All Groups Combined)

| Timepoint | Rate |
|---|---|
| 3 months | 96.0% |
| 6 months | 93.2% |
| 12 months | 88.9% |
| 24 months | 72.7% (subset) |

### Kaplan–Meier Survival at 12 Months

| Material | Survival (95% CI) |
|---|---|
| iRoot BP Plus | 87.7% (74.6%–100%) |
| MTA | 93.5% (85.3%–100%) |
| Ca(OH)₂ | 85.7% (73.5%–100%) |
| Overall | 89.3% (82.8%–96.4%) |

Log-rank p=0.66

### Key Prognostic Factors

| Factor | Timepoint | p | Effect Size |
|---|---|---|---|
| Proximal lesion only | 12 months | **0.001** | OR=18.18 |
| Proximal lesion only | 6 months | **0.002** | All 6 failures proximal |
| Proximal lesion only | 3 months | **0.012** | — |
| Anterior vs posterior | 6 months | **0.018** | OR=10.7 |
| Anterior vs posterior | 3 months | **0.049** | — |
| Exposure size >1 mm | 6 months | **0.024** | OR=9.2 |
| Age, sex, arch, cold test, bleeding time | all | NS | — |

**Clinical interpretation:** The proximal location and anterior tooth position are strongly correlated (OR=11.85), so their independent contributions cannot be separated. Both likely reflect difficult isolation, higher bacterial load, inferior restoration quality, and biomechanical disadvantage.

### Postoperative Pain
No significant difference among groups at 7 days (p=0.5). No adverse events reported.
