## Summary

> [!note] Abstract-only page
> No PMC open-access full text was available at ingest; under wiki Rule #1 only the PubMed abstract was used ([DOI 10.1186/s12903-026-08519-9](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12903-026-08519-9)).

According to PubMed, this SR+MA evaluates the diode laser as an adjunct in pulpotomy of primary teeth. Compared with conventional methods, diode-laser-assisted pulpotomy gave **no significant difference in clinical or radiographic success at any follow-up (p>0.05)** — i.e., it does not beat established techniques on the primary success metric. Its distinctive signal is a **safety/quality one**: diode laser significantly **reduced pathologic widening of the periodontal space (p<0.05)**, a radiographic marker of inflammatory failure, and the effect was reinforced when zinc oxide eugenol (ZOE) was the capping material. Laser wavelength did not influence outcome, and no publication bias was detected.

Clinically, this positions the diode laser as a viable but not superior alternative for pulp-stump management — potentially useful for cleaner radiographic healing, especially with ZOE — rather than a new standard. It sits alongside the wiki's primary-tooth pulp pages and the broader VPT material/technique debate.

## Key Contributions

- Diode-laser pulpotomy ≈ conventional on success rates (no significant difference).
- Significantly less pathologic periodontal-space widening with diode laser (and with ZOE capping).
- Wavelength-independent.

## Methodology

## Results

- Clinical/radiographic success: NS vs other methods at all follow-ups (p>0.05).
- Pathologic periodontal-space widening: lower with diode laser (p<0.05); further with ZOE (p<0.05).
- Wavelength NS; no publication bias.
