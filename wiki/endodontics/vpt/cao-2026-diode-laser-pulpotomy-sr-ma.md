## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 15 RCTs (n=1,092 primary teeth): diode-laser pulpotomy achieved clinical/radiographic success rates equivalent to all comparators at 3–12 months (RR≈1.00, p>0.05), while significantly reducing pathologic PDL-space widening (RR=0.39, 95% CI 0.17–0.90, p=0.03); wavelength and capping material did not affect success.

## Summary

This SR+MA (PRISMA 2020) evaluated diode-laser pulpotomy against all conventional pulpotomy methods across 15 RCTs (2005–2024) totaling 1,092 primary molar teeth in children aged 3–9 years. Studies came from India (n=7), Iran (4), Brazil, Canada, Turkey, and Saudi Arabia.

**Primary finding**: Diode-laser-assisted pulpotomy is **not superior** to any established technique on clinical or radiographic success — RR approximated 1.00 at every follow-up point (3, 6, 9, and 12 months), with I²=0% at most timepoints indicating high consistency.

**Safety signal**: The single outcome where diode laser outperformed controls was pathologic widening of the periodontal ligament (PDL) space — a radiographic marker of periradicular inflammation. The pooled RR was 0.39 (95% CI 0.17–0.90, p=0.03, I²=30%), meaning diode laser reduced this adverse event by ~61% compared to controls. The effect was amplified in the ZOE capping subgroup (RR=0.29, 95% CI 0.11–0.78, p=0.01).

**Important caveat**: The PDL-widening benefit is statistically fragile — it lost significance when Yavagal 2022 was excluded in sensitivity analysis. No other adverse-event category (pain, swelling, tenderness, mobility, furcal/periapical radiolucency, resorption) differed significantly.

**Wavelength**: 632–660 nm and 810–980 nm diode bands performed identically on all outcomes.

**Practical implication**: Diode laser is a viable — but not superior — alternative for pulp-stump management in primary teeth. Its main advantage is avoidance of chemical toxicity (vs FC) and tooth discoloration (vs MTA), combined with photobiomodulation properties promoting dentin bridge formation. However, laser parameter standardization (power, frequency, energy density, irradiation mode) remains an unsolved problem across trials.

## Key Contributions

- Provides pooled RR estimates with 95% CIs for clinical and radiographic success across four follow-up periods in primary teeth — largest and most comprehensive dataset to date on diode-laser pulpotomy.
- First SR+MA to include a systematic safety analysis covering 7 postoperative adverse-event categories rather than limiting to success rates.
- Demonstrates wavelength independence (632–660 nm ≡ 810–980 nm) — a clinically useful finding given the variety of devices in use.
- ZOE + diode laser combination shows a specific safety benefit on PDL-space widening (RR=0.29, p=0.01), though sensitive to one study.

## Methodology

- **Search**: PubMed, Embase, Cochrane Library; inception to 31 August 2025; English only; MeSH: "pulpotomy", "Lasers", "Lasers, Semiconductor".
- **Study selection**: 348 records → 15 RCTs included (n=1,092 primary teeth).
- **PICOS**: Children 3–10 years, primary molars, vital pulp (reversible pulpitis), RCTs only; excluded n≤10, histologic studies, incomplete follow-up.
- **Control arms**: FC (8 studies), FS (4), MTA (3), electrosurgery (2), plus SG, CH, ADM, C-MTA, CEM, Biodentine, NaClO, Er,Cr:YSGG (1 each).
- **Quality**: Cochrane RoB tool; adequate randomization in 9/15; allocation concealment unclear in 12/15; blinding largely unclear — overall limited methodological quality.
- **Statistics**: RR + 95% CI; fixed-effects (I²<50%); random-effects (I²≥50%); subgroup analyses by wavelength, capping material, control group; sensitivity analysis (sequential exclusion); funnel plots (no publication bias detected).

### Postoperative adverse events (all n=1,076)

| Adverse event | RR | 95% CI | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Spontaneous pain | 0.63 | 0.29–1.38 | 0.25 |
| Swelling/abscess/sinus tract | 1.01 | 0.54–1.88 | 0.97 |
| Tenderness | 0.89 | 0.29–2.73 | 0.83 |
| Pathological mobility | 0.92 | 0.34–2.46 | 0.87 |
| **PDL-space widening** | **0.39** | **0.17–0.90** | **0.03** |
| Furcal/periapical radiolucency | 1.18 | 0.79–1.78 | 0.42 |

PDL-space widening with ZOE capping (N=8, n=602): RR=0.29, 95% CI 0.11–0.78, p=0.01.

**Sensitivity caveat**: Both the overall PDL-widening result and the ZOE subgroup result lost significance when Yavagal 2022 was excluded — findings require cautious interpretation.
