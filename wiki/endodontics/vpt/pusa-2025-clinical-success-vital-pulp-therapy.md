## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 21 RCTs (1,816 participants, 1,948 permanent teeth) finding direct pulp capping with MTA significantly more successful than calcium hydroxide (RR 1.21, 95% CI 1.02–1.42, P=0.03), with no significant MTA-vs-Biodentine or partial-vs-full-pulpotomy difference.

## 한줄요약

21편 RCT(참가자 1,816명·치아 1,948개)의 SR+MA로, 직접치수복조에서 MTA가 수산화칼슘보다 유의하게 높은 성공률(RR 1.21, 95% CI 1.02–1.42, P=0.03)을 보였고 MTA vs Biodentine, 부분 vs 전체 치수절단술 간에는 유의차가 없었다.

## Summary

This broad multi-database systematic review and meta-analysis (including grey literature) of vital pulp therapy on pulp-exposed permanent teeth pooled 21 RCTs covering direct pulp capping and partial/full pulpotomy with calcium hydroxide, MTA, and Biodentine. The single significant finding among six poolable comparisons was that MTA outperforms calcium hydroxide in direct pulp capping (RR 1.21). MTA and Biodentine were comparable, and pulpotomy depth (partial vs full) did not change outcomes.

## Key Contributions

- Independent SR+MA sample (1,816 participants / 1,948 teeth) corroborating MTA > Ca(OH)2 in DPC.
- Spans DPC and both pulpotomy depths across three capping materials.
- Documents universal high performance-bias risk from non-blindable materials.

## Methodology

- PRISMA SR+MA; RCTs/quasi-/cluster-RCTs; 8 databases incl. grey literature to Dec 2022.
- Permanent teeth with pulp exposure; follow-up ≥6 months; outcome = clinical success.
- Cochrane 7-domain RoB; RR (95% CI), RevMan 5.4, I² heterogeneity.

## Results

- DPC MTA vs Ca(OH)2: RR 1.21 (95% CI 1.02–1.42, P=0.03) — MTA superior.
- DPC MTA vs Biodentine: NS.
- Partial vs full pulpotomy: NS.
- Only 6 of 17 comparison types poolable; all 21 studies high performance-bias risk.
