## One-line Summary

Abstract-only narrative review (7 clinical studies + 2 meta-analyses): pulpotomy for primary teeth clinically diagnosed with irreversible pulpitis achieved 95–100% clinical and 90–100% radiographic success at 6–12 months, supporting it as a minimally invasive alternative to pulpectomy and calling for guideline revision.

## Summary

> [!note] Abstract-only page
> No PMC open-access full text was available at ingest; under wiki Rule #1 only the PubMed abstract was used ([DOI 10.1007/s40368-026-01199-7](https://doi.org/10.1007/s40368-026-01199-7)).

According to PubMed, this narrative review argues that the long-standing rule — "irreversible pulpitis in a primary tooth ⇒ pulpectomy or extraction" — is outdated. Synthesizing 7 clinical studies and 2 meta-analyses, it reports pulpotomy clinical success of **95–100%** and radiographic success of **90–100%** at 6-month–1-year follow-up in primary teeth clinically diagnosed with irreversible pulpitis. The mechanistic premise is that the clinical diagnosis overestimates radicular pulp inflammation, so removing only the coronal pulp often suffices. The authors propose revised diagnostic considerations and procedural guidance and explicitly **call for revision of international guidelines** to reduce unnecessary invasive treatment.

This is the lower-evidence (narrative) companion to the SR+MA [[endodontics/vpt/chawla-2026-pulpotomy-vs-pulpectomy-primary-teeth-sr-ma]] (which found no significant difference vs pulpectomy on only 2 studies, very low certainty). Together they mark a clear directional shift toward pulpotomy for the carefully selected irreversibly-inflamed primary tooth — with case selection (true pulp status, absence of swelling/periapical pathology) as the decisive caveat.

## Key Contributions

- High reported pulpotomy success (clinical 95–100%, radiographic 90–100%) for irreversible pulpitis in primary teeth.
- Calls for guideline revision toward minimally invasive pulpotomy.

## Methodology

Narrative review; MEDLINE/Embase/WoS/Cochrane search; clinical studies + SRs of pulpotomy for primary-tooth irreversible pulpitis. Confidence `narrative-review`; abstract-only ingest (no PMC OA).

## Results

- 7 clinical studies + 2 meta-analyses.
- Clinical success 95–100%; radiographic 90–100% at 6–12 mo.
- Pulpotomy a viable alternative to pulpectomy.
