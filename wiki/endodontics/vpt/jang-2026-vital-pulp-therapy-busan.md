## Summary
This 69-slide clinical lecture, presented in Busan on April 12, 2026, outlines a comprehensive protocol for Vital Pulp Therapy (VPT) with emphasis on Vital Pulpectomy — a technique that removes only the coronal and upper-root pulp tissue while preserving healthy apical pulp to stimulate tertiary dentin formation. The presenter advocates Endoseem (a bioceramic material) as the barrier material, placed at ≥3 mm thickness between the resin core and the residual vital pulp. Working length is intentionally set 2–3 mm short of the apical constriction, distinguishing this technique from full root canal treatment. The lecture covers specific indications (cracked teeth, geriatric patients, over-erupted opposing teeth), step-by-step protocol, hemostasis management, FAQ, and clinical cases.

## Key Contributions
- **Vital Pulpectomy concept**: Distinct from full RCT; coronal pulp removed to ~2–3 mm short of apex; bioceramic barrier seals the vital apical pulp
- **Endoseem barrier requirement**: Minimum 3 mm thickness; must be interposed between core and living pulp; acts as defense layer against secondary infection
- **Irrigation protocol revision**: Replace EDTA with NaOCl 5.25% + ultrasonic (simultaneously dissolves pulp surface tissue and removes smear layer, minimizing wound)
- **Pressure hemostasis**: Wet cotton pellet expansion + sustained pressure; Endoseem used for hemostasis of persistently bleeding canals
- **Crown mandatory**: Long-term Endoseem stability requires crown restoration; resin-only closure leads to dissolution
- **Partial pulpotomy tradeoff**: Higher post-op sensitivity and discomfort vs. Vital Pulpotomy; less suitable when large prep required

## Methodology
| Parameter | Vital Pulpectomy Protocol |
|---|---|
| Design | Clinical expert lecture (not RCT/SR) |
| Material | Endoseem (bioceramic) |
| Working length | 2–3 mm short of apex |
| Canal enlargement | 2–3 sizes above initial file (removed 2–3 years later) |
| Irrigation | NaOCl 5.25% + ultrasonic; 10s × 3–9 cycles |
| Hemostasis | Pressure (wet cotton pellet); Endoseem for persistent bleed |
| Barrier thickness | ≥3 mm Endoseem |
| Restoration | Crown (mandatory for long-term seal) |

**Working length reference values**:
- Maxillary molars: access opening 2 mm, orifice to apex 6 mm → WL ~10 mm (or 8 mm with severe occlusal wear)
- Mandibular molars: access opening 1.5 mm, orifice to apex 6 mm → WL ~10 mm (or 8 mm with severe occlusal wear)

## Results

| Scenario | Clinical outcome noted |
|---|---|
| Vital Pulpotomy (general) | High success rate; internationally validated |
| Cracked teeth | Vital Pulpotomy/Pulpectomy particularly effective |
| Geriatric/mobility-impaired | Fast, predictable treatment; VPT ideal |
| Over-erupted implant opposing teeth | VPT resolves post-prosthetic sensitivity |
| Furcation lesions | Endoseem lining of chamber floor → lesion resolution (2-yr cases) |
| Delayed crowning after VPT | Rarely causes problems |
| Partial pulpotomy before crown | Higher frequency of cold/heat sensitivity and percussion pain vs. vital pulpotomy |

**Irrigation sequence (Vital Pulpectomy)**:
- Before orifice exposure: NaOCl 5.25%, 10 s × 3 cycles
- After orifice exposure: NaOCl 5.25% + ultrasonic, 10 s × 5–9 cycles
- EDTA not used; NaOCl + ultrasonic handles smear layer and surface pulp dissolution

**Calcified canals**:
- Mild calcification: ultrasonic alone sufficient if orifice easily found and bleeding minimal
- Severe calcification: enlarge canals fully (oval cross-section → lateral pulp remnants must be cleared)
- Severe pulp stone: abundant enlargement + irrigation
