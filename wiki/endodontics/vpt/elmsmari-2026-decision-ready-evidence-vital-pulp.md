## One-line Summary

Bayesian network meta-analysis (35 RCTs; 24 in mature permanent teeth, 2,906 teeth) finding VPT success generally >93% across modalities, with hydraulic calcium-silicate cements (MTA, Biodentine) beating Ca(OH)2 (high-certainty for MTA, moderate for Biodentine) but no clear difference between silicate cements.

## 한줄요약

35편 RCT(성숙영구치 24편, 2,906개 치아)의 베이지안 네트워크 메타분석으로, VPT 성공률은 modality 전반 대체로 >93%였고, 수경성 규산칼슘시멘트(MTA·Biodentine)가 Ca(OH)2보다 우월(MTA는 high-certainty, Biodentine은 moderate)했으나 규산칼슘시멘트 간 차이는 명확하지 않았다.

## Summary

This Bayesian network meta-analysis adds CINeMA certainty grading to deliver "decision-ready" evidence on bioactive materials for vital pulp therapy in mature permanent teeth. Across modalities (full/partial pulpotomy, direct/indirect pulp capping) success rates were uniformly high (>93%). Hydraulic calcium-silicate cements outperformed calcium hydroxide — with high certainty for MTA and moderate for Biodentine — but head-to-head silicate comparisons showed overlapping credible intervals (no clear winner). Adjunctive therapies (PRP, laser) were only low-certainty promising.

## Key Contributions

- Couples network meta-analysis with CINeMA certainty grading, separating high- from low-certainty contrasts.
- Confirms >93% VPT success across all modalities.
- Frames prognosis around diagnostic frame, apex maturity, and restoration interface.

## Methodology

- PRISMA 2020 / PRISMA-NMA; arm-based Bayesian random-effects NMA + frequentist sensitivity (REML–Hartung–Knapp); PROSPERO CRD420251074110.
- Six databases; composite (clinical + radiographic) success at 6/12/24/36 months.
- 35 RCTs / 2,906 teeth (24 RCTs mature permanent); coherence p>0.10; τ²=0.09 (low).

## Results

- Success generally >93% across VPT types/time points.
- MTA & Biodentine > Ca(OH)2 in DPC and partial pulpotomy.
- Certainty: high (MTA vs Ca(OH)2), moderate (Biodentine vs Ca(OH)2).
- Silicate-vs-silicate: overlapping credible intervals, no explicit difference.
- Adjunctive therapies: low-certainty, interpret cautiously.
