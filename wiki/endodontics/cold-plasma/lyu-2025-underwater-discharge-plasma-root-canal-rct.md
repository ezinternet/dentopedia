## One-line Summary

First RCT pilot (n=28, 4 months) of underwater discharge plasma (UDP) vs. 6% NaOCl for root canal irrigation — comparable pain reduction and periapical healing, no adverse effects.

## Summary

This prospective parallel-group RCT from Hallym University Sacred Heart Hospital (Seoul, Korea) is the **first clinical trial** to evaluate underwater discharge plasma (UDP) as an endodontic irrigant in human patients. Thirty adults requiring root canal treatment (RCT) were randomized 1:1 to UDP (PLAZEN RCT®, Dentory) or 6% sodium hypochlorite (NaOCl). Twenty-eight completed the 4-month follow-up (n=14 per group).

UDP generates plasma within the aqueous root canal environment through high-frequency, high-voltage electrical discharge. The system ionizes water molecules into hydroxyl radicals (•OH), hydrogen peroxide (H₂O₂), and ozone (O₃) — reactive oxygen species (ROS) with strong antimicrobial activity — at temperatures below 40°C. A real-time impedance feedback mechanism automatically stops energy delivery when canal impedance exceeds 5000 Ω, preventing thermal injury.

**Key outcomes at 4 months**:
- VAS pain scores: both groups reduced significantly over time; no significant between-group difference at any point (p>0.05 at all standard time points)
- PAI scores: comparable reduction in both groups (UDP 1.66±0.79 vs. NaOCl 1.43±0.62, p=0.401)
- Clinical success (PAI ≤2): UDP 71.4% vs. NaOCl 92.9% (p=0.326, NS)
- Overall healing: 82.1% (23/28)
- Safety: no adverse effects or thermal damage in UDP group

The study concludes that UDP demonstrates short-term clinical efficacy and safety **comparable** to NaOCl, though the numerically lower healing rate in the UDP group (71.4% vs. 92.9%) underscores the need for larger, longer-term trials before clinical adoption.

## Key Contributions

1. **First human RCT of UDP** as a root canal irrigant — bridges the gap between extensive in vitro evidence and clinical application.
2. Demonstrates non-inferiority of UDP for short-term pain (VAS) and radiographic healing (PAI) outcomes vs. the gold-standard irrigant (6% NaOCl).
3. Validates the **impedance-guided safety system** (>5000 Ω cutoff) in clinical use — no thermal injury across 15 treated cases.
4. Documents the PLAZEN RCT® workflow: saline fill → K-file insertion (3 mm short of WL) → plasma activation → NiTi rotary shaping → second plasma discharge before obturation.
5. Highlights UDP's theoretical advantages: enhanced penetration into anatomically complex areas (isthmuses, lateral canals), lower cytotoxicity than high-concentration NaOCl.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Prospective, single-operator, parallel-group RCT pilot; IRB-approved (No. 2024-03-016)
- **n**: 30 randomized → 28 completed (1:1, 14 per group); 2 lost to follow-up
- **Setting**: Hallym University Sacred Heart Hospital, Anyang, Republic of Korea (Aug–Dec 2024)
- **Intervention**: UDP via PLAZEN RCT® device (Dentory, Seoul); IEC 60601-2-2 compliant
- **Control**: 6% NaOCl (Sense Cleaner; Sunjin Bio, Seoul)
- **Shaping**: VDW.ROTATE NiTi rotary (25/0.06 standard; 30/0.05 or 35/0.04 for wider canals)
- **Obturation**: MTA-based sealer (Ceraseal®); CaOH intervisit if persistent infection
- **Outcomes**: VAS (0–10) at preop/1wk/2mo/4mo; PAI (1–5) at preop/post-fill/2mo/4mo
- **Blinding**: Two blinded, board-certified OMF radiologists for PAI (kappa=0.721)
- **Statistics**: Friedman test (within-group VAS); Mann-Whitney U (between-group); Fisher's exact (healing rates)
- **Power**: 0.65 (acknowledged underpowered; pilot design)

## Results

| Outcome | Time | UDP | NaOCl | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| VAS mean | 4 months | 0.07±0.27 | 0.14±0.54 | NS (both near zero) |
| PAI mean | 4 months | 1.66±0.79 | 1.43±0.62 | 0.401 |
| Success (PAI ≤2) | 4 months | 71.4% (10/14) | 92.9% (13/14) | 0.326 |
| Adverse effects | — | 0 | 0 | — |

Inter-rater reliability for radiographic PAI: quadratic-weighted κ=0.721 (substantial).

Within-group VAS reduction: both statistically significant (UDP p=0.010; NaOCl p=0.017 by Friedman test).

At 4 months, numerically lower but statistically non-significant healing rate in UDP group (71.4% vs. 92.9%) likely reflects underpowering (n=14/group, power=0.65) rather than true inferiority.

## Clinical Significance

UDP represents the first plasma-based endodontic irrigant tested in humans. Its mechanism — generating ROS at <40°C in aqueous medium — addresses two classic NaOCl limitations: risk of apical extrusion injury and limited penetration into anatomically inaccessible canal areas. The 4-month pilot data support safety and suggest comparable short-term efficacy, but larger RCTs with microbiological endpoints and ≥1-year follow-up are needed before routine clinical recommendation.

The device (PLAZEN RCT®) is commercially available in Korea; institutional COI should be noted as the study was conducted at Hallym University, which is closely affiliated with the device developer (Dentory, Seoul).
