## One-line Summary

In-vitro study (n=210 mandibular premolars) showing CAP plasma jet achieves substantial E. faecalis CFU reduction after 5 min contact time, inferior to 5.25% NaOCl (zero growth at 5 min) but demonstrating clinical potential as a novel endodontic disinfectant.

## Summary

This in-vitro study (Kumar et al., 2023) evaluated Cold Atmospheric Pressure (CAP) plasma jet as a root canal disinfectant against E. faecalis, compared to the established irrigants 5.25% NaOCl and QMix across three contact times (2, 5, and 10 min). Using 210 single-rooted mandibular premolars infected with E. faecalis (ATCC 29212, 10^5 CFU/mL, 7-day anaerobic incubation), the study found that 5.25% NaOCl remained the most effective disinfectant, achieving zero bacterial growth at ≥5 min. CAP plasma jet required a minimum of 5 min for substantial CFU reduction but could not achieve complete sterilization even at 10 min. QMix required 10 min for optimal reduction. Both ANOVA and Zero-Inflated Poisson statistical models confirmed significant inter-group differences at all time points.

## Key Contributions

1. **First time-comparative study of CAP plasma vs NaOCl and QMix** in E. faecalis root canal infection model.
2. **Minimum contact time thresholds established**:
   - NaOCl 5.25%: ≥5 min → zero CFU
   - QMix: ≥10 min → optimal CFU reduction
   - CAP plasma jet: ≥5 min → substantial CFU reduction (not zero)
3. **CAP plasma jet validated as antibacterial adjunct** in endodontics — statistically significant over control at 5 and 10 min.
4. **Novel statistical approach**: Zero-Inflated Poisson (ZIP) model applied to excess-zero CFU data, more rigorous than standard ANOVA for this data type.

## Methodology

- **Specimens**: 210 caries-free single-rooted mandibular premolars (root length 12 mm, curvature ≤25°, ProTaper Universal F3 shaping)
- **Infection**: E. faecalis ATCC 29212 at 10^5 CFU/mL; 7-day anaerobic incubation in BHI broth
- **CAP plasma device**: Dielectric barrier discharge jet; 5 kV sinusoidal HV; helium gas 2.5 L/min; plume via 23-gauge needle
- **Irrigant delivery**: 5 mL syringe with 24-gauge needle, 2 mm short of working length
- **Groups**: 3 experimental (CAP jet, NaOCl, QMix) × 3 time points (2/5/10 min) + 1 control (saline 10 min) = 10 groups × n=21
- **CFU measurement**: Barbed broach + F3 paper point → BHI broth culture → blood agar plating
- **Statistics**: ANOVA + Tukey's post-hoc; Zero-Inflated Poisson (ZIP) model; p<0.05

## Results

| Group | 2 min | 5 min | 10 min |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5.25% NaOCl | Significant reduction | **Zero CFU** | Zero CFU |
| QMix | Comparable to NaOCl at 2 min (some analyses) | Moderate reduction | Optimal reduction |
| CAP plasma jet | Minimal effect | **Substantial reduction** | Substantial reduction (not zero) |
| Saline (control) | — | — | No reduction |

- 5.25% NaOCl significantly superior to CAP plasma jet at all time points (p<0.001)
- CAP plasma jet significantly outperformed control at 5 and 10 min
- At 2 min, CAP plasma jet was not significantly different from control in some models
