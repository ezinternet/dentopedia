## One-line Summary

In vitro 4-arm study (n=40 primary incisors) found NTPP jet achieved 98.79% E. faecalis CFU reduction in primary root canals — superior to CHX, diode laser (810 nm), and propolis.

## 한줄요약

유치 전치 40개 in vitro 연구에서 비열 압력 플라스마(NTPP) 처치가 E. faecalis 군집 98.79% 감소로 CHX·다이오드 레이저·프로폴리스보다 우수한 근관 소독 효과를 보임.

## Summary

El Shishiny et al. (2025, BMC Oral Health) compared four disinfection modalities for primary anterior root canals contaminated with *Enterococcus faecalis* in a controlled in vitro setting. Forty extracted single-rooted primary incisors were prepared, sterilized by gamma irradiation (25 kGy), inoculated with E. faecalis, and divided into four groups (n=10 each): CHX irrigation, diode laser (810 nm), propolis (ethanolic extract), and NTPP (non-thermal pressure plasma) jet. CFU counts were measured before and after treatment.

NTPP achieved the highest log CFU reduction (4.06 ± 0.88) and 98.79% colony count reduction — the best performer. Propolis achieved the lowest reduction (81.99%, p=0.035). All modalities produced statistically significant reductions (CHX, diode laser, NTPP: p<0.001; propolis: p=0.035).

**Context — why primary teeth matter**: NaOCl (the gold standard in permanent teeth) risks toxicity to permanent tooth follicles through open apical foramina in primary teeth, making CHX and alternative disinfectants more relevant here. NTPP's ability to deliver bactericidal reactive species (ROS/RNS) without temperature damage or chemical toxicity makes it an appealing alternative for pediatric endodontics.

## Key Contributions

1. **NTPP superiority in primary root canals**: First direct comparison of NTPP vs CHX, diode laser, and propolis in a primary tooth model — NTPP wins on CFU log reduction (4.06 ± 0.88).
2. **Propolis as weakest option**: Despite natural/biocompatible appeal, ethanolic propolis achieved only 81.99% reduction with marginal statistical significance — limiting its standalone use.
3. **NTPP mechanism in enclosed spaces**: Reactive oxygen/nitrogen species (ROS/RNS), UV photons, charged particles, and electric field effects can penetrate dentin tubules and root canal biofilm without thermal side effects.
4. **Standardization via gamma irradiation**: 25 kGy gamma sterilization provided a reproducible contamination baseline — model can serve as a reference protocol for future plasma endodontic studies.
5. **All modalities effective**: Even the weakest (propolis, 81.99%) achieved significant bacterial reduction — none tested was ineffective.

## Methodology

- **Model**: 40 extracted single-rooted primary anterior teeth; 8 mm standardized root length (decoronation below CEJ); apical foramen sealed with composite
- **Pathogen**: *E. faecalis* (gram-positive, primary endodontic persistent pathogen)
- **Sterilization**: Cobalt-60 gamma irradiation (25 kGy); sterility verified by multi-agar culture before inoculation
- **Groups**: CHX (n=10), diode laser 810 nm (n=10), propolis 100% w/v ethanolic extract (n=10), NTPP (n=10)
- **Outcome**: CFU reduction (log scale and %) pre vs. post treatment; one-way ANOVA

## Results

| Modality | Log CFU reduction (mean ± SD) | % reduction | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| CHX | — | — | < 0.001 |
| Diode laser (810 nm) | — | — | < 0.001 |
| Propolis | — | 81.99% | 0.035 |
| **NTPP** | **4.06 ± 0.88** | **98.79%** | **< 0.001** |

- NTPP achieved the highest CFU reduction of all four groups
- Propolis: lowest reduction, weakest p-value — least reliable as sole irrigant
- All four significantly reduced E. faecalis colony counts

## Clinical Implications

- **Primary tooth pulpectomy**: NTPP may offer a biologically safe and highly effective adjunct disinfection step — particularly valuable because NaOCl carries permanent-successor follicle toxicity risk through open apices
- **CHX remains a safe conventional option** but was outperformed by NTPP in this model
- **Propolis**: not recommended as sole disinfectant based on this data — may suit adjunctive/medication roles
- **Translation barriers**: NTPP requires specialized plasma jet equipment adapted for narrow pediatric root canal delivery; clinical protocols are not yet standardized
- **Curved canals in primary teeth**: NTPP plasma jet delivery to curved/narrow canals (common in primary molars) not tested — straight anterior teeth only
