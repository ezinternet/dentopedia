## One-line Summary

In vitro study (n=100 extracted teeth, 1-week E. faecalis biofilm) showing He/O₂ cold plasma jet achieves the greatest CFU reduction (~5.2 log; log 5.39→0.2) and complete SEM-confirmed biofilm elimination — outperforming PDT and pure He plasma — in the first direct head-to-head comparison of these modalities.

## Summary

This in vitro study by Armand et al. (2019) is the first to directly compare cold atmospheric plasma jet (He and He/O₂) against photodynamic therapy (PDT) for disinfection of E. faecalis-contaminated root canals. One hundred extracted single-rooted human teeth were standardised to 12 mm, sterilised, inoculated with E. faecalis (ATCC 29212), and incubated for one week to establish biofilm. Phase 1 established dose–response for He plasma and He/O₂ plasma (2–8 min exposures). Phase 2 directly compared 8-min He plasma, 8-min He/O₂ plasma, and PDT against a control.

He/O₂ plasma delivered a ~5.2 log CFU reduction — near-complete eradication — while PDT ranked second and pure He plasma ranked third, all significantly outperforming the control. SEM confirmed complete biofilm disappearance and obliteration of dentinal tubule openings in the He/O₂ group, suggesting deep reactive-species penetration. The study concludes He/O₂ plasma is a strong sterilisation method against E. faecalis biofilm and a promising adjunct in endodontic treatment.

## Key Contributions

- **First direct comparison** of cold plasma jet (He, He/O₂) vs. PDT for root canal E. faecalis biofilm — fills a gap acknowledged in prior literature.
- Quantified dose–response: He/O₂ plasma CFU drops from log 5.39 (control) to 0.2 at 8 min; He plasma from 5.55 to 3.42.
- SEM evidence: He/O₂ eliminates biofilm and obliterates dentinal tubule openings — suggests better depth penetration than PDT.
- Established **ranking**: He/O₂ plasma > PDT > He plasma for anti-E. faecalis efficacy.
- Highlighted plasma's delivery advantage: gas phase can reach complex root canal anatomy where liquid photosensitiser distribution may be uneven.

## Methodology

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Sample | 100 single-rooted human extracted teeth, standardised 12 mm length |
| Pathogen | E. faecalis ATCC 29212, 1-week biofilm |
| Groups (Phase 1) | He plasma: 5 exposure times (0/2/4/6/8 min, n=4/subgroup); He/O₂ plasma: same; PDT: control vs. treatment |
| Groups (Phase 2) | Control, 8-min He plasma, 8-min He/O₂ plasma, PDT (n=10 each) |
| Outcome measures | CFU/mL counting (quantitative bactericidal); SEM (structural) |
| Plasma device | Low-temperature atmospheric pressure cold plasma jet |
| Feeding gases | Pure He; He/O₂ mixture |
| PDT protocol | Photosensitiser + specific-wavelength light + O₂ |
| Canal preparation | Crown-down technique; sterilised with 5.25% NaOCl prior to inoculation |

## Results

**CFU reduction (8-min / PDT treatment vs. control)**:

| Modality | Baseline log CFU/mL | Post-treatment log CFU/mL | Reduction |
|---|---|---|---|
| He/O₂ plasma (8 min) | 5.39 | 0.20 | ~5.2 log |
| PDT | ~5.5 | Significant (between He and He/O₂) | >2 log |
| He plasma (8 min) | 5.55 | 3.42 | ~2.1 log |
| Control | 5.5 | No change | — |

- **Dose–response confirmed**: each additional 2-min plasma increment progressively reduced CFU for both gas types.
- **SEM**: He/O₂ group — complete biofilm elimination, dentinal tubule openings obliterated; He plasma group — partial biofilm disruption; PDT — variable biofilm reduction.
- Statistical significance: all treatment groups significantly different from control (p-values reported in full text).
