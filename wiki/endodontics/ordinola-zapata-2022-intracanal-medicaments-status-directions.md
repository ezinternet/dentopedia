## Summary
Ordinola-Zapata and colleagues review the clinical rationale for intracanal medicaments, arguing that the binary presence/absence model of apical periodontitis fails to capture disease severity. They propose a four-stage AP classification (analogous to oncological staging) to stratify indications for multi-visit treatment with intracanal medicaments. Calcium hydroxide (pH ~12.5) remains the cornerstone agent, valued for bactericidal activity, LPS inactivation, and confirmation of initial healing. Key prognostic factors from an 800-patient prospective cohort (Ng et al. 2011) are synthesised, and postoperative pain incidence data (19% severe at 1 week; 10% chronic at 6 months) provide quantitative clinical thresholds. Future directions include validated AP staging RCTs and antimicrobial peptide translation.

## Key Contributions
- Four-stage AP classification: Stage 1 (minimal/no changes) → Stage 2 (lesion >5 mm + sinus tract) → Stage 3 (through-and-through bone loss, furcation, J-shaped lesion) → Stage 4 (imminent tooth loss).
- Calcium hydroxide pH ~12.5: primary mechanism via hydroxyl radical bactericidal action, LPS inactivation, necrotic tissue dissolution.
- Debridement prerequisite: medicament efficacy is unpredictable without thorough prior irrigation/instrumentation.
- Prognostic factors for failure (Ng 2011, n=800): AP, lesion size, sinus tract, flare-up, diabetes/steroids, preoperative pain, deep/narrow probing.
- AMPs as next-generation agents: broad-spectrum, biofilm-penetrating, low resistance risk.

## Methodology
Narrative (non-systematic) review commissioned for IEJ 2022 supplement. Key quantitative data drawn from cohort studies (Ng et al. 2011, n=800), prospective pain studies (Law 2015, Nixdorf 2016), and RCT on retreatment (Yoldas 2004, n=218). Funded by NIH/NCATS UL1TR002494.

## Results
- Postoperative severe pain: ~19% at 1 week (Law et al. 2015); chronic pain ≥6 months: ~10% (Nixdorf et al. 2016).
- Two-visit retreatment with calcium hydroxide (n=218): reduced postoperative pain and flare-ups vs single visit in previously symptomatic teeth.
- Calcium hydroxide less effective against E. faecalis and C. albicans in biofilm — a recognised limitation.
- AP prognostic factors from cohort of 800 patients: lesion size and immune status interact to define stage and prognosis.
