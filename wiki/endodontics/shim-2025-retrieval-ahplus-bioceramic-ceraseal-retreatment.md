## One-line Summary

In-vitro micro-CT study (36 mandibular premolars, n=12/group) showing AH Plus Bioceramic (AHB) and Ceraseal (CER) single-cone fills are more retrievable during retreatment than epoxy-resin AH Plus Jet (AHJ): 94.8% / 92.5% vs 87.1% removed after WaveOne Gold + XP-endo Finisher.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

Shim et al. (Kyung Hee University) used micro-CT to ask whether biomineralizing calcium silicate-based sealers (CSBSs) — which adhere to and deposit mineral within dentin — can still be removed during endodontic retreatment. Thirty-six single-oval-canal mandibular premolars were obturated with a single-cone (sealer-based) technique using one of three sealers: the epoxy-resin reference **AH Plus Jet (AHJ)**, **AH Plus Bioceramic (AHB)**, or **Ceraseal (CER)** (n=12 each), set for 3 weeks, then retreated with WaveOne Gold (WOG) files followed by the **XP-endo Finisher (XPF)**. Both CSBSs were *more* retrievable than the epoxy sealer: after WOG + XPF, the mean removed filling volume reached **94.8% (AHB)** and **92.5% (CER)** versus **87.1% (AHJ)**, leaving residual volumes of 0.55, 0.82, and 1.35 mm³ respectively. The supplementary XPF step added meaningful removal, particularly apically. SEM/EDS showed AHJ plugging dentinal-tubule orifices, whereas **CER left numerous mineral deposits** in the tubules (more than AHB), reflecting its di- plus tricalcium silicate composition. The authors conclude AHB and CER are safe, retrievable obturation options, with CER's greater tubular mineral deposition making it a favourable bioceramic choice. According to PubMed, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.3390/jcm14061826).

## Key Contributions

- First micro-CT head-to-head **retrievability** comparison of AHB and Ceraseal against epoxy AH Plus in a single-cone protocol — seeds the wiki's first bioceramic-sealer *retreatment* evidence.
- Refutes the worry that biomineralizing CSBSs are hard to retrieve: both removed **more** material than AH Plus.
- Quantifies the value of **supplementary XP-endo Finisher** in raising removal, especially in the apical region.
- Establishes that **Ceraseal deposits more intra-tubular mineral** than AHB (SEM/EDS), with higher Ca:P ratios for both CSBSs vs AH Plus.

## Methodology

- **Design**: in-vitro, 36 single-oval-canal human mandibular premolars (IRB KH-DT23017), single endodontist operator.
- **Preparation**: crowns reduced to 20 mm; WaveOne Gold to apical #35 (primary #25/7%, medium #35/6%); 3% NaOCl, final 17% EDTA + saline; dried with paper points.
- **Obturation**: single-cone (sealer-based) with WOG medium gutta-percha + AHJ / AHB / CER; set 3 weeks at 36.5 °C, 95% humidity.
- **Retreatment**: WOG primary + medium to working length (apical patency confirmed with #15 K-file), then XPF 1 min at 1000 rpm over 7–8 mm (to 2 mm short of WL), NaOCl warmed to 36 °C.
- **Imaging**: SkyScan 1273 micro-CT (110 kV, 25 µm) at three time points; CTAn 3D volumetry of residual filling (total, middle-cervical, apical 4 mm); FE-SEM + EDS on minimal-residual specimens.
- **Stats**: one-way ANOVA + Tukey, Kruskal-Wallis + Bonferroni, three-way ANOVA (sealer × region × instrument); alpha 5%.

## Results

| Metric (total canal) | AHJ (AH Plus) | AHB | CER (Ceraseal) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Initial filling volume (mm³) | 10.56 ± 2.95 | 10.79 ± 2.73 | 11.26 ± 2.47 (NS) |
| % removed after WOG | 76.0 ± 7.1 | 84.4 ± 7.2 | 81.6 ± 7.7 |
| % removed after WOG + XPF | 87.1 ± 4.4 | 94.8 ± 2.1 | 92.5 ± 3.1 |
| Residual volume after XPF (mm³) | 1.35 | 0.55 | 0.82 |

- After **WOG alone**: no significant overall difference in residual volume; AHB > AHJ for % removed only in the middle-cervical region.
- After **WOG + XPF**: AHB and CER removed significantly more than AHJ in apical, middle-cervical, and total (p < 0.05); AHJ retained the greatest residue (AHJ residual > AHB apically).
- **Apical patency** verified with #15 K-file at retreatment completion.
- Three-way ANOVA: sealer, root region, and instrument each significant; only 'root region × instrument' interaction significant.
- **SEM/EDS**: AHJ plugged tubule orifices; AHB few globular particles; **CER numerous mineral deposits**. AHB/CER higher Ca:P, AHJ higher P:Ca; zirconium/silica not detected in tubules.
- **Limitations**: 3-week retreatment interval (clinically longer → stronger bond), canal not enlarged during retreatment, canals dried before filling (favors epoxy bond).
