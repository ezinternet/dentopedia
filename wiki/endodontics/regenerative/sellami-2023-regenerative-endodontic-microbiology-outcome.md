## Summary
Prospective clinical microbiology sub-study (n=14) from a parent non-randomized controlled cohort (n=29) evaluating REP disinfection at KU Leuven. Published November 2023, Front Dent Med 4:1281337. qPCR measured bacterial load at 4 time points across 2 treatment sessions. Key finding: standard 1.5% NaOCl + 17% EDTA protocol effectively reduces bacterial load; LPRF (leukocyte and platelet-rich fibrin) as scaffold is associated with retreatment and persistent apical periodontitis at 3 years — LPRF NOT recommended.

## Key Contributions
- **Most prevalent species (100% of cases)**: Fusobacterium nucleatum, Treponema denticola, Enterococcus faecalis — all three present in every analyzed root canal
- **Session 1 (1.5% NaOCl)**: significant (p<0.05) total bacterial load reduction; maintained through session 2
- **17% EDTA (session 2)**: further maintained disinfection; smear layer removal component
- **LPRF group adverse outcomes**: 2/14 required retreatment (early flare-up); 2 additional with persistent apical periodontitis at 3-year recall
- **Control group** (no LPRF): all cases clinically asymptomatic; periapices healed radiographically
- **Clinical conclusion**: LPRF should NOT be used as scaffold in REPs

## Methodology
- Design: Non-randomized controlled; microbiology sub-study of 29-patient parent cohort
- Sample collection: S1 (pre-irrigation session 1), S2 (post-1.5% NaOCl/saline), S3 (pre-EDTA session 2), S4 (post-17% EDTA)
- Detection: qPCR with species-specific primers for 10 periodontopathogenic/endodontic species
- Follow-up: 3-year clinical and radiographic recall
