## Summary
Narrative review comparing different REP approaches in immature permanent teeth (IPT) following dental trauma. Front Dent Med 2022, Huazhong University of Science and Technology, Wuhan. Focus on case reports of direct pulp capping, partial pulpotomy, Ca(OH)₂ apexification, MTA apexification, apexogenesis, and pulp revascularization.

Key clinical distinction: apexification = apical barrier formation only (walls remain thin); REP/revascularization = resumed root development + continued dentin deposition → thicker, stronger walls. Dental trauma affects 7.4–58% of children 8–11 years; IPT with thin dentinal walls at high fracture risk if root development halted.

## Key Contributions
- **IPT vulnerability**: thin dentinal walls + large open apex + incomplete mineralization → fracture prone; loss likely if root development permanently arrested
- **Traditional RCT failure for IPT**: removes necrotic pulp but cannot revitalize; walls remain thin; no further root development
- **Goal hierarchy**: apexification → closure only; REP → development resumes
- **Major approaches and comparative outcomes**:

| Approach | Goal | Key Feature | Limitation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ca(OH)₂ apexification | Apical barrier | Long established | Lengthy treatment; ↑ root fracture risk over time |
| MTA apexification | Faster apical closure | 1–2 visits | No root elongation; thin walls persist |
| Partial pulpotomy (MTA/Biodentine) | Preserve vital pulp base | Continued development if vital pulp remains | Requires some vital pulp present |
| Pulp revascularization / REP | Root elongation + wall thickening | Potential full development | Unpredictable; tissue ≠ true pulp-dentin |
| Apexogenesis | Full root development | Ideal outcome | Limited to partial necrosis |

- **REP prerequisites**: adequate disinfection + scaffold (blood clot / PRP / PRF) + biocompatible coronal barrier (MTA / Biodentine)
- Histological reality: "regenerated" tissue often cementum/bone-like, not true pulp-dentin complex

## Methodology
- Narrative review of case reports and small clinical studies
- Comparison of advantages and limitations across REP approaches
- Evidence level: predominantly case reports and small series (low)
