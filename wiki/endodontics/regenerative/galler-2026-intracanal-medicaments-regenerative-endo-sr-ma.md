## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 24 studies (n=791) finds TAP (96.7%) and Ca(OH)₂ (97.4%) equivalent for 1-year REP success in permanent necrotic immature teeth, both superior to DAP (84.2%); GRADE high for TAP/CH, low for DAP.

## Summary

This SR+MA (Alovisi, Arduino, Petti 2026, *Int Endod J* 59:1337–1357) is the first to quantitatively compare the three main intracanal medicament protocols used in regenerative endodontic procedures (REPs) for permanent necrotic immature teeth. Twenty-four studies of average quality (19 TAP, 3 DAP, 6 CH) were identified through December 2025, encompassing 791 patients (TAP n=544, DAP n=64, CH n=183).

The protocol addresses an unresolved clinical gap: both the ESE (Galler et al. 2016) and AAE (2021) recommend TAP, DAP, or CH without specifying which is superior, leaving clinicians to choose. Because no trial has directly randomised all three protocols against each other, the authors employed naïve indirect comparison with a predefined equivalence margin of ±2.5% (derived from the 95% CI range of the overall REP success estimate of 95.6% from Li et al. 2023).

The central finding: TAP and calcium hydroxide (CH) are clinically equivalent — pooled difference −0.7% (95% CI −0.9% to +2.5%), entirely within the ±2.5% equivalence margin. Both TAP and CH are superior to DAP by approximately 12–13 percentage points. GRADE evidence is high for TAP and CH, but only low for DAP given its small sample (n=64, 3 studies only) and serious imprecision.

The REP disinfection phase is critical: 79% of failed REP cases show persistent infection (Almutairi et al. 2019), and thin immature dentinal walls preclude aggressive mechanical instrumentation — making chemical disinfection the decisive step. This SR+MA confirms that the choice between TAP and CH can be made on secondary criteria (discoloration risk, antibiotic stewardship, SCAP toxicity profile) rather than efficacy, since their 1-year success rates are statistically indistinguishable.

## Key Contributions

1. **TAP = CH for REP**: First pooled quantification demonstrating statistical equivalence between triple antibiotic paste and calcium hydroxide at 1-year follow-up (difference −0.7%, within ±2.5% margin); clinicians may choose between them based on secondary factors
2. **DAP inferiority confirmed**: Both TAP (96.7%) and CH (97.4%) outperform DAP (84.2%) by ~13 percentage points — a clinically and statistically significant gap; DAP falls to 84% due to limited disinfection depth in dentinal tubules and possible ciprofloxacin impairment of angiogenesis
3. **GRADE-differentiated guidance**: High confidence for TAP and CH; low confidence for DAP — signals need for dedicated DAP trials before routine use can be endorsed
4. **Scaffold type does not alter 1-year clinical success**: Subgroup analysis found no significant difference between blood clot, PRP, PRF, and collagen membrane scaffolds for primary outcome (consistent with Sabeti et al. 2024)
5. **Literature through Dec 2025**: Most current synthesis of REP intracanal medicament evidence available as of 2026; 791 patients across 24 studies

## Methodology

- **Study type**: Systematic review + naïve indirect-comparison meta-analysis
- **PROSPERO**: CRD42023484189
- **Databases**: MEDLINE, Scopus, Embase, Web of Science, Google Scholar (to December 2025)
- **Population**: Permanent necrotic immature teeth treated with REP, ≥12 months follow-up
- **Comparators**: TAP (ciprofloxacin + metronidazole + minocycline), DAP (ciprofloxacin + metronidazole), CH (calcium hydroxide)
- **Primary outcome**: 1-year clinical success — asymptomatic tooth, not tender to percussion, no sinus tract/swelling, radiographic absence/reduction of periapical radiolucency
- **Secondary outcomes**: Root wall thickening/lengthening, apical closure, pulp sensitivity — deliberately excluded from pooled analysis due to heterogeneity and contested clinical meaning
- **Risk of bias**: Revised Cochrane RoB2; 24 studies rated average quality; no serious risk-of-bias limitations across TAP/CH arms
- **Heterogeneity**: Cochran's Q + I²; random-effects model preferred when I² > 50%
- **Statistical handling**: Double arcsine transformation used (conventional variance = 0 when proportion = 1, as occurred in several 100%-success studies)
- **Publication bias**: Peters regression on log odds vs inverse sample size; estimates adjusted for bias
- **Equivalence analysis**: Fixed-effect model; ΔEQ ±2.5% from mixed clinical-statistical derivation (one-half the 95% CI range of overall pooled success from Li et al. 2023)
- **Sensitivity analyses**: Study quality, publication bias, leave-one-out study inclusion, meta-analytic method
- **Subgroup analyses**: Scaffold type (blood clot vs other), world region
- **Evidence grading**: GRADE (5 downgrade domains evaluated; recommendations deliberately not issued)
- **Indirect comparison design**: Naïve method used (no common comparator available for network MA)

## Results

| Arm | Studies | Patients | Design |

| TAP | 19 | 544 | RCT + NRSI |
| DAP | 3 | 64 | RCT + NRSI |
| CH | 6 | 183 | RCT + NRSI |
| Total | 24 | 791 | |

### Pooled 1-Year Success Rates

| Medicament | Pooled Success | 95% CI | GRADE |
|---|---|---|---|
| TAP | 96.7% | 94.8%–98.0% | High ⨁⨁⨁⨁ |
| DAP | 84.2% | 73.2%–92.0% | Low ⨁⨁◯◯ |
| CH | 97.4% | 93.9%–99.1% | High ⨁⨁⨁⨁ |

### Pairwise Comparisons (Naïve Indirect, Equivalence Margin ±2.5%)

| Comparison | Difference | 95% CI | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|
| TAP vs DAP | +12.5% | 2.8%–22.1% | TAP superior |
| TAP vs CH | −0.7% | −0.9% to +2.5% | Equivalent |
| CH vs DAP | +13.2% | 3.3%–23.0% | CH superior |

### Blood Clot Subgroup (reduced power)

| Comparison | Difference | 95% CI | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|
| TAP vs DAP | +11.0% | 0.8% to 21.3% | Superiority not shown |
| TAP vs CH | +2.0% | −2.8% to +6.8% | Equivalence not shown |
| CH vs DAP | +9.0% | −1.9% to +19.9% | Superiority not shown |

Note: Blood clot subgroup results non-significant due to reduced power after stratification, not because the direction reversed.

### Key Secondary Observations

- Sensitivity and subgroup analyses consistently corroborated primary results
- DAP GRADE downgraded for serious consistency limitations (only 3 studies) and serious imprecision (n=64, wide CI)
- Study sample sizes ranged 7–76 patients; geographic concentration in Middle East and Asia
- No studies from Europe, Oceania, Africa — limits external validity
- All three medications present clinical drawbacks: TAP/DAP ciprofloxacin may impair angiogenesis; minocycline in TAP causes crown discoloration; CH may have limited bactericidal depth in dentinal tubules
