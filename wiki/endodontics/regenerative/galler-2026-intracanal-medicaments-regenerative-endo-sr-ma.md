## Summary

This SR+MA (Alovisi et al. 2026, *Int Endod J*) is the first to quantitatively compare the three main intracanal medicament protocols used in regenerative endodontic procedures (REPs) for permanent necrotic immature teeth. Twenty-four studies of average quality were identified through December 2025, encompassing 791 patients (TAP n=544, DAP n=64, CH n=183). Using naïve indirect comparison (no common head-to-head comparator available), the authors calculated pooled 1-year success rates and applied an equivalence margin of ±2.5%.

The central finding: TAP and calcium hydroxide (CH) are equivalent — pooled difference −0.7% (95% CI −0.9% to +2.5%), entirely within the equivalence margin. Both TAP and CH are superior to DAP by approximately 12–13 percentage points. GRADE evidence is high for TAP and CH, but only low for DAP given its small sample and few studies.

The absence of direct protocol comparisons and the naïve (vs. network) meta-analytic design are the primary methodological limitations. Despite this, sensitivity and subgroup analyses (scaffold type, world region) consistently corroborated the primary results.

## Key Contributions

1. **TAP = CH for REP**: First pooled quantification demonstrating statistical equivalence between TAP and calcium hydroxide at 1-year follow-up (difference −0.7%, within ±2.5% margin)
2. **DAP inferiority confirmed**: Both TAP (96.7%) and CH (97.4%) outperform DAP (84.2%) by ~13 percentage points — a clinically and statistically significant gap
3. **GRADE-differentiated guidance**: High confidence for TAP and CH recommendations; DAP evidence remains low — signals need for dedicated DAP trials
4. **Literature through Dec 2025**: Most current synthesis of REP medicament evidence available as of 2026

## Methodology

- **Study type**: Systematic review + naïve indirect-comparison meta-analysis
- **PROSPERO**: CRD42023484189
- **Databases**: MEDLINE, Scopus, Embase, Web of Science, Google Scholar (to December 2025)
- **Population**: Permanent necrotic immature teeth treated with REP
- **Comparators**: TAP (ciprofloxacin + metronidazole + minocycline), DAP (ciprofloxacin + metronidazole), CH (calcium hydroxide)
- **Primary outcome**: 1-year clinical success (tooth survival + periapical healing + absence of signs/symptoms)
- **Risk of bias**: Revised Cochrane RoB2
- **Heterogeneity**: Cochran's Q + I²; random-effects model for I² > 50%
- **Equivalence margin**: ±2.5% for success-rate differences
- **Evidence grading**: GRADE
- **Sensitivity analyses**: study quality, publication bias, study inclusion
- **Subgroup analyses**: scaffold type, world region
- **Note**: abstract-only — full text not retrieved

## Results

| Medicament | n | Pooled 1-yr Success | 95% CI | GRADE |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| TAP | 544 | 96.7% | 94.8%–98.0% | High |
| DAP | 64 | 84.2% | 73.2%–92.0% | Low |
| CH | 183 | 97.4% | 93.9%–99.1% | High |

| Comparison | Difference | 95% CI | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|
| TAP vs DAP | +12.5% | 2.8%–22.1% | TAP superior |
| TAP vs CH | −0.7% | −0.9%–+2.5% | Equivalent |
| CH vs DAP | +13.2% | 3.3%–23.0% | CH superior |

- Total included studies: 24 (average quality by RoB2)
- Sensitivity and subgroup analyses corroborated primary findings
- Statistical power for DAP comparisons limited by small sample
