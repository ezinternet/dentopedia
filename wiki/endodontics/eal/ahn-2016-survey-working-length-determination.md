## 한줄요약
설문조사(치과대학): EAL 89%가 근관장 측정 우선; 방사선적 근단 0.5–1.0mm 단에서 근관장 설정(78%)

## Summary
A questionnaire survey of endodontic faculty at Korean dental schools (90% response rate) found that **89% prefer electronic apex locators (EAL)** over radiography for working length determination, and **78% favor an apical limit of 0.5–1.0 mm short of the radiographic apex**. When EAL and radiographic measurements conflict, 74% trust the EAL result. The most common EAL protocol: read the APEX mark, then subtract 0.5 mm (41%).

## Key Contributions
- Establishes Korean academic consensus on EAL as the primary working length tool
- Apical limit of choice: **0.5–1.0 mm short of radiographic apex** (CDJ zone)
- When readings conflict, EAL takes priority over radiograph in 74% of cases
- Preferred EAL reading: `APEX mark − 0.5 mm`

## Methodology
- Cross-sectional questionnaire distributed to endodontic teachers at multiple Korean dental schools
- Two-domain survey: (1) working length method preference; (2) apical limit preference
- Response rate: 90%

## Results

| Clinical question | Dominant answer | % |
|---|---|---|
| Preferred WL determination method | Electronic apex locator (EAL) | 89% |
| Preferred apical limit | 0.5–1.0 mm short of radiographic apex | 78% |
| Preferred EAL reading | APEX mark − 0.5 mm | 41% |
| EAL vs. radiograph conflict → prefer | EAL measurement | 74% |

**Interpretation**: EAL is accepted as the primary reference; radiograph is used for confirmation, not as the deciding factor.
