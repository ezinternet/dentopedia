## 한줄요약
Ex vivo 10개 치아(근첨공 0.6–0.8mm): 파일 직경이 근첨공 직경에 근접할수록 EAL 정확도·안정성 모두 향상; SAF도 유효; 광근첨공 시 파일 매칭이 핵심

## Summary
Ex vivo study (10 teeth, IDF Medical Corps Israel) evaluating EAL accuracy and stability in wide apical foramina (0.6–0.8mm). Introduces **measurement stability** (constant reading ≥5 seconds) as a new validity parameter. Key finding: accuracy and stability both improve as file size approaches foramen diameter — and the two parameters correlate significantly.

## Key Findings

| File-to-Foramen Diameter Match | EAL Accuracy | EAL Stability |
|---|---|---|
| Large gap (undersized file) | ↓ Low | ↓ Unstable |
| Close match | ↑ High | ↑ Stable |
| SAF (self-adapting) | ↑ High | ↑ Stable |

- Accuracy and stability **statistically correlated** (p<0.05)
- Clinical implication: **unstable reading = inaccurate reading** — use stability as a warning sign

## Why Wide Foramen Is Dangerous for EAL
- EAL impedance measurement depends on file contact with canal walls and foramen
- Undersized file in wide foramen → poor contact → fluctuating impedance → unstable/long reading → risk of over-instrumentation

## Clinical Protocol Implication
- **Widen to a snug file** before trusting EAL reading in wide foramina
- If reading is unstable for <5 seconds: do not trust the measurement
- SAF is a valid alternative in wide, straight canals
