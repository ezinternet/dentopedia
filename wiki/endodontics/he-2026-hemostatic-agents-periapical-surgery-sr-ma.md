## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 10 RCTs/CCTs (388 teeth) on hemostatic agents in periapical surgery: pooled efficacy 63 ± 6.6%, no significant between-agent differences overall, but network meta-analysis shows epinephrine and aluminum chloride superior to collagen (P < .05); smoking trends toward significance (P = .074); hemostatic efficacy does not predict prognosis.

## Summary

This 2026 SR+MA (Genchi et al., *J Endod* 52:850-860) is the first to systematically pool hemostatic agent efficacy data from periapical surgery trials. Searching PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, and Cochrane Library through October 2024, the authors identified 10 studies (388 teeth) meeting inclusion criteria (RCTs and CCTs, ≥12-month follow-up). Three complementary meta-analytic approaches were applied: global pooled effect, meta-regression, and network meta-analysis.

The pooled hemostatic efficacy was **63 ± 6.6%**. Head-to-head comparisons via global meta-analysis and meta-regression found no statistically significant differences among agents (epinephrine, ferric sulfate, aluminum chloride, bone wax, tranexamic acid, collagen). However, the **network meta-analysis** — which leverages both direct and indirect evidence — found epinephrine and aluminum chloride to be statistically superior to collagen (P < .05).

No relationship was detected between hemostatic efficacy and periapical surgery prognosis. Among patient- and tooth-related variables, only **smoking** showed a trend toward significance (P = .074).

**Clinical bottom line**: All commonly used hemostatic agents perform similarly (~63% efficacy). Collagen appears to be the weakest option per network MA. Agent choice may be guided by cost, availability, and tissue safety profile rather than differential efficacy. Ferric sulfate (황산제이철, Ferric Sulfate) vs. epinephrine (에피네프린, Epinephrine) remains a practical debate unresolved by this SR+MA — neither was significantly different from the other.

## Key Contributions

- Quantified **pooled hemostatic efficacy = 63 ± 6.6%** across periapical surgery trials (first such pooled estimate).
- Network MA identifies **epinephrine and AlCl₃ > collagen** (P < .05); no other pairwise significant differences.
- Demonstrated **no hemostatic efficacy–prognosis link** — high intraoperative hemostasis does not guarantee better healing outcomes.
- **Smoking** flagged as the only variable with near-significant effect (P = .074) — clinically relevant for patient selection/counseling.
- Establishes baseline evidence quality: 10 studies, 388 teeth — evidence base is small, highlighting need for larger RCTs.

## Methodology

- **Design**: SR+MA, PRISMA-compliant
- **Databases**: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, Cochrane Library (to October 2024)
- **Included study types**: RCTs + controlled clinical trials
- **Prognosis follow-up threshold**: ≥12 months
- **Risk of bias**: RoB2 (RCTs), ROBINS-I-V2 (CCTs)
- **Meta-analysis approaches**:
  1. Global pooled effect meta-analysis
  2. Meta-regression (clinical covariates)
  3. Network meta-analysis (comparative rankings)
- **N**: 10 studies, 388 teeth
- **Agents evaluated**: epinephrine, ferric sulfate, aluminum chloride, bone wax, tranexamic acid, collagen
