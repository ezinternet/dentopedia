## Summary

Gaafar et al. (2025, BMC Oral Health) tested the biocompatibility of two premixed calcium-silicate **bioceramic root-canal sealers — CeraSeal (MetaBiomed, South Korea) and NeoSEALER Flo (Avalon BioMed/Nusmile, USA)** — against the AH Plus epoxy-resin gold standard, using human gingival fibroblasts (HGFs, line HGF-1 / ATCC CRL-2014). HGFs are the first cells to meet sealer that extrudes past the apex or leaks, so their viability and inflammatory reaction are a relevant safety readout. Sealer eluates were tested at 25%, 50%, 75%, 100% (v/v) over 1, 3, and 7 days, with cell viability measured by MTT and IL-6/IL-8/TNF-α measured at both gene-expression (qRT-PCR, 2^-ΔΔCt vs GAPDH) and cytokine-protein (pg/mL) levels.

Both bioceramics produced **concentration- and time-dependent cytotoxicity** and upregulated all three pro-inflammatory cytokines. **NeoSEALER Flo was slightly more cytotoxic and more pro-inflammatory than CeraSeal**, attributed to its composition and faster setting kinetics (more leached byproducts / reactive species). At the cytokine-protein level the epoxy-resin control **AH Plus produced the highest levels overall**. The null hypothesis was rejected. The authors recommend CeraSeal where minimal tissue irritation is critical (apical periodontitis, compromised periapical tissue, high sealer-extrusion risk), while stressing that all bioceramic sealers carry inherent cytotoxic/pro-inflammatory potential and require careful selection and precise placement.

## Key Contributions

- First wiki page on **CeraSeal HGF biocompatibility / inflammatory response**, with a direct CeraSeal vs NeoSEALER Flo head-to-head plus AH Plus reference.
- Biocompatibility ranking for HGFs: **CeraSeal > NeoSEALER Flo**; both bioceramics below AH Plus on protein-level cytokine output.
- Pairs viability (MTT) with mechanistic cytokine data (IL-6, IL-8, TNF-α) at gene and protein level.

## Methodology

- **Design**: in-vitro; Group A AH Plus (control), Group N NeoSEALER Flo, Group C CeraSeal.
- **Cells**: HGF-1 (ATCC CRL-2014); DMEM + 10% FBS + 1% pen-strep; 37 °C / 5% CO₂; 1×10⁴ cells/well in 96-well plates.
- **Eluates**: Teflon disks 5 mm × 2 mm, 10/group, UV-sterilized, incubated 24 h, filtered (0.22 μm); concentrations 25/50/75/100%; exposure 1/3/7 days.
- **Cytotoxicity**: MTT (OD 570 nm) → viability %.
- **Inflammation**: qRT-PCR (2^-ΔΔCt, GAPDH) for IL-6/IL-8/TNF-α; cytokine protein in pg/mL.
- **Sample size**: PASS 15, α 0.05, power 80% → 60 discs (10/subgroup). Triplicate experiments.
- **Stats**: SPSS v25; Shapiro-Wilk; one-way ANOVA + Tukey; Kruskal-Wallis; p<0.05. IRB Sinai University (Egypt) SU.REC.2024 (12).

## Results

**Cytotoxicity (concentration):** dose-dependent (p<0.05). At 100% eluate, NeoSEALER Flo reduced HGF viability by **45%**, CeraSeal by **35%**.

**Cell viability % (mean ± SD):**

| Day | NeoSEALER Flo | CeraSeal | AH Plus | Between-group |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 88.78 ± 3.84 | 93.32 ± 2.88 | 87.95 ± 5.53 | p=0.317 (NS) |
| 3 | 78.02 ± 1.89 | 82.74 ± 2.56 | 77.81 ± 4.49 | p=0.18 (NS) |
| 7 | 71.89 ± 3.44 | 75.43 ± 2.14 | 69.80 ± 1.97 | CeraSeal > AH Plus, p=0.028 |

Between-material differences mostly NS; CeraSeal viability significantly exceeded AH Plus on **day 7** (p=0.028). Within each material, viability fell significantly across days.

**Pro-inflammatory cytokines at 100% (pg/mL):**

| Cytokine | NeoSEALER Flo | CeraSeal | AH Plus |
|---|---|---|---|
| IL-6 | 150 ± 12 | 130 ± 10 | 190 ± 20 |
| IL-8 | 120 ± 10 | 100 ± 8 | 140 ± 8 |
| TNF-α | 90 ± 6 | 80 ± 5 | 100 ± 4 |

Cytokine output rose with concentration in all groups (p<0.05); AH Plus highest at the protein level.

**Gene expression at 100% (fold change):** NeoSEALER Flo IL-6 3.5 / IL-8 2.8 / TNF-α 2.2; CeraSeal IL-6 3.0 / IL-8 2.5 / TNF-α 1.9; AH Plus IL-6 4.2 / IL-8 3.2 / TNF-α 2.7. AH Plus vs both bioceramics significant (p=0.013, 0.006); **the two bioceramics did not differ significantly (p>0.05)** — CeraSeal showed the least expression. Null hypothesis rejected.
