## One-line Summary
PRISMA-ScR scoping review (10 studies from 3,697 records): conservative access cavities (ConsAC) trend toward more residual debris, while traditional/guided access (TradAC) offers superior canal orifice localization precision.

## 한줄요약

## Summary
PRISMA-ScR scoping review, 10 studies from 3,697 records. Compares **ConsAC vs TradAC** on canal orifice localization and debris presence. Trend: conservative = more debris; traditional/guided = superior orifice detection precision.

## ConsAC vs TradAC

| Design | Canal Orifice Localization | Debris |
|---|---|---|
| **TradAC** (traditional/guided) | ↑ Superior precision | Less |
| **ConsAC** (ninja/truss/contracted) | Harder — less dentin removed | **More** debris potential |

## Clinical Implication
- Conservative access preserves tooth structure but may **compromise visibility of canal orifices** and leave more debris
- When MB2 or calcified canals expected: traditional access + magnification preferred
- ConsAC = appropriate for straightforward single-canal teeth; risk increases with complex anatomy

## Evidence Caveat
- Only 10 studies, high heterogeneity — **trend, not conclusive evidence**
- Most studies in vitro/ex vivo; clinical validity limited
