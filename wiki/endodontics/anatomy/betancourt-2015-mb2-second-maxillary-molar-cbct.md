## One-line Summary
Cross-sectional in vivo CBCT study (n=225 Chilean maxillary second molars): MB2 prevalence was 48%, with PMB2 located ~2.2 mm palatal and ~0.98 mm mesial to PMB1 and more frequent in males (63%); serves as the methodological precursor to the same group's larger 2016 1,100-molar study.

## 한줄요약
Cross-sectional CBCT (n=225 Chilean 2nd maxillary molars): MB2 prevalence 48%; PMB2 located 2.2 mm palatal + 0.98 mm mesial to PMB1; more frequent in males (63%); precursor to the same group's 2016 1,100-molar study.

## Summary
Earlier same-group CBCT study limited to the maxillary second molar. The MB2 canal was identified in 48% of 225 teeth, with consistent geometric offsets from PMB1 (palatal and mesial). Establishes the measurement protocol later scaled up in the 2016 1,100-molar paper.

## Key Contributions
- 2MM-only prevalence baseline in a Chilean cohort
- PMB2 offset relative to PMB1 expressed as both palatal and mesial components
- Methodological precursor to the larger 2016 study

## Methodology
- Design: cross-sectional in vivo CBCT analysis
- Sample: 225 permanent maxillary second molars
- Reference plane: 1 mm apical to pulpal floor
- Measurements: PMB1-PMB2 distance, palatal/mesial decomposition, T point (perpendicular from PMB1-P line)
- Statistics: descriptive + chi-square, α=0.05

## Results
- MB2 prevalence: **48%** (108/225)
- PMB2 location: 2.2 ± 0.54 mm palatal + 0.98 ± 0.35 mm mesial to PMB1
- Mean age: 26.36 ± 10.85 years
- Sex: more frequent in males (63%)
- No significant hemi-arch difference

## Limitations
- Single Chilean population, single tooth type
- Sample size modest (n=225)
- Voxel size / inter-observer reliability not reported
