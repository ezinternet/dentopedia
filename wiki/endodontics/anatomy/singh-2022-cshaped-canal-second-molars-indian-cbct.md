## One-line Summary

CBCT study of 500 Indian patients (Greater Noida): overall C-shaped canal prevalence 22% in second molars (maxillary 25.1% vs mandibular 18.9%, NS); most common configuration continuous/Category 1 (52.7%), then semicolon (37.3%) and separate (10%).

## 한줄요약

## Summary

This Indian-subpopulation cross-sectional CBCT study reports a notably high overall C-shaped-canal prevalence of 22% in second molars — substantially higher than the low Israeli (4.6% mandibular second molar, Shemesh 2017) and Iranian (2%, Shekarian 2023) sibling figures, underscoring how much C-shaped prevalence varies by population and counting method. Unusually, it covers both maxillary and mandibular second molars in the same cohort, finding the maxillary second molar slightly higher (25.1% vs 18.9%) but not significantly so. Using the Melton classification, the continuous (Category 1) configuration was the most common, and the distribution across configuration types was statistically significant. The high headline figure is partly a function of per-patient (not per-tooth) reporting and classification choices, so it should be compared cautiously with per-tooth studies.

## Key Contributions

- Fills a gap in Indian C-shaped prevalence data for second molars.
- Reports maxillary and mandibular second molars together (no significant jaw-type difference).
- Provides a Melton-category configuration distribution (continuous dominant).

## Methodology

- 500 archival CBCT records (CS 9300, 0.18 mm isotropic voxel), ages 15–40.
- Inclusion: second molars with fully formed apices, no resorption; standard exclusions (prior RCT, immature apices, pathology).
- Two calibrated endodontists scored twice (2-month interval) per Melton categories; radiology specialist arbitrated; χ² analysis.

## Results

- **Overall C-shaped prevalence: 22%** (110/500 patients).
- Maxillary second molar **25.1%** vs mandibular second molar **18.9%** — NOT significant (χ²=3.87, P=0.276).
- Configuration (of 110 C-shaped): continuous/Cat 1 **52.7%**, semicolon/Cat 2 **37.3%**, separate/Cat 3 **10%** — significant (χ²=8.26, P=0.024).
