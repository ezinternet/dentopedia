## One-line Summary

Case series (n=2) of mandibular right second molar C-shaped canals (Melton type I/C1 and type II/C2) managed under operating microscope with ultrasonic irrigation, MTA apical plug (case 1), and thermoplasticized gutta-percha; asymptomatic at 1-year recall.

## Summary

Two mandibular right second molar C-shaped cases treated under a dental operating microscope, illustrating that direct visualization is the practical key ("see-through C") to negotiating, debriding, and obturating this anatomy. Case 1 had a **C1 (continuous) orifice** with a single common apical exit and was complicated by mid-treatment loss of the apical constriction, salvaged with an **orthograde MTA apical plug** before Thermafil obturation. Case 2 had a **C2 (semicolon) orifice** with a separate mesiolingual canal. Both used 5.25% NaOCl + 17% EDTA with ultrasonic file activation and thermoplasticized gutta-percha; both were asymptomatic with no periapical lesion at 1-year recall. The series is a chairside worked-example of the classification and management principles catalogued in the C-shaped literature.

## Key Contributions

- **Microscope-guided management** of two distinct C-shaped orifice types (C1, C2) in mandibular second molars.
- **MTA apical plug** as the rescue for a lost apical constriction in a thin-walled C-shaped canal (case 1).
- Reinforces **ultrasonic irrigation (NaOCl + EDTA) + thermoplasticized obturation** as the workflow for cleaning/sealing C-shaped fins.

## Methodology

Two-case clinical series. IOPA radiograph (single conical root) + microscope confirmation after access (Möller Wedel ×2–×24). ProFile rotary + circumferential hand filing + ultrasonic activation; 5.25% NaOCl + 17% EDTA alternated. Obturation: Thermafil thermoplasticized GP; case 1 with ProRoot MTA apical barrier. Outcome by clinical + radiographic 1-year recall.

## Results

- **Case 1** (45 M, C1): single common apical exit; MTA plug after constriction breakage; asymptomatic at 1 mo and 1 yr, no periapical lesion / no PDL widening.
- **Case 2** (32 F, C2): separate mesiolingual canal; asymptomatic at 1 mo and 1 yr (later lost to follow-up).
- Classified per **Melton with Fan's modification** (C1–C5).
