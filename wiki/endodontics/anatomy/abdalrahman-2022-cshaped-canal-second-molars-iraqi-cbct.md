## One-line Summary

CBCT cross-sectional study (368 mandibular + 369 maxillary second molars, Iraqi subpopulation): C-shaped canal prevalence 17.4% in mandibular vs 7.9% in maxillary second molars; significantly higher in females (23% vs 10.4%, p=0.001); C2 most common mandibular type (56.3%).

## 한줄요약

CBCT 횡단 연구(이라크 인구, 하악 제2대구치 368개·상악 제2대구치 369개): C-shaped 근관 유병률 하악 17.4% vs 상악 7.9%, 여성에서 유의하게 높음(23% vs 10.4%, p=0.001); 하악은 C2형(56.3%)이 가장 흔함.

## Summary

This retrospective CBCT study evaluated C-shaped canal morphology in an Iraqi subpopulation, covering both mandibular (n=368) and maxillary (n=369) second molars in the same cohort. Mandibular second molars showed a 17.4% C-shaped canal prevalence, with a strong and significant female predominance (23% vs 10.4% in males, p=0.001). Maxillary second molars showed fused-root ("C-shaped") configurations in 7.9%, with MB-DB fusion the dominant type. Prevalence did not vary significantly by age or tooth position. Mandibular C-shaped canals were predominantly bilateral, whereas maxillary fused roots were predominantly unilateral. The data extend C-shaped canal epidemiology to a Middle Eastern population.

## Key Contributions

- Same-cohort mandibular AND maxillary second molar C-shaped/fused-root prevalence in an Iraqi subpopulation.
- Confirms significant female predominance of mandibular C-shaped canals (p=0.001).
- Laterality contrast: mandibular predominantly bilateral; maxillary predominantly unilateral.
- C2 dominant mandibular configuration (56.3%); MB-DB fusion dominant maxillary type (65.5%).

## Methodology

- Retrospective cross-sectional CBCT (GALILEOS Sirona comfort PLUS; 0.125–0.25 mm voxel).
- Inclusion: Iraqi patients >18y, mature apex, no caries/filling/post-crown.
- Mandibular classification: Fan et al. modified Melton (C1–C5); maxillary: Jo et al. 9-type root fusion.
- Two observers (endodontist + radiologist), interexaminer kappa = 0.87.
- Chi-square / Fisher's exact tests, p ≤ 0.05, SPSS v25.

## Results

- Mandibular second molars: 17.4% C-shaped (64/368). Female 23% vs Male 10.4% (p=0.001). No age (p=0.734) or side (p=0.747) difference.
  - Configuration: C2 56.3%, C3 23.4%, C1 15.6%, C4 4.7%.
- Maxillary second molars: 7.9% fused-root C-shaped; MB-DB fusion 65.5% (most common).
- Laterality: mandibular mostly bilateral; maxillary mostly unilateral.
- Mandibular > maxillary second-molar prevalence.
