## One-line Summary

CBCT study of 356 mandibular premolars (149 western Chinese patients): first premolars 98% one-rooted (Vertucci Type I 86.8%, Type V 9.8%), with C-shaped canals in only 1.1% of first and 0.6% of second premolars.

## 한줄요약

하악 소구치 356개(서부 중국인 149명) CBCT 분석 — 제1소구치 98% 단일근, Vertucci Type I(86.8%)·Type V(9.8%) 주류, C-shaped canal은 제1소구치 1.1%·제2소구치 0.6%로 드묾.

## Summary

This cross-sectional CBCT study evaluated root and canal morphology of mandibular first and second premolars in a western Chinese population (a region with high non-Han ethnic diversity). The clinically salient point is that while mandibular premolars are notoriously difficult to treat endodontically because of canal variation, in this population the dominant pattern is a single root with a single Vertucci Type I canal, and true C-shaped configurations are rare (about 1%). Most canal bifurcations, when present, occur in the middle-to-apical third — exactly where they are hardest to negotiate. The study extends C-shaped/canal-morphology prevalence work beyond the usual mandibular-second-molar focus to the premolar dentition.

## Key Contributions

- Population-specific CBCT prevalence of root/canal number and Vertucci type for mandibular premolars in western Chinese individuals.
- Documented low C-shaped prevalence in premolars (1.1% first, 0.6% second), contrasting sharply with an earlier Taiwanese report of 18% in first premolars.
- Established that canal bifurcation predominantly occurs at the middle-apical third, informing access and shaping strategy.

## Methodology

- 149 archival CBCT scans (3D Accuitomo, 80 kV/5.0 mA, 0.125 mm voxel, 1.0 mm slice) → 178 first + 178 second premolars.
- Inclusion: fully erupted, mature apices, no fillings/posts/crowns.
- Two endodontists scored roots, canals, bifurcation level, and Vertucci configuration by consensus; oral radiologist arbitrated disagreements.

## Results

- **First premolars (n=178)**: one root 98%, two roots 2%; one canal 87.1%, two canals 11.2%, three canals 0.6%; C-shaped 1.1%. Vertucci Type I 86.8%, Type III 1.7%, Type V 9.8%, Type VIII 0.6%.
- **Second premolars (n=178)**: all single-rooted; one canal 97.2%, two canals 2.2%; C-shaped 0.6%. Vertucci Type I 97.2%, Type II 0.55%, Type V 1.7%.
- Canal bifurcation in Type V/VIII teeth clustered in the middle-apical third (87% first, 75% second premolar variations).
