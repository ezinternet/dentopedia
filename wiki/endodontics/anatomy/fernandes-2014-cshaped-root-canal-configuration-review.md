## One-line Summary

Narrative review (PubMed 1979–2012) synthesizing etiology, incidence, anatomy, classification (Melton/Fan), diagnosis, and management of C-shaped root canals, predominantly in mandibular second molars.

## 한줄요약

C-shaped 근관(주로 하악 제2대구치)의 병인·발생률·해부·분류(Melton/Fan)·진단·치료를 종합한 서술적 문헌고찰(PubMed 1979–2012).

## Summary

This narrative review is the conceptual backbone for the C-shaped canal topic: it gathers the etiology, the ethnic-predilection incidence data, the anatomy, the competing classification systems, and the stepwise diagnosis-and-management workflow into one reference. The C-shaped configuration arises when **Hertwig's epithelial root sheath fails to fuse** on the buccal or lingual root surface, leaving a longitudinal groove and a fused (conical/square) root; this is now considered genetic rather than traumatic, consistent with the strong racial predilection. It is most common in mandibular second molars (2.7–45.5%), with the highest rates in East Asian populations (Korean 31.3–45.5%, Chinese up to 41.27%), and is bilateral in 70–81% of cases.

## Key Contributions

- **Etiology** — HERS fusion failure; buccal-fusion → C closed to lingual, lingual-fusion → C closed to buccal.
- **Incidence table** — ethnic predilection; mandibular second molar 2.7–45.5%; also premolars, maxillary/mandibular third molars; no age/gender correlation.
- **Classification** — Cooke & Cox (1979 first description), Manning, Melton (C1–C3 orifice), Fan (C1–C5 cross-section), Fan radiographic types I–III.
- **Diagnosis** — preoperative radiograph clues, working-length radiograph (most useful), microscope clinical exam; combined yield highest (41.27%).
- **Management** — modified access, file count by orifice shape, small-file exploration (#8/10/15), ultrasonic/sonic-assisted debridement of fins, modified obturation for 3-D fill.

## Methodology

Narrative review. Manual + electronic PubMed search 1979–2012 (keywords: C-shaped canals, mandibular second molar, mandibular first premolar, root canal morphology). Qualitative synthesis with a compiled incidence table; no meta-analysis or risk-of-bias appraisal.

## Results

- Mandibular second molar prevalence **2.7–45.5%**; Korean 31.3–45.5%, Chinese 0.6–41.27%.
- **Minimum canal wall** thinner on lingual; lowest measured **0.26 mm** (strip-perforation risk).
- Accessory/lateral canals, inter-canal communications, apical delta in **11–41%** of the apical region.
- **Bilateral** occurrence 70–81%.
- C-shaped premolar grooves start ~3 mm below CEJ, mean depth ~1.5 mm.
