## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 22 studies on maxillary canine (MxC) root canal configuration: Vertucci Type I (1-1-1/1) dominates at 75.4–100%, anatomical variation reaches up to ~25% of cases, CBCT is the most-used method, and males show significantly more Vertucci III (OR 2.07) and V (OR 2.93) configurations.

## Summary

This is the first systematic review + meta-analysis dedicated to the internal morphology and root canal configuration (RCC) of **maxillary canines (MxC)**. Across 22 included studies (PRISMA-compliant, PROSPERO CRD42023394478), the single-canal **Vertucci Type I (1-1-1/1)** configuration dominates (75.4–100%), confirming the clinical default expectation of one canal from pulp chamber to apex. However, the key clinical message is the long tail: **up to roughly 25% of maxillary canines carry anatomical variation** — second canals, connecting canals, or accessory canals that cannot be mechanically instrumented — which makes adequate chemical irrigation and careful obturation decisive for outcome. CBCT is the most frequently used research method; micro-CT remains the ex vivo gold standard but is unsuitable for in vivo human use due to radiation.

## Key Contributions

- Pooled RCC frequency distribution for MxC (Vertucci classification):

| Vertucci type | RCC (Briseño) | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Ve I | 1-1-1/1 | 75.4–100% (dominant) |
| Ve II | 2-2-1/1 | 0.1–20% |
| Ve III | 1-2-1/1 | 0.1–11.6% |
| Ve IV | 2-2-2/2 | 0.1–0.4% |
| Ve V | 1-1-2/2 | 0.1–2.4% |
| Ve VI | 2-1-2/2 | 0.5–1.2% |
| Ve VII | 1-2-1/2 | 0.1–0.2% |

- Quantifies the "single-canal expectation vs exceptions" rule: Type I is overwhelmingly the norm, yet ~25% deviation must be anticipated.
- Sex-difference meta-analysis (6 studies, Europe vs Asia).

## Methodology

- Five databases (MEDLINE/PubMed, Embase, Cochrane, LILACS, Scopus) + grey literature, up to June 2024.
- Funnel: 200 identified → 127 after dedup → 14 full-text (4 excluded) → +12 hand-searched → **22 included**.
- RCC classified with Vertucci (1984), Weine et al., and Briseño Marroquín et al. (2015).
- Risk of bias: **AQUA** tool; quality: NHLBI tool; two independent reviewers + third for consensus.
- Meta-analysis: RevMan 5.4, odds ratios, I² heterogeneity, 95% CI, p≤0.05.

## Results

- **Type I (1-1-1/1)**: 75.4–100% across all studies regardless of method/sample (lowest: Weng et al. 75.4%, Somalinga Amardeep et al. 81.6%).
- **Type II (2-2-1/1)**: second most common, in half of studies, 0.1–20%.
- **Type III (1-2-1/1)**: third most common, 0.1–11.6% (absent from the Weine classification).
- **Sex differences** (OR [95% CI], more in males):
  - Ve III (1-2-1/1): **OR 2.07 [1.01, 4.26]** (significant)
  - Ve V (1-1-2/2): **OR 2.93 [1.07, 8.07]** (significant)
  - Ve II (2-2-1/1): OR 1.34 [0.53, 3.41] (NS)
  - Ve IV (2-2-2/2): OR 0.08 [0.00, 4.00] (directionally female, wide CI)
  - **No sex difference** for Ve I (1-1-1/1) and Ve VII (1-2-1/2)
- Type I seen in up to 99.5% of men and up to 100% of women.
- **CBCT** most used method; radiographic, staining-and-clearing, and micro-CT less frequent. Sex/origin (Europe vs Asia) differences attributed to methodology and ethnic origin.
