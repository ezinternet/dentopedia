## One-line Summary
Cross-sectional in vivo CBCT survey (n=1,100 Chilean maxillary molars): MB2 prevalence 69.8% in first molars (1MM) vs 46.9% in second molars (2MM); mean PMB1-PMB2 distance 2.68 mm (1MM) / 2.41 mm (2MM); 1MM MB2 more frequent in females, 2MM MB2 more frequent in males.

## 한줄요약

## Summary
Large in vivo CBCT survey from a single Chilean population that quantifies both prevalence and geometric location of the MB2 canal in the mesiobuccal root of permanent maxillary first and second molars. Standardized measurement plane: 1 mm apical to the pulpal floor. Three reference points (PMB1, PMB2, palatal canal PP) were used to express MB2 location both as a direct PMB1-PMB2 distance and as a perpendicular offset from the PMB1-PP line.

## Key Contributions
- Direct same-cohort comparison of 1MM (550 teeth) vs 2MM (550 teeth) MB2 prevalence using identical CBCT protocol
- Geometric coordinates (PMB1, PMB2, PP) measured at a fixed 1 mm sub-pulpal plane — reusable as an intra-operative search heuristic
- Sex-stratified data: 1MM MB2 more common in females, 2MM MB2 more common in males

## Methodology
- Design: cross-sectional in vivo CBCT image analysis
- Sample: 1,100 maxillary molars (550 1MM + 550 2MM)
- Reference plane: 1 mm apical to pulpal chamber floor
- Measurements: PMB1-PP, PMB1-PMB2, and perpendicular distance from PMB2 to the line PMB1-PP
- Statistics: descriptive, chi-square; α=0.05

## Results
- **1MM**: MB2 prevalence **69.82%** (more in females, p=0.005); PMB1-PP 7.64 ± 1.04 mm; PMB1-PMB2 2.68 ± 0.49 mm; PMB2-to-(PMB1-PP)-line 1.25 ± 0.34 mm
- **2MM**: MB2 prevalence **46.91%** (more in males, p<0.001); PMB1-PP 7.02 ± 1.30 mm; PMB1-PMB2 2.41 ± 0.64 mm; PMB2-to-(PMB1-PP)-line 0.98 ± 0.33 mm
- Clinical implication: search MB2 roughly 2.5 mm palatal to MB1, slightly mesial to the PMB1-PP line, at the level just apical to the chamber floor

## Limitations
- Single ethnic population (Chilean) — prevalence figures cannot be globalized
- CBCT cannot replace direct visualization or micro-CT for ground truth
- Prevalence depends on measurement plane; alternate planes might yield different counts
