## One-line Summary

Systematic review + meta-analysis of 30 micro-CT studies (double-rooted permanent mandibular first molars): pooled Vertucci Type IV dominates the mesial root (58.7%) and Type I the distal root (83.0%); middle mesial canal prevalence up to 46%, mesial-root isthmus 10–100% — all with high heterogeneity (I²=86–87%) and "moderate" risk of bias in 27/30 studies (AQUA).

## Summary

This SR+MA pools 30 micro-computed tomography (micro-CT) studies of **double-rooted permanent mandibular first molars (MFM)** — the most-treated and most fracture-prone molar — to give the highest-resolution available picture of its root and canal anatomy. Micro-CT (voxel 9.9–50 µm here) is the field's "gold standard" because, unlike CBCT or clearing, it resolves isthmuses, the middle mesial canal (MMC), accessory canals, and apical foramina non-destructively.

The headline pooled finding: the **mesial root is dominated by Vertucci Type IV (two separate canals; 58.7%)** and the **distal root by Type I (single canal; 83.0%)**. Mesial canal anatomy is consistently more complex and variable than distal — with frequent "non-classifiable" configurations, MMCs up to 46%, and mesial-root isthmuses spanning 10–100% across populations. Heterogeneity is high (I²=86% MMC, 87% Fan's isthmus), so these are population-sensitive ranges, not single thresholds. AQUA appraisal rated 27/30 studies "moderate" risk of bias, mainly from missing sample-size justification (93%) and absent calibration (only 20%).

> Scope note: the review **excludes three-rooted MFM (radix entomolaris) and taurodont teeth** by design, so it does not speak to three-rooted prevalence — that is a separate evidence stream.

## Key Contributions

1. **Highest-resolution anatomy reference** for the double-rooted MFM, synthesizing micro-CT (the gold-standard modality) rather than CBCT/clearing.
2. **Pooled Vertucci prevalence**: mesial Type IV 58.7% (95%CI 0.186–0.627), distal Type I 83.0% (95%CI 0.623–0.880).
3. **MMC and isthmus meta-analyses** quantifying both the central tendency and the large between-population heterogeneity (I²=86–87%).
4. **AQUA risk-of-bias map** exposing systematic methodological gaps in micro-CT anatomy literature (sample-size calculation, calibration).

## Methodology

- **Design**: PRISMA 2020 SR + MetaXL meta-analysis; OSF protocol reg. 10.17605/OSF.IO/EZP7K (2022-06-20).
- **Search**: Web of Science + PubMed + Scopus. 1358 identified → 921 de-dup → 831 excluded by title → 56 by abstract → 34 eligible → 4 excluded on full-text → **30 included**. Selection κ = 0.88.
- **Population**: permanent **double-rooted** MFM only; three-rooted, taurodont, primary teeth, second molars, case reports, reviews, non-English, comparison-only studies excluded.
- **ROB**: AQUA tool, 5 domains. Outcome 27/30 "moderate", 2 "low", 1 "high".
- **Meta-analyzed entities**: Vertucci main-canal configurations, MMC (Pomeranz) configurations, Fan's isthmus types. Sample-size range across studies 19–140 teeth; isotropic resolution 9.9–50 µm.

## Results

**Main canal configuration (Vertucci, pooled prevalence)**

| Root | Dominant type | Pooled prevalence (95% CI) |
|---|---|---|
| Mesial | Type IV (two separate canals) | **0.587 (58.7%)** (0.186–0.627) |
| Distal | Type I (single canal) | **0.830 (83.0%)** (0.623–0.880) |

Mesial root: two canals is the most frequent finding (Type IV separate, or Type V single→split). High "non-classifiable" rates in micro-CT studies. Distal root: single canal dominant; 3–4 distal canals seen in Brazilians but not Egyptians (ethnic variation).

**Middle mesial canal (MMC)** — prevalence **up to 46%**; configurations independent / fin / confluent (confluent ± isthmus), confluent predominant overall (fin most frequent in Burmese). Meta-analysis I²=86%. Orifice location (Keleş & Keskin): 77.41% at CEJ needing no troughing; 5.38% need 1-mm and 9.69% need 2-mm troughing; 7.52% inaccessible (>2 mm below CEJ). MMC major-foramen deviation from apex is the largest of all canals (>2 mm in six cases, up to 5.67–6.2 mm) — so MB/ML working length cannot be transferred to the MMC.

**Isthmus** — mesial-root prevalence highly variable **10–100%**; more frequent in middle/apical thirds and more common in mesial than distal canals. Fan types: I sheet, II separate, III mixed, IV cannular; separate & mixed dominate the FIRST molar (sheet connections dominate the second molar). Meta-analysis I²=87%. Older age → higher isthmus occurrence.

**Other anatomy** — root length ~13.16±1.24 mm (Chinese, two-rooted); thinnest mesial dentine ("danger zone") directed mesially in 48%, mainly apical third; accessory canals up to 85% (more mesial, concentrated in the apical 3 mm, more in two-rooted than three-rooted); mean MAF-to-apex 0.85 mm (mesial); MB canal greater curvature than ML, largest apically (3-canaled avg MB 23° / ML 25° / DB 11°). VRF in MFM 51.8%; ~2× the maxillary first molar in endodontically-treated teeth — explaining the clinical weight of mesial-root anatomy.

**Reporting/ROB gaps** — population/ethnicity 73%, micro-CT specs 83%, ethical approval 40%, sample-size method missing 93%, calibration only 20%.

## Clinical Implications

- Expect **two mesial canals (Type IV)** as the baseline and actively hunt the **MMC** (up to ~46%) in the MB–ML developmental groove — most orifices sit at the CEJ, but ~7.5% need deep troughing.
- The **distal root is usually a single canal (Type I, 83%)** but can carry extra canals in some populations.
- **Mesial-root isthmus** is common in the middle/apical thirds — plan adjunctive irrigant activation; it cannot be cleaned by filing alone.
- Respect the mesial-root **danger zone** (thinnest dentine mesial, apical third) with anti-curvature technique to avoid strip perforation; mesial roots drive the high MFM vertical-root-fracture rate.
