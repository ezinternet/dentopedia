## One-line Summary

CBCT cross-sectional study (2173 mandibular molars from 724 Mexican CBCT scans): C-shaped canal prevalence 0.2% in first vs 14.1% in second mandibular molars; female predominance (27.3% vs 13.3%, p=0.004, OR 1.78); C2 most common type (39.3%); radix entomolaris in 3.4% of first and 1.4% of second molars.

## 한줄요약

CBCT 횡단 연구(멕시코 인구, CBCT 724건에서 하악대구치 2173개): C-shaped 근관 유병률 제1대구치 0.2% vs 제2대구치 14.1%, 여성 우세(27.3% vs 13.3%, p=0.004, OR 1.78); C2형이 가장 흔함(39.3%); radix entomolaris는 제1대구치 3.4%·제2대구치 1.4%.

## Summary

This retrospective CBCT study characterized C-shaped canal and radix entomolaris morphology in mandibular molars of a Mexican population (Durango City). Across 2173 molars, C-shaped canals were overwhelmingly a second-molar phenomenon (14.1% vs 0.2% in first molars), with the left second molar (FDI 3.7) showing the highest prevalence (14.8%). A significant female predominance was observed (OR 1.78, p=0.004), and C2 was the most common Fan configuration (39.3%). Radix entomolaris, in contrast, was more frequent in first molars (3.4% vs 1.4%, p<0.001). The study adds a Latin American data point with a co-reported supernumerary-root variation in the same cohort.

## Key Contributions

- Mexican-population C-shaped + radix prevalence with Fan-type breakdown in one cohort.
- Strong second > first mandibular molar gradient for C-shaped canals (14.1% vs 0.2%).
- Significant female predominance (OR 1.78).
- Radix entomolaris more common in first molars (opposite tooth-position pattern to C-shaped canals).

## Methodology

- Retrospective cross-sectional CBCT (Newtom VGiEvo; RadiAnt 2023.1), 724 scans, 2173 molars.
- Three specialists (endodontics, endoperiodontics, oral radiology).
- Fan et al. C1–C5 classification; radix entomolaris = distolingual supernumerary root.
- FDI nomenclature; chi-square testing; SPSS v25.
- Exclusions: RCT-treated, impacted, developmental anomalies, open apices, calcified, third molars.

## Results

- C-shaped canals (160 teeth): first molar 0.2%, second molar 14.1%.
  - Left second (3.7) 14.8%, right second (4.7) 13.5% (p<0.001).
  - Female 27.3% vs male 13.3% (p=0.004, OR 1.78).
  - C2 most common (39.3%).
- Radix entomolaris (52 teeth): first molar 3.4%, second 1.4% (p<0.001, more in first).
- Cited global context: overall 13.9%, China 44.0%, Brazil 6.8%, Mexico ~14.2%.
