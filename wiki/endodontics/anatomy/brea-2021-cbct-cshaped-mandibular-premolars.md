## One-line Summary

CBCT cross-sectional study (380 mandibular first + 308 second premolars, 292 Venezuelan patients): overall 19.2% (132/688) had a C-shaped root canal system, 83.3% of which were in first premolars vs 16.7% in second premolars; C3 most common in first premolars (59.1%); no significant gender difference; bifurcation mostly in the middle third.

## Summary

This retrospective CBCT study evaluated C-shaped root and canal morphology in mandibular premolars of a Venezuelan population — a site less studied than molars but clinically demanding because of narrow canals, root concavities, and thin dentinal walls. Across 688 premolars, 19.2% showed a C-shaped root canal system. Unlike the molar pattern (where second molars dominate), C-shaped morphology was concentrated in first premolars: 83.3% of all C-shaped cases were first premolars and only 16.7% second premolars. C3 (two separate canals) was the dominant Fan configuration in first premolars (59.1%). No significant gender effect was found, and canal bifurcation occurred mostly in the middle third of the root. The authors conclude C-shaped anatomy is relatively low in second premolars compared with first premolars.

## Key Contributions

- Premolar-specific C-shaped prevalence in a Latin American cohort, complementing molar-dominated data.
- First-premolar predominance of C-shaped configuration (tooth-position pattern opposite to molars).
- Reports bifurcation level and radicular groove location.
- High observer agreement (Kappa 0.90–0.94; ICC 0.89–0.91).

## Methodology

- Retrospective cross-sectional CBCT (Kodak 9000 3D; 76 μm voxel).
- 688 premolars (380 first + 308 second) from 292 patients.
- Two endodontists (duplicate reads), radiologist arbitration.
- Fan et al. classification extended for premolars (C1–C6; C4 subtyped a/b/c).
- Variables: tooth position, root/canal number, configuration, bifurcation level, radicular groove.
- Chi-square (α=0.05); SPSS 21.0.

## Results

- Overall C-shaped: 19.2% (132/688). Male 9.16%, Female 10.03% (no significant difference).
- Distribution of C-shaped cases: first premolar 83.33%, second premolar 16.66%.
- First premolar Fan: C3 59.09% dominant (also C4a 21.81%, C2 9.09%, C1 3.63%, C4b 1.8%).
- Second premolar Fan: C4a 27.27% and C2 18.18% leading.
- Bifurcation in middle third: ~53% (first), ~50% (second premolars).
- No significant gender association with C-shaped system or configuration.
