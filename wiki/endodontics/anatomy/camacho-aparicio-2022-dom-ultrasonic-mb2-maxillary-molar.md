## Summary
In vivo 3-stage validity study (84 patients, UNAM Mexico City) using CBCT as gold standard to assess MB2 detection. Each successive technique ÔÇö explorer, DOM, DOM + ultrasonic selective dentin removal ÔÇö improves sensitivity while maintaining 100% specificity. MB2 prevalence by CBCT: 79%.

## Validity Metrics by Stage

| Stage | Sensitivity | Specificity | PPV | NPV |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Explorer (naked eye) | 79% | 100% | 100% | 56% |
| + DOM | 82% | 100% | 100% | 60% |
| + DOM + Ultrasonic | **86%** | **100%** | **100%** | **67%** |

- Kappa = 91% between examiners
- MB2 prevalence by CBCT gold standard: **79%**
- Clinical overall detection without tools: 68% (vs 79% CBCT) ÔÇö systematic under-detection

## Key Methodological Strength
- **CBCT as gold standard** (blinded expert reader) ÔÇö distinguishes this from Sujith 2014 which had no objective reference
- 100% specificity + PPV at all stages: **zero false positives** reported
- NPV improves with each stage: reflects incremental value of adding tools

## Clinical Implications
- Ultrasonic dentin removal (Stage 3) is **irreversible** ÔÇö should be justified by anatomy/suspicion
- DOM alone adds meaningful sensitivity (79% Ôćĺ 82%)
- Full protocol (DOM + ultrasonic) achieves 86% sensitivity ÔÇö best available clinical method
