## One-line Summary

Multicenter cross-sectional study with meta-analytic pooling (20 countries, 6,000 participants / 12,000 bilateral mandibular first premolars, CBCT): C-shaped canal global prevalence 10% (95% CI 7–12%; Asia 19%, Europe 3%), radicular grooves 20%, taurodontism 7%; all three show strong bilateral symmetry and inter-correlation suggesting shared developmental pathways.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1038/s41598-025-02666-9](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-025-02666-9)), this is the largest standardized multinational CBCT dataset on anatomical variation of the mandibular first premolar: 6,000 participants and 12,000 bilateral teeth across 20 countries (300 each), collected June 2023–April 2024 under a common AAE/AAOMR protocol. It quantifies three clinically relevant variations — radicular grooves (RGs), C-shaped canals, and taurodontism — in one population, allowing co-occurrence and bilateral-symmetry analysis that single-feature prevalence studies cannot provide.

The headline endodontic number is a **C-shaped canal global prevalence of 10% (95% CI 7–12%)** in mandibular first premolars, with a strong geographic gradient: **Asia 19% (Yemen 31%)** versus **Europe 3%**, Australia 12%, North America 7%, South America 9%, Africa 5%. Heterogeneity is very high (I²=98%) and a single study anchors some regions (e.g., Australia), so the pooled figure must be read with regional caution; sensitivity analysis kept the estimate near 12%. Radicular grooves were the most common variation (~20%, highest in Africa) and taurodontism the least (~7%). All three features showed prominent **bilateral symmetry** (taurodontism highest at 98.33%) and were inter-correlated (Cramer's V), which the authors interpret as shared developmental pathways.

Clinically, the bilateral-symmetry finding is the practical takeaway echoed across the C-shaped literature: encountering a C-shaped canal (or RG/taurodontism) on one side raises the prior probability of the same variation contralaterally, supporting contralateral CBCT vigilance and population-specific expectation setting. The premolar focus complements molar-dominated C-shaped data: the mandibular *second molar* remains the classic high-prevalence C-shaped tooth ([[yousefi-2025-cshaped-canal-prevalence-cbct-sr-ma]] 17.3%), while this paper sharpens the *first premolar* picture and ties C-shaped morphology to RGs and taurodontism.

Reliability was solid: intra-observer Cohen's kappa 0.59–0.87 (highest for C-shaped), inter-observer Fleiss' kappa 0.72–0.78 (substantial) across 20 observers.

## Key Contributions

- Standardized 20-country / 12,000-tooth CBCT dataset on mandibular first premolar variation — co-quantifies RGs, C-shaped canals, taurodontism together.
- C-shaped canal global prevalence 10% with a steep Asia>Europe gradient (19% vs 3%).
- Strong bilateral symmetry for all three features → contralateral-vigilance rationale.
- Inter-feature correlation suggesting shared developmental origin.

## Methodology

Retrospective evaluation of pre-acquired CBCT datasets under a common protocol (AAE/AAOMR), 6,000 participants / 12,000 bilateral mandibular first premolars, 20 countries × 300. Inclusion required bilateral premolars without prior RCT/restoration/resorption/open apex or anatomy-affecting syndromes. Prevalence stratified by region/gender/age; chi-square + Cramer's V for association strength; random-effects pooling with subgroup and outlier-exclusion sensitivity analyses. Intra-observer Cohen's kappa 0.59–0.87; inter-observer Fleiss' kappa 0.72–0.78. Confidence labeled `cross-sectional` (primary multicenter prevalence data analyzed with meta-analytic pooling), not a literature systematic review.

## Results

- **C-shaped canal**: global 0.10 (95% CI 0.07–0.12); Asia 0.19 (Yemen 0.31), Australia 0.12, South America 0.09, North America 0.07, Africa 0.05, Europe 0.03; I²=98%.
- **Radicular grooves**: ~0.20 global, highest in Africa.
- **Taurodontism**: ~0.07 global, marked regional variability; highest bilateral rate 98.33%.
- **Bilateral symmetry** prominent for all three; significant inter-correlation among features.
- Demographics: female 55.2%; age peak 20–30 y (25.7%).
