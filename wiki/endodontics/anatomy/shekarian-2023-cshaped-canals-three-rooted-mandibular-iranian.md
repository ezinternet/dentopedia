CBCT study of 292 Iranian patients (291 first, 402 second, 200 third mandibular molars): C-shaped canal prevalence 1.7%/2%/2% respectively; three-rooted molars most common in first molars (2.7%), most molars two-rooted.

## Summary

This Iranian-population cross-sectional CBCT study reports two clinically important anatomical variants of mandibular molars in a single dataset spanning all three molar positions: C-shaped canals and three-rooted molars (radix entomolaris). C-shaped prevalence was low and broadly similar across positions (1.7–2%), with second and third molars slightly higher than first molars. Three-rooted molars were uncommon overall but most frequent in first molars (2.7%) — the classic radix-entomolaris pattern whose missed extra root is a recognized cause of endodontic failure. The vast majority of molars were two-rooted. Using high-resolution CBCT and a larger sample, the study addresses prior conflicting Iranian prevalence reports.

## Key Contributions

- Single-dataset Iranian prevalence for both C-shaped canals and three-rooted molars across first/second/third molars.
- Confirms first-molar three-rooted (radix entomolaris) prevalence (2.7%) as the dominant supernumerary-root pattern in this population.
- Provides a higher-resolution, larger-sample correction to earlier inconsistent Iranian data.

## Methodology

- Descriptive cross-sectional design; 1248 archival CBCT scans screened → 292 eligible (simple random sampling).
- 893 molars (291 first, 402 second, 200 third) scored in all three planes by three calibrated examiners; oral radiologist arbitrated; 25% re-read for agreement.
- Sirona CBCT (85 kV/5.2 mA, 0.180 mm voxel, 1 mm slice); C-shaped classified per Fan et al. (2004); χ² for gender.

## Results

- **First molars (n=291)**: single-rooted 0.7%, two-rooted 96.6%, three-rooted 2.7%; C-shaped 1.7%.
- **Second molars (n=402)**: single-rooted 8.5%, two-rooted 90.5%, three-rooted 1%; C-shaped 2%.
- **Third molars (n=200)**: single-rooted 21.5%, two-rooted 77.5%, three-rooted 1%; C-shaped 2%.
- Most molars two-rooted; three-rooted molars most common in first molars; second/third molars had higher C-shaped prevalence.
