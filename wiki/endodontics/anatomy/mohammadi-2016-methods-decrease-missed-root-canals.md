## Summary
Narrative review synthesizing 5 clinical strategies to reduce missed root canals — a major cause of endodontic treatment failure. Authors: Iranian Center for Endodontic Research + University of Western Australia. No formal systematic methodology.

## Five Strategies to Reduce Missed Canals

| Strategy | Key Role |
|---|---|
| **1. Pre-op radiographs** | Standard + angled views to identify extra roots/canals |
| **2. CBCT** | Complex anatomy, inconclusive radiograph; identifies extra canals pre-surgery |
| **3. Access cavity design** | Must be conservative but complete; inadequate removal → orifice hidden |
| **4. Ultrasonic tips** | Remove calcified bridges without excessive dentin sacrifice |
| **5. DOM + magnification** | Increases detection rate for difficult canals (MB2, C-shape, etc.) |

**Transillumination**: adjunct technique; reveals root anatomy by light transmission patterns through thin dentin

## Clinical Significance
- Missed canals = major cause of endodontic failure
- No single method sufficient; **stepwise/combined approach** recommended
- CBCT before surgery when complex anatomy suspected (MB2, fused roots, dens invaginatus)

## Limitations
- Narrative review — no systematic search reported
- 2016 — pre-dates post-2016 developments (CBCT-guided navigation, digital planning software)
