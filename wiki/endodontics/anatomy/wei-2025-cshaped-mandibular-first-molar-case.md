## One-line Summary

Case report (CBCT-confirmed) of bilateral C-shaped mandibular first molars — the first documented mandibular first molar with four separate canals (two mesial + two distal) inside a single fused C-shaped root.

## 한줄요약

양측 하악 제1대구치 C-shaped 근관 증례(CBCT 확진) — 융합된 단일 C-shaped 근 내에 4개의 분리된 근관(근심 2 + 원심 2)을 가진 최초 보고 하악 제1대구치.

## Summary

A 35-year-old male presented with symptomatic irreversible pulpitis in the right mandibular first molar and proved to have **bilateral C-shaped first molars** — a rare location, since C-shaped anatomy is typically a mandibular second molar finding. The index (right) molar revealed a fused C-shaped chamber with **four distinct orifices (two mesial, two distal)**; CBCT confirmed a C3c configuration coronally, two mesial + two distal canals in the middle third, and individual canals apically, in a fused C-shaped root extending buccally to the apex. The authors state this is the **first reported mandibular first molar with four separate canals within a single fused C-shaped root**. The contralateral (left) molar separately showed a single oval mesial canal and a semicolon-shaped distal canal. Treatment used microscope visualization, controlled-memory NiTi, 3% NaOCl + 17% EDTA with sonic activation, and an apex locator. The paper underscores CBCT's role in avoiding missed canals (≥1 missed canal ≈3.66× higher periapical-lesion odds) and the thin-wall strip-perforation/fracture risk of C-shaped systems.

## Key Contributions

- **Rare C-shaped configuration in mandibular first molars**, bilateral.
- **First documented four separate canals (2 mesial + 2 distal) in a single fused C-shaped first-molar root**, CBCT-confirmed.
- Compiled **prevalence table** of first-molar C-shaped configuration: 0.16%–24.33% across populations.
- Reinforces CBCT necessity and missed-canal risk in atypical anatomy.

## Methodology

Single-patient case report + literature review. Diagnosis: periapical radiograph + operating microscope (Carl Zeiss) + CBCT. Negotiation with 06#/08#/10# C-files; Raypex 6 apex locator; controlled-memory NiTi (PLEX-V) to size 25/.06; 3% NaOCl + 17% EDTA with sonic activation.

## Results

- **First-molar C-shaped prevalence (review)**: 0.16–24.33%; most populations <1% (Portuguese 0.6%, Israeli 0.16%, Mexican 0.2%, Thai 0.39%, Saudi 0–0.19%), some high (Brazilian 24.01%, Saudi 24.33%).
- **Index (right) molar**: four separate canals in a fused C-shaped root; C3c → C3(2 mesial/2 distal) → C3c (coronal/middle/apical).
- **Left molar**: oval mesial + semicolon (C2) distal.
- Second-molar C-shaped prevalence cited up to ~44.5–48.7%.
