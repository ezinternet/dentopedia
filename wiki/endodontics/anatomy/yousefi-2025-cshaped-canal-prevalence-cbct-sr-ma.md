## One-line Summary

Systematic review + meta-analysis of 101 CBCT studies (from 5548 screened): highest C-shaped canal prevalence in mandibular second molars (17.3%), lowest in maxillary first molars (0.8%); female predominance in mandibular second molars (23.6% vs 16.7%); highest in the Asian continent; right/left difference not significant (21.2% vs 23.1%).

## Summary

This PRISMA-registered systematic review and meta-analysis pooled 101 CBCT studies (screened from 5548) to estimate C-shaped canal prevalence across premolar and molar teeth, stratified by tooth type, gender, geographic region, and side. The mandibular second molar carried the highest prevalence (17.3%) and the maxillary first molar the lowest (0.8%). C-shaped canals in mandibular second molars were more common in females (23.6% vs 16.7% in males) and in the Asian continent than elsewhere. There was no significant right-vs-left difference (21.2% vs 23.1%), supporting the clinical heuristic that a contralateral C-shaped canal is likely when one side is found. The review updates Martin et al. 2019 by adding Fan-based morphological classification. As the wiki's highest-evidence anchor for this topic, it consolidates the single-population cross-sectional pages into pooled, subgroup-stratified estimates.

## Key Contributions

- Largest CBCT-based pooled C-shaped prevalence estimate by tooth type / gender / geography / side.
- Establishes the tooth-type gradient: mandibular 2nd molar 17.3% (highest) to maxillary 1st molar 0.8% (lowest).
- Quantifies female and Asian-continent predominance.
- Adds Fan morphological classification absent from the prior (Martin 2019) review.
- Supports contralateral-symmetry clinical heuristic (no significant side difference).

## Methodology

- PRISMA; PROSPERO CRD42024522710; databases PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science (to 2024).
- Inclusion: CBCT-diagnosed C-shaped prevalence studies, English. Exclusion: reviews/MA, in vitro, non-CBCT imaging.
- Risk of bias: Newcastle-Ottawa scale (cross-sectional).
- Heterogeneity by I²; Comprehensive Meta-Analysis software; effect = correlation coefficient with 95% CI.
- Subgroups: tooth type, gender, side, continent, Fan C1–C5 type.

## Results

- Mandibular second molar: 17.3% (highest of all tooth types).
- Maxillary first molar: 0.8% (lowest).
- Gender (mandibular 2nd molar): female 23.6% vs male 16.7%.
- Geography: Asian continent highest for mandibular 2nd molar.
- Side (mandibular 2nd molar): right 21.2% vs left 23.1% — not significant.
- Clinical takeaway: anticipate contralateral C-shaped canal; use CBCT for preoperative morphology assessment.
