## 한줄요약
문헌고찰: 최소침습 접근와동(MIA)은 근거 불충분 — 전통적 직선 접근와동이 기구 조작·의원성 합병증 예방에 더 안전한 표준.

## Summary
Literature review evaluating traditional 근관 접근와동 (Traditional Endodontic Cavity, TEC) vs 최소침습 접근와동 (Minimally Invasive Access, MIA) cavity designs. Evidence does not support replacing TEC with MIA: MIA increases difficulty of 근관구 탐색 (canal orifice location) and iatrogenic complication risk, while fracture resistance benefits are conflicting. 직선 접근 (straight-line access) remains essential for safe NiTi instrument operation. Guided endodontics (static/dynamic) is noted as a promising approach for 근관 석회화 (pulp canal obliteration) but lacks cost-benefit data.

## Key Contributions
- Refutes the claim that MIA improves tooth prognosis over conventional TEC
- Identifies straight-line access as still the gold standard for preventing instrument separation
- Reviews guided endodontics as PCO-specific solution — not a universal access approach

## Methodology
- Literature review; PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, Science Direct, OpenGrey
- Two independent reviewers; supplemented by manual search and expert contact

## Results
| Feature | TEC | MIA |
|---|---|---|
| Canal location | Easier | More difficult |
| Instrument operation | Better (straight-line) | Restricted |
| Fracture resistance | Standard | Conflicting; no clear benefit |
| Iatrogenic risk | Lower | Higher |
| Evidence level | Well-established | Insufficient |
