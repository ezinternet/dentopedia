## One-line Summary

Systematic review (PRISMA, 2005-2025) of oral HSV therapy: topical agents shorten lesion duration when applied early but do not reduce recurrence; systemic antivirals (aciclovir, valaciclovir, famciclovir) address both resolution and recurrence prevention.

## Summary

This PRISMA systematic review separates the distinct goals of topical versus systemic therapy for oral HSV infection. Topical agents (aciclovir cream, docosanol, newer formulations) reduce lesion duration and local symptoms when applied early, with minimal systemic absorption and no effect on recurrence. Systemic antivirals (aciclovir, valaciclovir, famciclovir) target both lesion resolution and recurrence prevention, supporting episodic and suppressive regimens — including short-course high-dose protocols that improve adherence. Topical and systemic roles are complementary; systemic therapy is indicated for recurrent, disseminated, or high-risk infection.

## Key Contributions

- Distinguishes topical (local control) from systemic (recurrence prevention) goals.
- Supports short-course high-dose systemic regimens.
- Reviews combination/novel delivery (aciclovir-hydrocortisone, PDT).
- Points to latency/immune-modulation/gene-editing as future directions.

## Methodology

PRISMA-compliant SR. Databases: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science (2005-2025). Eligible: RCTs and observational studies with validated clinical outcomes for topical or systemic HSV treatment.

## Results

Topical agents shorten lesion duration/local symptoms only with early application; no recurrence reduction. Systemic antivirals effective for episodic and suppressive therapy; indicated for recurrent/disseminated/high-risk disease. Combination and novel-delivery approaches may enhance local efficacy.
