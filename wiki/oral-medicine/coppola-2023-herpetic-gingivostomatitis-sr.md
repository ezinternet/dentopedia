## One-line Summary

Systematic review (5 studies, 364 patients, mean age 7.6 y) of primary herpetic gingivostomatitis: only weak evidence that early aciclovir improves healing and shortens symptoms; no consensus regimen exists.

## Summary

Primary herpetic gingivostomatitis (PHGS) is the commonest symptomatic manifestation of primary HSV-1 in children, usually self-limiting but occasionally requiring hospitalization for dehydration. This PRISMA systematic review pooled five studies (364 patients) and found the evidence base too small and heterogeneous to define a standard regimen. Most catalogued treatments are symptomatic (analgesics, chlorhexidine, topical anesthetics) and do not affect viral replication. Only one study gave weak evidence that aciclovir shortens lesion healing and symptom duration, and a ~72 h diagnostic delay further blunts antiviral efficacy.

## Key Contributions

- First PRISMA/PICO synthesis of the fragmented PHGS treatment literature.
- Shows most regimens are empiric symptomatic care, not antiviral.
- Identifies diagnostic delay (~72 h) as the key efficacy barrier.
- Explains why RCTs are scarce (rapid onset/remission).

## Methodology

PRISMA-compliant SR (PROSPERO CRD42023391386). Databases: PubMed, Scopus, Embase. Risk of bias by RoB 2 and ROBINS-I. Five studies, 364 immunocompetent patients, mean age 7.6 years.

## Results

Only 1 of 5 studies provided weak evidence favoring aciclovir for faster oral-lesion healing and reduced symptom duration. No regimen consensus; antiviral benefit is contingent on early (<72 h) initiation. Symptomatic agents dominate clinical practice.
