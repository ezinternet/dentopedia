## One-line Summary

Triple-blind RCT (n=69 HNC radiotherapy patients, 3 arms ×23) showing both L-arginine and L-glutamine oral suspensions reduced WHO mucositis severity, pain, and weight loss vs maltodextrin control by week 5–7, with L-arginine non-inferior to glutamine and a numerically higher week-7 healing rate (56% vs 13%).

## Summary

Radiation-induced oral mucositis (RIOM) is a major dose-limiting toxicity of head-and-neck-cancer (HNC) radiotherapy, yet the oral-medicine literature in this wiki centers on oral lichen planus, recurrent aphthous stomatitis (RAS), and burning mouth syndrome rather than RIOM. This triple-blind, three-arm RCT (n=69) is the first to test an **L-arginine oral suspension** for RIOM and to benchmark it head-to-head against the better-established **L-glutamine**, using a neutral **maltodextrin** control to isolate the amino-acid effect from generic caloric/nutritional support. Both amino acids significantly outperformed maltodextrin on WHO mucositis severity, Pain-VAS, BMI maintenance, and OHIP-14 quality of life by weeks 5–7, and a pre-specified non-inferiority analysis established L-arginine as not clinically worse than glutamine across all outcomes — positioning arginine as an alternative supportive therapy during radiotherapy.

## Key Contributions

- First RCT to test **L-arginine oral suspension for RIOM** and benchmark it directly against L-glutamine.
- Used a true **neutral control (maltodextrin 10 g)** rather than placebo-absent comparison, isolating the amino-acid effect from generic nutritional support — a gap in prior glutamine trials.
- Both amino acids significantly outperformed maltodextrin on **WHO mucositis severity, Pain-VAS, BMI maintenance, and OHIP-14 quality of life**.
- Formal **non-inferiority analysis** (pre-specified margins) established L-arginine as not clinically worse than glutamine across all outcomes.

## Methodology

- **Design**: triple-blind (patients, primary investigator, outcomes assessor), parallel-arm, 1:1:1 RCT; central computer-generated randomization, sealed opaque envelopes, identical-appearing treatments. ClinicalTrials.gov NCT06764420.
- **Population**: 69 HNC patients (stages I–IV), aged 20–70, with RIOM appearing at the 2nd–3rd week of RT, recruited at Ahmed Maher Teaching Hospital. All had surgical tumor removal then IMRT (2 Gy/fraction, 5/week, total 50–70 Gy over 5–7 weeks, ≥50 Gy to oral cavity). Excluded: diabetes, salivary gland tumors, renal/hepatic insufficiency, prior irradiation, sepsis, distant metastases.
- **Arms (n=23 each)**: Group I = L-arginine 5 g + maltodextrin 5 g; Group II = glutamine 5 g + maltodextrin 5 g; Group III = maltodextrin 10 g (neutral control). All swish-and-swallow, 30 min before meals, 3×/day, weeks 2–7 of RT.
- **Outcomes** at weeks 2, 5, 7: WHO oral toxicity scale (0–4), Pain-VAS (0–10), Effectiveness Index (no/moderate/marked improvement/healed), BMI, OHIP-14 (Arabic).
- **Statistics**: Kruskal–Wallis / Friedman + Wilcoxon for ordinal; ANOVA / repeated-measures for parametric; pre-specified non-inferiority t-tests (margins: WHO 5, VAS 1.0, BMI 1.0, OHIP-14 3 points; binary −10%).

## Results

- **WHO scale** (primary mucositis-severity outcome): no group difference at week 2 (p=0.6861); significant by week 5 (p<0.001) and 7. Arginine ≈ glutamine (pairwise NS); both significantly better than maltodextrin (p<0.001). Week-7 WHO stage 0 (complete): arginine 52%, glutamine 43%, maltodextrin 0% (70% of maltodextrin remained stage III). Favorable WHO (0/I) at day 7: arginine 82.6% vs glutamine 69.6% — L-arginine non-inferior (p=0.048).
- **Pain-VAS**: similar at week 2 (5.3 / 5.17 / 5.65, p=0.80); diverged at week 5 (4.2 / 4.3 / 6.3, p=0.004) and week 7 (1.6 / 2.4 / 4.9, p<0.001). Both amino acids better than maltodextrin; arginine ≈ glutamine.
- **Effectiveness Index**: week 7 healed — arginine 56% vs glutamine 13% vs maltodextrin 0% (descriptive difference; non-inferiority confirmed but not powered for superiority).
- **BMI**: maltodextrin dropped 21.56 → 18.93 (p=0.0005); arginine (p=0.87) and glutamine (p=0.170) stable — both prevented RT-associated weight loss.
- **OHIP-14**: arginine and glutamine improved at weeks 5 and 7; maltodextrin worsened (p<0.001). WHO–OHIP-14 correlation r=0.885 (p<0.001). Total radiation dose positively correlated with RIOM severity (p=0.012).
