## Summary

Oral mucositis (OM) is a common, serious complication of cancer treatment; chemotherapy-induced OM incidence in children can reach **91.5%** with major quality-of-life impact. This systematic review + meta-analysis (MEDLINE + Scopus, Jan 2000–Mar 2023) assessed interventions/agents for managing OM in children undergoing chemo/radiotherapy or hematopoietic stem cell transplantation (HSCT). **34 RCTs** met inclusion criteria; **5 RCTs** (those investigating Low Level Laser Therapy [LLLT] or honey) were pooled. The headline finding: **topical honey** significantly shortened hospital stay in severe OM, while **LLLT** showed no demonstrable benefit for OM grade >=II in the pediatric pooled analysis. This page opens the chemo/radiotherapy-induced oral-mucositis sub-topic within `oral-medicine`. (Abstract-level record — PMC full-text body not retrieved.)

## Key Contributions

- First focused pediatric OM prevention/treatment SR+MA in the wiki, distinct from the recurrent-aphthous and immune-mediated mucosal disease pages.
- Identifies **honey** as the only meta-analytically supported agent and finds **LLLT** unsupported in pooled pediatric data — a clinically actionable head-to-head signal for supportive cancer care.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review + meta-analysis of RCTs.
- **Databases / period**: MEDLINE + Scopus, January 2000 – March 2023.
- **Population**: Children on chemotherapy/radiotherapy or HSCT.
- **Included**: 34 RCTs met inclusion criteria; 5 pooled (LLLT or honey subsets).
- **Outcomes / measures**: mean hospital-stay duration (MD, honey); risk of OM grade >=II (RR, LLLT — prevention and therapeutic use).

## Results

| Intervention | Outcome | Effect | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Honey (topical, 2 RCTs)** | Mean hospital-stay duration, severe OM | **MD = -4.33 days, p = 0.002** | Significant benefit |
| **LLLT** | Prevention/treatment of OM grade >=II | **RR = 0.99, p = 0.99** | No effect |
| **LLLT (therapeutic)** | Lower risk of OM grade >=II | **RR = 0.48, p = 0.58** | Favorable point estimate, not significant |

- **Bottom line**: Honey is a promising candidate for treating pediatric OM; LLLT was not supported by the pooled pediatric evidence. Authors call for further high-quality RCTs.
- Only 5 of 34 RCTs were poolable; the honey estimate rests on 2 RCTs. The remaining agents from the 34 RCTs were in the full text not retrieved here (abstract-only record).
