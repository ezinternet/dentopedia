## One-line Summary

PRISMA-adherent SR of 15 studies (2 RCTs, 5 NRCTs, 8 observational; MMAT mean 5.57/7) on pharmacological management of desquamative gingivitis: clobetasol propionate 0.05% (53.3% of studies) and tacrolimus 0.1% (26.7%) are first-line agents with significant pain and lesion-severity reduction; topical routes are well tolerated (mild/transient AEs); systemic agents carry higher risk (candidiasis, haemolysis, anaemia, alopecia); PRP and propolis/nano-vitamin gels are promising adjuncts; formal QoL assessment (OHIP-14) performed in only 1/15 studies.

## Summary

Desquamative gingivitis (DG) is a clinical presentation — not a standalone diagnosis — characterized by chronic gingival epithelial desquamation, erosion, atrophy, and erythema. It is strongly associated (88–98% of cases) with mucocutaneous immune-mediated diseases, principally oral lichen planus (OLP), mucous membrane pemphigoid (MMP), and pemphigus vulgaris (PV). It is most prevalent in women over 30 years, affecting labial gingiva of anterior teeth and edentulous ridges, with symptoms ranging from mild discomfort to severe pain limiting oral function and speech.

This PROSPERO-registered SR (CRD42024556589) searched 7 databases (January 1990 – December 2023) and ultimately included 15 studies: 2 RCTs, 5 NRCTs, and 8 observational studies. Quality was "above average" in all 15 (MMAT scores 5–7; mean 5.57). Studies were predominantly European (Italy, Spain) with participants aged 42–62 years and a strong female predominance (221 F vs 53 M).

The review established that pharmacological management is the cornerstone of DG treatment (especially in immune-mediated presentations), while non-pharmacological strategies (plaque control, periodontal therapy, dietary modification) are adjunctive and cannot address the immunopathological mechanisms. No international guidelines exist specifically for DG; management is derived from guidelines addressing OLP, MMP, and PV individually.

## Key Contributions

1. **First SR comprehensively addressing both efficacy and safety** of pharmacological DG treatment across OLP, MMP, and PV presentations (not disease-specific).
2. **Dual first-line agents confirmed**: clobetasol propionate 0.05% (topical) and tacrolimus 0.1% (topical) — both with significant efficacy across the DG spectrum regardless of underlying diagnosis.
3. **Emerging therapies documented**: heterologous PRP (weekly application × 2 months) and propolis + nano-vitamin C + nano-vitamin E bio-adhesive gel — both with no reported AEs and promising early outcomes.
4. **Critical QoL gap identified**: only 1/15 studies (González-Serrano 2023) used a validated QoL instrument (OHIP-14); QoL is a major under-reported outcome in DG research.
5. **Systematic safety hierarchy established**: topical agents (mild, localized, transient AEs) << systemic agents (candidiasis, haemolysis from SMXP, anaemia/alopecia from immunosuppressants).

## Methodology

- **Design**: PRISMA-adherent systematic review; Cochrane Handbook followed
- **Registration**: PROSPERO CRD42024556589 (registered 24 June 2024)
- **Search**: 7 databases, January 1990 – December 2023; 624 records retrieved, 239 duplicates removed, 15 finally included (14 from database + 1 from reference list)
- **PICO**: P = DG patients (clinical + histological ± DIF diagnosis); I = any pharmacological agent; C = no treatment or placebo; O = pain reduction, gingival improvement, QoL, recurrence rates, adverse effects
- **Diagnostic criteria in included studies**: All 15 applied clinical + histological criteria; 9/15 (60%) used DIF; only 1 cited standardized guidelines
- **Quality appraisal**: MMAT v2018 (0–7 scale); RCTs mean 6.5, NRCTs mean 5.75, descriptive studies mean 6.17; all "above average"
- **Data synthesis**: Narrative synthesis (meta-analysis not feasible — heterogeneity in agents, dosages, outcome measures, follow-up durations)
- **Study design mix**: 2 RCTs (13.3%), 5 NRCTs (33.3%), 8 observational (53.3%)
- **Participants**: 221 female, 53 male; mean ages 42–62 years; follow-up 4–262 weeks across studies
- **Geography**: Italy (5), Spain (2), Brazil (2), UK (1), Bulgaria (1), Norway (1), Iran (1); no studies from Africa, Australia, Oceania

## Results

### Pharmacological agents studied

**Topical corticosteroids — most frequently used category**:
- Clobetasol propionate 0.05% appeared in 8/15 studies (53.3%); applied twice daily (standard) or three times daily (severe cases), via direct application, custom trays, or gauze pads; sometimes combined with nystatin (Orabase) to prevent secondary candidiasis
- Triamcinolone 0.1% and dexamethasone 0.5 mg/5 mL rinse also used (fewer studies)

**Topical immunosuppressants**:
- Tacrolimus 0.1% in 4/15 studies (26.7%); applied via ointment or custom trays (20-minute applications, twice then once daily); fasting 30 minutes post-application recommended

**Antibiotics and antimicrobials**:
- SMXP 500 mg/day → 1 g/day (systemic; for MMP unresponsive to topical steroids for >4 months): 62% complete resolution, 65% ulceration resolution, significant pain improvement (p<0.001); but haemolysis risk
- Fusidic acid 2% ointment (topical, 4× daily × 6 weeks): VAS pain 5.4±1.12 → 1.16±0.97; lesion size 72.47% reduction; no recurrence at 12 months; 1 allergic reaction
- Tetracycline/doxycycline 100 mg/day (systemic): positive treatment response; minor GI discomfort

**Biological and emerging therapies**:
- Heterologous PRP (Gabusi 2024): VAS 6.4 → 2.0 (mean reduction 4.4±2, p<0.05 Wilcoxon); ODSS reduction 8.2±5.3 (p<0.05); 89% showed improvement; no AEs
- Propolis + nano-vitamin C + nano-vitamin E bio-adhesive gel (González-Serrano 2023, RCT): significant OHIP-14 improvement (p=0.002); significant DGCS reduction; no AEs; vs control gel: not statistically significantly different at 4 weeks

**Systemic combination therapies** (severe/autoimmune-associated cases):
- Prednisone ± azathioprine, mycophenolate mofetil, methotrexate, rituximab — used for PV and refractory MMP; rituximab (2 g) reduced gingival PDAI 4.76±0.74 → 3.26±0.63 at 6 months

### Safety profiles

**Topical agents (mild/transient AEs)**:
- Clobetasol: oral candidiasis (most common, with prolonged use), xerostomia; clobetasol + nystatin combination eliminated candidiasis risk
- Tacrolimus: burning sensation (50% in Corrocher 2006), altered taste, oral candidiasis (1 case), abdominal discomfort (20% in Kavlakova 2022)
- Fusidic acid: 1 allergic reaction with mucosal ulceration
- Sodium iodide + salicylic acid: unpleasant taste (100% of patients)

**Systemic agents (higher risk)**:
- SMXP: haemolysis (mild to significant); treatment cessation required in severe haemolysis; allergic reaction (1 case)
- Tetracycline: minor abdominal discomfort, 1 oral candidiasis
- Prednisone/immunosuppressant combinations: oral candidiasis, anaemia (dapsone/IVIG), alopecia (mycophenolate mofetil)

**Emerging therapies**: PRP and propolis/nano-vitamin gel — no adverse effects reported in any study

### Recurrence

- 9/15 studies (60%) did not report recurrence data
- Clobetasol: low recurrence (0 relapses in Kavlakova 2022 at 2–3 months)
- Tacrolimus: 30% relapse rate within 3 months (Kavlakova 2022)
- SMXP: slight relapses in some cases, managed with topical steroids or repeat SMXP course
- PV (systemic treatment): 16% progression to extra-oral involvement
- Fusidic acid: no significant recurrence at 12 months

### QoL

- Only 1/15 studies used a validated QoL tool: González-Serrano 2023 (OHIP-14; significant improvement p=0.002 in physical pain, psychological disability, social disability domains)
- Leite 2015: indirect improvement in emotional well-being and social interactions (no validated tool)
- Gonzalez-Moles 2003: 93.9% resumed normal daily activities (proxy indicator, no validated tool)
- Overall: QoL is a significant evidence gap in DG pharmacological management

### Clinical implications

- **First-line (topical)**: Clobetasol propionate 0.05% OR tacrolimus 0.1% — both are appropriate for OLP, MMP, and PV presentations of DG
- **Clobetasol + nystatin combination**: preferred when candidiasis risk is a concern
- **Refractory to topical steroids**: consider SMXP (MMP) or systemic immunosuppressants (PV/MMP) with careful monitoring
- **Adjuncts/alternatives**: PRP and propolis + nano-vitamin gels for patients with insufficient response to conventional therapy or when corticosteroids are contraindicated
- **Individualized treatment**: patient age, comorbidities, and prior treatment response must guide agent selection
