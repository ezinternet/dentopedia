## Summary

D'Amario et al. (2025, *Dentistry Journal*) reviewed treatments for recurrent aphthous stomatitis (RAS) — recurring painful oral mucosal ulcers affecting ~20% of the population — drawing on 45 RCTs published in a 10-year window (2013–2023). Using pain (VAS) as the primary comparable outcome, the review concludes that **non-pharmacological therapies offer the best risk–benefit ratio**, frequently matching or surpassing cortisone-based treatment while avoiding its adverse effects. **Low-level laser therapy (LLLT)** emerges as the single most promising modality, mainly for its immediate (day-1) pain relief with no significant adverse effects. The authors frame management as a RAS-form-specific treatment ladder, distinguishing short-term care for minor/infrequent lesions from long-term management of recurrent disease.

## Key Contributions

- Synthesizes 45 RCTs (2013–2023) on RAS treatment with pain management as the common comparable endpoint.
- Argues non-pharmacological therapies (laser, supplements, natural mucoadhesives) give the best risk–benefit ratio, often equalling or beating topical corticosteroids with fewer adverse effects.
- Identifies LLLT as the most promising single modality for its immediate pain relief.
- Builds a practical, RAS-form-specific treatment ladder (short-term vs long-term management).

## Methodology

- PRISMA-structured literature review; protocol registered on PROSPERO (CRD42023382368).
- Inclusion: human RCTs, 2013–2023, any language, with a comparator (placebo or active). Databases: PubMed, EMBASE, CINAHL.
- Exclusion: RAS secondary to a primary disease (Crohn's, Behçet's); polytherapy/multi-agent confounding.
- Flow: 123 records → 28 duplicates removed → 95 screened → 47 off-topic + 3 criteria excluded → **45 studies** included.
- Risk of bias: two independent assessors, Cochrane RevMan v5.4; results stratified single-blind vs double-blind. Selection/performance/attrition bias generally low; detection/reporting often unclear.

## Results

**Topical**
- Acemannan (Aloe vera): pain 7.5 vs control 6.5 vs corticosteroid 8.3 — corticosteroid best, Aloe vera a viable alternative.
- Myrrh + Aloe vera mucoadhesive gels: Aloe vera 76.6% complete healing by day 6; myrrh 76.7% near-painless by day 6 (myrrh > Aloe vera).
- Curcumin: 1% nanomicelle vs 2% gel, day-4 VAS 2.58 vs 3.75; both reached 0 by day 7. Curcumin vs triamcinolone: no significant difference.
- Silver nitrate / propolis / rhubarb / walnut (n=125): silver nitrate fastest symptom resolution (mean 1.16 days).
- Triester glycerol oxide (TGO) gel: outperformed 0.1% triamcinolone on pain and lesion size (days 2–6), aided by mucosal adhesion.

**Systemic supplements**
- Omega-3 (three 6-month RCTs): all significantly better than placebo with no side effects; one test-group VAS fell to 1.48 by 6 months vs placebo ~6.6; reduced recurrence.
- Probiotics: improved healing, pain, episode frequency vs placebo.
- Levamisole ± low-dose prednisolone (n=350; n=50): reduced ulcer number, size, pain, frequency.

**Laser**
- CO₂ single session: VAS 8.48 → 0.68 immediately post-treatment (p<0.001).
- 660 nm diode: day-6 efficacy index 100 in all cases.
- Diode vs pharmacological: day-1 VAS 2.04 vs 4.85 favoring laser; converged near zero by day 7.
- Diode in children with MiRAS (n=60): significant from day 1 (VAS 1 vs 3, p=0.0001).
- 809 nm LLLT: VAS 84.7 → 31.5 by day 2 vs near-flat placebo.
- Er,Cr:YSGG: significant, most notable immediately post-treatment (p<0.01).

Double-blind studies provided the most robust evidence; LLLT, omega-3, and curcumin gels showed the strongest, most consistent effects. Heterogeneity across studies and single-blind observer bias temper certainty, and many promising compounds lack validated commercial oral formulations.
