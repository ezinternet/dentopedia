## One-line Summary

Cochrane SR+MA (35 RCTs, 1474 patients): topical corticosteroids may resolve OLP pain better than placebo (RR 1.91, 95% CI 1.08-3.36, low certainty); tacrolimus may beat clobetasol for pain (very low certainty).

## 한줄요약

코크란 SR+MA(35 RCT, 1474명): 국소 스테로이드가 위약보다 OLP 통증 해소 우월 가능(RR 1.91, 낮은 근거), 타크롤리무스가 클로베타솔보다 통증에 나을 수 있음(매우 낮은 근거).

## Summary

This Cochrane review updates the 2011 OLP review, focusing exclusively on corticosteroid therapies. Across 35 RCTs (1474 participants), topical corticosteroids in an adhesive base were more likely than placebo to resolve pain, but with low certainty and inconclusive clinical-resolution and adverse-effect data. Head-to-head, topical tacrolimus showed a very-low-certainty signal of better pain resolution than clobetasol. Corticosteroids remain the pragmatic first line while the evidence quality is acknowledged as weak.

## Key Contributions

- Definitive Cochrane synthesis isolating corticosteroid evidence in OLP.
- Quantifies modest pain benefit over placebo (RR 1.91).
- Flags tacrolimus as a possible superior pain-resolver (low confidence).
- Documents inconclusive clinical-resolution and adverse-effect evidence.

## Methodology

RCTs of any corticosteroid vs placebo, calcineurin inhibitor, another corticosteroid, other drug, or corticosteroid+adjunct. RR (95% CI) for dichotomous, MD for continuous outcomes; GRADE certainty. 35 studies/1474 participants (7 low, 11 unclear, 17 high risk of bias).

## Results

Corticosteroid vs placebo — pain resolution RR 1.91 (95% CI 1.08-3.36; low certainty); clinical resolution RR 6.00 (95% CI 0.76-47.58; very low); adverse effects RR 1.48 (very low). Clobetasol vs tacrolimus — very-low-certainty signal favoring tacrolimus for pain resolution.
