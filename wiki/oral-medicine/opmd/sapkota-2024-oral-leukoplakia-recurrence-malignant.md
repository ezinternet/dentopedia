## One-line Summary
SR+MA of 80 studies (n=7,614): pooled OL recurrence after surgery = 22%; laser excision+vaporization combination best; recurrent OL carries 7.39× higher malignant transformation risk.

## 한줄요약
80개 연구 SR+MA(n=7,614): 구강백반증 (Oral Leukoplakia, OL) 수술 후 재발률 22%; 레이저 절제+기화 복합술이 최적; 재발 병변은 악성변환 (Malignant Transformation, MT) 위험 7.39배.

## Summary
This comprehensive SR+MA examined OL recurrence after surgical management across 80 studies. The pooled recurrence rate was 22%, with laser-based approaches consistently outperforming scalpel surgery and cryosurgery. Network meta-analysis identified the combination of laser excision and vaporization as the optimal strategy. Critically, recurrent OL lesions carried a 7.39-fold elevated MT risk compared to non-recurrent lesions, making recurrence prevention a direct cancer prevention strategy. Clinicopathological risk factors for recurrence included non-homogeneous subtype, retromolar/multiple sites, female sex, advanced age, inadequate margins, and betel quid chewing.

## Key Contributions
- First network meta-analysis comparing all major surgical modalities for OL recurrence
- Establishes that recurrence ≠ treatment failure alone — it is an independent MT risk amplifier (RR 7.39)
- Laser excision + vaporization identified as the evidence-based preferred approach
- Quantifies clinicopathological recurrence predictors enabling risk-stratified surveillance

## Methodology
SR+MA following PRISMA guidelines. EMBASE, PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science searched through April 2024. N=80 studies, n=7,614. Surgical modalities: laser excision, laser vaporization, scalpel, cryosurgery, PDT, combinations. Subgroup analyses by site, size, dysplasia grade, margin, risk habits. Network meta-analysis for treatment comparison. Meta-regression for age, sex, follow-up.

## Results
- Pooled recurrence: **22%** across all modalities
- Conventional scalpel surgery: up to 49% recurrence at 5 years
- Laser-based combined approach (excision + vaporization): lowest recurrence in network meta-analysis
- Recurrence → MT risk: **RR = 7.39** vs. non-recurrent OL
- Independent recurrence risk factors: NHOL, retromolar/multiple sites, older age, female sex, inadequate margin, betel quid chewing
