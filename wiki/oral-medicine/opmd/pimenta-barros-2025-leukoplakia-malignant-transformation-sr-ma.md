## Summary
This is the most comprehensive SR+MA on oral leukoplakia (OL) malignant transformation to date, spanning 90 years of literature (1934–June 2024). Across 55 longitudinal cohort studies enrolling 41,231 patients with OL, the pooled MT rate was 6.64% — lower than the prior landmark estimate of 9.8% (Aguirre-Urizar 2021) but higher than the 3.5% estimate of Warnakulasuriya & Ariyawardana 2016. The authors attribute the lower-than-Aguirre-Urizar figure to the broader time span and larger n dampening the effect of high-MT small-cohort outliers. Importantly, MT rates did not significantly change across historical time periods (pre-1978, 1979–2007, post-2007; p=0.75), suggesting that despite advances in diagnosis and treatment, the underlying natural history of OL remains stubbornly stable. The strongest MT amplifiers are lesion morphology (non-homogeneous subtype RR=4.23), histological grade (epithelial dysplasia present RR=2.75), anatomical site (lateral tongue RR=2.09, carrying a 12.71% absolute MT rate — the highest of any subsite), and tobacco exposure (RR=1.64). These findings directly inform the OLK risk-stratification ladder: non-homogeneous morphology, dysplasia, and lateral tongue location each independently mandate biopsy and active surveillance regardless of patient habits.

## Key Contributions
- Largest-ever OL MT meta-analysis: 55 studies, n=41,231, 1934–June 2024, exceeding all prior SR+MAs in scope
- Revised pooled MT estimate: 6.64% (95% CI 5.21–8.21) — resolves the 3.5% vs 9.8% discrepancy between earlier SR+MAs
- First to demonstrate that MT rates are temporally stable (p=0.75 across three historical eras) — no secular improvement in natural history
- Quantifies RR for four major modifiable and non-modifiable risk factors with precise CIs, enabling evidence-based risk stratification
- Establishes lateral tongue as the highest-risk subsite with a 12.71% absolute MT rate (RR=2.09)
- Identifies non-homogeneous morphology as the strongest clinical predictor (RR=4.23) — surpassing dysplasia (RR=2.75) as a readily visible bedside sign

## Methodology
- Design: SR+MA following Cochrane guidelines and Joanna Briggs Institute CoCoPop framework (Condition–Context–Population)
- PROSPERO registration: CRD42024574224
- Databases: MEDLINE, Embase, Web of Science, Scopus; no lower date limit; searched through June 2024
- Inclusion criteria: longitudinal cohort studies reporting OL MT probability; no language restriction
- Exclusion criteria: proliferative verrucous leukoplakia (PVL), oral lichen planus (OLP), oral submucous fibrosis (OSMF), case-control designs, cross-sectional studies, RCTs altering natural OL history
- Risk of bias: Joanna Briggs Institute tool for meta-analyses of proportions
- Heterogeneity: explored via subgroup meta-analyses by time period, morphological subtype, site, and risk-habit exposure
- 55 studies included; n=41,231

## Results
- **Pooled MT rate: 6.64%** (95% CI 5.21–8.21)
- By historical era: 5.35% (pre-1978) / 7.06% (1979–2007) / 6.97% (post-2007) — no significant difference (p=0.75)
- **Non-homogeneous OL: RR = 4.23** (95% CI 3.31–5.39, p<0.001) — strongest clinical predictor
- **Epithelial dysplasia present: RR = 2.75** (95% CI 2.26–3.35, p<0.001)
- **Larger lesion size: RR = 2.08** (95% CI 1.45–2.96, p<0.001)
- **Lateral tongue: MT = 12.71%; RR = 2.09** (95% CI 1.48–2.95, p<0.001) — highest absolute MT of any subsite
- **Smoking: RR = 1.64** (95% CI 1.25–2.15, p<0.001)
- Comparison with prior SR+MAs: Aguirre-Urizar 2021 = 9.8%; Warnakulasuriya & Ariyawardana 2016 = 3.5%; this study = 6.64%
