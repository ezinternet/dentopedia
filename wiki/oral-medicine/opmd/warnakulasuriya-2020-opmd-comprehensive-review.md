## One-line Summary
Comprehensive narrative review of oral potentially malignant disorders (OPMD), covering WHO classification (leukoplakia, erythroplakia, OLP, OSF, actinic cheilitis), risk factors, malignant transformation rates (OLP ~0.5–1.4%, leukoplakia ~3–17.5% with non-homogeneous highest), and management via risk-factor elimination, excision, and surveillance.

## Summary
Comprehensive narrative review covering the OPMD spectrum: definition, WHO classification, risk factors, malignant transformation rates, and management.

## Key Contributions
- WHO OPMD classification: leukoplakia, erythroplakia, OLP, OSF, actinic cheilitis
- Malignant transformation rates: OLP 0.4–12.5%, leukoplakia 1–17.5%
- Management: smoking cessation, lesion excision, surveillance protocols

## Methodology
Narrative review based on PubMed and Cochrane literature.

## Results
OLP MTR ~0.5–1.4% (strict criteria). Leukoplakia MTR ~3–17%; non-homogeneous higher risk. Risk factor elimination is first-line management.
