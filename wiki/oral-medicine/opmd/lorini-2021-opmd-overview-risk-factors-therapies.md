## One-line Summary
Narrative overview mapping oral potentially malignant disorder (OPMD) subtypes (OLK, OLP, OE, OSF, actinic cheilitis) to their risk factors, malignant transformation rates (OLK ~3–17%, OLP ~0.5–3%), and therapies (OLP: topical corticosteroids first-line, tacrolimus second-line; OSF: surgery when mouth opening <35mm).

## Summary
Narrative overview mapping OPMD subtypes (OLK, OLP, OE, OSF, actinic cheilitis) to their respective risk factors, transformation rates, and available therapies.

## Key Contributions
- OLK risk stratification: homogeneous vs non-homogeneous; size >200mm²
- OLP treatment: topical corticosteroids (1st line), tacrolimus (2nd line)
- OSF: areca nut primary cause; severity-based surgical algorithm

## Methodology
PubMed narrative review.

## Results
OLK MTR ~3–17%; non-homogeneous highest. OLP MTR ~0.5–3%. OSF requires surgical intervention when mouth opening <35mm.
