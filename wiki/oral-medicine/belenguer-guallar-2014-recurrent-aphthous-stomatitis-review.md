## One-line Summary
Narrative literature review of recurrent aphthous stomatitis (RAS) treatment: topical corticosteroids (triamcinolone, clobetasol) remain the gold standard for pain and healing-time reduction, with low-level laser therapy (LLLT) a safe effective adjunct and chlorhexidine giving modest reduction in ulcer frequency.

## Summary
Literature review comparing topical corticosteroids, antimicrobials, NSAIDs, and low-level laser therapy for RAS.

## Key Contributions
- Topical steroids (triamcinolone, clobetasol) strongest evidence for pain and healing time reduction
- Low-level laser therapy (LLLT): safe and effective adjunct reducing pain and ulcer size
- Chlorhexidine mouthwash: modest reduction in ulcer frequency

## Methodology
PubMed narrative review.

## Results
Topical corticosteroids remain gold standard. LLLT effective and safe alternative especially for frequent recurrences.
