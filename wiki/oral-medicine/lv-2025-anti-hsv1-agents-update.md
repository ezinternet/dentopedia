## Summary

This pharmacology review updates the anti-HSV-1 therapeutic landscape. Nucleoside/nucleotide analogs (aciclovir, valaciclovir, famciclovir) remain first-line but face resistance via thymidine-kinase and DNA-polymerase mutations, especially in immunocompromised hosts. Non-nucleoside helicase-primase inhibitors (pritelivir, amenamevir) offer activity against resistant strains. The review also surveys immunomodulatory and natural-product candidates and emerging latency-targeting/gene-editing strategies, motivated by the continued lack of an approved HSV vaccine.

## Key Contributions

- Updates mechanisms of approved and investigational anti-HSV-1 agents.
- Centers nucleoside-analog resistance as the core clinical problem.
- Reviews helicase-primase inhibitors as a resistance-active class.
- Surveys immunomodulatory, natural-product, and latency-targeting directions.

## Methodology

Narrative review of HSV-1 replication (entry via gB/gC/gD and heparan sulfate; sensory-ganglion latency) and the pharmacology of antiviral drug classes.

## Results

Global HSV-1 seroprevalence ~64% (<50 y). Nucleoside analogs remain standard despite resistance; helicase-primase inhibitors provide an alternative mechanism. No approved vaccine exists, sustaining the drug-development imperative. Most novel agents are preclinical/early-phase.
