## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 21 RCTs (n=1668): thalidomide significantly raised complete response (vs placebo RR 6.95; vs other drugs RR 2.18), prolonged recurrence interval (MD +26.91 d), accelerated healing and reduced ulcer number/size and TNF-α, but showed no pain (VAS) benefit and significantly more adverse events (RR 1.87) — best reserved for major or topical-refractory RAS at 25 mg/d.

## Summary

This is the first systematic review + meta-analysis evaluating thalidomide's efficacy and safety for recurrent aphthous stomatitis (RAS), pooling 21 RCTs (1668 patients) from English and Chinese databases. Thalidomide produced a large complete-response benefit across all comparisons (vs placebo, vs other drugs, and as add-on combination) and improved several secondary outcomes — recurrence interval, healing time, ulcer number/diameter, and TNF-α. However, it did not significantly reduce pain (VAS), and adverse events were significantly more frequent (RR 1.87), including peripheral neurotoxicity and thromboembolism. The authors position thalidomide as a reserve systemic agent for major or topical-refractory RAS rather than first-line, converging on 25 mg/d as the efficacy-safety sweet spot, with immediate cessation on peripheral-neuropathy symptoms and absolute contraindication in pregnancy.

## Key Contributions

- First systematic evaluation of thalidomide for RAS, pooling 21 RCTs (1668 patients; 15 Chinese / 6 English publications, 17 of 21 trials from China).
- Quantifies a large complete-response benefit explicitly weighed against a significant rise in adverse events, supporting thalidomide as a reserve agent (major RAS or topical-refractory cases), not first-line.
- Provides a dosing synthesis converging on 25 mg/d as the efficacy-safety sweet spot.

## Methodology

- Databases (inception–Dec 2023): Cochrane, PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, Embase, CNKI, CBM, Wanfang, VIP. Search: (thalidomide OR sedoval) AND RAS terms. PROSPERO CRD42024495038; PRISMA-compliant.
- PICOS: RAS patients (including AIDS/Behçet/acute leukaemia comorbidity); oral thalidomide alone or combined; comparator placebo or any non-thalidomide intervention; RCTs/CCTs.
- Primary outcomes: complete response, overall response (= complete + partial). Secondary: recurrence interval (RI), ulcer number, healing time, VAS, ulcer diameter, immunological data, adverse events.
- Three comparison subgroups: (a) thalidomide vs placebo, (b) thalidomide vs other drug, (c) thalidomide + other drug vs other drug alone.
- Cochrane RoB tool; RevMan 5.4; RR (Mantel-Haenszel) for dichotomous, MD for continuous; I² >50% → random-effects.

## Results

**Complete response (all significant):**
- vs placebo (3 studies): RR 6.95 (95% CI 3.04–15.92), I²=39%, p<0.00001.
- vs other drug (8 trials): RR 2.18 (1.38–3.45), I²=68%, p=0.008.
- combination vs other drug alone (10 trials): RR 2.44 (1.55–3.84), I²=59%, p=0.0001.

**Overall response:**
- vs placebo (1 study): RR 3.59 (1.87–6.89).
- vs other drug (7 studies): RR 1.18 (0.99–1.41), I²=80%, p=0.07 — NOT significant.
- combination vs other drug alone (10 studies): RR 1.32 (1.23–1.42), I²=0%, p<0.00001 — significant.

**Secondary:**
- Recurrence interval (3 studies): MD +26.91 d (2.29–51.54), p=0.03, I²=99% — prolonged.
- Healing time (4 studies): MD −1.31 d (−2.06 to −0.56), p=0.0006, I²=70% — faster.
- Ulcer number (4 studies): MD −0.98 (−1.87 to −0.08), p=0.03, I²=91% — reduced.
- Ulcer size/diameter (2 RCTs): MD −0.85 (−1.02 to −0.68), p<0.00001, I²=4% — reduced.
- VAS pain (3 studies): MD −0.64 (−1.75 to 0.47), p=0.26, I²=91% — NO benefit.
- Immunology: 3 of 4 TNF-α studies showed reduction; thalidomide+cimetidine lowered IgA/IgG/IgM.

**Adverse events:** thalidomide-containing regimens RR 1.87 (1.17–2.99), p=0.009, I²=36%. Withdrawal due to AEs: 12 (thalidomide) vs 1 (control). AEs: peripheral neurotoxicity, thrombosis/embolism, constipation, somnolence, dizziness, rash.

**Dosing:** Early 200 mg/d regimens caused high AE incidence; 25 mg/d gave long-term recurrence-interval prolongation with better safety, and 50 mg/d was not superior to 25 mg/d as maintenance. Authors favor 25 mg/d, stopping immediately on peripheral-neuropathy symptoms; absolute contraindication in pregnancy/women planning conception.

**Limitations:** low methodological quality of most trials (poor blinding/allocation-concealment → high RoB); considerable clinical heterogeneity (I² up to 91–99% in several pools); 17/21 trials from China → limited generalizability.
