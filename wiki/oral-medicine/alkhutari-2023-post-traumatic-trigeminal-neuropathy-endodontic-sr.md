## One-line Summary

Systematic review of 10 studies (n = 8 to 1,331) finding that endodontic therapy accounts for 6-8% of post-traumatic trigeminal neuropathy (PTTN) cases, with non-odontogenic neuropathic pain emerging 3-48 months after root canal treatment, predominantly in women in their mid-40s, frequently misdiagnosed as atypical odontalgia / persistent dentoalveolar pain.

## Summary

Painful post-traumatic trigeminal neuropathy (PTTN) is a trauma-induced trigeminal nerve lesion with sensory dysfunction; when it follows dental work it is the iatrogenic injury most directly attributable to the operative dentist. This systematic review (10 studies, level 1-2 evidence, low-to-moderate risk of bias, published 2009-2020) isolates **endodontic therapy / root canal treatment (RCT)** as a discrete cause. Across the largest series, endodontics accounts for **6-8% of all trigeminal nerve injuries** — behind third molars, implants, extractions, and local anesthesia, but a non-trivial and under-recognized share. The neuropathic pain that follows is overwhelmingly **non-odontogenic**, yet is repeatedly mislabeled atypical odontalgia (AO) or persistent dentoalveolar pain (PDAP), driving patients into unnecessary repeat procedures and chronic pain.

The onset window after endodontic treatment is **3-48 months**; the demographic signature is a **female (65-85% of PDAP cases) in her mid-40s**. Mechanistically, PTTN after failed endodontics arises from over-instrumentation/perforation communicating with the external root surface, internal/external resorption defects, root fracture, and — most relevant to **mandibular molars** — root apices lying close to the **inferior dental (alveolar) canal and/or mental foramen**. The review endorses Graff-Radford & Solberg (1992) diagnostic criteria and a workup combining clinical exam, diagnostic local anesthetic testing, CBCT, and neuropathic-pain screens (VAS, S-LANSS, OHIP-14, QST).

## Key Contributions

- **Endodontics quantified as an iatrogenic PTTN cause**: 6% (Klazen 2018, IANI series) to 8% (Van der Cruyssen 2020, n=1,331; Renton & Yilmaz IANI) of trigeminal nerve injuries.
- **Onset interval**: PTTN appears 3-48 months after RCT; non-odontogenic pain after ≥6 months post-RCT = 34% (95% CI 1.4-5.5).
- **Post-RCT prevalence of persistent non-odontogenic pain**: 3.1% (Klasser 2011), 3.4% (Pigg), 3-6% AO (Ram), 5.7% phantom pain (Jacobs, women:men 9:1).
- **Misdiagnosis warning**: insufficient awareness of the RCT–PTTN link leads to wrong diagnoses and avoidable repeat treatment.
- **Predictive factors** (Philpott 2018): post-RCT, female gender, history of chronic pain, pulp-test-responsive teeth, preoperative pain, preexisting tooth crack, diameter of preoperative radiolucency.

## Methodology

- Databases: PubMed, Web of Science, Cochrane CENTRAL; English only; 2009-2020; human subjects.
- Five independent reviewers; third reviewer adjudicated disagreements; PRISMA flow.
- 10 included studies: 2 retrospective clinical trials, 4 retrospective, 1 retrospective observational, 1 retrospective+prospective cohort, 1 prospective cohort, 1 prospective longitudinal cohort; sample sizes 8-1,331; mean age 40-55.4 yrs.
- Systematic review without meta-analysis (heterogeneous terminology/outcomes precluded pooling).

## Results

- **Cause ranking** (Van der Cruyssen 2020, n=1,331): third molar 519 (48%) > dental implants 146 (13%) = non-third-molar extraction 136 (13%) > local anesthesia 125 (12%) > **endodontic treatment 86 (8%)** > trauma 74 (6-7%).
- **IANI causes** (Renton & Yilmaz): TM surgery 60%, LA 19%, implants 18%, **endodontics 8%**.
- **Symptoms**: continuous/almost-continuous unilateral aching/burning/throbbing pain >4 months; can spread bilaterally in up to one-third; allodynia/hyper-or-hypoalgesia in 96% of PPTTN cases (Benoliel 2012); impaired conditioned pain modulation (CPM) and prolonged cold after-sensation distinguish PTTN from controls.
- **Arch**: conflicting (Oshima 87.5% maxillary; List 56% upper vs 45% lower; Nixdorf 53% maxillary, 89% posterior; Klasser higher mandibular, no sex preference) → review concludes no consistent maxilla/mandible predilection.
- **Mechanism after failed endodontics** (most relevant to operative dentists): over-instrumentation/perforation to external root surface, resorption defects, root fracture, mandibular molar apices close to the inferior dental canal/mental foramen, dens invaginatus, periapical lesions, cysts.
- **Diagnosis**: Graff-Radford & Solberg (1992) criteria; AO criteria = pain in a tooth/site, continuous, >4 months, no local/referred signs, equivocal somatic nerve block; diagnostic LA / anesthetic test (benzocaine 20% then 2% lidocaine, VAS at 3-min intervals).
- **Management for pain**: amitriptyline 50-75 mg/day and/or carbamazepine 400-600 mg/day + vitamin B complex.
