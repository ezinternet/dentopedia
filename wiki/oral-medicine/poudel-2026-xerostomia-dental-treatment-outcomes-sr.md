## One-line Summary

Systematic review (16 studies, 1227 patients) showing xerostomia roughly doubles–triples restoration failure (HR ~2.6–2.9, recurrent caries) while implant survival stays >90% except in radiation-induced cases; no major periodontal-therapy penalty; evidence GRADE low-to-moderate, RoB moderate-to-serious.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1186/s12903-026-07815-8](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12903-026-07815-8)), this systematic review (16 studies, 1227 patients; no meta-analysis due to heterogeneity) is the first to synthesize xerostomia's impact across all dental treatment modalities — restorations, implants, periodontal therapy, prostheses, and patient-reported outcomes — spanning Sjögren's, radiation-induced, medication-induced, and other etiologies. The dominant finding is that xerostomia substantially shortens **restoration longevity** (hazard ratio ~2.6–2.9 vs controls; 5-year survival ~55% vs ~75% in Sjögren's), mainly through recurrent caries at restoration margins. **Implant survival** stays high (~94% average, moderate certainty) and comparable to controls in autoimmune xerostomia (97–100% in Sjögren's cohorts), but **radiation-induced xerostomia carries a distinctly higher implant failure risk** (failures within the first year; success ~86.7% under Albrektsson criteria). **Periodontal therapy** may heal more slowly short-term but reaches equivalent long-term outcomes with diligent maintenance. All rehabilitative treatments improved patient-reported OHRQoL, though Sjögren's patients stayed worse than controls. Overall certainty is low-to-moderate; 10/16 studies were at serious risk of bias.

Clinically: in xerostomic patients (구강건조증), anticipate higher preventive/maintenance needs — high-fluoride regimens, sealants, frequent recall — and prefer fluoride-releasing or moisture-tolerant restoratives (특히 fluoride 비순응 환자). Implants are a viable option in autoimmune dry mouth but warrant more cautious case selection and follow-up in **radiation-induced** xerostomia.

## Key Contributions

- First cross-modality synthesis of xerostomia outcomes across restorations, implants, periodontal therapy, prostheses, and PROs, stratified where possible by etiology.
- Quantifies the restoration-failure penalty (HR ~2.6–2.9) absent from prior implant-only reviews.
- Separates the **autoimmune vs radiation** implant story: high survival for Sjögren's, elevated early failure for post-radiotherapy.
- Provides material guidance (fluoride-releasing GIC) for radiation-induced xerostomia restorations.
- Identifies prosthesis-retention evidence as a near-empty research gap (single n=8 study).

## Methodology

- **Design**: systematic review (PRISMA-P; PROSPERO CRD420251035790); no meta-analysis (heterogeneity).
- **Search**: PubMed, Embase, Google Scholar (first 200 results) + reference screening; up to June 2025; English, adults ≥18 y.
- **PICO**: clinical outcomes of dental treatment in patients with xerostomia/hyposalivation (any etiology).
- **Included designs**: 1 RCT, 1 non-randomized interventional, 5 prospective cohorts, 7 retrospective cohorts, 2 cross-sectional.
- **Risk of bias**: RoB 2 (RCT), ROBINS-I (non-randomized); **certainty**: GRADE per outcome.
- **Screening funnel**: 244 records → 217 de-duped → 48 full-text → 16 included.

## Results

1. **Sample**: 16 studies, **1,227 patients**; n per study 8–292; mean ages 50–70 y; female-predominant Sjögren's cohorts.
2. **Restoration survival (5 studies, GRADE low)**: HR ~2–3 for failure in xerostomia. Gomez 2024 HR ≈2.9 (95% CI 2.3–3.7), ~1 y earlier failures, 5-y survival ~55% vs ~75%; Leinonen 2021 HR ≈2.6 (p<0.001), Sjögren's/radiation worse than unspecified hyposalivation. Failures driven by **recurrent caries** at margins.
3. **Restorative materials**: fluoride-releasing GIC reduced recurrent caries vs composite (McComb 2002, De Moor 2011; p<0.05) but more marginal erosion; Haveman 2003 — fluoride users had no recurrent caries regardless of material.
4. **Implant survival (8 studies, ~300 implants, GRADE moderate)**: Sjögren's cohorts 97–100% at 18 mo–5 y (Maarse 2022 100% vs 96% control p=0.06; Hosseini 2025 100% both; Korfage 2015 97% vs 100% ns; Albrecht 2016 ~100% both). **Overall ~94%**.
5. **Radiation-induced implant risk**: higher failure — ~95.2% survival at 1 y, success ~86.7% (Albrektsson); failures occur within first year (vascular/ischemic mechanism). Siddiqui 2017 87.6% vs 95.7% controls (p<0.001); Bidyasagar 2023 10% vs 0%.
6. **Periodontal (2 studies, GRADE low)**: Kaur 2018 slower early gingival improvement (mouth-breathers), equalized by 12 weeks; Sparrow 2021 no maintenance-phase difference in probing depth/plaque. **No major long-term periodontal-therapy difference.**
7. **Patient-reported outcomes (GRADE moderate)**: consistent OHRQoL gains post-implant rehab (Hosseini OHIP-49 47.5→30.4; Maarse OHIP-14 33.7→28.8 over 18 mo), but Sjögren's scores persistently worse than controls.
8. **Prosthesis retention (very low)**: only Isidor 1999 (n=8) — no implant-retained dentures lost/remade over 2 y.
9. **Risk of bias / certainty**: 10/16 (62.5%) serious RoB; single RCT high RoB; GRADE low-to-moderate across outcomes.
