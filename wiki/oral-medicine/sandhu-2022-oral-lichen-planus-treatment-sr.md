## One-line Summary

Systematic review comparing efficacy and treatment costs of OLP interventions: corticosteroids remain the efficacy-cost benchmark; tacrolimus is effective but costlier; cost reporting across the literature is inconsistent.

## 한줄요약

OLP 치료의 효능·비용 비교 체계적 문헌고찰: 스테로이드가 효능-비용 기준, 타크롤리무스는 효과적이나 고가, 문헌의 비용 보고 일관성 부족.

## Summary

This systematic review is among the few to weigh OLP treatment efficacy alongside direct cost. Across topical corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, and systemic agents, topical corticosteroids deliver favorable efficacy at relatively low cost and remain the pragmatic first line, while tacrolimus is effective but more expensive. The authors surface cost as an under-reported decision variable: inconsistent cost reporting across studies prevents formal cost-effectiveness conclusions. The review complements efficacy-only syntheses by adding a value-based selection lens.

## Key Contributions

- Jointly weighs efficacy and direct treatment cost in OLP.
- Provides a comparative framework across drug classes.
- Surfaces cost as an under-reported decision variable.
- Confirms corticosteroids as first-line on efficacy-cost grounds.

## Methodology

PRISMA-based systematic literature search of OLP intervention studies reporting efficacy and/or cost; qualitative comparative synthesis.

## Results

Topical corticosteroids: favorable efficacy at low cost. Calcineurin inhibitors (tacrolimus): effective but costlier. Inconsistent cost reporting limits formal cost-effectiveness modeling.
