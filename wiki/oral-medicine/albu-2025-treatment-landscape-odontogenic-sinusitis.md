## Summary
Narrative review synthesizing diagnosis, microbiology, and management of odontogenic sinusitis (ODS). ODS accounts for 25-40% of maxillary-sinus chronic rhinosinusitis, mostly iatrogenic or from apical periodontitis, and is dominated by anaerobes with frequent beta-lactamase positivity. Empirical antibiotics (amoxicillin or amoxicillin-clavulanate; fluoroquinolones if penicillin-allergic) provide symptom relief but rarely cure when a treatable dental focus persists. Eliminating the dental source is essential; for oroantral fistulas, infected sinus bone grafts, or implants, concurrent one-stage dental + endoscopic sinus surgery (ESS) gives the highest success (~97%). In uncomplicated ODS, maxillary antrostomy alone may suffice, while apical-periodontitis ODS shows low short-term recurrence after primary ESS.

## Key Contributions
- Positions amox-clav as empirical first-line but reframes antibiotics as adjunctive, not curative, for ODS.
- Summarizes evidence that concurrent dental + ESS minimizes reintervention/recurrence (~97% success).
- Clarifies surgical extent debate (maxillary antrostomy alone vs full ESS) and recurrence risk factors.

## Methodology
Narrative review, PubMed/Web of Science/Cochrane, Jan 2008-Aug 2025; final analysis 6 systematic reviews + 24 clinical studies; descriptive synthesis; two independent reviewers.

## Results
- Empirical therapy amoxicillin/amox-clav first-line; endodontic-infection bacteria show 90-100% susceptibility to penicillin/amoxicillin/amox-clav.
- Antibiotic monotherapy not curative for treatable-source ODS (expert consensus).
- Concurrent dental + ESS ~97-98% success; recurrence <1-6% across cohorts.
- Maxillary antrostomy alone achieved high resolution in uncomplicated cases incl. frontal involvement.
- Smoking, diabetes, higher Lund-Mackay scores predict failure/recurrence; iatrogenic (implant) ODS frequently needs ESS (~79%).
