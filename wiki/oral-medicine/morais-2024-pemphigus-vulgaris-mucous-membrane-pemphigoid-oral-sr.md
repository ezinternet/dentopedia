## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1016/j.jormas.2024.101960](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jormas.2024.101960)), this systematic review compared pemphigus vulgaris (PV) and mucous membrane pemphigoid (MMP) — two mucocutaneous autoimmune blistering diseases with very similar clinical pictures — across demographic, clinical, diagnostic, and treatment axes, using only cases confirmed by direct immunofluorescence (DIF) that showed intraoral manifestations. Thirty-two articles (18 PV, 15 MMP) yielded 50 PV and 123 MMP cases. The review is **abstract-only** here (full text not retrieved), so quality-appraisal and pooling methods are not captured. Both diseases presented predominantly in women (PV 64 %, MMP 81.3 %) in the fifth (PV) and sixth (MMP) decades, with the mouth as the primary involvement site (PV 71.4 %, MMP 91 %). The distinguishing intraoral feature: PV favors the cheek (buccal) mucosa (30 %) whereas MMP favors the gingiva (64.2 %, fitting its desquamative-gingivitis presentation). DIF was IgG-positive in 100 % of both. Treatment defaults diverged — systemic corticosteroids for PV (50 %), topical therapy for MMP (53.7 %).

Clinically: because both PV and MMP often present first in the mouth, the dentist is frequently the first clinician to see them — a gingiva-dominant desquamative/erosive picture should raise MMP, a buccal-mucosa-dominant picture PV, and any persistent vesiculo-erosive mucosal lesion warrants DIF biopsy (IgG-positive in both) before committing to therapy.

## Key Contributions

- Isolates the features that separate two clinically near-identical autoimmune blistering diseases (PV vs MMP) using DIF-confirmed intraoral cases.
- Confirms the oral cavity as the dominant presenting site for both — underscoring the dentist's role in first detection.
- Quantifies the key intraoral discriminator: PV → cheek/buccal mucosa; MMP → gingiva.
- Anchors diagnosis on DIF IgG positivity (100 % in both) and documents the divergent first-line treatments (systemic vs topical).

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review of literature describing DIF-diagnosed PV and MMP cases with intraoral manifestations.
- **Included studies**: 32 articles (18 PV, 15 MMP).
- **Cases**: 50 PV, 123 MMP.
- **Outcomes**: clinical/demographic profile, diagnostic methods, treatment choice.
- **Caveat**: abstract-only — risk-of-bias / quality-appraisal method not recoverable; underlying evidence is case reports/series, so percentages are descriptive, not comparative-statistical.

## Results

1. **Sex**: women predominate — PV 64 %, MMP 81.3 %.
2. **Age**: PV peaks in the 5th decade; MMP in the 6th decade.
3. **Mouth as primary site**: PV 71.4 %, MMP 91 %.
4. **Top intraoral site**: PV → cheek (buccal) mucosa 30 %; MMP → gingiva 64.2 %.
5. **Diagnosis**: direct immunofluorescence IgG-positive in 100 % of both conditions.
6. **First-line treatment**: PV → systemic corticosteroids 50 %; MMP → topical therapy 53.7 %.
7. **Bottom line**: differences in intraoral site predilection, extraoral involvement, and diagnostic-test results define distinct PV vs MMP profiles that support differential diagnosis and therapeutic management.
