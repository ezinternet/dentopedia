## One-line Summary

PRISMA systematic review (PROSPERO CRD42024555839) of small fiber neuropathy in BMS: across included studies, tongue biopsies show 30–60% reductions in nerve fiber density plus altered TRPV1/P2X3/NGF expression, Nav1.7 overexpression, and sensory threshold abnormalities, strongly supporting SFN as a core pathophysiologic mechanism.

## Summary

Burning mouth syndrome (BMS) is a chronic idiopathic orofacial pain disorder with prevalence ranging from ~1.73% in the general population to ~7.72% in clinical settings (≈5.58–6.46% in Europe), most common in postmenopausal women (female-to-male ratio ~3:1). This PRISMA systematic review, pre-registered in PROSPERO (CRD42024555839), evaluates evidence that small fiber neuropathy (SFN) underlies BMS. Eight studies reported reduced tongue/intraepithelial nerve fiber density (30–60%) with morphologic changes indicative of small-fiber damage. Molecular analyses showed increased TRPV1-, NGF-, and P2X3-positive fibers, overexpression of Nav1.7, slight underexpression of Nav1.9 mRNA, and increased artemin mRNA. Quantitative Sensory Testing in seven studies revealed reduced cool detection and cold pain thresholds, and blink reflex and corneal confocal microscopy indicated both peripheral and central small-fiber damage. The evidence strongly supports SFN as a key factor in BMS pathophysiology, though high between-study variability precludes meta-analysis and causal inference.

## Key Contributions

- First PRISMA-registered systematic synthesis dedicated to SFN evidence in BMS.
- Consolidates histopathologic evidence: 30–60% nerve fiber density reduction across eight biopsy studies.
- Aggregates molecular markers: ↑TRPV1, ↑NGF, ↑P2X3 fibers; ↑Nav1.7; slight ↓Nav1.9 mRNA; ↑artemin mRNA.
- Aggregates functional/imaging evidence: QST cool-detection and cold-pain threshold reductions; blink reflex and corneal confocal microscopy abnormalities.

## Methodology

Systematic review following PRISMA, protocol pre-registered in PROSPERO (CRD42024555839). Synthesized evidence streams: tongue/mucosal biopsy nerve fiber density and immunohistochemistry; QST; blink reflex; corneal confocal microscopy; ion-channel and neurotrophic-factor mRNA. Narrative synthesis without meta-analytic pooling due to heterogeneity.

## Results

- Nerve fiber density: 30–60% reduction (8 biopsy studies).
- Molecular: ↑TRPV1, ↑NGF, ↑P2X3; ↑Nav1.7; slight ↓Nav1.9 mRNA; ↑artemin mRNA.
- QST (7 studies): reduced cool detection and cold pain thresholds — small-fiber dysfunction signature.
- Conclusion: SFN is a key factor in BMS pathophysiology, especially via reduced nerve fiber density and altered sensory thresholds; causality not established.
