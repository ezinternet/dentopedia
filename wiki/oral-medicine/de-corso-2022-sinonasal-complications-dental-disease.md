## One-line Summary

Retrospective cohort (n=262, Rome 2015–2022) of SCDDT managed with endoscopic endonasal FESS: 96.5% overall success rate, SNOT-22 improved from 43.4 to 6 at 15 days; 55.34% iatrogenic (implant/extraction) vs 44.65% dental pathology; simultaneous ENT+dental treatment feasible in 58% of cases.

## Summary

De Corso et al. report a real-world tertiary-center experience managing 262 patients with sinonasal complications of dental disease or treatment (SCDDT) using a standardized multidisciplinary protocol (ENT + dental + radiology). All patients underwent endoscopic endonasal sinus surgery (FESS) as the primary surgical modality — no Caldwell-Luc or external approach was used. The overall treatment success rate was 96.5%, with rapid QoL improvement documented by SNOT-22 (43.4 pre-op → 6 at 15 days, p < 0.05). The SCDDT terminology and classification by Felisati (2013) was applied throughout, distinguishing pre-implantologic (Group I, 8.4%), implantologic (Group II, 23.7%), and classic dental disease/treatment complications (Group III, 67.9%).

## Key Contributions

1. **Largest single-center real-world FESS series for SCDDT** (n=262, 7-year experience): confirms FESS as the definitive primary surgical approach with 96.5% success
2. **Felisati classification validated at scale**: full breakdown across all subclasses (3a/3b, 2a/2b/2c/2d, 1) reported; Group III predominates (67.9%)
3. **Simultaneous ENT + dental surgery is safe and feasible** in 58% of cases — reduces patient burden and rehabilitation time
4. **Implant-sparing policy**: for Group IIa (peri-implantitis without dislocation), FESS alone without implant removal was the default; removal reserved for frank implant dislocation (Groups 2b–2d)
5. **SNOT-22 as QoL benchmark**: 43.4 → 6 within 15 days demonstrates rapid, measurable improvement reproducible in clinical practice
6. **All involved sinuses should be treated**: authors advocate opening all diseased sinuses (including frontal when indicated) rather than restricting to maxillary antrostomy alone
7. **Iatrogenic causes now predominate** (55.34% vs 44.65% dental pathology) — rising implant/sinus lift volumes driving caseload

## Methodology

- **Design**: Monocentric retrospective cohort
- **Setting**: Tertiary ENT department, Gemelli IRCCS, Rome — 75.5% self-referred, 24.5% referred from dentist
- **Inclusion**: All SCDDT patients undergoing FESS, confirmed by CT + nasal endoscopy + dental evaluation
- **Classification**: Felisati 2013 SCDDT taxonomy (Group I: pre-implantologic; II: implantologic; III: classic)
- **Outcome measure**: Treatment success = symptom resolution + endoscopic sinus healing; SNOT-22 pre-op and at day 15
- **Surgery**: Endonasal endoscopic approach under general anesthesia; inferior uncinectomy, middle meatal antrostomy; additional sinusotomies for extended disease; prophylactic antibiotics 5 days pre-op and 10 days post-op
- **Timing policy**: Simultaneous dental+ENT preferred (58%); dental treatment postponed when clinically indicated

## Results

**Patient profile**: 262 patients; female 59.55%; mean age 50.84 years (range 21–78)

**Etiology split**:
- Iatrogenic 55.34% (145/262): implant-related 42.7%, extraction-related 34.5%, foreign bodies 13.1%
- Dental pathology 44.65% (117/262): endodontic complications 35%, dentigerous cyst/intrusion 29.9%, radicular cyst 24.8%, caries 10.2%

**SCDDT classification (Felisati)**:

| Group | Subclass | n (%) |
|---|---|---|
| I – Pre-implantologic | Sinusitis after sinus lift | 22 (8.4%) |
| II – Implantologic | 2a: Peri-implantitis ± OAF | 36 (13.8%) |
| | 2b: Implant dislocation + OAF | 6 (2.3%) |
| | 2c: Implant dislocation (no OAF) | 15 (5.7%) |
| | 2d: Implant dislocation only | 5 (1.9%) |
| III – Classic | 3a: Odontogenic sinusitis + OAF | 57 (21.7%) |
| | 3b: Odontogenic sinusitis (no OAF) | 121 (46.2%) |

**Symptoms**: anterior discharge 86.2%, posterior discharge 74.8%, nasal obstruction 61.4%, cacosmia 43.5%, facial pressure 50.8%, hyposmia 21.7%

**Disease extent (CT)**: maxillary only 38.5%, maxillary+ethmoid 50.4%, +frontal 9.9%, +sphenoid 1.1%

**Treatment outcomes**:
- Overall success: 96.5%
- OAF repair success: 95.8%
- Failures: 4 required prolonged antibiotics; 3 re-surgery for antrostomy closure/OAF; 2 revision for frontal sinus re-stenosis
- SNOT-22: 43.4 → 6 at 15 days (p < 0.05)

**Teeth most frequently involved**: 2nd molar (41%) > 1st molar (33%) > 2nd premolar+1st molar (11%)
