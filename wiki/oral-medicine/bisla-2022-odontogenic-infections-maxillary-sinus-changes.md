## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional CBCT study (n=213, 404 sinuses) — periodontal bone loss (OR 2.2) significantly outweighs periapical lesion size or proximity as a predictor of maxillary sinus mucosal thickening; overall mucosal change prevalence 49.5%.

## 한줄요약

단면연구(213명, 404개 상악동 CBCT)에서 치주골소실이 치근단병소보다 상악동 점막비후의 더 강한 예측인자(OR 2.2)이며 전체 점막변화 유병률 49.5%.

## Summary

This cross-sectional analytical CBCT study from India evaluated 213 systemically healthy patients (404 maxillary sinuses) referred for posterior maxillary CBCT imaging. Patients were divided into an odontogenic infection group (111 patients, 208 sinuses) and a control group (102 patients, 196 sinuses). Sinus mucosal changes were classified using a 0–6 MS evaluation score on CBCT; periapical lesions were graded by diameter (CBCTPI 1–5); periodontal bone loss was classified as mild/moderate/severe.

**Key finding**: Periodontal bone loss was 2.2 times more likely than periapical pathology to be associated with sinus mucosal changes (binary logistic regression). Periapical lesion size and distance from sinus floor showed no statistically significant association with mucosal thickening.

## Key Contributions

1. **Periodontal bone loss is the dominant odontogenic driver** of sinus mucosal thickening — more than periapical lesions alone (OR 2.2).
2. **Periapical lesion size does not predict mucosal change** severity (p=0.646) — contradicts some prior literature (Nunes et al.) but consistent with others (Aksoy et al.).
3. **Proximity of periapical lesion to sinus floor is non-significant** (p=0.49) — even juxtaposed lesions are not reliably more damaging than distant ones.
4. **Severe periodontal bone loss → 72% mucosal thickening** (p=0.008), versus 58.1% mild and 62.2% moderate.
5. **Rhinosinusitis history as confounder was controlled for** — equal distribution between groups ensures mucosal changes reflect odontogenic etiology.
6. **Mean mucosal thickness**: 6.19 ± 2.85 mm; consistent with prior literature threshold of >5 mm.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Cross-sectional analytical observational
- **n**: 213 patients (404 sinuses); study group 111, control 102
- **CBCT**: CS 9300 (Carestream), 60–90 kVp, 90–300 µm voxel, limited FOV
- **Sinus scoring**: MS evaluation score 0–6 (mucosal thickening ≥3 mm = score 1)
- **Periapical scoring**: CBCTPI 1–5 by lesion diameter
- **Periodontal bone loss**: mild <25%, moderate 25–50%, severe >50% of tooth height below sinus
- **Statistics**: Chi-square, binary logistic regression, SPSS
