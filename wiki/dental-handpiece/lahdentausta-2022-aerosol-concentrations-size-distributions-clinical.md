## One-line Summary

Prospective clinical aerosol-measurement study (84 patients, 253 procedures, optical particle sizer) showing air turbine handpieces generated the highest <1 μm and total aerosol concentrations, while high-/low-speed handpieces and ultrasonic scalers elevated aerosol vs oral examination.

## Summary

This study quantified aerosol generation across eight categories of routine dental procedures performed on 84 real patients (253 procedures) in a Helsinki specialist clinic during the COVID-19 pandemic, using an Optical Particle Sizer (0.3–10 μm) with per-procedure background subtraction. The air-driven **air turbine handpiece** was the dominant aerosol source, significantly elevating sub-micron (<1 μm) and total particle number concentrations and <1 μm particle mass versus background. **Low-speed handpieces** preferentially raised coarse (>5 μm) particle number and mass, while **high-speed (electric) handpieces** did not significantly exceed their own background but did exceed oral-examination levels. Because sub-micron particles penetrate deeper into the lungs and carry more viral copies, and because air-turbine aerosol clears slowly (median half-life ~21.7 min; estimated fallow time ~98.5 min), the authors support substituting the air turbine with an electric high-speed handpiece, alongside rubber dam, high-volume evacuation, and FFP respirators, when aerosol must be minimized.

## Key Contributions

- Rare **real-clinical** (not bench) quantification of handpiece aerosol with routine high-volume suction already in use.
- Size-resolved head-to-head: air turbine dominates **<1 μm**; low-speed handpiece dominates **>5 μm**.
- High-speed electric handpiece showed no significant rise over its own background — empirical basis for the air-turbine → high-speed substitution recommendation.
- Quantified clearance kinetics (half-life ~21.7 min; air-turbine fallow ~98.5 min) exceeding typical room-turnover intervals.

## Methodology

Prospective observational aerosol-measurement study. 84 patients (mean age 53.3 y), 253 procedures, 3 dentists, 6 operating rooms (air change rate 2.76–6.19 /h). Optical Particle Sizer TSI 3330 (0.3–10 μm, 16 channels) positioned ~50 cm above the patient head; 1-min background captured before each procedure. Procedure classes (n): oral exam 52, air turbine 8, high-speed 6, low-speed 30, ultrasonic scaling 31, hand-instrument periodontal 60, endodontic 12, intraoral radiograph 24, local anesthesia 31. Particle number and mass concentrations analyzed in <1 μm / 1–5 μm / >5 μm / total bins; emission rate and half-life modeled via the Indoor Aerosol Model. Non-parametric Mann-Whitney / Kruskal-Wallis (SPSS v27).

## Results

- **Air turbine:** <1 μm median p=0.013, maximum p=0.016; <1 μm particle mass p=0.046 / p=0.006 vs background. Total particle mass increase NS.
- **Low-speed:** >5 μm median p=0.023, maximum p=0.013; >5 μm mass p=0.021; maximum total particle mass p=0.022.
- **High-speed:** NS vs own background; significant vs oral examination.
- **Ultrasonic scaler:** slight >5 μm maximum number/mass elevation.
- Significant concentration peaks: air turbine 7/8; low-speed 2/30; high-speed 3/6; root canal 6/12.
- Emission rate: air turbine ~5.2×10⁸ #/min; root canal ~1.5×10⁸ #/min.
- Clearance: median half-life 21.7 min (range 8.5–47.9); estimated air-turbine fallow time ~98.5 min.
