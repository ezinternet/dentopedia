## One-line Summary

CDHA/ADHA consensus position paper (78 studies, 6 databases, 2012–2022) endorsing combined eyewear+mask+face-shield PPE, high/low-volume evacuation + saliva ejector + rubber dam, and appropriate ventilation/filtration to reduce aerosol transmission during dental AGPs; mouthrinses reduce bacterial contamination but show limited effect against SARS-CoV-2.

## Summary

Prompted by COVID-19, this joint position paper from the Canadian Dental Hygienists Association (CDHA) and the American Dental Hygienists' Association (ADHA) summarizes the evidence for measures that reduce infection-transmission risk during aerosol-generating procedures (AGPs) in dentistry and dental hygiene. It synthesizes four control axes — PPE, aerosol-reduction devices, mouthrinses, and operatory ventilation/filtration — and concludes that implementing evidence-supported practices improves both patient and provider safety, while calling for more research in real clinical environments.

Note: **abstract-only — full text not retrieved** (PMC full text was empty). All statements below are grounded strictly in the structured abstract; no quantitative effect sizes are reported there.

## Key Contributions

- A joint CDHA/ADHA consensus statement translating the AGP-mitigation literature into practice-level guidance for oral-health settings.
- Evidence organized across four control axes: PPE, aerosol-reduction devices (evacuation/suction/rubber dam), mouthrinses, and ventilation/filtration.
- Explicit flag that transmission-risk evidence between hygienists and patients — especially for SARS-CoV-2 — remains limited.

## Methodology

- Structured literature search of **6 databases**: MEDLINE, EMBASE, Scopus, Web of Science, Cochrane Library, Google Scholar.
- Publication window **January 2012 – December 2022**.
- Organized around **6 research questions** on transmission risk, methods, devices, and PPE for reducing pathogen contact and aerosol spread.
- **78 studies** met eligibility criteria and informed the position statement.
- (Study-level grading and per-question evidence tables are not available from the abstract-only artifact.)

## Results

- **Transmission risk**: Literature on infection transmission (including SARS-CoV-2) between dental hygienists and patients is **limited**.
- **Mouthrinses**: Several reduce **bacterial** aerosol contamination, but effectiveness against **SARS-CoV-2** is limited.
- **PPE**: Combined **eyewear + mask + face shield** effectively prevents contamination of the facial and nasal region during AGPs.
- **Aerosol-reduction devices**: **High-volume evacuation** (± intraoral suction), **low-volume evacuation**, **saliva ejector**, and **rubber dam** (when appropriate) reduce aerosol transmission beyond the generation site.
- **Operatory air**: An appropriate combination of **ventilation and filtration** limits aerosol spread.
- No pooled effect sizes, particle-count reductions, or numeric benchmarks are provided in the abstract.
