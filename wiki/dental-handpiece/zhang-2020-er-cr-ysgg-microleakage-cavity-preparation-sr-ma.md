## One-line Summary

First SR+MA (13 in-vitro studies, 11 meta-analyzed) finding Er,Cr:YSGG laser cavity preparation yields microleakage equivalent to traditional rotary burs, with adjunctive acid etching significantly improving self-etch adhesive sealing after laser preparation.

## Summary

This first systematic review and meta-analysis addressed the **instrument-indication** question of whether the Er,Cr:YSGG erbium laser is a worthwhile alternative to the rotary bur for cavity preparation, judged by restoration microleakage. Across 13 in-vitro studies (11 meta-analyzed, all moderate risk of bias), laser-prepared and bur-prepared cavities showed **no significant difference in microleakage** at enamel, dentin, or whole-margin level. The laser's theoretical advantages — no direct tooth contact, less vibration, noise, pressure, and pulpal thermal damage with water-mist cooling — do not translate into a better marginal seal, and its ablated surface (collagen fusion, acid-resistant enamel) can even impair adhesive penetration. The clinically actionable secondary finding is that **prior acid etching markedly improves the seal of self-etch adhesives** after laser preparation (RR=2.69), but not etch-and-rinse adhesives. Thus laser vs bur is an indication trade-off (patient comfort/no-anesthesia vs slower speed and no seal advantage), not a microleakage decision.

## Key Contributions

- First synthesis of Er,Cr:YSGG laser vs rotary bur for cavity-preparation microleakage.
- Laser ≈ bur on marginal seal — neither instrument superior for microleakage.
- Adjunctive acid etching strongly benefits self-etch adhesives after laser (RR=2.69) but not etch-and-rinse.
- Reframes laser-vs-bur as a comfort/speed indication trade-off rather than a sealing decision.

## Methodology

PRISMA SR+MA (PubMed, EBSCO, Embase, Cochrane CENTRAL to July 2019). Inclusion: RCT/quasi-RCT on human teeth comparing Er,Cr:YSGG vs bur microleakage, and laser ± acid etching; dropout <20%. 357 records → 13 included (11 meta-analyzed); published 2001–2018; Class V cavities in 10/13; 5 primary-teeth studies. RevMan 5.3; RR/MD + 95% CI; I²>50% → random-effects; Cochrane risk-of-bias (7 domains); reviewer agreement κ=0.93.

## Results

- **Microleakage (laser vs bur)**, random-effects, I²=80%: NS overall — dentin margin RR=1.26 (95% CI 0.67–2.38, p=0.47); whole margin RR=1.27 (0.44–3.67, p=0.66); enamel margin RR=0.87 (0.60–1.27, p=0.47).
- **Acid etching after laser, self-etch adhesives**: RR=2.69 (1.74–4.15, p<0.00001; I²=0%); enamel RR=3.0 (1.54–5.83); dentin RR=2.44 (1.38–4.34).
- **Acid etching after laser, etch-and-rinse**: NS, RR=1.18 (0.63–2.22, p=0.60; I²=81%).
- All included studies in vitro; dye-penetration microleakage; all moderate risk of bias.
