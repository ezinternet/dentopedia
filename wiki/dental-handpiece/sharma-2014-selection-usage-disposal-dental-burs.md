## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional questionnaire survey (131/150 practitioners, abstract-only ingest) showing diamond burs preferred (75%) over tungsten carbide (15%), most reused until worn out (85%), with variable sterilization and universal dustbin disposal.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

This cross-sectional questionnaire survey of 131 practitioners documents real-world bur-selection behavior that anchors the "bur types and their roles" question clinically. Diamond (abrasive/grinding) burs were preferred by 75% versus 15% for tungsten-carbide (bladed/cutting) burs, reflecting the perception that diamonds cut more efficiently irrespective of grit size. Most clinicians reused burs until worn out (85%), only a third recognized that cutting efficiency declines with wear, sterilization frequency was inconsistent (daily 35% / per-patient 35%), and worn burs were universally discarded into the general dustbin (100%, an infection-control concern). **This page is an abstract-only ingest** — the PMC full-text body was returned empty — so it captures attitudes/practice rather than measured cutting efficiency.

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies clinician bur preference: diamond 75% >> tungsten carbide 15%.
- Documents reuse-until-worn behavior (85%) and limited awareness of efficiency decline (33%).
- Flags inconsistent sterilization and non-segregated sharps disposal (100% dustbin).

## Methodology

Cross-sectional questionnaire: 20 items (4 options each) across selection / usage-sterilization / disposal domains; mailed to 150 local practitioners with telephone follow-up; 131 responses (87%); results expressed as percentages. (Abstract-only — no further methodological detail available.)

## Results

- Bur preference: diamond 75%, tungsten carbide 15%.
- Reuse until worn out: 85%; agreed efficiency decreases with use: 33%.
- Sterilization: once daily 35%, every patient 35%.
- Disposal: dustbin 100%.
