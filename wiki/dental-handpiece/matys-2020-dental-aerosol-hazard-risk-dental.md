## One-line Summary

In-vitro manikin study measuring aerosol (0.3–10 μm) at three sites during caries removal, scaling, and polishing — the high-speed handpiece produced the most aerosol, low-speed less than the high-speed turbine, Er:YAG laser the least, and wide customized high-volume evacuators removed the most aerosol.

## 한줄요약

마네킹 인비트로 실험(0.3–10 μm, 3지점) — 하이스피드 핸드피스가 에어로졸 최다, 로우스피드는 하이스피드 터빈보다 적게, Er:YAG 레이저가 최소 생성했고, 넓은 맞춤형 고용량 흡입기(HVE)가 가장 효과적으로 제거(Zirc 88% 감소).

## Summary

This controlled bench study used a three-dimensional manikin with a natural tooth to compare aerosol generation (0.3–10 μm, PC200 laser particle counter) at the manikin, operator, and assistant positions during caries removal, ultrasonic scaling, and tooth polishing, paired with seven suction modalities. Caries removal with a **high-speed handpiece + saliva ejector** produced the highest aerosol at every site, whereas the **low-speed handpiece** generated significantly less aerosol than the high-speed turbine, and **Er:YAG laser** caries removal produced the least aerosol of all modalities. Silicone-rubber polishing on a low-speed contra-angle was the single highest particle generator. The dominant determinant of operator/assistant exposure was the suction device: wide customized high-volume evacuators (HVEs) removed 2× more aerosol than a standard HVE and 8× more than a saliva ejector, and the Zirc evacuator achieved an 88% reduction during high-speed caries removal. The study was performed without a rubber dam.

## Key Contributions

- Controlled modality comparison: high-speed handpiece > low-speed handpiece > Er:YAG laser for aerosol output.
- Quantified relative aerosol-reduction efficiency of 7 suction modalities, including two author-built wide HVEs.
- Confirms low-speed handpiece produces significantly less aerosol than the high-speed turbine.
- Identifies silicone-rubber polishing on a low-speed contra-angle as the highest particle generator.
- Establishes suction choice (HVE >> saliva ejector) as the primary controllable exposure factor.

## Methodology

In-vitro manikin (FDI tooth #35) aerosol experiment, no rubber dam. PC200 laser particle counter (0.3/0.5/1.0/2.5/5.0/10.0 µm summed) at three sites — manikin (2 cm), operator (40 cm), assistant (40 cm); sensor on 60 s post-start; 6 repeats per site. Office air standardized to 28,000–30,000 baseline particles (NV1050 purifier, 800 m³/h, ~5 min reset). Procedures: caries removal (high-speed bur, low-speed bur, Er:YAG 300 mJ/20 Hz/6 W), ultrasonic scaling, silicone-rubber polishing at 1000 and 10,000 RPM. Suction: saliva ejector EM15, HVE EM19 EVO, each ± extraoral vacuum (MAcURAY PRO), Zirc Mr. Thirsty One-step, customized white HVE, customized black HVE. ANOVA + Tukey post-hoc (p<0.05).

## Results

- High-speed handpiece + saliva ejector: highest spray particles at all sites; manikin >> operator/assistant (p<0.001).
- Low-speed handpiece: significantly lower aerosol than high-speed turbine; HVE cut aerosol ~4× (manikin), ~3× (operator) vs saliva ejector.
- Er:YAG + HVE: lowest aerosol (manikin 28.6 ± 1.3; operator 29.2 ± 0.8; assistant 29.0 ± 1.1; p<0.001).
- Silicone-rubber polishing (low-speed): highest particle number overall; RPM 1000→10,000 with HVE reduced manikin aerosol (p<0.001).
- Ultrasonic scaling: lower aerosol than rotary caries removal/polishing; HVE reduced manikin (p=0.005), operator (p=0.003) vs saliva ejector.
- Suction efficiency: wide customized HVE removed 2× (vs HVE) / 8× (vs saliva ejector); extraoral added ~33%; Zirc evacuator 88% reduction (32.1 ± 2.0 vs HVE 60.5 ± 3.0).
