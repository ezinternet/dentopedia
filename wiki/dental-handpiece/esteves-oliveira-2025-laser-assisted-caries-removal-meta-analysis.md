## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 20 in-vivo RCTs (≥2263 teeth, >1090 patients) finding laser caries excavation gives restoration survival and pulp vitality equivalent to conventional burs, significantly reduces anesthesia need and pain, but takes significantly longer — at low/very-low evidence certainty.

## Summary

This is the most current meta-analysis (prior syntheses stopped at 2016/2017) directly answering the **laser-vs-bur caries-removal indication** question on hard clinical endpoints. Across 20 in-vivo controlled trials (mostly Er:YAG; ≥2263 teeth, >1090 patients), laser excavation produced **restoration survival and pulp vitality statistically indistinguishable from burs**, while delivering meaningful patient-comfort benefits — **significantly fewer patients needed anesthesia (RR 0.29) and significantly less pain (RR 0.35)**. The trade-off is time: laser excavation was **significantly slower (MD +2.23)**. All estimates carry low/very-low GRADE certainty (7/20 high risk of bias), so the comfort advantages are real but should be weighed against chair-time cost and weak evidence. Combined with the held finding that laser ≈ bur for microleakage (Zhang 2020), the picture is consistent: **lasers are a comfort/indication choice, not a restoration-quality upgrade.**

## Key Contributions

- Up-to-date (to Feb 2025) quantitative laser-vs-bur synthesis on patient + technique outcomes.
- Hard-endpoint equivalence: restoration survival and pulp vitality not affected by laser.
- Quantified comfort benefit: anesthesia need RR 0.29; pain RR 0.35.
- Quantified cost: significantly longer excavation time (MD +2.23).
- Transparently grades all evidence low/very-low — fills the held overview's explicit gap without overstating it.

## Methodology

PROSPERO-registered PRISMA 2020 SR+MA. PICOS: patients of any age with initial caries; laser vs conventional bur excavation; outcomes = restoration survival, excavation speed, pulp sensitivity/vitality, anesthesia need, pain. Cochrane CENTRAL, MEDLINE, EMBASE, Web of Science to 10 Feb 2025 (EN/DE/PT). 589 → 20 included (≥2263 teeth, >1090 patients, age 3–72). Lasers: Er:YAG (majority), 2 Er,Cr:YSGG, 1 CO₂, 1 Nd:YAG; 17 split-mouth. RoB 2.0 (4 low / 9 unclear / 7 high) + GRADE; sensitivity analyses excluding high-RoB and split-mouth studies → minor changes only.

## Results

- **Restoration survival:** RR 1.51 (95% CI 0.87–2.64), NS — low evidence.
- **Pulp vitality:** RR 1.46 (95% CI 0.33–6.46), NS — low evidence.
- **Need of anesthesia:** RR 0.29 (95% CI 0.11–0.75), significantly fewer with laser — very low evidence.
- **Pain sensation:** RR 0.35 (95% CI 0.22–0.54), significantly less with laser — very low evidence.
- **Excavation speed:** MD 2.23 (95% CI 1.89–2.57), significantly more time with laser — low evidence.
- Median follow-up 12 months (1 day–48 months).
