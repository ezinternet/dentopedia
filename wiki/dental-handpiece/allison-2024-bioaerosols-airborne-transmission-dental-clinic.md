## One-line Summary

Narrative review synthesising dental bioaerosols and airborne transmission — air-turbine handpieces disperse ~1000× the bacterial CFU of quiet breathing, the classical <5 µm droplet/airborne cut-off is invalid (particles up to 100 µm stay suspended and travel >2 m), and a 3-tier control hierarchy (reduce production, reduce microbial load, capture/remove) plus PPE lowers exposure risk.

## 한줄요약

치과 bioaerosol과 공기전파를 종합한 서술적 리뷰 — 에어터빈 핸드피스는 조용한 호흡의 약 1000배 세균을 분산시키고, 5 µm 미만만 공기전파된다는 통념은 틀렸으며(최대 100 µm 입자도 부유·2 m 초과 이동), 러버댐·술전 가글·고용량 흡인·국소배기·환기·PPE의 3단계 통제로 노출을 낮춘다.

## Summary

Dental instruments (air-turbine handpieces, ultrasonic scalers) operating in the microbially laden oral cavity generate **bioaerosols** — aerosols carrying oral and dental-unit-waterline microbes — which placed high-speed dental procedures among WHO **high-risk aerosol-generating procedures (AGPs)** during COVID-19. The paper argues the disruption to dental services stemmed from *unknown* rather than demonstrably high risk, and that missed learning opportunities from earlier SARS/MERS outbreaks left dental infection-prevention guidance thin on bioaerosols.

A central conceptual contribution is that the conventional contact/droplet/airborne trichotomy — with its <5 µm "airborne" cut-off — is a misinterpretation: particles up to **100 µm** can remain suspended and travel **>2 m**, and larger microbe-laden droplets can deposit on oral/pharyngeal/ocular mucosae to initiate infection. Aerosol fate is governed by momentum, size (Stokes' law), temperature, and humidity; deposition site scales with particle size and correlates with disease severity.

The review then organises **control measures** into three tiers plus PPE, each with quantitative effect estimates from the primary/SR literature, and covers ventilation and fallow-time reasoning.

## Key Contributions

- Reframes physical dental **aerosols** as **bioaerosols** and connects dentistry to the broader airborne-infection-transmission evidence base.
- Debunks the 5 µm droplet/airborne dichotomy (up to 100 µm suspendable, travels >2 m).
- Catalogues three bioaerosol **measurement** paradigms — dispersed oral microbes, physical particle counting, and tracer studies (chemical dyes, fluorescein, bacterial/bacteriophage tracers) — with strengths and pitfalls.
- Provides an actionable **3-tier control hierarchy + PPE** with effect sizes for chairside decision-making.

## Methodology

Narrative (non-systematic) synthesis of primary aerobiology studies, historical (1960s–70s) dental bioaerosol work, and multiple systematic reviews of bioaerosol control. Thematically structured; effect estimates are cited from underlying studies rather than pooled. Confidence: narrative-review.

## Results

**Aerosol production & physics**
- Talking ~35× and coughing ~370× more respiratory aerosol than quiet breathing.
- Stokes' law: 100 µm particle settles from 1.5 m in ~5 s; 5 µm particle takes ~33 min.
- Deposition: >5 µm → nasopharynx; <5 µm → deeper bronchial/alveolar; <0.1 µm → bronchiolar/alveolar (deeper deposition → more severe disease).

**Dental bioaerosol magnitude**
- Air-turbine handpiece: **1000× the bacterial CFU** of quiet breathing, **2× a sneeze**; high-volume suction cut dispersion by **99.4%** (Micik).
- Bacteria detected on surfaces **1.8 m** away; highest settling on patient's chest; bioaerosol bacteria arise from **both oral cavity and dental unit waterlines** (DNA sequencing).
- Viral detection: SARS-CoV-2 RNA on surfaces in **5/24** COVID-19 cases; RNA in **36%** of air samples (Iran, high prevalence); HBV DNA in aerosols from **1/3** carriers after orthodontic debonding.
- Seroprevalence: **~16%** clinical dental team vs **~6%** non-clinical/general population (pre-vaccination NW England) — dentistry among the highest bioaerosol-exposure professions.

**Control measures (effect estimates)**
- *Reduce production*: electric micromotor (water-only cooling) yields far less aerosol and fewer carried microbes than the compressed-air air-turbine handpiece.
- *Reduce microbial load*: rubber dam ↓ bacterial dispersion up to **99% at 1 m, 70% at 2 m** (little beyond); bacteriophage tracers show **92–100%** viral-aerosol reduction. Pre-procedural mouthrinse ↓ **33–94%** (viral-dispersion effect unknown). Antimicrobial irrigants (chlorhexidine/NaOCl/H₂O₂) ↓ **43–88%**; waterline disinfectant ↓ **99%** infectious phage at 0.5 m.
- *Capture/remove*: high-volume suction ↓ **80–90%** (esp. ≤0.3 m); imaging **92%**; fluorescent tracer **75% at 0.5 m / 67% at 1.5 m**. LEV / extra-oral suction (~**3,000–5,000 L/min**) ↓ **89–93%** at 0.1 m vs 0.2 m; simulation ↓ up to **90% at 0.5 m, 75% at 2 m**.
- *Ventilation / fallow time*: ~**10 ACH** proposed; small-aerosol clearance ~**30 min at 10 ACH, 60 min at 5 ACH**; large droplets settle ~10 min; HEPA filtration effective where ventilation can't be upgraded.

**PPE**: standard precautions baseline; **FFP3/N95** for airborne (and droplet-route AGP) infections; per-patient risk assessment advised given the flawed transmission trichotomy; PPE evidence low-certainty simulation.
