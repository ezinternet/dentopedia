## One-line Summary
In-vitro fatigue + load-to-failure study (n=72, IPS e.max ZirCAD Prime Esthetic 4Y/5Y composition-gradient multilayer zirconia molar restorations): all survived 1.2M-cycle fatigue (97.2% crack-free); at 0.5 mm ultrathin, partial-coverage restorations far outperformed crowns (2047 vs 695 N, p<0.001), equal at 1.0 mm, crowns superior at 1.5 mm (3503 vs 2777 N, p=0.005); crowns need ≥1.0 mm.

## Summary

Spitznagel et al. (2025) tested whether composition-gradient multilayer zirconia (4Y-PSZ dentin zone / 5Y-PSZ incisal zone; IPS e.max ZirCAD Prime Esthetic) can be used for ultrathin, minimally invasive posterior restorations, and whether a non-retentive partial-coverage ("full-veneer") design competes with a full crown. Seventy-two CAD/CAM molar restorations (6 groups, n=12) were adhesively bonded to dentin-analogue resin dies, fatigued (1.2M cycles, 49 N, 1.6 Hz, thermocycled — ≈5 years), then loaded to failure.

All specimens survived fatigue (100% survival, 97.2% crack-free; the only two cracks were one each in the 0.5 mm crown and 0.5 mm PCR groups). The headline finding is a **crossed interaction between design and thickness**: the relative ranking of crown vs PCR flips with thickness. At 0.5 mm the PCR design is dramatically stronger; at 1.5 mm the crown wins; at 1.0 mm they are equal. Practically, **PCRs are mechanically viable at every tested thickness, but crowns must be ≥1.0 mm** — the 0.5 mm crown (mean 695 N) sits at the edge of physiological/parafunctional load and is the only group that did not clear the normal posterior force range.

## Key Contributions

- First in-vitro fatigue/failure-load evaluation of *full-veneer* molar restorations from composition-gradient multilayer zirconia.
- Establishes that ultrathin (0.5 mm) non-retentive PCRs outperform same-thickness crowns by ~3× failure load — challenging the manufacturer's crowns-only ≥1.0 mm indication.
- Maps failure mode by design: crowns fail catastrophically (Type IV longitudinal tooth/die fracture) at 1.0/1.5 mm; PCRs fail as combined ceramic+substrate (Type III) across thicknesses.

## Methodology

- 2×3 factorial: restoration type (PCR vs crown) × ceramic thickness (occlusal/buccal 0.5/0.4, 1.0/0.6, 1.5/0.8 mm); 6 groups × n=12 = 72.
- Material: IPS e.max ZirCAD Prime Esthetic — continuous 4Y-PSZ→5Y-PSZ gradient; restorations milled within the transition zone, 3.3 mm from blank top.
- PCR = non-retentive full-veneer (occlusal+buccal+proximal, no box, 2 diagonal seating notches, chamfer margin); crown = same dimensions, no notches.
- Substrate: Filtek Z100 resin-composite dies (~18 GPa ≈ dentin), epoxy-embedded.
- Cementation: 50 µm Al₂O₃ abrasion + Monobond Plus (MDP primer) + Adhese Universal + Variolink Esthetic DC.
- Fatigue: 1.2M cycles, 49 N, 1.6 Hz, thermocycle 5–55 °C, steatite sphere sliding 0.5 mm (≈5 yr). Then single-load-to-failure (Zwick, 3 mm ball, 1.5 mm/min).
- ANOVA + Tukey + independent t-tests (α=0.05); fractography by light microscopy + SEM.

## Results

**Fatigue:** 100% survival both designs; 97.22% overall success. Cracks only in C-0.5 (@802,485 cycles) and PCR-0.5 (@551,960 cycles), radial from intaglio surface, confined to ceramic.

**Mean failure load (single-load-to-failure):**

| Group | Thickness (occl/bucc) | Mean (N) | SD |
|---|---|---|---|
| C-1.5 | 1.5/0.8 mm | **3503** | 628 |
| PCR-1.5 | 1.5/0.8 mm | 2777 | 509 |
| PCR-0.5 | 0.5/0.4 mm | 2047 | 298 |
| PCR-1.0 | 1.0/0.6 mm | 2018 | 346 |
| C-1.0 | 1.0/0.6 mm | 1957 | 274 |
| C-0.5 | 0.5/0.4 mm | **695** | 224 |

**Per-thickness comparisons (PCR vs crown):**
- 0.5 mm → PCR ≫ crown: **2047 vs 695 N, p<0.001**
- 1.0 mm → no difference: 2018 vs 1957 N, p=0.63
- 1.5 mm → crown > PCR: **3503 vs 2777 N, p=0.005**

Restoration type (p=0.02) and thickness (p<0.001) both significant; crossed interaction p<0.001. Within PCRs, 0.5 mm = 1.0 mm (p=0.983). All groups except C-0.5 exceeded the normal posterior masticatory range (289–700 N); C-0.5 (~695 N) approaches the parafunctional ceiling (~900 N).

**Failure modes:** C-1.0/C-1.5 mostly Type IV (severe longitudinal tooth/die fracture); C-0.5 mostly Type II (cohesive ceramic, intact die); PCRs (all thicknesses) mostly Type III (combined ceramic+substrate).

**Clinical bottom line:** non-retentive PCRs suitable at all tested thicknesses (incl. ultrathin 0.5 mm) when adhesively bonded; single crowns require ≥1.0 mm.
