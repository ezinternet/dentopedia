## One-line Summary

2-year double-blind RCT (30 participants, MOD ETT molars): CAD/CAM nano-hybrid composite overlay (with cuspal reduction) showed better marginal adaptation, less discoloration/fracture, and less caries than the inlay (no cuspal reduction) design at 24 months.

## Summary

Fouda et al. ran a 2-year, double-blind, superiority RCT in 30 patients with MOD cavities in endodontically treated molars, randomizing to a CAD/CAM nano-hybrid composite inlay (no cuspal reduction) versus an overlay (full cuspal reduction), both milled from Brilliant Crios blocks over a bulk-fill flowable pulp-chamber base. Across modified USPHS evaluations at 6, 12, and 24 months, the overlay design consistently outperformed the inlay: better marginal adaptation, less discoloration and tooth/restoration fracture, and — by 24 months — less caries. Marginal integrity, which differed early, equalized by 24 months. The clinical conclusion is that cuspal coverage is more reliable than cusp preservation for mutilated ETT, addressing a long-standing controversy on whether to reduce cusps. This reinforces and adds prospective clinical depth to the indirect-restoration evidence base, with the caveat of a small single-center sample.

## Key Contributions

- RCT-level resolution of inlay (cusp-preserving) vs overlay (cusp-covering) design for indirect CAD/CAM composite in ETT.
- Prospective clinical data on a CAD/CAM nano-hybrid composite block in a high-risk indication.
- Favors cuspal reduction (overlay) for badly broken-down ETT molars.

## Methodology

30 MOD ETT molars randomized 1:1 (15/group; 26 completed). Group 1 inlay (no cuspal reduction); Group 2 overlay (cuspal reduction). Bulk-fill flowable pulp-chamber base; nano-hybrid composite milled blocks (Brilliant Crios, Coltène). Modified USPHS at baseline/6/12/24 months. Power 80%, α=0.05, n=24 base +20% → 30. Shapiro-Wilk/Kolmogorov-Smirnov normality; P≤0.05. ClinicalTrials.gov NCT04561167.

## Results

- 26/30 completed.
- 6 & 12 months: overlay significantly better marginal adaptation, less discoloration/fracture; marginal integrity and caries similar.
- 24 months: overlay better marginal adaptation, less discoloration/fracture, less caries; marginal integrity no longer different.
- Cuspal reduction (overlay) more reliable than cusp preservation (inlay).
