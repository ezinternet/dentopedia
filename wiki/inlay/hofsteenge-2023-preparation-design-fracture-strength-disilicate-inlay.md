In vitro + FEA study (n=64 human molars) comparing four lithium disilicate preparation designs (Undermined Inlay, Extended Inlay, Restricted Overlay, Extended Overlay) on compromised molars: overlay (cusp-coverage) designs showed significantly superior fracture resistance, and FEA confirmed cusp coverage reduces tooth deformation; IDS was applied to all groups.

## Summary
In vitro + finite element analysis study (n=64 human molars) comparing four preparation designs for lithium disilicate restorations on structurally compromised molars: Undermined Inlay (UI), Extended Inlay (EI), Restricted Overlay (RO), Extended Overlay (EO). Overlay designs showed superior fracture resistance. IDS applied to all groups. FEA confirmed cusp coverage reduces tooth deformation.

## Key Contributions
- Four designs: Undermined Inlay (UI), Extended Inlay (EI), Restricted Overlay (RO), Extended Overlay (EO)
- Overlay designs (RO, EO): significantly superior fracture resistance vs inlay designs
- IDS (Immediate Dentin Sealing) applied before temporization in all groups
- FEA: cusp coverage designs reduced tooth deformation under load
- Polymerization-induced cracks more frequent in deeper inlay preparations

## Methodology
n=64 extracted human molars with standardized simulated cuspal defects. Four preparation designs. IDS applied with a two-step self-etch adhesive followed by TEGDMA-based resin. IPS e.max CAD restorations milled and adhesively cemented. Thermomechanical fatigue: 1.2×10⁶ cycles, 50N, 5–55°C. Load-to-failure at 1 mm/min. FEA models constructed from micro-CT scans. Polymerization cracks evaluated by optical microscopy.

## Results
- RO and EO (overlay): significantly higher fracture resistance than UI and EI
- FEA: extended overlay reduced peak stress by ~30% vs inlay designs
- Polymerization cracks: more frequent in UI and EI (deeper cavities)
- IDS promoted bond stability across all groups
- Recommendation: cusp coverage preferred for compromised molars
