## 한줄요약
스플릿마우스 RCT(n=20, 2년): e.max CAD vs Voco Grandio 온레이 — 양군 모두 KM 생존율 90%; 재료 간 유의차 없음; 변연변색·변연적합·표면질감·술후과민 시간에 따라 유의하게 변화

## Summary
Split-mouth RCT (n=20, 2-year) comparing IPS e.max CAD (lithium disilicate) vs Voco Grandio Blocks (hybrid resin nano-ceramic) CAD/CAM onlays. Both materials achieved 90% Kaplan-Meier survival at 2 years with no statistically significant difference between groups. Within-group changes in marginal discoloration, adaptation, surface texture, and postoperative sensitivity were observed over time.

## Key Contributions
- Both groups: 90% Kaplan-Meier survival at 2 years
- No significant difference between e.max CAD and Voco Grandio (p>0.05)
- Within-group changes: marginal discoloration, adaptation, surface texture, sensitivity significantly changed with time
- Voco Grandio: color match within-group change observed
- Modified USPHS criteria at baseline, 1 year, 2 years

## Methodology
Split-mouth randomized clinical trial. n=20 patients (40 onlays: 20 e.max CAD, 20 Voco Grandio Blocks). Matching contralateral posterior teeth. CAD/CAM milling of all restorations. Cementation with adhesive resin cement. Blinded evaluation with modified USPHS criteria at baseline, 12 months, 24 months. Kaplan-Meier survival analysis. Wilcoxon signed-rank test for within-group changes.

## Results
- 90% KM survival: both groups at 2 years (no difference, p>0.05)
- 2 failures per group (debonding or fracture)
- Marginal discoloration, adaptation, surface texture: significant within-group changes over time
- Voco Grandio color match: significant within-group change
- e.max CAD: numerically better surface texture over time
