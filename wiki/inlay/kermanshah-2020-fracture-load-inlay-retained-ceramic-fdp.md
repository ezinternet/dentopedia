## 한줄요약
인비트로(n=64, 5000회 열순환): 지르코니아 IRFPD 파절강도 672N ≈ 전통 FDP와 동등; ZrC-IRFPD 638N; VS-IRFPD 391N으로 유의하게 낮음; 커넥터 파절이 주 실패 양식

## Summary
In vitro study (n=64) comparing fracture load of 3-unit ceramic FDPs after 5000 thermocycles. Zirconia IRFPD (672±123N) achieved fracture load equivalent to full-coverage Zr-FDP (672±183N). Feldspathic-coated Zr-IRFPD (638N) was also not significantly different. Zirconia-reinforced lithium silicate IRFPD (391N) was significantly weaker. All failures occurred at connector area.

## Key Contributions
- Zr-IRFPD: 672±123N ≈ full-coverage Zr-FDP: 672±183N (no significant difference, p>0.05)
- ZrC-IRFPD (feldspathic coating): 638±59N — not significantly different from Zr groups
- VS-IRFPD (zirconia-reinforced lithium silicate): 391±136N — significantly lower (p<0.05)
- Failure mode: connector fracture in all groups
- Surface treatment type (sandblasting vs coating) did not affect fracture load significantly

## Methodology
n=64 extracted premolars and molars prepared for 3-unit inlay-retained FDPs. Four groups (n=16): (1) Zr-IRFPD (sandblasted), (2) ZrC-IRFPD (feldspathic porcelain coating), (3) VS-IRFPD (Vita Suprinity, zirconia-reinforced lithium silicate), (4) Zr-FDP full-coverage control. After 5000 thermocycles (5°–55°C), fracture load testing at crosshead 1 mm/min. Kruskal-Wallis and Tukey post hoc tests.

## Results
- Zr-IRFPD and Zr-FDP: equivalent fracture resistance (~672N)
- ZrC-IRFPD: 638N, not significantly different (p>0.05)
- VS-IRFPD: 391N, significantly weaker (p<0.05)
- All connectors fractured first
- Practical implication: zirconia (not ZrLS) preferred for inlay-retained FDP connector strength
