## One-line Summary
In vitro study (n=52, 13 per group) comparing marginal/internal fit and 3D accuracy of Class II inlays made by four methods (conventional resin, milled hybrid ceramic, milled zirconia, 3D-printed resin): all CAD/CAM methods showed clinically acceptable marginal fit (<120 μm) with no significant internal-fit differences, and 3D-printed inlays were comparable to milled options.

## Summary
In vitro comparison (n=52) of marginal/internal fit and 3D accuracy of Class II inlays fabricated by four methods: conventional resin (TS), milled hybrid ceramic (Lava Ultimate/LU), milled zirconia (Zolid Fx/ZR), and 3D-printed resin (NextDent C&B). All CAD/CAM methods demonstrated acceptable marginal fit. Digital fabrication methods compared for chairside and laboratory applications.

## Key Contributions
- Four fabrication methods: conventional resin, milled hybrid (LU), milled zirconia (ZR), 3D-printed (NextDent)
- All CAD/CAM methods: acceptable marginal fit
- 3D accuracy assessed via digital superimposition software
- Marginal/internal fitness evaluated at 6 measurement points per cross-section
- 3D-printed inlays: comparable accuracy to milled options at 1-year study window

## Methodology
n=52 inlays (13 per group) fabricated for Class II preparations on typodont teeth. Groups: (1) conventional resin (Tescera/TS), (2) milled hybrid ceramic (Lava Ultimate/LU), (3) milled zirconia (Zolid Fx/ZR), (4) 3D-printed resin (NextDent C&B). Digital microscope at 160× for marginal/internal fit (6 points per section). 3D accuracy: digital scans superimposed in software.

## Results
- All CAD/CAM groups: marginal gap within clinically acceptable range (<120 μm)
- 3D-printed group: comparable marginal accuracy to milled groups
- Zirconia (ZR): highest dimensional accuracy by 3D superimposition
- Internal fit: no statistically significant differences among groups
- Conventional (TS): similar marginal fit to digital methods
