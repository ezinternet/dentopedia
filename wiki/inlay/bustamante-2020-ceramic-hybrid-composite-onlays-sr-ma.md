## Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis (PRISMA) of clinical behavior of onlay restorations in the posterior region, comparing three material categories: (1) ceramic (lithium disilicate, feldspathic, leucite-reinforced), (2) hybrid (resin-ceramic nanoceramic), (3) composite resin. Databases: PubMed, Scopus, Embase, Cochrane; no language/date restrictions. 29 articles for qualitative analysis; 27 for quantitative meta-analysis.

Overall weighted mean survival rate: 94.2%. Ceramic onlays show the highest survival; composite resin onlays significantly lower vs ceramic. Hybrid materials show intermediate survival. Material type and follow-up duration are significant predictors of restoration success. Main complications: chipping/fracture (ceramic), secondary caries (composite).

## Key Contributions
- Establishes overall onlay survival benchmark: ~94.2%
- Ceramic onlays (especially lithium disilicate): best survival and complication profile for posterior indirect restorations
- Composite resin onlays: significantly lower survival — not equivalent to ceramic for high-load posterior regions
- Hybrid/nanoceramic materials: insufficient long-term evidence but intermediate performance
- Material + follow-up duration are key predictors — longer follow-up and composite = more failures

## Methodology
- **Databases**: PubMed, Scopus, Embase, Cochrane Library (no date/language restriction)
- **PICO**: Patients needing indirect posterior restoration; ceramic/hybrid/composite onlay
- **Outcomes**: Survival rate, clinical complications, material-specific failure patterns
- **Meta-analysis**: Random-effects; subgroup by material type

## Results

| Material | Relative Survival | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Ceramic (LD, feldspathic, leucite) | Highest | Reference standard |
| Hybrid (resin-ceramic/nanoceramic) | Intermediate | Limited long-term data |
| Composite resin | Significantly lower | Main complication: secondary caries |
| **Overall** | **~94.2%** | Weighted mean across all materials |

**Survival predictors**: material type (ceramic > composite, p<0.05) and follow-up duration
