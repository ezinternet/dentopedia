## 한줄요약
후향적연구(n=91, 평균 7.8년): 리튬디실리케이트 생존율 96.8% vs 간접레진 84.9%; 연간실패율 LidiSi 0.2% vs RBC 1.0%; 15년째 RBC 60%로 급락

## Summary
Retrospective study (n=91 restorations, mean 7.8-year follow-up) comparing lithium disilicate (LidiSi) vs indirect resin-based composite (RBC) partial posterior restorations. LidiSi demonstrated significantly better survival (96.8% vs 84.9%; p<0.05) and 5× lower annual failure rate (0.2% vs 1.0%). By year 15, RBC survival dropped to ~60% while LidiSi remained >95%.

## Key Contributions
- LidiSi survival: 96.8% vs RBC: 84.9% at mean 7.8 years (p<0.05)
- Annual failure rate: LidiSi 0.2%, RBC 1.0% (5-fold difference)
- Both materials: >98% survival at 6 years
- By year 15: RBC survival ~60%; LidiSi remains >95%
- LidiSi failures: tooth fracture; RBC failures: marginal discoloration, secondary caries

## Methodology
Retrospective evaluation of inlay/onlay/overlay restorations placed 2007–2017 in a single practice. n=91 (LidiSi: IPS e.max CAD/Press; RBC: Filtek P60, Gradia, Ceram.x). Mean observation 7.8±3.3 years. Modified USPHS criteria. Kaplan-Meier analysis with log-rank test. Annual failure rate calculated.

## Results
- LidiSi 96.8% vs RBC 84.9% survival: statistically significant (p<0.05)
- LidiSi annual failure rate: 0.2%; RBC: 1.0%
- RBC survival curve: stable to 6 years then accelerated decline
- LidiSi failures: mainly tooth fracture (abrupt; not debonding)
- Both materials: favorable short-term (<6yr) performance
