## Summary
Split-mouth RCT (n=20, 1-year) comparing IPS e.max CAD (lithium disilicate) vs Lava Ultimate (resin composite) CAD/CAM onlays. Both materials showed no failures and no statistically significant overall clinical performance difference at 1 year. However, e.max CAD showed significant within-group deterioration in surface lustre (p=0.020) and color match/translucency (p=0.039).

## Key Contributions
- Both materials: no failures at 1 year; excellent baseline and 12-month performance
- No significant difference between e.max CAD and Lava Ultimate overall (p>0.05)
- e.max CAD: surface lustre deterioration (p=0.020), color match/translucency change (p=0.039) within group
- SEM: no significant micromorphological differences at 1 year
- FDI criteria, two blind observers; split-mouth design controls for patient variables

## Methodology
Split-mouth RCT (n=20 patients). Matching contralateral posterior teeth received either IPS e.max CAD or Lava Ultimate (resin composite) CAD/CAM onlays. CEREC system for fabrication. Adhesive cementation with Variolink Esthetic. FDI criteria evaluated by two blinded calibrated observers at baseline and 1 year. SEM analysis of marginal area at 1 year.

## Results
- No failures in either group at 1 year
- No statistically significant inter-group differences (p>0.05)
- e.max CAD within-group: surface lustre p=0.020; color match/translucency p=0.039
- Lava Ultimate: stable color match at 1 year
- SEM: no significant marginal gap or crack differences
