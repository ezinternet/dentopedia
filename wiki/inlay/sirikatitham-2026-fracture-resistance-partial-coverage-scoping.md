## One-line Summary

Scoping review of 34 in-vitro studies (9 premolar, 25 molar) mapping fracture resistance and fracture pattern of ceramic partial coverage restorations (PCRs) by preparation design: MOD overlays lowered molar fracture loads vs anatomic overlays, thicker occlusal reduction generally helped, proximal boxes/MOD worsened fracture severity, and every design still exceeded maximum bite forces.

## Summary

This scoping review (PRISMA-guided, RoBDEMAT risk of bias) synthesizes 34 in-vitro studies of non-endodontically-treated posterior teeth restored with ceramic partial coverage restorations (PCRs), organized along three factors: restoration type (overlay / MOD overlay / MOD onlay), preparation design (occlusal thickness, anatomic-only, proximal box, peripheral shelf, immediate dentin sealing, deep margin elevation), and ceramic type (lithium disilicate LDS, zirconia-reinforced lithium silicate ZLS, polymer-infiltrated ceramic network PICN, resin-nanoceramic RNC).

The central practical message: molars and premolars behave differently to an internal MOD box. In molars, lithium-silicate MOD overlays carried **lower** fracture loads than anatomic overlays and produced **more severe** fracture patterns; in premolars the MOD-box penalty was minimal or even slightly favorable. Increasing occlusal thickness generally raised fracture resistance, but the effect interacted strongly with preparation design (especially peripheral shelves), bonded substrate (enamel vs dentin), and ceramic type. Crucially, every PCR design — even the weakest — exceeded maximum bite forces (premolar 392 N, molar 602 N), so the authors argue design choice should weigh seating stability and adhesive support, not fracture numbers alone.

## Key Contributions

- First review to tabulate posterior PCR **fracture resistance AND fracture pattern** simultaneously across restoration type, preparation design, and ceramic group.
- A practical **load-threshold map**: premolars 350-1,954 N vs molars 880-4,995 N, cross-indexed by material and occlusal thickness.
- Tooth-type-specific MOD-box guidance: penalty real in molars, negligible in premolars.
- Quantifies that severe/unfavorable fracture patterns track with proximal boxes (premolars), MOD preparation (molars), greater occlusal thickness, and deep margin elevation.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Scoping review (OSF-registered protocol); PRISMA flow; search last updated June 2025.
- **Databases**: PubMed, Web of Science, ScienceDirect; compound terms (onlay/overlay/occlusal veneer/partial coverage × fracture resistance × preparation × lithium OR hybrid ceramic).
- **Flow**: 193 → 113 (de-dup) → 61 (title/abstract) → 24 included + 10 cross-reference = **34 final** (9 premolar, 25 molar). Model-teeth (non-human-substrate) studies excluded.
- **Quality**: RoBDEMAT 9-domain tool → 8 low / 26 medium / 0 high risk of bias.
- **Outcome**: vertical (and where reported oblique) load-to-fracture in newtons + fracture-pattern severity.

## Results

**Premolars (overall 350-1,954 N)**
- LDS overlay: 0.5/0.6 mm 471-1,781 N; 1 mm 866-1,495 N; 1.5 mm 1,005 N (aging).
- LDS-MOD overlay: 0.5 mm 833-997 N; 1 mm 1,087-1,108 N; 2 mm 963-1,361 N. LDS-MOD onlay: 0.5 mm 1,192 N; 2 mm 837-1,182 N.
- 2-mm LDS onlay (no aging) 682-938 N; 2-mm ZLS 624-756 N (oblique).
- **MOD box effect minimal/positive** (Gierthmuehlen: MOD overlay ≥ overlay at matched thickness).
- ↑resistance modifiers: IDS; wider proximal box (more load but more fracture); preheated resin composite luting (1,954/1,505 N) > dual-cure (1,495/1,158 N) > flowable (~900-1,068 N).

**Molars (overall 880-4,995 N)**
- LDS overlay scales with thickness/design: 0.5 mm 880-3,391 N; 1 mm up to 3,390 N (anatomic+others to 3,216 N); 1.5 mm anatomic up to **4,995 N**; 2/2.4 mm 2,869-4,766 N.
- **Headline finding**: LDS-MOD overlay **1,295-1,326 N** and ZLS-MOD overlay **2,066-2,189 N** were LOWER than their non-MOD overlay counterparts (LDS 1,406-4,995 N; ZLS 2,177-2,738 N).
- Hybrids: PICN 1,839-3,540 N; RNC 1,024-3,620 N. LDS > hybrid at thick occlusal, hybrid ≥ LDS at thin (0.5-1 mm) — superiority **controversial**.
- Preparation ranking (Ferraris, LDS): anatomic-only 3,216 N > two proximal boxes 2,462 N > shoulder shelf 2,350 N. Shoulder-shelf effect controversial overall.
- ↑resistance modifiers: IDS, dual-cure cement (vs self-cure), enamel phosphoric-acid + self-etch (vs adhesive alone). Deep margin elevation neutral on load (1,986 ≈ 1,776 N) but ↑fracture severity.

**Fracture pattern**
- Premolars: 2 proximal boxes → 75-100% severe mixed fractures (worst design); thicker occlusal and wider boxes both ↑severity.
- Molars: LDS-MOD overlays → more severe mixed fractures (up to 100%) than LDS anatomic overlays; RNC-MOD onlays 0% mild fractures; proximal-box effect on pattern NOT seen in molars (contrast with premolars).

**Clinical anchor**: maximum bite force 392 N (premolar) / 602 N (molar) — all PCR designs exceeded it. A separately cited MA (24-180 mo) found LDS (98%) ≈ hybrid onlay (99%) clinical survival.
