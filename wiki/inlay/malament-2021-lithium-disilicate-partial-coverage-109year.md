## One-line Summary
Long-term retrospective study (n=551 pressed acid-etched lithium disilicate partial-coverage restorations: 246 inlays, 305 onlays; up to 10.9 years) reporting 95.6% 10-year cumulative survival and 0.3% annual failure rate, with onlays outperforming inlays (98.3% vs 93.9%), higher molar failure risk, and bulk fracture as the only failure mode (no debonding).

## Summary
Long-term retrospective study of pressed acid-etched lithium disilicate (IPS e.max Press) partial coverage restorations in private practice. 551 restorations (246 inlays, 305 onlays), up to 10.9-year follow-up. 10-year cumulative survival 95.6%, annual failure rate 0.3%. Onlays outperformed inlays (98.3% vs 93.9%). Only failure mode: bulk fracture; no debonding reported.

## Key Contributions
- Longest follow-up for pressed LD inlay/onlay in this wiki: up to 10.9 years
- 10-year cumulative survival: 95.6%
- Annual failure rate: 0.3% (6 total failures)
- Inlays (n=246): 93.9% at 9.9yr; Onlays (n=305): 98.3% at 9.8yr
- Molar teeth: higher failure risk
- Only failure mode: bulk fracture (no debonding reported)
- Patient age and sex: no significant effect on survival

## Methodology
Private practice retrospective study. n=551 IPS e.max Press partial coverage restorations (inlays + onlays). Mean patient age 62 years. Follow-up range: several months to 10.9 years. Kaplan-Meier analysis. Log-rank test for inlay vs onlay comparison. Cox proportional hazards for failure risk factors. All restorations: acid-etched with HF + silane + adhesive resin cement.

## Results
- Cumulative survival at 10 years: 95.6%
- Annual failure rate: 0.3%/year (6 failures total)
- Inlay (9.9yr): 93.9%; Onlay (9.8yr): 98.3% (p<0.05)
- Molar location: significantly higher risk of failure
- All failures: bulk fracture; no adhesive failures
- Patient age and sex: not significant predictors
