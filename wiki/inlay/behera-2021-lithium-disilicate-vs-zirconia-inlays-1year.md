## One-line Summary
1-year prospective RCT (n=30; 15 per group) comparing pressed lithium disilicate (IPS e.max Press) vs zirconium dioxide (Dentcare) Class II inlays: both materials achieved satisfactory 1-year clinical performance with no statistically significant difference and no failures/debonding at 12 months; longer follow-up needed to differentiate materials.

## Summary
One-year prospective RCT (n=30) comparing pressed lithium disilicate (IPS e.max Press) vs zirconium dioxide (Dentcare) Class II inlay restorations. Both materials achieved satisfactory 1-year clinical performance with no statistically significant differences. Longer follow-up is needed to reveal material differences.

## Key Contributions
- n=30 patients (15 LD, 15 ZrO₂): well-matched Class II inlay groups
- IPS e.max Press vs Dentcare Zirconia: equal 1-year clinical outcome
- Both passed standard clinical evaluation criteria at 12 months
- ZrO₂ surface treatment protocol documented
- Study calls for longer follow-up to differentiate material performance

## Methodology
Prospective RCT (n=30, 15 per group). Class II inlay restorations in posterior teeth. IPS e.max Press (lithium disilicate, pressed) vs Dentcare Zirconia. Clinical evaluation at 1 year using standard criteria. Internal surfaces of ZrO₂ treated per manufacturer's protocol.

## Results
- Both materials: satisfactory 1-year clinical performance
- No statistically significant difference between groups
- No failures or debonding reported at 12 months
- Further long-term study warranted
