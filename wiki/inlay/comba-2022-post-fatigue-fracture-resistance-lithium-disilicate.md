## One-line Summary

In-vitro study (n=84 ETT molars, 6 groups, chewing simulator + static fracture): overlay (1.6 mm) was a valid alternative to adhesive crown (2.0 mm) in endodontically-treated molars; occlusal veneers (1.2 mm) showed significantly lower fracture resistance than adhesive crown (p=0.042); LS outperformed PICN on static fracture load (p=0.0002).

## 한줄요약

인비트로 연구(n=84 근관치료 대구치): 오버레이 (Overlay, 1.6 mm)는 접착 크라운 (Adhesive Crown, 2.0 mm)의 유효 대안 — 교합면 비니어 (Occlusal Veneer, 1.2 mm)는 ETT에서 파절강도 열위(p=0.042); 리튬 디실리케이트 (Lithium Disilicate, LS)가 고분자 침윤 세라믹 네트워크 (Polymer-Infiltrated Ceramic Network, PICN)보다 정적 파절강도 유의하게 우수(p=0.0002).

## Summary

This in-vitro study (Polymers 2022) evaluated post-fatigue fracture resistance of indirect restorations on endodontically-treated (ETT) maxillary molars using a 2×3 factorial design: two CAD/CAM materials (LS = E-max CAD, PICN = Vita Enamic) crossed with three preparation designs (occlusal veneer 1.2 mm, overlay 1.6 mm, adhesive crown 2.0 mm). After core build-up with bulk-fill composite, 84 specimens underwent endodontic treatment, standardized MOD cavity preparation, and luting with universal adhesive + dual-cure cement. A chewing simulator (50 N, 1,500,000 cycles) preceded static fracture testing on survivors. Both preparation design (p=0.0429) and material (p=0.0002) significantly influenced fracture resistance; their interaction was non-significant (p=0.8218).

Key findings: overlay was not significantly different from adhesive crown (the most protective preparation), while occlusal veneer was significantly weaker than adhesive crown (p=0.042). LS had higher static fracture resistance than PICN across all preparation types. During cyclic loading, PICN showed no catastrophic failures, while the LS adhesive crown group experienced one core fracture. Clinically, overlay is recommended as the minimally invasive alternative of choice for ETT with extensive structural loss; occlusal veneers should be reserved for worn dentition, not heavily compromised non-vital molars.

## Key Contributions

1. **Overlay validated as ETT alternative**: No statistically significant difference between overlay and adhesive crown in post-fatigue fracture resistance — supports cusp-preserving overlay over full-crown preparation
2. **Occlusal veneer contraindicated in ETT**: Significantly lower fracture resistance than adhesive crown (p=0.042) in the setting of high structural loss
3. **LS superior to PICN on static load**: LS significantly outperformed PICN under increasing static load (p=0.0002); however PICN showed better cyclic load tolerance (zero catastrophic cyclic failures vs LS's one core fracture)
4. **Failure mode patterns**: Adhesive crown group had the highest catastrophic fracture rate (50% for LS adhesive crown) vs minimally invasive preparations (≤29% catastrophic)
5. **SEM documentation** of marginal gap progression and wear pattern differences between LS and PICN restorations

## Methodology

- **Sample**: 84 human maxillary molars (donors age 41–67), standardized MOD cavities, all ETT (ProTaper Next X1-X2; 5% NaOCl + 10% EDTA)
- **Core build-up**: Bulk-fill composite (Grandioso X-Tra, Voco) before preparation
- **Preparation groups** (n=14/group): occlusal veneer (1.2 mm) / overlay (1.6 mm + 1.5 mm shoulder) / adhesive crown (2.0 mm + chamfer)
- **Materials**: E-max CAD (LS, Ivoclar Vivadent) vs Vita Enamic (PICN, Vita)
- **Scanning and milling**: Omnicam 2.0 + Cerec 4.5.2 CAD/CAM chairside
- **Luting**: 5% HF etching (20 s LS, 60 s PICN) → Futurabond U + Bifix QM dual-cure cement
- **Fatigue**: CS-4.4 chewing simulator, 50 N, 1,500,000 cycles, 1 Hz, distilled water, steatite antagonist balls (6 mm)
- **Fracture test**: Instron universal testing machine, 6 mm steel crosshead, 1 mm/min
- **Statistics**: Two-way ANOVA + Tukey post-hoc (α=0.05)

## Results

**Mean fracture resistance (N ± SD)**:

| Preparation | PICN | LS |
|---|---|---|
| Occlusal veneer | 1806.6 ± 270.1 | 2029.5 ± 295.3 |
| Overlay | 1726.9 ± 301.1 | 2059.5 ± 308.0 |
| Adhesive crown | 1555.4 ± 393.9 | 1859.2 ± 232.0 |

- Preparation design: p=0.0429 (significant); Material: p=0.0002 (significant); Interaction: p=0.8218 (NS)
- Occlusal veneer vs Adhesive crown: p=0.042 (veneer inferior); Overlay vs Adhesive crown: NS; Occlusal veneer vs Overlay: NS (p=0.095)
- LS > PICN: p=0.002

**Cyclic loading events**: Occlusal veneers and overlays — chippings only (repairable); Adhesive crowns — 1 debonding + 1 core fracture (LS), 2 debondings (PICN)

**Catastrophic fracture rates**: Adhesive crown LS: 50%; all other groups: 25–35%

**Clinical takeaway**: Overlay preferred for ETT with compromised crowns; occlusal veneer appropriate only for worn (vital) dentition, not structurally compromised ETT.
