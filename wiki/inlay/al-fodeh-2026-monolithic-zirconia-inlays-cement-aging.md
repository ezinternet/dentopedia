## One-line Summary

In-vitro study (n=64 extracted maxillary premolars, MOD inlays): contemporary monolithic zirconia inlays (4Y-TZP Zolid Gen-X vs 3Y/5Y-gradient IPS e.max ZirCAD Prime) achieved clinically acceptable marginal fit regardless of material; RelyX Unicem gave smaller marginal gaps than Panavia F 2.0, 1.2M-cycle thermomechanical aging significantly widened gaps, and internal fit (111.95-163.16 μm) was unaffected by any factor.

## Summary

This three-way factorial bench study (PMID 41690135) tested whether zirconia generation, resin cement, or thermomechanical aging drives the marginal and internal fit of monolithic zirconia inlays. Sixty-four extracted maxillary premolars received MOD inlay preparations and were assigned to two zirconia materials — Zolid Gen-X (4Y-TZP) and IPS e.max ZirCAD Prime (3Y-TZP cervical / 5Y-TZP incisal gradient) — each cemented with either self-adhesive RelyX Unicem or MDP-containing Panavia F 2.0, and either stored 24 hr or aged with 1.2 million dynamic loading cycles plus thermocycling (5/55 °C). The central finding: zirconia material did not change marginal fit (both stayed within the clinically acceptable 50-120 μm band), but cement choice and aging did. RelyX produced significantly smaller marginal gaps than Panavia, and aging significantly widened gaps. Internal fit was uniformly stable across factors but sat above ideal values.

## Key Contributions

- No significant marginal-fit difference between 4Y-TZP and 3Y/5Y-gradient monolithic zirconia inlays — material generation is not the decisive variable for fit.
- Self-adhesive RelyX Unicem outperformed MDP-based Panavia F 2.0 on marginal gap at both occlusal and proximal margins.
- Thermomechanical aging (1.2M cycles + thermocycling) significantly degrades marginal seal — a long-term performance signal.
- Internal fit (111.95-163.16 μm) is robust to material/cement/aging but exceeds ideal targets, decoupling internal from marginal adaptation.

## Methodology

- **Design**: in-vitro, 3-way factorial (material × cement × aging), n=64 caries-free extracted maxillary premolars, MOD inlay preps by one operator.
- **Material arm**: Zolid Gen-X 4Y-TZP (n=32) vs IPS e.max ZirCAD Prime 3Y/5Y gradient (n=32).
- **Cement arm**: RelyX Unicem self-adhesive (n=16/material) vs Panavia F 2.0 (n=16/material).
- **Aging arm**: 37 °C/24 hr control vs 1,200,000 dynamic loading cycles with thermocycling at 5 & 55 °C.
- **Outcomes**: marginal fit at 20 locations (stereomicroscope 20×); internal fit at 7 locations (SEM 200×).
- **Analysis**: three-way ANOVA + multivariate tests, α = 0.05.

## Results

| Factor | Marginal fit | Internal fit |
|---|---|---|
| Zirconia material (4Y vs 3Y/5Y) | No significant difference; both within 50-120 μm | No significant difference |
| Resin cement | RelyX < Panavia gap (P < 0.05), occlusal + proximal | No significant difference |
| Thermomechanical aging | Significantly increased gap (P < 0.05) | No significant difference |
| Internal fit range | — | 111.95-163.16 μm (above ideal) |

Clinical takeaway: for monolithic zirconia inlays, prioritize cement selection (self-adhesive RelyX over Panavia here) and account for aging-driven marginal degradation; the choice between 4Y-TZP and 3Y/5Y-gradient zirconia does not materially affect fit.
