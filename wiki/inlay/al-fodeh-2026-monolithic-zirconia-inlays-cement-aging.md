## One-line Summary

In-vitro study (n=64 extracted maxillary premolars, MOD inlays): contemporary monolithic zirconia inlays (4Y-TZP Zolid Gen-X vs 3Y/5Y-gradient IPS e.max ZirCAD Prime) achieved clinically acceptable marginal fit regardless of material; RelyX Unicem gave smaller marginal gaps than Panavia F 2.0 (occlusal: 60.5 vs 71.2 μm, P=0.031; proximal: 78.0 vs 89.8 μm, P=0.006), 1.2M-cycle thermomechanical aging significantly widened gaps (occlusal: +16.9 μm, P<0.001), and internal fit (111.95-163.16 μm) was unaffected by any factor.

## 한줄요약

In-vitro 연구 (발거 상악 소구치 64개, MOD 인레이): 최신 단일구조 지르코니아 인레이(4Y-TZP Zolid Gen-X vs 3Y/5Y 그라디언트 IPS e.max ZirCAD Prime)는 재료 종류와 무관하게 임상적으로 허용 가능한 변연 적합도를 보였고, RelyX Unicem이 Panavia F 2.0보다 변연 간격이 작았으며(교합면: 60.5 vs 71.2 μm, P=0.031; 인접면: 78.0 vs 89.8 μm, P=0.006), 120만 회 열기계적 노화는 변연 간격을 유의하게 넓혔으나(교합면: +16.9 μm, P<0.001), 내면 적합도(111.95-163.16 μm)는 어떤 인자에도 영향받지 않았다.

## Summary

This three-way factorial in-vitro study (J Mech Behav Biomed Mater 2026;177:107369) investigated whether zirconia generation, resin cement type, or thermomechanical aging drives the marginal and internal fit of monolithic zirconia MOD inlays. Sixty-four extracted maxillary premolars received standardized MOD inlay preparations and were assigned to two contemporary zirconia materials — Zolid Gen-X (4Y-TZP uniform color-gradient, 700-1000 MPa flexural strength, Amann Girrbach) and IPS e.max ZirCAD Prime (3Y-TZP/5Y-TZP strength-gradient, ~1200 MPa cervical, Ivoclar Vivadent) — each cemented with either self-adhesive RelyX Unicem or MDP-containing Panavia F 2.0, and either stored 37 °C/24 h or subjected to 1,200,000 dynamic loading cycles (50 N, 1.6 Hz) with thermocycling at 5/55 °C (representing ~5 years of mastication). Inlays were fabricated via Trios 3 IOS scan → Ceramill CAD software (50 μm design spacer) → 5-axis milling → sintering.

The central finding: zirconia material did not significantly affect marginal fit (occlusal P = 0.417; proximal P = 0.108) — both materials stayed within the clinically acceptable 50-120 μm band. However, cement choice and aging both had significant independent effects on marginal gap. RelyX Unicem consistently produced smaller marginal gaps than Panavia F 2.0. Thermomechanical aging significantly widened gaps for all groups. A significant Cement × Aging interaction (occlusal P = 0.008) showed Panavia degraded more under aging (+37.9% gap increase) than RelyX (+20.8%). Internal fit exceeded the conventionally cited 50-100 μm ideal range but was uniform across all factors; adjusting for the 50 μm design spacer reduces actual cement thickness to 77.4 μm (Zolid Gen-X) and 98.1 μm (IPS e.max ZirCAD Prime), both within acceptable limits.

## Key Contributions

- **Zirconia generation is not the decisive fit variable**: no significant marginal-fit difference between 4Y-TZP and 3Y/5Y-gradient zirconia inlays under identical CAD/CAM fabrication conditions.
- **Cement choice matters for marginal seal**: self-adhesive RelyX Unicem outperformed MDP-based Panavia F 2.0 at both occlusal and proximal margins; attributed to Panavia's higher polymerization shrinkage generating residual stress at the margins.
- **Aging degrades marginal fit, not internal fit**: 1.2M-cycle thermomechanical aging significantly increased marginal gaps across all groups, while internal fit remained stable — decoupling the two fit endpoints.
- **Differential cement aging susceptibility**: Panavia showed greater marginal degradation under thermomechanical stress than RelyX (Cement × Aging interaction P = 0.008), indicating clinically relevant long-term performance differences between cement types.
- **Internal fit values above ideal but correctable by spacer accounting**: raw internal fit (127-148 μm) appeared elevated, but subtracting the 50 μm CAD spacer yields actual cement thicknesses within the 50-100 μm recommended range.

## Methodology

- **Design**: in-vitro, 3-way factorial (material × cement × aging), n=64 caries-free extracted maxillary premolars, MOD inlay preps by one operator; n=8 per subgroup.
- **Preparations**: standardized MOD (2 mm isthmus, 2 mm occlusal reduction, proximal box 2-2.5 mm, gingival floor 1 mm above CEJ, 6-10° convergence); verified with silicone indices.
- **CAD/CAM**: Trios 3 IOS → Ceramill software (50 μm virtual spacer) → Ceramill Motion 2 five-axis milling → sintered per manufacturer.
- **Material arm**: Zolid Gen-X 4Y-TZP (n=32) vs IPS e.max ZirCAD Prime 3Y/5Y gradient (n=32).
- **Cement arm**: RelyX Unicem self-adhesive (n=16/material) vs Panavia F 2.0 (n=16/material); all preceded by Z-PRIME Plus primer and 15 s selective enamel etching; cemented at 10 N for 5 min.
- **Aging arm**: 37 °C/24 hr control (n=8/subgroup) vs 1,200,000 cycles 50 N 1.6 Hz + thermocycling 5/55 °C (n=8/subgroup).
- **Marginal fit measurement**: stereomicroscope 20× at 20 locations (6 occlusal, 7 mesial, 7 distal); pre-marked reference points for reproducibility in aged specimens.
- **Internal fit measurement**: mesiodistal sectioning (IsoMet Low Speed; 15HC Diamond blade) → gold-palladium sputter-coating → SEM 200× at 7 anatomical locations (N-T: gingival floor, axiogingival, axiopulpal for mesial/distal boxes; Q: pulpal floor midpoint).
- **Statistics**: Kolmogorov-Smirnov + Shapiro-Wilk normality → three-way ANOVA + Tukey post-hoc, α = 0.05, SPSS v29.

**Key quantitative takeaways**
- Marginal gaps: all mean values within clinically acceptable 50-120 μm range regardless of material, cement, or aging.
- Cement effect (marginal): RelyX mean occlusal 60.5 ± 15.47 μm vs Panavia 71.16 ± 26.59 μm; RelyX proximal 77.96 ± 15.93 μm vs Panavia 89.80 ± 22.42 μm.
- Aging effect (marginal): occlusal 57.4 → 74.3 μm (+16.9 μm); proximal 74.6 → 93.2 μm (+18.6 μm).
- Cement × Aging differential: Panavia occlusal gap +21.1 μm (37.9%); RelyX +12.3 μm (20.8%) after aging.
- Internal fit regional variation (P < 0.001): axiogingival angles ~175 ± 42 μm (highest); axiopulpal angles ~121 ± 38 μm (lowest).
- Actual cement thickness (after -50 μm spacer): Zolid Gen-X ~77.4 μm; IPS e.max ZirCAD Prime ~98.1 μm — both within 50-100 μm target.
