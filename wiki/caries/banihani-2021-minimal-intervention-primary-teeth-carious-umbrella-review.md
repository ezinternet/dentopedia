## One-line Summary

Umbrella review of 18 systematic reviews (95 underlying studies) on minimal intervention dentistry (MID) for dentinal caries in primary teeth: 38% SDF, Hall Technique, selective caries removal, and single-surface ART effectively arrest dentinal caries vs no treatment/conventional restoration — while fissure sealants and resin infiltration are explicitly NOT recommended for dentinal lesions (their evidence is dominated by enamel/outer-third-dentine lesions).

## Summary

This umbrella review (Cochrane methodology v6; PROSPERO CRD42020202434) appraised 18 systematic reviews — 5 narrative, 13 with meta-analysis — covering 95 underlying studies on minimal intervention dentistry (MID) for **dentinal** carious lesions (ICDAS 4 & 5) in primary teeth, performed without local anaesthetic. The included SRs clustered heavily on two techniques (8 on 38% SDF, 8 on ART), with single SRs each for the Hall Technique and selective caries removal.

For sealant surveillance, the load-bearing finding is a boundary, not a benchmark: SRs on **fissure sealants and resin infiltration were excluded by design**, and the authors conclude verbatim that *"both fissure sealants and resin infiltration are not recommended for the management of dentinal caries lesions in primary teeth"* — because their pooled caries-arrest data mixed enamel and outer-third-dentine lesions, the majority being limited to enamel. In this umbrella's taxonomy, sealants sit in the **enamel-lesion control** column (alongside fluoride varnish/gel), NOT among the dentinal-caries MID options. The four techniques that *are* endorsed for cavitated dentine are 38% SDF, the Hall Technique, selective/stepwise caries removal, and single-surface ART.

SR quality was modest: 10 of 18 SRs were at high risk of bias (ROBIS), 6 low, 2 unclear — so even the endorsed options rest on heterogeneous, often biased underlying evidence.

## Key Contributions

- **Defines where sealants do and do not belong**: fissure sealants / resin infiltration = enamel and non-cavitated lesions; for dentinal (ICDAS 4–5) primary-tooth caries they are explicitly not recommended.
- **Ranks the dentinal-caries MID toolkit** with quantitative arrest/success data: 38% SDF, Hall Technique (PMC), selective/stepwise removal, single-surface ART.
- **Reframes MID as mainstream**, not a fallback used only when conventional restoration is impossible.
- **Flags evidence quality**: most underlying SRs at high risk of bias; HT and selective-removal evidence thin.

## Methodology

- Umbrella review; MEDLINE, Embase, Cochrane CDSR, Epistemonikos, JBI, NIHR Journals Library + PROSPERO; English, Jan 2000–Aug 2020.
- 252 records → 177 after dedup → 181 eligible → 19 full text → **18 SRs** included (reporting 95 studies; RCTs 1969–2018).
- Two independent reviewers for screening/extraction; **ROBIS** for SR risk of bias; study overlap via **Corrected Covered Area (CCA)** — weak overlap in both SDF and ART clusters.
- SRs analysed separately by technique (SDF / HT / selective / stepwise / ART).

## Results

**Sealants & resin infiltration (the surveillance verdict)** — verbatim: *"SRs assessed the sealing efficacy of fissure sealants and resin infiltration in carious primary teeth were excluded due to pooled data reporting on caries arrest in both enamel and outer third of dentine with the majority of these carious lesions being limited to enamel. Therefore … both fissure sealants and resin infiltration are not recommended for the management of dentinal caries lesions in primary teeth."*

**38% SDF (8 SRs, 33 RCT/CCT)** — significant caries arrest (p<0.05); more effective than 5% NaF varnish and GIC sealing. Single application arrest 31–79%, biannual 53–91%; 38% > 12% SDF (p<0.001). Meta-analyses: 81% overall arrest (Gao, p<0.001); OR 2.44–3.63 favouring SDF (Trieu). Comparators: 5% NaF varnish 41%, GIC 82%, no treatment 15–34%. Main drawback: black staining (one study 0.6% reversible white mucosal lesions). No SDF-vs-conventional-restoration trials existed.

**Hall Technique (2 SRs / 3 overlapping RCTs)** — restoration failure 3% (HT) vs 37% (control); no difference in pulpal signs/symptoms; 77% children / 83% carers preferred HT; 89% HT children reported "no pain" vs 78% control. PMCs reduce major failure/pain (esp. via HT); more gingival bleeding but inconclusive (RR 1.09, CI 0.42–2.86).

**Selective caries removal (1 SR / 3 RCTs)** — pulp-exposure risk reduction 77% (one-step selective; RR 0.24, 95% CI 0.06–0.90, p=0.03) and 69% (stepwise; RR 0.31, 95% CI 0.17–0.57, p=0.0002) vs non-selective removal to hard dentine.

**ART (8 SRs / 56 trials)** — single-surface success 95–100% (1y) → 86% (3y); multi-surface significantly worse (p<0.0001): 73–100% (1y) → 48.7–88% (3y); mean annual failure 17% (multi) vs 4.7–5% (single). 3 SRs: ART ≈ amalgam/composite; one SR (Dorri): ART may increase failure (OR 1.60, CI 1.13–2.27).

**SR risk of bias (ROBIS)**: 10 high, 6 low (Ricketts, Innes, Dorri, Tedesco, Trieu, Tolba), 2 unclear (Gao, Tedesco 2018). Substantial heterogeneity; mostly clinical (not radiographic) outcomes — may underestimate failures.
