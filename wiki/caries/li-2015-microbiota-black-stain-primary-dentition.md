## One-line Summary

16S rRNA cross-sectional study in 25 caries-free children found that black stain (BS) plaque is enriched in Actinomyces, Haemophilus, and Treponema and shows lower salivary microbial diversity vs controls, with BS+ plaque samples splitting into two heterogeneous subgroups by PCA.

## Summary

This cross-sectional 16S rRNA amplicon sequencing study characterized the oral microbiome of black stain (BS) in caries-free Chinese children. Ten BS+ and 15 BS− children provided plaque and saliva samples. The total community spanned 10 phyla, 19 classes, 32 orders, 61 families, and 102 genera. Salivary microbial diversity (Shannon and Simpson indices) was significantly lower in BS+ children. In plaque, BS+ samples were enriched in Actinomyces, Cardiobacterium, Haemophilus, Corynebacterium, Tannerella, and Treponema, and depleted in Campylobacter. PCA showed control plaque samples clustered tightly while BS+ samples split into two subgroups, indicating that BS is a heterogeneous microbiome phenotype. The authors concluded that Actinomyces is the likely key player in BS formation, consistent with iron sulfide precipitation hypotheses.

## Key Contributions

- First 16S rRNA-based characterization of BS microbiota in the primary dentition
- Demonstrated reduced salivary alpha diversity (Shannon/Simpson) in BS+ children
- Identified Actinomyces as the candidate keystone taxon in BS plaque
- Revealed microbiome heterogeneity within BS+: two distinct PCA subgroups suggest mechanistic or host subtypes
- All participants were caries-free, isolating the BS microbiome signal from caries-associated dysbiosis

## Methodology

- **Design**: Cross-sectional
- **n**: 25 caries-free children (BS+ n=10, BS− n=15); primary dentition
- **Inclusion**: BS on ≥3 primary teeth (BS+); no stain, no caries (BS−)
- **Samples**: Supragingival plaque and unstimulated saliva
- **Sequencing**: 16S rRNA V3–V4 amplicons on Illumina MiSeq
- **Analysis**: OTU clustering (97% identity), Shannon/Simpson alpha diversity, PCA beta diversity, differential abundance testing
