## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 7 RCTs (700 crowns; 6 pooled for retention): prefabricated zirconia crowns (PZCs) and stainless steel crowns (SSCs) showed equivalent retention in primary molars (OR 0.96, 95% CI 0.40–2.32; p=0.93; I²=0%), with PZCs consistently demonstrating better gingival health and less plaque accumulation, possible increased antagonist wear due to zirconia hardness, and strongly superior parental esthetic satisfaction despite higher cost.

## Summary

This PROSPERO-registered (CRD420251016795) SR+MA from Nagpur, India evaluates the clinical efficiency of prefabricated zirconia crowns (PZCs) versus stainless steel crowns (SSCs) in primary molars in children aged 3–10 years. Following PRISMA 2020 guidelines and restricting inclusion to RCTs published 2018–2024, 7 studies (n=700 crowns) were included in qualitative synthesis; 6 contributed to the quantitative meta-analysis using a fixed-effect model.

**Crown retention** was the primary outcome analyzed quantitatively. The pooled OR was 0.96 (95% CI 0.40–2.32; Z=0.09; p=0.93; I²=0%), establishing statistically equivalent retention for both crown types. Most individual studies showed numerically similar or slightly higher retention with ZR; only Taran 2018 and Agrawal 2022 found marginally better short-term SSC retention, while Vishnu 2024 showed 100% ZR vs 85% SSC at 12 months (OR 8.20, NS due to small sample).

**Gingival health** results favored PZC in the majority of studies (Mathew 2020 p=0.047; Abuelniel 2018; Taran 2018), attributed to PZC's glazed, nonporous surface that resists bacterial adhesion and gingival inflammation. Agrawal 2022 (3-month follow-up) found better outcomes with SSC — likely operator and time-related — while Vishnu 2024 found no significant difference.

**Plaque accumulation** was lower around PZCs in most studies (Abuelniel 2018, Taran 2018, Mathew 2020). Agrawal 2022 found the inverse (higher plaque with ZR), possibly influenced by patient and technique factors.

**Antagonist tooth wear** was higher with PZCs in 2 of 3 studies evaluating this outcome (Prabhu 2022, Taran 2018), reflecting zirconia's greater material hardness. Murali 2022 found no significant difference. Wear was consistently described as clinically minor but warrants attention in children with parafunctional habits.

**Parental satisfaction** was uniformly and strongly in favor of PZC across all four studies assessing this (Murali 2022, Mathew 2020, Prabhu 2022, Vishnu 2024), driven by natural tooth-like esthetics. Parents and children preferred PZC despite higher cost and technique demands.

Risk of bias was assessed with RoB 2: most studies demonstrated low risk in randomization and outcome measurement; Prabhu 2022 and Vishnu 2024 had some concerns over allocation concealment and blinding. Overall quality: acceptable.

Clinically: PZCs are a reliable, esthetically superior alternative to SSCs for primary molars — particularly appropriate when esthetics and caregiver preference are priorities. SSCs remain the cost-effective gold standard (88–96% success at 10 years). Cost, technique sensitivity, and antagonist wear should inform case selection.

## Key Contributions

- Pooled retention equivalence (OR 0.96, I²=0%) across 6 RCTs — strongest quantitative confirmation that PZC ≈ SSC for primary molar retention.
- PZC gingival advantage: consistently better in majority of studies; mechanism = glazed nonporous surface reducing plaque adhesion.
- Antagonist wear signal: PZC harder than natural enamel — 2/3 studies found increased wear; minor but clinically relevant for bruxers.
- Parental satisfaction: uniformly PZC-favored across all esthetic-reporting studies — fills the gap noted in the Cochrane review (Innes 2015), which found esthetic outcomes underreported.
- Comprehensive scope: first SR+MA (2018–2024) to cover retention, gingival health, plaque, antagonist wear, and parental satisfaction simultaneously in a strict RCT-only design.

## Methodology

- Design: SR+MA (PRISMA 2020; PROSPERO CRD420251016795)
- Databases: PubMed, Cochrane Library, Google Scholar, ProQuest, K-Hub (2018–2024; English-only); search date January–March 2025
- Inclusion: RCTs; pediatric patients 3–10y; primary molar full-coverage restoration; PZC vs SSC; ≥6-month follow-up
- Study selection: 233 identified → 7 included (qualitative); 6 pooled (quantitative)
- Risk of bias: Cochrane RoB 2; overall acceptable
- Meta-analysis: fixed-effect model for retention (I²=0%, χ²=3.73, df=5, p=0.59); funnel plot: symmetric (minimal publication bias)
- Remaining outcomes (gingival, plaque, wear, satisfaction): narrative synthesis

## Results

**Crown retention meta-analysis (6 RCTs; fixed-effect)**:
- OR 0.96 (95% CI 0.40–2.32; p=0.93; I²=0%)
- No statistically significant difference; equivalence conclusion supported
- Trend numerically toward ZR in most studies; wide CIs preclude definitive superiority claim
- Vishnu 2024: OR 8.20 (CI 0.40–169.90) favoring ZR — not significant (small sample/low events)

**Gingival health** (narrative): PZC superior in majority; mechanism = polished, nonporous surface. Minority result (Agrawal 2022) likely confounded by short follow-up.

**Plaque accumulation** (narrative): Lower with ZR in 3/5 reporting studies. Agrawal 2022 showed inverse (higher ZR) — patient and technique factors implicated.

**Antagonist wear** (narrative): Higher with ZR in 2/3 reporting studies (zirconia hardness); clinically minor; functional complications not reported; caution in parafunctional habits.

**Parental satisfaction**: Unanimously and strongly PZC-favored in all 4 reporting studies; esthetic appearance primary driver despite higher cost.

**Overall conclusion**: PZC = SSC for retention and durability; PZC > SSC for gingival health and parental satisfaction; PZC < SSC for cost and ease; PZC vs SSC uncertain for antagonist wear (likely slightly higher with PZC).
