## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1007/s00520-026-10909-2](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00520-026-10909-2)), this 3-arm randomized controlled trial evaluated whether a salivary substitute (Group I), fluoride varnish (Group II), or both combined (Group III) could control radiation caries (RC) in irradiated head and neck cancer (HNC) patients. DMFS was measured before radiotherapy and at 3, 6, and 12 months post-RT. **(Note: abstract-only — full text not retrieved; all data are from the published abstract.)** Across all three arms, DMFS rose significantly (P<0.05) at every timepoint, and the Bonferroni intergroup comparison was non-significant — no arm outperformed another. The authors conclude that neither saliva substitute nor fluoride varnish, alone or combined, halts RC progression, and that a multimodal strategy is essential.

**Clinically:** in irradiated HNC patients, do not rely on fluoride varnish or saliva substitute as stand-alone caries control — they slow nothing on their own in this cohort. Combine salivary gland-sparing radiotherapy, professional dental care, stringent home fluoride/hygiene, frequent recall, and patient education as a package; the agents are adjuncts within that package, not a substitute for it.

## Key Contributions

- First large head-to-head RCT comparing saliva substitute vs fluoride varnish vs both for radiation-caries prevention in irradiated HNC patients (n=482).
- Negative but important result: no single agent and no combination halted DMFS progression, and no arm was superior.
- Reframes fluoride varnish in the radiation-caries context — it is one component of a multimodal protocol, not a sufficient intervention by itself.

## Methodology

- **Design**: 3-arm randomized controlled trial; patients randomized after comprehensive oral care.
- **Arms**: Group I = salivary substitute (advised liberally at home before major meals); Group II = fluoride varnish (applied professionally before RT, immediately after RT, then at 3-month intervals); Group III = both.
- **Outcome**: Decayed, Missing, and Filled Surfaces (DMFS) recorded before radiotherapy (RT) and at 3, 6, and 12 months post-RT.
- **Analysis**: intergroup comparison by Bonferroni test.

## Results

- **Recruited**: 482 patients — 407 males, 75 females; mean age 51.32 y (range 18–79).
- **12-month complete data**: 67 (Group I) / 73 (Group II) / 80 (Group III).
- **DMFS increase**: statistically significant (P<0.05) in all three groups across all time points (3, 6, 12 mo).
- **Intergroup comparison**: Bonferroni test non-significant — no group superior.
- **Conclusion**: neither saliva substitute nor fluoride varnish, alone or combined, halted radiation-caries progression; multimodal care (gland-sparing RT + professional care + home care + follow-up + education) is paramount.
