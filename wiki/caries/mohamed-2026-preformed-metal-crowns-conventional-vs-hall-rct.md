## One-line Summary

12-month non-inferiority RCT (n=80 carious primary molars, 73 returned): preformed metal crowns by conventional vs Hall technique both achieved 100% "Successful" clinical outcomes and 100% survival; HT-induced OVD increase (1.18±0.68 mm) reverted to baseline within 1 month (P=0.32).

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1016/j.jdent.2026.106686](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jdent.2026.106686)), this non-inferiority, primary care-based, two-arm parallel-group RCT randomized 80 carious primary molars to receive preformed metal crowns (PMCs) by either the conventional technique (CT, caries removal + preparation) or the Hall technique (HT, sealing-in without preparation or local anesthesia). At 12 months, 73 of 80 participants returned: every PMC-restored tooth in both arms was graded "Successful" with no Major or Minor failures, and all crowns were functionally present (100% survival in both groups). The HT produced a transient occlusal vertical dimension (OVD) increase of 1.18±0.68 mm immediately after seating, which reverted to pre-treatment values within one month (no significant baseline-vs-1-month difference, P=0.32).

This entry is **abstract-only** — full text was not retrieved (no open-access copy), so per-arm follow-up counts, randomization/blinding detail, and confidence intervals are unavailable.

**Clinically:** The non-invasive Hall technique is a viable alternative to conventional PMC placement for restoring carious primary molars — equivalent clinical success and survival at 1 year, and the bite "step" patients/parents notice right after seating self-corrects within a month, so it should not deter use of HT.

## Key Contributions

- Direct 12-month head-to-head RCT of conventional vs Hall PMC placement, with clinical outcome and survival as endpoints.
- Establishes clinical non-inferiority of the minimally invasive HT (no drilling, no anesthesia) versus the traditional preparation-based CT.
- Quantifies the transient post-HT OVD increase and demonstrates spontaneous resolution within 1 month, addressing a frequent clinical concern.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Non-inferiority, primary care-based, two-arm parallel-group randomized controlled trial.
- **Sample**: 80 carious primary molars indicated for PMC restoration, randomly allocated to CT and HT.
- **Intervention**: Standardized PMC placement in both arms — CT with caries removal/preparation, HT with sealing-in and no preparation.
- **Follow-up**: 12 months; 73/80 participants returned for assessment.
- **Outcomes**: Clinical grade (Successful / Minor / Major failure); restoration survival (functional presence); OVD at baseline, immediately post-HT, and 1 month (HT arm).

## Results

- 73/80 participants returned at the 12-month follow-up.
- All PMC-restored teeth in BOTH CT and HT groups graded "Successful" — no Major or Minor failures in either arm.
- 100% restoration survival in both groups — all crowns functionally present at 12 months.
- HT OVD increase immediately after placement: 1.18 ± 0.68 mm.
- OVD reverted to pre-treatment values within 1 month — no significant difference between baseline and 1-month measurements (P = 0.32).
- Conclusion: clinical success and survival were similar for CT and HT; the non-invasive HT is a viable alternative to the traditional CT.
