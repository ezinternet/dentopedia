## One-line Summary

Narrative review (Sichuan University / HKU): an acidic microenvironment is the unifying driver across dental caries (selecting acidogenic/aciduric cariogenic bacteria, accelerating demineralization), pulpitis (low pH impairs pulpal function and raises bacterial virulence), and apical periodontitis (acid disrupts osteoblast/osteoclast/enzymatic processes → bone resorption), and it also compromises endodontic treatment effectiveness — while acid-mitigation strategies remain better developed for caries than for pulpal/periapical disease. (abstract-only)

## 한줄요약

내러티브 리뷰(쓰촨대·홍콩대): 산성 미세환경(acidic microenvironment)이 우식·치수염·근단치주염을 관통하는 공통 동인으로, 우식에서 산생성·내산성(acidogenic/aciduric) 우식원성 세균을 선택하고 탈회를 가속하며, 치수염에서 pH 저하가 치수 기능을 손상시키고 세균 독성을 높이고, 근단치주염에서 산이 골모·파골세포·효소 과정을 교란해 골흡수를 유발하며 근관치료 효과까지 떨어뜨린다 — 그러나 산 완화 전략은 우식에 비해 치수·근단 질환에서 덜 연구되어 있다. (초록만)

## Summary

This narrative review reframes dental caries, pulpitis, and apical periodontitis not as three separate diseases but as one **acid-driven continuum**. A persistently low-pH microenvironment is presented as the common pathophysiologic thread: it both demineralizes mineralized tissue directly and reshapes the local biology of host cells and microorganisms in a way that propagates disease forward and undermines treatment. The English-language evidence base is drawn from PubMed and Web of Science; this page is **abstract-level only** (no PMC open access), so it captures the conceptual framework rather than numeric benchmarks.

## Key Contributions

- **Unifying acid-mechanism thesis** — positions the acidic microenvironment as the shared driver from caries through pulpitis to apical periodontitis.
- **Caries**: the acidic milieu promotes proliferation and adaptation of **acidogenic and aciduric cariogenic bacteria**, accelerating the disease process and impairing treatment outcomes.
- **Pulpitis**: a drop in pH impairs normal pulpal functions and **heightens the virulence** of pathogenic bacteria.
- **Apical periodontitis**: the acidic periapical microenvironment perturbs **osteoblast, osteoclast, and enzymatic processes**, tipping bone homeostasis toward **resorption**.
- **Treatment**: the acidic environment also **compromises the effectiveness of endodontic treatment**.
- **Translational gap**: many acid-mitigation strategies are established for caries, but comparable approaches for pulpal and periapical disease remain underdeveloped.

## Methodology

Narrative (non-systematic) review; English references from PubMed and Web of Science. No meta-analysis or formal risk-of-bias appraisal. Abstract-only ingest — detailed search/inclusion methods not retrieved.

## Results

(Conceptual, abstract-level — no pooled effect estimates available)

- An acidic environment **demineralizes dental hard tissues** and disrupts the function of both host cells and microorganisms.
- Acid-driven selection favors acidogenic/aciduric bacteria in the caries lesion; low pulpal pH increases bacterial virulence; acidic periapical tissue shifts the osteoblast/osteoclast balance toward bone resorption.
- The net effect is **forward progression** of the caries → pulpitis → apical periodontitis continuum and **reduced treatment effectiveness** at each stage.
