## One-line Summary

Abstract-only RCT (80 carious primary molars): vs the conventional technique, the Hall technique for preformed metal crowns required less than half the chair time (7.1 vs 17.4 min, p<0.001), caused significantly less child pain (p=0.03), and earned significantly better parental acceptability (ease, duration, satisfaction, all p<0.001).

## Summary

> [!note] Abstract-only page
> No PMC open-access full text was available at ingest; under wiki Rule #1 only the PubMed abstract was used ([DOI 10.1007/s40368-025-01128-0](https://doi.org/10.1007/s40368-025-01128-0)).

According to PubMed, this RCT (80 carious primary molars) quantifies *why* the Hall technique is so attractive in practice — not survival (covered by [[caries/konukman-turker-2026-hall-vs-modified-hall-primary-molars-rct]]), but the chairside experience. The Hall technique (HT) took **7.1 ± 2.8 min versus 17.4 ± 2.9 min for the conventional technique (CT)** — under half the chair time (p<0.001). Children reported significantly **less pain** with HT (p=0.03), and while parent-rated comfort during the procedure did not differ (p=0.20), parents rated HT significantly better on procedural ease, treatment duration, satisfaction, and willingness to choose it again (all p<0.001). Operator-rated behaviour was comparable (positive: CT 65%, HT 85%).

Clinically, this is the patient-experience case for HT: faster, less painful for the child, and more acceptable to parents — complementing the durability evidence and reinforcing HT as a practical first choice for cooperative-challenged young children. From the same group (Philip) as the companion survival trials.

## Key Contributions

- Hall technique roughly halves chair time vs conventional PMC placement.
- Less child pain and better parental acceptability with HT.

## Methodology

Two-arm parallel RCT, 80 participants, single pediatric dentist; outcomes procedural time, Wong-Baker FACES pain, Frankl behaviour, parental 5-pt Likert. Confidence `rct`; abstract-only ingest (no PMC OA).

## Results

- Chair time: HT 7.1±2.8 vs CT 17.4±2.9 min (p<0.001).
- Child pain higher in CT (χ=12.27; p=0.03); behaviour comparable.
- Parental ease/duration/satisfaction/repeat all favor HT (p<0.001); comfort perception NS (p=0.20).
