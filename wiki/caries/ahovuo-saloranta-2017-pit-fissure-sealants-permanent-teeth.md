## One-line Summary
Cochrane SR+MA (38 RCTs, 7924 children aged 5–16): resin-based fissure sealants reduced occlusal caries vs no sealant by 11–51% at 24 months (OR 0.12, moderate certainty); GIC sealant and head-to-head material comparisons remain inconclusive.

## Summary
This Cochrane review is the reference source for the efficacy of pit-and-fissure sealants against no treatment. Pooling 38 RCTs, resin-based sealants applied to occlusal surfaces of first permanent molars in children aged 5–10 produced a large, consistent reduction in caries at 24 months (OR 0.12, 95% CI 0.08–0.19; moderate-certainty GRADE). The effect translates to clinically meaningful absolute reductions across baseline-risk strata and is maintained to roughly 48 months, after which evidence thins. Evidence for glass ionomer sealant versus no sealant, and for one sealant material versus another, is very low certainty and inconclusive. No adverse events were reported in the four trials assessing them. All trials carry high risk of bias for outcome-assessment blinding, which is intrinsic to the visibility of sealant.

## Key Contributions
- Anchors the "sealant works vs no sealant" premise that downstream sealant-vs-varnish comparisons build on.
- Provides baseline-risk-stratified absolute effect estimates (control decay 16/40/70% → sealed-surface decay 5.2/6.25/19%).
- Demonstrates durability of effect to ~48 months with declining evidence afterward.
- Flags persistent uncertainty for GIC sealants and material head-to-head questions.

## Methodology
RCTs in participants ≤20 years with ≥12-month follow-up; resin-based, glass ionomer, and head-to-head sealant comparisons. Outcomes as OR/RR for caries presence and MD for caries increment, all with 95% CI. Random-effects model for >3 trials; GRADE for certainty. 38 trials / 7924 children (15 resin-vs-no-sealant; 3 GIC-vs-no-sealant; 24 head-to-head). Search to 3 August 2016.

## Results
- Resin-based vs no sealant, 24 mo: OR 0.12 (95% CI 0.08–0.19), 7 trials, 1322 evaluated — moderate certainty; 11–51% relative reduction.
- Resin-based vs no sealant, 48–54 mo: OR 0.21 (0.16–0.28), 4 trials; RR 0.24 (0.12–0.45).
- Glass ionomer vs no sealant: inconclusive (very low certainty).
- One sealant vs another: unknown (very low certainty).
- Adverse events: none reported (4 trials).
