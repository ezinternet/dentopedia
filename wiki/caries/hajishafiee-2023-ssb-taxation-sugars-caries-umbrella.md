## Summary
Hajishafiee et al. conducted the first umbrella review assessing how SSB taxation affects dental caries in both HIC and LMIC, commissioned to inform WHO's Global Strategy on Oral Health. From 519 identified systematic reviews, 14 were included for SSB-tax/consumption questions and 5 for the sugars-caries dose-response. The best available evidence (high-AMSTAR SR by Teng et al.) showed a 10% SSB tax reduces consumption by 10.0% (95% CI: −5.0, 14.7%) in HIC. A 20% volumetric tax would reduce free sugars by approximately 4.4 g/day (HIC) and 4.0 g/day (LMIC). Applying the dose-response thresholds from Moynihan & Kelly 2014, this translates to 0.03 fewer carious teeth per adult and 2.7–2.9% reduction in caries occurrence in children over 10 years. The conclusion is that SSB taxation produces a modest but real population-level caries benefit in both income settings, supporting its inclusion in the WHO oral health action plan.

## Key Contributions
- First umbrella review quantifying SSB-tax impact on dental caries in HIC and LMIC using a unified framework.
- PED estimate (HIC, high-AMSTAR): 10% tax → 10.0% SSB consumption reduction (95% CI: −5.0, 14.7%; Teng et al.).
- PED estimate (LMIC): 10% tax → ~9% reduction (range −6.0 to 12.0%).
- 20% volumetric tax: free sugars reduction ~4.4 g/day (HIC) and ~4.0 g/day (LMIC).
- Modelled 10-year caries impact: 0.03 fewer carious teeth per adult (HIC and LMIC); 2.9% reduction in child caries occurrence (HIC); 2.7% (LMIC).

## Methodology
Umbrella review of systematic reviews; JBI methodology; PROSPERO-registered (CRD42022293187). Databases: PubMed, Embase, Web of Science, Scopus, CINAHL, Dentistry and Oral Sciences Source, Cochrane, JBI SR Register, PROSPERO; 2000–2021. Quality assessment: AMSTAR (11-point). Included: 14 SRs (Q1+Q2: SSB tax → consumption/sugars; 4 high, 9 moderate, 1 low quality) and 5 SRs (Q3: sugars → caries; 2 high, 3 moderate). Modelling: PED-derived sugars reduction × dose-response coefficients → estimated caries prevented over 10 years.

## Results
- Primary (SSB→consumption, HIC): 10% tax → 10.0% reduction (95% CI: −5.0, 14.7%; PED = −1.0; high-AMSTAR Teng et al.).
- Secondary (SSB→consumption, US subset): 10% tax → 7.0% reduction (95% CI: −3.0, −10.0%; Afshin et al. high-AMSTAR).
- Sugars reduction: 20% tax → ~4.4 g/day (HIC); ~4.0 g/day (LMIC).
- Caries prevented (10 years, 20% tax): 0.03 carious teeth/adult (HIC and LMIC); 2.9% caries occurrence reduction in children (HIC); 2.7% (LMIC).
- Overall: modest individual-level effect; population-level benefit non-trivial given global disease prevalence.
