## One-line Summary
RCT (1016 children, 36 mo) — on first permanent molars, fissure sealant vs fluoride varnish gave no significant difference in dentine caries (19.6% vs 17.5%); in a community program, fluoride varnish may be the more cost-effective option.

## 한줄요약
RCT(아동 1016명·36개월): 1차 영구대구치에서 점열구실란트와 불소바니시의 상아질우식 예방 효과가 유의차 없음(19.6% vs 17.5%); 지역사회 프로그램에선 바니시가 더 비용효과적일 수 있음.

## Summary
This pragmatic RCT compared the two dominant occlusal caries-prevention technologies head-to-head — most prior evidence compared each only against no treatment. In 1016 high-deprivation children receiving 6-monthly applications over 36 months, the proportion developing dentine caries on at least one first permanent molar was statistically equivalent between fissure sealant (19.6%) and fluoride varnish (17.5%). Because fluoride varnish requires less chair time, fewer materials, and tolerates partial molar eruption, the authors position it as at least as cost-effective in a community/school program. Chairside individual-risk decisions may still favor sealants for fully erupted, high-risk molars.

## Key Contributions
- Direct FS-vs-FV comparison with a clinically meaningful 36-month caries endpoint.
- Equivalence supports FV as a viable primary preventive in population programs.

## Methodology
RCT, 1016 children (FS 514, FV 501), high-deprivation, 6-monthly applications, 36 mo; 82.2% completed. Outcome: dentine caries (D4-6MFT) on ≥1 first permanent molar.

## Results
- Dentine caries at 36 mo: FS 19.6% vs FV 17.5% (NS).
- Sealant retention: maxillary 74.5% / mandibular 91.4% intact.
- No adverse effects.
