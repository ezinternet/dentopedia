## One-line Summary
NMA of root caries restorative strategies (9 RCTs, 1,263 lesions): at 24 months, 2–3-step etch-and-rinse + composite or RMGIC showed a tendency toward better survival than GIC, but results were not statistically significant; CINeMA confidence low — no evidence-based hierarchy can currently be established.

## 한줄요약
치근 우식 수복법 NMA (9 RCT, 1,263 lesion): 24개월 시점에서 2-3단계 EAR + 컴포지트 또는 RMGIC가 GIC보다 생존율 경향 우세하나 통계적으로 NS, CINeMA 신뢰도 낮음. 근거 기반 우선 선택은 불가능.

## Summary
SR + NMA of 9 RCTs (1,263 root caries lesions, 473 patients) comparing restorative strategies (adhesive class × restorative material). Network meta-analysis stratified by 6/12/24-month follow-up. At 24 months, 2-3-step EAR + conventional composite vs GIC showed OR 2.65 (95% CI 1.45–4.84); RMGIC vs GIC OR 2.05 (1.17–3.61). However NMA confidence (CINeMA) was low and findings were not statistically significant after adjustment. Authors conclude: an evidence-based hierarchy for root caries restoration cannot currently be established. Calls for standardized, well-designed RCTs.

## Key Contributions
- First NMA covering full adhesive (1-SE / 2-3ER) × material (CC / GIC / RMGIC) matrix for root caries
- Demonstrates the evidence vacuum for root caries restoration despite aging population
- Includes special populations: nursing home residents, head-and-neck radiotherapy patients
- Useful as a "don't pretend we know" reference for clinical decision-making in geriatric/xerostomic populations

## Methodology
- **Design**: SR + NMA
- **Search**: Medline, Web of Science, Embase, Cochrane, Scopus, grey literature
- **Inclusion**: RCTs with ≥2 restorative strategies for cavitated root caries in adults
- **Outcome**: Survival rate at 6/12/24 months
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane RoB-2; NMA confidence: CINeMA framework (low)
- **Analysis**: Random-effects NMA stratified by follow-up

## Results
- 547 screened → 9 eligible
- Population: 473 patients, 1,263 root caries lesions
- Patient subgroups: healthy (6 trials), nursing home (1), post-radiotherapy (2)

**24-month NMA (point estimates)**:
| Comparison | OR | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|
| 2-3ER/CC vs GIC | 2.65 | 1.45–4.84 |
| RMGIC vs GIC | 2.05 | 1.17–3.61 |

Caveat: NS after NMA confidence adjustment; CINeMA confidence low.

## Clinical Implications
- No restorative strategy has clear evidence-based superiority for root caries
- Material choice should consider patient factors: isolation feasibility, xerostomia, caries risk
- GIC remains defensible despite tendency for lower survival (fluoride release, simple application in xerostomic patients)
- RMGIC or composite + 2-3ER may have a survival edge but evidence is weak
