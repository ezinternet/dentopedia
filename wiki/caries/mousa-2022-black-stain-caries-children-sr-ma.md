## One-line Summary

SR+MA (14 studies, children) finding black stain associated with significantly lower odds of caries (OR 0.67, 95% CI 0.54–0.82, I²=37%) and fewer caries-affected teeth (MD −0.98) and surfaces (MD −2.34), though causal direction is unresolved.

## Summary

This systematic review and meta-analysis pooled 14 observational studies to quantify the association between black stain (BS) and caries experience in children. BS children had 33% lower odds of caries (OR 0.67), approximately 1 fewer caries-affected tooth, and approximately 2.3 fewer caries-affected surfaces compared to non-BS peers. Heterogeneity was moderate for the likelihood outcome (I²=37%) but substantial for tooth and surface counts (I²=79–85%), reflecting variation in diagnostic criteria and study populations across included studies.

The mechanistic interpretation remains open: BS may directly confer protection via iron-sulfur antimicrobial activity and favorable salivary chemistry, or the association may reflect that children with constitutionally low-cariogenicity microbiome (Actinomyces-dominant, low *S. mutans*) are simultaneously predisposed to BS formation. The authors could not distinguish between these models from observational data.

## Key Contributions

- First meta-analysis quantifying the BS–caries association in children (OR 0.67 for caries likelihood)
- Provides mean difference estimates for affected teeth (MD −0.98) and surfaces (MD −2.34)
- Acceptable heterogeneity for primary outcome (I²=37%) supports pooling validity
- Explicitly articulates competing causal models (direct protection vs shared microbiome origin)

## Methodology

- Study design: SR+MA; PRISMA-compliant
- Databases: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, and others
- Inclusion: observational studies in children comparing caries experience (BS vs non-BS)
- Studies included: 14
- Pooling: random-effects model
- Outcomes: (1) likelihood of caries (OR), (2) number of affected teeth (MD), (3) number of affected surfaces (MD)
- Heterogeneity: I² statistic

## Results

| Outcome | Statistic | 95% CI | I² | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Likelihood of caries | OR = 0.67 | 0.54–0.82 | 37% | ~33% lower odds in BS children |
| No. of teeth affected | MD = −0.98 | −1.54 to −0.42 | 79% | ~1 fewer tooth |
| No. of surfaces affected | MD = −2.34 | −4.23 to −0.44 | 85% | ~2.3 fewer surfaces |

**Causal models proposed:**
1. BS microflora (Actinomyces-dominated) directly suppresses cariogenic bacteria
2. Iron/sulfur compounds in BS have intrinsic antimicrobial effects
3. Shared host/salivary factors (high Ca²⁺, high buffering capacity) simultaneously favor BS formation AND resist caries — confounding by constitution
