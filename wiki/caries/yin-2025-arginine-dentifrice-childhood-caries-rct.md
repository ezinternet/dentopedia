## One-line Summary

2-year phase-3 double-blind 3-arm RCT (n=6,000 children, 10–14 y, 3 centers China): 8.0% arginine dentifrice gave a 26.0% DMFS / 25.3% DMFT reduction vs 0.32% NaF (P<.001), while 1.5% arginine was equivalent to NaF — arginine is an effective, dose-dependent fluoride-free anticaries alternative.

## Summary

This phase-3, randomized, double-blind, 3-arm parallel-group trial tested whether **fluoride-free arginine dentifrices** can prevent caries in caries-risk children compared with a standard 1,450-ppm NaF toothpaste. Across 3 centers in China (Chengdu, Chongqing, Xi'an), 6,000 children aged 10–14 with ≥2 active caries lesions were randomized to 8.0% arginine (CaCO₃ base), 1.5% arginine (CaCO₃ base), or 0.32% NaF (silica base, positive control), brushing twice daily over 24 months (April 2019 – March 2022).

After 2 years, the **8.0% arginine** dentifrice was *superior* to fluoride — a 26.0% reduction in incremental DMFS and a 25.3% reduction in DMFT — while the **1.5% arginine** dentifrice was *equivalent* (noninferior parity) to NaF. The anticaries effect of arginine is therefore **dose-dependent**, with a threshold concentration needed to exceed fluoride-level protection. The result positions arginine as a viable non-fluoride caries-prevention agent for patients in whom fluoride is contraindicated, refused, or where dental fluorosis is a concern.

## Key Contributions

- Largest long-term head-to-head trial of **fluoride-free** arginine dentifrices for childhood caries prevention.
- 8.0% arginine **beats** standard fluoride; 1.5% arginine **matches** it — clean dose-response.
- Supplies clinical evidence for fluoride-alternative counselling and for policy on dentifrice composition.

## Methodology

- **Design**: phase-3 randomized, double-blind, 3-cell parallel-group RCT; intention-to-treat analysis.
- **Sites / dates**: 3 centers in China (West China College of Stomatology–Chengdu, Chongqing Medical University, Xi'an Jiaotong University); 15 Apr 2019 → 12 Mar 2022.
- **Participants**: 6,000 children 10–14 y (mean 12.35 y; 56.8% female), each with ≥2 active caries lesions (ICDAS ≥2) and DMFS ≥2.
- **Arms** (NaF 2,000 / 1.5% arg 2,002 / 8.0% arg 1,998): 8.0% arginine (CaCO₃), 1.5% arginine (CaCO₃), 0.32% NaF (silica, 1,450 ppm) control. Twice-daily brushing; overwrapped/coded tubes; compliance via 3-monthly resupply + leftover weighing.
- **Outcomes**: primary = incremental subject-wise DMFS and DMFT at 2 y; secondary = increments at 6 mo and 1 y. ICDAS scoring (only cavitated lesions, ICDAS 3–6, counted as decayed) by 6 calibrated examiners (intra-kappa >0.70, inter-kappa >0.61). No radiographs.
- **Statistics**: noninferiority margin **0.2545** (FDA-guided, Cochrane fluoride data); MICE imputation; bootstrapping (200×) + imputation; baseline-adjusted propensity-score weighting (baseline DMFS/DMFT imbalance noted); 2-sided α=0.05; sensitivity analyses for sex/age/site.

## Results

- **Completion**: 5,347/6,000 (89.1%); low attrition.
- **Baseline imbalance** (addressed by adjustment): DMFS 4.26 / 4.13 / 3.91 and DMFT 3.08 / 3.15 / 2.92 across 8.0% arg / 1.5% arg / NaF (both P<0.001).

**2-year baseline-adjusted incremental scores vs 0.32% NaF:**

| Arm | DMFS Δ (95% CI) | DMFS reduction | P | DMFT Δ (95% CI) | DMFT reduction | P |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 8.0% arginine | −0.16 (−0.22 to −0.10) | **26.0%** | **<.001** | −0.17 (−0.24 to −0.11) | **25.3%** | **<.001** |
| 1.5% arginine | −0.01 (−0.07 to 0.05) | 0.7% | .819 (NS) | −0.01 (−0.07 to 0.05) | 1.1% | .739 (NS) |
| 0.32% NaF | reference | — | — | reference | — | — |

- **Time course**: at 6 mo both arginine arms significant-but-noninferior vs control; at 1 y 8.0% arginine superior and 1.5% at parity — benefit consolidates over 1–2 y.
- **Safety**: no serious adverse events; 37 nonserious events (75.7% unrelated) — occasional toothache (4), dry mouth (2), unpalatability (2), acne (1).
- **Conclusion**: 8.0% arginine = effective fluoride-free anticaries alternative *superior* to NaF; 1.5% arginine *equivalent* to NaF. Effect is dose-dependent.
