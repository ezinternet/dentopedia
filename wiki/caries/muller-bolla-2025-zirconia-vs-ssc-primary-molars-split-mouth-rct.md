## One-line Summary

Abstract-only multicentre split-mouth RCT (105 children, 210 teeth): zirconia crowns and stainless steel crowns had comparable 2-year success (RR 0.99, 95% CI 0.96–1.01; p=0.350) in primary molars, but zirconia retention was lower (RR 0.95, 95% CI 0.92–0.98; p=0.001); ~2/3 of parents preferred zirconia.

## Summary

> [!note] Abstract-only page
> No PMC open-access full text was available at ingest; under wiki Rule #1 only the PubMed abstract was used ([DOI 10.1111/ipd.70026](https://doi.org/10.1111/ipd.70026)).

According to PubMed, this multicentre split-mouth RCT (105 children, 210 teeth, each child receiving both crown types) is high-quality primary evidence on zirconia crown (ZC) vs stainless steel crown (SSC) for primary molars. Over 2 years, **success rates were comparable (RR 0.99, 95% CI 0.96–1.01; p=0.350)** — both crowns reliably avoided major failure. The trade-off is **retention: significantly lower for ZC (RR 0.95, 95% CI 0.92–0.98; p=0.001)**, i.e. zirconia crowns came off more often. Preference split predictably: about **two-thirds of parents preferred zirconia** (esthetics), while children showed no clear preference.

Clinically, this supplies the patient-level primary data behind the SR+MA [[caries/chavhan-2026-zirconia-vs-ssc-primary-dentition-sr-ma]]: choose ZC when esthetics/parental preference dominate and cementation conditions are favorable, accepting a modest retention disadvantage; SSC remains the more retentive default. Together they round out the wiki's primary-molar crown evidence alongside the Hall-technique pages.

## Key Contributions

- 2-yr success equal for ZC vs SSC (RR 0.99, NS).
- ZC retention significantly lower (RR 0.95, p=0.001).
- Parents prefer ZC (esthetics); children indifferent.

## Methodology

Split-mouth multicentre RCT; 105 children, 210 teeth; 2-yr success (absence of major failure) primary outcome; Poisson regression, paired t-tests, ANCOVA. Confidence `rct`; abstract-only ingest (no PMC OA).

## Results

- Success: RR 0.99 (95% CI 0.96–1.01; p=0.350), NS.
- Retention: RR 0.95 (95% CI 0.92–0.98; p=0.001), lower for ZC.
- ~2/3 parents prefer ZC; children no preference.
