## One-line Summary

Systematic review (20 studies, 1,231 children, search to Sept 2025) of restorative interventions for MIH-affected first permanent molars (FPMs) and hypomineralized second primary molars (HSPM): overall clinical success 84% (12 mo) → 94% (18 mo) → 92% (24 mo) → 83% (36 mo); lowest for GIC (53% at 12 mo), highest (100%) for CAD/CAM fabricated ceramic (18 mo) and preformed metal crowns (PMCs, 24 mo); heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis, no evidence-based best-approach recommendation possible.

## Summary

This EAPD-commissioned systematic review (Gizani et al. 2026, European Archives of Paediatric Dentistry) synthesized clinical and radiographic success of restorative interventions for **molar incisor hypomineralization (MIH)**-affected first permanent molars (FPMs) and the primary-dentition counterpart, **hypomineralized second primary molars (HSPM)**, plus their impact on pulp health, periodontal health, and oral health-related quality of life (OHRQoL). Twenty papers (19 MIH, 1 HSPM) were included from a search of seven databases up to September 2025 (PROSPERO-registered). Total sample: 1,231 children.

Overall mean clinical success for MIH-FPMs ranged from 84% at 12 months to 94% at 18 months, 92% at 24 months, and 83% at 36 months. Glass-ionomer cement (GIC) was the weakest material (53% at 12 months); CAD/CAM fabricated ceramic restorations (18 months) and preformed metal crowns (PMCs, 24 months) achieved 100% success. Clinical and methodological heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis.

Clinically: for MIH-affected first permanent molars, full-coverage options (PMCs, indirect ceramic) outperform plain GIC long-term, though GIC retains value as an interim material. Restorative treatment does not appear to worsen pulp or periodontal health. The single HSPM study (Hall-technique PMCs) showed high success but ~1/3 gingival deterioration. The evidence base is too heterogeneous and mostly medium-quality to support a firm "best material" recommendation.

## Key Contributions

- First SR with search to Sept 2025 pooling restorative-intervention success for **MIH-affected FPMs** plus the **HSPM** primary-molar analogue.
- Adds three outcome domains beyond survival not covered in prior SRs: pulp health, periodontal health, and OHRQoL.
- Establishes a material success hierarchy: GIC lowest, CAD/CAM ceramic and PMCs highest across follow-up timepoints.
- Vital pulp therapy (VPT) shown viable for MIH teeth with deep caries (>95% success, RCT).
- Resin-based etch-and-rinse sealants effective for white opacities without enamel breakdown (retention 68% at 12 mo vs 8% for giomer self-etch); do not prevent post-eruptive breakdown.
- Restoring MIH-FPMs neither worsens pulp/periodontal health nor measurably improves OHRQoL.
- Flags periodontal trade-off for Hall-technique PMCs on HSPM (~1/3 gingival deterioration from subgingival crown margins).

## Methodology

- Systematic review; **no meta-analysis** (clinical + methodological heterogeneity; unclear risk of bias in most included studies).
- **PROSPERO**: CRD420251126788 (MIH); CRD420251126713 (HSPM). PRISMA-reported.
- **Databases**: Embase, Web of Science, DOSS, Medline/PubMed, Scopus, LILACS, Cochrane Library; plus grey literature (ClinicalTrials.gov, OpenGrey, ProQuest). Search date: 25 September 2025.
- **Eligibility**: Children/adolescents 3–18 years with HSPM or MIH; any restorative/non-restorative intervention; follow-up ≥12 months; outcomes: clinical/radiographic success, pulp status, periodontal status, OHRQoL, cooperation, patient/parental acceptance. Excluded: in vitro, case reports, reviews, <12 months follow-up, emergency/desensitization treatment, anterior teeth.
- **Included studies**: 10 RCTs, 5 retrospective cohort, 2 retrospective cross-sectional, 2 prospective cohort; n=1,231 children.
- **Risk of bias**: RoB 2.0 (RCTs); ROBINS-I (non-randomized); Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (cohort/cross-sectional). GRADE used for evidence certainty.
- 4 RCTs at low overall risk; 6 RCTs with some concerns. Good-quality cohorts: Bagattoni, Seremidi, Sekundo, de Farias.

## Results

### Overall success rates (MIH-FPMs)

| Follow-up | Mean clinical success |
|---|---|
| 12 months | 84% |
| 18 months | 94% |
| 24 months | 92% |
| 36 months | 83% |

Re-treatment needs: 1% at 12 months → 23% at 24 months.

### By material type

**Sealants (non-restorative)**
- Resin-based (etch-and-rinse): 68% retention at 12 mo; GIC-based sealants 83% retention; giomer (self-etch): 8% retention. Sealants prevent caries (OR 0.2) but not post-eruptive breakdown. Higher retention with white opacities, ≤¼ occlusal surface involvement.

**Direct GIC / glass hybrids**
- GIC (Bal 2024, SMART technique): 53% at 12 mo — lowest overall. HVGIC: 87.5% survival at 24 mo. Glass hybrid: initially acceptable but 78% failure at 72 mo (Sezer 2025). GIC + orthodontic bands: 98% at 18 mo when retention enhanced (Bagattoni 2025). GIC positioned as interim material, not definitive long-term solution.

**Direct resin composites**
- Acceptable short-to-medium term. Etch-and-rinse outperforms self-etch (81% vs 62% at 12 mo). Short-fiber reinforced composite comparable to glass hybrid at 36 mo. Indirect composite (=direct) in survival; preferred by patients due to shorter chair time. Overall composite survival on MIH teeth lower than on non-MIH teeth.

**Indirect restorations / full-coverage crowns**
- CAD/CAM ceramic (Montaser 2023): endocrowns 100% at 18 mo; occlusal veneers ~82% at 18 mo.
- Full-coverage crowns — cast metal, zirconia, lithium disilicate (Singh 2022): 96.7% overall retention at 24 mo. Radiographic success: 51/55 FPMs showed continued root development without pathology; 4 needed endodontic treatment.
- PMC (de Farias 2022): significantly higher survival than direct composite at 24 mo. PMC (pulp-therapy context, Al-Batayneh 2022): 100% at 6, 12, 24 mo.

**Pulp therapy**
- VPT (indirect pulp capping or cervical pulpotomy) in deeply carious MIH-FPMs: >95% clinical and 100% radiographic success at 24 mo; no statistically significant difference between techniques (Al-Batayneh & Abdelghani 2022, high-quality RCT).

**Periodontal status**
- Three studies — inconclusive. Treatment did not aggravate periodontal health. Two studies found improvement in plaque/gingival indices (attributed to hypersensitivity reduction enabling brushing). One study (indirect ceramic, 18 mo) showed no effect.

**OHRQoL**
- One high-quality RCT (Hajdarevic 2025, n=79): no significant CPQ11-14 improvement after treatment; no difference between extraction vs restorative treatment; no substantial long-term effect on dental fear/anxiety.

**HSPM (single study)**
- Declerck & Mampay 2021 (prospective cohort, 15 children, 39 Hall-technique PMCs, mean age 4.6 yr): none lost at 12 mo; 64% fully successful; 36% clinically acceptable gingival deterioration; radiographic success 93%; well tolerated. Gingival deterioration attributed to subgingival crown margins causing plaque retention.

### Key conclusions drawn by the authors

1. Resin-based etch-and-rinse sealants effective for white opacities without enamel loss.
2. Adhesive direct restorations effective short-term; GIC inadequate long-term in stress-bearing areas.
3. Indirect adhesively-luted restorations effective, with an advantage in child acceptance.
4. Full-coverage crowns provide the most reliable long-term performance for severely damaged MIH teeth.
5. VPT viable option for MIH-FPMs with deep caries, preserving tooth structure and function.
6. Periodontal and OHRQoL effects unclear due to limited studies.
7. Hall-technique PMCs acceptable and well-tolerated for HSPM management in young children.
