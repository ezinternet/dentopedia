## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1007/s40368-026-01221-y](https://doi.org/10.1007/s40368-026-01221-y)), this systematic review synthesized clinical and radiographic success of restorative interventions for **molar incisor hypomineralization (MIH)**-affected first permanent molars (FPMs) and the primary-dentition counterpart, **hypomineralized second primary molars (HSPM)**, plus their impact on pulp health, periodontal health, and oral health-related quality of life (OHRQoL). Twenty papers (19 MIH, 1 HSPM) were included from a search of seven databases up to September 2025. Overall mean clinical success for MIH-FPMs was 84% at 12 months and 83% at 36 months; glass-ionomer cement (GIC) was lowest (53% at 12 months) and fabricated ceramic (18 months) and preformed metal crowns (PMCs, 24 months) highest (100%). Clinical/methodological heterogeneity and unclear risk of bias precluded meta-analysis. **Note: abstract-only — full text not retrieved.**

Clinically: for MIH-affected first permanent molars, favor more durable, full-coverage options (preformed metal crowns, fabricated ceramic) over plain GIC, which had the weakest 12-month survival; restoring these teeth does not appear to worsen pulp/periodontal health or improve OHRQoL, and the single HSPM Hall-technique study warns that ~1/3 of cases saw gingival deterioration — but the evidence base is too heterogeneous and biased to support a firm "best material" recommendation.

## Key Contributions

- First SR pooling restorative-intervention success for **MIH-affected FPMs** plus the **HSPM** primary-molar analogue, across three extra outcome domains (pulp, periodontal, OHRQoL).
- Establishes a material success hierarchy: GIC lowest, PMC and fabricated ceramic highest.
- Shows restoring MIH-FPMs neither significantly worsens pulp/periodontal health nor improves OHRQoL.
- Flags a periodontal trade-off for Hall-technique PMCs on HSPM (~1/3 gingival deterioration).

## Methodology

- Systematic review; **no meta-analysis** (heterogeneity + unclear risk of bias).
- Seven electronic databases searched for studies published **up to September 2025**.
- Independent, previously calibrated reviewers performed eligibility selection and data extraction.
- Risk of bias assessed with the tool appropriate to each study design.
- **20 papers included** (19 MIH, 1 HSPM).

## Results

- Overall mean clinical success, MIH-affected FPMs: **84% at 12 months**, **83% at 36 months**.
- **Lowest** success: **glass-ionomer cement (GIC) — 53% at 12 months**.
- **Highest** success (**100%**): **fabricated ceramic restorations at 18 months** and **preformed metal crowns (PMCs) at 24 months**.
- Restorative treatment of MIH-FPMs did **not significantly aggravate pulp or periodontal health**, nor **improve OHRQoL**.
- HSPM (single study): **Hall-technique PMCs** = high success, but **~1/3 of cases** showed **gingival condition deterioration**.
- Heterogeneity + unclear risk of bias → **no meta-analysis**; **no solid evidence-based best-approach recommendation** for HSPM or MIH-FPMs.
