## 한줄요약
GIC 수복은 아말감 대비 이차 우식을 유의하게 적게 발생시키고(영구치 RR=0.20, 유치 RR=0.55), 레진 컴포지트와는 동등한 예방 효과를 보였다 (64 RCT 메타분석, 2023).

## Summary
SR/MA of 64 RCTs (8,310 GIC restorations vs 5,857 amalgam/composite, 1–10y follow-up) compared secondary caries incidence. GIC outperformed amalgam in both primary (RR=0.55, CI 0.41–0.72) and permanent dentition (RR=0.20, CI 0.11–0.38). GIC was equivalent to resin-composite in both dentitions (NS). Conventional GIC and RMGIC showed similar secondary caries prevention. Authors emphasize WHO's 2021 listing of GIC as essential medicine and growing clinical relevance.

## Key Contributions
- First contemporary meta-analysis stratifying GIC vs amalgam vs composite by dentition for secondary caries outcome
- Refutes legacy belief that amalgam's metal content provides superior anti-caries effect
- Confirms GIC ≈ composite for secondary caries (clinically reassuring for high-caries-risk patients where GIC's fluoride release may be desired)
- No advantage of RMGIC over conventional GIC for caries prevention

## Methodology
- **Design**: SR + meta-analysis (PRISMA), PROSPERO CRD42022380959
- **Search**: PubMed, Web of Science, Cochrane, Scopus (last 3 Jan 2023)
- **PICOS**: P=all ages; I=cGIC or RMGIC; C=amalgam or composite; O=secondary caries incidence; S=RCTs
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane Handbook 5.4.1
- **Analysis**: RevMan 5.4, fixed-effects, RR with 95% CI
- **Stratification**: dentition (primary/permanent), follow-up (short/medium/long)

## Results
| Comparison | Dentition | RR | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|---|
| GIC vs amalgam | Primary | 0.55 | 0.41–0.72 |
| GIC vs amalgam | Permanent | 0.20 | 0.11–0.38 |
| GIC vs composite | Primary | 0.92 | 0.77–1.10 (NS) |
| GIC vs composite | Permanent | 0.77 | 0.39–1.51 (NS) |
| cGIC vs RMGIC | Primary | 1.12 | 0.67–1.87 (NS) |
| cGIC vs RMGIC | Permanent | 1.63 | 0.34–7.84 (NS) |

- 21 studies (4,807 restorations) on primary, 38 studies (4,885 restorations) on permanent
- 5 studies excluded from meta-analysis (non-standard outcome criteria)

## Clinical Implications
- GIC is a defensible material choice in high-caries-risk patients, pediatric dentistry, and geriatric/root-caries patients
- Composite remains comparable for caries prevention — material selection can prioritize esthetics, durability, occlusal load
- Amalgam's perceived caries-prevention advantage is not supported
