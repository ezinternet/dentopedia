## Summary

This cross-sectional metagenomics study from Turkey (n=52 adults) profiled the microbial composition of black stain (BS) plaque in permanent dentition using NGS (16S rRNA), stratified into four groups: BS+/caries-free, BS+/carious, BS−/caries-free, and BS−/carious (n=13 per group). Caries was classified with ICDAS II.

BS+ patients had significantly more caries-free teeth than BS− patients (p=0.007), confirming the inverse association in adults. The BS+ biofilm showed a complex multi-genus community: Neisseria (5.4%), Capnocytophaga (4.8%), and Corynebacterium (3.9%) were most abundant across all BS+ samples. In the BS+/caries-free subgroup specifically, Capnocytophaga reached 10.8%, with Cardiobacterium (3.6%) and Rothia (1.72%) also elevated. Notably, Actinomyces — the classically cited genus — was not the dominant organism in this NGS dataset, pointing to a more complex community structure than culture-based studies suggest.

This study is among the first to apply metagenomic sequencing to black stain in adult permanent dentition, filling a gap left by culture-based and pediatric-focused literature.

## Key Contributions

- First/early NGS metagenomic profiling of black stain in adult permanent dentition
- Identifies Capnocytophaga, Neisseria, Corynebacterium as dominant BS+ genera — challenges Actinomyces-only narrative
- BS+/caries-free subgroup has highest Capnocytophaga (10.8%) + Cardiobacterium + Rothia enrichment
- Confirms inverse BS–caries association in adults via ICDAS II standardized scoring (p=0.007)
- Demonstrates intra-BS+ microbiome heterogeneity between carious and caries-free patients

## Methodology

- Design: Cross-sectional, 4-group (BS+/caries-free, BS+/carious, BS−/caries-free, BS−/carious; n=13 each)
- Total n=52 adults; permanent dentition only
- Caries assessment: ICDAS II
- Microbiome: NGS 16S rRNA amplicon sequencing of plaque samples
- Statistics: Caries-free teeth counts compared across BS+/BS− (p<0.05 threshold); genus abundances compared between groups

## Results

**Caries:** BS+ patients had significantly more caries-free teeth vs BS− (p=0.007).

**All BS+ plaque (genus-level, p<0.05):**

| Genus | Abundance |
|---|---|
| Neisseria | 5.4% |
| Capnocytophaga | 4.8% |
| Corynebacterium | 3.9% |

**BS+/caries-free subgroup (p<0.05):**

| Genus | Abundance |
|---|---|
| Capnocytophaga | 10.8% |
| Cardiobacterium | 3.6% |
| Rothia | 1.72% |

- Complex multi-genus biofilm, not reducible to Actinomyces alone
- Micro-community shifts within BS+ may correlate with caries protection level
