## One-line Summary
Cochrane SR+MA (11 RCTs, 3374 children aged 5–10): cannot determine whether resin sealant or fluoride varnish is superior for preventing occlusal caries (OR 0.67, 95% CI 0.37–1.19, I²=84%, very low certainty); combined sealant + varnish may beat varnish alone (OR 0.30, very low certainty).

## Summary
This is the current (pub5, 2020) Cochrane review on the *relative* effectiveness of pit-and-fissure sealants versus fluoride varnish — the comparator question that sits one step beyond the no-treatment baseline established by Ahovuo-Saloranta 2017. Across 11 RCTs, the pooled resin-sealant-vs-varnish estimate was imprecise and highly heterogeneous (OR 0.67, 95% CI 0.37–1.19, I²=84%), so neither intervention could be declared superior; all results were graded very low certainty. A single split-mouth trial suggested adding sealant on top of varnish reduces caries by ~77% over varnish alone at two years. No adverse events were reported. The authorship changed at this update (Kashbour-led, with a health-economics co-author), and pub5 supersedes the pub4 (2016) Ahovuo-Saloranta version. Fourteen ongoing trials are expected to sharpen future conclusions.

## Key Contributions
- Current best evidence on sealant-vs-varnish relative effectiveness: indeterminate (very low certainty).
- Adds 3 trials and a cost-effectiveness objective relative to pub4.
- Reinforces a combination-therapy (sealant + FV) signal over varnish alone, though from a single trial.
- Catalogs 14 ongoing trials as the pipeline for resolving the question.

## Methodology
RCTs, participants <20 years, ≥12-month follow-up; all included children 5–10. Grouped by sealant material (resin vs GIC-based) and follow-up. OR (Becker-Balagtas for split-mouth), RR for one cluster-RCT, MD for DMFS/DMFT; 95% CI; random-effects when ≥4 studies; GRADE. 11 trials / 3374 participants; search to 19 March 2020.

## Results
- Resin sealant vs FV, 2–3 yr: OR 0.67 (95% CI 0.37–1.19), I²=84%, 4 studies, 1683 children — uncertain, very low certainty.
- DMFS/DMFT increment at 2 yr: MD −0.09 / −0.08 — small, possibly not clinically meaningful.
- Single small high-RoB trial: 4 yr RR 0.42 (0.21–0.84); 9 yr RR 0.48 (0.29–0.79).
- GIC sealant vs FV: no consistent advantage; very low certainty.
- Sealant + FV vs FV alone: OR 0.30 (0.17–0.55), ~77% reduction at 2 yr, 1 trial — very low certainty.
- Adverse events: none reported (5 trials, 1801 participants).
