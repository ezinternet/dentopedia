## One-line Summary

Epidemiologic survey (n=2,675 preschool children) found EBS prevalence of 12.10% with significantly lower caries/dmft in EBS children; 16S rRNA profiling showed Actinomyces/Rothia enrichment in EBS+ caries-free plaque and, for the first time, a Lactobacillus-enriched gut microbiome signature in EBS+ children.

## Summary

This large cross-sectional study combined an epidemiologic survey of 2,675 Chinese preschool children (aged 3–6 years) with 16S rRNA microbiome profiling of both supragingival plaque and fecal samples. EBS (early black stain) prevalence was 12.10%. EBS children had significantly lower caries prevalence and lower dmft scores (p<0.01), reinforcing the caries-protective phenotype. Microbiome participants were divided into four plaque groups (BCP, BP, CP, P) and four fecal groups (BCF, BF, CF, F) by EBS and caries status. The BP group (EBS+, caries-free) showed enrichment of Actinomyces and Rothia in plaque, while the CP group (caries, no EBS) showed enrichment of Streptococcus mutans. The healthy P group had the most complex plaque microbiome. Crucially, this is the first study to profile the gut microbiome in EBS children: the BF group (EBS+, caries-free) had a distinctive gut microbiome with Lactobacillus as a biomarker, suggesting a possible systemic or host-immune component to EBS susceptibility and caries protection.

## Key Contributions

- Large-scale EBS prevalence benchmark: 12.10% among Chinese preschool children aged 3–6
- Confirmed epidemiologic caries-protection signal: EBS children had significantly lower caries prevalence and dmft (p<0.01)
- Replicated Actinomyces enrichment in EBS+ caries-free plaque (extending Li 2015)
- Added Rothia as a co-enriched taxon in EBS+ caries-free plaque
- Streptococcus mutans confirmed as enriched in caries-only (no EBS) group
- First gut microbiome characterization in EBS children: Lactobacillus distinctive in EBS+ caries-free fecal samples
- Four-group design (BCP/BP/CP/P) enables dissection of EBS × caries interaction effects

## Methodology

- **Design**: Cross-sectional (two-phase)
- **Phase 1**: Epidemiologic survey, n=2,675 preschool children aged 3–6 years; clinical EBS and caries examination (dmft index)
- **Phase 2**: Microbiome — supragingival plaque + fecal samples; four plaque groups (BCP, BP, CP, P) and four fecal groups (BCF, BF, CF, F)
- **Sequencing**: 16S rRNA amplicon (oral plaque and fecal)
- **Analysis**: Alpha/beta diversity; differential abundance; biomarker identification

## Results

| Group | Plaque features | Fecal features |
|---|---|---|
| BP (EBS+, caries−) | Actinomyces ↑, Rothia ↑ | — |
| CP (EBS−, caries+) | S. mutans ↑ | — |
| P (healthy) | Most complex/diverse | — |
| BF (EBS+, caries−) | — | Lactobacillus ↑ (unique EBS gut signature) |

- EBS prevalence: **12.10%** (n=2,675)
- EBS vs caries: EBS children had significantly lower caries prevalence and lower dmft (p<0.01)
- EBS proposed as possible protective factor against early childhood caries (ECC)
