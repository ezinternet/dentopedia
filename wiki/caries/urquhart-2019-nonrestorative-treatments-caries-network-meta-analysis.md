## Summary
This network meta-analysis, produced by the ADA Center for Evidence-Based Dentistry to underpin its nonrestorative caries clinical practice guideline, is the first to compare the full menu of non- and micro-invasive caries interventions simultaneously, by lesion type, dentition, and surface. By combining direct and indirect evidence, it ranks fluoride- and nonfluoride-based interventions for arresting or reversing noncavitated and cavitated lesions. Silver diamine fluoride and sealant-plus-fluoride combinations emerge among the most effective, with minor adverse events (notably SDF staining). It serves as the synthesis anchor over single-intervention trials such as Chestnutt's sealant-vs-varnish RCT.

## Key Contributions
- First comprehensive NMA of nonrestorative caries treatments across all relevant surfaces.
- Direct evidence base for the ADA nonrestorative caries guideline.

## Methodology
SR + NMA of parallel and split-mouth RCTs (any follow-up); MEDLINE, Embase, Cochrane CENTRAL. Outcomes: arrest/reversal of noncavitated and cavitated lesions (primary + permanent teeth) and adverse events.

## Results
- Multiple effective agents; SDF and sealant + fluoride rank highly.
- Effectiveness depends on lesion type, dentition, surface.
- Minor adverse events (SDF staining).
