## One-line Summary

RCT (single-blind parallel, n=78 young adults with moderate gingivitis, ≥4 IDB-accessible interdental spaces/quadrant): water flosser (WF, n=40) vs interdental brush (IDB, n=38) adjunct to manual toothbrushing over 4 weeks — both reduced BOMP/BOPP (p=0.000), but WF was significantly more effective for marginal gingival health (all-sites BOMP p=0.003, BOPP p=0.030; interdental BOMP p=0.019 but interdental BOPP NS p=0.219), with no difference in gingival abrasion.

## Summary

This 4-week single-centre RCT directly compared two interdental-cleaning devices — a **water flosser (WF, Waterpik)** versus an **interdental brush (IDB, Interprox premium nano)** — each used once daily as an adjunct to twice-daily manual toothbrushing (Bass technique) in young adults with moderate gingivitis. Direct WF-vs-IDB comparisons were previously limited to single-use and 2-week pilots, so this trial fills a head-to-head gap. Bleeding was measured by two non-interchangeable indices — **bleeding on marginal probing (BOMP)** and **bleeding on pocket probing (BOPP)** — in two contralateral quadrants, plus a **Gingival Abrasion Score (GAS)** as a soft-tissue safety endpoint.

Both devices significantly reduced bleeding from baseline. The WF outperformed the IDB for marginal gingival inflammation (BOMP) at 4 weeks, while equally safe on abrasion. The all-sites BOPP advantage was statistically significant but weaker, and was confounded by a baseline BOPP imbalance and absent at interdental sites. The study is industry-sponsored (Water Pik employee as first author), and only a single IDB size was used in selected fitting locations, both of which temper the strength of the WF-favouring conclusion.

## Key Contributions

- Head-to-head 4-week RCT of **WF vs IDB** (not WF vs floss), addressing the device-selection question for interdental cleaning.
- Dual-index bleeding assessment (**BOMP** for marginal inflammation, **BOPP** for pocket inflammation), showing the WF advantage is index-dependent — clearest for marginal (BOMP), weaker/absent for pocket (BOPP) bleeding.
- Built-in **safety endpoint (GAS)** showing the WF is no more abrasive than the IDB.

## Methodology

- **Design**: single-blind, single-centre, parallel, 4-week RCT (CONSORT/TIDieR). ACTA, Amsterdam. NTR6081. Conducted Oct–Dec 2016.
- **Participants**: systemically healthy non-smokers 18–65 y, ≥20 natural teeth (5 evaluable/quadrant), moderate-severe gingivitis (≥50% BOPP), no pockets >5 mm, buccal-accessible interdental spaces fitting the IDB in **≥4 spaces per quadrant** (2 involving molars). Mostly university students. Analyzed: WF n=40, IDB n=38 (82 enrolled, 78 completed).
- **Interventions** (once daily, adjunct to manual brushing):
  - **WF**: Waterpik Water Flosser, 500 mL lukewarm water, Classic Tip, tip 90° to long axis at gingival margin, pressure dial 8.
  - **IDB**: Interprox premium nano conical brush (1.9 mm brush / 0.38 mm core), 5 in-out strokes/site, buccal approach, only fitting spaces.
- **Outcomes** at baseline / 2 wk / 4 wk: BOPP (0.25 N plastic force-controlled probe, 6 sites/tooth), BOMP (WHO ball-ended probe, ~2 mm at ~60°, 6 sites/tooth), assessed in two contralateral quadrants; GAS via Mira-2-Ton disclosing solution.
- **Analysis**: SPSS 23, examiners blinded; paired tests within-group, independent tests between-group; p<0.05.

## Results

**Within-group**: both WF and IDB significantly reduced BOMP and BOPP from baseline (p=0.000) at W2 and W4, for all sites and interdental-only sites.

**Bleeding on marginal probing (BOMP)** — WF significantly lower than IDB:
- All sites: WF 0.82→0.54, IDB 0.95→0.75; between-group **W2 p=0.002, W4 p=0.003** (mean diff 0.21). Baseline NS (p=0.082).
- Interdental sites: WF 0.80→0.52, IDB 0.92→0.70; between-group **W2 p=0.003, W4 p=0.019** (diff 0.18). Baseline NS (p=0.130).

**Bleeding on pocket probing (BOPP)** — WF advantage weaker/confounded:
- All sites: WF 0.60→0.50, IDB 0.66→0.56; between-group **W4 p=0.030**. *But baseline already differed (p=0.030, diff 0.06 ≈ W4 diff)* → the all-sites BOPP difference partly reflects the baseline imbalance.
- Interdental sites: between-group **W4 p=0.219 (NS)** — no WF advantage.

**Gingival abrasion (GAS)**: **no between-group difference** at any timepoint (all sites W4 p=0.256; small lesions p=0.230; interdental area p=0.362). Both devices equally non-traumatic to soft tissue.

**Safety**: 6 adverse events; only 1 (localized interdental inflammation + sensitivity) attributed to IDB use, resolved without withdrawal.

**Conclusion**: In moderate gingivitis over 4 weeks, the WF was more effective than the IDB for obtaining **marginal** gingival health (BOMP), with equal soft-tissue safety. The interdental-BOPP outcome and the baseline-confounded all-sites BOPP advantage qualify how strongly "WF > IDB" can be stated, and industry sponsorship plus single-IDB-size design warrant cautious interpretation.
