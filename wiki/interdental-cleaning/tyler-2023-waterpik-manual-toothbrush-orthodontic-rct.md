## One-line Summary

Single-centre two-arm parallel single-blind RCT (1:1, n=40, ages 10-20, upper+lower fixed appliances, follow-up to 56 weeks): adding a Waterpik to a manual toothbrush gave NO statistically significant difference in plaque (OMPI diff 0.199, p=0.88), gingival (GI diff −0.008, p=0.94), or interdental-bleeding (IBI diff 5.60, p=0.563) indices vs manual toothbrush alone; trial stopped at interim analysis — no evidence of Waterpik benefit.

## Summary

Whether a **Waterpik (oral irrigator) added to a manual toothbrush (Waterpik + MTB)** outperforms a **manual toothbrush alone (MTB)** for oral hygiene during fixed orthodontic treatment, tested over a clinically realistic course (up to 56 weeks). The marketing premise is that the orthodontic-tip Waterpik mechanically removes plaque around brackets alongside its pulsing-water action, and a single prior 28-day trial (Sharma) reported a plaque benefit. This RCT, with a much longer follow-up and patients recruited before bond-up, found **no benefit on any index**. The likely reasons earlier results did not replicate: the prior trial enrolled only poor-hygiene patients, used a short follow-up that captured a **novelty effect**, and recruited patients already in braces. Bottom line: in fixed-appliance orthodontic patients with adequate baseline hygiene, a Waterpik adds nothing measurable to manual toothbrushing.

## Key Contributions

1. First RCT to test the orthodontic-tip Waterpik **as an adjunct to** (not replacement for) a manual toothbrush over 56 weeks in fixed-appliance patients.
2. Null result across plaque, gingival, and interdental-bleeding indices — a long-follow-up counterweight to the single prior short-term positive trial.
3. Articulated why short-term positive findings may not generalise: baseline-hygiene selection bias, novelty effect, and recruitment timing.

## Methodology

- **Design**: single-centre, single-operator, two-arm, parallel-group, stratified, **single-blind RCT**, **1:1** allocation. Department of Orthodontics, York Hospital, UK (NHS, non-fee-paying patients).
- **Participants**: **n=40**, **ages 10-20**, fit/well, **full upper + lower arch fixed appliances** (pre-adjusted edgewise MBT brackets, Transbond XT), normal periodontal health, brushing >=2x/day, not already using a Waterpik.
- **Arms**: **Control = MTB** (Oral-B manual toothbrush, 1450 ppm F toothpaste, mouthwash) + standardised oral-health education. **Intervention = Waterpik + MTB** (Waterpik Water Flosser WP-560 with orthodontic tip, used once nightly ~1 min) + identical MTB regime.
- **Outcomes / indices**: plaque (Orthodontic Modification of Plaque Index, **OMPI**; Plaque Index at baseline), gingival (Gingival Index, **GI**), interdental bleeding (Interdental Bleeding Index, **IBI**), plus trauma, adherence, satisfaction. Recorded at **baseline / 8 / 32 / 56 weeks**. Operator performing indices blinded to allocation.
- **Randomisation**: stratified block (gender, age <15 vs >=15, baseline IBI <=20% vs >20%; 8 blocks, sealed opaque envelopes); switched to coin toss after participant 33 due to COVID-19 recruitment constraints.
- **Analysis**: intention-to-treat; **generalised linear mixed model** (handles non-normal, repeated-measures, missing data). Powered on OMPI (alpha 0.05, effect size 0.5, SD 0.3, power 0.9 → min 7/group; recruited 20/group).

## Results

- **Interim analysis at trial stop**: 40 recruited, **85% of data collected**; analysable n at 8/32/56 wk = 40/32/19. Trial stopped because sample size was reached, dropouts were fewer than expected, and continuing during COVID-19 was deemed unethical.
- **Baseline (Intervention vs Control)** — balanced: age 14.9 vs 14.8 yr; %female 50 vs 65; PI 0.95 vs 0.94; GI 0.77 vs 0.74; IBI 27.1% vs 34.7%.
- **Mixed-model overall mean differences (treatment − control)** — all non-significant:

| Outcome | Difference | p | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|---|
| Plaque (OMPI) | **0.199** | **0.88** | −0.24 to 0.27 |
| Gingival (GI) | **−0.008** | **0.94** | −0.22 to 0.20 |
| Interdental bleeding (IBI) | **5.60** | **0.563** | −13.22 to 24.42 |

- **No statistically significant difference for any variable.**
- OMPI intra-rater reliability **ICC = 0.911** (95% CI 0.700-0.977). **No trauma** detected in either group at any visit; **no harms**.
- Adherence uninterpretable (<25% of diaries returned); satisfaction questionnaire unvalidated.
- **Conclusion**: in the population studied, **no benefit** in oral hygiene from adding a Waterpik to a manual toothbrush in fixed-appliance orthodontic patients.
