## One-line Summary

Multicentre single-blind RCT (secondary analysis, n=323 nulliparous women at preeclampsia risk, intact periodontium, enrolled at 12 wk gestation): daily calibrated interdental brushes + individualised instruction vs routine antenatal care reduced BOP from 56% to 12% (−79.9%, p<0.001) by 8 months — most in the first week (−56.8%) — while controls stayed flat; interdental brushing was the strongest independent predictor of bleeding resolution (OR=3.14, 95% CI 2.01–4.90).

## Summary

Pregnancy-associated gingivitis affects up to ~70% of pregnant women — a plaque-induced condition modified by hormonal/immunological changes (2018 EFP/AAP classification) that raises gingival bleeding even when plaque is stable. This secondary analysis of a multi-centre, single-blind RCT in Dakar, Senegal (Mar 2022 – Jul 2023) tested whether early, individualised **calibrated interdental brush (IDB)** use can reduce gingival bleeding across gestation. 323 nulliparous women at high preeclampsia risk with intact periodontium were enrolled at 12 weeks and randomised 1:1 to daily calibrated IDBs + instruction versus routine antenatal care, with recalls at 1 week, 4, 5 and 8 months.

By 8 months the intervention group's bleeding on probing fell from 56% to 12% (−79.9%, p<0.001) while controls stayed flat; most of the reduction happened within the **first week** (−56.8%). The effect was strongest among women with severe baseline bleeding (−84.3%) and held across all baseline-plaque strata. Interdental brushing was the single strongest independent predictor of bleeding resolution (OR=3.14, 95% CI 2.01–4.90). The authors position individualised interdental hygiene as a feasible primary-prevention component of antenatal oral care — while cautioning that BOP is a surrogate of inflammation and that the same intervention did not improve composite obstetric outcomes (reported separately).

## Key Contributions

- First RCT to evaluate calibrated interdental brushing across the **whole gestational period** (12 wk → 8 months) in women at high preeclampsia risk with an intact periodontium.
- Shows pregnancy-associated gingival bleeding is **rapidly reversible** by mechanical interdental biofilm disruption (bulk of effect in week 1).
- Establishes interdental brushing as the **strongest independent predictor** of bleeding resolution, robust to a priori covariate adjustment and to baseline plaque imbalance.
- Supports integrating individualised interdental hygiene as **primary prevention** in antenatal care — a population rarely targeted in preventive periodontal trials.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Multi-centre, single-blind, two-arm (1:1) RCT; six antenatal clinics, Dakar, Senegal. Outcome assessors and statisticians masked; participants unblinded.
- **Population**: n=323 nulliparous sub-Saharan African women, 18–40 y, enrolled at 12 wk gestation, ≥20 natural teeth, intact periodontium (periodontitis excluded). Active n=162, control n=161. No smokers. Baseline gingival diagnosis: generalised gingivitis ~80% of each arm.
- **Intervention**: Personalised calibrated interdental brushes (Curaprox CPS, Curaden; ISO 16409:2016) sized to the embrasure, daily evening use, baseline demonstration + standardised compliance booklet; adherence 87.0%. **Control**: routine toothbrushing + toothpaste, no interdental devices/mouthrinses.
- **Recalls/measures**: T1 (3 mo +1 wk), T2 (4 mo), T3 (5 mo), T4 (8 mo). Full-mouth BOP, CAL, PD, GI, PI at 6 sites/tooth, Williams probe at 20 g pressure.
- **Outcome/analysis**: Primary = BOP (% bleeding sites) at T4 (ITT). Mixed linear models for longitudinal change; logistic regression for predictors of resolution. R 4.4.0, two-sided p<0.05. Per-protocol and baseline-PI-stratified sensitivity analyses.
- Trial was **powered for preeclampsia**; BOP was a prespecified secondary outcome. Registration NCT04989075. Funded by Curaden AG.

## Results

| Time | Active BOP (median) | Within-group change | Between-group |
|---|---|---|---|
| T0 (baseline) | 0.56 | — | NS (p=0.202) |
| T1 (3 mo +1 wk) | 0.26 | −56.8% (p<0.001) | p<0.001 |
| T2 (4 mo) | 0.19 | −28.7% | p<0.001 |
| T3 (5 mo) | 0.11 | −23.1% | p<0.001 |
| T4 (8 mo) | 0.12 | −18.2% | p<0.001 |
| **T4 vs T0** | — | **−79.9%** (−92.8 to −65.6, p<0.001) | mean diff −0.43 (95% CI −0.49 to −0.36) |

- **Control arm**: stable throughout — overall change +5.3% (p=0.685). MLM treatment × time interaction p<0.001.
- **Severe baseline subgroup** (BOP > Q3, >0.81; active n=40): −84.3% (p<0.05); univariate OR=14.8 (95% CI 4.9–44.7) but unstable/non-significant in penalised model.
- **Predictor of resolution at T4**: interdental brushing strongest independent predictor — OR=3.14 (95% CI 2.01–4.90) per abstract; multivariable model OR=3.84 (95% CI 2.01–7.32, p<0.001).
- **Robustness**: per-protocol mean difference −0.42 (−0.50 to −0.33), MLM p<0.001 — concordant with ITT. Effect significant across all baseline-PI strata (p<0.001), no PI × treatment interaction (all p>0.09), addressing the higher baseline plaque index in controls (p<0.001). Adjusted attrition lower in intervention arm (20.8% vs 34.1%, p=0.015). No adverse effects.
- **Caveats**: BOP is a surrogate of inflammation, not tissue destruction; the intervention did **not** significantly reduce composite obstetric outcomes (preeclampsia, preterm birth, low birth weight, SGA — reported separately); single Dakar population limits generalisability; industry-funded.
