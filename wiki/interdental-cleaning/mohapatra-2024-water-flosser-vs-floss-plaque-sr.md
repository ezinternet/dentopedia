## One-line Summary

Systematic review (PRISMA/PROSPERO-registered, 7 RCTs, ~155 vs 156 adults; qualitative synthesis, NO meta-analysis due to I²=97% heterogeneity): the majority of trials (4/7) favored water flossers over dental floss for plaque reduction — particularly at inaccessible interproximal areas — while 3/7 found no between-group difference; GRADE certainty "moderate."

## Summary

This systematic review asked whether water flossers (oral irrigators) outperform dental floss — the conventional "gold standard" interdental aid — for **plaque reduction** in adults. Searching six sources (PubMed, Scopus, Cochrane, ScienceDirect, Lilac, Google Scholar) plus hand search over Jan 2002–Oct 2022, the authors included 7 RCTs (n=34–83 each; 4 parallel, 3 split-mouth) and synthesized them qualitatively, because clinical and statistical heterogeneity (I²=97%, p<0.00001) made meta-analysis inappropriate.

**Clinical bottom line:** most included trials favored water flossers over dental floss for plaque reduction, and water flossers consistently reached inaccessible interproximal/posterior surfaces that floss struggles to clean. The authors recommend water flossers as an effective alternative for patients with **limited manual dexterity, orthodontic appliances, or dental prostheses** — while noting cost as an overlooked drawback. Because this is a qualitative SR of only 7 small, heterogeneous trials (GRADE "moderate," imprecision "serious"), the conclusion is directional rather than a pooled effect.

This finding should be read against [[interdental-cleaning/yiamwattana-2025-oral-irrigator-vs-floss-orthodontic-sr-ma]], a later SR+MA in **orthodontic** patients that found **no significant superiority** of oral irrigators over floss (also with high heterogeneity). The two reviews differ in population (general adults vs orthodontic), endpoint emphasis, and included trials — so the present "water flosser favored" signal is best treated as population- and endpoint-dependent, not a settled superiority claim.

## Key Contributions

- First SR to compare floss vs water flosser specifically on the plaque-reduction endpoint in adults (earlier SRs emphasized gingival/bleeding outcomes and found little plaque-score change).
- Majority direction (4/7 trials) favors water flossers, with a recurring advantage at inaccessible interproximal/posterior surfaces.
- Honest non-pooling: reports the heterogeneity (I²=97%) that precludes meta-analysis rather than forcing a misleading pooled estimate.
- Translates evidence into a patient-selection recommendation (dexterity-limited, orthodontic, prosthesis-wearing patients).

## Methodology

- **PICO**: adults >18 y with plaque (P); water flosser (I); dental floss (C); plaque reduction (O). PROSPERO-registered, PRISMA 2020.
- **Databases / window**: PubMed, Scopus, Cochrane, ScienceDirect, Lilac, Google Scholar + hand search; **Jan 1, 2002 – Oct 31, 2022**.
- **Eligibility**: RCTs in healthy adults comparing dental floss vs water flosser with plaque-score reduction as primary outcome; English.
- **Flow**: 2103 records → 2098 de-duplicated → 45 title-eligible → 10 full-text → **7 included** (qualitative analysis; **NO meta-analysis**).
- **Appraisal**: JBI 13-item RCT checklist; GRADE certainty; heterogeneity via I²/Chi-square (RevMan 5.4.1). Designs: 4 parallel + 3 split-mouth; 6 single-blind; sample sizes 34–83; adjunct to manual toothbrushing in 6/7.

## Results

- **All 7 trials**: significant plaque reduction with both devices.
- **4/7 favored water flosser** (Goyal 2013, Akram 2015, Sarlati 2016, Batool 2021). Goyal 2013 example: floss 57.7% whole-mouth / 63.4% approximal vs **water flosser 74.4% / 81.6%**.
- **3/7 no between-group difference** (Sasikumar 2016, Abdellatif 2021, Sawan 2022); Sawan still favored water flosser at the **distal molar** surface.
- **Inaccessible interproximal areas**: water flosser repeatedly outperformed floss at hard-to-reach posterior/proximal sites — the review's central qualitative theme.
- **GRADE moderate**; imprecision "serious" (unequal baseline plaque scores). A pooled SMD of 2.47 (95% CI 0.75–4.18) favoring water flosser was reported across 6 studies but **not relied upon** because I²=97%.
- Best methodological quality: Akram 2015 (11/13 JBI); lowest: Sasikumar 2016 (9/13).
- **Caveats**: small samples, narrow geography, unmeasured compliance, possible Hawthorne effect, and unaddressed device cost.
