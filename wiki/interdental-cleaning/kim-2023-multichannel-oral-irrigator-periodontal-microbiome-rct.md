## One-line Summary

Small preliminary RCT (n=29 healthy adults, 3-day no-brushing model): a multichannel oral irrigator (COMORAL, 45° gingival-margin jet + suction) kept PI/SBI stable and significantly lowered % BOP, and prevented the Prevotella (+114%, p=0.003) and Bacteroidetes (p=0.031) increase seen in the no-cleaning control, instead enriching pioneer non-mutans streptococci.

## 한줄요약

소규모 예비 무작위대조시험 (Randomized Controlled Trial, RCT; 건강한 성인 29명, 3일 무칫솔질 모델): 다채널 구강세정기 (Multichannel Oral Irrigator, MCOI; COMORAL, 잇몸변연 45° 분사 + 석션)는 치태지수·치은열구출혈지수를 유지하고 탐침시출혈 (Bleeding on Probing, BOP) 비율을 유의하게 낮췄으며, 무세정 대조군의 Prevotella(+114%, p=0.003)·Bacteroidetes(p=0.031) 증가를 막고 대신 선구균(비뮤탄스 연쇄상구균)을 늘렸다.

## Summary

This Seoul National University randomized two-group preliminary trial tested whether a **multichannel oral irrigator (MCOI; COMORAL)** — a mouthpiece that fires dozens of water jets simultaneously at the gingival margin, with synchronized suction to prevent aspiration — could protect periodontal health and oral-microbiome ecology under a deliberately harsh **3-day no-brushing challenge**. In healthy adults who stopped brushing for 3 days, the no-cleaning control group deteriorated (rising plaque index, sulcus bleeding, calculus, and a dysbiotic microbiome shift toward Prevotella and Bacteroidetes), whereas the MCOI group held its clinical indices steady, **significantly reduced bleeding on probing (BOP)**, and maintained a healthier microbial community enriched in pioneer commensal streptococci. The value of this paper to the wiki is that it links **oral irrigation to oral-microbiome shifts** — a mechanistic angle that complements [[liu-2025-water-flossing-adjunct-nspt-periodontitis-rct]], which also used 16S rRNA sequencing but in periodontitis patients undergoing non-surgical therapy. The device did not effectively remove attached supragingival plaque (plaque still accumulated), so its benefit appears concentrated at the gingival margin / subgingival biofilm and gingival inflammation rather than total plaque clearance.

## Key Contributions

- First clinical evaluation of a **multichannel** (vs one-channel) oral irrigator, whose synchronized suction addresses aspiration risk — relevant for disabled, elderly, and dysphagia patients.
- Pairs clinical periodontal indices with **16S rRNA amplicon + shotgun metagenome** sequencing, connecting an irrigation device to oral-microbiome ecology and functional metabolic pathways.
- Demonstrates that under a no-brushing challenge, the MCOI **blunts dysbiotic shifts** (Prevotella, Bacteroidetes) and enriches pioneer commensals, while reducing cariogenic/acidogenic metabolic pathways.

## Methodology

- **Design**: randomized two-group preliminary/pilot clinical trial; **n=29 analyzed** (30 recruited, 1 lost; planned 42 but IRB cut the sample). Healthy adults, mean age 33.7 ± 8.2 y (16 F / 13 M). IRB Seoul National University Dental Hospital.
- **Groups**: **MCOI group** (COMORAL device, 45° gingival-margin jets + suction, twice daily, 3 min, medium intensity, **no brushing**) vs **control group** (no brushing for 3 days, light tap-water gargle before bed).
- **3-day no-brush model**: baseline scaling for all → 2-week wait → phase-1 measurement + saliva → 3-day intervention → phase-2 measurement + saliva.
- **Outcomes**: modified Quigley-Hein **Plaque Index (PI)**, Mühlemann-Son **Sulcus Bleeding Index (SBI)**, **% BOP**, swelling (single examiner) + **16S rRNA amplicon** (V3–V4, MiSeq, all 29) and **shotgun metagenome** (NovaSeq, selected; MetaPhlAn4/HUMAnN3). Wilcoxon signed-rank, p=0.05.

## Results

**Clinical**
- Control: PI and SBI **significantly increased**; MCOI: no significant change in either.
- **% BOP significantly lower in the MCOI group** (decreased), increased in control — gingival inflammation alleviated by the device.
- Supragingival calculus after 3 days only in control (14.28%, 2 of 14); none in MCOI.

**Microbiome (16S rRNA)**
- Alpha diversity: no significant between-group difference.
- Control: **Bacteroidetes ↑ (15.3% → 20.4%, p=0.031)**; **Prevotella spp. ↑ 114.28% (p=0.003)** (opportunistic periodontal pathogen, possible disease-progression signal). MCOI: **no significant Prevotella change**, near-basal distribution.
- Beta diversity differed between groups (PCoA ANOSIM p=0.023 / 0.006).
- LDA: **pioneer non-mutans streptococci (oral commensals) enriched in MCOI (LDA score 3.06)**; cariogenic mutans streptococci seldom detected in either group.

**Microbiome (shotgun metagenome)**
- MCOI: metabolic pathways (amino-acid synthesis, energy metabolism, nucleotide biosynthesis) increased almost exclusively in gram-positive bacteria (non-mutans streptococci, Actinomyces).
- **Cariogenic/acidogenic pathways reduced** — fermentation pathways present but no high-acidogenicity species detected; multiple biosynthetic pathways reduced in gram-negative bacteria.

**Key caveats**: 3-day no-brushing model (artificial, short, ethically capped), small pilot (n=29), healthy adults only, and a single specific commercial device (COMORAL) — the irrigator did not clear attached supragingival plaque, so the benefit is on gingival inflammation and the microbiome rather than total plaque removal.
