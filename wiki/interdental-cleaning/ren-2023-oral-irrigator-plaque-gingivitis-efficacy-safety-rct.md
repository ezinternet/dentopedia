## One-line Summary

12-week single-blind parallel RCT (n=90 gingivitis patients; FAS 88) — adding a WaterPik oral irrigator (10–100 psi, adjustable) to manual toothbrushing significantly outperformed toothbrushing alone on gingival inflammation (MGI/BI/BOP% from 4 weeks; all p<0.001 by 8–12 weeks) and on plaque (T-QH significant from 8 weeks), with no serious adverse events and no increase in pain or dentin hypersensitivity.

## 한줄요약

12주 단일맹검 평행 RCT(치은염 환자 90명; FAS 88) — 수동 칫솔질에 WaterPik 구강세정기(Oral Irrigator, 10–100 psi 조절형)를 더하면 칫솔질 단독보다 치은염(MGI/BI/BOP%, 4주부터·8–12주 모두 p<0.001)과 치태(T-QH, 8주부터 유의)를 유의하게 개선했고, 중대 이상반응·통증·상아질과민증 증가는 없었다.

## Summary

This is a 12-week, single-blind, parallel-group RCT evaluating an oral irrigator (OI / water flosser) as an adjunct to manual toothbrushing in **gingivitis patients drawn from a general population** — explicitly excluding periodontitis, gingival recession, smokers, and orthodontic-appliance wearers. Its value to the wiki is twofold: (1) it supplies general-population (non-orthodontic, non-periodontitis) OI efficacy data that most water-flosser RCTs lack, and (2) it treats **safety as a co-primary endpoint**, being the first OI trial to formally measure self-reported pain and dentin hypersensitivity (VAS) alongside gingival recession and adverse-event monitoring.

Bottom line: the OI adjunct significantly improved gingival inflammation indices from 4 weeks onward and plaque from 8 weeks onward, with a pressure dose-response on bleeding-on-probing reduction, and with no serious safety hazards.

## Key Contributions

- General-population gingivitis efficacy: OI + toothbrush > toothbrush alone on plaque and gingival inflammation over 12 weeks.
- Co-primary safety: VAS pain/dentin-hypersensitivity (first for OIs), gingival recession, and adverse-event surveillance — all reassuring.
- Dose-response: higher tolerable irrigation pressure correlated with greater BOP% reduction (rho=0.330, p=0.027).
- Longer-than-usual 12-week follow-up with both ITT (FAS, prioritized) and per-protocol analyses.

## Methodology

- **Design**: randomized, single-blind, parallel-group RCT, 12 weeks; West China Hospital of Stomatology (ChiCTR2100054254). Central computer randomization by an independent third party; investigators/examiners blinded (participants not blinded → single-blind).
- **Participants**: 298 screened → 90 enrolled (1:1), gingivitis (≥20 BOP sites and T-QH >1.5 at baseline), age 18–65, ≥20 permanent teeth. Excluded periodontitis, gingival recession, smokers, regular adjunct-tool users, orthodontic appliances, relevant systemic disease/medications. Power: 76 needed (10% MGI difference, 5% alpha, 80% power), 90 recruited for ~20% attrition.
- **Groups / comparator**: Test = toothbrush + OI (WaterPik ION Professional Cordless Water Flosser, GT17; 10 pressure settings 10–100 psi), used ~90 s twice daily before brushing at the most comfortable pressure ≥ level 4. Control = identical toothbrush + toothpaste alone. Both: modified Bass brushing twice daily; reinforced at each visit.
- **Duration / visits**: baseline, 4, 8, 12 weeks (±3 days). 12 h oral-hygiene refrain and 2 h fast before each exam.
- **Outcome indices**: T-QH plaque index (disclosing agent, 4 sites/tooth); MGI (4 sites/tooth); BI (0–5 scale); BOP% (6 sites/tooth, CPI probe). Primary = gingivitis indices at 12 weeks; secondary = plaque indices, 4-week indices, safety.
- **Safety measures**: adverse events via electronic diary + clinical exam; gingival recession (apical margin migration); pain and dentin hypersensitivity by electronic VAS; soft/hard-tissue trauma and allergic/mucosal lesions across all intraoral sites.
- **Analysis**: FAS (ITT-based, LOCF imputation), PPS (completers), SS (safety); FAS prioritized over PPS when results conflicted. Compliance = ≥2×/day on >80% of days, electronic diary.

## Results

- **Disposition**: 2 control dropouts post-baseline → FAS n=88 (test 45 / control 43); PPS n=71 (80.7%; test 33 / control 38). Baseline demographics and indices well-balanced (mean age ~30.5 y; no baseline index difference, p>0.05).
- **Gingival inflammation (FAS, test vs control)**:
  - 4 weeks: MGI **p=0.017**, BI **p=0.001**, BOP% **p=0.001** favoring OI; T-QH not yet significant (p=0.102).
  - 8 & 12 weeks: MGI, BI, BOP% all **p<0.001** favoring OI.
- **Plaque (T-QH, FAS)**: significantly lower in test at 8 weeks (**p=0.033**) and 12 weeks (**p=0.006**); not at 4 weeks. T-QH trended up in both groups after 4 weeks; control T-QH rose significantly at 8–12 weeks (p=0.004) while test was stable — consistent with the known mixed evidence that OIs help gingival indices more reliably than visible plaque.
- **Reduction-percentage**: greater reductions in test for BI and BOP% over baseline–4 weeks (both p<0.001), and MGI and BOP% over 4–8 weeks (p=0.007, p<0.001); 8–12 week interval reductions similar between groups.
- **Pressure dose-response**: mean used pressure 52.13 ± 11.04 psi (range 38.55–82.35); positively correlated with BOP% reduction (rho=0.330, p=0.027); no significant correlation with T-QH/MGI/BI reduction.
- **Safety**: 37 adverse events, **none severe**. Transient gingival bleeding possibly OI-associated in 3 test participants (6.67%, 3/45) — gingival bleeding is itself a gingivitis symptom. Overall AE rates similar (test 18/45 = 40.0%; control 19/43 = 44.2%; **p=0.829**). VAS pain and dentin hypersensitivity near 0 in both groups with no between-group difference (p>0.05). **No gingival recession change, no OI-related soft/hard-tissue trauma.** OI compliance high (median 98.81% of days; 25th percentile 96.45%).
- **Conclusion**: as a toothbrushing adjunct, the OI was significantly more effective for plaque and gingival health, with efficacy positively correlated with irrigation pressure and baseline plaque, and with no substantial safety hazards over 12 weeks.
