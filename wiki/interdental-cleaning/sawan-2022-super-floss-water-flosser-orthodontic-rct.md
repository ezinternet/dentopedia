## One-line Summary

Single-blind split-mouth RCT (n=34 fixed-orthodontic patients) — Super Floss and a Waterpik water flosser were equally effective at immediate plaque removal (both RMNPI ~0.6→0.13, between-group p=0.951), with the water flosser marginally better only at the distal molar interproximal surface (MD −0.21, 95% CI −0.37 to −0.04, p=0.033).

## Summary

Fixed orthodontic appliances trap plaque around brackets, bands, and archwires, making interproximal cleaning hard and raising the risk of white-spot lesions, gingivitis, and decay. This single-blind, split-mouth RCT compared two patient-administered interproximal aids — **Super Floss** and a **Waterpik water flosser** — in 34 young-adult orthodontic patients near the end of treatment. Each patient received Super Floss on one randomly assigned side and the water flosser on the other, then plaque was scored with the Rustogi modified Navy Plaque Index (RMNPI) immediately before and after a single cleaning session.

Both aids produced a large, significant drop in proximal plaque (roughly 4-fold, p<0.001) and were **statistically indistinguishable overall** (between-group p=0.951). The only significant divergence was at the **distal interproximal surface of the molar** — a site behind the appliance that threaded floss reaches poorly — where the water flosser removed significantly more plaque. The clinical bottom line: for immediate plaque removal in orthodontic patients, Super Floss and a water flosser are **essentially interchangeable**, both safe and efficient, with the water flosser holding a small edge at the hard-to-reach distal molar.

## Key Contributions

- Direct **head-to-head split-mouth RCT** of Super Floss vs a Waterpik water flosser in fixed-orthodontic patients — each subject is their own control, removing inter-patient variability.
- **Equivalence** of the two aids for overall immediate plaque removal (between-group p=0.951; both ~0.6→0.13 RMNPI, p<0.001 within-group).
- A **site-specific exception**: the water flosser is significantly more effective at the **distal molar interproximal** surface (MD −0.21, 95% CI −0.37 to −0.04, p=0.033), the region least accessible to threaded floss behind brackets/wires.
- Practical reassurance that either aid is a defensible recommendation for orthodontic interproximal cleaning, letting patient preference/dexterity drive the choice.

## Methodology

- **Design**: single-blind, randomized, controlled, parallel clinical trial with **split-mouth** protocol (CONSORT-followed); registered ISRCTN83875016.
- **Participants**: n=34 (62 screened), equal male/female, mean age 23.7 ± 7.7 y, aged 18–35, near the end of fixed orthodontic treatment (braces right first molar to left first molar), pocket depth ≤3 mm, no flossing in the prior 24 h. Excluded: systemic disease, craniofacial anomaly, periodontal problems, spacing/missing teeth in the examined arch, smokers. Sample size by GPower (effect size 1, α=5%, power 80%) → minimum 34.
- **Intervention (single visit)**: baseline RMNPI (WHO probe) → standardized oral-hygiene instruction (modified Bass, soft brush, fluoridated toothpaste) → 2 min brushing + 2 min interproximal cleaning. **Super Floss (Oral-B)** on one randomly assigned side, **Waterpik Cordless Freedom water flosser** on the other (tip 90° at gingival margin, 45–75 PSI, 8 oz/min, 1200 pulses/min). Plaque re-scored **immediately after** cleaning.
- **Outcome**: RMNPI on proximal areas (sections A, D, C, F) at canine, one premolar, one molar per side; sections G/H omitted due to brackets. Examiners blinded to floss type per side; calibration Cohen's kappa 0.8.
- **Analysis**: SPSS v22; normality tests; paired t-tests within and between aids; p<0.05.
- **Key limitation**: only **immediate post-cleaning plaque** at a single visit was measured — no longitudinal gingival or bleeding outcome.

## Results

- **Overall (both p<0.001)**: Super Floss 0.56 ± 0.35 → 0.13 ± 0.26; Water flosser 0.61 ± 0.35 → 0.13 ± 0.28.
- **Between-group**: no significant difference in plaque reduction (**p=0.951**); balanced baseline (preintervention p=0.379).
- **By tooth**: both aids significantly reduced plaque at canine, premolar, molar (p<0.001); mean reductions trended higher for the water flosser at every tooth but not significantly.
- **Surface exception**: comparable on mesial/distal surfaces of all teeth **except the distal molar**, where the water flosser was significantly better (**MD −0.21, 95% CI −0.37 to −0.04, p=0.033**).
- **Split-mouth (side)**: both interventions significantly reduced plaque on left and right sides with no between-group difference.

> **What is Super Floss?** Oral-B Super Floss is a pre-cut floss with three integrated segments: a **stiffened threader end** (to thread floss under orthodontic archwires, bridge pontics, or implant prostheses), a **spongy/fuzzy middle** (cleans wide embrasures and around brackets), and a **regular floss end** (removes subgingival-margin plaque). It is purpose-built for fixed appliances and prostheses, which is why it is a natural comparator to a water flosser in orthodontic patients.

**Limitations to weigh**: single-visit immediate measurement only (no longitudinal gingival/bleeding endpoint), small single-center sample (n=34), and a possible Hawthorne effect (observed patients clean better than usual). These cap the strength of the equivalence claim to the short-term plaque-removal outcome.
