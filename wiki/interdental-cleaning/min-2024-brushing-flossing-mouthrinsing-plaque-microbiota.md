## One-line Summary

12-week parallel RCT (n=256 gingivitis subjects, 5 arms) using quantitative shotgun metagenomics showed brushing+flossing had no additional effect on supragingival plaque microbiota vs. brushing alone, while essential-oil mouthrinses (with/without alcohol) significantly reduced microbial diversity, species richness, and total bacterial abundance; flossing+rinsing showed subgingival synergy not seen with either alone.

## 한줄요약

12주 평행군 RCT (치은염 256명, 5군)에서 샷건 메타게놈 정량분석 결과, 치솔질+치실(BF)은 치솔질 단독(B) 대비 치은연상 플라크 미생물군집(Supragingival Plaque Microbiome)에 유의한 차이 없었으나, 에센셜오일 가글(BA·BZ·BFZ)은 다양성(Shannon-Weaver)·종 풍부도·총 세균량을 유의하게 감소시켰고, 치실+가글 병용(BFZ)만이 치은연하(Subgingival)에서 상승효과(Synergy)를 나타냈다.

## Summary

This 12-week RCT (n=288 analyzed; 258 randomized gingivitis subjects) compared five oral hygiene regimens — brushing only (B), brushing+flossing (BF), brushing+Listerine Antiseptic (BA), brushing+Listerine Zero (BZ), and brushing+flossing+Listerine Zero (BFZ) — using quantitative shotgun metagenomic sequencing with spiked-DNA standards (CMU quantification) and viability PCR (vPCR). The key finding was that flossing added no measurable antimicrobial effect at the supragingival level — Shannon-Weaver diversity, species richness, and total bacterial abundance were statistically indistinguishable between B and BF. In contrast, all three mouthrinse-containing regimens showed significant reductions in these metrics at 4 and 12 weeks, with alcohol-containing BA demonstrating the greatest reductions (75–93% for gingivitis-associated species vs. BF). For subgingival plaque measured by vPCR at 12 weeks, flossing alone showed selective activity against *F. nucleatum* only, but the combined BFZ regimen demonstrated significant synergistic reductions in total bacteria, *P. gingivalis*, and *F. nucleatum*. The authors propose a mechanistic model in which EO mouthrinses exert rapid bactericidal action, permit attenuated commensal-enriched regrowth, and prevent pathogen accumulation through twice-daily use. **Industry COI is prominent**: four of seven authors are Johnson & Johnson employees/consultants, and J&J produced the test mouthrinses.

## Key Contributions

- First quantitative absolute-abundance metagenomics RCT for oral hygiene comparison (CMU via spiked-DNA standard curves vs. conventional relative-abundance 16S)
- Established microbiome-level evidence that flossing alone does not reduce supragingival plaque microbiota vs. brushing alone
- Demonstrated EO mouthrinses (alcohol and alcohol-free) significantly reduce plaque microbial diversity and biomass — effect sustained at 12 weeks
- Alcohol-containing BA > alcohol-free BZ/BFZ for supragingival bacterial load reduction
- Identified subgingival synergy of combined flossing+rinsing (BFZ): only regimen significantly reducing total subgingival bacteria and periopathogens
- Proposed three-phase mechanistic model: rapid bactericidal kill → commensal-enriched re-seeding → repeated use prevents pathogen accumulation (eubiosis maintenance)
- 574 total species identified at species level; classified into commensal (158), gingivitis (67), malodor (30), acidogenic (21), unknown (109), transient (228)

## Methodology

**Design**: Examiner-blind, randomized, parallel-group, single-center RCT (Salus Research, Inc., Fort Wayne, Indiana; April–July 2022)

**Participants**: 300 adults enrolled; 288 analyzed (258 gingivitis randomized + 30 periodontally healthy reference). Mean age 43.5y; 78.6% female; 88.2% Caucasian; 97.5% non-smokers.

**Inclusion**: ≥20 natural teeth; gingivitis subjects with MGI evidence of gingivitis, ≥10% EBI bleeding sites, PPD ≤5 mm.

**Groups** (gingivitis arm, n~50–53 each):
| Group | Regimen |
|---|---|
| B | Brushing ×2/day × 1 min (control) |
| BF | Brushing + flossing ×1/day |
| BA | Brushing + Listerine Cool Mint Antiseptic ×2/day × 30 s (alcohol EO) |
| BZ | Brushing + Listerine Cool Mint Zero ×2/day × 30 s (alcohol-free EO) |
| BFZ | Brushing + flossing + Listerine Cool Mint Zero ×2/day × 30 s |

**Microbiome assessment** (supragingival plaque, teeth #3, 7, 18, 23):
- Shotgun metagenomics at baseline, 4 wk, 12 wk
- ZymoBIOMICS Spike-in Control II → Illumina HiSeq (3–4M paired-end 2×150 bp reads) → CMU quantification
- α-diversity: Shannon-Weaver Diversity Index + observed species richness
- β-diversity: Weighted UniFrac PCoA + PERMANOVA

**vPCR** (subgingival plaque, week 12 only): PMAxx™ dye viability exclusion → SYBR GREEN qPCR (total bacteria, *P. gingivalis*, *F. nucleatum*, *F. periodonticum*)

**Statistics**: Mixed effects model for repeated measures (MMRM), pairwise comparisons at α=0.05 two-sided.

## Results

### Flossing effect (BF vs. B) — supragingival
- Shannon-Weaver diversity: **ns** (4 wk and 12 wk)
- Species richness: **ns**
- Total bacteria abundance: **ns**
- β-diversity: **ns**
- All bacterial groups (commensal, gingivitis, malodor, acidogenic): **ns**
- Only 11 commensal species marginally increased in BF; TPI plaque index ns at 4 wk (p=0.696) and 12 wk (p=0.164)

### Mouthrinse effect (BA, BZ, BFZ vs. B and BF) — supragingival

All rinsing groups showed significant reductions at 4 and 12 weeks:

| Comparison | Group | Commensal reduction | Gingivitis-associated reduction | Malodor reduction |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| vs. BF (4 wk / 12 wk) | BA | 82.0% / 75.4% | 93.6% / 91.3% | 88.5% / 85.2% |
| vs. BF (4 wk / 12 wk) | BZ | 58.2% / 46.6% | 85.8% / 80.2% | 68.5% / ns |
| vs. BF (4 wk / 12 wk) | BFZ | 52.6% / ns | 84.5% / 75.9% | 60.7% / ns |

- BA showed greater reductions than BZ/BFZ; BZ and BFZ showed similar reductions
- No significant effect against acidogenic bacteria (low representation due to active-caries exclusion)
- Microbial ecology remained eubiotic (commensals ≫ pathogens in all rinsing groups): ~0.3–1.1 × 10³ commensal CMU vs. ~1.5–3.8 × 10² gingivitis/malodor CMU after rinsing

### Subgingival vPCR (week 12)
- **BF alone**: no effect on total bacteria; significant reduction of *F. nucleatum* only
- **BA and BZ alone**: no significant subgingival effect
- **BFZ (synergy)**: significant reductions in total bacteria, *P. gingivalis*, and *F. nucleatum*
- Clinical scores (EBI, MGI) for BFZ showed the largest gingival bleeding/inflammation reductions, consistent with subgingival synergy

### Healthy vs. gingivitis (baseline, n=30 vs. n=258)
- α-diversity and β-diversity: no significant difference
- Total bacteria: gingivitis subjects had 44% higher abundance (log₁₀ Δ=0.25, p=0.012)
- 36 species significantly more abundant in gingivitis (*Leptotrichia* spp., *Fusobacterium nucleatum*, *Prevotella* spp., *Streptococcus* spp., *Veillonella* spp.)
