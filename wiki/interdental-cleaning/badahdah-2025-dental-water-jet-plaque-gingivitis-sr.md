## One-line Summary

Systematic review + meta-analysis (18 RCTs; 15 in MA; n≈1005) of a water jet (WJ) added to manual brushing vs floss or brushing-alone, in BOTH regular and orthodontic patients: WJ gives slight, time- and population-dependent bleeding (moderate certainty) and gingival benefits, no consistent plaque advantage; benefits are larger vs brushing-alone than vs floss and in orthodontic patients.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰+메타분석(RCT 18편, MA 15편, n≈1005) — 물제트(Water Jet, WJ)를 칫솔질에 추가했을 때, 일반·교정환자 모두에서 출혈(중등도 근거)과 치은지수에 시기·인구집단에 따라 약간의 이득은 있으나 치면세균막 감소엔 일관된 우위 없음. 이득은 치실보다 '칫솔질 단독' 대비, 일반환자보다 교정환자에서 더 뚜렷하며, WJ는 칫솔질을 대체하지 못하는 보조수단.

## Summary

Badahdah 2025 (Healthcare, PROSPERO CRD42022296752, PRISMA) is the broadest WJ review the wiki holds: it pools **both regular and fixed-orthodontic patients** in a single 2×2 design — WJ vs floss and WJ vs brushing-alone, each split into the two populations — and further stratifies every outcome by short (2–3 wk), intermediate (4–6 wk), and long (>6 wk) follow-up. From 6494 records it included **18 RCTs** (15 meta-analyzed, n≈1005), graded with Cochrane ROB 2 and GRADE.

The calibrated bottom line: WJ confers **slight benefits in bleeding index (BI) and gingival index (GI)**, but **no consistent plaque (PI) advantage**. Benefits are (1) larger when WJ is compared to **brushing-alone** than to floss, and (2) larger in **orthodontic** patients than regular patients. BI is the only outcome reaching moderate GRADE certainty; PI and GI are low to very low. The authors recommend WJ as an easy-to-use adjunct — particularly for orthodontic brackets, fixed prostheses, or poor flossing compliance — explicitly **not** a brushing substitute.

This page sits at a useful tension/agreement junction. It **disputes** [[mohapatra-2024-water-flosser-vs-floss-plaque-sr]]'s claim that WJ beats floss on plaque, attributing that result to four of six Mohapatra studies lacking any follow-up (before/after-cleaning assessment) — Badahdah required ≥2-week follow-up and found PI parity with floss. It **extends** the ortho-only [[yiamwattana-2025-oral-irrigator-vs-floss-orthodontic-sr-ma]] by adding the regular-patient arm and a brushing-alone comparator; both reviews agree there is no strong floss-beating plaque effect in orthodontics (Badahdah's Group B PI was NS at 4 wk).

## Key Contributions

- **Single analysis spanning regular + orthodontic populations**, the broadest scope among the wiki's WJ reviews.
- **Four comparison cells**: A = WJ vs floss/regular (4 studies), B = WJ vs floss/ortho (2), C = WJ vs MTB-alone/regular (9), D = WJ vs MTB-alone/ortho (5).
- **Time-stratified** (short/intermediate/long) subgroups showing benefits concentrate in the short-to-intermediate window and fade long-term.
- **GRADE-honest messaging**: BI moderate; PI/GI low–very low.
- **Explicit reconciliation of conflicting prior MAs** (disputes Mohapatra on PI; concordant with Kotsakis, Worthington, Almoharib).

## Methodology

- **Databases**: MEDLINE (PubMed), Cochrane Library, Google Scholar; 1990–July 2022; manual reference screening.
- **Included studies**: 18 RCTs (15 in meta-analysis), mostly parallel design (1 crossover, 2 split-mouth); samples 20–117; **n≈1005**.
- **Populations**: regular patients (ages 18–75) AND fixed-orthodontic-appliance patients (ages 11–48, including children).
- **Comparators**: WJ + manual toothbrushing (MTB) vs (a) floss + MTB or (b) MTB alone.
- **Outcomes**: plaque index (PI, Silness & Löe), bleeding index (BI / BoP), gingival index (GI, Löe & Silness); short (2–3 wk) / intermediate (4–6 wk) / long (>6 wk).
- **Exclusions**: peri-implantitis, impaired dexterity / uncontrolled systemic disease, in-office or medicated irrigation, powered brushes, powered non-WJ interdental devices, non-floss interdental aids in control.
- **Synthesis**: mean difference (MD) with 95% CI in RevMan 5.4; fixed/random-effects by I²; Egger/Begg bias tests; sensitivity analysis; ROB 2 + GRADE.

## Results

**Plaque Index (PI) — no consistent WJ advantage**
- vs floss, regular (A): NS at 2–3 wk (MD −0.04, p=0.92) and 4–6 wk (MD −0.29 [−0.65, 0.07], p=0.1; low).
- vs floss, ortho (B): WJ slightly better at 2 wk (MD −0.62 [−0.74, −0.50], p<0.00001, 1 trial); NS at 4 wk (MD −0.58, p=0.35; low).
- vs MTB-alone, regular (C): very slight WJ benefit at 2–3 wk, but **brushing-alone superior** at 4 wk (MD 0.90, p<0.00001) and 2–3 mo (MD 0.08, p=0.04) → inconsistent.
- vs MTB-alone, ortho (D): **WJ superior at 4 wk** (MD −0.36 [−0.67, −0.04], p=0.03, 5 trials); NS at 2–3 mo (MD −0.05, p=0.24; very low).

**Bleeding Index (BI) — strongest signal, moderate certainty**
- vs floss, regular (A): WJ slightly better at 4–6 wk (MD −0.12 [−0.13, −0.10], p<0.00001, 3 trials); NS at 2–3 wk (p=0.75).
- vs floss, ortho (B): WJ better at 2 wk (MD −0.21 [−0.24, −0.18], p<0.00001); NS at 4 wk (MD −0.11, p=0.31).
- vs MTB-alone, regular (C): WJ better at 2 wk (MD −0.16, p<0.00001); equal at 1 mo (MD 0.00, p=1.00).
- vs MTB-alone, ortho (D): WJ superior at 1 mo (MD −0.67 [−0.77, −0.57]) and 2 mo (MD −0.19, p<0.00001; 1 trial).

**Gingival Index (GI) — comparable to floss, slight edge over brushing-alone**
- vs floss, regular (A): no difference at 2–3 wk (MD −0.01, p=0.95) or 4–6 wk (MD −0.14, p=0.63; very low).
- vs MTB-alone, regular (C): minimal WJ improvement at 4 wk (MD −0.06 [−0.10, −0.02], p=0.004; moderate); NS at 2–3 mo (p=0.11).
- vs MTB-alone, ortho (D): little to no difference at 4–6 wk (MD −0.06) and NS long-term (MD −0.12, p=0.11; very low). No Group-B GI data reported.

**Synthesis**: WJ benefits are real but slight, concentrated short/intermediate-term, larger vs brushing-alone than vs floss, and larger in orthodontic patients. Plaque parity (not superiority) vs floss. Recommended as a compliance-friendly adjunct, never a brushing replacement.
