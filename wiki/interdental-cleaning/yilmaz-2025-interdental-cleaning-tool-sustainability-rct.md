## One-line Summary

Examiner-blinded RCT (n=54 completers, 24 weeks, periodontally healthy adults): rubber interdental picks were used regularly (≥3 days/week) for a mean 12.61/24 weeks vs 4.96/24 weeks for dental floss (p=0.003); between-group plaque/bleeding were similar but both negatively correlated with the number of weeks of regular use — adherence, not intrinsic efficacy, separated the tools.

## Summary

This is the **first RCT to compare the long-term sustainability of regular use** (adherence/consistency over 24 weeks) of two common interdental cleaning tools — **rubber interdental picks** vs **dental floss** — in periodontally healthy adults, framed as primary prevention.

**Important terminology note**: "sustainability" here means **how consistently the tool keeps being used over time** (number of weeks of regular use), NOT environmental/ecological sustainability. A separate literature (e.g. Abed 2022) covers the environmental footprint of cleaning aids; this paper is about adherence.

Prior interdental-cleaning RCTs run only 2–4 weeks and found no plaque-removal superiority of one tool over another. Yılmaz et al. argue the real-world bottleneck is whether patients keep using the device, so they tracked use over 6 months with **deliberately infrequent recalls (only weeks 8 and 24)** to mimic real life and minimize the Hawthorne effect. Rubber picks were used regularly far longer than floss, and within both groups more weeks of regular use tracked with lower plaque and bleeding.

**Clinical bottom line**: rubber interdental picks were used **much more consistently than floss over 6 months** — adherence/sustainability of use favors picks **where the interdental space allows passage**. This supports the broader thesis that **adherence matters more than intrinsic device efficacy** for interdental-cleaning outcomes; the easiest tool to keep using wins.

## Key Contributions

- First head-to-head RCT on **adherence/sustainability** (not short-term efficacy) of rubber picks vs floss in periodontally healthy adults.
- Quantifies a large adherence gap favoring picks: **12.61 vs 4.96 of 24 weeks** of regular use (p=0.003).
- Shows a **dose–response of adherence**: more weeks of regular use → lower full-mouth plaque and bleeding (negative correlation, p<0.05) in both arms.
- Real-world design with **infrequent recalls (weeks 8, 24 only)** to suppress the Hawthorne effect, plus return of used-tool waste to corroborate self-reported calendars.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Randomized (coin toss), parallel two-arm, examiner-blinded RCT; 24-week follow-up; single center (Ege University, Turkey).
- **Participants**: 60 enrolled → **54 completers** (28 rubber pick, 26 floss; 5 no-shows, 1 excluded for pregnancy). Periodontally healthy adults 18–70, ≥20 teeth, manual-toothbrush users, baseline full-mouth plaque & bleeding <15%, with interdental spaces large enough for both floss and pick. **Dentists/dental students excluded.** Power: 80%, large effect (d=0.8), min 52.
- **Arms**: GUM rubber interdental pick (small) vs GUM dental floss; both told to clean interdentally ≥1×/day, brush twice daily (Modified Bass, fluoride paste); one standardized 10-min instruction.
- **Adherence measure**: per-day **calendar** (4 dentition zones) + return of used-tool waste at recall. **Regular use = ≥3 days/week**; **sustainability = number of weeks of regular use**.
- **Outcomes**: Primary = weeks of regular/sustainable use over 24 wk. Secondary = full-mouth dichotomous plaque index (PI) and bleeding on probing (BOP) %, single calibrated blinded examiner (kappa >0.85), disclosing tablet, Williams probe.
- **Statistics**: nonparametric (Shapiro–Wilk rejected normality) — Brunner–Langer F1-LD-F1/LD-F1 for repeated measures with Bonferroni, Mann–Whitney, chi-square/Fisher, Spearman; α=0.05 two-sided.

## Results

- **Sustainability / regular use (≥3 days/week) — primary outcome**:
  - **24 weeks**: rubber pick **12.61/24 weeks** vs floss **4.96/24 weeks**, **p=0.003** (picks used regularly >2.5× as long).
  - **8 weeks**: rubber pick 5.14/8 vs floss 3.19/8, p=0.037.
  - Both tools fluctuated week-to-week, but the count of participants using regularly stayed higher in the pick group throughout.
- **Baseline imbalance**: rubber-pick group significantly **older** (43.54 vs 34.15 yr, p=0.044) — an uncontrolled confounder (picks may suit lower manual dexterity in older patients). Sex, smoking, baseline PI/BOP similar.
- **Plaque / bleeding (PI, BOP)**:
  - **Similar between groups** at every timepoint (numerically minimal changes; healthy-mouth ceiling/floor effect).
  - **Rubber pick**: BOP fell significantly T0→T8 (p<0.001), no significant T0→T24 change — healthy status maintained.
  - **Floss**: BOP rose significantly T8→T24 and T0→T24 — interpreted as declining application quality (higher dexterity demand).
  - **Negative correlation (key finding)**: number of weeks of regular use was negatively correlated with both PI and BOP in both groups at both recalls (**p<0.05**) — more adherence, less plaque/bleeding.
- **Conclusion**: rubber interdental picks recommended for **superior sustainability** in periodontally healthy individuals, **provided the interdental space allows passage**.
