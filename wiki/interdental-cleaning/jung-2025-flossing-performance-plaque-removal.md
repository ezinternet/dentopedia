## One-line Summary

Prospective single-cohort pre/post intervention (n=37 young adults): video instruction raised flossing technique (FPS 2.0→2.83, p<.001) but plaque removal did not improve (PSPI difference 0.17 vs 0.21, p=.112) and was uncorrelated with technique — even correct flossing does not substantially reduce interdental plaque.

## Summary

A persistent defense of dental floss is that its weak trial record reflects poor *technique*, not the device itself — so teaching people to floss correctly should restore efficacy. This German single-cohort intervention (n=37 young, motivated university students) tested that claim directly by measuring flossing technique and plaque removal at four time points: habitual flossing, then again after a video instruction plus one week of home practice. A video-based Flossing Performance Score (FPS) captured technique; a Proximal Surface Plaque Index (PSPI) scored on aligned intraoral scans captured plaque.

Instruction worked on technique — FPS rose from 2.0 to 2.83 (p<.001), with large gains in correct floss adaptation and vertical movements, and flossing time grew from 60 to 89 seconds. But the cleaning result barely moved: plaque removal was 0.17 (habitual) vs 0.21 (instructed), p=.112, only about 3% more plaque cleared. Crucially, flossing performance was **not correlated** with plaque removed, and neither was time spent. Instead, the strongest predictor of post-floss plaque was simply how much plaque was present before. The authors conclude that even high-quality flossing does not substantially reduce interdental plaque, refuting the "inadequate-application" explanation and reinforcing prior evidence questioning floss efficacy.

## Key Contributions

- **Separates technique from outcome.** Directly tests whether better flossing technique → better plaque removal, and finds it does not.
- **Objective dual metrics.** Unobserved video scoring (FPS) plus aligned intraoral-scan plaque scoring (PSPI), avoiding the subjective dichotomous indices of older flossing studies.
- **Null-correlation result.** No FPS↔plaque-removal relationship and no duration↔plaque-removal relationship; pre-floss plaque predicted post-floss plaque.
- **Reframes the debate** from "instruct patients better" toward investigating alternative interdental approaches and the mechanics of biofilm removal.

## Methodology

- **Design**: prospective single-cohort pre/post intervention (no control arm), DRKS00034902. 37 students (30F/7M; 23.1 ± 3.2 y), no dropouts.
- **Three appointments**: T0 screen + suspend interdental hygiene 1 wk → T1 brush, disclose plaque, scan 1, videotape habitual flossing, scan 2, watch instruction video, practice 1 wk → T2 videotape instructed flossing, scan 3 (before) + scan 4 (after).
- **FPS** (technique, 0–3): per interdental space, dichotomous coding of space reached + correct adaptation (floss against mesial & distal surfaces) + correct movement (vertical); summed across the three fractions. 12 spaces around Ramfjord teeth (16, 21, 24, 36, 41, 44). Filmed behind a color-filtered mirror so stained plaque was invisible and didn't cue performance.
- **PSPI** (plaque, mean of 0/1/2 scores): disclosed plaque on aligned 3D intraoral scans (Carestream CS 3800, MeshLab); fixed ROI near each interdental space, 4 areas/tooth. Plaque removal = PSPI before − after.
- **Stats**: Wilcoxon (non-Gaussian data); linear regression for FPS↔plaque; median [95% CI], bootstrapped CIs, α=0.05. Powered on flossing improvement (effect size 0.48, power 0.80). Inter-rater κ: reached 0.846 / technique 0.663 / adaptation 0.486; plaque κ 0.679.

## Results

- **Technique improved (instruction worked).** FPS 2.0 [1.48; 2.54] → 2.83 [2.45; 2.95], p<.001. Adaptation 0.42 → 0.92 (p<.001); vertical movements 0.64 → 1.00 (p=.012). Flossing time 60.3 s → 89.2 s (p<.001), ~7.5 s/space. (Residual: 8 subjects never performed vertical movements; 5 adapted correctly in <half of spaces.)
- **Plaque removal did NOT improve.** Removal 0.17 [0.04; 0.25] habitual vs 0.21 [0.13; 0.25] instructed, **p=.112**. Pre-floss PSPI ~1.29–1.33 (no visit difference, p=.182). Habitual flossing reduced plaque ~13%, instructed ~16% — only ~3% more. Score-2 (≥50%) areas fell 46%→33%; only ~29% became plaque-free.
- **Site detail**: small but significant instruction benefit on buccal areas (0.08→0.17, p=.002), none on oral areas (0.17→0.17, p=.332); oral sites consistently had more plaque.
- **No correlation.** FPS not related to plaque removal or post-floss PSPI; duration not related (habitual R²=0.011, p=.544; instructed R²=0.001, p=.825). Pre-floss PSPI strongly predicted post-floss PSPI — the dominant determinant of how clean a surface ended up was how dirty it started, not how well it was flossed.
- **Bottom line**: correct flossing technique does not translate into effective interdental plaque removal; the limited efficacy of floss is not merely a matter of poor application.
