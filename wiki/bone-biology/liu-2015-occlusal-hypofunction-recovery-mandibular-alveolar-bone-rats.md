## One-line Summary

Rat micro-CT study (n=48 growing male SD rats; normal 24, hypofunction 12, recovery 12) showing an anterior bite-raising appliance reduces mandibular alveolar trabecular microarchitecture (↓BV/TV, ↓Tb.Th, ↓Tb.N; ↑Tb.Sp, ↑bone surface density, most marked at week 2), and that these changes reverse to normal after appliance removal and occlusal recovery.

## Summary

This in vivo rat experiment tests how loss of occlusal/masticatory loading reshapes the 3D microarchitecture of mandibular alveolar bone during growth — and whether the effect is reversible. An anterior bite-raising appliance was placed between the incisors of growing Sprague–Dawley rats to remove posterior occlusal contact (hypofunction). After two weeks the appliance was removed to study recovery over two further weeks. Micro-CT showed that hypofunction degraded trabecular bone (lower bone volume fraction, thinner and fewer trabeculae, wider trabecular separation, higher bone surface density), accompanied by masseter muscle atrophy and a higher/narrower alveolar process. Critically, restoring occlusion reversed both the muscular and the bony changes back to control levels, supporting a mechanically driven, reversible model of alveolar bone adaptation. (Extraction is abstract-only; numeric effect sizes were not recoverable.)

## Key Contributions

- Shows occlusal hypofunction drives measurable 3D trabecular degradation of growing-rat mandibular alveolar bone via micro-CT.
- Establishes **reversibility**: occlusal recovery restores both masseter weight and alveolar bone architecture to normal (P > 0.05 vs control).
- Links a masticatory-muscle outcome to an alveolar-bone microarchitecture outcome, framing occlusion as a mechanical regulator of alveolar bone quality.

## Methodology

- **Design**: randomized animal experiment; 48 growing male Sprague–Dawley rats → normal (n=24), hypofunction (n=12), recovery (n=12).
- **Hypofunction**: bite-raising appliance inserted between maxillary and mandibular incisors to reduce occlusal loading.
- **Recovery**: appliance removed at 2 weeks; experiment continued 2 more weeks.
- **Sacrifice**: animals killed weekly.
- **Outcomes**: body weight; masseter muscle weight; mandibular alveolar bone histomorphology and micro-CT trabecular indices (trabecular cross-sectional area, BV/TV, Tb.Th, Tb.N, Tb.Sp, bone surface density; alveolar process height/width).
- **Statistics**: P < 0.05 threshold.

## Results

- **Masseter**: lighter (atrophied) in hypofunction vs control (P < 0.05).
- **Alveolar process**: higher and narrower in hypofunction vs control (P < 0.05).
- **Trabecular microarchitecture (hypofunction, especially week 2, P < 0.05)**: ↓ trabecular cross-sectional area, looser trabeculae, ↓ BV/TV, ↓ Tb.Th, ↓ Tb.N, ↑ bone surface density, ↑ Tb.Sp.
- **Recovery**: after appliance removal, masseter weight and alveolar architecture gradually reversed and reached normal levels by end of experiment (P > 0.05 vs control).
- **Conclusion**: loss of occlusal stimuli → mandibular alveolar bone remodeling; restored occlusion → restored architecture in growing rats.
