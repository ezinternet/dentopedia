## One-line Summary

Qualitative systematic review (PRISMA, 15 clinical studies — RCTs/cohorts/case series, baseline ridge 2.5-4.5 mm) of transverse ridge expansion (TRE) with vs without bone grafting: both achieve predictable widening of 3.2-6.1 mm with implant survival 92-100%; grafting adds only a modest, clinically non-significant gain — so TRE alone is reliable for ridges ≥3 mm, while grafting is reserved for ridges <3 mm or when long-term volume preservation is the goal.

## Summary

Transverse ridge expansion (TRE) — controlled separation of the buccal and lingual/palatal cortical plates to widen a horizontally deficient ridge — leaves a biological space that can either heal spontaneously or be filled with graft material. Whether that graft is necessary has long been clinician-preference-driven. This PRISMA systematic review (PROSPERO CRD420251158137) screened 356 records (PubMed/Embase/Cochrane, 2000-2024), assessed 48 full texts, and included 15 human clinical studies directly comparing TRE with vs without adjunctive grafting.

The bottom line: both approaches widen the ridge predictably (3.2-6.1 mm) and carry implant survival of 92-100% with no significant between-group difference. Grafting buys a numerically larger horizontal gain and better volume stability, but the increment is modest and not clinically significant. The actionable rule is width-based: ridges ≥3 mm respond reliably to expansion alone, whereas grafting is a selective adjunct for ridges <3 mm or where long-term dimensional/volume preservation matters. No pooled meta-analysis was possible because of heterogeneity in technique, biomaterials, and follow-up — quality was moderate-to-low — so the threshold is evidence-supported but not high-certainty.

## Key Contributions

- Converts the "graft or not" decision from preference to a width threshold: **≥3 mm → expansion alone; <3 mm → graft** (also graft when long-term volume preservation is wanted).
- Quantifies the grafting benefit as small and clinically non-significant (grafted 4.5-6.1 mm vs non-grafted 3.2-5.0 mm horizontal gain; identical survival).
- Reframes grafting as a selective, case-dependent strategy rather than a routine adjunct to ridge expansion.
- Ranks adjuncts: piezosurgery lowers complications; xenografts/alloplasts outlast autograft dimensionally; biologics (PRGF, i-PRF) promising but under-evidenced.

## Methodology

- PRISMA 2020; PROSPERO CRD420251158137. PubMed + Embase + Cochrane, human, English, Jan 2000 - Dec 2024.
- PICO: horizontally deficient ridges needing implants; TRE + graft (intervention) vs TRE without graft (comparison); outcomes = horizontal bone gain, implant stability, survival, marginal bone loss, complications.
- Inclusion: RCTs, prospective/retrospective cohorts, case series ≥10 patients, follow-up ≥6 months. Exclusion: animal/in vitro, case reports <10, narrative reviews, opinion, no outcome data.
- Two reviewers, Cohen's kappa = 0.86. 356 → 48 full text → 15 included. Baseline ridge widths 2.5-4.5 mm; samples 20-120.
- Risk of bias: RoB-2 (RCTs), ROBINS-I (non-randomized). No quantitative synthesis (heterogeneity) — qualitative SR only, hence `confidence: sr`.
- Caveat: the paper's own study-design tally is internally inconsistent (Results: 5 RCTs/6 cohorts/4 case series; Risk-of-bias: 2 RCTs/6 cohorts/7 case series).

## Results

- **Horizontal bone gain:** 3.2-6.1 mm overall. Grafted 4.5-6.1 mm; non-grafted 3.2-5.0 mm. Difference modest, not clinically significant.
- **Implant survival:** 92-100%, no significant grafted-vs-non-grafted difference. Success (MBL <1.5 mm, no mobility, no peri-implant infection) comparable.
- **Primary stability (insertion torque/ISQ):** trend higher in grafted groups; not statistically significant in most studies.
- **Complications:** buccal plate fractures 4-10%; soft tissue dehiscence 3-8%; occasional autogenous graft resorption; non-grafted sites showed minor horizontal relapse <1 mm (grafted ridges more volume-stable). Overall complication rates similar.
- **Biomaterials:** xenografts (Bio-Oss) and alloplasts more dimensionally stable than autograft (autograft prone to partial resorption).
- **Technique:** piezosurgery consistently reduced complications and improved expansion control vs rotary burs/chisels.
- **Site:** TRE more predictable in the maxilla than mandible (lower cortical density, higher elasticity → fewer plate fractures).
- **Threshold:** ridges ≥3 mm → expansion alone predictable; <3 mm or long-term volume preservation → graft.
- Cited supporting figures: Waechter SR+MA 3.8 mm gain across 732 patients; Blus & Szmukler-Moncler 3.25 → 6.5 mm over 230 sites; Nkenke 6.1 mm autogenous block.
