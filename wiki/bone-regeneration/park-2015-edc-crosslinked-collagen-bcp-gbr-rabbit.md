## One-line Summary

Animal study (n=12 rabbits, 8-mm calvarial CSDs, 2 and 8 weeks) showing EDC-crosslinked collagen membrane (Rapigide) remains structurally intact at 8 weeks with good vascularization and tissue integration, and that membrane + BCP graft produces the greatest new bone and augmented area.

## Summary

Park et al. (Yonsei University College of Dentistry, Seoul; Choi SH senior author) evaluated a commercially available EDC-crosslinked type-I collagen membrane (Rapigide, Dalim Tissen, Korea) in 8-mm rabbit calvarial defects at 2 and 8 weeks. Twelve rabbits received four defects each, randomly assigned to: sham control, EDC membrane alone, BCP graft alone (MBCP™, 60% HA + 40% β-TCP), and BCP graft + EDC membrane. At 2 weeks, the EDC membrane showed close adaptation to defect margins with early vascularization and direct new bone apposition on the collagen matrix. Central collapse was noted in the membrane-only group, underscoring the need for a graft space-maintainer. At 8 weeks, the overall shape of the EDC membrane was still maintained, confirming sustained barrier function. Combined BCP + EDC membrane (B-EDC) produced the greatest total augmented area and new bone area. No significant foreign body reactions were observed. This study is the first reported evaluation of EDC-crosslinked collagen membrane for GBR in a calvarial defect model, providing proof-of-concept for EDC crosslinking as a biocompatible, sustained-barrier strategy.

## Key Contributions

- First reported rabbit calvarial study of an EDC-crosslinked collagen membrane for GBR
- Sustained barrier function confirmed at 8 weeks (overall shape maintained)
- Vascularization within the EDC membrane at 2 weeks indicates transmembraneous angiogenesis — favorable for healing
- Direct new bone apposition on collagen matrix demonstrates osteoconductive tissue integration
- Membrane collapse without graft support highlights critical dependency on graft as space-maintainer
- Hierarchy established: B-EDC > BG > EDC membrane alone > sham for new bone and augmented area

## Methodology

- **Model**: 8-mm diameter full-thickness calvarial defects, New Zealand White rabbits (male, 2.8–3.2 kg), n=12
- **Groups** (4 defects per rabbit, randomized): sham control, EDC membrane, BCP graft (MBCP™), BCP + EDC membrane (B-EDC)
- **EDC membrane**: Rapigide (Dalim Tissen, Korea); type-I collagen crosslinked in 5 mM EDC at 4°C for 24h; washed, re-lyophilized; cut 10×10 mm
- **BCP graft**: MBCP™ (Biomatlante, France); 60% HA + 40% β-TCP; 1–2 mm granules, 70% porosity
- **Sacrifice**: 2 weeks (n=6) and 8 weeks (n=6)
- **Assessment**: H&E histology (qualitative) + histometric analysis (new bone area, total augmented area)

## Results

- **2 weeks**: EDC membrane adapted closely to defect margins; vascularization present within membrane; direct bone apposition on collagen; membrane largely unresorbed; central collapse in membrane-only group; B-EDC and BG had greater augmented and new bone areas than EDC membrane alone
- **8 weeks**: EDC membrane overall shape maintained; sustained barrier function confirmed; B-EDC remained the highest-performing group
- **Biocompatibility**: No significant foreign body reaction; adequate host tissue integration
- **Key finding**: Central membrane collapse without graft → space maintenance requires graft support
- **Outcome rank**: B-EDC > BG > EDC alone > sham
