## One-line Summary

Randomized beagle dog study (n=9, 3 groups, 8-week OTM) showing BMP2-functionalized biomimetic calcium phosphate (BioCaP) yields 1.61× more new bone than empty control and 1.25× more than DBB during orthodontic tooth movement, without obstructing movement.

## Summary

Alveolar bone defects during orthodontic treatment — from extraction of neighboring teeth, surgical-orthodontic procedures, or severe periodontitis — pose a challenge because conventional bone grafts (like DBB) may impede tooth movement or cause excessive root resorption. This study tested BMP2-functionalized biomimetic calcium phosphate (BioCaP), a novel graft with sustained BMP2 release and 10-fold dose reduction vs. conventional rhBMP2.

Nine beagle dogs were assigned to control (no graft), DBB, or BioCaP groups. Maxillary first premolars were extracted, the alveolar defect was grafted, and the second premolar was immediately protracted into the defect over 8 weeks. BioCaP significantly outperformed both control and DBB on new bone formation, bone mineral density, and alveolar recession reduction, while matching control on root resorption and not slowing orthodontic tooth movement.

**Critical finding**: DBB caused significantly more root resorption than control or BioCaP, likely because rigid slow-resorbing DBB particles create localized stress concentration under orthodontic force. BioCaP's faster resorption allows progressive bone ingrowth, reducing this risk.

## Key Contributions

1. First large-animal study demonstrating BMP2-functionalized CaP graft efficacy specifically during orthodontic tooth movement
2. BioCaP achieves superior bone regeneration vs. DBB while causing less root resorption
3. OTM rate unaffected by BioCaP graft — clinically important safety finding
4. BioCaP anti-inflammatory effect (lower GCF IL-1β) — reduced peri-implant inflammation during bone remodeling under OTM force
5. Biomimetic coating strategy delivers sustained BMP2 at 10× lower dose than conventional rhBMP2 — reduces adverse effects of high-dose BMP2 (ectopic bone, seroma)

## Methodology

- **Animals**: 9 beagle dogs, 3 groups (n=3 each): Control / DBB / BioCaP
- **Defect model**: Maxillary first premolar extraction → alveolar surgical defect → graft → immediate OTM (second premolar protracted into defect site over 8 weeks)
- **BioCaP fabrication**: Biomimetic CaP granules (250–1000 μm) + BMP2 co-precipitated into CaP coating at 1 μg/mL (1 mL = 140 μg BMP2)
- **Outcomes**: OTM rate, histology/histomorphometry (new bone %), CBCT (bone mineral density), root resorption index, alveolar crest level, periodontal probing depth, GCF IL-1β (ELISA weeks 4 and 8)

## Results

| Outcome | BioCaP vs Control | BioCaP vs DBB |
|---|---|---|
| New bone formation % | 1.61× higher | 1.25× higher |
| Bone mineral density | 1.92× higher | 1.36× higher |
| Root resorption index | Similar (no excess) | 1.39× less |
| Alveolar recession (CEJ-crest) | 1.45× less | 1.69× less |
| OTM rate (8 weeks) | No significant difference | No significant difference |
| GCF IL-1β | Significantly lower | — |
| BioCaP resorption | — | 1.42× faster than DBB |

**DBB risk**: Root resorption in DBB group was 1.87× control and 1.39× BioCaP — clinically relevant risk in orthodontic settings where DBB particles persist under occlusal/orthodontic forces.
