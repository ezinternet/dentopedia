## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 20 RCTs / prospective controlled studies (1980–2014): collagen-membrane + particulate-graft lateral ridge GBR is effective; cross-linked membranes exposed ~30% more often than non-cross-linked, simultaneous and staged implant placement gave similar survival (favoring simultaneous placement when feasible), and membrane fixation + decortication added vertical/horizontal bone-gain benefit.

## Summary

This systematic review with meta-analysis evaluates guided bone regeneration (GBR) for **lateral alveolar ridge augmentation** using resorbable **collagen membranes combined with particulate graft materials**, restricted to RCTs and prospective controlled studies published 1980–2014. Twenty studies met inclusion. Primary outcomes were implant survival, membrane exposure, bone gain / defect reduction, and vertical bone loss at follow-up, with meta-analysis of four procedural moderators.

The key practical findings: implant survival was **similar for simultaneous vs staged (subsequent) implant placement**, so the authors recommend simultaneous placement when anatomy permits. **Cross-linked collagen membranes had ~30% higher exposure** than non-cross-linked membranes. **Anorganic bovine bone mineral (ABBM)** produced sufficient regenerated bone with high implant survival. **Membrane fixation** was weakly associated with more vertical bone gain, and **decortication** with more horizontal bone gain (defect depth reduction). Overall, collagen-membrane particulate-graft GBR is an effective lateral ridge augmentation technique, with fixation and decortication as potentially beneficial adjuncts.

## Key Contributions

- Controlled-evidence-only (RCT + prospective controlled) synthesis of collagen-membrane particulate GBR for lateral ridge augmentation.
- Quantifies the cross-linking trade-off: longer-lasting cross-linked membranes are exposed ~30% more.
- Supports **simultaneous** implant placement (survival equivalent to staged).
- Identifies **membrane fixation** (→ vertical gain) and **decortication** (→ horizontal gain) as beneficial adjuncts.

## Methodology

- Electronic + hand search, 1980–2014.
- Inclusion: RCTs and prospective controlled studies only.
- Outcomes: survival, membrane exposure, bone gain/defect reduction, vertical bone loss.
- Meta-analysis of cross-linking, implant timing, membrane fixation, decortication.
- 20 included studies.

## Results

- Simultaneous vs staged implant placement: similar implant survival.
- Cross-linked vs non-cross-linked membrane: ~30% higher exposure for cross-linked.
- ABBM: sufficient regenerated bone, high survival.
- Membrane fixation: weak association with increased vertical bone gain.
- Decortication: higher horizontal bone gain (defect depth).
