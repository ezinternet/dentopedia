## One-line Summary
In vivo study (J Periodontal Implant Sci 2017): n=7, DBBM + porcine collagen matrix socket preservation, 6-month histology — width resorption 1.21 mm, height resorption 0.46 mm; new bone 16%, residual xenograft 32%; uneventful healing throughout.

## Summary
Clinical + histological study (n=7, University of Milan) evaluating post-extraction socket preservation with demineralized bovine bone mineral (DBBM) and non-crosslinked porcine collagen matrix. Six-month outcomes showed limited dimensional loss and histomorphometric evidence of bone formation alongside residual xenograft.

## Key Contributions
- Horizontal resorption: 1.21mm (p=0.005)
- Vertical resorption: 0.46mm (p=0.004) — minimal loss
- Histomorphometry at 6 months:
  - Residual xenograft: 31.97±3.52%
  - New bone: 16.02±7.06%
  - Connective tissue: 50.67±8.42%
- Collagen matrix: resorbed physiologically → vascularized connective tissue
- All sockets: uneventful clinical healing
