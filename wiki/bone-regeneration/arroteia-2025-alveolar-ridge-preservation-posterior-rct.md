## Summary
4-arm RCT (UNICAMP/Buffalo, 2025) specifically focused on **posterior** extraction sockets — an underrepresented area in ARP literature. CBCT at 6 months shows that bone grafting (with or without titanium membrane) improves vertical preservation, but horizontal loss occurs in all groups. USH in posterior leads to significant sinus and implant-size consequences.

## Key Contributions
- First high-quality RCT focused on **posterior** sites (vs most ARP evidence from anterior/premolar)
- n=88 (22/group × 4 groups); UNICAMP + University at Buffalo
- TM+XG and MFGG+XG: significantly better **vertical** ridge preservation vs USH and MFGG alone
- **Horizontal reduction: significant in ALL groups** — no group avoids it
- USH (unassisted socket healing) consequences: 67% maxillary sinus elevation needed; 71% required short implants (<8.5mm)
- MFGG groups: significantly more postoperative pain vs USH
- Grafting reduces need for additional augmentation surgery

## Groups & Outcomes
| Group | Vertical | Horizontal | Pain | Sinus elevation |
|-------|---------|-----------|------|----------------|
| TM + XG | Best preservation ✓ | Significant loss | Moderate | Low |
| MFGG + XG | Better preservation ✓ | Significant loss | High | Low |
| MFGG alone | No better than USH | Significant loss | High | Moderate |
| USH | Worst | Significant loss | Low | 67% |

## Clinical Message
- Posterior ARP: grafting (xenograft ± titanium membrane) is worth it to avoid sinus surgery
- Horizontal loss cannot be prevented — inform patients of likely contouring need
- MFGG without graft: not recommended — adds pain without dimensional benefit
- Short implant use as proxy for inadequate ridge height after USH
