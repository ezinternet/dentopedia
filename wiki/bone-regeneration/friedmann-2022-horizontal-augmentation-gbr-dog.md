## One-line Summary

Randomized beagle dog study (5 dogs, 20 chronic mandibular defects, 4 membrane/graft combinations) showing DBBM + ribose cross-linked collagen membrane (unfixed) produces significantly more new bone than DBBM + native collagen membrane with 4-pin fixation.

## Summary

This randomized dog study compared 4 membrane/graft combinations for horizontal ridge augmentation in chronic mandibular defects. L-shaped alveolar defects were created bilaterally in 5 beagle dogs and allowed to heal 6 weeks to produce chronic-type defects, then treated with one of 4 protocols and assessed by histomorphometry at 3 months.

The key finding: prolonged-barrier cross-linked collagen membranes (RCLC; Ossix Plus) without mechanical pin fixation outperform the widely used native collagen membrane (NCM; Bio-Gide) with 4-pin fixation for new bone formation. This challenges the routine practice of membrane tacking as a mandatory step.

Additionally, calcium sulfate (CS) + RCLC showed the least residual non-bone graft area, suggesting faster material resorption and more complete bone replacement — though new bone formation was lower than DBBM+RCLC.

## Key Contributions

1. Cross-linked collagen membrane (prolonged degradation) + DBBM generates more new bone than native collagen membrane + DBBM, even without fixation pins
2. Membrane fixation (4-pin tacking) is not necessary when using cross-linked membranes with adequate intrinsic stability
3. Ca-sulfate graft resorbs more completely than DBBM — less residual non-bone material, potentially supporting faster bone ingrowth
4. All 4 protocols achieved uneventful healing — no membrane exposure reported, supporting the CAF technique

## Methodology

- **Animals**: 5 adult male beagles (≥12 months, 8.5–11.5 kg)
- **Defect**: Bilateral mandibular premolar extraction → L-shaped buccal defect (6–7 mm × 8–10 mm × 2.5–3 mm) → 6-week chronic healing
- **Treatment groups** (5 defects each, randomized):
  - a. DBBM (Bio-Oss) + native collagen membrane (Bio-Gide) + 4-pin fixation
  - b. DBBM + ribose cross-linked collagen membrane (Ossix Plus) — no fixation
  - c. DBBM + native porcine pericardium membrane (Smartbrane) — no fixation
  - d. Ca-sulfate (3DBond) + ribose cross-linked collagen membrane (Ossix Plus) — no fixation
- **Closure**: CAF (coronally advanced flap), tension-free, resorbable suture (Monocryl 5.0)
- **Assessment**: 3-month healing → histomorphometry (non-decalcified processing, toluidine blue, ImageJ analysis)
- **Statistics**: ANOVA with post hoc; medians + IQR
- Note: 2/5 specimens lost in group b due to processing error

## Results

**New bone area** (primary outcome):

| Comparison | Result | p |
|---|---|---|
| b (DBBM+RCLC) > a (DBBM+NCM+tacking) | Significantly greater | 0.001 |
| b > c (DBBM+NPPM) | Significantly greater | 0.002 |
| b > d (CS+RCLC) | Significantly greater | 0.046 |

**Residual non-bone graft area** (secondary):

| Comparison | Result | p |
|---|---|---|
| d (CS+RCLC) < a (DBBM+NCM) | Significantly less | 0.026 |
| d < c (DBBM+NPPM) | Significantly less | 0.021 |

**Clinical implication**: For horizontal augmentation, a cross-linked collagen membrane with DBBM may be preferable to the traditionally dominant Bio-Oss + Bio-Gide + tacking protocol. Membrane fixation (tacking) appears redundant with appropriate membrane stability.
