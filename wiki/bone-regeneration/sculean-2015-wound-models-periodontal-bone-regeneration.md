## Summary
Periodontol 2000 series review covering research models used in periodontal and bone regeneration research (in vitro cell culture, animal models, human trials). Discusses translational challenges and summarizes evidence for key regenerative modalities: GTR, EMD (enamel matrix derivative), PDGF, BMP-2, and scaffolds.

## Key Contributions
- Three-tier model hierarchy: in vitro → animal → human RCT
- Bundle bone/PDL-dependent bone: lost after extraction, not restored by implant alone
- GTR: best-supported by combined animal + human evidence
- EMD: well-supported; promotes periodontal regeneration via PDL progenitor cells
- BMP-2: effective for vertical ridge augmentation; dose/carrier (collagen sponge) issues remain
- PDGF (GEM 21S): RCT-supported for infrabony and furcation defects
- Translational gap: animal models (rat, rabbit, dog, swine) do not always predict human outcomes

## Research Models Overview
| Model | Strengths | Limitations |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Cell culture | Mechanism study, rapid | No vascularization, 3D context lacking |
| Rat/mouse calvaria | Cost-effective, transgenic possible | Non-load-bearing, thin bone |
| Rabbit calvaria | Larger defect, cartilaginous healing | Faster healing than humans |
| Beagle dog | Periodontal anatomy close to human | Cost, ethical constraints |
| Swine | Bone quality similar to human | High cost, limited labs |
| Human RCT | Definitive evidence | Ethical limits, cost, heterogeneity |
