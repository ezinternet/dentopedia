## One-line Summary

Three-arm RCT (n=50 completed, 180-day CBCT + histology + ISQ) showing DBBM-C (collagenated xenograft) with and without FGG socket seal maintained alveolar ridge volume better than spontaneous healing during 84–180 days; FGG seal additionally reduced horizontal resorption at crest (p=0.049); ISQ ≥70 in all groups.

## Summary

This single-blind, three-arm RCT evaluated DBBM-C (OCS-B Collagen / Straumann XenoFlex; deproteinized cancellous bovine bone mineral granules + 10% porcine collagen) for alveolar ridge preservation versus spontaneous healing, with or without free gingival graft (FGG) socket sealing. Fifty-four patients were randomized 1:1:1 to: (1) spontaneous healing, (2) DBBM-C open healing, (3) DBBM-C + FGG seal. Comprehensive assessment at 180 days included CBCT, 3D volumetric analysis, bone biopsy histology, and implant stability quotient (ISQ). Between 84 and 180 days, DBBM-C groups maintained volume at 3 mm below crest (+0.72% change) while controls lost volume (−2.10%, p=0.026). FGG sealing additionally reduced horizontal resorption at the crestal 1 mm level (p=0.049). All groups achieved ISQ ≥70, indicating adequate osseointegration regardless of ARP method.

## Key Contributions

- RCT of DBBM-C — collagen-containing composite xenograft — vs spontaneous healing with rigorous multimodal endpoints (CBCT + histology + 3D volumetrics + ISQ)
- Isolates the contribution of FGG socket sealing as an independent variable within the same graft material
- 84–180 day subperiod analysis reveals late-phase volume maintenance advantage of DBBM-C over spontaneous healing
- FGG seal beneficial specifically at the crestal 1 mm horizontal dimension — clinically important for esthetic implant positioning
- All groups achieved ISQ ≥70: ARP method does not significantly impair osseointegration
- Demonstrates clinical viability of open-healing DBBM-C without additional membrane

## Methodology

**Design**: Single-blind, parallel, 3-arm RCT (1:1:1 computer-generated randomization).

**Setting**: Department of Periodontology, Seoul National University Dental Hospital; June 2021 – July 2023; KCT0008266.

**Sample**: 54 enrolled (18 per group), 50 completed (4 dropouts: loss-to-follow-up and osseointegration failure).

**Inclusion**: 18–80 years, single tooth requiring extraction + future implant, >2-wall alveolar bone, healthy systemic status, plaque index and BOP ≤20%, ≥20 remaining teeth.

**Study groups**:
- **Control**: Spontaneous healing — extraction only, suturing
- **DBBM-C**: Collagenated xenograft (OCS-B Collagen / Straumann XenoFlex, NIBEC) placed in socket, open healing (no membrane/FGG), suturing
- **DBBM-C/FGG**: DBBM-C placed in socket + FGG from hard palate to seal socket orifice

**Graft material**: DBBM-C (cancellous bovine granules + 10% porcine collagen, mouldable block or syringe form); organic inflammatory components removed by manufacturing; structure resembles human bone.

**Outcome assessment at 180 days**:
- CBCT (Kodak CS 9300; 250 µm voxel, 80 kVP, 8 mA): immediately post-extraction, 84 days, 180 days
- 3D volumetric analysis: ridge contour superimposition
- Bone biopsy histology at implant placement (180 days)
- ISQ measurement at implant placement (resonance frequency analysis)

## Results

| Outcome | Control | DBBM-C | DBBM-C/FGG | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Volume Δ at 3 mm below crest (84–180 days) | −2.10% ± 8.37% | +6.05% ± 6.69% | maintained | 0.026 |
| Horizontal width at 1 mm below crest (pre-extraction to 84 days) | reference | reference | −73.67% ± 32.53% (−9.19 ± 5.09 mm) | 0.049 |
| ISQ at implant placement | ≥70 | ≥70 | ≥70 | NS |
| Primary CBCT endpoint (overall dimensional change) | NS across groups | | | NS |

- Primary endpoint (total CBCT change) not statistically significant — likely underpowered for small overall differences
- Late-phase volume maintenance (84–180 days, 3 mm below crest) significantly favors DBBM-C groups
- FGG seal advantage is crestal and horizontal — may be most clinically important for esthetic anterior sites
- Histology: all groups showed bone formation at 180 days; DBBM-C groups showed residual graft particles (expected for slowly resorbing xenograft)

## Clinical Implications

- DBBM-C can be placed without a separate membrane (open healing) and still maintain ridge volume over spontaneous healing
- FGG socket seal should be considered when horizontal crestal dimension is a priority (anterior esthetic zone, thin biotype)
- ISQ ≥70 across all groups: ARP with DBBM-C does not impair implant stability at 6 months
- DBBM-C handling (mouldable block/syringe form) simplifies clinical placement compared to loose particulate
