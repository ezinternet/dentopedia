## One-line Summary

Split-mouth dog study (n=9, 5×3 mm buccal dehiscence, 6-week healing): resorbable collagen membrane over DBBM tended to retain more bone substitute (%BS, p=0.052) but showed no significant increase in new bone formation vs DBBM alone; bone ingrowth occurred mainly from lateral defect walls.

## Summary

Darby et al. (2024) investigated whether adding a resorbable collagen membrane to DBBM grafting of buccal dehiscence defects at early (type 2) implant placement improves bone regeneration outcomes. Using a split-mouth design in 9 dogs, standardized 5×3 mm buccal dehiscence defects were created at the time of maxillary second incisor (I2) early implant placement, grafted with DBBM, and either covered (test) or not covered (control) with a resorbable collagen membrane.

At 6 weeks, histomorphometric analysis revealed no significant difference in first bone-to-implant contact (fBIC), buccal bone thickness, regenerated area, new bone area or percentage between the two groups. The membrane tended to retain more bone substitute and mineralized tissue at central sections (p = 0.052), suggesting a containment function, but did not translate into additional bone formation.

A key secondary finding was that **lateral sections showed significantly more new bone than central sections**, confirming that bone ingrowth in dehiscence defects proceeds centripetally from the lateral walls rather than from the graft core. This has implications for defect geometry and graft dimensioning in clinical GBR.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrated that in 5×3 mm buccal dehiscence defects at early implant placement in the anterior maxilla, resorbable membrane does not significantly enhance new bone formation over DBBM alone at 6 weeks.
- Confirmed membrane's role as bone substitute retainer (containment function) rather than bone formation promoter in this model.
- Established centripetal bone ingrowth pattern in dehiscence defects: lateral walls > central graft region.
- Utilized maxillary I2 canine model — first dedicated anterior maxilla dehiscence model more clinically translatable than posterior mandible models.

## Methodology

- Animal: 9 female Mongrel Hound dogs, split-mouth randomized design
- Timeline: extraction (week 0) → 6-week healing → implant placement + 5×3 mm dehiscence creation + grafting → 6-week healing → sacrifice
- Test: DBBM + resorbable collagen membrane; Control: DBBM alone
- Histomorphometry: fBIC, buccal bone thickness (1 mm increments), regenerated area, B, %B, BS, %BS, MT, %MT
- Sections analyzed: central and lateral (to capture spatial bone ingrowth patterns)

## Results

| Outcome | Membrane+DBBM vs DBBM alone | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| fBIC | No difference | NS |
| Buccal bone thickness | No difference | NS |
| Regenerated area (RA) | No difference | NS |
| New bone (B, %B) | No difference | NS |
| Bone substitute (%BS) | Membrane: more at central sections | p=0.052 (trend) |
| Mineralized tissue (%MT) | Membrane: more at central sections | p=0.052 (trend) |
| Lateral vs central | Significantly more B, %B, MT at lateral | p<0.05 |

Conclusion: Membrane preserves graft material, but does not enhance new bone formation at 6 weeks in this model.
