## 한줄요약
전향적 임상·방사선학 추적 (Prospective Clinical-Radiographic Study, n=46) — 단일 소구치/대구치 발치 후 12개월간 치조제 흡수의 약 2/3가 첫 3개월 내 발생하며 협설측 폭은 약 50% 감소하여, 발치 후 치조제 보존술 (Alveolar Ridge Preservation, ARP) 필요성의 가장 인용도 높은 임상 근거를 제공한다.

## Summary
Schropp et al. (2003, Int J Periodontics Restorative Dent) is the foundational 12-month prospective clinical and radiographic study quantifying post-extraction ridge changes in humans. Forty-six patients with single premolar or molar extractions were followed without socket management, with measurements at extraction and at 3, 6, and 12 months using study casts, standardised linear radiographs, and digital subtraction radiography. The headline findings — approximately 50% reduction in buccolingual ridge width over 12 months, with about two-thirds of that loss occurring within the first three months — became the most-cited human reference for unassisted post-extraction ridge resorption and the comparator baseline for two decades of alveolar ridge preservation (ARP) intervention studies. Although later CBCT and meta-analytic syntheses (e.g., Couso-Queiruga 2021) refined the magnitudes by tooth type, Schropp 2003 established the time-course principle: most of the ridge collapse is early.

## Key Contributions
- Quantifies the magnitude and time-course of unassisted post-extraction ridge resorption in humans.
- Establishes early-phase dominance: ~two-thirds of horizontal loss within the first 3 months.
- Provides the long-standing benchmark for any ARP or immediate-implant intervention study.
- Combines casts, linear radiographs, and subtraction radiography for multi-modal validation.

## Methodology
- 46 patients, single premolar or molar extraction, no socket grafting.
- Measurements at 0, 3, 6, and 12 months via study casts and standardised radiographs.
- Digital subtraction radiography for fine intrasocket bone-density change.
- Outcomes: buccolingual width, vertical mid-buccal height, soft-tissue contour.

## Results
- Buccolingual ridge width: ~50% reduction over 12 months.
- ~Two-thirds of total horizontal resorption within the first 3 months.
- Vertical mid-buccal change: smaller in magnitude but measurable.
- Subtraction radiography: active intrasocket bone formation peaked early.
- Soft-tissue contour mirrored underlying bone collapse.
