## One-line Summary

Animal study (n=190 Wistar rats, 5-mm CSD, 4–12 weeks) showing GA-crosslinked mineralized polyanionic collagen membranes produced more new bone than a commercial membrane and resisted resorption over 12 weeks while reducing TNF-α.

## Summary

Veríssimo et al. (Federal University of Ceará, Brazil) evaluated three novel glutaraldehyde (GA)-crosslinked polyanionic collagen (PAC) membranes from bovine intestinal serosa, mineralized with 0 (GA), 25 (25GA), or 75 (75GA) hydroxyapatite (HA) alternate-soaking cycles in a 5.25-mm critical-size rat calvarial defect (CSD) model. Animals (n=190 Wistar rats) were sacrificed at 24h, 4, 8, and 12 weeks. All three GA-crosslinked membranes significantly outperformed a commercial demineralized bovine cortical bone membrane (Genderm) and sham controls in new bone formation as measured by digital radiography and CT. The commercial membrane showed resorption by 12 weeks, while all experimental membranes remained structurally intact, enclosed in a fibrous capsule. Inflammatory marker assessment at 24h showed decreased TNF-α in the GA-crosslinked groups relative to control, with no significant differences in MPO activity or IL-1β. Increasing HA mineral cycles (0 → 25 → 75) did not produce statistically significant differences among GA, 25GA, and 75GA groups, indicating GA crosslinking itself drives the improved bone regeneration outcome.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrated that GA crosslinking of PAC membranes prevents resorption over 12 weeks — a meaningful advantage for large or slow-healing defects requiring prolonged barrier function
- Showed mineralized PAC membranes (0–75 HA cycles) uniformly outperform commercial collagen membrane for bone formation in rat CSD
- Confirmed anti-inflammatory profile: reduced TNF-α vs control, no elevation of MPO or IL-1β
- Established that increasing HA mineral cycling alone (without GA) does not drive superior bone outcomes at tested timepoints
- Identified fibrous capsule formation around GA-crosslinked membranes at 12 weeks — a soft tissue response to monitor in translation

## Methodology

- **Model**: 5.25-mm diameter full-thickness trephine defect in rat calvaria including sagittal suture; n=190 Wistar rats (200–300 g)
- **Groups**: Sham (no membrane), Control (Genderm commercial collagen), GA (0 HA cycles + GA crosslink), 25GA (25 HA cycles + GA crosslink), 75GA (75 HA cycles + GA crosslink)
- **Sacrifice**: 24h (n=6/group), 4w (n=6/group), 8w (n=8/group), 12w (n=12/group)
- **Assessments**: Digital radiography (NIHImageJ, radiolucent area as % of baseline), CT, H&E histology, MPO activity, TNF-α, IL-1β (at 24h)
- **Membrane fabrication**: Bovine intestinal serosa PAC, mineralized at 25°C at pH 9.0 by alternate soaking, then GA crosslinked at low concentration (progressive method to minimize cytotoxicity)

## Results

- All three GA-crosslinked membranes: significantly greater new bone formation vs sham and control at all post-baseline timepoints
- Commercial membrane (Genderm): resorption observed; not present at 12 weeks
- GA, 25GA, 75GA membranes: structurally intact at 12 weeks within thick fibrous capsule
- TNF-α: significantly lower in GA, 25GA, 75GA vs control group at 24h
- MPO, IL-1β: no significant inter-group differences
- HA cycling effect: no significant differences among GA vs 25GA vs 75GA groups
