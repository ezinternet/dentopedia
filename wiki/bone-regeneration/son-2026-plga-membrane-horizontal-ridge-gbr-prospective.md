## One-line Summary

Prospective single-arm clinical study (20 patients / 21 sites) of a 3D-preformed resorbable PLGA membrane (3D-PRPM) with simultaneous implant placement and xenograft: mean horizontal bone augmentation 2.99 ± 1.15 mm and hard tissue gain 2.64 ± 1.10 mm at 5 months (retention rate 87.2%), with zero membrane exposure, infection, or dehiscence.

## Summary

This is the first clinical study of a 3D-preformed resorbable PLGA membrane (3D-PRPM; r-Builder, Osstem) designed to combine the rigidity of titanium mesh with the resorbability of collagen membranes for guided bone regeneration (GBR). In 20 systemically healthy patients (21 localized horizontal ridge defects, 5 anterior / 16 posterior), implants (TS III) were placed simultaneously with particulate xenograft (A-Oss), covered by the 3D-PRPM (stabilized by a cover cap, no separate fixation) plus an overlying resorbable collagen membrane, under tension-free primary closure. CBCT at preop, immediate postop, and 5 months quantified horizontal dimensions.

Horizontal augmentation averaged 2.99 ± 1.15 mm, with 2.64 ± 1.10 mm of hard tissue retained at 5 months (resorption only 0.35 mm; hard tissue gain rate 87.2%, significantly above the 80% titanium-mesh reference). Bony-envelope analysis showed the regenerated contour extended beyond the native envelope in 66.7% of sites after augmentation and remained beyond it in most at 5 months, indicating high volumetric stability. Critically, there were no membrane exposures, infections, or dehiscences across all 21 sites — addressing the principal weakness of titanium mesh (15–50% exposure, frequent secondary removal). The membrane is fully resorbable, so no second-stage removal is needed.

## Key Contributions

- First clinical evidence that a fixation-free, fully resorbable 3D-preformed PLGA membrane achieves titanium-mesh-comparable horizontal augmentation (~3 mm) and retention (~87%) at 5 months.
- Zero exposure/infection/dehiscence — eliminates the chief morbidity of titanium-mesh GBR and the need for secondary removal.
- Bony-envelope quantification of contour stability beyond the native ridge envelope through healing.
- In vitro characterization: compressive strength > 16 N maintained through 4 months (matching the bone-regeneration window), with slower, less-acidic degradation than lower-MW PLGA.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Prospective, single-arm clinical trial; 20 patients / 21 sites; mean age 60.5 y; 5-month follow-up. IRB CUDHIRB 2405-006.
- **Membrane**: 3D-PRPM, PLGA (MW 90,000; lactide:glycolide 82:18), injection-molded, ~0.3 mm thick, 1.0-mm pores.
- **Procedure**: Single periodontist; implant + anchor screw, peri-implant defect grafted with particulate xenograft, covered by 3D-PRPM + overlying collagen membrane; tension-free closure; second-stage at 5 months.
- **Outcomes**: CBCT (0.2 mm voxel) — bone augmentation (BA), hard tissue gain (HG), bone resorption (BR = BA−HG), hard tissue gain rate (HGR = HG/BA×100), bony-envelope distance. Single blinded examiner (ICC > 0.90).
- **Stats**: Wilcoxon signed-rank (BA vs HG), Friedman + Bonferroni pairwise (envelope), one-sample t-test of HGR vs 80%.

## Results

- BA 2.99 ± 1.15 mm; HG (5 mo) 2.64 ± 1.10 mm; BR 0.35 ± 0.23 mm (BA > HG, p < 0.001, r = 0.88).
- HGR 87.2% ± 8.2% (95% CI 83.4–90.8%); significantly > 80% reference (p < 0.001, d = 0.87).
- Bony envelope: baseline +2.60 mm → post-aug −0.39 mm → 5 mo −0.04 mm (Friedman p < 0.001); 5-mo ridge significantly greater than baseline (P < 0.001). 14/21 sites beyond envelope after augmentation; 10/14 still beyond at 5 months.
- Safety: 0 membrane exposures, 0 infections, 0 dehiscences.

**Limitations**: small single-arm sample, no control/membrane-type comparison, 3D-PRPM always combined with collagen membrane, 5-month follow-up only, no histology, no survival data.
