## One-line Summary

Dog study (4 dogs, 32 implants, 8 weeks): L-shaped loosely-packed vs compactly-packed soft block bone substitutes showed equivalent GBR outcomes for peri-implant dehiscence — no significant bone width difference at any level; both failed coronally without membrane fixation; micro-CT overestimated by 0.31 mm but correlated strongly with histomorphometry (r=0.971).

## Summary

This pre-clinical in vivo study compared two application techniques for soft block bone substitutes (BBS) in guided bone regeneration (GBR) for chronic peri-implant dehiscence defects in a dog model. The LP (L-shaped, loosely packed) group used DPBM-C trimmed to an L-shape and placed without compression; the CP (compactly packed) group used non-trimmed DPBM-C manually adapted with gentle pressure. Both groups used non-fixed resorbable collagen membranes (no pins or screws).

At 8 weeks, neither micro-CT nor histomorphometry revealed significant differences in bone width between groups at any level from the implant shoulder to 5 mm apical. Both groups showed negligible bone width at the shoulder (0 mm) and at 1 mm apical (~0.1 mm), with progressive increase apically (~2.9–3.3 mm at 5 mm apical). Coronal bone retention was consistently poor in both groups.

The study also validated micro-CT against histomorphometry: mean bias was +0.31 mm (micro-CT overestimates), with 95% limits of agreement of −0.63 to +1.25 mm, and total correlation r=0.971 — supporting radiographic assessment as a reliable surrogate in pre-clinical GBR research.

## Key Contributions

1. **No compaction technique advantage**: LP vs CP packing technique does not influence bone regeneration when membrane fixation is absent — the hypothesis (significant difference) was rejected.
2. **Coronal collapse with non-fixed membranes**: Both groups showed near-zero bone at the implant shoulder and ≤0.1 mm at 1 mm apical, confirming that non-fixed collagen membranes cannot prevent morphological collapse in the coronal region, regardless of BBS application technique.
3. **Micro-CT validation**: Strong correlation with histomorphometry (r=0.971) and a small, consistent overestimation bias (+0.31 mm) — acceptable for pre-clinical studies.
4. **Clinical implication**: BBS compaction technique alone is insufficient; enhanced membrane stabilization (pins, tacks, periosteal sutures) is necessary for coronal bone regeneration.

## Methodology

- **Animal model**: 4 male mongrel dogs (25–35 kg); box-shaped buccal defects (5×5 mm) created bilaterally after tooth extraction; 4-week healing before implant placement
- **Implants**: 32 × Dentis Ø4.0 mm, L11.5 mm; 2 per defect (16 LP, 16 CP)
- **Graft material**: Porcine DPBM-C 10% collagen blocks (THE Graft Collagen, Purgo Biologics) — same product in both groups
- **Membrane**: Non-cross-linked porcine collagen membrane (BioCover, Purgo Biologics); no fixation
- **Sacrifice**: 8 weeks; micro-CT (Skyscan 1173) + histomorphometry (Goldner's trichrome)
- **Statistical analysis**: Mann–Whitney U, Bland–Altman, Spearman correlation; ICC >0.90 for examiner reliability
