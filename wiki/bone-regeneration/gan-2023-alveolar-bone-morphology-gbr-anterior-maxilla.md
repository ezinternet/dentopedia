## One-line Summary

Retrospective study (n=28) demonstrating that pre-operative bone concavity depth ≥1.03 mm and angulation <155.3° are significant CBCT predictors of lower grafted bone resorption rate after simultaneous GBR + delayed implant in the anterior maxilla.

## 한줄요약

전방 상악 GBR+지연 임플란트 28명 후향적 연구 — 술전 골오목 깊이 ≥1.03 mm·각도 <155.3°에서 이식재 흡수율 유의하게 낮아 술전 CBCT 예측 인자로 활용 가능.

## Summary

In the anterior maxilla, post-extraction horizontal bone loss frequently requires guided bone regeneration (GBR) for successful implant placement. This retrospective study at Zhongshan Hospital (Fudan University) enrolled 28 patients undergoing delayed single anterior implant + simultaneous GBR using DBBM (Bio-Oss) + resorbable collagen membrane (Bio-Gide). 3D CBCT models were generated at three time points using Mimics software, and graft volumes were tracked from immediate postop (T1) to 6 months (T2).

The mean resorption rate of grafted bone (RRGB) at 6 months was **49.78%** — nearly half of all grafted material resorbs within 6 months. Critically, this resorption rate was not predicted by conventional clinical variables (age, gender, implant brand, angulation, graft material type). Only pre-operative bone morphology — specifically bone concavity depth (BCD) and bone concavity angulation (BCA) — showed strong correlation.

**Predictive thresholds**:
- **BCD ≥1.03 mm**: significantly lower RRGB (p<0.001) — deeper concavity provides "tent effect" mechanical support reducing membrane collapse
- **BCA <155.30°**: significantly lower RRGB (p<0.001) — narrower (more contained) concavity retains graft material better against soft tissue pressure

## Key Contributions

1. Quantified mean graft resorption (49.78%) at 6 months for anterior maxilla GBR with standard collagen membrane + DBBM
2. Identified BCD and BCA as the first quantitative CBCT-based predictors of GBR outcome in anterior maxilla
3. Established specific threshold values (BCD ≥1.03 mm; BCA <155.30°) for clinical decision-making
4. Demonstrated that conventional clinical variables (age, gender, implant brand, material) are NOT predictive of graft resorption

## Methodology

- **Design**: Single-center retrospective study
- **Period**: September 2015 – August 2021
- **Subjects**: 28 patients, single missing maxillary anterior tooth ≥6 months, horizontal bone defect only
- **Procedure**: Delayed implant + simultaneous GBR (Bio-Oss granules/collagen + Bio-Gide, submerged healing)
- **Imaging**: CBCT at T0/T1/T2; 3D reconstruction with Mimics 21.0; sagittal slice segmentation (0.2 mm)
- **Variables**: RRGB (%), LBW at 0/2/4 mm, BCD, BCA, IA, MBGT, BGV
- **Statistics**: Pearson correlation, ANOVA, optimal binning (for threshold derivation)

## Results

| Predictor | r | p |
|---|---|---|
| BCD (bone concavity depth) | −0.872 | <0.001 |
| BCA (bone concavity angulation) | 0.686 | <0.001 |
| Age, gender, material, angulation, brand | NS | >0.05 |

**Mean RRGB**: 49.78% at 6 months

**Threshold significance**:
- BCD ≥1.03 mm → significantly lower RRGB (p<0.001)
- BCA <155.30° → significantly lower RRGB (p<0.001)
