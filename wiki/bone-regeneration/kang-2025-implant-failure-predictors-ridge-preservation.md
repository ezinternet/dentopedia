## 한줄요약
retrospective(JPIS 2025, n=528 임플란트 412명, 3.5년): ARP 후 임플란트 실패 예측인자 — PBE <1.1mm → 실패 위험 HR 2.50(P=0.004); 누적 성공률 89.0%; 원발골 맞물림 최소 1.1mm 확보가 핵심.

## Summary
Retrospective cohort (Kyung Hee University, 2014–2020) evaluating predictors of implant failure after alveolar ridge preservation (ARP). Among n=528 implants from 412 patients followed for 3.5±1.8 years, pristine bone engagement (PBE) < 1.1 mm was the only significant predictor of implant failure in Cox proportional hazards modeling.

## Key Contributions
- **n=528 implants, 412 patients** after ARP; mean follow-up 3.5±1.8 years
- **Cumulative success rate: 89.0%** (95%CI 85.4–92.8%); 43 failed implants
- **Cox model**: PBE <1.1 mm → HR **2.50** (95%CI 1.34–4.67, **P=0.004**)
- PBE = apical length of implant engaging pristine (non-grafted) native bone
- Demographic/clinical/radiographic factors otherwise not significant

## What is PBE and Why It Matters
- After ARP, grafted socket material has lower initial quality than native bone
- Implant must anchor into sufficient native bone apical to graft → PBE
- PBE ≥1.1 mm ensures adequate native bone engagement for osseointegration
- Surgical planning: choose implant length to guarantee ≥1.1 mm beyond graft zone
