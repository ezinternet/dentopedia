## One-line Summary
Randomized single-blinded RCT (n=28 peri-implant dehiscence sites) comparing dehydrothermally cross-linked (DCM) vs non-cross-linked (NCM) collagen membrane for GBR; both significantly improved clinical/CBCT parameters at 16 weeks, DCM showed slower degradation (residual leaflets) and good handling without increased complications.

## 한줄요약
임플란트 열개결손 GBR에서 열탈수가교막(DCM) vs 비가교막(NCM)을 비교한 단일맹검 RCT(n=28). 16주에 두 막 모두 임상·CBCT 지표 유의 개선, DCM이 분해 지연(잔존막 관찰)·양호한 조작성을 보이며 합병증 증가는 없었음.

## Summary
At buccal dehiscence defects exposed during implant placement, both a dehydrothermally cross-linked collagen membrane (DCM) and a non-cross-linked native collagen membrane (NCM) produced significant clinical and radiographic bone improvement over 16 weeks. The clinically relevant difference is degradation kinetics: only the DCM group showed partially resorbed membrane leaflets histologically, consistent with a longer effective barrier time — without the elevated exposure/complication signal sometimes attributed to chemically cross-linked membranes.

## Key Contributions
- Direct RCT of cross-linking effect at the peri-implant dehiscence indication specifically.
- Combined clinical (probe), radiographic (CBCT horizontal thickness + density at 1/2/3 mm), surgeon-rated handling (VAS), and histologic degradation outcomes.

## Methodology
- 28 patients, buccal dehiscence after implant placement, randomized DCM vs NCM.
- Baseline + 16-week probe measurements of defect width/height; CBCT thickness/density at 16 weeks.
- VAS handling score at baseline; histologic assessment of membrane remnants at re-entry.

## Results
- 5 sites (2 NCM, 3 DCM) had early membrane exposure; no severe complications either arm.
- Significant improvement in all clinical/radiographic parameters in both groups.
- Partially resorbed membrane leaflets only in DCM (slower resorption → extended barrier).
- DCM: comparable safety, favorable handling vs NCM.
