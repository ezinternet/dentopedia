## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 29 RCTs (1,068 patients, 2010–2023): rhBMP-2 significantly reduces horizontal and vertical bone resorption post-extraction; L-PRF shows no significant benefit over natural healing but remains a cost-effective autologous alternative.

## 한줄요약

29개 RCT(1,068명) SR+MA: rhBMP-2는 수평(p=.01)·수직(p<.0008) 골흡수를 모두 유의하게 감소시키며, L-PRF는 자연치유 대비 유의하지 않으나 자가유래·비용효과적 대안으로 권고됨.

## Summary

This SR+MA evaluated regenerative biologic materials — blood-derived growth factor preparations, L-PRF, and rhBMP-2 — for alveolar socket preservation following tooth extraction. Six meta-analyses were conducted from 29 RCTs. rhBMP-2 produced the strongest evidence for reducing both horizontal and vertical radiographic bone loss. Blood-derived preparations (non-L-PRF) showed significant height but not width preservation. L-PRF alone did not differ significantly from natural healing in either dimension, though the authors highlight its autologous, cost-effective profile as a clinical reason to consider it. Most included studies (15/29) carried high risk of bias.

## Key Contributions

- Provides a biologics-focused SR+MA specifically for socket preservation — a distinct layer above xenograft/allograft SRs
- Head-to-head rhBMP-2 vs L-PRF comparison: width favors rhBMP-2 (p<.0001); height NS (p=.057)
- Quantifies the null result for L-PRF vs natural healing — clinicians choosing L-PRF should frame this as autologous/cost advantage, not as superior dimensional preservation
- High-RoB majority (15/29) tempers certainty of all conclusions

## Methodology

- Databases: PubMed, EMBASE, MEDLINE, Cochrane CENTRAL, Google Scholar
- Period: January 2010 – December 2023
- Inclusion: English human RCTs only
- Six subgroup meta-analyses by agent type
- Primary outcomes: radiographic alveolar height and width change post-extraction
- RoB: 6 low / 8 moderate / 15 high

## Results

| Agent vs comparator | Horizontal (width) | Vertical (height) |
|---|---|---|
| Blood-derived preparations vs control | NS | p = .001 |
| L-PRF vs natural healing | NS | NS |
| rhBMP-2 vs control | p = .01 | p < .0008 |
| rhBMP-2 vs L-PRF | p < .0001 (rhBMP-2 better) | p = .057 (NS) |

- rhBMP-2 is the only agent to significantly preserve both dimensions
- L-PRF clinical role: autologous, cost-effective, no evidence of harm — but no dimensional superiority over natural healing
