## One-line Summary
Periodontology 2000 narrative review (2018) presenting three ARP timing windows for the esthetic zone — soft-tissue preservation at 6–8 weeks, hard+soft preservation at 4–6 months, and hard-tissue preservation beyond 6 months — classified according to buccal plate status and implant placement timing.

## 한줄요약
서술적 리뷰 (Periodontology 2000, 2018): 심미영역에서 발치 후 폐포융선 보존술 (Alveolar Ridge Preservation, ARP)의 세 가지 시기 (6–8주 연조직 / 4–6개월 경+연조직 / 6개월+ 경조직)를 협측 골판 (buccal plate) 상태와 임플란트 식립 시점에 맞춰 분류 제시.

## Summary
This Periodontology 2000 review by Jung and the Zürich group reframes the post-extraction decision in the esthetic zone as a choice among three options: spontaneous socket healing, immediate implant placement (Type 1), or alveolar ridge preservation (ARP) followed by delayed placement. ARP is itself heterogeneous, and the authors group published protocols into three biologically defined healing windows. The selection criterion is driven less by graft material and more by buccal plate integrity, gingival phenotype, and the timing window the clinician intends to use for the eventual implant.

## Key Contributions
- Articulates a three-window ARP framework (soft-tissue only, hard + soft, hard only) keyed to healing time and tissue priorities.
- Centers the buccal bone plate as the dominant esthetic-risk variable.
- Connects each ARP strategy back to ITI Type 1/2/3/4 implant placement timing.
- Defines soft-tissue-only ARP (6–8 wk) as a legitimate stand-alone goal — not all ARP requires bone grafting.

## Methodology
- Narrative review of extraction-socket dimensional change and ARP literature (no PRISMA / no meta-analysis).
- Synthesizes evidence from RCTs, prospective cohorts, and dog/human histology to support each timing window.
- Discusses materials descriptively: DBBM, allograft, collagen membrane, free gingival graft, connective tissue graft, socket-seal punch grafts.

## Results
- Post-extraction horizontal ridge reduction is predominantly buccal, mostly within the first 3 months (consistent with Schropp 2003, Araújo 2005).
- Three timing windows:
  - **6–8 weeks (soft-tissue preservation)**: indicated when buccal plate is severely compromised and Type 2 implant placement is planned shortly.
  - **4–6 months (hard + soft preservation)**: most common ARP indication — graft + membrane / socket seal — when buccal plate is partially intact.
  - **>6 months (hard-tissue preservation)**: for sites needing complete re-mineralization before staged GBR or sinus-lift augmentation.
- Quantitative pooled effects not reported (this is a narrative review; see Avila-Ortiz 2014, 2019 and Couso-Queiruga 2021 for numbers).
- Decision-making variables: buccal plate height, gingival biotype, neighboring tooth biology, expected implant survival.
