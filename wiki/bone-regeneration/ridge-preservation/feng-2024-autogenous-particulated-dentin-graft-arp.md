## One-line Summary
SR+MA (10 RCTs, n=182): autogenous particulated dentin (APD) is superior to other grafted materials for horizontal ridge width and labial bone height preservation, with +10.51% higher vital bone area and −8.76% lower residual graft area compared with other bone substitutes.

## Summary
PRISMA-compliant meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD42023409339) pooling 10 RCTs (182 patients) on **autogenous particulated dentin (APD)** as an ARP graft, comparing against blood clot healing (BCH) or other grafted materials (OGM: DBBM, β-TCP, allograft). APD significantly outperformed both comparators on ridge dimensional preservation, and outperformed OGM on vital bone formation and graft resorption — establishing APD as a credible same-day, low-cost autograft for ARP.

## Key Contributions
- First quantitative pooled analysis of **APD vs BCH** and **APD vs OGM** for ARP.
- Reports parallel clinical (radiologic) and histomorphometric (vital bone, residual graft, connective tissue) outcomes.
- Demonstrates that APD's bone-forming capacity is comparable or superior to xenograft/alloplast at typical ARP re-entry timepoints.
- Provides effect-size estimates clinicians can use for ARP material selection.

## Methodology
- PICOS: extraction sockets → APD vs BCH or OGM → ridge/histology outcomes → RCT only.
- Search: MEDLINE, Embase, Web of Science, Scopus, Cochrane through 2 Aug 2023.
- Bias assessment: Cochrane Risk of Bias tool (7 domains); evidence quality: GRADE.
- Analysis: Review Manager 5.4, mean differences (MD), 95% CI; heterogeneity I².
- Two parallel meta-analyses (Group I = vs BCH; Group II = vs OGM).
- APD prep: extraction → enamel/cementum/restoration removal → grinding → cleaning, disinfection, ± demineralization.

## Results
- **Horizontal ridge width preservation (APD better)**:
  - vs BCH: MD = +1.61 mm (95% CI 0.76–2.46).
  - vs OGM: MD = +1.28 mm (95% CI 1.08–1.48).
- **Labial bone height preservation (APD better)**:
  - vs BCH: MD = +1.75 mm (95% CI 0.56–2.94).
  - vs OGM: significant (P < 0.05).
- **Histomorphometry (vs OGM, APD favored)**:
  - Vital bone area: MD = +10.51% (95% CI 4.70–16.32).
  - Residual graft area: MD = −8.76% (95% CI −12.81 to −4.71) — APD resorbs/integrates faster than xenograft.
  - vs BCH: no statistically significant histomorphometric differences.
- **Secondary outcomes**: implant stability quotient at re-entry and postoperative complications — no significant between-group differences.

## Clinical Implications (Author Memo)
- APD is a viable autograft alternative when **chairside dentin grinders** are available; turns "to-be-extracted" teeth into immediate graft material.
- Preserves ~1.3–1.6 mm horizontal width and ~1.75 mm labial height beyond blood-clot healing.
- vs OGM (xenograft/β-TCP): same or better ridge preservation **and** more vital bone, less residual particle.
- Caveat: small pooled sample (n = 182), heterogeneous APD prep protocols — confirm with larger RCTs before declaring APD superior to DBBM.
