## One-line Summary

Retrospective CBCT cohort (n=80; 60 with complete imaging) shows ridge preservation with Bio-Oss Collagen still incurs ~3–18% residual alveolar volume loss vs pre-extraction root volume (maxillary molars worst ~18%, maxillary premolars best ~3%); 78/80 sites reached implant placement without further augmentation.

## Summary

This German private-practice retrospective cohort (2018–2022) is one of the few human studies reporting **volumetric (mm³)** rather than linear alveolar change after alveolar ridge preservation (ARP) with a collagenated bovine/porcine xenograft (Bio-Oss Collagen, BO). Using 3D Slicer manual segmentation on CBCT, it compared pre-extraction tooth-root volume (a surrogate for "ideal" alveolar volume) against post-preservation alveolar volume in posterior teeth (premolars/molars).

The headline finding: ARP does **not** fully prevent resorption — sockets still lost about **3–18%** of volume, with the loss strongly site-dependent (maxillary molar worst, maxillary premolar best). Crucially, this residual loss is far smaller than the ~40–60% bucco-oral / 10–20% vertical loss expected without grafting, and 78 of 80 sites could receive an implant without additional augmentation — the clinically meaningful endpoint.

## Key Contributions

- Volumetric (mm³) human ARP outcome with a collagenated xenograft at a relatively large sample (n=80; comparative subset n=60).
- Site-specific resorption mapping: maxillary molar ~18% (worst), maxillary premolar ~3% (best).
- Clinical translation: implant placement without further augmentation in 78/80 sockets.
- Negative covariate analysis: no patient/treatment covariate independently predicted preservation degree once tooth type/defect size were accounted for.

## Methodology

- **Design**: retrospective cohort; predictor = pre-extraction root volume (apex → crestal bone); outcome = post-ARP alveolar volume (socket + BO segments).
- **n**: 80 subjects (43 M; mean age 59 ± 12.5 y; 20 premolars, 60 molars). Comparative subset **n=60** with complete imaging (20 excluded for non-evaluable analog PR/periapical only).
- **Protocol**: atraumatic extraction, curettage, dense BO fill (100/250 mg) without pressure, gingival approximation + silk suture; surgeon-discretion antibiotics; ibuprofen 600 mg.
- **Volumetry**: 3D Slicer v5.3.0; BO segmented by higher radiopacity; PR→volume estimation when no pre-op CBCT (premolar factor 5.38 ± 1.94 from 8 teeth).
- **Stats**: SPSS v31; paired t-test/ANOVA, Pearson, multivariable regression; post hoc G-Power (d=0.63, ≥22 pairs for 80% power; 60 analyzed).

## Results

- **Subset (n=60)**: predictor 451.64 ± 186.86 mm³ → post-ARP 394.86 ± 174.85 mm³ (significant decrease, p<0.001); mean 334.70 mm³ filled with BO.
- **Site-specific (pre → post)**: maxillary premolar 195.20 → 192.07 (~3%); maxillary molar 470.41 → 381.96 (~18%); mandibular premolar 220.42 → 199.86; mandibular molar 544.76 → 475.85 mm³.
- **Range** 3–18% depended on tooth type / medical condition (p<0.008).
- **Regression**: no covariate significant (all p>0.05); anticoagulants largest coefficient (B=110.1, p=0.075, NS) — early univariate signals attributed to confounding.
- **Clinical**: implant without further augmentation in **78/80** (2 failed for low bone quality).
- **Caveat**: 13 cases showed apparent volume gain, judged measurement artifact (septum loss, PDL overfill, lytic-area bone fill); root-volume surrogate excludes PDL/periapical lesions and may overestimate ARP success.
