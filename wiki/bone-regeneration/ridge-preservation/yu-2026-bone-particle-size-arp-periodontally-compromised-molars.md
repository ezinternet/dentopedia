## Summary

This single-center retrospective cohort is the first to examine how DBBM (Bio-Oss) **particle size** affects alveolar ridge preservation (ARP) in **periodontally compromised molar** extraction sockets — a high-prevalence but under-studied scenario (severe periodontitis projected to affect 1.56 billion people by 2050). 96 stage III/IV periodontitis patients (one molar socket each, each with a bone dehiscence over >50% of root length) were grafted with either small-particle (SP, 0.25–1 mm; n=48) or large-particle (LP, 1–2 mm; n=48) DBBM plus a resorbable collagen membrane (Bio-Gide), under flapless extraction with secondary-intention healing. CBCT at extraction (T0) and 4–8 months post-ARP (T1) drove linear and volumetric measurements; implant feasibility was scored by a 4-level LIPD scheme.

Bottom line: **alveolar ridge dimensions were maintained regardless of particle size.** LP showed only a non-significant trend toward greater vertical central socket height (VC 8.54 vs 7.76 mm, P=.077) and greater volume gain (369 vs 239 mm³, both NS). No intergroup difference reached significance for any dimensional/volumetric outcome. In multivariate regression, **socket integrity** (each additional wall ≥5 mm → +1.027 mm VC, P<.001) and **jaw** (mandible favored, P=.007) outweighed particle size (P=.041) as determinants of central socket height. Standard implant placement without augmentation (LIPD Level I) was achievable in 73% of sites; particle size did not change augmentation needs (P=.500).

## Key Contributions

- First evidence on DBBM particle-size choice for ARP specifically in periodontally compromised molar sockets.
- Reframes the decision: graft particle size is a minor lever; baseline **socket integrity** and **jaw** dominate the central-height outcome and should drive treatment planning.
- 73% of these compromised molar sites still allowed standard implant placement after ARP — supporting ARP as feasible (not just safe) in severe periodontitis.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Retrospective cohort, STROBE, single-center (Shanghai Ninth People's Hospital), Jan 2020–Dec 2024; IRB SH9H-2025-T423-1.
- **n**: 96 patients/96 molar sockets (37 F, 59 M; mean age 48.89 y). SP (0.25–1 mm) 48 vs LP (1–2 mm) 48; baseline variables comparable.
- **Graft**: particulate Bio-Oss + Bio-Gide collagen membrane, flapless extraction, secondary-intention healing.
- **Outcomes**: CBCT T0 (≤3 mo pre/at extraction) and T1 (4–8 mo); linear (buccal/lingual plate thickness, HW1/HW3/HW5, vertical heights, primary = vertical central height VC) via Romexis, volumetric via 3D Slicer; implant feasibility via LIPD Levels I–IV; single calibrated examiner (ICC >0.9).
- **Stats**: powered for 0.7 mm VC difference (≥28/group); t-test/Mann–Whitney + paired tests; multivariate linear regression on VC adjusting for age, gender, jaw, tooth position, healing duration, socket wall thickness, socket integrity, particle size.

## Results

- **VC (primary)**: LP 8.54 ± 2.34 vs SP 7.76 ± 1.93 mm — non-significant trend (P = .077).
- **All other dimensional/volumetric outcomes**: no significant SP-vs-LP difference; VB/VL/HW1/HW3/HW5 decreased and volume increased significantly T0→T1 in both groups.
- **Volume gain**: LP 369.44 mm³ (23.78%) vs SP 239.13 mm³ (17.73%), NS.
- **Buccal > lingual height resorption** in both groups (−1.90/−1.97 mm vs −0.97/−0.99 mm). Mean healing 6.4 mo.
- **Implant feasibility**: Level I in 70/96 (73%); 26 needed augmentation (13 Level II, 13 Level III); slightly fewer complex (Level III, all maxillary LSFE) in LP (5/48) vs SP (8/48); no Level IV; distribution similar (P = .500).
- **Multivariate regression on VC** — significant: socket integrity (+1.027 mm per wall ≥5 mm, P<.001), jaw (P=.007; mandible 8.844 vs maxilla 7.394 mm), particle size (P=.041). Non-significant: age, gender, tooth position, healing duration, wall thickness.
- **Limitations**: retrospective (uncalibrated surgery); no immediate post-extraction CBCT; no soft-tissue/histology/PRO; modest n; smokers excluded.
