## One-line Summary

Prospective case series (n=10 patients, 130–319 day healing): ARP with species-specific collagen-enriched DBBM block (C-DBBM) + bovine cross-linked collagen membrane using open-healing technique resulted in histological bone formation via appositional membranous osteogenesis and 0% requiring GBR at implant placement; 2.5–4.5 year follow-up with no complications.

## Summary

Van Orten et al. (2024) reported the first histological data for a novel species-specific, collagen-enriched DBBM block (C-DBBM: 95% DBBM + 5% bovine collagen) used for alveolar ridge preservation (ARP). Ten patients underwent tooth extraction followed by ARP with the C-DBBM block and a double-folded bovine cross-linked collagen membrane (xCM) using an open-healing technique (membrane exposed to oral cavity, secured by crisscross suture). Healing periods ranged from 130 to 319 days.

Implant placement was performed using static computer-aided implant surgery (s-CAIS), with trephine cores harvested for histological processing. Histological examination revealed **lattice-pattern cancellous bone trabeculae via appositional membranous osteogenesis** at multiple concurrent stages, surrounding residual C-DBBM granules. **No patient required GBR at implant placement** (0/10). Clinical follow-up over 2.5–4.5 years showed no biological or technical complications.

This study is notable for its all-bovine species-specific material chain — C-DBBM block + bovine xCM — which addresses religious or ethical objections to porcine-derived membranes common in standard GBR protocols. While the 0% GBR rate appears dramatically better than the 6–19% augmentation-free success rate reported in Fischer et al.'s SR+MA of standard DBBM, the open-label prospective case series design with no control limits direct comparison.

## Key Contributions

- First histological evidence for species-specific C-DBBM (95% DBBM + 5% bovine collagen) in ARP.
- Open-healing technique with bovine cross-linked membrane: no adverse outcomes despite membrane exposure to oral environment.
- 0% GBR requirement at implant placement in 10 consecutive patients.
- All-bovine material chain: clinically relevant for halal-compliant, porcine-free practice contexts.
- Long-term follow-up (2.5–4.5 years): no implant failures or peri-implant complications reported.

## Methodology

- Design: Prospective case series, no control group
- n: 10 patients; each with 1 extraction socket + ARP
- ARP protocol: C-DBBM block (95% DBBM + 5% bovine collagen) + double-folded bovine xCM, open-healing technique (no primary closure)
- Healing: 130–319 days (individualized)
- Implant placement: s-CAIS (static computer-aided) with guided trephine drilling for core harvest
- Histological analysis: Trephine cores; descriptive histology (no quantitative histomorphometry reported)
- Clinical follow-up: 2.5–4.5 years

## Results

- **GBR at implant placement**: 0/10 (no patient required additional augmentation)
- **Histological pattern**: Cancellous bone trabeculae lattice by appositional membranous osteogenesis surrounding C-DBBM granules; compact ossicles present; minimal residual material in some cores
- **Bone formation stages**: Multiple concurrent osteogenic stages — active remodeling at time of implant placement
- **Membrane behavior**: Open-healing xCM without primary closure was well-tolerated
- **Complications**: 0 biological, 0 technical over 2.5–4.5 year follow-up

Note: The dramatic difference from Fischer et al.'s SR+MA (6–19% augmentation-free vs 0% GBR rate here) likely reflects: (1) case selection bias in a private practice setting, (2) longer healing periods (mean >6 months vs 3–4 months in most RCTs), (3) block-form vs particulate DBBM with integrated collagen, and (4) absence of a standardized measurement protocol for "adequate bone."
