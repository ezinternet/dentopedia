## One-line Summary
Systematic review (12 human studies): after non-preserved post-extraction socket healing, weighted-mean horizontal alveolar ridge width reduction was 3.87 mm vs mid-buccal vertical loss 1.67 mm and radiographic crestal loss 1.53 mm — horizontal resorption exceeds vertical, the canonical baseline justifying ARP/immediate implant decisions.

## Summary
This 2009 systematic review by Van der Weijden, Dell'Acqua, and Slot establishes the canonical baseline for the magnitude of post-extraction alveolar ridge dimensional change in humans receiving no preservation procedure. After screening 1350 records, 12 publications met eligibility. Weighted-mean horizontal alveolar ridge width reduction was 3.87 mm, clinical mid-buccal vertical loss was 1.67 mm, radiographic crestal vertical loss was 1.53 mm, and socket fill in height (relative to the original socket floor) averaged 2.57 mm at follow-ups of 3–12 months. The dominant clinical message: **horizontal resorption exceeds vertical resorption** during natural socket healing, with the buccal plate carrying the largest share of the loss. This paper is the most-cited reference value used to justify alveolar ridge preservation (ARP) and immediate implant placement (IIP) decisions.

## Key Contributions
- Provides the **first systematic human-only weighted means** for post-extraction ridge change, superseding reliance on animal data.
- Quantifies that **width loss (~3.87 mm) is roughly 2× the height loss (~1.5–1.7 mm)** — a key planning number for implant-site assessment.
- Clarifies that clinical mid-buccal measurements detect more loss than panoramic/radiographic crestal measurements (radiographs underestimate buccal plate loss because they look at the inter-proximal crest).
- Calibrates the "natural healing" benchmark against which alveolar ridge preservation (ARP) trials should be judged.

## Methodology
- Two-reviewer screening of 1244 PubMed + 106 Cochrane records (search through March 2009).
- Inclusion: human studies reporting at least one of six pre-defined dimensional outcomes for extraction sockets without ridge preservation; method of measurement explicitly described.
- 12 studies finally included (mix of prospective and retrospective; mostly single-tooth anterior/premolar).
- Mean ± SD extracted per study; **weighted means** calculated by sample size; no pooled meta-analysis due to measurement heterogeneity.

## Results
- **Alveolar width reduction**: 3.87 mm (weighted mean across studies reporting horizontal change).
- **Mid-buccal clinical height reduction**: 1.67 mm.
- **Mid-crestal radiographic height reduction**: 1.53 mm.
- **Socket fill in height (from original socket floor)**: 2.57 mm — i.e., socket fills to within ~2–3 mm of its original floor.
- Follow-up window: 3–12 months across studies; majority at 6 months.
- Authors conclude post-extraction loss is **greater in width than in height** when measured clinically or radiographically.
- (Per-study CIs and SDs are tabulated in the original paper; the review reports weighted means without pooled CI.)
