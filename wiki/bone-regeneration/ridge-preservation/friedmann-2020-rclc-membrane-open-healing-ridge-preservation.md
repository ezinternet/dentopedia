## Summary

This pilot study evaluated whether RCLC (ribose cross-linked collagen) membranes could serve as ridge preservation barriers with open healing — intentionally leaving the membrane exposed to the oral cavity without primary soft tissue closure.

**Key insight**: Conventional collagen membranes degrade in 4–6 weeks when exposed (collagenase attack), failing as barriers. Ribose cross-linking provides resistance to collagenase, making open healing viable.

20 sockets in 18 patients (10 contained, 10 non-contained) received OSSIXPlus RCLC membrane draped across the socket with margins sutured in original position — no coronal advancement. Weekly monitoring for 4 weeks. All 20 implants placed at 4 months without additional augmentation.

**Critical advantages of open healing**:
1. Technically simpler — no tension-free flap advancement required
2. Keratinized mucosa preserved at original level (no apical displacement of the mucogingival junction)
3. No second surgery for membrane removal (RCLC resorbable, ~24 weeks)

## Key Contributions

- First validation of RCLC open healing for BOTH contained and non-contained sockets in the same study
- Histological proof: new woven bone with RCLC residues showing no inflammatory infiltrate — confirms "osteopromotive character"
- Muco-gingival junction maintained at original position in all 20 sites (vs ~4 mm apical displacement with coronal advancement)
- Defines open-healing monitoring protocol: weekly × 4 weeks (CHX rinse + H₂O₂ disinfection)
- Comparable ridge dimensions vs baseline (no significant resorption) → implants possible without augmentation

## Methodology

- **Design**: Pilot prospective series (no control)
- **Material**: OSSIXPlus (porcine RCLC, Datum Dental, Israel)
- **N**: 18 patients (mean age 59.6 ±8.2 yr), 20 sockets
  - 10 contained (intact walls), 10 non-contained (>30% buccal/lingual wall loss)
- **Non-contained sockets**: calcium sulfate or bovine bone substitute added first to prevent membrane collapse
- **Closure**: double-interrupted sutures fixing flap margins in ORIGINAL position — no coronal advancement
- **Post-op**: CHX 0.2% 3×/day × 1 week; H₂O₂ 3% at weekly clinic visits × 4 weeks
- **Assessment**: occlusal photos + ImageJ 2D measurement; periapical X-rays; core + crestal histological biopsies
- **Implant timing**: 4 months after ridge preservation

## Results

| Outcome | Value |
|---|---|
| Implants placed without additional augmentation | 20/20 (100%) |
| Soft tissue closure by 3 weeks | 15/20 (75%) |
| Soft tissue closure by 4 weeks | 20/20 (100%) |
| Vertical height change (median) | 0.45 mm (p=0.450, NS) |
| Cross-sectional area change | −9.60 mm² (~9.75%) (NS overall; contained sockets p=0.007) |
| MG junction displacement | 0 mm (maintained at original) |
| Bone quality at implant placement | D2–D3 |

Histology: new woven bone with vital osteoblasts; RCLC residues present without inflammatory infiltrate; perimembranous and intramembranous osteogenesis confirmed.
