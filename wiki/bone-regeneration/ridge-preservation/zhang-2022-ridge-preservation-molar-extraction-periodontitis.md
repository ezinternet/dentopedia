## One-line Summary
RCT protocol (BMC Oral Health 2022): 2-arm parallel design (n=70 molar sites) comparing ARP vs natural healing at severe-periodontitis (stage III/IV) molar extraction sockets; primary outcomes = CBCT vertical/horizontal ridge change, 3D soft-tissue contour, mucosa thickness, histology, periodontal parameters — no results reported.

## Summary
A SPIRIT-compliant, registered RCT **protocol** from Peking University targeting a sparse-evidence niche: alveolar ridge preservation (ARP) at **periodontally compromised molar** extraction sockets in **severe (stage III/IV) periodontitis**. Most ARP evidence derives from anterior/intact sockets; this trial is designed to compare ARP against natural (unassisted) healing across hard tissue (CBCT), 3D soft tissue (intraoral scanning/STL superimposition), and histology in 70 molar sites. **No outcomes are reported** — the page documents the design only.

## Key Contributions
- Targets an under-studied indication: **ARP at infected/periodontally compromised molar sockets** (not anterior/intact).
- Multi-modal endpoints in one trial: CBCT hard-tissue change + 3D soft-tissue contour/mucosa thickness (STL superimposition) + **histology** (biopsy at implant surgery) — soft-tissue profile at infected molar sites is specifically under-reported.
- Registered (ChiCTR2200056335) and SPIRIT-reported; planned follow-up extends to ≥1 year post-implant-loading for soft-tissue endpoints.

## Methodology
- **Design**: two-group parallel RCT; test = ARP, control = natural healing.
- **Sample**: 70 molar extraction sites (power analysis).
- **Inclusion**: age > 25; good compliance/oral hygiene; stage III/IV periodontitis; ≥1 hopeless molar with severe bone loss; ≥2 socket walls beyond apex or ≥3 mm of socket; ≥1 adjacent proximal tooth.
- **Exclusion**: pregnancy/lactation; bone-healing-impairing meds/disease; uncontrolled HTN/DM or surgical contraindications; head-neck radiotherapy; smoking >10 cig/day; absence of both adjacent teeth; acute pathology; teeth extracted for caries/endo failure/fracture.
- **Randomization**: Excel random numbers, sequentially numbered opaque sealed envelopes (SNOSE).
- **Procedures**: atraumatic extraction → ARP (test) vs natural healing (control); CBCT, intraoral STL scans, periodontal exam over follow-up; bone biopsy at implant surgery (~6-month healing).

## Results
**None — this is a protocol.** Planned outcomes only:

| Type | Planned outcome | Method / timing |
|------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Primary | Vertical & horizontal ridge change | CBCT, baseline → 6 mo |
| Primary | Soft-tissue contour change | Superimposed digital impressions |
| Primary | Mucosa thickness change | CBCT + STL superimposition |
| Primary | Histological features of implant sites | Biopsy at implant surgery (~6 mo) |
| Primary | Periodontal parameter change | Over follow-up |
| Secondary | Patient-reported post-op reaction | Questionnaire |
| Secondary | Need for simultaneous bone graft / sinus lift at implant placement | At implant surgery |
