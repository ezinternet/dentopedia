## One-line Summary
RCT (20 completers): applying greater compressive force to the graft during ARP yields equivalent dimensional preservation but significantly higher new bone formation (P<0.05); optimal compressive force remains undefined.

## 한줄요약
RCT(완료 20명): ARP 시 graft에 더 큰 압축력을 가하면 치조제 치수 보존은 동등하면서 신생골 형성 비율은 유의하게 증가 (P<0.05). 최적 압축력은 미정.

## Summary
Isolates a rarely-studied ARP variable — the compressive force applied when packing graft. Greater force boosted histomorphometric new-bone formation without compromising (or improving) dimensional preservation.

## Key Contributions
- Compressive force during grafting as an explicit ARP variable
- Histomorphometric dose-response signal (more force → more new bone)
- Dimensions and Periotest values unaffected by force level

## Methodology
- RCT (4 control dropouts; 20 completers); test vs control compressive force; CBCT + histomorphometry + Periotest.

## Results
- Ridge volume preserved comparably in both groups (P>0.05).
- New bone ratio significantly greater in greater-force group (P<0.05).
- PTV: no difference (P>0.05).

## Limitations
- Small sample with dropouts; optimal force undefined; single-center.
