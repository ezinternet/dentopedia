## One-line Summary

Retrospective comparative study (n=45 single-tooth sites; 23 spontaneous healing vs 22 ARP with bovine xenograft + resorbable pericardium membrane) with 6-month CBCT volumetric superimposition: ARP roughly halved horizontal ridge loss at 1 mm (0.86 vs 2.03 mm, P<0.001) and volumetric shrinkage (62.66 vs 106.41 mm³, P=0.004), improved PES (11.6 vs 9.9, P=0.007), trended toward less later grafting (9% vs 26%, NS), with equal pain/satisfaction and 100% 1-year implant survival.

## Summary

A single-center Italian retrospective comparison of two post-extraction socket management strategies in single-tooth sites destined for implant rehabilitation: **spontaneous healing (SH, n=23)** vs **alveolar ridge preservation (ARP, n=22)** using cortico-spongious bovine granules (Re-Bone, particle 0.25–1 mm) covered by a resorbable bovine pericardium membrane (Shelter Fast). The distinguishing feature is **3D volumetric quantification** — CBCT taken 1 week before and 6 months after extraction, with the two digital models superimposed — alongside conventional linear measurements (horizontal width at 1/3/5 mm; buccal/palatal vertical height) and outcome layers spanning esthetics (PES), patient-reported pain/satisfaction, and downstream clinical need for additional grafting and implant survival.

ARP reduced both linear and volumetric shrinkage and improved esthetics, while pain, patient satisfaction, and 1-year implant survival were equivalent between groups. Allocation was by patient preference (those refusing ARP received SH), so this is observational, not randomized.

## Key Contributions

- Adds **CBCT volumetric superimposition** data (not just linear) to the ARP-vs-spontaneous-healing question — ARP cut volumetric loss from 106.41 to 62.66 mm³ (P=0.004).
- Confirms phenotype modifiers: **thick FSTT and thick buccal bone** independently predict less HW-1 loss in both groups.
- Flags a flap effect: in the **SH group, flapless** management preserved more ridge width than flap elevation (P<0.001) — consistent with the flap-vs-flapless literature.
- Links ARP to **downstream simplification**: PES improvement (11.6 vs 9.9, P=0.007) and a trend toward fewer additional grafting procedures (9% vs 26%, P=0.11).

## Methodology

- **Design**: Retrospective comparative, single center (Catholic University, Rome). 45 patients (from 58 eligible; 13 lost). Allocation by patient choice (non-randomized).
- **SH group**: socket curetted/irrigated, margins stabilized with 4-0 PGA suture, healing by secondary intention.
- **ARP group**: alveolus packed with low-temperature-processed cortico-spongious bovine granules to the coronal bone margin, covered with resorbable bovine pericardium membrane under buccal/lingual flap, compressive sutures.
- **Imaging/measures**: CBCT at T0 (−1 wk) and T1 (+6 mo), superimposed for volume; HW-1/3/5, buccal/palatal vertical height; preoperative FSTT (thick >1 mm / thin ≤1 mm) and buccal bone thickness (thick/thin).
- **Other outcomes**: PES at 1 year post-prosthesis; patient pain and satisfaction; need for additional grafting before/at implant placement; 1-year implant survival.
- **Stats**: paired (intragroup) and independent-samples (intergroup) t-tests, STATA, α=0.05.

## Results

| Outcome (6 mo unless noted) | SH (n=23) | ARP (n=22) | P |
|---|---|---|---|
| HW-1 loss (mm) | 2.03 ± 0.54 | 0.86 ± 0.49 | <0.001 |
| HW-3 loss (mm) | 1.35 ± 0.50 | 0.55 ± 0.42 | <0.001 |
| HW-5 loss (mm) | 0.94 ± 0.46 | 0.46 ± 0.37 | <0.001 |
| Volumetric loss (mm³) | 106.41 ± 24.32 | 62.66 ± 17.50 | 0.004 |
| Buccal vertical loss (mm) | 0.9 ± 0.7 | 0.31 ± 0.1 | <0.05 |
| Palatal vertical loss (mm) | 0.75 ± 0.04 | 0.25 ± 0.03 | <0.05 |
| PES (1 yr) | 9.9 ± 1.8 | 11.6 ± 2.2 | 0.007 |
| Patient satisfaction (12 mo) | 8.1 ± 1.1 | 8 ± 1.2 | 0.79 (NS) |
| Additional grafting needed | 6/23 (26%) | 2/22 (9%) | 0.11 (NS) |
| 1-yr implant survival | 100% | 100% | — |

- **Modifiers**: thick FSTT and thick buccal bone → less HW-1 loss (both groups). SH flapless → less loss (P<0.001); ARP complications → more loss (P<0.001).
- **Complications**: 2 ARP patients (9%) delayed wound healing, no infection/adjunctive therapy; no peri-implantitis; pain equivalent (P=0.89).

**Clinical bottom line**: ARP with xenograft + resorbable membrane meaningfully limits both horizontal and volumetric post-extraction shrinkage and improves peri-implant esthetics in single-tooth sites, at no cost in pain or satisfaction — but the reduction in later grafting (9% vs 26%) did not reach significance in this small, non-randomized sample. Thick phenotype and flapless extraction (in SH) are protective modifiers.
