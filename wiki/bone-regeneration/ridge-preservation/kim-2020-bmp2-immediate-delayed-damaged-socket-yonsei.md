## One-line Summary

Beagle dog study (n=5): immediate BMP-2 application in damaged extraction sockets produced significantly more new bone than 2-week delayed injection (10.8 vs 6.3 mm², p=0.043), with no ridge-width difference.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

This preclinical study by the Yonsei University Periodontology group compared immediate versus 2-week delayed application of BMP-2 solution in surgically damaged extraction sockets in beagle dogs. After creating standardized buccal bone defects (4 mm × 9 mm) at mandibular premolars, the control group received CBCP soaked in BMP-2 immediately at surgery, while the test group received saline-soaked CBCP with BMP-2 injected 2 weeks later. Both groups were sacrificed 2 weeks post-grafting. Immediate BMP-2 application yielded significantly greater new bone formation (10.8 ± 7.0 mm² vs 6.3 ± 3.1 mm²; p=0.043). Inflammatory reactions were more pronounced in the immediate group early on, but this did not translate into inferior outcomes. Ridge width was comparable between groups.

## Key Contributions

- Provides the only direct temporal comparison of BMP-2 delivery timing in damaged socket ARP
- Challenges the hypothesis that delayed BMP-2 injection (after initial inflammation subsides) would be superior
- Supports immediate biologics loading as the preferred protocol when BMP-2 is used with CBCP in ridge preservation

## Methodology

- **Model**: 5 beagle dogs, bilateral split-mouth (10 extraction sites)
- **Defect**: 4 mm wide × 9 mm high buccal bone defect, mandibular 4th premolar distal root
- **Control**: CBCP + BMP-2 immediate (at time of extraction/grafting)
- **Test**: CBCP + saline immediate; BMP-2 injection at 2 weeks
- **Follow-up**: Sacrifice at 2 weeks post-grafting
- **Assessments**: Clinical (swelling, inflammation), radiographic (ridge width), histomorphometric (new bone area)
- **Limitation**: Abstract-only; sample size small (n=5); short observation; single BMP-2 concentration

## Results

| Outcome | Immediate BMP-2 | Delayed BMP-2 | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| New bone area (mm²) | 10.8 ± 7.0 | 6.3 ± 3.1 | 0.043 |
| Ridge width | No significant difference | — | NS |
| Swelling/inflammation at 2 wk | More pronounced | Less | — |

- Delayed BMP-2 offered no histomorphometric advantage despite reduced early inflammation
- Authors conclude immediate application is preferred for BMP-2 in ARP of damaged sockets
