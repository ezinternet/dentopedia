## One-line Summary
Narrative review: combining autologous platelet-rich fibrin (PRF) with bone grafts in alveolar ridge preservation (ARP) accelerates soft- and hard-tissue healing via growth-factor release (PDGF/TGF-β/VEGF/IGF) and angiogenesis; ~50% of implant sites overall (75% in the anterior maxilla) require augmentation, and PRF is framed as a bio-enhancer of scaffolds rather than a standalone graft.

## Summary
Narrative review by Ucer and Khan (Salford, UK) on why socket augmentation (SA) matters and how autologous PRF acts as a bio-enhancer of healing when combined with xenografts, allografts, or alloplasts. Post-extraction the alveolar ridge undergoes physiological remodeling — most aggressively at the buccal plate — and roughly 50% of implant sites (75% in the anterior maxilla) require some form of bone augmentation. PRF, the second-generation platelet concentrate, releases PDGF/TGF-β/VEGF/IGF and supports angiogenesis and tissue regeneration; the review argues for its routine adjunctive use during SA when immediate implant placement (IIP) is contraindicated.

## Key Contributions
- Restates the clinical rationale and timing decisions for SA vs. IIP.
- Frames PRF as a biological enhancer of conventional bio-scaffolds rather than a standalone graft.
- Highlights the 6–8 month integration window for xeno/allo/alloplast scaffolds and how PRF may accelerate early phases.

## Methodology
- Narrative review including RCTs and case reports.
- Two illustrative clinical cases described.
- No meta-analysis; selection of literature reflects authors' clinical perspective.

## Results
- ~50% of implants overall and ~75% in anterior maxilla require augmentation.
- Buccal bone thickness <2 mm is a key risk factor for severe post-extraction collapse (citing Maia 2016).
- PRF + scaffold reportedly improves early soft-tissue closure and bone formation across cited studies (no pooled numbers).
