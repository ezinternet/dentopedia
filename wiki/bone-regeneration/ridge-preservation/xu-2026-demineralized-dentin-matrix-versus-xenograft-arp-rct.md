## Summary

This single-center, single-blind RCT (n=50) compared autogenous demineralized dentin matrix (DDM, chairside-processed from the patient's own extracted tooth, 400–800 µm particles) against deproteinized bovine bone mineral (DBBM/Bio-Oss) for alveolar ridge preservation after single-tooth extraction (premolar/molar). Both groups received flapless extraction, graft, Bio-Gide collagen membrane, and crisscross sutures, with CBCT at post-op/3 mo/6 mo and 6-month core biopsies (5/group).

DDM resorbed faster early — at 3 months the mid-socket (50%) horizontal width loss was significantly greater than DBBM (0.77 vs 0.44 mm, p=0.006). However, by 6 months **no level differed significantly** (horizontal coronal/middle/apical and vertical buccal/lingual all p>0.05): DDM's dimensional outcome was equivalent to the xenograft gold standard. Histology distinguished the two materials — DDM particles were actively remodeling (osteoclastic lacunae) and fused with mature, vascularized new bone (dense Ki-67, strong SATB2), whereas DBBM was a quiescent, non-resorbing osteoconductive scaffold. All sockets were adequately preserved for implant placement with satisfactory primary stability. The authors conclude DDM is a biologically viable, autogenous, cost-effective ("waste-to-resource") ARP alternative whose early contraction reflects physiological creeping substitution rather than failure.

## Key Contributions

- Direct head-to-head RCT of autogenous DDM vs gold-standard DBBM for ARP, with combined CBCT dimensional + 6-month histomorphometric/immunohistochemical readout.
- Establishes a kinetics distinction: DDM contracts faster early (3 mo) but matches DBBM at 6 mo.
- Histological evidence of DDM biodegradation and superior osteogenic integration (Ki-67, SATB2, osteoclastic lacunae) vs the inert DBBM scaffold.
- Standardized H_ref proportional CBCT protocol (fixed 25/50/75% levels) reducing crestal-resorption measurement bias.

## Methodology

- Prospective single-blind RCT, n=50 (DDM 25 / DBBM 25); single non-restorable premolar/molar, ≥3 mm apical bone; SNOSE allocation concealment; blinded radiographic/histological assessors.
- DDM: extracted tooth cleaned, processed (TSBK-ATB-01) to 400–800 µm partially demineralized particles. DBBM: Bio-Oss. Both: flapless extraction + Bio-Gide membrane + crisscross sutures.
- CBCT T0/T1(3 mo)/T2(6 mo) in Mimics 21.0; horizontal width at fixed 25/50/75% levels, vertical buccal/lingual height. Landry healing index at 7 d/3 mo/6 mo. 6-month bone-core histology (HE, Ki-67, SATB2) in 10 patients (5/group). SPSS v27; intra-examiner ICC >0.9; p<0.05.

## Results

- **3 mo**: mid-socket (50%) horizontal loss DDM 0.77 ± 0.59 vs DBBM 0.44 ± 0.45 mm — **significant (p=0.006)**. Other levels NS (apical p=0.069, coronal p=0.684; vertical buccal p=0.197, lingual p=0.846).
- **6 mo (all NS)**: vertical buccal 2.12 vs 1.72 mm (p=0.205); vertical lingual 1.47 vs 1.21 mm (p=0.238); coronal 2.10 vs 1.95 mm (p=0.628); middle 1.42 vs 1.14 mm (p=0.426); apical 0.85 vs 0.73 mm (p=0.118).
- **Soft tissue (Landry 7 d)**: DDM 3.88 vs DBBM 3.76, NS (p=0.419); no infection/rejection.
- **Histology**: DDM = active remodeling, mature new bone fused with particles, osteoclastic lacunae, dense Ki-67 + strong SATB2. DBBM = quiescent, new bone along particle surfaces, no particle resorption.
- **Clinical**: all sockets preserved; all implants placed with satisfactory primary stability; no dropouts.
- **Limitations**: small sample (histology n=10), 6-month follow-up only, single-center/single-surgeon, surgeon unblinded.
