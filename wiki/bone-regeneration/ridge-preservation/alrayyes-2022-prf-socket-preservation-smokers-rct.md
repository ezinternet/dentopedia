## One-line Summary

Four-arm single-blind RCT (n=18 heavy smokers, 40 upper molar sockets) comparing A-PRF, sticky bone+A-PRF, FDBA+collagen membrane, and resorbable collagen plug for soft-tissue healing: A-PRF groups significantly superior on mesio-distal closure (p=0.012) and healing index (p<0.0001); collagen plug alone was worst.

## 한줄요약

흡연자 대상 4군 RCT (n=18, 소켓 40개): A-PRF 및 A-PRF+sticky bone이 콜라겐 플러그 단독 대비 유의하게 우수한 연조직 폐합 (p=0.012) 및 치유 지수 (p<0.0001) — 흡연 환경에서 PRF의 부분적 보상 효과 확인.

## Summary

This single-blind RCT is among the first to evaluate platelet-rich fibrin specifically in heavy smokers (≥10 cigarettes/day) undergoing upper molar extraction and alveolar ridge preservation. Eighteen smokers contributing 40 extraction sockets were randomized to four groups: A-PRF alone, sticky bone (FDBA + autologous fibrin glue) covered with A-PRF, FDBA + crosslinked collagen membrane, or resorbable collagen plug alone. Over 28 days, both PRF groups showed significantly superior soft-tissue closure (mesio-distally) and healing index scores. The collagen plug alone group showed the least favorable outcomes, and PRF partially compensated for smoking-related impaired healing.

## Key Contributions

- First (or among very few) RCTs examining PRF in heavy smokers for ARP — fills a gap left by trials that exclude smokers (e.g., Kollati 2019)
- Four-arm design directly compares PRF formulations, conventional graft, and passive plug within the same population
- Reports patient-reported experience measures (PREMs): pain, swelling, satisfaction
- A-PRF vs A/S-PRF not significantly different — sticky bone formulation does not add soft-tissue benefit over A-PRF alone in smokers
- Collagen plug alone is inferior in smokers — passive socket coverage insufficient in the smoking context

## Methodology

**Design**: Single-blind, parallel-group, 4-arm RCT; socket-level randomization.

**Setting**: Dental University Hospital, King Saud University, Riyadh; June 2021 – April 2022; NCT05161455.

**Participants**: 18 current heavy smokers (≥10 cigs/day), ≥21 years, upper molar requiring extraction, 4-wall socket, adequate oral hygiene.

**Groups**:
- Group I (A-PRF): Advanced PRF alone (200 g × 14 min centrifugation)
- Group II (A/S-PRF): Sticky bone — FDBA 250–1000 µm + autologous fibrin glue + A-PRF membrane
- Group III (FDBA/CM): FDBA + crosslinked collagen membrane (positive control)
- Group IV (RCP): Resorbable collagen plug alone (negative control)

**Follow-up**: Baseline, 10, 21, 28 days.

**Primary outcome**: Socket mesio-distal soft-tissue closure (periodontal probe measurement).

**Secondary outcomes**: Healing index (0–3 scale); PREMs questionnaire at each visit.

## Results

| Outcome | A-PRF | A/S-PRF | FDBA/CM | RCP |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mesio-distal closure (p) | p=0.012 (sig.) | p=0.012 (sig.) | intermediate | worst |
| Healing pattern index | p<0.0001 | p<0.0001 | intermediate | least favorable |
| PREMs (comfort) | favorable | favorable | moderate | less favorable |

Key findings:
- Both PRF groups significantly outperformed collagen plug (RCP) on all healing parameters
- A-PRF and A/S-PRF were statistically equivalent — sticky bone formulation offers no additional soft-tissue healing benefit at 28 days in smokers
- FDBA/CM performed between PRF groups and collagen plug
- No radiographic bone data at 28-day endpoint (soft tissue only)
