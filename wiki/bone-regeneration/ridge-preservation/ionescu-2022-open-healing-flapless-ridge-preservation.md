Prospective study (Biology 2022): n=104 patients, 135 extractions, open-healing flapless ARP (DBBM + intentionally exposed collagen membrane) — 5-year implant survival 98.5%, success 94.8%; CBCT confirms 3D ridge preservation with minimally invasive technique.

## Summary
Prospective case series (n=104, 135 teeth, 5-year follow-up) validating an "open healing" flapless ridge preservation protocol using deproteinized bovine bone mineral (DBBM) + resorbable collagen membrane, intentionally left exposed during healing. Demonstrates excellent implant outcomes and ridge preservation without flap elevation.

## Key Contributions
- **Flapless protocol**: no flap raised at extraction or grafting — minimized surgical trauma
- **Open healing**: collagen membrane intentionally exposed (not closed primarily) — safe with collagen
- DBBM (deproteinized bovine bone mineral) granules fill socket
- N1=26 immediate implants; N2=109 delayed implants; all tissue-level
- **Implant survival: 98.5% at 5 years**
- **Implant success: 94.8% at 5 years**
- CBCT at 6 months confirms 3D ridge dimension preservation (width + height)
- Uneventful healing — no major soft-tissue complications despite membrane exposure

## Protocol Summary
1. Atraumatic tooth extraction (no flap)
2. Socket filled with DBBM granules
3. Collagen membrane placed over socket — **purposely left exposed**
4. CBCT at 6 months → assess bone volume
5. Immediate (N1) or delayed (N2) implant placement

## Why Open Healing Works
- Collagen membranes are biocompatible and resorb spontaneously → exposure not critical
- Avoids primary closure tension → less tissue trauma
- DBBM slow resorption maintains space for bone formation
- Flapless approach preserves periosteal blood supply
