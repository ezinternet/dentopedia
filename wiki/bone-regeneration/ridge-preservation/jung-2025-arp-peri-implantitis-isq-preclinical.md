## One-line Summary
Beagle study (n=6): ARP and tooth infection status had no significant effect on implant ISQ, radiographic bone loss, or histological bone composition; no correlation between newly formed bone and ISQ — ARP does not improve initial implant stability or retard peri-implantitis progression in this model (JPIS 2025).

## Summary
Secondary analysis of a beagle dog preclinical study examining whether alveolar ridge preservation (ARP) vs spontaneous healing (SH), and infected (IT) vs non-infected teeth (NIT), affect implant stability (ISQ) and progression of experimental peri-implantitis. Six beagle dogs; bilateral mandibular premolars randomly assigned to four groups. No significant differences were found among groups for ISQ, radiographic marginal bone loss, or histological bone composition. Linear regression showed no significant correlation between newly formed bone and ISQ in any group (all p>0.05). A weak correlation between newly formed bone and marginal bone loss was observed only in the IT/SH group (p=0.036).

## Key Contributions
- ARP does not confer measurable benefit in implant primary stability (ISQ) or resistance to peri-implantitis progression in this animal model
- Infection status of the extracted tooth (IT vs NIT) does not significantly influence peri-implantitis susceptibility after implant placement
- Newly formed bone quantity (histological) does not reliably predict ISQ — challenges ISQ as a direct bone-quality proxy in this context
- Weak but significant bone-MBL correlation only in IT/SH suggests infected-site spontaneous healing may have unique bone remodeling characteristics

## Methodology
Beagle dogs (n=6), bilateral mandibular 3rd/4th premolars. Four groups: IT/SH, IT/ARP, NIT/SH, NIT/ARP. Core biopsies at implant placement; ISQ measured (RFA). 3-month peri-implantitis induction → 3-month spontaneous progression. Radiographs at each timepoint. Linear regression analysis.

## Results
- ISQ differences among groups: not significant
- Radiographic MBL among groups: not significant
- Histological composition (bone substitute %, new bone %, fibrovascular connective tissue %): not significant
- New bone ↔ ISQ correlation: p>0.05 (all groups)
- New bone ↔ MBL correlation: p=0.036 in IT/SH group only (weak)
