## 한줄요약
체계적 문헌고찰 및 메타분석(7편) + 시험 순차 분석(Trial Sequential Analysis, TSA): 발치 후 동종/이종골 이식 + 흡수성 차폐막 조합이 자연치유 대비 치조제 수평 폭 흡수를 2.19 mm, 수직 높이 흡수를 1.72 mm 감소시키며, TSA로 결론이 충분히 입증됨(이질성 높음에도).

## Summary
This systematic review, meta-analysis, and trial sequential analysis evaluated alveolar ridge preservation (ARP) using allogenic/xenogenic bone graft combined with a resorbable membrane versus spontaneous healing after extraction. Across 7 studies, the combination significantly reduced horizontal ridge width reduction by 2.19 mm (95% CI −2.67 to −1.71) and vertical ridge height reduction by 1.72 mm (95% CI −2.14 to −1.30). TSA confirmed that the body of evidence has reached the required information size to validate this benefit despite substantial heterogeneity in graft and membrane choices.

## Key Contributions
- Among the first ARP meta-analyses to apply TSA, providing power-adjusted evidence sufficiency rather than relying on pooled P-values alone.
- Establishes ~2 mm horizontal and ~1.7 mm vertical preservation benefit as a clinically usable expectation.
- Concludes that further RCTs are not strictly necessary to confirm overall efficacy, redirecting research toward graft × membrane optimization.

## Methodology
- Independent electronic database search; inclusion of comparative trials of allogenic/xenogenic graft + resorbable membrane vs spontaneous healing.
- Primary outcomes: horizontal ridge width reduction (HRWR) and vertical ridge height reduction (VRHR) in mm; secondary: volume change (VC).
- Random-effects inverse-variance meta-analysis; heterogeneity assessed.
- Trial sequential analysis with monitoring boundaries to adjust for type I/II errors and required information size.

## Results
- 7 studies pooled.
- HRWR (combination vs control): −2.19 mm (95% CI −2.67 to −1.71 mm).
- VRHR: −1.72 mm (95% CI −2.14 to −1.30 mm).
- VC: insufficient data for synthesis.
- TSA boundaries crossed: evidence base sufficient despite high heterogeneity (graft type, membrane type, site, follow-up).
