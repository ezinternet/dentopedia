## Summary
Bassir and colleagues conducted a systematic review with meta-analysis of 21 controlled studies that compared alveolar ridge preservation (ARP) procedures against extraction alone. Pooled across studies, ARP yielded a horizontal ridge dimensional advantage of **1.86 mm (95% CI 1.44 – 2.28; P < .001)** over unassisted socket healing. Six of seven dimensional outcome variables significantly favored ARP. Crucially, subgroup analyses showed that the magnitude of benefit is not uniform — it depends on **wound closure type, grafting material, barrier membrane, growth factor adjunct, and socket morphology**.

## Key Contributions
- Establishes a defensible numerical effect size (~1.86 mm horizontal preservation) clinicians can quote when counseling patients about ARP.
- Demonstrates ARP superiority across multiple ridge dimensions (not only horizontal width).
- Identifies five modifiable/observable factors that change ARP outcome:
  - Wound closure (primary vs open)
  - Type of grafting material
  - Use of a barrier membrane
  - Use of growth factors / biologics
  - Socket morphology (intact vs damaged buccal plate)
- Reinforces that **socket morphology is the most decisive modifier** (P < .001 for interaction).

## Methodology
- PICO design: P = postextraction sockets in humans; I = ARP (graft ± membrane ± growth factor); C = extraction alone; O = hard tissue dimensional change (mm).
- Twenty-one studies met inclusion. Random-effects meta-analysis for seven outcome variables, plus prespecified subgroup analyses.
- Outcomes: horizontal ridge change, buccal vertical change, lingual/palatal vertical change, midcrestal change, and additional dimensional metrics.
- Subgroup variables: wound closure technique, flap elevation, graft type, barrier membrane, growth factor, socket morphology, tooth position.

## Results
- **Horizontal ridge change**: ARP advantage **1.86 mm (95% CI 1.44 – 2.28; P < .001)**.
- Six of seven hard-tissue outcomes significant in favor of ARP.
- Significant modifier P-values for horizontal outcome:
  - Wound closure: P = .033
  - Grafting material type: P = .001
  - Barrier membrane: P = .006
  - Growth factor: P = .003
  - Socket morphology: P < .001
- Flap elevation and tooth position (anterior vs posterior) were not significant interaction terms in the pooled horizontal analysis.
