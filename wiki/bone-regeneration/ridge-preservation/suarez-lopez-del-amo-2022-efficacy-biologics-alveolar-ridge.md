## One-line Summary

AAP Best Evidence Consensus systematic review of 39 RCTs (qualitative only, heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis) concluding biologics (ABP, EMD, rhPDGF-BB, rhBMP-2) generally do not yield superior clinical/radiographic ARP/ARR/ARA/MSFA outcomes versus controls, but improve histomorphometric bone formation and wound healing.

## 한줄요약

39편 RCT의 AAP Best Evidence Consensus SR(이질성으로 정량분석 불가)로, biologics(ABP·EMD·rhPDGF-BB·rhBMP-2)는 ARP/ARR/ARA/상악동거상 임상·방사선 결과에서 대조군 대비 우월하지 않았으나, 골조직형태계측상 신생골 형성과 창상치유는 개선했다.

## Summary

This American Academy of Periodontology Best Evidence Consensus systematic review assesses four biologic classes (autologous blood-derived products, enamel matrix derivative, rhPDGF-BB, rhBMP-2) across alveolar ridge preservation/reconstruction and implant site development (ridge augmentation, sinus floor augmentation). The qualitative synthesis of 39 RCTs concludes that biologics deliver clinical and radiographic outcomes comparable to conventional protocols, but consistently improve histomorphometric (new bone) outcomes and accelerate wound healing, with no severe adverse events.

## Key Contributions

- Codifies the AAP consensus stance on biologics across ARP/ARR, ARA, and MSFA as distinct indications.
- Separates "no clinical/radiographic superiority" from "improved histomorphometry and healing."
- Establishes a safety baseline (no severe adverse events).

## Methodology

- AAP Best Evidence Consensus systematic review; MEDLINE/EMBASE/CENTRAL, Jan 2000–Nov 2021.
- RCTs, English, ≥10 sites/arm, follow-up ≥2 mo (ARP/ARR) or ≥4 mo (MSFA/ARA); Cochrane RoB1.
- 39 articles; qualitative synthesis only — high heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis.

## Results

- ARP/ARR: biologics ≈ conventional; vs unassisted/collagen-sponge, biologics attenuated atrophy; histomorphometry improved.
- ARA: no clinical/radiographic superiority; ABPs increased new bone and reduced early dehiscence.
- MSFA: no clinical/radiographic superiority; some earlier-stage bone formation.
- PROMs (4 trials): modest pain/swelling benefit. No severe adverse events.
