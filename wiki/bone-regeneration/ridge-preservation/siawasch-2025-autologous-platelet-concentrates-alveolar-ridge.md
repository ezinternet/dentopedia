## Summary

This Periodontology 2000 systematic review with meta-analyses evaluates autologous platelet concentrates (APC) used as a single biomaterial during alveolar ridge preservation (ARP). Against unassisted (blood-clot) healing, APC alone (PRP, PRGF, and the L-PRF family) consistently produced faster soft-tissue healing, less post-extraction pain, reduced ridge resorption, more socket bone fill, and higher bone density. The magnitude of benefit was heavily modulated by surgical approach, and head-to-head comparisons against other biomaterials remain too sparse to conclude.

## Key Contributions

- Isolates APC-alone (no co-graft) effect in ARP, separating PRP / PRGF / L-PRF.
- Stratifies by surgical approach (flap vs flapless; fill vs fill-and-cover).
- Synthesizes hard-tissue, soft-tissue, and pain endpoints from RCT-only data.

## Methodology

- SR with meta-analyses; PubMed, EMBASE, Web of Science, Scopus to Dec 2023.
- 35 papers / 33 studies: 1 PRP, 6 PRGF, 28 L-PRF family.
- RCTs ≥10 healthy humans, split-mouth or parallel; non-third-molar sites; APC-plus-other-material combinations excluded.

## Results

- APC alone vs unassisted: faster soft-tissue healing, less post-extraction pain, less resorption, more bone fill, higher density in most studies.
- Benefit significantly influenced by surgical approach.
- APC vs other biomaterials: inconclusive (limited literature).
- Cited reference benchmarks: post-extraction horizontal resorption ~2.7 mm (95% CI 2.4–3.1), vertical ~1.7 mm (95% CI 1.3–2.1); ARP (Cochrane) width MD −1.2 mm and height MD −1.4 mm vs unassisted.
