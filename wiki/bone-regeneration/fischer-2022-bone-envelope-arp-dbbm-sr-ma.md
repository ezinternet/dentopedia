## Summary

Fischer et al. (2022) conducted a systematic review and meta-analysis to address the clinically critical question: after alveolar ridge preservation (ARP) with DBBM, can standard-diameter implants be placed without further bone augmentation? Nine RCTs meeting PRISMA criteria were included, encompassing 177 implant sites that underwent ARP with DBBM (with or without membrane) and 130 spontaneous healing (SH) controls.

The primary finding was a mean ridge width difference of **1.13 mm** favoring ARP with DBBM (95% CI 0.28–1.98, I² = 68%, p < 0.01). While this is statistically significant, the clinical translation into augmentation-free implant placement is limited. Probability calculations showed that only **6–19% of ARP sites** would have ≥2 mm circumferential bone for augmentation-free placement, depending on implant diameter (3.25 mm: 19%; 4.0 mm: 14%; 5.0 mm: 6%).

The clinical message is sobering: **4 out of 5 cases** following ARP with DBBM will still require additional bone augmentation at implant placement or the procedure was unnecessary. ARP reduces ridge shrinkage and improves the odds marginally, but cannot routinely avoid staged augmentation.

## Key Contributions

- First SR+MA framing ARP outcomes as **probability of augmentation-free implant placement** (not just dimensional change).
- Calculated risk differences (RD) and NNT for three standard implant diameters.
- Mean width gain: 1.13 mm (modest but statistically significant; substantial heterogeneity I² = 68%).
- Probability hierarchy: smaller implants benefit more from ARP (3.25 mm: 19% vs 5.0 mm: 6%).

## Methodology

- Databases: PubMed, Embase, CENTRAL (2013–August 2022)
- PICO: P = extraction patients; I = DBBM ± membrane; C = spontaneous healing; O = probability of ≥2 mm bone housing without additional GBR
- Inclusion: RCTs only, ≥10 participants, DBBM test vs SH control
- n = 9 RCTs included; initial screen: 2583 studies
- Statistical method: random-effects meta-analysis, risk difference calculation per implant diameter

## Results

| Implant Diameter | Probability (no further augmentation) | p-value |
|---|---|---|
| 3.25 mm | 19% (RD 0.19, CI 0.06–0.32) | <0.01 |
| 4.0 mm | 14% (RD 0.14, CI 0.05–0.23) | <0.01 |
| 5.0 mm | 6% (RD 0.06, CI 0.00–0.12) | 0.06 |

Ridge width gain: 1.13 mm (95% CI 0.28–1.98; I² = 68%)

Heterogeneity was substantial, driven by variation in DBBM formulation (plain vs collagenated), membrane use, healing duration, and measurement methods across included RCTs.
