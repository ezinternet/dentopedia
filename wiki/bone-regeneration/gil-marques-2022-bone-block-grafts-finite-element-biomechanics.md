## One-line Summary

Finite-element analysis crossing two bone-block graft designs (right-angled vs rounded) with two suture tensions (0.05 vs 0.2 N): all soft-tissue stress/deformation/displacement rose at 0.2 N; block design did not change transferred stress or deformation, but the right-angled/chamfer design produced ~25% greater mucosal displacement (dehiscence tendency), so low suture tension and a rounded block are recommended.

## Summary

This finite-element analysis (FEA) models the soft tissue overlying an autologous **bone-block graft** (Split Bone Block, SBB, technique) on the alveolar ridge during the early inflammatory healing phase, to predict the tendency toward **primary-closure failure / wound dehiscence**. Two independent variables were crossed: block-graft **morphology** (right-angled/chamfer vs rounded) and **suture tension** (0.05 N vs 0.2 N). Outputs were oral-mucosa displacement, transferred Von Mises stress, and soft-tissue deformation.

All three outputs were **greater at 0.2 N** than at 0.05 N suture tension. **Block design did not change transferred stress or deformation**, but the **right-angled/chamfer design produced ~25% greater mucosal displacement**, interpreted as a greater dehiscence tendency. The authors therefore recommend **low suture tension and a rounded block design** to reduce dehiscence risk. The clinical anchors are prior tension thresholds: De Stavola & Tunkel (2013) reported no dehiscence tendency below ~5 g, and Burkardt & Lang (2009) found 0.01–0.15 N gave lower dehiscence while >0.1 N significantly increased it. As a computational model, this is a hypothesis-generating framework rather than clinical validation.

## Key Contributions

- A novel FEA framework to quantify how block-graft shape and suture tension transfer load to the overlying mucosa.
- Decouples two surgeon-controllable factors (block edge shape, suture tension) from clinical confounders.
- Practical recommendation: round the block edges and keep suture tension low to protect primary closure.

## Methodology

- FEM simulation of mucosa over a block graft (SBB context), early inflammatory phase.
- Designs: right-angled (chamfer) vs rounded block.
- Suture tensions: 0.05 N vs 0.2 N.
- Outcomes: mucosal displacement, Von Mises stress, soft-tissue deformation.

## Results

- 0.2 N > 0.05 N for all of displacement, stress, and deformation.
- Right-angled vs rounded: no difference in transferred stress or deformation.
- Right-angled/chamfer: ~25% greater mucosal displacement (dehiscence tendency).
- Recommendation: low suture tension + rounded block design.
