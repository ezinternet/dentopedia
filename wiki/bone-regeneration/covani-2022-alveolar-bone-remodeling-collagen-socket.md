## Summary

This animal study used high-resolution micro-CT to quantify three-dimensional alveolar bone remodeling in 36 rabbits following lower incisor extraction, comparing collagen plug–filled sockets to empty sockets closed by suture. Animals were euthanized in groups of six at 2, 7, 15, 30, 60, and 90 days post-extraction, providing a kinetic profile of healing. By 90 days, the collagen plug group lost only −4.6% of baseline bone volume versus −58.1% in empty-socket controls. Despite collagen plug resorption within approximately 15 days, the volumetric protection was maintained throughout the 90-day observation, suggesting that early-phase clot stabilization and vascularization scaffolding — rather than space maintenance — are the primary mechanisms of collagen plug benefit.

## Key Contributions

- Provides 3D volumetric kinetics at 6 time-points — resolves when bone loss accelerates vs stabilizes after extraction
- Demonstrates that collagen plug (without bone graft) prevents socket collapse by ~12-fold vs empty socket at 90 days
- Establishes that the protective effect persists well beyond collagen resorption (~15 days) — mechanism is scaffold/clot stabilization, not physical volume
- Non-destructive micro-CT allows internal comparisons across all 6 time-points from same bone blocks
- Part of a larger study; companion histological and molecular analyses published separately

## Methodology

**Model**: 36 adult male New Zealand white rabbits (~2 kg), lower right or left incisor extraction.

**Groups**:
- Control: Empty socket, tension-free suture closure
- Test: Socket filled with native heterologous collagen type 1 (lyophilized sterile bovine, ACE plug); no suture

**Sacrifice time-points**: 2, 7, 15, 30, 60, 90 days (6 animals per group per time-point).

**Micro-CT imaging**:
- Scanner: IRIS (Inviscan Sas, Strasbourg); 59 µm isotropic voxel
- Parameters: 80 kV, 1 mA, 112 s scan
- Reconstruction: FBP with beam hardening correction
- Bone threshold: ~500 HU
- ROI: Cylindrical region, crest to 10 mm apical

**Primary endpoint**: % bone volume change (ΔBV) from baseline.

**Secondary**: Linear bucco-lingual dimension at 1 mm intervals.

**Sample size**: E-value method (E=24, slightly >20; adequate for ANOVA-type analysis with 10% mortality buffer).

## Results

| Time-point | Control ΔBV | Collagen Plug ΔBV |
|---|---|---|
| 15 days | Progressive collapse beginning | Minimal change |
| 30 days | Ongoing significant resorption | Near-stable |
| 60 days | Severe collapse | Minimal |
| 90 days | −58.1% | −4.6% |

- Collagen group maintained near-baseline bone volume throughout the entire 90-day period
- Buccal plate collapse in the control group became evident by day 15
- Volumetric protection persisted after collagen resorption (days 15–90) — clot stabilization is the primary mechanism
- Linear bucco-lingual dimension significantly smaller losses in collagen group at all time-points
