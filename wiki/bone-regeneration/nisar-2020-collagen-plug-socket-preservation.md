## Summary

This split-mouth prospective study evaluated collagen plug (KOLSPON) saturated with platelet-rich plasma (PRP) versus extraction alone in 30 patients requiring bilateral extractions with matching root anatomy. The primary radiographic outcome (crestal bone height via RVG) was significantly better in the test group at both 3 months and 6 months (p<0.001). However, alveolar ridge width, measured with bone calipers at three levels, did not differ significantly between groups (p>0.05). This indicates that a collagen plug with PRP effectively prevents vertical bone loss at the crest but is insufficient for horizontal width preservation, supporting its use as an adjunct rather than a complete ARP solution when ridge width maintenance is required.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates height preservation benefit of collagen plug + PRP with a simple, low-cost, widely available technique
- Exposes the limitation: width is not preserved — collagen plug alone cannot replace buccal plate support
- PRP prepared with minimal blood volume (2 mL) and double centrifugation — practical in routine oral surgery settings
- Supports collagen plug as the primary coverage strategy while underscoring the need for additional particulate graft for width maintenance

## Methodology

**Design**: Split-mouth prospective comparative study.

**Sample**: 30 patients (18–30 years), bilateral extractions with matching root anatomy in same arch. Exclusions: acute periapical pathology, medically compromised, radiation history.

**Randomization**: Even serial number → right quadrant = test; odd serial number → left quadrant = test.

**Test procedure**:
1. PRP preparation: 2 mL venous blood + sodium citrate → soft spin 2500 rpm × 10 min → buffy coat → hard spin 3400 rpm × 10 min → ~1 mL PRP → activated with 0.1 mL calcium gluconate
2. Atraumatic extraction (2% LOX, 1:200,000 adrenaline), socket curettage
3. KOLSPON collagen plug saturated with PRP inserted to crestal level
4. Sutured with 3-0 silk interrupted sutures

**Control procedure**: Extraction + suturing only.

**Outcome measurements**:
- *Width*: Bone calipers at crestal, mid-root, apical levels immediately post-extraction, 3 and 6 months
- *Height*: RVG with radiopaque millimeter grid; CD measurement (perpendicular distance from CEJ connecting line AB to lowest socket crest C)

**Follow-up visits**: Immediate post-extraction, 10 days (suture removal), 3 months, 6 months.

## Results

| Outcome | Test (Collagen + PRP) | Control | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Crestal bone height (3 months) | Significantly preserved | Progressive loss | p<0.001 |
| Crestal bone height (6 months) | Maintained | Continued loss | p<0.001 |
| Ridge width (3 months) | No significant change | No significant change | p>0.05 (NS) |
| Ridge width (6 months) | No significant change | No significant change | p>0.05 (NS) |

- Collagen + PRP significantly prevented vertical crestal bone loss
- No significant difference in width preservation — horizontal resorption continued in both groups
- No complications; uneventful soft-tissue healing at 10-day suture removal

## Clinical Implications

- Collagen plug + PRP is effective for crestal height maintenance — suitable as a simple ARP strategy where vertical preservation is the priority
- For sites requiring both height and width preservation (e.g., anterior esthetic zone, thin buccal plate), additional particulate graft is needed
- PRP can be prepared from 2 mL blood — feasible adjunct even in resource-limited settings
