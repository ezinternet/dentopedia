## One-line Summary

Animal study (21 Wistar rats, 4×2 mm cortical femoral metaphyseal defects, 4 treatments, 4/12/16 wk): a European-carp-skin EDC/NHS-crosslinked collagen membrane used ALONE trended toward higher healing scores, more new bone, and smaller residual defect (non-significant but large effect sizes) and was non-irritant throughout; pairing it with Bio-Oss gave no benefit and underperformed Bio-Oss + Bio-Gide.

## Summary

Bujda et al. (2026) tested a novel resorbable barrier membrane made from **European carp skin collagen** (EDC/NHS-crosslinked, gamma-sterilized) in a rat femoral cortical-defect model, the first in vivo bone-healing evaluation of this fish-derived material. The motivation is to sidestep the zoonotic, ethical/religious, and cost concerns of mammalian (porcine/bovine) collagen membranes. In standardized 4×2 mm metaphyseal defects assessed at 4, 12, and 16 weeks by micro-CT and histology, the **carp membrane used alone** consistently trended toward the best healing (highest median healing scores, most new bone, smallest residual defect) and was **non-irritant at all time points**, though most between-group differences were not statistically significant (limited by the non–critical-size defect, which heals substantially on its own). When combined with the bovine xenograft **Bio-Oss**, the carp membrane gave no added benefit and performed worse than the standard **Bio-Oss + Bio-Gide** pairing. The clinical implication suggested by the authors — restricted to this animal model — is that fish-derived collagen may be a promising GBR membrane where barrier function alone is sufficient, but it does not yet match an established porcine membrane in a graft-supported configuration. Findings require validation in large-animal critical-size defect / true augmentation models.

## Key Contributions

- First in vivo bone-healing test of a carp-skin collagen GBR membrane (prior work was soft-tissue/subcutaneous only).
- Direct comparison vs porcine Bio-Gide and bovine Bio-Oss in a fixed-site, four-treatment design.
- Reinforces that in a contained, non–critical-size defect, the membrane alone trends best and adding particulate graft (Bio-Oss) confers no benefit.

## Methodology

- 21 male Wistar rats (12 wk), 3 groups (n=7), euthanized at 4 / 12 / 16 wk.
- Four fixed-site cortical defects (4×2 mm) per animal: empty control; carp membrane alone; Bio-Oss + carp membrane; Bio-Oss + Bio-Gide.
- Carp membrane: 4 wt% collagen, lyophilized, EDC/NHS crosslinked (4/1), 25 kGy gamma, 8×6×2 mm.
- Outcomes: micro-CT healing score (1–4), histology Han score (0–40), Lindner-adapted soft-tissue irritancy. Non-parametric stats (Kruskal–Wallis/Dunn's), medians/IQR, effect sizes η².
- Femoral fractures in 4 animals + multiple defect exclusions reduced evaluable n (<7 in several cells).

## Results

- **micro-CT**: no significant between-group difference at any time (4 wk p=0.055 η²=0.20; 12 wk p=0.060 η²=0.19; 16 wk p=0.731). Carp-alone trended highest; only carp-alone showed significant temporal improvement (p=0.012).
- **Histology**: carp-alone highest at 4 wk (median 32/40; 5/6 defects 80–90% filled) and 12 wk (between-group p=0.020; carp-alone > Bio-Oss+carp post-hoc p=0.007; 5/6 ≥90% filled). New-bone subscore non-significant but large effect (12 wk η²=0.24, 16 wk η²=0.18). Untreated controls largely healed spontaneously by 16 wk (17→24.5→25), compressing differences.
- **Bio-Oss combos**: Bio-Oss+carp consistently lower than Bio-Oss+Bio-Gide (non-significant trend).
- **Soft tissue**: carp-alone non-irritant throughout (median 0,0,2); excellent biocompatibility. Between-group irritancy significant at 4 wk (p=0.025) and 12 wk (p=0.018).
- Histology distinguished groups earlier/more clearly than micro-CT (notably at 4 wk).

**Limitations**: non–critical-size defect (high spontaneous healing), small n, fracture-related exclusions, partial pseudoreplication (multiple defects per animal), metaphyseal four-walled defect not replicating clinical ridge augmentation. Animal-only — no clinical generalization.
