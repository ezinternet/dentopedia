## One-line Summary

Rat critical-size defect study (n=30 defects, 5 groups, 4 weeks): α-TCP or BBCP mixed 1:1 with DBBM rescued new bone formation to empty-defect levels; DBBM alone paradoxically showed significantly less new bone than the empty defect control (p=0.025).

## Summary

Fujioka-Kobayashi et al. (2022) tested whether adding resorbable synthetic bone substitutes (α-TCP or biphasic calcium-phosphate, BBCP) to DBBM improves bone formation in rat critical-size calvarial defects. Thirty defects across 15 rats were randomized to five groups: empty (NC), DBBM alone, α-TCP+DBBM (1:1), BBCP 3%HA/97%α-TCP+DBBM (1:1), BBCP 6%HA/94%α-TCP+DBBM (1:1), with 4-week healing.

The most surprising finding was that **DBBM alone resulted in significantly less new bone than the empty defect** (p=0.0252). All three synthetic additive combinations rescued new bone formation to levels comparable with the empty defect control. Micro-CT confirmed that the synthetic components actively resorbed (decreased material density) without significant volume loss, while DBBM remained largely non-resorbed.

The degree of HA coating (3% vs 6%) on the BBCP did not significantly influence bone formation or resorption over the 4-week observation period. This study suggests that hybrid grafting — DBBM providing space maintenance + resorbable synthetic providing early osteoconductive scaffold — may better balance the resorption/bone-formation equation than DBBM alone.

## Key Contributions

- Documented the **paradoxical short-term suppression** of bone formation by DBBM alone vs empty defect in a rat calvarial model.
- Demonstrated that adding resorbable synthetics (α-TCP, BBCP) restores early new bone formation to natural healing levels.
- Showed synthetic components resorb (density decrease) while maintaining volume at 4 weeks — no premature collapse of the augmented area.
- HA coating degree (3% vs 6%) had minimal effect on outcomes at 4 weeks.

## Methodology

- Model: Rat calvarial critical-size defects (bilateral, 2/rat, n=15 rats = 30 defects)
- Groups (n=6 each): NC (empty), DBBM alone, α-TCP+DBBM (1:1), BBCP 3%HA+DBBM (1:1), BBCP 6%HA+DBBM (1:1)
- Healing: 4 weeks
- Assessments: micro-CT (MV/TV, mineral density, horizontal defect closure) + histomorphometry (new bone area, residual material area)
- 1 defect excluded (dura mater injury); 29 defects analyzed

## Results

| Group | New bone vs NC | Mineral density vs DBBM | Volume change |
|---|---|---|---|
| DBBM alone | Significantly less (p=0.0252) | Reference | No significant change |
| α-TCP + DBBM | Similar to NC | Higher (p=0.0319) | No significant change |
| BBCP 3%HA + DBBM | Similar to NC | Higher (p=0.0050) | No significant change |
| BBCP 6%HA + DBBM | Similar to NC | Higher (p=0.0191) | No significant change |

All material groups showed greater MV/TV than empty defect, confirming osteoconductive scaffold function. BBCP 3% vs 6% HA: no significant differences.
