## One-line Summary

SR of 7 RCTs: PRF addition to DBBM/BioOss does not significantly improve histomorphometric outcomes in sinus lift, but may reduce graft resorption in mandibular GBR.

## 한줄요약

7편 RCT SR: PRF+DBBM은 상악동거상술 조직형태계측 지표에서 유의한 이점 없음; 하악 GBR에서 이식재 흡수 감소 가능성 시사.

## Summary

This systematic review (PROSPERO CRD42023411160, PRISMA 2020) evaluated the efficacy of adding PRF or L-PRF to bovine xenograft (DBBM/BioOss) in bone ridge augmentation procedures, specifically sinus floor elevation and horizontal/vertical alveolar ridge augmentation. Three databases (PubMed, Cochrane, Web of Science) were searched through March 2023. Seven RCTs met inclusion criteria. Across sinus lift studies, PRF+DBBM did not produce statistically significant improvements in new bone formation percentage, residual graft percentage, or soft tissue content compared to DBBM alone. One mandibular GBR study demonstrated significant reduction in graft resorption with PRF. High clinical heterogeneity in PRF preparation protocols prevented meta-analysis. The authors conclude that PRF adjunction to DBBM in sinus lift lacks sufficient evidence for routine recommendation, while cautiously noting potential benefit in horizontal ridge augmentation contexts.

## Key Contributions

- Confirms that PRF benefit in the DBBM-sinus lift context is not statistically demonstrated at SR level
- Distinguishes sinus lift vs. ridge augmentation contexts — PRF may behave differently depending on surgical site
- Highlights preparation heterogeneity (solid PRF vs L-PRF, variable centrifugation protocols) as a key source of inconsistency across RCTs
- Sets the groundwork for more targeted reviews: compare PRF subtypes (L-PRF, A-PRF) separately rather than pooling all PRF generations

## Methodology

- Design: Systematic review; PICOS framework; PRISMA 2020
- Inclusion: Adult patients; RCTs only; DBBM ± PRF/L-PRF in sinus lift or horizontal/vertical ridge augmentation; histomorphometric or radiological outcomes
- Exclusion: SR/MA, case reports, in vitro/in situ, non-DBBM grafts, non-PRF adjuncts
- Risk of bias: Cochrane Collaboration tool
- No meta-analysis due to heterogeneity

## Results

| Outcome | Sinus Lift | Mandibular GBR |
|---|---|---|
| New bone formation (%) | No significant difference (PRF vs control) | Variable |
| Residual graft (%) | No significant difference | Reduced with PRF (1 study) |
| Soft tissue (%) | Inconsistent | — |
| Radiological bone height | No consistent advantage | — |
| Implant survival | High in both groups | — |

Key finding: 7 RCTs analyzed; PRF+DBBM = DBBM alone in sinus lift across all primary histomorphometric endpoints.
