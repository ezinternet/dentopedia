## One-line Summary
RCT (n=32) comparing a gelatin/polylactic-acid (GT/PLA) barrier membrane vs commercial Guidor® PLA membrane for GBR; 100% implant survival both arms, no significant difference in bone resorption, osteogenic efficacy, soft-tissue index, wound healing, exposure, or infection at 6 months — GT/PLA non-inferior.

## Summary
A gelatin/PLA composite barrier membrane performed non-inferiorly to an established commercial bioresorbable PLA matrix (Guidor®) across CBCT bone-resorption volume, osteogenic efficacy, and soft-tissue/complication endpoints at 6 months. Numerically GT/PLA showed less resorption (148.5 vs 185.3 mm³) and higher osteogenic efficacy (75% vs 56%), but neither reached significance at 16/arm. Clinically this positions a low-cost synthetic composite as a viable alternative barrier where collagen membranes are the default, pending larger/histologic confirmation.

## Key Contributions
- First human clinical comparison of a gelatin/PLA composite vs commercial PLA matrix in GBR.
- Adds a synthetic-membrane data point to a collagen-dominated evidence base.

## Methodology
- 32 patients, single-tooth gap + alveolar defect; envelope randomization to GT/PLA vs Guidor® (n=16 each), both over bone substitute.
- CBCT at baseline and 6 months → resorption volume + osteogenic efficacy; STI, wound healing, exposure, infection recorded.

## Results
- Implant survival 100% both groups.
- Bone resorption 148.54±107.42 vs 185.25±85.31 mm³ (P=0.163, NS).
- Osteogenic efficacy 75% vs 56% (P=0.458, NS).
- STI, wound healing, exposure, infection: no significant difference (P>0.05).
