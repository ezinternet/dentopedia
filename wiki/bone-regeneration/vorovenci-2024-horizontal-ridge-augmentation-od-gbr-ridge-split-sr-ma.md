## One-line Summary

SR+MA (18 studies, 17 meta-analyzed as 21 groups; 336 patients / 665 implants; Jan 2017–Dec 2022) head-to-head ranking of horizontal ridge augmentation by mean bone gain: **GBR 4.04 mm > RS 3.66 mm > OD 2.15 mm** (ANOVA P=0.002), with implant survival ~99% for all three and OD applied to the widest ridges, RS to the narrowest.

## 한줄요약

수평 치조제 증대 3종을 직접 비교한 SR+MA(18편, 메타분석 17편, 환자 336명/임플란트 665개): 평균 수평 골증대량은 **가이드골재생(GBR) 4.04 mm > 치조제분할(RS) 3.66 mm > 골밀도화 치조제확장(OD) 2.15 mm** (P=0.002). 임플란트 생존율은 세 방법 모두 ~99%로 차이 없음. OD는 가장 넓은 치조제(4.37 mm)에, RS는 가장 좁은 치조제(3.43 mm)에 적용됨.

## Summary

This is the first meta-analysis to pool **osseodensification (OD) ridge expansion** and benchmark it head-to-head against **guided bone regeneration (GBR)** and the **ridge-split (RS) technique** for horizontal ridge augmentation. Across 17 meta-analyzed studies (split into 21 independent comparison groups; 336 patients, 665 implants), the mean horizontal bone gain ranked **GBR (4.036 mm) > RS (3.661 mm) > OD (2.151 mm)**, significant by ANOVA (P=0.002). The GBR↔RS difference was not significant (P=0.09 — the two overlap), whereas both clearly beat OD (GBR vs OD P=0.001; RS vs OD P=0.004).

Critically, the three techniques are applied to **different starting ridge widths**: OD is used on the widest ridges (4.37 mm), GBR on intermediate (3.87 mm), and RS on the narrowest (3.43 mm) — so OD's lower gain partly reflects that its real indication is low-density (D3/D4) bone, not the truly narrow ridge. **Implant survival was technique-independent (~99% all three)**, moving the modality decision onto bone-gain need, ridge width, complication profile and operator skill rather than survival. Autologous/mixed graft gave the largest gain (4.98 mm) vs no graft (2.47 mm). The authors conclude OD is a worthwhile, predictable, easy-to-apply addition to the lateral-augmentation toolkit, even though GBR and RS yield more bone.

## Key Contributions

- **Quantitative modality hierarchy for the narrow ridge**: GBR 4.04 > RS 3.66 > OD 2.15 mm (global 3.31 mm), P=0.002 — a single ranking to anchor technique selection.
- **First pooled OD horizontal-gain estimate** placed alongside the established GBR/RS literature.
- **Ridge-width context**: OD 4.37 mm (widest) → GBR 3.87 → RS 3.43 mm (narrowest); RS is the modality for the thinnest crests (RS vs OD initial width P=0.001).
- **Survival is not the deciding factor** (~99% for all), so choose on gain magnitude, ridge width, complications and skill.

## Methodology

- **Design**: PRISMA + STROBE SR+MA. PubMed-Medline, Cochrane CENTRAL, EMBASE, Cochrane Oral Health Group Trials Register, Web of Science + hand search; Jan 2017–Dec 2022 (window set by OD's recent emergence, ~2018). English, ≥10 subjects, pre/post ridge width + ≥6-week follow-up required.
- **Appraisal**: MINORS (mean 17/24 comparative; 12.44 single-arm) for non-randomized; Cochrane RoB for 6 RCTs (3 low, 2 medium risk). Reviewer Cohen κ=0.96.
- **Synthesis**: Comprehensive Meta-Analysis V3, random-effects (DerSimonian-Laird); multi-arm studies split into independent groups (17 studies → 21 groups). Meta-regression moderators: technique, graft type. Heterogeneity I²/Q/τ²; bias via funnel plots + Egger's test (all subgroup Egger P NS).
- **Funnel**: 2,205 hits → 18 included → 17 meta-analyzed (1 excluded: no implant count). Pools: OD 4 studies/5 groups (73 pt/93 implants), GBR 7/8 (149/334), RS 7/8 (105/229).

## Results

| Technique | Mean horizontal bone gain (95% CI) | Initial ridge width (95% CI) | Implant survival |
|---|---|---|---|
| **GBR** | **4.036 mm** (3.351–4.772) | 3.873 mm (3.418–4.327) | 99.7% |
| **RS (ridge-split)** | **3.661 mm** (2.991–4.399) | 3.433 mm (2.995–3.872) — narrowest | 98.7% |
| **OD (osseodensification)** | **2.151 mm** (1.327–2.975) | 4.373 mm (3.828–4.917) — widest | 98% |
| Global | 3.305 mm (2.211–4.399) | 3.875 mm (3.325–4.426) | 99.1% |

- **Bone gain ANOVA P=0.002**. Pairwise: GBR vs OD P=0.001; **GBR vs RS P=0.09 (NS, overlap)**; RS vs OD P=0.004. Heterogeneity I²=94.6% (GBR group bimodal: low 2.50 / high 4.99 mm, each I²=0%).
- **Initial-width ANOVA P=0.03**; RS vs OD P=0.001 (RS thinnest); GBR vs OD P=0.08; GBR vs RS P=0.18.
- **Graft moderator (P<0.001)**: autologous/mix 4.98 mm > synthetic 3.69 > xenogeneic 3.18 > allogeneic 2.73 > no-graft 2.47 mm. Autologous vs no-graft P<0.001; autologous vs xeno P=0.014; no-graft vs xeno P=0.248 (NS).
- **Complications** — GBR: membrane dehiscence/exposure 20% (commonest), oedema 9.5%, hematoma 3.8%, mucositis 0.9%. RS: site dehiscence 12.3%, paresthesia 7.7%, oedema 6.2%. OD: ease/predictability emphasized, GBR/RS operator-skill dependent.
- **Minimum-width indications cited**: GBR 3 mm, RS 2.5–3 mm; OD's primary indication is low-density bone (depends on basal bone 5–10 mm from crest), not narrow ridges per se.
