## Summary

This single-center pilot RCT (n=25) compared a partially demineralized allogeneic cancellous bone plug (ACBP) against the xenograft benchmark deproteinized bovine bone mineral (DBBM, Bio-Oss) for guided bone regeneration, with collagen membrane coverage in both arms. Two indications were pooled under one membrane-based protocol: posterior maxillary lateral-window sinus floor elevation and mandibular posterior extraction socket preservation. Outcomes at 6 months (implant placement) were CBCT-based dimensional change plus histomorphometry of a 3-mm trephine core.

ACBP and DBBM were statistically equivalent on CBCT dimensional stability (sinus height gain and socket ridge height/width) and on newly formed vital bone, but ACBP left significantly less residual graft material at re-entry. The authors interpret the lower residual graft as faster graft turnover rather than greater osteogenesis — external contour stability can mask material-specific remodeling behavior during intermediate healing.

## Key Contributions

- First direct head-to-head clinical comparison of a commercial partially demineralized allograft plug (ACBP) vs DBBM in GBR, combining 3D CBCT dimensional analysis with histomorphometry.
- Separates two distinct endpoints: contour/dimensional stability (comparable) vs internal tissue composition (residual graft differs, vital bone does not).
- Reframes "lower residual graft" as a turnover signal, not a proxy for more bone formation.

## Methodology

- **Design**: single-center prospective pilot RCT, CONSORT-reported, not prospectively registered; allocation concealed, outcome assessors and statistician blinded, surgeon unblinded.
- **n=25**: ACBP 12 (7 socket, 5 sinus), DBBM 13 (8 socket, 5 sinus). Adults 18–65 y needing GBR before implant.
- **Materials**: DBBM = Bio-Oss (Geistlich); ACBP = 9×9×12 mm allograft plug (Hubei Osteolink). Both with absorbable collagen membrane (Heal All); single surgeon; tension-free closure.
- **Primary**: histomorphometric residual graft (%) on 3-mm trephine core at 6 mo. **Secondary**: vital bone (%); CBCT sinus height gain; socket ridge height + width at 8/10/12 mm above mandibular canal.
- **Stats**: independent t / Welch tests, P<0.05; sinus subgroup ANCOVA adjusting baseline subantral height; no a priori power calculation.

## Results

- All 25 completed 6-mo follow-up; no intervention-related adverse events.
- **Vital bone**: ACBP 38.84 ± 8.37% vs DBBM 32.18 ± 11.04%, **P=0.105** (NS).
- **Residual graft (primary)**: ACBP 11.18 ± 5.48% vs DBBM 19.80 ± 8.23%, **P=0.006** (ACBP lower).
- **Sinus subgroup**: height gain 7.82 ± 1.52 vs 8.21 ± 4.30 mm, P=0.852 (ANCOVA-adjusted P=0.801); baseline subantral height imbalanced (2.82 vs 1.47 mm).
- **Socket subgroup**: ridge height change −0.30 vs +0.51 mm (P=0.079); ridge width change at 8/10/12 mm all NS (P=0.437 / 0.957 / 0.945).
- **Bottom line**: ACBP ≈ DBBM on dimension and vital bone; ACBP lower residual graft → graft-turnover difference, not osteogenic superiority. Limited by small underpowered sample, two pooled indications, and short 6-mo follow-up.
