Narrative review proposing an indication-driven framework for three minimally invasive implant bone regeneration strategies (Bone Core Technique, SPAL, IDR); Bone Core has the strongest dedicated evidence (n=186, ≥5y), SPAL is biologically plausible but early-stage, and IDR relies heavily on the broader immediate implant literature.

## Summary

This narrative review by Porczyk & Górski (JCM 2026) critically appraises minimally invasive bone regeneration strategies in implant dentistry and organizes them within an indication-driven clinical decision-making framework. The review argues that "minimally invasive" should not be defined narrowly by flap size or absence of donor site, but rather by whether reduced invasiveness is compatible with adequate regenerative control for the given defect morphology.

Three techniques are reviewed: (1) the **Bone Core Technique**, which harvests an autogenous bone cylinder from the implant osteotomy to augment localized peri-implant defects without a second donor site; (2) **SPAL**, which places graft material into a subperiosteal pouch to preserve the periosteal vascular envelope during buccal dehiscence correction; and (3) **IDR**, which combines extraction, immediate implant placement, and simultaneous dentoalveolar reconstruction in a single esthetic-zone procedure.

A clinical decision algorithm (Figure 1 in the paper) guides technique selection based on defect morphology, implant timing, and biologic priorities. Conventional guided bone regeneration (유도골재생술, Guided Bone Regeneration, GBR) remains the reference standard for advanced or extensive defects and is not replaced by these approaches.

## Key Contributions

- **Biologic framework**: Each technique preserves a distinct biologic compartment — osteogenic potential (Bone Core), periosteal vascularity (SPAL), socket architecture/contour (IDR).
- **Evidence hierarchy established**: Bone Core Technique has the strongest dedicated dataset; SPAL has biologic plausibility with limited retrospective/histologic data; IDR relies on broader immediate implant literature rather than protocol-specific validation.
- **Decision algorithm**: Indication-driven pathway integrating defect morphology, timing, and operator expertise — prevents misapplication of minimally invasive technique in cases requiring conventional augmentation.
- **Critical stance on technique sensitivity**: All three techniques are technically demanding; reduced invasiveness does not imply reduced complexity or learning curve.

## Methodology

- Structured, non-systematic literature search: PubMed/MEDLINE, Scopus, Web of Science; January 2000 – April 2026.
- No PRISMA, risk-of-bias assessment, or meta-analysis; narrative format.
- Priority to clinical studies, prospective/retrospective cohorts, case series, and biologically oriented conceptual papers.

## Results

### Bone Core Technique
- Biologic rationale: local autogenous osteogenic support without second donor-site morbidity.
- Key evidence: prospective study, n=186, ≥5-year clinical and radiographic follow-up — favorable outcomes for localized 골막주위결손 (peri-implant dehiscence defects).
- Indication: localized peri-implant dehiscence-type defects; limited graft volume restricts use in large horizontal/vertical deficiencies.

### SPAL (Sub-Periosteal Peri-implant Augmented Layer)
- Biologic rationale: subperiosteal pouch preserves 골막혈관공급 (periosteal blood supply), minimizing vascular disruption during buccal contour augmentation.
- Evidence: original technique description + retrospective cases + one histologic case report; no comparative prospective data.
- Indication: buccal peri-implant dehiscence where periosteal preservation is priority; limited phenotype and operator dependence.

### IDR (Immediate Dentoalveolar Restoration)
- Biologic rationale: one-stage preservation/reconstruction of dentoalveolar architecture in high-risk esthetic zone.
- Combines extraction, immediate implant, autogenous bone graft (e.g., 상악결절 (maxillary tuberosity) harvest), and immediate provisionalization.
- Evidence: case reports and technique-driven descriptions; much cited evidence derives from broader 즉시식립 (immediate implant placement) literature, not IDR-specific studies.
- Indication: highly selected esthetic-zone sockets with achievable primary stability, prosthetic control, and advanced operator expertise.

### Comparative Appraisal
| Technique | Biologic Priority | Evidence Level | Best Indication |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bone Core | Osteogenic potential | Prospective (n=186, ≥5y) — strongest | Localized peri-implant dehiscence |
| SPAL | Periosteal vascularity | Retrospective + histologic case reports | Buccal dehiscence, periosteal preservation priority |
| IDR | Socket architecture/contour | Case reports + immediate implant literature | Esthetic zone, highly selected sockets |
| Conventional GBR | — | Extensive RCT/SR evidence | Advanced horizontal/vertical defects |
