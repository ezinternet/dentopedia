## One-line Summary
Case report (44-year-old female): peri-implantitis + periodontitis managed by implant/tooth explantation → customized CAD/CAM titanium-mesh vertical GBR (autogenous bone + bovine HA, 9-month healing) → fully guided re-implantation (OneGuide, hydrophilic SOI implants) → monolithic zirconia, demonstrating a successful fully digital retreatment workflow.

## Summary
Case report demonstrating a fully digital, prosthetically driven workflow for implant retreatment after peri-implantitis failure. Key steps: CAD/CAM titanium mesh fabrication for vertical GBR → fully guided re-implantation (OneGuide) → monolithic zirconia. Successful outcome at follow-up.

## Workflow

```
1. Diagnosis: advanced peri-implantitis + periodontitis (#44F, posterior mandible)
2. Explantation + adjacent tooth extraction
3. Healing period
4. Digital planning: IOS + CBCT → virtual GBR planning
5. CAD/CAM titanium mesh fabrication (customized)
6. GBR surgery: autogenous bone + A-Oss (bovine HA) + titanium mesh
7. 9 months healing → mesh removal
8. Guided implant placement: OneGuide system → 2 hydrophilic SOI implants
9. Soft tissue graft
10. Final prosthesis: monolithic zirconia
```

## Key Concepts Illustrated

| Concept | Detail |
|---|---|
| Prosthetically driven planning | Implant position designed backward from ideal crown position |
| CAD/CAM titanium mesh | Space maintenance + vascularization for vertical GBR |
| Composite graft | Autogenous bone (osteogenic) + bovine HA (osteoconductive scaffold) |
| Fully guided surgery | OneGuide — precision placement in regenerated bone |
| Hydrophilic surface | SOI surface: enhanced osseointegration in less-than-ideal regenerated bone |
| Monolithic zirconia | Reduced ceramic fracture risk; simplified prosthetics |

## Clinical Takeaway
- Peri-implantitis explantation + re-implantation is feasible with proper staged protocol
- CAD/CAM titanium mesh enables predictable vertical bone gain
- Digital workflow (IOS + CBCT + guided surgery) reduces surgical error in compromised anatomy
- 9-month healing after GBR is sufficient before implant placement
