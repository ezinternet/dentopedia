## One-line Summary

SR+MA (10 studies, n=241; PROSPERO CRD42025646738) — bone expansion / compaction / osteodensification in narrow (≤2.5 mm horizontal-atrophy) alveolar crests significantly favors the experimental group for bone density (SMD −0.71, p=0.002), crestal expansion (SMD −1.12, p=0.04 after sensitivity test), and ISQ (SMD −8.88, p=0.0005); CE and ISQ carry high heterogeneity and publication bias.

## 한줄요약

SR+MA (10편, n=241; PROSPERO 등록) — 좁은(수평위축 ≤2.5 mm) 치조제에서 골확장·압축·골밀도화(Osseodensification, OD)가 대조군 대비 골밀도(Bone Density, BD; SMD −0.71, p=0.002)·치조정확장(Crestal Expansion, CE; SMD −1.12, p=0.04)·임플란트안정성지수(Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ; SMD −8.88, p=0.0005)를 유의하게 향상시킴. 단 CE·ISQ는 이질성(I²≥75%, 96%)과 출판편향이 높아 결론은 신중히 해석.

## Summary

This systematic review with meta-analysis (Frontiers in Bioeng. Biotechnol., 2025) pools three normally-separate technique families used to make a **narrow horizontally-atrophic alveolar crest** implant-ready without staged grafting: alveolar ridge **expansion** (manual / motorized threaded expanders, crestal split / corticotomy), bone **compaction**, and **osteodensification (ODT, Huwais / Densah burnishing drills)**. From 2,464 records, 10 studies (241 subjects; 5 RCTs, 2 prospective, 3 retrospective) met inclusion (horizontal atrophy ≤2.5 mm, means+SD reported, last 15 years, English). Three outcome-specific meta-analyses (RevMan 5.4.1, standardized mean difference) all favored the experimental techniques. The decisive nuance is **evidence quality by outcome**: bone density is homogeneous and trustworthy, whereas crestal expansion and ISQ are highly heterogeneous with marked funnel-plot asymmetry (publication bias) — so the headline "all three improve" should be read as "BD solidly, CE/ISQ suggestively."

## Key Contributions

- Consolidates expansion + compaction + osseodensification under one **"narrow ridge"** indication and quantifies BD, CE, and ISQ in a single analysis — a one-stop effect-size reference for the no-staged-graft pathway.
- Cleanly stratifies trust by outcome: BD I²=0% (homogeneous, fixed-effect) vs CE I²≥75% and ISQ I²=96% (random-effects, plus publication bias).
- Foregrounds the **clinical-vs-preclinical contradiction**: clinical pooling favors densification, but sheep/porcine/murine models show no BIC or osseointegration gain, and one RCT (Rizk 2024) attributed 3 implant failures to over-densifying the narrow crest (reduced blood supply, heat) — leading the authors to position these techniques behind GBR / bone blocks / crestal split when those are feasible.

## Methodology

- **Design**: PRISMA SR+MA, PROSPERO CRD42025646738.
- **PICOS**: P = adults with horizontal ridge atrophy ≤2.5 mm; I = expansion/compaction/densification; C = other techniques / each other; O = Δ BD (HU), Δ CE (mm), Δ ISQ; s = RCT, prospective, retrospective, case series.
- **Search**: PubMed/MEDLINE, Embase, Cochrane Central, Dentistry & Oral Sciences Source, Web of Science + GreyNet; English, 15 years.
- **Appraisal**: JBI MAStARI (≥7/13 = adequate); Cochrane RoB2.
- **Synthesis**: RevMan 5.4.1; SMD + 95% CI. Fixed-effect (homogeneous BD), random-effects (heterogeneous CE, ISQ); sensitivity analysis on CE. Heterogeneity bands: low 0–30%, moderate 40–50%, substantial 60–75%, high ≥75%.
- **Yield**: 2,464 → 47 full-text → 10 included (241 subjects). Follow-up immediate → 6 months. No survival endpoint, no adverse-event meta-analysis (data scarce).

## Results

**Bone density (BD, HU)** — 2 studies, I²=0% (homogeneous): **SMD −0.71, 95% CI [−1.15, −0.27], p=0.002**, favoring experimental. Vaddamanu 2024 RCT: 821.3±485.5 → 1126.7±373.6 HU with ODT. No publication bias (symmetric funnel).

**Crestal expansion (CE, mm)** — 8 studies, I²≥75% (high): before sensitivity test **not significant** (0.41, 95% CI [−1.02, 0.84], p=0.58); after removing Yadav, Koutouzis, and Anitua & Alkhraisat (5 studies remain), **SMD −1.12, 95% CI [−2.21, −0.03], p=0.04** (moderate). Absolute width gains across primaries roughly **1.1–2.4 mm** (e.g. Salman & Bede 4.04→5.3 mm; Yadav 3.64→5.62 mm crestal split; Koutouzis mean 5.2 mm coronal; Anitua 3.1→5.1 mm). High publication bias.

**Implant stability (ISQ)** — 3 studies, I²=96% (high): **SMD −8.88, 95% CI [−13.85, −3.91], p=0.0005**, favoring experimental. Bergamo 2021: ISQ 74±1.5 (osteodensifying drills) vs 66±1.5 (threaded osteotomes). High publication bias.

**Head-to-head signals**: Rizk 2024 (RCT) — osteodensifying drills > implant expanders for width gain (immediate p=0.018/0.022; 6 mo p=0.025/0.040); Tofan 2024 — no width difference between drills and threaded expanders (5.48±0.57 vs 5.53±0.71) but ODT faster; Ahmed 2022 — ODT higher insertion torque and shorter duration than crestal split, but 6-mo horizontal bone gain not significantly different (1.99±0.68 ODT vs 2.63±0.74 split).

**Bottom line**: expansion / compaction / osseodensification can render a narrow crest implant-ready and improve BD, CE, and ISQ, but the CE and ISQ evidence is fragile (heterogeneity + publication bias) and clinical benefit is contradicted by preclinical models — use cautiously, with adequate operator experience, and reserve for cases where staged augmentation is not preferable.
