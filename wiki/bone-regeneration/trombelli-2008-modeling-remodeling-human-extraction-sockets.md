## 한줄요약
인체 발치공 6개월 조직학 연구 (27 biopsy): 발치 2–4주에 우세하던 육아조직 (granulation tissue)이 6주 전후로 잠정 기질 (provisional matrix)과 직조골 (woven bone)로 대체되며, 골모세포 (osteoblast) 활성은 6–8주에 정점을 찍고 이후 안정 상태로 유지되지만 무기질화의 시점은 개인차가 매우 크다.

## Summary
Trombelli and coworkers performed one of the most cited **human histological** studies of postextraction socket healing. They collected 27 biopsies from human extraction sockets across early (2–4 wk), intermediate (6–8 wk), and late (12–24 wk) phases. The dominant biological narrative — granulation tissue → provisional matrix → woven bone with osteoblast activity peaking by week 6–8 — gives clinicians a defensible mental model for when implant placement and grafting decisions are biologically supported. The key clinical caveat: the timing of mineralized bone deposition is highly variable between individuals, which is why deterministic "wait 8 weeks" protocols can fail.

## Key Contributions
- Provides the human-tissue counterpart to Cardaropoli/Araujo dog models of socket healing.
- Establishes a tissue-cell timeline:
  - Weeks 2–4: granulation tissue, high vascularity, abundant macrophages
  - Weeks 4–6: replacement with provisional matrix and woven bone
  - Weeks 6–8: peak osteoblast density
  - Weeks 12–24: osteoblast presence stable; ongoing low-level osteoclast activity
- Documents **inter-individual variability** in mineralization timing — a key driver for ARP and case-by-case timing of implant placement.
- Reinforces the biologic rationale for alveolar ridge preservation (ARP): even with intact soft tissue closure, mineralized fill is not predictable on a fixed schedule.

## Methodology
- Prospective biopsy series; 27 socket biopsies across three healing phases.
- Sample sizes: early (2–4 wk) n = 10; intermediate (6–8 wk) n = 6; late (12–24 wk) n = 11.
- Semi-quantitative histological scoring of tissue composition and cell populations: granulation tissue, provisional matrix, woven bone, vascular structures, macrophages, osteoblasts, osteoclasts.
- Standardized biopsy harvest and processing.

## Results
- Granulation tissue dominated weeks 2–4; replaced by provisional matrix and woven bone between weeks 4 and 6.
- Vascular structure density and macrophage density decreased steadily from week 2 onward.
- Osteoblast density peaked at weeks 6–8 and remained roughly stable through week 24.
- Small but consistent osteoclast presence at every timepoint — indicating modeling/remodeling overlap.
- **Marked variability** in the timing of mineralized bone deposition — soft tissue/provisional matrix sequence is predictable; bone fill is not.
