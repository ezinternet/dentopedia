## One-line Summary
Multicenter RCT at dehisced single implant sites comparing a new resorbable non-cross-linked collagen membrane (creos xenoprotect, CXP) vs a reference membrane (BG) for GBR; CXP trended toward higher mean bone gain and lower membrane exposure (interim results).

## Summary
At dehisced single-implant sites treated with simultaneous placement + GBR, a new non-cross-linked porcine collagen membrane (creos xenoprotect) produced bone gain adequate to support the implant, with an interim trend toward greater mean bone gain and lower exposure than the reference membrane. The direction matches the broader signal that non-cross-linked membranes tolerate exposure better than chemically cross-linked ones; however these are interim results and the trend was flagged for further investigation, not established superiority. This RCT is one of the primary trials later pooled in the same group's 2018 SR+MA.

## Key Contributions
- Multicenter RCT validating a specific non-cross-linked collagen membrane at the dehiscence indication.
- Primary comparative data (bone gain, exposure) feeding subsequent meta-analysis.

## Methodology
- Expected dehiscence defects after single implant placement (esthetic + premolar zones).
- Randomized CXP vs reference membrane (BG), both with particulate graft, simultaneous placement.
- Outcomes: bone gain, membrane exposure, healing complications.

## Results
- Both membranes gave bone gain sufficient for implant support.
- CXP: trend to higher mean bone gain + lower exposure vs BG (interim, not confirmed significant).
