Multicentre RCT (40 subjects, 10 centres) of ridge preservation in posterior sockets with substantial buccal dehiscence: DBBM-collagen + native bilayer collagen membrane preserved ~1.76 mm more horizontal ridge at 6 months (p=0.0256) and gave better early soft-tissue closure than demineralized allograft + cross-linked collagen membrane.

## Summary

This practice-based, multicentre RCT (The McGuire Institute, ClinicalTrials.gov NCT02330523) compares two contrasting graft+membrane systems for **alveolar ridge preservation in posterior sockets with substantial buccal dehiscence** (Elian Class II/III, ≥1/3 of socket height/width). Forty subjects across 10 standardized centres were 1:1 randomized to:

- **Control — DFDBA + RECXC:** demineralized freeze-dried bone allograft + reconstituted, cross-linked bovine collagen membrane (longer-lasting, stiffer).
- **Test — DBBMC + NBCM:** deproteinized bovine bone mineral with collagen + native bilayer porcine collagen membrane (Bio-Oss Collagen + Bio-Gide; shorter-lasting, more flexible).

Socket dimensions were measured with indexed stents at baseline and 6 months; wound closure, soft-tissue inflammation, and 6-month biopsy histomorphometry were recorded. The **test group (DBBMC + NBCM) preserved ~1.76 mm more horizontal ridge at 6 months (p=0.0256)** — the primary endpoint. Vertical changes did not differ between arms. Histomorphometry showed similar % new bone but more residual graft remnants with DBBMC. The control arm had worse early soft-tissue closure (more open incision lines / gaps at 1 month). 37/40 sites were implant-ready at 6 months; all 3 insufficient sites were DFDBA + RECXC. Two morphology findings: higher early (1-week) inflammation tended to predict worse preservation, and **deeper sockets with thinner walls preserved better** (more contained defect). Note the test products were supplied by the study's educational-grant sponsor (Geistlich).

## Key Contributions

- Head-to-head RCT of a stiff cross-linked allograft system vs a native-bilayer DBBM-collagen system for dehisced-socket ridge preservation.
- Establishes a ~1.76 mm horizontal preservation advantage for DBBMC + NBCM with better early wound closure.
- Identifies socket morphology (depth, wall integrity) and early inflammation as predictors of preservation success.

## Methodology

- 40 subjects (18–70 y), posterior sockets (1st premolar–1st molar) with buccal dehiscence ≥1/3 socket height/width (Elian II/III).
- 1:1 block randomization across 10 centres, balanced per investigator.
- Stent-indexed horizontal (primary) + vertical (secondary) measures at baseline and 6 months; soft-tissue closure/inflammation; 6-month biopsy histomorphometry.

## Results

- Horizontal preservation at 6 months: DBBMC + NBCM 1.76 mm greater (p=0.0256).
- Vertical (buccal/lingual) change: no significant difference.
- Histomorphometry: similar % new bone; more graft remnants with DBBMC.
- Early closure: more open incision lines / larger gaps at 1 month with DFDBA + RECXC.
- Higher 1-week inflammation → trend to worse preservation; deeper/thinner-walled sockets → better preservation.
- 37/40 implant-ready at 6 months; 3 insufficient sites all DFDBA + RECXC.
