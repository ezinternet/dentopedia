## One-line Summary

Case series (n=5): a single-stage split-crest that longitudinally divides the ridge to provoke a greenstick fracture, spreads the cortical plates with a chisel, places immediate implants, and covers with e-PTFE (GTR) — yielding 1–4 mm width gain (greater in the maxilla) with histologic interpositional bone regeneration.

## Summary

Simion, Baldoni, and Zaffe describe one of the early split-crest techniques for horizontal ridge augmentation: in 5 patients with adequate vertical height but insufficient ridge width, the alveolar ridge is split longitudinally to provoke a greenstick fracture, the two cortical plates are spread apart with a chisel, implants are placed immediately into the inter-cortical space, and the implants and residual defects are covered with expanded-PTFE membranes for guided tissue regeneration. Biometric assessment showed a 1–4 mm gain in ridge width (greater in the maxilla), and histology confirmed bone regeneration between the two split portions. The paper establishes the deliberate-greenstick split-crest concept with membrane-assisted regeneration that later staged and osseodensification-assisted variants build on.

## Key Contributions

- Early codification of the **split-crest** technique: longitudinal ridge division provoking a controlled **greenstick fracture**, then chisel-spreading of cortical plates.
- Couples the split with **immediate implant placement** into the widened ridge.
- Adds **GTR with e-PTFE membranes** over implants and defects to maintain space for bone.
- Provides **histologic confirmation** of interpositional bone regeneration between the split halves.

## Methodology

Clinical case series with biometric and histologic assessment. 5 patients with sufficient vertical bone height but insufficient width. Technique: longitudinal alveolar ridge split (greenstick fracture) → fine chisel cut and lateral spreading of cortical plates → immediate implant placement → e-PTFE membrane coverage (GTR). Outcomes: biometric ridge-width gain and histologic evaluation of regenerated bone. No control group.

## Results

- Ridge-width gain: **1–4 mm**.
- Maxillary sites showed **greater** enlargement than mandibular sites.
- Histology: bone regeneration between the two portions of the split crest.
- Evidence level: small case series (`case-report`, n<10); descriptive only; abstract-only ingest (full text not retrieved).
