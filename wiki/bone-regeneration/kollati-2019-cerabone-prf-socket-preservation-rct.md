## Summary

This split-mouth RCT evaluated naturally derived bovine hydroxyapatite (Cerabone™) combined with platelet-rich fibrin (PRF) matrix and a collagen plug against unassisted natural healing in extraction sockets. Each of the 25 patients (17–50 years) provided one test site (Cerabone + PRF + collagen plug) and one control site (atraumatic extraction alone). At 6 months, the test group showed significantly less ridge width loss (1.47 ± 1.44 mm vs 2.75 ± 1.49 mm) and approximately 2.31 mm greater bone fill (8.7% higher radiographic bone fill %). The authors concluded that socket preservation with this combination is a reliable method to minimize post-extraction alveolar bone loss.

## Key Contributions

- Cerabone™ (naturally derived BCHA, not sintered) evaluated in an RCT setting — product-specific evidence for a commercially distinct xenograft
- PRF matrix serves as autologous growth factor reservoir (PDGF, TGF-β, VEGF) to enhance xenograft integration
- Collagen plug used for socket orifice sealing without membrane flap advancement — simple technique
- Demonstrates clinically meaningful width preservation without requiring a separate GBR membrane

## Methodology

**Design**: Split-mouth RCT; coin-flip randomization of test vs control site within each patient.

**Sample**: 25 patients, 17–50 years; each with ≥2 non-adjacent extraction sites. Exclusions: smokers, systemic disease, pregnancy, radiation history, immediate implant candidates.

**Test procedure**:
1. PRF preparation: 10 mL blood, centrifuged 3000 RPM × 10 min; PRF clot collected and pressed into membrane
2. Atraumatic extraction (2% lignocaine + 1:200,000 adrenaline)
3. Cerabone™ granules packed into socket
4. PRF membrane placed over graft
5. Collagen plug inserted at socket orifice; suturing

**Outcome measures**:
- Ridge width: Digital Vernier Caliper (buccal-lingual at crestal level)
- Ridge height: Occlusal stent with standardized holes at mid-buccal, mid-lingual, mesial, distal
- Bone fill %: Standardized IOPA (long-cone paralleling technique) at baseline and 6 months; 1 mm² counting grid + AUTOCAD measurement

## Results

| Outcome | Test (Cerabone + PRF) | Control |
|---|---|---|
| Mean ridge width loss at 6 mo | 1.47 ± 1.44 mm | 2.75 ± 1.49 mm |
| Radiographic bone fill | +2.31 mm (+8.7%) | baseline reference |

Both outcomes favored the test group with statistical significance. No adverse events or postoperative infections reported.
