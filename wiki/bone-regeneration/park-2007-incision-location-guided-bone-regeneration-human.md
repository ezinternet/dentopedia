## One-line Summary
Prospective human study (25 patients, 29 buccal dehiscence defects) showing incision location relative to the mucogingival junction governs flap survival and early membrane exposure; sites not re-covered by 1 month stayed exposed, and graft-alone flaps survived significantly farther beyond adjacent keratinized gingiva width than membrane-covered flaps (1.6±0.4 mm, P=0.002).

## 한줄요약
인체 전향연구(25명, 협측 열개결손 29개): 점막치은경계 기준 절개선 위치가 판막 생존·조기 막노출을 좌우. 1개월 내 재피개 안 된 부위는 계속 노출, 막 없는 이식 판막이 막 피개보다 인접 각화치은 폭 너머로 유의하게 더 길게 생존(1.6±0.4 mm, P=0.002).

## Summary
Membrane exposure in GBR is reframed here as a flap-design problem. Incision location relative to the mucogingival junction and the resulting "surviving flap length" — benchmarked against adjacent keratinized gingiva (KG) width — predicted early exposure better than membrane material alone. Membrane-covered flaps survived only about as far as adjacent KG width, whereas graft-alone flaps survived significantly farther (the avascular/stiff membrane appears to compromise overlying flap perfusion). Practically: plan the incision so that the closed flap margin sits over vascular bed (within/beyond adjacent KG), and treat thin tissue + membrane as a compounded exposure risk.

## Key Contributions
- Establishes incision location / flap survival as an independent driver of membrane exposure.
- Provides a clinical predictor: surviving flap length vs adjacent KG width.

## Methodology
- 29 buccal dehiscence defects (25 patients), particulate mineralized allograft.
- Arms: collagen membrane (10), acellular dermal matrix (10), graft alone (9).
- Incision-to-MGJ distance measured at baseline, 2 weeks, 1 month; compared to adjacent buccal KG width; gingival thickness recorded.

## Results
- 10 sites exposed by 2 weeks; not re-covered by 1 month → stayed exposed.
- Membrane groups: 2-week surviving flap length ≈ adjacent KG width (NS difference).
- Graft-alone flaps survived 1.6±0.4 mm farther beyond KG width (P=0.002).
- Thinner gingiva associated with exposure.
