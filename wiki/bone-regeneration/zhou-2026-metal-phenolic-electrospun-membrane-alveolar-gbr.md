## One-line Summary

In-vitro + rat alveolar bone defect study: a silk fibroin/PCL electrospun GBR membrane loaded with curcumin–strontium metal-phenolic nanoparticles (Cur-Sr/SF/PCL) gave ~13.9 MPa tensile strength and slow degradation (<8% at 8 weeks), and synergistically drove osteogenesis (Wnt/Ca/CaN), angiogenesis (VEGF/HIF-1), and M1→M2 macrophage reprogramming — highest BV/TV and new-bone fraction in vivo.

## Summary

Conventional collagen GBR membranes (e.g., Bio-Gide) are passive barriers that degrade quickly, weaken when wet, and lack osteoinductivity. Zhou et al. built a **multifunctional bioactive electrospun fibrous membrane (EFM)** of silk fibroin (SF) + polycaprolactone (PCL) loaded with **curcumin–strontium metal-phenolic nanoparticles (Cur-Sr NPs)**. The membrane keeps a true barrier role (cell-infiltration depth ~30 μm under its 60 μm thickness) while adding three coupled bioactivities — osteogenesis, angiogenesis, and immune reprogramming — from the sustained synergistic release of curcumin and strontium.

This is a bench (cell) + small rat-model study, so the evidence weight is `in-vitro`; the rat arm is confirmatory rather than the predominant body of work.

## Key Contributions

- A single GBR membrane that is barrier + osteoinductive + pro-angiogenic + immunomodulatory, addressing the three named weaknesses of clinical collagen membranes (mechanics, degradation matching, bioactivity).
- Curcumin–strontium metal-phenolic nanoparticles (~12 nm) for sustained, controlled co-delivery (curcumin's phenolic-OH coordinates Sr²⁺).
- RNA-seq + Western mechanism: pro-osteogenic effect via the non-canonical **Wnt/Ca/calcineurin (CaN)** cascade; Sr²⁺ via calcium-sensing receptor (CaSR).

## Methodology

- **Membranes**: co-electrospun SF/PCL, Cur/SF/PCL, Cur-Sr/SF/PCL; Cur-Sr NPs optimized at 1 mg/mL; fiber ~250–350 nm, thickness ~60 μm.
- **In vitro**: OMSCs and HGFs (biocompatibility/barrier), OMSCs (osteo + angio paracrine), HUVECs (angiogenesis), RAW 264.7 + LPS (M1→M2). CCK-8, live/dead, ALP (d7), ARS (d28), RT-qPCR/IF/Western, Transwell/scratch, tube formation, CLSM, tensile, degradation/release, RNA-seq.
- **In vivo**: 16 SD rats, 4 groups (sham/blank/SF-PCL/Cur-Sr-SF-PCL); palatal maxillary first-molar defect (~3×1.5×2 mm); 8-week harvest; micro-CT (BV/TV, CEJ–ABC), H&E, Masson, IF (OPN/VEGF/iNOS/CD206). One-way ANOVA, n=3.

## Results

- **Mechanics/degradation**: Cur-Sr/SF/PCL tensile 13.92±0.30 MPa, elongation 129.7%, Young's ~9.7 MPa (Bio-Gide reported ≈0.74 MPa / ≈30% elongation). Degradation <4% (4 wk), <8% (8 wk) vs Bio-Gide fragmenting ~5 days (reported); ~70% drug release by day 42 with sustained Cur-Sr profile. Barrier: ~30 μm infiltration < 60 μm thickness.
- **Osteogenesis**: highest ALP, mineralized nodules, OPN/RUNX2/BMP2/OCN/COL1/BSP gene + RUNX2/OPN/OCN protein, and strongest OMSC recruitment.
- **Angiogenesis**: ↑VEGF/ANG-1 (OMSCs), ↑VEGF/HIF-1 (HUVECs), best tube formation and HUVEC migration.
- **Immune**: under LPS, ↓iNOS/TNF-α/IL-6 (M1) and ↑TGF-β/ARG-1 (M2) → strongest M1→M2 reprogramming.
- **Mechanism**: RNA-seq 126↑/87↓ genes; Calcium/MAPK/Wnt pathways enriched; Western ↑Wnt/CaM/CaN → Wnt/Ca/CaN pathway.
- **In vivo (8 wk)**: greatest defect bone volume, most reduced CEJ–ABC, highest BV/TV, highest new-bone area + collagen fraction, minimal residual fibers; strongest OPN/VEGF and M2 (low iNOS / high CD206) in the defect.
