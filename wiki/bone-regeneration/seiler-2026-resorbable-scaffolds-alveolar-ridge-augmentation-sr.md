## One-line Summary

Systematic review (PRISMA/PROSPERO; 7 human studies, 39 patients, 45 ridges; 1 RCT) finding resorbable scaffolds (PLA/PCL/PLGA/β-TCP) are a viable, second-surgery-avoiding alternative to titanium meshes for alveolar ridge augmentation — but evidence is too sparse and heterogeneous for meta-analysis, risk of bias is high, and degradation kinetics were unreported in every study.

## Summary

Guided bone regeneration (GBR) for alveolar ridge augmentation has long relied on non-resorbable barriers — expanded/dense PTFE and customized titanium meshes — which give excellent space maintenance but require a **second surgical procedure for removal**, increasing morbidity, operating time, cost, and risk (mesh exposure/infection, crestal osseointegration of the mesh). Resorbable scaffolds promise to obviate the second surgery while still stabilizing graft and maintaining space. This SR asked, via a registered PICO, how resorbable scaffolds compare with titanium meshes / conventional augmentation for bone gain, implant survival/success, and complications.

After screening 3704 records, only **7 human studies (39 patients, 45 ridges)** qualified — **one RCT**, one prospective comparative study, three case series, and two case reports — using PLA, PCL, PLGA, poly-D-lactide, P(LA/CL), or β-TCP, two of them customized/additive-manufactured. Outcomes were generally favorable (four studies 100% implant survival; primary stability ~35 Ncm; PES 7/WES 9 esthetics in one), but heterogeneity in materials, outcome metrics (volumetric vs linear bone gain), follow-up, and reporting **precluded meta-analysis**, and risk of bias was moderate-to-high (even the RCT had incomplete outcome data). Critically, **degradation time was reported in none of the seven studies**, with residual scaffold particles seen at intermediate follow-up. The conclusion is cautiously positive but explicitly non-confirmatory: resorbable scaffolds *seem* a viable alternative, customized ones may add esthetic value, but larger standardized RCTs with CBCT volumetric/histomorphometric endpoints are needed before evidence-based recommendations.

## Key Contributions

- First SR dedicated to resorbable scaffolds for ridge augmentation, framed head-to-head against titanium meshes/PTFE.
- Defines and isolates **container-type (cage) scaffolds** — contoured frameworks that stabilize particulate graft and resist soft-tissue collapse, used *with* graft material — distinct from block-type osteoconductive scaffold matrices (which were excluded).
- Names **degradation kinetics** (mismatch between polymer resorption and bone-healing timeline) as the dominant, near-unstudied gap.

## Methodology

- **Design**: PRISMA-P 2020; PROSPERO CRD420250650843; descriptive synthesis (no meta-analysis).
- **PICO**: edentulous ridge-augmentation patients (P); resorbable scaffolds (I); non-resorbable devices/Ti-mesh or conventional augmentation (C); bone gain, implant survival/success, complications (O).
- **Search**: PubMed/MEDLINE, Cochrane, Web of Science + Google Scholar; 06 Mar 2025, updated 22 Nov 2025; English, no date limit. Animal/in vitro studies excluded.
- **Selection**: 3704 → 7 included. Two reviewers + third-author adjudication.
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane RoB (RCTs), Newcastle–Ottawa Scale (non-randomized comparative), JBI tools (case series/reports).

## Results

| Outcome | Finding |
|---|---|
| Included studies | 7 (39 patients, 45 sites): 1 RCT, 1 prospective comparative, 3 case series, 2 case reports |
| Materials | PLA, PCL, PLGA, poly-D-lactide, P(LA/CL), β-TCP; 2 customized/3D-printed |
| Implant survival | 4 studies 100%; 1 study 20% loss; 2 not reported |
| Primary stability | ~35 Ncm (Ivanovski, Hsu); equal ISQ across groups (Shido) |
| Complications | 4 studies none (2 customized); others: mucosal rubefaction/fistula → exposure, bone decrease, graft loss (5 graft-loss cases) |
| Esthetics | PES 7 / WES 9 (Ivanovski); "satisfying" (Schönegg) |
| Degradation time | **Reported in 0/7 studies**; residual scaffold particles at intermediate follow-up |
| Meta-analysis | Not possible — clinical/methodological heterogeneity |
| Risk of bias | Moderate-to-high overall; RCT had incomplete outcome data |

**Resorbable scaffold vs titanium mesh (the review's central comparison):** resorbable scaffolds match the space-making/cage function while **eliminating the second-stage mesh removal surgery** and lowering exposure/infection and crestal-osseointegration risk that plague Ti-mesh/PTFE. The trade-off is **uncontrolled degradation kinetics** (residual polymer fragments, slower-than-expected resorption) and a far weaker evidence base — Ti-mesh GBR has predictable validated outcomes, whereas resorbable-scaffold evidence is 7 small, heterogeneous, mostly non-comparative studies. The review does **not** establish superiority of any scaffold type and stops short of clinical recommendation.
