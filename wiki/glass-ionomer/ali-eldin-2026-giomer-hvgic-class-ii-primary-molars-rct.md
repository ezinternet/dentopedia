## One-line Summary

Split RCT (124 primary molars, Class II) over 12 months: a highly viscous glass-ionomer (EQUIA Forte HT) and a giomer injectable composite (Beautifil Flow Plus X) had statistically comparable clinical success (88.3% vs 93.4%, NS) and equal functional/biological FDI outcomes; the giomer was significantly better only in esthetics (surface luster p=0.002, color match p<0.001).

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1186/s12903-026-08509-x](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12903-026-08509-x)), this RCT addresses material selection for the demanding stress-bearing Class II cavity in primary molars, comparing a highly viscous glass-ionomer / glass hybrid (EQUIA Forte HT) against a giomer-based injectable composite (Beautifil Flow Plus X) — both fluoride-releasing but mechanically and esthetically different.

Across 124 primary molars randomized 1:1 and evaluated by FDI criteria at 3, 6 and 12 months, **12-month clinical success was statistically equivalent**: 88.3% for the HVGIC vs 93.4% for the giomer (p>0.05). Functional outcomes (fracture and retention, marginal adaptation) and biological outcomes were comparable, with **no recurrent caries or postoperative sensitivity** in either group. The only significant separation was esthetic: the giomer gave better **surface luster (p=0.002)** and **color match (p<0.001)**.

Clinically, this supports the HVGIC (glass hybrid, bulk-placeable, moisture-tolerant — practical in young/uncooperative children) as a durable Class II option whose function matches the more technique-sensitive giomer; the giomer is preferable where esthetics dominate. This supplies primary RCT data directly under the wiki's HVGIC-vs-composite-in-primary-teeth synthesis [[glass-ionomer/krishnakumar-2024-hvgic-composite-primary-teeth-sr]] and the pediatric surveillance line. Limitations: single-center, 12-month follow-up, primary teeth.

## Key Contributions

- 12-mo head-to-head RCT: HVGIC ≈ giomer for clinical success and function in primary-molar Class II.
- Giomer advantage confined to esthetics (luster, color match).
- No recurrent caries/sensitivity in either material.

## Methodology

RCT (NCT06000085), 124 primary molars, 1:1 (EQUIA Forte HT Fil vs Beautifil Flow Plus X F00); FDI criteria at 3/6/12 mo; failure = FDI 4/5; Mann-Whitney U + Wilcoxon signed-rank, p≤0.05; Ain Shams University. Confidence `rct`; PMC OA full text retrieved.

## Results

- 12-mo success: HVGIC 88.3% vs giomer 93.4% (p>0.05, NS).
- Esthetics: giomer better surface luster (p=0.002), color match (p<0.001).
- Fracture/retention, marginal adaptation: comparable.
- No recurrent caries or postoperative sensitivity in either group.
