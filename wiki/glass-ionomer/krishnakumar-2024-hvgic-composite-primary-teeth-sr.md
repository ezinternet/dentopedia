## One-line Summary
Systematic review of 4 clinical trials (Int J Clin Pediatr Dent 2024) finding no statistically significant difference between high-viscosity GIC and composite resin restorations in primary teeth.

## 한줄요약

## Summary
This review compared the clinical effectiveness of high-viscosity GIC (HVGIC) versus direct composite resin (CR) in single- and multi-surface cavities in primary teeth of children aged 3–13. Major databases were searched for publications 2000–2021. Four studies met inclusion (three RCTs, one non-randomized controlled trial). No statistically significant difference between HVGIC and CR was found in any included study, though the literature overall reports contradictory durability findings for primary-teeth restorations.

## Key Contributions
- Directly addresses the HVGIC-vs-CR debate in deciduous dentition with trial-level evidence.
- Highlights the small, heterogeneous evidence base (only 4 trials) as the key limitation.

## Methodology
SR of clinical trials (2000–2021), children 3–13 yr, primary teeth, HVGIC vs CR. 4 studies (3 RCT + 1 non-RCT).

## Results
No statistically significant difference between HVGIC and CR in any included study. Evidence base small; durability findings across the wider literature remain contradictory.
