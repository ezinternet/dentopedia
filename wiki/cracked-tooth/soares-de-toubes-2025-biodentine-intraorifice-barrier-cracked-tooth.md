## One-line Summary

Case report (n=1): a root-extended cracked mandibular molar with an endo-periodontal lesion and pulp necrosis treated by root canal therapy + a Biodentine intraorifice barrier (placed 2 mm apical to the crack); 48-month CBCT showed complete periradicular and alveolar bone healing with the tooth retained and functional.

## Summary

A 37-year-old woman presented with chewing pain on tooth #36, a >5 mm mesial/buccal periodontal pocket, and pulp necrosis (negative thermal/electric tests). The tooth had a complex anatomy — three roots including a supernumerary lingual root (radix entomolaris) and five canals — with a crack originating at the mesial marginal ridge that extended into the mesial canal walls. CBCT confirmed a periapical/furcation lesion and buccal cortical bone resorption but **no complete root fracture**, classifying this as a root-extended crack with a secondary endo-periodontal lesion of endodontic origin — the highest-risk cracked-tooth presentation.

Rather than extract, the operators sealed the radicular crack from inside the canal. After standard rotary chemomechanical preparation (NaOCl + EDTA, ultrasonic activation) and a 14-day calcium hydroxide dressing, the canals were obturated, then the mesial-canal filling was removed back to **2 mm beyond the apical-most point of the crack** and that space was filled with condensed Biodentine to form a dense intraorifice barrier sealing the crack. The access was bonded over the Biodentine (Clearfil SE Bond) and restored with composite under a pre-existing CAD-CAM onlay; a night occlusal plate was prescribed. At 12 months the periodontal pocket had resolved and periapical tissues had healed; at 48 months CBCT confirmed alveolar bone repair and complete periradicular healing with a fully functional, asymptomatic tooth.

This is a single case (lowest evidence level) and the outcome is confounded by several favorable factors (experienced single operator, operative microscope, CBCT control, early cusp-protective onlay). It is hypothesis-generating and does not establish a survival rate, but it provides a concrete worked protocol for the scenario that outcome-level cracked-tooth syntheses flag as worst-prognosis.

## Key Contributions

- Worked clinical protocol for the **Biodentine intraorifice barrier** in a root-extended cracked tooth complicated by an endo-perio lesion: obturate, then remove canal filling to 2 mm beyond the crack's apical-most point and condense Biodentine into that space to seal/reinforce the crack segment.
- Demonstrates that an **endo-periodontal lesion of endodontic origin** associated with a root crack (>5 mm pocket, buccal bone resorption) can resolve — pocket closure at 12 months, bone repair at 48-month CBCT — when the crack is sealed and the canal disinfected, without periodontal surgery.
- Reinforces adjuncts the authors credit for success: early cusp-protective CAD-CAM onlay (crack stabilization), operative magnification, CBCT-based crack/healing assessment, and parafunction control via a night occlusal plate.

## Methodology

- **Diagnosis**: tooth #36, pulp necrosis (negative Frigi-Dent thermal + Digitest electric), painful percussion, probing depth >5 mm mesial/buccal; CBCT — three roots (radix entomolaris), five canals, periapical/furcation lesion, buccal cortical resorption, no complete root fracture; crack confirmed on pulp-chamber floor at access.
- **Endodontics**: minimally invasive access through the onlay under rubber dam + Top Dam gingival barrier; 2.5% NaOCl; Race Evo 4% rotary; Root ZX2 apex locator; 17% EDTA + ultrasonic (Irrisonic) activation; UltraCal SX Ca(OH)2 dressing for 14 days.
- **Second visit**: Ca(OH)2 removed ultrasonically; obturation with AH-Plus Bioceramic + gutta-percha; mesial-canal filling removed to 2 mm beyond the crack apex.
- **Intraorifice barrier**: Biodentine (Septodont) mixed 30 s at 4,200 rpm, carried with MTA carrier to the apical-most crack point and condensed; 15-min set; Clearfil SE Bond primer/adhesive over the set Biodentine; access sealed with Filtek Z350 composite; occlusal adjustment + night plate.
- **Crack measurement**: periodontal probe under transillumination, externally to the clamp wing.
- **Follow-up**: clinical/radiographic/CBCT at 3, 6, 12 months and 48 months.

## Results

- **12 months**: periapical healing, no inflammatory signs, resolution of the >5 mm periodontal pocket.
- **48 months**: radiograph + CBCT confirmed alveolar bone repair adjacent to the mesial root and complete periradicular healing; tooth fully functional and asymptomatic.
- Context (from discussion, not this case's own data): flowable-resin intraorifice barriers reported 53% survival at 5 yr (Malentacca) and 96.6% at 4 yr (Davis & Shariff); de Toubes' preliminary prospective series of 12 root-extended cracked teeth with a Biodentine barrier reported 100% survival at 1–3 yr.
- **Caveat**: n=1, lowest evidence level; favorable confounders prevent attributing the outcome to the barrier alone.
