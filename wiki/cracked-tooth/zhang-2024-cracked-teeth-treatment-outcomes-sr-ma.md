## One-line Summary

Abstract-only SR+MA (27 studies, 26 pooled): for cracked teeth, direct restorations without cuspal coverage on vital teeth raise pulpal-complication risk (RR 3.2) and extraction risk (RR 8.1) vs full crowns, and endodontically treated cracked teeth without full crowns have 11.3× higher extraction risk — full-crown restoration strongly recommended for symptomatic/RCT cracked teeth, while asymptomatic teeth may be monitored.

## Summary

> [!note] Abstract-only page
> No PMC open-access full text exists (J Dent, Elsevier subscription); under wiki Rule #1 the full text was not retrieved. Content below is grounded in the PubMed abstract only ([DOI 10.1016/j.jdent.2024.104843](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jdent.2024.104843)).

According to PubMed, this is the largest CTS treatment-outcome synthesis in the wiki — 27 studies (26 meta-analyzed) spanning cracked teeth retaining vital pulp (CT-VDP) and cracked teeth undergoing root canal treatment (CT-RCT). It is the quantitative backbone for the wiki's central CTS restorative message: **cuspal coverage matters**.

Baseline outcomes were generally favorable: CT-VDP tooth survival 92.8–97.8% (1–6 yr), pulp survival 85.6–90.4% (1–3 yr), success 80.6–89.9%; CT-RCT tooth survival 90.5–91.1%, success 83.0–91.2%. For asymptomatic cracked teeth, monitoring without restoration still yielded 80% success at 3 years — supporting watchful waiting in the absence of symptoms.

The decisive findings are the risk ratios. In vital cracked teeth, **direct restorations without cuspal coverage** raised the risk of pulpal complications **3.2-fold (95% CI 1.51–6.82)** and of extraction **8.1-fold (1.05–62.5)** versus full crowns. In endodontically treated cracked teeth, omitting a full crown carried an **11.3-fold higher extraction risk**. The authors therefore strongly recommend full-crown (cuspal-coverage) restoration for any symptomatic cracked tooth and for all CT-RCT, reserving monitoring for the asymptomatic.

This dovetails with [[cracked-tooth/gavriil-2025-single-vs-multiple-stage-cracked-teeth-pulp]] (indirect onlay best for pulp success; direct inferior) and [[cracked-tooth/fiyaz-ghani-2025-cracked-teeth-external-splinting-pulp-survival]] (splinting preserves vitality), and reinforces the [[cracked-tooth/kaur-2026-cracked-tooth-syndrome-umbrella-review]] umbrella's coverage recommendation.

## Key Contributions

- Largest CTS treatment-outcome SR+MA (27 studies; CT-VDP + CT-RCT).
- Quantified cuspal-coverage benefit: pulpal-complication RR 3.2, extraction RR 8.1 (CT-VDP direct vs full crown); CT-RCT extraction risk 11.3× without full crown.
- Monitoring viable (80% SR at 3 yr) for asymptomatic cracked teeth.

## Methodology

SR+MA; Medline/Embase/PubMed/Cochrane; TSR/PSR/SR with ≥1-yr follow-up; Newcastle-Ottawa risk of bias; 27 qualitative / 26 pooled. Confidence `sr+ma`; abstract-only ingest (no PMC OA).

## Results

- Monitoring (asymptomatic): SR 80% at 3 yr.
- CT-VDP: TSR 92.8–97.8%, PSR 85.6–90.4%, SR 80.6–89.9%.
- CT-RCT: TSR 90.5–91.1%, SR 83.0–91.2%.
- Direct w/o cuspal coverage (CT-VDP): pulpal complications RR 3.2 (1.51–6.82), extraction RR 8.1 (1.05–62.5).
- CT-RCT w/o full crown: extraction risk 11.3× higher (p<0.001).
