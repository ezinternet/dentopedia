## One-line Summary

Abstract-only SR+MA (14 cohort studies, symptomatic vital cracked teeth): single- vs multiple-stage treatment did not differ for pulp survival/success, but single-stage with indirect restorations gave the best pulp success, onlays outperformed crowns, direct restorations were inferior for pulp success, and short interim duration (1–2 wk) beat long; 1-yr tooth survival 96.1–100%; very low certainty.

## Summary

> [!note] Abstract-only page
> No PMC open-access full text exists (J Endod, Elsevier subscription); under wiki Rule #1 the full text was not retrieved. Content below is grounded in the PubMed abstract only ([DOI 10.1016/j.joen.2025.06.013](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.joen.2025.06.013)).

According to PubMed, this SR+MA addresses a practical CTS question: should a symptomatic but vital cracked tooth be restored in one visit (single-stage, SST) or in phases (multiple-stage, MST), and which restoration type best preserves the pulp? Across 14 cohort studies, **SST and MST did not differ significantly** for 1- or 2-year pulp survival or 1-year pulp success.

The discriminating factor was restoration **type/timing**, not staging per se. **Single-stage with indirect restorations** gave the highest pulp success — significantly better than MST-with-indirect (P<.05) and than SST-with-direct (P<.001). **Indirect onlays outperformed crowns** (P<.05). **Direct restorations** matched onlays for pulp *survival* but were inferior for pulp *success* (P<.001), i.e. teeth stayed vital but more often symptomatic. When MST was used, a **short interim duration (1–2 weeks)** gave better pulp success than a long one (2–3 months). One-year tooth survival was uniformly high (96.1–100%). Certainty was very low (observational evidence, heterogeneity).

Together with [[cracked-tooth/zhang-2024-cracked-teeth-treatment-outcomes-sr-ma]] — which shows direct restorations without cuspal coverage sharply raise pulpal-complication and extraction risk — the practical convergence is: for the vital cracked tooth, favor prompt cuspal-coverage indirect restoration (onlay), don't drag out the interim phase, and reserve direct restorations cautiously. This reinforces the cuspal-protection theme running through the wiki's CTS pages.

## Key Contributions

- Staging (SST vs MST) alone does not change pulp outcomes; restoration type/timing does.
- Indirect onlay = best pulp success; crown second; direct inferior for success.
- Short interim duration favored in multiple-stage treatment.

## Methodology

SR+MA; search to Dec 3 2024; symptomatic vital cracked teeth, ≥1-yr follow-up after complete SST/MST; Newcastle-Ottawa + Cochrane RoB2; single-arm random-effects MA; GRADE. Confidence `sr+ma`; abstract-only ingest (no PMC OA).

## Results

- 14 cohort studies. SST vs MST: no significant difference (1-/2-yr pulp survival, 1-yr pulp success, P>.05).
- SST+indirect > MST+indirect (P<.05), > SST+direct (P<.001) for pulp success.
- Indirect onlays > crowns (P<.05); direct ≈ onlays survival, inferior success (P<.001).
- Short interim (1–2 wk) > long (2–3 mo) (P<.05). 1-yr tooth survival 96.1–100%. Very low certainty.
