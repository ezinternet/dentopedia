## One-line Summary

Single case report (n=1): a cracked, endodontically treated maxillary first molar (#16) restored in **one visit** using the morphology-driven preparation technique (MDPT) — a minimally invasive lithium-disilicate (IPS e.max Press) occlusal veneer with butt-joint proximal margins (1.2–1.3 mm) and hollow-chamfer axial walls, bonded over a short-fiber-reinforced composite (SFRC) access build-up; complete pain resolution and intact, healthy result at 15-month follow-up.

## Summary

A 36-year-old healthy male presented with 2 months of pain on biting plus 1 day of recent spontaneous/nocturnal pain in tooth #16. Cold testing was positive, with no percussion/probing response or mobility; methylene-blue staining revealed an occlusal crack. Diagnosis: cracked tooth syndrome with reversible pulpitis, with the crack (AAE classification) extending into the pulp chamber but not the pulpal floor. Because the patient was relocating, treatment was completed in a single visit: microscopic root canal therapy followed immediately by a bonded lithium-disilicate occlusal veneer prepared by MDPT. At 15 months the restoration was intact with healthy periapical and periodontal status.

This is a single uncontrolled case — it illustrates a conservative, anatomy-guided restorative option for cracked teeth but does **not** establish effectiveness or superiority over full-coverage crowns.

## Key Contributions

- A worked clinical example of **MDPT** (morphology-driven preparation technique): anatomy-guided, minimally invasive reduction instead of uniform full-crown preparation.
- Demonstrates a feasible **single-visit** chairside workflow — root canal therapy plus immediate bonded ceramic occlusal veneer — chosen for a patient-driven (relocation) constraint, on a tooth with pulpal but no periapical involvement.
- Documents a specific minimally invasive design: occlusal reduction 1.0–1.5 mm, **butt-joint proximal margins (1.2–1.3 mm)**, **hollow-chamfer axial walls (20°–30°)** coronal to the equator, with an SFRC access build-up placed 1 mm sub-occlusally to support the ceramic.

## Methodology

- **Diagnosis**: CTS + reversible pulpitis (#16); cold (+), no percussion/probing response, no mobility; crack confirmed by methylene-blue staining; crack into pulp chamber, not pulpal floor (microscopic, AAE).
- **MDPT preparation**: tapered diamond bur for proximal boxes (90° butt-joint, no bevel, contacts preserved); rounded-end bur for anatomic occlusal reduction 1.0–1.5 mm (silicone-index guided); chamfer bur for hollow-chamfer axial walls (20°–30°) to expose enamel prisms; final thickness ≥ 1.2 mm checked for undercuts.
- **Digital workflow**: 3Shape TRIOS 5 scan; monolithic IPS e.max Press (min 1.2 mm), shade 2R2.5; chairside fabrication during the endodontic phase.
- **Endodontics**: rubber dam, 16× microscope; Raypex 6 + radiographs (WL: MB-16, MB2-16, DB-17, P-19 mm); Reciproc Blue R25/.06; 0.5% NaOCl + 17% EDTA with ultrasonic activation; warm hybrid obturation (System B 200°C + Thermafil) with AH Plus sealer.
- **Cementation**: intaglio 5% HF (20 s) + Monobond Plus silane (90 s); enamel 37% phosphoric acid (30 s); Single Bond Universal Adhesive; flowable + SFRC access build-up (light-cured 1200 mW/cm²); dual-cure RelyX Ultimate; sectional polywave LED curing; Sof-Lex finishing.

## Results

- Trial verification: even occlusal contacts (8-µm articulating paper), marginal discrepancy < 40 µm (20×), proximal contact passing floss with moderate resistance.
- Immediate outcome: complete resolution of masticatory and spontaneous pain; functional and esthetic single-visit restoration.
- **15-month follow-up**: intact restoration, preserved tooth structure, healthy periodontal tissues, normal periapical radiograph.
- No comparative or quantitative effectiveness data — single uncontrolled case with short follow-up; the SFRC–ceramic interface under long-term functional loading remains untested (authors' caveat).
