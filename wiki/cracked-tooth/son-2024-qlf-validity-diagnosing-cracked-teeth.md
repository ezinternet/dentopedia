## One-line Summary

Prospective STARD diagnostic-accuracy study (26 cracked molars, 2 examiners) on QLF imaging for crack-line localization: the 405 nm fluorescent and combination images reached substantial agreement vs reference standard (kappa F-R 0.662, C-R 0.742) while natural-color images alone were only slight (0.164); inter-examiner agreement was moderate for fluorescent/combination (0.449/0.493) vs fair for natural color (0.394).

## 한줄요약

전향적 STARD 진단정확도 연구 (균열치 26개, 평가자 2명): QLF 405 nm 형광·복합 영상은 기준표준 대비 substantial 일치도(kappa 형광 0.662, 복합 0.742)로 균열선 위치를 검출했으나 자연색 영상 단독은 slight(0.164)에 그침. 평가자 간 일치도는 형광·복합 moderate(0.449/0.493), 자연색 fair(0.394).

## Summary

Cracked teeth lack a reliable, reproducible method to pinpoint crack-line *location*. This prospective clinical trial (STARD 2015) tested whether a single intraoral capture-type QLF device (Q-ray penC, 405 nm blue light) can localize crack lines validly and reliably. From 81 screened teeth, 26 cracked molars (all requiring restoration removal or root canal treatment) were imaged in three modes — natural color, fluorescent, and a combination view — before treatment. A reference standard for crack location across five occlusal zones (central/mesial/distal/buccal/lingual) was set by an experienced dentist using post-removal internal QLF images; two pre-trained first-year residents then read each mode independently. Cohen kappa quantified inter-examiner agreement and agreement against the reference standard. The fluorescent channel was decisive: the natural-color image alone barely beat chance against the reference standard (κ = 0.164, slight), whereas fluorescent (0.662) and combination (0.742) reached substantial agreement. Restoration material/type biased reading — gold reflection drove natural-color underestimation and some fluorescent overestimation — and natural-color reading failed on all unrestored teeth (cracks confused with anatomical grooves).

## Key Contributions

- First prospective clinical quantification (per authors) of QLF **validity + inter-examiner reliability** for *localizing* crack lines, not just detecting them.
- Direct three-way comparison (natural color vs fluorescent vs combination) from one device, with a post-removal QLF reference standard.
- Identifies the **405 nm fluorescent image** as the operative modality; combination view yields the highest true-reading rate (76.9%).
- Documents how **restoration material** (gold reflection) and **absence of restorations** (groove confusion) systematically bias natural-color reading.

## Methodology

- **Design**: prospective diagnostic-accuracy/reliability study, STARD 2015; IRB PNUDH-2021-006-MD (Pusan National University).
- **Sample**: 81 teeth/59 patients screened → 37 diagnosed cracked/crack-pulpitis (microscopy, bite test, pulp vitality, radiographs) → **26 molars** finally analyzed.
- **Device/imaging**: Q-ray penC (AIOBIO), single-shot natural-color + 405 nm fluorescent images; W block for mouth opening, no rubber dam. Internal QLF images captured after crack/restoration removal.
- **Reference standard**: experienced dentist mapped crack lines to five occlusal zones from before/after QLF images, excluding grooves/craze lines.
- **Readers**: two pre-trained first-year residents; independent reads of natural-color, fluorescent, combination images at one-day intervals → consensus final reading.
- **Stats**: Cohen kappa for inter-examiner (A vs B) and for N-R / F-R / C-R vs reference; false reads classed over-/under-estimated; cross-analysis by restoration material and presence. SPSS v20.

## Results

**Sample**: 57.7% female; modal age 50–59 (42.3%); maxillary first molar most common (11/26). Restorations — gold 38.5%, composite 23.1%, amalgam 9.2%, none 15.4%. Class I 53.8%, Class II 30.8%. Pulp dx — irreversible pulpitis 69.2%, necrosis 19.2%, reversible pulpitis 11.5%.

**Inter-examiner agreement (κ)**: natural color 0.394 (fair) < fluorescent 0.449 < combination 0.493 (both moderate).

**Vs reference standard (κ)**: combination (C-R) **0.742** (substantial, best) > fluorescent (F-R) **0.662** (substantial) ≫ natural color (N-R) **0.164** (slight, worst).

**By location**: N-R worst for lingual cracks (0.283), best for mesial (0.615); F-R & C-R both 0.923 for mesial; C-R perfect (1.000) for lingual — fluorescence helps both far (lingual) and near (mesial) cracks.

**True/false**: N-R 6/26 true (19/20 false readings underestimated; gold worst with 7 underestimations). F-R 18/26 true (false skewed to overestimation 3:5 — fluorescence can over-read). C-R 20/26 true (gold highest true count).

**By restoration presence**: natural-color reading judged **all 4 unrestored teeth false (100%)** (cracks confused with grooves), while fluorescent judged all 4 **true (100%)** and combination 75% true.

**Bottom line**: the 405 nm fluorescent channel — alone or combined with natural color — provides reliable, valid crack-line localization; natural-color QLF imaging alone is inadequate. Restoration material and presence must be weighed when interpreting images.
