## One-line Summary

Abstract-only SR+MA (10 studies, 711 cracked teeth with normal pulp/reversible pulpitis): external splinting yields 85% pooled 12-month pulp survival (95% CI 79–91%), higher without interim orthodontic banding (89% vs 79%) and with a direct vs stepwise approach (89% vs 81%); very low GRADE certainty.

## Summary

> [!note] Abstract-only page
> No PMC open-access full text exists (J Endod, Elsevier subscription); under wiki Rule #1 the full text was not retrieved. Content below is grounded in the PubMed abstract only ([DOI 10.1016/j.joen.2025.07.005](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.joen.2025.07.005)). Forest plots and per-study detail are not available.

According to PubMed, this systematic review and meta-analysis is the first to isolate **external splinting** as a conservative management line for cracked teeth that still retain a vital pulp (normal pulp or reversible pulpitis). Across 10 studies and 711 cracked teeth with at least 12 months follow-up, the pooled 12-month pulp survival was **85% (95% CI 79–91%)**, with considerable heterogeneity (I²=82.6%).

Two protocol signals emerged from subgroup analysis. Teeth managed **without** interim orthodontic banding survived better (89%, 95% CI 83–95%) than those with banding (79%, 67–90%), and a **direct** definitive approach (89%, 82–97%) outperformed a **stepwise** approach (81%, 72–90%). The authors caution that risk of bias was moderate and the GRADE certainty very low, so these are hypothesis-generating rather than practice-defining. Clinically, the paper supports splinting + cuspal-coverage protection as a vitality-preserving option for the reversibly-inflamed cracked tooth, while flagging that the wide protocol variation precludes a standardized recommendation.

This complements the wiki's restorative-outcome evidence: [[cracked-tooth/zhang-2024-cracked-teeth-treatment-outcomes-sr-ma]] (full-crown vs direct, CT-VDP vs CT-RCT) and [[cracked-tooth/gavriil-2025-single-vs-multiple-stage-cracked-teeth-pulp]] (staging), and extends the [[cracked-tooth/kaur-2026-cracked-tooth-syndrome-umbrella-review]] umbrella by adding the splinting dimension it did not pool.

## Key Contributions

- 12-month pulp survival benchmark for vital cracked teeth after external splinting: 85%.
- Interim orthodontic banding associated with lower pulp survival (89% vs 79%).
- Direct approach > stepwise (89% vs 81%).

## Methodology

SR+MA; 6-database search to Jan 31 2025; permanent mature cracked teeth (normal pulp/reversible pulpitis), ≥12-mo follow-up; Newcastle-Ottawa risk of bias; GRADE; random-effects pooling. Confidence `sr+ma`; ingested abstract-only (no PMC OA).

## Results

- 10 studies, 711 teeth; pooled 12-mo pulp survival 85% (95% CI 79–91%), I²=82.6%.
- No banding 89% vs banding 79%; direct 89% vs stepwise 81%.
- Risk of bias moderate; QoE very low; no publication bias.
