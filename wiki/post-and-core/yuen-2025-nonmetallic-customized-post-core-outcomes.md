## One-line Summary

Systematic review (12 studies, 829 teeth): CAD/CAM zirconia customized posts match cast metal survival; PEEK and PEKK posts show high debonding rates; individually formed fiber-reinforced composite systems demonstrate favorable (repairable) failure patterns.

## Summary

This 2025 systematic review specifically evaluates **customized** (individually fabricated, canal-morphology-replicating) nonmetallic post-and-core systems — a category that has expanded significantly with CAD/CAM technology and direct fiber-reinforced composites. Twelve studies encompassing 829 endodontically treated teeth were included from a five-database search.

Customized indirect systems used CAD/CAM milled zirconia, PEEK, or PEKK. Direct chairside customized methods included individually formed fiber-reinforced composites (EverStick Post, Ribbond) and rigid fiber-reinforced composite systems (bundle fiber posts; prefabricated fiber posts relined with composite resin), plus corono-radicular direct composite.

The key clinical finding: zirconia CAD/CAM posts performed comparably to conventional cast posts, while PEEK and PEKK showed lower survival with frequent debonding. Individually formed FRC systems displayed favorable failure modes (repairable rather than catastrophic root fractures). Overall, the evidence base remains limited with significant heterogeneity.

## Key Contributions

- Distinguishes **customized** post systems from standard prefabricated fiber posts — a clinically important but underreviewed category
- Establishes zirconia CAD/CAM as the most evidence-backed nonmetallic customized post (comparable to cast metal)
- Identifies PEEK/PEKK debonding as a primary failure mechanism — a practical clinical warning
- Frames individually formed FRC (EverStick, Ribbond) as low-catastrophic-failure-risk options

## Methodology

- Study design: Systematic review
- Databases: 5 (comprehensive)
- Included: 12 studies, 829 teeth
- Materials evaluated:
  - **Indirect**: CAD/CAM zirconia, PEEK, PEKK
  - **Direct**: EverStick Post, Ribbond (individually formed FRC); bundle fiber posts; prefabricated fiber posts + composite reline; corono-radicular direct composite
- Primary outcomes: clinical survival, failure mode
- Limitation: limited data, considerable variability, significant heterogeneity

## Results

| Post system | Category | Key finding |
|---|---|---|
| CAD/CAM milled zirconia | Indirect customized | Comparable to conventional cast posts |
| PEEK | Indirect customized | Lower survival; high debonding incidence |
| PEKK | Indirect customized | Lower survival; high debonding incidence |
| EverStick Post / Ribbond | Direct individually formed FRC | Favorable failure patterns |
| Bundle fiber posts / fiber + composite reline | Direct rigid FRC | Included; specific outcomes variable |
| Corono-radicular direct composite | Direct | Included |

Overall conclusion: "promising" across all categories, but evidence is limited and heterogeneous.
