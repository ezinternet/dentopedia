## One-line Summary

In-vitro photoelastic study (PSM-5 blocks, 3 posts × 3): rigid ParaPost (metal) concentrates stress apically under vertical load (FO=4) and cervically under oblique load (FO=5), while fiber and cast posts distribute stress evenly (FO=2+); a 1.25 mm glass fiber post fractured before 90 N oblique load.

## Summary

Rezaei et al. (2010) used photoelasticity to compare stress distribution induced by three prefabricated/custom posts of identical 1.25 mm parallel-sided geometry: a FiberLux glass fiber post, a titanium **ParaPost** (Coltène/Whaledent), and a Ni-Cr cast post. In nine dentin-analogue PSM-5 epoxy blocks, posts were cemented with Panavia F and loaded vertically (135 N) and obliquely (90 N, 26°). Stress magnitude was read as photoelastic fringe order (FO) at coronal, middle, and apical thirds. The rigid ParaPost concentrated stress — apically under vertical load and cervically under oblique load — whereas fiber and cast posts spread stress more evenly along their length. The narrow fiber post could not withstand 90 N oblique loading and fractured. The study provides a mechanistic rationale for the clinical observation that rigid metal posts transfer stress to dentin, while also cautioning that very thin fiber posts are mechanically weak under lateral load.

## Key Contributions

- Head-to-head photoelastic comparison of fiber, ParaPost (metal), and cast posts at matched width/geometry.
- Mechanistic evidence that rigid ParaPost concentrates stress (apical FO=4 vertical; cervical FO=5 oblique) rather than distributing it.
- Documents that a 1.25 mm glass fiber post fractures below 90 N oblique load — a width-dependent weakness.
- All specimens stress-free after cementation, isolating loading effects.

## Methodology

- In-vitro photoelastic stress analysis. Nine PSM-5 epoxy blocks (10×45×45 mm, dentin-like modulus), each with a 9 mm × 0.8 mm canal.
- 3 groups (n=3): FiberLux fiber post, ParaPost, Ni-Cr cast post — all 1.25 mm, parallel-sided, flat-ended; Panavia F cementation; 4 mm supracanal length.
- Polariscope imaging under no load, 135 N vertical, 90 N oblique (26°). Fringe order read at coronal/middle/apical; ΔFO ≥ 1 significant. Non-anatomic model (no PDL/root form).

## Results

| Loading | ParaPost (metal) | Fiber post | Cast post |
|---|---|---|---|
| Vertical 135 N | Apical FO=4 (coronal/middle 2+) | Even, FO=2+ | Even, FO=2+ |
| Oblique 90 N | Cervical FO=5 (mid 1, apical 2) | Fractured before 90 N | Cervical FO=3+ (mid/apical 1+) |
| No load | Stress-free | Stress-free | Stress-free |

- ParaPost showed the highest and most localized stress concentrations of the three.
- Authors advise against 1.25 mm glass fiber posts where high oblique loads are expected.
