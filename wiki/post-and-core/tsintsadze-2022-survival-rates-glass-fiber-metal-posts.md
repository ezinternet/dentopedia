## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 7 RCTs (≥2-yr follow-up): endodontically treated teeth restored with glass-fiber posts had 92.8% survival vs 78.1% for metal posts, but the difference was NOT statistically significant; both are reliable when a post is indicated.

## Summary

This PRISMA/PICOS systematic review and meta-analysis (Tsintsadze 2022) restricted inclusion to randomized controlled trials with ≥2-year follow-up to compare clinical survival, success, and failure of endodontically treated teeth (ETT) restored with glass-fiber-reinforced posts versus metal posts. Across 7 extracted RCTs, glass-fiber posts pooled to 92.8% survival versus 78.1% for metal posts — a 14.7-percentage-point gap that did NOT reach statistical significance. The authors conclude both post materials are clinically reliable when a substantial amount of coronal tooth structure is missing and a post is indicated. Abstract-only ingest (no PMC full text).

## Key Contributions

- RCT-only head-to-head clinical comparison of glass-fiber vs metal post survival in ETT.
- Pooled survival benchmark: 92.8% (glass-fiber) vs 78.1% (metal).
- Established that material choice, in isolation, does not drive a statistically significant survival difference.

## Methodology

Medline/Embase/Scopus + hand search → 188 records → 23 full-text → 7 RCTs. Random-effects meta-analysis (RevMan); chi-square / I-square heterogeneity; odds ratio + 95% CI (alpha=.05); publication-bias check (funnel, Begg, Egger).

## Results

- Overall survival: glass-fiber **92.8%** vs metal **78.1%**.
- No statistically significant difference in survival, success, or failure rates (P > .05).
- Both materials judged reliable for ETT with significant structural loss when a post is indicated.
