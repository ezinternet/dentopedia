## One-line Summary

SR of 8 prospective clinical studies (≥5yr): ferrule/cavity wall preservation is the dominant factor for ETT survival; 7/8 studies show no independent benefit from endodontic post placement.

## Summary

Naumann et al. conducted the first systematic review to specifically compare post versus no-post restoration in endodontically treated teeth (ETTs) using only prospective long-term clinical evidence (≥5 years). Of 7 RCTs and 1 prospective clinical trial included (Cochrane bias rating: 5 studies high risk of bias), ferrule effect was supported by 2 of 3 studies examining it, while 7 of 8 studies found no positive effect of post placement. The authors concluded that ferrule effect and preservation of cavity walls are the predominant survival factors, and that routine post placement is not supported by long-term prospective clinical evidence.

## Key Contributions

- Established clinical evidence hierarchy: ferrule/cavity wall preservation > post placement
- Showed that the "post is necessary" doctrine is not supported by ≥5yr prospective data
- Cochrane-rated quality assessment of long-term ETT restoration studies
- Title framing ("Fake News and Alternative Facts?") explicitly challenged the prevailing clinical dogma

## Methodology

- **Study type**: Systematic review
- **Inclusion**: Prospective clinical studies; humans; post vs. no-post comparison; ≥5yr follow-up
- **Studies**: 7 RCTs + 1 prospective clinical trial (n=8)
- **Quality assessment**: Cochrane risk-of-bias tool (5/8 high risk)
- **Outcomes**: Tooth survival, restoration survival
- **Note**: Abstract-only — detailed subgroup data not available

## Results

| Outcome | Finding |
|---|---|
| Ferrule effect | 2/3 studies support ferrule concept |
| Post effect | 7/8 studies: no independent benefit |
| Predominant survival factor | Ferrule + cavity wall preservation |
| Post placement | Not recommended as routine based on this evidence |
