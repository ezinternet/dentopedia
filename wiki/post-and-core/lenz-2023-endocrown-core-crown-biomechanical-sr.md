## Summary

This systematic review evaluated 31 in-vitro studies comparing the biomechanical performance of endocrowns versus traditional core-crown restorations (with and without intracanal post) for endodontically treated teeth with severe coronal structure damage. The review covered molars (9 studies), premolars (14 studies), and anterior teeth (8 studies).

Key findings: in the majority of studies, endocrowns showed either similar or greater survival rates under both fatigue and monotonic loading than (post-)core-crown restorations, irrespective of tooth type. Endocrowns consistently produced more favorable failure patterns — failures tended to be reparable (adhesive/cohesive in ceramic) rather than catastrophic root fractures seen more often with post-retained restorations.

From a stress analysis perspective, endocrowns generated lower stresses in the restorative material for molars and premolars and in the luting cement for premolars compared to (post-)core-crown restorations. The overall conclusion supports endocrowns as a biomechanically viable — often preferable — conservative alternative to post-retained restorations.

## Key Contributions

- Comprehensive SR across all tooth types (anterior + posterior) — first to include anterior teeth alongside molars and premolars
- Demonstrates endocrown superiority in failure mode, not just fracture load
- Stress-distribution data (FEA + experimental) shows endocrown reduces load on luting interface
- Supports post-free rehabilitation as the default consideration for severely damaged ETT

## Methodology

- **Search**: MEDLINE/PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science
- **Included**: 31 in-vitro biomechanical studies
  - 9 molar studies
  - 14 premolar studies
  - 8 anterior studies
- **Comparator**: endocrown vs (post-)core-crown (with or without intracanal post)
- **Outcomes**: survival/fracture resistance (fatigue and monotonic), failure mode analysis, stress in restorative and luting material
- **Limitation**: abstract-only — full text not retrieved; quantitative meta-analysis limited by protocol heterogeneity

## Results

| Outcome | Endocrown vs (post-)core-crown |
|---|---|
| Survival under fatigue/monotonic load | Similar or greater (majority of studies, all tooth types) |
| Failure mode | More favorable (reparable > catastrophic root fracture) |
| Stress in restorative material | Lower for molars and premolars |
| Stress in luting material | Lower for premolars |
| Overall conclusion | Similar or greater biomechanical performance |
