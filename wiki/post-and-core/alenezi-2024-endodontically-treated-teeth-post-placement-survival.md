## Summary

This SR+MA (Alenezi 2024) addresses the field's most fundamental and controversial decision: does an endodontically treated tooth (ETT) actually need a post? Searching PubMed/Scopus/Web of Science without time restriction, 57 studies entered qualitative synthesis and 17 clinical studies (11 prospective, 6 retrospective; 7,278 patients / 7,330 ETT, mean age 45.5 yr) entered the quantitative analysis. Pooled, post placement was associated with a statistically significant survival advantage over no-post restoration (P<0.001). The clinical caveat — not resolved by this pooled estimate — is that the modern view holds post benefit to be **conditional on the degree of coronal substance loss**; in-vitro/biomechanical reviews repeatedly find no post benefit when an adequate ferrule and coronal walls remain. Abstract-only ingest.

## Key Contributions

- Large pooled clinical cohort (7,330 ETT) directly testing the post-vs-no-post question on survival.
- Statistically significant survival advantage for post placement (P<0.001).
- Provides the clinical counterweight to minimally-invasive no-post / endocrown advocacy.

## Methodology

Four-component search strategy (ETT, fixed prosthesis, post restoration, survival rate); no time restriction; English. 57 studies qualitative → 17 clinical (prospective + retrospective) quantitative.

## Results

- Statistically significant difference in survival between ETT with vs without posts, favoring **posts** (P<0.001).
