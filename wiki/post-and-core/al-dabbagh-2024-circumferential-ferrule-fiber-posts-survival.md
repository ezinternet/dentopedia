## Summary

The ferrule effect — a circumferential band of sound coronal dentin encircled by the crown — is the most-cited predictor of restored-ETT longevity. This PRISMA SR+MA (Al-Dabbagh 2024) isolated that variable specifically in teeth restored with **prefabricated fiber posts**, including RCTs and prospective cohorts with ≥6-month follow-up. From 3731 records, 8 studies (407 teeth) were eligible. In the direct analysis (2 studies, 123 teeth), a uniform circumferential ferrule significantly increased success (RR=1.28, 95% CI 1.06-1.54, P<.05). However, the broader indirect analysis pooling all 8 studies showed no statistically significant difference between ferrule and no-ferrule groups (P>.05). The signal favors preserving a circumferential ferrule, but the evidence base is small and the authors call for higher-quality RCTs. Abstract-only ingest.

## Key Contributions

- First SR+MA to isolate the circumferential-ferrule variable in fiber-post-restored ETT with clinical data.
- Direct evidence of a success benefit from a uniform circumferential ferrule (RR=1.28).
- Honest tempering: the all-study indirect analysis was non-significant, flagging confounders and a thin evidence base.

## Methodology

PRISMA search of major databases → 3731 records → 8 eligible (RCTs + prospective cohorts, ≥6-mo follow-up). Two strata: direct success comparison (2 studies, 123 teeth) and indirect pooled survival/success (8 studies, 407 teeth). RR + 95% CI; chi-square heterogeneity (alpha=.05).

## Results

- **Direct success analysis**: RR=1.28 (95% CI 1.06-1.54), P<.05 favoring a uniform circumferential ferrule.
- **Indirect (all-study) analysis**: no significant survival/success difference (P>.05).
