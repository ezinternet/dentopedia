## One-line Summary

15-year RCT (131 patients, 182 restorations): glass-fiber posts trended toward higher success/survival (prefab 59.2%/68.5%) than metal posts (48.0%/53.6%), but post material had NO statistically significant effect; metal posts failed more by root fracture.

## 한줄요약

15년 추적 RCT(환자 131명, 수복 182건): 유리섬유 포스트가 금속 포스트보다 성공·생존율이 높은 경향(기성 59.2%/68.5% vs 금속 48.0%/53.6%)이었으나 포스트 재료 자체의 통계적 유의 효과는 없었고, 금속 포스트는 치근파절로 실패하는 비율이 더 높았음.

## Summary

This is one of the longest-running randomized controlled trials on post material (Van Landuyt 2025, KU Leuven). 131 patients received 182 post-and-single-crown restorations, randomized to metal post (Parapost XT) or glass-fiber post, with the fiber arm subdivided by canal morphology — prefabricated posts (Parapost Fiberlux) for small round canals and custom fiber posts (Everstick) for wide oval canals. At a mean 15-year follow-up (179.6 months, 91.6% recall), Kaplan-Meier analysis showed a consistent trend favoring glass-fiber posts, strongest for the prefabricated type, but post material had no statistically significant effect on outcome (low effect size). Clinically the more important finding is the failure mode: metal posts failed more frequently by root fracture (often non-restorable), whereas fiber posts produced more repairable failures — the practical argument for the dentin-modulus-matched fiber post. ## Key Contributions

- Rare 15-year prospective randomized clinical data on post material in single-crowned ETT.
- Consistent (non-significant) trend favoring prefabricated glass-fiber posts in both success and survival.
- Differential failure mode documented: metal → root fracture; fiber → repairable failures.

## Methodology

131 patients / 182 restorations randomized metal vs fiber; fiber arm split by canal shape (prefab small RC, custom wide oval RC). Follow-up at 3, 5, 15 years. Kaplan-Meier success/survival curves corrected for clustering.

## Results

- **Success**: metal 48.0% · prefab fiber 59.2% · custom fiber 49.5%.
- **Survival**: metal 53.6% · prefab fiber 68.5% · custom fiber 55.3%.
- No statistically significant effect of post material (low effect size).
- Metal-post failures more often due to **root fracture**; custom fiber posts (wide canals) performed closer to metal than prefab fiber.
