## One-line Summary

In-vitro fluid-filtration study (70 single-rooted teeth, 4 groups × 15 + 2 controls): microleakage of a resin-cemented ParaPost increased significantly over 3 months regardless of sealer type or cementation timing; immediate cementation after AH26 (non-eugenol) gave the least leakage. ## Summary

Al Kahtani (2010) tested whether root canal sealer chemistry (eugenol vs non-eugenol) and the timing of post cementation (immediate vs delayed) affect the coronal seal of a **ParaPost** luted with resin cement, using fluid-filtration microleakage in 70 single-rooted teeth. Neither sealer type nor timing significantly changed overall microleakage. Instead, microleakage rose significantly over time (24 h → 2 months → 3 months) in every group, with persistent voids at the resin–dentin interface. The least leakage occurred with immediate cementation following AH26 (non-eugenol) obturation. Practically, the resin-cemented post seal degrades over time regardless of these two variables, so the post should be relied on for retention rather than as a coronal seal — the definitive coronal restoration remains the primary barrier. This record is abstract-only; group-level numeric leakage values were not retrievable.

## Key Contributions

- Sealer type (eugenol vs non-eugenol) and cementation timing (immediate vs delayed) do NOT significantly change resin-cemented ParaPost microleakage.
- Time is the dominant factor — leakage increases significantly from 24 h to 3 months.
- Voids at the resin–dentin interface are a consistent failure feature.
- Immediate cementation after AH26 gave the lowest leakage among conditions.

## Methodology

- In-vitro microleakage (fluid filtration), 70 extracted single-rooted human teeth.
- Crown-down instrumentation (ProFile size 50 .04; Gates Glidden 2–5).
- 2×2 factorial (n=15): sealer (eugenol vs non-eugenol/AH26) × timing (immediate vs delayed) with resin-cement ParaPost; 2 control groups (n=5).
- Leakage measured at 24 h, 2 months, 3 months.

## Results

- Significant increase in microleakage over time in all groups (24 h < 2 mo < 3 mo).
- No significant effect of sealer type or cementation timing on overall leakage.
- Least microleakage: immediate cementation after AH26 (non-eugenol) at all three time points.
- Voids at the resin–dentin interface present across groups.
