## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1111/ipd.70086](https://doi.org/10.1111/ipd.70086)), this 4-week randomised, controlled, two-treatment, parallel-group, examiner-blind trial compared an advanced oscillating-rotating (OR) electric toothbrush (Oral-B iO2 with Gentle Care brush head) against a manual toothbrush (Paro Junior Soft) in 60 children aged 6–10 years. Both groups brushed twice daily with a fluoridated dentifrice; plaque was scored at baseline and 4 weeks with the Turesky modification of the Quigley-Hein Plaque Index and analysed by ANCOVA. At 4 weeks the electric group achieved 51% greater whole-mouth plaque reduction (0.67 vs 0.44; p=0.003) and 42–64% greater reductions across lingual, buccal, approximal, and molar subregions (p≤0.021). This page is **abstract-only** — full text was not retrieved, so methodological detail and exact subregion values beyond the abstract are unavailable.

Clinically: in 6–10-year-old children, an advanced oscillating-rotating electric toothbrush meaningfully outperforms a manual brush for plaque control over 4 weeks, including hard-to-reach approximal and molar surfaces — a relevant option when a child's manual brushing dexterity is still developing, though caries/gingival and longer-term outcomes were not measured.

## Key Contributions

- Direct pediatric (6–10 yo) head-to-head efficacy data for an advanced OR electric toothbrush vs a manual toothbrush — a population under-represented in the powered-vs-manual literature.
- Statistically significant whole-mouth plaque-reduction superiority (51% greater) for the OR electric toothbrush at 4 weeks.
- Consistent benefit across all measured subregions (lingual, buccal, approximal, molar), not limited to easy-access surfaces.

## Methodology

- **Design**: 4-week randomised, controlled, two-treatment, parallel-group, examiner-blind clinical study; analysed by ANCOVA.
- **Participants**: 60 children aged 6–10 (30/group); mean (SD) age 7.7 (±1.33) years; 58 completed.
- **Test**: Oral-B iO2 advanced OR electric toothbrush with Gentle Care brush head.
- **Control**: Paro Junior Soft manual toothbrush.
- **Protocol**: twice-daily brushing with fluoridated dentifrice.
- **Outcome**: plaque at baseline and 4 weeks via Turesky modification of the Quigley-Hein Plaque Index.

## Results

- Enrolment 60 (30/group); 58 completed the trial.
- Whole-mouth plaque reduction at week 4: test 0.67 vs control 0.44 — **51% greater** for the OR electric toothbrush; **p = 0.003**.
- Subregion reductions (lingual, buccal, approximal, molar): all significantly greater for the test group, **p ≤ 0.021**, ranging **42%–64% greater** than control.
- Conclusion: the advanced OR electric toothbrush produced significantly greater plaque reductions than a manual toothbrush in this pediatric population.
