## One-line Summary

4-week examiner-blind parallel-group RCT (n=60, 6–10 yo children, Israel): an advanced oscillating-rotating electric toothbrush (Oral-B iO2) gave 51% greater whole-mouth plaque reduction than a manual toothbrush (TQHPI change 0.67 vs 0.44; p=0.003), with 42–64% greater reductions across all subregions (lingual, buccal, approximal, molar; p≤0.021); no adverse events.

## Summary

This 4-week randomised, controlled, two-treatment, parallel-group, examiner-blind trial (Int J Paediatr Dent 2026; 36:585–592; DOI 10.1111/ipd.70086) evaluated the plaque-reduction efficacy of an advanced oscillating-rotating (OR) electric toothbrush (Oral-B iO2 with Gentle Care brush head) versus a manual toothbrush (Paro Junior Soft) in 60 children aged 6–10 years at Hadassah–Hebrew University Medical Center, Jerusalem. Both groups brushed twice daily with fluoridated dentifrice (1450 ppm F). Plaque was scored at baseline and at 4 weeks using the Turesky Modified Quigley-Hein Plaque Index (TQHPI) and analysed by ANCOVA with baseline TQHPI as covariate.

At 4 weeks, both groups achieved significant within-group plaque reductions (p<0.001). The advanced OR electric toothbrush delivered a statistically significantly greater whole-mouth plaque reduction than the manual toothbrush: adjusted mean change 0.670 vs 0.444 (51% greater; p=0.003). The electric toothbrush showed significantly greater reductions across all subregions: lingual (+64.3%, p<0.001), approximal (+52.4%, p=0.002), molar (+42.8%, p=0.011), and buccal (+41.8%, p=0.021). No adverse events were observed or reported.

Clinically, in 6–10-year-old children — an age group where manual-brushing dexterity is still developing — an advanced OR electric toothbrush meaningfully outperforms a manual brush for plaque control over 4 weeks, including hard-to-reach approximal and molar surfaces (where 95% of deciduous caries occur). The study did not assess gingivitis or long-term caries outcomes, and all three P&G-affiliated authors present conflicts of interest, but the examiner-blind RCT design is methodologically sound. Note: the tested brush (plain white) lacked the compliance-enhancing features (music timer, character handle) of the commercial Oral-B iO Kids device, so real-world results with the marketed version may be more favourable.

## Key Contributions

- First published clinical RCT of the Oral-B iO2 advanced oscillating-rotating electric toothbrush in a paediatric population (ages 6–10).
- Demonstrates superior whole-mouth plaque-reduction efficacy (51% greater than manual) with consistent benefit across all measured subregions — lingual, approximal, molar, buccal — at 4 weeks (p≤0.021 for all).
- Provides subregion-level quantitative data (TQHPI change scores with SE and 95% CI) for each surface type, enabling comparison with existing paediatric powered-toothbrush literature.
- Confirms safety profile: no adverse events with either device.
- Contextualises within the oral-health trajectory: high plaque at age 5 predicts quadrupled tooth loss by age 32 (Broadbent et al. 2011), framing childhood plaque control as a high-return preventive investment.

## Methodology

- **Design**: 4-week, single-centre, randomised, controlled, two-treatment, parallel-group, examiner-blind RCT (January–February 2025, Jerusalem, Israel).
- **Participants**: 60 children aged 6–10 years (30/group); mean (SD) age 7.7 (±1.33); 58 completed (2 excluded from manual group for antibiotic use during study).
- **Stratification**: block size 4, stratified by baseline TQHPI (≤4.0 vs >4.0) and sex; sponsor-generated randomisation sequence.
- **Test**: Oral-B iO2 advanced OR electric toothbrush (Gentle Care brush head); plain white model without Kids' compliance features; 2×/day, 2 min/session per manufacturer instructions.
- **Control**: Paro Junior Soft manual toothbrush; 2×/day in customary manner.
- **Common**: Colgate Junior fluoridated toothpaste (1450 ppm F); supervised initial brushing without mirror; 3-h pre-visit fast from food/drink; afternoon clinic visits.
- **Outcome measure**: Turesky Modified Quigley-Hein Plaque Index (TQHPI; 0–5 per surface, 6 sites/tooth) applied after red disclosing solution; assessed at baseline and week 4; same calibrated examiner throughout.
- **Primary variable**: 4-week whole-mouth TQHPI change from baseline.
- **Analysis**: ANCOVA (baseline TQHPI as covariate); paired t-test for within-group significance; subregion analyses for lingual, buccal, approximal, and molar.
- **Power**: ≥80% (min 28 completers/group) to detect 0.356 difference, SD=0.467, α=0.05 two-sided.
- **Safety**: absence of irreversible adverse events.

## Results

### Whole-Mouth Plaque (Primary Outcome)

| Group | n | Baseline | Week 4 | Adj. Change (SE) | %Change | p (vs baseline) | Between-group p | %Greater vs Manual |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Electric (Oral-B iO2) | 30 | 4.04 | 3.37 | 0.670 (0.0501) | 16.6% | <0.001 | 0.003 | 51.0% |
| Manual (Paro Junior) | 28 | 4.04 | 3.60 | 0.444 (0.0518) | 11.0% | <0.001 | — | — |

Treatment difference: −0.227 (SE 0.0721); 95% CI (−0.371, −0.082).

### Subregion Results (Electric vs Manual, Week 4)

| Region | Electric Adj. Change | Manual Adj. Change | %Greater (Electric) | p (between) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Lingual | 0.670 | 0.408 | +64.3% | <0.001 |
| Approximal | 0.673 | 0.442 | +52.4% | 0.002 |
| Molar | 0.615 | 0.431 | +42.8% | 0.011 |
| Buccal | 0.674 | 0.475 | +41.8% | 0.021 |

The lingual region showed the largest differential benefit. All subregion between-group comparisons statistically significant (p≤0.021).

### Safety
No adverse events in either group. Both treatments well tolerated.

### Baseline Balance
Groups were well matched: demographics p≥0.848; all baseline TQHPI subregion scores p≥0.252.
