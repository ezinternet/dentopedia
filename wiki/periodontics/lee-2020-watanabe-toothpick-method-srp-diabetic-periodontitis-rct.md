## One-line Summary

Double-blind RCT (n=60 T2DM chronic-periodontitis patients; 3 arms — control / SRP / SRP + Watanabe-method toothpick toothbrushing once weekly ×5; 12 wk): both SRP and SRP+toothbrushing (SRPAT) improved HbA1c and periodontal status, but vs SRP alone the SRPAT arm had significantly less bleeding on probing (−16.5% vs −7.3%) and was the only arm with a significant serum-endotoxin decrease (−0.78 EU/mL, p=0.003).

## Summary

In type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM) patients, periodontal infection feeds a systemic inflammatory/endotoxaemic load that worsens glycaemic control, and these patients are unusually susceptible to infection and bleeding even during *non-surgical* periodontal therapy. This Seoul National University double-blind RCT asked whether adding a **professional manual-toothbrushing adjunct — the Watanabe method (a "toothpick" brushing technique with a 2-row toothbrush)** — to standard scaling and root planing (SRP) gives extra clinical and systemic benefit. The rationale for the toothpick method is mechanical: prior histology shows it raises gingival basement-cell proliferation roughly 2.5× versus a scaler and activates fibroblasts and junctional epithelium, producing changes resembling the repair phase of wound healing rather than mere plaque removal.

Sixty T2DM patients (20 per arm) were randomized to **control**, **SRP**, or **SRP + additional toothbrushing (SRPAT)**, the latter receiving the Watanabe-method brushing once weekly across five visits. Over 12 weeks, both treatment arms lowered HbA1c and improved pocket depth, calculus index, and bleeding on probing, while the control arm worsened. The distinguishing findings: SRPAT cut bleeding on probing far more than SRP alone, and **only** SRPAT achieved a statistically significant fall in serum endotoxin — making the toothpick-method adjunct the better choice for bleeding/infection-prone diabetic patients.

## Key Contributions

- First T2DM-specific RCT positioning the **Watanabe / toothpick toothbrushing method as a professional adjunct to SRP**, judged on systemic markers (serum endotoxin, IL-1β, HbA1c) rather than only local gingival-crevicular-fluid markers.
- Demonstrates the adjunct's value lies in **mechanical wound-healing stimulation**, not just plaque control.
- Identifies a **uniquely SRPAT effect on serum endotoxin** and superior bleeding control — clinically meaningful for the infection-susceptible diabetic patient.

## Methodology

- **Design**: double-blind, parallel-group RCT (preliminary report); trial reg. KCT000416. Patients from a community public-health centre (Seoul), June 2013–June 2014.
- **n = 60** T2DM patients analysed, **20 per arm** (15 dropouts due to age / long protocol).
- **Inclusion**: PD >5 mm + attachment loss in ≥2 quadrants, BOP at those sites, ≥20 teeth, HbA1c ≥6.5%, non-smoker. **Exclusion**: alcohol/drug abuse, chronic liver disease, BMI ≥40.
- **Three arms**:
  1. **Control** — diabetes screening only, no periodontal treatment (treated after study).
  2. **SRP** — supragingival scaling (visit 1), root planing (2 wk later), recall at 12 wk.
  3. **SRPAT** — SRP **plus** the **Watanabe method** of professional toothbrushing with a 2-row toothbrush, performed by a trained dentist **once a week from visit 1 through visit 5**.
- All arms got identical baseline oral-health education + brushing instruction (to neutralize home-care behaviour bias).
- **Outcomes**: primary — HbA1c, serum endotoxin (LAL/Pyrotell-T turbidimetric), serum IL-1β (hs-ELISA); periodontal — PD (UNC-15 probe, 6 sites/tooth), calculus index, BOP. Follow-up 12 weeks; intra-examiner kappa 0.976.
- **Stats**: repeated-measures ANOVA; paired t-test / Wilcoxon (within-group); Tukey / Mann-Whitney (between-group); Bonferroni-Holm in figures. No baseline differences among arms (all p>0.05).

## Results

| Outcome (baseline → 3 mo, change) | SRP | SRPAT | Control |
|---|---|---|---|
| **HbA1c (%)** | 6.64 → 6.47 (−0.17, p=0.009) | 6.68 → 6.43 (−0.25, p=0.001) | 6.76 → 6.98 (**+0.22**, p=0.001) |
| **Serum endotoxin (EU/mL)** | 1.48 → 1.08 (−0.39, **p=0.287 NS**) | 1.70 → 0.92 (**−0.78, p=0.003**) | 1.49 → 2.35 (**+0.91**, p=0.004) |
| **Serum IL-1β (pg/mL)** | −0.19 (p=0.506 NS) | −0.21 (p=0.243 NS) | +0.18 (p=0.360 NS) |
| **BOP (%)** | 18.93 → 11.66 (−7.27, p=0.013) | 22.25 → 5.73 (**−16.52, p<0.001**) | 21.29 → 22.66 (+1.37, NS) |
| **PD (mm)** | −0.08 (p=0.043) | −0.09 (p=0.039) | +0.18 (p=0.001) |
| **Calculus index** | −1.90 (p=0.006) | −1.55 (p=0.001) | +0.50 (NS) |

- **HbA1c**: both treatment arms decreased (~0.2–0.25%, concordant with the ~0.4% SR estimate); control worsened. Between-group p=0.000.
- **Serum endotoxin**: **only SRPAT** showed a significant within-group decrease; the SRP-alone fall was non-significant; control rose. Between-group p=0.000.
- **IL-1β**: unchanged in all arms.
- **BOP**: SRPAT reduction (−16.5%) significantly exceeded SRP alone (−7.3%) (p<0.025) — the headline clinical advantage of the toothpick-method adjunct.
- **Correlations**: baseline endotoxin strongly tracked PD (Spearman rho 0.809, p<0.001); changes in endotoxin and HbA1c correlated with changes in PD/CI/BOP.

**Bottom line**: In T2DM chronic periodontitis, SRP improves both glycaemia and periodontal status; **adding the Watanabe / toothpick professional-toothbrushing method delivers extra benefit precisely where diabetics are most vulnerable** — gingival bleeding control and systemic endotoxin load — though the extra-visit confound and short follow-up temper the strength of the conclusion.
