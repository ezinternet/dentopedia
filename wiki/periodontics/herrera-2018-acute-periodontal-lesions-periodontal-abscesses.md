## One-line Summary
2017 World Workshop review proposing classification frameworks for three acute/combined periodontal conditions — periodontal abscesses (by etiology), necrotizing periodontal diseases (by host immune status), and endo-periodontal lesions (by fractures/perforations and presence of periodontitis).

## Summary
This 2017 World Workshop paper sets the current classification for three clinically urgent periodontal conditions. Periodontal abscesses are best classified by etiology, the most common type arising in pre-existing periodontal pockets. Necrotizing periodontal diseases are tied to the host immune response, which becomes a classification axis (e.g., immunocompromise). Endo-periodontal lesions are classified by the features that actually drive prognosis and treatment — presence or absence of root fractures/perforations and of periodontitis — rather than by historical etiologic schemes. Although these lesions are individually uncommon, they present acutely with pain and rapid destruction, so a prognosis-oriented classification has direct treatment value.

## Key Contributions
- Etiology-based periodontal-abscess classification.
- Host-immune-status axis for necrotizing periodontal diseases.
- Prognosis-driven endo-periodontal-lesion classification (fractures/perforations, periodontitis).

## Methodology
Narrative review of PA, NPD, and EPL literature commissioned for the 2017 World Workshop, synthesizing objective, prognosis-relevant classification criteria.

## Results
PA: classify by etiologic factor (commonly pocket-associated). NPD: incorporate host immune status. EPL: classify by root fractures/perforations and presence/absence of periodontitis — the features governing prognosis and treatment.

## Clinical Application
- For an endo-perio lesion, first determine fracture/perforation and periodontitis status — these decide whether the tooth is restorable and which discipline leads.
- NPD presentation should prompt assessment of the patient's immune/systemic status, not just local debridement.
