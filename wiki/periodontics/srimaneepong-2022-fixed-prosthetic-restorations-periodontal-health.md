## One-line Summary

Narrative review (search 1981–2021) concluding that supragingival margins and CAD/CAM zirconia restorations preserve periodontal health better than subgingival margins and conventional cast-alloy restorations, with marginal discrepancy driving biofilm accumulation, increased GCF flow, recurrent caries and eventual bone loss.

## Summary

This narrative review maps how fixed prosthetic restorations interact with the periodontium. The central thesis: a poor margin is a periodontal liability. Marginal discrepancy leaves a thick, oral-environment-exposed cement layer that dissolves, seeds dental biofilm and microleakage, and produces margin discoloration, increased gingival crevicular fluid (GCF) flow, recurrent caries, pulp infection, and ultimately periodontal lesions and bone loss — the failure cascade of fixed prosthodontics. The review argues margin **position** matters as much as margin **fit**: supragingival (and equigingival) margins permit hygiene maintenance and do not provoke secondary caries or periodontal disease, whereas subgingival margins impede hygiene and risk biologic-width violation. On materials, CAD/CAM zirconia restorations are favored for superior marginal fit, lower inflammation, easier maintenance, and better preservation of periodontal health/oral hygiene compared with conventional-fabrication and non-zirconia alloy constructions; surface roughness is flagged as a biofilm driver, making good polish/finish protective. The practical message is pre-prosthetic periodontal assessment plus prosthodontist–periodontist collaboration.

## Key Contributions

- Frames the restoration–periodontium interface (margin fit, margin position, material, surface finish, contour) as a unified periodontal-health problem.
- Identifies marginal discrepancy as the central failure pathway through to bone loss.
- Provides directional clinical guidance: supragingival margins and CAD/CAM zirconia over subgingival margins and conventional alloys.

## Methodology

- Narrative review; literature search 1981–2021 across PubMed/Medline, Scopus, Google Scholar, Science Direct (English).
- Thematic synthesis (margin fit/position, materials, surface finish, biologic width, maintenance); no meta-analysis, no formal risk-of-bias appraisal.

## Results

- **Margin gap**: no agreed optimum — cited thresholds <120 µm, <100 µm, with an "adequate" 20–75 µm range; larger gaps worsen periodontal sequelae.
- **Margin position**: supragingival best for hygiene; subgingival impedes hygiene and risks periodontal breakdown.
- **Materials**: CAD/CAM zirconia → better marginal fit, less inflammation, easier maintenance vs conventional/other-alloy restorations.
- **Surface finish**: rougher surfaces favor microbial colonization; proper polish is protective.
