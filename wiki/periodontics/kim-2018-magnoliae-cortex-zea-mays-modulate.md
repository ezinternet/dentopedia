## One-line Summary
SNU Periodontology + DongKook Pharm-funded in vitro: combined Magnoliae Cortex 60 µg/mL + maize unsaponifiable extract (Insadol active ingredient) 300 µg/mL on P. gingivalis-stimulated RAW 264.7 macrophages — NF-κB, ERK1/2, iNOS suppression; reduced NO, PGE₂, IL-1β, IL-6; combination more effective than either alone (synergy).

## Summary
In vitro mechanistic study from Seoul National University Periodontology, funded by DongKook Pharmaceutical (manufacturer of Insadol). RAW 264.7 murine macrophages were stimulated with Porphyromonas gingivalis or the TLR1/2 ligand Pam3CSK4 (10 µg/mL), then treated with Magnoliae cortex extract (M, 60 µg/mL), titrated unsaponifiable Zea mays L. extract (Z, 300 µg/mL), or the combination (MZ), with ibuprofen (10 mM) as positive control. The MZ combination suppressed nuclear NF-κB translocation, p44/42 (ERK1/2) MAPK phosphorylation, and iNOS protein expression more strongly than either single extract, with parallel reductions of NO (Griess), PGE₂, IL-1β, and IL-6 (ELISA). This is the in vitro mechanism that the same group's [[drug/kim-2024-magnoliae-cortex-zea-mays-extracts|2024 canine in vivo study]] later confirmed at the tissue level.

## Key Contributions
- **Identifies molecular targets** of the Magnoliae + Zea mays L. combination in periodontal inflammation: NF-κB, MAPK (ERK1/2), iNOS.
- **Demonstrates synergy** between Magnoliae cortex extract and Zea mays L. unsaponifiable fraction — the combined treatment outperforms either single extract.
- **Bridges traditional Korean periodontal supplement (Insadol active ingredient) to modern host-modulation framework** alongside SDD (subantimicrobial-dose doxycycline), NSAIDs, and bisphosphonates.
- Provides the **mechanistic rationale** that justified the subsequent canine in vivo study (Kim 2024) at Inje University.

## Methodology
- **Cell model**: RAW 264.7 murine macrophage cell line (KCLB)
- **Stimuli**: P. gingivalis whole-cell; Pam3CSK4 (TLR1/2 agonist) 10 µg/mL
- **Test compounds** (all in 1% DMSO):
  - M (Magnoliae cortex 75% ethanol extract): 0.6, 6, 60 µg/mL — reference 60 µg/mL
  - Z (Zea mays L. titrated unsaponifiable fraction): 3, 30, 300 µg/mL — reference 300 µg/mL
  - MZ: combined treatment
  - IBU (positive control): ibuprofen 10 mM
- **Assays**:
  - MTT for cell viability
  - Griess reaction (540 nm) for nitrite
  - ELISA (R&D Systems) for PGE₂, IL-1β, IL-6
  - Western blot for NF-κB nuclear translocation, p44/42 MAPK phosphorylation, iNOS

## Results
- **Viability**: M ≤ 60 µg/mL and Z ≤ 300 µg/mL not cytotoxic at working concentrations.
- **Nitrite (NO) production**: MZ > M ≈ Z in reducing nitrite compared to stimulated control.
- **Western blot**:
  - NF-κB nuclear translocation: suppressed by M, Z, and most strongly by MZ
  - p44/42 (ERK1/2) MAPK: phosphorylation reduced by MZ
  - iNOS: protein expression suppressed by MZ
- **ELISA**:
  - PGE₂, IL-1β, IL-6: all reduced by MZ vs stimulated control
- **MZ outperforms single agents** for all cytokine and signaling endpoints — consistent with synergy.

## Clinical Implications
1. **Mechanistic plausibility — but clinical relevance is separate.** This study explains *how* Magnoliae cortex + Zea mays L. extract could be anti-inflammatory in periodontitis (NF-κB / MAPK / iNOS suppression). It does not show *that* they reduce periodontal disease in humans.
2. **The Insadol active ingredient (Zea mays L. unsaponifiable fraction) alone is less potent** in this assay than the combination with Magnoliae cortex. Marketed Korean Insadol contains only the Zea mays unsaponifiable fraction — so this study does **not** validate the registered drug formulation; rather, it suggests that combining it with Magnoliae cortex would improve potency.
3. **Concentrations matter**: 60 µg/mL Magnoliae cortex extract and 300 µg/mL Zea mays L. extract in cell culture are high relative to plausible systemic levels achievable from oral dosing in humans. The translation from in vitro IC values to oral efficacy is not guaranteed.
4. **Funding by Insadol manufacturer (DongKook Pharm)** — bias should be considered. Mechanistic findings of NF-κB/MAPK inhibition are biologically plausible and well-precedented for honokiol/magnolol, so the directional findings are credible, but the magnitude and translational claim need independent replication.
5. **Patient counseling**: For patients asking about Insadol, this paper supports a story of "active ingredient can reduce inflammation signaling in macrophages in lab" — but clinical effectiveness in humans remains weakly supported (see [[evidence-appraisal/choi-2015-statistical-validity-insadol-clinical-effectiveness|Choi 2015]] critical appraisal).
