## One-line Summary

Double-blind RCT (n=251, Chinese adults with gingivitis, 26 weeks): a fluoride toothpaste containing enzymes + proteins (lysozyme, lactoferrin, colostrum-IgG, lactoperoxidase + amyloglucosidase/glucose oxidase) significantly reduced Gingival Index (between-product diff −0.53 at 26 wk, p<0.0001) and supragingival plaque (MQHPI diff −0.96, p<0.0001) vs control fluoride toothpaste, raising non-bleeding sites from 35.7% to 93.8% (control 45.3%).

## 한줄요약

## Summary

This 26-week, double-blind, two-arm parallel RCT (Xi'an, China; NCT06932211) tested a silica fluoride toothpaste (1450 ppm NaF) augmented with a three-enzyme system (amyloglucosidase, glucose oxidase, lactoperoxidase) and three salivary-defence proteins (colostrum IgG, lactoferrin, **lysozyme**) against a plain fluoride control (Sensodyne Pronamel) in adults with mild-to-moderate gingivitis (GI ≥1.0, MQHPI ≥1.5). The enzyme-protein toothpaste produced statistically significant, time-growing improvements in gingival inflammation and supragingival plaque from week 4 onward, replicating earlier UK trial and Danish epidemiological findings in a new population. The between-product GI difference reached −0.53 by week 26, and the proportion of non-bleeding sites in the test group climbed to 93.8% versus 45.3% in control. A key design caveat: with no pre-baseline prophylaxis/run-in, the control arm itself improved substantially (Hawthorne / brushing-instruction effect), so absolute test benefit may be understated at intermediate timepoints. Industry-sponsored (Unilever test product).

## Key Contributions

- Replicates the gingival-health benefit of an enzyme + protein fluoride toothpaste in a **Chinese adult population**, supporting cross-population generalisability of the formulation.
- Benefit emerges by **4 weeks** and the between-product gap **widens through 26 weeks** for both GI and plaque.
- Documents a large status shift: "healthy" participants (<10% bleeding sites) rose 0.8% → 78.3% (test) vs 1.8% → 16.3% (control).
- Provides **ingredient-level evidence for topical lysozyme** (with lactoferrin and a lactoperoxidase enzyme system) — the same lysozyme active present in the 이가탄/CELC systemic tablet, delivered here as a toothpaste.

## Methodology

- **Design**: 26-week, double-blind (participant/examiner/statistician/investigator), randomised, two-group parallel; single calibrated examiner (intra-examiner kappa 0.85).
- **Population**: 251 randomised (test 127 / control 124); 198 completed; 235 in analysis (test 122 / control 113). Age 18–70, ≥20 natural teeth, GI ≥1.0 + MQHPI ≥1.5; excluded smokers, diabetics, pregnancy, recent prophylaxis/antimicrobials.
- **Products**: Test = silica + 1450 ppm NaF + lactoferrin, colostrum, amyloglucosidase, glucose oxidase, lactoperoxidase, K-thiocyanate (lysozyme per ingredient narrative). Control = silica + 1450 ppm NaF only (negative control).
- **Regimen**: ≥1 min twice-daily brushing × 26 wk; supervised brushing at baseline, week 4, week 13; **no prophylaxis or run-in** before baseline.
- **Outcomes**: Löe-Silness GI (0–3) and Modified Quigley-Hein MQHPI (0–5), 6 sites/tooth, all teeth except 3rd molars, at baseline/4/13/26 wk. Primary = GI vs control at week 13.
- **Statistics**: ANCOVA (baseline covariate; product + sex; product×sex retained for MQHPI only) for between-product; paired t-test within-product; sex-stratified block randomisation; no imputation.

## Results

**Gingival Index — between-product (Test − Control; negative favours test):**

| Timepoint | Test (SD) | Control (SD) | Diff (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Week 4 | 1.42 (0.19) | 1.58 (0.41) | −0.18 (−0.23, −0.12) | <0.0001 |
| Week 13 (primary) | 1.15 (0.09) | 1.31 (0.23) | −0.16 (−0.20, −0.11) | <0.0001 |
| Week 26 | 1.06 (0.11) | 1.59 (0.38) | **−0.53 (−0.60, −0.46)** | <0.0001 |

**Supragingival plaque (MQHPI) — between-product:**

| Timepoint | Test (SD) | Control (SD) | Diff (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Week 4 | 3.73 (0.29) | 3.99 (0.42) | −0.29 (−0.38, −0.21) | <0.0001 |
| Week 13 | 3.25 (0.25) | 3.54 (0.41) | −0.35 (−0.45, −0.25) | <0.0001 |
| Week 26 | 2.85 (0.31) | 3.77 (0.40) | **−0.96 (−1.06, −0.85)** | <0.0001 |

- **Non-bleeding sites (test)**: 35.7% (baseline) → 59.0% (4 wk) → 84.8% (13 wk) → **93.8% (26 wk)**; control 45.3% at 26 wk (between-product p<0.01 at all timepoints).
- Both arms improved vs own baseline at all timepoints, but the control arm's GI (1.59) and MQHPI (3.77) regressed toward baseline at week 26 (vs week 13) — attributed to the 3-month gap after the last brushing instruction.
- **Safety**: no product-related AEs; 1 non-product SAE (control), all resolved.
- **Caveats**: sex imbalance (~70% F), differential dropout (control 32 vs test 21, non-product reasons), no pre-baseline prophylaxis (inflates control), industry sponsorship.
