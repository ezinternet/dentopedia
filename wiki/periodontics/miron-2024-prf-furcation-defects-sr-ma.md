## One-line Summary

Systematic review + meta-analysis of 21 RCTs (all class II furcation defects, 786 patients, mean 9.42-mo follow-up): adding PRF to OFD or to a bone graft significantly improves PPD/VCAL/HCAL (OFD vs OFD/PRF: PPD MD 1.73 mm, VCAL MD 1.42 mm, HCAL MD 1.21 mm), PRF alone is clinically equivalent to bone graft/collagen membrane/PRP/rhBMP2, and layering a small biomolecule (metformin, bisphosphonate, statin) or amniotic membrane onto PRF further improves outcomes — amid very high heterogeneity (I²≈85–94%).

## Summary

This Periodontology 2000 systematic review with meta-analysis (Miron, Moraschini et al., 2024) pools **21 RCTs published 2011–2023** (786 participants, mean follow-up 9.42 months, no smokers) to answer three clinical questions about **platelet-rich fibrin (PRF)** in **class II periodontal furcation defects** — the only furcation grade with sufficient RCT evidence. The 11 comparisons are grouped as: (1) does adding PRF help; (2) is PRF as good as established regenerative modalities; (3) does adding a biomaterial/biomolecule to PRF help further.

The bottom line is threefold. **Adding PRF works**: PRF on top of open flap debridement (OFD), or on top of a bone graft (BG), significantly improves probing depth and both vertical and horizontal attachment. **PRF alone is not inferior** to bone graft, collagen membrane, PRP, or rhBMP2 — every head-to-head comparison was statistically non-significant, i.e., PRF is a low-cost, autologous equivalent to more expensive biomaterials. **PRF is a useful carrier**: adding a small biomolecule (metformin, bisphosphonate, statin) or amniotic membrane to PRF significantly outperforms PRF alone, whereas simply adding a bone graft to PRF gives only mild/inconsistent benefit. The authors stress **very high heterogeneity** (I²≈85–94% in Groups 1 and 3), the absence of human **histology** confirming true periodontal regeneration, wide PRF-preparation variability (RCF under-reported), and follow-up shorter than the EFP-recommended 12 months. Results should be read as directional rather than precise.

## Key Contributions

- Most comprehensive **furcation-specific** PRF synthesis: 11 comparisons across 3 clinical questions, with pooled mean differences (95% CI).
- Quantifies PRF's **additive** benefit over OFD and over bone graft for class II furcations.
- Establishes PRF's **clinical equivalence** to bone graft, collagen membrane, PRP, and rhBMP2 — supporting PRF as an autologous, inexpensive alternative.
- Positions PRF as a **drug-delivery matrix**: small biomolecules (metformin/bisphosphonate/statin) or amniotic membrane add significant benefit; a bone graft added to PRF does not (PPD NS).
- Names the evidence gaps blocking a guideline recommendation: no human histology, non-standardized PRF protocols, <12-month follow-up.

## Methodology

- **Design**: PRISMA SR + meta-analysis; PRISMA-P protocol; INPLASY registration 2023100045; no protocol deviations.
- **Eligibility**: RCTs in periodontitis-related class II furcation defects comparing PRF with, or in combination with, another modality; ≥6-month follow-up. Case reports, animal, and retrospective studies excluded.
- **Search**: PubMed/MEDLINE, Cochrane CENTRAL, Scopus, Embase, Lilacs (before Oct 2023) + gray literature; 45 full-text screened → **21 RCTs** included.
- **Analysis**: mean difference with 95% CI, generic inverse-variance, **random-effects** model; heterogeneity by χ² (≤25% low / 25–50% moderate / >50% high); p<0.05; RevMan 5.2.8.
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane **RoB 2** (five domains); operator/surgical-performance bias not rated (cannot be blinded).
- **PRF protocols in the RCTs**: Remi centrifuge 10/21 (48%); 3000 rpm × 10 min 12/21 (57%); 4/21 did not report the centrifuge.

## Results

**Group 1 — addition of PRF (favors PRF):**
- **PPD reduction** (11 studies, I²=94%): OFD vs OFD/PRF **MD 1.73 mm** (95% CI 1.13–2.33); BG vs BG/PRF **MD 0.73 mm** (0.25–1.21).
- **VCAL gain** (11 studies, I²=90%): OFD/PRF **1.42 mm** (1.04–1.79); BG/PRF **0.82 mm** (0.28–1.37).
- **HCAL gain** (10 studies, I²=86%): OFD/PRF **1.21 mm** (0.70–1.73); BG/PRF **1.29 mm** (0.68–1.90).
- Narrative for the 5 OFD-vs-OFD/PRF RCTs: ~1.3 mm relative PPD decrease and ~1.5 mm CAL gain.
- **Furcation closure**: Sharma & Pradeep — 66.7% full closure + 27.7% class I conversion (OFD/PRF); Lohi — class I conversion 60% (BG) vs 100% (BG/PRF).

**Group 2 — PRF vs other modalities (all NS → equivalence):**
- PPD (6 studies, I²=45%): BG p=0.26, CM p=0.07, PRP p=0.20, rhBMP2 p=0.93.
- VCAL (5 studies, I²=56%) and HCAL (4 studies, I²=0%): all subgroups non-significant.
- → OFD/PRF ≈ OFD/BG ≈ OFD/CM ≈ OFD/PRP ≈ OFD/rhBMP2 for every parameter.

**Group 3 — add biomaterial/biomolecule to PRF (favors the addition):**
- **PPD** (8 studies, I²=85%): +AM 1.20 mm (p=0.0003); +metformin 1.06 mm (p=0.001); +bisphosphonate 0.83 mm (p<0.00001); +statin 0.94 mm (p=0.0001); **+BG non-significant** 0.20 mm (−0.28–0.68, p=0.42).
- **VCAL** (8 studies, I²=0%): +BG 1.20 mm (p=0.003); +AM 0.86 mm; +metformin 0.89 mm; +bisphosphonate 0.86 mm — all significant.
- **HCAL** (7 studies, I²=0%): +AM 0.80 mm (p=0.02); +metformin 0.80 mm; +bisphosphonate 0.71 mm; +statin 1.08 mm — all significant.
- Furcation closure (PRF vs PRF+metformin): 52% vs 76% full closure.

**Risk of bias (RoB 2):** 1/21 low across all domains; 3/21 high (mainly dropout >22%); 17/21 "some concerns" (mostly missing allocation-concealment detail).

**Caveats:** very high heterogeneity in Groups 1 & 3; hard-tissue outcomes (VBL/HBL/RBF) too sparse to meta-analyze in most comparisons; all class II only, no class III, no smokers.
