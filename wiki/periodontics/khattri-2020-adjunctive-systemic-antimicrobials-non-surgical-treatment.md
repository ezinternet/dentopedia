## One-line Summary

Cochrane SR+MA of 45 RCTs: adjunctive systemic antimicrobials (most studied: amoxicillin+metronidazole) added to SRP show only very-low-certainty, mostly clinically marginal benefits on CAL/PD/closed pockets/BOP for non-surgical periodontitis treatment, with no reliable evidence one antibiotic is superior.

## Summary

This Cochrane systematic review (search to 9 March 2020) assessed whether **systemic antimicrobials** added to scaling and root planing (SRP) improve outcomes in the non-surgical treatment of periodontitis. Forty-five RCTs were included, comparing SRP + antibiotic versus SRP alone/placebo, or one antibiotic versus another, in patients with previously untreated chronic or aggressive periodontitis (pre-2017 classification). Outcomes — percentage of closed pockets, clinical attachment level (CAL), probing pocket depth (PD), and bleeding on probing (BOP) — were evaluated at short, intermediate, and long term, with certainty graded by GRADE.

The bottom line is that the **evidence is of very low certainty for every comparison and outcome**, particularly at the clinically relevant long-term (≥1 year) follow-up. Some antibiotic regimens (notably amoxicillin + metronidazole, and metronidazole alone) produced statistically significant improvements, but most effects were small and only occasionally crossed a minimally important clinical difference (MICD). There was insufficient evidence to conclude that any specific antibiotic is better than another. No serious adverse events were reported, though common GI side effects (nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea, metallic taste) were frequent. This review supports a cautious, stewardship-minded stance: systemic antibiotics should not be a routine adjunct to SRP.

## Key Contributions

- Comprehensive Cochrane synthesis of **systemic** (not local) antibiotic adjuncts to SRP across all agents and combinations.
- 45 RCTs pooled with GRADE-graded certainty — a reference evidence base for periodontal antibiotic stewardship.
- Demonstrates that statistically significant effects frequently fail to reach an MICD, separating statistical from clinical significance.
- Finds no reliable basis to prefer one antibiotic over another alongside SRP.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review with random-effects meta-analysis; mean differences (MD) with 95% CI.
- **Eligibility**: RCTs in untreated chronic/aggressive periodontitis; SRP + systemic antibiotic vs SRP alone/placebo or vs another antibiotic.
- **Included / excluded**: 45 RCTs included (14 multi-arm; 7 contributed no meta-analysis data); 58 excluded (e.g., 19 used adjunctive *local* antibiotics).
- **Outcomes & timepoints**: closed pockets, CAL, PD, BOP at ≤3 mo, >3 mo, and ≥1 year.
- **Quality**: risk of bias in seven domains, in duplicate; certainty by GRADE. Most trials at unclear or high risk of bias.

## Results

All estimates **very low certainty**. Selected long-term (≥1 yr) findings:

- **Amoxicillin + metronidazole + SRP vs SRP**: closed pockets MD −16.20% (95% CI −25.87 to −6.53; 44 pts); CAL −0.47 mm (−0.90 to −0.05; 389 pts); PD −0.30 mm (−0.42 to −0.18; 389 pts); BOP −8.06% (−14.26 to −1.85; 389 pts). MICD reached for **closed pockets and BOP** only.
- **Metronidazole + SRP vs SRP**: CAL −1.12 mm (−2.24 to 0); PD −1.11 mm (−2.84 to 0.61). MICD reached for **CAL and PD**.
- **Azithromycin + SRP vs SRP**: no difference across outcomes.
- **Doxycycline + SRP vs SRP** (aggressive): PD MICD reached (−1.00 mm).
- **Tetracycline + SRP vs SRP** (aggressive): CAL −2.30 mm (26 pts).
- **Clindamycin + SRP vs SRP** (aggressive): CAL −1.70 mm, PD −1.80 mm favouring clindamycin.
- **Head-to-head**: metronidazole favoured over doxycycline; clindamycin vs metronidazole and clindamycin vs doxycycline showed no difference.
- **Safety**: common GI adverse events; **no serious adverse events** reported in any trial.
