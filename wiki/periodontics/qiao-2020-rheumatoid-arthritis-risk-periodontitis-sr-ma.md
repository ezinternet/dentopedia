## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1016/j.jbspin.2020.04.024](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jbspin.2020.04.024)), this systematic review with meta-analysis (abstract-only — full text not retrieved) pooled 13 studies comprising 706,611 periodontitis patients and 349,983 controls to test whether periodontitis is a risk factor for rheumatoid arthritis (RA). Periodontitis patients had a 69% greater RA risk overall (pooled OR 1.69, 95% CI 1.31–2.17, P<0.0001). The elevation was largest for incident (newly diagnosed) RA and for RA with disease duration exceeding 5 years, consistent with the shared inflammatory and citrullination pathway (the *Porphyromonas gingivalis* PPAD → anti-citrullinated protein antibody hypothesis) linking the two diseases. Clinically: periodontitis is an epidemiologically validated risk marker for RA, so periodontal screening and treatment are reasonable adjuncts in at-risk patients, and conversely RA patients warrant active periodontal surveillance — though the wide subgroup confidence intervals (several crossing 1) and observational design mean this is an association, not proof of causation.

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies the periodontitis → RA direction of the periodontal–systemic link in a very large pooled population (>1 million subjects across 13 studies).
- Reports a robust overall association (OR 1.69, P<0.0001) plus stratification by RA disease type and duration.
- Supports the shared citrullination/inflammatory mechanism at the epidemiologic level, reinforcing the bidirectional periodontitis–RA narrative.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review + meta-analysis.
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane Library, Embase, Web of Science, Wanfang.
- **Comparison**: periodontitis patients vs controls; outcome = occurrence of RA.
- **Effect measure**: pooled odds ratio (OR) with 95% CI.
- **Subgroups**: RA disease type (incident, mixed) and RA disease duration (<5 y, >5 y, mixed).
- abstract-only — full text not retrieved; risk-of-bias, heterogeneity (I²), and study weights are not recoverable from the abstract.

## Results

- **Included studies**: 13 (706,611 periodontitis patients; 349,983 controls).
- **Overall RA risk**: OR 1.69 (95% CI 1.31–2.17; P<0.0001) — 69% greater RA risk in periodontitis patients.
- **Incident RA**: OR 1.70 (95% CI 0.75–3.85; P<0.001).
- **Mixed RA**: OR 1.61 (95% CI 1.26–2.06; P<0.001).
- **RA duration >5 y**: OR 2.88 (95% CI 0.66–12.62; P=0.018).
- **RA duration <5 y**: OR 2.59 (95% CI 0.83–8.11; P<0.001).
- **RA duration mixed**: OR 1.53 (95% CI 1.05–2.22; P<0.001).
- **Conclusion**: increased RA risk in periodontitis vs healthy controls; higher for incident RA and for RA with >5-year duration.
