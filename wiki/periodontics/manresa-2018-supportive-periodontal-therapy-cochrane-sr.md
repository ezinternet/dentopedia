## Summary
This Cochrane SR (CD009376.pub2) evaluated the evidence for different SPT interventions in adults following active periodontal treatment. Three comparisons were made: (1) specialist vs non-specialist SPT delivery, (2) antimicrobial + mechanical debridement vs mechanical alone, and (3) photonic + mechanical vs mechanical alone. Primary outcomes included BOP, probing depth, CAL, and tooth loss. The review found very limited high-quality RCT evidence for any SPT comparison, with most studies having unclear or high risk of bias.

## Key Contributions
- Highest-level synthesis (Cochrane) of SPT intervention evidence
- Identifies critical gap: very few RCTs exist for SPT protocol comparisons
- Antimicrobial adjuncts may provide marginal additional benefit but evidence quality is low
- Photonic therapy evidence insufficient for conclusions
- Specialist vs non-specialist delivery comparison lacks definitive evidence

## Methodology
- **Design**: Cochrane SR (no meta-analysis due to heterogeneity)
- **Comparisons**: 3 main comparisons with multiple sub-outcomes
- **Outcomes**: BOP (%), probing depth (mm), CAL (mm), tooth loss
- **Analysis**: Analysis 1.1–1.2 (specialist), 2.1–2.3 (antimicrobial), 3.1–3.2 (photonics)

## Results
- **Specialist vs non-specialist**: Very limited evidence; uncertain outcomes for BOP and PD
- **Antimicrobial + mechanical vs mechanical alone**: Some additional benefit suggested but low-quality evidence
- **Photonics + mechanical vs mechanical alone**: Insufficient evidence for conclusions
- Overall: Evidence base for SPT protocols is thin; no strong RCT data to strongly favor one approach
