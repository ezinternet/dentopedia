## One-line Summary

EFP 21st Workshop systematic review + meta-analysis (11 studies; mostly RCTs) showing that, for dental biofilm-induced gingivitis, OHI is first-line and professional mechanical plaque removal (PMPR) adds benefit only as an adjunct to OHI (low certainty), with air polishing + ultrasonics as effective as but faster than ultrasonic + rubber-cup polishing (very low certainty) and diode laser offering no adjunctive benefit.

## 한줄요약

치태-유발 치은염에서 구강위생교육(OHI)이 1차 치료이고 전문가 기계적 치태제거(PMPR)는 OHI에 더해질 때만 이득(low certainty)을 주며, 에어폴리싱+초음파가 초음파+러버컵 폴리싱과 동등하면서 더 빠르고, 다이오드 레이저는 부가 이득이 없다는 EFP SR+MA(11편).

## Summary

Prepared for Working Group 1 of the 21st European Workshop on Periodontology, this SR+MA asks how **professional mechanical plaque removal (PMPR)** should be used to treat naturally occurring dental biofilm-induced gingivitis (2018 classification), across three focused questions: whether PMPR helps vs no-treatment/OHI (FQ1), which delivery modality is best (FQ2), and whether professionally administered local adjuncts add value (FQ3). Across 11 studies (mostly RCTs), the evidence supports an **OHI-first hierarchy**: PMPR has no efficacy when patients continue ineffective self-care, but adds benefit when combined with OHI — chiefly in generalised gingivitis (low certainty). For modality, **air polishing + ultrasonics is as effective as ultrasonic + rubber-cup polishing but faster** and better tolerated (very low certainty); glycine air polishing removes stain better. A diode laser adds nothing, and miscellaneous local adjuncts (FQ3) remain unproven. The gingivitis treatment endpoint is conversion to full-mouth BOP < 10%.

## Key Contributions

- First SR to comprehensively map **PMPR modalities and adjuncts specifically for gingivitis** using the 2018 case definition.
- Establishes **OHI as first-line**, PMPR as an adjunct — reframing "prophylaxis" as behaviour-change-anchored rather than instrument-anchored.
- Provides SR+MA support that **air polishing + US ≈ US + rubber-cup polishing** in efficacy but is shorter and preferred — direct input to the guided-biofilm-therapy debate.
- Explicitly finds **no adjunctive benefit of diode laser**, aligning with periodontitis-therapy guidelines against laser adjuncts.
- Flags baseline gingivitis severity/extent as a modifier of PMPR benefit (benefit clearer in generalised cases).

## Methodology

- **Design**: SR + pairwise meta-analysis; common-effect model when <5 studies; Cochrane ROB 2.0 (RCTs) / ROBINS-I (CTs); GRADE certainty.
- **Search**: MEDLINE, Scopus, Embase (inception–2 May 2025) + Cochrane Oral Health register + grey literature; no language restriction; PROSPERO-registered, PRISMA-compliant.
- **Population**: adults with naturally occurring biofilm-induced gingivitis, free of plaque-retentive factors and systemic modifiers; gingival inflammation at **patient level, 2–6 weeks** post-treatment.
- **Included**: 11 studies — FQ1: 4 (3 RCTs + 1 CT); FQ2: 5 RCTs; FQ3: 2 RCTs.
- **Primary outcome**: gingival inflammation scores (BOP, GBS, GI, PBI); target endpoint full-mouth BOP < 10%.

## Results

- **FQ1 — PMPR vs no treatment**: no significant effect on gingival indices at 2–6 weeks in patients with continuing ineffective oral hygiene (significant plaque reduction only); bleeding relapsed by 3 months. → PMPR alone insufficient.
- **FQ1 — PMPR + OHI vs OHI alone**: meta-analysis favours PMPR + OHI for gingival inflammation (**low certainty**); driven by generalised gingivitis (baseline BOP ~33–63%); plaque trend positive but 95% CI crossed no-effect by 0.01.
- **FQ2 — modality**: US + rubber-cup vs air polishing + US → no significant difference in inflammation/plaque (very low certainty); air polishing + US **shorter** (e.g., 24.9 vs 34.1 min; 18.4 vs 20.3 min) and patient-preferred; glycine air polishing superior for stain.
- **FQ2 — diode laser** (810 nm) adjunct to US: no adjunctive benefit.
- **FQ3 — local adjuncts** (topical coconut/sesame oil microneedling, etc.): isolated positive signals, not meta-analysable, limited applicability, uncertain cost-effectiveness.
- **Certainty**: FQ1 low, FQ2 very low; most RCTs "some concerns" on ROB 2.0.
