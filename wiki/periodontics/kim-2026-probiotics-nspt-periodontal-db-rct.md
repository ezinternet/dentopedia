## One-line Summary

Double-blind RCT (n=80; 61 completers, 12 weeks); Lactobacillus PB01 + Enterococcus EB10 lozenges as adjunct to NSPT significantly reduced BoP% (p=0.03) and number of PPD ≥5 mm sites (p=0.01) vs. placebo, with concurrent reductions in red/orange complex periodontal pathogens in subgingival and salivary microbiota.

## 한줄요약

이중맹검 무작위대조시험(n=80; 완료 61명, 12주); Lactobacillus PB01 + Enterococcus EB10 로젠지를 비수술치주치료(NSPT) 보조제로 사용 시 위약 대비 출혈양성률(BoP%, p=0.03)과 치주낭깊이(PPD) ≥5 mm 부위 수(p=0.01)가 유의하게 감소하고 치주낭·타액의 적색·오렌지색 복합군 병원균이 함께 감소함.

## Summary

This double-blind, randomised, placebo-controlled trial (University of Copenhagen, 2022–2023) enrolled 80 adults with stage II or III periodontitis to evaluate whether probiotic lozenges containing Lactobacillus PB01 DSM14870 and Enterococcus EB10 DSM32307 (each ≥1 × 10^9 CFU, with 491 mg xylitol) augmented the effect of NSPT on the subgingival and salivary microbiota. Participants received standard NSPT (per EFP 2022 guideline, Steps 1–2) and were then randomised 1:1 to twice-daily probiotic or placebo lozenges for 12 weeks. Clinical assessments (PPD, BoP, CAL, plaque index), subgingival 16S rRNA sequencing (V1–V3 / MiSeq), salivary metagenomics (NovaSeq 6000), and salivary cytokine/protease immunoassays were performed at baseline, Week 6, and Week 12.

Sixty-one participants completed the trial. The primary microbiological outcome was supported: the placebo group showed significantly higher abundance of red/orange complex pathogens (P. gingivalis, T. denticola, T. forsythia, F. nucleatum) at Week 12, whereas the probiotic group was enriched with commensal health-associated species (Rothia dentocariosa, Neisseria elongata). The secondary clinical outcomes were also supported: BoP% and sites with PPD ≥5 mm were significantly lower in the probiotic group at 12 weeks, both before and after smoking adjustment. CAL and plaque index did not differ significantly between groups. Salivary cytokines (IL-1β, IL-8, MCP-1, MIF) showed no significant between-group differences, highlighting the limitation of saliva as an immunological matrix.

## Key Contributions

1. **Most comprehensive methodology to date** for probiotics in periodontitis: combined 16S rRNA sequencing (subgingival) + shotgun metagenomics (salivary) + full clinical periodontal assessment + immunological biomarkers in a single double-blind RCT.
2. **Confirmed BoP% and PPD ≥5 mm improvement** as adjunct benefit of probiotic over NSPT alone — both significant after smoking adjustment.
3. **Identified strain-specific microbiome signature**: Lactobacillus PB01 + Enterococcus EB10 combination selectively enriched health-associated taxa and depleted Socransky red/orange complex pathogens.
4. **Salivary metagenomics pathway analysis**: probiotic group showed broad reduction in anaerobic metabolic gene abundance (oxidative phosphorylation, fatty acid biosynthesis, anaerobic amino acid catabolism) — mechanistic insight beyond species-level changes.
5. **Immunological null finding is informative**: salivary cytokine panel failed to detect between-group differences — points toward GCF as superior matrix; underpowering acknowledged.

## Methodology

### Design
- **Type**: Double-blind, randomised, placebo-controlled superiority trial
- **Setting**: Department of Odontology, University of Copenhagen
- **Duration**: 12-week intervention (October 2022 – June 2023)
- **Randomisation**: 1:1 computer-generated; single unblinded allocator; lozenges identical in appearance

### Participants
- **N randomised**: 80 (stage II–III periodontitis, 2017 Classification; ≥18 yr; ≥20 teeth)
- **N completed**: 61 (probiotics n=32, placebo n=29)
- **Dropouts** (n=19): antibiotics (6), absence (7), consent withdrawal (2), extraction (2), surgery (1), non-compliance (1)

### Intervention
- **Probiotic lozenge**: Lactobacillus PB01 DSM14870 + Enterococcus EB10 DSM32307 (each ≥1 × 10^9 CFU) + 491 mg xylitol; twice daily for 12 weeks
- **NSPT**: Steps 1–2 EFP 2022 (OHI + professional supra/subgingival debridement with curettes + ultrasonics); no antimicrobials or host modulators

### Clinical Outcomes
- **Primary microbiological**: mean abundance of periodontitis-associated species in subgingival/salivary microbiota at 12 weeks
- **Secondary clinical**: PPD ≥5 mm site count, BoP%, mean PPD
- **Secondary immunological**: salivary IL-8, IL-1β, MCP-1, MIF, albumin, amylase, TPA, chitinase
- **Examiner reliability**: ICC inter-rater PPD 0.800, CAL 0.797 (good–excellent)

### Analysis
- ANCOVA between groups (baseline-adjusted); ANOVA + Tukey within groups; ITT for clinical; LEfSe (LDA >2) + DESeq2 for microbiota; Benjamini-Hochberg FDR; Spearman correlations

## Results

### Clinical (Key between-group comparisons, baseline → Week 12)

| Outcome | Placebo | Probiotics | p | p (smoking-adj) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| BoP% | 29.2 → 18.7% | 32.7 → 15.6% | 0.04 | **0.03** |
| PPD mean | 3.0 → 2.9 mm | 3.0 → 2.7 mm | 0.03 | 0.07 |
| PPD ≥5 mm sites | 22.4 → 14.8 | 20.4 → 8.9 | **0.01** | **0.01** |
| PPD <5 mm sites | 136.9 → 144.5 | 138.8 → 150.3 | 0.03 | **0.02** |
| CAL | 1.57 → 1.49 mm | 1.65 → 1.58 mm | 0.8 | 0.94 |
| PI% | 84.4 → 35.1% | 83.3 → 37.4% | 0.65 | 0.51 |

- 14 vs. 5 participants achieved BoP <10% at Week 12 (probiotics vs. placebo; p=0.03)
- ITT analysis: only BoP% remained significant between groups (p=0.014)

### Microbiological

**Subgingival (16S)**: α-diversity decreased more in probiotic group (Shannon p=0.01; Simpson p=0.03). LEfSe identified 30 differentially abundant species. P. gingivalis and T. denticola enriched in placebo; Rothia dentocariosa and Neisseria elongata enriched in probiotics at Week 12.

**Salivary (metagenomics)**: PERMANOVA — individual 77.4%, NSPT 1%, probiotic intervention 2% (p<0.001) of variance. DESeq2 identified 23 differentially abundant species. P. gingivalis, T. denticola, F. nucleatum, T. forsythia all decreased more in probiotic group. Functional GSEA: probiotic group showed greater reduction in oxidative phosphorylation, anaerobic amino acid catabolism, fatty acid biosynthesis; increase in B6 vitamin and ascorbate metabolism genes.

### Immunological
- No significant between-group differences for any cytokines (IL-8, IL-1β, MCP-1, MIF)
- Albumin increased more in placebo group (p=0.008)
- Chitinase decreased more in probiotic group (p=0.009)

### Limitations
- Short-term (12 weeks) — microbiological relapse at longer follow-up unknown
- Pilot sample size — immunological analyses underpowered
- Saliva vs. GCF — immune markers diluted; recommend GCF for future studies
- Confounders: smokers + diabetics included (post-hoc sub-analyses comparable)
- Single-centre university clinic — generalisability limited
