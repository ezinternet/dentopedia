## One-line Summary

Narrative review (Periodontology 2000) of acute periodontal lesions — periodontal abscesses, necrotizing periodontal diseases, and other acute gingival conditions — covering etiology, classification, differential diagnosis, and emergency/definitive management.

## Summary

This Periodontology 2000 review consolidates the clinical knowledge on **acute conditions affecting the periodontal tissues**. It organizes these into three groups: (1) **abscesses in the periodontium**, (2) **necrotizing periodontal diseases**, and (3) **other acute gingival lesions** caused by processes not associated with oral bacterial biofilms — infectious diseases, mucocutaneous disorders, and traumatic or allergic lesions. The review highlights that, although several of these conditions are uncommon, they frequently present as dental emergencies, and accurate differential diagnosis is essential for proper management.

## Key Contributions

- Integrated framework for the full spectrum of acute periodontal lesions, separating **biofilm-associated** (abscesses, necrotizing periodontal diseases) from **non–biofilm-associated** (infectious, mucocutaneous, traumatic, allergic) conditions.
- Classifies **periodontal abscesses** primarily by etiology, distinguishing those arising in a **pre-existing periodontal pocket** from those at **previously healthy sites**.
- Flags the systemic relevance of the periodontal abscess: a frequent emergency that can compromise tooth prognosis and seed infection at distant sites.
- Lays out host-modifying predisposing factors for **necrotizing periodontal diseases** (HIV, malnutrition, stress, tobacco), positioning them as the most severe and most rapidly destructive biofilm-associated diseases.

## Methodology

Narrative / state-of-the-art review (not systematic). The authors synthesize the literature — clinical studies, surveys, case reports, and classification consensus documents — and structure each lesion category by clinical features, etiology/predisposing factors, diagnosis, and treatment (acute management followed by definitive treatment of the underlying condition).

## Results

- **Periodontal abscess** — Management: drainage and tissue debridement; systemic antimicrobials decided case-by-case based on local and systemic factors. Definitive treatment of the pre-existing periodontal condition is undertaken only **after** the acute phase is controlled.
- **Necrotizing periodontal diseases** — Triad: **papilla necrosis, gingival bleeding, pain**. Low prevalence but most severe biofilm-associated conditions, with very rapid tissue destruction. Treatment: superficial debridement, careful mechanical oral hygiene, chlorhexidine rinsing, daily re-evaluation; adjunctive systemic antimicrobials (**metronidazole** as first choice) in severe or nonresponding cases. Definitive treatment of the pre-existing gingivitis/periodontitis follows control of the acute phase.
- **Other acute gingival lesions** — Gingival involvement usually mild but conditions are common and may prompt emergency visits. Present typically as erythematous, sometimes erosive lesions; erosive lesions arise from trauma or from rupture of vesicles/bullae. **Differential diagnosis** is the key to adequate management.
