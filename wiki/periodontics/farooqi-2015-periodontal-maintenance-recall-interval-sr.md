## 한줄요약
치주유지치료(PM) 적정 주기 SR (JEBDP 2015): 8편 코호트, RCT 없음; 3–6개월 단기 주기 → 치아보존 유리; 모든 환자 일률적 주기 권고 근거 부족; RCT 필요.

## Summary
Systematic review evaluating evidence for specific recall intervals for periodontal maintenance (PM) visits. Databases: MEDLINE, EMBASE, PubMed; until April 2014; 1,095 abstracts screened. Only 8 cohort studies met inclusion criteria. Zero RCTs found — a major evidence gap.

All 8 studies assessed compliance with a recommended 3–6 month PM interval as primary outcome. Shorter PM intervals (3–6 months) favored tooth retention in compliant patients. In 2 studies reporting mean recall intervals, significant tooth loss differences emerged when intervals approached 12 months. Conclusion: evidence for prescribing a specific interval for all patients is weak; risk-stratified intervals likely more appropriate.

## Key Contributions
- First SR specifically on PM recall interval: confirms the 3–6 month clinical standard is practice-based, not RCT-based
- Zero RCTs identified — large evidence gap remains; systematic evidence unavailable to individualize intervals
- Approaching 12-month intervals: associated with significantly more tooth loss — suggests there is a meaningful lower bound
- Implies need for risk-stratified PM intervals (high vs low caries/periodontitis risk) rather than uniform prescription

## Methodology
- **Databases**: MEDLINE, EMBASE, PubMed (until April 2014)
- **Screened**: 1,095 abstracts/titles
- **Included**: 8 cohort studies (0 RCTs)
- **Quality assessment**: CASP (Critical Appraisal Skills Programme)
- **Primary outcome**: Tooth retention by PM compliance status (regular complier vs erratic/non-complier)

## Results
- Regular compliers (3–6 months): better tooth retention vs erratic/non-compliers
- Non-compliance → progressive tooth loss, significantly worse as interval extends toward 12 months
- No RCT evidence; current 3–6 month recommendation is observational consensus only
