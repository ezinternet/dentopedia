## One-line Summary

In-vitro (human PDLSC) + in-vivo (rat, n=6/group) study: the oxysterol combination 22(S)- + 20(S)-hydroxycholesterol (SS) drives PDLSC osteogenesis via reciprocal LXR↔Hedgehog signaling and promotes rat alveolar socket bone regeneration with efficacy comparable to rhBMP-2 (μCT bone-volume difference between groups was not statistically significant).

## 한줄요약

시험관(사람 치주인대줄기세포 PDLSC) + 동물(rat 발치와, 군당 n=6) 연구: 옥시스테롤 조합(SS = 22(S)- + 20(S)-hydroxycholesterol)이 LXR↔Hedgehog 상호신호를 통해 PDLSC 골분화를 유도하고 발치와 골재생을 rhBMP-2와 동등 수준으로 촉진했다 (단, μCT 신생골 부피의 군간 차이는 통계적으로 유의하지 않았다).

## Summary

Periodontal regeneration needs osteoinductive factors; rhBMP-2 works but carries safety/cost concerns (ectopic bone, inflammation, resorption, carcinogenicity worries). This study tested a naturally occurring **oxysterol combination (SS)** — equimolar 22(S)-hydroxycholesterol + 20(S)-hydroxycholesterol — as a BMP-2 alternative, the first such test in a periodontal context. Using human PDLSCs (in-vitro) and a rat maxillary first-molar extraction-socket defect (in-vivo), SS induced osteogenic differentiation and socket bone fill at a level the authors describe as equivalent to rhBMP-2.

## Key Contributions

- First demonstration that oxysterols promote **periodontal/PDLSC** osteogenesis (prior oxysterol osteogenesis work was in ESC/MSC and spinal/cranial models).
- Identifies a **reciprocal LXR ↔ Hedgehog cross-talk** as the mechanism: LXRα/β knockdown suppresses Hh (Smo/Gli1), and Smo inhibition (22-NHC) suppresses LXRα/β.
- Frames SS oxysterol as a low-cost, naturally occurring **BMP-2 alternative** for alveolar/periodontal bone regeneration.

## Methodology

- **In-vitro**: STRO-1(+) human PDLSCs (passage 4–7), SS 0.5/1/5 μM in osteogenic medium. Outcomes: ALP, intracellular Ca²⁺, Alizarin Red mineralization, RT-qPCR + Western + immunofluorescence for OCN/OSX/OPN/RUNX2. Mechanism: LXRα/β, ABCA1, Smo, Gli1; LXRα/β siRNA knockdown; Smo inhibitor 22-NHC.
- **In-vivo**: 18 male Sprague–Dawley rats; 3 groups (control / rhBMP-2 / SS), n=6 each. Bilateral maxillary first molar extraction; SS injected into sockets days 3 + 5, rhBMP-2 day 3; sacrifice day 15. Outcomes: μCT new bone volume %, socket Western blot (COLIA/ALP/RUNX2/OCN), H&E + IHC (OCN/ALP) at 10/15 days. Stats: Kruskal–Wallis + Tukey HSD.

## Results

- **PDLSC**: SS significantly increased ALP activity, intracellular Ca²⁺, and matrix mineralization, and raised osteogenic marker (OCN/OSX/OPN/RUNX2) mRNA and protein vs control (effect not strictly dose-dependent).
- **Signaling**: SS upregulated LXRα/LXRβ + ABCA1 and Smo/Gli1. LXR siRNA blocked SS-induced Smo/Gli1; 22-NHC blocked SS-induced LXRα/β; either intervention abolished SS-induced osteogenic markers — confirming bidirectional LXR↔Hh dependence.
- **Alveolar bone (in-vivo)**: μCT showed BMP-2 and SS sockets filled to the alveolar crest while control sockets had unfilled centers. **New-bone volume showed no statistically significant difference among the three groups**; SS appeared visually higher than BMP-2 on μCT but this was not statistically supported. Socket Western blot and H&E/IHC showed higher COLIA/ALP/RUNX2/OCN and more new bone in BMP-2 and SS groups vs control.
- **Conclusion**: SS oxysterol combination promotes alveolar bone regeneration comparable to rhBMP-2, acting through PDLSC osteogenesis with LXR↔Hh cross-talk.

> [!note] Interpretation caveat
> The headline "equivalent to BMP-2" rests on a μCT comparison that was **not statistically significant** (small n, 15-day endpoint), supported qualitatively by Western blot/histology. The in-vivo model is a socket bone-fill, not a true periodontal (intrabony/furcation) defect, and no cementum/PDL-fiber regeneration was assessed despite the "periodontal regeneration" framing.
