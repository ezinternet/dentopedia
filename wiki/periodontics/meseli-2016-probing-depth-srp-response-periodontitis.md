## One-line Summary
Prospective clinical study (JIUFD 2016; 15 chronic periodontitis patients, 1,672 pockets of 3–9 mm IPD) on initial probing depth (IPD) and SRP response: PD reduction was significant in all groups except IPD=3 mm and correlated positively with IPD; single-rooted teeth gained attachment (AG) significantly at all IPD ≥4 mm, whereas multi-rooted teeth showed significant AG only at IPD ≥7 mm.

## Summary
Prospective clinical study quantifying the relationship between initial probing depth (IPD) and clinical response to non-surgical periodontal treatment (SRP) in 15 chronic periodontitis patients. Total: 1,672 periodontal pockets (3–9 mm IPD). Treatment: OHI + SRP in two sessions. Measurements at baseline and 8 weeks post-treatment: probing depth (PD), clinical attachment level (CAL), gingival recession (GR). Pockets grouped by IPD value and root number (single- vs multi-rooted).

Key result: positive correlation between IPD and PD reduction (deeper = greater reduction). Single-rooted teeth: significant AG at all IPD ≥4 mm; multi-rooted: AG significant only at IPD ≥7 mm. IPD=3 mm: no significant AG in any group, and no significant PD reduction in some subgroups.

## Key Contributions
- Confirms depth-dependent response: SRP most effective at moderate-to-deep pockets
- Multi-rooted teeth are harder to treat: AG only at IPD ≥7 mm vs all levels ≥4 mm in single-rooted — clinically important threshold
- IPD=3 mm pockets: no meaningful AG; if attachment loss is concern, deeper probing is prerequisite for clinical benefit
- Positive IPD-PD correlation independent of root anatomy (single and multi-rooted)

## Methodology
- **Design**: Prospective; 15 chronic periodontitis patients; 1,672 pockets (3–9 mm IPD)
- **Treatment**: OHI + SRP in 2 sessions
- **Measurements**: PD, CAL, GR at baseline and 8 weeks
- **Groups**: By IPD (3–9 mm) × root number (single- vs multi-rooted)
- **Statistics**: Paired tests; p<0.001 threshold; correlations within subgroups

## Results

| Group | PD Reduction | AG | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Single-rooted, IPD 4–9mm | Significant (p<0.001) | Significant | Positive IPD correlation |
| Single-rooted, IPD=3mm | Significant | Not significant | No meaningful AG |
| Multi-rooted, IPD ≥7mm | Significant (p<0.05) | Significant | Only high-IPD group gains |
| Multi-rooted, IPD <7mm | Significant | Not significant | No AG despite PD reduction |
