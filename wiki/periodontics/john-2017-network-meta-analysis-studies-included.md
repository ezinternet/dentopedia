## Summary

The 2015 ADA Clinical Practice Guideline (Smiley et al.) assembled the most exhaustive RCT library on scaling and root planing (SRP) with or without adjuncts for chronic periodontitis, but compared each adjunct only against SRP alone — never against one another. This network meta-analysis fills that gap by reusing the Guideline's primary studies. Starting from 72 Guideline studies, the authors removed 11 SRP-versus-untreated-control trials, leaving 61 RCTs with 74 SRP+adjunct vs SRP-alone comparisons across nine adjuncts. Using a star-shaped (common-comparator) frequentist NMA, 36 indirect comparisons were generated. No adjunct was statistically superior to any other; all pairwise differences carried wide confidence intervals. Local doxycycline hyclate gel and diode-laser photodynamic therapy had the highest probabilities of ranking first and second. Adjuncts collectively improved CAL by only about a third of a millimeter over 6-12 months versus SRP alone, and a contour-enhanced funnel plot showed publication bias that inflated the observed effects by roughly 20%. The authors stress that CAL is a clinician-measured surrogate, so the patient-perceived value of such small gains is uncertain.

## Key Contributions

- First head-to-head comparative-efficacy synthesis of the ADA Guideline's SRP adjuncts, complementary by design (same primary studies).
- Comparative ranking of nine adjuncts via ranking probabilities and SUCRA.
- Quantified publication bias (~20% effect inflation) using a contour-enhanced funnel plot.
- Reframes interpretation toward patient relevance: a ~0.3 mm CAL gain is a clinician-measured physical outcome, not patient-reported.

## Methodology

- Star-shaped frequentist NMA; nine adjuncts (plus SRP) connected through SRP-alone → 9×8/2 = 36 indirect comparisons.
- Primary outcome: change in clinical attachment level (CAL) at ≥6 months.
- Sample: 61 RCTs, 74 pairwise SRP+adjunct vs SRP comparisons (from 72 Guideline studies, 11 control-only trials excluded).
- Nine adjuncts: SDD, systemic antimicrobials, chlorhexidine chips, doxycycline hyclate gel, minocycline microspheres, PDT (diode laser), diode laser (non-PDT), Nd:YAG laser, erbium laser.
- Five progressive grouping schemes (9 → 4 → 3 → 2 → all combined) to test whether broader mechanistic groupings changed effects.
- Parametric bootstrap (50,000 reps); SUCRA computed; I² for heterogeneity; contour-enhanced funnel plot for publication bias.

## Results

- All 36 pairwise differences were non-significant with wide CIs; no adjunct beat another.
- Doxycycline hyclate gel ranked first; diode-laser PDT ranked second (highest ranking probabilities).
- Adjuncts improved CAL by ~0.3-0.35 mm over 6-12 months vs SRP alone.
- NMA effects closely matched the original Guideline meta-analysis effects.
- Publication bias evident; missing small-effect studies inflated observed effects by ~20%.
