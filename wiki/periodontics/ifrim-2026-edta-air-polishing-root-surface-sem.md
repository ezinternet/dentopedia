## One-line Summary

In-vitro SEM study (10 periodontitis-affected human teeth, 20 samples) showing SRP+EDTA leaves a continuous smear layer, adjunctive erythritol air polishing lowers instrumentation-mark scores (Se<S, p=0.001) but raises crack scores (Se>S, p=0.001), while smear-layer scores are comparable across all protocols (p=0.950) and CEJ behaves like mid-root.

## Summary

This ex-vivo scanning electron microscopy (SEM) study evaluated how adjuncts to scaling and root planing (SRP) alter the micromorphology of periodontitis-affected human root surfaces. Ten extracted teeth (premolars, molars) yielded 20 samples, with 2 teeth as descriptive controls. Three mid-root protocols were compared — **S** (SRP + 24% EDTA), **Se** (SRP + erythritol air polishing + 24% EDTA), and **Sg** (SRP + glycine air polishing + 24% EDTA) — plus a CEJ group **Js** (SRP + 24% EDTA). Ordinal scores (0-3) were assigned for instrumentation marks (×100), surface cracks (×100), and smear layer/residual detritus (×1000).

The central clinical message: adding low-abrasive air-polishing powder and EDTA to SRP does **not** measurably reduce the smear layer (scores comparable across all three protocols), and erythritol air polishing produces a trade-off — fewer instrumentation marks but more surface cracks. Cementum was preserved in all treated groups and no residual calculus microislands were seen after instrumentation. The CEJ region responded to controlled debridement much like mid-root surfaces (differing only in marks), suggesting careful instrumentation of this anatomically sensitive zone does not add surface damage. Findings are exploratory (2 teeth/group, no power calculation) and should be interpreted with caution.

## Key Contributions

- Tests the **combined** effect of SRP + EDTA ± air-polishing powders on root surface, rather than isolating each agent.
- Uses **periodontitis-affected human roots with real calculus** as substrate (more clinically relevant than bovine/healthy teeth).
- Distinguishes three independent root-surface outcomes — marks vs cracks vs smear layer — that respond differently to the same protocol.
- Adds a CEJ-vs-mid-root comparison for the anatomically challenging cementoenamel junction.

## Methodology

- **Design**: ex-vivo SEM, semi-quantitative ordinal scoring; 8 evaluations per group per parameter.
- **Samples**: 10 extracted periodontitis-affected human teeth → 20 samples (~4×4×2 mm); 2 control teeth.
- **Groups**: Ct (uninstrumented control), S (SRP+EDTA), Sg (SRP+glycine AP+EDTA), Se (SRP+erythritol AP+EDTA), Js (CEJ; SRP+EDTA).
- **SRP**: ultrasonic scaler (1S tip, Acteon) → Gracey 5/6 curettes, 45-90° angulation, 4 light standardized strokes, single trained operator.
- **Air polishing**: glycine (AIR-FLOW PERIO) or erythritol (AIR-FLOW PLUS), EMS, 5 s at 5 mm working distance.
- **EDTA**: 24% solution, 2-min immersion, water-jet rinse.
- **SEM**: graded ethanol dehydration, gold-palladium sputter coating, 100×-15,000×, single blinded reader.
- **Statistics**: Kruskal-Wallis (S/Se/Sg) + Bonferroni pairwise; Mann-Whitney U (S vs Js); p<0.05; exploratory, no power calculation.

## Results

| Outcome | Overall test (S vs Se vs Sg) | Key pairwise finding |
|---|---|---|
| Instrumentation marks (×100) | Kruskal-Wallis **H = 13.411, p = 0.001** | **Se < S (p = 0.001)**; Sg vs S p = 0.054 (NS); Se vs Sg p = 0.646 (NS) |
| Surface cracks (×100) | Kruskal-Wallis **H = 12.038, p = 0.002** | **Se > S (p = 0.001)**; Sg vs S p = 0.314 (NS); Se vs Sg p = 0.018 (NS after Bonferroni) |
| Smear layer (×1000) | Kruskal-Wallis **H = 0.102, p = 0.950 (NS)** | No difference across protocols |

- **CEJ (Js) vs mid-root (S)** — Mann-Whitney: significant **only for marks** (U = 9.000, Z = -2.604, **p = 0.009**); cracks p = 0.116 (NS); smear layer p = 0.913 (NS).
- **Qualitative**: S group showed a continuous smear layer obscuring the substrate; Se/Sg showed pebble-like cementum microtexture with discrete smear layer + scratches; no calculus microislands on instrumented samples; dentinal tubules not seen; cementum preserved across treated groups.
- **Bottom line**: EDTA did not achieve complete smear-layer removal; air-polishing powder choice (erythritol) shifts the marks/cracks balance but not the smear layer; controlled debridement at the CEJ is comparable to mid-root.
