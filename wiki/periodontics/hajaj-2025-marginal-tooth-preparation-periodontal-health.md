## One-line Summary

Narrative review (112 studies) comparing horizontal preparations (chamfer/shoulder) with the vertical Biologically Oriented Preparation Technique (BOPT); BOPT is associated with greater gingival thickness, stable probing depths (<0.2 mm variation), lower BOP (<10%), and up to 10-year margin stability, but evidence is limited and superiority over horizontal designs is unproven.

## Summary

This review compares two finish-line philosophies for full-coverage restorations and their periodontal consequences. **Horizontal** designs (chamfer, shoulder) are predictable and widely taught but, particularly when extended subgingivally, predispose to plaque accumulation, inflammation, and marginal recession; the review links subgingival chamfer/shoulder margins to BOP >25% and progressive papilla loss in anterior esthetic regions. The **vertical** Biologically Oriented Preparation Technique (BOPT, Loi 2008) is an edgeless concept that removes the pre-existing emergence profile and uses immediate temporization to guide gingival adaptation. Prospective studies (n 40–85, 6–10 year follow-up) associate BOPT with increased gingival thickness, stable probing depths (mean variation <0.2 mm), and lower BOP (<10%); a 10-year/77-restoration trial reported superior periodontal stability and gingival-volume preservation versus chamfer/shoulder. BOPT also preserves more anterior tooth structure in vitro, and digital workflows (intraoral scanning, CAD/CAM, virtual gingiva modeling) improve reproduction of its emergence profile. The authors stress that the evidence is heterogeneous and limited: BOPT is *promising* — especially in thin biotypes and esthetic cases — but its superiority over conventional designs is not established, and large-cohort long-term RCTs are needed. Clinically, the choice should be individualized (BOPT anterior/thin biotype; horizontal posterior/high biomechanical demand).

## Key Contributions

- Adds the preparation-geometry axis (vertical BOPT vs horizontal chamfer/shoulder) to the restoration–periodontium discussion.
- Aggregates periodontal endpoints (PD, gingival thickness, BOP, recession) plus mechanical, esthetic, and digital-workflow domains across 112 studies.
- Delivers a calibrated, non-overstated conclusion: BOPT is promising but not proven superior.

## Methodology

- Narrative review with structured/scoping-style search across PubMed/MEDLINE, Scopus, Web of Science, Google Scholar (search Sep 2023–Jan 2025; sources Jan 2000–Jan 2025); 112 studies included; two independent reviewers.
- No meta-analysis and no formal risk-of-bias tool (narrative scope; heterogeneity acknowledged).

## Results

- **Periodontal**: BOPT — stable PD (<0.2 mm variation), increased gingival thickness, 10-year stability; horizontal — recession/papilla loss anteriorly.
- **Inflammation**: subgingival chamfer/shoulder → BOP >25% at 2–3y; BOPT → BOP <10%.
- **Biomechanics**: BOPT/BOPTm preserve more anterior tooth structure (in vitro).
- **Digital**: IOS + CAD/CAM enhance precision/reproducibility of vertical-margin emergence profiles.
