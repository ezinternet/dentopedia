## Summary
This concise clinical review by Kwon, Lamster, and Levin synthesises the aetiology, risk factors, and treatment continuum for periodontitis. It applies the 2017 World Workshop staging (I–IV) and grading (A–C) classification to guide clinical decision-making. Key risk data include smoking ORs of 3.25–7.28 for alveolar bone loss and diabetes ORs of 2.81–3.43. Treatment follows a stepwise sequence: home care optimisation → full-mouth SRP (with optional adjunctive systemic antibiotics) → periodontal re-evaluation → surgical therapy (regenerative or resective) → regular maintenance.

## Key Contributions
- Operationalises 2017 World Workshop staging (I–IV based on CAL/bone loss/complexity) and grading (A–C based on rate of progression, smoking, HbA1c).
- Quantifies smoking risk: bone loss OR 3.25 (light) / 7.28 (heavy); attachment loss OR 2.05 / 4.07.
- Quantifies diabetes risk: CAL OR 2.81; bone loss OR 3.43 (type 2 DM).
- Adjunctive antibiotics + SRP: additional PD reduction 0.448 mm, CAL gain 0.389 mm at 6 months (meta-analysis, 28 RCTs).
- Covers regenerative options (GTR, bone grafts) and resective surgery for Stage III–IV residual pockets.

## Methodology
Narrative (non-systematic) concise clinical review. Synthesises NHANES prevalence data, epidemiological odds ratios, and RCT/meta-analysis data on adjunctive therapies. No formal PRISMA search protocol.

## Results
- US prevalence: 42% adults with periodontitis; 7.8% severe (NHANES 2009–2014).
- Global severe periodontitis: ~11% (743 million people).
- Stage I: CAL 1–2 mm; Stage II: 3–4 mm; Stage III/IV: ≥5 mm with increasing complexity (furcation, mobility, ridge defects).
- Grade modifiers shift grade upward: smoking ≥10 cigarettes/day or HbA1c ≥7.0%.
- Systemic antibiotic benefit persists at 12 months: PD 0.485 mm, CAL 0.285 mm additional gain.
