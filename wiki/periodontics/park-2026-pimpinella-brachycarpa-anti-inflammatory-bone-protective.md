## One-line Summary

Rat ligature-induced periodontitis study (6 groups, n=5–7/group): Pimpinella brachycarpa extract (PBE, 150–300 mg/kg/day) dose-dependently reduced alveolar bone loss, TRAP+ osteoclasts, and CD45/IL-1β/TNF-α, with efficacy comparable to positive control ISD (250 mg/kg/day = the Insadol Plus formula, Zea mays + Magnolia cortex extract).

## 한줄요약

## Summary

This rat study tested whether *Pimpinella brachycarpa* extract (PBE; the edible Korean herb 참나물/Chamnamul) protects against periodontitis, and benchmarked it against established natural agents. Importantly for Insadol-family surveillance, the **positive-control reference drug was ISD — explicitly defined as a *Zea mays* L. extract + *Magnolia cortex* extract herbal formulation, "Insadolplus" (Dongkook Pharmaceutical), at 250 mg/kg/day** — i.e. the 인사돌플러스 (Insadol Plus) composition itself.

In a ligature-induced periodontitis model (4-0 silk around maxillary second molars, oral gavage × 14 days), PBE at 150/200/300 mg/kg/day dose-dependently reduced alveolar bone loss, TRAP+ osteoclast counts, and CD45/IL-1β/TNF-α immunostaining. The Discussion states PBE showed **"comparable efficacy"** to ISD in suppressing inflammatory markers and osteoclast presence — treating ISD (Insadol Plus) as an established, efficacious plant-based periodontal therapeutic rather than a weak control. A separate arm dissected PBE's constituents: green tea catechin (GTC), chlorogenic acid (CGA, the most abundant PBE component at 13.7 mg/g), and their combination (MIX) each independently reduced bone loss and osteoclasts.

## Key Contributions

- First in vivo evaluation of PBE in experimental periodontitis; dose-dependent bone-protective + anti-inflammatory effect.
- **Uses ISD = Insadol Plus formula (Zea mays + Magnolia cortex extract) at 250 mg/kg/day as the positive control**, with PBE judged "comparable" — a direct, paper-level benchmark of Insadol's reference efficacy inside an animal model.
- Mechanistic dissection via GTC / CGA / MIX arms implicating chlorogenic acid and catechins.
- HPLC profile of PBE (CGA 13.7, luteolin-7-O-glucuronide 3.6, apigenin-7-O-glucuronide 3.8, luteolin-3-O-glucuronide 2.5 mg/g; GTC absent).

## Methodology

- **Model**: 7-week-old male Sprague-Dawley rats; 4-0 silk ligature around maxillary 2nd molars (bilateral); test substances by oral gavage once daily × 14 days; euthanasia day 15.
- **Groups (n=5–7 each)**: Normal control (n=5); LIG (ligature only, n=7); treatment groups (n=7).
  - **Dose-response**: PBE 150 / 200 / 300 mg/kg/day vs **ISD 250 mg/kg/day (positive control = Insadol Plus formula)**.
  - **Component arm**: PBE 150 mg/kg/day vs GTC 20 mg/kg, CGA 20 mg/kg, MIX (GTC+CGA 20+20 mg/kg, 1:1).
- **Readouts**: gross bone-loss area (CEJ→crest, ImageJ); H&E CEJ-ABC distance; TRAP+ osteoclast count; IHC for CD45, IL-1β, TNF-α; HPLC phytochemistry.
- **Statistics**: one-way ANOVA + Dunnett's post hoc vs LIG; p<0.05.
- IACUC approval JBNUH-IACUC-2024-22.

## Results

- **Bone loss**: severe in LIG; significantly reduced in ISD and all PBE-dose groups (p<0.05–0.001). Histology (CEJ-ABC) corroborated.
- **Osteoclasts**: PBE reduced TRAP+ cells **dose-dependently**; higher doses strongest.
- **Inflammation**: PBE lowered CD45, IL-1β, TNF-α positive area vs LIG.
- **ISD (Insadol) benchmark**: PBE "exhibited comparable efficacy" to ISD on inflammatory markers and osteoclast presence — so the Insadol Plus formula performed as a credible, active reference standard (≈ the new herb's effect ceiling). Note: comparison is qualitative; no exact PBE-vs-ISD numeric contrast or mean ± SD table is reported.
- **Component arm**: GTC, CGA, MIX, and PBE all significantly reduced bone loss / CEJ-ABC / TRAP+ vs LIG; CGA and catechins each contribute, MIX suggesting additivity/synergy.
- **Limitations**: no molecular pathway assay; short 14-day course; small n; bioavailability untested; rodent dose far above human dietary intake.
