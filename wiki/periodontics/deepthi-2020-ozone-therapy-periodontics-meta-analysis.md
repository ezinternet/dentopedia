SR+MA of 7 RCTs (n=13-113 per study) finding ozone as an adjunct to scaling and root planing produced small, mostly non-significant improvements in PPD, CAL, PI, GI under high heterogeneity (I2=80.49%).

## Summary
This systematic review with meta-analysis evaluates ozone therapy as an adjunct to mechanical scaling and root planing (SRP) in patients with chronic and aggressive periodontitis. Ozone is a strong oxidant with antimicrobial action (without inducing bacterial resistance) and host immune-modulating, wound-healing properties; aqueous ozone is biocompatible to fibroblasts, cementoblasts, and epithelial cells. Searching seven databases for human RCTs (>10 patients, >6 weeks follow-up) published 2010-2018 yielded 7 eligible studies. Pooled random-effects estimates for PPD, CAL, PI, and GI all favored ozone numerically but had confidence intervals crossing zero (non-significant), and heterogeneity was high (I2 = 80.49%). The authors nonetheless conclude ozone can be used effectively as an SRP adjunct, a conclusion that overstates the modest, non-significant pooled effect sizes.

## Key Contributions
- Reports the first human meta-analysis (per authors) of ozone as an adjunct to SRP for periodontitis.
- Provides pooled WMDs across the core periodontal outcomes (PPD, CAL, PI, GI).
- Documents that funnel plots were symmetrical (no evident publication bias) but heterogeneity was very high.
- Serves as a cautionary example: a favorable narrative conclusion paired with non-significant pooled estimates.

## Methodology
- Design: systematic review + random-effects meta-analysis.
- PICO: chronic/aggressive periodontitis adults; ozone (gaseous, aqueous, or ozonated oil gel) adjunct to SRP; vs placebo/no-treatment; outcomes PPD, CAL, BOP, PI, GI.
- Databases: PubMed, Medline, Embase, Cochrane, Science Direct, Google Scholar, Sci-hub; Jan 2010-Jul 2018; two independent reviewers (kappa agreement).
- Inclusion: English human RCTs, >10 patients, >6 weeks follow-up. Exclusion: in vitro, animal, retrospective cross-sectional.
- Screening: 123 → 109 excluded by title/abstract → 14 full-text → 7 eligible (abstract states 6; methodological inconsistency noted).
- Effect measure: weighted mean difference (WMD); heterogeneity via Q and I2; publication bias via funnel plots.

## Results
- 7 studies; n = 13-113 per study; ages 20-72; chronic or aggressive periodontitis.
- PPD: WMD 0.03 (95% CI -0.44, 0.50) — no significant difference.
- PI: WMD -0.23 (95% CI -0.64, 0.19) — non-significant.
- GI: WMD -0.45 (95% CI -1.37, 0.46) — non-significant.
- CAL: WMD -0.26 (95% CI -0.89, 0.37) — non-significant.
- Heterogeneity: Q = 10.241, I2 = 80.49% (high).
- Funnel plots symmetrical → no evident publication bias.
- Individual trials (e.g., ozonated olive oil gel, oxygen-ozone) reported clinical improvements sustained 3-6 months.
