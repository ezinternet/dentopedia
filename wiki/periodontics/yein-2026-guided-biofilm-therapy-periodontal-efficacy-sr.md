## One-line Summary

PRISMA systematic review (5 RCTs, n=231, Cochrane RoB-2 + GRADE very-low) finds Guided Biofilm Therapy (GBT) improves patient satisfaction, pain, and treatment time over scaling/root debridement (SRD) but does NOT consistently outperform SRD on PPD/CAL/BOP — no clinical superiority on disease-related outcomes.

## Summary

Professional scaling and root debridement (SRD) is the gold standard in non-surgical periodontal therapy, but it is technically demanding, time-consuming, and uncomfortable. Guided Biofilm Therapy (GBT) — a protocolized workflow of plaque-disclosing, supra/subgingival air-polishing (erythritol powder), and piezo scaling — is marketed as an alternative. This PRISMA systematic review asked whether GBT, used as a **standalone** intervention, is clinically superior to conventional SRD.

Across **5 RCTs (231 participants)**, GBT delivered clear **patient-centered** advantages (higher comfort, less perceived discomfort, often reduced chairtime) but **inconsistent** effects on the primary clinical periodontal outcomes (PPD, CAL, BOP). Four of five trials found no significant between-group difference in PPD/CAL/GR. The overall certainty of evidence was **very low** (GRADE), downgraded for risk of bias, indirectness, imprecision, and inconsistency. Bottom line: current evidence does **not** support clinical superiority of GBT over SRD for disease-related measures — the value proposition is comfort and efficiency, not better periodontal healing.

## Key Contributions

- First SR appraising GBT vs SRD as a standalone (not adjunctive) non-surgical periodontal therapy.
- Cleanly separates patient-centered benefit (consistent) from clinical-outcome benefit (inconsistent / absent).
- Quantifies the evidence base: 5 RCTs, n=231, GRADE very low — a cautionary read against marketing-driven adoption claims.

## Methodology

- PRISMA SR; PROSPERO CRD420251010127. Descriptive synthesis (meta-analysis not feasible due to heterogeneity).
- 8 databases (PubMed, Cochrane, LILACS, Scopus, WoS, Google Scholar, OpenGrey, ClinicalTrials.gov); forward + backward searching; no language/year limits.
- PICOS: adults with periodontal disease; GBT standalone vs SRD/ultrasonic/hand; primary PPD, secondary CAL/BOP/GR/treatment time/plaque/pain; RCTs + non-randomized clinical trials.
- Cochrane RoB 2 (robvis) + GRADE (Murad adaptation). Cohen's kappa 0.80-0.86 across stages.

## Results

- **Flow**: 620 records -> 541 screened -> 12 full-text -> 5 included (all RCTs: 3 split-mouth, 2 parallel-arm; sample 32-60). All used EMS erythritol/PLUS powder with 0.3% chlorhexidine.
- **Included trials**: Vouros 2021 (n=50, SPC); Stahli 2024 (n=41, SPC); Mensi 2024a (n=32, gingivitis); Cyris 2024 (n=60, Stage III/IV); Mensi 2024b (n=41, Stage III/IV).
- **PPD**: 3.8-6.2 mm -> ~2.9-3.3 mm (2-3 mm reduction), comparable both arms.
- **CAL**: 4.6-6.7 mm -> 4.0-4.1 mm, no clinically relevant difference.
- **BOP**: largest change, 56-91% -> 9-32%, similar both arms (Stahli: significant in SRD; Cyris: significant within GBT).
- **GR**: stable (~1.0-2.3 -> 1.2-2.2 mm). **Plaque**: 83-91% -> 29-31% (3M); 65% -> 17-19% (12M), GBT slightly better in difficult-access sites.
- **Treatment time**: reduced with GBT in some trials (Cyris 4.3 min/quadrant, 12.43%; 30.3 vs 34.6 min) but not others (Stahli 42.4 vs 39.6; Mensi 2024a 55.44 vs 51.13 min).
- **Pain / satisfaction**: negligible pain overall (0.1 both arms); but Stahli reported higher VAS pain (15 vs 7.6) and higher satisfaction in SRD (97.3 vs 84.8); Mensi 2024b reported less GBT discomfort (5.2% vs 33.4% at 12M).
- **RoB**: 1 high (Mensi 2024a), 2 some concerns, 3 low. **GRADE**: very low (downgraded for RoB, indirectness, imprecision n=231, inconsistency).
