## One-line Summary

RCT (n=73, 3 groups, 12 weeks at 4-week intervals): a multi-component dental hygiene care programme (10-min flash video + brochures + toothpick method, delivered by 5 hygienists) significantly reduced plaque index after each session in patients with mental disorders (P<0.0001; between-group P=0.036); oral dryness improved, but stimulated saliva and caries activity did not.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

This Korean RCT (Yonsei University, Wonju) developed and tested a dental hygiene care programme tailored to patients with mental disorders — a special-needs population with characteristically poor oral self-care. Seventy-three patients were randomly allocated to three groups and followed for 12 weeks with sessions every 4 weeks. The programme combined a short (~10 min) flash-based educational video, brochures, and instruction in the **toothpick method**, all delivered by five dental hygienists. Plaque index dropped significantly after each session in all groups, and a significant between-group difference was found. Subjective oral dryness improved, but stimulated salivary flow and caries activity were unchanged.

This record is **abstract-only** (full text not retrieved); only the figures reported in the abstract are captured here.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates a brief, repeatable, hygienist-delivered programme can reduce plaque index in patients with mental disorders.
- Applies the **toothpick method** in a special-needs context, embedded within a video + brochure education package.
- Provides practical-guideline framing for improving oral health care in psychiatrically vulnerable patients.

## Methodology

- **Design**: randomized controlled trial.
- **Participants**: n = 73 patients with mental illness, randomly classified into **three groups**.
- **Follow-up**: 12 weeks, sessions at **4-week intervals**.
- **Intervention**: dental hygiene care programme = **~10-min flash-based video + brochures + toothpick method**, implemented by **five dental hygienists**.
- **Outcomes**: plaque index, stimulated saliva, subjective oral dryness, dental caries activity.

## Results

- **Plaque index**: significantly decreased after each session in all three groups, **P < 0.0001**; significant **between-group** difference, **P = 0.036**.
- **Subjective oral dryness**: decreased significantly.
- **Stimulated saliva**: no improvement.
- **Dental caries activity**: no improvement.
- **Conclusion**: the programme (10-min flash video + brochures every 4 weeks) was effective in reducing plaque index in patients with mental disorders.

> [!caution] Multi-component intervention — toothpick effect not isolable
> The toothpick method was only **one component** of the programme (alongside the video and brochures). The plaque reduction cannot be attributed to the toothpick method alone; its isolated effect is unrecoverable from this design and this abstract-only record.
