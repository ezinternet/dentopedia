## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 44 RCTs (search 1995–2023): in periodontitis patients with concurrent systemic conditions (diabetes, CVD, RA, pregnancy, etc.), NSPT vs untreated/SGS/OHI controls gave 0.55 mm lower mean PD at 3 months (95%CI −0.69 to −0.41, low/some-concern bias studies), plus reduced CAL and %BOP and increased %PD≤3 mm.

## Summary

This PRISMA-conformant systematic review and meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD42021241517) pooled randomized controlled trials testing **non-surgical periodontal therapy (NSPT) as monotherapy** against untreated, supragingival-scaling (SGS), or oral-hygiene-instruction (OHI) controls in periodontitis patients **with a concurrent systemic condition** (type 2 diabetes, cardiovascular disease, erectile dysfunction, chronic kidney disease, rheumatoid arthritis, PCOS, obesity, pregnancy, COPD). It leverages the "periodontal medicine" trial design, in which a periodontally diseased control arm has treatment delayed (usually ≤3 months) so a medical endpoint can be assessed, repurposing those trials to answer whether NSPT improves periodontal parameters versus no treatment. Across 44 trials (1995–2023), NSPT consistently outperformed controls, supporting its efficacy and policy inclusion even in comorbid populations.

## Key Contributions

- First pooled, comorbidity-spanning estimate of NSPT-vs-control efficacy specifically in periodontitis patients with systemic conditions, restricted to NSPT monotherapy (no antibiotics, adjuncts, or surgery).
- Quantifies the core periodontal benefit: **0.55 mm lower mean PD at 3 months** in low/some-concern-bias studies (95% CI −0.69 to −0.41).
- Directly answers health-policy/reimbursement uncertainty (CADTH, German IQWiG) with pooled effect sizes.
- Provides comorbidity-specific subgroup analyses (diabetes, CVD, erectile dysfunction, pregnancy, RA).

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review + random-effects meta-analysis; PRISMA reporting; Cochrane RoB2 risk-of-bias assessment.
- **Search**: PubMed + Embase (1995-01-01 to 2023-09-30) + Google Scholar + manual reference search; two independent reviewers, third for consensus.
- **Eligibility**: RCTs, adults ≥18 y, periodontitis + systemic disease/condition; NSPT monotherapy as test; no-treatment / OHI / SGS as control; mean PD with ≥3-month follow-up; English only.
- **Outcomes**: Primary = mean PD; secondary = mean CAL, %PD≤3 mm, %BOP (extracted at 3 and 6 months). Mean difference + 95% CI; negative favours NSPT (except %PD≤3 mm).
- **Analysis**: I² for heterogeneity; Egger's test/funnel plots for publication bias; median→mean and SD imputation per Cochrane handbook; random-effects meta-regression on comorbidity type, risk of bias, and publication year. Pooled estimates split by low/some-concern vs high risk of bias.

## Results

- 2,403 records → 1,565 screened → 126 full text → **44 included** RCTs (1995–2023); mostly single-centre (all but 4).
- Follow-up: 3 m (25), 6 m (20), 12 m (2), 24 m (1); multinational sample.
- **Primary**: NSPT gave 0.55 mm lower mean PD at 3 months vs control (95% CI −0.69 to −0.41) in low/some-concern-bias studies.
- Versus untreated controls NSPT **reduced mean PD, mean CAL, and %BOP and increased %PD≤3 mm**.
- **Conclusion**: NSPT is an effective procedure for managing periodontitis in patients with concurrent systemic conditions, supporting its routine use and reimbursement in these populations.
