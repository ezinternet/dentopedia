## One-line Summary

Systematic review + network meta-analysis (109 RCTs, 4712 RT1 recessions, 3188 patients; Periodontology 2000, 2024): among autologous platelet concentrates (APCs) added to a coronally advanced flap (CAF), only CAF+PRF significantly increased mean root coverage over CAF alone (+6.12%, 95% CI 1.83–10.42, p=0.02) — PRP and CGF did not — and the PRF benefit rose with more baseline keratinized tissue width (KTW), while no APC improved KTW or gingival thickness (GT).

## Summary

This Periodontology 2000 review pairs a systematic review with a **network meta-analysis (NMA)** to rank autologous platelet concentrate (APC) preparations for the treatment of **RT1 (Cairo type 1) gingival recession** — i.e., root coverage. The clinical problem it addresses is that the autogenous connective tissue graft (CTG), while the gold standard for percent root coverage, carries substantial palatal-harvest morbidity, motivating APCs (PRF, PRP, CGF, etc.) as fully autologous, low-morbidity alternatives.

Across **109 RCTs (4712 recessions, 3188 patients)**, the NMA used **CAF alone as the reference** and could statistically compare only CAF, CAF+PRF, CAF+PRP, and CAF+CGF (the arms with ≥3 trials and a shared comparator; T-PRF and PCG had single arms and were not compared). The headline result: **only CAF+PRF significantly increased mean root coverage** over CAF alone (+6.12%), whereas CAF+PRP and CAF+CGF did not. The authors attribute PRF's edge to its sustained ~10-day growth-factor release versus the shorter kinetics of first-generation PRP.

Two clinically decisive qualifiers emerged. First, the PRF benefit was **effect-modified by baseline KTW** — the more keratinized tissue present at baseline, the greater the PRF root-coverage gain (interaction +4.98, p=0.03). Second, **no APC improved KTW or GT**; predictable KTW/GT gain remains a property of autogenous grafts. The practical framing: prefer CTG where KTW is deficient or recession is deep; consider PRF (ideally with a scaffold/matrix to add GT) where an adequate KTW band already exists, trading a small mRC concession for markedly lower morbidity.

## Key Contributions

- First **NMA** placing PRF, PRP, and CGF on a common comparator (CAF alone) for RT1 root coverage, yielding indirect comparisons absent from head-to-head RCTs.
- **PRF is the only APC that significantly augments mRC** of a coronally advanced flap; PRP and CGF are non-significant.
- Identifies **baseline KTW as a positive effect modifier** of the PRF benefit.
- Confirms **APCs do not increase KTW or GT** — positioning them as adjuncts in KTW-adequate sites, with matrices recommended to add GT.

## Methodology

- **Design**: PROSPERO-registered SR + mixed-model NMA; PRISMA-NMA reporting; Cochrane methodology; last search 2023-01-30 across MEDLINE/EMBASE/CENTRAL/LILACS/Web of Science/Scopus + grey/hand search.
- **PICOS**: RT1 gingival recession; APC (PRF/PRP/CGF/L-PRF/T-PRF/PCG) or flap alone; comparator CAF alone; outcomes mRC, KTW gain, GT gain; RCTs ≥10 patients/arm, ≥6-month follow-up.
- **Model**: CAF-referenced mixed-model NMA per outcome; baseline Rec/GT/KT and single- vs multiple-site as covariates; arms weighted by treated n; multivariate handling of multi-arm correlation. Only 92 studies (CAF, PRF, PRP, CGF) entered the comparable network.
- **Risk of bias**: RoB-2 — 30.6% low, 28.8% some concerns, 40.5% high (mostly unblinded outcome assessment, missing data). Screening kappa 0.93 / 0.97. R 4.2.1 (lme4/lmerTest).

## Results

- **mRC vs CAF alone**: **CAF+PRF +6.12%** (95% CI 1.83–10.42, **p=0.02**) — only significant arm. CAF+CGF +7.47% (−3.88–18.82, p=0.33, NS); CAF+PRP −0.61% (−14.43–13.21, p=0.92, NS).
- **Baseline KTW × CAF+PRF interaction**: +4.98 (95% CI 0.85–9.12, p=0.03) — PRF benefit grows with more baseline KTW. Time × mRC: −0.146 (p<0.01), modest relapse.
- **KTW gain vs CAF (all NS)**: PRP −0.66 mm (p=0.77), PRF −0.10 mm (p=0.79), CGF +0.84 mm (p=0.42); time +0.003 mm (p=0.03).
- **GT gain vs CAF (all NS)**: PRP −0.31 mm (p=0.461), PRF +0.04 mm (p=0.78), CGF +0.25 mm (p=0.53); time −0.004 mm (p<0.001).
- **CTG vs PRF head-to-head (descriptive)**: CTG generally ≥ PRF on mRC (e.g., Uraz 2015 96.5% vs 75.3%; Joshi 2020 93.3% vs 70.6%), though several near-equivalent (Tunali 2015 77.4% vs 76.6%; Uzun 2018 93.2% vs 93.3%). CTG remains superior on the average defect.
- **Clinical guideline (authors)**: APCs reduce recession overall; only PRF raises mRC of CAF; baseline KTW is decisive; APCs do not improve KTW/GT; use PRF where KTW present and pair with a matrix/scaffold to gain GT.
