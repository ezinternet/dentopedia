## One-line Summary

Egyptian RCT (n=24; 12 SRP+ozonated gel vs 12 SRP alone) in controlled T2DM + stage III grade B periodontitis: both arms significantly improved CAL/PPD/BOP/PI/HbA1c with NO significant intergroup difference, but the ozone arm uniquely achieved significant within-group radiographic gains (RLDD 3.44→2.66 mm p=0.008; defect angle p=0.007; PDL space p=0.008) plus greater PPD reduction at 3 months (p=0.045) and less postoperative pain (VAS p=0.017).

## 한줄요약

조절된 제2형 당뇨 + stage III periodontitis 환자 24명(SRP+오존젤 12 vs SRP 단독 12) RCT — 양 군 모두 CAL·PPD·BOP·PI·HbA1c가 유의하게 개선됐으나 군간 차이는 없었고, 오존군에서만 방사선학적 골결손(RLDD 3.44→2.66 mm, p=0.008; 결손각 p=0.007; 치주인대공간 p=0.008)이 유의하게 개선되고 3개월 PPD 감소(p=0.045)와 술후 통증(VAS p=0.017)이 더 좋았다.

## Summary

This single-center, assessor-blinded RCT (Cairo University; NCT05538078) tested whether subgingival **ozonated gel** (oil-based bio-ozonized olive oil, 20 mEq O2/Kg) added to scaling and root planing (SRP) outperforms SRP alone in **controlled type 2 diabetic** patients (HbA1c < 7) with **stage III grade B periodontitis**. 24 patients were randomized 1:1 (n=12/group). The gel was held in the pocket ≥30 min via a customized acrylic stent.

The dominant finding is that **mechanical SRP drives the bulk of the improvement**: both groups significantly improved every clinical and glycemic parameter over 6 months (all intra-group p<0.001), and intergroup comparisons were non-significant at nearly every time point. Ozone added a measurable benefit only in three places: (1) greater PPD reduction at 3 months, (2) lower postoperative dentinal-hypersensitivity pain at 1 week, and (3) significant within-group radiographic improvement of the intrabony defect (RLDD, defect angle, PDL space) that SRP alone did not produce. HbA1c fell significantly in both groups with no ozone advantage. No adverse events occurred.

The trial is consistent with prior meta-analytic evidence that adjunctive ozone yields small, mostly statistically non-significant clinical gains over SRP — but extends it to a diabetic cohort and adds a radiographic intrabony-defect signal. Given n=12/group, "no intergroup difference" findings are likely underpowered.

## Key Contributions

- First RCT of **subgingival ozonated (oily) gel + SRP vs SRP alone** specifically in controlled T2DM stage III periodontitis, reporting both clinical and radiographic intrabony-defect outcomes.
- Adds a radiographic signal: only the ozone arm achieved significant within-group reduction in radiographic linear defect depth and PDL space and increase in defect angle.
- Methodological choices (oily ozone vehicle + acrylic stent ≥30 min contact) proposed to explain the 3-month PPD advantage vs prior gaseous-ozone studies (e.g. Uraz 2019) that found no benefit.

## Methodology

- **Design**: two-arm parallel RCT, 1:1, assessor- and statistician-blinded (PI/patients unblinded).
- **n=24** (18 F, 6 M); controlled T2DM (HbA1c < 7); stage III grade B (interdental CAL ≥5 mm, PD ≥6 mm, bone loss to middle/apical third). Smokers, pregnancy, recent perio/antibiotic therapy excluded.
- **Test**: one-visit full-mouth SRP (ultrasonic + Gracey/universal curettes) + 1–1.5 mL ozonated gel subgingivally, held ≥30 min via stent. **Control**: SRP alone. No antibiotics/antiplaque/anti-inflammatories.
- **Outcomes**: primary CAL; secondary PPD, PI%, BOP%, VAS (dentinal hypersensitivity), RLDD, defect angle, PDL space, HbA1c. Clinical at 0/3/6 mo; radiographic (standardized periapical + ImageJ) at 0/6 mo; HbA1c at 0/6 mo; VAS at 0/1 wk.
- **Power**: 9/group required (d=1.5, SD 1.09, power 0.8); inflated to 12 for dropout.
- **Stats**: RM-ANOVA+Bonferroni (CAL/PD/HbA1c), Mann–Whitney/Friedman/Dunn/Wilcoxon (non-parametric), P ≤ 0.05.

## Results

| Outcome (6 mo unless noted) | SRP alone | SRP + Ozone | Intergroup p |
|---|---|---|---|
| CAL (mm) | 6.75→3.7 | 7.5→4.11 | 0.541 (NS) |
| PPD (mm) | 6.1→3.1 | 6.44→3.11 | 0.980 (NS) |
| **PPD reduction at 3 mo** | 2.25 ± 1.29 | 3.3 ± 0.67 | **0.045** |
| BOP% | 58.5→10.6 | 61.2→9.3 | 0.869 (NS) |
| PI% | 70.2→16.2 | 72.5→19 | 0.485 (NS) |
| **VAS at 1 week** | 2.92 ± 1.62 | 1.42 ± 1 | **0.017** |
| HbA1c% | 6.81→6.56 | 6.79→6.52 | 0.656 (NS) |
| RLDD (mm), within-group p | 3.63→3.42 (p=0.888, NS) | 3.44→2.66 (**p=0.008**) | 0.220 |
| Defect angle (°), within-group p | 68.3→66.8 (p=0.092, NS) | 62.8→70 (**p=0.007**) | 0.870 |
| PDL space (mm), within-group p | 0.92→1.0 (p=0.593, NS) | 0.28→0.2 (**p=0.008**) | 0.014 |

- All clinical/glycemic intra-group changes p<0.001 in both groups.
- Ozone advantage limited to: 3-month PPD reduction, 1-week VAS pain, and within-group radiographic improvement (SRP alone produced no significant radiographic change; SRP-alone RLDD change −0.21±0.42 mm).
- No CAL predictor was significant in regression at 6 months. No adverse effects.

**Clinical bottom line**: SRP remains the gold standard and delivers most of the benefit; adjunctive ozonated gel is a safe, non-invasive add-on with a modest early-PPD and postoperative-comfort edge plus a within-group radiographic-defect signal, but no proven intergroup clinical superiority in this small, controlled-diabetic cohort.
