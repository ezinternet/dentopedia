## Summary
Single-investigator retrospective chart review of 1135 periodontal and dental implant surgeries performed by 8 periodontics residents at the US Army Postgraduate Dental School (Fort Eisenhower, GA) from July 2018 to June 2022. Intraoperative complications occurred in 2.8% of cases and postoperative complications in 15.2%. Procedure type was the dominant driver of complications, far outweighing operator-, patient-, or site-level factors.

## Key Contributions
- Largest single-site complication series across the **full periodontal/implant surgical spectrum** (20+ procedure types) with standardized data collection.
- Quantifies relative risk of each procedure type using a uniform reference (delayed implant placement).
- Applies the Askar et al. (2019) 5-grade severity scale to postoperative complications.
- Identifies that, within a residency teaching setting, **operator was not a significant independent predictor** of complications once procedure type was accounted for.

## Methodology
- Design: retrospective observational, STROBE-compliant.
- Sample: 1135 procedures, 8 operators (PGY-2 and PGY-3), 4-year window.
- Independent variables: operator, year of training, age, sex, diabetes, smoking, region (ant/post), site type, perioperative antibiotic, procedure type.
- Outcomes: intraoperative complications (e.g., SM perforation, flap tear, bleeding); postoperative complications (infection, dehiscence, hypersensitivity, etc.).
- Severity: Askar 2019 grading 1–5.
- Statistics: binomial logistic regression with hierarchical model build; reference category = delayed implant placement.

## Results
- **Intraoperative complications**: 2.8% (31/1135). Most common — Schneiderian membrane (SM) perforation 1.3%, flap perforation/tear 1.0%.
  - Lateral sinus elevation surgery (Lateral SES ± implant): **OR 102.5** (95% CI 12.3–852.9, p < 0.001).
  - Transalveolar SES ± implant: OR 22.4 (95% CI 2.2–224.5, p = 0.008).
  - Open flap debridement (OFD): OR 36.4 (95% CI 3.0–440.7, p = 0.005).
  - Surgically facilitated orthodontic therapy (SFOT): OR 20.5 (95% CI 1.2–358.4, p = 0.039).
- **Postoperative complications**: 15.2% (173/1135). Most common — dentin hypersensitivity 2.6%, excessive pain 2.5%, infection 2.2%.
  - SCTG: OR 3.2 (95% CI 1.6–6.1, p < 0.001).
  - GBR alone: OR 3.0 (95% CI 1.4–6.5, p = 0.004).
  - GBR + implant: OR 3.1 (95% CI 1.3–7.6, p = 0.011).
- **Severity**: 31.8% grade 1, 45.7% grade 2, 22.5% grade 3; no grade 4 or 5 events.
- **Operator and patient covariates** (age, sex, smoking, diabetes, antibiotic) were not independent predictors after adjusting for procedure type.

## Clinical Implications (Author Memo)
- For complication counseling: **lateral sinus elevation has by far the highest intraoperative risk** — patient consent should reflect this.
- **SCTG and GBR carry the highest postoperative complication odds** — schedule longer follow-ups and prepare hypersensitivity/pain protocols.
- In residency or DSO settings, focusing on **procedure selection and case complexity triage** matters more than focusing on the specific operator.
