## One-line Summary

SR+MA (23 papers / 39 comparisons, fixed-appliance orthodontic patients): powered toothbrushes (PTBs) beat manual (MTBs) on plaque (SMD -0.77), gingivitis (SMD -0.82), and bleeding (SMD -0.51) by incremental-difference meta-analysis, but the evidence is GRADE-LOW (I2 80–93%, possible publication bias, TSA inconclusive); descriptively most comparisons showed no difference, and the PS/GS advantage disappeared when restricted to low-risk or non-industry studies. Only counter-rotational PTBs beat MTBs on plaque by action mode (very low quality); ORP/OR/sonic did not.

## Summary

European Journal of Orthodontics 2026 SR+MA (ACTA Amsterdam; PROSPERO CRD42023480974) asking whether powered toothbrushes (PTBs) outperform manual toothbrushes (MTBs) for plaque/gingivitis control in orthodontic patients with **fixed appliances (FAs)** — a high plaque-retention population where four prior SRs (last updated 2021) reached conflicting verdicts. From 841 records, 23 papers (39 comparisons) were synthesized via Cochrane/PRISMA methods with RoB 2 / ROBINS-I, descriptive vote-counting, random-effects meta-analysis on incremental-difference scores, sensitivity analysis, trial sequential analysis (TSA), and GRADE.

The headline meta-analytic result favors PTBs across all three parameters, but every layer of the appraisal tempers it: descriptively most comparisons were non-significant, heterogeneity was substantial-to-considerable (I2 80–93%), publication bias could not be excluded, TSA never reached the required information size (results inconclusive), and overall GRADE quality was **LOW** so **no recommendation was issued**. The PS and GS advantages were fragile — they vanished when analyses were restricted to low-risk-of-bias or non-industry-funded studies, whereas the bleeding (BS) benefit was the most robust (persisted across all sensitivity restrictions).

## Key Contributions

- First action-mode subgroup analysis (ORP, OR, CR, sonic) of PTB vs. MTB in the fixed-appliance orthodontic population.
- Most current synthesis (search to Nov 2025), reconciling four conflicting earlier SRs.
- Isolated the mechanical effect by restricting to rechargeable, single-head, motor-driven PTBs with no concurrent interdental device.
- Flags an **evidence–product gap**: the only mode that beat MTBs on plaque (counter-rotational) is largely off-market, while the dominant OR/sonic modes lack significant subgroup benefit.

## Methodology

- **PICOS** — P: orthodontic patients with buccal FAs; I: PTB; C: MTB; O: plaque scores (PS), gingivitis scores (GS), gingival bleeding scores (BS); S: (R)CCTs (parallel + crossover; split-mouth excluded).
- **Search**: PubMed-MEDLINE + Cochrane-CENTRAL to Nov 2025 + reference lists; two independent reviewers; Rayyan.
- **Excluded**: replaceable-battery PTBs, multi-head PTBs, concurrent interdental cleaning, split-mouth, non-English.
- **Risk of bias**: RoB 2 (RCTs) / ROBINS-I (CCTs).
- **Synthesis**: descriptive vote-counting + random-effects meta-analysis on **incremental-difference** scores (SDΔ reconstructed at within-subject r = 0.5); SMD (Hedges' g) across scales, MD within scale; I2 heterogeneity; funnel-plot publication bias (Egger/Begg/trim-and-fill); TSA (O'Brien-Fleming, power 80%); GRADE.

## Results

**Inclusion**: 841 → 54 full-text → 23 papers / 39 comparisons (all PS; 21 GS; 24 BS). Samples 20–144, ages 10–53; only 9/23 met ADA ≥30/group. RoB: 19 RCTs (3 low / 4 some-concern / 12 high) + 4 CCTs (1 low / 2 moderate / 1 serious).

**Descriptive (vote-counting)** — no difference in most comparisons: PS 64%, GS 76%, BS 75% NS.

**Overall incremental-difference meta-analyses** (random-effects SMD; negative favors PTB):

| Parameter | Studies (comp) | SMD (95% CI) | P | I2 | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Plaque (PS) | 13 (14) | **-0.77** (-1.33; -0.21) | .007 | 92% | medium |
| Gingivitis (GS) | 10 (10) | **-0.82** (-1.55; -0.09) | .03 | 93% | large |
| Bleeding (BS) | 9 (10) | **-0.51** (-0.88; -0.13) | .008 | 80% | medium |

**Action-mode subgroup (PS)** — only CR significant:

| Action mode | Comp | SMD (95% CI) | P |
|---|---|---|---|
| ORP/OR | 6 | -0.46 (-1.20; 0.28) | .23 (NS) |
| ORP | 3 | -1.13 (-2.74; 0.49) | .17 (NS) |
| OR | 3 | 0.15 (-0.21; 0.51) | .42 (NS) |
| **CR (counter-rotational)** | 2 | **-1.45 (-2.85; -0.05)** | **.04 (large)** |
| Sonic | 4 | -1.20 (-2.67; 0.28) | .11 (NS) |

GS and BS action-mode subgroups were all non-significant — no action mode beat MTBs for gingival parameters.

**Same-index MD highlights**: GI gingivitis MD -0.22 (-0.43; -0.02) P=.04; MPBI bleeding MD -0.54 (-0.71; -0.36) P<.00001; OMPI plaque, BOP bleeding, EIBI all NS.

**Sensitivity**: BS benefit persisted under every restriction (small-to-medium). RCT-only kept PS (-0.73), GS (-1.02), BS (-0.44) significant. **But restricting to low-RoB-only or non-industry-only made PS and GS non-significant** — 5 studies drove PS, 3 GS, 4 BS; 2 were industry-supported, 5 had moderate-high RoB.

**TSA**: RIS not reached, Z-curve did not cross O'Brien-Fleming boundaries → **inconclusive**.

**GRADE**: LOW quality for PS, GS, BS (downgraded for RoB, inconsistency, possible publication bias) → **no clinical recommendation**. Effect-size magnitudes: PS medium, GS large, BS medium. By action mode (PS): OR moderate-quality (no effect), CR very-low (large effect), ORP/sonic low-to-very-low.

**Clinical bottom line**: PTBs *may* modestly help fixed-appliance orthodontic patients (most robustly on bleeding), but the evidence is low quality and fragile to study-quality restriction — far weaker than the established PTB > MTB benefit in non-orthodontic patients. Recommend either brush executed well; reinforce oral-hygiene instruction over device type.
