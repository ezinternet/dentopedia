## One-line Summary

Systematic review (13 studies: 5 RCTs, 7 experimental non-RCT, 1 in vitro) comparing 10 manual toothbrushing techniques — no single technique is proven superior for plaque/gingivitis due to excessive heterogeneity; the toothpick method beat Bass in the only study that tested it (Morita 1998), and modified Bass beat unstructured brushing (Poyato-Ferrera 2003).

## Summary

Manual toothbrushing remains the predominant mechanical plaque-control method (≈80% of toothbrush sales in Australia), yet there is **no consensus on which manual technique to teach**. Rajwani et al (2020) is the first systematic review focused specifically on manual toothbrushing *techniques* (earlier SRs addressed manual-vs-powered, or bristle/duration). From 3190 screened articles, 40 were read in full and **13 met inclusion** — 5 RCTs, 7 experimental non-randomised studies, and 1 in vitro study. Ten techniques were compared (roll, horizontal scrub, modified Bass, Bass, Charter's, Fones, modified Stillman, toothpick, vertical, circular scrub).

The clinical bottom line is **insufficient evidence**: excessive variability in technique mix, toothbrush brands (23), plaque indices (10), training methods, and surfaces scored makes meta-analysis impossible and no single technique can be declared superior for either plaque removal or gingivitis reduction. The authors call for **CONSORT-compliant RCTs**.

## Key Contributions

- **First technique-specific SR.** Distinguishes this from manual-vs-powered SRs — it asks *which manual technique*, not *manual or electric*.
- **Toothpick (Watanabe) method benchmark.** Tested in exactly one included study (Morita et al 1998), where the **toothpick method removed more plaque than the Bass method**. This is the single direct piece of comparative evidence the wiki holds for the toothpick/Watanabe approach.
- **A "statistically significantly superior" tally** (from the abstract): Bass (1 study), modified Bass (1), Charter's (2), Fones (2), scrub (2), roll (1), modified Stillman (1), toothpick (1); **4 studies found no significant difference** between compared techniques.
- **Quantitative anchor for Fones vs modified Bass**: Harnacke 2016 reported overall plaque reduction of **70.3% (Fones) vs 77.9% (modified Bass)**, with no gingivitis (BOP) difference at 12 weeks — illustrating that "significant" findings were inconsistent in direction across studies.

## Methodology

- **Search**: Medline (Ovid), PubMed, EBSCO Dentistry & Oral Sciences Source up to May 2018; 36 search terms; hand-searching of references; PRISMA flow.
- **Inclusion**: age ≥17, manual techniques, baseline plaque or BOP, designs RCT/split-mouth/observational/in vitro.
- **Exclusion**: <17 years, fixed orthodontic appliances, removable prostheses, compromised dexterity; electric brushes, interdental cleaning, mouthrinse; non-English; unavailable full text.
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane Collaboration tool, two independent blinded reviewers, consensus on disagreements. All 5 RCTs were low risk except Arai & Kinoshita 1977 (vague methodology/blinding); every experimental and in vitro study had ≥1 high or unclear-risk domain.

## Results

**Included evidence base**: 13 studies (5 RCT, 7 experimental non-RCT, 1 in vitro). Technique frequency: roll 6, horizontal scrub 6, modified Bass 5, Bass 5, Charter's 3, Fones 3, modified Stillman 1, **toothpick 1**, vertical 1.

Selected findings:

| Study | Comparison | Result |
|---|---|---|
| **Morita 1998** | Bass vs **toothpick** | Toothpick removed more plaque than Bass |
| Poyato-Ferrera 2003 | normal vs modified Bass | Modified Bass statistically significantly better at 21 days (TQHI) |
| Kanchanakamol 1993 | Bass vs roll | Bass ('Concept 45' brush) best mean reduction (MNPI) |
| Mastroberardino 2014 | horizontal vs vertical | Vertical significantly better overall + interdental plaque |
| Harnacke 2012 | Fones vs modified Bass | Fones superior for oral hygiene **and** gingivitis (PBI) |
| Harnacke 2016 | Fones vs modified Bass | Fones better for OH (70.3% vs 77.9%); **no** gingivitis (BOP) difference |
| Gibson & Wade 1977 | roll vs Bass | No significant difference |
| Schlueter 2013 | modified Bass / instruction | No significant difference between any groups |
| Jansiriwattana 2018 (in vitro) | scrub vs modified Bass | No difference between brushes or techniques |

**Gingivitis**: only **2 of 13** studies used a true gingival index (both Harnacke), comparing only Fones vs modified Bass, and they conflicted (2012 PBI favored Fones; 2016 BOP showed no difference). Gingivitis conclusions are therefore weak.

**Heterogeneity drivers** preventing pooling: 10 techniques, 23 toothbrush brands, 10 plaque indices (Silness–Löe, TQHI [4 studies], MPI, RMNPI, MNPI, O'Leary, PMI, Podshadley–Haley, Modified Volpe's, Bergenholtz), varied training (verbal/leaflet/video/computer/professional brushing), and varied surfaces scored (Ramfjord vs full dentition; 4/6/8/18 surfaces).

**Conclusion**: current evidence is inadequate to declare any one manual toothbrushing technique superior for plaque removal or gingival inflammation. CONSORT-compliant RCTs are required; the Marginal Plaque Index (MPI) is suggested for higher sensitivity in future trials.
