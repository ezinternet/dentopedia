## Summary

This 6-month randomized controlled trial evaluated daily home-use **water flossing** — alone or combined with an **herb-containing mouthrinse** — as an adjunct to **non-surgical periodontal therapy (NSPT)** in **stage I-II periodontitis**. All 72 patients first underwent full-mouth scaling and subgingival instrumentation, then were randomized to one of three home-care arms (toothbrushing control; toothbrushing + water flossing; toothbrushing + water flossing with herb-containing mouthrinse). Over follow-up at 1, 3, and 6 months, water flossing (with or without the mouthrinse) significantly improved the dental plaque index and periodontal indices versus toothbrushing alone, and reshaped the subgingival microbiota profiled by 16S rRNA sequencing. The herb-containing mouthrinse added to water flossing altered the microbiota further (e.g. reduced *Aggregatibacter*, increased *Fretibacterium* at 6 months) but did **not** confer additional clinical improvement beyond water flossing alone. The authors conclude that daily home-use water flossing is an effective adjuvant that supports plaque control, reduces periodontal inflammation, and helps maintain periodontal stability after NSPT, whereas the herb-containing mouthrinse offers limited incremental benefit. ## Key Contributions

- Tests water flossing as a home-care adjunct to NSPT in **genuine stage I-II periodontitis** patients, not a gingivitis or orthodontic-hygiene cohort.
- Demonstrates **significant improvement in plaque and periodontal indices** with water flossing over a toothbrushing-only control.
- Couples clinical outcomes with a **16S rRNA subgingival-microbiome** analysis, identifying taxon-level shifts.
- Provides a **negative incremental finding**: an herb-containing mouthrinse on top of water flossing changes the microbiota but adds **no clinical benefit**.

## Methodology

- **Design**: 6-month, 3-arm randomized controlled trial.
- **n = 72** patients with **stage I-II periodontitis**.
- **Baseline therapy (all arms)**: full-mouth scaling + subgingival instrumentation (NSPT).
- **Arms**:
  1. **Control** — toothbrushing only.
  2. **Water-flossing** — toothbrushing + water flossing.
  3. **Water-flossing / herb-containing mouthrinse** — toothbrushing + water flossing with herb-containing mouthrinse.
- **Assessments**: periodontal parameters and subgingival plaque samples at **baseline, 1, 3, and 6 months**.
- **Microbiome**: subgingival microbiota by **16S rRNA sequencing**.

## Results

- **Clinical**: water flossing with/without herb-containing mouthrinse → **significant improvement in dental plaque index and periodontal indices** vs control.
- **Microbiome shifts**:
  - **Both water-flossing groups**: depletion of ***Gemella***.
  - **Water-flossing / herb-containing mouthrinse group at 6 months**: **reduced *Aggregatibacter*** and **increased *Fretibacterium***.
- **Incremental herb mouthrinse**: further altered the oral microbiota but provided **no additional clinical improvement** vs water flossing alone.
- **Conclusion**: daily home-use water flossing promotes plaque control, reduces periodontal inflammation, and contributes to maintenance of periodontal stability — an effective NSPT adjuvant; herb-containing mouthrinse adds limited incremental benefit.
