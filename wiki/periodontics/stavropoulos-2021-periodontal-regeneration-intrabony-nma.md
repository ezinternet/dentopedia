## One-line Summary
SR + Bayesian random-effects network meta-analysis of 30 RCTs (21 in NMA; follow-up 3–20 years) of periodontal regenerative/reconstructive procedures in intrabony defects: OFD was least efficacious, while regenerative approaches gave significantly shallower residual PD (MD −2.37 to −0.60 mm) and larger CAL gain (MD +1.26 to +2.66 mm), with combination approaches (GTR+graft, EMD+graft) appearing most efficacious and tooth loss far lower after regeneration (0.4% vs 2.8% for OFD).

## Summary
This open-access SR with Bayesian random-effects NMA included 30 RCTs reporting ≥3 years of post-operative outcomes for periodontal regenerative/reconstructive procedures in intrabony defects. NMA of 21 RCTs showed OFD was the least efficacious. Regenerative approaches produced significantly shallower residual PD (MD: −2.37 to −0.60 mm, 4/8 comparisons significant) and larger CAL gain (MD: 1.26 to 2.66 mm, 6/8 comparisons significant). Combination approaches (GTR+grafting, EMD+grafting) appeared most efficacious. Tooth loss was rare after regenerative treatment (0.4%) vs OFD (2.8%). A clear hierarchy among regenerative approaches could not be established due to limited evidence.

## Key Contributions
- OFD is clearly the least efficacious option for intrabony defects at medium- to long-term
- All regenerative approaches outperform OFD for residual PD and CAL gain
- Combination approaches (GTR+graft, EMD+graft) appear most efficacious
- Tooth survival highly favorable after regeneration: 0.4% loss vs 2.8% for OFD
- Radiographic evidence sparse — primary evidence remains clinical (PD, CAL)
- Evidence quality overall weak — clear hierarchy not yet established

## Methodology
- **Design**: SR + Bayesian random-effects NMA (Open Access)
- **Included**: 30 RCTs (overall); 21 in NMA; follow-up 3–20 years
- **Treatments**: Grafting, GTR, EMD, combinations, blood-derived growth factors, OFD
- **Outcomes**: Residual PD, CAL gain, tooth loss; radiographic (RDD, RBL)
- **Focused question**: Medium- and long-term benefits of regenerative procedures vs OFD in intrabony defects

## Results
| Outcome | Regenerative vs OFD | Range of MD | Significant comparisons |
|---|---|---|---|
| Residual PD | Favors regeneration | −2.37 to −0.60 mm | 4/8 |
| CAL gain | Favors regeneration | +1.26 to +2.66 mm | 6/8 |
| Tooth loss | 0.4% vs 2.8% | — | Sparse evidence |

- Combination approaches (GTR+grafting, EMD+grafting) appeared most efficacious overall
- Clear hierarchy among individual modalities could not be established
