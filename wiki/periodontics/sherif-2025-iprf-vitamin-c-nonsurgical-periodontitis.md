## One-line Summary

3-arm double-blind RCT (n=45, stage-II grade A periodontitis) showing that injectable platelet-rich fibrin (I-PRF) with or without vitamin C added NO clinical benefit (BOP/PD/CAL/PI/bone) over professional mechanical plaque removal (PMPR) alone at 6 months; the only adjunct advantage was lower post-operative pain.

## 한줄요약

3군 이중맹검 RCT (n=45, 2기 grade A 치주염): 비타민C 함유 주사형 혈소판농축피브린(I-PRF)이든 일반 I-PRF든 기계적 치석제거(PMPR) 단독 대비 6개월 시점 BOP·PD·CAL·PI·골 변화에 추가 임상이득이 없었고, 차이는 술후 통증이 더 낮았던 것뿐이다.

## Summary

This double-blinded, parallel-arm RCT (Cairo University) tested whether vitamin-C-loaded I-PRF, delivered locally into the deepest pocket as an adjunct to non-surgical periodontal therapy (NSPT), improves outcomes in stage-II grade A periodontitis. Forty-five patients were randomized 1:1:1 to **PMPR + I-PRF/VitC**, **PMPR + I-PRF**, or **PMPR alone**, with 6-month follow-up and zero dropouts.

The result is a clean negative: all three groups improved significantly over time (PD reduction, CAL gain, BOP decline), but **there was no significant intergroup difference** in the primary outcome (BOP) or in any secondary clinical/radiographic outcome (PD, CAL, GM, PI, horizontal/vertical/total bone). A transient CAL advantage for the VitC group appeared at 3 months (p=0.029) but vanished by 6 months. The **only** durable adjunct benefit was lower post-operative pain in the two I-PRF arms vs PMPR alone (day-2 p=0.021, day-3 p=0.001). Logistic regression found **gender** (female protective, OR 0.059) the sole significant predictor of 6-month BOP — treatment group was not.

The authors attribute the null to the shallow-to-moderate (stage-II) defects: mechanical debridement alone captures most of the achievable improvement, leaving little headroom for biologic/nutraceutical adjuncts (the same pattern seen with EMD in moderate pockets). This contrasts with the *surgical*, stage-III intraosseous setting where VitC-PRF did improve healing. Conclusion: **apart from reduced post-operative pain, I-PRF ± vitamin C does not add clinical benefit over PMPR alone in stage-II periodontitis.**

## Key Contributions

- First RCT testing vitamin-C-loaded I-PRF as a **non-surgical** adjunct (prior VitC-PRF evidence was surgical/stage-III).
- Demonstrates a **floor effect** for adjuncts in stage-II disease: PMPR alone is hard to beat when baseline severity is moderate.
- Adds to the recurring evidence that **vitamin-C-containing periodontal adjuncts have limited clinical efficacy beyond mechanical therapy** — relevant to OTC products like 이가탄 CELC whose actives include ascorbic acid.
- Isolates a real but narrow PRF benefit: **post-operative pain reduction** (patient-centered outcome), independent of clinical/radiographic gains.

## Methodology

- **Design**: double-blind (assessor + biostatistician), parallel 3-arm RCT, 1:1:1, concealed computer-generated randomization, 6-month follow-up.
- **n = 45** (15/arm); stage-II grade A periodontitis (interdental CAL 3–4 mm, bone loss ≤33%, no tooth loss); smokers/pregnant/recent-drug excluded; single deepest pocket per patient treated/analyzed. No dropouts.
- **I-PRF**: low-speed centrifugation (60 g / 700 rpm, 3 min); test arm added vitamin C to 250 µg/ml (2500 µg in 10 ml blood); delivered via stent, retained 15 min.
- **Primary outcome**: BOP. **Secondary**: PD, CAL, GM, PI, horizontal/vertical/total radiographic bone change, post-op pain (VAS days 2 & 3). Measured baseline / 3 mo / 6 mo.
- **Stats**: RM-ANOVA (PD); Kruskal-Wallis / Friedman / Dunn (non-parametric outcomes); binary logistic regression for BOP predictors. Powered on BOP.

## Results

- **BOP (primary)** — no intergroup difference (3 mo p=0.185; 6 mo p=0.301). 6-mo BOP+ rates: VitC 20%, I-PRF 33.3%, PMPR 46.7% (numerically lowest in VitC, not significant). All groups improved over time.
- **PD reduction (mm)** — 3 mo: VitC 1.73, I-PRF 1.60, PMPR 1.67 (p=0.833); 6 mo: 1.73 / 1.67 / 1.73 (p=0.961). Significant within-group reduction; no intergroup difference.
- **CAL gain (mm)** — 3 mo: 1.33 / 1.07 / 0.93 (intergroup p=0.029, favoring VitC — transient); 6 mo: 1.33 / 1.20 / 0.93 (intergroup p=0.056, NS). Difference disappears by 6 mo.
- **GM change (mm, recession)** — intergroup NS (3 mo p=0.258; 6 mo p=0.205). 6 mo: 0.4 / 0.33 / 0.73.
- **PI** — rose over time in all groups (compliance drift); no intergroup difference.
- **Radiographic bone** — no intergroup difference in amount of change at any timepoint; within-group total-bone gain in VitC and I-PRF arms (not PMPR), but between-group NS. Baseline vertical-bone imbalance noted.
- **Pain (VAS median)** — day 2: 1 / 0 / 4 (p=0.021); day 3: 0 / 0 / 4 (p=0.001). I-PRF arms significantly lower than PMPR alone.
- **Regression** — gender only significant 6-mo BOP predictor (female OR 0.059, p=0.040); treatment group NS.
- **Conclusion** — apart from lower post-op pain, I-PRF ± vitamin C does not improve clinical outcomes over PMPR alone in stage-II grade A periodontitis.
