## One-line Summary

Split-mouth RCT (n=60 stage III/IV periodontitis patients, treated by supervised dental students): Guided Biofilm Therapy (erythritol air-polishing + ultrasonic) and conventional SRP (hand curettes/sonic + rotary polishing) gave equivalent PPD reduction and pocket closure in NSPT, with GBT significantly faster (30.3 vs 34.6 min/quadrant, p<0.001).

## Summary

This split-mouth randomized controlled trial from the University of Kiel compared the Guided Biofilm Therapy (GBT) protocol against conventional instrumentation during the first/second steps of non-surgical periodontal therapy (NSPT) in 60 patients with stage III/IV periodontitis. Crucially, all treatment was delivered by inexperienced practitioners (third-clinical-semester dental students) under supervision, testing whether the choice of protocol matters for less-experienced operators. After ~4.5 months, both protocols produced significant and clinically comparable improvements in PPD, CAL, GR, BOP and pocket closure; the only consistent advantage of GBT was a shorter treatment time per quadrant. The authors' null hypothesis (that GBT would be significantly *better*) was rejected: the methods are clinically equivalent.

## Key Contributions

- First RCT comparing GBT vs conventional SRP specifically in NSPT (not supportive therapy) for stage III/IV periodontitis.
- Tests the protocols under inexperienced-operator conditions, addressing the GBT learning-curve question.
- Confirms clinical equivalence of pocket closure while documenting a treatment-time advantage for GBT.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Split-mouth RCT; contralateral quadrant pairs randomized (random.org, sealed envelopes) to GBT (test) or conventional SRP (control).
- **GBT arm**: plaque staining → supragingival erythritol air-polishing → ultrasonic scaler (PERIO SLIM PIEZON, EMS) on PPD ≥ 4 mm sites → supra/subgingival erythritol air-polishing (AIRFLOW Prophylaxis Master).
- **Conventional arm**: plaque staining → sonic scaler (Proxeo) + Gracey curettes on PPD ≥ 4 mm sites → rubber-cup rotary polishing (RDA 170 paste) + interdental devices.
- **Operators**: 100 third-semester dental students, supervised by ≥5-year-experience dentists; trained on manikins and mutual exercises.
- **Outcomes**: PPD, GR, CAL, BOP, O'Leary PCR, treatment time, VAS pain — 6 sites/tooth with UNC-PCP15 probe, two calibrated blinded examiners.
- **Timepoints**: T0 baseline, T1 at 5 ± 2 months (mean 4.47 mo); no professional intervention between.
- **Primary endpoint**: % of surfaces achieving closed pocket (PPD ≤ 4 mm without BOP).
- **Sample**: 171 screened → 60 analyzed (stage III=36/IV=24; grade A/B/C=1/31/28; mean age 46.3 y; 16 smokers).
- **Statistics**: multilevel (surface/tooth/patient); Wilcoxon (within) and Mann–Whitney U (between); deliberately overpowered (n=60 vs ~15 needed).

## Results

- **PPD reduction (all diseased surfaces)**: 57.0% GBT vs 58.7% control (p=0.067, NS); mean PPD change -0.9 mm both arms.
- **Target endpoint (PPD ≤ 4 mm without BOP)**: 11.5% GBT vs 11.2% control (p=0.714, NS).
- **Pocket closure**: at T1, 91.1% GBT / 90.9% control sites reached PPD ≤ 4 mm; 95.1% / 95.0% reached PPD ≤ 5 mm — favorable vs literature (~74% in Suvan SR).
- **BOP at T1**: 15.9% GBT vs 14.3% control — statistically significant (p=0.037) but BOP *change* T0→T1 not different (p=0.621); clinical relevance questionable.
- **Treatment time/quadrant**: 30.3 vs 34.6 min — significantly shorter for GBT (p<0.001).
- **Pain (VAS)**: during ~2.4–2.5, post-op ~0.1 — no difference.
- **CAL, GR, tooth mobility**: no significant between-group differences.
- **Bottom line**: Comparable clinical outcomes with both protocols; GBT's edge is efficiency (time), not effectiveness.
