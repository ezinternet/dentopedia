## Summary

This prospective, randomized clinical study compared two non-surgical periodontal debridement modalities in 80 nonsmoking, systemically healthy patients with Stage 1–2 periodontitis: Guided Biofilm Therapy (GBT) versus conventional therapy (CT = Gracey curette + ultrasonic device). Beyond standard clinical indices, it measured gingival crevicular fluid (GCF) inflammatory biomarkers, providing a molecular read-out of the host inflammatory response. Both modalities improved all measured parameters from baseline (p<0.001), but GBT produced a significantly greater reduction in plaque, gingival inflammation, and the pro-inflammatory cytokine IL-1β at 1 and 3 months. The biomarker advantage did not extend to TNF-α, IL-10, or MMP-8, so GBT's immunological benefit appears confined to IL-1β rather than a broad anti-inflammatory effect.

## Key Contributions

- Head-to-head GBT vs conventional curette+ultrasonic debridement in non-surgical periodontal therapy.
- Adds GCF biomarker (immunological) evidence to a question usually argued on clinical indices alone.
- Localizes the GBT advantage to plaque control (PI), gingival inflammation (GI), and IL-1β.

## Methodology

- **Design**: prospective, 2-arm randomized clinical study.
- **Population**: 80 nonsmoking, systemically healthy volunteers, Stage 1 or Stage 2 periodontitis.
- **Groups**: GBT vs conventional therapy (Gracey curette + ultrasonic device).
- **Timepoints**: baseline, 1 month, 3 months.
- **Outcomes**: PD, GI, PI, BOP; GCF IL-1β, IL-10, TNF-α, MMP-8.
- Abstract-only — detailed protocol, GCF assay, and statistics not retrieved.

## Results

- Both approaches significantly improved all indicators from baseline (p<0.001).
- At 1 and 3 months, **GBT > CT on GI, PI, and IL-1β** (p<0.05).
- **No significant between-group difference** in TNF-α, IL-10, or MMP-8.
- Conclusion: GBT is a viable non-surgical option, especially for plaque management and lowering the IL-1β inflammatory response; larger/longer studies needed.
