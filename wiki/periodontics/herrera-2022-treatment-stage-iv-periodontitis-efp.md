## Summary
The EFP S3 level clinical practice guideline for treatment of stage IV periodontitis, the most severe form characterised by masticatory dysfunction, tooth mobility ≥2, severe ridge defects, and fewer than 20 remaining teeth. Based on 13 commissioned SRs and GRADE. Complements the Sanz 2020 CPG for stages I–III, requiring an interdisciplinary approach including orthodontics, prosthodontics, and high-intensity SPT.

## Key Contributions
- Defines stage IV uniquely as requiring interdisciplinary rehabilitation beyond standard periodontal therapy
- Recommends completing active periodontal therapy (per Sanz 2020) before rehabilitation planning
- Addresses orthodontic tooth movement, splinting, occlusal adjustment, implant-supported prostheses
- Hazard ratio for tooth loss in stage IV vs stage I: 3.73× over 10–30 years

## Stage IV — Defining Features
- Tooth loss leaving <20 teeth (<10 opposing pairs)
- Masticatory dysfunction
- Tooth mobility grade ≥2
- Severe alveolar ridge defects
- Occlusal collapse (tooth flaring/drifting)

## Key Recommendations
| Intervention | Recommendation |
|--------------|---------------|
| Periodontal therapy | Follow Sanz 2020 CPG steps 1–4 first |
| Orthodontic movement | Evidence-supported for correction of pathological migration |
| Tooth splinting | Semi-permanent splints post-stabilisation |
| Implant rehabilitation | After periodontal stabilisation; ISQ/loading protocols apply |
| SPT recall | ≤3 months initially; adapt to risk profile |
