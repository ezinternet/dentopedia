## Summary
Systematic review assessing periodontal disease progression in patients receiving supportive periodontal therapy (SPT) after active treatment. Focused question: In adult treated-periodontitis patients, what is the disease progression (CAL loss) after surgical or non-surgical therapy over ≥5 years of SPT?

17 publications from 14 independent investigations; 964 patients; follow-up range 5–15 years. Primary outcome: CAL change from end of active treatment to latest follow-up. 10/14 studies: mean CAL loss ≤0.5 mm (minimal progression). 3 studies: 0.5–1.0 mm. 4 studies: >1.0 mm (worse outcomes correlated with poor compliance). Sites with CAL loss ≥2 mm: 3–20%. Predictors: poor oral hygiene, smoking, poor SPT compliance.

## Key Contributions
- Periodontal treatment outcomes are stable over 5–15 years when patients comply with SPT — strong evidence for long-term maintenance value
- 10/14 studies show ≤0.5 mm mean CAL loss: clinically insignificant progression for compliant patients
- Non-compliance, smoking, and poor oral hygiene are the primary drivers of residual progression — modifiable risk factors
- Sites with ≥2 mm CAL loss represent 3–20% — identifies the subset of sites requiring vigilance

## Methodology
- **Inclusion**: RCTs, prospective cohorts, longitudinal observational studies; ≥5-year follow-up post-treatment; reported CAL + PD; surgical or non-surgical treatment
- **Outcomes**: CAL loss from end of active treatment; PD changes; site-level progression rates; patient-level predictors
