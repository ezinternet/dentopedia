## One-line Summary

Meta-analysis of 13 RCTs: omega-3 fatty acid intake (supplement or diet) as a periodontitis intervention gave significant PPD reduction (−0.44 mm), CAL gain (−0.51 mm), and BOP reduction (−9.45%); low risk of bias, no publication bias.

## Summary

This Journal of Clinical Periodontology meta-analysis pooled **13 interventional RCTs** (11 supplement, 2 omega-3-rich diet) testing omega-3 polyunsaturated fatty acids (PUFA) in periodontitis patients. Omega-3 is the prototypical **host-modulation / nutraceutical adjunct**: instead of killing bacteria, the EPA/DHA-derived specialized pro-resolving mediators (resolvins, protectins) are proposed to resolve gingival inflammation and limit osteoclast-driven alveolar bone loss.

The pooled effect was **statistically significant for all three core parameters** but **small in absolute terms** (sub-millimetre PPD/CAL). This positions omega-3 as a possible +α on top of mechanical therapy, not a standalone treatment.

The clinically important tension is with the **EFP S3-level guideline for stage I–III periodontitis (Sanz 2020)**, which gave a **Grade A recommendation *against*** using omega-3 PUFA as an adjunct to subgingival instrumentation — based on a smaller RCT pool than this MA later assembled. So the evidence picture is "positive effect signal in meta-analysis, but the top-tier guideline is still negative." Clinicians should treat omega-3 as low-certainty, mechanical-therapy-dependent, and not yet guideline-endorsed.

## Key Contributions

- Quantitative effect-size anchor for omega-3 on PPD (−0.44 mm), CAL (−0.51 mm), BOP (−9.45%).
- All-RCT evidence base with reported low risk of bias and no publication bias.
- Establishes the omega-3 node of the wiki's periodontal-adjunct supplement map (paired with CoQ10).

## Methodology

- 13 interventional RCTs, English, 2010–2020 (Cochrane Library, EMBASE, PubMed).
- Random-effects model; SMD + absolute MD; sensitivity and intervention-period subgroup analyses.
- Outcomes referenced against amount of omega-3 intake.

## Results

| Parameter | SMD | Absolute MD |
|---|---|---|
| PPD reduction | −0.81 | −0.44 mm |
| CAL gain | −0.77 | −0.51 mm |
| BOP reduction | −0.65 | −9.45% |

All significant overall; low risk of bias; no publication bias. Effect sizes small → adjunctive, not curative. Conflicts with EFP Grade-A-against recommendation.
