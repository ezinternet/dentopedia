## Summary

This 2025 European Journal of Oral Sciences systematic review (10 RCTs, PROSPERO-registered, search to May 2024) is the most current and most carefully designed appraisal of **coenzyme Q10 (CoQ10)** as an adjunct to non-surgical periodontal treatment (SRP). Its decisive move is **route stratification** — separating the four trials of *local* CoQ10 (one topical, three intra-pocket) from the six trials of *oral* supplementation, rather than pooling them.

The result reframes the marketing claim: the **gel route — the one sold as a "gum" product — showed no significant effect** on PD or CAL. Only **oral supplementation at 120 mg/day** produced statistically significant but small improvements (PD −0.41 mm, CAL −0.52 mm) at ~12 weeks. Crucially, the overall **certainty of evidence is very low**, so the review stops short of an evidence-based recommendation.

This contradicts the earlier pooled meta-analysis **Rasoolzadeh 2022**, which mixed routes and gingivitis-leaning indices (Plaque/Bleeding/Gingival index) and concluded *in favour* of CoQ10 gel. The disagreement is methodological: pooling routes inflates the apparent gel benefit. For practice, CoQ10 should be presented to patients as low/very-low-certainty, route-dependent, and adjunctive only — not a substitute for scaling and maintenance.

## Key Contributions

- Route-stratified analysis isolates that the **gel route lacks effect** while only **oral 120 mg/day** moved PD/CAL.
- Formal GRADE certainty: **very low** — no firm recommendation.
- Anchors the CoQ10 node of the periodontal-adjunct supplement map and exposes the conflict with the pooled 2022 MA.

## Methodology

- 10 RCTs, adults with periodontitis, SRP ± CoQ10; five databases to May 2024.
- Subgroups: local (topical n=1; intra-pocket n=3) vs oral supplementation (n=6).
- Risk of bias + GRADE certainty assessed; meta-analytic mean differences for PD and CAL.

## Results

| Route | PD reduction | CAL gain | Significant? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Local CoQ10 gel (topical/intra-pocket) | — | — | No significant effect |
| Oral 120 mg/day | −0.41 mm (95% CI 0.02–0.80) | −0.52 mm (95% CI 0.26–0.78) | Yes (12 wk) |

Overall certainty of evidence: **very low**.
