## One-line Summary

Single-blinded RCT (n=38) found locally delivered piperacillin + tazobactam equivalent to systemic amoxicillin + metronidazole as an NSPT adjunct for stage III periodontitis at 6 months across all clinical, microbiological, and immunological endpoints.

## Summary

Adjunctive antibiotics during non-surgical periodontal therapy (NSPT) improve clinical outcomes beyond mechanical debridement alone, but the 2020 EFP guidelines now restrict systemic antibiotics to specific patient subgroups (generalised stage III, younger patients) because of concerns about antimicrobial resistance and adverse systemic effects. Locally delivered antibiotics offer a potential alternative: targeted subgingival delivery theoretically limits systemic exposure while maintaining therapeutic drug concentrations at the site of infection. However, head-to-head evidence comparing local versus systemic antibiotic protocols during NSPT has been lacking.

This single-blinded RCT assigned 38 adults aged 18–40 with generalised stage III periodontitis (grade B or C) equally to one of two adjunct protocols alongside full-mouth NSPT: local delivery of piperacillin + tazobactam (Gelcide) 24 h post-NSPT in all pockets ≥4 mm (LA group, n=19) or systemic amoxicillin 500 mg + metronidazole 400 mg three times daily for 7 days (SA group, n=19). Participants were recruited at the University of Belgrade and followed for 6 months. The outcome assessor was blinded to group assignment; there were no dropouts.

At 6 months, both groups achieved highly significant improvements in PPD, CAL, BOP, and plaque index (all p<0.001 within groups) and a roughly 10-fold reduction in total subgingival bacterial count. Critically, no statistically significant differences were detected between the LA and SA groups for any clinical, microbiological, or immunological outcome at 6 months. GCF levels of IL-17 and TNF-α did not change significantly from baseline in either group, and did not differ between groups — consistent with prior literature suggesting these cytokines do not track short-term clinical improvement after periodontal treatment. The authors concluded that a single session of locally delivered piperacillin + tazobactam was a viable alternative to the 7-day systemic antibiotic course, potentially reducing systemic antibiotic burden while maintaining equivalent periodontal outcomes through 6 months.

## Key Contributions

1. First RCT directly comparing local piperacillin + tazobactam vs systemic amoxicillin + metronidazole as NSPT adjuncts.
2. Demonstrated equivalent PPD reduction (primary endpoint) and all secondary clinical/microbiological outcomes at 6 months — null hypothesis not rejected.
3. Showed that full-mouth single-delivery local antibiotic protocol (all pockets ≥4 mm, single visit) can achieve results comparable to a 7-day systemic regimen.
4. Confirmed GCF IL-17 and TNF-α are not useful markers for monitoring short-term periodontal treatment response.
5. Provided rationale aligned with EFP 2020 antibiotic-stewardship framework: locally delivered alternatives may reduce systemic antibiotic resistance pressure while preserving clinical efficacy.

## Methodology

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| Design | Single-blinded RCT; allocation concealment (opaque sealed envelopes) |
| n | 38 (19 LA, 19 SA); no dropouts |
| Population | Adults 18–40 y, generalised stage III grade B or C periodontitis, non/light smokers, penicillin-tolerant |
| Setting | University of Belgrade, Serbia; Jan 2023 – Mar 2024 |
| LA intervention | Piperacillin + tazobactam (Gelcide) local delivery, 24 h post-NSPT, single session, all pockets ≥4 mm (6 injection points/tooth, all quadrants) |
| SA intervention | Amoxicillin 500 mg + metronidazole 400 mg TID × 7 days post-NSPT |
| Clinical outcomes | PPD, CAL, BOP, PI (6 sites/tooth, UNC-15 probe, calibrated single examiner) |
| Microbiological | Total bacterial count via universal 16S rRNA qPCR from GCF paper-point samples |
| Immunological | IL-17 and TNF-α relative gene expression via RT-qPCR (GCF), normalised to GAPDH |
| Follow-up | 6 months |
| Statistics | Wilcoxon signed-rank (within), Mann-Whitney U (between); α=0.05 |

## Results

**Clinical parameters at baseline vs 6-month follow-up:**

| Parameter | LA baseline | LA 6m | SA baseline | SA 6m |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| PPD (mm) | 3.93 ± 0.54 | 3.25 ± 0.51 | 4.08 ± 0.58 | 3.29 ± 0.55 |
| CAL (mm) | 4.40 ± 0.85 | 3.83 ± 0.91 | 4.50 ± 0.77 | 4.03 ± 0.80 |
| BOP (%) | 0.631 ± 0.12 | 0.123 ± 0.08 | 0.680 ± 0.19 | 0.124 ± 0.04 |
| PI | 0.654 ± 0.13 | 0.101 ± 0.06 | 0.704 ± 0.25 | 0.115 ± 0.04 |

Within-group: p<0.001 for all parameters. Between-group: p>0.05 (no significant difference) for all parameters.

**Total bacterial count**: ~10-fold reduction in both groups (LA: p=0.001; SA: p=0.013). No between-group difference at 6 months (p>0.05).

**Cytokines**: IL-17 and TNF-α — no significant within-group change; no between-group difference (p>0.05 for all).
