## One-line Summary

Systematic review (no meta-analysis; 7 PICOS questions, ~21 included studies) of periodontal abscess and endodontic-periodontal lesion (EPL) management: combined drainage + periodontal therapy reduces PD 2–3 mm and BOP to 30–44% in abscesses (recurrence 13–23% at 15 mo), while EPL interventions yield PD reductions of 2.5–9.3 mm and tooth-loss 0–25% over 3–120 mo, on limited, heterogeneous, low-to-high-bias evidence.

## Summary

This PRISMA-conformant systematic review (PROSPERO CRD420251078257) evaluates the clinical management of two acute periodontal lesions defined by the 2018 World Workshop Classification: **periodontal abscesses** and **endodontic-periodontal lesions (EPLs)**. The authors built 7 focused PICOS questions — two for periodontal abscesses (acute and post-acute) and five for EPLs (stratified by presence of root damage, acute abscess vs pulpal inflammation, and post-acute/chronic phases). Searches across three electronic databases plus manual sources yielded 987 abscess and 545 EPL articles; 5 abscess studies (3 RCTs, 2 prospective case series) and 18 EPL studies (9 RCTs, 6 prospective, 2 retrospective, 1 case series) were ultimately included. Meta-analysis was not feasible because of heterogeneous designs, small samples, and follow-up spanning 3–120 months. The bottom line is pragmatic but cautious: periodontal abscesses are effectively managed by initial drainage followed by periodontal therapy (limited/weak evidence), whereas for EPLs no strong conclusions can be drawn.

## Key Contributions

- First systematic synthesis of the **management** of acute periodontal lesions since the 2018 Classification, complementing the diagnostic/classification framework.
- Operationally separates EPLs by **root damage** (root perforation, external root resorption, cracks/fracture) — a key prognostic axis.
- Supplies clinician-usable outcome ranges for PD, BOP, ovoid elevation, abscess recurrence, and tooth loss/survival.
- Confirms periodontal abscess as one of the most frequent dental emergencies and reports EPL prevalence ~4.9% (patient level) / 0.4% (tooth level).

## Methodology

- **Design**: systematic review (no meta-analysis), PRISMA statement, PROSPERO-registered.
- **Sources**: PubMed, Embase (via Ovid), a third database, plus manual searches.
- **Eligible studies**: any intervention study (RCT, prospective, retrospective, case series).
- **Focused questions**: FQ1–2 periodontal abscesses; FQ3–4 EPL with root damage; FQ5–7 EPL without root damage (FQ6 targets pulpal inflammation/pain).
- **Outcomes**: PD, BOP, SOP, ovoid elevation, tooth loss/survival, abscess recurrence, pain reduction.
- **Risk of bias**: low to high across studies; precluded quantitative pooling.

## Results

- **Periodontal abscesses** (drainage + periodontal therapy combined):
  - PD reduction 2–3 mm.
  - BOP from 100% down to 30%–44%.
  - Ovoid elevation from 92%–100% down to 0%–11%.
  - Abscess recurrence 13.3%–23% within 15 months.
- **EPLs** (endodontic and/or periodontal, non-surgical/surgical, alone or combined):
  - PD reduction 2.5–9.3 mm.
  - Tooth loss 0%–25% over 3–120 months.
- **Conclusion**: abscesses manageable by drainage then periodontal therapy on weak evidence; EPL conclusions limited by heterogeneity and small samples; optimal endodontic-vs-periodontal treatment sequencing remains unresolved.
