## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 3 RCTs (149 patients; 2 pooled, n=94) — systemic melatonin (3 mg nightly) adjunct to NSPT in T2DM + periodontitis significantly improved PPD (SMD −1.99), CAL (SMD −1.49), HbA1c (SMD −1.15) and hs-CRP (SMD −0.97) vs NSPT/placebo, but all GRADE-low certainty.

## Summary

According to PubMed, this systematic review and meta-analysis ([DOI 10.3390/jcm15114071](https://doi.org/10.3390/jcm15114071)) asks whether **systemic melatonin supplementation** (oral tablets), added to non-surgical periodontal therapy (NSPT = scaling and root planing + oral hygiene instructions), gives additional periodontal and systemic benefit in patients with **type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM)** and periodontitis, versus NSPT alone or NSPT + placebo.

The rationale leans on the well-established **bidirectional periodontitis–diabetes link**: hyperglycemia drives AGE-RAGE signaling, oxidative stress, and low-grade systemic inflammation that worsen periodontal breakdown, while periodontal inflammation worsens glycemic control. Melatonin — antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, immunomodulatory, pro-osteoblastic/anti-osteoclastic, and metabolically active (diabetes suppresses endogenous melatonin) — is a plausible adjunctive host-modulating agent.

Only **3 RCTs** (149 participants total) met eligibility; **2 placebo-controlled trials** (Bazyar, Anton; n=94, 8-week follow-up) were pooled. The third (Sarac Gul, Stage III/IV grade C, 3–6 mo follow-up) was qualitative-only. Pooled effects significantly favored melatonin for PPD, CAL, HbA1c, and hs-CRP — but **every outcome was rated GRADE-low certainty**, so the authors frame the findings as exploratory rather than confirmatory.

## Key Contributions

- First SR+MA isolating **systemic oral melatonin** (not local orabase/gel/nanoparticle delivery) as an NSPT adjunct in T2DM + periodontitis.
- Pools both **periodontal** (PPD, CAL) and **systemic** (HbA1c, hs-CRP, TNF-α, IL-6, TAC) endpoints in one analysis.
- Honest, conservative appraisal: thin evidence base, RoB concerns, uniformly low GRADE certainty.

## Methodology

- PRISMA 2020; prospectively registered (PROSPERO CRD420251155847). PICOS as above; minimum 1.5-month follow-up; English only; **local** melatonin delivery excluded.
- Search through 5 Feb 2026 across PubMed, Web of Science, Scopus, Cochrane Library + gray literature; 182 + 1 records → 13 full-text → 3 included (Cohen's κ > 0.90).
- Risk of bias: **Cochrane RoB 2** (1 trial "low risk"; 2 pooled trials "some concerns" from poor protocol pre-registration). Certainty: **GRADE**.
- Pooling: **SMD (Cohen's d)**, 95% CI, random-effects (RevMan 5.3), single 8-week time point; heterogeneity by Cochran's Q + I².
- Melatonin regimen in pooled trials: 2 × 250 mg tablets each containing **3 mg melatonin, once nightly ~1 h before bed × 8 weeks**, adjunct to NSPT.

## Results

Pooled (2 RCTs, n=94, 8 weeks) — all favoring adjunctive melatonin; all GRADE-**low**:

| Outcome | SMD (95% CI) | p | I² | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **PPD** | −1.99 (−2.54, −1.43) | <0.00001 | 48% | moderate–large benefit |
| **CAL** | −1.49 (−2.03, −0.95) | <0.00001 | 69% | significant benefit (heterogeneous) |
| **HbA1c** | −1.15 (−1.63, −0.68) | <0.00001 | 38% | moderate glycemic benefit |
| **serum hs-CRP** | −0.97 (−1.22, −0.73) | <0.00001 | 0% | consistent anti-inflammatory benefit |
| serum TAC | +0.08 (−0.05, +0.20) | 0.23 | 83% | not significant |
| serum TNF-α | −0.51 (−1.92, +0.90) | 0.48 | 0% | not significant |
| serum IL-6 | −5.04 (−13.97, +3.89) | 0.27 | 99% | not significant (very heterogeneous) |

- **Significant** pooled benefit: PPD, CAL, HbA1c, hs-CRP. **Non-significant**: TAC, TNF-α, IL-6.
- Individual trials: melatonin groups showed greater PPD/CAL reductions (e.g., Bazyar test PPD 4.45 → 2.59 mm; Anton test CAL 3.05 → 1.24 mm) while placebo PPD/CAL often did not change significantly. Narrative-only signals (incl. Sarac Gul): greater reductions in BoP/GI/PI, GCF MMP-8 and RANKL, MDA and TOS; increases in SOD/CAT/GPx.

**Clinical takeaway**: In T2DM patients with periodontitis, nightly 3 mg systemic melatonin as a host-modulating adjunct to SRP may add modest periodontal (PPD/CAL) and metabolic/anti-inflammatory (HbA1c, hs-CRP) benefit beyond NSPT alone — but the evidence is sparse (only 3 RCTs) and uniformly low-certainty, so it cannot yet be recommended as standard adjunctive therapy.
