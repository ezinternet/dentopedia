## One-line Summary

Single-center RCT (n=42; 21/group) — adjunctive amino-acid NaOCl + cross-linked HA ("clean and seal") added to subgingival re-instrumentation (SRI) of residual pockets in maintenance patients gave an extra 0.50 mm PD reduction and 0.57 mm CAL gain at 6 months, 88.1% vs 38.1% pocket closure, and significant decrease of 5/8 periopathobionts; effect largest in deeper pockets.

## 한줄요약

단일기관 RCT (환자 42명, 군당 21명) — 유지치료 단계 잔존 포켓의 재기구조작(SRI)에 아미노산-차아염소산나트륨(AA-NaOCl) + 가교 히알루론산(xHA, "clean and seal")을 보조로 추가하니 6개월에 PD 0.50 mm 추가 감소·CAL 0.57 mm 추가 회복, 포켓 폐쇄율 88.1% 대 38.1%, 8개 중 5개 치주병원균 유의 감소했고, 깊은 포켓일수록 효과가 더 컸다.

## Summary

In supportive periodontal care (SPC) patients with residual pockets, mechanical subgingival re-instrumentation (SRI) alone produces only modest probing-depth reduction, and prior evidence found no reliable benefit from adjuncts at this stage. This RCT tested the "clean and seal" local protocol — amino-acid–buffered sodium hypochlorite gel (AA-NaOCl, Perisolv) for biofilm/matrix degradation, followed by cross-linked hyaluronic acid gel (xHA, hyaDent BG) for clot stabilization (re-applied after 1 week) — as an adjunct to SRI.

Adding AA-NaOCl + xHA produced a statistically significant additional probing-depth reduction (0.50 mm) and clinical-attachment gain (0.57 mm) at 6 months versus SRI alone, dramatically higher pocket closure (88.1% vs 38.1%), and a significant reduction of 5 of 8 periopathobionts. Inflammatory biomarkers (IL-1β, MMP-8) did not change significantly. The benefit was greater in deeper pockets, supporting selective rather than universal use. No adverse effects occurred. Magnitude is smaller than when the same protocol is used during initial therapy (where SI added 1.1–2.1 mm), because SRI sites are previously-treated non-responders.

## Key Contributions

- First clinical evaluation (per authors) of subgingival microbiota change after AA-NaOCl + xHA applied specifically during SRI in maintenance (not initial therapy).
- Reverses, in this setting, the prior negative systematic-review signal on adjuncts to SRI — a measurable extra clinical benefit was found.
- Establishes a PD-dependent effect: greater benefit in deeper pockets → use selectively.

## Methodology

- Single-center, prospective, 2-arm RCT (1:1 block randomization, block size 2), Germany, Feb 2022–Dec 2023. Registered DRKS00017415.
- Periodontitis stage III/IV grade B/C, post-active therapy, in SPC; ≥16 teeth, ≥40 y, 2–8 residual sites (PD ≥5 mm, or PD ≥4 mm + BOP+), API ≤35%.
- **n = 42 analyzed** (77 screened → 44 randomized, 22/arm → 1 dropout/arm → 21 control / 21 test). 71% female both groups.
- Per patient, the **2 highest-PD non-adjacent pockets** chosen as study sites.
- Both groups: SPC + SRI (Gracey curettes, air scaler, erythritol air polishing, saline rinse) under LA.
- Test group: AA-NaOCl gel instilled 60 s × 3 (before, mid, after instrumentation) → saline rinse → xHA gel; xHA re-applied subgingivally at 1 week. Persisting pockets re-treated at T1 (3 mo) per allocation.
- Outcomes at T0 / T1 (3 mo) / T2 (6 mo): primary ΔPD; secondary ΔCAL, BOP, plaque, pocket closure, GCF IL-1β & MMP-8 (ELISA), 8 periopathobionts (qPCR). Lab analyst blinded; treatment blinding impossible (NaOCl smell/taste).
- Stats: a priori power ≥80% for 1 mm ΔPD at T2; Friedman (intragroup), Mann–Whitney U + Bonferroni (intergroup), Pearson Chi-square (nominal); p<0.05.

## Results

**Probing depth (PD, mm):**

| | Baseline | 3 mo | 6 mo |
|---|---|---|---|
| Control | 4.93 (±1.05) | 4.26 (±1.04) | 4.05 (±0.96) |
| Test | 4.69 (±0.87) | 3.60 (±0.73) | 3.31 (±0.72) |

- **Additional ΔPD (test vs control):** +0.43 mm at 3 mo (1.10 [95% CI 0.87–1.32] vs 0.67 [0.46–0.87]; p=0.007); **+0.50 mm at 6 mo** (1.38 [1.11–1.65] vs 0.88 [0.62–1.14]; p=0.008).
- **Additional ΔCAL at 6 mo:** **+0.57 mm** (1.07 [0.70–1.44] vs 0.50 [0.24–0.76]; p=0.017).
- **BOP+ reduction at study sites (6 mo):** 78.6% test vs 19.0% control (p<0.001).
- **Pocket closure (PD ≤4 mm + BOP-) at 6 mo:** residual pockets down **88.1% (n=37) test vs 38.1% (n=16) control** (p<0.001).
- **IL-1β, MMP-8:** no significant change, no intergroup difference.
- **Microbiology:** significant decrease of **5 of 8 periopathobionts in test group only** (incl. A. actinomycetemcomitans); control stable.
- **Effect modifier:** PD reduction more pronounced in deeper pockets.
- No adverse effects.

**Benchmarks (discussion):** AA-NaOCl alone +0.21–0.31 mm ΔPD; HA alone +0.2 mm; sustained-release CHX/tetracycline fibres +0.6–0.7 mm; flapless EMD +0.79 mm. The combined effect here likely reflects both adjuncts cumulatively.

## Clinical Bottom Line

For residual pockets in maintenance, adding the "clean and seal" (AA-NaOCl + xHA) protocol to SRI gives ~0.5 mm extra PD reduction, ~0.57 mm extra CAL gain, and roughly doubles pocket-closure rate at 6 months, with a real microbiological benefit but no biomarker change. Reserve it for deeper residual pockets where the gain is largest, to balance the added material cost and chairtime.
