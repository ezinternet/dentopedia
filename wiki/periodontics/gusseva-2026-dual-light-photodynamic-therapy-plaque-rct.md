## One-line Summary

Crossover pilot RCT (n=30 healthy adults, 6-week design) — daily 10-min home-use dual-light (405+810 nm) aPDT with indocyanine green, added to conventional oral care, cut plaque (Greene-Vermillion) ~21% vs baseline (pooled p=0.0001), while conventional care alone showed no significant change (-6.5%, p=0.11).

## Summary

This six-week randomized crossover pilot RCT, conducted at the University of Tartu (February–May 2024), tested whether daily 10-minute home-use **dual-light antimicrobial photodynamic therapy (aPDT)** — the Lumoral® device emitting simultaneous 405 nm antibacterial blue light and 810 nm near-infrared light, activated by indocyanine green (ICG) photosensitizer — adds meaningful plaque control beyond conventional toothbrushing and flossing in healthy young adults.

The key finding: in the pooled within-subject analysis, aPDT adjunct periods produced a **−21.2% reduction** in Greene-Vermillion plaque scores (1.0 [1.0–1.5] → 0.5 [0.5–1.0], p=0.0001), while conventional self-care alone produced no statistically significant change (−6.5%, p=0.11). Reductions were confirmed at premolars (−18.6%, p=0.0028) and incisors (−20.7%, p=0.0008) specifically.

**Clinical context**: Conventional toothbrushing may leave up to ~50% of plaque even with proper technique. Chlorhexidine, the standard chemical adjunct, carries risks of tooth staining, taste alteration, microbiome disruption, and antimicrobial cross-resistance. Dual-light aPDT offers a localized, non-antibiotic alternative that does not select for resistant strains. This pilot demonstrates proof-of-concept in a healthy population with already-good oral hygiene — the population where mechanical self-care should be most effective, making the added benefit notable.

**Limitations to keep in mind**: Small pilot (n=30), short follow-up (6 weeks), retrospective registration, no placebo device (partial blinding only), baseline group imbalance (p=0.026), no microbiological data, and COI (corresponding author holds equity in device manufacturer Koite Health Ltd.).

## Key Contributions

- First randomized crossover RCT of home-use dual-light aPDT (Lumoral®/Lumorinse®) in a **healthy adult population** with good baseline oral hygiene — fills the gap left by prior trials in diseased populations (periodontitis, peri-implantitis).
- Demonstrates significant plaque reduction beyond mechanical self-care using a within-subject design — each participant serves as their own control, controlling for hygiene habit variation.
- Provides tooth-location specificity: premolar and incisor sites showed significant reductions during aPDT, not during standard care.
- Establishes the 10-minute, once-daily, pre-brushing protocol as clinically workable in healthy young adults (0% dropout).
- Positions dual-light aPDT as an adjunct for plaque-retentive scenarios (orthodontic appliances, implants, fixed prostheses, reduced manual dexterity) — extrapolated hypothesis for future trials, not directly tested here.

## Methodology

- **Study design**: Prospective, randomized, crossover; 6 weeks; two groups.
- **Population**: n=30 healthy adults, age 20–25 years (dental students excluded), no comorbidities, GV index 0.3–1.9, no gum disease (BOP <10%, no probing depths ≥4 mm), no daily mouthwash use.
- **Crossover sequence**:
  - **Phase 1 (weeks 1–2)**: Group 1 → dual-light aPDT + standard care; Group 2 → standard care only.
  - **Crossover/washout (weeks 3–4)**: both groups → standard care only.
  - **Phase 2 (weeks 5–6)**: Group 1 → standard care only; Group 2 → dual-light aPDT + standard care.
- **Intervention**: Lumoral® device (48-LED mouthpiece, 30 J/cm², 10-min); Lumorinse® tablet (ICG 250 µg/mL, swished 60 seconds before light activation). Used daily **before** toothbrushing, interdental cleaning, and flossing.
- **Standard care**: twice-daily fluoride toothbrushing (mechanical or electric per preference), daily interdental cleaning (floss/IDB/silicone picks). No mouthrinse.
- **Primary outcome**: Greene-Vermillion Oral Hygiene Index at four time points (Baseline 1, week 2, Baseline 2, week 6); six index teeth (11, 14, 24, 31, 34, 44); buccal surfaces scored 0–3.
- **Statistics**: Wilcoxon signed-rank (within-group), Mann-Whitney U (between-group); medians [IQR]; two-sided α=0.05. Means ± SEM reported descriptively. Post-hoc sample size: 14/group needed (80% power), 30 enrolled (buffer for dropout).
- **Calibration**: Two calibrated investigators; joint scoring exercises on standardized intraoral photographs; PI confirmed borderline findings. Baseline 1 assessed pre-randomization (blinded).

## Results

| Phase | Group | Baseline median [IQR] | Post median [IQR] | Change | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Phase 1 | Group 1 (aPDT) | 1.0 [0.75–1.5] | 0.75 [0.5–1.0] | −28.4% | <0.0001 |
| Phase 1 | Group 2 (std care) | 1.0 [1.0–1.5] | 1.0 [0.5–1.25] | −24.1% | <0.0001 |
| Phase 2 | Group 2 (aPDT) | 1.125 [0.94–1.5] | 0.75 [0.5–1.25] | −14.1% | 0.011 |
| Phase 2 | Group 1 (std care) | 0.5 [0.44–0.81] | 0.63 [0.5–1.19] | +22.4% | 0.11 (ns) |

**Pooled analysis** (all aPDT periods vs all standard care periods):
- aPDT: 1.0 [1.0–1.5] → 0.5 [0.5–1.0], **−21.2%**, **p = 0.0001**
- Standard care: 1.0 [0.5–1.5] → 1.0 [0.5–1.5], −6.5%, p = 0.107 (ns)

**Tooth-site analysis** (pooled aPDT periods):
- Premolars: 1.125 [0.75–1.25] → 0.75 [0.5–1.06], −18.6%, p = 0.0028
- Incisors: 1.0 [1.0–1.5] → 0.5 [0.5–1.0], −20.7%, p = 0.0008
- Standard care periods: no significant change at either site (premolars −11.4% ns; incisors −2.0% ns)

**Key contextual point**: The Phase 1 standard-care Group 2 also showed p<0.0001 improvement (−24.1%), attributed to the oral hygiene instruction effect at baseline visit — not a treatment effect of standard care alone. In Phase 2, without a fresh instruction reinforcement, standard care produced no significant change (+22.4%, p=0.11), while aPDT continued to work (−14.1%, p=0.011).

**Individual variability**: A minority of participants showed no improvement or slight increase during aPDT periods; plaque scores were more variable during standard-care periods than during aPDT periods.
