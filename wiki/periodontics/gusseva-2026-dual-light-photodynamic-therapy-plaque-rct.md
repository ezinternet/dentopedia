## One-line Summary

Crossover pilot RCT (n=30 healthy adults, 6-week design) — daily 10-min home-use dual-light (405+810 nm) aPDT with indocyanine green, added to conventional oral care, cut plaque (Greene-Vermillion) ~21% vs baseline (pooled p=0.0001), while conventional care alone showed no significant change (-6.5%, p=0.11).

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1016/j.jdent.2026.106641](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jdent.2026.106641)), this is a six-week randomized crossover pilot RCT in 30 young healthy adults testing whether a daily 10-minute home-use **dual-light antimicrobial photodynamic therapy (aPDT)** — 405 nm + 810 nm light with an indocyanine green photosensitizer — adds plaque control beyond conventional toothbrushing and flossing. This page is **abstract-only** (full text not retrieved); Summary and Results reflect the PubMed abstract only. In the pooled within-subject analysis, the aPDT adjunct reduced plaque (Greene-Vermillion index) by 21.2% from baseline (1.0 [1.0–1.5] → 0.5 [0.5–1.0], p=0.0001), whereas conventional care alone produced no significant change (−6.5%, p=0.11). Authors note up to ~50% of plaque can persist even with proper mechanical technique, motivating a non-mechanical adjunct.

**Clinically:** A light-based chemical adjunct (dual-light aPDT) can give a statistically meaningful extra plaque reduction even in patients who already brush and floss well — most plausibly useful where mechanical access is compromised (orthodontic appliances, implants, fixed prostheses, reduced manual dexterity). This is a small pilot, so treat it as hypothesis-generating, not as a standard-of-care recommendation.

## Key Contributions

- Tests a home-use dual-light aPDT device (405 nm + 810 nm + indocyanine green) as a daily adjunct to mechanical self-care.
- Shows an additional, significant plaque reduction even in healthy adults with good baseline hygiene.
- Within-subject crossover controls for individual hygiene variation.
- Positions aPDT for plaque-retentive populations (extrapolated, not directly tested).

## Methodology

- **Design**: 6-week randomized crossover; 2-week treatment phases with a 2-week crossover/switch phase.
- **n=30** young healthy adults, two groups:
  - **Group 1**: 2 weeks daily dual-light aPDT + conventional care, then switched.
  - **Group 2**: regular oral care first, then crossed over to aPDT.
- **Intervention**: daily 10-min dual-light aPDT (405 + 810 nm) with indocyanine green photosensitizer.
- **Outcome**: dental plaque via the **Greene-Vermillion index** at four time points.
- **Analysis**: per-phase group comparisons + pooled aPDT-vs-conventional analysis (medians [IQR]).

## Results

- **Baseline**: Group 1 1.0 [0.75–1.5] vs Group 2 1.0 [1.0–1.5], p = 0.026 (imbalanced).
- **First treatment phase** (both improved): Group 1 → 0.75 [0.5–1.0], −28.4%, p < 0.0001; Group 2 → 1.0 [0.5–1.25], −24.1%, p < 0.0001.
- **Second treatment phase**:
  - Group 2 (now aPDT): 1.125 [0.94–1.5] → 0.75 [0.5–1.25], −14.1%, p = 0.011 (significant).
  - Group 1 (standard care only): 0.5 [0.44–0.81] → 0.63 [0.5–1.19], +22.4%, p = 0.11 (no significant change).
- **Pooled**:
  - Adjunct dual-light aPDT: 1.0 [1.0–1.5] → 0.5 [0.5–1.0], −21.2%, **p = 0.0001** (significant).
  - Conventional care alone: 1.0 [0.5–1.5] → 1.0 [0.5–1.5], −6.5%, p = 0.11 (no significant change).
