## One-line Summary
Systematic review + meta-analysis (12 RCTs, follow-up ≥6 mo) finding that sustained-release subgingival antiseptics (gels/chips/varnish) as adjuncts to SRP significantly improve PD reduction, CAL gain, and BOP (all p=0.001), whereas subgingival antiseptic irrigation adds no significant benefit over SRP alone.

## Summary
Scaling and root planing (SRP) is the cornerstone of nonsurgical periodontal therapy, but its efficacy is limited in deep pockets, intrabony defects, and furcations where mechanical instrumentation is incomplete (up to ~30% of debrided root surface may retain residual calculus). This systematic review with meta-analysis evaluates whether adding chemical antiseptics to SRP improves clinical outcomes. Twelve RCTs (follow-up ≥6 months) published 2000–2019 were pooled, stratified by antiseptic **delivery vehicle**. The central finding is a delivery-dependent effect: **sustained-release antiseptics** (gels, chips, varnish) significantly outperform SRP alone, whereas **subgingival irrigation** with antiseptic solutions does not.

## Key Contributions
- First-line synthesis distinguishing **how** the antiseptic is delivered (sustained-release vs single-pass irrigation) rather than only **which** agent is used.
- Provides pooled significance for the three core periodontal outcomes (PD, CAL, BOP) under each delivery mode.
- Practical implication: when choosing a local adjunct, the **vehicle** (sustained-release) is decisive for clinical benefit; one-time irrigation is unlikely to add measurable value over SRP.

## Methodology
- Databases: MEDLINE (Ovid) + Cochrane CENTRAL; English-language RCTs; published 1 Jan 2000 – 22 Sep 2019.
- Inclusion: RCTs comparing ≥1 antiseptic agent as adjunct to SRP, follow-up ≥6 months.
- Outcomes: probing depth (PD) reduction, clinical attachment level (CAL) gain, bleeding on probing (BOP).
- Pooling: random-effects meta-analysis; 12 RCTs (parallel + split-mouth) across 9 countries, chronic periodontitis cohorts, follow-up 6–12 months.
- Agents/vehicles tested: CHX (chip, xanthan gel, varnish, 0.12% solution), 7.5%/10% PVI, 0.5% NaOCl, 0.75% boric acid gel.

## Results
- **Sustained-release antiseptics (gels/chips/varnish) + SRP** vs SRP alone — significant benefit:
  - PD reduction: p = 0.001
  - CAL gain: p = 0.001
  - BOP: p = 0.001
- **Antiseptic irrigation + SRP** vs SRP alone — no significant benefit:
  - PD: p = 0.321 | CAL: p = 0.7568 | BOP: p = 0.3549
- Conclusion: adjunctive subgingivally delivered antiseptics with a **sustained-release** vehicle have significant clinical benefits over SRP alone; irrigation does not.
