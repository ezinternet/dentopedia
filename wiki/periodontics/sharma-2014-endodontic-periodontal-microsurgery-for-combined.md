Narrative overview arguing that microsurgical management of combined endodontic-periodontal lesions — endodontic microsurgery plus concurrent bone grafting, membrane barriers, and papilla-preserving flaps for primary closure — outperforms traditional surgical approaches.

## Summary
Sharma and colleagues review how to surgically manage the hardest periodontal-endodontic problem: the combined endo-perio lesion. Their thesis is that microsurgical technique has surpassed traditional endodontic and periodontal surgery for isolated defects, and that combined lesions require all of it at once — endodontic microsurgery plus concurrent bone grafting and barrier membranes. Success hinges on preventing epithelial downgrowth and regenerating periodontal cementum, fiber, and bone, with minimally invasive papilla-preserving flaps and submergence of grafts improving guided-tissue-regeneration outcomes and achieving primary closure. As a narrative overview it offers technique principles rather than comparative outcome data, but it operationalizes the treatment side of Herrera's endo-perio classification.

## Key Contributions
- Microsurgical framework for combined endo-perio lesions.
- Stresses concurrent regeneration (graft + membrane) to block epithelial downgrowth.
- Primary closure via papilla-preserving flaps and graft submergence as GTR success factors.

## Methodology
Narrative overview of endodontic and periodontal microsurgical techniques and regenerative adjuncts applied to combined endo-perio lesions.

## Results
Microsurgery reports higher success than traditional surgery for isolated defects. Combined lesions need endodontic microsurgery + bone grafting + barrier membranes simultaneously. Preventing epithelial downgrowth and achieving primary closure with graft submergence are decisive for regeneration.

## Clinical Application
- For a combined endo-perio lesion judged restorable (per Herrera's fracture/perforation/periodontitis criteria), plan endodontic microsurgery and regenerative periodontal surgery as one staged effort, not sequentially.
- Tension-free primary closure and graft coverage are the make-or-break technical steps.
