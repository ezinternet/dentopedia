## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 55 RCTs on PRF for periodontal intrabony defects: adding PRF to OFD gives ~1.3 mm PPD reduction, ~1.2 mm CAL gain, and ~1.6 mm radiographic bone fill vs OFD alone; PRF is clinically equivalent to EMD/PRP/bone graft/barrier membrane; adding PRF to a bone graft — and adding metformin/bisphosphonates/statins to PRF — yields further significant gains.

## 한줄요약

치주 골내결손(intrabony defect)에 대한 PRF 55개 RCT SR+MA: OFD 단독 대비 PRF 병용 시 탐침깊이 약 1.3mm 감소·임상부착수준 약 1.2mm 획득·방사선 골충전 약 1.6mm 개선; PRF는 EMD·PRP·골이식재·차폐막과 임상적으로 대등하고, 골이식재에 PRF를 더하거나 PRF에 메트포르민·비스포스포네이트·스타틴을 더하면 추가 유의 개선.

## Summary

This is the largest and most current systematic review with meta-analysis (SR+MA) on **platelet-rich fibrin (PRF)** for the treatment of periodontal **intrabony defects**, pooling **55 RCTs** (596 records screened, searches to Oct 1, 2023). The RCTs were organized into 21 comparison categories under five Groups, with weighted mean differences for probing pocket depth (PPD), clinical attachment level (CAL), and radiographic bone fill (RBF).

The central messages: (1) as an adjunct to open flap debridement (OFD), PRF produces robust, statistically significant clinical benefit — on the order of ~1.3 mm PPD reduction, ~1.2 mm CAL gain, and ~1.6 mm bone fill over OFD alone; (2) head-to-head, PRF is **equivalent** to established biologics/materials (EMD, PRP, bone graft, barrier membrane) — an autogenous, inexpensive material matching commercial options; (3) **adding PRF to a bone graft** further improves all three outcomes over graft alone; and (4) adding small biomolecules (metformin, bisphosphonates, statins — but not antibiotics or LLLT) to PRF adds further benefit. This positions PRF as a low-cost autogenous alternative or additive within the intrabony-defect regenerative armamentarium, while flagging major protocol heterogeneity (35% of studies did not report the centrifuge system) and a senior-author PRF conflict of interest.

## Key Contributions

- Largest PRF-in-intrabony-defect SR+MA to date (55 RCTs vs ≤21 in prior SRs).
- Quantifies OFD/PRF benefit vs OFD alone: PPD MD 1.27 mm, CAL MD 1.22 mm, RBF MD 1.59 mm (all p<0.00001).
- Establishes PRF clinical **equivalence** to EMD, PRP, bone graft, and barrier membrane (Group III — no overall advantage either way).
- Shows **PRF added to a bone graft** significantly beats graft alone (Group IV), and biomolecule addition to PRF (Group V) adds further gains.
- Documents protocol underreporting/heterogeneity and near-total exclusion of smokers (only 3/55 RCTs).

## Methodology

- **Design**: PRISMA SR+MA; protocol registered INPLASY 202430001; searches (PubMed/MEDLINE, Cochrane CENTRAL, Scopus, Embase, Lilacs + gray literature) to Oct 1, 2023, no language limit; two independent reviewers with Cohen's κ concordance.
- **Groups**: I = OFD vs OFD/PRF (23 studies); II = L-PRF vs T-PRF (2); III = comparisons to PRF — PRP/EMD/BG/barrier-membrane/PRGF (15); IV = addition of PRF to BG/GTR/EMD/BG+BM (20); V = addition of biomaterial/biomolecule (BG, antibiotic, metformin, bisphosphonate, statins, LLLT) to PRF (13).
- **Outcomes**: PPD, CAL, RBF (weighted mean differences, mm; random-effects; forest plots).
- **Reporting/quality**: centrifuge system unreported in 19/55 (35%); 32/55 used 3000 rpm × 10 min; Cochrane RoB mostly "unclear" (e.g., Group I: 5 low / 16 unclear / 1 high).

## Results

**Group I — OFD/PRF vs OFD alone (22 pooled):**
- PPD: MD **1.27 mm** (95% CI 1.03–1.51; p<0.00001; I²=87%).
- CAL: MD **1.22 mm** (95% CI 0.91–1.53; p<0.00001; I²=89%).
- RBF: MD **1.59 mm** (95% CI 1.35–1.82; p<0.00001; I²=90%).
- Narrative (23 RCTs): 20 significant for PPD, 16 for CAL, 18 for bone fill (~1.3 / ~1.2 / ~1.5 mm respectively).

**Group II — L-PRF vs T-PRF (2):**
- PPD: MD **0.64 mm favoring T-PRF** (95% CI 0.18–1.09; p=0.006; I²=0%).
- CAL: no difference (MD 0.40 mm; 95% CI −0.19 to 0.99; p=0.19).

**Group III — comparisons to PRF (15):** no overall PRF advantage.
- PPD MD **−0.11 mm** (p=0.43); CAL MD **−0.13 mm** (p=0.39); RBF MD **−0.01 mm** (p=0.95).
- Subgroup exceptions (CAL): OFD/BG/PRF > OFD/BG/BM by 0.54 mm (p=0.02); OFD/BG > OFD/PRF by 0.40 mm (6 studies; p=0.004 — graft slightly better for CAL, space-maintenance dependent).
- Interpretation: PRF ≈ EMD ≈ PRP ≈ barrier membrane.

**Group IV — addition of PRF (20):** favors PRF.
- PPD MD **0.59 mm** (95% CI 0.16–1.02; p=0.007); CAL MD **0.57 mm** (95% CI 0.03–1.12; p=0.04); RBF MD **0.63 mm** (95% CI 0.40–0.86; p<0.00001).
- BG vs BG/PRF drove the effect (PPD 0.63, CAL 0.66, RBF 0.60 mm; all significant).

**Group V — biomolecule/graft addition to PRF (13):** favors combinations.
- PPD MD **0.68 mm** (p<0.00001); CAL MD **0.85 mm** (95% CI 0.68–1.02; p<0.00001; I²=36%); PRF vs PRF/BG RBF MD **0.80 mm** (5 studies; p<0.0001).
- Metformin, bisphosphonates, and statins added benefit; **PRF/antibiotic and PRF/LLLT did not**.

**Limitations**: high heterogeneity (I² often 80–95%), sparse RCTs per specific comparison (many single-trial subgroups), unclear/high RoB in many studies, near-absence of smokers (3/55), no human histology or pooled PROMs, and a senior-author PRF conflict of interest.
