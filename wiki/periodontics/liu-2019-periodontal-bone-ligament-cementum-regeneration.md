## One-line Summary

Systematic review of stem cell and scaffold strategies — including 3D bioprinting, gene therapy, and layered biomimetic constructs — for simultaneous bone–PDL–cementum complex regeneration in periodontitis.

## Summary

This is the first systematic review to comprehensively address simultaneous regeneration of all three periodontal tissues — bone, periodontal ligament (PDL), and cementum — rather than each tissue in isolation. The review covers four major engineering paradigms: (1) transplantation of exogenous mesenchymal stem cells (PDLSCs, BMSCs, DPSCs, iPSC-MSCs) on absorbable scaffolds; (2) endogenous cell-homing via chemokines (SDF-1α) and growth factors (BMP7) delivered in 3D-printed scaffolds; (3) gene therapy using viral vectors (Ad-PDGF-B) to transiently upregulate regenerative signaling; and (4) layered biomimetic (LBL) constructs that spatially segregate cementum, PDL, and bone compartments. The critical challenge is generating oriented Sharpey's fibers that insert perpendicularly into both new cementum and new bone — a feat currently only achievable via PCL/PGA fiber-guiding 3D-printed scaffolds. Clinical translation is limited: only autograft/allograft bone, Bio-Oss, and collagen membranes are approved; stem cell therapies are in early clinical trials; gene therapy and LBL materials remain laboratory-stage.

## Key Contributions

- PDLSCs seeded on biphasic calcium phosphate (BCP) scaffolds achieve regeneration of bone + PDL (with reorganized Sharpey's fiber insertion at correct angle) + cementum + blood vessels in dogs at 12 weeks
- 3D bioprinting of fiber-guiding PCL/PGA scaffolds generates geometrically controlled, perpendicularly oriented PDL fibers — the only technique demonstrating reliable Sharpey's fiber direction control (250–300 µm PDL compartment)
- Layered biomimetic (LBL) constructs achieve complete periodontal reconstruction (cementum + 45° oblique PDL + cribriform bone + Sharpey's fibers) within 20 days in dogs — superior to single-membrane approach
- Endogenous cell-homing (SDF-1α + BMP7 delivery in 3D-printed anatomic scaffold) generates PDL-like connective tissue (42%) and new bone without exogenous cell transplantation
- Long-term (120 months) GTR outcome: absorbable barrier CAL-V gain 3.5 ± 2.5 mm superior to non-resorbable 1.5 ± 1.2 mm; no short-term (9–12 month) difference

## Methodology

**Design**: Systematic review (narrative synthesis; no meta-analysis or PRISMA)
**Scope**: All preclinical and early clinical studies on periodontal bone/PDL/cementum/complex regeneration
**Models**: Rat, dog, rabbit (most), immunodeficient mouse (ectopic), early clinical trials
**Key interventions reviewed**: Bone grafts (autograft/FDBA/DFBA/Bio-Oss), injectable CPC scaffolds with encapsulated stem cells, PDLSC cell sheets, 3D bioprinted PCL/PGA/PCL-HA scaffolds, gene therapy (Ad-PDGF-B, Porphyromonas gingivalis FimA vaccination), LBL tissue-engineered constructs

## Results

| Approach | Model | Key Finding |
|---|---|---|
| hBMSC-MB/CPC (injectable) | Rat bone defect | New bone 42.1% ± 7.8% at 12 wks (3× control) |
| Tri-culture + CPC | Rat cranial defect | New bone 45% (4.5×), vessels 50% (2.5×) at 12 wks |
| PDLSC-seeded BCP | Dog periodontal defect | PDL + cementum + bone at 12 wks; correct Sharpey's angle |
| PDL cell sheet + β-TCP | Dog furcation defect | Well-oriented PDL fibers at 6 wks |
| Fiber-guiding PCL/PGA | Rat ectopic model | Controlled perpendicular PDL fiber orientation |
| LBL construct (tissue-engineered) | Dog periodontal defect | Complete PDL + cementum + bone at 20 days |
| Ad-PDGF-B gene therapy | Rat periodontal defect | 4× bone bridge, 6× cementum repair vs. control at 14 days |
| GTR absorbable barrier (clinical) | Periodontal patients | CAL-V: 3.5 ± 2.5 mm at 120 months (3.3 ± 1.6 mm at 12 months) |
