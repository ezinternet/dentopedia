## One-line Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis (Dent J 2024; 5 databases, 10,775 articles screened, 8 qualitative / 10 quantitative) on implant health in treated periodontitis patients: a history of treated periodontitis confers ~4.8× higher odds of peri-implantitis, plus reduced implant survival and greater pocket depth and peri-implant bone loss versus periodontally healthy controls.

## Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis evaluating the role of periodontitis history on implant failure outcomes. Searched 5 databases (MEDLINE, Embase, ScienceDirect, LILACS, Cochrane Library; June 2024). 10,775 potentially eligible articles screened; 8 included in qualitative analysis, 10 in quantitative synthesis. Primary outcomes: peri-implantitis incidence and implant survival rate. Secondary outcomes: mean pocket depth and mean peri-implant bone loss.

Patients with a history of treated periodontitis had approximately 4.8× higher odds of developing peri-implantitis vs periodontally healthy controls. Implant survival rates were also lower. Pocket depth and peri-implant bone loss were greater in periodontitis history patients.

## Key Contributions
- Quantifies risk: peri-implantitis ~4.8× more likely in treated periodontitis patients — clinical implication for pre-implant counseling
- Implant survival also reduced (not only complications), suggesting that periodontitis history is a systemic host factor, not just local inflammation
- Reinforces that periodontal health must be established and maintained before and after implant placement
- Strong search (5 databases, June 2024); heterogeneity acknowledged as limitation

## Methodology
- **Databases**: MEDLINE, Embase, ScienceDirect, LILACS, Cochrane Library + manual search
- **Search date**: June 2024
- **Qualitative analysis**: 8 studies; **Quantitative synthesis**: 10 studies
- **Primary outcomes**: Peri-implantitis incidence, implant survival rate
- **Secondary outcomes**: Mean pocket depth, mean peri-implant bone loss

## Results

| Outcome | Result |
|---|---|
| Peri-implantitis risk | ~4.8× higher (statistically significant) |
| Implant survival rate | Reduced vs healthy controls |
| Mean pocket depth | Increased in periodontitis history |
| Mean peri-implant bone loss | Greater in periodontitis history |
