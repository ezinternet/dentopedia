## Summary

Periodontitis affects ~700 million people worldwide (~11% of the global population) and is driven by dysbiotic subgingival biofilms that disrupt the RANKL/RANK/OPG axis, tipping periodontal homeostasis toward progressive osteoclast-mediated bone loss. This 2026 Bone Research review provides the most comprehensive molecular map to date of the four intracellular pathways governing periodontal osteoclastogenesis: (1) NF-κB (canonical + non-canonical arms), (2) JAK/STAT (IL-6, IL-17A/STAT3), (3) MAPK (p38, JNK, ERK), and (4) PI3K/Akt/mTOR. The master transcription factor NFATc1 integrates signals from all four pathways to drive osteoclast-specific gene expression (TRAP, cathepsin K, MMP-9). A novel concept — "trained osteoclastogenesis" — is introduced: monocytes exposed to P. gingivalis acquire MITF-mediated epigenetic memory that amplifies bone resorption upon re-challenge. Current and emerging therapeutic strategies (denosumab, JAK inhibitors, p38 inhibitors, natural compounds, site-specific drug delivery) are critically evaluated, with key translational barriers identified.

## Key Contributions

- NFATc1 is established as the convergent master regulator integrating NF-κB + MAPK + PI3K/Akt signals for terminal osteoclast differentiation
- "Trained osteoclastogenesis" via MITF-mediated innate immune memory explains why chronic periodontitis exhibits burst-phase amplification on re-exposure to dysbiotic biofilm
- NF-κB non-canonical pathway (IKKα/RelB:p52) sustains chronic bone loss beyond acute inflammatory events — distinct target from canonical IKKβ/p65:p50 (acute) arm
- GCF MMP-8 and IL-1β identified as candidate biomarkers for precision timing of biologic intervention (active vs. quiescent disease)
- Site-specific delivery platforms (injectable hydrogels, bioresorbable microspheres, mucoadhesive gels) identified as essential to translate systemic-toxic pathway inhibitors into periodontal clinical use

## Results

| Pathway | Key Mediators | Effect on Bone |
|---|---|---|
| RANKL/OPG axis | RANKL↑/OPG↓ by dysbiotic biofilm | Central osteoclastogenesis driver |
| NF-κB (canonical) | IKKβ → p65:p50 → TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-6 | Acute inflammatory bone resorption |
| NF-κB (non-canonical) | IKKα → RelB:p52 | Chronic/sustained bone loss |
| JAK/STAT | IL-6/IL-17A → STAT3 → NFATc1↑ | Accelerated osteoclastogenesis |
| MAPK-p38 | TRAF6 → p38 → NFATc1, COX-2 | Osteoclast survival + resorption |
| MAPK-JNK | AP-1/c-Fos → NFATc1 | Osteoclast differentiation |
| MAPK-ERK | Ras-Raf-MEK-ERK → proliferation | Osteoclast precursor expansion |
| PI3K/Akt | Akt → mTOR/FOXO1/NFATc1 | Osteoclast survival + activity |
| SPM pathway | Lipoxins/resolvins — resolution failure | Chronic non-resolving inflammation |
| Trained osteoclastogenesis | MITF epigenetic memory in monocytes | Amplified resorption on re-challenge |

**Therapeutic evaluation:**
- **Denosumab (anti-RANKL)**: Effective in preclinical models; MRONJ + immunosuppression risk limits long-term periodontal use
- **JAK inhibitors (tofacitinib class)**: Promising anti-inflammatory; systemic toxicity requires local delivery
- **p38 MAPK inhibitor (SD282)**: Reduces bone loss in preclinical models; clinical translation limited
- **Bortezomib**: Reduces RANKL/OPG ratio + inhibits NF-κB + p38 + ERK; but toxicity concerns
- **Natural compounds** (curcumin, resveratrol, flavonoids): Favorable safety; poor bioavailability; insufficient standalone clinical evidence
