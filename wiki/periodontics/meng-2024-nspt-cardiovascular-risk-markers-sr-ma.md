## One-line Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis (21 RCTs) on non-surgical periodontal therapy (NSPT/SRP) and cardiovascular risk markers in periodontitis patients: NSPT significantly reduces CRP (low certainty), IL-6 (moderate), and systolic blood pressure (moderate), but produces no significant change in LDL, HDL, total cholesterol, TG, DBP, TNF-α, IL-1β, or FMD.

## Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis of 21 RCTs evaluating the effect of non-surgical periodontal therapy (NSPT — primarily subgingival scaling and root planing) on CVD-related biomarkers in periodontitis patients. Key finding: NSPT significantly reduces CRP (low certainty), IL-6 (moderate certainty), and systolic blood pressure (moderate certainty), but does not significantly change LDL, HDL, total cholesterol, TG, DBP, TNF-α, IL-1β, or FMD.

## Key Contributions
- Demonstrates anti-inflammatory benefit of NSPT beyond the oral cavity
- Quantifies differential effects: inflammatory markers improve but lipid profile does not
- Moderate certainty evidence for SBP reduction — clinically significant for hypertension management
- Supports perio-systemic link without claiming causation

## Methodology
- PICOS: periodontitis patients; NSPT (SRP ± OHI); vs no treatment or supragingival scaling only; 11 CVD markers
- 4 databases, inception–April 2023; 1353 studies screened; 21 RCTs included
- Random and fixed-effects models; WMD with 95% CI; GRADE certainty

## Results Summary
| Marker | Effect | Certainty |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| CRP | Significant ↓ | Low |
| IL-6 | Significant ↓ | Moderate |
| SBP | Significant ↓ | Moderate |
| LDL/HDL/TC/TG | No significant change | Moderate |
| IL-1β / TNF-α / DBP / FMD | No significant change | Low |
