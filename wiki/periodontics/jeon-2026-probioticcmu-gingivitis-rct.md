## One-line Summary

Double-blind placebo-controlled RCT (n=80) of 8-week OraCMU/ProbioticCMU oral probiotic tablets vs placebo in adults with gingivitis/incipient periodontitis: probiotic gave significantly greater reductions in gingival index (GI −0.19 vs −0.08, p=.035) and bleeding on probing (BOP −7.74 vs −2.82, p=.030), modulated inflammatory markers and oral microbiota, no serious adverse events.

## Summary

According to PubMed ([DOI 10.1177/1096620X261430298](https://doi.org/10.1177/1096620X261430298)), this randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial enrolled 80 adults with gingivitis and incipient periodontitis, randomized 1:1 to OraCMU (ProbioticCMU) tablets (n=40) or placebo (n=40) taken twice daily for 8 weeks. The primary outcome was the gingival index (GI); secondary outcomes included bleeding on probing (BOP), probing depth, clinical attachment level, gingival recession, plaque index, inflammation-related proteins, and oral microbiota, assessed at six preselected index teeth (#16, #12, #24, #32, #36, #44). At week 8 the probiotic group showed significantly greater reductions in GI and BOP than placebo and significant changes in host inflammatory markers (FGF-5, TSLP, RANKL/OPG ratio) and oral microbiota, with no serious adverse events.

**Note: this is an abstract-only ingest** — full text was not retrieved, so exact probiotic strain identity, the CFU dose exponent, the complete inflammatory-marker panel, specific microbial taxa, and the between-group results for probing depth / CAL / recession / plaque index are not recoverable.

**Clinically:** an 8-week course of the OraCMU oral probiotic, taken as a standalone daily supplement, produced small but statistically significant improvements in gingival inflammation (GI, BOP) over placebo in gingivitis/early periodontitis, with a clean short-term safety profile — supporting probiotics as a low-risk adjunct to (not a replacement for) mechanical plaque control and professional care.

## Key Contributions

- Standalone-supplement RCT evidence (no concurrent non-surgical therapy required by the design) that an oral probiotic improves gingival health in gingivitis/incipient periodontitis.
- Couples clinical periodontal outcomes with mechanistic readouts — host inflammatory proteins (FGF-5, TSLP, RANKL/OPG ratio) and oral microbiota composition.
- Demonstrates short-term (8-week) safety with no serious adverse events.

## Methodology

- **Design**: randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial (RCT).
- **Participants**: 80 adults with gingivitis and incipient periodontitis; OraCMU n=40 vs placebo n=40.
- **Intervention**: OraCMU tablets (2.0 × 10^? CFU/g — exponent not recoverable from abstract) twice daily for 8 weeks; placebo identical schedule.
- **Primary outcome**: gingival index (GI).
- **Secondary outcomes**: BOP, probing depth, clinical attachment level, gingival recession, plaque index, inflammation-related proteins, oral microbiota.
- **Assessment**: six index teeth (#16, #12, #24, #32, #36, #44); baseline and week 8.

## Results

- **GI (primary)**: −0.19 ± 0.03 (probiotic) vs −0.08 ± 0.04 (placebo); p = .035 — significantly greater reduction with OraCMU.
- **BOP**: −7.74 ± 1.54 vs −2.82 ± 1.60; p = .030 — significantly greater reduction with OraCMU.
- **Inflammatory markers significantly decreased** in the probiotic group: FGF-5 (p = .003), TSLP (p = .017), RANKL/OPG ratio (p = .021).
- **Other markers/taxa**: three significantly reduced (p = .001; p = .005; p = .046) and one significantly increased (p < .001) in the probiotic group — identities not recoverable from the abstract.
- **Safety**: no serious adverse events during the study period.
- **Conclusion**: 8-week OraCMU supplementation improved gingival health and modulated oral microbiota and inflammatory response, supporting it as a probiotic adjunct for managing gingivitis.
