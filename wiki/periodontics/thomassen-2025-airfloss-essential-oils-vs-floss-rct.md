## One-line Summary

Examiner-blind parallel RCT (n=82 healthy adults, ≥25% lower-jaw BOMP, no periodontitis; 3-wk experimental gingivitis then 4-wk reversal): AirFloss Ultra + essential oils (AFeo) vs waxed dental floss (DF), both adjunct to twice-daily fluoride toothbrushing — both significantly reduced BOMP, plaque (MPI) and gingival abrasion (GAS), but NO between-group difference at any time point (p>0.05); no serious adverse events. AFeo and waxed floss are clinically equivalent and both safe.

## Summary

This examiner-blind, parallel randomised controlled trial from ACTA (Amsterdam) asked whether a powered air-flosser charged with an essential-oil mouthwash (Philips Sonicare AirFloss Ultra + Listerine Cool Mint, **AFeo**) outperforms conventional **waxed dental floss (DF)** when each is added to twice-daily manual fluoride toothbrushing. Using a reversal-of-experimental-gingivitis model — 21 days of refraining from lower-jaw plaque control to re-establish gingival inflammation, followed by a 4-week healing phase — 82 systemically healthy young adults (mean age 22.9 y) with ≥25% gingival bleeding and no periodontitis were randomised 1:1.

The result is a **clean null between groups**: both AFeo and DF produced statistically significant within-group improvements in gingival bleeding, plaque, and abrasion, but the two interventions were **statistically indistinguishable at every time point** (BOMP, MPI, GAS, and patient appreciation all p>0.05). No serious adverse events occurred; the only minor events were six floss-related gingival irritations. The clinical message is that the choice between a powered air-flosser and floss is driven by **compliance, manual dexterity, and cost (value-based oral health care)** — not by efficacy. Because AFeo bundles a chemical (essential-oil) agent with mechanical action while DF is mechanical only, the trial cannot isolate the essential-oil contribution; the authors call for an AF-with-vs-without-essential-oils comparison.

## Key Contributions

- First head-to-head of **AirFloss Ultra + essential oils vs waxed floss** inside a reversal-of-experimental-gingivitis design.
- Establishes **clinical equivalence** of the two interdental adjuncts on bleeding, plaque, and abrasion — neither superior.
- Shifts the device-selection rationale to **patient compliance, dexterity, and cost-effectiveness**, since efficacy does not discriminate.
- Confirms **safety** of both modalities (no serious adverse events).

## Methodology

- **Design**: parallel, single-blind (examiner), randomly assigned; reversal-of-experimental-gingivitis model (Van der Weijden). CONSORT + TIDieR. ACTA, the Netherlands, 2015.
- **Participants**: 104 screened → 82 completed (47 F / 35 M; mean age 22.9 ± 3.3 y). Inclusion: systemically healthy, ≥25% BOMP lower jaw, DPSI 0–3⁻ ("gingivitis", 2017 EFP/AAP), floss fits ≥3 interdental spaces/quadrant (≥2 molar). Exclusion: overt caries, ortho appliances, removable dentures, piercings, oral lesions, pregnancy.
- **Arms** (n=41 each): **AFeo** (AirFloss Ultra + Listerine Cool Mint) vs **DF** (waxed floss, J&J Ultraclean). Both + twice-daily manual toothbrushing (Lactona iQ Soft) with fluoride toothpaste.
- **Protocol**: familiarisation → Day 0 prophylaxis + 21-day refrain from lower-jaw brushing (upper jaw brushed) → 4-week treatment with assessments at Weeks 1, 2, 4. Lower-jaw partial-mouth model (Putt).
- **Outcomes**: primary **BOMP** (6 sites/tooth, 0–2, WHO ball-ended probe); secondary **MPI** (Van der Weijden modification of Silness-Löe, 6 surfaces, 0–3), **GAS** (interdental/cervical/midgingival abrasions), and product appreciation.
- **Power**: 39/arm for an 80%-powered detection of a 0.16 BOMP difference (SD 0.25), α=0.05.
- **Blinding/concealment**: atmospheric-noise randomization, SNOSE envelopes, examiners blinded, instruction in a separate area. SAS analysis, significance p<0.05.

## Results

| Outcome | AFeo (n=41) Wk4 mean (SD) | DF (n=41) Wk4 mean (SD) | Between-group p (Wk4) | Within-group baseline→Wk4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **BOMP** | 0.95 (0.32) | 0.92 (0.32) | 0.72 (NS) | both p<0.01 (Δ AFeo 0.60, DF 0.70) |
| **MPI** (plaque) | 0.95 (0.45) | 1.08 (0.45) | 0.26 (NS) | both p<0.01 (Δ AFeo 1.04, DF 0.90) |
| **GAS** (abrasion) | 19.33 (7.42) | 19.75 (7.42) | 0.800 (NS) | both p<0.01 |

- **BOMP (primary)** — between-group NS at every visit (Day0 0.27, Wk1 0.37, Wk2 0.87, Wk4 0.72; Δ p=0.17). Both groups improved significantly from baseline.
- **MPI / GAS (secondary)** — both significant within-group, **NS between groups** at all time points.
- **Product appreciation** — AFeo 6.06 (SD 2.09) vs DF 5.62 (SD 2.21); t(78)=0.91, p=0.365 (NS).
- **Safety** — six study-related adverse events, all in the **dental-floss** arm (gum irritation, soreness, gingival lesions, pain, redness/swelling); **no serious adverse events** in either group.
- **Caveat** — AFeo's intervention combined mechanical + essential-oil antiseptic action vs DF's mechanical-only, so the essential-oil contribution cannot be isolated; participants could not be blinded to device (Hawthorne risk); cost of AFeo markedly higher than DF.
