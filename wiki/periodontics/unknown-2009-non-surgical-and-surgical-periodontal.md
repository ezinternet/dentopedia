## One-line Summary
Evidence-based literature review establishing the chronic periodontitis treatment hierarchy: non-surgical therapy (SRP + biofilm control) is primary with surgery only adjunctive, a mandatory 4–6 week re-evaluation precedes any surgical decision, and supportive periodontal therapy (SPT, recall every 3–6 months) is required indefinitely regardless of treatment type.

# Non-Surgical vs. Surgical Periodontal Therapy: Treatment Hierarchy

**Source**: [[periodontics/unknown-2009-non-surgical-and-surgical-periodontal]]  
**Year**: 2009 | **Review Type**: Evidence-based literature review | **Focus**: Chronic periodontitis treatment options

## Foundational Principle: Non-Surgical First

### Treatment Hierarchy
**Non-surgical therapy is primary treatment for chronic periodontitis**. Surgical intervention is **adjunctive**, indicated only when non-surgical therapy insufficient.

**Key Evidence**: Landmark studies (Axelsson & Lindhe, Badersten et al.) show similar long-term outcomes whether surgical intervention added or not in many cases.

## Non-Surgical Therapy: What Works

### Core Components
1. **Mechanical Debridement**: Scaling and root planing (SRP)
   - Hand instruments or ultrasonic
   - Subgingival calculus removal
   - Biofilm control
   - Root surface smoothing

2. **Biofilm Control**: Patient oral hygiene instruction
   - Manual toothbrushing technique
   - Interdental cleaning (floss, interdental brushes)
   - Antimicrobial rinses (adjunctive)
   - Motivation and compliance

3. **Subgingival Antimicrobial Therapy** (Adjunctive)
   - Chlorhexidine rinses
   - Minocycline or doxycycline subgingival delivery
   - Not required for initial success but may enhance results

### Non-Surgical Outcomes Achieved
- **Probing pocket depth reduction** (typically 1-3mm)
- **Clinical attachment level improvement** (bone support recovery)
- **Gingival inflammation elimination**
- **Bleeding on probing resolution**
- **Bacterial flora shift** toward health-compatible organisms

## Mandatory Re-evaluation Phase

### Critical Step Between Non-Surgical and Surgical
**Do not proceed to surgery without assessing non-surgical response**:

1. **Timing**: Complete SRP, then wait 4-6 weeks
2. **Assessment**: Probe entire mouth systematically
3. **Measure**:
   - Probing pocket depth reduction
   - Bleeding on probing
   - Clinical attachment level change
   - Areas of residual inflammation

4. **Decision Point**:
   - **Successful response** (pockets ≤3mm or significant depth reduction, no bleeding) → Supportive therapy
   - **Partial response** (some pockets 4-5mm, less bleeding) → Possible surgical adjunct
   - **Poor response** (deep pockets >5-6mm remain, continued bleeding) → Consider surgical intervention

**This re-evaluation visit is NOT optional.** Proceeding to surgery without re-evaluation increases morbidity risk.

## Surgical Therapy: Adjunctive Role

### Indications for Surgical Intervention
1. **Residual pockets >4mm after non-surgical therapy** (risk of continued progression)
2. **Need for periodontal regeneration** (bone fill of defects)
3. **Surgical access requirements** (deep furca, complex anatomy limiting SRP effectiveness)
4. **Esthetic concerns** (gingival reshaping, graft procedures)
5. **Severe anterior bone loss** with exposed roots

### Surgical Options
- **Access flaps**: Eliminate deep pockets by apical repositioning
- **Guided tissue regeneration (GTR)**: Membrane barriers to regenerate periodontal structures
- **Bone grafting**: Fill osseous defects
- **Osseous surgery**: Reshape and contour bone
- **Soft tissue grafts**: Root coverage procedures

### Surgical Outcomes
- Additional pocket depth reduction (1-2mm beyond non-surgical)
- Periodontal attachment regeneration (some cases)
- Root coverage (if included)
- Access for plaque control improvements
- May extend treatment timeline (healing delays)

## Patient Compliance: Make-or-Break Factor

### Why Non-Surgical Success Requires Compliance
Non-surgical therapy depends entirely on:
- **Patient's daily oral hygiene** (brushing, interdental cleaning)
- **Professional maintenance** (recall therapy every 3-6 months)
- **Sustained motivation** (disease is chronic, not cured)

**Patient non-compliance = therapy failure** → residual/recurrent disease → unnecessary surgery

### Patient Selection for Non-Surgical
- Assess motivation and realistic expectations
- Establish baseline oral hygiene ability
- Frame disease as chronic requiring lifetime management
- Clear communication about non-surgical success rates
- Consider supportive periodontal therapy frequency upfront

## Supportive Periodontal Therapy (SPT): Maintenance

### Essential for All Patients Post-Treatment
**Supportive therapy required indefinitely** whether treatment surgical or non-surgical:

**SPT Components**:
- **Professional plaque removal** (prophylaxis/debridement)
- **Frequency**: Every 3-6 months (individualized based on risk)
- **Monitoring**: Probing to detect early recurrence
- **Patient reinforcement**: Compliance encouragement
- **Local antimicrobials**: If selected sites problematic
- **Adjunctive therapy**: Antimicrobial prescriptions if needed

**SPT success** determines long-term prognosis more than initial therapy type.

## Treatment Decision Framework

**For newly diagnosed chronic periodontitis**:
```
Diagnosis: Chronic periodontitis
│
├─ Phase 1: Non-surgical therapy (ALWAYS first)
│  ├─ SRP (comprehensive)
│  ├─ Biofilm control instruction
│  └─ 4-6 week healing and patient compliance
│
├─ Phase 2: Re-evaluation
│  ├─ Assess response
│  ├─ Measure probing depths
│  ├─ Check compliance
│  └─ Decision point
│      ├─ Good response → SPT + recall
│      ├─ Partial response → Surgical adjunct considered
│      └─ Poor response → Surgical intervention + reassess factors
│
└─ Phase 3: Long-term supportive therapy
   └─ Every 3-6 months indefinitely
```
