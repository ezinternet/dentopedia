## Summary
This consensus report is the umbrella document of the 2017 World Workshop's workgroup 3, integrating four underlying reviews into an updated classification. It establishes that systemic diseases and conditions can alter the course of periodontitis or damage the attachment apparatus; that mucogingival conditions, especially gingival recession, are highly prevalent and clinically consequential (hypersensitivity, root caries, non-carious cervical lesions, esthetics); that traumatic occlusal forces can injure teeth and the attachment apparatus; and that developmental or acquired tooth- and prosthesis-related factors can predispose to periodontal disease. It supplies case definitions and diagnostic considerations, and is the structural framework into which the occlusal-trauma review (Fan 2018) and mucogingival/recession evidence fit.

## Key Contributions
- Updated 1999 classification for systemic-disease periodontal manifestations and developmental/acquired conditions.
- Integrates systemic disease, mucogingival, occlusal-force, and prosthesis/tooth-factor evidence into one framework.
- Provides case definitions and diagnostic considerations across these categories.

## Methodology
Consensus report built on four commissioned reviews (systemic diseases; mucogingival conditions; traumatic occlusal forces; prostheses/tooth-related factors), agreed by expert working group.

## Results
Systemic conditions modify periodontitis/attachment apparatus. Recessions are highly prevalent with downstream consequences. Occlusal forces can injure the attachment apparatus. Tooth/prosthesis factors can predispose to disease. Case definitions provided for each.

## Clinical Application
- Use this framework to locate where a patient's periodontal problem sits — disease vs developmental/acquired condition vs systemic manifestation — before treatment planning.
- The occlusal-force category here is the classificatory home of Fan 2018's case definitions.
