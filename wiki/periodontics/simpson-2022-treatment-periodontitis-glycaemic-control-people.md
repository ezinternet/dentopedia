## One-line Summary

Cochrane SR+MA (35 RCTs, 3249 randomised participants, mostly type 2 diabetes): periodontal treatment by subgingival instrumentation produced a moderate-certainty absolute HbA1c reduction of 0.43% (4.7 mmol/mol) at 3-4 months vs no treatment/usual care, sustained at 6 and 12 months.

## Summary

This Cochrane systematic review and meta-analysis (2022 update, CD004714.pub4) evaluates whether treating periodontitis improves glycaemic control in people with diabetes, compared with no active treatment or usual care. It pools 35 parallel-group RCTs (3249 randomised participants, predominantly type 2 diabetes) with 3–12 month follow-up. The intervention was periodontal treatment by subgingival instrumentation (scaling and root planing), sometimes with oral hygiene instruction or antimicrobial adjuncts; the comparator was no active treatment or usual care. The 2022 update doubled prior study and participant counts, raising the conclusion to **moderate-certainty evidence** of a **clinically significant** improvement in glycaemic control. Authors conclude further no-treatment/usual-care comparison trials are unlikely to change this finding; a planned Part two will compare different periodontal treatments.

## Key Contributions

- Highest-tier (Cochrane SR+MA) evidence that periodontal treatment improves glycaemic control in diabetic patients.
- 2022 update upgraded certainty to moderate and characterised the effect as clinically significant.
- Establishes that additional RCTs vs no treatment/usual care are unlikely to alter the conclusion.

## Methodology

- Systematic review + meta-analysis of parallel-group RCTs only; ≥2 authors independently screened, extracted, and assessed Cochrane risk of bias.
- Population: diabetes (mostly type 2) + periodontitis; mixed baseline metabolic control; mostly secondary care.
- Intervention: subgingival instrumentation (SRP) ± adjuncts. Comparator: no active treatment / usual care.
- Primary outcome: HbA1c (% or mmol/mol). Secondary: adverse effects, periodontal indices (BOP, CAL, gingival index, plaque index, PPD), quality of life, cost, diabetic complications.
- Inclusion threshold: RCTs ≥3 months measuring HbA1c. Search current to 7 September 2021.
- Sensitivity analysis restricted to low-risk-of-bias studies supported the primary result.

## Results

- 35 studies, 3249 participants; follow-up 3–12 months.
- **HbA1c reduction vs no treatment/usual care:**
  - 3–4 months: −0.43% (4.7 mmol/mol); 95% CI −0.59% to −0.28%; 30 studies, 2443 participants; **moderate-certainty**.
  - 6 months: −0.30% (3.3 mmol/mol); 95% CI −0.52% to −0.08%; 12 studies, 1457 participants.
  - 12 months: −0.50% (5.4 mmol/mol); 95% CI −0.55% to −0.45%; 1 study, 264 participants.
- Risk of bias: 2 low, 14 high, 19 unclear.
- Adverse effects: where reported, no/mild harms and similar serious-event rates across arms; harms under-evaluated in most studies.
