## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 9 RCTs: scaling and root planing in type 2 diabetics with periodontitis significantly reduced HbA1c (mean difference 0.56%, 0.36-0.75) and CRP (DM 1.89, 1.70-2.08), both p<0.01 with I²=0% heterogeneity.

## Summary

This PRISMA-compliant systematic review and meta-analysis evaluated whether conventional non-surgical periodontal treatment (scaling and root planing, SRP) improves metabolic control and reduces systemic inflammation in patients with periodontitis and type 2 diabetes (T2D). Earlier reviews had largely restricted themselves to HbA1c as the sole endpoint; this review adds C-reactive protein (CRP) as a co-primary outcome to capture the systemic-inflammation pathway linking periodontitis to cardiovascular risk in diabetic patients. Searching MEDLINE/PubMed and Cochrane CENTRAL through July 2018 and including only RCTs, the authors pooled 9 trials and found that SRP significantly lowered both HbA1c and CRP, with no detectable heterogeneity, supporting periodontal therapy as an adjunct relevant to both glycemic and cardiovascular-inflammatory outcomes in T2D.

## Key Contributions

- Pools RCT evidence that SRP improves glycemic control (HbA1c) in type 2 diabetics with periodontitis: DM = 0.56% (95% CI 0.36–0.75), p<0.01.
- Extends the outcome set beyond HbA1c to systemic inflammation: SRP reduces CRP, DM = 1.89 (95% CI 1.70–2.08), p<0.01.
- Frames periodontal treatment as a potential lever for cardiovascular risk reduction in diabetics via CRP, an independent predictor of cardiovascular disease.
- Reports zero between-study heterogeneity (I²=0%) for both pooled effects.

## Methodology

- Design: systematic review + random-effects meta-analysis, Cochrane standards, PRISMA reporting.
- Databases: MEDLINE via PubMed and Cochrane CENTRAL, oldest records to 10 July 2018 (English-language records).
- Eligibility: RCTs only; participants with T2D + periodontitis; ≥3 months follow-up; HbA1c and CRP measured at baseline and end.
- Intervention vs. comparator: oral hygiene instruction + SRP (with or without flap surgery) vs. no periodontal treatment.
- Risk of bias: Cochrane Collaboration tool; two independent reviewers (Cohen's kappa threshold 0.61); consensus for disagreements.
- Effect measure: weighted difference in means (DM) between groups; heterogeneity by Q-statistic (χ², I²); significance p<0.05.

## Results

- 9 RCTs met inclusion criteria.
- HbA1c: significant reduction, DM = 0.56 (0.36–0.75), p<0.01.
- CRP: significant reduction, DM = 1.89 (1.70–2.08), p<0.01.
- Heterogeneity: I² = 0% (p>0.05) — no heterogeneity detected.
- Conclusion: SRP has a positive impact on metabolic control and reduces systemic inflammation in patients with periodontitis and T2D.
