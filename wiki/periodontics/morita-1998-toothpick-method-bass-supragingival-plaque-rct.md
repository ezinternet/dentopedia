RCT (n=20 dental students, split-mouth, modified Quigley-Hein index, 6 sites/tooth): the Watanabe Toothpick toothbrushing method removed significantly more proximal-surface plaque than the Bass method.

## Summary

This 1998 split-mouth randomized clinical trial is the **cornerstone primary study of the Watanabe Toothpick toothbrushing method** — coauthor T. Watanabe is the method's eponym. It is the single direct head-to-head comparison establishing that the Toothpick method outperforms the long-standard **Bass method** for supragingival plaque removal, with the advantage concentrated at the **proximal (interproximal) tooth surfaces**. The trial supplies the empirical basis later cited by the Rajwani 2020 systematic review of manual toothbrushing techniques.

## Key Contributions

- First direct RCT comparing the **Toothpick method** vs the **Bass method** for plaque removal.
- Identifies the **proximal surfaces** as where the Toothpick method's benefit lies — the sites conventional brushing methods clean least effectively.
- Provides the primary-trial evidence underpinning subsequent reviews and clinical applications of the Watanabe method.

## Methodology

- **Subjects**: 20 male university dental students.
- **Pre-experiment washout**: 48 h abstention from all oral hygiene before each experiment.
- **Two experiments**:
  - **Exp. I — professional brushing**: a dentist brushed each mouth, **2 quadrants with the Toothpick method and 2 quadrants with the Bass method** (split-mouth allocation); **total 2 min brushing per mouth**.
  - **Exp. II — self-brushing**: subjects brushed their own teeth after receiving toothbrushing instruction.
- **Outcome / scoring**: a **blind examiner** assessed plaque using a **modification of the Quigley and Hein plaque index** at **6 sites around each tooth**.

## Results

- The Toothpick method removed **significantly more plaque from proximal tooth surfaces** than the Bass method.
