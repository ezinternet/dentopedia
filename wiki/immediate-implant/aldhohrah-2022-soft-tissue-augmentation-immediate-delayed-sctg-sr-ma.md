## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 12 studies showing subepithelial connective tissue grafting (SCTG) at immediate or delayed implant placement significantly improves buccal tissue thickness (MD 0.74 mm), mid-buccal gingival level (MD 0.5 mm), pink esthetic score (MD 0.79), and reduces marginal bone loss (MD −0.11 mm) versus other treatment modalities.

## Summary

This meta-analysis (12 studies) tested whether subepithelial connective tissue grafting (SCTG) at immediate or delayed implant placement outperforms other modalities — no augmentation (± GBR), acellular dermal matrix, or xenogeneic collagen matrix. SCTG significantly improved buccal tissue thickness, mid-buccal gingival level, and pink esthetic score, and reduced marginal bone loss across pooled comparisons. The results provide quantitative, statistically significant support for SCTG as the soft-tissue augmentation of choice around implants when esthetics and peri-implant tissue stability are priorities.

## Key Contributions

- Pools SCTG against a broad comparator set at both immediate and delayed placement.
- Reports significant SCTG benefit on four esthetic/biologic outcomes simultaneously.

## Methodology

- SR+MA of RCTs (>3-mo follow-up); SCTG vs no augmentation (±GBR)/ADM/XCM; cumulative MD (95% CI).

## Results

- BTT MD 0.74 mm (0.51–0.97); MGL MD 0.5 mm (0.21–0.80); PES MD 0.79 (0.29–1.29); MBL MD −0.11 mm (−0.14 to −0.08).
