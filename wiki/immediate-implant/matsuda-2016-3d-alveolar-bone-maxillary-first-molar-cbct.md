## One-line Summary

Retrospective CBCT cross-sectional study (95 patients, 150 healthy maxillary first molars): mean buccal/palatal plate 1.58/1.34 mm, sinus floor-to-furcation 6.51 mm, palatal root apex intrudes a mean −2.2 mm into the sinus (deeper than MB/DB roots); ~32% of MFMs judged contraindicated for immediate implant placement on residual-bone grounds.

## Summary

This CBCT morphometric study maps the 3-dimensional alveolar bone anatomy of 150 healthy maxillary first molars (MFMs) to inform immediate implant placement (IIP) and endodontic risk management. The central anatomical finding is that the **palatal root apex intrudes deepest into the maxillary sinus** (mean −2.2 mm, where negative = inside the sinus), significantly deeper than the mesio-buccal (−0.36 mm) and disto-buccal (+0.32 mm) apices, while MB vs DB do not differ. The mean sinus floor (SF)-to-furcation distance is 6.51 mm (range 0–16). The buccal plate (1.58 mm) is thicker and more often favorable than the palatal plate (1.34 mm; 25% of palatal sites <1 mm vs 8% buccal). The wide socket (mesio-distal 7.3 mm, bucco-palatal 10.5 mm) makes primary stability inherently hard, so anchorage must come from the inter-radicular septum and apical/residual bone. The authors translate these dimensions into a 3-tier favorable/intermediate/contraindicated framework, classifying ~32% of MFMs as contraindicated for IIP without staging.

## Key Contributions

- **Palatal root is the deepest sinus intruder** — the per-root vertical relations rank the palatal root apex (−2.2 mm) below MB and DB apices, anatomically grounding why the palatal root socket (deep, dense, central) is the preferred anchorage target for MFM IIP.
- **Buccal vs palatal plate asymmetry** — buccal plate is thicker (1.58 mm, 92% ≥1 mm) and a better gap-healing prognosis; the palatal plate is thinner and more often <1 mm.
- **Socket dimensions** quantified at crest (7.3 × 10.5 mm), explaining the primary-stability challenge of molar IIP.
- **3-tier decision framework** keyed to SF-to-apex, SF-to-furcation, and inter-root distance, with explicit numeric thresholds and a ~32% contraindication rate.

## Methodology

Retrospective single-center CBCT review (Picasso Duo) of 150 MFMs from 95 patients (mean age 37.2 y), restricted to anatomically healthy molars (no sinus/periodontal/periapical pathology, no prior augmentation, no metal-artifact teeth). One examiner reoriented each volume and measured on three axial slices — Mid (mid-tooth), MB-P (mesio-buccal-to-palatal), DB-P (disto-buccal-to-palatal). Captured: buccal/palatal wall width at 2 mm from crest; SF→furcation; SF→apex for each of the three roots; MB→palatal and DB→palatal inter-root apex distances; mesio-distal and bucco-palatal socket size at crest. Descriptive stats with 95% CIs in SPSS 22; negative SF-to-apex values indicate sinus intrusion.

## Results

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Buccal plate (2 mm from crest) | 1.58 (0.6) mm |
| Palatal plate (2 mm from crest) | 1.34 (0.54) mm |
| SF → furcation | 6.51 (2.94) mm, range 0–16 |
| SF → MB apex | −0.36 mm (95% CI −0.91 to 0.19) |
| SF → DB apex | 0.32 mm (95% CI −0.27 to 0.9) |
| SF → palatal apex | −2.2 mm (95% CI −2.7 to −1.7) |
| Mesio-distal socket (crest) | 7.3 (0.84) mm |
| Bucco-palatal socket (crest) | 10.5 (0.90) mm |

- Significant SF-to-apex differences: MB vs palatal and DB vs palatal (palatal deeper); MB vs DB not significant.
- Thin plate: 25% palatal vs 8% buccal sites <1 mm.
- 46% of MFMs had >5 mm engaging apical bone (favorable).
- Cited background: sinus intrusion increases posteriorly (first premolar 1.5% → second molar 44.7%).

**Decision framework for MFM immediate placement:**

- **Favorable** — SF→apex >5 mm OR SF→furcation >5 mm, AND inter-root >5 mm → adequate primary stability; place in socket center, graft the jumping gap.
- **Intermediate** — SF→apex 2–4 mm OR SF→furcation 2–4 mm with inter-root ≥5 mm → width adequate, height marginal; technically demanding, may need simultaneous sinus augmentation (perforation risk).
- **Contraindicated (~32%)** — SF→apex <2 mm and inter-root <5 mm, OR SF→furcation <2 mm → stage the case.

Stated prerequisites: ~5 mm residual apical bone height and, for a 4 mm implant, ~5 mm inter-radicular crestal width. Healthy-molar-only sampling and clinical-experience-derived thresholds are the main limitations.
