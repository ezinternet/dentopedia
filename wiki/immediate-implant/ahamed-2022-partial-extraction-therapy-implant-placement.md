## Summary

This narrative review consolidates partial extraction therapy (PET) — the socket shield technique, pontic shield, and root submergence — as approaches that retain root structure to preserve buccal bone and peri-implant soft tissue. The authors argue PET yields excellent emergence profiles and esthetics without papilla loss or arch collapse, while reducing the need for bone and mucogingival grafting and shortening treatment time. They emphasize that no published study clearly standardizes shield design with a proven track record, positioning the article as a practitioner-oriented primer rather than outcome evidence.

## Key Contributions

- Practitioner-oriented synthesis of the PET family and shield-design scenarios.
- Highlights the absence of standardized, evidence-based shield protocols.

## Methodology

- Narrative review (no quantitative synthesis).

## Results

- Retained root preserves buccal bone, improving emergence profile and esthetics.
- PET reduces grafting and treatment time; pontic shield supports ovate-pontic sites.
