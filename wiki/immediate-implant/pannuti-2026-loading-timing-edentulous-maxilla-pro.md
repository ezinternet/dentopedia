## One-line Summary

Systematic review of 5 prospective studies (4 RCT + 1 non-RCT; 121 patients, 724 implants) on immediate vs early/delayed loading in the edentulous maxilla: VAS dominated patient-reported outcome measurement (80% of studies, 7 PROs), supported by OHIP-19/20 and the McGill Pain Questionnaire, while the 12 identified clinician-reported outcomes had no standardized approach — concluding PROM/CROM reporting must be standardized.

## Summary

This systematic review (PROSPERO CRD42024519360, PRISMA 2020) asked not whether immediate (IL), early (EL), or delayed (DL) loading produces better outcomes in the edentulous maxilla, but *which* patient-reported outcomes (PROs) and clinician-reported outcomes (ClinROs), and *which* measurement instruments (PROMs/CROMs), have actually been used. Of 2232 records, only 5 prospective comparative studies (4 RCTs, 1 non-RCT; 121 patients, 724 implants; all European university settings) qualified — four comparing IL vs DL and one IL vs EL. The visual analog scale (VAS) was the dominant PROM (80% of studies), capturing 7 PROs (pain, overall satisfaction, comfort, speech, masticatory function, esthetics, self-esteem), with OHIP-19/20 and the McGill Pain Questionnaire adding validated quality-of-life and pain depth. On the clinician side, 12 distinct ClinROs (implant stability/ISQ, insertion torque, occlusal force, EMG, bone loss, periodontal indices, placement accuracy, surgical time, esthetic VAS, complications) were reported with no inter-study consensus and almost no assessor calibration. The clinical bottom line is methodological: outcome reporting in maxillary loading-timing research is heterogeneous and not standardized, so cross-study comparison of IL vs EL vs DL on patient-centered grounds is currently unreliable.

## Key Contributions

- Reframes the maxillary loading-timing literature around *measurement* rather than effect size, exposing that no PROM is validated to detect timing-specific differences.
- Quantifies the patient-side instrument landscape: VAS in 80% of studies covering 7 PROs; OHIP-19/20 and McGill Pain Questionnaire as the validated minority.
- Catalogs 12 clinician-reported outcomes used with no standardized approach across just 4 studies — the core heterogeneity finding.
- Surfaces a calibration gap: only 1 of 5 studies reported assessor reliability testing, undermining subjective CROM comparability.
- Links the gap to ID-COSM (satisfaction/comfort mandatory) and calls for timing-sensitive PROM development.

## Methodology

Systematic review of prospective interventional/observational studies (RCT, non-RCT, cohort, case series ≥10) directly comparing ≥2 loading protocols (IL ≤1 wk, EL 1 wk–2 mo, DL >2 mo) in the completely edentulous maxilla receiving full-arch fixed/removable implant-supported prostheses. Retrospective, cross-sectional, case-report, in vitro, preclinical, single-arm, and pterygoid/zygomatic-implant studies were excluded. Search across MEDLINE, Embase, Scopus, Web of Science, Cochrane CENTRAL (Jan 2014–May 2024, English). Two reviewers screened via Rayyan (Cohen's kappa 0.81). Risk of bias by Cochrane RoB 2 (RCTs) and ROBINS-I (non-RCT). Synthesis was descriptive only (frequencies/percentages, STATA 16.1); no meta-analysis was feasible given heterogeneity.

## Results

- Selection: 2232 → 777 duplicates removed → 1455 screened → 68 full-text → 5 included (4 IL-vs-DL, 1 IL-vs-EL); 121 patients, 724 implants; 6–8 implants/patient; 80% externally funded; follow-up 7 days to 2 years.
- PROMs: VAS in 4/5 studies (80%) assessing 7 PROs (pain, satisfaction, comfort, speech, mastication, esthetics, self-esteem). OHIP-19 (Markovic) / OHIP-20 (Montero) for oral health-related QoL; McGill Pain Questionnaire + HRQOL-15 (Vercruyssen); condition-specific chewing/masticatory performance (Montero). OHIP-19/20 favored over OHIP-14 for edentulous populations.
- ClinROs: 12 across 4 studies, no consensus — implant stability (RFA Osstell/Penguin, ISQ 1–100), insertion torque, clinician esthetic-satisfaction VAS, surgical/prosthetic complications, occlusal force (Dental PRESCALE), EMG (MYOMED_932TM), bone loss, probing depth, BoP, plaque index, placement accuracy (CT/CBCT matching), surgical time.
- Risk of bias: 4 RCTs overall "some concerns" (low across most domains); 1 non-RCT moderate. Only Markovic reported assessor reliability; calibration otherwise not reported.
- Conclusion: no PROM/CROM standardization; VAS+OHIP dominate PROs; ClinROs fragmented — standardization and timing-sensitive PROM validation are needed.
