## One-line Summary
RCT (n=30, 1-year, UIC) comparing inverted body-shift (Inverta co-axis 12°) vs conventional conical implants in immediate anterior maxillary placement: 100% survival, ISQ medians 56–62, comparable marginal bone loss and PES esthetics, no statistically significant intergroup differences.

## 한줄요약
전향적 단일맹검 RCT (n=30, 1년, UIC): 역전형 body-shift (co-axis 12°) vs 통상 conical 임플란트를 전상악 즉시식립에서 비교 — 생존율 100%, ISQ 중앙값 56–62, 수평·수직 골흡수 및 PES 미학적 결과 모두 군간 유의차 없음; inverted 디자인은 중앙 소켓 위치·팔라탈 스크류 접근 실용적 이점 제공.

## Summary
Single-blind RCT from Universitat Internacional de Catalunya enrolling 30 patients for immediate anterior maxillary implant placement (central/lateral incisors, canines, premolars) with immediate provisionalization, comparing the Inverta co-axis 12° inverted body-shift implant (test, n=15) against a conventional internal conical implant (control, n=15).

## Key Contributions
Provides RCT-level evidence for the novel inverted body-shift implant design (pilot in Galve-Huertas 2024) versus standard implants in IIP.

## Methodology
Single-blind RCT. Anterior IIP patients randomized to test (inverted body-shift, n=15) vs control (conical, n=15) with immediate provisionalization. 1-year clinical, CBCT-volumetric, and PES esthetic outcomes.

## Results
At 1 year, survival was 100% in both groups. Overall success (Misch, <2 mm MBL) was 83.3% (control 93.3% vs test 73.3%, p=0.327, NS). Primary stability (IT, ISQ), CBCT horizontal/vertical marginal bone loss, and PES were statistically equivalent. The inverted body-shift design reached marginally higher median ISQ (62 vs 56–60) and IT (42 vs 35 Ncm) without significance; buccal resorption was slightly greater and palatal bone preservation better in the test group. Median PES 11–12 in both arms.

## Limitations
1-year follow-up; industry-aligned implant; single-site team.
