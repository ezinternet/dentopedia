## Summary

This short narrative review evaluates socket shielding (retention of the buccal root fragment, a.k.a. the root membrane technique) for the esthetic zone. It frames the buccal-dominant pattern of post-extraction ridge resorption and argues that retaining the buccal root segment preserves the bundle bone, minimizing buccal resorption and contour change with a minimally invasive, graft-optional protocol. The author cautions that evidence remains limited and that large-population studies are needed before routine adoption.

## Key Contributions

- Concise rationale and appraisal of socket shielding as an esthetic-zone preservation option.
- Restates buccal-plate resorption physiology motivating root-fragment retention.

## Methodology

- Narrative review (PubMed, Google Scholar, Cochrane).

## Results

- Buccal-dominant ridge resorption after extraction; socket shielding minimizes buccal bone/contour loss; graft optional.
- Limited evidence base; larger studies required.
