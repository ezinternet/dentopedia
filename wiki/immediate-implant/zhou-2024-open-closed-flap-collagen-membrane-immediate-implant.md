## One-line Summary

Two retrospective cohorts (118 patients; 58 complete closure vs 60 open healing) of immediate implant + simultaneous GBR with Bio-Gide: open healing of the exposed collagen membrane gave bone and soft-tissue outcomes equivalent to flap-released complete closure when initial crestal wound width was ≤6 mm, with less postoperative pain and swelling.

## Summary

This study compares two wound-management strategies for collagen-membrane coverage at **immediate implant placement with simultaneous GBR**, using two retrospective cohorts treated sequentially at one Shanghai center (1999–2021). In the **complete closure (CC)** group (n=58, pre-2020 protocol) gingival flaps were released to fully cover the Bio-Gide membrane; in the **open healing (OH)** group (n=60, since Jan 2020) the membrane was deliberately left exposed without flap release. Outcomes were crestal wound width (W0, W1, W2, W16), CBCT bone change (placement → W16), and W16 histology in 16 OH patients.

The cohorts were balanced at baseline (age, gender, implant site, brand; P > 0.05). The CC group showed no dehiscence. Critically, **vertical and horizontal bone changes did not differ between OH and CC**. In the OH group the open wound closed progressively (5.8 mm at W0 → 0.1 mm at W16) and soft tissue fully healed by W16 when the initial wound was **≤6 mm**; for initial widths **≥7 mm**, 5/16 patients had cover-screw exposure at W16 but final restorations were unaffected. Histology showed keratinized mucosa and new bone over the implant. The authors conclude that for immediate-implant GBR requiring membrane coverage, **releasing flaps or using tissue grafts is unnecessary** when the crestal exposure is ≤6 mm, sparing the patient the extra surgery, pain, and swelling.

## Key Contributions

- First evaluation of the exposed-membrane **open healing** technique specifically for immediate implant + simultaneous GBR.
- Defines a **≤6 mm initial crestal wound width** threshold below which open healing matches complete closure.
- Demonstrates that the morbidity of flap-release / CTG / rotated palatal flap closure is often avoidable.

## Methodology

- Retrospective two-cohort comparison, single center.
- 118 patients (20 anterior, 98 posterior teeth); CC n=58, OH n=60.
- All received Bio-Gide membrane, amoxicillin + analgesics 7 days.
- Crestal wound width at W0/W1/W2/W16; CBCT bone change at W16; staining histology in 16 OH patients.

## Results

- CC group: no dehiscence W0→W16.
- Vertical/horizontal bone change: not significantly different between OH and CC.
- OH wound width: 5.8 (±1.3) → 4.7 (±1.3) → 3.3 (±1.2) → 0.1 (±0.4) mm at W0/W1/W2/W16.
- OH soft tissue fully healed at W16 if initial width ≤6 mm; cover-screw exposure in 5/16 when ≥7 mm (final restorations unaffected).
- Histology: keratinized mucosa + new bone above the implant in OH.
