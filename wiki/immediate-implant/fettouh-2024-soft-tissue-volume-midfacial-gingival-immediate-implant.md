## One-line Summary

1-year RCT (39 maxillary esthetic-zone sites, thin labial bone) comparing immediate implant placement with bone graft vs CTG vs customized healing abutment (CHA) alone; CTG produced the least midfacial gingival recession (−0.74 mm) and reduced volumetric soft-tissue loss ~5-fold, while CHA alone failed to maintain contour (−1.54 mm; bone graft −0.98 mm).

## 한줄요약

1년 RCT(심미부 39부위, 얇은 협측골) — 즉시식립 시 결합조직이식(CTG)군이 중안면 치은퇴축이 가장 적고(−0.74 mm) 부피 손실을 약 5배 줄인 반면, 맞춤형 치유지대주(CHA) 단독군은 윤곽 유지에 실패했다(−1.54 mm; 골이식재군 −0.98 mm).

## Summary

This 3-arm RCT (39 thin-labial-bone maxillary esthetic sites) compared immediate implant placement combined with a xenograft, a connective tissue graft (CTG), or a customized healing abutment (CHA) alone. At 1 year, CTG sites showed the smallest midfacial gingival margin change and a ~5-fold reduction in volumetric soft-tissue loss versus the other arms. CHA alone produced the greatest recession, indicating that a socket-sealing abutment without added soft tissue is insufficient to maintain peri-implant contour in thin-phenotype esthetic sites.

## Key Contributions

- Direct RCT comparison of CTG vs bone graft vs CHA-alone at immediate-implant esthetic sites with thin labial bone.
- Combines clinical, volumetric (mm³) and CBCT outcomes.
- Shows CTG decouples soft-tissue volume loss from midfacial recession (no correlation in CTG arm).

## Methodology

- 39 non-restorable maxillary esthetic teeth randomized 1:1:1 (n≈13/arm); flapless immediate placement; all with a customized healing abutment.
- Primary outcome: midfacial gingival margin change (mm) at 1 year; secondary: contour change, interdental height, total volume (mm³), labial bone, crestal bone (CBCT).

## Results

- Midfacial margin change: −0.74 (CTG) vs −0.98 (bone graft) vs −1.54 mm (CHA); P ≤ 0.05.
- CTG reduced total volume loss ~5-fold.
- Volume-loss ↔ margin-change correlation significant in bone-graft and CHA arms; absent in CTG (P = 0.63).
- CBCT: significant crestal bone difference; no difference in labial bone amount.
