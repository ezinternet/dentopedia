## One-line Summary

CBCT virtual study (179 patients, 716 maxillary premolars, two virtual implant orientations): immediate-implant cortical perforation occurred in 84.1% of prosthetically-driven placements vs 40.5% of bone-driven placements, with mean buccal tilt (ILAA) of 18.3° needed to escape perforation; female sex, older age, shorter tooth-apex-to-nasal/sinus distance, and smaller labial concavity angle raised risk.

## 한줄요약

CBCT 가상 연구(환자 179명, 상악 소구치 716개, 가상 식립 방향 2종): 즉시식립 시 보철주도(prosthetically-driven) 위치는 84.1%, 골주도(bone-driven) 위치는 40.5%에서 순측/비강/상악동 천공이 발생했고, 천공을 피하려면 평균 18.3°의 순측 경사(ILAA)가 필요했다. 여성·고령·치근첨-구조물 거리 짧음·작은 순측 함몰각(LCA)이 위험을 높였다.

## Summary

This is the first CBCT virtual study to quantify the **risk of cortical bone perforation during immediate implant placement (IIP, 즉시식립) in the maxillary premolar region**. Using 179 retrospective CBCT exams (716 premolar teeth: bilateral first and second maxillary premolars), the authors virtually placed a 3.75-mm parallel implant in each socket in two orientations and compared perforation of the labial plate, nasal floor, or maxillary sinus floor.

The two orientations capture the core clinical tension of premolar IIP:

- **Prosthetically-driven** (보철주도): implant along the tooth long axis (line A) for ideal crown emergence — perforated cortex in **84.1%** of teeth (only 2.2% were clean; another 13.7% encroached the 2-mm safety margin).
- **Bone-driven** (골주도): the shortest non-perforating orientation keeping ≥4 mm apical native-bone anchorage and 2-mm margins — still perforated in **40.5%**.

The bottom line: the prosthetically ideal axis is almost never anatomically achievable in maxillary premolars without buccal tilt, and even the optimized bone-driven axis perforates in roughly 4 of 10 cases — far higher than the ~5.6% previously reported for the anterior maxilla. The mean buccal tilt required (**ILAA 18.3°**) implies most premolar IIP single crowns would need a custom angled or angled-screw-channel abutment.

## Key Contributions

- First quantification of perforation risk for IIP specifically in the **maxillary premolar** region (vs prior anterior-maxilla data).
- Head-to-head, same-socket comparison of **prosthetically-driven vs bone-driven** virtual placement: 84.1% vs 40.5% perforation (p<0.001).
- Two CBCT planning metrics: **ILAA** (buccal tilt to escape perforation, mean 18.3°, larger for 2PM) and **labial concavity angle (LCA)** (mean ~156°).
- Multivariate risk model: female sex, older age, shorter tooth-apex distance, smaller LCA all independently raise perforation/safety-margin-invasion risk.

## Methodology

- **Design**: retrospective CBCT virtual planning study (cross-sectional), single center, Belo Horizonte, Brazil.
- **Sample**: 574 maxillary CBCT exams screened → 179 included (716 premolars; 83 male/96 female; ages 14.2–78.1 y). i-CAT CBCT, 120 kV, 136 µSv, 1.0-mm slices.
- **Implant simulation**: parallel 3.75-mm implant, 1 mm subcrestal, ≥4 mm apical native-bone anchorage; 2-mm safety margin from adjacent teeth, nasal/sinus floor, labial/palatal plates.
- **Two placements**: prosthetically-driven (line A) and bone-driven (shortest non-perforating). ILAA = angle between them; LCA = buccal-plate concavity angle.
- **Statistics**: Mann-Whitney, Pearson χ², correlations, univariate + multivariate binary logistic regression (OR, 95% CI); SPSS v28; powered to 80% (α=5%) for a 4-fold perforation difference. ImageJ angle measurement; 3-author calibration on 10 exams.

## Results

- **Perforation**: prosthetically-driven 84.1% (clean 2.2%, within-2-mm 13.7%); bone-driven 40.5% (clean 22.9%, within-2-mm 36.6%); p<0.001.
- **ILAA**: 18.3°±8.0° (0–40.1°); larger for second premolar than first (p<0.001) — 15: 20.6°, 14: 17.5°, 24: 16.7°, 25: 20.3°.
- **Minimum implant length (bone-driven)**: 13.0±2.1 mm globally (range 6.5–19.0 mm); 1PM marginally longer than 2PM (NS, p=0.127).
- **LCA**: 156.5±10.3° globally; larger in males (158.2°) than females (155.3°), p<0.001.
- **Multivariate risk factors (bone-driven perforation/invasion)**:
  - Female sex: OR 2.358 (1.492–3.725), p<0.001
  - Age: OR 1.034 per year (1.018–1.049), p<0.001
  - Tooth-apex distance: OR 0.697 per mm (0.650–0.747), p<0.001 (more bone apically = safer)
  - LCA: OR 0.964 per degree (0.938–0.990), p=0.008 (larger LCA = shallower concavity = safer)
  - Tooth region: NS in multivariate (OR 0.925, p=0.750), though 2PM was significant univariately (OR 2.031).
- **Clinical takeaway**: premolar IIP almost always requires buccal tilt (~18°), favoring custom angled / angled-screw-channel abutments; preoperative CBCT is essential because 2D radiographs cannot reveal this perforation risk.
