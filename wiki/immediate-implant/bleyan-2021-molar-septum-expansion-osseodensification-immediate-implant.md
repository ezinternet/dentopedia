## 한줄요약
다기관 후향 코호트 (retrospective), 5개 센터, 131 환자/145 즉시 대구치 임플란트, 추적 최대 5년 — 골밀도화 (Osseodensification, OD)로 평균 치근간 격벽폭 3.3 mm → 골 절삭 직경 4.65 mm로 확장, 누적 임플란트 생존율 93.1% (10개 실패: 치유기 7, 부하 후 3). 비교군 없는 후향 디자인, 5년 시점 생존 곡선 분리 보고 없음.

## Summary
Bleyan and Gaspar et al. retrospectively pooled cases from five centers where the osseodensification technique was used to place immediate implants in molar extraction sockets. The interradicular septum was the target for osteotomy (the only sound bone column typically available in a fresh molar socket). Across 131 patients and 145 implants between August 2015 and September 2020 with up to five years of follow-up, septum geometry change and implant survival were the primary outcomes.

Mean baseline septum width was 3.3 mm; mean post-instrumentation osteotomy diameter was 4.65 mm — i.e. the septum was plastically expanded by roughly 1.35 mm on average. Cumulative implant survival was 93.1% with 10 total failures: 7 during the healing period and 3 after loading. The authors propose a new molar socket classification system to organize OD-based case selection (specific classes are defined in the body of the paper, not extractable from front/back text). They position OD as superior to conventional drilling in this setting because conventional drills tend to skid off a narrow septum into a residual root socket; the bone-preserving compaction action of Densah burs allows a stable osteotomy to be created within the septum.

## Key Contributions
- Largest multicenter OD-for-immediate-molar-implant dataset (n = 145 implants).
- Quantifies septum expansion: 3.3 mm → 4.65 mm mean osteotomy diameter.
- Cumulative implant survival 93.1% over up-to-5-year follow-up.
- 10 failures (7 healing-phase, 3 post-loading).
- Introduces an OD-aware molar socket classification system.
- Reinforces clinical rationale for OD in molar IIP: bone-preserving compaction avoids septum perforation.

## Methodology
Retrospective multicenter chart review across five centers. Inclusion: molar extraction + immediate implant placement using OD. Primary outcome: pre-instrumentation septum width (mm) and post-instrumentation osteotomy diameter (mm). Secondary outcomes: insertion torque, ISQ, implant survival. Follow-up window August 2015 – September 2020 (up to 5 years). No comparator group, no blinding, no randomization. Survival reported as cumulative rate.

## Results
- Patients: 131; implants placed: 145.
- Mean baseline septum width: 3.3 mm.
- Mean post-instrumentation osteotomy diameter: 4.65 mm (≈ 1.35 mm net expansion).
- Cumulative implant survival: 93.1%.
- Failures: 10 total — 7 within healing period, 3 after loading.
- IT and ISQ collected but specific group means not extractable from front/back text.

## Limitations
- Retrospective, no comparator group (no conventional drilling, no delayed placement arm).
- "Up to 5 years" mixes follow-up windows — Kaplan-Meier curve and time-stratified survival not given in extractable text.
- 93.1% cumulative survival is below the 97–98% benchmarks cited from prior molar IIP series (Smith-Tarnow 11-yr 97.3%; Ketabi SR 98%) but in line with Ragucci's 1-year SR+MA (93.3%); the OD technique does not appear to outperform standard immediate molar implant survival in this dataset.
- Inventor/licensor (Huwais) and Versah-affiliated authors are co-authors — conflict of interest.
- Septum expansion of ~1.35 mm is modest; very thin septa may still be inadequate for primary stability without additional augmentation.
- New socket classification has no inter-rater reliability data in extractable text.
- Living-document note: the abstract's framing as "predictable method" is supported by the survival rate but not by any controlled comparison; treat as descriptive feasibility data, not as evidence of superiority.
