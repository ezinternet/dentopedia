## One-line Summary

Systematic review with single-arm meta-analysis (Int Dent J 2024; 7 studies, 202 patients / 215 immediate implants) of digital-template (static CAIS)-guided immediate implant placement accuracy: pooled coronal 0.74 mm, apical 1.01 mm, depth 0.50 mm, angular 2.34° deviation — generally acceptable, but with very high between-study heterogeneity (I² 75-99%) and timing (immediate vs delayed) not materially affecting accuracy.

## Summary

This systematic review (PROSPERO CRD42023456132) is the first to focus specifically on the accuracy of **immediate** implant placement guided by **digital templates** (static computer-assisted implant surgery, sCAIS), a context distinct from the healed-ridge setting that dominated earlier accuracy reviews. Seven studies (5 retrospective, 2 prospective; 202 patients, 215 implants) were pooled by random-effects single-arm meta-analysis. Template-guided immediate placement achieved pooled deviations of 0.74 mm (coronal), 1.01 mm (apical), 0.50 mm (depth), and 2.34° (angular), which the authors judge "generally acceptable" and numerically lower than a healed-ridge benchmark (Schneider et al: 1.16 mm coronal, 1.96 mm apical). However, heterogeneity was very high (I² 75-99%), all evidence was observational (no RCTs), and the relatively favourable accuracy likely reflects the predominance of fully-guided protocols among included studies. A delayed-vs-immediate sub-analysis found no significant timing effect on coronal, apical, or depth deviation.

## Key Contributions

- First SR isolating accuracy of **immediate** implant placement under **digital templates** (prior reviews/NMAs were healed-ridge or mixed-timing).
- Pooled single-arm deviation benchmarks for chairside expectation-setting: coronal 0.74 mm, apical 1.01 mm, depth 0.50 mm, angular 2.34°.
- Timing analysis: immediate vs delayed placement did not significantly change coronal/apical/depth deviation; angular difference small but significant.
- Synthesises accuracy-modifying factors: guide support (tooth > bone/mucosa), fabrication (CAD/CAM vs lab-manual), arch (mandible > maxilla), flap vs flapless, fully vs half guided.
- Names an immediate-specific failure mode: guides cannot be tried in before extraction, so fit is unverifiable preoperatively; and lingual-vs-buccal bone resistance biases the drill buccally.

## Methodology

- Design: systematic review + random-effects single-arm meta-analysis (Stata v16.0); meta-regression for subgroup comparisons; heterogeneity by I². Significance p < .05.
- PICO: edentulous patients; immediate implantation with digital guides; vs preoperative software-planned implant; outcome = 3D accuracy (deviation of placed from planned).
- Search: PubMed, Cochrane CENTRAL, Embase, Web of Science (inception–July 2023), English; RCTs + retrospective/prospective cohorts.
- Selection: 436 records → 321 de-duplicated → 7 included (5 retrospective, 2 prospective). n = 202 patients, 215 implants.
- Outcomes: coronal, apical, depth, angular deviation (placed vs planned).
- Risk of bias: Newcastle-Ottawa scale (cohorts scored 6-8 → moderate-to-high); Cochrane tool (RCTs). Study mix: maxilla 5 / mandible 2; intraoral scan 5 / extraoral 1 / unspecified 1; lab-manual guides dominant (1 CAD/CAM); flap 4 / flapless 4; fully guided 4, full+half 1, unspecified 2.

## Results

**Global pooled accuracy (all 95% CIs exclude 0 → statistically significant):**

| Metric | Pooled deviation | 95% CI | I² | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Coronal | 0.74 mm | 0.41–1.08 | 99.0% | .000 |
| Apical | 1.01 mm | 0.83–1.20 | 94.0% | .000 |
| Depth | 0.50 mm | 0.36–0.65 | 75.3% | .007 |
| Angular | 2.34° | 1.68–3.00 | 94.5% | .000 |

**Delayed vs immediate timing (5 studies):** coronal 0.07 (95% CI −0.09 to 0.22), apical 0.05 (95% CI −0.12 to 0.22), depth −0.18 (95% CI −0.38 to 0.02) — all CIs include 0 (no significant timing effect); angular 0.16 (95% CI 0.01–0.31) — small significant difference.

**Benchmark vs healed-ridge guided surgery (Schneider et al):** lower coronal (0.74 vs 1.16 mm) and apical (1.01 vs 1.96 mm) deviation — interpret cautiously given I² up to 99% and predominant fully-guided protocols.

**Factor effects (qualitative):** fully guided > half guided; tooth-supported > bone/mucosa-supported; mandible > maxilla; CAD/CAM ≤ conventional angular error. Maxillary and anterior subgroup tables are reported in the paper but contain internal label-metric inconsistencies — treat those numbers with caution.

**Certainty caveat (living-document note):** all included evidence is observational with I² near 99% on key metrics, so the pooled point estimates are weak — best read as order-of-magnitude expectations, not precise thresholds.
