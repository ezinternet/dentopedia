## One-line Summary

Single-center retrospective (106 patients / 148 immediate maxillary-molar implants; 26 analyzed in detail, 29 tapered-SLA fixtures) proposing a paired case-selection classification — alveolar bone height (ABH) Grade A/B/C × interradicular-septum Type I/II/III — to drive socket-lifting, bone-graft, and wide-implant decisions; 93.10% survival with marginal bone stable (~0.01–0.03 mm loss) after 1 year of loading.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

Immediate implant placement in the maxillary molar is biomechanically and anatomically the hardest immediate site (wide socket, poor bone quality, sinus proximity), and the authors note that — despite reported high survival — there was no published guideline for case selection and technique. This Seoul National University Dental Hospital retrospective (2011–2019; 106 patients, 148 implants, with 26 patients / 29 implants analyzed in detail) fills that gap with a **two-axis classification** that maps directly onto surgical decisions:

- **Vertical axis — ABH (alveolar bone height, crestal bone → sinus floor)**: Grade A (>8 mm, no socket lift), Grade B (6–8 mm, socket lift for longer implants), Grade C (<6 mm, socket lift mandatory). Governs *apical* primary stability and the socket-lifting decision.
- **Lateral axis — interradicular septum**: Type I (supports implant, no graft), Type II (weak, graft required), Type III (absent, use wide/ultra-wide implant to engage socket walls). Governs *lateral* primary stability and the graft/wide-implant decision.

Using tapered SLA implants (6–10 mm preferred length), atraumatic flapless extraction with diamond-bur root separation, and transcrestal osteotome socket lifting where indicated, the cohort achieved **93.10% survival**; the 2 early failures (peri-implantitis, sinusitis) were immediately reinstalled with wider-diameter implants ± sinus lifting and continued to function. Marginal bone showed a characteristic fluctuating pattern — early regeneration, loss starting ~6 months — then stabilized after 1 year of loading (0.01±0.01 mm distal, 0.03±0.03 mm mesial), supporting a **6-month re-entry** interval.

## Key Contributions

- First explicit maxillary-molar guideline pairing **ABH grade** (vertical/sinus decision) with **septum type** (lateral/graft decision) — a practical 3×3 decision matrix.
- Quantified 93.10% survival and a fluctuating-then-stable MBL trajectory over T0–T4 (T0 immediate → T4 1 year post-loading).
- Documented salvage pathways: immediate reinstallation with wide-diameter implant ± transcrestal sinus lifting after early failure.
- Reinforced technique anchors: atraumatic flapless extraction, septum/buccal-bone preservation, diamond-bur root separation with irrigation, sub-crestal placement, and 6-month healing before re-entry.

## Methodology

- **Design**: retrospective clinical case series / classification study, STROBE-reported, single center (IRB S-D20200007).
- **Cohort**: 106 patients / 148 immediate implants; detailed analytic group 26 patients / 29 tapered-SLA implants (14 Luna, 14 Straumann, 1 Anyone; 20 bone-level, 9 tissue-level). Sites 10×#16, 5×#17, 10×#26, 4×#27. Mean age 64.88 y.
- **Exclusions**: simultaneous lateral-window sinus augmentation; incomplete pre/postoperative records.
- **Failure**: ICOI Pisa Consensus implant health scale.
- **MBL**: panoramic radiographs at T0/T1(1mo)/T2(re-entry 6mo)/T3(1mo post-load)/T4(1y post-load); implant shoulder → most coronal BIC, mesial+distal, mean of 3; paired t-test, p<0.05.

## Results

- **Survival 93.10%** — 2/26 patients failed before re-entry (one peri-implantitis with fistula, one sinusitis); both reinstalled with wider-diameter implants (+ sinus lifting in the sinusitis case) and functioned well.
- **MBL**: early bone gain (mesial T1 −0.44±0.62 mm; distal T2/T3/T4 −0.27/−0.16/−0.28 mm vs T0), loss beginning ~6 months, then **stable after 1 year loading** (0.01±0.01 mm distal, 0.03±0.03 mm mesial).
- **Implant length** preferred 6–10 mm; short dental implants (<8 mm) used immediately in 5 patients (incl. 2 reinstallations) with favorable outcomes.
- Socket-lifting thresholds endorsed: lift at ABH ≤8 mm; lateral approach + delayed at ABH ≤4 mm; transcrestal lift at ABH >5 mm.
