## Summary

This systematic review and meta-analysis (12 studies) compared early implant placement with immediate and delayed protocols. Implant failure rates were statistically equivalent across all three timings. However, early placement produced significantly less marginal bone loss than immediate placement into fresh extraction sockets, with no differences for probing depth or soft-tissue level. The authors conclude that early placement matches immediate/delayed protocols for survival while offering superior peri-implant hard-tissue stability over immediate placement — supporting early placement as a balanced timing choice.

## Key Contributions

- Quantitative positioning of early placement against immediate and delayed protocols.
- Identifies a hard-tissue stability advantage of early over immediate placement.

## Methodology

- SR+MA; primary outcome failed implants; secondary marginal bone level, probing depth, soft-tissue level.

## Results

- Failure: early vs immediate RD −0.018 (p=0.416); early vs delayed RD −0.008 (p=0.670) — both NS.
- MBL: early vs immediate WMD −0.14 mm (−0.22 to −0.05, p=0.001), favoring early.
