## Summary
Evidence-Based Dentistry critical commentary on the Meijer et al. JPeriodontol 2025 10-year RCT (University Medical Center Groningen). The original RCT enrolled 40 patients with a failing tooth in the aesthetic region and a buccal bony defect of **≥5 mm** — historically considered a contraindication to immediate implant placement.

Patients were randomized to:
- **Immediate Group**: immediate implant placement with bone grafting and delayed provisionalization
- **Delayed Group**: ridge preservation followed by delayed implant placement and provisionalization

Ten-year CBCT and clinical outcomes were acceptable in both groups. The commentary's framing: an intact buccal wall is **not absolute prerequisite** for immediate implant placement in the aesthetic zone — provided the protocol uses GBR and delayed provisionalization rather than immediate provisionalization.

## Key Contributions
- 10-year RCT — rare and high-evidence-level data for the immediate implant + bony defect indication.
- Refines the long-standing teaching that intact buccal wall is required for immediate placement.
- Specifies the protocol elements that make the broader indication safe (GBR + delayed provisionalization).

## Methodology (Original RCT)
UMCG single-center; 10-year follow-up; n=40 patients with failing tooth + buccal bony defect ≥5 mm; randomized; CBCT outcome assessment.

## Results (Original RCT)
[근거강함] 10-year outcomes acceptable in both groups; immediate + GBR + delayed provisionalization viable for ≥5 mm buccal defects.

## Limitations
- Original RCT single-center
- n=40
- Delayed provisionalization protocol — does NOT validate immediate + immediate provisionalization in defect sockets
- Aesthetic zone only

## Clinical Implication (Korean Practice)
For Korean implant practice: in carefully selected aesthetic-zone cases with ≥5 mm buccal defect, immediate implant + GBR + **delayed** provisionalization is a defensible long-term option per this 10-year RCT. Do **not** extrapolate to immediate provisionalization in these defect cases — that requires separate evidence.
