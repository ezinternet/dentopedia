## Summary
Narrative review (Johannes Gutenberg University Mainz, Charité Berlin) evaluating current evidence for immediate implant placement and restoration in full-arch edentulous or fully extracted situations. Consistently high survival rates with improved quality of life, though mechanical complications remain clinically significant.

## Key Contributions
- **Implant survival >97%** across immediate full-arch protocols in well-selected patients
- **Implant success >95%** when appropriate patient selection applied
- **MBL: 0.29–1.48 mm** over 1–5 years — within acceptable range
- **Prosthesis survival often >98%**; mechanical complications: veneer fractures, screw loosening
- **Patient satisfaction and QoL improved significantly** post-treatment
- Key challenges: anatomical variation, bone augmentation needs, primary stability thresholds

## Patient Selection Criteria
- Adequate bone volume for primary stability (insertion torque threshold)
- No active infection at extraction sites
- Restorative space assessment for provisional prosthesis
- Medical status permitting immediate procedure
