## One-line Summary
1-year RCT (n=24): tuberosity autograft vs xenograft for filling buccal defects at immediate implant sites are equally effective (buccal bone resorption 0.61 vs 0.58 mm; 100% survival at 1 year), with autograft avoiding additional donor-site morbidity.

## Summary
1-year RCT comparing tuberosity autograft vs xenograft for filling buccal defects at immediate implant sites.

## Key Contributions
- No significant difference in buccal bone resorption: autograft 0.61mm vs xenograft 0.58mm
- Tuberosity autograft: donor site without additional morbidity
- Both groups: 100% survival at 1 year

## Methodology
Prospective RCT; n=24; 1-year follow-up.

## Results
Tuberosity autograft and xenograft equally effective for buccal defect filling at immediate implant sites. Autograft avoids additional surgical site when tuberosity bone available.
