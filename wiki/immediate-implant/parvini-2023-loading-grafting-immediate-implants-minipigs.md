## One-line Summary

Minipig RCT (n=56 TLX implants, 2×2 factorial, 4 and 12 weeks) found that neither immediate loading nor gap grafting with bovine bone mineral significantly altered BIC (74–84%) or peri-implant soft-tissue dimensions at immediately placed progressive-thread tissue-level implants.

## Summary

This histomorphometric animal study by Parvini et al. (2023) tested whether immediate loading and/or simultaneous gap grafting with xenogenic bone mineral affect osseointegration and soft-tissue healing at immediately placed Straumann TLX implants in a minipig model. Using a 2×2 split-mouth factorial design across 14 minipigs (56 implants; 4-week and 12-week sacrifice groups), no significant differences in BIC were detected between loaded vs. unloaded or grafted vs. non-grafted groups at either time point. The single significant finding was that grafted-unloaded sites showed significantly lower (more apical) first BIC (fBIC) values than non-grafted-unloaded sites at 12 weeks (p=.01), interpreted as grafting shifting bone-implant contact apically — which may protect against supracrestal exposure of the rough implant surface. Soft-tissue dimensions (JE, CTC, biological width) were comparable across all groups and consistent with published reference values for tissue-level implants.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrated that **immediate loading does not compromise osseointegration** of TLX implants in fresh extraction sockets, at least when loading is via healing abutments (no occlusal contact confirmed)
- BIC values at 4 and 12 weeks (74–84%) were high and comparable to or exceeded those of conventionally delayed-placed tissue-level implants in prior literature
- **Grafting shifted fBIC apically** (significant at 12 wk, unloaded groups; trend at loaded groups), suggesting grafting may reduce risk of rough-surface supracrestal exposure rather than increasing total bone-implant contact
- **Peri-implant biological width was unaffected** by either loading or grafting protocol, indicating soft-tissue healing proceeds independently of these variables under the study conditions
- The progressive thread design of TLX (bone-condensing effect) and hydrophilic SLActive® surface are proposed to explain the robust BIC regardless of grafting or loading condition

## Methodology

**Design**: 2×2 factorial, split-mouth; 4 groups (unloaded+grafted / unloaded+non-grafted / loaded+grafted / loaded+non-grafted)

**Animal**: 14 female Ellegaard Goettingen minipigs, 22–29 months, ~40 kg; Lund University Biomedical Dept., ARRIVE 2.0

**Implant**: Straumann TLX RT Roxolid® SLActive® (Ø 3.75 × 6 mm); fully tapered progressive thread; 2.8 mm machined neck; smooth-rough border placed at buccal crestal level; insertion torque capped at 80 Ncm

**Graft**: Cerabone® bovine bone mineral (0.5–1.0 mm) packed into circumferential peri-implant gap and adjacent extraction sockets

**Loading**: Loaded = healing abutment (4.5 mm) with no confirmed occlusal contact; Unloaded = subcrestal closure cap (1.5 mm)

**Endpoints**: Primary — BIC%; Secondary — fBIC (buccal, lingual), JE, CTC, BW (= JE+CTC), SE; measured on 200 µm non-decalcified paragon-stained bucco-lingual sections

**Analysis**: Multivariate mixed linear regression; random effect = animal; Dunnett-Hsu correction; Wilcoxon signed-rank for paired comparisons

**Sample**: 48 of 56 implants analyzable (7 excluded: healing abutment loss; 1 failed: fibrous encapsulation)

## Results

| Group | BIC 4 wk | BIC 12 wk | fBIC trend 12 wk |
|---|---|---|---|
| G1: Unloaded + Grafted | 83.8 ± 13.3% | 79.9 ± 8.6% | Decreased (lower = more apical) * |
| G2: Unloaded + Non-grafted | 74.5 ± 11.6% | 75.5 ± 7.9% | Increased |
| G3: Loaded + Grafted | 83.8 ± 13.3% | ~78% | Decreased |
| G4: Loaded + Non-grafted | ~77% | ~77% | Partial decrease (buccal) |

*G1 vs G2 fBIC at 12 wk significantly different, p=.01

**BIC**: No significant between-group differences at 4 or 12 weeks (multivariate regression)

**fBIC**: At 12 weeks, G1 (unloaded+grafted) had significantly lower fBIC than G2 (unloaded+non-grafted) at buccal and lingual — meaning bone-implant contact started more apically when graft particles were present; G3 vs G4 trend did not reach significance

**Soft tissues** (JE, CTC, BW): No significant between-group differences at 4 or 12 weeks; JE higher buccal than lingual; CTC higher lingual than buccal — consistent with established anatomy

**BW values**: 2481–4091 µm at 4 wk; 2501–3802 µm at 12 wk — within published reference range for tissue-level implants (Schwarz et al. 2007, 2014)

**Clinical interpretation**: Neither immediate loading nor grafting detracted from osseointegration; grafting may shift BIC apically (protective against rough-surface exposure) without improving total BIC; robust BIC likely attributable to progressive-thread bone-condensing design + SLActive® surface
