## Summary
This retrospective case series followed 19 patients (22 implants) who received immediate implant placement into fresh maxillary molar sockets combined with simultaneous transcrestal sinus floor elevation (Summers' BAOSFE) and early loading at 6–8 weeks. Over a mean 33.12-month follow-up, 100% implant survival and 95.45% success (Buser criteria) were observed, with negligible mean crestal bone resorption (−0.054 ± 0.56 mm).

## Key Contributions
- Combines three normally staged steps — extraction, transcrestal sinus lift, and implant placement — into a single visit with early loading.
- Demonstrates predictable mid-term outcomes when ≥5–6 mm of interradicular bone is available in maxillary molars.
- Reports minimal mesial-distal asymmetry of crestal bone loss when soft tissue sealing (coronally advanced flap or palatal pedicle CTG) is employed.

## Methodology
- 19 patients (12 F / 7 M, mean age 56.28 y) requiring extraction of compromised maxillary molars.
- Atraumatic extraction via tooth sectioning and piezosurgery; socket debridement.
- Pilot drill stopped 1 mm short of sinus floor in interradicular septum; Summers' BAOSFE with bovine bone graft (Cerabon or Tutodent); Valsalva maneuver verified membrane integrity.
- Xive (Dentsply) or Axiom (Antogyr) implants; non-submerged technique; healing abutments at placement.
- Soft tissue closure with acellular dermal allograft or collagen membrane plus coronally advanced flap / palatal connective tissue pedicle graft.
- Early loading after 6–8 weeks once Periotest confirmed stability; periapical radiographs with X-Pod calibration for FBIC measurement.

## Results
- Mean follow-up 33.12 ± 16.98 mo (max 5 y).
- Implant survival: 100%; Buser success: 95.45%.
- Crestal bone loss: mean −0.054 ± 0.56 mm (range −0.86 to +0.90 mm).
- Mesial vs distal bone loss: −0.02 ± 0.55 mm vs −0.09 ± 0.59 mm (P = 0.232, NS).
- 31.8% of surfaces showed bone gain coronal to platform; 22.7% no change; 45.5% measurable loss.
- One peri-implantitis case (excess cement / marginal gap at 15 mo) treated with GBR.
