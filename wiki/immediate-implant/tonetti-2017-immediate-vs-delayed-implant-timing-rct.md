## One-line Summary

Multicenter RCT (124 subjects, anterior/premolar single teeth) comparing immediate vs 12-week delayed implant placement; immediate placement required more bone augmentation (72% vs 43.9%), had more wound failures (26.1% vs 5.3%), deeper 1-year probing depths, and more inadequate pink-esthetic scores (42% vs 19%), leading to a recommendation against immediate placement where esthetics matter.

## Summary

The "timing" RCT randomized 124 subjects to immediate (at-extraction) or delayed (12-week) implant placement for single anterior/premolar teeth across seven practices with masked examiners. Immediate placement was unfeasible in 7.5% of intended cases and, when performed, more often required bone augmentation, produced more wound-healing failures, deeper 1-year probing depths, and a higher proportion of inadequate pink-esthetic outcomes, with a trend toward greater radiographic bone loss. The authors conclude immediate placement should be limited to selected cases and not recommended where esthetics are important — a key counterweight to enthusiasm for routine immediate placement.

## Key Contributions

- Rare well-controlled multicenter RCT on placement timing.
- Quantifies augmentation burden, wound-failure risk, and esthetic penalty of immediate placement.

## Methodology

- 124 subjects randomized to immediate vs delayed (12 wk); single anterior/premolar teeth; restoratively driven; masked examiners; up to 3-yr radiographic follow-up.

## Results

- Augmentation: 72% (IMI) vs 43.9% (delayed), p = 0.01.
- Wound failure: 26.1% vs 5.3%, p = 0.02.
- 1-yr probing depth: 4.1 vs 3.3 mm, p < 0.01.
- Inadequate PES: 42% vs 19%, p = 0.03; PROMs equivalent.
