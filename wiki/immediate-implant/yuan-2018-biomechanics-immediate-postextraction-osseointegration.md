## One-line Summary

Murine first-molar extraction + palatal-root-socket osteotomy model showing low-strain peri-implant zones (buccal, PDL-filled gap) osseointegrate via PDL-derived Wnt-responsive osteogenic cells, while high-strain zones (palatal, direct bone contact) undergo osteocyte death and bone resorption — with exogenous liposomal WNT3A rescuing osseointegration where PDL/Wnt cells are absent.

## 한줄요약

마우스 제1대구치 발치 후 구개근 소켓 (palatal root socket)에 골삭제·식립한 동물 모델. 변형률(strain)이 낮은 협측(PDL 잔존 gap)은 PDL 유래 Wnt 반응 골형성 세포로 골유착되고, 변형률이 높은 구개측(골 직접 압박)은 골세포 사멸·골흡수가 일어났으며, PDL/Wnt 세포가 없는 부위에 외인성 리포좀 WNT3A를 투여하면 골유착이 회복됨.

## Summary

This experimental murine study dissects *why* immediate postextraction implants osseointegrate (or fail to), combining finite element (FE) biomechanics with histology and Wnt-lineage biology. The authors mimic clinical immediate placement by extracting the first molar and preparing an osteotomy **specifically in the palatal root socket**, positioned so that it removes periodontal ligament (PDL) **only on the palatal aspect** while leaving the buccal-aspect PDL undisturbed. This single construct therefore contains two opposite peri-implant environments at once: a **palatal aspect in direct implant–bone contact** and a **buccal aspect with a PDL-filled implant–bone gap**.

The two environments diverge along both a mechanical and a biological axis. FE modeling shows **high strain palatally** (bone compressed by the implant), which coincides with osteocyte death, bone resorption, and net loss of peri-implant bone. The **buccal aspect is low-strain**, with minimal osteocyte death and robust new peri-implant bone formation. Crucially, the buccal PDL remnants are not passive: they **directly supply Wnt-responsive cells** that drive new bone formation and osseointegration. Where this PDL/Wnt source is absent (palatal aspect), applying **exogenous liposomal WNT3A** reconstitutes an osteogenic environment and restores rapid peri-implant bone formation.

The clinical translation is twofold: (1) **low mechanical strain** favors osseointegration of immediate implants, and (2) the **PDL is an inherently osteogenic tissue** that, when healthy, is worth preserving during immediate placement. This provides the mechanistic underpinning for the clinically favored maxillary-molar strategy of engaging the **palatal root socket** — the palatal root provides bone volume for primary stability, while preserved socket PDL contributes Wnt-responsive osteogenic cells to the healing interface.

## Key Contributions

- **Two-environment immediate-placement model**: palatal-root-socket osteotomy removes PDL palatally only → palatal direct-bone-contact zone vs. buccal PDL-filled-gap zone in one implant.
- **Strain ↔ outcome coupling**: high palatal strain → osteocyte death + resorption + peri-implant bone loss; low buccal strain → minimal osteocyte death + robust osseointegration.
- **PDL as a direct cell source**: buccal PDL remnants supply Wnt-responsive cells responsible for new bone and osseointegration (PDL is osteogenic, not just space-filling).
- **Pharmacologic rescue**: exogenous liposomal WNT3A restores osteogenesis/osseointegration on the PDL-deficient palatal aspect.
- **Clinical principle**: low strain + high Wnt favor immediate-implant osseointegration; preserve healthy PDL during immediate placement.

## Methodology

- **Model**: murine first maxillary molar extraction, then immediate osteotomy in the **palatal root socket**.
- **Design**: osteotomy removes PDL only on the palatal aspect; buccal PDL left intact → palatal = direct bone contact, buccal = PDL-filled gap.
- **Biomechanics**: finite element modeling of peri-implant strain distribution (high palatal vs. low buccal).
- **Biology**: osteocyte viability/death, peri-implant resorption vs. formation, identification of PDL-derived Wnt-responsive cells.
- **Intervention**: exogenous **liposomal WNT3A** applied to the PDL-/Wnt-deficient palatal aspect.
- Confidence: `animal` (murine experimental study).

## Results

- **Palatal aspect** (high strain, no PDL): osteocyte death + bone resorption → loss of peri-implant bone.
- **Buccal aspect** (low strain, PDL-filled gap): minimal osteocyte death + robust peri-implant bone formation / osseointegration.
- **Mechanism**: buccal PDL remnants directly provided Wnt-responsive cells driving osseointegration.
- **Rescue**: liposomal WNT3A on the palatal aspect created an osteogenic environment for rapid peri-implant bone formation.
- **Conclusion**: osseointegration of immediate postextraction implants is favored by low strain and high Wnt signaling; healthy PDL is reasonable to preserve during immediate placement.
