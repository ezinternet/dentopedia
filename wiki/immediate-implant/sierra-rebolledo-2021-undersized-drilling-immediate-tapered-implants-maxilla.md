## One-line Summary

RCT (n=30 immediate tapered implants, anterior maxilla; 16 conventional vs 14 undersized drilling): undersized drilling did NOT significantly improve primary stability — insertion torque slightly higher in UD (41.36 vs 38.44 Ncm, p=0.654) but RFA/ISQ consistently higher in the conventional group at all timepoints, with no significant between-group ISQ difference across 12 weeks.

## 한줄요약

RCT (즉시식립 tapered 임플란트 30개, 상악 전치부; 통상 16 vs 축경 14): 축경(undersized) 드릴링은 1차 안정성을 유의하게 개선하지 못함 — 삽입 토크는 축경군이 약간 높았으나(41.36 vs 38.44 Ncm, p=0.654) RFA/ISQ는 오히려 통상군이 모든 시점에서 더 높았고 12주간 군간 ISQ 차이는 유의하지 않았다.

## Summary

This is probably the first randomized controlled trial to test whether undersized drilling improves the primary stability of **immediate** implants in the esthetically demanding anterior maxilla. Thirty tapered 3.75 × 13 mm implants were placed immediately after extraction and randomized 1:1 to a conventional complete drilling sequence (final 3.0 mm twist drill used) or an undersized sequence (3.0 mm twist drill omitted, leaving a narrower bone bed). Stability was tracked by insertion torque (IT) and resonance frequency analysis (RFA/ISQ) at insertion, 6 weeks, and 12 weeks.

The classically expected IT increase with undersizing appeared but was not significant (UD 41.36 vs CD 38.44 Ncm, p=0.654), and — counterintuitively — RFA-measured stability was always **higher in the conventional group** at every timepoint, with no significant between-group ISQ difference over 12 weeks. The authors conclude undersized drilling does not afford significantly improved stability for immediate maxillary implants, and clinically may be unnecessary. They attribute the dissociation to high IT causing bone compression/warming and greater early remodeling, which can erode RFA stability even as torque rises — reinforcing that IT and RFA are not interchangeable stability surrogates.

## Key Contributions

- First RCT on undersized drilling for **immediate** implant primary stability (prior data were bench/animal/cadaver and a 3-implant case series).
- Demonstrates a clear IT–RFA dissociation: undersizing nudged IT up but RFA stability favored conventional drilling at all timepoints.
- Practical message: undersized drilling is likely unnecessary for immediate anterior-maxillary implants when adequate bone is present; and because mean ISQ never reached 70, occlusal loading should wait ~12 weeks.

## Methodology

- **Design**: randomized, comparative, parallel-group RCT; 1:1 coin-toss allocation by an external observer immediately before drilling; ClinicalTrials.gov registered.
- **Powered** for a minimum ISQ effect of 7 (SD 5), 5% α / 10% β, 15% dropout → ≥28 (14/group); 30 enrolled.
- **Patients**: adults >18 y needing single implant + maxillary anterior extraction; medically/periodontally screened; CBCT-confirmed ≥5 mm bone apex-to-nasal-fossa/sinus and no crestal vertical defect >4 mm.
- **Implants**: 30 tapered, conical-connection grade-23 Ti-6Al-4V ELI, dual acid-etched + sandblasted, 3.75 × 13 mm (C1, MIS).
- **Drilling groups**:
  - **Conventional (CD, n=16)**: 1.9 mm marking @1500 rpm → 2.4 mm/13 mm pilot @800 rpm → 3.0 mm twist @400 rpm.
  - **Undersized (UD, n=14)**: 1.9 mm marking @1500 rpm → 2.4 mm/13 mm pilot @400 rpm; **3.0 mm twist drill omitted**.
- **Insertion**: 20:1 handpiece @20 rpm/10 Ncm, ratchet to 4 mm subgingival; minimally traumatic extraction; two calibrated operators.
- **Outcomes**: peak IT (MIS torque meters); RFA/ISQ (Osstell, Smartpeg #49) at insertion / 6 wk / 12 wk.
- **Stats**: SPSS 20; Student t-test (IT); repeated-measures ANOVA (ISQ); p<0.05.

## Results

- **Cohort**: 30 patients (19 F / 11 M), mean age 48.87 ± 14.84 y. No adverse events or failures over 12 weeks.
- **Insertion torque**: overall median 35, mean 39.80 ± 7.14 Ncm (range 17–75). UD 41.36 ± 18.86 vs CD 38.44 ± 15.99 Ncm — **not significant (p=0.654)**.
- **ISQ**: range 50.0–79.5; insertion mean 64.83 ± 6.63. **CD higher than UD at every timepoint** (insertion CD 65.50 ± 6.03 vs UD 64.07 ± 7.41); no significant between-group difference.
- **Trajectory**: repeated-measures ANOVA — progressive, similar ISQ increase in both groups; overall 12-week mean 68.10 ± 6.21 (still lower in UD). Mean ISQ never reached 70.
- **Conclusion**: undersized drilling does not significantly improve immediate maxillary implant stability during osseointegration; loading should wait ~12 weeks.
