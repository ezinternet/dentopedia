## Summary

This PRISMA-compliant systematic review and meta-analysis (University of Salford / Lancaster, 2023) set out to update the immediate-vs-delayed placement question and refresh Chrcanovic et al. (2015). Searching CENTRAL, MEDLINE and Scopus (2014–2022), it pooled **10 comparative studies** — 6 RCTs and 4 non-randomised comparative studies — totalling **341 immediately placed and 359 delayed implants**.

The pooled fixed-effects analysis of implant survival gave a **risk ratio of 0.99 (95% CI 0.96–1.02)**, which did not reach significance (Z = 0.78, p = 0.44), with **zero heterogeneity (I² = 0%)**. Aggregate survival was essentially identical: **97.4% immediate (332/341)** vs **97.5% delayed (350/359)**. The bottom line is therefore **equivalent survival** between the two timing protocols.

A secondary, non-significant directional signal favoured delayed placement: more individual immediate-group studies dipped into the 90–95% survival range, while most delayed-group studies exceeded 95%, and delayed placement was numerically superior in most trials. The two exceptions where immediate exceeded delayed (Han 2016, Woods 2019) had short follow-up or an atypical cohort (Woods = head/neck cancer patients receiving post-operative radiotherapy, with 20% SLActive failures). The clinical implication: survival is not the deciding factor between immediate and delayed placement, so the choice should be driven by the trade-offs that live elsewhere — esthetics, treatment time, complication/wound-healing risk, and patient/site selection.

## Key Contributions

- **No significant survival difference**: pooled RR 0.99 (95% CI 0.96–1.02), I²=0% — high certainty, equivalent survival.
- **Quantified aggregate survival**: 97.4% immediate vs 97.5% delayed across 700 implants.
- **Evidence base characterised**: 5/6 RCTs at low risk of bias; the 4 non-randomised studies at moderate-to-serious risk.
- **Directional caveat**: slight numerical favouring of delayed placement (more immediate studies in the 90–95% band), not statistically significant and confounded by short follow-up and small samples.
- **Updates** the prior Chrcanovic et al. (2015) placement-timing review.

## Methodology

- **Design**: SR + meta-analysis, PRISMA-compliant, PROSPERO-registered.
- **PICO**: adults ≥18 y; immediate placement (fresh socket at extraction) vs delayed placement (healed site, ≥2 months / typically 3–4 months); outcome = implant survival (present at examination).
- **Databases**: CENTRAL, MEDLINE, Scopus (Nov 2022) + snowballing + Google Scholar grey literature.
- **Inclusion**: RCTs and non-randomised comparative studies, 2014–2022, English, peer-reviewed. Excluded case-control, case series, editorials, children.
- **Quality**: RoB 2.0 (RCTs) and ROBINS-I (non-randomised) via Review Manager 5.4.1.
- **Synthesis**: RevMan v.5.3, fixed-effects model, risk ratio; funnel plot for publication bias.
- **Included studies (n=10)**: Bömicke 2017, Cucchi 2017, Esposito 2015, Hakobyan 2020, Han 2016, Malchiodi 2016, S. Raes 2018, Slagter 2016, Tonetti 2017, Woods 2019.

## Results

- **Primary (pooled survival)**: RR **0.99, 95% CI 0.96–1.02**; Z = 0.78, p = 0.44 (abstract: Z=0.75, p=0.45); **I² = 0%** (heterogeneity p = 0.60).
- **Aggregate survival**: immediate **332/341 = 97.4%**; delayed **350/359 = 97.5%**.
- **Per-study survival (immediate vs delayed)**: Bömicke 94.7% vs 100%; Cucchi 95.9% vs 100%; Esposito 96.3% vs 100%; Hakobyan 97.8% vs 98.1%; Han 100% vs 96.9%; Malchiodi 100% vs 100%; Raes 93.8% vs 100%; Slagter 100% vs 100%; Tonetti 98.3% vs 100%; Woods 97.4% vs 90.5%.
- **Risk of bias**: 5/6 RCTs low risk; non-randomised studies moderate-to-serious.
- **Secondary signals (not pooled)**: mixed marginal/crestal bone loss and ISQ; some immediate-placement studies showed more wound/residual-bone complications (e.g. Tonetti: wound failure ~5× more likely with immediate; Tonetti: ~0.8 mm greater residual bone loss with immediate) — direction varies by study.
- **Publication bias**: minor funnel-plot asymmetry, judged unlikely to change the pooled effect.
