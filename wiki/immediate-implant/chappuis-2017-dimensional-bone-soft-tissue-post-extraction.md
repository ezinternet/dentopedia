## Summary
Companion review to Buser 2017 (same Periodontol 2000 issue), synthesizing animal and human evidence on the biology and clinical extent of post-extraction dimensional alterations. Identifies modulating factors and their implications for treatment planning in the esthetic zone.

## Key Contributions
- **Facial bone wall**: <1mm in 90% of anterior maxilla; <0.5mm in 66%
- Bundle bone (tooth-dependent, 0.2–0.4mm): resorbs completely post-extraction regardless of implant
- Facial bone vertical loss: ~2.2mm in mandibular premolar dog model
- Immediate implant: does NOT prevent socket remodeling — BIC 2.6mm apical on buccal
- **2mm facial wall** threshold needed to maintain dimensions after immediate implant
- Socket grafting: partially counteracts marginal ridge contraction
- Human ridge: up to 50% width loss in first year; **two-thirds within first 3 months**
- Meta-analysis: 2.6–4.5mm width loss; 0.4–3.9mm height loss in healed sockets

## Modulating Factors
| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Thin facial wall (<1mm) | Greatest dimensional loss |
| Tooth angulation | Variable resorption pattern |
| Flap elevation | Increases resorption |
| Socket grafting | Partially protective |
| Immediate implant | Does not prevent; may reduce if 2mm wall present |

## Clinical Implications
- Assess facial bone wall thickness before deciding on timing (CBCT or intraoperative probing)
- Sites with <1mm facial wall are high-risk for Type I (immediate) — consider Type II/III
- Socket preservation (grafting) recommended when delayed approach planned
- 3-month window is critical — most resorption occurs here
