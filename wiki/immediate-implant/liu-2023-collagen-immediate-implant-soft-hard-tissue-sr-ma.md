## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 7 RCTs (205 patients, 279 implants) finding that adding collagen to bone-grafting material in the immediate-implant jumping gap significantly improves buccal bone thickness (MD −0.43 mm) and esthetic score (MD −1.23), with no difference in implant failure or soft-tissue thickness.

## Summary

This systematic review and meta-analysis pooled 7 RCTs (205 patients, 279 implants) to isolate the effect of **collagen** when used together with bone-grafting material in the jumping gap of immediate implant placement. Collagen incorporation conferred a significant advantage on buccal bone thickness and pink-esthetic outcomes, while implant failure rate and soft-tissue thickness were unaffected. The authors conclude that grafting the gap with a collagen-containing bone substitute is preferable for hard-tissue and esthetic preservation in immediate implants.

## Key Contributions

- Isolates the collagen variable in immediate-implant gap grafting via an RCT-only meta-analysis.
- Demonstrates a measurable buccal-bone and esthetic benefit attributable to collagen.
- Shows no failure-rate or soft-tissue-thickness penalty from collagen addition.

## Methodology

- RCT-only SR+MA; PubMed, Embase, Cochrane CENTRAL + hand search; Cochrane ROB 2.0 (2 low / 3 moderate / 2 high risk).
- Two subgroup comparisons: collagen+bone graft vs bone graft alone, and vs no graft.
- Outcomes: implant failure, buccal bone resorption/thickness, soft-tissue thickness, esthetic score. Fixed- and random-effects models.

## Results

- Implant failure: no significant difference (95% CI 0.17–11.84).
- Soft-tissue thickness: no significant difference.
- Buccal bone thickness: MD −0.43 mm (95% CI −0.72 to −0.41), favoring collagen.
- Esthetic score: MD −1.23 (95% CI −1.90 to −0.55), favoring collagen.
