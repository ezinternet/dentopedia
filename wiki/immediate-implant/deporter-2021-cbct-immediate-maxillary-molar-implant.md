## One-line Summary
Retrospective CBCT study (427 maxillary molar sites, 223 patients): only 61.7% of first molars and 34% of second molars had an inter-radicular septum (IRS) large enough for a ≥5 mm immediate molar implant (IMI) to be fully contained, and the mean furcation-to-sinus-floor distance (~9-9.7 mm) means concurrent sinus elevation is often needed — mandating pre-extraction CBCT for IMI planning.

## 한줄요약
원뿔형 빔 컴퓨터 단층촬영 (Cone-Beam Computed Tomography, CBCT) 후향연구 (n=427 부위): 상악 1대구치 61.7%, 2대구치 34%만 직경 ≥5 mm 즉시 대구치 임플란트 (Immediate Molar Implant, IMI) 가능한 치근간 격벽 (Inter-Radicular Septum, IRS) 크기 보유; 평균 함기화후 잔존골 (Furcation-Sinus-Floor distance, FSD) ~9 mm로 다수에서 상악동거상술 필요.

## Summary
Retrospective CBCT review of 427 maxillary molar sites (223 patients). Measured IRS width (buccopalatal and mesiodistal) at three levels plus furcation- and apex-to-sinus-floor distances. Quantifies pre-extraction anatomical eligibility for IMI placement and likely need for concurrent sinus elevation.

## Key Contributions
- Numerical reference values for IRS dimensions at crestal/middle/apical levels.
- Side-by-side first vs second molar comparison.
- Per-root sinus intrusion rates — predicts perforation/sinus communication risk.

## Methodology
- 223 patients, 427 maxillary molar CBCT sites, two centers (Toronto + Lahijan).
- Sirona CBCT scans, Galileos viewer 1.9.
- IRS BPW + MDW at crestal/middle/apical; FSD; per-root apex-to-SF distance.
- Two reads, 4-week interval, examiner reliability assessed.

## Results
- **First molar coronal IRS**: BPW 6.52 mm / MDW 7.33 mm.
- **Second molar coronal IRS**: BPW 5.85 mm / MDW 6.86 mm (p = 0.008).
- **FSD**: 9.69 mm (first molar) / 8.84 mm (second molar).
- **Apex-to-SF**: mean <3 mm for all roots.
- **Sinus intrusion**: first molar palatal roots 28.85%; second molar mesiobuccal roots 37.65%.
- **IMI candidacy (≥5 mm implant fully contained)**: 61.7% of first molars; 34% of second molars.

## Clinical Relevance
- Pre-extraction CBCT mandatory for IMI planning of maxillary molars.
- Second molars: only 1 in 3 qualify for full-containment IMI — counsel patient on staged or alternative plan.
- Concurrent transcrestal sinus elevation often needed (FSD <10 mm).
- Mesiobuccal root of second molars highest perforation risk — careful extraction technique.
