## One-line Summary
Beagle dog experimental study (n=7, mandibular premolar sockets with immediate SLA implant placement): woven-bone bone-to-implant contact (BIC) formed at the marginal gap by 4 weeks, but buccal-wall resorption from bundle-bone loss shifted the buccal crest >2 mm apical of the implant shoulder by 12 weeks, exposing previously osseointegrated surface — immediate implants do not prevent buccal ridge collapse.

## 한줄요약
비글견 동물실험 (7마리, 하악 소구치 발치와에 즉시 즉시식립 (Immediate Implant Placement, IIP)): 4주에 형성된 골-임플란트 접촉률 (Bone-to-Implant Contact, BIC)이 12주에는 협측 골벽 흡수로 인해 부분적으로 소실되어, 협측 변연골 (Buccal Crest)이 임플란트 어깨 (Shoulder) 보다 2 mm 이상 치근단 방향으로 이동.

## Summary
Seven beagle dogs underwent mandibular premolar extraction with **immediate implant placement** into the distal sockets, healing semi-submerged with SLA-surface implants. Histologic ground sections at multiple time points showed that the marginal gap between implant and socket wall **filled with woven bone in contact with the SLA surface by 4 weeks** — i.e., early osseointegration was achieved. However, in parallel, the **buccal bone wall underwent marked surface and vertical resorption**, driven by loss of bundle bone after tooth removal. By **12 weeks**, the buccal crest had shifted **>2 mm apical** of the implant shoulder, meaning previously osseointegrated implant surface was exposed and BIC at the buccal crest was lost. The lingual wall, being thicker, showed less change. The paper established a core principle: **immediate implants do not prevent buccal ridge collapse**.

## Key Contributions
- Histologic proof that immediate-implant osseointegration **regresses** at the buccal aspect as bundle bone is resorbed.
- Defined the **>2 mm apical migration of the buccal crest by 12 weeks** as a benchmark expectation in thin-walled sockets.
- Underpinned the modern clinical rules of **palatal positioning, jumping-gap grafting, and contour augmentation** in immediate placement.
- Validated the beagle dog mandibular premolar as the standard preclinical model for ridge-implant interaction studies.

## Methodology
- 7 beagle dogs, mandibular P3/P4 extracted bilaterally; SLA implants placed into fresh sockets; semi-submerged.
- Time-staggered surgery in 5 dogs (right then left, 2 months apart, sacrificed 1 month after second) → 4-week vs 12-week healing comparison in each animal.
- 2 dogs bilateral in a single session, biopsied immediately as baseline.
- Donath cutting–grinding technique → non-decalcified ground sections, histologic and morphometric analysis.

## Results
- 4 weeks: woven bone fills implant–socket gap and contacts SLA surface (early integration).
- 4 weeks: marked surface resorption of both buccal and lingual cortical walls; buccal wall height already reduced.
- 12 weeks: buccal crest **>2 mm apical** of marginal SLA border.
- Lingual wall: less vertical resorption (thicker, less bundle-bone dependence).
- Clinical implication: BIC achieved during early healing is partially lost where the buccal wall is thin.
