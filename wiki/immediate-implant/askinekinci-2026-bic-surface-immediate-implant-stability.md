## One-line Summary

Pilot RCT (n=28; immediate+graft vs immediate no-graft vs healed) finding grafting does not improve immediate-implant primary stability, healed sites are significantly higher (insertion torque P=0.007, intraoperative ISQ P=0.014), and virtual bone-implant contact (BIC) correlates with early ISQ only in the no-graft immediate group (intraop r=0.62 P=0.03; ISQ-60 r=0.58 P=0.05).

## 한줄요약

파일럿 무작위대조시험 (Randomized Controlled Trial, RCT, n=28): 즉시식립 시 골이식은 초기 안정성을 높이지 않고 (치유부 골 부위가 유의하게 우월, 식립토크 P=0.007, 술중 ISQ P=0.014), 가상 골-임플란트 접촉면적 (virtual Bone-Implant Contact surface, BIC)은 비이식 즉시식립군에서만 초기 임플란트 안정성 지수 (Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ)와 상관 (술중 r=0.62; 60일 r=0.58).

## Summary

This single-center pilot RCT used a novel digital workflow — preoperative CBCT segmentation superimposed on postoperative intraoral scanning with scanbodies — to estimate virtual bone-implant contact surface area (BIC) and non-contact area (BINC) for identical macro-design implants placed under three conditions: immediate placement with grafting (Group I), immediate placement without grafting (Group II), and conventional placement in healed sites (Group III). Of 30 patients enrolled, 28 (single missing upper premolar/molar) were analyzed. Healed sites achieved significantly greater primary stability (insertion torque and intraoperative ISQ) than the immediate groups, while grafting the residual peri-implant gap conferred no primary-stability advantage over leaving it ungrafted. Crucially, virtual BIC predicted early mechanical stability (torque, intraoperative ISQ, ISQ-60) only in the ungrafted immediate group, indicating that native apical bone engagement drives early stability and that graft placement decouples ISQ from native BIC.

## Key Contributions

- Validates a **guide-free non-invasive workflow** (CBCT segmentation + postoperative IOS, no surgical guide) to estimate virtual BIC clinically, avoiding CBCT metal-artifact distortion around the placed implant.
- Direct three-arm comparison with **identical implant macro-design/surface** isolates the bone-condition variable (fresh ± graft vs healed).
- Shows **grafting the gap does not improve early mechanical stability** of immediate implants — a clinically actionable null result for the primary-stability question (graft is justified by dimensional/esthetic, not initial-stability, goals).
- BIC predicts early ISQ **only without graft**, clarifying that virtual BIC is a meaningful early-stability indicator specifically in ungrafted immediate placement.

## Methodology

Pilot RCT (CONSORT), n=30 enrolled / 28 analyzed (10M/18F, mean age 42 y), single missing upper premolar/molar with ≥4 mm apical bone and intact socket walls (or healed site ≥6 mo for Group III). Heavy smokers, untreated periodontitis, acute infection, and systemic contraindications excluded. Sealed-envelope randomization revealed after extraction; analysts blinded. All implants (aggressive-thread tapered screw-type Ti, SLA surface, DXL GmbH) placed freehand by one surgeon with identical drilling sequence, drill extended 2–3 mm apically, seated at 40 Ncm/20 rpm then torque ratchet. Group I gap filled with bovine xenograft (Cerabone) + collagen membrane; Group II ungrafted; Group III standard. Outcomes: insertion torque (ratchet), ISQ (Osstell RFA, ≥3 repeats, highest value) at intraoperative/60 d/90 d, and BIC/BINC via Slicer segmentation → Meshmixer surface-area math after CBCT–IOS registration. Stats: Shapiro–Wilk; unpaired t/Mann–Whitney; one-way ANOVA/Friedman with Dunn post hoc; Pearson/Spearman correlation; G-Power sample size (alpha 0.05, effect size 0.82, power 95% → 27, expanded to 30).

## Results

- Insertion torque (Ncm): GI 32.0±4.2, GII 36.3±7.1, GIII 41.1±6.8 — GIII > GI (P=0.007); GI vs GII NS.
- Intraoperative ISQ: GI 65.0±7.6, GII 62.0±10.0, GIII 71.6±6.2 — GIII > GII (P=0.014); GI vs GII NS.
- BIC (mm²): GI 206.2±30.3, GII 206.8±48.2, GIII 302.2±64.5 — GIII > GI (P=0.003), GIII > GII (P=0.002); GI vs GII NS. BINC: GI 101.0±24.6, GII 106.6±22.9, GIII NA.
- BIC correlations significant only in Group II (no graft): insertion torque r=0.66 (P=0.02), intraoperative ISQ r=0.62 (P=0.03), ISQ-60 r=0.58 (P=0.05). NS in Groups I and III.
- ISQ-90 NS in every subgroup; pooled across all implants BIC–ISQ90 r=0.44 (P=0.01, moderate).
- Stability rose over time in all groups irrespective of grafting (secondary stability), consistent with osseointegration; per-group n (~9–10) underpowers subgroup correlations and the short 60/90-day follow-up captures only early healing.
