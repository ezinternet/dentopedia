## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 5 RCTs (245 subjects; 4 pooled) finding that connective tissue grafting at immediate implant placement produces a non-significant but favorable trend in buccal gingival level (MD 0.09 mm, 95% CI −0.54 to 0.72, p = 0.05), concluding CTG helps maintain — but not increase — the gingival level for esthetic success.

## 한줄요약

5개 RCT(245명; 4편 합성) SR+MA — 즉시식립 시 결합조직이식(CTG)은 협측 치은 수준에 유의하지 않으나 유리한 경향(MD 0.09 mm, p = 0.05)을 보였고, CTG가 치은 수준을 '유지'시켜 심미 성공에 도움된다는 결론.

## Summary

This RCT-only meta-analysis (5 RCTs, 245 subjects; 4 pooled) evaluated connective tissue grafting (CTG) — simultaneous or deferred — at immediate implant placement in the esthetic zone. The pooled buccal gingival level difference favored CTG but was not statistically significant. The authors conclude that CTG helps maintain the gingival margin level rather than increase soft-tissue volume, and is favorable for esthetic outcomes when immediate placement with a provisional restoration is planned. The wide confidence interval reflects a small, heterogeneous evidence base.

## Key Contributions

- RCT-only synthesis of CTG at immediate implants.
- Frames CTG as level-maintaining rather than volume-increasing.

## Methodology

- SR+MA; PubMed/Scopus/Cochrane; PRISMA; RCTs 2017–2024, esthetic zone, ≥6-mo follow-up.

## Results

- 5 RCTs (n=245); 4 pooled.
- Buccal gingival level: MD 0.09 mm (−0.54 to 0.72, p=0.05) — NS, favorable trend.
