## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 4 RCTs (n=82 implants) found that pre-extractive inter-radicular site preparation for mandibular molar immediate implant placement produced significantly higher ISQ at placement (MD +3.39, p<.0001, I²=0%) and near-perfect central positioning (RR 0.05, p=.002) vs postextractive approach; ISQ advantage converged by 3–6 months and overall evidence certainty was low to very low.

## Summary

Based on an article retrieved from PubMed ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.identj.2025.109316)), this SR+MA evaluated anatomically guided pre-extractive implant site preparation for mandibular molar immediate implant placement. The technique uses intact retained roots as a natural guide to direct drilling through the furcation zone before root extraction, engaging the inter-radicular septum and centering the implant optimally.

Four RCTs (82 participants, 82 implants; 41 per group) met inclusion criteria from 120 initially retrieved studies. Studies were predominantly from Egypt (3/4) with one from Italy. Follow-up ranged from 6 to 12 months.

**Primary finding**: Pre-extractive site preparation significantly increased ISQ at the time of implant placement (MD −3.39; 95% CI −4.20 to −2.59; p < .0001; I² = 0%). This early stability advantage was not sustained at 3 months (p = .17) or 6 months (p = .29, I² = 83%).

**Positional accuracy**: 100% of pre-extractive implants were centrally positioned in the inter-radicular bone, compared to only ~10% in the postextractive group (RR 0.05; 95% CI 0.01–0.33; p = .002). In the postextractive group, 83% of implants showed mesial or distal tilt.

**Marginal bone level**: No significant differences at 6 months. At 12 months, a marginally significant advantage favoured the pre-extractive group in bucco-lingual MBL (MD −0.62; p = .04).

**Survival**: Zero implant failures in both groups throughout follow-up.

**GRADE certainty**: Low for primary stability and positioning; very low for MBL and 6-month stability.

## Key Contributions

1. First SR+MA restricted to RCTs comparing these two timing strategies for mandibular molar site preparation.
2. Confirmed that using retained roots as a drilling guide significantly improves primary ISQ and implant centration.
3. ISQ convergence at 3–6 months suggests that initial stability advantage does not necessarily translate to long-term differential — osseointegration quality appears comparable once healing occurs.
4. Establishes that prosthetically optimal implant positioning (central, not tilted) is reliably achievable only with pre-extractive guidance.
5. Provides rationale for pre-extractive technique as adjunct to (not replacement for) surgical templates.

## Methodology

**Review design**: PRISMA/Cochrane SR+MA; PROSPERO CRD42025645764.

**PICOS**:
- P: Adults ≥18 with hopeless mandibular molar, ≥6 mm bucco-lingual width, ≥1 mm buccal cortical bone, intact socket walls
- I: Pre-extractive site preparation (drill through furcation of retained roots before extraction)
- C: Postextractive site preparation (conventional drilling after extraction)
- O: ISQ (primary); MBL, implant position, failure rate (secondary)
- S: RCTs only

**Search**: CENTRAL, MEDLINE, EMBASE, ClinicalTrials.gov through February 2025.

**Statistical approach**: Random-effects model; MD ± 95% CI; RR for positional outcomes; I² for heterogeneity; leave-one-out sensitivity analysis; GRADE.

**Risk of bias (RoB2)**: 3/4 high risk (outcome assessor blinding absent, inadequate allocation concealment); 1/4 some concerns.

**Surgical protocol common elements**: Full mucoperiosteal flap; coronectomy (Lindermann/diamond/fissure bur); CBCT-guided planning; implant 1–2 mm apical to buccal crest; GBR with deproteinised bovine bone mineral; Osstell Mentor for ISQ.

## Results

| Outcome | Time point | MD / RR | 95% CI | p | I² |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ISQ | Placement | MD −3.39 | −4.20 to −2.59 | < .0001 | 0% |
| ISQ | 3 months | MD −1.90 | −4.63 to 0.83 | .17 | — |
| ISQ | 6 months | MD −2.72 | −7.72 to 2.29 | .29 | 83% |
| MBL bucco-lingual | 6 months | MD −0.05 | −0.32 to 0.21 | .70 | 71% |
| MBL bucco-lingual | 12 months | MD −0.62 | −1.62 to −0.01 | .04 | — |
| Central positioning | — | RR 0.05 | 0.01–0.33 | .002 | 0% |
| Implant failure | — | 0/82 both groups | — | — | — |

*Negative MD convention: pre-extractive had higher ISQ (favorable direction).*

**Sensitivity analysis**: Excluding any single study yielded pooled MD range −3.55 to −3.33 for primary ISQ; I² remained 0%–14%. Findings robust.

**Evidence certainty (GRADE)**:
- Primary stability at placement: Low
- Implant positioning: Low
- MBL at 6 months: Very low
- Stability at 6 months: Very low
