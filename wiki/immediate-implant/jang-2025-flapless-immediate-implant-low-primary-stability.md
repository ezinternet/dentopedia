## One-line Summary

Retrospective case series (16 flapless immediate SLA implants with low primary stability, insertion torque <10 Ncm and mean initial ISQ <55) — mean ISQ rose from 42.2 to 68.7 and survival was 100% at mean 65.18 months, indicating low/no primary stability is not an absolute contraindication for immediate placement.

## Summary

This single-center retrospective case series asks a narrow but practical question that most immediate-implant literature sidesteps: what happens to immediately placed implants that have **low or even no measurable primary stability** at insertion? Sixteen SLA-surfaced implants were placed flapless in 16 patients (mean age 48.8), all meeting a strict low-stability definition — insertion torque value (ITV) <10 Ncm **and** mean initial Implant Stability Quotient (ISQ) <55. Six implants spun at placement and gave no measurable ISQ at all.

Despite these unfavorable starting conditions, every implant osseointegrated. Mean ISQ climbed from 42.2 ± 7.5 at placement to 68.7 ± 7.6 by impression-taking (~13 weeks later, p<0.01), and survival was 100% over a mean follow-up of 65.18 months. The authors frame this through the primary→secondary stability transition and the "stability dip": with adequate undermovement control (undersized drilling, bone grafting in all but one case, sometimes extended healing), biological secondary stability fully rescued implants that mechanical primary stability did not provide. The clinical message is that the **rising trajectory of ISQ matters more than the absolute initial value**, and that low primary stability — long treated as a near-contraindication for immediate placement — may be a manageable condition rather than a hard stop.

## Key Contributions

- Fills a literature gap: prognosis of **immediately** placed implants with **low/no** primary stability (vs the well-studied high-stability immediates or low-stability delayed cases).
- 100% survival at mean 65.18 months (range 12–128) despite all implants having ITV <10 Ncm and mean initial ISQ <55.
- Documents large, significant ISQ gain (42.2 → 68.7; 95% CI of difference 17.2–32.9; large effect size).
- Reinforces "ISQ trend > absolute initial ISQ" as the clinically meaningful signal of osseointegration.
- Practical tactics for the low-stability immediate case: one-step undersized drilling, near-universal bone grafting, and willingness to extend healing (6–14 extra weeks) when ISQ lags.

## Methodology

- **Design**: retrospective chart review, single surgeon, flapless (no flap elevation); implants placed Apr 2014–May 2022, Inje University Sanggye Paik Hospital (IRB 2024-12-017).
- **Cohort**: 16 SLA implants in 16 patients (7 M / 9 F; mean age 48.8). Sites — 13 maxilla, 3 mandible; 6 maxillary anterior, 2 maxillary premolar, 5 maxillary molar, 3 mandibular molar. Extraction causes: caries, fracture, apical disease, periodontal disease.
- **Low primary stability**: ITV <10 Ncm AND mean initial ISQ <55.
- **Surgical modification**: one-step undersized drilling; bone grafting in all but one case; submerged protocol in one patient only.
- **Stability**: Osstell ISQ (RFA, 3rd-gen) in four directions (M/D/B/L) at placement and at impression; spinning/unmeasurable cases treated as missing.
- **MBL**: panoramic radiographs (prosthesis delivery and final follow-up), implant shoulder to crestal bone at mesial/distal.
- **Analysis**: Wilcoxon signed-rank test (SPSS v25).

## Results

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Implants / patients | 16 / 16 |
| Initial ISQ (mean ± SD) | 42.2 ± 7.5 |
| Final ISQ (mean ± SD) | 68.7 ± 7.6 (p<0.01) |
| 95% CI of ISQ difference | 17.2–32.9 (large effect size) |
| Initial ISQ <50 | 8 implants (mean 39.6) |
| Initial ISQ ≥50 | 2 implants (mean 51.0) |
| Unmeasurable (spinning) | 6 implants |
| Time to impression | mean ~13 wk (range 8–25) |
| Survival | 100% |
| Mean follow-up | 65.18 months (range 12–128) |

- 6 of 16 implants reached ≈70+ ISQ by impression; the rest rose but had not yet reached target, still with favorable prognosis.
- Patients 3, 5, 10 needed extended healing (low ISQ of 48.25 / 26.5 etc.) — 6 to 14 additional weeks.
- MBL positive (loss) in most cases; 4 cases negative (slight gain, –0.1 to –0.3 mm).
- No clinical infection, inflammation, or bleeding at final follow-up.
- 2 patients with systemic disease (diabetes, hypertension, prior MI) showed ISQ gains comparable to healthy patients — well-controlled diabetes did not impair secondary stability in this small set.
