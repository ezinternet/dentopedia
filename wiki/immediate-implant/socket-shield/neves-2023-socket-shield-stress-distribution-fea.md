## One-line Summary

Finite element analysis comparing socket shield (SS, 2.0-mm dentin fragment), heterologous bone graft (HBG), and a fully bone-embedded control (C) under oblique loading; both SS and HBG produced higher peri-implant bone stress than the control, with SS showing the highest stress concentration in peri-implant tissues.

## Summary

This finite element analysis modeled a maxillary central incisor implant crown under combined oblique loads and compared three buccal-wall conditions: a 2.0-mm socket-shield dentin fragment, a heterologous bone graft, and a control implant fully embedded in healed bone. Both the socket shield and bone graft transmitted higher stress to peri-implant bone than the control, and the socket shield produced the highest peri-implant stress concentration. The study adds a biomechanical caution to the otherwise favorable clinical/esthetic socket-shield literature, though FEA stress does not directly equate to clinical failure.

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies peri-implant stress consequences of retaining a buccal root fragment.
- Provides a biomechanical counterpoint to clinical socket-shield outcome studies.

## Methodology

- 3D FEA; SS (2.0-mm dentin fragment) vs HBG (bovine graft) vs control; oblique loads 100 N lingual + 25.5 N incisal; tensile/shear/compressive stress, displacement, von Mises analyzed.

## Results

- SS and HBG bone stresses > control; SS highest peri-implant stress concentration.
- Minor differences in dentin fragment, graft, implant, and prosthetic components.
