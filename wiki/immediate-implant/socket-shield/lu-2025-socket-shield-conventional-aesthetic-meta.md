## One-line Summary

Meta-analysis (27 studies = 22 RCT + 5 NRSI, 1307 implants) finding the socket shield technique (SST) significantly reduces horizontal (MD −0.50 mm) and vertical (MD −0.56 mm) buccal bone loss and improves PES (+1.25) and ISQ (+5.83) vs conventional immediate implant placement (CIIP), with comparable implant success (RR 1.00) — the largest SST pool to date, confirming the established direction across shield-height/thickness/grafting subgroups.

## Summary

This 2025 meta-analysis is the **largest pooled comparison of SST vs CIIP in the esthetic zone to date** — 27 studies (22 RCTs, 5 NRSI), 1307 implants — and notably folds in 13 Chinese-language studies that English-only predecessors omitted. It does **not overturn** the existing wiki thesis ([[wiki/overviews/socket-shield-technique-overview]]): SST again wins on buccal-bone preservation, PES, and ISQ, with success rate indistinguishable from CIIP. Its added value is **confirmation at scale plus subgroup granularity** — SST's horizontal-bone-loss advantage held whether the shield was above, at, or below the crest; ≤ or >1.5 mm thick; and whether or not the gap was grafted.

The honest caveats are unchanged and material: heterogeneity is extreme (I²=90–97%) on the continuous outcomes, NRSI quality is low (two studies "High risk"), operator blinding is impossible, and there is still **no high-quality RCT beyond 5 years**. The single outlier where SST did not win (Abdullah 2022) is explained by a confounded design (implant in direct shield contact, grafting only in CIIP). Net clinical read: SST remains a technique-sensitive option for experienced operators in selected esthetic-zone cases — the buccal-bone/esthetic upside is now backed by the largest evidence pool, but standardized protocols and long-term data are still missing.

## Key Contributions

1. **Largest SST-vs-CIIP evidence pool (27 studies / 1307 implants)** — reinforces the established direction with the biggest sample, incorporating Chinese-language RCTs prior MAs missed.
2. **Quantified effects**: horizontal buccal bone loss MD −0.50 mm, vertical −0.56 mm, PES +1.25, ISQ +5.83, success RR 1.00 (I²=0%).
3. **Subgroup robustness**: SST horizontal-bone advantage persists across shield height (above −0.59 / at −0.46 / below −0.28), thickness (≤1.5 mm −0.55 / >1.5 mm −0.66), and grafting (grafted −0.59 / non-grafted −0.22).

## Methodology

- PRISMA SR+MA; PICOS = esthetic-zone IIP, SST vs CIIP, outcomes buccal bone loss (primary horizontal) / PES / ISQ / success; RCT + NRSI; ≥3-month follow-up.
- 7 databases (incl. CNKI/VIP/Wanfang) to 2024-10-09; 1043 → 27 included; RevMan 5.4, random-effects when I²>50%.
- RoB2 (RCT: 3 "Low", rest "Some concerns") + ROBINS-I (NRSI: 3 "Moderate", 2 "High"); sensitivity by excluding NRSI and inconsistent-grafting studies; funnel plots (low publication-bias signal).

## Results

| Outcome | Studies / implants | SST vs CIIP | I² |
|---|---|---|---|
| Horizontal buccal bone loss | 22 / 1155 | MD −0.50 [−0.60, −0.41] | 97% |
| Vertical buccal bone loss | 8 / 252 | MD −0.56 [−0.64, −0.48] | 78% |
| ISQ | 8 / 456 | MD +5.83 [4.08, 7.57] | 69% |
| PES | 21 / 1001 | MD +1.25 [0.93, 1.57] | 90% |
| Implant success | 18 / 883 | RR 1.00 [0.98, 1.02] | 0% |

Long-term (>12 mo) horizontal subgroup MD −0.62; only Abdullah 2022 (confounded) failed to show SST advantage. Complication frame (per Ogawa 2022): internal shield exposure most common (46%); most complications non-progressive, first-year onset, resolvable.
