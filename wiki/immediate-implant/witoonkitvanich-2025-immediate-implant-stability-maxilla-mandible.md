## One-line Summary

Controlled prospective non-randomized trial (n=30; 14 maxilla / 16 mandible) of computer-guided immediate molar implants: both arches reached primary ISQ >73; mandible trended higher throughout and was significantly higher at 24 weeks (81.47 vs 78.23, P=0.018), with a 2–4-week stability dip seen only in the maxilla.

## 한줄요약

대조 전향 비무작위 임상시험(n=30; 상악 14 / 하악 16): 컴퓨터 가이드(computer-guided) 즉시 대구치 임플란트에서 상·하악 모두 초기 임플란트 안정성 지수(Implant Stability Quotient, ISQ) >73, 하악이 전 기간 더 높고 24주에서 유의하게 높음(81.47 vs 78.23, P=0.018); 안정성 딥(stability dip)은 상악 2–4주에만 관찰되고 하악에는 없음.

## Summary

This Mahidol University trial asked whether immediate molar implants placed with computer-guided, tooth-supported surgery differ in stability between the maxilla (test, the "clinical challenge") and mandible (control, "standard"). Thirty patients with a single unrestorable molar received a SuperLine II tapered implant (Dentium) positioned into the inter-radicular septum/socket via a 3Shape-planned, tooth-supported guide; the gap was filled with alloplastic graft (Osteon III) and a customized titanium healing abutment placed. Stability was tracked by resonance frequency analysis (RFA; SmartPeg + Mega ISQ) from baseline to 24 weeks.

Both arches achieved high primary stability (mean ISQ >73 at baseline, no significant difference, P=0.864), showing that computer-guided placement engaging septal bone delivers >70 primary ISQ even in challenging fresh molar sockets. Across follow-up, the mandible was higher at every time point and significantly higher at 24 weeks (81.47 vs 78.23, P=0.018), confirming the hypothesis of superior mandibular stability. Critically, the early stability dip (transition from mechanical to biological stability) was arch-dependent: the maxilla dropped significantly at 2 and 4 weeks (GEE b=−2.607, P=0.009; b=−2.054, P=0.024) before recovering, whereas the mandible never dipped and rose monotonically from baseline. No implants failed. The arch gap is partly confounded by longer implants and mixed molar positions in the mandible, and is mechanistically consistent with higher posterior-mandible bone density.

## Key Contributions

- Direct maxilla-vs-mandible ISQ comparison for **immediate molar** implants under a computer-guided, tooth-supported protocol over 24 weeks.
- Demonstrates **primary ISQ >73 in both arches** for fresh molar sockets via septal-bone engagement and guided planning.
- Shows the **stability dip is arch-specific** — present in the maxilla (2–4 wk), absent in the mandible.
- Confirms **mandibular molar implants are more stable**, statistically significant at 24 weeks (P=0.018), consistent with higher mandibular bone density.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Single-center controlled, prospective, non-randomized clinical trial (CONSORT 2010 reported). Maxilla = test, mandible = control.
- **Sample**: 30 patients (14 maxilla, 16 mandible), single unrestorable molar, intact buccal/lingual socket walls; insertion torque ≥10 N·cm required.
- **Workflow**: i500 IOS (STL) + CBCT (DICOM) merged in 3Shape Implant Studio; prosthetically driven, tooth-supported digital surgical guide; SuperLine II bone-level tapered implant (Dentium); Osteon III alloplastic graft; customized titanium healing abutment; one operator.
- **Stability**: RFA with SmartPeg type 7 (Osstell) + Mega ISQ monitor (Megagen), four directions, three repeats per visit, at baseline and 1/2/4/6/8/10/12/24 weeks.
- **Statistics**: Independent t-test (between-arch per time point), GEE model (within-arch vs baseline), Fisher's exact test (demographics); P<0.05.

## Results

- **Primary stability (baseline)**: Maxilla 73.98 ± 5.40, Mandible 73.59 ± 6.74; P=0.864 (NS). Both >70.

| Time | Maxilla ISQ | Mandible ISQ | Between-arch P |
|---|---|---|---|
| Baseline | 73.98 ± 5.40 | 73.59 ± 6.74 | 0.864 |
| 2 wk | 71.38 ± 8.39 | 74.25 ± 6.81 | 0.309 |
| 4 wk | 71.93 ± 6.73 | 74.77 ± 6.57 | 0.253 |
| 6 wk | 74.32 ± 5.47 | 77.06 ± 5.44 | 0.181 |
| 12 wk | 77.09 ± 3.66 | 79.92 ± 4.38 | 0.067 |
| 24 wk | 78.23 ± 3.73 | 81.47 ± 3.32 | **0.018 \*** |

- **Mandible higher at every time point**; significant only at 24 weeks.
- **GEE (vs baseline)**: Maxilla *dipped* at 2 wk (b=−2.607, P=0.009) and 4 wk (b=−2.054, P=0.024), then rose (significant from 10 wk). Mandible *never dipped*; significant rise from 6 wk onward (24 wk b=+7.875, P<0.001).
- **Stability dip** in maxilla only (2 weeks); none in mandible — attributed to rigid tooth-supported guide + posterior-mandible bone density.
- **No implant failures**; all osseointegrated, one-stage protocol.
- **Confounders**: implant length (maxilla shorter, P=0.011) and tooth position (maxilla 100% first molar vs mandible 56% first/44% second, P=0.007) differed significantly between arches; longer mandibular implants cited as a contributing reason for higher stability. Cited bone density: posterior mandible 674 HU vs maxilla 455 HU (Turkyilmaz).
