즉시식립 (Immediate Implant Placement, IIP) 라틴아메리카 임상·동물 연구 리뷰: 즉시식립은 골유착·생존율은 우수하나 발치 후 치조제 흡수 자체는 막지 못하며, 연조직·경조직 그라프트 동반 시 협측 함몰을 일부 보완할 수 있다.

## Summary
Periodontology 2000 narrative review (Araújo et al. 2019) compiling Latin-American clinical and dog-model studies on socket healing with vs. without immediate implant placement. Across two decades of work the central conclusion is consistent: placing an implant into a fresh extraction socket does not arrest the biological remodelling of the alveolar ridge. The buccal bundle bone — being part of the periodontal attachment apparatus — resorbs after extraction whether or not an implant is present, producing horizontal and vertical contour loss especially on the buccal aspect. Immediate implants nonetheless show high osseointegration and 1- to 3-year survival rates (>95%) when primary stability is achieved in apical bone. Adjunctive procedures (xenograft fill of the buccal gap, connective tissue graft, immediate provisionalization) reduce but do not abolish ridge collapse. The review reframes immediate placement as a restorative, not preservative, technique.

## Key Contributions
- Reaffirms that the buccal bundle bone, not the implant, governs post-extraction ridge resorption.
- Synthesises Latin-American evidence largely overlooked in European/North-American reviews.
- Frames augmentation (gap grafting, CTG, flapless approach) as compensatory rather than preventive.

## Methodology
- Electronic search of MEDLINE, Scopus, SciELO, Lilacs, Embase.
- Inclusion required Latin-American institutional authorship and clinical or histological data on socket healing with/without immediate implant.
- Animal (dog) and human clinical studies pooled; narrative synthesis, no meta-analysis.

## Results
- Immediate implant survival: typically >95% short-term; comparable to staged placement.
- Bundle bone resorption: 1.5–2 mm vertical buccal loss at ~12 weeks in dog model regardless of implant.
- Horizontal ridge loss reduced ~30–50% by xenograft-filled buccal gap but not eliminated.
- CTG at time of placement improves mid-facial mucosal level stability over 1–3 years.
- No prevention of ridge modeling by implant placement alone.
