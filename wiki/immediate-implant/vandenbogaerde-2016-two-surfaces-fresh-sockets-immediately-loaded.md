## One-line Summary

Randomized within-patient case-series (11 patients, 22 implants — 11 bimodal control vs 11 proactive hydrophilic/rough test, Neoss) placed in fresh maxillary sockets and immediately loaded: both surfaces showed a ~2-week ISQ dip, but the proactive test implant dipped less and recovered to significantly higher stability at 12 weeks (test +3.5±4.6 vs control −0.3±5.6 ISQ from baseline; p=0.021), with 95.5% overall survival.

## Summary

This split-mouth randomized case-series isolates **implant surface** as the single variable in the highest-risk implant scenario: fresh maxillary extraction socket + same-day immediate loading. Each of 11 patients received one **bimodal** (minimally rough control, Sa ≈ 0.6 µm) and one **proactive** (moderately rough + hydrophilic test, Sa ≈ 1.0 µm) Neoss implant in two adjacent sockets, randomized by sealed envelope, both restored immediately with a screw-retained provisional out of occlusion. Stability was tracked by RFA (Osstell ISQ) at baseline, 2, 4, 6, and 12 weeks.

Both surfaces had **equal insertion torque** (control 38.0±11.4 vs test 39.5±9.1 Ncm, NS) and equal placement ISQ. The key finding is in the **stability-dip dynamics**: both implants dipped at ~2 weeks then recovered, but the **dip was deeper for the bimodal control** and the **proactive test recovered faster to a significantly higher 12-week stability**. From baseline to 12 weeks the test gained +3.5±4.6 ISQ while the control lost −0.3±5.6 ISQ (p=0.021). The advantage was significant in the buccopalatal direction and in pooled 2–12-week data, but **not in the mesiodistal direction**. Marginal bone loss was equivalent between surfaces (≈0.4–0.6 mm at 3 years, NS).

Clinically: when an immediate-loading protocol drives a fresh-socket implant through its stability nadir, a **moderately rough + hydrophilic surface buffers the dip and accelerates secondary-stability gain** — favoring such surfaces for high-risk immediate scenarios. The single failure (95.5% survival) was a proactive implant with the lowest IT (20 Ncm) and lowest baseline ISQ, grafted with a synthetic substitute over a tooth with a periapical lesion — a confluence of low primary stability and adverse local conditions rather than a surface verdict.

## Key Contributions

- **Surface variable isolated within-patient**: identical macrodesign, only topography/wettability differs, removing patient/site confounders.
- **High-resolution ISQ trajectory** through the critical 2-week dip under immediate loading.
- **Hydrophilic/rough surface dips less and recovers faster** to significantly higher 12-week stability — direct clinical support for surface choice in immediate-loading fresh-socket cases.
- Surface advantage shows in **BP (coronal/buccal) but not MD**, consistent with buccal-gap bone fill driving the coronal RFA signal.
- Equivalent marginal bone loss between surfaces to 3 years; repeated provisional removal for RFA did not increase bone loss.

## Methodology

- **Design**: split-mouth randomized case-series; 11 patients, 22 maxillary implants (incisor/canine/bicuspid sites), one bimodal + one proactive each, envelope-randomized.
- **Protocol**: atraumatic piezo/periotome extraction, buccal plate preserved, palatal-biased placement leaving a buccal gap; gaps <1 mm left untreated (n=9), wider gaps grafted (12 autologous chips, 1 synthetic NanoBone), no membranes; same-day screw-retained provisional **out of occlusion**.
- **Measures**: peak insertion torque at placement; **RFA (Osstell ISQ)** in MD and BP at baseline, 2, 4, 6, 12 weeks; standardized Rinn radiographs read by independent radiologist (3-year follow-up).
- **Statistics**: Wilcoxon matched-pair signed-rank test (paired design) per time point + pooled 2–12-week ISQ; p ≤ 0.05.

## Results

| Outcome | Bimodal (control) | Proactive (test) | Sig. |
|---|---|---|---|
| Insertion torque (Ncm) | 38.0 ± 11.4 (20–50) | 39.5 ± 9.1 (25–50) | NS |
| 2-week ISQ dip | more pronounced | less pronounced | — |
| ΔISQ baseline→12 wk (mean) | −0.3 ± 5.6 | +3.5 ± 4.6 | p=0.021 |
| 12-wk ISQ, BP direction | lower | higher | p=0.021 |
| ISQ pooled 2–12 wk | lower | higher | p=0.043 |
| ISQ, MD direction | — | — | NS |
| Marginal bone loss @ 3 y (mm) | 0.4 ± 0.7 | 0.6 ± 0.5 | NS |
| Survival | 11/11 | 10/11 | overall 95.5% |

- **Failure**: 1 proactive implant removed at 6 weeks — lowest IT (20 Ncm), lowest baseline ISQ (46–51), synthetic-graft defect, periapical lesion on the extracted tooth.
- Both surfaces recovered stability from the ~2-week nadir to 12 weeks; the test surface's faster recovery yielded a net stability **gain** vs a net **loss/plateau** for control.
