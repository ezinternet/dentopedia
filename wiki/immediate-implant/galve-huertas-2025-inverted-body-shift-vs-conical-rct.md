## One-line Summary

RCT (n=30, 1-year) at UIC comparing inverted body-shift (Inverta co-axis 12°) vs conventional conical implants in immediate anterior maxillary placement: 100% survival, ISQ medians 56–62, comparable marginal bone loss and PES esthetics, no statistically significant intergroup differences.

## 한줄요약

전향적 단일맹검 RCT (n=30, 1년, UIC): 역전형 body-shift (co-axis 12°) vs 통상 conical 임플란트를 전상악 즉시식립에서 비교 — 생존율 100%, ISQ 중앙값 56–62, 수평·수직 골흡수 및 PES 미학적 결과 모두 군간 유의차 없음; inverted 디자인은 중앙 소켓 위치·팔라탈 스크류 접근 실용적 이점 제공.

## Summary

This single-blind RCT from Universitat Internacional de Catalunya enrolled 30 patients for immediate anterior maxillary implant placement (central/lateral incisors, canines, premolars) with immediate provisionalization, comparing the Inverta co-axis 12° inverted body-shift implant (test, n=15) against a conventional internal conical implant (control, n=15). At 1-year follow-up, survival was 100% in both groups. Overall success (Misch criteria, <2mm MBL) was 83.3%: 93.3% control vs 73.3% test (p=0.327, non-significant). Primary stability (IT, ISQ), CBCT-volumetric horizontal and vertical marginal bone loss, and PES esthetic scores were all statistically equivalent between groups. The inverted body-shift design achieved marginally higher median ISQ (62 vs 56–60) and IT (42 vs 35 Ncm), without reaching significance. Slight buccal resorption was greater in the test group; palatal bone preservation was better in the test group. Aesthetic results were comparable (median PES 11–12 in both groups).

## Key Contributions

1. **Quantitative ISQ/IT benchmark for Inverta co-axis 12°** in IIP context: ISQ median 62.0 (buccal and palatal), IT median 42.0 Ncm — sufficient for immediate loading per the ≥62 threshold.
2. **CBCT-volumetric MBL characterization:** horizontal loss concentrated at 1mm from platform (-0.83mm global), diminishing at 3mm (-0.45mm) and 5mm (-0.25mm); vertical buccal loss slightly greater with inverted design, palatal loss slightly greater with conventional design.
3. **Non-inferiority of novel design:** No statistically significant differences in any outcome vs conventional conical implant at 1 year.
4. **Practical advantage delineation:** Central socket positioning in inverted design allows 2–3mm buccal gap for graft without compressing coronal bone; 12° angled platform enables palatal screw access without compromising emergence profile.
5. Confirms ISQ ≥62 as a practical threshold for immediate provisionalization in IIP: 40% test vs 20% control achieved immediate aesthetic provisional (p=0.426, ns).

## Methodology

- **Design:** Single-blind RCT; computer-generated randomization with sealed opaque envelopes
- **Setting:** UIC dental university clinic, Spain; six postgraduate oral surgery students as operators
- **Inclusion:** ASA-I, ≥18y, maxillary anterior single tooth for extraction, Elian type I socket (intact buccal plate, no recession), adequate bone for primary stability
- **Test implant:** Inverta co-axis 12° (inverted body-shift): 3.5mm coronal diameter, widens to 4.5mm at middle third, 12° angled platform, central socket positioning
- **Control implant:** Conventional internal conical implant, slightly palatal positioning
- **Protocol:** Atraumatic extraction (no flap), buccal gap ≥2mm filled with Bio-Oss®, subepithelial CTG from tuberosity/palate; immediate screw-retained provisional if ISQ ≥62
- **Bone assessment:** Three CBCT time points (T0 pre-op, T1 post-op, T2 1-year) with LimaGuide volumetric superposition via individual bite fork; horizontal measurements at 1/3/5mm; vertical at buccal/palatal peaks
- **Stability:** Penguin RFA device; SmartPeg at 5Ncm; buccal 90° and palatal directions; highest value recorded
- **Aesthetics:** PES (Pink Esthetic Score) at 1 year, 7 parameters, 0–14 scale, vs contralateral reference tooth

## Results

### Implant Survival and Success (1 Year)

| Outcome | Control (n=15) | Test (n=15) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Survival | 100% | 100% | — |
| Success (<2mm MBL) | 93.3% | 73.3% | 0.327 (ns) |
| Satisfactory survival (2–4mm MBL) | 6.7% | 26.7% | — |

### Primary Stability

| Measure | Control (median) | Test (median) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Insertion torque (Ncm) | 35.0 (IQR 25–40; range 11–45) | 42.0 (IQR 27–55; range 16–60) | 0.074 (ns) |
| ISQ buccal | 56.0 (IQR 50–71) | 62.0 (IQR 60–64) | ns |
| ISQ palatal | 60.0 (IQR 51–71) | 62.0 (IQR 60–66) | ns |
| Immediate provisional rate | 20% | 40% | 0.426 (ns) |

### Horizontal Bone Loss (T0→T2, CBCT volumetric)

| Level | Global median | Control | Test | Time p | Intergroup p |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1mm from platform | -0.83mm | -0.19mm | -0.97mm | <0.001 | 0.208 (ns) |
| 3mm | -0.45mm | -0.08mm | -0.53mm | 0.035 | 0.400 (ns) |
| 5mm | -0.25mm | -0.29mm | -0.20mm | 0.070 | 0.492 (ns) |

### Vertical Bone Loss (T1→T2)

| Region | Global median | Control | Test | Intergroup p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Buccal | -0.58mm | -0.38mm | -0.86mm | 0.124 (ns) |
| Palatal | -0.59mm | -0.74mm | -0.34mm | 0.533 (ns) |

*Buccal loss slightly greater in test (central positioning leaves smaller buccal graft space); palatal loss slightly greater in control (palatal positioning). Neither difference statistically significant.*

### Esthetics (PES at 1 Year)

| Group | Median PES | Mean (95% CI) | Range |
|---|---|---|---|
| Control | 12 (IQR 10–12) | 11.4 (10.38–12.42) | 7–14 |
| Test | 11 (IQR 9–13) | 11.2 (10.13–12.27) | 9–14 |

No significant associations between PES and any mechanical/dimensional parameter (all p>0.05).
