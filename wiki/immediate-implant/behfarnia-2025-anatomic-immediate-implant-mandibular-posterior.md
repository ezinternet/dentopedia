## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 12 CBCT studies (1,834 patients): pooled root-apex-to-inferior-alveolar-canal distance in the posterior mandible is 5.14 mm (2nd premolar), 6.32 mm (1st molar) and 4.65 mm (2nd molar), with undercut (U-type) ridge morphology dominating the 2nd molar site (73%) and driving lingual plate perforation risk during immediate implant placement.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

This systematic review and meta-analysis (PRISMA, 12 CBCT studies, 1,834 patients) quantifies the anatomical limits of immediate implant placement (IIP) in the mandibular posterior region. The two governing risks are proximity to the inferior alveolar canal (IAC) and lingual plate perforation (LPP) in an undercut ridge. Pooled root-apex-to-IAC distance is shortest at the second molar (4.65 mm) — paradoxically closer than the second premolar (5.14 mm) and first molar (6.32 mm) — making the second molar the highest-risk site for inferior alveolar nerve (IAN) injury. The second molar is also where undercut (U-type) ridge morphology dominates (73%), so the same site that is closest to the nerve is also the most prone to lingual cortical perforation. The clinical message is a ≥ 2 mm IAC safety margin plus mandatory preoperative CBCT assessment of cross-sectional ridge morphology before posterior mandibular IIP.

## Key Contributions

- First SR+MA to pool CBCT root-apex-to-IAC distances across the full posterior mandibular sextant, giving site-specific safety numbers.
- Identifies the second molar as a double-risk site: closest to the IAC (4.65 mm) AND most undercut (73% U-type).
- Maps ridge morphology (Chen C/P/U) prevalence to reported LPP rates (Yahya 70%/76% for 1M/2M; LPP probability −34% per 1 mm extra root-to-canal distance).
- Flags sex/age modifiers: females and patients < 40 years have shorter root-to-IAC distances and higher IAN injury risk.

## Methodology

Design: systematic review + random-effects meta-analysis (Comprehensive Meta-Analysis V3). Databases: PubMed, Cochrane, Web of Science, ProQuest, Scopus + manual search; English, 2006–Oct 2024. Screening 1,151 → 933 unique → 21 full-text → 12 included (9 cross-sectional, 3 retrospective); kappa 0.91. Quality: Modified STROBE (12 items); all 12 studies scored 11–12 (mean 11.42), low risk of bias. Outcomes: primary = mean root-apex-to-IAC distance (mm) + high-risk IAN proportion; secondary = lingual undercut and mandibular cross-sectional morphology. Heterogeneity by I²; publication bias by funnel plot, Egger's, Begg's.

## Results

Pooled mean root-apex-to-IAC distance (random-effects, all p < 0.001):
- 2nd premolar: **5.146 mm** (95% CI 3.832–6.460; 4 studies; I² = 97.1%)
- 1st molar: **6.329 mm** (95% CI 4.477–8.182; 3 studies; I² = 98.0%)
- 2nd molar: **4.653 mm** (95% CI 4.057–5.249; 3 studies; I² = 81.1%) — shortest

Ridge morphology (Chen C/P/U), pooled event rates:
- 1st molar: C 29.5% / P 35% / **U 41.1%** (95% CI 27.6–56%)
- 2nd molar: C 9.0% / P 15.4% / **U 73%** (95% CI 62.5–81.4%)
- Combined: C 25% / P 25.2% / U 49.2%

Lingual plate perforation (LPP): highest in U-type cross-sections and posterior sites. Yahya 70% (1M) and 76% (2M), high-risk 18%/44%; Sun U-type 2M with 4.8 mm implant → 22.2%; Huang −34% LPP probability per +1 mm root-to-canal distance; Rameswaran low overall (1.2%) but rising with implant diameter.

Clinical thresholds: ≥ 2 mm safety margin from IAC; ≥ 4 mm native apical bone for primary stability; mandatory CBCT cross-sectional morphology assessment, especially at the second molar.

Caveats: extreme heterogeneity (I² up to 98%), few studies per tooth (3–4), funnel-plot asymmetry indicating possible publication bias; descriptive review without survival/failure outcomes.
