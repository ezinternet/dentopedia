## One-line Summary

RCT (n=50, healed maxillary anterior/premolar single-tooth sites): immediate loading (IL) vs delayed loading (DL) of single implants gave statistically equivalent 5-year survival (100% vs 95.8%), marginal bone loss (−0.50 vs −0.54 mm, p=0.782), papilla index, and PES/WES — the early DL papilla advantage disappears by 5 years and smoking, not loading protocol, drove bone loss (p=0.021).

## Summary

This 5-year prospective RCT (Malmö, Sweden; NCT02770846) is the long-term extension of a previously published 1-year trial, comparing **immediate loading (IL)** vs **delayed loading (DL)** of single-tooth implants in the maxillary aesthetic zone (teeth 15–25). Fifty patients with **healed** edentulous sites (≥4 months post-extraction, no grafting, gaps flanked by natural roots) were randomized; all IL implants required **≥30 Ncm insertion torque**. At 5 years the two protocols were **clinically and esthetically indistinguishable** across every outcome — survival, success, marginal bone loss, papilla index, PES, and WES.

The clinically important nuance: the original 1-year report had shown a **significant DL advantage in papilla fill** (distal papilla p=0.002 at definitive crown placement). By 5 years that gap had **closed** (complete bilateral papilla fill IL 33.3% vs DL 56.5%, p=0.147, NS), and **PES improved significantly between 1 and 5 years in both groups** (IL p=0.017, DL p=0.046) as soft tissue matured. The only significant predictor of bone loss was **smoking** (total MBL smokers −1.90 vs non-smokers −0.40 mm, p=0.021), and the single failure (DL, 3 months) was in a smoker. Conclusion: in healed aesthetic-zone single-tooth sites with adequate primary stability and adjacent natural roots, **IL is as safe as DL at 5 years**, so loading protocol can be chosen on patient preference for immediate function.

## Key Contributions

- **5-year equivalence of IL vs DL** for single-tooth maxillary implants on all clinical and aesthetic endpoints — long-term confirmation of short-term safety.
- **Time resolves the early papilla deficit**: the DL papilla advantage seen at 1 year is no longer significant at 5 years, evidence for progressive papilla regeneration around immediately loaded crowns.
- **Soft-tissue maturation raises PES over years** (significant 1→5 yr gain in both arms) while WES stays flat.
- **Smoking — not loading protocol — is the bone-loss driver** (p=0.021); reframes the risk conversation away from immediacy and toward patient factors.
- Very low attrition (4% at 5 years), independent blinded MBL/esthetic assessment.

## Methodology

- **Design**: prospective RCT, two parallel arms; sealed-envelope randomization; surgeon blinded; independent blinded examiner for MBL + esthetics.
- **Sample**: 62 recruited → 12 excluded → **50 randomized** (IL 25 / DL 25); one DL implant failed at 3 mo → 5-yr analysis IL n=24, DL n=23.
- **Inclusion**: ≥18 y, single-tooth gap maxillary 15–25, **healed bone** (≥4 mo post-extraction or agenesis). **Exclusion**: surgical contraindications, plaque >25%, need for grafting/augmentation.
- **Implants**: BioHorizons Tapered Internal (Laser-Lok collar), none subcrestal. **IL eligibility = ≥30 Ncm insertion torque.**
- **IL**: immediate screw-retained temporary in light centric, no eccentric contacts, 2 months → definitive. **DL**: two-stage, 4-month submerged healing → temporary (emergence shaping) → definitive.
- **Definitive crowns**: Ti-base + individually fabricated zirconia abutment, veneered; screw-retained preferred (cemented only when access compromised esthetics).
- **Outcomes**: follow-up 3/6/12 mo + 5 yr. Survival; success (Albrektsson MBL criteria); MBL (ImageJ on long-cone PA radiographs, calibrated to 1.00 mm thread pitch, mesial+distal mean); Jemt papilla index; PES (Fürhauser) and WES (Belser).
- **Statistics**: SPSS v26; Student's t / Mann–Whitney, chi-square / Fisher, Wilcoxon signed-rank; p<0.05.

## Results

**Survival / success (5 yr)**: survival IL **100%** vs DL **95.8%**; success IL **91.7%** vs DL **83.3%**. Single failure = DL implant at 3 months (smoker).

**MBL (mm, negative = loss)**: 1 yr IL −0.57 ± 0.53 vs DL −0.70 ± 0.58 (p=0.430); **5 yr IL −0.50 ± 0.73 vs DL −0.54 ± 0.65 (p=0.782, NS)** — slight mean bone gain 1→5 yr. **Smoking**: total MBL smokers −1.90 ± 0.90 (n=7) vs non-smokers −0.40 ± 0.58 (n=40), **p=0.021**. No difference by sex or cement vs screw retention.

**Papilla index**: complete bilateral fill at 5 yr IL 33.3% vs DL 56.5% (p=0.147, NS). DL advantage significant only at definitive crown placement (distal p=0.002); all 1- and 5-yr comparisons NS.

**Aesthetics (5 yr)**: PES IL 11.00 ± 2.08 vs DL 11.17 ± 2.39 (p=0.501); WES IL 7.63 ± 1.53 vs DL 7.91 ± 1.53 (p=0.365) — all NS. **PES rose significantly 1→5 yr** (IL p=0.017, DL p=0.046); WES unchanged. Combined "almost perfect" outcome IL 20.8% / DL 30.4%; aesthetic failure IL 12.5% / DL 13.0%.

**Complications**: only 2 over the whole study — one DL technical (Ti-base retention loss, 52 mo); one IL crown replaced for esthetic reasons (non-technical, 47 mo). 5-yr peri-implant redness/edema + BOP 8.5% overall.

**Caveat**: applies to **healed** sites flanked by **natural roots** with ≥30 Ncm stability; no ISQ/RFA reported; single system/operator; small subgroups under-powered.
