## 한줄요약
즉시식립 3증례 케이스 레터로, 임플란트 나사산 (Thread) 최소 5개 이상이 원래 골에 접촉해야 1차 안정성 (Primary Stability)을 예측할 수 있다는 '5-TG' 가이드라인을 CBCT 가상 계획으로 적용함.

## Summary
This case letter introduces the Five Thread Guideline (5-TG), recommending that a minimum of five implant cylinder threads must engage native bone to predict adequate primary stability in immediate or healed implant placement. Three cases of immediate extraction and implant placement were planned using CBCT with SIMPLANT 17 software, where bone rendering was toggled off to visualize and count thread engagement virtually. Additional stability was assessed by "socket stabilization" — bi-socket (2 socket walls) or tri-socket (3 walls) engagement. All three cases achieved successful osseointegration and final restoration without complications.

## Key Contributions
- Proposes 5-TG as a pre-surgical CBCT-based decision tool for primary stability prediction
- Introduces "socket stabilization" as a supplementary stability concept (bi- or tri-socket wall engagement)
- Demonstrates software workflow: bone rendering off + segmented tooth layer allows thread-count visualization
- Provides a communication framework for sharing likelihood of surgical success with patients

## Methodology
- **Type**: Case letter (3 cases)
- **Software**: SIMPLANT 17 (Dentsply Sirona); CBCT + optical scan alignment; bone layer toggled off for thread visualization
- **Cases**:
  - Case 1 (#28, mandibular premolar): 3 apical threads + bi-socket → success
  - Case 2 (#19, mandibular molar): 5 apical threads + bi-socket → success
  - Case 3 (#14, maxillary molar): 5 apical threads + tri-socket → success
- **Surgical protocol**: Atraumatic extraction → immediate implant → gap grafting (mineralized cortical/cancellous chips) → healing abutment; no provisionalization in posterior cases
- **Follow-up**: 4–4.5 months; ISQ measured; screw-retained crowns placed

## Results
- All 3 cases: uneventful healing, successful osseointegration, successful crown delivery
- 5-TG correctly predicted good primary stability pre-surgically in all cases
- No complications reported in any case
