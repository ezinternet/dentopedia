## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 20 RCTs (848 patients, 916 sites) showing bone-substitute grafting of the immediate-implant gap reduces horizontal buccal bone resorption (MD −0.52 mm) and improves esthetic score (MD +1.49) but increases complications (RR 3.50), with no effect on implant failure (RR 0.92); GRADE certainty mostly moderate.

## Summary

This large RCT-based meta-analysis (20 trials, 848 patients, 916 sites) evaluated filling the fixture–socket gap with a bone-substitute material (BSM) versus no BSM at immediate implants. BSM significantly reduced horizontal buccal bone resorption and improved pink-esthetic outcomes, with no difference in implant failure. Notably, BSM use was associated with a 3.5-fold increase in overall complications (low-certainty), tempering an otherwise favorable hard-tissue/esthetic profile. GRADE certainty was moderate for most outcomes. This is a foundational reference for gap-grafting decisions at immediate implants.

## Key Contributions

- Largest RCT meta-analysis of gap bone-substitute grafting in immediate implants.
- Quantifies the buccal-bone and esthetic benefit and flags a complication trade-off.
- GRADE-rated certainty per outcome.

## Methodology

- SR+MA of RCTs; MEDLINE/EMBASE/CENTRAL; ROB 2.0; GRADE; random-effects (generic inverse variance).
- Primary: implant failure, complications, soft-tissue esthetics; secondary: vertical/horizontal buccal and interproximal bone resorption, mid-buccal recession.

## Results

- Implant failure: RR 0.92 (0.34–2.46) — NS.
- Horizontal buccal bone resorption: MD −0.52 mm (−0.74 to −0.30).
- Esthetic score: MD +1.49 (0.46–2.53).
- Complications: RR 3.50 (1.11–11.1).
