## One-line Summary

Cochrane SR+MA of 7 RCTs (2010) found no convincing advantage for any implant placement timing (immediate vs. immediate-delayed vs. delayed), with possible aesthetic benefit for early placement offset by higher complication risk.

## Summary

This Cochrane systematic review (pub3, 2010) evaluated whether immediate, immediate-delayed, or delayed implant placement in fresh extraction sockets yields better outcomes in terms of implant success, complications, aesthetics, and patient satisfaction. Fourteen RCTs were identified through comprehensive database searches up to June 2010, but only 7 met inclusion criteria (≥1 year follow-up after functional loading). Four RCTs addressed placement timing and three addressed bone augmentation techniques at immediate sites. Due to small sample sizes and high risk of bias in most trials, authors concluded evidence was insufficient to recommend any specific timing strategy. The review also found no superiority of autogenous bone over xenograft substitutes for gap filling at immediate implant sites.

## Key Contributions

- **Definitional framework**: Clearly delineated three placement timing categories — immediate (Type 1: same-day extraction), immediate-delayed (Type 2: weeks to ~2 months, after soft tissue healing), and delayed (Type 3: after bone healing)
- **Timing comparisons**: No statistically significant differences between immediate and delayed implants (2 RCTs, n = 126); immediate-delayed showed better aesthetics and satisfaction at 2 years vs. delayed but significantly more complications at 5 years
- **Complication quantification**: RR = 4.20 (95% CI 1.01–17.43) for complications in immediate-delayed vs. delayed at 5 years
- **Augmentation evidence**: No significant difference found between autogenous bone and bone substitutes (anorganic bovine bone acceptable); no augmentation technique proven superior
- **Evidence gap**: Only 7/14 identified RCTs eligible; all underpowered; most at high risk of bias — establishes clear evidence gap as of 2010

## Methodology

- **Design**: Cochrane systematic review with meta-analysis
- **Search**: Cochrane Oral Health Group's Trials Register, CENTRAL (Issue 2/2010), MEDLINE (1950–June 2010), EMBASE (1980–June 2010); handsearching of dental journals
- **Inclusion criteria**: RCTs comparing immediate/immediate-delayed/delayed implant placement or bone augmentation procedures at immediate implant sites; follow-up ≥1 year after functional loading
- **Data analysis**: Random-effects models; RR with 95% CI (dichotomous outcomes); mean difference (continuous outcomes); patient as statistical unit
- **Quality assessment**: Independent duplicate screening and data extraction; trial authors contacted for missing information

## Results

| Comparison | RCTs (n patients) | Key outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Immediate vs. delayed | 2 (126 patients) | No significant differences in any outcome |
| Immediate-delayed vs. delayed | 1 (46 patients) | Better aesthetics/satisfaction at 2y; more complications at 5y (RR 4.20, CI 1.01–17.43) |
| Immediate vs. immediate-delayed | 1 (16 patients) | No differences at 2 years |
| Autogenous vs. no augmentation | 1 (26 patients) | No significant difference |
| Augmentation technique A vs. B | 2 (56 patients) | No technique superiority |

**Mucosal aesthetics at 2 years (immediate-delayed vs. delayed)**: RR = 1.68 (95% CI 1.04–2.72) — significantly more patients judged to have appropriate peri-implant mucosal level by blinded assessor  
**Patient-perceived treatment time**: Immediate-delayed group perceived time to functional loading as significantly shorter at 2 years  
**5-year complications**: Differences in aesthetics/satisfaction disappeared, but complication burden remained significantly higher in immediate-delayed group  
**Bone substitutes**: Anorganic bovine bone (ABB) can substitute autogenous graft at immediate placement sites without significant difference in outcomes

**Authors' conclusions**: "There is insufficient evidence to determine possible advantages or disadvantages of immediate, immediate-delayed or delayed implants… There is a suggestion that immediate and immediate-delayed implants may be at higher risks of implant failures and complications than delayed implants, on the other hand the aesthetic outcome might be better when placing implants just after teeth extraction."
