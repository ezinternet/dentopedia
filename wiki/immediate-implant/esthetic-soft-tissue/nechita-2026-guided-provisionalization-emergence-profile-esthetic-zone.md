## One-line Summary

Retrospective single-arm cohort (n=20 single esthetic-zone implants) of a digitally planned, guide-delivered immediate provisionalization protocol using prefabricated crowns on **non-antirotational 5° Morse taper** implants: provisional retention without major intervention 75%, 1-year marginal bone loss 0.37 mm, buccal contour gain 1.41 mm, complete papilla preserved in 70%, 100% implant survival.

## 한줄요약

심미부위 단일 임플란트 20개 후향 단일군 코호트 — 디지털 계획·가이드 전달 즉시 프로비저널라이제이션(무회전방지 5° Morse taper) 결과: 주요 개입 없는 프로비저널 유지율 75%, 1년 변연골소실 0.37 mm, 협측 윤곽 증가 1.41 mm, 완전 유두 보존 70%, 임플란트 생존율 100%.

## Summary

This single-center, single-operator retrospective cohort tests an unusual variant of immediate provisionalization for emergence-profile shaping in the esthetic zone: a digitally designed, **guide-delivered prefabricated screw-retained provisional crown seated on a non-antirotational (indexless) 5° Morse taper implant–abutment connection**. The rationale is that omitting the anti-rotation index lets the clinician rotationally fine-tune the prefabricated crown intraoperatively, compensating for minor guided-placement deviations so the planned cervical/facial contour seats optimally against the peri-implant soft tissue — something a fixed antirotational index would prevent.

Across 20 consecutive single-tooth cases (18 anterior, 2 premolar; mostly thin phenotype, 35% immediate post-extraction, 90% grafted) followed ≥1 year after definitive crown, all implants integrated (100% survival) and every case reached definitive crown delivery. The primary outcome — provisional retention without major intervention — was 75%, with the remaining 25% being simple chairside re-screwing/re-cementation events that did not damage the connection or disrupt soft-tissue conditioning. Peri-implant outcomes were favorable: 12-month marginal bone loss 0.37 mm (all <1 mm), facial contour gain of 1.41 mm in all cases, and complete papilla fill in 70%. The authors emphasize this is a feasibility signal, not a comparative claim — there is no antirotational control, the sample is small and 95% female, and contour gain reflects the entire grafted protocol rather than the provisional alone.

## Key Contributions

- First focused clinical report on a **non-antirotational (indexless) 5° Morse taper** immediate provisionalization workflow for emergence-profile shaping, distinct from conventional antirotational single-crown protocols.
- Documents that an indexless screw-retained provisional can shape the esthetic-zone emergence profile with low biologic complications and favorable 1-year tissue stability, at the cost of a 25% (benign, chairside-managed) dislodgement rate.
- Frames provisional contour design via critical/subcritical contour and the Esthetic Biological Contour model.

## Methodology

- **Design**: retrospective single-arm cohort (STROBE-reported), single center/operator, no comparator group; no a priori power calculation. α = 0.05; Wilson 95% CIs, Fisher/Freeman–Halton, Mann–Whitney U, Wilcoxon, Spearman.
- **Sample**: 20 consecutive single esthetic-zone implants (1/patient), 19F/1M, mean age 38.1 y; 18 anterior + 2 premolar. Thin phenotype 60%; healed sockets 65% vs immediate placement 35%; flapless 95%; grafting in 90% (soft-tissue alone 50%, combined soft+hard 40%).
- **Implants**: all primary insertion torque ≥30 N·cm (mean 34.75); 3.5/4.0 mm diameters; immediately restored with a digitally designed non-antirotational composite provisional on a 5° Morse taper.
- **Timepoints**: T0 surgery+provisional → T1 1–2 wk → T2 maturation + definitive impression → T3 definitive crown → T4 12 mo.
- **Outcomes**: primary = provisional retention without major intervention; secondary = biologic complications, papilla score, marginal bone change (panoramic/OPT, ImageJ) at T0–T3 & T3–T4, buccal contour change (T0 vs T2 intraoral-scan superimposition, Exocad).

## Results

| Outcome | Value |
|---|---|
| Implant survival | 100% (20/20); definitive crown 100% |
| **Provisional retention w/o major intervention** | **75.0% (15/20; 95% CI 53.1–88.8)** |
| Provisional dislodgement (all chairside-managed) | 25.0% (5/20; 95% CI 11.2–46.9) |
| Biologic/technical complications (each 5%, 1/20) | BoP, suppuration, midfacial recession (1 mm), chairside occlusal adjustment |
| Complete papilla (score 3) | 70.0% (14/20; 95% CI 48.1–85.5) |
| MBL early T0–T3 | 0.24 ± 0.20 mm |
| MBL late T3–T4 | 0.14 ± 0.03 mm |
| **Total 12-mo MBL** | **0.37 ± 0.20 mm** (all <1 mm; 80% <0.5 mm) |
| **Buccal contour gain (T0→T2)** | **+1.41 ± 0.48 mm** (positive in all 20) |
| Unplanned visits during maturation | mean 1.0 ± 1.4 (60% had none) |

- Most peri-implant bone remodeling occurred in the early healing/conditioning phase; limited late change after definitive crown (Wilcoxon W=53.5, p=0.054).
- Buccal contour gain categorized mild <1 mm 25% / moderate 1.0–1.9 mm 60% / pronounced ≥2 mm 15%; no negative change.
- Exploratory: no significant predictors of dislodgement (phenotype p=0.092, socket status, grafting, torque category, etc.); contour gain confounded by grafting in 90% of sites.
- **Caveats**: no antirotational control, n=20, 95% female, panoramic (not periapical/CBCT) bone measurement, contour gain reflects the whole protocol — feasibility signal only, not a superiority claim.
