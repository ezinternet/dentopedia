## One-line Summary

Prospective clinical study (n=12 implant sites, 10 patients) showing that peri-implant soft tissue emergence profile discrepancy (EPD) begins immediately after interim restoration removal and grows logarithmically over time, while papilla and gingival margin height remain stable (≤0.27 mm at 20 min).

## Summary

During digital intraoral scanning (IOS) for esthetic implant restorations, the peri-implant soft tissue collapses after the interim restoration (IR) is removed, creating a mismatch between the established emergence profile and the contour captured by the scanner. This prospective study is the first to quantify the **time course** of this collapse.

Ten participants with 12 single-implant sites in the esthetic zone underwent serial optical scans: one with the IR in place (reference) and multiple scans from 0 seconds to 20 minutes after IR removal. Emergence profile discrepancy (EPD) — the deviation from the IR-supported contour — was the primary outcome.

**Key finding**: EPD was significant immediately (0 seconds) and increased logarithmically with time (EPD ∝ ln[time]). In contrast, mesial papilla, distal papilla, and gingival margin height changes were minimal and clinically insignificant (≤0.27 mm at 20 minutes). Palatal soft tissue thickness increased over time. Gingival thickness and implant depth showed significant positive correlations with EPD magnitude at certain sites.

**Clinical implication**: The emergence profile cannot be reliably captured once the IR is removed — even a delay of seconds introduces a measurable error that worsens over time. Proximal contact design is not affected (papilla is stable), but the circumferential emergence zone requires the IR to be in place or a customized scan body replicating the emergence profile.

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies soft tissue collapse kinetics: EPD follows a **logarithmic time function**, meaning the steepest rate of change is in the first seconds/minutes
- Establishes that **immediate scanning after IR removal is insufficient** — discrepancy is already present at 0 sec
- Differentiates stable (papilla/gingival margin) vs. unstable (emergence profile) dimensions — allows targeted clinical strategy
- Thicker gingiva and deeper implant placement amplify EPD — high-risk anatomy for emergence profile inaccuracy
- Supports use of **IR as a scan body** or a customized emergence profile scan body to capture contour without removing the provisional

## Methodology

- **Design**: Prospective clinical study
- **N**: 10 patients, 12 single-implant sites (esthetic zone)
- **Intervention**: Serial IOS scans from 0 sec to 20 min after IR removal; reference scan with IR in place
- **Outcomes**: Mesial/distal papilla height, gingival margin height, facial/palatal thickness, EPD
- **Statistics**: Linear mixed model; ln(time) predictor; Pearson correlation with gingival thickness and implant depth
