## One-line Summary

Single-center retrospective cohort (n=120; 60 immediate vs 60 delayed, single maxillary anterior teeth): despite significantly lower primary stability at placement (ISQ 63.1 vs 71.5, P<.001), immediate placement achieved equivalent 12-month success (98.3% vs 96.7%) and final ISQ (P=.589) once thresholds (ITV ≥20 N·cm, ISQ ≥50) were met, with less marginal bone loss (0.48 vs 0.85 mm) and higher Pink Esthetic Score (11.47 vs 10.05).

## Summary

According to PubMed, this retrospective cohort study from Wuhan University ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1097/MD.0000000000048502)) compared immediate (n=60) versus delayed (n=60) implant placement for single maxillary anterior teeth, with the central question being whether **primary stability** governs the success and esthetics of immediate placement. The immediate group started with significantly lower primary stability — both insertion torque (ITV 31.5 vs 36.3 N·cm, P=.003) and resonance-frequency ISQ (63.1 vs 71.5, P<.001) — because fresh sockets offer less bone-to-implant contact than healed/preserved sites. Yet by 12 months the difference had vanished (final ISQ 69.7 vs 70.6, P=.589) and implant success was statistically equivalent (98.3% vs 96.7%, P=.559).

Crucially, the cohort was restricted to cases meeting **minimum primary-stability thresholds (ITV ≥20 N·cm, ISQ ≥50)** plus an intact labial plate and thick gingival biotype — so the message is conditional: under strict indications and adequate primary stability, immediate placement matches delayed placement biologically while delivering an esthetic and patient-experience advantage (less MBL, higher PES, higher satisfaction). The discussion additionally cites ITV ≥30 N·cm as the practical adequacy target.

## Key Contributions

- Reframes the immediate-vs-delayed esthetic-zone debate around **primary stability as the gating variable**: lower starting ISQ does not predict worse outcomes when a stability floor is met.
- Shows the immediate-vs-delayed **ISQ gap closes by 12 months** (secondary stability catches up), consistent with Granić et al.
- Documents a consistent **esthetic + MBL + satisfaction advantage for immediate placement with immediate provisionalization** in carefully selected anterior cases.

## Methodology

- Retrospective cohort, single maxillary anterior teeth (incisor/canine), 2022–2024, n=120 (60/60). No a priori sample size calculation.
- **Immediate group**: atraumatic extraction → palatal-initiated osteotomy → WeGo implant crestal/1–3 mm subcrestal, >1.5 mm jumping gap grafted with Bio-Oss + Bio-Gide → immediate provisional → definitive at 6 mo.
- **Delayed group**: extraction → socket preservation (Bio-Oss + collagen membrane) → 6-mo heal → implant + immediate provisional → definitive at 6 mo.
- **Inclusion floor**: ITV ≥20 N·cm, ISQ ≥50, intact labial plate, thick gingival biotype, good hygiene, no bruxism.
- **Outcomes** at placement and 12 mo: ITV, ISQ (Osstell IDx, mesial+distal mean), success/survival, MBL (parallel periapical radiographs), soft-tissue indices, PES (7-item 0–14, 2-examiner mean), VAS satisfaction, adverse events.

## Results

| Outcome | Immediate (n) | Delayed (n) | P |
|---|---|---|---|
| 12-mo success | 98.33% (59/60) | 96.67% (58/60) | .559 |
| Primary ITV (N·cm) | 31.53 ± 8.72 | 36.27 ± 7.95 | .003 |
| Primary ISQ | 63.10 ± 5.72 | 71.46 ± 4.28 | <.001 |
| Final ITV (12 mo) | 36.49 ± 7.28 | 37.15 ± 5.77 | .588 |
| Final ISQ (12 mo) | 69.74 ± 9.17 | 70.62 ± 8.35 | .589 |
| MBL (12 mo, mm) | 0.48 ± 0.07 | 0.85 ± 0.18 | <.001 |
| PES (0–14) | 11.47 ± 0.96 | 10.05 ± 1.36 | <.001 |
| Total VAS satisfaction | 54.47 ± 5.25 | 46.24 ± 4.57 | <.001 |

- Significant PES items favoring immediate: labial mucosa curvature (P=.006), labial mucosa height (P=.032), soft-tissue texture (P<.001); papillae, color, root convexity NS.
- Soft-tissue indices (plaque, recession, BoP, PD) all NS between groups.
- Satisfaction higher for immediate in keratinized-mucosa height (P=.024), overall esthetics (P=.004), treatment-duration perception (P<.001), surgical-procedure-count perception (P<.001).
- Adverse events 10.0% vs 13.33% (P=.570); 3 peri-implantitis-driven failures (1 immediate, 2 delayed).
- **Limitations**: selected cases with adequate primary stability only; single-center, strict inclusion; modest n, 12-mo follow-up, no power calculation; PES inter-examiner reliability not formally assessed.
