## One-line Summary

Single-arm prospective clinical trial (n=26 implants/26 patients) of anterior-maxilla immediate implant placement planned with the "Safe Angle Concept" and executed with a novel plateau-drill 2-guide protocol: planned-vs-actual deviation was 4.56 ± 3.14° angular, 0.91 ± 0.61 mm platform, 1.47 ± 0.88 mm apex — all implants stayed within the safe angle, none required angled abutments, and 9-month PES was 11.89 ± 1.75.

## Summary

Immediate implants in the anterior maxilla tend to be pushed **facially** because osteotomy drills follow the path of least resistance down the palatal socket slope — facially placed shoulders carry ~3× more midfacial recession and frequently force the use of angled abutments. This trial tests a workflow designed to keep the implant in a biologically/prosthetically safe palatal position:

1. **Planning** with the *Safe Angle Concept* (incisal root angle defines a safe range for the implant shoulder), and
2. **Execution** with a novel **2-guide "plateau drilling" protocol** — a plateau guide first drives a flat milling cutter down the planned long axis to the socket apex, flattening the palatal slope, *then* a second drilling guide carries the conventional osteotomy drills. The implant is finally seated free-hand.

Across 26 anterior-maxilla sites (mostly lateral incisors), deviation fell within clinically accepted guided-surgery ranges, every implant ended up within the safe angle, all were restorable with straight abutments, and esthetics (PES 11.89) and labial bone (avg 2.13 mm) were good at 9 months. It is a **single-arm proof-of-concept** — there is no control group, so it cannot yet claim superiority over standard guided IIP.

## Key Contributions

- First in-patient clinical application of the **Safe Angle Concept** for IIP positioning (previously only a CBCT planning proposal).
- A **plateau-drill 2-guide protocol** that mechanically eliminates the palatal socket slope to counter drill slippage and labial implant inclination.
- Quantifies the resulting planned-vs-actual deviation and shows it does not compromise 9-month esthetic/labial-bone outcomes.

## Methodology

- **Design**: prospective single-arm clinical trial, NCT05750004; n=26 patients/26 implants (19F/7M, age 32.69 ± 6.27). Sites: lateral incisor 15, central incisor 7, canine 4. Inclusion required a thin (<1 mm) intact labial plate and ≥3–5 mm apical bone.
- **Workflow**: lab-scanned cast (STL) + pre-op CBCT (DICOM) superimposed in Implant Studio (3Shape, 3-point + ICP). Safe-Angle virtual plan → plateau guide + drilling guide 3D-printed with Straumann T-sleeves. Tapered Ti-Zr implant (Straumann BLT Roxolid SLA) placed free-hand, ≥35 Ncm, non-functional immediate loading.
- **Outcomes**: 3-month scan-body capture of actual position → planned-vs-actual superimposition on Exocad (angular / global platform / global apex deviation). 9-month CBCT (labial bone at platform/2/5 mm; safe-angle verification; root inclination), PES (2 examiners, k=0.82), FMPS/FMBS.

## Results

| Outcome | Result (mean ± SD) |
|---|---|
| Angular deviation | **4.56 ± 3.14°** |
| Global platform deviation | **0.91 ± 0.61 mm** |
| Global apex deviation | **1.47 ± 0.88 mm** |
| Implants within safe angle | **26/26 (100%)** |
| Angled abutments needed | **0 (all straight)** |
| Labial bone (avg of platform/2/5 mm) | 2.13 ± 0.99 mm |
| PES (9 mo) | 11.89 ± 1.75 (median 12.5, range 8–14) |
| Root inclination angle | 26.64 ± 5.43° |

- Deviation is comparable to a guided-surgery SR+MA benchmark (~1 mm entry, 1.6 mm apex, 5–6° axis).
- **Regression**: lateral/canine sites and female gender were significant positive predictors of **platform** (R=0.593, p=0.003) and **apical** (R=0.355, p=0.036) deviation; the **angular** model was unfit (ANOVA p=0.114). Two lateral-incisor sites had the largest angular deviation (narrow labio-palatal socket, long/wide roots, limited apical bone).
- FMPS 15.54→10.0 and FMBS 11.08→7.04 (both p<0.001).
- **Caveat**: the baseline table reports 26/26 (100%) survival while the text mentions "two implants that failed" — an unresolved internal inconsistency in the paper.

**Bottom line**: planning with the Safe Angle Concept and executing with the plateau-drill 2-guide protocol kept all anterior-maxilla immediate implants within a safe palatal range with accepted deviation and good 9-month esthetics — but as a single-arm study it is hypothesis-supporting, not comparative.
