## Summary
ITI-affiliated narrative review (Periodontol 2000, Buser et al.) tracing the historical development of implant timing protocols in the esthetic zone from 1975 to 2013. Provides current clinical recommendations for when to use ITI Type I (immediate), Type II (early 4–8wk), Type III (early 12–16wk), or Type IV (late >6mo) approaches.

## Key Contributions
- Established ITI Type I–IV timing classification (originally Hämmerle et al. 2003)
- Type I (immediate): highest risk — 20–30% risk of >1mm midfacial recession without selection criteria
- Proper selection (intact facial wall + medium/thick biotype) substantially reduces recession risk
- Type II/III (early): lower recession risk; better for compromised biotype or borderline sockets
- All timing types: survival >95%
- Three dominant risk factors for esthetic failure: thin biotype, buccal implant malposition, thin/damaged facial wall

## ITI Timing Summary
| Type | Timing | Socket Status | Recommendation |
|------|--------|---------------|----------------|
| Type I | Immediate | Infection-free; intact facial wall; thick biotype | High skill; strict criteria |
| Type II | Early 4–8wk | Soft tissue healed; no bone fill | Safer; reduced recession risk |
| Type III | Early 12–16wk | Partial bone fill | Compromised sockets |
| Type IV | Late >6mo | Healed ridge | Most predictable; often needs augmentation |

## Esthetic Risk Factors
1. **Thin tissue biotype** — increased mucosal recession
2. **Buccal implant shoulder position** — 3× more recession (Evans 2008)
3. **Thin or damaged facial bone wall** — primary driver of buccal collapse
